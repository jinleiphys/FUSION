# Constraints on the nuclear Schiff moment from its correlation with electromagnetic properties

> We study the nuclear Schiff moments of $^{129}$Xe and $^{199}$Hg induced by the nucleon electric dipole moment using large-scale shell model calculations. For $^{129}$Xe, we find a linear relation between the leading-order contribution and magnetic moment, which would be useful in reducing the theoretical uncertainty. The conventional model space does not contain the $ 0g_{ 9/2 } $ and $ 0h_{ 9/2 } $ orbitals, which are connected to the spin-orbit partners by large matrix elements. Thus, to evaluate the influence of the relevant single-particle orbitals outside the conventional model space, we apply the quasiparticle vacua shell model method. Moreover, the next-to-leading-order contribution arises from parity and time-reversal violation inside the nucleus. We demonstrate that these secondary effects do not induce any significant disturbance to the correlation. Additionally, we report the shell model results for the nuclear Schiff moment coefficients of $^{199}$Hg. Compared to previous studies, the results obtained in this study are rather large, indicating a higher sensitivity to the neutron electric dipole moment.

## Key claim

The paper claims a linear correlation between the leading-order nuclear Schiff moment coefficient \( s_n \) and the magnetic moment for \(^{129}\)Xe, which can reduce theoretical uncertainty; however, this correlation is established only for the ground state and does not hold for \(^{199}\)Hg, where the magnetic moment is useless for diagnosing wave-function contamination.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell model (LSSM) calculations using the SNV and SN100PN effective interactions for \(^{129}\)Xe and the Kuo-Herling interaction for \(^{199}\)Hg, within the standard model space between magic numbers 50 and 82. To include the \(0g_{9/2}\) and \(0h_{9/2}\) orbitals outside this space, they applied the quasiparticle vacua shell model (QVSM) with extended model spaces (SNV-\(sdgh\) and SNJKV interactions). They also revisited pair-truncated shell model (PTSM) calculations with corrected harmonic oscillator frequencies and compared all results to identify correlations between the Schiff moment coefficients and electromagnetic properties.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{129}\)Xe, the LSSM (SNV) calculation gives \( s_n = 0.42 \, \text{fm}^2 \) and the QVSM (SNV-\(sdgh\)) gives \( s_n = 0.48 \, \text{fm}^2 \); the PTSM (YH04) gives \( s_n = -0.32 \, \text{fm}^2 \), showing a sign discrepancy.
- Using the correlation with magnetic moment, the authors obtain a weighted average \( s_n = 0.29 \pm 0.10 \, \text{fm}^2 \) for \(^{129}\)Xe.
- For \(^{129}\)Xe, the third-order contribution from the nucleon EDM is \( a_n = 0.053 \, \text{fm}^2 \) and \( a_p = -0.001 \, \text{fm}^2 \) (LSSM with SNV).
- The combined sensitivity of the \(^{129}\)Xe NSM to the neutron EDM is \( s_n + a_n = 0.47 \, \text{fm}^2 \).
- For \(^{199}\)Hg, the LSSM ground-state calculation yields \( s_n = 2.44 \, \text{fm}^2 \) and \( s_p = 0.02 \, \text{fm}^2 \); the combined sensitivity is \( s_n + a_n = 2.3 \, \text{fm}^2 \).
- Using the \(^{199}\)Hg atomic EDM experimental limit from Graner (2016) and atomic calculations from Sahoo (2018), the authors derive a constraint on the neutron EDM: \( |d_n| < 1.8 \times 10^{-26} \, e \, \text{cm} \).

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of nuclear Schiff moments for \(^{129}\)Xe and \(^{199}\)Hg, specifically addressing discrepancies between the PTSM calculations of Yoshinaga (2010) and the LSSM and QVSM results. The correlation with magnetic moment provides a new method to reduce theoretical uncertainty, which was not exploited in earlier mean-field calculations by Ban (2010) or Dmitriev (2003). The results enable tighter constraints on the neutron electric dipole moment from atomic EDM experiments, complementing the direct neutron EDM measurements.
