# Microscopic shell-model description of the exotic nucleus ^{16}C

> The structure of the neutron-rich carbon nucleus ^{16}C is described by introducing a new microscopic shell model of no-core type. The model space is composed of the 0s, 0p, 1s0d, and 1p0f shells. The effective interaction is microscopically derived from the CD-Bonn potential and the Coulomb force through a unitary transformation theory. Calculated low-lying energy levels of ^{16}C agree well with the experiment. The B(E2;2_{1}^{+} \to 0_{1}^{+}) value is calculated with the bare charges. The anomalously hindered B(E2) value for ^{16}C, measured recently, is well reproduced.

## Key claim

The anomalously hindered B(E2; 2₁⁺ → 0₁⁺) value for ¹⁶C is well reproduced using bare charges (no effective charges) within a no-core-type shell model, but only after a minimal refinement of neutron one-body energies based on unitary-model-operator approach (UMOA) results for ¹⁵C; the "original" calculation without this refinement gives B(E2) = 1.30 e²fm⁴, while the "dressed" calculation gives 0.84 e²fm⁴, compared to the experimental value of 0.63 e²fm⁴.

## Method

The authors construct a no-core-type shell model with a model space comprising the 0s, 0p, 1s0d, and 1p0f shells. The effective two-body interaction is derived from the CD-Bonn potential and the Coulomb force through a double unitary transformation (the unitary-model-operator approach, UMOA), with a boundary parameter ρ₁ = 6 defining the large P₁ space and a smaller P₂ space for the actual calculation. Nucleon excitations from the hole states of ¹⁴C are restricted to up to two nucleons, and excitations to the 1p0f shell are also limited to up to two nucleons; a minimal refinement of neutron one-body energies (Δεⁿ(1s₁/₂) = -2.18 MeV, Δεⁿ(0d₅/₂) = -0.04 MeV) is applied to reproduce the correct single-particle ordering in ¹⁵C from UMOA.

## Key numbers

- Calculated B(E2; 2₁⁺ → 0₁⁺) for ¹⁶C: "original" = 1.30 e²fm⁴, "dressed" = 0.84 e²fm⁴; experimental = 0.63 e²fm⁴ (with statistical uncertainty +0.11/-0.11 and systematic uncertainty ±0.16)
- Calculated excitation energies for ¹⁶C (dressed): 2₁⁺ = 1.67 MeV, 0₂⁺ = 3.82 MeV, 2₂⁺ = 4.31 MeV, 3₁⁺ = 4.62 MeV, 4₁⁺ = 4.98 MeV; experimental values: 1.77, 3.03, 3.99, 4.09, 4.14 MeV respectively
- Calculated B(E2; 2₁⁺ → 0₁⁺) for ¹⁴C (both original and dressed): 3.42 e²fm⁴; experimental = 3.74 ± 0.50 e²fm⁴
- Calculated B(E2; 2₁⁺ → 0₁⁺) for ¹⁸C (dressed): 2.10 e²fm⁴ (no experimental value given)
- Neutron one-body energy refinements: Δεⁿ(1s₁/₂) = -2.18 MeV, Δεⁿ(0d₅/₂) = -0.04 MeV
- Calculated ground-state energy of ¹⁴C (dressed, with 4p4h): -90.90 MeV; experimental = -105.28 MeV
- Ratio Mₙ/Mₚ for ¹⁶C (dressed): 4.68; experimental values: 7.6 ± 1.7 (Elekes 2004) and 6.7 ± 1.3 (Ong 2006)
- For ¹⁵C, calculated B(E2; 5/2₁⁺ → 1/2₁⁺) with bare charges = 0.28 e²fm⁴; experimental = 0.97 ± 0.02 e²fm⁴

## Context

This work extends the no-core shell model (NCSM) and unitary-model-operator approach (UMOA) to the neutron-rich carbon isotope ¹⁶C, where previous conventional shell-model calculations using effective charges (Warburton 1992, Maddalena 2001, Fujimoto 2003, Sagawa 2004) and anti-symmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations (Thiamova 2004, Enyo 2005) overpredicted the B(E2) value. The present approach is the first microscopic description using bare charges that reproduces the anomalously hindered B(E2) in ¹⁶C, though it cannot simultaneously describe the B(E2) in ¹⁵C. The method enables future inclusion of effective three-body interactions from bare two- and three-body forces and suggests that effective charges should be smaller as neutron richness increases in carbon and boron isotopes.
