# Correlated nucleons in configuration space

> Several recent studies have dealt with the effects of short range correlations on the momentum distribution of nucleons in nuclei. Here we investigate the correlation effects on the density and spectral distribution in coordinate space. A combination of experimental data and spectral functions calculated from realistic N-N interactions allows us to resolve a recently uncovered discrepancy with occupation of quasi-particle states derived from (e,e'p) data.

## Key claim

The paper resolves a discrepancy between low-\(q\) and high-\(q\) (e,e'p) measurements of quasi-particle occupation numbers in \(^{12}\text{C}\) by showing that only the high-\(q\) occupation, after correcting for the correlated (continuum) contribution, is compatible with the independent constraint from elastic electron scattering; the low-\(q\) occupation is too low due to uncorrected final-state interaction and meson-exchange current effects.

## Method

The authors construct the quasi-particle (QP) density in coordinate space for \(^{12}\text{C}\) from (e,e'p) data, using Woods-Saxon radial wave functions fitted by Lapikas et al. (2000) and occupation numbers renormalized to the high-\(q\) measurement of Rohe et al. (2004). The total point-proton density is obtained from elastic electron scattering data via a model-independent SOG (sum-of-Gaussians) analysis, unfolding the finite proton and neutron charge distributions. The correlated density is then derived as the difference between the total point density and the QP density, and compared to a theoretical correlated density calculated from a Green's-function method using the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential.

## Key numbers

- The summed 1p plus 1s quasi-particle strength from low-\(q\) (e,e'p) data is 3.4 protons (56% occupation); from high-\(q\) data it is 5.0–5.6 protons (~87% occupation) before correction.
- After correcting the high-\(q\) data for the continuum contribution using the calculated spectral function, the QP occupation becomes 4.5 protons, compared to 3.4 from low-\(q\) data.
- The correlated density contributes ~30% of the central density in \(^{12}\text{C}\), despite correlated nucleons representing only ~20% of the total nucleon number.
- The 1s QP momentum distribution from Lapikas et al. (2000) required an 11% reduction in its extent in \(k\)-space to remove the contamination from correlated strength.
- The elastic electron scattering data cover momentum transfer between 0.13 and 3.7 fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of short-range correlations (SRC) from momentum space to coordinate space, building on prior calculations for light nuclei and infinite nuclear matter by Pandharipande (1997), Pieper (2001), Benhar (1989), and Muther (2000). It directly addresses the discrepancy in quasi-particle occupations reported by Lapikas et al. (2000) between low-\(q\) and high-\(q\) (e,e'p) experiments. The resulting correlated density in \(r\)-space explains why independent-particle-model wave functions fail to reproduce total nuclear densities and form factors, and it enables a consistency check that validates the high-\(q\) (e,e'p) interpretation over the low-\(q\) one.
