# Relativistic bound states in Yukawa model

> The bound state solutions of two fermions interacting by a scalar exchange are obtained in the framework of the explicitly covariant light-front dynamics. The stability with respect to cutoff of the J$^π$=$0^+$ and J$^π$=$1^+$ states is studied. The solutions for J$^π$=$0^+$ are found to be stable for coupling constants $α={g^2\over4π}$ below the critical value $α_c\approx 3.72$ and unstable above it. The asymptotic behavior of the wave functions is found to follow a ${1\over k^{2+β}}$ law. The coefficient $β$ and the critical coupling constant $α_c$ are calculated from an eigenvalue equation. The binding energies for the J$^π$=$1^+$ solutions diverge logarithmically with the cutoff for any value of the coupling constant. For a wide range of cutoff, the states with different angular momentum projections are weakly split.

## Key claim

The authors find that bound state solutions for two fermions interacting via scalar exchange in covariant light-front dynamics are stable (finite binding energy as cutoff goes to infinity) for the Jπ=0+ state only when the coupling constant α is below a critical value αc≈3.72, while for Jπ=1+ states the binding energies diverge logarithmically with cutoff for any coupling constant value.

## Method

The authors solved the two-fermion bound state equations in the explicitly covariant light-front dynamics (CLFD) framework using the ladder approximation with one-boson exchange kernel for scalar coupling. They derived systems of two-dimensional integral equations for the scalar components of the Jπ=0+ wave function (two components) and for the Jπ=1+ wave function (split into a two-component subsystem with a=0 and a four-component subsystem with a=1), then solved these equations numerically to study the cutoff dependence of binding energies and the asymptotic behavior of wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Critical coupling constant αc ≈ 3.72 for Jπ=0+ states, below which solutions are stable with respect to cutoff and above which the system collapses
- Wave function asymptotic behavior follows a 1/k^(2+β) law, where β and αc are calculated from an eigenvalue equation
- For Jπ=1+ states, binding energies diverge logarithmically with cutoff for any value of the coupling constant

## Context

This work extends previous studies of two-fermion bound states in the Yukawa model using the Tamm-Dancoff method by Glazek and Wilson (1993), where it was found that the J=0+ binding energy is cutoff dependent due to the kernel approaching a constant at large momenta. The authors use the explicitly covariant light-front dynamics formalism developed by Karmanov (1976) and show that their CLFD equations are strictly equivalent to those of Glazek and Wilson after a linear transformation of wave function components and change of variables. The results enable further investigation of renormalization procedures for light-front Hamiltonians and provide a framework for calculating electromagnetic form factors of relativistic bound states.
