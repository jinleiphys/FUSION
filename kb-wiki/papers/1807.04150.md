# Transport phenomena with chiral fermions in strong magnetic fields

> I discuss the electrical conductivity, bulk viscosity, and heavy-quark diffusion coefficient in strong magnetic fields putting emphasis on consequences of the chirality conservation on the perturbative relaxation dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper argues that chirality conservation in the lowest Landau level (LLL) of quarks in a strong magnetic field (eB >> T²) fundamentally alters perturbative relaxation dynamics, leading to transport coefficients that are enhanced by inverse powers of the current quark mass, even in the high-temperature limit where T >> m_f.

## Method

The author derives the longitudinal electrical conductivity and bulk viscosity by solving a (1+1)-dimensional Boltzmann equation for LLL quarks, where the transverse momentum serves only as a label for degenerate states. The collision term is computed using perturbative QCD with appropriate resummations in finite temperature and strong magnetic field, finding that leading-order contributions come from 1-to-2 (and 2-to-1) processes rather than the usual 2-to-2 processes, because the gluon transverse momentum acts as an effective mass in the (1+1)-dimensional kinematics. For heavy-quark diffusion, the Langevin equation is employed with momentum diffusion coefficients computed from one-gluon exchange diagrams involving thermal light-quark and gluon scatterers.

## Key numbers

* Longitudinal electrical conductivity: σ_zz = N_c Σ_f e_f² (|e_f B|/2π) × [4T/(g² C_F m_f² ln(T/M))], where M² = min[m_f², (g²/2π) Σ_f |e_f B|/(2π)]
* Longitudinal bulk viscosity: ζ_∥ = N_c Σ_f (|e_f B|/2π) × [m_f²/(g² C_F ln(T/M))] × [4/π² - (56/3)×0.0304...]
* Heavy-quark transverse diffusion coefficient from quark scatterers: κ_⟂^(quark) ∼ α_s² T × eB × ln(T²/(α_s eB))
* Heavy-quark longitudinal diffusion coefficient from gluon scatterers: κ_∥^(gluon) ∼ α_s² T × T² × ln(T²/(α_s eB))
* Anisotropy ratio: κ_∥/κ_⟂ ∼ T²/(eB)

## Context

This work extends perturbative calculations of transport coefficients in strong magnetic fields, where previous attention focused on anomaly-induced transport such as the chiral magnetic effect. The results for electrical conductivity and bulk viscosity (Hattori 2016, Hattori 2017) show that chirality conservation produces m_f⁻² enhancements, contrasting with the m_f⁴ suppression found in zero-field calculations by Arnold (2006). The heavy-quark diffusion analysis (Fukushima 2015) predicts anisotropic momentum diffusion that could generate v₂ of open heavy-flavor mesons, providing a new probe of the initial magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions. Related progress in weaker magnetic fields where higher Landau levels participate is noted in work by Li (2017) and Fukushima (2017).
