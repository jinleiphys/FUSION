# Thermodynamics of SU(N) Yang-Mills theories in 2+1 dimensions I - The confining phase

> We compute the equation of state in the confining phase of SU(N) Yang-Mills theories with N=2, 3, 4, 5 and 6 colors in 2+1 dimensions, via lattice simulations. At low enough temperatures, the results are accurately described by a gas of glueballs, including all known states below the two-particle threshold. Close to the deconfinement temperature, however, this prediction underestimates the numerical results, and the contribution from heavier glueballs has to be included. We show that the spectral density of the latter can be accurately described using a simple bosonic string model.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the equation of state in the confining phase of SU(N) Yang-Mills theories in 2+1 dimensions is accurately described by a gas of non-interacting glueballs only at low temperatures; close to the deconfinement temperature, the contribution from heavier glueballs must be included, and the spectral density of these heavier states can be accurately described using a simple bosonic string model.

## Method
The authors performed lattice simulations of SU(N) Yang-Mills theories with N=2, 3, 4, 5, and 6 colors in 2+1 dimensions, using the standard Wilson lattice gauge action on isotropic cubic lattices. They computed the trace of the energy-momentum tensor from differences of plaquette expectation values at finite temperature and zero temperature, and obtained the pressure via the integral method, then derived the energy and entropy densities from thermodynamic identities. The temperature scale was set using lattice results from Liddle (2008) for the ratio T_c/√σ, and continuum extrapolations were performed by repeating simulations at multiple lattice spacings (N_t=6, 7, 8).

## Key numbers
- For the SU(2) gauge group, simulations were performed on lattices with N_t=6, 7, and 8, with statistics of 1×10^5 configurations at each β-value for both zero and finite temperature.
- For the SU(3) gauge group, simulations on 64²×8 lattices used 5×10^5 to 8×10^5 configurations at finite temperature across β-ranges from 15.0 to 32.0.
- The ratio T_c/√σ in the large-N limit is reported as 0.9026(23), with a finite-N correction coefficient of 0.880(43) from Liddle (2008).
- The uncertainty on the temperature scale determination is estimated to be of the order of 1%.
- The lightest glueball in the SU(3) spectrum has mass about 1.4 GeV in physical units.

## Context
This work extends the study of Meyer (2009) for SU(3) Yang-Mills in 3+1 dimensions to the lower-dimensional case of 2+1 dimensions, and generalizes it to multiple color numbers (N=2 through 6). The results are compared with the hadron resonance gas model using glueball masses from Teper (1998), and with a bosonic string model inspired by Isgur and Paton (1984). The paper enables tests of the large-N limit predictions for the equation of state in the confining phase, and provides a more stringent test of string-inspired models for the glueball spectrum because in 2+1 dimensions the Hagedorn temperature is expected to equal the deconfinement temperature.
