# Magnetic Dipole Sum Rules for Odd-Mass Nuclei

> Sum rules for the total- and scissors-mode M1 strength in odd-A nuclei are derived within the single-j interacting boson-fermion model. We discuss the physical content and geometric interpretation of these sum rules and apply them to ^{167}Er and ^{161}Dy. We find consistency with the former measurements but not with the latter.

# Magnetic Dipole Sum Rules for Odd-Mass Nuclei

## Key claim
The summed M1 strength to the scissors mode in an odd-A nucleus equals the summed M1 strength in the neighboring even-even nucleus to order O(1/N), but only under the assumptions of good F-spin symmetry, a single-j orbital for the odd fermion, and the large-N approximation for the boson core. The total M1 strength additionally depends on the average cosine of the angle between the odd nucleon angular momentum and the total angular momentum, which can be extracted from magnetic moments.

## Method
Sum rules for total and scissors-mode M1 strength were derived within the single-j interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM) using tensor operator identities, the Wigner-Eckart theorem in F-spin space, and F-spin Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. The formalism was applied to two odd-mass nuclei with an odd neutron in the 1i13/2 orbital: 161Dy and 167Er, using experimental B(M1) values from neighboring even-even nuclei and magnetic moments to determine the average core angular momentum and the angle between the single-particle and total angular momenta. Pauli-forbidden contributions were subtracted using the strong coupling limit of the IBFM.

## Key numbers
- For 161Dy: measured scissors strength is 0.71(0.10) μN², while the core B(M1) from 160Dy is 2.42(0.18) μN², indicating significant missing strength.
- For 167Er: measured scissors strength is 3.14(1.12) μN², while the core B(M1) from 166Er is 2.67(0.19) μN², showing consistency within experimental errors.
- Magnetic moment of 161Dy: μJ = -0.480 μN; magnetic moment of 167Er: μJ = -0.56385 μN.
- Bare Schmidt g-factor for the 1i13/2 neutron orbital: g_j,Schmidt = -0.2943 μN; quenched value: 0.7 g_j,Schmidt = -0.2060 μN.
- Weighted boson g-factor from 160Dy: ḡ = 0.362 μN; from 166Er: ḡ = 0.318 μN.

## Context
This work extends the IBM sum rule for even-even nuclei derived by Ginocchio (1991), which related M1 strength to the number of quadrupole bosons in the ground state, to odd-mass nuclei within the IBFM framework. It addresses puzzling experimental results where initial measurements on odd nuclei (Dy, Gd, Tb) by Kneissl and collaborators indicated missing B(M1) strength compared to neighboring even-even nuclei, while 167Er showed comparable strengths with contributions from higher energies. The sum rules enable extraction of the average number of quadrupole bosons and the relative orientation of single-particle and total angular momenta from experimental data, and identify 161Dy as having unexpectedly missing strength that may reside at higher or lower energies not yet accessed.
