# Strangeness Production Process $pp \to nK^ + Σ^ +$ within Resonance Model

> We explore production mechanism and final state interaction in the $pp \to nK^ + Σ^ +$ channel based on the inconsistent experimental data published respectively by COSY-11 and COSY-ANKE. The scattering parameter $a> 0$ for $nΣ^ +$ interaction is favored by large near-threshold cross section within a nonrelativistic parametrization investigation, and a strong $nΣ^ +$ interaction comparable to $pp$ interaction is also indicated. Based on this analysis we calculate the contribution from resonance $Δ^* (1920)$ through $π^ +$ exchange within resonance model, and the numerical result suggests a rather small near-threshold total cross section, which is consistent with the COSY-ANKE data. With an additional sub-threshold resonance $Δ^*(1620)$, the model gives a much better description to the rather large near-threshold total cross section published by COSY-11.

# Strangeness Production Process $pp \to nK^+ \Sigma^+$ within Resonance Model

## Key claim

The paper argues that the large discrepancy between COSY-11 and COSY-ANKE data for the $pp \to nK^+ \Sigma^+$ channel near threshold can be explained by different $n\Sigma^+$ final-state interaction strengths, but only if the near-threshold cross section is as large as the COSY-11 data indicate; the authors caution that the data are too scarce and uncertain to draw a definite conclusion about an anomalous near-threshold behavior.

## Method

The authors use a resonance model at tree level with effective Lagrangians, considering only $\Delta^*(1920)$ through $\pi^+$ exchange as the primary contribution, and later adding a sub-threshold $\Delta^*(1620)$ resonance with both $\rho^+$ and $\pi^+$ exchange. Final-state interactions (FSI) for the $n\Sigma^+$ system are included via a Watson-Migdal factorization using a Jost function parameterization, with scattering length $a$ and effective range $r$ as free parameters. A nonrelativistic parametrization of the total cross section is also employed to explore the near-threshold region independently of the full resonance model.

## Key numbers

- The scattering length for $n\Sigma^+$ interaction extracted from COSY-11 data is $a = 7.1\ \text{fm}$ (with $r = 1.0\ \text{fm}$), compared to the $pp$ interaction values $a = -7.8\ \text{fm}$ and $r = 2.8\ \text{fm}$.
- The $\Delta^*(1920)$ resonance has a width of $200\ \text{MeV}$, with a branching ratio to $N\pi$ of 0.125 (adopted from a range of 0.05-0.20) and to $\Sigma K$ of 0.021 (adopted from a range of 0.01-0.03), giving coupling constants $g^2/4\pi = 0.0034$ and $0.0292$, respectively.
- The cut-off parameter for the $\pi$ meson form factor is $\Lambda_\pi = 1.3\ \text{GeV}$.
- The nonrelativistic parametrization uses $\left| M \right|^2 = 18.9 e^{-1.3\varepsilon} \cdot 10^6\ \mu\text{b}$ (with $\varepsilon$ in GeV) for the FSI parameters $a = -1.6\ \text{fm}$, $r = 3.2\ \text{fm}$ (from $\alpha = -75.0\ \text{MeV}$, $\beta = 200.0\ \text{MeV}$).
- For the COSY-11 data, the parametrization uses $\left| M \right|^2 = 20 e^{-1.7\varepsilon} \cdot 10^6\ \mu\text{b}$ with $\alpha = 30\ \text{MeV}$, $\beta = 360\ \text{MeV}$.

## Context

This work addresses the inconsistency between COSY-11 data (2004) and COSY-ANKE data (2006) for the $pp \to nK^+ \Sigma^+$ channel, extending prior resonance-model studies of strangeness production in $pp \to pK^+ \Lambda$ and $pp \to pK^+ \Sigma^0$ by various authors (e.g., the resonance model framework of Penner and Mosel, and the FSI parametrization of Faldt and Wilkin). The analysis enables future experimental resolution of the discrepancy through planned measurements at COSY-ANKE and the Cooling Storage Ring (CSR) in Lanzhou, China, which could provide proton beams of $1$ to $2.8\ \text{GeV}$ for accurate differential observables and Dalitz plot analysis.
