# From Non-interacting to Interacting Picture of Quark Gluon Plasma in presence of magnetic field and its fluid property

> We have attempted to build a parametric based simplified and analytical model to map the interaction of quarks and gluons in presence of magnetic field, which has been constrained by quark condensate and thermodynamical quantities like pressure, energy density etc., obtained from the calculation of lattice quantum chromodynamics. To fulfill that mapping, we have assumed a parametric temperature and magnetic field dependent degeneracy factor, average energy, momentum and velocity of quarks and gluons. Implementing this QCD interaction in calculation of transport coefficient at finite magnetic field, we have noticed that magnetic field and interaction both are two dominating sources, for which the values of transport coefficients can be reduced. Though the methodology is not so robust, but with the help of its simple parametric expressions, one can get a quick rough estimation of any phenomenological quantity, influenced by temperature and magnetic field dependent QCD interaction.

### Key claim
The authors propose a simplified parametric quasi-particle model that maps the temperature- and magnetic-field-dependent QCD interaction by fitting lattice QCD thermodynamics, but they explicitly note that the methodology is "not so robust" and is intended only for "quick rough estimation" of phenomenological quantities.

### Method
The authors construct a quasi-particle description of quark-gluon plasma by introducing temperature- and magnetic-field-dependent tuning parameters: a degeneracy factor \(g(T,B)\), average energy \(E_{\rm av}(T,B)\), average momentum \(k_{\rm av}(T,B)\), and average velocity \(v_{\rm av}(T,B)\). These parameters are fitted to reproduce lattice QCD data for pressure, energy density, and entropy density at finite magnetic field (Bali et al., 2014; Bali et al., 2012). The fitted parameters are then used to compute the anisotropic components of shear viscosity (\(\eta_{\parallel}, \eta_{\perp}\)) and electrical conductivity (\(\sigma_{\parallel}, \sigma_{\perp}\)) in the presence of a magnetic field, using expressions derived from kinetic theory with a constant relaxation time \(\tau_c\).

### Key numbers
- The Stefan-Boltzmann limit for pressure of non-interacting QGP is \(P \approx 5.2\,T^4\), and for energy density \(\epsilon \approx 15.6\,T^4\).
- The hadronic matter (pion+kaon) massless limits are \(P_{HM} \approx 0.76\,T^4\) and \(\epsilon_{HM} \approx 2.28\,T^4\).
- At zero magnetic field, the ratio of interacting to non-interacting shear viscosity shows a reduction of 50% at \(T = 0.300\) GeV and up to 98.5% at \(T = 0.120\) GeV; for electrical conductivity, the reduction ranges from 30% at \(T = 0.300\) GeV to 90% at \(T = 0.120\) GeV.
- The magnetic field strengths considered are \(eB = 0.0, 0.2, 0.4\) GeV\(^2\).

### Context
This work extends the earlier quasi-particle model of Satapathy et al. (2019) for zero magnetic field to include finite magnetic field effects, fitting lattice QCD thermodynamics from Bali et al. (2012, 2014). It provides a simple parametric tool for estimating transport coefficients (shear viscosity and electrical conductivity) in magnetized QGP, enabling quick phenomenological estimates for heavy-ion collision modeling, though the authors note that a more robust treatment of the dissipation interaction (relaxation time) remains framework-dependent and is not addressed here.
