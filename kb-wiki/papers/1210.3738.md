# Interaction of vector mesons with baryons and nuclei

> After some short introductory remarks on particular issues on the vector mesons in nuclei, in this paper we present a short review of recent developments concerning the interaction of vector mesons with baryons and with nuclei from a modern perspective using the local hidden gauge formalism for the interaction of vector mesons. We present results for the vector baryon interaction and in particular for the resonances which appear as composite states, dynamically generated from the interaction of vector mesons with baryons, taking also the mixing of these states with pseudoscalars and baryons into account. We then venture into the charm sector, reporting on hidden charm baryon states around 4400 MeV, generated from the interaction of vector mesons and baryons with charm, which have a strong repercussion on the properties of the $J/ΨN$ interaction. We also address the interaction of $K^*$ with nuclei and make suggestions to measure the predicted huge width in the medium by means of the transparency ratio. The formalism is extended to study the phenomenon of $J/ψ$ suppression in nuclei via $J/ψ$ photoproduction reactions.

## Key claim

Using the local hidden gauge formalism, the authors show that vector meson-baryon interactions dynamically generate baryon resonances with nearly degenerate spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 partners, and that the predicted large in-medium width of the $K^*$ meson (about five times its vacuum width) can be measured via transparency ratio experiments, though the results depend on the specific treatment of pseudoscalar-baryon mixing and self-consistency in the medium.

## Method

The authors employ the local hidden gauge formalism to construct vector meson-vector meson and vector meson-baryon interactions, solving the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation in the on-shell factorization approach. For the baryon sector, they incorporate mixing between vector-baryon and pseudoscalar-baryon channels via box diagrams and Kroll-Ruderman contact terms, including both octet and decuplet baryons in intermediate states. The in-medium $K^*$ self-energy is calculated self-consistently from two sources: the decay $K^* \to K\pi$ modified by the medium, and the $K^* N$ interaction including resonant structures identified with $\Lambda(1800)$ and $\Sigma(1750)$.

## Key numbers

- The $K^{*-}$ width in vacuum is calculated as 42 MeV, close to the experimental value of $50.8 \pm 0.9$ MeV.
- At normal nuclear matter density $\rho_0$ and zero momentum, the $K^*$ acquires a width of 260 MeV, about five times its vacuum width.
- A hidden charm baryon state with $(I,S) = (1/2,0)$ is found at a pole position $4415 - 9.5i$ MeV, corresponding to a mass of 4412 MeV and total width 47.3 MeV, with a partial width to $J/\psi N$ of 19.2 MeV.
- Two hidden charm baryon states with $(I,S) = (0,-1)$ are found at pole positions $4368 - 2.8i$ MeV (mass 4368 MeV, width 28.0 MeV) and $4547 - 6.4i$ MeV (mass 4544 MeV, width 36.6 MeV), with $J/\psi\Lambda$ partial widths of 5.4 MeV and 13.8 MeV respectively.
- For the $N^*(1650)$ ($J^P = 1/2^-$) the theory gives a pole at $1690 + i24$ MeV (without $\rho$ and $K^*$ width convolution), with a real-axis mass of 1658 MeV and width 98 MeV, compared to PDG mass 1645-1670 MeV and width 145-185 MeV.
- For the $N^*(1700)$ ($J^P = 3/2^-$) the theory gives a pole at $1703 + i4$ MeV (without convolution), with a real-axis mass of 1705 MeV and width 103 MeV, compared to PDG mass 1650-1750 MeV and width 50-150 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to pseudoscalar meson-baryon interactions (Kaiser 1995, Oller and Meissner 2001, Jido 2003) to the vector meson sector using the hidden gauge formalism of Bando et al. (1985). It builds on earlier vector-baryon studies by the same group (Angelsvec 2011) and incorporates pseudoscalar-baryon mixing following the SU(6) framework of Gamermann et al. (2011). The results enable predictions for baryon spectroscopy that can be tested at Jefferson Lab (Price 2004) and provide a framework for interpreting $J/\psi$ suppression in nuclear photoproduction reactions, as well as suggesting specific transparency ratio measurements to observe the predicted large $K^*$ width in nuclei.
