# Analytic self-gravitating $4$-Baryons, traversable NUT-AdS wormholes, flat space-time multi-Skyrmions at finite volume and a novel transition in the $SU(3)$-Skyrme model

> We construct the first analytic self-gravitating Skyrmions with higher Baryon charge in four dimensions for the $SU(3)$-Skyrme-Einstein-$Λ$ theory by combining the generalized hedgehog ansatz with the approach developed by Balachandran et al. to describe the first (numerical) example of a non-embedded solution. These are genuine $SU(3)$ analytic solutions instead of trivial embeddings of $SU(2)$ into $SU(3)$ and its geometry is that of a Bianchi IX Universe. The Skyrme ansatz is chosen in such a way that the Skyrme field equations are identically satisfied in the sector with Baryon charge 4. The field equations reduce to a dynamical system for the three Bianchi IX scale factors. Particular solutions are explicitly analyzed. Traversable wormholes with NUT-AdS asymptotics supported by a topologically non-trivial $SU(3)$-sigma soliton are also constructed. The self-gravitating solutions admit also a suitable flat limit giving rise to Skyrmions of charge 4 confined in a box of finite volume maintaining the integrability of the $SU(3)$ Skyrme field equations. This formalism discloses a novel transition at finite Baryon density arising from the competition between embedded and non-embedded solutions in which the non-embedded solutions prevail at high density while are suppressed at low densities.

## Key claim

The paper constructs the first analytic self-gravitating Skyrmions with Baryon charge 4 in the SU(3)-Skyrme-Einstein-Λ theory, but the hedge is that these solutions exist only for a specific ansatz that identically satisfies the Skyrme field equations, reducing the Einstein equations to a dynamical system for the Bianchi IX scale factors; the claimed novel transition between embedded and non-embedded solutions at finite Baryon density is demonstrated only for static configurations in a flat box of finite volume.

## Method

The authors combine the generalized hedgehog ansatz with the SO(3) subgroup of SU(3) generated by Gell-Mann matrices {λ₇, -λ₅, λ₂}, following the approach of Balachandran et al. for a diBaryon. They promote the four profile functions to depend on curved space-time coordinates and choose them so that the SU(3) Skyrme field equations are identically satisfied on any metric of the form ds² = -dt² + ρ(t)²[(dγ + cosθ dφ)² + dθ² + sin²θ dφ²] with compact spatial sections. The Einstein equations then reduce to ordinary differential equations for the scale factor ρ(t), and the construction is extended to a general Bianchi IX metric and to a flat metric with finite volume. For the flat case, the eight coupled SU(3) Skyrme field equations collapse to a single Sine-Gordon equation for a scalar profile H(t,z).

## Key numbers

- Baryon charge of the SU(3) configuration: B = 4, computed from the topological density ρ_B^(SU(3)) = 6 sinθ integrated over the compact spatial coordinates.
- Topological density of the SU(2) embedded configuration: ρ_B^(SU(2)) = (3/2) sinθ, giving B = 1.
- Ratio of static volumes: Δ = Vol(SU(3))/Vol(SU(2)) = (1/(2√2)) * ((2Kκ - 1)/(Kκ - 2))^(3/2).
- For the NUT-AdS wormhole: ρ(γ) = √(3(κK - 2)/(4|Λ|)) cosh(√(|Λ|/3) γ) and Q² = κK, with Λ < 0.
- Phase transition occurs at box size L₀* ≈ 1.4 (with K = 2, λ = 1, lengths in fm). For L₀ = 0.01, E^(SU(3)) = 147.81 vs. E^(SU(2))_(2,2) = 283.131; for L₀ = 1.4, E^(SU(3)) = 20.899 vs. E^(SU(2))_(2,2) = 20.455; for L₀ = 10, E^(SU(3)) = 141.528 vs. E^(SU(2))_(2,2) = 129.980.

## Context

This work extends the first analytic self-gravitating SU(2) Skyrmions constructed by Canfora et al. (2018) to genuine SU(3) configurations with higher Baryon charge, using the non-embedded ansatz first introduced numerically by Balachandran et al. for a diBaryon in flat space. The construction enables analytic study of self-gravitating multi-Baryons in Bianchi IX cosmology, traversable wormholes with NUT-AdS asymptotics, and a novel phase transition at finite Baryon density where non-embedded SU(3) solutions prevail at high density while trivially embedded SU(2) solutions prevail at low density, a phenomenon that could not be studied with previous numerical approaches.
