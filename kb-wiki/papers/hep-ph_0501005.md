# Quasilocal Quark Models as Effective Theory of Non-perturbative QCD

> We consider the Quasilocal Quark Model of NJL type (QNJLM) as an effective theory of non-perturbative QCD including scalar (S), pseudoscalar (P), vector (V) and axial-vector (A) four-fermion interaction with derivatives. In the presence of a strong attraction in the scalar channel the chiral symmetry is spontaneously broken and as a consequence the composite meson states are generated in all channels. With the help of Operator Product Expansion the appropriate set of Chiral Symmetry Restoration (CSR) Sum Rules in these channels are imposed as matching conditions to QCD at intermediate energies. The mass spectrum and some decay constants for ground and excited meson states are calculated.

## Key claim

The Quasilocal Quark Model of NJL type (QNJLM) can reproduce the lower part of the QCD meson spectrum, including ground and first excited states, by imposing Chiral Symmetry Restoration (CSR) Sum Rules as matching conditions to QCD at intermediate energies, but only within the large-\(N_c\) approximation and the large-log approximation \(\ln(\Lambda^2/M_0^2) \gg 1\).

## Method

The authors construct a two-channel quasilocal four-fermion Lagrangian with scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector interactions containing derivative formfactors. They integrate out quark fields in the large-\(N_c\) limit, retain quadratic fluctuations in auxiliary boson fields, and solve secular equations for meson masses. The coupling constants are tuned near a polycritical point to cancel quadratic divergences, and CSR Sum Rules derived from the Operator Product Expansion of QCD correlators are imposed as matching conditions.

## Key numbers

- \(m_\sigma = 2 M_0\) (Nambu relation), with the prediction \(m_\sigma \simeq 800\ \text{MeV}\) for the \(\sigma\) meson (identified with \(f_0(400-1200)\)).
- \(m_{\pi'} \simeq 1300 \pm 100\ \text{MeV}\) (identified with \(\pi(1300)\)); \(m_{\sigma'} \simeq 1200\div1500\ \text{MeV}\) (identified with \(f_0(1370)\)).
- \(m_\rho \simeq 770 \pm 0.8\ \text{MeV}\) (identified with \(\rho(770)\)); \(m_{\rho'} \simeq 1465 \pm 25\ \text{MeV}\) (identified with \(\rho(1450)\)); \(m_{a_1} \simeq 1230 \pm 40\ \text{MeV}\) (identified with \(a_1(1230)\)).
- Predicted mass range for the first axial-vector excitation: \(m_{a_1'} \cong 1465 \div 1850\ \text{MeV}\); most plausible mass difference \(m_{a_1'} - m_{\rho'} \approx 30\ \text{MeV}\).
- Decay constants from CSR Sum Rules: \(f_\rho \approx 0.15\) (experimental \(f_\rho = 0.20 \pm 0.01\)); \(f_a \approx 0.06\) (experimental \(f_a = 0.10 \pm 0.02\)).
- Chiral constant \(L_{10} \approx -6.0 \times 10^{-3}\) (experimental \(L_{10} = -(6.36 \pm 0.09 \mid_{\text{expt}} \pm 0.16 \mid_{\text{theor}}) \times 10^{-3}\)).
- Electromagnetic pion-mass difference estimated as \(\Delta m_\pi^4 \mid_{\text{em}} \simeq (3.85 \pm 0.16)\ \text{MeV}\) (experimental \(\Delta m_\pi \mid_{\text{em}}^{\text{expt}} \simeq (4.42 \pm 0.03)\ \text{MeV}\)).

## Context

This work extends the standard Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model (Nambu and Jona-Lasinio, 1961) by including higher-dimensional derivative four-fermion operators, following earlier quasilocal approaches (e.g., Andrianov and Andrianov, 1993; 1999). It applies CSR Sum Rules derived from the Operator Product Expansion of QCD correlators (Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov, 1979) as matching conditions, enabling the description of radial excitations beyond the ground-state mesons. The results enable predictions for excited meson masses and decay constants that can be tested against experimental data from the Particle Data Group (2004) and provide a framework for modeling Higgs particles in extensions of the Standard Model.
