# Baryon-baryon interactions from chiral effective field theory

> Results from an ongoing study of baryon-baryon systems with strangeness S=-1 and -2 within chiral effective field theory are reported. The investigations are based on the scheme proposed by Weinberg which has been applied rather successfully to the nucleon-nucleon interaction in the past. Results for the hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions obtained to leading order are reviewed. Specifically, the issue of extrapolating the binding energy of the H-dibaryon, extracted from recent lattice QCD simulations, to the physical point is addressed. Furthermore, first results for the hyperon-nucleon interaction at next-to-leading order are presented and discussed.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory, applied to baryon-baryon systems with strangeness S=-1 and S=-2, can describe hyperon-nucleon scattering data at next-to-leading order with quality comparable to advanced meson-exchange models, and can be used to extrapolate lattice QCD results for the H-dibaryon binding energy to the physical point, though the leading-order results for the S=-2 sector depend on an additional low-energy constant that cannot be reliably fixed from scarce data.

## Method

The baryon-baryon potentials were derived using the Weinberg power-counting scheme, consisting at leading order of four-baryon contact terms without derivatives and one-pseudoscalar-meson exchanges constrained by SU(3) flavor symmetry, and at next-to-leading order of contact terms with two derivatives and two-pseudoscalar-meson exchange diagrams. The reaction amplitudes were obtained by solving a regularized coupled-channels Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a regulator function exp[-(p'^4+p^4)/Lambda^4], using cutoff values in the range 500-700 MeV. For the S=-1 sector, the low-energy coefficients were fitted to 35 low-energy YN scattering data points and the hypertriton binding energy; for the S=-2 sector, the additional isospin I=0 contact term was fine-tuned to reproduce H-dibaryon binding energies from lattice QCD at unphysical meson and baryon masses, then the masses were varied toward physical values.

## Key numbers

- Hypertriton binding energy: -2.31 to -2.34 MeV at NLO (cutoff 500-700 MeV), compared to experimental value -2.354(50) MeV
- Lambda-proton singlet scattering length at NLO: -2.88 to -2.89 fm (cutoff 500-700 MeV)
- Lambda-proton triplet scattering length at NLO: -1.59 to -1.61 fm (cutoff 500-700 MeV)
- Sigma-plus-proton singlet scattering length at NLO: -3.90 to -3.83 fm (cutoff 500-700 MeV)
- Sigma-plus-proton triplet scattering length at NLO: 0.51 to 0.47 fm (cutoff 500-700 MeV)
- Lambda-Lambda scattering length at LO: -1.52 fm (for cutoff 600 MeV with C_{LambdaLambda->LambdaLambda}=0)
- Physical thresholds for S=-2 system: Lambda-Lambda at 2231.2 MeV, Xi-N at 2257.7 MeV, Sigma-Sigma at 2385.0 MeV (isospin-averaged masses)
- For the NPLQCD scenario: H-dibaryon binding energy 13.2 MeV at M_pi=389 MeV; at physical masses an unstable bound state appears in Xi-N about 5 MeV below threshold with a resonance at 21 MeV kinetic energy in Lambda-Lambda
- For the HAL QCD scenario: H-dibaryon binding energy 35 MeV at M_P=673 MeV and m_B=1485 MeV; at physical masses the bound state disappears entirely

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT approach previously applied successfully to the nucleon-nucleon interaction by Epelbaum (2005) to the hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon sectors, following earlier leading-order studies by Polinder (2006, 2007). The NLO results for YN scattering improve upon the LO description and are comparable to the Juelich '04 meson-exchange potential by Haidenbauer (2005) and the Nijmegen NSC97f potential by Rijken (1999). The H-dibaryon analysis connects to lattice QCD calculations by the NPLQCD Collaboration (Beane, 2011) and the HAL QCD Collaboration (Inoue, 2011), providing a chiral EFT framework for extrapolating their results to physical quark masses. The findings enable future studies of hypernuclear systems and guide experimental searches for the H-dibaryon.
