# Power Counting and the Renormalization Group for an Effective Description of NN Scattering

> I outline the power counting scheme recently introduced by M. Savage, M. Wise and myself for the effective field theory treatment of nucleon-nucleon scattering. It is particularly useful for describing systems with a large scattering length, and differs from Weinberg's power counting. A renormalization group analysis plays a big role in determining the order of a given operator. Pions are ignored in this discussion; how to incorporate them is discussed in M. Savage's talk, nucl-th/9804034.

# Power Counting and the Renormalization Group for an Effective Description of NN Scattering

## Key claim

The paper argues that Weinberg's approach of expanding the potential and then resumming via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation is not more accurate than directly expanding the S-matrix, and that the power divergence subtraction (PDS) scheme makes the power counting manifest for systems with a large scattering length, unlike the minimal subtraction (MS) scheme which fails because individual Feynman graphs do not scale as their nominal order in the momentum expansion.

## Method

The author develops an effective field theory for nonrelativistic nucleon-nucleon scattering using contact interactions in a derivative expansion, employing dimensional regularization with the PDS subtraction scheme that removes both logarithmic poles in D=4 and power-law poles in D=3. The renormalization group running of the contact couplings C_{2n}(mu) is computed from one-loop beta-functions, and the boundary conditions are fixed by matching to the effective range expansion at mu=p=0. The scattering amplitude is expanded directly rather than expanding the potential, with the leading order coming from summing bubble diagrams with C_0 vertices to all orders.

## Key numbers

- The scattering length a can diverge as the critical coupling eta approaches eta_1=1.7, where a bound state appears at threshold in the toy model
- For natural-size scattering length |a| ~ 1/Lambda, the couplings scale as C_{2n} ~ (4pi/M Lambda)(1/Lambda^{2n})
- For large scattering length |a| >> 1/Lambda, the PDS scheme gives C_{2n}(mu) ~ 4pi/(M Lambda^n mu^{n+1}), so that each vertex C_{2n} nabla^{2n} contributes p^{n-1} to the amplitude
- The PDS-subtracted loop integral is I_n^{PDS} = -(ME)^n (M/4pi)(mu + ip), compared to the MS result I_n^{MS} = -i(M/4pi) p^{2n+1}
- The leading amplitude CA_{-1} scales as p^{-1}, CA_0 scales as p^0, and CA_1 scales as p^1

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to NN scattering introduced by Weinberg (1990) and implemented by several groups including Bira (1993), Park (1993), and Cohen (1995). The PDS scheme was introduced in Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) and differs fundamentally from the MS scheme used in earlier work. The paper enables analytic calculations of two-nucleon processes on the same footing as chiral perturbation theory, and the author notes that the dibaryon approach of Kaplan (1997) corresponds to resumming the leading mu behavior of all C_{2n} coefficients. The three-body problem is identified as the next major challenge, with the three-body interaction being scheme-dependent due to renormalization by two-body interactions.
