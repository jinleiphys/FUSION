# Molecular charmed baryons and pentaquarks from light-meson exchange saturation

> The spectrum of the $c qq$ baryons contains a few states whose nature is not clearly a three-quark composite and which might have a sizable baryon-meson component. Examples include the $Σ_c(2800)$ or the $Λ_c(2940)$. Here we explore the spectrum of two-body systems composed of a light, octet baryon and a charmed meson (or antimeson) within a simple contact-range theory in which the couplings are saturated by light-meson exchanges. This results in the prediction of a series of composite anticharmed pentaquarks ($\bar{c} q qqq $) and singly-charmed baryons ($c \bar{q} qqq $). Among the later we find $J=\tfrac{1}{2}$ $ΞD$ and $J=\tfrac{3}{2}$ $ΞD^*$ bound states with masses matching those of the recently observed $Ω_c(3185)$ and $Ω_c(3327)$ baryons.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the recently observed $\Omega_c(3185)$ and $\Omega_c(3327)$ baryons can be explained as molecular $\Xi D$ and $\Xi D^*$ bound states with spins $J=\tfrac{1}{2}$ and $J=\tfrac{3}{2}$, respectively, but this identification depends on which reference state is used for calibration and the results carry significant uncertainties from the scalar meson mass.

## Method

The authors construct a contact-range effective potential for the interaction between a light octet baryon and a charmed meson or antimeson, where the coupling constants are saturated by the exchange of light scalar ($\sigma$) and vector ($\rho$, $\omega$, $\phi$) mesons. The scalar and vector meson couplings to hadrons are determined from the linear sigma model and vector meson dominance, with the magnetic couplings expressed in terms of constituent quark magnetic moments. The saturated couplings are evolved via renormalization group to a common scale, and the bound state equation (Lippmann-Schwinger equation) is solved with a Gaussian regulator at cutoff $\Lambda_H = 0.75$ GeV, using either the $\Lambda_c(2940)$ or $\Sigma_c(2800)$ as a reference state to fix the overall normalization.

## Key numbers

- Scalar meson coupling to nucleon: $g_S = 10.2$, derived from $g_S = \sqrt{2} m_N / f_\pi$ with $f_\pi \simeq 132$ MeV
- Vector meson coupling: $g_V = 2.9$; light quark magnetic moment: $\mu_u = 1.9$ (in nuclear magnetons); strange quark magnetic moment: $\mu_s = -0.6$
- Average vector meson mass: $m_V = (m_\rho + m_\omega)/2 = 775$ MeV; $\phi$ meson mass: $m_\phi = 1020$ MeV; scalar meson mass window: $m_\sigma = (400-550)$ MeV
- Calibrated coupling for $\Sigma_c(2800)$ reference: $C^{\rm sat}_{\rm ref} = -1.76$ fm$^2$; for $\Lambda_c(2940)$ reference: $C^{\rm sat}_{\rm ref} = -1.74$ fm$^2$
- Predicted masses for $\Omega_c(3185)$ and $\Omega_c(3327)$ as $\Xi D$ ($J=\tfrac{1}{2}$) and $\Xi D^*$ ($J=\tfrac{3}{2}$): thresholds are $3185.5$ MeV and $3326.9$ MeV respectively, with binding energies depending on reference state
- Inverse scattering length for $I=0$ $N\bar{D}$ system: $f_0^{-1} = +0.34$ fm$^{-1}$ (using $\Lambda_c(2940)$ reference) or $f_0^{-1} = -0.24$ fm$^{-1}$ (using $\Sigma_c(2800)$ reference), both within the ALICE constraint $f_0^{-1} \in [-0.4, 0.9]$ fm$^{-1}$

## Context

This work extends the contact-range saturation approach previously applied by the same authors to the $X(3872)$ and $Y(4230)$ (Peng 2020, Peng 2021) to the sector of charmed meson-light baryon systems. It provides a systematic SU(3)-flavor decomposition of the interaction, predicting a hierarchy of binding energies for molecular singly-charmed baryons and anticharmed pentaquarks. The results support molecular interpretations for the $\Lambda_c(2940)$ and $\Sigma_c(2800)$ (previously proposed by He 2006, Haidenbauer 2010, Sakai 2020) and offer candidate explanations for the $\Xi_c(3055)$, $\Xi_c(3123)$, and the newly observed $\Omega_c(3185/3327)$ from LHCb (2023). The predictions for anticharmed pentaquarks are compared with earlier work by Gignoux (1987), Lipkin (1987), Hofmann and Lutz (2005), and Yalikun and Zou (2021), showing qualitative agreement but quantitative differences that await experimental clarification.
