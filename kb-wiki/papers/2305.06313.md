# Constraint of pionless EFT using two-nucleon spectra from lattice QCD

> Finite-volume pionless effective field theory (FVEFT$_{ π\!/ }$) at next-to-leading order (NLO) is used to analyze the two-nucleon lattice QCD spectrum of Ref.~\cite{Amarasinghe:2021lqa}, performed at quark masses corresponding to a pion mass of approximately $800 $ MeV. Specifically, the effective theory is formulated in finite volume, and variational sets of wave functions are optimized using differential programming. Using these wave functions projected to the appropriate finite-volume symmetry group, variational bounds from FVEFT$_{π\!/ }$ are obtained for the ground state, as well as excited states. By comparison with the lattice QCD GEVP spectrum, different low energy constants (LECs) are constrained. Relativistic corrections are incorporated, allowing for the extractions of NLO LECs, as well as the leading $s$-$d$-wave mixing term in the deuteron channel.

## Key claim

Finite-volume pionless effective field theory (FVEFT$_{\pi\!/}$) at next-to-leading order can be used to analyze not only ground-state but also excited-state two-nucleon spectra from lattice QCD, including states in different irreducible representations of the finite-volume symmetry group and with nonzero total momentum, provided that relativistic corrections and $s$-$d$-wave mixing terms are incorporated into the Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors formulated pionless EFT at NLO in a finite periodic volume, including the first relativistic correction to the kinetic energy and operators with up to two derivatives in the interaction potential. They constructed variational wave functions using correlated Gaussian ansätze optimized via differential programming and automatic differentiation, then solved the generalized eigenvalue problem to obtain variational bounds on the ground and excited states. By matching these variational bounds to the lattice QCD GEVP spectrum from Amarasinghe (2021) at a pion mass of approximately 800 MeV, they constrained the leading-order and next-to-leading-order low-energy constants for both the deuteron and dineutron channels.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass of approximately 800 MeV for the lattice QCD data used (from Amarasinghe 2021)
- Box size of $M_\pi L = 4$, corresponding to $L = 5.67$ fm and $M_N L = 27.05$ for the numerical examples with physical masses
- Regulator scale set as $r_0 = 0.2$ fm for the interaction potential in the numerical examples
- Wave function sums evaluated up to $q_\text{cut} = 50$ and $\mathbf{b}_\text{cut} = (50,50)$ for periodic boundary conditions
- Optimization performed over $10^4$ gradient steps for wave function ansätze with $N_g = 8$ correlated Gaussians

## Context

This work extends previous applications of finite-volume pionless EFT (Detmold 2021, Sun 2022) that matched leading-order calculations to lattice QCD data from NPLQCD (2012). It goes beyond those studies by incorporating NLO terms, relativistic corrections, and $s$-$d$-wave mixing, and by analyzing excited states and different irreducible representations of the cubic group. This enables direct matching of finite-volume lattice QCD spectra to an EFT without requiring infinite-volume extrapolation, and provides an alternative to finite-volume multi-particle formalisms based on quantization conditions (Luscher 1986, Hansen 2014, Hammer 2017). The constrained LECs can then be used for infinite-volume many-body calculations in pionless EFT for systems up to atomic number $A=40$ (Bansal 2017).
