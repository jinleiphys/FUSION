# Non-equilibrium modifications of the nuclear equation of state

> Non-equilibrium modifications of the nuclear equation of state are assessed in the framework of Extended Irreversible Thermodynamics. The expected size of the non-equilibrium corrections to quantities like the nuclear-matter incompressibility $K$ and the liquid-vapor critical temperature $T_c$ are compared to the uncertainties associated at present with the usual equilibrium values. The quantitative estimates are obtained in situations characterized by a stationary planar shear flow of realistic magnitude, or by a stationary temperature gradient.

## Key claim

Non-equilibrium corrections to the nuclear-matter incompressibility and the liquid-vapor critical temperature, estimated using Extended Irreversible Thermodynamics for stationary shear flow and temperature gradients, are comparable to the present uncertainties in the equilibrium values, suggesting that further efforts to reduce those uncertainties using nuclear collision data may not be meaningful.

## Method

The authors applied Extended Irreversible Thermodynamics (EIT) to derive expressions for the non-equilibrium corrections to the isothermal incompressibility and the critical temperature, using a stationary planar shear flow and a constant temperature gradient as representative scenarios. For the incompressibility correction, they used transport simulation results for a Au+Au system at 400 MeV/nucleon to extract realistic values for shear rate, density, temperature, and viscosity. For the critical temperature correction, they adopted a Van der Waals equation of state for nuclear matter and estimated the heat flux from expected temperature gradients in expanding matter.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium nuclear-matter incompressibility is approximately K = 190 ± 30 MeV, based on assessments from sideward flow data in transport models (Pan and Danielewicz, 1993).
- For a Au+Au system at 400 MeV/nucleon, with shear rate γ ≈ 0.07 c, density ρ ≈ 0.30 fm⁻³, temperature T ≈ 44 MeV, and viscosity η ≈ 55 MeV/fm² c, the non-equilibrium correction to the incompressibility is K - K₀ ∼ 20 MeV.
- The non-equilibrium correction to the critical temperature of the liquid-vapor phase transition is estimated to be ≲ 2 MeV, for a critical temperature T_c ∼ 15 MeV and heat flux q ≲ 0.1 MeV c/fm³.

## Context

This work extends the application of Extended Irreversible Thermodynamics, previously developed for macroscopic fluids (Muller, 1967; Perez-Garcia, 1983), to the nuclear-matter equation of state used in heavy-ion collisions. The estimated non-equilibrium corrections are comparable to the uncertainties in the equilibrium incompressibility determined from giant monopole resonance (Blaizot, 1980; Sharma, 1988), supernova calculations (Baron, 1987), and transport model analyses (Pan and Danielewicz, 1993). The results suggest that the equilibrium nuclear-matter incompressibility and critical temperature may not be directly applicable to nuclear collision scenarios, and that the limiting temperature concept (Levit and Bonche, 1985; Suraud, 1987) might be more relevant for fragment formation.
