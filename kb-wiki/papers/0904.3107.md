# Nearly Perfect Fluidity: From Cold Atomic Gases to Hot Quark Gluon Plasmas

> Shear viscosity is a measure of the amount of dissipation in a simple fluid. In kinetic theory shear viscosity is related to the rate of momentum transport by quasi-particles, and the uncertainty relation suggests that the ratio of shear viscosity eta to entropy density s in units of hbar/k_B is bounded by a constant. Here, hbar is Planck's constant and k_B is Boltzmann's constant. A specific bound has been proposed on the basis of string theory where, for a large class of theories, one can show that eta/s is greater or equal to hbar/(4 pi k_B). We will refer to a fluid that saturates the string theory bound as a perfect fluid. In this review we summarize theoretical and experimental information on the properties of the three main classes of quantum fluids that are known to have values of eta/s that are smaller than hbar/k_B. These fluids are strongly coupled Bose fluids, in particular liquid helium, strongly correlated ultracold Fermi gases, and the quark gluon plasma. We discuss the main theoretical approaches to transport properties of these fluids: kinetic theory, numerical simulations based on linear response theory, and holographic dualities. We also summarize the experimental situation, in particular with regard to the observation of hydrodynamic behavior in ultracold Fermi gases and the quark gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper reviews evidence that the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density, eta/s, can approach the string theory bound of hbar/(4 pi k_B) in three distinct classes of quantum fluids, but it does not claim that any fluid has definitively saturated this bound; the experimental values quoted are upper limits or approximate minima, and the bound itself remains a conjecture.

## Method

The authors survey theoretical approaches to computing transport coefficients in strongly coupled quantum fluids: kinetic theory for weakly coupled quasi-particles, numerical simulations based on linear response theory (Kubo relations), and holographic dualities (AdS/CFT correspondence) for strongly coupled gauge theories. They then compare these theoretical predictions with experimental data on three representative systems: liquid helium-4, ultracold Fermi gases near a Feshbach resonance, and the quark gluon plasma created at RHIC.

## Context

This review synthesizes theoretical and experimental progress on the viscosity bound conjecture of Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2004), which proposed eta/s >= hbar/(4 pi k_B) based on string theory. It extends earlier work on kinetic theory of weakly coupled gauge theory plasmas by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2000, 2003) and on holographic calculations by Policastro, Son, and Starinets (2001). The paper enables systematic comparison of three disparate quantum fluids (liquid helium, ultracold Fermi gases, and the quark gluon plasma) as testbeds for the perfect fluid concept, and identifies open questions about whether the bound is universal and what other transport coefficients might be bounded.
