# Particle-phonon coupling effects within theory of finite Fermi systems

> Recent results of the study of the particle-phonon coupling (PC) effects in odd magic and semi-magic nuclei within the self-consistent theory of finite Fermi systems are reviewed. In addition to the usual pole diagrams, the non-pole ones are considered. Their contributions are often of a crucial importance. PC corrections to the single-particle energies for $^{40}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb are presented. The quadrupole moments of odd In and Sb isotopes, the odd-proton neighbors of even Sn isotopes, are presented also with accounting for the PC corrections. At last, recently announced problem of extremely high values charge radii of heavy Ca isotopes is solved in terms of a consistent consideration of the PC effects. In all the cases, rather good description of the data is obtained.

# Particle-phonon coupling effects within theory of finite Fermi systems

## Key claim
Particle-phonon coupling (PC) corrections to single-particle energies, quadrupole moments, and charge radii in odd magic and semi-magic nuclei can be described within the self-consistent theory of finite Fermi systems (TFFS) using perturbation theory in the phonon vertex squared, provided that non-pole ("phonon tadpole") diagrams are included alongside the usual pole diagrams; the non-pole contributions are often of opposite sign and comparable magnitude to the pole ones, and their inclusion is crucial for achieving agreement with experimental data.

## Method
The authors use the self-consistent TFFS with the Fayans energy density functional DF3-a to compute PC corrections in odd magic and semi-magic nuclei. For single-particle energies, they solve the Dyson equation including both pole and non-pole (phonon tadpole) diagrams at order g_L^2, where g_L is the vertex for creating an L-phonon. For quadrupole moments of odd In and Sb isotopes (odd-proton neighbors of even Sn isotopes), they separate PC corrections into an "end correction" (involving Z-factor renormalization), an induced interaction term, a triangle (GDD) diagram, and a non-pole diagram, with the singular parts of the triangle and non-pole diagrams cancelling each other. For charge radii of heavy Ca isotopes, they apply a fluctuating-part ansatz that subtracts the PC correction for 48Ca to isolate the isotope-dependent contribution.

## Key numbers
- For 208Pb, the rms deviation of theoretical single-particle energies from experiment is 0.51 MeV without PC corrections and 0.34 MeV with PC corrections; for the Skyrme EDF HFB-17, the corresponding value is 1.15 MeV.
- For 40Ca, the pole PC correction to the neutron 2p_{3/2} state is -0.833 MeV, the non-pole correction is +0.383 MeV, giving a total 3^- contribution of -0.450 MeV; the final single-particle energy with PC corrections is -5.902 MeV versus the experimental value -6.42 MeV.
- For 208Pb, the total PC correction to the neutron 3d_{3/2} state (including 3^-_1 and eight other phonons) is -0.462 MeV, yielding a final single-particle energy of -1.171 MeV versus the experimental -1.40 MeV.
- For 16 odd In and Sb isotopes, the rms deviation of quadrupole moments from experiment is 0.27 b without PC corrections and 0.15 b with PC corrections.
- For 109In (1g_{9/2} state), the quadrupole moment without PC corrections is +1.113 b, with PC corrections it becomes +1.00 b, versus the experimental value +0.84(3) b.
- For 121Sb (2d_{5/2} state), the quadrupole moment without PC corrections is -0.72 b, with PC corrections it becomes -0.51 b, versus the experimental value -0.36(4) b.
- The Z-factor for the 1g_{9/2} state in 109In is 0.573; for the 2d_{5/2} state in 117Sb it is 0.582.

## Context
This work extends the self-consistent TFFS approach developed by Khodel (1976) and the Fayans EDF framework (Fayans, 1998; DF3-a, 2007) to include particle-phonon coupling effects beyond the mean-field level. It addresses the same problem of PC corrections to single-particle energies previously studied within Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (Bortignon, Dobaczewski, Baldo) and relativistic mean-field theory (Litvinova and Ring), but with the novel inclusion of non-pole diagrams that are often omitted. The quadrupole moment calculations extend earlier mean-field TFFS results (2014) and apply a PC model first developed for magnetic moments (2013). The charge radius results resolve the "puzzle" of unexpectedly large charge radii of neutron-rich Ca isotopes reported by the experimental group (2016). This work enables more accurate predictions for nuclear structure observables in exotic nuclei, particularly for spectroscopic properties of odd nuclei near closed shells.
