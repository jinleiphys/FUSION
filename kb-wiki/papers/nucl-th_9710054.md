# The energy dependence of the in-medium $ηN$ cross section evaluated from $η$-photoproduction

> Within the Glauber formalism and a BUU transport model we analyze the $η$-photoproduction data from nuclei and evaluate the in-medium $ηN$ cross section. Our results indicate that the $ηN$ cross section is almost independent of the $η$ energy up to 200 MeV.

## Key claim

The in-medium ηN cross section is almost independent of the η kinetic energy up to about 130–200 MeV, in strong contradiction with the energy-dependent ηN cross sections derived from vacuum scattering models. However, the authors note that the effect of the nucleon potential, which cannot be treated correctly in their semi-classical calculation, might impact this conclusion.

## Method

The authors analyzed experimental data on η-photoproduction from nuclei (¹²C, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁹³Nb, ²⁰⁷Pb) at photon energies below 800 MeV using two complementary approaches: a Glauber model to extract the in-medium ηN cross section from the A-dependence of the nuclear transparency, and a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model that includes Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, and final-state interactions. The in-medium ηN cross section was obtained by fitting the ratios of energy-differential cross sections for different target nuclei, minimizing χ² for each η kinetic energy bin.

## Key numbers

- The extracted in-medium ηN cross section is approximately constant at 30 mb (inelastic) from threshold up to η kinetic energy of 130 MeV, as indicated by the dashed line in Fig. 4.
- The BUU calculations used a constant inelastic cross section of 30 mb and an elastic cross section of 20 mb to reproduce the experimental data (curve labelled 'constant cross sections (1)').
- The analysis used experimental data for photon energies Eγ < 800 MeV, with the η-production threshold on a free nucleon at approximately 706 MeV.
- The Glauber analysis considered targets of ¹²C, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁹³Nb, and ²⁰⁷Pb.
- The modified η-rescattering model (Eq. 4) used a parameter c = 0.3 GeV and a normalization constant of 40 mb·GeV² for the inelastic cross section.

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses of η-meson final-state interactions in nuclei, which had been limited by large experimental uncertainties in pA collisions (Chiavassa 1994, Golubeva 1993, Sibirtsev 1993). The authors use the more detailed and accurate photoproduction data from Krusche (1995) at MAMI. The results contradict vacuum ηN cross section models from Green and Wycech (1995) and Lee et al. (1995), which show a strong energy dependence, and also disagree with the resonance model of Teis (1997) that failed to describe angular and energy differential cross sections. The findings enable future studies using coherent η-photoproduction, which is more sensitive to nuclear transparency and could resolve remaining uncertainties in the Glauber analysis.
