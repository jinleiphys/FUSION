# Solving the rho - pi puzzle by higher charmonium Fock states

> A new explanation for the longstanding puzzle of the tiny branching fraction of psi' --> rho pi relative to that for J/psi --> rho pi was proposed. In the case of J/psi, we argue that this decay is dominated by a higher Fock state in which the c cbar pair is in a color-octet ^3S_1 state and annihilates via the process c cbar --> q qbar. In the case of the psi', we argue that the probability for the c cbar c pair in this higher Fock state to be close enough to annihilate is suppressed by a dynamical effect related to the small energy gap between the mass of the psi' and the D Dbar threshold.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the longstanding puzzle of the tiny branching fraction of psi' to rho pi relative to that for J/psi to rho pi can be explained by a higher Fock state mechanism, where the decay is dominated by a color-octet ^3S_1 c-cbar pair annihilating via c-cbar to q-qbar, and the suppression in psi' arises from a dynamical effect related to the small energy gap between the psi' mass and the D-Dbar threshold.

## Method

The authors use nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) to analyze charmonium decays, expressing the decay amplitude in terms of Fock state decompositions where the probability of each Fock state scales as a definite power of v, the typical relative velocity of the charm quark. They argue that for J/psi, the decay to rho pi is dominated by endpoint contributions from the |c-cbar_8(^3S_1) + S⟩ Fock state via the hard-scattering process c-cbar to q-qbar, while for psi', the suppression arises because the small energy gap to the D-Dbar threshold allows the c-cbar pair to respond to the repulsive short-distance potential, suppressing the wavefunction at the origin for the color-octet Fock state.

## Key numbers

* The "15% rule" prediction for the ratio Q_h = B(psi' to h) / B(J/psi to h) is (14.7 ± 2.3)% from the ratio of e+e- decay widths.
* The Mark II collaboration found Q_{rho pi} < 0.6% and Q_{K*K} < 2%.
* The BES collaboration obtained Q_{rho pi} < 0.23%, Q_{K*+ K-} < 0.64%, and Q_{K*0 Kbar0} = (1.7 ± 0.6)%.
* The suppression of Q relative to the 15% rule is about 10 for K*0 Kbar0 and greater than 65 for rho pi.
* The J/psi is 640 MeV below the D-Dbar threshold, while the psi' is only 43 MeV below D+ D- threshold and 53 MeV below D0 Dbar0 threshold.
* Preliminary BES measurement gives Q_{b1 pi} = (24 ± 7)% and Q_{rho a2} = (2.9 ± 1.6)%.
* The BES data constrains the glueball mass to |m_O - m_{J/psi}| < 80 MeV and width 4 MeV < Gamma_O < 50 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the explanation of the rho-pi puzzle proposed by Chen and Braaten (1998), building on the NRQCD factorization framework of Bodwin, Braaten, and Lepage (1995). It contrasts with earlier explanations including the glueball mixing hypothesis of Hou and Soni (1982), the helicity selection rule analysis of Brodsky, Lepage, and Tuan (1987), the node-in-wavefunction approach of Pinsky (1990) and Brodsky and Karliner (1993), and the final-state interaction model of Li, Bugg, and Zou (1997). The explanation enables testable predictions for the flavor dependence, J-PC dependence, and angular distributions of two-body decays of J/psi and psi', which can be verified by future BES data and lattice NRQCD simulations with dynamical light quarks.
