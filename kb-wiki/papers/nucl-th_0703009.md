# On the ground--state energy of finite Fermi systems

> We study the ground--state shell correction energy of a fermionic gas in a mean--field approximation. Considering the particular case of 3D harmonic trapping potentials, we show the rich variety of different behaviors (erratic, regular, supershells) that appear when the number--theoretic properties of the frequency ratios are varied. For self--bound systems, where the shape of the trapping potential is determined by energy minimization, we obtain accurate analytic formulas for the deformation and the shell correction energy as a function of the particle number $N$. Special attention is devoted to the average of the shell correction energy. We explain why in self--bound systems it is a decreasing (and negative) function of $N$.

# On the ground-state energy of finite Fermi systems

## Key claim

The paper shows that in self-bound fermionic systems (like atomic nuclei), the shell correction energy has a non-zero average that is negative and decreases with particle number N, in contrast to systems with fixed external potentials where the average is positive. This negative bias arises because the system deforms away from magic numbers to keep the oscillatory part of the energy as negative as possible, and the authors provide analytic formulas for this effect using a 3D harmonic oscillator model with volume conservation.

## Method

The authors use semiclassical periodic orbit theory (trace formula) to compute the oscillatory part of the single-particle level density for non-interacting fermions in 3D harmonic oscillator potentials, then derive the shell correction energy from the integrated level density. For self-bound systems, they impose volume conservation (product of frequencies constant) and minimize the total energy with respect to axial deformation parameter theta = Q3/Q1, keeping only the leading term in each sum of the shell correction expression and using a Taylor expansion for small deformations around the isotropic shape.

## Key numbers

- For triaxial irrational frequencies (Q1=sqrt(2), Q2=sqrt(3), Q3=sqrt(5)), the correction to the mean value from the oscillatory part vanishes as N^{-2/3}
- For the isotropic harmonic oscillator, magic numbers occur at N_MAGIC = i^3/3 where i is a positive integer
- For triaxial rational frequencies (Q1=17, Q2=18, Q3=19), the transition between triaxial and isotropic regimes occurs at N_c ~ 131060 (N_c^{1/3} ~ 51)
- For self-bound systems at mid-shell, the deformation parameter epsilon = theta - 1 is given by epsilon(N) = +/- 1.49 N^{-1/3}
- The self-bound shell correction energy at mid-shell is E_tilde_SB(N) = -0.0152 N^{2/3}

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Leboeuf and collaborators (2005) on shell effects in finite Fermi systems, where the fluctuating part of the energy was shown to have non-zero average for fixed confining potentials. The authors apply this to self-bound systems like atomic nuclei, where the shape of the mean field potential adjusts with particle number, unlike in cold dilute Fermi gases in magnetic traps where the external harmonic oscillator potential dominates. The results are compared to realistic nuclear shell correction calculations from Moller and Nix (1995), and the harmonic oscillator model follows the tradition of the Nilsson model for nuclear deformation. The work enables analytic understanding of why nuclear shell corrections are systematically negative and provides a framework for studying deformation effects in mesoscopic Fermi systems.
