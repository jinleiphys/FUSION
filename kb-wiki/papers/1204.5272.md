# Density-dependent deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum

> The deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum with the density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings is developed. The formulism is briefly presented with the emphasis on handling the density-dependent couplings, meson fields, and potentials in axially deformed system with partial wave method. Taking the neutron-rich nucleus $^{38}$Mg as an example, the newly developed code is verified by the spherical relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations, where only the spherical components of the densities are considered. When the deformation is included self-consistently, it is shown that the spherical components of density-dependent coupling strengths are dominant, while the contributions from low-order deformed components are not negligible.

## Key claim

The density-dependent deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (DDDRHB) theory in continuum is developed for axially deformed nuclei, and for the test case of neutron-rich ³⁸Mg the spherical components of the density-dependent coupling strengths are dominant, but the contributions from low-order deformed components (λ=2,4,6) are not negligible.

## Method

The authors extend the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum to include density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings, using the partial wave method to expand densities, coupling strengths, meson fields, and potentials in Legendre polynomials for axially deformed systems with spatial reflection symmetry. The RHB equations are solved in a spherical Dirac Woods-Saxon basis with a box size of 20 fm and mesh size 0.1 fm, using the functional PKDD for the particle-hole channel and a zero-range density-dependent pairing force. The code is verified by comparing spherical-case results (λ_ρ,max=0) for ³⁸Mg against spherical relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) calculations.

## Key numbers

- Total energy of ³⁸Mg in the spherical DDDRHB case: −254.50 MeV, agreeing with RCHB value −254.61 MeV to within 0.11 MeV (∼0.04% accuracy).
- Matter rms radius of ³⁸Mg in the spherical DDDRHB case: 3.53 fm, identical to the RCHB value to 0.01 fm.
- Quadrupole deformation β of ³⁸Mg in the deformed DDDRHB case (λ_ρ,max=4): 0.46.
- Matter rms radius of ³⁸Mg in the deformed DDDRHB case: 3.60 fm, an increase of ∼2% relative to the spherical case.
- Cutoffs adopted for Legendre expansions: λ_g,max=10 for coupling strengths and λ_ϕ,max=6 for meson fields, where relative differences in total energy, matter rms radius, and β between these cutoffs and the next higher values are less than 0.001%.
- Pairing strength V₀ = 348.40 MeV·fm³ with energy cutoff E_cut^(q.p.) = 60 MeV in the quasiparticle space.

## Context

This work extends the deformed RHB theory in continuum, previously developed with nonlinear meson self-coupling interactions (Zhou 2010, Li 2012), to accommodate density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings, which have been shown to improve descriptions of the equation of state at high density and isovector properties of nuclei far from stability (Niksic 2002, Long 2004). The new DDDRHB code enables self-consistent studies of halo phenomena in deformed neutron-rich nuclei using modern density-dependent functionals, and the authors demonstrate its accuracy by benchmarking against the spherical RCHB code of Meng (1998).
