# Measurement of neutron star parameters: a review of methods for low-mass X-ray binaries

> Measurement of at least three independent parameters, for example, mass, radius and spin frequency, of a neutron star is probably the only way to understand the nature of its supranuclear core matter. Such a measurement is extremely difficult because of various systematic uncertainties. The lack of knowledge of several system parameter values gives rise to such systematics. Low-mass X-ray binaries, which contain neutron stars, provide a number of methods to constrain the stellar parameters. Joint application of these methods has a great potential to significantly reduce the systematic uncertainties, and hence to measure three independent neutron star parameters accurately. Here we review the methods based on (1) thermonuclear X-ray bursts; (2) accretion-powered millisecond-period pulsations; (3) kilohertz quasi-periodic oscillations; (4) broad relativistic iron lines; (5) quiescent emissions; and (6) binary orbital motions.

## Key claim

Accurately measuring at least three independent parameters (mass, radius, and spin frequency) of the same neutron star is probably the only way to understand supranuclear core matter, but such measurements are extremely difficult due to systematic uncertainties arising from lack of knowledge of several system parameter values; joint application of multiple methods for low-mass X-ray binaries has great potential to reduce these systematics.

## Method

The paper reviews six classes of observational methods for constraining neutron star parameters in low-mass X-ray binaries: (1) thermonuclear X-ray bursts (including continuum spectrum, spectral line, photospheric radius expansion burst, burst oscillation, and millihertz quasi-periodic oscillation methods); (2) accretion-powered millisecond-period pulsations; (3) kilohertz quasi-periodic oscillations; (4) broad relativistic iron lines; (5) quiescent emissions; and (6) binary orbital motions. For each method, the review describes the underlying physical phenomenon, how it constrains stellar parameters, the relevant systematic uncertainties, and plausible ways to reduce those uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Neutron star core densities are 5–10 times higher than nuclear density, at relatively low temperature (~10^8 K)
- Typical neutron star spin frequencies in LMXBs are ~300–600 Hz
- Thermonuclear bursts show X-ray intensity increase by a factor of ~10 in ~0.5–5 seconds, decaying over ~10–100 seconds, with typical energy ~10^39 ergs
- Photospheric radius expansion bursts occur when local X-ray luminosity exceeds the Eddington limit
- Accretion-powered millisecond pulsar sample size increased from 1 to 12 as of the review
- Burst oscillation method established by Chakrabarty et al. (2003) that burst oscillations give neutron star spin frequency
- Broad relativistic iron line from a neutron star LMXB was first established by Bhattacharyya and Strohmayer (2007b)
- First companion star detection in an LMXB using Bowen blend emission lines was by Steeghs and Casares (2002)

## Context

This review compiles all major neutron-star-parameter-measurement methods for low-mass X-ray binaries for the first time, including the recently established method based on broad relativistic iron lines (Bhattacharyya and Strohmayer 2007b). It extends earlier work on neutron star equation of state constraints (Lattimer and Prakash 2007; Ozel and Psaltis 2009) and burst oscillation methods (Chakrabarty et al. 2003). The review enables future joint application of multiple methods to measure three independent parameters of the same neutron star, which is required to distinguish between different equation of state models for supranuclear matter.
