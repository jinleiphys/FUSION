# Angular dependence of recoil proton polarization in high-energy γd \to p n

> We measured the angular dependence of the three recoil proton polarization components in two-body photodisintegration of the deuteron at a photon energy of 2 GeV. These new data provide a benchmark for calculations based on quantum chromodynamics. Two of the five existing models have made predictions of polarization observables. Both explain the longitudinal polarization transfer satisfactorily.. Transverse polarizations are not well described, but suggest isovector dominance.

## Key claim

The measured angular dependence of recoil proton polarization in deuteron photodisintegration at a photon energy of 2 GeV provides a benchmark for QCD-based calculations, but while both the quark-gluon string (QGS) and hard rescattering (HR) models explain the longitudinal polarization transfer satisfactorily, neither adequately describes the transverse polarizations, which instead suggest isovector dominance.

## Method

The experiment (E00-007) was performed in Hall A at Jefferson Lab using a 2.057-GeV longitudinally polarized electron beam incident on a copper radiator to produce circularly polarized bremsstrahlung photons, which then struck a liquid deuterium target. Recoil protons were detected in the left high resolution spectrometer (HRSL) at five kinematic settings covering center-of-mass angles from 36.9 degrees to 109.5 degrees, with polarization measured using a dual-analyzer focal plane polarimeter (FPP) consisting of polyethylene (CH2) and carbon analyzers. The induced polarization and polarization transfer components were extracted via a maximum likelihood method using the sum and difference of azimuthal distributions for the two beam helicity states, with calibration performed using elastic electron-proton scattering.

## Key numbers

- Average beam polarization was 76% with a statistical uncertainty of 0.3% and systematic uncertainty of 3.0%
- Photon polarization to beam polarization ratio was approximately 99.5%
- At center-of-mass angle 36.9 degrees: induced polarization p_y = -0.301 +/- 0.053 (stat) +/- 0.029 (syst), polarization transfer C'_x = -0.170 +/- 0.041 (stat) +/- 0.020 (syst), C'_z = 0.654 +/- 0.056 (stat) +/- 0.051 (syst)
- At center-of-mass angle 89.8 degrees: p_y = -0.090 +/- 0.049 (stat) +/- 0.045 (syst), C'_x = 0.065 +/- 0.074 (stat) +/- 0.034 (syst), C'_z = 0.453 +/- 0.162 (stat) +/- 0.065 (syst)
- At center-of-mass angle 109.5 degrees: p_y = 0.226 +/- 0.073 (stat) +/- 0.053 (syst), C'_x = 0.316 +/- 0.082 (stat) +/- 0.035 (syst), C'_z = 0.001 +/- 0.119 (stat) +/- 0.037 (syst)
- The HRSL spectrometer transverse angle and transverse position resolutions (FWHM) were 2.6 mrad and 4 mm, respectively

## Context

This work extends earlier polarization measurements by Wijesooriya (2001) at 1.86 GeV and provides the only polarization angular distribution in deuteron photodisintegration measured significantly above 1 GeV. The data test two existing models: the QGS model (based on Regge theory for 3-quark exchange) and the HR model (based on modeled pn helicity amplitudes), with the transverse polarization data suggesting isovector channel dominance over the isoscalar channel, consistent with the Sigma asymmetry situation. The next major test identified is high-energy photodisintegration of pp pairs, where the QGS and HR models give very different predictions.
