# On Excited States of Deuteron Nucleus

> For a long time it was known that deuteron, as a weakly coupled nucleon pair, has no excited states. However, A.M. Baldin et al, commenting results of the first physical experiment with accelerated nuclei at JINR synchrophasotron, assumed as far back as in 1979 that one of peaks in a differential cross-section may arise due to an "excited state of deuterium". We have established that one of the peaks in the cross-section may be explained indeed in this way and corresponds to the dibaryon reported by WASA-at-COSY Collaboration. Another peak in the same region is interpreted most likely by interference of several $N^*$-resonances, and this possibility was also mentioned in the paper by A.M. Baldin et al. Further experimental studies based on modern experimental facilities and more abundant statistics are necessary to verify these observations.

## Key claim

The authors establish that one peak in the differential cross-section of deuteron-deuteron scattering at 8.9 GeV/c can be explained by an excited state of the deuteron corresponding to the dibaryon reported by the WASA-at-COSY Collaboration, while another peak in the same region is most likely due to interference of several N*-resonances; further experimental studies with modern facilities and more abundant statistics are necessary to verify these observations.

## Method

The authors performed a partial-wave analysis (PWA) of the reaction D+D → M_X + D at t = -0.495 GeV², using data from the JINR synchrophasotron experiment by Baldin et al. (1979). They modeled the contribution of N* resonances N(1440), N(1520), and N(1535) via an SU(6)⊗O(3) quark spectroscopy framework, constructing T-matrices for N+D → N*+D transitions with spin-dependent amplitudes and including interference terms between resonances. Numerical optimization was carried out using MAPLE's NLPSolve procedure, minimizing the total deviation squared for 22 experimental points, with random initial parameter sets to find the best local minima.

## Key numbers

- The reaction D+D → M_X + D was measured at a primary momentum of 8.9 GeV/c and t = -0.495 GeV².
- A constituent quark mass of M_q = 0.351 GeV was obtained for cos θ = 0.396, compared to a modern quark model value of M_q = 0.318 GeV.
- The dibaryon reported by WASA-at-COSY has mass M_{2B} ≈ 2.38 GeV and width Γ_{2B} ≈ 70 MeV.
- The N* resonances included have widths: N(1440) Γ = 300 MeV, N(1520) Γ = 115 MeV, N(1535) Γ = 150 MeV.
- Decay probabilities used: for N(1440) and N(1520) w_{1π} ≈ 0.65, w_{2π} ≈ 0.35, w_η ≈ 0; for N(1535) w_{1π} ≈ 0.5, w_{2π} ≈ 0.1, w_η ≈ 0.4.
- The optimization found |A| = 0 and |C| = 0 in the T-matrix amplitude, indicating strong spin dependence of the N+D → N*+D transition.

## Context

This work extends the original suggestion by Baldin et al. (1979) that a peak in deuteron-deuteron scattering might arise from an excited state of deuterium, and connects it to the dibaryon observed by the WASA-at-COSY Collaboration (2011). The analysis contradicts the long-held view that the deuteron has no excited states, and proposes that the method of cumulative particles can be used to recognize quasi-resonance peaks in reaction cross-sections. The results enable future experimental searches for dibaryons and multibaryons, particularly using modern facilities with higher statistics, and suggest that lattice QCD calculations with physical quark masses are needed to address the existence of such states.
