# Phase diagram of dilute cosmic matter

> Enhancement of nuclear pasta formation due to multi-nucleus simultaneous collision is presented based on time-dependent density functional calculations with periodic boundary condition. This calculation corresponds to the situation with density lower than the known low-density existence limit of the nuclear pasta phase. In order to evaluate the contribution from three-nucleus simultaneous collisions inside the cosmic matter, the possibility of multi-nucleus simultaneous collisions is examined by a systematic Monte-Carlo calculation, and the mean free path of a nucleus is obtained. Consequently the low-density existence limit of the nuclear pasta phase is formed to be lower than believed up to now.

# Phase diagram of dilute cosmic matter

## Key claim
Multi-nucleus simultaneous collisions (specifically three-nucleus collisions) can enhance nuclear pasta formation at densities below the previously accepted low-density existence limit of the nuclear pasta phase, suggesting that this limit should be revised downward. The claim is conditional on the density exceeding approximately 0.01 ρ₀, where multi-nucleus collisions become dominant over two-nucleus collisions.

## Method
Three-dimensional time-dependent density functional calculations with periodic boundary conditions were performed using the Skyrme force parameter sets SLy6 and SKI3, simulating a 3-nucleus simultaneous collision of three ⁷⁸Zn nuclei in a 64 × 32 × 64 fm³ spatial box with 1.0 fm grid spacing. A systematic Monte-Carlo calculation was carried out to evaluate the statistical likelihood of multi-nucleus simultaneous collisions in cosmic matter, computing mean free paths and transition densities for nuclei with mass numbers A = 50, 100, and 200 at temperatures of 1, 5, and 10 MeV across densities from 0.02 ρ₀ to 0.20 ρ₀.

## Key numbers
- Total incident energy of the 3-nucleus collision: 100 MeV in the center-of-mass frame
- Density of the 3-nucleus pasta-forming calculation: 0.039 ρ₀
- Temperature of the 3-nucleus pasta-forming calculation: 0.5 MeV
- Box dimensions for the density functional calculation: 64 fm × 32 fm × 64 fm
- Mean free path for A = 100 at ρ = 0.02 ρ₀ and T = 1 MeV: 70.1 fm
- Mean free path for A = 100 at ρ = 0.10 ρ₀ and T = 1 MeV: 20.1 fm
- Mean free path for A = 100 at ρ = 0.20 ρ₀ and T = 1 MeV: 10.8 fm
- Mean free path for A = 50 at ρ = 0.02 ρ₀ and T = 1 MeV: 54.4 fm
- Mean free path for A = 200 at ρ = 0.02 ρ₀ and T = 1 MeV: 89.7 fm
- Transition density from 2-nucleus to multi-nucleus collision dominance for A = 100 at T = 1 MeV: 0.02 ρ₀ to 0.04 ρ₀
- Transition density for A = 50 and A = 100 at T = 10 MeV: 0.015 ρ₀ to 0.02 ρ₀
- Most frequent number of simultaneous collisions at ρ = ρ₀ and T = 1 MeV for A = 50: 77
- Most frequent number of simultaneous collisions at ρ = ρ₀ and T = 1 MeV for A = 100: 59
- Most frequent number of simultaneous collisions at ρ = ρ₀ and T = 1 MeV for A = 200: 47
- Laboratory density reference: ρ_Lab = 10⁻¹² ρ₀

## Context
This work extends the understanding of nuclear pasta formation, previously studied by Watanabe and Sowa in the context of supernova explosions, and by Ravenhall, Sato, Sonoda, and Lamb regarding the nuclear phase diagram and neutrino transparency. The calculation demonstrates that pasta can form at lower densities than the limit established by Sonoda, specifically through multi-nucleus collision dynamics not considered in earlier work. The results have implications for the outermost part of protoneutron stars and for revising the low-density, low-temperature portion of the nuclear phase diagram, potentially affecting models of supernova mechanisms as reviewed by Bethe. The fast charge equilibration process identified by Iwata determines the upper temperature boundary for pasta formation.
