# Initial state and pre-equilibrium effects in small systems

> We discuss the importance of initial state and early time effects with regard to the theoretical understanding of long range azimuthal correlations observed in high-multiplicity $p+p$ and $p+A$ collisions.

## Key claim
Initial state and pre-equilibrium effects can produce long-range azimuthal correlations in high-multiplicity p+p and p+A collisions that quantitatively describe experimental data, but it remains under debate whether these correlations originate predominantly from initial state or final state effects.

## Method
The paper reviews theoretical calculations of two-particle correlations based on Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory, using a k_T factorized approximation for momenta above the saturation scale Q_s. It compares Glasma graph and Jet graph contributions to experimental data from CMS and ATLAS at LHC energies, and discusses event-by-event simulations in classical Yang-Mills theory that extend perturbative calculations toward lower p_T.

## Key numbers
- Near-side ridge yield in p+p collisions shows good agreement between initial state calculations and experimental data at both 7 TeV and 13 TeV
- Classical Yang-Mills evolution up to tau = 0.4 fm/c leads to build up of a sizable v_3 on the parton level
- Sign change of four-particle cumulant c_2{4} observed around N_ch(|eta_lab| < 1) ~ 60 by ALICE
- Transverse momentum range 1-3 GeV is used for schematic expectation of azimuthal correlation strength versus event multiplicity
- Perturbative calculations apply for momenta p_T > 1 GeV in p+Pb collisions

## Context
This work extends the Color Glass Condensate framework developed by Gelis (2008), Dumitru (2010), and Dusling (2012-2015) to explain long-range correlations in small systems, which were first observed by CMS (2010) and ATLAS (2012) at the LHC. It contrasts with hydrodynamic explanations proposed by Bozek (2013), Bzdak (2014), and Werner (2013) that attribute correlations to final state response to initial geometry. The paper enables future systematic studies of initial state effects across different collision geometries in p+A, d+A, and 3He+A collisions at RHIC, as measured by Adare (2013, 2015) and Adamczyk (2015).
