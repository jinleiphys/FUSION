# Analysis of angular distributions in gamma N \to pi0 eta N

> Angular distributions in the final state of pi0-eta photoproduction on nucleons are considered. As a formal base the familiar isobar model is used in which the (pi0 eta N) state is a product of the resonance decay into eta-Delta(1232) and pi-S_{11}(1535) channels. One of the principal assumptions used is that in the actual energy region the reaction is dominated by a single resonance state. The developed formalism can serve as a tool for testing spin and parity of that resonance.

## Key claim

The paper argues that if the reaction $\gamma N \to \pi^0 \eta N$ is dominated by a single resonance state in the energy region of interest, then the angular distributions of the final-state particles can serve as a model-dependent tool to determine that resonance's spin and parity, even though the exact electromagnetic amplitudes (the ratio $a = (A^R_{3/2}/A^R_{1/2})^2$) are poorly known.

## Method

The authors use an isobar model where the $\pi^0 \eta N$ final state arises from the sequential decay of a single resonance $R(J^\pi)$ via two channels: $R \to \eta \Delta(1232) \to \pi^0 \eta N$ and $R \to \pi^0 S_{11}(1535) \to \pi^0 \eta N$. They derive analytic expressions for the angular distributions of the $\pi N$ system (angle $\Theta$) and the pion in the $\pi N$ rest frame (angles $\theta, \phi$), calculating the correlation coefficients $\rho_{mm'}$ for six candidate resonance states with isospin $T=3/2$ and varying spin-parity. The formalism is then tested numerically using a Breit-Wigner resonance with mass $M_R = 1.8$ GeV and total width $\Gamma_R = 300$ MeV, treating the background Born terms as negligible.

## Key numbers

- The measured total cross section for $\gamma p \to \pi^0 \eta p$ reaches about **4 $\mu$b** at $E_\gamma = 1.3$ GeV and does not essentially change up to $E_\gamma = 2.2$ GeV.
- The Born diagrams alone give only about **10%** of the measured cross section value, indicating the crucial role of resonances.
- The resonance mass is set to $M_R = 1.8$ GeV with a total width $\Gamma_R = 300$ MeV for the model calculations.
- For the $S_{11}(1535)$ isobar, the partial widths used are **45%** for both $\eta N$ and $\pi N$ modes and **10%** for $\pi\pi N$, with a total width $\Gamma_{N^*} = 150$ MeV.
- For the $D_{33}(1700)$ resonance, using the PDG ratio $A_{3/2}/A_{1/2} = 0.81$, the $\theta$ distribution at $\Theta=0$ is approximately $f(\theta) \approx 3 + \cos^2\theta$.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of $\pi^0 \eta$ photoproduction following the total cross section measurements by the Bonn-Gatchina group (2005), who identified the dominant contributions from $D_{33}(1700)$ and $D_{33}(1940)$ resonances in the region $1.07$ GeV $\leq E_\gamma \leq 1.45$ GeV. The paper also builds on the earlier proposal by Doring (2005) that $D_{33}(1700)$ decaying into $\eta\Delta(1232)$ is the dominant mechanism. The developed formalism enables future experimental analyses to extract resonance quantum numbers from angular distributions, particularly at forward angles $\Theta=0$ where $\eta$ and $\Delta$ overlap is minimal, and can help resolve ambiguities when multiple overlapping resonances contribute.
