# Parity-violating longitudinal response

> The parity-violating quasielastic electron scattering response is explored within the context of a model that builds antisymmetrized random phase approximation and Hartree-Fock correlations on a relativistic Fermi gas basis. Particular emphasis is put on the weak-neutral longitudinal response function, since this observable displays a strong sensitivity to isospin correlations: specifically, it is shown how, through a diagrammatic cancellation/filtration mechanism, this response acts as a magnifier of pionic correlations in the nuclear medium. The parity-violating longitudinal response function also displays appreciable sensitivity to the electric strangeness content of the nucleon, thus making quasielastic electron scattering a possible candidate to measure the nucleon electric strange form factor at relatively high momentum transfers. Finally, we discuss how observables, related to the asymmetry, can be constructed to disentangle the nuclear and nucleonic effects.

## Key claim

The parity-violating longitudinal response function in quasielastic electron scattering acts as a magnifier of pionic correlations in the nuclear medium through a diagrammatic cancellation/filtration mechanism, and also displays appreciable sensitivity to the electric strangeness content of the nucleon, making it a possible candidate to measure the nucleon electric strange form factor at relatively high momentum transfers.

## Method

The author employs a relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model with fully antisymmetrized random phase approximation (RPA) correlations built on a Hartree-Fock (HF) basis, using a version of the Bonn potential that accounts for π, ρ, σ, and ω exchange. The parity-violating longitudinal response function R^L_AV is calculated at various momentum transfers (300, 500, 800, and 1000 MeV/c) and compared across free RFG, HF-RPA, pure ring, and pure exchange approximations. The sensitivity to strangeness is tested using three parametrizations of the electric strange form factor G_E^(s), and integrated observables are constructed to disentangle nuclear and nucleonic effects.

## Key numbers

- The asymmetry scale factor is A_0 = 3.1 × 10^(-4) τ, where τ = |Q^2|/(4m_N^2)
- The Weinberg angle sin^2 θ_W = 0.227, giving β_V^(0) ≈ 1 - 2(0.227) = 0.546 and β_V^(1) = -2(0.227) = -0.454
- Free RFG calculations use k_F = 225 MeV/c; correlated HF-RPA calculations use k_F = 200 MeV/c for ¹²C
- The Galster-like parametrization for G_E^(s) uses λ_D^V = 4.97, with three parameter sets: (ρ_s = 0), (ρ_s = -3, λ_E^(s) = 5.6), and (ρ_s = -3, λ_E^(s) = 0)
- Calculations are shown at momentum transfers q = 300, 500, 800, and 1000 MeV/c
- The correlated response shows sizable effects even at 1 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Alberico et al. (1993) that showed the extraordinary sensitivity of the parity-violating longitudinal response to nuclear isospin correlations, and by Barbaro et al. (1994) who devised a general procedure for constructing observables that disentangle nuclear and nucleonic physics within the RFG scheme. The results enable future parity-violating quasielastic experiments to potentially measure the nucleon electric strange form factor at intermediate energies, though the author notes that further work is needed to account for meson-exchange currents, short-range correlations, and 2p-2h effects.
