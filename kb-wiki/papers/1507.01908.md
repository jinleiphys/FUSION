# On the Implementation of General Background Electromagnetic Fields on a Periodic Hypercubic Lattice

> Nonuniform background electromagnetic fields, once implemented in lattice quantum chromodynamics calculations of hadronic systems, provide a means to constrain a large class of electromagnetic properties of hadrons and nuclei, from their higher electromagnetic moments and charge radii to their electromagnetic form factors. We show how nonuniform fields can be constructed on a periodic hypercubic lattice under certain conditions and determine the precise form of the background U(1) gauge links that must be imposed on the quantum chromodynamics gauge-field configurations to maintain periodicity. Once supplemented by a set of quantization conditions on the background-field parameters, this construction guarantees that no nonuniformity occurs in the hadronic correlation functions across the boundary of the lattice. The special cases of uniform electric and magnetic fields, a nonuniform electric field that varies linearly in one spatial coordinate (relevant to the determination of quadruple moment and charge radii), nonuniform electric and magnetic fields with given temporal and spatial dependences (relevant to the determination of nucleon spin polarizabilities) and plane-wave electromagnetic fields (relevant to the determination of electromagnetic form factors) are discussed explicitly.

# On the Implementation of General Background Electromagnetic Fields on a Periodic Hypercubic Lattice

## Key claim

Nonuniform background electromagnetic fields can be implemented on a periodic hypercubic lattice in lattice QCD calculations, but only under the condition that the flux of the electric and magnetic fields through each plane on the lattice is independent of the coordinates transverse to that plane, allowing the flux to be quantized. The construction guarantees that no nonuniformity occurs in hadronic correlation functions across the boundary of the lattice only when supplemented by a set of quantization conditions on the background-field parameters.

## Method

The authors construct periodic U(1) gauge links by multiplying QCD gauge links with additional U(1) phase factors, then introduce modified links adjacent to the lattice boundary to compensate for phase mismatches that arise from periodic boundary conditions. They derive general quantization conditions by requiring that the net flux through each closed surface on the hypertorus be an integer multiple of 2π, and verify the periodicity numerically by evaluating neutral pion correlation functions in both uniform and linearly varying electric fields on a quenched lattice with volume V = (12a_s)³ × (24a_t) where a_s = a_t ≈ 0.145 fm.

## Key numbers

- Lattice volume used for numerical verification: V = (12a_s)³ × (24a_t) with a_s = a_t ≈ 0.145 fm
- Quantization condition for uniform electric field: E = 2πn / (eQTL) with n ∈ ℤ
- Quantization condition for linearly varying electric field (when R ≠ L/2): E₀ = 2πn / (eQTL(-R + L/2)) with n ∈ ℤ
- For the linearly varying field with offset R = L/2, no quantization condition is required and the additional link at x₃ = L - a_s equals unity
- Numerical correlation functions were evaluated at Euclidean time τ/a_t = 18 with source locations at x₃^(src) = 0 and x₃^(src) = 9a_s

## Context

This work extends the 't Hooft quantization condition (1979, 1981) for uniform electromagnetic fields on a torus to general nonuniform background fields, providing a systematic framework for implementing such fields in lattice QCD. Prior implementations of nonuniform fields by Lee (2011) and Engelhardt (2011) did not enforce periodic boundary conditions, while earlier uniform-field studies by Bernard (1982), Martinelli (1982), and others placed hadronic sources away from boundary effects. The method enables lattice QCD calculations of higher electromagnetic moments, charge radii, spin polarizabilities of nucleons (as suggested by Detmold in 2006), and electromagnetic form factors via plane-wave background fields (as proposed by Detmold in 2004 and implemented by Bali in 2015 for hadronic vacuum polarization).
