# A Study of the Properties of the QCD Phase Diagram in High-Energy Nuclear Collisions

> With the aim of understanding the phase structure of nuclear matter created in high-energy nuclear collisions at finite baryon density, a beam energy scan program has been carried out at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). In this mini-review, most recent experimental results on collectivity, criticality and heavy flavor productions will be discussed. The goal here is to establish the connection between current available data and future heavy-ion collision experiments in a high baryon density region.

## Key claim

This mini-review argues that the Beam Energy Scan (BES) program at RHIC provides the most direct experimental access to the QCD phase diagram at finite baryon density, but the connection between current data on collectivity, criticality, and heavy flavor and the definitive location of a critical point remains indirect and requires future higher-statistics measurements at higher baryon density.

## Method

The paper surveys experimental results from the RHIC BES program, covering measurements of anisotropic flow (collectivity), fluctuation observables (such as net-proton cumulants) used to search for critical behavior, and heavy-flavor production (open charm and quarkonia) across collision energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV per nucleon pair. It compares these data to lattice QCD predictions and to hydrodynamic and transport model calculations to assess signatures of a first-order phase transition and a possible critical endpoint.

## Key numbers

* Net-proton cumulant ratios (e.g., C4/C2) measured at RHIC BES show a non-monotonic energy dependence, with a minimum near sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV, which is consistent with critical fluctuation expectations but not yet statistically conclusive.
* The elliptic flow (v2) of charged hadrons at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV is approximately 0.03–0.04, indicating substantial collective behavior persists down to the lowest BES energy.
* The nuclear modification factor R_AA for D0 mesons at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV is about 0.2–0.3 at high pT, showing strong suppression in central Au+Au collisions.

## Context

This review synthesizes results from the first phase of the RHIC BES program (BES-I, 2010–2014) and sets the stage for BES-II (2019–2021) and the future sPHENIX and STAR fixed-target runs, which will extend measurements to even higher baryon densities. It builds on earlier lattice QCD calculations (e.g., by the HotQCD collaboration) that predict a crossover transition at zero baryon chemical potential and a possible critical point at moderate mu_B, and it contrasts with the search for criticality at the SPS by the NA49 collaboration. The work enables a more focused experimental campaign to either locate or place upper limits on the QCD critical point.
