# Joint Track Functions: Expanding the Space of Calculable Correlations at Colliders

> The theoretical description of observables at collider experiments relies on factorization theorems separating perturbative dynamics from universal non-perturbative matrix elements. Despite significant recent progress in extending these factorization theorems to increasingly differential jet substructure observables, the focus has been primarily on infrared safe observables sensitive only to correlations in the energy of final state hadrons. However, significant information about the dynamics of the underlying collision is encoded in how energy is correlated between hadrons of different quantum numbers. In this paper we extend the class of calculable correlations by deriving factorization theorems for a broad class of correlations, $\langle \mathcal{E}_{R_1}(n_1) \cdots \mathcal{E}_{R_k}(n_k) \rangle$, between the energy flux carried by hadrons specified by quantum numbers, $R_1, \cdots, R_k$. We show that these factorization theorems involve moments of a new class of universal non-perturbative functions, the "joint track functions", which extend the track function formalism to describe the fraction of energy carried by hadrons of multiple quantum numbers arising from the fragmentation of quarks or gluons. We study the general properties of these functions, and then apply this to the specific case of joint track functions for positive and negative electromagnetic charges. We extract these from parton shower Monte Carlo programs and use them to calculate correlations in electromagnetically charged energy flux. We additionally propose and study a C-odd $\mathcal{E}_{\mathcal{Q}}$ detector, which results in a qualitatively distinct scaling behavior compared to the standard energy correlators. Our formalism significantly extends the class of observables that can be computed at hadron colliders, with a wide range of applications from particle to nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The paper extends the track function formalism to derive factorization theorems for correlations between energy flux carried by hadrons with different quantum numbers, $\langle \mathcal{E}_{R_1}(n_1) \cdots \mathcal{E}_{R_k}(n_k) \rangle$, using newly defined "joint track functions", but the practical utility relies on the fact that only specific moments (numbers) of these functions, rather than the full multi-variable functions, appear in the correlation function calculations.

## Method

The authors define joint track functions as non-perturbative matrix elements that simultaneously measure the energy fraction from a fragmenting quark or gluon into hadrons with multiple specified quantum numbers, extending the standard track function definitions of Chang (2013). They derive the renormalization group evolution equations for these joint track functions in both momentum-fraction space and moment space, showing that the evolution is governed by the same perturbative kernels as standard track functions. They then apply the formalism to the specific case of positive and negative electromagnetic charges, extracting the joint track functions from the Pythia parton shower Monte Carlo program and using them to calculate charged energy flux correlators, including a proposed C-odd $\mathcal{E}_{\mathcal{Q}}$ detector.

## Key numbers

* The first three moments ($n+m \leq 3$) of the joint track functions are fixed by DGLAP anomalous dimensions up to terms proportional to $\Delta_i(\mu) = T_i(1;\mu) - T_g(1;\mu)$, where $\Delta \ll 1$ in QCD.
* The covariance $\sigma_i^{+-}(1,1;\mu) = T_i^{+-}(1,1;\mu) - T_i^{+}(1;\mu)T_i^{-}(1;\mu)$ evolves with the anomalous dimension matrix $\hat{\gamma}(3)$, which has positive eigenvalues in unitary theories.
* The asymmetry moment $T_i^{\mathrm{asy}}(N;\mu) = \int dx\,dy\, (x-y)^N T_i^{+-}(x,y;\mu)$ obeys the same renormalization group equations as one-dimensional track functions.

## Context

This work extends the track function formalism introduced by Chang (2013) and developed further by Chen (2022), Jaarsma (2022), and Li (2021), which previously only allowed calculation of correlations on energy flux for a single quantum number (e.g., $\langle \mathcal{E}_+(n_1) \mathcal{E}_+(n_2) \rangle$). The joint track functions enable calculation of mixed correlations such as $\langle \mathcal{E}_+(n_1) \mathcal{E}_-(n_2) \rangle$, analogous to how multi-hadron fragmentation functions extend single-hadron fragmentation functions. This formalism enables probing nontrivial correlations between different quantum numbers in the hadronization process and significantly expands the class of calculable jet substructure observables at hadron colliders.
