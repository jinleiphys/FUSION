# An improved formula for Wigner function and spin polarization in a decoupling relativistic fluid at local thermodynamic equilibrium

> We present an upgraded formula for Wigner function and spin polarization of fermions emitted by a relativistic fluid at local thermodynamic equilibrium at the decoupling which improves the one obtained in refs. [1, 2] and used in numerical simulations of relativistic nuclear collisions. By using a new expansion method, applicable to decoupling hypersurfaces with arbitrary geometry, we reproduce the known term proportional to thermal vorticity and obtain an upgraded form of the spin-shear term which captures the dependence on the geometry. The new method also includes additional contributions whose physical nature is to be assessed. The new expression also naturally excludes contributions from space-time gradients in the normal direction of the hypersurface, providing a theoretical justification for the isothermal condition previously imposed a priori. This framework can be extended to particles with arbitrary spin.

# An improved formula for Wigner function and spin polarization in a decoupling relativistic fluid at local thermodynamic equilibrium

## Key claim
The authors derive an upgraded formula for the Wigner function and spin polarization of fermions emitted from a relativistic fluid at local thermodynamic equilibrium on the decoupling hypersurface, which removes previous geometric approximations by using a new expansion method that exchanges the order of momentum and hypersurface integrations. The new expression naturally excludes contributions from space-time gradients in the normal direction of the hypersurface, providing a theoretical justification for the isothermal condition previously imposed a priori, though additional contributions from non-trivial world-line intersections require further assessment.

## Method
The authors start from the local thermodynamic equilibrium density operator and use linear response theory to compute the leading correction to the Wigner function. By inverting the order of integration over the decoupling hypersurface and the momentum variable q, they perform a small-q expansion of the function G^{μν}(q) in the hydrodynamic limit, which generates a gradient expansion in the β field. The resulting formula involves a sum over all intersections of the particle world-line with the decoupling hypersurface, with the operator D_y = -i Δ^{νρ}(ȳ) ∂_ρ^y ∂_ν^q acting on the integrand, where Δ^{νρ} projects out derivatives perpendicular to the hypersurface.

## Key numbers
- For central Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, the typical proper time τ ~ 10 fm/c, corresponding to a geometric scale L_G between 6.5 fm and 10 fm within the rapidity interval |ξ| < 1.
- The microscopic correlation length l_c ~ 1/T ~ 1.2 fm at temperature T = 160 MeV, giving l_c/L_G << 1 for mid-rapidity particles.
- The convergence conditions l_c/L_H << 1 and l_c/L_G << 1 are satisfied in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Context
This work extends the spin polarization formalism developed by Becattini (2021) and Liu (2021), which relied on significant geometric approximations such as rectangular regions with infinitely distant time-like boundaries or hypersurfaces perpendicular to the four-velocity field. The new method applies to decoupling hypersurfaces with arbitrary geometry, as obtained from numerical hydrodynamic simulations like CLVisc. The upgraded spin polarization formula replaces the factor t̂^μ/p·t̂ from Becattini (2021) or u^μ/p·u from Liu (2021) with n^μ/|p·n|, where n^μ is the unit normal to the hypersurface, resolving an ambiguity in the shear-induced polarization formula discussed by Liu (2022) and Alzhrani (2022). The framework can be extended to particles with arbitrary spin and enables more realistic numerical simulations of spin polarization in relativistic nuclear collisions.
