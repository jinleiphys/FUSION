# Ab initio calculations of 5H resonant states

> By solving the 5-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in configuration space with realistic nuclear Hamiltonians we have studied the resonant states of $^5$H isotope. Two different methods, allowing to bypass the exponentially diverging boundary conditions, have been employed providing consistent results. The existence of $^5$H broad J$^π$=1/2$^+$,3/2$^+$,5/2$^+$ states as S-matrix poles has been confirmed and compared with the, also calculated, resonant states in $^4$H isotope. We have established that the positions of these resonances only mildly depend on the nuclear interaction model.

## Key claim
The authors confirm the existence of broad Jπ=1/2+, 3/2+, 5/2+ resonant states in 5H as S-matrix poles, but note that the positions of these resonances only mildly depend on the nuclear interaction model, with the width of the Jπ=1/2+ state being almost independent of the employed interaction.

## Method
The authors solved the 5-body Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in configuration space using realistic nuclear Hamiltonians (I-N3LO chiral EFT, INOY non-local, and MT13 semi-realistic potentials). To access complex energy eigenvalues of resonant states, they employed two complementary methods: Analytic Continuation in the Coupling Constant (ACCC) and a variant of Smooth Exterior Complex Scaling (SECS), both designed to bypass exponentially diverging boundary conditions.

## Key numbers
- Jπ=1/2+ state of 5H with I-N3LO potential: ER=1.8(1) MeV, Γ=2.4(2) MeV (ACCC method)
- Jπ=1/2+ state of 5H with INOY potential: ER=1.7(1) MeV, Γ=2.4(2) MeV (ACCC method)
- Jπ=1/2+ state of 5H with MT13 potential: ER=1.4(1) MeV, Γ=2.4(2) MeV (ACCC method)
- Jπ=3/2+ and 5/2+ states of 5H with MT13 potential (SECS): ER=2.5(2) MeV, Γ=3.8(4) MeV for both states
- Jπ=2- state of 4H with I-N3LO: ER=1.15(5) MeV, Γ=3.97(7) MeV
- Jπ=1-1 state of 4H with I-N3LO: ER=0.90(3) MeV, Γ=3.80(8) MeV
- Jπ=0- state of 4H with I-N3LO: ER=0.78(15) MeV, Γ=7.7(7) MeV

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical studies of 5H that used either t-n-n three-body models (Shulgina 2000, Grigorenko 2007) or microscopic three-cluster models (Arai 2003, Descouvemont 2001) by providing the first ab initio solution of the 5-nucleon problem with no approximations on the dynamics. The results confirm that 5H is more stable (narrower width) than 4H, supporting the hypothesis that nn pairing over a 3H core could explain the extremely narrow parameters of 7H observed at GANIL in 2007. The calculations enable direct comparison with recent experimental measurements at RIKEN using the SAMURAI multineutron detector.
