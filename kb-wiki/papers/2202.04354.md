# Nuclear Spectroscopy with Heavy Ion Nucleon Knockout and (p,2p) Reactions

> Knockout reactions with heavy ion targets in inverse kinematics, as well as "quasi-free" (p,2p) and (p,pn) reactions are useful tools for nuclear spectroscopy. We report calculations on \textit{ab-initio} many-body wavefunctions based on the no-core shell model to study the nucleon removal reactions in light nuclei, including beryllium, carbon, and oxygen isotopic chains, and explore the importance of using an \textit{ab-initio} method. Our study helps clarifying how the extraction of spectroscopic factors from the experiments depend on the details of the many-body wavefunctions being probed. We show that recent advances with the ab-initio method can provide more insights on the spectroscopy information extracted from experiments.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that using *ab-initio* no-core shell model (NCSM) wavefunctions for nucleon removal reactions can provide more accurate spectroscopic information than standard shell-model calculations, but the agreement with experimental cross sections varies significantly across different nuclei and reactions, with reduction factors R ranging from 0.435 to 1.607, indicating that the extraction of spectroscopic factors depends critically on the details of the many-body wavefunctions being probed.

## Method
The authors performed no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations using the Daejeon16 interaction to compute energies, overlap functions, and spectroscopic factors (C²S) for light nuclei in the beryllium, carbon, and oxygen isotopic chains. These wavefunctions were used as input to reaction models: a Glauber-type eikonal model for heavy-ion knockout reactions and the theory from Aumann (2013) for quasi-free (p,2p) and (p,pn) reactions, with nucleon-nucleon cross sections parametrized following Bertulani and Conti (2010). The calculated cross sections were compared to experimental data to extract reduction factors R = σ_exp/σ_th.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of knockout reactions using ab-initio wavefunctions, following earlier studies by Brown (2002) on reduction factors and by Tostevin (2014) on spectroscopic factor quenching. The authors compare their NCSM results to prior calculations using variational Monte Carlo (VMC) wavefunctions from Pieper and Wiringa (2001) and standard shell-model calculations, finding that NCSM spectroscopic factors often lie between VMC and shell-model values. The study enables more reliable extraction of spectroscopic information from both heavy-ion knockout and quasi-free (p,2p) reactions, particularly addressing the ongoing debate about spectroscopic factor quenching, though the authors note that (p,2p) reactions show R values closer to unity (0.945-1.048) compared to many heavy-ion knockout cases.
