# Toroidal Quadrupole Form Factor of the Deuteron

> We calculate the toroidal quadrupole moment and form factor of the deuteron, which violate time-reversal symmetry but conserve parity, at leading order in two-flavor chiral effective field theory with perturbative pion exchange. We take into account time-reversal and parity violation due to the QCD vacuum angle combined with parity violation resulting from the weak interaction in the Standard Model. We also consider time-reversal and parity violation that at the quark-gluon level results from effective dimension-six operators originating from physics beyond the Standard Model.

## Key claim

The deuteron toroidal quadrupole form factor (TQFF), which violates time-reversal symmetry but conserves parity, is calculated at leading order in chiral effective field theory; the authors find that the Standard Model "background" value is very small, so any experimental evidence for a nonzero TQFF would likely indicate new parity- and time-reversal-violating physics beyond the Standard Model.

## Method

The authors compute the deuteron TQFF using two-flavor chiral effective field theory with perturbative pion exchange, taking into account time-reversal and parity violation from the QCD vacuum angle combined with parity violation from the weak interaction, as well as from effective dimension-six operators representing physics beyond the Standard Model. They evaluate long-range contributions from two-pion-exchange diagrams, short-range two-nucleon interactions, and nucleon anapole and electric dipole form factor contributions, using power-counting rules to determine the leading-order contributions for each source of time-reversal violation.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron binding momentum is approximately 45 MeV, and the nucleon mass is approximately 940 MeV.
- The pion mass is approximately 140 MeV, and the pion decay constant is approximately 186 MeV.
- The experimental bound on the neutron electric dipole moment is |d_n| < 2.9 × 10^(-13) e fm.
- The dimensionless measure of weak interaction strength at low energies is G_F F_π^2 ~ 4 × 10^(-7).
- The experimental bound on the parity-violating asymmetry in n + p → d + γ gives |h_1|/F_π ≲ 10^(-6).
- A lattice QCD calculation at m_π ≈ 389 MeV gives √2 h_1/F_π = [1.099 ± 0.505 (+0.058/-0.064)] × 10^(-7).
- The CKM Jarlskog invariant is J_CP ≈ 3 × 10^(-5).
- The bound on the QCD vacuum angle from the neutron EDM is θ̅ ≲ 10^(-10).

## Context

This work provides the first controlled calculation of a nuclear toroidal quadrupole form factor, extending previous chiral EFT calculations of deuteron electromagnetic form factors (charge, electric quadrupole, magnetic dipole) by the same group and the deuteron anapole form factor by Springer (2012). The calculation of the nucleon anapole form factor follows Maekawa (1999), and the nucleon electric dipole form factor follows Bira and Hockings (2002). The results enable interpretation of future searches for time-reversal violation in lepton-deuteron scattering and in experiments such as the planned TRIC experiment at COSY.
