# Multistrange Particle Production and the Statistical Hadronization Model

> We consider the chemical freeze-out of $Ξ$, $\overlineΞ$ and $ϕ$ multistrange hadrons within a Statistical Hadronization Model inspired approach. We study particle yields across a wide range of reaction energy and centrality from NA49 at SPS and STAR at RHIC experiments. We constrain the physical conditions present in the fireball source of strange hadrons, and anticipate results expected at LHC.

## Key claim

The relative yield of $\Xi$ to $\phi$ hadrons is practically constant across a wide range of collision energies and centralities at SPS and RHIC, which constrains the relationship between hadronization temperature and light quark phase-space occupancy; however, this constancy is consistent with multiple Statistical Hadronization Model variants, and the choice between them depends on the behavior of other ratios such as $\Xi/{\rm K}$ and $\phi/\pi$.

## Method

The authors analyzed published yields of $\Xi$, $\overline\Xi$, and $\phi$ hadrons from the NA49 experiment at SPS and the STAR experiment at RHIC, using data at several beam energies and centrality bins. To form particle ratios in a common centrality interval, they inter- or extrapolated individual yields as a function of the number of participants using a fitted functional form $f(N_{\mathrm{part}}) \equiv a\cdot N_{\mathrm{part}}^b + c$. They then compared the experimental ratios to theoretical predictions from the Statistical Hadronization Model using the SHAREv2 code, exploring the parameter space of temperature $T$, light-quark occupancy $\gamma_q$, and strange-quark occupancy $\gamma_s$.

## Key numbers

* The average value of the $\Xi/\phi$ ratio across all available SPS and RHIC data points is $0.281$, with an error at the 15% level.
* For the STAR 62.4 GeV experiment, the $\Xi/\phi$ ratio ranges from $3.04 \times 10^{-1}$ (0-5% centrality) to $2.49 \times 10^{-1}$ (60-80% centrality).
* The $\Xi/{\rm K}$ ratio varies by a factor of about 2.3 across the entire data range, from $4.19 \times 10^{-2}$ (STAR 62, 0-5%) to $1.85 \times 10^{-2}$ (SPS 30 AGeV, 7%).
* The $\phi/\pi$ ratio varies by a factor of about 3.5 across the data, from $2.54 \times 10^{-2}$ (STAR 200, 0-20%) to $0.72 \times 10^{-2}$ (SPS 30 AGeV, 7%).
* For the LHC, the predicted $\phi/\pi$ ratio is $2.95 \times 10^{-2}$ for the model with $T=140$ MeV and $\gamma_q=\gamma_q^{\mathrm{crit}}$, and $3.90 \times 10^{-2}$ for the model with $T=170$ MeV and $\gamma_q=1$.

## Context

This work extends previous global Statistical Hadronization Model fits (e.g., Kuznetsova (2009)) by focusing exclusively on multistrange hadron yields ($\Xi$, $\overline\Xi$, $\phi$) to test whether they share the same freeze-out conditions as bulk hadrons. The constancy of $\Xi/\phi$ supports the single-freeze-out hypothesis for particles with both large and small hadronic cross sections, as anticipated by Rafelski (2000) for a rapidly expanding QGP fireball. The variation of $\Xi/{\rm K}$ rules out the chemical equilibrium model (model a), while the behavior of $\phi/\pi$ favors chemical non-equilibrium models (model b) with $\gamma_q>1$ and $\gamma_s>1$, a signature predicted by Rafelski (1982) for QGP hadronization. The results enable predictions for LHC measurements, particularly that $\Xi/\phi$ should remain at 0.28 while $\phi/\pi$ should increase, providing a test between chemical non-equilibrium and semi-equilibrium models.
