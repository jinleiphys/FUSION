# Parton/hadron dynamics in heavy-ion collisions at FAIR energies

> Recent STAR data for the directed flow of protons, antiprotons and charged pions obtained within the beam energy scan program are analyzed within the Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics (PHSD/HSD) transport models. Both versions of the kinetic approach are used to clarify the role of partonic degrees of freedom. The PHSD results, simulating a partonic phase and its coexistence with a hadronic one, are roughly consistent with the STAR data. Generally, the semi-qualitative agreement between the measured data and model results supports the idea of a crossover type of quark-hadron transition which softens the nuclear EoS but shows no indication of a first-order phase transition. Furthermore, the directed flow of kaons and antikaons is evaluated in the PHSD/HSD approachesfrom $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx$ 3 - 200 GeV which shows a high sensitivity to hadronic potentials in the FAIR/NICA energy regime $\sqrt{s_{NN}} \le$ 8 GeV.

## Key claim

The PHSD transport model, which simulates a partonic phase and its coexistence with a hadronic phase, is roughly consistent with STAR beam energy scan data for directed flow of protons, antiprotons, and charged pions, and the semi-qualitative agreement supports a crossover type of quark-hadron transition with no indication of a first-order phase transition.

## Method

The authors analyzed recent STAR data for directed flow of protons, antiprotons, and charged pions from Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) to 39 GeV using two transport models: the Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics (PHSD) model, which includes partonic degrees of freedom and a crossover equation of state matched to lattice QCD, and its hadronic-only version HSD (Hadron-String-Dynamics). They compared the calculated directed flow \(v_1(y)\) distributions and their slopes at midrapidity \(F = dv_1/dy|_{y=0}\) to the experimental data, and also evaluated the directed flow of kaons and antikaons from \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 3\) to 200 GeV to study sensitivity to hadronic potentials.

## Key numbers

*   The directed flow slope \(F\) for protons from PHSD/HSD crosses the line \(F=0\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \sim 20\) GeV, which is twice larger than the experimental crossing point.
*   The calculated absolute values of the proton slope at high energies are on the level of -(0.010-0.015), while the measured values are about -0.005.
*   The antiproton slope \(F_{\bar{p}}\) from PHSD shows a wide but shallow negative minimum for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 30\) GeV.
*   The critical energy density for the phase transition is estimated to vary from 0.5 GeV/fm\(^3\) to 1.5 GeV/fm\(^3\).
*   The kaon and antikaon potentials show a large impact on the directed flow for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \leq 7\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the STAR beam energy scan data (STAR, 2014) for directed flow, which had previously been addressed by the Frankfurt group (SAP, 2014) for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} < 20\) GeV without conclusive results, leading to the notion of a "directed flow puzzle." The results contradict earlier expectations from ideal hydrodynamics (Stoecker, 2005) and 3D hydrodynamic expansions (Rischke, 1995; Rischke, 1996) that predicted a minimum or collapse in the directed flow excitation function due to a first-order phase transition. The findings enable further experimental studies at FAIR, NICA, or the BES II program at RHIC to probe hadron properties at high baryon densities and to resolve remaining discrepancies in the hadronic dynamics at lower SPS and FAIR/NICA energies.
