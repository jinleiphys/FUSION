# A systematic study of longitudinal and transverse helicity amplidutes in the hypercentral Constituent Quark Model

> We report in a systematic way the predictions of the non-relativistic hypercentral Constituent Quark model for the electromagnetic excitations of baryon resonances. The longitudinal and transverse helicity amplitudes are calculated with no free parameters for fourteen resonances, both for proton and neutron. The calculations lead to an overall fair description of data, specially in the medium $Q^2$ range, where quark degrees of freedom are expected to dominate.

## Key claim

The non-relativistic hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM) provides a parameter-free prediction of longitudinal and transverse helicity amplitudes for fourteen baryon resonances, achieving an overall fair description of data especially in the medium \(Q^2\) range where quark degrees of freedom dominate, though the model systematically underpredicts strength at low \(Q^2\) and for certain amplitudes like the \(A_{3/2}\) of the D13(1520) and F15(1680) resonances.

## Method

The authors used the hypercentral Constituent Quark Model (hCQM) with a linear-plus-Coulomb-like hypercentral potential \(V(x) = -\tau/x + \alpha x\), where parameters \(\alpha = 1.16\ \text{fm}^{-2}\) and \(\tau = 4.59\) were fixed by fitting the non-strange baryon spectrum. The three-quark wave functions were obtained by solving the hyperradial Schrödinger equation, and the hyperfine interaction was treated perturbatively to produce configuration mixing. Helicity amplitudes \(A_{1/2}\), \(A_{3/2}\), and \(S_{1/2}\) were calculated in the Breit frame using the non-relativistic form of the electromagnetic current, with no additional free parameters.

## Key numbers

* Photocoupling for proton P11(1440): hCQM predicts \(A_{1/2}^p = 88 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{GeV}^{-1/2}\), while PDG reports \(-65 \pm 4 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{GeV}^{-1/2}\).
* Photocoupling for proton D13(1520): hCQM predicts \(A_{1/2}^p = -66 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{GeV}^{-1/2}\) and \(A_{3/2}^p = 67 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{GeV}^{-1/2}\); PDG reports \(-24 \pm 9\) and \(166 \pm 5\) respectively.
* Photocoupling for proton S11(1535): hCQM predicts \(A_{1/2}^p = 109 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{GeV}^{-1/2}\); PDG reports \(90 \pm 30 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{GeV}^{-1/2}\).
* Photocoupling for proton P33(1232): hCQM predicts \(A_{1/2}^p = -97 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{GeV}^{-1/2}\) and \(A_{3/2}^p = -169 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{GeV}^{-1/2}\); PDG reports \(-135 \pm 6\) and \(-250 \pm 8\) respectively.
* Photocoupling for neutron P11(1440): hCQM predicts \(A_{1/2}^n = 58 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{GeV}^{-1/2}\); PDG reports \(40 \pm 10 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{GeV}^{-1/2}\).
* Photocoupling for neutron D13(1520): hCQM predicts \(A_{1/2}^n = -1 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{GeV}^{-1/2}\) and \(A_{3/2}^n = -61 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{GeV}^{-1/2}\); PDG reports \(-59 \pm 9\) and \(-139 \pm 11\) respectively.
* The proton r.m.s. radius from the model parameters is \(0.48\ \text{fm}\).
* The ratio \(R_{EM}\) for the \(N \to \Delta\) transition at the photon point is predicted to be about \(0.005\), compared to the experimental value \(-0.025 \pm 0.005\).

## Context

This work extends the hCQM predictions previously published for photocouplings (Santopinto and Giannini, 1999) and transverse helicity amplitudes for negative parity resonances (Aiello, Giannini, and Santopinto, 1998) to include longitudinal amplitudes and a systematic set of fourteen resonances for both proton and neutron targets. The results are compared with the Isgur-Karl model (Isgur and Karl, 1978), the Capstick-Isgur model (Capstick and Isgur, 1986), and the algebraic BIL model (Bijl, Leviatan, and Iachello, 1993), all of which share a common SU(6) structure and similarly underpredict low-\(Q^2\) strength. The missing strength at low \(Q^2\) is attributed to the lack of explicit quark-antiquark or meson cloud effects, as suggested by dynamical models like the DMT model (Drechsel, Kamalov, and Tiator, 1999), which show that pionic contributions dominate the \(N-\Delta\) \(S_{1/2}\) amplitude. The work enables future comparisons with new Jefferson Lab data and motivates relativistic extensions of the hCQM, such as the point-form approach already applied to elastic nucleon form factors (Santopinto, Giannini, and Vassallo, 2007).
