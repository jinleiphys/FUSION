# Multiplicity Fluctuations and Correlations in Limited Momentum Space Bins in Relativistic Gases

> Multiplicity fluctuations and correlations are calculated within thermalized relativistic ideal quantum gases. These are shown to be sensitive to the choice of statistical ensemble as well as to the choice of acceptance window in momentum space. It is furthermore shown that global conservation laws introduce non-trivial correlations between disconnected regions in momentum space, even in the absence of any dynamics.

## Key claim

Multiplicity fluctuations and correlations in thermalized relativistic ideal quantum gases are sensitive to the choice of statistical ensemble (micro-canonical, canonical, or grand-canonical) and to the choice of acceptance window in momentum space, with global conservation laws introducing non-trivial correlations between disconnected momentum-space regions even in the absence of any dynamics.

## Method

The authors calculate joint multiplicity distributions within the grand-canonical ensemble (GCE) using a multivariate normal approximation in the thermodynamic limit, then obtain canonical ensemble (CE) and micro-canonical ensemble (MCE) distributions through conditional GCE distributions at fixed conserved quantities (electric charge, energy, momentum). They apply this formalism to a relativistic ideal quantum gas of three degenerate massive particles (mass 0.140 GeV) with positive, negative, and zero charge, considering Maxwell-Boltzmann (MB), Fermi-Dirac (FD), and Bose-Einstein (BE) statistics, and compute scaled variances and correlation coefficients within limited bins of transverse momentum and rapidity.

## Key numbers

- Mass of particles in the model gas: m = 0.140 GeV
- Temperature used for all calculations: T = 160 MeV
- At low transverse momentum, the scaled variance shows suppression for FD statistics and enhancement for BE statistics relative to MB, while at high momentum tail ω_BE ≈ ω_MB ≈ ω_FD
- For rapidity bins, the scaled variance ordering is ω_BE > ω_MB > ω_FD
- The 4π integrated correlation coefficient between positively and negatively charged particles is ρ_4π = 1 in the CE and MCE, and 0 in the GCE
- In a small mid-rapidity window |y| < 0.3, MCE deviations from GCE results are relevant only for the highest momentum bins
- For vanishing bin size, the GCE scaled variance estimate is ω^{GCE}_δ ≈ 1/(1 ± e^{-β m_T cosh y + β μ})

## Context

This work extends previous studies of ensemble-specific multiplicity fluctuations (Hauer et al., 2008; Becattini et al., 2006) to correlations between particle multiplicities in limited bins of momentum space (rapidity y, transverse momentum p_T, and azimuthal angle φ). The results provide a baseline for interpreting experimental fluctuation measurements in heavy-ion collisions, such as those from the STAR and PHENIX collaborations, by showing that conservation-law-induced correlations can mimic or obscure signals of critical phenomena or deconfinement. The paper enables future comparisons between ideal gas baselines and data from experiments searching for the QCD critical point.
