# Jet evolution in a dense QCD medium

> To probe the properties of the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions, a very useful class of observables refers to the propagation of energetic jets. A jet is a collimated spray of particles generated via successive parton branchings, starting with a virtual quark or gluon produced by the collision. When such a jet is produced in the dense environment of a nucleus-nucleus collision, its interactions with the surrounding medium lead to a modification of its properties, phenomenon known as jet quenching. In this thesis, we develop a new theory to describe jet quenching. We compute for the first time the effects of the medium on multiple vacuum-like emissions, that is emissions triggered by the virtuality of the initial parton. We present a new physical picture for jet evolution, with notably a factorisation in time between vacuum-like emissions and medium-induced emissions. This picture is Markovian, hence well suited for a Monte Carlo implementation that we develop in the parton shower JetMed. We then investigate the phenomenological consequences of our new picture on jet observables and especially the jet nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$, the Soft Drop $z_g$ distribution and the jet fragmentation function. Our Monte Carlo results prove to be in good agreement with the LHC measurements.

## Key claim

The thesis develops a new theoretical picture for jet quenching in which vacuum-like emissions (triggered by the initial parton virtuality) and medium-induced emissions factorize in time, with the medium reducing the phase space for vacuum-like emissions and allowing the first emission outside the medium to violate angular ordering; this picture is Markovian and implemented in a Monte Carlo parton shower called JetMed.

## Method

Using a leading double logarithmic approximation in perturbative QCD, the author computed for the first time the effects of a dense QCD medium on multiple vacuum-like emissions from a highly virtual parton. The calculation employed the multiple soft scattering (harmonic oscillator) approximation for the medium, with the quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\) characterizing the medium properties. The resulting Markovian parton shower JetMed consistently combines both vacuum-like and medium-induced branchings, and was used to compute the jet nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\), the Soft Drop \(z_g\) distribution, and the jet fragmentation function.

## Key numbers

- Jet nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\): the energy loss by the jet increases with jet transverse momentum due to a rise in the number of partonic sources via vacuum-like emissions.
- Soft Drop \(z_g\) distribution: two main nuclear effects identified are incoherent jet energy loss and hard medium-induced emissions.
- Fragmentation function: pronounced nuclear enhancement at both ends of the spectrum; enhancement of hard-fragmenting jets is correlated with \(R_{AA}\) and controlled by jet energy loss, while enhancement of soft fragments is driven by violation of angular ordering and hard medium-induced emissions.
- The integrated medium-induced spectrum for a finite path length \(L\) yields \(k^+ \frac{dN^{\text{mie}}}{dk^+} = \frac{2\alpha_s C_R}{\pi} \log(|C(x_0^+, x_0^+ + L)|)\) where \(C\) is a solution of the differential equation for the effective propagator.
- For the brick model with constant \(\hat{q}_0\) and path length \(L\), the functions \(S(t,t_0)\) and \(C(t,t_0)\) are given explicitly with \(\Omega^2 \equiv i\hat{q}_0/(2k^+)\).
- The NLL cumulative fragmentation function for quark jets at large \(x\) is \(\Sigma_q^{\text{NLL}}(x, p_{T0}R) = \frac{\exp(L g_1(\alpha_s L, \alpha_s L_0) + f_{2,q}(\alpha_s L, \alpha_s L_0))}{\Gamma(1 - \partial_u g_1(u, \alpha_s L_0)|_{u=\alpha_s L})}\) with \(L = -\log(1-x)\) and \(L_0 = \log(p_{T0}R/k_{\perp\text{min}})\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions, building on prior approaches such as the multiple soft scattering approximation (Iancu 2000, Iancu 2014) and the dipole cross-section formalism (Mehtar-Tani 2006). The new factorization between vacuum-like and medium-induced emissions enables a Markovian Monte Carlo implementation, which was previously lacking. The results enable future comparisons with LHC data on jet observables and provide a framework for studying infrared-and-collinear-safe observables such as subjet fragmentation.
