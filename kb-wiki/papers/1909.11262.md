# Three-body Description of $2n$-Halo and Unbound $2n$-Systems: $^{22}$C and $^{26}$O

> We study the two-neutron correlations in the ground state of the weakly-bound two-neutron halo nucleus $^{22}$C sitting at the edge of the neutron-drip line and also in the unbound nucleus $^{26}$O sitting beyond the neutron dripline. For the present study, we employ a three-body (core$+n+n$) structure model designed for describing the two-neutron halo system by explicit inclusion of unbound continuum states of the subsystem (core$+n$). We use either a density-independent or a density-dependent contact-delta interaction to describe the neutron-neutron interaction and its strength is varied to fix the binding energy. We report the configuration mixing in the ground state of these systems for different choices of pairing interactions.

## Key claim

The three-body model shows that the weakly-bound two-neutron halo nucleus \(^{22}\)C and the unbound two-neutron system \(^{26}\)O both exhibit dominant di-neutron correlations in their ground states, but the configuration mixing in \(^{26}\)O is sensitive to the choice of density-independent versus density-dependent contact-delta pairing interactions, whereas for \(^{22}\)C the mixing changes little with the interaction choice.

## Method

The authors employ a three-body (core\(+n+n\)) structure model that explicitly includes unbound continuum states of the core\(+n\) subsystem, using Woods-Saxon potentials for the core-neutron interaction and either a density-independent or a density-dependent contact-delta interaction for the neutron-neutron pairing. Single-particle continuum states for \(^{21}\)C (seven orbits: \(s_{1/2}\), \(d_{3/2}\), \(f_{7/2}\), \(p_{3/2}\), \(f_{5/2}\), \(p_{1/2}\), \(d_{5/2}\)) and for \(^{25}\)O (three orbits: \(d_{3/2}\), \(p_{3/2}\), \(f_{7/2}\)) are calculated with a bin width of \(\Delta E = 0.1\) MeV, and the two-particle states are constructed by angular-momentum coupling. The pairing strength is varied to reproduce the known ground-state energies of \(^{22}\)C and \(^{26}\)O.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{22}\)C, the ground-state energy is fixed to \(-0.140\) MeV (Gaud12).
* For \(^{26}\)O, the ground-state energy is fixed to \(0.018\) MeV (Kon).
* For \(^{25}\)O, the \(d_{3/2}\) resonance is at \(749(10)\) keV with a width of \(88(6)\) keV.
* The free neutron-neutron scattering length used is \(a_{nn} = -15\) fm, with cutoff energy \(e_c = 30\) MeV, giving \(v_0 = 857.2\) MeV fm\(^3\) for the density-independent part of the DD interaction.
* For the DD interaction, \(v_\rho = 591.55\) MeV fm\(^3\) for \(^{22}\)C and \(v_\rho = 1058.70\) MeV fm\(^3\) for \(^{26}\)O.
* For \(^{22}\)C with DI interaction, the \((s_{1/2})^2\) configuration weight is 0.923; with DD interaction it is 0.899 (reference value 0.915 from Hori06).
* For \(^{26}\)O with DI interaction, the \((d_{3/2})^2\) configuration weight is 0.798; with DD interaction it is 0.643 (reference value 0.661 from Hag).
* For \(^{26}\)O with DI interaction, the \((f_{7/2})^2\) configuration weight is 0.178; with DD interaction it is 0.268 (reference value 0.183 from Hag).

## Context

This work extends three-body studies of two-neutron halo nuclei from the well-established \(p\)-shell case of \(^6\)He into the less-understood \(s\)-\(d\) shell region, specifically addressing \(^{22}\)C and the unbound system \(^{26}\)O. It builds on prior experimental measurements of \(^{22}\)C interaction cross-sections at 235 MeV/nucleon (Tog) and invariant-mass spectroscopy of \(^{26}\)O (Kon) at RIKEN, as well as earlier three-body calculations by Hori06 and Hag. The results enable further investigation into the role of pairing interactions and continuum coupling in determining the structure of neutron-rich systems near and beyond the dripline.
