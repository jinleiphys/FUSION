# Shell closure effects studied via cluster decay in heavy nuclei

> The effects of shell closure in nuclei via the cluster decay is studied. In this context, we have made use of the Preformed Cluster Model ($PCM$) of Gupta and collaborators based on the Quantum Mechanical Fragmentation Theory. The key point in the cluster radioactivity is that it involves the interplay of close shell effects of parent and daughter. Small half life for a parent indicates shell stabilized daughter and long half life indicates the stability of the parent against the decay. In the cluster decay of trans lead nuclei observed so far, the end product is doubly magic lead or its neighbors. With this in our mind we have extended the idea of cluster radioactivity. We investigated decay of different nuclei where Zirconium is always taken as a daughter nucleus, which is very well known deformed nucleus. The branching ratio of cluster decay and $α$-decay is also studied for various nuclei, leading to magic or almost doubly magic daughter nuclei. The calculated cluster decay half-life are in well agreement with the observed data. First time a possibility of cluster decay in $^{218}U$ nucleus is predicted.

# Shell closure effects studied via cluster decay in heavy nuclei

## Key claim

The Preformed Cluster Model (PCM) calculations show that shell closure effects in both spherical and deformed nuclei can be identified through cluster decay half-lives, with minima in half-life indicating magic numbers in the daughter nucleus. The authors predict for the first time the possibility of cluster decay in \(^{218}\)U, with \(^{8}\)Be, \(^{12}\)C, and \(^{22-24}\)Ne as the most probable cluster emission modes.

## Method

The authors used the Preformed Cluster Model (PCM) of Gupta and collaborators, based on the Quantum Mechanical Fragmentation Theory, to calculate cluster preformation probabilities and barrier penetrability via the WKB approximation. They computed half-lives for three sets of decays: (1) intermediate-mass parents (A=88 to A=186) decaying to zirconium daughters (Z=40) to search for shell closures; (2) known cluster decays from trans-lead nuclei for comparison with experimental data; and (3) possible cluster decay modes of \(^{218}\)U and other uranium isotopes. The fragmentation potential included Coulomb, nuclear proximity, and binding energy terms, with shell effects entering through experimental binding energies from Audi et al. (2003) and theoretical masses from Moller et al. (1995).

## Key numbers

- For \(^{218}\)U alpha-decay, the PCM-calculated half-life is \(T_{1/2} = 4.36\) ms, compared to the experimental value of \(T_{1/2} = 0.51\) ms.
- The calculated Q-value for \(^{218}\)U alpha-decay is \(E_{\alpha}^{PCM} = 8.788\) MeV, versus the experimental value \(E_{\alpha}^{exp} = 8.612\) MeV.
- The first experimentally identified cluster decay, \(^{223}\)Ra emitting \(^{14}\)C, has a half-life of \((3.7 \pm 1.1) \times 10^7\) yr and a branching ratio of \(B(^{14}C/\alpha) = 8.5(2.3) \times 10^{-10}\) (Rose and Jones, 1984).
- More than 24 cases of cluster radioactivity are known, with partial half-lives ranging from \(\log_{10} T = >3.63\) (s) to \(>29.20\) (s).
- The \(^{80}\)Zr nucleus has a quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2 \approx 0.4\), making it the most deformed nucleus in nature (Lister et al., 1987).

## Context

This work extends the Preformed Cluster Model approach of Gupta and collaborators (1997, 1988, 1989) to search for shell closure effects in both spherical and deformed nuclei, using zirconium as a fixed deformed daughter. It builds on the experimental identification of cluster radioactivity by Rose and Jones (1984) and the theoretical prediction by Sandulescu, Poenaru, and Greiner (1980). The prediction of cluster decay in \(^{218}\)U, whose alpha-decay was recently studied by Leppanen et al. (2007), opens the possibility of using cluster emission to probe shell structure in neutron-deficient uranium isotopes.
