# Dilepton production in proton-proton and Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV

> We study e^+e^- pair production in proton-proton and central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV within two models: an extended statistical hadronization model (SHM) and the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach. We find that the PHSD calculations roughly agree with the dilepton spectrum from hadronic sources with the 'cocktail' estimates from the statistical hadronization model matched to available data at LHC energies. The dynamical simulations within the PHSD show a moderate increase of the low mass dilepton yield essentially due to the in-medium modification of the rho-meson. Furthermore, pronounced traces of the partonic degrees of freedom are found in the PHSD results in the intermediate mass regime. The dilepton production from the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) exceeds that from the semi-leptonic decays of open charm and bottom mesons. Additionally, we observe that a transverse momentum cut of 1 GeV/c further suppresses the relative contribution of the heavy meson decays to the dilepton yield, such that the sQGP radiation strongly dominates the spectrum for masses from 1 to 3 GeV, allowing a closer look at the electromagnetic emissivity of the partonic plasma in the early phase of Pb+Pb collisions.

## Key claim

The PHSD transport calculations show that the dilepton yield from the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) exceeds that from semi-leptonic decays of open charm and bottom mesons in the intermediate mass regime (1 to 3 GeV) for central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, but only when a transverse momentum cut of 1 GeV/c is applied to further suppress the relative contribution of heavy meson decays.

## Method

The authors employed two complementary models: the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which simulates the full dynamical evolution of the collision including partonic and hadronic phases with off-shell vector meson propagation, and an extended Statistical Hadronization Model (SHM) that provides a static freeze-out cocktail estimate. Dilepton production from partonic sources in PHSD included Born q+bar-q annihilation, gluon Compton scattering, and quark-antiquark annihilation with gluon Bremsstrahlung, using off-shell cross sections from the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM). Hadronic sources included decays of light mesons, Dalitz decays, vector meson decays, and multi-meson interactions (pi+rho, pi+omega, rho+rho, pi+a1). Open charm and beauty production cross sections were taken from p+p collisions at the same energy and scaled with the number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions.

## Key numbers

- Total charm production cross section in p+p at 2.76 TeV: sigma_{c bar-c} = 4.8 +/- 0.8 mb (ALICE estimate)
- Total charm production cross section in p+p at 7 TeV: sigma_{c bar-c} = 6.4 mb (from fitting Gaussian to combined ALICE and LHCb D meson measurements)
- Pion rapidity distribution width from Landau scaling at 2.76 TeV: sigma_y^pi = 7.26
- Number of participants for 0-5% most central Pb+Pb: N_part = 383
- Average elementary volume per nucleon-nucleon collision: V_pp = 2.70 fm^3
- Temperature used in SHM: T = 170 MeV
- Strangeness suppression factor for hard scattering: gamma_s^hard = 0.3
- Scaling factor for hyperon spectra from 0-20% to 0-5% centrality: N_{5->20%} = 1.34

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dilepton production at SPS and RHIC energies (Linnyk 2011, Linnyk 2011) to LHC energies, comparing the PHSD transport approach with the SHM cocktail method previously applied at RHIC (Manninen 2010). The results enable identification of QGP radiation in the intermediate mass region at LHC energies, building on earlier findings that partonic channels dominate over multi-pion sources at SPS and RHIC. The study also tests the default PHSD version against ALICE data for charged particle pseudorapidity distributions, finding acceptable agreement without parameter readjustment.
