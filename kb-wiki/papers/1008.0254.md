# Non-relativistic bound states at finite temperature (II): the muonic hydrogen

> We illustrate how to apply modern effective field theory techniques and dimensional regularization to factorise the various scales which appear in QED bound states at finite temperature. We focus here on the muonic hydrogen atom. Vacuum polarization effects make the physics of this atom at finite temperature very close to that of heavy quarkonium states. We comment on the implications of our results for these states in the quark gluon plasma. In particular, we estimate the effects of a finite charm quark mass in the dissociation temperature of bottomonium.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that modern effective field theory techniques (NRQED, pNRQED, HTL) can be systematically applied to factorize the multiple scales in muonic hydrogen at finite temperature, and that vacuum polarization effects make this system a close analog to heavy quarkonium in the quark-gluon plasma, allowing estimates of finite charm quark mass effects on bottomonium dissociation temperatures.

## Method

The authors use a sequence of effective field theories to separate the relevant scales: NRQED for energies below the muon mass, pNRQED for energies below the soft scale (inverse Bohr radius), and Hard Thermal Loop effective theory for energies below the temperature. They analyze three temperature regimes relative to the soft scale p (T ≪ p, T ∼ p, T ≫ p) and two cases for the electron mass (m_e = 0 and m_e ≠ 0), calculating thermal corrections to the potential, energy shifts, and decay widths using dimensional regularization in the real-time formalism.

## Key numbers

- The Debye mass is m_D = eT/√3, where e is the electromagnetic coupling and T the temperature
- For the case T ≫ E with E ≫ eT, the leading thermal energy shift includes a term απT²/(3m_μ) from the LO potential
- The decay width for the case E ≫ eT includes a term 4α³T/(3n²) at leading order
- For the case eT ≫ E, the leading energy shift from the m_D scale is αm_D³⟨r²⟩_n/6
- The dissociation temperature for the m_e = 0 case scales as T_d ∼ m_μ α^{2/3}/ln^{1/3}α
- For lower lying states (n=1,2) with p ∼ m_e, the Uehling potential provides the leading O(α) correction to the Coulomb potential

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous effective field theory treatment of the hydrogen atom at finite temperature (Escobedo and Soto, 2008) to muonic hydrogen, where vacuum polarization effects are significant unlike in ordinary hydrogen. The muonic hydrogen case serves as a tractable analog for heavy quarkonium in the quark-gluon plasma, where similar scale hierarchies appear. The results enable quantitative estimates of how finite charm quark masses affect bottomonium dissociation temperatures, and suggest that muonic hydrogen traveling through electron-positron plasmas (which can now be produced experimentally) could provide an ideal test of thermal effects in non-relativistic bound states.
