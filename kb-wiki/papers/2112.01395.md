# Phase structure of 2+1-flavour QCD and the magnetic equation of state

> We determine the chiral phase structure of $2+1$-flavour QCD in dependence of temperature and the light flavour quark mass with Dyson-Schwinger equations. Specifically, we compute the renormalised chiral condensate and its susceptibility. The latter is used to determine the (pseudo)critical temperature for general light current quark masses. In the chiral limit we obtain a critical temperature of about 141\,MeV. This result is in quantitative agreement with recent functional renormalisation group results in QCD, and is compatible with the respective lattice results. We also compute the order parameter potential of the light chiral condensate and map out the regime in the phase diagram which exhibits quasi-massless modes, and discuss the respective chiral dynamics.

## Key claim

The authors determine the chiral phase structure of 2+1-flavour QCD using Dyson-Schwinger equations, obtaining a critical temperature in the chiral limit of about 141 MeV, but note that the approximation neglects the feedback of the order parameter potential and thermal splits in vertices, which may affect results in the critical scaling regime for very small light quark masses.

## Method

The authors solve the quark gap equation at finite temperature for different light current quark masses, using vacuum 2+1 flavour gluon propagator and quark-gluon vertex data from a combined Dyson-Schwinger equation and functional renormalisation group approach. The gluon propagator and quark-gluon vertex at finite temperature are obtained by expanding their Dyson-Schwinger equations about the vacuum two-flavour QCD results, computing only the thermal differences. The renormalised light chiral condensate and its susceptibility are then computed from the quark propagator to determine the (pseudo)critical temperature.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature in the chiral limit: about 141 MeV
- Chiral condensate in the chiral limit: (308.9(8) MeV)^3
- Physical light current quark mass at RG scale mu=40 GeV: m_l = 2.49 MeV
- Physical strange current quark mass at RG scale mu=40 GeV: m_s = 66.7 MeV
- Physical pion mass used for scale setting: m_pi,phys = 138 MeV
- Strange-to-light quark mass ratio at physical point: m_s/m_l = 27
- Pion decay constant in the chiral limit: f_pi,chi = 86.7 MeV
- Predicted physical pion decay constant: f_pi,phys = 92.4 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous functional QCD studies of the chiral phase transition, including the functional renormalisation group study by Braun (2020) and lattice QCD results by Ding (2019) and Kotov (2020). The approach combines Dyson-Schwinger equations and the functional renormalisation group, building on the methodology developed by Gao (2020, 2021) for QCD correlation functions. The results enable mapping of the regime in the QCD phase diagram with quasi-massless modes, which is relevant for determining the validity of chiral expansion schemes and constraining the location of the potential critical end point at finite density.
