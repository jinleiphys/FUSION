# Shape evolution of krypton isotopes calculated with axially deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov approach

> We perform a systematic study of the structure and properties of the krypton isotopic chain including both even-even and odd-$A$ nuclei based on the axially deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov approach. Five effective interactions of three families of covariant density functionals, i.e., PC-L3R, DD-PCX, DD-PC1, DD-MEX, and DD-ME2, are employed to calculate potential energy surfaces of krypton isotopes. $^{74,75}$Kr and $^{90,91,92}$Kr are determined as typical candidates of shape coexistence. The potential surfaces originating from the PC-L3R, DD-PCX, and DD-MEX interactions exhibit an abrupt shape transition from oblate to prolate for $^{73\text{-}74}$Kr, whereas DD-PC1 and DD-ME2 preserve an oblate ground-state shape. Such discrepancies are attributed to the occupations of single-particle levels at the vicinity of the Fermi surface described by these functionals. Moreover, the comparison between spherical and deformed calculations verifies the indispensability of deformation degrees of freedom in this region. The consideration of deformation effects improves the description of two-neutron separation energies, of which its evolution clearly demonstrates the $N=50$ and $82$ shell closures. Interestingly, PC-L3R predicts a more extended two-neutron drip line up to $^{132}$Kr, in agreement with the NL3* and PC-PK1 nonlinear effective interactions, whereas other functionals estimate a rather short isotopic chain up to $^{119}$Kr. This anomalous extension implies a significant softening or even collapse of the traditional $N=82$ shell closure near the neutron-rich drip line, highlighting the need for future studies based on triaxial deformation and beyond-mean-field correlations in this nuclear region.

## Key claim

The axially deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov approach predicts shape coexistence in 74,75Kr and 90,91,92Kr, but the existence and nature of the shape transition from oblate to prolate around 73-74Kr depends strongly on which of the five tested covariant density functionals is used, with PC-L3R, DD-PCX, and DD-MEX showing an abrupt transition while DD-PC1 and DD-ME2 preserve an oblate ground state.

## Method

The authors solved the axially symmetric relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) equations with constraints on the axial mass quadrupole moment to compute potential energy surfaces for krypton isotopes from the proton to neutron drip lines. Five effective interactions from three families of covariant density functionals were employed: PC-L3R (nonlinear point-coupling), DD-PCX and DD-PC1 (density-dependent point-coupling), and DD-MEX and DD-ME2 (density-dependent meson-exchange), each paired with its respective finite-range separable pairing force. Both even-even and odd-A nuclei were included in the systematic study.

## Key numbers

- PC-L3R predicts a two-neutron drip line up to 132Kr (N=96), while other functionals estimate the chain ends at 119Kr (N=83)
- For 74Kr, the energy difference between prolate and oblate minima ranges from 0.111 MeV (DD-ME2) to 0.579 MeV (DD-MEX)
- For 75Kr, the energy difference between minima ranges from 0.154 MeV (DD-PCX) to 1.179 MeV (DD-MEX)
- The Fermi energy for PC-L3R in 74Kr shifts upward by 0.077 MeV from the prolate global minimum (beta2=0.48, lambda=-12.382 MeV) to the oblate secondary minimum (beta2=-0.15, lambda=-12.305 MeV)
- For DD-PC1 in 74Kr, the Fermi energy at the oblate global minimum (beta2=-0.15, lambda=-12.490 MeV) is 0.188 MeV lower than at the prolate secondary minimum (beta2=0.48, lambda=-12.302 MeV)
- The separable pairing force parameters are G=728 MeV fm^3 and a=0.644 fm

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shape evolution in the krypton isotopic chain by Abusara (2017) and Zhang (2023), who used similar theoretical frameworks but focused primarily on even-even nuclei. The systematic inclusion of odd-A nuclei and the comparison of five different covariant density functionals reveals that the predicted neutron drip line and shape coexistence patterns are highly functional-dependent, with PC-L3R agreeing with the NL3* and PC-PK1 nonlinear interactions in predicting a more extended drip line. The results highlight the need for future studies incorporating triaxial deformation and beyond-mean-field correlations, as well as precise experimental measurements on 73Kr, 74Kr, 90Kr, 91Kr, and 92Kr to constrain the density functionals.
