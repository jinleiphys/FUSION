# Active-Sterile Neutrino Conversion: Consequences for the r-Process and Supernova Neutrino Detection

> We examine active-sterile neutrino conversion in the late time post-core-bounce supernova environment. By including the effect of feedback on the Mikheyev-Smirnov-Wolfenstein (MSW) conversion potential, we obtain a large range of neutrino mixing parameters which produce a favorable environment for the r-process. We look at the signature of this effect in the current generation of neutrino detectors now coming on line. We also investigate the impact of the neutrino-neutrino forward scattering-induced potential on the MSW conversion.

## Key claim

Active-sterile neutrino conversion in the late-time post-core-bounce supernova environment can produce a neutron-rich environment favorable for the r-process, but only when the feedback of the neutrino flavor transformation on the MSW potential is self-consistently included; without this feedback, the electron fraction is driven to a fixed point at Y_e = 1/3, which does not yield sufficient neutrons.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the two-flavor MSW evolution equations for electron neutrinos and antineutrinos converting to sterile species in the neutrino-driven wind above a protoneutron star, using analytic density and distance profiles with an outflow timescale of τ = 0.3 s and entropy per baryon of s = 100. At each time step, they compute the electron fraction by tracking the evolution of neutron and proton number densities from weak capture rates (ν_e + n ↔ p + e⁻ and ν̄_e + p ↔ n + e⁺) and nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) for alpha particles and heavy nuclei, then feed the updated Y_e back into the MSW potential. They also calculate the expected supernova neutrino event rates at SNO and SuperKamiokande under various active-sterile and active-active mixing scenarios.

## Key numbers

- For mixing parameters δm² = 20 eV² and sin² 2θ_v = 0.01, the electron fraction drops to approximately 0.1 by the time alpha particles form, compared to a higher value without transformation.
- An electron fraction below 0.18 is required to produce the requisite neutron-to-seed ratio for the r-process, for a timescale of 0.3 s and entropy per baryon of ~100.
- A galactic supernova at 10 kiloparsecs is estimated to produce about 8000 events in SuperK from ν̄_e + p → n + e⁺, plus about 300 events from neutrino-electron scattering.
- SNO is estimated to see about 500 events from neutral-current deuteron breakup and about 150 events each from the charged-current reactions ν_e + d → p + p + e⁻ and ν̄_e + d → n + n + e⁻.
- In the region producing the lowest electron fraction, the SNO charged-current event rate is suppressed by 90% relative to the no-transformation case.
- For the small mixing angle (SMA) solar solution (δm²_solar = 5 × 10⁻⁶ eV², sin² 2θ_solar ≈ 10⁻²), near-total conversion of 30 MeV neutrinos requires a density scale height R_s > 8 × 10¹⁰ cm, while no conversion requires R_s < 1 × 10⁸ cm.
- Representative initial neutrino temperatures are T_ν_e = 3.22 MeV and T_ν̄_e = 4.5 MeV, with luminosities L_ν_e = 1.08 × 10⁵¹ erg s⁻¹ and L_ν̄_e = 1.3 × 10⁵¹ erg s⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of active-sterile neutrino conversion in supernovae (Nunokawa et al., 1997; Peres & Smirnov, 2001) by including the full feedback loop between neutrino flavor transformation and the electron fraction, as well as alpha particle formation and nuclear statistical equilibrium, which were neglected in those prior analyses. The results enable predictions for the r-process nucleosynthesis yield and for the time-dependent neutrino signal at detectors like SNO and SuperKamiokande, potentially distinguishing active-sterile conversion from active-active mixing scenarios such as the large mixing angle (LMA) solar solution.
