# Point-Form Approach to Baryon Structure

> A critical discussion is given of the results for baryon electromagnetic and axial form factors obtained from relativistic constituent quark models in the framework of Poincaré-invariant quantum mechanics. The primary emphasis lies on the point-form approach. First we summarize the predictions of the Goldstone-boson-exchange constituent quark model for the electroweak nucleon structure when using a spectator-model current in point form. Then the influences of different dynamics inherent in various kinds of constituent quark models (Goldstone-boson-exchange, one-gluon-exchange, instanton-induced interactions) are discussed. Finally the point-form results are compared to analogous predictions calculated in instant form. Relativistic effects are always of sizeable magnitude. A nonrelativistic approach is ruled out. The instant-form results are afflicted with severe shortcomings. In the spectator-model approximation for the current, only the point-form results appear to be reasonable a-priori. In fact, the corresponding quark model predictions provide a surprisingly good description of all elastic electroweak observables in close agreement with existing experimental data, specifically for the Goldstone-boson-exchange constituent quark model.

## Key claim

The point-form spectator-approximation (PFSA) results from the Goldstone-boson-exchange constituent quark model provide a surprisingly good description of all elastic electroweak observables in close agreement with existing experimental data, but the approach relies on a model current and is not a complete field-theoretic treatment; the instant-form spectator-approximation results, by contrast, are frame-dependent and severely inadequate.

## Method

The authors calculated nucleon electromagnetic and axial form factors using relativistic constituent quark models within Poincaré-invariant quantum mechanics, specifically employing the point-form approach with a spectator-model current. They compared predictions from three different dynamics, Goldstone-boson-exchange (GBE), one-gluon-exchange (OGE), and instanton-induced (II) interactions, and contrasted point-form results with those obtained in instant-form spectator approximation (IFSA) and nonrelativistic impulse approximation (NRIA). The calculations used nucleon wave functions directly from the quark models without introducing quark form factors.

## Key numbers

- The GBE CQM predictions agree with experimental data up to momentum transfers of Q² ~ 4 GeV².
- The violation of current conservation in the PFSA remains below 1% over the range of momentum transfers considered.
- The induced pseudoscalar form factor misses contributions of more than an order of magnitude when the pion pole is neglected.
- The neutron electric form factor is practically zero when only the confinement interaction is present, due to the absence of a small mixed-symmetry spatial component in the wave function.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic constituent quark model program of the Graz-Pavia collaboration (Wagenbrunn 2000, Glozman 2001, Boffi 2001) and contrasts with earlier front-form calculations (Rome group) that required quark form factors to match data. The point-form results are compared to instant-form calculations, which are shown to be frame-dependent and fall closer to nonrelativistic results than to experiment. The paper enables future studies of two- and three-body current contributions and inelastic processes such as transition form factors within Poincaré-invariant quantum mechanics.
