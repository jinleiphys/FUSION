# Lattice Theories with Nonlinearly Realized Chiral Symmetry

> We present the lattice formulation of effective Lagrangians in which chiral symmetry is realized nonlinearly on the fermion fields. In this framework both the Wilson term removing unphysical doubler fermions and the fermion mass term do not break chiral symmetry. Our lattice formulation allows us to address non-perturbative questions in effective theories of baryons interacting with pions and in models involving constitutent quarks interacting with pions and gluons. With the presented methods, a system containing a non-zero density of static baryons interacting with pions can be studied on the lattice without encountering a complex action problem. This might lead to new insights into the phase diagram of strongly interacting matter at non-zero chemical potential.

# Lattice Theories with Nonlinearly Realized Chiral Symmetry

## Key claim

The paper presents a lattice formulation of effective Lagrangians where chiral symmetry is realized nonlinearly on fermion fields, allowing both the Wilson term (which removes doubler fermions) and the fermion mass term to preserve exact chiral symmetry on the lattice. This approach avoids the Nielsen-Ninomiya theorem and enables non-perturbative studies of systems with static baryons at non-zero chemical potential without a complex action problem, but only in the limit where the baryons are static and the chemical potential is taken to infinity such that the difference M−μ remains finite.

## Method

The authors construct a lattice action for a low-energy effective theory of pions and nucleons by discretizing the continuum nonlinear realization of chiral symmetry. They define a flavor gauge parallel transporter V_{x,μ} from the square root of the Goldstone boson field U_x, which transforms as a lattice gauge field under local flavor transformations V_x. The lattice action includes a Wilson term with weight r to remove fermion doublers, a nucleon mass term M, and an axial coupling term with strength g_A, all while maintaining exact chiral symmetry on the lattice.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant F_π appears in the leading-order Goldstone boson action: S[U] = ∫ d⁴x (F_π²/4) Tr[∂_μ U† ∂_μ U]
- The Wilson term is weighted by the parameter r in the lattice action
- The nucleon mass M is generated by spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and appears in the mass term M Σ_x \overline Ψ_x Ψ_x
- The axial coupling g_A appears in the interaction term i g_A/2 Σ_{x,μ} (\overline Ψ_x γ_μ γ_5 A^L_{x,μ} Ψ_x + \overline Ψ_{x+μ̂} γ_μ γ_5 A^R_{x,μ} Ψ_{x+μ̂})
- For the static baryon system at non-zero chemical potential, the grand canonical partition function takes the form Z(μ) = ∫ D U exp{-S[U] + e^{-β(M-μ)} Φ[U]}, where β = 1/T is the inverse temperature and Φ[U] is the Polyakov loop

## Context

This work extends the continuum nonlinear realization of chiral symmetry for baryons and pions originally developed by Weinberg (1966) and the chiral quark model of Manohar and Georgi (1983) to a lattice formulation. It provides an alternative to the Ginsparg-Wilson approach to chiral symmetry on the lattice, and enables non-perturbative lattice studies of effective theories for nuclear matter and constituent quarks interacting with pions and gluons. The method opens the possibility to investigate the QCD phase diagram at non-zero chemical potential without a complex action problem, and to test whether the chiral quark model correctly describes low-energy QCD, particularly regarding the relative scales of chiral symmetry breaking and confinement.
