# Relaxation of baryon diffusion flow in QGP medium and its impact on thermal photon spectra

> In the current work the time evolution of baryon diffusion flow within the scope of second order relativistic hydrodynamic theory has been studied for a 2-component quark-gluon system. The relaxation time for diffusion flow is estimated using the Grad's moment method of many particle kinetic theory. The temperature dependence of relaxation time for diffusion flow has been obtained for different quark chemical potentials. Finally, the diffusion equation has been solved for a $1+1$ dimensional boost invariant system and the effect of diffusion flow has been studied on the thermal photon spectra in a baryon rich environment. The present analysis on the role of baryon number diffusion in influencing the properties of hot QCD medium could be relevant for the baryon rich matter explorations in upcoming experimental facilities.

# Relaxation of baryon diffusion flow in QGP medium and its impact on thermal photon spectra

## Key claim

The baryon diffusion flow in a quark-gluon plasma with finite quark chemical potential decays exponentially in proper time, and its inclusion in the quark momentum distribution produces a small but quantitatively finite effect on thermal photon transverse momentum spectra, showing a 3–6% increment in the pT range 2.5–4 GeV. This effect is significantly small and only becomes visible at higher transverse momenta, contrary to the abstract's implication that the analysis is broadly relevant for baryon-rich matter explorations.

## Method

The author employs Grad's moment method of relativistic kinetic theory to derive a second-order Israel-Stewart type evolution equation for baryon diffusion flow in a two-component (quark-gluon) system. The first-order diffusion coefficient is obtained via the Chapman-Enskog technique with a variational approximation, and the relaxation time for diffusion flow is computed from the second-order transport coefficient. The diffusion equation is solved for a 1+1 dimensional boost-invariant expanding system, and the resulting out-of-equilibrium quark distribution function is used to compute thermal photon spectra from annihilation (q\bar{q} → gγ) and Compton (qg → qγ) processes.

## Key numbers

- The ratio D_F/τ_D approaches the Stefan-Boltzmann limit of approximately 3.1 for temperatures beyond 1 GeV, while in the temperature region 0.15–1 GeV the ratio shows strong temperature dependence ranging between 2 and 3.
- At small quark chemical potential, the relaxation time τ_D for the pQCD case is approximately 1 fm, while for the holographic scenario it is approximately 0.1 fm.
- The transition temperature is taken as T_c = 0.16 GeV with corresponding baryon chemical potential μ_c = 0.357 GeV, with initial conditions τ_i = 0.3 fm, T_i = 0.3 GeV, and μ_i = 0.67 GeV.
- The increment in thermal photon spectra due to baryon diffusion is approximately 3% at p_T ∼ 2.5 GeV, reaching up to 6% at p_T ∼ 4 GeV.

## Context

This work extends second-order hydrodynamic treatments of baryon diffusion, which have received less attention than shear viscosity and thermal conductivity in QGP studies. Prior theoretical works on net-baryon diffusion include those by Denicol, Monnai, Floerchinger, and Kapusta, while the suppression of baryon diffusion in strongly coupled QGP has been studied by Rougemont using holographic approaches. The results enable future extensions to (2+1)-dimensional and (3+1)-dimensional hot QCD systems and investigations of baryon richness effects on other transport coefficients such as shear and bulk viscosities, thermal conductivity, and electrical conductivity.
