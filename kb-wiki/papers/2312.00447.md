# Jet quenching parameter in QCD kinetic theory

> We study the jet quenching parameter $\hat q$ in a non-equilibrium plasma using the QCD effective kinetic theory. We discuss subleading terms at large jet momentum p, show that our expression for $\hat q$ reproduces thermal results at small and large transverse momentum cutoffs for infinite p and construct an interpolation between these limits to be used in phenomenological applications. Using simple non-equilibrium distributions that model pertinent features of the bottom-up thermalization scenario, we analytically assess how anisotropy, under- or overoccupation affect the jet quenching parameter. Our work provides more details on the $\hat q$ formula used in our preceding work [arXiv:2303.12595] and sets the stage for further numerical studies of jet momentum broadening in the initial stages of heavy-ion collisions from QCD kinetic theory.

## Key claim

The paper derives a leading-order formula for the jet quenching parameter $\hat q$ in QCD effective kinetic theory that is valid for arbitrary jet momentum and direction and for anisotropic particle distributions with azimuthal symmetry around the beam axis, but still neglects plasma instabilities by employing an isotropic approximation to the in-medium propagator.

## Method

The authors start from the elastic scattering rate of a hard jet parton with plasma particles and reduce the phase space integration to five integrals over the transferred momentum magnitude $q$, transferred energy $\omega$, the plasma particle momentum $k$, and two azimuthal angles, using kinematic constraints from energy-momentum conservation. They express the distribution functions in a "lab frame" where the beam axis defines the $z$ direction, while performing the angular integrals in rotated coordinate systems aligned with the jet momentum or the transferred momentum. The matrix elements for $2\leftrightarrow 2$ scattering processes are taken from leading-order pQCD with Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) screening modifications for soft gluon or fermion exchange, and the authors also discuss an isotropic screening approximation commonly used in EKT simulations.

## Key numbers

* The Debye mass in thermal equilibrium is $m_D^2(T) = \frac{g^2 T^2}{3}\left(N_C + \frac{n_f}{2}\right)$ with $N_C=3$ for QCD.
* The energy density in thermal equilibrium is $\epsilon(T) = \frac{\pi^2 T^4}{60}\left(4d_A + 7 n_f d_F\right)$ where $d_A = N_C^2 - 1$ and $d_F = N_C$.
* The quadratic Casimirs are $C_F = d_A/(2N_C)$ and $C_A = N_C$, with $N_C=3$ for the numerical results (which consider a purely gluonic system with $n_f=0$).
* The number of spin times color states for a given particle species is $\nu_a = 2d_a$, where $d_a$ is the dimension of its representation.

## Context

This work provides the explicit derivation of the $\hat q$ formula used in the authors' preceding numerical study of jet momentum broadening during the bottom-up thermalization scenario (Boguslavski et al., 2023, Phys. Lett. B 850 (2024) 138525). It extends previous calculations of $\hat q$ in thermal equilibrium at leading order (Aurenche, 2002; Arnold, 2008) and next-to-leading order (Caron-Huot, 2008) to non-equilibrium plasmas, and goes beyond parametric estimates of $\hat q$ in the bottom-up scenario (Baier, 2000; Kurkela, 2011; Berges, 2013). The formula sets the stage for further numerical studies of jet momentum broadening in the initial stages of heavy-ion collisions from QCD kinetic theory.
