# Short-Time Operator Product Expansion for rf Spectroscopy of a Strongly-interacting Fermi Gas

> Universal relations that hold for any state provide powerful constraints on systems consisting of fermions with two spin states interacting with a large scattering length. In radio-frequency (rf) spectroscopy, the mean shift in the rf frequency and the large frequency tail of the rf transition rate are proportional to the contact, which measures the density of pairs with small separations. We show that these universal relations can be derived and extended by using the short-time operator product expansion of quantum field theory. This is a general method for identifying aspects of many-body physics that are controlled by few-body physics.

## Key claim

The short-time operator product expansion (OPE) of quantum field theory provides a general method for deriving connections between few-body physics and many-body physics, and can be used to rederive and extend the universal relations for radio-frequency (rf) spectroscopy that were previously known only for specific limits of the scattering lengths.

## Method

The authors formulate the problem of ultracold fermionic atoms with two spin states interacting via a large scattering length as a local quantum field theory. They apply the short-time OPE to the product of rf transition operators \(\psi_2^\dagger \psi_3(\bm{R} + \frac12 \bm{r}, t) \psi_3^\dagger \psi_2(\bm{R} - \frac12 \bm{r}, 0)\), expanding it in local operators with Wilson coefficients determined by matching matrix elements between few-atom states. The inclusive rf transition rate \(\Gamma(\omega)\) is then expressed as a contour integral, and the OPE is used to derive sum rules and the large-frequency tail by evaluating the contour at infinity.

## Key numbers

* The first sum rule: \(\int_{-\infty}^\infty d\omega~\Gamma(\omega) = \pi \Omega^2 N_2\), where \(\Omega\) is the Rabi frequency and \(N_2\) is the number of atoms of type 2.
* The second sum rule: \(\int_{-\infty}^\infty d\omega~\omega~\Gamma(\omega) = (\Omega^2/4m) (a_{12}^{-1} - a_{13}^{-1}) C_{12}\), where \(a_{12}\) and \(a_{13}\) are scattering lengths and \(C_{12}\) is the contact.
* For the case \(a_{13} \approx 0\), the high-frequency tail of the rf transition rate is \(\Gamma(\omega) \longrightarrow \frac{\Omega^2}{4 \pi \sqrt{m} \omega^{3/2}} C_{12}\).
* For general large \(a_{13}\), the high-frequency tail is \(\Gamma(\omega) \longrightarrow \frac{\Omega^2 (a_{12}^{-1} - a_{13}^{-1})^2}{4 \pi \sqrt{m} \omega^{3/2} (a_{13}^{-2} + m \omega)} C_{12}\), which decreases asymptotically like \(\omega^{-5/2}\).
* The contact of the weakly-bound dimer associated with large positive \(a_{12}\) is \(C_{12} = 8 \pi / a_{12}\).

## Context

This work extends the universal relations derived by Tan (2008) for fermions with large scattering length, which were previously rederived using the short-distance OPE by Braaten and Platter (2008). The paper provides a general field-theoretic method for deriving sum rules and high-frequency tails for rf spectroscopy, and it enables the derivation of new sum rules that are insensitive to range effects, such as those using a Lorentzian weight function. The approach can be applied to other operators to obtain universal information about single-particle excitations, density fluctuations, and transport coefficients.
