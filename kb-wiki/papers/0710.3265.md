# Threshold Pion Electroproduction at Large Momentum Transfers

> We consider pion electroproduction close to threshold for $Q^2$ in the region 1-10 GeV$^2$ on a nucleon target. The momentum transfer dependence of the S-wave multipoles at threshold, $E_{0+}$ and $L_{0+}$, is calculated in the chiral limit using light-cone sum rules. Predictions for the cross sections in the threshold region are given taking into account P-wave contributions that, as we argue, are model independent to a large extent. The results are compared with the SLAC E136 data on the structure function $F_2(W,Q^2)$ in the threshold region.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the momentum-transfer dependence of the S-wave multipoles \(E_{0+}\) and \(L_{0+}\) for threshold pion electroproduction in the range \(Q^2 = 1-10\) GeV\(^2\) using light-cone sum rules (LCSR), and argue that the predictions interpolate between the standard low-energy theorems (LET) at low \(Q^2\) and the asymptotic perturbative QCD regime at high \(Q^2\), but only in the chiral limit \(m_\pi \to 0\) and with the caveat that the LET break down when \(m_\pi Q^2/m_N^3 = \mathcal{O}(1)\).

## Method

The authors reanalyze the LCSR for the generalized form factors \(G_1^{\pi N}(Q^2)\) and \(G_2^{\pi N}(Q^2)\) derived in Braun (2006), now including semi-disconnected pion-nucleon contributions in the dispersion relation to extend the sum rules to lower \(Q^2\). The LCSR calculation is performed in the chiral limit, and the resulting form factors are related to the S-wave multipoles \(E_{0+}\) and \(L_{0+}\) via standard kinematic relations. P-wave contributions from final-state pion emission are added using a model-independent expression in terms of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors, and the full amplitude is used to compute differential cross sections and structure functions near threshold.

## Key numbers

* The LCSR predictions cover the momentum transfer range \(Q^2 = 1-10\) GeV\(^2\).
* The dipole formula used for normalization is \(G_D(Q^2) = 1/(1+Q^2/\mu_0^2)^2\) with \(\mu_0^2 = 0.71\) GeV\(^2\).
* The integrated cross section \(Q^6 \sigma_{\gamma^*p \to \pi^0 p}\) is shown for \(W = 1.11\) GeV and \(W = 1.15\) GeV, exhibiting scaling \(\sigma \sim 1/Q^6\).
* The differential cross section \(d\sigma_{\gamma^*p \to \pi^0 p}/d\Omega_\pi\) is plotted for \(Q^2 = 4.2\) GeV\(^2\) and \(W = 1.11\) GeV.
* The axial coupling constant used is \(g_A \simeq 1.267\).

## Context

This work extends the LCSR technique previously applied to nucleon form factors by Braun (2001) and to pion electroproduction at large \(Q^2\) by Braun (2006), now bridging to the low-\(Q^2\) region where LET from current algebra (Nambu, 1960; Kroll and Ruderman, 1954) and chiral perturbation theory (Bernard, 1995) apply. The predictions enable experimental studies of threshold pion electroproduction at facilities like JLAB, and the authors compare their results with SLAC E136 data (Bosted, 1993) on the structure function \(F_2(W,Q^2)\) in the threshold region. The approach also opens the possibility of incorporating final-state interactions via the Watson theorem and improving the model with future lattice calculations of nucleon distribution amplitudes.
