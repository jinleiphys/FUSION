# Bound-to-continuum potential model for the $(p,γ)$ reactions of the CNO cycle

> The study of CNO cycle involves the examination of the proton radiative capture, or the $(p,γ)$ reactions below 2 MeV. The astrophysical $\mathcal{S}$ factor characterizing the $(p,γ)$ reaction is usually reduced to the electric dipole transition $E1$ from the scattering state to the bound state. In this work, the partial scattering and the single-particle bound wave functions in the reduced matrix element of the transition are obtained from the single self-consistent mean-field potential deduced from the Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculation. The astrophysical $\mathcal{S}$ factors of the $(p,γ)$ reactions in the CNO cycle were successfully reproduced. The self-consistent Hartree-Fock calculation from the discrete to the continuum is a promising approach for the microscopic analysis of the nucleon-induced reactions in nuclear astrophysics.

# Bound-to-continuum potential model for the $(p,\gamma)$ reactions of the CNO cycle

## Key claim
The astrophysical $\mathcal{S}$ factors of $(p,\gamma)$ reactions in the CNO cycle can be reproduced using a single self-consistent mean-field potential from Skyrme Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations, where both bound and scattering wave functions are obtained from the same potential. However, the central optical potential strength must be fine-tuned with a scaling factor $\lambda_c$ to reproduce low-energy resonances, and spectroscopic factors $S_F$ are adjusted to match experimental data.

## Method
The authors used the Skyrme HF approximation with the SLy4 interaction to generate both the single-particle bound wave functions and the partial scattering wave functions from a single self-consistent mean-field potential. The radial overlap integral of these wave functions was computed to obtain the $E1$ transition cross section, which was then converted to the astrophysical $\mathcal{S}$ factor. The strength of the central part of the real optical potential was scaled by a factor $\lambda_c$ (ranging from 1.00 to 1.32 across reactions) to reproduce resonance positions, while spectroscopic factors $S_F$ were adjusted to match experimental data for transitions to ground and excited states.

## Key numbers
- $^{12}$C($p,\gamma$)$^{13}$N: resonance at $E_p = 0.42$ MeV; scaling factor $\lambda_c = 1.15$; spectroscopic factor $S_F = 0.36$ for transition to $1/2^-$ ground state with $Q$-value 1.944 MeV
- $^{13}$C($p,\gamma$)$^{14}$N: resonance at $0.51$ MeV; $s$-wave with $\lambda_c = 1.03$, $S_F = 0.27$; $d$-wave with $\lambda_c = 1.30$, $S_F = 0.27$; $Q$-value 7.550 MeV
- $^{14}$N($p,\gamma$)$^{15}$O: resonances at $0.26$ MeV and $0.99$ MeV; $s$-wave $\lambda_c = 1.08$, $S_F = 2 \times 10^{-3}$; $d$-wave $\lambda_c = 1.32$, $S_F = 0.02$ for first resonance; $d$-wave $\lambda_c = 1.28$, $S_F = 5 \times 10^{-3}$ for second resonance; $Q$-value 7.297 MeV
- $^{16}$O($p,\gamma$)$^{17}$F: no resonance below 1 MeV; $\lambda_c = 1.00$; $S_F = 1.00$ for transition to $5/2^+$ ground state with $Q$-value 0.600 MeV
- $^{16}$O($p,\gamma$)$^{17}$F$^*$ (0.495 MeV excited state): $\lambda_c = 1.00$; $S_F = 1.00$ for $p$-wave with $J^\pi = 1/2^-$

## Context
This work extends the potential model approach for $(p,\gamma)$ reactions by using a self-consistent HF potential instead of phenomenological potentials such as Gaussian, Woods-Saxon, or square-well potentials used in previous works (e.g., Xu 2013, Dubovichenko 2011, Huang 2010, Rolfs 1973, Tian 2018). The method connects to the HF-based optical potential calculations for nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering up to 40 MeV using Skyrme interactions (Mizuyama 2014, Hao 2015) or Gogny interactions (Blazkiewicz 2015). This approach enables microscopic analysis of nucleon-induced reactions in nuclear astrophysics, with the potential to be extended to reactions beyond the CNO cycle and to include pairing and deformation corrections.
