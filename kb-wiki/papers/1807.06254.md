# Rapidity decorrelation from hydrodynamic fluctuations

> We discuss rapidity decorrelation caused by hydrodynamic fluctuations in high-energy nuclear collisions at the LHC energy. We employ an integrated dynamical model which is a combination of the Monte Carlo version of Glauber model with extension to longitudinal direction for initial conditions, full three-dimensional relativistic fluctuating hydrodynamics for the space-time evolution of created matter in the intermediate stage and a hadronic cascade model in the late stage. We switch on and off the hydrodynamic fluctuations in the hydrodynamic stage to understand the effects of hydrodynamic fluctuations on factorisation ratios $r_{n}(η^a_p, η^b_p)$. To understand the rapidity gap dependence of the factorisation ratio comprehensively, we analyse Legendre coefficients $A^k_2$ and $B^k_2$.

## Key claim

Hydrodynamic fluctuations (thermal fluctuations in the quark-gluon plasma stage) break the factorisation of anisotropic flow coefficients in the rapidity direction, making the factorisation ratio \(r_2(\eta^a_p, \eta^b_p)\) smaller than in viscous hydrodynamics alone, and bringing the model results closer to experimental CMS data, but the quantitative agreement depends on the choice of the Gaussian smearing width \(\lambda\) for the fluctuation-dissipation relation.

## Method

The authors employed an integrated dynamical model combining: (1) a Monte Carlo Glauber model extended to the longitudinal direction via a modified BGK wounded-nucleon picture for initial entropy density; (2) full three-dimensional relativistic fluctuating hydrodynamics with a second-order shear stress tensor equation including a stochastic noise term \(\xi^{\mu\nu}\) obeying a fluctuation-dissipation relation with a smeared delta function of width \(\lambda\); and (3) the hadronic cascade model JAM for the late stage. They switched hydrodynamic fluctuations on and off to compare ideal, viscous (\(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\)), and fluctuating hydrodynamics, and analysed the factorisation ratio \(r_2(\eta^a_p, \eta^b_p)\) and the Legendre coefficients \(A^k_2\) and \(B^k_2\) for the rapidity dependence of \(v_2\) and its event-plane angle \(\Psi_2\).

## Key numbers

* Factorisation ratio \(r_2(\eta^a_p, \eta^b_p)\) for rapidity regions \(2.0 < \eta^a_p < 2.5\) and \(3.0 < \eta^b_p < 4.0\): fluctuating hydrodynamics with \(\lambda = 1.0\) fm is close to CMS data in 0–10% centrality; with \(\lambda = 1.5\) fm it is close to CMS data in 10–40% centrality.
* Shear viscosity \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\) and relaxation time \(\tau_\pi = 3/4\pi T\).
* Switching temperature from hydrodynamics to hadronic cascade: \(T_{\mathrm{sw}} = 155\) MeV.
* Number of hydrodynamic events: 4000; oversampling for hadronic cascade: 100 per hydrodynamic event.
* Gaussian smearing widths for the fluctuation term: \(\lambda = 1.0\) fm and \(\lambda = 1.5\) fm.
* Legendre coefficients \(A^1_2\) (for \(v_2\)) and \(B^1_2\) (for \(\Psi_2\)) are larger in fluctuating hydrodynamics than in viscous hydrodynamics across all centralities.
* Second-order Legendre coefficient \(B^2_2\) (for \(\Psi_2\)) is finite and large, especially in central and peripheral collisions, and larger in fluctuating hydrodynamics than in viscous hydrodynamics.

## Context

This work extends studies of rapidity decorrelation of anisotropic flow, previously investigated using viscous hydrodynamics and compared to CMS data (2015), by incorporating thermal fluctuations (hydrodynamic fluctuations) in a full three-dimensional fluctuating hydrodynamics framework. It builds on the integrated dynamical model of Murase (2016) and the initial-condition model based on the wounded-nucleon picture. The results show that hydrodynamic fluctuations are a crucial ingredient for reproducing the factorisation ratios measured by CMS, and the newly proposed Legendre coefficients \(A^k_2\) and \(B^k_2\) provide a more comprehensive tool to quantify rapidity-dependent decorrelation, including higher-order twists of the event plane.
