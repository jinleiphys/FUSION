# From $χ$EFT to Multi-Region Modeling: Neutron star structure with a polytropic extension of $χ$EFT and MUSES Calculation Engine multi-layer modeling

> Neutron stars provide a unique environment to probe the properties of dense nuclear matter. In this work, we present a comparative study between two approaches to modeling the neutron star structure: a Chiral Effective Field Theory based approach and the MUSES Calculation Engine framework, which uses three different approaches for the three density regions. We analyze the resulting mass-radius relations, discussing the respective advantages and limitations of the two methods.

# From χEFT to Multi-Region Modeling: Neutron star structure with a polytropic extension of χEFT and MUSES Calculation Engine multi-layer modeling

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a polytropic extension of chiral effective field theory (PχEFT-npeμ) and the MUSES Calculation Engine multi-layer framework (MUSES-npeμ) produce mutually consistent neutron star equations of state in the density regime where microscopic χEFT input is most reliable, but diverge at higher densities where the stiffness of the EoS drives both maximum mass and stellar compactness. Neither approach is claimed to be universally superior; the polytropic continuation is a flexible parametric extrapolation while MUSES CE provides a more physically grounded microphysics-motivated framework for the high-density regime.

## Method

The author constructs two cold, β-stabilized npeμ equations of state for neutron stars, both restricted to nucleonic degrees of freedom only. The first approach (PχEFT-npeμ) uses a χEFT equation of state up to a matching density n₁ = 0.35 fm⁻³, then a single polytropic segment up to n₂ = 0.64 fm⁻³ with adiabatic index Γ₁ = 3.45, followed by a speed-of-sound extension parametrized as a Gaussian with a causality cap at v²_s,cap = 0.80. The second approach (MUSES-npeμ) uses the MUSES Calculation Engine composable workflow, combining a Crust Density Functional Theory module at low densities, χEFT around saturation density, and the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model with C4 coupling scheme at high densities, with modules synthesized via hyperbolic tangent. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved for both EoSs to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- PχEFT-npeμ maximum mass: M_max ≃ 2.17 M_⊙ with characteristic radius R(M_max) ≃ 10.13 km
- MUSES-npeμ maximum mass: M_max ≃ 2.04 M_⊙ with characteristic radius R(M_max) ≃ 11.3 km
- Matching density n₁ = 0.35 fm⁻³ for the polytropic extension
- Second matching density n₂ = 0.64 fm⁻³ for the speed-of-sound extension
- Polytropic adiabatic index Γ₁ = 3.45
- Sound speed cap v²_s,cap = 0.80
- Gaussian width parameter w = 0.55 fm⁻³
- χEFT regulator cutoff Λ = 450 MeV with exponent n = 3
- Three-nucleon force regulator scale Λ_3N = 700 MeV with low-energy constants c_D = -0.24, c_E = -0.106

## Context

This work extends the χEFT-based neutron star modeling approach of Bombaci and Logoteta (2018) by implementing the polytropic and speed-of-sound continuation strategy proposed by Sammarruca and Ajagbonna (2024). It provides a direct comparison with the MUSES Calculation Engine framework described by Cruz-Camacho et al. (2025) and the broader MUSES collaboration (2025), which uses the Chiral Mean Field model for the high-density core. The results are compared against observational constraints from Miller and Coleman (2021) and against other microscopic approaches including a χEFT+Δ(1232) model from Bombaci and Logoteta (2018) and a DDFGOS(APR) unified crust model from Davis et al. (2025). The work establishes a nucleonic-core baseline for future studies incorporating additional high-density degrees of freedom such as hyperons, Δ-isobars, or deconfined quarks, and enables systematic investigations of hybrid-star phenomenology and axion-nucleon coupling constraints from neutron star cooling.
