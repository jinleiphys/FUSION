# Expanding the concept of in-hadron condensates

> The in-pseudoscalar-meson condensate can be represented through the pseudoscalar-meson's scalar form factor at zero momentum transfer. With the aid of a mass formula for scalar mesons, revealed herein, the analogue is shown to be true for in-scalar-meson condensates. The concept is readily extended to all hadrons so that, via the zero momentum transfer value of any hadron's scalar form factor, one can readily extract the value for a quark condensate in that hadron which is a measure of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the so-called "vacuum quark condensate" is actually an intrinsic property of hadrons (specifically the chiral-limit value of the in-pion condensate) rather than a constant mass-scale filling all spacetime, and that this concept can be extended to all hadrons via the zero-momentum-transfer value of each hadron's scalar form factor. The hedge is that this identification is demonstrated rigorously for pseudoscalar and scalar mesons, while for other hadrons it is argued by analogy and appeal to heavy-quark symmetry.

## Method

The authors use the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity to derive a mass formula for pseudoscalar mesons (Eq. (gmorR)) and a novel mass formula for scalar mesons (Eq. (mSqQ)), relating each meson's mass to its leptonic decay constant and an in-hadron condensate. They then show that each meson's scalar form factor at zero momentum transfer, \( {\cal S}^\zeta_{P_{qQ}}(0) \), equals the derivative of the squared meson mass with respect to current-quark mass, and compute these quantities using a symmetry-preserving regularization and rainbow-ladder truncation of a vector×vector contact interaction. For baryons, they use the Faddeev equation framework to compute the nucleon scalar form factor and relate it to the dressed-quark mass function.

## Key numbers

- Chiral-limit value of the in-pion condensate: \( \kappa^0_{P_{qQ}} = (0.24\ \text{GeV})^3 \)
- Chiral-limit value of the pseudoscalar meson scalar form factor: \( {\cal S}^0_{P_{qQ}} = 1.39\ \text{GeV} \)
- Chiral-limit value of the scalar meson scalar form factor: \( {\cal S}^0_{S_{qQ}} = 2.06\ \text{GeV} \)
- Chiral-limit dressed-quark mass in the model: \( M^0 = 0.36\ \text{GeV} \)
- For the ground-state rho meson: \( {\cal S}_{\rho} = 1.33\ \text{GeV} \), \( f_\rho = 0.129\ \text{GeV} \), \( \chi_\rho^{1/3} = 0.281\ \text{GeV} \)
- For the ground-state a1 meson: \( {\cal S}_{a_1} = 2.30\ \text{GeV} \), \( f_{a_1} = 0.089\ \text{GeV} \), \( \chi_{a_1}^{1/3} = 0.263\ \text{GeV} \)
- Nucleon scalar form factor at \( m=7\ \text{MeV} \): \( {\cal S}_{N} = 1.42 \)
- Mass-normalised in-nucleon condensate: \( \tilde\chi_{N} = (0.623\ \text{GeV})^3 \)
- Chiral-limit value of the mass-normalised in-meson condensate: \( (0.243\ \text{GeV})^3 \), rising to \( (0.307\ \text{GeV})^3 \) in the heavy-quark limit

## Context

This work extends the concept of in-hadron condensates, previously established for pseudoscalar mesons by Brodsky (2010) and Maris (1997), to all hadrons including scalar mesons, vector mesons, axial-vector mesons, and baryons. It directly contradicts the traditional QCD sum-rule picture of vacuum condensates as constant mass-scales filling spacetime (Shifman 1978), which leads to a cosmological constant problem (Turner 2001, Brodsky 2009). The paper enables future extraction of in-hadron condensates from experimental measurements of hadron scalar form factors, providing a realistic measure of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking within each hadron.
