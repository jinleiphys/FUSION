# Stark effect and dissociation of mesons in holographic conductor

> We study the meson spectrum of the ${\cal{N}}=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory with ${\cal{N}}=2$ fundamental hypermultiplets for a finite electric field by using the D3/D7 model. The spectrum for scalar and vector mesons is computed by analyzing the (quasi-)normal modes for the fluctuations of the D7-brane embedding and gauge fields. In the presence of an electric field, two different phases in the background are realized: the meson and dissociation phases. In this paper, we analyze the meson spectrum of scalar and vector mesons for all ranges of the electric field and explore the effect of the electric field on the meson spectrum, that is, the Stark effect. In the meson spectrum, we observe the avoided crossing between different levels due to the coupling of fluctuations via the electric field.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the D3/D7 holographic model, the meson spectrum under a finite electric field exhibits a Stark effect with avoided crossings between different levels, but this behavior is conditional on the coupling between scalar and vector fluctuations induced by the electric field, and the spectrum depends on whether the system is in the meson (insulating) phase or dissociation (conducting) phase.

## Method

The authors study the D3/D7 holographic model of N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory with N=2 fundamental hypermultiplets, analyzing fluctuations of the D7-brane embedding and gauge fields to compute (quasi-)normal modes for scalar and vector mesons. They solve the equations of motion for the D7-brane background configurations (Minkowski embeddings for the meson phase and black hole embeddings for the dissociation phase) and then perturb these backgrounds to extract the meson spectrum across all ranges of the electric field, from zero field to the strong field limit corresponding to zero quark mass.

## Key numbers

* Critical electric field value: E_c/m^2 ≈ 0.57632, at which the D7-brane becomes the critical embedding between meson and dissociation phases
* The cone at the critical embedding has half-angle arctan(√6) at the apex, determined by force balance
* The quark mass m_q = (λ^(1/2)/(2π)) m and electric field ε_x = (λ^(1/2)/(2π)) E, where λ is the 't Hooft coupling

## Context

This work extends the earlier study of the Stark effect for the decoupled pseudo-scalar mode by Erdmenger (2007) to include all modes: coupled scalar and vector components parallel to the electric field, the decoupled pseudo-scalar, and decoupled vector components perpendicular to the electric field. The paper also connects to the condensed matter context by interpreting the meson/dissociation phases as insulator/conducting phases and meson spectra as exciton spectra, and relates the dynamical instability near the critical electric field to hysteresis loops in the E-J curve.
