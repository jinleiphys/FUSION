# Stochastic analysis of ultra-high energy cosmic ray interactions

> Photonuclear interactions between ultra-high-energy cosmic ray (UHECR) nuclei and surrounding photon fields are key to understanding the connection between the compositions observed at Earth and those emitted from the sources. These interactions can completely disintegrate a nucleus of iron over trajectory lengths of a few and up to hundreds of megaparsecs, depending on the energy of the UHECR. The stochastic nature of these interactions means that it is not possible to describe them deterministically for a single cosmic ray, and an exact formulation of the probability distributions is not yet available. Current approaches describe these interactions using either Monte Carlo simulations or solving ordinary differential equations that neglect stochasticity. Because of the limitations of these approaches, only partial capture of the process is achieved. This paper presents an analytic probabilistic description of UHECR interactions and the resulting nuclear cascades, establishing their connection to Markov jump processes. The fundamental properties of these cascades are presented, as is the computation of the usual quantities of interest, such as the horizon, spectrum, and composition. The benefits of this description are outlined using astrophysical examples related to extragalactic propagation and UHECR sources.

## Key claim

The paper presents an analytic probabilistic description of ultra-high-energy cosmic ray (UHECR) photonuclear interactions and the resulting nuclear cascades, establishing their connection to Markov jump processes; the authors hedge that this framework currently neglects continuous energy losses and magnetic field effects in its simplest form, and that the equivalence between the stochastic and deterministic descriptions is only exact for homogeneous, time-independent conditions without continuous energy losses.

## Method

The authors formulate the stochastic evolution of UHECR nuclear cascades as a Markov jump process, where nuclear species are transient states with transition probabilities determined by photonuclear interaction rates computed from cross-sections and target photon fields. They derive closed-form probability distributions for the distance until absorption (full disintegration) for three cascade types: regular serial cascades (RSeCs), where interaction rates scale proportionally to mass number; irregular serial cascades (ISeCs), where rates deviate from mass scaling due to nuclear structure effects and spontaneous decay; and concurrent cascades (CoCs), which include multiple disintegration channels and branching networks. The probability density for the distance until absorption is expressed as \( f(L) = -\boldsymbol{\phi} \exp(\boldsymbol{\Lambda} L) \boldsymbol{\Lambda} \boldsymbol{1} \), where \(\boldsymbol{\phi}\) is the initial composition vector and \(\boldsymbol{\Lambda}\) is the interaction rate matrix.

## Key numbers

- Photonuclear interactions can completely disintegrate a nucleus of iron over trajectory lengths of a few up to hundreds of megaparsecs, depending on the UHECR energy.
- The regular serial cascade (RSeC) probability density for stripping \(k\) nucleons from a nucleus of mass \(A\) is \( f^{\mathrm{RS}}_{A \to A-k}(L) = \lambda_{A-k+1}e^{-\lambda_{A-k+1}L} \binom{A}{k-1} \left(1 - e^{-\lambda_1 L}\right)^{k-1} \), where \(\lambda_1\) is the interaction rate per nucleon.
- The mean distance for a regular serial cascade to lose \(k\) nucleons is \( \mathbb{E}[L_k] = \lambda_1^{-1} \ln\left( \frac{A+1}{A-k+1} \right) \).
- The mean distance for an irregular serial cascade to lose \(k\) nucleons is \( \mathbb{E}[L_k] = \sum_{i=A-k+1}^A 1/\lambda_i \), the sum of mean interaction lengths of each species in the chain.
- The coefficient of variation for energy-weighted photodisintegration cross-sections divided by mass number is typically about 10% or less after the giant dipole resonance peak for models with many species, but remains above 30% for serial models with only one species per mass number.
- The system of ordinary differential equations for UHECR propagation typically comprises between approximately 50 and 200 nuclear species when including elements up to iron, with an energy grid of about 100 bins in logarithmic scale.

## Context

This work extends the continuous-limit approximation approaches used in prior studies such as Hill (1985), Berezinsky and Grigorieva (1988), and Aloisio et al. (2013, 2014), as well as Monte Carlo methods like those in CRPropa (Kampert et al., 2012; Alves Batista et al., 2022) and SimProp (Aloisio et al., 2017), by providing an analytic probabilistic framework that captures the stochastic nature of photonuclear interactions. The formalism enables computation of quantities such as the horizon, spectrum, and composition with arbitrary precision at modest computational effort, and facilitates studying the impact of input uncertainties in nuclear cross-sections and photon field models without exhaustive parameter space scans. The authors note that a subsequent publication will extend the stochastic analytic method to hadronic interactions.
