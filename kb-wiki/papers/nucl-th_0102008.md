# Statistical evolution of fragment isospin in nuclear multifragmentation

> Two instructive effects concerning fragment production at disintegration of finite nuclei are predicted with the statistical multifragmentation model: (1) a concentration of neutrons in intermediate mass fragments during the phase transition, (2) the break of the spatial symmetry of the fragment's isospin distribution, as well as of the symmetry of the fragment's emission from a statistical source, induced by the external Coulomb field.

# Statistical evolution of fragment isospin in nuclear multifragmentation

## Key claim
The statistical multifragmentation model predicts that during the liquid-gas type phase transition in finite nuclei, intermediate mass fragments become increasingly neutron-rich, and that an external Coulomb field from a target residue breaks the spatial symmetry of fragment isospin distributions, producing neutron-rich IMFs preferentially on the target side as a statistical alternative to dynamical "neck fragmentation" explanations.

## Method
The author used the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) with a new Markov chain method of partition generation on a microcanonical basis to calculate fragment production from a Au source (mass number 197, charge 79) at excitation energies of 3–8 MeV/nucleon. For peripheral collision simulations, the Au projectile source was placed at a fixed distance of 20 fm from another Au source simulating the target residue, with calculations including Coulomb interaction and angular momentum of 150 ℏ.

## Key numbers
- Excitation energy range studied: 3–8 MeV/nucleon, associated with the liquid-gas type phase transition in finite nuclei
- Freeze-out density used: ρ_s = ρ_0/6, where ρ_0 ≈ 0.15 fm⁻³ is normal nuclear density
- Distance between projectile and target sources in peripheral collision simulation: 20 fm
- Angular momentum value included in some calculations: 150 ℏ
- IMF charge range considered: Z = 3–20
- Characteristic break-up time for peripheral collisions: approximately 100 fm/c

## Context
This work extends the isospin fractionation phenomenon predicted for infinite nuclear matter by Mueller and collaborators to finite nuclear systems, showing how the neutron content of IMFs increases during the phase transition rather than the number of free neutrons. The spatial asymmetry results provide a statistical alternative to dynamical "neck fragmentation" explanations proposed by Dempsey and Larochelle for neutron-rich IMF emission at midrapidity, building on earlier work by Botvina (1999) on Coulomb-induced symmetry breaking in statistical emission. The results enable experimental identification of statistical versus dynamical fragmentation mechanisms through measurement of fragment isospin distributions.
