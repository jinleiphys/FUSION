# Shock wave collisions in AdS5: approximate numerical solutions

> We numerically study the evolution of a boost-invariant N=4 SYM medium using AdS/CFT. We consider a toy model for the collision of gravitational shock waves, finding that the energy density first increases, reaches a maximum and then starts to decrease, matching hydrodynamics for late times. For the initial conditions we consider, the hydrodynamic scale governing the late time behaviour is to very good approximation determined by the area of the black hole horizon at initial times. Our results provide a toy model for the early time evolution of the bulk system in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC.

# Shock wave collisions in AdS5: approximate numerical solutions

## Key claim

The authors numerically model the collision of gravitational shock waves in asymptotically Anti-de-Sitter space as a toy model for heavy-ion collisions, finding that the energy density rises to a maximum and then decreases, matching hydrodynamic behavior at late times. However, this result is obtained only in the limit of weak shock waves (μ ≪ 1) where the leading-order dynamics becomes boost-invariant, and the authors introduce a free parameter (c̄) to regularize the metric near the apparent horizon where the perturbative approximation breaks down.

## Method

The authors solve Einstein's equations in Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates using a pseudo-spectral method with spectral differentiation in the radial coordinate r and finite differences in proper time τ, starting from an ansatz for the metric that interpolates between the perturbative weak-shock solution and a numerically well-behaved form. Two algorithms are employed: one with gauge choice f = 0 (Alg. I) and one fixing the apparent horizon radius to unity (Alg. II), with the latter requiring smaller time steps for stability. The boundary energy-momentum tensor is extracted via holographic renormalization from the metric coefficient a₄.

## Key numbers

- The apparent horizon radius at τ = 0 for weak shocks is r_h(τ=0) ∼ (24 μ̄²/35)^{1/6}, where μ̄ = μ/κ is the dimensionless shock strength.
- The horizon area at τ = 0 is A_h(τ=0) = (192^{1/3} μ̄^{2/3} / 35^{1/3}) V, with V = ∫ dx_⟂ dη.
- The minimum allowed fudge parameter c̄_min at τ₀ = 0 is 2.88, rising to 19.84 at τ₀ = 1.0.
- The horizon area at the minimum allowed c̄ ranges from V⁻¹ μ̄^{-2/3} A_h = 0.83 at τ₀ = 0 to 2.85 at τ₀ = 1.0.
- For numerical stability, Alg. I requires dτ = 1/N² and Alg. II requires dτ = 10⁻²/N², with N = 128 collocation points and L_max = 20 used in the late-time tests.

## Context

This work extends the numerical approach pioneered by Chesler and Yaffe (2010, 2011) for solving gravitational shock wave collisions in AdS, using a different method that connects analytically derived initial conditions from the weak-shock approximation to the late-time hydrodynamic regime. The results validate those of Heller et al. (2011), who used a different algorithm with arbitrary initial conditions. The late-time behavior builds on the large-τ expansion of Janik and Peschanski (2005, 2006) and subsequent work by Kinoshita et al. (2008) and Booth et al. (2009). The model provides a qualitative toy for early-time evolution in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC, though the authors note that the shock wave collision violates Bjorken boost invariance except in the weak-shock limit.
