# Spontaneous fission half-lives of actinides and super-heavy elements

> Spontaneous fission half-lives of actinide and super-heavy nuclei are calculated, using the least-action integral, through the WKB tunneling probability of the barrier that appears in the deformation landscape obtained in the macroscopic-microscopic potential-energy surface. This deformation-energy landscape is obtained using a Fourier shape parametrization with 4 deformation parameters, taking into account the nuclear elongation, left-right asymmetry, neck formation and non-axiality degrees of freedom. The collective inertia tensor entering the WKB half-life expression is given through the so-called irrotational flow approach, successfully used in nuclear fission to reproduce observables that characterize the nuclear system in the vicinity of the scission configurations, such as fragment mass or charge distributions. For a comparisons, we have also used the so-called phenomenological mass parameter depending only on the center-of-mass difference of the forming fission fragments. Our approach is shown to be able to reproduce empirical fission half-lives of all here considered nuclei to within 3 orders of magnitude.

## Key claim
The authors calculate spontaneous fission half-lives for actinide and super-heavy nuclei using a least-action WKB tunneling approach, and they state that their method reproduces empirical half-lives to within 3 orders of magnitude. The hedge is that this agreement holds only for the specific set of nuclei considered (even-even isotopes from Z=90 to 110) and depends on the choice of collective inertia model (irrotational flow or phenomenological mass parameter).

## Method
The nuclear deformation-energy landscape was computed using a macroscopic-microscopic model with the Lublin-Strasbourg Drop (LSD) for the macroscopic part and Strutinsky shell corrections plus BCS pairing with particle-number projection for the microscopic part. The nuclear shape was parametrized using a Fourier expansion with four deformation parameters (q2 for elongation, q3 for left-right asymmetry, q4 for neck formation, and eta for non-axiality). The WKB tunneling probability was evaluated via the least-action integral, using the collective inertia tensor from the irrotational flow model (scaled by an overall factor) and, for comparison, a phenomenological mass parameter depending only on the center-of-mass difference of the fission fragments.

## Key numbers
- The macroscopic-microscopic model (LSD) reproduces ground-state masses of 2766 isotopes with an r.m.s. deviation of 0.70 MeV from experimental data.
- The fitted pairing strength parameters for protons are g0 = 18.35 MeV and g1 = 0.103 MeV; for neutrons, g0 = 24.1 MeV and g1 = -0.135 MeV.
- The largest deviation between calculated and empirical neutron pairing gaps is 0.35 MeV (for 236Th and 250Cf); for protons it is always lower than 0.2 MeV, with average largest deviations around 0.12 MeV.
- The approach reproduces empirical fission half-lives for all considered nuclei to within 3 orders of magnitude.

## Context
This work extends the macroscopic-microscopic approach to spontaneous fission half-lives using a Fourier shape parametrization, building on earlier liquid-drop models such as the semi-empirical formula of Swiatecki (1955) and the Lublin-Strasbourg Drop model of Pomorski and Dudek (2003). It contrasts with fully microscopic self-consistent methods (e.g., Bender et al. 2011, Schunck et al. 2014) which are numerically costly and have not yet achieved fully satisfactory accuracy. The results enable predictions for half-lives of nuclei where measurements have not yet been performed, particularly for super-heavy elements.
