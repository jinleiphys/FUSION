# Ternary fission within the temperature dependent relativistic mean field approach

> For the first time, we apply the temperature dependent relativistic mean field (TRMF) model to study the ternary fission of heavy nucleus using level density approach. The probability of yields of a particular fragment is obtained by evaluating the convolution integrals which employ the excitation energy and the level density parameter for a given temperature calculated within the TRMF formalism. To illustrate, we have considered the ternary fissions in 252Cf, 242Pu and 236U with fixed third fragment A3 = 48Ca, 20O and 16O respectively. The relative yields are studied for the temperatures T = 1, 2 and 3 MeV. For the comparison, the relative yields are also calculated from the single particle energies of the finite range droplet model (FRDM). In general, the larger phase space for the ternary fragmentation is observed indicating that such fragmentations are most probable ones. For T = 2 and 3 MeV, the Sn + Ni + Ca is the most probable combination for the nucleus 252Cf. However, for the nuclei 242Pu and 236U, the maximum fragmentation yields at T = 2 MeV differ from those at T = 3 MeV. For T = 3 MeV, the closed shell (Z = 8) light mass fragments with its corresponding partners has larger yield values. But, at T = 2 MeV Si/P/S are favorable fragments with the corresponding partners. It is noticed that the symmetric binary fragmentation along with the fixed third fragment for 242Pu and 236U are also favored at T = 1 MeV. The temperature dependence of the nuclear shape and the single particle energies are also discussed.

## Key claim

For the first time, the temperature dependent relativistic mean field (TRMF) model is applied to study ternary fission yields, but the results are limited to fixed third fragments (A3 = 48Ca, 20O, 16O) and do not consider all possible third fragments or competing decay modes such as neutron emission, alpha decay, or binary fragmentation.

## Method

The authors used the temperature dependent relativistic mean field (TRMF) model with the NL3 parameter set and BCS pairing to calculate single particle energies, excitation energies, and level density parameters for fission fragments of 252Cf, 242Pu, and 236U. Ternary fission probabilities were obtained by evaluating convolution integrals of the level densities of three fragments at temperatures T = 1, 2, and 3 MeV, with the third fragment fixed a priori. For comparison, the same calculations were performed using single particle energies from the finite range droplet model (FRDM) as in prior work by Balasubramaniam et al. (2014) and S. K. et al. (2016).

## Key numbers

- For 252Cf at T = 2 and 3 MeV, the most probable combination is 132Sn + 72Ni + 48Ca.
- For 242Pu at T = 1 MeV, the most favorable combination is 165Gd + 57Ti + 20O.
- For 236U at T = 1 MeV, symmetric binary fragments 109Mo + 111Mo + 16O and 110Mo + 110Mo + 16O have larger yield values.
- For 236U at T = 2 MeV, the most probable fragments include 180,181Tm + 40,39P + 16O.
- For 242Pu and 236U, shape transitions from prolate to spherical occur at critical temperatures Tc = 1.8 MeV and Tc = 1.6 MeV respectively; for 252Cf the deformation vanishes at Tc = 2.2 MeV.
- Pairing transitions occur at T = 0.4 MeV for 252Cf and at T = 0.2 MeV for 242Pu and 236U.
- The experimental CCT yield for 252Cf with ternary 48Ca is reported as 4.7 ± 0.2 × 10−3 per binary fission, and for 236U with ternary 34Si as 5.1 ± 0.4 × 10−3 per binary fission (from Pyatkov et al., 2010).

## Context

This work extends the statistical theory approach to ternary fission previously applied by Balasubramaniam et al. (2014) and S. K. et al. (2016) using FRDM single particle energies, by incorporating self-consistent temperature dependent single particle energies from the TRMF model. The results enable comparison with experimental collinear cluster tripartition (CCT) data from Pyatkov et al. (2010, 2012) and Koster et al. (1999), and with earlier three cluster model calculations by Manimaran et al. (2009). The temperature dependent effects on nuclear shape and single particle energies, including the transition from deformed to spherical shape and from pairing to normal phase, are shown to significantly affect the predicted yields.
