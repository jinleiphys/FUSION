# Deep crustal heating by neutrinos from the surface of accreting neutron stars

> We present a new mechanism for deep crustal heating in accreting neutron stars. Charged pions ($π^+$) are produced in nuclear collisions on the neutron star surface during active accretion and upon decay they provide a flux of neutrinos into the neutron star crust. For massive and/or compact neutron stars, neutrinos deposit $\approx 1\textrm{--} 2 \, \mathrm{MeV}$ of heat per accreted nucleon into the inner crust. The strength of neutrino heating is comparable to the previously known sources of deep crustal heating, such as from pycnonuclear fusion reactions, and is relevant for studies of cooling neutron stars. We model the thermal evolution of a transient neutron star in a low-mass X-ray binary, and in the particular case of the neutron star MXB~1659-29 we show that additional deep crustal heating requires a higher thermal conductivity for the neutron star inner crust. A better knowledge of pion production cross sections near threshold would improve the accuracy of our predictions.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a new mechanism for deep crustal heating in accreting neutron stars, where neutrinos from the decay of charged pions produced in nuclear collisions on the neutron star surface deposit approximately 1–2 MeV of heat per accreted nucleon into the inner crust, but only for massive and/or compact neutron stars; the strength of this heating is comparable to previously known sources such as pycnonuclear fusion, and the accuracy of the predictions depends on better knowledge of pion production cross sections near threshold.

## Method
The authors calculated the kinetic energy of infalling matter at the neutron star surface using general relativity, then modeled pion production from proton-proton, proton-iron, and alpha-iron collisions using experimental data and the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model. They computed the neutrino energy deposition in the inner crust by evaluating the transport optical depth for neutrinos from stopped pion decays, using four different equations of state (FSU2 soft, FSU2 stiff, HLPS soft, HLPS stiff) to account for uncertainties in neutron star structure.

## Key numbers
- For a 2.0 solar mass neutron star with the HLPS soft equation of state, the total energy deposited per accreted nucleon by neutrinos is 2.114 MeV for proton-proton collisions, 1.963 MeV for proton-iron collisions, and 2.822 MeV for alpha-iron collisions.
- For a 1.4 solar mass neutron star with the HLPS soft equation of state, the energy deposited is 0.000 MeV for proton-proton collisions, 0.000 MeV for proton-iron collisions, and 0.109 MeV for alpha-iron collisions.
- The kinetic energy of a nucleon at the surface of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star ranges from 178.0 MeV (FSU2 stiff) to 289.5 MeV (HLPS soft), and for a 2.0 solar mass star from 291.6 MeV (HLPS stiff) to 565.2 MeV (HLPS soft).
- The muon neutrino from pion decay has a monoenergetic energy of 29.8 MeV; the root-mean-square energies for electron and muon antineutrinos are 33.3 MeV and 37.7 MeV, respectively.
- The pion production threshold is approximately 290 MeV per nucleon.

## Context
This work extends the standard picture of deep crustal heating from non-equilibrium nuclear reactions such as pycnonuclear fusion (Haensel 1990, Haensel 2003) by introducing a new neutrino-based heating mechanism from pion decays on the neutron star surface. The results are relevant for interpreting the cooling light curves of transient neutron stars like MXB 1659-29, where previous studies (Cumming 2005, Deibel 2015) required additional heat deposition in the outer crust to match observations. The paper enables future work to refine pion production cross sections near threshold and to explore the role of electromagnetic acceleration in enhancing neutrino production.
