# On the width of N-Delta and Delta-Delta states

> It is seen by a coupled-channel calculation that in the two-baryon N-Delta or Delta-Delta system the width of the state is greatly diminished due to the relative kinetic energy of the two baryons, since the internal energy of the particles, available for pionic decay, is smaller. A similar state dependent effect arises from the centrifugal barrier in N-Delta or Delta-Delta systems with non-zero orbital angular momentum. The double-Delta width can become even smaller than the free width of a single Delta. This has some bearing to the interpretation of the d'(2380) resonance recently discovered at COSY.

# On the width of N-Delta and Delta-Delta states

## Key claim

The width of a two-baryon N-Delta or Delta-Delta state is greatly diminished compared to the free Delta width because the relative kinetic energy of the two baryons is not available for internal pionic decay, and this suppression is state-dependent, depending on the orbital angular momentum of the system. The double-Delta width can become even smaller than the free width of a single Delta, which has bearing on the interpretation of the d'(2380) resonance discovered at COSY.

## Method

The author performed coupled-channel Schrodinger equation calculations for NN scattering with N-Delta and Delta-Delta intermediate states, using the phenomenological Reid potential as the starting point with modifications to reproduce NN phase shifts. The effective width of the two-baryon system was calculated as an average over kinematically allowed momenta using the Fourier transform of the N-Delta or Delta-Delta wave function, with the free Delta width given by a p-wave fit to data, and the system was solved iteratively until stable width values were obtained.

## Key numbers

- The free Delta width is given by the fit: Gamma(q) = 142 * (0.81 q/mu)^3 / [1 + (0.81 q/mu)^2] MeV
- At the nominal N-Delta mass of 2.17 GeV, the effective width of the S-wave N-Delta state is significantly smaller than the free Delta width at the same energy
- At 2.38 GeV, the effective width of the S-3(Delta-Delta) state from the D-3 NN wave is about 50 MeV, lower than the reported d'(2380) width of 70 MeV
- Including D-wave Delta-Delta states increases the width to about 60 MeV
- The phase shift maximum for the D-3 NN partial wave with Delta-Delta coupling occurs at about 2.43 GeV c.m. energy

## Context

This work extends earlier coupled-channel calculations for pp -> d pi+ (Niskanen, 1978) that considered kinematic suppression of the Delta width but never published explicit width results. It also builds on a coupled-channels NN-N-Delta scattering calculation (Niskanen, 1982) that found a rotational spectrum of about 40 L(L+1) MeV on top of the Delta-nucleon mass difference. The results contradict the assumption in some works (Bashkanov, Clement) that the d'(2380) width should be twice the free Delta width, and instead support the possibility that the resonance could be of pure Delta-Delta origin without requiring exotic six-quark components. The work enables further investigation of whether the d'(2380) can be explained by conventional hadronic dynamics or requires coupling to a genuine six-quark configuration.
