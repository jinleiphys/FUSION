# Nuclear incompressibility using the density dependent M3Y effective interaction

> A density dependent M3Y effective nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction which was based on the G-matrix elements of the Reid-Elliott NN potential has been used to determine the incompressibity of infinite nuclear matter. The nuclear interaction potential obtained by folding in the density distribution functions of two interacting nuclei with this density dependent M3Y effective interaction had been shown earlier to provide excellent descriptions for medium and high energy $α$ and heavy ion elastic scatterings as well as $α$ and heavy cluster radioactivities. The density dependent parameters have been chosen to reproduce the saturation energy per nucleon and the saturation density of spin and isospin symmetric cold infinite nuclear matter. The result of such calculations for nuclear incompressibility using the density dependent M3Y effective interaction based on the G-matrix elements of Reid-Elliott NN potential predicts a value of about 300 MeV for nuclear incompressibility.

# Nuclear incompressibility using the density dependent M3Y effective interaction

## Key claim
The density dependent M3Y effective interaction based on the G-matrix elements of the Reid-Elliott NN potential, when used to calculate the incompressibility of spin and isospin symmetric cold infinite nuclear matter, predicts a value of about 300 MeV. The actual calculated range is 304–310 MeV, which agrees with the experimental estimate of 290 ± 50 MeV from hard photon production in heavy ion collisions, but the abstract omits the explicit range and the dependence on the chosen saturation density.

## Method
The density dependent M3Y effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, supplemented by a zero-range pseudo-potential representing single-nucleon exchange, was used to compute the energy per nucleon of spin and isospin symmetric cold infinite nuclear matter. The density dependent parameters β(ε) and C were fixed by simultaneously solving the saturation condition (∂ε/∂ρ = 0) and the energy per nucleon equation at the chosen saturation density and saturation energy per nucleon (−16 MeV). The incompressibility K∞ was then evaluated from the second derivative of the energy per nucleon with respect to density at the saturation point, using the derived parameters.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear incompressibility K∞ = 304–310 MeV for saturation densities in the range 0.150–0.170 fm⁻³, with saturation energy per nucleon ε = −16 MeV.
- At saturation density ρ∞ = 0.170 fm⁻³, K∞ = 309.6 MeV; at ρ∞ = 0.150 fm⁻³, K∞ = 304.0 MeV.
- The density dependence parameter β(ε) ranges from 1.551 fm² (at ρ∞ = 0.170 fm⁻³) to 1.705 fm² (at ρ∞ = 0.150 fm⁻³).
- The energy dependence parameter of the zero-range pseudo-potential was set to either 0.005 MeV⁻¹ or 0.100 MeV⁻¹; the incompressibility value was unchanged by this choice.
- The result agrees with the experimental estimate K∞ = 290 ± 50 MeV from hard photon production in heavy ion collisions (Schutz et al., 1996).
- The result is close to the theoretical estimate by the infinite nuclear matter model (INM) of K∞ = 288 ± 20 MeV (Satpathy, Uma Maheswari and Nayak, 1999).

## Context
This work extends the use of the density dependent M3Y-Reid-Elliott effective interaction, previously successful in describing medium and high energy α and heavy ion elastic scatterings (Chaudhuri, 1986) as well as α and cluster radioactivities (Basu, 2002, 2003), to the calculation of nuclear matter incompressibility. The result rules out lower values of K∞ around 180 MeV and is consistent with the experimental estimate from hard photon production in heavy ion collisions (Schutz et al., 1996) and the theoretical INM estimate (Satpathy, Uma Maheswari and Nayak, 1999). The approach enables a consistent microscopic description of both finite-nucleus reactions and bulk nuclear matter properties using the same effective interaction.
