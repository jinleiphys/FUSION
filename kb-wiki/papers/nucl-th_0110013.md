# Subthreshold phi-meson production in heavy-ion collisions

> Within a transport code of BUU type the production of phi-mesons in the reactions Ni+Ni at 1.93 AGeV and Ru+Ru at 1.69 AGeV is studied. New elementary reaction channels rho+N(Delta) to phi+N and pi+N(1520) to phi+N are included. In spite of a substantial increase of the ϕmultiplicities by these channels the results stay below the tentative numbers extracted from experimental data.

## Key claim

Within a BUU transport code, including new elementary reaction channels (ρN(Δ) → φN and πN(1520) → φN) substantially increases φ multiplicities, but the calculated yields still stay below the tentative numbers extracted from experimental data for Ni+Ni at 1.93 AGeV and Ru+Ru at 1.69 AGeV.

## Method

The authors used a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport code to simulate central heavy-ion collisions, implementing new elementary channels for φ production: ρN(Δ) → φN, πρ → φ, and πN(1520) → φN. Cross sections for these channels were estimated within a one-boson exchange model with effective interaction Lagrangians, using coupling constants and cutoff parameters from prior work (Machleidt, 1989; Chung et al., 1997). The φ meson multiplicities were calculated perturbatively, and the results were compared with experimental data from the FOPI collaboration.

## Key numbers

- Total φ multiplicity per central event for Ni+Ni at 1.93 AGeV: 1.7 × 10⁻³ (including all channels), compared to experimental value (8.7 ± 3.6) × 10⁻³ from Kotte et al.
- Total φ multiplicity per central event for Ru+Ru at 1.69 AGeV: 2.0 × 10⁻³, compared to experimental value (6.4 ± 2.5) × 10⁻³ from Kotte et al.
- Contribution from ρ+B channel: 8.9 × 10⁻⁴ (Ni+Ni) and 11.8 × 10⁻⁴ (Ru+Ru)
- Contribution from π+N(1520) channel: 0.5 × 10⁻⁴ (Ni+Ni) and 0.6 × 10⁻⁴ (Ru+Ru) using the standard Lagrangian (vec1520); when using the alternative Lagrangian (ten1520), the yield rises to 6.6 × 10⁻⁴ (Ni+Ni) and 7.7 × 10⁻⁴ (Ru+Ru)
- Maximum formation cross section for πρ → φ at resonance peak: 9.5 mb
- φ meson mass shift used in medium: mφ* = mφ − 0.025 mφ (n/n₀) from Hatsuda et al. (1993)
- Effective temperatures from thermal fit to experimental data: Teff = 59 MeV (Ni+Ni) and 72 MeV (Ru+Ru)
- Calculated slope parameters for Ru+Ru (Ni+Ni): T⊥ = 90 (100) MeV, T∥ = 113 (120) MeV

## Context

This work extends prior transport model calculations by Chung et al. (1997) and Hartnack et al., which only included BB → φBB and πB → φB channels and underestimated φ yields by more than a factor of ten compared to FOPI data (Herrmann, Kotte). The new ρB and πN(1520) channels increase yields by about a factor of 2.5, but the discrepancy with experiment persists. The results enable future measurements with the HADES detector via the φ → e⁺e⁻ decay channel, which could help resolve the "φ puzzle" and probe in-medium properties of mesons.
