# Neutron star cooling implications and magnetic field of the Vela Junior central compact object from all XMM-Newton and Chandra spectra

> The central compact object (CCO) in the Vela Junior supernova remnant is a young neutron star whose relatively low X-ray flux and small distance suggest it has a mass high enough to activate fast neutrino cooling processes. Here we analyse all XMM-Newton MOS and pn and Chandra ACIS-S spectra of the Vela Junior CCO, with observations taking place over the 9 years from 2001 to 2010. We find that the best-fit flux and spectral model parameters do not vary significantly when treating each observation independently, and therefore we fit all the spectra simultaneously using various spectral models to characterize the predominantly thermal emission from the neutron star surface. Our results indicate the Vela Junior CCO has an atmosphere composed of hydrogen, a hot spot temperature (unredshifted) of 3.5x10^6 K, and a colder surface temperature of (6.6-8.8)x10^5 K. Possible absorption lines at ~0.6 keV and 0.9 keV provide evidence for the first-time of an average surface magnetic field B~3x10^10 G for this CCO, which is similar to the magnetic field of other CCOs. At the accurate new Vela Junior distance of 1.4 kpc, the observed luminosity that is dominated by the hot spot is ~5x10^32 erg s^-1. The luminosity from the rest of the colder surface is (1.3-4.0)x10^32 erg s^-1. The cool luminosity and temperature imply the Vela Junior CCO is indeed colder than many other young neutron stars and probably has a high mass that triggered fast neutrino cooling.

## Key claim

The Vela Junior CCO has a high mass that likely triggers fast neutrino cooling (direct Urca processes), making it colder than other young neutron stars, but this conclusion depends on the newly adopted distance of 1.4 kpc and the interpretation that the observed thermal emission is dominated by a hydrogen atmosphere with a hot spot and cooler surface component.

## Method

The authors analyzed all XMM-Newton MOS and pn and Chandra ACIS-S spectra of the Vela Junior CCO from observations spanning 2001 to 2010, fitting each dataset individually and then simultaneously using Xspec 12.15.0. They applied single and multi-component spectral models including blackbodies, neutron star atmosphere models (nsx for non-magnetic hydrogen or carbon, nsmaxg for magnetized hydrogen), and power laws, with interstellar absorption modeled by tbabs and instrumental cross-normalization constants included.

## Key numbers

- Hot spot temperature (unredshifted): 3.5 x 10^6 K
- Colder surface temperature (unredshifted): (6.6-8.8) x 10^5 K
- Average surface magnetic field B ~ 3 x 10^10 G, inferred from possible absorption lines at ~0.6 keV and 0.9 keV
- Distance to Vela Junior: 1.4 kpc
- Hot spot luminosity: ~5 x 10^32 erg s^-1
- Colder surface luminosity: (1.3-4.0) x 10^32 erg s^-1
- Best-fit hydrogen column density NH = (4.27 +/- 0.07) x 10^21 cm^-2 for single blackbody fit to all data
- Blackbody temperature kT_infinity = 396 +/- 1 eV for single blackbody fit to all data
- Emission radius R_infinity/d = 275 +/- 3 m/kpc for single blackbody fit to all data
- Reduced chi-squared for BB+BB model: chi^2/dof = 508/470 = 1.08
- Reduced chi-squared for BB+PL model: chi^2/dof = 502/470 = 1.07

## Context

This work extends previous spectral analyses of the Vela Junior CCO by Kargaltsev et al. (2002), Becker et al. (2006), Potekhin et al. (2020), Wu et al. (2021), Alford and Halpern (2023), and Marino et al. (2024) by simultaneously fitting all available XMM-Newton and Chandra data for the first time. The results support the interpretation that the Vela Junior CCO is undergoing fast neutrino cooling via direct Urca processes, similar to the neutron star in Cassiopeia A studied by Page et al. (2011) and Shternin et al. (2011), and provide the first magnetic field estimate for this CCO from absorption features, placing it in the same low-field regime (B ~ 3 x 10^10 G) as other CCOs like PSR J0821-4300 and PSR J1852+0040.
