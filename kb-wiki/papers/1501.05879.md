# Top-quark production in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions at LHC energies and beyond

> Single and pair top-quark production in proton-lead (p-Pb) and lead-lead (Pb-Pb) collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC) and future circular collider (FCC) energies, are studied with next-to-leading-order perturbative QCD calculations including nuclear parton distribution functions. At the LHC, the pair-production cross sections amount to sigma(t-tbar) = 3.4 mub in Pb-Pb at sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV, and sigma(t-tbar) = 60 nb in p-Pb at sqrt(s) = 8.8 TeV. At the FCC energies of sqrt(s) = 39 and 63 TeV, the same cross sections are factors of 90 and 55 times larger respectively. In the leptonic final-state t-tbar --> W+b W-bbar --> b bbar l+l- nu+nu-, after typical acceptance and efficiency cuts, one expects about 90 and 300 top-quarks per nominal LHC-year and 4.7 10^4 and 10^5 per FCC-year in Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions respectively. The total t-tbar cross sections, dominated by gluon fusion processes, are enhanced by 3--8% in nuclear compared to p-p collisions due to an overall net gluon antishadowing, although different regions of their differential distributions are depleted due to shadowing or EMC-effect corrections. The rapidity distributions of the decay leptons in t-tbar processes can be used to reduce the uncertainty on the Pb gluon density at high virtualities by up to 30% at the LHC (full heavy-ion programme), and by 70% per FCC-year. The cross sections for single-top production in electroweak processes are also computed, yielding about a factor of 30 smaller number of measurable top-quarks after cuts, per system and per year.

## Key claim

Top-quark pair production in proton-lead and lead-lead collisions at LHC and FCC energies is dominated by gluon fusion and shows a net 3-8% enhancement relative to scaled proton-proton collisions due to gluon antishadowing, though differential distributions can be depleted by shadowing or EMC-effect corrections in specific kinematic regions.

## Method

The authors performed next-to-leading-order perturbative QCD calculations using the MCFM code (version 6.7) with CT10 proton PDFs and EPS09 nuclear PDFs for lead, computing cross sections for top-pair production (process 141) and single-top production via t-channel, s-channel, and associated tW processes. Nucleon-nucleon cross sections were scaled by the lead mass number A=208 for p-Pb and by A²=43264 for Pb-Pb collisions, with acceptance and efficiency cuts applied to estimate observable yields in fully-leptonic final states.

## Key numbers

- Top-pair production cross section in Pb-Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV: sigma(t-tbar) = 3.40 ± 0.42 ± 0.37 μb
- Top-pair production cross section in p-Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 8.8 TeV: sigma(t-tbar) = 58.8 ± 7.1 ± 3.8 nb
- At FCC energies (sqrt(s_NN) = 39 TeV for Pb-Pb, 63 TeV for p-Pb), top-pair cross sections are factors of 90 and 55 times larger respectively than at LHC
- Expected observable top-quarks per nominal LHC-year after cuts: about 90 in Pb-Pb and 300 in p-Pb
- Expected observable top-quarks per FCC-year after cuts: 4.7 × 10⁴ in Pb-Pb and 10⁵ in p-Pb
- Rapidity distributions of decay leptons from t-tbar can reduce uncertainty on Pb gluon density by up to 30% at LHC (full heavy-ion programme) and by 70% per FCC-year
- Single-top production yields about a factor of 30 fewer measurable top-quarks after cuts per system and per year compared to pair production

## Context

This work extends previous studies of electroweak boson production in heavy-ion collisions (W and Z bosons observed by CMS and ATLAS at LHC) to the heaviest Standard Model particle, the top quark. The calculations use the EPS09 nuclear PDF set, which is consistent with dijet measurements in p-Pb collisions at LHC (Chatrchyan 2014, Paukkunen 2014). The results enable future experimental searches for top quarks in nuclear collisions at LHC and FCC, and provide a pathway to constrain nuclear gluon densities at high virtualities (Q² ≈ 3 × 10⁴ GeV²) and Bjorken-x values around 5 × 10⁻³ to 0.05, a region characterized by net gluon antishadowing.
