# Gauge Dependence of ccbar Potential from Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter Wave Function in Lattice QCD

> We study the gauge dependence of $c\bar{c}$ potentials extracted from Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions. The potentials are obtained using the HAL QCD potential method, with an extension introduced by Kawanai and Sasaki for self-consistent determination of the charm quark mass. A systematic comparison is conducted between results obtained in the Coulomb and Landau gauges. The numerical calculations are performed using 2+1 flavor QCD gauge configurations with the charm quark treated in the quenched approximation. We find that the central potentials in both gauges show excellent agreement at short distances but exhibit discrepancies at large distances. We attribute these discrepancies to insufficient suppression of excited-state contamination in the Landau gauge, which affects the linear-rising behavior of the potential at large distance.

## Key claim

The central \(c\bar{c}\) potentials extracted from Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions in Coulomb and Landau gauges agree at short distances but disagree at large distances; the authors attribute the discrepancy not to a genuine gauge dependence of the potential, but to insufficient suppression of excited-state contamination in the Landau-gauge four-point correlators.

## Method

The authors applied the HAL QCD potential method extended by Kawanai and Sasaki to compute \(c\bar{c}\) potentials from equal-time Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions on 2+1 flavor QCD gauge configurations with a quenched charm quark treated via the relativistic heavy quark action. They fixed gauge configurations to both Coulomb and Landau gauges, extracted ground-state NBS wave functions from four-point correlators at \(t/a = 29\), and determined the charm quark mass self-consistently using the Kawanai-Sasaki condition that the spin-dependent potential vanishes at large distance. The central and spin-dependent potentials were then obtained by solving the stationary Schrödinger equation for the pseudoscalar and vector channels.

## Key numbers

* Charm quark mass in Coulomb gauge: \(m_c = 1932(8)\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* Charm quark mass in Landau gauge: \(m_c = 1522(5)\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* Pseudoscalar charmonium mass: \(M_{\mathrm{PS}} = 2.9729(16)\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
* Vector charmonium mass: \(M_{\mathrm{V}} = 3.0745(19)\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
* Lattice spacing: \(a = 0.0907(14)\ \mathrm{fm}\) (or \(a^{-1} = 2.17(53)\ \mathrm{GeV}\)).
* Lattice spatial size: \(La = 2.902(42)\ \mathrm{fm}\).
* Pion mass: \(m_\pi \simeq 155.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* Central potentials agree for \(r \lesssim 0.5\ \mathrm{fm}\); deviation becomes significant beyond \(r \sim 0.6\ \mathrm{fm}\).
* Spin-dependent potentials are spatially localized within \(r \lesssim 0.4\ \mathrm{fm}\).

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD potential method (Ishii et al., 2007; Aoki et al., 2010) and the Kawanai-Sasaki self-consistent charm quark mass prescription (Kawanai and Sasaki, 2011) to a systematic comparison of Coulomb and Landau gauges. Previous studies of \(c\bar{c}\) potentials from NBS wave functions predominantly used the Coulomb gauge (Kawanai and Sasaki, 2011; Ikeda et al., 2011; Iida et al., 2012), with one unpublished Landau-gauge study by Hideaki Iida suggesting possible long-distance differences. The results enable future work on diquark mass determination within quark-diquark systems, using the \(c\bar{c}\) system as a simplified model, and highlight the need for improved ground-state isolation in Landau-gauge analyses.
