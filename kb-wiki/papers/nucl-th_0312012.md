# Nuclear Equation of State and Neutron Star Structure

> The hadronic equation of state for a neutron star is discussed with a particular emphasis on the symmetry energy. The results of several microscopic approaches are compared and also a new calculation in terms of the self-consistent Green function method is presented. In addition possible constraints on the symmetry energy coming from empirical information from the neutron skin of finite nuclei are considered.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the hadronic equation of state for neutron stars with emphasis on the symmetry energy, presenting a new self-consistent Green function calculation that reduces the symmetry energy at saturation density compared to Brueckner-Hartree-Fock results, but notes that the spread among theoretical predictions remains large and that constraints from neutron skin measurements in finite nuclei may help narrow the uncertainty.

## Method

The authors compare several microscopic many-body approaches for nuclear matter: Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) with the continuous choice, self-consistent Green function (SCGF) theory, variational chain summation, Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock, relativistic mean-field models, and effective field theory. They present new SCGF calculations for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using the Reid93 nucleon-nucleon potential, where the SCGF method treats particles and holes on equal footing and generates realistic spectral functions, in contrast to the BHF approximation which restricts intermediate states to particle states only.

## Key numbers

* The symmetry energy at saturation density from SCGF with Reid93 potential is a4 = 24.9 MeV, compared to 28.9 MeV from continuous-choice BHF with the same interaction.
* The slope parameter p0 from SCGF is 2.0 MeV fm^-3, nearly unchanged from the BHF value of 1.9 MeV fm^-3.
* Inclusion of three-nucleon forces in BHF increases a4 from 28.9 to 30 MeV and p0 from 1.9 to 2.9 MeV fm^-3.
* Variational calculations with Av18+UIX three-nucleon interaction give a4 = 30 MeV and p0 = 3.1 MeV fm^-3.
* Relativistic mean-field models predict a4 in the range 30-36 MeV and p0 in the range 4-6 MeV fm^-3.
* The proton fraction at saturation density in beta equilibrium is x0 approximately 0.04.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of symmetry energy calculations across multiple many-body methods, following earlier BHF studies by Engvik et al. and variational calculations by Wiringa et al. and Akmal et al. The paper also builds on the correlation between the symmetry energy parameters and neutron skin thickness in heavy nuclei noted by Brown and Furnstahl, and shows that this correlation can be understood within the Landau-Migdal approach. The results enable future experimental constraints on the symmetry energy from neutron skin measurements in nuclei like 208Pb, which could help distinguish between the softer non-relativistic predictions and the stiffer relativistic mean-field predictions.
