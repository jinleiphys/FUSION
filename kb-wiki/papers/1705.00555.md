# Low density nuclear matter with light clusters in a generalized nonlinear relativistic mean-field model

> We systematically investigate the thermodynamic properties of homogeneous nuclear matter with light clusters at low densities and finite temperatures using a generalized nonlinear relativistic mean-field (gNL-RMF) model, in which light clusters up to $α$ ($1 \le A \le 4$) are included as explicit degrees of freedom and treated as point-like particles with their interactions described by meson exchanges and the medium effects on the cluster binding energies are described by density- and temperature-dependent energy shifts with the parameters obtained by fitting the experimental cluster Mott densities. We find that the composition of low density nuclear matter with light clusters is essentially determined by the density- and temperature-dependence of the cluster binding energy shifts. Compared with the values of the conventional (second-order) symmetry energy, symmetry free energy and symmetry entropy, their fourth-order values are found to be significant at low densities ($ρ\sim 10^{-3}$ fm$^{-3}$) and low temperatures ($T \lesssim 3$ MeV), indicating the invalidity of the empirical parabolic law for the isospin asymmetry dependence of these nuclear matter properties. Our results indicate that in the density region of $ρ\gtrsim 0.02$ fm$^{-3}$, the clustering effects become insignificant and the nuclear matter is dominated by nucleon degree of freedom. In addition, we compare the gNL-RMF model predictions with the corresponding experimental data on the symmetry energy and symmetry free energy at low densities and finite temperatures extracted from heavy-ion collisions, and reasonable agreement is found.

## Key claim

The composition of low-density nuclear matter with light clusters is essentially determined by the density- and temperature-dependence of the cluster binding energy shifts, and the fourth-order symmetry energy, symmetry free energy, and symmetry entropy become significant at low densities (around 10⁻³ fm⁻³) and low temperatures (T ≲ 3 MeV), indicating that the empirical parabolic law for isospin asymmetry dependence is invalid under those conditions.

## Method

The authors extended the nonlinear relativistic mean-field (NL-RMF) model to include light clusters (deuteron, triton, helium-3, and alpha) as explicit point-like degrees of freedom interacting via meson exchanges, with medium effects on cluster binding energies described by density- and temperature-dependent energy shifts parameterized to fit experimental Mott densities. They solved the coupled equations of motion for the meson fields and the Fermi-Dirac (for fermions) and Bose-Einstein (for bosons) distribution functions to obtain thermodynamic quantities, then expanded the internal energy, free energy, and entropy per baryon in powers of isospin asymmetry δ up to fourth order to extract symmetry coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The Mott density for alpha particles increases with temperature; at T ≈ 10 MeV, the revised parameter a_α,1 = 137330 MeV^(5/2) fm³ reproduces experimental data, compared to the original value of 164371 MeV^(5/2) fm³.
- The Mott density for deuterons is reproduced with revised parameter a_d,1 = 76500 MeV^(5/2) fm³, compared to the original value of 38386.4 MeV^(5/2) fm³.
- In symmetric nuclear matter at T = 3 MeV and density n ≈ 0.001 fm⁻³, the alpha-particle fraction exceeds the nucleon fraction, forming "alpha-matter".
- Clustering effects become insignificant at densities n ≳ 0.02 fm⁻³, where nuclear matter is dominated by nucleons.
- The fourth-order symmetry energy, symmetry free energy, and symmetry entropy are significant at densities n ∼ 10⁻³ fm⁻³ and temperatures T ≲ 3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the generalized density-dependent RMF model of Typel et al. (2010) by using a nonlinear RMF framework with constant meson couplings, which is simpler and more convenient for calculations. The results are compared with experimental symmetry energy and symmetry free energy data extracted from heavy-ion collisions by Natowitz et al. (2010), Kowalski et al. (2007), and Wada et al. (2012), and with experimental Mott densities from Hagel et al. (2012). The findings enable improved modeling of the equation of state for core-collapse supernovae and neutron star crusts, where clustering effects are important at low densities and finite temperatures.
