# Rosenbluth separation of $ \boldsymbol {dσ_{L}/dt}$ and $\boldsymbol {dσ_{T}/dt}$ in $\boldsymbol π^{\bf 0}$ deeply virtual electroproduction from the proton

> We report on a Rosenbluth separation using previously published data by the CLAS collaboration in Hall B, Jefferson Lab for exclusive $π^{0}$ deeply virtual electroproduction (DVEP) from the proton at a mean $Q^{2}$ of $\approx$ 2 (GeV/c)$^{2}$. The central question we address is the applicability of factorization in $π^0$ DVEP at these kinematics. The results of our Rosenbluth separation clearly demonstrate the dominance of the longitudinal contribution to the cross section. The extracted longitudinal and transverse contributions are in agreement with previous data from Hall A at Jefferson Lab, but over a much wider $-t$ range (0.12 - 1.8 (GeV/c)$^{2}$). The measured dominance of the longitudinal contribution at $Q^{2} \approx$ 2 (GeV/c)$^{2}$ is consistent with the expectation of the handbag factorization theorem. We find that $σ_L(t) \sim 1/(-t)$ for $-t >$ 0.5 (GeV/c)$^2$. Determination of both longitudinal and transverse contributions to the deeply virtual $π^{0}$ electroproduction cross section allows extraction of additional GPDs.

## Key claim

The authors perform a Rosenbluth separation on previously published CLAS data for exclusive π⁰ deeply virtual electroproduction from the proton at a mean Q² ≈ 2 (GeV/c)², and find that the longitudinal contribution to the cross section dominates over a broad -t range (0.12 – 1.8 (GeV/c)²), consistent with the handbag factorization theorem. The result is robust to inclusion of additional systematic uncertainties, but the limited Q² range of the CLAS data does not allow a precise investigation of the Q² dependence.

## Method

The authors analyzed published CLAS data from Hall B at Jefferson Lab for exclusive π⁰ electroproduction using a 5.75 GeV electron beam and a liquid hydrogen target. They performed a Rosenbluth separation by plotting the combined cross section dσ_T/dt + ε dσ_L/dt as a function of the virtual photon polarization parameter ε for each -t bin, extracting the longitudinal and transverse contributions from linear fits. The data were taken at average Q² ≈ 2 (GeV/c)² and average x_B ≈ 0.3, and the authors applied additional systematic uncertainty studies including normalization variations and constraints on the transverse cross section.

## Key numbers

- Mean Q² of the data set: ≈ 2 (GeV/c)²
- Average x_B: ≈ 0.3
- -t range covered: 0.12 – 1.8 (GeV/c)²
- For -t = 0.35 (GeV/c)²: dσ_L/dt = 503 ± 99 nb/(GeV/c)², dσ_T/dt = 28 ± 46 nb/(GeV/c)²
- For -t = 0.49 (GeV/c)²: dσ_L/dt = 542 ± 79 nb/(GeV/c)², dσ_T/dt = -53 ± 37 nb/(GeV/c)²
- For -t = 1.22 (GeV/c)²: dσ_L/dt = 350 ± 76 nb/(GeV/c)², dσ_T/dt = -113 ± 38 nb/(GeV/c)²
- For -t = 1.72 (GeV/c)²: dσ_L/dt = 124 ± 44 nb/(GeV/c)², dσ_T/dt = -36 ± 22 nb/(GeV/c)²
- σ_L(t) ∼ 1/(-t) for -t > 0.5 (GeV/c)²

## Context

This work extends previous Rosenbluth separation results from Hall A at Jefferson Lab (Hall A, 2007) to a much wider -t range, and is consistent with those earlier measurements at low -t. The dominance of the longitudinal cross section supports the handbag factorization theorem (Strikman, 2003) and contrasts with charged pion π⁺ electroproduction where the pion pole complicates interpretation (piPlus, 2007). The results motivate future precision measurements, including the approved Hall C experiment E12-13-010 using the Neutral Particle Spectrometer (Horn, 2019) and the CLAS12 experiment E12-06-108, as well as future studies at the Electron Ion Collider.
