# Baryon number fluctuations induced by hadronic interactions at low temperature and large chemical potential

> We investigate the density fluctuations induced by hadronic interactions at low temperatures and large chemical potentials after the hadronization of quark-gluon plasma~(QGP). We analyze the structures of net-baryon number kurtosis and skewness and elaborate on their relations with the nuclear liquid-gas~(LG) phase transition and hadronic interactions above the critical temperature. Combining with the relevant experimental projects at RHIC/NICA/FAIR/J-PARC/HIAF with collision energies from $200\,$GeV to $1.8\,$GeV, we propose a double-peak structure of kurtosis (skewness) as a function of collision energy, which can be taken to identify the chiral phase transition and the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition. In particular, the fluctuation distributions at low temperatures provide a new method to explore hadronic interactions and nuclear liquid-gas transition.

# Baryon number fluctuations induced by hadronic interactions at low temperature and large chemical potential

## Key claim
The authors propose that a double-peak structure in the energy dependence of net-baryon number kurtosis and skewness can serve as a combined signature of both the chiral phase transition and the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition, but only if the chemical freeze-out line passes through the critical regions of both transitions. The low-temperature peak arises from hadronic interactions even if the nuclear liquid-gas transition is not directly triggered.

## Method
The authors use the nonlinear Walecka model (quantum hadron dynamics) in the mean-field approximation to describe hadronic matter after QGP hadronization at low temperatures and large chemical potentials. They compute the net-baryon number kurtosis (kappa sigma^2) and skewness (S sigma) from generalized susceptibilities derived from the thermodynamic potential, and map these observables across the T-mu_B phase diagram. The nuclear liquid-gas phase transition line and spinodal region are determined from the equation of state using mechanical instability and Gibbs conditions.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Compression modulus: K = 240 MeV
- Symmetry energy: a_sym = 31.3 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation: m*_N = 0.75 m_N
- Binding energy: B/A = -16.0 MeV at saturation density
- Collision energy range considered: 200 GeV down to 1.8 GeV
- Chemical freeze-out at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.32 GeV: mu_B ~ 820 MeV, T ~ 50 MeV (from SIS data)
- Kurtosis diverges at the critical end point of the nuclear liquid-gas transition
- Kurtosis is negative in the blue region of the T-mu_B phase diagram and positive elsewhere

## Context
This work extends the study of net-proton fluctuation measurements from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan (STAR Collaboration, 2014) to lower collision energies relevant to NICA, FAIR, J-PARC, and HIAF. The authors compare their hadronic fluctuation results with chiral transition calculations from the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Shao, 2018) and with chemical freeze-out conditions extracted from STAR data (Adamczyk, 2017) and SIS data (Cleymans, 1999; Becattini, 2001; Averbeck, 2003). The nuclear liquid-gas transition treatment parallels work using a modified hadron resonance gas model with van der Waals interactions (Vovchenko, 2017). The results suggest that lower collision energies than currently planned may be needed to observe clear signals of the nuclear liquid-gas transition via fluctuation measurements.
