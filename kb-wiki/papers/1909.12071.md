# Implications of the fermion vacuum term in the extended SU(3) Quark Meson model on compact stars properties

> We study the impact of the fermion vacuum term in the SU(3) quark meson model on the equation of state and determine the vacuum parameters for various sigma meson masses. We examine its influence on the equation of state and on the resulting mass radius relations for compact stars. The tidal deformability $Λ$ of the stars is studied and compared to the results of the mean field approximation. Parameter sets which fulfill the tidal deformability bounds of GW170817 together with the observed two solar mass limit turn out to be restricted to a quite small parameter range in the mean field approximation. The extended version of the model does not yield solutions fulfilling both constraints. Furthermore, no first order chiral phase transition is found in the extended version of the model, not allowing for the twin star solutions found in the mean field approximation.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the fermion vacuum term in the SU(3) quark meson model (extended mean field approximation, eMFA) produces equations of state that are too soft to simultaneously satisfy the two-solar-mass limit and the tidal deformability bound from GW170817, whereas the standard mean field approximation (MFA) can satisfy both constraints, but only in a narrow parameter range.

## Method

The authors compute the grand canonical potential of the SU(3) quark meson model including the fermion vacuum term, regularized via dimensional regularization with a renormalization scale parameter Lambda_r = 200 MeV. They determine the renormalized vacuum parameters (lambda_1, lambda_2, m_0^2, c, h_n, h_s) for sigma meson masses m_sigma from 400 to 900 MeV, then solve the gap equations to obtain equations of state under charge neutrality with leptons. These EoSs are used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and the tidal deformability differential equation to produce mass-radius relations and Lambda values for compact stars.

## Key numbers

- The pion mass input is m_pi = 136 MeV, the kaon mass m_k = 496 MeV, the pion decay constant f_pi = 92.4 MeV, and the kaon decay constant f_k = 113 MeV.
- The sigma meson mass is varied over the range 400 <= m_sigma <= 800 MeV.
- The tidal deformability constraint from GW170817 for a 1.4 solar mass star is Lambda = 300 (+420, -230).
- The radius of a 1.4 solar mass star is constrained to be R <= 13.5 km.
- The mass constraint is M >= 2 solar masses.
- The renormalization scale parameter is set to Lambda_r = 200 MeV.
- The constituent quark mass is held fixed at m_q = 300 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the SU(3) quark meson model studies by Parganlija (2012) and Zacchi (2015, 2016) by including the fermion vacuum term, following the regularization procedure of Mocsy (2004), Fraga (2009), Skokov (2010), Gupta (2011), Chatterjee (2011), and Tiwari (2013). The results contradict the possibility of twin star solutions (two stable branches in the mass-radius relation) that were found in the MFA by Zacchi (2016). The paper enables future work to explore whether additional quark interactions could lower the tidal deformability Lambda at a given radius, as suggested in the abstract.
