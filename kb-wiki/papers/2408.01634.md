# Jet-Induced Enhancement of Deuteron Production in $pp$ and $p$-Pb Collisions at the LHC

> Jet-associated deuteron production in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV and $p$-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV is studied in the coalescence model by using the phase-space information of proton and neutron pairs from a multiphase transport (AMPT) model at the kinetic freezeout. In the low transverse momentum ($p_T$) region $p_T/A < 1.5$ GeV/$c$, where $A$ is the mass number of a nucleus, the in-jet coalescence factor $B_2^\text{In-jet}$ for deuteron production, given by the ratio of the in-jet deuteron number to the square of the in-jet proton number, is found to be larger than the coalescence factor $B_2$ in the medium perpendicular to the jet by a factor of about 10 in $pp$ collisions and of 25 in $p-$Pb collisions, which are consistent with the ALICE measurements at the LHC. Such large low-momentum enhancements mainly come from coalescence of nucleons inside the jet with the medium nucleons. Coalescence of nucleons inside the jet dominates deuteron production only at the higher $p_T$ region of $p_T/A\gtrsim 4$ GeV/$c$, where both the yield ratio $d/p$ of deuteron to proton numbers and the $B_2$ are also significantly larger in the jet direction than in the direction perpendicular to the jet due to the strong collinear correlation among particles produced from jet fragmentation. Studying jet-associated deuteron production in relativistic nuclear collisions thus opens up a new window to probe the phase-space structure of nucleons inside jets.

## Key claim

The in-jet coalescence factor \(B_2^\text{In-jet}\) for deuteron production is enhanced by a factor of about 10 in \(pp\) collisions and about 25 in \(p\)-Pb collisions relative to the coalescence factor in the medium perpendicular to the jet, but this low-momentum enhancement (\(p_T/A < 1.5\) GeV/\(c\)) arises primarily from coalescence of nucleons inside the jet with medium nucleons, not from coalescence of nucleons both inside the jet.

## Method

The authors used the multiphase transport (AMPT) model to simulate the evolution of \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=13\) TeV and \(p\)-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV, obtaining the phase-space distribution of kinetically freeze-out nucleons. Deuteron production was then calculated using the nucleon coalescence model, where the formation probability of a deuteron from a proton-neutron pair is given by the Wigner function of the deuteron internal wave function with a size parameter \(\sigma_d \approx 2.26\) fm. Events were selected with a leading particle of \(p_T > 5\) GeV/\(c\) in pseudorapidity \(|\eta| < 0.9\), and the transverse plane was divided into Toward (\(|\Delta\phi| < 60^\circ\)), Transverse (\(60^\circ < |\Delta\phi| < 120^\circ\)), and Away (\(|\Delta\phi| > 120^\circ\)) regions relative to the leading particle direction.

## Key numbers

- In-jet coalescence factor \(B_2^\text{In-jet}\) is larger than the coalescence factor \(B_2\) in the Transverse region by a factor of about 10 in \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=13\) TeV and about 25 in \(p\)-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV, for \(p_T/A < 1.5\) GeV/\(c\).
- Jet-jet coalescence dominates deuteron production only at \(p_T/A \gtrsim 4\) GeV/\(c\).
- The deuteron size parameter in the Wigner function is \(\sigma_d \approx 2.26\) fm, related to the deuteron root-mean-squared radius \(r_d\).
- The fraction of protons from the jet at low \(p_T\) (\(p_T/A \le 1.5\) GeV/\(c\)) is only about 10%.

## Context

This work extends the ALICE measurements of jet-associated deuterons in \(pp\) collisions (ALICE, 2020; ALICE, 2022) by providing a theoretical explanation using the AMPT model and nucleon coalescence. It distinguishes between jet-medium and jet-jet coalescence contributions, showing that the large low-momentum enhancement is not a genuine jet correlation effect but arises from the large number of medium nucleons. The study also extends predictions to \(p\)-Pb collisions and to higher \(p_T\) regions (\(p_T/A > 2\) GeV/\(c\)) not yet measured, and it links the enhanced deuteron production at high \(p_T\) to the collinear correlation of nucleons inside jets, suggesting that nucleosynthesis in jets can probe the phase-space structure of nucleons inside jets and the medium response to jet quenching in quark-gluon plasma.
