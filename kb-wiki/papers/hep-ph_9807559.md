# Quantum Groups as Flavor Symmetries: Account of Nonpolynomial SU(3)-Breaking Effects in Baryon Masses

> The use, for flavor symmetries, of the quantum (or q-) analogs of unitary groups SU(n_f) yields new, very accurate, baryon mass sum rules. We show, in the 3-flavor case, that such approach accounts for nonpolynomial SU(3)-breaking effects in the octet and decuplet baryon masses. A version of this approach with manifestly q-covariant mass operator is given. The obtained new version of the q-deformed mass relation is simpler than those derived before, but, for its empirical validity, the parameter q is to be fixed by fitting. As shown, the well-known Gell-Mann--Okubo octet mass sum rule results, besides usual SU(3), also from an exotic "symmetry" encoded in the singular case q=-1 of the q-algebra U_q(su_3).

# Quantum Groups as Flavor Symmetries: Account of Nonpolynomial SU(3)-Breaking Effects in Baryon Masses

## Key claim

The use of quantum (q-deformed) analogs of SU(3) as flavor symmetry can account for nonpolynomial SU(3)-breaking effects in octet and decuplet baryon masses, yielding mass sum rules with substantially better empirical accuracy than the standard Gell-Mann-Okubo formula. However, the new q-deformed mass relation (29) requires the parameter q to be fixed by fitting to data, unlike earlier versions where q could be determined from zeros of a polynomial.

## Method

The authors adopt the quantum algebra U_q(su_3) (broken to U_q(su_2) for isospin) as the flavor symmetry, with baryon octet states corresponding to basis vectors of the irreducible representation {p+2,p+1,p} of U_q(su_3). Masses are computed as diagonal matrix elements of a mass operator composed from operators representing basis elements of U_q(su_5), using the Gel'fand-Tsetlin formalism to evaluate matrix elements that depend on hypercharge Y and isospin I through q-numbers. An alternative approach uses q-tensor operators transforming as the 3 and 3* representations of U_q(su_3) under the adjoint action, constructing the mass operator from the operators V_3 V_{\bar{3}} and V_{\bar{3}} V_3.

## Key numbers

- The Gell-Mann-Okubo octet sum rule holds with 0.58% accuracy
- A q-deformed sum rule with q = exp(i*pi/6) shows precision of 0.22%
- A q-deformed sum rule with q = exp(i*pi/7) shows precision of 0.07%, described as the best possible
- For the decuplet, the q-average formula (18) is successful with q = exp(i*theta_10) where theta_10 is approximately pi/14
- The new q-deformed mass relation (29) yields q values of +/-1.035 or +/-0.903*sqrt(-1) when fitted to empirical baryon masses

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to apply q-deformed algebras to hadron phenomenology (Gavrilik and collaborators, mid-1990s) and the Gell-Mann-Okubo mass formula (Gell-Mann 1962, Okubo 1962) for octet baryons, as well as the equal spacing rule and decuplet formula (Okubo 1962). The approach improves upon the standard SU(3) treatment by incorporating higher-order SU(3)-breaking effects without introducing additional free parameters beyond the deformation parameter q. The results enable further investigation of the connection between the Cabibbo angle and the deformation parameter, and suggest that the singular case q = -1 of U_q(su_3) yields the classical GMO sum rule through an exotic operator algebra.
