# Effect of intense magnetic fields on reduced-MHD evolution in $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions

> We investigate the effect of large magnetic fields on the $2+1$ dimensional reduced-magnetohydrodynamical expansion of hot and dense nuclear matter produced in $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions. For the sake of simplicity, we consider the case where the magnetic field points in the direction perpendicular to the reaction plane. We also consider this field to be external, with energy density parametrized as a two-dimensional Gaussian. The width of the Gaussian along the directions orthogonal to the beam axis varies with the centrality of the collision. The dependence of the magnetic field on proper time ($τ$) for the case of zero electrical conductivity of the QGP is parametrized following [Deng 2012], and for finite electrical conductivity following [Tuchin 2013]. We solve the equations of motion of ideal hydrodynamics for such an external magnetic field. For collisions with non-zero impact parameter we observe considerable changes in the evolution of the momentum eccentricities of the fireball when comparing the case when the magnetic field decays in a conducting QGP medium and when no magnetic field is present. The elliptic-flow coefficient $v_2$ of $π^{-}$ is shown to increase in the presence of an external magnetic field and the increment in $v_2$ is found to depend on the evolution and the initial magnitude of the magnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how an external magnetic field affects the 2+1 dimensional hydrodynamic expansion of quark-gluon plasma produced in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, but only under the simplified assumption that the magnetic field points perpendicular to the reaction plane and is prescribed externally rather than solved self-consistently with Maxwell's equations. The authors find that the elliptic-flow coefficient v2 of π− increases in the presence of the magnetic field, but this increment depends on both the evolution and the initial magnitude of the field.

## Method

The authors solve the ideal hydrodynamic equations of energy-momentum conservation for a perfect fluid with zero magnetization, using a prescribed external magnetic field whose spatial profile is a two-dimensional Gaussian and whose time decay follows parametrizations from Deng (2012) for zero electrical conductivity and Tuchin (2013) for finite electrical conductivity of the QGP. The 2+1 dimensional reduced-MHD evolution is performed in Milne coordinates assuming Bjorken scaling in the longitudinal direction, using a modified version of the AZHYDRO code with the SHASTA flux-correcting algorithm, and employing the s95p-PCE165-v0 lattice-QCD-based equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Initial magnetic field magnitude: eB ∼ 10^18 – 10^19 G for √sNN = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions (from the introduction)
- Initial hydrodynamical evolution time: τ0 = 0.6 fm
- Impact parameter values considered: b = 0 fm and b = 10 fm for magnetic field evolution plots
- Fraction of hard scattering used in initial energy density: xh = 0.25 for all cases
- Magnetic susceptibility of QGP: χ ≲ 0.05 for eB ∼ 0.2 GeV² (from the text, used to justify neglecting magnetization)

## Context

This work extends earlier analytical studies of ideal-MHD solutions in simplified geometries by Roy (2015) and Pu (2016), and complements the full ideal-MHD simulations of Inghirami (2016) by using a realistic equation of state and varying the initial magnetic field strength, whereas Inghirami (2016) used a simpler ultrarelativistic equation of state and a comparatively small initial magnetic field. The results contrast with those of Pang (2016), who found that elliptic flow is reduced in the presence of a magnetic field when considering a temperature-dependent magnetic susceptibility, and with Voronyuk (2011), who found only very small impact on flow harmonics within the Parton Hadron String model. The study enables future work toward self-consistent MHD solutions that solve Maxwell's equations simultaneously with the hydrodynamic evolution.
