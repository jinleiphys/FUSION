# Effect of two-boson exchange on parity-violating e-p scattering

> We compute the corrections from two-photon and photon-Z exchange in parity-violating elastic electron-proton scattering, used to extract the strange form factors of the proton. We use a hadronic formalism that successfully reconciled the earlier discrepancy in the proton's electron to magnetic form factor ratio, suitably extended to the weak sector. Implementing realistic electroweak form factors, we find effects of the order 2-3% at Q^2 < 0.1 GeV^2, which are largest at backward angles, and have a strong Q^2 dependence at low Q^2. Two-boson contributions to the weak axial current are found to be enhanced at low Q^2 and for forward angles. We provide corrections at kinematics relevant for recent and upcoming parity-violating experiments.

## Key claim
Two-boson exchange corrections to parity-violating electron-proton scattering asymmetries are of order 2-3% at momentum transfers below 0.1 GeV², but the paper shows that approximating these corrections by their Q²=0 values is insufficient for actual experiments because the corrections have a strong Q² dependence at low Q² and are sensitive to the choice of hadronic form factors used in the loop integrals.

## Method
The authors computed two-photon and photon-Z exchange corrections to the parity-violating asymmetry in elastic electron-proton scattering using a hadronic formalism previously developed for two-photon exchange corrections to electromagnetic form factors. They implemented realistic empirical electromagnetic form factors for the proton and neutron, determined self-consistently from a global analysis including explicit two-photon exchange corrections, and calculated the separate contributions from Z(γγ) and γ(Zγ) exchange amplitudes to the radiative correction parameters Δρ and Δκ. The corrections were evaluated at kinematics relevant to several past and upcoming parity-violating experiments.

## Key numbers
- Total Δρ correction is approximately 1-2% over most of the ε range, increasing at small ε and small Q²
- Δκ corrections range from ~0.2-0.3% at Q² = 0.01 GeV² to less than 0.1% at Q² = 1 GeV²
- For SAMPLE experiment kinematics (Q²=0.1 GeV², θ=144°): (Δρ, Δκ) = (1.950 × 10⁻², 7.998 × 10⁻⁴)
- For G0 backward angle kinematics (Q²=0.23 GeV², θ=110°): (Δρ, Δκ) = (1.473 × 10⁻², -1.342 × 10⁻⁴)
- For G0 backward angle kinematics (Q²=0.62 GeV², θ=110°): (Δρ, Δκ) = (1.261 × 10⁻², 1.103 × 10⁻⁴)
- For Qweak kinematics (Q²=0.03 GeV², θ=8°): (Δρ, Δκ) = (3.755 × 10⁻³, -2.655 × 10⁻⁴)
- TBE correction δ to the proton PV asymmetry is ≤ 0.5% for most forward angle experiments, increasing to ~1.5% at backward angles
- Using monopole form factors gives δ values ~50% larger than with empirical form factors
- Correction δ_A to the axial PV asymmetry reaches 10% at ε ~ 0.95 at Q² = 0.01 GeV²

## Context
This work extends the hadronic formalism of Blunden, Melnitchouk, and Tjon (2005) that resolved the proton form factor discrepancy, applying it to the weak sector for parity-violating scattering. It improves upon the earlier calculation by Marciano and Sirlin (1984) at Q²=0 and the monopole-based calculation by Zhou et al. (2006), showing that realistic form factors produce significantly different corrections. The results provide corrections that can be applied to data from SAMPLE, G0, HAPPEX, PVA4, and Qweak experiments, enabling a reanalysis of strange form factor extractions that properly accounts for two-boson exchange effects.
