# Shell Model Symmetries-Honoring Franco Iachello

> We discuss L=0 vs L=2 couplings, symmetries of the pairing interaction for neutrons and protons, and the J_{max} interaction. We show that certain schematic interactions yield exponentially decreasing transition strengths.We compare shell model calculations of B(E2)'s and quadrupole moments in the p-f shell with collective model results.

# Shell Model Symmetries

## Key claim

The paper shows that certain schematic interactions (specifically the J_max interaction) yield wave functions that are proportional to unitary 9j coefficients to excellent approximation, and that these interactions produce exponentially decreasing transition strengths; however, the inter-band B(E2) transitions between yrast and second-group states in the p-f shell are very small and do not show a clear trend with increasing neutron number, unlike the better-understood yrast transitions.

## Method

The authors perform shell model calculations using the code NushellX in the f7/2 and p-f shell model spaces, comparing results from schematic interactions (J=0 pairing, J_max pairing, and mixed interactions) with realistic interactions such as CCGI and MBZE. They analyze wave function amplitudes for states in 44Ti, 48Cr, and 96Cd, and compute B(E2) values and quadrupole moments for Ti isotopes (A=44,46,48,50) and 48Cr, comparing with the rotational model of Bohr and Mottelson and with experimental data on 98Sr from Clement et al. They also study tridiagonal and pentadiagonal matrix models to understand exponential falloffs in transition strength distributions.

## Key numbers

- Two-body matrix elements for 42Sc (in MeV): T=1 matrix elements for J=0,2,4,6 are 0.000, 1.5803, 2.8153, and 3.2420; T=0 matrix elements for J=1,3,5,7 are 0.6111, 1.4903, 1.5161, and 0.6163
- In the ground state of 44Ti with MBZE interaction, the amplitude for protons coupling to Jp=2 and neutrons to Jn=2 is 0.5616, corresponding to 31.5% probability
- Yrast B(E2) 0→2 values (in e²fm⁴): 526 for 44Ti, 624 for 46Ti, 521 for 48Ti, 502 for 50Ti, and 1254 for 48Cr
- The ratio B(E2) 2(1)→2(2) / B(E2) 0(1)→2(1) is 0.2909 for 44Ti and 0.1694 for 46Ti, compared to 0.000767 for 98Sr
- Quadrupole moments (e fm²) for 2(1) states: 6.01 (44Ti), -13.6 (46Ti), -14.5 (48Ti), 6.53 (50Ti), -30.8 (48Cr)
- For 96Cd with the realistic Qi interaction, the energy differences E(J)-E(J+2) for J=0 through 16 show a gap of 1.681 MeV between J=8 and J=10
- The asymptotic form of the relevant U9j coefficient for large j is A j^(3/2) e^(-αj) with α = 4 ln(2)

## Context

This work honors Franco Iachello and discusses precursors to the interacting boson model (IBA/IBM) developed by Iachello and Arima. It extends earlier single-j shell calculations by McCullen, Bayman, and Zamick (1964) and by Ginocchio and French, as well as the MBZE calculations from 2006. The paper compares shell model results with the collective rotational model of Bohr and Mottelson and with experimental data on 98Sr from Clement et al. The matrix studies of exponential falloffs in transition strength relate to observed behavior in gamma cascades following neutron capture and in calculated magnetic dipole strength distributions. The work identifies open questions about inter-band B(E2) transitions and the behavior of seniority in mixed neutron-proton systems that deserve further experimental and theoretical study.
