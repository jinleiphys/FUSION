# New Developments in Relativistic Fluid Dynamics with spin

> In this work, we briefly review the progress made in the formulation of hydrodynamics with spin with emphasis on the application to the relativistic heavy-ion collisions. In particular, we discuss the formulation of hydrodynamics with spin for perfect-fluid and the first order viscous corrections with some discussion on the calculation of spin kinetic coefficients. Finally, we apply relativistic hydrodynamics with spin to the relativistic heavy-ion collisions to calculate the spin polarization of $Λ$-particles.

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes recent progress in formulating relativistic hydrodynamics that includes spin as an independent dynamical variable, but it notes that current models based on thermal vorticity alone fail to describe the experimentally observed sign of longitudinal spin polarization of Lambda hyperons as a function of azimuthal angle, indicating the need for a spin polarization tensor that is independent of thermal vorticity.

## Method

The authors review multiple approaches to spin hydrodynamics: (1) a Wigner function approach with semiclassical expansion in powers of h-bar to derive equilibrium distribution functions for spin-1/2 particles; (2) a classical treatment of spin degrees of freedom; and (3) an extension of the classical approach to include first-order dissipative corrections. They then apply the relativistic hydrodynamics with spin to heavy-ion collisions to calculate the spin polarization of Lambda particles.

## Key numbers

* The net charge density in the perfect-fluid formulation is given by n = 4 cosh(zeta) sinh(xi) n_0(T), where n_0(T) = (1/(2 pi^2)) m-hat^2 T^3 K_2(m-hat) with m-hat = m/T.
* The energy density is epsilon = 4 cosh(xi) epsilon_0(T), where epsilon_0(T) = (1/(2 pi^2)) m-hat^2 T^4 (3 K_2(m-hat) + m-hat K_1(m-hat)).
* The pressure is P = 4 cosh(xi) P_0(T), where P_0(T) = (1/(2 pi^2)) m-hat^2 T^4 K_2(m-hat) = n_0 T.
* Early predictions based on spin-orbit interaction and perturbative QCD gave global polarization of order 10 percent, while later relativistic hydrodynamic models predicted about 1 percent, which was later confirmed by STAR.

## Context

This work extends the earlier formulations of spin hydrodynamics by Becattini (2013, 2016, 2017) and Florkowski (2017, 2018), who developed equilibrium Wigner functions and perfect-fluid versions of spin-polarized fluids. It also builds on the kinetic theory approach of Weickgenannt (2020) and Speranza (2020) for including dissipative effects. The review addresses the discrepancy between hydrodynamic model predictions and STAR experimental data (2017, 2018) for longitudinal polarization of Lambda hyperons, where models give the opposite sign of oscillation as a function of azimuthal angle. This work enables future development of a complete dissipative spin-hydrodynamic framework that can treat the spin polarization tensor as an independent variable, potentially resolving the sign problem.
