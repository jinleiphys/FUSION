# Damping of sound waves in superfluid nucleon-hyperon matter of neutron stars

> We consider sound waves in superfluid nucleon-hyperon matter of massive neutron-star cores. We calculate and analyze the speeds of sound modes and their damping times due to the shear viscosity and non-equilibrium weak processes of particle transformations. For that, we employ the dissipative relativistic hydrodynamics of a superfluid nucleon-hyperon mixture, formulated recently [M.E. Gusakov and E.M. Kantor, Phys. Rev. D78, 083006 (2008)]. We demonstrate that the damping times of sound modes calculated using this hydrodynamics and the ordinary (nonsuperfluid) one, can differ from each other by several orders of magnitude.

## Key claim

The damping times of sound modes in superfluid nucleon-hyperon matter, calculated using the full superfluid relativistic hydrodynamics, can differ by several orders of magnitude from those calculated using ordinary (nonsuperfluid) hydrodynamics, even when the effects of superfluidity are included only in the kinetic coefficients.

## Method

The authors employed the dissipative relativistic hydrodynamics of a superfluid nucleon-hyperon mixture (Gusakov and Kantor, 2008) to study sound waves in neutron-star cores composed of neutrons, protons, electrons, muons, and Λ and Σ− hyperons. They linearized the hydrodynamic equations for small perturbations, considered the limits of both slow and fast nonleptonic weak reactions (n+n ↔ p+Σ−, n+p ↔ p+Λ, n+n ↔ n+Λ, n+Λ ↔ Λ+Λ), and calculated the sound speeds and damping times due to shear viscosity and the nonequilibrium weak processes.

## Key numbers

- Baryon number density used for numerical calculations: n_b = 3 n_0 = 0.48 fm⁻³, where n_0 = 0.16 fm⁻³ is the nucleon number density in atomic nuclei.
- Critical temperatures for baryon superfluidity adopted: T_cn = 10⁹ K (neutrons), T_cp = 2 × 10⁹ K (protons), T_cΛ = 3 × 10⁹ K (Λ hyperons), T_cΣ = 6 × 10⁹ K (Σ− hyperons).
- Pulsation frequency chosen for the analysis: ω = 10⁴ s⁻¹.
- In the limit of slow reactions, three sound modes exist when all baryon species are superfluid (T < T_cn); in the limit of fast reactions, two sound modes exist under the same conditions.
- At temperatures above T_cΛ, only one sound mode remains in both limiting cases.

## Context

This work extends the study of neutron-star pulsations from nucleon (npeμ) matter, previously investigated by Epstein (1988), Andersson and Comer (2001), and Gusakov (2007), to the more complex nucleon-hyperon composition found in massive neutron-star cores. It employs the relativistic superfluid hydrodynamics formulated by Gusakov and Kantor (2008) and uses the third equation of state of Glendenning (1985). The results enable future studies of global pulsations of neutron stars with superfluid hyperon cores, showing that the common approximation of using nonsuperfluid hydrodynamics with superfluid-modified kinetic coefficients can lead to damping time errors of several orders of magnitude.
