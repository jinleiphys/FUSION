# From (e,e'p) to Neutrino Scattering

> Since a long time electron scattering has been envisaged as a powerful and preferential tool to investigate nuclear properties. In particular, the (e,e'p) knockout reaction has provided a wealth of information on the single particle (s.p.) aspects of nuclear structure, on the validity and the limit of the independent particle shell model. The work done for electron scattering is extremely useful also for the analysis and the interpretation of neutrino oscillation experiments, where nuclei are used as neutrino detectors and it is crucial that nuclear effects in neutrino-nucleus interactions are well under control. In this contribution it is discussed if and how the work done for (e,e'p) can be exploited for the analysis of neutrino-nucleus scattering data.

## Key claim

The extensive theoretical and experimental framework developed for (e,e'p) knockout reactions can be exploited for neutrino-nucleus scattering analysis, but it is not guaranteed that a model able to describe electron scattering data will describe neutrino scattering data with the same accuracy, because the two situations present different aspects despite many similarities.

## Method

The paper reviews the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) and relativistic DWIA (RDWIA) frameworks for exclusive (e,e'p) reactions, where the cross section is computed using optical potentials for final-state interactions and phenomenological mean-field wave functions for bound states. For inclusive scattering and high missing energies, the Green's function (GF) model and a multi-step direct scattering theory based on Feshbach, Kerman, and Koonin are discussed, where the latter treats secondary collisions of the emitted nucleon via convolution of one-step knockout and multi-step cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factors extracted from (e,e'p) data show a depletion of quasi-hole states near the Fermi energy of about 30-40% relative to the independent particle shell model predictions
- Short-range correlations contribute only a few percent, at most 10-15% when tensor correlations are included, to the depletion of spectroscopic factors
- For the 16O(e,e'p) reaction at Jefferson Lab, sharp peaks appear at missing energies of 12 MeV and 18 MeV from the 1p1/2 and 1p3/2 shells, with RDWIA calculations describing the shape up to pm = 350 MeV/c
- At missing energies above 60 MeV or missing momenta above 200 MeV/c, the experimental cross section becomes flat and independent of missing energy
- In the 40Ca(e,e'p) example, calculations used incident electron energy Ek = 497 MeV, scattered electron energy Ek' = 350 MeV, electron scattering angle 52.9 degrees, and outgoing proton energy 87 +/- 10 MeV

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed for electron scattering by groups at Saclay, NIKHEF, MAMI, and Jefferson Lab (Bof93, Bof96, Fru84, Mou76, Ber82, Dew90, Lap93, Blo95, Gao00, Malov00) to the analysis of neutrino oscillation experiments where nuclei serve as neutrino detectors. The Green's function model, originally developed for inclusive electron scattering (Eenr, Eesym, Ee), has been extended to charged-current quasielastic neutrino scattering (Cc, Acta, Acta1, Confcc, Compmini, Prd, Prd1, Prd2, Compnc, Prd3, Prd4, Martin, GRFOP). The multi-step direct scattering approach of Viviani (Viv1, Viv2, Viv3) provides a method to include rescattering effects that become important at high missing energies and momenta, where direct one-nucleon knockout alone is insufficient.
