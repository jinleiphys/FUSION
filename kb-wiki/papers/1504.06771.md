# Neutron Resonance Widths and the Porter-Thomas Distribution

> Experimental evidence has recently put the validity of the Porter-Thomas distribution (PTD) for partial neutron widths into question. We identify two terms in the effective Hamiltonian that violate orthogonal invariance (the basis for the PTD). Both are due to the coupling to the decay channels. We show that realistic estimates for the coupling to the neutron channel and for non-statistical gamma decays yield significant modifications of the PTD.

## Key claim

The Porter-Thomas distribution (PTD) for reduced neutron widths is violated by two dynamical effects that break the orthogonal invariance of the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble: the Thomas-Ehrman shift (a real energy shift from coupling to the neutron channel) and non-statistical fluctuations in total gamma decay widths; both effects produce significant deviations from the PTD, but the paper does not claim that these effects definitively resolve the experimental discrepancies, only that they "may cause significant deviations" and "qualify as causes."

## Method

The authors construct an effective Hamiltonian for s-wave neutron scattering on a spin-zero target, starting from a GOE Hamiltonian and adding terms for the Thomas-Ehrman shift (a principal-value integral over the neutron channel coupling) and for gamma decay channels. They treat the Thomas-Ehrman shift and the spread of gamma decay widths as a single symmetry-breaking term \(Z\) acting on the superradiant state \(|1\rangle\), then compute the distribution of partial neutron widths \(x = N |\langle 1 | \phi_\mu \rangle|^2\) numerically for GOE ensembles of dimension \(N = 1000\) and fit the deviations from the PTD using a parametrized form.

## Key numbers

- The Thomas-Ehrman shift \(F\) is estimated as \(F \approx \lambda / \pi\), where \(\lambda\) defines the GOE spectral width \(4\lambda\) and the mean level spacing is \(d = \pi \lambda / N\).
- For medium-weight and heavy nuclei, typical average resonance spacings \(d\) are of order 10 eV; total gamma decay widths are of order 100 meV, with a half-width \(\sigma \approx 300\) meV, giving \(\Gamma_0 \approx \pi \lambda / (100 N)\) and \(\sigma \approx \pi \lambda / (30 N)\).
- The spreading width of the doorway state is \(\Gamma^{\downarrow} = 2\lambda\), comparable to the total width of the GOE spectrum.
- For \(\kappa = Z/\lambda = 0.8\) (real \(Z\)), the Gini coefficient \(\tau = 0.70\), corresponding to a \(\chi^2\) distribution with \(\nu = 0.72\) degrees of freedom (compared to \(\tau = 2/\pi \approx 0.64\) for the PTD with \(\nu = 1\)).
- The fit coefficients for real \(Z\) in the range \(0.4 \leq \kappa \leq 1\) follow \(A/\kappa^2 = -0.035 \pm 0.010 + (0.16 \pm 0.01)\kappa\) and \(B/\kappa^2 = 0.146 \pm 0.002 + (0.099 \pm 0.003)\kappa\).

## Context

This work addresses recent experimental results from Koehler et al. (2010, 2011) that excluded agreement with the PTD for Pt isotopes and the Nuclear Data Ensemble with very high probability, contradicting the standard random-matrix theory prediction. Previous theoretical attempts by Weidenmuller (2010), Celardo et al. (2011), and Volya (2011) had not definitively resolved the issue. The paper identifies two previously neglected dynamical effects that break orthogonal invariance, and its schematic model provides a framework for estimating deviations in individual nuclei using the neutron strength function and the spread of total gamma decay widths, enabling simultaneous analysis of neutron and gamma width distributions.
