# Resonances and scattering in microscopic cluster models with the complex-scaled generator coordinate method

> The generator coordinate method of a microscopic cluster model is developed to treat the resonance and scattering of nuclear clusters with complex scaling. We consistently derive the formulation of the complex scaling for the microscopic cluster model, in which only the relative motions between clusters are transformed in the generator coordinate wave function. We also reveal the applicability of this method to the cluster wave function. Furthermore, we demonstrate this framework in the 2$α$ system of $^8$Be and obtain the solutions of resonance and non-resonant continuum states. Using these solutions, we calculate the level density, which brings the phase shifts of the cluster-cluster scattering. This work becomes the foundation in the description of the multi-cluster scattering states of nuclei in a microscopic framework with complex scaling.

## Key claim

The paper provides a consistent derivation showing that applying complex scaling to the generator coordinate method (GCM) for microscopic cluster models is equivalent to transforming only the relative motions between clusters, with the condition that the internal cluster wave functions must be of the form that allows the complex scaling to be absorbed into a transformation of the range parameter \(\nu\).

## Method

The authors combine the Bloch-Brink (BB) \(\alpha\) cluster model with the generator coordinate method (GCM) and apply complex scaling by transforming the generator coordinates \(\{\bm{R}_i\}\) to \(\{\bm{R}_i e^{i\theta}\}\) in the BB basis states, while keeping the range parameter \(\nu\) unchanged. They solve the complex-scaled Hill-Wheeler equation for the \(2\alpha\) system of \(^8\)Be to obtain resonance energies and non-resonant continuum states, then use these solutions to compute the complex-scaled level density and extract the \(\alpha\)-\(\alpha\) scattering phase shifts via the continuum level density \(\Delta(E)\).

## Key numbers

* \(^8\)Be \(0^+\) resonance: energy 0.08 MeV, decay width \(< 1\times 10^{-3}\) MeV (experiment: 0.0918 MeV, \(5.57(25)\times 10^{-6}\) MeV)
* \(^8\)Be \(2^+\) resonance: energy 2.50 MeV, decay width 1.27 MeV (experiment: 3.12(1) MeV, 1.513(15) MeV)
* \(^8\)Be \(4^+\) resonance: energy 10.72 MeV, decay width 6.14 MeV (experiment: 11.44(15) MeV, \(\approx 3.5\) MeV)
* \(^8\)Be \(6^+\) resonance: energy 24.18 MeV, decay width 31.74 MeV (experiment: \(\approx 28\) MeV, \(\approx 20\) MeV)
* Range parameter \(\nu = 0.264\) fm\(^{-2}\) used in the BB wave function, giving \(\alpha\) particle energy \(E_\alpha = -27.96\) MeV and matter radius 1.46 fm
* 30 basis states used with mean relative distance between two \(\alpha\) clusters from 2/3 to 20 fm
* Scaling angle \(\theta = 27^\circ\) used for stable solutions

## Context

This work extends the complex scaling method, previously applied to the resonating group method (RGM) and orthogonality condition model (OCM) by Kruppa (1988) and Myo (2014), to the Bloch-Brink GCM framework. It builds on the recent work of Zhang (2022) who first applied complex scaling to BB+GCM by transforming generator coordinates, but provides a rigorous derivation and clarifies the physical meaning. The method enables microscopic description of multi-cluster scattering states, such as the \(3\alpha\) system, using complex-scaled Green's functions, which previously required phenomenological intercluster potentials in OCM as done by Myo (2014) and Yamamoto (2009).
