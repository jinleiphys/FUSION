# Spin observables of the reactions NN -> DeltaN and pd -> Delta (pp)(1S0) in collinear kinematics

> A general formalism for double and triple spin-correlations of the reaction NN -> DeltaN is developed for the case of collinear kinematics. A complete polarization experiment allowing to reconstruct all of the four amplitudes describing this process is suggested. Furthermore, the spin observables of the inelastic charge-exchange reaction pd -> Delta^0(pp)(1S0) are analyzed in collinear kinematics within the single pN scattering mechanism involving the subprocess pn -> Delta^0p. The full set of spin observables related to the polarization of one or two initial particles and one final particle is obtained in terms of three invariant amplitudes of the reaction pd -> Delta (pp)(1S0) and the transition form factor d->(pp)(1S0). A complete polarization experiment for the reaction pd -> Delta^0(pp)(1S0) is suggested which allows one to determine three independent combinations of the four amplitudes of the elementary subprocess NN -> DeltaN.

## Key claim

The paper develops a general formalism for double and triple spin-correlations of the reactions NN → ΔN and pd → Δ(pp)(¹S₀) specifically for collinear kinematics, where the spin structure simplifies because only a single physical direction (the beam momentum) exists; the authors suggest complete polarization experiments that would allow reconstruction of all four amplitudes for NN → ΔN and all three amplitudes for pd → Δ(pp)(¹S₀), but note that the analysis of polarization effects in collinear kinematics cannot be considered as a limiting case of the general formalism developed for binary reactions where the scattering plane is well defined.

## Method

The authors use Clebsch-Gordan coefficient techniques to derive expressions for spin observables in collinear kinematics for binary reactions of types 1/2+1/2 → 3/2+1/2 and 1/2+1 → 3/2+0, based only on parity and angular momentum conservation. For the reaction pd → Δ⁰(pp)(¹S₀), they additionally develop a model within the impulse approximation (single pN scattering mechanism) that connects the three invariant amplitudes of the three-body reaction to the four invariant amplitudes of the elementary subprocess pn → Δ⁰p via transition form factors S₀ and S₂ from the deuteron d(³S₁-³D₁) to the ¹S₀ state.

## Key numbers

- The reaction NN → ΔN in collinear kinematics is described by exactly four invariant amplitudes: B₁ = a₂²⁰, B₂ = a₂²¹, B₃ = a₁²¹, B₄ = a₁⁰¹
- The reaction pd → Δ⁰(pp)(¹S₀) in collinear kinematics is described by exactly three invariant amplitudes: A₁, A₂, A₃
- A complete polarization experiment for NN → ΔN requires measurement of ten observables: dσ₀, K₁₁,₁₋₁⁰⁰, K₁₀,₁₀²⁰, K₁₁,₁₁²⁻², K₀₀,₀₀²⁰, K₁₀,₀₀³⁰, K₁₁,₀₀³¹, K₁₁,₁₁³⁻², K₁₋₁,₁₁³⁰, and K₁₁,₁₀³⁻¹
- A complete polarization experiment for pd → Δ⁰(pp)(¹S₀) with transversally polarized particles requires nine observables; with combined longitudinal and transversal polarizations this reduces to eight observables
- The impulse approximation for pd → Δ⁰(pp)(¹S₀) is expected to be valid at beam energies T > 1 GeV, where the transferred momentum Q < 250 MeV/c is fairly small
- The transition form factors S₀ and S₂/S₀ are reasonably well constrained by existing NN data at transferred momenta Q < 300 MeV/c (corresponding to proton beam kinetic energy T_p > 800 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the formalism previously developed for collinear kinematics in backward elastic dp scattering by Rekalo (unreferenced in text) and for near-threshold polarization phenomena by Rekalo (unreferenced in text), as well as the formalism for the charge-exchange reaction dp → (pp)n developed by Bugg and Wilkin (unreferenced in text) which was successfully applied to determine spin-flip amplitudes in quasi-free charge-exchange scattering pn → np by Ellegaard (unreferenced) and Sams (unreferenced). The formalism enables complete polarization experiments for NN → ΔN and pd → Δ(pp)(¹S₀) that could provide stringent constraints on the spin dependence of the NN → ΔN transition amplitude, which is not yet well known despite its importance for understanding three-nucleon forces, pion production, and the energy dependence of spin-dependent pp cross sections Δσ_T and Δσ_L at beam energies 600-800 MeV. The formalism can also be applied to other baryon resonances with spin-parity J^π = 3/2⁺, such as reactions involving production of the Σ*(1385) baryon.
