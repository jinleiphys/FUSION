# A study of $ρ-ω$ mixing in resonance chiral theory

> The strong and electromagnetic corrections to $ρ-ω$ mixing are calculated using a SU(2) version of resonance chiral theory up to next-to-leading orders in $1/N_C$ expansion, respectively. Up to our accuracy, the effect of the momentum dependence of $ρ-ω$ mixing is incorporated due to the inclusion of loop contributions. We analyze the impact of $ρ-ω$ mixing on the pion vector form factor by performing numerical fit to the data extracted from $e^+e^-\rightarrow π^+π^-$ and $τ\rightarrow ν_τ2π$, while the decay width of $ω\rightarrow π^+π^-$ is taken into account as a constraint. It is found that the momentum dependence is significant in a good description of the experimental data. In addition, based on the fitted values of the involved parameters, we analyze the decay width of $ω\rightarrow π^+π^-$, which turns out to be highly dominated by the $ρ-ω$ mixing effect.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the momentum dependence of the ρ-ω mixing amplitude is significant for describing experimental data on the pion vector form factor, contrary to the constant or momentum-independent assumption used in earlier studies. The authors calculate both strong and electromagnetic corrections to ρ-ω mixing using resonance chiral theory up to next-to-leading order in the 1/N_C expansion, finding that loop contributions introduce substantial momentum dependence that improves the fit to data from e⁺e⁻ → π⁺π⁻ and τ → ν_τ2π.

## Method

The authors employed a SU(2) version of resonance chiral theory (RχT) to calculate the ρ-ω mixing amplitude Π_ρω(s), decomposing it into strong contributions proportional to the quark mass difference Δ_ud = m_u - m_d and electromagnetic contributions proportional to the fine-structure constant α. Tree-level and one-loop diagrams were computed up to next-to-leading order in the 1/N_C expansion, with ultraviolet divergences cancelled by introducing counterterms with sufficient derivatives. The resulting momentum-dependent mixing amplitude was then used to fit the pion vector form factor to experimental data from e⁺e⁻ → π⁺π⁻ and τ → ν_τ2π in the energy region of 650 to 850 MeV, with the decay width of ω → π⁺π⁻ included as a constraint.

## Key numbers

- The tree-level strong contribution to the mixing amplitude is S_ρω^(a) = 2 M_ρ, counted as O(N_C⁰)
- The tree-level electromagnetic contribution is E_ρω^(b) = F_ρ F_ω / 3, counted as O(N_C¹)
- The quark mass difference used is (m_u - m_d) = -2.49 MeV from PDG (2016)
- The ω meson mass is taken as M_ω = 782.65 MeV and width Γ_ω = 8.49 MeV from PDG
- The physical coupling F_ω is extracted as F_ω ≈ 138 MeV from the ω → e⁺e⁻ decay width
- The fit is performed in the energy region of 650 to 850 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier tree-level calculations of ρ-ω mixing in resonance chiral theory by Urech (1995) and Ulf-G. Meißner (2006), which yielded a momentum-independent mixing amplitude. The inclusion of one-loop contributions provides momentum dependence that was previously studied in other models such as the extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model by Shakin (1995), the global color model by Mitchell (1996), the hidden local symmetry model by Benayoun (2000, 2001, 2008), and the chiral constituent quark model by Yan (1998, 2000). The results enable improved theoretical descriptions of the pion vector form factor relevant for calculating hadronic vacuum polarization contributions to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, and demonstrate that the ω → π⁺π⁻ decay width is highly dominated by the ρ-ω mixing effect rather than direct coupling.
