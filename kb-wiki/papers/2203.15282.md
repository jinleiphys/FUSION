# Sliding Dumbbell Method to search for the CME in heavy ion collisions

> This study explores the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The CME, observed as back-to-back charge separation along the magnetic field axis, is investigated using the newly developed Sliding Dumbbell Method (SDM) applied to Au+Au events at a center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s}_{\mathrm{NN}}$ = 200 GeV generated by the AMPT model with string melting configuration. The CME-like signal is externally injected in events by flipping charges of pairs of the particles perpendicular to the reaction plane. The study reports a significant enhancement of the CME-sensitive 3-particle $γ$ correlator in events with high back-to-back charge separation, in a given collision centrality. Additionally, a linear relationship is observed between the $\sqrt{|γ|}$ correlator for same-sign charge pairs and positive charge asymmetry ($\langle A{^+}\rangle$) across the dumbbell in CME-enriched sub-samples. Furthermore, the fraction of CME in $Δγ$ (difference between opposite and same sign $γ$ correlators) is presented across different collision centralities having different percentages of externally injected CME-like signal. Overall, the research aims to understand and detect the Chiral Magnetic Effect through innovative experimental method and detailed analysis of event structure.

## Key claim
The Sliding Dumbbell Method (SDM) can isolate individual events with high back-to-back charge separation from a given collision centrality, enabling detection of a CME-like signal as small as approximately 1% externally injected signal, rather than relying on event-averaged observables alone.

## Method
The authors applied the SDM to 16.3 million Au+Au events at √sNN = 200 GeV generated by the AMPT model with string melting configuration. The method slides a 90°-wide dumbbell across the azimuthal plane in 1° steps to find the maximum fractional back-to-back charge separation (fDbCS) per event, then slices the fDbCS distribution into 10 percentile bins. CME-like signals were externally injected by flipping charges of particle pairs perpendicular to the reaction plane, and background contributions were estimated via charge-shuffled events and correlated backgrounds.

## Key numbers
- 16.3 million Au+Au events at √sNN = 200 GeV were generated using AMPT with string melting.
- Injected CME signal ranged from ~0.65% (10-20% centrality) to ~6.7% (60-70% centrality).
- For the top 10% fDbCS bin in 50-60% centrality (~4% injected CME), Δγnorm ≈ 8.
- For the top 10% fDbCS bin, fCME = 0.508 ± 0.079 (50-60% centrality) and 0.555 ± 0.094 (30-40% centrality) for CME samples; for AMPT samples without injection, fCME = 0.01 ± 0.124 (50-60%) and 0.045 ± 0.081 (30-40%).
- Linear fit of √|γSS| versus ⟨A+⟩ gave slope p1 = 1.04 ± 0.0502 and intercept p0 = -0.077 ± 0.00702 with χ² = 1.22.

## Context
This work extends the search for the Chiral Magnetic Effect beyond event-averaged γ correlator measurements reported by STAR (Abelev 2009, 2010; Adamczyk 2013, 2014) and ALICE (Abelev 2013; Acharya 2018, 2020), which placed upper limits on CME contributions (e.g., 7% at 95% CL by CMS in 2018). The SDM enables event-by-event isolation of CME-like candidates, complementing the participant-spectator plane method used by STAR (2021) and the Event Shape Engineering technique. Future application to Au+Au, isobaric Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at RHIC, and Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies is suggested.
