# Structures and decay properties of extremely proton-rich nuclei $^{11,12}$O

> ${\rm \bf Background:}$ The recent observation of the unbound nucleus $^{11}$O offers the unique possibility to study how the structure and dynamics of two-proton ($2p$) decay is affected by the removal of one neutron from $^{12}$O, and provides important information on the Thomas-Ehrman effect in the mirror pairs $^{11}_{~8}$O$_3$-$^{11}_{~3}$Li$_8$ and $^{12}_{~8}$O$_4$-$^{12}_{~4}$Be$_8$, which involve the $2p$ emitters $^{11}$O and $^{12}$O. ${\rm \bf Purpose:}$ We investigate how continuum effects impact the structure and decay properties of $^{11}$O and $^{12}$O, and their mirror partners. ${\rm \bf Methods:}$ We solve the three-body core-nucleon-nucleon problem using the Gamow coupled-channel (GCC) method. The GCC Hamiltonian employs a realistic finite-range valence nucleon-nucleon interaction and the deformed cores of $^{9,10}$C, $^{9}$Li, and $^{10}$Be. ${\rm \bf Results:}$ We calculate the energy spectra and decay widths of $^{11}$O and $^{12}$O as well as those of their mirror nuclei. In particular, we investigate the dynamics of the $2p$ decay in the ground state of $^{12}$O by analyzing the evolution of the $2p$ configuration of the emitted protons as well as their angular correlations in the coordinate space. We also show how the analytic structure of the resonant states of $^{10}$Li and $^{10}$N impacts the low-lying states of $^{11}$Li and $^{11}$O. ${\rm \bf Conclusions:}$ We demonstrate that, in both nuclei $^{11}$O and $^{12}$O, there is a competition between direct and "democratic" $2p$ ground-state emission. The broad structure observed in $^{11}$O is consistent with four broad resonances, with the predicted $3/2^-_1$ ground state strongly influenced by the broad threshold resonant state in $^{10}$N, which is an isobaric analog of the antibound (or virtual) state in $^{10}$Li.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in both $^{11}$O and $^{12}$O there is a competition between direct and "democratic" two-proton ground-state emission, with the broad structure observed in $^{11}$O consistent with four broad resonances rather than a single state, and the predicted $3/2^-_1$ ground state of $^{11}$O being strongly influenced by the broad threshold resonant state in $^{10}$N.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body core-nucleon-nucleon problem using the Gamow coupled-channel (GCC) method, which employs a realistic finite-range valence nucleon-nucleon interaction and deformed cores of $^{9,10}$C, $^{9}$Li, and $^{10}$Be. The wave functions were expanded in the Berggren basis defined in the complex-momentum $k$ space, which provides correct outgoing asymptotic behavior to describe two-proton decay. The core-valence potentials were optimized to reproduce the energy spectrum of $^{11}$N using a particle-plus-rotor model, with the Woods-Saxon depth readjusted to reproduce the spectra of $^{10}$N and $^{11}$N before being used for predictions.

## Key numbers

* $^{12}$O ground state: calculated $2p$ decay energy $Q_{2p} = 1.973$ MeV with decay width 120 keV; experimentally measured $Q_{2p} = 1.688(29)$ MeV with decay width 51(19) keV (Webb 2019)
* $^{12}$O ground state refit: decay width $18^{+4}_{-3}$ keV at the remeasured $Q_{2p}$ value
* $^{12}$O ground state dominant configuration: 65% (0, 0, 0) and 21% (1, 1, 1) in Jacobi coordinates; 36% ($s_{1/2}$, $s_{1/2}$) and 25% ($p_{1/2}$, $p_{1/2}$) in COSM coordinates
* $^{11}$O ground state: $Q_{2p} = 4.158$ MeV with $2p$ decay width $\Gamma_{2p} = 1.296$ MeV; dominant configuration 66% (0, 0, 0) and 33% (1, 1, 1) in Jacobi coordinates
* $^{11}$O ground state: estimated $Q_{2p} = 3.21(84)$ MeV from quadratic isobaric multiplet mass equation extrapolation
* $^{10}$N threshold resonant state at experimental $Q_{2p} = 4.13$ MeV: located at $k = 0.252-0.213i$ fm$^{-1}$ ($E = 0.38$ MeV, $\Gamma_p = 4.45$ MeV)
* $^{11}$Li ground state energy: $-0.242$ MeV; $^{12}$Be ground state energy: $-3.672$ MeV
* $^{11}$Li 5/2$_1^+$ state: $E_x = 0.906$ MeV with $\Gamma_{2p} = 0.258$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the Gamow coupled-channel framework previously applied to $^{11}$O in Webb (2018) to provide a unified description of the mirror pairs $^{11}$O-$^{11}$Li and $^{12}$O-$^{12}$Be, building on earlier three-body studies of two-proton emitters such as Grigorenko (2002). The results enable detailed comparison of the Thomas-Ehrman effect in extreme proton-rich and neutron-rich systems, and demonstrate how the antibound state in $^{10}$Li (studied by Thompson 1994, Betan 2004, Michel 2006) becomes a broad threshold resonance in its isobaric analog $^{10}$N, which strongly influences the structure of $^{11}$O.
