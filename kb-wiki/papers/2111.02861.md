# Analysis of the fusion mechanism in synthesis superheavy element 119 via $^{54}$Cr+$^{243}$Am reaction

> The combined dinuclear system (DNS) and statistical model implanted in KEWPIE2 have been used to study the prospects for the synthesis of a superheavy element (SHE) with $Z=119$ in the $^{54}$Cr+$^{243}$Am fusion reaction. The method of calculation has been verified by description of the evaporation residue cross sections measured for the $^{48}$Ca+$^{243}$Am reaction. The calculated results of the partial and total cross sections for the complete fusion, quasifission, fast fission and evaporation residues formation for both reactions are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the $^{54}$Cr+$^{243}$Am reaction is a viable pathway to synthesize the superheavy element with Z=119, but this conclusion is conditional on the calculated evaporation residue cross sections being extremely small (on the order of femtobarns), and the prediction depends on the accuracy of the combined dinuclear system (DNS) and statistical model implemented in the KEWPIE2 code, which was first validated against measured data for the $^{48}$Ca+$^{243}$Am reaction.

## Method

The authors employed the DNS model to calculate capture, complete fusion, quasifission, and fast fission cross sections for both the $^{48}$Ca+$^{243}$Am and $^{54}$Cr+$^{243}$Am reactions, averaging over orientation angles of deformed nuclei and vibrational states of spherical nuclei. The fusion probability was determined from the competition between complete fusion and quasifission along the charge asymmetry axis using the driving potential and intrinsic fusion barriers. The evaporation residue cross sections were then computed using the statistical model in the KEWPIE2 code, which calculates the survival probability against fission during the neutron evaporation cascade.

## Key numbers

- For the $^{54}$Cr+$^{243}$Am reaction leading to the compound nucleus $^{297}119$, the intrinsic fusion barrier $B^*_{\rm fus}$ is determined from the driving potential shown in Fig. 4 of the paper.
- The deformation parameters used: $^{54}$Cr has $\beta^+_2 = 0.250$ and $\beta^-_3 = 0.250$; $^{243}$Am has $\beta^+_2 = 0.293$ and $\beta^-_3 = 0.083$; $^{48}$Ca has $\beta^+_2 = 0.106$ and $\beta^-_3 = 0.101$.
- The damping of shell corrections with temperature uses $T_0 = 1.16$ MeV and $d = 0.3$ MeV; the angular momentum damping uses $\ell_{1/2} = 20\hbar$ and $\Delta\ell = 3\hbar$.
- The liquid-drop fission barrier for elements with $Z>105$ is less than 1 MeV according to the model.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies on the synthesis of superheavy elements with Z=119 and 120, such as those by Nasirov (2009), Shen (2008), Wang (2012), and others, by applying the DNS model specifically to the $^{54}$Cr+$^{243}$Am reaction. The method was validated against experimental evaporation residue cross sections for the $^{48}$Ca+$^{243}$Am reaction, which has been used in successful SHE synthesis experiments by Oganessian and collaborators (2006-2013). The results enable future experimental planning by providing predicted excitation functions and optimal beam energies for attempting the synthesis of element 119.
