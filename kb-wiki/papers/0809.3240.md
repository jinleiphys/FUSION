# Evaluation of Modern 3He(alpha,gamma)7Be Data

> In both the Sun and the early universe, the 3He(alpha,gamma)7Be reaction plays a key role. The rate of this reaction is the least certain nuclear input needed to calculate both the primordial 7Li abundance in big bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) and the solar neutrino flux. Taking advantage of several recent highly precise experiments, we analyse modern 3He(alpha,gamma)7Be data using a robust and minimally model dependent approach capable of handling discrepant data sets dominated by systematic rather than statistical errors. We find S34(0)=0.580 pm 0.043(0.054) keV b at the 68.3(95.4)% confidence level.

## Key claim

The authors find that the astrophysical S-factor for 3He(alpha,gamma)7Be at zero energy is S34(0)=0.580±0.043(0.054) keV b at the 68.3(95.4)% confidence level, which is about 9% higher than the previous recommendation of Adelberger et al. (1998) but consistent within uncertainties; the result depends on restricting the analysis to modern experimental data and using a minimally model-dependent fitting function that accounts for the subthreshold pole and systematic errors.

## Method

The authors analyzed five modern experimental datasets (Brown et al. 2007, Confortola et al. 2007, Gyurky et al. 2007, Nara Singh et al. 2004, and Bemmerer et al. 2006, with the latter subsumed into Gyurky et al.) using a three-parameter S-factor model based on external capture dominated by E1 transitions, expanding (E+Q)S(E) to account for the subthreshold pole and s-wave and d-wave contributions. They employed a Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) approach with floating normalizations for each dataset to handle systematic errors, using a chi-squared minimization that separates data and normalization components and accounts for correlated systematic uncertainties between prompt gamma-ray and activity measurements.

## Key numbers

- S34(0) = 0.580 ± 0.043 keV b at the 68.3% confidence level (7.4% relative uncertainty)
- S34(0) = 0.580 ± 0.054 keV b at the 95.4% confidence level
- S'(0)/S(0) = -0.92 ± 0.18 MeV^-1 at the 68.3% confidence level
- The recommended reaction rate implies an 8% increase in predicted 7Be and 8B solar neutrino fluxes compared to Adelberger et al. (1998)
- The S34 value in the BBN energy range is 17% larger than the value adopted in Cyburt (2004), resulting in a 17% increase in predicted primordial 7Li abundance
- The discrepancy between predicted and observed primordial 7Li abundances increases from ~3 sigma to ~5 sigma
- The total systematic uncertainty from the discrepancy error (5.3%) and normalization error (3.0%) yields 6.1%, consistent with the MCMC result

## Context

This work extends the evaluation of the 3He(alpha,gamma)7Be reaction rate beyond the review of Adelberger et al. (1998), which recommended S34(0)=0.53±0.05 keV b and noted a 2.5 sigma discrepancy between prompt gamma-ray and activity measurements. The analysis uses modern data from underground accelerators and improved detectors to provide a more precise and model-independent determination, finding that the shape of the S-factor differs substantially from the microscopic cluster model calculation of Kajino (1987) below 500 keV. The results increase the predicted primordial 7Li abundance, worsening the discrepancy with observations of old stars, and increase predicted solar neutrino fluxes, which can be tested by experiments like Borexino.
