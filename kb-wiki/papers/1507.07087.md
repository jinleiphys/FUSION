# Stellar matter with pseudoscalar condensates

> In this work we consider how the appearance of gradients of pseudoscalar condensates in dense systems may possibly influence the transport properties of photons in such a medium as well as other thermodynamic characteristics. We adopt the hypothesis that in regions where the pseudoscalar density gradient is large the properties of photons and fermions are governed by the usual lagrangian extended with a Chern-Simons interaction for photons and a constant axial field for fermions. We find that these new pieces in the lagrangian produce non-trivial reflection coefficients both for photons and fermions when entering or leaving a region where the pseudoscalar has a non-zero gradient. A varying pseudoscalar density may also lead to instability of some fermion and boson modes and modify some properties of the Fermi sea. We speculate that some of these modifications could influence the cooling rate of stellar matter (for instance in compact stars) and have other observable consequences. While quantitative results may depend on precise astrophysical details most of the consequences are quite universal and consideration should be given to this possibility.

## Key claim

The paper hypothesizes that gradients of pseudoscalar condensates (such as axion or pion condensates) in dense stellar matter can modify photon and fermion transport properties, potentially affecting radiative cooling in compact stars, but the authors note that "quantitative results may depend on precise astrophysical details" and that the consequences are "quite universal" only as a speculation.

## Method

The authors adopt a field-theoretic approach, extending the standard Lagrangian with a Chern-Simons interaction for photons and a constant axial-vector field for fermions in regions where pseudoscalar density gradients exist. They solve the modified Maxwell and Dirac equations in a layered geometry with a linearly varying pseudoscalar background, computing dispersion relations, reflection and transmission coefficients at boundaries between normal and parity-breaking regions, and decay rates for fermion and photon conversion processes.

## Key numbers

- The effective transverse photon mass in plasma is given by \( m_t^2 \simeq \frac{e^2}{3}\left( \frac13 T^2 + \frac{\mu^2}{\pi^2} \right) \), where \(T\) is temperature and \(\mu\) is chemical potential.
- The magnitude of the constant axial vector \(b_\mu\) associated with a pseudoscalar gradient is expected to be "of the order of a few keV at most."
- The decay width for high-energy photons with positive polarization in a linearly varying pseudoscalar background is \( \Gamma_+ \simeq \frac{\alpha \zeta}{3} \), where \(\zeta\) is the gradient magnitude and \(\alpha\) is the fine-structure constant.
- For photon decay into \(e^+e^-\) pairs, the threshold condition is \( \omega \simeq \frac{m_e^2}{\zeta} \), and for \(\omega \sim\) GeV and \(\zeta \geq\) keV, most positive-chirality photons decay within \(t > 10^{-9}\) s, corresponding to a distance scale of \(\sim 10\) cm.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Lorentz and parity violation in electrodynamics, notably the Carroll-Field-Jackiw model (Carroll, Field, Jackiw, 1990), and applies these ideas to dense nuclear matter where pseudoscalar condensation may occur. The authors connect their analysis to the Chiral Magnetic Effect in heavy ion collisions, to axion condensate scenarios (Andrianov, 1994; Arkani-Hamed, 2004), and to neutral pion condensation in dense matter (Muto, 1988). The paper enables future investigation of whether such parity-breaking effects could produce observable signatures in compact star cooling, glitch phenomena in pulsars, or anomalous lepton pair production.
