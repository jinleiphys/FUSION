# Nuclear fission with mean-field instantons

> We present a description of nuclear spontaneous fission, and generally of quantum tunneling, in terms of instantons - periodic imaginary-time solutions to time-dependent mean-field equations - that allows for a comparison with more familiar and used generator coordinate (GCM) and adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (ATDHF) methods. It is shown that the action functional whose value for the instanton is the quasiclassical estimate of the decay exponent fulfils the minimum principle when additional constraints are imposed on trial fission paths. In analogy with mechanics, these are conditions of energy conservation and the velocity-momentum relations. In the adiabatic limit the instanton method reduces to the time-odd ATDHF equation, with collective mass including the time-odd Thouless-Valatin term, while the GCM mass completely ignores velocity-momentum relations. This implies that GCM inertia generally overestimates instanton-related decay rate. The very existence of the minimum principle offers a hope for a variational search for instantons. After the inclusion of pairing, the instanton equations and the variational principle can be expressed in terms of the imaginary-time-dependent Hartree-Fock- Bogolyubov (TDHFB) theory. The adiabatic limit of this theory reproduces ATDHFB inertia.

## Key claim

The instanton method for nuclear spontaneous fission provides a quasiclassical description of quantum tunneling within mean-field theory, and its action functional satisfies a minimum principle when trial fission paths are constrained by energy conservation and velocity-momentum relations; this implies that the generator coordinate method (GCM) inertia generally overestimates the instanton-related decay rate, while the adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (ATDHF) method reproduces the correct inertia in the adiabatic limit.

## Method

The paper derives instanton equations as periodic imaginary-time solutions to time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) equations, obtained by the transformation t → -iτ. The instanton action S = ℏ∫⟨φ_k(-τ)|∂_τ φ_k(τ)⟩ dτ is shown to be the minimum of an action functional over trial fission paths defined in the space of Slater determinants, subject to constraints of constant energy and the velocity-momentum relations. The method is generalized to include pairing via imaginary-time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (TDHFB) theory, and the adiabatic limit reproduces ATDHFB inertia.

## Key numbers

- The decay rate of a metastable state is proportional to exp(-S/ℏ), where S is the action for the optimal instanton (no specific numerical value given for any system).
- The paper states that GCM inertia "generally overestimates instanton-related decay rate" but provides no numerical comparison.
- No explicit numerical results for fission half-lives, barrier heights, or collective masses are presented in the text.

## Context

This work extends the instanton approach previously applied to simple systems (LNP, Arve, PN, JN, super, JS) to the context of nuclear fission, providing a formal comparison with the generator coordinate method (GCM) in the Gaussian overlap approximation (GOA) and the adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock (ATDHF) method. The paper shows that the instanton method reduces to the time-odd ATDHF equation in the adiabatic limit, with collective mass including the Thouless-Valatin term (Thouless and Valatin, 1962), while GCM mass ignores velocity-momentum relations. The variational principle offers a potential path toward numerical searches for instantons in realistic fission calculations, which had previously been considered intractable.
