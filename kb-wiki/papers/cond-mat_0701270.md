# Reversal of the circulation of a vortex by quantum tunneling in trapped Bose systems

> We study the quantum dynamics of a model for a vortex in a Bose gas with repulsive interactions in an anisotropic, harmonic trap. By solving the Schrödinger equation numerically, we show that the circulation of the vortex can undergo periodic reversals by quantum-mechanical tunneling. With increasing interaction strength or particle number, vortices become increasingly stable, and the period for reversals increases. Tunneling between vortex and antivortex states is shown to be described to a good approximation by a superposition of vortex and antivortex states (a Schrödinger cat state), rather than the mean-field state, and we derive an analytical expression for the oscillation period. The problem is shown to be equivalent to that of the two-site Bose Hubbard model with attractive interactions.

## Key claim

By solving the Schrödinger equation numerically for a model of a vortex in a Bose gas with repulsive interactions in an anisotropic harmonic trap, the authors show that the circulation of the vortex can undergo periodic reversals by quantum-mechanical tunneling even when the system's energy is below the classical energy barrier between vortex and antivortex states, a regime where mean-field theory predicts no reversals.

## Method

The authors consider N identical bosons in a two-dimensional anisotropic harmonic trap, restricting the Hilbert space to the two first-excited single-particle states (p-like states). They solve the many-body Schrödinger equation numerically for the time evolution starting from a vortex state, and compute the expectation value of the angular momentum as a function of time. For large interaction strengths, they treat the anisotropy term as a perturbation and derive an analytical expression for the energy splitting between the two lowest states using Nth-order perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- For Γ = 4.0 and Δω/ω̄ = 0.01, the oscillation period T is approximately 2.41 T₀ for N = 2, 5.73 T₀ for N = 3, and 37.7 T₀ for N = 5, where T₀ = 2π/Δω is the period in the absence of interactions.
- For Γ ≪ 1, repetitive collapses and revivals of the angular momentum occur with a period ∼ 2πN/γω̄.
- For Γ ≥ 2, the energy splitting between the two lowest states is given by ΔE = ℏ|Δω| N (α(N)/Γ)^(N-1), where α(2) = 1 and α(∞) = e/2, and the oscillation period is T = 2πℏ/ΔE.
- The overlap of the numerical wave function with the optimized cat state for N = 5 and Γ = 8.0 has a mean value of approximately 0.97.

## Context

This work extends the classical mean-field (Gross-Pitaevskii) prediction by García-Ripoll et al. (2001) that a rotating Bose-Einstein condensate in a non-rotating anisotropic trap can undergo periodic reversals of vorticity only if the initial energy exceeds the energy barrier. The authors show that quantum tunneling allows reversals below this barrier, and that the wave function is better described as a Schrödinger cat state rather than a mean-field state. The problem is shown to be equivalent to the two-site Bose Hubbard model with attractive interactions, which has been studied previously by Flach (1996) and Ho (2006). The results enable experimental observation in few-body systems trapped on an optical lattice, as demonstrated by Campbell (2006) and Fölling (2006).
