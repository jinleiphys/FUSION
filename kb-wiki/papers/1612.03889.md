# Impact of resonance decays on critical point signals in net-proton fluctuations

> The non-monotonic beam energy dependence of the higher cumulants of net-proton fluctuations is a widely studied signature of the conjectured presence of a critical point in the QCD phase diagram. In this work we study the effect of resonance decays on critical fluctuations. We show that resonance effects reduce the signatures of critical fluctuations, but that for reasonable parameter choices critical effects in the net-proton cumulants survive. The relative role of resonance decays has a weak dependence on the order of the cumulants studied with a slightly stronger suppression of critical effects for higher-order cumulants.

# Impact of resonance decays on critical point signals in net-proton fluctuations

## Key claim

Resonance decays reduce the signatures of critical fluctuations in net-proton cumulant ratios, but for reasonable parameter choices critical effects survive; the suppression is slightly stronger for higher-order cumulants, with a weak dependence on cumulant order.

## Method

The authors model critical fluctuations by coupling protons and anti-protons to the order parameter field of the three-dimensional Ising model via a linear sigma-model inspired coupling, then fold the resulting primordial net-proton cumulants with probabilistic resonance decay chains using a hadron resonance gas baseline. They apply kinematic cuts matching the STAR beam energy scan acceptance (mid-rapidity |y| ≤ 0.5, 0.4 GeV ≤ k_T ≤ 2 GeV) and evaluate cumulant ratios along a chemical freeze-out curve extracted from STAR fluctuation data by Alba (2014).

## Key numbers

- For coupling strength g_p = 5, resonance decays suppress the critical signal in Sσ by about 40% and in κσ² by about 50%.
- The maximum correlation length ξ along the freeze-out curve is about 2 fm, comparable to dynamical model estimates.
- The critical point is placed at μ_B^cp = 0.39 GeV with T^cp ≃ 0.149 GeV, using pseudo-critical line parameters T_{c,0} = 0.156 GeV and κ_c = 0.007.
- Three coupling values are studied: g_p = 3, 5, and 7, motivated by chiral model and quark model calculations.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of resonance decay effects on net-proton fluctuations without critical point (Garg 2013, Nahrgang 2014) by adding critical fluctuations via universality class arguments. It addresses the concern raised by Kitazawa (2012) that isospin randomization through resonance regeneration might strongly suppress critical signals. The results provide a benchmark for non-equilibrium studies such as those by Herold (2016) and Mukherjee (2015), and are directly comparable to preliminary STAR data from the beam energy scan (Thader 2016).
