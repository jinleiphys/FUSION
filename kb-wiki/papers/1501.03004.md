# Hadron Wavefunctions as a Probe of a Two Color Baryonic Medium

> The properties of the ground state of two-color QCD at non-zero baryon chemical potential $μ$ present an interesting problem in strongly-interacting gauge theory; in particular the nature of the physically-relevant degrees of freedom in the superfluid phase in the post-onset regime $μ>m_π/2$ still needs clarification. In this study we present evidence for in-medium effects at high $μ$ by studying the wavefunctions of mesonic and diquark states using orthodox lattice simulation techniques, made possible by the absence of a Sign Problem for the model with $N_f=2$. Our results show that beyond onset the spatial extent of hadrons decreases as $μ$ grows, and that the wavefunction profiles are consistent with the existence of a dynamically-gapped Fermi surface in this regime.

# Hadron Wavefunctions as a Probe of a Two Color Baryonic Medium

## Key claim

The spatial extent of hadrons in two-color QCD decreases as baryon chemical potential increases beyond the onset transition, and the oscillatory structure of hadron wavefunctions is consistent with the existence of a dynamically-gapped Fermi surface in the quarkyonic regime, though the authors note that Fermi momentum is not a gauge-invariant quantity and that the extent to which lessons from N_c=2 can be generalized to QCD remains to be seen.

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of two-color QCD with two flavors of Wilson fermion on a 12^3 x 24 lattice at beta=1.9 and kappa=0.168, corresponding to lattice spacing a=0.178(5) fm and temperature T=44(2) MeV. They computed hadron correlation functions in which the quark-antiquark or quark-quark pair at the sink are spatially separated by a vector r, using Coulomb gauge fixing to maintain gauge invariance, and extracted wavefunction profiles for mesonic and diquark states across chemical potentials mu a in the range [0.0, 1.1].

## Key numbers

- Onset chemical potential: mu_o = 0.323(3) a^{-1}, corresponding to half the pion mass m_pi/2
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.178(5) fm, set by assuming string tension is (440 MeV)^2
- Mass ratio: m_pi/m_rho = 0.807(5)
- Temperature: T = 44(2) MeV on the 12^3 x 24 lattice
- The quarkyonic regime where baryon density, pressure and superfluid condensate scale with mu according to expectations of degenerate quarks lies approximately in the range mu a in (0.4, 0.8)
- For free massless quarks on a 24^4 lattice, wavefunctions were computed at mu a = 0.75 with temporal separation tau = 8

## Context

This work extends previous studies of two-color QCD at non-zero chemical potential, including thermodynamic measurements by Cotter et al. (2013) showing that density, pressure and energy density scale approximately as mu^3 in the quarkyonic regime, and hadron spectrum calculations by Hands et al. (2007) showing degeneracy between meson and diquark channels in the superfluid phase. The wavefunction approach was previously applied to the Z_2 Gross-Neveu model in 2+1 dimensions by Hands et al. (2003), where oscillatory wavefunctions with wavelength decreasing systematically with mu were observed and identified as characteristic of a Fermi liquid. The present study enables future investigation of the transition from BEC to BCS realizations of superfluidity as mu increases, and may help clarify the nature of the quarkyonic state as a degenerate quark system where only gauge-invariant excitations are permitted.
