# Triple charged-particle decays of resonances illustrated by 12C-states

> The hyperspherical adiabatic expansion is combined with complex scaling and used to calculate the energy distributions of the particles arising from three-body decaying low-lying $^{12}$C resonances. The large distance continuum properties of the wavefunctions are crucial and must be accurately calculated. The substantial changes from small to large distances determine the decay mechanisms. We illustrate by computing the energy distributions from decays of the $1^{-}$, $2^-$ and $4^{-}$-resonances in $^{12}$C. These states are dominated by sequential ($1^-$), through the $^{8}$Be ground state, and direct ($2^-$, $4^-$) decays.

# Triple charged-particle decays of resonances illustrated by 12C-states

## Key claim
The hyperspherical adiabatic expansion combined with complex scaling can compute the energy distributions of particles from three-body decaying low-lying 12C resonances, but the large-distance continuum properties of the wavefunctions are crucial and must be accurately calculated because the substantial changes from small to large distances determine the decay mechanisms.

## Method
The authors describe 12C as a three-alpha cluster system and use the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method of the Faddeev equations combined with complex scaling of the coordinates to compute resonance wavefunctions. They first choose a two-body interaction to reproduce low-energy two-body scattering properties, then add a three-body potential to adjust the energy position of each many-body resonance independently for each Jπ. The energy distribution is computed in coordinate space, except for a phase-space factor, as the integral of the absolute square of the total wave function for a large value of the hyperradius.

## Key numbers
- For the 1− resonance: computed (ER, ΓR) = (3.61, 0.475) MeV, compared to experimental (3.57, 0.315) MeV
- For the 2− resonance: computed (ER, ΓR) = (4.53, 0.452) MeV, compared to experimental (4.55, 0.260) MeV
- For the uncertain 2−/4− state: theoretical values (5.98, 1.035) MeV, experimental values (6.08, 0.375) MeV
- The asymptotic behaviour of the energy distributions is reached for hyperradii around 70 fm
- The direct decay component of the 1− resonance constitutes about 24% of the total distribution
- The 2− and 4− resonances are located at 4.5 MeV and 5.98 MeV above the triple-alpha threshold, respectively

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of 0+ and 1+ states in 12C (Alvarez-Rodriguez 2007b), where they showed agreement with accurately measured distributions from Diget (2006). The method addresses the long-standing difficulty of constructing correct asymptotic wave functions when both two- and three-body structures are present, a problem noted in experimental work by Fynbo (2005). The computed energy distributions can be used to decide spin-parity assignments, such as suggesting that the state at 6.08 MeV above threshold currently assigned as 2− should instead be 4−, and to understand the inverse triple-alpha process relevant to nuclear astrophysics.
