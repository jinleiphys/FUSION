# Effects of the initialization of nucleon momentum in heavy-ion collisions at medium energies

> Based on the Isospin-dependent transport model Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU), effects of the difference of the high momentum tails (HMTs) of nucleon momentum distribution in colliding nuclei on some isospin-sensitive observables are studied in the $^{197}\rm {Au}+^{197}\rm {Au}$ reactions at incident beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon. It is found that the nucleon transverse and elliptic flows, the free neutron to proton ratio at low momenta are all less sensitive to the specific form of the HMT, while the free neutron to proton ratio at high momenta and the yields of $π^{-}$ and $π^{+}$ as well as the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio around the Coulomb peak are sensitive to the specific form of the HMT. Combining the present studies with the experimental measurements at rare-isotope reaction facilities worldwide, one may get more insights into the nuclear short-range correlations in heavy nuclei or nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The specific form of the high-momentum tail (HMT) of nucleon momentum distribution in heavy nuclei affects some isospin-sensitive observables in heavy-ion collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon, but not all; nucleon flows and the free neutron-to-proton ratio at low momenta are insensitive to the HMT form, while the free neutron-to-proton ratio at high momenta and pion yields are sensitive.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model to simulate central and semi-central \(^{197}\mathrm{Au}+^{197}\mathrm{Au}\) collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon. Three different nucleon momentum distributions with high-momentum tails were initialized: IMD1 (extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock with AV18+three-body force, ~15% nucleons in HMT), IMD2 (simple parameterized n-p dominance form, ~20% nucleons in HMT), and IMD3 (more complicated parameterized form, ~28% nucleons in HMT). The cutoff momenta were about 2 (IMD1, IMD2) and 2.38 (IMD3) times the Fermi momentum.

## Key numbers

- Free neutron-to-proton ratio at very high momenta shows effects of HMT difference reaching about 20% for central collisions (b = 1.4 fm) and about 50% for semi-central collisions (b = 7.5 fm) at 400 MeV/nucleon.
- The three HMT initializations have cutoff values of about 2 (IMD1, IMD2) and 2.38 (IMD3) times the Fermi momentum.
- The fraction of nucleons in the HMT is about 15% for IMD1, 20% for IMD2, and 28% for IMD3.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of short-range correlations (SRC) in nuclei, which established that about 20% of nucleons form high-momentum pairs with neutron-proton pairs being about 18 times more common than like-nucleon pairs. The study contrasts with earlier work by Yong et al. (2016) and Xu et al. (2017) that examined HMT effects on free nucleon emission, by systematically comparing three distinct HMT parameterizations and their effects on multiple observables. The results caution that probes sensitive to HMT form should be avoided when constraining the nuclear symmetry energy, and suggest that measurements at rare-isotope reaction facilities could help constrain the specific form of nucleon momentum distributions above the Fermi momentum.
