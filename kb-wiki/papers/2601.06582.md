# Lattice QCD determination of the $γZ$ box contribution to the proton weak charge

> We present the first lattice QCD determination of the $γZ$ box contribution to parity-violating electron-proton scattering, $\square_{γZ}$ , a key ingredient for the precise tests of the Standard Model via the proton weak charge. Our calculation covers the electron beam energies up to $E =155 MeV$. For the axial-vector component, we achieve reduced uncertainties across the entire energy range compared with phenomenological estimates. For the vector component, the uncertainties remain slightly larger after continuum extrapolation. At $E = 0$, where the vector part vanishes, we obtain $\square_{γZ}= 0.00412(9)$ , reducing the uncertainty by a factor of $2$ relative to the most precise previous determination. Incorporating this result yields an updated weak charge of $Q_{W}^{p}= 0.06987(50)$ . The calculated energy dependence of $\square_{γZ}$ further provides a first-principles input for the upcoming P2 experiment at Mainz, which will operate at the optimized beam energy of $155 MeV$ to extract $Q_{W}^p$.

# Lattice QCD determination of the γZ box contribution to the proton weak charge

## Key claim
The first lattice QCD calculation of the γZ box contribution to parity-violating electron-proton scattering achieves a factor-of-two improvement in precision over the most precise previous phenomenological determination at zero beam energy, but the vector component uncertainties remain slightly larger than phenomenological estimates after continuum extrapolation, and the calculation covers beam energies only up to 155 MeV.

## Method
The authors compute the γZ box contribution using 2+1-flavor domain-wall fermion ensembles from the RBC-UKQCD Collaboration at near-physical pion mass (142.6-142.9 MeV) with two lattice spacings (a^{-1}=1.023 and 1.378 GeV). They separate the box diagram into a Wick (non-pole) contribution evaluated via Fourier transform of Euclidean four-point correlators and a residue (pole) contribution requiring Laplace transform, with the vector channel requiring treatment of intermediate Nπ states that become on-shell above approximately 150 MeV beam energy. The axial-vector component is split into a nonperturbative low-Q² region (Q²<2 GeV²) computed on the lattice and a high-Q² region treated with perturbative QCD.

## Key numbers
- At E=0: □_{γZ} = 0.00412(9) (combining statistical and excited-state uncertainties), reducing uncertainty by a factor of 2 relative to the previous best determination
- At E=0: □_{γZ}^A = 4.12(7)_stat(5)_ES × 10^{-3}
- At E=155 MeV: □_{γZ} = 5.77(32)_stat(21)_ES(11)_FV × 10^{-3}
- Updated proton weak charge: Q_W^p = 0.06987(20)_{s_W^2}(10)_{WW}(9)_{γZ}(44)_{γγ}
- Vector box at E=0: □_{γZ}^V(0) = 1.041 × 10^{-3} (analytic value)
- Axial-vector box at E=0 from continuum extrapolation: 4.121(74) × 10^{-3}
- Threshold beam energy for on-shell Nπ production: E_th ≈ 150 MeV
- Lattice volumes: L = 4.6 fm for both ensembles

## Context
This work provides the first lattice QCD determination of the γZ box contribution, which has been the dominant theoretical uncertainty in extracting the proton weak charge from parity-violating electron scattering. Previous determinations relied on dispersion relations and model assumptions, notably by Gorchtein (2015) for the vector part and Erler (2019) for the axial-vector part. The result at E=0 updates the weak charge determination from the Qweak experiment (2018), and the energy-dependent result provides first-principles input for the upcoming P2 experiment at Mainz, which will operate at 155 MeV beam energy. The calculation extends the lattice methodology previously applied to the γW box in beta decay by Feng (2020) and Ma (2021, 2023), but faces new challenges from finite beam energy, on-shell intermediate states, and the need to treat both vector and axial-vector hadronic components.
