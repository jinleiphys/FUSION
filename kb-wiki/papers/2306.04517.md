# Binding energies of ground and isomeric states in neutron-rich ruthenium isotopes: measurements at JYFLTRAP and comparison to theory

> We report on precision mass measurements of $^{113,115,117}$Ru performed with the JYFLTRAP double Penning trap mass spectrometer at the Accelerator Laboratory of University of Jyväskylä. The phase-imaging ion-cyclotron-resonance technique was used to resolve the ground and isomeric states in $^{113,115}$Ru and enabled for the first time a measurement of the isomer excitation energies, $E_x(^{113}$Ru$^{m})=100.5(8)$ keV and $E_x(^{115}$Ru$^{m})=129(5)$ keV. The ground state of $^{117}$Ru was measured using the time-of-flight ion-cyclotron-resonance technique. The new mass-excess value for $^{117}$Ru is around 36 keV lower and 7 times more precise than the previous literature value. With the more precise ground-state mass values, the evolution of the two-neutron separation energies is further constrained and a similar trend as predicted by the BSkG1 model is obtained up to the neutron number $N=71$.

## Key claim

The paper reports precision mass measurements of neutron-rich ruthenium isotopes that resolve ground and isomeric states separately, but the abstract's claim of agreement with the BSkG1 model for two-neutron separation energies up to N=71 is conditional on the new ground-state mass values constraining the trend, not on exact numerical agreement with the model.

## Method

The authors used the JYFLTRAP double Penning trap mass spectrometer at the University of Jyväskylä to perform mass measurements of 113,115,117Ru. The phase-imaging ion-cyclotron-resonance (PI-ICR) technique was employed to resolve ground and isomeric states in 113Ru and 115Ru, while the time-of-flight ion-cyclotron-resonance (TOF-ICR) technique with Ramsey excitation was used for 117Ru. Reference measurements were performed using 133Cs+ ions, and isomer excitation energies were determined from frequency ratios between ground and isomeric states.

## Key numbers

- Isomer excitation energy Ex(113Rum) = 100.5(8) keV, measured for the first time
- Isomer excitation energy Ex(115Rum) = 129(5) keV, measured for the first time
- Ground-state mass excess of 113Ru: -71874.6(15) keV
- Ground-state mass excess of 115Ru: -66054.7(28) keV
- Ground-state mass excess of 117Ru: -59526(64) keV, which is approximately 36 keV lower and 7 times more precise than the previous literature value
- The 115Rum isomer has half-life T1/2 = 76(6) ms, the shortest-lived state ever measured at JYFLTRAP

## Context

This work extends previous mass measurements of neutron-rich ruthenium isotopes at JYFLTRAP by Hager et al. (2007) and Hakala et al. (2011), which could not resolve ground and isomeric states in 113Ru and 115Ru due to insufficient resolving power of the TOF-ICR technique. The new measurements correct potential systematic shifts in earlier ground-state mass values that may have been contaminated by unresolved isomers, similar to effects observed in rhodium isotopes by Hukkanen et al. (2023). The results enable more precise constraints on two-neutron separation energy trends and provide benchmark data for comparison with the BSkG1 and BSkG2 microscopic models based on self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using Skyrme energy density functionals.
