# Charge-dependent anisotropic flow studies and the search for the Chiral Magnetic Wave in ALICE

> Theoretical calculations have shown the possibility of P-violating bubbles in the QCD vacuum, which in combination with the strong magnetic field created in off-central heavy-ion collisions lead to novel effects such as the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) and the Chiral Separation Effect (CSE). A coupling between the CME and the CSE produces a wave-like excitation called the Chiral Magnetic Wave (CMW). The CMW produces a quadrupole moment that always has the same sign and is therefore present in an average over events. In this talk we present a series of charge-dependent anisotropic flow measurements in Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV in ALICE, using two- and three-particle correlators with unidentified hadrons. The relation of these measurements to the search for the CMW is discussed.

## Key claim

The Chiral Magnetic Wave (CMW) produces a charge-dependent quadrupole moment that always has the same sign and is therefore present in an average over events, but the observed charge-dependent anisotropic flow signals may also arise from local charge conservation (LCC) backgrounds, and further theoretical input is needed to disentangle these contributions.

## Method

The analysis used two- and three-particle correlators with unidentified hadrons in Pb–Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV recorded by ALICE. The three-particle correlator ⟨cos(n(φ₁−φ₂))q₃⟩ was employed, where q₃ is the charge of the third particle, and the cos(n(φ₁−φ₂)) part was estimated using the cumulant method and denoted c_n{2}. Charge-dependent subtraction of ⟨q₃^±⟩ was applied to reduce local charge conservation backgrounds, and results were compared to HIJING Monte Carlo simulations.

## Key numbers

- The three-particle correlator for the second harmonic shows a substantial increase in correlation strength as collisions become more peripheral (centrality increases), with the magnetic field strength increasing as impact parameter increases
- For the third harmonic, a small but non-zero correlation is observed, which is removed almost entirely by charge-dependent subtraction
- Comparison to HIJING Monte Carlo found the three-particle correlator to be statistically insignificant, indicating jet-like correlations and 3-body decays play at most a very small role

## Context

This work extends the search for the Chiral Magnetic Wave previously performed by the STAR collaboration in Au–Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, where the slope of v_n versus event charge asymmetry A was measured. The analysis introduces a novel three-particle correlator proposed by Voloshin (2014) that is independent of tracking efficiency and requires no correction factor, unlike the STAR approach. The results enable future theoretical work to provide detailed constraints on the magnitude and range of local charge conservation versus CMW correlations, as discussed by Pratt (2014) regarding the charge balance function.
