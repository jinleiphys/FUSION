# The possible hidden-charm molecular baryons composed of anti-charmed meson and charmed baryon

> With the one-boson-exchange model we have studied the possible existence of the very loosely bound hidden-charm molecular baryons composed of anti-charmed meson and charmed baryon. Our numerical results indicate that there exist $Σ_c\bar{D}^*$ states with $I(J^P)=1/2(1/2^-), 1/2(3/2^-), 3/2(1/2^-), 3/2(3/2^-)$ and $Σ_c\bar{D}$ state with $3/2(1/2^-)$. But the $Λ_c \bar{D}$ and $Λ_c \bar{D}^*$ molecular states do not exist.

## Key claim

Using the one-boson-exchange (OBE) model, the authors find that bound hidden-charm molecular baryons exist only for systems containing a \(\Sigma_c\) baryon, not for those containing a \(\Lambda_c\) baryon, and that the binding is sensitive to the signs of the coupling constants in the heavy meson sector.

## Method

The authors constructed effective Lagrangians for S-wave anti-charmed mesons (\(\bar{D}, \bar{D}^*\)) and S-wave charmed baryons (\(\Lambda_c, \Sigma_c\)) interacting via light meson exchange (\(\pi, \eta, \rho, \omega, \sigma\)). They derived the OBE potentials in momentum space using the Breit approximation, introduced a monopole form factor with cutoff \(\Lambda\) to account for off-shell and structure effects, and then Fourier-transformed to coordinate space. The coupled-channel Schrodinger equation was solved numerically with the FESSDE program for cutoff values \(\Lambda\) between 0.8 and 2.2 GeV, considering only bound states with binding energy less than 50 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies and root-mean-square radii are presented as functions of the cutoff \(\Lambda\) (0.8 to 2.2 GeV) for five hidden-charm states: \(\Sigma_c\bar{D}^*\) with \(I(J^P)=1/2(1/2^-), 1/2(3/2^-), 3/2(1/2^-), 3/2(3/2^-)\) and \(\Sigma_c\bar{D}\) with \(3/2(1/2^-)\).
- The pion decay constant used is \(f_\pi = 132\) MeV.
- Coupling constants adopted include \(\beta = 0.9\), \(g = 0.59\), \(g_V = 5.8\), \(\lambda = 0.56\) GeV\(^{-1}\), \(g_S = 0.76\), \(\beta_B = -0.87\), \(\beta_S = 1.74\), \(\ell_B = -3.1\), \(\ell_S = 6.2\), \(g_1 = 0.94\), and \(\lambda_S = 3.31\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
- The \(\Lambda_c \bar{D}\) and \(\Lambda_c \bar{D}^*\) systems yield no bound state solutions for any cutoff in the range 0.8 to 2.2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the OBE model approach previously applied to the \(D^* N\) system (He, 2010) and to \(\Lambda_c N\) molecular states (Liu and Oka, 2011) to the hidden-charm sector composed of anti-charmed mesons and charmed baryons. It also complements studies using the chiral constituent quark model and resonating group method for \(\Sigma_c \bar{D}\) and \(\Lambda_c \bar{D}\) states (Wang, 2011). The predicted \(\Sigma_c \bar{D}^*\) and \(\Sigma_c \bar{D}\) molecular states, with masses around 4 GeV, are suggested as targets for future experiments such as PANDA, Belle II, and SuperB, while analogous hidden-bottom states could be searched for at J-PARC or LHCb.
