# A self-consistent study of magnetic field effects on hybrid stars

> It is understood that strong magnetic fields affect the structure of neutron stars. Nevertheless, many calculations for magnetized neutron stars are still being performed using symmetric solutions of Einstein's equations. In this conference proceeding, we review why this is not the correct procedure and we also discuss the effects of magnetic fields on the stellar population and temperature profiles.

# A self-consistent study of magnetic field effects on hybrid stars

## Key claim

Strong magnetic fields in neutron stars require fully axisymmetric solutions of Einstein-Maxwell equations, not spherical approximations, and self-consistent treatment must include both magnetic field effects on the equation of state and on the stellar structure. The authors show that for stars with central magnetic fields of order 10^18 G, the deformation is so large that spherical solutions are inadequate, and that magnetic fields significantly alter the particle population, neutrino distribution, and temperature profile.

## Method

The authors use the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model, a non-linear realization of the SU(3) sigma model within the mean field approximation, to describe hadrons and quarks interacting via meson exchange, including an effective potential for deconfinement. Magnetic field effects are incorporated through Landau quantization, the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) for hadrons, and magnetization. The coupled Einstein-Maxwell equations are solved using the LORENE C++ class library for numerical relativity, generating a macroscopic poloidal magnetic field through currents, with the equation of state tabulated as a function of baryon chemical potential and magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- Surface magnetic fields of magnetars observed as large as 10^14–10^15 G
- Internal magnetic field estimated up to 10^16 G from one calculation (Makishima 2014)
- Virial theorem arguments allow central magnetic fields up to 10^18 G depending on composition
- Maximum dipole magnetic moment used: μ = 3.5 × 10^32 A m^2
- Maximum central magnetic field found: ~2 × 10^18 G for the most massive star with μ = 3.5 × 10^32 A m^2
- Maximum stellar mass increase of about 0.6 M_⊙ larger than the field-free case for the maximum μ
- Ratio of magnetic pressure to matter pressure at the center: 0.79 for the most massive star with μ = 3.5 × 10^32 A m^2
- For fixed baryon mass of 2.2 M_⊙, maximum central magnetic field ~1 × 10^18 G and mass increase of about 0.1 M_⊙
- Magnetic field effects from the equation of state alone are visible only for μ ≳ 3 × 10^32 A m^2

## Context

This work extends prior self-consistent treatments of magnetized neutron stars by Dexheimer (2012, 2015) and Franzon (2015) using the CMF model, and contrasts with simpler spherical solutions such as those of Oppenheimer (1939) and Tolman (1939). The results enable more realistic modeling of proto-neutron star cooling, as the asymmetric temperature and neutrino distributions from magnetic fields must be included in cooling simulations, and show that exotic matter (hyperons, quarks) appears later in stellar evolution as magnetic fields decay, building on discussions of surface thermal distributions by Aguilera (2008).
