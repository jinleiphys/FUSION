# Relativistic Hamiltonian Dynamics and Few-Nucleon Systems

> We present a preliminary calculation of the electromagnetic form factors of $^3$He and $^3$H, performed within the Light-Front Hamiltonian Dynamics. Relativistic effects show their relevance even at the static limit, increasing at higher values of momentum transfer, as expected.

## Key claim

A preliminary calculation of the electromagnetic form factors of ^3He and ^3H within Light-Front Hamiltonian Dynamics shows that relativistic effects are relevant even at the static limit (Q^2=0) and increase at higher momentum transfer, but the results are limited to S, P, and D waves for static observables and S-wave only for the form factors, with no inclusion of two-body currents or pair contributions.

## Method

The authors constructed a Poincaré covariant electromagnetic current operator using the Bakamjian-Thomas procedure within Light-Front Relativistic Hamiltonian Dynamics, employing a minimal Ansatz built from the free one-body nucleon current and the transverse component of the angular momentum operator. For the trinucleon, they used the AV18 NN interaction wave function from Kievsky et al. (1994) without Coulomb interaction, and evaluated charge and magnetic form factors from matrix elements of the covariant current in a Breit frame with vanishing perpendicular momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron magnetic moment with CD-Bonn interaction: nonrelativistic 0.8523 nuclear magnetons, Light-Front result 0.8670 nuclear magnetons (experimental 0.857406(1))
- Deuteron quadrupole moment with CD-Bonn interaction: nonrelativistic 0.2696 fm^2, Light-Front result 0.2729 fm^2 (experimental 0.2859(3))
- ^3He magnetic moment with S+S'+P+D waves: nonrelativistic -1.769(2) nuclear magnetons, Light-Front result -1.839(2) nuclear magnetons (experimental -2.1276)
- ^3H magnetic moment with S+S'+P+D waves: nonrelativistic 2.579(2) nuclear magnetons, Light-Front result 2.674(2) nuclear magnetons (experimental 2.9789)
- ^3He charge radius with S+S'+P+D waves: nonrelativistic 1.882(4) fm, Light-Front result 1.906(4) fm (experimental 1.959(30) fm)
- ^3H charge radius with S+S'+P+D waves: nonrelativistic 1.714(4) fm, Light-Front result 1.754(4) fm (experimental 1.755(86) fm)
- Wave function percentages for AV18: S+S' 91.4%, P 0.07%, D 8.5%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Light-Front calculations for the deuteron (Pace et al., 2000) to the trinucleon system (A=3), providing the first Poincaré covariant calculation of ^3He and ^3H electromagnetic form factors within Relativistic Hamiltonian Dynamics. The approach contrasts with Bethe-Salpeter equation methods and 3-D reduction schemes (Gross for A=2; Stadler for A=3 in progress), and aims to construct a relativistic standard model for few-nucleon systems that retains successful nonrelativistic phenomenology while satisfying Poincaré covariance. The results enable future inclusion of two-body current operators satisfying the Ward-Takahashi identity and systematic analysis of pair (Z-diagram) contributions.
