# Photoinduced two-proton knockout and ground-state correlations in nuclei

> A factorized and analytical form for the A($γ$,pp) and A(e,e$'$pp) cross section is proposed. In the suggested scheme the two-proton knockout cross sections can be directly analyzed in terms of the ground-state correlation functions. Central, spin-spin and tensor correlations are considered. In the longitudinal channel, the (e,e$'$pp) cross section is predicted to exhibit a peculiar sensitivity to ground-state correlation effects.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a factorized and analytical form for the A(γ,pp) and A(e,e'pp) cross sections that allows direct analysis in terms of ground-state correlation functions, but this factorization relies on the "quasi-deuteron approximation" that the momentum exchanged between the two correlated nucleons is approximately zero, which limits the validity to moderate and larger momentum transfers (ω > 150 MeV).

## Method

The author derives factorized expressions for the (e,e'pp) and (γ,pp) cross sections using the plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) and the spectator approximation, with the key approximation that the relative momentum of the correlated pair is determined by setting the exchanged momentum to zero. The elementary cross section σ_epp is expanded to first order in the correlation functions (central, spin-spin, and tensor) using the "single-pair approximation" (SPA), yielding analytical expressions for the longitudinal (w_L), transverse (w_T), and transverse-transverse (w_TT) response functions, with the longitudinal channel being entirely determined by ground-state correlations.

## Key numbers

- The factorized form is expected to be a reasonable approximation at photon energies ω > 150 MeV (based on prior work on (γ,pn) reactions)
- For the Gaussian central correlation function, the form g(r) = 0.51 e^(1.52 fm^(-2) r^2) was used, as suggested in Ref. [co]
- Two excitation energy regions in the A-2 system were considered for 12C(e,e'pp) data comparison: E_x ≤ 13 MeV (attributed to (1p)^2 knockout) and 23 MeV ≤ E_x ≤ 48 MeV (attributed to (1p)(1s) and (1s)^2 knockout)
- The Δ(1232) current produces the largest contribution at the real photon point (Q^2 = 0) for the transverse response function

## Context

This work extends the factorized form approach previously applied to (e,e'p) reactions (as in the work of Fornal et al., 1983) and to (γ,pn) reactions (as in Boato and Giannini, 1991) to the case of two-proton knockout. The paper enables a simplified analysis framework for (e,e'pp) and (γ,pp) reactions that can be used to optimize kinematical conditions and identify major sensitivities, though the author notes that the factorized predictions should not be considered a fully-fledged alternative to unfactorized calculations (such as those by Ryckebusch et al., 1991 and 1994) that include distorted waves for outgoing particles.
