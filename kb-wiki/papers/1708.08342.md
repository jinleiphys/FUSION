# Tabulated Neutron Star Equations of State Modeled within the Chiral Mean Field Model

> In this special issue article, I review some of the accomplishments of the chiral mean field (CMF) model, which contains nucleon, hyperon, and quark degrees of freedom, and its applications to proto-neutron and neutron stars. I also present a set of equation of state and particle population tables built using the CMF model subject to physical constraints necessary to reproduce different environments, such as those present in cold neutron stars, core-collapse supernova explosions and different stages of compact star mergers.

## Key claim

The chiral mean field (CMF) model provides a unified, self-consistent description of dense matter across different astrophysical environments (cold neutron stars, proto-neutron stars, core-collapse supernovae, and compact star mergers) by including nucleon, hyperon, and quark degrees of freedom, but the tabulated equations of state currently released contain only hadronic degrees of freedom (with quark-containing tables promised for the future).

## Method

The CMF model is a relativistic quantum field theory based on a non-linear realization of the SU(3) sigma model, describing baryons (the full octet) interacting via scalar mesons (σ, ζ, δ) and vector mesons (ω, φ, ρ) in a chirally invariant manner. The model solves coupled equations of motion for each meson field at given baryon chemical potential and temperature, subject to constraints such as charge neutrality, fixed charge fraction, fixed lepton fraction, fixed entropy per baryon, or zero net isospin. Three sets of tabulated equations of state were produced: 1D tables for cold chemically-equilibrated neutron stars at zero temperature, and two sets of 3D tables for supernova/merger simulations (one without leptons, one with electrons), all covering baryon number densities from 0.01 to 3.01 fm⁻³ and temperatures from 0 to 160 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: B/A = -16 MeV
- Compressibility at saturation: K = 300 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation: E_sym = 30 MeV
- Symmetry energy slope: L = 88 MeV
- Pressure for neutron matter at saturation: P = 4.56 MeV/fm³
- Compressibility for neutron matter at saturation: K = 870 MeV
- Hyperon potentials at saturation: U_Λ = -28 MeV, U_Σ = 5 MeV, U_Ξ = -18 MeV
- Critical point for nuclear liquid-gas phase transition: T_c = 16.4 MeV, n_Bc = 0.05 fm⁻³, μ_Bc = 910 MeV
- Maximum neutron star mass (hadronic only): 2.1 M_⊙ with radius 12 km
- Maximum neutron star mass (with quarks): 1.93 M_⊙ with radius 13 km
- Canonical 1.4 M_⊙ star radius: 14 km
- Baryon number density range in 1D tables: 0.03 to 3.03 fm⁻³ with steps of 0.01 fm⁻³
- Temperature range in 3D tables: 0 to 160 MeV with steps of 2 MeV
- Charge fraction range in 3D tables: 0 to 0.53 with steps of 0.01
- Proto-neutron star central temperatures: up to approximately 30 MeV (citing Burrows 1986, Pons 1998)
- Compact star merger temperatures: approximately 80 MeV (citing Galeazzi 2013)

## Context

This work extends the chiral mean field model previously developed by Dexheimer (2008) and collaborators, which has been applied to studies of kaon condensation in neutron stars (Mishra 2009), chiral partners in stars (Dexheimer 2007, 2008, 2014, Mukherjee 2017), and neutron star cooling (Negreiros 2010, Dexheimer 2015). The tabulated equations of state are made available on the CompOSE website and the author's personal website for use in dynamical simulations of core-collapse supernovae, stellar cooling, and compact star mergers. The approach contrasts with conventional astrophysical equations of state that combine different degrees of freedom "by hand" across phase transitions, instead providing a self-consistent description where hadrons and quarks can coexist as a mixture, which is compatible with lattice QCD results suggesting a crossover transition at low baryon chemical potential.
