# Evolution of Surface Deformations of Weakly-Bound Nuclei in the Continuum

> We study weakly-bound deformed nuclei based on the coordinate-space Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach, in which a large box is employed for treating the continuum and surface diffuseness. Approaching the limit of core-halo deformation decoupling, calculations found an exotic "egg"-like structure consisting of a spherical core plus a prolate halo in $^{38}$Ne, in which the resonant continuum plays an essential role. Generally the halo probability and the decoupling effect in heavy nuclei are reduced compared to light nuclei, due to denser level densities around Fermi surfaces. However, deformed halos in medium-mass nuclei are possible with sparse levels of negative parity, for example, in $^{110}$Ge. The surface deformations of pairing density distributions are also influenced by the decoupling effect and are sensitive to the effective pairing Hamiltonian.

# Evolution of Surface Deformations of Weakly-Bound Nuclei in the Continuum

## Key claim
The paper claims that an exotic "egg"-like halo structure consisting of a spherical core plus a prolate halo exists in $^{38}$Ne, but this result depends on the use of the surface pairing interaction and a large 30 fm coordinate-space box; the prediction is model-dependent, as relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations do not find a pronounced halo in $^{38}$Ne.

## Method
The authors performed self-consistent coordinate-space Skyrme Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the HFB-AX code with B-spline techniques on a 2D lattice box of 30x30 fm, with a maximum mesh size of 0.6 fm and B-spline order of 12. The SLy4 force was used for the particle-hole channel, and density-dependent pairing interactions with both mixed and surface variants were employed, with pairing strengths fitted to the neutron gap of $^{120}$Sn. Hybrid MPI+OpenMP parallel computing on the Tianhe-1A supercomputer was required to handle the computational cost, which increased by an estimated factor of 40 compared to the typical 20 fm box size.

## Key numbers
- Box size of 30x30 fm used for calculations, compared to typical 2D box sizes of less than 20 fm in the literature
- Maximum mesh size of 0.6 fm and B-spline order of 12
- In $^{38}$Ne, the integrated neutron number inside a spherical core of radius 9.5 fm is 26.1, leaving about 2 neutrons in the halo
- Quadrupole deformation of neutrons in $^{38}$Ne is $\beta_2^{\rm n}=0.24$, entirely due to surface deformation
- With surface pairing, the isovector quadrupole deformations are 0.24 for $^{38}$Ne and -0.09 for $^{44}$Mg
- Quadrupole deformations of neutron pairing densities are 0.48 for $^{38}$Ne and 0.36 for $^{44}$Mg
- Near-threshold quasiparticle continuum states with energies below 1.5 MeV are mainly responsible for halo structures and surface deformations

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of deformed halo structures in light nuclei such as $^{14}$Be (Misu 1997) and $^{31}$Ne, and addresses the question of whether two-neutron deformed halos exist in heavier nuclei. The calculations go beyond spherical HFB studies of heavy halos (Meng 2002, Mizutori 2000, Schunck 2003, Rotival 2009, Zhang 2012) by performing the first self-consistent deformed HFB calculations with a large 30 fm box. The results enable future experimental searches at facilities like FRIB for exotic structures such as the "egg"-like halo in $^{38}$Ne or its neighbor $^{39}$Na, and suggest that deformed halos in medium-mass nuclei like $^{110}$Ge are possible when negative-parity levels are sparse.
