# The Neutrino Matrix

> To answer the very interesting questions raised by the discovery of neutrino mass, an effective, coherent strategy is needed. To foster the development of such a strategy, the American Physical Society's Divisions of Nuclear Physics and of Particles and Fields, together with the Divisions of Astrophysics and the Physics of Beams, have sponsored this yearlong Study on the Physics of Neutrinos. The study has endeavored to identify the most important open questions, to evaluate the physics reach of various proposed ways of answering them, and to determine an effective, fruitful U.S. role within a global experimental program. An important -- if challenging -- goal of the study has been to achieve consensus regarding the future of neutrino physics.

## Key claim

The paper presents a consensus-based strategic roadmap for U.S. neutrino physics, arguing that a coordinated program of solar, atmospheric, reactor, accelerator, and double-beta decay experiments is necessary to answer the open questions raised by the discovery of neutrino mass, but it hedges that the optimal strategy depends critically on the unknown value of the mixing angle θ₁₃, which determines whether conventional beams or more advanced facilities (Neutrino Factory, Beta Beam) will be required.

## Method

The American Physical Society's Divisions of Nuclear Physics, Particles and Fields, Astrophysics, and the Physics of Beams sponsored a yearlong Study on the Physics of Neutrinos, convening working groups on solar and atmospheric experiments, reactor experiments, superbeams, neutrino factories and beta beams, and neutrinoless double-beta decay and direct mass searches. Each working group produced an executive summary identifying the most important open questions, evaluating the physics reach of proposed experimental approaches, and formulating prioritized recommendations for a U.S. role within a global experimental program.

## Key numbers

- A reactor experiment with sensitivity of sin² 2θ₁₃ = 0.01 is recommended as a key element of the future neutrino program.
- The T2K and Nova long-baseline experiments can make significant measurements of CP violation and mass hierarchy if sin² 2θ₁₃ > 0.05, and with enhanced beam rates can improve their reach to sin² 2θ₁₃ > 0.02.
- For neutrinoless double-beta decay, the ~100-500 meV range for the effective Majorana mass ⟨m_ββ⟩ corresponds to the quasi-degenerate spectrum, requiring approximately 200 kg of the decaying isotope with a 3-5 year timeline.
- The ~20-55 meV range for ⟨m_ββ⟩ arises from atmospheric neutrino oscillation results and will require about 1 ton of isotope mass.
- The ~2-5 meV range for ⟨m_ββ⟩ arises from solar neutrino oscillation results and will require approximately 100 tons of the decaying isotope.
- The KATRIN tritium beta-decay experiment has a design sensitivity approaching 200 meV for direct neutrino mass measurement.
- The next generation of cosmological measurements hopes to observe a sum of neutrino masses as small as 40 meV.
- The 2001 HEPAP Report recommended an annual U.S. investment of $8M on Neutrino Factory R&D.
- The Standard Solar Model predicts the pp neutrino flux with a theoretical uncertainty of 1%, and pp and ⁷Be neutrinos together constitute more than 98% of the solar neutrino flux.
- A measurement of the ⁷Be solar neutrino flux with a precision of 5% is identified as an essential prerequisite.

## Context

This study extends the work of prior neutrino physics assessments, including the 2001 HEPAP Report which recommended Neutrino Factory R&D funding levels, and builds on discoveries from the Davis solar neutrino experiment, IMB, Kamiokande, and Super-Kamiokande. The paper enables a coordinated U.S. strategy that connects near-term experiments (KAMLAND, MINOS, Mini-BooONE) with longer-term facilities (superbeams, neutrino factories, ton-scale double-beta decay detectors), and identifies the measurement of θ₁₃ as the critical branching point that will determine whether conventional superbeams suffice or whether more advanced facilities like the Neutrino Factory become necessary.
