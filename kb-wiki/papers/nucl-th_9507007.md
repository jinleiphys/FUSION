# Effect of Nucleon Structure Variation in Super-allowed Fermi Beta-decay

> There is a well known anomaly between the value of the Fermi decay constant extracted from super-allowed Fermi beta-decay of nuclear isotriplets and that required by unitarity of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix. This discrepancy remains at the level of a few tenths of a percent after the most rigorous investigation of conventional nuclear and radiative corrections. Within the framework of the quark-meson coupling model of nuclear matter, which has been previously applied successfully to phenomena such as nuclear saturation and nuclear charge symmetry violation, we show that it is possible to understand a significant fraction of the observed anomaly.

## Key claim

Within the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model of nuclear matter, the change in nucleon internal structure due to nuclear binding produces a correction to the Fermi beta-decay form-factor that can account for a significant fraction of the observed anomaly between the Fermi decay constant extracted from super-allowed Fermi beta-decay and the value required by unitarity of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix, though the authors note that unambiguous conclusions require extension to finite nuclei.

## Method

The authors used the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, in which nuclear matter consists of non-overlapping nucleon bags bound by self-consistent exchange of sigma, omega, rho, and delta mesons in the mean-field approximation. They calculated the overlap integrals of quark wave functions for the d to u conversion and spectator quarks in symmetric nuclear matter as a function of density, for three choices of the free average bag radius (0.6, 0.8, and 1.0 fm). The coupling constants were determined to fit the binding energy (-16 MeV) and saturation density (0.17 fm^-3) of equilibrium nuclear matter, and the results were expressed as the fractional increase in the ft-value with density.

## Key numbers

- The deviation of the overlap integral ratio I_ii'(rho_B)/I_ii'(0) from unity is roughly linear in density, with coefficient a_ii' approximately 2.4 x 10^-4, 2.9 x 10^-4, and 3.3 x 10^-4 for free bag radii of 0.6, 0.8, and 1.0 fm, respectively.
- The fractional increase in the ft-value with density is given by b approximately 1.5 x 10^-3, 1.8 x 10^-3, and 2.0 x 10^-3 for free bag radii of 0.6, 0.8, and 1.0 fm, respectively.
- The increase in the ft-value at half nuclear matter density (rho_0/2) ranges from 0.075% to 0.10%, while at full nuclear matter density (rho_0) it lies between 0.15% and 0.20%.
- This is compared with a violation of unitarity of the CKM matrix of 0.35 plus or minus 0.15% in the most recent analysis by Towner and Hardy.
- The bag constant B^(1/4) was found to be 210.7, 169.8, and 143.6 MeV for free bag radii of 0.6, 0.8, and 1.0 fm, respectively.
- The effective quark mass parameters m_d were found to be 9.252, 9.242, and 9.233 MeV for free bag radii of 0.6, 0.8, and 1.0 fm, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling model previously applied to nuclear saturation and the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly in mirror nuclei (Saito and Thomas, 1994) to the problem of the CKM unitarity discrepancy. The authors note that conventional nuclear theory with point-like nucleons has been unable to resolve the anomaly after intensive study of nuclear mismatch corrections (Towner and Hardy, Wilkinson, and Savard et al.). The results suggest that the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer anomaly may be a "smoking gun" for quark degrees of freedom in nuclei, and the authors emphasize that the formalism must be extended from infinite nuclear matter to finite nuclei for quantitative application.
