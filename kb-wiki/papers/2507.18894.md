# Structure of heavy quarkonia in a strong magnetic field

> We investigate the structural modifications of heavy quarkonia in the presence of strong magnetic fields using a constituent quark model. By incorporating the effects of spin mixing and quark Landau levels, we employ a nonrelativistic Hamiltonian that captures the essential features of quark dynamics in a magnetic field. The two-body Schrödinger equation is solved using the cylindrical Gaussian expansion method, which respects the cylindrical symmetry induced by a magnetic field. We extract the corresponding light-front wave function (LFWF) densities and analyze their transverse and longitudinal structures, revealing characteristic features such as transverse momentum broadening. While the longitudinal structure is only slightly modified within the nonrelativistic Hamiltonian, we discuss some corrections that can significantly affect its longitudinal structure. Furthermore, we discuss the structure modifications of excited states and find notable changes in the LFWF densities, and state reshuffling near avoided crossings. These results demonstrate the sensitivity of hadron structure to external magnetic fields and help bridge our understanding to relativistic approaches.

## Key claim
The authors find that heavy quarkonia in strong magnetic fields exhibit transverse momentum broadening and structural modifications in their light-front wave function densities, but they emphasize that the longitudinal structure is only slightly modified within their nonrelativistic Hamiltonian approach, with substantial modifications requiring relativistic corrections not fully included in their model.

## Method
The authors solve a nonrelativistic two-body Schrödinger equation for heavy quarkonia in a magnetic field using the cylindrical Gaussian expansion method (CGEM), which respects the cylindrical symmetry induced by the field. They incorporate spin mixing between pseudoscalar and longitudinal vector states via the quark magnetic moment interaction, and quark Landau levels through the kinetic term. The resulting rest-frame wave functions are transformed into light-front wave functions (LFWFs) using a variable change from momentum-space coordinates to longitudinal momentum fraction and transverse momentum, combined with the Melosh transformation for spin-orbital structure.

## Key numbers
- Magnetic field strengths up to \(eB = 10\) GeV\(^2\) are explored, though the nonrelativistic approximation works best when the field is smaller than the quark mass
- The mass shift for transverse vector charmonia increases approximately linearly with the magnetic field, with the excited state (\(\psi(2S)_T\)) showing larger shifts than the ground state (\(J/\psi_T\))
- Avoided crossings between pseudoscalar and longitudinal vector states occur around \(eB = 1\)–\(3\) GeV\(^2\) for charmonia
- The constituent quark model parameters include charm quark mass \(m_c = 1.784\) GeV and bottom quark mass \(m_b = 4.808\) GeV, with string tension \(\sqrt{\sigma} = 0.402\) GeV
- Vacuum masses reproduced include \(J/\psi\) at 3.097 GeV (experiment: 3.097 GeV) and \(\Upsilon(1S)\) at 9.460 GeV (experiment: 9.460 GeV)

## Context
This work extends previous studies of heavy quarkonia in magnetic fields by Suzuki (2016) and Yoshida (2016), which examined spin mixing and energy shifts using potential models with the CGEM. The authors build a connection between magnetically-modified rest-frame wave functions and light-front wave functions, enabling access to partonic observables such as parton distribution functions (PDFs). This approach complements the direct light-front Hamiltonian diagonalization method proposed by Wen (2025) and provides input for lattice QCD simulations using Large Momentum Effective Theory (LaMET) as developed by Ji (2013).
