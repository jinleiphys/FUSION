# Benchmark for a quasi-bound state of the $\overset{\_}{K}pp$ system

> We present three-body nonrelativistic calculations within the framework of a potential model for the kaonic cluster ${K^-}pp$ using two completely different methods: the method of hyperspherical harmonics in the momentum representation and the method of Faddeev equations in configuration space. To perform a numerical benchmark, different $NN$ and antikaon-nucleon interactions are applied. The results of the calculations for the ground state energy for the ${K^-}pp$ system obtained by both methods are in reasonable agreement. Although the ground state energy is not sensitive to the $NN$ interaction, it shows very strong dependence on the $\overset{\_}{K}N$ potential. We show that the dominant clustering of the ${K^-}pp$ system in the configuration $Λ(1405)+p$ allows us to calculate the binding energy to good accuracy within a simple cluster approach for the differential Faddeev equations. The theoretical discrepancies in the binding energy and width for the ${K^-}pp$ system related to the different $NN$ and $\overset{\_}{K}N$ interactions are addressed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a benchmark calculation for the ${K^-}pp$ quasi-bound state using two different three-body methods, finding that the ground state energy shows very strong dependence on the $\bar{K}N$ potential but is not sensitive to the $NN$ interaction, with the qualification that the dominant clustering in the $\Lambda(1405)+p$ configuration allows accurate binding energy calculations within a simple cluster approach.

## Method

The authors performed nonrelativistic three-body calculations for the ${K^-}pp$ system using two completely different methods: the method of hyperspherical harmonics in the momentum representation and the method of Faddeev equations in configuration space. They applied three different $NN$ potentials (Argonne V14, Malfliet-Tjon MT-I-III, and Tamagaki G3RS) and two different $\bar{K}N$ interactions (the energy-independent Akaishi-Yamazaki phenomenological potential and the energy-dependent chiral HW potential) to perform a numerical benchmark. The Faddeev equations were solved in both an $s$-wave approach and a cluster approach assuming the dominant configuration $\Lambda(1405)+p$.

## Key numbers

- The AY $\bar{K}N$ potential has depth parameters $U^{I=0} = -595 - i83$ MeV and $U^{I=1} = -175 - i105$ MeV with range parameter $b = 0.66$ fm
- The HW $\bar{K}N$ potential has depth parameters $U^{I=0} = -908 - i181$ MeV and $U^{I=1} = -415 - i170$ MeV with range parameter $b = 0.47$ fm
- The two-body threshold for the effective averaged $\bar{K}N$ potential is about -9.6 MeV, compared to -30.2 MeV for the AY potential in the $I=0$ channel
- The FINUDA collaboration reported a binding energy of $115_{-5}^{+6}(\text{stat})_{-4}^{+3}(\text{syst})$ MeV and width $67_{-11}^{+14}(\text{stat})_{-3}^{+2}(\text{syst})$ MeV for the ${K^-}pp$ system
- The DISTO collaboration reported binding energy $103 \pm 3(\text{stat}) \pm 5(\text{syst})$ MeV and width $118 \pm 8(\text{stat}) \pm 10(\text{syst})$ MeV
- The predicted binding energy values from various theoretical approaches range from 9 to 95 MeV and widths from 20 to 110 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of the ${K^-}pp$ system that have used variational methods (Akaishi and Yamazaki, 2002; Dote et al., 2004), Faddeev equations (Nina et al.; Ikeda, Sato et al.), and hyperspherical harmonics (Barnea and Kezerashvili; Kezerashvili and Tsiklauri, 2014). The paper addresses the serious theoretical discrepancies in predicted binding energies and widths that arise from different $\bar{K}N$ interaction models and calculation methods. The benchmark enables future comparisons between different theoretical approaches and helps resolve the controversial experimental situation regarding the existence and properties of the ${K^-}pp$ bound state, which has been reported by FINUDA and DISTO collaborations but not confirmed by J-PARC E15 preliminary results.
