# Systematic study of the elliptic flow parameter using a heavy-ion collision model

> Elliptic flow parameter, $v_{2}$ is consider as a sensitive probe for early dynamics of the heavy-ion collision. In this work we have discussed the effect of detector efficiency, procedure of centrality determination, effect of resonance decay, procedure to obtain event plane resolution on the measured $v_{2}$ by standard event plane method within the framework of a transport model. The measured value of $v_{2}$ depends on the detector efficiency in particle number counting. The effect of centrality determination is found to be negligible. The method of event-by-event correction of event plane resolution for wide centrality bin yields in results closer to the true value of $v_{2}$. The effect of resonance decay is seen to decrease the $v_{2}$ of $π$, $K$ and $p$. We also propose a procedure to correct for an event bias effect on $v_{2}$ while comparing the minimum bias centrality $v_{2}$ values for different multi-strange hadrons. Finally we have presented a model based confirmation of the recently proposed relation between $v_{2}$ obtained using event plane method and scalar product method to the true value of $v_{2}$.

## Key claim

The paper systematically studies how various experimental conditions (detector efficiency, centrality determination method, event plane resolution correction, resonance decay, and event bias) affect the measured elliptic flow parameter \(v_{2}\) in heavy-ion collisions, using transport models where the true \(v_{2}\) is known. The abstract glosses over the fact that the quantitative impact of each effect is model-dependent and that the proposed corrections are demonstrated specifically for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV.

## Method

The authors used the A Multi Phase Transport (AMPT) model (string melting version 25t7d) and the Ultra relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model (version 3.3) to simulate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV at midrapidity. They introduced realistic detector efficiency functions, varied the centrality determination method (using charged particle multiplicity at different pseudorapidity ranges and spectator neutrons), compared event-by-event versus average event plane resolution corrections, switched resonance decays on and off in UrQMD, and applied an event bias correction based on participant eccentricity to study the effect on \(v_{2}\) for different hadron species.

## Key numbers

* The change in kaon \(v_{2}\) due to \(K^{0}_{S}\) reconstruction efficiency is about 10% to 30% as a function of \(p_{T}\), while for charged kaon reconstruction efficiency the change is less than 5%.
* The maximum difference in \(\langle v_{2} \rangle\) due to different centrality selection procedures is approximately 2%.
* The event bias correction factor for \(\Omega\) is 1.15, for \(\Xi\) it is 1.023, and for \(\phi\) it is 1.010, based on AMPT model data.
* Resonance decay causes a change of 10% to 15% in the \(v_{2}\) values of pions, less than 5% for kaons, and the proton \(v_{2}\) is almost unaffected, as seen in UrQMD model results.
* The study used 1.8 million minimum bias (0-80%) events from AMPT and 1.5 million minimum bias (0-80%) events from UrQMD.

## Context

This work extends systematic studies of experimental biases on elliptic flow measurements, building on prior transport model analyses and experimental methods. It provides a model-based verification of the relation proposed by Luzum (2012) that the event plane method yields \(\langle v_{2} \rangle\) in the high-resolution limit and \(\sqrt{\langle v_{2}^{2} \rangle}\) in the low-resolution limit. The results enable experiments to apply appropriate corrections for detector efficiency, event plane resolution, and event bias when comparing \(v_{2}\) values across different hadron species and centrality bins.
