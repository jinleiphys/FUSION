# Radiative capture on neutron rich nuclei

> The $({\rm n},γ)$--cross sections for neutron--rich oxygen isotopes have been calculated in the direct capture model. The experimental data for ${^{18}{\rm O}}({\rm n},γ){^{19}{\rm O}}$ can be reproduced using this model. Compared to previous work the cross section for ${^{19-21}{\rm O}}({\rm n},γ){^{20-22}{\rm O}}$ are enhanced considerably by factors between four and 300.

## Key claim

The direct capture model, using folding potentials and spectroscopic factors from shell-model calculations, can reproduce the experimental cross section for 18O(n,γ)19O and predicts that cross sections for 19-21O(n,γ)20-22O are enhanced by factors between four and 300 compared to previous work by Rauscher et al. (1994), primarily because earlier work assumed too low spectroscopic factors and neglected important transitions to excited final states.

## Method

The authors calculated direct capture (DC) cross sections for neutron capture on 18-21O using folding potentials derived from nuclear densities (experimental charge distributions for stable nuclei, relativistic mean field theory for unstable nuclei) folded with an energy- and density-dependent NN interaction. Spectroscopic factors for one-nucleon transfer were obtained from shell-model calculations in the sd-shell using the Wildenthal effective interaction, and isolated compound-nucleus resonances were incorporated into the DC cross section using the bound states embedded into continuum (BSEC) approach. The normalization factor λ for the folding potential of 18O+n was adjusted to reproduce the total elastic scattering cross section, while for the other reactions λ was adjusted to reproduce the same volume integral J = 578.33 MeV fm³.

## Key numbers

- Thermal cross section for 18O(n,γ)19O: σ_therm = 0.16 mb at 0.025 eV, reproduced with a spectroscopic factor of 0.331 for the transition to the 3/2⁻ state bound by about 12.1 keV.
- Cross section at 23.3 keV for 18O(n,γ)19O: ≈ 8 ± 1 μbarn.
- Total cross section at 30 keV for 18O(n,γ)19O: 53.33 μb/√MeV (this work) vs. 52.40 μb/√MeV (previous work).
- Total cross section at 30 keV for 19O(n,γ)20O: 30.72 μb/√MeV (this work) vs. 6.63 μb/√MeV (previous work).
- Total cross section at 30 keV for 20O(n,γ)21O: 45.63 μb/√MeV (this work) vs. 11.02 μb/√MeV (previous work).
- Total cross section at 30 keV for 21O(n,γ)22O: 48.67 μb/√MeV (this work) vs. 0.16 μb/√MeV (previous work).
- Volume integral of the folding potential for 18O+n: J = 578.33 MeV fm³.

## Context

This work extends the direct capture model, previously applied to solar nuclear reactions and the triple-alpha process, to neutron-rich oxygen isotopes relevant for the inhomogeneous big-bang scenario and the α-rich freeze-out in supernova type II neutrino bubbles. The calculated cross sections are considerably higher than those from Rauscher et al. (1994), which should lead to modifications of existing reaction network calculations for nucleosynthesis in these scenarios. The method enables improved predictions for neutron capture on unstable nuclei far from stability, where compound-nucleus formation may be suppressed due to the lack of available CN levels.
