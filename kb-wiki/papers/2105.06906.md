# Charged pion electric polarizability from lattice QCD

> We present a calculation of the charged pion electric polarizability using the background field method. To extract the mass-shift induced by the electric field for the accelerated charged particle we fit the lattice QCD correlators using correlators derived from an effective model. The methodology outlined in this study (boundary conditions, fitting procedure, etc.) is designed to ensure that the results are invariant under gauge transformations of the background field. We apply the method to four $N_f=2$ dynamical ensembles to extract $α_{π^\pm}$ at pion mass of $315$ MeV.

## Key claim

The paper presents a lattice QCD calculation of the charged pion electric polarizability using the background field method, but the result is extracted at a single pion mass of 315 MeV and relies on fitting lattice correlators to an effective model that accounts for finite-volume effects and the acceleration of the charged particle in the electric field.

## Method

The authors introduce an imaginary background electric field by multiplying SU(3) gauge links with U(1) phase factors and use Dirichlet boundary conditions in both the spatial (x) and temporal directions to ensure gauge invariance of the results. They fit lattice QCD two-point correlators for the charged pion to correlators derived from a discretized two-dimensional relativistic scalar effective model, using a gauge-invariant chi-squared minimization procedure that includes both zero-field and non-zero-field data simultaneously to extract the small mass shift. The effective spatial size of the Dirichlet box is calibrated by matching the zero-field pion mass from the effective model to the mass obtained from periodic boundary conditions on the same ensembles.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass: 315 MeV for all four ensembles used in the calculation.
* Lattice spacing: a = 0.1245 fm for all ensembles.
* Ensemble EN1: 16 x 16^2 x 32 lattice, 450 configurations, effective spatial size L_tilde/a = 14.00(13).
* Ensemble EN2: 24 x 24^2 x 48 lattice, 300 configurations, effective spatial size L_tilde/a = 21.50(17).
* Ensemble EN3: 30 x 24^2 x 48 lattice, 300 configurations, effective spatial size L_tilde/a = 26.50(18).
* Ensemble EN4: 48 x 24^2 x 48 lattice, 300 configurations, effective spatial size L_tilde/a = 44.00(72).
* Zero-field pion mass from periodic boundary conditions on the three larger ensembles: a m_pi = 0.1936(2); for the smallest ensemble EN1: a m_pi = 0.1986(22).

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of hadron polarizabilities using the background field method, particularly addressing the challenge of extracting the polarizability of charged particles that accelerate in an electric field. The methodology builds on earlier approaches by Detmold (2009) and Alexandru (2015) for treating charged particle correlators, and uses Dirichlet boundary conditions as an alternative to the quantized-field approach used in some prior work. The results enable future lattice calculations of charged pion polarizability at physical pion masses and can be extended to other charged hadrons such as kaons and protons.
