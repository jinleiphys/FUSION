# A mixing interpolation method to mimic pasta phases in compact star matter

> We present a new method to interpolate between two matter phases that allows for a description of mixed phases and can be used, e.g., for mimicking transitions between pasta structures occuring in the crust as well as in the inner core of compact stars. This interpolation method is based on assuming switch functions that are used to define a mixture of subphases while fulfilling constraints of thermodynamic stability. The width of the transition depends on a free parameter, the pressure increment relative to the critical pressure of a Maxwell construction. As an example we present a trigonometric function ansatz for the switch function together with a pressure increment during the transition. We note that the resulting mixed phase equation of state bears similarities with the appearance of substitutional compounds in neutron star crusts and with the sequence of transitions between different pasta phases in the hadron-to-quark matter transition. We apply this method to the case of a hadron-to-quark matter transition and test the robustness of the compact star mass twin phenomenon against the appearance of pasta phases modeled in this way.

# Mixing interpolation method to mimic pasta phases in compact star matter

## Key claim
The mixing interpolation method (MIM) can mimic pasta phase effects in the hadron-to-quark matter transition in compact stars, producing an intermediate stiffening of the equation of state that resembles substitutional compound structures in neutron star crusts; however, the high-mass twin star phenomenon is robust against such mixed phase effects only up to a dimensionless pressure increment of about 6%, above which the twin phenomenon disappears.

## Method
The authors construct a mixing interpolation between hadronic and quark matter equations of state using trigonometric switch functions that smoothly transition between phases within a bounded chemical potential region around the Maxwell construction critical point. A pressure increment function, parameterized by a single free parameter ΔP (the dimensionless pressure increment relative to the critical pressure), is added to model the contribution from structured mixed phases. The method is applied to a 4-polytrope hybrid equation of state (the ACB4 model) and the resulting equations of state are used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for static, spherically-symmetric compact star configurations.

## Key numbers
- Critical chemical potential μ_crit = 1189 MeV and critical pressure P_crit = 63.18 MeV/fm^3 for the Maxwell construction
- For ΔP = 0.01, the mixed phase region extends Γ_- = 11.9126 MeV and Γ_+ = 11.2956 MeV; for ΔP = 0.08, Γ_- = 130.078 MeV and Γ_+ = 78.2934 MeV
- The high-mass twin phenomenon is lost for ΔP ≥ 6% (ΔP ≥ 0.06)
- The maximum mass constraint from PSR J0348+0432 is 2.01 M_⊙ (Antoniadis 2013)
- The ACB4 model parameters include polytropic indices Γ_1 = 4.921, Γ_2 = 0.0, Γ_3 = 4.000, Γ_4 = 2.800 with corresponding density thresholds n_1 = 0.1650 fm^-3, n_2 = 0.3174 fm^-3, n_3 = 0.5344 fm^-3, n_4 = 0.7500 fm^-3

## Context
This work extends the replacement interpolation method (RIM) developed by Ayriyan et al. (2017) and Abgaryan et al. (2018) for mimicking pasta phase effects in the hadron-to-quark matter transition. Unlike the RIM which uses polynomial interpolation, the MIM produces an intermediate stiffening of the equation of state that qualitatively matches realistic pasta phase calculations by Maslov et al. (2018) with surface tension σ = 40 MeV/fm^2. The method is motivated by the construction of sequential transitions describing substitutional compounds in neutron star crusts (Chamel 2016) and builds on earlier interpolation techniques by Masuda et al. (2012) and Blaschke et al. (2013). The results enable future systematic investigations of hybrid star cooling behavior to distinguish between different mixed phase treatments.
