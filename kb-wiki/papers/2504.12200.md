# Global $Λ$ polarization in heavy-ion collisions at high baryon density

> Based on the model of three-fluid dynamics (3FD), the global $Λ$ polarization ($P_Λ$) is calculated in Au+Au collisions at 3 $\leq\sqrt{s_{NN}}\leq$ 9 GeV, in which high baryon density is achieved. Various contributions to $P_Λ$ are considered: those from the thermal vorticity, meson field, thermal shear and spin-Hall effect. Feed-down from higher-lying resonances is also taken into account. The results are compared with available data. Special attention is payed to the collision energies of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 3, 3.2, 3.5, 3.9, and 4.5 GeV, for which a thorough scan of the energy, rapidity, and centrality dependence of $P_Λ$ is performed. The results for 3 GeV reasonably well reproduce the corresponding STAR data. While the results at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 3.2, 3.5, 3.9, and 4.5 GeV can be considered as predictions for results of measurements within the STAR fixed-target (STAR-FXT) programthat are expected in the nearest future. It is predicted that a broad maximum of $P_Λ$ is reached at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\approx$ 3--3.9 GeV, exact position of which depends on the centrality and width of the midrapidity range of observation. Impact of the meson-field, thermal-shear and spin-Hall-effect contributions to $P_Λ$ is also studied.

## Key claim

The global \(\Lambda\) polarization in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3\)–\(9\) GeV is predicted to reach a broad maximum at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 3\)–\(3.9\) GeV, with the exact position depending on centrality and the width of the midrapidity observation window, rather than rising monotonically down to the lowest energies.

## Method

The author used the three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at beam energy scan energies from 3 to 9 GeV. The global \(\Lambda\) polarization was computed from the thermal vorticity, meson-field interaction, thermal shear (shear-induced polarization, SIP), and spin-Hall effect (SHE) contributions, with feed-down from higher-lying resonances included. The freeze-out was treated as isochronous, and the integration over the freeze-out hypersurface was performed with a rapidity acceptance cut based on the hydrodynamical rapidity.

## Key numbers

- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3\) GeV, the calculated \(P_\Lambda\) reasonably well reproduces the corresponding STAR data (STAR, 2021).
- Predictions are provided for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.2\), \(3.5\), \(3.9\), and \(4.5\) GeV as results expected from the STAR fixed-target (STAR-FXT) program.
- A broad maximum of \(P_\Lambda\) is reached at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 3\)–\(3.9\) GeV.
- The vector-meson mass used is \(m_V = m_\omega = 783\) MeV, with coupling constants \(\bar{g}_V = g_{VN} = 8.646\) and \(g_{V\Lambda} = 0.67 g_{VN}\) (Cohen and Weber, 1991).

## Context

This work extends previous 3FD calculations by Ivanov and Soldatov (2022) that studied the meson-field contribution at 2.4–7.7 GeV, and now includes the thermal-shear and spin-Hall-effect contributions (Liu and Yin, 2021; Liu and Yin, 2020) which were not evaluated in that earlier study. The predictions for 3.2–4.5 GeV are directly relevant to the STAR-FXT program and to forthcoming experiments at NICA (BM@N and MPD). The results contradict the scenario of Guo et al. (2021) which predicted a maximum around 7.7 GeV, and instead support a peak at lower energies consistent with the trend seen in STAR data at 3 GeV (STAR, 2021) and HADES data at 2.4 GeV (HADES, 2022).
