# Faddeev Calculation of $_Λ^3$H incorporating 2π-exchange $Λ$NN Interaction

> Faddeev calculations of hypertriton ($_Λ^3$H) separation energy are performed, incorporating $2π$-exchange $Λ$NN three-baryon force. Repulsive contributions of the three-baryon force in the order of 20 keV are found, depending on the NN interactions employed. The effect is not negligible compared with the small separation-energy of $_Λ^3$H.

## Key claim

The 2π-exchange ΛNN three-baryon force (3BF) provides a repulsive contribution to the hypertriton separation energy on the order of 20 keV, which is not negligible compared to the experimental separation energy of approximately 148 keV, but the exact magnitude depends on the coupling constants used (here set to 3b₀+b_D=0 and 2b₂+3b₄=-3.0 GeV⁻¹ based on a decuplet saturation model).

## Method

Faddeev equations for the ΛNN bound state were solved including the 2π-exchange ΛNN three-baryon force at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in chiral effective field theory, with the 3BF placed in the channel where the Λ hyperon is the spectator. The calculations employed 16 different semilocal momentum-space regularized chiral NN interactions (N²LO, N³LO, N⁴LO, and N⁴LO+ at cutoff scales 400, 450, 500, and 550 MeV) combined with three S=-1 YN interactions: chiral NLO13, NLO19, and the phenomenological Nijmegen NSC89 potential.

## Key numbers

- Hypertriton separation energy without 3BF using N⁴LO+(400) NN and NLO13(550) YN: 108 keV
- Hypertriton separation energy with 3BF using N⁴LO+(400) NN and NLO13(550) YN: 84 keV (repulsive 3BF effect of 24 keV)
- Hypertriton separation energy without 3BF using N⁴LO+(550) NN and NLO13(550) YN: 79 keV
- Hypertriton separation energy with 3BF using N⁴LO+(550) NN and NLO13(550) YN: 63 keV (repulsive 3BF effect of 16 keV)
- Hypertriton separation energy without 3BF using CD-Bonn NN and NSC89 YN: 153 keV
- Hypertriton separation energy with 3BF using CD-Bonn NN and NSC89 YN: 126 keV (repulsive 3BF effect of 27 keV)
- Experimental average separation energy from Mainz group: 148 ± 40 keV
- 3BF repulsive contribution for ChEFT ΛN interactions: approximately 20 keV
- 3BF repulsive contribution for NSC89 ΛN interaction: approximately 30 keV

## Context

This work extends previous Faddeev calculations of the hypertriton (Kamada, Miyagawa, Kohno 1993, 1995) by incorporating the 2π-exchange ΛNN three-baryon force from chiral effective field theory, following the formalism used for the triton with three-nucleon forces (Stadler, Gloeckle, Sauer 1991). The results contradict an earlier estimate by the same authors (Kamada, Kohno, Miyagawa 2022) where a Λ-deuteron folding potential was found to be weakly attractive using an older chiral N³LO interaction; with the newer semilocal chiral NN potentials the folding potential becomes repulsive. The work enables future inclusion of the remaining NNLO 3BFs (1π-exchange and contact terms) and provides a foundation for determining ΛNN 3BF coupling constants from light hypernuclei, which is relevant to the hyperon puzzle in neutron star matter.
