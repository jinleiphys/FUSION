# Rolling Skyrmions and the Nuclear Spin-Orbit Force

> We compute the nuclear spin-orbit coupling from the Skyrme model. Previous attempts to do this were based on the product ansatz, and as such were limited to a system of two well-separated nuclei. Our calculation utilises a new method, and is applicable to the phenomenologically important situation of a single nucleon orbiting a large nucleus. We find that, to second order in perturbation theory, the coefficient of the spin-orbit coupling induced by pion field interactions has the wrong sign, but as the strength of the pion-nucleon interactions increases the correct sign is recovered non-perturbatively.

## Key claim

The spin-orbit coupling induced by pion field interactions in the Skyrme model has the wrong sign when calculated to second order in perturbation theory, but the correct sign is recovered non-perturbatively when the strength of the pion-nucleon interactions is sufficiently large.

## Method

The authors model a single Skyrmion interacting with a planar nuclear surface approximated by a half-filled FCC lattice of Skyrmions in the lightly bound Skyrme model. They derive a potential that depends on the Skyrmion's position and orientation, then quantise the system and compute energy eigenvalues and expectation values of spin and momentum using both a matrix method (valid for strong coupling) and perturbation theory (valid for weak coupling). The analysis is first performed on a two-dimensional toy model of a coloured disc on a rail, then extended to the full three-dimensional Skyrmion system.

## Key numbers

- The critical potential strength for the bifurcation in the two-dimensional disc model is \(V_0 = \frac{1}{2}\); for \(V_0 < \frac{1}{2}\) the lower energy branch has two minima, while for \(V_0 > \frac{1}{2}\) there is a single minimum at \(r=0\).
- For strong potential (\(V_0 > \frac{1}{2}\)) on the lower branch near \(r=0\), the expectation values are \(\langle S \rangle \simeq \frac{1}{2V_0} r\) and \(\langle P \rangle \simeq \left(1 - \frac{1}{2V_0}\right) r\), giving positive correlation between spin and momentum.
- The FCC lattice sites are vectors \((x,y,z) \in \mathbb{Z}^3\) with \(x+y+z\) even; the preferred orientation at site \((x,y,z)\) is \(\mathbf{i}^x \mathbf{j}^y \mathbf{k}^z\).
- The shortest dual lattice vectors used in the Fourier expansion are \(\vec{a}_1 = \frac{\pi}{3}(2,-1,-1)\), \(\vec{a}_2 = \frac{\pi}{3}(-1,2,-1)\), \(\vec{a}_3 = \frac{\pi}{3}(-1,-1,2)\).

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to compute the nuclear spin-orbit coupling from the Skyrme model, which were based on the product ansatz and limited to two well-separated nuclei (Jackson et al. 1985; Nyman and Riska 1988; Riska and Dannbom 1988). The new method applies to the phenomenologically important case of a single nucleon orbiting a large nucleus. The authors build on a two-dimensional toy model by Halcrow and Manton (2015) and use the lightly bound Skyrme model point particle description developed by Gillard et al. (2017). The results suggest that the sign problem for the spin-orbit coupling in the Skyrme model is resolved by going beyond perturbation theory, analogous to the conventional view that pion exchange gives a wrong-sign contribution compensated by other meson exchanges (Kaiser and Weise 2008).
