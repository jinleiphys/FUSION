# Low-lying States of $^{6}$He and $^{6}$Be in Nodal Surface Structure Analysis

> The low-lying states of light nuclei $^6$He and $^6$Be are studied. Using the inherent nodal surface(INS) analysis approach, we deduce the quantum numbers and the spatial symmetries of the low-lying states with positive parity and negative parity of the two nuclei. The energy spectrum obtained agrees well with the experimental data.

## Key claim
The inherent nodal surface (INS) analysis predicts the quantum numbers and spatial symmetries of low-lying states in \(^{6}\)He and \(^{6}\)Be without solving the full dynamical equations, and the resulting energy spectrum agrees well with experimental data, except that the \(2^{+}_{2}\) state does not appear as a low-lying state in the analysis but is not excluded as a higher-energy resonance.

## Method
The authors applied the inherent nodal surface (INS) analysis approach to six-nucleon systems with isospin \(T=1\). They examined the accessibility of two high-symmetry geometric configurations, the regular octahedron (OCTA) and the regular pentagon pyramid (C-PENTA), to wavefunctions with given total angular momentum \(L\), parity \(\pi\), and spatial permutation symmetry \(\lambda\), by solving sets of homogeneous linear equations derived from the invariance of these configurations under specific rotation, permutation, and inversion operations. The components that are accessible to both the OCTA and C-PENTA are identified as inherent-nodeless and thus dominant for low-lying states; the quantum numbers of the resulting states are then deduced and compared to experimental energy levels.

## Key numbers
- The ground state of \(^{6}\)He is a \(J^{\pi}=0^{+}\) state at \(E_x = 0\) MeV (reference energy).
- The first excited state of \(^{6}\)He is a \(J^{\pi}=2^{+}\) state at \(E_x = 1.797\) MeV.
- A second \(0^{+}\) state in \(^{6}\)He is predicted at \(E_x = 5.6 \pm 0.6\) MeV with a width \(\Gamma = 10.9 \pm 1.9\) MeV.
- A \(1^{-}\) resonance in \(^{6}\)He is reported at \(E_x = 4 \pm 1\) MeV with \(\Gamma = 4 \pm 1\) MeV.
- A set of negative-parity resonances in \(^{6}\)He (including \(J^{\pi}=0^{-},1^{-},2^{-}\) for \(S=1\) and \(J^{\pi}=1^{-},2^{-},3^{-}\) for \(S=2\)) is reported at \(E_x = 14.6 \pm 0.7\) MeV with \(\Gamma = 7.4 \pm 1.0\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the INS analysis previously applied to \(^{4}\)He (Liu et al., earlier) and to the \(T=0\) spectrum of \(^{6}\)Li (Liu et al., earlier) to the \(T=1\) systems \(^{6}\)He and \(^{6}\)Be. The approach provides a symmetry-based classification that complements shell-model calculations (which converge poorly for \(A=5-10\)) and cluster-model studies. The results enable future dynamical calculations to be guided by the identified nodeless components, and they contradict a shell-model prediction (Dreizler et al., 1997) that the second \(0^{+}\) state of \(^{6}\)He lies at 12 MeV, instead placing it much lower near 5.6 MeV.
