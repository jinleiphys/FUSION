# Particle yields in heavy ion collisions and the influence of strong magnetic fields

> It is expected that the magnetic field in the surface of magnetars do not exceed $10^{15}$ G. However, in heavy ion collisions, this value is expected to be much higher. We investigate the effects of a magnetic fields varying from $10^{18}$, to $10^{20}$ G in strange matter (composed of $u$, $d$ and $s$ quarks). We model matter as a free gas of baryons and mesons under the influence of an external magnetic field. We study the effects of such strong fields through a $χ^2$ fit to some data sets of the STAR experiment. For this purpose we solve the Dirac, Rarita-Schwinger, Klein-Gordon and Proca equations subject to magnetic fields in order to obtain the energy expressions and the degeneracy for spin 1/2, spin 3/2, spin 0 and spin 1 particles, respectively. Our results show that a field of the order of $10^{19}$ G produces an improved fitting to the experimental data as compared to the calculations without magnetic field.

## Key claim

The inclusion of a strong, constant magnetic field of order \(10^{19}\) G (approximately \(6 m_\pi^2\)) improves the \(\chi^2\) fit to STAR experiment particle-yield data compared to calculations without a magnetic field, but only up to that field strength; stronger fields worsen the fit because they produce unrealistically high densities.

## Method

The authors model the hadronic matter produced in heavy-ion collisions as a free gas of 54 particles (baryon octet, baryon decuplet, pseudoscalar meson nonet, and vector meson nonet) under a constant, homogeneous, time-independent external magnetic field. They solve the Dirac, Rarita-Schwinger, Klein-Gordon, and Proca equations with minimal coupling to obtain the energy eigenvalues and degeneracies for spin-1/2, spin-3/2, spin-0, and spin-1 particles, respectively, in the presence of the field. Using the grand canonical formalism, they compute particle densities and perform a \(\chi^2\) fit to seven experimental particle ratios from Au+Au and d+Au collisions at RHIC, treating temperature, baryonic chemical potential, and magnetic field as free parameters.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field range investigated is \(10^{18}\) G to \(10^{20}\) G, corresponding to \(eB = 5\)–\(30\, m_\pi^2\) (where \(m_\pi\) is the pion mass).
- The best fit to STAR data is obtained for a magnetic field of approximately \(6\, m_\pi^2\), which corresponds to about \(10^{19}\) G.
- For Au+Au collisions at RHIC, the temperature and baryonic chemical potential from previous statistical-model fits lie in the ranges \(132 < T < 169\) MeV and \(30.5 < \mu_B < 62.8\) MeV; for Pb-Pb at SPS, \(99 < T < 156.1\) MeV and \(167.5 < \mu_B < 411\) MeV.
- The baryon density at the best-fit field is of order \(10^{-3}\) fm\(^{-3}\); at \(B = 10^{20}\) G the density becomes much higher, degrading the fit.
- The \(\chi^2\) fit uses 7 experimental particle ratios (\(\pi^-/\pi^+\), \(K^-/K^+\), \(\bar{p}/p\), \(K^-/\pi^-\), \(K^+/\pi^+\), \(p/\pi^+\)) with 5 degrees of freedom for \(B=0\) and 4 for \(B\neq0\).

## Context

This work extends earlier statistical-model calculations of particle yields in heavy-ion collisions (Munzinger et al., various years) and relativistic Walecka-type models (de Paoli and Menezes, 2010–2011) by adding the effect of strong magnetic fields, which were previously studied in the context of magnetars and QGP signatures (Kharzeev et al., various years; Tuchin, 2010). The authors note that the magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions is actually inhomogeneous and time-dependent (Skokov and Deng, 2010), but they restrict their analysis to high-centrality (80%) events where the electric field can be neglected. The results enable future comparisons with ALICE/LHC data and motivate inclusion of electric fields, anomalous magnetic moments, and pion-pion interactions.
