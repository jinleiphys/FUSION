# Exploring the Phase Diagram of V-QCD with Neutron Star Merger Simulations

> Determining the phase structure of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) and its Equation of State (EOS) at densities and temperatures realized inside neutron stars and their mergers is a long-standing open problem. The holographic V-QCD framework provides a model for the EOS of dense and hot QCD, which describes the deconfinement phase transition between a dense baryonic and a quark matter phase. We use this model in fully general relativistic hydrodynamic (GRHD) simulations to study the formation of quark matter and the emitted gravitational wave signal of binary systems that are similar to the first ever observed neutron star merger event GW170817.

## Key claim

The holographic V-QCD model, when used in fully general relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of neutron star mergers similar to GW170817, predicts three distinct post-merger stages where hot, warm, and cold quark matter form sequentially; however, the dominant effect on the gravitational wave signal is an early truncation due to phase-transition-triggered black hole collapse, while the impact on the post-merger kHz gravitational wave spectrum itself is small.

## Method

The authors constructed three variants (soft, intermediate, stiff) of a finite-temperature hybrid equation of state using the holographic V-QCD framework, which describes the deconfinement phase transition between baryonic and quark matter via a Maxwell construction. They performed fully general relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of binary neutron star systems with the chirp mass of GW170817 (1.186 solar masses) and mass ratios q=0.7 and q=1.0, using the Frankfurt University/Kadath (FUKA) code for initial data and the Einstein Toolkit with the FIL code for evolution, employing six refinement levels with finest grid-spacing of 221 meters.

## Key numbers

- Chirp mass of simulated binaries: \(\mathcal{M}_c = 1.186\,M_\odot\), matching the GW170817 event
- Mass ratios simulated: \(q = M_2/M_1 = 0.7\) and \(q = 1.0\)
- Finest grid spacing in simulations: 221 meters
- Temperatures reached locally after collision: beyond 50 MeV inside the hyper-massive neutron star
- Heavy pulsar PSR J0740+6620 mass: \(M = 2.08 \pm 0.07 M_\odot\) (from NANOGrav 2019, Fonseca 2021)
- Upper bound on binary tidal deformability respected: \(\tilde\Lambda < 720\) (low-spin priors from GW170817)
- Snapshots shown at times \(t - t_{\rm mer} = 1.4,\,3.3,\,5.6\) ms after merger

## Context

This work extends the holographic V-QCD framework developed by Demircik et al. (2021) and Jarvinen et al. (2021) to dynamical merger simulations, building on prior neutron star merger studies that used model-agnostic parameterizations (Annala 2019, 2022; Altiparmak 2022) and earlier general relativistic hydrodynamic codes (Most 2019, Papenfort 2021). The results enable future investigations into the threshold mass for promptly collapsing heavy binaries and the long-time stability of lighter binary systems, with the equation of state files made publicly available on the CompOSE database.
