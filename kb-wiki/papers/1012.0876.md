# Nuclear Physics from QCD : The Anticipated Impact of Exa-Scale Computing

> I discuss highlights in the progress that is being made toward calculating processes of importance in nuclear physics from QCD using high performance computing. As exa-scale computing resources are expected to become available around 2017, I present current estimates of the computational resources required to accomplish central goals of nuclear physics.

## Key claim

Exa-scale computing resources, anticipated around 2017, are required for lattice QCD calculations of nuclear systems and processes with quantifiable uncertainties, but conceptual and formal developments must occur hand-in-hand with computing power to accomplish all goals.

## Method

The paper reviews the current state and future needs of lattice QCD (LQCD) calculations for nuclear physics, where Euclidean space correlation functions are computed via Monte-Carlo evaluation of the path integral on a discretized space-time lattice. The author presents estimates from a 2009 series of US workshops that established scientific needs for exa-scale computing in nuclear physics, including resource requirements for calculating hadron structure, baryon-baryon scattering, nuclear spectra, and the QCD phase diagram.

## Key numbers

- LQCD calculations currently use lattice volumes of spatial extent L ≥ 2.5 fm and lattice spacings b ≤ 0.12 fm, with typical pion masses mπ ≤ 300 MeV
- Exploratory calculations at the physical pion mass (mπ ~ 139 MeV) have been performed but with lattice volumes too small for meaningful comparison with nature
- Baryon-baryon scattering phase-shifts were calculated at a pion mass of mπ ~ 390 MeV on a 20³ × 128 lattice with spatial lattice spacing a ~ 0.123 fm and anisotropy factor ξT = 3.5
- Simulated LQCD calculations show that 1% precision in the energy-splitting between two interacting nucleons and two free nucleons is required to extract the deuteron binding energy
- Fission calculations for heavy nuclei require a multi-dimensional grid of ~3 × 10⁸ points and need an exa-flop to execute one calculation in less than one day

## Context

This paper extends the program of connecting QCD to nuclear physics through LQCD, building on Wilson's (1974) formulation of lattice QCD and two decades of low-energy effective field theory development for nuclear physics. The work summarizes findings from the 2009 Scientific Grand Challenges Workshop Series and references specific calculations by the NPLQCD collaboration (Beane et al., 2009) on baryon-baryon phase-shifts, as well as GFMC calculations of light nuclei (Pieper et al., 2004). The anticipated exa-scale resources would enable calculations of three-nucleon interactions, alpha-cluster states in ⁸Be and ¹²C, and the QCD phase diagram at zero baryon number density, while the sign problem remains a major obstacle for finite-density calculations.
