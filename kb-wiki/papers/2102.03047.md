# A study of open shell nuclei using chiral two-body interactions

> We apply the Hybrid-Multi-Determinant method using the recent chiral two-body interactions of Entem-Machleidt-Nosyk (EMN) without renormalization to few nuclei up to A=48. Mostly we use the bare fifth order NN interaction N4LO-450. For ${}^{24}Mg$ and ${}^{48}Cr$ the excitation energies of the $2^+_1$ states are far larger than the corresponding experimental values.

## Key claim

The bare fifth-order chiral NN interaction N4LO-450, applied without renormalization via the Hybrid-Multi-Determinant method, yields excitation energies for the first 2+ states in 24Mg and 48Cr that are several times larger than experimental values, suggesting that either three-body forces, a much larger number of Slater determinants, or a renormalization procedure is needed to reproduce collective behavior in medium-mass open-shell nuclei.

## Method

The Hybrid-Multi-Determinant (HMD) method expands the nuclear wave function as a linear combination of generic Slater determinants, with coefficients and single-particle wave functions determined variationally using rank-3 gradient methods. The bare N4LO-450 chiral two-body interaction of Entem-Machleidt-Nosyk (EMN) was used in an harmonic oscillator basis with single-particle states satisfying 2n+l ≤ e_max (HMD-a version), without any renormalization step. For each nucleus, sequences of wave functions with increasing numbers of Slater determinants N_D were constructed, and excitation energies were extracted by reprojecting to good angular momentum and parity, relying on the cancellation of errors between ground-state and excited-state energies at the same level of approximation.

## Key numbers

- For 24Mg, the experimental excitation energy of the 2+_1 state is 1.368 MeV; the calculated values at several e_max and N_D values are far larger (see fig. 3, no single numerical result quoted in text).
- For 48Cr, the experimental excitation energy of the 2+_1 state is 0.752 MeV; the calculated values are similarly far larger (see fig. 4).
- For 24Mg, the EVE extrapolation using 200 Slater determinants with 13 major shells gave a ground-state energy estimate of a = (-226.269 ± 0.140) MeV, with slope b = (0.01523 ± 3.3×10⁻⁵) MeV⁻¹; the experimental binding energy is 198.256 MeV.
- For 48Cr, using only 25 Slater determinants with 11 major shells, the calculation reached the experimental binding energy (no numerical value given in text).

## Context

This work extends the application of the HMD method (Puddu, previous work) to open-shell nuclei using bare chiral NN interactions, contrasting with methods like the no-core shell model (NCSM), coupled-cluster (CC), and in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) that typically require renormalization or are limited to closed shells. The failure to reproduce low-lying 2+ excitation energies in 24Mg and 48Cr suggests that either three-nucleon forces (not included here) or a renormalization procedure such as the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) is necessary for collective behavior in medium-mass nuclei, which the author identifies as the goal of future work.
