# An extra push from entrance-channel effects

> Symmetric heavy-ion collisions are known to display an `extra-push' effect. That is, the energy at which the s-wave transmission is 0.5 lies significantly higher than the nominal Coulomb barrier. Despite this, however, the capture cross section is still greatly enhanced below the uncoupled barrier. It is shown that this phenomenon can be simply explained in terms of entrance-channel effects which account for long-range Coulomb excitations.

## Key claim

The "extra-push" effect in symmetric heavy-ion collisions, where the energy for 50% s-wave transmission lies significantly above the nominal Coulomb barrier, can be explained by entrance-channel effects including long-range Coulomb excitations and multi-phonon couplings, which previous simplified coupled-channels calculations (e.g., CCFUS) could only partially account for.

## Method

The authors performed full coupled-channels calculations using the code CCFULL for the symmetric system ¹⁰⁰Mo + ¹⁰⁰Mo, incorporating known quadrupole and octupole phonon states with empirical energies and deformation parameters. They compared calculations with varying numbers of phonon excitations to experimental s-wave transmission probabilities derived from evaporation-residue cross sections measured at GSI, which had been reconstructed using the statistical-model code HIVAP. To isolate the role of long-range Coulomb couplings, they also performed calculations with Coulomb deformation parameters set to zero (simulating the CCFUS approach) and compared the resulting barrier distributions.

## Key numbers

* For ¹⁰⁰Mo + ¹⁰⁰Mo, the width of the barrier distribution D∞ = B̄ − Bad was found to be approximately 20 MeV.
* The quadrupole phonon in ¹⁰⁰Mo has energy E(2⁺) = 0.536 MeV with deformation parameter β₂ = 0.21; the octupole phonon has E(3⁻) = 1.908 MeV with β₃ = 0.17.
* The full CCFULL calculation with [4,4] phonon couplings produced a shift of the T₀ = 0.5 point of about 10 MeV due to Coulomb couplings, accounting for roughly half of D∞.
* The uncoupled barrier height required to fit the ¹⁰⁰Mo + ¹⁰⁰Mo data was 201.7 MeV, compared to the Bass barrier of 195.2 MeV (a difference of 6.5 MeV).
* For ⁹⁰Zr + ⁹⁰Zr, the required barrier shift was 4.1 MeV; for ¹⁰⁰Mo + ¹¹⁰Pd, the required shift was 15 MeV (previously 29.0 MeV in earlier analyses).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Quint et al. (1993) on the extra-push effect in symmetric heavy-ion collisions around mass 100, where simplified CCFUS calculations could only account for about half of the observed barrier distribution width D∞. The authors show that including long-range Coulomb couplings and multi-phonon excitations in CCFULL reproduces the full D∞, resolving a discrepancy that had been attributed to an internal fusion barrier. The results enable more reliable predictions of capture cross sections for heavy-element synthesis and suggest that direct measurements of capture cross sections (or quasielastic barrier distributions) are needed to resolve remaining ambiguities in the statistical-model reconstruction for systems like ¹⁰⁰Mo + ¹¹⁰Pd.
