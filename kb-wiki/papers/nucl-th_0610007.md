# Thermodynamics of compact-star matter within an ising approach

> In the formation and evolution of compact stars, nuclear matter explores high thermal excursions and is the site of intense neutrino emission. Neutrino transport as well as structural properties of this matter depend on the presence of inhomogeneous phases (named "pasta" phases), which are the result of Coulomb frustration of the Liquid-Gas phase transition. We take into account charge fluctuations by employing a frustrated lattice-gas model to which we impose a neutrality constraint by the addition of an homogeneous background of charge, representing delocalised electrons. Within this schematic model we highlight a generaic feature of the phase-transition phenomenology: the temperature interval where pasta phases are formed is enhanced by Coulomb-frustration effects. This result is at variance with the behaviour of frustrated ferromagnetic systems as well as hot nuclei and mean-field approaches. Moreover, the region of phase coexistence is not found to end upon a critical point, indicating that no critical opalescence can occur in compact-star matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in compact-star matter, the temperature interval where inhomogeneous "pasta" phases form is enhanced by Coulomb-frustration effects, and that the region of phase coexistence does not end at a critical point. This result is conditional on the system being globally neutral (via a uniform electron background) and is at variance with the behavior of frustrated ferromagnets, hot nuclei, and mean-field approaches.

## Method

The authors constructed a cubic lattice-gas model with short-range attractive interactions (representing nuclear forces) and long-range Coulomb interactions, with a uniform negative background charge enforcing global neutrality. They performed Metropolis Monte Carlo simulations in a multi-grandcanonical ensemble, treating the nuclear and Coulomb energies with separate Lagrange multipliers, to compute phase diagrams for different lattice sizes and Coulomb field strengths.

## Key numbers

- The proton fraction was fixed to x = 1/3, a value expected for proto-neutron stars.
- The chemical potential was set to μ = μ_c = 3ε for all simulations.
- For the uncharged system (βC = 0), the limiting temperature T_lim followed finite-size scaling T_lim(L) - T_lim ∝ L^{-1/ν} with the critical exponent ν matching Ising model values.
- For the frustrated system (βC = βn), no finite-size scaling of the limiting temperature was observed, indicating suppression of critical behavior.

## Context

This work extends the study of frustrated systems from ferromagnets (Grousson, year not given) and nuclear matter (Gulminelli, 2003) to globally neutral compact-star matter. It contradicts mean-field calculations (Lattimer, 1985; Haensel, 2000; Ducoin, 2006) which predict a reduction of the coexistence region when Coulomb energy is introduced. The results are compatible with RMF calculations (Maruyama, 2005) that found a widening of the density range for pasta phases under global charge neutrality. The finding that no critical opalescence occurs in compact-star matter connects to discussions of neutrino opacity (Horowitz, 2005) and has implications for supernova dynamics and proto-neutron-star cooling.
