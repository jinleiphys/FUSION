# Multiple scattering and $p_t$-broadening at RHIC energies

> In ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, in the 2 GeV$<p_\perp<$ 6 GeV transverse momentum region, the soft and semi-hard multiple scattering of the incoming nucleons in the nuclear medium results in the broadening of the expected hadronic (e.g. pion) $p_\perp$ spectra relative to proton-proton ($pp$) collisions. Thus, higher transverse-momentum regions are populated than in $pp$ collisions. In a perturbative QCD based calculation we include the intrinsic transverse momentum ($k_\perp$) of the partons in the nucleon (determined from $pp$ collisions), augmented by the extra broadening obtained via a systematic analysis of proton-nucleus ($pA$) collisions in the energy range 17$<\sqrt{s}<$ 39 AGeV. The original polynomial spectra are modified, and a nearly exponential spectrum appears in the region 2$\lesssim p_\perp\lesssim 3.5$ GeV. At present RHIC energies ($\sqrt{s}=$130 AGeV), the slope of the calculated spectra is reminiscent of that of fluid-dynamical descriptions, but lacks any thermal origin. We determine and discuss the size of the modifications originating in multiple scattering, which lead to this state of affairs.

# Multiple scattering and $p_t$-broadening at RHIC energies

## Key claim

The observed nearly exponential slope of hadron spectra in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies in the 2–3.5 GeV transverse momentum window can be reproduced by a perturbative QCD calculation incorporating multiple scattering and intrinsic transverse momentum broadening, without invoking any thermal origin for the spectrum.

## Method

The authors use a leading-order perturbative QCD parton model with GRV parton distribution functions and KKP fragmentation functions, augmented by a Gaussian intrinsic transverse momentum ($k_\perp$) distribution fitted to proton-proton data. They then incorporate nuclear broadening in proton-nucleus collisions by adding an average momentum square impart per nucleon-nucleon collision ($C \approx 0.4$ GeV$^2$) with a saturating effectivity function, and extend the formalism to nucleus-nucleus collisions using a double nuclear thickness function integral.

## Key numbers

- Intrinsic transverse momentum width in $pp$ collisions: $\langle k_\perp^2 \rangle_{pp} = 1.6$ GeV$^2$, fitted to $pp$ experimental data for $p_\perp > 2$ GeV
- Average momentum square impart per nucleon-nucleon collision: $C_{n=4} \approx 0.4$ GeV$^2$, determined from fitting $pA$ data with saturation after $n=3-4$ collisions
- Energy range for $pA$ systematic analysis: $17 < \sqrt{s} < 39$ AGeV
- RHIC collision energy used for Au+Au calculations: $\sqrt{s} = 130$ AGeV
- Transverse momentum window for nearly exponential spectrum: $2 \lesssim p_\perp \lesssim 3.5$ GeV
- Discrepancy between pQCD calculation and experimental data in central collisions: factor of two underestimation of yield in $pp$ and $p\bar{p}$ reactions at $\sqrt{s}=17-1800$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the pQCD parton model approach previously applied to $pp$ and $pA$ collisions (Cronin effect, Cronin 1975) to nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC energies. The authors find that multiple scattering produces a thermal-looking exponential slope in central Au+Au collisions without requiring thermalization, contradicting fluid-dynamical interpretations. The observed strong suppression in central collisions relative to pQCD calculations points toward jet quenching (Quench 2001) as a necessary additional mechanism at RHIC energies.
