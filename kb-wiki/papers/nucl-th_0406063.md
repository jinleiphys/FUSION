# $ΛN$ space-exchange correlation effects in $_Λ^5$He hypernucleus

> A complete realistic study of $_Λ^5$He hypernucleus is presented using a realistic Hamiltonian and wave function. This study takes into account all relevant dynamical correlations along with $ΛN$ space-exchange correlation (SEC). We also compute $ΛNN$ force and the corelation induced by this force. The SEC affects the central repulsive $λN$ correlation significantly at $r \le 2.0$ fm, specially at its peak and in its vicinity. SEC significantly affects energy breakdown of the hypernucleus, $Λ$-seperation energy, nuclear core (NC) polarization and density profiles. A lage NC polarization is found with and without SEC, respectively. The SEC effect is relatively large in two-pion exchange component of $ΛNN$ force. Therefore, any attempt to pin down the strength of this force with no SEC would be flawed.

## Key claim

The ΛN space-exchange correlation (SEC) significantly modifies the central repulsive ΛN correlation at distances r ≤ 2.0 fm, and any attempt to determine the strength of the ΛNN force without including SEC would be flawed; the effect is particularly large in the two-pion exchange component of the ΛNN force.

## Method

The author performed variational Monte Carlo (VMC) calculations for the ⁵_ΛHe hypernucleus using a realistic Hamiltonian that includes the Argonne v₁₈ NN potential, Urbana model-IX NNN potential, a charge-symmetric ΛN potential, and a ΛNN force with dispersive and two-pion-exchange components. The wave function incorporated all relevant dynamical correlations including the ΛN space-exchange correlation (SEC) via a Majorana space-exchange operator, and the center-of-mass was recalculated after each exchange operation to maintain translational invariance.

## Key numbers

- Λ-separation energy B_Λ with SEC: 3.66(3) MeV; without SEC: 3.11(3) MeV (experimental value: 3.12(2) MeV)
- Total binding energy of ⁵_ΛHe with SEC: -31.40(2) MeV; without SEC: -30.85(2) MeV; difference: -0.55(4) MeV
- Contribution of SEC to central ΛN potential v₀(r)(1-ε): 0.50(8) MeV; to space-exchange part v₀(r)εP_x: -0.45(4) MeV
- Contribution of SEC to V_ΛNN²π: 0.42(4) MeV; to V_ΛNNᴰ: -0.12(2) MeV
- Nuclear core polarization with SEC: 3.78(4) MeV; without SEC: 4.18(4) MeV
- Point proton radius in ⁵_ΛHe with SEC: 1.62(1) fm; without SEC: 1.66(1) fm; for isolated ⁴He: 1.46(1) fm (experimental: 1.47 fm)
- SEC is effective at r ≤ 2.0 fm and vanishes at r ≥ 2.4 fm

## Context

This work extends earlier realistic studies of s-shell hypernuclei by Usmani (2003) and Sinha (2003) that used the Argonne v₁₈ and Urbana potentials but omitted the ΛN space-exchange correlation. It also relates to ab-initio calculations by Nemura et al. (2002) that included Σ degrees of freedom. The finding that SEC significantly affects the ΛNN force expectation values implies that previous attempts to determine ΛNN strengths without SEC (e.g., Usmani 1995, Sinha 2003) may need revision, and that resolving the A=5 anomaly requires including this correlation.
