# Gauge Invariant Descriptions of Gluon Polarizations

> We propose methods to construct gauge invariant decompositions of the nucleon spin, particularly gauge invariant descriptions of the gluon polarization. We show that gauge invariant decompositions of the nucleon spin can be derived naturally from the conserved current of a generalized Lorentz transformation by the Noether theorem. We propose a gauge invariant extension of the Chern-Simons current as a possible description of the gluon spin, and we also examine the problem of gauge dependence of this extended Chern-Simons current.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that gauge invariant decompositions of the nucleon spin, including a gauge invariant description of gluon polarization, can be derived naturally from the conserved Noether current of a generalized Lorentz transformation, and that a gauge invariant extension of the Chern-Simons current can serve as a candidate description of the gluon spin, though the authors hedge that the gauge independence of this extended current is verified only in the (1+1)-dimensional Schwinger model and at one-loop order in QCD.

## Method

The authors construct a gauge invariant extension of the non-Abelian Chern-Simons current by replacing the gauge field with the "physical" part of the field (the total field minus a pure gauge part defined via a unitary matrix V), and show it satisfies the same divergence equation as the conventional Chern-Simons current. They then derive the angular momentum current from the Noether theorem applied to a generalized Lorentz transformation that simultaneously rotates spacetime coordinates and performs a local gauge transformation, parameterized by a field N^mu, and show that different choices of N^mu reproduce the Jaffe-Manohar, Ji, and Chen et al. decompositions. The gauge independence of the extended Chern-Simons current is tested in the 2D Schwinger model using axial gauge and bosonization, and a one-loop forward matrix element calculation is performed in 4D QCD in Lorentz gauge.

## Key numbers

- In the 2D Schwinger model, the gauge invariant extension of the Chern-Simons current yields the gauge-independent matrix element ⟨0|K^mu|phi(q)⟩ = (i/(2m)) q^mu, where m = e/sqrt(pi) is the mass of the gauge field.
- In 4D QCD, the one-loop forward matrix element of the extended Chern-Simons current between massive quark states is ⟨p,s|K^mu|p,s⟩ = (alpha_s/(2pi)) * ((7/2)C_F + (3/2)C_F N_epsilon) * bar{u}(p,s) gamma^mu gamma^5 u(p,s), where alpha_s = g^2/(4pi), C_F = (N_c^2 - 1)/(2N_c), d = 4 - 2epsilon, N_epsilon = 1/epsilon - gamma_E + Log(4pi) + Log(mu^2/M^2), and M is the quark mass.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical discussion of nucleon spin decompositions by showing that the three popular decompositions of Jaffe and Manohar (1989), Ji (1996), and Chen et al. (2008) can be unified within a single Noether-theorem framework using a generalized Lorentz transformation. It also proposes an alternative gauge invariant description of gluon spin via an extended Chern-Simons current, addressing the gauge-dependence problem of the conventional Chern-Simons current noted by Manohar (1990) and others. The results enable further investigation into whether this extended Chern-Simons current can serve as a physically meaningful description of gluon polarization, with the authors noting that a subsequent paper (Guo and Schmidt, 2013) will discuss its application to Laguerre-Gauss laser modes and provide more details on the gauge invariant decompositions.
