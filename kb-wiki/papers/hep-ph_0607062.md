# An AdS/CFT Calculation of Screening in a Hot Wind

> One of the challenges in relating experimental measurements of the suppression in the number of J/ψmesons produced in heavy ion collisions to lattice QCD calculations is that whereas the lattice calculations treat J/ψmesons at rest, in a heavy ion collision a c\bar c pair can have a significant velocity with respect to the hot fluid produced in the collision. The putative J/ψfinds itself in a hot wind. We present the first rigorous non-perturbative calculation of the consequences of a wind velocity v on the screening length L_s for a heavy quark-antiquark pair in hot N=4 supersymmetric QCD. We find L_s(v,T) = f(v)[1-v^2]^{1/4}/πT with f(v) only mildly dependent on v and the wind direction. This L_s(v,T)\sim L_s(0,T)/\sqrtγ velocity scaling, if realized in QCD, provides a significant additional source of J/Ψsuppression at transverse momenta which are high but within experimental reach.

## Key claim

The authors present the first rigorous non-perturbative calculation of how a wind velocity v affects the screening length L_s for a heavy quark-antiquark pair, but only in hot N=4 supersymmetric QCD, not in real QCD. The central result L_s(v,T) ~ L_s(0,T)/√γ is a conjecture for QCD, with the caveat that the function f(v) in the full expression L_s(v,T) = f(v)[1-v^2]^{1/4}/πT is only mildly dependent on v and the wind direction.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to compute the thermal expectation value of a rectangular Wilson loop describing a heavy quark-antiquark dipole moving with velocity v through a hot N=4 super Yang-Mills plasma. They boost the five-dimensional AdS black hole metric to describe a hot wind, then extremize the Nambu-Goto action for the string world sheet whose boundary is the Wilson loop contour. The screening length L_s is defined as the maximum separation ℓ_max/πT for which an extremal world sheet exists, obtained by solving the equations of motion for the string embedding in the boosted black hole geometry.

## Key numbers

- At v=0, the screening length parameter ℓ_max = 0.869 (dimensionless, with ℓ = LπT)
- For large γ (high velocity), ℓ_max ≈ 0.743/√coshη = 0.743(1-v^2)^{1/4}
- The integration constant at v=0 is q_m = 0.96, and for large cosh²η, q_m ≈ 1.42 coshη
- Lattice QCD results cited for comparison: L_s ~ 0.5/T in hot QCD with two flavors of light quarks (Kaczmarek 2005), and L_s ~ 0.7/T in hot QCD with no dynamical quarks (Kaczmarek 2004)
- The J/ψ meson ceases to exist as a bound state above a temperature between 1.5 T_c and 2.5 T_c, based on lattice studies (Asakawa 2003, Morrin 2005)
- The J/ψ(1S) state dissociates at ~2.1 T_c, while χ_c(2P) and ψ'(2S) states cannot survive above ~1.2 T_c (Karsch 2005, Satz)

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD calculations of the screening potential between static quarks (Kaczmarek 2004, Kaczmarek 2005) to the case of moving dipoles, which is essential for connecting to heavy ion collision experiments where c-cbar pairs have significant velocity relative to the hot medium. The result that the string world sheet is not dragged by the wind (antithetical to the isolated quark case studied by Herzog 2006) agrees qualitatively with the conclusion that "mesons feel no drag" from Peeters, Sonnenschein and Zamaklar (2006). If the velocity scaling L_s ∝ 1/√γ holds in QCD, it would imply that J/ψ suppression at RHIC should increase markedly for transverse momenta above ~5-9 GeV, a regime accessible to future high-luminosity runs at RHIC and the LHC.
