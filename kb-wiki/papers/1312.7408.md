# An overview of $XYZ$ new particles

> In the past decade, more and more charmoniumlike and bottomoniumlike states have been reported in experiments, which have led us to extensive discussions on the underlying structure of these states. In this review paper, we briefly summarize the experimental and theoretical status of these observed states.

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes the experimental and theoretical status of 23 observed charmoniumlike and bottomoniumlike XYZ states, but emphasizes that the underlying structure of most states remains ambiguous, with multiple competing explanations (molecular states, tetraquarks, hybrids, conventional charmonia) that often cannot be definitively distinguished by current data.

## Method

The paper categorizes the observed XYZ states into five groups based on their production mechanisms: B meson decay, e+e- annihilation, double charm production, γγ fusion, and hidden-charm/bottom dipion and open-charm decays of higher charmonia/bottomonia. For each category, the author reviews the experimental observations and the various theoretical interpretations that have been proposed in the literature, including molecular state, tetraquark, hybrid charmonium, and conventional charmonium assignments.

## Key numbers

- 23 XYZ states have been reported in experiments as of the paper's writing
- X(3872) mass measurements from different experiments show values near the D0D*0 threshold; the mass difference between χc1' (2^3P1 charmonium) and X(3872) is 50-200 MeV
- Y(4140) mass: 4143.0 ± 2.9 (stat) ± 1.2 (syst) MeV, width: 11.7 +8.3 -5.0 (stat) ± 3.7 (syst) MeV (CDF)
- Y(3940) mass: 3943 ± 11 (stat) ± 13 (syst) MeV, width: 87 ± 22 (stat) ± 26 (syst) MeV (Belle)
- Z+(4430) mass: 4433 ± 4 ± 2 MeV, width: 45 +18+30 -13-13 MeV (Belle)
- Z+(4051) mass: 4051 ± 14 +20 -41 MeV, width: 82 +21+47 -17-22 MeV (Belle)
- Z+(4248) mass: 4248 +44+180 -29-35 MeV, width: 177 +54+316 -39-61 MeV (Belle)
- X(3940) mass: 3942 +7 -6 ± 6 MeV, width: 37 +26 -15 ± 8 MeV
- X(4160) mass: 4156 +25 -20 ± 15 MeV, width: 139 +111 -61 ± 21 MeV
- DD* scattering length from lattice simulation: a0 = -1.7 ± 0.4 fm, effective range r0 = 0.5 ± 0.1 fm (for X(3872) candidate)
- BR[X(3872) → γ J/ψ] / BR[X(3872) → J/ψ π+π-] = 0.14 ± 0.05 (Belle) and ≈ 0.25 (BaBar)
- BR[X(3872) → D0D0π0] / BR[X(3872) → π+π- J/ψ] = 9.4 +3.6 -4.3 (Belle)
- BR(X(3872) → ψ'γ) / BR(X(3872) → J/ψγ) = 3.4 ± 1.4 (BaBar)

## Context

This review extends the discussion of XYZ states following the discovery of X(3872) by Belle (2003) and subsequent observations by BaBar, CDF, D0, LHCb, CMS, and BESIII. It covers the theoretical landscape including molecular state models (Swanson 2003, Wong 2003, Tornqvist 2004), tetraquark proposals (Maiani 2004), hybrid charmonium interpretations (Zhu 2005, Close 2005), and conventional charmonium assignments (Li and Chao 2009). The paper enables future experimental work to distinguish between these competing explanations through measurements of radiative decays, open-charm decays, and line shapes, particularly noting that the molecular picture for X(3872) is challenged by several branching ratio measurements from Belle and BaBar.
