# Nuclear Many-Body Problem at Finite Temperature: A TFD Approach

> Based on the formalism of thermo-field dynamics a new approach for studying collective excitations in hot finite Fermi systems is presented. Two approximations going beyond the thermal RPA namely renormalized thermal RPA and thermal second RPA are formulated.

## Key claim

Based on thermo-field dynamics (TFD), the paper presents a new formalism for studying collective excitations in hot finite Fermi systems, formulating two approximations that go beyond the thermal random phase approximation (TRPA): renormalized thermal RPA and thermal second RPA. The authors claim that TFD allows straightforward extensions of well-established zero-temperature approximations to finite temperature, but note that the full thermal second RPA is "quite complicated for practical calculations."

## Method

The authors construct a temperature-dependent Fock space starting from thermal Hartree-Fock (THF) mean-field theory, using the TFD formalism which doubles the field degrees of freedom by introducing a tilde space. They derive the thermal Hamiltonian in terms of thermal quasiparticle operators and apply the equation-of-motion method with a Raleigh-Ritz variational principle to obtain TRPA equations. For the renormalized TRPA, they introduce bosonic operators with modified commutation relations and solve a nonlinear system of equations coupling mean-field and collective variables. For the thermal second RPA, they extend the trial wave function to include four-quasiparticle configurations and derive a supermatrix eigenvalue equation.

## Key numbers

- The thermal occupation probabilities are given by Fermi-Dirac factors: \( x_i = \sqrt{1-f_i} \), \( y_i = \sqrt{f_i} \), where \( f_i = 1/(1+\exp(\varepsilon_i/T)) \)
- The average particle number conservation condition is \( \sum_1 \rho_{11} = N \)
- The renormalized TRPA introduces bosonic operators via \( b_{12} = A_{12}/\sqrt{1-q_{12}} \) where \( q_{12} = q_1 + q_2 \)
- The TRPA vacuum is expressed as \( |\Psi_0(T)\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{N}} \prod_{\nu} \exp\left[\frac{1}{4} \sum_{1234}(\psi^{-1})^{\nu}_{12}\phi^{\nu}_{34} \beta^{+}_1\tilde{\beta}^{+}_2 \beta^{+}_3\tilde{\beta}^{+}_4\right] |0(T)\rangle \)

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature RPA and second RPA formalisms to finite temperature using thermo-field dynamics, building on prior TFD developments by Hatsuda and Tanabe for hot nuclei. The approach enables systematic improvements beyond the standard thermal RPA, including renormalized TRPA (which couples mean-field and collective variables) and thermal second RPA (which incorporates coupling to four-quasiparticle configurations). The authors note that the thermal second RPA can be simplified using boson or phonon operators as building blocks, extending the quasiparticle-phonon nuclear model of Soloviev to nonzero temperature.
