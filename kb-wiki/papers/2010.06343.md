# Initialization effects of nucleon profile on the $π$ yields in heavy-ion collisions at medium energies

> We study a problem of $π$ production in heavy ion collisions in the context of the Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model. We generated nucleon densities using two different models, the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) model and configuration interaction shell model (SM). Indeed, inter-nucleon correlations are explicitly taken into account in SM, while they are averaged in the SHF model. As an application of our theoretical frameworks, we calculated the $π^{-}$ and $π^{+}$ yields in collisions of nuclei with $A = 30-40$ nucleons. We used different harmonic oscillator lengths $b_{HO}$ to generate the harmonic oscillator basis for SM in order to study both theoretical and experimental cases. It is found that SM framework with $b_{HO}$ = 2.5 fm and SHF can be distinguished by the yield of $π$ mesons, in this case the density distribution calculated by the shell model produces more $π$ in the collision. In comparison, SM with $b_{HO}$ = 2.0 fm is characterized from SHF by the double $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratios with different large impact parameters, from which one can find the double $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratios of SM change smoother and are less than those of SHF.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the inclusion of inter-nucleon correlations via the configuration interaction shell model (SM) produces distinct signatures in pion yields and double pion ratios compared to the mean-field Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) model, but only when the harmonic oscillator length for the SM basis is chosen to either match the SHF density maximum (2.5 fm) or reproduce experimental charge radii (2.0 fm); the distinction between models depends on which observable (absolute yield vs. double ratio) and which impact parameter is considered.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model to simulate peripheral heavy-ion collisions of sulfur and chlorine isotopes (A = 30-40) at 1 GeV/u beam energy. Nucleon density distributions for the initial colliding nuclei were generated using two approaches: the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) model with the SkM* interaction, and the configuration interaction shell model (SM) with the USDB interaction, using two different harmonic oscillator lengths (b_HO = 2.0 fm for SM1 and b_HO = 2.5 fm for SM2). The SM densities were constructed by adding valence nucleon densities from the shell model to a 16O core density from SHF. Pion yields and ratios were calculated via Monte Carlo sampling of baryon-baryon collisions and Delta resonance decays within the IBUU04 code.

## Key numbers

- The harmonic oscillator length b_HO = 2.5 fm (SM2) produces pion yields that are "almost twice as large" as those from SHF under the same entrance channel conditions at impact parameter 9 fm and beam energy 1 GeV/u.
- The double pi-/pi+ ratio Dr = (pi-/pi+)_37Cl+37Cl / (pi-/pi+)_32S+32S at impact parameter 11 fm is 2.227 for SHF, 1.431 for SM1, and 1.552 for SM2, giving Dr(SHF)/Dr(SM1) approximately 1.56.
- At impact parameter 9 fm, the double pi-/pi+ ratio is 1.698 for SHF, 1.501 for SM1, and 1.494 for SM2.
- RMS nuclear charge radii for 32S are 3.256 fm (SHF), 3.282 fm (SM1), 3.804 fm (SM2), and 3.261 fm (experimental); for 37Cl they are 3.348 fm (SHF), 3.310 fm (SM1), 3.856 fm (SM2), and 3.384 fm (experimental).
- Pion yields decrease by 25-30% when impact parameter increases from 9 fm to 11 fm.
- The centroid of the Delta resonance m_Delta0 is 1.232 GeV.
- Maximal cross sections for pion-nucleon reactions: sigma_max(pi+ p -> Delta++) = sigma_max(pi- n -> Delta-) = 200 mb; sigma_max(pi- p -> Delta0) = sigma_max(pi+ n -> Delta+) = 66.67 mb; sigma_max(pi0 p -> Delta+) = sigma_max(pi0 n -> Delta0) = 133.33 mb.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Stone et al. (2015) on the sensitivity of neutron-to-proton ratios and pion production to initial-state density distributions in central collisions, and by Bao-An Li et al. (2002) on using pions to constrain the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter. It also builds on recent work by the same group (Yang et al., 2019) showing that pairing correlations affect proton emission in proton-target reactions. The paper enables future experimental tests at facilities like GSI (ASY-EOS experiment) and RIKEN to distinguish between mean-field and correlation-included descriptions of nuclear densities using peripheral collisions of medium-mass nuclei.
