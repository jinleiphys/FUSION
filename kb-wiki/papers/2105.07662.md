# Structure of $^{46,47}$Ca from the $β^-$ decay of $^{46,47}$K in the framework of nuclear shell model

> In the present work, we report a comprehensive theoretical study of the $\log ft$ values for the allowed and forbidden $β^-$ decay transitions of $^{46}$K and $^{47}$K corresponding to recently available experimental data from the GRIFFIN spectrometer at TRIUMF-ISAC [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 100}, 054327 (2019); Phys. Rev. C {\bf 102}, 054314 (2020)]. We perform the nuclear shell-model calculation in $sdpf$-valence space, with the SDPF-MU interaction, to calculate low-lying energy spectra of $^{46,47}$K and $^{46,47}$Ca. For further investigation, we also calculate spectroscopic properties of $^{46,47}$K, $^{46,47}$Ca and compare the results with the experimental data. We suggest spin-parity of several levels, which were previously tentative, in both the calcium isotopes. Based on the energy and $\log ft$ values, we conclude that the state at 3.984 MeV in $^{46}$Ca could be either $3^{-}$ or $2^{+}$ and a state at 4.432 MeV might be $3^{-}$. The spin-parity of the states at 2.875, 3.951 and 4.453 MeV are predicted to be $1/2^-$, $3/2^-$ and $1/2^-$ or $1/2^+$, respectively, in $^{47}$Ca. We also give the confirmation to spin-parity of 2.850, 3.889 and 4.606 MeV states for $^{47}$Ca. Our results of level schemes of $^{46,47}$K are in a reasonable agreement with the experimental data, while the agreement of calculated $\log ft$ values for the $β^-$ decay with the experimental data is limited.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first shell-model calculation of log ft values for allowed and forbidden β⁻ decay transitions of ⁴⁶K and ⁴⁷K using the SDPF-MU interaction in the sdpf-valence space, but the agreement between calculated and experimental log ft values is limited, in contrast to the reasonable agreement found for the level schemes.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear shell-model calculations in the sdpf-valence space (with ⁴⁶K and ⁴⁷K as initial states) using the phenomenological SDPF-MU interaction, with ¹⁶O as an inert core and seven orbitals (d₅/₂, s₁/₂, d₃/₂, f₇/₂, p₃/₂, f₅/₂, p₁/₂) available for valence nucleons. They computed log ft values for allowed Gamow-Teller and first-forbidden β⁻ decay transitions, extracting an effective axial-vector coupling constant g_A via chi-square fitting, and included meson-exchange current contributions with an enhancement factor ε_MEC = 1.6 for 0⁻ transitions. The one-body transition densities were computed using the NUSHELLX code.

- For ⁴⁶K(2⁻) → ⁴⁶Ca(0⁺₂): experimental log ft = 10.27(2), theoretical log ft = 10.017
- For ⁴⁷K(1/2⁺) → ⁴⁷Ca(1/2⁺₁): experimental log ft = 4.807(9), theoretical log ft = 4.879

## Context

This work extends the experimental β⁻ decay studies of ⁴⁶K and ⁴⁷K performed at TRIUMF with the GRIFFIN spectrometer (Phys. Rev. C 100, 054327 (2019); Phys. Rev. C 102, 054314 (2020)), providing the first theoretical shell-model description of the log ft values for these decays. The calculations build on prior shell-model work by Holt et al. (2014) who used MBPT with NN+3N forces in pf and pfg₉/₂ spaces for calcium isotopes, and by Bhoy et al. who used GXPF1Br + V_MU interaction for ⁴⁷–⁵⁸Ca. The inclusion of the sdpf-valence space with SDPF-MU interaction allows treatment of both positive and negative parity states, addressing limitations of earlier pf-shell calculations that could not reproduce negative parity states or the 0⁺₂ state in ⁴⁶Ca. The results enable spin-parity assignments for previously tentative levels in ⁴⁶Ca and ⁴⁷Ca, and provide predicted B(E2) and magnetic moments for states not yet measured experimentally.
