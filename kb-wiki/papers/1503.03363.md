# The beam energy dependence of collective flow in heavy ion collisions

> The major goals of heavy ion research are to explore the phase diagram of quantum chromodynamics (QCD) and to investigate the properties of the quark gluon plasma (QGP), a new state of matter created at high temperatures and/or densities. Collective anisotropic flow is one of the most promising observables to gain insights about the properties of the system created in relativistic heavy ion reactions. The current status of the beam energy dependence of the first three Fourier coefficients of the azimuthal distribution of the produced particles $v_1$ to $v_3$ within hybrid transport plus hydrodynamics approaches are summarized.

## Key claim

The beam energy dependence of anisotropic flow coefficients v1, v2, and v3 is summarized within hybrid transport plus hydrodynamics approaches, but the authors conclude that the old prediction of a dip in the directed flow slope as a signal of a first-order phase transition is not reproduced in modern hybrid calculations; there is no difference between calculations with and without a strong first-order phase transition.

## Method

The calculations use the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) approach coupled to 3+1 dimensional ideal relativistic fluid dynamics (SHASTA) for the hot and dense stage. The initial state is generated dynamically from Woods-Saxon nucleon distributions with event-by-event fluctuations, and the hydrodynamic evolution uses either a bag model equation of state matched to a hadron gas (strong first-order phase transition) or a chiral model fitted to lattice QCD at zero baryo-chemical potential (crossover). After hydrodynamic evolution, particles are sampled via the Cooper-Frye formula and hadronic rescattering and decays are treated by the hadronic transport approach.

## Key numbers

* Elliptic flow v2 of charged particles in Au+Au/Pb+Pb collisions at mid-centrality (b=5-9 fm) shows negative values at low energies (squeeze-out), turns positive, and grows with beam energy; at 160A GeV the hydrodynamic evolution is crucial to reproduce the data.
* The fraction of elliptic flow generated during hydrodynamic evolution decreases at lower beam energies; at 40A GeV almost all elliptic flow is generated by hadronic transport, while at higher energies the hydrodynamic evolution is necessary.
* Triangular flow v3 scales with the duration of the hydrodynamic evolution for different energies and centralities; in central collisions v3 and the dynamical fluctuations of v2 have roughly the same value, while at lower beam energies triangular flow decreases.
* The slope of directed flow dv1/dy for protons is very small around zero; pure one-fluid calculations overestimate directed flow, while pure hadron transport shows the best agreement with experimental data at the energies studied.
* For pions, the slope of directed flow is negative at low beam energies due to shadowing by spectators; the hybrid UrQHD approach describes the measurements rather well.

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic predictions by Brachmann (1999) and Csernai (1999) that the slope of proton directed flow would show a dip as a function of beam energy signaling a phase transition to the quark gluon plasma. The authors recalculate this observable with modern hybrid techniques and find that the freeze-out dynamics has a significant effect on the excitation function, and that the large difference between scenarios with and without a first-order phase transition disappears. The results enable future studies that include nuclear potentials and improved treatment of baryon stopping to reach final conclusions on the equation of state and transport properties at finite baryo-chemical potential.
