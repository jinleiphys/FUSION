# E2/M1 ratio from the Mainz $p(\vec γ, p)π^0$ data

> We suggest that the error quoted in the Mainz determination of the E2/M1 ratio (at the resonance energy) should be enlarged. A term dropped in expressions used by this group could be significant.

## Key claim

The E2/M1 ratio extracted by Beck et al. (1997) from Mainz $p(\vec \gamma, p)\pi^0$ data should have a larger systematic error than quoted, because a term dropped in their simplified expression for the ratio $C_{||}/(12 A_{||})$ introduces an error of about 17% for $R_{\rm EM}$, more than double the systematic error they reported.

## Method

Workman re-examined the multipole expansion formulas used by Beck et al. (1997) to extract the E2/M1 ratio from differential cross section and photon asymmetry data at the $\Delta(1232)$ resonance. He derived a corrected relation between the ratio of the constant ($A_{||}$) and $\cos^2\theta$ ($C_{||}$) coefficients and the multipole ratio $R_{\rm EM}$, showing that a term involving $R_{\rm EM}$ in the denominator was neglected. Using his own multipole amplitudes, he computed an alternative value for the E2/M1 ratio.

## Key numbers

- Beck et al. (1997) quoted E2/M1 ratio: $(-2.5 \pm 0.2 \pm 0.2)\%$ (statistical and systematic errors).
- Workman finds that neglecting the $R_{\rm EM}$ term in the denominator of the corrected expression introduces an error of about 17% for $R_{\rm EM}$.
- Using his own multipole amplitudes, Workman obtains a different value: $(-1.5 \pm 0.5)\%$ for the E2/M1 ratio.

## Context

This comment extends the analysis of Beck et al. (1997), who measured the E2/M1 ratio from $p(\vec \gamma, p)\pi^0$ data at the Mainz Microtron MAMI. Workman points out a neglected term in their extraction formula and suggests the systematic error should be enlarged. The discrepancy between the two values (Workman's $(-1.5 \pm 0.5)\%$ versus Beck et al.'s $(-2.5 \pm 0.2 \pm 0.2)\%$) worsens when the sign correction is applied, indicating the need for further experimental or theoretical clarification of the E2/M1 ratio at the $\Delta(1232)$ resonance.
