# Strangeness-changing scalar form factors

> We derive expressions for strangeness-changing scalar form factors, which incorporate known theoretical constraints both at low and high energies. Their leading behaviour in the limit of a large number of colours is calculated from the resonance chiral Lagrangian. A set of short-distance constraints on the scalar resonance couplings is obtained, imposing the form factors to vanish at infinite momentum transfer. Making use of previous results on S-wave KPi scattering [1], and a coupled--channel dispersion-relation analysis, the KPi, KEta and KEta' scalar form factors are calculated up to 2 GeV. These scalar form factors are a key ingredient in the determination of the corresponding scalar spectral function which is important in the extraction of the mass of the strange quark from QCD sum rules as well as hadronic tau decays.

## Key claim
The paper derives strangeness-changing scalar form factors (Kπ, Kη, Kη') up to 2 GeV by combining resonance chiral Lagrangian predictions at large Nc with coupled-channel dispersion relations, but the results depend on the specific unitarisation scheme and the assumed short-distance constraints on scalar resonance couplings, with uncertainties in the scalar couplings estimated at about 30%.

## Method
The authors first computed the scalar form factors at tree level using the resonance chiral Lagrangian in the large-Nc limit, imposing short-distance constraints that require the form factors to vanish at infinite momentum transfer. They then used S-wave Kπ scattering amplitudes from their previous work (Jamin, Oller, Pich 2000) as input to solve coupled dispersion integral equations for the Kπ, Kη and Kη' channels, implementing unitarity through iterative numerical solutions. The elastic single-channel case was treated analytically using the Omnès exponential solution.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analysis of S-wave Kπ scattering (Jamin, Oller, Pich 2000) by calculating the corresponding scalar form factors using the same resonance chiral framework with unitarisation. The form factors are essential for determining the strange quark mass from QCD sum rules (following approaches by Narison et al. 1983, Jamin and Münz 1995, and others) and from hadronic τ decays (as studied by Pich and Portolés 1998, ALEPH 1999, and collaborators). The results enable improved extractions of m_s by providing the scalar spectral function needed for QCD sum rule analyses up to 2 GeV.
