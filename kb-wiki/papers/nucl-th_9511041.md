# Do we have three $S_{11}$ resonances in the second resonance region?

> We review the status of the $S_{11}$ N$^*$ resonances in light of some recent theoretical and phenomenological results. Whereas the quark model predicts two such resonances around 1.6 GeV, there is considerable evidence for a third $S_{11}$ resonance in this energy range. This suggests that a $KΣ$ or $KΛ$ quasi-bound state may indeed exist below the kaon production threshold. We show that kaon production experiments, in particular $K^0$ photoproduction off nucleons, would be very sensitive to the existence of the third $S_{11}$ resonance.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a third $S_{11}$ resonance near 1.7 GeV likely exists, which would support the interpretation of a $K\Sigma$ or $K\Lambda$ quasi-bound state below kaon production threshold; however, the evidence is circumstantial and the third resonance has not been definitively established, as different analyses disagree on whether the structure is a true resonance or an artifact.

## Method

The authors review partial-wave analyses of $\pi N$ elastic scattering data (VPI group, up to 2.1 GeV), coupled-channel analyses of $\pi N \to \eta N$ and $\eta N \to \eta N$ (Batini\'c et al.), and $\eta$ photoproduction data from Bates, ELSA, and MAMI. They apply a chiral quark model to $\eta$ photoproduction, fitting the total width $\Gamma_T$, the coefficient $C_{S_{11}(1535)}$, and the $\eta NN$ coupling constant $\alpha_\eta$ to the Mainz data, and compare the resulting quantity $\xi$ to model-independent extractions from the RPI group.

## Key numbers

- Total width of $S_{11}(1535)$ from chiral quark model fit: $\Gamma_T = 198$ MeV (differs from Breit-Wigner parameterization by 4 to 5 MeV).
- Coefficient $C_{S_{11}(1535)} = 1.608$, indicating a 60.8% deviation from the naive $SU(6)\otimes O(3)$ quark model value of unity.
- $\xi = 0.220$ GeV$^{-1}$ for $S_{11}(1535)$ from the chiral quark model fit.
- Helicity amplitude $A^p_{1/2} = 98.9 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ for $S_{11}(1535)$, derived using $\chi_{\eta N} = 0.63$ and $\chi_{\pi N} = 0.31$ from the coupled-channel analysis of Batini\'c et al.
- Naive quark model prediction for $\xi$ of $S_{11}(1535)$: $\xi \approx 0.14$ GeV$^{-1}$.
- Naive quark model prediction for $\xi$ of $S_{11}(1650)$: $\xi = 0$ (Moorhouse selection rule).
- Third $S_{11}$ resonance from VPI analysis: mass $M = 1.712$ GeV, total width $\Gamma = 0.184$ GeV.
- Third $S_{11}$ resonance from coupled-channel analysis (Batini\'c et al.): mass $M = 1.705$ GeV, total width $\Gamma = 0.27$ GeV.
- Third $S_{11}$ resonance from older $\pi N \to K\Sigma$ analysis (Deans et al.): mass $1.70 \sim 1.75$ GeV, total width $0.210 \sim 0.270$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate about the number and nature of $S_{11}$ resonances in the 1.5–1.7 GeV region, where the quark model (e.g., Close (1979), Simon (1980)) predicts only two states ($S_{11}(1535)$ and $S_{11}(1650)$) but phenomenological analyses suggest a third. The paper builds on the chiral quark model approach of Li (1994, 1995) and the coupled-channel analysis of Batini\'c et al. (1995), and it contrasts with the VPI group's partial-wave analysis (1995) and the Kent State analysis. The existence of a third $S_{11}$ resonance would enable tests of the quasi-bound $K\Sigma$ or $K\Lambda$ state hypothesis proposed by Kaiser et al. (1995) and would motivate future kaon photoproduction experiments at CEBAF and ELSA.
