# Strong magnetic fields, nuclear matter, and anomalous magnetic moments

> Contribution for the Conference Proceedings of the Compact Stars in the QCD Phase Diagram III (CSQCD III) conference, December 12-15, 2012, Guarujá, Brazil. http://www.astro.iag.usp.br/~foton/CSQCD3

# Strong magnetic fields, nuclear matter, and anomalous magnetic moments

## Key claim

Including the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of baryons in a relativistic mean-field model of nuclear matter under a strong magnetic field of $3.3 \times 10^{19}$ G increases the strangeness content of matter at medium and high densities, and raises the proton and electron fractions, which the abstract does not quantify as being conditional on the specific magnetic field strength and coupling constant set chosen.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic mean-field (MF) theory of nuclear matter at zero temperature, including the full baryon octet (n, p, Λ, Σ, Ξ) and leptons (e, μ), with a uniform magnetic field along the z-axis. The anomalous magnetic moments of baryons are incorporated via a Pauli term in the Lagrangian, and the system is solved under β-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions using the coupling constants from Set 2 of Chiapparini (2009). Particle fractions are computed both with and without the AMM for a fixed magnetic field of $3.3 \times 10^{19}$ G.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strength used for illustration: $3.3 \times 10^{19}$ G.
- Nuclear magneton value given: $\mu_N = 3.15 \times 10^{-18}$ MeV G$^{-1}$.
- The anomalous magnetic moment of each baryon $b$ is defined as $\kappa_b = (\mu_b/\mu_N - q_b m_p/m_b) \mu_N$, where $\mu_b$ is the magnetic moment of baryon $b$.

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic mean-field studies of nuclear matter in strong magnetic fields, such as those by Glendenning and by Chakrabarty (1997), by explicitly including the anomalous magnetic moments of all baryons in the octet. The results are relevant for neutron star cooling models, as the increased proton and electron fractions enhance neutrino emissivity via the direct Urca process. The authors note that the coupling constants used are from Set 2 of Chiapparini (2009).
