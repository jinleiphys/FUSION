# Reproduction of exact solutions of Lipkin model by nonlinear random-phase approximation

> It is shown that the random-phase approximation (RPA) method with its nonlinear generalization, which was previously considered as approximation, reproduces the exact solutions of the Lipkin model. The nonlinear RPA is based on an equation nonlinear on eigenvectors and includes many-particle-many-hole components in the creation operator of the excited states. We demonstrate the exact character of solutions analytically for the particle number $N$ = 2 and, numerically, for $N$ = 20. This finding indicates that the nonlinear RPA is equivalent to the exact Schrödinger equation, which opens up new possibilities for realistic calculations in many-body problems.

## Key claim

The nonlinear higher random-phase approximation (RPA), previously considered an approximation except for a very limited case, is shown to reproduce the exact solutions of the Lipkin model for particle number N=2 analytically and for N=8 numerically, indicating equivalence to the exact Schrodinger equation.

## Method

The authors formulate the nonlinear higher RPA using an equation of motion for a phonon-creation operator that includes many-particle-many-hole components, without assuming boson commutation relations. They apply this to the Lipkin model, which consists of two fermion levels each with N-fold degeneracy, and solve the coupled equations iteratively: the eigenequation for excitation energies and amplitudes is solved, then the vacuum condition determines the ground state coefficients, and this cycle is repeated until convergence.

## Key numbers

- For N=2, the excitation energy of the odd-order subspace is E_10^o = sqrt(epsilon^2 + V^2), identical to the exact result.
- For N=8 with epsilon=1, the RPA breaking point occurs at V = -1/(N-1) = -0.143; calculations were performed up to about twice this strength.
- At V=-0.15 for N=8, the nonlinear higher RPA with matrix dimension d=2 reproduces the exact shell model result (E_10^o = 0.68405768907156 for k=1) with accuracy shown in the convergence plot.
- For N=20 at V=-0.072, the nonlinear higher RPA with d=2 gives comparable quality to the shell model with d=4.

## Context

This work extends the RPA framework beyond the standard 1p-1h truncation and the second RPA (which includes 2p-2h excitations), building on the equation-of-motion method of Rowe (1968). Prior self-consistent RPA studies by Jemmer and Knowles (2013) and Dukelsky and Schuck (1990) reproduced the ground and first excited states for N=2 but did not obtain the even-order excited states because their phonon operators lacked the c-number term or sufficient particle-hole components. The result enables exact treatment of the Lipkin model within the RPA framework and suggests possibilities for realistic many-body calculations without the symmetry-breaking problems that plague standard RPA at large interaction strengths.
