# On introducing Charge-Symmetry-Breaking terms to nuclear Energy Density Functionals

> The Charge-Symmetry-Breaking (CSB) character of the nucleon-nucleon interaction is well established. This work presents two different ways of introducing such effects into a nuclear Energy Density Functional (EDF). CSB terms are either coming from the effective theory expansion or are derived from electromagnetic mixing of $ρ^0$ and $ω$ mesons. These terms are then introduced to Skyrme and Quark-Meson-Coupling EDFs, respectively.

## Key claim

Charge-symmetry-breaking (CSB) terms can be introduced into nuclear Energy Density Functionals (EDFs) either by extending the Skyrme interaction in the spirit of effective theory or by modelling the electromagnetic mixing of ρ⁰ and ω mesons in the Quark-Meson-Coupling (QMC) EDF; however, the coupling constant derived from the ρ⁰-ω mixing mechanism is about twice as large as the value needed to reproduce experimental Mirror Displacement Energies (MDEs), suggesting that higher-order terms or additional mesonic CSB mechanisms are required.

## Method

The authors added class-III CSB contact terms to the Skyrme EDF at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) in the effective theory expansion, fitting the coupling constants to experimental MDE data for isospin doublets (A=7 to 75) and triplets (A=6 to 58). Separately, they derived a CSB term for the QMC EDF from the electromagnetic mixing of ρ⁰ and ω mesons, Fourier-transforming the NN scattering matrix to coordinate space and matching the resulting δ-function potential to the LO class-III Skyrme form. The QMC EDF with the ρ⁰-ω mixing term was then implemented in the HFODD code and its predictions for MDEs were compared to experimental values and to a fit of the coupling constant.

## Key numbers

- The ρ⁰-ω mixing coupling constant derived from meson parameters is t₀,ρω^III = -6.7(6) MeV fm³.
- For comparison, the fitted LO class-III coupling constants for Skyrme EDFs are: t₀,SV_T^III = -7.3(3) MeV fm³, t₀,SkM*^III = -5.4(2) MeV fm³, and t₀,SLy4^III = -5.5(2) MeV fm³.
- For the QMCπ-0 EDF, the fitted coupling constant is t₀,fit^III = -3.5(3) MeV fm³, which provides good agreement with experimental MDEs.
- The meson parameters used are: G_ρ = 4.90 fm², G_ω = 6.92 fm², m_ρ = 3.90 fm⁻¹, m_ω = 3.95 fm⁻¹, and ⟨ω|H_em|ρ⁰⟩ = -3500(300) MeV².

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme EDF approach of Baczyk (2018, 2019) by adding CSB terms and introduces a new CSB mechanism into the QMC EDF framework of Stone (2016) and Guichon (2004, 2006). The comparison shows that the ρ⁰-ω mixing term overestimates the CSB contribution needed to reproduce experimental MDEs, motivating future inclusion of higher-order terms from the expansion of Machleidt (2001) or other mesonic CSB effects. The results enable global DFT calculations of mirror nuclei that can be tested against ab initio Green Function Monte Carlo results using the AV18 potential of Wiringa (1995).
