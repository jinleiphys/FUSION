# Dynamical Model of Coherent Pion Production in Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering

> We study coherent pion production in neutrino-nucleus scattering in the energy region relevant to neutrino oscillation experiments of current interest. Our approach is based on a combined use of the Sato-Lee model of electroweak pion production on a nucleon and the Delta-hole model of pion-nucleus reactions. Thus we develop a model which describes pion-nucleus scattering and electroweak coherent pion production in a unified manner. Numerical calculations are carried out for the case of the 12C target. All the free parameters in our model are fixed by fitting to both total and elastic differential cross sections for pi-12C scattering. Then we demonstrate the reliability of our approach by confronting our prediction for the coherent pion photo-productions with data. Finally, we calculate total and differential cross sections for neutrino-induced coherent pion production, and some of the results are (will be) compared with the recent (forthcoming) data from K2K, SciBooNE and MiniBooNE. We also study effect of the non-locality of the Delta-propagation in the nucleus, and compare the elementary amplitudes used in different microscopic calculations.

## Key claim

The authors develop a microscopic model for coherent pion production in neutrino-nucleus scattering that combines the Sato-Lee model for the elementary electroweak pion production on a nucleon with a simplified Delta-hole model for pion-nucleus reactions, claiming that this approach provides a consistent framework for treating medium effects on the pion and electroweak pion production on the same footing with no adjustable parameters once the parameters relevant to medium effects are fixed by fitting to pion-nucleus scattering data.

## Method

The authors construct a pion-nucleus optical potential using the Sato-Lee pi-N scattering amplitudes as input, with medium modification of Delta propagation treated via a simplified Delta-hole model using local density approximation as proposed by Karaoglu and Moniz. All free parameters (spreading potential, phenomenological terms in the optical potential) are fixed by fitting to total and elastic differential cross sections for pi-12C scattering. The reliability of the approach is tested by comparing predictions for coherent pion photo-production with data before calculating neutrino-induced coherent pion production cross sections for a 12C target.

- The average hole energy is chosen as bar{e}_N = 16 MeV
- The non-local effect could reduce the cross section by a factor of approximately 2 for E_nu = 0.5 GeV and approximately 1.7 for E_nu = 1 GeV, as pointed out in prior work (Non-local, year not specified in text)

## Context

This work extends the Sato-Lee model, previously successful for pion production on single nucleons (Sato and Lee, year not specified; Sato, Uno, and Lee, year not specified), to nuclear targets by incorporating the Delta-hole model that has proved successful for pion-nucleus dynamics. The approach addresses the puzzling experimental situation where K2K (Hasegawa, year not specified) and SciBooNE (Hiraide, year not specified) report no observed CC coherent pion production while MiniBooNE (year not specified) reports observed NC coherent pion production, despite the expected isospin relation sigma_CC approximately 2 sigma_NC. The model enables predictions for neutrino-induced coherent pion production that can be compared with data from K2K, SciBooNE, and MiniBooNE, and provides a framework that treats the non-local effect of Delta-propagation in the nucleus, which was not included in the Rein-Sehgal model (Rein and Sehgal, year not specified) or previous microscopic calculations.
