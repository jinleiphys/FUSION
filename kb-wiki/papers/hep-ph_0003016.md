# Collisional Energy Loss of Fast Charged Particles in Relativistic Plasmas

> Following an argument by Kirzhnits we rederive an exact expression for the energy loss of a fast charged particle in a relativistic plasma using the quantum field theoretical language. We compare this result to perturbative calculations of the collisional energy loss of an energetic electron or muon in an electron-positron plasma and of an energetic parton in the quark-gluon plasma.

# Collisional Energy Loss of Fast Charged Particles in Relativistic Plasmas

## Key claim

The collisional energy loss of a fast charged particle in a relativistic plasma can be expressed exactly, to logarithmic accuracy, as a simple formula depending only on the effective thermal mass of high-frequency transverse plasma excitations, independent of any approximation to the full photon propagator or dielectric functions. This exact result is derived by translating Kirzhnits' argument based on a generalized Kramers-Kronig relation into quantum field theoretical language using self-energies, propagators, and spectral functions.

## Method

The author rederives the soft collisional energy loss from the imaginary part of the fermion self-energy in thermal field theory, using the full photon propagator in Coulomb gauge. A generalized Kramers-Kronig (Leontovich) relation is applied to the response function of the medium, relating the frequency integral appearing in the energy loss expression to the high-frequency and high-momentum limit of the transverse dielectric function. The hard contribution is determined by requiring independence from the arbitrary separation scale, yielding a total energy loss formula that depends only on the parameter ω₀, the effective thermal mass of transverse high-frequency plasma excitations.

## Key numbers

- For an electron or muon in an electron-positron plasma at high temperature, the collisional energy loss is given by dE/dx = (e²/4π) ω₀² ln(q_max/ω₀), where ω₀² = 3m_γ²/2 and the thermal photon mass m_γ = eT/3.
- The perturbative HTL-resummed result for v=1 is dE/dx = (e²/4π) ω₀² [ln(√(ET)/ω₀) + 0.120], confirming the general formula to logarithmic accuracy.
- For quarks in a quark-gluon plasma, the factor e² is replaced by C_F g² = 4g²/3; for gluons, by C_A g² = 3g².
- The effective gluon mass in a QGP with n_f thermalized quark flavors is m_g² = g²T²(1 + n_f/6)/3.
- The maximum energy transfer in the relativistic limit E ≫ Ω is q_max ∼ √(ET).

## Context

This work extends the argument of Kirzhnits (1987) by translating it into the quantum field theoretical language of self-energies, propagators, and spectral functions, showing equivalence between the plasma physics approach using dielectric functions and the thermal field theory approach using the imaginary part of the self-energy. The result reproduces the perturbative HTL-resummed calculations of Braaten and Thoma (1991) for electrons and muons in QED plasmas and for partons in QGP, while providing a simpler exact expression valid to logarithmic accuracy. The formula enables straightforward estimates of collisional energy loss for energetic leptons in supernova plasmas and for partons in heavy-ion collision fireballs, where it serves as a signature for quark-gluon plasma formation.
