# Pion Polarizability, Radiative Transitions, and Quark Gluon Plasma Signatures

> Can one expect gamma rays rates from the QGP to be higher than from the hot hadronic gas phase? Xiong, Shuryak, Brown (XSB) calculate photon production from a hot hadronic gas via the reaction $π^- + ρ^0 \rightarrow π^- + γ$. They assume that this reaction proceeds through the a$_1$(1260). They also use their estimated a$_1$ radiative width to calculate the pion polarizability; following a connection noted previously by Holstein. However, for a$_1$(1260) $\rightarrow πγ$, the experimental radiative width is more than two times less than the value 1.4 MeV estimated and used by XSB. We describe how the gamma production reaction can be studied in Fermilab E781 (via the inverse reaction, with detailed balance) via the Primakoff reaction $π^- + γ\rightarrow π^- + ρ^0$. Such a study can provide the data base for evaluations of the utility of gamma production experiments in QGP searches. One can experimentally check the a$_1$ dominance assumption of XSB. A remeasurement of the a$_1(1260)$ radiative width and of the pion polarizability in Fermilab E781 will also allow us to reevaluate the consistency of their expected relationship.

## Key claim

The gamma-ray production rate from a hot hadronic gas via the reaction π⁻ + ρ⁰ → π⁻ + γ, as calculated by Xiong, Shuryak, and Brown (XSB), may be significantly overestimated because the experimental radiative width of the a₁(1260) → πγ is more than two times smaller than the value of 1.4 MeV they assumed.

## Method

The paper proposes to study the inverse reaction π⁻ + γ → π⁻ + ρ⁰ via the Primakoff effect, where a high-energy pion interacts with a virtual photon in the Coulomb field of a target nucleus. This approach, planned for Fermilab E781, would measure the reaction rate for normal-mass pions and ρ mesons at normal temperatures, allowing a direct experimental check of the a₁ dominance assumption made by XSB. The invariant mass of the produced πρ system would serve as a signature for the reaction mechanism and reveal contributing resonances such as the a₁(1260), π(1300), a₂(1320), and a₁(1550).

## Key numbers

- The experimental radiative width for a₁(1260) → πγ is Γ = 0.64 ± 0.25 MeV, more than two times less than the value 1.4 MeV estimated and used by XSB.
- XSB calculated the pion polarizability as ᾱ_π = 1.8 × 10⁻⁴³ cm³ using their estimated a₁ radiative width.
- Holstein previously showed that meson exchange via a pole diagram involving the a₁ resonance provides a main contribution of 2.6 × 10⁻⁴³ cm³ to the pion polarizability.
- The photon production calculation by XSB applies to gamma rays above 0.7 GeV from the reaction π⁻ + ρ⁰ → π⁻ + γ.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of gamma-ray yields from quark-gluon plasma versus hot hadronic gas, following earlier studies by Chakrabarty et al. (1994) and Kapusta et al. (1991), and specifically critiques the calculation by Xiong, Shuryak, and Brown (XSB) which assumed a larger a₁ radiative width. The proposed Fermilab E781 experiment would provide the first direct experimental data on the π⁻ + ρ⁰ → π⁻ + γ reaction rate, enabling a reevaluation of whether gamma-ray production can distinguish QGP from hadronic gas, and would also remeasure the a₁(1260) radiative width and pion polarizability to test the relationship noted by Holstein (1990).
