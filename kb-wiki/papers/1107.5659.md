# System size dependence of nuclear modification and azimuthal anisotropy of jet quenching

> We investigate the system size dependence of jet-quenching by analyzing transverse momentum spectra of neutral pions in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\textrm{NN}}}$ =200 GeV for different centralities. The fast partons are assumed to lose energy by radiating gluons as they traverse the plasma and undergo multiple collisions. The energy loss per collision, $ε$, is taken as proportional to $E$(where $E$ is the energy of the parton), proportional to $\sqrt{E}$, or a constant depending on whether the formation time of the gluon is less than the mean path, greater than the mean free path but less than the path length, or greater than the path length of the partons, respectively. NLO pQCD is used to evaluate pion production by modifying the fragmentation function to account for the energy loss. We reproduce the nuclear modification factor $R_\textrm{AA}$ by treating $ε$ as the only free parameter, depending on the centrality and the mechanism of energy loss. These values are seen to explain the nuclear modification of prompt photons, caused by the energy lost by final state quarks before they fragment into photons. These also reproduce the azimuthal asymmetry of transverse momentum distribution for pions within a factor of two and for prompt photons in a fair agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a phenomenological model of parton energy loss with three distinct regimes (Bethe-Heitler, LPM, and complete coherence) can reproduce the nuclear modification factor R_AA for neutral pions in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, but the azimuthal anisotropy v_2(p_T) for pions is only reproduced within a factor of two, suggesting the uniform nuclear density assumption may contribute to the discrepancy.

## Method

The authors used NLO pQCD to calculate neutral pion and prompt photon production in nucleus-nucleus collisions, modifying the fragmentation functions to account for parton energy loss via gluon radiation. The energy loss per collision was taken as proportional to E, proportional to sqrt(E), or constant depending on whether the gluon formation time is less than the mean free path (Bethe-Heitler regime), greater than the mean free path but less than the path length (LPM regime), or greater than the path length (complete coherence regime). The average path length L was calculated using a Glauber model with uniform nuclear densities, and the energy loss coefficient was treated as the only free parameter, determined separately for each centrality and energy loss mechanism by fitting to R_AA data.

## Key numbers

- The Bethe-Heitler regime describes neutral pion R_AA for p_T up to about 5-6 GeV/c, with energy loss per collision dropping from about 10% to 8% of parton energy from near-central to mid-central Au+Au collisions
- The LPM regime describes R_AA for p_T in the range 6-10 GeV/c, with a parton of 10 GeV losing about 1 GeV in the first collision for most central Au+Au collisions and about 0.7 GeV for 50-60% centrality
- The constant energy loss regime describes R_AA for p_T greater than 8-10 GeV/c, with energy loss per collision decreasing from about 1.4 GeV for most central Au+Au collisions to about 1 GeV for 50-60% centrality
- The momentum transport coefficient q_hat varies from 0.25 GeV^2/fm for 0-10% centrality Au+Au to 0.39 GeV^2/fm for 50-60% centrality Au+Au
- For Cu+Cu collisions, q_hat is approximately 0.18 GeV^2/fm for all centralities considered
- The energy loss per unit length dE/dx for constant energy loss regime varies linearly with average path length L, with Au+Au showing 40-60% larger energy loss than Cu+Cu for the same L

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological model of Jeon et al. (2003) and the Wang, Huang, and Sarcevic model to study system size dependence of jet quenching by comparing Au+Au and Cu+Au collisions at the same energy. The authors find that the transition between Bethe-Heitler and LPM regimes occurs near p_T approximately 5 GeV/c, consistent with the LPM energy scale E_LPM approximately 5 GeV for mean free path 1 fm and k_T^2 approximately 1 (GeV/c)^2. The extracted q_hat values (0.25-0.39 GeV^2/fm for Au+Au) are lower than those reported by Wiedemann and Salgado (5-10 GeV^2/fm) and Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (about 2 GeV^2/fm), but comparable to values from Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev (0.35-0.85 GeV^2/fm). The work enables future studies to relax the assumption of equal mean free path and energy loss for quarks and gluons, and to incorporate more realistic nuclear density profiles to improve the description of azimuthal anisotropy.
