# Jet substructure of light and heavy flavor jets at RHIC

> Jet substructure studies at the Large Hadron Collider have been used to constrain parton distribution functions, test perturbative QCD, measure the strong-coupling constant, and probe the properties of the quark-gluon plasma. We extend these studies to lower energies at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider that would additionally allow us to test existing models of non-perturbative physics. In this study, we present a PYTHIA8-based Monte Carlo study of substructure of jets produced in $p+p$ collisions at 200 GeV. The selection criteria is adapted for a feasible measurement at sPHENIX. We consider different types of jet substructure observables such as jet angularities and primary Lund Plane with a special focus on suppression of collinear radiation around emitting heavy quark known as a dead cone effect.

## Key claim

This PYTHIA8-based Monte Carlo study demonstrates that jet substructure observables at RHIC energies (200 GeV p+p collisions) can reveal the dead cone effect, suppression of collinear radiation around heavy quarks, and that these measurements are feasible with the sPHENIX detector, though the PYTHIA8 approach to heavy quark radiation does not fully respect the dead cone effect and should be compared against other models.

## Method

The authors generated PYTHIA8 Monte Carlo events for p+p collisions at 200 GeV using the Detroit tune and NNPDF3.1 LO PDF set, with a minimum transverse momentum scale of 20 GeV. Jets were reconstructed using the anti-kT algorithm with radius R=0.4, and both ungroomed and soft-drop groomed (z_cut=0.2, beta=0) versions were analyzed. Jet angularities (lambda_1/2, lambda_1, lambda_2) and primary Lund Plane projections were computed for jets matched to light quarks, charm quarks, bottom quarks, and gluons, with fiducial cuts of pT1>30 GeV, pT2>20 GeV, and |eta_jet|<0.7 matching sPHENIX acceptance.

## Key numbers

- Minimum transverse momentum scale cutoff: PhaseSpace:pTHatMin = 20 GeV
- Leading jet transverse momentum threshold: pT1 > 30 GeV; subleading jet: pT2 > 20 GeV
- Jet pseudorapidity acceptance: |eta_jet| < 0.7, matching sPHENIX detector
- Jet radius parameter: R = 0.4
- Soft-drop grooming parameters: z_cut = 0.2, beta = 0
- Best operational point for quark-gluon discrimination: lambda_cut = 0.3, with quark efficiency epsilon_q = 0.43 and gluon efficiency epsilon_g = 0.85
- Dead cone suppression observed in b-jets within logarithmic range ln(1/Delta) approximately 2.5 to 3

## Context

This work extends jet substructure studies from LHC energies (extensively studied by ALICE, CMS, ATLAS) to the lower energy regime at RHIC, where non-perturbative effects like hadronization and underlying event contributions become more significant. The study builds on theoretical proposals by Cunqueiro (2018) to use the primary Lund Plane for dead cone observation and complements the first direct observation of the dead cone effect reported by the ALICE collaboration (2021). The results provide a framework for future sPHENIX measurements that will complement LHC jet substructure analyses and test QCD models across different energy regimes.
