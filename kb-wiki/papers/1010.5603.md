# Chiral unitary approach to η' N scattering at low energies

> We study the η' N interaction within a chiral unitary approach which includes πN, ηN and related pseudoscalar meson-baryon coupled channels. Since the SU(3) singlet does not contribute to the standard interaction and the η' is mostly a singlet, the resulting scattering amplitude is very small and inconsistent with experimental estimations of the η' N scattering length. The additional consideration of vector meson-baryon states into the coupled channel scheme, via normal and anomalous couplings of pseudoscalar to vector mesons, enhances substantially the η' N amplitude. We also exploit the freedom of adding to the Lagrangian a new term, allowed by the symmetries of QCD, which couples baryons to the singlet meson of SU(3). Adjusting the unknown strength to the η' N scattering length, we obtain predictions for the elastic η' N --> η' N and inelastic η' N --> ηN, πN, KΛ, KΣ cross sections at low η' energies, and discuss their significance.

## Key claim

The η'N scattering amplitude from standard chiral unitary coupled-channel methods is very small because the η' is mostly an SU(3) singlet and the singlet does not contribute to the standard interaction; only by adding a new symmetry-allowed term coupling baryons to the singlet meson, with its strength adjusted to match the experimental scattering length estimate, can the model reproduce the observed magnitude of the η'N interaction.

## Method

The authors use a chiral unitary approach solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in on-shell factorized form for coupled pseudoscalar-baryon channels (πN, ηN, η'N, KΛ, KΣ) with s-wave Weinberg-Tomozawa potentials. They then incorporate vector meson-baryon channels (K*Λ, K*Σ) via normal and anomalous pseudoscalar-vector couplings, including box diagrams and interacting terms that generate a dynamically produced resonance near 1970 MeV. Finally, they add a singlet-coupling term to the Lagrangian, parametrized by an unknown strength α, and adjust α to reproduce the empirical η'N scattering length modulus.

## Key numbers

- The η'p scattering length from the standard PB-only model is 0.0017 + i 0.0139 fm.
- Including PB-VB coupling with both normal and anomalous terms gives a η'p scattering length of 0.0210 + i 0.0192 fm.
- The experimental estimate from COSY is |a_η'N| ∼ 0.1 fm (Moskal 2000).
- The η-η' mixing angle used is θ_P = −14.34° (Ambrosino 2010).
- The subtraction constants used are: a_πN = 2.0, a_ηN = 0.9, a_η'N = 0.9, a_KΛ = 2.2, a_KΣ = −3.1, at scale μ = 1200 MeV.
- The π−p → η'n cross section at pion lab momentum 1600 MeV/c is measured to be around 0.1 mb (Rader 1973).
- The singlet strength α values that give |a_η'p| = 0.1 fm are α = −0.193 (attractive) and α = 0.256 (repulsive).

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Kaiser (1995), Oset and Ramos (1998), and Inoue (2002) for meson-baryon scattering to the η'N system, which had not been studied theoretically before. The dynamically generated resonance near 1970 MeV from vector-baryon interactions (Oset and Ramos 2010) lies close to the η'N threshold and influences the scattering. The results enable predictions for η' production reactions such as γp → η'p and pp → ppη', and provide robust predictions for inelastic η'N cross sections even when the elastic channel is adjusted to data.
