# Baryon Density and the Dilated Chiral Quark Model

> We calculate perturbatively the effect of density on hadronic properties using the chiral quark model implemented by the QCD trace anomaly to see the possibility of constructing Lorentz invariant Lagrangian at finite density. We calculate the density dependent masses of the constituent quark, the scalar field and the pion in one-loop order using the technique of thermo field dynamics. In the chiral limit, the pion remains massless at finite density. It is found that the tadpole type corrections lead to the decreasing masses with increasing baryon density, while the radiative corrections induce Lorentz-symmetry-breaking terms. We found in the large $N_c$ limit with large scalar mass that the tadpoles dominate and the mean-field approximation is reliable, giving rise a Lorentz-invariant Lagrangian with masses decreasing as the baryon density increases.

# Baryon Density and the Dilated Chiral Quark Model

## Key claim

The authors show that a Lorentz-invariant effective Lagrangian with density-dependent masses can be constructed at finite baryon density, but only in the large $N_c$ limit with a large dilaton mass, where tadpole diagrams dominate over radiative corrections that would otherwise break Lorentz symmetry. The pion remains exactly massless at finite density in the chiral limit, contrary to what might be expected from dynamical screening effects.

## Method

The authors start from a chiral quark model Lagrangian that includes the QCD trace anomaly via a dilaton field, and compute one-loop corrections to the masses of the constituent quark, the scalar dilaton field, and the pion at finite baryon density using thermo field dynamics at zero temperature. They evaluate the density-dependent self-energies from tadpole and radiative diagrams, and analyze the $N_c$ scaling of each contribution to determine when Lorentz invariance is preserved.

## Context

This work extends the dilated chiral quark model of Beane and van Kolck (1994) to finite baryon density, addressing whether the "mended symmetry" of Weinberg (1990) can be realized in dense matter. The authors connect their results to the Brown-Rho (BR) scaling hypothesis (Brown and Rho, 1991), which predicts that hadron masses scale with density in a universal way. The calculation shows that the mean-field approximation becomes reliable in the large $N_c$ limit with a massive dilaton, yielding the BR scaling relations $m^*/m = f_\pi^*/f_\pi \cong m_\chi^*/m_\chi$, and provides a foundation for constructing Lorentz-invariant effective Lagrangians at finite density.
