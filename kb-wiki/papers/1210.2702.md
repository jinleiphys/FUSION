# Double binding energy differences: Mean-field or pairing effect?

> In this paper we present a systematic analysis on the average interaction between the last protons and neutrons in atomic nuclei, which can be extracted from the double differences of nuclear binding energies. The empirical average proton-neutron interaction $V_{pn}$ thus derived from experimental data can be described in a very simple form as the interplay of the nuclear mean field and the pairing interaction. It is found that the smooth behavior as well as the local fluctuations of the $V_{pn}$ in even-even nuclei with $N\neq Z$ are dominated by the contribution from the proton-neutron monopole interactions. A strong additional contribution from the isoscalar monopole interaction and isovector proton-neutron pairing interaction is seen in the $V_{pn}$ for even-even $N=Z$ nuclei and for the adjacent odd-$A$ nuclei with one neutron or proton being subtracted.

# Double binding energy differences: Mean-field or pairing effect?

## Key claim

The empirical average proton-neutron interaction $V_{pn}$ extracted from double binding energy differences is dominated by mean-field (monopole) contributions, with pairing playing a relatively minor role; however, in $N=Z$ nuclei and adjacent odd-$A$ systems, a strong additional contribution arises from the isoscalar monopole interaction and isovector proton-neutron pairing, making $V_{pn}$ roughly two to three times larger than in $N\neq Z$ nuclei.

## Method

The author extracted the average proton-neutron interaction $V_{pn}$ from experimental nuclear binding energies using double difference formulas for even-even, odd-$A$, and odd-odd nuclei. The contributions from mean-field effects (one-nucleon separation energy differences $\delta S$) and pairing effects (differences in pairing gaps $\delta_n$, $\delta_p$) were separated and compared. Results were interpreted within a single-$j$ shell seniority model including monopole and pairing interactions, and compared with the Duflo-Zuker (DZ) mass model and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB-17) calculations.

## Key numbers

- The pairing gap differences $\delta_n$ and $\delta_p$ are mostly within $|\delta| \leq 100$ keV, indicating the minor role of pairing in $V_{pn}$ for $N\neq Z$ nuclei.
- The HFB-17 model reproduces experimental nuclear masses within a deviation of 581 keV; the simplified DZ model reproduces masses within around 550 keV.
- A refitted DZ model without explicit Wigner terms achieves a deviation from experiment of $\sigma = 0.537$ MeV.
- For $0d_{5/2}$ nuclei with $A\sim20$, the isoscalar monopole interaction $V_{m;T=0}\sim-5.5$ MeV and pairing strength $G\sim-0.8$ MeV.
- For $0f_{7/2}$ nuclei with $A\sim50$, $V_{m;T=0}\sim-1.6$ MeV.
- The $V_{pn}$ values in even-even $N=Z$ nuclei are roughly twice as large as those in neighboring $N\neq Z$ nuclei; in odd-odd $N=Z$ nuclei they are roughly three times as large.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Stoitsov et al. (2007), who showed that HFB calculations with Skyrme functionals reproduce global $V_{pn}$ properties but fail to explain local fluctuations. It also builds on the Duflo-Zuker mass model (Duf95) and the HFB-17 model of Goriely et al. (2009). The paper provides a simple decomposition of $V_{pn}$ into mean-field and pairing contributions, enabling a microscopic understanding of the Wigner effect in $N=Z$ nuclei and the bifurcation pattern in $V_{pn}$ around $N=126$ and $Z=82$ shell closures, as well as the sudden drop at $N=92$ in Er and Yb isotopes associated with shape transitions.
