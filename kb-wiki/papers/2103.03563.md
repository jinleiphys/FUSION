# Nuclear binding energy in holographic QCD

> Saturation of the nuclear binding energy is one of the most important properties of atomic nuclei. We derive the saturation in holographic QCD, by building a shell-model-like mean-field nuclear potential from the nuclear density profile obtained in a holographic multi-baryon effective theory. The numerically estimated binding energy is close to the experimental value.

## Key claim

The paper derives the saturation of nuclear binding energy (constant binding energy per nucleon for large mass number A) using holographic QCD, but the result depends on the approximation that the binding energy of nucleons at the Fermi level is negligible and on the specific harmonic approximation method used for the commutator square potential in the nuclear matrix model.

## Method

The authors first obtain the nuclear density profile from the nuclear matrix model, a holographic effective theory of baryons derived from the Sakai-Sugimoto model via AdS/CFT correspondence. They then invert the density profile using WKB approximation to derive a mean-field shell-model potential for nucleons. Using this holographic nuclear shell model, they calculate the total binding energy by summing the energies of all occupied states below the Fermi level, taking the Fermi energy as zero.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear binding energy per nucleon is calculated as B/A ≈ 9.66 MeV, compared to the experimental average of roughly 8 MeV and the Bethe-Weizsäcker volume term coefficient of about 16 MeV.
- The parameters of the nuclear matrix model are fixed using experimental nucleon mass M_N = 939 MeV and Δ mass M_Δ = 1232 MeV, yielding λ = 3.13 × 10^5 MeV^3 and M = 113 MeV.
- The result ranges from B/A ≈ 9.66 MeV to B/A ≈ 17.5 MeV depending on the harmonic approximation method used for the commutator square potential.
- The nuclear radius data for mononuclidic elements fit the holographic QCD prediction of a sub-leading A^{-2/3} correction with coefficients c_1 = 0.89 fm and c_2 = 1.5, while the model predicts c_1 ≈ 2.4 fm and c_2 ≈ 0.57.

## Context

This work extends the nuclear matrix model previously developed by Hashimoto (2010) and Hashimoto (2019), which had already reproduced nuclear radii, spectra, and magic numbers, to now also reproduce the saturation of nuclear binding energy. The result enables further holographic calculations of nuclear properties using the derived mean-field potential, and the predicted A^{-2/3} sub-leading correction to nuclear radii is confirmed by experimental data from mononuclidic elements. The approach contrasts with traditional nuclear shell models by deriving the potential from a holographic dual rather than phenomenological fitting.
