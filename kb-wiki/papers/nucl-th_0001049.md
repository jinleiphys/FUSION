# Freeze-out in hydrodynamical models in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Freeze-out of particles across 3-dimensional space-time hypersurface with space-like normal is discussed in a simple kinetic model. The final momentum distribution of emitted particles shows a non-exponential transverse momentum spectrum, which is in quantitative agreement with recently measured SPS pion and $h^-$ spectra.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the standard Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription fails for hypersurfaces with spacelike normal vectors, because the post-freeze-out distribution cannot be a thermal distribution; a kinetic model that enforces a cutoff for particles crossing the freeze-out front backwards yields a non-exponential transverse momentum spectrum that agrees quantitatively with SPS pion and h- spectra.

## Method
The authors construct a one-dimensional stationary kinetic model in the reference frame of the freeze-out front, where the momentum distribution is split into an interacting component (initially a Juttner distribution) and a free component. The interacting component evolves via a relaxation-time approximation that includes both particle loss to the free component (with a mean free path lambda) and re-thermalization (with a relaxation length lambda-prime), while the free component accumulates only particles crossing the front outwards. Conservation of baryon current and energy-momentum tensor across the front is enforced, and the parameters of the equilibrated Juttner distribution are updated locally as particles drain away.

## Key numbers
- The model is applied to a baryonfree, massless gas with an initial temperature T0 = 130 MeV.
- The calculation is performed at x = 100 lambda (where lambda is the mean free path for freeze-out) and at p_y = 0.
- The transverse momentum spectrum is shown to be curved, with the apparent slope parameter increasing with increasing transverse momentum, in agreement with SPS pion and h- spectra.

## Context
This work extends earlier efforts to improve the Cooper-Frye freeze-out description, as suggested in Bugaev (1996), Csernai et al. (1997), and Nagle et al. (1997). The kinetic model addresses the problem of spacelike freeze-out hypersurfaces, where the standard thermal post-freeze-out distribution is invalid. The results enable more accurate calculations of transverse momentum spectra and other observables in continuum and fluid dynamical models of heavy ion collisions, though further details (e.g., as noted in Bugaev and Greiner (1999)) remain to be worked out.
