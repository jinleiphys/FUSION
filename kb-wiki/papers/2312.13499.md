# Impact of the MARATHON data on \texorpdfstring{$F_{2n}/F_{2p}$}{F2n/F2p} and off-shell effects in light nuclei

> The neutron structure function, $F_{2n}$, has historically been extracted from measurements of the deuteron structure function, but our understanding of the nuclear effects on the bound proton and neutron limits the extraction of $F_{2n}$. The MARATHON collaboration recently extracted $F_{2n}$ from the comparison of $^3$H and $^3$He targets, where the nuclear effects are larger but nearly identical, yielding a precise extraction of $F_{2n}/F_{2p}$. This precise extraction can then be compared to deuteron extractions, providing important constraints on the nuclear effects in the deuteron. To ensure that this comparison is not biased by the specific model of nuclear effects used by MARATHON, we examine a range of models of the nuclear effects to obtain a more conservative, but more model-independent, extraction of $F_{2n}/F_{2p}$ for comparison with deuteron extractions. Even with the more conservative approach, the comparison suggests the need for significant off-shell corrections or other nuclear effects, beyond those include in most calculations, even for the weakly-bound deuteron.

## Key claim

Even after adopting a more conservative, less model-dependent extraction of \(F_{2n}/F_{2p}\) from the MARATHON \(^3\)H/\(^3\)He data, the comparison with deuteron-based extractions still requires significant off-shell corrections or other nuclear effects beyond those included in most calculations for the weakly-bound deuteron.

## Method

The authors extracted \(F_{2n}/F_{2p}\) from the MARATHON \(^3\)H/\(^3\)He structure function ratio using an average of multiple nuclear correction models \(\mathcal{R}_{ht}\) (the ratio of nuclear effects in \(^3\)He and \(^3\)H) from Afnan et al. (2003) and the Kulagin-Petti model, rather than relying on a single model. They applied a conservative renormalization of (1.25±1.25)% to the measured \(^3\)H/\(^3\)He ratio and compared the resulting \(F_{2n}/F_{2p}\) values to extractions from deuteron/proton measurements with and without off-shell effects from Arrington et al. (2011).

## Key numbers

- The MARATHON data cover the range \(0.195 < x < 0.825\) for the \(^3\)H/\(^3\)He structure function ratio.
- In the Kulagin-Petti model, nuclear effects in the \(^3\)H/\(^3\)He ratio were found to be <0.1% up to \(x=0.65\), growing to 0.43% at \(x=0.825\).
- The conservative renormalization applied to the \(^3\)H/\(^3\)He ratio is (1.25±1.25)%, which encompasses both the nominal 1.1% target thickness uncertainty and the MARATHON collaboration's (2.5±0.7)% adjustment.
- The conservative extraction increases \(F_{2n}/F_{2p}\) by about 0.025 compared to the original MARATHON result, with a slightly smaller effect at large \(x\).
- To reconcile the \(h/t\) and \(d/p\) extractions for \(x>0.6\) would require a 4\(\sigma\) shift in the normalization factor.

## Context

This work extends the MARATHON experiment (2021) by testing the model dependence of its \(F_{2n}/F_{2p}\) extraction, which originally relied solely on the Kulagin-Petti model. The results are compared to deuteron-based extractions from Arrington et al. (2011) and to global PDF analyses from the JAM collaboration (Cocuzza et al., 2021) and the CJ collaboration (Li et al., 2023). The discrepancy between \(A=3\) and deuteron extractions motivates further study of isospin-dependent nuclear corrections and flavor-dependent EMC effects, which can be probed by future Jefferson Lab measurements including tagged spectator proton experiments (BoNuS12) and parity-violating electron scattering in SoLID.
