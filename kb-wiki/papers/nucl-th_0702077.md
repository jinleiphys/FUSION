# J/psi absorption by nucleons in the meson-exchange model

> We reinvestigate the $J/ψ$ dissociation processes induced by the reactions with nucleons, $J/ψ+ N \to \bar{D}^{(*)} + Λ_c$, in the meson-exchange model. Main constraints used in this work are vector-meson dominance and charm vector-current conservation. We show that the cross section for $J/ψ+ N \to \bar{D} + Λ_c$ can be larger than that for $J/ψ+ N \to \bar{D}^{*} + Λ_c$ when these constraints are imposed. The dependence of the cross sections on the coupling constants is analyzed in detail, and the comparison with the recent quark-interchange model predictions is also made.

## Key claim
When vector-meson dominance and charm vector-current conservation are imposed as constraints, the meson-exchange model predicts that the cross section for \(J/\psi + N \to \bar{D} + \Lambda_c\) can be larger than that for \(J/\psi + N \to \bar{D}^{*} + \Lambda_c\), reversing the ratio found in the earlier meson-exchange model of LKL02.

## Method
The authors computed the total cross sections for the reactions \(J/\psi + N \to \bar{D}^{(*)} + \Lambda_c\) using effective Lagrangians with \(t\)-channel, \(u\)-channel, anomalous, and contact diagrams. Coupling constants for the \(J/\psi\) were fixed via vector-meson dominance (VMD) and charm vector-current conservation, while the \(DN\Lambda_c\) and \(D^*N\Lambda_c\) couplings were taken from SU(4) symmetry relations or QCD sum-rule predictions. A Gaussian form factor with a cutoff \(\Lambda\) was employed, and current conservation was restored using a universal form factor prescription.

## Key numbers
- The ratio \(R_{D/D^*} = \sigma(J/\psi + N \to \bar{D} + \Lambda_c) / \sigma(J/\psi + N \to \bar{D}^* + \Lambda_c)\) is approximately 10 with the SU(4) couplings and \(\kappa_{\psi\Lambda_c\Lambda_c} = \kappa_{D^*N\Lambda_c} = 0\) at \(\Lambda = 1.8\) GeV.
- With \(\kappa_{\psi\Lambda_c\Lambda_c} = -0.94\) and \(\kappa_{D^*N\Lambda_c} = 0\), the ratio becomes \(R_{D/D^*} \simeq 30\) at \(\Lambda = 1.8\) GeV.
- With both tensor couplings included (\(\kappa_{\psi\Lambda_c\Lambda_c} = -0.94\), \(\kappa_{D^*N\Lambda_c} = 2.65\)), the ratio drops to \(R_{D/D^*} \simeq 3\) at \(\Lambda = 1.8\) GeV.
- The peak cross section for \(J/\psi + p \to \bar{D}^0 + \Lambda_c\) is close to 2 mb with the SU(4) couplings and no tensor terms at \(\Lambda = 1.8\) GeV.
- Using QCD sum-rule couplings (\(|g_{DN\Lambda_c}| = 7.9\), \(|g_{D^*N\Lambda_c}| = 7.5\)) instead of SU(4) values, the ratio becomes \(R_{D/D^*} \sim 8\) (with \(\kappa_{D^*N\Lambda_c}=0\)) and \(\sim 0.7\) (with \(\kappa_{D^*N\Lambda_c}=2.65\)) at \(\Lambda = 1.8\) GeV.
- The VMD-derived coupling constants are \(g_{\psi DD} = g_{\psi D^*D^*} = 7.44\) and \(g_{\psi D^*D} = 3.84\) GeV\(^{-1}\); the tensor coupling \(\kappa_{\psi\Lambda_c\Lambda_c} \approx -0.94\) is obtained from the \(\Lambda_c\) anomalous magnetic moment.

## Context
This work extends the meson-exchange model of LKL02 for \(J/\psi\)-nucleon dissociation by imposing vector-meson dominance and charm vector-current conservation, which were not fully applied in that earlier study. The results are compared with the quark-interchange model predictions of Hilbert et al. (2007), which gave \(R_{D/D^*} > 50\) in contrast to the LKL02 value of \(R_{D/D^*} < 0.5\). The improved constraints bring the meson-exchange model closer to the quark-interchange model, but the ratio remains sensitive to the poorly known \(DN\Lambda_c\) and \(D^*N\Lambda_c\) coupling constants and tensor terms, motivating further theoretical studies of these couplings.
