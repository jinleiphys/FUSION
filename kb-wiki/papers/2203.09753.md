# Neutron skin systematics from microscopic equations of state

> This paper presents an analysis of neutron skins based on fully microscopic equations of state, including chiral two- and three-nucleon forces consistently at N3LO. Other theoretical predictions and recent constraints are also addressed, such as those from the PREX II experiment and the latest parity-violating electron scattering measurement of the 27Al neutron skin.

# Neutron skin systematics from microscopic equations of state

## Key claim
The paper argues that state-of-the-art microscopic equations of state based on chiral effective field theory favor a softer density dependence of the symmetry energy and smaller neutron skins than those extracted from the PREX II experiment, and that the tension between modern theoretical predictions and the PREX II result is "irreconcilable."

## Method
The author uses a droplet model that includes surface width contributions to compute neutron skins from microscopic equations of state derived from chiral two- and three-nucleon forces at N3LO. The symmetry energy J and its slope L at saturation density are taken from these microscopic equations of state and used as input to droplet model expressions that relate the neutron skin to J, L, and the surface stiffness coefficient Q. Predictions are compared across multiple nuclei including 208Pb, 48Ca, 27Al, and tin isotopes.

## Key numbers
- For 208Pb, the microscopic chiral EFT predictions give neutron skin S = 0.13...0.17 fm (from J = 31.3 ± 0.8 MeV, L = 52.6 ± 4.0 MeV)
- The PREX II experiment reports S = (0.283 ± 0.071) fm for 208Pb, with J = 38.29 ± 4.66 MeV and L = 109.56 ± 36.41 MeV
- For 48Ca, the predicted neutron skin range is S = 0.12...0.15 fm (from J between 25.2 and 30.4 MeV, L between 37.8 and 47.7 MeV)
- For 124Sn, the predicted neutron skin is S = 0.13...0.17 fm, compared to data of 0.185 ± 0.017 fm
- The surface width contribution ΔS reaches over 30% of the total skin near the region of Lead (I = 0.2 gives ΔS = 0.032...0.061 fm)
- Neutron star radius with M = 1.4 solar masses comes out between 11 and 13 km with the applied equations of state

## Context
This work extends the author's previous microscopic equations of state (Sammarruca 2021, 2022) for neutron and nuclear matter to predictions of neutron skins across multiple nuclei using a droplet model. It directly compares with the PREX II experiment (2021) for 208Pb and the parity-violating electron scattering measurement of 27Al (2021). The results are contrasted with phenomenological mean-field models (Skyrme and relativistic mean field) from Drischler (2019), DHW (2021), and LH (2019), which allow much larger variations in L. The paper also references ab initio coupled-cluster predictions for 208Pb from Hu (2021) and for 48Ca from CC (2016), finding consistency with those microscopic approaches. The work enables systematic comparison of neutron skin predictions across the nuclear chart using a consistent microscopic framework, and highlights the need for experimental benchmarks that can discriminate between soft and stiff symmetry energy predictions.
