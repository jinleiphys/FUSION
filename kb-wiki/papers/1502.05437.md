# Multistrangeness in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We discuss strangeness production in heavy-ion collisions in the broad energy range --- from SIS energies through AGS-SPS-RHIC and upto LHC energies. On several examples we demonstrate how the strange particle production can reveal information about the collision dynamics and about possible modifications of particle properties in medium. In particular the production of hadrons containing two and more strange quarks, like $Ξ$ and $Ω$ baryons, or a $ϕ$ meson is of interest. We conduct our discussion in the framework of the minimal statistical model, in which the total strangeness yield is fixed by the $K^+$ multiplicity. It is emphasized that in collisions with a small number of produced strange particles, the exact strangeness conservation in each collision event must be explicitly preserved.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while a minimal statistical model with exact strangeness conservation per event can describe singly-strange particle production across a broad energy range, the production of multistrange baryons (particularly $\Xi$ and $\Omega$) remains systematically underestimated at both low (SIS) and intermediate (AGS-SPS) energies, suggesting that these particles may decouple earlier from the fireball or require additional production mechanisms beyond thermal equilibrium.

## Method

The authors employ a hadronic kinetic model for strangeness production at AGS-SPS energies, integrating rate equations for kaon production with fireball expansion parameterizations between Bjorken and Landau hydrodynamic limits. At SIS energies, they implement exact strangeness conservation by dividing collision events into classes based on the number of produced $s\bar{s}$ pairs (single-, double-, triple-kaon events), with probabilities given by Poisson distributions and particle yields calculated from Gibbs statistics with in-medium potentials. For LHC energies, they use the DRAGON Monte Carlo event generator based on the blast-wave model with 277 hadron species to fit transverse momentum spectra.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon scalar potential $S_N \simeq -190$ MeV $\rho_B/\rho_0$ and vector potential $V_N \simeq +130$ MeV $\rho_B/\rho_0$ at nuclear saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.16/\text{fm}^3$
- Hyperon optical potentials at saturation: $U_\Lambda = -27$ MeV, $U_\Sigma = +24$ MeV, $U_\Xi = -14$ MeV
- Antikaon effective potential parameter $U_{\bar{K}} = -(70\text{--}150)$ MeV
- For Ar+KCl collisions at $1.76\,A$GeV (HADES): freeze-out baryon density $\rho_{B,\text{fo}} = 0.6\,\rho_0$ and temperature $T_{\text{fo}} = 69$ MeV with $U_{\bar{K}} = -75$ MeV
- Exact strangeness conservation reduces $R_{\Xi/\Lambda/K^+}$ by factor $\sim 0.5$, $R_{\Omega/\Lambda/K^-/K^+}$ by $\sim 0.2$, and $R_{\Omega/\Xi/K^+}$ by $\sim 0.4$ compared to calculations without it
- For Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\,A$TeV (LHC): kinetic freeze-out temperature $T = 98$ MeV, transverse flow gradient $\eta_f = 0.88$, exponent $n = 0.69$, average transverse velocity $\langle v_t \rangle = 0.65$
- Chemical freeze-out temperature at LHC: $T_{\text{ch}} = 152$ MeV with baryon chemical potential $\mu_B \simeq 0$

## Context

This work extends the minimal statistical model approach of Cleymans (2004) and the hadronic kinetic model of Kolomeitsev and Tomasik (2005) to address persistent puzzles in multistrange baryon production. The paper contradicts the ability of standard statistical models to reproduce the sharp $K^+/\pi^+$ "horn" at 30 $A$GeV without including broad resonances (as in Andronic et al., 2009), and shows that transport models (Kampfer, 2002) fail to describe $\phi$ enhancement at SIS energies. The results enable future investigations into early decoupling of multistrange baryons and catalytic production mechanisms involving hyperons, particularly relevant for HADES and ALICE experimental programs.
