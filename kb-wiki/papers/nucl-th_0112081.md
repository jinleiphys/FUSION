# Nonlocality of nucleon interaction and an anomalous off shell behavior of the two-nucleon amplitudes

> The problem of the ultraviolet divergences that arise in describing the nucleon dynamics at low energies is considered. By using the example of an exactly solvable model it is shown that after renormalization the interaction generating nucleon dynamics is nonlocal in time. Effects of such nonlocality on low-energy nucleon dynamics are investigated. It is shown that nonlocality in time of nucleon-nucleon interactions gives rise to an anomalous off-shell behavior of the two-nucleon amplitudes that have significant effects on the dynamics of many-nucleon systems.

## Key claim

The paper argues that after renormalization of effective field theories for nucleon-nucleon interactions, the resulting interaction operator is necessarily nonlocal in time, and this nonlocality causes the two-nucleon T-matrix to vanish at infinite energy rather than approach a constant, which is an anomalous off-shell behavior that standard Hamiltonian dynamics cannot describe.

## Method

The authors construct an exactly solvable separable-potential model where the form factor ψ(p) has asymptotic behavior |p|^{-α} with α ≤ 1/2, which causes ultraviolet divergences in the standard Lippmann-Schwinger equation. They solve the generalized dynamical equation of motion (Eq. 7) derived from their earlier generalized quantum dynamics framework, which replaces the Schrödinger equation for systems with time-nonlocal interactions. The T-matrix is obtained by solving a differential equation with an asymptotic boundary condition determined by the generalized interaction operator, and the model parameters are fitted to experimental nucleon-nucleon phase shift data.

## Key numbers

- The bound state energy in the ³S₁ channel is E_B = -2.2246 MeV, used as a pole condition for the T-matrix.
- For the ³S₁(np) channel, the fitted parameters are: α = 0.499, λ = 133.5 × 10², b·ρ = 433.8, d·ρ = 766.2, and b₂·ρ^{1-2α} = 1.696 × 10^{-7}, where ρ = 1 MeV^{-1}.
- For the ¹S₀(np) channel, the fitted parameters are: α = 0.499, λ = 131.8, b·ρ = 356.3, d·ρ = 3.651 × 10⁶, and b₂·ρ^{1-2α} = 1.694 × 10^{-7}.
- For the ¹S₀(pp) channel, the fitted parameters are: α = 0.499, λ = 320.0, b·ρ = 371.7, d·ρ = 6.763 × 10⁵, and b₂·ρ^{1-2α} = 1.695 × 10^{-7}.
- The off-shell behavior is shown at |p| = 500 MeV in the ³S₁ channel for np scattering.

## Context

This work extends the generalized quantum dynamics framework developed by Gainutdinov (2000) and the exactly solvable models from Gainutdinov and Mutygullina (2000) to the specific case of nucleon-nucleon interactions. It directly addresses the ultraviolet divergence problem in effective field theories for low-energy nucleon dynamics, showing that the renormalization procedure used by previous authors (e.g., the dimensional regularization of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation studied in 2000) leads to a non-Hamiltonian dynamics. The paper demonstrates that the resulting time-nonlocal interaction produces an anomalous off-shell behavior of two-nucleon amplitudes that makes the standard Faddeev equation ill-defined, which is identified as a serious problem in effective field theories. This opens the possibility of using the generalized dynamical equation directly for describing multinucleon systems instead of the Faddeev equation.
