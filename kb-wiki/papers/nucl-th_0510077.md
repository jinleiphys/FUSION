# e+e- pair production from nucleon targets in the resonance region

> We discuss two processes, the $πN \to e^+e^- N$ and the $γN \to e^+e^- N$ reactions, below and close to the vector meson production threshold ($1.40<\sqrt s <1.75$ GeV). The aim is to gain understanding of the coupling of vector fields (associated with $ρ^0$- and $ω$-mesons) to low-lying baryon resonances. These couplings are not well-known and important for baryon structure studies and for dynamical descriptions of vector meson propagation in the nuclear medium. The $e^+e^-$ pair production amplitudes are determined by the $πN \to ρ^0 N$, $πN \to ωN$, $γN\to ρ^0 N$ and $γN \to ωN$ amplitudes supplemented by the Vector Meson Dominance assumption. The vector meson production amplitudes are calculated consistently using a relativistic and unitary coupled-channel approach to meson-nucleon scattering. We display results showing the importance of the quantum interference between $ρ^0$- and $ω$-mesons in the $e^+e^-$ channel to unravel the strength of the coupling of $ρ^0$- and $ω$-mesons to specific baryon excitations. The $πN \to e^+e^- N$ and $γN \to e^+e^- N$ reactions underlie the more complex $πA \to e^+e^- X$ and $γA \to e^+e^- X$ nuclear processes whose measurement is planned at GSI (with the HADES detector) and under analysis at JLab (with the CLAS detector).

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the quantum interference between rho0- and omega-mesons in the e+e- channel provides a sensitive probe of the coupling strengths of these vector mesons to specific low-lying baryon resonances, but the approach is valid only for e+e- pair invariant masses not too far from (sqrt(s) - M_N) due to the assumption of relative S-wave between the vector meson and nucleon in the final state.

## Method

The authors compute the pi N -> e+e- N and gamma N -> e+e- N reaction amplitudes using a relativistic, unitary coupled-channel approach to meson-nucleon scattering, restricted to center-of-mass energies between 1.40 and 1.75 GeV. The vector meson production amplitudes (pi N -> rho0 N, pi N -> omega N, gamma N -> rho0 N, gamma N -> omega N) are calculated from an effective quasi-local Lagrangian, with baryon resonances N(1520), N(1535), N(1650), Delta(1620), and Delta(1700) generated dynamically. The e+e- pair production is then obtained by applying the Vector Meson Dominance assumption to relate the massive photon to the rho0 and omega fields, using coupling constants determined from the e+e- partial decay widths of the rho and omega mesons.

## Key numbers

* The Vector Meson Dominance coupling constants are |f_rho| = 0.036 GeV^2 and |f_omega| = 0.011 GeV^2, determined from the e+e- partial decay widths of the rho and omega mesons.
* For the pi- p -> e+e- n reaction at sqrt(s) = 1.55 GeV, the differential cross section is of the order of 10 nb GeV^{-2} due to destructive rho0-omega interference.
* For the pi+ n -> e+e- p reaction at sqrt(s) = 1.55 GeV, the differential cross section is of the order of 150 nb GeV^{-2} due to constructive rho0-omega interference.
* Crossing the omega production threshold at sqrt(s) = 1.75 GeV leads to a sharp increase in cross section: by two orders of magnitude for pi- p -> e+e- n and by one order of magnitude for pi+ n -> e+e- p.
* The antisymmetric (Bethe-Heitler-vector meson interference) cross section for gamma p -> e+e- p is more than two orders of magnitude smaller than the symmetric (total) cross section in the mass range of interest.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic coupled-channel approach of Lutz (2002) to compute vector meson production amplitudes and applies them to dilepton production processes. The results for the pi N -> e+e- N interference pattern are consistent with the earlier work of Titov (2003), though with different absolute cross section magnitudes due to different prescriptions for baryon resonance transition couplings. The reactions studied underlie the planned measurements at GSI with the HADES detector using secondary pion beams and the ongoing analysis of data from the CLAS detector at JLab (G7 experiment), enabling future extraction of vector meson-nucleon coupling strengths from experimental e+e- spectra.
