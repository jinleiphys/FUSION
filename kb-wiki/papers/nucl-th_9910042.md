# Particle-Number Reprojection in the Shell Model Monte Carlo Method: Application to Nuclear Level Densities

> We introduce a particle-number reprojection method in the shell model Monte Carlo that enables the calculation of observables for a series of nuclei using a Monte Carlo sampling for a single nucleus. The method is used to calculate nuclear level densities in the complete $(pf+g_{9/2})$-shell using a good-sign Hamiltonian. Level densities of odd-A and odd-odd nuclei are reliably extracted despite an additional sign problem. Both the mass and the $T_z$ dependence of the experimental level densities are well described without any adjustable parameters. The single-particle level density parameter is found to vary smoothly with mass. The odd-even staggering observed in the calculated backshift parameter follows the experimental data more closely than do empirical formulae.

# Particle-Number Reprojection in the Shell Model Monte Carlo Method: Application to Nuclear Level Densities

## Key claim
The particle-number reprojection method enables calculation of level densities for a series of nuclei (including odd-A and odd-odd nuclei) using Monte Carlo sampling for a single even-even nucleus, provided the Hamiltonian parameters vary only weakly with mass number A and the reprojection is performed on nuclei with similar N-Z values to minimize errors from the fixed single-particle energies.

## Method
The authors perform shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) sampling using a weight function proportional to the partition function of a single even-even nucleus (e.g., 56Fe), then reproject onto different particle numbers using ratios of partition functions and observables computed from the same Monte Carlo samples. The Hamiltonian includes single-particle energies from a Woods-Saxon potential plus spin-orbit, monopole isovector pairing with strength g0 ≈ 0.2, and a separable surface-peaked interaction expanded into quadrupole, octupole, and hexadecupole terms. ## Key numbers
- Pairing strength g0 ≈ 0.2, determined from experimental odd-even mass differences
- Thermal energies calculated for β between 0 and 1 MeV⁻¹ in steps of Δβ = 1/16, and between 1 and 2.5 MeV⁻¹ in steps of Δβ = 1/8
- Approximately 4000 independent Monte Carlo samples used at each β value
- Statistical error for energy at β ~ 2.5 MeV⁻¹ is ~0.5 MeV for odd-A and odd-odd nuclei
- Ground state energy of odd-A and odd-odd nuclei estimated to be reliable to about ~0.3 MeV
- Level densities fitted to the backshifted Bethe formula (BBF) in the range Ex < 20 MeV, with good fit down to ~1 MeV or smaller

## Context
This work extends the SMMC level density method previously developed by the same group (Alhassid et al., 1997) for even-even nuclei in the iron-to-germanium region (Alhassid et al., 1998), enabling calculations for odd-A and odd-odd nuclei that suffer from an additional sign problem. The method uses a good-sign Hamiltonian in the complete (pf + g9/2)-shell, following the approach of Alhassid et al. (1994) for overcoming the sign problem. The results enable systematic comparison with experimental level densities and empirical formulae (Dilg, 1973; Grimes, 1974), and reveal a Tz dependence of level densities that is usually ignored in empirical formulae but is important for nuclear reaction rate calculations in nucleosynthesis (e.g., s-process, r-process, rp-process).
