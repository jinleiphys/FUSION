# Phase Structure of Nambu-Jona-Lasinio Model at Finite Isospin Density

> In the frame of flavor SU(2) Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with $U_A(1)$ breaking term we found that, the structure of two chiral phase transition lines does not exist at low isospin density in real world, and the critical isospin chemical potential for pion superfluidity is exactly the pion mass in the vacuum.

# Phase Structure of Nambu-Jona-Lasinio Model at Finite Isospin Density

## Key claim

The two chiral phase transition lines in the temperature-baryon chemical potential plane predicted by the NJL model without the U_A(1) breaking term are cancelled by the strong U_A(1) breaking term at low isospin chemical potential in the real world with N_c=3, but the critical isospin chemical potential for pion superfluidity is exactly the vacuum pion mass independent of model parameters.

## Method

The authors use the flavor SU(2) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model including the 't Hooft determinant term for U_A(1) breaking, treating quarks in mean field approximation and mesons in Random Phase Approximation (RPA). They determine the two coupling constants G and K separately by fitting the vacuum meson masses, including the eta-prime meson mass, and then solve the gap equations for the chiral condensates and pion condensate as functions of temperature, baryon chemical potential, and isospin chemical potential. The critical isospin chemical potential is derived analytically from the gap equation for the pion condensate at zero temperature and zero baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The critical isospin chemical potential for pion superfluidity at T=0 and mu_B=0 is exactly the vacuum pion mass: mu_I^c = m_pi = 140.2 MeV (using the fitted vacuum parameters).
- The fitted vacuum parameters from prior work (NJL3) are: m_0 = 6 MeV, Lambda = 590 MeV, (G+K)Lambda^2/2 = 2.435, yielding sigma = 2(-241.5 MeV)^3, m_pi = 140.2 MeV, f_pi = 92.6 MeV.
- Using m_eta-prime = 958 MeV gives alpha = K/(G+K) = 0.29, which is larger than the critical value alpha = 0.11 for the two-line structure at mu_I = 60 MeV.
- For a wide eta-prime mass region 540 MeV < m_eta-prime < 1190 MeV, alpha > 0.11, and for 720 MeV < m_eta-prime < 1140 MeV, alpha > 0.21 (the value at which the two lines coincide at mu_I = mu_I^c).
- In the chiral limit with K=0, the difference in baryon chemical potential between the two critical points at fixed T and mu_I is Delta mu_B(T, mu_I) = 3 mu_I.

## Context

This work extends prior NJL model studies at finite isospin density (NJL1, NJL2, NJL3) by resolving two open problems: the existence of two chiral phase transition lines and the value of the critical isospin chemical potential. It contradicts the prediction of two chiral phase transition lines found in NJL1 (without U_A(1) breaking) and random matrix method (random) and ladder QCD (ladder), showing that the U_A(1) breaking term cancels this structure for realistic parameters. It confirms the lattice QCD result (LA) that mu_I^c is approximately m_pi, proving it exactly within the NJL framework. The work enables future studies of the temperature and density dependence of chiral and pion condensates and meson masses, and extension to flavor SU(3) NJL models.
