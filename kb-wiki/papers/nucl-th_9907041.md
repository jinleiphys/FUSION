# Gaps and Critical Temperature for Color Superconductivity

> Because of a logarithmic enhancement from soft, collinear magnetic gluons, in dense quark matter the gap for a color superconducting condensate with spin zero depends upon the QCD coupling constant g not as exp(-1/g^2), like in BCS theory, but as exp(-1/g). In weak coupling, the ratio of the transition temperature to the spin-zero gap at zero temperature is the same as in BCS theory. We classify the gaps with spin one, and find that they are of the same order in g as the spin-zero gap.

# Gaps and Critical Temperature for Color Superconductivity

## Key claim

In dense quark matter, the spin-zero color superconducting gap depends on the QCD coupling constant $g$ as $\exp(-1/g)$ rather than the BCS-like $\exp(-1/g^2)$, due to a logarithmic enhancement from soft, collinear magnetic gluons; however, the ratio of the critical temperature to the zero-temperature gap remains the same as in BCS theory to leading order in $g$, and spin-one gaps are of the same order in $g$ as the spin-zero gap.

## Method

The authors solved the one-loop gap equation for color superconducting condensates in dense quark matter, including hard dense loop (HDL) corrections to the gluon propagator and using spectral representations to evaluate Matsubara sums. They extended Son's (1999) renormalization group analysis by estimating the constant prefactor $b_0$ in the spin-zero gap expression, and solved the gap equation at non-zero temperature to determine the critical temperature. They also classified spin-one condensates into longitudinal and transverse types and derived their gap equations.

## Key numbers

- The spin-zero gap at zero temperature: $\phi_0 = 2 b \mu \exp(-\pi/(2\bar{g}))$, where $\bar{g} = g/(3\sqrt{2}\pi)$ and $b = b_0/g^5 = 256 \pi^4 (2/(g^2 N_f))^{5/2} b_0'$, with $b_0'$ an undetermined constant
- The exponential coefficient: $c_0 = 3\pi^2/\sqrt{2}$ in the expression $\phi_0 \sim b_0 \mu g^{-5} \exp(-c_0/g)$
- Ratio of critical temperature to zero-temperature gap: $T_c/\phi_0 = \zeta/2 + O(g)$, where $\zeta = 2 e^\gamma/\pi \simeq 1.13$ (with $\gamma \simeq 0.577$ the Euler-Mascheroni constant), giving $T_c/\phi_0 \simeq 0.567$
- Extrapolated to strong coupling, the gap peaks at $\phi_0 \sim 0.26 \mu$ with $T_c \sim 0.15 \mu$ when $g \sim 4.2$ (for $N_f=2$, $b_0'=1$)
- The gluon mass parameter: $m_g^2 = N_f g^2 \mu^2/(6\pi^2) + (N_c + N_f/2) g^2 T^2/9$

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Son (1999) on the non-BCS exponential dependence of the color superconducting gap, and provides the first explicit calculation of the constant prefactor $b_0$ in the gap expression. The result that $T_c/\phi_0$ equals the BCS value contradicts earlier assumptions that color superconductivity would have a different temperature dependence. The finding that spin-one gaps are of the same order as spin-zero gaps is important for quark star phenomenology, where large strange quark mass and charge neutrality suppress spin-zero condensates; this extends the earlier classification of spin-zero condensates by Bailin and Love (1984) and the work of Schafer and Wilczek (1999). The results enable quantitative estimates of color superconducting gaps at physically relevant densities, suggesting that heavy-ion collisions at AGS energies ($\mu \sim 600$ MeV, $T \sim 100$ MeV) might produce observable color superconducting phases.
