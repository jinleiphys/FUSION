# Sizes of the Nucleon

> Evidences are updated and strengthened for the two-scales picture of low-energy nucleon structure as a compact `hard' valence quark core surrounded by a `soft' cloud of quark-antiquark pairs (the meson cloud). These considerations are quantified by a spectral analysis of the mean-squared radii associated with the isoscalar and isovector electric form factors of the nucleon. Further supporting arguments come from corresponding studies of the axial and mass form factors and their inferred radii. Separating low-mass (mesonic) and high-mass (short-range) contributions in the spectral representations of each of these form factors, we conclude that a central core with an r.m.s. radius of about 1/2 fm results consistently as the common feature in all cases. Implications are discussed for baryonic matter at densities beyond that of equilibrium nuclear matter.

## Key claim
The paper argues that a compact central core with an r.m.s. radius of about 1/2 fm is a consistent feature across multiple nucleon form factors (isoscalar electric, axial, and mass), but this conclusion depends on separating low-mass mesonic contributions from high-mass short-range contributions in spectral representations, and the core radius is not directly measured but inferred from such spectral decompositions.

## Method
The authors performed spectral analyses of dispersion relations for the isoscalar electric, isovector electric, axial, and mass form factors of the nucleon. They separated the spectral functions into low-mass (mesonic cloud) and high-mass (core) regions using a cutoff at t_c approximately 1.05-1.1 GeV^2, with the mesonic contributions modeled by vector meson poles (omega, phi, a1) and continuum contributions (rho-pi, K-anti-K), while the core part was parametrized by a series of high-mass poles fitted to data in both spacelike and timelike domains.

## Key numbers
- Isoscalar electric core radius: sqrt(<r_S^2>_core) = 0.50 ± 0.01 fm, from precision fit analysis of the isoscalar electric form factor
- Isovector electric core radius: <r_V^2>_core = -0.025 fm^2, consistent with zero and reflecting isospin symmetry cancellation between proton and neutron cores
- Axial core radius: sqrt(<r_A^2>_core) approximately 0.53 ± 0.02 fm, derived from axial form factor analysis using a1 meson spectral distribution with m_a = 1.23 GeV
- Mass core radius: sqrt(<r_m^2>_core) = 0.48 ± 0.05 fm, estimated from the mass form factor using the empirical mass radius sqrt(<r_m^2>) = 0.55 ± 0.03 fm from J/psi photoproduction
- Proton charge radius: sqrt(<r_p^2>) = 0.840 ± 0.003 ± 0.002 fm, from electron scattering and muonic hydrogen measurements
- Isoscalar charge radius: sqrt(<r_S^2>) = 0.775 ± 0.011 fm
- Isovector charge radius: sqrt(<r_V^2>) = 0.901 ± 0.009 fm
- Axial radius from dipole fits: sqrt(<r_A^2>) = 0.67 ± 0.01 fm (from neutrino-deuteron scattering and pion electroproduction)
- Axial radius from conservative analysis: sqrt(<r_A^2>) = 0.68 ± 0.11 fm (from neutrino-deuteron scattering and muon-proton capture)

## Context
This work extends the two-scales picture of nucleon structure previously explored in chiral quark models such as the chiral and cloudy bag models (Brown and Rho 1979, Thomas 1984) and chiral soliton models with vector mesons (Meissner 1986, Meissner 1987). The analysis uses precision fits to form factor data from Lin (2022) and spectral function methods from Belushkin (2007) and Hoferichter (2016). The results enable quantitative estimates of nucleon core properties relevant for understanding baryonic matter at densities beyond equilibrium nuclear matter, as encountered in neutron stars, and connect to studies of nucleon size from high-energy nucleus-nucleus cross sections (Nijs 2022) and J/psi photoproduction (Kharzeev 2021).
