# Status of nuclear PDFs after the first LHC p-Pb run

> In this talk, I overview the recent progress on the global analysis of nuclear parton distribution functions (nuclear PDFs). After first introducing the contemporary fits, the analysis procedures are quickly recalled and the ambiguities in the use of experimental data outlined. Various nuclear-PDF parametrizations are compared and the main differences explained. The effects of nuclear PDFs in the LHC p-Pb hard-process observables are discussed and some future prospects sketched.

# Status of nuclear PDFs after the first LHC p-Pb run

## Key claim
The inclusion of LHC p-Pb data on W, Z, and jet production (realized only in the EPPS16 fit) and a less biased incorporation of neutrino DIS data have advanced nuclear PDF determinations, but the resulting uncertainties remain large for certain flavors (especially strange quarks and small-x gluons) and the results are tied to the choice of free-proton baseline PDFs.

## Method
The author reviews five contemporary global fits of nuclear parton distribution functions (EPS09, DSSZ, KA15, nCTEQ15, EPPS16), all using the Hessian method for uncertainty estimation with dynamical tolerance criteria. The fits parametrize nuclear modifications at a low scale Q^2_0, solve DGLAP evolution to higher scales, and minimize a global chi-squared function against datasets including fixed-target DIS, Drell-Yan, RHIC pion production, neutrino DIS, and LHC p-Pb data on dijets and heavy gauge bosons. The EPPS16 fit is distinguished by allowing flavor-dependent nuclear modifications for sea quarks and using isospin-uncorrected DIS data and normalized neutrino cross sections.

## Key numbers
- EPPS16 fit uses 1811 datapoints with 20 free parameters and a global tolerance Delta chi^2 = 52
- nCTEQ15 fit uses 708 datapoints with 17 free parameters and tolerance Delta chi^2 = 35
- EPS09 fit uses 929 datapoints with 15 free parameters and tolerance Delta chi^2 = 50
- DSSZ fit uses 1579 datapoints with 25 free parameters and tolerance Delta chi^2 = 30
- Q cut in DIS data varies from 1 GeV (DSSZ, KA15) to 2 GeV (nCTEQ15)
- EPPS16 nuclear modifications shown at parametrization scale Q^2 = 1.69 GeV^2 and at Q^2 = 10 GeV^2
- LHC p-Pb data at sqrt(s) = 5 TeV and new data at sqrt(s) = 8.16 TeV are discussed

## Context
This talk reviews the status of nuclear PDF global analyses following the first LHC p-Pb run, extending earlier work by Eskola et al. (2009, EPS09), de Florian et al. (2011, DSSZ), and Kovarik et al. (2015, nCTEQ15). The EPPS16 fit (Eskola et al., 2016) is the first to include LHC p-Pb dijet and W/Z data, and to use neutrino DIS data with normalized cross sections to reduce bias. The review identifies open questions including the treatment of heavy-flavor production, the need for correlated systematic uncertainties in p-Pb measurements, and the potential of exclusive vector meson production in ultraperipheral Pb-Pb collisions to constrain nuclear gluons.
