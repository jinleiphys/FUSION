# The gallium anomaly revisited

> The gallium anomaly, i.e. the missing electron-neutrino flux from $^{37}$Ar and $^{51}$Cr electron-capture decays as measured by the GALLEX and SAGE solar-neutrino detectors, has been among us already for about two decades. We present here a new estimate of the significance of this anomaly based on cross-section calculations using nuclear shell-model wave functions obtained by exploiting recently developed two-nucleon interactions. The gallium anomaly of the GALLEX and SAGE experiments is found to be smaller than that obtained in previous evaluations, decreasing the significance from 3.0$σ$ to 2.3$σ$. This result is compatible with the recent indication in favor of short-baseline $\barν_{e}$ disappearance due to small active-sterile neutrino mixing obtained from the combined analysis of the data of the NEOS and DANSS reactor experiments.

## Key claim

Using nuclear shell-model wave functions with the JUN45 effective Hamiltonian, the gallium anomaly significance decreases from the previously estimated 3.0 sigma to 2.3 sigma, making it compatible with the recent indication from NEOS and DANSS reactor experiments for short-baseline antineutrino disappearance due to active-sterile neutrino mixing.

## Method

The authors calculated charged-current neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections for electron neutrinos from 37Ar and 51Cr sources on 71Ga using nuclear shell-model wave functions obtained with the JUN45 effective Hamiltonian in the model space consisting of proton and neutron orbitals 0f5/2, 1p, and 0g9/2. They computed Gamow-Teller and tensor matrix elements for transitions to the ground state and excited states in 71Ge, then used these to derive cross sections and reassess the ratio of measured to expected 71Ge event rates in the GALLEX and SAGE experiments.

## Key numbers

- The gallium anomaly significance decreases from 3.0 sigma (using Frekers et al. cross sections) to 2.3 sigma (using JUN45 shell-model cross sections)
- Total cross section for 51Cr neutrinos on 71Ga: (5.67 +/- 0.10) x 10^-45 cm^2
- Total cross section for 37Ar neutrinos on 71Ga: (6.80 +/- 0.12) x 10^-45 cm^2
- Ground-state-to-ground-state cross section for 51Cr neutrinos: (5.53 +/- 0.07) x 10^-45 cm^2
- Ground-state-to-ground-state cross section for 37Ar neutrinos: (6.62 +/- 0.09) x 10^-45 cm^2
- Ratio of measured to expected event rates (JUN45): 0.88 +/- 0.05 (correlated average)
- BGT ratio BGT500/BGTgs from shell model: (1.59 +/- 0.79) x 10^-2, compared to Frekers et al. value of 0.207 +/- 0.016
- The indication for active-sterile mixing from gallium data with JUN45 cross sections is at 1.9 sigma (Delta chi^2 = 5.7 with 2 degrees of freedom)

## Context

This work revisits the gallium anomaly originally discovered using Bahcall cross sections (1997) based on the (p,n) experiment of Krofcheck et al. (1985), which gave a 2.6 sigma anomaly. Haxton (1998) performed a shell-model calculation that slightly reduced the significance to 2.5 sigma, while Frekers et al. (2011) reported charge-exchange measurements that increased the anomaly to 3.0 sigma. The present JUN45 shell-model calculation produces cross sections smaller than all previous estimates, reducing the anomaly to 2.3 sigma and making it compatible with the sterile neutrino interpretation of the NEOS and DANSS reactor experiments. The authors note that the tensor contributions ignored in the Frekers et al. analysis may lead to systematic overestimation of BGT values for excited states.
