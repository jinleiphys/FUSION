# Finite-temperature effects on D-meson properties

> We study the spectroscopy and transport properties of charmed mesons in a thermal medium by applying an effective field theory based on chiral and heavy-quark symmetries in the imaginary time formalism. Relying on unitarity constraints and self-consistency we extract the in-medium properties (masses and widths) of $D$ and $D_s$ mesons and their interactions with light hadrons. We report our findings on 1) dynamically generated states, 2) thermal evolution of chiral partners, 3) in-medium scattering amplitudes, and 4) transport coefficients below the chiral restoration temperature.

## Key claim
The paper claims that including finite-temperature and off-shell effects in the self-consistent calculation of D-meson properties brings the transport coefficients closer to lattice-QCD results above the chiral crossover temperature, but the authors cannot offer a conclusive answer on chiral partner mass degeneracy because their model lacks information about the phase transition and does not implement back-reaction of the quark condensate.

## Method
The authors construct an effective field theory based on chiral and heavy-quark spin-flavor symmetries, extended to finite temperature using the imaginary-time formalism. They solve a coupled set of Bethe-Salpeter equations for the T-matrix and Dyson equations for the D-meson self-energy self-consistently, using unitarized scattering amplitudes with low-energy constants fixed from vacuum lattice-QCD. They then derive an off-shell kinetic theory via the Kadanoff-Baym formalism, reducing it to a Fokker-Planck equation to compute transport coefficients in the static limit.

## Key numbers
- The D-meson thermal mass shows a "timid decrease" with temperature; the ground states are the most affected, with K and anti-K contributions amounting to a "very small correction even at T=150 MeV"
- The D_s0*(2317) state is a bound state strictly only at T=0, receiving a "very small" thermal width as temperature increases
- The momentum-space diffusion coefficient kappa(T) and coordinate-space diffusion coefficient 2pi T D_s(T) are computed up to the chiral restoration temperature T_c approximately 156 MeV
- The full self-consistent calculation (black solid line) shows a "considerable increase" of kappa and "decrease" of 2pi T D_s compared to calculations using T=0 T-matrices (dashed brown lines)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies (Montana 2020, Torres-Rincon 2021) on D-meson interactions in vacuum to finite temperature, using the same effective Lagrangian approach. It contrasts with earlier calculations by Tolos (2013) that used T=0 T-matrices and vacuum masses without thermal widths. The results enable improved modeling of charm quark diffusion in heavy-ion collisions, providing hadronic-phase transport coefficients that smoothly connect to lattice-QCD calculations above T_c and to Bayesian analyses of experimental data.
