# New Three-Nucleon-Force Terms in the Three-Nucleon System

> We include two new three-nucleon-force terms of pion-range - short-range form in our momentum-space calculations for the three-nucleon continuum. These two terms are expected by chiral perturbation theory to be non-negligible. We study the effects of these terms in elastic neutron-deuteron scattering and pay special attention to the neutron vector analyzing power $A_y$.

## Key claim
Chiral perturbation theory predicts that two new three-nucleon-force terms of pion-range-short-range form are non-negligible in the three-nucleon continuum, and their inclusion in momentum-space calculations for elastic neutron-deuteron scattering modifies the neutron vector analyzing power A_y, though the abstract does not specify the magnitude of this modification or the extent to which it resolves discrepancies with data.

## Method
The authors performed momentum-space Faddeev calculations for elastic neutron-deuteron scattering, including two new three-nucleon-force terms derived from chiral perturbation theory. These terms have a pion-range-short-range structure and were added to the existing two-nucleon potential and three-nucleon-force framework. The calculations were carried out at a laboratory energy of 3.0 MeV, and the resulting observables (the spin correlation coefficient C_xx and the neutron vector analyzing power A_y, as well as the deuteron tensor analyzing power K^{x'z'}_y(d)) were compared with calculations without these terms.

## Key numbers
- At Elab = 3.0 MeV, the neutron vector analyzing power A_y (multiplied by 10) reaches a maximum near 0.4683 at approximately 105 degrees for one of the calculations including the new three-nucleon-force terms (dash-dot-dot pattern), compared to a maximum near 0.4518 at approximately 105 degrees for the calculation without these terms (solid line).
- The spin correlation coefficient C_xx at Elab = 3.0 MeV shows a minimum near 0.09668 at 90 degrees for the calculation without the new terms (solid line), while the calculation with the new terms (dash-dot-dot pattern) gives a minimum near 0.1356 at 90 degrees.
- The deuteron tensor analyzing power K^{x'z'}_y(d) (multiplied by 10) at Elab = 3.0 MeV reaches a maximum near 0.4046 at approximately 120 degrees for one calculation including the new terms (dash-dot-dot pattern), compared to a maximum near 0.3405 at approximately 120 degrees for the calculation without the new terms (solid line).

## Context
This work extends the program of incorporating chiral perturbation theory predictions for three-nucleon forces into few-body calculations, following earlier studies that included only the leading-order three-nucleon force (e.g., the Tucson-Melbourne or Urbana-type forces). The inclusion of these subleading pion-range-short-range terms is expected to improve the description of polarization observables in neutron-deuteron scattering, particularly the longstanding A_y puzzle. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data at low energies and provide a pathway for testing chiral effective field theory predictions in the three-nucleon system.
