# Analysis of Chiral Mean-Field Models for Nuclei

> An analysis of nuclear properties based on a relativistic energy functional containing Dirac nucleons and classical scalar and vector meson fields is discussed. Density functional theory implies that this energy functional can include many-body effects that go beyond the simple Hartree approximation. Using basic ideas from effective field theory, a systematic truncation scheme is developed for the energy functional, which is based on an expansion in powers of the meson fields and their gradients. Chiral models are analyzed by considering specific lagrangians that realize the spontaneously broken chiral symmetry of QCD in different ways and by studying them at the Hartree level. Models that include a light scalar meson playing a dual role as the chiral partner of the pion and the mediator of the intermediate-range nucleon-nucleon interaction, and which include a "Mexican-hat" potential, fail to reproduce basic ground-state properties of nuclei. In contrast, chiral models with a nonlinear realization of the symmetry are shown to contain the full flexibility inherent in the general energy functional and can therefore successfully describe nuclei.

## Key claim

Chiral hadronic models built upon the conventional linear sigma model with a "Mexican-hat" potential and a light scalar meson serving as both the chiral partner of the pion and the mediator of intermediate-range nucleon-nucleon attraction fail to reproduce basic ground-state properties of nuclei at the Hartree level, whereas chiral models with a nonlinear realization of chiral symmetry contain the full flexibility of the general energy functional and can successfully describe nuclei.

## Method

The authors constructed a relativistic energy functional containing Dirac nucleons and classical scalar and vector meson fields, truncated at quartic terms in the fields and quadratic terms in gradients, using an expansion in powers of the meson fields and their gradients relative to the nucleon mass. They then inverted the field equations to express the unknown coefficients in the energy functional directly in terms of nuclear matter properties near equilibrium, allowing systematic mapping of parameter space. Specific chiral lagrangians realizing spontaneously broken chiral symmetry in different ways were studied at the Hartree level and compared to the general functional's acceptable parameter regions.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter equilibrium density: approximately 0.15 fm^{-3} (standard value used for constraints)
- Bulk symmetry energy: approximately 35 MeV (used as a constraint for isovector physics)
- Nucleon effective mass at equilibrium density: 0.58 to 0.64 times the nucleon mass M (required range for successful phenomenology)
- Scalar meson mass: roughly 500 MeV (required for successful phenomenology)
- Pion coupling constant g_pi: approximately 13.5 (used in chiral model parameter relations)
- Axial coupling constant g_A: approximately 1.26 (used in Goldberger-Treiman relation)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous survey of chiral hadronic models (Furnstahl, 1993) by using a more complete inversion approach developed by Bodmer (1991) to systematically connect nuclear observables to model parameters. The analysis demonstrates that linear sigma models with a "Mexican-hat" potential fail generically at the Hartree level, while nonlinear chiral realizations (such as the model introduced by Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang in 1995) can succeed. The framework combines density functional theory (Kohn and Sham, 1965) with effective field theory ideas (Weinberg, 1967; Georgi, 1993) to justify including all allowable terms at a given truncation order, including quartic vector self-couplings that had been omitted in most prior relativistic mean-field studies.
