# Probing fluctuations and correlations of strangeness by net-kaon cumulants in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7$ GeV

> We calculate the cumulants and correlation functions of net-kaon multiplicity distributions in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7$ GeV using a multiphase transport model (AMPT) with both a new coalescence mechanism and all charge conservation laws. The AMPT model can qualitatively describe the centrality dependences of the net-kaon cumulants and cumulant ratios measured by the STAR experiment. By focusing on the stage evolution of the cumulants, cumulant ratios, and correlation functions, we reveal several key effects on the fluctuations and correlations of strangeness during the dynamical evolution of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, including strangeness production and diffusion, hadronization, hadronic rescatterings, and weak decays. Without considering the quantum chromodynamics critical fluctuations in the dynamic model, we demonstrate that the net-kaon fluctuations can largely represent the net-strangeness fluctuations. Our results provide a baseline for understanding the net-kaon and net-strangeness fluctuations, which help to search for the possible critical behaviors at the critical end point in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that, within the AMPT model without QCD critical fluctuations, net-kaon cumulants can largely represent net-strangeness fluctuations in Au+Au collisions at 7.7 GeV, but only after accounting for the fact that weak decays (which break strangeness conservation) significantly enhance the net-strangeness mean while leaving net-kaon cumulants unchanged.

## Method
The study uses the AMPT model with a string melting mechanism, a new quark coalescence hadronization model, and a charge-conservation version that enforces strangeness conservation in all hadronic reaction channels. Cumulants and correlation functions of net-kaon and net-strangeness multiplicity distributions are calculated for five evolution stages (initial partonic state, after parton cascade, after hadronization, after hadronic rescatterings, and final state with weak decays) in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) GeV, using the same kinematic cuts as the STAR experiment (\(0.2 < p_T < 1.6\) GeV/\(c\), \(|y| < 0.5\)).

## Key numbers
- Cumulants \(C_n\) (n=1–4) of net-kaon multiplicity distributions increase monotonically from peripheral to central collisions; AMPT results are all slightly smaller than STAR data.
- The opposite-charge two-particle correlation \(\kappa_2^{(1,1)}\) between \(K^+\) and \(K^-\) is significantly greater than same-charge correlations \(\kappa_2^{(2,0)}\) and \(\kappa_2^{(0,2)}\), indicating pair production of strangeness is dominant.
- Three- and four-particle correlation functions for \(K^+\) and \(K^-\) are consistent with zero within statistical uncertainties.
- For net-strangeness, the first-order cumulant \(C_1\) is close to zero from initial state through after hadronic rescatterings, but becomes significantly enhanced after weak decays.
- Cumulant ratios \(M/\sigma^2\) and \(S\sigma\) for net-strangeness gradually decrease from initial state to after hadronic rescatterings, and then increase suddenly after weak decays; \(\kappa\sigma^2\) for net-strangeness is consistent with unity within errors.
- Net-kaon cumulants and cumulant ratios remain unchanged over the last three evolution stages (after hadronization, after hadronic rescatterings, final state).

## Context
This work extends the authors’ previous AMPT studies on net-charge (Huang, 2021) and net-proton fluctuations (Chen, 2022) to the strangeness sector, using the same charge-conservation version of the model. It provides a baseline for interpreting net-kaon measurements from the STAR Beam Energy Scan (STAR, 2017), which showed no nonmonotonic behavior in net-kaon moments. The results enable future searches for QCD critical fluctuations at the critical end point by showing that net-kaon fluctuations can serve as a proxy for net-strangeness fluctuations, even though net-kaon is not a conserved quantity.
