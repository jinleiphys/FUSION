# Towards the global description of nuclear charge radii: exploring the Fayans energy density functional

> In this work, we study the odd-even effect in binding energies and charge radii, and systematic behavior of differential radii, to identify the underlying components of the effective nuclear interaction. We apply nuclear density functional theory using a family of Fayans and Skyrme energy density functionals fitted to similar datasets but using different optimization protocols. We inspect various correlations between differential charge radii, odd-even staggering in energies and radii, and nuclear matter properties. Detailed analysis is carried out for medium-mass and heavy semi-magic nuclei with a particular focus on the Ca chain. By making the surface and pairing terms dependent on density gradients, the Fayans functional offers the superb simultaneous description of odd-even staggering effects in energies and charge radii. Conversely, when the data on differential radii are added to the pool of fit-observables, the coupling constants determining the strengths of the gradient terms of Fayans functional are increased by orders of magnitude. The Skyrme functional optimized in this work with the generalized Fayans pairing term offers results of similar quality. We quantify these findings by performing correlation analysis based on the statistical linear regression technique. The nuclear matter parameters characterizing Fayans and Skyrme functionals optimized to similar datasets are fairly close.

# Towards the global description of nuclear charge radii: exploring the Fayans energy density functional

## Key claim

The Fayans energy density functional, with its density-gradient-dependent surface and pairing terms, can simultaneously describe odd-even staggering in both binding energies and charge radii, but only when differential charge radii data are included in the fit does the functional achieve this; without such data, the gradient-term coupling constants remain orders of magnitude smaller and the description of radii staggering is poor.

## Method

The authors applied nuclear density functional theory (DFT) using both Fayans and Skyrme energy density functionals, optimized to various combinations of experimental binding energies, charge radii, differential mean-square charge radii, and odd-even staggering indicators. They employed a least-squares fitting protocol with multiple datasets (basic observables, odd-even energy gaps, even-even energy gaps, differential radii, and odd-even staggering in radii) and performed statistical covariance analysis to quantify correlations between functional parameters and observables. Calculations were carried out in the spherical Hartree-Fock+BCS approximation with a soft pairing cutoff and stabilized pairing to avoid numerical instabilities near closed shells.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant \(h_{\nabla}^{\rm s}\) (surface gradient term strength) increases from 0.0992 in Fy(std) to 0.6656 in Fy(\(\Delta r\)) and 0.4861 in Fy(\(\Delta r,\Delta r^{\rm oe}\)) when differential radii data are added to the fit.
- The pairing gradient coupling constant \(h_{\nabla}^{\xi}\) increases from 0.013 in Fy(std) to 3.227 in Fy(\(\Delta r\)) and 3.8732 in Fy(\(\Delta r,\Delta r^{\rm oe}\)), an increase of about two to three orders of magnitude.
- The symmetry energy slope \(L\) for Sk+PFy is 18 ± 18 MeV, notably lower than the 45 ± 26 MeV of SV-min, despite sharing the same particle-hole interaction.
- The equilibrium density \(\rho_{\rm eq}\) for all optimized Fayans functionals lies between 0.160 and 0.163 fm\(^{-3}\) with uncertainties of 0.001–0.002 fm\(^{-3}\).
- The incompressibility \(K\) for all functionals is in the range 219–229 MeV, with uncertainties of 5–18 MeV.
- The exponent \(\gamma\) in the Fayans pairing functional is fixed at 2/3 for the main fits, as this value is favored when differential radii data are included; Fy(std) is fairly insensitive to \(\gamma\), while Fy(\(\Delta r\)) shows a clear minimum in \(\chi^2\) at \(\gamma = 2/3\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier Fayans functional studies (Fayans 1994, 1998, 2000) that demonstrated the ability to describe charge radii in semi-magic nuclei, particularly the challenging Ca chain where the near-equality of radii in \(^{40}\)Ca and \(^{48}\)Ca and the large radius of \(^{52}\)Ca had been a long-standing theoretical problem (Garcia Ruiz 2016, Rossi 2015, Minamisono 2016). The paper systematically compares Fayans functionals with Skyrme functionals (SV-min from Kluepfel 2009) and introduces a mixed Skyrme+Fayans pairing functional (Sk+PFy) to isolate the effects of the Fayans surface and pairing gradient terms. The results enable future global descriptions of charge radii across the nuclear chart, though the authors note that an \(A\)-dependent scaling of the Fayans pairing functional is still needed to simultaneously describe differential radii in both medium-mass and heavy nuclei.
