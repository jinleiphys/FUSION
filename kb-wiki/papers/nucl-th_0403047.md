# Coulomb corrections for quasielastic (e,e') scattering: eikonal approximation

> We address the problem of including Coulomb distortion effects in inclusive quasielastic (e,e') reactions using the eikonal approximation. Our results indicate that Coulomb corrections may become large for heavy nuclei for certain kinematical regions. The issues of our model are presented in detail and the results are compared to calculations of the Ohio group, where Dirac wave functions were used both for electrons and nucleons. Our results are in good agreement with those obtained by exact calculations.

## Key claim

Coulomb corrections for inclusive quasielastic (e,e') scattering on heavy nuclei can be treated using the eikonal approximation, yielding results in good agreement with exact Dirac wave function calculations, but the approximation may lead to errors up to 50% in cross sections for cases like lead at 300 MeV initial energy if the focusing factor is not included.

## Method

The authors model inclusive quasielastic (e,e') scattering as a nucleon knockout process using the eikonal distorted wave Born approximation (eDWBA), where electron wave functions are modified by an eikonal phase and a focusing factor accounting for the attractive Coulomb potential of the nucleus. The eikonal phase is computed analytically from a parameterized potential approximating a Woods-Saxon charge distribution, while the focusing factor is derived from Knoll's high-energy partial wave expansion for a homogeneously charged sphere. The bound nucleon wave functions are described by harmonic oscillator or Woods-Saxon models, and the outgoing nucleon is treated with an effective momentum approximation using an energy-dependent optical model potential.

## Key numbers

- For lead with initial electron energy 300 MeV and energy transfer 100 MeV, the eikonal approximation without the focusing factor leads to errors up to 50% in the cross sections.
- The kinetic electron energy in the outer region of a heavy nucleus like lead (radius approximately 6 fm) is increased by about 20 MeV due to the Coulomb potential.
- The equivalent radius of a homogeneously charged sphere is given by \(R_e = 1.128 A^{1/3} \, \text{fm} + 2.24 A^{-1/3} \, \text{fm}\) for nuclei with A > 20.
- The electric Sachs form factor used is \(G_E(Q^2) = (1 + Q^2/(843 \, \text{MeV})^2)^{-2}\).
- The optical model potential depth parameters for protons are \(v_1 = 67.2\), \(v_2 = 7.9 \times 10^{-3}\), \(v_3 = 2.0 \times 10^{-5}\), \(v_4 = 7 \times 10^{-9}\), and \(E_f = -5.9\).
- For neutrons, the optical model parameters are \(v_1 = 50.6\), \(v_2 = 6.9 \times 10^{-3}\), \(v_3 = 1.5 \times 10^{-5}\), \(v_4 = 7 \times 10^{-9}\), and \(E_f = -5.65\).
- Second order derivative terms in the expansion of the matrix element contribute about one percent to the total cross section for the case presented in Figure 4.

## Context

This work extends previous treatments of Coulomb corrections for quasielastic electron scattering, particularly comparing with exact Dirac wave function calculations by Kim et al. (Ohio group) and Udias et al., and with the effective momentum approximation (EMA) which the authors find fails for quasielastic (e,e') reactions. The eikonal approach provides a computationally simpler alternative to exact DWBA calculations, enabling practical treatment of Coulomb distortion for heavy nuclei where corrections are large, and is motivated by the need to interpret inclusive quasielastic data for studies of nuclear Fermi momentum (Whitney 1974), high-momentum components (Benhar 1994), sum rules (Jourdan 1996), and scaling properties (Day 1990, 1989).
