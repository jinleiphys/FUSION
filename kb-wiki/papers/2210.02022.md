# Parity-doublet bands in the odd-\bm{$A$} isotones \element{237}U and \element{239}Pu by a particle-number-conserving method based on the cranked shell model

> Based on the reflection-asymmetric Nilsson potential, the parity-doublet rotational bands in odd-$A$ isotones \element{237}U and \element{239}Pu have been investigated by using the particle-number-conserving (PNC) method in the framework of the cranked shell model (CSM). The experimental kinematic moments of inertia (MOIs) and angular momentum alignments are reproduced very well by the PNC-CSM calculations. The significant differences of rotational properties between \element{237}U and \element{239}Pu are explained with the contribution of nucleons occupying proton octupole-correlation pairs of $π^{2} i_{13/2}f_{7/2}$. The upbendings of moments of inertia of the parity-doublet bands in \element{237}U are due to the interference terms of alignments of protons occupying $πf_{7/2}$ (${1/2}$) and the high-$j$ intruder $πi_{13/2}$ $(1/2, 3/2)$ orbitals. The splittings between the simplex partner bands of the parity-doublet bands in both \element{237}U and \element{239}Pu result from the contribution of alignment of neutron occupying the $νd_{5/2}$ ($1/2$) orbital.

## Key claim

The particle-number-conserving cranked shell model (PNC-CSM) reproduces the experimental kinematic moments of inertia and alignments for parity-doublet bands in 237U and 239Pu, and explains the sharp upbending in 237U (absent in 239Pu) as arising specifically from interference terms of protons occupying the octupole-correlation pair π² i₁₃/₂ f₇/₂, not merely from the pair breaking of high-j intruder orbitals alone.

## Method

The reflection-asymmetric Nilsson Hamiltonian with quadrupole (ε₂), octupole (ε₃), and hexadecapole (ε₄) deformation parameters was diagonalized in a truncated cranked many-particle configuration space, with monopole and quadrupole pairing correlations treated by the particle-number-conserving (PNC) method. The cranked shell model Hamiltonian included the Coriolis interaction −ωJₓ, and the eigenstates were obtained by direct diagonalization in a Fock space of about 1000 configurations for both protons and neutrons. The angular momentum alignment was decomposed into diagonal and off-diagonal (interference) contributions to identify the microscopic origin of rotational band properties.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters for 237U: ε₂ = 0.210, ε₃ = 0.071, ε₄ = −0.045; for 239Pu: ε₂ = 0.230, ε₃ = 0.010, ε₄ = −0.055.
- Effective pairing strengths for both nuclei: G₀ₚ = 0.25 MeV, G₂ₚ = 0.03 MeV (protons); G₀ₙ = 0.15 MeV, G₂ₙ = 0.01 MeV (neutrons).
- Sharp upbending in 237U occurs at ħω ≈ 0.25 MeV for both s = +i and s = −i alternating-parity bands.
- Simplex splittings in the parity-doublet bands of both 237U and 239Pu occur at ħω < 0.10 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the PNC-CSM method, previously applied to alternating-parity bands in even-even nuclei by He (2020), to odd-A nuclei 237U and 239Pu. It addresses the open question from Wiedenhoever (1999) regarding the absence of backbending in 239Pu compared to heavier Pu isotopes, and explains the strong backbending observed in 237U by Zhu (2005). The results enable microscopic understanding of how octupole correlation pairs (π² i₁₃/₂ f₇/₂) and specific neutron orbitals (ν d₅/₂) control rotational properties and simplex splittings in actinide nuclei.
