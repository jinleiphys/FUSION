# Strange Stars with Realistic Quark Vector Interaction and Phenomenological Density-dependent Scalar Potential

> We derive an equation of state (EOS) for strange matter, starting from an interquark potential which (i) has asymptotic freedom built into it, (ii) shows confinement at zero baryon density and deconfinement at high density and (iii) gives a stable configuration for chargeless, beta--stable quark matter. This EOS is then used to calculate the structure of Strange Stars, and in particular their mass-radius relation. Our present results confirm and reinforce the recent claim by Li et al (Astron. and Astrophys. 303 (1995) L1) and Bombaci (Phys.Rev. C55 (1997) 1587) that the compact objects associated with the x-ray pulsar Her X-1, and with the x-ray burster 4U 1820-30 are strange stars.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the compact objects associated with the x-ray pulsar Her X-1 and the x-ray burster 4U 1820-30 are strange stars, based on a new equation of state for strange matter that avoids the large, density-independent bag constant required by the MIT bag model to fit the same observational data.

## Method

The authors derive an equation of state for beta-stable strange quark matter using a Hamiltonian with a density-dependent quark mass (to model chiral symmetry restoration) and a screened Richardson potential (to model asymptotic freedom and deconfinement via Debye screening). They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation for non-rotating strange stars to obtain mass-radius relations, which they compare to semiempirical mass-radius relations for Her X-1 and 4U 1820-30 extracted from x-ray data.

## Key numbers

* The quark mass at zero density is taken as a constituent mass of 310 MeV, falling to current masses of m_u = 4 MeV, m_d = 7 MeV, and m_s = 150 MeV at high density, via the function M_i = m_i + (310 MeV) sech(ν ρ_B / ρ_0) with ρ_0 = 0.17 fm⁻³.
* The scale parameter for the Richardson potential is Λ = 100 MeV.
* The bag model requires B ≃ 110 MeV/fm³ (≈ (170.5 MeV)⁴) to fit the data, compared to the standard value B = 56 MeV/fm³ ((144 MeV)⁴) from hadron spectroscopy.
* The two semiempirical mass-radius relations overlap in a region indicating a compact object radius of 6–8 km.
* The measured mass of Her X-1 is (1.1 – 1.8) M⊙.
* The maximum mass configuration changes by less than 10% for a 20% change in the parameters ν and α₀ around ν = 0.33 and α₀ = 0.20.

## Context

This work extends the claim by Li et al. (1995) and Bombaci (1997) that Her X-1 and 4U 1820-30 are strange stars, but replaces the MIT bag model (which requires a large, density-independent bag constant) with a model incorporating a density-dependent scalar potential and a screened vector interaction. The new equation of state produces mass-radius relations that fall within the semiempirical constraints for both candidates, whereas neutron star models based on conventional equations of state cannot reproduce these constraints. The work enables further tests of the strange star hypothesis using alternative QCD-inspired models.
