# Searching for Color Coherent Effects at Intermediate $Q^2$ via Double Scattering Processes

> We propose that measuring the $Q^2$ dependence of the number of final-state interactions of the recoil protons in quasi-elastic electron scattering from light nuclei is a new method to investigate Color Coherent effects at {\bf intermediate} values of $Q^2$ ({$\sim$ few $(GeV/c)^2$}). This is instead of measuring events without final-state interactions. Our calculations indicate that such measurements could reveal significant color transparency effects for the highest of the energies initially available at CEBAF. Measurements that detect more than one hadron in the final state, which require the use of large acceptance ($4π$) detectors, are required.

## Key claim

Measuring the $Q^2$ dependence of the number of final-state interactions of recoil protons in quasi-elastic electron scattering from light nuclei, rather than measuring events without final-state interactions, could reveal significant color transparency effects at intermediate $Q^2$ (a few (GeV/c)$^2$) for the highest energies initially available at CEBAF, provided that large acceptance ($4\pi$) detectors are used to detect more than one hadron in the final state.

## Method

The authors propose detecting the $(e,e'pp)$ reaction where one proton carries momentum close to that of the virtual photon and a second proton has momentum of about 400 MeV/c, large enough that its production is dominated by final-state interactions. They perform two schematic calculations: a probabilistic Monte Carlo simulation using the quantum diffusion model of Frankfurt, Frankfurt, Strikman, and Zhalov (1993) for a range of nuclei, and a quantum mechanical three-state model of Frankfurt, Greenberg, Miller, and Strikman (1993) for a $^3He$ target, both comparing color transparency predictions to Glauber (eikonal) approximations.

## Key numbers

- The second proton momentum is chosen as $p_2 \approx 400$ MeV/c, corresponding to $-t = 0.15$ GeV$^2$ for the rescattering.
- The quantum diffusion model uses $\Delta M^2 = 0.7$ GeV$^2$ for the expansion scale, with $\langle r_t(Q^2)^2 \rangle / \langle r_t^2 \rangle \sim 1$ GeV$^2 / Q^2$ for $Q^2 \geq 1.5$ GeV$^2$.
- In the probabilistic approach, the ratio of CT to Glauber calculations for $T_{el}/T$ shows a ratio of about 1.5 for $Q^2 \approx 7$ GeV$^2$/c$^2$.
- In the three-state model, the ratio $\Sigma_{2/G;1/G}$ decreases by factors between 2 and 10 as $Q^2$ is varied from about 1.5 to 7 GeV$^2$/c$^2$, with the largest effects (order of magnitude) for $Q^2$ between approximately 6 and 8 GeV$^2$/c$^2$.
- The $(e,e'p)$ Glauber cross section for the nucleon remains constant at approximately 0.68, controlled by the $pp$ cross section $\sigma = 40$ mb.
- The three-state model uses a $^3He$ wavefunction parameter $\alpha = 1.5$ fm$^{-1}$, with excited state masses $M_{N^*} = 1.44$ GeV and $M_{N^{**}} = 1.80$ GeV for one set, and $M_{N^*} = 1.80$ GeV and $M_{N^{**}} = 3.0$ GeV for another set.

## Context

This work extends the color transparency (CT) concept from the standard $A(e,e'p)$ reactions, where effects at intermediate $Q^2$ are small due to wavepacket expansion, to double scattering $(e,e'pp)$ processes where the cross section can vanish in the CT limit, providing greater sensitivity. The approach builds on the quantum diffusion model of Frankfurt, Frankfurt, Strikman, and Zhalov (1993) and the three-state model of Frankfurt, Greenberg, Miller, and Strikman (1993), and is motivated by the need to distinguish between perturbative QCD and non-perturbative models of point-like configurations (PLC) as discussed by Frankfurt, Miller, and Strikman (1992). The results emphasize the need for experimental study at CEBAF and for more refined calculations including realistic wave functions of $^{3,4}He$ and short-range correlations for heavier nuclei.
