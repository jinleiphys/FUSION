# Domain-wall Skyrmion phase of QCD in magnetic field: Gauge field dynamics

> The ground state of QCD in sufficiently strong magnetic field at finite baryon density is an inhomogeneous state consisting of an array of solitons, called the chiral soliton lattice (CSL). It is, however, replaced in a region with higher density and/or magnetic field by the so-called domain-wall Skyrmion(DWSk) phase where Skyrmions are created on top of the CSL. This was previously proposed within the Bogomol'nyi-Prasad-Sommerfield (BPS) approximation neglecting a gauge field dynamics and taking into account its effect by a flux quantization condition. In this paper, by taking into account dynamics of the gauge field, we show that the phase boundary between the CSL and DWSk phases beyond the BPS approximation is identical to the one obtained in the BPS approximation. We also find that domain-wall Skyrmions are electrically charged with the charge one as a result of the chiral anomaly.

## Key claim

By including the full dynamics of the gauge field beyond the Bogomol'nyi-Prasad-Sommerfield (BPS) approximation, the phase boundary between the chiral soliton lattice (CSL) and domain-wall Skyrmion (DWSk) phases remains identical to that obtained in the BPS approximation, even though the gauge field dynamics were previously neglected except through a flux quantization condition.

## Method

The authors constructed a low-energy effective worldvolume theory on the CSL using the moduli (Manton) approximation, promoting the moduli parameters of the solitons to fields on the (2+1)-dimensional worldvolume and integrating over the codimension coordinate. They then solved the full gauged CP^1 model equations for lump and anti-lump solutions, without imposing the BPS approximation, to determine the existence and stability of regular solutions as a function of the gauge coupling. The energy and electric charge of these solutions were computed from the chiral Lagrangian including the Wess-Zumino-Witten (WZW) term.

## Key numbers

- The tricritical point where the DWSk phase, CSL phase, and QCD vacuum meet is located at (mu_c, B_c) = (16 pi f_pi^2 / (3 m_pi), 3 m_pi^2 / e) approximately equal to (1.03 GeV, 0.06 GeV^2 ~ 1.0 x 10^19 G).
- The critical magnetic field for the CSL ground state is given by B >= B_CSL = (16 pi m_pi / f_pi^2) (e mu_B).
- The charged pion condensation (CPC) instability occurs asymptotically at large B as B >= B_CPC ~ (16 pi^4 f_pi^4) / mu_B^2.
- Gauged anti-lumps (corresponding to Skyrmions in the bulk) carry electric charge one as a result of the chiral anomaly.

## Context

This work extends the previous studies by Eto, Nitta, and collaborators (2023) that proposed the DWSk phase within the BPS approximation, where gauge field dynamics were neglected and only included via a flux quantization condition. The authors' earlier paper (Amari, Eto, Nitta, 2024) found that for gauged lump solutions in a CP^1 model, the flux quantization was only 15% satisfied and the energy was about 10% less than the BPS lump. The present result shows that the phase boundary is robust against including gauge dynamics, while also revealing that domain-wall Skyrmions carry unit electric charge, which enables future studies of electromagnetic signatures of the DWSk phase in neutron star interiors or heavy-ion collisions.
