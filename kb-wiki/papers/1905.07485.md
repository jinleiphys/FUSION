# On the convergence of nuclear effective field theory with perturbative pions

> The classic paper by Fleming, Mehen and Stewart cast doubts on the convergence of spin-triplet nucleon-nucleon partial wave scattering amplitudes when following the proposal of Kaplan, Savage and Wise to construct nuclear effective field theory around the unitary fermion limit with perturbative pion exchange. FMS identified the subclass of iterated one-pion exchange potential graphs as the cause of this poor convergence, which they showed persisted in the chiral limit. Theoretical tools are developed here to compute these Feynman graphs analytically to high order in all angular momentum channels simultaneously, examining the amplitudes computed to seven loops in the $L=J$ channels, and three loops in the coupled $L=J\pm1$ channels. One finds that there is nothing pathological about the perturbative expansion of a $1/r^3$ potential in general, and that the expansion converges satisfactorily in all partial waves except those with the lowest angular momentum, particularly the ${}^3P_0$ and the coupled ${}^3S_1-{}^3D_1$ channels. The results corroborate work by Birse, which suggests possible avenues to explore for improving the range of validity of the EFT expansion.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the poor convergence of the KSW expansion in spin-triplet nucleon-nucleon partial waves, previously identified by Fleming, Mehen and Stewart (FMS) at next-to-next-to-leading order, is not a general pathology of the perturbative expansion of a 1/r^3 potential; instead, convergence is satisfactory in all partial waves except those with the lowest angular momentum, specifically the ³P₀ and coupled ³S₁-³D₁ channels.

## Method

The author develops theoretical tools to compute iterated one-pion exchange ladder Feynman graphs analytically to high order by transforming the problem into a recursive algebraic relation in coordinate space, using analytic continuation of angular momentum to non-integer values as a regularization scheme. The scattering amplitudes are computed for the spin-triplet L=J partial waves up to seven loops and for the coupled L=J±1 channels up to three loops, all in the chiral limit where the pion mass is zero and the potential scales as 1/r³. Renormalization is performed using a Minimal Angular Momentum Subtraction scheme that subtracts poles in angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- Λ_NN = 285 MeV, defined as the fundamental scale of the KSW expansion, computed from the axial coupling g_A = 1.27, nucleon mass M = 939 MeV, and pion decay constant f = 131 MeV
- In the ³D₃ partial wave, the ratio of leading to subleading contributions to the real part of the amplitude is (3π/7) k̂, implying corrections exceeding 50% for k ≳ 105 MeV
- In the ³P₂ channel, the ratio is (3π/5) k̂, with 50% corrections encountered for k ≳ 75 MeV
- The seven-loop diagram for L=J partial waves takes approximately 0.01 seconds to compute using Mathematica on a laptop
- Divergences first appear at order n = 2L for a given angular momentum L

## Context

This work directly addresses the concerns raised by Fleming, Mehen and Stewart (1999) about the convergence of the KSW expansion for nuclear effective field theory, which had stalled the program after their NNLO calculations showed poor convergence in spin-triplet channels. The results corroborate earlier work by Birse (2005) on the renormalization of the 1/r³ potential and its connection to atomic physics methods developed by Cavagnero and Gao (1994, 1999). The paper provides analytic machinery for high-order calculations that enables more precise statements about convergence than previously possible, particularly for asymptotically large angular momentum, and suggests possible avenues for improving the range of validity of the EFT expansion.
