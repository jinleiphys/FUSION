# Formation of eta'(958) bound states by (gamma, d) reaction

> We have investigated the 6Li(gamma, d) reaction theoretically for the formation of the eta'(958) mesic nucleus. We have reported the numerical results in this article.

# Formation of eta'(958) bound states by (gamma, d) reaction

## Key claim
The authors report the first calculated results for the formation of eta'(958) mesic nuclei via the 6Li(gamma, d) reaction, but only for one specific potential parameter set (V0, W0) = (-150, -5) MeV as an example, noting that the strength of the eta'-Nucleus potential remains controversial and has not been determined well.

## Method
The authors apply the effective number formalism to evaluate the formation rate of eta'-alpha bound systems in the two-nucleon pick-up (gamma, d) reaction on a 6Li target. The bound eta' meson wave functions in the final state are calculated by solving the Klein-Gordon equation with an optical potential proportional to the alpha particle density distribution. The formation cross section is expressed as the product of the elementary cross section for eta' production and the effective deuteron number, summed over all combinations of final states with 4He and eta'.

## Key numbers
- The recoilless condition is satisfied at E_gamma ~ 1.0 GeV for the 1s state, E_gamma ~ 1.46 GeV for the 2s state, and E_gamma ~ 1.30 GeV for the 2p state.
- The optical potential parameters used are (V0, W0) = (-150, -5) MeV, with normal nuclear density rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}.
- The eta' meson mass m_eta' = 958 MeV is implicit in the energy transfer definition.

## Context
This work extends the study of Ikeno et al. on two-nucleon pick-up reactions for heavy meson bound state formation, improving upon previous work by including distortion effects, phenomenological elementary cross sections, realistic alpha density distributions from Hiyama, and recoil effects. The results are based on the eta'-Nucleus potential strength of around -150 MeV evaluated by Nagahiro (2005, 2006) using the NJL model, and supported by Jido (2012) based on chiral symmetry restoration. The authors note that this potential strength is contested by experimental data from Czerwinski (2014) indicating a small eta'N scattering length and from Nanova (2013) suggesting a shallow eta'-Nucleus potential, as well as theoretical work by Bass (2014) indicating weak attractive potential. The comprehensive results including shallow potential cases are to be reported in a forthcoming paper by Miyatani.
