# Transition Form-Factor for $η_Q$ at NNLO in the strong coupling $α_s$ and with all-order $v^2$ resummation

> In this work, we discuss both relativistic and perturbative QCD corrections to the transition form-factor ${\cal F}_{η_Q}{(t_1,t_2)}$ for the process $γ^*(q_1) γ^*(q_2) \leftrightarrow η_Q(P)$ with dependencies on the normalised photon virtualities $t_1=q_1^2/m_Q^2$ and $t_2=q_2^2/m_Q^2$, where $m_Q$ is the heavy quark mass. We resum a class of relativistic corrections to all orders in the relativistic parameter $\langle v^2 \rangle_{η_Q}$. In addition, we include perturbative QCD corrections up to Next-to-Next-to-Leading Order (NNLO) in the strong coupling constant $α_s$. This involves the computation of two-loop amplitudes with two off-shell photons. We explore three different phenomenological applications of our transition form-factors for the charmonium case. We first study the ratio $\vert {\cal F}_{η_c}{(t_1,0)} \vert/ \vert {\cal F}_{η_c}{(0,0)} \vert$ for the single space-like photon case and compare our results with the existing $η_c$ measurements from the BaBar collaboration. Secondly, we consider observables for the case of double space-like photons and discuss the impact of the different corrections. The NNLO corrections for this case are presented here for the first time in the literature. Finally, we revisit the decay width $Γ[η_c \rightarrow γγ]$ and compare it with the existing PDG value.

# Transition Form-Factor for $\eta_Q$ at NNLO in the strong coupling $\alpha_s$ and with all-order $v^2$ resummation

## Key claim

The authors compute the transition form-factor for $\gamma^*\gamma^* \leftrightarrow \eta_Q$ including both NNLO perturbative QCD corrections in $\alpha_s$ and all-order resummation of relativistic corrections in $\langle v^2 \rangle$, but the NNLO corrections for the double off-shell case are obtained only numerically (not analytically) due to the complexity of the two-loop master integrals with two off-shell photons.

## Method

The authors work within the NRQCD framework, expanding the transition form-factor in both the strong coupling $\alpha_s$ and the relativistic parameter $v^2$. They compute the two-loop virtual amplitudes for $\gamma^*(q_1)\gamma^*(q_2) \to (Q\bar Q)[^1S_0^{[1]}]$ with two off-shell photons using dimensional regularisation, performing integration-by-parts reduction to 199 master integrals. The master integrals are evaluated numerically using the auxiliary mass flow method implemented in AMFlow, rather than analytically. Relativistic corrections are resummed to all orders in $\langle v^2 \rangle$ via a generalised Gremm-Kapustin relation, matching the perturbative amplitude to NRQCD long-distance matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The relativistic correction function $g(t_1,t_2,\langle v^2 \rangle_{\eta_Q})$ reaches corrections of 10% to 30% compared to the leading order term for the charmonium case with $\langle v^2 \rangle_{\eta_c} = 0.20 \pm 0.07$
- At two-loop level, the amplitude with two off-shell photons requires 199 master integrals; for one off-shell photon this reduces to 136 master integrals; for the double on-shell case it reduces to 61 master integrals
- Benchmark results for the two-loop coefficient functions are provided at three kinematic points, e.g. at $(t_1=-3/5, t_2=-3/4)$: $a_{FF} = -20.67715$, $a_{FA} = -4.86457$, $b_{Fh} = 0.65669 + i\,2.16026$, $b_{Fl} = -0.26215 - i\,1.99684$, $c_{Fh} = 0.21271$, $c_{Fl} = -0.57472$
- All master integrals were computed to a precision of at least 30 digits for the relevant phase-space region

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the $\eta_Q$ transition form-factor by including NNLO QCD corrections for the double off-shell case, which are presented here for the first time. It builds on the single off-shell NNLO calculation by Feng et al. (2015) and the double on-shell two-loop results by Abreu et al. (2022). The relativistic resummation follows the approach of Bodwin et al. (2002, 2007) using the generalised Gremm-Kapustin relation. The results enable improved predictions for exclusive $\eta_c$ production in lepton-lepton collisions with two tagged leptons, comparison with BaBar measurements for the single off-shell case, and updated determinations of the $\eta_c \to \gamma\gamma$ decay width.
