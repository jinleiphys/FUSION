# Hidden charm pentaquark states in a diquark model

> The mass spectrum of hidden charm pentaquark states composed of two diquarks and an antiquark are calculated by use of an effective Hamiltonian which includes explicitly the spin, color, and flavor dependent interactions. The results show that the $P_c(4312)^+$ and $P_c(4440)^+$ states could be explained as hidden charm pentaquark states with isospin and spin-parity $IJ^P=1/2\left(3/2^-\right)$, the $P_c(4457)^+$ state could be explained as a hidden charm pentaquark state with $IJ^P=1/2\left(5/2^-\right)$, and the $P_{cs}(4459)^+$ state could be explained as a hidden charm pentaquark state with $IJ^P=0\left(1/2^-\right)$ or $0\left(3/2^-\right)$. Predications for the masses of other possible pentaquark states are also given, and the possible decay channels of these hidden charm pentaquark states are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the experimentally observed pentaquark candidates \(P_c(4312)^+\), \(P_c(4440)^+\), \(P_c(4457)^+\), and \(P_{cs}(4459)^+\) can be explained as hidden charm pentaquark states within a diquark-diquark-antiquark model, but only under specific assignments of isospin and spin-parity quantum numbers that differ from some earlier diquark-model analyses.

## Method

The authors construct wave functions for all possible \(qqqc\bar{c}\), \(qqsc\bar{c}\), \(qssc\bar{c}\), and \(sssc\bar{c}\) configurations in a diquark-diquark-antiquark model, assuming orbital angular momentum \(L=0\). They then calculate the mass spectrum using an effective Hamiltonian that includes spin, color, and flavor dependent interactions, with parameters fixed by fitting to known heavy baryon masses and selected tetraquark states. The masses of the pentaquark states are obtained by diagonalizing mass matrices for each flavor multiplet (singlet, octet, decuplet) and total spin.

## Key numbers

* The \(P_c(4312)^+\) state (experimental mass \(4311.9 \pm 0.7^{+6.8}_{-0.6}\) MeV, width \(9.8 \pm 2.7^{+3.7}_{-4.5}\) MeV) is assigned \(IJ^P = 1/2(3/2^-)\).
* The \(P_c(4440)^+\) state (experimental mass \(4440.3 \pm 1.3^{+4.1}_{-4.7}\) MeV, width \(20.6 \pm 4.9^{+8.7}_{-10.1}\) MeV) is assigned \(IJ^P = 1/2(3/2^-)\).
* The \(P_c(4457)^+\) state (experimental mass \(4457.3 \pm 0.6^{+4.1}_{-1.7}\) MeV, width \(6.4 \pm 2.0^{+5.7}_{-1.9}\) MeV) is assigned \(IJ^P = 1/2(5/2^-)\).
* The \(P_{cs}(4459)^+\) state (experimental mass \(4458.8 \pm 2.9^{+4.7}_{-1.1}\) MeV, width \(17.3 \pm 6.5^{+8.0}_{-5.7}\) MeV) is assigned \(IJ^P = 0(1/2^-)\) or \(0(3/2^-)\).
* Model parameters include diquark masses: \(M_{qq} = 1024\) MeV, \(M_{qc} = 2059\) MeV, \(M_{qs} = 1098\) MeV; constituent quark masses: \(m_{u(d)} = 313\) MeV, \(m_s = 470\) MeV, \(m_c = 1650\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the diquark-diquark-antiquark model previously applied to tetraquark states (Shi 2020) to the hidden charm pentaquark sector. It contradicts some earlier diquark-model assignments: for example, Ali (2019) assigned \(P_c(4312)^+\), \(P_c(4440)^+\), and \(P_c(4457)^+\) as \(J^P = 3/2^-\), \(3/2^+\), and \(5/2^+\), respectively, while Semenova (2019) assigned them as \(1/2^-\), \(1/2^-\), and \(3/2^-\). The present results provide specific quantum number predictions that can be tested by future LHCb analyses and enable searches for predicted flavor partners of these states.
