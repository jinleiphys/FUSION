# Bessel-weighted asymmetries and the Sivers effect

> We consider the cross section in Fourier space, conjugate to the outgoing hadron's transverse momentum, where convolutions of transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions become simple products. Individual asymmetric terms in the cross section can be projected out by means of a generalized set of weights involving Bessel functions. Advantages of employing these Bessel weights are that they suppress (divergent) contributions from high transverse momentum and that soft factors cancel in (Bessel-) weighted asymmetries. Also, the resulting compact expressions immediately connect to previous work on evolution equations for transverse momentum dependent parton distribution and fragmentation functions and to quantities accessible in lattice QCD. Bessel-weighted asymmetries are thus model independent observables that augment the description and our understanding of correlations of spin and momentum in nucleon structure.

## Key claim

Bessel-weighted asymmetries provide a model-independent way to study transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs) in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) because the soft factor cancels in these observables, but the scale dependence of the resulting functions and consequently the Q² dependence of the Bessel-weighted asymmetries remains to be studied.

## Method

The authors rewrite the SIDIS cross section in Fourier (impact parameter) space conjugate to the outgoing hadron's transverse momentum, where convolutions of TMDs and fragmentation functions become simple products. They project out individual asymmetric terms using generalized weights involving Bessel functions J_n(|P_h⊥| B_T), where B_T is the Fourier conjugate variable, and demonstrate that the soft factor cancels in the resulting weighted asymmetries due to its universality and flavor-blind nature.

## Key numbers

- The Bessel-weighted Sivers asymmetry at finite B_T is given by: A_UT = [-2 Σ_a e_a² H_UT,T(Q²) f̃_1T^⊥(1)a(x,z²B_T²) S̃(B_T²) D̃_1^(0)a(z,B_T²)] / [Σ_a e_a² H_UU,T(Q²) f̃_1^(0)a(x,z²B_T²) S̃(B_T²) D̃_1^(0)a(z,B_T²)]
- In the limit B_T → 0, the asymmetry reduces to: A_UT = -2 [Σ_a e_a² H_UT,T(Q²) f_1T^⊥(1)a(x) D_1^(0)a(z)] / [Σ_a e_a² H_UU,T(Q²) f_1^(0)a(x) D_1^(0)a(z)]
- The Bessel-weighted average transverse momentum shift is: ⟨k_y(x)⟩_TU^B_T = M [f̃_1T^⊥(1)(x,B_T²;μ²,ζ,ρ)] / [f̃_1^(0)(x,B_T²;μ²,ζ,ρ)]
- At large transverse momentum, TMDs behave asymptotically as f_1T^⊥(x,p_T²) ∝ 1/p_T⁴ and f_1(x,p_T²) ∝ 1/p_T²

## Context

This work extends the conventional weighted asymmetry approach of Kotzinian (1997) and Boer (1997) by replacing power-law weights |P_h⊥|^n with Bessel functions, which suppress divergent contributions from high transverse momentum. The Bessel-weighted asymmetries connect to the TMD factorization framework of Collins (1981, 1984), Ji (2004), and Collins (2011), and to lattice QCD calculations of TMDs by Musch (2010). The approach enables systematic study of the scale dependence of TMDs and fragmentation functions, and the Bessel-weighted average transverse momentum shift can be directly computed in lattice QCD to provide numerical evidence for non-zero T-odd TMDs from first principles.
