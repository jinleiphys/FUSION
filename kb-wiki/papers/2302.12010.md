# Nonextensive effects on QCD chiral phase diagram and baryon-number fluctuations within Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> In this paper, a version of the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model based on nonextensive statistical mechanics is presented. This new statistics summarizes all possible factors that violate the assumptions of the Boltzmann-Gibbs (BG) statistics to a dimensionless nonextensivity parameter $q$, and when $q$ tends to 1, it returns to the BG case. Within the nonextensive PNJL model, we found that as $q$ increases, the location of the critical end point (CEP) exhibits non-monotonic behavior. That is, for $q<1.15$, CEP moves in the direction of lower temperature and larger quark chemical potential. But for $q>1.15$, CEP turns to move in the direction of lower temperature and lower quark chemical potential. In addition, we studied the moments of the net-baryon number distribution, that is, the variance ($σ^{2}$), skewness (S), and kurtosis ($κ$). Our results are generally consistent with the latest experimental data, especially for $\sqrt{S_{NN}}>19.6\ \mathrm{GeV}$, when $q$ is set to $1.07$.

# Nonextensive effects on QCD chiral phase diagram and baryon-number fluctuations within Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

## Key claim

Within a nonextensive (Tsallis) version of the PNJL model, the location of the critical end point (CEP) depends non-monotonically on the nonextensivity parameter q: for q < 1.15 the CEP moves toward lower temperature and larger quark chemical potential, but for q > 1.15 it reverses direction toward lower temperature and lower quark chemical potential. The authors emphasize that this non-monotonic behavior may be model-independent and was not seen in earlier studies that only considered q up to 1.1.

## Method

The authors generalize the standard two-flavor, three-color PNJL model by replacing Boltzmann-Gibbs exponential and logarithmic functions with their Tsallis q-deformed counterparts (exp_q and ln_q) in the thermodynamic potential and in the resulting gap equations for the constituent quark mass M and the Polyakov loop Phi. The Polyakov-loop effective potential U is kept in its standard form, so nonextensive effects enter only indirectly through the saddle-point equations. The model parameters are fixed at T=0, mu=0, q=1, and the q parameter is treated as a thermodynamic variable on the same footing as T and mu, scanned over the range 1 <= q <= 1.2.

## Key numbers

- At mu=0 and q=1, the pseudo-critical temperature is 173 MeV; at q=1.15 it decreases to 148 MeV.
- For q=1, the CEP is located at (mu_c, T_c) = (171 MeV, 159.5 MeV).
- For q=1.05: (mu_c, T_c) = (251.4 MeV, 135 MeV); q=1.08: (284.9 MeV, 119.8 MeV); q=1.1: (288.4 MeV, 115.7 MeV); q=1.12: (290 MeV, 113 MeV); q=1.15: (290.2 MeV, 109.4 MeV); q=1.17: (287.4 MeV, 107.7 MeV); q=1.2: (281 MeV, 104.7 MeV).
- The critical value q_c where the CEP motion reverses is 1.15.
- The calculated moments S sigma and kappa sigma^2 are generally consistent with experimental data for sqrt(S_NN) > 19.6 GeV when q is set to 1.07.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Zhao 2021) by using a PNJL model with a Polyakov-loop-dependent coupling constant G(Phi), which doubles the value of T_c at q=1 compared to that earlier work and makes the nonextensive effects more pronounced at high temperatures. It contrasts with earlier Tsallis-statistics studies of the QCD phase diagram (Eur.Phys.J.A2016; shen2017chiral) that did not observe the non-monotonic CEP behavior because they only considered q up to 1.1. The results suggest that the search for the CEP in heavy-ion collisions depends on a critical q value, and that nonextensive effects should be considered when interpreting experimental data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program.
