# Measurement of the $^{229}$Th isomer energy with a magnetic micro-calorimeter

> We present a measurement of the low-energy (0--60$\,$keV) $γ$ ray spectrum produced in the $α$-decay of $^{233}$U using a dedicated cryogenic magnetic micro-calorimeter. The energy resolution of $\sim$$10\,$eV, together with exceptional gain linearity, allow us to measure the energy of the low-lying isomeric state in $^{229}$Th using four complementary evaluation schemes. The most accurate scheme determines the $^{229}$Th isomer energy to be $8.10(17)\,$eV, corresponding to 153.1(37)$\,$nm, superseding in precision previous values based on $γ$ spectroscopy, and agreeing with a recent measurement based on internal conversion electrons. We also measure branching ratios of the relevant excited states to be $b_{29}=9.3(6)\%$ and $b_{42}=0.3(3)\%$.

## Key claim

The isomer energy of 229mTh is measured to be 8.10(17) eV using a magnetic micro-calorimeter, superseding the precision of previous gamma spectroscopy measurements and agreeing with a recent measurement based on internal conversion electrons; the most accurate scheme extracts the energy directly from experimental data without relying on calculations of electronic state distributions.

## Method

The gamma ray spectrum from 0 to 60 keV produced in the alpha-decay of 233U was recorded using a cryogenic magnetic micro-calorimeter (maXs-30) with 8x8 pixel pairs, each having a 20-micrometer-thick gold absorber and a Ag:Er3+ paramagnetic temperature sensor read out by dc-SQUIDs. The isomer energy was extracted using four complementary evaluation schemes: lineshape analysis of the 29.2 keV doublet, energy differences between closely spaced line pairs at 29 keV and 42 keV, a decay loop involving the 42.4 keV and 13.2 keV lines, and subtraction of the measured intra-band transition energy from a synchrotron-based excitation energy reported by Masuda (2019).

## Key numbers

- 229Th isomer energy: 8.10(17) eV (153.1(37) nm) from the most accurate scheme using energy differences of closely spaced line pairs
- 229Th isomer energy from lineshape analysis: 7.84(29) eV
- 229Th isomer energy from decay loop: 8.1(1.3) eV
- 229Th isomer energy from absolute energy scheme: 7.8(0.8) eV
- Branching ratio b29: 9.3(6)% for the 5/2+ state inter-band decay
- Branching ratio b42: 0.3(3)% for the 42.4 keV state inter-band decay (upper one-sigma bound <0.7%)
- Energy resolution of the detector: approximately 10 eV (FWHM)
- Calibration uncertainty: 0.76 eV per peak
- Combined one-sigma interval for isomer energy: 7.88 eV < E_is < 8.16 eV

## Context

This work extends the gamma spectroscopy approach of Beck (2007, 2009) and Yamaguchi (2019) using a magnetic micro-calorimeter with improved energy resolution (approximately 10 eV versus approximately 30-40 eV in prior calorimetric experiments) and exceptional gain linearity. The result agrees with the isomer energy of 8.28(17) eV reported by Seiferle (2019) from internal conversion electron spectroscopy, but is extracted directly from experimental data without relying on calculations of initial/final electronic state distributions. The measurement enables more precise determination of the isomer energy for applications including a nuclear clock, tests of fundamental constant variation, and dark matter searches.
