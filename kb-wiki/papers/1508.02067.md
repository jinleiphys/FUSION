# Semi-relativistic wave-phase approximation for two-body spinless bound states in 1+1 dimensions

> An approximate quantum-mechanical two-body equation for spinless particles incorporating relativistic kinematics is derived. The derivation is based on the relativistic energy-momentum relation $mc^{2}+ε= \sqrt{m^{2}c^{4}+p^{2}c^{2}}+V$ for each single particle, where $mc^2$ is the particle rest mass energy, $p$ its linear momentum, $ε$ its dynamical energy, and $V$ being the time-like vector interaction potential. The resulting two-body equation assumes rapid wave oscillations in a single, slowly varying potential well. A Bohr-Sommerfeld-type quantization condition is obtained. The approximation is compared to exact results for the harmonic potential.

# Semi-relativistic wave-phase approximation for two-body spinless bound states in 1+1 dimensions

## Key claim

The authors derive an approximate two-body equation for spinless particles that incorporates relativistic kinematics via a wave-phase (WP) approximation, yielding a Bohr-Sommerfeld-type quantization condition; the approximation assumes rapid wave oscillations in a single, slowly varying potential well and is valid only when the potential range is significantly larger than the Compton wavelength of the relative system.

## Method

The derivation starts from the relativistic energy-momentum relation for each particle and expands the square-root kinetic operators in a power series in momentum. The authors apply an amplitude-phase decomposition of the wavefunction, imposing a relation between amplitude and phase that eliminates imaginary terms in the Schrödinger equation. The leading relativistic corrections are retained only in the oscillatory phase factors, while the non-relativistic differential amplitude terms are kept unchanged, resulting in a nonlinear amplitude equation (labeled WP) that is solved numerically with specific initial conditions at the potential minimum.

- For μ=5, m1=100, n=0: ε_R = 0.220584, ε_WP = 0.2202, ε_NR = 0.224
- For μ=5, m1=100, n=20: ε_R = 7.591316, ε_WP = 7.580, ε_NR = 9.2
- For μ=1, m1=2, n=0: ε_R = 0.480097, ε_WP = 0.476, ε_NR = 0.50

- For μ=1, m1=10, n=20: ε_R = 11.760647, ε_WP = 11.66, ε_NR = 20.5
- WP predictions are consistently lower than exact predictions by a factor in the range of 95-100%

## Context

This work extends the amplitude-phase method (Milne) and semiclassical Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization (Friedrich 2004) to include relativistic kinematics for two-body spinless systems. It addresses a deficiency of the local approximation approach used by Ikhdair and Rajabi, which is inapplicable to classically confining systems with potentials that increase indefinitely at large separations; the present method does not suffer from this limitation. The results enable approximate calculations of bound-state spectra for spin-0 states in sub-atomic and nuclear physics, though the method currently applies only to non-singular potential wells.
