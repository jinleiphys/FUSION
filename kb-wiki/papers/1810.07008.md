# Nonextensive quasiparticle description of QCD matter

> The dynamics of QCD matter is often described using effective mean field (MF) models based on Boltzmann-Gibbs (BG) extensive statistics. However, such matter is normally produced in small packets and in violent collisions where the usual conditions justifying the use of BG statistics are not fulfilled and the systems produced are not extensive. This can be accounted for either by enriching the original dynamics or by replacing the BG statistics by its nonextensive counterpart described by a nonextensivity parameter $q\neq 1$ (for $q \to 1$ one returns to the extensive situation). In this work we investigate the interplay between the effects of dynamics and nonextensivity. Since the complexity of the nonextensive MF models prevents their simple visualization, we instead use some simple quasi-particle description of QCD matter in which the interaction is modelled phenomenologically by some effective fugacities, $z$. Embedding such a model in a nonextensive environment allows for a well-defined separation of the dynamics (represented by $z$) and the nonextensivity (represented by $q$) and a better understanding of their relationship.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the effects of nonextensivity (parameterized by $q \neq 1$ in Tsallis statistics) and the effects of dynamics (parameterized by effective fugacities $z$) in QCD matter can be separated and understood independently, using a quasiparticle model where the interaction is encoded in temperature-dependent fugacities rather than in effective masses.

## Method

The authors embed the existing $z$-QPM (quasiparticle model with effective fugacities $z^{(i)}(T)$ for gluons and quarks) into a nonextensive Tsallis statistics environment characterized by a parameter $q$, creating a $qz$-QPM. They then solve for the nonextensive fugacities $z_q^{(i)}(\tau)$ that reproduce the same pressure as the original extensive $z$-QPM (which itself fits lattice QCD data), using the condition $P_{q=1}(T) = P_q(T)$ for temperatures above the critical temperature $T_c$. The analysis is limited to $T > T_c$ because lattice data only exist in that regime.

## Key numbers

* For $q=1$ (extensive case), the gluon fugacity parameters are $a_g = 0.803$, $b_g = 1.837$, $a'_g = 0.978$, $b'_g = 0.942$; for quarks $a_q = 0.810$, $b_q = 1.721$, $a'_q = 0.960$, $b'_q = 0.846$ (from fits to lattice data using Eq. (zzz) with cross-over at $\tau_g = 1.68$ for gluons and $\tau_q = 1.7$ for quarks).
* For $q=0.96$, the gluon parameters become $a_g = 0.985$, $b_g = 1.581$, $a'_g = 1.168$, $b'_g = 0.860$; for quarks $a_q = 1.030$, $b_q = 1.510$, $a'_q = 1.200$, $b'_q = 0.747$.
* For $q=1.04$, the gluon parameters become $a_g = 0.600$, $b_g = 2.221$, $a'_g = 0.766$, $b'_g = 1.180$; for quarks $a_q = 0.580$, $b_q = 2.061$, $a'_q = 0.712$, $b'_q = 1.050$.
* The change in fugacity $\delta z_q^{(q,g)} = z_q^{(q,g)} - z_{q=1}^{(q,g)}$ is less than 1 for all $q$ values considered.
* The chemical potential $\mu$ is varied between 0 and 100 MeV, with the condition $\mu/T \ll 1$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous nonextensive NJL model (Rozynek and Wilg, 2015) by replacing the complex dynamics of mean-field models with a simpler quasiparticle description (the $z$-QPM of Blaschke et al., 2010-2014) that uses effective fugacities to encode interactions. The approach enables a cleaner separation of dynamical effects (represented by $z$) from nonextensive effects (represented by $q$) than was possible in the $q$-NJL model, where dynamical masses implicitly depend on $q$. The results allow future work to identify which physical mechanisms (e.g., finite size, fluctuations, correlations) are responsible for the observed nonextensivity, with the expectation that as these mechanisms are incorporated into the dynamics, $|q-1|$ will diminish toward zero.
