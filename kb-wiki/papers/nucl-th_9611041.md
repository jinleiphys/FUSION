# Expanding Quark-Gluon Plasmas: Transverse Flow, Chemical Equilibration and Electromagnetic Radiation

> We investigate the chemical equilibration of the parton distributions in collisions of two heavy nuclei. We use initial conditions obtained from a self-screened parton cascade calculation and, for comparison, from the HIJING model. We consider a one-dimensional, as well as a three-dimensional expansion of the parton plasma and find that the onset of the transverse expansion impedes the chemical equilibration. At energies of 100 GeV/nucleon, the results for one-dimensional and three-dimensional expansion are quite similar except at large values of the transverse radius. At energies of several TeV/nucleon, the plasma initially approaches chemical equilibrium, but then is driven away from it, when the transverse velocity gradients develop. We find that the total parton multiplicity density remains essentially unaffected by the flow, but the individual concentrations of quarks, antiquarks, and gluons are sensitive to the transverse flow. The consequences of the flow are also discernible in the transverse momenta of the partons and in the lepton pair spectra, where the flow causes a violation of the so-called $M_T$ scaling.

## Key claim

The authors find that transverse expansion of the quark-gluon plasma impedes chemical equilibration, and at LHC energies (several TeV/nucleon) the plasma initially approaches chemical equilibrium but is then driven away from it when transverse velocity gradients develop, contrary to the expectation from purely longitudinal expansion models.

## Method

The authors model the quark-gluon plasma as an ideal fluid undergoing boost-invariant longitudinal expansion with cylindrically symmetric transverse expansion, solving hydrodynamic equations coupled to chemical master equations for gluon fusion/splitting (gg ↔ ggg) and quark-antiquark production (gg ↔ qq̅). Initial conditions are taken from the Self-Screened Parton Cascade (SSPC) model and, for comparison, from the HIJING model, with the evolution terminated when the energy density reaches a critical value of 1.45 GeV/fm³.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC energies (100 GeV/nucleon), the plasma lifetime is approximately 3–5 fm/c; at LHC energies (several TeV/nucleon), the lifetime exceeds 10 fm/c.
- The rarefaction wave from transverse expansion reaches the center of the plasma after about 12 fm/c at LHC energies.
- At RHIC, transverse flow leads to approximately 12% fewer quarks and antiquarks but about 10% more gluons compared to the no-flow case at the end of the QGP phase.
- At LHC, chemical reactions produce an increase in quark and antiquark numbers by a factor of about 2.5, a decrease in gluon numbers by about 25%, and a net increase in total parton multiplicity by approximately 40%.
- The critical energy density for terminating the QGP phase is taken as ε_f = 1.45 GeV/fm³.
- The initial thermalization time is τ_i ≈ 0.2–0.3 fm/c under conditions expected at collider energies.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of chemical equilibration in longitudinally expanding plasmas (Biro et al., 1993; Geiger, 1995; Xu et al., 1996) by incorporating three-dimensional expansion with transverse flow, using initial conditions from the Self-Screened Parton Cascade model (Geiger and Müller, 1996) and the HIJING model (Wang and Gyulassy, 1991). The results enable more realistic predictions for electromagnetic probes (thermal photons and lepton pairs) at RHIC and LHC energies, and suggest that the violation of M_T scaling in lepton pair spectra could serve as a signature of transverse flow.
