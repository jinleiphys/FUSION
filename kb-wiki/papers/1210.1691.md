# Quadrupole moments of odd-odd near-magic nuclei

> Ground state quadrupole moments of odd-odd near double magic nuclei are calculated in the approximation of no interaction between odd particles. Under such a simple approximation, the problem is reduced to the calculations of quadrupole moments of corresponding odd-even nuclei. These calculations are performed within the self-consistent Theory of Finite Fermi Systems based on the Energy Density Functional by Fayans et al. with the known DF3-a parameters. A reasonable agreement with the available experimental data has been obtained for odd-odd nuclei and odd near-magic nuclei investigated. The self-consistent approach under consideration allowed us to predict the unknown quadrupole moments of odd-even and odd-odd nuclei near the double-magic $^{56,78}$Ni, $^{100,132}$Sn ones.

## Key claim

Ground state quadrupole moments of odd-odd near-magic nuclei can be calculated using the approximation of no interaction between the two odd particles, reducing the problem to the quadrupole moments of the corresponding odd-even nuclei. The authors acknowledge this is a "simple approximation" and state that it allows them to "check this approximation rather successfully in a pure phenomenological way," rather than claiming it is a complete or exact treatment.

## Method

The authors used the self-consistent Theory of Finite Fermi Systems (TFFS) based on the Energy Density Functional by Fayans et al. with the DF3-a parameters. For odd-odd nuclei, they applied the approximation that there is no interaction between the two odd quasi-particles, which allows the quadrupole moment to be expressed in terms of the quadrupole moments of the corresponding odd-even nuclei using angular momentum coupling formulas (Eqs. 3 and 4). The quadrupole moments of the odd nuclei were calculated by solving the TFFS equation for the effective field in the static external quadrupole field, taking into account the single-particle continuum completely through the use of single-particle Green functions.

## Key numbers

- For the phenomenological approach (using experimental odd-nucleus moments): $^{40}_{19}$K: Q_phen = -0.106(6) e b vs Q_exp = -0.071(1) e b; $^{92}_{41}$Nb: Q_phen = -0.43(7) e b vs Q_exp = -0.35(3) e b; $^{210}_{83}$Bi: Q_phen = +0.22(6) e b vs Q_exp = +0.19(6) e b
- Self-consistent prediction for odd-odd $^{56}_{27}$Co (4+): Q_theor = 0.30 e b vs Q_exp = +0.25(9) e b
- Self-consistent prediction for odd-odd $^{208}_{83}$Bi (5+): Q_theor = -0.35 e b vs Q_exp = -0.64(6) e b
- Self-consistent prediction for odd-odd $^{210}_{83}$Bi (1-): Q_theor = 0.16 e b vs Q_exp = +0.136(1) e b
- Self-consistent prediction for odd-odd $^{56}_{29}$Cu (4+): Q_theor = 0.28 e b (no experimental value available)
- Self-consistent prediction for odd-odd $^{132}_{49}$In (7-): Q_theor = -0.29 e b (no experimental value available)
- Self-consistent prediction for odd-neutron $^{131}_{50}$Sn (3/2+): Q_theor = 0.10 e b vs Q_exp = -0.04(8) e b
- Self-consistent prediction for odd-proton $^{209}_{83}$Bi (9/2-): Q_theor = -0.34 e b vs experimental values ranging from -0.37(3) to -0.77(1) e b

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent TFFS approach previously applied to odd near- and semi-magic nuclei (Voitenkov et al. 2012, Tolokonnikov and Saperstein 2010) to the more complicated case of odd-odd near-magic nuclei. The approximation of no interaction between odd particles is tested against the few available experimental data points, including $^{40}$K, $^{92}$Nb, and $^{210}$Bi. The approach enables predictions for quadrupole moments of exotic unstable nuclei near double-magic $^{56}$Ni, $^{78}$Ni, $^{100}$Sn, and $^{132}$Sn, where experimental data are scarce or not yet achievable.
