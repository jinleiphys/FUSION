# On the Strength of Spin-Isospin Transitions in A=28 Nuclei

> The relations between the strengths of spin-isospin transition operators extracted from direct nuclear reactions, magnetic scattering of electrons and processes of semi-leptonic weak interactions are discussed.

# On the Strength of Spin-Isospin Transitions in A=28 Nuclei

## Key claim
The authors show that for the strongest isovector $0^{+} \rightarrow 1^{+}$ transitions in A=28 nuclei, shell-model wave functions modified by an orthogonal transformation to reproduce experimental GT and M1 strength function shapes yield muon capture rates and B(M1) values close to experiment, but the resulting B(GT) values are 1.5 times larger than those extracted from (p,n) reaction cross sections, contradicting the expected proportionality between these observables.

## Method
The authors performed many-particle shell-model calculations in the unrestricted sd-shell space using the Wildenthal Hamiltonian and the OXBASH code for A=28 nuclei. They constructed an orthogonal transformation (Householder reflection) acting on the subspace of isovector $1^{+}$ state wave functions, with parameters chosen so that the theoretical GT and M1 strength functions match the shapes (not absolute magnitudes) of experimental strength functions from the $^{28}$Si(p,n)$^{28}$P reaction and electron scattering. The transformed wave functions were then used to calculate exclusive ordinary muon capture (OMC) rates, B(GT) values, and B(M1) values for transitions from the $0^{+}$ ground state to $1^{+}$ excited states.

## Key numbers
- Summed experimental B(GT) strength up to 12.6 MeV excitation energy: 2.595; summed B(GT) over states shown in the figure: 2.301
- Summed theoretical B(GT) over the first 10 eigenstates with $J^{\pi},T = 1^{+},1$: 3.492
- GT quenching factor $\Sigma B_{\text{exper.}}(GT) / \Sigma B_{\text{theor.}}(GT) = 0.66$
- Summed experimental B(M1): 7.360; summed theoretical B(M1) with free $g_s$: 8.623; ratio = 0.85
- After transformation, theoretical B(GT) values are 1.5 times larger than those extracted from the $^{28}$Si(p,n)$^{28}$P reaction cross sections

## Context
This work extends the discussion of discrepancies between GT strengths extracted from charge-exchange reactions and beta decay, previously noted by Goodman (1980) and others, to the comparison with muon capture rates in A=28 nuclei. The authors build on the approach of Kuzmin and Tetereva (1999) for transforming shell-model wave functions using experimental strength function shapes. The results challenge the standard assumption that $0^{\circ}$ (p,n) cross sections are proportional to B(GT) for strong transitions, and suggest that spin-quadrupole transitions and two-step processes may contribute significantly to charge-exchange reaction cross sections. The work enables further investigation of the relationship between weak interaction processes and nuclear reaction measurements for spectroscopic information.
