# State-Selective High-Energy Excitation of Nuclei by Resonant Positron Annihilation

> In the annihilation of a positron with a bound atomic electron, the virtual gamma photon created may excite the atomic nucleus. We put forward this effect as a spectroscopic tool for an energy-selective excitation of nuclear transitions. This scheme can efficiently populate nuclear levels of arbitrary multipolarities in the MeV regime, including monopole transitions and giant resonances. In certain cases, it may have a higher cross sections than the conventionally used Coulomb excitation.

## Key claim

The paper proposes nuclear excitation by resonant positron annihilation (NERPA) as a spectroscopic tool that can efficiently populate nuclear levels of arbitrary multipolarities in the MeV regime, including monopole transitions and giant resonances, with cross sections that in certain cases may be comparable to or an order of magnitude higher than Coulomb excitation, though the authors note that attempts to observe NERPA have not been conclusive so far and only an upper bound of its cross section has been determined experimentally.

## Method

The authors derive the NERPA transition rate by extending the formalism of internal pair conversion, factorizing it as a product of the nuclear gamma-decay rate and a coefficient β_N that depends on the positron and electron angular momenta and the nuclear transition multipolarity. They calculate cross sections for specific nuclear level schemes involving a metastable state, using the non-penetration approximation where the overlap integral of the bound-electron wave function within the nuclear volume is neglected, and present results for the 1s_{1/2} electron orbital which yields the highest NERPA rate.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁷⁴Hf (Z=72): resonance energy 281 keV, NERPA cross section σ_N(E_res) = 1.1×10⁻⁴ b, NERPA-γ cross section σ_{Nγ}(E_res) = 6.1×10⁻⁶ b
- For ¹¹⁵Sn (Z=50): resonance energy 430 keV, σ_N(E_res) = 1.5×10⁻⁴ b, σ_{Nγ}(E_res) = 3.1×10⁻⁶ b, metastable level lifetime τ₂ = 3.26 μs
- For ¹¹⁵In (Z=49): resonance energy 90 keV, σ_N(E_res) = 1.7×10⁻⁵ b, σ_{Nγ}(E_res) = 1.1×10⁻⁶ b, metastable level lifetime τ₂ = 4.5 h
- For the 13.5-MeV giant dipole resonance in ²⁰⁸Pb: integrated NERPA cross section ∫σ_N(E)dE = 3.4×10⁴ b·eV, exceeding previous values for ¹¹⁵In by 8 orders of magnitude
- For ¹⁶O: NERPA cross section σ_N = 2.1×10⁻⁴ b at resonance energy 5899 keV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical description of NERPA first proposed by Present (1952) and later refined by Greiner (1978), Pisko (1985), Horowitz (1988), and Kalman (2010), following an experiment by Castelli (2001) that only established an upper bound for the cross section. The authors propose using intense positron jets generated in laser-plasma interactions (Chen 2010, Sarri 2013, Sarri 2014) as a practical source, making NERPA complementary to photo-excitation with X-ray free electron lasers and to Coulomb excitation with massive charged particles. The scheme enables energy-selective excitation of giant resonances and monopole transitions, which could provide a novel means for investigating nuclear compressibility, deformed nuclear structure, and the low-energy limit of radiative dipole strength relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis simulations.
