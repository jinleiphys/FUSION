# Description of proton-rich nuclei in the $A \sim 20$ region with the Gamow shell model

> The Gamow shell model is utilized to describe nuclear observables of the weakly bound and resonance isotonic states of $^{16}$O at proton drip-line. It is hereby shown that the presence of continuum coupling leads to complex Coulomb contributions in the spectrum of these isotones. The necessity to include the effects of three-body forces, either by a direct calculation or by adding an $A$-dependence to the nucleon-nucleon interaction, already noticed in other theoretical models, is pointed out. It is also demonstrated that our approach is predictive for reaction observables.

## Key claim

The Gamow shell model (GSM) can describe nuclear observables of proton-rich isotones of \(^{16}\)O at the proton drip-line, but only if three-body forces are included, either explicitly or via an \(A\)-dependent Hamiltonian, because continuum coupling produces complex Coulomb contributions that the isobaric multiplet mass equation (IMME) cannot capture for excited states.

## Method

The authors used the Gamow shell model in a core-plus-valence-protons picture with an \(^{16}\)O core represented by a Woods-Saxon potential, employing a Berggren basis that includes bound, resonance, and scattering states for partial waves up to \(\ell=3\). Two residual proton-proton interactions were tested: a local Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki (FHT) interaction and a non-local interaction from effective field theory (EFT), both with and without an \(A\)-dependent factor applied to the one-body and two-body Hamiltonian terms to mimic missing three-body forces. The Coulomb Hamiltonian was separated into a one-body potential part (treated exactly at basis level) and a finite-range residual two-body part expanded in harmonic oscillator states.

## Key numbers

- The \(A\)-dependent Hamiltonian reproduces experimental binding energies of \(^{17}\)F through \(^{22}\)Si; without \(A\)-dependence, the overbinding reaches about 2.5 MeV for \(^{22}\)Si.
- The \(A\)-independent FHT interaction overbinds more than the \(A\)-independent EFT interaction; the discrepancy is already a few hundred keV for \(^{19}\)Na.
- The \(2^+_1\) state of \(^{18}\)Ne is reproduced very well; the \(4^+_1\), \(2^+_2\), and \(3^+_1\) states differ from experiment by at most a few hundred keV, while the \(0^+_2\) state is too high by about 1 MeV.
- The \(1/2^+_1\) state of \(^{19}\)Na is very close to experiment; the \(3/2^+_1\) state is too low by about 500 keV.
- The total Coulomb contribution to ground states of \(^{16}\)O isotones is almost equal to the IMME value, but for excited states of \(^{18}\)Ne it differs by 0.7 to 1.5 MeV from the ground-state value.
- The two-body part of the Coulomb Hamiltonian adds about 200 to 500 keV for \(^{18}\)Ne states.

## Context

This work extends the Gamow shell model to proton-rich nuclei in the \(A\sim20\) region, where earlier GSM studies focused on neutron-rich oxygen isotopes (PhysRevC.96.024308) and the \(^{14}\)O(p,p) reaction (DeGrancey 2016). The need for three-body forces or \(A\)-dependent Hamiltonians at the proton drip-line was previously noted in many-body perturbation theory calculations by PhysRevLett.110.022502. The results enable predictive calculations of reaction observables such as \(^{18}\)Ne(p,p) elastic scattering cross sections, and demonstrate that the IMME formula, which works for ground states, fails for excited states due to continuum-induced Coulomb effects.
