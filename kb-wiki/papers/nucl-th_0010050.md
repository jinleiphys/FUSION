# At the edge of nuclear stability: nonlinear quantum amplifiers

> We show that nuclear states lying at the edge of stability may show enormously enhanced response to small perturbations. For example, a 0.1% change in the strength of the strong nucleon-nucleon interaction can cause almost a hundred times bigger change in the resonance energy of the 0+_2 state of 12C.

## Key claim

Nuclear states at the edge of stability can act as nonlinear quantum amplifiers, where a 0.1% change in the strength of the strong nucleon-nucleon interaction can produce a roughly 7% change in the resonance energy of the physical 0+_2 state of 12C, but this amplification only occurs when the residual interaction between clusters decreases more slowly than the binding energy itself as the system approaches the breakup threshold.

## Method

The author studied three nuclear systems (deuteron, ground state of 7Li, and 0+_2 state of 12C) using cluster models where the relative motion between clusters is the key degree of freedom. For the deuteron, the Schrodinger equation was solved with the Reid93 realistic interaction and with effective interactions (Eikemeier-Hackenbroich and Minnesota forces); for 7Li and 12C, a variational expansion in Gaussian basis functions was used within microscopic cluster models. The binding or resonance energy was computed for each system as the strength of the nucleon-nucleon force was scaled by a factor p, and the response was measured as the energy difference between systems with force strength p and p multiplied by 1.001 (a 0.1% increase).

## Key numbers

- A deuteron bound by only 20 keV would change its binding energy by 9% in response to a 0.1% change in the strength of the nucleon-nucleon force.
- The deuteron becomes unbound when all strengths in the Reid93 force are multiplied by p=0.82, at which point the artificial deuteron has a 16.48 fm radius and 0.0201 MeV binding energy.
- The Minnesota force requires a stronger reduction (p=0.72) to make the deuteron unbound, compared to p=0.82 for the Reid93 and Eikemeier-Hackenbroich forces.
- A 0.1% change in the strength of the strong nucleon-nucleon force leads to a roughly 7% change in the resonance energy of the physical 0+_2 state of 12C in the author's model.
- The 0+_2 state of 12C is situated only 380 keV above the 3-alpha threshold.

## Context

This work extends the study of unusual properties of nuclei at the edge of stability, such as the halo structure described by Hansen and Jonson (1987), by identifying a new phenomenon of nonlinear quantum amplification. The paper connects to astrophysical carbon synthesis studies by Oberhummer, Csoto, and Schlattl (2000), which showed that a 0.5% change in the strength of the nucleon-nucleon force would lead to a Universe with virtually no carbon or oxygen, making carbon production one of the most fine-tuned processes in astrophysics. The amplification mechanism could apply to halo nuclei and other weakly coupled few-body systems near the breakup threshold.
