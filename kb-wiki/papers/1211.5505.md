# Exotic Behavior of Heavy-Flavored Meson Matter

> In this work, we study the thermodynamic behavior of heavy-flavored meson matter in the framework of $(σ,ω)$-meson-exchange model in relativistic mean field theory. We find a decreasing of the effective masses of $D$ and $B$ mesons as the temperature increases. By using the effective mass and maximum value of dissociation temperatures available from lattice QCD, the masses of the bound states $D \bar{D}$ and $B \bar{B}$ are estimated in 2 MeV for both molecules. For the $B$-meson matter, the pressure presents an exotic behavior, being negative for temperatures above 6.6 times the deconfinement transition temperature $T_c$. In addition, the ratio of pressure to energy density is similar to the value predicted for systems that behave as dark energy matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for B-meson matter, the pressure becomes negative for temperatures above 6.6 times the deconfinement transition temperature T_c, and the ratio of pressure to energy density falls in the range typical of dark energy matter; however, the authors note that this exotic behavior may be discarded if bottomonium dissociates at lower temperatures than 6.6 T_c, as predicted by other studies.

## Method

The authors apply a (σ,ω)-meson-exchange model within relativistic mean field theory to study the thermodynamic behavior of heavy-flavored meson matter. They use the path-integral formalism at finite temperature and chemical potential, deriving the thermodynamic potential, gap equations, and expressions for pressure, entropy, and energy density. The effective masses of D and B mesons are computed as functions of temperature by solving the gap equation for the sigma field, with the omega field vanishing when the numbers of mesons and anti-mesons are equal.

## Key numbers

- The deconfinement transition temperature used is T_c = 172 MeV for two-flavor dynamical quarks (Bornyakov 2011).
- For D-meson matter with coupling constant g_{D D σ} = 2.85 GeV, the critical temperature where effective mass nearly vanishes is T_D ≈ 1.137 GeV or 6.6 T_c.
- The binding energy of a D D̅ molecule is estimated as 2 MeV at a temperature of 2.09 T_c, where the effective mass is 1.869 GeV.
- For B-meson matter with coupling constant g_{B B σ} = 8.04 GeV, the critical temperature T_B ≈ 1.137 GeV or 6.6 T_c, equal to that of D-mesons.
- The binding energy of a B B̅ molecule is estimated as 2 MeV at a temperature of 3.953 T_c, where the effective mass is 5.278 GeV.
- For D-meson matter with g_{D D σ} = 9 GeV, a first-order phase transition occurs at T_0 ≈ 0.582 GeV, and pressure becomes negative for temperatures above 2.09 T_c.
- For B-meson matter with vacuum coupling constant, the pressure becomes negative for temperatures above T_B (6.6 T_c).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean field approach previously applied to nucleon-anti-nucleon systems (Walecka 1974, Walecka 2004) and to D-meson matter (Freire 2010) to both charmed and bottomed meson systems. The results for D D̅ binding energy (2 MeV) are close to the 1.4 MeV reported by Liu (2010) for S-wave D D̅ bound states at T=0, but differ significantly from the 60.7 MeV binding energy for B B̅ reported by the same group. The dissociation temperatures found (6.6 T_c) are much higher than lattice QCD predictions for charmonium dissociation (between 1.6 T_c and 2.35 T_c from Asakawa 2003 and Ding 2012) and bottomonium dissociation (up to 4.18 T_c from Emerick 2011 and Rapp 2008). The exotic negative pressure behavior for B-meson matter could inform models of dark energy that treat interacting fields in a mean-field framework.
