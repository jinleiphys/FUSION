# Isospin equilibration in multinucleon transfer reaction at near-barrier energies

> The isospin equilibration process in multinucleon transfer reaction is investigated by using the improved quantum molecular dynamics model. The collision processes of $^{124}$Xe+$^{208}$Pb at near-barrier energy are studied with different symmetry energy coefficients. We find that neutrons transfer happens earlier than protons. The large symmetry energy promote the transfer of neutrons. The neutron flow from the target to projectile is along the low-density path of neck. The isospin equilibration process in $^{58}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb reaction is also investigated and compared with available experimental data. It shows that $N/Z$ values of the projectile-like products increase rapidly with increasing mass transfer. The complete isospin equilibration events are located in the region of $120<A<150$ which are produced in symmetric fission-type reactions.

# Isospin equilibration in multinucleon transfer reaction at near-barrier energies

## Key claim
The isospin equilibration process in multinucleon transfer reactions at near-barrier energies is driven by neutron transfer occurring earlier than proton transfer, with the neutron flow proceeding along the low-density path of the neck; however, complete isospin equilibration is only achieved in symmetric fission-type reactions (mass region 120 < A < 150), not in quasi-elastic or few-nucleon transfer channels.

## Method
The authors used the improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) model with the IQ2 parameter set (Skyrme-type energy density functional without spin-orbit term) to simulate head-on collisions of 124Xe+208Pb at Ec.m. = 450 MeV and 58Ni+208Pb at Elab = 345 and 328.4 MeV. They calculated drift coefficients for neutrons and protons through a separation plane between projectile-like and target-like nuclei, and compared primary fragment N/Z distributions with experimental data from Krolas et al. (2005) for the 58Ni+208Pb system.

## Key numbers
- Neutron drift coefficient peak value for 124Xe+208Pb at Ec.m. = 450 MeV with Cs = 32 MeV: about 0.3 × 10^22 s^-1, close to TDHF calculations by Ayik et al. (2015).
- Incompressibility coefficient K∞ = 195 MeV for the IQ2 parameter set used in the model.
- Initial isospin asymmetry δ = 0.13 for 124Xe and δ = 0.21 for 208Pb.
- Coulomb barrier for 124Xe+208Pb: 436 MeV; incident energy Ec.m. = 450 MeV (slightly above barrier).
- Complete isospin equilibration events located in mass region 120 < A < 150 for 58Ni+208Pb at Elab = 345 MeV.
- N/Z values: 1.071 for 58Ni projectile, 1.537 for 208Pb target.
- After neutron evaporation, 67Ni detected with production cross section about 15 μb in the 58Ni+208Pb reaction.

## Context
This work extends the study of isospin transport in multinucleon transfer reactions, building on experimental investigations by Krolas et al. (2005) for 64Ni+130Te and 58Ni+208Pb systems, and on TDHF calculations by Ayik et al. (2015) for 40Ca+238U and 48Ca+238U systems. The ImQMD model had previously been successfully applied to fusion reactions and multinucleon transfer in 136Xe+208Pb and 136Xe+198Pt systems. The results enable predictions for producing very neutron-rich projectile-like fragments via isospin equilibration in near-barrier collisions, and provide a benchmark for comparing microscopic transport models with experimental fragment distributions.
