# Critical Behaviour in the Dense Planar NJL Model

> We present results of a Monte Carlo simulation of a 2+1 dimensional Nambu - Jona-Lasinio model including diquark source terms. A diquark condensate <qq> is measured as a function of source strength j. In the vacuum phase <qq> vanishes linearly with j as expected, but simulations in a region with non-zero baryon density suggest a power-law scaling and hence a critical system for all mu > mu_c. There is no diquark condensation signalling superfluidity. Comparisons are drawn with known results in two dimensional theories, and with the pseudogap phase in cuprate superconductors. We also measure the dispersion relation E(k) for fermionic excitations, and find results consistent with a sharp Fermi surface. Any superfluid gap Delta is constrained to be much less than the constituent quark mass scale Sigma_0.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in the high-density phase of the 2+1 dimensional Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, the diquark condensate follows a power-law scaling with source strength rather than a linear one, implying a critical system for all chemical potentials above the critical value, but with no actual superfluid condensate or spontaneous symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of the 2+1 dimensional NJL model with staggered lattice fermions, introducing explicit diquark source terms to probe the response of the system. They measured the diquark condensate as a function of source strength on lattice sizes ranging from 16^3 to 48^3, at chemical potentials μ=0, 0.2 (low-density chirally-broken phase) and μ=0.8 (high-density chirally-restored phase). They also measured the fermion propagator to extract the dispersion relation E(k) for fermionic excitations, extrapolating results to zero source strength.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass at μ=0: Σ₀ ≈ 0.71 (in cutoff units)
- Critical chemical potential for chiral symmetry restoration: μ_c ≈ 0.65
- At μ=0.8, baryon density n ≈ 0.25 quarks of each isospin component per lattice site
- Power-law exponent for diquark condensate scaling at μ=0.8: α ≈ 0.31, corresponding to δ ≈ 3
- Upper bound on any superfluid gap: Δ < 0.05, much less than Σ₀ ≈ 0.7
- Fermi momentum from dispersion fit: k_F ≈ 0.90, slightly larger than the Fermi energy μ

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the NJL model in 2+1 dimensions with non-zero chemical potential (Hands and Morrison, 1999), which found evidence for non-zero diquark correlators but no extensive condensate. The results contradict expectations from self-consistent gap equation approaches (Berges and Rajagopal, 1999) that predicted a BCS superfluid gap. The observed critical behavior is compared to the low-temperature phase of the 2d X-Y model (Kosterlitz and Thouless, 1973) and to the pseudogap phase in cuprate superconductors, suggesting that long-wavelength fluctuations destroy long-range phase coherence even though dynamical pairing is present. The work enables future studies of whether the critical exponent δ depends on μ, and whether superfluid behavior might appear at higher densities or closer to the continuum limit.
