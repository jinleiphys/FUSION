# Influence of the variation of fundamental constants on the primordial nucleosynthesis

> We investigate the effect of a variation of fundamental constants on primordial element production in Big Bang nucleosynthesis (BBN). We focus on the effect of a possible change in the nucleon-nucleon interaction on nuclear reaction rates involving the A=5 (5Li and 5He) and A=8 (8Be) unstable nuclei. The reaction rates for 3He(d,p)4He and 3H(d,n)4He are dominated by the properties of broad analog resonances in 5Li and 5He compound nuclei respectively. While the triple-alpha process 4He(aa,g)12C is normally not effective in BBN, its rate is very sensitive to the position of the "Hoyle state" and could in principle be drastically affected if 8Be were stable during BBN. We found that the effect of the variation of constants on the 3He(d,p)4He, 3H(d,n)4He nd 4He(aa,g)12C reaction rates is not sufficient to induce a significant effect on BBN, even with a stable 8Be. The main influences come from the weak rates and the A=2, n(p,g)d, bottleneck reaction.

# Influence of the variation of fundamental constants on the primordial nucleosynthesis

## Key claim

The variation of fundamental constants, specifically the nucleon-nucleon interaction, does not produce a significant effect on Big Bang nucleosynthesis through the A=5 (5Li, 5He) and A=8 (8Be) reaction channels, even if 8Be were stable; the dominant influences on primordial abundances instead come from the weak interaction rates and the A=2 n(p,gamma)d bottleneck reaction.

## Method

The authors used a microscopic cluster model with the Minnesota nucleon-nucleon force, parameterized by a variation parameter delta_NN, to calculate how the resonance energies and widths of the 3/2+ states in 5He and 5Li (for the 3H(d,n)4He and 3He(d,p)4He reactions) and the 8Be ground state and 12C Hoyle state (for the triple-alpha process) change with the nuclear interaction. They then implemented these rate variations into a BBN code to compute primordial light element abundances as a function of delta_NN, and compared the results to observational constraints on D, 4He, and 7Li.

## Key numbers

- 8Be ground state energy relative to the 2-alpha threshold: E_g.s.(8Be) = (0.09208 - 12.208 x delta_NN) MeV, becoming bound (negative) for delta_NN >= 7.52 x 10^-3
- Hoyle state resonance energy in 12C: E_R(12C) = (0.2877 - 20.412 x delta_NN) MeV
- Level shift for the 3/2+ resonance in 5He (3H(d,n)4He): Delta_E_R = -0.327 x delta_NN MeV
- Level shift for the 3/2+ resonance in 5Li (3He(d,p)4He): Delta_E_R = -0.453 x delta_NN MeV
- Allowed range for delta_NN from combined observational constraints: -0.0025 < delta_NN < 0.0006
- Maximum carbon production via the triple-alpha path during BBN: C/H approximately 10^-21 at delta_NN approximately 0.006, which is six orders of magnitude below the standard BBN carbon abundance of CNO/H = (0.5-3.0) x 10^-15
- 5He is unbound by 0.798 MeV, 5Li by 1.69 MeV, and 8Be by 0.092 MeV with respect to their respective decay channels

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Coc 2007) by including the A=5 and A=8 nuclear reaction channels that were not modeled in detail before. It contradicts the expectation from Bethe (1939) that stable 8Be would trigger heavy element production in BBN, showing instead that even with bound 8Be the carbon yield remains negligible due to BBN's low densities and short timescales compared to stellar environments. The results disagree with the stronger energy dependence found by Berengut (2010) for the A=5 resonances, which the authors attribute to differences in the treatment of broad resonances versus ground states. The work enables improved constraints on fundamental constant variation from BBN and highlights that the n(p,gamma)d rate dependence on the deuteron binding energy remains a critical uncertainty, particularly as the recent work by Carrillo (2012) challenges the earlier parameterization by Dmitriev (2004).
