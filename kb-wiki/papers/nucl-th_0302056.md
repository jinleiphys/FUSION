# Systematics of Fission Barriers in Superheavy Elements

> We investigate the systematics of fission barriers in superheavy elements in the range Z = 108-120 and N = 166-182. Results from two self-consistent models for nuclear structure, the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model as well as the non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach are compared and discussed. We restrict ourselves to axially symmetric shapes, which provides an upper bound on static fission barriers. We benchmark the predictive power of the models examining the barriers and fission isomers of selected heavy actinide nuclei for which data are available. For both actinides and superheavy nuclei, the RMF model systematically predicts lower barriers than most Skyrme interactions. In particular the fission isomers are predicted too low by the RMF, which casts some doubt on recent predictions about superdeformed ground states of some superheavy nuclei. For the superheavy nuclei under investigation, fission barriers drop to small values around Z = 110, N = 180 and increase again for heavier systems. For most of the forces, there is no fission isomer for superheavy nuclei, as superdeformed states are in most cases found to be unstable with respect to octupole distortions.

# Systematics of Fission Barriers in Superheavy Elements

## Key claim
The relativistic mean-field (RMF) model systematically predicts lower fission barriers and fission isomer excitation energies than most Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) interactions for both actinide and superheavy nuclei, but the authors qualify that their restriction to axially symmetric shapes provides only an upper bound on static fission barriers, and that the RMF's underestimation of fission isomer energies casts doubt on recent predictions about superdeformed ground states in some superheavy nuclei.

## Method
The authors performed systematic self-consistent mean-field calculations of fission barriers for superheavy nuclei with proton numbers Z=108-120 and neutron numbers N=166-182, using both the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with parameterizations NL3 and NL-Z2 and the non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) approach with interactions SkP, SLy6, SkI3, and SkI4. They constrained calculations to axially symmetric shapes with reflection-symmetric and reflection-asymmetric fission paths, using a constraint on the mass quadrupole moment Q20, and benchmarked the models against experimental data for heavy actinide nuclei (Z=90-98) before extrapolating to superheavy systems.

## Key numbers
- The range of nuclei studied covers proton numbers Z=108-120 and neutron numbers N=166-182.
- For the actinide benchmark nucleus 240Pu, the calculated ground-state deformation is β2=0.29, in perfect agreement with the experimental value of β2=0.29 deduced from the B(E2)↑ value of 13.33±0.18 e²b².
- The calculated excitation energy of the fission isomer in 240Pu is 3.0 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 2.25±0.20 MeV.
- Mean deviation of inner barrier height from experimental values (in MeV): SLy6=4.5, SkP=2.7, SkI3=4.1, SkI4=4.1, NL-Z2=1.5, NL3=2.3.
- Mean deviation of outer barrier height from experimental values (in MeV): SLy6=2.2, SkP=4.7, SkI3=1.4, SkI4=3.8, NL-Z2=1.2, NL3=0.8.
- Fission barriers drop to small values around Z=110, N=180 and increase again for heavier systems.

## Context
This work extends earlier self-consistent mean-field studies of fission barriers in superheavy nuclei, including comparisons between SHF and RMF models by Rutzen (1995) and Bender (2000), and addresses conflicting predictions for spherical magic numbers in superheavy elements from Rutzen (1997) and Bender (1999). The results enable systematic benchmarking of theoretical models against the limited experimental data for actinide nuclei, and provide upper-bound estimates for fission barriers that can guide experimental searches for new superheavy elements, particularly in the context of recent synthesis claims for elements Z=114 and Z=116 from experiments at Dubna.
