# Azimuthal asymmetries in semi-inclusive DIS with polarized beam and/or target and their nuclear dependences

> Using the formalism obtained from collinear expansion, we calculate the differential cross section and azimuthal asymmetries in semi-inclusive deeply inelastic lepton-nucleon (nucleus) scattering process $e^-+N (A) \to e^-+q+X$ with both polarized beam and polarized target up to twist-3. We derive the azimuthal asymmetries in terms of twist-3 parton correlation functions. We simplify the results by using the QCD equation of motion that leads to a set of relationships between different twist-3 functions. We further study the nuclear dependence of azimuthal asymmetries and show that they have similar suppression factors as those in the unpolarized reactions.

## Key claim

Using the collinear expansion formalism, the authors derive the differential cross section and azimuthal asymmetries in semi-inclusive deeply inelastic lepton-nucleon (nucleus) scattering with polarized beam and/or polarized target up to twist-3, and show that the nuclear dependence of these asymmetries has similar suppression factors as those in unpolarized reactions, contingent on the QCD equation-of-motion relations that simplify the twist-3 parton correlation functions.

## Method

The authors apply the collinear expansion technique to the semi-inclusive process e^- + N (A) ‚Üí e^- + q + X, considering Feynman diagrams with zero, one, and two gluon exchanges (j=0,1,2) and performing Taylor expansion of hard parts around collinear momenta. They decompose the hadronic tensor into contributions from twist-2 and twist-3 transverse momentum dependent (TMD) parton distribution and correlation functions, then use QCD equations of motion to relate the twist-3 quark-gluon-quark correlators (involving covariant derivatives) to the twist-2 quark correlators, thereby expressing all twist-3 contributions in terms of the same set of functions appearing in the twist-2 decomposition.

## Context

This work extends the collinear expansion formalism previously applied to inclusive DIS by Ellis (1982) and Qiu (1990) and to unpolarized SIDIS by Liang (2006) and Song (2010), and to transversely polarized targets by Liang (2006). It provides a systematic framework for calculating azimuthal asymmetries with both beam and target polarization up to twist-3, enabling future experimental studies of nuclear dependence in these asymmetries at facilities such as HERMES, COMPASS, and Jefferson Lab. The results contrast with earlier approaches by Mulders (1995) and Bacchetta (2006) that did not use collinear expansion and required manual insertion of gauge links.
