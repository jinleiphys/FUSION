# Internal and external radiative widths in the combined R-matrix and potential model formalism

> Using the $R$-matrix approach we calculate the radiative width for a resonance decaying to a bound state through electric dipole, $E1$, transitions. The total radiative width is determined by the interference of the nuclear internal and external radiative width amplitudes. For a given channel radius the external radiative width amplitude is model independent and is determined by the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of the bound state to which the resonance decays. It also depends on the partial resonance width. To calculate the internal radiative width amplitude we show that a single particle potential model is appropriate. We compare our results with a few experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the total radiative width for a resonance decaying via E1 transition can be calculated by combining the model-independent external (channel) radiative width amplitude, determined by the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of the bound state and the partial resonance width, with the internal radiative width amplitude obtained from a single-particle potential model, rather than treating the internal amplitude as a free fitting parameter.

## Method

The authors employ a single-level R-matrix formalism to split the radiative capture amplitude into internal (r ≤ R) and external (r ≥ R) components, where R is the channel radius. The external radiative width amplitude is calculated using experimental values of the partial resonance width and the ANC of the final bound state, while the internal radiative width amplitude is computed from Woods-Saxon potential wave functions normalized to unity over the internal region. The total radiative width is obtained from the modulus square of the sum of the internal and complex channel radiative width amplitudes, including their interference.

* For ¹³O 1/2⁺ resonance (E_R = 1.17 MeV) decaying to 3/2⁻ ground state (ε_f = 1.515 MeV): total radiative width Γ_γ = 0.8 ± 0.2 eV, with internal amplitude γ_γ(int) = 0.286 eV¹/² and channel amplitude γ_γ(ch) = 0.601 + i 0.187 eV¹/² at R = 4.6 fm.
* For ¹⁷F 1/2⁻ resonance (E_R = 2.504 MeV) decaying to 1/2⁺ excited state (ε_f = 0.105 MeV): total radiative width Γ_γ = (3.5 ± 0.6) × 10⁻² eV, with internal amplitude γ_γ(int) = 0.15 eV¹/² and channel amplitude γ_γ(ch) = -0.202 - i 0.179 eV¹/² at R = 4.9 fm.
* For ¹⁷F 5/2⁻ resonance (E_R = 3.257 MeV) decaying to 5/2⁺ ground state (ε_f = 0.6 MeV): total radiative width Γ_γ = 0.046 ± 0.005 eV, with internal amplitude γ_γ(int) = -0.164 eV¹/² and channel amplitude γ_γ(ch) = -0.049 - i 0.0062 eV¹/² at R = 4.9 fm.
* For ¹²N 2⁻ resonance (E_R = 0.591 MeV) decaying to 1⁺ ground state (ε_f = 0.6 MeV): total radiative width Γ_γ = (6.0 ± 5.4) × 10⁻³ eV at R = 4.5 fm, varying from 1.34 × 10⁻² to 1.36 × 10⁻³ eV for R between 4.2 and 4.8 fm.

## Context

This work extends the R-matrix formalism developed by Holt (1978), Barker (1991), and Mukhamedzhanov (2015) by calculating the internal radiative width amplitude and its sign from a potential model rather than treating them as fitting parameters. The approach enables more reliable determination of radiative widths for resonant capture reactions in nuclear astrophysics, particularly for the ¹²C(p,γ)¹³N reaction initiating the CNO cycle, the ¹⁵N(p,γ)¹⁶O reaction bridging CN and CNO cycles, and the ¹¹C(p,γ)¹²N reaction important for alternative nucleosynthesis paths. The method also provides a lower limit on the radiative width from the imaginary part of the channel amplitude, as previously noted by Mukhamedzhanov (2015).
