# Relating RHIC and SPS Heavy Ion Data

> We have demonstrated recently that RHIC heavy ion data concerning particle production at small and intermediate transverse momenta become very transparent, if one realizes that there are two sources of particles production: the corona -- due to the interactions of the peripheral nucleons of either nucleus, and the core -- representing the high density central zone. We extend our analysis to SPS heavy ion data. Again, we find that the nontrivial centrality dependence of particle production (being stronger than at RHIC) is simply due to an increasing corona contribution with decreasing centrality. The core contribution shows no centrality dependence (concerning particle ratios), and can be described in exactly the same way as at RHIC, with one exception: there is somewhat less collective radial flow at SPS compared to RHIC. Apart of this (and a trivial volume effect), the core portions produced in different colliding systems at different energies (RHIC, SPS) look the same.

## Key claim

The centrality dependence of particle production at SPS energies, which appears stronger than at RHIC, is shown to be a trivial consequence of an increasing corona contribution from peripheral nucleons as centrality decreases; after subtracting this corona background, the core contribution shows no centrality dependence and is described with the same parameters as at RHIC, except for a smaller collective radial flow at SPS.

## Method

The author uses the EPOS parton model to simulate nuclear collisions, distinguishing string segments in high-density areas (core) from those in low-density areas (corona) at an early proper time. The corona is treated with standard EPOS particle production (validated for pp and pA), while the core is parameterized as collectively expanding clusters that hadronize at a given energy density with a linear radial rapidity profile, using the same procedure and parameters as in a prior RHIC study (Werner, 2005) except for the proper time and the radial flow parameter.

## Key numbers

- Radial flow parameter: \(y_{\mathrm{rad}} = 0.60\) at SPS, compared to \(0.83\) at RHIC (30% more radial flow at RHIC).
- For central (0-5%) PbPb collisions at 158 GeV, the core contribution dominates at approximately 90% of the total particle production.
- The calculation of \(dn/dy(y=0)\) for lambda production is about 40% above the NA49 results, while being close to the NA57 values.
- The proper time used at SPS is \(\tau_{0} = \tau_{\mathrm{rea}}\) (the finite reaction time, somewhat more than 1 fm), whereas at RHIC the results were insensitive to variations in the range 1-2 fm.

## Context

This work extends the corona-core decomposition approach previously applied to RHIC AuAu data (Werner, 2005) to SPS PbPb, SiSi, and CC collisions at 158 GeV. It directly addresses the strong enhancement of strange and multi-strange baryons observed at SPS (Rafelski, 1982) by showing that after subtracting the corona background, the core exhibits constant particle ratios independent of centrality and system size. The results enable a unified description of heavy ion data across RHIC and SPS energies, with the only energy-dependent parameter being the radial flow, and highlight that the complicated experimental centrality dependence arises from the nontrivial admixture of corona contributions rather than from the core physics itself.
