# Regularities of Many-body Systems Interacting by a Two-body Random Ensemble

> The ground states of all even-even nuclei have angular momentum, $I$, equal to zero, I=0, and positive parity, $π=+$. This feature was believed to be a consequence of the attractive short-range interaction between nucleons. However, in the presence of two-body random interactions, the predominance of $I^π=0^+$ ground states (0 g.s.) was found to be robust both for bosons and for an even number of fermions. For simple systems, such as $d$ bosons, $sp$ bosons, $sd$ bosons, and a few fermions in single-$j$ shells for small $j$, there are a few approaches to predict and/or explain spin $I$ ground state ($I$ g.s.) probabilities. An empirical approach to predict $I$ g.s. probabilities is available for general cases, such as fermions in a single-$j$ ($j>7/2$) or many-$j$ shells and various boson systems, but a more fundamental understanding of the robustness of 0 g.s. dominance is still out of reach. Further interesting results are also reviewed concerning other robust phenomena of many-body systems in the presence of random two-body interactions, such as the odd-even staggering of binding energies, generic collectivity, the behavior of average energies, correlations, and regularities of many-body systems interacting by a displaced two-body random ensemble.

# Regularities of Many-body Systems Interacting by a Two-body Random Ensemble

## Key claim

The predominance of $I^\pi=0^+$ ground states in even-even nuclei, previously attributed to attractive short-range nucleon-nucleon interactions, is robust under completely random two-body interactions for both bosons and even-number fermion systems, but this dominance is not universal, it fails for specific cases such as four fermions in $j=7/2$ or $j=13/2$ shells, where $P(2)$ exceeds $P(0)$.

## Method

The authors diagonalize Hamiltonians whose two-body matrix elements are independent random variables drawn from a Gaussian distribution with mean zero, width 1 for diagonal elements and $1/\sqrt{2}$ for off-diagonal elements (the two-body random ensemble, TBRE). They compute ground-state spin probabilities for fermions in single-$j$ shells ($j=7/2$ to $33/2$) and many-$j$ shells, bosons with spin $l$, and $sp$, $sd$, and $sdg$ boson systems, using 1000 Monte Carlo samplings per system.

## Key numbers

- For four fermions in a single-$j$ shell, $P(0)$ is smaller than $P(2)$ for $j=7/2$ and $j=13/2$, contradicting the general 0 g.s. dominance pattern.
- $P(0)$ for four fermions in a single-$j$ shell oscillates synchronously with an increase in the number of $I=0$ states when $(j-3/2)=3k$ (k positive integer), where the number of $I=0$ states is the largest integer not exceeding $(j-3/2)/3$.
- For four bosons with spin $l$, $P(0)$ staggers at an interval of $\delta_l = 3$, with exceptions at $l=2$ and $l=8$ where $P(0)$ is not dominant.
- $P(I_{\rm max})$ for four fermions in a single-$j$ shell decreases with increasing $j$; for four bosons with spin $l$, $P(l)$ exhibits odd-even staggering (large when $l$ is even, small when $l$ is odd).
- For five fermions in a single-$j$ shell, $P(j)$ is very large compared to other $P(I)$ values, though not as large as $P(0)$ for even-number neighbors.

## Context

This work reviews and extends the discovery by Johnson, Bertsch, and Dean (2000) that 0 g.s. dominance arises from random two-body interactions, not solely from attractive pairing. It systematizes the empirical approach to predicting $I$ g.s. probabilities for complex systems (fermions in large-$j$ or many-$j$ shells, $sdg$ bosons) where analytic understanding is unavailable, and connects these results to earlier studies of $sd$ bosons by Bijker and Frank (2000-2001) and to the odd-even staggering of binding energies found by Papenbrock (2000). The review enables future work toward a fundamental explanation of 0 g.s. robustness and its relation to generalized seniority concepts from Talmi (1970s).
