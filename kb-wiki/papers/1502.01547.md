# Generalized Bloch theorem and chiral transport phenomena

> Bloch theorem states the impossibility of persistent electric currents in the ground state of nonrelativistic fermion systems. We extend this theorem to generic systems based on the gauged particle number symmetry and study its consequences on the example of chiral transport phenomena. We show that the chiral magnetic effect can be understood as a generalization of the Bloch theorem to a nonequilibrium steady state, similarly to the integer quantum Hall effect. On the other hand, persistent axial currents are not prohibited by the Bloch theorem and they can be regarded as Pauli paramagnetism of relativistic matter. An application of the generalized Bloch theorem to quantum time crystals is also discussed.

## Key claim

The Bloch theorem, which prohibits persistent electric currents in the ground state of nonrelativistic fermion systems, can be extended to generic systems based on gauged U(1) particle number symmetry; this generalized theorem shows that the chiral magnetic effect (CME) cannot exist as a ground-state current in the thermodynamic limit, but can be understood as a nonequilibrium steady state current, while axial currents such as the chiral separation effect (CSE) are not forbidden because no U(1) axial gauge symmetry exists.

## Method

The author generalizes the original Bloch theorem by considering a trial state obtained by applying a coordinate-dependent phase transformation (a gauge transformation) to the ground state, then showing that any nonvanishing total particle number current would allow lowering the ground-state energy, contradicting the assumption of minimal energy. For circulating currents, the same argument is applied on a ring geometry in the thermodynamic limit (radius r → ∞). The CME is then rederived using a Laughlin-type argument on a torus with magnetic flux, showing it arises as a nonequilibrium steady current, while the CSE is derived from Pauli paramagnetism of relativistic fermions at finite chemical potential.

## Key numbers

* CME current density: ⟨j_CME⟩ = (1/(2π²)) μ₅ B, where μ₅ = (μ_R − μ_L)/2 is the chiral chemical potential and B is the magnetic field (units ħ = c = e = 1).
* CVE current density: ⟨j_CVE⟩ = (1/π²) μ μ₅ ω, where μ is the vector chemical potential and ω is the vorticity.
* CSE axial current density: ⟨j₅⟩ = (1/(2π²)) μ B.
* For a nonrelativistic ring, the bound on circulating current is |⟨J_NR⟩|/N ≤ 1/(2mr), which vanishes in the thermodynamic limit r → ∞.
* Degeneracy of lowest Landau level per unit transverse area: g_n = eB/(2πħc).
* Density of states at the Fermi surface for relativistic fermions: N(μ) = μ²/π².

## Context

This work extends the original Bloch theorem (Bohm, 1949) and its finite-temperature extension (Ohashi, 2004) to generic relativistic and interacting systems. It directly addresses the question of whether the chiral magnetic effect (Vilenkin, 1980; Fukushima, 2008) and chiral vortical effect (Vilenkin, 1979; Landsteiner, 2011) can exist as ground-state currents, showing they cannot in the thermodynamic limit. The argument parallels Laughlin's (1981) and Thouless's (1983) treatment of the integer quantum Hall effect as a nonequilibrium steady state, and also applies to the question of quantum time crystals (Wilczek, 2012), ruling out time-dependent persistent circulating currents in the thermodynamic limit.
