# Dirac-Brueckner Approach to Hyperon Interactions and Hypernuclei

> The density dependent relativistic hadron (DDRH) theory is introduced as an effective field theory for nuclei and hypernuclei. A Dirac-Brueckner approach to in-medium nucleon-hyperon interactions is presented. Density dependent meson-baryon vertices are determined from DBHF self-energies in infinite matter. Scaling laws for hyperon vertices are derived from a diagrammatic analysis of self-energies. In a local density approximation the DB vertices are applied in relativistic DDRH Hartree calculations to finite hypernuclei. Lambda single particle spectra and spin-orbit splittings are described reasonably well over the whole mass region.

## Key claim

The Density Dependent Relativistic Hadron (DDRH) theory, extended to hypernuclei, describes Λ single-particle spectra and spin-orbit splittings reasonably well over the whole mass region, but the authors note that the scaling factors for hyperon vertices are treated as phenomenological constants (Rσ = 0.490, Rω = 0.553) rather than being fully derived from first principles, and that deviations of up to 2.5 MeV arise for light nuclei below about ²⁸SiΛ.

## Method

The authors extend the DDRH theory, originally developed for isospin nuclei, to hypernuclei by including the full SU(3)f octet of baryons (Λ, Σ, Ξ) and strangeness-neutral scalar (σ, σs) and vector (ω, φ, ρ) meson fields. Density-dependent meson-baryon vertex functionals are derived from Dirac-Brueckner Hartree-Fock (DBHF) self-energies in infinite symmetric nuclear matter, and scaling laws relating hyperon to nucleon vertices are obtained from a diagrammatic analysis. These vertices are then applied in relativistic DDRH Hartree mean-field calculations for finite single-Λ hypernuclei using a local density approximation.

## Key numbers

- Λ central potential depth: about -30 MeV, compared to -70 MeV for neutrons.
- Scalar scaling factor Rσ = 0.490, derived from free-space NΛ scattering using the Jülich potential.
- Vector scaling factor Rω = 0.553, determined from fitting DDRH calculations to hypernuclear data from (π,K) experiments.
- A 2% reduction of Rω to 0.542 improves the description of light-mass hypernuclei data.
- Deviations between DDRH calculations and data for masses below about ²⁸SiΛ are up to 2.5 MeV.
- Spin-orbit splitting in ¹³CΛ is overestimated with the standard coupling constants, but a 2% reduction of the vector coupling (Rω = 0.542) gives a better description.

## Context

This work extends the DDRH theory previously introduced for isospin nuclei by Lenske and collaborators (1995) to hypernuclei, providing a microscopic alternative to phenomenological relativistic mean-field (RMF) models by Walecka (1986) and others (Rufa, 1990; Glendenning, 1992; Vretenar, 1998) that used empirically adjusted meson-hyperon vertices. The approach enables future Dirac-Brueckner calculations for the full baryon octet and can be applied to study hypernucleus production in hadronic reactions such as (π⁺,K⁺) and (K⁻,π⁻), as well as electro-production of hypernuclei.
