# Giant neutron halo in nuclei beyond beta-stability line

> The radii of nucleon distribution and neutron skin in nuclei beyond the β-stability line are studied within the extended Thomas-Fermi approximation. We show that the growth of neutron skin in unstable nuclei does not obey the saturation condition because of the neutron coat. The neutron coat indicates the possibility of giant neutron halo which is growing with moving away from the beta stability line. We demonstrate the presence of strong shell oscillations in the charge radius R_C and the relation of R_C to the isospin shift of neutron-proton chemical potentials Δλ=λ_n-λ_p for nuclei beyond the beta-stability line at fixed value of mass number A.

# Giant neutron halo in nuclei beyond beta-stability line

## Key claim
The growth of neutron skin in neutron-rich unstable nuclei does not obey the saturation condition R ~ A^{1/3} because of the "neutron coat", a surface layer of excess neutrons, which can lead to a giant neutron halo that increases as one moves away from the beta-stability line, but this effect depends on the specific Skyrme force parametrization and the sign of the partial pressure P_A.

## Method
The authors applied the extended Thomas-Fermi approximation (ETFA) combined with a direct variational method, using Skyrme-type effective nucleon-nucleon interactions and profile functions for the total density ρ₊ and neutron excess density ρ₋ that are powers of the Fermi function. The variational parameters (bulk densities, radius R, diffuseness a, neutron skin parameter Δ, and exponent η) were determined by minimizing the total energy functional under the constraints of neutron and proton number conservation, with Lagrange multipliers λₙ and λₚ representing chemical potentials.

## Key numbers
- The neutron skin parameter Δ as a function of asymmetry X = (N-Z)/(N+Z) for A=120 with SkM forces: Δ(X) ≈ 0.90 X + 1.47 X²
- The shift of bulk density in neutron-rich nuclei relative to the beta-stability line: ρ₀,X = ρ₀,eq − 9 (P_A/K_A) (X − X*)²
- The isospin shift of chemical potentials: Δλ(X) = λₙ − λₚ = 4 [b*_sym(A) + e*_C(A)] (X − X*)
- The Coulomb energy parameter: e*_C(A) = 0.15 A e² / R_C

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of neutron skin and halo phenomena in neutron-rich nuclei, particularly the concept of "neutron coat" introduced by Meto (2002). It builds on the direct variational method developed by Kosa (2008) and the extended Thomas-Fermi framework of Kirzh and others. The results enable systematic testing of Skyrme force parametrizations against experimental data on neutron and charge radii, and provide a method to extract the "experimental" Coulomb radius R_C(A) from binding energy differences using the relation between Δλ and the symmetry energy. The authors compare their ETFA results for Na isotopes with experimental data from Suge (1995) and with the nuclear mass evaluation of AWT (2003).
