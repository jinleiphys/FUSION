# D-state effects in the electromagnetic N-Delta transition

> We consider here a manifestly covariant quark model of the nucleon and the Delta, where one quark is off-shell and the other two quarks form an on-shell diquark pair. Using this model, we have shown previously that the nucleon form factors and the dominant form factor for the gamma N -> Delta transition (the magnetic dipole (M1) form factor) can be well described by nucleon and Delta wave functions with S-state components only. In this paper we show that non-vanishing results for the small electric (E2) and Coulomb (C2) quadrupole form factors can be obtained if D-state components are added to the Delta valence quark wave function. We present a covariant definition of these components and compute their contributions to the form factors. We find that these components cannot, by themselves, describe the data. Explicit pion cloud contributions must also be added and these contributions dominate both the E2 and the C2 form factors. By parametrizing the pion cloud contribution for the transition electric and Coulomb form factors in terms of the neutron electric form factor, we estimate that the contributions of the Delta D-state coupled to quark core spin of 3/2 is of the order of 1%, and the contributions of the Delta D-state coupled to quark core spin 1/2 is of the order of 4%.

## Key claim

The paper shows that adding D-state components to the Delta valence quark wave function yields non-zero electric (E2) and Coulomb (C2) quadrupole form factors for the gamma N -> Delta transition, but these valence quark contributions alone cannot describe the data; explicit pion cloud contributions must also be added and dominate both the E2 and C2 form factors.

## Method

The authors used a manifestly covariant quark model in the spectator formalism, where one quark is off-shell and the other two quarks form an on-shell diquark pair. They constructed covariant D-state wave functions for the Delta by defining a D-wave operator in the Delta rest frame and using spin projection operators to separate the two possible core spin configurations (S=1/2 and S=3/2). The contributions of these D-state components to the transition form factors were computed, and the pion cloud contributions were parametrized in terms of the neutron electric form factor to estimate the relative weight of the D-state admixtures.

## Key numbers

- The contribution of the Delta D-state coupled to quark core spin of 3/2 is of the order of 1%
- The contribution of the Delta D-state coupled to quark core spin of 1/2 is of the order of 4%
- The model covers the momentum transfer region Q^2 less than or equal to 6 GeV^2
- The D-state wave functions use power 4 in the denominators to reproduce expected pQCD behavior (G_E^* ~ 1/Q^4, G_C^* ~ 1/Q^6)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous covariant quark model (Ramalho, Pena, Gross, 2008) which described nucleon form factors and the dominant M1 transition form factor using only S-state wave functions. The results agree with other quark models (Isgur and Karl, 1982; Giannini, 2007) that valence quark effects are insufficient to describe the E2 and C2 data. The paper enables future systematic treatment of pion cloud mechanisms within the spectator formalism, and supports the EBAC program's goal of deriving bare vertices from quark models for use in dynamical models like Sato-Lee (2001), Dubna-Mainz-Taipei (Kamalov, 1999), and Utrecht-Ohio (Pascalutsa, 2004).
