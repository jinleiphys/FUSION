# Phase Randomization and Doppler Peaks in the CMB Angular Power Spectrum

> Using the Boltzmann equation with a Langevin-like term describing the stochastic force in a baryon-photon plasma, we investigate the influence of the incoherent electron-photon scattering on the subhorizon evolution of the cosmic microwave radiation. The stochastic fluctuation caused by each collision on average is found to be small. Nevertheless, it leads to a significant Brownian drifting of the phase in the acoustic oscillation, and the coherent oscillations cannot be maintained during their dynamical evolution. As a consequence, the proposed Doppler peaks probably do not exist.

# Phase Randomization and Doppler Peaks in the CMB Angular Power Spectrum

## Key claim
The coherent acoustic oscillations in the baryon-photon plasma that would produce Doppler peaks in the CMB angular power spectrum cannot be maintained during their dynamical evolution because stochastic fluctuations from incoherent electron-photon scattering cause Brownian drifting of the oscillation phase. The authors argue that the proposed Doppler peaks probably do not exist, contrary to the standard theoretical prediction that treats the oscillations coherently.

## Method
The authors introduce a Langevin-like stochastic force term into the Boltzmann equation for the photon distribution function in a baryon-photon plasma, representing fluctuations from incoherent Compton scattering. They derive the correlation function of this stochastic force and calculate the resulting phase fluctuations of the acoustic oscillation modes by solving the perturbed Boltzmann hierarchy for the brightness temperature anisotropy \(\Theta\). The cumulative effect of phase fluctuations is evaluated over the entire subhorizon evolution period from horizon entry to recombination.

## Key numbers
- The RMS phase fluctuation for mode \(l\) satisfies \(\sqrt{\langle (\delta \phi_l)^2 \rangle} \geq 1.8 \times 10^{-3} \sqrt{(2l+1)N_c}\), where \(N_c \equiv \tau(\eta_{re}-\eta_{en})\) is the mean number of collisions during subhorizon evolution.
- For Doppler peaks, \(l \geq 100\) and \(N_c \geq 10^3\), yielding an RMS phase drift of order 1.
- The fluctuation amplitude per mode is \(\langle |\delta \Theta_l|^2 \rangle / (2l+1) \simeq 1.30 \times 10^{-5} |\Delta T/T|^2\).
- The resolution-dependent fluctuation is \(\delta (\Delta T/T) \simeq 3.61 \times 10^{-3} \sqrt{l} (\Delta T/T)\).

## Context
This work challenges the standard treatment of CMB acoustic oscillations developed by Kodama and Sasaki (1986), Bond and Efstathiou (1987), and Hu and Sugiyama (1995), which assumes coherent phase evolution and predicts Doppler peaks whose positions depend on cosmological parameters. The authors extend the fluctuation-dissipation framework of Onsager and Machlup (1953) and Landau and Lifshitz (1980) to the cosmological Boltzmann equation, following the stochastic Boltzmann approach of Fox and Uhlenbeck (1970). The paper suggests that the observed dispersion in degree-scale CMB anisotropy measurements, which had not conclusively shown Doppler peaks, may reflect the stochastic nature of the phases rather than experimental uncertainty. This work enables a reinterpretation of CMB power spectrum measurements as statistical realizations of a stochastic process rather than deterministic predictions.
