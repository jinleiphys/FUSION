# Nucleon polarization in three-body models of polarized \bbox{^6}Li

> Just as $^3\roarrow{\rm He}$ can be approximately characterized as a polarized neutron target, polarized \Li6D has been advocated as a good {\em isoscalar} nuclear target for the extraction of the polarized gluon content of the nucleon. The original argument rests upon a presumed ``alpha + deuteron'' picture of \Li6, with the polarization of the nucleus carried by the polarization of the deuteron. We have calculated the polarization of the constituents of \Li6 as a three-body bound state of $α+ n + p$ interacting with local potentials fitted to the scattering data. It is necessary to include partial waves up to $j=17/2$ (75 channels, or, when including the $T=1$ state, 150 channels) in the Faddeev equations before the energy eigenvalue converges. The longitudinal formfactors are then described well by the wave function. Various combinations of $α$N and NN strong and Coulomb potentials yield a straight line in the charge radius {\em vs.} energy plane which, unlike those of previous calculations, passes through the experimental datum. We find for all cases a polarization of the valence neutron in excess of 90\%. This may make polarized \Li6D an attractive target for many nuclear physics purposes, since its neutrons are effectively 45\% polarized.

# Nucleon polarization in three-body models of polarized ^6Li

## Key claim

Polarized ^6LiD is an attractive isoscalar nuclear target because the valence neutron polarization exceeds 90% in all three-body models considered, yielding an effective neutron polarization of 45% in the target material, though this is not as high as the 87% polarization of neutrons in polarized ^3He, the solid, dense nature of ^6LiD may make it an attractive alternative for some nuclear physics purposes.

## Method

The authors solved configuration-space Faddeev equations for ^6Li modeled as a three-body bound state of an alpha particle, a neutron, and a proton interacting with local phenomenological potentials fitted to scattering data. They included partial waves up to j=17/2 (75 channels for the pure T=0 case, 150 channels when including the T=1 state) to achieve convergence of the energy eigenvalue, and used the spline method for numerical solution. Various combinations of alpha-nucleon potentials (Sack-Biedenharn-Breit, Satchler, Cooper-Mackintosh) and nucleon-nucleon potentials (Reid soft core, super soft core, Malfliet-Tjon I-III) were tested, with the Pauli principle handled by suppressing forbidden bound states via a projection method.

## Key numbers

- Valence neutron polarization exceeds **90%** in all cases, implying an effective neutron polarization of **45%** in polarized ^6LiD
- The Faddeev eigenvalue converges only when including partial waves up to **j=17/2**, corresponding to **75 channels** (or **150 channels** when including the T=1 state)
- The total P-state probability in the wave function is approximately **3%**
- The charge radius vs. energy plane yields a straight line passing through the experimental datum, unlike previous calculations

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion (Fermilab proposal) that polarized ^6LiD could serve as an isoscalar polarized target for extracting the polarized gluon distribution in nucleons, which was based on a simple "alpha + deuteron" picture. The authors provide a more sophisticated three-body calculation that validates the optimistic polarization estimates. The results are relevant to experiments at Fermilab and the UNK proton accelerator, and complement the established use of polarized ^3He as a polarized neutron target (Friar, 1990). The work enables future experimental tests of the predictions via knockout reactions and scattering experiments, similar to the program that tests ^3He wave functions.
