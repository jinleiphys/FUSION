# pi-NN Coupling Constants from NN Elastic Data between 210 and 800 Mev

> High partial waves for $pp$ and $np$ elastic scattering are examined critically from 210 to 800 MeV. Non-OPE contributions are compared with predictions from theory. There are some discrepancies, but sufficient agreement that values of the $πNN$ coupling constants $g_0^2$ for $π^0$ exchange and $g^2_{c}$ for charged $π$ exchange can be derived. Results are $g^2_0 = 13.91 \pm 0.13 \pm 0.07$ and $g^2_c = 13.69 \pm 0.15 \pm 0.24$, where the first error is statistical and the second is an estimate of the likely systematic error, arising mostly from uncertainties in the normalisation of total cross sections and $dσ/dΩ$.

## Key claim

The paper derives values of the pi-NN coupling constants from NN elastic scattering data between 210 and 800 MeV, finding that the neutral coupling constant g0^2 = 13.91 +/- 0.13 +/- 0.07 is significantly larger than the Nijmegen group's value of 13.54 +/- 0.13, while the charged coupling constant gc^2 = 13.69 +/- 0.15 +/- 0.24 is close to recent pi-N dispersion relation results, though the authors caution that systematic uncertainties in normalisation of total cross sections and differential cross sections limit the precision.

## Method

The authors performed a critical partial-wave analysis of high partial waves for pp and np elastic scattering from 210 to 800 MeV, using the Bonn peripheral model to predict heavy-boson-exchange (HBE) contributions from rho, omega, and correlated 2-pi exchange. They isolated the one-pion-exchange (OPE) contribution from tensor components in mixing parameters epsilon_4, epsilon_5, epsilon_6 and from tensor combinations of H, I, and K waves, comparing predictions with experimental Wolfenstein parameters D_NN, K_NN, and K_LL together with differential cross sections. Corrections for discrepancies in specific partial waves were applied using an impact-parameter prescription, and systematic errors were estimated from normalisation uncertainties in the cross-section data.

## Key numbers

- g0^2 = 13.91 +/- 0.13 (statistical) +/- 0.07 (systematic) for neutral pion exchange
- gc^2 = 13.69 +/- 0.15 (statistical) +/- 0.24 (systematic) for charged pion exchange (preferred value from epsilon_5 alone)
- gc^2 = 13.67 +/- 0.29 from epsilon_5 and higher partial waves with inflated error
- gc^2 rises to 13.90 if the total cross section data of Lisowski et al. are dropped
- The Nijmegen group's values: g0^2 = 13.54 +/- 0.13, gc^2 = 13.39 +/- 0.09
- Arndt et al. (1993) preliminary value: gc^2 = 13.72 +/- 0.15
- Earlier Bugg-Carter-Carter value from pi-N dispersion relations: g^2 = 14.28 +/- 0.18
- Form factor correction to epsilon_4 from a cutoff Lambda = 1.4 GeV/c^2 is +0.04 degrees at 800 MeV, negligible
- Systematic error on g0^2 from d-sigma/d-Omega normalisation: +/- 0.07
- Systematic error on gc^2 from normalisation uncertainties: +/- 0.24

## Context

This work extends the determination of pi-NN coupling constants to higher energies (210-800 MeV) than the Nijmegen group's analyses (Bergervoet et al., 1987; Klomp et al., 1991), which used NN data up to 350 MeV and found lower values. The results are consistent with the higher values from pi-N fixed-t dispersion relations (Markopoulou-Kalamara and Bugg, 1993) and marginally above the preliminary value of Arndt et al. (1993). The paper enables improved determinations by noting that further precise measurements of Wolfenstein parameters in the 140-300 MeV range below the inelastic threshold, in progress at IUCF, could reduce the error on g0^2 to about +/- 0.06.
