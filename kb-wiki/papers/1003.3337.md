# Investigation of Phase Diagram and Bulk Thermodynamic Properties using PNJL Model with Eight-Quark Interactions

> We present the bulk thermodynamic properties and phase diagram of strongly interacting matter in an extension of the 3-flavor NJL and PNJL models of QCD. Using a three momentum cut-off scheme, we have extended the multiquark interaction terms up to eight order so that the stability of the vacuum is ensured in these models. We explore the effects of various combinations of the two eight-quark couplings $g_1$ and $g_2$ and present a comparative study between the NJL and PNJL models as well as Lattice QCD data. The main effect of the eight-quark interaction term is to shift the critical end point in the $T-μ$ phase diagram to a lower value of $μ$ and higher value of $T$, thus bringing them closer to Lattice QCD results.

# Investigation of Phase Diagram and Bulk Thermodynamic Properties using PNJL Model with Eight-Quark Interactions

## Key claim

The addition of eight-quark interaction terms to the 3-flavor PNJL model shifts the critical end point (CEP) in the temperature-chemical potential phase diagram to lower chemical potential and higher temperature, bringing it closer to lattice QCD predictions; however, this result depends on the specific parameter set chosen, and only the PNJL model with eight-quark interactions satisfies the lattice criterion that the ratio μ_C/T_C be less than or equal to 2.5.

## Method

The authors extended the 2+1 flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) and Polyakov loop extended NJL (PNJL) models by including eight-quark interaction terms (with couplings g₁ and g₂) in addition to the standard four-quark and six-quark ('t Hooft) interactions, using a three-momentum cutoff regularization scheme. They employed a mean-field approximation to derive the thermodynamic potential and gap equations for the constituent quark masses, and incorporated a Vandermonde term in the Polyakov loop potential to constrain the Polyakov loop value below unity. Four parameter sets were constructed for both the six-quark-only and eight-quark-inclusive cases, fitted to reproduce physical observables including pion mass (138 MeV), kaon mass (494 MeV), pion decay constant (93 MeV), and either the sigma meson mass (680 MeV) or the u-quark condensate.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass m_π = 138 MeV, kaon mass m_K = 494 MeV, eta mass m_η = 480 MeV, eta prime mass m_η′ = 957 MeV, pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV, kaon decay constant f_K = 117 MeV, u-quark condensate σ_u^(1/3) = -248 MeV, sigma meson mass m_σ = 680 MeV (used as fitting inputs)
- For the PNJL model with eight-quark interactions, the critical end point locations across four parameter sets range from (μ_C = 213 MeV, T_C = 134.75 MeV) to (μ_C = 260 MeV, T_C = 118.5 MeV); for the PNJL model with only six-quark interactions, CEP locations range from (μ_C = 313 MeV, T_C = 92.85 MeV) to (μ_C = 320 MeV, T_C = 89.15 MeV)
- For the NJL model with eight-quark interactions, CEP locations range from (μ_C = 263 MeV, T_C = 61.2 MeV) to (μ_C = 305 MeV, T_C = 46.55 MeV); for the NJL model with only six-quark interactions, CEP locations range from (μ_C = 323 MeV, T_C = 48.5 MeV) to (μ_C = 328 MeV, T_C = 46.45 MeV)
- Transition temperatures at zero chemical potential for PNJL with eight-quark interactions range from 168.5 MeV to 171.8 MeV across parameter sets; for PNJL with six-quark interactions, from 180.5 MeV to 183.5 MeV
- The shift in pressure due to eight-quark interactions ranges from 10% to 20% across the temperature range

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the 2+1 flavor PNJL model with four-quark and six-quark interactions (Deb, 2009) by incorporating eight-quark interaction terms to stabilize the vacuum, following the theoretical framework of Osipov, Hiller, and collaborators (2006-2007) who showed that eight-quark interactions resolve the unbounded-from-below vacuum problem in the functional integral approach. The results contradict earlier NJL model predictions (Hatsuda and Kunihiro, 1994) that placed the CEP at lower temperature (48 MeV), and bring the CEP location closer to lattice QCD estimates (Ejiri, 2008) which suggest μ_C/T_C ≤ 2.5. The study enables more reliable comparisons between effective QCD models and lattice data for bulk thermodynamic properties such as pressure, trace anomaly, and quark number density at finite temperature and chemical potential.
