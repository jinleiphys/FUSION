# Statistical Fluctuations of Electromagnetic Transition Intensities and Electromagnetic Moments in pf-Shell Nuclei

> We study the fluctuation properties of $ΔT=0$ electromagnetic transition intensities and electromagnetic moments in $A \sim 60$ nuclei within the framework of the interacting shell model, using a realistic effective interaction for $pf$-shell nuclei with a $^{56}$Ni core. The distributions of the transition intensities and of the electromagnetic moments are well described by the Gaussian orthogonal ensemble of random matrices. In particular, the transition intensity distributions follow a Porter-Thomas distribution. When diagonal matrix elements (i.e., moments) are included in the analysis of transition intensities, we find that the distributions remain Porter-Thomas except for the isoscalar $M1$. The latter deviation is explained in terms of the structure of the isoscalar $M1$ operator.

# Statistical Fluctuations of Electromagnetic Transition Intensities and Electromagnetic Moments in pf-Shell Nuclei

## Key claim

The distributions of ΔT=0 electromagnetic transition intensities and electromagnetic moments in A~60 nuclei are well described by the Gaussian orthogonal ensemble (GOE) of random matrices, with transition intensities following a Porter-Thomas distribution; however, when diagonal matrix elements (moments) are included, the isoscalar M1 operator in self-conjugate (Tz=0) nuclei shows a significant deviation from Porter-Thomas statistics, which the abstract does not qualify as an exception.

## Method

The authors performed interacting shell model calculations for A~60 nuclei using a ⁵⁶Ni core with the F5P effective interaction, considering configurations in the 0f5/2, 1p3/2, and 1p1/2 orbitals. They computed ΔT=0 E2 and M1 transition intensities and electromagnetic moments for J=2⁺, T=1 states in A=60 and A=62 nuclei, using the OXBASH shell model code. The transition intensities were renormalized by dividing out the secular energy variation using a Gaussian smoothing method with γ≈2.5, and the resulting distributions were compared to the Porter-Thomas (χ² with ν=1) distribution predicted by RMT.

## Key numbers

- 4290 transition matrix elements were sampled for each transition operator (66² − 66) for the A=60 calculations
- The smoothing parameter γ was set to approximately 2.5 for the Gaussian averaging procedure
- Effective charges of e_p=1.5 and e_n=0.5 were used for E2 transitions
- Calculations were performed for A=60 nuclei (⁶⁰Co, ⁶⁰Zn, ⁶⁰Cu) and A=62 nuclei (⁶²Ga)
- 66 diagonal matrix elements were available for the magnetic moment distribution analysis

## Context

This work extends previous statistical fluctuation studies from lighter sd-shell nuclei (A≲40) to the pf-shell region (A~60), where complete 0ħω calculations are not feasible. It builds on earlier shell model studies of B(M1) and B(E2) transitions in ²²Na by Adams et al. (2000) and on the broader framework of RMT applications to nuclear systems established by Bohigas et al. (1984) and Alhassid (2001). The results enable systematic testing of RMT predictions for electromagnetic observables in medium-mass nuclei and identify the isoscalar M1 operator as a special case where the simple structure of the operator (involving total orbital angular momentum L and total spin S) leads to anomalously large diagonal matrix elements that break the Porter-Thomas statistics.
