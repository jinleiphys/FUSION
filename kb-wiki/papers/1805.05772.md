# Neutrino Emissivity in the Quark-Hadron Mixed Phase

> In this work we investigate the effect a crystalline quark-hadron mixed phase can have on the neutrino emissivity from the cores of neutron stars. To this end we use relativistic mean-field equations of state to model hadronic matter and a nonlocal extension of the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model for quark matter. Next we determine the extent of the quark-hadron mixed phase and its crystalline structure using the Glendenning construction, allowing for the formation of spherical blob, rod, and slab rare phase geometries. Finally we calculate the neutrino emissivity due to electron-lattice interactions utilizing the formalism developed for the analogous process in neutron star crusts. We find that the contribution to the neutrino emissivity due to the presence of a crystalline quark-hadron mixed phase is substantial compared to other mechanisms at fairly low temperatures ($\lesssim 10^9$ K) and quark fractions ($\lesssim 30\%$), and that contributions due to lattice vibrations are insignificant compared to static-lattice contributions. There are a number of open issues that need to be addressed in a future study on the neutrino emission rates caused by electron-quark blob bremsstrahlung. Chiefly among them are the role of collective oscillations of matter, electron band structures, and of gaps at the boundaries of the Brillouin zones on bremsstrahlung, as discussed in the summary section of this paper. We hope this paper will stimulate studies addressing these issues.

## Key claim

The neutrino emissivity from a crystalline quark-hadron mixed phase in neutron star cores is substantial compared to other mechanisms only at fairly low temperatures (≲ 10⁹ K) and low quark fractions (≲ 30%), and contributions from lattice vibrations (phonons) are insignificant compared to static-lattice contributions.

## Method

The authors used relativistic mean-field (RMF) equations of state to model hadronic matter and a nonlocal extension of the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (n3NJL) model for quark matter. They determined the extent and crystalline structure of the quark-hadron mixed phase using the Glendenning construction, allowing for spherical blob, rod, and slab rare phase geometries, and calculated the neutrino emissivity from electron-lattice interactions using the formalism developed for analogous processes in neutron star crusts by Kaminker et al. (1999) and Pethick and Thorsson (1997).

## Key numbers

- The neutrino emissivity for spherical blobs is given by ε_MPB^blobs ≈ 5.37×10²⁰ n T₉⁶ Z² L erg s⁻¹ cm⁻³.
- The neutrino emissivity for rods and slabs is given by ε_MPB^rods,slabs ≈ 4.81×10¹⁷ k_e T₉⁸ J erg s⁻¹ cm⁻³.
- The MPB emissivity is comparable to the modified Urca process in the temperature range 10⁸ K ≲ T ≲ 10⁹ K.
- The lattice melting temperature is Γ_melt = 175, above which the mixed phase behaves as a Coulomb crystal.
- The surface tension parameter η = 0.08 was chosen so that surface tension falls below 70 MeV fm⁻² for all parametrizations.
- The vector coupling constant G_V was restricted to the range 0 < G_V < 0.05 G_S to satisfy the 2.01 M⊙ mass constraint from PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis 2013).
- The hypernuclear potentials used are U_Λ^(N) = -28 MeV, U_Σ^(N) = +30 MeV, and U_Ξ^(N) = -18 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Na and collaborators (2012) and Spinella (2016) by using nuclear equations of state that are in better agreement with the latest nuclear matter constraints at saturation density and consistent with the 2.01 M⊙ mass constraint from PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis 2013). The quark matter description uses the n3NJL parametrization "Set I" from Sandoval et al. (2016), which is in better agreement with empirical quark masses than earlier parametrizations. The results enable future studies on the role of collective oscillations of matter, electron band structures, and Brillouin zone boundary gaps on electron-quark blob bremsstrahlung.
