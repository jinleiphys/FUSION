# Neutrino-Induced Reactions on Nuclei

> Background: Long-baseline experiments such as the planned Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment (DUNE) require theoretical descriptions of the complete event in a neutrino-nucleus reaction. Since nuclear targets are used this requires a good understanding of neutrino-nucleus interactions. Purpose: Develop a consistent theory and code framework for the description of lepton-nucleus interactions that can be used to describe not only inclusive cross sections, but also the complete final state of the reaction. Methods: The Giessen-Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) implementation of quantum-kinetic transport theory is used, with improvements in its treatment of the nuclear ground state and of 2p2h interactions. For the latter an empirical structure function from electron scattering data is used as a basis. Results: Results for electron-induced inclusive cross sections are given as a necessary check for the overall quality of this approach. The calculated neutrino-induced inclusive double-differential cross sections show good agreement with data from neutrino- and antineutrino reactions for different neutrino flavors at MiniBooNE and T2K. Inclusive double-differential cross sections for MicroBooNE, NOvA, MINERvA and LBNF/DUNE are given. Conclusions: Based on the GiBUU model of lepton-nucleus descriptions a good theoretical description of inclusive electron-, neutrino- and antineutrino-nucleus data over a wide range of energies, different neutrino flavors and different experiments is now possible. Since no tuning is involved this theory and code should be reliable also for new energy regimes and target masses. \end{description}

## Key claim

The GiBUU transport-theory framework, without any tuning, can describe inclusive electron-, neutrino-, and antineutrino-nucleus cross sections across a wide range of energies and targets, but the agreement relies on an empirical 2p2h structure function extracted from electron scattering data and on the assumption that the dominant 2p2h contributions are purely transverse.

## Method

The authors used the GiBUU implementation of quantum-kinetic transport theory, which includes a nuclear ground state with a coordinate- and momentum-dependent potential and a local Fermi-gas momentum distribution. They improved the treatment of 2p2h interactions by using an empirical transverse structure function \(W_1^{\rm MEC}\) from Bosted and Mamyan (2012) and Christy (2015), derived from electron inclusive data, and related it to neutrino structure functions using the formalism of O'Connell (1972). The code also handles quasielastic scattering, pion production through nucleon resonances, and deep inelastic scattering via PYTHIA 6.4, all within a consistent framework that allows calculation of both inclusive cross sections and full final-state events.

## Key numbers

* The axial mass for the dipole form factor in quasielastic scattering is set to \(M_A = 1.03\) GeV.
* The axial mass for the dipole form factor in pion production is set to \(M_A = 1\) GeV.
* The vector mass cut-off parameter for form factors is 0.84 GeV.
* The default Fermi energy for the nuclear ground state is fixed at \(E_F = -8\) MeV.
* For electron scattering on carbon at 560 MeV and 60 degrees (\(Q^2 = 0.24\) GeV\(^2\) at the QE peak), the 2p2h contribution amounts to about 10% under the quasielastic peak.
* For electron scattering on carbon at 560 MeV and 145 degrees (\(Q^2 = 0.55\) GeV\(^2\) at the QE peak), the 2p2h contribution again amounts to about 10% under the quasielastic peak.
* For the T2K muon-neutrino beam on carbon, at the largest scattering angle (\(\cos \theta = 0.42\)), the 2p2h contribution amounts to about 20% of the total inclusive double-differential cross section.

## Context

This work extends the GiBUU framework, previously tested on \(eA\), \(\gamma A\), \(\pi A\), \(pA\), and \(AA\) reactions, to provide a consistent description of neutrino-nucleus interactions for long-baseline experiments. It addresses shortcomings of existing generators like GENIE (Andreopoulos, 2009) and NEUT (Hayato, 2009), which rely on free-particle Monte Carlo cascades and neglect nuclear binding, and it provides a unified alternative to approaches that separately treat nuclear many-body theory (e.g., Benhar, 2006; Lovato, 2015) or RPA correlations (e.g., Kolbe, 2003; Martini, 2016). The results enable reliable predictions for future experiments such as DUNE, MicroBooNE, NOvA, and MINERvA without requiring tuning to new energy regimes or target masses.
