# Effect of compound nucleus spin correlations on fission fragment angular distribution

> We extend a conventional description of the fusion-fission fragment angular distributions by introducing the correlation between compound nucleus states carrying different total angular momenta. This correlation results in the strong anisotropy and mass-angle correlation of fission fragments for compact saddle-point nuclear shapes for which the conventional description predicts almost isotropic angular distributions. The spin off-diagonal phase relaxation timescale, $\simeq 10^{-19}$ sec, obtained from analysis of anomalous fission fragment angular distributions in $^{12}$C+$^{236}$U, $^{16}$O+$^{232}$Th and $^{16}$O+$^{238}$U collisions at the sub-barrier energies is three orders of magnitude longer than the timescale of the compound nucleus thermalization. Expression for the angle-dependent time power spectrum for quasifission is also presented.

## Key claim

The paper extends the conventional transition-state statistical model (TSSM) of fission fragment angular distributions by introducing correlations between compound nucleus states with different total angular momenta, which can produce strong anisotropy and mass-angle correlation even for compact saddle-point shapes where the standard model predicts almost isotropic distributions. The authors argue that the spin off-diagonal phase relaxation timescale is about three orders of magnitude longer than the compound nucleus thermalization timescale, contrary to the conventional assumption that such correlations vanish.

## Method

The authors modify the standard TSSM expression for the angular distribution W(θ) by including off-diagonal terms with J1 ≠ J2, using a pole expansion of the S-matrix with residues described by Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble statistics. The spin off-diagonal correlations are characterized by a correlation length β in the resonance energy space, and the final expression includes a factor [1 + (β/Γ)|J1 − J2|]⁻¹ that suppresses correlations between states with large spin differences. The model is applied to fit experimental fission fragment angular distributions from three sub-barrier fusion-fission reactions, using the orientation-dependent fusion concept with a pre-fusion dinuclear system preferentially oriented along the beam direction.

## Key numbers

- For ¹²C+²³⁶U at Ec.m. = 59 MeV: TSSM predicts A_TSSM = W(π)/W(π/2) = 1.1–1.15 while experimental value is A_exp ≈ 1.7, giving (A_exp − 1)/(A_TSSM − 1) ≈ 6
- For ¹²C+²³⁶U: β/Γ = 2.8, with Γ ≈ 1.2 keV and β ≈ 3.4 keV, giving ℏ/β ≈ 2 × 10⁻¹⁹ sec for the spin off-diagonal phase relaxation timescale
- The thermalization timescale ℏ/Γ_spr ≈ 1.3 × 10⁻²² sec (using Γ_spr ≈ 5 MeV), making the spin correlation timescale about 3 orders of magnitude longer
- For ¹⁶O+²³²Th at Ec.m. = 74.5 MeV: A_exp ≈ 1.8 while TSSM gives (A_TSSM − 1) ≈ 0.1; fit requires β/Γ = 2, β ≈ 3 keV
- For ¹⁶O+²³⁸U at Ec.m. = 72.8 MeV: A_exp = 2.6, fit yields β/Γ = 1.66; at Ec.m. = 75.6 MeV: A_exp = 1.85, reproduced with β/Γ = 1.66 and |Φ̇| = 3.6°
- For ¹⁶O+²³⁸U: Γ ≈ 2.5 keV, β ≈ 4 keV, with β ≪ Γ_spr ≈ 5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the transition-state statistical model of Halpern and Strutinsky (1958) by introducing compound nucleus spin correlations that were previously assumed to vanish based on Bohr's independence hypothesis. The authors connect their approach to earlier work on fore-aft asymmetry in evaporation spectra by Gugelot (1954) and Benet (2008), and to the scission-point statistical model of Rossner (1984). The results enable reinterpretation of anomalous fission fragment angular distributions in sub-barrier fusion-fission reactions, suggesting that mass-angle correlation does not necessarily indicate quasifission but can arise from compound nucleus spin off-diagonal phase correlations. The paper also presents an expression for the angle-dependent time power spectrum for quasifission, relating to dissipative heavy-ion collision work by Kun (1997).
