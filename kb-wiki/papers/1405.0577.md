# Polyakov linear SU(3) sigma model: features of higher order moments in dense and thermal hadronic medium

> In order to characterize the higher order moments of the particle multiplicity, we implement the linear-sigma model with Polyakov-loop correction. We first studied the critical phenomena and estimated some thermodynamic quantities. Then, we compared all these results with the first--principle lattice QCD calculations. Then, the extensive study of non-normalized four moments is followed by investigating their thermal and density dependence. We repeat this for moments normalized to temperature and chemical potential. The fluctuations of the second order moment is used to estimate the chiral phase--transition. Then, we implement all these in mapping out the chiral phase transition, which shall be compared with the freeze-out parameters estimated from the lattice QCD simulations and the thermal models are compared with the chiral phase--diagram.

## Key claim

The Polyakov linear SU(3) sigma model (PLSM) can reproduce the pressure and trace anomaly from lattice QCD calculations by HotQCD (2012) but fails to reproduce the more recent lattice QCD results from QCDL (2013), suggesting that the polynomial form of the Polyakov-loop potential may be insufficient to capture the gluonic interactions needed for the newer data.

## Method

The authors implement a linear sigma model with three quark flavors (two light and one strange) coupled to a Polyakov-loop potential in the mean-field approximation. They minimize the thermodynamic potential with respect to the chiral condensates (sigma_x and sigma_y) and the Polyakov-loop order parameters (phi and phi*) to obtain their thermal and chemical-potential dependence. The first four non-normalized and normalized moments of particle multiplicity are then computed from the resulting thermodynamic quantities.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for light quarks at vanishing chemical potential: T_c,q ~ 175 MeV
- Critical temperature for strange quarks at vanishing chemical potential: T_c,s ~ 275 MeV
- Critical temperature for combined light and strange quarks at vanishing chemical potential: T_c,q+s ~ 240 MeV
- Vacuum chiral condensate for light quarks: sigma_x0 = 92.4 MeV
- Vacuum chiral condensate for strange quarks: sigma_y0 = 94.5 MeV
- Deconfinement temperature in pure gauge sector: T_0 = 270 MeV
- Quark chemical potential at which a prompt chiral phase transition appears: ~310 MeV
- Stefan-Boltzmann limit for pressure: 5.2 (normalized units)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the linear sigma model with Polyakov-loop corrections (Schaefer 2008, Mao 2010) by focusing on higher-order moments of particle multiplicity rather than only thermodynamic quantities. The results are compared with lattice QCD calculations from HotQCD (2012) and QCDL (2013), and with freeze-out parameters from thermal models (Tawfik 2005, 2004). The paper enables future mapping of the chiral phase diagram and comparison with experimental freeze-out parameters from heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
