# Supernova electron capture rates on odd-odd nuclei

> At densities between 10^8 and 10^10} g/cm^3 electron capture in a presupernova collapse is believed to mainly occur on odd-odd nuclei. We have derived the rates for six of the most important electron capturing nuclei, {54,56,58}Mn and {56,58,60}Co, based on calculations of the Gamow-Teller strength distributions for the ground states and first excited states. These calculations have been performed by shell model diagonalization in the pf shell using a recently modified version of the KB3 interaction. The shell model rates are noticeably smaller than the presently adopted rates as the latter have been derived by placing the Gamow-Teller (GT) resonance at too low excitation energies.

## Key claim

The shell model calculations place the centroid of the Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distribution for odd-odd nuclei at excitation energies typically more than 2 MeV higher than assumed in the standard parametrizations of Fuller, Fowler and Newman (FFN) and Aufderheide et al., leading to electron capture rates that are significantly smaller than previously adopted.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell model diagonalization calculations in the pf shell using a modified version of the KB3 interaction to compute the Gamow-Teller strength distributions for the ground states and first excited states of six odd-odd nuclei: 54,56,58Mn and 56,58,60Co. The GT strength distributions were calculated in truncated model spaces that fulfilled the Ikeda sum rule and allowed up to 4 particles to be excited from the f7/2 shell, with the resulting strength scaled by a universal quenching factor of (0.74)2. Electron capture rates at finite temperature (T ≈ 4-7 × 109 K) were then computed including contributions from thermally excited states in the parent nucleus below 1 MeV, using experimental excitation energies and mass splittings where available.

## Key numbers

- GT centroids for ground states: 7.15 MeV (54Mn), 5.9 MeV (56Mn), 5.5 MeV (58Mn), 8.2 MeV (56Co), 7.35 MeV (58Co), 6.35 MeV (60Co); these are typically more than 2 MeV higher than the FFN and Aufderheide parametrizations, except for 56Mn where the difference is about 0.6 MeV
- Total B(GT) values (in units of gA2): 4.4 (54Mn), 2.7 (56Mn), 1.5 (58Mn), 7.7 (56Co), 5.9 (58Co), 3.7 (60Co); independent particle values in parentheses are 8.6, 8.6, 7.2, 12.0, 12.0, 10.0 respectively
- Shell model rates are smaller than FFN rates by factors of 4 (56Mn) to 12 (54Mn) for Mn isotopes, and by factors of 30 (58Co) to 400 (60Co) for Co isotopes
- For 56Co, the difference EGT - Ex is within 60 keV for the lowest J=2-5 states (≈ 8.2 MeV), supporting the Brink hypothesis for the bulk of GT strength

## Context

This work extends previous shell model studies that successfully reproduced measured GT distributions for even-even and odd-A nuclei in the mass range A=50-64 (Radha et al., 1997; Martinez-Pinedo et al., 1999) to the odd-odd nuclei that dominate electron capture at presupernova densities. The results contradict the standard rate compilations of Fuller, Fowler and Newman (1980-1985) and Aufderheide et al. (1994), which placed the GT centroid at too low excitation energies for odd-odd parent nuclei. The findings suggest that total electron capture rates at densities ρ7 ≤ 1000 may be smaller than currently believed, potentially affecting the collapse trajectory, though the authors caution that a complete set of shell model rates for all relevant nuclei and the competition with β-decay must be evaluated before drawing conclusions about the collapse mechanism.
