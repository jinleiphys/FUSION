# Neutron-Deuteron System and Photon Polarization Parameter at Thermal Neutron Energies

> Effective Field Theory(EFT) is, the unique, model independent and systematic low-energy version of QCD for processes involving momenta below the pion mass. A low-energy photo-nuclear observable in three-body systems, photon polarization parameter at thermal neutron energies is calculated by using pionless EFT up to next-to-next to leading order(N$^2$LO). In order to make a comparative study of this model, we compared our results for photon polarization parameter with the realistic Argonne $v_{18}$ two-nucleon and Urbana IX or Tucson-Melbourne three-nucleon interactions. Three-body currents give small but significant contributions to some of the observables in the neutron-deuteron radiative capture cross section at thermal neutron energies. In this formalism the three-nucleon forces are needed up to N$^2$LO for cut-off independent results. Our result converges order by order in low energy expansion and also cut-off independent at this order.

## Key claim

The photon polarization parameter \(R_c\) for neutron-deuteron radiative capture at thermal energies is calculated using pionless Effective Field Theory (EFT) up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N\(^2\)LO), yielding a value that converges order by order and is cut-off independent, with the final result \(R_c = -0.412 \pm 0.003\), within 2% of the experimental value, but the abstract does not highlight that this agreement is achieved only after including three-nucleon forces up to N\(^2\)LO and that the calculation is dominated by the magnetic dipole \(M_1\) transition from S-wave capture.

## Method

The Faddeev integral equation for neutron-deuteron scattering was solved numerically up to N\(^2\)LO in pionless EFT, using point-like nucleon interactions and a momentum cut-off regulator. Photon interactions were added by minimally coupling the photon to nucleons, two-body systems (via the \(L_1\) vertex fixed by the thermal cross section), and three-body vertices. Three-nucleon forces were included up to N\(^2\)LO to achieve cut-off independent results, with parameters fixed by the triton binding energy and the neutron-deuteron scattering length in the triton channel.

## Key numbers

* Photon polarization parameter at thermal neutron energies (0.0253 eV): \(R_c = -0.412 \pm 0.003\) from pionless EFT at N\(^2\)LO, compared to the experimental value \(-0.42 \pm 0.03\) from Konijnenberg et al.
* Order-by-order convergence of \(R_c\): LO = \(-0.387\), NLO = \(-0.403\), N\(^2\)LO = \(-0.412\).
* Total cross section for \(nd \rightarrow {}^3\mathrm{H}\gamma\) at thermal energies: \(\sigma_{\mathrm{tot}} = [0.485(\mathrm{LO}) + 0.011(\mathrm{NLO}) + 0.007(\mathrm{N}^2\mathrm{LO})] = [0.503 \pm 0.003]\ \mathrm{mb}\).
* Deuteron binding momentum: \(\gamma_t = 45.7025\ \mathrm{MeV}\); effective range in triplet channel: \(\rho_t = 1.764\ \mathrm{fm}\); singlet scattering length: \(a_s = -23.714\ \mathrm{fm}\); singlet effective range: \(r_s = 2.73\ \mathrm{fm}\).
* The \(L_1\) coupling constant fixed at leading order by the thermal cross section: \(L_1 = -4.5\ \mathrm{fm}\).

## Context

This work extends the application of pionless EFT to three-body photo-nuclear observables, following earlier calculations of the \(nd\) radiative capture cross section by Sadeghi (2006). It provides a model-independent comparison to potential-model calculations using Argonne \(v_{18}\) two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon interactions by Viviani et al. (1996) and gauge-invariant current calculations by Marcucci (2005), which gave cross sections of 0.578 mb and 0.556 mb respectively, above the experimental value by 14% and 10%. The pionless EFT result for \(R_c\) (\(-0.412\)) is closer to the experimental value than several potential-model predictions, and the systematic error is now smaller than the experimental uncertainty, enabling future precision tests of short-range physics in low-energy nuclear reactions.
