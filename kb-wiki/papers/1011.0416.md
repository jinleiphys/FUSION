# Hard-thermal-loop QCD Thermodynamics

> Naively resummed perturbative approximations to the thermodynamic functions of QCD do not converge at phenomenologically relevant temperatures. Here we review recent results of a three-loop hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory calculation of the thermodynamic functions of a quark-gluon plasma for general N_c and N_f. We show comparisons of our recent results with lattice data from both the hotQCD and Wuppertal-Budapest groups. We demonstrate that the three-loop hard-thermal-loop perturbation result for QCD thermodynamics agrees with lattice data down to temperatures T ~ 2 T_c.

## Key claim

Three-loop hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) predictions for QCD thermodynamics agree with lattice data down to temperatures T ~ 2 T_c, but only when using a perturbative Debye mass from dimensional reduction rather than a variational mass prescription, and with the fermion thermal mass set to zero.

## Method

The authors performed a three-loop (NNLO) calculation of the thermodynamic potential for SU(N_c) Yang-Mills theory with N_f fermions using hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory, which reorganizes finite-temperature perturbation theory by expanding around an ideal gas of massive quasiparticles with gauge-invariant screening and Landau damping. The thermodynamic potential was evaluated in a double expansion in g_s, m_D/T, and m_q/T, keeping terms through order g_s^5, with the Debye mass taken from the NLO expression of three-dimensional electric QCD and m_q set to zero. The results were compared to continuum-extrapolated lattice data from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration and N_tau=8 results from the hotQCD collaboration.

## Key numbers

- The three-loop HTLpt result for QCD pressure agrees with lattice data down to T ~ 2 T_c ~ 340 MeV for both N_f=3 and N_f=4.
- For N_f=3, using three-loop running with Lambda_MSbar = 344 MeV gives alpha_s(5 GeV) = 0.2034.
- The correction from NLO to NNLO reaches 100% near T_c.
- The naive weak-coupling expansion requires alpha_s <= 0.07 for the g_s^3 contribution to be smaller than the g_s^2 contribution, corresponding to T ~ 10^5 GeV or T ~ 5 x 10^5 T_c.

## Context

This work extends previous LO and NLO HTLpt calculations (HTLpt, 2000s) and addresses the failure of naive perturbative expansions for QCD thermodynamics, which oscillate wildly at phenomenologically relevant temperatures as shown by standard perturbation theory results (BN, 1990s). The results enable application of HTLpt to real-time quantities at LHC-relevant temperatures, and are consistent with the quasiparticle picture for the quark-gluon plasma down to T ~ 2 T_c, in contrast to strongly-coupled approaches based on AdS/CFT. The pure Yang-Mills NNLO results (pg, 2009) showed worse agreement with lattice data than the quark-inclusive results presented here.
