# Sivers Asymmetry for the proton and the neutron

> A formalism is presented to evaluate the Sivers function in constituent quark models. A non-relativistic reduction of the scheme is performed and applied to the Isgur-Karl model. The results obtained are consistent with a sizable Sivers effect and the signs for the u and d flavor contributions turn out to be opposite. The Burkardt Sum Rule is fulfilled to a large extent. After the estimate of the QCD evolution of the results from the momentum scale of the model to the experimental one, a reasonable agreement with the available data is obtained. A calculation of nuclear effects in the extraction of neutron single spin asymmetries in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering off 3He is also described. In the kinematics of forth-coming experiments at JLab, it is found that the nuclear effects arising within an Impulse Approximation approach are under control.

## Key claim

The authors present a constituent quark model (CQM) calculation of the Sivers function, finding that after QCD evolution from the model scale to the experimental scale, the results show reasonable agreement with available data, but they caution that the evolution procedure applied to the transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) Sivers function is not rigorously correct and should be taken only as an indication.

## Method

The formalism evaluates the valence quark contribution to the Sivers function using the Isgur-Karl constituent quark model, with leading-twist final-state interactions treated at leading order via one-gluon-exchange. A non-relativistic reduction of the relevant operator is performed, and the resulting Sivers function is computed from the spin-flavor wave function of the proton in momentum space. The model results are then evolved from the hadronic scale (μ₀² ≈ 0.1 GeV²) to the experimental scale using NLO evolution equations, assuming the same anomalous dimensions as for unpolarized parton distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- The hadronic scale of the model is set to μ₀² ≈ 0.1 GeV², yielding α_s(μ₀²)/(4π) ≈ 0.13, with Λ_QCD^(NLO) ≈ 0.24 GeV.
- At the model scale, the average transverse momentum for u quarks is ⟨k_x^u⟩ = 10.85 MeV and for d quarks is ⟨k_x^d⟩ = -11.25 MeV.
- The Burkardt sum rule is fulfilled to a precision of r ≈ 0.02, where r = (⟨k_x^d⟩ + ⟨k_x^u⟩) / (⟨k_x^d⟩ - ⟨k_x^u⟩).
- Nuclear effects in the extraction of neutron single spin asymmetries from ³He are found to be around 10-15% when using the simple dilution formula, but reduce to a few percent when using the effective polarization extraction scheme.

## Context

This work extends previous constituent quark model calculations of parton distributions (e.g., Traini et al. (1997)) and generalized parton distributions (e.g., Boffi et al. (2003)) to the TMD Sivers function, and it is motivated by the first SIDIS data from HERMES (2007) and COMPASS (2007) showing a strong flavor dependence of the Sivers effect. The calculation improves upon earlier model evaluations in quark-diquark models (Brodsky et al. (2002)) and the MIT bag model (Yuan (2003)), particularly in its fulfillment of the Burkardt sum rule. The nuclear part enables future JLab experiments (CEBAF) to extract neutron Sivers asymmetries from polarized ³He targets, with the proposed extraction scheme accounting for nuclear effects through effective nucleon polarizations.
