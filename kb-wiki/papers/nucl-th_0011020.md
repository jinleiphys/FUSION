# Microscopic systems with and without Coulomb interaction, fragmentation and phase transitions in finite nuclei

> We test the influence of the Coulomb interaction on the thermodynamic and cluster generation properties of a system of classical particles described by different lattice models. Numerical simulations show that the Coulomb interaction produces essentially a shift in temperature of quantities like the specific heat but not qualitative changes. We also consider a cellular model. The thermodynamic properties of the system are qualitatively unaltered.

## Key claim

The Coulomb interaction produces a systematic shift in temperature of thermodynamic quantities like the specific heat by about 1.5 MeV, but does not induce any qualitative changes in the thermodynamics or cluster size distributions of finite nuclear systems described by classical lattice models.

## Method

The authors performed Metropolis Monte Carlo simulations of classical particles on a cubic lattice using an Ising Model with Fixed Magnetization (IMFM), with short-range nearest-neighbor interactions (either undifferentiated with V=5 MeV or differentiated with V_np=5.33 MeV and V_nn=V_pp=0) and a long-range Coulomb term between protons. They also implemented a cellular model where particles occupy random positions within cells of volume d^3 and interact through a short-range two-body potential, without Coulomb. Both canonical and microcanonical ensemble calculations were performed for systems with fixed Z/A=0.4, lattice spacing d=1.8 fm, and open boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

- Temperature shift of about 1.5 MeV in the temperature corresponding to the maximum of specific heat C_V when Coulomb interaction is included, for both undifferentiated and differentiated potential cases
- Short-range interaction strengths: V_nn=V_pp=V_np=epsilon=5 MeV (undifferentiated case U) or V_nn=V_pp=0, V_np=epsilon_np=5.33 MeV (differentiated case D)
- Lattice spacing fixed to d=1.8 fm; Coulomb nearest-neighbor energy e^2/d approximately 0.8 MeV compared to epsilon approximately 5 MeV
- System size L=10 with density rho=0.3 for the caloric curve and specific heat calculations
- N/Z ratio drops from approximately 1.6 for A=3 to 1.2 for the largest masses in case D with Coulomb interaction

## Context

This work extends lattice model studies of nuclear fragmentation, such as those by Samaddar and Das Gupta (1999) and Campi (1986), by explicitly incorporating the long-range Coulomb interaction on the same footing as the short-range nuclear potential. The results confirm earlier findings by Biro (1999) regarding the temperature shift from Coulomb effects. The cellular model comparison shows that simpler lattice models are sufficient for schematic descriptions of nuclear fragmentation when systems are in thermodynamic equilibrium, though the authors note that quantum effects at lower temperatures remain an open question.
