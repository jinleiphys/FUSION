# The Dilute Bose-Einstein Condensate with Large Scattering Length

> We study a dilute Bose gas of atoms whose scattering length a is large compared to the range of their interaction. We calculate the energy density of the homogeneous Bose-Einstein condensate to second order in the low-density expansion, expressing it in terms of a and a second parameter Lambda_* that determines the low-energy observables in the 3-body sector. The second-order correction to the energy density has a small imaginary part that reflects the instability due to 3-body recombination. In the case of a trapped Bose-Einstein condensate with large negative scattering length, we calculate the coefficient of the 3-body mean-field term in the energy density in terms of a and Lambda_*. It can be very large if there is an Efimov state near threshold.

# The Dilute Bose-Einstein Condensate with Large Scattering Length

## Key claim
For a dilute Bose-Einstein condensate with scattering length large compared to the range of the interaction, the energy density to second order in the low-density expansion can be expressed in terms of the scattering length a and a single 3-body parameter Λ_*, but the second-order correction acquires a small imaginary part that signals instability from 3-body recombination; the real part of the coefficient can be very large if an Efimov state lies near threshold.

The constant B in the energy density expansion was extracted from the matching.

## Key numbers
- The coefficient of the √(na³) correction in the energy density is 128/(15√π), first calculated by Lee and Yang (1957).
- The constant s₀ = 1.0064 appears in the functional form of B for a < 0: B = b₁ + b₂ tan(s₀ ln(|a|Λ_*) + β), with b₁ = 1.23, b₂ = -3.16, and β = 0.19.
- For a > 0, the imaginary part of B is Im B = 0.022 cos²(s₀ ln(aΛ_*) + 1.76), and the real part is Re B = 1.22 + 0.021 cos²(s₀ ln(aΛ_*) + 1.0).
- The 3-body recombination rate vanishes at values of aΛ_* that differ by multiplicative factors of 22.7.
- The constant A_MS in the dimensional-regularization expression is -0.77268.

## Context
This work extends the low-density expansion for a homogeneous Bose-Einstein condensate, previously known to first order (Lee and Yang, 1957) and to the logarithmic part of the na³ correction (Wu, 1959; Braaten and Nieto, 1999), by determining the full constant B in terms of the 3-body parameter Λ_* introduced by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999). It connects the energy density to Efimov's discrete scaling symmetry (Efimov, 1970) and to the 3-body recombination rate calculations of Macek and Greene and of Bedaque, Braaten, and Hammer (2000). The results enable quantitative predictions for the stability of trapped condensates with large negative scattering length and raise open questions about whether a dilute BEC with na³ ≫ 1 has universal properties determined solely by a and Λ_*.
