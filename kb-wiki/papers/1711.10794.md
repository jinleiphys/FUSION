# Gravitational waves from isolated neutron stars: mass dependence of r-mode instability

> In this work we study the r-mode instability windows and the gravitational wave signatures of neutron stars in the slow rotation approximation using the equation of state obtained from the density dependent M3Y effective interaction. We consider the neutron star matter to be $β$-equilibrated neutron-proton-electron matter at the core with a rigid crust. The fiducial gravitational and viscous timescales, the critical frequencies and the time evolutions of the frequencies and the rates of frequency change are calculated for a range of neutron star masses. We show that the young and hot rotating neutron stars lie in the r-mode instability region. We also emphasize that if the dominant dissipative mechanism of the r-mode is the shear viscosity along the boundary layer of the crust-core interface, then the neutron stars with low $L$ value lie in the r-mode instability region and hence emit gravitational radiation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that young, hot rotating neutron stars lie in the r-mode instability region and that, if the dominant dissipative mechanism is shear viscosity along the crust-core boundary layer, neutron stars with a low value of the nuclear symmetry energy slope parameter \(L\) lie in the instability region and thus emit gravitational radiation; this conclusion is conditional on the specific equation of state derived from the density-dependent M3Y effective interaction and on the assumption of a rigid crust.

## Method

The authors used the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective nucleon-nucleon interaction to construct the equation of state for \(\beta\)-equilibrated neutron-proton-electron matter with a rigid crust, in the slow rotation approximation. They calculated the fiducial gravitational and shear-viscous timescales, the critical angular velocities, and the time evolution of the rotational frequency and its rate of change for a range of neutron star masses, considering only the \(l=2\) r-mode and shear viscosity (from neutron-neutron and electron-electron scattering) as the dominant dissipative mechanism for temperatures below \(10^{10}\) K.

## Key numbers

- The symmetric nuclear matter incompressibility \(K_\infty\) obtained from the DDM3Y interaction is \(274.7 \pm 7.4\) MeV.
- The nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density \(E_{\text{sym}}(\rho_0)\) is \(30.71 \pm 0.26\) MeV.
- The saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter used is \(\rho_0 = 0.1533\) fm\(^{-3}\) and the saturation energy per nucleon is \(\epsilon_0 = -15.26\) MeV.
- The isospin asymmetry \(X\) values for which the isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter incompressibility \(K_s\) is tabulated range from \(X=0.0\) (\(K_s = 274.69\) MeV) to \(X=0.7\) (\(K_s = 78.38\) MeV).
- The critical angular velocity \(\Omega_c\) for the \(l=2\) r-mode is given by \(\left(\frac{\Omega_c}{\Omega_0}\right) = \left(-\frac{\widetilde{\tau}_{GR}}{\widetilde{\tau}_{SV}}\right)^{2/11} \left(\frac{10^8 \text{ K}}{T}\right)^{2/11}\), where \(\Omega_0 = \sqrt{\pi G \bar{\rho}}\).
- The critical temperature below which gravitational radiation is completely suppressed by viscosity is given by \(\frac{T_c}{10^8 \text{ K}} \approx (3/2)^{11/2} \left(-\frac{\widetilde{\tau}_{GR}}{\widetilde{\tau}_{SV}}\right)\).
- The shear viscosity coefficients used are \(\eta_{SV}^{ee} = 6 \times 10^{6} \rho^{2} T^{-2}\) g cm\(^{-1}\) s\(^{-1}\) (electron-electron) and \(\eta_{SV}^{nn} = 347 \rho^{9/4} T^{-2}\) g cm\(^{-1}\) s\(^{-1}\) (neutron-neutron).

## Context

This work extends the study of r-mode instabilities by Moustakidis (2015) and the foundational formalism of Lindblom, Owen, and collaborators (1998, 2000), applying it specifically to the DDM3Y equation of state. The paper enables future comparisons of gravitational wave signatures from isolated neutron stars with observations from LIGO and VIRGO, potentially constraining the nuclear symmetry energy slope parameter \(L\) and the high-density equation of state. It also builds on the earlier estimate by Bildsten and Ushomirsky (2000) of the effect of a solid crust on r-mode damping.
