# rp-Process weak-interaction mediated rates of waiting-point nuclei

> Electron capture and positron decay rates are calculated for neutron-deficient Kr and Sr waiting point nuclei in stellar matter. The calculation is performed within the framework of pn-QRPA model for rp-process conditions. Fine tuning of particle-particle, particle-hole interaction parameters and a proper choice of the deformation parameter resulted in an accurate reproduction of the measured half-lives. The same model parameters were used to calculate stellar rates. Inclusion of measured Gamow-Teller strength distributions finally led to a reliable calculation of weak rates that reproduced the measured half-lives well under limiting conditions. For the rp-process conditions, electron capture and positron decay rates on $^{72}$Kr and $^{76}$Sr are of comparable magnitude whereas electron capture rates on $^{78}$Sr and $^{74}$Kr are 1--2 orders of magnitude bigger than the corresponding positron decay rates. The pn-QRPA calculated electron capture rates on $^{74}$Kr are bigger than previously calculated. The present calculation strongly suggests that, under rp-process conditions, electron capture rates form an integral part of weak-interaction mediated rates and should not be neglected in nuclear reaction network calculations as done previously.

## Key claim
Under rp-process conditions (T₉ = 1–3 K, ρYₑ = 10⁶–10⁷ g cm⁻³), electron capture rates on the waiting-point nuclei ⁷²Kr and ⁷⁶Sr are of comparable magnitude to the corresponding positron decay rates, while for ⁷⁸Sr and ⁷⁴Kr the electron capture rates are 1–2 orders of magnitude larger. The paper argues that electron capture should not be neglected in reaction network calculations, as was done previously.

## Method
The proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model was used to calculate Gamow-Teller strength distributions and weak-interaction rates for ⁷²,⁷⁴Kr and ⁷⁶,⁷⁸Sr. Single-particle energies and wave functions were obtained from the Nilsson model with pairing treated in the BCS approximation; particle-hole and particle-particle interaction parameters (χ and κ) were fine-tuned to reproduce measured terrestrial half-lives, and deformation parameters were taken from experimental data or mass formulas. Measured Gamow-Teller strength distributions from prior experiments were inserted where available, and all excited-state strength functions were calculated microscopically without assuming Brink’s hypothesis.

## Key numbers
- For ⁷²Kr at rp-process conditions, positron decay rates are bigger than electron capture rates by up to a factor of 23 at low T and ρ, but at ρYₑ = 10⁶–10⁷ g cm⁻³ the two rates become comparable.
- For ⁷⁴Kr under rp-process conditions, electron capture rates are 1–2 orders of magnitude bigger than positron decay rates.
- For ⁷⁶Sr at ρYₑ = 10⁶ g cm⁻³, positron decay rates are a factor 2–3 bigger; at ρYₑ = 10⁶.⁵ g cm⁻³ the rates are comparable; at ρYₑ = 10⁷ g cm⁻³ electron capture rates are a factor 4 bigger.
- For ⁷⁸Sr at ρYₑ = 10⁶ g cm⁻³ the two rates are equal; at ρYₑ = 10⁶.⁵–10⁷ g cm⁻³ electron capture rates are a factor 3–10 bigger.
- The pn-QRPA calculated electron capture rates on ⁷⁴Kr are a factor 3–7 bigger than those calculated by Sarriguren (2011) under rp-conditions.
- The particle-hole interaction parameter χ was set to 0.27 MeV for Kr isotopes and 0.36 MeV for Sr isotopes; the particle-particle parameter κ was 0.045 MeV for Kr and 0.044 MeV for Sr.
- The deformation parameter for ⁷²Kr was −0.2647 (oblate shape).

## Context
This work extends the pn-QRPA calculations of stellar weak-interaction rates previously performed by Nabi and Klapdor-Kleingrothaus (1999, 2004) for sd- and fp-shell nuclei, now applied specifically to neutron-deficient Kr and Sr waiting-point nuclei relevant to the rp-process in Type I X-ray bursts. The results are compared with the deformed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations of Sarriguren (2011), showing significant differences for ⁷⁴Kr. The paper confirms and strengthens the earlier conclusion by Sarriguren that electron capture rates must be included in rp-process network calculations, contrary to earlier practice (e.g., Schatz et al. 1998).
