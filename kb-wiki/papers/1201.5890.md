# A New Mechanism for Generating a Single Transverse Spin Asymmetry

> We propose a new mechanism for generating a single transverse spin asymmetry (STSA) in polarized proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions in the high-energy scattering approximation. In this framework the STSA originates from the q->q G splitting in the projectile (proton) light-cone wave function followed by a perturbative (C-odd) odderon interaction, together with a C-even interaction, between the projectile and the target. We show that some aspects of the obtained expression for the STSA of the produced quarks are in qualitative agreement with experiment: STSA decreases with decreasing projectile x_F and is a non-monotonic function of the transverse momentum k_T. In our framework the STSA peaks at k_T near the saturation scale Q_s. Our mechanism predicts that the quark STSA in proton-nucleus collisions should be much smaller than in proton-proton collisions. We also observe that in our formalism the STSA for prompt photons is zero.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new perturbative mechanism for generating a single transverse spin asymmetry (STSA) in high-energy polarized proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions, where the asymmetry arises from a $q \to q G$ splitting in the projectile's light-cone wave function combined with a $C$-odd (odderon) interaction and a $C$-even interaction with the target, yielding qualitative agreement with experimental trends but only for specific kinematic conditions (the STSA peaks at $k_T$ near the saturation scale $Q_s$ and decreases with decreasing $x_F$).

## Method

The authors calculate the light-cone wave function for a transversely polarized quark splitting into a quark and a gluon using light-cone perturbation theory (LCPT) in the $A^+ = 0$ gauge, then convolute the squared wave function with eikonal Wilson line interactions describing the quark and gluon scattering off the target's small-$x$ color fields within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) / saturation formalism. The spin-dependent part of the squared wave function is isolated and combined with the target interaction, requiring both a $C$-odd (odderon) and a $C$-even exchange to produce a non-zero asymmetry; the general expressions for quark, gluon, and photon STSA are derived, and a closed-form estimate is obtained using the Glauber-Mueller approximation.

## Key numbers

- The E581 and E704 collaborations at Fermilab reported STSA values of up to 30-40% for hadron production in transversely polarized proton-proton scattering at $\sqrt{s} = 200$ GeV.
- The STSA for produced quarks peaks at $k_T$ near the saturation scale $Q_s$ (no specific numerical value for $Q_s$ given in the text).
- The STSA is a non-monotonic function of transverse momentum $k_T$ and an increasing function of $x_F$, in qualitative agreement with experimental data shown for $0.7 \le k_T \le 2.0$ GeV/c (Fermilab data) and for pion $k_T$ (STAR data).
- The quark STSA in proton-nucleus collisions is predicted to be much smaller than in proton-proton collisions.
- The STSA for prompt photons is zero in this formalism.

## Context

This work extends the study of transverse spin asymmetries beyond the traditional collinear factorization mechanisms (Sivers effect, Collins effect, and higher-twist contributions) into the small-$x$ saturation/CGC regime, where multiple rescatterings are resummed. It builds on prior work on spin in the McLerran-Venugopalan model (Metz, 2011) and Mueller's dipole model (Dominguez, 2011), and on earlier studies of STSA in the CGC formalism (Boer, 2002; Boer, 2008; Kang, 2011). The mechanism enables a perturbative, interaction-based explanation for STSA that can be tested against existing RHIC data and future proton-nucleus measurements, and it suggests that the odderon (a $C$-odd exchange) plays a crucial role in generating the asymmetry.
