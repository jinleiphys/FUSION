# Chirality of $^{135}$Nd reexamined: Evidence for multiple chiral doublet bands

> One new pair of positive-parity chiral doublet bands have been identified in the odd-$A$ nucleus $^{135}$Nd which together with the previously reported negative-parity chiral doublet bands constitute a third case of multiple chiral doublet (M$χ$D) bands in the $A\approx130$ mass region. The properties of the M$χ$D bands are well reproduced by constrained covariant density functional theory and particle rotor model calculations. The newly observed M$χ$D bands in $^{135}$Nd represents an important milestone in supporting the existence of M$χ$D in nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper reports the identification of a new pair of positive-parity chiral doublet bands (D3 and D4) in the odd-A nucleus 135Nd, which together with the previously known negative-parity chiral doublet bands (D5 and D6) constitute a third case of multiple chiral doublet (MχD) bands in the A ≈ 130 mass region. The claim is hedged by the fact that the chiral interpretation relies on the similarity of the observed bands to those in the isotone 133Ce and on the agreement with constrained covariant density functional theory and particle rotor model calculations, rather than on a direct measurement of chirality.

## Method

High-spin states in 135Nd were populated using the 100Mo(40Ar,5n) reaction at a beam energy of 152 MeV provided by the K130 Cyclotron at the University of Jyväskylä, Finland. Emitted gamma rays were detected by the JUROGAM II array comprising 39 Compton suppressed Ge detectors. Spin and parity assignments were based on measured directional correlation of oriented states ratios (RDCO) and two-point angular correlation ratios (Rac), while linear polarization measurements using the IPDCO method established the electric character of key transitions and thus the parity of the bands. The experimental results were compared with constrained covariant density functional theory (CDFT) calculations for deformation parameters and particle rotor model (PRM) calculations for energy spectra and B(M1)/B(E2) ratios.

## Key numbers

- The four transitions of 830, 1015, 1161 and 1184 keV have IPDCO values of 0.043(8), 0.089(28), 0.008(2) and 0.018(5), respectively, establishing the positive parity of bands D2, D3 and D4.
- The energy separation between bands D3 and D4 is nearly constant at approximately 200 keV, supporting the chiral doublet bands interpretation.
- The single-particle alignment ix of bands D2-D4 is approximately 9 hbar, while that of band D5 is larger by 2-3 hbar and that of band D6 is larger than band D5 by approximately 2 hbar.
- The deformation parameters from CDFT for the positive-parity doublet bands D3 and D4 are (beta = 0.23, gamma = 21.0 degrees), with J0 = 28.0 hbar^2 MeV^{-1} and Coriolis attenuation factor xi = 0.94 used in PRM calculations.
- The deformation parameters from CDFT for the negative-parity bands D5 and D6 are (beta = 0.24, gamma = 22.2 degrees), with J0 = 23.5 hbar^2 MeV^{-1} and xi = 0.98 used in PRM calculations.

## Context

This work extends the study of multiple chiral doublet (MχD) bands in the A ≈ 130 mass region, following the identification of MχD bands in 133Ce by Ayangeakaa (2013) and in 136Nd by Petrache (2018). The observation of a second set of chiral doublet bands in 135Nd, in addition to the previously reported negative-parity chiral doublet bands by Zhu (2003), provides strong support for the existence of the MχD phenomenon in triaxial nuclei. The results enable further experimental studies such as lifetime measurements and searches for chiral doublets in other nuclei, as well as continued theoretical development of the chiral symmetry framework in nuclear structure.
