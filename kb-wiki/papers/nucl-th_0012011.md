# The d* dibaryon in the extended quark-delocalization, color-screening model

> The quark-delocalization, color-screening model, extended by inclusion of a one-pion-exchange (OPE) tail, is applied to the study of the deuteron and the d* dibaryon. The results show that the properties of the deuteron (an extended object) are well reproduced, greatly improving the agreement with experimental data as compared to our previous study (without OPE). At the same time, the mass and decay width of the d* (a compact object) are, as expected, not altered significantly.

## Key claim

Adding a one-pion-exchange (OPE) tail with a short-distance cutoff to the quark-delocalization, color-screening model (QDCSM) greatly improves the description of the deuteron (an extended object) but changes the mass and decay width of the compact d* dibaryon by only a few percent, as expected.

## Method

The authors extended the QDCSM by including a one-pion-exchange (OPE) potential with a step-function short-range cutoff at distance r0, applied to the six-quark Hamiltonian. They solved the resonating-group method (RGM) equation as an algebraic eigenvalue problem using delocalized single-particle quark wave functions and generator coordinates, with five coupled channels (NN S=1 L=0, ΔΔ S=1 L=0, ΔΔ S=3 L=2, NN S=1 L=2, ΔΔ S=1 L=2). The d* decay width into the NN D-wave state was computed using Fermi's Golden Rule with the tensor parts of one-gluon exchange and OPE as the interaction Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron mass: 1876 MeV for both cutoff values (r0 = 0.6 fm and r0 = 1.0 fm) and without OPE.
- Deuteron root-mean-square radius: 2.1 fm (r0 = 0.6 fm), 1.9 fm (r0 = 1.0 fm), compared to 1.5 fm without OPE.
- Deuteron D-wave probability (P_D): 5.2% (r0 = 0.6 fm), 4.5% (r0 = 1.0 fm), compared to 0.2% without OPE.
- d* mass: 2186 MeV (r0 = 0.6 fm), 2165 MeV (r0 = 1.0 fm), compared to 2116 MeV without OPE.
- d* root-mean-square radius: 1.3 fm for both cutoff values, compared to 1.2 fm without OPE.
- d* decay width: 7.92 MeV (r0 = 0.6 fm), 5.76 MeV (r0 = 1.0 fm), compared to 4.02 MeV without OPE.
- Quark mass parameter: 313 MeV for all cases.
- Quark wave function size parameter b: 0.6010 fm (r0 = 0.6 fm), 0.6021 fm (r0 = 1.0 fm), 0.6034 fm (without OPE).

## Context

This work extends the QDCSM approach previously applied to baryon-baryon interactions and dibaryons (Ping et al., 1999; Ping et al., 2000) by adding the long-range OPE tail that was missing in earlier versions, where the deuteron was poorly reproduced with too small a radius and D-wave mixing. The results enable future application to NN scattering, which the authors state is left to a later publication, and suggest that quark delocalization and color screening can replace the phenomenological short-range repulsive core and sigma-meson exchange of traditional meson-exchange models (Machleidt, 1989; Paris group, 1980).
