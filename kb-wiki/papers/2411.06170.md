# The effect of pressure anisotropy on quark stars structure in the Starobinsky model

> The structure and stability of quark stars (QSs) made of interacting quark matter are discussed in this study, taking color superconductivity and perturbative QCD corrections into account. By combining this EoS with the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations, we explore the mass-radius ($M-R$) relations of QSs. The analysis is conducted within the framework of $R^2$ gravity, where the gravity model is described by $f(R) = R + a R^2$. Our primary goal is to investigate how variations in the $R^2$ gravity parameter $a$ affect the mass-radius and mass-central density ($M-ρ_c$) relationships of QSs. Furthermore, we study the dynamical stability of these stars by analyzing the impact of anisotropy parameters $β$ and the interaction parameter $λ$ derived from the EoS, on their stability. Our results demonstrate that the presence of pressure anisotropy plays a significant role in increasing the maximum mass of QSs, with potential implications for the existence of super-massive pulsars. These findings are in agreement with recent astronomical observations, which suggest the possibility of neutron stars exceeding $2M_{\odot}$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that pressure anisotropy can significantly increase the maximum mass of quark stars in Starobinsky $R^2$ gravity, but the effect of the $R^2$ gravity parameter $a$ itself on mass-radius relations is almost negligible for the range $a = 5$ to $500$ km$^2$, contrary to what the abstract's emphasis on $a$ might suggest.

## Method

The authors solve the modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations in the Einstein frame of Starobinsky $f(R)=R+aR^2$ gravity, using a conformal transformation to introduce a scalar field. They employ an interacting quark matter equation of state that includes color superconductivity and perturbative QCD corrections, combined with a quasi-local anisotropy ansatz $\Delta = P_{\perp} - P_r = \beta P_r \zeta$, where $\zeta = 2m(r)/r$ is the compactness parameter. The system is solved numerically for various combinations of the $R^2$ parameter $a$, the interaction parameter $\bar{\lambda}$, and the anisotropy parameter $\beta$.

## Key numbers

* Maximum mass of quark stars increases from $2.239\,M_\odot$ to $2.545\,M_\odot$ as the anisotropy parameter $\beta$ increases from $-0.35$ to $0.35$ (with $B=60$ MeV/fm$^3$, $\bar{\lambda}=0.2$, $a=50.0$ km$^2$)
* Maximum mass increases from $2.301\,M_\odot$ to $2.545\,M_\odot$ as the interaction parameter $\bar{\lambda}$ increases from $0.05$ to $0.20$ (with $\beta=0.35$, $B=60$ MeV/fm$^3$, $a=50.0$ km$^2$)
* For varying $a$ from $5$ to $500$ km$^2$, the maximum mass changes only from $2.247\,M_\odot$ to $2.237\,M_\odot$ (with $\beta=-0.35$, $\bar{\lambda}=0.2$, $B=60$ MeV/fm$^3$)
* Corresponding radii range from $11.871$ km to $12.834$ km across all parameter variations
* Compactness parameter $M/R$ ranges from $0.266$ to $0.294$, remaining below the Buchdahl limit of $4/9$

## Context

This work extends studies of compact stars in $f(R)$ theories by Astashenok (2020, 2021) and Tangphati (2024) by specifically incorporating pressure anisotropy into quark star models within the Starobinsky framework. The results are compared with observational constraints from NICER measurements of pulsars PSR J0030+0451 (Riley 2019) and PSR J0740+6620 (Miller 2021), as well as gravitational wave events GW170817 and GW190814. The findings suggest that anisotropic quark stars in $R^2$ gravity can reach masses exceeding $2.5\,M_\odot$, potentially explaining super-massive pulsar observations, while the $R^2$ correction itself has minimal effect on mass-radius relations, indicating that tidal deformability measurements may be needed to distinguish between different values of $a$.
