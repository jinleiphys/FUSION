# Anomalous-magnetic-moment-enhanced Casimir effect

> We theoretically investigate the impact of the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of Dirac fermions on the fermionic Casimir effect under magnetic fields. We formulate it as an extension of the well-known Lifshitz formula. From our formula, we find that the AMM increases the fermionic Casimir energy. In particular, when the AMM is large enough, the Casimir energy is significantly enhanced by the gapless behavior of the lowest Landau level. We also quantitatively estimate the Casimir energy from electron, muon, and constituent quark fields under magnetic fields and propose possible phenomena at finite temperature and fermion density.

## Key claim

The authors theoretically show that the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of Dirac fermions enhances the fermionic Casimir energy under magnetic fields, with a particularly significant enhancement occurring when the AMM is large enough to make the lowest Landau level (LLL) gapless; this enhancement is conditional on the AMM parameter \(\kappa\) approaching or exceeding the critical value \(\kappa^{\text{cri}(0)} = m/qB\).

## Method

The authors formulate the fermionic Casimir effect with AMM as an extension of the Lifshitz formula, starting from the Dirac Lagrangian with an AMM term \(\frac{\kappa q}{2} \sigma^{\mu\nu} F_{\mu\nu}\). They derive the dispersion relations for Dirac fermions under a uniform magnetic field, including the AMM-modified Landau levels, and compute the zero-point energy with periodic boundary conditions in the \(z\)-direction. The Casimir energy is obtained via the Lifshitz formula, integrating over imaginary frequencies and summing over Landau levels and spin states.

## Key numbers

- For electrons: \(\kappa_e = 0.00113469\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\) (derived from \(a_e = 0.00115965218062\) and \(m_e = 0.51099895000\ \mathrm{MeV}\)); at \(qB = m_e^2\) and \(L_z = 100\ \mathrm{fm}\), \(\Delta E_\mathrm{Cas} \sim 2.3 \times 10^{-10}\ \mathrm{MeV/fm}^2\); at the critical field \(qB^\mathrm{cri} \sim 450\ \mathrm{MeV}^2\) and \(L_z = 100\ \mathrm{fm}\), \(\Delta E_\mathrm{Cas} \sim 7.9 \times 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{MeV/fm}^2\).
- For muons: \(\kappa_\mu = 5.51741 \times 10^{-6}\ \mathrm{MeV}^{-1}\) (derived from \(a_\mu = 0.00116592059\) and \(m_\mu = 105.6583755\ \mathrm{MeV}\)); at \(qB = m_\mu^2\) and \(L_z = 1\ \mathrm{fm}\), \(\Delta E_\mathrm{Cas} \sim 1.57 \times 10^{-3}\ \mathrm{MeV/fm}^2\); at the critical field \(qB^\mathrm{cri} \sim 19\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) and \(L_z = 1\ \mathrm{fm}\), \(\Delta E_\mathrm{Cas} \sim 6100\ \mathrm{MeV/fm}^2\).
- For constituent quarks: \(\kappa_u = 0.00952827\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\) and \(\kappa_d = 0.0831157\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\) (from baryon mass and magnetic moment fits); the \(d\)-quark Casimir energy enhancement is larger than that of the \(u\)-quark due to its larger AMM.
- The critical AMM for the LLL is \(\kappa^{\text{cri}(0)} = m/qB\), and for the \(l\)-th Landau level it is \(\kappa^{\text{cri}(l)} = \sqrt{m^2 + 2l|qB|}/qB\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the fermionic Casimir effect under magnetic fields (e.g., Cougo-Pinto (1998), Sitenko (2014), Nakayama (2022), Fujii (2024)) by incorporating the previously neglected anomalous magnetic moment. It provides a theoretical foundation for future lattice QED and QCD simulations of the Casimir effect, and enables quantitative estimates for systems such as thin films of Dirac/Weyl semimetals (Nakayama (2022)) and thin quark matter (Fujii (2024)). The results also connect to studies of the QCD phase diagram in magnetic fields using NJL-type models with AMM (e.g., Fayazbakhsh (2014), Chaudhuri (2019), Kawaguchi (2022)).
