# Effects of Many-body Interactions in Hypernuclei with Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU Functionals

> We investigate the properties of $螞$ hyperon in $螞$-hypernuclei using an effective nuclear density functional theory which is based on the low-energy effective field theory. It expands the energy density in the power of Fermi momentum, and consequently has multiple density dependence for the effective many-body interactions. Starting from the effective density functional for nucleons, we determine the parameters for the two- and many-body $螞$-$N$ interactions added to the nucleon energy density functional by fitting to $螞$-hypernuclear data. The experimental data consist of the energy levels of a $螞$ hyperon in the $p$-, and $d$-states as well as $s$-state of $螞$-hypernuclei in the mass range from $_螞^{16}$O to $_螞^{208}$Pb. The results turn out to properly explain the data relevant to hypernuclei owing to the effective many-body interaction apart from a few data in light hypernuclei. This hyperon functional is applied to study the $螞$ hyperon binding energy of the neutron-rich $^{124-136}_螞$Sn isotopes which are under consideration for the measurement at J-PARC. Our results are shown to be insensitive to the density dependence of symmetry energy. We also examine the nuclear matter including $螞$ hyperon. We note that the hyperon threshold density depends on the nuclear matter properties.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that expanding the $\Lambda$-nucleon energy density functional in powers of Fermi momentum (multiple density-dependent terms) systematically improves the description of $\Lambda$ hyperon binding energies in hypernuclei, but the model still underestimates experimental data for light hypernuclei (below $^{16}_\Lambda$O) even with the optimal number of terms.

## Method

The authors used the KIDS (Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU) density functional formalism, which expands the energy density in powers of the Fermi momentum (equivalent to $\rho^{1/3}$), to construct $\Lambda$-nucleon interactions. They fitted parameters for two-body and many-body ($\Lambda NN$) interactions to experimental $\Lambda$ binding energies in $s$-, $p$-, and $d$-states for hypernuclei from $^{16}_\Lambda$O to $^{208}_\Lambda$Pb, determining the optimal number of density-dependent terms (four, labeled Y4) by monitoring the mean deviation (MD) from experimental data. The resulting models were then applied to predict binding energies in light hypernuclei and neutron-rich $^{124-136}_\Lambda$Sn isotopes, and extended to study $\Lambda$ hyperon fractions in $\beta$-equilibrium neutron star matter.

## Key numbers

- Mean deviation (MD) values for the Y4 model: 0.1844 (KIDS0), 0.2096 (KIDS-A), 0.2229 (KIDS-B), 0.2085 (KIDS-C), 0.2093 (KIDS-D)
- $\Lambda$-$N$ potential depth at saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$ is about $-30$ MeV, consistent with empirical optical potential
- Maximum difference in $\Lambda$ binding energy predictions for Sn isotopes between models is 0.18 MeV (at $A=126$ between KIDS-B and KIDS-D), about 0.7% of the binding energy
- Pairing effects change $\Lambda$ binding energies in Sn isotopes by within 0.35 MeV (less than 1.5% of binding energy)
- Critical density for $\Lambda$ onset in neutron star matter: 2.8$\rho_0$ (SLy4), 4.4$\rho_0$ (KIDS0), 3.5$\rho_0$ (KIDS-B), 3.3$\rho_0$ (KIDS-C), 3.2$\rho_0$ (KIDS-D); no $\Lambda$ creation predicted for KIDS-A

## Context

This work extends the KIDS density functional approach, previously applied to nucleonic systems (KIDS0, KIDS-A through KIDS-D models with different symmetry energy parameters), to include $\Lambda$ hyperons. It improves upon standard Skyrme force models with single density-dependent terms (such as SLy4) used in prior hypernuclear studies by Guleria (2012) and Hiyama (2014), which showed discrepancies for either light or heavy hypernuclei. The results enable systematic predictions for $\Lambda$ binding energies in neutron-rich Sn isotopes planned for measurement at J-PARC, and provide constraints on the hyperon puzzle in neutron star cores by showing that the hyperon threshold density depends strongly on nuclear matter properties.
