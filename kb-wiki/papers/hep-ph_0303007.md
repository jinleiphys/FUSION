# The nonperturbative origin of delta-function singularity in the chirally-odd twist-3 distribution function e(x)

> We analytically prove that the existence of the delta-function singularity in the chirally-odd twist-3 distribution $e(x)$ of the nucleon is inseparably connected with the nonvanishing quark condensate as a signal of the spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking of the QCD vacuum. This singularity in $e(x)$, which would be observed as a sizable violation of the 1st moment sum rule, is then interpreted as giving a very rare case that the nontrivial vacuum structure of QCD manifests in an observable of a localized QCD excitation, i.e. the nucleon.

## Key claim

The delta-function singularity at x=0 in the chirally-odd twist-3 distribution e(x) of the nucleon originates from the nonvanishing quark condensate of the QCD vacuum, but the proof is given within the chiral quark soliton model (CQSM) and the authors acknowledge that the literal result e(x) = sigma-bar * delta(x) is misleading because it comes from an approximate derivative expansion that neglects nonzero contributions at other x values.

## Method

The authors start from the general definition of e(x) as a Fourier transform of a nucleon matrix element of quark bilocal operators, then evaluate it within the CQSM using the leading contribution in the 1/N_c expansion. They separate the vacuum contribution E_vac(z_0) from the full soliton contribution E_U(z_0), apply Pauli-Villars regularization with two subtraction terms to eliminate quadratic and logarithmic divergences, and use a derivative expansion of the quark Green's functions to extract the constant term that survives in the z_0 to infinity limit.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum contribution E_vac^{reg}(z_0) equals V * <psi-bar psi>_{vac}, where V is the total volume and <psi-bar psi>_{vac} is the vacuum quark condensate.
- The regularized E(z_0) equals the nucleon scalar charge sigma-bar, which is related to the pi-N Sigma-term by Sigma_{pi N} = m_0 * sigma-bar.
- The first moment sum rule gives integral_{-1}^{1} e(x) dx = sigma-bar = Sigma_{pi N} / m_0.
- The Pauli-Villars regularization conditions are: 1 - sum_i c_i (Lambda_i/M)^2 = 0 and 1 - sum_i c_i (Lambda_i/M)^4 = 0.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD finding of Burkardt and Koike (2002) that a delta-function singularity at x=0 in e(x) is needed to satisfy the Sigma-term sum rule, and addresses the conjecture of Efremov et al. (2003) that the singularity originates from the vacuum quark condensate. The authors provide the first explicit analytical proof within the CQSM framework, building on the model's previous applications to nucleon structure by Diakonov et al. (1996, 1997) and Wakamatsu and Kubota (1999). The result enables future numerical calculations of the full x-dependence of e(x) beyond the derivative expansion approximation, and raises the question of whether similar delta-function singularities appear in other distribution functions such as h_L(x).
