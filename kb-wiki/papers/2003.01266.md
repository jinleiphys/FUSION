# Chemical freeze-out parameters of net-kaons in heavy-ion collisions

> We study chemical freeze-out parameters for heavy-ion collisions by performing two different thermal analyses. We analyze results from thermal fits for particle yields, as well as, net-charge fluctuations in order to characterize the chemical freeze-out. The Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model is employed for both methods. By separating the light hadrons from the strange hadrons in thermal fits, we study the proposed flavor hierarchy. For the net-charge fluctuations, we calculate the mean-over-variance ratio of the net-kaon fluctuations in the HRG model at the five highest energies of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan (BES) for different particle data lists. We compare these results with recent experimental data from the STAR collaboration in order to extract sets of chemical freeze-out parameters for each list. We focused on particle lists which differ largely in the number of resonant states. By doing so, our analysis determines the effect of the amount of resonances included in the HRG model on the freeze-out conditions. Our findings have potential impact on various other models in the field of relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper finds evidence for two separate chemical freeze-out temperatures in heavy-ion collisions for light and strange particles, but notes that including more (strange) resonances in the Hadron Resonance Gas model is not enough to close the separation between strange and non-strange particles.

## Method

The authors performed thermal fits of particle yields using the Thermal FIST package with the ideal Hadron Resonance Gas model in the Grand Canonical Ensemble, analyzing STAR data for AuAu collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV at 0-5% centrality. Separately, they calculated net-kaon fluctuations (mean-over-variance ratio) in the HRG model at the five highest energies of the RHIC Beam Energy Scan and compared these with STAR experimental data to extract chemical freeze-out parameters. Both analyses were performed with different particle data lists, focusing on the PDG2016+ list versus older lists with fewer resonant states.

## Key numbers

* Single freeze-out temperature: T = 156.4 +/- 2.2 MeV with mu_B = 21.1 +/- 6.1 MeV and chi^2/d.o.f. = 14.5/9 for AuAu collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, 0-5% centrality
* Light hadron freeze-out temperature: T = 152.5 +/- 4.9 MeV with mu_B = 22.4 +/- 13.9 MeV and chi^2/d.o.f. = 2.9/3
* Strange hadron freeze-out temperature: T = 158.4 +/- 3.1 MeV with mu_B = 20.3 +/- 7.8 MeV and chi^2/d.o.f. = 12.7/5
* Single freeze-out volume: 2248 +/- 304 fm^3; light hadron volume: 2631 +/- 654 fm^3; strange hadron volume: 1981 +/- 403 fm^3

## Context

This work extends the flavor hierarchy proposal of Bellwied (2013) by using both thermal fits and net-charge fluctuations to study separate freeze-out temperatures for light and strange hadrons. The analysis builds on the fluctuation-based freeze-out determination method of Alba (2014) and applies it with the updated PDG2016+ particle list. The results enable future investigations into the remaining tension with strange baryons and provide constraints for other models in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly within the Beam Energy Scan Theory collaboration framework.
