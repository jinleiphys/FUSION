# Explaining Angular Correlations Observed at RHIC with Flow and Local Charge Conservation

> The observation of fluctuations of parity-odd angular observables at RHIC has been interpreted as a signal of a local parity violation. We show how the observed signal can be explained by local charge conservation at freeze-out combined with elliptic flow. Calculations from a blast wave model, which overlays thermal emission onto a collective flow profile, are shown to account for the experimentally observed signal.

# Explaining Angular Correlations Observed at RHIC with Flow and Local Charge Conservation

## Key claim
Local charge conservation at freeze-out combined with elliptic flow can explain the observed positive values of the parity-odd angular correlation observable $\gamma_P$ measured at RHIC, without requiring a local parity violation or the chiral magnetic effect. The blast wave model calculations overpredict the signal for peripheral collisions, suggesting the assumption of perfectly localized charge emission is strongest for central collisions and less exact for peripheral events.

## Method
The authors modified a thermal blast wave model to incorporate local charge conservation by generating arrays of particles from a canonical ensemble with fixed volume (64 fm³) and chemical temperature $T_{\rm chem}=175$ MeV, where electric charge, strangeness, and baryon number sum to zero. Particles were assigned momenta according to the kinetic breakup temperature and collective flow parameters fitted to STAR data for elliptic flow $v_2$ and spectra, with balancing charges constrained to originate from the same source velocity. The parity observable $\gamma_P$ was then expressed in terms of moments of the charge balance function $B(\phi,\Delta\phi)$, decomposed into three contributions: having more balancing pairs in-plane than out-of-plane ($v_2\langle c_b\rangle$), in-plane pairs being more tightly correlated in $\Delta\phi$ ($v_{2c}$), and balancing charges preferentially emitted toward the event plane ($v_{2s}$).

## Key numbers
- The blast wave model reproduces STAR data for central collisions, but for more peripheral collisions (40-50% centrality) the model produces higher correlations than experimentally observed.
- The chemical temperature used in the canonical ensemble was $T_{\rm chem}=175$ MeV.
- The fixed volume for particle generation was 64 fm³.
- The three contributions to $\gamma_P$ are: $v_2\langle c_b(\phi)\rangle$ (more balancing pairs in-plane), $v_{2c}\equiv\langle c_b(\phi)\cos(2\phi)\rangle-v_2\langle c_b(\phi)\rangle$ (tighter in-plane correlations), and $v_{2s}\equiv\langle s_b(\phi)\sin(2\phi)\rangle$ (balancing charge toward event plane).
- The balance function normalization $z_b(\phi)$ represents the probability of detecting the balancing charge given observation of a charge at $\phi$, and would be unity for a perfect detector.

## Context
This work extends the suggestion by Scott Pratt (2010) that local charge conservation combined with elliptic flow might explain a major part of the observed signal, and provides a quantitative blast wave calculation to test this mechanism. The results contradict the interpretation of the STAR collaboration's parity-odd fluctuations as evidence for the chiral magnetic effect proposed by Kharzeev, and instead show that the signal can be explained by conventional physics. The calculations enable future work using more detailed models of collision dynamics and decoupling to independently describe both same-sign and opposite-sign correlations, and to reproduce differential correlations such as charge balance functions alongside the integrated $\gamma_{\alpha\beta}$ observables.
