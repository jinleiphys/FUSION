# Numerical study of renormalization group flows of nuclear effective field theory without pions on a lattice

> We formulate the next-to-leading order nuclear effective field theory without pions in the two-nucleon sector on a spatial lattice, and investigate nonperturbative renormalization group flows in the strong coupling region by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian numerically. The cutoff (proportional to the inverse of the lattice constant) dependence of the coupling constants is obtained by changing the lattice constant with the binding energy and the asymptotic normalization constant for the groundstate being fixed. We argue that the critical line can be obtained by looking at the finite-size dependence of the groundstate energy. We determine the relevant operator and locate the nontrivial fixed point, as well as the physical flow line corresponding to the deuteron in the two-dimensional plane of dimensionless coupling constants. It turns out that the location of the nontrivial fixed point is very close to the one obtained by the corresponding analytic calculation, but the relevant operator is quite different.

## Key claim

The authors numerically determine the location of the nontrivial fixed point and the relevant operator for nuclear effective field theory without pions defined on a lattice, finding that while the fixed point location is very close to the analytic calculation, the relevant operator is quite different from the analytic result.

## Method

The authors formulate next-to-leading order (NLO) nuclear effective field theory without pions on a spatial cubic lattice with periodic boundary conditions, then diagonalize the Hamiltonian numerically to investigate nonperturbative renormalization group flows in the strong coupling region. They fix the binding energy and asymptotic normalization constant (ANC) for the ground state as the lattice constant is varied, and determine the critical line by examining the finite-size dependence of the groundstate energy. The discretization of derivatives uses both three-point and five-point finite difference formulas to reduce rotational symmetry breaking effects.

## Key numbers

- The nontrivial fixed point in the continuum sharp-cutoff regularization is located at (X_star, Y_star) = (-0.717691..., -0.401924...)
- With lattice regularization using the three-point formula, the nontrivial fixed point shifts to (X_star, Y_star) = (-0.76602..., 0.17501...)
- With lattice regularization using the five-point formula, the nontrivial fixed point is at (X_star, Y_star) = (-0.63338..., -0.098805...)
- The binding energy and ANC vanish at a cutoff of approximately 57.2 MeV, corresponding to a length scale of 3.4 fm
- The ANC obtained in the NLO NEFT is 0.244 fm^{-1/2}, close to the recommended value of 0.2495(2) fm^{-1/2} from de Swart (1995)

## Context

This work extends the analytic approach of Seki and van Kolck (2005), who studied NEFT without pions with lattice regularization but did not produce a genuine lattice result due to their use of continuum partial-wave equations. The numerical diagonalization method provides the first determination of the nontrivial fixed point location and RG flow for NEFT genuinely defined on a lattice, which is an important step toward chirally-symmetric NEFT with pions on a lattice as pursued by Harada, Sasabe, and Yahiro. The results enable future lattice simulations to determine input parameters by identifying which flow line corresponds to the physical deuteron system.
