# Effective Field Theories and Lattice for Hard Probes

> Effective Quantum Field Theories and QCD Lattice methods have become more and more complementary and mutually supportive in the study of Hard Probes. I present some of the progress that this alliance already delivered and I discuss future opportunities.

## Key claim

Effective Field Theories (EFTs) and QCD lattice methods have become complementary and mutually supportive in studying hard probes, but the paper emphasizes that this alliance works specifically when the EFT factorizes out high-energy scales, leaving low-energy nonperturbative contributions that can be evaluated on the lattice in terms of gauge-invariant, purely gluonic objects.

## Method

The paper reviews the combination of several EFTs, including pNRQCD for heavy quarks, NRQCD, SCET for jets, and HTL/EQCD/MQCD for the hot medium, with lattice QCD calculations. For quarkonium in medium, the author describes how pNRQCD at finite temperature defines the in-medium potential (with both real and imaginary parts) and how an open quantum system (OQS) framework rooted in pNRQCD yields Lindblad master equations for the nonequilibrium evolution of color singlet and octet states. The transport coefficients κ (heavy quark momentum diffusion) and γ (its dispersive counterpart) are extracted from lattice data on thermal widths and mass shifts of quarkonium states, and κ is also computed directly from chromoelectric field correlators on the lattice in pure SU(3) gauge theory.

## Key numbers

- Crossover temperature to the quark-gluon plasma: 153 +6.5 −5 MeV, extracted from the entropy of the Polyakov loop.
- Screening sets in at rT ∼ 0.3–0.4 (observable dependent), consistent with a screening length of about 1/m_D.
- Asymptotic screening masses are about 2m_D (observable dependent).
- The thermal width of the Υ(1S) goes linear in T in the dominant term, confirmed by lattice calculations of the spectrum.
- The heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient κ/T^3 was computed directly on the lattice in pure SU(3) gauge theory over a temperature range 1.1 < T/T_c < 10^4.
- The first non-perturbative determination of γ/T^3 was obtained from lattice data for the thermal mass shift of J/ψ and Υ(1S) at two different temperatures.
- The Polyakov loop was computed up to order g^6; the subtracted Q̅Q free energy at short distances up to corrections of order g^7(rT)^4, g^8; the singlet free energy at short distances up to corrections of order g^4(rT)^5, g^6.

## Context

This work extends the program of combining EFTs with lattice QCD for hard probes, building on prior developments in pNRQCD at finite temperature (Brambilla 2008, 2010, 2013, 2017) and on lattice calculations of the static potential and Polyakov loops (Bazavov 2016, 2018; Rothkopf 2011, 2019). The paper enables future calculations of quarkonium suppression observables like R_AA and v_2 by providing a framework where lattice input from equilibrium QCD can be used to describe nonequilibrium evolution in the quark-gluon plasma, and it opens the door to analogous EFT/lattice treatments of jet quenching via the parameter q̂.
