# Bayesian analysis of quark spectral properties from the Dyson-Schwinger equation

> We report results on the quark spectral function in the Landau gauge at finite temperature determined from its Dyson-Schwinger equation. Compared to earlier quenched results this study encompasses unquenched $N_f=2+1$ fermion flavors in the medium. For the reconstruction of real-time spectra we deploy a recent Bayesian approach (BR method) and develop a new prior in order to better assess the inherent systematic uncertainties. We identify the quark quasi-particle spectrum and analyze the (non-)appearance of zero modes at or around the pseudo-critical temperature. In both, the fully unquenched system and a simpler truncation using a model for the gluon propagator we observe a characteristic three-peak structure at zero three-momentum. The temperature dependence of these structures in case of the gluon propagator model is different than observed in previous studies. For the back-coupled and unquenched case we find interesting modifications at and around the pseudo-critical transition temperature.

## Key claim

The authors report that the quark spectral function in Landau gauge at finite temperature exhibits a characteristic three-peak structure at zero three-momentum in both the fully unquenched system with N_f=2+1 flavors and a simpler model truncation, but caution that the temperature dependence of these structures in the model case differs from previous studies, and that the back-coupled unquenched case shows modifications at and around the pseudo-critical transition temperature.

## Method

The quark propagator at finite temperature was determined from its Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) in Landau gauge using the Matsubara formalism, with two truncation schemes: a model gluon propagator with a bare quark-gluon vertex, and a fully unquenched truncation with N_f=2+1 flavors where the gluon propagator includes explicit quark-loop contributions. The real-time spectral function component ρ_4 was reconstructed from the Euclidean imaginary-frequency correlator using a Bayesian approach (BR method), and a new "low-ringing" prior functional was developed to suppress numerical ringing artifacts and distinguish genuine peak structures.

## Key numbers

- The model gluon parameters include σ=0.5 GeV, σD = (0.8 GeV)^3, Λ_QCD=0.234 GeV, m_t=0.5 GeV, γ_m=12/25, and τ=e^2-1.
- Mock data tests used N_data=128 discretely spaced imaginary frequencies between ω_p ∈ [0,8] GeV, with relative errors ΔD/D ranging from 10^{-3} (realistic for DSE computations) down to 10^{-8}.
- For the original BR method with ΔD/D ≤ 10^{-3}, the peak position of a second peak at ω=2 GeV was accurate within 15%.
- The smooth BR method unambiguously identified two peaked features in a two-peak mock scenario with one structure at the origin and one at ω=2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier quenched DSE studies by Mueller (2010) to include unquenched N_f=2+1 fermion flavors, and compares with previous model truncation results from Qin (2010, 2013) and Gao (2014) that identified a zero mode in the spectral function. The Bayesian reconstruction method (BR method) developed by Burnier (2013) is deployed and improved with a new prior to assess systematic uncertainties. The unquenched gluon propagator used here was shown by Fischer (2012) to agree with lattice results from Aouane (2012), and the temperature dependence of the chiral condensate reproduces lattice results from Borsanyi (2010). The results enable more reliable extraction of quark spectral properties near the chiral phase transition, relevant for understanding dilepton production and transport coefficients in the quark-gluon plasma.
