# Enhancement of baryon-to-meson ratios around jets as a signature of medium response

> We present a unique signal of jet-induced medium excitations: the enhancement of baryon-to-meson ratios around the quenched jets.To illustrate this, we study jet-particle correlations and the distributions of jet-induced identified particles with respect to the jet direction in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC via a multi-phase transport model.We find a strong enhancement of baryon-to-meson ratios for associated particles at intermediate transverse momentum around the triggered jets in Pb+Pb collisions relative to p+p collisions, due to the coalescence of jet-excited medium partons.Since the lost energy from jets can diffuse to large angles, such baryon-to-meson-ratio enhancement is more pronounced for larger relative distance from the jet axis.We argue that the experimental confirmation of the enhancement of jet-induced baryon-to-meson ratios around the jets will provide an unambiguous evidence for the medium response to jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions.

# Enhancement of baryon-to-meson ratios around jets as a signature of medium response

## Key claim
The enhancement of baryon-to-meson ratios (p/π and Λ/K) for associated particles at intermediate transverse momentum (2–6 GeV) around quenched jets in Pb+Pb collisions relative to p+p collisions provides an unambiguous signature of jet-induced medium excitations, arising from the coalescence of medium partons excited by the lost energy from jets. This enhancement is more pronounced for larger relative distance from the jet axis and in more central collisions, but the authors note the AMPT model does not include radiative processes, so the illustration is only qualitative.

## Method
The authors used the string-melting version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model with a partonic cross section of 1.5 mb to simulate Pb+Pb and p+p collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV at the LHC. They applied a jet-particle correlation method with mixed-event and side-band background subtraction to compute the (Δη, Δφ) distributions of identified pions, kaons, protons, and lambdas around triggered jets (pjetT > 120 GeV, R = 0.4, |ηjet| < 1.6), then extracted the p/π and Λ/K ratios as functions of associated particle pT and radial distance Δr from the jet axis.

## Key numbers
- Jet trigger threshold: pjetT > 120 GeV with jet radius R = 0.4 and |ηjet| < 1.6
- Associated particles selected with |η| < 2.4 and pT bins bounded by 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12 GeV
- Partonic cross section set to 1.5 mb in the AMPT model
- Baryon-to-meson ratio enhancement observed for associated particles at intermediate pT = 2–6 GeV
- Radial distribution constructed in annular rings of width 0.1 for Δr, with the last bin combining Δr = 0.6–1
- Enhancement of p/π and Λ/K ratios around jets shown for four centrality classes: 0–10%, 10–30%, 30–50%, and 50–100%

## Context
This work extends previous studies of jet-induced medium response, including the CMS measurements of low-pT particle enhancement around quenched jets (CMS 2016, CMS 2018, CMS 2021) and the theoretical finding by Tachibana (2017) that jet-induced medium flow can dominate jet shape at large radius. The authors build on the established coalescence explanation for baryon-to-meson enhancement in bulk matter (Fries 2003, Molnar 2003, Greco 2003) and apply it to the jet vicinity. The proposed observable can be tested experimentally using the same jet-particle correlation method already employed by CMS, and the authors suggest future application to proton-nucleus collisions as a probe of jet quenching in small systems.
