# Molecular components in P-wave charmed-strange mesons

> Results obtained by various experiments show that the $D_{s0}^{\ast}(2317)$ and $D_{s1}(2460)$ mesons are very narrow states located below the $DK$ and $D^{\ast}K$ thresholds, respectively. This is markedly in contrast with the expectations of naive quark models and heavy quark symmetry. Motivated by a recent lattice study which addresses the mass shifts of the $c\bar{s}$ ground states with quantum numbers $J^{P}=0^{+}$ ($D_{s0}^{\ast}(2317)$) and $J^{P}=1^{+}$ ($D_{s1}(2460)$) due to their coupling with $S$-wave $D^{(\ast)}K$ thresholds, we perform a similar analysis within a nonrelativistic constituent quark model in which quark-antiquark and meson-meson degrees of freedom are incorporated. The quark model has been applied to a wide range of hadronic observables and thus the model parameters are completely constrained. The coupling between quark-antiquark and meson-meson Fock components is done using a $^{3}P_{0}$ model in which its only free parameter $γ$ has been elucidated performing a global fit to the decay widths of mesons that belong to different quark sectors, from light to heavy. We observe that the coupling of the $0^{+}$ $(1^{+})$ meson sector to the $DK$ $(D^{\ast}K)$ threshold is the key feature to simultaneously lower the masses of the corresponding $D_{s0}^{\ast}(2317)$ and $D_{s1}(2460)$ states predicted by the naive quark model and describe the $D_{s1}(2536)$ meson as the $1^{+}$ state of the $j_{q}^{P}=3/2^{+}$ doublet predicted by heavy quark symmetry, reproducing its strong decay properties. Our calculation allows to introduce the coupling with the $D$-wave $D^{\ast}K$ channel and the computation of the probabilities associated with the different Fock components of the physical state.

## Key claim

The coupling of the $0^{+}$ ($1^{+}$) $c\bar{s}$ state to the $DK$ ($D^{\ast}K$) threshold is the key feature to simultaneously lower the masses of the $D_{s0}^{\ast}(2317)$ and $D_{s1}(2460)$ states predicted by the naive quark model and describe the $D_{s1}(2536)$ meson as the $1^{+}$ state of the $j_{q}^{P}=3/2^{+}$ doublet, but the resulting probabilities of the different Fock components are model-dependent and contrast with the analysis of lattice data by Torres (2014).

## Method

The authors used a nonrelativistic constituent quark model that incorporates both quark-antiquark and meson-meson degrees of freedom. The coupling between these sectors was performed using a $^{3}P_{0}$ model, with its single free parameter $\gamma$ fixed by a global fit to meson decay widths across light to heavy quark sectors. The coupled-channel equations were solved using the Resonating Group Method (RGM) and the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM), allowing the inclusion of $D$-wave $D^{\ast}K$ channels for the $1^{+}$ sector.

## Key numbers

*   The naive quark model predicts the $D_{s0}^{\ast}(2317)$ mass at $2383$ MeV (with one-loop OGE corrections), while the experimental value is $2318.0\pm1.0$ MeV; coupling to the $DK$ threshold yields a mass of $2323.7$ MeV.
*   The $D_{s1}(2460)$ mass predicted by the naive quark model is $2570$ MeV (with one-loop OGE corrections), compared to the experimental value of $2459.6\pm0.9$ MeV.
*   The $D_{s1}(2536)$ mass predicted by the naive quark model is $2560$ MeV (with one-loop OGE corrections), compared to the experimental value of $2535.18\pm0.24$ MeV.
*   The $D_{s2}^{\ast}(2573)$ mass predicted by the naive quark model is $2609$ MeV (with one-loop OGE corrections), compared to the experimental value of $2571.9\pm0.8$ MeV.
*   The $D_{s0}^{\ast}(2317)$ state is found to have a $c\bar{s}$ probability of $66.3\%$ and a $DK$ ($S$-wave) probability of $33.7\%$.
*   The $^{3}P_{0}$ model strength used for the charmed-strange sector is $\gamma=0.38$, with a vertex form factor cutoff $\Lambda=0.84$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD studies of Mohler (2013) and Lang (2014), which showed that including $DK$ and $D^{\ast}K$ scattering operators brings the masses of these states into agreement with experiment. The authors' calculation goes beyond those lattice studies by incorporating the $D$-wave $D^{\ast}K$ channel and computing the probabilities of different Fock components, which lattice QCD cannot directly provide. The results contradict the analysis of Torres (2014), who found a $70\%$ $DK$ component in the $D_{s0}^{\ast}(2317)$ using the Weinberg compositeness condition on the lattice data; the authors argue their lower $DK$ probability arises from a different dynamical framework where the residual $DK$ interaction is constrained by other observables.
