# Hadronic Parity Violation and Inelastic Electron-Deuteron Scattering

> We compute contributions to the parity-violating (PV) inelastic electron-deuteron scattering asymmetry arising from hadronic PV. While hadronic PV effects can be relatively important in PV threshold electro- disintegration, we find that they are highly suppressed at quasielastic kinematics. The interpretation of the PV quasielastic asymmetry is, thus, largely unaffected by hadronic PV.

## Key claim

Hadronic parity-violating (PV) effects in inelastic electron-deuteron scattering are highly suppressed at quasielastic kinematics, contributing at most a few parts per thousand correction to the asymmetry at the SAMPLE experiment's momentum transfer of Q² = 0.1 (GeV/c)², contrary to the possibility that such effects could explain the anomalously small measured value of the isovector axial vector form factor.

## Method

The authors computed the parity-violating asymmetry for inelastic electron-deuteron scattering by including contributions from hadronic PV through parity-mixing in the deuteron ground state, parity-mixing in the final scattering states, and PV two-body electromagnetic currents. They used the Argonne V18 potential to generate realistic two-body wave functions for both bound and scattering states, solving the inhomogeneous Schrödinger equation to obtain the parity-admixed wave functions, and evaluated the resulting multipole matrix elements for both threshold electro-disintegration and quasielastic kinematics.

## Key numbers

- At Q² = 0.1 (GeV/c)², the ratio of the hadronic PV asymmetry to the Z⁰-exchange asymmetry is of order a few 0.1%, based on the scaling relation ALRγ/ALRZ ∼ (mN²/Q²) × ⟨e^(-mπx)/(mN x)⟩ × 10⁻³, with ⟨e^(-mπx)/(mN x)⟩ ∼ 0.1 for x ∼ 1 fm
- The hadronic PV coupling scale is set by gπ = 3.8 × 10⁻⁸, with the strong pion-nucleon coupling gπNN ≈ 13.45 and the weak PV coupling hπ ∼ 4.5 × 10⁻⁷
- The isovector axial vector form factor at tree level is GAeV(Q²=0) = -1.267, with radiative corrections reducing the magnitude by roughly 40 ± 20%
- The SAMPLE experiment performed measurements at Q² = 0.1 (GeV/c)²
- The nuclear magnetic form factor at small Q² is approximately μV = 4.70

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculations of Hwang and Henley (1980, 1981) on hadronic PV in threshold deuteron electro-disintegration and the analysis of Kurasawa and Arima (1997) on PV quasielastic electron-deuteron scattering, which only considered parity-mixing in the deuteron wave function. The present study provides a more complete treatment by including parity-mixing in final scattering states and PV two-body currents, and demonstrates that hadronic PV effects cannot account for the discrepancy between the theoretical and experimental values of the isovector axial vector form factor reported by the SAMPLE collaboration. The results enable a cleaner interpretation of future PV quasielastic asymmetry measurements for extracting the strangeness magnetic form factor and the isovector axial vector form factor.
