# Fusion and breakup in the reactions of 6,7Li and 9Be

> We develop a three body classical trajectory Monte Carlo (CTMC) method to dicsuss the effect of the breakup process on heavy-ion fusion reactions induced by weakly bound nuclei. This method follows the classical trajectories of breakup fragments after the breakup takes place, and thus provides an unambiguous separation between complete and incomplete fusion cross sections. Applying this method to the fusion reaction $^{6}$Li + $^{209}$Bi, we find that there is a significant contribution to the total complete fusion cross sections from the process where all the breakup fragments are captured by the target nucleus (i.e., the breakup followed by complete fusion).

## Key claim

The authors develop a three-body classical trajectory Monte Carlo (CTMC) method to show that, for the reaction 6Li + 209Bi, there is a significant contribution to the total complete fusion cross section from the process where both breakup fragments are captured by the target nucleus after breakup occurs, a process not accounted for in standard coupled-channels calculations.

## Method

The authors solve the classical Hamiltonian for a projectile consisting of two cluster fragments interacting with a target nucleus in a two-dimensional plane, using Monte Carlo sampling of initial conditions (random orientation and separation of the fragments at the breakup Q-value). They monitor the relative distances between particles to determine when breakup occurs (when the fragment-fragment separation exceeds the barrier radius of their mutual potential) and when a fragment is absorbed by the target (when the fragment-target distance falls below 1.1 (A_i^{1/3} + A_T^{1/3}) fm). Partial cross sections for a given impact parameter are computed by sampling many trajectories, and total cross sections are obtained by integrating over impact parameter.

## Key numbers

- For the 6Li + 209Bi reaction at E_cm = 40 MeV, sample trajectories are shown for impact parameters b = 2.5 fm (complete fusion without breakup), b = 4.65 fm (breakup followed by complete fusion), and b = 5.5 fm (incomplete fusion where only the deuteron is captured).
- The absorption criterion for a fragment by the target is r_iT < 1.1 (A_i^{1/3} + A_T^{1/3}) fm.
- The initial projectile center of mass is placed at R_max = 200 fm from the target.

## Context

This work extends the three-body classical trajectory Monte Carlo method, previously developed in atomic and molecular physics for ion-atom ionization and charge transfer collisions (Abrines and Percival 1966, Cohen 1983, Fiol et al. 2000), to nuclear fusion reactions of weakly bound nuclei. The method addresses a limitation of continuum-discretized coupled-channels calculations (Hagino et al. 2000, Diaz-Torres et al. 2002), which cannot easily compute cross sections for breakup followed by complete fusion. The results suggest that experimental data and theoretical calculations require a consistent definition of complete fusion, and the approach can be applied to other weakly bound projectiles such as 7Li and 9Be.
