# Meson-induced pentaquark productions

> Production cross sections of the pentaquark Theta+ baryon in the meson-induced reactions are calculated for JP=1/2+- and 3/2+- cases. Through the comparison with the previous measurements at KEK, several quantum numbers are excluded. The remaining possibilities can be used to cast the upper limit on the Theta+ width, combining with the result from the J-PARC E19 experiment.

# Meson-Induced Pentaquark Productions

## Key claim
The production cross sections of the pentaquark Theta+ baryon in meson-induced reactions are calculated for four possible spin-parity assignments, and the results show that spin 3/2 cases are almost excluded by existing experimental upper limits, while spin 1/2 cases remain possible only if the Theta+ width is extremely small (below 1 MeV), under the assumption that nucleon Born diagrams dominate and no accidental destructive interference from other diagrams occurs.

## Method
The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach with Born diagrams: s-channel nucleon exchange for the π⁻ p → K⁻ Θ⁺ reaction and u-channel nucleon exchange for the K⁺ p → π⁺ Θ⁺ reaction. Both pseudoscalar (PS) and pseudovector (PV) coupling schemes were used for the πNN and KNΘ vertices, with static and covariant form factors to estimate theoretical uncertainties. Cross sections were calculated for four quantum number cases: Jᴾ = 1/2⁺, 1/2⁻, 3/2⁺, and 3/2⁻.

## Key numbers
- For the πK reaction at √s = 2124 MeV (P_lab ~ 1920 MeV), the total cross section for Jᴾ = 1/2⁺ in the PS scheme with static form factor is 9.2⁺¹·⁴₋₁·₃ μb per 1 MeV width; in the PV scheme it is 0.51⁺⁰·⁰⁷₋₀·⁰⁸ μb per 1 MeV width.
- For the πK reaction at √s = 2124 MeV, the Jᴾ = 1/2⁻ case gives 0.18⁺⁰·⁰²₋₀·⁰³ μb per 1 MeV width (PS, static) and 0.40⁺⁰·⁰⁶₋₀·⁰⁶ μb per 1 MeV width (PV, static).
- For the Kπ reaction at √s = 1888 MeV (P_lab ~ 1200 MeV), the Jᴾ = 3/2⁻ case gives 8572⁺¹⁰¹⁹₋₉₉₂ μb per 1 MeV width (static form factor) and 40544⁺¹⁵¹¹₋₁₈₂₄ μb per 1 MeV width (covariant form factor).
- Combining theoretical predictions with the J-PARC E19 upper limit of less than 1 μb for the πK cross section, the upper limit on the Θ⁺ width is 0.72 MeV for Jᴾ = 1/2⁺ and 3.1 MeV for Jᴾ = 1/2⁻.
- The background process π⁻ p → K⁺ K N has measured cross sections of ~25 μb (direct), ~21 μb (via Λ(1520)), and ~30 μb (via φ).

## Context
This work extends earlier effective Lagrangian studies of meson-induced Θ⁺ production by Hyodo (2003), Liu (2003), Oh (2003), Ko (2003), and Hyodo (2005), now incorporating all four spin-parity possibilities and comparing with the most recent experimental constraints. The calculations are motivated by the KEK-PS experiments (Miwa, 2006 and 2007) which gave upper limits of several μb, and the more stringent J-PARC E19 result (Shirotori, 2012) of less than 1 μb for the πK reaction. The results enable experimentalists to set quantitative upper limits on the Θ⁺ width for each remaining quantum number possibility, and the angular dependence predictions (weak for 1/2⁻, forward-peaked for 1/2⁺) can be used for comparison with experiments having limited angular coverage.
