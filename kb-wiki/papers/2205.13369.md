# Phonon modes of magnetic vortex lattices in finite isospin chiral perturbation theory

> We study phonon modes associated with magnetic vortex lattices of finite isospin chiral perturbation theory near the upper critical point by introducing quasimomentum fluctuations to the lattice and calculate dispersion relations associated with the optical and acoustic modes. We find that one of the acoustic modes is massless and that its energy for small transverse quasimomentum is quartic (due the presence of an isospin chemical potential), which is significantly softer than the "supersoft" (quadratic) massless mode of the Abelian Higgs Model (AHM). Due to the presence of derivative interactions, which is absent in the AHM, the speed of the longitudinal mode depends on both the isospin chemical potential and the external magnetic field. Our results suggest that the standard assumption of an ordered lattice in finite isospin QCD should be revisited and the existence of a disordered spaghetti phase of a vortex liquid or gas, should be considered.

## Key claim

The authors find that one acoustic phonon mode of the magnetic vortex lattice in finite isospin chiral perturbation theory is massless with a quartic dispersion relation for small transverse quasimomentum, which is significantly softer than the quadratic ("supersoft") massless mode of the Abelian Higgs Model. This result suggests that the standard assumption of an ordered lattice in finite isospin QCD should be revisited and a disordered spaghetti phase of a vortex liquid or gas should be considered.

## Method

The authors study phonon modes of triangular magnetic vortex lattices in finite isospin chiral perturbation theory near the upper critical magnetic field by introducing quasimomentum fluctuations to the lattice solution in the lowest Landau level approximation. They expand the pion fields around the static triangular vortex lattice solution, compute the quadratic effective action for the fluctuations, and extract the dispersion relations for the optical and acoustic modes. The calculation incorporates derivative interactions present in chiral perturbation theory that are absent in the Abelian Higgs Model.

## Key numbers

- The Abrikosov ratio for the triangular lattice is \(\beta_A = 1.159595\cdots\)
- The upper critical field is \(H_{c2} = B_c = (\mu_I^2 - m_\pi^2)/e\)
- The coefficient in the quartic dispersion relation is \(\alpha_4 \equiv 2(2q_4 - \tilde{q}_4) \approx 5.43\)
- For \(\mu_I = 1.25 m_\pi\) and \(B_{\rm ext} = 0.99 B_c\), the transverse speed of the massless mode is approximately a factor of a thousand smaller than the speed of light for small transverse quasimomentum in the range \(30 \lesssim |\mathbf{k}| \lesssim 70\ {\rm MeV}\)
- The longitudinal speed of the massless mode increases monotonically from zero to approximately \(0.25\) (in units of the speed of light) as longitudinal momentum increases

## Context

This work extends the study of magnetic vortex lattices in finite isospin chiral perturbation theory (Adhikari, 2018) by analyzing phonon modes near the upper critical field, and contrasts with the Abelian Higgs Model (Chernodub, 2014; Rosenstein, 1999) where the massless acoustic mode has a quadratic dispersion. The results suggest that the magnetic vortex lattice in finite isospin QCD may melt into a disordered vortex liquid or gas phase, which would require revisiting the standard assumption of an ordered lattice in this system.
