# Physics-based method for generating probability table using random-matrix approach

> We develop a new method for generating probability tables based on a solid theoretical foundation. The fluctuating cross sections are calculated using the GOE-$S$-matrix model, in which the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) is incorporated into the calculation of the scattering ($S$) matrix. The calculated cross sections are then converted into the probability tables in the same manner as in NJOY. Using $^{238}$U and $^{239}$Pu as target nuclei, we determine the optimal model parameters based on the convergence behavior of the average cross sections. The statistical uncertainty of the probability tables is examined as a function of the number of ladders. We demonstrate that the probability tables calculated at 0 K are qualitatively comparable with those calculated using the conventional single-level Breit-Wigner formalism, albeit we observe some local differences due to requisite unitality for the $S$ matrix.

## Key claim

The GOE-$S$-matrix model provides a theoretically grounded alternative to the conventional single-level Breit-Wigner (SLBW) formalism for generating probability tables in the unresolved resonance region (URR), but the resulting tables at 0 K show only qualitative agreement with SLBW tables, with local differences arising from the unitarity requirement of the $S$ matrix.

## Method

The authors implemented the GOE-$S$-matrix model, which replaces the nuclear Hamiltonian with a Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble random matrix and uses transmission coefficients (as in the Hauser-Feshbach model) instead of average resonance parameters. They generated resonances over the energy range $E_{\lambda}=-\lambda/2$ to $\lambda/2$ (where level density is approximately constant) and converted the calculated cross sections into probability tables using the same procedure as the NJOY nuclear data processing code. The optimal model parameters were determined by studying the convergence behavior of average cross sections for $^{238}$U and $^{239}$Pu targets, and the statistical uncertainty was evaluated as a function of the number of resonance ladders $L$.

## Key numbers

- The number of levels $n=25$ was found to be sufficient for the GOE-$S$-matrix model, balancing accuracy against computational time.
- The root mean square percentage error (RMSPE) of the product of probability and average cross section drops below 5% at $L=1000$ ladders and approaches 1% at $L=10000$ ladders.
- The resonance generation energy range was restricted to $E_{\lambda}=-\lambda/2$ to $\lambda/2$ (from the full range $E_{\lambda}=-2\lambda$ to $2\lambda$) to maintain approximately constant level density.

## Context

This work extends the GOE-$S$-matrix model developed by Verbaarschot, Weidenmuller, and Zirnbauer (1985) and further advanced by Kawano, Talou, and Weidenmuller (2015), who showed that Monte Carlo techniques could reproduce the three-fold integration results for the average $S$ matrix. The authors' previous study (Fujio 2025) extended the model to account for actual cross sections by introducing a phase shift. This method addresses deficiencies of the conventional SLBW approach (Breit 1936) and Reich-Moore approximation, which do not properly account for resonance interference and suffer from broken unitarity. The work enables a more physically consistent treatment of probability tables for Monte Carlo transport codes such as MCNP (Rising 2025), OpenMC (Romano 2015), and Serpent (Leppanen 2025), and future work will incorporate temperature effects and improve boundary cross section determination.
