# Study of bound and scattering states in three-nucleon systems

> A variational technique to describe the ground and scattering states below the break-up threshold for a three-nucleon system is developed. The method consists in expanding the wave function in terms of correlated Harmonic Hyperspherical functions suitable to handle the large repulsion contained in the nuclear potential at short distances; three body forces have also been considered. The inclusion of the pp Coulomb repulsion in the p-d processes does not cause any particular problem, since no partial wave decomposition of the interaction is performed. Accurate numerical results are given for ground state properties and scattering lenghts, phase shifts and mixing parameters at three different energies of the incident nucleon. The agreement with other available results and with experimental analyses is higlly satisfactory.

## Key claim

The paper develops a variational technique using correlated Harmonic Hyperspherical (PHH) functions that can describe both bound and scattering states of three-nucleon systems below the break-up threshold, with the claim that the inclusion of pp Coulomb repulsion in p-d processes does not cause particular problems because no partial wave decomposition of the interaction is performed. The hedge is that the method is demonstrated only for states below the deuteron break-up threshold, with break-up reactions deferred to a subsequent paper.

## Method

The wave function is written as a sum of three Faddeev amplitudes, each expanded in correlated Pair correlated Hyperspherical Harmonic (PHH) basis functions. For bound states, the Rayleigh-Ritz variational principle yields a set of second-order differential equations for the hyperradial functions; for scattering states, the Kohn-Hultén variational principle is used, with the total wave function split into a core part (expanded in PHH functions) and an asymptotic part (regular and irregular Coulomb functions). The resulting inhomogeneous differential equations are solved on a transformed grid mapping the infinite hyperradius range to a finite interval.

## Key numbers

- Triton binding energy calculations use the AV14 potential with up to 18 channels and hyperradial functions up to M=6; results agree with 34-channel configuration space Faddeev calculations from the Los Alamos group
- For the Malfliet-Tjon I-III potential (s-wave only): doublet n-d scattering length = 0.702 fm, doublet p-d scattering length = 0.003 fm, quartet n-d scattering length = 6.442 fm, quartet p-d scattering length = 13.96 fm
- Three-body force cutoff parameters adjusted to reproduce experimental triton binding: Λ = 5.13μ for AV14+TM and Λ = 4.99μ for AV14+BR models
- Scattering calculations performed at incident nucleon energies E_N = 1.0, 2.0, and 3.0 MeV
- For the grid transformation, the penultimate w-grid point corresponds to ρ ≈ 65.0 fm; N = 40 to 50 grid points used
- The regularization parameter ξ in the irregular Coulomb function is taken as ξ = 0.25 fm⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous PHH variational method for bound states (Kievsky, Viviani, Rosati, ref.[1]) to scattering states, enabling comparison with experimental phase shift analyses for p-d and n-d elastic scattering (ref.[8]). The results agree with configuration space Faddeev calculations from the Los Alamos group (refs.[3,17,20]) and with the semi-realistic Malfliet-Tjon potential results (ref.[28]). The method enables future extension to break-up reactions and provides a framework where the Coulomb interaction is treated without partial wave decomposition, addressing a known difficulty in Faddeev approaches.
