# Bayesian inferences on covariant density functionals from multimessenger astrophysical data: The impacts of likelihood functions of low density matter constraints

> We systematically investigate how the choice between Gaussian and uniform likelihood functions in Bayesian inference affects the inferred bulk properties of compact stars and nuclear matter within covariant density functional-based equations of state. To enable direct comparison between the two approaches, we designed the uniform likelihood function with a Gaussian-equivalent normalization factor and marginalization behavior. Across three representative astrophysical scenarios, both approaches yield nearly identical mass-radius relations, density-pressure relations, and overlapping 95.4\% confidence level regions. Although our inference analysis is carried out using parameters of the density functional, we subsequently determine the associated nuclear matter characteristic coefficients derived from the Taylor expansion of the energy density around the saturation density. We observe significant variation in the predicted isoscalar channel coefficients (e.g., the nuclear incompressibility) across different astrophysical scenarios, while the isovector channel (e.g., the slope of symmetry energy) exhibits only minimal variation.

## Key claim

The choice between Gaussian and uniform-Gaussian combination likelihood functions for low-density nuclear matter constraints has a negligible effect on the inferred mass-radius relations and density-pressure relations of compact stars, but the width of the uniform region (UG1 vs UG2) can broaden posterior distributions by approximately 0.2 km on both sides of the mass-radius relation.

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian inference using a covariant density functional (CDF) approach with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings, sampling seven adjustable parameters (three coupling constants at saturation density and four density-dependence parameters) with uniform priors. They applied nuclear matter constraints from symmetric nuclear matter characteristics (Dirac effective mass, saturation density, saturation energy, incompressibility, skewness, symmetry energy, and symmetry energy slope) and chiral effective field theory (N3LO) results for pure neutron matter, combined with astrophysical constraints from massive pulsar masses, gravitational wave events (GW170817 and GW190425), and NICER mass-radius measurements for four pulsars across three astrophysical scenarios (Baseline, B, and F).

## Key numbers

- The incompressibility parameter K_sat from giant monopole resonance experiments is 230 ± 40 MeV
- The symmetry energy slope L_sym from PREX-II is 106 ± 37 MeV, while CREX gives 20 ± 30 MeV
- The symmetry energy slope L_sym from LHC neutron skin measurements of 208Pb is 79 ± 39 MeV
- The incompressibility K_sat from elliptic flow measurements with IQMD simulations is 190 ± 30 MeV
- The incompressibility K_sat from Bayesian analysis of STAR flow data is 285 ± 67 MeV
- The symmetry energy slope L_sym from heavy-ion collision charged pion spectra is constrained to 42 < L_sym < 117 MeV
- The posterior mass-radius region broadens by approximately 0.2 km on both sides when using the wider UG2 likelihood compared to Gaussian likelihood

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of covariant density functional models (Malik 2022a, Malik 2022b, Beznogov 2023, Scurto 2024) by systematically comparing Gaussian and uniform-Gaussian combination likelihood functions for low-density matter constraints. The study builds on the uniform-Gaussian combination method proposed by Scurto (2024) and applies it to CDF-based equations of state with multimessenger astrophysical data. The results enable more robust statistical inference of nuclear matter properties by demonstrating which constraints are most sensitive to the choice of likelihood function, particularly showing that isoscalar channel coefficients (like nuclear incompressibility) vary significantly across astrophysical scenarios while isovector channel coefficients (like symmetry energy slope) show minimal variation.
