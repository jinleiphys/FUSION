# Shape effects on double beta decay of $^{70}$Zn and $^{150}$Nd in deformed shell model

> Using deformed shell model based on Hartree-Fock intrinsic states with $^{70}$Zn as an example and employing two realistic effective interactions, namely jj44b that produces deformed shape and JUN45 that generates spherical shape, it is demonstrated that the $0ν$ and $2ν$ nuclear transition matrix elements for double beta decay will reduce considerably as we go from spherical shapes to deformed shapes. This result is further substantiated by using $^{150}$Nd as another example.

## Key claim

Using the deformed shell model with two realistic effective interactions (jj44b producing deformed shapes and JUN45 producing spherical shapes), the authors demonstrate that both the 0ν and 2ν nuclear transition matrix elements for double beta decay are considerably reduced when going from spherical to deformed shapes, with the caveat that the results are independent of the scale factor used in the transition operator because only ratios of matrix elements are studied.

## Method

The authors employed the deformed shell model based on Hartree-Fock intrinsic states, projecting states of good angular momentum and performing band mixing calculations. For 70Zn, they used the jj44b interaction (which yields deformed solutions) and the JUN45 interaction (which yields nearly spherical solutions) within a model space of the 2p3/2, 1f5/2, 2p1/2, and 1g9/2 orbitals with a 56Ni core. For 150Nd, they used a modified Kuo-Herling interaction with a Z=50, N=82 core, and artificially reduced the interaction to 1/10th of its value to generate nearly spherical intrinsic states for comparison with the deformed solutions.

## Key numbers

- For 70Zn with jj44b (deformed) interaction: M0ν = 2.13; with JUN45 (spherical) interaction: M0ν = 4.56; a reduction factor of approximately 2 in M0ν from spherical to deformed shape.
- For 70Zn 2ν double beta decay half-life: T2ν1/2 = 3.39 × 10^23 yr for jj44b (deformed) and 4.94 × 10^22 yr for JUN45 (spherical), using a phase space factor of 0.32 × 10^(-21) yr^(-1); the NTME is reduced by a factor of approximately 2.6 from spherical to deformed shape.
- For 150Nd with deformed shape: M0ν = 1.35; with nearly spherical shape (interaction reduced to 1/10th): M0ν = 3.06; an increase by a factor of 2.33 from deformed to spherical shape.
- For 70Zn with jj44b: calculated E(2+1) = 0.355 MeV (experimental 0.885 MeV) and B(E2: 2+1 → 0+1) = 14 W.u. (experimental 16.5 W.u.); for 70Ge: calculated E(2+1) = 0.64 MeV (experimental 1.04 MeV) and B(E2: 2+1 → 0+1) = 18.8 W.u. (experimental 20.9 W.u.).

## Context

This work extends previous studies showing that quadrupole deformation reduces the 0ν nuclear transition matrix element, including energy density functional calculations with the Gogny force by Vaquero (showing deformation effects from 11% in 82Se to 38% in 128Te), proton-neutron QRPA calculations by Fang, and shell model calculations by Menendez where a quadrupole-quadrupole term was added to the effective interaction. The authors' deformed shell model approach provides a transparent determination of shapes using two realistic interactions within the same model space, and the results enable future calculations with larger sets of single-particle orbits and new effective interactions to address the Ikeda sum rule violation present in the current model space.
