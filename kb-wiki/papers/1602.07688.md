# Remarks on the pion-nucleon sigma-term

> The pion-nucleon $σ$-term can be stringently constrained by the combination of analyticity, unitarity, and crossing symmetry with phenomenological information on the pion-nucleon scattering lengths. Recently, lattice calculations at the physical point have been reported that find lower values by about $3σ$ with respect to the phenomenological determination. We point out that a lattice measurement of the pion-nucleon scattering lengths could help resolve the situation by testing the values extracted from spectroscopy measurements in pionic atoms.

# Remarks on the pion-nucleon sigma-term

## Key claim

The phenomenological determination of the pion-nucleon sigma-term from Roy-Steiner equations yields σπN = (59.1 ± 3.5) MeV, but recent lattice calculations at the physical point find values lower by about 3σ; the authors argue that a lattice measurement of the pion-nucleon scattering lengths could help resolve this discrepancy by testing the values extracted from spectroscopy measurements in pionic atoms.

## Method

The authors use Roy-Steiner equations, a dispersion-theory framework that enforces analyticity, unitarity, and crossing symmetry, to derive a linear relation between the pion-nucleon sigma-term and the S-wave scattering lengths in the two s-channel isospin channels. They then compare the constraints on the scattering lengths from pionic-atom data (pionic hydrogen and pionic deuterium level shifts and widths) with the constraints implied by lattice sigma-term values, and quantify the required precision for a lattice calculation of the scattering lengths to be conclusive.

## Key numbers

- σπN = (59.1 ± 3.5) MeV from Roy-Steiner equations combined with pionic-atom scattering lengths
- BMW lattice collaboration: σπN = 38(3)(3) MeV, tension of 3.8σ with the phenomenological value
- χQCD lattice collaboration: σπN = 44.4(3.2)(4.5) MeV, tension of 2.2σ
- ETMC lattice collaboration: σπN = 37.22(2.57)(+0.99/-0.63) MeV, tension of 4.9σ
- Reference scattering lengths from pionic atoms: a^{1/2} = (169.8 ± 2.0) × 10^{-3} Mπ^{-1}, a^{3/2} = (-86.3 ± 1.8) × 10^{-3} Mπ^{-1}
- Isospin-limit scattering lengths (corrected for electromagnetic effects): a_c^{1/2} = (178.8 ± 3.8) × 10^{-3} Mπ^{-1}, a_c^{3/2} = (-77.5 ± 3.5) × 10^{-3} Mπ^{-1}
- If both isospin channels could be calculated at 5-10% accuracy, the sigma-term uncertainty would be 5.0-8.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Roy-Steiner equation formalism developed by the same authors (Hoferichter et al., 2015) for extracting σπN, which improved upon the earlier dispersion-theory approach of Gasser et al. (1988, 1990) that gave σπN ~ 45 MeV and the partial-wave analysis of Pavan et al. (2001) that gave (64 ± 8) MeV. The paper highlights a tension analogous to the K→ππ puzzle, where lattice results for the isospin-0 S-wave ππ phase shift disagreed with Roy-equation phenomenology (Bai et al., 2015; Colangelo et al., 2001). The authors propose that a lattice calculation of πN scattering lengths, similar to how a lattice calculation of the ππ scattering length a_0^0 could resolve the K→ππ discrepancy, would provide an independent cross-check on σπN and help identify whether the origin of the discrepancy lies in the lattice calculations or in the phenomenological extraction.
