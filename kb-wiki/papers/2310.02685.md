# Coupling strength induced BCS-BEC crossover on phase boundary of pion superfluid

> Coupling strength effect on the quark matter with finite isospin chemical potential is studied in a Pauli-Villars regularized NJL model. A BCS-BEC crossover occurs along the phase boundary of pion superfluid phase transition, as increasing coupling strength $G$. For strong coupling cases, the critical isospin chemical potential for pion superfluid phase transition $μ_I^c$ is exactly the same as pion mass in vacuum $M_π$. Around the critical point $μ_I^c$, the pion superfluid quark matter is in BEC state, associated with a fast increase of pion condensate. For weak coupling cases, we obtain $μ_I^c<M_π$, and a mass jump of the Goldstone boson at the critical point $μ_I^c$. The pion superfluid quark matter is in BCS state even around $μ_I^c$, accompanied by a slow increase of pion condensate. Note that $μ_I^c$ is a non-monotonic function of coupling strength $G$. On the other hand, coupling strength effect changes the bulk properties of quark matter. In strong (weak) coupling cases, the EoS of quark matter at finite isospin chemical potential is stiff (soft). Therefore, the compact stars composed of strong (weak) coupling pion superfluid quark matter has a heavier (lighter) mass and larger (smaller) radius.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that increasing the four-quark coupling strength \(G\) in a Pauli-Villars regularized NJL model induces a BCS-BEC crossover along the phase boundary of the pion superfluid phase transition, with the critical isospin chemical potential \(\mu_I^c\) being exactly equal to the vacuum pion mass \(M_\pi\) only for strong coupling (\(G \geq G_0 = 2.89\) GeV\(^{-2}\)), while for weak coupling (\(G < G_0\)) one finds \(\mu_I^c < M_\pi\) and a mass jump of the Goldstone boson.

## Method

The authors study two-flavor quark matter at finite isospin chemical potential \(\mu_I\) and zero temperature and baryon chemical potential using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model regularized with the Pauli-Villars scheme. They solve the mean-field gap equations for the chiral condensate (effective quark mass \(m\)) and the pion condensate \(\Delta\), and compute meson masses via the random phase approximation (RPA) using polarization functions. The equation of state (EoS) is then used in the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain the mass-radius relation of compact stars composed of pion superfluid quark matter.

## Key numbers

*   The critical isospin chemical potential \(\mu_I^c\) for the pion superfluid phase transition is 128, 123, 134, and 126 MeV at coupling strengths \(G = 2.5, 2.8, 3.44, 3.8\) GeV\(^{-2}\), respectively.
*   The threshold coupling strength for the BCS-BEC crossover is \(G_0 = 2.89\) GeV\(^{-2}\), where the effective chemical potential at the critical point \(\mu_{\text{eff}}^c = 0\).
*   For weak coupling (\(G = 2.5\) GeV\(^{-2}\)), the sound velocity saturates at \(c_s^2 \simeq 0.4\) at high isospin chemical potential; for strong coupling (\(G = 3.44\) GeV\(^{-2}\)), it saturates at \(c_s^2 \simeq 0.6\).
*   The maximum star mass and radius for weak coupling (\(G = 2.5\) GeV\(^{-2}\)) are \(M \sim 2 M_\odot\) and \(R \sim 9\) km; for strong coupling (\(G = 3.44\) GeV\(^{-2}\)) they are \(M \sim 3 M_\odot\) and \(R \sim 14\) km.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of density-induced BCS-BEC crossover in quark matter (reviewed in works such as those by the authors of "reviewbcs1" and "reviewbcs2") by instead investigating the coupling-strength-driven crossover in the pion superfluid phase. It uses the NJL model, a standard effective model for QCD at finite density, and applies Pauli-Villars regularization (as in earlier work by the authors of "pv1", "pv2", and "pv3"). The results enable predictions for the mass-radius relation of compact stars, suggesting that weak-coupling pion superfluid quark matter could explain observed stars like PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0740+6620, while strong-coupling matter could account for massive stars like PSR J0952-0607 and GW190814.
