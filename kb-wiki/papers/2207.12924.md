# Microscopic study of the compound nucleus formation in cold-fusion reactions

> The understanding of the fusion probability is of particular importance to reveal the mechanism of producing superheavy elements. We present a microscopic study of the compound nucleus formation by combining time-dependent density functional theory, coupled-channels approach, and dynamical diffusion models. The fusion probability and compound nucleus formation cross sections for cold-fusion reactions $^{48}$Ca+$^{208}$Pb, $^{50}$Ti+$^{208}$Pb, and $^{54}$Cr+$^{208}$Pb are investigated and it is found that the deduced capture barriers, capture cross sections for these reactions are consistent with experimental data. Above the capture barrier, our calculations reproduce the measured fusion probability reasonably well. Our studies demonstrate that the restrictions from the microscopic dynamic theory improve the predictive power of the coupled-channels and diffusion calculations.

## Key claim

By combining time-dependent density functional theory (TDHF) with coupled-channels and fusion-by-diffusion (FbD) models, the authors show that the injection-point distance extracted from TDHF evolution can replace the adjustable injection parameter in the FbD model, but the calculated fusion probabilities and compound-nucleus formation cross sections agree well with experimental data only for above-barrier collisions; below-barrier results are systematically smaller than the measured upper limits.

## Method

The authors performed TDHF simulations with the SLy5 density functional for central collisions of ⁴⁸Ca, ⁵⁰Ti, and ⁵⁴Cr with ²⁰⁸Pb to determine capture thresholds and injection-point distances. The capture thresholds from TDHF were used to fix Woods-Saxon potential parameters, which were then input into the coupled-channels code CCFULL to compute capture cross sections. The injection-point distances from TDHF were used as the sole input to the FbD model to compute fusion probabilities and compound-nucleus formation cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Capture threshold energies from TDHF: 173.3 MeV for ⁴⁸Ca+²⁰⁸Pb, 191.4 MeV for ⁵⁰Ti+²⁰⁸Pb, and 209.4 MeV for ⁵⁴Cr+²⁰⁸Pb; these agree with experimental barriers of 173.4±0.1, 192.6±0.1, and 207.3±0.3 MeV, respectively.
- Woods-Saxon potential depths: 165.42 MeV for ⁴⁸Ca+²⁰⁸Pb, 251.998 MeV for ⁵⁰Ti+²⁰⁸Pb, and 225.07 MeV for ⁵⁴Cr+²⁰⁸Pb.
- Radius parameters from static HF densities: 1.126 fm for ²⁰⁸Pb, 1.161 fm for ⁴⁸Ca, 1.088 fm for ⁵⁰Ti, and 1.087 fm for ⁵⁴Cr.
- Root-mean-square matter radii from static HF: 3.56 fm (⁴⁸Ca), 3.59 fm (⁵⁰Ti), 3.67 fm (⁵⁴Cr), and 5.55 fm (²⁰⁸Pb).

## Context

This work extends the fusion-by-diffusion model of Swiatecki (2005) and Cap (2011) by replacing the empirical injection parameter with a microscopically determined value from TDHF simulations, following the approach of Sekizawa (2019) for estimating injection points. It also builds on the TDHF-based capture-barrier determination of Guo (2018) and the experimental fusion-probability measurements for cold-fusion reactions by Banerjee (2019). The method enables systematic predictions for superheavy-element synthesis reactions, particularly for the search for elements Z=119 and Z=120, by reducing the adjustable parameters in coupled-channels and diffusion calculations.
