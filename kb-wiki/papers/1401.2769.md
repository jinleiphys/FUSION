# A class of nonperturbative nonlinear covariant gauges in Yang-Mills theories

> We report on the recent proposal of a class of nonlinear covariant gauges that can be formulated as an extremization procedure which admits a simple discretization well-suited to numerical minimization techniques. This class of gauges is continuously connected to the Landau gauge and, in the ultraviolet, where one can ignore Gribov ambiguities, it reduces to the Curci-Ferrari-Delbourgo-Jarvis gauges.

# A class of nonperturbative nonlinear covariant gauges in Yang-Mills theories

## Key claim
The paper proposes a class of nonlinear covariant gauges that can be formulated as an extremization procedure suitable for lattice implementation, which reduces to the Curci-Ferrari-Delbourgo-Jarvis (CFDJ) gauges in the ultraviolet where Gribov ambiguities can be ignored, but the proposal is not restricted to infinitesimal gauge transformations as previous attempts were.

## Method
The gauge condition is defined as the extremum of a functional involving the gluon field, a matrix field eta, and the gauge transformation g, with a Gaussian distribution over eta. The Faddeev-Popov procedure is applied to obtain the gauge-fixed action, and averaging over eta yields the CFDJ gauge-fixing action. A lattice discretization is given using link variables and a rescaled matrix field M, with the extremization functional being linear in the gauge transformation matrix at each lattice site, enabling standard numerical minimization techniques.

## Key numbers
- The Gaussian distribution for eta has parameter xi_0, with the Landau gauge corresponding to xi_0 = 0.
- The lattice discretization uses the rescaled matrix field M(x) = a^2 g_0^2 eta(x)/2, where a is the lattice spacing and g_0 is the bare coupling constant.
- The lattice noise field distribution for the proposal is p_STT(M) = exp{-(1/(xi_0 g_0^2)) tr[M^dagger M]}, with the decomposition M = M_0/sqrt(2N) 1 + sum_{a=1}^N M_a t^a.
- The continuum formulation yields five independent renormalization factors at one-loop order in four dimensions.

## Context
This work extends the proposal of Cucchieri, Mendes and Santos (2009-2011) for lattice covariant gauges, which was limited to infinitesimal gauge transformations, by allowing arbitrary gauge transformations and a different sampling over the auxiliary field. The paper also generalizes the author's previous approach for averaging over Gribov copies in Landau gauge (Serreau 2012, 2013) to this class of nonlinear covariant gauges, providing a local renormalizable action. This enables one-loop calculations of gluon and ghost correlators in a gauge continuously connected to Landau gauge, potentially allowing lattice studies of Yang-Mills correlators beyond the Landau gauge.
