# Nonperturbatively-renormalized glue momentum fraction at physical pion mass from Lattice QCD

> We present the first nonperturbatively-renormalized determination of the glue momentum fraction $\langle x \rangle_g$ in the nucleon, based on lattice-QCD simulations at physical pion mass using the cluster-decomposition error reduction (CDER) technique. We provide the first practical strategy to renormalize the glue energy-momentum tensor (EMT) nonperturbatively in the RI/MOM scheme, and convert the results to the $\overline{\textrm{MS}}$ scheme with 1-loop matching. The simulation results show that the CDER technique can reduce the statistical uncertainty of its renormalization constant by a factor of ${\cal O}$(300) in calculations using typical state-of-the-art lattice volume, and the nonperturbatively-renormalized $\langle x \rangle_g$ is shown to be independent of the lattice definitions of the glue EMT up to discretization errors. We determine the renormalized $\langle x \rangle_g^{\overline{\textrm{MS}}}(2\textrm{ GeV})$ to be 0.47(4)(11) at physical pion mass, which is consistent with the experimentally-determined value.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first nonperturbatively-renormalized determination of the glue momentum fraction in the nucleon from lattice QCD at the physical pion mass, using the cluster-decomposition error reduction (CDER) technique. The result is consistent with the experimentally-determined value, but the authors note that the major systematic uncertainty comes from mixing with the quark momentum fraction, which could be eliminated with a similar nonperturbative calculation using quark external states.

## Method

The authors perform lattice QCD simulations on a 2+1-flavor domain-wall fermion ensemble (48I) with lattice spacing a=0.114 fm, pion mass mπ=140 MeV, and volume 48³×96. They define the gauge energy-momentum tensor (EMT) using both clover and plaquette definitions, with and without hypercubic (HYP) smearing, and renormalize it nonperturbatively in the RI/MOM scheme with 1-loop matching to the MSbar scheme. The cluster-decomposition error reduction (CDER) technique is applied to the renormalization constant calculation by introducing cutoffs r1=0.9 fm between the glue operator and gauge field, and r2=1.3 fm between the gauge fields in the gluon propagator, reducing statistical uncertainty by a factor of approximately 300.

## Key numbers

- Renormalized glue momentum fraction ⟨x⟩gMSbar(2 GeV) = 0.47(4)(11) at physical pion mass, where the first error is statistical and the second is systematic
- CDER technique reduces statistical uncertainty of the renormalization constant by a factor of O(300) on the 48I ensemble with cutoffs r1=0.9 fm and r2=1.3 fm
- Renormalization constant ZT-1(0 HYP) = 0.257(25)(5) for the clover definition without HYP smearing
- Renormalization constant ZT-1(1 HYP) = 0.946(26)(19) for the clover definition with 1-step HYP smearing
- Renormalization constant ZT-1(1 HYP) = 1.05(35)(21) for the plaquette definition with 1-step HYP smearing
- Lattice spacing a = 0.114 fm for the 48I ensemble
- Pion mass mπ = 140 MeV for the 48I ensemble
- Lattice volume 48³ × 96 (L = 5.5 fm) for the 48I ensemble

## Context

This work extends previous lattice calculations of the glue momentum fraction by Horsley (2012), Deka (2013), and Alexandrou (2016, 2017), which used 1-loop lattice perturbation theory for renormalization and gave varying results (0.43(9), 0.33(6), and 0.267(22)(30) respectively). The nonperturbative renormalization strategy introduced here enables consistent results independent of the lattice definition of the gauge EMT, and the CDER technique makes such calculations feasible on large volumes. This approach is applicable to other glue operator calculations such as the glue spin contribution to the proton spin and glue transversity in hadrons, and will be essential for accurate predictions from approaches targeting the entire glue PDF like large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) and the lattice cross-section approach.
