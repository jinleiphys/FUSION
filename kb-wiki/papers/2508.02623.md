# Nuclear modification factor $I_{AA}$ in $AA$ collisions at RHIC and LHC energies in scenarios with and without quark-gluon plasma formation in $pp$ collisions

> We calculate the away-side hadron-triggered modification factor $I_{AA}$ in $AA$ collisions at RHIC and LHC energies for scenarios with and without quark-gluon plasma formation in $pp$ collision. We find that for both scenarios theoretical results for $I_{AA}$ agree well with the available data for 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb and 0.2 TeV Au+Au collisions. We make predictions for $I_{AA}$ in 7 TeV O+O collisions that are planned at the LHC. Our results show that measuring $I_{OO}$ in the whole centrality interval and at small centrality ($\lesssim 5$%) may give information on the presence of jet quenching in $pp$ collisions.

# Nuclear modification factor $I_{AA}$ in $AA$ collisions at RHIC and LHC energies in scenarios with and without quark-gluon plasma formation in $pp$ collisions

## Key claim

The away-side hadron-triggered modification factor $I_{AA}$ in heavy ion collisions can be described equally well by jet quenching models both with and without quark-gluon plasma formation in $pp$ collisions, but measuring $I_{OO}$ in 7 TeV O+O collisions across the full centrality interval and at small centrality (less than about 5%) may discriminate between these two scenarios.

## Method

The author calculates the away-side hadron-triggered modification factor $I_{AA}$ using the light-cone path integral (LCPI) approach to induced gluon emission, treating collisional energy loss as a perturbation. The medium-modified fragmentation functions are computed with a temperature-dependent running coupling $\alpha_s(Q,T)$ featuring a plateau around $Q \sim \kappa T$, where $\kappa$ is fitted to LHC data on $R_{AA}$ in heavy ion collisions. The QGP fireball evolution is described within Bjorken's 1+1D model with uniform transverse density, using initial temperatures determined from charged particle multiplicity via the Glauber wounded nucleon model.

## Key numbers

- For 0.2 TeV Au+Au collisions at 0-20% centrality with $9 < p_T^t < 12$ GeV, theoretical $I_{AA}$ results for all three scenarios (with QGP in $pp$, without QGP in $pp$, and intermediate) agree with PHENIX data for $p_T^a \sim 2-9$ GeV
- For 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at 0-5% centrality with $8 < p_T^t < 15$ GeV, theoretical $I_{AA}$ agrees with ALICE data; the deviation of $I_{AA}^{pdf}$ from unity is $I_{AA}^{pdf} - 1 \sim 0.05-0.07$, and $(1 - I_{pp}) \sim 0.03-0.06$
- For 7 TeV O+O collisions at $8 < p_T^t < 15$ GeV, the ratio $|I_{AA}^{pdf} - 1| / |I_{AA}^{pdf} - I_{AA}|$ is about 0.25 for 0-100% centrality and about 0.15 for 0-5% centrality in the scenario with QGP formation in $pp$ collisions
- For 7 TeV O+O collisions, the ratio $(1 - I_{AA})_{0-5\%} / (1 - I_{AA})_{0-100\%}$ at $p_T^a \sim 4-8$ GeV is about 1.9 for the scenario with QGP formation in $pp$ collisions and about 1.4 for the scenario without
- Initial temperatures from the Glauber model: $T_0 \sim 400$ MeV for 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb, $T_0 \sim 320$ MeV for 0.2 TeV Au+Au, and $T_0 \sim 280$ MeV for 7 TeV O+O collisions at centralities of about 5-10%
- Effective fireball radii for $pp$ collisions: $R_f \approx [1.26, 1.44, 1.51]$ fm for $\sqrt{s} = [0.2, 2.76, 7]$ TeV respectively

## Context

This work extends the author's previous global analysis of $R_{AA}$ data (Zakharov, 2024) to the two-particle correlation observable $I_{AA}$, which probes jet fragmentation functions in the region $z_T \sim 0.1-0.5$ rather than the $z_T$ near unity probed by $R_{AA}$. The study is motivated by the possibility of QGP formation in $pp$ collisions, supported by observations of the ridge effect at LHC (CMS, ATLAS) and strangeness enhancement (ALICE). The predictions for 7 TeV O+O collisions, planned at the LHC in 2025, aim to resolve whether jet quenching occurs in $pp$ collisions, a question that remains open due to theoretical uncertainties from nuclear PDF effects in $R_{AA}$ measurements.
