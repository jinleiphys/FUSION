# The chiral structure of the Lamb shift and the definition of the proton radius

> The standard definition of the electromagnetic radius of a charged particle (in particular the proton) is ambiguous once electromagnetic corrections are considered. We argue that a natural definition can be given within an effective field theory framework in terms of a matching coefficient. The definition of the neutron radius is also discussed. We elaborate on the effective field theory relevant for the hydrogen and muonic hydrogen, specially for the latter. We compute the hadronic corrections to the lamb shift (for the polarizability effects only with logarithmic accuracy) within heavy baryon effective theory. We find that they diverge in the inverse of the pion mass in the chiral limit.

# The chiral structure of the Lamb shift and the definition of the proton radius

## Key claim

The standard definition of the proton electromagnetic radius becomes infrared divergent once electromagnetic corrections beyond leading order are included; a natural, scale-dependent definition can instead be given within an effective field theory framework by identifying the radius with a matching coefficient of pNRQED, though this makes the radius a scheme-dependent quantity whose hadronic part is not directly observable.

## Method

The author constructs a chain of effective field theories (HBET → NRQED → pNRQED) to compute hadronic corrections to the Lamb shift of hydrogen and muonic hydrogen. Within heavy baryon effective theory (HBET) coupled to pions, nucleons, deltas, and leptons, the hadronic contributions to the spin-independent delta-potential matching coefficient are computed, including the Zemach correction (with full dependence on the pion mass and the delta-nucleon mass splitting) and polarizability corrections (with logarithmic accuracy). The matching between HBET and NRQED is performed at scales between the pion mass and the proton mass, then further matched to pNRQED at scales below the lepton-nucleon Bohr radius.

## Key numbers

- The Zemach correction to the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift (n=2) is -0.01922 meV when delta contributions are included, compared to -0.01008 meV in the limit Δ → ∞ (pure pion contribution).
- The Zemach correction to the hydrogen Lamb shift (n=2) is -3.49652 Hz with delta contributions, compared to -1.83404 Hz in the pure pion limit.
- The polarizability correction to the hydrogen Lamb shift (n=2) is -77.6037/n³ Hz including delta contributions, compared to -64.4841/n³ Hz in the pure pion limit.
- The leading pion-loop contribution to the derivative of the hadronic vacuum polarization at zero momentum is Π′_{h,π}(0) = α/(30π) × 1/(4m_π²).
- The point-like contribution to the matching coefficient c_{3,NR} is δc_{3,point-like} = (m_p/m_{l_i}) × (ln(m_{l_i}²/ν²) + 1/3) in the MS scheme.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach previously applied to hyperfine splitting in hydrogen and muonic hydrogen (Pineda 2004, HF) to the Lamb shift, and addresses the proton radius definition problem raised by Pachucki (1999). The chiral perturbation theory computation of the Zemach correction provides a model-independent result that reproduces about half the phenomenological dipole-model estimate (Pachucki 1999) from pure pions, with delta contributions bringing the total close to the phenomenological value. The polarizability corrections are compared with experimental proton polarizabilities (Karshenboim 1997) and with earlier chiral calculations (Bernard 1992, Hemmert 1999). The work enables a consistent separation of hadronic and electromagnetic contributions to the proton radius at the precision required by upcoming muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurements at PSI.
