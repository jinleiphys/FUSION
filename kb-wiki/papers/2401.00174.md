# Spin current generation due to differential rotation

> We study nonequilibrium spin dynamics in differentially rotating systems, deriving an effective Hamiltonian for conduction electrons in the comoving frame. In contrast to conventional spin current generation mechanisms that require vorticity, our theory describes spins and spin currents arising from differentially rotating systems regardless of vorticity. We demonstrate the generation of spin currents in differentially rotating systems, such as liquid metals with Taylor-Couette flow. Our alternative mechanism will be important in the development of nanomechanical spin devices.

## Key claim

The paper claims that spin currents can be generated in differentially rotating systems even when the vorticity is zero or uniform, which is a condition where conventional spin-vorticity coupling mechanisms would not produce any spin current. The mechanism relies on the spatial gradient of the angular velocity rather than the vorticity gradient required by prior theories.

## Method

The authors derive an effective Hamiltonian for conduction electrons in a comoving frame that follows the differential rotation, introducing emergent gauge fields from the time and spatial derivatives of the rotation angle. They compute the spin current in linear response to these gauge fields using nonequilibrium Green's functions with Born approximation for impurity scattering, including ladder vertex corrections. The calculation yields a spin transport equation showing that the spin current arises from diffusion of the spin density generated by the generalized Barnett effect.

## Key numbers

- For Taylor-Couette flow in (Ga,In)Sn liquid metal with conductivity σ₀ = 3.26 × 10⁶ (Ω m)⁻¹, gap d ∼ 1 μm, and outer cylinder rotation Ω₂ ∼ 10² kHz: estimated spin current in charge current units ejₛ ∼ 1.07 × 10² A m⁻²
- For a copper cantilever with conductivity σ₀ = 6.45 × 10⁷ Ω⁻¹ m⁻¹, Lamé constant μ = 48.3 GHz, mass density ρₘ = 8.96 × 10³ kg/m³, torsion angle φ₀ ∼ 0.01, and aspect ratio b/l = 1/4: total spin current J₁ ∼ 0.15 μA for the first torsional oscillation mode
- Spin relaxation time τₛ = 9τ/8ℏ²λₛₒ²k_F⁴, where τ = 1/2γ is the momentum relaxation time
- Spin diffusion length lₛ = √(Dτₛ) with diffusion constant D = v_F²τ/3

## Context

This work extends the Barnett effect (Barnett 1915, Einstein 1915) from rigidly rotating systems to differentially rotating systems, and contrasts with prior spin-vorticity coupling mechanisms proposed by Matsuo (2013, 2017) and Takahashi (2016) that require vorticity gradients or time-dependent vorticity. The mechanism enables spin current generation in systems such as Taylor-Couette flow where the vorticity is constant, and in torsional cantilever oscillations (Wallis 2006, Harii 2019), broadening the scope for nanomechanical spin devices and micro-electromechanical systems (MEMS).
