# Quarkonia and QGP studies

> We summarize results of recent studies of heavy quarkonia correlators and spectral functions at finite temperatures from lattice QCD and systematic T-matrix studies using QCD motivated finite-temperature potentials. We argue that heavy quarkonia dissociation shall occur in the temperature range $1.2 \le T_d/T_c \le 1.5$ by the interplay of both screening and absorption in the strongly correlated plasma medium. We discuss these effects on the quantum mechanical evolution of quarkonia states within a time-dependent harmonic oscillator model with complex oscillator strength and compare the results with data for $R_{\rm AA}/R_{\rm AA}^{\rm CNM}$ from RHIC and SPS experiments. We speculate whether the suppression pattern of the rather precise NA60 data from In-In collisions may be related to the recently discovered X(3872) state. Theoretical support for this hypothesis comes from the cluster expansion of the plasma Hamiltonian for heavy quarkonia in a strongly correlated medium.

## Key claim

Heavy quarkonia dissociation in a quark-gluon plasma occurs in the temperature range 1.2 ≤ T_d/T_c ≤ 1.5 through the interplay of both screening and absorption, rather than through screening alone; the authors further speculate that a dip in the NA60 In-In suppression data may be related to the X(3872) state via a resonance-like strengthening of the heavy-quark potential near the chiral/deconfinement transition.

## Method

The authors employ a time-dependent harmonic oscillator (THO) model with complex oscillator strength to describe the quantum mechanical evolution of quarkonia in an expanding QGP fireball. The temperature evolution is given by longitudinal Bjorken hydrodynamic expansion with entropy conservation, using an entropy density parametrized from lattice QCD results from Borsanyi (2010). The complex oscillator strength ω²(τ) = ω_R²(τ) + iω_I²(τ) encodes screening (real part) and absorption/dissociation (imaginary part), and the survival probability R_AA/R_AA^CNM is computed from the time evolution operator via path integral methods and compared to experimental data from NA50, NA60, and PHENIX.

## Key numbers

- Dissociation temperature range: 1.2 ≤ T_d/T_c ≤ 1.5
- Mott temperature for charmonium below 1.4 T_c
- Initial time τ_0 = 0.6 fm/c for RHIC experiments, 1.0 fm/c for SPS experiments
- Entropy density parametrization: s(T) = 9.0 T³ [1 + tanh((T - T_0)/(0.54 T))] with T_0 = 0.189 GeV
- The dip in NA60 In-In data occurs at dN_ch/dη ∼ 150 - 250
- Traditional threshold for onset of anomalous suppression at dN_ch/dη ∼ 300
- Thermal dissociation of Υ may occur at T ∼ 250 MeV while its Mott temperature is ∼ 470 MeV

## Context

This work extends the classical picture of sequential quarkonium suppression via the Mott effect (Digal, 2001) by incorporating both screening and absorption on equal footing within a T-matrix approach (Cabrera, 2006; vanHees, 2007; Riek, 2010). The THO model generalizes the earlier harmonic oscillator model of Matsui (1989) to include complex time-dependent oscillator strengths. The speculation linking the NA60 In-In dip to the X(3872) state builds on cluster expansion techniques for the plasma Hamiltonian (Blaschke, 2009) and on earlier work on quark exchange processes for charmonium dissociation (Martins, 1994; Wong, 1999). This framework enables future analysis of LHC heavy-ion data and refined understanding of charmonium regeneration in strongly correlated QGP.
