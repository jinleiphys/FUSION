# Statistical Properties of 56-57Fe with Multi-Shell Degrees of Freedom

> We investigate the statistical properties of 56-57Fe within a model capable of treating all the nucleons as active in an infinite model space including pairing effects. Working within the canonical ensemble, our model is built on single-particle states, thus it includes shell and subshell closures. We include spin correlation parameters to account for nucleon spin multiplicities. Pairing and shell effects are important in the low-temperature region where the quantum effects dominate. The validity of the model is extended to higher temperatures, approaching the classical regime, by including continuum states. We present results for excitation energy, heat capacity and level density of 56-57Fe. The resulting favorable comparisons with experimental data and with other approaches, where applicable, indicate that the methods we develop are useful for statistical properties of medium to heavy nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their Statistical Mechanics using Single Particle States (SMSPS) method, which treats all nucleons as active in an infinite model space with pairing effects, produces favorable comparisons with experimental data and other approaches for the statistical properties of 56-57Fe, but this agreement is conditional on including a phenomenological pairing energy shift and selecting an appropriate continuum volume (radius equal to 2.3 times the mean nuclear radius) to match the binding energy per nucleon.

## Method

The authors construct a canonical ensemble partition function from single-particle (SP) states generated by a Woods-Saxon mean field potential, including a temperature-dependent pairing gap applied only to states above the Fermi level. They use a configuration-restricted recursion technique that separates positive and negative magnetic projections to enforce average spin-paired configurations, then compute excitation energy, heat capacity, and level density from the total nuclear partition function. The model space is expanded from a 4-orbital valence shell to 11 orbitals and finally to a no-core space of 18 orbitals plus continuum states, with continuum states treated as a spherical cavity of radius 2.3 times the mean nuclear radius.

## Key numbers

- For 56Fe, the pairing gap Δ is constant at 2.01 MeV for T ≤ 0.885 MeV, then drops rapidly until T = 1 MeV, and decreases slowly for T > 1 MeV.
- The heat capacity exhibits a prominent peak near T = 0.8 MeV, identified as "Total Valence Melting" (TVM), and a second maximum at T = 8.8 MeV marking the transition from resonance to continuum states.
- The continuum volume radius is set to 2.3 times the mean nuclear radius (R = 4.78 fm for 56Fe, R = 4.81 fm for 57Fe, using r0 = 1.25 fm).
- The classical heat capacity limit is 3A/2, giving 84 for 56Fe and 85.5 for 57Fe.
- The energy spacing between the 1p3/2 and 0f5/2 single-neutron states is 0.78 MeV.
- The backshift parameter δ = 1.38 MeV is used for 56Fe and δ = 0 MeV for 57Fe in the Bethe backshifted formula comparison.
- The level density parameter a = 8 MeV−1 for 56Fe and a = 8.143 MeV−1 for 57Fe in the Bethe backshifted formula.

## Context

This work extends the statistical spectroscopy approach of Nakada and Alhassid (1997) using the Shell Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method and the Monte Carlo shell model calculations of Rombouts, Heyde, and Jachowicz (1998) for 56Fe, by incorporating all nucleons as active and including continuum states to reach higher temperatures. The SMSPS method enables computation of thermal properties for medium to heavy nuclei with significantly reduced computational cost compared to SMMC, and the results enable future applications to nuclear astrophysics scenarios such as neutron star mergers and iron-rich stellar environments.
