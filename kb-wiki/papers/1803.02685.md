# Reconstruction of light-cone parton distribution functions from lattice QCD simulations at the physical point

> We present the unpolarized and helicity parton distribution functions calculated within lattice QCD simulations using physical values of the light quark mass. Non-perturbative renormalization is employed and the lattice data are converted to the MSbar-scheme at a scale of 2 GeV. A matching process is applied together with target mass corrections leading to the reconstruction of light-cone parton distribution functions. For both cases we find a similar behavior between the lattice and phenomenological data, and for the polarized PDF a nice overlap for a range of Bjorken-x values. This presents a major success for the emerging field of direct calculations of quark distributions using lattice QCD.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD simulations at the physical pion mass, combined with non-perturbative renormalization and matching, can produce unpolarized and helicity parton distribution functions that show similar behavior to phenomenological data, with the polarized PDF showing a "nice overlap" for a range of Bjorken-x values, but only for the highest nucleon momentum of 1.38 GeV and with residual unphysical oscillations at large x.

## Method

The authors computed matrix elements of non-local fermionic operators with a finite-length Wilson line between boosted nucleon states on a single gauge ensemble with two degenerate light quarks at maximal twist, using the Iwasaki improved gluon action and twisted mass fermions with clover improvement at the physical pion mass. They applied non-perturbative renormalization in the RI' scheme, converted to the MSbar scheme at 2 GeV, performed a Fourier transform to extract quasi-PDFs, and then applied a one-loop perturbative matching procedure together with target mass corrections to reconstruct light-cone PDFs for three nucleon momenta.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.0938(3)(2) fm
- Pion mass: m_pi = 0.1304(4) GeV (approximately physical)
- Nucleon mass: m_N = 0.932(4) GeV
- Three nucleon momenta used: 0.83 GeV (6pi/L), 1.11 GeV (8pi/L), and 1.38 GeV (10pi/L)
- Number of measurements: 4800-9600 for 6pi/L, 38250 for 8pi/L, 58950 for 10pi/L
- Renormalization scale: mu = 2 GeV in the MSbar scheme
- Lattice volume: 48^3 x 96, with L = 4.5 fm and m_pi L = 2.98(1)

## Context

This work extends the quasi-PDF approach proposed by Ji (2013) and subsequent refinements by Xiong (2013), Chen (2016), Wang (2017), Stewart (2017), and Izubuchi (2018), and is the first lattice calculation of unpolarized and helicity PDFs at the physical pion mass with non-perturbative renormalization. It directly improves upon earlier work by Alexandrou (2016) at m_pi = 375 MeV (the B55 ensemble), demonstrating that the physical pion mass is crucial for agreement with phenomenological data from CJ15, ABMP16, NNPDF31 (unpolarized) and DSSV08, JAM17 (polarized). The results enable future investigations of discretization and volume effects, and improved treatment of unphysical oscillations in the Fourier transform.
