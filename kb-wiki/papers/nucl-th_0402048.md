# QCD sum rules at finite density in the large-N_c limit: The coupling of the rho-nucleon system to the D_{13}(1520)

> QCD sum rules are studied for the vector-isovector current at finite baryon density in the limit of large number of colors N_c. For the condensate side it is shown that in this limit the four-quark condensate factorizes also for the finite density case. At the hadronic side the medium dependence is expressed in terms of the current-nucleon forward scattering amplitude. Generalizing vector meson dominance we allow for a direct coupling of the current to the nucleon as well as a coupling via the rho meson. We discuss the N_c dependence of (a) modifications of the pion cloud of the rho meson, (b) mixing with other mesons (in particular a_1 and omega) and (c) resonance-hole excitations R N^{-1}. We show that only the last effect survives in the large-N_c limit. Saturating the sum rules with a simple hadronic ansatz which allows for the excitation of the D_{13}(1520) we determine the coupling of the latter to the rho-nucleon and the photon-nucleon system. These couplings are hard to determine from vacuum physics alone.

## Key claim

The authors show that in the large-\(N_c\) limit, only resonance-hole excitations (specifically the \(D_{13}(1520)\)) survive as the leading in-medium modification of the \(\rho\)-meson spectral function, while pion-cloud modifications and meson mixing are suppressed; this allows them to extract the coupling of the \(D_{13}(1520)\) to the \(\rho\)-nucleon and photon-nucleon systems from QCD sum rules, a determination that is otherwise model-dependent from vacuum data alone.

## Method

The authors study QCD sum rules for the vector-isovector current at finite baryon density in the large-\(N_c\) limit, using the linear density approximation. They analyze the \(N_c\) scaling of each term on the condensate side (four-quark condensate, gluon condensate, nucleon structure function moments) and on the hadronic side (pion-cloud effects, meson mixing, resonance-hole excitations). They then derive weighted finite energy sum rules (Eqs. 13–15) that are insensitive to the continuum threshold modeling, and saturate them with a hadronic ansatz that includes only the \(D_{13}(1520)\) resonance coupled to the \(\rho\)-nucleon system via a non-relativistic Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum \(\rho\)-meson mass \(m_V = 770\ \text{MeV}\) (scales as \(N_c^0\)).
- The nucleon mass \(m_N = 940\ \text{MeV}\) (scales as \(N_c\)).
- The sigma term \(\sigma_N = 45\ \text{MeV}\) (scales as \(N_c\)).
- The vacuum quark condensate \(\langle \bar q q \rangle_{\text{vac}} = (-240\ \text{MeV})^3\) (scales as \(N_c\)).
- The continuum threshold \(s_0 \approx 1.3\ \text{GeV}^2\) (scales as \(N_c^0\)).
- The \(D_{13}(1520)\) mass \(m_{D13} = 1520\ \text{MeV}\) (scales as \(N_c\)); the mass difference \(m_{D13} - m_N = 580\ \text{MeV}\) (scales as \(N_c^0\)).
- The pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92\ \text{MeV}\) (scales as \(\sqrt{N_c}\)).
- The strong coupling \(\alpha_s = 0.36\) (scales as \(1/N_c\)).
- The nucleon structure function moments: \(a_2 = 0.9\) (scales as \(N_c^0\)), \(a_4 = 0.12\) (scales as \(1/N_c^2\)).

## Context

This work extends the in-medium QCD sum rule approach for the \(\rho\)-meson channel, which had previously suffered from large model dependence on the hadronic side (e.g., Hatsuda (1992) predicted a mass drop; Klingl (1997) found broadening without mass shift). By applying large-\(N_c\) scaling arguments, the authors show that only resonance-hole excitations (like the \(D_{13}(1520)\)) contribute at leading order, suppressing pion-cloud and mixing effects. This enables a determination of the \(\rho\)-nucleon-\(D_{13}\) coupling from sum rules, complementing earlier hadronic model analyses by Peters (1998) and Post (2003) that relied on two-pion production data. The method also parallels the work of Friman (1999) on momentum-dependent sum rules but focuses on \(\rho\)-mesons at rest and uses \(N_c\)-scaling as a new QCD-based constraint.
