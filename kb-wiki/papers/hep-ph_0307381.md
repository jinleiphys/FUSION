# Decay Probability Ratio of Pentaquark Theta^+ State

> The pentaquark state of $Θ^{+}(uudd\bar{s})$ has been observed to decay with two decay modes: $Θ^+\to n K^+$ and $Θ^+ \to p K^0$. The decay probability ratio of the two decay modes is studied with general symmetry consideration of isospin, spin, and parity. We arrive at a result of the ratio $\frac{Γ(Θ^+\to nK^+)}{Γ(Θ^+\to pK^0)} =\frac{(α-β)^2}{(α+β)^2}(\frac{k_1}{k_2})^{2L+1}$, which is valid for the $Θ^+$ state to be a pure isoscalar or isovector state, or an isoscalar and isovector mixing state, or an isotensor state with mixture of isoscalar and isovector components with coefficients $α$ and $β$. The dependence on spin and parity of the pentaquark $Θ^+$ state is found to be small due to small difference between the center of mass decay momenta $k_1$ and $k_2$ of the two decay modes. We also provide an analysis on the constraint of the isospin of $Θ^+$ from the absence of a peak in the $pK^+$ invariant mass distribution in the $γp \to pK^+K^-$ process. Future experimental results about the decay probability ratio may provide information about the properties of the pentaquark $Θ^+$ state.

## Key claim

The decay probability ratio of the two observed decay modes of the pentaquark Theta^+ state, Gamma(Theta^+ -> n K^+) / Gamma(Theta^+ -> p K^0), is derived from general symmetry considerations of isospin, spin, and parity, and is expressed as a function of two mixing coefficients alpha and beta, with a small correction from the spin-parity dependent factor (k1/k2)^(2L+1). The authors note that the dependence on spin and parity is small due to the small difference between the center-of-mass decay momenta of the two modes.

## Method

The authors constructed the general isospin configuration of the Theta^+ state as a linear combination of isotensor (I=2), isovector (I=1), and isoscalar (I=0) components with coefficients a, alpha, and beta. They expanded the Theta^+ state in terms of the final-state isospin wavefunctions for the n K^+ and p K^0 channels using Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, assuming isospin conservation in the strong decay. The decay probability ratio was then obtained from the squared amplitudes of the isospin projections, and a centrifugal barrier factor (k1/k2)^(2L+1) was included to account for the dependence on the orbital angular momentum L and the center-of-mass decay momenta k1 and k2 of the two decay modes.

## Key numbers

*   The Theta^+ mass is taken as 1541 MeV, yielding a center-of-mass decay momentum k1 = 283.1 MeV for the Theta^+ -> n K^+ mode and k2 = 279.6 MeV for the Theta^+ -> p K^0 mode.
*   For a mixing scenario with alpha = 0.98 and beta = 0.02, the isospin factor (alpha - beta)^2 / (alpha + beta)^2 is approximately 0.92.
*   For a larger isospin breaking with beta approximately 0.05, the isospin factor (alpha - beta)^2 / (alpha + beta)^2 is approximately 0.81.
*   The spin-parity dependent factor (k1/k2)^(2L+1) ranges from 1.013 for L=0 (J^P = 1/2^-) to 1.091 for L=3 (J^P = 5/2^+).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical discussion following the experimental observations of the Theta^+ pentaquark by the LEPS, DIANA, CLAS, SAPHIR, and HERMES collaborations (2003). It addresses the unresolved properties of the Theta^+, such as its spin, isospin, and parity, which were debated in the context of predictions from the chiral quark soliton model by Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997) and the isotensor hypothesis proposed by Capstick, Page, and Roberts (2003) to explain its narrow width. The derived ratio provides a testable prediction that can help distinguish between the isoscalar, isovector, and isotensor assignments for the Theta^+ state, and it can also reveal isospin breaking effects in its decays.
