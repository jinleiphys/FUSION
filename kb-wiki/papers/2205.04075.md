# Mixing effects on 1S and 2S state heavy mesons in the light-front quark model

> The mass spectra and wave functions of both $1S$ and $2S$ state heavy pseudoscalar ($P$) and vector ($V$) mesons are analyzed within the light-front quark model. Important empirical constraints employed in our analysis of the mass spectra and wave functions are the experimental mass-gap relation, $ΔM_P > ΔM_V$, where $ΔM_{P(V)}=M^{2S}_{P(V)}-M^{1S}_{P(V)}$ and the hierarchy of the decay constants, $f_{1S}>f_{2S}$, between $1S$ and $2S$ meson states. We maintain the orthogonality of the trial wave functions of the $1S$ and $2S$ states in our variational calculation of the Hamiltonian with the Coulomb plus confining potentials and treat the hyperfine interaction perturbatively for the heavy-heavy and heavy-light $P$ and $V$ mesons due to the nature of the heavy quark symmetry. Realizing that the empirical constraints cannot be satisfied without mixing of the $1S$ and $2S$ states, we find the lower bound of the mixing angle $θ$ between $1S$ and $2S$ states as $θ_c = {\rm cot}^{-1}(2\sqrt{6})/2\simeq 6^{\circ}$ and obtain the optimum value of the mixing angle around $12^\circ$ to cover both the charm and bottom flavors of the heavy quark. The mixing effects are found to be more significant to the $2S$ state mesons than to the $1S$ state mesons. The properties of $1S$ and $2S$ state mesons including the mass spectra, decay constants, twist-2 distribution amplitudes, and electromagnetic form factors are computed. Our results are found to be in a good agreement with the available data and lattice simulations. In particular, the $2S$ state pseudoscalar $D_s$ meson is predicted to have a mass of $2600$ MeV, which is very close to the mass of the newly discovered $D_{s0}(2590)^+$ meson by the LHCb Collaboration. This supports the interpretation of the observed state as a radial excitation of the $D^+_s$ meson.

## Key claim

The authors find that mixing between the 1S and 2S harmonic oscillator basis states is necessary to satisfy the empirical constraints that the mass gap between pseudoscalar mesons exceeds that between vector mesons (ΔM_P > ΔM_V) and that the decay constant hierarchy f_1S > f_2S holds; without such mixing, the pure harmonic oscillator wave functions produce the opposite ordering.

## Method

The authors use a light-front quark model with a QCD-motivated effective Hamiltonian containing kinetic energy, linear confining potential, Coulomb potential, and a perturbatively treated hyperfine interaction. They perform a variational calculation using trial wave functions that are linear combinations (mixing) of the 1S and 2S harmonic oscillator basis states, maintaining orthogonality between the resulting 1S and 2S wave functions. Model parameters including constituent quark masses, potential parameters, and the mixing angle are determined by fitting to the masses of η_b(1S) and B*(1S) mesons and satisfying the empirical constraints on mass gaps and decay constants.

## Key numbers

- The lower bound of the mixing angle θ between 1S and 2S states is determined as θ_c = cot^{-1}(2√6)/2 ≃ 6°
- The optimum mixing angle is found to be around 12° to cover both charm and bottom flavors
- The 2S state pseudoscalar D_s meson is predicted to have a mass of 2600 MeV, close to the D_{s0}(2590)^+ meson mass of 2591 ± 6 ± 7 MeV observed by LHCb
- For the pure (θ=0°) scenario, the D_s(2S) mass is 2546 MeV; for the mixed (θ=12°) scenario it is 2600 MeV
- The χ² value for mass predictions is 0.009 for the mixed scenario and 0.024 for the pure scenario
- The mass gap difference ΔM_P - ΔM_V is 62 MeV for (D,D*), 64 MeV for (η_c, J/Ψ), and 37 MeV for (η_b, Υ) using measured masses

## Context

This work extends previous light-front quark model analyses of ground state mesons by Choi and Ji (2007, 2009) and Choi et al. (2015) to include radially excited 2S states, addressing the reverse ordering problem of decay constants (f_1S < f_2S) found in earlier harmonic oscillator basis calculations for Υ(b\bar{b}) systems by Kim et al. (2010). The results support the interpretation of the D_{s0}(2590)^+ discovered by LHCb as a standard quark-antiquark radial excitation, contrary to predictions from the relativized quark model of Godfrey and Isgur (1985) and the relativistic quark model of Ebert et al. (2002, 2009) which gave higher masses around 2680-2688 MeV. The approach enables systematic computation of decay constants, distribution amplitudes, and electromagnetic form factors for both 1S and 2S heavy meson states.
