# Production of two $c \bar c$ pairs in double-parton scattering

> We discuss production of two pairs of $c \bar c$ within a simple formalism of double-parton scattering (DPS). Surprisingly very large cross sections, comparable to single-parton scattering (SPS) contribution, are predicted for LHC energies. Both total inclusive cross section as a function of energy and differential distributions for $\sqrt{s}$ are shown. We discuss a perspective how to identify the double scattering contribution.

## Key claim

The authors predict that at LHC energies, the double-parton scattering (DPS) contribution to inclusive charm production becomes comparable to the conventional single-parton scattering (SPS) contribution, a situation they describe as "completely new," but they note that strong dependence on factorization and renormalization scales produces almost order-of-magnitude uncertainties that preclude a more precise estimation.

## Method

The authors compute leading-order (LO) cross sections for single-parton scattering production of a single \(c \bar c\) pair via gluon-gluon fusion, then use a factorized probabilistic model for double-parton scattering where the DPS cross section is given by \(\sigma^{DPS} = \frac{1}{2 \sigma_{eff}} \sigma^{SPS} \cdot \sigma^{SPS}\) with \(\sigma_{eff} = 15\) mb. They employ several LO collinear gluon distribution sets (GRV94, CTEQ6, GJR08, MSTW08) and present total cross sections as a function of center-of-mass energy as well as differential distributions in rapidity, transverse momentum, rapidity difference, invariant mass, and pair transverse momentum at \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV.

## Key numbers

- At LHC energies, the DPS cross section for two \(c \bar c\) pairs is predicted to be comparable to the SPS cross section for a single \(c \bar c\) pair.
- The effective cross section parameter used is \(\sigma_{eff} = 15\) mb, taken from Tevatron data.
- At \(\sqrt{s} = 7\) TeV, the DPS contribution produces a broad distribution in the transverse momentum of the \(c \bar c\) pair extending to large values, in contrast to the LO SPS prediction which is a Dirac delta function.
- The ALICE forward muon spectrometer covers pseudorapidity interval \(2.5 < \eta < 4\), which combined with the central detector allows pseudorapidity differences up to 5, a region where DPS is expected to dominate.
- The CMS detector has a lower transverse momentum threshold of about 1.5 GeV at \(\eta = \pm (2 - 2.4)\).

## Context

This work extends earlier DPS studies from the 1970s and 1980s (e.g., Landshoff and Polkinghorne 1978, Takagi 1979) which had found negligible contributions at lower energies, and builds on more recent DPS estimates (e.g., Del Fabbro and Treleani 1996, Korotkikh and Snigirev 2000, Bahr et al. 2009). The authors note that the production of \((c \bar c)(c \bar c)\) four-parton final states via DPS had not been carefully discussed before, and they identify correlation observables such as rapidity difference and pair transverse momentum as promising signatures for experimental identification at LHC detectors like ATLAS, CMS, and ALICE. The results also have implications for high-energy atmospheric and cosmogenic neutrino production, referencing earlier work by Gelmini et al. (1996) and Enberg et al. (2008).
