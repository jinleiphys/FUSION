# Drag and diffusion co-efficients of heavy quarks in hard thermal loop approximations

> The drag and diffusion coefficients of heavy quarks propagating through quark gluon plasma (QGP) have been evaluated using Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) approximations. The HTL corrections to the relevant propagators and vertices have been considered. It is observed that the magnitudes of both the transport coefficients are changed significantly from values obtained by earlier approaches where either (i) the $t$ channel divergence in T=0 pQCD matrix element is shielded simply by Debye mass. or (ii) only HTL resummed propagator is used ignoring the HTL corrections at the interaction vertices. The implications of these changes in the transport coefficients on the heavy ion phenomenology have been discussed.

## Key claim

The drag and diffusion coefficients of heavy quarks in quark-gluon plasma are significantly larger when both the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) resummed gluon propagator and the HTL corrections to the three-gluon and quark-gluon vertices are included, compared to earlier calculations that either used only a static Debye mass cutoff or omitted vertex corrections.

## Method

The authors evaluated the drag and diffusion coefficients for charm and beauty quarks propagating through a thermal QGP by computing the squared matrix elements for the elastic scattering processes Q+q -> Q+q and Q+g -> Q+g using HTL-approximated gluon propagators and one-loop HTL-corrected three-gluon and quark-gluon vertices. The transport coefficients were obtained from the Fokker-Planck equation using the Landau approximation, with the momentum and temperature dependence computed for heavy quarks of mass m_c and m_b at temperatures relevant to RHIC and LHC.

## Key numbers

- At T = 400 MeV and p = 1 GeV, the HTL drag coefficient for charm is about 2 times the value obtained with bare propagators and vertices (γ_HTL ≈ 2γ).
- At T = 400 MeV and p = 1 GeV, the HTL drag coefficient for bottom is about 40% larger than the bare value (γ_HTL ≈ 1.4γ).
- At T = 300 MeV and p = 5 GeV, the HTL drag coefficient for charm is about 3 times the bare value (γ_HTL ≈ 3γ).
- At T = 300 MeV and p = 1 GeV, the HTL diffusion coefficient for charm is almost 2.5 times the bare value (D_HTL ≈ 2.5D).
- At T = 300 MeV and p = 1 GeV, the HTL diffusion coefficient for bottom is about 1.4 times the bare value (D_HTL ≈ 1.4D).
- At T = 300 MeV and p = 5 GeV, the HTL diffusion coefficient for charm is 3.3 times the bare value (D_HTL ≈ 3.3D).

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Heiselberg (1993) and Wang (2001) that used only a static Debye mass to regulate the t-channel divergence, and by Rapp (2009) that omitted HTL vertex corrections. The results show that including both HTL propagator and vertex corrections substantially increases the drag and diffusion coefficients, which has direct implications for understanding heavy flavor suppression (R_AA) and elliptic flow (v_2) measurements at RHIC and LHC experiments.
