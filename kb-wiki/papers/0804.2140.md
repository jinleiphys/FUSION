# Universal Charge-Radius Relation for Subatomic and Astrophysical Compact Objects

> Electron-positron pair creation in supercritical electric fields limits the net charge of any static, spherical object, such as superheavy nuclei, strangelets, and Q-balls, or compact stars like neutron stars, quark stars, and black holes. For radii between $4\times10^2$ fm and $10^4$ fm the upper bound on the net charge is given by the universal relation $Z=0.71R_{fm}$, and for larger radii (measured in fm or km) $Z = 7 \times 10^{-5} R_{fm}^2 = 7 \times 10^{31} R_{km}^2$. For objects with nuclear density the relation corresponds to $Z \approx 0.7 A^{1/3}$ ($10^{8} < A < 10^{12}$) and $Z \approx 7\times10^{-5} A^{2/3}$ ($A > 10^{12}$), where $A$ is the baryon number. For some systems this universal upper bound improves existing charge limits in the literature.

## Key claim
Electron-positron pair creation in supercritical electric fields imposes a universal upper bound on the net charge of any static, spherical object, but the bound depends on whether infinite time is available for pair formation; for radii above about 10⁴ fm the finite time available for pair creation raises the allowed charge from a linear to a quadratic dependence on radius.

## Method
The author derived the charge limit analytically using a relativistic Thomas-Fermi model following the approach of Müller and Rafelski (1975), where the effective chemical potential is set to the top of the negative energy sea to represent maximal filling of electron states from vacuum decay. Poisson's equation was solved numerically for two idealized core charge distributions, a uniformly charged sphere and a uniformly charged spherical shell, to confirm the universal relation. Finite-time effects were incorporated using the Schwinger pair-creation rate to derive a timescale-dependent charge limit.

## Key numbers
- For radii between 4×10² fm and 10⁴ fm: Z = 0.71 R_fm (infinite time limit).
- For radii larger than 10⁴ fm (finite time, τ = 1 s): Z = 7×10⁻⁵ R_fm² = 7×10³¹ R_km².
- For objects at nuclear density with 10⁸ < A < 10¹²: Z ≈ 0.7 A¹/³.
- For objects at nuclear density with A > 10¹²: Z ≈ 7×10⁻⁵ A²/³.
- The transition between regimes occurs at R_fm ≈ 1.0×10⁴ and A ≈ 10¹².
- The characteristic width of the screening electron cloud is of order m_e⁻¹ ≈ 4×10² fm.
- For τ = 1 s: Z_1s ≈ 11.2 ξ², where ξ = m_e R.
- For τ = 10¹⁰ years, the numerical prefactors are divided by 2.0; for τ = 10⁻¹⁰ s, they are multiplied by 2.4.

## Context
This work extends the earlier treatment of supercritical field screening by Müller and Rafelski (1975) and Greiner, Müller, and Rafelski (1985) to arbitrary spherical objects, providing a universal charge-radius relation that applies across scales from superheavy nuclei to black holes. For strangelets, the result improves upon earlier charge-mass relations from Heiselberg (for non-color-flavor locked strangelets with A ≲ 10⁷) and Madsen (2001) for color-flavor locked strangelets, and it reproduces a result for massive black holes from Ruffini. The finite-time limit is less restrictive than gravitational stability limits for neutron stars from Glendenning, but for self-bound strange stars the universal limit could in principle be saturated.
