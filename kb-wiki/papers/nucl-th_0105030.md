# Order parameter fluctuations and thermodynamic phase transitions in finite spin systems and fragmenting nuclei

> We show that in small and low density systems described by a lattice gas model with fixed number of particles the location of a thermodynamic phase transition can be detected by means of the distribution of the fluctuations related to an order parameter which is chosen to be the size of the largest fragment. We show the correlation between the size of the system and the observed order of the transition. We discuss the implications of this correlation on the analysis of experimental fragmentation data.

# Order parameter fluctuations and thermodynamic phase transitions in finite spin systems and fragmenting nuclei

## Key claim
The distribution of fluctuations of the largest fragment size can detect a thermodynamic phase transition in small, low-density systems described by a lattice gas model, but the apparent scaling behaviour and the inferred order of the transition depend on system size: for small systems (linear size L ≤ 40) the fluctuations show scale invariance characteristic of a continuous transition, while for larger systems (L ≥ 50) the first-order nature of the thermodynamic transition becomes manifest and scaling fails.

## Method
The authors used the Ising Model with Fixed Magnetization (IMFM), a lattice gas model in the canonical ensemble with fixed particle number, to simulate finite nuclear systems at various densities and temperatures. They computed the specific heat, the second moment of the fragment size distribution, and the probability distribution of the largest fragment size A_max, then tested for scale invariance of the fluctuation distribution Φ(z) = ⟨A_max⟩^Δ P(A_max) as a function of system size at fixed density.

## Key numbers
- For ρ = 0.13 and L = 10, 20, 30, the caloric curve shows no clear signature of transition order; first-order behaviour appears only for L ≥ 60, consistent with microcanonical calculations of Pleimling and Hüller.
- At T_f = 4.5 MeV, the tail exponent of the fluctuation distribution is ν̃ = 1.78 ± 0.20 for ρ = 0.50 and ν̃ = 1.15 ± 0.15 for ρ = 0.30, compared with ν̃ = 1.6 ± 0.4 from experimental Xe+Sn data of Botet and Ploszajczak.
- At ρ = 0.13 and T_f = 3.85 MeV, scaling with Δ = 1 holds for systems with L = 10, 20, 30, 40; when temperature is smeared over T = 3.85 ± 0.20 MeV, scaling survives for L = 10 and 20 but fails for L ≥ 30.
- A system with L = 10 at ρ = 0.3 corresponds to 300 particles; L = 20 at ρ = 0.13 corresponds to 1040 particles; experimental Xe+Sn data involve around 245 particles.

## Context
This work extends the scaling analysis of order parameter fluctuations proposed by Botet and Ploszajczak (2000) for detecting continuous phase transitions in finite systems, applying it to the lattice gas model (LGM) and its canonical fixed-magnetization variant (IMFM) previously developed by the authors. The results connect to experimental caloric curve measurements (Pochodzalla, 1995; D'Agostino, 1999) and fragment size distribution analyses (Campi, 1994; Gulminelli, 1999), and show that the apparent continuous transition observed in small systems (L ≤ 40) gives way to first-order behaviour for larger systems, consistent with microcanonical simulations by Pleimling and Hüller (2000). The work enables experimental selection of events with scaling properties to locate phase transition points in the (ρ, T) plane, but cautions that the scaling signature may be lost for systems much larger than typical nuclei or with large temperature dispersion.
