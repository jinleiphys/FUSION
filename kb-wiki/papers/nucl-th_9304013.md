# Electromagnetic Scattering from Relativistic Bound States

> The quasipotential formalism for elastic scattering from relativistic bound states is formulated based on the instant constraint in the Breit frame. The quasipotential electromagnetic current is derived from Mandelstam's five-point kernel and obeys a two-body Ward identity. Breit-frame wave functions are obtained directly by solving integral equations with nonzero total three-momentum, thus accomplishing a dynamical boost. Calculations of electron-deuteron elastic form factors illustrate the importance of the dynamical boost versus kinematic boosts of the rest frame wave functions.

## Key claim

The quasipotential formalism for elastic electromagnetic scattering from relativistic bound states can be formulated using an instant constraint in the Breit frame, which yields a gauge-invariant current that obeys a two-body Ward identity, but the required Breit-frame wave functions must be obtained by a dynamical boost (solving integral equations with nonzero total three-momentum) rather than by a kinematic boost of rest-frame wave functions.

## Method

The authors start from the Mandelstam five-point function and apply a quasipotential reduction using the instant constraint \(p^0 = p'^0 = 0\) in the Breit frame, where the photon momentum satisfies \(q^0 = 0\). They derive a conserved impulse-approximation current operator from the box and crossed-box five-point diagrams, using a one-boson-exchange potential with scalar, pseudo-vector, and vector mesons (sigma, delta, eta, pi, omega, rho) with modified Bonn B parameters. The Breit-frame deuteron wave functions are obtained by solving the homogeneous quasipotential equation in three dimensions using a Dirac plane-wave basis, with the Malfliet-Tjon iteration procedure for \(M_J = 0\) states and raising/lowering operators for polarized states.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy increases from approximately 2.2 MeV at \(\mathbf{q}^2 = 0\) to approximately 4.2 MeV at \(\mathbf{q}^2 = 200\ \mathrm{fm}^{-2}\) when the potential scaling factor \(\lambda(\mathbf{q}^2) = 1\) is used (i.e., without adjusting the potential to maintain the correct deuteron total energy).
- The meson-exchange-current coupling constants used are \(g_{\rho\pi\gamma} = 0.563\), \(g_{\omega\sigma\gamma} = -0.4\), and \(g_{\omega\eta\gamma} = -0.206\).
- The potential scaling factor \(\lambda(\mathbf{q}^2)\) varies linearly over a wide range of \(q^2\) values to produce the correct deuteron total energy \(\epsilon_D = (M_D^2 + \mathbf{q}^2/4)^{1/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the quasipotential formalism of Blankenbecler and Sugar (1966) and Logunov and Tavkhelidze (1963) and the Dirac two-body propagator approach of Mandelzweig and Wallace (1989) by formulating the instant constraint in the Breit frame for electromagnetic interactions. It addresses the long-standing problem of boosting three-dimensional wave functions with the instant constraint, showing that kinematic boosts of rest-frame wave functions (as used in some previous formulations by Faustov (1972) and Jaus (1976, 1984)) produce significantly different results for deuteron electromagnetic form factors compared to the dynamical boost calculated directly in the Breit frame. The formalism enables gauge-invariant calculations of electron-deuteron elastic scattering with consistent treatment of particle-exchange symmetry and four-momentum conservation.
