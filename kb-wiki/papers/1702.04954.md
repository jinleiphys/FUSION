# Influence of target deformation and deuteron breakup in (d,p) transfer reactions

> The effect of core excitations in transfer reactions of the form A(d, p)B has been found to affect significantly the calculated cross sections and to depend strongly and non-linearly on the incident deuteron energy in Faddeev/AGS calculations. Our goal is to investigate these effects within a coupled-channels formulation of the scattering problem which, in addition of being computationally less demanding than the Faddeev counterpart, may help shed some light into the physical interpretation of the cited effects. We use an extended version of the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method with explicit inclusion of target excitations within a coupled-channels Born approximation (CDCC-BA) formulation of the transfer transition amplitude. We compare the calculated transfer cross sections with those obtained with an analogous calculation omitting the effect of target excitation. We consider also an adiabatic coupled channels (ACC) method. Our working example is the 10 Be(d,p) 11 Be reaction. We find that both formalisms are able to reproduce the effects of deformation found in Faddeev calculations, whose origin stems from the interference of the direct one-step transfer, and the two-step transfer following target excitation.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the strong, energy-dependent reduction of the calculated (d,p) transfer cross section due to target deformation, previously found in Faddeev/AGS calculations, can be reproduced by a coupled-channels Born approximation (CDCC-BA) method that includes both deuteron breakup and target excitations; the reduction originates from destructive interference between the direct one-step transfer and the two-step transfer that proceeds via target excitation, not from a simple scaling of spectroscopic factors.

## Method

The authors extended the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method to include target excitations explicitly, using a coupled-channels Born approximation (CDCC-BA) for the transfer transition amplitude. They applied this formalism to the 10Be(d,p)11Be reaction at several deuteron laboratory energies, comparing full calculations with calculations that selectively omitted specific transfer paths (elastic, inelastic, breakup, and inelastic-breakup transfer). They also compared results with an adiabatic coupled channels (ACC) method and with prior Faddeev/AGS calculations.

## Key numbers

* The 11Be ground state wave function contains three components with spectroscopic factors: S = 0.846 for the s1/2 configuration, 0.130 for d5/2, and 0.023 for d3/2.
* The 10Be first excited state (2+) has an energy of 3.368 MeV and a deformation length of delta2 = 1.664 fm.
* The ratio Rx (defined as the peak differential cross section with deformation divided by that without deformation, normalized by the spectroscopic factor of the 0+ component) was computed at deuteron energies from approximately 10 to 60 MeV; the full CDCC-BA calculation reproduced the Faddeev results, with Rx values deviating significantly from unity (e.g., Rx ~0.6 at 60 MeV).
* At Ed = 20 MeV, the main contributor to the transfer cross section after elastic transfer is deuteron breakup (path III), while at Ed = 60 MeV the second main contributor is target excitation (path II).

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev/AGS calculations of Delaunay et al. (2013, 2015, 2016), who found that core excitations in (d,p) reactions produce strong, energy-dependent effects on cross sections that are not captured by simple spectroscopic factor scaling. The CDCC-BA method provides a computationally less demanding alternative to Faddeev calculations and clarifies the physical origin of the effect as interference between one-step and two-step transfer paths. The method enables future analyses of transfer reactions induced by weakly-bound projectiles, including halo nuclei, where both breakup and target excitation are important.
