# Effects of ground-state correlations on magnetic dipole excitations in $^{40}$Ca

> The effects of ground-state correlations on the magnetic dipole excitations in $^{40}$Ca are studied using an extended random phase approximation (ERPA) derived from the time-dependent density-matrix theory. Comparison is made with other extended RPA approaches, the renormalized RPA, the self-consistent RPA and the extended second RPA which also include the effects of ground-state correlations. It is pointed out that direct excitations from two particle - two hole space which are properly treated in ERPA cause strong magnetic dipole transitions in $^{40}$Ca.

## Key claim

The extended random phase approximation (ERPA) properly treats direct excitations from the two particle–two hole (2p–2h) space, which cause strong magnetic dipole transitions in \(^{40}\)Ca that are not captured by other extended RPA approaches; however, the analysis remains qualitative because the interactions used were simple and self-consistency was not fully respected.

## Method

The author derived an extended random phase approximation (ERPA) from the small-amplitude limit of the time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM), solving coupled equations for one-body and two-body transition amplitudes that include ground-state correlations via the occupation probability \(n_\alpha\) and the correlated part of the two-body density matrix \(C_2\). The ground state was obtained as a stationary solution of the TDDM equations using a truncated single-particle basis with the Skyrme III force, and the M1 strength distributions were calculated for \(^{40}\)Ca and compared with results from the renormalized RPA (rRPA), self-consistent RPA (SCRPA), and extended second RPA (ESRPA).

## Key numbers

* The most collective state in ERPA appears at 8.7 MeV with \(B(M1)\uparrow = 1.83\ \mu_0^2\), compared to the experimental state at 10.32 MeV with \(1.17 \pm 0.06\ \mu_0^2\).
* In rRPA, the state at 9.1 MeV has \(4.32\ \mu_0^2\) and consists mainly of the neutron \(1f_{7/2} \rightarrow 1f_{5/2}\) component (84.9% contribution to normalization); the state at 8.5 MeV has \(0.67\ \mu_0^2\) with 85.0% proton \(1f_{7/2} \rightarrow 1f_{5/2}\) component.
* SCRPA gives a collective state at 14.3 MeV with \(4.07\ \mu_0^2\), with proton and neutron \(1f_{7/2} \rightarrow 1f_{5/2}\) contributions of 38.5% and 59.7%, respectively.
* The summed \(B(M1)\uparrow\) values are 5.63 \(\mu_0^2\) in rRPA, 4.53 \(\mu_0^2\) in SCRPA, and 12.6 \(\mu_0^2\) in ERPA; the experimental summed value for the two observed M1 states is \(1.4\ \mu_0^2\).
* The transition strengths in rRPA and SCRPA exhaust 57% and 65% of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) value from the spin-orbit force, respectively; the ERPA strength fulfills 75% of the EWSR value including contributions from both the spin-orbit force and the \(t_0(1+x_0 P^\sigma)\) term.

## Context

This work extends the application of ERPA, previously used for electric dipole and quadrupole excitations in oxygen and calcium isotopes (Tohyama 2007, 2017), to magnetic dipole transitions in \(^{40}\)Ca. It contrasts with the renormalized RPA (Rowe 1968), self-consistent RPA (Hirata 2000), and extended second RPA (Nishi 2012) by including the coupling to two-body amplitudes and the diagonal part of one-body amplitudes, which produce strong fragmentation and collectivity. The results enable future quantitative studies using realistic interactions with appropriate spin-isospin properties to better reproduce experimental excitation energies and transition strengths.
