# Scattering of a spin-1/2 particle off a spin-0 target in a simple three-dimensional basis

> Scattering of a spin-1/2 particle off a spin-0 target is formulated based on a simple three-dimensional momentum-spin basis. The azimuthal behaviour of both the potential and the T-matrix elements leads to a set of integral equations for the T-matrix elements in two variables only, namely the momentum's magnitude and the scattering angle. Some symmetry relations for the potential and the T-matrix elements reduce the number of the integral equations to be solved by a factor of one half. A complete list of the spin observables in terms of the two-dimensional T-matrix elements is presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents a formulation of spin-1/2 + spin-0 scattering using a three-dimensional momentum-spin basis that is simpler than the basis used in prior work by Irga (no year given), and yields integral equations whose solutions can be directly used to calculate observables, which was not the case in that prior work.

## Method

The authors define a 3D basis state as a direct product of a free momentum state and a spin-1/2 state quantized along the z-axis. Using this basis, they derive a Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the T-matrix elements, assume a general potential structure with two spin-independent functions, and exploit the azimuthal behavior of the potential and T-matrix elements to reduce the integral equations to depend only on momentum magnitude and scattering angle. Symmetry relations further reduce the number of coupled integral equations to be solved by a factor of one half, requiring only the two equations for T_{1/2,1/2} and T_{-1/2,1/2}.

## Key numbers

- The integral equations for the T-matrix elements are reduced to depend on only **two variables**: momentum magnitude and scattering angle.
- The number of integral equations to be solved is reduced by a factor of **one half** using symmetry relations.
- The spin-averaged differential cross section is given by: I_0 = (4π²μ)² [ |T_{1/2,1/2}(p,p,θ')|² + |T_{-1/2,1/2}(p,p,θ')|² ]
- Polarization and analyzing power are equal: P_y = A_y = (2/I_0)(4π²μ)² Im{ T*_{1/2,1/2}(p,p,θ') T_{-1/2,1/2}(p,p,θ') }

## Context

This work extends the three-dimensional (3D) technique for scattering calculations, which was previously applied to boson systems by Thomas (no year given) and Liu (no year given), to two-nucleon scattering by Holz (no year given), Rice (no year given), NN3D (no year given), and Golak (no year given), and to three-nucleon scattering by Gloeckle (no year given). The paper specifically improves upon the formulation by Irga (no year given) for the same spin-1/2 + spin-0 system by using a simpler basis and providing integral equations whose solutions directly yield observables. This enables efficient calculation of kaon-nucleon scattering at higher energies where partial-wave methods become cumbersome due to the need for many angular momentum states.
