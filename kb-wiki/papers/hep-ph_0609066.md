# Drell-Yan production and Lam-Tung relation in the Color Glass Condensate formalism

> We study the Drell-Yan production cross section and structure functions in proton (deuteron)-nucleus collisions using the Color Glass Condensate formalism. The nucleus is treated in the Color Glass Condensate framework which includes both higher twist effects due to the inclusion of multiple scatterings and leading twist pQCD shadowing due to the small x resummation, while the proton (or deuteron) is treated within the DGLAP improved parton model. In particular, the Drell-Yan structure functions are used in order to investigate the Lam-Tung relation at small x, which is known to be identically zero at leading twist up to Next-to-Leading order, and is thus a good playground for studying higher twist effects. In agreement with this, we find that violations of this relation are more important for low momentum and invariant mass of the Drell-Yan pair, and also in the region of rapidity that corresponds to smaller values of x in the nucleus.

## Key claim

The Lam-Tung relation, which is identically zero at leading twist up to next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD, receives non-zero contributions from higher-twist effects in the Color Glass Condensate formalism; violations are largest for low transverse momentum and invariant mass of the Drell-Yan pair and in rapidity regions corresponding to small momentum fraction \(x\) in the nucleus.

## Method

The authors compute the Drell-Yan production cross section and hadronic tensor for proton(deuteron)-nucleus collisions using a hybrid approach: the proton is described by DGLAP-evolved parton distributions, while the nucleus is treated as a Color Glass Condensate, resumming multiple scatterings via Wilson lines. The hadronic tensor is decomposed into structure functions \(W_{TL}, W_L, W_\Delta, W_{\Delta\Delta}\), from which the angular coefficients \(A_0, A_1, A_2\) are derived; the Lam-Tung relation \(A_0 = A_2\) is then tested. The dipole cross section needed for the nuclear correlator is obtained both from numerical solutions of the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation (with an initial saturation scale \(Q_s^2 = 2\ \text{GeV}^2\) at \(x_2^0 = 10^{-2}\)) and from two phenomenological parameterizations, KKT and DHJ.

## Key numbers

* The initial saturation scale for the BK evolution is set to \(Q_s^2 = 2\ \text{GeV}^2\) at a reference momentum fraction \(x_2^0 = 10^{-2}\).
* The extrapolation of the dipole correlator for \(x_2 > x_2^0\) uses a factor \((1-x_2)^4 / (1-x_2^0)^4\) based on quark counting rules.
* The BK equation is solved with a fixed coupling constant \(\overline{\alpha}_s \equiv \alpha_s N_c / \pi\), chosen to reproduce the growth rate of the saturation scale inferred from geometrical scaling at HERA.

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate treatment of photon and dilepton production in proton-nucleus collisions (Gelis and Jalilian-Marian, 2002; 2003) to the specific case of the Lam-Tung relation, which is known to vanish at leading twist up to \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s)\) (Lam and Tung, 1978; 1980). The study provides quantitative predictions for the PHENIX experiment at RHIC and for CMS and ATLAS at the LHC, and enables tests of whether the suppression pattern seen in forward hadron production at RHIC (predicted by saturation models such as KKT and DHJ) also appears in electromagnetic probes, thereby distinguishing saturation from alternative mechanisms like parton recombination or cold-matter energy loss.
