# Decomposition of jet fragmentation function in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Based on a multi-phase transport model, the measured jet fragmentation function ratio of Pb+Pb collisions to p+p collisions in CERN Large Hadron Collider experiments is decomposed into two parts, corresponding to the two contributions of jet hadronization from fragmentation and coalescence. The results suggest an existence of distinct competitions between two jet hadronization mechanisms for different $ξ$=ln(1/$z$) ranges in different centrality bins. The jet fragmentation functions for different types of hadrons (mesons and baryons) are proposed as a good probe to study the competition between fragmentation and coalescence for the jet hardonization in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The measured jet fragmentation function ratio in Pb+Pb collisions can be decomposed into two components from fragmentation and coalescence hadronization mechanisms, with the competition between them varying with $\xi$ and centrality; however, the decomposition relies on the assumption that the contribution factors $\lambda_f$ and $\lambda_c$ are independent of $\xi$ and that the functional forms of $R_f(\xi)$ and $R_c(\xi)$ can be taken from specific stages of the AMPT model.

## Method

The study uses the AMPT model with string-melting scenario to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}$ = 2.76 TeV, triggering dijets of $p_T \sim$ 90 GeV/$c$ via the HIJING initial condition. Jets are reconstructed with the anti-$k_t$ algorithm (cone size 0.3, $p_T >$ 100 GeV/$c$, $0.3 < |\eta| < 2$), and the jet fragmentation function D($\xi$) is obtained for charged hadrons with $p_T >$ 1 GeV/$c$ inside the jet cone. The measured CMS ratio $R(\xi) = D_{Pb+Pb}(\xi)/D_{p+p}(\xi)$ is then fitted with a two-component formula $R(\xi) = \lambda_f R_f(\xi) + \lambda_c R_c(\xi)$, where $R_f(\xi)$ is taken from the AMPT result after parton cascade and $R_c(\xi)$ from after hadronic rescatterings.

## Key numbers

- Jet cone size set to 0.3; jet $p_T >$ 100 GeV/$c$; charged hadron $p_T >$ 1 GeV/$c$; pseudorapidity range $0.3 < |\eta| < 2$ for jet analysis.
- For 0-10% centrality: $\lambda_f = 0.377 \pm 0.147$, $\lambda_c = 0.612 \pm 0.120$.
- For 10-30% centrality: $\lambda_f = 0.346 \pm 0.156$, $\lambda_c = 0.616 \pm 0.131$.
- For 30-50% centrality: $\lambda_f = 0.599 \pm 0.168$, $\lambda_c = 0.386 \pm 0.137$.
- For 50-100% centrality: $\lambda_f = 0.379 \pm 0.370$, $\lambda_c = 0.527 \pm 0.338$.
- Two partonic interaction cross sections used: 0 mb (hadronic interactions only) and 1.5 mb (partonic + hadronic interactions).

## Context

This work extends the AMPT model studies of jet observables at LHC, following earlier successful descriptions of $\gamma$-jet imbalance (Ma 2013) and dijet asymmetry (Ma 2013). It contrasts with models that assume only parton-hadron duality or Lund string fragmentation, such as JEWEL (Zapp 2012) and the linearized Boltzmann transport model (Wang and Zhu 2013), by explicitly including both fragmentation and coalescence hadronization mechanisms. The decomposition approach enables future measurements of jet fragmentation functions for different hadron types (mesons vs. baryons) to probe the competition between these two hadronization mechanisms in heavy-ion collisions.
