# First Principles Prediction of the Landau Parameter for Fermi Liquids near the Unitarity Limit

> This paper explores the behavior of systems of cold fermions as they approach unitarity above the critical temperature. As we move away from unitarity, by decreasing the scattering length, the dilaton, the Goldstone boson resulting from the spontaneous breaking of Schrodinger symmetry by the Fermi sea, becomes gapped. At energies below this gap, the interaction between quasi-particles will be dominated by local interactions generated by off-shell dilaton exchange. The dilaton mass can, in turn, be related via anomaly matching, to the scattering length and contact parameter within the confines of a systematic expansion. We use this relation to predict the s-wave Landau parameter to be $f=\frac{4πa (2ε(p_F)-p_F^2/m_\star)^2 m}{3p_F^4 \tilde{C}(a)}$ where $a$ is the scattering length, $m$ the atomic mass, $m_\star$, the effective mass which can be extracted from heat capacity, and $\tilde {C}(a)$ is the dimensionless contact parameter. The range of validity of this prediction (given in eq.(21)) is determined by the value of contact parameter and Fermi velocity, which depend upon the scattering length. It is expected to be valid in a range above $k_F a \sim 1$, but the actual window will depend upon the values of aforementioned parameters. Given this result for $f$, we predict the compressibility, spin susceptibility and the quasi-particle life-time.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the s-wave Landau parameter for a Fermi liquid near (but not at) unitarity, where the dilaton (the pseudo-Goldstone boson from broken scale invariance) acquires a small mass. The prediction is valid only in a narrow window above \(k_F a \sim 1\) where dilaton exchange dominates the quasi-particle interaction, and the result depends on the scattering length, effective mass, and contact parameter in a non-perturbative way.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory for fermions and a dilaton field using the spacetime coset construction to non-linearly realize the broken Schrödinger symmetry (boosts, dilatations, and special conformal transformations) at the unitary point. They then perturb away from unitarity by introducing a finite scattering length, which gives the dilaton a mass; this mass is determined by matching the conformal anomaly commutator between the effective theory and the microscopic four-fermion theory. The dilaton is integrated out to generate an effective four-fermion interaction, from which the s-wave Landau parameter, compressibility, spin susceptibility, and quasi-particle lifetime are derived.

## Key numbers

* The predicted s-wave Landau parameter is \(f = \frac{4\pi a (2\epsilon(p_F)-p_F^2/m_\star)^2 m}{3p_F^4 \tilde{C}(a)}\), where \(a\) is the scattering length, \(m\) the atomic mass, \(m_\star\) the effective mass, and \(\tilde{C}(a)\) the dimensionless contact parameter.
* The dilaton mass is related to the contact density via \(m_\phi^2 \Lambda^2 = \frac{3}{4\pi a} \mathcal{C}(a)\), where \(\mathcal{C}(a)\) is the contact density.
* The compressibility is \(\kappa = \kappa_0 / \left(1 - \frac{1}{3\pi} \frac{m^\star}{m} \frac{k_F a \, \delta E^2}{\tilde{C}(a) E_F^2}\right)\), where \(\kappa_0\) is the free Fermi gas compressibility and \(\delta E = 2\epsilon(k_F) - v_F k_F\).
* The spin susceptibility is \(\chi = \chi_0 / \left(1 + \frac{1}{3\pi} \frac{m^\star}{m} \frac{k_F a \, \delta E^2}{\tilde{C}(a) E_F^2}\right)\), where \(\chi_0\) is the free Fermi gas susceptibility.
* The regime of validity for the prediction requires \(\tilde{\mathcal{C}}(a) (E_F/(E,T)_{\text{max}})^2 \gg a k_F \gg \tilde{\mathcal{C}}(a) (E_F/\delta E)^2 k_F f_0\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of broken Schrödinger symmetry in Fermi liquids at unitarity by Rothstein and Shrivastava (2018, 2020), who showed that the unitary limit cannot be described by a canonical Fermi liquid and that a dilaton (or a dynamical inverse Higgs constraint) must be present. By moving slightly away from unitarity, the authors make the dilaton massive and thus recover Fermi liquid behavior, enabling first-principles predictions for the Landau parameter in a regime where weak-coupling \(k_F a\) expansions (e.g., Hammer (2004)) fail. The results allow experimental tests via measurements of compressibility, spin susceptibility, and quasi-particle lifetime in uniform quantum gases near a Feshbach resonance.
