# Thermodynamics of dense hadronic matter in a parity doublet model

> We study thermodynamics of nuclear matter in a two-flavored parity doublet model within the mean field approximation. Parameters of the model are chosen to reproduce correctly the properties of the nuclear ground state. The model predicts two phase transitions in nuclear matter, a liquid-gas phase transition at normal nuclear density and a chiral transition at higher density. At finite temperature the pion decay constant exhibits a considerable reduction at intermediate values of chemical potential, which is traced back to the presence of the liquid-gas transition, and approaches zero at higher chemical potential associated with the chiral symmetry restoration. A "transition" from meson-rich to baryon-rich matter is also discussed.

## Key claim

The parity doublet model predicts two distinct phase transitions in dense hadronic matter, a liquid-gas transition at normal nuclear density and a chiral transition at higher density, but the order and location of the chiral transition depend strongly on the mass of the negative-parity nucleon partner, with a heavier partner (1500 MeV) giving a weak first-order transition at about 10 times saturation density and a lighter partner (1200 MeV) giving only a crossover at about 3 times saturation density.

## Method

The authors apply a two-flavor parity doublet model within the mean field approximation, introducing a negative-parity nucleon field that transforms oppositely under chiral rotations to allow a chirally invariant mass term \(m_0\). They fix model parameters (couplings \(a\), \(b\), \(g_\omega\), meson masses, and the chiral invariant mass \(m_0\)) to reproduce the nuclear matter saturation point at zero temperature: binding energy per nucleon of -16 MeV at baryon density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\). The thermodynamic potential is then minimized to obtain the sigma field expectation value and omega meson field as functions of temperature and baryon chemical potential, from which phase boundaries are extracted.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) with binding energy \(E/A(\rho_0) - m_{N+} = -16\) MeV.
- Liquid-gas phase transition at zero temperature occurs at baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B = 923\) MeV.
- For parameter set A (negative-parity mass \(m_{N-} = 1500\) MeV), a weak first-order chiral transition occurs at \(\rho \sim 10\,\rho_0\).
- For parameter set B (\(m_{N-} = 1200\) MeV), a chiral crossover occurs at \(\rho \sim 3\,\rho_0\).
- The liquid-gas transition survives up to temperature \(T = 27\) MeV.
- The chiral crossover at zero chemical potential occurs at \(T_c \sim 190\) MeV in the parity doublet model.
- The meson-to-baryon transition line (where \(\rho_M/\rho_B = 1\)) intersects the chiral crossover at \((T,\mu_B) \sim (150, 450)\) MeV.
- Incompressibility \(K\) ranges from 430–510 MeV for the two parameter sets with \(g_4=0\).

## Context

This work extends the parity doublet model, previously applied to vacuum phenomenology and nuclear matter at zero temperature (Detar and Kunihiro, 1989; Jido et al., 2000), to finite temperature and density, providing a unified description of the liquid-gas transition and chiral symmetry restoration. The model contrasts with nucleonic Nambu–Jona-Lasinio models (Bentz and Thomas, 2001) which predict unrealistically high chiral crossover temperatures (\(\sim 510\) MeV) at zero chemical potential, while the parity doublet model yields \(T_c \sim 190\) MeV, more consistent with lattice QCD predictions. The results enable further study of the quarkyonic matter concept (McLerran and Pisarski, 2007) and the possible triple point where hadronic, quarkyonic, and quark-gluon plasma phases may coexist (Andronic et al., 2006).
