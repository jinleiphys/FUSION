# Combined Description of $\bf{\overline{N}N}$ Scattering and Annihilation With A Hadronic Model

> A model for the nucleon-antinucleon interaction is presented which is based on meson-baryon dynamics. The elastic part is the $G$-parity transform of the Bonn $NN$ potential. Annihilation into two mesons is described in terms of microscopic baryon-exchange processes including all possible combinations of $π,η,ρ,ω,a_0,f_0,a_1,f_1,a_2,f_2,K,K^*$. The remaining annihilation part is taken into account by a phenomenological energy- and state independent optical potential of Gaussian form. The model enables a simultaneous description of nucleon-antinucleon scattering and annihilation phenomena with fair quality.

## Key claim
The paper presents a hadronic model for nucleon-antinucleon (N̅N) scattering and annihilation that achieves a "fair quality" simultaneous description of both scattering and annihilation data using only about 10 energy-independent free parameters, but the authors acknowledge that remaining discrepancies, especially in spin observables, indicate important physics is still missing.

## Method
The elastic N̅N interaction is taken as the G-parity transform of the full Bonn NN potential. Annihilation into two mesons is described microscopically via baryon-exchange processes including all combinations of π, η, ρ, ω, a₀, f₀, a₁, f₁, a₂, f₂, K, and K* mesons. The remaining annihilation contribution is represented by a phenomenological energy- and state-independent optical potential of Gaussian form with two parameters (W = -1 GeV, r₀ = 0.4 fm). The coupled channels framework yields both N̅N → N̅N and N̅N → M₁M₂ amplitudes consistently, with form factor parameters at annihilation vertices adjusted to reproduce empirical annihilation data.

## Key numbers
- The phenomenological optical potential uses parameters W = -1 GeV and r₀ = 0.4 fm, independent of energy, spin, and isospin.
- The cutoff mass for nucleon exchange in baryon-exchange diagrams is Λ_N = 1.5 GeV, mainly determined from annihilation channels involving π, ρ, and ω.
- The model includes 53 annihilation channels for two-meson final states, with the largest contributions from combinations of two vector mesons.
- The effective microscopic model C of Ref. [NNbI] gives charge-exchange cross sections about a factor of 2 too large compared to empirical data.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous models A(BOX) and C (Mull and Holinde, 1994) by considerably enlarging the set of explicitly included meson channels beyond the earlier limited set (π, ρ, ω, σ, δ, K, K*). It follows the coupled channels approach first applied to N̅N physics by Liu and Tabakin (1980s), who used about 20 parameters for ππ and K̅K channels. The model enables a consistent treatment of both N̅N scattering and annihilation into specific two-meson channels, providing a framework to test whether conventional hadronic dynamics can describe short-range N̅N interactions without invoking explicit quark-gluon effects.
