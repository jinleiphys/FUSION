# Explicit inclusion of nonlocality in (d,p) transfer reactions

> Background: Traditionally, nucleon-nucleus optical potentials are made local for convenience. In recent work we studied the effects of including nonlocal interactions explicitly in the final state for (d,p) reactions, within the distorted wave Born approximation. Purpose: Our goal in this work is to develop an improved formalism for nonlocal interactions that includes deuteron breakup and to use it to study the effects of including nonlocal interactions in transfer (d,p) reactions, in both the deuteron and the proton channel. Method: We extend the finite-range adiabatic distorted wave approximation to include nonlocal nucleon optical potentials. We apply our method to (d,p) reactions on 16O, 40Ca, 48Ca, 126Sn, 132Sn, and 208Pb at 10, 20 and 50 MeV. Results: We find that nonlocality in the deuteron scattering state reduces the amplitude of the wave function in the nuclear interior, and shifts the wave function outward. In many cases, this has the effect of increasing the transfer cross section at the first peak of the angular distributions. This increase was most significant for heavy targets and for reactions at high energies. Conclusions: Our systematic study shows that, if only local optical potentials are used in the analysis of experimental (d,p) transfer cross sections, the extracted spectroscopic factors may be incorrect by up to 40% due to the local approximation.

# Explicit inclusion of nonlocality in (d,p) transfer reactions

## Key claim
If only local optical potentials are used in the analysis of experimental (d,p) transfer cross sections, the extracted spectroscopic factors may be incorrect by up to 40% due to the local approximation, but this effect depends strongly on target mass, beam energy, and whether nonlocality is included in the entrance channel, exit channel, or both.

## Method
The authors extended the finite-range adiabatic distorted wave approximation (ADWA) to include nonlocal nucleon optical potentials of the Perey-Buck form, then applied this formalism to (d,p) reactions on six targets (16O, 40Ca, 48Ca, 126Sn, 132Sn, and 208Pb) at three deuteron energies (10, 20, and 50 MeV). They compared full nonlocal calculations with local phase-equivalent (LPE) calculations that reproduce the same elastic scattering, and also isolated the effects of nonlocality in the deuteron channel only versus the proton channel only.

## Key numbers
- Spectroscopic factors extracted using only local optical potentials may be incorrect by up to 40% due to the local approximation.
- For 208Pb(d,p)209Pb at 50 MeV, the full nonlocal calculation increased the cross section at the first peak by 64.8% relative to the local calculation.
- For 208Pb(d,p)209Pb at 10 MeV, the full nonlocal calculation increased the cross section at the first peak by 52.5% relative to the local calculation.
- For 132Sn(d,p)133Sn at 50 MeV, the full nonlocal calculation decreased the cross section at the first peak by 10.9% relative to the local calculation.
- For 48Ca(d,p)49Ca at 50 MeV, the full nonlocal calculation increased the cross section at the first peak by 41.9% relative to the local calculation.
- For 16O(d,p)17O (1d5/2 state) at 10 MeV, the full nonlocal calculation increased the cross section at the first peak by 27.2% relative to the local calculation.
- For 40Ca(d,p)41Ca (1f7/2 state) at 10 MeV, the full nonlocal calculation increased the cross section at the first peak by 48.5% relative to the local calculation.
- For 208Pb(d,p)209Pb at 50 MeV, including nonlocality only in the entrance channel increased the cross section by 86.5% relative to the local calculation.
- For 132Sn(d,p)133Sn at 50 MeV, including nonlocality only in the exit channel decreased the cross section by 26.2% relative to the local calculation.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Titus (2014) and Ross (2015) that investigated nonlocality in (p,d) reactions using DWBA, but only included nonlocality in the proton channel. The present study upgrades that work by replacing DWBA with ADWA (which includes deuteron breakup to all orders) and by including nonlocality in both the deuteron and proton channels. The results are compared with the energy-shift method proposed by Timofeyuk (2013) for effectively incorporating nonlocality in the deuteron channel. The systematic study across six targets and three energies provides a benchmark for understanding when the local approximation is most unreliable, enabling improved extraction of spectroscopic factors from experimental (d,p) data.
