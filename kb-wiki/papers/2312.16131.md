# Exact modulated hadronic tubes and layers at finite volume in a cloud of $π$ and $ω$ mesons

> We construct topological soliton solutions describing baryonic tubes and layers with modulation in the $SU(2)$ non-linear sigma model coupled with $ω$-mesons in $3+1$ dimensions. Using appropriate Asäntze for the pionic matter field and the $ω$-mesons vector potential, the complete set of seven coupled partial differential equations can be solved analytically. These solutions represent modulated tubes and layers at finite volume with arbitrary baryon number, where the modulation of the solitons in one direction is determined by one of the three degrees of freedom of the pionic field, satisfying the equation of a two-dimensional free massless chiral scalar field. As expected, the inclusion of the $ω$-mesons to the Non-linear sigma model allows to reduce the repulsion energy between baryons, which leads to a flattening of the tubes and layers in one direction, forming a kind of ``nuclear linguine phase''. Also, we show that this construction can be carried out even when higher order terms in the large $N_c$ expansion are included -- in particular the Skyrme term -- without spoiling the integrability of the field equations.

## Key claim

The paper constructs exact analytical topological soliton solutions describing modulated baryonic tubes and layers in the SU(2) non-linear sigma model coupled with omega-mesons, but only under the restrictive condition that the pionic degree of freedom G satisfies the equation of a two-dimensional free massless chiral scalar field (projecting out one of the two light-cone modes), rather than the full wave equation.

## Method

The authors use two distinct Ansatze for the SU(2) pionic field: the Euler angles representation for baryonic layers and the exponential (hedgehog) representation for baryonic tubes, combined with a specific Ansatz for the omega-meson vector potential that satisfies a decoupling condition. The metric is taken as a flat box with adimensional spatial coordinates ranging from 0 to 2 pi in x and z and 0 to pi in y, with size parameters L_x, L_y, L_z. The field equations reduce to a solvable system: a linear function for the layer profile H(x), an elliptic ODE for the tube profile alpha(x), a wave equation for G(t,z) that is further restricted to a chiral condition, and a Poisson-type equation for the omega-meson profile.

## Key numbers

- Baryon number for layers: B = p q, where p and q are integers from boundary conditions on G and the y-coordinate dependence respectively
- Baryon number for tubes: B = n p, where n is the integer from boundary conditions on alpha and p from G
- The omega-meson profile for layers: u(x) = -12 gamma L_x (2n+1) p q / [L_y L_z (4 L_x^4 M_omega^2 + (2n+1)^2)] sin((n+1/2)x)
- The parameter Q in the tube Ansatz must be an odd integer to ensure non-vanishing baryon number
- For the figures, the authors set K=1, L_x = L_y = L_z = 1, and for the comparison plots K=2, gamma=0.1, p=q=1, M_omega=0

## Context

This work extends the analytical construction of topological soliton crystals at finite volume from the non-linear sigma model and Skyrme model, previously developed by Alvarez, Hidalgo, Barriga, and others (crystal1-crystal5, SU(N)1, SU(N)2, Torres), to include coupling with omega-mesons. The inclusion of omega-mesons reduces the repulsion energy between baryons, as previously shown by Adkins, Jackson, and Meissner, leading to a flattening of the tubes into oval cross-sections that the authors call a "nuclear linguine phase." The solutions are also shown to remain valid when higher-order terms such as the Skyrme term are included, without spoiling integrability. This enables analytical study of nuclear pasta phases (lasagna and spaghetti) expected in neutron star crusts and supernovae cores, which previously had only been studied via numerical simulations.
