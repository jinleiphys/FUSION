# Quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions from coherent energy loss in cold nuclear matter

> The effect of parton energy loss in cold nuclear matter on the suppression of quarkonia (J/psi, Upsilon) in heavy-ion collisions is investigated, by extrapolating a model based on coherent radiative energy loss recently shown to describe successfully J/psi and Upsilon suppression in proton-nucleus collisions. Model predictions in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC (Au-Au, Cu-Cu, and Cu-Au) and LHC (Pb-Pb) show a sizable suppression arising from the sole effect of energy loss in cold matter. This effect should thus be considered in order to get a reliable baseline for cold nuclear matter effects in quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions, in view of disentangling hot from cold nuclear effects.

## Key claim

The paper argues that parton energy loss in cold nuclear matter, specifically coherent radiative energy loss, produces a sizable suppression of quarkonia (J/ψ, Υ) in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and that this effect must be accounted for as a baseline before attributing additional suppression to hot medium effects; the model extrapolates from successful descriptions of proton-nucleus data without invoking nuclear parton distribution functions as the dominant mechanism.

## Method

The authors generalize their coherent energy loss model from proton-nucleus to nucleus-nucleus collisions by treating rescattering in projectile and target nuclei as independent processes that populate distinct rapidity regions, then combining them probabilistically via a double convolution of quenching weights with the proton-proton production cross section. The effective path length in each nucleus is computed using the Glauber model as a function of centrality, and the transport coefficient q̂₀ is fixed from fits to J/ψ suppression in p-W collisions at fixed-target energies.

## Key numbers

- J/ψ suppression in central Au-Au collisions at RHIC (√s = 200 GeV): R_AuAu ≈ 0.75 at y = 0 from energy loss alone, compared to measured R_AuAu ∼ 0.2–0.4
- J/ψ suppression in central Cu-Cu collisions at RHIC: R_CuCu ≈ 0.8 at y = 0 from energy loss alone
- J/ψ suppression in minimum bias Pb-Pb collisions at LHC (√s = 2.76 TeV): R_PbPb ≈ 0.7 at y = 0, decreasing to ≈ 0.55 at |y| ∼ 5 from energy loss alone
- Υ suppression in minimum bias Pb-Pb collisions at LHC: R_PbPb ≈ 0.85 at y = 0, decreasing to ≈ 0.75 at |y| ∼ 5 from energy loss alone
- Transport coefficient central value: q̂₀ = 0.075 GeV²/fm, with range 0.070–0.09 GeV²/fm
- Effective path length in minimum bias Au-Au: ⟨L_eff^A⟩ = 10.21 fm; in Pb-Pb: ⟨L_eff^A⟩ = 10.11 fm
- Critical rapidity for model validity at RHIC (√s = 200 GeV): y^crit ≈ −1.2 for Au-Au
- Critical rapidity for model validity at LHC (√s = 2.76 TeV): y^crit ≈ −3.8 for Pb-Pb

## Context

This work extends the coherent energy loss model previously applied to J/ψ and Υ suppression in proton-nucleus collisions (Arleo 2012, Arleo 2013) to nucleus-nucleus collisions, providing a cold nuclear matter baseline that competes with traditional approaches based on nuclear absorption or nuclear parton distribution functions. The authors show that the approximation R_AB(y) ≈ R_pA(−y) × R_pB(+y), used empirically by the ALICE collaboration (Abelev 2013, Hadjidakis 2014), holds well in their model but differs by 10–20% in the ALICE muon acceptance. The discrepancies between the energy-loss-only predictions and heavy-ion data (stronger suppression observed at RHIC and LHC) are interpreted as evidence for additional hot medium effects (Debye screening) and, for J/ψ at LHC, possible recombination processes.
