# Statistical Properties of E(5) and X(5) Symmetries

> We study the energy level statistics of the states in E(5) and X(5) transitional dynamical symmetries. The calculated results indicate that the statistics of E(5) symmetry is regular and follows Poisson statistics, while the statistics of X(5) symmetry involves two maxima in the nearest neighbor level spacing distribution $P(s)$ and the $Δ_{3}$ statistics follows the GOE statistics. It provides an evidence that the X(5) symmetry is at the critical point exhibiting competing degree of freedom.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the E(5) symmetry exhibits regular (Poisson) level statistics, while the X(5) symmetry shows chaotic (GOE) statistics with a distinctive two-maximum structure in the nearest-neighbor spacing distribution, but this result for X(5) depends on the relative strength of the beta and gamma degrees of freedom (parameter ratio A/B ≥ 16) and the two-maximum feature only appears when the competing terms are comparable.

## Method

The authors calculated energy levels from the analytic solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian for E(5) (infinite square well in beta) and X(5) (square well in beta plus harmonic oscillator in gamma) symmetries. They unfolded the resulting spectra by fitting a smooth polynomial to the staircase function N(E) to remove the non-universal average part, then computed the nearest-neighbor spacing distribution P(S) and the spectral rigidity Δ₃(L) for various truncation parameters and relative strengths A/B of the gamma and beta terms.

## Key numbers

- For E(5) symmetry with low spin-parity J^π=0^+ states, the Brody parameter ω approaches 0 (Poisson statistics) as the number of levels increases, indicating regular behavior.
- For X(5) symmetry, the relative strength A/B is estimated to be ≥ 16 based on the "true" potential width β_w ≈ 0.8 and gamma restriction γ < 0.1.
- Empirical example ¹⁰⁴Mo gives A ≈ 800 keV and B ≈ 30 keV, yielding A/B ≥ 16 consistent with the theoretical estimate.
- For X(5) with A/B not very large, the Δ₃ statistics follows the GOE prediction Δ₃(L) ≈ (ln L − 0.0687)/π² for large L.
- For X(5) with large A/B, both P(S) and Δ₃ statistics become close to Poisson statistics (Δ₃(L) = L/15).

## Context

This work extends the study of shape phase transitions in nuclei by Iachello (2000, 2001) who proposed the E(5) and X(5) analytic symmetries at critical points. The statistical analysis confirms earlier results by Alhassid and Whelan (1991) showing that the U(5)-SU(3) transition region (corresponding to X(5)) is the most chaotic, while the U(5)-O(6) transition (corresponding to E(5)) is regular. The two-maximum feature in the X(5) spacing distribution provides a statistical signature for first-order shape phase transitions with competing degrees of freedom, which could be used to identify critical-point nuclei in future experimental studies.
