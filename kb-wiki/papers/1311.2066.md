# Spatial Wilson loops in the classical field of high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> It has been previously shown numerically that the expectation value of the magnetic Wilson loop at the initial time of a heavy-ion collision exhibits area law scaling. This was obtained for a classical non-Abelian gauge field in the forward light cone and for loops of area $A\gtrsim 2/Q_s^2$. Here, we present an analytic calculation of the spatial Wilson loop in the classical field of a collision within perturbation theory. It corresponds to a diagram with two sources, for both projectile and target, whose field is evaluated at second order in the gauge potential. The leading non-trivial contribution to the magnetic loop in perturbation theory is proportional to the square of its area.

## Key claim

The leading non-trivial perturbative contribution to the magnetic Wilson loop in the classical field of a heavy-ion collision is proportional to the square of the loop area, not the area itself; this analytic result applies only to small loops (area \(A \ll 1/Q_s^2\)) and may deviate from the lattice result for large loops which resums screening corrections.

## Method

The author computes the expectation value of the spatial (magnetic) Wilson loop at proper time \(\tau=0\) in the classical non-Abelian gauge field produced by two colliding ultra-relativistic nuclei, using perturbation theory to second order in the gauge potential. The gauge fields of projectile and target are expanded perturbatively from the pure-gauge solution, and the Wilson loop is averaged over color charge distributions using a Gaussian (McLerran-Venugopalan) action; the calculation reduces to evaluating a four-point function of the gauge potential in momentum space with an infrared cutoff \(\Lambda\).

## Key numbers

- For SU(2), the magnetic string tension from the lattice computation is \(\sigma_M \simeq 0.12 Q_s^2\) (for areas \(A \gtrsim 2/Q_s^2\)).
- The perturbative result for the Wilson loop expectation value is \(W_M(R) \simeq 1 - \frac{\pi^2 N_c^6}{64(N_c^2-1)^3} \frac{Q_{s1}^4 Q_{s2}^4}{\Lambda^4} A^2\).
- From a fit to lattice data for small areas, \(W_M(R) \simeq 1 - 2 (A Q_s^2)^2\); comparing with the analytic result for SU(2) gives \(Q_s^4 / \Lambda^4 \approx 5.477\).
- The typical radius of uncorrelated chromo-magnetic vortices is \(\sim 0.8 / Q_s\).

## Context

This work provides an analytic perturbative calculation that complements the numerical lattice study of Dumitru, Nara, and Petreska (2012), which found area-law scaling of the magnetic Wilson loop for large loops in the classical field of a collision. The perturbative result applies only to small loops and yields area-squared scaling, in contrast to the area-law behavior seen for large loops; the author notes that area-law scaling presumably requires resummation of screening effects and condensation. The calculation uses the McLerran-Venugopalan model for the color charge distributions and extends the classical field approach of Kovner, McLerran, and Weigert (1995).
