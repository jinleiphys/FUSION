# D mesons at finite temperature and density in the PNJL model

> We study D meson resonances in hot, dense quark matter within the NJL model and its Polyakov-loop extension. We show that the mass splitting between D^+ and D^- mesons is moderate, not in excess of 100 MeV. When the decay channel into quasifree quarks opens (Mott effect) at densities above twice saturation density, the decay width reaches rapidly the value of 200 MeV which entails a spectral broadening sufficient to open J/psi dissociation processes. Contrary to results from hadronic mean-field theories, the chiral quark model does not support the scenario of a dropping D meson masses so that scenarios for J/psi dissociation by quark rearrangement built on the lowering of the threshold for this process in a hot and dense medium have to be reconsidered and should account for the spectral broadening.

## Key claim

The authors show that within the PNJL model, D meson masses do not drop in hot, dense matter but instead stay almost constant or increase, contrary to predictions from hadronic mean-field theories; the spectral broadening from the Mott effect, rather than a mass shift, should be the basis for J/psi dissociation scenarios.

## Method

The authors solve the gap equations for dynamically generated light quark masses at finite temperature and chemical potential, then solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for D meson masses and widths in the complex plane. They use the NJL model and its Polyakov-loop extension (PNJL) with a four-flavor interaction kernel, employing the Matsubara formalism to generalize from vacuum to finite temperature and density.

## Key numbers

- Mass splitting between D^+ and D^- mesons is moderate, not in excess of 100 MeV.
- When the decay channel opens (Mott effect) at densities above twice saturation density, the decay width reaches rapidly the value of 200 MeV.
- The three-momentum cutoff is at Lambda = 602.3 MeV, with coupling constant G_S * Lambda^2 = 2.32.
- In vacuum, the D meson mass is 1869.3 MeV, the charm current quark mass is 1279.9 MeV, and the dynamically generated charm quark mass is 1828.8 MeV.
- The nucleon density of nuclear matter at saturation is n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends earlier exploratory NJL calculations of D mesonic correlations in quark matter (Blaschke 2003) to finite baryon densities relevant for the CBM experiment at FAIR. The results contradict the dropping-mass scenario of Sibirtsev (1999) and Fuchs (2004) from hadronic mean-field models, but support the spectral broadening picture found in self-consistent coupled channel approaches by Tolos (2007, 2009). The findings imply that J/psi suppression scenarios based on quark rearrangement (Burau 2000, Blaschke 2003) should account for spectral broadening rather than mass shifts.
