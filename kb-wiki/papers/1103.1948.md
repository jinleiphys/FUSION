# Nucleon properties in nuclear matter

> We present recent studies on the effective mass of the nucleon in infinite and homogeneous nuclear matter and its relation to nuclear matter properties within the framework of the in-medium modified Skyrme model. Medium modifications are achieved by introducing optical potential for pion fields and parametrization of the Skyrme parameter in nuclear medium. The present approach is phenomenologically well justified by pion physics in nuclear matter and describe successfully bulk nuclear matter properties.

# Nucleon properties in nuclear matter

## Key claim

The in-medium modified Skyrme model can describe bulk nuclear matter properties, but the compressibility of nuclear matter depends strongly on the P-wave pion-nucleus scattering volume \(c_0\), and only values of \(c_0\) smaller than those used in pionic atom analysis yield compressibilities consistent with experimental values or with Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approaches.

## Method

The authors modify the Skyrme Lagrangian by introducing medium functionals for the pion kinetic term (\(\alpha_p\)), the pion mass term (\(\alpha_s\)), and the Skyrme quartic term (\(\gamma\)), where \(\alpha_s\) and \(\alpha_p\) are determined from phenomenological S- and P-wave pion-nucleus scattering lengths and volumes, and \(\gamma\) is parametrized with two variational parameters \(\gamma_{\rm num}\) and \(\gamma_{\rm den}\). Using a spherically symmetric hedgehog ansatz for the boson field, they minimize the binding energy per nucleon at normal nuclear matter density to fix the variational parameters, then compute the compressibility and symmetry energy for different values of the P-wave scattering volume \(c_0\).

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant \(F_\pi = 108.78\) MeV and Skyrme parameter \(e = 4.85\) are fitted to reproduce free-space nucleon and \(\Delta\) masses; pion mass fixed to \(m_\pi = m_{\pi^o}^{\rm exp} = 134.98\) MeV.
- For \(c_0 = 0.21 m_\pi^{-3}\), the compressibility is \(K \sim 1640\) MeV; for \(c_0 = 0.09 m_\pi^{-3}\), \(K \sim 580\) MeV; for \(c_0 = 0.06 m_\pi^{-3}\), \(K \sim 300\) MeV.
- Symmetry energy at normal nuclear matter density: \(E_{\rm sym}(\rho_0) \approx 14.19\) MeV for \(c_0 = 0.09 m_\pi^{-3}\) and \(E_{\rm sym}(\rho_0) \approx 8.71\) MeV for \(c_0 = 0.21 m_\pi^{-3}\).
- S-wave scattering length \(b_0 = -0.024 m_\pi^{-1}\); correlation parameter \(g_0' = 0.7\).

## Context

This work extends the in-medium modified Skyrme model of Rakhimov (1996) and Yakhshiev (2010) by incorporating core modifications of the nucleon via a density-dependent Skyrme parameter, going beyond earlier treatments that only modified the pion sector. The results are compared with the Walecka model (Serot, 1984) and relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approaches (Ter Haar, 1986; Brockmann, 1990), which give much smaller compressibilities; the present model can match those values only by using smaller P-wave scattering volumes than those from pionic atom data. The approach enables further study of isospin breaking effects and finite nuclei corrections to improve the symmetry energy, which is currently underestimated relative to the experimental range of 20-30 MeV.
