# Real-Time dynamics and phase separation in a holographic first order phase transition

> We study the fully nonlinear time evolution of a holographic system possessing a first order phase transition. The initial state is chosen in the spinodal region of the phase diagram, and includes an inhomogeneous perturbation in one of the field theory directions. The final state of the time evolution shows a clear phase separation in the form of domain formation. The results indicate the existence of a very rich class of inhomogeneous black hole solutions.

## Key claim
The fully nonlinear time evolution of a holographic system with a first order phase transition, starting from an inhomogeneous perturbation in the spinodal region, dynamically produces a final state with clear phase separation into domains whose energy densities match those at the critical temperature to within less than 1%.

## Method
The authors solve the time-dependent Einstein-matter equations in 3+1-dimensional anti-de Sitter (AdS) spacetime, using an Eddington-Finkelstein metric ansatz and a bottom-up holographic model with Einstein gravity coupled to a real scalar field. The initial state is placed in the spinodal region of the phase diagram, and an x-dependent perturbation (either a cosine mode or a Gaussian-shaped perturbation) is added to the metric function S. The system is evolved numerically using characteristic formulation of General Relativity with spectral Fourier discretization in the periodic spatial direction and Chebyshev methods in the holographic radial direction.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature of the first order phase transition: T_c = 0.246 (in units where the source scale Lambda = 1).
- For the configuration with scalar field horizon value phi_H = 2, the maximum and minimum energy densities of the final state approach the energy densities at T_c within less than 1%.
- The perturbation amplitudes used are in the range S_0 = 0.1 to 0.5.
- The momentum of the cosine perturbation is k = 1/6; the Gaussian perturbation uses tilde{k} = 1/12 with width parameter w_0 = 10.
- The system starting from phi_H = 2 settles to an inhomogeneous final state after around 400 units of simulation time; the phi_H = 3 configuration settles after about 150 units.

## Context
This work extends the linear response analysis of holographic first order phase transitions by Janik (2015) and Janik (2016), which identified the spinodal region, into the fully nonlinear regime. It demonstrates dynamical phase separation, in contrast to the inhomogeneous configurations approached within the hydrodynamic approximation reported by Attems (2017). The results enable quantitative study of domain formation dynamics and bubble growth in strongly coupled field theories, and open possibilities for applications in condensed matter physics where translation invariance is spontaneously broken without explicit inhomogeneous sources.
