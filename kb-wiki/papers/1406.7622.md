# Nuclear force and the EMC effect

> A linear correlation is shown quantitatively between the magnitude of the EMC effect measured in electron deep inelastic scattering (DIS) and the nuclear residual strong interaction energy (RSIE) obtained from nuclear binding energy subtracting the Coulomb energy contribution. This phenomenological relationship is used to extract the size of in-medium correction (IMC) effect on deuteron and to predict the EMC slopes $|dR_{EMC}/dx|$ of various nuclei. We further investigate the correlations between RSIE and other quantities which are related to the EMC effect. The observed correlations among RSIE, EMC slope and SRC ratio $R_{2N}N_{total}/N_{np(^3S_1)}$ imply that the local nuclear environment drives the modification of quark distributions.

## Key claim
A linear correlation is shown between the magnitude of the EMC effect (measured as the slope |dR_EMC/dx|) and the nuclear residual strong interaction energy (RSIE) per nucleon, defined as the binding energy minus the Coulomb energy contribution. The correlation is phenomenological and the authors note that the error of RSIE is not included in the fits, while errors of other quantities are.

## Method
The authors define RSIE per nucleon by taking experimental nuclear binding energies and subtracting the Coulomb energy term from the Bethe-Weizsäcker mass formula (a_c Z(Z-1)A^{-1/3} with a_c = 0.71 MeV). They then perform linear fits of the EMC slope (from combined SLAC and JLab data compiled by Arrington et al. and from global fits by Malace et al.) versus RSIE per nucleon, with a constraint that the deuteron point lies on the line. They also examine correlations between RSIE and short-range correlation (SRC) ratios a_2, R_2N, and R_2N N_total / N_np(^3S_1).

## Key numbers
- The slope of the linear correlation between EMC slope and RSIE per nucleon is 0.041 ± 0.002 (from Fit 4 to the global analysis data of Malace et al.).
- The intercept of the linear fit (corresponding to the EMC slope at zero RSIE) is −0.046 ± 0.003, which gives the extracted in-medium correction (IMC) slope for the deuteron as |dR_IMC(d)/dx| = 0.046 ± 0.003.
- The χ²/ndf values for the linear fits of EMC slope versus RSIE are 0.897 (Arrington data) and 1.18 (Malace data).
- The IMC slope for the deuteron from the local density fit in prior work (Arrington et al.) was 0.051 ± 0.003.
- The per-nucleon RSIE values range from 1.112 MeV (deuteron) to 11.73 MeV (¹⁹⁷Au), with binding energies per nucleon from 1.112 MeV (deuteron) to 8.790 MeV (⁵⁶Fe).
- Predicted EMC slopes for JLab targets include: ⁴He: 0.254 ± 0.013, ¹²C: 0.301 ± 0.015, ⁴⁰Ca: 0.386 ± 0.019, ⁴⁸Ca: 0.373 ± 0.019.

## Context
This work extends the search for scaling variables of the EMC effect beyond atomic mass number A and average nuclear density, which were found to break down for light nuclei by the JLab experiment (J. Arrington et al., 2012). It builds on the observed correlation between the EMC effect and short-range correlations (SRC) reported by Hen et al. (2012) and the local density explanation of the EMC effect. The RSIE correlation enables predictions for upcoming JLab 12 GeV experiments (E12-10-008 and E12-10-103) that will measure the EMC effect on very light to medium-heavy nuclei, including mirror nuclei ³H and ³He.
