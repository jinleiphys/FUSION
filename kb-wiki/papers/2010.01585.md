# A novel theoretical approach for the study of resonances in weakly bound systems

> In this paper, a novel theoretical scheme is presented to investigate resonant levels in weakly bound nuclear systems by the use of isospectral potentials. In this scheme, a new potential is constructed which is strictly isospectral with the original shallow-well potential and has properties that are desirable to calculate resonances more accurately and easier. Effectiveness of the method has been had been demonstrated in terms of its application to the first $0^+$ resonances in the neutron-rich isotopes $^A$C ($\equiv ^{A-2}$C+n+n) in the three-body cluster model for A=18, 20.

# A novel theoretical approach for the study of resonances in weakly bound systems

## Key claim
The paper presents a scheme combining supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SSQM) with the hyperspherical harmonics expansion method (HHEM) to construct a one-parameter family of isospectral potentials that are strictly isospectral with the original shallow-well potential. The method is claimed to enable more accurate calculation of resonance energies in weakly bound systems, but the actual resonance energy is independent of the adjustable parameter λ; the parameter only enhances numerical accuracy by deepening the well and raising the barrier.

## Method
The authors adopt a three-body cluster model (core + n + n) for the neutron-rich carbon isotopes ¹⁸C and ²⁰C, solving the Schrödinger equation via the hyperspherical harmonics expansion method (HHEM) with the hyperspherical adiabatic approximation (HAA) to obtain the ground state energy and wavefunction. Using the ground state wavefunction, they construct a one-parameter family of strictly isospectral potentials following the algebra of supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SSQM), where the parameter λ (chosen in the intervals -∞ < λ < -1 or 0 < λ < ∞) controls the depth of the well and height of the barrier. They then solve the single differential equation for the isospectral potential at positive energies and compute the probability of finding the particle within the well region as a function of energy; the resonance energy is identified as the peak in this probability.

## Key numbers
- For ¹⁸C, the converged two-neutron separation energy (S₂ₙ) is approximately 4.91 MeV (extrapolated from calculations up to K_max = 24).
- For ²⁰C, the converged two-neutron separation energy (S₂ₙ) is approximately 3.51 MeV (extrapolated from calculations up to K_max = 24).
- For ¹⁸C, the resonance energy E_R is approximately 1.89 MeV.
- For ²⁰C, the resonance energy E_R is approximately 3.735 MeV.
- For ¹⁸C, when λ changes from 0.1 to 0.0001, the well depth increases from -24.2 MeV at 2.7 fm to -247.6 MeV at 1.3 fm, and the barrier height increases from 5.4 MeV at 5.1 fm to 121.5 MeV at 1.9 fm.
- The calculated two-neutron separation energies agree with experimental values: 4.910 ± 0.030 MeV for ¹⁸C and 3.510 ± 0.240 MeV for ²⁰C (Audi 2003).

## Context
This work extends prior theoretical approaches for halo nuclei, including the three-body cluster model and the hyperspherical harmonics method (Ballot 1982), by incorporating supersymmetric quantum mechanics (Cooper 1995, Khare 1989) to construct isospectral potentials. The method is tested on the first 0⁺ resonances in ¹⁸C and ²⁰C, which are two-neutron halo nuclei previously studied experimentally by Tanihata (1985), Kobayashi (2012), and Tanaka (2010), and theoretically by Yamaguchi (2011). The approach enables more accurate computation of resonance energies in weakly bound systems where the original shallow-well potential produces excessively wide resonances that are numerically difficult to resolve.
