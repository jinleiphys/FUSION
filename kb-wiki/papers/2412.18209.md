# Fermionic equations of motion in strongly-correlated media: applications to the nuclear many-body problem

> These notes summarise the lectures given at the International School of Physics "Enrico Fermi" in Summer 2024 in Varenna (Italy) about the strongly coupled quantum many-body theory and its applications to nuclear structure. The lectures present a rather short overview of the subject with an emphasis on the analytical aspects of the nuclear many-body problem, aiming at a deep understanding of the complexity of strongly coupled nucleonic states and emergent collective phenomena. The major pedagogical focus is recognizing how all the models describing nuclear dynamics follow from a unified model-independent framework formulated in the universal language of quantum field theory. In particular, connections between the classes of ab initio, density functional theory, and beyond mean-field approaches are made accessible. Approximations of varying complexity are discussed in applications to excited states of medium-heavy nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper argues that all nuclear many-body models, from ab initio to density functional theory and beyond-mean-field approaches, can be derived from a unified, model-independent framework based on fermionic equations of motion (EOMs) for propagators, but the practical implementation requires effective interactions because the exact static kernels are not well-known due to the difficulty of their accurate determination.

## Method

The author derives EOMs for one-fermion and two-fermion propagators (Green functions) starting from a many-body Hamiltonian with a two-body interaction, showing how the Dyson form emerges with static and dynamical (energy-dependent) interaction kernels. The dynamical kernels are decomposed via cluster decomposition into products of one-fermion and two-fermion correlation functions, which leads to the quasiparticle-vibration coupling (qPVC) approximation as the leading non-perturbative treatment of emergent collective effects. The formalism is extended to superfluid systems using the Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov basis, unifying normal and pairing phonon modes under average particle number conservation.

## Key numbers

- The quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) implementations have been advanced to 3p3h (three-phonon) configuration complexity for medium-heavy nuclei in model spaces limited by low energy.
- Recent relativistic frameworks include model spaces up to high energy of 25-30 MeV for including 3p3h configurations.
- The spectral expansion of the single-fermion propagator shows poles at energies \(E^{(N+1)}_n - E^{(N)}_0\) and \(-(E^{(N-1)}_m - E^{(N)}_0)\) for the (N+1)-particle and (N-1)-particle systems, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the line of research by Schuck and collaborators (Adachi and Schuck 1989, Danielewicz 1994, Dukelsky, Roepke, and Schuck 1998) on symmetric dynamical kernels for non-perturbative approximations, and builds on the nuclear field theory (NFT) developed by the Copenhagen-Milano school, particularly Broglia (Bohr and Mottelson 1969, 1975; Broglia 1976; Bortignon, Broglia, Bes et al. 1977). It connects to the quasiparticle-phonon model of Soloviev (1992) and enables a path toward including higher configuration complexity (3p3h and beyond) for accurate spectral calculations of open-shell nuclei, which is needed for applications across the nuclear chart.
