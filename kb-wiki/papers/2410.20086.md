# Re analysis of Temperature Dependent Neutron Capture Rates and Stellar \b{eta} decay Rates of 95 98Mo

> The neutron capture rates and temperature dependent stellar beta decay rates of Mo isotopes are investigated within the framework of the statistical code (Talys v1.96) and proton neutron quasi particle random phase approximation (pnQRPA) models. The Maxwellian average cross section (MACS) and neutron capture rates for 95 98Mo(n,γ) 96 99Mo radiative capture processes are analyzed within the framework of statistical code Talys v1.96 based on the phenomenological nuclear level density (NLD) parameters and gamma strength functions (GSFs). The current model based computed data for MACS provide a good comparison with the existing measured data. The temperature dependent stellar \b{eta} decay rates of 95 98Mo are investigated at different densities within the framework of the pn-QRPA model. For the considered temperature range, the neutron capture rates were found to be higher, by orders of magnitude, than the stellar \b{eta} rates.

## Key claim

The neutron capture rates for \(^{95-98}\)Mo are orders of magnitude higher than the stellar beta decay rates across the entire temperature range relevant to the s-process, with the ratio \(\lambda_{(n,\gamma)}/\lambda_{(\beta^{-}+pc)}\) reaching values as high as \(10^{35}\) for \(^{96}\)Mo at T\(_9\)=0.5, confirming that neutron capture dominates over beta decay in determining the abundances of these isotopes in AGB stars.

## Method

The Maxwellian average cross-sections (MACS) for \(^{95-98}\)Mo(n,\(\gamma\))\(^{96-99}\)Mo were computed using the Talys v1.96 statistical code based on Hauser-Feshbach theory, employing the back-shifted Fermi gas model (BSFM) for nuclear level densities and testing three gamma strength functions (Brink-Axel, Gogny D1M QRPA, and Kopecky-Uhl). The stellar beta decay and positron capture rates for \(^{95-98}\)Mo were calculated using the proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model with a deformed Nilsson potential basis, BCS pairing, and nuclear deformation parameters from the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM). The neutron capture rates were then computed from the MACS using the stellar neutron density formula for the s-process environment at \(\rho=10^4\) g cm\(^{-3}\) and helium mass fraction \(X_4=0.2\).

## Key numbers

- MACS at \(kT=30\) keV for \(^{96}\)Mo(n,\(\gamma\))\(^{97}\)Mo: 121.35 mb (Brink-Axel GSF), 124.31 mb (Gogny GSF), 152.29 mb (Kopecky-Uhl GSF), compared to 113 mb from Winters (reference in paper)
- MACS at \(kT=30\) keV for \(^{98}\)Mo(n,\(\gamma\))\(^{99}\)Mo: 101.28 mb (Brink-Axel GSF), 98.42 mb (Gogny GSF), 78.80 mb (Kopecky-Uhl GSF)
- MACS at \(E_n=30\) keV for \(^{95}\)Mo: 276 mb; for \(^{97}\)Mo: 347 mb
- Neutron capture rates: \(\lambda_{(n,\gamma)}=6.60\times10^{-17}n_n\) for \(^{95}\)Mo, \(8.305\times10^{-17}n_n\) for \(^{97}\)Mo, \(2.90\times10^{-17}n_n\) for \(^{96}\)Mo, \(2.34\times10^{-17}n_n\) for \(^{98}\)Mo
- At T\(_9=0.1\) for \(^{95}\)Mo: \(\lambda_{(n,\gamma)}=5.1\times10^{-31}\) s\(^{-1}\) vs \(\lambda_{(\beta^{-}+pc)}=0.97\times10^{-100}\) s\(^{-1}\)
- At T\(_9=0.5\) for \(^{95}\)Mo: \(\lambda_{(n,\gamma)}=3.5\times10^{-02}\) s\(^{-1}\) vs \(\lambda_{(\beta^{-}+pc)}=2.37\times10^{-26}\) s\(^{-1}\)
- Ratio \(\lambda_{(n,\gamma)}/\lambda_{(\beta^{-}+pc)}\) at T\(_9=0.5\): \(1.50\times10^{24}\) for \(^{95}\)Mo, \(4.27\times10^{35}\) for \(^{96}\)Mo, \(3.22\times10^{13}\) for \(^{97}\)Mo, \(4.48\times10^{27}\) for \(^{98}\)Mo
- Nuclear deformation parameters \(\beta_2\) from FRDM: 0.000 (\(^{95}\)Mo), 0.150 (\(^{96}\)Mo), 0.172 (\(^{97}\)Mo), 0.206 (\(^{98}\)Mo)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Mo isotopes in the s-process nucleosynthesis context, including the MACS calculations by Saumi et al. (reference in paper) using the folding DDM3Y potential and the experimental measurements by Winters (reference in paper) and Massimi et al. (reference in paper) at the n_TOF facility. The computed MACS values at \(kT=30\) keV agree well with the experimental data from Winters (reference in paper), validating the BSFM+Brink-Axel combination as the best-fitting model. The dominance of neutron capture over beta decay for these isotopes provides input for s-process network calculations in AGB stars, particularly for understanding the contamination patterns observed in pre-solar silicon carbide grains as discussed by Liu (reference in paper) and Cescutti (reference in paper).
