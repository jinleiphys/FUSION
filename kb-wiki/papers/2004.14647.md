# $^8$He and $^9$Li cluster structures in light nuclei

> The possibility of the $^8$He and $^{9}$Li clusters in atomic nuclei is discussed. Until now most of the clusters in the conventional models have been limited to the closures of the three-dimensional harmonic oscillators, such as $^4$He, $^{16}$O, and $^{40}$Ca. In the neutron-rich nuclei, however, the neutron to proton ratio is not unity, and it is worthwhile to think about more neutron-rich objects with $N>Z$ as the building blocks of cluster structures. Here the nuclei with the neutron number six, which is the subclosure of the $p_{3/2}$ subshell of the $jj$-coupling shell model, are assumed to be clusters, and thus we study the $^8$He and $^9$Li cluster structures in $^{16}$Be ($^8$He+$^8$He), $^{17}$B ($^8$He+$^9$Li), $^{18}$C ($^9$Li+$^9$Li), and $^{24}$C ($^8$He+$^8$He+$^8$He). Recent progress of the antisymmetrized quasi cluster model (AQCM) enables us to utilize $jj$-coupling shell model wave functions as the clusters rather easily. It is shown that the $^8$He+$^9$Li and $^9$Li+$^9$Li cluster configurations cover the lowest shell-model states of $^{17}$B and $^{18}$C, respectively. To predict the cluster states with large relative distances, we increase the expectation value of the principal quantum numbers by adding the nodes to the lowest states under the condition that the total angular momentum is unchanged (equal to $J^π=0$). As a result, developed cluster states are obtained around the corresponding threshold energies. The rotational band structure of $^{24}$C, which reflect the symmetry of equilateral triangular configuration ($D_{3h}$ symmetry) of three $^8$He clusters, also appears around the threshold energy.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that neutron-rich nuclei with neutron number six (subclosure of the $p_{3/2}$ subshell in the $jj$-coupling shell model), specifically $^8$He and $^9$Li, can serve as cluster building blocks in light nuclei, extending beyond the traditional closures of the three-dimensional harmonic oscillator ($^4$He, $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca). The claim is hedged by the fact that these cluster configurations cover the lowest shell-model states of the studied nuclei, but the developed cluster states appear around the corresponding threshold energies, not necessarily as ground states.

## Method

The authors used the antisymmetrized quasi cluster model (AQCM) to construct $^8$He and $^9$Li cluster wave functions from $jj$-coupling shell model configurations. They placed these clusters at varying relative distances for $^{16}$Be ($^8$He+$^8$He), $^{17}$B ($^8$He+$^9$Li), $^{18}$C ($^9$Li+$^9$Li), and $^{24}$C (three $^8$He clusters in an equilateral triangular configuration), using the Tohsaki interaction (F1 parameter set) for the central potential and the G3RS interaction for the spin-orbit part. To predict developed cluster states, they increased the expectation value of the principal quantum numbers by adding nodes to the lowest states while keeping total angular momentum $J^\pi = 0$, then orthogonalized to the lowest states.

## Key numbers

* $^{16}$Be: the two-neutron separation energy is $-1.35$ MeV (unbound), but the ground state is lower than the $^8$He+$^8$He threshold by 5.77 MeV; the optimal $0^+$ state energy is obtained at a relative distance of $\sim 3$ fm between the two $^8$He clusters.
* $^{17}$B: the nucleus is bound from the $^9$Li+$^8$He threshold by 12.86 MeV experimentally; the developed cluster state minimum appears at a relative distance of 4 fm around the threshold energy.
* $^{18}$C: the ground state is lower than the two-$^9$Li threshold by 24.99 MeV; the optimal energy for the lowest $0^+$ state is obtained at a relative distance of 2.5 fm, and the developed cluster state appears at $\sim 4$ fm.
* $^{24}$C: the expectation values of the principal quantum number for the $0^+$ state converge to 4 for protons and 26 for neutrons at small relative distances; the three-$^8$He threshold is located more than $E_x = 25$ MeV in excitation energy.
* $^8$He: two-neutron separation energy is 2.12 MeV; $^9$Li: neutron separation energy is 4.06 MeV.
* The size parameter $\nu = 0.23$ fm$^{-2}$ was used for the Gaussian single-particle wave functions.

## Context

This work extends the traditional cluster model, which has been limited to closures of the three-dimensional harmonic oscillator such as $^4$He, $^{16}$O, and $^{40}$Ca (Brink model), by introducing neutron-rich clusters based on the $jj$-coupling shell model subclosures. It builds on previous AQCM developments by Itagaki et al. (2016, 2018) and earlier work on $^{14}$C clusters (Itagaki et al., 2020). The study also connects to the molecular-orbit and atomic-orbit structures in Be isotopes studied with the generalized two-center cluster model (GTCM) by Ito et al. (2012), and to the $D_{3h}$ symmetry rotational bands in $^{12}$C (Maruhn et al., 2014). The results enable future investigations of molecular-orbital structures by adding neutrons to the developed cluster states in $^{17}$B and $^{18}$C, and studies of cluster resonances relevant to Big Bang nucleosynthesis.
