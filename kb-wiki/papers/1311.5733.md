# Survival of Bc mesons in a hot plasma within a potential model

> We extend a previous work on the study of heavy charmonia and bottomonia in a deconfined quark-gluon plasma by considering the Bc family of mesons. With the introduction of this bound state of a charm and a beauty quark, we investigate at finite temperature the behavior of the quarkonium, in an energy region between the ψ and the Υ states.

## Key claim
The Bc meson family can survive above the deconfinement temperature in a quark-gluon plasma, with the ground state persisting up to approximately twice the critical temperature, while the excited and P-wave states dissociate near Tc.

## Method
The authors employ a non-relativistic potential model fitted to lattice QCD calculations of the color singlet free energy for Nf=2 light flavors. They solve the Schroedinger equation with a temperature-dependent effective potential derived from the singlet internal energy, using constituent quark masses of mc=1.4 and 1.6 GeV for charm and mb=4.3 and 4.7 GeV for beauty. The resulting energy eigenvalues determine dissociation temperatures where binding vanishes, and the radial wave functions at the origin are used to construct spectral functions via a non-relativistic QCD expansion.

## Key numbers
- Ground state Bc dissociation temperature ranges from 1.87 Tc to 2.02 Tc depending on the quark mass combination, with Tc=202 MeV
- First radially excited state Bc' dissociation temperature ranges from 1.03 Tc to 1.05 Tc
- First P-wave state χBc dissociation temperature ranges from 1.05 Tc to 1.06 Tc
- The variation in meson masses from including dynamical thermal masses is below 50 MeV for T < Tc and below 20 MeV for T > Tc
- CMS measured the Bc mass as 6.272 ± 0.003 GeV for the J/Ψ + π+ channel and 6.265 ± 0.004 GeV for the J/Ψ + 3π channel
- Zero temperature potential models give |R(0)|^2 = 1.68 for the S-wave and |R'(0)|^2 = 0.20 for the P-wave state

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous potential model studies of charmonia and bottomonia (Alberico et al., 2005; Alberico et al., 2007) to the Bc meson family, which lies between the J/ψ and Υ states in energy. The Bc meson is particularly interesting because its production may be enhanced in nucleus-nucleus collisions compared to proton-proton collisions, and its longer lifetime compared to other heavy quarkonia makes it a promising probe of deconfinement dynamics in heavy ion experiments at the LHC, especially for the ALICE detector.
