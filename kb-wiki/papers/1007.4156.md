# Heavy Quarkonium in a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma below the melting temperature

> We calculate the heavy quarkonium energy levels and decay widths in a quark-gluon plasma, whose temperature T and screening mass m_D satisfy the hierarchy m alpha_s >> T >> m alpha_s^2 >> m_D (m being the heavy-quark mass), at order m alpha_s^5. We first sequentially integrate out the scales m, m alpha_s and T, and, next, we carry out the calculations in the resulting effective theory using techniques of integration by regions. A collinear region is identified, which contributes at this order. We also discuss the implications of our results concerning heavy quarkonium suppression in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim
The paper calculates heavy quarkonium energy levels and decay widths in a quark-gluon plasma at order \(m\alpha_s^5\), under the specific hierarchy \(m\alpha_s \gg T \gg m\alpha_s^2 \gg m_D\) (where \(m\) is the heavy-quark mass, \(T\) the temperature, and \(m_D\) the screening mass). This temperature regime is below the melting temperature, and the thermal bath modifies the Coulombic bound state as a small perturbation.

## Method
The authors sequentially integrate out the scales \(m\), \(m\alpha_s\), and \(T\) using effective field theories (NRQCD, then pNRQCD, then pNRQCD\(_\mathrm{HTL}\)). They compute the thermal correction to the singlet potential by evaluating the leading dipole diagram (singlet-to-octet transition via chromoelectric gluon emission and reabsorption) and a two-loop diagram with a one-loop self-energy insertion in the longitudinal gluon propagator, using techniques of integration by regions and dimensional regularization. A collinear region is identified that contributes at this order.

## Key numbers
- The leading thermal correction to the singlet potential from the linear term in the octet propagator expansion is \(\delta V_s^{\rm (linear)} = \frac{\pi}{9} N_c C_F \alpha_s^2 T^2 r + \frac{2\pi}{3m} C_F \alpha_s T^2\).
- The corresponding energy shift from this linear term is \(\delta E_{n,l}^{\rm (linear)} = \frac{\pi}{9} N_c C_F \alpha_s^2 T^2 \frac{a_0}{2}[3n^2 - l(l+1)] + \frac{2\pi}{3m} C_F \alpha_s T^2\), where \(a_0 = 2/(m C_F \alpha_s)\).
- The two-loop contribution to the potential includes a real part: \(\delta V_s^{\rm (2\,loops)} = -\frac{3}{2} \zeta(3) C_F \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} r^2 T m_D^2 + \frac{2}{3} \zeta(3) N_c C_F \alpha_s^2 r^2 T^3\), with the Debye mass \(m_D^2 = \frac{g^2 T^2}{3}(N_c + T_F n_f)\).
- The two-loop contribution to the thermal width contains an infrared-divergent term: \(\Gamma_{n,l}^{\rm (2\,loops)}\) proportional to \(\frac{C_F}{6} \alpha_s T m_D^2\) times a divergent factor \((-2/\epsilon + \gamma_E + \ln\pi - \ln(T^2/\mu^2) + 2/3 - 4\ln 2 - 2\zeta'(2)/\zeta(2))\), plus a finite term \(-\frac{4\pi}{9} \ln 2 \, N_c C_F \alpha_s^2 T^3\), all multiplied by \(\langle r^2 \rangle_{n,l} = a_0^2 n^2 [5n^2+1-3l(l+1)]/2\).

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory approach to heavy quarkonium in a thermal bath initiated by Escobedo (2008) for QED and by Brambilla (2009) for QCD in the static limit, merging and completing those findings by including contributions from a large but finite quark mass. The results differ from phenomenological potentials (reviewed by Petreczky (2005) and Satz (2005)) by developing an imaginary part from Landau damping (Laine (2006)) and color-singlet breakup (Brambilla (2009)). The paper enables systematic calculations of quarkonium suppression in heavy ion collisions below the melting temperature, with the ground states of bottomonium and, to a lesser extent, charmonium as the most relevant applications.
