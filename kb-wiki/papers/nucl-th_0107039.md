# The relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov description of charge-changing cross section for C,N,O and F isotopes

> The ground state properties including radii, density distribution and one neutron separation energy for C, N, O and F isotopes up to the neutron drip line are systematically studied by the fully self-consistent microscopic Relativistic Continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory. With the proton density distribution thus obtained, the charge-changing cross sections for C, N, O and F isotopes are calculated using the Glauber model. Good agreement with the data has been achieved. The charge changing cross sections change only slightly with the neutron number except for proton-rich nuclei. Similar trends of variations of proton radii and of charge changing cross sections for each isotope chain is observed which implies that the proton density plays important role in determining the charge-changing cross sections.

## Key claim

The Relativistic Continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory, combined with the Glauber model, reproduces measured charge-changing cross sections for C, N, O, and F isotopes, but the agreement is qualified by the neglect of deformation effects, which may explain discrepancies in one-neutron separation energies for some nuclei.

## Method

The ground-state properties of C, N, O, and F isotopes up to the neutron drip line were calculated using the fully self-consistent RCHB theory, which extends the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach by including pairing correlations and the continuum via Bogoliubov transformation in coordinate space. The RCHB equations were solved in a box of radius 20 fm with a step size of 0.1 fm using the NL-SH parameter set and a density-dependent zero-range pairing force. The resulting proton density distributions were then used as input to the Glauber model with free nucleon-nucleon cross sections to compute total charge-changing cross sections on a carbon target at relativistic energies.

## Key numbers

* One-neutron separation energy for 19C was calculated as -0.003 MeV (unbound), whereas experiment shows 19C is bound.
* The pairing force used a density-dependent delta interaction with rho_0 = 0.152 fm^-3.
* The continuum contribution was restricted by a cut-off energy E_cut of about 120 MeV.
* The RCHB equations were solved in a box of size R = 20 fm with a step size of 0.1 fm.
* The neutron drip line for fluorine isotopes was predicted to be 30F.

## Context

This work extends the RCHB theory, previously applied successfully to interaction cross sections in Na isotopes by Meng, Tanihata, and Yamaji (1998), to the calculation of charge-changing cross sections. It builds on the experimental measurements of charge-changing cross sections for light stable and neutron-rich nuclei by Chulkov et al. (2000). The results enable future studies that include deformation effects, which the authors note are neglected in the present spherical calculation and are planned for subsequent work.
