# Hydrodynamic Collectivity in Proton--Proton Collisions at 13 TeV

> In this paper, we investigate the hydrodynamic collectivity in proton--proton (pp) collisions at 13 TeV, using iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model with HIJING initial conditions. With properly tuned parameters, our model simulations can remarkably describe all the measured 2-particle correlations, including integrated and differential elliptic flow coefficients for all charged and identified hadrons ($K_S^0$, $Λ$). However, our model calculations show positive 4-particle cumulant $c_{2}\{4\}$ in high multiplicity pp collisions, and can not reproduce the negative $c_{2}\{4\}$ measured in experiment. Further investigations on the HIJING initial conditions show that the fluctuations of the second order anisotropy coefficient $\varepsilon_{2}$ increases with the increase of its mean value, which leads to a similar trend of the flow fluctuations. For a simultaneous description of the 2- and 4- particle cumulants within the hydrodynamic framework, it is required to have significant improvements on initial condition for pp collisions, which is still lacking of knowledge at the moment.

## Key claim

The iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model with HIJING initial conditions can describe all measured 2-particle correlations in 13 TeV proton-proton collisions, but it fails to reproduce the negative 4-particle cumulant \(c_{2}\{4\}\) observed in experiment because the HIJING initial conditions produce a fluctuation pattern where the eccentricity fluctuations increase with the mean value, preventing simultaneous description of 2- and 4-particle cumulants.

## Method

The authors used the iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model, which combines (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics (VISH2+1) with the UrQMD hadron cascade model, with initial conditions generated by the modified HIJING model. They tuned four parameter sets (varying Gaussian smearing factors \(\sigma_R\) and \(\sigma_0\), hydrodynamic starting time \(\tau_0\), specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s\), normalization factor \(K\), and switching temperature \(T_{\rm sw}\)) to roughly fit the slope of \(p_{\rm T}\) spectra at 7 TeV and the measured \(v_2\{2\}\) from either CMS or ATLAS at 13 TeV. They then calculated 2-particle cumulants with a pseudorapidity gap \(|\Delta\eta| > 0\) and 4-particle cumulants using the standard Q-cumulant method, and compared the results to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Integrated \(v_2\{2\}\) values from model calculations reproduce CMS and ATLAS measurements across multiplicities from approximately 30 to 160 charged hadrons, with parameter sets I, II, and III fitting CMS and ATLAS peripheral-subtraction data, and para-IV fitting ATLAS template-fit data.
- For \(v_3\{2\}\), only para-III roughly reproduces CMS data; para-I, para-II, and para-IV overestimate the data.
- The model predicts positive \(c_2\{4\}\) values for all four parameter sets in high multiplicity pp collisions, while CMS and ATLAS measure negative \(c_2\{4\}\) values.
- The \(\varepsilon_2\) distributions from HIJING show that for para-I, II, and IV, the 4-particle cumulant of eccentricity \(c_2\{4\}^{\varepsilon}\) is positive; for para-III it has a small negative value but does not translate into a negative \(c_2\{4\}\) in final hadrons.

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic modeling of collectivity from heavy-ion collisions (Pb-Pb at LHC) to the smallest colliding system, proton-proton, following the discovery of long-range ridge structures in high-multiplicity pp collisions by CMS (Khachatryan 2010) and ATLAS (Aad 2015). The failure to reproduce negative \(c_2\{4\}\) with HIJING initial conditions contrasts with successful descriptions of 2-particle correlations and indicates that, unlike in Pb-Pb collisions where the standard changing-sign pattern of cumulants is observed (ALICE 2011, Adam 2016), the initial condition models for pp collisions require significant improvements. This work enables future development of alternative initial condition models for small systems and motivates investigation of 2- and 4-particle cumulants within other theoretical approaches beyond hydrodynamics.
