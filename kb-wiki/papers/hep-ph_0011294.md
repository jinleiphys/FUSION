# Point form relativistic quantum mechanics, antiparticles and exchange currents

> It is shown that the dynamical observables calculated with the point form relativistic quantum mechanics incorporate effects of particle-antiparticle creation from the vacuum by interactions. The electromagnetic observables obtained with the point form impulse approximation include contributions from the ``pair'' exchange currents that are associated with the interactions between particles. This implies that the recently calculated nucleon electromagnetic formfactors with the chiral constituent quark model automatically take into account effects of ``pair'' exchange currents that are associated with the Goldstone boson exchange between the constituent quarks as well as with the confining interaction.

## Key claim

The paper argues that electromagnetic observables calculated using the point form relativistic impulse approximation automatically include contributions from "pair" exchange currents (particle-antiparticle creation from the vacuum by interactions), even though the formalism explicitly contains only particle degrees of freedom and no antiparticle degrees of freedom are introduced.

## Method

The author uses Feynman's space-time interpretation of quantum mechanics to analyze the physical content of the point form relativistic quantum mechanics formalism. By examining the time-dependent Green function and the path integral representation, the paper shows that virtual amplitudes involving space-like intervals between interaction points are necessarily present, and these space-like intervals appear as antiparticle propagation when viewed from a moving reference frame. The argument is made using the amplitude quadratic in the interaction as an example, then extended to all higher-order amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The electric proton and neutron formfactors as well as radii from the chiral constituent quark model calculation fit experiment within the error bars (no specific numerical values given for these fits).
- Magnetic moments and magnetic formfactors deviate from experimental data by "a few percent" in magnitude.
- The magnetic formfactors follow the required dipole form below 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the point form relativistic quantum mechanics formalism developed by Dirac (1949) and Klink (1998), and specifically interprets the nucleon electromagnetic formfactor calculations of Wagenbrunn et al. (2000) using the chiral constituent quark model of Glozman et al. (1998). The paper connects to the long-standing understanding in nuclear physics that "pair" exchange currents are important, as discussed by Riska (1985) and others, but shows that in the point form approach these currents are automatically included to all orders in the interaction rather than requiring explicit nonrelativistic v/c expansion. This enables a proper physical interpretation of the successful formfactor predictions without introducing additional parameters.
