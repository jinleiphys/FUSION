# Neutrino Pair Emission from Hot Nuclei During Stellar Collapse

> We present shell-model calculations showing that residual interaction-induced configuration mixing enhances the rate of neutral current de-excitation of thermally excited nuclei into neutrino-antineutrino pairs. Though our calculations reinforce the conclusions of previous studies that this process is the dominant source of neutrino pairs near the onset of neutrino trapping during stellar collapse, our shell-model result has the effect of increasing the energy of these pairs, possibly altering their role in entropy transport in supernovae.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations show that residual interaction-induced configuration mixing enhances the rate of neutral current de-excitation of thermally excited nuclei into neutrino-antineutrino pairs, and this has the effect of increasing the energy of these pairs, possibly altering their role in entropy transport in supernovae. The calculations reinforce previous conclusions that this process is the dominant source of neutrino pairs near the onset of neutrino trapping during stellar collapse, but the higher neutrino energies may mean the neutrinos are trapped rather than escaping.

## Method

The authors performed full shell-model calculations using the Oxbash and NuShellX codes for three nuclei: full sd-shell calculation of 28Si using the USDB Hamiltonian (closed 16O core with 12 valence nucleons), full fp-shell calculation of 47Ti using the GPFX1 Hamiltonian (closed 40Ca core with 7 valence nucleons), and a truncated fp-shell calculation for 56Fe using the GPFX1 Hamiltonian. They computed transition strengths and energy loss rates over excitation energies from 0 to 40 MeV, averaging over multiple initial states with spins near the peak of the state density distribution, and applied a quenching factor of 0.50 to the Gamow-Teller strengths based on experimental beta-decay comparisons.

## Key numbers

- For 28Si at 27.6 MeV excitation (rescaled temperature 2.0 MeV for A=56), the energy loss rate is 3.6 MeV/s/baryon
- For 56Fe at 27.6 MeV excitation (temperature 2.0 MeV), the energy loss rate is 4.6 MeV/s/baryon
- For 28Si at 30 MeV excitation with spin-orbit splitting set to zero, the rate drops to 0.21 MeV/s/baryon (at rescaled temperature 2.1 MeV), compared to 3.4 MeV/s/baryon with realistic spin-orbit splitting
- The de-excitation rate formula is lambda_if = 1.71 x 10^(-4) s^(-1) x (Delta E / MeV)^5 x B(GT)_if
- For typical conditions near neutrino trapping, core density is ~10^12 g/cm^3, electron fraction Ye ~ 0.32, electron Fermi energy ~ 35 MeV, temperature ~ 1-2 MeV, and mean nuclear mass A ~ 100 with mean excitation energy ~ 50 MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculations of Fuller and Meyer (1991) and Kolb and Mazurek (1979) on neutrino pair emission from thermally excited nuclei during stellar collapse, and the plasmon-mediated channel identified by Horowitz (1992). The shell-model approach provides a more realistic treatment of nuclear structure effects than the plane-wave approximation used by Kolb and Mazurek (1979), and reveals that spin-orbit splitting significantly enhances the rate by spreading Gamow-Teller strength to higher transition energies. The results enable future inclusion in full core collapse neutrino transport simulations to determine whether the more energetic neutrino pairs produced are trapped or escape, and whether this process acts as a thermostat for the collapsing core or accelerates the approach to beta equilibrium.
