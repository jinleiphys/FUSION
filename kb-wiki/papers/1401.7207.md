# Parton density constraints from massive vector boson production at the LHC

> We demonstrate that not only the production of virtual photons decaying into low-mass lepton pairs, but also the one of weak bosons at large transverse momenta is dominated by quark-gluon scattering. Measurements of these processes at the LHC can therefore provide useful constraints on the parton densities in the proton, in particular the one of the gluon, and their nuclear modifications.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that weak \(W\) and \(Z\) bosons produced at large transverse momenta (\(p_T > M_{W,Z}\)) are dominated by quark-gluon scattering (the QCD Compton process), not just low-mass lepton pairs from virtual photons, and can therefore constrain the gluon parton density in the proton at large momentum fraction \(x\), provided the transverse momentum is sufficiently large for the Compton process to dominate.

## Method

The author uses next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD calculations, resummed to next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy at low \(p_T\), to compute transverse-momentum distributions for \(Z\) and \(W\) bosons produced in \(pp\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV. The relative contributions from partonic subprocesses (quark-antiquark annihilation vs. quark-gluon scattering) are analyzed as a function of \(p_T\). For nuclear modifications, cross-section ratios of proton-lead over proton-proton collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV are computed for low-mass muon pairs, using different nuclear PDF parametrisations (EPS09, nCTEQ).

## Key numbers

- \(Z\) and \(W\) boson production at the LHC with \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV has been measured out to \(p_T\) values of 600 GeV by ATLAS and CMS.
- At \(p_T > 65\) GeV, the NLO perturbative calculation for \(Z\) bosons is sufficient; below this, resummation is required.
- The values of \(x\) probed by current data at central rapidities, estimated as \(x_T = 2p_T/\sqrt{s}\), extend only to 0.01 ... 0.15.
- At \(\sqrt{s}=14\) TeV, the \(x\)-axes of the figures can be rescaled by a factor of two.
- For low-mass muon pairs in proton-lead collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV, the uncertainty in the shadowing effect is \(\pm 10\%\) at low \(p_T\) according to EPS09, and reaches \(-20\%\) in the nCTEQ prediction.
- For lead-proton collisions, the EMC effect for EPS09 is predicted for \(p_T > 40\) GeV and \(x > 0.4\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of using prompt-photon production to constrain the gluon density, as previously studied by Arleo (2007, 2011), to massive vector bosons, overcoming fragmentation uncertainties that affect prompt photons. It builds on the observation by Berger (1998, 1999) that low-mass lepton pairs from virtual photons serve as a surrogate for prompt photons. The paper enables future constraints on gluon PDFs at large \(x\) using high-luminosity LHC runs at \(\sqrt{s}=14\) TeV, and on nuclear PDF modifications using proton-ion runs, with cross-section ratios cancelling theoretical and experimental uncertainties.
