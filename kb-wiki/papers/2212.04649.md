# Axial coupling constant in a magnetic background

> The nucleon axial coupling constant $g_A$ is calculated in the presence of an external uniform magnetic field using finite energy sum rules. The correlation function of proton, neutron and axial-vector currents is calculated both the hadronic and the QCD sector. Once the axial contribution in the form factor is isolated, a double sum rule is considered, i.e., the usual QCD contour integration for the external moments of both the proton and neutron current. The effects of the external magnetic field come mainly through the in-medium current-nucleon coupling constants $λ_N(B)$ and the hadronic thresholds $s_0(B)$ provided by the nucleon-nucleon correlators. As a result, the axial coupling constant decreases in the presence of the magnetic field.

# Axial coupling constant in a magnetic background

## Key claim

The nucleon axial coupling constant $g_A$ decreases in the presence of an external uniform magnetic field, with the effect arising primarily from the magnetic-field dependence of the nucleon-current couplings $\lambda_N(B)$ and hadronic thresholds $s_0(B)$. The decrease is approximately linear for the lowest values of the magnetic field, and the result is obtained at lowest order in the expansion of the fermion propagator in powers of $eB$, meaning that the difference between parallel and perpendicular components of the axial form factor is neglected as being of order $(eB)^2/s_0$.

## Method

The author calculates the three-current correlator of proton, neutron, and axial-vector currents in both the hadronic and QCD sectors, then isolates the axial form factor $G_A$ by tracing the correlator with a specific Dirac structure. A double finite energy sum rule (FESR) is applied, performing contour integrations over the external momenta of both the proton and neutron currents, matching the hadronic discontinuity to the perturbative QCD contribution on the complex circle. The magnetic field is introduced via the expanded fermion propagator in powers of $eB$ using the Fock-Schwinger gauge, and the nucleon-current couplings and hadronic thresholds as functions of the magnetic field are taken from the nucleon-nucleon correlator calculated in Dominguez (2020).

## Key numbers

- In vacuum, using condensate values $\langle\bar qq\rangle = -(0.24\text{ GeV})^3$ and $\langle G^2\rangle = (0.33\text{ GeV})^4$, the axial coupling constant is $g_A = 1.52$.
- To reproduce the experimental value $g_A \approx 1.275$, the author fixes $\langle -\bar qq\rangle^{1/3} = 0.267$ GeV and $\langle G^2\rangle^{1/4} = 0.3775$ GeV, yielding $\lambda_N = 0.022\text{ GeV}^3$ and $s_0 = 1.429\text{ GeV}^2$.
- At $eB = 0.1\text{ GeV}^2$, the axial coupling constant changes by about 10%, and the factor $1+3g_A^2$ (proportional to neutron decay width and neutrino emissivity in the Urca process) decreases by 16%.

## Context

This work extends the study of medium effects on the axial coupling constant, which is known to exhibit quenching at high baryon density (Carter, 2001) and to decrease near the deconfinement/chiral phase transition at finite temperature (Dominguez, 1999; Abu-Shady, 2012). The magnetic-field treatment follows the series expansion approach used in sum-rule calculations by Ioffe (1983), Ayala (2015), and Dominguez (2018, 2020), allowing values of $eB$ higher than $10\,m_\pi^2$ relevant for peripheral heavy-ion collisions and magnetars. The result provides the first determination of the magnetic evolution of the nucleon axial coupling constant, enabling future studies of combined temperature, density, and magnetic field effects in compact stars and relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
