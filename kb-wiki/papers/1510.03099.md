# New Signals of Quark-Gluon-Hadron Mixed Phase Formation

> Here we present several remarkable irregularities at chemical freeze-out which are found using an advanced version of the hadron resonance gas model. The most prominent of them are the sharp peak of the trace anomaly existing at chemical freeze-out at the center of mass energy 4.9 GeV and two sets of highly correlated quasi-plateaus in the collision energy dependence of the entropy per baryon, total pion number per baryon, and thermal pion number per baryon which we found at the center of mass energies 3.8-4.9 GeV and 7.6-10 GeV. The low energy set of quasi-plateaus was predicted a long time ago. On the basis of the generalized shock-adiabat model we demonstrate that the low energy correlated quasi-plateaus give evidence for the anomalous thermodynamic properties inside the quark-gluon-hadron mixed phase. It is also shown that the trace anomaly sharp peak at chemical freeze-out corresponds to the trace anomaly peak at the boundary between the mixed phase and quark gluon plasma. We argue that the high energy correlated quasi-plateaus may correspond to a second phase transition and discuss its possible origin and location. Besides we suggest two new observables which may serve as clear signals of these phase transformations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that several irregularities observed at chemical freeze-out in heavy-ion collisions, specifically a sharp peak in the trace anomaly at a center-of-mass energy of 4.9 GeV and two sets of correlated quasi-plateaus in entropy and pion-number ratios, are signals of quark-gluon-hadron mixed phase formation and possibly a second phase transition. The authors hedge that the trace anomaly peak is produced by only one data point and its significance should be considered with caution, though they note the error bars at 4.9 GeV and 6.3 GeV do not overlap.

## Method

The authors used an advanced version of the hadron resonance gas model (HRGM) with multicomponent hard-core repulsion, assigning different hard-core radii for pions, kaons, Lambda hyperons, other mesons, and baryons. They performed a global fit of 121 independent hadron multiplicity ratios measured at AGS, SPS, and RHIC across 14 collision energies from 2.7 GeV to 200 GeV, minimizing chi-squared to determine local fitting parameters (temperature, baryonic chemical potential, isospin chemical potential, strangeness suppression factor) and global hard-core radii. The generalized shock-adiabat model was then used to interpret the low-energy quasi-plateaus as evidence for anomalous thermodynamic properties inside the mixed phase.

## Key numbers

- The sharp peak of the trace anomaly (epsilon - 3p)/T^4 at chemical freeze-out occurs at center-of-mass energy sqrt(s_NN) = 4.9 GeV.
- The low-energy set of correlated quasi-plateaus in entropy per baryon, total pion number per baryon, and thermal pion number per baryon is found at sqrt(s_NN) = 3.8-4.9 GeV (laboratory energies 6.9-11.6 GeV).
- The high-energy set of correlated quasi-plateaus is found at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.6-10 GeV (laboratory energies 30-45 GeV).
- The effective number of degrees of freedom p/T^4 increases by 70% while the collision energy changes by 15% or the CFO temperature changes by 30% in the range sqrt(s_NN) = 4.3-4.9 GeV.
- The mixed phase in baryonic density ranges from about 2 fm^{-3} at the hadronic side to about 10 fm^{-3} at the QGP side.
- The best global fit of 111 hadronic multiplicities gave chi^2/dof approximately 0.95 with hard-core radii R_b = 0.355 fm, R_m = 0.4 fm, R_pi = 0.1 fm, R_K = 0.38 fm, and R_Lambda = 0.11 fm.
- The fit including the Lambda/p ratio (121 ratios) gave chi^2/dof = 63.98/65 approximately 0.98 with hard-core radii R_b = 0.385 fm, R_m = 0.41 fm, R_pi = 0.19 fm, R_K = 0.39 fm, and R_Lambda = 0.09 fm.
- The meta-analysis predicts the most probable collision energy range of the second phase transition is sqrt(s_NN) = 10-13.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous findings (Bugaev 2013, 2014) on irregularities at chemical freeze-out, including a sudden pressure jump and trace anomaly peak, and confirms a long-standing prediction (Bugaev 1989, 1990, 1991) of quasi-plateaus based on relativistic hydrodynamics. The results are independently supported by a meta-analysis (2015) comparing hadron models and QGP models, which validates the shock-adiabat model framework and the identification of the mixed phase between sqrt(s_NN) = 4.3 and 4.9 GeV. The paper enables experimental searches for two phase transitions at NICA and FAIR, suggesting new observables (Delta Lambda / Delta p and Delta K+ / Delta p) as indicators, and connects to lattice QCD studies where the trace anomaly maximum is used to determine the pseudo-critical temperature of the crossover transition.
