# On Lowest Lying $\frac{1}{2}^-$ Octet Baryons

> The recently proposed $N^*(890)$ $1/2^-$ baryon is studied in a flavor $SU(3)$ scheme with $K$ matrix unitarization, by fitting to low energy cross section and phase shift data. It is found that $N^*(890)$ co-exists with low lying poles in other channels, which have been extensively discussed in the literature, though they belong to different octets, in the $SU(3)$ limit. Hence the existence of $N^*(890)$ is further verified.

## Key claim

The paper verifies the existence of the recently proposed \(N^*(890)\) \(1/2^-\) baryon by showing that it co-exists with well-accepted low-lying poles in other strangeness sectors (such as \(\Lambda^*(1405)\) and \(\Lambda^*(1380)\)) within a unified flavor \(SU(3)\) framework, though these poles belong to different octets in the \(SU(3)\) limit.

## Method

The authors constructed a flavor \(SU(3)\) chiral perturbation theory (\(\chi\)PT) Lagrangian at \(O(p^2)\) for meson-baryon interactions and unitarized the scattering amplitudes using a \(K\)-matrix scheme with dimensional regularization. They performed coupled-channel fits to low-energy cross section and phase shift data for \(\pi N \to \pi N\) and several \(K^- p\) reactions in the \(s=-1\) sector, using an \(s\)-wave approximation, and then searched for poles in the unitarized amplitudes on different Riemann sheets.

## Key numbers

* \(N^*(890)\) pole location: \(1.066 - 0.280 i\) GeV, with couplings \(|g_{N\pi}| = 0.617\) and \(|g_{N\eta}| = 0.436\).
* \(N^*(1535)\) pole location: \(1.553 - 0.056 i\) GeV, with couplings \(|g_{N\pi}| = 0.645\) and \(|g_{N\eta}| = 1.031\).
* \(\Lambda^*(1380)\) pole location: \(1.345 - 0.143 i\) GeV, with couplings \(|g_{\Sigma\pi}| = 1.032\) and \(|g_{N\bar{K}}| = 0.702\).
* \(\Lambda^*(1405)\) pole location: \(1.423 - 0.017 i\) GeV, with couplings \(|g_{\Sigma\pi}| = 0.453\) and \(|g_{N\bar{K}}| = 0.966\).
* \(\Sigma^*(1380)\) pole location: \(1.305 - 0.392 i\) GeV, with couplings \(|g_{\Sigma\pi}| = 0.578\) and \(|g_{N\bar{K}}| = 1.346\).
* \(\Sigma^*(1650)\) pole location: \(1.444 - 0.109 i\) GeV, with couplings \(|g_{\Sigma\pi}| = 0.940\) and \(|g_{N\bar{K}}| = 1.084\).
* \(\Xi^*\) Pole1 location: \(1.636 - 0.0001 i\) GeV, with couplings \(|g_{\Xi\pi}| = 0.262\) and \(|g_{\Lambda\bar{K}}| = 0.335\).
* \(\Xi^*\) Pole2 location: \(1.696 - 0.246 i\) GeV, with couplings \(|g_{\Xi\pi}| = 1.087\) and \(|g_{\Lambda\bar{K}}| = 1.208\).
* Fit quality: \(\chi^2_{d.o.f} = 5.1\).
* Renormalization scale parameters: \(\mu_{S=0} = 0.703 \pm 0.053\) GeV, \(\mu_{S=-1} = 0.727 \pm 0.008\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the \(K\)-matrix analysis of \(N^*(890)\) by Ma (2020) and the \(N/D\) studies by Li (2021) into a full flavor \(SU(3)\) framework, connecting the nucleon pole to the well-studied twin-pole structure of \(\Lambda^*(1405)\) and \(\Lambda^*(1380)\) found by Guo (2012) and the \(\Sigma^*\) poles found by Jido (2003) and Oller (2006). The results confirm that these low-lying \(1/2^-\) baryons are dynamically generated (molecular-type) states rather than elementary fields, and the \(SU(3)\) limit analysis shows that \(N^*(890)\) and \(N^*(1535)\) belong to different octets, further supporting the existence of \(N^*(890)\).
