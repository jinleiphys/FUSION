# Constructing a neutron star in G2-QCD

> The inner structure of neutron stars is still an open question. To make progress and understand the qualitative impact of gauge interactions on the neutron star structure we study neutron stars in a modified version of QCD. In this modification the gauge group of QCD is replaced by the exceptional Lie group G$_2$, which has neutrons and is accessible at finite density in lattice calculations. Using an equation of state constructed from lattice calculations we determine the mass-radius-relation for a neutron star in this theory using the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation. The results exhibit an influence of the non-trivial interactions on the mass-radius relation. However, the masses of the quarks are found to have little influence. We also give density profiles and the phase structure inside the neutron star. If the results carry over to full QCD, much of the internal structure of neutron stars could already be inferred from a precise measurement of the mass-radius relation.

## Key claim

Using lattice-derived equations of state for G₂-QCD (a sign-problem-free QCD-like theory with neutrons), the mass-radius relation of a neutron star can be constructed, and the results suggest that the non-trivial gauge interactions imprint themselves on the mass-radius relation, but the authors emphasize that the results are qualitative rather than (semi-)quantitative due to numerous approximations and systematic uncertainties.

## Method

The authors used lattice data from Wellegehausen (2013) for one-flavor G₂-QCD at two different Goldstone (diquark) masses (247 MeV and 326 MeV), with the neutron mass set to 938 MeV to fix the scale. They constructed an equation of state by taking the baryon density from lattice simulations as a function of baryo-chemical potential, assuming the energy density is entirely given by the neutron rest mass times the baryon density (neglecting thermal kinetic energy and binding energy), and computing pressure via \( p = n\mu - \epsilon \). They then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation for a static, spherically symmetric metric and ideal fluid to obtain the mass-radius relation.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of the G₂-QCD neutron star: substantially increased compared to the free Fermi gas, but still much lower than the maximum observed masses of real neutron stars (no explicit numerical value given for the maximum mass in solar masses).
- Central density of the most massive stable neutron stars: roughly 2–2.5 per fm³, which is about fifteen times the QCD nuclear matter density of 0.17 per fm³.
- Goldstone (diquark) masses used: 247 MeV (light ensemble) and 326 MeV (heavy ensemble), from Wellegehausen (2013).
- Neutron mass set to 938 MeV to fix the scale.
- Transition chemical potential from free-gas approximation to lattice data: chosen as \(\mu_t = 5.07\) fm⁻¹ for the light ensemble and \(\mu_t = 5.04\) fm⁻¹ for the heavy ensemble.
- Strong change of slope in the mass-radius relation occurs at a radius of about 10 km and a mass of 0.4 solar masses for both ensembles.
- The velocity of sound was checked and found to be always below 1, consistent with thermodynamic stability.

## Context

This work extends the study of neutron star structure using first-principles lattice calculations, addressing the sign problem that prevents direct lattice QCD simulations at finite density. It builds on prior lattice studies of G₂-QCD by Wellegehausen (2013) and Maas (2012), which provided the equation of state and spectrum data. The results suggest that features of the mass-radius relation, such as changes in slope, could potentially be linked to different physical regimes inside neutron stars, implying that precise astronomical measurements of the mass-radius relation might reveal internal structure, though the authors caution that the approximations used (neglecting thermal kinetic energy, diquark contributions, and hybrid states) need to be lifted for quantitative predictions.
