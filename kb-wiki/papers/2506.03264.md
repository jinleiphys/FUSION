# Modelling Coincident Particle Production in Ultraperipheral Heavy Ion Collisions

> In this paper, we present an analysis of coincident particle production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions. In particular, we present the first detailed and differential predictions for coincident $ρ^0$ meson production in association with muon pair production, motivated by the recent ATLAS measurement of this process. These are found to describe the data very well, including the dependence of the coincidence fraction on the ZDC selection and/or other kinematic constraints on the muons. Differential predictions at the level of various kinematic variables are presented, and the calculation is made publicly available in the SuperChic MC generator. We also present general results for coincident two-photon initiated production focussing on muon and electron pair production; while the former is rather suppressed, the latter will be ubiquitous at threshold. The impact of coincident production on exclusivity vetos in ultraperipheral measurements is in addition discussed.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first detailed and differential predictions for coincident ρ⁰ meson production in association with muon pair production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions, finding that the data are described very well when accounting for the dependence of the coincidence fraction on ZDC selection and kinematic constraints on the muons, though the normalization is tied to ALICE data and the non-resonant π⁺π⁻ contribution is estimated rather than precisely calculated.

## Method
The calculation uses the SuperChic Monte Carlo generator, extending the standard equivalent photon approximation framework to include coincident particle production by replacing the ion dissociation cross section with the exclusive photoproduction cross section for ρ⁰ mesons in the impact parameter space formulation. The γA → ρ⁰A subprocess cross section is normalized using the experimental value from ALICE (2020) at 5.02 TeV, assuming a constant cross section over photon energy, and the coincident production probability is integrated over impact parameter with the ion-ion survival factor and breakup probabilities for different ZDC selections.

## Key numbers
- The γA → ρ⁰A cross section is set to 2.3 mb, giving a UPC cross section of 560 mb in the central |y_ρ⁰| < 0.2 bin, compared to the ALICE experimental value of 537 ± 4.6 (stat) +46.1/-42.0 (sys) mb.
- The non-resonant π⁺π⁻ contribution to the ATLAS measurement is estimated at approximately 20% based on STARLight predictions.
- Approximately 76% of ρ⁰ events with |y_ρ⁰| < 2.5 pass the ATLAS pion fiducial selection (|η_π| < 2.5, p_⊥,π > 100 MeV) according to STARLight.
- For coincident two-photon initiated dilepton production, the flux component F_γγ(b_⊥) at b_⊥ = 2R_A is approximately 0.3 (Z/Z_Pb)⁴ mb⁻¹, leading to a suppression of ~10⁻⁶ at dilepton invariant mass 10 GeV and ~10⁻⁴ at 1 GeV.
- For muon pair production at threshold, the integrated cross section σ_X,γγ is approximately 0.02 GeV⁻²; for electron pair production it is approximately 600 GeV⁻².

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework for multiple photon exchange in UPCs previously discussed by Klein (1999), Klusek-Gawenda (2016), and van Hameren (2017), and directly addresses the first observation of coincident ρ⁰ production with muon pairs by ATLAS (2025). The predictions are implemented in the SuperChic MC generator and enable future differential comparisons with ATLAS data, as well as providing a basis for understanding the impact of coincident production on exclusivity vetos in UPC measurements. The treatment of coincident two-photon initiated lepton pair production follows similar approaches to Hencken (2006) and Krachkov (2014), with the finding that multiple electron pair production near threshold is expected to be ubiquitous.
