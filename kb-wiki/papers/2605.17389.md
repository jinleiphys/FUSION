# An Analysis on the Parton Distribution Functions of Heavy Mesons

> In this work, we investigate the constituent parton distribution functions (PDFs) of the kaon and heavy pseudoscalar mesons within the light-cone quark model. Starting from the initial scale quark and antiquark PDFs, obtained by evaluating the quark-quark correlation functions for individual mesons, we perform quantum chromodynamics (QCD) evolution to determine the partonic structure at higher energy scales. The QCD evolution has been carried out through the next-to-leading order (NLO) Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi (DGLAP) equations. We further compute the average longitudinal momentum fractions carried by the individual constituents both at the model scale and evolved scale. In addition, the NLO structure functions for the kaon are predicted at higher energy scales relevant for the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). For the COMPASS++/AMBER experiment, we further present detailed predictions for the NLO Drell-Yan cross sections corresponding to both isospin states of the kaon, employing Carbon, Tungsten, and Aluminum as nuclear targets. We demonstrate the dominance of the heavy constituents over the lighter constituents within their heavy mesons in terms of their possessed momentum fractions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that within heavy mesons, the heavier constituent quark carries a larger fraction of the longitudinal momentum than the lighter constituent, and that this dominance persists after QCD evolution to higher energy scales. The claim is conditional on the light-cone quark model (LCQM) at the lowest Fock-state, where gluon and sea-quark contributions vanish at the initial scale.

## Method

The authors computed valence quark and antiquark parton distribution functions (PDFs) for kaons and heavy pseudoscalar mesons (D, B, Bc, eta_c, eta_b) by solving quark-quark correlation functions within the light-cone quark model, using the BHL prescription for the radial wave function and a spin wave function derived from the quark-meson vertex. The initial-scale PDFs were evolved to higher energy scales using next-to-leading order (NLO) Dokshitzer-Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi (DGLAP) evolution equations. For kaons, the NLO structure functions and Drell-Yan cross sections were predicted for Carbon, Tungsten, and Aluminum targets relevant to the COMPASS++/AMBER experiment.

## Key numbers

- For the kaon K⁻ (s ubar) at the initial scale: the s quark carries average momentum fraction ⟨x⟩ = 0.551, while the ubar antiquark carries ⟨x⟩ = 0.449.
- For the D⁺ meson (c dbar) at the initial scale: the c quark carries ⟨x⟩ = 0.714, while the dbar antiquark carries ⟨x⟩ = 0.286.
- For the B⁺ meson (u bbar) at the initial scale: the u quark carries ⟨x⟩ = 0.145, while the bbar antiquark carries ⟨x⟩ = 0.855.
- For the Bc⁺ meson (c bbar) at the initial scale: the c quark carries ⟨x⟩ = 0.290, while the bbar antiquark carries ⟨x⟩ = 0.710.
- For eta_c (c cbar) at the initial scale: both c and cbar carry ⟨x⟩ = 0.500.
- For eta_b (b bbar) at the initial scale: both b and bbar carry ⟨x⟩ = 0.500.
- The kaon PDFs were evolved from an initial scale Q0 in the range [0.50, 1.00] GeV up to Q² = 100 GeV², with a central value of Q0 = 0.75 GeV.
- The D-meson PDFs were evolved from an initial scale Q0 in the range [1.68, 3.36] GeV, with a central value of Q0 = 2.52 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of pion PDFs (Puhan 2026) to kaons and heavy pseudoscalar mesons within the same light-cone quark model framework. It provides predictions for the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and COMPASS++/AMBER experiments, where kaon structure functions and Drell-Yan cross sections can be measured. The results are compared with recent JAM extractions (Barry 2025) and lattice QCD simulations (Alexandrou 2021), and they confirm the SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking observed in the NA-003 experiment, where the ubar quark in K⁻ is softer than in pi⁻.
