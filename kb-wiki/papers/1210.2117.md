# Investigating the nuclear Schiff moment of $^{207}$Pb in ferroelectric PbTiO$_3$

> A positive experimental measurement of the nuclear Schiff moment would have important implications for physics beyond the standard model. To aid in the interpretation of a proposed experiment to measure the nuclear Schiff moment of $^{207}$Pb in the ferroelectric PbTiO$_3$, three-dimensional Hartree-Fock calculations have been performed to model the local electronic structure in the vicinity of the Pb nucleus. The energy shift due to the Schiff moment is found to be a factor of 2 smaller in comparison to existing estimates.

## Key claim

The energy shift due to the nuclear Schiff moment of \(^{207}\)Pb in ferroelectric PbTiO\(_3\) is approximately a factor of 2 smaller than a previous estimate, with an expected uncertainty of 30–50%.

## Method

Three-dimensional Hartree-Fock calculations were performed for a PbO\(_{12}\) cluster representing the first coordination sphere of Pb\(^{2+}\) in PbTiO\(_3\), using the GAMESS-US quantum chemistry code with a WTBS basis set. The electron density asymmetry around the Pb nucleus due to the ferroelectric displacement was calculated and matched to an analytical expression involving Pb atomic orbitals to extract the parameter \(\beta\), which was then used in a semi-relativistic formula to obtain the Schiff-moment-induced energy shift.

## Key numbers

- The energy shift due to the nuclear Schiff moment \(S\) of \(^{207}\)Pb in PbTiO\(_3\) is \(\Delta\epsilon \simeq -0.59 \times 10^6 \, (S / e a_B^3) \, \text{eV}\), compared to a previous estimate of \(-1.1 \times 10^6 \, (S / e a_B^3) \, \text{eV}\).
- The extracted parameter \(\beta\) ranges from \(-0.1\) to \(-0.125\) for effective Ti charges between \(+3\) and \(+4\), with an upper limit of \(\beta \simeq -0.125\) for a Ti charge of \(+3.5\); this is smaller than the prior estimate of \(\beta = -0.29\).
- The Pb displacement in the ferroelectric phase is \(0.47 \, \text{\AA}\), and the lattice parameters are \(a = 3.902 \, \text{\AA}\) and \(c = 4.156 \, \text{\AA}\) with \(c/a = 1.065\).
- The internal electric field in ferroelectric PbTiO\(_3\) is estimated as \(E_{\text{int}} \simeq 10^8 \, \text{V/cm}\).

## Context

This work refines the earlier estimate of the Schiff-moment energy shift in PbTiO\(_3\) by Sushkov (2009), which used a \(\beta\) value derived from a GdO\(_8\) cluster in a garnet structure. The present Hartree-Fock calculation for a PbO\(_{12}\) cluster yields a smaller effect, and the authors recommend that their result supersede the earlier one. The study supports ongoing experimental efforts to measure the nuclear Schiff moment in condensed matter systems, which could provide sensitivity orders of magnitude better than existing atomic limits from mercury (Griffith 2009), and is also relevant to proposals for detecting axion dark matter.
