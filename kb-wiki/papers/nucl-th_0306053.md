# Clarification of the relationship between bound and scattering states in quantum mechanics: Application to 12C + alpha

> Using phase-equivalent supersymmetric partner potentials, a general result from the inverse problem in quantum scattering theory is illustrated, i.e., that bound-state properties cannot be extracted from the phase shifts of a single partial wave, as a matter of principle. In particular, recent R-matrix analyses of the 12C + alpha system, extracting the asymptotic normalization constant of the 2+ subthreshold state, C12, from the l=2 elastic-scattering phase shifts and bound-state energy, are shown to be unreliable. In contrast, this important constant in nuclear astrophysics can be deduced from the simultaneous analysis of the l=0, 2, 4, 6 partial waves in a simplified potential model. A new supersymmetric inversion potential and existing models give C12=144500+-8500 fm-1/2.

## Key claim

Using phase-equivalent supersymmetric partner potentials, the paper demonstrates that bound-state properties (specifically the asymptotic normalization constant, ANC) cannot be extracted from the phase shifts of a single partial wave as a matter of principle, contrary to the assumptions made in recent R-matrix analyses of the 12C + alpha system.

## Method

The author constructs phase-equivalent potentials for the 12C + alpha system using supersymmetric quantum mechanics inversion techniques, starting from a phase-shift analysis of elastic-scattering data below 4.966 MeV. For the single partial wave case (l=2), a repulsive potential with no bound state is built that reproduces the background phase shifts, then bound states with arbitrary ANC values are added without changing the phase shifts. For the multi-partial-wave case, a deep local potential with Pauli forbidden states is constructed by simultaneously fitting l=0, 2, 4, and 6 data, with the regularization parameters and ANC varied to optimize the fit.

## Key numbers

- The 2+ subthreshold state energy is -244.9 +/- 0.6 keV (center-of-mass frame)
- The 0+ bound state energy is -1113 +/- 1 keV
- The 4+ resonance energy is 3194 +/- 3 keV with width 26 +/- 3 keV
- The 6+ resonance energy is 9113 +/- 7 keV with width 420 +/- 20 keV
- The supersymmetric inversion potential gives C12 = 137 x 10^3 fm^{-1/2}
- The Gaussian potential of Langanke (1985) gives C12 = 144 x 10^3 fm^{-1/2}
- The Woods-Saxon potential of Langanke (1985) gives C12 = 153 x 10^3 fm^{-1/2}
- The potential of Buck (1984) gives C12 = 136 x 10^3 fm^{-1/2}
- The combined theoretical prediction from all potential models is C12 = 144.5 +/- 8.5 x 10^3 fm^{-1/2}
- The experimental value from transfer reactions (Brune, 1999) is C12 = 114 +/- 10 x 10^3 fm^{-1/2}
- The R-matrix analysis of Angulo (2000) gave C12 = 402 x 10^3 fm^{-1/2}
- The R-matrix analysis of Tischhauser (2002) gave C12 = 154 +/- 18 x 10^3 fm^{-1/2}

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of Bargmann (1949) and the inverse-problem theory of Chadan (1977) by explicitly constructing phase-equivalent potentials for the 12C + alpha system, demonstrating that bound-state ANCs cannot be deduced from single-partial-wave scattering data. The paper contradicts the R-matrix analyses of Angulo (2000) and Tischhauser (2002), which attempted to extract the ANC of the 2+ subthreshold state from l=2 phase shifts alone. The multi-partial-wave inversion approach enables a reliable theoretical determination of C12 = 144.5 +/- 8.5 x 10^3 fm^{-1/2}, which is consistent with the potential models of Buck (1984) and Langanke (1985) but excludes the value from Angulo (2000) and the cascade-transition value from Buchmann (2001).
