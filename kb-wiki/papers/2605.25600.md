# Superfluid fraction and effective ion mass in the crystalline crust of a neutron star: role of interband response

> Neutron superfluidity in the inner crust of a neutron star is further investigated, focusing on the role of the interband response in the superfluid fraction and the effective mass of crustal ions induced by their motion through the superfluid. Calculations are performed within the linear response theory of the self-consistent time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations with Skyrme nuclear energy density functionals in the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer approximation. The absence of interband response in previous analyses is clarified. The neutron superfluid density is formally shown to be consistent with the entrainment matrix derived earlier in homogeneous neutron-proton superfluid mixture, thus providing a unified description of entrainment effects in the inner crust and outer core of a neutron star within the same microscopic framework. The relative importance of the intraband and interband responses in different regions of the crust is numerically assessed from three-dimensional band-structure calculations, taking into account quantum zero-point motion of ions about their equilibrium position. The neutron superfluid fraction is found to be enhanced by the interband response, resulting in effective ion masses that remain close to the mass of quantum mechanically bound nucleons for realistic neutron pairing gaps. Results are compared to predictions from classical hydrodynamics with different prescriptions for the permeability of ions to superfluidity.

## Key claim

The neutron superfluid fraction in the inner crust of a neutron star is enhanced by the previously neglected interband response, leading to effective ion masses that remain close to the mass of quantum mechanically bound nucleons for realistic neutron pairing gaps, contrary to earlier band-structure calculations that predicted very small superfluid fractions and large effective ion masses.

## Method

The author performed three-dimensional band-structure calculations within the linear response theory of the self-consistent time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations using Skyrme nuclear energy density functionals in the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) approximation. The relative importance of intraband and interband responses was numerically assessed in different crustal layers, taking into account quantum zero-point motion of ions about their equilibrium position.

## Key numbers

- The neutron superfluid fraction from classical hydrodynamics with impenetrable ions (Sedrakian 1996 prescription) ranges from about 0.6 to 0.8 depending on the crustal layer.
- In the opposite limit of vanishingly small pairing, three-dimensional band-structure calculations predicted a very small superfluid fraction of about 0.1 in the intermediate region of the inner crust.
- The effective ion mass formula from Martin and Urban (2016) is given by \( m_I^\star = Z m_p + N m_n \left(1-\delta+ \frac{(\delta-\Upsilon)^2}{\delta+2\Upsilon}\right) \), where \(\delta=\rho_{s,i}/\rho_{n,i}\) and \(\Upsilon=\rho_{n,o}/\rho_{n,i}\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies on neutron superfluid density in the inner crust, where the first paper (Chamel 2025) focused on BCS pairing and the second (Chamel 2025b) addressed quantum zero-point fluctuations and effective ion mass. The results resolve a discrepancy between earlier band-structure calculations predicting large effective ion masses and time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov simulations by Pecak (2024) showing weaker entrainment, as also confirmed by Almirante and Urban (2025, 2026) who demonstrated that the interband response was previously neglected. The findings enable more accurate modeling of pulsar glitch activity, neutron star cooling, and crustal oscillations.
