# Coulomb and nuclear effects in breakup and reaction cross sections

> We use a three-body Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) model to investigate Coulomb and nuclear effects in breakup and reaction cross sections. The breakup of the projectile is simulated by a finite number of square integrable wave functions. First we show that the scattering matrices can be split in a nuclear term, and in a Coulomb term. This decomposition is based on the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, and requires the scattering wave functions. We present two different methods to separate both effects. Then, we apply this separation to breakup and reaction cross sections of 7Li + 208Pb. For breakup, we investigate various aspects, such as the role of the alpha + t continuum, the angular-momentum distribution, and the balance between Coulomb and nuclear effects. We show that there is a large ambiguity in defining the 'Coulomb' and 'nuclear' breakup cross sections, since both techniques, although providing the same total breakup cross sections, strongly differ for the individual components. We suggest a third method which could be efficiently used to address convergence problems at large angular momentum. For reaction cross sections, interference effects are smaller, and the nuclear contribution is dominant above the Coulomb barrier. We also draw attention on different definitions of the reaction cross section which exist in the literature, and which may induce small, but significant, differences in the numerical values.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while the total breakup cross section from CDCC calculations is unambiguous, the separation into "Coulomb" and "nuclear" components is highly ambiguous: two different exact decomposition methods (Method 1 and Method 2) give strongly different individual Coulomb and nuclear contributions even though they yield identical total breakup cross sections.

## Method

The authors use a three-body Continuum Discretized Coupled Channel (CDCC) model where the 7Li projectile is described as an alpha + triton two-body system, with breakup simulated by square-integrable pseudostates. They derive two exact methods to decompose the scattering matrix into Coulomb and nuclear terms based on the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, requiring the full scattering wave functions. The methods are applied to 7Li + 208Pb breakup and reaction cross sections at center-of-mass energies of 26, 34, and 42 MeV, using alpha+208Pb and t+208Pb optical potentials from the literature.

## Key numbers

* Coulomb barrier height for 7Li + 208Pb: 27.7 MeV; barrier radius: 11.9 fm
* Maximum pseudostate energy used: Emax = 15 MeV; maximum angular momentum in 7Li: lmax = 3
* The 7/2- narrow resonance in 7Li is at 2.19 MeV experimentally, approximated at 2.1 MeV in the model
* Grazing angular momentum Lg ≈ 26 at E = 42 MeV and Lg ≈ 17 at E = 34 MeV
* At large angular momenta, the full CDCC and pure Coulomb distributions match starting around Lω ≈ 80 for E = 34 MeV and Lω ≈ 100 for E = 42 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Coulomb-nuclear interference in breakup reactions (Descouvemont et al. 1999, Hussein et al. 2006, Kibedi and Brown 2012) by providing exact decomposition methods within the CDCC framework, rather than relying on the DWBA approximation or the weak-coupling approximation used by Canto et al. (2015). The paper enables future analyses to properly account for the ambiguity in defining Coulomb and nuclear breakup components, and suggests a third method to address convergence problems at large angular momentum. The authors also clarify differences between two definitions of the reaction cross section found in the literature (Thompson 1988, Tostevin and Nunes 2009).
