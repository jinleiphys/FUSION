# Approximating the $S$ matrix for solving the Marchenko equation: the case of channels with different thresholds

> This work extends previous results on the inverse scattering problem within the framework of Marchenko theory (fixed-$l$ inversion). In particular, I approximate an $n$-channel $S$-matrix as a function of the first-channel momentum $q$ by a sum of a rational term and a truncated sinc series for each matrix element. Relativistic kinematics are taken into account through the correct momentum-energy relation, and the necessary minor generalization of Marchenko theory is given. For energies where only a subset of scattering channels is open, the analytic structure of the $S$-matrix is analyzed. I demonstrate that the submatrix corresponding to closed channels, particularly near their thresholds, can be reconstructed from the experimentally accessible submatrix of open channels.The convergence of the proposed method is verified by applying it to data generated from a direct solution of the scattering problem for a known potential, and comparing the reconstructed potential with the original one. Finally, the method is applied to the analysis of $S_{31}$ $πN$ scattering data.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Marchenko inverse scattering method to coupled channels with different thresholds, demonstrating that the submatrix corresponding to closed channels can be reconstructed from the experimentally accessible open-channel submatrix, but only under the condition that the arbitrary real matrix contribution to the closed-channel submatrix is irrelevant for the potential reconstruction.

## Method

The author approximates each element of the multichannel S-matrix as a function of the first-channel momentum q by a sum of a rational term (a unitary rational-fraction expansion) and a truncated sinc series correction. The input kernel of the Marchenko equation is then expressed in a manifestly separable form, allowing the integral equation to be solved analytically for the potential. Relativistic kinematics are incorporated through the correct momentum-energy relation, and the method is tested on synthetic data from a known Gaussian potential and applied to S31 πN scattering data from partial wave analysis.

## Key numbers

- The test potential used Gaussian form V(r) = V0 exp(-a r^2) with V0 = -1.5 fm^{-2} and a = 2 fm^{-2}
- Particle masses: m_N = 939 MeV (≈ 4.75 fm^{-1}), m_π = 139.6 MeV (≈ 0.708 fm^{-1}), m_22 = 3m_π = 418.8 MeV (≈ 2.12 fm^{-1})
- The inelastic threshold opens at q ≈ 1.69 fm^{-1} (corresponding to M = m_N + 3m_π)
- Input S_e(q) matrix calculated up to q ≈ 19.6 fm^{-1} with step δq ≈ 0.0491 fm^{-1}
- Polynomial degrees used: 36 for S^{(0)}_{11}, 44 for S^{(0)}_{12}, and 36 for S^{(0)}_{22}
- Basis parameters: R ≈ 13.95 fm and τ = π/(2R) ≈ 0.113 fm^{-1}
- The coupling parameter α ≈ 0.07 was extracted from sin(2ε) near threshold
- πN PWA data available up to E_lab ≈ 2.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous single-channel method (Khokhlov, 2024) to the multichannel case with different thresholds, generalizing earlier Marchenko theory developments by Cox (1975a, 1975b) for coupled partial waves. The approach addresses the challenge of spurious poles that arise when fitting high-precision partial wave analysis data using rational-fraction expansions, which had previously required artificial smoothing in NN scattering analyses up to 3 GeV (Geramb, 1994; Kohlhoff, 1994; Khokhlov, 2006, 2007). The method enables the inversion of scattering data for systems like πN scattering where relativistic effects are significant and multiple channels with different thresholds are present.
