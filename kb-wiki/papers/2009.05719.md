# Constraint on phase transition with the multimessenger data of neutron stars

> The equation of state (EoS) of the neutron star (NS) matter remains an enigma. In this work we perform the Bayesian parameter inference with the gravitational wave data (GW170817) and mass-radius observations of some NSs (PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0437-4715, and 4U 1702-429) using the phenomenologically constructed EoS models to search for a potential first-order phase transition. Our phenomenological EoS models take the advantages of current widely used parametrizing methods, which are flexible enough to resemble various theoretical EoS models. We find that the current observation data are still not informative enough to support/rule out phase transition, due to the comparable evidences for models with and without phase transition. However, the bulk properties of the canonical $1.4\,M_\odot$ NS and the pressure at around $2ρ_{\rm sat}$ are well constrained by the data, where $ρ_{\rm sat}$ is the nuclear saturation density. Moreover, strong phase transition at low densities is disfavored, and the $1σ$ lower bound of transition density is constrained to $1.84ρ_{\rm sat}$.

## Key claim

Current multimessenger data (GW170817 and mass-radius observations of three neutron stars) are not informative enough to support or rule out a first-order phase transition in neutron star matter, as the Bayesian evidence for models with and without phase transition are comparable (Bayes factor ~1.2). However, strong phase transitions at low densities are disfavored, with a 1-sigma lower bound on the transition density of 1.84 times the nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors constructed two phenomenological equation-of-state (EoS) models (phase transition PT and no phase transition NPT) using a hybrid parametrization combining piecewise polytrope, causal spectral representation, and constant-speed-of-sound methods. They performed Bayesian parameter inference with the Bilby and dynesty packages, jointly analyzing gravitational wave strain data from GW170817 and mass-radius posterior samples from PSR J0030+0451 (NICER), PSR J0437-4715, and 4U 1702-429, using the likelihood that includes both the GW likelihood and kernel density estimates of the mass-radius measurements.

## Key numbers

- Radius of canonical 1.4 solar mass neutron star: R_{1.4} = 12.67_{-0.53}^{+0.64} km (PT model) and 12.67_{-0.51}^{+0.54} km (NPT model), both at 90% confidence.
- Tidal deformability of canonical 1.4 solar mass neutron star: Lambda_{1.4} = 508_{-126}^{+209} (PT model) and 506_{-119}^{+163} (NPT model), at 90% confidence.
- 1-sigma lower bound on phase transition onset density: rho_2 > 1.84 rho_sat, where rho_sat is nuclear saturation density.
- 90% lower bound on transition pressure: p_2 > 1.70 x 10^34 dyn/cm^2.
- 90% upper limit on adiabatic index for NPT model: Gamma_m < 4.68.
- Bayes factor between PT and NPT models: B_{NPT}^{PT} ~ 1.2.

## Context

This work extends Bayesian analyses of neutron star EoS (e.g., Jiang 2020, Landry 2020) by using a flexible phenomenological parametrization that can mimic various theoretical EoS models, including those with first-order hadron-quark phase transitions. The results are consistent with the finding of Annala (2020) that a sizable quark core may be present in massive neutron stars, and with the constraint from Chatziioannou (2020) that strong phase transition below 1.7 rho_sat (1-sigma) was ruled out. The comparable Bayes factor indicates that future precise mass-radius measurements of massive neutron stars (e.g., PSR J0740+6620 from NICER) will be needed to distinguish between models with and without phase transition.
