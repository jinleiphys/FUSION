# Masses of the Sub-Nuclear Particles

> The masses of the quarks and leptons are for the most part a mystery to particle physicists. Currently there seems to be no correlation between the masses of the elementary particles. This paper is an attempt to formulate a theory that would begin to explain the relationship between the sub-nuclear particle masses.

## Key claim

The paper attempts to formulate a theory that would begin to explain the relationship between the sub-nuclear particle masses, despite the current lack of correlation between elementary particle masses. The author proposes that there are only three generations of quarks and leptons, and presents a table of currently accepted masses for these particles.
