# Phase Variation of Hadronic Amplitudes

> The phase variation with angle of hadronic amplitudes is studied with a view to understanding the underlying physical quantities which control it and how well it can be determined in free space. We find that unitarity forces a moderately accurate determination of the phase in standard amplitude analyses but that the nucleon-nucleon analyses done to date do not give the phase variation needed to achieve a good representation of the data in multiple scattering calculations. Models are examined which suggest its behavior near forward angles is related to the radii of the real and absorptive parts of the interaction. The dependence of this phase on model parameters is such that if these radii are modified in the nuclear medium (in combination with the change due to the shift in energy of the effective amplitude in the medium) then the larger magnitudes of the phase needed to fit the data might be attainable, but only for negative values of the phase variation parameter.

## Key claim

Unitarity forces a moderately accurate determination of the phase in standard amplitude analyses, but the nucleon-nucleon analyses done to date do not give the phase variation needed to achieve a good representation of the data in multiple scattering calculations; if the radii of the real and absorptive parts of the interaction are modified in the nuclear medium, larger magnitudes of the phase might be attainable, but only for negative values of the phase variation parameter.

## Method

The authors used eikonal approximations and partial-wave expansions to study the phase variation of hadronic amplitudes with momentum transfer. They examined three potential models (Gaussian, exponential, and Woods-Saxon) to relate the phase parameter a_I to the geometric structure of the interaction, and performed a fitting analysis for K+p scattering to determine how well the phase can be constrained by unitarity and experimental data. The nucleon-nucleon amplitude was extracted from the phase shift analysis of Arndt et al. (2007) and averaged over spin and isospin.

## Key numbers

- The phase parameter a_I from the Arndt et al. (2007) NN analysis at 2.0 GeV/c for the exponential potential fit was -2.01 (GeV/c)^{-2}; increasing the real potential radius by 10% changed this to -3.52 (GeV/c)^{-2}, and by 20% to -5.70 (GeV/c)^{-2}
- For the Woods-Saxon potential at 2.0 GeV/c, a_I went from -1.96 to -3.13 to -4.68 (GeV/c)^{-2} as the real potential radius increased by 0%, 10%, and 20%
- The uncertainty in a_I from the K+p phase analysis at 1.3 GeV/c was estimated as ±0.2 (GeV/c)^{-2} based on an increase in chi-squared of 4
- Franco and Yin (1974) found that a_I values of +10 and -15 (GeV/c)^{-2} at 1.75 GeV/c per nucleon gave dramatic improvement in multiple scattering calculations for light nuclei
- The Gaussian slope parameter a_R was taken as 5.6 (GeV/c)^{-2} at 1.75 GeV/c beam momentum

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies of Franco and Yin (1974), who found that the phase variation parameter a_I strongly influenced multiple scattering in light nuclei, and Ahmad and Alvi (2002), who estimated a_I should have magnitude of order 1 (GeV/c)^{-2} or less using a potential with equal ranges for real and imaginary parts. The authors show that the discrepancy arises because the real and imaginary parts of the nucleon-nucleon interaction have significantly different ranges, and that the phase parameter a_I is directly related to the difference in radii of these components. The results enable improved multiple scattering calculations in nuclear media by providing constraints on how the phase variation parameter depends on the underlying interaction geometry.
