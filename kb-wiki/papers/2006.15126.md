# The JIMWLK evolution and the s-channel unitarity

> Further developing ideas set forth in \cite{KLL}, we discuss QCD Reggeon Field Theory (RFT) and formulate restrictions imposed on its Hamiltonian by the unitarity of underlying QCD. We identify explicitly the QCD RFT Hilbert space, provide algebra of the basic degrees of freedom (Wilson lines and their duals) and the algorithm for calculating the scattering amplitudes. We formulate conditions imposed on the "Fock states" of RFT by unitary nature of QCD, and explain how these constraints appear as unitarity constraints on possible RFT hamiltonians that generate energy evolution of scattering amplitudes. We study the realization of these constraints in the dense-dilute limit of RFT where the appropriate Hamiltonian is the JIMWLK Hamiltonian $H_{JIMWLK}$. We find that the action $H_{JIMWLK}$ on the dilute projectile states is unitary, but acting on dense "target" states it violates unitarity and generates states with negative probabilities through energy evolution.

## Key claim

The JIMWLK Hamiltonian, when acting on dense target states, violates s-channel unitarity by generating states with negative probabilities during energy evolution, even though it preserves unitarity when acting on dilute projectile states.

## Method

The authors formulate QCD Reggeon Field Theory (RFT) by identifying the Hilbert space and operator algebra of Wilson lines and their duals, then derive unitarity constraints on the RFT Hamiltonian. They analyze the JIMWLK Hamiltonian in the dense-dilute limit, testing its action on both projectile and target Fock states to determine whether the evolution preserves the probabilistic interpretation required by unitarity.

## Key numbers

- The toy model commutation relation is \([P,\bar P]=-\gamma\) with \(\gamma\sim O(\alpha_s^2)\), where \(\gamma\) is the zero-dimensional proxy for the dipole-dipole scattering amplitude.
- The toy model BK Hamiltonian is \(H_{BK}=-\frac{1}{\gamma}[P\bar P - P\bar P^2]\).
- For the toy model projectile evolution over infinitesimal interval \(\Delta\): \(e^{\Delta H}|\bar n\rangle \approx (1-\Delta \bar n)|\bar n\rangle + \Delta \bar n |\bar n+1\rangle\).
- For the toy model target evolution over infinitesimal interval \(\Delta\): \(\langle m|e^{\Delta H} = (1+\Delta m)\langle m| - \Delta m[1+\gamma(m-1)]\langle m-1| + \Delta\gamma m(m-1)\langle m-2|\).
- The unitarized toy model Hamiltonian is \(H_{UTM}=-\frac{1}{\gamma}\bar P P = -\frac{1}{\gamma}(1-\bar d)(1-d)\).
- For the unitarized toy model: \(\langle m|e^{\Delta H_{UTM}} = [1-\frac{\Delta}{\gamma}[1-(1-\gamma)^m]]\langle m| + \frac{\Delta}{\gamma}[1-(1-\gamma)^m]\langle m+1|\).

## Context

This work extends ideas from Kovner, Levin, and Lublinsky (KLL) on unitarity constraints in QCD Reggeon Field Theory, building on the Gribov RFT framework and the JIMWLK evolution equation from the Color Glass Condensate formalism. The authors show that the widely used JIMWLK Hamiltonian violates unitarity when evolving dense target states, a problem previously identified in the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) and Braun Hamiltonians. This analysis enables future work to construct modified RFT Hamiltonians that preserve unitarity while reproducing the correct dense-dilute limit, analogous to the unitarized toy model solution found in KLL.
