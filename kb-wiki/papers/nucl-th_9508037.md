# $g_{K N Λ}$ and $g_{K N Σ}$ from QCD sum rules

> $g_{K N Λ}$ and $g_{K N Σ}$ are calculated using a QCD sum rule motivated method used by Reinders, Rubinstein and Yazaki to extract Hadron couplings to goldstone bosons. The SU(3) symmetry breaking effects are taken into account by including the contributions from the strange quark mass and assuming different values for the strange and the up down quark condensates. We find $g_{K N Λ}/\sqrt{4 π} = - 1.96 $ and $g_{K N Σ}/\sqrt{4 π} = 0.33 $

# g_{KNΛ} and g_{KNΣ} from QCD sum rules

## Key claim

The authors extract the kaon-hyperon-nucleon coupling constants g_{KNΛ} and g_{KNΣ} using a QCD sum rule method, finding values that are smaller than those extracted from phenomenological fits to kaon-nucleon scattering and photoproduction data. The results carry a ±30% uncertainty from neglecting continuum contributions, and only the absolute values are determined because the signs of the baryon current couplings cannot be fixed from the baryon sum rules.

## Method

The authors compute three-point correlation functions constructed from baryon currents (nucleon, Λ, Σ) and a pseudoscalar kaon current, following the method of Reinders, Rubinstein and Yazaki (1981) for hadron couplings to Goldstone bosons. The operator product expansion (OPE) side includes the leading quark condensate contributions with SU(3) symmetry breaking from the strange quark mass (m_s = 150 MeV) and a reduced strange quark condensate ⟨0|s̄s|0⟩ = 0.8 ⟨0|ūu|0⟩, plus dimension-7 operators proportional to m_s ⟨0|s̄s|0⟩ ⟨0|q̄q|0⟩. The phenomenological side is modeled with pole contributions from the ground-state baryons and kaon, and a Borel transformation is applied to suppress excited-state contributions.

## Key numbers

- |g_{KNΛ}/√(4π)| ≈ 1.96, with ±30% uncertainty from neglecting continuum contributions
- |g_{K⁻NΣ⁰}/√(4π)| ≈ 0.33, obtained using the same approximation with M ≈ ½(M_N + M_Σ)
- The SU(3) symmetric limit (QSR I) gives g_{KNΛ}/√(4π) = -2.76 and g_{KNΣ}/√(4π) = 0.44, using α_D = 7/12
- The strange quark mass is taken as m_s = 150 MeV; the strange condensate ratio is γ ≡ ⟨0|s̄s|0⟩/⟨0|q̄q|0⟩ - 1 ≈ -0.2
- The leading quark condensate parameter a ≡ -(2π)² ⟨0|q̄q|0⟩ ≈ 0.5 GeV³; the gluon condensate parameter b ≡ π² ⟨0|(α_s/π)G²|0⟩ ≈ 0.17 GeV⁴

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule method of Reinders, Rubinstein and Yazaki (1981, 1985) from pion-nucleon couplings to the kaon sector with explicit SU(3) symmetry breaking. The results are compared to phenomenological extractions from kaon-nucleon scattering analyses by Martin (1981) and Antolin (1986), and from photoproduction analyses by Adelseck and Saghai (1990, 1992) and Mart et al. (1995), which give g_{KNΛ}/√(4π) ranging from -4.4 to -3.0 and g_{KNΣ}/√(4π) from +0.9 to +1.3. The QCD sum rule values fall short of these experimental limits but are closer than predictions from Skyrme model calculations (Gobbi, Riska and Scoccola, 1992; Kim et al., 1993). The authors note that experimental extractions involve simultaneous determination of many unknown parameters and model-dependent subprocesses, motivating further theoretical and experimental investigation.
