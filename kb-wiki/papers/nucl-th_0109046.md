# $Δ(1232)$ Isobar Excitations and the Ground State of Nuclei

> The influence of $Δ$ isobar components on the ground state properties of nuclear systems is investigated for nuclear matter as well as finite nuclei. Many-body wave functions, including isobar configurations, and binding energies are evaluated employing the framework of the coupled-cluster theory. It is demonstrated that the effect of isobar configurations depends in a rather sensitive way on the model used for the baryon-baryon interaction. As examples for realistic baryon-baryon interactions with explicit inclusion of isobar channels we use the local ($V28$) and non-local meson exchange potentials (Bonn$_{2000}$) but also a model recently developed by the Salamanca group, which is based on a quark picture. The differences obtained for the nuclear observables are related to the treatment of the interaction, the $π$-exchange contributions in particular, at high momentum transfers.

## Key claim

The effect of Δ-isobar configurations on nuclear ground-state properties depends sensitively on the baryon-baryon interaction model used, with differences in the treatment of π-exchange at high momentum transfers and short-range behavior leading to variations in predicted Δ probabilities by more than a factor of two.

## Method

The authors employed the coupled-cluster method (restricted to two-particle–two-hole excitations, S₂) to compute ground-state energies and Δ-isobar probabilities for both infinite nuclear matter and the finite nucleus ¹⁶O. They compared three baryon-baryon interaction models that explicitly include NΔ and ΔΔ channels: the local Argonne V28 potential, the non-local meson-exchange Bonn₂₀₀₀ potential, and the Salamanca Chiral Quark Cluster (CQC) model. For nuclear matter, the coupled-cluster equations were solved as integral equations with the angle-average approximation for the Pauli operator; for ¹⁶O, the correlated two-body wave function was expanded in a relative-coordinate basis within a box.

## Key numbers

- For nuclear matter at the empirical saturation density (Fermi momentum k_F = 1.36 fm⁻¹), the total Δ probability differs by more than a factor of two between interaction models.
- For ¹⁶O, the calculated Δ probability P_Δ is 1.98% for the Salamanca CQC model and 3.87% for the Argonne V28 potential.
- The binding energy per nucleon for ¹⁶O is smallest for Argonne V28, about 0.7 MeV larger for Bonn₂₀₀₀, and about 2.4 MeV larger for the Salamanca CQC model.
- All calculations including isobar configurations yield less attractive binding energies than the conventional calculation without explicit isobars (using Argonne V14).

## Context

This work updates earlier studies from more than 20 years prior (e.g., Weber, Green, Anastasio, Manz) that used simple local π-exchange transition potentials and found strong quenching of binding. It extends those results by employing modern interaction models: the local Argonne V28 potential, the non-local Bonn₂₀₀₀ meson-exchange potential (Machleidt 2000), and the Salamanca Chiral Quark Cluster model (Entem et al.). The results show that the local approximation of π-exchange overestimates isobar contributions compared to non-local treatments, as previously noted by Frick et al. (2001). The findings enable future inclusion of three-body terms involving isobars and provide a baseline for interpreting experiments that attempt to measure isobar components in nuclei (exp98).
