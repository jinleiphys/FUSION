# The pygmy dipole strength, the neutron radius of ${}^{208}$Pb and the symmetry energy

> The accurate characterization of the nuclear symmetry energy and its density dependence is one of the outstanding open problems in nuclear physics. A promising nuclear observable in order to constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy at saturation is the neutron skin thickness of medium and heavy nuclei. Recently, a low-energy peak in the isovector dipole response of neutron-rich nuclei has been discovered that may be correlated with the neutron skin thickness. The existence of this correlation is currently under debate due to our limited experimental knowledge on the microscopic structure of such a peak. We present a detailed analysis of Skyrme Hartree-Fock (HF) plus random phase approximation (RPA) predictions for the dipole response in several neutron-rich nuclei and try to elucidate whether models of common use in nuclear physics confirm or dismiss its possible connection with the neutron skin thickness. Finally, we briefly present theoretical results for parity violating electron scattering on ${}^{208}$Pb at the conditions of the PREx experiment and discuss the implications for the neutron skin thickness of ${}^{208}$Pb and the slope of the symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the low-energy peak in the dipole response (pygmy dipole resonance) of neutron-rich nuclei is correlated with the neutron skin thickness and the slope of the symmetry energy, finding that the collectivity of the isoscalar dipole response supports such a correlation while the isovector counterpart is model dependent.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme Hartree-Fock (HF) plus random phase approximation (RPA) calculations for the dipole response of 208Pb using three Skyrme interactions (SGII, SLy5, and SkI3) that differ in their predictions of the symmetry energy slope parameter L. They analyzed the reduced transition strengths and particle-hole contributions to determine the collective character of the low-energy states. Additionally, they calculated parity violating asymmetries for electron scattering on 208Pb at PREx kinematics using distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) with more than forty mean-field models.

## Key numbers

- The Skyrme models used have L parameters of 37.6 MeV (SGII), 48.3 MeV (SLy5), and 100.5 MeV (SkI3)
- Experimental centroid energies for 208Pb are 7.37 MeV for the pygmy dipole resonance and 13.43 MeV for the isovector giant dipole resonance
- The parity violating asymmetry calculations were performed for 1.06 GeV electrons at a 5 degree scattering angle
- The PREx collaboration aims to determine the neutron radius of 208Pb within a 1 percent error

## Context

This work extends the debate on whether the pygmy dipole resonance correlates with the neutron skin thickness, as proposed by Klimkiewicz (2007), Carbone (2010), and Inakura (2011), but questioned by Reinhard (2010). The analysis uses the same approach that previously allowed determination of nuclear incompressibility from the giant monopole resonance (Colo, 2004) and the effective nucleon mass from the giant quadrupole resonance (Reinhard, 1999). The results enable future constraints on the symmetry energy slope parameter L from the planned second run of the PREx experiment at Jefferson Laboratory.
