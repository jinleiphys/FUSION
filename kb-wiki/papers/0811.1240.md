# Eigenvalues correlations and the distribution of ground state angular momenta for random many-body quantum systems

> The observed preponderance of ground states with angular momentum L=0 in many-body quantum systems with random two-body interactions is analyzed in terms of correlation coefficients (covariances) among different eigenstates. It is shown that the geometric analysis of Chau {\it et al.} can be interpreted in terms of correlations (covariances) between energy eigenvalues thus providing an entirely statistical explanation of the distribution of ground state angular momenta of randomly interacting quantum systems which, in principle, is valid for both fermionic and bosonic systems. The method is illustrated for the interacting boson model.

# Eigenvalues correlations and the distribution of ground state angular momenta for random many-body quantum systems

## Key claim

The observed preponderance of ground states with angular momentum L=0 in many-body quantum systems with random two-body interactions can be explained entirely by statistical correlations (covariances) between energy eigenvalues, rather than by dynamical mechanisms such as induced pairing or geometric chaoticity. This statistical explanation is exact for diagonal Hamiltonians where energies depend linearly on random two-body matrix elements, and can be extended to non-diagonal Hamiltonians via first-order perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors derived an explicit relation between the probability that a given state becomes the ground state and the covariance coefficients of energy eigenvalues, using the geometric framework of convex polytopes developed by Chau et al. (2007). For diagonal Hamiltonians where energy eigenvalues are linear combinations of random two-body matrix elements, the ground-state probability at a vertex of the convex polytope is expressed in terms of cosines of angles defined by covariance coefficients. For non-diagonal Hamiltonians, the method was extended by treating off-diagonal interactions in first-order perturbation theory to obtain approximately linear energy eigenvalues, then applying the covariance relation. The approach was illustrated for two schematic Hamiltonians of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM).

## Key numbers

- For the IBM Hamiltonian H1 with N=16 bosons, the exact total probability for a v=0 ground state is 75.0%, reproduced exactly (75.0%) by the diagonal approximation using covariances.
- For N=15 bosons with the same Hamiltonian, the exact total probability for v=0 ground state is 50.0%, reproduced as 49.0% in the diagonal approximation.
- For the IBM Hamiltonian H2 with N=3k bosons (k=1,2,...), the ground state has L=0 in 75% of cases and L=2N (maximum angular momentum) in 25% of cases.
- For all other values of total boson number N in H2, the ground state has L=0 in 50%, L=2 in 25%, and L=2N in 25% of cases.
- For the spherical solution of H1 with N=16 bosons, the exact v=0 probability is 83.3%, reproduced exactly (83.3%) in the diagonal approximation.

## Context

This work extends the geometric analysis of Chau et al. (2007), who showed that diagonal Hamiltonians correspond to convex polytopes and that ground-state probabilities depend on vertex angles. The authors reinterpret this geometry in terms of energy eigenvalue covariances, providing a statistical explanation that contrasts with earlier dynamical explanations such as induced pairing (Johnson, Bertsch, and Dean, 1998) and mean-field analysis (Barea, Bijker, and Frank, 2005). The method is valid for both fermionic systems (nuclear shell model) and bosonic systems (IBM), and the authors state it will be applied to shell-model problems in future work.
