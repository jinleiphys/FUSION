# Exact versus Taylor-expanded energy density in the study of the neutron star crust-core transition

> The importance of the fourth and higher order terms in the Taylor series expansion of the energy of the isospin asymmetric nuclear matter in the study of the neutron star crust-core phase transition is investigated using the finite range simple effective interaction. Analytic expressions for the evaluation of the second and fourth order derivative terms in the Taylor series expansion for any general finite range interaction of Yukawa, exponential or Gaussian form have been obtained. The effect of the nuclear matter incompressibility, symmetry energy and slope parameters on the predictions for the crust-core transition density is examined. The crustal moment of inertia is calculated and the prediction for the radius of the Vela pulsar is analyzed using different equations of state.

## Key claim

The fourth and higher order terms in the Taylor series expansion of the energy density of isospin asymmetric nuclear matter, often neglected in the parabolic approximation, have a significant influence on the predicted neutron star crust-core transition density; the inclusion of these terms reduces the transition density compared to the parabolic approximation, and using the exact (non-expanded) energy density yields results that differ from both the second-order and fourth-order Taylor expansions.

## Method

The authors used the finite-range simple effective interaction (SEI) with a Yukawa form factor to compute the energy density of asymmetric nuclear matter. They derived analytic expressions for the second-order and fourth-order derivative terms in the Taylor series expansion of the energy density for any general finite-range interaction of Yukawa, exponential, or Gaussian form. The crust-core transition density was determined by examining the onset of violation of the thermodynamical stability condition (V_thermal > 0) for beta-equilibrated charge-neutral matter, comparing results obtained with the exact energy density, the second-order (parabolic) approximation, and the fourth-order Taylor expansion.

## Key numbers

- For the SEI with gamma=1/2 (incompressibility K=240 MeV), the crust-core transition density rho_t obtained with the exact calculation is approximately 0.075 fm^{-3}, while the second-order Taylor expansion gives about 0.085 fm^{-3} and the fourth-order expansion gives about 0.078 fm^{-3}.
- For the SEI with gamma=1 (incompressibility K=283 MeV), the exact calculation yields a transition density of about 0.065 fm^{-3}, the second-order expansion gives about 0.075 fm^{-3}, and the fourth-order expansion gives about 0.068 fm^{-3}.
- The symmetry energy at saturation density E_s(rho_0) is 30.0 MeV for gamma=1/2 and 31.5 MeV for gamma=1, with slope parameters L(rho_0) of 46.5 MeV and 56.0 MeV, respectively.
- The saturation density rho_0 is 0.161 fm^{-3} for gamma=1/2 and 0.165 fm^{-3} for gamma=1, with binding energies e(rho_0) of -16.0 MeV and -15.8 MeV, respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Xu et al. (2009) and Cai et al. (2012) that examined the influence of higher-order terms in the Taylor expansion on the crust-core transition, but using the finite-range SEI rather than Skyrme-type or relativistic mean-field interactions. The results show that the parabolic approximation systematically overestimates the transition density, and that the exact calculation gives values between the second-order and fourth-order approximations. The findings enable more accurate predictions of the crustal moment of inertia and the radius of pulsars such as the Vela pulsar, which are relevant for interpreting pulsar glitch observations.
