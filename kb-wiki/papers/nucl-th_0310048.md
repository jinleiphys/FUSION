# Renormalization of EFT for nucleon-nucleon scattering

> The renormalization of EFT for nucleon-nucleon scattering in nonperturbative regimes is investigated in a compact parametrization of the $T$-matrix. The key difference between perturbative and nonperturbative renormalization is clarified. The underlying theory perspective and the 'fixing' of the prescriptions for the $T$-matrix from physical boundary conditions are stressed.

## Key claim
The paper argues that in nonperturbative nucleon-nucleon scattering, conventional perturbative counter-term renormalization of the potential fails to remove divergences from the T-matrix; instead, the divergent quantity \(\mathcal{G}\) (containing all loop iterations) must be separately renormalized, and the renormalization prescription must be "locked" with the potential to satisfy physical boundary conditions.

## Method
The author reformulates the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for nucleon-nucleon scattering into a compact nonperturbative parametrization \(T^{-1} = V^{-1} - \mathcal{G}\), where \(V\) is the potential and \(\mathcal{G}\) encodes all rescattering or loop effects. Using this form, the paper demonstrates analytically that introducing counter terms into \(V\) cannot simultaneously cancel divergences in both the numerator and denominator of \(\mathcal{G}\) for any nontrivial potential, except in the simplest case of a constant contact term. The analysis is then extended to discuss how renormalization must instead be performed at the integral level or via "endogenous" counter terms that enter before the nonperturbative summation.

## Key numbers
- The on-shell relation between the \(K\)-matrix and \(T\)-matrix is given as \(T^{-1}_{os} = K^{-1}_{os} + i\mu \frac{\sqrt{2\mu E}}{2\pi}\).
- A primary example shows phase shifts for the \(^1D_2\) channel predicted with a single parameter \(Re(\mathcal{G}_{l=2}) \approx g_{l=2;0}\) for energies up to 200 MeV, using potentials at leading order (LO), next-to-leading order (NLO), and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO).
- The low-energy expansion of \(\mathcal{G}\) is given as \(Re(\mathcal{G}_l(p)) = g_{l;0} + g_{l;1} p^2 + g_{l;2} p^4 + \cdots\), with coefficients to be determined from empirical phase shifts at low energies (e.g., \(E \leq 50\) MeV).

## Context
This work extends the ongoing debate on renormalization and power counting in chiral effective field theory for nuclear forces, following foundational work by Weinberg (1990s) and subsequent proposals by van Kolck (1999), Epelbaum et al. (2000), Cohen (1997), and the KSW scheme by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998). The paper clarifies why the Schrödinger equation approach (Beane et al., 2001) succeeds where perturbative counter-term subtraction fails, and it supports the view that renormalization consistency, rather than power counting alone, is the central issue. The proposed parametrization enables a new semi-phenomenological method to test chiral potentials without explicit loop calculations, and it points toward future work on determining the coefficients \(g_{l;n}\) from empirical phase shift data.
