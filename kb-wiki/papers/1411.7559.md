# A Light-Front approach to the $^3$He spectral function

> The analysis of semi-inclusive deep inelastic electron scattering off polarized $^3$He at finite momentum transfers, aimed at the extraction of the quark transverse-momentum distributions in the neutron, requires the use of a distorted spin-dependent spectral function for $^3$He, which takes care of the final state interaction effects. This quantity is introduced in the non-relativistic case, and its generalization in a Poincaré covariant framework, in plane wave impulse approximation for the moment being, is outlined. Studying the light-front spin-dependent spectral function for a J=1/2 system, such as the nucleon, it is found that, within the light-front dynamics with a fixed number of constituents and in the valence approximation, only three of the six leading twist T-even transverse-momentum distributions are independent.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, within light-front dynamics with a fixed number of constituents and in the valence approximation, only three of the six leading twist T-even transverse-momentum distributions (TMDs) for a nucleon are independent, a result that is not valid in full QCD but could serve as a test of the light-front description of semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) in the valence region.

## Method

The authors introduce a distorted spin-dependent spectral function for polarized $^3$He to account for final state interaction (FSI) effects in SIDIS, using a generalized eikonal approximation (GEA) with a Glauber operator. They also develop a light-front (LF) relativistic spectral function for $^3$He within relativistic Hamiltonian dynamics, using Melosh rotations to connect the LF spectral function to the non-relativistic instant-form spectral function. For the nucleon, they relate the LF spectral function to the quark-quark correlator and derive relations among the six T-even TMDs by expressing them in terms of three independent scalar functions $B_0$, $B_1$, $B_2$.

## Key numbers

* Proton effective polarization in $^3$He: $p_p = -0.023$ (non-relativistic, AV18 interaction)
* Neutron effective polarization in $^3$He: $p_n = 0.878$ (non-relativistic, AV18 interaction)
* Proton effective polarization with FSI: $p_p^{FSI} = -0.026$
* Neutron effective polarization with FSI: $p_n^{FSI} = 0.76$
* Longitudinal LF proton polarization: $-0.02231$ (compared to non-relativistic $-0.02263$)
* Longitudinal LF neutron polarization: $0.87248$ (compared to non-relativistic $0.87805$)
* Transverse LF proton polarization: $-0.02268$ (compared to non-relativistic $-0.02263$)
* Transverse LF neutron polarization: $0.87494$ (compared to non-relativistic $0.87805$)

## Context

This work extends previous non-relativistic calculations of nuclear effects in inclusive deep inelastic scattering off $^3$He (using the AV18 interaction) to SIDIS processes, incorporating FSI via GEA as previously applied to unpolarized SIDIS off the deuteron by the same group. The LF approach builds on Dirac's relativistic Hamiltonian dynamics and the Bakamijan-Thomas construction, aiming to provide a Poincare covariant framework for extracting neutron TMDs from $^3$He data at JLab at 12 GeV. The results enable a controlled extraction of neutron Sivers and Collins asymmetries from $^3$He measurements, and the LF spectral function can be applied to other processes such as deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS).
