# Higher Order Corrections to Density and Temperature of Fermions from Quantum Fluctuations

> A novel method to determine the density and temperature of a system based on quantum Fermionic fluctuations is generalized to the limit where the reached temperature T is large compared to the Fermi energy εf . Quadrupole and particle multiplicity fluctuations relations are derived in terms of T . The relevant Fermi integrals are numerically solved for any values of T and compared to the analytical approximations. The classical limit is obtained, as expected, in the limit of large temperatures and small densities. We propose simple analytical formulas which reproduce the numerical results, valid for all values of T . The entropy can also be easily derived from quantum fluctuations and give important insight for the behavior of the system near a phase transition. A comparison of the quantum entropy to the entropy derived from the ratio of the number of deuterons to neutrons gives a very good agreement especially when the density of the system is very low.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previously proposed method for determining density and temperature from quantum fermionic fluctuations to the regime where temperature is large compared to the Fermi energy, and provides analytical parameterizations valid for all values of T/εf, not just the low-temperature expansion.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the Fermi-Dirac integrals for multiplicity fluctuations (Eq. 4) and quadrupole fluctuations (Eq. 3) for arbitrary values of T/εf, then fit these numerical results with simple analytical formulas. They apply the method to CoMD (Constrained Molecular Dynamics) simulations of ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca collisions at beam energies from 4 to 100 MeV/A and fixed impact parameter b=1 fm, extracting temperature and density from the fitted fluctuation relations.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi energy of nuclear matter is given as εf = 36(ρ/ρ₀)^(2/3) MeV, with ground state density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- The lowest-order expansion for multiplicity fluctuations is ⟨(ΔN)²⟩/⟨N⟩ = (3/2)(T/εf), valid up to T/εf ≈ 0.5
- The lowest-order expansion for quadrupole fluctuations is valid only up to T/εf ≈ 0.2
- The fit for T/εf versus multiplicity fluctuations is: T/εf = -0.442 + 0.442/(1 - ⟨(ΔN)²⟩/⟨N⟩)^(0.656) + 0.345⟨(ΔN)²⟩/⟨N⟩ - 0.12(⟨(ΔN)²⟩/⟨N⟩)²
- The fit for the quantum correction factor F_QC is: F_QC|_fit = 0.2(T/εf)^(-1.71) + 1
- A rapid variation in energy density is observed around T ≈ 2 MeV for neutrons and T ≈ 3 MeV for protons, indicating a first-order phase transition

## Context

This work generalizes the fluctuation-based method of Zheng and Bonasera (2011) and the approach applied to trapped Fermi gases by Sanner et al. (2010), extending beyond the low-temperature expansion to cover all T/εf values. The method enables direct extraction of both density and temperature from experimental data or simulations without assuming classical statistics, and allows comparison of quantum entropy with entropy derived from deuteron-to-nucleon ratios using the law of mass action as in Csernai and Siemens.
