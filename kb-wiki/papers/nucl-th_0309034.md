# On the stability and the similarity of N=82 isotones

> Here we study the stability and the similarity of all even $58\leq Z\leq 70, N=82 isotones. We confirm the two decades old study of one of the authors (AA) regarding the extra-ordinary stability and the similarity of these nuclei. We present here a new evidence which shows very strongly the said magicity of those nuclei and as such there exists a new ``plateau'' of magicity. Three well studied theoretical models which have been successfully applied in nuclear physics are used here to study the above phenomena. None of these model is able to reproduce the similarity and the doubly magic character of these nuclei. Therefore this hints at ``new physics'' in these N=82 isotones.

# On the stability and the similarity of N=82 isotones

## Key claim
The authors argue that all even-Z N=82 isotones from Z=58 to Z=70 should be considered doubly magic, forming a "plateau of magicity," based on a new plot of single-neutron separation energies as a function of proton number. However, none of the three theoretical models tested (Skyrme-HFB, RMF, or INM) can reproduce the observed similarity and doubly-magic character, which the authors interpret as evidence for "new physics" beyond current nuclear models.

## Method
The authors re-examined the 20-year-old empirical claim of Abbas (1984) by plotting experimental single-neutron separation energies S1n as a function of proton number Z for N=82 isotones, comparing the pattern to known magic numbers at N=28 and N=50. They then tested three theoretical models, Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB), relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with NL3 interaction, and the infinite nuclear matter (INM) model, against the experimental S1n data for the N=82 isotonic chain, varying pairing treatments and deformation parameters in the RMF calculations.

## Key numbers
- The lowest first excited state (2+ or 3-) in Z=58, 60, 62, 64, 66, 68, 70 N=82 isotones is "very high and steady at about 1.6 MeV for all the nuclei."
- The ratio E1(4+)/E1(2+) for all these nuclei is approximately 1.3, consistent with doubly-magic character.
- The magnitude of S1n at Z=62, 64, 68, and 70 for N=82 is comparable to the magnitude of S1n at Z=50, which is one of the best-known magic numbers.
- RMF calculations used harmonic oscillator shells between NF=NB=12 to 14 and varied the basis deformation parameter β0 between -0.4 and 0.4 in steps of 0.1.

## Context
This work extends the two-decade-old empirical study of Abbas (1984), who first proposed that all even-Z N=82 isotones from Z=58 to Z=70 form a "plateau" of doubly-magic nuclei with striking similarity. The authors contrast this with the then-emerging concept of "changing magicity" observed in low-mass nuclei at N=6, 14, and 34 (Tan01, Thi00, Dlo03). The failure of the HFB, RMF, and INM models to reproduce the experimental S1n staggering suggests that missing physics, possibly A=3,4 clustering (Abbas 2001) or higher-order couplings in RMF theory (Arumugam 2003), must be incorporated, and the authors indicate that work in this direction is in progress.
