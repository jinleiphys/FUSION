# Coulomb breakup of drip-liners

> We show how to derive nuclear structure information on loosely bound nuclei far from the beta stability line from their Coulomb dissociation into the continuum. The 19C -> 18C + n process is taken as an example and it is shown that basic properties such as ground-state spin and parity and the neutron binding energy of the halo nucleus 19C can be derived from a comparison of theoretical calculations to experimental data.

# Coulomb breakup of drip-liners

## Key claim

The Coulomb dissociation cross section for ¹⁹C → ¹⁸C + n can discriminate between a J^π = 1/2⁺ and J^π = 5/2⁺ ground state for ¹⁹C, but only when shell-model calculations with realistic spectroscopic factors are used rather than simple single-particle Woods-Saxon potentials; the experimental data from RIKEN are consistent with J^π = 1/2⁺ and a neutron binding energy of 0.530 MeV, which is higher than the mass-evaluation value of 0.240 ± 0.100 MeV.

## Method

The Coulomb dissociation cross section for ¹⁹C on a Pb target at 67A MeV was calculated using the virtual photon method, relating the cross section to the E1 photo-disintegration cross section via detailed balance. Bound-state wave functions were computed both with a Woods-Saxon potential (r₀ = 1.236 fm, d = 0.62 fm, V_so = 7.0 MeV) and with shell-model calculations using the WBP and WBT interactions in a π(p)ν(sd) model space with a ¹⁴C core, implemented via the OXBASH code. The calculated cross sections were compared to experimental data from the RIKEN RIPS facility.

## Key numbers

- Neutron binding energy of ¹⁹C from mass evaluations: S_n = 160 ± 110 keV (AuWa95) and S_n = 240 ± 100 keV (Viei86, Gill87, Wout88, Orr91)
- Woods-Saxon potential parameters: r₀ = 1.236 fm (radius), d = 0.62 fm (diffuseness), V_so = 7.0 MeV; well depths for 2s_{1/2} and 1d_{5/2} orbits were V₀ = 40.14 MeV and V₀ = 40.31 MeV respectively, for a binding energy of 0.240 MeV
- Integrated cross section for J^π = 5/2⁺ assumption: maximum σ_CD ≈ 450 mb
- Experimental integrated cross section: σ_CD^exp = 1.19 ± 0.11 b
- Best-fit calculation for J^π = 1/2⁺ used neutron binding energy of 0.530 MeV and spectroscopic strength S_b = 0.67

## Context

This work extends earlier nuclear breakup experiments at MSU (Bazin 1995, 1998) and GSI (Baumann 1998) that had failed to determine the ground-state spin and parity or neutron separation energy of ¹⁹C. The analysis resolves the spin-parity ambiguity in favor of J^π = 1/2⁺ and confirms a higher neutron binding energy (~0.530 MeV) previously suggested by Bazin et al. from fragment momentum distributions. The approach enables future Coulomb dissociation studies of other halo candidates near the neutron drip-line, provided that shell-model calculations guide the interpretation of experimental data.
