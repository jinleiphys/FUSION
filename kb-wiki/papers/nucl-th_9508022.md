# Correlated $\bbox{πρ}$ Exchange in the $\bbox{NN}$ Interaction

> We evaluate the contribution to the nucleon-nucleon interaction due to correlated $πρ$ exchange in the $π$, $ω$, and $A_1$/$H_1$ channels by means of dispersion-theoretic methods based on a realistic meson exchange model for the interaction between $π$ and $ρ$ mesons. These processes have substantial effects: In the pionic channel it counterbalances the suppression generated by a soft $πNN$ form factor of monopole type with a cutoff mass of about 1 GeV; in the $ω$-channel it provides nearly half of the empirical repulsion, leaving little room for explicit quark-gluon effects.

## Key claim

Correlated πρ exchange provides a substantial contribution to the NN interaction that can compensate for a soft πNN form factor (cutoff mass ~1 GeV) in the pionic channel and supply nearly half of the empirical repulsion in the ω-channel, thereby reducing the need for explicit quark-gluon effects.

## Method

The authors used dispersion-theoretic methods to evaluate the NN potential from correlated πρ exchange, starting from a microscopic meson-exchange model for πρ scattering. They first computed the N̅N → πρ amplitude including πρ correlations via a Blankenbecler-Sugar scattering equation, then derived spectral functions from unitarity relations, and finally transformed these into the s-channel (NN) using dispersion integrals. The resulting potential was parametrized as effective sharp-mass meson exchange for the π and ω channels.

## Key numbers

- The correlated πρ exchange in the pionic channel counterbalances the suppression from a πNN form factor with cutoff mass Λ_πNN ≈ 1 GeV (compared to 1.3 GeV used in the full Bonn potential).
- The effective π' meson from correlated πρ exchange has a mass of 1020 MeV and coupling constant g²_π'NN/4π ≈ 9.
- The bare ωNN coupling constant was fixed at g⁰_ωNN/4π = 4.40.
- The π and ρ masses used were m_π and m_ρ = 769 MeV.
- The πNN cutoff mass of 1.3 GeV in the full Bonn potential was reduced to 1.0 GeV when including correlated πρ exchange.

## Context

This work extends the Bonn potential framework (Machleidt 1987) by including correlated πρ exchange, which was missing from earlier versions that already contained correlated ππ exchange (as σ' exchange). It addresses the long-standing discrepancy between the hard πNN form factor (Λ_πNN = 1.3 GeV) required by NN data and the softer values (Λ_πNN ≈ 0.8 GeV) favored by other sources (Liu 1994, Coon 1981/1990, Thomas 1989). The results enable a consistent description of NN interactions with softer form factors and reduce the need for explicit quark-gluon effects, while also suggesting that correlated πσ exchange (Ueda 1992) may contribute to the remaining phenomenological π' strength.
