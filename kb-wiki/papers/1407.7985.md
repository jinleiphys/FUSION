# Energy loss and (de)coherence effects beyond eikonal approximation

> The parton branching process is known to be modified in the presence of a medium. Colour decoherence processes are known to determine the process of energy loss when the density of the medium is large enough to break the correlations between partons emitted from the same parent. In order to improve existing calculations that consider eikonal trajectories for both the emitter and the hardest emitted parton, we provide in this work, the calculation of all finite energy corrections for the gluon radiation off a quark in a QCD medium that exist in the small angle approximation and for static scattering centres. Using the path integral formalism, all particles are allowed to undergo Brownian motion in the transverse plane and the offspring allowed to carry an arbitrary fraction of the initial energy. The result is a general expression that contains both coherence and decoherence regimes that are controlled by the density of the medium and by the amount of broadening that each parton acquires independently.

## Key claim

The authors derive a general expression for medium-induced single gluon emission off a quark that includes finite energy corrections beyond the eikonal approximation, valid in the small angle approximation and for static scattering centers. The result unifies energy loss, independent broadening of all propagating particles, and colour (de)coherence effects into a single formalism, with the transition between coherent and decoherent regimes controlled by the medium density and the amount of broadening each parton acquires independently.

## Method

The calculation uses the path integral formalism to describe the Brownian motion of all partons (quark and emitted gluon) in the transverse plane, allowing the offspring to carry an arbitrary fraction of the initial energy. Medium averages are performed by decomposing the medium length into regions with fixed numbers of propagators, factorising colour structure from transverse momentum dynamics, and evaluating path integrals using the dipole approximation with a semi-classical method. The colour structure is computed systematically via an infinitesimal expansion of Wilson lines, keeping field correlators up to second order, and at large \(N_c\) the 6-point function factorises into a dipole and an independent quadrupole.

## Key numbers

* The gluon carries longitudinal momentum fraction \(\zeta \in [0,1]\) and transverse momentum \(\mathbf{k}\), while the final quark carries longitudinal momentum fraction \((1-\zeta) p_{0+}\) and transverse momentum \(\mathbf{q}\).
* The dipole cross-section is expanded as \(n(\xi) \sigma (\mathbf{r}) \simeq \frac{1}{2} \hat{q} \mathbf{r}^2 + \mathcal{O} \left( \mathbf{r}^2 \ln \mathbf{r}^2 \right)\), where \(\hat{q}\) is the transport coefficient characterising the typical squared transverse momentum acquired per mean free path \(\lambda\).
* The formation time is defined as \(\tau_{form} = x_{2+} - x_{1+}\), where \(x_{1+}\) and \(x_{2+}\) are the emission points in the amplitude and conjugate amplitude respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations that considered eikonal trajectories for both the emitter and the hardest emitted parton, going beyond the soft limit and infinite medium approximations. It unifies three previously separate effects, radiative energy loss (Apolinario 2012), broadening (Ovanesyan 2011), and decoherence (Mehtar-Tani 2010, Casalderrey-Solana 2011, Armesto 2011), into a single expression. The kinematic setup differs from Ovanesyan (2011) by considering multiple soft interactions with the medium instead of a single hard scattering, and goes beyond Blaizot (2012) by considering a finite medium, yielding a decoherence parameter \(\Delta_{coh}\) that controls the factorisation of the spectrum of the two outgoing particles.
