# Relativistic superfluid hydrodynamics from field theory

> It is well known that the hydrodynamics of a zero-temperature superfluid can be formulated in field-theoretic terms, relating for example the superfluid four-velocity to the gradient of the phase of a Bose-condensed scalar field. At nonzero temperatures, where the phenomenology of a superfluid is usually described within a two-fluid picture, this relationship is less obvious. For the case of a uniform, dissipationless superfluid at small temperatures and weak coupling we discuss this relationship within a phi^4 model. For instance, we compute the entrainment coefficient, which describes the interaction between the superfluid and the normal-fluid components, and the velocities of first and second sound in the presence of a superflow. Our study is very general, but can also be seen as a step towards understanding the superfluid properties of various phases of dense nuclear and quark matter in the interior of compact stars.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates a one-to-one correspondence between a microscopic field-theoretic calculation (a complex scalar field with quartic interactions) and the covariant two-fluid hydrodynamic description of a relativistic superfluid, but only for the restricted case of a uniform, dissipationless superfluid at small temperatures and weak coupling.

## Method

The authors start from a massless, complex scalar field with a quartic interaction term (λφ⁴) and compute the effective action, stress-energy tensor, and current in the presence of a Bose-Einstein condensate with a nonzero superfluid velocity. They perform a small-temperature expansion (up to T⁶) and a weak-coupling expansion (λ ≪ 1) to evaluate the thermodynamic quantities analytically in the normal-fluid rest frame. The field-theoretic results are then translated into the covariant two-fluid formalism by identifying the effective action density with the generalized pressure and computing the entrainment coefficient A and the other coefficients B and C that relate the currents to their conjugate momenta.

## Key numbers

* The critical superfluid velocity beyond which the system becomes dissipative even at zero temperature is |v_s| = 1/√3.
* The effective action density up to T⁴ is given by (T/V)Γ ≈ μ⁴(1−v_s²)²/(4λ) + π²T⁴(1−v_s²)²/(10√3 (1−3v_s²)²).
* The T⁶ correction to the effective action density is −4π⁴T⁶(1−v_s²)²(5+30v_s²+9v_s⁴)/(105√3 μ² (1−3v_s²)⁵).
* The mass of the massive mode is √2 μ √(3−v_s²).
* The velocity of first sound in the superfluid rest frame is isotropic and equals 1/√3 within the approximation.

## Context

This work extends the field-theoretic understanding of zero-temperature superfluids to nonzero temperatures by explicitly computing the entrainment coefficient and sound velocities from a microscopic φ⁴ model. It builds on the covariant two-fluid formalism developed by Carter (1995) and Son (2000), and provides a concrete microscopic foundation for the "master function" approach used in superfluid hydrodynamics. The results enable future calculations of superfluid properties in dense nuclear and quark matter phases inside compact stars, though the authors note that dissipation and non-uniform flows remain to be addressed.
