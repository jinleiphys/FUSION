# Form Factors and Wave Functions of Vector Mesons in Holographic QCD

> Within the framework of a holographic dual model of QCD, we develop a formalism for calculating form factors of vector mesons. We show that the holographic bound states can be described not only in terms of eigenfunctions of the equation of motion, but also in terms of conjugate wave functions that are close analogues of quantum-mechanical bound state wave functions. We derive a generalized VMD representation for form factors, and find a very specific VMD pattern, in which form factors are essentially given by contributions due to the first two bound states in the Q^2-channel. We calculate electric radius of the rho-meson, finding the value <r_rho^2>_C = 0.53 fm^2.

## Key claim

Within the holographic QCD model of Erlich, Katz, Son, and Stephanov (2005-2006), the authors show that vector meson form factors can be expressed using two distinct sets of wave functions, and that the resulting vector meson dominance (VMD) pattern is dominated by the first two bound states in the Q^2-channel, not just the lowest one.

## Method

The authors work in a 5D Yang-Mills theory with SU(2) gauge group in the background of sliced AdS_5 space, with an infrared brane at z = z_0. They use the Kneser-Sommerfeld expansion to express the bulk-to-boundary propagator as a sum over bound state poles, and derive form factors from the three-point function of vector currents. They introduce two types of wave functions: psi_n(z) satisfying Neumann boundary conditions at the IR brane, and phi_n(z) satisfying Dirichlet boundary conditions, and relate the form factors to overlaps of these wave functions with the non-normalizable mode.

## Key numbers

* Electric radius of the rho-meson: <r_rho^2>_C = 0.53 fm^2
* For the form factor F_{11}(Q^2) at small Q^2: F_{11}(Q^2) ≈ 1 - 1.192 Q^2/M^2 + 1.229 Q^4/M^4 + O(Q^6), where M = m_rho
* For the form factor F_{11}(Q^2) at small Q^2: F_{11}(Q^2) ≈ 1 - 1.192 Q^2/M^2 + 1.229 Q^4/M^4 + O(Q^6), where M = m_rho
* For the electric form factor G_C^{(1)}(Q^2) at small Q^2: G_C^{(1)}(Q^2) ≈ 1 - 1.359 Q^2/M^2 + 1.428 Q^4/M^4 + O(Q^6)
* VMD coefficients for F_{11}(Q^2): F_{1,11} ≈ 1.237, F_{2,11} ≈ -0.239, F_{3,11} ≈ 0.002
* VMD coefficients for F_{11}(Q^2): F_{1,11} ≈ 0.619, F_{2,11} ≈ 0.391, F_{3,11} ≈ -0.012, F_{4,11} ≈ 0.002
* Asymptotic coefficient for F_{11}(Q^2) exceeds the simple VMD expectation M_1^2/Q^2 by a factor of 2.566
* The 5D gauge coupling is fixed by matching to QCD: g_5^2 = 12 pi^2 / N_c

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD approach of Erlich, Katz, Son, and Stephanov (2005) and Da Rold and Pomarol (2005) by developing a systematic formalism for vector meson form factors using Kneser-Sommerfeld expansions. It reconciles two different formulations of hadronic wave functions used in earlier holographic work: the Neumann-type wave functions of Polchinski and Strassler (2002) and Hong, Yoon, and Strassler (2004), and the Dirichlet-type wave functions of Brodsky and de Teramond (2006). The calculated rho-meson electric radius agrees closely with the Dyson-Schwinger equation result of Bhagwat and Maris (2006) (0.54 fm^2) and with lattice gauge calculations by Lasscock et al. (2006) in the chiral limit. The formalism enables further study of transition form factors and provides a framework for interpreting holographic results in terms of light-cone wave functions.
