# Flow Distribution Analysis as A Probe of Nuclei Deformity

> We study the flow harmonic distribution in deformed nuclei. To do this, we use the standard Gram-Charlier method to find the higher-order correction to the well-known Bessel-Gaussian distribution. We find that, apart from the necessity of including a shift parameter $\bar v_n$, the modified flow distribution describes the flow distribution of quadrupole and octupole deformation accurately. Using the shifted radial distribution, arising from this method, we scrutinize the effect of deformation on flow distribution. Assuming a linear relation between observables of spherical and deformed collisions, $\mathcal{O}_D=\mathcal{O}_S+\left(\sum_{m=2}a_{k,m} β_m\right)^{2k}$, for events with a fixed centrality, we compare the flow distribution of deformed and spherical nuclei. We also propose a way to measure $\bar{v}_2$ in asymmetric nuclei collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the standard Bessel-Gaussian distribution, which describes flow harmonic distributions for spherical nuclei, is insufficient for deformed nuclei; a shifted radial distribution including higher-order Gram-Charlier corrections (specifically the fourth-order cumulant \(R_n\{4\}\)) is required to accurately describe the elliptic and triangular flow distributions in collisions of deformed nuclei such as uranium and zirconium.

## Method

The authors use the standard Gram-Charlier series method to derive higher-order corrections to the Bessel-Gaussian distribution for flow harmonics \(v_n\). They generate initial-state data using the T\(_{\text{R}}\)ENTO model for PbPb, UU, and ZrZr collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\;\text{TeV}\) and \(200\;\text{GeV}\), assuming a linear response \(v_n = \alpha_n \varepsilon_n\). They compute shifted cumulants \(K_n\{2k\}\) and radial shifted cumulants \(R_n\{2k\}\) (up to \(k=3\)) and compare the flow distributions of spherical and deformed nuclei, including quadrupole (\(\beta_2\)) and octupole (\(\beta_3\)) deformation parameters.

## Key numbers

* For UU collisions, three deformation parameter sets are used: (1) \(\beta_2=0,\ \beta_3=0\) (spherical U), (2) \(\beta_2=0.265,\ \beta_3=0\), (3) \(\beta_2=0.265,\ \beta_3=0.1\).
* The radial shifted cumulant \(R_2\{4\}\) for deformed UU at \(0-5\%\) centrality shows a noticeable difference from spherical nuclei; the mini-panel in Fig. 4 displays \(R_2\{4\}\) values where maximum deformity is expected.
* For PbPb at \(0-5\%\) centrality, the Bessel-Gaussian distribution is a reliable estimation, while for UU at the same centrality, the term involving \(R_2\{4\}\) is comparable to the leading term, indicating non-Bessel-Gaussianity.
* The estimated coefficients for fitting deformed UU distributions using PbPb data at \(0-5\%\) centrality are: \(\delta_{1,est} = 0.014 \pm 0.0082\), \(\delta_{2,est} = 0.020 \pm 0.0014\), \(\delta_{3,est} = -0.0002 \pm 0.00002\).

## Context

This work extends the study of flow harmonic distributions from spherical nuclei (where the Bessel-Gaussian distribution was observed by ATLAS (2013) for PbPb collisions) to deformed nuclei, motivated by experimental differences seen by STAR (2015) between deformed and spherical nuclei. It builds on the linear response relation between \(v_n\) and initial anisotropy \(\varepsilon_n\) from Giacalone (2017, 2018) and the shifted cumulant formalism of Mehrabpour (2020). The results enable the use of flow distribution analysis as a probe of nuclear quadrupole and octupole deformation, and propose a method to measure the shift parameter \(\bar{v}_2\) in asymmetric nucleus collisions.
