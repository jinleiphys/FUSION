# Finite temperature effects on the antikaon optical potential

> By solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation, we have obtained the $\bar{K}$ optical potential from the $\bar{K}N$ effective interaction in nuclear matter at T=0. We have extended the model by incorporating finite temperature effects in order to adapt our calculations to the experimental conditions in heavy-ion collisions. In the rank of densities ($0-2ρ_0$), the finite temperature $\bar{K}$ optical potential shows a smooth behaviour if we compare it to the T=0 outcome. Our model has also been applied to the study of the ratio between $K^+$ and $K^-$ produced at GSI with $T$ around 70 MeV. Our results point at the necessity of introducing an attractive $\bar{K}$ optical potential.

## Key claim

The finite-temperature antikaon optical potential remains attractive even at momenta as large as 500 MeV, but the inclusion of temperature produces a smoother momentum dependence compared to the T=0 case; the authors argue that reproducing the measured K-/K+ ratio in heavy-ion collisions requires an attractive antikaon optical potential, though the "broad-band equilibration" hypothesis is not reproduced when hyperons like Sigma are included.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the antikaon-nucleon G-matrix using a meson-exchange potential, incorporating coupled channels (Kbar N, pi Sigma, pi Lambda). The single-particle potential for antikaons is computed self-consistently within Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory, using mean-field single-particle propagators for baryons (sigma-omega model for nucleons, parameterization from Balberg and Gal (1997) for Lambda and Sigma) and a dressed pion self-energy from Oset and Ramos (2000). Finite temperature effects are introduced through the nucleon momentum distribution in the Fermi-Dirac form, which modifies the Pauli blocking of intermediate nucleon states in the G-matrix.

## Key numbers

- At T=0 and nuclear matter density rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3, the real part of the antikaon optical potential at zero momentum changes from -84 MeV (without pion dressing) to -73 MeV (with pion dressing), a difference of about 13%
- The ratio K+/K- measured in Ni+Ni collisions at GSI energies is approximately 30, corresponding to a temperature T = 70 +/- 10 MeV and baryon chemical potential mu_B = 720 +/- 20 MeV in a thermal model
- The antikaon optical potential remains attractive at momenta up to 500 MeV at T = 70 MeV, with the momentum dependence reduced by a factor of three compared to T = 0

## Context

This work extends the T=0 antikaon-nucleus optical potential calculations of Tolos et al. (2001) by incorporating finite temperature effects relevant for heavy-ion collision conditions at GSI. The results are applied to the K-/K+ ratio measurements from Barth et al. (1997), Laue et al. (1999), and Menzel et al. (2000), which previous transport model calculations by Cassing, Bratkovskaya, Mosel, Teis, and Sibirtsev (1997) and Li, Lee, and Brown (1997) had interpreted as requiring an attractive antikaon potential. The study enables more realistic comparisons between microscopic in-medium calculations and heavy-ion data, while raising questions about the "broad-band equilibration" mechanism proposed by Brown, Rho, and Song (2001) when hyperons beyond Lambda are included.
