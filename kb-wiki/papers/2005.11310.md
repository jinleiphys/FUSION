# On the connection between quark propagation and hadronization

> We investigate the properties and structure of the recently discussed "fully inclusive jet correlator", namely, the gauge-invariant field correlator characterizing the final state hadrons produced by a free quark as this propagates in the vacuum. Working at the operator level, we connect this object to the single-hadron fragmentation correlator of a quark, and exploit a novel gauge invariant spectral decomposition technique to derive a complete set of momentum sum rules for quark fragmentation functions up to twist-3 level; known results are recovered, and new sum rules proposed. We then show how one can explicitly connect quark hadronization and dynamical quark mass generation by studying the inclusive jet's gauge-invariant mass term. This mass is, on the one hand, theoretically related to the integrated chiral-odd spectral function of the quark, and, on the other hand, is experimentally accessible through the $E$ and $\tilde{E}$ twist-3 fragmentation function sum rules. Thus, measurements of these fragmentation functions in deep inelastic processes provide one with an experimental gateway into the dynamical generation of mass in Quantum Chromodynamics.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the fully inclusive jet correlator, which describes the hadronic final state produced by a free quark propagating in the vacuum, can be formally connected to the single-hadron fragmentation correlator at the operator level, and that the gauge-invariant mass of this inclusive jet provides a theoretical link between quark hadronization and dynamical quark mass generation in Quantum Chromodynamics, measurable through twist-3 fragmentation function sum rules.

## Method

The authors work at the operator level to connect the fully inclusive jet correlator to the single-hadron fragmentation correlator of a quark, using a novel gauge-invariant spectral decomposition technique. They derive a complete set of momentum sum rules for quark fragmentation functions up to twist-3 level, recovering known results and proposing new sum rules. The analysis is performed in the parton frame, and the authors also derive the Lorentz transformation rules between the parton frame and the hadron frame for the fragmentation functions, their equation-of-motion relations, and the associated sum rules.

## Key numbers

- The transformation from parton frame to hadron frame mixes twist-2 fragmentation functions with twist-3 functions: $D_p^\perp(z,P_{Tp}^2) = D_h^\perp(z,k_{Th}^2) - z D_{1h}(z,k_{Th}^2)$ and $H_p(z,P_{Tp}^2) = H_h(z,k_{Th}^2) - z (k_{Th}^2/M^2) H_{1h}^\perp(z,k_{Th}^2)$
- The hadron's collinear momentum fraction $z = P^-_p/k^-_p = P^-_h/k^-_h$ is invariant between parton and hadron frames
- The transverse momentum relation between frames: $\vect{P}_{Tp} = -z \vect{k}_{Th}$
- In the parton frame, the sum rule for $\widetilde{D}^\perp$ measures $\langle P_{Tp}^2/z^2 \rangle$, while the $D^\perp$ sum rule is zero; in the hadron frame, the $D^\perp$ sum rule measures $\langle \vect{k}_{Th}^2 \rangle$, while the $\widetilde{D}^\perp$ sum rule is zero

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of the fully inclusive jet correlator introduced by Sterman (1986) and developed by Chen, Collins, and collaborators (2006-2007), and by Accardi and collaborators (2008-2019). The authors connect this object to the standard TMD fragmentation correlator formalism of Bacchetta and collaborators (2006) and Collins (2011), providing a rigorous operator-level derivation of momentum sum rules. The results enable experimental access to dynamical quark mass generation through measurements of the $E$ and $\tilde{E}$ twist-3 fragmentation functions in deep inelastic scattering processes, linking hadronization phenomenology to the fundamental mechanism of mass generation in Quantum Chromodynamics.
