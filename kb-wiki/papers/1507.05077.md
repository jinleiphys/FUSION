# Magnetotransport in Dirac metals: chiral magnetic effect and quantum oscillations

> Dirac metals are characterized by the linear dispersion of fermionic quasi-particles, with the Dirac point hidden inside a Fermi surface. We study the magnetotransport in these materials using chiral kinetic theory to describe within the same framework both the negative magnetoresistance caused by chiral magnetic effect and quantum oscillations in the magnetoresistance due to the existence of the Fermi surface. We discuss the relevance of obtained results to recent measurements on ${\rm Cd_3As_2}$.

## Key claim
The paper develops a unified semiclassical kinetic theory for Dirac metals that simultaneously describes both the negative magnetoresistance from the chiral magnetic effect and the Shubnikov-de Haas quantum oscillations in magnetoresistance, but the theory applies only in the regime where magnetic field is weak enough that many Landau levels are filled and the system is clean enough that quasiparticles perform many cyclotron orbits before scattering.

## Method
The authors use chiral kinetic theory with Berry curvature corrections to the Boltzmann equation, treating the distribution function in the linear response regime. They model elastic scattering on weak, dilute, point-like impurities with a scattering rate that suppresses backscattering due to spin-momentum locking, and incorporate the discreteness of Landau levels via Bohr-Sommerfeld quantization and Poisson summation to extract both the smooth and oscillatory parts of the longitudinal conductivity.

## Key numbers
- The dimensionless magnetic field parameter is defined as $\zeta_F = eB/(2\hbar k_F^2)$, where $k_F$ is the Fermi momentum.
- The non-oscillating longitudinal conductivity per chirality is $\sigma_{zz}^{(0)} = (n_e e^2 v_F / \hbar k_F) \left( (1 - \frac{12}{5}\zeta_F^2)/(i\omega+1/\tau) + 3\zeta_F^2/(i\omega+\eta) \right)$, where $n_e = k_F^3/(3\pi^2)$.
- The oscillating part of the conductivity is $\sigma_{zz}^{(l)} = (n_e e^2 v_F / \hbar k_F) (1/(i\omega+1/\tau)) (3/(2\pi)) (\lambda l / \sinh \lambda l) (2\zeta_F/l)^{3/2}$, with $\lambda = \pi^2 T/(\hbar k_F v_F \zeta_F)$.
- The DC conductivity ratio is $\sigma_{zz}(B)/\sigma_0 = 1 + 3(\tau_v/\tau - 4/5)\zeta_F^2 + (3/\pi) \sum_{l=1}^\infty e^{-\lambda_D l} (\lambda l / \sinh \lambda l) (2\zeta_F/l)^{3/2} \cos(\pi l/(2\zeta_F) + \pi/4)$, where $\lambda_D = \pi\Gamma/(\hbar v_F k_F \zeta_F)$.
- The zero-field Drude conductivity for Dirac spectrum is $\sigma_0 = n_e e^2 \tau / (\hbar k_F / v_F)$.
- Numerical values used for comparison with Cd$_3$As$_2$ data: $k_F = 3.8 \times 10^8$ m$^{-1}$, $v_F = 9.3 \times 10^5$ m/s, $\tau = 8 \times 10^{-13}$ s, $T = 2.5$ K.

## Context
This work extends the chiral kinetic theory approach previously applied to Weyl semimetals by Son and Spivak (2013) and by Basar et al. (2013) to the case of Dirac metals where the Fermi surface exists and the Dirac point is hidden inside it. The theory aims to explain the complex magnetotransport pattern observed in Cd$_3$As$_2$ by Ong (2014), which shows both quantum oscillations and a tendency to negative magnetoresistance at stronger fields, though the positive magnetoresistance at weak fields seen in that experiment remains unexplained and is left for future study.
