# Photon parton distributions in nuclei and the EMC effect

> Photons as well as quarks and gluons are constituents of the infinite momentum frame (IMF) wave function of an energetic particle. They are mostly equivalent photons whose amplitude follows from the Lorentz transformation of the particle rest frame Coulomb field into the IMF and from the conservation of the electromagnetic current. We evaluate in a model independent way the dominant photon contribution \propto α_{em}(Z^2/A^{4/3})\ln(1/R_{A}m_{N}x) to the nuclear structure functions as well as the term \propto α_{em}Z/A. In addition we show that the definition of x consistent with the exact kinematics of eA scattering (with exact sum rules) works in the same direction as the nucleus field of equivalent photons. Combined, these effects account for the bulk of the EMC effect for x\le 0.5 where Fermi motion effects are small. In particular for these x the hadronic mechanism contribution to the EMC effect does not exceed \sim 3% for all nuclei. Also the A-dependence of the hadronic mechanism of the EMC effect for x > 0.5 is significantly modified.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the photon component of the nuclear light-cone wave function, combined with a proper definition of the Bjorken x variable for nuclear targets, accounts for the bulk of the EMC effect for x ≤ 0.5, leaving a hadronic mechanism contribution of no more than ~3% for all nuclei in that region.

## Method

The authors calculate the coherent contribution of equivalent photons to the nuclear parton distribution using the Fermi-Weizsacker-Williams approximation, generalized for off-mass-shell photons, and express it in terms of the nuclear electric form factor. They also compute the correction arising from using the correct Bjorken x variable (x = A x_A) consistent with the momentum sum rule, rather than the experimentally used x_p = Q^2/2 m_p q_0. These two effects are combined additively and compared to experimental EMC ratio data for several nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The fraction of light-cone momentum carried by photons in nuclei, λ_γ, is: 0.08% for ⁴He, 0.27% for ¹²C, 0.51% for ²⁷Al, 0.84% for ⁵⁶Fe, and 1.56% for ¹⁹⁷Au.
- For x = 0.5, the combined Coulomb and kinematic correction to the EMC ratio R_A is: 0.979 for ⁴He, 0.971 for ¹²C, 0.962 for ²⁷Al, 0.950 for ⁵⁶Fe, and 0.932 for ¹⁹⁷Au.
- For x = 0.6, the combined correction to R_A is: 0.968 for ⁴He, 0.956 for ¹²C, 0.943 for ²⁷Al, 0.927 for ⁵⁶Fe, and 0.898 for ¹⁹⁷Au.
- The coherent contribution to the momentum sum rule is given by λ_γ^coherent = α_em (Z²/A) (1.759 / m_N R_A).
- For heavy nuclei like lead, the correction to the gluon momentum sum rule is approximately 3.3%.

## Context

This work extends the application of the Fermi-Weizsacker-Williams approximation and Gribov's generalization to nuclear parton distributions, providing a model-independent evaluation of the photon contribution. It directly addresses the EMC effect, previously reviewed in data compilations, and modifies the interpretation of its hadronic mechanism, particularly for x ≤ 0.5. The results enable a reanalysis of global nuclear parton distribution function fits, as done by MRST (Martin 2007), and have implications for interpreting ultraperipheral processes at the LHC (Baltz 2007) and the NuTeV Weinberg angle measurement.
