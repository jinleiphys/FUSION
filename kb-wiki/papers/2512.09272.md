# Anomalous collective modes in atomic nuclei within the proton-neutron interacting boson model

> Novel collective modes characterized by a $B_{4/2}$ ratio ($\equiv B(E2;4_1^+\rightarrow 2_1^+)/B(E2;2_1^+\rightarrow 0_1^+)$) less than 1.0 that were observed recently have been identified within the proton-neutron interacting boson model (IBM-2) using the consistent-$Q$ Hamiltonian. These modes are shown to give rise to triaxial spectral features, including significant band mixing. The results provide a compelling explanation for the deeply suppressed $B_{4/2}$ ratio observed in $^{166}$W, $^{168,170}$Os, and $^{172}$Pt, offering new insights into the $B(E2)$ anomaly phenomenon in neutron-deficient nuclei.

## Key claim

Novel collective modes with a \(B_{4/2}\) ratio less than 1.0, previously observed in neutron-deficient nuclei, can be produced within the proton-neutron interacting boson model (IBM-2) using only the consistent-\(Q\) Hamiltonian without high-order terms, provided the proton and neutron quadrupole operators have opposite signs (\(\chi_\pi = -\chi_\nu\)).

## Method

The authors diagonalized the consistent-\(Q\) Hamiltonian of IBM-2, which contains a \(d\)-boson number term and a proton-neutron quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, using boson numbers \(N_\pi=3\) and \(N_\nu=5\) corresponding to \(^{168}\)Os. They calculated the energy ratio \(R_{4/2} = E(4_1^+)/E(2_1^+)\) and the \(B(E2)\) ratio \(B_{4/2} = B(E2;4_1^+\rightarrow 2_1^+)/B(E2;2_1^+\rightarrow 0_1^+)\) as functions of the parameters \(\eta\) (controlling the U(5) weight) and \(\chi_\mathrm{c}\) (where \(\chi_\nu = -\chi_\pi = \chi_\mathrm{c}\)), with equal effective charges \(e_\pi = e_\nu\). A mean-field analysis using coherent states was performed to understand the potential energy surface and the origin of the anomalous modes.

## Key numbers

*   For \(N_\pi=3\), \(N_\nu=5\), \(\eta=0.11\), and \(\chi_\nu = -\chi_\pi = -1.2\), the calculation yields \(B_{4/2} = 0.73\) and \(R_{4/2} = 2.91\), demonstrating the anomalous \(B(E2)\) suppression.
*   The \(B_{4/2}\) ratio drops to values \(B_{4/2} \ll 1.0\) when \(\chi_\mathrm{c} < -1.1\), and stabilizes at around \(B_{4/2} = 1.35\) within \(\chi_\mathrm{c} \in [-0.8, 0]\).
*   For the case \(\chi_\nu = -\chi_\pi = -1.2\), the mean-field analysis yields two degenerate quadrupole deformations: \((\bar{\beta}_\pi, \bar{\beta}_\nu, \bar{\gamma}_\pi, \bar{\gamma}_\nu) = (0.73, 1.37, 0^\circ, 0^\circ)\) and \((1.37, 0.73, 60^\circ, 60^\circ)\).
*   The potential barrier height between the two degenerate minima is given by \(\Delta E = \frac{\chi_{\mathrm{c}}^2}{14 + \chi_{\mathrm{c}}^2} N_\pi N_\nu\).
*   With fixed parameters \(\eta=0.036\) and \(\chi_\pi = -\chi_\nu = 1.18\), the model reproduces the experimental \(B(E2;2_1^+\rightarrow 0_1^+)\) values for \(^{172}\)Pt (49 W.u.), \(^{170}\)Os (97 W.u.), \(^{168}\)Os (74 W.u.), and \(^{166}\)W (150 W.u.), and gives corresponding \(B(E2;4_1^+\rightarrow 2_1^+)\) values of 30, 26, 18, and 46 W.u., respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous IBM-1 explanations of the \(B(E2)\) anomaly (Zhang 2022, Zhang 2024, Pan 2024), which required high-order interaction terms to simulate triaxial rotor dynamics, by showing that the microscopic IBM-2 can produce the same effect with only the standard quadrupole-quadrupole interaction when proton and neutron bosons have opposite quadrupole configurations. The results provide a compelling explanation for the deeply suppressed \(B_{4/2}\) ratios observed in \(^{166}\)W, \(^{168,170}\)Os, and \(^{172}\)Pt (Grahn 2016, Saygi 2017, Cederwall 2018, Goasduff 2019), and enable future microscopic studies of shape coexistence and triaxiality in neutron-deficient nuclei using the IBM-2 framework.
