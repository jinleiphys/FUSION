# Two-Component Approach to $J/Ψ$ Production in High-Energy Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The production of charmonia in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions is investigated including two sources. These are a primordial contribution coupled with various phases of dissociation, and a statistical coalescence of $c$ and $\bar{c}$ quarks at the hadronization phase transition. Within a schematic fireball evolution, SPS data on $J/Ψ$ production can be reasonably well reproduced. Remaining discrepancies in the $Ψ'/Ψ$ ratio are discussed. Predictions for the $J/Ψ$ centrality dependence at RHIC energies are confronted with first data from PHENIX. The pertinent excitation function of the $N_{J/Ψ}/N_{c\bar{c}}$ ratio exhibits a characteristic minimum structure signaling the transition from the standard $J/Ψ$ suppression scenario (SPS) to predominantly statistical production (RHIC).

# Two-Component Approach to $J/\Psi$ Production in High-Energy Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The paper argues that $J/\Psi$ production in heavy-ion collisions must include both a primordial component (subject to nuclear, QGP, and hadronic dissociation) and a statistical coalescence component at hadronization, with the transition between these regimes occurring between SPS and RHIC energies; however, the model's success depends on not invoking any anomalous open-charm enhancement beyond $N$-$N$ collision scaling, and the $\Psi'/\Psi$ ratio shows significant discrepancies that the authors attribute to shortcomings in the hadronic dissociation treatment.

## Method

The authors construct a schematic thermal fireball evolution that reproduces basic hadro-chemistry and expansion dynamics at both SPS and RHIC energies. The direct component uses a Glauber nuclear absorption cross-section of 6.4 mb, quasifree QGP dissociation with in-medium modified binding energies, and hadronic dissociation from an SU(4)-symmetric effective theory; the statistical component uses thermal weights at the critical temperature $T_c$ with a fugacity factor enforcing charm conservation, multiplied by a factor $\mathcal{R}$ accounting for incomplete charm-quark thermalization via a relaxation time approach.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear absorption cross-section: $\sigma_{\Psi N} \simeq 6.4$ mb (Glauber picture, constant)
- $pp$ cross-section at RHIC ($\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV): $\sigma_{J/\Psi}^{pp} = 3.8 \pm 0.6 \text{(stat)} \pm 1.3 \text{(sys)}\ \mu$b (from PHENIX measurements)
- $pp$ open-charm cross-section at RHIC ($\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV): $\sigma_{c,\bar{c}}^{pp} \simeq 650\ \mu$b (from PHENIX)
- SPS energy: $Pb$(158 AGeV)-$Pb$ collisions; first drop in $J/\Psi$ yield occurs around $E_T \simeq 40$ GeV
- SPS $S$-$U$ system: 200 AGeV
- Direct component feeddown fractions: 0.6 from $J/\Psi$, 0.032 from $\chi$, 0.08 from $\Psi'$

## Context

This work extends the standard suppression picture of Matsui and Satz (1986) and Shuryak (1980) by incorporating the statistical coalescence mechanism suggested by Gorenstein et al. (1999), Braun-Munzinger and Stachel (2000), and others. The model reproduces NA50 data for $Pb$-$Pb$ at SPS and is confronted with first PHENIX data at RHIC, finding that standard suppression-only scenarios and more extreme recombination scenarios are disfavored. The excitation function of $N_{J/\Psi}/N_{c\bar{c}}$ is proposed as a diagnostic for the transition between direct and statistical production regimes, with a characteristic minimum structure that could be tested at RHIC and future colliders.
