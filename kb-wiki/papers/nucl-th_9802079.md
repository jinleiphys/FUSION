# Nucleon generalized polarizabilities within a relativistic Constituent Quark Model

> Nucleon generalized polarizabilities are investigated within a relativistic framework, defining such quantities through a Lorentz covariant multipole expansion of the amplitude for virtual Compton scattering. The key physical ingredients in the calculation of the nucleon polarizabilities are the Lorentz invariant reduced matrix elements of the electromagnetic transition current, which can be evaluated from off-energy-shell helicity amplitudes. The evolution of the proton paramagnetic polarizability, $β_{para}(q)$, as a function of the virtual-photon three-momentum transfer $ q,$ is explicitly evaluated within a relativistic constituent quark model by adopting transition form factors obtained in the light-front formalism. The discussion is focussed on the role played by the effects due to the relativistic approach and to the transition form factors, derived within different models.

## Key claim

The paper presents a fully relativistic, Lorentz-covariant formalism for calculating nucleon generalized polarizabilities from virtual Compton scattering, but the actual numerical results are limited to the paramagnetic polarizability of the proton from the Delta(1232) resonance only, using an off-energy-shell prescription for the baryon current.

## Method

The authors develop a Lorentz covariant multipole expansion of the virtual Compton scattering amplitude, expressing the residual (non-Born) part of the Compton tensor in terms of reduced matrix elements of the electromagnetic transition current. These reduced matrix elements are evaluated from off-energy-shell helicity amplitudes using transition form factors obtained within a relativistic constituent quark model formulated in light-front Hamiltonian dynamics, with the calculation restricted to the dominant Delta(1232) resonance contribution to the dipole magnetic polarizability.

## Key numbers

- The real-photon limit of the proton paramagnetic polarizability, obtained using experimental helicity amplitudes from PDG as input, is (13.1 ± 1.6) × 10⁻⁴ fm³.
- Lorentz covariance effects in the u-channel contribution become important at three-momentum transfer values q ≥ 0.5 GeV/c.
- The relativistic transition form factors show a slower fall-off than the gaussian prediction of the non-relativistic constituent quark model for q > 0.9 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic constituent quark model calculation of generalized polarizabilities by Guichon et al. (1996) to a fully relativistic framework, and provides a formalism that can be applied to future experimental data from Jefferson Lab, MIT-Bates, and MAMI. The results are compared with the non-relativistic constituent quark model of Guichon et al. (1996) and the effective Lagrangian model of Vanderhaeghen et al. (1996), showing that both relativistic effects and the choice of transition form factors significantly affect the q-evolution of the paramagnetic polarizability. The formalism enables future calculations including additional resonance contributions and full treatment of final-state interactions in the Nπ system.
