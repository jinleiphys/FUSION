# Condensate Fragmentation in a New Exactly Solvable Model for Confined Bosons

> Based on Richardson's exact solution of the pairing model and the Gaudin model for spin systems we derive a new class of exactly solvable models for finite boson system. As an example we solve a particular hamiltonian which displays a transition to a fragmented condensate for repulsive pairing interactions.

## Key claim

The authors derive a new class of exactly solvable models for finite boson systems, based on a generalization of Richardson's exact solution of the pairing model. For repulsive pairing interactions, the model displays a transition to a fragmented condensate with macroscopic occupation of the two lowest harmonic oscillator shells, which the authors claim is the first example of fragmentation in a confined scalar boson system.

## Method

The authors construct a generalized pairing Hamiltonian for bosons by exploiting the freedom in the parameters of the commuting operators (constants of motion) derived from the SU(1,1) pseudo-spin algebra. They modify the single-particle energies to cancel the undesired dependence of the effective pairing matrix elements on the level degeneracies, producing a Hamiltonian with both pairing and monopole-monopole particle-hole interactions. The exact eigenstates are obtained as pair-product wavefunctions, with pair energies determined by solving Richardson's equations, and occupation numbers are computed from derivatives of the pairing Hamiltonian with respect to single-boson energies.

## Key numbers

* For a system of 1000 bosons in 50 harmonic oscillator shells with attractive pairing at g = -2.0, the depletion factor is 0.608.
* The critical interaction strength for the phase transition to a fragmented condensate is g_c = (hbar omega) / (4 N), where N is the total number of bosons.
* The dimensionless parameter x = (4 N g) / (hbar omega) defines the transition: for x ≤ 1 the ground state has a single condensate (beta = 0), while for x > 1 the fragmented state appears with beta = sqrt((x-1)/(x+1)).
* Beyond the critical point, the occupation numbers are n_0 = (x+1)/(2x) and n_1 = (x-1)/(2x) for the two lowest levels.
* The total occupation of all levels beyond the first two is always lower than 10^{-2} in the fragmented phase.

## Context

This work extends Richardson's exact solution of the pairing model (Richardson, 1963-1968) and the Gaudin model for spin systems to boson systems, providing a testing tool for many-body approximations like Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov and number-conserving extensions. The finding of a fragmented condensate for repulsive interactions contradicts earlier mean-field results by Nozieres and Saint-James (1982) and Nozieres (1996) who argued that fragmentation is energetically unfavored for scalar bosons, and extends discussions by Holzmann et al. (1999) on possible fragmentation in two dimensions. The model enables future studies of whether this new quantum phase can arise in realistic trapped boson systems and can serve as a benchmark for approximate many-body methods.
