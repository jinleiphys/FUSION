# Linear chain structure of four-$α$ clusters in $^{16}$O

> We investigate the linear-chain configurations of four-$α$ clusters in $^{16}$O using a Skyrme cranked Hartree-Fock method and discuss the relationship between the stability of such states and angular momentum. We show the existence of a region of angular momentum (13-18 $\hbar$) where the linear chain configuration is stabilized. For the first time we demonstrate that stable exotic states with a large moment of inertia ($\hbar^2/2Θ$ $\sim$ 0.06-0.08 MeV) can exist.

# Linear chain structure of four-α clusters in ¹⁶O

## Key claim
Using a Skyrme cranked Hartree-Fock method, the authors demonstrate that a four-α linear chain configuration in ¹⁶O can be stabilized, but only within a specific angular momentum window of 13–18 ℏ; below this range the configuration decays to low-lying states and above it fission occurs.

## Method
Cranked Hartree-Fock calculations were performed on a three-dimensional Cartesian grid (0.8 fm spacing) using three different Skyrme forces (SLy6, SkI3, SkI4). The initial wave function was a twisted four-α linear chain configuration, and the system was rotated about the y-axis (perpendicular to the deformation axis). The rigid-body moment of inertia was computed from the total nucleon density at each iteration step, and convergence behavior was monitored as a function of rotational frequency ω.

## Key numbers
- The four-α linear chain configuration is stabilized for rotational frequencies between 1.8 and 2.2 MeV/ℏ (SkI3), 1.9 and 2.2 MeV/ℏ (SkI4), and 2.0 and 2.1 MeV/ℏ (SLy6).
- The corresponding angular momentum range for stabilization is 13–18 ℏ (SkI3), 14–18 ℏ (SkI4), and 16–18 ℏ (SLy6).
- The coefficient of rotational energy ℏ²/2Θ for the stabilized linear chain is 0.065 MeV (SkI3 and SkI4) and 0.06 MeV (SLy6), consistent with a rigid-body value of 0.063 MeV for four α particles in a linear chain of length 12 fm.
- The band head energy at J = 0 is estimated at approximately −82 MeV, with energies around −70 MeV at J ∼ 14 ℏ.

## Context
This work extends previous experimental and theoretical searches for α-chain states in light 4N nuclei, following candidates suggested by Chevallier et al. and Suzuki et al. in ¹⁶O, and the six-α chain studies by Wuosmaa et al. and Rae et al. The authors use a mean-field approach (Skyrme forces) rather than conventional cluster models with effective interactions tuned to cluster properties, providing an independent test of the linear chain hypothesis. The results enable future time-dependent Hartree-Fock studies of the ⁸Be + ⁸Be fusion reaction as a possible experimental pathway, and open the question of whether longer linear chains or polygonal α-cluster configurations (as speculated by Wilkinson (1986)) might exist.
