# Gluon gravitational structure of hadrons of different spin

> The gravitational form factors (GFFs) of hadrons encode the matrix elements of the energy momentum tensor of QCD. These quantities describe how energy, spin, and various mechanical properties of hadrons are carried by their quark and gluon constituents. We present the gluon GFFs of the pion, nucleon, $ρ$ meson, and $Δ$ baryon as functions of the squared momentum transfer $t$ in the region $0 \leq -t < 2 \; \text{GeV}^2$, as determined in a lattice QCD study with pion mass $m_π = 450(5) \; \text{MeV}$. By fitting the extracted GFFs using multipole and z-parameter expansion functional forms, we extract various gluon contributions to the energy, pressure, and shear force distributions of the hadrons in the 3D and 2D Breit frames as well as in the infinite momentum frame. We also obtain estimates for the corresponding gluon mechanical and mass radii, as well as the forward-limit gluon contributions to the momentum fraction and angular momentum of the hadrons.

## Key claim

This lattice QCD study presents the gluon gravitational form factors (GFFs) of the pion, nucleon, rho meson, and Delta baryon at a single unphysically heavy pion mass of 450(5) MeV, finding that the gluon contributions to the D-term of the nucleon and rho meson may exhibit nonmonotonic behavior at low momentum transfer that cannot be captured by standard multipole fits, though this could be a statistical fluctuation or systematic uncertainty.

## Method

The authors computed hadronic matrix elements of the symmetric traceless gluon energy-momentum tensor on a single ensemble of 2820 lattice configurations with volume 32^3 x 96, N_f=2+1 clover-improved Wilson quarks, and lattice spacing 0.1167(16) fm. They extracted the renormalized gluon GFFs by fitting ratios of three-point and two-point correlation functions, then modeled the t-dependence using both a tripole form (n=3) and a modified z-expansion with kmax=2, renormalizing at mu=2 GeV in the MSbar scheme via a nonperturbative RI-MOM prescription with one-loop perturbative matching.

## Key numbers

- Pion: gluon momentum fraction A_g^pi(0) = 0.537(45) (tripole) or 0.544(46) (z-expansion); gluon D-term D_g^pi(0) = -0.793(84) (tripole) or -0.74(21) (z-expansion), renormalized at mu=2 GeV
- Nucleon: gluon momentum fraction A_g^N(0) = 0.429(39) (tripole) or 0.414(40) (z-expansion); gluon angular momentum J_g^N(0) = 0.263(26) (tripole) or 0.211(57) (z-expansion); gluon D-term D_g^N(0) = -1.93(53) (tripole) or 0.4(1.2) (z-expansion)
- Rho meson: gluon momentum fraction A_g^rho(0) = 0.485(41) (tripole) or 0.482(42) (z-expansion); gluon angular momentum J_g^rho(0) = 0.491(42) (tripole) or 0.469(42) (z-expansion); gluon D-term D_g^rho(0) = -1.16(14) (tripole) or -0.81(38) (z-expansion)
- Delta baryon: gluon momentum fraction A_g^Delta(0) = 0.393(36) (tripole) or 0.378(38) (z-expansion); gluon angular momentum J_g^Delta(0) = 0.588(78) (tripole) or 0.41(18) (z-expansion); gluon D-term D_g^Delta(0) = -1.80(69) (tripole) or 0.9(1.5) (z-expansion)
- All results at pion mass m_pi = 450(5) MeV, renormalized at mu=2 GeV in MSbar scheme

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of gluon GFFs for the pion and nucleon by Shanahan (2018) and others, incorporating spin-nonconserving channels and improved statistics, and provides the first lattice QCD determination of the complete set of gluon GFFs for the rho meson and Delta baryon. The results enable comparisons with model predictions such as the NJL model for the rho meson by Freese (2019) and the light-front constituent quark model by Sun (2020), and provide theory input for future experimental programs at JLab and the EIC that aim to constrain gluon GFFs through J/psi and Upsilon photoproduction.
