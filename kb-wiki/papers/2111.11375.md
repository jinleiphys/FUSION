# Transport near the chiral critical point

> The evolution of a heavy ion collision passes close to the O(4) critical point of QCD, where fluctuations of the order parameter are expected to be enhanced. Using the appropriate stochastic hydrodynamic equations in mean field near the the pseudo-critical point, we compute how these enhanced fluctuations modify the transport coefficients of QCD. Finally, we estimate the expected critical enhancement of soft pion yields, which provides a plausible explanation for the excess seen in experiment relative to ordinary hydrodynamic computations.

## Key claim

Using mean-field stochastic hydrodynamics near the O(4) pseudo-critical point, the enhanced fluctuations of the order parameter modify the shear viscosity and isovector conductivity of QCD, and the resulting critical enhancement of soft pion yields may explain the excess seen in experiment relative to ordinary hydrodynamic computations.

## Method

The authors linearize the stochastic hydrodynamic equations for the O(4) order parameter around the mean-field solution, obtaining coupled equations for the pion field, axial chemical potential, and sigma condensate. They compute the retarded and symmetrized propagators from these linearized equations, then use the Kubo formula to calculate the correction to shear viscosity from a one-loop diagram involving the pion and sigma propagators. For the broken phase, they also employ a Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation for soft pions.

## Key numbers

- At the pseudocritical point \(z_{\rm pc}=1.19\), the pion velocity squared is \(v_0^2(z_{\rm pc})\simeq 0.25\), the pion mass is \(m_{0p}(z_{\rm pc})\simeq 0.1\ \text{GeV}\), and the vacuum pion mass is \(m_{\rm vac}^2=0.14\ \text{GeV}\).
- The shear viscosity correction in the unbroken phase (large \(z\)) is \(\Delta \eta_\infty = -T m_\sigma/(8\pi \Gamma)\), where \(m_\sigma\) is the sigma mass and \(\Gamma\) a kinetic coefficient.
- The finite part of the pion contribution to shear viscosity is \(-\frac{2 T^2 m_{\sigma}}{32\pi \Gamma}\) (after subtracting a divergent term \(\frac{2 T^2}{30\pi^2 \Gamma}\Lambda\)).
- The sigma contribution to shear viscosity has a finite piece \(-\frac{T^2 m_\sigma}{16\pi \Gamma}\) after subtracting the divergent term \(\frac{T^2\Lambda}{15\pi^2\Gamma}\).

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic framework for chiral physics developed by Grossi et al. (2020, 2021), building on the O(4) effective theory of Rajagopal and Wilczek (1992). It computes the critical scaling of transport coefficients in mean field, complementing real-time studies of the O(4) critical point by Florio et al. (2021). The estimated soft pion enhancement provides a testable prediction for the ALICE Inner Tracking System upgrade, and the Euclidean parameters (pion velocity, mass) can be measured more precisely on the lattice.
