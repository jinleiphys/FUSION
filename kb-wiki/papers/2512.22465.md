# Quark Coalescence: Formation of Mesons Including Excited States

> We discuss the quantum mechanics of coalescence of quark-antiquark pairs into mesons using a non-relativistic quark model. We derive the coalescence probabilities assuming a harmonic oscillator potential and generic Gaussian wave packet shapes for the initial quarks and antiquarks. Our particular emphasis is on modeling excited states of the meson spectrum consistently. We provide the formalism to systematically include excited states from the Particle Data Book, and many more predicted by the quark model, up to $L=4$ and masses of about 2.2 GeV for light flavors. We provide estimates of masses and decay branching ratios for unconfirmed states. We use a phase space picture which is appropriate for the quasi-classical nature of the information typically available for the quarks and antiquarks in applications like Monte Carlo simulations. We demonstrate that for typical parton configurations expected in jets, excited meson states are populated abundantly.

## Key claim

The paper provides a quantum mechanical formalism for quark-antiquark coalescence into mesons that systematically includes excited states up to orbital angular momentum L=4 and masses around 2.2 GeV for light flavors, but the authors acknowledge this is an approximation to full quantum field theory and that the non-relativistic quark model with harmonic oscillator potential is chosen primarily for analytical tractability rather than high-accuracy predictions.

## Method

The authors derive coalescence probabilities using a non-relativistic quark model with an isotropic harmonic oscillator potential, computing spatial wave function overlaps via the Wigner phase space formalism with Gaussian wave packets for initial quarks and antiquarks. They factor the coalescence probability into color, flavor, spin, and spatial components, summing over magnetic quantum numbers to obtain expressions depending only on the dimensionless phase-space distance s and squared angular momentum t of the quark-antiquark pair. The formalism is implemented within the Hybrid Hadronization framework in the JETSCAPE event generator, incorporating 330 meson states (not counting spin degeneracies) from the Particle Data Group and additional quark model predictions.

## Key numbers

- Meson states included: 330 total, not counting spin degeneracies, covering states up to L=4 and masses of about 2.2 GeV for light flavors
- Color singlet probability factor: P_c = 1/9 for random color averaging
- Coalescence probability for ground state (k=0, l=0): P_00 = e^(-s), where s is the dimensionless phase-space distance
- Coalescence probability for first excited P-wave (k=0, l=1): P_01 = e^(-s) * s
- Coalescence probability for first excited D-wave (k=0, l=2): P_02 = (1/2) * e^(-s) * (2/3 * s^2 + 1/3 * t), where t is the squared dimensionless angular momentum
- Coalescence probability for first radially excited S-wave (k=1, l=0): P_10 = (1/2) * e^(-s) * (1/3 * s^2 - 1/3 * t)

## Context

This work extends the quark coalescence approach that was revived in the early 2000s for heavy-ion collisions (Greco 2003, Fries 2003, Molnar 2003) and is implemented within the Hybrid Hadronization framework (Han 2016, JETSCAPE 2023, JETSCAPE 2025), which combines coalescence with string fragmentation. The formalism enables systematic inclusion of excited meson states in Monte Carlo event generators, addressing a limitation of earlier coalescence models that typically considered only ground-state mesons. The authors note that a similar treatment for baryons is in preparation (Virupapuram 2025).
