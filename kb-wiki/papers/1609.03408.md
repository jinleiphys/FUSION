# Deuteron structure in the deep inelastic regime

> We study nuclear effects on the deuteron in the deep inelastic regime using the newest available data analyzing their $Q^2$ dependence. We conclude that precise EMC ratios for large $Q^2$ (> 30, GeV$^2$) cannot be obtained without considering these nuclear effects. For this purpose we use a scheme which parametrizes these effects in a simple manner and compare our results with other recent proposals.

## Key claim
Precise EMC ratios for the deuteron at high momentum transfer (Q² > 30 GeV²) cannot be obtained without accounting for nuclear effects in the deuteron itself; the magnitude of these effects depends strongly on Q², being negligible at low Q² but considerable at high Q².

## Method
The authors parametrize nuclear effects in the deuteron via a factor β, defined by F₂′ᵖ = (1/β) F₂ᵖ, applied equally to proton and neutron structure functions due to isospin symmetry. They extract 1/β from experimental F₂ᴰ/F₂ᵖ ratios (NMC 1997 data) using the MSTW parton distribution parametrization, then perform separate fits for low (6, 8, 11 GeV²), intermediate (14, 19, 26 GeV²), and high (35, 47, 63, 90 GeV²) Q² bins, comparing with other recent nuclear correction schemes.

## Key numbers
- The deuteron binding energy is 2.22 MeV, cited as the reason the free-proton-plus-free-neutron approximation is often considered reasonable.
- The analysis uses NMC 1997 data for F₂ᴰ/F₂ᵖ at Q² values of 6, 8, 11, 14, 19, 26, 35, 47, 63, and 90 GeV².
- The antishadowing maximum in 1/β appears around x ≈ 0.2 in both Morfin-Tung and MSTW parametrizations.
- For high Q² (Q² > 30 GeV²), nuclear effects in the deuteron are found to be "considerable" and cannot be neglected when computing EMC ratios.
- For intermediate Q² (14–26 GeV²), effects are irrelevant for x < 0.5, and above that region better data are required, though effects are expected to be no more than 1%.

## Context
This work extends the earlier β-scheme analysis of Canal, Tarutina, and Vento (previous1) that was originally applied to NMC data from 1991, now using the newer NMC 1997 data and the MSTW parton distributions. It compares with the nuclear correction function c(x) of Martin et al. (2014), the Rᴰ_EMC values from Griffioen et al. (2015) using BONuS data, and the in-medium correction slope analysis of Weinstein et al. (2011). The results enable a reliable baseline for searching for more exotic nuclear effects such as the EMC effect or non-nucleonic degrees of freedom in the deuteron.
