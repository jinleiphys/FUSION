# Isospin equilibration processes and dynamical correlations: study of the system $^{40}Cl+^{28}Si$ at 40 MeV/nucleon

> The asymptotic time derivative of the total dipole signal is proposed as an useful observable to investigate on Isospin equilibration phenomenon in multi-fragmentation processes. The study has been developed to describe charge/mass equilibration processes involving the gas and liquid "phases" of the total system formed during the early stage of a collision. General properties of this observable and the links with others isospin dependent phenomena are discussed. In particular, the $^{40}Cl+^{28}Si$ system at 40 MeV/nucleon is investigated by means of semiclassical microscopic many-body calculations based on the CoMD-II model. The study of the dynamical many-body correlations produced by the model also shows how the proposed observable is rather sensitive to different parameterizations of the isospin dependent interaction.

## Key claim

The asymptotic time derivative of the total dipole signal, \(\langle \overrightarrow{V} \rangle\), is proposed as a useful observable to investigate isospin equilibration in multi-fragmentation processes, with the condition that equilibration corresponds to \(\langle \overrightarrow{V} \rangle = 0\); the observable is sensitive to different parameterizations of the isospin-dependent interaction, but this sensitivity is strongly reduced if two-body neutron-proton correlations are neglected (the I.M.F.A. limit).

## Method

Semiclassical microscopic many-body calculations using the Constrained Molecular Dynamics-II (CoMD-II) model were performed for the \(^{40}\text{Cl}+^{28}\text{Si}\) system at 40 MeV/nucleon. The ensemble average of the time derivative of the total dipole, \(\langle \overrightarrow{V}(t) \rangle\), was computed for protons (bound and free) in the center-of-mass frame, and its asymptotic values were extracted for different impact parameters and different parameterizations of the iso-vectorial potential (Stiff1, Stiff2, and Soft options, corresponding to \(K_{sym}\) values of 94 MeV, -27 MeV, and -88 MeV respectively). The role of dynamical many-body correlations was studied by comparing full CoMD-II results with those obtained in the Iso-vectorial Mean Field Approximation (I.M.F.A.), where neutron-neutron, proton-proton, and neutron-proton overlap integrals are set equal.

## Key numbers

*   For the \(^{40}\text{Cl}+^{28}\text{Si}\) system at 40 MeV/nucleon, at impact parameter \(b = 3\) fm and for the Stiff2 option, the asymptotic "liquid" component values are \(\langle V^{x}_{L} \rangle = -0.120 c\) and \(\langle V^{z}_{L} \rangle = 0.162 c\), while the total asymptotic values are \(\langle V^{x} \rangle = 0.044 c\) and \(\langle V^{z} \rangle = -0.027 c\).
*   The form factors for the iso-vectorial potential correspond to \(K_{sym}\) values of 94 MeV (Stiff1), -27 MeV (Stiff2), and -88 MeV (Soft), all with an \(S_0\) value of about 40 MeV.
*   In the I.M.F.A. case, for \(b_r = 0.4\), the values of \(|\langle V^{z} \rangle|\) are about four times larger than those obtained with full CoMD-II calculations, indicating a reduced capacity to obtain isospin equilibration along the \(\hat{z}\) direction.

## Context

This work extends studies of isospin diffusion in binary processes, such as those by Tsang et al. (2004) on \(^{124,112}\text{Sn}\) collisions at 50 MeV/nucleon, by proposing a global observable for isospin equilibration that also applies to complex multi-fragmentation reactions. The observable \(\langle \overrightarrow{V} \rangle\) is linked to pre-equilibrium Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) \(\gamma\)-ray emission and to the neutron-proton differential flow proposed by the authors in earlier work (Bao et al., 2000; Bao et al., 2008). The results enable future experimental studies of the density dependence of the symmetry energy by measuring the asymptotic dipole signal in asymmetric heavy-ion collisions, and highlight the importance of dynamical \(np\) correlations beyond the mean-field approximation.
