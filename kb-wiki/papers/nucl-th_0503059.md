# Spherical Hartree-Fock calculations with linear momentum projection before the variation.Part I: Energies, form factors, charge densities and mathematical sum rules

> Spherical Hartree--Fock calculations with projection onto zero total linear momentum before the variation are performed for the nuclei 4He, 12C, 16O, 28Si, 32S and 40Ca using a density--independent effective nucleon--nucleon interaction. The results are compared to those of usual spherical Hartree--Fock calculations subtracting the kinetic energy of the center of mass motion either before or after the variation and to the results obtained analytically with oscillator occupations. Total energies, hole--energies, elastic charge form factors and charge densities and the mathematical Coulomb sum rules are discussed.

## Key claim

Spherical Hartree-Fock calculations with projection onto zero total linear momentum before the variation yield total binding energies, hole-energies, charge form factors, and charge densities that differ considerably from standard approaches that subtract the center-of-mass kinetic energy either before or after the variation, with effects far beyond the commonly assumed 1/A level even for nuclei as heavy as 40Ca.

## Method

Spherical Hartree-Fock calculations with projection into the center-of-mass rest frame before the variation (CMPSHF) were performed for six doubly even nuclei (4He, 12C, 16O, 28Si, 32S, 40Ca) using the Brink-Boeker B1 effective nucleon-nucleon interaction complemented with a short-range two-body spin-orbit term and the Coulomb force. For each nucleus, single-particle bases up to 19 major oscillator shells (N=18) were employed, and the results were compared with normal spherical Hartree-Fock calculations where the center-of-mass kinetic energy was subtracted either after or before the variation, as well as with analytic oscillator occupations and with calculations using a Lagrangian multiplier to penalize center-of-mass excitations.

## Key numbers

- For 40Ca in the largest basis (N=18), the projected binding energy is 1.25 MeV lower than the corrected result (where COM subtraction is done before variation), while the corrected result is only 136 keV lower than the normal approach (COM subtraction after variation)
- In 16O, the projected p-hole energies are more than 6 MeV lower than the corresponding corrected results
- In 40Ca, the differences between projected and corrected hole energies are still about 2.5 MeV for both the p- and the lowest s-holes
- The 1.25 MeV energy gain for 40Ca amounts to almost 20 percent of the total major shell mixing obtained in the corrected approach

## Context

This work extends earlier model investigations by Rodriguez-Guzman and Schmid (2004) and others that studied center-of-mass effects using simple oscillator wave functions for 4He, 16O, and 40Ca, where projection yielded no additional effect because pure oscillator A-nucleon configurations are non-spurious. The present paper provides more realistic Hartree-Fock calculations that demonstrate substantial Galilei-invariance restoration effects on total energies, hole-energies, charge form factors, charge densities, and Coulomb sum rules, contradicting the textbook argument that such effects can be safely neglected for nuclei beyond oxygen. The results enable future studies of spectral functions and spectroscopic factors using the projected wave functions, to be presented in a forthcoming second paper.
