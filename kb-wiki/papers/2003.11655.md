# Nucleon elastic form factors at accessible large spacelike momenta

> A Poincaré-covariant quark+diquark Faddeev equation is used to compute nucleon elastic form factors on $0\leq Q^2\leq 18 \,m_N^2$ ($m_N$ is the nucleon mass) and elucidate their role as probes of emergent hadronic mass in the Standard Model. The calculations expose features of the form factors that can be tested in new generation experiments at existing facilities, e.g. a zero in $G_E^p/G_M^p$; a maximum in $G_E^n/G_M^n$; and a zero in the proton's $d$-quark Dirac form factor, $F_1^d$. Additionally, examination of the associated light-front-transverse number and anomalous magnetisation densities reveals, inter alia: a marked excess of valence $u$-quarks in the neighbourhood of the proton's centre of transverse momentum; and that the valence $d$-quark is markedly more active magnetically than either of the valence $u$-quarks. The calculations and analysis also reveal other aspects of nucleon structure that could be tested with a high-luminosity accelerator capable of delivering higher beam energies than are currently available.

## Key claim

Using a Poincaré-covariant quark+diquark Faddeev equation with a statistical implementation of the Schlessinger point method (SPM) for extrapolation, the authors predict nucleon elastic form factors on $0 \leq Q^2 \leq 18\,m_N^2$, the farthest reach of approved Jefferson Lab experiments, but caution that the extrapolation beyond $Q^2/m_N^2 \approx 9$ relies on SPM approximants whose reliability is expressed by a $1\sigma$ confidence band, and that meson-cloud effects omitted from the calculation are significant for $Q^2 \lesssim 2\,m_N^2$.

## Method

The nucleon is described by a quark+diquark Faddeev equation solved with ten Chebyshev polynomials for the amplitude's momentum dependence, using diquark masses $m_{[ud]}=0.80$ GeV and $m_{\{uu\}}=m_{\{ud\}}=m_{\{dd\}}=0.89$ GeV, and a Euclidean constituent quark mass $M_{u,d}^E=0.33$ GeV. The electromagnetic current is computed from six diagrams (Fig. 2), with the three two-loop diagrams evaluated using $10^8$ Monte Carlo points. For $Q^2/m_N^2 \gtrsim 9$, where direct calculation becomes imprecise, the authors apply a statistical SPM: from 500 directly computed points on $0 \leq x \leq 4.5$ ($x=Q^2/m_N^2$), they randomly select 12-point samples, generate approximately 1000 surviving continued-fraction interpolants, and use their spread to define the extrapolation band out to $x=18$.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon core mass from Faddeev equation: $m_N = 1.18$ GeV (deliberately larger than physical mass to represent the dressed-quark core, with meson-cloud effects lowering it by $\approx 0.2$ GeV).
- Proton anomalous magnetic moment: $\kappa_p = 1.50$ (experimental: 1.79); neutron: $\kappa_n = -1.59$ (experimental: -1.91).
- Proton charge radius squared: $r_E^2 = (0.76)^2$ fm$^2$ (experimental: $(0.84)^2$); neutron charge radius squared: $r_E^2 = -(0.26)^2$ fm$^2$ (experimental: $-(0.34)^2$).
- Zero in $\mu_p G_E^p/G_M^p$ predicted at $Q_z^2 = 10.3^{+1.1}_{-0.7}$ GeV$^2$.
- Zero in $\mu_n G_E^n/G_M^n$ predicted at $Q_{z_n}^2 = 20.1^{+10.6}_{-3.5}$ GeV$^2$ (beyond reach of 12 GeV JLab beams at $1\sigma$).
- Zero in proton $d$-quark Dirac form factor $F_1^d$ at $x_{z_1} \approx 7.9_{-0.5}^{+1.2}$ ($Q^2 \approx 9.3$ GeV$^2$).
- Zero in proton $d$-quark Pauli form factor $F_2^d$ at $x_{z_2} \approx 13.6_{-1.9}^{+4.4}$ ($Q^2 \approx 16.0$ GeV$^2$).

## Context

This work extends the quark+diquark Faddeev equation calculations of Segovia et al. (2014) by overcoming numerical limitations that previously prevented reliable predictions for $Q^2/m_N^2 \gtrsim 10$, using the statistical SPM technique first applied to nucleon-to-Roper transition form factors by Chen et al. (2018). The predictions are directly testable by approved JLab experiments (E12-07-109, E12-07-108, E12-09-016, E12-09-019) that will measure proton and neutron form factors to $Q^2$ up to 15.5 GeV$^2$, and by the flavour-separated measurements anticipated by Wojtsekhowski et al. (2020). The results also complement lattice QCD calculations (Chambers et al., 2017; Kallidonis et al., 2018) that face different obstacles at high $Q^2$.
