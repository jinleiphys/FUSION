# Mach-like emission from nucleon scattering in proton-nucleus reaction

> The fast-stage nucleon emission of proton-nucleus (pA) reactions from 300A MeV to 1.8A GeV has been investigated using the quantum molecular-dynamics model. It is found that the sideward angular spectrum of nucleon emission presents an interesting Mach-like structure at the early stage of the collision (tens of fm/c). The sideward angular peak value varies from about 45\circ to near 73\circ, depending on the bombarding energy. Nucleons emitted from the vicinity of the sideward peak tend to have a fixed momentum value about 0.5 GeV/c, independent of the bombarding energy as well as the impact parameter. Additionally, the sideward angular peak value is almost independent of the equation of state, indicating that binary collision at the early fast stage in the intermediate energy pA reaction plays an important role in the emergence of Mach-like emission.

# Mach-like emission from nucleon scattering in proton-nucleus reaction

## Key claim

Using the quantum molecular dynamics model, the authors find a Mach-like sideward angular peak in nucleon emission from proton-nucleus reactions at early times (tens of fm/c), but this structure exists only when averaging over many events, at the single-event level, only 2–3 nucleons are emitted, so no Mach cone exists event-by-event. The sideward peak arises primarily from binary nucleon-nucleon collisions rather than from bulk hydrodynamic shock waves.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin-Dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IDQMD) model to simulate p + ²⁰⁸Pb reactions at bombarding energies from 330A MeV to 1.81A GeV, scanning impact parameters from 1 fm to 5 fm and testing both hard and soft equations of state. They tracked the time evolution of nucleon emission, recording kinetic energy spectra, polar angular distributions, and momentum-angle correlations at successive time steps (15 fm/c, 25 fm/c, etc.). The sideward angular peak value was obtained by averaging five consecutive peak values sampled at 1 fm/c intervals after the projectile proton punched through the target.

## Key numbers

- Sideward angular peak varies from about 45° at 330A MeV to a limiting value near 73° at 1.81A GeV for p + ²⁰⁸Pb.
- Nucleons near the sideward peak have a fixed momentum of about 0.53 GeV/c (corresponding to kinetic energy ~140 MeV) at 15 fm/c, independent of bombarding energy and impact parameter.
- At 15 fm/c, only about 2.5 nucleons per event are emitted on average, meaning fewer than three nucleons are available to form a Mach-like structure in any single event.
- The kinetic energy spectrum shows a fixed peak near 140 MeV before 20 fm/c, which later submerges as lower-energy nucleons appear.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Mach-like emission in heavy-ion collisions, notably the QMD simulations of Aichelin (1988) for ²⁰Ne + ¹⁹⁷Au at 1050A MeV and the hydrodynamic and UrQMD simulations of Rau (2010) for ²⁰Ne + ²³⁸U. It directly addresses the experimental findings of Hsi (1998, 1999), who observed sideward peaks for intermediate mass fragments but not for light charged particles and argued against shock-wave interpretations. The results suggest that the Mach-like structure in pA reactions is a statistical scattering effect from binary collisions rather than a collective hydrodynamic phenomenon, which may inform interpretations of the away-side double-peak structure observed in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC.
