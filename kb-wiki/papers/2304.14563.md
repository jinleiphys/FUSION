# Causality and stability in first-order conformal anisotropic hydrodynamics

> We formulate the first-order dissipative anisotropic hydrodynamical theory for a relativistic conformal uncharged fluid, which generalizes the Bemfica-Disconzi-Noronha-Kovtun first-order viscous fluid framework. Our approach maintains causal behavior in the nonlinear regime with or without general relativity coupling, and we derive and analyze the constraints on transport coefficients imposed by causality. We demonstrate the causal and stable behavior of our theory in specific cases, including the discussion of nonlinear causality as well as stability for linearized perturbations. We apply our newly developed first-order anisotropic theory to the Bjorken flow and show how causality and stability impose constraints on the behavior of the early-time attractor.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a first-order dissipative anisotropic hydrodynamical theory for a relativistic conformal uncharged fluid that generalizes the Bemfica-Disconzi-Noronha-Kovtun (BDNK) framework, claiming causal behavior in the nonlinear regime with or without general relativity coupling, but only under specific constraints on the transport coefficients derived from the characteristic equation.

## Method

The authors construct the most general energy-momentum tensor for a conformal uncharged fluid with a spatial anisotropy along a spacelike vector \(l^\mu\), then impose the second law of thermodynamics up to first order on-shell to determine the form of the dissipative fluxes. They analyze nonlinear causality by examining the principal part of the quasilinear partial differential equations derived from energy-momentum conservation, solving the characteristic equation for the normal vectors \(\xi_\mu\) to characteristic hypersurfaces, and deriving conditions on the eight transport coefficients \(\{\chi,\chi_l,\chi_\perp,\eta_{l},\eta_{ll},\eta_{\perp},\lambda_l,\lambda_\perp\}\) that ensure all roots are real and spacelike.

## Context

This work extends the BDNK first-order viscous fluid framework (Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, and Kovtun, 2017-2020) to include a spatial anisotropy, addressing the gap in understanding causality constraints in anisotropic hydrodynamics used for heavy-ion collisions (Martinez and Florkowski, 2010; Molnar, 2016) and neutron star mergers. It enables future studies of anisotropic dissipative effects in gravitational-wave signals from neutron star mergers and provides a foundation for testing causality in simulations where violations have been observed (Plumberg et al., 2021; Chiu and Shen, 2021).
