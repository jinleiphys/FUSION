# Formation of a supergiant quantum vortex in a relativistic Bose-Einstein condensate driven by rotation and a parallel magnetic field

> Analysis based on the energy spectrum of noninteracting bosons shows that, under the circumstance of parallel rotation and magnetic field, charged bosons form a Bose-Einstein condensate because of the lift of the Landau level degeneracy by rotation [\textcolor{blue}{Y. Liu and I. Zahed, Phys. Rev. Lett. {\bf120}, 032001 (2018)}]. In this work, we study the interaction effect on the ground state of this Bose-Einstein condensate of charged bosons from the viewpoint of spontaneous symmetry breaking. We employ a minimal model for charged bosons with repulsive self-interaction. We find that the ground state of such a Bose-Einstein condensate is a supergiant quantum vortex, i.e., a quantized vortex with a large circulation. The size of the vortex is as large as the system size. The low-energy dispersion of the excitation spectra exhibits quadratic behavior, which is an anisotropic realization of the type-II Goldstone boson. Our study may give some implications to off-central relativistic heavy ion collisions, where large vorticity and magnetic fields can be generated.

## Key claim

The ground state of a relativistic Bose-Einstein condensate of charged bosons under parallel rotation and magnetic field, with repulsive self-interaction, is a supergiant quantum vortex (winding number w ≫ 1) whose size is as large as the system size, rather than a vortex lattice or a singly quantized vortex.

## Method

The authors employ a minimal model of a relativistic complex scalar field with repulsive self-interaction (λ|Φ|⁴) in a constant magnetic field B along the z-direction and a global rigid rotation Ω along the same axis. They work in the rotating frame using imaginary-time formalism at zero temperature, decompose the field into a classical condensate and quantum fluctuations, and minimize the Gross-Pitaevskii-type potential via variational expansion in the eigenfunctions of the noninteracting Klein-Gordon equation in parallel rotation and magnetic field. The condensate profile and excitation spectra are computed numerically for system sizes corresponding to Landau level degeneracy N = 25 and N = 100.

## Key numbers

- For eB = m_π² and N = 25 (system radius R ≈ 10 fm), the critical angular velocity for BEC is Ω_c R ≈ 0.59; for λ = 1, the winding numbers are w = 23, 29, 39 at ΩR = 0.6, 0.7, 0.8 respectively.
- For eB = m_π² and N = 100 (R ≈ 20 fm), Ω_c R ≈ 0.25; for λ = 1, the winding numbers are w = 84, 86, 93 at ΩR = 0.26, 0.3, 0.4 respectively.
- The location of the unique minimum of K_{0l} for N = 25 is l_0 = 24, 30, 42 at ΩR = 0.6, 0.7, 0.8; for N = 100, l_0 = 84, 88, 98 at ΩR = 0.26, 0.3, 0.4.
- The critical angular velocity for N → ∞ (R → ∞) approaches Ω_c R ≈ 2/√N, which is vanishingly small.

## Context

This work extends the noninteracting analysis of Liu and Zahed (2018), who showed that parallel rotation and magnetic field induce Bose-Einstein condensation of charged pions by lifting the Landau level degeneracy. The present study introduces repulsive self-interaction and finds that the ground state is a supergiant quantum vortex rather than a vortex lattice, which is the typical outcome in rotating superfluids. The results may have implications for off-central relativistic heavy ion collisions, where large vorticity (Ω ≈ 9×10²¹ Hz ≈ 0.05 m_π from STAR Collaboration data) and magnetic fields (eB ∼ m_π² from numerical simulations) are generated. The quadratic low-energy dispersion of the Goldstone mode provides an anisotropic realization of the type-II Goldstone boson.
