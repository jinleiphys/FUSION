# Many-body perturbation theory for strongly correlated effective Hamiltonians using effective field theory methods

> Introducing low-energy effective Hamiltonians is usual to grasp most correlations in quantum many-body problems. For instance, such effective Hamiltonians can be treated at the mean-field level to reproduce some physical properties of interest. Employing effective Hamiltonians that contain many-body correlations renders the use of perturbative many-body techniques difficult because of the overcounting of correlations. In this work, we develop a strategy to apply an extension of the many-body perturbation theory starting from an effective interaction that contains correlations beyond the mean field level. The goal is to re-organize the many-body calculation to avoid the overcounting of correlations originating from the introduction of correlated effective Hamiltonians in the description. For this purpose, we generalize the formulation of the Rayleigh-Schrödinger perturbation theory by including free parameters adjusted to reproduce the appropriate limits. In particular, the expansion in the bare weak-coupling regime and the strong-coupling limit serves as a valuable input to fix the value of the free parameters appearing in the resulting expression. This method avoids double counting of correlations using beyond-mean-field strategies for the description of many-body systems. The ground state energy of various systems relevant for ultracold atomic, nuclear, and condensed matter physics is reproduced qualitatively beyond the domain of validity of the standard many-body perturbation theory. Finally, our method suggests interpreting the formal results obtained as an effective field theory using the proposed reorganization of the many-body calculation. The results, like ground state energies, are improved systematically by considering higher orders in the extended many-body perturbation theory while maintaining a straightforward polynomial expansion.

# Many-body perturbation theory for strongly correlated effective Hamiltonians using effective field theory methods

## Key claim

The authors develop an extended many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) that allows using effective Hamiltonians containing beyond-mean-field correlations without double-counting, by introducing free parameters adjusted to reproduce both the weak-coupling expansion and the strong-coupling limit. The method works qualitatively beyond the domain of validity of standard MBPT, but the ground state energy acquires an imaginary part at higher orders that must be removed by a minimal subtraction procedure.

## Method

The authors generalize Rayleigh-Schrödinger perturbation theory by introducing a free parameter beta in the resolvent operator, replacing the standard choice omega = E0 with omega = H0 + beta(E0 - H0). They then use an effective Hamiltonian of the form H_eff = H0 + (lambda h)/(1 + lambda gamma1/a), where the parameter a is fixed by imposing the high-scale (strong-coupling) limit. At each perturbative order, the beta parameters are adjusted to match the known low-scale expansion coefficients gamma_n up to that order, while the high-scale parameter a is determined from the strong-coupling constraint. For third order and beyond, multiple beta parameters are introduced via an arithmetic mean of energy shifts.

## Key numbers

- Bertsch parameter (unitary limit): xi_0 = 0.376(4) for the spin-saturated ultracold Fermi gas
- First-order low-scale coefficient for attractive interaction: gamma_1 = 10k_F/(9pi) where k_F is the Fermi momentum
- Second-order low-scale coefficient for attractive interaction: gamma_2 = 4(11-2ln2)k_F^2/(21pi^2)
- Third-order low-scale coefficient for attractive interaction: gamma_3 = 0.032 k_F^3
- Fourth-order low-scale coefficient for attractive interaction: gamma_4 = 0.451 k_F^4
- First-order low-scale coefficient for repulsive interaction: gamma_1 = k_F/(6pi)
- Free Fermi gas energy per particle: E_0/N = 3k_F^2/(10m)

## Context

This work extends prior efforts to incorporate beyond-mean-field correlations into effective Hamiltonians while avoiding double counting, such as the parameter adjustment approach of Moghrabi (2016) and the counter-term method of Yang (2017). The method builds on the Padé[1/1] form for ultracold atom ground state energies proposed by Adhikari (2008) and Lacroix (2016), which used the unitary limit as a constraint at the Hartree-Fock level. The new formalism enables systematic inclusion of higher-order perturbative corrections while maintaining the correct low-scale expansion and finite strong-coupling limit, and suggests an effective field theory interpretation where the expansion parameter is lambda/f_lambda with f_lambda playing the role of a high-scale cutoff.
