# Neutron skin of $^{208}$Pb from Coherent Pion Photoproduction

> Information on the size and shape of the neutron skin on $^{208}$Pb has been extracted from coherent pion photoproduction cross sections measured using the Crystal Ball together with the Glasgow tagger at the MAMI electron beam facility. On exploitation of an interpolated fit of a theoretical model to the measured cross sections the half-height radius and diffuseness of the neutron distribution are found to be 6.70$\pm 0.03(stat)$ fm and 0.55$\pm 0.01(stat)$$^{+0.02}_{-0.03}(sys)$ fm respectively, corresponding to a neutron skin thickness $Δr_{np}$=0.15$\pm 0.03(stat)$$^{+0.01}_{-0.03}(sys)$ fm. The results give the first successful extraction of a neutron skin with an electromagnetic probe and indicate the skin of $^{208}$Pb has a halo character. The measurement provides valuable new constraints on both the structure of nuclei and the equation of state for neutron-rich matter.

## Key claim
The neutron skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb is extracted from coherent pion photoproduction cross sections, yielding $\Delta r_{np}=0.15\pm 0.03(stat.)^{+0.01}_{-0.03}(sys.)$ fm, but the result depends on an interpolated fit of a theoretical model that treats the pion-nucleus final-state interaction with a complex optical potential; the extraction is restricted to the photon energy range 180-240 MeV where the model assumptions are most reliable.

## Method
Coherent $\pi^0$ photoproduction cross sections on an isotopically enriched $^{208}$Pb target were measured using the Crystal Ball detector and Glasgow photon tagger at the MAMI electron beam facility, for incident photon energies from 180 to 240 MeV. The differential cross sections as a function of momentum transfer were compared to predictions from a unitary isobar model (Drechsel et al.) that includes a complex optical potential for the pion-nucleus interaction, with the nucleon density parameterized as separate two-parameter Fermi distributions for protons and neutrons. A grid of 35 points in neutron half-height radius ($c_n$) and diffuseness ($a_n$) was used to interpolate the model cross sections, and a two-dimensional interpolation fit extracted the best-fit neutron distribution parameters.

## Key numbers
- Neutron half-height radius: $c_n = 6.70 \pm 0.03\ (\text{stat.})$ fm
- Neutron diffuseness: $a_n = 0.55 \pm 0.01\ (\text{stat.})^{+0.02}_{-0.03}\ (\text{sys.})$ fm
- Neutron skin thickness: $\Delta r_{np} = 0.15 \pm 0.03\ (\text{stat.})^{+0.01}_{-0.03}\ (\text{sys.})$ fm
- Proton distribution parameters fixed from electron scattering: $c_p = 6.680$ fm, $a_p = 0.447$ fm
- Photon energy range of the analysis: 180-240 MeV
- Momentum transfer range of the fit: $q = 0.3$ to $0.9$ fm$^{-1}$
- Experimental $q$ resolution: $\sigma_q = 0.02$-$0.03$ fm$^{-1}$ depending on $E_\gamma$

## Context
This work provides the first successful extraction of a neutron skin thickness using an electromagnetic probe (coherent pion photoproduction), complementing previous measurements with strongly interacting probes such as proton scattering (Zenihiro et al., giving $\Delta r_{np}=0.211\pm0.06$ fm), pion scattering (Friedman et al., $\Delta r_{np}=0.16\pm0.07$ fm), antiproton annihilation ($\Delta r_{np}=0.18\pm0.04\pm0.05$ fm), and isospin diffusion in heavy-ion collisions ($\Delta r_{np}=0.22\pm0.04$ fm), as well as the electroweak PREX measurement ($\Delta r_{np}=0.33\pm0.17$ fm). The result discriminates among nuclear structure models and provides a new constraint on the density dependence of the symmetry energy and the equation of state for neutron-rich matter, with implications for neutron star structure.
