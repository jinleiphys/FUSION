# Simultaneous chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement - Consequences for the QCD phase diagram

> For studies of quark matter in astrophysical scenarios the thermodynamic bag model (tdBag) is commonly employed. Although successful, it does not account for dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (D$χ$SB) and repulsions due to the vector interaction which is crucial to explain recent observations of massive, two solar mass neutron stars. In Klähn & Fischer (2015) we developed the novel vBag quark matter model which takes these effects into account. This article extends vBag to finite temperatures and isospin asymmetry. Another particular feature of vBag is the determination of the deconfinement bag constant $B_{\rm dc}$ from a given hadronic equation of state (EoS) in order to ensure that chiral and deconfinement transitions coincide. We discuss consequences of this novel approach for the phase transition construction, the phase diagram, and implications for protoneutron stars.

# Simultaneous chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement - Consequences for the QCD phase diagram

## Key claim

The vBag quark matter model, extended to finite temperatures and isospin asymmetry, enforces simultaneous chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement by introducing a medium-dependent deconfinement bag constant $B_{\rm dc}$ derived from the hadronic equation of state, which shifts the deconfinement onset to match the chiral transition chemical potential, but this simultaneity is an assumption imposed on the model, not a general property of vBag.

## Method

The authors extend their previously developed vBag model (Klähn & Fischer 2015) from zero temperature to finite temperatures and isospin asymmetry by replacing zero-temperature Fermi-gas integrals with finite-temperature expressions and treating up and down quark chemical potentials independently. The deconfinement bag constant $B_{\rm dc}$ is determined by equating the hadronic and quark matter pressures at the chiral transition chemical potential $\mu_{B,\chi}$ (defined by the condition that the total light-quark pressure vanishes), making $B_{\rm dc}$ a function of temperature $T$ and charge chemical potential $\mu_C$. This procedure introduces medium-dependent correction terms to the quark matter charge density, entropy density, and energy density via derivatives of $B_{\rm dc}$ with respect to $T$ and $\mu_C$. The hadronic equation of state used for illustration is the HS(DD2) model from Hempel (2009) based on the relativistic mean-field parametrization DD2 from Typel (2009).

## Key numbers

- The chiral bag constant used for illustration is $B_{\chi}^{1/4} = 152.7$ MeV (single flavor), taken from the NJL model parameterization of Grigorian (2006).
- In the "standard" approach ($B_{\rm dc}=0$), deconfinement occurs at $\mu_{B,{\rm dc}} \approx 1400$ MeV while chiral restoration occurs at $\mu_{B,\chi} \approx 1150$ MeV (at low temperature, $\mu_C=-150$ MeV, $T=30$ MeV).
- With the vBag approach, the onset of the phase transition shifts from about $3.5\,n_S$ to about $2.5\,n_S$ (where the saturation density for HS(DD2) is $n_S = 0.155$ fm$^{-3}$), and the density range of the phase coexistence region shrinks from $2\,n_S$ to less than $0.5\,n_S$.
- Lattice QCD predicts a crossover transition in the temperature range $150 < T < 170$ MeV, with a currently considered value of $154 \pm 9$ MeV (Katz 2012, Laermann 2012, Bazavov 2014).
- The observed neutron star mass constraint is about $2$ M$_\odot$, with a recent revision to $1.928 \pm 0.017$ M$_\odot$ from Fonseca (2016).

## Context

This work extends the vBag model (Klähn & Fischer 2015) from zero temperature to finite temperatures and isospin asymmetry, building on the thermodynamic bag model (tdBag) of Farhi (1984) and NJL-type models (Nambu 1961, Klevansky 1992, Buballa 2003). The approach contrasts with standard Maxwell or Gibbs constructions (Rischke 1988, Barz 1989) where hadronic and quark equations of state are treated independently, and with the work of Pagliara (2007) who previously discussed enforcing deconfinement at the chiral transition at zero temperature. The resulting hybrid equation of state is intended for astrophysical applications such as neutron star mergers, core collapse supernovae, and protoneutron star cooling (Pons 2001, Sagert 2008, Fischer 2011), where the simultaneous treatment of chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement may have significant consequences for the phase diagram and stellar structure.
