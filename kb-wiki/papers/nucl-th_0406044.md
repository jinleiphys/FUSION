# Covariant propagator of the Rarita-Schwinger field in the nuclear medium

> A formalism for representing the fully relativistic propagator of the Rarita-Schwinger field in the nuclear medium is developed. Using a convenient basis for expanding the propagator, or the self energy, it is shown that they can be represented by 40 energy and momentum dependent quantities which can be combined into a 2x2 and a 6x6 matrix. In this way calculations reduce to matrix multiplication. Using the presented formalism the full relativistically covariant contribution of the pion-nucleon loop to the isobar self energy and propagator in isospin-symmetric spin-saturated nuclear medium is computed. Utilizing this propagator the photoabsorption cross-section on in-medium nucleons in the isobar region is calculated and the result compared with experimental data.

# Covariant propagator of the Rarita-Schwinger field in the nuclear medium

## Key claim

The authors develop a fully relativistic formalism for the in-medium Rarita-Schwinger propagator using a 40-component basis that separates into a 2x2 matrix (helicity-3/2) and a 6x6 matrix (helicity-1/2), and show that in the resonance region the spin-3/2 components dominate by about two orders of magnitude over spin-1/2 components, allowing a simplified treatment with only two propagator components in the medium while maintaining few-percent accuracy.

## Method

The propagator and self energy of the Rarita-Schwinger field are expanded in a basis of 40 Lorentz-covariant terms constructed from the medium four-velocity, the particle four-momentum, Dirac matrices, and helicity projectors, which separate into two orthogonal subalgebras forming a 2x2 and a 6x6 matrix. The Dyson equation reduces to matrix inversion in this basis. The pion-nucleon loop contribution to the isobar self energy is computed in isospin-symmetric spin-saturated nuclear matter using a pion-nucleon-delta interaction Lagrangian with the off-shell parameter set to zero, and the resulting dressed propagator is used to calculate the photoabsorption cross section on free and in-medium nucleons.

## Key numbers

- The free isobar propagator expansion coefficients include: g^{(Q)-1}_{[11]} = sqrt(p^2) - M, g^{(Q)-1}_{[22]} = -(sqrt(p^2) + M), g^{(P)-1}_{[55]} = sqrt(p^2) - M, g^{(P)-1}_{[66]} = -(sqrt(p^2) + M), where M is the bare isobar mass.
- The pion-nucleon-delta coupling constant fitted to pion-nucleon scattering phase shifts is g_{pi N Delta} = 20.2 GeV^{-1}, with a form factor cutoff Lambda = 0.97 GeV.
- The magnetic dipole coupling strength for the gamma-N-Delta vertex is g_m = 3.
- In the nuclear medium calculation, the Fermi momentum is k_F = 270 MeV (saturation density).
- The mean-field shift of the isobar mass relative to the nucleon is -30 MeV.
- The pion-nucleon-delta form factor cutoff parameters used are Lambda_{pi N Delta} = 0.7 GeV and 0.8 GeV, with a pion-nucleon form factor cutoff Lambda_{pi NN} = 0.5 GeV.
- The Migdal parameters used are g'_NN = 0.8, g'_N Delta = g'_Delta Delta = 0.6.
- The helicity-3/2 component contributes three times as much as the helicity-1/2 component to the photoabsorption cross section, consistent with the factor of sqrt(3) expected for magnetic dipole transitions.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic treatment of the Rarita-Schwinger field in vacuum, previously developed by Davidson (1991) and Kaloshin (2004), to the nuclear medium using the in-medium partial-wave expansion basis introduced by Lutz (2002). It contrasts with earlier nonrelativistic approaches such as Korpa (1995) and with treatments assuming the in-medium isobar propagator is proportional to the spin-3/2 projector, as done by Xia (1994) and Van Daele (2002), which neglect helicity splitting. The formalism enables fully covariant calculations of processes involving the delta isobar in nuclear matter, demonstrated here through photoabsorption cross sections compared to experimental data from Armstrong (1972) and Ahrens (1984).
