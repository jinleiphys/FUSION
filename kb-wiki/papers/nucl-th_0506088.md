# Multiplicity distribution and source deformation in full-overlap U+U collisions

> We present a full Monte Carlo simulation of the multiplicity and eccentricity distributions in U+U collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 A GeV. While unavoidable trigger inefficiencies in selecting full-overlap U+U collisions cause significant modifications of the multiplicity distribution shown in PRL94, 132301 (2005), a selection of source eccentricities by cutting the multiplicity distribution is still possible.

## Key claim

While unavoidable trigger inefficiencies in selecting full-overlap U+U collisions destroy the double-hump structure of the idealized multiplicity distribution, a selection of source eccentricities by cutting the multiplicity distribution is still possible, provided sufficiently tight cuts on spectator nucleon counts are applied.

## Method

The authors performed a full Monte Carlo simulation of U+U collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 A GeV using a Glauber model parametrization of initial entropy production, with a Woods-Saxon density distribution and a combination of wounded nucleon and binary collision densities (with wounded nucleon scaling fraction alpha = 0.75). They integrated over the five-dimensional parameter space of impact parameter and two Euler angles for each nucleus, introducing Gaussian event-by-event fluctuations of the multiplicity with width parameter a = 0.6, to generate multiplicity and eccentricity distributions for approximately 200,000 events.

## Key numbers

- The non-linear dependence of charged multiplicity on participant nucleons leads to a ~15% variation of charged particle multiplicity among full-overlap U+U collisions as the relative orientation of symmetry axes varies.
- For the 0.5% of events with the lowest spectator count, selecting the 5% with lowest multiplicity yields an average source eccentricity of about 18%, corresponding to Au+Au collisions with impact parameters around 5.5 fm.
- For the same 0.5% spectator cut, selecting the 5% with largest multiplicities gives an eccentricity distribution peaking at epsilon_x = 0 with very small average spatial deformation.
- With a looser 5% spectator cut, the average eccentricities shift from 17.7% to 14.2% for low-multiplicity selection and from 2.2% to 4.3% for high-multiplicity selection.
- The eccentricity of the nuclear overlap region can exceed the ~25% ground state deformation of the single-uranium density distribution when looser spectator cuts allow non-zero impact parameters.
- The tightest selections (0.5% spectator cut plus 5% multiplicity cut) correspond to only 0.025% of all U+U collisions taking place in the accelerator.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier proposal in Heinz (2005) for using full-overlap collisions of deformed uranium nuclei to probe elliptic flow and parton energy loss at RHIC, by quantitatively assessing the effects of realistic trigger inefficiencies. The calculations use a Glauber model approach similar to KSH00 and incorporate multiplicity fluctuations following Kharzeev (2000). The results enable a practical experimental program at RHIC using simultaneous cuts on spectator nucleons (via zero degree calorimeters) and charged particle multiplicity to select collision events with well-defined source deformations, though the authors note that Color Glass Condensate initial conditions as discussed by Hirano (2004) might produce somewhat larger eccentricities and narrower distributions.
