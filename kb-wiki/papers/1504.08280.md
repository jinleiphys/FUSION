# Critical point in the QCD phase diagram for extremely strong background magnetic fields

> Lattice simulations have demonstrated that a background (electro)magnetic field reduces the chiral/deconfinement transition temperature of quantum chromodynamics for eB < 1 GeV^2. On the level of observables, this reduction manifests itself in an enhancement of the Polyakov loop and in a suppression of the light quark condensates (inverse magnetic catalysis) in the transition region. In this paper, we report on lattice simulations of 1+1+1-flavor QCD at an unprecedentedly high value of the magnetic field eB = 3.25 GeV^2. Based on the behavior of various observables, it is shown that even at this extremely strong field, inverse magnetic catalysis prevails and the transition, albeit becoming sharper, remains an analytic crossover. In addition, we develop an algorithm to directly simulate the asymptotically strong magnetic field limit of QCD. We find strong evidence for a first-order deconfinement phase transition in this limiting theory, implying the presence of a critical point in the QCD phase diagram. Based on the available lattice data, we estimate the location of the critical point.

## Key claim

The paper provides strong lattice evidence that the QCD phase diagram contains a critical point at extremely strong background magnetic fields, where the analytic crossover transitions into a first-order deconfinement phase transition; however, the exact location of this critical point is estimated rather than precisely determined, and the transition remains an analytic crossover at the simulated field strength of eB = 3.25 GeV².

## Method

The authors performed lattice simulations of 1+1+1-flavor QCD with stout smeared rooted staggered quarks and tree-level Symanzik improved gauge action on Nt=16 lattices at a fixed magnetic flux corresponding to eB = 3.25 GeV², using spatial volumes of 32³ and 48³ to control finite-size effects. They also developed an algorithm to directly simulate the asymptotically strong magnetic field limit of QCD, which reduces to an anisotropic pure gauge theory with an anisotropy coefficient κ ∝ B, and simulated this theory on 12³×4, 16³×4, 20³×4, 24³×4, and 16³×8 lattices to study the nature of the deconfinement transition.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature from the average light quark condensate at eB = 3.25 GeV²: Tc = 112(3) MeV
- Transition temperature from the Polyakov loop at eB = 3.25 GeV²: Tc = 109(3) MeV
- Transition temperature from the strange quark number susceptibility at eB = 3.25 GeV²: Tc = 109(3) MeV
- Transition temperature from the light quark susceptibility peak at eB = 3.25 GeV²: Tc = 113(4) MeV
- Up and down quark condensate transition temperatures at eB = 3.25 GeV²: Tc{Σu} = 112(3) MeV and Tc{Σd} = 111(3) MeV
- Critical inverse gauge coupling for the anisotropic theory at κ = ∞ on 16³×4 lattices: βc = 2.42(5)
- Zero-field transition temperatures from fit: Tc(0) = 160(2) MeV for Σud and Tc(0) = 174(2) MeV for c2s

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD results at magnetic fields eB < 1 GeV² (Bali et al., 2011; Bali et al., 2012; Bruckmann et al., 2013) to an unprecedentedly strong field of eB = 3.25 GeV², confirming that inverse magnetic catalysis persists and the transition remains a crossover at this field strength. The finding of a first-order transition in the asymptotic B → ∞ limit, simulated as an anisotropic gauge theory following the effective action derived by Miransky and Shovkovy (2002), confirms the conjecture by Cohen (2013) that a critical point exists in the strong-field region of the QCD phase diagram, and provides a target for effective model builders to reproduce this behavior.
