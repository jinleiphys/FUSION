# Real Time Dynamics of Colliding Gauge Fields and the ''Glue Burst''

> The Yang Mills equations provide a classical mean field description of gauge fields. In view of developing a coherent description of the formation of the quark gluon plasma in high energetic nucleus-nucleus collisions we study pure gauge field dynamics in 3+1 dimensions. In collisions of wave packets, numerically simulated on a SU(2) gauge lattice, we study transverse and longitudinal energy currents. For wave packets with different polarizations in color space, we observe a time delayed fragmentation after the collision resulting in a rapid expansion into transverse directions. We call this phenomenon the ''glue burst''. An analysis of the Yang Mills equations reveals the explanation for this behavior. We point out that this effect could play a role in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors observe a time-delayed fragmentation of colliding non-Abelian gauge field wave packets, termed the "glue burst", which results in a rapid expansion of energy into transverse directions, but only when the wave packets have different polarizations in color space; the effect is absent for equally polarized (Abelian) wave packets.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the classical SU(2) Yang-Mills equations on a 3+1 dimensional gauge lattice in the temporal gauge, using link variables and color electric fields as dynamical variables. They simulate collisions of Gaussian wave packets with opposite longitudinal momenta, initialized with either equal or orthogonal color polarizations, and compute transverse and longitudinal energy densities and energy currents (Poynting vectors) as functions of time. The simulations are performed on lattices of sizes up to 20x20x2000 points for plane waves and 4x200x800 points for wave packets with finite transverse extent.

## Key numbers

- The simulation uses a lattice spacing of \(a = 0.1\,\text{fm}\) and a time step size of \(\Delta t = a/10 = 0.01\,\text{fm}\).
- The wave packets have average momentum \(\overline{k}_3 = \pi/(2a_3)\) and momentum spread \(\Delta k_3 = \pi/(100a)\), corresponding to a FWHM of \(10\,\text{fm}\) (approximately the diameter of a \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) nucleus).
- The total energy of both wave packets is \(24.76\,\text{GeV}\) for the chosen numerical parameters.
- The longitudinal energy density in the overlap region grows by more than two orders of magnitude from time step \(t_{1000}\) to \(t_{1500}\), and later grows by five orders of magnitude around \(t_{10000}\).
- The time delay \(\tau\) between maximum overlap and the onset of the glue burst scales as \(\tau \sim 1/g^2\) and, for large \(\overline{k}_3\), as \(\tau \sim \overline{k}_3^{-3/4}\).
- For coupling \(g=4\), the delay from maximum overlap to the regrowth of transverse energy current is about 3500 time steps or \(\tau \simeq 35\,\text{fm}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier 1+1 dimensional studies of colliding Yang-Mills wave packets by Hu and Müller (1995), which showed that non-Abelian wave packets decay into low-frequency modes, while Abelian ones do not interact. The present study adds transverse dynamics in 3+1 dimensions, revealing the "glue burst" phenomenon where energy is transferred from longitudinal to transverse degrees of freedom after a coupling-dependent delay. The results are motivated by the McLerran-Venugopalan (1994) approach to describing the initial state of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions via coherent color fields, and the authors suggest the glue burst could play a role in quark-gluon plasma formation. The work enables future studies combining gauge lattice descriptions with transport models for color-charged particles, as previously proposed by the authors (BMP 1998, PM 1998).
