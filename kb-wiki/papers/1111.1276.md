# Modification of Eckart theory of relativistic dissipative fluid by introducing extended matching conditions

> We deal with a novel approach to formulation of the relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics by extending the so-called matching conditions widely used in the literature. The form of the non-equilibrium entropy current can be determined by requiring thermodynamical stability of the entropy current under extended matching conditions. We derive equations of motion for the relativistic dissipative fluid based on the Eckart theory and show that linearized equations obtained from them are stable against small perturbations. It is also shown that the required fluid stability conditions are related to the causality of the model.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that by relaxing the standard matching conditions (which set the dissipative corrections to energy density and baryon density to zero) and instead allowing first-order corrections to the energy density and second-order corrections to the baryon density, the resulting first-order Eckart-type relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics can be made stable against small perturbations, contrary to the well-known instability of the standard Eckart theory.

## Method

The author introduces extended matching conditions where the dissipative part of the energy-momentum tensor's time-time component is taken as a first-order infinitesimal quantity (proportional to bulk pressure via a parameter κ), and the dissipative part of the baryon current's time component is taken as a second-order infinitesimal quantity (expressed in terms of squares of dissipative fluxes). The form of the non-equilibrium entropy current is then determined by requiring thermodynamic stability under these extended conditions, leading to a modified entropy production and constitutive equations with relaxation times. Linear stability analysis is performed by considering plane-wave perturbations around a homogeneous non-equilibrium background state and examining the dispersion relations for transverse and longitudinal modes.

## Key numbers

- The extended matching conditions are: u_μ u_ν δT^{μν} = Λ = κΠ (first order, κ dimensionless) and u_μ δN^{μ} = δn = -(ξ/μ_b)Π² + (ξ'/μ_b)W^μ W_μ - (ξ''/μ_b)π^{μν}π_{μν} (second order, with ξ, ξ', ξ'' having dimension [GeV]⁻⁴)
- Relaxation times are given by: τ_Π = ζ(2ξ + κ/Π₀), τ_w = 2ξ'λT, τ_π = 2ξ''η
- The transverse mode stability condition is: λT/τ_w < h_eq (where h_eq is the equilibrium enthalpy density)
- For the standard matching conditions (τ_Π = τ_w = τ_π = 0), the transverse mode dispersion relation gives Γ = h_eq/(2λT) ± √[(h_eq/(2λT))² + ηk²/(λT)], which has two real solutions leading to instability

## Context

This work extends the Eckart theory (Eckart, 1940) of relativistic dissipative fluids by modifying the matching conditions that fix the dissipative parts of the energy-momentum tensor and particle current. It contrasts with the Israel-Stewart theory (Israel and Stewart, 1976, 1979), which introduces second-order terms in the entropy current to achieve stability and causality as shown by Hiscock and Lindblom (1983, 1985). The author's previous work (Osada, 2010) argued that for relativistic fluids with long-range correlations, u_μ u_ν δT^{μν} ≠ 0, motivating the extended conditions. The approach enables a first-order theory that avoids the instability problems of standard Eckart theory while remaining simpler than the full Israel-Stewart framework, and may be applicable to modeling hot matter in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
