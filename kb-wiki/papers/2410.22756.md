# Exploring the $ Ōē{\text -}őĪ$ interaction via femtoscopic study

> Very recently the Wood-Saxon (WS) type interaction in the single-folding potential approach are constructed to simulate the $ Ōē{\text -}őĪ$ potentials. One set of the $ Ōē{\text -}őĪ$ potentials are based on the first principle HAL QCD $Ōē{\text -}N $ interactions in $^{4}S_{3/2}$ channel, and in another set, the $Ōē$-meson-nucleus potentials were calculated by employing the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model. By utilizing these two set of $ Ōē{\text -}őĪ$ potentials, the two-particle momentum correlation of $ Ōē{\text -}őĪ$ in high-energy heavy ion collisions is explored. The numerical results show that the correlation functions at small source size (high density nuclear medium) depends on the employed potential model. Also, the correlation functions are obtained within the Lednicky-Lyuboshits (LL) formalism. For small source size, it is found that, the LL formula returns significantly different values due to the large interaction range of the $Ōē{\text -}őĪ$ potential.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the phi-alpha momentum correlation function, calculated using the Koonin-Pratt formula, is sensitive to the choice of potential model (HAL QCD or QMC) only at small source sizes (high density nuclear medium) of 1-3 fm, while for larger source sizes of 5 fm the correlation functions become nearly indistinguishable.

## Method

The author constructs six Wood-Saxon type phi-alpha potentials via a single-folding approach: three based on first-principles HAL QCD phi-N interactions in the 4S3/2 channel using a Gaussian matter density distribution for the alpha particle, and three based on the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model with different cutoff parameters. The two-particle momentum correlation functions are then computed using the Koonin-Pratt formula with the CATS toolkit for source sizes of 1, 3, and 5 fm, and compared with results from the Lednicky-Lyuboshits (LL) formalism using scattering lengths and effective ranges extracted from the potentials.

## Key numbers

* HAL QCD potential with rms radius 1.84 fm: binding energy 2.97 MeV, scattering length 4.0 fm, effective range 1.7 fm
* HAL QCD potential with rms radius 1.70 fm: binding energy 4.78 MeV, scattering length 3.4 fm, effective range 1.5 fm
* HAL QCD potential with rms radius 1.56 fm: binding energy 5.98 MeV, scattering length 3.0 fm, effective range 1.3 fm
* QMC potential with cutoff Lambda_K = 2 GeV: binding energy 0.80 MeV, scattering length 6.7 fm, effective range 2.0 fm
* QMC potential with cutoff Lambda_K = 3 GeV: binding energy 3.19 MeV, scattering length 3.9 fm, effective range 1.7 fm
* QMC potential with cutoff Lambda_K = 4 GeV: binding energy 4.71 MeV, scattering length 3.4 fm, effective range 1.6 fm
* The interaction range for both potential types is about 3 fm
* The experimental root-mean-square radius of 4He is 1.70 +/- 0.14 fm

## Context

This work extends recent femtoscopic studies of hadron-nucleus correlations, specifically following the theoretical investigations of Lambda-alpha correlations by Jinno (2024), Xi-alpha correlations by Kamiya (2024), and Omega-alpha correlations by Etminan (2024). It is motivated by the ALICE collaboration's measurement of proton-phi correlation functions (2021) and a research plan at JLab to investigate phi binding to 4He. The paper uses the phi-alpha potentials recently constructed by Filikhin (2024) from both HAL QCD and QMC models, and provides predictions that could be tested at future experiments at FAIR, NICA, and J-PARC HI.
