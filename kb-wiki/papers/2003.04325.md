# Entropy production far from equilibrium in a chiral charged plasma in the presence of external electromagnetic fields

> We report on the time evolution of a charged strongly coupled $N=4$ SYM plasma with an axial anomaly subjected to strong electromagnetic fields. The evolution of this plasma corresponds to a fully backreacted asymptotically AdS$_5$ solution to the Einstein-Maxwell-Chern-Simons theory. We explore the evolution of the axial current and production of axial charges. As an application we show that after a sufficiently long time both the entropy and the holographic entanglement entropy of a strip-like topology ( both parallel to and transverse to the flow of axial current) grow linearly in time.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a strongly coupled chiral plasma subjected to aligned external electric and magnetic fields, the anomalous production of axial charges leads to linear growth of both thermal entropy and holographic entanglement entropy at late times, despite the chiral magnetic effect current itself being dissipationless. The authors note that their homogeneous, translationally invariant system acts as a perfect conductor, causing the heat current coefficient and total charge to grow without bound.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the fully backreacted Einstein-Maxwell-Chern-Simons equations in asymptotically AdS$_5$ spacetime using a characteristic formulation, with aligned external electric and magnetic fields along the $x_3$-direction. They employ Chebyshev spectral methods with domain decomposition (6 sub-domains, 24 grid points each) and a 4th order Runge-Kutta time stepping scheme, using subtracted functions to handle logarithmic near-boundary terms. The holographic entanglement entropy for strip-like subsystems is computed via a relaxation method on a cutoff surface at $z_{UV}=0.075$ with 350 grid points.

## Key numbers

- The electric field $E$ and magnetic field $\mathcal{B}$ are aligned along the $x_3$-direction, with the magnetic field entering as a constant $\mathcal{B}$ in the gauge field ansatz.
- The apparent horizon is fixed at $z_h=1$ during evolution.
- The entanglement entropy calculation uses strip widths of $\ell=0.8$, with cutoff dependence on the order of $10^{-2}$ for $z_{UV}\in[0.05,0.1]$.
- The time step for evolution is of order $dt\approx 1/(4N^2)$ where $N=24$ is the number of grid points per sub-domain.
- The total charge density grows without bound as $\braket{J^0}=11(p_0+Et)\mathcal{B}\gamma/3+4\rho$, where $\gamma$ is the dimensionless Chern-Simons coupling.

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of strongly coupled plasmas with magnetic fields by Fuini and Yarom (2015) and Cartwright (2019) to include the Chern-Simons term, which provides an axial anomaly in the dual field theory. It builds on the characteristic formulation techniques developed by Chesler and Yaffe (2008, 2010) and others for studying far-from-equilibrium dynamics. The results enable future studies of anomalous transport in more realistic setups that include momentum relaxation (e.g., via massive gravity or Q-lattice models) to avoid the unbounded growth of currents seen in this translationally invariant system.
