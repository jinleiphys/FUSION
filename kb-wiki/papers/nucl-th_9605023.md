# Dynamical Formation of Disoriented Chiral Condensates

> We study the dynamical formation of disoriented chiral condensates in very high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions using Bjorken hydrodynamics and relativistic nucleation theory. It is the dynamics of the first order confinement phase transition which controls the evolution of the system. Every bubble or fluctuation of the new, hadronic, phase obtains its own chiral condensate with a probability determined by the Boltzmann weight of the finite temperature effective potential of the linear sigma model. We evaluate domain size and chiral angle distributions, which can be used as initial conditions for the solution of semiclassical field equations.

## Key claim
The paper argues that disoriented chiral condensates (DCC) can form dynamically via a first-order confinement phase transition through homogeneous nucleation of hadronic bubbles, rather than through the second-order or near-second-order transitions assumed in prior work. The authors qualify that the chiral contribution to the free energy is treated perturbatively and that the resulting domain size and angle distributions depend on whether the chiral condensate relaxes rapidly (overdamped) or slowly (underdamped) within each bubble.

## Method
The authors model the expansion of quark-gluon plasma using Bjorken longitudinal scaling hydrodynamics and describe the nucleation of hadronic bubbles via homogeneous nucleation theory, with the nucleation rate determined by the free energy difference between phases and the surface tension. They compute the time evolution of temperature and the fraction of space converted to hadronic phase, then assign each nucleated bubble a chiral condensate orientation using the Boltzmann weight of the finite-temperature effective potential from the linear sigma model. Two parameter sets are considered: set A (u,d quarks; hadrons include pi, eta, omega, rho) and set B (u,d,s quarks; all Particle Data Book states with repulsive interactions), both with T_c = 160 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature T_c = 160 MeV for both parameter sets.
- Bag constant B^{1/4} = 220 MeV for set A, 232 MeV for set B.
- Minimum final bubble volume V_min = 22 fm^3 for set A, 10 fm^3 for set B.
- Supercooling reaches about 0.80 T_c (approximately 128 MeV) before reheating begins.
- Chiral critical temperature T_ch = 130.9 MeV from the linear sigma model parameters (f_pi = 94.5 MeV, m_pi = 140 MeV, m_sigma = 1 GeV).
- Chiral condensate v_0(T) at T = 160 MeV is about 7.9 MeV.
- Saddle point temperature T_saddle = 114.9 MeV for physical pion mass.
- Bubble eccentricity along beam direction is about t_f/t_sc ≈ 3–4.
- The distribution dn/dV at large volumes is fitted to exp[-a (V/V_min)^(2/3)] with coefficient a given in Table 1 (values not explicitly stated in text but agreement with analytic estimate to better than a factor of 2).

## Context
This work extends the study of disoriented chiral condensates beyond the near-second-order phase transition scenarios of Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993) and the quenching and annealing approaches of subsequent authors, which produced domain sizes too small for experimental observation. By assuming a first-order confinement phase transition with supercooling and homogeneous nucleation, the authors obtain domain sizes of order the critical bubble radius R_*, which can be significantly larger than 1/T_c. The resulting domain size and chiral angle distributions can serve as initial conditions for semiclassical field equations, enabling future studies of DCC evolution and observables in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
