# Implications from GW170817 for $Δ$-isobar admixed hypernuclear compact stars

> The effects of $Δ$ isobars on the equation of state of dense matter and structure of compact stars (CSs) are explored within the covariant density functional theory and confronted with the data on tidal deformability (TD) extracted from the GW170817 event. We show that the presence of $Δ$ isobars substantially softens the tension between the predictions of the hypernuclear density functionals and the inference from the observations of relatively small radius and small TD of canonical mass CSs. The TDs deduced from GW170817 are compatible with the existence of hypernuclear CSs containing an admixture of $Δ$ isobars. We thus argue that the GW170817 event is consistent with a merger of a binary CS system having both strangeness (hyperons) and $Δ$ isobars in the stellar core.

# Implications from GW170817 for Δ-isobar admixed hypernuclear compact stars

## Key claim

The presence of Δ isobars in hypernuclear compact stars substantially softens the tension between hypernuclear density functional predictions (which give large tidal deformabilities) and the GW170817 observational inference of relatively small radii and small tidal deformabilities for canonical-mass stars. The authors argue that GW170817 is consistent with a merger of binary compact stars containing both strangeness (hyperons) and Δ isobars in the stellar core, but note that an alternative explanation involving a strong phase transition to quark matter also remains viable.

## Method

The authors use covariant density functional theory with density-dependent meson-baryon couplings to construct equations of state for hypernuclear matter with an admixture of Δ isobars. They vary the Δ-isobar potential in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density from V_Δ/V_N = 1 to 5/3, and explore different values of the nuclear characteristic parameters Q_sat (isoscalar skewness, 300-800 MeV) and L_sym (isovector slope, 40-60 MeV). They then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations to compute mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities, and compare the predicted mass-weighted tidal deformabilities with the constraints from the GW170817 event.

## Key numbers

- For purely hyperonic models, the dimensionless tidal deformability of a canonical 1.4 M_⊙ star is Λ_{1.4} > 600, with radii R_{1.4} ≳ 13 km
- For hyperon-Δ admixed models with V_Δ/V_N = 5/3, the radius of a 1.4 M_⊙ star is reduced by about 1 km compared to its purely hyperonic counterpart, and the dimensionless tidal deformability Λ_{1.4} is reduced by about 300
- For the hyperon-Δ admixed model with (Q_sat, L_sym) = (480, 40) MeV and V_Δ/V_N = 5/3, Λ_{1.4} ranges from 575 to 348; for (480, 60) MeV, Λ_{1.4} ranges from 701 to 426
- The maximum masses for hyperon-Δ admixed models range from 1.98 to 2.10 M_⊙ across the four parameter sets studied
- The mass-weighted tidal deformability lower bound from Δ-isobar featuring models is about 350
- The chirp mass of GW170817 is assumed to be M = 1.186 M_⊙, with the binary mass ratio q ≳ 0.73 at 90% confidence

## Context

This work extends prior studies of hypernuclear compact stars using covariant density functional theory (Li 2018a, Li 2018b) and addresses the long-standing tension between hypernuclear models and the existence of 2 M_⊙ stars. Previous purely hyperonic models (Katayama 2015, Fortin 2016, Tolos 2016) predicted radii R_{1.4} ≳ 13 km and Λ_{1.4} > 600, which are challenged by the GW170817 constraints. The authors show that including Δ isobars, as previously explored by Drago (2014) and Li (2018b), resolves this tension. The work enables future gravitational wave observations to further constrain the nuclear characteristic parameters Q_sat and L_sym, with smaller chirp mass events narrowing L_sym and larger chirp mass events constraining Q_sat.
