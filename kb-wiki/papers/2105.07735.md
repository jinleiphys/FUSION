# The drip lines of kaonic nuclei

> The effects of an additional $K^-$ meson on the neutron and proton drip lines are investigated within Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach combined with a Skyrme-type kaon-nucleon interaction. While an extension of the proton drip line is observed due to the strongly attractive $K^-p$ interaction, contrasting effects (extension and reduction) on the neutron drip line of Be, O, and Ne isotopes are found. The origin of these differences is attributed to the behavior of the highest-occupied neutron single-particle levels near the neutron drip line.

## Key claim

The paper claims that adding a single $K^-$ meson to a nucleus can either extend, reduce, or leave unchanged the neutron drip line depending on the specific highest-occupied neutron single-particle level, while it always extends the proton drip line; this contrasts with the abstract's simpler statement of "contrasting effects" by specifying that the outcome depends on whether the valence neutron orbit is a spin-orbit-split upper level (reduction) or a lower level or non-split orbit (extension or no shift).

## Method

The authors used a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) approach with the SLy4 nucleonic force and a Skyrme-type kaon-nucleon interaction with a density-dependent linear energy density functional, parameterized by a strength $a_0 = 500$ MeV fm$^3$ and a symmetry parameter $x_0 = 0$ (symmetric $K^-p$ and $K^-n$ interaction). They solved the SHF equations self-consistently in an axially-deformed harmonic-oscillator basis for Be, O, and Ne isotopes and their corresponding $K^-$ nuclei, including a density-dependent delta pairing force with strength $V_0 = 410$ MeV fm$^3$ and a smooth energy cutoff in BCS calculations.

## Key numbers

- The neutron drip line for normal Be isotopes is at $^{12}$Be ($N=8$); adding a $K^-$ does not shift this point, but $^{13}_{K^-}$Be ($N=9$) becomes marginally bound (though $S_n$ remains negative).
- For O isotopes, the neutron drip line shifts from $^{24}$O ($N=16$) in normal nuclei to $^{24}_{K^-}$O ($N=16$) with a reduction: $^{25}_{K^-}$O becomes unbound.
- For Ne isotopes, the theoretical neutron drip line remains at $^{30}$Ne ($N=20$) with or without the $K^-$; experimentally it is at $^{32}$Ne ($N=22$).
- The proton drip line for Be isotopes extends from $^6$Be ($N=2$) to $^{\ \ 5}_{K^-}$Be ($N=1$), though $S_p$ remains negative.
- The kaon-nucleon interaction strength used is $a_0 = 500$ MeV fm$^3$; for Ne isotopes, a weak $K^-N$ strength ($a_0 = 100, 200$ MeV fm$^3$) increases binding of the $1f_{7/2}$ level, while repulsion sets in for $a_0 \gtrsim 300$ MeV fm$^3$.
- The central potential $V_n(r)$ and spin-orbit potential $W_n(r)$ of $^{12}_{K^-}$Be are deeper than normal for $r \lesssim 2.9$ fm and $r \lesssim 3.0$ fm, respectively; for $^{28}_{K^-}$O, deeper for $r \lesssim 3.2$ fm and $r \lesssim 3.8$ fm.

## Context

This work provides the first investigation of drip lines in kaonic nuclei, extending prior studies of normal nuclear drip lines and of $\Lambda$ hypernuclei where neutron drip lines were examined (e.g., PhysRevC.78.054306, Samanta (2008), PhysRevC.92.044313). It builds on the authors' earlier SHF studies of kaonic nuclei (Zhou (2013), Jin (2019)) and contrasts with RMF results that found level inversion in light kaonic nuclei (Yang (2019)), which is not reproduced here. The results enable future mapping of the "strange nuclear chart" with strangeness as a third dimension and call for improved dynamical treatments including imaginary parts of the kaon optical potential, guided by recent J-PARC data (Ichikawa (2020)).
