# More on Odd-J Pairing in Nuclei

> We point out a simplicity that arises when we use an interaction in which only an energy with odd J is non-zero. The emphasis is on J=J_{\text{max}} and in particular J=9^{+} in the g_{9/2} shell. It is noted that high overlaps can be deceptive. In many cases a single set of unitary 9-j coefficients gives either an exact or a surprisingly good approximation to the wave function of a non-degenerate state. The many degeneracies that occur in these calculations are discussed and explained. As a counterpoint, we compare the results with an interaction in which both the J=0 and J=J_{\text{max}} two-body matrix elements are equal (and attractive). Comparisons with a more realistic interaction are also made.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for a simple interaction where only a single odd-\(J\) two-body matrix element (specifically \(J=J_{\text{max}}=9\) in the \(g_{9/2}\) shell) is non-zero, the eigenfunction components of the lowest \(I=0^{+}\) state are exactly proportional to a single set of unitary 9-\(j\) coefficients; however, despite a high overlap (0.9451) between the E(9) interaction and a realistic interaction for the \(I=0^{+}\) ground state, the full even-\(I\) spectrum of E(9) is drastically different from the realistic one, with the ground state becoming \(I=16^{+}\) rather than \(I=0^{+}\).

## Method

The authors perform shell-model calculations for two protons and two neutrons in a single \(j\)-shell (the \(g_{9/2}\) shell, corresponding to \(^{96}\)Cd or \(^{44}\)Ti). They consider schematic interactions where only specific two-body matrix elements are non-zero and attractive (set to \(-2.0000\) MeV): E(9) with only \(J=9^{+}\); E(0) with only \(J=0^{+}\); E(0,9) with both \(J=0^{+}\) and \(J=9^{+}\); and E(1) with only \(J=1^{+}\). They diagonalize the four-particle Hamiltonian in a basis of \(|[pp(J_p)nn(J_n)]^I\rangle\) states and compare the resulting wave functions and spectra to those from a realistic interaction (CCGI). They also analyze the separability of the Hamiltonian matrix and the role of unitary 9-\(j\) symbols in constructing exact or approximate eigenfunctions.

## Key numbers

- The E(9) interaction gives an overlap of 0.9451 with the realistic CCGI interaction for the \(I=0^{+}\) ground state of \(^{96}\)Cd.
- The E(0,9) interaction (both \(J=0\) and \(J=9\) set to \(-2.0000\) MeV) gives an overlap of 0.9944 with CCGI for the \(I=0^{+}\) state.
- For the E(9) interaction, the energies of the \(I=0^{+},2^{+},4^{+},6^{+},8^{+}\) states are all within 0.002 MeV of each other (near 1.0588 MeV), while the \(I=16^{+}\) state lies at 0.0000 MeV (the ground state).
- The overlap between the E(9) eigenstate and the normalized \(U9\)-\(j\) coefficients for the lowest \(I=2^{+}\) state is 0.99996; for \(I=8^{+}\) the overlap is 0.9998.
- The value of the \(U9\)-\(j\) symbol coupling \(J_x=9\) and \(J_x=7\) for \(I=2\) in the \(g_{9/2}\) shell is \(-0.0001822\), giving an overlap of 0.0002577 between the two \(U9\)-\(j\) sets.
- For the \(I=0^{+}\) state with the E(9) interaction, there is one non-degenerate state at energy \(2V(9)\) and four degenerate states at zero energy; for \(I=1^{+}\) there is one non-degenerate state at \(V(9)\) and three at zero energy.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Moya de Guerra et al., 2003) on odd-\(J\) pairing interactions in a single-\(j\) shell, where they showed that the Hamiltonian matrix for \(I=0^{+}\) states is separable. It directly tests the ansatz of Qi et al. (2011) that overlap factors expressed as 9-\(j\) symbols form the components of eigenfunctions for \(J_{\text{max}}\) pairing. The paper also compares with the realistic CCGI interaction (derived from experiment) and with the work of Zhao and Arima (2005) on \(J\)-pairing interactions. The results enable further study of schematic interactions as counterpoints to the well-studied \(J=0\) pairing interaction, and they highlight the need for caution when interpreting high wave-function overlaps as indicators of realistic spectra.
