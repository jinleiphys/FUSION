# Effects of isospin mixing in the A=32 quintet

> For the A=32 T=2 quintet we provide a unified theoretical description for three related aspects of isospin mixing: the necessity of more than three terms in the isobaric mass multiplet equation, isospin-forbidden proton decay, and a correction to the allowed Fermi beta decay. We demonstrate for the first time that all three effects observed in experiment can be traced to a common origin related to isospin mixing of the T=2 states with T=1 states.

## Key claim

For the first time, all three observed isospin-mixing effects in the A=32 T=2 quintet, the need for higher-order terms in the isobaric mass multiplet equation (IMME), isospin-forbidden proton decay in 32Cl, and a correction to the allowed Fermi beta decay of 32Ar, can be traced to a common origin: isospin mixing of the T=2 states with T=1 states, with the specific magnitude depending on the proximity of the mixing levels and the choice of shell-model interaction.

## Method

Shell-model calculations were performed in the sd model space using the USD, USDA, and USDB interactions with the OXBASH code, in a proton-neutron formalism. Charge-independence-breaking (CIB) and charge-symmetry-breaking (CSB) interactions from Ormand and Brown were added to the Hamiltonians, with strengths fitted to experimental b and c coefficients of the T=1 IMME. Energy levels, spectroscopic factors, isospin-mixing matrix elements, and one-body transition densities were computed for all 0+ states in the A=32 quintet, and the shifts of the T=2 states due to mixing with T=1 and T=0 states were evaluated via second-order perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- The experimental mass excesses of the T=2 states in the A=32 quintet: 32Si at -24077.68(30) keV, 32P at -19232.58(12) keV, 32S at -13967.57(28) keV, 32Cl at -8288.34(70) keV, and 32Ar at -2200.2(18) keV.
- A d coefficient (cubic term in Tz) of 0.93(12) is required when using the direct 32Si mass, or 0.53(11) with the indirect 32Si mass, to fit the experimental IMME; the USDB interaction gives d = 0.28 keV.
- The calculated isospin-forbidden proton decay width from the T=2 state in 32Cl is 41.4 eV with USDB, compared to the experimental value of 20(5) eV; the calculated gamma decay width is 1.11 eV versus 1.8(5) eV experimentally.
- The isospin-breaking correction δC for the superallowed Fermi beta decay of 32Ar is 0.43(20)% from the average of the three interactions, which when combined with a radial overlap component of 1.4% gives a total of 1.8%, agreeing with experimental values of 2.1(8)% and 1.8(8)%.

## Context

This work provides a unified theoretical description for three previously separate isospin-mixing effects in the A=32 quintet, extending the IMME analysis of JYFL (2009) and Trimble (2009) and the isospin-forbidden proton decay measurement of Bhatt (2009). The calculations use the shell-model framework of Ormand and Brown (1989) for the isospin-nonconserving interaction and the USD family of interactions (Wildenthal 1984, Brown and Richter 2006). The results enable a consistent extraction of the weak mixing angle vud from superallowed Fermi decays by providing a theoretically grounded δC correction, and suggest that similar isospin-mixing effects may be observable in other multiplets where small energy differences and non-negligible mixing matrix elements occur.
