# Isovector pair correlations in analytically solvable models

> The eigensolutions of the collective Hamiltonian with different potentials suggested for description of the isovector pair correlations are obtained, analyzed and compared with the experimental energies. It is shown that the isovector pair correlations in nuclei around $^{56}$Ni can be described as anharmonic pairing vibrations. The results obtained indicate the presence of the $α$-particle type correlations in these nuclei and the existence of the interaction different from isovector pairing which also influences on the isospin dependence of the energies.

# Isovector pair correlations in analytically solvable models

## Key claim

The isovector pair correlations in nuclei around $^{56}$Ni can be described as anharmonic pairing vibrations rather than static pair correlations, but the model systematically underestimates binding energies for $T=0$ nuclei (those with equal proton and neutron numbers) and produces a larger moment of inertia for isospin rotations than observed experimentally, indicating that an interaction beyond isovector pairing also influences the isospin dependence of the energies.

## Method

The authors solve the collective Hamiltonian for isovector monopole pairing, which contains six collective variables (addition and removal modes each with three isospin projections), by separating the variables $\Delta$ (pairing strength) and $\theta$ (isospin structure angle). They consider four analytically solvable potentials for the $\Delta$-dependence: harmonic oscillator, static limit (deep minimum at $\Delta_0 \neq 0$), infinite square well, and Davidson potential; for the $\theta$-dependence they consider either a deep minimum at $\theta=0$ or a $\theta$-independent potential. The calculated ground-state energies of nuclei around $^{56}$Ni are compared with experimental binding energies after subtracting a liquid-drop background without symmetry energy or pairing terms.

## Key numbers

- Liquid-drop parameters used: $a_v = 15.75$ MeV, $a_s = 17.8$ MeV, $a_c = 0.711$ MeV
- Davidson potential calculations use parameter $\rho_0 = 1$ (dimensionless, defined as $(BC)^{1/4}\Delta_0$)
- For the harmonic oscillator potential, the energy eigenvalues are $E = \sqrt{C/B}(2n + \lambda + 3)$
- For the static (rotational) limit, excitation energies are $E_{NT} = [T(T+1) + N^2] / (2B\Delta_0^2)$
- For the square well potential, energies are $E = x_{nTN}^2 / (2B\Delta_l^2)$ where $x_{nTN}$ are zeros of Bessel functions
- For Davidson potential with $\theta$-independent case, $E_{\lambda,n} = \sqrt{C/B}(2n + \sqrt{(\lambda+2)^2 + \rho_0^4} + 1)$
- In the rotational limit ($\rho_0 \rightarrow \infty$), energies follow $E(N,T) = \frac{1}{3}(T(T+1) + N^2)$

## Context

This work extends the collective Hamiltonian approach for isovector pairing developed by Dussel et al. and Jolos et al., which was constructed by analogy with the Bohr collective Hamiltonian for quadrupole motion. The authors apply the concept of critical symmetries from Iachello (for quadrupole excitations) to the pairing case, considering harmonic oscillator, square well, and Davidson potentials as analytically solvable limits. The results contradict the static pairing rotational limit and instead support an anharmonic vibrational picture for nuclei around $^{56}$Ni. The observed staggering in experimental energies for odd-$T$ states (not reproduced by the model) and the systematic deviation for $T=0$ $\alpha$-particle-like nuclei point to the need for including $\alpha$-particle type correlations (as discussed by Sandulescu and collaborators) and an interaction beyond isovector pairing, which the authors plan to address in a following paper.
