# Asymptotics of three-body bound state radial wave functions of halo nuclei involving two charged particles

> Asymptotic expressions for the radial and full wave functions of a three{body bound halo nuclear system with two charged particles in relative coordinates are obtained in explicit form, when the relative distance between two particles tends to infinity. The obtained asymptotic forms are applied to the analysis of the asymptotic behavior of the three-body (pn?) wave functions for the halo ($E^*=3.562$ MeV, $J^π=0^+$, $T=1$) state of $^6$Li derived by D. Baye within the Lagrange-mesh method for two forms of the $αN$ -potential. The agreement between the calculated wave function and the asymptotic formula is excellent for distances up to 30 fm. Information about the values of the three-body asymptotic normalization functions is extracted. It is shown that the extracted values of the three-body asymptotic normalization function are sensitive to the form of the $αN$ -potential. The mirror symmetry is revealed for the three-body asymptotic normalization functions derived for the isobaric ($^6$He, $^6$Li) pair.

## Key claim

The paper derives explicit asymptotic expressions for three-body bound state radial wave functions of halo nuclei containing two charged particles, valid when the relative distance between two particles tends to infinity. The author shows that the extracted three-body asymptotic normalization functions (TBANFs) for the halo state of \(^6\)Li are sensitive to the form of the \(\alpha N\) potential, and reveals mirror symmetry between the TBANFs for the isobaric (\(^6\)He, \(^6\)Li) pair.

## Method

The author derived the proper three-body asymptotic form of the radial wave function \(\Psi_{\nu}(r_{23}, r_{13})\) for a three-body bound system with two charged particles by extracting the contribution from the three-body branch point singularity in the momentum-space wave function, using a saddle-point method for the integrals over the relative momenta. The asymptotic expression was then compared with the approximate three-body (\(pn\alpha\)) radial wave functions for the halo (\(E^* = 3.562\) MeV, \(J^\pi = 0^+\), \(T = 1\)) state of \(^6\)Li, which were obtained by D. Baye using the Lagrange-mesh method with two forms of the \(\alpha N\) potential (denoted M1 and M2 models). The TBANFs \(C^{(j)}_{Ll}(r)\) were extracted by fitting the asymptotic formula to the numerical wave functions over a range of distances.

## Key numbers

- The binding energy of the \(^6\)Li halo state relative to the \(pn\alpha\) channel is \(\varepsilon_a = 3.562\) MeV (the excitation energy of the state).
- The agreement between the calculated wave function and the asymptotic formula is excellent for distances up to 30 fm.
- For the dominant partial wave (\(L=0, l=0\)) at ratio \(r = r_{13}/r_{23} = 1.1\), the asymptotic expression and the numerical wave function agree within about 10% for \(r_{23}\) from about 14 to 24 fm for the M1 model and from about 14 to 27 fm for the M2 model.
- For the (\(L=0, l=0\)) partial wave at \(r = 2.5\), the fit is excellent from about 6 to 11.5 fm for the M1 model and from about 6 to 12.5 fm for the M2 model.
- For the (\(L=0, l=1\)) partial wave at \(r = 1.1\), an accuracy of about 10% is obtained up to \(r_{23}\) from about 16 to 23 fm for the M1 model and from about 16 to 24 fm for the M2 model.
- For the (\(L=1, l=2\)) component at \(r = 1.3\), the quality of agreement is reached from about 17 to 24 fm for the M1 model and from about 17 to 29 fm for the M2 model.
- The ratio \(C^{(2)}_{Ll}/C^{(1)}_{Ll}\) is very stable across different fitting points.
- The uncertainty for the ratio \(R^{(j)}_{Ll} = C^{(j)}_{Ll}(^6\text{Li}^*)/C^{(j)}_{Ll}(^6\text{He})\) is about \(\pm 14\%\).

## Context

This work extends the asymptotic analysis of three-body bound state wave functions previously developed for systems with two neutrons (short-range nuclear interactions) by Blokhintsev et al. (1999) and Yarmukhamedov (2009) to systems involving two charged particles, where Coulomb interactions introduce a power branch point singularity. The asymptotic expressions are applied to the \(^6\)Li halo state, which is the isobaric analog of the \(^6\)He ground state studied earlier by Yarmukhamedov (2002) using the Lagrange-mesh technique. The results enable systematic extraction of TBANFs for different halo nuclei, which carry information about two-particle interactions and can be used to test and constrain nuclear potentials.
