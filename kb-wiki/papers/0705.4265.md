# Retrieving Nuclear Information from Protons Propagating through A Thick Target

> The multiple scattering of high-energy particles in a thick target is fromulated in an impact parameter representation. A formalism similar but not identical to that of Moliere is obtained. We show that calculations of particle beam broadening due to multiple Coulomb scattering alone can be given in closed form. The focus of this study is on whether or not the broadening of the Coulomb angular distribution prevents the retrieval of nuclear-interaction information from mesauring the angular distributions of charged partiles scattered from a thick target. For this purpose, we study multiple scatterings with both the nuclear and Coulomb interactions included and we do not make a small-angle expansion. Condition for retrieving nuclear infomration from high-energy protons propagating through a block of material are obtained.

## Key claim

The authors show that nuclear information (such as diffractive oscillations) can be retrieved from protons propagating through a thick target only under specific conditions, namely when the momentum transfer is significantly larger than the momentum transfer at the first nuclear diffraction minimum, and when the average number of collisions (nu) is below a critical value determined by the ratio of the Coulomb peak half-width to the first diffraction minimum.

## Method

The authors formulate multiple scattering of high-energy protons in an impact parameter representation, deriving a formalism similar to but not identical to Moliere theory, with an extra exponential term that ensures proper behavior for thin targets. They separate the single-scattering cross section into Coulomb and nuclear components, then exponentiate the profile functions to obtain multistep probability distributions for thick targets. Using semi-analytical models with closed-form profile functions, they study the broadening of angular distributions for both pure Coulomb scattering and combined Coulomb-nuclear scattering without making a small-angle expansion.

## Key numbers

* For a Gaussian single-scattering distribution, the mean-square width broadens by a factor nu/(1-exp(-nu)), where nu = T/Lambda is the average number of collisions; for large nu this factor is approximately nu.
* The model uses sigma_1^tot approximately 5.7, with sigma_1c^tot approximately 3.0 and sigma_1n^tot approximately 2.7 (exaggerated nuclear contribution).
* The critical value for observing nuclear signals is nu_crit approximately r^(-2), where r = q_(1/2)/q_(min1) is the ratio of the Coulomb peak half-width to the momentum transfer at the first nuclear diffraction minimum.
* For protons on a thin 208Pb target at approximately 20 GeV, the calculated cross sections show a narrow forward Coulomb peak and diffractive oscillations at larger angles.

## Context

This work extends the Moliere theory of multiple Coulomb scattering (Moliere, Bethe, Mott and Massey) to include nuclear interactions explicitly, addressing a gap in the literature where hadronic projectile multiple scattering had not received sufficient attention. The formalism enables determination of target parameters (Z, A, and nu) from experimental measurements of the multiple-scattering cross section, which would be valuable for radiographic studies using hadronic beams. The authors find that nuclear information is washed away at small momentum transfers even with exaggerated nuclear cross sections, but can survive at larger momentum transfers under the conditions they derive.
