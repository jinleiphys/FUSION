# Cluster structures, ellipsoidal shapes and nuclear molecules in light A=12-50 nuclei

> The transition from cluster structures to extremely elongated ellipsoidal shapes and nuclear molecules in light $A=12-50$ $(N \sim Z)$ nuclei has been studied within the framework of covariant density functional theory. Nodal structure of the occupied single-particle states plays a critical role in microscopic understanding of this transition. This is illustrated by the analysis of dominant types of single-particle density distributions and their evolution (from the bottom of nucleonic potential) with deformation and particle number. The microscopic mechanism of the transition from clustered structures to ellipsoidal shapes and nuclear molecules and between them is discussed.

# Cluster structures, ellipsoidal shapes and nuclear molecules in light A=12-50 nuclei

## Key claim
The transition from cluster structures to ellipsoidal shapes and nuclear molecules in light N≈Z nuclei (A=12-50) is microscopically governed by the nodal structure of occupied single-particle states, specifically the competition between [NN0]1/2-type orbitals (which favor α-clusterization) and [N,N-1,1]Ω-type orbitals (which favor ellipsoidal shapes or nuclear molecules). The abundance of the latter increases with particle number, explaining why cluster structures are frequent only in the lightest nuclei (A∼12) while ellipsoidal shapes and nuclear molecules dominate for A>20.

## Method
The study employs covariant density functional theory (CDFT), specifically the cranked relativistic mean field (CRMF) approach without pairing correlations, to calculate configurations in light N≈Z nuclei at high spin. ## Context
This work extends CDFT studies of cluster structures and extremely deformed shapes in light nuclei (A=12-50) previously reported by the author and collaborators (Ray and Abusara, 2016; Afanasjev and Abusara, 2018) and by other groups using Skyrme DFT (Ichikawa et al., 2011; Yoshida et al., 2014; Zao et al., 2015). It provides a microscopic mechanism for the transition between shape types that complements cluster model approaches (which are limited by initial assumptions about clusters) and earlier DFT descriptions. The results enable systematic predictions of which nuclei may exhibit nuclear molecules versus α-cluster chains, and identify specific particle-hole excitations (e.g., [202]5/2+[321]3/2 → [440]1/2+[550]1/2 in ⁴²Sc) that drive transitions from ellipsoidal shapes to molecular configurations.
