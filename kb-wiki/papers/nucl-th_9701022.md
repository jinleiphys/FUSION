# eta-meson photoproduction off protons and deuterons

> We present a unitary and gauge-invariant model with coupled channels, which provides a consistent description of pion photoproduction off nucleons in the E$_{0+}$ channel and eta-meson photoproduction off protons and deuterons. An effective field theory with hadrons and photons is constructed, which includes non-resonant Born terms as well as the S$_{11}$(1535) and S$_{11}$(1650) baryon resonances. Due to the coupling between the channels, the production of eta-mesons is strongly affected by the S$_{11}$(1650) although its direct coupling to the $η$N channel is negligible. The rho- and omega-meson exchange terms are important for achieving a consistent description of both pion- and photon-induced reactions.

# η-meson photoproduction off protons and deuterons

## Key claim

The authors present a unitary and gauge-invariant coupled-channels model that provides a consistent description of both pion and eta photoproduction, but the key hedge is that the S11(1650) resonance, despite having negligible direct coupling to the ηN channel, strongly affects eta production through intermediate πN states, and that ρ- and ω-meson exchange terms become essential only when this second resonance is included.

## Method

The hadronic part of the model is a K-matrix approach solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation for three coupled channels (πN, ηN, ζN), with parameters fixed exclusively by fitting elastic πN scattering data from the KA84 partial-wave analysis. The electromagnetic interaction is then coupled gauge-invariantly, introducing resonance photoexcitation via γNN* couplings, vector-meson exchange (ρ and ω) contributions, and non-resonant Born terms from a linear sigma-model. The model is applied to eta photoproduction off protons and, in impulse approximation, off deuterons using the Paris potential wave function.

## Key numbers

- Helicity amplitudes for S11(1535): A₁/₂ᵖ = 102 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/², A₁/₂ⁿ = –82 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/²
- Helicity amplitudes for S11(1650): A₁/₂ᵖ = 83 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/², A₁/₂ⁿ = –24 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/²
- Corresponding coupling constants: k^S_N*(1535) = 0.09, k^V_N*(1535) = 0.84; k^S_N*(1650) = 0.25, k^V_N*(1650) = 0.45
- Vector-meson coupling constants: f_ρNN = 2.52, f_ωNN = 3 × f_ρNN, κ_ρ = 6.6
- Decay constants: λ_ωηγ = 0.329, λ_ρηγ = 1.02, λ_ωπγ = 0.32
- Effective scalar field ζ mass: m_ζ = 400 MeV
- At E_γ ≈ 850 MeV, the deuteron-to-proton cross section ratio is about 2, in agreement with experiment
- The ratio σ_n/σ_p is almost constant for E_γ ≤ 900 MeV and reaches unity at E_γ ≈ 950 MeV
- Near threshold, the model underestimates deuteron data by approximately 50%

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channels hadronic model of Deutsch-Sauermann, Friman, and Noerenberg (1997) for πN scattering and pion-induced eta production by adding electromagnetic interactions in a gauge-invariant way. It contradicts earlier one-resonance models such as Bennhold and Tanabe (1991) and Mukhopadhyay et al. (1995) by showing that the S11(1650) resonance and vector-meson exchange are essential for a consistent description. The model enables extraction of resonance helicity amplitudes testable against quark-model predictions, and provides a framework for interpreting eta photoproduction data from MAMI and ELSA, as well as deuteron measurements from Krusche et al. (1995).
