# Tritium $β$-decay in pionless effective field theory

> We calculate the $β$-decay of tritium at next-to-leading order in pionless effective field theory. At this order, a low-energy parameter $L_{1, A}$ enters the calculation that is also relevant for a high-accuracy prediction of the solar proton-proton fusion rate. We use the tritium half-life to determine this parameter and provide uncertainty estimates. We show proper renormalization of our calculation analyzing the residual cutoff dependence of observables. We find that next-to-leading order corrections contribute about $4\%$ to the triton decay Gamow-Teller strength. We show that these conclusions are insensitive to different arrangements of the effective range expansion.

# Tritium β-decay in pionless effective field theory

## Key claim

The paper calculates tritium β-decay at next-to-leading order in pionless effective field theory and finds that NLO corrections contribute about 4% to the Gamow-Teller strength, but this result depends on determining the low-energy constant \(L_{1,A}\) (equivalently \(l_{1,A}\)) from the experimental triton half-life, and the conclusions are shown to be insensitive to different arrangements of the effective range expansion.

## Method

The authors use pionless effective field theory with dibaryon fields to compute the three-nucleon bound-state wave functions for \(^3\)H and \(^3\)He by solving homogeneous Faddeev integral equations regularized with a momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\). They calculate the Fermi and Gamow-Teller reduced matrix elements for the β-decay transition, including one-body and two-body axial-vector currents up to NLO, with the Coulomb interaction treated perturbatively via one-photon exchange diagrams. The two-body axial counterterm \(L_{1,A}\) is then fixed by matching the calculated half-life to the experimental value \(fT_{1/2} = 1129.6 \pm 3\) s.

## Key numbers

- The experimental triton comparative half-life used: \(fT_{1/2} = 1129.6 \pm 3\) s
- NLO corrections contribute about 4% to the triton decay Gamow-Teller strength
- The deuteron asymptotic S-state normalization \(Z_d = 1.690(3)\) from Phillips (1999)
- The Fermi matrix element at LO with Coulomb (ERE parameterization): \(\langle \| \text{F} \| \rangle = 0.9971\)
- The Fermi matrix element at LO with Coulomb (Z parameterization): \(\langle \| \text{F} \| \rangle = 0.9985\)
- The Fermi matrix element from standard nuclear physics approach (Schiavilla et al., 1998): \(\langle \| \text{F} \| \rangle = 0.9993\)
- Two-nucleon input parameters: deuteron binding momentum \(\gamma_t = 45.701\) MeV, triplet effective range \(\rho_t = 1.765\) fm, singlet scattering length \(a_s = -23.714\) fm, singlet effective range \(\rho_s = 2.73\) fm, proton-proton scattering length \(a_p = -7.8063 \pm 0.0026\) fm, proton-proton effective range \(\rho_C = 2.794 \pm 0.014\) fm

## Context

This work extends previous pionless EFT calculations of three-nucleon systems (Konig et al., 2013-2015) and electroweak currents (Kong and Ravndal, 1996; Butler et al., 1999; Ando et al., 2005) by providing a consistent NLO calculation of tritium β-decay that properly renormalizes the two-body axial counterterm. The determination of \(l_{1,A}\) from tritium half-life is directly relevant for high-accuracy predictions of the solar proton-proton fusion rate, which has been a matter of discussion in the literature (Kong and Ravndal, 1996; Butler et al., 1999; Ando et al., 2005). The results enable future NLO calculations of other few-body electroweak processes within pionless EFT.
