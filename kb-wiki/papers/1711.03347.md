# Dynamical and many-body correlation effects in the kinetic energy spectra of isotopes produced in nuclear multifragmentation

> The properties of the kinetic energy spectra of light isotopes produced in the breakup of a nuclear source and during the deexcitation of its products are examined. The initial stage, at which the hot fragments are created, is modeled by the Statistical Multifragmentation Model, whereas the Weisskopf-Ewing evaporation treatment is adopted to describe the subsequent fragment deexcita- tion, as they follow their classical trajectories dictated by the Coulomb repulsion among them. The energy spectra obtained are compared to available experimental data. The influence of the fusion cross-section entering into the evaporation treatment is investigated and its influence on the qual- itative aspects of the energy spectra turns out to be small. Although these aspects can be fairly well described by the model, the underlying physics associated with the quantitative discrepancies remains to be understood.

## Key claim
The hybrid Monte Carlo model combining the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) with Weisskopf-Ewing evaporation and classical many-body Coulomb dynamics can reproduce the qualitative trend that proton-rich isotopes have higher kinetic energies than neutron-rich ones, but quantitative discrepancies remain for light and heavy isotope families.

## Method
The authors used a canonical version of the Statistical Multifragmentation Model to generate primary fragment partitions from a source with A0=168 and Z0=75 at breakup temperature T=5 MeV and flow energy per particle E_flow/A=0.5 MeV. Fragment momenta were assigned via a Boltzmann distribution, and the fragments were randomly placed inside a breakup volume V_bk = (1+χ)V0 with χ=2. The subsequent deexcitation of primary fragments was modeled using the Weisskopf-Ewing evaporation treatment, with decay widths calculated using fusion cross-sections parameterized by the Rowley-Hagino formula and a WKB approximation below the Coulomb barrier. The fragments' classical trajectories under mutual Coulomb repulsion were integrated using the Runge-Kutta-Cash-Karp method until the total Coulomb energy fell below 0.1 MeV and all particle-unstable excited fragments had decayed.

## Key numbers
- Source parameters: A0=168, Z0=75 (corresponding to 75% of the 112Sn+112Sn system, allowing 25% pre-equilibrium emission)
- Breakup temperature: T=5 MeV
- Flow energy per particle: E_flow/A=0.5 MeV
- Breakup volume parameter: χ=2 (giving V_bk = 3 V0)
- Coulomb energy threshold for stopping dynamics: 0.1 MeV
- The fusion cross-section parameterizations differed by up to a factor of 2 between models (e.g., SMM cross-section approximately two times larger than others for 3He+9Be collisions)
- The model underpredicted kinetic energies of light nuclei from He to B isotopes, with a crossover at Carbon isotopes where overprediction began

## Context
This work extends the earlier analysis by Souza et al. (2006) which compared the prompt breakup scenario (using the Improved Statistical Multifragmentation Model, ISMM) with the sequential emission Expanding Evaporating Source (EES) model of Friedman and Lynch (1983). The previous study favored the EES model because the ISMM could not reproduce the higher kinetic energies of proton-rich isotopes. The present hybrid model includes many-body correlations absent in the earlier ISMM treatment, where each fragment interacted with a static core. The improved treatment puts the SMM and EES scenarios on equal footing for this energy spectra analysis, though quantitative discrepancies remain that may require more realistic treatments such as Hauser-Feshbach calculations or inclusion of source and temperature distributions.
