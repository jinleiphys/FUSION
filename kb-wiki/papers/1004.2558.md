# Higher Moments of Net-Baryon Distribution as Probes of QCD Critical Point

> It is crucially important to find an observable which is independent on the acceptance and late collision process, in order to search for the possible Critical Point predicted by QCD. By utilizing A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model and Ultra Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model, we study the centrality and evolution time dependence of higher moments of net-baryon distribution in Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3$ GeV. The results suggest that Kurtosis and Skewness are less sensitive to the acceptance effect and late collision process. Thus, they should be good observables providing the information of the early stage of heavy ion collision. In addition, our study shows that the Kurtosis times $σ^{2}$ of net-proton distribution are quite different to that of net-baryon when collisions energy is lower than $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 20 GeV, the Monte Calor calculations on Kurtosis$\cdotσ^{2}$ have a deviation from the theoretical predictions.

## Key claim

Kurtosis and Skewness of the net-baryon distribution are less sensitive to acceptance effects and late-stage hadronic processes than lower-order moments, making them promising observables for probing the early-stage dynamics of heavy ion collisions and the QCD Critical Point, provided that the product Kurtosis·σ² of net-proton distribution is not used as a direct substitute for net-baryon at collision energies below √s_NN = 20 GeV.

## Method

The study used two Monte Carlo event generators: A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model (both default and string-melting versions) and the Ultra Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model. For Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 17.3 GeV, the authors computed the centrality dependence and evolution-time dependence of higher moments (Skewness, Kurtosis, and Kurtosis·σ²) of net-baryon, net-cBaryon (excluding neutrons), and net-proton distributions. They isolated different stages of the collision by varying the hadronic cascade termination time in AMPT (setting NTMAX values) and by analyzing separate scenarios: the partonic phase, the hadronization stage, resonance decay, and full hadronic rescattering.

## Key numbers

- Kurtosis of net-baryon distribution was studied at √s_NN = 17.3 GeV in Au+Au collisions, with transverse momentum windows of 0 < p_T < 0.4 GeV/c, 0.4 < p_T < 0.8 GeV/c, and 0.8 < p_T < 1.2 GeV/c; the Kurtosis value approached zero in central collisions, indicating a near-Gaussian distribution.
- In UrQMD model calculations at √s_NN = 17.3 GeV with impact parameter b = 2, rapidity |y| < 0.5, and 0 < p_T < 1 GeV/c, the Kurtosis of net-baryon, net-cBaryon, and net-proton distributions remained constant as a function of evolution time.
- In AMPT StringMelting at √s_NN = 17.3 GeV, comparing scenario (c) with NTMAX = 3 (termination time ~0.6 fm/c, no hadron rescattering) to scenario (d) with NTMAX = 150 (termination time 30 fm/c, full hadronic rescattering), the Kurtosis values and their centrality trends remained nearly unchanged.
- The product Kurtosis·σ² of net-proton distribution showed a strong deviation from that of net-baryon when collision energy was lower than √s_NN = 20 GeV, as calculated by AMPT StringMelting.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Zhou et al. (2009) on the rapidity and transverse momentum window dependence of higher moments, and it addresses the need for observables that are robust against experimental acceptance limitations and late-stage hadronic effects. The results support the use of Kurtosis and Skewness as probes for the QCD Critical Point, as proposed by theoretical work such as Stephanov (2009) and lattice QCD calculations. The finding that Kurtosis·σ² of net-proton differs from net-baryon at low energies contradicts the assumption that proton fluctuations directly reflect baryon fluctuations (as suggested by Hatta and Stephanov, 2003), and it calls for caution in experimental analyses at Beam Energy Scan energies. The study enables future experimental searches at RHIC BES and FAIR to focus on Kurtosis and Skewness as early-stage signatures.
