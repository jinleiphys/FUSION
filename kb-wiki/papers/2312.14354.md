# Heavy flavor transport and observables in heavy-ion collisions within the MARTINI+MUSIC framework

> We study the transport dynamics of charm quarks within an expanding quark-gluon plasma for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV. The analysis incorporates the hydrodynamical approach-MUSIC with fluctuating IP-Glasma initial state and Bayesian-quantified viscous coefficients. We study the interaction strength of charm quarks in the medium, including elastic collisional processes with medium constituents, gluon emission processes, and the impact of non-perturbative interactions on heavy quark transport. Further, we analyze the dynamics of heavy flavors using a hybrid framework that incorporates the MARTINI event generator, with PYTHIA8.1 for the initial production of heavy quarks, and Langevin dynamics to describe the evolution of heavy quarks.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a hybrid MARTINI+MUSIC framework, incorporating IP-Glasma fluctuating initial conditions and Bayesian-quantified viscous coefficients, can describe charm quark transport in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, but the results depend on the specific treatment of non-perturbative interactions and the choice of heavy quark diffusion coefficient.

## Method
The authors couple the MUSIC relativistic hydrodynamical model (with IP-Glasma initial conditions and Bayesian-calibrated shear and bulk viscosities) to the MARTINI event generator, which handles charm quark production via PYTHIA8.1 and their subsequent evolution through the quark-gluon plasma using Langevin dynamics. The framework incorporates elastic collisional processes with medium constituents, gluon emission via the higher-twist formalism, and non-perturbative interactions modeled through a T-matrix approach for the heavy quark transport coefficients.

## Key numbers
* Pb+Pb collisions at center-of-mass energy 2.76 TeV per nucleon pair are the system studied.

* Charm quark transport is modeled with Langevin dynamics that includes both elastic and radiative energy loss mechanisms.

## Context
This work extends the MARTINI framework (previously applied to light parton energy loss) to heavy flavor transport, building on prior hydrodynamical modeling by the MUSIC collaboration (Schenke et al., 2012) and Bayesian parameter estimation work (Bernhard et al., 2016). The inclusion of non-perturbative T-matrix interactions goes beyond the perturbative-only treatments used in earlier heavy quark studies such as those by Moore and Teaney (2005). The framework enables systematic comparison with experimental observables like the nuclear modification factor R_AA and elliptic flow v_2 of D mesons at LHC energies.
