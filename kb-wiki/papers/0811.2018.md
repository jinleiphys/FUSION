# Form Factors and Dyson-Schwinger Equations

> A synopsis exemplifying the employment of Dyson-Schwinger equations in the calculation and explanation of hadron electromagnetic form factors and related phenomena. In particular the contribution: presents the pion form factor computed simultaneously at spacelike and timelike momenta; reports aspects of the evolution of the nucleon and Delta masses with current-quark mass and the correlation of their mass difference with that between scalar and axial-vector diquarks; describes an estimate of the s-quark content of a dressed u-quark and its impact on the nucleon's strangeness magnetic moment; and comments upon the domain within which a pseudoscalar meson cloud can materially contribute to hadron form factors.

## Key claim
Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) provide a Poincaré covariant framework for calculating hadron electromagnetic form factors that simultaneously describes spacelike and timelike regions, but the rainbow-ladder truncation used for light-quark systems produces results that are uniformly about 35% too large in units of mass, requiring systematic corrections to achieve agreement with experiment.

## Method
The authors employ Dyson-Schwinger equations with a renormalisation-group-improved rainbow-ladder truncation of the gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations, using a single parameter ω fitted to reproduce the pion's leptonic decay constant. For baryons, a Poincaré covariant Faddeev equation is solved that retains only scalar (J^P=0^+) and axial-vector (J^P=1^+) diquark correlations in the colour-antitriplet channel, with binding effected by iterated exchange between the bystander and diquark-participant quarks. The strangeness content is estimated using a Nambu–Jona-Lasinio-like model with meson-loop corrections added to the rainbow gap equation, employing experimental values of leptonic decay constants and a mean-square momentum approximation.

## Key numbers
- Dressed-quark mass at infrared momenta is ~100 times larger than the current-quark mass
- Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking responsible for roughly 98% of a proton's mass
- Rainbow-ladder truncation results uniformly ~35% too large in units of mass for light-quark systems
- Dimensionless product r_π f_π = 0.31(1), constant independent of current-quark mass (experimental value: 0.315±0.005)
- At physical pion mass, nucleon mass M_N = 1.26(2) GeV from Faddeev equation (with current-quark mass m̂ = 6.1 MeV giving m_π = 0.138 GeV)
- Δ-N mass splitting M_Δ - M_N = 0.29 GeV; scalar-axial-vector diquark mass difference m_av - m_sc = 0.27(3) GeV at physical pion mass
- With m_u=0.005 GeV, m_s=0.12 GeV: 2% chance of finding an s-quark in a dressed u- or d-quark, 7% chance of finding u- or d-quark in a dressed s-quark
- Estimated strangeness magnetic moment μ_p^S ≈ -0.02 nuclear magnetons
- Pion loop contribution: dressed u-quark contains 7% π^+ and 4% π^0 (7% probability of finding dressed d-quark in dressed u-quark)
- With m_u=0, m_s=0.12 GeV and no meson loops: M_u = 0.56 GeV, M_s = 0.70 GeV, ⟨q̄q⟩ = (-0.29 GeV)^3
- With pion and kaon loops: M_u = 0.32 GeV, M_s = 0.63 GeV, ⟨q̄q⟩ = (-0.25 GeV)^3

## Context
This work extends the DSE approach to hadron structure developed by Roberts and collaborators (Roberts 1994, Roberts 2007) and builds upon the Poincaré covariant Faddeev equation formulated by Cahill (1988). The pion form factor calculation improves upon earlier work by Maris (2000) and is compared with lattice results from Brommel (2006) and an AdS/QCD model by Grigoryan (2008). The nucleon mass evolution is compared with lattice-QCD simulations by Ali Khan (2003) and Frigori (2007) and chiral extrapolations by Leinweber (2003). The strangeness estimate addresses questions raised by parity-violating electron-proton scattering experiments reviewed by Arrington (2006) and Beise (2004). The pseudoscalar meson cloud analysis enables future quantification of cloud effects and location of the transition from nonperturbative to perturbative QCD domains.
