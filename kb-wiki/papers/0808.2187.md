# Ab-initio shell model with a core

> We construct effective 2- and 3-body Hamiltonians for the p-shell by performing 12\hbarΩab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for A=6 and 7 nuclei and explicitly projecting the many-body Hamiltonians onto the 0\hbarΩspace. We then separate these effective Hamiltonians into 0-, 1- and 2-body contributions (also 3-body for A=7) and analyze the systematic behavior of these different parts as a function of the mass number A and size of the NCSM basis space. The role of effective 3- and higher-body interactions for A>6 is investigated and discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that effective two-body Hamiltonians for the p-shell can be constructed from ab-initio no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations by projecting many-body Hamiltonians onto the 0ℏΩ space, but that for nuclei with A≥7, the two-body valence cluster approximation fails to reproduce NCSM results, requiring explicit three-body effective interactions whose strength grows with mass number.

## Method

The authors performed NCSM calculations for A=6 and A=7 nuclei using the INOY (inside nonlocal outside Yukawa) interaction in basis spaces up to 12ℏΩ, then applied a unitary transformation to project the many-body Hamiltonians onto the 0ℏΩ (p-shell) space. They decomposed the resulting effective Hamiltonians into core (0-body), one-body, and two-body contributions using a valence cluster expansion, and tested the two-body valence cluster approximation for A=7, 8, 9, and 10 by comparing SSM calculations using the derived effective interactions against full NCSM results.

## Key numbers

- The converged binding energy of ⁶Li at Nmax=12 is 31.681 MeV, obtained with ℏΩ=14 MeV and the INOY interaction.
- The dimension of the configurational space for the Nmax=12 ⁶Li calculation is 48 million in M-scheme.
- The excitation energy of the highest lying p-space state (Jπ=0⁺₂) in ⁶Li is lowered by 2.1 MeV when going from Nmax=6 to Nmax=12.
- For A=7, the difference between NCSM and SSM ground state energies using the two-body valence cluster approximation shows a strong isospin dependence, with deviations reaching several MeV depending on Tz=(N-Z)/2.
- The three-body effective interaction required to compensate for A-independent core and one-body parts would need an unrealistic strength of about 10 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the original projection method of Navratil (1997) for constructing effective interactions in the p-shell, using significantly larger basis spaces (up to Nmax=12 versus previous Nmax=6 studies). The approach contrasts with earlier shell-model treatments of core polarization by Barrett (1970) and Vary (1973), which showed slow convergence with perturbative treatments. The results enable systematic construction of mass-dependent effective interactions for p-shell nuclei and quantify the importance of three-body effective forces for A≥7 systems, with implications for extending ab-initio methods to heavier nuclei.
