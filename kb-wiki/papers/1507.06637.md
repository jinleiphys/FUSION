# Diagonal and off-diagonal quark number susceptibilities at high temperatures

> We present continuum extrapolated lattice QCD results for up to fourth-order diagonal and off-diagonal quark number susceptibilities in the high temperature region of 300-700 MeV. Lattice QCD calculations are performed using 2+1 flavors of highly improved staggered quarks with nearly physical quark masses and at four different lattice spacings. Comparisons of our results with recent weak coupling perturbative calculations yield reasonably good agreements for the entire temperature range.

## Key claim

Continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD results for up to fourth-order diagonal and off-diagonal quark number susceptibilities at 300-700 MeV show reasonably good agreement with weak coupling perturbative calculations, but the three-loop hard thermal loop (HTL) calculations cannot simultaneously reproduce the lattice results for both second-order and fourth-order diagonal susceptibilities with a single renormalization scale.

## Method

Lattice QCD calculations were performed using 2+1 flavors of highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ) with nearly physical quark masses (pion mass 161 MeV in the continuum limit) on lattices with temporal extent Nτ = 6, 8, 10, and 12 and fixed aspect ratio Nσ/Nτ = 4. The quark number susceptibilities were evaluated using the random noise method with 150-250 random source vectors, and continuum extrapolations were performed using fits motivated by lattice free theory, with total errors estimated as twice the statistical error from three-parameter fits.

## Key numbers

- Fourth-order light quark susceptibility χ4u at 660 MeV: 0.5067(26) (normalized by ideal gas value 6/π2), showing 15% deviation from the ideal gas limit at the highest temperature studied
- Fourth-order strange quark susceptibility χ4s at 660 MeV: 0.5103(32) (normalized by 6/π2)
- Second-order off-diagonal susceptibility χ11ud at 340 MeV: -3.62(46) × 10-3
- Fourth-order off-diagonal susceptibility χ22us at 340 MeV: 0.0203(21)
- χ31ud = 0.00020 ± 0.00015, χ31us = 0.00071 ± 0.00030, χ13us = 0.00060 ± 0.00030 (constant fits over 280-710 MeV)
- Continuum result for χ2u at similar temperatures is only 6% below the ideal gas limit (Bazavov 2013)

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of second-order quark number susceptibilities (Bazavov 2013) to fourth-order diagonal and off-diagonal susceptibilities, providing stringent tests of weak coupling approaches at high temperatures. The results are compared with three-loop HTL perturbation theory (Haque 2014) and dimensionally reduced electrostatic QCD (EQCD) calculations (Mogliacci 2013; Hietanen 2008), finding that while individual observables can agree with specific scale choices, no single renormalization scale simultaneously reproduces both χ2q and χ4q in HTL. The agreement between lattice and weak coupling results suggests that for T > 300 MeV quark degrees of freedom are weakly coupled, and the continuum-extrapolated results enable further refinement of perturbative calculations. A similar study using a different fermionic discretization scheme appeared concurrently (Bellwied 2015).
