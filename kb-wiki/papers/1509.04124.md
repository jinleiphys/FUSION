# Two-particle correlations in pseudorapidity in a hydrodynamic model

> Two-particle pseudorapidity correlations of hadrons produced in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV at the CERN Large Hadron Collider are analyzed in the framework of a model based on viscous 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamics with the Glauber initial condition. Based on our results, we argue that the correlation from resonance decays, formed at a late stage of the evolution, produce significant effects. In particular, their contribution to the event averages of the coefficients of the expansion in the Legendre basis explain 60-70% of the experimental values. We have proposed an accurate way to compute these <a_n a_m> coefficients, independent of the binning in pseudorapidity, and tested a double expansion of the two-particle correlation function in the azimuth and pseudorapidity, which allows us to investigate the pseudorapidity correlations between harmonics of the collective flow. In our model, these quantities are also dominated by non-flow effects from the resonance decays. Finally, our method can be used to compute higher-order cumulants for the expansion in orthonormal polynomials which offers a suitable way of eliminating the non-flow effects from the correlation analyses.

## Key claim

The paper argues that resonance decays occurring at the late stage of the fireball evolution produce the dominant contribution (60-70% of the experimental values) to the two-particle pseudorapidity correlation coefficients measured by ATLAS, rather than initial-state fluctuations or collective flow effects. This claim is hedged by the caveat that the model does not include all possible correlation sources such as jets or local charge conservation.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic simulations of Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV using Glauber Monte Carlo initial conditions from GLISSANDO, with two scenarios for the longitudinal entropy profile (torque and no-torque). Hadron emission and resonance decays were simulated with THERMINATOR at a freeze-out temperature of 150 MeV, and the two-particle pseudorapidity correlation function was computed for charged particles with transverse momentum above 0.5 GeV and pseudorapidity within |eta| < 2.5. The correlation coefficients in the Legendre polynomial basis were calculated using a binning-independent method based on sums over individual hadrons in each event.

## Key numbers

- The resonance decay contribution to the event-averaged Legendre expansion coefficients <a_n a_m> explains 60-70% of the experimental values measured by ATLAS
- The freeze-out temperature used in the simulation was T_f = 150 MeV
- Charged particles were selected with p_T > 0.5 GeV and |eta| < 2.5 to match the ATLAS acceptance
- The correction to the normalization of the <a_n a_m> coefficients from the rescaling procedure was less than 2% for the centrality bins studied
- The simulation used 20000 events in each centrality class for the fourth-order cumulant calculations

## Context

This work extends the analysis framework of Bzdak and Teaney (2012) for expanding two-particle pseudorapidity correlations in orthogonal polynomials, and directly compares to the experimental measurements from the ATLAS Collaboration (2015). The results contradict the expectation that the observed correlation structure primarily reflects initial-state fluctuations in rapidity; instead, the dominant signal comes from late-stage resonance decays. The paper proposes a binning-independent method for computing correlation coefficients and introduces a double expansion in azimuth and pseudorapidity that can be used to study rapidity correlations between harmonic flow coefficients. The method enables future elimination of non-flow effects through higher-order cumulants as proposed by Bzdak (2015), though the authors note that experimental statistics are required for such analyses.
