# A Cloudy Bag Model for the S-D Wave $\Kbar-N$ System

> The cloudy quark bag model is applied to the coupled ($\Kbar N$, $Σπ$, $Λπ$) system for $S-D$ partial waves. Energy--dependent separable potentials are derived, as are new numerical algorithms for solving the coupled Lippmann-Schwinger equations. The parameters are fit to $K^-p$ scattering and reaction cross sections, branching ratios, and mass spectra from $K^-p\rightarrow Σπππ, Λπππ$. The $Σ_{P13}(1385)$ and $Λ_{D03}(1520)$ resonances are found to be predominately elementary bag states with considerable dressing for the $Σ_{P13}$. The $Λ_{S01}(1405)$ appears as a complicated composite systems arising from two poles.

# A Cloudy Bag Model for the S-D Wave \(\bar{K}-N\) System

## Key claim

The cloudy bag model, when extended to include \(S\), \(P\), and \(D\) partial waves in the coupled (\(\bar{K}N\), \(\Sigma\pi\), \(\Lambda\pi\)) system, finds that the \(\Lambda_{S01}(1405)\) resonance arises from two poles and is a complicated composite system, while the \(\Sigma_{P13}(1385)\) and \(\Lambda_{D03}(1520)\) are predominantly elementary bag states; however, within the constraints of the model, the branching ratio and \(\Sigma\pi\) mass spectrum data are in conflict, and the predicted two sign changes in the real part of the \(\bar{K}N\) scattering amplitude near threshold are not at the correct energies to match the kaonic hydrogen strong interaction shift.

## Method

The authors applied the cloudy bag model (CBM) with SU(3) × SU(3) chiral symmetry to the coupled (\(\bar{K}N\), \(\Sigma\pi\), \(\Lambda\pi\)) system for \(S\), \(P\), and \(D\) partial waves, deriving energy-dependent separable potentials from the CBM Lagrangian. They solved the coupled Lippmann-Schwinger equations using an extended Haftel-Tabakin technique with Gaussian elimination, and fit seven parameters (bag radius \(R\), coupling constants \(f^{I=0}\), \(f^{I=1}_{\bar{K}}\), \(f^{I=1}_{\pi}\), and bare masses \(M_{S01}\), \(M_{P13}\), \(M_{D03}\)) to \(K^-p\) scattering and reaction cross sections, branching ratios, and mass spectra from \(K^-p \rightarrow \Sigma\pi\pi\pi\) and \(\Lambda\pi\pi\pi\) up to 520 MeV/c kaon lab momentum.

## Key numbers

- The \(\bar{K}N\) threshold is at 1432 MeV center-of-mass energy; the coupled channels include thresholds at \(-5\) MeV (\(\bar{K}^0n\)), \(+95\) to \(+104\) MeV (\(\Sigma\pi\) channels), and \(+181\) MeV (\(\Lambda\pi^0\)) relative to the \(\bar{K}N\) threshold.
- The model was fit to 300 measurements of two-body scattering and reaction cross sections for kaon lab momenta \(70 \leq K_{lab} \leq 513\) MeV/c, corresponding to center-of-mass energies \(1435 \leq E_{cm} \leq 1567\) MeV.
- The \(\Sigma(1385)\) resonance parameters from the fit to the \(\Lambda\pi\) mass spectrum are mass \(M_0 = 1384\) MeV and width \(\Gamma_0 = 34.8\) MeV.
- The \(\Lambda_{D03}(1520)\) resonance appears as a peak near 390 MeV/c kaon lab momentum in the cross section data.
- The bag radius used in the model is approximately 1 fm for the MIT bag, while the "little brown bag" of Brown and Rho compresses to 0.3 fm.

## Context

This work extends the cloudy bag model calculations of Veit et al. (1985) for the \(\bar{K}N\) system from \(S\)-waves to include \(P\) and \(D\) partial waves, addressing the need to describe the \(P\)-wave \(\Sigma_{P13}(1385)\) and \(D\)-wave \(\Lambda_{D03}(1520)\) resonances. The model builds on the MIT bag model of Chodos et al. (1974) and the chiral symmetry restoration work of Brown and Rho (1979) and Jaffe (1979). The results enable further investigation of the puzzling experimental measurements of the 1S strong interaction level shift in kaonic hydrogen from Davies et al. (1979), Izycki et al. (1980), and Bird et al. (1983), and the nature of the \(\Lambda_{S01}(1405)\) as a two-pole composite system.
