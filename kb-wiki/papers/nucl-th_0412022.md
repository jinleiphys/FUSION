# Nonequilibrium Models of Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We review the results from the various hydrodynamical and transport models on the collective flow observables from AGS to RHIC energies. A critical discussion of the present status of the CERN experiments on hadron collective flow is given. We emphasize the importance of the flow excitation function from 1 to 50 A$\cdot$GeV: here the hydrodynamic model has predicted the collapse of the $v_1$-flow and of the $v_2$-flow at $\sim 10$ A$\cdot$GeV; at 40 A$\cdot$GeV it has been recently observed by the NA49 collaboration. Since hadronic rescattering models predict much larger flow than observed at this energy we interpret this observation as evidence for a first order phase transition at high baryon density $ρ_B$. Moreover, the connection of the elliptic flow $v_2$ to jet suppression is examined. It is proven experimentally that the collective flow is not faked by minijet fragmentation. Additionally, detailed transport studies show that the away-side jet suppression can only partially ($<$ 50%) be due to hadronic rescattering. Furthermore, the change in sign of $v_1, v_2$ closer to beam rapidity is related to the occurence of a high density first order phase transition in the RHIC data at 62.5, 130 and 200 A$\cdot$GeV.

# Nonequilibrium Models of Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The collapse of both directed flow ($v_1$) and elliptic flow ($v_2$) for protons observed by the NA49 collaboration at 40 A$\cdot$GeV in Pb+Pb collisions constitutes evidence for a first-order phase transition at high baryon density, but only if the hydrodynamic prediction of flow collapse at approximately 10 A$\cdot$GeV is taken as the baseline, the actual observed collapse occurs at a higher energy (40 A$\cdot$GeV) than the original hydrodynamic prediction of 8 A$\cdot$GeV, and hadronic rescattering models without a phase transition predict much larger flow than observed at this energy.

## Method

The authors review and compare results from ideal and viscous hydrodynamic models, three-fluid hydrodynamics, and microscopic hadron-string transport models (UrQMD and HSD) for collective flow observables (directed flow $v_1$ and elliptic flow $v_2$) across beam energies from AGS (approximately 11 A$\cdot$GeV) through SPS (40 and 158 A$\cdot$GeV) to RHIC (62.5, 130, and 200 A$\cdot$GeV). They also examine high-$p_T$ hadron suppression and jet angular correlations using HSD transport with PYTHIA for hard processes, comparing to PHENIX and STAR data.

## Key numbers

- Critical baryochemical potential from lattice QCD: $\mu_B^c \approx 400 \pm 50$ MeV, with critical temperature $T_c \approx 150-160$ MeV (Fodor 2004, Karsch 2004)
- Energy density at the critical point: approximately 1 GeV/fm$^3$ or slightly below
- Linear extrapolation of AGS data indicates collapse of directed proton flow at $E_{Lab} \approx 30$ A$\cdot$GeV
- At RHIC, baryon chemical potential at forward rapidity $y=3$: $\mu_B \simeq 130$ MeV (BRAHMS Collaboration)
- HSD model underestimates elliptic flow $v_2$ at low $p_T$ by approximately 30% at RHIC; at high $p_T > 2$ GeV/c, $v_2$ is underestimated by a factor of three
- Hadronic rescattering can account for up to 50% of the attenuation of high $p_T$ hadrons at RHIC; the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ from HSD reaches about 0.5 at 5 GeV/c for central collisions, while data show $R_{AA} \approx 0.25$
- For particles with $p_T \geq 4$ GeV/c, HSD predicts 1/3 of final hadrons have suffered one or more interactions, while 2/3 escape freely
- Away-side jet correlations in HSD show only approximately 50% reduction, while STAR data show complete disappearance of the away-side jet
- Proton yield at high $p_T \geq 5$ GeV/c is a factor 5-10 too small in the HSD approach

## Context

This review extends the long-standing program of using collective flow as a barometer for the equation of state, originating with the hydrodynamic flow and shock formation proposals by Hofmann (1974, 1976) and the identification of bounce-off, squeeze-out, and antiflow by Stocker (1979-1986). The observed flow collapse at 40 A$\cdot$GeV by NA49 provides the first experimental evidence for a first-order phase transition at high baryon density, consistent with lattice QCD predictions by Fodor (2004) and Karsch (2004) that a first-order transition occurs only at high baryochemical potential. The failure of hadronic transport models (UrQMD, HSD) to reproduce the elliptic flow at RHIC by factors of 3 or more indicates that effective partonic degrees of freedom are needed in the initial phase, enabling future studies of the QGP equation of state and transport coefficients at both the forward fragmentation region at RHIC and the future FAIR facility at GSI.
