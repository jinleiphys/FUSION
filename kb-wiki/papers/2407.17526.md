# On the sensitivity of nuclear clocks to new physics

> The recent demonstration of laser excitation of the $\approx 8$ eV isomeric state of Thorium-229 is a significant step towards a nuclear clock. The low excitation energy likely results from a cancellation between electromagnetic and strong contributions, which new physics can disrupt. In this Letter, we quantify the enhancement of a nuclear clock's sensitivity to new physics using a geometric model and a novel $d$-wave halo model of the nucleus that reproduces measured differences between Thorium-229 states. We find likely enhancements of order $10^4$ while a worst case scenario with enhancement $\ll 1$ is unlikely.

## Key claim

The paper quantifies the sensitivity enhancement of a thorium-229 nuclear clock to new physics, finding that likely enhancements are of order 10^4, while a worst-case scenario with enhancement much less than 1 is possible but requires fine-tuning of nuclear model parameters at the percent or per mille level.

## Method

The authors compute the electromagnetic energy shift between the ground state and isomeric state of Thorium-229 using two complementary nuclear models: a classical geometric model parameterized by charge radius, quadrupole moment, higher multipole moments, and skin thickness; and a quantum d-wave halo model describing Thorium-229 as a Thorium-228 core orbited by a halo neutron in an l=2 state. The enhancement factor K_em is defined as the ratio of the electromagnetic energy shift to the total transition energy, and the authors evaluate how likely it is that this factor is small.

## Key numbers

- The isomeric state energy difference is Delta E = 8.355733554021(8) eV (from Zhang 2024)
- In the geometric model, typical enhancement factors K are of order 10^4 to 10^5
- In the halo model, the spin-orbit contribution to the electromagnetic energy difference is approximately 144 p keV, where p is an order-one factor, corresponding to K approximately 10^4
- The halo model predicts the difference in mean squared charge radii from spin-orbit effects as Delta <r^2>_SO = 0.0047 fm^2, compared to the measured Delta <r^2> = 0.012(2) fm^2 (from Thielking 2017)
- The halo model predicts the quadrupole moment difference Q_SO^iso - Q_SO^gs = 0.185 fm^2, compared to the experimental value 0.173(9) fm^2 (from Zhang 2024)
- The measured intrinsic quadrupole moment is Q_0 = 9.8(1) fm^2 (from Thielking 2017)
- The mean squared charge radius is <r^2> = (5.7557(143) fm)^2 (from Safronova 2018 and Thielking 2017)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nuclear clock sensitivity by Flambaum (2006), Fadeev (2020), and Berengut (2009), who modeled the Thorium-229 nucleus using geometric approaches. The paper introduces a novel d-wave halo model inspired by halo Effective Field Theory developed by Braun (2018) that correctly predicts the near-degeneracy of the ground and isomeric states. The results enable quantitative assessment of how likely the worst-case scenario of no sensitivity enhancement is, and suggest future neutron capture experiments to constrain the asymptotic normalization constant and verify the halo model predictions.
