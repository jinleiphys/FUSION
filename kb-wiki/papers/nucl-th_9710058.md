# Properties of Pairing Correlations in Infinite Nuclear Matter

> We discuss various properties of singlet S pairing in neutron and nuclear matter in terms of the bare nuclon-nucleon interaction. Relations to the NN phase shifts are discussed, as well as various properties of the BCS wave function and the coherence length for fermion superfluids in infinite matter.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the quantitative features of $^1S_0$ pairing in neutron matter can be obtained directly from the $^1S_0$ phase shifts, because the nucleon-nucleon interaction is very nearly rank-one separable in this channel due to the presence of a nearly bound state at zero energy. This agreement is not likely to survive in a more refined calculation that includes density and spin-density fluctuations in the effective pairing interaction.

## Method

The authors solved the BCS gap equation for infinite neutron and nuclear matter using the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction in the $^1S_0$ channel, with free single-particle energies. They compared results from three modern potentials (CD-Bonn, Nijmegen I, Nijmegen II) with a calculation that reconstructed the interaction directly from experimental $^1S_0$ phase shifts using the inverse scattering solution for rank-one separable potentials, and also with the effective range approximation.

## Key numbers

- Maximum value of the $^1S_0$ energy gap in neutron matter varies from 2.98 MeV (Nijmegen I potential) to 3.05 MeV (Nijmegen II potential)
- The $^1S_0$ phase shift changes sign from positive to negative at laboratory energy $E_{\rm lab}\approx 248.5$ MeV, corresponding to momentum $k\approx 1.74$ fm$^{-1}$
- The $^1S_0$ potential is attractive for momenta $k \leq 1.74$ fm$^{-1}$ (or interparticle distances $r \geq 0.57$ fm)
- Singlet neutron-neutron scattering length $a_0=-18.8\pm 0.3$ fm and effective range $r_0=2.75\pm 0.11$ fm
- Below $k_F=0.5$ fm$^{-1}$, the energy gap can be calculated with reasonable accuracy using the effective range approximation

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the superfluid energy gap in dilute neutron matter (Clark et al., 1976; various authors 1993-1997) by explaining why all modern nucleon-nucleon potentials give nearly identical results for the $^1S_0$ gap in lowest-order BCS calculations. The paper shows that at densities below $k_F=0.5$ fm$^{-1}$, the superfluid properties are determined by just two parameters: the free-space scattering length and effective range. The results enable future work to incorporate density and spin-density fluctuations (as in recent work by other authors, 1997) and to extend calculations to other partial waves where the simple separable approximation will not apply.
