# Longitudinal Structure of Quark-Gluon Plasma Unveiled Through Nuclear Deformations

> The study of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is hindered by our limited understanding of its initial conditions, particularly its longitudinal structure. We propose a novel approach that entails analyzing collisions involving nuclei of similar masses but different deformations. This strategy allows us to vary the initial conditions and collective expansion of the QGP, while minimizing the influence of non-flow correlations. Using a dynamical transport model, we have for the first time extracted the complete longitudinal structure of elliptic flow ($v_2$). Our findings reveal that although deformation significantly enhances the overall magnitude of $v_2$, it does not alter its longitudinal profile. This approach not only enables the separation of the rapidity dependence of flow from its rapidity decorrelations but also prompts further investigation into other nuclear structural features, such as nuclear skin thickness, to advance our understanding of the QGP's initial conditions.

## Key claim

Using a transport model simulation, the authors demonstrate that comparing collisions of nuclei with similar masses but different deformations (specifically $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U and $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au) can isolate the deformation-induced component of elliptic flow and extract its full longitudinal structure, revealing that while deformation significantly enhances the overall magnitude of $v_2$, it does not alter its longitudinal profile.

## Method

The authors performed AMPT transport model simulations of $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=193$ GeV and $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=200$ GeV, using the string-melting mode with a partonic cross-section of 3 mb. They calculated the two-particle correlation elliptic flow $V_{2\Delta}(\eta_1,\eta_2)$ for each system and subtracted the spherical baseline and non-flow contributions by taking the difference between systems with different quadrupole deformation parameters $\beta$, obtaining the deformation-induced component $V_{2\Delta,\beta}$. The factorization ratio $R(\eta_1,\eta_2)$ was then computed from $V_{2\Delta,\beta}$ to isolate flow decorrelations from the rapidity dependence of flow.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation parameters used: $\beta=0.28$ for $^{238}$U, $\beta=0$ for spherical $^{238}$U, and $\beta=-0.14$ for $^{197}$Au
- Nuclear radii: $R_0=6.81$ fm for $^{238}$U, $R_0=6.62$ fm for $^{197}$Au; skin thickness: $a_0=0.55$ fm for $^{238}$U, $a_0=0.52$ fm for $^{197}$Au
- Transverse momentum range for particle selection: $0.2 < p_{\mathrm{T}} < 3$ GeV/$c$
- Pseudorapidity range for analysis: $|\eta| < 5$
- Deformation enhances $V_{2\Delta}$ by about a factor of two in 0--5% central U+U collisions
- The deformation-induced flow signal $v_{2,\varepsilon_{\mathrm{B},\beta}}$ is approximately 0.75 times the two-particle correlation flow $v_{2,\eta_{\mathrm{ref},\beta}}$ when reference particles are chosen from $\eta_{\mathrm{ref}}=4-5$

## Context

This work extends the concept of event-shape engineering (Schukraft, 2012) but circumvents its inherent selection bias by using a data-driven "event-shape design" approach comparing systems with different nuclear shapes. The method enables separation of the rapidity dependence of flow from rapidity decorrelations, correcting the common misconception (CMS Collaboration, 2015; ATLAS Collaboration, 2020) that the $r_2$ correlator measures decorrelation between $\eta$ and $-\eta$ independent of the reference rapidity choice. The approach can be applied to existing RHIC data on $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U and $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au collisions, as well as future comparisons of $^{129}$Xe+$^{129}$Xe and $^{208}$Pb+$^{208}$Pb at the LHC, and could be extended to probe other nuclear structural features such as nuclear skin thickness using $^{96}$Ru+$^{96}$Ru and $^{96}$Zr+$^{96}$Zr collisions.
