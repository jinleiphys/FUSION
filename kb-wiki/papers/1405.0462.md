# Short History of Nuclear Many-Body Problem

> This is a very short presentation regarding developments in the theory of nuclear many-body problems, as seen and experienced by the author during the past 60 years with particular emphasis on the contributions of Gerry Brown and his research-group. Much of his work was based on Brueckner's formulation of the nuclear many-body problem. It is reviewed briefly together with the Moszkowski-Scott separation method that was an important part of his early work. The core-polarisation and his work related to effective interactions in general are also addressed.

# Short History of Nuclear Many-Body Problem

## Key claim
This paper presents a personal historical overview of developments in nuclear many-body theory over 60 years, with the explicit hedge that it covers only a small fraction of Gerry Brown's work and that the convergence of higher-order Brueckner theory corrections is not well understood.

## Method
The author reviews the Brueckner reaction-matrix formulation of the nuclear many-body problem, the Moszkowski-Scott separation method for evaluating the reaction matrix, and the core-polarisation corrections introduced by Kuo and Brown for shell-model calculations. The paper also discusses the low-momentum potential $V_{low-k}$ approach and its relation to the separation method, using Brueckner calculations at normal nuclear matter density to illustrate the role of correlations and the dispersion term.

## Key numbers
- The separation distance $d$ in coordinate space is chosen such that the scattering phase-shift for the short-ranged potential $V_s$ is zero, with $d = d(k)$ being momentum-dependent.
- The wound-integral $I_D = \int (\psi - 1)^2 d\mathbf{r}$ measures correlations, where $\psi$ is the in-medium NN-correlated wave-function.
- Correlated wave functions are shown for cut-off $\Lambda = 9.8\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$ (left curves) and $\Lambda = 2\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$ (right curves) for triplet and singlet interactions.
- The potential energy per particle is shown as a function of cut-off $\Lambda$, with binding increasing dramatically for $\Lambda \leq 2.5\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$.
- The dispersion term $K_{disp} \sim I_D \times U$ is repulsive and important for providing nuclear saturation; a too small momentum cut-off quenches correlations so that $K_{disp} \rightarrow 0$ with no saturation.

## Context
This paper extends the historical narrative of Brueckner theory (Brueckner, 1950s) and its application to the nuclear shell-model by Gerry Brown and collaborators, particularly the Kuo-Brown interaction (Kuo and Brown, 1966-1968) which used the Hamada-Johnston potential with the separation method. The work relates to more recent developments in effective field theory (Weinberg, 1990) and $V_{low-k}$ (Bogner et al., 2001-2002), with numerical equivalence shown by Holt and Brown (2004). The paper enables future work on three-body forces and extrapolation to higher densities relevant to astrophysics and heavy-ion collisions, while noting that Brueckner theory as usually practiced includes medium effects up to three-body terms.
