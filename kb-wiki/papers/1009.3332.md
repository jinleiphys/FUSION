# Charmonium-nucleon potential from lattice QCD

> We present results for charmonium-nucleon potential $V_{c\bar{c}-N}(r)$ from quenched lattice QCD, which is calculated from the equal-time Bethe-Salpeter amplitude through the effective Schrödinger equation. Detail information of the low-energy interaction between the nucleon and charmonia ($η_c$ and $J/ψ$) is indispensable for exploring the formation of charmonium bound to nuclei. Our simulations are performed at a lattice cutoff of $1/a\approx 2.1$ GeV in a spatial volume of $(3 fm)^3$ with the nonperturbatively $O(a)$ improved Wilson action for the light quarks and a relativistic heavy quark action for the charm quark. We have found that the potential $V_{c\bar{c}-N}(r)$ for either the $η_c$ and $J/ψ$ states is weakly attractive at short distances and exponentially screened at large distances. The spin averaged $J/ψ$-$N$ potential is slightly more attractive than that of the $η_c$-$N$ case.

## Key claim

The charmonium-nucleon potential for both the eta_c and J/psi states is weakly attractive at short distances and exponentially screened at large distances, but the attraction is not strong enough to form a bound state in either system. The spin-averaged J/psi-nucleon potential is slightly more attractive than the eta_c-nucleon potential, though the authors note this result is obtained in quenched lattice QCD at relatively heavy pion masses (640-870 MeV) and may change near the physical point.

## Method

The authors compute the equal-time Bethe-Salpeter amplitude from four-point correlation functions in quenched lattice QCD, then extract the effective central potential through the stationary Schrodinger equation using the discrete Laplacian of the BS wave function. Simulations use a lattice cutoff of 1/a approximately 2.1 GeV in a spatial volume of (3 fm)^3 with nonperturbatively O(a) improved Wilson fermions for light quarks and a relativistic heavy quark action for charm quarks, at three pion masses (0.64, 0.72, and 0.87 GeV).

## Key numbers

- Lattice cutoff: 1/a approximately 2.1 GeV (beta=6.0, a=0.0931 fm)
- Spatial volume: (3.0 fm)^3 on 32^3 x 48 lattice; supplementary data on 16^3 x 48 lattice (1.5 fm)^3
- Pion masses studied: 0.64, 0.72, and 0.87 GeV (corresponding nucleon masses 1.43, 1.52, 1.70 GeV)
- Charmonium masses: M_eta_c = 2.92 GeV, M_J/psi = 3.00 GeV
- Yukawa fit parameters for eta_c-N potential: gamma approximately 0.1, alpha approximately 0.6 GeV (compared to phenomenological values gamma=0.6, alpha=0.6 GeV from Brodsky et al. (1990))
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for Yukawa fit: approximately 2.5; for inverse power law -1/r^7: approximately 34.3
- Statistics: 602 configurations for L=32, 200 configurations for L=16

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD approach to hadron-hadron potentials, originally developed for the nucleon-nucleon force by Ishii, Aoki, and Hatsuda (2007), to the charmonium-nucleon system. It improves upon earlier lattice studies by Yokokawa, Sasaki, Hatsuda, and Hayashigaki (2006) by directly computing the potential rather than just scattering lengths, and finds a significantly weaker attraction than the phenomenological potential used by Brodsky, Schmidt, and de Teramond (1990) based on Pomeron exchange. The results enable future few-body calculations of nuclear-bound charmonium states such as eta_c-He-3 or J/psi-He-3, but the authors emphasize that extension to dynamical lattice QCD at lighter quark masses is necessary for reliable predictions.
