# Multi-antikaonic nuclei in the relativistic mean-field theory

> Properties of multi-antikaonic nuclei (MKN), where several numbers of $K^-$ mesons are bound, are studied in the relativistic mean-field model, combined with chiral dynamics for kaonic part of the thermodynamic potential. The density profiles for nucleons and $K^-$ mesons, the single particle energy of the $K^-$ mesons, and binding energy of the MKN are obtained. The effects of the $\bar K-\bar K$ interactions on these quantities are discussed in comparison with other meson ($σ$, $ω$, and $ρ$)-exchange models. It is shown that the $\bar K-\bar K$ interactions originate from two contributions: One is the contact interaction between antikaons inherent in chiral symmetry, and the other is the one generated through coupling between the $K^-$ and meson mean fields. Both effects of the $\bar K-\bar K$ repulsive interactions become large on the ground state properties of the MKN as the number of the embedded $K^-$ mesons increases. A relation between the multi-antikaonic nuclei and kaon condensation in infinite and uniform matter is mentioned.

## Key claim

Multi-antikaonic nuclei (MKN) with several bound K- mesons can be described using a relativistic mean-field model combined with chiral dynamics, and the repulsive K-K interactions, arising both from contact terms inherent in chiral symmetry and from couplings through meson mean fields, become increasingly important for the ground state properties as the number of embedded K- mesons grows, though the authors note that the case of U_K = -120 MeV should be regarded as an extreme case of strongly attractive K-N interaction.

## Method

The authors solve coupled equations of motion for the K- field, sigma, omega, and rho meson mean fields, and the Coulomb potential, derived from a thermodynamic potential that combines a relativistic mean-field model for nucleons with a chiral Lagrangian for the kaonic part. The density profiles, single-particle energies, and binding energies are obtained self-consistently for a reference nucleus of 15O (A=15, Z=8) with varying numbers of K- mesons (|S| = 0, 1, 2, 4, 8, 12, 16), using two values of the K- optical potential depth (U_K = -80 MeV and -120 MeV). Results are compared with two meson-exchange models (MEM1 and MEM2) that omit some or all of the nonlinear chiral terms.

## Key numbers

- Central baryon density for 15O (|S|=0) is 1.03 rho_0, where rho_0 = 0.153 fm^-3
- Binding energy per nucleon for 15O is 6.93 MeV/A (empirical value is 7.46 MeV/A)
- For U_K = -80 MeV, the KN sigma term Sigma_KN is estimated as 332 MeV
- For U_K = -120 MeV, the KN sigma term Sigma_KN is estimated as 754 MeV
- The scalar meson-K coupling constant g_sigmaK = 0.97 for U_K = -80 MeV, and g_sigmaK = 2.21 for U_K = -120 MeV
- The vector meson-K coupling constants are g_omegaK = 2.91 (g_omegaN/3) and g_rhoK = 4.27 (g_rhoN)
- The meson decay constant f = 93 MeV
- The chiral angle at the center theta^(0) is small (less than about 0.5 rad) for |S| = 1,2 for both U_K values
- Stable solutions for MEM2 are not obtained for |S| >= 12 in the case of U_K = -120 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaon condensation in neutron-star matter (Kn86, Mtt93, Tpl94, Lbm95, Fmmt96, Pbp97) and deeply bound kaonic nuclear states (Ay02) to the regime of multi-antikaonic nuclei with several K- mesons. The authors build on their preliminary results (Mmt07) and compare their chiral model approach with meson-exchange models (MEM1 and MEM2) used in earlier work on kaon condensation (Kpe95, Sm96, P00, P01, G01, Cgs00, Nr01, Vyt03, Mtv06, Mtec06) and kaonic nuclei (Mfg05, Zpln06, Gfgm07). The results enable investigation of the possible continuity between finite MKN systems and uniform kaon condensation in infinite matter that may occur in neutron stars.
