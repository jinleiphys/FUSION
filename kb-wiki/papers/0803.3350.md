# Hyperfine Mixing and the Semileptonic Decays of Double-Heavy Baryons in a Quark Model

> The semileptonic decays of the lowest-lying double-heavy baryons is treated in a quark model. For the $Ξ_{bb}$, hyperfine mixing in the spin wave function leaves the total rate for decay into the lowest lying daughter baryons essentially unchanged, but changes the relative rates into the $Ξ_{bc}$ and $Ξ_{bc}^\prime$. The same pattern is obtained in the decays of the $Ω_{bb}$. For the $Ξ_{bc}$, this mixing leads to factor of about 17 suppression in the decay rate to the $Ξ_{cc}^*$, and a factor of two suppression in the total decay rate. For the $Ω_{bc}$, the decay to the $Ω_{cc}^*$ is suppressed by a factor of more than 30 from the unmixed case, and the total decay rate is decreased to about 40% of the decay rate obtained when mixing is ignored.

## Key claim

Hyperfine mixing in the spin wave functions of double-heavy baryons with three different quark flavors (Ξbc, Ωbc) is large in this quark model, contrary to heavy quark spin symmetry expectations that it should be small; this mixing significantly suppresses exclusive decay rates to spin-3/2 daughters and reduces total decay rates, while for baryons with identical heavy quarks (Ξbb, Ωbb) it leaves total rates unchanged but alters relative branching fractions.

## Method

The authors construct baryon wave functions using Jacobi coordinates with harmonic oscillator spatial wave functions and spin wave functions that are symmetric (χS), mixed-symmetric (χλ), or mixed-antisymmetric (χρ) under exchange of the first two quarks. They compute semileptonic decay matrix elements by permuting quark order so that the active heavy quark becomes the third quark, then evaluate spin matrix elements Γρρ, Γλλ, and ΓSλ. Decay rates are calculated for transitions between ground-state double-heavy baryons, comparing results with and without the hyperfine mixing that mixes the χλ and χρ components in the Ξbc and Ωbc states.

## Key numbers

- Ξbb → Ξbc ℓν rate: 0.72 × 10¹⁰ s⁻¹ (cbq unmixed), 0.29 × 10¹⁰ s⁻¹ (cbq mixed)
- Ξbb → Ξbc′ ℓν rate: 0.47 × 10¹⁰ s⁻¹ (cbq unmixed), 0.90 × 10¹⁰ s⁻¹ (cbq mixed)
- Ξbb → Ξbc* ℓν rate: 4.64 × 10¹⁰ s⁻¹ (unmixed, independent of representation)
- Ξbc → Ξcc* ℓν rate: 3.56 × 10¹⁰ s⁻¹ (cbq unmixed), 0.20 × 10¹⁰ s⁻¹ (cbq mixed), a suppression factor of about 17
- Ξbc total decay rate: 5.10 × 10¹⁰ s⁻¹ (cbq unmixed), 2.38 × 10¹⁰ s⁻¹ (cbq mixed), a suppression factor of about 2
- Ωbc → Ωcc* ℓν rate: 3.87 × 10¹⁰ s⁻¹ (cbq unmixed), 0.12 × 10¹⁰ s⁻¹ (cbq mixed), a suppression factor of more than 30
- Ωbc total decay rate decreased to about 40% of the unmixed value when mixing is included
- Combined total decay rate of Ξbc and Ξbc′ into Ξcc* is approximately 15 × 10¹⁰ s⁻¹ regardless of mixing representation

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous quark model study of double-heavy baryon spectra (Roberts and Pervin, 2008) by examining the impact of the large hyperfine mixing found there on semileptonic decays. It contrasts with heavy quark spin symmetry arguments (White and Savage) that would predict small mixing, and with other quark model calculations by Albertus et al., Flynn and Nieves, Ebert et al., Hernandez et al., and Faessler et al. that did not include this mixing. The results enable future studies of strong and electromagnetic transitions of these states, where similar mixing effects may appear, and suggest that experimental searches for Ξbc and Ωbc decays must account for the suppressed rates to spin-3/2 daughters.
