# Description of η-distributions at RHIC energies in terms of a stochastic model

> To explain η-distributions at RHIC energies we consider the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process. To account for hadrons produced in the central region, we assume existence of third source located there (y \approx 0) in addition to two sources located at the beam and target rapidities (\pm y_{max} = \pm \ln[\sqrt{s_{NN}}/m_{N}]). This results in better χ^2/n.d.f. than those for only two sources when analysing data.

## Key claim

The Ornstein-Uhlenbeck (O-U) stochastic process, extended with a third source at central rapidity (y ≈ 0) in addition to the two sources at beam and target rapidities, provides a better description of η-distributions at RHIC energies than the two-source model alone, as measured by improved χ²/n.d.f. values.

## Method

The authors model the rapidity distribution of produced hadrons using the Fokker-Planck equation for the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process, with initial conditions of two delta-function sources at ±y_max. They add a third Gaussian source at y ≈ 0 to account for particles produced in the central region, with a weight ratio r_f between the two peripheral sources and the central source. The model includes Jacobian transformations from rapidity y to pseudorapidity η, with separate mass-to-transverse-momentum ratios (m/p_t) for the peripheral and central sources, and fits the evolution parameter p and variances V² to PHOBOS and BRAHMS data.

## Key numbers

- For PHOBOS data at √s_NN = 130 GeV (0-6% centrality), best χ²/n.d.f. = 0.62/47 achieved with weight ratio c₁/2:c₀:c₁/2 = 1:7:1 and 1:8:1
- For PHOBOS data at √s_NN = 200 GeV (0-6% centrality), best χ²/n.d.f. = 0.63/47 achieved with weight ratio 1:2:1
- For PHOBOS data at 130 GeV with ratio 1:7:1: p = 0.73 ± 0.03, V₀² = 4.42 ± 0.84, V₁² = 1.17 ± 0.17
- For PHOBOS data at 200 GeV with ratio 1:7:1: p = 0.74 ± 0.33, V₀² = 5.19 ± 1.24, V₁² = 1.36 ± 0.23
- For z_r scaling of PHOBOS data at 130 GeV: χ²/n.d.f. = 21.2/318 across 5 centrality cuts
- For z_r scaling of PHOBOS data at 200 GeV: χ²/n.d.f. = 11.5/318 across 5 centrality cuts
- For BRAHMS data at 200 GeV (0-5% centrality) with p=0.75 fixed and ratio 1:20:1: χ²/n.d.f. = 4.37/31
- For BRAHMS z_r scaling with ratio 1:10:1: χ²/n.d.f. = 29.8/186

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies using the Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process (Biyajima 2002) and follows the approach of Wolschin (1999, 2003) and Morita (2002) for modeling rapidity distributions in heavy-ion collisions. The three-source model improves upon the two-source formulation by accounting for hadrons produced in the central region, and successfully reproduces the z_r = η/η_rms scaling observed by the PHOBOS Collaboration (Back 2001) across multiple centrality cuts. This enables more realistic descriptions of particle production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies.
