# Rotating Hybrid Stars with Color-Flavor-Locked Quark Matter

> In the present work we have achieved phase transition from $β$ stable hadronic matter to color-flavor locked (CFL) quark matter with Maxwell construction. The hybrid equation of state (EoS), obtained for different values of bag pressure $B$ and gap parameter $Δ$, have been used to compute the speed of sound in hybrid star (HS) matter. The structural properties of the HSs in both static and rotating conditions have been calculated in the light of various constraints from different astrophysical and empirical perspectives. The effects of $B$ and $Δ$ on the EoS and structural properties have been investigated. At a certain density, shortly after phase transition, the HSs become unstable. In static conditions, the mass-radius solutions satisfy the constraints from GW190425, NICER experiment for PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 and from massive pulsars like PSR J0348+0432 and PSR J0740+6620. In rapidly rotating conditions at Kepler frequency, the constraints on maximum mass from the secondary component of GW190814 and that on rotational frequency from fast pulsars like PSR B1937+21 and PSR J1748-2446ad are all satisfied. In slowly rotating conditions, the universality relations in terms of normalized moment of inertia also hold quite well for most of our HS configurations.

## Key claim

The authors show that even a simplistic color-flavor-locked (CFL) quark matter formalism, when combined with a stiff hadronic equation of state (NL3ωρ6 model) and Maxwell construction for the phase transition, can produce hybrid star configurations that satisfy most current astrophysical constraints, but only within a narrow stability window shortly after the phase transition.

## Method

The authors constructed hybrid equations of state by applying Maxwell construction to achieve a sharp phase transition from β-equilibrated hadronic matter (described by the NL3ωρ6 model, without hyperons) to color-flavor-locked quark matter, using the formalism of Alford (2003) with equal number densities of u, d, and s quarks. They varied the bag pressure B^(1/4) between 185 and 200 MeV and the gap parameter Δ between 0 (unpaired quark matter), 35, and 50 MeV, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for static stars and used the rotating neutron star (RNS) code based on the Komatsu-Eriguchi-Hachisu method for rotating configurations at both Kepler frequency and 500 Hz.

## Key numbers

- Maximum gravitational mass in static conditions: 2.14 M⊙ (with radius 14.06 km) for (B^(1/4), Δ) = (200 MeV, 0 MeV); lowest maximum mass is 1.96 M⊙ (radius 14.03 km) for (185 MeV, 50 MeV)
- Transition densities: hadronic density at transition ranges from 2.07 ρ0 (for B^(1/4)=185 MeV, Δ=50 MeV) to 2.70 ρ0 (for B^(1/4)=200 MeV, Δ=0 MeV); CFL density at transition ranges from 6.10 ρ0 to 7.20 ρ0
- Speed of sound squared peaks at average 0.75 for B^(1/4)=200 MeV and average 0.62 for B^(1/4)=185 MeV in the hadronic phase; maximum in pure CFL phase is 0.53
- Radius at 1.4 M⊙ (R_1.4) is 13.75 km, unaffected by phase transition for all parameter combinations
- Average change in maximum mass from static to slow rotation (500 Hz) is 11.5%; from static to rapid rotation (Kepler frequency) is 21.1%
- The ratio M_max^R/M_max^S for rapid rotation averages 1.21

## Context

This work extends prior studies of hybrid stars with CFL quark matter by Alford (2003), Roupas (2021), and Li (2021) by applying Maxwell construction (rather than Gibbs construction) to a stiff hadronic model (NL3ωρ6 from Horowitz and Pais). The results show that even a simple CFL formalism can satisfy constraints from GW190425, NICER measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, and massive pulsars PSR J0348+0432 and PSR J0740+6620, as well as the secondary component mass of GW190814 under rapid rotation. The work enables future studies to incorporate more sophisticated CFL treatments (such as those by Buballa) and to investigate whether the narrow stability window after phase transition is an artifact of Maxwell construction or a physical feature.
