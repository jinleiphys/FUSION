# New exact solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics

> A new class of simple and exact solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics is presented, and the consequences are explored in data analysis. The effects of longitudinal work and acceleration are taken into account in an advanced estimate of the initial energy density and the life-time of the reaction.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new class of exact, explicit, and accelerating solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics that generalize the Hwa-Bjorken solution; the key hedge is that the solutions are valid only for specific combinations of the equation-of-state parameter, dimensionality, and acceleration parameter, as listed in Table I, and the application to data uses the special case (e) which is restricted to 1+1 dimensions with a speed of sound \(c_s^2 = 1\).

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Euler and energy conservation equations for a perfect fluid with an equation of state \(\varepsilon - B = \kappa(p+B)\) (where \(\kappa = 1/c_s^2\) and \(B\) is a bag constant) in Rindler coordinates \((\tau, \eta)\). They derive a family of analytic solutions for the pressure \(p\) and velocity \(v = \tanh(\lambda \eta)\) that depend on the acceleration parameter \(\lambda\), the number of spatial dimensions \(d\), and the equation-of-state parameter \(\kappa\). The solution labeled case (e) in Table I, valid only in 1+1 dimensions with \(\kappa = 1\), is then used to compute the rapidity distribution \(dn/dy\) via a saddle-point integration, and the parameter \(\lambda\) is extracted by fitting to BRAHMS data for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV.

## Key numbers

- The fit to BRAHMS \(dn/dy\) data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV yields \(\lambda = 1.18 \pm 0.01\), indicating the presence of acceleration.
- Using the Bjorken estimate \(\varepsilon_{Bj} = 5\) GeV/fm\(^3\) and \(\tau_f/\tau_0 = 8 \pm 2\) fm/c, the new analytic formula gives \(\varepsilon_c = (2.0 \pm 0.1) \varepsilon_{Bj} = 10.0 \pm 0.5\) GeV/fm\(^3\) for \(\lambda = 1.18 \pm 0.01\).
- For a realistic equation of state with \(c_s^2 = 0.1\) in \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV Au+Au reactions, the acceleration increases the initial energy density estimate to 15 GeV/fm\(^3\) and the life-time estimate by 36\%.
- The life-time correction from the new estimate is \(\tau_c = \lambda \tau_{Bj}\), implying an \(18 \pm 1\%\) increase for \(\lambda = 1.18\).

## Context

This work extends the classic Hwa-Bjorken solution (Hwa, 1974; Bjorken, 1983), which lacks acceleration and yields a flat rapidity distribution, by providing accelerating exact solutions that better describe the measured \(dn/dy\) data from the BRAHMS collaboration (Bearden, 2004). The new solutions enable improved estimates of the initial energy density and reaction life-time in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, correcting the well-known Bjorken estimate and the Sinyukov-Makhlin life-time estimate (Makhlin, 1987) for the effects of longitudinal work and acceleration. The results are relevant for characterizing the nearly perfect fluid created at RHIC, whose kinematic shear viscosity was found to be smaller than that of superfluid \(^4\)He (Lacey, 2006).
