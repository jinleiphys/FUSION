# A Possible Mechanism to Alter Gyromagnetic Factor

> Dirac has predicted that the $g$ factor of an electron is strictly equal to 2 in the framework of relativistic quantum mechanics. However, later physicists have found that this factor can be slightly deviated from 2 (i.e., the problem of anomalous magnetic moments of leptons) when they consider quantum filed theory. This fact thus renders the $g$ factors of free leptons serving as precision tests for quantum electrodynamics, the standard model and beyond. In this work, we re-examine the problem of $g$ factor within the framework of relativistic quantum mechanics. We propose a possible mechanism called the ``electron-braidon mixing'', such that the $g$ factor of an electron can be visibly altered. Our results are hopeful to be verified in experiments and also shed new light to the problem of the anomalous magnetic moments of leptons.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the gyromagnetic factor (g factor) of an electron can be altered within relativistic quantum mechanics alone, without invoking quantum field theory, through a mechanism called "electron-braidon mixing" where the electron Hamiltonian is mixed with those of two unitary-transformed "braidon" states via mixing angles.

## Method

The authors construct generalized Pauli matrices from Dirac's matrices and define two new Hamiltonian operators (type-I and type-II Dirac's braidons) that are unitary rotations of the standard Dirac electron Hamiltonian. They then form a mixed Hamiltonian as a linear combination of the electron Hamiltonian and the two braidon Hamiltonians with mixing angles theta and phi, and derive the resulting g factor by taking the non-relativistic limit of the Dirac equation in an electromagnetic field.

## Key numbers

- The derived g factor formula is g_s = 2 [1 - (cos(theta) sin(theta) cos(phi) / (cos^2(theta) + sin^2(theta) sin^2(phi))) * (m_e c / p)], where theta is in [-pi/4, pi/4]
- For the simplified case of electron-braidon type-I mixing only (phi=0), the g factor becomes g_s = 2 [1 - tan(theta) * (m_e c / p)]
- A "magic mixing angle" theta_magic = arctan(p / (m_e c)) is predicted to yield g_s = 0
- The electron g factor g_e ≈ 2.0023 corresponds to mixing angle theta_e ≈ -arctan[(g_e - 2)p / (2 m_e c)]
- The muon g factor g_mu ≈ 2.0023 corresponds to mixing angle theta_mu ≈ -arctan[(g_mu - 2)p / (2 m_mu c)]
- The proton g factor g_p ≈ 5.5856 corresponds to mixing angle theta_p ≈ -arctan[(g_p - 2)p / (2 m_p c)]

## Context

This work extends the framework of Dirac's relativistic quantum mechanics (1928) by introducing a mixing mechanism inspired by quark mixing and neutrino mixing in particle physics. It contrasts with the standard quantum field theory approach to anomalous magnetic moments initiated by Schwinger (1948), who calculated the electron's anomalous g factor as a radiative correction in QED. The mechanism offers an alternative interpretation of anomalous magnetic moments without quantum fluctuations, and the authors suggest it could be experimentally verified by observing a tunable g factor, potentially reaching zero at the magic mixing angle.
