# Quarkonium at T>0

> We report recent progress on theoretical investigations of quarkonia at finite temperature. We discuss medium modification of charmonia and bottomonia from a viewpoint of local operators and point out that while charmonia are sensitive to the deconfinement transition bottomonia will be modified at much higher temperatures.

# Quarkonium at T>0

## Key claim

While charmonia are sensitive to the deconfinement transition and show drastic modification around T_c, bottomonia will be modified only at much higher temperatures (above 2T_c), with the caveat that the separation scale assumption underlying the operator product expansion approach may be questionable for charmonium at high temperature.

## Method

The paper reviews medium modification of heavy quarkonia using an operator product expansion (OPE) approach, where the interaction between a heavy quarkonium and soft gluons is expressed as a sum of Wilson coefficients times expectation values of local operators. For deeply bound systems, the mass shift is derived from the chromoelectric condensate extracted from lattice gauge theory via the QCD trace anomaly and enthalpy density; for charmonium where the separation scale condition is questionable, the author turns to QCD sum rules with maximum entropy method (MEM) applied to the current correlation function in the deep Euclidean region.

## Key numbers

- Mass shift formula: Δm_{Q̅Q} = -(7π²/18)(a₀²/ε)⟨(α_s/π)ΔE²⟩, where a₀ is the Bohr radius of the Coulombic bound state and ε is the binding energy
- The chromoelectric condensate ⟨(α_s/π)ΔE²⟩ is expressed in terms of M₀(T) (gluonic part of QCD trace anomaly ε-3p) and M₂(T) (gluonic part of enthalpy density ε+p) extracted from pure SU(3) lattice gauge theory
- Quarkonium melting requires a width Γ > 50 MeV based on energy density estimates from RHIC heavy ion collisions and hydrodynamic evolution
- J/ψ shows drastic spectral change around T_c, while Υ shows no significant modification at T_c but exhibits sizable modification above 2T_c

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Peskin (1979) for heavy quarkonium interactions with soft gluons, and builds on lattice QCD studies of spectral functions using the maximum entropy method pioneered by Asakawa and Hatsuda (2001). The OPE approach connects to earlier finite-temperature QCD sum rule calculations by Hatsuda, Koike, and Lee (1993) and the author's own prior work on J/ψ spectral change (Morita and Lee, 2008). The results enable comparison with heavy ion collision data at RHIC and LHC energies, where bottomonia are expected to be more amenable to effective field theory treatment than charmonia.
