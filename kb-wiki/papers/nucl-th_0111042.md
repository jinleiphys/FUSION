# Renormalization Group Equation for Low Momentum Effective Nuclear Interactions

> We consider two nonperturbative methods originally used to derive shell model effective interactions in nuclei. These methods have been applied to the two nucleon sector to obtain an energy independent effective interaction V_{low k}, which preserves the low momentum half-on-shell T matrix and the deuteron pole, with a sharp cutoff imposed on all intermediate state momenta. We show that V_{low k} scales with the cutoff precisely as one expects from renormalization group arguments. This result is a step towards reformulating traditional model space many-body calculations in the language of effective field theories and the renormalization group. The numerical scaling properties of V_{low k} are observed to be in excellent agreement with our exact renormalization group equation.

# Renormalization Group Equation for Low Momentum Effective Nuclear Interactions

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the energy-independent low-momentum effective interaction \(V_{\text{low k}}\), derived from traditional shell-model effective interaction methods (Kuo-Lee-Ratcliff folded diagram theory and Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation), scales with the momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\) according to an exact renormalization group equation, establishing that these traditional nuclear many-body methods are equivalent to RG decimations in the two-nucleon sector. This equivalence is shown analytically and confirmed numerically, but the authors explicitly restrict the analysis to the two-body problem in free space, noting that extension to the shell-model effective interaction with many-body correlations remains an open goal.

## Method

The authors derive the RG flow equation for \(V_{\text{low k}}\) from three independent approaches: (1) direct differentiation of the half-on-shell \(T\) matrix equation under the condition \(dT/d\Lambda = 0\); (2) the Kuo-Lee-Ratcliff folded diagram series, by considering an infinitesimal decimation of the cutoff from \(\Lambda\) to \(\Lambda - \delta\Lambda\) and summing the resulting series; and (3) the Lee-Suzuki iterative similarity transformation method, again via infinitesimal decimation. The resulting exact RG equation is \(\frac{d}{d\Lambda} V_{\text{low k}}(k',k) = \frac{2}{\pi} \frac{V_{\text{low k}}(k',\Lambda) T(\Lambda,k;\Lambda^2)}{1-(k/\Lambda)^2}\). Numerical verification is performed by computing the numerical derivative of \(V_{\text{low k}}\) from the Paris potential across a range of \(\Lambda\) values and comparing with the theoretical beta function.

## Key numbers

- The RG decimation is carried out for cutoffs \(\Lambda \lesssim 2 \; \text{fm}^{-1}\), where the high-momentum modes of all studied potential models (Paris, Bonn-A, Argonne V-18, Idaho-A) renormalize to a unique \(V_{\text{low k}}\).
- The numerical derivative \(dV_{\text{low k}}/d\Lambda\) is compared with the theoretical beta function in the \(^1S_0\) partial wave for the Paris potential, showing excellent agreement (Figure 1).
- The beta function is observed to be nearly zero in the neighborhood of \(\Lambda \approx 2 \; \text{fm}^{-1}\), consistent with the cutoff independence of shell-model results using \(V_{\text{low k}}\).
- The s-wave matrix element \(V_{\text{low k}}(0,0)\) exhibits flow behavior that agrees with the power divergence subtraction scheme of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise for small model spaces.

## Context

This work bridges traditional nuclear shell-model effective interaction methods (Kuo-Lee-Ratcliff folded diagram theory and Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation) with modern effective field theory and renormalization group approaches. It extends the earlier work of Bogner et al. (2001) on constructing \(V_{\text{low k}}\) from realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials, and contrasts with the energy-dependent RG treatment of two-body scattering by Birse et al. (1999). The paper is a step toward reformulating traditional model-space many-body calculations in the language of EFT and RG, enabling systematic error estimation and power counting for energy-independent effective interactions. The ultimate goal is to extend these RG arguments to the shell-model effective interaction for many-body systems, which remains restricted to two-particle correlations in the nucleus at this stage.
