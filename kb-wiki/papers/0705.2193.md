# Forward hadron production in high energy pA collisions: from RHIC to LHC

> We present a calculation of Pi, D and B mesons production at RHIC and LHC energies based upon the KKT model of gluon saturation. We discuss dependence of the nuclear modification factor on rapidity and transverse momentum.

# Forward hadron production in high energy pA collisions: from RHIC to LHC

## Key claim

The KKT model of gluon saturation, previously successful at RHIC, predicts that at LHC energies the nuclear modification factor for pion, D, and B meson production becomes a very slow function of rapidity, with less than 20% variation between pseudorapidity 0 and 6, because the fast onset of saturation observed at RHIC is replaced by virtually y-independent behavior at LHC.

## Method

The author calculates inclusive hadron production cross sections using the KKT phenomenological model for the gluon dipole forward scattering amplitude, parameterized with an anomalous dimension that interpolates between the quasi-classical and small-x evolution regimes. For light hadrons, the cross section sums inclusive gluon production and valence quark production, each convolved with LO fragmentation functions; for heavy quarks, the single-inclusive quark production cross section is derived from a kT-factorized formula in the dipole model, then convolved with Peterson fragmentation functions for D and B mesons.

## Key numbers

- Gluon saturation scale: Q_s^2(y) = 0.13 GeV^2 * e^(0.3 y) * N_coll, with parameters Lambda = 0.6 GeV and lambda = 0.3 fixed by DIS data
- Minimal saturation scale rapidity: y_0 = 0.5, fit to RHIC data
- Anomalous dimension at saturation scale: gamma = 0.44 from numerical BK solution, compared to 0.5 in KKT (double logarithmic approximation) and 0.628 in saddle-point approximation models
- Nuclear modification factor for pions: R_pA = 0.60 for minimum bias events and R_pA = 0.46 for 0-20% centrality cut at RHIC
- Geometric scaling breakdown scale at LHC: Q_geom = 2.7 GeV at y=0, Q_geom = 5.0 GeV at y=2
- Peterson fragmentation parameters: epsilon = 0.043 for c-quark, epsilon = 0.006 for b-quark

## Context

This work extends the KKT model of Kharzeev, Kovchegov, and Tuchin (2004) from RHIC to LHC energies, building on earlier theoretical analyses of coherent effects in hadron production (Kharzeev 2002, 2003; Albacete 2003; Baier 2003) and open charm production (Gelis 2003; Tuchin 2004; Blaizot 2004; Kovchegov 2006; Kharzeev 2003). The calculations enable predictions for the nuclear modification factor across a wide range of rapidities, transverse momenta, and quark masses at LHC, and suggest that measuring the mass and rapidity dependence of R_pA can map the transition region between geometric scaling and collinear factorization.
