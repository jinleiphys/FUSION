# Effective field theory for hydrodynamics: thermodynamics, and the derivative expansion

> We consider the low-energy effective field theory describing the infrared dynamics of non-dissipative fluids. We extend previous work to accommodate conserved charges, and we clarify the matching between field theory variables and thermodynamical ones. We discuss the systematics of the derivative expansion, for which field theory offers a conceptually clear and technically neat scheme. As an example, we compute the correction to the sound-wave dispersion relation coming from a sample second-order term. This formalism forms the basis for a study of anomalies in hydrodynamics via effective field theory, which is initiated in a companion paper.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that non-dissipative hydrodynamics can be reformulated as a conventional effective field theory of Goldstone bosons, where the derivative expansion is systematic and unambiguous, but this equivalence holds only for fluids without dissipation and requires imposing a new "chemical shift" symmetry to enforce that charge flows with the fluid.

## Method

The authors construct a low-energy effective action for a relativistic fluid using d scalar fields φ^I (comoving coordinates) plus a phase field ψ with a U(1) shift symmetry, imposing Poincaré invariance and internal symmetries including a new "chemical shift" symmetry ψ → ψ + f(φ^I). They organize the Lagrangian as a derivative expansion, identify the leading-order term as a function F(b, y) where b is the norm of the identically conserved current J^μ and y = u^μ ∂_μ ψ, and derive the stress-energy tensor and Noether current to establish the dictionary between field theory variables and thermodynamic quantities. They then extend the analysis to higher-derivative corrections following the Israel-Stewart procedure for defining local thermodynamic variables out of equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- The entropy density s is identified with b, the norm of the identically conserved current J^μ: s = b
- The temperature T is given by T = -F_b, where F_b is the derivative of the Lagrangian with respect to b
- The chemical potential μ equals y: μ = y
- The charge density n is n = F_y, the derivative of F with respect to y
- The energy density ρ and pressure p are ρ = F_y y - F and p = F - F_b b

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to fluids with conserved charges, building on prior formulations for zero-temperature superfluids by Son (2002) and for barotropic normal fluids by Dubovsky, Greiner, Nicolis, and Rattazzi (2006). The formalism provides a systematic framework for computing higher-derivative corrections to hydrodynamics, such as corrections to sound-wave dispersion relations, and forms the basis for studying anomalies in hydrodynamics via effective field theory, which is initiated in a companion paper. The approach contrasts with traditional hydrodynamic derivative expansions that become ambiguous at higher orders, and it is more general than holographic fluid models because it does not rely on AdS/CFT duality.
