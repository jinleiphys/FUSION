# Aspects of quarkonium propagation in a thermal medium as seen by string models

> We use gauge/string duality to model a heavy quark-antiquark pair in a color singlet moving through a thermal plasma. In particular, we explore the effect of velocity on the string tension and Debye screening mass. Then we apply the results to the analysis of heavy quarkonium bound states. With some assumptions, we estimate the characteristic size of quarkonium and its dissociation temperature.

## Key claim

Using gauge/string duality, the paper models a heavy quark-antiquark pair moving through a thermal plasma and finds that the velocity dependence of the string tension and Debye screening mass can be captured by simple rescaling of the static results, but only under the assumption that the pair does not lose energy (no drag force) and that the string configuration remains static in the pair rest frame.

## Method

The author uses two five-dimensional effective string models with metrics that include a warp factor and a blackening factor, one given by the Schwarzschild AdS blackening factor and another derived from Einstein equations. A Nambu-Goto string describes the quark-antiquark pair, and by boosting the metric to the pair rest frame, the effect of a hot wind is introduced. The string tension is extracted from the large-distance behavior of the connected string configuration, and the Debye screening mass is obtained from the binding energy defined as the difference between connected and disconnected string energies, parameterized by an exponential form.

## Key numbers

* The string tension at zero temperature and velocity is given by \(\sigma = e \gamma \mathfrak{g} s\), with \(\mathfrak{g}=0.176\).
* For the model with blackening factor \(f(r)=1-(r/r_h)^4\), the confinement condition is \(\sqrt{\gamma}\, T/T_{pc} < 1\), where \(T_{pc} = \frac{1}{\pi}\sqrt{2s/(3\sqrt{3})}\).
* The string tension on the critical curve \(\sqrt{\gamma}\, T/T_{pc}=1\) is approximately \(0.9\sigma\).
* For the model with the other blackening factor, the string tension at the lower temperature limit (\(4\times 10^{-7} T_{pc}\)) is approximately \(\sigma_n = 1.63 \mathfrak{g} s\) for \(v=0\), and vanishes near \(v\approx 0.27\).
* The Debye screening mass in the limit \(v\rightarrow 1\) for the first model scales as \(\sqrt[4]{1-v^2}\, m/T = C\), with \(C\) given by \(\Gamma^2(1/4)/\sqrt{2\pi}\) times a factor depending on the choice of constant term.
* The critical temperature for \(SU(3)\) pure gauge theory is set to \(T_c = 0.629\sqrt{\sigma} = 0.435\sqrt{s}\).
* The Debye screening mass is plotted for temperatures up to \(5T_c\) and velocities up to \(v\approx 0.72\) for the second model, beyond which it increases steeply.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of five-dimensional string models (Andreev 2015, Andreev 2017) to include the effect of velocity on quarkonium propagation, building on the gauge/string duality approach first proposed by Maldacena. It contrasts with lattice QCD calculations, which are limited to static quarkonium, and provides analytic estimates for the characteristic size and dissociation temperature of moving quarkonium. The results enable phenomenological estimates for charmonium (non-relativistic velocities) and bottomonium (up to \(v\lesssim 0.82\)) in heavy-ion collisions, and could be tested against effective field theories or numerical methods.
