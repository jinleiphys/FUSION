# Probing Quark Gluon Plasma by Heavy Flavors

> The drag and diffusion coefficients of charm and bottom quarks propagating through quark gluon plasma (QGP) have been evaluated within the framework of perturbative Quantum Chromodynamics (pQCD). Both radiative and collisional processes of dissipation are included in evaluating these transport coefficients. The dead cone as well as the LPM effects on radiative energy loss of heavy quarks have also been considered. The Fokker Planck equation has been solved to study the dissipation of heavy quarks momentum in QGP. The nuclear suppression factor, $R_{\mathrm AA}$ and the elliptic flow $v_2^{HF}$ of the non-photonic electrons resulting from the semi-leptonic decays of hadrons containing charm and bottom quarks have been evaluated for RHIC and LHC nuclear collision conditions. We find that the observed $R_{\mathrm AA}$ and $v_2$ at RHIC can be reproduced simultaneously within the pQCD framework.

# Probing Quark Gluon Plasma by Heavy Flavors

## Key claim
The observed nuclear suppression factor ($R_{\mathrm AA}$) and elliptic flow ($v_2^{HF}$) of non-photonic electrons from heavy-flavor decays at RHIC can be reproduced simultaneously within perturbative QCD, but only when both collisional and radiative energy loss processes are included together with a non-ideal equation of state (velocity of sound $c_s = 1/\sqrt{4}$ rather than the Stefan-Boltzmann limit).

## Method
The drag and diffusion coefficients for charm and bottom quarks in quark-gluon plasma were evaluated using perturbative QCD, including both collisional processes ($gQ \rightarrow gQ$, $qQ \rightarrow qQ$, $\bar{q}Q \rightarrow \bar{q}Q$) and radiative processes with dead cone and Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effects. The Fokker-Planck equation was solved with these effective transport coefficients, using initial heavy quark momentum distributions from proton-proton collisions. The resulting heavy quark distributions were convoluted with fragmentation functions to obtain $D$ and $B$ meson spectra, then decayed to electrons, with the QGP evolution described by (2+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamics with boost invariance.

## Key numbers
- Initial temperature $T_i = 400$ MeV and thermalization time $\tau_i = 0.2$ fm/c for Au+Au collisions at RHIC ($\sqrt{s_{\mathrm NN}} = 200$ GeV)
- Initial temperature $T_i = 700$ MeV and thermalization time $\tau_i = 0.08$ fm/c for LHC conditions
- The elliptic flow $v_2^{HF}$ reaches a maximum of about 8% then saturates for $p_T > 2$ GeV at RHIC
- Effective impact parameter $b = 10.2$ fm for minimum bias Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm NN}} = 200$ GeV
- Velocity of sound $c_s = 1/\sqrt{4}$ used to reproduce both $R_{\mathrm AA}$ and $v_2^{HF}$ simultaneously

## Context
This work extends earlier perturbative QCD calculations of heavy quark transport by including both collisional and radiative energy loss mechanisms, where previous studies often considered only one process. The authors note that recent evaluations of drag and diffusion coefficients in hot hadronic matter (Ghosh, Das3, Laine, MinHe, Abreu) show significant interactions of heavy mesons with hadrons, indicating that hadronic phase contributions must be subtracted to reliably characterize QGP properties. The analysis enables future work to disentangle QGP and hadronic contributions to heavy-flavor observables at both RHIC and LHC energies.
