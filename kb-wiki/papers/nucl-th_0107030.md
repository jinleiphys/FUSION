# A self-consistent quantal description of high-K states in the tilted-axis cranking model

> A self-consistent and quantal description of high-$K$ bands is given in the framework of the tilted-axis cranking model. (With a $θ=90^{\circ}$ tilt angle with respect to $x$-axis, this cranking model is equivalent to the $z$-axis cranking.) The numerical results of the HFB calculations in this framework are compared with experimental data for two quasi-particle excited bands with $K^π=6^+$ in $^{178}$W.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a self-consistent tilted-axis cranking Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach, combined with angular momentum projection, can quantitatively describe high-K two-quasiparticle rotational bands, but the agreement with experiment is limited by the choice of the phenomenological pairing-plus-Q·Q interaction and the mean-field approximation's inability to describe band crossing.

## Method

The authors performed tilted-axis cranking HFB calculations for the nucleus ¹⁷⁸W using a Hamiltonian consisting of a spherical Nilsson Hamiltonian plus a pairing-plus-Q·Q residual interaction, with constraints on particle numbers and angular momentum vectors. They solved the HFB equation via the gradient method to obtain energy curves as functions of the tilt angle θ for spins J = 6–11 ħ, then performed angular momentum projection onto the z-axis cranked HFB states to obtain quantal energy spectra. The results were compared with experimental data for the K^π = 6⁺ two-quasiparticle band in ¹⁷⁸W.

## Key numbers

- Band head energy (I = K = 6 ħ): 1.67 MeV (experiment), 1.21 MeV (z-axis cranked HFB), 1.79 MeV (angular momentum projection)
- Self-consistently calculated quadrupole deformations: β = 0.28 and γ = 0.0° for the band head state
- About 98% of the total angular momentum of the band head (J = K = 6 ħ) comes from two neutron single-particle orbits: f₇/₂ and h₉/₂
- Tilt angles for rotational members: θ = 90.0° (α = 0), 58.5° (α = 1), 48.0° (α = 2), 42.0° (α = 3), 38.0° (α = 4), 33.5° (α = 5), compared with classical values 90.0°, 59.0°, 48.6°, 41.8°, 36.9°, 33.1°
- For the tilted-rotation minimum at θ = 38° for J = 10 ħ, the z-axis spin components of f₇/₂ and h₉/₂ in the neutron negative-parity sector occupy about 97% of J_z (≈ 6 ħ)
- Probability distribution sum rule satisfied to 99.9% up to I = 29 ħ
- Major K-component ratio W^I_{K=6}/∑W^I_K is always more than 99.99% for 6 ħ ≤ I ≤ 28 ħ
- Tilted-rotation minima reproduce experimental data with errors of roughly 400 keV

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent cranking (SCC) approach, previously applied mainly to yrast states with rotation about the x-axis, to describe high-K bands via tilted-axis cranking. It builds on the tilted-axis cranking (TAC) model developed by Frauendorf (1993, 2000), which was not fully self-consistent as it fixed deformation and gap parameters, and on the authors' previous work on ¹⁸²Hf (Oi, Walker, Ansari, 2001). The method enables microscopic, self-consistent descriptions of multi-quasiparticle high-K states and can be applied to predict properties of unknown high-K isomers in neutron-rich regions around A ≈ 190, as explored by Podolyák et al. (2000).
