# Hartree-Fock and Many-Body Perturbation Theory with Correlated Realistic NN-Interactions

> We employ correlated realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions for the description of nuclear ground states throughout the nuclear chart within the Hartree-Fock approximation. The crucial short-range central and tensor correlations, which are induced by the realistic interaction and cannot be described by the Hartree-Fock many-body state itself, are included explicitly by a state-independent unitary transformation in the framework of the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM). Using the correlated realistic interaction V_UCOM resulting from the Argonne V18 potential, bound nuclei are obtained already on the Hartree-Fock level. However, the binding energies are smaller than the experimental values because long-range correlations have not been accounted for. Their inclusion by means of many-body perturbation theory leads to a remarkable agreement with experimental binding energies over the whole mass range from He-4 to Pb-208, even far off the valley of stability. The observed perturbative character of the residual long-range correlations and the apparently small net effect of three-body forces provides promising perspectives for a unified nuclear structure description.

## Key claim

Using the correlated realistic interaction V_UCOM derived from the Argonne V18 potential via the unitary correlation operator method, bound nuclei are obtained at the Hartree-Fock level across the entire mass range from He-4 to Pb-208, but the binding energies are smaller than experimental values because long-range correlations have not been accounted for; inclusion of these residual correlations through many-body perturbation theory yields remarkable agreement with experiment, contingent on the perturbative character of the long-range correlations and the apparently small net effect of three-body forces.

## Method

The authors employed the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) to transform the bare Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential into a correlated interaction V_UCOM, using state-independent unitary operators that encode short-range central and tensor correlations. They performed Hartree-Fock calculations with this correlated interaction in a spherical harmonic-oscillator basis for closed-shell nuclei, then included residual long-range correlations via many-body perturbation theory based on the Hartree-Fock ground state. The tensor correlation functions were constrained by a range parameter I_theta = 0.09 fm^3, fixed by reproducing experimental binding energies for A <= 4 in no-core shell model calculations.

## Key numbers

* Binding energies from Hartree-Fock with V_UCOM are smaller than experimental values across the mass range from He-4 to Pb-208, but bound nuclei are obtained already at the Hartree-Fock level.
* Many-body perturbation theory leads to remarkable agreement with experimental binding energies over the whole mass range from He-4 to Pb-208, including nuclei far off the valley of stability.
* The optimal tensor correlator for the triplet-even channel uses I_theta = 0.09 fm^3, with parameters alpha = 536.67, beta = 1.2608 fm, and gamma = 1000.0 fm for the AV18 potential.
* Central correlation function parameters for the S=0, T=0 channel: alpha = 0.7971 fm, beta = 1.2638 fm, gamma = 0.4621 fm (parameterization II).
* Central correlation function parameters for the S=0, T=1 channel: alpha = 1.3793 fm, beta = 0.8853 fm, eta = 0.3724 (parameterization I).
* Central correlation function parameters for the S=1, T=0 channel: alpha = 1.3265 fm, beta = 0.8342 fm, eta = 0.4471 (parameterization I).
* Central correlation function parameters for the S=1, T=1 channel: alpha = 0.5665 fm, beta = 1.3888 fm, gamma = 0.1786 fm (parameterization II).

## Context

This work extends the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) previously applied to light and medium-mass nuclei (Roth et al., 2005; Roth et al., 2004) to the entire nuclear chart from He-4 to Pb-208. It contrasts with traditional approaches like the Brueckner G-matrix method (Day, 1967) and more recent V_low-k renormalization group methods (Bogner et al., 2003) by providing an explicit operator form of the effective interaction rather than working solely with matrix elements. The results enable a unified nuclear structure description based on realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions without explicit three-body forces, and suggest that the combination of UCOM with Hartree-Fock and perturbation theory can be extended to open-shell and exotic nuclei far from stability.
