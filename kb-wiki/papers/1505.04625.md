# Neutron skin and halo in medium and heavy nuclei within the extended Thomas-Fermi theory

> The neutron skin and halo distributions in medium and heavy nuclei are calculated within the extended Thomas-Fermi approximation. Calculations are carried out for the effective Skyrme-like forces using the direct variational method. The analytical expression for the isovector shift of the rms radii $Δr_{np}$ as a sum of skin- and halo-like terms is obtained. The contribution of halo and skin terms to $Δr_{np}$ are found to be approximately equal.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the neutron-skin thickness Δr_np can be decomposed into separate skin and halo contributions, and that for the quantum-mechanically motivated value η=4 these two contributions are approximately equal, resolving an ambiguity in describing experimental data with two-parameter Fermi distributions.

## Method

The authors use the extended Thomas-Fermi approximation (ETFA) with a direct variational method, taking trial nucleon densities as a power of the Fermi function with variational parameters ρ_0,q, R_q, a_q, and ξ. The total energy of the nucleus, including Skyrme effective interactions (parametrizations SIII, SkM, SLy230b) and Coulomb energy, is minimized subject to fixed neutron and proton numbers. The resulting densities are used to compute root-mean-square radii and the isovector shift Δr_np, which is analytically separated into skin and halo terms within the leptodermous approximation (a/R ≪ 1).

## Key numbers

- For η=4, the skin and halo partial contributions to Δr_np are approximately equal (each about 50%), whereas for η=2 the skin contribution is near 100% and the halo contribution near 0%.
- The leptodermous parameter a/R does not exceed 0.1 for nuclei with mass numbers A from 90 to 238.
- The calculated values of the partial contributions change by about 20% for different Skyrme force parametrizations (SIII, SkM, SLy230b) in 120Sn.
- The calculation with η=4 gives a better description of experimental Δr_np data than η=2 for both the asymmetry parameter X=(N-Z)/A and for tin isotopes as a function of A.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of Waviroce (2010) on the ambiguity in describing experimental data with two-parameter Fermi distributions having different combinations of R_n-R_p and a_n-a_p. It provides an analytical decomposition of the neutron-skin thickness into skin and halo terms within the ETFA framework, building on the direct variational method of Kosa (2008). The results enable a more precise interpretation of experimental neutron-skin data from Trzaska (2001) and clarify the role of the semiclassical parameter η in determining the relative importance of skin versus halo effects.
