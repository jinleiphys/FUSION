# Chemical Equilibration in Pb+Pb Collisions at the SPS

> An improved statistical model with excluded volume corrections and resonance decays is introduced and applied to the complete presently available set of particle ratios as measured by the various experiments at the SPS in Pb+Pb collisions. The results imply that a high degree of hadrochemical equilibration is reached at chemical freeze-out in Pb+Pb collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a high degree of hadrochemical equilibration is reached at chemical freeze-out in Pb+Pb collisions at the SPS, but this conclusion depends on using a specific excluded volume correction with baryon and meson radii of 0.3 fm; using larger radii (0.8 fm for baryons, 0.62 fm for mesons) yields much poorer agreement with data.

## Method

The authors applied a grand-canonical statistical model with excluded volume corrections and resonance decays to the complete set of available particle ratios from SPS Pb+Pb collisions. They adjusted the temperature T and baryochemical potential μB to minimize χ² between model predictions and experimental data for 18 particle ratios, while fixing the strangeness chemical potential μS and isospin chemical potential μI3 by conservation laws for baryon number, strangeness, and charge. The hadronic mass spectrum included all mesons below 1.5 GeV and baryons below 2 GeV, with an eigenvolume of 0.3 fm radius assigned to all hadrons.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit temperature: T = 168 ± 2.4 MeV, baryochemical potential: μB = 266 ± 5 MeV (from χ² minimization)
- Minimal quadratic deviation found at T = 164 MeV, μB = 274 MeV
- Fireball volume at chemical freeze-out: 2800 fm³ (with R=0.3 fm)
- Pion density at chemical freeze-out: 0.60 pions/fm³ (at T=168 MeV)
- Pion density after isentropic expansion to 125 MeV: 0.084 pions/fm³
- Mean ratio of experimental to calculated yields for ΔS=1 ratios: 0.96 ± 0.05
- For AGS data (same model): T = 125 (+3 - 6) MeV, μB = 540 ± 7 MeV
- At AGS: π⁺ density 0.051/fm³, proton density 0.053/fm³

## Context

This work extends the statistical model approach previously applied to AGS data by Braun-Munzinger et al. (1995) and SPS data by Braun-Munzinger et al. (1996), now incorporating excluded volume corrections and a more complete set of particle ratios including multi-strange baryons. The results contradict the findings of Becattini, Gazdzicki, and Sollfrank (1998), who required a separate strangeness suppression factor using a more restricted set of ratios. The extracted freeze-out parameters lie close to the predicted phase boundary between hadronic matter and quark-gluon plasma, suggesting the system crosses this boundary shortly before hadrochemical freeze-out, and enables future studies of the QCD phase diagram across different collision energies.
