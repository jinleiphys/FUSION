# Spectral Properties of Three-Dimensional Quantum Billiards with a Pointlike Scatterer

> We examine the spectral properties of three-dimensional quantum billiards with a single pointlike scatterer inside. It is found that the spectrum shows chaotic (random-matrix-like) characteristics when the inverse of the formal strength $\bar{v}^{-1}$ is within a band whose width increases parabolically as a function of the energy. This implies that the spectrum becomes random-matrix-like at very high energy irrespective to the value of the formal strength. The predictions are confirmed by numerical experiments with a rectangular box. The findings for a pointlike scatterer are applied to the case for a small but finite-size impurity. We clarify the proper procedure for its zero-size limit which involves non-trivial divergence. The previously known results in one and two-dimensional quantum billiards with small impurities inside are also reviewed from the present perspective.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in three-dimensional quantum billiards with a pointlike scatterer, the spectrum shows chaotic (random-matrix-like) characteristics when the inverse of the formal strength \(\bar{v}^{-1}\) lies within a band whose width increases as the square root of the energy, implying that at very high energy the spectrum becomes random-matrix-like for any nonzero formal strength, a sharp contrast to the two-dimensional case where the system exhibits asymptotic freedom.

## Method

The authors derived the eigenvalue equation for a three-dimensional billiard with a pointlike scatterer using self-adjoint extension theory, obtaining \(\bar{G}(z) = \bar{v}^{-1}\) where \(\bar{G}(z)\) is a renormalized sum over unperturbed eigenstates. They analytically estimated the inflection points of \(\bar{G}(z)\) and the width of the strong-coupling region by replacing the sum with a principal integral using the average level density \(\rho_{av}(z) = (M^{3/2}V / 2^{1/2}\pi^2) \sqrt{z}\). They then related the formal strength \(\bar{v}\) to the bare strength \(v = U_0 \Omega\) of a finite-size scatterer of volume \(\Omega\) and potential height \(U_0\), clarifying the zero-size limit procedure. Numerical experiments with a rectangular box confirmed the predictions.

## Key numbers

- Average level density in three dimensions: \(\rho_{av}(z) = \frac{M^{3/2}V}{2^{1/2}\pi^2}\sqrt{z}\).
- Strong coupling condition for formal strength: \(\bar{v}^{-1} \simeq -\frac{M^{3/2}\Lambda^{1/2}}{2\pi}\), independent of energy.
- Width of the strong-coupling band: \(\Delta(z) \simeq \frac{M^{3/2}}{2^{3/2}}\sqrt{z}\), proportional to the square root of energy.
- For a finite-size scatterer of volume \(\Omega\), the strong coupling condition at low energy is \(\left| v^{-1} + \frac{2^{1/2}M}{\pi^2\Omega^{1/3}} \right| \alt \frac{M^{3/2}}{2^{3/2}}\sqrt{z}\), where \(v = U_0 \Omega\).
- In two dimensions, the width \(\Delta\) is energy-independent: \(\Delta \simeq \frac{\pi M}{2}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of two-dimensional quantum billiards with pointlike scatterers (Shigehara 1994, Shigehara and Cheon 1996), where chaotic spectra appear along a "logarithmic strip" due to scale anomaly and asymptotic freedom. The three-dimensional case is fundamentally different because the average level density grows as \(\sqrt{z}\), causing the strong-coupling band to broaden with energy rather than remain logarithmic. The paper also clarifies the proper zero-size limit of a finite-range impurity, showing that in three dimensions the bare strength must scale as \(v(\Omega) \sim -C/\Omega^{1/3}\) to yield a well-defined pointlike scatterer, and defines an effective strength \(v_{eff} = 1/r(0)\) that directly measures coupling. This enables future studies of realistic small impurities in three-dimensional quantum dots and mesoscopic systems.
