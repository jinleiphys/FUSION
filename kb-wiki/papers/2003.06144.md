# TDHF and a macroscopic aspect of low-energy nuclear reactions

> Time-dependent Hartree--Fock (TDHF) method has been applied to various low-energy nuclear reactions, such as fusion, fission, and multinucleon transfer reactions. In this Mini Review, we summarize recent attempts to bridge a microscopic nuclear reaction theory, TDHF, and a macroscopic aspect of nuclear reactions through nucleus--nucleus potentials and energy dissipation from macroscopic degrees of freedom to microscopic ones obtained from TDHF in various colliding systems from light to heavy mass regions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the macroscopic nucleus–nucleus potential and energy dissipation extracted from TDHF via the DD-TDHF method can explain both the dynamical reduction of the fusion barrier in light-to-medium systems and the fusion hindrance in heavy systems, but the authors note that the extracted potential in heavy systems differs significantly from that obtained by the density-constrained TDHF (DC-TDHF) method due to different interpretations of internal excitation energy.

## Method

The authors apply the dissipative-dynamics TDHF (DD-TDHF) method, which maps microscopic TDHF time evolution onto a one-dimensional macroscopic equation of motion for the relative distance between colliding nuclei. They extract the nucleus–nucleus potential V(R) and friction coefficient γ(R) by solving two equations obtained from TDHF trajectories at slightly different incident energies, using the SLy4d Skyrme energy density functional without pairing. For off-central collisions, they extend the method to extract both radial and tangential friction coefficients from a single TDHF trajectory.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁰Ca + ⁴⁰Ca, the Coulomb barrier height decreases from ≈ 54.5 MeV at E_c.m. = 90, 100 MeV (and in the frozen-density approximation) to ≈ 53.4 MeV at E_c.m. = 55 MeV from DD-TDHF.
- The charge product threshold for fusion hindrance is Z₁Z₂ ≥ 1600, above which quasifission competes with compound nucleus formation.
- For ⁹⁰,⁹²,⁹⁴,⁹⁶Zr + ¹²⁴Sn, the difference between the frozen-density Coulomb barrier V_B^FD and the Bass barrier V_Bass is at most ≈ 1 MeV.
- For ¹⁶O + ¹⁶O at E/V_B = 1.4, the DD-TDHF method yields nearly identical nucleus–nucleus potentials across impact parameters, and the reduced radial and tangential friction coefficients show no significant dependence on impact parameter.

## Context

This Mini Review extends the DD-TDHF approach developed by Washiyama et al. (2008, 2009, 2015) to bridge microscopic TDHF dynamics with macroscopic reaction models. It contrasts the DD-TDHF potential with the density-constrained TDHF (DC-TDHF) method of Umar et al. (2006, 2009, 2010, 2014), noting that in heavy systems the two methods give different potentials because DD-TDHF includes part of the internal excitation energy as potential energy. The work enables systematic studies of angular momentum dissipation in off-central collisions and future investigations of orientation effects in reactions with deformed nuclei.
