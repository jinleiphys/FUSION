# Confronting lattice parton distributions with global QCD analysis

> We present the first Monte Carlo based global QCD analysis of spin-averaged and spin-dependent parton distribution functions (PDFs) that includes nucleon isovector matrix elements in coordinate space from lattice QCD. We investigate the degree of universality of the extracted PDFs when the lattice and experimental data are treated under the same conditions within the Bayesian likelihood analysis. For the unpolarized sector, we find rather weak constraints from the current lattice data on the phenomenological PDFs, and difficulties in describing the lattice matrix elements at large spatial distances. In contrast, for the polarized PDFs we find good agreement between experiment and lattice data, with the latter providing significant constraints on the spin-dependent isovector quark and antiquark distributions.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first Monte Carlo based global QCD analysis that simultaneously fits spin-averaged and spin-dependent parton distribution functions (PDFs) to both experimental data and lattice QCD matrix elements in coordinate space, finding that the current lattice data provide weak constraints on unpolarized PDFs but significant constraints on polarized PDFs, with the caveat that the lattice data alone cannot constrain PDFs without experimental input due to limited kinematic coverage.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian likelihood analysis using the Jefferson Lab Angular Momentum (JAM) Collaboration framework, parametrizing PDFs at an input scale and solving DGLAP evolution equations in Mellin space. They directly matched lattice QCD-calculated nucleon matrix elements of nonlocal operators in coordinate space to light-cone PDFs using perturbative matching kernels at next-to-leading order, treating the lattice data on the same footing as experimental observables from inclusive deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan production. The analysis used Monte Carlo sampling of the posterior distribution with data resampling, fitting 27 free parameters for unpolarized PDFs and 31 for polarized PDFs.

## Key numbers

- Total of 2,930 experimental data points (unpolarized DIS and Drell-Yan) plus 61 lattice data points from ETMC19 were used in the combined fit
- The experiment-only fit gave chi-squared per datum of approximately 1.3 for unpolarized PDFs
- The combined experiment + lattice fit gave chi-squared per datum of 1.6 for unpolarized PDFs
- For the lattice data alone, chi-squared per datum values were 4.7 for the real part and 22.7 for the imaginary part of the isovector unpolarized matrix element
- The lattice calculation used a lattice volume of 48^3 x 96 with lattice spacing a = 0.0938(2)(3) fm, spatial extent L approximately 4.5 fm, and pion mass m_pi L = 2.98
- The lattice data used the largest momentum P_3 = 1.38 GeV, obtained with 72,990 measurements
- Source-sink separations ranged from t_s = 0.75 to 1.13 fm (t_s/a = 8, 9, 10, 12)

## Context

This work extends the JAM Collaboration global QCD analyses (JAM15, JAM17, JAM19) by incorporating lattice QCD data from the ETM Collaboration (Alexandrou 2018) into a simultaneous fit with experimental data, using the direct matching approach in coordinate space rather than the traditional discrete Fourier transform method. The analysis enables future investigations of universality between lattice and experimental determinations of PDFs, and identifies that current lattice data at large spatial distances are difficult to describe simultaneously with experimental data for unpolarized PDFs, while polarized PDFs show better compatibility. A related analysis within the NNPDF framework using lattice data was performed by Cichy (2019).
