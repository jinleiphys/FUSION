# Azimuthal correlations of forward di-hadrons in d+Au collisions at RHIC in the Color Glass Condensate

> We present a good description of recent experimental data on forward di-hadron azimuthal correlations measured in deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC, where monojet production has been observed. Our approach is based on the Color Glass Condensate effective theory for the small-x degrees of freedom of the nuclear wave function, including the use of non-linear evolution equations with running QCD coupling. Our analysis provides further evidence for the presence of saturation effects in RHIC data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the suppression of the away-side peak in forward di-hadron azimuthal correlations in d+Au collisions at RHIC is quantitatively described by the Color Glass Condensate effective theory, but only under the condition that the initial saturation scale and rcBK evolution parameters are taken directly from a previous analysis of single-inclusive hadron production, making the di-hadron calculation parameter-free.

## Method

The authors compute the double-inclusive hadron production cross section in proton-nucleus collisions using the CGC framework, where the proton is treated with collinear parton distribution functions (CTEQ6 NLO) and the nucleus is described by an unintegrated gluon distribution evolved via the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation with running coupling corrections (rcBK). The calculation involves a quark from the proton splitting into a quark-gluon pair, which then scatters off the saturated small-x gluon field of the nucleus, with the outgoing partons fragmenting independently into hadrons using KKP NLO fragmentation functions. The initial condition for the rcBK evolution is taken from a previous analysis of single-inclusive forward hadron production in d+Au collisions, with the starting point at x0=0.02 and an initial saturation scale for quarks of 0.4 GeV^2.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: sqrt(s)=200 GeV for d+Au collisions at RHIC
- Kinematic range probed: x_p ~ 0.4 and x_A ~ 10^-3 for forward rapidities y_i ~ 3
- Initial saturation scale for quarks in the nucleus: Qbar_s0^2 = 0.4 GeV^2 at x0=0.02, corresponding to impact parameter b=5.47 fm
- Trigger hadron transverse momentum: |p_1_perp| > 2 GeV; associated hadron: 1 GeV < |p_2_perp| < |p_1_perp|
- Rapidity range for both hadrons: 2.4 < y_1,2 < 4
- Initial saturation scale for the proton: Q_0^2 = 0.2 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends previous CGC-based calculations of single-inclusive forward hadron production in d+Au collisions (Albacete 2010) to the more exclusive observable of di-hadron azimuthal correlations, providing a parameter-free prediction. It contradicts alternative explanations that invoke energy loss effects (alternatives) to explain the suppression of forward hadron yields, and it quantitatively confirms qualitative predictions that monojet production would result from saturation effects (Kharzeev 2004, Marquet 2007). The analysis enables further tests of saturation physics at RHIC and provides a baseline for interpreting similar measurements at the LHC.
