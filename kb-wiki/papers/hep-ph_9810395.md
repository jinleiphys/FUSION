# A coupled channel unitary chiral approach to the meson-meson interaction and pion-pion scattering in a nuclear medium

> A new tool which combines chiral perturbation theory and unitarity in coupled channels is applied with success to the study of the meson-meson interaction, extending the theoretical predictions up to sqrt{s} = 1.2 GeV, hence improving considerably the convergence radius of conventional chiral perturbation theory. The method is applied to obtain the meson-meson phase shifts and inelasticities as well as the isoscalar pion-pion scattering amplitude inside a nuclear medium.

# A coupled channel unitary chiral approach to the meson-meson interaction and pion-pion scattering in a nuclear medium

## Key claim
The authors present a method combining chiral perturbation theory with unitarity in coupled channels that extends the convergence radius of conventional chiral perturbation theory up to sqrt(s) = 1.2 GeV, but the approach relies on fitting low-energy constants to data rather than predicting them from first principles, and the simpler option (c) works only for the scalar sector (J=0) with a specific cutoff around 1 GeV.

## Method
The method starts from unitarity in coupled channels expressed via the K-matrix, then expands Re T^{-1} in powers of p^2 analogously to the effective range formula in quantum mechanics. Three variants are presented: (a) full one-loop calculation of T_4 with s, t, u channels and tadpoles; (b) omission of crossed loops and tadpoles with their effects reabsorbed into fitted L_i coefficients; (c) a Bethe-Salpeter-like equation where T_2 serves as a potential and the T_4^p polynomial contribution is made negligible by a suitable cutoff. The meson-meson states considered are K bar-K, pi-pi, eta-eta, pi-eta, pi-K, pi-bar-K, eta-K and eta-bar-K.

## Key numbers
- The method extends theoretical predictions up to sqrt(s) = 1.2 GeV, improving the convergence radius of conventional chiral perturbation theory.
- For option (c), the cutoff needed for a good description of scalar data is around 1 GeV.
- The fit in option (b) uses 7 parameters (L_i coefficients, with L_6 and L_8 appearing only in the combination 2 L_6 + L_8).
- The sigma resonance in I=0 appears around 500 MeV.
- Results for Im T_{00} in J=I=0 pi-pi scattering are shown for different values of the Fermi momentum k_F (labels correspond to k_F values in MeV).

## Context
This work extends the inverse amplitude method of earlier studies to coupled channels, building on the chiral Lagrangians of Gasser and Leutwyler (1984). The approach reproduces all meson resonances below 1.2 GeV, including the sigma, f_0(980), rho, K*, and a_0, which standard chiral perturbation theory cannot obtain. The application to pi-pi scattering in a nuclear medium follows earlier work by the same group and others (1990s) on pion Cooper pairs and chiral constraints, and the enhanced strength near threshold could explain the increased pi+ pi- invariant mass distribution observed in (pi, 2pi) reactions in nuclei.
