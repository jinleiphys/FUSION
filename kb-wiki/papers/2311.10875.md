# Exploration of hadronization through heavy flavor production at the future Electron-Ion Collider

> The future Electron-Ion Collider will utilize high-luminosity high-energy electron+proton ($e+p$) and electron+nucleus ($e+A$) collisions to solve several fundamental questions in the high energy nuclear physics field. Heavy flavor products play an important role in constraining the initial-state nucleon/nucleus parton distribution functions especially in the high and low Bjorken-x ($x_{BJ}$) region and exploring the final-state parton propagation and hadronization processes under different nuclear medium conditions. Latest simulation studies of heavy flavor hadron and jet measurements with the EIC project detector conceptual design will be discussed. The projected statistical accuracy of heavy flavor jet and heavy flavor hadron inside jet measurements in comparison with latest theoretical calculations will be presented.

## Key claim

The paper claims that simulation studies with the current ePIC detector conceptual design validate the capability to reconstruct charm and bottom hadrons and jets at the future Electron-Ion Collider, but this capability is demonstrated specifically for 63.2 GeV electron+proton collisions with an integrated luminosity of 10 fb^-1, and the projected statistical accuracy depends on the specific kinematic selections and detector performance assumptions used in the standalone simulation framework.

## Method

The authors used a standalone simulation framework incorporating PYTHIA 8 for event generation, a particle smearing package with ePIC detector performance evaluated in GEANT4, and beam remnant background to reconstruct heavy flavor hadrons and jets in electron+proton collisions. Jets were reconstructed using the anti-k_T algorithm with a cone radius of 1.0, and jet flavor was tagged by identifying the associated hadron flavor with the most displaced secondary vertex inside the jet. Charm di-jet correlations and heavy flavor hadron inside jet measurements were evaluated with specific transverse momentum and pseudorapidity selection cuts to reduce background and isolate initial-state and final-state effects.

## Key numbers

- Reconstructed D± mesons (D± → K∓π±π±) and B± mesons (B± → J/ψ (→ l+l-) + K±) were simulated in 10 GeV electron and 100 GeV proton collisions (center-of-mass energy 63.2 GeV)
- Charm di-jet reconstruction required minimum jet pT > 5 GeV/c, leading charm jet pT > 7 GeV/c, sub-leading charm jet pT > 5 GeV/c, pseudorapidity within -2.0 < η < 3.5, and back-to-back region Δφ1,2 > 2π/3
- Reconstructed D0 (D0̅) inside jet and Λc± inside jet were studied with reconstructed jet pT within 3-5 GeV/c and reconstructed charm hadron pT within 2-3 GeV/c, all within -2 < η < 2 pseudorapidity region
- Projected accuracy of hadron momentum fraction z_proj,D0 dependent nuclear modification factor R_eAu was evaluated with 10 fb^-1 e+p integrated luminosity and 500 pb^-1 e+Au integrated luminosity
- The e+p and e+Au collisions were simulated at center-of-mass energy 63.2 GeV

## Context

This work extends simulation studies for the future Electron-Ion Collider, which will begin construction in 2025 at Brookhaven National Laboratory, operating at center-of-mass energies of 29-141 GeV. The paper builds on previous heavy flavor measurements at RHIC and LHC that challenged the universality of charm quark fragmentation functions, particularly through Λc over D0 ratio measurements in p+p and A+A collisions. The results enable future differential analyses mapping charm quark hadronization under different cold nuclear medium conditions by comparing e+p and e+A collisions with mass numbers from 2 to 238, and will help constrain heavy quark fragmentation functions in the little-known high hadron momentum fraction region using Next-to-Leading Order theoretical calculations based on the parton energy loss model.
