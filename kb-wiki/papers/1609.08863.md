# Tidal deformability of neutron and hyperon star with relativistic mean field equations of state

> We systematically study the tidal deformability for neutron and hyperon stars using relativistic mean field (RMF) equations of state (EOSs). The tidal effect plays an important role during the early part of the evolution of compact binaries. Although, the deformability associated with the EOSs has a small correction, it gives a clean gravitational wave signature in binary inspiral. These are characterized by various love numbers kl (l=2, 3, 4), that depend on the EOS of a star for a given mass and radius. The tidal effect of star could be efficiently measured through advanced LIGO detector from the final stages of inspiraling binary neutron star (BNS) merger.

## Key claim
The tidal deformability of neutron and hyperon stars is systematically studied using relativistic mean field (RMF) equations of state, showing that while the deformability associated with the EOSs gives a small correction, it provides a clean gravitational wave signature in binary inspiral that could be measured through advanced LIGO.

## Method
The authors solved the Tolmann-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations using four RMF parameter sets (NL3, G2, FSUGold, and FSUGold2) to obtain mass-radius relations for both neutron stars and hyperon stars. They then computed the tidal Love numbers k_l (l=2, 3, 4) by solving a first-order differential equation for y(r) with boundary condition y(0)=l, and derived the tidal deformability parameter λ from the Love numbers and stellar radii. Hyperon couplings were taken as χ_σ = 0.7 and χ_ω = χ_ρ = 0.783 from the quark model.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass of G2 nucleonic matter compact star: 1.99 M_⊙ with radius 11.25 km
- NL3 neutron star maximum mass: 2.81 M_⊙; with hyperons reduced to ~2.25 M_⊙
- FSUGold2 neutron star: mass 2.12 M_⊙, radius 12.12 km; FSUGold: mass 1.75 M_⊙, radius 10.76 km
- For a 1.4 M_⊙ neutron star, radii range from 12.236 km (FSUGold) to 14.422 km (NL3)
- Tidal deformability λ for 1.4 M_⊙ neutron star: NL3 = 7.466 × 10^36 g cm^2 s^2; FSUGold = 2.418 × 10^36 g cm^2 s^2
- Tidal deformability λ for 1.4 M_⊙ hyperon star: NL3 = 7.527 × 10^36 g cm^2 s^2; G2 = 2.641 × 10^36 g cm^2 s^2
- Dimensionless tidal deformability Λ for 1.4 M_⊙ neutron star: NL3 = 1288.81; FSUGold = 414.13
- NL3 quadrupole Love number k_2 for 1.4 M_⊙ neutron star: 0.1197; for hyperon star: 0.1203

## Context
This work extends the tidal deformability formalism developed by Flanagan and Hinderer (2008) and Hinderer (2008, 2009) to multiple RMF parameter sets including the newer FSUGold2 and G2 forces, and systematically compares neutron stars with hyperon stars. The results are relevant for interpreting gravitational wave signals from binary neutron star mergers expected with advanced LIGO, VIRGO, and KAGRA detectors, and the predicted tidal deformabilities fall within the range 0.6 to 6 × 10^36 g cm^2 s^2 for 1.4 M_⊙ stars estimated by Steiner et al. (2015) with 95% confidence. The study enables constraints on the high-density equation of state from future gravitational wave measurements, though the authors note that most hyperon coupling sets used cannot reproduce the observed ~2 M_⊙ neutron stars PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432.
