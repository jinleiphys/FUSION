# Symplectic symmetry approach to clustering in atomic nuclei: The case of $^{24}$Mg

> Symplectic symmetry approach to clustering (SSAC) in atomic nuclei, recently proposed, is modified and further developed in more detail. It is firstly applied to the light two-cluster $^{20}$Ne + $α$ system of $^{24}$Mg, the latter exhibiting well developed low-energy $K^π = 0^{+}_{1}$, $K^π = 2^{+}_{1}$ and $K^π = 0^{-}_{1}$ rotational bands in its spectrum. A simple algebraic Hamiltonian, consisting of dynamical symmetry, residual and vertical mixing parts is used to describe these three lowest rotational bands of positive and negative parity in $^{24}$Mg. A good description of the excitation energies is obtain by considering only the $SU(3)$ cluster states restricted to the stretched many-particle Hilbert subspace, built on the leading Pauli allowed $SU(3)$ multiplet for the positive- and negative-parity states, respectively. The coupling to the higher cluster-model configurations allows to describe the known low-lying experimentally observed $B(E2)$ transition probabilities within and between the cluster states of the three bands under consideration without the use of an effective charge.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the symplectic symmetry approach to clustering (SSAC) can describe the three lowest rotational bands of positive and negative parity in \(^{24}\)Mg using only the leading Pauli-allowed \(SU(3)\) multiplet for each parity, with the caveat that the coupling to higher cluster-model configurations is required to reproduce the observed \(B(E2)\) transition probabilities without an effective charge.

## Method

The authors constructed an algebraic Hamiltonian with dynamical symmetry, residual, and vertical mixing parts, expressed in terms of generators of the \(Sp(6,R)_R \otimes Sp(6,R)_C\) group substructure. They restricted the model space to the stretched many-particle Hilbert subspace built on the leading Pauli-allowed \(SU(3)\) multiplet for positive-parity states \((8,4)\) and negative-parity states, using the \(^{20}\)Ne + \(\alpha\) two-cluster channel for \(^{24}\)Mg. The \(E2\) transition operator was defined using the full symplectic quadrupole generators of both the intercluster (\(Sp(6,R)_R\)) and internal cluster (\(Sp(6,R)_C\)) excitations, with bare electric charge and the factor \(\sqrt{5/16\pi}\) to match experimental Weisskopf units.

## Key numbers

- The leading Pauli-allowed \(SU(3)\) irrep for the \(^{20}\)Ne cluster ground state is \((8,0)\), and for the combined \(^{20}\)Ne + \(\alpha\) system with \(E_R = 8\), the Pauli-allowed \(SU(3)\) states are \((8,4)\), \((4,6)\), and \((0,8)\).
- The Wildermuth condition requires a minimum of \(E_R = 8\) oscillator quanta for the intercluster excitations in the \(^{20}\)Ne + \(\alpha\) system.
- For a pure \(SU(3)\) structure \((8,4)\), the \(B(E2)\) transition probability produced without the factor \(\sqrt{5/16\pi}\) is 124.7 W.u., compared to the experimental value of 20.3 W.u.
- The Weisskopf unit factor for \(A = 20-24\) is approximately 2.2, while for \(A \approx 100\) it is about 0.8.

## Context

This work extends the symplectic symmetry approach to clustering (SSAC) previously proposed by Ganev (2021) for the \(^{16}\)O + \(\alpha\) system, now applied to the \(^{20}\)Ne + \(\alpha\) system where one cluster has a non-scalar \(U_C(3)\) representation. It contrasts with prior approaches such as the semimicroscopic algebraic cluster model (SACM) and the no-core symplectic model (NCSpM) used by Launey (2016) and Dytrych (2013), which compute overlaps between symplectic and cluster bases; here the cluster wave functions are computed directly using the symplectic symmetry. The results enable future applications of SSAC to other two-cluster systems and potentially to multi-cluster configurations.
