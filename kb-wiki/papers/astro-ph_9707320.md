# The Proton-Proton Reaction, Solar Neutrinos, and a Relativistic Field Theoretic Model of the Deuteron

> In a series of recent papers, Ivanov et al. and Oberhummer et al. have calculated the rate for the $p + p \to d + e^+ + ν_e$ reaction with a zero-range four-fermion effective interaction and find a result 2.9 times higher than the standard value calculated from non-relativistic potential theory. Their procedure is shown to give a wrong answer because their assumed interaction disagrees with low-energy $pp$ scattering data.

# The Proton-Proton Reaction, Solar Neutrinos, and a Relativistic Field Theoretic Model of the Deuteron

## Key claim
The authors show that the calculation by Ivanov et al. and Oberhummer et al., which found a rate for the p + p → d + e⁺ + νₑ reaction 2.9 times higher than the standard value, is incorrect because their assumed zero-range four-fermion effective interaction disagrees with low-energy pp scattering data. The error arises specifically from using an effective range ρₚ = 0, which is inconsistent with the experimentally measured value.

## Method
The authors compare the effective-range approximation, which uses experimentally measured pp scattering parameters (scattering length and effective range) and deuteron properties, against the relativistic field-theoretic model of Ivanov et al. They demonstrate that the Ivanov et al. Lagrangian gives a delta-function potential with effective range ρₚ = 0, and then use the effective-range approximation to show how incorrect input values for the scattering length and effective range propagate into order-unity errors in the pp reaction cross section.

## Key numbers
- The experimentally measured pp scattering length is aₚ = −7.8196(26) fm, and the effective range is ρₚ = 2.790(14) fm (Bergervoet et al.).
- Ivanov et al. used a scattering length aₚ = −17.1 fm, which is approximately twice the measured value.
- If the pp effective range were zero (with all other parameters fixed at experimental values), the cross section would be about 1.5 times higher than the canonical value.
- If additionally the scattering length were twice the measured value (−17.1 fm instead of −7.8196 fm), the cross section would be 3 times larger than the standard result.

## Context
This paper refutes the claims of Ivanov et al. and Oberhummer et al. (both 1997) who used a relativistic field-theoretic model with a zero-range four-fermion interaction to calculate the pp reaction rate. The work extends the long history of calculations beginning with Bethe and Critchfield (1938) and continuing through Salpeter (1952), Bahcall and May (1968), and Kamionkowski and Bahcall (1994), all of which found consistent cross sections within a few percent. The authors note that Degl'Innocenti et al. (1997) independently pointed out that a factor-2.9 enhancement would contradict helioseismological data. The paper enables future work by reaffirming that any calculation consistent with measured low-energy pp scattering and deuteron properties will yield a cross section differing from the best estimate by no more than a few percent.
