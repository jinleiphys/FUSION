# Centre Vortex Structure of QCD-Vacuum Fields and Confinement

> The non-trivial ground-state vacuum fields of QCD form the foundation of matter. Using modern visualisation techniques, this presentation examines the microscopic structure of these fields. Of particular interest are the centre vortices identified within the ground-state fields of lattice QCD. Our current focus is on understanding the manner in which dynamical fermions in the QCD vacuum alter the centre-vortex structure. The impact of dynamical fermions is significant and provides new insights into the role of centre vortices in underpinning both confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in QCD.

## Key claim

The introduction of dynamical fermions in QCD significantly alters the centre-vortex structure of ground-state vacuum fields, bringing the vortex-only string tension into excellent agreement with the full string tension, unlike in the pure-gauge sector where the vortex-only string tension is only about 60% of the full value.

## Method

The authors identify centre vortices by first gauge-fixing lattice configurations to Maximal Centre Gauge and then projecting link variables to the centre elements of SU(3). They compare centre-vortex structures from a pure-gauge ensemble with those from a PACS-CS (2+1)-flavour full-QCD ensemble, using 3D visualisation techniques to render the flow of centre charge. The static quark potential is extracted from Wilson loops via variational analysis, and the vortex-only and vortex-removed ensembles are analysed alongside the original untouched configurations.

## Key numbers

- Average number of vortices composing the primary cluster in 32^2 x 64 spatial slices: 3,277 ± 156 in pure gauge theory versus 5,924 ± 239 in full QCD, from an ensemble of 200 configurations.
- The pure-gauge ensemble uses a 32^3 x 64 lattice with Iwasaki gauge action at β = 2.58, giving a lattice spacing of a = 0.100 fm.
- The dynamical-fermion ensemble uses a 32^3 x 64 lattice with β = 1.90, a pion mass of 156 MeV, and a lattice spacing of a = 0.0933 fm.
- The vortex-only string tension in pure gauge theory is only about 60% of the original string tension.
- For dynamical fermions with a pion mass of 701 MeV, the vortex-only string tension is in excellent agreement with the original untouched ensemble at large distances.

## Context

This work extends previous studies showing that centre vortices in pure Yang-Mills theory produce a linear static quark potential and infrared enhancement in the gluon propagator (Langfeld 2003, Biddle 2018), but with the vortex-only string tension consistently at about 62% of the full value. The authors build on the finding that removal of centre vortices leads to loss of dynamical mass generation and restoration of chiral symmetry (Trewartha 2015, 2017). The new results show that dynamical fermions bring centre-vortex phenomenology much closer to a perfect encapsulation of QCD's nonperturbative features, enabling quantitative studies of confinement and chiral symmetry breaking within the centre-vortex framework.
