# Effective field theory for vibrations in odd-mass nuclei

> Heavy even-even nuclei exhibit low-energy collective excitations that are separated in scale from the microscopic (fermion) degrees of freedom. This separation of scale allows us to approach nuclear vibrations within an effective field theory (EFT). In odd-mass nuclei collective and single-particle properties compete at low energies, and this makes their description more challenging. In this article we describe odd-mass nuclei with ground-state spin $I=\sfrac{1}{2}$ by means of an EFT that couples a fermion to the collective degrees of freedom of an even-even core. The EFT relates observables such as energy levels, electric quadrupole ($E2$) transition strengths, and magnetic dipole ($M1$) moments of the odd-mass nucleus to those of its even-even neighbor, and allows us to quantify theoretical uncertainties. For isotopes of rhodium and silver the theoretical description is consistent with data within experimental and theoretical uncertainties. Several testable predictions are made.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an effective field theory (EFT) for odd-mass nuclei with ground-state spin I=1/2 by coupling a fermion to the collective quadrupole vibrations of an even-even core, and shows that the description is consistent with data for isotopes of rhodium and silver within experimental and theoretical uncertainties, but only for nuclei where the odd fermion occupies a j=1/2 orbital and the positive-parity low-lying states can be neglected.

## Method

The authors couple a fermion in a j=1/2 orbital to the quadrupole phonon degrees of freedom of an even-even vibrational nucleus using an EFT with a power counting in the small parameter ω/Λ ≈ 1/3, where ω ≈ 0.6 MeV is the vibrational frequency and Λ ≈ 2-3 MeV is the breakdown scale from fermionic degrees of freedom. They construct the Hamiltonian up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) including boson-fermion interactions such as a Coriolis-like term g_{Jj} J·j and a phonon-number-fermion coupling ω₂ N n, and fit the low-energy constants to energy levels of even-even and odd-mass neighbors simultaneously. The EFT also constructs electric quadrupole (E2) and magnetic dipole (M1) operators and uses Bayesian methods with log-normal priors to quantify theoretical uncertainties.

## Context

This work extends the boson EFT for nuclear vibrations in even-even nuclei developed by Coello Perez and Papenbrock (2015) to odd-mass nuclei, building on the separation of scales between collective vibrations (≈0.6 MeV) and fermionic excitations (≈2-3 MeV). It provides a systematically improvable alternative to traditional particle-vibrator and boson-fermion models (Iachello 1979, Vervier 1982) that lacked uncertainty quantification, and enables testable predictions for E2 and M1 observables in rhodium and silver isotopes that can be compared with recent g-factor measurements (Chamoli 2011, Stuchbery 2016).
