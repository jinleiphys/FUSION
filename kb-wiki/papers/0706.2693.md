# Reaction cross sections for proton scattering from stable and unstable nuclei based on a microscopic approach

> Microscopic optical model potential results for reaction cross sections of proton elastic scattering are presented. The applications cover the 10-1000 MeV energy range and consider both stable and unstable nuclei. The study is based on in-medium g-matrix full-folding optical model approach with the appropriate relativistic kinematic corrections needed for the higher energy applications. The effective interactions are based on realistic NN potentials supplemented with a separable non-Hermitian term to allow optimum agreement with current NN phase-shift analyzes, particularly the inelasticities above pion production threshold. The target ground-state densities are obtained from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations based on the finite range, density dependent Gogny force. The evaluated reaction cross sections for proton scattering are compared with measurements and their systematics is analyzed. A simple function of the total cross sections in terms of the atomic mass number is observed at high energies. At low energies, however, discrepancies with the available data are observed, being more pronounced in the lighter systems.

## Key claim

The microscopic full-folding optical model approach, using a nuclear-matter g-matrix based on the Argonne V18 potential with a separable non-Hermitian term to match the SP00 phase-shift analysis, provides a reasonable description of proton reaction cross sections above 200 MeV but shows systematic discrepancies below 100 MeV, particularly for light nuclei like 12C and 16O where differences of approximately 100 mb are observed at 30 MeV.

## Method

The authors constructed a nonlocal optical potential by folding a density-dependent, in-medium g-matrix effective interaction with target ground-state densities obtained from Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using the Gogny force. The g-matrix was derived from the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone equation for symmetric nuclear matter, using the Argonne V18 NN potential supplemented with a separable energy-dependent term to reproduce the SP00 phase-shift analysis, including inelasticities above pion production threshold. The full-folding optical potential was then solved exactly within numerical accuracy to obtain reaction cross sections for proton elastic scattering from stable and unstable even-even isotopes across the 10-1000 MeV energy range.

## Key numbers

- For 12C and 16O targets at 30 MeV, the calculated reaction cross sections overestimate the data by approximately 100 mb, with the calculations showing a monotonic decrease instead of the observed maxima near 25 MeV.
- For heavier targets (40Ca, 58,60Ni, 90Zr, 208Pb) between 20 and 100 MeV, the calculated cross sections systematically overestimate the data by a uniform 50 mb.
- At 1 GeV, the calculated reaction cross sections follow a power law σ_R ∼ A^0.644, and the total cross sections follow σ_T ∼ A^0.748, both differing from the A^(2/3) geometric law.
- The maximum deviation of calculated reaction cross sections from the A^p power law for oxygen isotopes at 1.0 GeV is bounded by 3%.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic full-folding optical model approach of Arellano (1995, 2002) to a systematic study of reaction cross sections across a wide energy range and mass range, including unstable isotopes. It contrasts with the coordinate-space g-folding model of Amos (2000, 2006) and Deb (2001, 2003, 2005), which uses a local medium-dependent effective interaction and explicit shell-model mixed densities, and which reports closer agreement with low-energy data. The results enable future investigations into the role of nucleon-phonon couplings (suggested by Dupuis, 2006) to improve low-energy descriptions, and highlight the need for better handling of target mixed densities for light, asymmetric systems.
