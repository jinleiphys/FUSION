# Signatures of an $α$ + core structure in $^{44}$Ti + $^{44}$Ti collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV by a multiphase transport model

> It is important to understand whether $α$-clustering structures can leave traces in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. Using the modified AMPT model, we simulate three $α$ + core configurations of $^{44}$Ti in $^{44}$Ti+$^{44}$Ti collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV as well as other systems with Woods-Saxon structures. One of these configurations has no additional constraint, but the other two have the Mott density edge $r_{\mathrm{Mott}}$ set as either a lower or upper bound on the cluster position $r_α$ to check the influence of $α$ dissolution. This is the first time that the initial stage of the geometric properties in heavy-ion collisions has been configured using the traditional treatment of the nuclear structure. We compare the radial nucleon density, multiplicity distribution, transverse momentum spectra, eccentricity, triangularity, elliptic flow and triangular flow of these six systems. $α$ + core structures can alter all these observations especially in the most-central collisions, among which elliptic flow is the most hopeful as a probe of such structures.

## Key claim

Using a modified AMPT model with a non-localized α + core structure for ⁴⁴Ti, the authors find that α-clustering signatures can alter observables in ultra-relativistic collisions, but the effect is conditional: it appears primarily in the most-central collisions and depends on whether the α cluster position respects the Mott density edge (r_Mott), with only configurations having r_α > r_Mott or unconstrained r_α being physically realistic.

## Method

The authors modified the initial geometry of the AMPT (string-melting version 2.26t7b) model by replacing the standard Woods-Saxon distribution of ⁴⁴Ti with a non-localized α + ⁴⁰Ca structure derived from the local potential model (LPM). They simulated three α + core configurations (free r_α, r_α > r_Mott, r_α < r_Mott) plus three Woods-Saxon systems (⁴⁰Ca, ⁴⁴Ti, ⁵⁰Ti) in collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV, each with 8 × 10⁵ events divided into ten centrality classes. They then compared radial nucleon density, multiplicity distributions, transverse momentum spectra, eccentricity (ε₂), triangularity (ε₃), elliptic flow (v₂), and triangular flow (v₃) extracted via two-sub-event cumulant and direct two-particle correlation methods.

## Key numbers

- The Mott density for ⁴⁰Ca is ρ_Mott = 0.02917 fm⁻³, corresponding to r_Mott = 4.498 fm.
- About 55% of α clusters lie outside r_Mott (r_α > r_Mott) in the classical binary cluster model.
- The α + core configurations produce up to 10% deviation in elliptic flow (v₂) compared to the natural system size ordering (⁴⁰Ca > ⁴⁴Ti(W-S) > ⁵⁰Ti).
- The LPM parameters for ⁴⁴Ti include V₀ = 220 MeV, a = 0.65 fm, b = 0.3, λ = 0.14, R = 4.551 fm, σ = 0.425 fm, and α decay energy E_α = −5.1271 MeV.
- The Woods-Saxon parameters for α are c = 0.964 fm, z = 0.322 fm, ω = 0.517; for ⁴⁰Ca: c = 3.766 fm, z = 0.586 fm, ω = −0.161.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of α-clustering effects in light nuclei (e.g., ¹²C and ¹⁶O by Zhang and Ma, 2015–2020) to a heavier α + doubly magic core system (⁴⁴Ti). It introduces a non-localized cluster treatment from the local potential model (Souza, 2019; 2023) and incorporates the Mott density constraint (Röpke, Wang) to account for α dissolution via Pauli blocking. The results suggest that elliptic flow in a system scan near ⁴⁴Ti at LHC energies could serve as a probe for α-cluster structures, enabling experimental tests of binary cluster models in heavy nuclei.
