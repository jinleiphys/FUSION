# The Constituent Counting Rule and Omega Photoproduction

> The constituent counting ruling (CCR) has been found to hold for numerous hard, exclusive processes. It predicts the differential cross section at high energies and fixed $\cos θ_{c.m.}$ should follow $\frac{d σ}{dt} \sim \frac{1}{s^{n-2}}$, where $n$ is the minimal number of constituents involved in the reaction. Here we provide an in-depth analysis of the reaction $γp \rightarrow ωp$ at $θ_{c.m.}\sim 90^\circ$ using CLAS data with an energy range of $s = 5 - 8$ GeV$^2$, where the CCR has been shown to work in other reactions. We argue for a stringent method to select data to test the CCR and utilize a Taylor-series expansion to take advantage of data from nearby angle bins in our analysis. Naïvely, this reaction would have $n=9$ (or $n=10$ if the photon is in a $q\bar{q}$ state) and we would expect a scaling of $\sim s^{-7}$ ($s^{-8}$). Instead, a scaling of $s^{-(9.08 \pm 0.11)}$ was observed. Explanations for this apparent failure of the naïve CCR assumptions are examined.

# The Constituent Counting Rule and Omega Photoproduction

## Key claim

The constituent counting rule (CCR) predicts that for the reaction γp → ωp at θ_c.m. ∼ 90°, the differential cross section should scale as s⁻⁷ (n=9) or s⁻⁸ (n=10 if the photon is treated as a qq̅ state), but the observed scaling is s⁻⁹·⁰⁸±⁰·¹¹, which the authors argue can be explained by a careful application of angular momentum conservation that adds two extra powers of s.

## Method

The authors analyzed CLAS g11 data for γp → ωp, selecting events with -t > 2 GeV² to ensure hard scattering, and used data from four cosθ bins (−0.15, −0.05, +0.05, +0.15) near 90°. They performed fits of the form dσ/dt = (A + B cosθ) s⁻ᴺ using three independent methods: χ² minimization, Bayesian estimation with Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling, and non-parametric bootstrapping, with a Taylor-series expansion in cosθ to combine multiple angle bins.

## Key numbers

- Observed scaling exponent: N = 9.08 ± 0.11 (combined systematic uncertainty from fit, angle dependence, and -t cutoff dependence)
- χ² minimization result: N = 9.07 ± 0.08 (χ²/df = 2.23)
- Bayesian uniform prior estimate: N = 9.08 ± 0.05
- Bootstrap result: N = 9.07 ± 0.06
- Energy range analyzed: s = 5–8 GeV²
- -t cutoff: 2.0 GeV², yielding 118 data points
- Naïve CCR prediction for n=9: scaling ∼ s⁻⁷
- Naïve CCR prediction for n=10 (photon as qq̅): scaling ∼ s⁻⁸

## Context

This work extends studies of the constituent counting rule to ω photoproduction, where earlier work by Battaglieri found N = 7.2 ± 0.7 using only 5 data points, and Dey (2014) found N = 9.4 ± 0.1. The results are consistent with a Jefferson Lab Compton scattering experiment that found an extra 1/s² suppression beyond the naïve CCR prediction. The authors argue that proper application of helicity conservation and angular momentum in spin-1 meson photoproduction explains the discrepancy, and predict similar scaling for ρ⁰ photoproduction. This analysis enables future tests with CLAS12, GlueX, and CLAS g12 data at higher energies, and suggests the CCR could be applied to study exotic hadrons like hybrid mesons.
