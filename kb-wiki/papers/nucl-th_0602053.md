# Superscaling and neutral current quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering

> The superscaling approach is applied to studies of neutral current neutrino reactions in the quasielastic regime. Using input from scaling analyses of electron scattering data, predictions for high-energy neutrino and antineutrino cross sections are given and compared with results obtained using the relativistic Fermi gas model. The influence of strangeness content inside the nucleons in the nucleus is also explored.

## Key claim

The superscaling approach, previously validated for charged-current neutrino reactions, can be extended to neutral-current quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering by using the empirical scaling function extracted from electron scattering data, provided the integration domains for the t-channel (electron scattering) and u-channel (neutral-current neutrino scattering) are sufficiently similar at high momentum transfer.

## Method

The authors apply the superscaling formalism to neutral-current neutrino and antineutrino reactions on 12C, using the empirical scaling function obtained from inclusive electron scattering data. They derive the u-channel kinematics and cross section formalism, showing that the effective single-nucleon cross section can be factorized from the integral over missing momentum and excitation energy, similar to the t-channel case. The approach is tested against the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model, and the influence of nucleon strangeness content is explored by varying the strange form factors.

## Key numbers

* For 12C, the Fermi momentum used in the RFG model is k_F = 228 MeV/c.
* The effective single-nucleon cross section ratio deviates from unity by at most 5-10% for excitation energies below twice the maximum RFG value (approximately 50 MeV).
* The RFG scaling function is given by F_RFG(psi) = (3/4) k_F (1 - psi^2) theta(1 - psi^2), where k_F = 228 MeV/c for 12C.
* Neutrino and antineutrino double differential cross sections are computed for 1 GeV incident energy on 12C at scattering angles of 20 degrees and 60 degrees.

## Context

This work extends the superscaling approach of Amaro et al. (2004) from charged-current to neutral-current neutrino reactions, building on the scaling analyses of electron scattering data by Alberico (1988), Day (1990), Donnelly (1998, 1999), Maieron (2001), and Barbaro (2003). The results enable predictions for neutral-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections relevant for neutrino oscillation experiments, using the same empirical scaling functions that successfully reproduce electron scattering data, in contrast to direct modeling approaches that typically fail to achieve better than 25% accuracy.
