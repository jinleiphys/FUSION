# Hadron Physics at COSY

> The workshop 'Hadron Physics at COSY' held from July 7th to July 10th 2003 in Bad Honnef brought together experimentalists and theoreticians from various fields of hadron physics to identify the key physics questions, which can be addressed with (un-) polarized proton and deuteron induced reactions at COSY. Topics discussed include charge symmetry breaking, elastic and inelastic nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-deuteron interactions, hadrons in the medium, mesonic bound states, meson and baryon resonances, and the hyperon-nucleon system. In addition prospects of the planned GSI facility were discussed. These Miniproceedings contain besides short summaries of the presentations the most relevant references for the field.

## Key claim

The workshop aimed to identify key physics questions addressable with (un)polarized proton and deuteron induced reactions at COSY, but the resulting Miniproceedings serve primarily as a collection of references and short summaries rather than presenting a unified new result or a definitive physics program.

## Method

Experimentalists and theoreticians from hadron physics gathered from July 7th to 10th, 2003 in Bad Honnef to discuss topics including charge symmetry breaking, elastic and inelastic nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-deuteron interactions, hadrons in the medium, mesonic bound states, meson and baryon resonances, and the hyperon-nucleon system. The proceedings contain short summaries of presentations and the most relevant references for the field, with transparencies available online.

## Key numbers

- COSY delivers polarized and unpolarized phase-space cooled proton and deuteron beams in the momentum range between 300 and 3650 MeV/c for internal and external experimental installations.
- The K+ potential in nuclei was determined from cross section ratio measurements between different nuclei (Au/C) to be 20 +/- 3 MeV at normal nuclear density.
- The cross section ratio between Lambda and Sigma production close to threshold at COSY-11 was measured to be about 28, compared to a factor of 2.5 at high energies.
- The pi0-eta mixing angle extracted from hadronic reactions is 0.026 +/- 0.007 rad.

## Context

This workshop proceedings extends the reviews by Machner and Haidenbauer (1999) and Moskal, Wolke, Khoukaz and Oelert (2002) on meson production in hadronic interactions. The discussions and summaries identify open questions in charge symmetry breaking, hyperon-nucleon interactions, and meson production mechanisms that could be addressed with the COSY facility, and also covered prospects for the planned GSI facility. The work enables future experimental programs at COSY, particularly those requiring full control of spin and isospin degrees of freedom and new photon detection capabilities.
