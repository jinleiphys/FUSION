# Renormalization of chiral two-pion exchange NN interactions. Momentum vs. coordinate space

> The renormalization of the chiral np interaction in the 1S0 channel to N3LO in Weinberg counting for the long distance potential with one single momentum and energy independent counterterm is carried out. This renormalization scheme yields finite and unique results and is free of short distance off-shell ambiguities. We observe good convergence in the entire elastic range below pion production threshold and find that there are some small physical effects missing in the purely pionic chiral NN potential with or without inclusion of explicit Delta degrees of freedom. We also study the renormalizability of the standard Weinberg counting at NLO and N2LO when a momentum dependent polynomial counterterm is included. Our numerical results suggest that the inclusion of this counterterm does not yield a convergent amplitude (at NLO and N2LO).

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in the $^1S_0$ channel of chiral np interactions, renormalization with a single momentum- and energy-independent counterterm $C_0$ yields finite and unique results, while the standard Weinberg counting that includes an additional momentum-dependent counterterm $C_2$ at NLO and N2LO does not produce a convergent amplitude when the cutoff is removed.

## Method

The authors solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in momentum space with a sharp three-momentum cutoff $\Lambda$ for the $^1S_0$ np channel, using chiral potentials up to N3LO in Weinberg counting. They also perform coordinate-space calculations with a short-distance cutoff $r_c$ and boundary conditions, establishing a mapping between the two regularization schemes. The renormalization procedure fixes the counterterm(s) by fitting the experimental scattering length, and the resulting phase shifts and effective range parameters are examined as the cutoff is taken to infinity.

## Key numbers

- Experimental scattering length: $\alpha_0 = -23.74(2)\ \mathrm{fm}$; experimental effective range: $r_0^{\mathrm{exp}} = 2.77(5)\ \mathrm{fm}$
- Leading order (LO) effective range from OPE plus one counterterm: $r_0^{\mathrm{LO}} = 1.44\ \mathrm{fm}$
- Renormalized NLO prediction with $C_2=0$: $r_0^{\mathrm{NLO}} = 2.29\ \mathrm{fm}$
- Renormalized N2LO prediction with $C_2=0$: $r_0^{\mathrm{N2LO}} = 2.86\ \mathrm{fm}$ (less than 3% above the experimental value)
- LO phase shift is approximately constant at $75^\circ$ around $k=250\ \mathrm{MeV}$
- For the pion-less theory with a sharp cutoff, the effective range from finite cutoff corrections is $r_0(\Lambda) = 4/(\pi \Lambda)$, yielding $\Lambda = 90.7\ \mathrm{MeV}$ to match $r_0^{\mathrm{exp}}$

## Context

This work extends the coordinate-space renormalization analysis of Valderrama and Arriola (2005, 2006) to N3LO and to momentum space, confirming that only one counterterm is needed for renormalizability in the $^1S_0$ channel. It contradicts the standard Weinberg counting approach used by Epelbaum et al. (1998-2004) and Entem and Machleidt (2001-2003), which includes a $C_2$ counterterm at NLO and N2LO but produces cutoff-dependent results. The paper enables a consistent renormalization scheme for chiral NN potentials that can be extended to higher partial waves and to include explicit Delta degrees of freedom.
