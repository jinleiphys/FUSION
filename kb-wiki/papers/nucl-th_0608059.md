# Theoretical Standard Model Rates of Proton to Neutron Conversions Near Metallic Hydride Surfaces

> The process of radiation induced electron capture by protons or deuterons producing new ultra low momentum neutrons and neutrinos may be theoretically described within the standard field theoretical model of electroweak interactions. For protons or deuterons in the neighborhoods of surfaces of condensed matter metallic hydride cathodes, such conversions are determined in part by the collective plasma modes of the participating charged particles, e.g. electrons and protons. The radiation energy required for such low energy nuclear reactions may be supplied by the applied voltage required to push a strong charged current across a metallic hydride surface employed as a cathode within a chemical cell. The electroweak rates of the resulting ultra low momentum neutron production are computed from these considerations.

## Key claim

The authors claim that proton-to-neutron conversion via electron capture can occur at appreciable rates near metallic hydride cathode surfaces, provided that collective surface plasmon radiation renormalizes the electron mass by a factor β > 2.531, thereby overcoming the 0.78233 MeV threshold. The abstract glosses over the condition that this mechanism requires the electron mass renormalization parameter β to exceed approximately 2.531 for the reaction to proceed.

## Method

The authors employ standard model electroweak theory with Fermi four-fermion interactions, computing neutrino production rates from composite charged fields of electrons and effective condensed-matter W-bosons. They incorporate electron mass renormalization from strong local surface plasmon radiation fields using Hamilton-Jacobi theory in the presence of electromagnetic fields, and treat the neutron production as isotopic spin waves in the non-relativistic nucleon limit. The neutron production rate per unit volume is derived from the four-divergence of the neutrino flux, using lepton number, charge, and baryon number conservation to equate neutrino creation with neutron creation.

## Key numbers

- Energy threshold for proton-to-neutron conversion: Q_in ≈ 0.78233 MeV (from the mass difference M_n - (M_p + m))
- Fine structure constant: α ≈ 7.2973526 × 10^{-3}
- Fermi coupling constant scaled by proton mass: G_F M_p^2/(ħc) ≈ 1.02682 × 10^{-5}
- Fermi coupling constant scaled by electron mass: G_F m^2/(ħc) ≈ 3.04563 × 10^{-12}
- Threshold electron mass renormalization factor: β_0 ≈ 2.531
- Estimated transition rate for a single heavy electron-proton pair: Γ ∼ 7 × 10^{-4} Hz × (β - β_0)^2
- Estimated neutron production rate per unit surface area: ϖ_2 ∼ (10^{13} Hz/cm^2) × (β - β_0)^2, with n_2 ∼ 10^{16} pairs/cm^2
- For β ∼ 5 (2β_0), estimated rate: ϖ_2 ∼ 10^{13} Hz/cm^2
- Neutron absorption mean free path: Λ ∼ 10^{-6} cm for n_a ∼ 10^{22}/cm^3, b ∼ 10^{-13} cm, λ ∼ 10^{-3} cm
- Gamma-ray mean free path: L_γ ∼ 3.4 × 10^{-8} cm for photon energies 0.5 MV < (ħω_γ/e) < 10 MV
- Vector and axial vector coupling ratio: λ ≡ g_A/g_V ≈ 1.2695
- Cabibbo angle cosine: cos θ_C ≡ g_V ≈ 0.9742

## Context

This work extends prior speculations by Mizuno (1998) and Kozima (1998) on the role of neutrons in low-energy nuclear reactions, providing a standard model mechanism for neutron production that those authors could not articulate. The paper builds on the authors' earlier work (Widom 2006) on electron mass renormalization and neutron absorption, and addresses experimental observations of excess heat and nuclear transmutations in metallic hydride cathodes reported by Iwamura (1998), Violante (2002), Dash (2002), and Miley (1996, 1997, 2005). The mechanism enables explanation of nuclear transmutations in light-water cells where deuterons are absent, and predicts negligible external neutron and gamma-ray fluxes due to short absorption mean free paths in the cathode material.
