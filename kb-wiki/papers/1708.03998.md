# Microscopic study of induced fission dynamics of $^{226}$Th with covariant energy density functionals

> Static and dynamic aspects of the fission process of $^{226}$Th are analyzed in a self-consistent framework based on relativistic energy density functionals. Constrained relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations in the collective space of axially symmetric quadrupole and octupole deformations, based on the energy density functional PC-PK1 and a $δ$-force pairing, are performed to determine the potential energy surface of the fissioning nucleus, the scission line, the single-nucleon wave functions, energies and occupation probabilities, as functions of deformation parameters. Induced fission dynamics is described using the time-dependent generator coordinate method in the Gaussian overlap approximation. A collective Schrödinger equation, determined entirely by the microscopic single-nucleon degrees of freedom, propagates adiabatically in time the initial wave packet built by boosting the ground-state solution of the collective Hamiltonian for $^{226}$Th. The position of the scission line and the microscopic input for the collective Hamiltonian are analyzed as functions of the strength of the pairing interaction. The effect of static pairing correlations on the pre-neutron emission charge yields and total kinetic energy of fission fragments is examined in comparison with available data, and the distribution of fission fragments is analyzed for different values of the initial excitation energy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a fully microscopic framework based on covariant energy density functionals, combined with the time-dependent generator coordinate method in the Gaussian overlap approximation (TDGCM+GOA), can reproduce the main features of induced fission fragment charge and mass distributions for $^{226}$Th, but the quantitative agreement with experimental data depends sensitively on the strength of static pairing correlations and the initial excitation energy.

## Method

Constrained relativistic mean-field (RMF) plus BCS calculations with the PC-PK1 energy density functional and a delta-force pairing interaction were performed to generate the potential energy surface, single-nucleon wave functions, and collective mass tensor in the two-dimensional collective space of axially symmetric quadrupole ($\beta_2$) and octupole ($\beta_3$) deformations. The induced fission dynamics was then described by solving a time-dependent Schrodinger-like equation derived from the TDGCM+GOA, using the FELIX code to propagate an initial wave packet built by boosting the collective ground state of $^{226}$Th. The flux of the probability current through the scission hyper-surface, defined by a Gaussian neck operator criterion, provided the pre-neutron emission charge yields and total kinetic energies of fission fragments.

## Key numbers

* Fission barrier heights for $^{226}$Th with 100% pairing strength: inner barrier $B_I = 7.10$ MeV, asymmetric barriers $B_{II}^{\rm asy} = 8.58$ MeV and $B_{III}^{\rm asy} = 7.32$ MeV, symmetric barriers $B_{II}^{\rm sym} = 14.21$ MeV and $B_{III}^{\rm sym} = 5.72$ MeV.
* Range of collective variables: $\beta_2$ from -0.83 to 6.01 with step $\Delta\beta_2 = 0.04$, $\beta_3$ from 0.01 to 3.53 with step $\Delta\beta_3 = 0.08$.
* Pairing strength parameters: $V_n = 360$ MeV fm$^3$ and $V_p = 378$ MeV fm$^3$ for the 100% pairing case, scaled to 90% (324, 340.2) and 110% (396, 415.8) for sensitivity studies.
* Time step for TDGCM+GOA evolution: $\delta t = 5 \times 10^{-4}$ zs; absorption rate $r = 20 \times 10^{22}$ s$^{-1}$.
* Fission time (time for total flux to reach 1/5) varies from approximately 30 zs at 90% pairing strength to approximately 7 zs at 110% pairing strength.
* Scission point on the static fission path for all three pairing strengths: $(\beta_2, \beta_3) \sim (3.3, 2.0)$.

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic mean-field studies of fission barriers and spontaneous fission (Li 2010, Abusara 2010, Abusara 2012, Agbemava 2017) to the dynamics of induced fission, and complements non-relativistic TDGCM+GOA calculations based on Skyrme and Gogny functionals (Regnier 2016, Regnier 2016c). The results are compared to photo-induced fission data for $^{226}$Th (Schmidt 2001) and to Gogny D1S calculations (Dubray 2008, Younes 2009). The study enables future investigations of non-perturbative cranking inertia effects on induced fission mass distributions and the inclusion of dynamic pairing correlations in the TDGCM+GOA collective space.
