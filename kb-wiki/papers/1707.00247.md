# General formalism of collective motion for any deformed system

> Based on Bohr model, we have presented a general formalism describing the collective motion for any deformed system, in which the collective Hamiltonian is expressed as vibrations in the body-fixed frame, rotation of whole system around the laboratory frame, and coupling between vibrations and rotation. Under the condition of decoupling approximation, we have derived the quantized Hamiltonian operator. Based on the operator, we have calculated the rotational spectra for some special octupole and hexadecapole deformed systems, and shown their dependencies on deformation. The result indicates that the contribution of octupole or hexadecapole deformations to the lowest band is regular, while that to higher bands is dramatic. These features reflecting octupole and hexadecapole deformations are helpful to recognize the properties of real nuclei with octupole and/or hexadecapole deformations coexisting with quadrupole deformations.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general formalism for collective motion in any deformed nuclear system, but the quantized Hamiltonian is derived only under a "decoupling approximation" that freezes certain deformation parameters to eliminate vibration-rotation coupling. The claim that the formalism applies to "any deformed system" is qualified by the need to remove three superfluous variables, which is straightforward only for quadrupole deformations and requires an approximate method for higher multipoles.

## Method

The author starts from the Bohr model, expanding the nuclear surface in spherical harmonics with deformation parameters α_lm in the laboratory frame, then transforms to a body-fixed frame using Wigner D-functions to separate vibrational, rotational, and coupling terms. To quantize the classical Hamiltonian, three superfluous deformation variables are removed by freezing them (e.g., a_l1, b_l1, b_l2 for quadrupole), which eliminates the coupling term T_cou under the decoupling approximation. The resulting quantized kinetic operator is then applied to pure octupole and hexadecapole deformed systems, and rotational spectra are calculated using axially symmetric spheroidal wave functions as basis states.

## Key numbers

- For a pure octupole deformed system, the moments of inertia are given as: 𝒥₁ = B₃β₃²[1 + 8 sin²(γ₃ + γ₀)], 𝒥₂ = B₃β₃²[1 + 8 sin²(γ₃ − γ₀)], 𝒥₃ = 4B₃β₃² sin²γ₃, where γ₀ = arc tan(√(5/3)).
- For a pure hexadecapole deformed system, the kinetic operator contains a 5th power of β₄ in the first term (1/β₄⁵ ∂/∂β₄ β₄⁵ ∂/∂β₄), compared to the 4th power for quadrupole and octupole systems.
- The rotational matrix element includes the factor (1/𝒥₁ + 1/𝒥₂)/4 multiplying I(I+1) and (1/𝒥₃ − 1/(2𝒥₁) − 1/(2𝒥₂))/2 multiplying K².

## Context

This work extends the Bohr model (Bohr, 1952) beyond quadrupole deformations to include octupole and hexadecapole degrees of freedom, building on prior parameterizations by Rohozinski (1988, 1990), Hamamoto (1991), and Wexler (1999) for octupole systems, and by Nazarewicz (1981), Sobiczewski (1981), and Rohozinski (1997) for hexadecapole systems. The formalism enables future studies of nuclei with tetrahedral or octahedral shapes as predicted by Dudek (2002, 2006) and observed in ¹⁶O by Bijker (2014), and can be applied to systems where octupole or hexadecapole deformations coexist with quadrupole deformations, as in ²²⁴Ra studied by Bizzeti (2013).
