# Some Multi-Quark Potentials, Pseudo-Potentials and AdS/QCD

> The static three-quark potential and pseudo-potential of a pure SU(3) gauge theory are studied in a five-dimensional framework known as AdS/QCD. The results support the Y-ansatz for the baryonic area law. A comparison with the quark-antiquark calculations shows the universality of the string tension as well as the spatial string tension. We also discuss extensions to SU(N) gauge theories.

## Key claim

The static three-quark potential in pure SU(3) gauge theory, computed within a five-dimensional AdS/QCD framework, supports the Y-ansatz for the baryonic area law and shows universality of the string tension with the quark-antiquark case, but only under specific assumptions about the baryon vertex potential and in the limit where the vertex location saturates an upper bound at r0 = 1/√s.

## Method

The static three-quark potential is computed from the minimal action of a configuration in a deformed Euclidean AdS5 metric (with warp factor w = e^{s r^2}/r^2) where three fundamental strings connect heavy quarks at the boundary to a baryon vertex in the interior. The action includes Nambu-Goto terms for the strings and a potential term for the vertex, and the configuration is extremized with respect to the worldsheet fields and the vertex coordinates. For the symmetric equilateral case, the vertex potential is taken as V = m R sqrt(w(r0)), and the potential is obtained in parametric form from equations relating the interquark distance l and energy E to the parameter λ = s r0^2.

## Key numbers

- The string tension from the three-quark potential at long distances is σ = e g s, where g = R^2/(2πα') and s = 0.45 GeV^2 is the free parameter fixed from the quark-antiquark potential.
- The upper bound on the baryon vertex depth is r0 ≤ 1/√s, with s = 0.45 GeV^2 giving 1/√s ≈ 1.49 GeV^{-1}.
- The critical temperature for the spatial string tension phase transition is Tc = (1/π)√s, which for s = 0.45 GeV^2 gives Tc ≈ 0.214 GeV.
- For the symmetric equilateral configuration, the Coulomb coefficient α in the short-distance potential E = -3α/L + O(1) depends on κ; at κ = 0.25 (from the D5-brane estimate) the ratio Υ = α/α_q\bar{q} is 0.27, compared to Υ ≈ 0.5 in lattice QCD.

## Context

This work extends the AdS/QCD model of Andreev and Zakharov (2006, 2007) for quark-antiquark potentials and pseudo-potentials to the multi-quark case, providing a five-dimensional string description of baryonic Wilson loops. The results support the Y-law found in lattice QCD simulations (e.g., Bali et al., 2000) and confirm the universality of the string tension between the three-quark and quark-antiquark systems. The analysis of pseudo-potentials at finite temperature extends the spatial string tension calculations of Andreev (2007) to baryonic configurations, enabling future comparisons with lattice data for the temperature dependence of the three-quark spatial string tension.
