# Parity violation and electric dipole moments in atoms and molecules

> We review the current status of the study of parity and time invariance violation in atoms, nuclei and molecules. We focus on parity non-conservation in cesium and three of the most promising areas of research: (i) parity non-conservation in a chain of isotopes, (ii) search for nuclear anapole moments, and (iii) search for permanent electric dipole moments (EDMs) of atoms and molecules, which in turn are caused by either an electron EDM or nuclear $T,P$-odd moments such as a nuclear EDM or nuclear Schiff moment.

# Parity violation and electric dipole moments in atoms and molecules

## Key claim
The paper reviews the current status of parity and time invariance violation studies in atoms, nuclei and molecules, focusing on parity non-conservation in cesium and three promising research areas: parity non-conservation in isotope chains, nuclear anapole moments, and permanent electric dipole moments (EDMs). The authors note that while the cesium PNC measurement initially showed disagreement with the Standard Model, inclusion of Breit and QED corrections resolved this discrepancy, though recent work by Porsev et al. (2009) claiming 0.26% theoretical uncertainty may have underestimated correlation corrections.

## Method
The authors survey atomic and molecular calculations of parity-violating amplitudes using methods including correlation potential combined with perturbation theory in screened Coulomb interaction (CP+PTSCI), coupled cluster with single and double excitations (CC SD), and coupled cluster with single, double and valence triple excitations (CC SDvT). For nuclear anapole moments and EDMs, they review calculations of electron structure factors, Schiff moments, and nuclear enhancement mechanisms, including effects of octupole deformation in nuclei such as radium and radon.

- Correlated PNC amplitude in Cs from most calculations: 0.9078(45) x 10^(-11)i(-Q_W/N) a.u. (Dzuba et al. 2002); Porsev et al. (2009) obtained 0.8998(24) x 10^(-11)i(-Q_W/N) a.u.
- Final PNC amplitude in Cs: E_PNC = 0.8977(45) x 10^(-11)i(-Q_W/N) a.u.
- Weak nuclear charge of Cs-133: Q_W = -72.58(29)_expt(36)_theory, compared to Standard Model prediction Q_W^SM = -73.23(2)
- Deviation from Standard Model: delta Q_W = 0.65(46), corresponding to sin^2(theta_W) = 0.2356(21) versus Standard Model value 0.2386(1)
- Limit on extra Z boson mass: M_Zchi > 700 GeV/c^2
- Best limit on electron EDM from thallium: d_e = (6.9 +/- 7.4) x 10^(-28) e cm
- Limit on electron EDM from mercury: |d_e| < 3 x 10^(-27) e cm

- Schiff moment of Hg-199: S = (-1.8 +/- 4.6 +/- 2.7) x 10^(-13) e cm
- T,P-odd neutron-proton interaction constant: eta_np = (1 +/- 3 +/- 2) x 10^(-5)
- Schiff moment for Ra-225: -8.2 x 10^(-17) e cm (per unit eta)
- Schiff moment for Rn-223: 3.3 x 10^(-17) e cm (per unit eta)
- Enhancement factor for electron EDM in heavy atoms: K ~ 3Z^3 alpha^2 R(Zalpha) ~ 10^2 to 10^3
- Molecular enhancement factors for electron EDM: K ~ 10^7 to 10^11

## Context
This review extends the work of Ginges and Dzuba (2002) and Dzuba and Kozlov (2002) on parity violation in atoms, and addresses the controversy between Porsev et al. (2009) and earlier calculations by Dzuba et al. (2002), Novikov (1997), and others regarding the cesium PNC amplitude. The paper discusses how isotope chain measurements may be more sensitive to new physics than parity-violating electron scattering experiments (by a factor of 10 for atoms like Cs, Ba, and Dy). It enables future searches for new physics beyond the Standard Model through measurements of nuclear anapole moments in molecules (experiments at Yale and Groningen KVI) and EDMs in systems with octupole deformation such as Ra-225 and Rn-223, where nuclear enhancement can reach factors of 500 compared to spherical nuclei like Hg.
