# Equivalence of Nonstatic Two-Pion-Exchange Nucleon-Nucleon Potentials

> Off-shell aspects of the one-pion-exchange potential and their relationship to different forms of the nonstatic (subleading-order) chiral two-pion-exchange nucleon-nucleon potential are discussed. Various types of off-shell behavior are categorized and numerous examples are given. Recently derived potentials based on chiral approaches are supplemented by a rather general form of the two-pion-exchange potential derived using old-fashioned methods. The latter is closely related to a general form of one-pion-exchange relativistic corrections and nonstatic two-pion-exchange three-nucleon forces developed long ago.

# Equivalence of Nonstatic Two-Pion-Exchange Nucleon-Nucleon Potentials

## Key claim
Different forms of the nonstatic (subleading-order) chiral two-pion-exchange nucleon-nucleon potential are equivalent to each other up to unitary transformations and off-shell parameter choices, provided the same dynamical equation and one-pion-exchange potential form are used. The paper demonstrates that the apparent differences between several modern potentials arise from three types of ambiguity (energy-dependence, unitary field redefinitions, and form of the dynamical equation) rather than from physical disagreements.

## Method
The author derives a general form of the nonstatic two-pion-exchange potential using old-fashioned time-dependent perturbation theory with a Foldy-Wouthuysen reduction of a relativistic Lagrangian containing pseudovector pion-nucleon coupling, Weinberg-Tomozawa seagull terms, and chiral seagull terms (c1, c3, c4). The resulting potential is expressed in terms of two off-shell parameters (mu and nu) that encode unitary ambiguities from field redefinitions and quasipotential choices. This general form is then specialized to match the specific off-shell choices and dynamical equations used in the Nijmegen partial-wave-analysis program, the Urbana relativistic potential model, the Bonn potential, and a Munich calculation.

## Key numbers
- The off-shell parameter nu takes values nu = 0 (standard), nu = 1/2 (no retardation), and nu = 1 (soft), with Delta E_i^2 terms of order (1/M^2) where M is the nucleon mass.
- The off-shell parameter mu takes values mu = -1 (pseudoscalar free spinors), mu = 0 (minimal nonlocality), mu = 1 (pseudovector), and mu = 3 (soft).
- The minimal nonlocality (MNL) choice corresponds to mu = 0 and nu = 1/2, which eliminates complicated spin-momentum coupling terms from the one-pion-exchange potential.
- The Nijmegen partial-wave-analysis potential uses Delta V_{2pi}^{Nij} = Delta V_{2pi}^{MNL} + V_pi^2/(4M), while the Munich result uses Delta V_{2pi}(Munich) = Delta V_{2pi}^{MNL} + 3 V_pi^2/(4M).
- The Bonn potential corresponds to mu = -1 and nu = 1/2 in the general formula.
- The chiral seagull coefficients c_i appear as dimensionless combinations bar{c}_i = c_i M/g_A^2, with magnitudes of order 1 or larger due to Delta isobar contributions to c3 and c4.

## Context
This work extends the classic calculations of Sugawara and Okubo (S-O) from the 1960s, who first developed the nonstatic two-pion-exchange potential but lacked a complete chiral theory of pion-nucleon interactions. The paper unifies several modern approaches: the Nijmegen partial-wave-analysis program (based on the Blankenbecler-Sugar equation), the Urbana relativistic potential model, the Bonn potential, and a Munich calculation. It also connects to the nonstatic two-pion-exchange three-nucleon forces developed earlier by the author and collaborators. The general form derived here enables direct comparison between these different potentials and clarifies that their differences are purely formal, arising from different off-shell parameter choices and different dynamical equations rather than from physical discrepancies.
