# Jet physics in heavy-ion collisions

> Jets are expected to play a prominent role in the ongoing efforts to characterize the hot and dense QCD medium created in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. The success of this program depends crucially on the existence of a full theoretical account of the dynamical effects of the medium on the jets that develop within it. By focussing on the discussion of the essential ingredients underlying such a theoretical formulation, we aim to set the appropriate context in which current and future developments can be understood.

## Key claim

This review argues that a full theoretical account of jet modification in heavy-ion collisions requires understanding the interplay between vacuum-like jet evolution (characterized by angular-ordered, collinear radiation) and medium-induced radiation (dominated by the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect), with the transport coefficient \(\hat q\) serving as the key phenomenological parameter linking medium properties to jet observables, though the authors note that the medium is treated as a set of independent scattering centers in the weak-coupling limit and that strong-coupling approaches via AdS/CFT represent a conceptually orthogonal direction.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical ingredients for describing jet development in a quark-gluon plasma, starting from the vacuum baseline of factorizable QCD cross sections with DGLAP evolution and angular-ordered parton branching. They then model the medium via a two-point correlator of the medium gauge field, assuming a translationally invariant, weakly-coupled plasma where the interaction is instantaneous and momentum exchange is purely transverse, and define the transport coefficient \(\hat q\) from the second moment of the scattering potential. The review discusses medium-induced radiation through the LPM effect, where multiple soft scatterings interfere when the formation time exceeds the mean free path, and addresses color decoherence of the radiated gluons from the jet.

## Key numbers

* The LHC heavy-ion collision energy is 2.76 TeV per nucleon pair, compared to 200 GeV/c available at RHIC.
* In pPb collisions at LHC energies (\(\sqrt{s} = 5.02\) TeV per nucleon pair), the nuclear modification factor is close to unity for \(p_T \geq 5\) GeV/c.
* The Debye screening mass in the high-temperature plasma scales as \(m_D \sim gT\).
* The mean free path in the weakly coupled regime scales as \((g^2 T)^{-1}\).
* The scattering rate in the harmonic oscillator approximation is \(\Gamma_2(\mathbf{r},t) \simeq \frac{1}{2} \hat q(t) \mathbf{r}^2\).
* The UV regulator \(q^\ast\) in the definition of \(\hat q\) scales with the probe energy as \(q^\ast \propto E^{1/4}\).

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework of jet quenching developed in early works by Bjorken (1982), Appel (1985), Blaizot (1986), Gyulassy (1990), and Wang (1991), and synthesizes the common ground among the BDMPS-Z, GLV, HT, and AMY approaches to medium-induced radiation. It sets the stage for interpreting LHC-era measurements of dijet energy imbalance (Aad 2010, Chatrchyan 2011, 2012), photon-jet correlations (Chatrchyan 2012, ATLAS 2012), and jet fragmentation patterns (CMS 2012, ATLAS 2012), and enables future extraction of the transport coefficient \(\hat q\) from data, either from microscopic calculations in high-temperature QCD or as a phenomenological parameter.
