# Quarkonia at $T>0$ and lattice QCD

> We report here on recent progress in the determination of S-wave and P-wave heavy-quarkonium states at finite temperature. Our results are based on the combination of effective field theories with numerical lattice QCD simulations. These non-perturbative tools allow us to compute the heavy-quarkonium in-medium spectral functions, from which we in turn determine the melting temperatures of individual states and estimate phenomenologically relevant observables, such as the $蠄^\prime$ to J/$蠄$ ratio in heavy-ion collisions.

# Quarkonia at $T>0$ and lattice QCD

## Key claim
The melting temperatures of heavy-quarkonium states are bracketed from above and below by two different Bayesian spectral reconstruction methods (BR and MEM), meaning the actual disappearance temperature lies between the estimates from each method; the results depend on the assumption of full kinetic equilibration of heavy quarks in a thermal medium at fixed temperature.

## Method
The authors combine effective field theories (NRQCD and pNRQCD) with lattice QCD simulations to compute in-medium heavy-quarkonium spectral functions. For the direct NRQCD approach, heavy quarks are treated as non-relativistic Pauli-spinors propagating in thermal backgrounds from HotQCD lattices ($48^3\times12$, $m_\pi=161$ MeV), and spectral functions are reconstructed using both the Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) and the Bayesian Reconstruction (BR) method. For the indirect pNRQCD approach, the in-medium heavy-quark potential is extracted from lattice QCD Wilson line correlators in Coulomb gauge, parametrized via a generalized Gauss law with a single temperature-dependent Debye mass $m_D$, and then used to solve a Schr枚dinger equation for the spectral functions.

## Key numbers
- Maximum departure from unity in correlator ratios at $T=407$ MeV: $1.75\%$ for Upsilon, $6.5\%$ for $\chi_b$
- Melting temperatures for S-wave states ($T_{\rm melt}^{\Gamma=E_{\rm bind}}/T_C$): $J/\psi(1S)$ = $1.37_{-0.07}^{+0.08}$, $\psi'(2S)$ < $0.95$, $\Upsilon(1S)$ = $2.66_{-0.14}^{+0.49}$, $\Upsilon(2S)$ = $1.25_{-0.05}^{+0.17}$, $\Upsilon(3S)$ = $1.01_{-0.03}^{+0.03}$, $\Upsilon(4S)$ < $0.95$
- Melting temperatures for P-wave states ($T_{\rm melt}^{\Gamma=E_{\rm bind}}/T_C$): $\chi_c(1P)$ = $1.04(3)$, $\chi_c(2P)$ < $0.95$, $\chi_b(1P)$ = $1.41(6)$, $\chi_b(2P)$ = $1.06(3)$, $\chi_b(3P)$ = $0.98(2)$
- Estimated $\psi'/J/\psi$ number ratio at $T=T_C$: $0.052\pm0.009$; after branching ratio correction: $0.0069(11)$
- Lattice simulations span temperatures $T\in[140\ldots 407]$ MeV with $m_b a\in[2.759\ldots 0.954]$
- Chiral crossover temperature $T_{\rm PC}=155\pm5$ MeV

## Context
This work extends the program of computing heavy-quarkonium in-medium properties from first principles using lattice QCD, building on prior EFT developments by Brambilla (2004) and lattice NRQCD studies by the FASTSUM collaboration (Aarts 2010, 2011, 2014, 2013). The direct NRQCD approach follows the methodology of Kim (2014) and uses HotQCD lattices from Bazavov (2014). The indirect pNRQCD approach extends perturbative studies of the in-medium potential by Laine (2006) and Beraudo (2007) to non-perturbative lattice data, using the generalized Gauss law parametrization from Burnier (2015). The results enable first-principles estimates of phenomenologically relevant observables such as the $\psi'/J/\psi$ ratio, which can be compared to ALICE measurements and the statistical model of hadronization.
