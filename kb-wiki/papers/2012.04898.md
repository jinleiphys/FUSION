# Elastic $α$ transfer in the $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C scattering and its impact on the nuclear rainbow

> Elastic $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C scattering is known to exhibit the nuclear rainbow pattern at incident energies $E_\text{lab}\gtrsim 200$ MeV, with the Airy structure of the far-side scattering cross section clearly seen at medium and large angles. Such a rainbow pattern is well described by the deep real optical potential (OP) given by the double-folding model (DFM). At lower energies, the extensive elastic $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C scattering data show consistently that the nuclear rainbow pattern at backward angles is deteriorated by an oscillating enhancement of elastic cross section that is difficult to describe in the conventional optical model (OM). Given a significant $α$ spectroscopic factor predicted for the dissociation $^{16}$O$\toα+^{12}$C by the shell model and $α$-cluster models, the contribution of the elastic $α$ transfer (or the core-core exchange) to the elastic $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C scattering should not be negligible and is expected to account for the enhanced elastic cross section at backward angles. To reveal the impact of the elastic $α$ transfer, a systematic coupled reaction channels analysis of the elastic $^{16}$O+$^{12}$C scattering has been performed, with the coupling between the elastic scattering and elastic $α$ transfer channels treated explicitly, using the real OP given by the DFM. We found that the elastic $α$ transfer enhances the near-side scattering significantly at backward angles, giving rise to an oscillating distortion of the smooth Airy structure. The dynamic polarization of the OP by the coupling between the elastic scattering and elastic $α$ transfer channels can be effectively taken into account in the OM calculation by an angular-momentum (or parity) dependent potential added to the imaginary OP, as suggested by Frahn and Hussein 40 years ago.

# Elastic α transfer in the ¹⁶O+¹²C scattering and its impact on the nuclear rainbow

## Key claim
The oscillating enhancement of elastic ¹⁶O+¹²C cross sections at backward angles at low energies (E_lab ≲ 132 MeV), which deteriorates the nuclear rainbow pattern, is caused by elastic α transfer (core-core exchange) and can be described by a two-channel coupled reaction channels (CRC) calculation; the dynamic polarization of the optical potential by this coupling can be effectively represented in a one-channel optical model by an angular-momentum (parity) dependent term added to the imaginary optical potential, as suggested by Frahn and Hussein (1980).

## Method
A two-channel CRC analysis was performed coupling the elastic scattering channel ¹²C(¹⁶O,¹⁶O)¹²C and the direct elastic α transfer channel ¹²C(¹⁶O,¹²C)¹⁶O, using the real optical potential from the double-folding model (DFM) and a Woods-Saxon imaginary potential with normal diffuseness (a_V ≈ 0.5–0.6 fm). The total elastic amplitude was obtained as a coherent sum of the elastic scattering amplitude and the elastic transfer amplitude at angle (π−θ), leading to a total S-matrix S_ℓ = S_ℓ^ES + (−1)^ℓ S_ℓ^ET. The near-side and far-side decomposition of the cross section was performed using Fuller's method to isolate the impact on the nuclear rainbow Airy structure.

## Key numbers
- Incident energies studied: E_lab = 115.9, 124, and 132 MeV for elastic ¹⁶O+¹²C scattering.
- Effective α spectroscopic factor used in the two-channel CRC: S_α ≈ 1.96, consistent with earlier DWBA and CRC analyses.
- Best-fit parameters for the ℓ-dependent Majorana potential (Gaussian form) at E_lab = 115.9 MeV: W_s ≈ 1.834 MeV, β ≈ 0.775 fm, R_s ≈ 5.55 fm; at E_lab = 132 MeV: W_s ≈ 1.885 MeV, β ≈ 0.806 fm, R_s ≈ 5.55 fm.
- The elastic α transfer occurs mainly at R ≈ 5.4 fm, i.e., at the surface of the ¹⁶O+¹²C system.

## Context
This work extends the earlier multichannel CRC analysis by Phuc (2018) that included direct and indirect α transfer channels with spectroscopic factors from large-scale shell-model calculations (Volya, 2015) and the 4α cluster model (Yamada, 2012). It confirms that the anomalous large-angle scattering (ALAS) observed in light heavy-ion systems at low energies (Braun-Munzinger, 1982) arises from core-core exchange, analogous to the Mott oscillation in symmetric systems like ¹²C+¹²C and ¹⁶O+¹⁶O (Khoa, 2016). The results validate the ℓ-dependent optical model approach proposed by Frahn and Hussein (1980) and von Oertzen and Bohlen (1975), and enable future one-channel optical model analyses of elastic light heavy-ion scattering at low energies to account for cluster transfer effects without explicit CRC calculations.
