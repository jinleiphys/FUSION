# A Holographic Dual of Bjorken Flow

> We propose a consistent setup for a holographic dual of Bjorken flow of strongly coupled large-Nc N=4 SYM-theory plasma. We employ Eddington-Finkelstein type coordinates for the dual geometry, and we propose a late-time expansion there. We construct the dual geometry order by order, and we show that the transport coefficients are determined by the regularity of the geometry. We also show that the dual geometry has an apparent horizon hence an event horizon, which covers the singularity at the origin. We prove that the dual geometry is regular at all orders under an appropriate choice of the transport coefficients. Our model is a concrete well-defined example of time-dependent AdS/CFT.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a consistent holographic dual of Bjorken flow using Eddington-Finkelstein type coordinates rather than the previously used Fefferman-Graham coordinates, and proves that the dual geometry is regular at all orders in a late-time expansion under an appropriate choice of transport coefficients, thereby resolving a previously reported logarithmic singularity.

## Method

The authors construct a five-dimensional dual geometry on ingoing Eddington-Finkelstein type coordinates for strongly coupled large-Nc N=4 SYM theory plasma undergoing boost-invariant one-dimensional expansion (Bjorken flow). They employ a late-time expansion in powers of τ^{-2/3} (where τ is proper time) and solve the five-dimensional Einstein's equation with a negative cosmological constant, imposing the boundary condition that the metric asymptotes to the Bjorken flow metric in the local rest frame. The transport coefficients are determined by requiring regularity of the bulk geometry, specifically by examining the Riemann tensor projected onto a regular orthonormal basis, and the presence of an apparent horizon is computed explicitly to confirm the geometry describes a dynamical black hole.

## Key numbers

* The late-time expansion is performed with respect to τ^{-2/3}, where τ is the proper time of the Bjorken flow.
* The energy density of the Bjorken fluid at leading order scales as τ^{-4/3}, consistent with the equation of state of conformal plasma.
* The shear viscosity η0 is determined from the regularity condition at one order lower than in previous works using Fefferman-Graham coordinates.
* The location of the apparent horizon is computed explicitly to the second order in the late-time expansion, with the candidate position at v = z/τ^{1/3} = 3^{1/4} in the Fefferman-Graham coordinate analysis.
* The stress tensor components are expressed in terms of a single function a^{(4)}(τ) as: Tττ = -E a^{(4)}, Tyy = E τ^2 [a^{(4)} + τ ∂τ a^{(4)}], Tx⊥x⊥ = -E [a^{(4)} + (1/2) τ ∂τ a^{(4)}], where E = (3/2)(Nc^2/(4π^2)).

## Context

This work extends the program initiated by Janik and Peschanski (2006) on holographic descriptions of Bjorken flow, and addresses problems identified in subsequent studies by Nakamura and Sin (2006), Janik (2007), and Heller and Janik (2007) regarding the presence of event horizons and logarithmic singularities in the dual geometry. The use of Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates follows the approach of Bhattacharyya et al. (2008) and Minwalla et al. (2008) for perturbed static plasmas, and enables the authors to prove regularity at all orders and demonstrate the existence of an apparent horizon, thereby establishing a concrete well-defined example of time-dependent AdS/CFT. The results enable future computations of transport coefficients for more complicated models, such as Bjorken flow at finite coupling.
