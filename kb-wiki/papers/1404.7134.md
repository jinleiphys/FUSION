# Improved predictions for $μ\to e$ conversion in nuclei and Higgs-induced lepton flavor violation

> Compared to $μ\to e γ$ and $μ\to e e e$, the process $μ\to e$ conversion in nuclei receives enhanced contributions from Higgs-induced lepton flavor violation. Upcoming $μ\to e$ conversion experiments with drastically increased sensitivity will be able to put extremely stringent bounds on Higgs-mediated $μ\to e$ transitions. We point out that the theoretical uncertainties associated with these Higgs effects, encoded in the couplings of quark scalar operators to the nucleon, can be accurately assessed using our recently developed approach based on $SU(2)$ Chiral Perturbation Theory that cleanly separates two- and three-flavor observables. We emphasize that with input from lattice QCD for the coupling to strangeness $f_s^N$, hadronic uncertainties are appreciably reduced compared to the traditional approach where $f_s^N$ is determined from the pion--nucleon $σ$-term by means of an $SU(3)$ relation. We illustrate this point by considering Higgs-mediated lepton flavor violation in the Standard Model supplemented with higher-dimensional operators, the two-Higgs-doublet model with generic Yukawa couplings, and the Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model. Furthermore, we compare bounds from present and future $μ\to e$ conversion and $μ\to e γ$ experiments.

# Improved predictions for μ→e conversion in nuclei and Higgs-induced lepton flavor violation

## Key claim

Higgs-mediated lepton flavor violation in μ→e conversion receives enhanced contributions compared to μ→eγ and μ→eee, and the theoretical uncertainties from quark scalar couplings to nucleons can be substantially reduced by using lattice QCD input for the strangeness coupling f_s^N rather than the traditional SU(3) relation, which introduces an artificial dependence on the pion-nucleon σ-term.

## Method

The authors computed the μ→e conversion rate using an effective Lagrangian below the electroweak scale containing dipole, vector, and scalar operators up to dimension 8. They expressed the u- and d-quark scalar couplings to nucleons in terms of the pion-nucleon σ-term and isospin-breaking corrections derived from SU(2) Chiral Perturbation Theory, while taking the s-quark coupling f_s^N directly from lattice QCD averages. They then applied this framework to constrain Higgs-mediated lepton flavor violation in three scenarios: the Standard Model extended with a dimension-6 operator, the two-Higgs-doublet model with generic Yukawa couplings, and the Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model with heavy SUSY particles.

## Key numbers

- Current experimental limit on μ→e conversion in gold: Br(μ→e)^Au ≤ 7 × 10^{-13} at 90% confidence level (SINDRUM II)
- Current limit on μ→eγ: Br(μ→eγ) < 5.7 × 10^{-13} (MEG experiment)
- Current limit on μ→eee: Br(μ→eee) < 1.0 × 10^{-12}
- Future μ→e conversion experiments (Mu2e, COMET) aim to improve sensitivity by four orders of magnitude compared to SINDRUM II
- Lattice QCD average for strangeness coupling: f_s^N = 0.043 ± 0.011
- Quark mass ratio: m_s/hat(m) = 27.4 ± 0.4, with hat(m) = (m_u + m_d)/2
- Isospin-breaking parameter: ξ = (m_d - m_u)/(m_d + m_u) = 0.36 ± 0.04
- u/d quark mass ratio: m_u/m_d = 0.47 ± 0.04
- Isospin-breaking corrections to nucleon scalar couplings: Δf_u^p = (1.0 ± 0.2)×10^{-3}, Δf_u^n = (-1.0 ± 0.2)×10^{-3}, Δf_d^p = (-2.1 ± 0.4)×10^{-3}, Δf_d^n = (2.0 ± 0.4)×10^{-3}
- Overlap integrals for gold target: D = 0.189, S^p = 0.0614, V^p = 0.0974, S^n = 0.0918, V^n = 0.146
- Overlap integrals for aluminum target: D = 0.0362, S^p = 0.0155, V^p = 0.0161, S^n = 0.0167, V^n = 0.0173
- Future μ→eγ sensitivity projections: 1.6×10^{-14} and 6×10^{-15}
- CMS excludes heavy Higges in 2HDM with masses below 480 GeV for tanβ = 50
- Charged Higgs mass lower limit from b→sγ: 380 GeV in 2HDM type II

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SU(2) Chiral Perturbation Theory approach (Crivellin, Hoferichter, Procura 2013) for nucleon scalar couplings to the specific case of μ→e conversion, improving upon the traditional method of Cheng (1988) and Ellis (2000) that used SU(3) relations to extract the strangeness content. The analysis enables more precise predictions for upcoming experiments such as Mu2e at FNAL and COMET at J-PARC, which will probe μ→e conversion rates four orders of magnitude below current SINDRUM II limits. The results also connect to earlier work by Cirigliano (2009) on the effective Lagrangian for μ→e conversion and by Kitano (2002) on the overlap integrals for muon capture.
