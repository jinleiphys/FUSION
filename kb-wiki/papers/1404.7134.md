# Improved predictions for $Ој\to e$ conversion in nuclei and Higgs-induced lepton flavor violation

> Compared to $Ој\to e Оі$ and $Ој\to e e e$, the process $Ој\to e$ conversion in nuclei receives enhanced contributions from Higgs-induced lepton flavor violation. Upcoming $Ој\to e$ conversion experiments with drastically increased sensitivity will be able to put extremely stringent bounds on Higgs-mediated $Ој\to e$ transitions. We point out that the theoretical uncertainties associated with these Higgs effects, encoded in the couplings of quark scalar operators to the nucleon, can be accurately assessed using our recently developed approach based on $SU(2)$ Chiral Perturbation Theory that cleanly separates two- and three-flavor observables. We emphasize that with input from lattice QCD for the coupling to strangeness $f_s^N$, hadronic uncertainties are appreciably reduced compared to the traditional approach where $f_s^N$ is determined from the pion--nucleon $Пғ$-term by means of an $SU(3)$ relation. We illustrate this point by considering Higgs-mediated lepton flavor violation in the Standard Model supplemented with higher-dimensional operators, the two-Higgs-doublet model with generic Yukawa couplings, and the Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model. Furthermore, we compare bounds from present and future $Ој\to e$ conversion and $Ој\to e Оі$ experiments.

## Method

The authors computed the ОјвҶ’e conversion rate using an effective Lagrangian below the electroweak scale containing dipole, vector, and scalar operators up to dimension 8. They expressed the u- and d-quark scalar couplings to nucleons in terms of the pion-nucleon Пғ-term and isospin-breaking corrections derived from SU(2) Chiral Perturbation Theory, while taking the s-quark coupling f_s^N directly from lattice QCD averages. They then applied this framework to constrain Higgs-mediated lepton flavor violation in three scenarios: the Standard Model extended with a dimension-6 operator, the two-Higgs-doublet model with generic Yukawa couplings, and the Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model with heavy SUSY particles.
