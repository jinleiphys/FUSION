# Nucleon Electric Dipole Moments and the Isovector Parity- and Time-Reversal-Odd Pion-Nucleon Coupling

> The isovector time-reversal- and parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling $\bar{g}_π^{(1)}$ is uniquely sensitive to dimension six interactions between right-handed light quarks and the Standard Model Higgs doublet that naturally arises in left-right symmetric models. Recent work has used the $\bar{g}_π^{(1)}$-induced one-loop contribution to the neutron electric dipole moment $d_n$, together with the present experimental $d_n$ bound, to constrain the CP-violating parameters of the left-right symmetric model. We show that this and related analyses are based on an earlier meson theory $d_n$ computation that is not consistent with the power-counting appropriate for an effective field theory. We repeat the one-loop calculation using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory and find that the resulting $d_n$ sensitivity to $\bar{g}_π^{(1)}$ is suppressed, implying more relaxed constraints on the parameter space of the left-right symmetric model. Assuming no cancellations between this loop contribution and other contributions, such as the leading order EDM low-energy constant, the present limit on $d_n$ implies $|\bar{g}_π^{(1)}| \lesssim 1.1\times 10^{-10}$.

## Key claim

The authors show that previous calculations of the neutron electric dipole moment (EDM) sensitivity to the isovector parity- and time-reversal-odd pion-nucleon coupling \(\bar{g}_\pi^{(1)}\) overestimated the effect because they used a relativistic meson theory that is not consistent with effective field theory power counting. When recalculated using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, the loop contribution is suppressed by \((m_\pi/m_N)^2 \sim 0.02\), implying more relaxed constraints on left-right symmetric model parameters than previously claimed.

## Method

The authors performed a one-loop calculation of the nucleon EDM induced by the isovector TVPV pion-nucleon coupling \(\bar{g}_\pi^{(1)}\) using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT), which implements consistent power counting by expanding about the static nucleon limit (\(m_N \to \infty\)) and the chiral limit (\(m_\pi \to 0\)). They constructed the relevant \(P\)- and \(T\)-conserving and TVPV Lagrangians at leading, next-to-leading, and next-to-next-to-leading order in the \(Q/m_N\) expansion, then computed the one-loop diagrams contributing to the neutron and proton EDMs, including contributions from the anomalous magnetic moments that appear at next-to-next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers

- The neutron EDM contribution proportional to \(\bar{g}_\pi^{(1)}\) and the anomalous magnetic moments is suppressed by \((m_\pi/m_N)^2 \sim 0.02\) relative to the earlier relativistic calculation.
- Assuming no cancellations with other contributions, the present experimental limit \(|d_n| < 2.9 \times 10^{-26}\,e\,\text{cm}\) from Baker (2006) implies \(|\bar{g}_\pi^{(1)}| \lesssim 1.1 \times 10^{-10}\).
- The isoscalar TVPV coupling \(\bar{g}_\pi^{(0)}\) is suppressed relative to \(\bar{g}_\pi^{(1)}\) by the ratio \(\bar{g}_\pi^{(0)}/\bar{g}_\pi^{(1)} = \delta m_N/(2\Delta m_N) \simeq 0.02\) for the four-quark operator considered.
- The bound on \(\bar{g}_\pi^{(1)}\) limits the deuteron EDM to \(d_d < 10^{-24}\,e\,\text{cm}\) using the results of de Vries (2011).

## Context

This work corrects earlier calculations by He (1992) that used a relativistic meson-nucleon field theory with pseudoscalar pion-nucleon coupling, which gave a leading contribution proportional to \(\bar{g}_\pi^{(1)}\kappa_n\) without the expected \(m_\pi/m_N\) suppression. The corrected HBChPT calculation implies weaker constraints on the left-right symmetric model parameters than those derived by Zhang (2007) and Ji (2008) from the neutron EDM bound. The results enable more reliable constraints on the dimension-six operator \(\mathcal{O}_{\varphi ud}\) that arises in left-right symmetric models, and the authors note that diamagnetic atom EDMs and storage-ring searches for light nuclei EDMs (such as the deuteron) may provide cleaner probes of this operator in the future.
