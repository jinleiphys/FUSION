# Exact solution of the nonlinear boson diffusion equation for gluon scattering

> An exact analytical solution of the nonlinear boson diffusion equation (NBDE) is presented. It accounts for the time evolution towards the Bose-Einstein equilibrium distribution through inelastic and elastic collisions in case of constant transport coefficients. As a currently interesting application, gluon scattering in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is investigated. An estimate of time-dependent gluon-condensate formation in overoccupied systems through number-conserving elastic scatterings in Pb-Pb collisions at relativistic energies is given.

## Key claim

The paper presents an exact analytical solution of the nonlinear boson diffusion equation (NBDE) for gluon scattering, including boundary conditions at the singularity epsilon = mu, which was previously only solved numerically. The authors note that due to the faster timescale of inelastic collisions, transient gluon-condensate formation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is actually unlikely to occur, although it initially appeared as an intriguing possibility.

## Method

The authors solve the NBDE with constant transport coefficients using a nonlinear transformation that maps it to a linear diffusion equation for a time-dependent partition function. For bosons, they impose boundary conditions at the singularity epsilon = mu (with mu <= 0) using a bounded Green's function that vanishes at the singularity, then compute the partition function and its energy derivative analytically for theta-function initial conditions representing gluon saturation. The time-dependent gluon distribution is obtained through the logarithmic derivative of the partition function, and the transport coefficients are determined from the equilibrium temperature and local equilibration time.

## Key numbers

- Equilibrium temperature T = 443 MeV for Q_s = 1 GeV and initial occupation n_i^0 = 1
- Transport coefficients for overoccupied system with n_i^0 = 1: D = 0.67 GeV^2/fm, v = -1.51 GeV/fm
- Local equilibration time tau_eq = 0.13 fm used for the calculation
- Initial central temperature in sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions determined to be T_ini approximately 480 MeV, corresponding to n_i^0 approximately 1.38
- Upper limit of equilibration time from coupled kinetic equations: tau_eq <= 0.25 fm
- Freezeout time in central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies: 8-10 fm
- Critical initial occupation for particle-number conservation: n_i^0 = 0.154

## Context

This work extends the nonlinear diffusion model for parton thermalization proposed by Wolschin (2018, 2022) by providing the exact analytical solution for gluons with boundary conditions, which was previously computed numerically. The results are consistent with QCD-based numerical calculations in the small-angle approximation by Blaizot et al. (2017) that are possible only in the infrared. The analytical solution enables transparent study of both inelastic (number-violating) and elastic (number-conserving) gluon scattering, including time-dependent gluon-condensate formation in overoccupied systems as initially proposed by Kurkela and Moore (2012).
