# Hyperon mixing and universal many-body repulsion in neutron stars

> A multi-pomeron exchange potential (MPP) is proposed as a model for the universal many-body repulsion in baryonic systems on the basis of the Extended Soft Core (ESC) bryon-baryon interaction. The strength of MPP is determined by analyzing the nucleus-nucleus scattering with the G-matrix folding model. The interaction in $ΛN$ channels is shown to reproduce well the experimental $Λ$ binding energies. The equation of state (EoS) in neutron matter with hyperon mixing is obtained including the MPP contribution, and mass-radius relations of neutron stars are derived. It is shown that the maximum mass can be larger than the observed one $2M_{\odot}$ even in the case of including hyperon mixing on the basis of model-parameters determined by terrestrial experiments.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a universal many-body repulsion among three baryons (the multi-pomeron exchange potential, MPP) can stiffen the neutron-star equation of state enough to produce maximum masses above 2 solar masses even when hyperon mixing softens the EoS, provided the MPP strength is determined from terrestrial nucleus-nucleus scattering data rather than from astronomical observations.

## Method

The authors construct a baryon-baryon interaction model combining the Extended Soft Core (ESC) potential with a multi-pomeron exchange potential (MPP) for universal three- and four-body repulsion and a phenomenological three-nucleon attraction (TNA). The MPP strengths are determined by fitting G-matrix double-folding model calculations to experimental angular distributions of 16O+16O elastic scattering at E/A=70 MeV. The resulting interaction is then used in lowest-order G-matrix calculations with continuous choice to derive the equation of state for beta-stable neutron-star matter containing neutrons, protons, electrons, muons, and hyperons (Λ and Σ-), and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation is solved to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron-star masses: 2.5 M⊙ for MPa, 2.2 M⊙ for MPb, and 2.1 M⊙ for MPc
- Saturation density ρ0 = 0.16 fm-3 for all three parameter sets
- Energy per nucleon at saturation: -15.8 MeV for MPa/MPb, -15.5 MeV for MPc
- Symmetry energy Esym at ρ0: 33.1 MeV (MPa, MPb), 32.7 MeV (MPc)
- Slope parameter L: 70 MeV (MPa), 69 MeV (MPb), 67 MeV (MPc)
- Incompressibility K at ρ0: 310 MeV (MPa), 280 MeV (MPb), 260 MeV (MPc)
- Λ potential energy UΛ at normal density: -38.1 MeV (MPa), -38.3 MeV (MPb), -37.4 MeV (MPc), -40.0 MeV (ESC)
- MPP parameters: gP(3)=2.34, gP(4)=30.0 (MPa); gP(3)=2.94, gP(4)=0.0 (MPb); gP(3)=2.34, gP(4)=0.0 (MPc)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Yamamoto et al. (2013) by using the MPP determined from 16O+16O scattering to construct neutron-star equations of state, and it addresses the hyperon puzzle identified by Baldo et al. (2000) and Vidana et al. (2000), where hyperon mixing softens the EoS and reduces maximum masses. The paper contrasts its approach with relativistic mean-field models by Weissenborn et al., Bednarek et al., and Jiang et al., emphasizing that it uses no adjustable parameters beyond those determined from terrestrial experiments. The results enable future studies of whether the universal MPP repulsion can simultaneously explain nuclear saturation, hypernuclear binding energies, and massive neutron stars.
