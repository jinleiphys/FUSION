# Probing momentum dependence of hydrodynamization in heavy-ion collisions

> The fluidity of the hot and dense QCD matter is a key characteristic of the medium created in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. We extend the framework of the relativistic hydrodynamic model to incorporate non-thermal momentum distributions that may emerge during the dynamical evolution of the collision system. Numerical simulations are performed to elucidate the phenomenological implications of these modifications on charged hadrons and direct photons measured in collider experiments.

# Probing momentum dependence of hydrodynamization in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The paper extends relativistic hydrodynamic modeling to incorporate non-thermal momentum distributions that may emerge during heavy-ion collisions, but the applicability of these extensions is momentum-dependent: Tsallis statistics with parameter q=1.07 extends the hydrodynamic description of charged hadron spectra up to 6-8 GeV, while elliptic flow v₂ suggests a narrower applicability range than the spectra alone would indicate.

## Method

The author develops two modified hydrodynamic frameworks: (i) a "violet" model using Tsallis statistics (parameter q) to include high-pT tails in the hydrodynamic description, and (ii) a "red" model imposing a momentum cutoff p_c to exclude medium-high momentum components from the bulk medium. Numerical (2+1)-dimensional inviscid hydrodynamic simulations with Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions for 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions are performed, and the effects on charged hadron spectra, elliptic flow, and direct photon observables are computed using Tsallis-extended Cooper-Frye particlization and modified photon emission rates.

## Key numbers

- Tsallis parameter q = 1.07 extends charged hadron pT spectra description from 2-3 GeV (thermal, q=1) up to 6-8 GeV for 0-5% and 20-30% centrality classes in 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions
- Connection temperature T_c = 0.14 GeV used for the equation of state connecting hadron resonance gas and parton gas phases
- Photon emission rates are connected around T_ph = 0.17 GeV using a hyperbolic tangent switching function
- Momentum cutoff values p_c are applied in the range including 2 GeV, where cutoffs have negligible effects on the hadronic phase pressure when p_c ≥ 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the relativistic hydrodynamic model framework by incorporating non-thermal momentum distributions, building on prior Tsallis statistics applications by Osada (2008), Takacs (2019), and Kyan (2022), and on the "red" hydrodynamic model by Monnai (2023). The results enable future investigations of how momentum-dependent hydrodynamization affects flow observables, with prospects for introducing viscosity and event-by-event analyses to further test the two scenarios against experimental data from ALICE (2012).
