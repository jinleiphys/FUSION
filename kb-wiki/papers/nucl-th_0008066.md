# Algebraic model of baryon structure

> We discuss properties of baryon resonances belonging to the Nucleon, Delta, Sigma, Lambda, Xi and Omega families in a collective string-like model for the nucleon, in which the radial excitations are interpreted as rotations and vibrations of the string configuration. We find good overall agreement with the available data. The main discrepancies are found for low lying S-wave states, in particular N(1535), N(1650), Sigma(1750), Lambda*(1405), Lambda(1670) and Lambda(1800).

# Algebraic model of baryon structure

## Key claim
The collective string-like qqq model with U(7) spectrum generating algebra provides good overall agreement with baryon mass spectra and strong decay widths, but the main discrepancies occur for low-lying S-wave states (N(1535), N(1650), Sigma(1750), Lambda*(1405), Lambda(1670), Lambda(1800)) whose masses lie near meson-baryon thresholds, suggesting these resonances may contain exotic non-qqq configurations such as quasi-molecular qqq-qbar states.

## Method
The authors constructed a mass formula combining a U(7) spectrum generating algebra for radial excitations (treated as rotations and vibrations of an oblate symmetric top) with a Gürsey-Radicati spin-flavor interaction, and fitted nine coefficients to 48 three- and four-star baryon resonances assigned as octet and decuplet states. Strong decay widths were calculated using an elementary emission model with a collective form factor, fitting the transition operator to N pi and Delta pi channels and applying the same operator to all resonances and decay channels.

## Key numbers
- r.m.s. deviation of mass fit: delta = 33 MeV for 48 resonances across N, Delta, Sigma, Lambda, Xi, Omega families (compared to delta = 39 MeV in a previous fit to 25 N and Delta resonances)
- M_0^2 = 0.882 GeV^2 (determined by the nucleon mass)
- Lambda*(1405) overpredicted by 236 MeV, Lambda*(1520) by 121 MeV, Lambda*(2100) by 97 MeV (singlet states not included in the fit)
- Third P_11 nucleon resonance predicted at 1713 MeV (compared to 1740 +/- 11 MeV from Zagreb group analysis and 1880 MeV in the relativized quark model)
- New D_13 resonance at 1895 MeV assigned to a vibrational excitation (v1,v2)=(0,1) with configuration 2_8_{3/2}[56,1^-] at 1847 MeV
- Delta(1232)P_33 N pi decay width: calculated 116 MeV vs experimental 119 +/- 5 MeV
- Calculated eta decay widths for N(1535), Sigma(1750), Lambda(1670) are less than 0.5 MeV, compared to experimental partial widths of 74 +/- 39, 39 +/- 28, and 9 +/- 5 MeV respectively
- Lambda*(1405) decay width into Sigma pi: 50 +/- 2 MeV (experimental)

## Context
This work extends the algebraic U(7) approach previously applied to N and Delta resonances (Bijker and Leviatan, 1998) to the full baryon octet and decuplet including strange baryons, achieving improved r.m.s. deviation (33 MeV vs 39 MeV). The results are compared to the relativized quark model (Capstick and Isgur, 1986) which requires separate energy shifts for positive and negative parity states. The analysis identifies specific resonances near meson-baryon thresholds that may indicate exotic configurations, building on coupled-channel studies by the Zagreb group and chiral meson-baryon approaches (Kaiser, 1995; Arima, 1997). The work enables future experimental searches for non-qqq configurations in baryon spectroscopy.
