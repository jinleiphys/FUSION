# Quantum baryon number fluctuations in subsystems of a hot and dense relativistic gas of fermions

> Quantum features of the baryon number fluctuations in subsystems of a hot and dense relativistic gas of fermions are analyzed. We find that the fluctuations in small systems are significantly increased compared to their values known from the statistical physics, and diverge in the limit where the system size goes to zero. The numerical results obtained for a broad range of the thermodynamic parameters expected in heavy-ion collisions are presented. They can be helpful to interpret and shed new light on the experimental data.

## Key claim

Quantum baryon number fluctuations in small subsystems of a hot and dense relativistic fermion gas are significantly larger than classical statistical fluctuations and diverge as the subsystem size approaches zero, but the effect is only present for subsystem sizes below about 1 fm, above which the quantum result reproduces the standard thermodynamic susceptibility.

## Method

The authors define a baryon number density operator using a Gaussian spatial smearing profile of width \(a\) placed at the origin, applied to the Dirac field operator for spin-1/2 fermions. They compute the variance of this smeared operator using thermal expectation values of products of creation and annihilation operators with Fermi-Dirac distributions, then numerically evaluate the resulting integrals for a range of temperatures, baryon chemical potentials, and particle masses relevant to heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- Normalized fluctuation \(\sigma_n\) diverges as the subsystem size \(a\) approaches zero for all parameter values studied.
- For a fixed mass \(m = 1000\) MeV and chemical potential \(\mu_B = 400\) MeV, \(\sigma_n\) decreases with increasing temperature in the range \(100\) MeV \(\leq T \leq 250\) MeV.
- For fixed \(m = 1000\) MeV and \(T = 150\) MeV, \(\sigma_n\) decreases with increasing \(\mu_B\) in the range \(300\) MeV \(\leq \mu_B \leq 900\) MeV.
- For fixed \(T = 100\) MeV and \(\mu_B = 400\) MeV, \(\sigma_n\) increases with particle mass in the range \(0 \leq m \leq 1000\) MeV.
- The volume-scaled fluctuation \(V_a \sigma^2\) approaches the classical thermodynamic limit \(T^3 \chi_2^{(B)}\) for \(a > 1\) fm, with the ratio \(V_a \sigma^2 / (T^3 \chi_2^{(B)})\) approaching unity in the large-\(a\) limit.
- The internal degeneracy factor used in numerical results is \(g = 10\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous framework for energy density fluctuations in boson and fermion gases (Das 2020, Das 2021) to the case of baryon number fluctuations, which are directly relevant to the beam energy scan (BES) program searching for the QCD critical endpoint. The results show that for subsystem sizes below about 0.5 fm, fluctuations become comparable to mean values, challenging the standard fluid-cell picture used in relativistic hydrodynamics for heavy-ion collisions. The formalism enables future generalization to hadron gases and may provide a more appropriate baseline for interpreting experimental fluctuation data, particularly in small collision systems where enhanced fluctuations could be misattributed to critical phenomena.
