# One-pion exchange current corrections for nuclear magnetic moments in relativistic mean field theory

> The one-pion exchange current corrections to isoscalar and isovector magnetic moments of double-closed shell nuclei plus and minus one nucleon with $A=15,17,39$ and 41 have been studied in the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory and compared with previous relativistic and non-relativistic results. It has been found that the one-pion exchange current gives a negligible contribution to the isoscalar magnetic moments but a significant correction to the isovector ones. However, the one-pion exchange current doesn't improve the description of nuclear isovector magnetic moments for the concerned nuclei.

## Key claim

The one-pion exchange current gives a negligible contribution to isoscalar magnetic moments but a significant positive correction to isovector magnetic moments; however, this correction does not improve the agreement with experimental data for the isovector magnetic moments of double-closed shell nuclei plus or minus one nucleon with A=15, 17, 39, and 41.

## Method

The authors performed axially deformed relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations using the PK1 effective interaction, including non-zero space-like components of the vector meson fields, to obtain single-particle Dirac spinors for the nuclei of interest. The one-pion exchange current corrections to magnetic moments were then computed using spherical Dirac spinors from the corresponding double-closed shell nuclei, evaluating the seagull and in-flight diagrams with a pion-nucleon coupling constant fπ=1 and pion mass mπ=138 MeV.

## Key numbers

- One-pion exchange current correction to isovector magnetic moment for A=15: 0.091 (RMF+PK1), compared to 0.102 from linear RMF (Morse 1990) and 0.092 from non-relativistic calculation (Towner 1983)
- One-pion exchange current correction to isovector magnetic moment for A=17: 0.092 (RMF+PK1), compared to 0.151 from linear RMF (Morse 1990)
- One-pion exchange current correction to isovector magnetic moment for A=39: 0.190 (RMF+PK1), compared to 0.174 from linear RMF (Morse 1990)
- One-pion exchange current correction to isovector magnetic moment for A=41: 0.184 (RMF+PK1), compared to 0.270 from linear RMF (Morse 1990)
- Isoscalar magnetic moment for A=15: 0.216 (RMF+MEC), with MEC contribution of 0.000; experimental value is 0.218
- Isoscalar magnetic moment for A=17: 1.467 (RMF+MEC), with MEC contribution of -0.002; experimental value is 1.414
- Isoscalar magnetic moment for A=39: 0.707 (RMF+MEC), with MEC contribution of 0.000; experimental value is 0.706
- Isoscalar magnetic moment for A=41: 1.988 (RMF+MEC), with MEC contribution of -0.003; experimental value is 1.918
- Isovector magnetic moment for A=15: -0.339 (RMF+MEC), with MEC contribution of +0.091; experimental value is -0.501
- Isovector magnetic moment for A=17: 3.576 (RMF+MEC), with MEC contribution of +0.092; experimental value is 3.308
- Isovector magnetic moment for A=39: -0.115 (RMF+MEC), with MEC contribution of +0.190; experimental value is -0.316
- Isovector magnetic moment for A=41: 4.322 (RMF+MEC), with MEC contribution of +0.184; experimental value is 3.512

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic calculations by Morse (1990) using linear RMF with L3 interaction, and non-relativistic calculations by Towner (1987) and Arima (1987) that included second-order core polarization, meson exchange currents, and delta isobar currents. The study shows that while deformed RMF with time-odd fields reproduces isoscalar magnetic moments well, the one-pion exchange current correction worsens the already poor agreement for isovector magnetic moments, indicating that additional effects (second-order core polarization, delta isobar current, exchange currents from other mesons, and rotational symmetry restoration) must be included in future relativistic calculations.
