# Rare Isotope Formation in Complete Fusion and Multinucleon Transfer Reactions in Collisions of 48Ca +248Cm around Coulomb Barrier Energies

> Within the framework of the dinuclear system model, the reaction mechanisms for synthesizing target-like isotopes from Bk to compound nuclei Lv are thoroughly investigated in complete and incomplete fusion reaction of $^{48}$Ca +$^{248}$Cm around Coulomb barrier energies. Production cross-section of $^{292,293}$Lv as a function of excitation energy in fusion-evaporation reactions and target-like isotopic yields in multinucleon transfer reactions are evaluated, in which a statistical approach is used to describe the decay process of excited nuclei. The available experimental data can be reproduced well with the model reasonably. The products of all possible formed isotopes in the dynamical pre-equilibrium process for collision partners at incident energy $E_{\rm lab}$ = 5.5 MeV/nucleon are exported, systematically. It is found that the quasi-fission fragments are dominant in the yields. The optimal pathway from the target to compound nuclei shows up along the valley of potential surface energy. The effective impact parameter of two colliding partners leading to compound nuclei is selected from head-on collision to semi-central collision with $L$ = 52 $\rm \hbar$. The timescale boundary between complete fusion and multinucleon transfer reactions is about 5.7$\times 10^{-21}$ s with effective impact parameters. Synthesis cross-section of unknown neutron-rich actinides from Bk to Rf have been predicted around several nanobarns.

## Key claim
The dinuclear system model reproduces available experimental data for both fusion-evaporation and multinucleon transfer products in the $^{48}$Ca + $^{248}$Cm reaction, but the claim that quasi-fission fragments dominate the yields is conditional on the incident energy of $E_{\rm lab}$ = 5.5 MeV/nucleon and on the specific dynamical pre-equilibrium treatment used.

## Method
The authors solve master equations for the time-dependent probability distribution of fragment proton number, neutron number, excitation energy, and quadrupole deformation within the dinuclear system (DNS) framework. The local excitation energy is determined from dissipation of relative motion and the potential energy surface, with interaction times obtained from the deflection function method. The decay of excited primary fragments is treated with a statistical Weisskopf evaporation approach, and cross-sections for both fusion-evaporation residues and multinucleon transfer survival fragments are calculated by summing over angular momenta with barrier distributions.

## Key numbers
- Coulomb barrier of the $^{48}$Ca + $^{248}$Cm colliding system: approximately 185 MeV
- Inner fusion barrier $B_{\rm fus}$ = 5.24 MeV for the collision partners
- Timescale boundary between complete fusion and multinucleon transfer reactions: about $5.7 \times 10^{-21}$ s with effective impact parameters
- Effective impact parameter leading to compound nuclei: $L$ = 52 $\hbar$, from head-on to semi-central collisions
- For the 4n evaporation channel producing $^{292}$Lv at $E^*$ = 38.9 MeV: cross-section of ($3.3^{+2.5}_{-1.4}$) pb (FLNR data)
- For the 3n evaporation channel producing $^{293}$Lv at $E^*$ = 33.0 MeV: cross-section of ($0.5^{+0.5}_{-0.26}$) pb (FLNR data)
- For the 4n channel at $E^*$ = 40.9 MeV: cross-section of ($3.4^{+2.7}_{-1.6}$) pb (GSI data)
- For the 4n channel at $E^*$ = 41.3 MeV: cross-section of ($3.1^{+2.8}_{-1.8}$) pb (RIKEN data)
- Synthesis cross-sections of unknown neutron-rich actinides from Bk to Rf predicted around several nanobarns
- Deformation relaxation time $\tau_{\beta} = 4 \times 10^{-21}$ s

## Context
This work extends the dinuclear system model approach previously applied by Feng (2009) and others to the specific system $^{48}$Ca + $^{248}$Cm, which has been studied experimentally at FLNR (Oganessian, 2000), GSI (Hofmann, 2012), and RIKEN (Kaji, 2017) for superheavy element synthesis. The calculations reproduce the measured excitation functions for 3n and 4n evaporation channels producing Livermorium isotopes and the target-like fragment yields from Bk to Fm measured at GSI (Hofmann, 1985) and LBL (Gaggeler, 1986). The results enable predictions for unknown neutron-rich actinide production cross-sections and clarify the competition between complete fusion and multinucleon transfer pathways in heavy-ion collisions near the Coulomb barrier.
