# Nuclear size, electric monopole transitions, and the location of $0^+_2$ states

> The work addresses the isotopic shift of nuclear radii for the even-even $^{36-52}$Ca isotopes using the interacting boson model (IBM) that includes the mixing from normal and intruder configurations. We obtain a good agreement between the calculated and experimental data, particularly for the dip at $^{48}$Ca. A direct correlation between nuclear size and electric monopole transitions is established to compute the electric monopole transition strengths, $ρ^2(E0)$. We further study the isotopic shift for the even-even $^{32-46}$Ar and $^{44-50}$Ti isotopes.

## Key claim
The paper establishes a direct correlation between nuclear charge radii (isotopic shifts) and electric monopole (E0) transition strengths within the interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM), showing that the same deformation parameters extracted from charge radii data can predict ρ²(E0) values, particularly for the even-even calcium isotopes. The abstract's claim of "good agreement" is qualified by the authors' explicit statement that the IBM-CM does not explain the isotopic shift of ³⁸Ca and ⁴²Ca due to the limited number of active bosons influencing the n̂_d matrix element contributions.

## Method
The authors employed the interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM), incorporating normal [n_b] and intruder [n_b+2] boson spaces to describe 0p-0h and 2p-2h particle-hole excitations, respectively. The Hamiltonian parameters (ε, a₁, a₂, χ, Δ, mixing strengths α=β) were fitted to reproduce low-lying level energies, with ε fixed from the 2₁⁺ state energy and Δ from the 0₂⁺ state energy. Charge radii were computed using an operator with linear and deformation-dependent terms, and the same deformation parameters (η_reg, η_int) were then used to calculate ρ²(E0; 0₂⁺→0₁⁺) strengths, establishing the claimed correlation. Calculations were performed for even-even ³⁶⁻⁵²Ca, ³²⁻⁴⁶Ar, and ⁴⁴⁻⁵⁰Ti isotopes, with ⁴⁰Ca as the inert core.

## Key numbers
- The dip in isotopic shift at ⁴⁸Ca is reproduced, with the charge radius of ⁴⁸Ca being almost equal to that of ⁴⁰Ca (consistent with doubly-magic nature).
- The 0₂⁺ state in ⁴⁸Ca has a half-life of 0.22 ns with a 22.5% E0 branch; the 0₂⁺ state in ⁴⁰Ca has a half-life of 2.17 ns.
- The 2₁⁺ level in ⁵⁰Ca displays a sharp dip at 1.026 MeV, compared to 3.831 MeV in ⁴⁸Ca and 2.563 MeV in ⁵²Ca.
- The deformation parameters used for Ca isotopes are η_reg = 8.69 fm² and η_int = 0.59 fm², with κ_reg = 0.0005 fm².
- The mixing probabilities for the ground state of ⁵⁰Ca show 98% [n_b+2] space (0.02 regular, 0.98 intruder), while for ⁴⁸Ca the ground state is 22% regular and 78% intruder.

## Context
This work extends the IBM-CM framework previously applied by Maheshwari (2024) to ⁴²⁻⁴⁶Ca isotopes, now covering the full ³⁶⁻⁵²Ca chain including proton-rich and neutron-rich isotopes. The results contradict earlier shell-model calculations (Caurier, 2001) that explained the ⁴⁸Ca dip using the number of protons lifted from the sd- to pf-shell, but which cannot reproduce the 0₂⁺ state location in ³⁶⁻³⁸Ca. The correlation between charge radii and E0 transitions enables future predictions of ρ²(E0) values for nuclei where only charge radii data exist, particularly for ⁵⁰⁻⁵²Ca where spectroscopic data are limited. The work also addresses the Ar and Ti isotopic chains as two-proton-hole and two-proton-particle systems relative to Ca, respectively.
