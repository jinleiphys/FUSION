# Thermal, chemical and spectral equilibration in heavy-ion collisions

> We have considered the equilibration in a relativistic heavy ion collision using our transport model. We applied periodic boundary conditions to close the system in a box. We found that the thermal equilibration takes place in the first 20-40 fm/c which time is comparable to the duration of a heavy ion collision. The chemical equilibration is a much slower process and the system does not equilibrate in a heavy ion collision. We have also shown that the mass spectra of broad resonances immediately follows their in-medium spectral functions.

## Key claim
Thermal (kinetic) equilibration in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at 1-7 AGeV occurs within 20-40 fm/c, a timescale comparable to the collision duration, but full chemical equilibration requires much longer times (100-500 fm/c depending on density) and therefore does not occur during a real heavy-ion collision. Additionally, the mass spectra of broad resonances in a testparticle simulation of the Boltzmann equation instantaneously follow their in-medium spectral functions, showing no memory effects, in contrast to the full Kadanoff-Baym equations where substantial memory effects were found by Schenke and Greiner.

## Method
The authors used a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model with periodic boundary conditions to confine the system to a cubic box, simulating strongly interacting matter from initialized C+C and Ca+Ca collisions at beam energies of 1, 3, and 7 AGeV. They studied thermalization by monitoring the time evolution of velocity distributions of nucleons and momentum distributions of pions, and chemical equilibration by tracking particle multiplicities over time, with total energy fixed by rescaling baryon momenta each time step. For vector meson spectral function studies, they inserted 10000 ρ and ω testparticles with density-dependent self-energies into the equilibrated medium and evolved them using the equations of Cassing, Juchem, and Leupold derived from the Kadanoff-Baym equations via gradient expansion.

## Key numbers
- Thermal equilibration time: 20-40 fm/c for 7 AGeV C+C collisions; approximately 20 fm/c for 1 and 3 AGeV C+C collisions.
- Chemical equilibration time: approximately 200 fm/c for 7 AGeV C+C collisions (density 0.192 fm⁻³); 300 fm/c for 3 AGeV; 500 fm/c for 1 AGeV; reduces to 100 fm/c for 7 AGeV Ca+Ca collisions (density 0.64 fm⁻³).
- Box edge length: a = 5 fm.
- Normal nuclear density: ρ₀ = 0.168 fm⁻³; average density in C+C box: 0.192 fm⁻³; in Ca+Ca box: 0.64 fm⁻³.
- Pion momentum distributions converge to Boltzmann distributions with T = 80 MeV (1 AGeV) and T = 125 MeV (7 AGeV).
- Vacuum masses and widths used: M₀^ρ = 768 MeV, Γ₀^ρ = 150 MeV; M₀^ω = 782 MeV, Γ₀^ω = 8.5 MeV.
- Self-energy parameters: M_x = 682 MeV, Γ_x = 8.5 or 40 MeV for ω; M_x = 668 MeV, Γ_x = 150 MeV for ρ.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of thermal and chemical equilibration in heavy-ion collisions by Belkacem et al. and Bravina et al. (2000) at higher AGS and SPS energies, and by Bratkovskaya et al. at low SIS energies, by providing a systematic comparison of thermal and chemical equilibration temperatures. The authors also compare their transport-model results for vector meson spectral functions to the quantum field theoretical results of Schenke and Greiner, who solved the full Kadanoff-Baym equations and found memory effects absent in the gradient-expansion approach of Cassing, Juchem, and Leupold. This work enables a clearer understanding of which equilibration assumptions are valid in thermal and hydrodynamical models versus transport models for heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies.
