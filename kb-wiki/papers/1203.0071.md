# Complete Fusion Enhancement and Suppression of Weakly Bound Nuclei at Near Barrier Energies

> We consider the influence of breakup channels on the complete fusion of weakly bound systems in terms of dynamic polarization potentials. It is argued that the enhancement of the cross section at sub-barrier energies may be consistent with recent experimental observations that nucleon transfer, often leading to breakup, is dominant compared to direct breakup. The main trends of the experimental complete fusion cross section for $^{6,7}$Li + $^{209}$Bi are analyzed in the framework of the DPP approach.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed enhancement of complete fusion cross sections at sub-barrier energies for weakly bound nuclei can be explained by the dominance of nucleon transfer (often leading to breakup) over direct breakup, producing an attractive real polarization potential that lowers the fusion barrier; this interpretation is presented as consistent with experimental observations rather than definitively proven.

## Method

The authors analyze experimental complete fusion cross sections for the \(^{6,7}\)Li + \(^{209}\)Bi systems using the dynamic polarization potential (DPP) approach, comparing renormalized fusion functions to the Universal Fusion Function (UFF) benchmark. They interpret the energy-dependent behavior of the real and imaginary parts of the polarization potential arising from couplings to breakup and transfer channels, distinguishing between effects of direct breakup (short-lived unbound states) and breakup triggered by nucleon transfer (delayed breakup).

## Key numbers

* Complete fusion suppression above the Coulomb barrier: 34% for \(^{6}\)Li + \(^{209}\)Bi and 26% for \(^{7}\)Li + \(^{209}\)Bi, relative to calculations without breakup effects.
* Breakup threshold energies: 1.48 MeV for \(^{6}\)Li and 2.47 MeV for \(^{7}\)Li (with one bound excited state at 0.478 MeV).
* Complete fusion cross sections at sub-barrier energies are enhanced by nearly one order of magnitude for \(^{6}\)Li + \(^{209}\)Bi.
* The ratio of complete fusion cross sections (\(\sigma_{CF}(^{6}\text{Li}) / \sigma_{CF}(^{7}\text{Li})\)) is smaller than unity above the barrier and larger than unity at sub-barrier energies.

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of complete and total fusion cross sections for weakly bound nuclei, building on the Universal Fusion Function (UFF) methodology introduced by Canto et al. (2009) and the first experimental demonstration of complete fusion suppression by Dasgupta et al. (2002). The analysis contradicts the results of Diaz-Torres and Thompson (2002), whose CDCC calculations for \(^{11}\)Be + \(^{208}\)Pb showed suppression both above and below the barrier, and instead supports the interpretation that nucleon transfer (as observed in experiments by Luong et al. (2011)) dominates over direct breakup. The findings enable a qualitative framework for understanding fusion enhancement and suppression in other weakly bound systems including \(^{9}\)Be, \(^{6}\)He, \(^{8}\)He, and \(^{11}\)Be.
