# Investigation of $K^{bar}NN$ resonances with a coupled-channel Complex Scaling Method + Feshbach projection

## Key claim

The $K^-pp$ system (with $J^\pi=0^-$, $T=1/2$) is shallowly bound with a binding energy of 20-30 MeV, but the mesonic decay width depends strongly on the ansatz used for the self-consistent $\bar{K}N$ energy, ranging from 20 to 65 MeV; for $J^\pi=1^-$ states, no three-body $\bar{K}NN$ resonant states are found.

## Method

The authors combine the coupled-channel Complex Scaling Method (ccCSM) with the Feshbach projection method to reduce the three-body coupled-channel problem ($\bar{K}NN$, $\pi\Sigma N$, $\pi\Lambda N$) to a single-channel $\bar{K}NN$ problem. The $\pi Y$ channels are eliminated at the two-body level by constructing an effective energy-dependent $\bar{K}N$ potential using the extended closure relation in the complex scaling method, and this effective potential is then used in a three-body $\bar{K}NN$ calculation with correlated Gaussian basis functions. Self-consistency for the $\bar{K}N$ energy is treated via two ansatze (anti-kaon as a field versus as a particle), and the complex eigenvalues are searched for poles independent of the scaling angle $\theta$.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of $K^-pp$ ($J^\pi=0^-$, $T=1/2$): 20-30 MeV (range over $f_\pi$ variation from 90 to 120 MeV and two ansatze)
- Mesonic decay width of $K^-pp$: 20-65 MeV (range over $f_\pi$ variation and two ansatze)
- With $\bar{K}N$ energy fixed to $\Lambda^*$ energy: pole position at $(-28.6, -21.6)$ MeV (binding energy 28.6 MeV, half-width 21.6 MeV)
- Self-consistent results for NRv2c potential with $f_\pi=110$ MeV: Ansatz 1 gives $(-25.6, -11.6)$ MeV; Ansatz 2 gives $(-27.3, -18.9)$ MeV
- For $J^\pi=1^-$, $T=1/2$: no $\bar{K}NN$ three-body resonant states found

## Context

This work extends earlier variational calculations of the $K^-pp$ system using chiral SU(3)-based potentials, such as the study by Dote, Hyodo, and Weise (2008) which used a similar potential type and found comparable binding energies. The ccCSM+Feshbach method allows direct treatment of resonance poles on the complex energy plane, unlike variational approaches that handle only real energies. The results contradict the interpretation of the J-PARC E27 collaboration's reported bump at approximately 2260 MeV in the $\Lambda p$ invariant-mass spectrum, which would imply a binding energy of about 110 MeV if interpreted as a $K^-pp$ state. The authors plan future fully coupled-channel $\bar{K}NN$-$\pi\Sigma N$-$\pi\Lambda N$ calculations without channel elimination to clarify the role of three-body dynamics.
