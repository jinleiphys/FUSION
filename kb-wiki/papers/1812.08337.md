# Event-shape engineering and heavy-flavour observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Traditionally, events collected at relativistic heavy-ion colliders are classified according to some centrality estimator (e.g. the number of produced charged particles) related to the initial energy density and volume of the system. In a naive picture the latter are directly related to the impact parameter of the two nuclei, which sets also the initial eccentricity of the system: zero in the case of the most central events and getting larger for more peripheral collisions. A more realistic modelling requires to take into account event-by-event fluctuations, in particular in the nucleon positions within the colliding nuclei: collisions belonging to the same centrality class can give rise to systems with different initial eccentricity and hence different flow harmonics for the final hadron distributions. This issue can be addressed by an event-shape-engineering analysis, consisting in selecting events with the same centrality but different magnitude of the average bulk anisotropic flow and therefore of the initial-state eccentricity. In this paper we present the implementation of this analysis in the POWLANG transport model, providing predictions for the transverse-momentum and angular distributions of charm and beauty hadrons for event-shape selected collisions. In this way it is possible to get information on how the heavy quarks propagating (and hadronizing) in a hot environment respond both to its energy density and to its geometric asymmetry, breaking the perfect correlation between eccentricity and impact parameter which characterizes a modelling of the medium based on smooth average initial conditions

## Key claim

Event-shape engineering, which selects events of the same centrality but different initial eccentricity, can break the naive correlation between impact parameter and eccentricity; the paper shows that while the nuclear modification factor of charmed hadrons shows only a mild (10-20%) sensitivity to such eccentricity cuts, the elliptic flow coefficient displays a much larger response, with high-eccentricity events yielding a v2 almost twice that of the unbiased sample, though the effect is partially washed out by hadronization.

## Method

The authors performed a Glauber Monte-Carlo simulation of Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV to generate initial entropy-density distributions, then evolved the medium using the (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic code ECHO-QGP under longitudinal boost-invariance. Heavy-quark (charm and beauty) transport through the quark-gluon plasma was simulated via a relativistic Langevin equation using two independent sets of transport coefficients (from weak-coupling Hard-Thermal-Loop and from lattice-QCD calculations), and hadronization was modelled by recombining heavy quarks with light thermal partons at freeze-out followed by Lund-string fragmentation in PYTHIA 6.4.

## Key numbers

* For the 0-10% centrality class, the average number of binary collisions (weighted by N_coll) is 1658 for unbiased events, 1471 for high-epsilon_2 selection, and 1730 for low-epsilon_2 selection.
* For the 0-10% centrality class, the average number of binary collisions is 1604 for high-epsilon_3 selection and 1675 for low-epsilon_3 selection.
* The effect of eccentricity cuts on the nuclear modification factor of charmed hadrons is at most of order 10-20% when considering the high-epsilon_2 sample.
* For all three centrality classes (0-10%, 10-30%, 30-50%), the 20% highest-epsilon_2 events display an average v2 coefficient almost twice as large as that found in the unbiased sample.
* The ratio v2^{low-epsilon_2}/v2^{unbiased} is flat around 0.7-0.8 for a broad range of pT.

## Context

This work extends the event-shape-engineering approach, previously applied to light hadrons by the ALICE collaboration (Adam 2015), to heavy-flavour observables using the POWLANG transport model. It builds on prior studies of heavy-quark transport in relativistic heavy-ion collisions (Beraudo 2017, Nahrgang 2014) and provides predictions that can be compared with recent ALICE data on heavy-flavour event-shape engineering (Acharya 2018). The results enable future extraction of the heavy-quark diffusion coefficient by disentangling the response of heavy quarks to the medium's energy density from their response to its geometric asymmetry.
