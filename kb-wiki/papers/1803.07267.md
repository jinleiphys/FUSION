# Does the chiral magnetic effect change the dynamic universality class in QCD?

> In QCD matter under an external magnetic field, the chiral magnetic effect (CME) leads to the collective gapless mode called the chiral magnetic wave (CMW). Since dynamic universality class generally depends on low-energy gapless modes, it is nontrivial whether the CME and the resulting CMW change that of the second-order chiral phase transition in QCD. To address this question, we study the critical dynamics near the chiral phase transition in massless two-flavor QCD under an external magnetic field. By performing the dynamic renormalization-group analysis within the epsilon expansion, we find that the presence of the CME changes the dynamic universality class to that of model A. We also show that the transport coefficient of the CME is not renormalized by the critical fluctuations of the order parameter.

## Key claim

The presence of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and the resulting chiral magnetic wave (CMW) changes the dynamic universality class of the second-order chiral phase transition in massless two-flavor QCD under an external magnetic field from model E to model A, provided one ignores the effects of plasma motion and the chiral vortical effect.

## Method

The authors constructed non-linear generalized Langevin equations for the order parameter fields (sigma and neutral pion), the isospin density, and the axial isospin density, incorporating the CME through an anomalous Poisson bracket. They then performed a dynamic renormalization-group analysis within the epsilon expansion (spatial dimension d = 4 - epsilon) at one-loop order using the Martin-Siggia-Rose-Janssen-de Dominicis (MSRJD) path-integral formalism.

## Key numbers

- The CME coefficient is fixed as C = 1/(2*pi^2), related to the anomaly coefficient away from the second-order chiral phase transition.
- The analysis is performed in spatial dimension d = 4 - epsilon, with epsilon treated as a small expansion parameter.
- The magnetic field is taken to be of order O(p) in the derivative expansion, with p being the characteristic momentum.
- The charged pions pi^{1,2} acquire a mass proportional to sqrt(|B|) under the external magnetic field.

## Context

This work extends the previous classification of dynamic universality classes for the chiral phase transition in QCD, where Rajagopal (1992) found that at zero magnetic field the system belongs to the O(4) antiferromagnet class. The authors show that with a magnetic field but without the CME the system falls into model E, while including the CME shifts it to model A in the Hohenberg-Halperin classification. This enables future studies of the interplay between anomaly-induced transport and critical dynamics, and the authors note that the speed of the chiral magnetic wave tends to zero as the second-order chiral phase transition is approached, similar to critical attenuation of sound waves in the liquid-gas phase transition studied by Onuki (1997) and Minami (2011).
