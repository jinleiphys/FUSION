# Nonlinear extension of the u(2) algebra as the symmetry algebra of the planar anisotropic quantum harmonic oscillator with rational ratio of frequencies and ``pancake'' nuclei

> The symmetry algebra of the two-dimensional anisotropic quantum harmonic oscillator with rational ratio of frequencies, which is characterizing ``pancake'' nuclei, is identified as a non-linear extension of the u(2) algebra. The finite dimensional representation modules of this algebra are studied and the energy eigenvalues are determined using algebraic methods of general applicability to quantum superintegrable systems. For labelling the degenerate states an ``angular momentum'' operator is introduced, the eigenvalues of which are roots of appropriate generalized Hermite polynomials. In the special case with frequency ratio 2:1 the resulting algebra is identified as the finite W algebra W$_3^{(2)}$.

## Key claim

The symmetry algebra of the two-dimensional anisotropic quantum harmonic oscillator with rational frequency ratio m/n, which describes the single-particle level spectrum of "pancake" nuclei (triaxially deformed nuclei with ω_x >> ω_y, ω_z), is identified as a non-linear extension of the u(2) algebra of order m+n-1, rather than the standard u(2) algebra that applies only in the symmetric 1:1 case.

## Method

The authors constructed creation and annihilation operators for the anisotropic oscillator with frequencies in rational ratio m/n (with m and n coprime natural numbers), then defined generators S_+, S_-, S_0, and H in the enveloping algebra. They showed these generators satisfy commutation relations of a non-linear generalization of u(2), and used the formalism of a deformed oscillator algebra with an energy-dependent structure function to find finite-dimensional representation modules. The energy eigenvalues were determined by requiring the existence of these finite-dimensional modules, and an "angular momentum" operator was introduced whose eigenvalues are roots of generalized Hermite polynomials.

## Key numbers

- Energy eigenvalues are given by E = N + (2p-1)/(2m) + (2q-1)/(2n), where p = 1,2,...,m, q = 1,2,...,n, and N = 0,1,2,...; each N yields m·n energy levels, each with degeneracy N+1.
- For the special case m:n = 1:2, the resulting non-linear algebra is identified as the finite W algebra W_3^(2), with commutation relations [A, A^†] = 3 S_0^2 - H^2/4 - H S_0 + 3/16.
- In the symmetric case m/n = 1/1, the algebra reduces to the ordinary u(2) algebra, and the "angular momentum" eigenvalues are uniformly distributed and differ by 2.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the two-dimensional anisotropic harmonic oscillator by Jauch and Hill (1940), Demkov (1959), and others, and the special cases with frequency ratios 1:2 by Holt (1978) and by Bonatsos and collaborators (1994) and 1:3 by Flessas and Leach (1983). The paper provides an algebraic framework for describing "pancake" nuclei, as introduced by Rae (1992), and the method is noted as extendable to the three-dimensional anisotropic oscillator relevant for superdeformed and hyperdeformed nuclei, as studied by Mottelson (1992).
