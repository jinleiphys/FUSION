# Natural orbitals for ab initio no-core shell model calculations

> We explore the impact of optimizations of the single-particle basis on the convergence behavior and robustness of ab initio no-core shell model calculations. Our focus is on novel basis sets defined by the natural orbitals of a correlated one-body density matrix that is obtained in second-order many-body perturbation theory. Using a perturbatively improved density matrix as starting point informs the single-particle basis about the dominant correlation effects on the global structure of the many-body state, while keeping the computational cost for the basis optimization at a minimum. Already the comparison of the radial single-particle wavefunctions reveals the superiority of the natural-orbital basis compared to a Hartree-Fock or harmonic oscillator basis, and it highlights pathologies of the Hartree-Fock basis. We compare the model-space convergence of energies, root-mean-square radii, and selected electromagnetic observables with all three basis sets for selected p-shell nuclei using chiral interactions with explicit three-nucleon terms. In all cases the natural-orbital basis provides the fastest and most robust convergence, making it the most efficient basis for no-core shell model calculations. As an application we present no-core shell model calculations for the ground-state energies of all oxygen isotopes and assess the accuracy of the normal-ordered two-body approximation of the three-nucleon interaction in the natural-orbital basis.

## Key claim

Natural orbitals constructed from a second-order many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) density matrix provide the fastest and most robust convergence for no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations compared to harmonic oscillator or Hartree-Fock bases, while eliminating the oscillator frequency as a relevant parameter. The method works because the perturbatively improved density matrix informs the single-particle basis about dominant correlation effects at minimal computational cost, though the basis does not guarantee formal separation of intrinsic and center-of-mass motion.

## Method

The authors construct a correlated one-body density matrix using second-order MBPT starting from a Hartree-Fock reference state, then diagonalize this density matrix in each (l,j,t) block to obtain spherical natural orbitals. They transform the matrix elements of nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions from the initial harmonic oscillator representation to the natural-orbital basis, then perform standard NCSM calculations using the conventional N_max truncation. For three-nucleon interactions, they apply the normal-ordered two-body approximation with respect to the Hartree-Fock reference state.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of 4He converges by N_max=10 in the natural-orbital basis, comparable to the harmonic oscillator basis at optimal frequency
- For 16O, the normal-ordered two-body approximation produces overbinding of about 1 MeV or 1% compared to explicit three-nucleon interactions at large N_max
- Center-of-mass expectation values in natural-orbital calculations are on the order of 100 keV and systematically decrease with N_max
- Natural-orbital calculations for oxygen isotopes use a standard frequency of hbar-Omega=20 MeV and model spaces up to N_max=10
- The difference between N_max=10 results and extrapolated ground-state energies for oxygen isotopes is smaller than the symbol size in the presented figure
- The single-particle basis for Hartree-Fock and natural-orbital calculations contains states up to e_max = (2n+l)_max = 12, with three-nucleon matrix elements truncated at E_3max = 14

## Context

This work extends prior investigations of natural orbitals in nuclear physics by CoCa16 and builds on quantum chemistry studies by Ha73 and SiHa74 showing that approximate natural orbitals perform well in configuration interaction applications. The authors compare their natural-orbital approach against standard harmonic oscillator basis NCSM calculations and Hartree-Fock basis calculations, demonstrating that the Hartree-Fock basis exhibits severe pathologies for unoccupied single-particle states that inhibit meaningful NCSM calculations. The method enables efficient NCSM calculations for medium-mass nuclei and can be applied to other many-body methods such as in-medium similarity renormalization group and coupled-cluster calculations.
