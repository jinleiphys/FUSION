# Superfluidity in beta-stable neutron star matter

> In this work we present results for pairing gaps in $β$--stable neutron star matter with electrons and muons using a relativistic Dirac--Brueckner--Hartree--Fock approach, starting with modern meson--exchange models for the nucleon--nucleon interaction. Results are given for superconducting $^1S_0$ protons and $^3P_2$ and $^1D_2$ neutron superfluids. A comparison is made with recent non--relativistic calculations and the implications for neutron star cooling are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors present self-consistent relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of pairing gaps in beta-stable neutron star matter, finding that relativistic effects reduce the neutron $^3P_2$ gap by more than half compared to non-relativistic calculations, with the relativistic gap becoming almost negligible for suppressing modified URCA cooling processes.

## Method

The authors first solve the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equations self-consistently using the Bonn A meson-exchange potential to obtain single-particle energies for protons and neutrons in beta-stable matter that includes electrons and muons. They then compute pairing gaps for protons in the $^1S_0$ channel and neutrons in the $^3P_2$ and $^1D_2$ channels by solving the gap equation with an effective interaction that sums two-particle excitations above a cutoff momentum of 3 fm$^{-1}$.

## Key numbers

- Proton $^1S_0$ pairing gap reaches a maximum of 1.0173 MeV at total baryonic density $\rho = 0.2007$ fm$^{-3}$ (proton fraction $\chi_p = 0.0462$)
- Neutron $^3P_2$ pairing gap reaches a maximum of 0.017 MeV at total baryonic densities between $\rho = 0.0949$ and $0.1056$ fm$^{-3}$
- The relativistic $^3P_2$ gap is more than half less than the corresponding non-relativistic one
- The $^1D_2$ neutron pairing gap vanishes in both relativistic and non-relativistic calculations
- For a neutron star with central density $\rho_c = 0.4$ fm$^{-3}$, the total mass is $1.6 M_{\odot}$ and direct URCA processes are only allowed for densities larger than $0.52$ fm$^{-3}$

## Context

This work extends the relativistic pairing calculations of Kucharek and Ring (1990), who studied the $^1S_0$ gap in symmetric nuclear matter, to beta-stable neutron star matter with electrons and muons. The results contradict the assumption of Page (1994) that baryon pairing must be present throughout most of the stellar core to explain observed temperatures, instead supporting the findings of Schaab et al. (1996) that $^3P_2$ gaps of order 0.05 MeV can explain observed surface temperatures. The calculations enable more realistic modeling of neutron star cooling through modified URCA processes, with the main suppression coming from superconducting protons in the $^1S_0$ state for stars with central densities around 0.4 fm$^{-3}$.
