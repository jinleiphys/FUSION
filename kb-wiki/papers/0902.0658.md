# Multipolar correlations and deformation effect on nuclear transition matrix elements of double-$β$ decay

> The two neutrino and neutrinoless double beta decay of $^{94,96}$Zr, $^{98,100}$Mo, $^{104}$Ru, $^{110}$Pd, $^{128,130}$Te and $^{150}$Nd isotopes for the $0^{+}\to 0^{+}$ transition is studied within the PHFB framework along with an effective two-body interaction consisting of pairing, quadrupole-quadrupole and hexadecapole-hexadecapole correlations. It is found that the effect of hexadecapolar correlations can be assimilated substantially as a renormalization of the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction. The effect of deformation on nuclear transition matrix elements is investigated by varying the strength of quadrupolar correlations in the parent and daughter nuclei independently. The variation of the nuclear transition matrix elements as a function of the difference in deformation parameters of parent and daughter nuclei reveals that in general, the former tend to be maximum for equal deformation and they decrease as the difference in deformation parameters increases, exhibiting a very similar trend for the $(β^{-}β^{-})_{2ν}$ and $(β^{-}β^{-})_{0ν}$ transition matrix elements.

## Key claim

The effect of hexadecapole-hexadecapole (HH) correlations on nuclear transition matrix elements (NTMEs) for double-beta decay can be substantially assimilated as a renormalization of the quadrupole-quadrupole (QQ) interaction, and the NTMEs for both two-neutrino and neutrinoless modes exhibit a maximum when parent and daughter nuclei have equal deformation, decreasing as the deformation difference increases.

## Method

The authors employed the Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) framework with an effective Hamiltonian containing pairing, quadrupole-quadrupole (QQ), and hexadecapole-hexadecapole (HH) two-body interactions. They calculated NTMEs for the 0+ to 0+ transition of 94,96Zr, 98,100Mo, 104Ru, 110Pd, 128,130Te, and 150Nd isotopes, first comparing results with and without HH correlations, then systematically varying the quadrupolar correlation strength in parent and daughter nuclei independently to study the deformation dependence.

## Key numbers

- For 94Zr, the NTME M2nu changes by 17% when HH correlations are added (from 0.076 to 0.063), and M0nu changes from 2.034 to 1.866.
- For 130Te, M2nu changes by 26% (from 0.042 to 0.031) and M0nu changes by 18% (from 2.216 to 1.814) with HH inclusion.
- For 150Nd, M2nu changes by 21% (from 0.033 to 0.026) and M0nu changes by 25% (from 1.616 to 1.210) with HH inclusion.
- The deformation effect ratio D2nu ranges from 1.86 (98Mo with PQQ) to 5.94 (150Nd with PQQ), with maximum change up to 15% when HH is included.
- The deformation effect ratio D0nu ranges from 1.94 (98Mo with PQQ) to 6.16 (150Nd with PQQ), with maximum change up to 16% when HH is included.
- For 150Nd with PQQ interaction at zeta_qq=0.96, the M0nu value is 0.7015.
- The suppression factor for NTMEs evaluated without and with QQ interaction ranges between 2 and 6 for both decay modes in the mass range A=90-150.

## Context

This work extends previous PHFB calculations by Chandra et al. (2005), Raina et al. (2006), and Singh et al. (2007) that studied two-neutrino double-beta decay with pairing-plus-quadrupole interactions, and Chaturvedi et al. (2008) that examined neutrinoless decay. It confirms the finding by Menendez et al. (2008) in the Interacting Shell Model that NTMEs decrease rapidly as the deformation difference between parent and daughter grows, and extends the deformed QRPA studies by Alvarez-Rodriguez et al. (2004) and Pace et al. (2004) to a broader set of nuclei. The results enable more reliable predictions of double-beta decay half-lives by demonstrating that the deformation difference between initial and final nuclei is a crucial parameter, and that hexadecapolar correlations can be effectively absorbed into the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strength.
