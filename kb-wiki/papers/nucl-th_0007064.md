# Universal trend of the information entropy of a fermion in a mean field

> We calculate the information entropy of single-particle states in position-space $S_{r}$ and momentum-space $S_{k}$ for a nucleon in a nucleus, a $Λ$ particle in a hypernucleus and an electron in an atomic cluster. It is seen that $S_{r}$ and $S_{k}$ obey the same approximate functional form as functions of the number of particles, $S_{r}$ ({\rm or} $S_{k}) = a+bN^{1/3}$ in all of the above many-body systems in position- and momentum- space separately. The net information content $S_{r}+S_{k}$ is a slowly varying function of $N$ of the same form as above. The entropy sum $S_{r}+S_{k}$ is invariant to uniform scaling of coordinates and a characteristic of the single-particle states of a specific system. The order of single-particle states according to $S_r +S_k$ is the same as their classification according to energy keeping the quantum number $n$ constant. The spin-orbit splitting is reproduced correctly. It is also seen that $S_{r}+S_{k}$ enhances with excitation of a fermion in a quantum-mechanical system. Finally, we establish a relationship of $S_r +S_k$ with the energy of the corresponding single-particle state i.e. $S_r +S_k = k \ln (μE +ν)$. This relation holds for all the systems under consideration.

# Universal trend of the information entropy of a fermion in a mean field

## Key claim
The information entropy sum S_r + S_k for single-particle states of a fermion in a mean field follows a universal functional form S = a + b N^{1/3} across nuclei, hypernuclei, and atomic clusters, and additionally obeys S = k ln(μE + ν) as a function of single-particle energy, but these relations are approximate fits, not exact laws, and the functional form for the total density distributions found in prior work was S = a + b ln N rather than the N^{1/3} dependence reported here for single-particle states.

## Method
The authors calculated position-space entropy S_r and momentum-space entropy S_k for single-particle wave functions of a nucleon in a nucleus (using harmonic oscillator and Skyrme Sk III mean fields), a Λ particle in a hypernucleus (using a relativistic Dirac equation with rectangular scalar and vector potentials), and an electron in atomic clusters (using a Woods-Saxon potential). For each system, they fitted the expressions S_r = a + b N^{1/3} and S_k = a + b N^{1/3} to the numerical entropy values as functions of particle number N, and fitted S_r + S_k = k ln(μE + ν) against single-particle energy E.

## Key numbers
- For a nucleon in a nucleus with the harmonic oscillator model, the entropy sum S_r + S_k for the 1s state is 6.4341, for 1p is 7.8388, for 1d is 8.6651, and for 2s is 8.3015 (dimensionless, in nats).
- The lower bound of the entropic uncertainty relation is S_r + S_k ≥ 3(1 + ln π) ≈ 6.434 (with ℏ = 1).
- For the harmonic oscillator model of the nucleus with A = 208 (Pb) and n = 1, the fitted parameters for S = k ln(μE + ν) are k = 2.0206, μ = 3.5373 MeV⁻¹, and ν = −12.5320.
- For a proton in a nucleus with Skyrme Sk III interaction for ²⁰⁸Pb, the fitted parameters are k = 1.5262, μ = 17.3043 MeV⁻¹, and ν = 793.109.
- For an electron in an atomic cluster with N = 198, the fitted parameters are k = 1.2386, μ = 1481.48 eV⁻¹, and ν = 8730.52.
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters for atomic clusters are V₀ = 6 eV, r₀ = 2.25 Å, and a = 0.74 Å.
- For the hypernucleus model, the potential depths are D₊ = 30.55 MeV, D₋ = 300 MeV, and the radius parameter r₀ = 1.01 fm.

## Context
This work extends the authors' prior study (Panos, Massen, and Koutroulos, 1999) which found a universal property S = a + b ln N for total density distributions of many-fermion systems, by now examining single-particle states rather than total densities. The paper builds on the entropic uncertainty relation discovered by Bialynicki-Birula and Mycielski (1975) and uses the Ghosh-Berkowitz-Parr (1984) density functional framework. The results enable connecting information entropy to single-particle energy via S = k ln(μE + ν), which the authors note is the first such relationship observed in their research, and suggest that information entropy may serve as a characteristic of single-particle states that is invariant to uniform scaling of coordinates.
