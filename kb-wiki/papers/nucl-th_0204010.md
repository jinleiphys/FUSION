# A microscopic description for the alpha decay of nuclei using a realistic effective interaction

> The process of alpha disintegration has been studied theoretically in the framework of a microscopic superasymmetric fission model (MSAFM). The nuclear interaction potential required for the alpha decay process has been calculated by folding in the density distribution functions of the alpha nucleus and the daughter nucleus with a realistic effective interaction. The nuclear microscopic alpha-nucleus potential thus obtained has been used along with the Coulomb interaction potential to calculate the action integral within the WKB approximation. This subsequently results in a parameter free calculation for the half lives of the alpha decays of nuclei. The model is successful in calculating the half lives of the alpha disintegration processes of nuclei.

# A microscopic description for the alpha decay of nuclei using a realistic effective interaction

## Key claim
The microscopic superasymmetric fission model (MSAFM), using a double-folding nuclear potential derived from the M3Y effective interaction and density distributions, can reproduce experimental alpha decay half-lives with accuracy comparable to phenomenological cluster model calculations, provided a normalization factor of 0.9 is applied to the microscopic nuclear potential.

## Method
The nuclear interaction potential for alpha decay was calculated by double-folding the density distribution of the alpha particle (Gaussian form) and the daughter nucleus (Fermi form) with the finite-range M3Y effective interaction. This microscopic potential was combined with the Coulomb potential and centrifugal barrier to compute the WKB action integral between two turning points, using zero-point vibration energies proportional to the Q-value with different proportionality constants for even-even, odd-even, even-odd, and odd-odd parent nuclei. Half-lives were then obtained from the WKB barrier penetration probability and assault frequency derived from the zero-point vibration energy.

## Key numbers
- The M3Y effective interaction used: v(s) = 7999 * exp(-4s)/(4s) - 2134 * exp(-2.5s)/(2.5s) (in MeV)
- Alpha particle density distribution: ρ₁(r) = 0.4229 * exp(-0.7024 r²)
- Daughter nucleus density parameters: r_ρ = 1.13 A_d^(1/3) fm, diffuseness a = 0.54 fm
- Zero-point vibration energy proportionality constants: 0.1045*Q for even-even, 0.0962*Q for odd-even, 0.0907*Q for even-odd, 0.0767*Q for odd-odd parent nuclei
- For ²²¹Fr (Z=87, A=221) decay: MSAFM gives log T(s) = 2.37 vs experimental log T(s) = 2.50 ± 0.20
- For ²²⁶Ra (Z=88, A=226) decay: MSAFM gives log T(s) = 10.94 vs experimental log T(s) = 10.703 ± 0.002
- For ²³⁸Pu (Z=94, A=238) decay: MSAFM gives log T(s) = 9.70 vs experimental log T(s) = 9.4423 ± 0.0004
- For ²⁴²Cm (Z=96, A=242) decay: MSAFM gives log T(s) = 7.42 vs experimental log T(s) = 7.1485 ± 0.0002

## Context
This work extends the analytical superasymmetric fission model (ASAFM) of Poenaru, Greiner et al. (1986, 1991) and the cluster model (CM) of Buck, Merchant and Perez (1993) by replacing their phenomenological nuclear potentials with a microscopically derived double-folding potential based on the M3Y effective interaction. The model provides a parameter-free framework (aside from a 0.9 normalization factor) for calculating alpha decay half-lives, and the author suggests it could be extended to exotic nuclei where good Q-value estimates are available, with possible future refinements including dissipation during tunneling and dynamic shape deformations.
