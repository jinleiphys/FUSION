# Study of single-particle resonant states with Green's function method

> The relativistic mean field theory with the Green's function method is taken to study the single-particle resonant states. Different from our previous work [Phys.Rev.C 90,054321(2014)], the resonant states are identified by searching for the poles of Green's function or the extremes of the density of states. This new approach is very effective for all kinds of resonant states, no matter it is broad or narrow. The dependence on the space size for the resonant energies, widths, and the density distributions in the coordinate space has been checked and it is found very stable. Taking $^{120}$Sn as an example, four new broad resonant states $2g_{7/2}$, $2g_{9/2}$, $2h_{11/2}$ and $1j_{13/2}$ are observed, and also the accuracy for the width of the very narrow resonant state $1h_{9/2}$ is highly improved to be $1\times 10^{-8}$ MeV. Besides, our results are very close to those by the complex momentum representation method and the complex scaling method.

## Key claim

The paper claims that identifying single-particle resonant states by searching for the poles of Green's function or the extremes of the density of states is effective for all kinds of resonant states, whether broad or narrow, and that this new approach improves accuracy compared to the previous method of comparing density of states between nucleons and free particles.

## Method

The authors applied the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory combined with the Green's function method to study single-particle resonant states in the nucleus ¹²⁰Sn. Resonant states were identified by scanning the complex energy plane in the fourth quadrant and searching for poles of the Green's function, which appear as extremes in the density of states (DOS) that reverse sign when the imaginary energy crosses the resonance width. The calculations were performed in coordinate space with different box sizes (20, 25, and 30 fm) using both PK1 and NL3 effective interactions.

## Key numbers

- The width of the very narrow resonant state 1h₉/₂ in ¹²⁰Sn was determined with an accuracy of 1×10⁻⁸ MeV.
- The resonant state 2f₅/₂ was found at energy ε = 0.8705 − i0.0325 MeV using the PK1 interaction, independent of box size.
- The broad resonant state 2g₉/₂ was found at energy ε = 5.4428 − i1.6948 MeV (width Γ/2 = 1.6948 MeV) using the PK1 interaction.
- Four new broad resonant states were observed in ¹²⁰Sn: 2g₇/₂ (6.3585 − i3.1052 MeV), 2g₉/₂ (5.4428 − i1.6948 MeV), 2h₁₁/₂ (10.5130 − i6.7681 MeV), and 1j₁₃/₂ (18.1846 − i3.1531 MeV).
- With the NL3 interaction, the GF method gave results consistent with other methods, e.g., 2f₅/₂ at 0.674 − i0.015 MeV (GF) versus 0.678 − i0.015 MeV (CMR method).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RMF-GF study (Chen et al., 2014) by replacing the earlier approach of comparing DOS between nucleons and free particles with a direct pole-searching method. The results are compared with those from the complex momentum representation method (Li et al., 2016), the complex scaling method (Guo et al., 2010), the real stabilization method (Zhang et al., 2008), and the analytical continuation of the coupling constant method (Zhang et al., 2004), showing good agreement. This enables more reliable identification of broad resonant states and improved precision for narrow resonances, which is important for understanding halo formation in exotic nuclei.
