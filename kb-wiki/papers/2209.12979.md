# The Equation of State with the EPOS3 model

> Transitions between different states of matter and their thermodynamic properties are described by the Equation of State (EoS). A universal representation of the EoS of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) for the wide range of phase diagram has yet to be determined. The expectation of the systems to undergo various types of transitions depending on the temperature (T), the chemical potential (μB), and other thermodynamic features make solving that puzzle challenging. Furthermore, it needs to be apparent which experimentally measurable observables could provide helpful information for determining EoS. The application of different EoS for hydrodynamical evolution was introduced in the EPOS3 generator, which allows one to study its changing effect on the experimental observables. The family of EoS proposed by the BEST Collaboration was implemented. The Critical Point (CP) location and the strength of criticality variations were investigated with particle yield, transverse momentum spectra, flow, and moments of the net-proton distributions.

## Key claim
The EPOS3 model, when equipped with the BEST Collaboration family of equations of state (EoS) that include a critical point, shows no significant sensitivity to the choice of EoS for most bulk observables such as particle yields, transverse momentum spectra, and elliptic flow; only the higher moments (skewness and kurtosis) of net-proton distributions exhibit some variation between different EoS parameter sets, and even those differences remain within statistical uncertainties.

## Method
The authors implemented eight distinct EoS tables from the BEST Collaboration into the EPOS3 event generator, which uses 3D+1 viscous hydrodynamics with event-by-event initial conditions and a core/corona separation scheme. They simulated Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies of 7.7 GeV and 27 GeV, varying only the EoS while keeping all other model components fixed, and compared the resulting particle yields, transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flow, and net-proton moments against STAR experimental data.

## Key numbers
- Au+Au collisions simulated at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV and 27 GeV, corresponding to below and at the onset-of-QGP energy (11.5 GeV) from STAR.
- BEST EoS covers mu_B from 0 to 450 MeV and T from 30 MeV to 800 MeV, respecting lattice QCD results up to O(mu_B^4).
- Critical point locations tested: T_C from 138 to 170 MeV, mu_BC from 300 to 440 MeV (Table 1).
- Global scaling parameter omega varied from 0.5 to 10, with omega*T_C ranging from 76 to 1432 MeV.
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV, the kurtosis times variance (kappa*sigma^2) of net-proton distributions ranges from negative values (BEST4, weak criticality) to the highest positive value (BEST8, CP at high T=170 MeV, low mu_B=300 MeV).
- Simulated pion yields are approximately twice the STAR data values at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV, 0-5% centrality.

## Context
This work extends the EPOS3 framework (Werner 2019) by implementing the BEST Collaboration EoS family (Parotto 2018), which maps the 3D Ising model critical thermodynamics onto QCD coordinates. The study tests whether varying the EoS, including the location and strength of the critical point, produces observable differences in heavy-ion collision simulations. The negative result (most observables insensitive to EoS changes) motivates further development toward the EPOS4 model, where higher statistics and improved treatment of critical fluctuations may enable discrimination between EoS scenarios. The approach contrasts with lattice QCD calculations at zero baryon chemical potential (Allton 2002, Redlich 2004, Gavai 2008) and with earlier EoS that introduce first-order phase transitions without a critical point.
