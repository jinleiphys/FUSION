# Nuclear effects in $g_{1A}(x,Q^2)$ at small $x$ in deep inelastic scattering on $^7$Li and $^3$He

> We suggest to use polarized nuclear targets of $^7$Li and $^3$He to study nuclear effects in the spin dependent structure functions $g_{1A}(x,Q^2)$. These effects are expected to be enhanced by a factor of two as compared to the unpolarized targets. We predict a significant $x$ dependence at $10^{-4} ÷10^{-3} \leq x \leq 0.2$ of $g_{1A}(x,Q^2)/g_{1N}(x,Q^2)$ due to nuclear shadowing and nuclear enhancement. The effect of nuclear shadowing at $x \approx 10^{-3}$ is of an order of 16% for $g_{1A=7}^{n.s. 3/2}(x,Q^2)/g_{1N}^{n.s.}(x,Q^2)$ and 10% for $g_{1A=3}^{n.s}(x,Q^2)/g_{1N}^{n.s.}(x,Q^2)$. By imposing the requirement that the Bjorken sum rule is satisfied we model the effect of enhancement. We find the effect of enhancement at $x \approx 0.125 (0.15)$ to be of an order of $20 (55)%$ for $g_{1A=7}^{n.s. 3/2}(x,Q^2)/g_{1N}^{n.s.}(x,Q^2)$ and $14 (40)%$ for $g_{1A=3}^{n.s}(x,Q^2)/g_{1N}^{n.s.}(x,Q^2)$, if enhancement occupies the region $0.05 \leq x \leq 0.2$ ($0.1 \leq x \leq 0.2$). We predict a 2% effect in the difference of the scattering cross sections of deep inelastic scattering of an unpolarized projectile off $^7$Li with $M_{J}$=3/2 and $M_{J}$=1/2. We also show explicitly that the many-nucleon description of deep inelastic scattering off $^7$Li becomes invalid in the enhancement region $0.05 < x \leq 0.2$.

## Key claim

The authors propose that polarized nuclear targets of \(^7\)Li and \(^3\)He can be used to study nuclear effects in spin-dependent structure functions, with the expectation that these effects are enhanced by a factor of two compared to unpolarized targets. However, this enhancement is conditional on the target being polarized with the maximal spin projection (\(M_J = 3/2\) for \(^7\)Li), as the effects are suppressed by approximately a factor of three for the \(M_J = 1/2\) configuration.

## Method

The authors extended the Gribov theory of nuclear shadowing in deep inelastic scattering to spin-dependent structure functions, treating the virtual photon as an effective hadronic state with a cross section \(\sigma_{eff} = 17\) mbarn. They applied the Gribov-Glauber formalism to calculate the total cross section for scattering of this effective state off polarized \(^7\)Li and \(^3\)He, using shell-model wave functions for \(^7\)Li and a wave function where only the neutron is polarized for \(^3\)He. The effect of nuclear enhancement at \(x \sim 0.1\) was modeled by imposing the Bjorken sum rule constraint that the integrated nuclear-to-nucleon ratio of non-singlet spin-dependent structure functions must equal the ratio of axial coupling constants \(g_A(A=7)/g_A(A=1)\).

## Key numbers

- Nuclear shadowing at \(x \approx 10^{-3}\) is 16% for \(g_{1A=7}^{n.s. 3/2}(x,Q^2)/g_{1N}^{n.s.}(x,Q^2)\) and 10% for \(g_{1A=3}^{n.s}(x,Q^2)/g_{1N}^{n.s.}(x,Q^2)\).
- Nuclear enhancement at \(x \approx 0.125\) (0.15) is 20% (55%) for \(g_{1A=7}^{n.s. 3/2}(x,Q^2)/g_{1N}^{n.s.}(x,Q^2)\) and 14% (40%) for \(g_{1A=3}^{n.s}(x,Q^2)/g_{1N}^{n.s.}(x,Q^2)\), depending on whether enhancement occupies the region \(0.05 \leq x \leq 0.2\) or \(0.1 \leq x \leq 0.2\).
- The predicted effect in the difference of scattering cross sections of an unpolarized projectile off \(^7\)Li with \(M_J=3/2\) and \(M_J=1/2\) is 2%.
- Shadowing for the unpolarized case is 9.2% at \(x \leq 0.01\), confirming the factor-of-two enhancement for the polarized case.

## Context

This work extends the Gribov theory of nuclear shadowing (Gribov, 1969) from unpolarized structure functions \(F_{2A}\) to spin-dependent structure functions \(g_{1A}\), building on earlier work by the same authors (Guzey and Strikman, 1998) that first noted the factor-of-two enhancement for polarized targets. The paper enables experimental tests of nuclear shadowing and enhancement using polarized \(^7\)Li and \(^3\)He targets, which could resolve ambiguities in extracting the neutron spin-dependent structure function \(g_{1n}\) from \(^3\)He data. The authors also show that the many-nucleon description of deep inelastic scattering off \(^7\)Li becomes invalid in the enhancement region \(0.05 < x \leq 0.2\), requiring explicit introduction of non-nucleonic degrees of freedom.
