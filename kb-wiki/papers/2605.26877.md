# Azimuthal asymmetry in exclusive quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus interactions

> In neutrino oscillation experiments, exclusive measurements of neutrino-nucleus interactions play a critical role, by providing the theoretical and experimental input needed for a reliable estimation of the neutrino energy. In this paper, we derive the general form of the azimuthal angle distribution for quasi-elastic scattering, focusing on a dependency that has been routinely overlooked. We demonstrate that the outgoing nucleon exhibits a preference for emission outside the lepton scattering plane, with an asymmetric azimuthal distribution. In the context of neutrino-nucleus scattering, we argue that this asymmetry is caused by parity violation in the weak interaction. Furthermore, we show that in cross section calculations the asymmetry is sensitive to nuclear modeling choices and to the shell structure of the initial nucleus, thus providing a novel source of information for energy reconstruction in neutrino experiments. We study the experimental feasibility of observing this effect by applying a realistic momentum detection threshold and an intranuclear cascade. We estimate that the asymmetry is observable with $\mathcal{O}$($10^4$) events at the 99% confidence level for neutrino interactions on $^{12}$C, suggesting that the effect is within reach of the current generation of neutrino detectors.

# Azimuthal asymmetry in exclusive quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus interactions

## Key claim

The outgoing nucleon in charged-current quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering exhibits a preference for emission outside the lepton scattering plane, producing an asymmetric azimuthal distribution; this asymmetry arises from parity violation in the weak interaction and is sensitive to nuclear modeling choices and the shell structure of the initial nucleus, but the effect is only present when the outgoing nucleon is described as a distorted wave rather than a plane wave.

## Method

The authors extended the general formalism for exclusive charged-current interactions from Moreno et al. and Donnelly (1985) to include higher-order terms in momenta, deriving the hadron tensor decomposition with 19 response functions. They performed non-relativistic Hartree-Fock calculations using the Skyrme-type interaction SkE2 for bound nucleon wave functions on a 12C target, and compared plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) with distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) treatments of the outgoing nucleon. The NEUT intranuclear cascade model was applied to account for final-state interactions, and a realistic momentum detection threshold of 300 MeV/c was used to simulate T2K-like experimental conditions.

## Key numbers

- The asymmetry was estimated to be observable with O(10^4) events at the 99% confidence level for neutrino interactions on 12C.
- A generated sample of 5 x 10^3 events is sufficient to observe a non-zero asymmetry in the combined-shell sample at the 68% confidence level.
- The asymmetry remains statistically separated from zero at the 95% level for samples of 10^4 events, and at the 99% level for samples of 1.5 x 10^4 events.
- For the combined-shell sample with 5000 events, the up-down asymmetry A = 2.30% with 68% confidence interval +1.38%/-1.38%, 95% interval +2.78%/-2.78%, and 99% interval +3.62%/-3.62%.
- For the combined-shell sample with 20000 events, A = 2.31% with 68% confidence interval +0.70%/-0.71%, 95% interval +1.37%/-1.40%, and 99% interval +1.77%/-1.87%.
- A fixed average binding energy of 28 MeV was adopted for the 12C target.
- The proton detection threshold was set at p_thr = 300 MeV/c, representative of the T2K SuperFGD detector.
- Neutrino energies were sampled from the T2K flux truncated at 1.3 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the general formalism for exclusive charged-current neutrino-nucleus interactions developed by Moreno et al. and Donnelly (1985) to include higher-order momentum terms, and builds on prior neutrino-nucleus scattering studies using the Hartree-Fock approach with Skyrme interactions by Ryckebusch (1989), Pandey (2015, 2016), and Van Dessel (2017, 2019). The finding that PWIA approaches do not produce directional asymmetries while DWIA does demonstrates that plane wave descriptions of the final nucleon have limited validity for exclusive studies. The results enable a new observable for neutrino energy reconstruction in long-baseline experiments like T2K, where the azimuthal asymmetry provides information on nuclear shell structure that could help constrain systematic uncertainties in oscillation measurements.
