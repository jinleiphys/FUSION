# Medium-mass hypernuclei and the nucleon-isospin dependence of the three-body hyperon-nucleon-nucleon force

> We report quantum Monte Carlo calculations of single-$Λ$ hypernuclei for $A<50$ based on phenomenological two- and three-body hyperon-nucleon forces. We present results for the $Λ$ separation energy in different hyperon orbits, showing that the accuracy of theoretical predictions exceeds that of currently available experimental data, especially for medium-mass hypernuclei. We show the results of a sensitivity study that indicates the possibility to investigate the nucleon-isospin dependence of the three-body hyperon-nucleon-nucleon force in the medium-mass region of the hypernuclear chart, where new spectroscopy studies are currently planned. The importance of such a dependence for the description of the physics of hypernuclei, and the consequences for the prediction of neutron star properties are discussed.

## Key claim
The paper claims that quantum Monte Carlo calculations of single-Λ hypernuclei for A<50 can predict Λ separation energies with statistical errors smaller than current experimental uncertainties, and that these calculations are sensitive enough to resolve the nucleon-isospin dependence of the three-body ΛNN force, but only if future high-precision measurements on medium-mass targets like A=40,48 potassium hypernuclei provide sufficiently accurate data.

## Method
The authors used the auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) method to solve the many-body Schrödinger equation for single-Λ hypernuclei with A between 3 and 49. They employed a phenomenological Hamiltonian including the Argonne v4' two-nucleon potential plus the central component of the Urbana IX three-nucleon force, together with phenomenological two- and three-body Λ-nucleon forces. The three-body ΛNN force parameters were fit to reproduce the Λ separation energy of ⁵_ΛHe, ¹⁶_ΛO, and ⁴⁰_ΛCa, and a control parameter C_T was introduced to artificially vary the strength of the nucleon-isospin triplet component of the three-body force.

## Key numbers
- Λ separation energy for ⁵_ΛHe in s-orbit: 2.75(5) MeV (AFDMC) vs 3.12(2) MeV (experimental)
- Λ separation energy for ¹⁶_ΛO in s-orbit: 13.0(2) MeV (AFDMC) vs 13.0(2) MeV (experimental)
- Λ separation energy for ⁴⁰_ΛK in s-orbit: 18.6(3) MeV (AFDMC prediction)
- Λ separation energy for ⁴⁸_ΛK in s-orbit: 20.4(4) MeV (AFDMC prediction)
- Λ separation energy for ⁴⁰_ΛCa in d-orbit: 1.0(5) MeV (experimental) vs 3.9(3) MeV (AFDMC for ⁴⁰_ΛK)
- Binding energy of ⁴⁰Ca with AV4'+UIX_c: -383.3(3) MeV (AFDMC) vs -342.1 MeV (experimental)
- Binding energy of ⁴⁸Ca with AV4'+UIX_c: -413.2(3) MeV (AFDMC) vs -416.0 MeV (experimental)

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological ΛN and ΛNN force model developed by Lonardoni and Pederiva in earlier studies (Lonardoni 2013, Lonardoni 2014) to medium-mass hypernuclei up to A=49, and addresses the nucleon-isospin dependence of the three-body force that was previously neglected. The calculations are motivated by the planned hypernuclear spectroscopy experiment E12-15-008 at Jefferson Lab targeting A=40,48 potassium hypernuclei. The results are relevant for constraining hyperon-nucleon interactions needed to predict neutron star properties, where Λnn triplets are expected to dominate in the neutron-rich core, in contrast to the Λpn triplets that dominate in symmetric hypernuclei.
