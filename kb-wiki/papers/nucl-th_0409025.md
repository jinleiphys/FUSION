# Charge symmetry breaking and the d d -> alpha pi0 reaction -- recent theoretical developments

> The status of the theoretical effort to calculate charge-symmetry-breaking pion production reactions will be presented. The main emphasis will be on the d d -> alpha pi0 reaction, especially in relation to the recent IUCF experiment. Suggestions for future theoretical and experimental work will be made.

# Charge symmetry breaking and the \(dd \to \alpha \pi^0\) reaction, recent theoretical developments

## Key claim

The leading-order (LO) charge-symmetry-breaking (CSB) diagrams for the \(dd \to \alpha \pi^0\) reaction are strongly suppressed by symmetry, while subleading (NNLO and higher) short-range amplitudes dominate the cross section; however, the simplified model agrees with experiment only when using Gaussian wave functions, while realistic wave functions without initial-state distortions overshoot the measured cross section considerably.

## Method

The authors performed a chiral perturbation theory (\(\chi\)PT) power-counting analysis of CSB amplitudes for \(dd \to \alpha \pi^0\) near threshold, identifying the leading-order \(\pi\)-rescattering diagram (driven by the strong and electromagnetic components of the neutron-proton mass difference, \(\delta M\) and \(\bar\delta M\)) and subleading diagrams including one-body \(\eta\)-\(\pi^0\) mixing, heavy-meson exchanges (\(\sigma\), \(\rho\), \(\omega\)), \(\rho\)-\(\omega\) mixing, and electromagnetic contributions. Cross sections were computed using simplified Gaussian wave functions and, in plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA), with realistic wave functions from CD-Bonn+Urbana and AV18+Urbana potentials, without initial-state distortions.

## Key numbers

- Experimental cross sections at IUCF: \(\sigma = 12.7 \pm 2.2\) pb at \(T_d = 228.5\) MeV and \(15.1 \pm 3.1\) pb at \(T_d = 231.8\) MeV, consistent with \(s\)-wave pion production near threshold (\(T_d = 225.6\) MeV).
- Simplified Gaussian wave function model total cross section: 22.955 pb at the lowest IUCF energy.
- PWIA with CD-Bonn+Urbana realistic wave functions: total cross section 79.832 pb; with AV18+Urbana: 46.508 pb.
- With \(\eta\)-\(\pi^0\) mixing turned off, the simplified model gives 1.925 pb, and the realistic PWIA models give 9.065 pb (CD-Bonn) and 3.049 pb (AV18).
- The one-body (\(\eta\)-\(\pi^0\) mixing) contribution alone: 0.688 pb (simplified), 12.561 pb (CD-Bonn PWIA), 10.341 pb (AV18 PWIA).
- The \(\rho\)-\(\omega\) mixing contribution: 1.645 pb (simplified), 4.191 pb (CD-Bonn PWIA), 2.647 pb (AV18 PWIA).
- The forward-backward asymmetry in \(np \to d\pi^0\) from TRIUMF: \(A_{\rm fb} = [17.2 \pm 8({\rm stat}) \pm 5.5({\rm sys})] \times 10^{-4}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier CSB studies of \(np \to d\pi^0\) (Niskanen, 1999; van Kolck et al.) to the \(dd \to \alpha \pi^0\) reaction, which is a pure CSB observable (no interfering CS amplitude) and thus uniquely sensitive to the strong and electromagnetic components of the neutron-proton mass difference (\(\delta M\) and \(\bar\delta M\)). Previous higher-energy calculations of \(dd \to \alpha \pi^0\) (Cheung, 1982; Cote and Pirner, 1986) used only \(\eta\)-\(\pi^0\) mixing; the present work provides the first chiral treatment near threshold. The results enable future extraction of \(\delta M\) and \(\bar\delta M\) when combined with \(np \to d\pi^0\) data, and motivate new experiments at COSY and further theoretical work including initial-state interactions, pion loops, and \(\Delta\)-isobar contributions.
