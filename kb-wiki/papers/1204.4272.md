# Conformal transformations and doubling of the particle states

> The 6D and 5D representations of the four-dimensional (4D) interacted fields and the corresponding equations of motion are obtained using equivalence of the conformal transformations of the four-momentum $q_μ$ ($q'_μ=q_μ+h_μ$, $q'_μ=Λ^ν_μq_ν$, $q'_μ=λq_μ$ and $q'_μ=-M^2q_μ/q^2$) and the corresponding rotations on the 6D cone $κ_Aκ^A=0$ $(A=μ;5,6\equiv 0,1,2,3;5,6)$ with $q_μ=M\ κ_μ/(κ_{5}+κ_{6})$ and the scale parameter $M$. The 4D reduction of the 6D fields on the cone $κ_Aκ^A=0$ require the intermediate 5D projection of the fields which are placed into two 5D hyperboloids $q_μq^μ+ q_5^2= M^2$ and $q_μq^μ- q_5^2=- M^2$ in order to cover the whole domain $(-\infty,\infty)$ of $q^2\equiv q_μq^μ$ with $(q_5^2\ge 0$. The resulting 5D and 4D fields $φ(x,x_5=0)=Φ(x)$ in the coordinate space consist of two parts $φ=φ_1+φ_2$ and $Φ=Φ_1+Φ_2$, where the Fourier conjugate of $φ_1(x,x_5)$ and $φ_2(x,x_5)$ are defined on the hyperboloids $q_μq^μ+ q_5^2= M^2$ and $q_μq^μ- q_5^2=- M^2$ respectively. The present relationship between the 6D, 5D and 4D fields require two kinds of 5D fields $φ_{\pm}=φ_1\pmφ_2$ and their 4D reductions $φ_{\pm}(x_5=0)=Φ_{\pm}=Φ_1\pmΦ_2$ with the same quantum numbers and with the different masses and the source operators. This doubling of the 4D fields $Φ_{\pm}=Φ_1\pm Φ_2$ is in agreement with the observed mass splitting of the electron and muon, $π$ and $π(1300)$-mesons, N and N(1440)-nucleons etc [1].

## Key claim

The paper claims that the 4D reduction of 6D fields on a cone, via intermediate 5D projections onto two distinct hyperboloids, necessarily produces two independent 4D fields \(\Phi_{\pm} = \Phi_1 \pm \Phi_2\) with the same quantum numbers but different masses and sources, and that this doubling can explain observed mass splittings such as those between the electron and muon, \(\pi\) and \(\pi(1300)\)-mesons, and N and N(1440)-nucleons.

## Method

The author establishes an equivalence between conformal transformations of the four-momentum \(q_\mu\) (translations, rotations, dilatations, and inversions) and rotations on a 6D cone \(\kappa_A \kappa^A = 0\). The 6D fields are projected onto two 5D hyperboloids, \(q_\mu q^\mu + q_5^2 = M^2\) and \(q_\mu q^\mu - q_5^2 = -M^2\), to cover the full range of \(q^2\). From these, two independent 5D fields \(\varphi_{\pm} = \varphi_1 \pm \varphi_2\) are constructed, and their 4D reductions at \(x_5 = 0\) yield the doublet \(\Phi_{\pm}(x) = \Phi_1(x) \pm \Phi_2(x)\), with the consistency of their equations of motion and boundary conditions examined for scalar and fermion fields.

## Key numbers

* The scale parameter \(M\) is introduced as a mass parameter that ensures the correct dimension of \(q_\mu\) in the inversion transformation \(q'_\mu = -M^2 q_\mu / q^2\).
* The two 5D hyperboloids are defined as \(q_\mu q^\mu + q_5^2 = M^2\) and \(q_\mu q^\mu - q_5^2 = -M^2\), with \(q_5^2 \ge 0\).
* The domains of \(q^2\) on the hyperboloids are: Domain I: \(0 \le q^2 \le M^2\); Domain II: \(M^2 < q^2 < \infty\); Domain III: \(-\infty < q^2 < -M^2\); Domain IV: \(-M^2 \le q^2 < 0\).
* The domain \(M^2 < q_5^2 < 2M^2\) is explicitly excluded from the construction of \(\varphi_{1,2}(x,x_5)\) and does not contribute to the 4D fields \(\Phi_{\pm}(x)\).

## Context

This work extends the Dirac geometrical model (Dirac, 1936) and earlier manifestly conformal invariant formulations (e.g., Mack and Salam, Todorov) by constructing the 4D reduction of 6D fields via two specific 5D hyperboloids rather than a single stereographic projection. It also builds on prior 5D quantum field theories (K1, K2, K3) where \(M\) was interpreted as a fundamental maximal mass. The resulting field doubling is proposed as a framework for a unified description of particles with identical quantum numbers but different masses, such as the electron and muon, and enables further study of 5D extensions of the standard \(SU(2) \times U(1)\) model for fermion generations.
