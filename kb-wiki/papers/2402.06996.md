# Transport coefficients of transient hydrodynamics for the hadron-resonance gas and thermal-mass quasiparticle models

> We calculate all transport coefficients of second order transient hydrodynamics in two effective kinetic theory models: a hadron-resonance gas and a quasiparticle model with thermal masses tuned to reproduce QCD thermodynamics. We compare the corresponding results with calculations for an ultrarelativistic single-component gas, that are widely employed in hydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions. We find that both of these effective models display a qualitatively different normalized bulk viscosity, when compared to the calculation for the single-component gas. Indeed, $ζ/[τ_Π(\varepsilon_{0} + P_{0})] \simeq 16.91(1/3-c_{s}^{2})^{2}$, for the hadron-resonance gas model, and $ζ/[τ_Π(\varepsilon_{0} + P_{0})] \simeq 5 (1/3-c_{s}^{2})$ for the quasiparticle model. Differences are also observed for many second-order transport coefficients, specially those related to the bulk viscous pressure. The transport coefficients derived are shown to be consistent with fundamental linear stability and causality conditions.

## Key claim

The paper calculates all second-order transient hydrodynamic transport coefficients for two effective kinetic models, a hadron-resonance gas (HRG) and a thermal-mass quasiparticle model (QPM), and finds that the normalized bulk viscosity in both models differs qualitatively from the widely used ultrarelativistic single-component gas result, with the HRG result depending on mass-weighted averages of the particle species rather than a universal constant.

## Method

The authors derive transient hydrodynamic equations from the relativistic Boltzmann equation using the method of moments, employing the Anderson-Witting relaxation time approximation (RTA) with a momentum- and species-independent relaxation time \(\tau_R\). For the HRG model, Landau matching conditions are used, and the transport coefficients are expressed in terms of thermodynamic integrals \(J_{n,q}^{(i)}\) that depend on the masses and degeneracies of all hadron species. For the QPM, a temperature-dependent thermal mass \(M(T)\) tuned to reproduce lattice QCD thermodynamics is introduced, and a trace-anomaly matching condition is employed to simplify the derivation, with a subsequent transformation to Landau matching for consistent comparison.

## Key numbers

* Normalized bulk viscosity for the HRG model: \(\zeta/[\tau_{\Pi}(\varepsilon_0 + P_0)] \simeq 16.91\,(1/3 - c_s^2)^2\) for the UrQMD particle content, and \(\zeta/[\tau_{\Pi}(\varepsilon_0 + P_0)] \simeq 19.36\,(1/3 - c_s^2)^2\) for the SMASH particle content.
* Normalized bulk viscosity for the QPM: \(\zeta/[\tau_{\Pi}(\varepsilon_0 + P_0)] \simeq 5\,(1/3 - c_s^2)\).
* Coefficient \(\lambda_{\Pi\pi}/\tau_{\Pi}\) for the HRG model: \(\lambda_{\Pi\pi}/\tau_{\Pi} \simeq 2\,(1/3 - c_s^2)\), differing from the MUSIC code value of \((8/5)(1/3 - c_s^2)\).
* Shear viscosity to entropy ratio for the HRG model in the high-temperature limit: \(\eta/[\tau_{\pi}(\varepsilon_0 + P_0)] \simeq 1/5 - (1/60)(\sum_j g_j z_j^2 / \sum_j g_j)\), where \(z_j = m_j/T\).
* Bulk relaxation time: \(\tau_{\Pi} = \tau_R\) for both models.
* Shear relaxation time: \(\tau_{\pi} = \tau_R\) for both models.

## Context

This work extends the single-species RTA calculations of Denicol (2014) that are currently implemented in the MUSIC hydrodynamic simulation code for heavy-ion collisions, updating them to multi-species HRG and QPM descriptions that incorporate basic features of QCD thermodynamics. The HRG model builds on the hadron-resonance gas approach of Hagedorn (1965) and Venugopalan (1992), while the QPM follows the quasiparticle framework of Romatschke (2011) and Albright (2015). The results enable more realistic hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions by providing transport coefficients that reflect the actual particle content and equation of state of QCD matter, and the derived coefficients are shown to satisfy linear stability and causality conditions.
