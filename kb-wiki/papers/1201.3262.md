# The vacuum nature of the QCD condensates

> Recently it was claimed that QCD condensates were associated with the internal dynamics of the hadrons. We challenge this "in-hadron" picture of the QCD condensates and show that it conflicts well established concepts and experimental facts for the example of the quark condensate. We explicitly demonstrate the vacuum nature of the quark condensate by a model calculation.

# The vacuum nature of the QCD condensates

## Key claim

The quark condensate is a property of the QCD vacuum, not an "in-hadron" property as claimed by Brodsky (2008, 2010); the authors argue that the in-hadron picture contradicts established chiral symmetry phenomenology and experimental facts, and they demonstrate via a model calculation that the condensate is translationally invariant in the vacuum and only distorted (not created) inside hadrons.

## Method

The authors use the Nambu–Jona–Lasinio (NJL) model in the two-flavor sector, bosonized via standard functional methods, and evaluate the path integral in the saddle-point approximation. For the vacuum sector, they solve the gap equation for the constituent quark mass and compute the quark condensate using proper-time regularization. For the baryon sector, they construct a static soliton (hedgehog) configuration on the chiral circle, minimize the total energy functional to obtain the self-consistent chiral angle, and compute the spatially dependent quark condensate from the functional derivative of the fermion energy with respect to the scalar field.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass set to m = 450 MeV for the numerical evaluation.
- Pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV and pion mass m_π = 135 MeV used to fix model parameters via equations (f_π² = m² (N_C/4π²) ∫₀¹ dx Γ(0, (m² − x(1−x)m_π²)/Λ²) and m_π² f_π² = m₀ m / G).
- The soliton is localized in the regime r ≤ 2/m (approximately r ≤ 0.88 fm for m = 450 MeV).
- The vacuum quark condensate is given by ⟨ūu⟩_V = −(N_C/4π²) m³ Γ(−1, m²/Λ²) with N_C = 3.
- The total scalar condensate in the soliton background is computed from equation (⟨ūu⟩_S = (N_C/2) ∫ (dΩ/4π) {θ(ε_val) Ψ_val† β Ψ_val − (1/2) Σ_ν sign(ε_ν) erfc(ε_ν/Λ) Ψ_ν† β Ψ_ν}).

## Context

This paper directly challenges the "in-hadron" picture of QCD condensates proposed by Brodsky (2008, 2010), which claimed that condensates like ⟨q̄q⟩ are properties of hadron internal dynamics rather than the vacuum. The authors argue that this interpretation contradicts the experimental observation of pions as light Goldstone bosons outside hadrons and the existence of a quarkyonic phase at high density (McLerran, 2007). The model calculation extends earlier NJL soliton work (Alkofer, 1994; Reinhardt, 1988) and lattice QCD measurements of the quark condensate in the presence of static sources (Buerger, 1993). The results enable a clear distinction between vacuum condensates and their local modification inside hadrons, while leaving open the puzzle of the cosmological constant contribution from condensates as a problem requiring quantum gravity.
