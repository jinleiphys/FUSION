# Shell gaps and pn pairing interaction in N = Z nuclei

> We analyze the observed shell gaps in N=Z nuclei determined from the binding energy differences. It is found that the shell gaps can be described by the combined contributions from the single-particle level spacing, the like-nucleon pairing, and the proton-neutron pairing interaction. This conclusion is consistent with that of Chasman in Phys. Rev. Lett. 99 (2007) 082501. For the double-closed shell N=Z nuclei, the single-particle level spacings calculated with Woods-Saxon potential are very close to those obtained by subtracting the nn pairing interaction from the observed shell gap. For the sub-closed or non-closed shell N=Z nuclei, the pn pairing interaction is shown to be important for the observed shell gaps.

## Key claim

The observed shell gaps in N=Z nuclei can be described by the combined contributions from the single-particle level spacing, the like-nucleon (nn) pairing, and the proton-neutron (pn) pairing interaction, with the pn pairing being important for sub-closed or non-closed shell nuclei but negligible for double-closed shell nuclei.

## Method

The authors analyzed experimental binding energy differences for N=Z nuclei using the three-point odd-even mass difference formula to extract the observed shell gap. They subtracted the nn pairing gap (obtained from neighboring odd-mass N=Z+1 nuclei) from the observed gap to define an extracted gap, which was then compared to single-particle level spacings calculated with a Woods-Saxon potential. For sub-closed and non-closed shell nuclei, the remaining contribution was compared to the pn pairing gap estimated from a double difference of binding energies, and shell model calculations with the USD interaction were performed to test the decomposition.

## Key numbers

- For double-closed shell nuclei 16O, 40Ca, and 56Ni, the extracted gaps (twice the difference between the observed gap and the nn pairing gap) agree fairly well with the Woods-Saxon single-particle spacings.
- The odd-even mass differences Δ(3)n(Z,Z) for even-even N=Z nuclei range from A=12 to A=61, with large values at low A that decrease steadily with increasing particle number.
- Quenching in the nn pairing interaction is clearly seen at the magic or semi-magic numbers N or Z = 14 and 28.
- For sub-closed shell nuclei 12C and 28Si, the difference between the extracted gap and the Woods-Saxon calculation is quite large, indicating significant many-body interactions beyond nn pairing.
- In the sd shell, the T=0 monopole matrix elements of the USD interaction have values about -3 MeV and are strongly attractive, while the T=1 monopole components are quite small.
- For 20Ne and 24Mg, the extracted gaps are larger than the pn pairing interaction, and shell model calculations show that the residual interaction (beyond J=0 pairing and monopole) contributes to the shell gaps.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Chasman (2007), who pointed out that correlation energy due to pairings can resolve the discrepancy between mean-field calculations and observed shell gaps. The authors build on their own previous studies (Kaneko 1999, 2001, 2004) of empirical pn pairing interactions in N≈Z nuclei using odd-even mass differences and double differences of binding energies. The results enable a decomposition of observed shell gaps into single-particle, nn pairing, and pn pairing contributions, and show that for nuclei like 20Ne and 24Mg, residual multipole interactions beyond pairing also contribute.
