# Evolving Nuclear Many-Body Forces with the Similarity Renormalization Group

> In recent years, the Similarity Renormalization Group has provided a powerful and versatile means to soften interactions for ab initio nuclear calculations. The substantial contribution of both induced and initial three-body forces to the nuclear interaction has required the consistent evolution of free-space Hamiltonians in the three-particle space. We present the most recent progress on this work, extending the calculational capability to the p-shell nuclei and showing that the hierarchy of induced many-body forces is consistent with previous estimates. Calculations over a range of the flow parameter for 6Li, including fully evolved NN+3N interactions, show moderate contributions due to induced four-body forces and display the same improved convergence properties as in lighter nuclei. A systematic analysis provides further evidence that the hierarchy of many-body forces is preserved.

## Key claim

The Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) evolution of nuclear Hamiltonians in the three-particle space preserves the hierarchy of induced many-body forces, with induced four-body contributions remaining moderate for p-shell nuclei such as 6Li, provided that both initial and induced three-body forces are consistently included.

## Method

The authors evolve free-space nuclear Hamiltonians using the SRG flow equation in a discrete basis of Jacobi-coordinate harmonic oscillator wave functions, first in the A=2 subsystem to fix two-body matrix elements, then in the A=3 subsystem to determine combined two-plus-three-body matrix elements. The three-body matrix elements are isolated by subtracting the evolved two-body contributions in the A=3 basis, and the resulting NN and NNN matrix elements are applied unchanged to any nucleus. Calculations are performed within the No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) framework, using both Jacobi coordinate and Slater determinant oscillator bases, with ground-state energies extrapolated to infinite model space using an exponential fit.

## Key numbers

- For 4He with chiral N3LO NN and N2LO NNN interactions evolved to lambda = 2.0 fm^-1, the difference between SRG-evolved and converged bare/Lee-Suzuki results is no larger than 50 keV due to omitted induced four-body contributions; at lambda = 1.0 fm^-1 the difference can be as large as 100 keV
- For 4He using the Argonne V18 potential evolved to lambda = 2.0 fm^-1 with hw = 44 MeV, NN-only results are converged with respect to variation in the two-body basis truncation (A2max) to within 1 keV at A2max = 196
- For 4He using CD-Bonn potential, A3max = 40 and Nmax = 20 was sufficient to converge results to within 30 keV
- For 6Li with chiral interactions, the three-body input file at Nmax = 8 is 13 Gb of matrix elements; at Nmax = 10 the three-body file is 33 Gb
- The best existing binding energy for 6Li with N3LO NN interaction using Lee-Suzuki renormalization up to Nmax = 14 is 28.5 +/- 0.5 MeV; with NNN included the converged value is 32.5 +/- 0.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the SRG evolution of three-body forces in free space, first presented in Jurgenson (2009), from A=3 and A=4 systems to the p-shell nucleus 6Li. The approach contrasts with Lee-Suzuki transformations used in earlier NCSM calculations by Navratil (2003), which produce model-space dependent results, whereas SRG yields nucleus-independent matrix elements that enable smooth extrapolation. The results provide evidence that the SRG preserves the initial EFT many-body hierarchy from chiral effective field theory as described by Epelbaum (2008), and enable future calculations of larger p-shell nuclei such as 8Be, 10B, and 12C using distributed-memory codes like MFDn.
