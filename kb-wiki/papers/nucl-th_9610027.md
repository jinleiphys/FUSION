# Influence of the in-medium pion dispersion relation in heavy ion collisions

> We investigate the influence of medium corrections to the pion dispersion relation on the pion dynamics in intermediate energy heavy ion collisions. To do so a pion potential is extracted from the in-medium dispersion relation and used in QMD calculations and thus we take care of both, real and imaginary part of the pion optical potential. The potentials are determined from different sources, i.e. from the $Δ$--hole model and from phenomenological approaches. Depending on the strength of the potential a reduction of the anti-correlation of pion and nucleon flow in non-central collisions is observed as well as an enhancement of the high energetic yield in transverse pion spectra. A comparison to experiments, in particular to $p_t$-spectra for the reaction Ca+Ca at 1 GeV/nucleon and the pion in-plane flow in Ne+Pb collisions at 800 MeV/nucleon, generally favours a weak potential.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether medium corrections to the pion dispersion relation, specifically the real part of the pion optical potential, significantly affect pion dynamics in heavy ion collisions, and finds that a comparison to experimental data generally favours a weak potential, but none of the tested potentials reproduces the full transverse momentum spectrum with high accuracy.

## Method

The authors extract a pion potential from the in-medium dispersion relation and incorporate it into Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) calculations, treating both the real and imaginary parts of the pion optical potential consistently. The potentials are derived from two sources: a microscopic Δ-hole model (yielding a weak potential, Pot.1) and a phenomenological ansatz by Gale and Kapusta (yielding a strong potential, Pot.2), with a third intermediate potential (Pot.3) constructed by modifying the phenomenological parametrization. The calculations include Δ(1232) and N*(1440) resonances, and the results are compared to experimental π0 transverse momentum spectra from the TAPS collaboration for Ca+Ca at 1 GeV/nucleon and to pion in-plane flow data from the DIOGENE collaboration for Ne+Pb at 800 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- For Ca+Ca at 1 GeV/nucleon: without a pion potential, the high pt range (pt ≥ 0.7 GeV/c) is underestimated by about a factor of 3 compared to TAPS π0 data.
- For Ca+Ca at 1 GeV/nucleon: with the strong phenomenological potential (Pot.2), high energy pions are overestimated by nearly one order of magnitude.
- For Ca+Ca at 1 GeV/nucleon: with the intermediate potential (Pot.3), the low pt range is overestimated by about a factor of 3.
- For Ne+Pb at 800 MeV/nucleon at impact parameter b=3 fm: the ratio R of pions with positive to negative px is about 1.34 in experiment; calculations without potential and with Pot.1 give reasonable agreement, while Pot.2 and Pot.3 yield R close to unity.
- The Δ resonance bare decay width is taken as Γ0Δ = 120 MeV; the N*(1440) bare decay width is Γ0N* = 200 MeV.
- The saturation density used is ρ0 = 0.16 fm−3.

## Context

This work extends prior QMD and BUU transport calculations (e.g., Li (1991), Bass (1995)) that included pion interactions only through collision processes (the imaginary part of the optical potential) by adding the real part of the pion optical potential. The study tests the Δ-hole model (Weise (1988), Fröhlich (1981)) against the phenomenological dispersion relation of Gale and Kapusta (1987), and compares to experimental data from the TAPS collaboration (Schwalb (1994)) and the DIOGENE collaboration (1980s). The results indicate that the in-medium pion dispersion relation significantly affects both transverse momentum spectra and collective flow, and that further analysis of high-energy subthreshold spectra and pion flow in symmetric systems (e.g., from the FOPI collaboration) could help clarify the role of medium corrections.
