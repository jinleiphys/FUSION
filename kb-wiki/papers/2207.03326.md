# Possible condensation of Cooper triples

> We theoretically discuss the possible condensation of Cooper triples, which correspond to a three-body version of Cooper pairs, in three-component Fermi systems with three-body attractive interactions. A macroscopic number of Cooper triples can occupy a zero center-of-mass momentum state in the presence of a Fermi surface of constituent particles, even though the three-body operator exhibits anti-commutation relation associated with the Fermi-Dirac statistics. Such a condensation with internal degrees of freedom is similar to bosonization in a system of infinite-component fermions. We propose a variational wave function for condensed Cooper triples and show that in the ground state, the condensed state is energetically favored compared to the normal state. Also, we discuss effects of the Fermi-surface distortion in a lattice system described by a three-component Hubbard model.

## Key claim

Cooper triples, three-body analogues of Cooper pairs, can macroscopically occupy a zero center-of-mass momentum state and form a condensate in a three-component Fermi system with three-body attractive interactions, provided the Fermi surface of constituent particles exists; this condensation is possible despite the three-body operator obeying Fermi–Dirac statistics, because internal degrees of freedom (relative momenta) allow many such triples to coexist without violating the Pauli exclusion principle.

## Method

The authors propose a variational wave function for condensed Cooper triples, inspired by BCS theory, in a three-component Fermi gas with a contact-type attractive three-body interaction acting only near the Fermi surface. They minimize the ground-state energy with respect to variational parameters to obtain a self-consistency equation for the order parameter \(\Delta_{\bm k}\), and derive the condensation energy in the weak-coupling limit. They also evaluate the probability \(P\) of realizing the three-point-type configuration (TPTC) in a two-dimensional square-lattice Hubbard model, using Monte Carlo sampling to assess the effect of Fermi-surface anisotropy.

## Key numbers

- The order parameter amplitude in the weak-coupling limit: \(\Delta \approx 2\Lambda \exp\left(-\frac{3}{2\rho(0)U_0}\right)\), where \(\Lambda\) is the energy cutoff, \(\rho(0)\) is the density of states at the Fermi level, and \(U_0\) is the positive coupling constant.
- Condensation energy difference: \(E_0 - E_0^{\rm FS} \simeq -3\rho(0)\Lambda^2 \exp\left(-\frac{3}{\rho(0)U_0}\right) < 0\), showing the Cooper-triple condensed state is energetically favored over the normal Fermi gas.
- For the lattice model, the probability \(P\) of TPTC is evaluated as a function of \(E_{\rm F}/t\) with \(\Lambda/t = 0.04\); \(P\) decreases exponentially as \(E_{\rm F}/t\) increases, indicating TPTC are frequent only in the low-filling regime.

## Context

This work extends the concept of Cooper-pair condensation (BCS theory, 1957) to three-body clusters, building on prior studies of Cooper triples by Hammer (2012) and Tajima (2021) and on the authors’ own earlier work (Akagami, 2021). It also connects to bosonization in SU(\(N\)) fermions at large \(N\) (Song, 2020) and to discussions of Cooper quartets in nuclear and condensed matter systems (Kamei, Baran, Guo, 2022). The results open the possibility of realizing exotic fermionic condensates in ultracold atomic gases with three-body interactions, and raise questions about the applicability of the Mermin–Wagner–Hohenberg theorem to such systems.
