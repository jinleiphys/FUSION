# Instability windows of relativistic r-modes in stably stratified neutron stars with hyperonic cores

> (abridged) $R$-modes are oscillations in rotating stars, primarily restored by the Coriolis force. These oscillations are the most susceptible to the Chandrasekhar-Friedman-Schutz (CFS) instability driven by gravitational wave emission, which makes them promising targets for current and future gravitational wave searches. In order to develop, the instability must overcome dissipative processes within the star. As a result, $r$-modes become unstable only for certain combinations of stellar angular velocity $Ω$ and (redshifted) temperature $T^\infty$, defining the so-called instability window on the $(Ω, T^\infty)$ plane. At high temperatures, bulk viscosity $ζ$, arising from out-of-equilibrium chemical reactions, is the dominant dissipative agent. Dissipation due to $ζ$ can be greatly enhanced by two independent mechanisms: (1) the presence of hyperons, which significantly increases the bulk viscosity, and (2) the distinctive properties of relativistic $r$-modes in nonbarotropic matter, which further amplify dissipation beyond Newtonian predictions. In this work, we present the first investigation of the combined impact of these mechanisms on $r$-mode instability windows. Our calculations also account for the fact that chemical reactions modify the adiabatic index, in addition to producing bulk viscosity. We further estimate the influence of nucleon pairing effects on the instability windows. By comparing our predictions with recent observations of neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries, we find that bulk viscosity in hyperonic matter may provide the necessary dissipation to stabilize $r$-modes in the fastest-spinning and moderately hot stars, even when nucleon superfluidity and superconductivity are taken into account. These results have important implications for the interpretation of observations and for the broader understanding of relativistic $r$-mode physics.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first investigation of the combined impact of two mechanisms that enhance bulk viscosity dissipation in neutron stars, hyperonic composition and relativistic r-mode peculiarities in nonbarotropic matter, on r-mode instability windows, finding that hyperonic bulk viscosity may stabilize r-modes in the fastest-spinning and moderately hot stars even when nucleon superfluidity and superconductivity are taken into account, though this conclusion depends on the specific equation of state and stellar mass.

## Method

The authors model neutron stars with a barotropic crust (BSk24 EOS) and a nonbarotropic core described by either FSU2H or TM1C EOS, allowing for \(\Lambda\) and \(\Xi^-\) hyperons. They apply the previously developed theory of nonanalytic relativistic r-modes to compute the energy dissipation from bulk viscosity arising from weak nonleptonic processes involving hyperons, while also accounting for the modification of the adiabatic index by chemical reactions and estimating the influence of nucleon pairing effects via reduction factors. The instability windows are determined by comparing the CFS driving power against the total dissipative losses, and the results are compared with observational data of neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries.

## Key numbers

- Bulk viscosity in hyperonic matter is primarily determined by weak nonleptonic processes whose rates at low temperatures are suppressed only as \((T/T_{\rm F})^2\), compared to \((T/T_{\rm F})^6\) for modified Urca processes and \((T/T_{\rm F})^4\) for direct Urca processes in nucleonic matter.
- Relativistic r-modes in nonbarotropic matter amplify damping due to bulk viscosity by at least two orders of magnitude compared to Newtonian predictions, as shown in previous work (Kraav et al., 2024).
- Neutron star masses considered range from \(1.4 M_\odot\) to \(1.9 M_\odot\), with central densities and pressures shown for the FSU2H EOS.
- The crust-core interface is determined by the density \(\rho_{\rm cc}\) where the equilibrium pressure of the core EOS matches that of the crust EOS.
- Interior temperatures of neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries typically fall below \(10^9\, \rm K\), which is significantly lower than Fermi temperatures \(T_{\rm F}\sim 10^{12}-10^{13} \, \rm K\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on nonanalytic relativistic r-modes (Kraav et al., 2022a, 2022b) and their instability windows in nucleonic matter (Kraav et al., 2024) by incorporating hyperonic composition, building on the bulk viscosity calculations for hyperonic matter by Ofengeim et al. (2019). It contradicts earlier calculations (Lo and Lin, 2002; Nayyar and Bhattacharya, 2006; Reisenegger and Bonacic, 2003) that found hyperonic bulk viscosity insufficient to stabilize r-modes in observed neutron stars, by using updated hyperon species (\(\Lambda\) and \(\Xi^-\) rather than \(\Sigma^-\) and \(\Lambda\)) and accounting for meson-exchange channels in weak nonleptonic reaction rates. The results enable future gravitational-wave searches to better interpret observations of neutron stars in low-mass X-ray binaries and may help discriminate between different equations of state for ultradense matter.
