# Coherent dibaryons

> There are encouraging evidences for registration of excitations of quark oscillator levels in compressed 2-nucleon systems, which were hidden in two independently published experimental works for many years. Data obtained by EVA collaboration can be considered as the third possible confirmation of this conjecture. Moreover, they drop hints about existence of coherent dibaryons which may be interpreted as usual or generalized coherent states of the oscillator. These data may also be an explicit example of a physical process described by Landau and Peierls in their reasoning concerning the energy-time uncertainty relation. Two models of the coherent dibaryon creation are formulated. Phase transitions of baryon matter in few-nucleon systems are discussed.

# Coherent dibaryons

## Key claim
The paper argues that three independent experimental datasets (Troyan experiment, Stavinskii group experiment, and EVA collaboration) together provide evidence for excited quark oscillator levels in compressed two-nucleon systems, which may be interpreted as coherent dibaryons; however, none of the individual experiments alone contains sufficient grounds for a definite conclusion, and the claim rests on striking interconnections between them rather than any single definitive measurement.

## Method
The author reanalyzes published data from the EVA collaboration on short-range correlated (SRC) proton-neutron pairs in carbon, extracting transverse and longitudinal momentum distributions of the pair's center-of-mass and relative momenta. A discrepancy between longitudinal and transverse momentum spreads is interpreted via the Landau-Peierls uncertainty relation for finite-time measurements, yielding an interaction time estimate. The author then formulates two quantum-optical models, Glauber coherent states and generalized (SU(1,1)) coherent states of a two-dimensional oscillator, to describe the hypothesized dibaryon excitations, and discusses their connection to phase transitions in few-nucleon systems via Bogoliubov transformations.

## Key numbers
- Longitudinal center-of-mass momentum: ⟨p_z^cm⟩ ≈ 0 GeV/c, σ_z^cm ≈ 0.1 GeV/c
- Longitudinal relative momentum: ⟨p_z^rel⟩ ≈ 0.3 GeV/c, σ_z^rel ≈ 0.1 GeV/c
- Transverse center-of-mass momentum: ⟨p_x^cm⟩ ≈ 0 GeV/c, σ_x^cm ≈ 0.6 GeV/c
- Transverse relative momentum: ⟨p_x^rel⟩ ≈ 0.6 GeV/c, σ_x^rel ≈ 0.2 GeV/c
- Landau-Peierls measurement time: Δt ∼ 10^{-23} s, corresponding energy uncertainty ΔE ∼ 66 MeV
- Dibaryon mass formula from Troyan experiment: M_{2B} ≈ M_{pp} + 10n MeV, where n is a positive integer
- Ground state dibaryon mass: 1.886 GeV/c^2 (from Troyan experiment)
- Number of oscillator levels observed in incident deuteron: 31 consecutive levels; in target deuteron: 2 levels (from Baldin/Stavinskii group experiment)
- JLab Hall A beam energy spread: (ΔE/E)_{Hall A} = 10^{-4}, corresponding to projectile localization > 214 fm
- EVA beam momentum spread: (Δp/p)_{EVA} = 5 × 10^{-2}

## Context
This work extends earlier proposals by Kostenko (2015, 2017) to use cumulative particles as triggers for multibaryon detection, and reanalyzes data from Troyan (1990s), Baldin/Stavinskii group (1979), and the EVA collaboration (1990s). The author connects these to the Landau-Peierls uncertainty principle (Landau and Peierls, 1931) and to the quark counting rules of Matveev (1973) and Brodsky (1973). The paper contrasts its findings with the Frankfurt-Strikman SRC model (Frankfurt and Strikman, 1980s) and its recent revision by the CLAS collaboration (2018), suggesting that the newly reported "flucton phase" in neutron-proton SRC pairs may correspond to the ground state of the coherent dibaryon excitations proposed here. The work enables future experimental searches for phase transitions in few-nucleon systems and motivates verification of the three key experimental datasets with improved precision.
