# Reaction Operator Approach to Non-Abelian Energy Loss

> A systematic expansion of the induced inclusive gluon radiation associated with jet production in a dense QCD plasma is derived using a reaction operator formalism. Analytic expressions for the transverse momentum and light-cone momentum distributions are derived to all orders in powers of the gluon opacity of the medium, $Nσ_g/A=L/λ_g$. The reaction operator approach also leads to a simple algebraic proof of the ``color triviality'' of single inclusive distributions and to a solvable set of recursion relations. The analytic solution generalizes previous continuum solutions (BDMPS) for applications to mesoscopic QCD plasmas. The solution is furthermore not restricted to uncorrelated geometries and allows for evolving screening scales as well as the inclusion of finite kinematic constraints. The later is particularly important because below LHC energies the kinematic constraints significantly decrease the non-abelian energy loss. Our solution for the inclusive distribution also generalizes the finite order exclusive (tagged) distribution case studied previously (GLV1). The form of the analytic solution is well suited for numerical implementation in Monte Carlo event generators to enable more accurate calculations of jet quenching in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions. Numerical results illustrating the constributions of the first three orders in opacity are compared to the ``self-quenching'' hard radiation intensity. A surprising result is that the induced gluon radiation intensity is dominated by the (quadratic in $L$) first order opacity contribution for realistic geometries and jet energies in nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper derives a systematic opacity expansion for inclusive gluon radiation from jets in a dense QCD plasma using a reaction operator formalism, and finds that the induced gluon radiation intensity is dominated by the first order opacity contribution (quadratic in path length \(L\)) for realistic geometries and jet energies in nuclear collisions, contrary to expectations that higher orders would dominate.

## Method

The authors construct a reaction operator \(\hat{R}_n\) from diagrammatic rules for direct and virtual (double Born) amplitudes, enabling recursion relations for the inclusive gluon distribution to all orders in the gluon opacity \(\bar{n} = L/\lambda_g = N\sigma_g/A\). They model interactions via static color-screened Yukawa potentials with screening mass \(\mu\), assume soft gluon and eikonal kinematics, and perform ensemble averages over scattering center positions to derive analytic expressions for transverse momentum and light-cone momentum distributions. The solution generalizes previous continuum solutions (BDMPS) and the finite-order exclusive tagged case (GLV1), and is not restricted to uncorrelated geometries.

## Key numbers

*   Opacity \(\bar{n} = L/\lambda_g = N\sigma_g/A\) is the expansion parameter; for RHIC energies (\(\sqrt{s} \simeq 200\) AGeV) with \(dN/dy \simeq 1000\) for \(A=200\), \(\sigma_{el} \sim 2\) mb, and plasma formation time \(\sim 0.5\) fm/c, the opacity is moderately small \(\bar{n} < 10\).
*   The screening mass is taken as \(\mu \simeq \omega_{pl} \simeq 0.5\) GeV for numerical estimates.
*   For SPS energies (\(\sqrt{s} \simeq 20\) AGeV), no quenching of moderate \(p_\perp < 4\) GeV hadrons was observed in \(Pb+Pb\) collisions.
*   The first order opacity contribution to the induced gluon radiation intensity is quadratic in \(L\) and dominates over higher orders for realistic geometries and jet energies.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous exclusive (tagged) calculation (GLV1) to the inclusive case where fluctuations in the number of collisions are allowed, and generalizes the continuum solutions of Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigné, and Schiff (BDMPS) and the path integral formulations of Zakharov and Wiedemann for application to mesoscopic QCD plasmas. The analytic solution is designed for numerical implementation in Monte Carlo event generators to enable more accurate calculations of jet quenching in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.
