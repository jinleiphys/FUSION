# Alternative Large Nc Schemes and Chiral Dynamics

> We compare the dependences on the number of colors of the leading pion pion scattering amplitudes using the single index quark field and two index quark fields. These are seen to have different relationships to the scattering amplitudes suggested by chiral dynamics which can explain the long puzzling pion pion s wave scattering up to about 1 GeV. This may be interesting for getting a better understanding of the large Nc approach as well as for application to recently proposed technicolor models.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the large-\(N_c\) extrapolation of QCD is not unique, and that using a two-index antisymmetric quark representation (the Corrigan-Ramond scheme) yields a \(\pi\pi\) scattering amplitude that becomes unitary at \(N_c=4\) rather than \(N_c=6\) as in the standard 't Hooft scheme, and that this alternative scheme naturally accommodates a four-quark sigma meson at leading order, which is needed to explain the observed s-wave scattering up to about 1 GeV.

## Method

The authors compare the \(N_c\) scaling of the leading \(\pi\pi\) scattering amplitude in two large-\(N_c\) extrapolation schemes: the standard 't Hooft scheme (single-index fundamental quarks) and the Corrigan-Ramond scheme (two-index antisymmetric quarks). They compute the real part of the \(I=J=0\) partial wave amplitude from current algebra plus \(\rho(770)\) exchange, using the known \(N_c\)-dependent scaling of \(F_\pi\) and \(g_{\rho\pi\pi}\), and determine the minimum \(N_c\) for which the amplitude satisfies the unitarity bound \(|R_0^0| \le 1/2\) up to 1 GeV. They also analyze the large-\(N_c\) counting of diagrams with internal quark loops to assess whether four-quark states (like the sigma) appear at leading order.

## Key numbers

- In the 't Hooft scheme, the unitarity bound \(|R_0^0| \le 1/2\) is satisfied for \(N_c \ge 6\) up to beyond 1 GeV, but violated for \(N_c = 3, 4, 5\).
- In the two-index antisymmetric scheme, the condition \(0.9 \times 6/[N_c(N_c-1)] < 1/2\) shows unitarity is already achieved for \(N_c = 4\).
- The pion decay constant scales as \(F_\pi(N_c) = 131\sqrt{N_c}/\sqrt{3}\) MeV in the 't Hooft scheme (so \(F_\pi(3)=131\) MeV), and as \(F_\pi^2(N_c) = [N_c(N_c-1)/6] F_\pi^2(3)\) in the two-index antisymmetric scheme.
- The \(\rho\pi\pi\) coupling scales as \(g_{\rho\pi\pi}(N_c) = 8.56\sqrt{3}/\sqrt{N_c}\); the \(\rho\) mass is \(m_\rho = 771\) MeV and the pion mass \(m_\pi = 137\) MeV, both taken as \(N_c\)-independent.
- The peak value of the \(I=J=0\) partial wave amplitude from current algebra plus \(\rho\) at \(N_c=3\) is numerically about 0.9.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Harada et al. (2003), who found that the 't Hooft large-\(N_c\) tree-level amplitude (pion contact plus \(\rho\) exchange) violates unitarity for \(N_c=3\) but becomes unitary for \(N_c\ge 6\), and who inferred a sigma mass around 550 MeV from the unitarity violation point. The paper contrasts this with the Corrigan-Ramond scheme (Corrigan and Ramond, 1979) and its further study by Kiritsis and Papavassiliou (1989), showing that the alternative scheme yields better convergence in \(N_c\) and allows four-quark states (like the sigma) to appear at leading order. The results are relevant for constructing minimal technicolor models (e.g., Sannino and collaborators, 2004-2007) and for understanding the non-uniqueness of the large-\(N_c\) extrapolation of QCD.
