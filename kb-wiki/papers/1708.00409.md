> This research article is a follow up of earlier work by M. Ikram et al., reported in International Journal of Modern Physics E {\bf{25}}, 1650103 (2016) wherein we searched for $őõ$ magic numbers in experimentally confirmed doubly magic nucleonic cores in light to heavy mass region (ie.$^{16}O - ^{208}Pb$) by injecting $őõ$'s into them. In present manuscript, working within the state-of-art relativistic mean field theory with inclusion of $őõN$ and $őõőõ$ interaction in hypernuclei using the predicted doubly magic nucleonic cores ie. $^{292}$120, $^{304}$120, $^{360}$132, $^{370}$132, $^{336}$138, $^{396}$138 of elusive superheavy mass regime. In analogy to well established signatures of magicity in conventional nuclear theory, the prediction of hypernuclear magicity are made on the basis of one-, two-$őõ$ separation energy ($S_őõ, S_{2őõ}$) and two lambda shell gaps ($őī_{2őõ}$) in multi-$őõ$ hypernuclei. The appearance of new lambda shell closures other than the nucleonic ones predicted by various relativistic and non-relativistic theoretical investigations can be attributed to the relatively weak strength of spin-orbit coupling in hypernuclei compared to normal nuclei.

This work extends the authors' previous search for őõ magic numbers in experimentally confirmed doubly magic nucleonic cores from light to heavy mass region (¬Ļ‚Ā∂O to ¬≤‚Āį‚ĀłPb) reported in Ikram et al.
