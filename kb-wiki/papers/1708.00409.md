# A Study of Multi$Λ$ hypernuclei within Spherical Relativistic Mean-field Approach

> This research article is a follow up of earlier work by M. Ikram et al., reported in International Journal of Modern Physics E {\bf{25}}, 1650103 (2016) wherein we searched for $Λ$ magic numbers in experimentally confirmed doubly magic nucleonic cores in light to heavy mass region (ie.$^{16}O - ^{208}Pb$) by injecting $Λ$'s into them. In present manuscript, working within the state-of-art relativistic mean field theory with inclusion of $ΛN$ and $ΛΛ$ interaction in hypernuclei using the predicted doubly magic nucleonic cores ie. $^{292}$120, $^{304}$120, $^{360}$132, $^{370}$132, $^{336}$138, $^{396}$138 of elusive superheavy mass regime. In analogy to well established signatures of magicity in conventional nuclear theory, the prediction of hypernuclear magicity are made on the basis of one-, two-$Λ$ separation energy ($S_Λ, S_{2Λ}$) and two lambda shell gaps ($δ_{2Λ}$) in multi-$Λ$ hypernuclei. The calculations suggest that the $Λ$ numbers 92, 106, 126, 138, 184, 198, 240, and 258 might be the $Λ$ shell closures after introducing the $Λ$'s in elusive superheavy nucleonic cores. Moreover, in support of $Λ$ shell closure the investigation of $Λ$ pairing energy and effective $Λ$ pairing gap has also been made. The appearance of new lambda shell closures other than the nucleonic ones predicted by various relativistic and non-relativistic theoretical investigations can be attributed to the relatively weak strength of spin-orbit coupling in hypernuclei compared to normal nuclei.

# A Study of Multi-Λ Hypernuclei within Spherical Relativistic Mean-field Approach

## Key claim

The calculations suggest that Λ numbers 92, 106, 126, 138, 184, 198, 240, and 258 might be Λ shell closures in multi-Λ hypernuclei built on predicted doubly magic superheavy nucleonic cores, though the authors note that Λ = 120 appears only in some systems and is quenched in others, making it a "very feeble magic number" rather than a strong one.

## Method

The authors employed the spherical relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with the NL3* nucleon-meson parameterization, incorporating ΛN and ΛΛ interactions through additional strange scalar (σ*) and vector (φ) mesons. They injected multiple Λ hyperons into predicted doubly magic superheavy nucleonic cores (²⁹²120, ³⁰⁴120, ³⁶⁰132, ³⁷⁰132, ³³⁶138, ³⁹⁶138) and analyzed one- and two-Λ separation energies (S_Λ, S_{2Λ}), two-Λ shell gaps (δ_{2Λ}), Λ pairing energy, effective Λ pairing gap, and Λ single-particle energy spectra to identify Λ shell closures.

## Key numbers

- Λ numbers 92, 106, 126, 138, 184, 198, 240, and 258 are identified as possible Λ shell closures from separation energies and shell gaps
- Λ = 156 appears as a magic candidate only in the Λ effective pairing gap and pairing energy, not in other observables
- The Λ spin-orbit interaction is confirmed to be weaker than the nucleonic case, with coupling ratios set as R_ω = 2/3, R_σ = 0.6104, R_φ = -√2/3, and R_σ* = 0.69
- The pairing gap is calculated using Δ = 11.2 MeV/√A in the constant gap BCS approximation
- Center-of-mass correction is included as E_cm = -(3/4)41A^{-1/3}

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous search for Λ magic numbers in experimentally confirmed doubly magic nucleonic cores from light to heavy mass region (¹⁶O to ²⁰⁸Pb) reported in Ikram et al. (2016) into the superheavy mass regime. The study is motivated by prior predictions of nucleonic magic numbers in superheavy nuclei from Zhang et al. (2005) using relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov theory and Ismail et al. (2016) on ultra-heavy closed shells. The results enable future searches for triply magic multi-Λ systems with doubly magic superheavy nucleonic cores and provide theoretical guidance for experimental efforts in hypernuclear physics, where YN and YY scattering data remain extremely scarce (approximately 40 differential cross-section data points and no YY scattering data).
