# The freeze-out properties of hyperons in a microscopic transport model

> The excitation function of freeze-out time, average freeze-out temperature and freeze-out energy density of (multi-) strange baryons created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is investigated in the framework of a microscopic transport model. We find that the Omega on average freezes out earlier than the nucleon, Xi and Lambda. The average freeze-out temperature and energy density as well as the spread between the different baryonicstates increase monotonously with increasing beam energy and should approach a universal value in the case of a hadronizing Quark-Gluon-Plasma.

## Key claim

Using the UrQMD microscopic transport model, the authors find that the Omega baryon freezes out earlier on average than the nucleon, Lambda, and Xi, and that the average freeze-out temperature and energy density of all baryon species increase monotonically with beam energy, approaching values consistent with the critical temperature and energy density of the QCD deconfinement transition at 160 GeV/nucleon incident beam energy.

## Method

The authors performed simulations of central Au+Au collisions at incident beam energies of 10.6, 20, 40, 80, and 160 GeV/nucleon using the UrQMD hadronic transport model. For each baryon species (nucleon, Lambda, Xi, Omega), they extracted the freeze-out time as the time of its last interaction, and calculated the local temperature and energy density at that point by averaging over all hadrons within a 1 fm radius sphere around the baryon, using the equipartition theorem for temperature.

## Key numbers

- At 160 GeV/nucleon incident beam energy, the average freeze-out temperatures for N, Lambda, Xi, and Omega are 86.5, 115, 136, and 145 MeV respectively.
- At 160 GeV/nucleon, the average freeze-out energy densities for N, Lambda, Xi, and Omega are 0.3, 0.57, 0.85, and 0.84 GeV/fm^3 respectively.
- The average freeze-out time for Omega ranges from 16.7 fm/c at 10.6 GeV/nucleon to 16.2 fm/c at 160 GeV/nucleon, while for nucleons it ranges from 18.8 fm/c to 21.2 fm/c over the same energy range.
- For Xi, the average freeze-out time is 24.1 fm/c at 10.6 GeV/nucleon and 25.5 fm/c at 160 GeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the study of freeze-out dynamics of strange baryons from RHIC energies (where reduced slope parameters in transverse momentum spectra were observed by vanHecke (1998) and others) down to lower beam energies, using the UrQMD model developed by Bass (1998) and Bleicher (1999). The finding that Omega freeze-out temperature and energy density approach the critical values for deconfinement at 160 GeV/nucleon suggests that the freeze-out properties of multi-strange baryons could serve as an indicator for the transition from an excited hadron gas to a Quark-Gluon Plasma, motivating further experimental studies at the RHIC low energy run and the FAIR project at GSI.
