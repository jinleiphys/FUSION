# Open-charm systems in cold nuclear matter

> We study the spectral distributions of charmed meson with $J^P=0^-$ quantum numbers in cold nuclear matter applying a self-consistent and covariant many-body approach established previously for the nuclear dynamics of kaons. At leading orders the computation requires as input the free-space two-body scattering amplitudes only. Our results are based on the s-wave meson-nucleon amplitudes obtained recently in terms of a coupled-channel approach. The amplitudes are characterized by the presence of many resonances in part so far not observed. This gives rise to an intriguing dynamics of charmed mesons in nuclear matter. At nuclear saturation density we predict a pronounced two-mode structure of the $D^+$ mesons with a main branch pushed up by about 32 MeV. The lower branch reflects the coupling to two resonance-hole states that are almost degenerate. For the $D^-$ we obtain a single mode pushed up by about 18 MeV relative to the vacuum mode. Most spectacular are the results for the $D^+_s$ meson. The presence of an exotic resonance-hole state gives rise to a rather broad and strongly momentum dependent spectral distribution.

# Open-charm systems in cold nuclear matter

## Key claim

The spectral distributions of charmed mesons in cold nuclear matter are predicted to show non-trivial multi-mode structures driven by resonance-hole dynamics, in contrast to simple mean-field mass shifts. The results depend critically on the specific coupled-channel scattering amplitudes used, which include several resonances that have not yet been experimentally observed.

## Method

A self-consistent and covariant many-body approach, previously developed for kaon-nuclear dynamics, was applied to compute the in-medium spectral functions of D⁺, D⁻, Dₛ⁺, and Dₛ⁻ mesons. The computation uses free-space s-wave meson-nucleon scattering amplitudes from a coupled-channel approach as input, then solves the in-medium Bethe-Salpeter equation iteratively to account for the feedback of the modified meson spectral function on the resonance-hole structure.

## Key numbers

- D⁺ meson at nuclear saturation density: main branch pushed up by about 32 MeV relative to the vacuum mode, with a two-mode structure reflecting coupling to two almost degenerate resonance-hole states
- D⁻ meson at nuclear saturation density: single mode pushed up by about 18 MeV relative to the vacuum mode
- D⁻ scattering lengths: a⁽ᴵ⁼⁰⁾_{D⁻N} ≃ -0.16 fm, a⁽ᴵ⁼¹⁾_{D⁻N} ≃ -0.26 fm
- Low-density theorem mass shift for D⁻: approximately 17 MeV at saturation density (ρ/ρ₀)
- D⁺ scattering lengths: a⁽ᴵ⁼⁰⁾_{D⁺N} ≃ -0.43 fm, a⁽ᴵ⁼¹⁾_{D⁺N} ≃ -0.41 fm
- Coupling strengths: |g^{(DN)}_{Λc(2594)}| ≃ 6.6, |g^{(DN)}_{Σc(2620)}| ≃ 5.8
- Dₛ⁻ scattering length: a_{Dₛ⁻N} ≃ -0.33 fm; main mode pushed up by less than 10 MeV at saturation density
- Dₛ⁺ scattering length: a_{Dₛ⁺N} ≃ -1.21 + i 0.01 fm; exotic state N_{c̄s}(2892) predicted about 75 MeV below Dₛ⁺N threshold
- Coupling strength for exotic state: |g^{(Dₛ⁺N)}_{N_{c̄s}(2892)}| ≃ 2.8

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent many-body approach of Lutz and Korpa (2002), previously applied to kaons and antikaons, to the charmed meson sector. It uses the improved coupled-channel scattering amplitudes of Hofmann and Lutz (2005), which include channels such as KΞc, KΞc', DₛΛ, and DₛΣ that were absent in earlier work by Tolos, Schaffner-Bielich, and Mishra (2004). The results differ significantly from earlier mean-field approaches by Tsushima et al. (1999), which predicted attractive shifts of about 60 MeV for D⁺ and D⁻, and by Sibirtsev et al. (1999), which predicted an attractive shift of about 140 MeV for D⁺ but a repulsive shift of about 20 MeV for D⁻. The predicted two-mode structure for D⁺ and the broad momentum-dependent spectral distribution for Dₛ⁺ are analogous to the antikaon case where the Λ(1405) resonance-hole state produces broad spectral features. Experimental confirmation of the predicted exotic states N_{s̄c}(2780) and N_{c̄s}(2892) is needed, and more detailed studies of D-meson nucleon scattering are required to improve the reliability of the in-medium predictions.
