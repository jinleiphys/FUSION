# New and efficient method for solving the eigenvalue problem for the two-center shell model with finite-depth potentials

> We propose a new method to solve the eigen-value problem with a two-center single-particle potential. This method combines the usual matrix diagonalization with the method of separable representation of a two-center potential, that is, an expansion of the two-center potential with a finite basis set. To this end, we expand the potential on a harmonic oscillator basis, while single-particle wave functions on a combined basis with a harmonic oscillator and eigen-functions of a one-dimensional two-center potential. In order to demonstrate its efficiency, we apply this method to a system with two $^{16}$O nuclei, in which the potential is given as a sum of two Woods-Saxon potentials.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new method for solving the two-center shell model eigenvalue problem that combines the separable representation of a two-center potential (expanded on a harmonic oscillator basis) with direct matrix diagonalization of the single-particle Hamiltonian, thereby avoiding the difficulty of the earlier Lippmann-Schwinger approach when two eigen-energies are close to each other.

## Method

The two-center potential is expanded on a harmonic oscillator basis using the separable representation method of Gareev et al. (1977) and Münchow and Reif (1986). Single-particle wave functions are expanded on a combined basis: harmonic oscillator functions for the radial direction perpendicular to the symmetry axis and eigen-functions of a one-dimensional two-center potential (solved numerically with the Numerov method) for the direction along the symmetry axis. The Hamiltonian matrix is then constructed and diagonalized directly, yielding both bound and continuum states in a single calculation.

## Key numbers

- Woods-Saxon potential parameters used for each \(^{16}\)O well: \(V_0 = -50.2\) MeV, \(R = 1.24 \times 16^{1/3}\) fm, \(\kappa = 0.524\) fm\(^2\), \(a = 0.63\) fm, with nucleon mass \(m = 939.6\) MeV/\(c^2\).
- For the unified \(^{32}\)S nucleus at zero separation, the depth parameter is adjusted to \(V_0 = -51.823\) MeV with \(R = 1.24 \times 32^{1/3}\) fm to satisfy volume conservation.
- The harmonic oscillator frequency for expanding the potential is \(\hbar\omega_0 = 41 \times 16^{-1/3}\) MeV, using 12 major shells.
- For the wave function basis along the \(z\)-direction, eigen-functions are included up to \(\epsilon_z = 20\) MeV with a box size of \(z_{\text{box}} = z/2 + 10\) fm.
- Convergence is demonstrated for the second and fourth positive-parity states with \(j_z^\pi = 1/2^+\) at separation distances of \(z = 3\) fm and \(z = 10\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the separable representation approach of Gareev et al. (1977) and Münchow and Reif (1986) by replacing the energy-dependent solvability condition with direct matrix diagonalization, which handles level crossings more robustly. It also improves upon the two-center harmonic oscillator basis method of Holzer et al. (1969) and the deformed single-center oscillator basis of Iwamoto and Maruhn (2013) by using eigen-functions of a one-dimensional two-center potential for the \(z\)-direction, yielding fast convergence at both small and large separation distances. The method enables efficient calculation of single-particle levels for heavy-ion fusion and fission studies, and the authors suggest extension to deformed potentials for superheavy element synthesis reactions.
