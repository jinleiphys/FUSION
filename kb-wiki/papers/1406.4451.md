# Parity-odd effects in heavy-ion collisions in the HSD model

> Helicity separation effect in non-central heavy ion collisions is investigated using the Hadron-String Dynamics (HSD) model. Computer simulations are done to calculate velocity and hydrodynamic helicity on a mesh in a small volume around the center of the reaction. The time dependence of hydrodynamic helicity is observed for various impact parameters and different calculation methods. Comparison with a similar earlier work is carried out. A new quantity is used to ananlyze particles in the final state. It is used to probe for p-odd effects in the final state.

## Key claim

The paper investigates parity-odd (P-odd) effects in non-central heavy-ion collisions using the Hadron-String Dynamics (HSD) model, finding that hydrodynamic helicity (the volume integral of velocity times vorticity) shows a time-dependent signal that depends on impact parameter, but the final-state observable used to probe P-odd effects is presented as a new quantity rather than a definitive measurement.

## Method

The authors performed computer simulations with the HSD model to calculate velocity fields and hydrodynamic helicity on a spatial mesh in a small volume around the reaction center. They computed the time dependence of the helicity integral (integral of v dot rot v over volume) and related quantities for different impact parameters and using different calculation methods (separating particles by sign of vy). A new quantity, eta-bar, was introduced to analyze particles in the final state by dividing the azimuthal angle into octants and plotting eta-bar versus 1/sqrt(N) to probe for P-odd effects.

## Key numbers

* Hydrodynamic helicity integral (integral of v dot rot v dV) reaches values up to approximately 20 c^2 fm^2 for the total signal and up to approximately 30 c^2 fm^2 for the vy>0 component at times around 10-12 fm/c
* The squared helicity integral (integral of v dot rot v dV)^2 ranges from about 10^-2 to 10^4 (arbitrary units) over time from 7.5 to 17.5 fm/c
* The normalized helicity ratio (integral of v dot rot v dV)^2 / (integral of (rot v)^2 dV * integral of v^2 dV) ranges from about 10^-6 to 10^-2 over time from 7.5 to 17.5 fm/c
* The eta-bar observable for octants 1, 3, 4, 6 shows values ranging from approximately -0.015 to 0.02 as 1/sqrt(N) varies from 0.005 to 0.03
* The eta-bar observable for octants 0, 2, 5, 7 shows values ranging from approximately -0.01 to 0.012 as 1/sqrt(N) varies from 0.005 to 0.03

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations of the helicity separation effect in non-central heavy-ion collisions by using the HSD transport model to compute hydrodynamic helicity directly from simulated velocity fields. The study introduces a new final-state observable (eta-bar) designed to probe for P-odd effects, enabling comparison with previous theoretical work on parity violation in heavy-ion collisions. The results provide a foundation for future experimental searches for P-odd effects in heavy-ion collision data.
