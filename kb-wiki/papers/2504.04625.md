# Transverse-momentum-dependent pion structures from lattice QCD: Collins-Soper kernel, soft factor, TMDWF, and TMDPDF

> We present the first lattice quantum chromodynamics (QCD) calculation of the pion valence-quark transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution function (TMDPDF) within the framework of large-momentum effective theory (LaMET). Using correlators fixed in the Coulomb gauge (CG), we computed the quasi-TMD beam function for a pion with a mass of 300 MeV, a fine lattice spacing of $a = 0.06$ fm and multiple large momenta up to 3 GeV. The intrinsic soft functions in the CG approach are extracted from form factors with large momentum transfer, and as a byproduct, we also obtain the corresponding Collins-Soper (CS) kernel. Our determinations of both the soft function and the CS kernel agree with perturbation theory at small transverse separations ($b_\perp$) between the quarks. At larger $b_\perp$, the CS kernel remains consistent with recent results obtained using both CG and gauge-invariant TMD correlators in the literature. By combining next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) factorization of the quasi-TMD beam function and the soft function, we obtain $x$-dependent pion valence-quark TMDPDF for transverse separations $b_\perp \gtrsim 1$ fm. Interestingly, we find that the $b_\perp$ dependence of the phenomenological parameterizations of TMDPDF for moderate values of $x$ are in reasonable agreement with our QCD determinations. In addition, we present results for the transverse-momentum-dependent wave function (TMDWF) for a heavier pion with 670 MeV mass.

## Key claim
This work presents the first lattice QCD calculation of the pion valence-quark TMDPDF using Coulomb gauge fixed correlators rather than gauge-invariant Wilson line operators, with the claim that the CG approach avoids Wilson line renormalization difficulties and improves signal-to-noise ratios, but the results are obtained at a single lattice spacing (a = 0.06 fm) and a pion mass of 300 MeV (with a heavier 670 MeV pion used for the soft function), meaning continuum and physical mass extrapolations remain to be performed.

## Method
The authors computed quasi-TMD beam function matrix elements for a pion with mass 300 MeV using Coulomb gauge fixed quark bilinear correlators on a 48^3 x 64 lattice with a = 0.06 fm, at momenta up to 3.04 GeV. The intrinsic soft function was extracted from form factors with large momentum transfer using a heavier 670 MeV pion at P^z = 2.58 GeV, and the Collins-Soper kernel was obtained from ratios of quasi-TMDWFs at different momenta. The quasi-TMDPDF was matched to the light-cone TMDPDF using next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) factorization combining the quasi-TMD beam function, the intrinsic soft function, and the hard kernel.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing a = 0.06 fm, volume 48^3 x 64
- Valence pion mass 300 MeV for quasi-TMD beam function; heavier valence pion mass 670 MeV for form factor and soft function extraction
- Maximum hadron momentum P^z = 3.04 GeV for quasi-TMD calculations (off-axis momenta with n^x = n^y = 5)
- Maximum momentum P^z = 4.30 GeV for quasi-TMDWF calculations (n^z = 10)
- Form factor computed at single momentum P^z = 2.58 GeV (n = (0,0,6))
- Transverse separations b_perp studied up to 1.2 fm
- Source-sink separations t_sep/a = 6, 8, 10, 12 used for ground-state extraction
- 553 gauge configurations used for quasi-TMD analysis; 100 configurations for form factor calculation
- Collins-Soper kernel and soft function agree with perturbation theory at small b_perp; CS kernel consistent with literature at larger b_perp
- TMDPDF obtained for transverse separations b_perp greater than or approximately equal to 1 fm

## Context
This work extends the Coulomb gauge approach to TMDs proposed by Zhao (2023) and Gao (2023), which avoids the Wilson line complications present in gauge-invariant TMD calculations by Shanahan (2020), the Lattice Parton Collaboration (2020, 2022, 2023), and Avkhadiev (2023, 2024). The results are compared with phenomenological parameterizations from global analyses by Bacchetta (2017, 2019, 2022, 2024), Scimemi (2017, 2019), and Vladimirov (2019), showing reasonable agreement for moderate x values. This enables first-principles lattice QCD constraints on pion TMDs that can be used in analyses for the Electron-Ion Collider and Jefferson Lab 12 GeV program.
