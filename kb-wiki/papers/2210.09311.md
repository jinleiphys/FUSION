# Beautiful and Charming Energy Correlators

> Understanding the detailed structure of energy flow within jets, a field known as jet substructure, plays a central role in searches for new physics, and precision studies of QCD. Many applications of jet substructure require an understanding of jets initiated by heavy quarks, whose description has lagged behind remarkable recent progress for massless jets. In this Letter, we initiate a study of correlation functions of energy flow operators on beauty and charm jets to illuminate the effects of the intrinsic mass of the elementary particles of QCD. We present a factorization theorem incorporating the mass of heavy quarks, and show that the heavy quark jet functions for energy correlators have a simple structure in perturbation theory. Our results achieve the very first full next-to-leading-logarithmic calculation of the heavy quark jet substructure observable at the LHC. Using this framework, we study the behavior of the correlators, and show that they exhibit a clear transition from a massless scaling regime, at precisely the scale of the heavy quark mass. This manifests the long-sought-after dead-cone effect and illustrates fundamental effects from the intrinsic mass of beauty and charm quarks in a perturbative regime, before they are confined inside hadrons. Our theoretical framework for studying energy correlators using heavy jets has many exciting applications for improving the description of mass effects in next generation parton shower event generators, probing the QGP, and studying heavy flavor fragmentation functions.

# Beautiful and Charming Energy Correlators

## Key claim

The paper claims that correlation functions of energy flow operators on beauty and charm jets exhibit a clear transition from a massless scaling regime to a mass-dominated regime at precisely the scale of the heavy quark mass, manifesting the dead-cone effect in an observable that can be systematically computed in perturbation theory. The hedge is that this holds under the condition that the jet scale is much larger than the heavy quark mass (μ_J ≫ m_Q), so that the mass dependence factorizes completely into the heavy flavor jet function.

## Method

The authors derive a factorization theorem for projected N-point energy correlators (ENC) on heavy quark jets, separating the hard function describing collinear source production from the energy correlator jet function encoding heavy quark mass effects. They compute the heavy quark EEC jet function at NLO using both massive splitting functions and massive SCET, verifying the factorization theorem by comparing the full angle EEC for e+e- → Q Qbar g expanded in the small angle limit against the direct operator definition of the jet function. The calculation achieves NLL accuracy for heavy quark jet substructure at the LHC, incorporating NLO hard functions, NLO fragmenting jet functions, and NLO energy correlator jet constants.

## Key numbers

- The heavy quark EEC jet function at NLO is given by an integral expression (Eq. 6) containing the parameter δ = i m_Q / (p_T R_L), where m_Q is the heavy quark mass, p_T is the jet transverse momentum, and R_L is the angular scale.
- For integer N up to N=6, explicit results for the jet function are expressed in terms of transcendental functions of weight 1 (logarithms) with alphabet {δ, δ̄, 1+δ, 1+δ̄}.
- The turnover in the two-point correlator occurs at angular scale R_L ∼ m_Q/p_T, analogous to the turnover at R_L ∼ Λ_QCD/p_T for light quark jets.
- Ratios of projected correlators for N>2 to N=2 are shown up to six-point correlators, exhibiting scaling behavior identical to massless calculations.

## Context

This work extends the recent theoretical progress in energy correlators for massless jets (Dixon 2019, Chen 2020, Lee 2022) to heavy quark jets, where theoretical description has lagged behind. It provides the first full NLL calculation of a heavy quark jet substructure observable at the LHC. The framework enables systematic perturbative description of the dead-cone effect (Dokshitzer 1991), recently measured by ALICE (2021) using a declustering procedure, but now in an infrared-safe observable. The results have applications for improving heavy quark descriptions in next-generation parton showers (Li 2016, Hoche 2017, Dasgupta 2020, Hamilton 2021), probing the quark-gluon plasma, and studying heavy flavor fragmentation functions.
