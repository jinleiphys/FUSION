# Nonextensive quantum statistics and saturation of the PMD-SQS optimality limit in hadron-hadron scattering

> In this paper, new results on the analysis in hadron-hadron scattering are obtained by using the nonextensive quantum entropy and principle of minimum distance in the space of quantum states (PMD-SQS). Using Tsallis-like scattering entropies, the optimality as well as the nonextensive statistical behavior of the quantum scattering systems are investigated in an unified manner. A connection between optimal states obtained from the principle of minimum distance in the space of quantum states and the most stringent (MaxEnt) entropic bounds on Tsallis-like entropies for quantum scattering, is established. The generalized entropic uncertainty relations as well as the correlation between the nonextensivities p and q of the scattering statistics are proved. New results on the experimental tests of the saturation of the optimality limit}, as well as on the test of optimal entropic bands obtained by using the experimental pion-nucleon, kaon-nucleon, antikaon-nucleon phase shifts, are presented. The nonextensivity indices p and q are determined from the experimental entropies by a fit with the optimal entropies obtained from the principle of minimum distance in the space of states. Strong experimental evidences for the p-nonextensivity index in the range p=0.6 with q=p/(2p-1)=3, is obtained from the experimental data.

# Nonextensive quantum statistics and saturation of the PMD-SQS optimality limit in hadron-hadron scattering

## Key claim
The paper claims that the quantum scattering states produced in pion-nucleon, kaon-nucleon, and antikaon-nucleon scattering saturate the PMD-SQS (principle of minimum distance in the space of quantum states) optimality limit, with the nonextensivity indices p and q correlated via the Riesz-Thorin relation 1/(2p) + 1/(2q) = 1. The hedge is that this saturation is observed only when the nonextensivity indices take the specific values p = 0.6 and q = 3, and the claim is restricted to the pure isospin scattering states investigated.

## Method
The authors reconstructed experimental pion-nucleon, kaon-nucleon, and antikaon-nucleon scattering amplitudes from available phase-shift analyses. They then computed Tsallis-like scattering entropies S_J(p) for the angular-momentum (J) quantum states and S_theta(q) for the angular (theta) quantum states, and compared these to the optimal entropies S_J^o1(p) and S_theta^o1(q) derived from the PMD-SQS principle. The nonextensivity indices p and q were determined by fitting the experimental entropies to the optimal entropies using chi-squared test functions, with the optimal angular momentum J_o estimated from experimental cross sections.

## Key numbers
- The nonextensivity index p for the J-quantum states is found in the range 1/2 <= p <= 0.6, indicating superextensive (superadditive) statistics.
- The nonextensivity index q for the theta-quantum states is found to be q >= 3, indicating subextensive (subadditive) statistics.
- The best-fit correlated pair is p = 0.6 with q = p/(2p-1) = 3, obtained from experimental data on (pi N, KN, anti-K N) scattering.
- The optimal angular momentum J_o is given by (J_o + 1)^2 - 1/4 = (4 pi / sigma_el) * (d sigma / d Omega)(1), where sigma_el is the elastic integrated cross section.
- The chi-squared test yields confidence levels greater than 99% for the agreement between experimental entropies and optimal entropies with the correlated nonextensivity indices.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous development of the PMD-SQS principle (Ion and Ion, 2004) and applies it to hadron-hadron scattering using Tsallis nonextensive statistics, which has been applied to phenomena such as electron-positron annihilations (Refs. 4,5), quark-gluon plasma (Ref. 6), and hadronic collisions (Refs. 7-13). The paper establishes a connection between PMD-SQS optimal states and the most stringent MaxEnt entropic bounds on Tsallis-like entropies for quantum scattering. The results enable future investigations into whether the observed saturation of optimality limits and nonextensive statistical behavior represents a new universal law of quantum scattering, and may be interpreted as an indirect manifestation of quarks and gluons as fundamental constituents in the strong-coupling long-range regime required by Quantum Chromodynamics.
