# Constraining the Phase-Transition EoS using the Energy Dependence of Directed Flow

> We propose a hybrid equation of state (VDF+MIT EoS) to describe the hadron-quark phase transition in dense nuclear matter. By coupling this EoS with the AMPT-HC transport model and comparing to recent experimental data on proton and $Λ$ directed flow $v_1$, we constrain the transition to likely occur near $5ρ_0$--$6ρ_0$, ruling out transitions below $3ρ_0$. Furthermore, we introduce the energy derivative of the mid-rapidity $v_1$ slope, $d(dv_1/dy)/d(\sqrt{s_{NN}})$, as a weakly model-dependent observable. Its zero crossing provides a direct signature of the phase transition critical point, offering a new tool for mapping the QCD phase diagram in future experiments.

## Key claim

The hadron-quark phase transition in dense nuclear matter is constrained to likely occur near 5ρ₀–6ρ₀, with transitions below 3ρ₀ ruled out, based on comparing a hybrid VDF+MIT equation of state coupled to the AMPT-HC transport model with experimental proton and Λ directed flow data. The energy derivative of the mid-rapidity v₁ slope, d(dv₁/dy)/d(√s_NN), is introduced as a weakly model-dependent observable whose zero crossing provides a direct signature of the phase transition critical point.

## Method

The authors constructed a hybrid equation of state (VDF+MIT EoS) by extending the relativistic Vector Density Functional (VDF) model with the MIT bag model to describe the hadron-quark phase transition, then coupled this EoS into the AMPT-HC transport model (a hadronic cascade mode of the AMPT framework). They simulated Au+Au collisions at fixed-target energies (√s_NN = 3.0, 4.5, and 7.7 GeV) and calculated the directed flow v₁ for protons and Λ hyperons as a function of rapidity, comparing results to RHIC-STAR experimental data. The energy derivative of the mid-rapidity v₁ slope was obtained by scanning multiple energy points and taking the derivative of a polynomial fit to the v₁ slope versus √s_NN distribution.

## Key numbers

- The phase transition is constrained to occur near 5ρ₀–6ρ₀, with transitions below 3ρ₀ ruled out.
- Three hybrid EoSs were constructed: VDF1+MIT (transition at ~3ρ₀, incompressibility K₀ = 261 MeV), VDF2+MIT (transition at ~4ρ₀, K₀ = 279 MeV), and VDF3+MIT (transition at ~6ρ₀, K₀ = 356 MeV).
- The VDF3+MIT EoS shows the strongest agreement with experimental proton and Λ v₁ data at √s_NN = 3.0, 4.5, and 7.7 GeV from RHIC-STAR.
- The maximum compressed baryon density in Au+Au collisions exceeds the VDF1+MIT transition threshold at 3 GeV, the VDF2+MIT threshold at 4.5 GeV, and the VDF3+MIT threshold at 7.7 GeV.
- The zero crossing of d(dv₁/dy)/d(√s_NN) occurs at different energies for each EoS: VDF1+MIT reaches zero at a significantly lower energy than VDF3+MIT, which requires higher collision energy.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Vector Density Functional (VDF) model of nuclear matter (VDForigin, VDFlate) by incorporating the MIT bag model to describe quark matter at high density, and applies it within the AMPT-HC transport framework (Yong 2021). The results contradict EoSs predicting phase transitions below 3ρ₀ and are consistent with the RHIC-STAR Beam Energy Scan program's measurements of directed flow (STAR 2022). The proposed observable d(dv₁/dy)/d(√s_NN) provides a new tool for mapping the QCD phase diagram that can be tested in future experiments at HIAF, FAIR, and GSI-CBM.
