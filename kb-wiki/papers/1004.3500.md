# Heavy Ion Collisions with Transverse Dynamics from Evolving AdS Geometries

> Currently there exists no known way to construct the Stress-Energy Tensor $(T_{μν})$ of the produced medium in heavy ion collisions at strong coupling from purely theoretical grounds. In this paper, some steps are taken in that direction. In particular, the evolution of $T_{μν}$ at strong coupling and at high energies is being studied for early proper times $(τ)$. This is achieved in the context of the AdS/CFT duality by constructing the evolution of the dual geometry in an AdS$_5$ background. Improving the earlier works in the literature, the two incident nuclei have an impact parameter $b$ and a non-trivial transverse profile. The nuclear matter is modeled by two shock waves corresponding to a non-zero five dimensional bulk Stress-Energy Tensor $J_{MN}$. An analytic formula for $T_{μν}$ at small $τ$ is derived and is used in order to calculate the momentum anisotropy and spatial eccentricity of the medium produced in the collision as a function of the ratio $\fracτ{b}$. The result for eccentricity at intermediate $\fracτ{b}$ agrees qualitatively with the results obtained in the context of perturbation theory and by using hydrodynamic simulations. Finally, the problem of the negative energy density and its natural connection to the eikonal approximation is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives an analytic formula for the stress-energy tensor of the medium produced in heavy ion collisions at strong coupling and early proper times, using AdS/CFT duality with two shock waves that have a nonzero impact parameter and nontrivial transverse profiles; however, the result applies only for early proper times and relies on a logarithmic approximation to the transverse nuclear profile that is valid when the transverse distance and proper time are much smaller than the inverse of the infrared cutoff scale.

## Method

The author models two colliding nuclei as gravitational shock waves in an AdS$_5$ background, each corresponding to a five-dimensional bulk stress-energy tensor that is point-like in the transverse plane and light-cone coordinates but extends along the fifth dimension. Einstein's equations are solved perturbatively to second order in the shock wave strength, and the gauge theory stress-energy tensor is extracted via holographic renormalization using the Fefferman-Graham coordinate system. The calculation incorporates back-reaction effects to ensure conservation of the bulk stress-energy tensor and uses retarded propagators that enforce causal structure in the forward light cone.

## Key numbers

* The energy density in regions III and III' (intermediate proper times) behaves as $\sim \tau^2 \log(\tau/b)$ at central rapidities, with an additional overall factor of proper time squared compared to perturbative QCD results from Lappi (2003) and Fries (2006).
* The spatial eccentricity at intermediate $\tau/b$ is calculated as a function of the ratio $\tau/b$ and shows qualitative agreement with results from perturbative QCD (Lappi 2006, Jas 2007) and hydrodynamic simulations (Kolb 2000, Kolb 2003).
* The transverse pressure components in the two limiting cases investigated are equal, leading to a momentum anisotropy close to zero.
* The infrared cutoff $k$ is related to the nucleus size as $k \sim \Lambda_{QCD} / A^{1/3}$ where $A$ is the atomic number.
* The parameter $\mu$ scales as $\mu \sim p^- \Lambda^2_{QCD} A^{1/3}$ where $p^-$ is the light-cone momentum per nucleon.

## Context

This work extends earlier AdS/CFT approaches to heavy ion collisions by Kovchegov (2007) and Albacete (2008, 2009) by incorporating a nonzero impact parameter and nontrivial transverse profiles for the colliding nuclei, which were previously treated as uniform in the transverse plane. The result enables calculation of the spatial eccentricity and momentum anisotropy of the produced medium at strong coupling for early proper times, and the qualitative agreement with perturbative QCD results from Lappi (2006) and Jas (2007) and hydrodynamic simulations by Kolb (2000, 2003) suggests that the AdS/CFT approach can capture essential features of the collision dynamics. The paper also discusses the problem of negative energy density and its connection to the eikonal approximation, and proposes a phenomenological model for the energy density evolution using a Woods-Saxon profile.
