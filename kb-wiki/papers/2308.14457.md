# New covariant density functionals of nuclear matter for compact star simulations

> We generate three families of extended covariant density functionals of nuclear matter that have varying slope of symmetry energy and skewness at nuclear saturation density, but otherwise share the same basic parameters (symmetry energy, compressibility, saturation parameters, etc.) with the standard DDME2, DD2, and MPE functionals. Tables of the parameters of these new density functionals are given, which can be straightforwardly used in DDME2, DD2, and MPE parameterization-based codes. Furthermore, we provide tables of a large number of equations of state (81 for each family) that can be used in astrophysical simulations to assess the impact of variations of not-well-known slope of symmetry energy and skewness of nuclear systems on the astrophysics of compact objects. We also provide tables of computed integral parameters (mass, radius, and tidal deformability) that can be used, e.g., for modeling gravitational waveforms. Finally, for the extended DDME2-based parameterization, we implement a first-order phase transition to quark matter to obtain a family of equations of state that accommodates a phase transition to quark matter. Analogous tables of the equations of state and integral parameters are provided for this case as well.

## Key claim

The authors generate three families of extended covariant density functionals that share the same low-order nuclear matter parameters (symmetry energy, compressibility, saturation parameters) with the standard DDME2, DD2, and MPE functionals, but allow systematic variation of the not-well-determined slope of symmetry energy ($L_{\text{sym}}$) and skewness ($Q_{\text{sat}}$) at saturation density, with the hedge that the functionals are constructed to cover a substantial range of the mass-radius diagram compatible with current astrophysical constraints rather than being uniquely determined by data.

## Method

Starting from the three reference covariant density functionals (DDME2, DD2, MPE) with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings, the authors fix the couplings at saturation density for the isoscalar $\sigma$ and $\omega$ mesons and at cross density $\rho_c = 0.11$ fm$^{-3}$ for the isovector $\rho$ meson, then modify the density dependence of these couplings to vary $Q_{\text{sat}}$ and $L_{\text{sym}}$ while preserving the lower-order saturation characteristics. They compute the equation of state of cold $\beta$-equilibrium stellar matter, match it to crust equations of state, and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass, radius, and tidal deformability for static spherically symmetric stars. For the DDME2-based family, they additionally implement a first-order phase transition to quark matter using a constant-speed-of-sound parameterization.

## Key numbers

- 81 equations of state generated for each of the three families (DDME2, DD2, MPE) by varying $Q_{\text{sat}}$ from -600 to 1000 MeV in steps of 200 MeV and $L_{\text{sym}}$ from 30 to 110 MeV in steps of 10 MeV
- For $Q_{\text{sat}} = -600$ MeV, the maximum mass is about $2.0\,M_\odot$, matching the mass measurement of PSR J0740+6620
- For $Q_{\text{sat}} \geq 600$ MeV, the maximum mass reaches $2.59^{+0.08}_{-0.09}\,M_\odot$, the value deduced for the light companion of GW190814 if interpreted as a neutron star
- The choice of the specific form of the CDF has a small impact ($< 2\%$) on the radius of a canonical $1.4\,M_{\odot}$ mass star
- Differences in maximum masses predicted by DDME2/DD2 and MPE CDFs can reach up to 5\%, corresponding to $\sim 0.1\,M_\odot$
- For the MPE family, a slightly higher value $Q_{\text{sat}} \geq -500$ MeV is required to reach the high mass $2.08^{+0.07}_{-0.07}\,M_{\odot}$ of PSR J0740+6620
- Phase transition scenarios considered at $\rho_{\text{tran}}/\rho_{\text{sat}} \lesssim 2.0$ and $\rho_{\text{tran}}/\rho_{\text{sat}} \gtrsim 3.0$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous smaller sample of covariant density functional equations of state (Li 2019) and provides a systematic alternative to purely phenomenological expansions used in metamodels (Margueron 2018) and Bayesian analyses (Char 2020, Traversi 2020, Malik 2023). The generated tables enable systematic studies of the impact of the slope of symmetry energy and skewness on compact star astrophysics, including gravitational-wave modeling for events like GW170817 (LIGO-Virgo 2017, 2018, 2019) and GW190814 (LIGO-Virgo 2020), and can be used to study hypernuclear matter and phase transitions to quark matter in future work.
