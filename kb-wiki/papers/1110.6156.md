# Chiral effective theory with a light scalar and lattice QCD

> We extend the usual chiral perturbation theory framework ($χ$PT) to allow the inclusion of a light dynamical isosinglet scalar. Using lattice QCD results, and a few phenomenological inputs, we explore the parameter space of the effective theory. We discuss the S-wave pion-pion scattering lengths, extract the average value of the two light quark masses and evaluate the impact of the dynamical singlet field in the low--energy constants $\bar{l}_1$, $\bar{l}_3$ and $\bar{l}_4$ of $χ$PT. We also show how to extract the mass and width of the sigma resonance from chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD data.

## Key claim

The paper extends standard chiral perturbation theory (χPT) by including a light dynamical isosinglet scalar field as an explicit degree of freedom, but finds that at current precision, lattice QCD data cannot distinguish this extended theory (χPT_S) from standard χPT.

## Method

The authors construct the most general SU(2) chiral Lagrangian including an isospin-zero scalar field at order p^4, calculate the pion mass, pion decay constant, scalar two-point function, and S-wave pion-pion scattering lengths at next-to-leading order (NLO), and perform fits to lattice QCD data for m_π and F_π from Baron et al. (2009) and for ππ scattering lengths from Fu et al. (2011). They compare χPT_S results with standard χPT and show how to extract the sigma resonance mass and width from chiral extrapolations.

## Key numbers

- Sigma resonance mass: m_σ = 441^{+16}_{-8} MeV, half-width Γ/2 = 272^{+9}_{-12.5} MeV (from Caprini et al. 2005, Leutwyler 2008)
- Chiral cutoff scale Λ_χ is taken of order the rho mass m_ρ ~ 770 MeV
- Pion mass m_π ~ 140 MeV, kaon mass m_K ~ 490 MeV, eta mass m_η ~ 550 MeV
- The scalar mass at NLO scales almost linearly with the pion mass up to the threshold region where the ππ production channel closes
- The scalar decay width at LO depends only on the single unknown LEC c_{1d}: Γ/2 = (3c²_{1d})/(8π F² m_S) √(1 - 4m²_π/m²_S) (m²_S - 2m²_π)²

## Context

This work extends the standard χPT framework of Weinberg (1978) and Gasser and Leutwyler (1983) by relaxing the assumption of a mass gap for all states except Goldstone bosons, motivated by the relatively low mass of the sigma resonance compared to the chiral cutoff. It builds on earlier observations by Weinberg (1963) that explicit inclusion of resonances improves perturbation theory, and differs from the linear sigma model of Gell-Mann and Levy (1960) by setting scalar self-interactions to zero (or small values) and introducing four additional free parameters for scalar-pion interactions. The approach enables extraction of the sigma resonance mass and width from lattice QCD data and provides a framework for comparing χPT_S versus standard χPT against future, more precise lattice calculations.
