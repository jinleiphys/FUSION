# Correspondence between Israel-Stewart and first-order casual and stable hydrodynamics for the boost-invariant massive case with zero baryon density

> Exact correspondence between Israel-Stewart theory and first-order causal and stable hydrodynamics is established for the boost-invariant massive case with zero baryon density and the same constant relaxation times used in the shear and bulk sectors. Explicit expressions for the temperature dependent regulators are given for the case of a relativistic massive gas. The stability and causality conditions known in the first-order approach are applied and one finds that one of them is violated in this case.

## Key claim

An exact correspondence between Israel-Stewart (IS) theory and first-order causal and stable (FOCS) hydrodynamics can be established for the boost-invariant massive case with zero baryon density, but only under the restrictive assumption that the same constant relaxation time is used in the shear and bulk sectors; even then, one of the FOCS causality conditions is violated for the massive case.

## Method

The authors start from the boost-invariant Israel-Stewart equations for a baryon-free system (Eqs. 8-10) and the FOCS boost-invariant formulation (Eq. 20), both of which take the form of Ricatti equations. By assuming equal constant relaxation times in the shear and bulk sectors (\(\tau_\pi = \tau_\Pi = \tau_R\)), they match the coefficients of the two Ricatti equations term-by-term, deriving explicit expressions for the FOCS regulator functions \(\varepsilon_1, \varepsilon_2, \pi_1, \pi_2\) in terms of the IS parameters and the equation of state. The matching is then applied to a relativistic massive gas obeying classical statistics, using the equation of state given by modified Bessel functions and the bulk and shear viscosity coefficients from the relaxation-time approximation.

## Key numbers

- For the massless limit, the regulators are \(\varepsilon_1^{(m=0)} = 4a\tau_R T^4\), \(\varepsilon_2^{(m=0)} = \frac{4}{3}a\tau_R T^4\), \(\pi_1^{(m=0)} = \beta \varepsilon_1^{(m=0)}\), and \(\pi_2^{(m=0)} = \beta \varepsilon_2^{(m=0)}\), where \(a\) is a constant proportional to the number of internal degrees of freedom.
- For the massive classical case with pion mass \(m = m_\pi = 140\) MeV, the regulators approach their massless limits at high temperature; the leading-order deviations for \(\varepsilon_1\) are \(\varepsilon_1 = \varepsilon_1^{(m=0)} [1 - z^2/24 - z^4/192 + \mathcal{O}(z^6)]\) where \(z = m/T\).
- For the massive case, the relation \(\pi_2 = \beta \varepsilon_2\) is only approximate; the expansion gives \(\pi_2 = \pi_2^{(m=0)} [1 - z^2/8 + (z^4/64)(1 - 20/(27\beta)) + \mathcal{O}(z^6)]\).
- In the temperature range \(0 \leq T \leq 200\) MeV, all FOCS causality and stability conditions are satisfied numerically except for one condition (Eq. 30 in the paper), which is violated.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous matching between IS and FOCS for massless, conformal systems (Das et al., 2020) to the massive case, using the same constant relaxation time assumption. The FOCS framework was recently developed by Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, and Kovtun (2017-2019) as a first-order causal and stable alternative to IS theory. The matching allows causality and stability criteria from FOCS to be applied to the corresponding IS framework, revealing that the special IS case considered here is not causal. This result motivates future direct studies of IS causality and stability, such as those in Bemfica et al. (2020).
