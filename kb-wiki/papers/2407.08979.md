# Neutron matter from local chiral effective field theory interactions at large cutoffs

> Neutron matter is an important many-body system that provides valuable constraints for the equation of state (EOS) of neutron stars. Neutron-matter calculations employing chiral effective field theory (EFT) interactions have been extensively used for this purpose. Among the various many-body methods, quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) methods stand out due to their nonperturbative nature and the achievable precision. However, QMC methods require local interactions as input, which leads to the appearance of stronger regulator artifacts compared to non-local interactions. To circumvent this, we employ large-cutoff interactions derived within chiral EFT ($400 \mev \leq Λ_c \leq 700 \mev$) for studies of pure neutron matter. These interactions have been adjusted to nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts, the triton binding energy, as well as the triton $β$-decay half-life. We find that regulator artifacts significantly decrease with increasing cutoff, leading to a significant reduction of uncertainties in the neutron-matter EOS. We discuss implications for the symmetry energy and demonstrate how our new calculations lead to a reduction in the theoretical uncertainty of predicted neutron-star radii by up to 30\% for low-mass stars.

# Neutron matter from local chiral effective field theory interactions at large cutoffs

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that using local chiral EFT interactions with large momentum-space cutoffs (up to ~700 MeV) in quantum Monte Carlo calculations of pure neutron matter reduces regulator artifacts to below the size of EFT truncation uncertainties, thereby decreasing the overall uncertainty in the neutron-matter equation of state by a factor of 3 compared to lower-cutoff results. This reduction in theoretical uncertainty propagates to a 20-30% reduction in the uncertainty of predicted neutron-star radii for low-mass stars.

## Method

The authors perform auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations of pure neutron matter using local chiral EFT interactions up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N^2LO) with coordinate-space cutoffs ranging from R0 = 1.0 fm to 0.6 fm, corresponding to momentum-space cutoffs of approximately 400 MeV to 670 MeV. The interactions are fitted to nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts, and the three-nucleon low-energy couplings c_D and c_E are determined by fitting to the triton binding energy and the triton beta-decay half-life via Faddeev equations. Each AFDMC simulation uses 66 neutrons in a finite box, employing the constrained-path algorithm with transient estimates to extract ground-state energies.

## Key numbers

- Regulator artifacts at saturation density (n_sat = 0.16 fm^-3) decrease from variations at the ~5 MeV level for low cutoffs (~400 MeV) to ~200 keV for the highest cutoff (R0 = 0.6 fm, ~670 MeV), compared to an EKM truncation uncertainty of ~1 MeV
- At R0 = 0.6 fm, the energy difference between the 3N TPE and V_E,1 interaction is only 10 keV at the constrained-path level, and ~200 keV after transient estimate
- At 2n_sat, the attractive 3N regulator artifact (V_E,tau) contributes ~2 MeV additional attraction, compared to an EKM truncation uncertainty of ~5 MeV
- The symmetry energy at saturation density is predicted as E_sym(n_sat) = 31.9 ± 1.3 MeV, with slope L = 40.4 ± 8.1 MeV
- For a 1.0 solar mass neutron star, the radius is R_1.0 = 11.62^{+0.38}_{-0.46} km (68% confidence), compared to R_1.0 = 11.70^{+0.48}_{-0.68} km from previous lower-cutoff QMC results
- For a 1.4 solar mass neutron star, the radius is R_1.4 = 11.70^{+0.38}_{-0.51} km

## Context

This work extends previous quantum Monte Carlo calculations of neutron matter by Lynn et al. (2015) and Tews et al. (2018) that used local chiral interactions with typical cutoffs of ~400-500 MeV, where regulator artifacts were comparable to truncation uncertainties. The authors build on the local chiral interaction framework of Gezerlis et al. (2014) and Somasundaram et al. (2023) to explore higher cutoffs up to ~700 MeV, demonstrating that regulator artifacts become negligible at these scales. The improved neutron-matter equation of state provides tighter constraints for the symmetry energy and neutron-star structure calculations, enabling more precise predictions for multimessenger astrophysics analyses of neutron-star mergers and pulsar observations.
