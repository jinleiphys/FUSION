# Polarization phenomena in elastic $e^{\mp}N$ scattering, for axial parametrization of two-photon exchange

> We analyze polarization phenomena for elastic lepton-nucleon scattering, parametrizing the $2γ$-exchange contribution, as the product of lepton and nucleon axial vector currents, that correspond to $C=+1$ exchange in the annihilation channel. We found two combinations of polarization observables (one for three T-odd observables and another one - for five T-even observables), which allow to measure the ratio $G_E(Q^2)/G_M(Q^2)$ of nucleon electromagnetic form factors in model independent way, without any specific assumption about the $2γ$-exchange mechanism. Both these combinations have a general character and do not depend on the choice of the spin structure for the $2γ$-exchange. We show the inconsistency of other approximations recently used in the literature.

# Polarization phenomena in elastic $e^{\mp}N$ scattering, for axial parametrization of two-photon exchange

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two specific combinations of polarization observables (one involving three T-odd observables and another involving five T-even observables) can determine the ratio $G_E(Q^2)/G_M(Q^2)$ of nucleon electromagnetic form factors in a model-independent way, without any assumption about the spin structure of the two-photon exchange contribution. However, this model independence holds only for the physical form factor ratio; the expressions for individual generalized form factors do depend on the chosen parametrization (axial versus tensor) of the two-photon exchange amplitude.

## Method

The authors parametrize the two-photon exchange contribution to elastic lepton-nucleon scattering using an axial vector current structure (product of lepton and nucleon axial currents), corresponding to $C=+1$ exchange in the annihilation channel. They derive expressions for the differential cross section, final nucleon polarization components $P_x$, $P_z$, T-odd observables ($P_y$, $A_y$, depolarization tensor components $D_{xy}$, $D_{yx}$, $D_{yz}$, $D_{zy}$), and T-even observables (depolarization tensor components $D_{xx}$, $D_{yy}$, $D_{zz}$, $D_{xz}$) in terms of three complex amplitudes: $F_{1,2}^{(\mp)}(Q^2,\epsilon)$ and ${\cal A}^{(\mp)}(Q^2,\epsilon)$. They then construct combinations of these observables that isolate the physical form factor ratio, and compare the results with those from the alternative tensor parametrization of Rekalo and Tomasi-Gustafsson (2003).

## Key numbers

- The two-photon exchange amplitudes $\Delta F_{1,2}(Q^2,\epsilon)$ and ${\cal A}(Q^2,\epsilon)$ are of order $\alpha \simeq 1/137$, while the leading form factors $F_{1,2}(Q^2)$ are of order $\alpha^0$.
- The T-odd polarization observables are small in absolute value, being of order $\alpha = 1/137$.
- The T-even observables require measurement with accuracy better than 1% to extract the form factor ratio.
- The variable $\epsilon$ (virtual photon polarization) ranges from $0$ (at $\theta_e = \pi$) to $1$ (at $\theta_e = 0$).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of polarization phenomena in elastic electron-nucleon scattering using a tensor parametrization of the two-photon exchange (Rekalo and Tomasi-Gustafsson, 2003). It addresses the controversy between form factor extractions from Rosenbluth separation (Andivahis et al., 1975) and polarization transfer methods (Rekalo and Tomasi-Gustafsson, 2003; Rekalo, 1968), which Guichon and Vanderhaeghen (2003) and Blunden et al. (2003) suggested could be resolved by two-photon exchange contributions. The paper shows that simplifying assumptions about the two-photon exchange structure can contradict C-invariance and crossing symmetry, and that the proposed model-independent combinations of observables are necessary for a rigorous extraction of the physical form factors. The results enable future experiments at Jefferson Lab to determine whether two-photon exchange effects are sizeable in the momentum transfer range already investigated, which would require reanalysis of all one-photon-based extractions.
