# Quantum Collective QCD String Dynamics

> The string breaking model of particle production is extended in order to help explain the transverse momentum distribution in elementary collisions. Inspired by an idea of Bialas', we treat the string using a collective coordinate approach. This leads to a chromo-electric field strength which fluctuates, and in turn implies that quarks are produced according to a thermal distribution.

# Quantum Collective QCD String Dynamics

## Key claim

The string breaking model of particle production is extended by treating the QCD string as a collective quantum excitation of the gluon field, which causes the chromo-electric field strength to fluctuate according to a quantum probability distribution; folding this distribution with the Schwinger pair production formula yields an exponential (thermal) transverse momentum spectrum, resolving the long-standing discrepancy between the Gaussian prediction of the classical string model and the observed thermal spectra in elementary collisions.

## Method

The authors separate the longitudinal and transverse dynamics of the QCD string, treating the longitudinal motion classically while quantizing the transverse collective coordinates. They construct a Hamiltonian for the string energy density per unit length consisting of a chromo-electric field term and a bag pressure term, introduce canonically conjugate operators \(x = \sqrt{A}\) and \(p = \sqrt{gE_L/2}\) (where \(A\) is the string cross-sectional area and \(E_L\) the longitudinal chromo-electric field), and solve the resulting harmonic oscillator wave equation in the \(p\)-representation. The probability distribution for the field strength obtained from the ground state wavefunction is then folded with the Schwinger-Casher-Neuberger-Nussinov formula for quark pair production to obtain the transverse momentum spectrum.

## Key numbers

- The classical string tension is \(\sigma = g\sqrt{2B}/2\) where \(g\) is the color charge and \(B\) the bag constant.
- The quantized string tension eigenvalues are \(\sigma_n = \sqrt{2Bg^2}(n + 1/2)\) for integer \(n\).
- The ground state expectation values satisfy \(\langle E_L \rangle = \sqrt{2B}\) and \(\langle A \rangle = g/(2\sqrt{2B})\), matching the classical picture.
- Using a standard string constant \(\sigma_0 \approx 0.9\) GeV/fm yields a temperature parameter \(T_0 \approx 165\) MeV.
- The resulting quark transverse production probability per unit 4-volume is \(dP_{\mathrm{quark}}(p_\perp) = (C T_0^2/g)(1 + m_\perp/T_0) e^{-m_\perp/T_0} d^2p_\perp\).

## Context

This work extends the idea of Bialas (no year given) that random fluctuations of the string tension could produce a thermal spectrum, and further develops the approach of Florkowski (no year given) using dynamical fluctuations of the string in time. The paper provides a quantum justification for these fluctuations by treating the string as a collective excitation of the gluon sea, analogous to collective coordinate approaches in nuclear many-body physics such as the liquid drop model of nuclear vibrations and rotations described by Greiner (no year given). The result enables a natural explanation of the thermal transverse momentum spectra observed in \(e^+e^-\) and \(p\bar{p}\) collisions without requiring rescattering, and the predicted temperature of approximately 165 MeV is consistent with experimental fits.
