# Constraining slope parameter of symmetry energy from nuclear structure

> Four quantities deducible from nuclear structure experiments have been claimed to correlate to the slope parameter $L$ of the symmetry energy; the neutron skin thickness, the cross section of low-energy dipole (LED) mode, dipole polarizability $α_D$, and $α_D S_0$ (i.e. product of $α_D$ and the symmetry energy $S_0$). By the calculations in the Hartree-Fock plus random-phase approximation with various effective interactions, we compare the correlations between $L$ and these four quantities. The correlation derived from different interactions and the correlation from a class of interactions that are identical in the symmetric matter as well as in $S_0$ are simultaneously examined. These two types of correlations may behave differently, as exemplified in the correlation of $α_D$ to $L$. It is found that the neutron skin thickness and $α_DS_0$ correlate well to $L$, and therefore are suitable for narrowing down the value of $L$ via experiments. The LED emergence and upgrowth makes the $α_DS_0$-$L$ correlation strong, although these correlations are disarranged when neutron halo appears in the ground state.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the neutron skin thickness and the product of dipole polarizability and symmetry energy ($\alpha_D S_0$) correlate well with the slope parameter $L$ of the symmetry energy and are suitable for narrowing down $L$ via experiments, but these correlations are disarranged when a neutron halo appears in the ground state, and the correlation of dipole polarizability alone behaves differently when examined across different interactions versus within a single class of interactions.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock plus random-phase approximation (HF+RPA) calculations in fully self-consistent manner for a number of spherical nuclei, using 13 different effective interactions (Skyrme, Gogny, and M3Y-type) covering a wide range of $L$ values from 18 to 129 MeV. They introduced an additional density-dependent term to each interaction that allows varying $L$ without changing the symmetric nuclear matter equation of state or the symmetry energy $S_0$, enabling them to distinguish between correlations among different interactions (CDI) and correlations within a single class of interactions (CSI). The four quantities (neutron skin thickness, LED cross section, dipole polarizability, and $\alpha_D S_0$) were computed and their correlation coefficients with $L$ were evaluated.

## Key numbers

- Correlation coefficient $R[L,\Delta r_{np}]=0.959$ for $^{132}$Sn, with linear fit $\Delta r_{np} = 0.00114 L + 0.160$ fm and standard deviation 0.014 fm (equivalent to 12 MeV uncertainty in $L$)
- Correlation coefficient $R[L,\alpha_D S_0]=0.953$ for $^{132}$Sn, with linear fit $\alpha_D S_0 = 1.13 L + 170$ e$^2$fm$^2$ and standard deviation 15 e$^2$fm$^2$ (equivalent to 13 MeV uncertainty in $L$)
- Correlation coefficient $R[L,\Delta r_{np}]=0.965$ for $^{208}$Pb, with linear fit $\Delta r_{np} = 0.00107 L + 0.103$ fm and standard deviation 0.013 fm (equivalent to 12 MeV uncertainty in $L$)
- Correlation coefficient $R[L,\alpha_D]=0.90$ for $^{132}$Sn, but CSI slopes (0.031-0.051 e$^2$fm$^2$/MeV$^2$) differ significantly from CDI behavior
- For $^{48}$Ca, $R[L,\Delta r_{np}]=0.785$ with linear fit $\Delta r_{np} = 0.000546 L + 0.138$ fm
- For $^{68}$Ni, $R[L,\Delta r_{np}]=0.901$ with linear fit $\Delta r_{np} = 0.000761 L + 0.133$ fm
- The effective interactions used cover $L = 18-129$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies that separately proposed correlations between $L$ and individual observables: neutron skin thickness in $^{208}$Pb (RocaMaza 2011), LED cross section in $^{68}$Ni and $^{132}$Sn (Carbone 2010), dipole polarizability in $^{208}$Pb (Tamii 2011), and $\alpha_D S_0$ (RocaMaza 2013). The paper directly compares all four quantities in a unified framework, distinguishing between CDI and CSI correlations, which had not been done systematically except in Reinhard (2013). The results enable future experimental campaigns to constrain $L$ by measuring neutron skin thickness in heavy nuclei like $^{208}$Pb or $\alpha_D S_0$ in neutron-rich medium-mass nuclei without neutron halos, though the authors note that the droplet model is not quantitatively reliable for justifying the $\alpha_D S_0$-$L$ correlation.
