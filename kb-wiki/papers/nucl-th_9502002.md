# Numerical Analyses of CERN 200GeV/A Heavy-Ion Collisions Based on a Hydrodynamical Model with Phase Transition

> We numerically analyze recent high energy heavy-ion collision experiments based on a hydrodynamical model with phase transition and discuss a systematic change of initial state of QGP-fluid depending on colliding-nuclei's mass. In a previous paper, we formulated a (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamical model for quark-gluon plasma with phase transition and discussed numerically the space-time evolution in detail. We here compare the numerical solution with the hadronic distributions given by CERN WA80 and NA35. Systematic analyses of the experiments with various colliding nuclei enable us to discuss the dependences of the initial parameters of the hydrodynamical model on colliding nuclei's mass. Furthermore, extrapolating the present experiments, we derive the possible hadronic distributions for lead-lead 150GeV/A collision.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamical model with a phase transition from quark-gluon plasma to hadrons can reproduce the hadronic distributions measured by CERN WA80 and NA35, but only if the equation of state includes a phase transition; a pure hot hadron-gas model (the limit of infinite critical temperature) fails to fit the data because it requires an unphysical inelasticity larger than unity.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the relativistic perfect-fluid hydrodynamical equations for cylindrically symmetric expansion, using an equation of state derived from a simple mode spectrum that mimics a first-order phase transition at a critical temperature of 160 MeV. They initialized the fluid at a formation time of 1 fm with a temperature distribution parameterized by initial temperature, longitudinal spread, and transverse spread, and used Bjorken's scaling solution for the longitudinal flow. They then fitted the three remaining free parameters (initial central temperature, longitudinal size, and longitudinal spread) to reproduce the measured pseudorapidity and transverse-momentum distributions from CERN WA80 and NA35 experiments for sulphur and oxygen beams on various targets, fixing the freeze-out temperature at 140 MeV from the transverse-momentum slope.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for the phase transition: \( T_{\rm C} = 160 \) MeV.
- Freeze-out temperature fixed at \( T_{\rm f} = 140 \) MeV.
- Formation time set to \( \tau_0 = 1 \) fm.
- For the equation of state, the mode spectrum parameters are \( A = 1.89 \), \( M = 200 \) MeV, and \( d = 2 \) MeV.
- Extrapolated prediction for lead-lead collisions at 150 GeV/A: initial central temperature \( T_0 = 250 \) MeV, longitudinal size parameter \( \eta_0 + \sigma_\eta = 0.8 \), and total charged hadronic multiplicity (pions and kaons) of 2095.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamical model with phase transition (Muroya, Nakamura, and Namiki, 1994) by comparing its numerical solutions directly to experimental data from CERN WA80 and NA35. It contradicts the simple Landau-type stopping picture, which would predict lower energy density for heavier targets, whereas the authors find that larger target nuclei produce higher initial temperatures and larger inelasticities. The results enable extrapolation to future lead-lead collisions at CERN and RHIC, providing a phenomenological baseline for planning and interpreting those experiments.
