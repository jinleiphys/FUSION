# Chiral perturbation theory with vector mesons and the pion form factors

> The chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) of pions is extended to include vector mesons as well as pertinent degrees of freedom. By counting the typical momentum scale of vector mesons as order of $Q$ and vector meson masses as of the order of $Λ_χ$, a consistent theory could be obtained. The explicit renormalization procedure of the theory is presented for the form factors of the pion up to one-loop accuracy. The resulting theory prediction for the form factors is in good agreement with the experimental data for a wide range of momentum transfers. The vector-meson dominance mechanism is also discussed in the systematic framework of ChPT.

## Key claim

The paper extends chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) to include vector mesons as explicit degrees of freedom by counting vector meson masses as order $\Lambda_\chi$ and their momenta as order $Q$, achieving a consistent renormalizable theory where the vector-meson dominance (VMD) mechanism emerges naturally at one-loop accuracy for the special choice of parameter $\ar=2$, without requiring fitting to pion form factor data beyond the charge radius and rho decay widths.

## Method

The author constructs a power-counting scheme where the leading-order Lagrangian contains no kinetic term for vector mesons (making their propagator momentum-independent at LO), with the kinetic term appearing only at next-to-leading order (NLO). The one-loop divergences are identified and absorbed by a counter-term Lagrangian, and the renormalization procedure is explicitly carried out for the vertex functions relevant to the pion electromagnetic form factors, including the 1PI functions for $\pi\to\pi$, ${\cal V}\to\pi\pi$, $\rho\to\pi\pi$, and $\rho\to\rho$ transitions.

## Key numbers

- Rho meson mass: $m_\rho = (775.26 \pm 0.23)$ MeV
- Pion decay constant: $f_\pi = (92.1 \pm 0.8)$ MeV
- Experimental $\rho^0 \to \pi^+\pi^-$ decay width: $(147.8 \pm 0.9)$ MeV, corresponding to $g_{\rho\pi\pi} = 5.942 \pm 0.017$
- Experimental $\rho \to e^+e^-$ decay width: $(7.04 \pm 0.06)$ keV, corresponding to $\Gamma_{\rho{\cal V}}(m_\rho^2) = (0.1213 \pm 0.0005)$ GeV$^2$
- Extracted coupling constants for $\ar=2$: $C_{1:3} = (4.3 \pm 1.0)\times 10^{-3}$, $C_{2:1} = (5.5 \pm 0.7)\times 10^{-3}$
- Extracted rho-meson coupling: $\zg \simeq 6.21 \pm 0.06$
- Effective rho mass at low momentum: $\tilde M_\rho \simeq (0.989 \pm 0.001) \, m_\rho$
- Pion charge radius (experimental): $\langle r_\pi^2\rangle = (0.434 \pm 0.005)$ fm$^2$
- The theory reproduces experimental data up to $Q^2 \sim 1$ GeV$^2$

## Context

This work extends the standard ChPT framework of Gasser and Leutwyler (1984, 1985) by promoting vector mesons from integrated-out fields to explicit degrees of freedom, similar in spirit to the inclusion of Delta isobars in baryon ChPT. The approach differs from the hidden local gauge symmetry (HLS) formalism of Bando et al. and the power-counting scheme of Djukanovic et al. (which assigns the rho kinetic term to LO), as the present scheme avoids problematic ultraviolet divergences from $\rho\rho\rho$ vertices that appear in those approaches. The resulting theory enables systematic one-loop calculations of pion form factors with VMD emerging naturally, and can be extended to include $\rho-\omega$ mixing effects for comparison with $e^+e^-\to\pi^+\pi^-$ data from KLOE and SND.
