# $α$ scattering cross sections on $^{12}$C with microscopic coupled-channel calculation

> $α$ elastic and inelastic scattering on $^{12}$C is investigated with the coupled-channel calculation using microscopic $α$-$^{12}$C potentials, which are derived by folding the Melbourne $g$-matrix $NN$ interaction with the matter and transition densities of $^{12}$C. These densities are obtained by a microscopic structure model of the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics combined with and without the $3α$ generator coordinate method. The calculation reproduces satisfactorily well the observed elastic and inelastic cross sections at incident energies of $E_α=130$~MeV, 172.5~MeV, 240~MeV, and 386~MeV with no adjustable parameter. Isoscalar monopole and dipole excitations to the $0^+_2$, $0^+_3$, and $1^-_1$ states in the $α$ scattering are discussed.

## Key claim

The microscopic coupled-channel calculation using the Melbourne $g$-matrix folding model, with no adjustable parameter beyond scaling factors to match experimental electric transition strengths, reproduces the observed elastic and inelastic alpha scattering cross sections on carbon-12 at incident energies of 130, 172.5, 240, and 386 MeV, and does not suffer from the "missing monopole strength" overshooting problem for the 0+2 state that plagued earlier phenomenological approaches.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-channel calculations using microscopic alpha-carbon-12 potentials derived by folding the Melbourne g-matrix effective nucleon-nucleon interaction with matter and transition densities of carbon-12. These densities were obtained from antisymmetrized molecular dynamics calculations, both with and without combination with the 3-alpha generator coordinate method, and were scaled to reproduce experimental electric transition strengths. The coupled-channel calculations included up to 13 states of carbon-12 and were compared with experimental data at four incident energies.

## Key numbers

- Incident energies studied: E_alpha = 130 MeV, 172.5 MeV, 240 MeV, and 386 MeV
- Experimental B(E2;2+1 to 0+1) = 7.59 e^2 fm^4 (error 0.42), used to set scaling factor f_tr = 0.94 for AMD and 0.91 for AMD+GCM
- Experimental B(E0;0+2 to 0+1) = 29.2 (error 0.2), with scaling factor f_tr = 0.82 for all three structure models
- Experimental B(E2;2+2 to 0+1) = 1.57 e^2 fm^4 (error 0.13), requiring large scaling factors f_tr = 1.99 (AMD) and 1.93 (AMD+GCM)
- Experimental B(E3;3-1 to 0+1) = 103 e^2 fm^6 (error 17), with scaling factor f_tr = 1.20 for AMD and AMD+GCM
- RMS proton radius of ground state: experimental 2.33 fm, AMD 2.53 fm, AMD+GCM 2.54 fm, RGM 2.40 fm
- RMS proton radius of 0+2 state: AMD 3.27 fm, AMD+GCM 3.62 fm, RGM 3.47 fm
- Estimated isoscalar dipole transition strength B(IS1;1-1 to 0+1)/4 = 6-9 fm^6

## Context

This work extends the g-matrix folding model approach previously applied by Minomo and Ogata (2016) using RGM transition densities, which successfully reproduced the 0+2 cross sections without overshooting. The present calculation uses AMD and AMD+GCM structure models that include alpha-cluster breaking effects, unlike earlier 3-alpha cluster models such as the resonating group method of Kamimura (1981) that failed to describe low-lying states properly. The approach enables reliable extraction of isoscalar monopole and dipole transition strengths from alpha scattering data, and predicts cross sections for the 1-2 toroidal dipole state that can be tested by future experiments.
