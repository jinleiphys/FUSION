# The nonflow issue in connecting anisotropy measurements to hydrodynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Hydrodynamics can describe majority of the measured azimuthal anisotropies in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Many of the anisotropy measurements are contaminated by nonflow correlations (i.e., those unrelated to global event-wise correlations). Those nonflow contamination can cause incorrectness or compromise the accuracy of the physics extracted from data-hydrodynamics comparison, particularly when one relies on subtle difference in the measurements. In the recent preprint by STAR (arXiv:2401.06625) extracting the Uranium nucleus deformation parameter, nonflow contamination is assessed by subevents in the limited STAR acceptance. In this note, we demonstrate that such assessment is inadequate and illustrate how large an effect nonflow can cause by using the HIJING model, in which all correlations are nonflow and non-hydrodynamic. We thereby conclude that the extracted Uranium deformation parameter is premature and emphasize the importance of an earnest assessment of or correction for nonflow contamination, not only for this STAR analysis but more generally for studies relying on comparing anisotropy measurements to hydrodynamic calculations.

## Key claim

Nonflow correlations (those unrelated to global event-wise correlations) are inadequately assessed in the STAR extraction of the Uranium deformation parameter, and correcting for these non-hydrodynamic contributions using the HIJING model would shift the extracted value beyond the quoted total uncertainty, making the result premature.

## Method

The author uses the HIJING event generator (version 1.41) with jet-quenching switched on to simulate 3.8×10^8 minimum-bias events each for U+U collisions at 193 GeV and Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, keeping both nuclei spherical. The two-particle correlation quantities ⟨V₂⟩, ⟨(δpT)²⟩, and ⟨v₂²δpT⟩ are computed as functions of centrality and compared to STAR data from arXiv:2401.06625, with the subevent method (using pseudorapidity gaps) assessed for its effectiveness in removing nonflow.

## Key numbers

- HIJING nonflow ⟨V₂⟩ in top 0-5% centrality: approximately 0.6×10⁻⁴ in Au+Au and 0.5×10⁻⁴ in U+U collisions
- Subevent method removes only 12% of nonflow in most central collisions (from full-event to subevent)
- HIJING nonflow ⟨V₂⟩ is about 12% of the measured ⟨v₂²⟩ = 5×10⁻⁴ in central Au+Au collisions
- HIJING ⟨(δpT)²⟩ is approximately 20% of the measured value in central Au+Au and 10% in central U+U
- HIJING ⟨v₂²δpT⟩ in top 0-5% central Au+Au is approximately 5×10⁻⁸, about 10% of measured data; in U+U it is approximately 3.5×10⁻⁸, about 6% of the magnitude of the measured negative correlation
- Correcting for HIJING nonflow would increase the U+U/Au+Au ratio R_{v₂²} by 6% in top 0-5% centrality, approximately 6 times the estimated systematic uncertainty from nonflow in the STAR analysis

## Context

This work directly critiques the STAR preprint (arXiv:2401.06625) which extracted the Uranium quadruple deformation parameter β₂ᵤ = 0.297±0.013 by comparing ratios of ⟨v₂²⟩, ⟨(δpT)²⟩, and ⟨v₂²δpT⟩ between U+U and Au+Au collisions to IP-Glasma+MUSIC hydrodynamic calculations. The author argues that nonflow contamination is severely underestimated, extending earlier concerns about nonflow in isobar ratio analyses (STAR 2023) and data-driven nonflow estimates from central Au+Au collisions (Abdelwahab 2014). The work emphasizes that earnest nonflow correction or assessment with faithful systematic uncertainties is needed before drawing physics conclusions from comparisons of anisotropy measurements to hydrodynamic calculations.
