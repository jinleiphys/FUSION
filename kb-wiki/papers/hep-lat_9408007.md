# Nucleon Structure from Lattice QCD

> We report results on the nucleon structure obtained from the lattice quantum chromodynamics calculation. These include the axial, electromagnetic, $πNN$, and scalar form factors. The calculation is carried out at $β= 6$ on a $16^3 \times 24$ lattice with 24 quenched gauge configurations. The chiral limit results are extrapolated from several light quark cases. For the disconnected insertion (sea-quark contribution), we used the stochastic estimation with the $Z_2$ noise to calculate the diagonal and off-diagonal traces of the inverse matrices with a size of $10^6 \times 10^6$. It is found that the $Z_2$ noise is the optimal choice and its comparison with the Gaussian noise for our quark matrix is given. For the sea-quark contribution, we report results on the strange condensate in the nucleon and the $πN σ$ term.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations at moderate lattice size and with the quenched approximation can reproduce experimental nucleon form factors and predict the pion-nucleon-nucleon form factor, but the results are limited by the modest lattice size, heavy quark masses, and the omission of dynamical fermion effects.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations at β = 6.0 on a 16³ × 24 lattice with 24 gauge configurations generated using the Cabibbo-Marinari pseudo-heatbath algorithm. Quark propagators were computed using the conjugate-gradient algorithm with red-black preconditioning, and nucleon two-point and three-point correlation functions were evaluated to extract electromagnetic, axial, pseudoscalar, and scalar form factors. For disconnected insertions (sea-quark contributions), the stochastic estimation method with Z₂ noise was employed to calculate diagonal and off-diagonal traces of the inverse quark matrices of dimension 10⁶ × 10⁶.

## Key numbers

- Axial dipole mass M_A = 1.09 ± 0.05 GeV for the point-split current and M_A = 1.06 ± 0.04 GeV for the local current, compared to the experimental value of 1.09 ± 0.03 ± 0.02 GeV from Brookhaven E734
- Electric dipole mass M_E = 0.857 ± 0.036 GeV, compared to the experimental dipole mass of 0.828 ± 0.006 GeV
- Axial coupling constant g_A = 1.20(11) from the point-split current and 1.18(11) from the local current, compared to the experimental value of 1.254(6)
- Monopole cutoff for the πNN form factor Λ_πNN = 0.75 ± 0.14 GeV, with g_πNN at q² = m_π² equal to 12.7 ± 2.4 (phenomenological value 13.40 ± 0.17)
- Disconnected insertion matrix element ⟨N|ūu + d̄d|N⟩_dis = 5.36 ± 1.04, about twice the connected insertion value of 2.86(6)
- πN σ term = 41.1 ± 5.2 MeV, in agreement with the 45 MeV from πN scattering
- Strange condensate ⟨N|s̄s|N⟩ = 1.72 ± 0.28, contributing approximately 224 ± 36 MeV to the nucleon mass

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice QCD calculations of nucleon structure by Dong, Wilcox, and Liu (1990) and Wilcox, Dong, and Liu (1992) to include disconnected insertions for sea-quark contributions, using the stochastic estimation method with Z₂ noise. The results for the πN σ term agree with a similar calculation by Fukugita et al. (1994) on a 12³ × 20 lattice with volume source. The calculation demonstrates that lattice QCD can provide first-principles predictions for quantities not directly accessible experimentally, such as the πNN form factor, and enables future work with larger lattices, smaller quark masses, and unquenched (dynamical fermion) simulations to test QCD as the fundamental theory of strong interactions.
