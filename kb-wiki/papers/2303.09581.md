# Light-front Wave Functions of Vector Mesons in an Algebraic Model

> Inspired by the recent development of an algebraic model which provides an adequate and unified description of the internal structure of the lowest-lying pseudo-scalar mesons, belonging both to the light quarks sector and to the one of heavy quarks, we perform its first extension to the vector-meson case. The algebraic model describes meson's structure in terms of the spectral density function that appears in a Nakanishi integral representation of the covariant quark-antiquark bound-state amplitude, \emph{i.e.}, the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. We compute the leading-twist light-front wave functions of the $ρ(770)$, $ϕ(1020)$, $J/ψ$ and $Υ(1S)$ mesons through their connection with the parton distribution amplitudes. Among the results we present, the following are of particular interest: (i) transverse light-front wave functions can be obtained algebraically from the corresponding parton distribution amplitudes, whereas that is not the case for longitudinal light-front wave functions, which requires an intermediate step where a spectral density function must be derived from the particular parton distribution amplitude; (ii) the derived spectral density functions show marked differences between light and heavy vector mesons, the latter being narrower as compared to the former; these are also non-positive definite, although the integral over the entire curve is larger than zero as expected; and (iii) the longitudinal and transverse light-front wave functions of vector mesons with light quark content exhibit steep $x$- and $p_\perp^2$-dependence, while those of the $J/ψ$ and $Υ(1S)$ mesons are characterized by narrow distributions in the $x$-range but, comparatively, much more gradual fall-offs with respect to the $p_\perp^2$-range depicted.

## Key claim

The paper extends an algebraic model previously developed for pseudoscalar mesons to vector mesons, computing light-front wave functions for the rho(770), phi(1020), J/psi, and Upsilon(1S) mesons. The authors find that transverse light-front wave functions can be obtained algebraically from parton distribution amplitudes, but longitudinal light-front wave functions require an intermediate step involving a spectral density function derived from the parton distribution amplitude.

## Method

The authors construct an algebraic model for the quark propagator and Bethe-Salpeter amplitude of vector mesons using a spectral density function within a Nakanishi integral representation. They compute the leading-twist light-front wave functions by first obtaining the Mellin moments of the parton distribution amplitudes, then deriving the spectral density functions from known Dyson-Schwinger equation results for the vector meson parton distribution amplitudes. The longitudinal light-front wave functions require a regularization procedure where the parameter nu is shifted to 1+delta with delta=0.01 to handle logarithmic divergences in the transverse momentum integration.

## Key numbers

- The parameter delta used for regularization is set to 0.01, making the new value of nu just 1 percent larger than the value producing the power law part of the ultraviolet asymptotic behavior of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude
- The vector mesons studied are rho(770), phi(1020), J/psi, and Upsilon(1S), with quark content n\bar{n} (n=u or d), s\bar{s}, c\bar{c}, and b\bar{b} respectively
- The parameter nu=1 is identified as the most natural choice, yielding the correct power law of the asymptotic behavior for mesons

## Context

This work extends the algebraic model of Albino (2022) from pseudoscalar mesons to vector mesons, where the pseudoscalar model had successfully described the internal structure of lowest-lying pseudoscalar mesons with light or heavy quark content. The results enable future computation of generalized parton distributions in the DGLAP kinematic region through the overlap representation of light-front wave functions, as well as form factors and parton distribution functions. The approach builds upon prior Dyson-Schwinger equation and Bethe-Salpeter equation calculations of parton distribution amplitudes by Gao (2014) and Ding (2015).
