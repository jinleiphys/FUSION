# First application of a microscopic $K^-NN$ absorption model in calculations of kaonic atoms

> Strong interaction energy shifts and widths in kaonic atoms are calculated for the first time using microscopic $K^- N$ + $K^- NN$ potentials derived from $K^- N$ scattering amplitudes constructed within SU(3) chiral coupled-channels models of meson-baryon interactions. The in-medium modifications of the free-space amplitudes due to the Pauli correlations are taken into account. The $K^-N+K^-NN$ potentials evaluated for 23 nuclear species are confronted with kaonic atoms data. The description of the data significantly improves when the $K^-NN$ absorption is included. To get $χ^2$ as low as for the $K^-N+$phenomenological multi-nucleon potential an additional phenomenological term, accounting for $K^{-}-3N(4N)$ processes, is still needed. However, density dependence of this phenomenological term points out some deficiencies in the microscopic potentials and further improvements of the applied model are thus desirable. The calculated branching ratios for $K^-N$ and $K^-NN$ absorption channels in the $^{12}$C$+K^-$ atom are in reasonable agreement with the old bubble chamber data, as well as with the latest data from the AMADEUS Collaboration.

# First application of a microscopic $K^-NN$ absorption model in calculations of kaonic atoms

## Key claim

The inclusion of a microscopic $K^-NN$ absorption potential derived from chiral SU(3) coupled-channels models significantly improves the description of kaonic atom data compared to using only $K^-N$ potentials, but an additional phenomenological term accounting for $K^-$ absorption on three or more nucleons is still needed to achieve $\chi^2$ values as low as those obtained with purely phenomenological multi-nucleon potentials. The density dependence of this phenomenological term reveals deficiencies in the microscopic potentials, indicating that further model improvements are desirable.

## Method

The authors constructed microscopic $K^-N$ and $K^-NN$ optical potentials from chiral $K^-N$ scattering amplitudes derived within the Barcelona (BCN) and Prague (P) SU(3) chiral coupled-channels models, incorporating in-medium modifications due to Pauli correlations using two approaches: direct Pauli blocking and the multiple scattering method of Wass, Rho, and Weise (WRW). These potentials were used to solve the Klein-Gordon equation for 23 nuclear species from lithium to uranium, calculating strong-interaction energy shifts and widths for 65 data points of kaonic atoms, and were supplemented by a phenomenological term $V_{\rm phen} = -4\pi B (\rho/\rho_0)^\alpha \rho$ to account for missing $K^-$ absorption on three or more nucleons.

## Key numbers

- $\chi^2(65)$ for $K^-N$ potentials based on Pauli-modified BCN amplitudes: 825; for $K^-N+K^-NN$: 565; for $K^-N+K^-NN+$phen.: 105
- $\chi^2(65)$ for $K^-N$ potentials based on WRW-modified BCN amplitudes: 2378; for $K^-N+K^-NN$: 1123; for $K^-N+K^-NN+$phen.: 116
- Best-fit complex amplitude $B$ for additional phenomenological term with Pauli BCN amplitudes: Re$B$ = -1.97(13) fm, Im$B$ = -0.93(11) fm, $\alpha = 1.4$
- Best-fit complex amplitude $B$ for additional phenomenological term with WRW BCN amplitudes: Re$B$ = -0.90(9) fm, Im$B$ = 0.72(10) fm, $\alpha = 0.6$
- Total $K^-$ single-nucleon absorption ratio for $^{12}$C$+K^-$ (l=1 state) with Pauli BCN amplitudes: 77.4%; total two-nucleon absorption ratio: 22.6%
- Total $K^-$ single-nucleon absorption ratio for $^{12}$C$+K^-$ (l=1 state) with WRW BCN amplitudes: 79.0%; total two-nucleon absorption ratio: 21.0%
- Experimental total two-nucleon absorption ratio for $^{12}$C: 25.7 $\pm$ 3.1% (bubble chamber) and $16 \pm 3(\text{stat.})^{+4}_{-5}(\text{syst.})$ (AMADEUS Collaboration)

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological analyses of Friedman and Gal (2017), who showed that $K^-$ optical potentials based on chiral SU(3) models fail to reproduce kaonic atom data unless a phenomenological multi-nucleon term is added, and that only the Kyoto-Munich, Prague, and Barcelona models were acceptable when constrained by $K^-$ single-nucleon absorption fractions. The microscopic $K^-NN$ absorption model is inspired by the work of Hrtankova and Ramos (2020), who developed a meson-exchange formalism for $K^-NN$ absorption in symmetric nuclear matter, and by Sekihara et al. (2012), who developed a chiral unitary approach to $\bar{K}N$ interactions. The present results enable more reliable extrapolation of $K^-$ optical potentials to higher densities, which is crucial for calculations of $K^-$ nuclear quasi-bound states, and suggest that new measurements of kaonic sulfur are needed to resolve discrepancies in the data.
