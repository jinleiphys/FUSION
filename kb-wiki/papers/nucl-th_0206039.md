# Induced pseudoscalar coupling of the proton weak interaction

> The induced pseudoscalar coupling $g_p$ is the least well known of the weak coupling constants of the proton's charged--current interaction. Its size is dictated by chiral symmetry arguments, and its measurement represents an important test of quantum chromodynamics at low energies. During the past decade a large body of new data relevant to the coupling $g_p$ has been accumulated. This data includes measurements of radiative and non radiative muon capture on targets ranging from hydrogen and few--nucleon systems to complex nuclei. Herein the authors review the theoretical underpinnings of $g_p$, the experimental studies of $g_p$, and the procedures and uncertainties in extracting the coupling from data. Current puzzles are highlighted and future opportunities are discussed.

# Induced pseudoscalar coupling of the proton weak interaction

## Key claim

The induced pseudoscalar coupling $g_p$ remains the least well determined of the proton's weak couplings, and while most experimental data are consistent with the PCAC prediction within their uncertainties, the results from muon capture on hydrogen, which should be the simplest and cleanest process, are puzzling and inconsistent with PCAC, particularly the TRIUMF radiative muon capture result.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical foundations of $g_p$ from PCAC and chiral perturbation theory, then survey experimental determinations from ordinary muon capture (OMC) and radiative muon capture (RMC) on hydrogen, deuterium, $^3$He, and complex nuclei. For hydrogen, they update the theoretical calculations to include current best values of other weak couplings and their $q^2$ dependences, and re-analyze the Saclay OMC experiment and TRIUMF RMC experiment using the world-average positive muon lifetime and standard muon chemistry parameters.

## Key numbers

- PCAC prediction for $g_p$ at the muon capture momentum transfer $q^2 = -0.88 m_\mu^2$: $g_p^{PCAC} = 8.13$ (including constant term from chiral perturbation theory)
- From TRIUMF RMC on hydrogen: $g_p = 12.2 \pm 1.1$ (using $\Lambda_{op} = 4.1 \times 10^4$ s$^{-1}$)
- From Saclay OMC on hydrogen: $g_p = 10.6 \pm 2.7$
- World average of all OMC experiments on hydrogen: $g_p = 10.5 \pm 1.8$
- From $^3$He OMC (Ackerbauer et al. 1998): $g_p = 8.53 \pm 1.54$, the best individual determination of $g_p$
- Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy $\Delta_{GT}$: now 2% or less using $g_{\pi NN}(m_\pi^2) = 13.05 \pm 0.08$, updated $g_a$ and $F_\pi$
- Pion decay constant: $F_\pi = 92.4 \pm 0.3$ MeV
- Axial radius of the nucleon: $r_A^2 = 0.42 \pm 0.04$ fm$^2$

## Context

This review updates the classic review of Mukhopadhyay (1977) on the induced pseudoscalar coupling, incorporating two decades of new experimental data from muon capture on hydrogen, few-nucleon systems, and complex nuclei. The work extends the theoretical framework from PCAC to modern chiral perturbation theory calculations by Bernard et al. (1994), Fearing et al. (1997), and others. The unresolved discrepancy between hydrogen capture data and the PCAC prediction motivates ongoing experiments at TRIUMF and PSI, and points toward the need for future facilities such as an intense muon source at a neutrino factory to achieve 2-3% precision on $g_p$.
