# Intertwined quantum phase transitions in the zirconium and niobium isotopes

> Nuclei in the $A\approx100$ region exhibit intricate shape-evolution and configuration crossing signatures. Exploring both even-even and their adjacent odd-mass nuclei gives further insight on the emergence of deformation and shape-phase transitions. We employ the algebraic frameworks of the interacting boson model with configuration mixing and the new interacting boson-fermion model with configuration mixing in order to investigate the even-even zirconium with neutron number 52-70 ($^{40}$Zr) and odd-mass niobium ($_{41}$Nb) isotopes with 52-62. We compare between the evolution in energy levels, configuration and symmetry content of the wave functions, two neutron separation energies and $E2$ transition rates. The comparisons between the two chains of isotopes denote the occurrence of intertwined quantum phase transitions (IQPTs) in both chains. Such a situation occurs when two configurations, normal and intruder, cross through the critical point of a Type II quantum phase transition (QPT), and the intruder configuration undergoes on its own a Type I shape-evolution QPT from a spherical shape (weak coupling scenario) to axially deformed rotor (strong coupling scenario) in the Zr (Nb) isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper claims that both the even-even zirconium (Zr) and odd-mass niobium (Nb) isotope chains exhibit intertwined quantum phase transitions (IQPTs), where a Type II QPT (abrupt crossing of two configurations) occurs simultaneously with a Type I QPT (gradual shape evolution within the intruder configuration), with the critical point near neutron number 60 (A≈100).

## Method

The author employed the interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM) for even-even Zr isotopes (neutron numbers 52–70) and the interacting boson-fermion model with configuration mixing (IBFM-CM) for odd-mass Nb isotopes (neutron numbers 52–62). The normal configuration (A) corresponds to no active protons above Z=40, while the intruder configuration (B) corresponds to 2p-2h excitations across the subshell gap. For Nb, a single proton in the 1g9/2 orbit was coupled to the respective Zr boson cores. The analysis examined energy levels, two-neutron separation energies, E2 transition rates, and the configuration and U(5) symmetry content of wave functions.

## Key numbers

- In Zr isotopes, the ratio R4/2 for configuration A at neutron numbers 52–56 is R4/2(A) ≈ 1.6, while for configuration B it is R4/2(B) ≈ 2.3.
- In 104Zr, the intruder B configuration shows E(2_1^+) = 139.3 keV and R4/2(B) = 3.24, indicating strong axial deformation.
- In 106Zr, experimental energies are E(2_2^+) = 607.0 keV and E(4_1^+) = 476.5 keV; in 110Zr, E(4_1^+) = 565 keV and E(2_2^+) = 485 keV, signatures of SO(6) symmetry.
- The intruder component of the ground state wave function jumps from small values at neutron number 58 to about 87% in Zr and 99% in Nb at neutron number 60.
- The n_d=0 component of the intruder configuration drops from ≈90% at neutron numbers 52–58 to ≈30% at neutron number 60 in Zr, indicating the onset of deformation.
- For two-neutron separation energies, the deformed part S2n(def) for the intruder B configuration jumps at neutron number 58 and flattens at 60, with Δ_n = 2 MeV for neutron numbers 58–70 (Zr) and 58–62 (Nb).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shape coexistence and quantum phase transitions in the A≈100 region, specifically building on the IBM-CM calculations for Zr isotopes by Gavrielov (2019, 2020, 2022) and the IBFM-CM framework for Nb isotopes by Gavrielov (2022c, 2023a). The concept of IQPTs was previously identified in even-even Zr isotopes, and this paper demonstrates that the same phenomenon occurs in the adjacent odd-mass Nb chain, showing that odd-mass nuclei can serve as test cases for shape coexistence in even-even cores. The results enable further systematic studies of configuration mixing and shape evolution across isotopic chains in this mass region.
