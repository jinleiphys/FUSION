# Causality Bounds on Dissipative General-Relativistic Magnetohydrodynamics

> We derive necessary and sufficient conditions under which a large class of relativistic generalizations of Braginskii's magnetohydrodynamics with shear, bulk, and heat diffusion effects is causal and strongly hyperbolic in the fully nonlinear regime in curved spacetime. We find that causality severely constrains the size of nonideal effects and the onset of kinetic instabilities. Our results are crucial for assessing the regime of validity of fluid dynamical simulations of plasmas near supermassive black holes.

## Key claim

The paper derives necessary and sufficient conditions for causality and strong hyperbolicity in a large class of relativistic generalizations of Braginskii's magnetohydrodynamics, but the abstract glosses over the fact that these conditions are fully nonlinear and apply only when the dissipative fluxes (shear stress, heat flux, bulk viscosity) are aligned with the magnetic field direction.

## Method

The authors formulate a general-relativistic extension of Braginskii's magnetohydrodynamics using Israel-Stewart theory, where the shear-stress tensor, heat flux, and bulk viscosity are aligned with the comoving magnetic field. They cast the equations of motion into a quasilinear first-order system and analyze the characteristic determinant to derive algebraic inequalities that ensure causality and strong hyperbolicity in curved spacetime without linearizing about equilibrium. The analysis is performed for the case with only shear viscosity, and then generalized to include all dissipative fluxes.

## Key numbers

* The Alfven characteristic velocity constraint requires \(0 < (\Delta P + b^2)/E < 1\), where \(E = e + P + b^2 + \Delta P/3\), for causality in the nonlinear regime.
* The firehose instability threshold \(\Delta P < -b^2\) is forbidden by the nonlinear causality bound in Eq. (H1) when heat diffusion is absent.
* For the general case including heat diffusion, the necessary causality constraints are \(0 \leq q^2 + E(\Delta P + b^2)\) and \(0 \leq |q|[|q| + \sqrt{q^2 + E(\Delta P + b^2)}] \leq (1/2)E(E - \Delta P - b^2)\).
* The numerical example uses a polytropic equation of state \(P = K\rho^\Gamma\) with \(\Gamma = 5/3\) and \(K = 0.0043\), following parameters from Foucart (2017).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic generalization of Braginskii's magnetohydrodynamics formulated by Chandra (2015) in the Israel-Stewart framework, which had previously only been analyzed using linearized causality and stability conditions. The nonlinear constraints derived here can be applied to numerical simulations of accretion flows onto supermassive black holes such as those performed by Foucart (2017) using the extended magnetohydrodynamic model. The results enable systematic checks of whether fluid simulations of weakly collisional plasmas near black holes remain within the regime where causality is respected, and they show that the firehose instability cannot occur in causal shear-viscous models without heat diffusion, a result inaccessible to linear analysis.
