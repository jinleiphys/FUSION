# Direct Instantons and Nucleon Magnetic Moments

> We calculate the leading direct-instanton contributions to the operator product expansion of the nucleon correlator in a magnetic background field and set up improved QCD sum rules for the nucleon magnetic moments. Remarkably, the instanton contributions are found to affect only those sum rules which had previously been considered unstable. The new sum rules show good stability and reproduce the experimental values of the nucleon magnetic moments with values of $χ$, the quark condensate magnetic susceptibility, consistent with other estimates.

# Direct Instantons and Nucleon Magnetic Moments

## Key claim

The leading direct-instanton contributions to the operator product expansion of the nucleon correlator in a magnetic background field affect almost exclusively the chirally-odd amplitude Π₃, which had previously been considered unstable in QCD sum rules for nucleon magnetic moments; when these instanton contributions are included, the Π₃ sum rule shows good stability and reproduces experimental values with a quark condensate magnetic susceptibility χ consistent with other estimates.

## Method

The authors calculated the leading direct-instanton contributions to the nucleon correlator in a constant electromagnetic background field by evaluating the correlator in the background of instanton and anti-instanton fields in semiclassical approximation, treating the zero-mode sector of the quark propagator exactly and approximating continuum modes by plane waves. They then incorporated these contributions into improved QCD sum rules for the nucleon magnetic moments using the background-field sum-rule techniques of Ioffe and Smilga (1984) and Balitsky and Yung (1983), fitting the sum rules by minimizing relative deviations between the OPE side and the phenomenological side while keeping the magnetic moments fixed at their experimental values.

## Key numbers

- Average instanton size: \(\bar{\rho} \simeq \frac{1}{3}\) fm (approximately 0.33 fm)
- Average separation between neighboring (anti)instantons: \(\bar{R} \simeq 1\) fm
- Mixed quark condensate parameter: \(m_0^2 = 0.8\) GeV²
- Fiducial Borel mass domain for both proton and neutron sum rules: \(0.8\) GeV \(\lesssim M \lesssim 1.15\) GeV
- Predicted quark condensate magnetic susceptibility from proton sum rule: \(\chi \simeq -4.96\) GeV⁻² at OPE scale \(\mu = 0.5\) GeV
- Predicted quark condensate magnetic susceptibility from neutron sum rule: \(\chi \simeq -4.73\) GeV⁻² at OPE scale \(\mu = 0.5\) GeV
- Excited-state transition parameter for proton: \(A_p \simeq 0.28\) GeV²
- Excited-state transition parameter for neutron: \(A_n \simeq -0.27\) GeV⁻²
- Coupling constant: \(\tilde{\lambda}^2 = 2.93\) GeV⁶
- Continuum threshold: \(W = 1.66\) GeV
- Susceptibility \(\kappa = -0.34 \pm 0.1\) (from Kogan and Wyler, 1992)
- Susceptibility \(\xi = -0.74 \pm 0.2\) (from Kogan and Wyler, 1992)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Forkel and Banerjee (1993) and Forkel and Nielsen (1995, 1997) for incorporating direct-instanton contributions into QCD sum rules, applying it for the first time to background-field sum rules for nucleon magnetic moments. The paper resolves a long-standing puzzle from Ioffe and Smilga (1984), where the Π₃ sum rule failed to show stability while the Π₁ and Π₂ sum rules worked satisfactorily; the authors demonstrate that the previously neglected instanton contributions selectively stabilize the unstable sum rule, consistent with a pattern observed in nucleon mass and isospin splitting sum rules. This enables a third reliable sum rule for nucleon magnetic moments and strengthens the consistency of the background-field sum rule approach, while also supporting the adequacy of semiclassical instanton implementations into the operator product expansion.
