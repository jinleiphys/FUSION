# Chiral NN interaction in nuclear matter

> The effective field theory of NN interactions in nuclear matter is considered. Due to the Pauli principle the effective NN amplitude is not affected by the shallow bound states. We show that the next-to-leading order terms in the chiral expansion of the effective NN potential can be interpreted as corrections so the expansion is systematic. The value of potential energy per particle is calculated and some issues concerning the chiral effective theory of nuclear matter are outlined.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in nuclear matter, the Pauli principle and mean-field effects eliminate the shallow bound states that cause large scattering lengths in vacuum, making the chiral expansion of the effective NN potential systematic. However, the author notes that nonperturbative treatment remains useful for densities lower than normal nuclear matter density and for processes involving both nonzero density and temperature.

## Method

The author constructs a chiral effective Lagrangian for NN interactions in the \(^{1}S_0\) state and solves the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the G-matrix in nuclear matter, using a separable potential with parameters adjusted to reproduce the empirical potential energy per particle. The low-energy constants \(C_0\) and \(C_2\) are determined by equating the EFT and phenomenological G-matrices at fixed kinematical points, with renormalization performed by subtracting divergent integrals at a subtraction point \(\mu = 0\). The potential energy per particle is then calculated from the in-medium chiral NN scattering amplitude in both leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO).

## Key numbers

- Vacuum scattering length: \(a = -23.71 \pm 0.013\) fm; effective range: \(r_e = 2.73 \pm 0.03\) fm.
- Separable potential parameters for vacuum fit: \(\lambda = 1.95\), \(\beta = 0.8\) fm.
- Separable potential parameters for nuclear matter fit: \(\lambda = 2.4\), \(\beta = 1.1\) fm.
- Fermi momentum assumed: \(p_F = 1.37\) fm.
- In LO: \(C_0 = -1.86\) fm\(^2\); in NLO: \(C_0 = 2.64\) fm\(^2\) and \(C_2 = 0.84\) fm\(^4\) (approximately 40% change in \(C_0\)).
- Potential energy per particle in the \(^{1}S_0\) channel: LO \(\simeq -17\) MeV, NLO \(\simeq -13.1\) MeV.
- Potential energy per particle in the \(^{3}S_1\) channel: LO \(\simeq -17.3\) MeV, NLO \(\simeq -13.2\) MeV.
- Estimated expansion parameter: \(\langle p \rangle / \Lambda \sim \langle m_\pi \rangle / \Lambda \sim 0.3\)–\(0.4\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to NN interactions, previously developed for vacuum by Weinberg (1991) and others, into the nuclear matter domain. It builds on earlier studies of chiral Lagrangians in nuclear matter by Serot and collaborators (1997) and Lutz (1998), and follows the strategy of using the Bethe-Goldstone equation, analogous to the work of Bethe and Goldstone (1957) with phenomenological potentials. The results enable a systematic chiral EFT description of nuclear matter, though the author notes that pionic effects, many-body forces, and consistent chiral counting rules remain to be incorporated.
