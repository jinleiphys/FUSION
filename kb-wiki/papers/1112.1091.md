# Bose-Einstein Interference in the Passage of a Jet in a Dense Medium

> When a jet collides coherently with many parton scatterers at very high energies, the Bose-Einstein symmetry with respect to the interchange of the virtual bosons leads to a destructive interference of the Feynman amplitudes in most regions of the momentum transfer phase space but a constructive interference in some other regions of the momentum transfer phase space. As a consequence, the recoiling scatterers have a tendency to come out collectively along the incident jet direction, each carrying a substantial fraction of the incident jet longitudinal momentum. The manifestation of the Bose-Einstein interference as collective recoils of the scatterers along the jet direction may have been observed in angular correlations of hadrons associated with a high-pT trigger in high-energy heavy-nuclei collisions at RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

When a jet collides coherently with many parton scatterers at very high energies, the Bose-Einstein symmetry with respect to the interchange of virtual bosons leads to destructive interference in most regions of momentum transfer phase space but constructive interference in other regions, causing recoiling scatterers to emerge collectively along the incident jet direction. The paper proposes that this effect may have been observed in angular correlations of hadrons associated with high-pT triggers at RHIC and LHC, but the claim is hedged by noting that the analysis applies to "mini-jets" with initial momentum of order 10 GeV rather than fully developed jets.

## Method

The author develops a Feynman amplitude approach for coherent (1+n)-body collisions of a fast fermion with n fermion scatterers in Abelian gauge theory, then generalizes to non-Abelian gauge theory and gluon scatterers. The analysis compares the longitudinal coherence length Δz_coh ~ 2ħp0/qT^2 with the mean free path λ to determine when multiple collisions become coherent rather than a sequence of incoherent two-body collisions. The total Feynman amplitude is constructed as the symmetrized sum of n! permutations of exchanged bosons, and the cross section for the (1+n)-body collision is derived.

## Key numbers

- Longitudinal coherence length Δz_coh is of order 25 fm for a jet of momentum p0 ~ 10 GeV/c with typical transverse momentum transfer qT ~ 0.4 GeV/c
- The condition for coherent collisions is Δz_coh ~ 2ħp0/qT^2 ≫ R ≫ λ, where R is the nuclear radius
- The near-side jet collides with about 4-6 medium partons in central AuAu collisions at RHIC and LHC
- The recoiling scatterers each acquire approximately 1/(2n) fraction of the longitudinal momentum of the incident jet
- The displaced parton momentum distribution shows a peak at pT ~ 1 GeV/c for kicked medium partons
- The kicked medium partons possess a correlation of |p1T| ~ |p2T| ~ 1 GeV for both near side and away side

## Context

This work extends the momentum kick model (Wong, 2007-2012) which successfully described ridge phenomena in angular correlations observed by STAR, PHENIX, PHOBOS, CMS, ATLAS, and ALICE collaborations at RHIC and LHC. The paper provides theoretical foundations for the longitudinal momentum kick assumption in that model, which could not be explained by incoherent two-body collisions. The analysis contrasts with the Glauber-type potential model approach (Glauber, 1959; Gyulassy et al., 1994-2004) which treats scatterers as static potential centers and cannot accommodate independent longitudinal recoil degrees of freedom. The work enables a general treatment of coherent collisions where scatterers can acquire substantial fractions of the incident jet longitudinal momentum.
