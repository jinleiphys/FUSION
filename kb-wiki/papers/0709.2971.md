# New method for extracting quasi-bound states from the continuum

> A new parameter-free method is proposed for treatment of single-particle resonances in the real-energy continuum shell model. This method yields quasi-bound states embedded in the continuum which provide a natural generalization of weakly bound single-particle states.

# New method for extracting quasi-bound states from the continuum

## Key claim

The paper presents a parameter-free method for extracting quasi-bound states (called "anamneses" of resonances) from the single-particle scattering continuum, which replaces the earlier Wang and Shakin (1970) method that depended on arbitrary cutting parameters and produced states without correct bound-state asymptotics. The new method yields states that are square-integrable and provide a natural continuation of weakly bound single-particle states into the positive-energy continuum.

## Method

The method selects the scattering state at a real energy equal to the real part of the complex resonance energy, then matches this regular solution in the inner region to a Jost solution with an imaginary wave number (bound-state asymptotic) in the outer region. The matching radius is uniquely determined by requiring continuity of the wave function and its first derivative, which yields a condition that fixes the matching point without free parameters. The extracted resonance anamnesis is then orthogonalized to other bound states, and a modified Hamiltonian is constructed to generate a non-resonant scattering continuum from which the resonant phase shift has been removed.

## Key numbers

- For the $0d_{5/2}$ proton resonance in a Woods-Saxon potential (radius 3.5 fm, diffuseness 0.5 fm, spin-orbit strength 3.5 MeV, target mass 16 amu, charge Z=8, central depth 40 MeV): the resonance pole is at $Re(k^{res}) = 0.265$ fm$^{-1}$, $Im(k^{res}) = -0.0014$ fm$^{-1}$, corresponding to energy $e^{res} = 1.55$ MeV and width $\Gamma^{res} = 32.692$ keV; the matching radius is $r_m = 18$ fm.
- For $1p_{1/2}$ neutron resonances at three energies: $e^{res} = 0.036$ MeV, 0.112 MeV, and 0.47 MeV, with widths varying from approximately 10 keV to approximately 560 keV.
- For $1s_{1/2}$ proton resonances at three energies: $e^{res} = 0.53$ MeV, 1.11 MeV, and 1.29 MeV, with widths varying from approximately 20 keV to approximately 600 keV.
- For the RMS radius study of $1p_{1/2}$ neutron orbits, the central depth $V_0$ varies from 69.012 MeV to 61.44 MeV; for $1s_{1/2}$ proton orbits, $V_0$ varies from 50.233 MeV to 39.06 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the continuum shell model (CSM) and shell model embedded in the continuum (SMEC) formalisms developed by Mahaux (1969), Barz (1977), Philpott (1977), and more recently by Bennaceur (1999, 2000), Okołowicz (2003), and Rotureau (2005). The new method replaces the Wang and Shakin (1970) regularization procedure, which introduced arbitrary parameters (cutting radius and cutting function shape) that affected calculated partial decay widths. The resulting resonance anamneses enable a complete single-particle basis in Hilbert space for CSM/SMEC studies and may allow direct calculation of multi-particle resonant states and extraction of resonance contributions from many-body observables in weakly-bound or unbound nuclear systems.
