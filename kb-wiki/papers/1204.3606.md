# Hyperon-Nucleon Interactions and the Composition of Dense Nuclear Matter from Quantum Chromodynamics

> The low-energy neutron-Sigma^- interactions determine, in part, the role of the strange quark in dense matter, such as that found in astrophysical environments. The scattering phase shifts for this system are obtained from a numerical evaluation of the QCD path integral using the technique of Lattice QCD. Our calculations, performed at a pion mass of m_pi ~ 389 MeV in two large lattice volumes, and at one lattice spacing, are extrapolated to the physical pion mass using effective field theory. The interactions determined from QCD are consistent with those extracted from hyperon-nucleon experimental data within uncertainties, and strengthen theoretical arguments that the strange quark is a crucial component of dense nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first Lattice QCD predictions for hyperon-nucleon scattering phase shifts, specifically for the $n\Sigma^-$ system, and uses these to estimate that the $\Sigma^-$ hyperon is likely present in dense neutron star matter. The key hedge is that the calculation is performed at a single lattice spacing and at an unphysical pion mass of $m_\pi \sim 389$ MeV, requiring extrapolation to the physical pion mass using effective field theory, and that the many-body estimate of the $\Sigma^-$ energy shift uses Fumi's theorem for a non-interacting Fermi gas, neglecting neutron-neutron correlations.

## Method

The NPLQCD Collaboration performed Lattice QCD calculations on two ensembles of $n_f=2+1$ anisotropic clover gauge-field configurations at a pion mass of $m_\pi \sim 389$ MeV, with spatial extents of $L \sim 3.0$ fm and $3.9$ fm. Using Luscher's method, they extracted the $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1$ $n\Sigma^-$ scattering phase shifts from the finite-volume energy shifts of the two-particle systems. The LQCD results were then used to determine the leading-order contact operator coefficients in a chiral effective field theory, which allowed extrapolation of the phase shifts to the physical pion mass and calculation of the $\Sigma^-$ energy shift in dense neutron matter via Fumi's theorem.

## Key numbers

- The $^1S_0$ $n\Sigma^-$ channel at $m_\pi \sim 389$ MeV has a bound state with binding energy $B = 25 \pm 9.3 \pm 11$ MeV.
- At a neutron density of $\rho_n \sim 0.4$ fm$^{-3}$, the energy shift of a single $\Sigma^-$ in a non-interacting Fermi gas of neutrons is $\Delta E = 46 \pm 13 \pm 24$ MeV.
- The calculations were performed at a spatial lattice spacing of $b_s \sim 0.123(1)$ fm and an anisotropy of $\xi \sim 3.5$.
- The $^1S_0$ channel becomes unbound at $m_\pi \lesssim 300$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous Lattice QCD calculations of hyperon-nucleon interactions by the NPLQCD Collaboration (Beane 2006, Beane 2009) and the HALQCD Collaboration (Nemura 2008, Inoue 2010, Inoue 2011) by providing the first LQCD predictions for $n\Sigma^-$ phase shifts at the physical pion mass. The results are consistent with experimental data and phenomenological models such as the Nijmegen NSC97f model (nij99) and the Julich '04 model (HM05), and strengthen theoretical arguments that strange quarks are a crucial component of dense nuclear matter. The work enables future many-body calculations using the LQCD-determined interactions to obtain a more precise determination of the $\Sigma^-$ energy shift in neutron star interiors, which is needed to address questions raised by the observation of a $1.9 M_\odot$ neutron star (Demorest 2010).
