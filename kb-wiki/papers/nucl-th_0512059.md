# Resonances and final state interactions in the reaction pp->pK^+Lambda

> A study of the strangeness production reaction pp->pK^+Lambda for excess energies of epsilon \le 150 MeV, accessible at high-luminosity accelerator facilities like COSY, is presented. Methods to analyze the Dalitz plot distribution and angular spectra in the Jackson and helicity frames are worked out and suitable observables for extracting information on low lying resonances that couple to the K-Lambda system and for determining the Lambda-p effective-range parameters from the final state interaction are identified and discussed. Furthermore, the chances for identifying the reaction mechanism of strangeness production are investigated.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that, for the reaction pp→pK⁺Λ at excess energies up to 150 MeV, it is possible to discriminate between pion-exchange and kaon-exchange production mechanisms and to extract the Λp effective-range parameters from final-state interactions, but only if the analysis is restricted to unpolarized experiments and the two mechanisms are treated as representatives of broader classes of reaction scenarios rather than as concrete processes.

## Method
The authors construct reaction amplitudes for pion-exchange and kaon-exchange mechanisms separately, using the Jülich meson-exchange model for the KN→KN amplitude and the partial-wave analysis of Sotona and Zofka for the πN→KΛ amplitude (including s-channel resonances and t-channel K* exchange). They compute total cross sections, Dalitz plot distributions, and angular spectra in the Jackson and helicity frames for the pp→pK⁺Λ reaction, incorporating the Λp final-state interaction via a factorized amplitude. The cut-off masses for the πNN and ΛNK vertex form factors are treated as free parameters adjusted to data.

## Key numbers
- Excess energy range considered: ε ≤ 150 MeV, accessible at COSY (proton beam energy T ≤ 2.88 GeV).
- g_πNN = 13.45; g_ΛNK = –13.98 (from SU(3) symmetry with α = 2/5).
- For K* exchange in πN→KΛ, adjusted coupling constants: g₀ = –24.0 and g₁ = –83.3.
- Λp scattering lengths expected to be in the order of 1 to 2 fm.
- The KN amplitude is needed up to √s₂ ≤ 1.9 GeV for COSY energies; the πN→KΛ amplitude is needed up to √s₂ ≤ 2.05 GeV.
- The πN→KΛ reaction shows nonzero Λ polarization already at √s₂ = 1633 MeV (excess energy ε = 19.67 MeV).

## Context
This work extends earlier theoretical studies of strangeness production in NN collisions (e.g., Sibirtsev et al., Tsushima et al., Faldt, Shyam et al.) that suggested resonance excitation in the KΛ channel may dominate near threshold. It provides a framework for analyzing data from the COSY facility (e.g., Balewski et al., Sewerin, Moskal, Wolke, Kowina et al.) and aims to improve the determination of Λp effective-range parameters, which are poorly known from existing scattering data (Holzenkamp, Par, Rijken). The methods developed here can be applied to any meson production in NN collisions (π, η, ω, etc.) and enable future extraction of parameters for the S₁₁(1650) and P₁₁(1710) resonances from strangeness production reactions.
