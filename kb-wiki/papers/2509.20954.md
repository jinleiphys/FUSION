# Probing Initial State Clustering through Photon Anisotropic Flow in 7A TeV $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O Collisions at the LHC

> The presence of $α$ clustered structures in light nuclei can enhance the initial spatial anisotropies in relativistic nuclear collisions relative to those arising from nuclei with uniform density distributions. Thus, observables that are strongly sensitive to the initial geometry can be a more efficient probe of the clustered structures than observables dominated by final state dynamics. We investigate the collisions of $α$ clustered oxygen nuclei at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7$A TeV at the LHC using the GLISSANDO initial state model along with the MUSIC event-by-event hydrodynamical framework. The tetrahedral $α$ clustered structure of $^{16}$O leads to significantly larger initial triangular eccentricity $ε_3$ than collisions with uniform density distributions especially in the most central events. The spatial eccentricity $ε_2$ is found to be relatively less sensitive to the initial state clustered structure. The production of thermal photons is estimated to be only marginally influenced by clustering for both central as well as peripheral collisions. In contrast, the photon triangular flow coefficient $v_3(p_T)$ is strongly affected by initial state clustering resulting in substantially larger values in both central and peripheral collisions. An experimental determination of photon anisotropic flow together with the ratios of flow coefficients in $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O collisions therefore expected to provide valuable insight into the possible clustered structure in light nuclei and also to constrain parameters in theoretical modeling.

# Probing Initial State Clustering through Photon Anisotropic Flow in 7A TeV $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O Collisions at the LHC

## Key claim

The tetrahedral alpha clustered structure of $^{16}$O leads to significantly larger initial triangular eccentricity $\epsilon_3$ and consequently larger photon triangular flow $v_3(p_T)$ than collisions with uniform density distributions, especially in the most central events; however, the photon spectra and elliptic flow are only marginally or oppositely affected, making the ratio $v_2/v_3$ a qualitatively distinct experimental signature of clustering.

## Method

The authors used the GLISSANDO Monte Carlo initial state model to generate spatial distributions of participant nucleons for both tetrahedral alpha clustered and uniform (unclustered) $^{16}$O nuclei at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7$A TeV. The initial entropy density was evolved using the (2+1)-dimensional ideal relativistic hydrodynamic framework MUSIC with a lattice-based equation of state, and thermal photon production was estimated by integrating NLO QGP rates and parametrized hadronic rates over the space-time history. Prompt photon contributions were calculated using the JETPHOX framework with CT14 parton distribution functions and EPS09 nuclear shadowing.

## Key numbers

- Tetrahedral cluster side length $l = 3.2$ fm and cluster radius $r_\alpha = 1.1$ fm for the clustered $^{16}$O configuration.
- Root mean square radius of the unclustered oxygen nucleus set to approximately 2.4 fm, matching the clustered case.
- Initial formation time $\tau_0 = 0.14$ fm/$c$ and freeze-out temperature of 137 MeV used for both clustered and unclustered collisions.
- Impact parameter ranges: 0--1.37 fm for 0--5% centrality and 2.6--3.0 fm for 30--40% centrality.
- Number of binary collisions for prompt photon scaling: approximately 53 for 0--5% centrality and 24 for 30--40% centrality.
- Charged particle multiplicity $dn_{\rm ch}/d\eta$ for most central O+O collisions set to approximately 180.
- Smearing radius $\sigma = 0.4$ fm and mixing parameter $\alpha = 0.12$ in the two-component Glauber model.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of alpha clustered $^{12}$C+Au collisions at RHIC energies (Chatterjee and collaborators, 2021) to the case of symmetric $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O collisions at LHC energies, addressing whether collisions between two light clustered nuclei can reveal clear signatures of clustering without requiring a heavy target. The results build on earlier findings that triangular flow of thermal photons is strongly enhanced by clustered structures in $^{12}$C+Au collisions, while elliptic flow is less sensitive. The paper enables experimental verification using the recent LHC $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O run data from ALICE, CMS, and ATLAS, and suggests that the ratio $v_2/v_3$ of photon anisotropic flow coefficients provides a qualitatively distinct observable for distinguishing clustered from uniform nuclear configurations.
