# Dynamical Schwinger effect and high-intensity lasers. Realising nonperturbative QED

> We consider the possibility of experimental verification of vacuum e^+e^- pair creation at the focus of two counter-propagating optical laser beams with intensities 10^{20}-10^{22} W/cm^2, achievable with present-day petawatt lasers, and approaching the Schwinger limit: 10^{29} W/cm^2 to be reached at ELI. Our approach is based on the collisionless kinetic equation for the evolution of the e^+ and e^- distribution functions governed by a non-Markovian source term for pair production. As possible experimental signals of vacuum pair production we consider e^+e^- annihilation into gamma-pairs and the refraction of a high-frequency probe laser beam by the produced e^+e^- plasma. We discuss the dependence of the dynamical pair production process on laser wavelength, with special emphasis on applications in the X-ray domain (X-FEL), as well as the prospects for μ^+μ^- and π^+π^- pair creation at high-intensity lasers. We investigate perspectives for using high-intensity lasers as ``boosters'' of ion beams in the few-GeV per nucleon range, which is relevant, e.g., to the exploration of the QCD phase transition in laboratory experiments.

## Key claim

The authors argue that dynamical (time-dependent) vacuum electron-positron pair creation could be observable with present-day petawatt lasers at intensities of 10^20-10^22 W/cm^2, far below the static Schwinger limit of 10^29 W/cm^2, but only if one considers the mean pair density during the laser pulse rather than the residual density after the field is switched off.

## Method

The authors solve a collisionless kinetic equation with a non-Markovian source term for pair production, derived from the Dirac equation via Bogoliubov transformations, for a spatially uniform time-dependent electric field representing two counter-propagating laser beams. They compute the quasiparticle distribution functions and their moments (number density, current, energy density) using a regularization procedure that subtracts divergent leading-order asymptotic terms. For the refraction experiment, they linearize the kinetic equations around the exact solution for the strong laser field to obtain the transverse permittivity of the produced electron-positron plasma.

## Key numbers

- Critical electric field for electron-positron pair creation: E_cr = 1.3 × 10^16 V/cm
- For an optical Ti:sapphire laser with E_m = 3 × 10^(-5) E_cr, wavelength λ = 795 nm: mean pair density ~10^7 pairs per λ^3, corresponding to ~10^20 cm^(-3)
- Ratio of mean density to residual density for the optical laser case: approximately 10^11
- For a planned X-FEL with E_m = 0.24 E_cr, λ = 0.15 nm: residual density exceeds that of the optical laser by a large factor, but mean density is lower
- Estimated annihilation events per laser pulse for I = 10^20 W/cm^2, τ_L ~85 fs, λ=795 nm, beam crossing diameter ~2.5 μm: 5-10 events per pulse
- For the Astra-Gemini laser at I = 5 × 10^21 W/cm^2, λ_0 = 800 nm: the refraction index variation is tiny and poses a challenge for experimental observation

## Context

This work extends the kinetic equation approach developed by Smolyansky et al. (1990s) and applied to periodic fields by Roberts et al. (2000s), contrasting with the imaginary time method of Popov (1970s) which does not give information about the mean pair density during the pulse. The results contradict earlier estimates by Brezin and Itzykson (1970) and others that vacuum pair creation is unlikely with available lasers, by showing that the mean density during the pulse is much larger than the residual density. The paper enables experimental searches for dynamical Schwinger effect signals via gamma-pair annihilation detection or probe beam refraction, and suggests applications to muon and pion pair creation and laser boosting of ion beams for QCD phase transition studies.
