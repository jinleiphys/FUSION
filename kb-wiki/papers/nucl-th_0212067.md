# A simple plan for the Delta resonance

> We construct the $Δ$ resonance as a superposition of a bare $Δ$ state and the $πN$ continuum. It is parametrized by three coupling constants for local $πN Δ$ and $ππN N$ couplings and the $Δ$ mass. The latter incorporates the mass renormalization due to the $πN Δ$ interaction, while the results depend only weakly, if at all, on its wave-function renormalization. Three more renormalization constants are needed for the derivative contact interaction. They allow one to generate the $Δ$ resonance dynamically. A large number of fits test the quality of different model assumptions in the $P_{33}$, $P_{31}$ and $P_{13}$ p-wave $πN$ scattering channels.

## Key claim

The Delta resonance can be constructed as a superposition of a bare Delta state and the pi-N continuum using a local field theory with no cut-offs or form factors, but the best fit to the P33 channel data requires a quasi-local interaction with n=1/2 that has "wrong dimensionality" rather than the theoretically expected n=0 or n=1 couplings.

## Method

The author constructs a Hamiltonian with separable but local interactions coupling a bare Delta state to the pi-N continuum, parametrized by coupling functions W_n (for pi-N-Delta vertices) and V_n (for pi-pi-N-N contact interactions) with momentum dependence k^(2n+2). The scattering problem is solved exactly via an eigenvalue equation yielding the ratio z of homogeneous to inhomogeneous solutions, from which the phase shift is obtained as tan(delta) = pi/z. Renormalization is handled through subtractions that absorb divergences into the bare Delta mass and wave function, with finite renormalization constants fixed by data fitting across the P33, P31, and P13 p-wave pi-N scattering channels.

## Key numbers

- For the P33 channel fit with only g0 (range 1.1-1.6 GeV): chi^2/# = 120, g0 = 0.082, c0^(0) = 1.294
- For the P33 channel fit with only g1 (range 1.1-1.6 GeV): chi^2/# = 208, g1 = 76000, c0^(1) = 10100; with c1^(1)=1: g1 = 3.78, c0^(1) = 0.493
- For the P33 channel fit with only lambda (range 1.1-1.6 GeV): chi^2/# = 140, lambda = 2.31, c0^(lambda,1) = 2.26, c1^(lambda,1) = -1.47, c0^(lambda,0) = 8.69
- For the P33 channel fit with g0 and g1 (range 1.1-1.6 GeV): chi^2/# = 82, g0 = 0.060, g1 = 0.79, c1^(0) = 0.69, c1^(1) = 0.69
- For the P33 channel fit with only g_(1/2) (range 1.1-1.6 GeV): chi^2/# = 56, g_(1/2) = 0.44, c0^(1/2) = 1.227
- For the P33 channel fit with only g_(1/2) (extended range 1.1-1.8 GeV): chi^2/# = 45, g_(1/2) = 0.44, c0^(1/2) = 1.227; consistent coupling strength g_(1/2) = 47 +/- 2
- For the P33 channel fit with two g_(1/2) resonances (range 1.1-2.0 GeV): chi^2/# = 13, g_(a,1/2) = 0.48, c0^(a,1/2) = 1.231, g_(b,1/2) = 1.75, c0^(b,1/2) = 3.74; second resonance around 2.5-4.0 GeV
- The Delta resonance crossing 90 degrees phase shift occurs at 1.232 GeV for every fit within a few MeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of local field theory without cut-offs or form factors to the Delta resonance, addressing problems with non-renormalizable higher-derivative couplings and off-shell ambiguities in the Delta propagator noted by previous authors (Pascalutsa and collaborators, 2000). The model enables systematic fitting of pi-N scattering data across multiple p-wave channels (P33, P31, P13) and demonstrates that a quasi-local n=1/2 coupling outperforms the theoretically expected n=0 and n=1 couplings for the P33 channel. The results show that the rising phase shift beyond 180 degrees after the Delta resonance cannot be explained as a background effect but requires a second resonance around 2.5-4.0 GeV, and that for the P31 and P13 channels both contact interactions and bare resonance states can fit data below 1.5 GeV equally well.
