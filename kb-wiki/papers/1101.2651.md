# Measurements of the EMC effect in few-body nuclei at large x

> Recent data on the EMC effect from Jefferson Lab experiment E03103 suggest that the nuclear dependence of the high x quark distribution may depend on the local nuclear environment, rather than being a purely bulk effect. We briefly discuss the analysis and results of the E03103 experiment focusing on the light nuclei and high x region. In addition, we also discuss a future experiment at the upgraded 12 GeV Jefferson Lab facility which will further investigate the role of the local nuclear environment and the influence of detailed nuclear structure on the modification of quark distributions.

## Key claim

The EMC effect in light nuclei depends on the local nuclear environment (e.g., clustering of nucleons) rather than simply on the total mass number or average nuclear density, as shown by the much smaller EMC effect in ³He compared to ⁴He and ⁹Be despite similar average densities.

## Method

Experiment E03103 at Jefferson Lab measured inclusive electron scattering cross sections from few-body and heavy nuclei (¹H, ²H, ³He, ⁴He, Be, C, Cu, Au) using a 5.766 GeV electron beam at scattering angles of 40° and 50°, covering a Q² range from 3 (GeV/c)² at x=0.3 to 6 (GeV/c)² at x=0.9. Additional data at four other angles (18° to 32°) and a beam energy of 5.01 GeV were collected to study Q² dependence and verify scaling behavior. The EMC ratios were extracted after applying isoscalar corrections and Coulomb corrections using the Effective Momentum Approximation.

## Key numbers

- EMC effect slope for ³He between x=0.35 and x=0.7 is significantly smaller than for ⁴He and ⁹Be, inconsistent with both A-dependent and density-dependent parameterizations
- Coulomb correction for ¹⁹⁷Au at high x near the quasi-elastic peak is about 18%
- Data cover Q² range from 3 (GeV/c)² at x=0.3 to 6 (GeV/c)² at x=0.9
- Beam currents ranged from 30 to 80 μA for the 5.766 GeV measurements
- For future experiment E10-008, precise scaling will extend to x=0.8 for W²>4 GeV² and up to x=0.92 for W²>2 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the SLAC E139 measurements (Gomez 1994) by providing the first valence-region measurement of the EMC effect in ³He and significantly improved data on ⁴He at high x. The results contradict simple A-dependent or density-dependent scaling models and instead support the idea that local nuclear environment and clustering effects are important, as suggested by recent work from Hirai (2010). The future experiment E10-008 at the upgraded 12 GeV Jefferson Lab will enable measurements on additional light nuclei (including ⁶,⁷Li, ¹⁰,¹¹B, ⁴⁰,⁴⁸Ca) to further test local density models and search for possible flavor dependence of the EMC effect.
