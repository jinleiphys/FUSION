# Phenomenological study on the decay widths of $Υ(nS)\rightarrow \bar{d}^{\ast}(2380)+X$

> The decay widths of $Υ(nS)$ $\rightarrow$ $\bar{d}^{\ast}(2380)+X$ with $n=1,2,3$ are studied in a phenomenological way. With the help of crossing symmetry, the decay widths are obtained by investigating the imaginary part of the forward scattering amplitudes between $d^\ast$ and $Υ(nS)$. The wave functions of $d^\ast$ and deuteron obtained in previous studies are used for calculating the amplitude. The interaction between $d^{\ast}$ ($d$) and $Υ$ is governed by the quark-meson interaction where the coupling constant is determined by fitting the observed widths of $Υ(nS)$ $\rightarrow$ $\bar{d}+X$. The numerical results show that the decay widths of $Υ(nS)$ $\rightarrow$ $\bar{d}^{\ast}+X$ are about $2 - 10$ times smaller than that of $\bar{d}+X$. The calculated momentum of $\bar{d^*}$ is in the range $0.3-0.8$ GeV. Therefore, it is very likely that one can find $\bar{d}^\ast(2380)$ in these semi-inclusive decay processes.

## Key claim

The decay widths of $\Upsilon(nS) \rightarrow \bar{d}^{\ast}(2380)+X$ are about 2–10 times smaller than those of $\Upsilon(nS) \rightarrow \bar{d}+X$, making detection of $\bar{d}^{\ast}(2380)$ in these semi-inclusive decays likely but dependent on the unknown momentum distribution of the $\bar{d}^{\ast}$.

## Method

The decay widths are obtained via the optical theorem by calculating the imaginary part of the forward scattering amplitude for $d^{\ast}+\Upsilon(nS) \rightarrow d^{\ast}+\Upsilon(nS)$, using crossing symmetry. The interaction is modeled as $s$-channel $B$-meson exchange governed by a quark-meson coupling, with the coupling constant $g_{\bar{b}qB}$ determined by fitting the observed widths of $\Upsilon(nS) \rightarrow \bar{d}+X$. Wave functions for the deuteron and $d^{\ast}$ come from previous chiral SU(3) quark model calculations, while $\Upsilon(nS)$ wave functions are obtained from the Schrodinger equation with a Cornell potential. A phenomenological Gaussian form factor is introduced for the momentum distribution of the final-state baryon, with parameters $p_0$ and $\Lambda$ fitted to experimental $\bar{d}$ momentum distributions and then varied over plausible ranges for $\bar{d}^{\ast}$.

## Key numbers

- For $\Upsilon(1S) \rightarrow \bar{d}+X$, the fitted decay width is $154 \times 10^{-5}$ keV, with coupling constant $g_{\bar{b}qB} = 2.5 \times 10^{-3}$, $p_0 = 0.24$ GeV, and $\Lambda = 0.80$ GeV.
- For $\Upsilon(2S) \rightarrow \bar{d}+X$, the fitted decay width is $89 \times 10^{-5}$ keV, with $g_{\bar{b}qB} = 1.9 \times 10^{-3}$, $p_0 = 0.25$ GeV, and $\Lambda = 0.78$ GeV.
- For $\Upsilon(3S) \rightarrow \bar{d}+X$, the fitted decay width is $47 \times 10^{-5}$ keV, with $g_{\bar{b}qB} = 1.3 \times 10^{-3}$, $p_0 = 0.27$ GeV, and $\Lambda = 0.87$ GeV.
- For $\Upsilon(1S) \rightarrow \bar{d}^{\ast}+X$, the predicted decay width ranges from $16 \times 10^{-5}$ keV to $71 \times 10^{-5}$ keV, for $p_0$ in $0.05-0.2$ GeV and $\Lambda$ in $0.3-0.6$ GeV.
- For $\Upsilon(2S) \rightarrow \bar{d}^{\ast}+X$, the predicted decay width ranges from $9.6 \times 10^{-5}$ keV to $42 \times 10^{-5}$ keV, for $p_0$ in $0.05-0.2$ GeV and $\Lambda$ in $0.3-0.6$ GeV.
- For $\Upsilon(3S) \rightarrow \bar{d}^{\ast}+X$, the predicted decay width ranges from $4.4 \times 10^{-5}$ keV to $24 \times 10^{-5}$ keV, for $p_0$ in $0.05-0.2$ GeV and $\Lambda$ in $0.3-0.7$ GeV.
- The calculated momentum of $\bar{d}^{\ast}$ is in the range $0.3-0.8$ GeV.
- The peaks of the wave functions for the deuteron, the $\Delta\Delta$ component of $d^{\ast}$, and the $C_8C_8$ component of $d^{\ast}$ are located around $1.5$ fm, $0.8$ fm, and $0.5$ fm, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the study of the exotic hexaquark candidate $d^{\ast}(2380)$, previously observed in nuclear reactions by the WASA-at-COSY collaboration (Bashkanov 2009, Adlarson 2011), to a new production channel: semi-inclusive decays of bottomonium. It uses the same wave functions for $d^{\ast}$ and the deuteron from the chiral SU(3) quark model calculations of Huang (2015), Shen (2015, 2016), and Zhang (2017). The results suggest that $\Upsilon(nS)$ decays at facilities such as BESIII or Belle could provide an independent test of the $d^{\ast}$ structure, complementing existing nuclear reaction studies. The approach also enables future comparisons between the compact hexaquark model and the quasi-molecular $\Delta N\pi$ model of Gal (2013, 2014) if experimental data become available.
