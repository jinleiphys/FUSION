# On the Quark and Gluon Substructure of the Sigma and other Scalar Mesons

> Within the increasing experimental evidence of the existence of the I=0 scalar low mass and wide σmeson, we review the {\it first} analysis of its quark and gluon substructure from QCD spectral sum rules and some low-energy theorems. The present data favour equal components of \bar uu+ \bar dd and of gg in its wave function, which make the wide sigma and the narrow f_0(980) as η'-like particles. A coherent picture of the other I=0 scalar mesons spectra within this mixing scheme is shortly discussed. We also expect the a_0(980) to be the lowest isovector \bar ud state, and the K^*_0(1430) its \bar ds partner.

## Key claim

The sigma meson's wave function contains roughly equal components of quark (¯uu + ¯dd) and gluon (gg) content, making it and the f0(980) "η'-like particles", but this conclusion depends on a maximal decay mixing angle of about 40–45 degrees, while the mass mixing angle from the off-diagonal correlator is only about 15 degrees.

## Method

The analysis uses QCD spectral sum rules (QSSR) à la SVZ, combining unsubtracted Laplace sum rules and subtracted sum rules with the operator product expansion including condensates up to dimension 6, plus low-energy theorems based on Ward identities for the trace of the energy-momentum tensor. The spectral function is parametrized by a "naïve" duality ansatz of a single resonance plus a QCD continuum above a threshold t_c, and the mixing between bare gluonium (σ_B) and bare quarkonium (S_2) is studied via the off-diagonal two-point correlator of the gluon and quark operators.

## Key numbers

- Unmixed scalar gluonium mass: 1.5 ± 0.2 GeV (from USR), with decay constant f_G = 390 ± 145 MeV
- Bare σ_B mass taken as ≈ 1 GeV, with f_σ_B ≈ 1 GeV
- Γ(σ_B → ππ) ≈ 0.8 GeV; Γ(σ'_B → ππ) ≈ 2 GeV (for M_σ' ≈ 1.37 GeV)
- Γ(G → ηη') ≈ 5–10 MeV; Γ(G → ηη)/Γ(G → ηη') ≈ 0.22
- Γ(G → σ_Bσ_B → 4π) ≈ 60–140 MeV
- a_0(980) mass ≈ 1 GeV; f_a0 ≈ 0.5–1.6 MeV; Γ(a_0 → ηπ) ≈ 37 MeV; Γ(a_0 → γγ) ≈ 0.3–1.5 keV
- S_2 (¯uu+¯dd) mass ≈ M_a0; Γ(S_2 → π⁺π⁻) ≈ 120 MeV; Γ(S_2 → γγ) ≈ 0.7 keV
- K*_0(1430) mass ≈ 1430 MeV; S_3 (¯ss) mass ≈ 1474 MeV; f_S3 ≈ 43 ± 19 MeV; Γ(S_3 → K⁺K⁻) ≈ 73 ± 27 MeV
- Mass mixing angle ≈ 15°; decay mixing angle |θ_s| ≈ 40–45°
- Γ(σ → π⁺π⁻) ≈ 300–700 MeV; Γ(σ → γγ) ≈ 0.2–0.5 keV
- Br[φ → γ + f_0(980)] ≈ 1.3 × 10⁻⁴ (compared to experimental (1.93 ± 0.46 ± 0.5) × 10⁻⁴)

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach of Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979) to the scalar meson sector, building on earlier analyses by Venugopal and Bramon (1990s) and the author's own work (SNG). It contradicts the interpretation of the low-mass broad σ as an ordinary ¯qq state (which would be narrow) and instead supports a maximal quarkonium-gluonium mixing scheme. The results enable future lattice QCD calculations with dynamical fermions to test the predicted decay mixing 3-point function V(0), and provide selection rules to distinguish the nature of the f_0(1.37), f_0(1.5), f_0(1.6), and f_0(1.7) states.
