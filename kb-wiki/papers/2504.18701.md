# Asymmetric warm nuclear matter described by Gogny and Skyrme-version models

> In this work, we perform a detailed study of the thermodynamical properties of asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperatures by means of the Gogny force, with a particular focus on its D1 family. We emphasize the investigation of the liquid-gas phase transition with the respective analysis of phase-coexistence boundaries (binodal sections) and instability regions (spinodal contours). Furthermore, the phenomenon of isospin distillation, intrinsically related to the unstable part of the system, is also described. In order to estimate the impact of the finite range of the nuclear interaction, for each Gogny parametrization we provide a respective zero-range Skyrme version, for which the free parameters of the model are obtained with the aim of reproducing at zero temperature the same saturation density, binding energy, incompressibility, isoscalar effective mass, isovector effective mass, symmetry energy, and symmetry energy slope. As a main result, we verify systematic deviations between the Gogny models and their Skyrme-version models, particularly at higher temperatures, where the Skyrme-version parametrizations exhibit reduced binodal and spinodal regions.

## Key claim

The paper systematically compares the finite-temperature liquid-gas phase transition in asymmetric nuclear matter predicted by finite-range Gogny forces (D1S, D1N, D1M) against zero-range Skyrme-version models calibrated to reproduce the same zero-temperature saturation properties; the authors find that Skyrme-version parametrizations systematically yield reduced binodal and spinodal regions compared to their Gogny counterparts, with deviations growing at higher temperatures.

## Method

The authors solved the finite-temperature mean-field equations for asymmetric nuclear matter using the Gogny D1 family (D1S, D1N, D1M) and constructed corresponding zero-range Skyrme-version models by fitting seven Skyrme parameters to reproduce the Gogny predictions for saturation density, binding energy, incompressibility, isoscalar effective mass, isovector effective mass, symmetry energy, and symmetry energy slope at zero temperature. They then computed the liquid-gas phase coexistence boundaries (binodals) and instability regions (spinodals) for both model classes using the Gibbs criteria for two-component systems, and analyzed isospin distillation effects.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hot asymmetric nuclear matter using Skyrme forces (Lattimer 1978, Lamb 1981, Brack 1985) and finite-range Gogny forces (Heyer 1988, Kurchareck 1989, Zhang 1996) by providing a systematic comparison between the two interaction classes for the D1 family. The authors previously compiled critical points for symmetric nuclear matter across many models (Dutra 2021). The results enable quantification of how finite-range effects alter the liquid-gas phase diagram and isospin distillation, with implications for understanding heavy-ion collision data and the properties of neutron star crusts.
