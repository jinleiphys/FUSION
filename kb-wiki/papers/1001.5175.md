# Numerical investigations of the three-dimensional proton-proton screened Coulomb t-matrix

> We demonstrate behaviour of the momentum space screened Coulomb t-matrix, obtained by a numerical solution of the three-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger equation. Examples are given for different types of screening. They prove that it is possible to obtain numerically a reliable three-dimensional screened Coulomb t-matrix, what is important in view of its application in few-body calculations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a reliable three-dimensional screened Coulomb t-matrix can be obtained numerically for any type of screening, but the numerical procedure is computationally demanding and requires careful handling of oscillatory integrals, especially for exponential screening with power n > 1.

## Method

The authors solve the three-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the screened Coulomb t-matrix using Neumann series expansion with Pade summation, for three screening types: sharp cut-off, localized, and exponential. For exponential screening with n > 1, they compute the potential matrix elements via two-dimensional numerical integration, using Filon's integration formula for the radial integral and a polynomial interpolation of the ratio I_{n,R}(q)/I_{1,R}(q) to handle the 10-order-of-magnitude variation in the integrand. The equation is solved on grids of up to 120 momentum points and 190 angular points, requiring over 1.5 × 10^8 evaluations of the potential function.

## Key numbers

- The on-shell momentum for E=13 MeV is p0 ≈ 0.396 fm^{-1}
- For exponential screening with n=4 and R=20 fm, the real part of the t-matrix has a maximum at small momenta at θ=134°, evolving to a ridge along p'=p for smaller angles
- For exponential screening with R=120 fm and n=4 at E=13 MeV, the real part of the ratio Δ = (t_c^R - V_c^R)/V_c^R does not exceed 12% at all angles; the imaginary part of Δ is below 3%
- For on-shell elements with exponential screening (n=4), the ratio of real t-matrix to potential does not exceed 4% for R=20 fm and increases to about 10% for R=120 fm
- For sharp cut-off screening at R=20 fm and R=80 fm, oscillations appear in both real and imaginary parts of the t-matrix
- For exponential screening with R=20 fm at E=13 MeV, partial waves up to l_max=10 suffice to reproduce the full 3D t-matrix; for R=120 fm, even l_max=50 is insufficient

## Context

This work extends the approach of Elster (1998) for the Malfliet-Tjion potential to general screening forms, and builds on the formalism of Witala (various years) for including the Coulomb force in three-nucleon Faddeev calculations via the screened Coulomb t-matrix. The direct three-dimensional solution avoids the slow convergence of partial wave decomposition for large screening radii, enabling more efficient inclusion of the full pp Coulomb interaction in three-nucleon scattering calculations without restriction to a finite number of partial waves.
