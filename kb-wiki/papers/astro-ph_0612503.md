# A microscopic equation of state for protoneutron stars

> We study the structure of protoneutron stars within the finite temperature Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone many-body theory. If nucleons, hyperons, and leptons are present in the stellar core, we find that neutrino trapping stiffens considerably the equation of state, because hyperon onsets are shifted to larger baryon density. However, the value of the critical mass turns out to be smaller than the ``canonical'' value 1.44 $M_\odot$. We find that the inclusion of a hadron-quark phase transition increases the critical mass and stabilizes it at about 1.5--1.6 $M_\odot$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for protoneutron stars, the inclusion of a hadron-quark phase transition increases the critical mass and stabilizes it at about 1.5–1.6 solar masses, but this result depends on the specific choice of bag constant (B = 90 MeV fm⁻³) and the use of the Maxwell construction for the phase transition; without quark matter, hyperonic protoneutron stars have a maximum mass below the canonical 1.44 solar masses.

## Method

The authors employed the finite-temperature Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone many-body theory with the Argonne V₁₈ nucleon-nucleon potential and phenomenological three-body forces based on the Urbana model. They solved self-consistent equations for the K-matrix and single-particle potential using the Frozen Correlations Approximation, then determined stellar compositions via chemical equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions for both neutrino-free and neutrino-trapped matter, including nucleons, hyperons (Σ⁻ and Λ), and leptons. For the hadron-quark phase transition, they used the MIT bag model with a bag constant B = 90 MeV fm⁻³ and performed a Maxwell construction between the baryon and quark phases, then integrated the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear matter saturation point is ρ₀ ≈ 0.17 fm⁻³ with E/A ≈ −16 MeV
- The critical temperature for the liquid-gas phase transition in symmetric matter is T_c ≈ 16 MeV
- For cold neutron stars with hyperons, the critical mass is about 1.3 M⊙
- For protoneutron stars with trapped neutrinos and hyperons, the critical mass is about 1.5 M⊙
- The maximum mass of hyperonic protostars is larger than that of cold neutron stars by about 0.3 M⊙
- With the hadron-quark phase transition, the critical mass for a protoneutron star at T = 30 MeV is 1.53 M⊙ with central density 8.2 ρ₀
- With the hadron-quark phase transition, the critical mass for a protoneutron star at T = 50 MeV is 1.53 M⊙ with central density 7.9 ρ₀
- With the hadron-quark phase transition, the critical mass for a cold neutron star at T = 0 is 1.5 M⊙ with central density 9.5 ρ₀
- The window in baryonic mass for metastable hyperonic stars during deleptonization is about 0.31 M⊙ (from about 1.15 M⊙ to about 0.84 M⊙)

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone approach previously applied to nuclear matter (Baldo and Ferreira 1999) and to cold hyperonic neutron stars (Schulze et al. 2006) by incorporating neutrino trapping and finite temperature effects relevant for protoneutron stars. The results contradict the findings of Prakash et al. (1997) regarding the temperature dependence of the equation of state for nucleonic matter and the existence of metastable stars when quark matter is included. The paper enables future studies of protoneutron star evolution and cooling, particularly the possibility of delayed collapse during deleptonization, and calls for more realistic temperature profiles and isentropic descriptions within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach.
