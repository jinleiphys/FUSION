# Directed flow of D mesons at RHIC and LHC: non-perturbative dynamics, longitudinal bulk matter asymmetry and electromagnetic fields

> We present a study of the directed flow $v_1$ for $D$ mesons discussing both the impact of initial vorticity and electromagnetic field. Recent studies predicted that $v_1$ for $D$ mesons is expected to be surprisingly much larger than that of light charged hadrons; we clarify that this is due to a different mechanism leading to the formation of a directed flow with respect to the one of the bulk matter at both relativistic and non-relativistic energies. We point out that the very large $v_1$ for $D$ mesons can be generated only if there is a longitudinal asymmetry between the bulk matter and the charm quarks and if the latter have a large non-perturbative interaction in the QGP medium. A quite good agreement with the data of STAR and ALICE is obtained if the diffusion coefficient able to correctly predict the $R_{AA}(p_T)$, $v_{2}(p_T)$ and $v_{3}(p_T)$ of $D$ meson is employed. Furthermore, the mechanism for the build-up of the $v_1(y)$ is associated to a quite small formation time that can be expected to be more sensitive to the initial high-temperature dependence of the charm diffusion coefficient. We discuss also the splitting of $v_1$ for $D^0$ and $\bar D^0$ due to the electromagnetic field that is again much larger than the one observed for charged particles and in agreement with the data by STAR that have however still error bars comparable with the splitting itself, while at LHC standard electromagnetic profile assuming a constant conductivity is not able to account for the huge splitting observed.

## Key claim

The very large directed flow $v_1$ of $D$ mesons, more than an order of magnitude larger than that of light hadrons, can be generated only if there is a longitudinal asymmetry between the bulk matter and the charm quarks and if the charm quarks have a large non-perturbative interaction in the QGP medium; the mechanism for the build-up of $v_1(y)$ is associated to a quite small formation time that can be expected to be more sensitive to the initial high-temperature dependence of the charm diffusion coefficient.

## Method

The authors performed simulations using a relativistic Boltzmann transport equation for heavy quarks coupled to Maxwell equations for electromagnetic fields, with the bulk medium evolved via a Boltzmann approach with a fixed $\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)$. The initial conditions broke boost invariance by implementing a tilted fireball distribution in the reaction plane, parametrized by emission functions $f_\pm(\eta_s)$ that generate a longitudinal asymmetry and vorticity, while charm quarks were initialized symmetrically according to binary collisions. The electromagnetic fields were computed analytically assuming a constant electrical conductivity $\sigma_{el}$ of the QGP, and the charm-bulk interaction used a non-perturbative diffusion coefficient previously tuned to reproduce $R_{AA}(p_T)$, $v_2(p_T)$ and $v_3(p_T)$ of $D$ mesons.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{\sigma_{NN}}=200$ GeV with impact parameter $b=7$ fm, the event-averaged total angular momentum of the QGP is $J_y \approx -10400$ (in $\hbar$ units), with other components four orders of magnitude smaller.
- The directed flow slope for $D^0$ mesons at RHIC energy is predicted to be $dv_1/dy \simeq 0.07$, and the splitting slope between $D^0$ and $\bar D^0$ is $d\Delta v_1/dy \simeq 0.01$.
- The maximum electromagnetic field at both RHIC and LHC is about 0.1 GeV/fm; however in vacuum the maximum at RHIC is some factor larger while at LHC is more than 50 times larger.
- The electromagnetic field calculations used conductivity values $\sigma_{el}=0.0115, 0.023, 0.046$ fm$^{-1}$, corresponding to lattice QCD computations.
- The initial temperature at the center of the fireball for RHIC collisions is $T_0=410$ MeV at initial time $t_0=0.2$ fm$/c$.

## Context

This work extends earlier predictions by Das (2016) and Chatterjee (2017) that heavy quarks can manifest a directed flow more than one order of magnitude larger than light hadrons, and that electromagnetic fields induce a splitting between $D^0$ and $\bar D^0$ directed flow. The authors improve upon those studies by including both initial vorticity and electromagnetic fields simultaneously, using a transport approach that simultaneously describes $R_{AA}$, $v_2$ and $v_3$ of $D$ mesons, and by comparing to recent STAR and ALICE data. The results enable a more precise determination of the charm diffusion coefficient at high temperatures and clarify that the large $v_1$ of $D$ mesons arises from a different mechanism than the bulk matter directed flow, requiring both longitudinal asymmetry between charm and bulk and strong non-perturbative interactions.
