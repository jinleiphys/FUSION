# Bayesian uncertainty quantification on nuclear level density data and their impact on $(p,γ)$ reactions of astrophysical interest

> The $p$ process nucleosynthesis is responsible for the synthesis of 35 neutron-deficient nuclei from $^{35}$Se to $^{196}$Hg. An important input that can affect the modeling of this process is the nuclear level density at the relevant excitation energies of the nuclei involved in the reaction network. The OSLO method has been extensively used for the measurement of level densities in excitation energies of several MeV. In this work, Bayesian optimization has been used in order to estimate the 95% high density intervals for the parameters of two level density models optimized on the OSLO data. These uncertainties are then propagated on the cross sections of $(p,γ)$ reactions leading to the compound nuclei $^{105,106}$Pd and $^{105,106}$Cd inside the astrophysically relevant energy range. Imposing constraints in this region of the isotopic chart is important for network calculations involving the nearby $p$ nuclei $^{102}$Pd and $^{106}$Cd. We discuss the reduction of the range of cross sections due to the uncertainties arising from the level density data compared to the range of the six default level density models available in TALYS and we highlight the need for level density data inside the astrophysically relevant energy ranges.

## Key claim

Bayesian optimization on OSLO nuclear level density data yields 95% high density intervals for the parameters of two level density models (BSFG and HFB+comb.), and propagating these uncertainties to (p,γ) cross sections for 105,106Pd and 105,106Cd reduces the cross-section range compared to the spread of the six default TALYS level density models, but only when the level density data cover the astrophysically relevant Gamow window.

## Method

The authors used Bayesian inference with a Gaussian likelihood and Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling (EMCEE) to fit the parameters of the Back-shifted Fermi Gas (BSFG) model and the scaled Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov plus combinatorial (HFB+comb.) model to experimental level densities from the OSLO database for 105,106Pd and 105,106Cd. The posterior distributions were then used to compute 95% high density intervals for the level densities, which were input into the Hauser-Feshbach code TALYS to calculate (p,γ) cross sections for the reactions 104,105Rh(p,γ)105,106Pd and 104,105Ag(p,γ)105,106Cd within the Gamow window (center-of-mass energies 1.2–6.4 MeV). The Koning-Delaroche optical potential and Kopecky-Uhl standard Lorentzian γ-strength function were kept fixed.

## Key numbers

- The Gamow window for the studied reactions at stellar temperatures T9=1.7–3.3 corresponds to center-of-mass energies of 1.54–4.55 MeV for the Rh reactions and 1.59–4.67 MeV for the Ag reactions.
- For 105Pd, the BSFG model uncertainty is smaller than the HFB+comb. model uncertainty by approximately a factor of 2.
- For 105Pd, the two models and their uncertainties differ by approximately a factor of 4.5 when extrapolated to the Gamow window, with differences reaching almost half an order of magnitude.
- The most probable BSFG level-density parameter a for 105Pd is 12.32, with a 95% high density interval of (12.03, 12.61).
- The most probable BSFG level-density parameter a for 106Pd is 11.83, with a 95% high density interval of (11.54, 12.12).
- The most probable BSFG level-density parameter a for 105Cd is 10.98, with a 95% high density interval of (10.67, 11.28).
- The most probable BSFG level-density parameter a for 106Cd is 11.19, with a 95% high density interval of (10.9, 11.38).

## Context

This work extends Bayesian uncertainty quantification methods previously applied to optical model potentials (Lovell 2017, Lovell 2018) to nuclear level densities, and addresses the underproduction of the p nuclei 102Pd and 106Cd in astrophysical network calculations (Rapp 2006). The results highlight the need for experimental level density measurements at excitation energies inside the Gamow window (above approximately 8–14 MeV for these compound nuclei), as the lack of data in that range causes model-dependent extrapolation uncertainties. The study enables more reliable reaction rate calculations for the γ-process network by providing quantified level-density uncertainties rather than relying on the spread of default TALYS models.
