# Imprint of Nucleon Radius on the Slowly Rotating Neutron Star Properties

> We have studied the effect of free space nucleon radius on the nuclear matter and slowly rotating neutron star properties within a relativistic mean field model with the inclusion of omega-rho mixing nonlinear term. We use the same density dependent radius as in our previous work. However, in the present work we utilize the parameter sets with more proper symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) predictions for a number of different nucleon radii. To this end, the isoscalar parameters in each parameter set and the cutoff parameter $β$ are adjusted by fitting the corresponding SNM properties at sub-saturation density to the prediction of IUFSU parameter set. We have obtained that the assumption of composite nucleons with free space radius $r \le 0.83$ fm is still compatible with the currently acceptable nuclear matter (NM) properties beyond the saturation density. We have also found that the effect of free space nucleon radius could be imprinted in the radius, moment of inertia, and crust properties of the slowly rotating neutron star.

## Key claim

The assumption of composite nucleons with a free-space radius up to 0.83 fm remains compatible with currently acceptable nuclear matter properties beyond saturation density, provided the isoscalar parameters and cutoff parameter beta are adjusted so that symmetric nuclear matter properties at sub-saturation density match those of the IUFSU parameter set.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic mean field (RMF) model including an omega-rho mixing nonlinear term, incorporating a density-dependent nucleon radius of the same functional form as in their previous work (Mart and Sulaksono, 2013). They generated multiple parameter sets for different free-space nucleon radii by adjusting the isoscalar coupling constants and the cutoff parameter beta, fitting the symmetric nuclear matter properties at sub-saturation density to the predictions of the IUFSU parameter set (Fattoyev et al., 2010). The equation of state was then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for slowly rotating neutron stars, with the crust-core transition determined via the random phase approximation method.

## Key numbers

- Upper bound on free-space nucleon radius: r ≤ 0.83 fm, derived from compatibility with symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter experimental data from Danielewicz et al. (2002)
- For r = 0.83 fm, the canonical neutron star radius is R(1.4 solar masses) ≈ 15.5 km
- Maximum moment of inertia difference between r = 0 and r = 0.83 fm: approximately 1.4 × 10^45 g·cm^2, with a ratio of about 0.64
- Crust thickness difference between r = 0 and r = 0.83 fm: about 1 km
- Crust moment of inertia ratio I_cr/I differs by almost 50% between r = 0 and r = 0.83 fm
- Crust mass ratio M_cr/M differs by nearly 40% between r = 0 and r = 0.83 fm
- The effect of nucleon radius on crust properties starts to appear globally at r ≥ 0.4 fm
- The effect on moment of inertia becomes sizable for r ≥ 0.7 fm

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Mart and Sulaksono (2013) by using parameter sets with more proper symmetric nuclear matter predictions, rather than a fixed IUFSU parameter set. The results are compared with experimental constraints from Danielewicz et al. (2002) and the FOPI data (Le Fevre et al., 2016), as well as with chiral effective field theory calculations (Krueger et al., 2013). The findings connect to the proton radius puzzle by noting that the upper bound of 0.83 fm is close to the muonic hydrogen result of 0.84 fm (Pohl et al., 2010; Antognini et al., 2013). The work enables future constraints on the free-space nucleon radius from precise measurements of the moment of inertia of PSR J0737-3039A, and suggests that the nucleon radius effect could be imprinted in Vela pulsar glitch observations, complementing the analyses of Delsate et al. (2016) and Piekarewicz et al. (2014).
