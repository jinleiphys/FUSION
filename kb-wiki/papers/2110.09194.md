# Strangelets at finite temperature in an equivparticle model

> The properties of strangelets at finite temperature are studied within the framework of an equivparticle model, where a new quark mass scaling and self-consistent thermodynamic treatment are adopted. The effects of finite volume and Coulomb energy are taken into account. Our results show that the temperature T, baryon number A and perturbation interactions have strong influences on the properties of strangelets. It is found that the energy per baryon M/A and charge-to-mass ratio fz decrease with baryon number A, while the mechanically stable radius R and strangeness per baryon fs are increasing. For a strangelet with a fixed baryon number, we note that as temperature T increases the quantites M/A, R, and fs are increasing while fz is decreasing. The effects of confinement and perturbative interactions are investigated as well by readjusting the corresponding parameters.

## Key claim
The paper claims that strangelets (small nuggets of strange quark matter) can be absolutely stable at zero temperature for certain parameter sets, but become unstable at a temperature of 20 MeV, contradicting the abstract's general statement that temperature has "strong influences" without specifying the loss of absolute stability.

## Method
The authors use an equivparticle model with a density- and temperature-dependent quark mass scaling that includes both confinement and perturbative interactions, combined with a self-consistent thermodynamic treatment starting from the free energy density. Finite-size effects are incorporated via the multiple reflection expansion (MRE) method for the density of states, and Coulomb energy contributions are added. For a given baryon number A, the chemical potentials are fixed by weak equilibrium (with zero electron chemical potential) and the mechanically stable radius is determined by requiring zero total pressure.

## Key numbers
- Energy per baryon of strangelets at T=0 MeV for parameter set (C = -0.1, sqrt(D) = 160 MeV) is below 930 MeV (the energy per baryon of 56Fe) for baryon numbers A greater than approximately 10, indicating absolute stability.
- At T=20 MeV, the energy per baryon for the same parameter set exceeds 939 MeV (the neutron mass) for all shown baryon numbers, meaning strangelets are not absolutely stable at this temperature.
- For A=100, the charge-to-mass ratio f_z decreases with temperature and shows a maximum point at a temperature T_max that depends on the parameter set; for (C = -0.1, sqrt(D) = 160 MeV), T_max is approximately 30 MeV.
- The mechanically stable radius R for A=100 increases monotonically with temperature, from about 3.5 fm at T=0 MeV to about 4.5 fm at T=50 MeV for parameter set (C = -0.1, sqrt(D) = 160 MeV).
- The entropy per baryon for A=100 approaches zero as temperature approaches 0 MeV, and increases to about 2.5 (dimensionless) at T=50 MeV for parameter set (C = -0.1, sqrt(D) = 160 MeV).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of strangelets at zero temperature using the equivparticle model (Peng 2008, Peng 2010) to finite temperature, and complements earlier finite-temperature studies using the MIT bag model by Mustafa (1996) and Zhang (2003). The new quark mass scaling with both confinement and perturbative terms, previously applied at zero temperature by Xia (2014), is here generalized to finite temperature using a temperature-dependent form from Lu (2016). The results enable predictions for the stability and properties of strangelets that might be produced in heavy-ion collisions or astrophysical events, and provide constraints on the parameter space where strangelets could be absolutely stable at low temperatures.
