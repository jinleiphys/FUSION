# Neutron Skins and Halo Orbits in the $sd$ and $pf$ shells

> The strong dependence of Coulomb energies on nuclear radii makes it possible to extract the latter from calculations of the former. The resulting estimates of neutron skins indicate that two mechanisms are involved. The first one---isovector monopole polarizability---amounts to noting that when a particle is added to a system it drives the radii of neutrons and protons in different directions, tending to equalize the radii of both fluids independently of the neutron excess. This mechanism is well understood and the Duflo-Zuker (small) neutron skin values derived 14 years ago are consistent with recent measures and estimates. The alternative mechanism involves halo orbits whose huge sizes tend to make the neutron skins larger and have a subtle influence on the radial behavior of $sd$ and $pf$ shell nuclei. In particular, they account for the sudden rise in the isotope shifts of nuclei beyond $N=28$ and the near constancy of radii in the $A=40-56$ region. This mechanism, detected here for the first time, is not well understood and may well go beyond Efimov physics usually associated to halo orbits.

## Key claim

The paper argues that neutron skins in $sd$ and $pf$ shell nuclei arise from two distinct mechanisms: the well-understood isovector monopole polarizability (which tends to equalize neutron and proton radii) and a newly identified mechanism involving halo orbits whose large sizes produce larger neutron skins and explain previously puzzling isotope shift patterns, though this second mechanism is "not well understood and may well go beyond Efimov physics."

## Method

The authors extract nuclear radii from Coulomb displacement energies (MDE) between mirror nuclei using a schematic Coulomb formula, then fit proton radii to experiment through a parameterized formula (Eqs. 2-3) that includes a shell-effect term $\lambda$. They perform 0$\hbar\omega$ no-core shell model calculations with $V_{\text{low-}k}$ potentials (AV18, CDB, N3LO) using different oscillator frequencies $\hbar\omega_\pi$ and $\hbar\omega_\nu$ for protons and neutrons, adjusting the skin parameter $\zeta$ to reproduce experimental MDE values for individual states in $A=15, 17, 39, 41$ mirror nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For $^{48}$Ca, Eq. (zeta) gives neutron skin $\Delta r_{\nu\pi}=0.14$ fm, compared to estimate 0.135(15) fm from Hagen et al.
- For $^{68}$Ni, Eq. (zeta) gives $\Delta r_{\nu\pi}=0.14$ fm, compared to estimate 0.17(2) fm from Rocamora et al.
- For $^{120}$Sn, Eq. (zeta) gives $\Delta r_{\nu\pi}=0.13$ fm, compared to estimate 0.14(2) fm from Rocamora et al.
- For $^{208}$Pb, Eq. (zeta) gives $\Delta r_{\nu\pi}=0.17$ fm, compared to estimate 0.16(3) fm from Rocamora et al.
- For $^{128}$Pb, Eq. (zeta) gives $\Delta r_{\nu\pi}=0.17$ fm, compared to experimental value 0.15(3) fm from Pbskin.
- The $s_{1/2}$ orbit in $A=17$ has an rms radius about 1.2 fm larger than the $d_{5/2}$ orbit.
- In $A=41$, the $p_{3/2}$ and $p_{1/2}$ orbits have rms radii exceeding those of the $f$ orbits by about 0.7 fm.
- The 4-parameter fit to known radii for $A\le 60$ gives rms deviation of about 42 mf with $\lambda=0$, reduced to about 18 mf when varying $\lambda$.

## Context

This work extends the Duflo-Zuker (DZ) approach from 14 years prior, which used Coulomb displacement energies to estimate neutron skins via Eq. (zeta), and reconciles those small skin values with recent measurements and estimates from electric dipole polarizability (Rocamora et al.) and ab initio calculations (Hagen et al.). The paper also addresses the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly (NSA) identified by Nolen and Schiffer (1969) and the Thomas-Ehrman shift (TES) studied by Ehrman and Thomas, showing that halo orbits in $A=17$ and $A=41$ explain the sudden rise in isotope shifts beyond $N=28$ and the near constancy of radii in the $A=40-56$ region, which previous relativistic mean field calculations (RMF) had only qualitatively explained.
