# New Topological Structures of Skyrme Theory: Baryon Number and Monopole Number

> Based on the observation that the skyrmion in Skyrme theory can be viewed as a dressed monopole, we show that the skyrmions have two independent topology, the baryon topology $π_3(S^3)$ and the monopole topology $π_2(S^2)$. With this we propose to classify the skyrmions by two topological numbers $(m,n)$, the monopole number $m$ and the shell (radial) number $n$. In this scheme the popular (non spherically symmetric) skyrmions are classified as the $(m,1)$ skyrmions but the spherically symmetric skyrmions are classified as the $(1,n)$ skyrmions, and the baryon number $B$ is given by $B=mn$. Moreover, we show that the vacuum of the Skyrme theory has the structure of the vacuum of the Sine-Gordon theory and QCD combined together, which can also be classified by two topological numbers $(p,q)$. This puts the Skyrme theory in a totally new perspective.

# New Topological Structures of Skyrme Theory: Baryon Number and Monopole Number

## Key claim

Skyrmions carry two independent topological numbers, the baryon number from π₃(S³) and the monopole number from π₂(S²), so that the baryon number B equals the product of the monopole number m and a shell (radial) number n, provided the SU(2) target space S³ admits the Hopf fibration S³ ≃ S² × S¹ and the Skyrme theory is described by two variables naturally representing S² and S¹.

## Method

The authors re-express the Skyrme Lagrangian in terms of a massless scalar field ω (representing the S¹ manifold) and a normalized pion field n̂ (representing the S² manifold), making explicit a hidden U(1) gauge symmetry via a CP¹ field ξ. They then compute the baryon number as a product of the monopole number (the π₂(S²) winding of n̂) and the shell number (the π₁(S¹) winding of ω), and classify skyrmions by the pair (m,n). The vacuum structure is analyzed by noting that ω = 2πp (p integer) gives multiple vacua, while n̂ adds a π₃(S²) knot topology to each vacuum.

## Key numbers

- Baryon number B = m n, where m is the monopole number and n is the shell (radial) number.
- For spherically symmetric skyrmions with boundary condition ω(0) = 2πn, ω(∞) = 0, the baryon number is B = n (e.g., n = 1,2,3,4,5,6,7 shown in Fig. 2).
- The monopole number of the spherically symmetric solutions is M = 1 (from the rational map n̂ = ±r̂).
- Energy of spherically symmetric skyrmions follows E_n ≃ [n(n+1)/2] E₁, where E₁ is the energy of the unit skyrmion.
- The vacuum solutions are ω = 2πp (p integer), independent of n̂.

## Context

This work extends the observation that skyrmions can be viewed as dressed monopoles (Cho, 2001; Cho, 2004; Cho, 2008) and that the rational map construction of multi-skyrmions (Houghton, 1998; Manton, 2004; Piette, 1995) already involves π₂(S²) topology. The authors explicitly separate the baryon topology into monopole and shell contributions, contrasting with the traditional classification solely by π₃(S³). The shell number was previously introduced by Manton and Piette (1995) for multi-shell skyrmions. The vacuum structure parallels that of Sine-Gordon theory and SU(2) QCD (Cho, 2007). This classification enables construction of (m,n) skyrmions by adding shell structure to existing (m,1) solutions, and raises questions about generalizing Faddeev-Niemi knots to include π₁(S¹) topology.
