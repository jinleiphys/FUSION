# Studying freeze-out and hadronization in the Landau hydrodynamical model

> We study the rapidity spectra in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions in the framework of the Landau hydrodynamical model. We find that thermal smearing effects improve the agreement with experimental results on pion rapidity spectra. We describe a simple model of the hadronization and discuss its consequences regarding the pion multiplicity and the increasing entropy condition.

## Key claim

Thermal smearing effects from the Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription improve the agreement of the Landau hydrodynamical model with experimental pion rapidity spectra, but the model's simple pion-gas post-freeze-out medium violates the increasing entropy condition unless a constituent quark/antiquark plus pion gas is used, and even then only for QGP temperatures above approximately 140 MeV.

## Method

The authors start from the Landau hydrodynamical model's approximate solution for the longitudinal expansion of a thin disk of quark-gluon plasma, assuming a Stefan-Boltzmann equation of state (P = ε/3) and a freeze-out hypersurface defined by τ_TR = 2a. They compute the pion rapidity distribution using the Cooper-Frye formula, first without thermal smearing (recovering the Wong (2008) result), then including a thermal phase-space distribution for massless quarks in the QGP phase, and finally after modeling the hadronization phase transition by imposing energy-momentum conservation across the freeze-out hypersurface and using a Jüttner (relativistic Boltzmann) distribution for a post-freeze-out pion gas. The temperature on the freeze-out hypersurface is obtained by numerically inverting the hadronic equation of state, and the entropy condition s_PFO/s_QGP > 1 is checked for three different post-freeze-out models: a simple pion gas, a hadron gas including low-mass hadrons, and a constituent quark/antiquark plus pion gas.

## Key numbers

* For Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, the Lorentz contraction factor is γ = 106.6, the gold nucleus radius is r = 6.98 fm, and the initial disk parameters are a = 2r and Δ = a/γ.
* The normalization factor for the pion rapidity distribution using the simple pion gas post-freeze-out model is N = 9.29, meaning the primary model overestimates the experimental data by this factor.
* The total energy in the primary Landau model on the freeze-out hypersurface is a factor of 5.16 larger than the reaction energy A√s_NN, and centrality selection (5% most central events) adds a factor of 1.14, together explaining a factor of 5.88, implying that 63% of the final-state energy is carried by pions.
* The K⁺/π⁺ ratio from BRAHMS (2005) is 0.173 ± 0.017, and the K⁻/π⁻ ratio is 0.143 ± 0.014.
* The entropy density ratio s_PFO/s_QGP for the simple pion gas model is approximately 0.1–0.15, violating the increasing entropy condition.
* The constituent quark/antiquark plus pion gas model satisfies the increasing entropy condition for QGP temperatures greater than approximately 140 MeV, which holds for fluid rapidities y ≲ 3.

## Context

This work extends the Landau hydrodynamical model as applied to ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions, following the approach of Landau (1953) and Wong (2008). It introduces thermal smearing via the Cooper-Frye formula (Cooper and Frye, 1974) and a detailed treatment of the hadronization phase transition, which was not included in earlier Landau-model calculations. The results are compared to BRAHMS experimental data (Bearden et al., 2005; Arsene et al., 2005) for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV. The finding that a simple pion gas violates the entropy condition motivates the need for more realistic post-freeze-out models, such as the constituent quark/antiquark plus pion gas, and highlights the limitations of the Landau model for describing transverse momentum distributions and freeze-out dynamics at large rapidities.
