# Fluctuations and the axial anomaly with three quark flavors

> The role of the axial anomaly in the chiral phase transition at finite temperature and quark chemical potential is investigated within a non-perturbative functional renormalization group approach. The flow equation for the grand potential is solved to leading-order in a derivative expansion of a three flavor quark-meson model truncation. The results are compared with a standard and an extended mean-field analysis, which facilitates the exploration of the influence of bosonic and fermionic fluctuations, respectively, on the phase transition. The influence of U(1)_A-symmetry breaking on the chiral transition, the location of a possible critical endpoint in the phase diagram and the quark mass sensitivity is studied in detail.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how the axial U(1)_A anomaly affects the chiral phase transition in three-flavor QCD using a non-perturbative functional renormalization group approach, finding that when mesonic fluctuations are included, the presence of the anomaly pushes the critical endpoint toward larger chemical potentials and lower temperatures, opposite to the behavior seen in mean-field approximations.

## Method

The authors solve the Wetterich flow equation for the grand potential of a three-flavor quark-meson model to leading order in a derivative expansion, using a Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft determinant term to implement U(1)_A symmetry breaking. They compare results from the full functional renormalization group (FRG) analysis with a standard mean-field approximation (MFA) and an extended mean-field approximation (eMFA) that includes fermionic vacuum contributions. The flow equation is solved numerically with optimized regulators, and model parameters are fixed to reproduce experimental vacuum observables such as the pion decay constant and meson masses.

## Key numbers

* Pseudocritical temperature for the non-strange condensate varies by 10% without the anomaly and by 20% including the anomaly across different approximations (FRG, MFA, eMFA) at zero chemical potential.
* Critical endpoint coordinates (T_c, mu_c) in MeV for the full FRG with anomaly: (17, 295); without anomaly: (43, 280).
* Critical endpoint coordinates for standard MFA with anomaly: (98, 155); without anomaly: (93, 173).
* Critical endpoint coordinates for eMFA with anomaly: (35, 293); without anomaly: (31, 298).
* The sigma meson mass is fixed to 480 MeV for the critical endpoint analysis, with an additional comparison at 400 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous two-flavor analyses by Schaefer and collaborators (2004, 2006) and three-flavor mean-field studies by Schaefer (2008) to include mesonic fluctuations beyond mean-field approximations. The results contradict earlier mean-field findings that the anomaly pushes the critical endpoint to smaller chemical potentials and higher temperatures; instead, with full FRG treatment the anomaly shifts the endpoint to larger chemical potentials and lower temperatures. The paper enables future investigations of U(1)_A restoration effects in two-flavor models and studies of the eta and a_0 meson contributions to the phase diagram, with work in progress cited as "mmanomalie."
