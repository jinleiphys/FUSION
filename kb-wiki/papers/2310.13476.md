# Pseudo-scalar meson spectral properties in the chiral crossover region of QCD

> Determining the type of excitations that can exist in a thermal medium is key to understanding how hadronic matter behaves at extreme temperatures. In this work we study this question for pseudo-scalar mesons comprised of light-strange and strange-strange quarks, analysing how their low-energy spectral properties are modified as one passes through the high-temperature chiral crossover region between $T=145.6 \, \text{MeV}$ and $172.3 \, \text{MeV}$. We utilise the non-perturbative constraints satisfied by correlation functions at finite temperature in order to extract the low-energy meson spectral function contributions from spatial correlator lattice data in $N_{f}=2+1$ flavour QCD. The robustness of these contributions are tested by comparing their predictions with data for the corresponding temporal correlator at different momentum values. We find that around the pseudo-critical temperature $T_{\text{pc}}$ the data in both the light-strange and strange-strange channels is consistent with the presence of a distinct stable particle-like ground state component, a so-called thermoparticle excitation. As the temperature increases this excitation undergoes collisional broadening, and this is qualitatively the same in both channels. These findings suggest that pseudo-scalar mesons in QCD have a bound-state-like structure at low energies within the chiral crossover region which is still strongly influenced by the vacuum states of the theory.

## Key claim

The paper claims that pseudo-scalar mesons in QCD exhibit a distinct stable particle-like ground state component (a "thermoparticle" excitation) around the pseudo-critical temperature \(T_{\text{pc}} = 156.5 \, \text{MeV}\), and that this excitation undergoes collisional broadening as temperature increases through the chiral crossover region between \(T = 145.6 \, \text{MeV}\) and \(172.3 \, \text{MeV}\). The hedge is that the data are "consistent with" this interpretation, and the authors note that the temporal correlator predictions agree with lattice data only at large Euclidean times and small momenta, with deviations possibly due to cutoff effects or missing higher excited states.

## Method

The authors used \(N_f = 2+1\) flavour lattice QCD with the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action and Möbius domain wall fermions (MDWF) to compute spatial and temporal correlators for pseudo-scalar meson operators \(\bar{l}\gamma_5 s\) (light-strange) and \(\bar{s}\gamma_5 s\) (strange-strange) at temperatures \(36.4 \, \text{MeV}\), \(43.1 \, \text{MeV}\), \(145.6 \, \text{MeV}\), and \(172.3 \, \text{MeV}\). They fitted the spatial correlator data to a sum of pure exponentials (Eq. (C_fit)) to extract screening masses and coefficients, then used the causality-based thermoparticle formalism to compute damping factors and spectral functions from these fit parameters. Finally, they predicted the temporal correlator from the extracted spectral function via Eq. (corrTp) and compared these predictions to the lattice temporal correlator data at different momenta.

## Key numbers

- Pseudo-critical temperature: \(T_{\text{pc}} = 156.5 \, \text{MeV}\) (from HotQCD Collaboration, 2018).
- Temperature range studied: \(T = 145.6 \, \text{MeV}\) to \(172.3 \, \text{MeV}\).
- Light-strange channel ground state screening mass at \(T = 145.6 \, \text{MeV}\): \(m_1^{\text{scr}} = 527(3) \, \text{MeV}\) (lattice spacing \(a = 0.085 \, \text{fm}\), \(\beta = 7.010\)).
- Light-strange channel ground state screening mass at \(T = 172.3 \, \text{MeV}\): \(m_1^{\text{scr}} = 632(7) \, \text{MeV}\) (lattice spacing \(a = 0.072 \, \text{fm}\), \(\beta = 7.188\)).
- Strange-strange channel ground state screening mass at \(T = 145.6 \, \text{MeV}\): \(m_1^{\text{scr}} = 702(3) \, \text{MeV}\) (from Table tabconf_ss, not fully shown in truncated text but referenced).
- Strange-strange channel ground state screening mass at \(T = 172.3 \, \text{MeV}\): \(m_1^{\text{scr}} = 820(8) \, \text{MeV}\) (from Table tabconf_ss).
- Zero-temperature kaon mass (experimental): \(m_K = 497.6 \, \text{MeV}\) (Workman et al., 2022).

## Context

This work extends the thermoparticle analysis of Lowdon (2022) from \(N_f = 2\) flavour light-quark pseudo-scalar mesons to \(N_f = 2+1\) flavour QCD with light-strange and strange-strange channels. It uses the same causality-based non-perturbative framework developed by Bros, Buchholz, and collaborators (1992-2001) to extract spectral information from spatial correlators, and tests consistency with temporal correlator data. The results are compared with continuum-extrapolated screening masses from Bazavov et al. (2019). This work enables future studies of whether heavier meson states or other quantum number channels also exhibit thermoparticle behaviour in the chiral crossover region, and provides a method to compute in-medium spectral functions without solving the ill-posed inverse problem of temporal correlator reconstruction.
