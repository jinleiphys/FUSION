# Cooking pasta with Lie groups

> We extend the (gauged) Skyrme model to the case in which the global isospin group (which usually is taken to be $SU(N)$) is a generic compact connected Lie group $G$. We analyze the corresponding field equations in (3+1) dimensions from a group theory point of view. Several solutions can be constructed analytically and are determined by the embeddings of three dimensional simple Lie groups into $G$, in a generic irreducible representation. These solutions represent the so-called nuclear pasta state configurations of nuclear matter at low energy. We employ the Dynkin explicit classification of all three dimensional Lie subgroups of exceptional Lie group to classify all such solutions in the case $G$ is an exceptional simple Lie group, and give all ingredients to construct them explicitly. As an example, we construct the explicit solutions for $G=G_{2}$. We then extend our ansatz to include the minimal coupling of the Skyrme field to a $U(1)$ gauge field. We extend the definition of the topological charge to this case and then concentrate our attention to the electromagnetic case. After imposing a "free force condition" on the gauge field, the complete set of coupled field equations corresponding to the gauged Skyrme model minimally coupled to an Abelian gauge field is reduced to just one linear ODE keeping alive the topological charge. We discuss the cases in which such ODE belongs to the (Whittaker-)Hill and Mathieu types.

## Key claim

The authors extend the (gauged) Skyrme model to any compact connected Lie group \(G\) (not just \(SU(N)\)) and show that analytic solutions representing nuclear pasta configurations are determined by embeddings of three-dimensional simple Lie groups into \(G\), classified via Dynkin's theory; the hedge is that the solutions exist only for specific choices of the coefficients \(c_j\) that ensure periodicity of \(e^{\gamma \kappa}\), and the construction requires a "free force condition" on the gauge field to reduce the coupled field equations to a single linear ODE.

## Method

The authors generalize the Skyrme field ansatz to a generic compact connected Lie group \(G\) using a generalized Euler parameterization for Lasagna states and direct exponentiation of the Lie algebra for Spaghetti states. They employ the Dynkin classification of all three-dimensional Lie subalgebras of exceptional Lie algebras to classify inequivalent families of solutions, and then extend the ansatz to include minimal coupling to a \(U(1)\) gauge field, imposing a "free force condition" to decouple the Skyrme and Maxwell equations. The resulting system reduces to a single linear ODE of Whittaker-Hill or Mathieu type for the gauge field component.

## Key numbers

* For Lasagna states in a box of volume \(V = (2\pi)^3 L_r L_\gamma L_\phi\), the energy is given by an expression involving \(\|\underline c\|^2 = \sum_{j=1}^r |c_j|^2\), \(\|v_\epsilon\|^2 = -\mathrm{Tr} v_\epsilon^2\), and parameters \(\sigma\), \(m\), \(\lambda\), \(L_r\), \(L_\gamma\), \(L_\phi\) (no single numerical value is provided for a specific system).
* For Spaghetti states in a box of volume \(V = 4\pi^3 L_r L_\theta L_\phi\), the energy expression is analogous but with different geometric factors (no explicit numerical result is given).
* The period of \(e^{\kappa z}\) is \(n \frac{2\pi}{b}\) where \(n = 1\) for the adjoint representation and \(n = 2\) for some other representations (Proposition 4).
* The constant \(a\) in the Lasagna solution is fixed to \(a = \frac12\) to cover a topological cycle with \(r \in [0, 2\pi]\) (Proposition 3).

## Context

This work extends previous analytic constructions of nuclear pasta solutions in the Skyrme model for \(SU(N)\) groups (Cacciatori et al., 2021; Canfora et al., 2013-2018) to arbitrary compact connected Lie groups, using the Dynkin classification of three-dimensional subgroups (Dynkin, 1957). It enables the systematic construction of Lasagna and Spaghetti configurations in exceptional Lie groups (e.g., \(G_2\)) and their gauged versions coupled to Maxwell theory, reducing the complicated coupled field equations to solvable linear ODEs. The results open the way to analytic studies of electromagnetic properties of nuclear pasta in neutron star crusts, previously accessible only via numerical methods.
