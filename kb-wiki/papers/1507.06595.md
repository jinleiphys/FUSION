# Transient oscillations in a macroscopic effective theory of the Boltzmann equation

> A new transient effective theory of the relativistic Boltzmann equation is derived for locally momentum-anisotropic systems. In the expansion of the distribution function around a local "quasi-equilibrium" state a non-hydrodynamic dynamical degree of freedom is introduced at leading order that breaks local momentum isotropy. By replacing the deviation of the distribution function from this quasi-equilibrium state in terms of moments of the leading-order distribution and applying a systematic power counting scheme that orders the non-hydrodynamic modes by their microscopic time scales, a closed set of equations for the dynamical degrees of freedom is obtained. Truncating this set at the level of the slowest non-hydroynamic mode we find that it exhibits transient oscillatory behavior -- a phenomenon previously found only in strongly coupled theories, where it appears to be generic. In weakly coupled systems described by the Boltzmann equation, these transient oscillations depend on the breaking of local momentum isotropy being treated non-perturbatively at leading order in the expansion of the distribution function.

## Key claim

The paper claims that transient oscillatory behavior of non-hydrodynamic modes, previously thought to be generic only in strongly coupled theories, also arises in weakly coupled systems described by the Boltzmann equation, but only if the expansion of the distribution function treats the breaking of local momentum isotropy non-perturbatively at leading order rather than perturbatively around an isotropic local equilibrium state.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Boltzmann equation and expand the distribution function around a locally momentum-anisotropic quasi-equilibrium state parametrized by an anisotropy parameter ξ and a transverse temperature β_RS. They replace the deviation from this quasi-equilibrium state in terms of irreducible moments of the distribution, apply a systematic power counting scheme in Knudsen and residual inverse Reynolds numbers to order the non-hydrodynamic modes by their microscopic time scales, and truncate the resulting infinite hierarchy at the level of the slowest non-hydrodynamic mode. The linearized equations of motion are then analyzed in the zero-wavenumber limit to obtain dispersion relations for the eigenmodes.

## Key numbers

* The dispersion relation for the non-hydrodynamic modes in the long wavelength limit (k→0) yields frequencies of the form ω_± ≡ ±ω_r − iω_i, with ω_r, ω_i > 0, indicating damped oscillations.
* The determinant of the linearized matrix A reduces to a fourth-order polynomial in ω(0) ≡ lim_{k→0} ω(k), with two degenerate zeros at ω_0 = 0 describing hydrodynamic modes and two non-hydrodynamic modes corresponding to solutions of a quadratic equation (Eq. ω in the text).
* The coefficients in the linearized equations include terms proportional to ω² arising from second time derivatives of ξ, which vanish when the moment expansion is truncated at N₂ = 0, requiring N₂ ≥ 1 for oscillatory behavior.

## Context

This work extends the DNMR theory of Denicol et al. (2012), which expanded around an isotropic local equilibrium state and found only exponentially damped non-hydrodynamic modes. It contradicts the previous belief that transient oscillations in macroscopic effective theories were exclusive to strongly coupled plasmas described by the fluid/gravity correspondence (Starinets 2002, Nunez 2003, Chesler 2008, Noronha 2011, Heller 2014). The result enables the construction of improved hydrodynamic models for the early, highly anisotropic stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where large momentum anisotropies must be treated non-perturbatively.
