# The Scalar Strange Content of the Nucleon from Lattice QCD

> The scalar strange-quark matrix element of the nucleon is computed with lattice QCD. A mixed-action scheme is used with domain-wall valence fermions computed on the staggered MILC sea-quark configurations. The matrix element is determined by making use of the Feynman-Hellmann theorem which relates this strange matrix element to the change in the nucleon mass with respect to the strange-quark mass. The final result of this calculation is m_s < N | s-bar s| N > = 49 +-10 +- 15 MeV and, correspondingly f_s = m_s < N | s-bar s |N > / m_N = 0.051 +- 0.011 +- 0.016. Given the lack of a quantitative comparison of this phenomenologically important quantity determined from various lattice QCD calculations, we take the opportunity to present such an average. The resulting conservative determination is f_s = 0.043 +- 0.011.

## Key claim

The scalar strange-quark matrix element of the nucleon is determined using the Feynman-Hellmann theorem on lattice QCD ensembles, but the chiral extrapolation is limited to a simple linear fit in m_pi^2 because the authors have nonzero results at only a single lattice spacing and a limited number of light quark mass points, preventing a rigorous mixed-action extrapolation.

## Method

The authors use a mixed-action lattice QCD scheme with domain-wall valence fermions computed on the n_f = 2+1 asqtad-improved rooted staggered MILC sea-quark configurations. The Feynman-Hellmann theorem relates the strange matrix element to the derivative of the nucleon mass with respect to the strange-quark mass, which is approximated by computing nucleon masses on pairs of ensembles with strange-quark masses straddling the physical value while keeping other parameters approximately fixed. The Matrix-Prony method is used to extract nucleon masses from two-point correlation functions, and results are converted to physical units using the MILC Collaboration's scale setting procedure with r_1 = 0.31174(20) fm.

## Key numbers

* m_s < N | s-bar s | N > = 49 +- 10 +- 15 MeV (final result after chiral extrapolation to physical pion mass)
* f_s = m_s < N | s-bar s | N > / m_N = 0.051 +- 0.011 +- 0.016 (from the authors' calculation)
* f_s = 0.043 +- 0.011 (conservative average of various lattice QCD determinations compiled by the authors)
* On the coarse ensembles (b approx 0.125 fm), m_s^phy < N | s-bar s | N > = 62(8)(11)(1) MeV at m_pi = 383.4 MeV and 79(8)(13)(2) MeV at m_pi = 674.6 MeV
* On the fine ensembles (b approx 0.09 fm), m_s^phy < N | s-bar s | N > = 50(40)(65)(1) MeV at m_pi = 240 MeV (too imprecise to determine a nonzero value)
* r_1^phy = 0.31174(20) fm (used for scale setting)

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD determinations of the nucleon's strange content using the Feynman-Hellmann method, following approaches by Young (2009) and others, while also providing a compilation and average of results from multiple lattice groups including Takeda (2010), Babich (2010), Bali (2011), and Dinter (2012). The result is phenomenologically important for dark-matter detection experiments, where the spin-independent elastic scattering cross section is proportional to the square of scalar matrix elements, and for studies of kaon condensation in dense nuclear environments as proposed by Kaplan (1986) and Nelson (1987). The authors note that lattice calculations of f_s still need improvement but show encouraging consistency across a wide variety of methods.
