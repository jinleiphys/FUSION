# Unified Wronskian formulation of inverse scattering with supersymmetric quantum mechanics

> The Wronskian formulation of supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SUSYQM) confluent transformation pairs is applied to the construction of phase-equivalent potentials with different bound spectra, replacing integral formulas. This allows to unify the two steps of a SUSYQM inversion scheme consisting in (i) the construction of a unique bound-state-less potential, possibly singular, from phase-shift inversion by a chain of non-confluent SUSYQM transformations, and (ii) the phase-equivalent addition of bound states by confluent SUSYQM pairs. Both steps are now combined in a single Wronskian formula, providing an elegant complete solution to the fixedangular-momentum inversion problem. This formalism is applied to the inversion of 3S1 and 1S0 neutron-proton data and its numerical implementation is discussed.

# Unified Wronskian formulation of inverse scattering with supersymmetric quantum mechanics

## Key claim

The paper claims to unify the two steps of the supersymmetric inversion method, constructing a bound-state-less potential from phase-shift data, then adding bound states phase-equivalently, into a single Wronskian formula, but this unification is fully analytic only when the original potential $V_0$ is simple enough; for the singlet neutron-proton case with six prior transformations, the resulting Wronskian is too large for symbolic computation and numerically unstable.

## Method

The authors apply confluent supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SUSYQM) transformation pairs, replacing earlier integral formulas for bound-state addition, removal, and modification of asymptotic normalization constant. They derive a Wronskian formula (equation \eref{final_formula}) that expresses the final potential $V_{M+2}$ entirely in terms of factorization solutions of the original potential $V_0$, combining non-confluent transformations (via the Crum-Krein formula) with confluent phase-equivalent pairs. The formalism is then applied to neutron-proton $^3S_1$ and $^1S_0$ partial waves, starting from $V_0=0$ and using hyperbolic sine and exponential factorization functions.

## Key numbers

- For the $^3S_1$ state, factorization parameters $\kappa_0 = 0.9090\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$ and $\kappa_1 = 0.2315\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$ reproduce the low-energy phase shift via $\delta_2(k) = -\arctan(k/\kappa_0) - \arctan(k/\kappa_1)$.
- The deuteron binding energy is ${\cal E}_2 = -2.225\ \mathrm{MeV}$, corresponding to $\kappa_2 = \kappa_1 = 0.2315\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$ in the coinciding case.
- For the $^1S_0$ state, six non-confluent transformations use factorization parameters $\kappa_{0,\dots,5} = 0.6152, 2.0424, 4.1650, 4.6, -0.0401, -0.7540\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$.
- The resulting $^1S_0$ potential $V_6$ has singularity parameter $n_6 = 2$ at the origin.

## Context

This work extends the supersymmetric inversion method developed by Baye (1987a, 1987b, 1994) and Sparenberg (1997), which previously required separate numerical integration for bound-state addition. It builds on confluent SUSYQM results from Bermudez (2012) and Schulze (2013) to replace the integral formula of Baye (2004) with a Wronskian. The formalism enables systematic construction of deep Moscow-type potentials with Pauli forbidden states, as explored phenomenologically by Sparenberg (2002), but the singlet-state potential $V_8$ requires a more robust numerical evaluation method before it can be used in nuclear physics calculations.
