# Event-by-event hydrodynamics $+$ jet energy loss: A solution to the $R_{AA} \otimes v_2$ puzzle

> High $p_T > 10$ GeV elliptic flow, which is experimentally measured via the correlation between soft and hard hadrons, receives competing contributions from event-by-event fluctuations of the low $p_T$ elliptic flow and event plane angle fluctuations in the soft sector. In this paper, a proper account of these event-by-event fluctuations in the soft sector, modeled via viscous hydrodynamics, is combined with a jet energy loss model to reveal that the positive contribution from low $p_T$ $v_2$ fluctuations overwhelms the negative contributions from event plane fluctuations. This leads to an enhancement of high $p_T > 10$ GeV elliptic flow in comparison to previous calculations and provides a natural solution to the decade long high $p_T$ $R_{AA} \otimes v_2$ puzzle. We also present the first theoretical calculation of high $p_T$ $v_3$, which is shown to be compatible with current LHC data. Furthermore, we discuss how short wavelength jet-medium physics can be deconvoluted from the physics of soft, bulk event-by-event flow observables using event shape engineering techniques.

## Key claim

The long-standing high $p_T$ $R_{AA} \otimes v_2$ puzzle can be naturally solved not by invoking non-perturbative energy loss mechanisms, but by computing high $p_T$ elliptic flow using its experimental definition that includes event-by-event fluctuations of the soft bulk medium, where the positive contribution from low $p_T$ $v_2$ fluctuations overwhelms the negative contributions from event plane fluctuations.

## Method

The soft sector was modeled via 2+1 dimensional event-by-event viscous relativistic hydrodynamic simulations using the v-USPhydro code with MCGlauber and MCKLN initial conditions for mid-central 20-30% Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV. The hard sector was described using the BBMG jet energy loss model with a pQCD-inspired parametrization ($dE/dL \sim L$) coupled to the hydrodynamic energy density and flow profiles, with the KKP pion fragmentation functions. High $p_T$ flow coefficients were computed using the experimental scalar-product correlation between soft and hard hadrons, $v^{exp}_n(p_T)=\langle v^{soft}_n v_n^{hard}(p_T) \cos[n(\psi^{soft}_n-\psi^{hard}_n)]\rangle / \sqrt{\langle (v^{soft}_n)^2\rangle}$.

## Key numbers

* The $\kappa$ parameter in the BBMG energy loss model was fixed by matching the computed $\pi^0$ $R_{AA}(p_T = 10$ GeV) to all-charged data in 0-5% most central LHC collisions.
* The initial time for all hydrodynamic simulations was 0.6 fm, with freeze-out temperatures $T_F = 120$ MeV for MCKLN and $T_F = 130$ MeV for MCGlauber.
* The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio was set to $\eta/s = 0.08$ for MCGlauber and $\eta/s = 0.11$ for MCKLN initial conditions.
* The correlation $\langle\cos[2\delta\psi_2]\rangle \sim 0.99$ was found for the event plane alignment, while $\langle\cos[3\delta\psi_3]\rangle \sim 0.33$ for triangular flow.
* The energy loss model used the pQCD scenario with parameters $(a=0, z=1, c=3, q=0)$, giving $dE/dL \sim L$.

## Context

This work extends the program of jet quenching phenomenology initiated by Bjorken (1982) and developed through the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ at RHIC and LHC, and addresses the long-standing puzzle that previous model calculations (e.g., Betz 2014, Xu 2014) could not simultaneously describe $R_{AA}(p_T)$ and high $p_T$ elliptic flow. The key advance is incorporating event-by-event hydrodynamic fluctuations (as established by Alver 2010, Luzum 2013) into jet energy loss calculations, which enables the first theoretical calculation of high $p_T$ $v_3$ and allows future deconvolution of short-wavelength jet-medium physics from bulk flow observables using event shape engineering techniques (Schukraft 2012, Aad 2015, Adam 2015).
