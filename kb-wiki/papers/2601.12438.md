# Revisiting $^7$Be Weak and Radiative Transition Rates in Big Bang Nucleosynthesis: Implications for the Primordial Lithium Problem

> The primordial 7Li abundance predicted by standard Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) exceeds that inferred from old, metal-poor stars by a factor of about 3-4. In standard BBN, most primordial 7Li is produced as 7Be in the early Universe and later converted by electron capture. Additional production or destruction channels of 7Be, such as proton capture or antineutrino capture during BBN, may therefore affect the final lithium yield. We quantify the depletion of 7Be due to in-situ electron capture, including the associated antineutrino channel, positron decay from nuclear excited states, and proton capture through the radiative 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction. We also investigate stimulated emission induced by the dense photon background during the nuclear statistical equilibrium epoch, as well as a three-body Auger-like variant transferring the capture energy to a continuum electron. Decay rates are computed using first-order perturbation theory, modelling weak interactions with a Fermi contact term and factorising hadronic and leptonic currents. Thermally averaged rates are obtained by folding cross-sections with Maxwell-Boltzmann distributions and accounting for particle densities in the temperature range 10-100 keV. We find that the electron-capture rate decreases rapidly with temperature and is significantly enhanced by the inclusion of the antineutrino channel. Stimulated emission and plasma screening increase the radiative proton-capture rate by only 1-3 percent at temperatures around 87 keV. The Auger-like channel contributes at the level of a few thousandths of a percent and becomes negligible at lower temperatures. Overall, our total rate revises previous estimates by nearly an order of magnitude. Electron capture, proton capture, and positron decay provide corrections to the dominant depletion channel 7Be(n,p)7Li.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their first-principles recalculation of \(^7\)Be electron capture, proton capture, and positron decay rates revises previous estimates based on \(\log(ft)\) systematics by nearly an order of magnitude, but these processes still provide only percent-level corrections to the dominant \(^7\)Be\((n,p)^7\)Li depletion channel, making them unlikely on their own to fully resolve the cosmological lithium discrepancy.

## Method

The authors computed weak interaction rates using first-order perturbation theory with a Fermi contact interaction, factorising hadronic and leptonic currents in the transition operator matrix element. Field operators for hadrons and leptons were determined using mean-field methods (Dirac-Hartree-Fock for electrons, relativistic Woods-Saxon potential for nucleons). Thermally averaged nuclear rates were obtained by folding cross-sections with Maxwell-Boltzmann distributions and accounting for particle number densities in the temperature range 10 keV \(< k_{\rm B}T <\) 100 keV, below the nuclear statistical equilibrium epoch. For the \(^7\)Be\((p,\gamma)^8\)B reaction, the astrophysical \(S_{17}\) factor was used with Debye screening, while stimulated emission was addressed using quantum field theory and the Auger-like cross-section was derived using a plane-wave electron approximation.

## Key numbers

* The thermally averaged \(^7\)Be\((p,\gamma)^8\)B rate increases by only 1-3 percent at \(k_{\rm B}T \simeq 87\) keV when stimulated emission and plasma screening are included.
* The Auger-like three-body capture cross-section is approximately a few thousandths of a percent of the radiative channel at all temperatures, becoming negligible (about \(10^{-10}\) of the radiative channel) by \(k_{\rm B}T \simeq 10\) keV.
* The total rate revises previous estimates based on systematics by nearly a factor of ten.
* The half-life correction is approximately one day.
* The primordial \(^7\)Li abundance predicted by standard BBN is \((4-5) \times 10^{-10}\) versus the observed value of \((1.3-1.6) \times 10^{-10}\), a factor of 3-4 discrepancy.

## Context

This work extends the first-principles framework previously developed by Taioli, Simonucci, and collaborators (2021, 2022) for calculating beta decay rates in plasma, which was applied to stellar environments such as red giant and asymptotic giant branch stars (Simonucci 2013, Palmerini 2016). It directly challenges the semi-empirical \(\log(ft)\) systematics approach used by Fuller (1982) and Takahashi and Yokoi (1983, 1987), who concluded these channels were negligible with half-lives of approximately 10 days. The results enable a complete reassessment of the nuclear reaction network for \(^7\)Li abundance within standard nuclear and atomic physics before invoking physics beyond the Standard Model.
