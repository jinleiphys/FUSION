# Renormalizing Chiral Nuclear Forces: Triplet Channels

> We discuss the subleading contact interactions, or counterterms, of the triplet channels of nucleon-nucleon scattering in the framework of chiral effective field theory, with S and P waves as the examples. The triplet channels are special in that they allow the singular attraction of one-pion exchange to modify Weinberg's original power counting (WPC) scheme. With renormalization group invariance as the constraint, our power counting for the triplet channels can be summarized as a modified version of naive dimensional analysis in which, when compared with WPC, all of the counterterms in a given partial wave (leading or subleading) are enhanced by the same amount. More specifically, this means that WPC needs no modification in 3S1-3D1 and 3P1, whereas a two-order enhancement is necessary in both 3P0 and 3P2 - 3F2.

## Key claim

Weinberg's power counting (WPC) requires no modification in the 3S1-3D1 and 3P1 channels, but a two-order enhancement of all counterterms (leading and subleading) is necessary in both 3P0 and 3P2-3F2, based on renormalization group invariance as the constraint.

## Method

The authors performed a momentum-space calculation of nucleon-nucleon scattering in triplet channels using chiral effective field theory, with one-pion exchange (OPE) iterated nonperturbatively at leading order and subleading two-pion exchanges (TPEs) treated as perturbations via distorted-wave expansion. They determined the required subleading contact interactions (counterterms) by examining the cutoff dependence of the subleading amplitudes in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, using a sharp momentum cutoff regulator. The counterterm values were fitted to reproduce Nijmegen partial-wave analysis results at specific laboratory kinetic energies.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV; pion mass: \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV; axial vector coupling constant: \(g_A = 1.29\)
- Low-energy constants for \(\pi \pi NN\) seagull couplings (in GeV\(^{-1}\)): \(c_1 = -0.81\), \(c_3 = -4.7\), \(c_4 = 3.4\)
- In 3P0, the leading counterterm \(C_{3P0}\) scales as \(4\pi/(m_N M_{\text{lo}}^3)\) under the modified counting, versus \(4\pi/(m_N M_{\text{lo}} M_{\text{hi}}^2)\) under WPC, where \(M_{\text{lo}} \sim 100\) MeV and \(M_{\text{hi}} \sim m_\sigma\)
- In 3P2-3F2, the leading counterterm \(C_{3P2}\) scales as \(4\pi/(m_N M_{\text{lo}}^3)\) under the modified counting, versus \(4\pi/(m_N M_{\text{lo}} M_{\text{hi}}^2)\) under WPC
- The residual cutoff dependence of the LO amplitude in attractive triplet channels scales as \(\mathcal{O}(\Lambda^{-5/2})\)
- The EFT expansion is expected to break down around \(Q \sim 300\) MeV (the delta-nucleon mass splitting)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the 3P0 channel (Long, 2011) to all triplet channels, and generalizes the modified naive dimensional analysis (mNDA) framework from a toy model with \(-1/r^2\) long-range potential (Long, 2007) to realistic nuclear forces. It contradicts the conclusion of Valderrama and collaborators (2009, 2011) that six counterterms are needed in the coupled 3S1-3D1 and 3P2-3F2 channels up to \(\mathcal{O}(Q^3)\), finding instead that three counterterms suffice. The results enable systematic renormalization-group-invariant calculations of nucleon-nucleon scattering in triplet channels up to next-to-next-to-leading order, and provide a foundation for extending the analysis to singlet channels in future work.
