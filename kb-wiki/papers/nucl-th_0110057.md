# The Z=82 shell closure in neutron-deficient Pb isotopes

> Recent mass measurements show a substantial weakening of the binding energy difference delta_{2p} (Z,N) = E(Z-2,N) - 2 E(Z,N) + E(Z+2,N) in the neutron-deficient Pb isotopes. As delta_{2p} is often attributed to the size of the proton magic gap, it might be speculated that the reduction in delta_{2p} is related to a weakening of the spherical Z=82 shell. We demonstrate that the observed trend is described quantitatively by self-consistent mean-field models in terms of deformed ground states of Hg and Po isotopes.

## Key claim

The observed weakening of the two-proton shell gap δ₂p in neutron-deficient Pb isotopes does not indicate a quenching of the spherical Z=82 shell closure; instead, the trend is quantitatively reproduced by self-consistent mean-field models when the deformed ground states of neighboring Hg and Po isotopes are taken into account.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent mean-field calculations using both non-relativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) interactions (SkP, SLy6, SkI3, SkI4) and relativistic mean-field (RMF) parameterizations (NL3, NL-Z2). They computed the two-proton shell gap δ₂p, single-proton energies, and proton shell-correction energies for Pb isotopes under spherical constraints, then repeated the δ₂p calculations allowing for deformation in the ground states of Hg, Pb, and Po isotopes. Pairing was treated within the BCS approximation using a zero-range delta force.

## Key numbers

- The experimental δ₂p value for the chain of Pb isotopes decreases monotonously from ²⁰⁸Pb towards the proton drip line, while spherical calculations show δ₂p slightly *decreases* with Z (i.e., the shell gap stays large and grows slightly approaching the drip line).
- Deformed calculations predict gradually decreasing δ₂p towards the proton drip line in accordance with experiment, with deformation energies ΔE of several MeV for most Hg and Po nuclei.
- All SHF forces overestimate δ₂p for the doubly magic ²⁰⁸Pb, even though this nucleus and its neighbors are spherical.
- The largest discrepancy between experiment and mean-field calculations for δ₂p occurs in the transitional region around N=120, where the onset of static deformation takes place.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of shell quenching in neutron-rich regions (e.g., N=20 and N=28 isotones, as discussed by Guillemaud-Mueller (1984), Vieira (1986), Orr (1991), and Zhou (1991)) to the proton-rich side of the nuclear chart. It directly addresses recent mass measurements at GSI (Radon, 2000) and Qα systematics (Toth, 1999) that had been interpreted as evidence for weakening of the Z=82 shell. The paper enables a reinterpretation of binding-energy differences in shape-coexisting regions and motivates future configuration-mixing calculations beyond the mean field, such as the generator coordinate method work by Heenen (2001) and Meyer (1995).
