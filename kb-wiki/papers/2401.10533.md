# Barrier penetration in a discrete-basis formalism

> The dynamics of a many-particle system are often modeled by mapping the Hamiltonian onto a Schrödinger equation. An alternative approach is to solve the Hamiltonian equations directly in a model space of many-body configurations. In a previous paper the numerical convergence of the two approaches was compared with a simplified treatment of the Hamiltonian representation. Here we extend the comparison to the nonorthogonal model spaces that would be obtained by the generator-coordinate method. With a suitable choice of the collective-variable grid, a configuration-interaction Hamiltonian can reproduce the Schrödinger dynamics very well. However, the method as implemented here requires that the barrier height is not much larger than the zero-point energy in the collective coordinates of the configurations.

## Key claim

The discrete-basis configuration-interaction Hamiltonian can reproduce Schrödinger dynamics for barrier penetration very well with a suitable choice of the collective-variable grid, but the method as implemented requires that the barrier height is not much larger than the zero-point energy in the collective coordinates of the configurations.

## Method

The authors construct a discrete-basis Hamiltonian from Gaussian wave packets centered on a mesh of points along a collective coordinate, with matrix elements computed from overlaps and a separable Hamiltonian containing an intrinsic part and a collective kinetic part. They solve the inhomogeneous matrix equation with modified boundary conditions to impose incoming and outgoing plane waves, then extract the transmission probability from the wave function amplitudes at the first two sites. The method is tested for both tridiagonal (nearest-neighbor) and next-to-nearest-neighbor approximations to the Hamiltonian matrix.

## Key numbers

- The zero-point energy of the configuration is defined as \(E_q = \hbar^2/(4 M_q s^2)\), where \(s\) is the size parameter of the collective wave packets.
- For the tridiagonal approximation with mesh spacing \(\Delta q = 5^{1/2}s\), the discrete-basis energies are acceptable up to energies \(\sim 2 E_q\).
- For a barrier with height \(V_0 = 1 E_q\) and width \(\sigma = 2 s\), the discrete-basis approach with \(N_{od}=2\) and \(\Delta q = (2/3)5^{1/2}s\) reproduces the Schrödinger transmission probability accurately even at deep subbarrier energies.
- The energy \(E_{1/2}\) at which transmission probability reaches 1/2 is accurate up to \(V_0 = 3 E_q\) for both \(N_{od}=1\) and \(N_{od}=2\) treatments.
- For well-known fissile nuclei, barrier heights are of order 6 MeV above ground-state energy, while zero-point energies \(E_q\) from Ref. BH2023 are in the range 1.5–2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of discrete-basis and Schrödinger approaches from the previous paper by Bertsch and Hagino (2022) to nonorthogonal model spaces typical of the generator-coordinate method, following the Hill-Wheeler (1953) barrier penetration formula for fission. The method enables testing whether configuration-interaction Hamiltonians built from constrained mean-field states can reproduce one-dimensional barrier dynamics, and it opens the question of whether collective flow or diffusive flow dominates in large-amplitude shape changes, with implications for fission theory at both subbarrier energies (Schunck, 2016) and higher energies (Bulgac, 1992).
