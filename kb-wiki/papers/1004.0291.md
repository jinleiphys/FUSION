# Exploring Hadron Physics in Black Hole Formations: a New Promising Target of Neutrino Astronomy

> The detection of neutrinos from massive stellar collapses can teach us a lot not only about source objects but also about microphysics working deep inside them. In this study we discuss quantitatively the possibility to extract information on the properties of dense and hot hadronic matter from neutrino signals coming out of black-hole-forming collapses of non-rotational massive stars. Based on our detailed numerical simulations we evaluate the event numbers for SuperKamiokande with neutrino oscillations being fully taken into account. We demonstrate that the event numbers from a Galactic event are large enough not only to detect it but also to distinguish one hadronic equation of state from another by our statistical method assuming the same progenitor model and non-rotation. This means that the massive stellar collapse can be a unique probe into hadron physics and will be a promising target of the nascent neutrino astronomy.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that for a Galactic black-hole-forming collapse (failed supernova), the time profile of detected neutrino events can statistically distinguish between different hadronic equations of state (EOS) even when the total duration of neutrino emission is similar, provided the same progenitor model and non-rotation are assumed. This claim is conditional on the progenitor being in the Galaxy and on the use of a Kolmogorov-Smirnov test that is free from distance ambiguity.

## Method

The authors performed general relativistic neutrino-radiation-hydrodynamics simulations of the collapse of a non-rotating 40 solar mass progenitor, using four different hadronic equations of state (LS180, LS220, Shen, and Hyperon). They computed neutrino luminosities and spectra up to black hole formation, then incorporated Mikheyev-Smirnov-Wolfenstein neutrino oscillations for two limiting cases of mixing parameters, and finally evaluated event numbers for SuperKamiokande. A Kolmogorov-Smirnov test was applied to the normalized cumulative time profile of events in the first 0.5 seconds after bounce to assess whether two EOS models could be distinguished at 99% confidence level.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino emission durations: Shen-EOS 1.345 sec, LS220-EOS 0.784 sec, Hyperon-EOS 0.682 sec, LS180-EOS 0.566 sec.
- Total event numbers at 10 kpc for SuperKamiokande range from 9952 (Hyperon-EOS, inverted hierarchy) to 49513 (Shen-EOS, normal hierarchy).
- For a Galactic source (10000 events in first 0.5 sec), all 100000 Monte Carlo realizations distinguish Hyperon-EOS from both LS180-EOS and LS220-EOS at 99% confidence level, for both mixing parameter sets.
- For a Large Magellanic Cloud source (400 events in first 0.5 sec), with inverted mass hierarchy and sin^2(theta_13)=10^-2, Hyperon-EOS is distinguishable from LS180-EOS in 99.5% of realizations and from LS220-EOS in 94.8% of realizations.
- The critical Kolmogorov-Smirnov measure for rejection at 99% confidence level is 0.01622 for 10000 events and 0.0811 for 400 events.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Sumiyoshi et al. 2006, 2007, 2008) which showed that failed supernova neutrino emission duration is sensitive to EOS stiffness but could not distinguish EOS models with similar durations, such as a soft nucleonic EOS and a hyperonic EOS. The new statistical method using the Kolmogorov-Smirnov test breaks this degeneracy by analyzing the time variation of event numbers rather than just the total duration. The results enable future neutrino astronomy to use Galactic failed supernovae as a probe of dense hadronic matter, and motivate the construction of larger detectors such as Deep-TITAND (5 Mton fiducial volume) to extend the reach to the Andromeda galaxy.
