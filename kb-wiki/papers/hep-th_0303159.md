# Heavy Fermion Quantum Effects in SU(2)_L Gauge Theory

> We explore the effects of a heavy fermion doublet in a simplified version of the standard electroweak theory. We integrate out the doublet and compute the exact effective energy functional of spatially varying gauge and Higgs fields. We perform a variational search for a local minimum of the effective energy and do not find evidence for a soliton carrying the quantum numbers of the decoupled fermion doublet. The fermion vacuum polarization energy offsets the gain in binding energy previously argued to be sufficient to stabilize a fermionic soliton. The existence of such a soliton would have been a natural way to maintain anomaly cancellation at the level of the states. We also see that the sphaleron energy is significantly increased due to the quantum corrections of the heavy doublet. We find that when the doublet is slightly heavier than the quantum--corrected sphaleron, its decay is exponentially suppressed owing to a new barrier. This barrier exists only for an intermediate range of fermion masses, and a heavy enough doublet is indeed unstable.

## Key claim

The authors find no evidence for a soliton carrying the quantum numbers of a decoupled heavy fermion doublet in a simplified SU(2)L gauge theory, because the fermion vacuum polarization energy offsets the gain in binding energy that previous work had argued could stabilize such a soliton. The existence of such a soliton would have provided a natural way to maintain anomaly cancellation at the level of the states.

## Method

The authors integrate out a heavy fermion doublet from a simplified version of the electroweak theory (without U(1) hypercharge, with degenerate fermion masses, and with diagonal CKM matrix) and compute the exact one-loop effective energy functional of static, spherically symmetric gauge and Higgs fields. They perform a variational search for a local minimum of the effective energy in the fermion number 1 sector, using physically motivated ansatze including twisted Higgs configurations and paths over the sphaleron, while including the full fermion vacuum polarization energy via phase shift methods.

## Key numbers

- The fermion vacuum polarization energy makes a "significant positive contribution" when the Yukawa coupling is large enough that the perturbative fermion mass is comparable to the classical energy of the sphaleron.
- The sphaleron energy is "significantly increased" due to the quantum corrections of the heavy doublet.
- When the doublet is slightly heavier than the quantum-corrected sphaleron, its decay is exponentially suppressed owing to a new barrier; this barrier exists only for an "intermediate range of fermion masses," and a heavy enough doublet is "indeed unstable."
- The minimal number of required Born subtractions for the vacuum polarization energy calculation is N=4.
- The degeneracy factor for each grand spin channel G is D_G = 2G+1.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of fermion decoupling in chiral gauge theories, following the observation by D'Hoker and Farhi that integrating out a heavy chiral fermion induces a Wess-Zumino term in the effective Lagrangian. The authors directly address the earlier work of Nolte and Kunz, who found stable non-topological solitons by omitting the vacuum polarization contribution; the present paper shows that including the full Casimir energy destabilizes those solitons. The results also relate to the Witten non-perturbative SU(2) anomaly, where a theory with an odd number of left-handed fermion doublets has no gauge-invariant states, and to the work of Farhi and collaborators showing that such anomalies can be understood in terms of the Hamiltonian of the theory. The calculation enables future investigation of whether new states carrying fermion quantum numbers must exist in the low-energy theory, or whether a mechanism to suppress the decay of perturbative fermion states is sufficient.
