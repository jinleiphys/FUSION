# Phenomenological study of the pp -> pi+ pn reaction

> Fully constrained bubble chamber data on the pp -> pi+ pn and pp -> pi+ d reactions are used to investigate the ratio of the counting rates for the two processes as function of the pn excitation energy Q. Though it is important to include effects associated with the p-wave nature of pion production, the data are insufficient to establish unambiguously the dependence on Q. The angular distributions show the presence of higher partial waves which seem to be anomalously large at small Q. The dispersion relation method to determine scattering lengths is extended to encompass cases where, as for the pp -> pi+ pn reaction, there is a bound state and, in a test example, it is shown that the values deduced for the low energy neutron-proton scattering parameters are significantly influenced by the pion p-wave behavior.

# Phenomenological study of the pp → π⁺ pn reaction

## Key claim

The ratio of counting rates between pp → π⁺ pn and pp → π⁺ d as a function of pn excitation energy Q cannot be unambiguously established from the bubble chamber data, even after accounting for the p-wave nature of pion production; however, the angular distributions reveal anomalously large higher partial waves at small Q, and the dispersion relation method for extracting scattering lengths is extended to bound-state cases where the deduced neutron-proton scattering parameters are significantly influenced by the pion p-wave behavior.

## Method

The authors analyzed fully constrained bubble chamber data from the PNPI experiment at three beam energies between 900 and 1000 MeV, comparing the double-differential cross sections for pp → π⁺ pn and pp → π⁺ d using the final state interaction (FSI) theorem relation. They extracted the normalization factor F(x) and its p-wave-corrected version F*(x) as functions of the excitation energy Q, and fitted the Gottfried-Jackson angular distributions of the pn relative momentum with Legendre polynomials to quantify higher partial wave contributions. The dispersion relation method of Gasparyan et al. (2004, 2005) was generalized to handle the bound-state deuteron pole by constructing a reduced Jost function, and tested on a Bargmann potential model with and without a pion p-wave factor.

## Key numbers

- The normalization factor F(x) at low Q (x ≲ 9) was found to be 2.2 ± 0.1, significantly above the FSI theorem prediction of unity, based on inclusive data at 951 MeV.
- Fits to F*(x) in the range 20 < Q < 180 MeV with the constraint A = 1 gave B = −0.054 ± 0.026 and C = 0.0223 ± 0.0007.
- Free fits for the parameter A (expected to be unity) yielded values of 3.3 ± 1.6, 9.0 ± 2.0, and 12.0 ± 2.3 at the three beam energies (900.2, 940.7, and 988.6 MeV).
- The spin-triplet np scattering length and effective range used as input were a = 5.414 fm and r = 1.757 fm, corresponding to Bargmann potential parameters α = 0.2315 fm⁻¹ and β = 0.9055 fm⁻¹.
- The virtual state pole position from the dispersion relation remained stable at α₀ = −0.2135 fm⁻¹, with α₁ = −α₀ for the bound state.

## Context

This work extends the FSI theorem approach of Faeldt and Wilkin (1997) and Boudard et al. (1996) to analyze bubble chamber data from the PNPI collaboration (ERM2014, ERM2011, ERM2017), which had previously been described through Δ(1232) isobar excitation. The study contradicts the assumption that S-wave dominance holds at low Q, showing that higher partial waves appear even below 20 MeV, possibly due to the tensor force coupling S and D states. The dispersion relation method, originally developed by Gasparyan et al. (2004, 2005) for the pp → K⁺Λp reaction and applied to Λp scattering length extraction, is generalized here to handle the deuteron bound state. The results enable future extraction of the bound-state pole position from pp → π⁺ pn data, analogous to the virtual pole determination in the Λp system discussed by Faeldt and Wilkin (2016), provided purely S-wave events can be isolated.
