# Experimental Chiral Dynamics: New Opportunities with Polarized Internal Targets and Almost-Real Photon Tagging

> Experiments on pion (Goldstone Boson) photoproduction from the nucleon tests the ability to make QCD predictions at confinement scale energies. Experiments with both polarized beams and targets have the potential sensitivity to demonstrate the dynamic isospin breaking effects of the up and down quark mass difference, whereas experiments on Compton scattering from the nucleon will incisively probe its chiral structure by measuring all of the spin dependent amplitudes. These and other types of photo-induced measurements on nuclei could be possible at BLAST with the addition of an almost-real photon tagging system and a forward low energy recoil ion hodoscope.

## Key claim

The paper argues that adding an almost-real photon tagging system (SMASH) to the BLAST facility at the Bates storage ring would enable a new class of precision tests of chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), particularly measurements of the imaginary part of the s-wave electric dipole amplitude \(E_{0+}\) in neutral pion photoproduction, which is sensitive to the predicted 2–3% isospin breaking effect from the up and down quark mass difference.

## Method

The authors present a conceptual design for a chicane-based very small angle electron scattering spectrometer (SMASH) to be inserted into the Bates storage ring downstream of the BLAST polarized internal target position. The design uses the first chicane dipole as an electron spectrometer to detect electrons scattered at angles below 0.5°, thereby tagging the energy and polarization of the exchanged almost-real virtual photons. The authors perform ray-tracing simulations to estimate acceptance and calculate virtual photon flux, polarization, and four-momentum transfer using exact kinematic formulas that account for the finite electron mass.

## Key numbers

- The chiral symmetry breaking scale \(\Lambda_x \simeq 4\pi f_\pi \simeq 1\) GeV, where \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV is the pion decay constant.
- The s-wave scattering length scale is \(a_0 = m_\pi/(4\pi f_\pi^2) \simeq 1.5/\Lambda_x\), vanishing in the chiral limit \(m_\pi \rightarrow 0\).
- The \(\pi^0 p\) and \(\pi^+ n\) thresholds are at 144.7 MeV and 151.4 MeV respectively, with the unitary cusp in \(E_{0+}\) occurring between them.
- The predicted isospin breaking effect in the cusp parameter \(\beta\) due to the up and down quark mass difference is \(\simeq 2\) to 3\%.
- The s-wave \(\pi^0 N\) elastic scattering length is \(\simeq 0.01 /m_\pi\) (isospin conserving term very small), while the charge exchange scattering length is \(\simeq 0.13/m_\pi\).
- The ratio of electric dipole amplitudes \(R = E_{0+}^{\gamma p \rightarrow \pi^+ n} / E_{0+}^{\gamma p \rightarrow \pi^0 p} \simeq -20\).
- For the SMASH design with 1 GeV beam, the virtual photon four-momentum transfer averaged over \(\theta < 0.33^\circ\) is very small (essentially real photons), with transverse polarization averaging about 70\% over the tagging range.
- The expected luminosity at BLAST for the internal polarized proton target is \(7.5 \times 10^{31}\) cm\(^{-2}\)s\(^{-1}\).
- The chicane must introduce an extra path length of \(n \cdot \lambda = 10.497\) cm, where \(n=5\) in the conceptual design.

## Context

This work extends the program of experimental chiral dynamics testing ChPT predictions for pion photoproduction, following earlier unpolarized threshold \(\gamma p \rightarrow \pi^0 p\) experiments at Mainz (1990s) and Saskatoon (1990s), and one-loop ChPT calculations to \(O(p^4)\) by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1990s). The proposed SMASH facility would enable measurements that existing tagged photon facilities cannot perform, particularly polarized target experiments to measure \(Im(E_{0+})\) and fully polarized Compton scattering to access the spin-dependent polarizabilities. The authors note that coherent bremsstrahlung at Mainz provides linear polarization only over a narrow energy range, whereas almost-real photon tagging provides high transverse and circular polarization over the full tagging range. The paper enables future detailed design studies for the SMASH chicane and detector systems at Bates.
