# Renormalization group invariant mean-field model for QCD at finite isospin density

> QCD at nonzero isospin chemical potentials has phenomenological relevance for a series of physical systems and provides an ideal testground for the modeling of dense strongly interacting matter. The two-flavor quark-meson model is known to effectively describe the condensation of charged pions in QCD that occurs in this setting. In this paper, we derive a renormalization-group invariant mean-field formulation of the model and demonstrate that the resulting phase diagram and equation of state are in quantitative agreement with data from lattice QCD simulations at small and intermediate isospin chemical potentials. In particular, the speed of sound from the model shows an excess over the conformal bound as previously seen in lattice computations in agreement with chiral perturbation theory. We then consider the speed of sound in the limit of large isospin chemical potentials and see that it approaches the conformal limit from above, in qualitative agreement with recent lattice results and in quantitative agreement with perturbation theory in the presence of a BCS gap. Finally, we consider the phase diagram in the approach to the chiral limit. We find that within the model the chiral phase transition connects to the pion condensation phase boundary in the chiral limit and we discuss the implications for the properties of the chiral transition point.

## Key claim

The authors derive a renormalization-group invariant mean-field formulation of the two-flavor quark-meson model and demonstrate that the resulting phase diagram and equation of state are in quantitative agreement with lattice QCD data at small and intermediate isospin chemical potentials, with the speed of sound approaching the conformal limit from above at large isospin chemical potentials, in qualitative agreement with recent lattice results and in quantitative agreement with perturbation theory including a BCS gap.

## Method

The authors construct a renormalization-group invariant mean-field effective potential for the two-flavor quark-meson model at finite isospin chemical potential, using renormalization conditions that fix the scale parameter M0 to the constituent quark mass in the vacuum and ensure the correct pion pole residue. They then solve the gap equations for the chiral condensate and pion condensate fields, and compute thermodynamic quantities including isospin density, energy density, and speed of sound, comparing these to lattice QCD data from Brandt et al. (2022) and Abbott et al. (2024), as well as to chiral perturbation theory results from Andersen et al. (2023) and perturbative estimates from Fukushima et al. (2024).

## Key numbers

- M0 = 350 MeV (central value for the RG-invariant scale parameter, varied between 300 and 400 MeV for uncertainty bands)
- f_pi = 90 MeV (pion decay constant giving best fit to lattice data, varied between 88 and 92 MeV)
- m_pi = 135 MeV (pion mass used for comparison with lattice data from Brandt et al. 2022)
- m_pi = 139.57 MeV and f_pi = 92 MeV (values used for comparison with lattice data from Abbott et al. 2024)
- m_sigma = 470 MeV (sigma meson mass parameter, adjusted to match chiral crossover temperature at mu_I = 0)
- Onset of pion condensation at mu_I = m_pi/2
- Speed of sound approaches conformal limit c_s^2 = 1/3 from above at large isospin chemical potentials
- The finite subtraction constant C_phi = -F_q^0(m_pi/(2M0)) ≈ 0.013 for m_pi = 139 MeV and M0 = 350 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous quark-meson model studies of QCD at finite isospin density (Kamikado et al. 2012, Adhikari et al. 2018, Folkestad et al. 2018) by implementing a fully renormalization-group invariant mean-field formulation that eliminates regularization scheme dependence. The model results are compared to lattice QCD simulations from Brandt et al. (2017, 2022) and Abbott et al. (2024), chiral perturbation theory from Andersen et al. (2023), and perturbative QCD estimates from Fukushima et al. (2024). The RG-invariant formulation enables quantitative agreement with lattice data across a wide range of isospin chemical potentials, from the onset of pion condensation up to the perturbative regime, and allows the authors to study the chiral limit where the chiral phase transition connects to the pion condensation phase boundary.
