# Resolving the Solar Neutrino Problem with KamLAND

> We study how well KamLAND, the first terrestrial neutrino experiment capable of addressing the solar neutrino problem, will perform in ascertaining whether or not the large mixing angle MSW solution (with $10^{-5}<Δm_{21}^2< 10^{-4} eV^2$ and oscillation amplitude $sin^2 2 θ_{12}>0.3$) is correct. We find that in a year of operation KamLAND will provide unequivocal evidence for or against this solution. Furthermore, its sensitivity to the three-neutrino oscillation parameters in this region is sufficiently acute as to determine $Δm_{21}^2$ to approximately $\pm 10$ % (for $sin^2 2 θ_{12}>0.7$) and to fix $sin^2 2 θ_{12}$ to within $\pm 0.1$ (at the $2σ$ level) with three years of accumulated data, independent of the value of $θ_{13}$.

## Key claim

KamLAND will provide unequivocal evidence for or against the large mixing angle (LMA) MSW solution of the solar neutrino problem within one year of operation, but only if the true oscillation parameters lie within the LMA region ($10^{-5} < \Delta m_{21}^2 < 10^{-4} \, \text{eV}^2$ and $\sin^2 2\theta_{12} > 0.3$); for values of $\Delta m_{21}^2$ above $2 \times 10^{-4} \, \text{eV}^2$, KamLAND can only place a lower bound and cannot uniquely determine the value.

## Method

The authors simulated the expected positron energy spectrum from inverse beta decay of reactor antineutrinos at KamLAND, using the known reactor flux contributions from all nuclear reactors within 350 km of the detector and a typical fission fraction from four fissile isotopes. They performed a chi-squared analysis over 17 energy bins (each 0.4 MeV wide) fitting only $\Delta m_{21}^2$ and $\sin^2 2\theta_{12}$, with $\sin^2 2\theta_{13}$ fixed at either 0 or 0.1, and generated simulated datasets by randomly sampling from Gaussian distributions centered at theoretical values.

## Key numbers

- KamLAND expects approximately 800 events per year with no oscillations, with a background of about 40 events per year.
- The antineutrino flux above the 1.804 MeV threshold is $1.3 \times 10^6 \, \text{cm}^{-2} \, \text{s}^{-1}$, known to a precision of about 1.4%.
- With three years of data, $\Delta m_{21}^2$ can be determined to approximately $\pm 10\%$ (at $2\sigma$) for $\sin^2 2\theta_{12} > 0.7$ and $\Delta m_{21}^2$ in the range $10^{-5}$ to $2 \times 10^{-4} \, \text{eV}^2$.
- With three years of data, $\sin^2 2\theta_{12}$ can be fixed to within $\pm 0.1$ (at $2\sigma$) throughout almost all of the LMA parameter space.
- The cross section for inverse neutron beta decay is $0.952 \times (E_\nu - \Delta M)[(E_\nu - \Delta M)^2 - m_e^2] / (1 \, \text{MeV}^2) \times 10^{-43} \, \text{cm}^2$, with theoretical error less than one percent.

## Context

This work extends the study of the solar neutrino problem by examining KamLAND's capability to test the LMA MSW solution, which was favored by SuperKamiokande data (SuperKamiokande, 2000) over the small mixing angle, low, and vacuum oscillation solutions. The paper builds on the null results from reactor experiments CHOOZ (1999) and Palo Verde (2000), which ruled out oscillations for $\Delta m_{21}^2 \gtrsim 10^{-3} \, \text{eV}^2$ at maximal mixing, and shows that KamLAND can provide a precise determination of $\Delta m_{21}^2$ that would be essential for future studies of CP-violation in the neutrino sector at very long baseline accelerator experiments.
