# $Λ$ and $Σ$ potentials in neutron stars, hypernuclei, and heavy-ion collisions

> With an appropriate $YNN$ force, the $Λ$ single-particle potential ($Λ$ potential) can be made strongly repulsive at high density, and one can solve the hyperon puzzle of neutron stars. We investigate the consistency of such a $Λ$ potential, evaluated recently from $YN$ and $YNN$ forces based on chiral effective field theory, with hypernuclear data and heavy-ion collision data. It is found that model calculations with such a $Λ$ potential can reproduce the data of the $Λ$ hypernuclear spectroscopy and the $Λ$ directed flow in heavy-ion collisions. Also, we evaluate the $Σ$ potential, which can be calculated by using the same hyperon forces as for the $Λ$ potential. Specifically, we show that the low-energy constants characterizing the strength of the $YNN$ force can be chosen to suppress the appearance of the $Λ$'s in neutron stars while at the same time the empirical value of the $Σ$ potential is reproduced.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that certain combinations of low-energy constants (LECs) for the three-baryon force (YNN) can simultaneously produce a strongly repulsive Λ potential at high density (solving the hyperon puzzle of neutron stars) and reproduce the empirical Σ potential at saturation density, but only for specific LEC sets; other combinations fail to meet all constraints.

## Method

The authors used Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations with three Λ-potential models (Chi3, Chi2, and LY-IV) to compute Λ separation energies of hypernuclei, and the relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (RQMDv) model in the JAM2 transport code to simulate Λ directed flow in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 4.5 GeV. For the Σ potential, they employed the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock method with a continuous choice, using the N³LO NN potential with N²LO NNN three-body forces and the NLO13(500) YN potential, implementing the YNN force as an effective density-dependent two-body force with LECs H₁ and H₂ constrained by the decuplet dominance approximation.

## Key numbers

- The Λ potential at saturation density is constrained to UΛ(ρ₀) ≈ −30 MeV, with empirical range −31.5 < UΛ(ρ₀) < −28.0 MeV
- The empirical Σ potential at saturation density is UΣ(ρ₀) = 30 ± 20 MeV
- The Chi2 potential (without three-body forces) overbinds Λ hypernuclei by several MeV at saturation density ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³
- The LEC combinations considered range from (H₁, H₂) = (−2.650, 0.100) to (−0.900, −0.300) in units of f⁻² where f ≈ 92 MeV
- The Σ potential at ρ₀ varies from about 10 to 30 MeV across the different LEC sets
- Heavy-ion collisions were simulated at √sNN = 4.5 GeV for mid-central Au+Au collisions

## Context

This work extends the approach of Gerstung et al. (2020), who constructed YNN forces based on chiral effective field theory with decuplet dominance approximation to solve the hyperon puzzle. The authors test those Λ potentials against hypernuclear spectroscopy data and STAR collaboration data on Λ directed flow, finding that the repulsive potentials are consistent with both. The paper also evaluates the Σ potential, which was not previously constrained by the same three-body forces, and shows that LEC sets solving the hyperon puzzle can simultaneously reproduce the empirical Σ potential constraint from Gal (2016). This enables a unified approach integrating nuclear experiments, heavy-ion collisions, and neutron star observations to constrain the equation of state of dense matter with strangeness.
