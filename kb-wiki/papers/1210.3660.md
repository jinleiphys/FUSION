# Update on Chiral Symmetry Restoration in the Context of Dilepton Data

> We evaluate currently available information on low-mass dilepton and direct-photon emission spectra measured in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. In the first part an attempt is made to develop a consistent picture of the in-medium effects on the electromagnetic spectral function and pinpoint its emission history by utilizing its radial and elliptic flow signatures. In the second part we elaborate on the implications of the empirical information on the nature of chiral symmetry restoration. We indicate how the melting of the $ρ$ resonance in hot and dense matter is related to, and compatible with, the reduction of chiral order parameters as "measured" in thermal lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The melting (broadening) of the ρ meson resonance in hot and dense hadronic matter, as observed in dilepton data, is compatible with the reduction of chiral order parameters measured in thermal lattice QCD, but the broadening mechanism itself is not a direct consequence of chiral mixing alone and requires additional resonance interactions with heat-bath particles.

## Method

The author evaluates low-mass dilepton and direct-photon emission spectra from ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions (URHICs) at SPS, RHIC, and lower energies, comparing them to hadronic many-body calculations of the in-medium ρ spectral function evolved over a thermal fireball evolution constrained by hadron spectra. The connection to chiral symmetry restoration is then examined using QCD sum rules (QCDSRs) and Weinberg sum rules (WSRs), where the in-medium vector spectral function from the dilepton calculations is inserted and tested against the temperature dependence of chiral condensates from lattice QCD and hadron-resonance gas models.

## Key numbers

- The average temperature extracted from the NA60 In-In data at √s=17.3 AGeV is T̄ ≈ 150–160 MeV, with an average baryochemical potential μ̄_B^tot ≈ 250–300 MeV (or average baryon density ρ̄_B^tot ≈ 0.7–1 ρ₀, where ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is nuclear matter saturation density).
- The average in-medium ρ-meson width extracted from the NA60 data is Γ̄_ρ^med ≈ 350–400 MeV, reaching Γ_ρ(T_pc) ≈ 600 MeV near the pseudocritical temperature for chiral restoration T_pc^χ ≈ 160 MeV.
- The inverse slope of the PHENIX direct-photon excess spectra in Au-Au at √s=200 AGeV is T_eff ≈ (221 ± 19^stat ± 19^sys) MeV.
- The low-temperature chiral mixing parameter is ε(T) = T²/6f_π², reaching ε ≈ 0.2 at T = 150 MeV and ε ≈ 0.5 (full mixing) at T ≈ 225 MeV (or 160 MeV in the chiral limit).
- The hadron-resonance gas expression for the chiral condensate reduction gives ⟨q̄q⟩(T,μ_B)/⟨q̄q⟩₀ ≈ 1 − T²/8f_π² − (1/3)(ρ_N/ρ₀) for pions and nucleons, with the nucleon sigma term Σ_N ≈ 45–60 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the hadronic many-body approach to the ρ spectral function developed by Rapp and collaborators (Rapp 1999) and applied to CERES and NA60 data (vanHees 2007), and places it in the context of chiral sum-rule analyses (Weinberg 1967, Das 1967) and thermal lattice QCD results for the chiral condensate (Borsanyi 2010). The analysis shows that the in-medium ρ broadening from resonance interactions and pion-cloud modifications is consistent with the reduction of chiral order parameters, and that the dilepton and photon data from SPS through RHIC energies point to emission temperatures around T_pc, supporting a universal origin of the excess radiation. This enables future quantitative tests of chiral restoration mechanisms using precision dilepton data from the RHIC beam-energy scan and LHC.
