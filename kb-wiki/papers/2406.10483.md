# QCD-Gravity double-copy in the Regge regime: shock wave propagators

> In recent work, we demonstrated a double-copy relation between inclusive gluon radiation in shock wave collisions of ultrarelativistic nuclei and inclusive graviton radiation in trans-Planckian gravitational shock wave collisions. We compute here the corresponding gravitational shock wave propagators in general relativity and demonstrate that they too obey a double copy relation to gluon shock wave propagators computed previously. These results provide key input in a renormalization group approach towards computing the high frequency radiation spectrum in close black hole encounters.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that gravitational shock wave propagators in general relativity obey a double-copy relation to previously computed gluon shock wave propagators in QCD, providing key input for a renormalization group approach to computing high-frequency radiation in close black hole encounters, specifically in the Regge (high-energy) regime where the eikonal approximation holds.

## Method

The authors compute scalar and graviton propagators in the background of a gravitational shock wave spacetime, following the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism and the eikonal approximation where changes in transverse directions are negligible compared to longitudinal ones. They solve the small fluctuation equations for scalar fields and graviton fields in the shock wave background, obtaining the propagators in both position and momentum space. The gravitational shock wave propagators are then compared to their QCD counterparts via the classical double-copy replacement rules: color charges replaced by momenta, structure constants replaced by the three-graviton vertex, and the QCD coupling replaced by the gravitational coupling.

## Key numbers

* The gravitational shock wave metric contains a term \(8\,\mu \,G_N \,\delta(x^-)\log(\Lambda |\bsx|) (dx^-)^2\), where \(\mu\) is the total energy of the boosted black hole and \(G_N\) is Newton's constant.
* The gravitational coupling is \(\kappa^2 = 8\pi G_N\), where \(G_N\) is Newton's constant.
* The QCD BFKL pomeron intercept at \(t=0\) gives \(\alpha_R(t=0)\sim 0.5\) for \(\alpha=0.2\), describing the cross-section growth \(\sigma \propto s^{\alpha_R(t=0)}\).
* The gravitational shock wave propagator effective vertex in momentum space is \(\mathcal{T}_R^{\mu \nu}(p,p') = -4 \pi i \Lambda^{\mu \nu} p^- \delta(p^{-}-(p')^{-}) \int d^2 \boldsymbol{z}~ e^{i \boldsymbol{z} \cdot(\boldsymbol{p}-\boldsymbol{p}^{\prime})} (U(\boldsymbol{z})-1)\), where \(U(\boldsymbol{z})\) is the gravitational analog of the Wilson line.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous demonstration (Raj and Venugopalan, 2023) of a double-copy relation between inclusive gluon radiation in shock wave collisions and inclusive graviton radiation in trans-Planckian gravitational shock wave collisions. It builds on Lipatov's (1982) result that the gravitational Lipatov vertex satisfies a double-copy relation to the QCD Lipatov vertex, and on the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory framework developed by McLerran, Venugopalan, and others for QCD. The results enable the development of a renormalization group approach analogous to the BK/JIMWLK equations in QCD, aimed at computing gravitational radiation from the merger phase of black hole scattering where numerical relativity simulations become challenging.
