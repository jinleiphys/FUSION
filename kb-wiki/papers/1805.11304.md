# Sensitivity study of experimental measures for the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition in the statistical multifragmentation model

> The experimental measures of the multiplicity derivatives, the moment parameters, the bimodal parameter, the fluctuation of maximum fragment charge number (NVZ), the Fisher exponent ($τ$) and Zipf's law parameter ($ξ$), are examined to search for the liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear multifragmention processes within the framework of the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM). The sensitivities of these measures are studied. All these measures predict a critical signature at or near to the critical point both for the primary and secondary fragments. Among these measures, the total multiplicity derivative and the NVZ provide accurate measures for the critical point from the final cold fragments as well as the primary fragments. The present study will provide a guide for future experiments and analyses in the study of nuclear liquid-gas phase transition.

## Key claim

All six experimental measures (multiplicity derivatives, moment parameters, bimodal parameter, NVZ, Fisher exponent, and Zipf's law parameter) predict a critical signature at or near the critical point from the specific heat capacity in the statistical multifragmentation model, but the total multiplicity derivative and NVZ provide the most accurate and sensitive measures for the critical point from both primary and secondary fragments, while other measures show slight deviations depending on whether primary or secondary fragments are analyzed.

## Method

The authors performed statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) calculations for a source with mass number A_s = 100 and charge number Z_s = 45 at a fragmenting volume V = 6V_0, with excitation energy varying from 1 to 15 MeV/nucleon in steps of 0.25 MeV/nucleon, generating over 1 million events per excitation energy. They then computed six different experimental measures (multiplicity derivatives, moment parameters M_2 and gamma_2, bimodal parameter, normalized variance of Z_max (NVZ), Fisher exponent tau, and Zipf's law parameter xi) as functions of source temperature for both primary and secondary fragments, and evaluated each measure's sensitivity by determining the temperature uncertainty Delta T when a 5% deviation from the maximum or minimum value is observed.

## Key numbers

- Critical point temperature from specific heat capacity: T = 5.3 MeV for the SMM source with A_s = 100, Z_s = 45
- Total multiplicity derivative and NVZ both show critical temperature exactly at T = 5.3 MeV for both primary and secondary fragments
- IMF multiplicity derivative shows critical temperature at T = 5.3 MeV for primary fragments but approximately 0.1 MeV lower for secondary fragments
- M_2 of primary fragments shows maximum at approximately 0.2 MeV above T = 5.3 MeV; M_2 of secondary fragments shows maximum at T = 5.3 MeV
- Gamma_2 deviations for both primary and secondary fragments are less than 0.1 MeV from T = 5.3 MeV
- Fisher exponent tau minima appear at approximately 0.15 MeV below T = 5.3 MeV for both primary and secondary fragments
- Bimodal parameter shows inflection point at lower temperature than T = 5.3 MeV for both primary and secondary fragments
- Zipf's law parameter xi equals 1 at temperature slightly above T = 5.3 MeV for both primary and secondary fragments

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of phase transition measures by Ma et al. (2005), who examined most of these measures (except multiplicity derivatives) in light reaction systems of 40Ar+27Al, 48Ti and 58Ni at 47 MeV/nucleon and found critical behavior around E*/A ~ 5.6 MeV. The study uses the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) of Bondorf, Botvina, and collaborators, which exhibits a liquid-gas type phase transition, to test the recently proposed multiplicity derivatives of Mallik et al. (2017) alongside established measures. The results provide a guide for future experiments by identifying which observables remain accurate after secondary decay processes, particularly recommending total multiplicity derivative and NVZ as the most robust measures for experimental determination of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition critical point.
