# Phenomenology of neutron-antineutron conversion

> We consider the possibility of neutron-antineutron ($n-\bar n$) conversion, in which the change of a neutron into an antineutron is mediated by an external source, as can occur in a scattering process. We develop the connections between $n-{\bar n}$ conversion and $n-{\bar n}$ oscillation, in which a neutron spontaneously tranforms into an antineutron, noting that if $n-{\bar n}$ oscillation occurs in a theory with B-L violation, then $n-{\bar n}$ conversion can occur also. We show how an experimental limit on $n-{\bar n}$ conversion could connect concretely to a limit on $n-{\bar n}$ oscillation, and vice versa, using effective field theory techniques and baryon matrix elements computed in the M.I.T. bag model.

## Key claim

The paper argues that neutron-antineutron conversion, mediated by an external source such as the electromagnetic current in a scattering process, can occur even when neutron-antineutron oscillation is suppressed by matter or magnetic fields, and that the conversion amplitude need not be much smaller than the oscillation amplitude despite the higher mass dimension of the relevant operators.

## Method

The authors construct effective field theory operators at both the hadron and quark levels, connecting neutron-antineutron conversion to neutron-antineutron oscillation by dressing the six-quark oscillation operators with photons from the electromagnetic current. They compute the matching between quark-level and hadron-level operators using the M.I.T. bag model to evaluate baryon matrix elements, and derive the spin-dependent transition probability in the presence of non-uniform magnetic fields.

## Key numbers

- The most sensitive free neutron-antineutron oscillation experiment yields a lifetime limit of tau_{n n-bar} = 0.85 x 10^8 s at 90% confidence level (Baldo-Ceolin et al., 1994)
- The corresponding limit on the oscillation parameter delta is delta <= 5 x 10^{-32} GeV at 90% confidence level
- The most stringent lower limit on bound neutron-antineutron oscillation lifetime in oxygen-16 is 1.9 x 10^{32} years at 90% confidence level (Super-Kamiokande, Abe et al., 2011)
- The equivalent free neutron lifetime from the oxygen-16 bound search is 2.7 x 10^8 s at 90% confidence level
- The bound neutron lifetime in deuterium yields an equivalent free neutron lifetime no less than 1.23 x 10^8 s at 90% confidence level (SNO, Aharmim et al., 2017)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for baryon-number-violating processes beyond neutron-antineutron oscillation, which was studied by Mohapatra (1980) and others, and which has been constrained by experiments such as Baldo-Ceolin (1994) and Super-Kamiokande (2011). The paper develops the connection between conversion and oscillation using effective field theory, enabling future experimental searches for neutron-antineutron conversion through scattering processes that avoid the magnetic field and matter suppression problems that plague oscillation searches, as noted by Kerbikov (2017).
