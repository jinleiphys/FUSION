# Isovector splitting of nucleon effective masses, ab-initio benchmarks and extended stability criteria for Skyrme energy functionals

> We study the effect of the splitting of neutron and proton effective masses with isospin asymmetry on the properties of the Skyrme energy density functional. We discuss the ability of the latter to predict observable of infinite matter and finite nuclei, paying particular attention to controlling the agreement with ab-initio predictions of the spin-isospin content of the nuclear equation of state, as well as diagnosing the onset of finite size instabilities, which we find to be of critical importance. We show that these various constraints cannot be simultaneously fulfilled by the standard Skyrme force, calling at least for an extension of its P-wave part.

## Key claim

Standard Skyrme energy functionals cannot simultaneously reproduce ab-initio predictions of the spin-isospin content of the nuclear equation of state, control finite-size instabilities, and achieve the correct sign of the neutron-proton effective mass splitting in neutron-rich matter, calling at least for an extension of the P-wave part of the functional.

## Method

The authors constructed three new Skyrme parameterizations (f₋, f₀, f₊) with identical isoscalar effective mass (mˢ/m = 0.7) but different isovector effective masses, using two density-dependent zero-range terms to decouple compressibility from the isoscalar effective mass. They compared infinite nuclear matter properties against variational chain summation results, computed Hartree-Fock single-particle energies and pairing gaps for spherical nuclei from ⁷⁸Ni to ²⁰⁸Pb, and evaluated isovector giant resonance energies via sum-rule parameterizations. The onset of finite-size instabilities was diagnosed using random-phase approximation response functions.

## Key numbers

- Isoscalar effective mass mˢ/m = 0.7 for all three fₓ forces, close to the SLy5 value of 0.697
- Saturation density ρ_sat = 0.162 fm⁻³ and energy per particle E/A = -16.03 MeV for all fₓ forces
- Effective mass splitting at I=1: Δm*/m = -0.284 (f₋), +0.001 (f₀), +0.170 (f₊)
- Isovector effective mass mᵛ/m = 0.870 (f₋), 0.700 (f₀), 0.625 (f₊)
- Symmetry energy fixed at 32 MeV and compressibility at 230 MeV for SLy5 and all fₓ forces
- Experimental GDR centroid in ²⁰⁸Pb: 14.3 ± 0.1 MeV; f₊ predicts 14.01 MeV, f₀ predicts 13.60 MeV, f₋ predicts 12.68 MeV
- Experimental IVGMR centroid in ²⁰⁸Pb: 26.3 ± 1.1 MeV; f₀ predicts 26.43 MeV, f₊ predicts 27.25 MeV, f₋ predicts 24.55 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Saclay-Lyon (SLy) series of parameterizations (Chabanat 1997, 1998) by attempting to reconcile the sign of the effective mass splitting predicted by ab-initio Brueckner-Hartree-Fock and Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations with the constraints from neutron matter equation of state and finite nuclei. The authors show that the SLy functionals, while reproducing the neutron matter equation of state, yield the wrong sign of the effective mass splitting (Δm* < 0 in neutron-rich matter), whereas older functionals such as SIII (Beiner 1975) and SkM* (Bartel 1982) had the correct sign but suffered from pathological behavior in neutron matter. The work enables future development of Skyrme functionals with extended P-wave terms that can simultaneously satisfy constraints from ab-initio predictions, finite nuclei properties, and stability criteria.
