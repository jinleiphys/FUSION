# Probing nucleon strange and charm distributions with lattice QCD

> We present the first lattice-QCD calculation of the unpolarized strange and charm parton distribution functions using large-momentum effective theory (LaMET). We use a lattice ensemble with 2+1+1 flavors of highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ) generated by MILC collaboration, with lattice spacing $a\approx 0.12$ fm and $M_π\approx 310$ MeV, and clover valence fermions with two valence pion masses: 310 and 690 MeV. We use momentum-smeared sources to improve the signal up to nucleon boost momentum $P_z=2.18$ GeV, and determine nonperturbative renormalization factors in RI/MOM scheme. We compare our lattice results with the matrix elements obtained from matching the PDFs from CT18NNLO and NNPDF3.1NNLO global fits. Our data support the assumptions of strange-antistrange and charm-anticharm symmetry that are commonly used in global PDF fits, and we find smaller than expected parton distribution at mid to small $x$.

## Key claim

This first lattice-QCD calculation of unpolarized strange and charm PDFs using LaMET finds that the real matrix elements are consistent with zero, supporting the strange-antistrange and charm-anticharm symmetry assumptions commonly used in global PDF fits, but the imaginary matrix elements are smaller than those from CT18NNLO and NNPDF3.1NNLO at large $zP_z$, suggesting deviations at moderate to small $x$; however, the matching kernel used is only valid for nonsinglet structure and does not fully account for the sum distributions.

## Method

The authors compute disconnected quark-loop contributions to nucleon three-point correlators on 898 configurations of a $24^3\times 64$ MILC ensemble with 2+1+1 flavors of HISQ fermions at lattice spacing $a\approx 0.12$ fm and $M_\pi\approx 310$ MeV, using clover valence fermions at two pion masses (310 and 690 MeV). They apply momentum-smeared sources to achieve nucleon boost momenta up to $P_z=2.18$ GeV, extract ground-state matrix elements via two-state fits to two-point and three-point correlators, and perform nonperturbative renormalization in the RI/MOM scheme at $\mu^\text{RI}=2.3$ GeV with $p_z^\text{RI}=0$. The renormalized matrix elements are compared with those obtained from matching CT18NNLO and NNPDF3.1NNLO global-fit PDFs to the same scheme.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing $a\approx 0.12$ fm, $M_\pi\approx 310$ MeV, spatial length $\approx 2.88$ fm, $M_\pi^\text{val}L \leq 4.55$
- Nucleon boost momenta $P_z = n_z \frac{2\pi}{L}$ with $|n_z| \in [0,5]$, corresponding to 0 to 2.18 GeV
- Total of 57,472 measurements for two-point correlators; 3,592,000 low-precision and 71,840 high-precision measurements for disconnected loops
- RI/MOM renormalization scales fixed to $p_z^\text{RI}=0$, $\mu^\text{RI}=2.3$ GeV
- Strange imaginary matrix elements are about one order of magnitude larger than charm imaginary matrix elements; charm matrix elements are about a factor of 5 smaller than strange ones at large $zP_z$
- Real matrix elements for both strange and charm are consistent with zero at 95% confidence level for most $zP_z$ points

## Context

This work extends the LaMET (quasi-PDF) approach, previously applied only to connected contributions of PDFs (Lin 2017, Chen 2018, Alexandrou 2018), to the challenging disconnected contributions needed for flavor-dependent strange and charm PDFs. The results support the $s(x)=\bar{s}(x)$ and $c(x)=\bar{c}(x)$ assumptions used in global analyses such as CT18 (Hou 2019) and NNPDF3.1 (Ball 2017), but the smaller imaginary matrix elements at large $zP_z$ suggest deviations at moderate to small $x$ that require future study with a full matching kernel including light-flavor and gluon mixing. This enables first-principles constraints on strange and charm PDFs that have large uncertainties from experimental extractions using neutrino scattering and $W+c$ production at the LHC.
