# Extracting Kinetic Freeze-out Properties in High Energy Collisions Using a Multi-source Thermal Model

> We study the transverse momentum ($p_T$) spectra of neutral pions and identified charged hadrons produced in proton--proton ($pp$), deuteron--gold ($d$--Au), and gold--gold (Au--Au) collisions at the center of mass energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. The study is made in the framework of a multi-source thermal model used in the partonic level. It is assumed that the contribution to the $p_T$-value of any hadron comes from two or three partons with an isotropic distribution of the azimuthal angle. The contribution of each parton to the $p_T$-value of a given hadron is assumed to obey any one of the standard (Maxwell-Boltzmann, Fermi-Dirac, and Bose-Einstein) distributions with the kinetic freeze-out temperature and average transverse flow velocity. The $p_T$-spectra of the final-state hadrons can be fitted by the superposition of two or three components. The results obtained from our Monte Carlo method are used to fit the experimental results of the PHENIX and STAR Collaborations. The results of present work serve as a suitable reference baseline for other experiments and simulation studies.

## Key claim
The paper claims that kinetic freeze-out temperature and average transverse flow velocity can be extracted from transverse momentum spectra using a multi-source thermal model at the parton level, where the contribution of each parton to a hadron's transverse momentum follows a standard (Maxwell-Boltzmann, Fermi-Dirac, or Bose-Einstein) distribution. The hedge is that the extracted parameters are approximately equal to those of the first (soft) component only, because the second and third components contribute less than 0.2% to the total and affect the final values by less than 0.5%.

## Method
The authors used a multi-source thermal model in which each hadron's transverse momentum is built from the vector sum of two (for mesons) or three (for baryons) parton transverse momenta, each drawn from a standard distribution with kinetic freeze-out temperature and average transverse flow velocity. A Lorentz-like transformation was applied to remove the collective flow effect, and the resulting probability density function was used in Monte Carlo calculations to generate transverse momentum spectra. The experimental data from PHENIX and STAR Collaborations for neutral pions and charged hadrons in pp, d-Au, and Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV were fitted by a superposition of two or three such components.

## Key numbers
- For neutral pions in Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, the first-component kinetic freeze-out temperature T_01 ranges from 0.175 +/- 0.002 GeV (0-5% centrality) to 0.181 +/- 0.002 GeV (70-80% centrality).
- The first-component average transverse flow velocity <beta_t>_1 for neutral pions in Au-Au collisions ranges from 0.238 +/- 0.002 c (0-10% centrality) to 0.240 +/- 0.002 c (70-80% centrality).
- For charged pions in Au-Au collisions at 0-5% centrality, T_01 = 0.132 +/- 0.012 GeV and <beta_t>_1 = 0.128 +/- 0.012 c.
- For antiprotons/protons in pp collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, T_01 = 0.151 +/- 0.014 GeV and <beta_t>_1 = 0.118 +/- 0.008 c.
- The contribution fraction of the first component (k_1) for neutral pions in Au-Au collisions is greater than 99.8% across all centralities.

## Context
This work extends the multi-source thermal model previously developed by Liu and collaborators (2010-2020) to simultaneously extract kinetic freeze-out temperature and transverse flow velocity using standard distributions with a Lorentz-like transformation, a method the authors state is rarely reported. It contrasts with the blast-wave model (which is model-dependent) and the intercept-slope method (which is hard to connect with basic physics processes). The results provide a reference baseline for other experiments and simulation studies at RHIC energies, and the authors find that the extracted parameters are nearly independent of centrality for neutral pions in Au-Au collisions.
