# Suppression of High Transverse Momentum $π^0$ Spectra in Au+Au Collisions at RHIC

> Au+Au, $s^{1/2} = 200$ A GeV measurements at RHIC, obtained with the PHENIX, STAR, PHOBOS and BRAHMS detectors, have all indicated a suppression of neutral pion production, relative to an appropriately normalized NN level. For central collisions and vanishing pseudo-rapidity these experiments exhibit suppression in charged meson production, especially at medium to large transverse momenta. In the PHENIX experiment similar behavior has been reported for $π^0$ spectra. In a recent work on the simpler D+Au interaction, to be considered perhaps as a tune-up for Au+Au, we reported on a pre-hadronic cascade mechanism which explains the mixed observation of moderately reduced $p_\perp$ suppression at higher pseudo-rapidity as well as the Cronin enhancement at mid-rapidity. Here we present the extension of this work to the more massive ion-ion collisions. Our major thesis is that much of the suppression is generated in a late stage cascade of colourless pre-hadrons produced after an initial short-lived coloured phase. We present a pQCD argument to justify this approach and to estimate the time duration $τ_p$ of this initial phase. Of essential importance is the brevity in time of the coloured phase existence relative to that of the strongly interacting pre-hadron phase. The split into two phases is of course not sharp in time, but adequate for treating the suppression of moderate and high $p_\perp$ mesons.

## Key claim

The suppression of high transverse momentum π⁰ spectra in Au+Au collisions at RHIC can be explained primarily by a late-stage cascade of colourless pre-hadrons produced after an initial short-lived coloured phase, rather than necessarily signaling quark-gluon plasma formation; the authors argue that the brevity of the coloured phase relative to the pre-hadron phase is essential, though the split between phases is not sharp in time.

## Method

The authors used the LUCIFER Monte Carlo simulation code, which divides the heavy-ion collision into two phases: Phase I treats initial nucleon-nucleon interactions in parallel using a group structure based on spatial contiguity, producing pre-hadrons; Phase II is a standard sequential cascade where these pre-hadrons interact and decay with hadron-sized cross-sections. The production time τp for pre-hadrons was estimated using pQCD arguments from Kopeliovich et al. (2004), and the simulation was constrained by fits to proton-proton data including elastic, single-diffractive, and non-single-diffractive cross-sections, as well as KNO scaling for multiplicities.

## Key numbers

- The production time τp for pre-hadrons in the center-of-momentum frame was varied between 0.2 and 0.4 fm/c, with the effective delay time for phase II collisions found to be 0.25–0.35 fm/c
- The initial transverse energy densities at phase II initialization were ≤ 1.8 GeV/fm³ for the shortest τp chosen
- The simulated π⁰ transverse momentum spectrum for Au+Au at √s = 200 A GeV and η = 0 was compared to PHENIX data over a p⊥ range from approximately 1 to 10 GeV/c
- The ratio R[AA/NN] showed a dip at p⊥ = 1.25 GeV/c in earlier PHENIX data, which the authors note disappeared in more recent data
- The simulation overestimates suppression at the lowest p⊥ but yields an overall accurate description of the spectra

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous pre-hadronic cascade mechanism applied to D+Au collisions (Kahana and Kahana, 2006) to the more massive Au+Au system, where suppression is considerably greater. The approach contrasts with conventional pQCD views of jet suppression and with parton-based simulations such as those by Wang (1997) and Geiger (1997), instead being closest in spirit to RQMD and work by Gallmeister, Greiner, and Xu (2005) as well as Cassing (2005). The results suggest that hadronic measurements may not necessarily probe the earliest coloured stages of the AA interaction, and the authors propose that dilepton or direct γ signals may be needed to illuminate the coloured phase.
