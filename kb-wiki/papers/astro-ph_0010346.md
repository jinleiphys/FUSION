# Solar Models: current epoch and time dependences, neutrinos, and helioseismological properties

> We calculate accurate solar models and report the detailed time dependences of important solar quantities. We use helioseismology to constrain the luminosity evolution of the sun and report the discovery of semi-convection in evolved solar models that include diffusion. In addition, we compare the computed sound speeds with the results of p-mode observations by BiSON, GOLF, GONG, LOWL, and MDI instruments. We contrast the neutrino predictions from a set of eight standard-like solar models and four deviant (or deficient) solar models with the results of solar neutrino experiments. For solar neutrino and for helioseismological applications, we present present-epoch numerical tabulations of characteristics of the standard solar model as a function of solar radius, including the principal physical and composition variables, sound speeds, neutrino fluxes, and functions needed for calculating solar neutrino oscillations.

## Key claim

The authors present a refined standard solar model (BP2000) that incorporates improved input data (Grevesse & Sauval 1998 composition, updated nuclear reaction rates, and corrected opacity interpolation) and report the discovery of semi-convection in evolved solar models that include element diffusion, a phenomenon not previously noted because it does not affect external quantities like luminosity or effective temperature.

## Method

The standard solar model was constructed using the OPAL equation of state and OPAL opacities supplemented by low-temperature opacities, with helium and heavy element diffusion included via the Thoul subroutine, and evolved from the zero-age main sequence to the present epoch (age 4.57 × 10⁹ yr) using a mixing-length formalism for convective flux. The model was required to fit the observed solar luminosity, radius, and surface heavy-element-to-hydrogen ratio (Z/X = 0.0230) without using helioseismological constraints, and the computed sound speeds were compared with p-mode observations from BiSON, GOLF, GONG, LOWL, and MDI instruments.

## Key numbers

- Solar radius increases from 0.869 R⊙ at zero-age main sequence to 1.0 R⊙ at present epoch and 1.166 R⊙ at 8 billion years age (Table 1)
- Solar luminosity rises from 0.677 L⊙ at zero-age main sequence to 1.003 L⊙ at present epoch and 1.363 L⊙ at 8 billion years age (Table 2)
- At present epoch, p-p + ³He-³He reactions produce 87.8% of solar luminosity, ³He-⁴He branches produce 10.7%, and CNO cycle produces 1.5% (Figure 4)
- The S₀-factor for the ³He(p,e⁺+νₑ)⁴He (hep) reaction is taken as 10.1 × 10⁻²⁰ keV b, a factor of 4.4 larger than the previous best estimate
- The interpolation error in OPAL 95 opacities caused errors in sound speed of order 0.1% for solar radii 0.3 R⊙ to 0.7 R⊙ and errors of order 3% in the ⁸B neutrino flux
- The product L⊙(t) × R⊙(t)⁻²·⁵ is constant to ±4% from zero-age main sequence to 8 billion years; from 4 to 8 billion years, L⊙(t) ∝ R⊙(t)² to ±0.5%
- Solar effective temperature varies by only ±1.5% from zero-age main sequence to 8 billion years age (Table 3)

## Context

This work extends the series of precision solar models by Bahcall and collaborators (Bahcall 1989; Bahcall & Pinsonneault 1992, 1995; Bahcall, Basu, & Pinsonneault 1998) by incorporating the improved Grevesse & Sauval (1998) solar composition and correcting an opacity interpolation error that affected sound speeds and neutrino fluxes. The discovery of semi-convection in evolved models with diffusion, triggered by metal accumulation below the convective zone, is reported for the first time and relates to phenomena discussed by Richer, Michaud, & Turcotte (2000) in A and F stars. The detailed time-dependent tables and radial profiles of electron density and neutrino production enable more precise studies of solar neutrino oscillations (MSW effect) and helioseismological inversions, while the comparison of eight standard-like and four deviant models with neutrino experiments (chlorine, Kamiokande, GALLEX, SAGE, Super-Kamiokande, GNO) provides a robust testbed for neutrino parameter determination.
