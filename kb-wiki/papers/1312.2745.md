# Recent results on N* spectroscopy with ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels approach

> We report our recent effort on the extraction of N*- and Delta*-resonance parameters (pole masses, coupling constants, etc.) through the comprehensive analysis of pion- and photon-induced pi N, eta N, K Lambda, and K Sigma production reactions. The analysis was performed with the ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels approach, which satisfies the multichannel unitarity of the S-matrix in the channel space spanned by pi N, eta N, K Lambda, K Sigma, and also three-body pi pi N that contains rho N, pi Delta, and sigma N resonant components. Ongoing projects and future plans are also discussed.

# Recent results on N* spectroscopy with ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels approach

## Key claim

The ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels analysis of pion- and photon-induced meson production reactions can extract N* and Delta* resonance parameters, but the second Delta(J^P = 3/2^+) resonance (the Roper-like state of the Delta baryons) still has sizable ambiguity in its parameters because the P33 pi N partial wave amplitude is dominated by Delta(1232) at low energies and the second resonance contributes little to it.

## Method

The authors solved coupled-channels integral equations for partial wave amplitudes in the channel space spanned by pi N, eta N, K Lambda, K Sigma, pi Delta, rho N, sigma N, and gamma N, using a potential that separates non-resonant meson-baryon processes from s-channel bare N* states. They fitted model parameters to more than 22,000 data points, including SAID energy-independent solutions for pi N scattering up to W=2.3 GeV and actual unpolarized and polarized observables up to W=2.1 GeV for the other reactions. The Green's functions for the quasi-two-body channels pi Delta, rho N, and sigma N include self energies from their respective decays, ensuring proper branch points for three-body pi pi N channels in the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers

- More than 22,000 data points were fitted in the combined analysis of pi N -> pi N, eta N, K Lambda, K Sigma and gamma N -> pi N, eta N, K Lambda, K Sigma reactions.
- The analysis covers energies up to W=2.3 GeV for pi N scattering and up to W=2.1 GeV for other reactions.
- N* resonances were extracted with Re(M_R) < 2 GeV and Im(M_R) < 0.2 GeV, defining the region where parameters are expected to be determined with confidence.
- The sharp peak in the pi- p -> K0 Lambda total cross section around W=1.7 GeV is explained as a cusp effect from opening of the K Sigma channel.
- Planned future work aims to extract N -> N* electromagnetic transition form factors up to Q^2 = 6 (GeV/c)^2 for N* resonances with Re(M_R) <= 1.6 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier analysis (sjklms10, 2010) which showed that multichannel reaction dynamics can generate many physical resonances from a single bare state, challenging the naive one-to-one correspondence between static hadron model baryons and physical resonances. The analysis also builds on the meson-cloud effect demonstrated for the N -> Delta(1232) M1 transition form factor (sl2, 2002). The completed 8-channel DCC model enables future extraction of electromagnetic transition form factors from CLAS data, extension to omega N and double pion production channels planned at J-PARC, and applications to Y* spectroscopy, meson spectroscopy, and neutrino-nucleon reactions relevant to neutrino oscillation experiments.
