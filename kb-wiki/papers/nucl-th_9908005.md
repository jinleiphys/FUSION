# Describing Nuclear Matter with Effective Field Theories

> An accurate description of nuclear matter starting from free-space nuclear forces has been an elusive goal. The complexity of the system makes approximations inevitable, so the challenge is to find a consistent truncation scheme with controlled errors. The virtues of an effective field theory approach to this problem are discussed.

## Key claim

Effective field theory (EFT) techniques can provide a systematic, controlled truncation scheme for nuclear matter calculations, with the truncation error scaling as a power of the Fermi momentum over the free-space breakdown scale, \(k_F/\Lambda\), but the nature of the power counting for many-body forces is still under investigation and three- and four-body contact terms may be needed even at leading order.

## Method

The authors use a perturbative matching calculation to compare an EFT description of nuclear matter with model data generated from an exactly solvable potential with weak coupling \(\lambda\). They fit EFT contact interactions to this data order-by-order in \(\lambda\) and verify that the in-medium \(T\)-matrix match remains regulator independent to the same order as in free space. The analysis is extended to discuss nonperturbative EFT calculations using the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, where the effective potential includes contact interactions expanded in powers of momentum, and the need for three-body contact interactions to ensure regularization independence is demonstrated.

## Key numbers

- The breakdown scale for an EFT without explicit pions is of order \(m_\pi/2\) (approximately 70 MeV), as determined from the convergence of error curves in \(p\cot\delta\) for singlet \(np\) scattering.
- Including one-pion exchange explicitly extends the range of validity to about \(\Lambda = 300\) MeV.
- Preliminary results suggest that with careful removal of long-distance contamination, the EFT range of validity may extend as high as \(\Lambda \sim m_\rho\) (approximately 770 MeV).
- The truncation error in nuclear matter from the EFT expansion is a power of \(k_F/\Lambda\), with phenomenologically successful mean-field descriptions having a related expansion with \(\Lambda \simeq 600\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of effective field theory techniques, previously developed for few-nucleon systems, to the many-body problem of nuclear matter. It builds on the effective range expansion of Bethe and earlier EFT analyses of nucleon-nucleon scattering (e.g., Steele and Furnstahl 1998). The paper enables systematic EFT calculations of nuclear matter that could help settle long-standing issues such as nuclear saturation, justify traditional expansions like the mean-field approximation, and ultimately connect nuclear physics to QCD.
