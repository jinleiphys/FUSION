# Stable First-order Particle-frame Relativistic Hydrodynamics for Dissipative Systems

> We propose a stable first-order relativistic dissipative hydrodynamic equation in the particle frame (Eckart frame) for the first time. The equation to be proposed was in fact previously derived by the authors and a collaborator from the relativistic Boltzmann equation. We demonstrate that the equilibrium state is stable with respect to the time evolution described by our hydrodynamic equation in the particle frame. Our equation may be a proper starting point for constructing second-order causal relativistic hydrodynamics, to replace Eckart's particle-flow theory.

## Key claim

The authors claim to have derived the first stable first-order relativistic dissipative hydrodynamic equation in the particle (Eckart) frame, but the stability is demonstrated only for a rarefied gas using a specific equation of state and transport coefficients obtained from the Boltzmann equation with a constant cross section; the stability condition involves a new inequality that depends on the equation of state and the viscosities, and the authors note that the Marle-Stewart equation, which also satisfies the Grad-Marle-Stewart constraints, is unstable.

## Method

The authors perform a linear stability analysis of their proposed hydrodynamic equation around the thermal equilibrium state, using the equation of state and transport coefficients (bulk viscosity, shear viscosity, heat conductivity) for a rarefied relativistic gas, derived from the Boltzmann equation with a constant cross section using the Galerkin approximation with Ritz polynomial expansion. They derive the dispersion relations for transverse and longitudinal modes from the linearized hydrodynamic equations and check the Routh-Hurwitz stability conditions (positivity of certain coefficients) numerically for a range of momenta and cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The transverse mode dispersion relation is \(k^0 = - i \, \eta\, |\Vec{k}|^2/(\epsilon + p)\), showing the mode is stable.
- The stability condition for longitudinal modes with zero heat conductivity is \(4\eta/3 + \zeta_P \, [ 1 - 3 \, (\partial p/{\partial \epsilon})_n ] \ge 0\), where \(\zeta_P \equiv \zeta \, (3 \, \gamma - 4)^{-2}\).
- For a massless relativistic gas (\(\epsilon = 3p\)), the stability condition reduces to \(\eta \ge 0\).
- Numerical results for the coefficients \(a\), \(b\), and \(b c - a d\) in the cubic dispersion equation are shown for \(|\Vec{k}|/T = 0.1, 1, 10\) and cross section values \(\sigma T^2 = 0.01\) to \(10\), with all quantities found positive.
- The transport coefficients for a rarefied gas are given explicitly, e.g., \(\eta = \frac{15}{32 \pi} \frac{T}{\sigma} \mathrm{e}^{\mu/T} [z^2 K^2_2(z) \hat{h}^2]/[(5 z^2 + 2) K_2(2 z) + (3 z^3 + 49 z) K_3(2 z)]\), where \(z = m/T\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous derivation of covariant hydrodynamic equations from the relativistic Boltzmann equation using the renormalization group method (Tsumura, Kunihiro, and Ohnishi, 2007), which showed that the Eckart constraint \(u_{\mu} u_{\nu} \delta T^{\mu\nu} = 0\) is incompatible with the Boltzmann equation. The paper directly addresses the well-known instability of the Eckart equation (Hiscock and Lindblom, 1985) and contrasts the proposed equation with the Marle-Stewart equation, which also satisfies the Grad-Marle-Stewart constraints but is found to be unstable. The authors argue that their stable first-order equation provides a proper starting point for constructing second-order causal relativistic hydrodynamics, such as the Israel-Stewart equations, which are typically formulated in the particle frame.
