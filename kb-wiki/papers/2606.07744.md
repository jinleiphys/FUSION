# Open quantum system approach to the transverse momentum broadening of a colour dipole

> Using the open quantum systems formalism, we study the propagation of a quark-antiquark pair propagating through a dense QCD plasma of size $L$ and transverse momentum broadening transport coefficient $\hat q$, and we derive the Lindblad evolution equation for the density matrix of the system. We focus on the boosted regime where the opening angle $θ_{q\bar q}$ of this effective colour dipole satisfies $θ_{q\bar q}\ll 1$. In the correlation limit where the quark-antiquark relative transverse momentum $p_\perp$ is much larger than the imbalance $q_\perp$ as well as the medium typical transverse momentum scale $Q_s=\sqrt{\hat q L}$, we demonstrate that the resulting Wigner distribution displays quasi factorisation between a hard factor describing the hard splitting producing the $q\bar q$ pair and the transverse momentum imbalance $q_\perp$-distribution encoding the broadening induced by the medium. The factorisation is violated by a "colour decoherence" factor that controls the $θ_{q\bar q}$ dependence of the $q_\perp$-distribution through the ratio $θ_{q\bar q}/θ_c$, with $θ_c \sim (\hat q L^3)^{-1/2}$. The open quantum systems approach enables us to clarify the role of this critical angle $θ_c$ and its associated critical time $t_c$ in the genuine quantum decoherence of the density matrix in colour and kinematic space: in particular, $t_c$ controls both the suppression of the off-diagonal elements of the density matrix in colour space and the transition between singlet and octet states. We find, however, that colour decoherence sets in earlier than the full decoherence of the density matrix, thereby marking the onset of classical behaviour in the system. Finally, we investigate the corrections beyond the quasi-factorised picture due to the quantum diffusion term in $p_\perp$ of the Lindblad equation and we find that these corrections are mild.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Wigner distribution of a boosted quark-antiquark dipole propagating through a dense QCD plasma displays quasi factorisation between a hard splitting factor and a medium-induced transverse momentum imbalance distribution, but this factorisation is violated by a "colour decoherence" factor that depends on the ratio of the dipole opening angle to a critical angle \(\theta_c \sim (\hat q L^3)^{-1/2}\). The authors find that colour decoherence sets in earlier than full decoherence of the density matrix, marking the onset of classical behaviour in colour space while the system remains quantum in momentum space.

## Method

The authors derive a Lindblad quantum master equation for the density matrix of a quark-antiquark pair propagating through a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma in thermal equilibrium, using the open quantum systems formalism in the high-energy (boosted) regime where the opening angle \(\theta_{q\bar q} \ll 1\). They solve this master equation analytically for the Wigner function in the back-to-back limit where the relative transverse momentum \(p_\perp\) is much larger than both the imbalance \(q_\perp\) and the medium saturation scale \(Q_s = \sqrt{\hat q L}\), neglecting the quantum diffusion term in \(p_\perp\) initially, and then assessing corrections from that term perturbatively.

## Key numbers

- Critical angle: \(\theta_c = 2 / \sqrt{\hat q L^3}\), which represents the minimal opening angle above which the medium resolves the internal colour structure of the dipole.
- For wide-angle dipoles with \(\theta_{q\bar q} \gg \theta_c\), the squared second moment of the transverse momentum imbalance distribution scales as \(\langle q_\perp^2 \rangle = 2\, \hat q L\).
- Critical time: \(t_c \sim (\hat q \theta_{q\bar q}^2)^{-1/3}\), which governs the suppression of off-diagonal elements of the density matrix in colour space and the transition between singlet and octet states.
- Classicalization time: \(t_2 \sim (E^2/(\hat q \Lambda^2))^{1/3}\), where \(\Lambda\) is the typical width of off-diagonal support in momentum basis; this is parametrically larger than \(t_c\) for high-energy dipoles.
- For typical LHC parameters with \(\hat q \sim 1.5\) GeV\(^2\)/fm and \(L \sim 4\) fm, \(\theta_c \sim 0.05\) and \(Q_s \sim 5\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the open quantum systems approach to transverse momentum broadening previously applied to single quarks by Barata et al. (2023), who identified novel time scales governing the quantum-to-classical transition in quark propagation. The paper builds on the colour decoherence literature initiated by Mehtar-Tani et al. (2010-2012) and Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2011-2012), which established that a colour dipole initially in a singlet state can emit large-angle gluons when propagating through a dense medium, characterised by the critical angle \(\theta_c\). The results enable future phenomenological applications to jet substructure observables such as the \(\theta_g\) distribution from Soft Drop grooming and energy-energy correlators within jets, where the critical angle \(\theta_c\) can be pinned down experimentally.
