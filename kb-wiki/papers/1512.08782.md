# Direct-photon+hadron correlations to study parton energy loss with the STAR experiment

> We report new results of $γ_{dir}$+hadron and $π^{0}$+hadron azimuthal correlations as a measure of the away-side jet-like correlated yields in central Au+Au and p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV in the STAR experiment from years 2011 and 2009 of data taking, respectively. The charged-hadron per-trigger yields at mid-rapidity $(|η| < 1)$ and for transverse momenta $p_{T}^{assoc} > 1.2$ ~GeV/$c$ associated with $γ_{dir}$~ and $π^{0}$~(for triggers $|η| < $0.9, 12 $< p_{T}^{trig} <$ 20 GeV/$c$) in central Au+Au collisions are compared with p+p collisions. The $z_{T}$ ($= \frac{p_{T}^{assoc}}{p_{T}^{trig}}$) dependence, now extending down to $z_{T}$=0.1, of the suppression of the away-side associated yields is presented. We observed that the suppression of away-side associated yields tends to vanish at lower \zT. The dependence of the suppression on both $p_{T}^{assoc}$ and $p_{T}^{trig}$ is also discussed. Finally, these results are compared with various model predictions.

## Key claim

The suppression of away-side jet-like correlated yields in central Au+Au collisions relative to p+p collisions, measured via direct-photon+hadron and pion+hadron azimuthal correlations, tends to vanish at low \(z_T\) (below about 0.3), and the suppression for direct-photon-triggered and pion-triggered yields is similar within uncertainties.

## Method

The STAR experiment measured azimuthal correlations of charged hadrons (mid-rapidity, \(|\eta|<1\), \(p_T^{assoc}>1.2\) GeV/\(c\)) triggered by either direct photons or \(\pi^0\) mesons (trigger \(|\eta|<0.9\), \(12<p_T^{trig}<20\) GeV/\(c\)) in central Au+Au (0-12% centrality) and p+p collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV. Direct-photon and \(\pi^0\) triggers were separated using a transverse shower profile (TSP) analysis of clusters in the Barrel Electromagnetic Calorimeter and Barrel Shower Maximum Detector. The away-side per-trigger yields were extracted after subtracting the combinatorial background (including elliptic flow modulation in Au+Au) and correcting for pair acceptance, and the medium modification factor \(I_{AA}\) was computed as the ratio of per-trigger yields in Au+Au to those in p+p.

## Key numbers

* Integrated luminosity: 2.8 nb\(^{-1}\) for Au+Au collisions and 23 pb\(^{-1}\) for p+p collisions.
* Trigger transverse momentum range: \(12 < p_T^{trig} < 20\) GeV/\(c\) for both \(\gamma_{dir}\) and \(\pi^0\) triggers.
* Associated charged hadron threshold: \(p_T^{assoc} > 1.2\) GeV/\(c\).
* The fraction of direct photons in the \(\gamma_{rich}\) trigger sample (\(1-R\)) was 40% for p+p and 70% for central Au+Au collisions.
* The \(z_T\) range extended down to \(z_T = 0.1\).
* At low \(z_T\) (0.1 \(< z_T <\) 0.3), both \(I_{AA}^{\gamma_{dir}}\) and \(I_{AA}^{\pi^0}\) show less suppression than at high \(z_T\).
* \(I_{AA}^{\gamma_{dir}}\) shows no dependence on \(p_T^{trig}\) in the range 8–20 GeV/\(c\) for the \(0.3 < z_T < 0.4\) bin.

## Context

This work extends previous STAR measurements (STAR_GJet) of direct-photon+hadron and pion+hadron correlations to lower \(z_T\) (down to 0.1) to probe the behavior of the medium modification factor at low fragment momentum. The results are compared with theoretical model predictions from Qin and Wang, neither of which includes redistribution of lost energy to lower-\(p_T\) jet fragments. The similar suppression observed for direct-photon- and pion-triggered yields constrains models of parton energy loss and its path-length and color-factor dependence in the quark-gluon plasma.
