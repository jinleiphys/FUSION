# A Perspective on Pentaquarks

> Recent discoveries of manifestly exotic, narrow baryon resonances present a fundamental challenge for our theoretical understanding of low-energy QCD. This is a brief survey of their interpretation, emphasizing the possibility that diquark correlations are centrally involved. Many predictions and suggestions for future directions follow from that idea.

## Key claim

The observed narrow pentaquark resonances can be understood within QCD without modifying the theory, provided one abandons the naive uncorrelated quark model in favor of a picture where strong diquark correlations in the color antitriplet, flavor antisymmetric, spin-zero channel are centrally involved; the authors acknowledge this interpretation is "by no means established or universally accepted" and is "readily falsifiable."

## Method

The authors survey the experimental evidence for the Theta+(1540) and cascade Xi resonances, then critique three theoretical approaches: kaon-nucleon molecular models, the chiral soliton (Skyrme) model, and the naive uncorrelated quark model. They develop a diquark model where two diquarks in the color 3-bar, flavor 6-bar representation are coupled in a relative p-wave to an antiquark, constructing explicit Lorentz-covariant interpolating fields for spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 pentaquark states.

## Key numbers

- Theta+(1540) mass: 1540 MeV, with width Gamma < 15 MeV
- Cascade Xi resonances: masses close to 1860 MeV (Xi--, Xi-, Xi0)
- For a simple attractive potential with range 1 fermi, a p-wave resonance 100 MeV above threshold would have width > 175 MeV; to obtain width ~10 MeV would require range ~0.05 fermi
- The light scalar meson nonet (f0(600), kappa(800), f0(980), a0(980)) lies in the 1100-1500 MeV range where conventional q-qbar mesons would be expected

## Context

This paper extends the diquark correlation ideas previously applied to scalar meson spectroscopy and color superconductivity at high baryon density, and directly addresses the pentaquark discoveries by Nakano (2003), Barmin (2003), Stepanyan (2003), and the NA49 collaboration (2003). It contrasts with the chiral soliton predictions of Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (which predicted a much heavier Xi-- and a 27-plet isovector analog of the Theta that has not been found), and with the uncorrelated quark model calculations of Strottman (1979). The work enables lattice QCD studies using the specific interpolating fields constructed here, and suggests searches for narrow charmed and bottom pentaquarks that might be stable against strong decay.
