# Excited Baryon Structure Using Exclusive Reactions with CLAS12

> Studying excited nucleon structure through exclusive electroproduction reactions is an important avenue for exploring the nature of the non-perturbative strong interaction. Electrocouplings for $N^*$ states in the mass range below 1.8~GeV have been determined from analyses of CLAS $πN$, $ηN$, and $ππN$ data. This work made it clear that consistency of independent analyses of exclusive channels with different couplings and non-resonant backgrounds but the same $N^*$ electro-excitation amplitudes, is essential to have confidence in the extracted results. In terms of hadronic coupling, many high-lying $N^*$ states preferentially decay through the $ππN$ channel instead of $πN$. Data from the $KY$ channels will therefore be critical to provide an independent analysis to compare the extracted electrocouplings for the high-lying $N^*$ states against those determined from the $πN$ and $ππN$ channels. A program to study excited $N^*$ decays to non-strange and strange exclusive final states using CLAS12 will measure differential cross sections to be used as input to extract the $γ_vNN^*$ transition form factors for the most prominent $N^*$ states in the range of invariant energy $W$ up 3~GeV in the virtually unexplored domain of momentum transfers $Q^2$ up to 12~GeV$^2$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that extracting electrocoupling amplitudes for excited nucleon states from multiple independent exclusive channels (including strange final states) is essential for reliable results, and that the CLAS12 program will extend these studies to momentum transfers up to 12 GeV², where quark degrees of freedom are expected to dominate and the meson-baryon cloud contributions become small.

## Method

The CLAS and CLAS12 programs measure differential cross sections and spin observables for exclusive electroproduction reactions (πN, ηN, ππN, K⁺Λ, K⁺Σ⁰) over broad kinematic ranges. These data are used as input to advanced reaction models that extract the γᵥNN* electrocoupling amplitudes (A₁/₂, A₃/₂, S₁/₂) for the dominant s-channel N* resonances. The consistency of results from independent analyses of channels with different couplings and non-resonant backgrounds provides model-independent validation of the extracted amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Electrocouplings for N* states below 1.8 GeV have been extracted from CLAS data for Q² up to 5 GeV² (πN channels), up to 4 GeV² (ηp channel), and up to 1.5 GeV² (π⁺π⁻p channel)
- CLAS KY electroproduction data span Q² from 0.5 to 4.5 GeV² and W from 1.6 to 3.0 GeV
- CLAS12 will measure up to Q² = 12 GeV², the highest photon virtualities ever probed in exclusive electroproduction reactions
- CLAS12 experiments will use an 11 GeV electron beam on liquid hydrogen at luminosity 1 × 10³⁵ cm⁻²s⁻¹
- The meson-baryon dressing amplitudes are maximal for Q² < 1 GeV², with quark degrees of freedom expected to fully dominate for Q² > 5 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the CLAS N* program (Mokeev 2013, 2015) that extracted electrocouplings for low-lying states like Δ(1232)3/2⁺, N(1440)1/2⁺, N(1520)3/2⁻, and N(1535)1/2⁻ from πN and ππN channels. The CLAS12 program will probe the transition from confinement to perturbative QCD domains, enabling comparisons with Lattice QCD and Dyson-Schwinger Equation calculations (Roberts, Bhagwat) to study dressed quark mass functions and confinement dynamics. The KY channels provide an isospin filter (K⁺Λ couples only to N* states, K⁺Σ⁰ couples to both N* and Δ* states) for independent extraction of electrocouplings for high-lying states above 1.6 GeV that preferentially decay through ππN.
