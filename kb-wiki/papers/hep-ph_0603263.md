# Dimension-two gluon condensate from large-Nc Regge models

> It is shown that in the large-N_c limit radial Regge trajectories give rise in a natural way to the presence of the dimension-2 gluon condensate, <A^2>, in meson correlators. We match these models to QCD and provide estimates for <A^2> in terms of other physical quantities. In particular, in the simplest strictly linear radial Regge model with equal residues <A^2> is proportional to the pion decay constant squared. However, the linear model fails a consistency condition based on matching the short- and long-distance string tensions, nor reproduces the phenomenological values of the gluon condensates. On the contrary, in Regge models departing from strict linearity one may reproduce both the consistency condition and the signs of condensates. We demonstrate this in a simple explicit model.

## Key claim

Radial Regge trajectories in the large-\(N_c\) limit naturally generate a dimension-2 gluon condensate \(\langle A^2 \rangle\) in meson correlators, but the simplest strictly linear Regge model with equal residues fails a consistency condition based on matching short- and long-distance string tensions and cannot reproduce phenomenological values of the gluon condensates; only Regge models that depart from strict linearity can satisfy both the consistency condition and the correct signs of condensates.

## Method

The authors match the operator product expansion (OPE) of QCD, including a non-standard \(1/Q^2\) power correction in the \(V+A\) meson correlator, to large-\(N_c\) meson correlators saturated by sharp, non-interacting meson states on radial Regge trajectories. They enforce the two Weinberg sum rules and impose a novel consistency condition equating the short-distance string tension \(\sigma_0\) (derived from the tachyonic gluon mass) to the long-distance string tension \(\sigma\) (from the Regge slope). Two explicit models are compared: a strictly linear Regge model with constant residues for all states, and a modified model where the lowest \(\rho\) mass is shifted while keeping universal residues and slopes for higher states.

## Key numbers

- In the strictly linear model, the dimension-2 condensate is \(-\alpha_S \lambda^2 / \pi = 0.3\ \text{GeV}^2\), compared to the phenomenological value \(0.12\ \text{GeV}^2\) from Chetyrkin (1998) and Zakharov (2005).
- The strictly linear model gives \(\sqrt{\sigma_0} = 782\ \text{MeV}\) from the short-distance string tension, while the long-distance string tension from the Regge slope is \(\sqrt{\sigma} = 546\ \text{MeV}\) (using \(F = 154\ \text{MeV}\)), violating the consistency condition.
- In the modified model, the consistency condition \(\sigma = \sigma_0\) is satisfied at \(\sqrt{\sigma} = 497\ \text{MeV}\).
- The modified model yields \(L_{10} = -5.2 \times 10^{-3}\) (experimental: \(-5.5 \pm 0.7 \times 10^{-3}\)) and the electromagnetic pion mass splitting \(m^2_{\pi^\pm} - m^2_{\pi^0} = (34.4\ \text{MeV})^2\) (experimental: \((35.5\ \text{MeV})^2\)).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Afonin, Andrianov, Andrianov, and Espriu (2004) by explicitly including the dimension-2 gluon condensate in the OPE matching, which earlier Regge-model studies such as Golterman (2001), Beane (2001), and Simonov (2001) had systematically ignored. The analysis enables a consistency check between short-distance and long-distance string tensions, and shows that the parameters of low-lying meson states encode the same information as the QCD condensates, though the sensitivity of condensate values to spectral parameters makes precise determination difficult.
