# Chemical factors in canonical statistical models for relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We study the effect of enforcing exact conservation of charges in statistical models of particle production for systems as large as those relevant to relativistic heavy ion collisions. By using a numerical method developed for small systems, we have been able to approach the large volume limit keeping the exact canonical treatment of all relevant charges, namely baryon number, strangeness and electric charge. Hence, we hereby give the information needed in a hadron gas model whether the canonical treatment is necessary or not in actual cases. Comparison between calculations and experimental particle multiplicities is shown. Also, a discussion on relative strangeness chemical equilibrium is given.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for relativistic heavy ion collisions, the full canonical treatment enforcing exact conservation of baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge is only necessary for systems with baryon number up to about 10; for larger systems, the grand-canonical treatment of baryon number and electric charge is sufficient, while exact strangeness conservation is needed only at lower energies such as GSI SIS.

## Method

The authors developed a numerical method to compute the canonical partition function for large baryon numbers by analytically eliminating the baryon number integration using the fact that no elementary hadron has baryon number greater than 1, reducing the triple integral to a double integral over strangeness and electric charge phases. They then computed mean particle numbers as ratios of canonical partition functions with different quantum numbers, defining chemical factors that quantify the deviation from grand-canonical results. The method was applied to fixed freeze-out temperatures and baryon densities taken from previous fits to experimental data, without performing new fits.

## Key numbers

- Canonical baryon chemical factor relative difference from grand-canonical limit decreases from 2.1% at baryon number B=2 to 0.7% at B=10 at GSI SIS energies (T~50 MeV, mu_B~800 MeV)
- At AGS energies (T=120 MeV, normal nuclear density), relative difference between canonical baryon chemical factor and grand-canonical limit decreases from 23% at B=2 to 1% at B=40
- Relative difference between full canonical and strangeness-canonical results for kaon production at GSI energies is 13% for B=2, decreasing to 4% for B=10 and to 1% for B=40
- At SPS energies (T=160 MeV, n_B=0.17 fm^-3), total relative enhancement from canonical B=Q=2 to grand-canonical with Q/B=0.4 is 30% for Lambda baryons, 241% for Xi baryons, and 918% for Omega baryons
- The K+/pi+ enhancement ratio from pp to central Pb-Pb at SPS with T=160 MeV and n_B=0.17 fm^-3 is 1.47 with gamma_S=1, while the experimental ratio is about 2

## Context

This work extends the canonical statistical model calculations of Becattini (2000) and Cleymans (1999) from small systems to the large baryon numbers relevant for heavy ion collisions, using a numerical method that reaches baryon numbers up to O(100) where previous methods were limited to B~20. The results enable assessment of when the simpler grand-canonical or strangeness-canonical approximations are adequate, showing that for central Au-Au and Pb-Pb reactions at AGS and SPS energies even strangeness can be handled grand-canonically, while at GSI SIS energies exact strangeness conservation is still needed. The paper also discusses the gamma_S parameter for relative strangeness chemical equilibrium, noting that the weak dependence of K/pi ratios on thermal parameters provides evidence for incomplete strangeness equilibration at SPS, consistent with previous findings by Becattini (2001) and Keranen (2001).
