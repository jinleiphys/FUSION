# Chiral Doubling of Heavy-Light Hadrons: BaBar 2317 MeV and CLEO 2463 MeV Discoveries

> We point out that the very recent discoveries of BaBar (2317) and CLEO II (2460) are consistent with the general pattern of spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry in hadrons built of heavy and light quarks, as originally suggested by us in 1992, and independently by Bardeen and Hill in 1993. The splitting between the chiral doublers follows from a mixing between the light constituent quark mass and the velocity of the heavy quark, and vanishes for a zero constituent quark mass. The general strictures of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking constrain the axial charges in the chiral multiplet, and yield a mass splitting between the chiral doublers of about 345 MeV when the pion coupling to the doublers is half its coupling to a free quark. The chiral corrections are small. This phenomenon is generic and extends to all heavy-light hadrons. We predict the mass splitting for the chiral doublers of the excited mesons (D1,D2). We suggest that the heavy-light doubling can be used to address issues of chiral symmetry restoration in dense and/or hot hadronic matter. In particular, the relative splitting between D and D* mesons and their chiral partners decreases in matter, with consequences on charmonium evolution at RHIC.

# Chiral Doubling of Heavy-Light Hadrons: BaBar 2317 MeV and CLEO 2463 MeV Discoveries

## Key claim

The newly discovered narrow charmed meson states at 2317 MeV (BaBar) and 2463 MeV (CLEO) are the predicted chiral partners of the known D_s and D_s* mesons, with the mass splitting between parity partners arising from the mixing between the light constituent quark mass and the heavy quark velocity, vanishing only when the constituent quark mass goes to zero. The observed splitting of about 345 MeV is consistent with the pion coupling to the chiral multiplet being half its coupling to a free constituent quark.

## Method

The authors derived the heavy-light effective action to one-loop order in a constituent quark model (specifically the effective chiral quark model of Manohar and Georgi), using a momentum cutoff regularization to separate hard modes of order m_h from soft modes. They computed the two-point functions for the (0^-,1^-) H-multiplet and the (0^+,1^+) G-multiplet from one-loop diagrams involving light constituent quarks of mass Sigma and heavy non-relativistic fields, finding that the induced chiral mass flips sign between the two parity multiplets due to the gamma_5 difference in their field definitions. Generalized Goldberger-Treiman relations were derived from chiral Ward identities to constrain the axial charges and pion couplings in the chiral multiplet.

## Key numbers

- Mass splitting between D_s(2317) and D_s^+: 348.3 MeV/c^2 (BaBar); 350.4 ± 1.2 ± 1.0 MeV/c^2 (CLEO II)
- Mass splitting between D_s*(2463) and D_s*^+: 351.6 ± 1.7 ± 1.0 MeV/c^2 (CLEO II)
- Constituent quark mass Sigma ≈ 345 MeV/c^2, used as the scale for the chiral splitting
- Predicted masses for excited chiral partners: m(tilde(D)_s1) = 2721 ± 10 MeV; m(tilde(D)_s2) = 2758 ± 10 MeV
- Predicted splitting for non-strange D meson chiral partners: about 368 MeV
- Predicted splitting for B_s and B_s* chiral partners: 323 MeV heavier; for B and B*: 345 MeV heavier
- Axial couplings from one-loop calculation: g_H ≈ g_G ≈ 1/3

## Context

This work extends the authors' original prediction from Nowak, Rho, Zahed (1992) and the independent work of Bardeen and Hill (1993) that spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking requires chiral partners for heavy-light hadrons. The results contradict the initial press reports that the BaBar discovery disagreed with theoretical predictions. The chiral doubling framework provides a natural explanation for the observed narrow widths (the isospin-violating decay mode tilde(D) → D pi^0 is kinematically allowed while the natural kaon mode is shut off) and enables predictions for excited states and bottom meson partners. The authors suggest that the relative splitting between D and D* mesons and their chiral partners should decrease in hot/dense matter, with implications for charmonium evolution at RHIC, and that this can serve as a probe of chiral symmetry restoration.
