# How much nuclear physics do we need, to understand the neutrino nucleus cross section ?

> Over the past two decades, electron scattering experiments have clearly exposed the limits of the independent particle model description of atomic nuclei. I will briefly outline the dynamics leading to the appearance of strong correlation effects, and their impact on the electroweak nuclear cross sections in the impulse approximation regime.

## Key claim

Dynamical nucleon-nucleon correlations, long known to be critical for electron-nucleus scattering, also significantly affect neutrino-nucleus cross sections; the answer to how much nuclear physics is needed depends on the observable, but for extracting nucleon properties like the axial form factor from nuclear data, a fully quantitative control of nuclear effects is required.

## Method

The paper reviews the formalism of nuclear many-body theory (NMBT), in which the nucleus is described as a collection of pointlike nucleons interacting via realistic two- and three-nucleon potentials. The hole spectral function, computed from correlated basis functions or stochastic methods for nuclear matter and extended to finite nuclei via the local density approximation, is used within the impulse approximation to compute inclusive electron-nucleus and charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections. Final-state interactions are included using the eikonal approximation based on measured NN scattering amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: \(\rho_0 \sim 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), nearly constant and independent of mass number \(A\).
- Maximum neutron star mass predicted by the noninteracting Fermi gas model: \(\sim 0.8\) M\(_\odot\), compared to observed values \(\sim 1.4\) M\(_\odot\).
- The inclusive electron scattering cross section off \(^{16}\)O at beam energies 700–1200 MeV and scattering angle 32\(^\circ\) is reproduced with accuracy better than \(\sim 10\%\) in the quasi-elastic peak region using the spectral function approach.
- The total quasi-elastic cross section for \(\nu_e + ^{16}O \rightarrow e^- + X\) shows a \(\sim 20\%\) overall change due to nuclear effects when comparing the spectral function approach to the free-nucleon stationary target model.

## Context

This work extends the application of nuclear many-body theory, previously validated against electron scattering data from experiments such as those at LNF (reported in Anghinolfi et al.), to neutrino-nucleus interactions. It directly contrasts the independent particle Fermi gas model, which fails to reproduce the measured electron scattering cross sections and neutron star masses, with the correlated spectral function approach of Benhar et al. (2005, 2007). The formalism enables improved Monte Carlo simulations for neutrino oscillation experiments, particularly for energy reconstruction in charged-current quasi-elastic events, and is being extended to pion production and deep inelastic scattering regimes.
