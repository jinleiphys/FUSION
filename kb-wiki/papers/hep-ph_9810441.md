# Isospin-Breaking Vector Meson Decay Constants From Continuous Families of Finite Energy Sum Rules

> The isospin-breaking vector meson decay constants are determined from a QCD sum rule analysis of the vector current correlator $<O| T(V^3_μV^8_ν)| O>$, using a recently proposed implementation of the finite energy sum rule approach. The analysis employs the three-loop version of the OPE, and two different families of weight functions. It is shown that the requirement of consistency between results obtained using these two different weight families leads to a rather good determination of the parameter describing the deviation of the D=6 condensate term in the OPE from its vacuum saturation value, and that the ability to determine this value has non-trivial numerical consequences to the analysis. The phenomenological relevance of the results to the extraction of the strange quark mass and the determination of the $6^{th}$ order chiral low energy constant, $Q$, is also briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The isospin-breaking vector meson decay constants can be determined from a QCD sum rule analysis of the isospin-breaking vector current correlator, using a recently proposed implementation of finite energy sum rules (FESR) with two different families of weight functions; the requirement of consistency between results from these two weight families leads to a determination of the D=6 condensate deviation parameter, which has non-trivial numerical consequences for the analysis.

## Method

The analysis employs the three-loop version of the operator product expansion (OPE) for the vector current correlator ⟨0|T(V³_μ V⁸_ν)|0⟩, using two different families of weight functions in a continuous family of finite energy sum rules (FESR) approach. The sum rules are based on Cauchy's theorem with a circular contour of radius s₀, where the OPE is used on the circular part and spectral data/ansatz on the hadronic side. The requirement of consistency between results obtained using the two different weight families determines the parameter describing the deviation of the D=6 condensate term from its vacuum saturation value.

## Key numbers

- The central value for the real part of the ρ-ω mixing self-energy from earlier analyses is \(\tilde{\Pi}_{\rho\omega}(m_\rho^2) = -3844 \pm 271\) MeV² (obtained under the assumption G=0).
- Values of \(\tilde{\Pi}_{\rho\omega}\) between -4000 and -8000 MeV² are allowed by present experimental data, with a central value of approximately -6800 MeV².
- The mixing parameter G (ratio of 1PI ωππ coupling to ρππ coupling) has a central value of approximately 0.1, with G=0 only 2.5σ distant.
- Using central values from fits, F³_ρ is less than F^EM_ρ by between 0.3% and 3.8%, and F⁸_ω is greater than F^EM_ω by between 2.6% and 24.6%.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to isospin-breaking vector meson decay constants, building on the FESR methodology and the three-loop OPE framework. The analysis is motivated by the need to correct for isospin-breaking effects in extracting the strange quark mass (as in Narison's work) and the 6th order chiral low-energy constant Q (as in Gasser and Leutwyler (1996)), where the difference of spectral functions ρ³³-ρ⁸⁸ enhances the relative importance of isospin breaking. The results enable improved precision in tests of CVC (comparing ρ³³ with charged isovector spectral functions from hadronic τ decays, as in the ALEPH collaboration analysis) and in determinations of the strange quark mass and the chiral low-energy constant Q.
