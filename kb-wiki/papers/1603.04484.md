# Shape coexistence and the role of axial asymmetry in $^{72}$Ge

> The quadrupole collectivity of low-lying states and the anomalous behavior of the $0^+_2$ and $2^+_3$ levels in $^{72}$Ge are investigated via projectile multi-step Coulomb excitation with GRETINA and CHICO-2. A total of forty six $E2$ and $M1$ matrix elements connecting fourteen low-lying levels were determined using the least-squares search code, gosia. Evidence for triaxiality and shape coexistence, based on the model-independent shape invariants deduced from the Kumar-Cline sum rule, is presented. These are interpreted using a simple two-state mixing model as well as multistate mixing calculations carried out within the framework of the triaxial rotor model. The results represent a significant milestone towards the understanding of the unusual structure of this nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper presents evidence that the low-lying states of $^{72}$Ge can be described by the coexistence of two triaxially-deformed configurations, with the ground-state band exhibiting a triaxial deformation of $\gamma \sim 30^\circ$, and that the $0^+_1$ and $0^+_2$ states have essentially the same $\left<Q^2\right>$ value, which is interpreted as maximum mixing between two distinct intrinsic configurations rather than identical shapes.

## Method

The authors performed projectile multi-step Coulomb excitation of $^{72}$Ge on a $^{208}$Pb target at 301 MeV using the ATLAS facility, detecting $\gamma$ rays with the GRETINA array and scattered particles with the CHICO-2 position-sensitive detector array. A total of 46 $E2$ and $M1$ matrix elements connecting 14 low-lying levels were extracted using the least-squares search code gosia, with data partitioned into seven angular subsets and supplemented by known spectroscopic data (lifetimes, branching ratios, $E2/M1$ mixing ratios) as constraints. Model-independent shape invariants were deduced from the Kumar-Cline sum rule, and the results were interpreted using a two-state mixing model and multi-state mixing calculations within the triaxial rotor model.

## Key numbers

- $0_1^+ \rightarrow 2_1^+$ reduced $E2$ matrix element: 0.457(4) $e$b
- $2_1^+ \rightarrow 2_1^+$ static quadrupole moment: -0.16$_{-0.02}^{+0.07}$ $e$b
- $2_2^+ \rightarrow 2_2^+$ static quadrupole moment: 0.179$_{-0.06}^{+0.03}$ $e$b
- $0_2^+ \rightarrow 2_3^+$ reduced $E2$ matrix element: 0.279$_{-0.004}^{+0.002}$ $e$b (determined for the first time)
- $E3$ matrix element for the $3^-_1$ state: 0.199(4) $e$b$^{3/2}$
- Two-state mixing model: unmixed basis states initially separated by $\Delta_{int} = 32$ keV are shifted by 330 keV by a mixing matrix element of 345 keV; wavefunction of mixed $0_1^+$ state contains amplitude $\cos\theta_0 = 0.72(3)$ of unmixed ground-state wavefunction, indicating $\cos^2\theta_0 = 0.52(4)$
- $E0$ monopole strength: $\rho^2(E0; 0^+_2 \rightarrow 0^+_1) \times 10^3 = 9.18(2)$
- Triaxial rotor model parameters: $Q_0 = 1.45$ $e$b, $\gamma = 27.0^\circ$, $\Gamma = -23.2^\circ$
- Sum of $2^+$ quadrupole moments for $2^+_1$ and $2^+_2$: 0.02$_{-0.06}^{+0.08}$ $e$b; sum over all three $2^+$ states: 0.00$_{-0.15}^{+0.08}$ $e$b

## Context

This work extends earlier Coulomb excitation measurements on $^{72}$Ge by Kotlinski et al. (1990) and provides a significantly more extensive set of $E2$ matrix elements, enabling the first model-independent shape invariants for any nucleus with a first-excited $0^+$ state. The results are compared with the triaxial rotor model (TRM), the symmetric rotor model, and the Davydov-Filippov model, with the TRM providing the best description. The paper addresses the long-standing puzzle of the $0^+_2$ first excited state in $^{72}$Ge, which is far from closed shells, unlike other known examples such as $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, $^{68}$Ni, $^{90}$Zr, and Pb isotopes. The findings enable future work to clarify the nature of the $2^+_3$ state, which may require introduction of gamma softness or two-phonon character beyond the present triaxial rotor description.
