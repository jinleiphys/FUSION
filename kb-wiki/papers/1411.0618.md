# Entropic destruction of heavy quarkonium in non-Abelian plasma from holography

> Lattice QCD indicates a large amount of entropy associated with the heavy quark-antiquark pair immersed in the quark-gluon plasma. This entropy grows as a function of the inter-quark distance giving rise to an entropic force that can be very effective in dissociating the bound quarkonium states. In addition, the lattice data show a very sharp peak in the heavy quark-antiquark entropy at the deconfinement transition. Since the quark-gluon plasma around the deconfinement transition is strongly coupled, we employ the holographic correspondence to study the entropy associated with the heavy quark-antiquark pair in two theories: i) ${\cal{N}}=4$ supersymmetric Yang-Mills and ii) a confining Yang-Mills theory obtained by compactification on a Kaluza-Klein circle. In both cases we find the entropy growing with the inter-quark distance and evaluate the effect of the corresponding entropic forces. In the case ii), we find a sharp peak in the entropy near the deconfinement transition, in agreement with the lattice QCD results. This peak in our holographic description arises because the heavy quark pair acts as an eyewitness of the black hole formation in the bulk -- the process that describes the deconfinement transition. In terms of the boundary theory, this entropy likely emerges from the entanglement of a "long string" connecting the quark and antiquark with the rest of the system.

## Key claim

The holographic calculation shows that the sharp peak in heavy quark-antiquark entropy near the deconfinement transition, observed in lattice QCD, arises from the entropy of a long fundamental string at the bottom of the confining geometry that is absorbed into a black hole horizon at the deconfinement transition; this peak is absent in conformal N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory but emerges in a confining Yang-Mills theory obtained by Kaluza-Klein compactification.

## Method

The authors used the holographic gauge/gravity correspondence to compute the free energy and entropy of a static heavy quark-antiquark pair in two strongly coupled gauge theories: (i) N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory, using the AdS5-Schwarzschild black hole geometry, and (ii) a confining Yang-Mills theory obtained by compactifying five-dimensional SYM on a Kaluza-Klein circle with anti-periodic boundary conditions for fermions, using the black D4-brane metric. The entropy was derived from the on-shell Nambu-Goto action of the fundamental string connecting the quark and antiquark in the dual gravity background, as a function of inter-quark distance L and temperature T.

## Key numbers

- Lattice QCD entropy at the peak: S_infinity(T=T_c) = 16.5, corresponding to exp(S_infinity) = 1.5 x 10^7 states (Kaczmarek 2005)
- Debye screening length at T_c: L_D = 0.3 fm (Kaczmarek 2005)
- Entropy density at T_c = 155 MeV: s_c = (5.34 +/- 0.65/0.62) T_c^3 (HotQCD Collaboration 2014)
- Critical distance for screening in N=4 SYM: L > c/T with c = 0.240
- Critical distance for screening in confining YM: L > c'/T with c' = 0.278
- Holographic entropy at large L in N=4 SYM: S_str^(2) = sqrt(2 lambda), with lambda = 5.5 giving S_infinity = 3.3
- Holographic entropy at large L in confining YM at T = 1.3 T_c: S_infinity = (8 lambda/9) T/T_c = 6.3 for lambda = 5.5
- Integral over entropy peak: holographic model gives 0.8 GeV, lattice data gives 0.6 GeV (using T_c = 200 MeV)
- Strong coupling dissociation distance in N=4 SYM at T = 200 MeV: x_0 = 0.18 fm

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Kharzeev (2014) that entropic forces from lattice QCD entropy data can dissociate quarkonium, by providing a microscopic holographic derivation. It contrasts with the Debye screening scenario (Matsui 1986) and thermal activation (Shuryak 1978, Bhanot 1979) which cannot explain the sharp entropy peak. The holographic confining Yang-Mills model (Witten 1998) reproduces the qualitative features of lattice QCD results (Kaczmarek 2002, 2005; Petreczky 2004), including the entropy peak at deconfinement, while conformal N=4 SYM does not. This enables a semi-quantitative description of quarkonium dissociation near the deconfinement transition and suggests that the entropy originates from entanglement of a "long string" connecting the quark and antiquark with the rest of the system.
