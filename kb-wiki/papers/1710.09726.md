# Study of Lambda polarization at RHIC BES and LHC energies

> In hydrodynamic approach to relativistic heavy ion collisions, hadrons with nonzero spin, produced out of the hydrodynamic medium, can acquire polarization via spin-vorticity thermodynamic coupling mechanism. The hydrodynamical quantity steering the polarization is the thermal vorticity, that is minus the antisymmetric part of the gradient of four-temperature field. Based on this mechanism there have been several calculations in hydrodynamic and non-hydrodynamic models for non-central heavy ion collisions in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan energy range, showing that the amount of polarization of produced $Λ$ hyperons ranges from few percents to few permille, and decreases with collision energy. We report on an extension of our existing calculation of global $Λ$ polarization in UrQMD+vHLLE model to full RHIC and LHC energies, and discuss the component of polarization along the beam direction, which is the dominant one at high energies.

## Key claim

The global polarization of Λ hyperons decreases with collision energy from RHIC to LHC, but the dominant polarization component at high energies shifts to the direction along the beam axis, which shows a much milder energy dependence and remains detectable at LHC energies.

## Method

The authors used a hybrid transport model combining the UrQMD hadron/string cascade for the initial state with a 3+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamic expansion (vHLLE). At a particlization hypersurface defined by a critical energy density of 0.5 GeV/fm³, Λ hyperons and their decay resonances acquire polarization via the spin-vorticity coupling mechanism, where the relevant quantity is the thermal vorticity (the antisymmetric part of the gradient of the four-temperature field). The calculations were extended to LHC energies using a Monte Carlo Glauber initial state with parametrized rapidity dependence.

## Key numbers

- Global Λ polarization in 20-50% central Au-Au collisions decreases from about 2% at √sNN = 7.7 GeV to 0.2% at √sNN = 200 GeV
- Resonance feed-down (including cascade processes X → Σ⁰ → Λ) results in a 15% reduction of the polarization of all Λ at all collision energies considered
- The second-order Fourier harmonic f₂ of the beam-direction polarization component for pT = 3 GeV Λ reaches 1% at 2.76 TeV LHC energy
- The f₂ harmonic decreases by only 35% between √sNN = 7.7 and 200 GeV, compared to the global polarization which decreases by almost an order of magnitude over the same range

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of global Λ polarization in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan range (Karpenko, 2016) to full RHIC and LHC energies using a Monte Carlo Glauber initial state. The results are consistent with the first observation of nonzero global Λ polarization reported by the STAR collaboration (2017) and with calculations from other hydrodynamic models and the AMPT model. The finding that the longitudinal polarization component has a quadrupole structure linked to anisotropic transverse flow enables experimental measurements via pair correlations that are independent of the event plane orientation, and the covariance functions are qualitatively consistent with results from Pang (2016).
