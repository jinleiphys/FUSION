# On the role of secondary pions in spallation targets

> We use particle-transport simulations to show that secondary pions play a crucial role for the development of the hadronic cascade and therefore for the production of neutrons and photons from thick spallation targets. In particular, for the n_TOF lead spallation target, irradiated with 20 GeV/c protons, neutral pions are involved in the production of ~90% of the high-energy photons; charged pions participate in ~40% of the integral neutron yield. Nevertheless, photon and neutron yields are shown to be relatively insensitive to large changes of the average pion multiplicity in the individual spallation reactions. We characterize this robustness as a peculiar property of hadronic cascades in thick targets.

## Key claim

Secondary pions play a crucial role in the development of the hadronic cascade in thick spallation targets, with neutral pions involved in approximately 90% of high-energy photon production and charged pions participating in about 40% of the integral neutron yield for the n_TOF lead spallation target irradiated with 20 GeV/c protons; however, photon and neutron yields are relatively insensitive to large changes in the average pion multiplicity in individual spallation reactions, a robustness characterized as a peculiar property of hadronic cascades in thick targets.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo particle-transport simulations using the Geant4 toolkit with the Liège Intranuclear Cascade model (INCL) coupled to de-excitation codes, comparing the extended version (INCL++) that includes multipion collisions against the older version (INCL4.6) without multipion extension. They simulated the n_TOF lead spallation target irradiated by 20 GeV/c protons and analyzed the contributions of secondary pions to neutron and photon yields, also comparing their model predictions against experimental data from the HARP experiment and Chemakin data for pion-production cross sections on lead and gold targets.

## Key numbers

- Neutral pions are involved in the production of approximately 90% of the high-energy photons from the n_TOF lead spallation target irradiated with 20 GeV/c protons
- Charged pions participate in approximately 40% of the integral neutron yield from the same target
- The Binary Cascade (BIC) and Bertini models overestimate the experimentally evaluated neutron production by as much as 70% compared to INCL-based simulations
- The older INCL model (without multipion extension) saturates around 5 GeV projectile energy, never exceeding approximately 1 pion per reaction, while the extended model yields an almost linear increase up to 20 GeV
- HARP data cover incident momenta of 3, 5, 8, and 12 GeV/c for proton- and pion-induced reactions on various targets
- Chemakin data provide double-differential pion-emission cross sections for 12.3 and 17.5 GeV/c protons on gold targets

## Context

This work extends previous Geant4 characterization studies of the n_TOF neutron and photon fluxes by Lo Meo (2015) and Lerendegui-Marco, which had shown large relative differences between physics lists that were suspected to relate to different treatments of pion production and pion-induced reactions. The paper builds on the IAEA benchmark of spallation models (David, 2007; Leray, 2009) that validated INCL for neutron, proton, and light charged particle production below 3 GeV but devoted little attention to secondary pions. The findings enable improved simulations of spallation neutron sources such as n_TOF and Accelerator-Driven Systems, while highlighting the need for new high-acceptance data on pion production in high-energy proton-induced reactions, including direct measurements of neutral pion production.
