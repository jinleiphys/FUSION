# Delta K, Lambda K, and Sigma K states in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model

> By use of the resonating group method, the Delta K, Lambda K, and Sigma K states are further dynamically studied in the extended chiral SU(3) quark model based on our previous work. Similar to the results given by the original chiral SU(3) quark model, the calculated results here still show that the interactions of Delta K with isospin I=1 and Sigma K with isospin I=1/2 are attractive, which can consequently lead to Delta K and Sigma K quasibound states. When the channel coupling of Lambda K and Sigma K is considered, the calculated phase shifts show a sharp resonance between the thresholds of these two channels with spin-parity J^P=1/2^-.

## Key claim

The extended chiral SU(3) quark model, where the one-gluon exchange (OGE) is nearly replaced by vector-meson exchanges, still predicts attractive interactions in the Delta K (I=1) and Sigma K (I=1/2) channels and a sharp Lambda K-Sigma K resonance with J^P=1/2^-, yielding results qualitatively similar to those from the original chiral SU(3) quark model despite the different short-range interaction mechanisms.

## Method

The authors solved the resonating group method (RGM) equation for the Delta K, Lambda K, and Sigma K systems using the extended chiral SU(3) quark model, which includes scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector meson exchanges between quarks and between quarks and antiquarks. Model parameters were fixed by baryon ground-state masses, deuteron binding energy, and nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts, with two parameter sets corresponding to different vector-meson tensor coupling ratios (f_chv/g_chv = 0 and 2/3). Phase shifts and binding energies were computed in both single-channel and coupled-channel (Lambda K + Sigma K) calculations.

## Key numbers

- Delta K binding energy: 3 MeV (original chiral SU(3) quark model, set I), 20 MeV (extended model set II, f_chv=0), 15 MeV (extended model set III, f_chv=2/3 g_chv)
- Sigma K binding energy: 18 MeV (set I), 44 MeV (set II), 33 MeV (set III)
- Lambda K-Sigma K resonance mass: 1670 MeV (set I), 1646 MeV (set II), 1655 MeV (set III)
- Lambda K-Sigma K resonance width: approximately 5 MeV (set I), approximately 4 MeV (sets II and III)
- Quark masses: m_u = 313 MeV, m_s = 470 MeV for all parameter sets
- Oscillator parameter b_u = 0.5 fm (set I), 0.45 fm (sets II and III)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous dynamical studies of Delta K, Lambda K, and Sigma K states (Huang and Zhang, 2004; 2005) from the original chiral SU(3) quark model to the extended version that includes vector-meson exchanges, following the approach of Dai et al. (2003) and Huang et al. (2005) for nucleon-nucleon and kaon-nucleon systems. The resonance found near 1650 MeV with J^P=1/2^- is discussed in relation to the S_11(1650) nucleon resonance, which has been argued by Kaiser et al. (1995) and Inoue et al. (2002) to be a quasibound Lambda K-Sigma K state on the hadron level, while Schutz et al. (1998) concluded it requires a genuine three-quark component. The results enable future studies incorporating s-channel q-qbar annihilation, couplings to N pi, N eta, N pi pi channels, and genuine three-quark components to determine the exact nature of the resonance.
