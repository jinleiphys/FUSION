# Three-particle scattering amplitudes from lattice QCD

> Lattice QCD already offers the possibility of extracting three-hadron scattering quantities from first principles. In the last few years, significant progress has been achieved in developing and applying the finite-volume three-body formalism. The formalism is now able to treat physically relevant systems of three mesons, including those with resonances, as well as three-body decays. In this talk, I will review the state of the art, and comment on recent applications to lattice QCD data for systems of three pions and kaons.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD can now extract three-hadron scattering quantities from first principles using finite-volume three-body formalism, but the extracted three-particle K-matrix is scheme-dependent and requires solving integral equations to obtain the physical scattering amplitude.

## Method

The finite-volume three-body formalism connects lattice QCD energy levels to infinite-volume scattering quantities through a two-step process: first, a three-particle quantization condition relates the finite-volume spectrum to the two-particle K-matrix (K2) and a scheme-dependent three-particle K-matrix (Kdf,3); second, integral equations map these K-matrices into the physical three-to-three scattering amplitude M3. The formalism has been developed following three approaches: a relativistic effective field theory (RFT), a nonrelativistic effective field theory (NREFT), and a finite-volume unitarity (FVU) approach, which are now understood to be equivalent.

## Key numbers

- Three-particle amplitude at threshold in complex phi4 theory: Tbar = -19(4) x 10^4, extracted from N-particle energy levels with N=2-5
- Energy shift of two-particle ground state: Delta E = E-2m = 4pi a0/(mL^3) + O(L^-4), where a0 is the s-wave scattering length
- Three-body interactions contribute to energy shifts with a relative 1/L^3 suppression compared to the two-body scattering length a0
- In the threshold expansion of Kdf,3 for identical particles, up to quadratic order in Delta = (s-9M_pi^2)/(9M_pi^2), only five terms are present: Kdf,3^iso,0, Kdf,3^iso,1, Kdf,3^iso,2, K_A, and K_B
- For three-pion systems, O(100) energy levels per ensemble were determined across different irreducible representations and frames
- The d-wave three-pion scattering quantity K_B shows chiral behavior: M_pi^2 K_B proportional to (M_pi/F_pi)^6

## Context

This work reviews the state of the art in three-particle finite-volume formalism, extending the foundational two-particle Luscher formalism (Luscher, 1986, 1991) to three-body systems. The formalism has been applied to lattice QCD data for three-pion and three-kaon systems by groups including Blanton et al. (2019, 2021), Fischer et al. (2020), and Mai et al. (2018, 2019, 2021). The review covers recent developments including three-body decays (Muller et al., 2020; Hansen et al., 2021) and the first steps toward studying three-particle resonances such as the h1(1170) and omega(782). Open questions include extending the formalism to particles with spin, multi-channel three-particle processes, and ultimately computing properties of XYZ particles and other exotic hadrons from first principles.
