# Non-Abelian Stokes Theorem and Quark Confinement in SU(3) Yang-Mills Gauge Theory

> We derive a new version of SU(3) non-Abelian Stokes theorem by making use of the coherent state representation on the coset space $SU(3)/(U(1)\times U(1))=F_2$, the flag space. Then we outline a derivation of the area law of the Wilson loop in SU(3) Yang-Mills theory in the maximal Abelian gauge (The detailed exposition will be given in a forthcoming article). This derivation is performed by combining the non-Abelian Stokes theorem with the reformulation of the Yang-Mills theory as a perturbative deformation of a topological field theory recently proposed by one of the authors. Within this framework, we show that the fundamental quark is confined even if $G=SU(3)$ is broken by partial gauge fixing into $H=U(2)$ just as $G$ is broken to $H=U(1) \times U(1)$. An origin of the area law is related to the geometric phase of the Wilczek-Zee holonomy for U(2). Abelian dominance is an immediate byproduct of these results and magnetic monopole plays the dominant role in this derivation.

## Key claim

The authors derive a new version of the SU(3) non-Abelian Stokes theorem using coherent state representation on the flag space \(F_2 = SU(3)/(U(1)\times U(1))\), and outline a derivation of the area law for the Wilson loop in SU(3) Yang-Mills theory in the maximal Abelian gauge, claiming that the fundamental quark is confined even when SU(3) is broken by partial gauge fixing into \(H=U(2)\) just as when broken to \(H=U(1)\times U(1)\). The derivation is presented as a framework combining the non-Abelian Stokes theorem with a reformulation of Yang-Mills theory as a perturbative deformation of a topological field theory, with the detailed exposition promised for a forthcoming article.

## Method

The authors construct coherent states on the coset space \(SU(3)/(U(1)\times U(1)) = F_2\) (the flag space) using the highest-weight state of the fundamental representation, obtaining explicit expressions for the Kähler potential, the inner product, and the invariant measure. They then derive the non-Abelian Stokes theorem for SU(3) by writing the Wilson loop as a path integral over the coset variables, expressing it as an integral over a surface of an Abelian field strength plus a Kähler two-form \(\Omega_K\). The area law is argued by combining this theorem with the reformulation of Yang-Mills theory as a perturbative deformation of a topological field theory, leading to dimensional reduction to a two-dimensional coset nonlinear sigma model (NLSM) on \(F_{N-1}\), where instanton configurations in the NLSM produce the area law via the integral of \(\Omega_K\).

## Key numbers

* The dimension of the representation for SU(3) with Dynkin index \([m,n]\) is given by \(D(m,n) = \frac{1}{2}(m+1)(n+1)(m+n+2)\).
* The Kähler potential for \(F_2\) is \(K(w,\bar w) = \ln[(\Delta_1)^m (\Delta_2)^n]\), where \(\Delta_1 = 1+|w_1|^2+|w_2|^2\) and \(\Delta_2 = 1+|w_3|^2+|w_2-w_1 w_3|^2\).
* The one-loop running coupling in the reduced Abelian gauge theory has beta function coefficient \(b_0 = 11n/3 > 0\).
* For instanton configurations in the coset NLSM, the action is \(S_{NLSM} = (4\pi^2/g^2)|Q|\) with topological charge \(Q\).
* The topological charge integral is \(\int_{\mathbf{R}^2} \Omega_K = \pi Q = \pi \sum_{\ell=1}^n d_\ell Q_\ell\), where \(Q_\ell\) are integer-valued topological charges.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses of quark confinement in SU(2) Yang-Mills theory (Kondo 1998a, 1998b, 1999) to the SU(3) case, generalizing the non-Abelian Stokes theorem derived for SU(2) by Diakonov and Petrov (1989) and Kondo (1999). The approach builds on the dual superconductor picture of 't Hooft (1981) and the idea of Abelian projection, but shows that for the fundamental representation of SU(3), the relevant coset is \(CP^2\) (from \(SU(3)/U(2)\)) rather than the maximal torus \(U(1)\times U(1)\), implying that a single species of magnetic monopole (from \(\pi_2(SU(3)/U(2)) = \mathbf{Z}\)) suffices for confinement. The results enable further analytical study of quark confinement in QCD using the framework of perturbative deformation of topological field theory, with the authors noting that the detailed exposition and large-N expansion results will appear in a subsequent paper (Kondo and Taira 1999).
