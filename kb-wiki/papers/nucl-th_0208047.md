# Emission angle dependent HBT at RHIC and beyond

> We study the geometrical features of non-central heavy ion collisions throughout their dynamical evolution from equilibration to thermal freeze-out within a hydrodynamic picture. We discuss resulting observables, in particular the emission angle dependence of the HBT radii and the relation of these oscillations to the geometry at the final stage.

## Key claim

The emission-angle-dependent HBT radii oscillations can change sign at low transverse momentum if the fireball becomes elongated in the reaction plane at freeze-out, meaning that the true source geometry is only reflected in correlations of particles with the smallest transverse momenta.

## Method

The authors initialized two hydrodynamic scenarios (RHIC1 and IPES) for non-central Au+Au collisions at impact parameter 7 fm, using ideal hydrodynamics with longitudinal boost-invariance. They computed the freeze-out hypersurface and source function for pions, then extracted the spatial correlation tensor and rotated it into the pair-momentum direction to obtain the HBT radii as functions of emission angle and mean transverse momentum \(K_\perp\).

## Key numbers

- RHIC1 scenario: maximum initial temperature \(T_0 = 340\) MeV at equilibration time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\), decoupling temperature \(T_{\rm dec} = 130\) MeV.
- IPES scenario: maximum initial temperature \(T_0 = 2\) GeV at \(\tau_0 = 0.1\) fm/\(c\), decoupling temperature \(T_{\rm dec} = 100\) MeV.
- For Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 130\) GeV, hydrodynamic behavior breaks down above impact parameter \(b \sim 7-8\) fm.
- In the IPES calculation, the HBT radius oscillations flip sign at \(K_\perp \sim 0.15\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic studies of non-central heavy ion collisions that previously focused on anisotropic flow (Kolb et al., 2001; Teaney et al., 2001; Hirano, 2001) to the azimuthal dependence of HBT radii. It addresses persisting problems in interpreting HBT data (Heinz and Kolb, 2002) and shows that the emission-angle-dependent HBT oscillations can reveal whether the source is elongated out of plane or in plane at freeze-out, which is relevant for understanding the dynamics at RHIC and future LHC energies.
