# $A_β[E_β]$ in $^{37}$K decay: new physics with opposite $β$ helicity

> By extending our analysis and simulations of our $^{37}$K $β$-decay data set to allow the $β$ asymmetry with respect to nuclear spin to vary with $β$ energy $E_β$, we have gained sensitivity to new physics that depends on a helicity factor for the $β$, $m_β/E_β$. In particular, we constrain Lorentz scalar and tensor quark-lepton interaction strengths at a sensitivity complementary to the similar Fierz interference term in neutron $β$ decay. Our result for that new physics is a Fierz interference term $b$ = 0.033 $\pm$ 0.084 (stat) $\pm$ 0.039 (syst), consistent with the standard model electroweak interaction value $b=0$. We consider presently achieved complementarity to $β$-decay and particle physics experiments, along with projectable technical improvements to our method.

## Key claim

By extending the analysis of their $^{37}$K beta-decay asymmetry data to allow the asymmetry parameter to vary with beta energy, the authors constrain Lorentz scalar and tensor quark-lepton interaction strengths via the Fierz interference term $b$, finding $b = 0.033 \pm 0.084$ (stat) $\pm 0.039$ (syst), consistent with the Standard Model value of zero, but with the caveat that the result is systematics-limited and the energy dependence complicates simulations considerably.

## Method

The authors used the TRIUMF Neutral Atom Trap (TRINAT) to trap approximately 10,000 $^{37}$K atoms, polarized them to $I_z/I = 0.991 \pm 0.001$ via optical pumping, and measured the beta asymmetry with respect to nuclear spin as a function of beta energy using double-sided silicon strip detectors backed by plastic scintillators. They extracted the Fierz interference term $b$ and the asymmetry parameter $A_\beta$ by comparing the measured superratio asymmetry to GEANT4 simulations with varied parameters, calculating chi-squared from the difference between data and simulations.

## Key numbers

- Fierz interference term: $b = 0.033 \pm 0.084$ (stat) $\pm 0.039$ (syst)
- Asymmetry parameter (model-independent): $A_\beta = -0.5738 \pm 0.0082$ (stat) $\pm 0.0041$ (syst)
- Asymmetry parameter with $b=0$ forced: $A_\beta = -0.5712 \pm 0.013$ (stat)
- Nuclear polarization: $I_z/I = 0.991 \pm 0.001$
- Linear correlation between $A_\beta$ and $b$: $A_\beta = -0.5707 - 0.090 \times b$
- Largest systematic uncertainty from beta scattering: 0.031 on $b$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous measurement of the $^{37}$K beta asymmetry (Fenker, 2018), which constrained new physics coupling to flipped helicity for both beta and neutrino (V+A currents), by now allowing sensitivity to new physics that depends on the helicity factor $m_\beta/E_\beta$ (Fierz interference). The result is complementary to similar Fierz interference measurements in neutron beta decay and to constraints from other nuclear beta decay experiments such as $^6$He and $^{21}$Na recoil momentum spectra measurements. The method enables future improvements by using low-Z materials to reduce beta scattering systematics and thinner mirrors to lower the energy threshold, potentially improving statistical sensitivity to the $m_\beta/E_\beta$ term by a factor of two.
