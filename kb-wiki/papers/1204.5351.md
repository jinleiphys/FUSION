# Uniform description of bulk observables in the hydrokinetic model of A+A collisions at RHIC and LHC

> A simultaneous description of hadronic yields; pion, kaon, and proton spectra; elliptic flows; and femtoscopy scales in the hydrokinetic model of A+A collisions is presented at different centralities for the top BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC) 2.76-TeV energies. The initial conditions are based on the Glauber Monte-Carlo simulations. When going from RHIC to LHC energy in the model, the only parameters changed are the normalization of the initial entropy defined by the number of all charged particles in most central collisions, contribution to entropy from binary collisions and baryonic chemical potential. The hydrokinetic model is used in its hybrid version (hHKM), which provides the correct match (at the isochronic hypersurface) of the decaying hadron matter evolution with hadronic ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics cascade. The results are compared with the standard hybrid models where hydrodynamics and hadronic cascade are matching just at the non-space-like hypersurface of chemical freeze-out or on the isochronic hypersurface. The modification of the particle number ratios at LHC caused, in particular, by the particle annihilations at the afterburn stage is also analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a simultaneous description of hadronic yields, transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flow, and femtoscopy scales at both RHIC and LHC energies is possible within the hybrid hydrokinetic model (hHKM), but only when the matching between hydrodynamics and the UrQMD hadronic cascade is performed on an isochronic hypersurface using the hydrokinetic approach rather than a simple Cooper-Frye prescription; the authors note that the "hybrid-isochronic" model that uses pure hydrodynamics up to the isochronic surface fails to describe the data, while both hHKM and the standard "hybrid" model (matching at the chemical freeze-out isotherm) give satisfactory results.

## Method

The authors used the hybrid hydrokinetic model (hHKM) with Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions, where the initial entropy density profile is a linear combination of wounded nucleon density and binary collision density, with parameters fixed by charged particle multiplicity at each energy. The evolution proceeds through ideal 3+1D hydrodynamics with a lattice QCD-inspired equation of state, followed by a hydrokinetic stage that accounts for non-equilibrium effects, and finally a switch to the UrQMD hadronic cascade at an isochronic hypersurface. The results are compared with two alternative matching procedures: the standard "hybrid" model using Cooper-Frye at the chemical freeze-out isotherm, and a "hybrid-isochronic" model that switches from pure hydrodynamics to UrQMD at the same isochronic hypersurface.

## Key numbers

- For RHIC Au+Au at 200 GeV: initial entropy density parameter delta = 0.14 in Eq. (Glauber); chemical freeze-out temperature T_ch = 165 MeV, baryochemical potential mu_B = 28 MeV, mu_S = 7 MeV, mu_E = -1 MeV
- For LHC Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV: delta = 0.08; nucleon-nucleon cross section sigma = 64 mb (compared to sigma = 42 mb for RHIC)
- Initial time tau_0 = 0.1 fm/c with zero initial transverse flow
- hHKM yields for most central (0-5%) Au+Au at RHIC: N_pi- = 330, N_K- = 47.3, N_p = 29.9, N_antip = 20.5 (compared to STAR data: 327 +/- 33, 49.5 +/- 7.4, 34.7 +/- 6.2, 26.7 +/- 4.0)
- Centrality cuts for RHIC Au+Au 0-5%: N_part > 328; for LHC Pb+Pb 0-5%: N_part > 360

## Context

This work extends the hydrokinetic model (HKM) previously developed by the same group (Sinyukov et al., 2006; Karpenko and Sinyukov, 2010) to simultaneously describe bulk observables at both RHIC and LHC energies, building on earlier findings that a relatively hard equation of state with crossover transition, prethermal anisotropic flow, and shear viscosity effects are needed to describe spectra and femtoscopy together. The results are compared with other hydrodynamic-based approaches such as EPOS2 (Werner et al., 2010) and the viscous hydrodynamic model of Bozek (2011), and the paper demonstrates that the hHKM can serve as a unified framework for soft observables across collision energies, enabling predictions for still-unmeasured observables at LHC.
