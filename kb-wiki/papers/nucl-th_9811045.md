# Alternative evaluations of halos in nuclei

> Data for the scattering of 6He, 8He, 9Li, and 11Li from hydrogen are analyzed within a fully microscopic folding model of proton-nucleus scattering. Current data suggest that of these only 11Li has a noticeable halo. For 6He, we have also analysed the complementary reaction 6Li(gamma,pi)6He(gs). The available data for that reaction support the hypothesis that 6He may not be a halo nucleus. However, those data are scarce and there is clearly a need for more to elicit the microscopic structure of 6He.

## Key claim

Based on a fully microscopic folding model analysis of proton-nucleus scattering data, only 11Li shows clear evidence of a halo among the four nuclei studied (6He, 8He, 9Li, 11Li), but the available data for 6He are too scarce to definitively rule out a halo structure, and the complementary 6Li(gamma,pi+)6He reaction data favor the non-halo hypothesis only tentatively.

## Method

The authors performed shell model calculations for 6He and 8He in a complete 4-hbar-omega model space using a G-matrix interaction, and for 9Li and 11Li using 0-hbar-omega and 2-hbar-omega model spaces respectively with phenomenological interactions. They then used the resulting one-body density matrix elements in a fully microscopic folding model of proton-nucleus scattering to predict differential cross sections for elastic scattering of these nuclei from hydrogen at 72A MeV (for He isotopes) and 62A MeV (for Li isotopes), comparing halo and non-halo specifications of the single-particle wave functions. For 6He specifically, they also analyzed the 6Li(gamma,pi+)6He reaction at E_gamma = 200 MeV using a DWIA model.

## Key numbers

- 6He r.m.s. radius: non-halo = 2.301 fm, halo = 2.586 fm, Glauber model = 2.54 +/- 0.04 fm
- 8He r.m.s. radius: non-halo = 2.627 fm, halo = 2.946 fm, Glauber model = 2.60 fm
- 9Li r.m.s. radius: non-halo = 2.238 fm, halo = 2.579 fm, Glauber model = 2.30 +/- 0.02 fm
- 11Li r.m.s. radius: non-halo = 2.447 fm, halo = 2.964 fm, Glauber model = 3.53 +/- 0.10 fm
- Single neutron separation energy for 6He: 1.87 MeV (used to set 0p-shell binding energy to 2 MeV for halo specification)
- Single neutron separation energies: 2.137 MeV for 8He, 4.063 MeV for 9Li
- For halo specification in 8He, 9Li, and 11Li, the binding energy for 0p1/2 and higher orbits was set to 0.5 MeV
- 6He scattering data available only to 50 degrees (q ~ 1.6 fm^-1), insufficient to discriminate halo from non-halo
- Overlap of harmonic oscillator and Woods-Saxon 0p3/2 radial wave functions in the 6Li(gamma,pi+) calculation: 0.96

## Context

This work extends the microscopic folding model approach previously applied to stable nuclei from 6Li to 238U by Dortmans et al. (1998) to exotic neutron-rich nuclei, and contrasts with heavy-ion breakup studies (Hansen et al., 1995) which probe only the asymptotic part of halo wave functions. The analysis builds on earlier shell model calculations by Navratil and Barrett (1996, 1998) and the authors' own previous work on 9Li and 11Li (Karataglidis et al., 1997). The results enable future experiments to use proton scattering in inverse kinematics as a definitive probe of halo structure, provided data are taken to larger angles (beyond 50 degrees for 6He), and demonstrate the need for complementary reaction channels such as charged pion photoproduction to resolve the halo status of 6He.
