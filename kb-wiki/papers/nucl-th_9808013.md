# Hadronic Transport Model with a Phase Transition

> We specify a tractable transport model with thermodynamic properties close to those expected for the strongly interacting matter. In particular, at high temperatures, the matter undergoes a phase transition, such as to the quark-gluon plasma, with a drop in masses of elementary excitations and a rapid increase in the number of degrees of freedom. We show that a softening of the equation of state such as associated with the transition to quark-gluon plasma should be observable in the elliptic-flow excitation function from heavy-ion reactions.

## Key claim

The authors specify a tractable hadronic transport model whose thermodynamic properties approximate those expected for strongly interacting matter, including a phase transition to quark-gluon plasma at high temperatures. They show that a softening of the equation of state associated with such a transition should be observable in the elliptic-flow excitation function from heavy-ion reactions, with data from the EOS, E895, and E877 Collaborations pointing to a softening at a beam energy of about 3 AGeV.

## Method

The authors formulate a relativistic transport model within Landau theory, where the energy density is a functional of phase-space distributions and single-particle energies are functional derivatives. Particle masses are reduced by a common factor S that depends on scalar density, and the number of hadronic degrees of freedom is cut off to roughly match the 40 fundamental degrees of freedom of quarks and gluons. The model includes nucleons, deltas, antiparticles, pions, and rho mesons, with low-energy collision processes that obey detailed balance and high-energy processes parametrized from experimental data. The elliptic flow is calculated as the Fourier coefficient v2 = ⟨cos 2φ⟩ for baryons at midrapidity in Au + Au reactions.

## Key numbers

- The phase transition in the model occurs at T ≈ 170 MeV along the μ = 0 axis, matching lattice QCD results from Kar98.
- The mass reduction factor S is given by S = (1 − 0.54 (fm³/GeV) ρs)², a weaker dependence than the Walecka model's S = 1 − 2.6 (fm³/GeV) ρs.
- At T = 0, the model reproduces nuclear matter ground-state properties: minimum energy per baryon of 939 − 16 = 923 MeV at ρv = ρ0 = 0.16 fm⁻³, with incompressibility K ≈ 210 MeV.
- A first-order phase transition at T = 0 takes the system from ρv ∼ 3.5 ρ0 to ρv ∼ 7 ρ0.
- In head-on Au + Au reactions at 10.7 GeV/nucleon with collisions included, the particle number increases by a factor of 2.5 compared to the initial state and masses at the center drop to values behind the transition.
- The elliptic flow changes sign from negative at beam energies around 0.2 AGeV to positive at around 20 AGeV.
- Data from the EOS, E895, and E877 Collaborations point to a softening of the equation of state at a beam energy of about 3 AGeV, corresponding to baryon density ρv ∼ 4 ρ0.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Landau theory approach to transport, generalizing results obtained by Ko et al. (1987) within the Walecka model. The model is designed to be consistent with lattice QCD results from Kar98 at μ = 0 and with known nuclear matter ground-state properties, while providing a tractable framework for nonequilibrium reaction simulations. The results enable experimental searches for the quark-gluon plasma phase transition through elliptic flow measurements in the 1–10 AGeV energy range (SIS-AGS), where the crossing of the phase transition may be observed in excitation functions.
