# Resolutions of Identity for Some Non-Hermitian Hamiltonians. II. Proofs

> This part is a continuation of the Part I where we built resolutions of identity for certain non-Hermitian Hamiltonians constructed of biorthogonal sets of their eigen- and associated functions for the spectral problem defined on entire axis. Non-Hermitian Hamiltonians under consideration are taken with continuous spectrum and the following cases are examined: an exceptional point of arbitrary multiplicity situated on a boundary of continuous spectrum and an exceptional point situated inside of continuous spectrum. In the present work the rigorous proofs are given for the resolutions of identity in both cases.

## Key claim

The paper provides rigorous proofs for the resolutions of identity previously constructed in Part I for non-Hermitian Hamiltonians with continuous spectrum, specifically for the cases of an exceptional point of arbitrary multiplicity situated on a boundary of continuous spectrum and an exceptional point situated inside of continuous spectrum; the validity of these resolutions is established in the space of distributions over test function spaces with specific growth conditions, not for all test functions.

## Method

The author defines spaces of test functions CL_gamma and distributions CL'_gamma with weight (1+|x|)^gamma, then proves biorthogonality relations between eigenfunctions for the continuous spectrum using integration by parts and induction. The resolutions of identity are proved by evaluating integrals over deformed contours in the complex k-plane, taking limits as the contour endpoints go to infinity, and showing convergence in the sense of distributions for test functions satisfying specific gamma conditions.

## Key numbers

* The biorthogonality relation (Theorem 3.1) holds for test functions with gamma > -2m + 2n - 1, where m and n are nonnegative integers
* The initial resolution of identity (Theorem 3.2) holds for any gamma > -1
* The resolution of identity for the case with exceptional point inside continuous spectrum (Theorem 4.1) holds for any gamma > -1
* The functionals defined in equations (2.39) and (2.40) from Part I are nontrivial for any gamma < 3 but trivial (equal to zero) for any gamma > 3
* The functionals in Remark 3.4 are discontinuous for any gamma < 3

## Context

This work extends the constructions of Part I (Sokolov, 2011) by providing the rigorous mathematical proofs that were omitted there. The paper builds on the Gel'fand triple framework (Gel'fand, 1964) for handling distributions and test functions. The results enable the application of these resolutions of identity to non-Hermitian quantum mechanical systems with exceptional points, including supersymmetric quantum mechanics models, and provide the mathematical foundation for using these expansions with bounded and slowly increasing test functions as shown in Theorem 3.3.
