# Neutron matter at low density and the unitary limit

> Neutron matter at low density is studied within the hole-line expansion. Calculations are performed in the range of Fermi momentum $k_F$ between 0.4 and 0.8 fm$^{-1}$. It is found that the Equation of State is determined by the $^1S_0$ channel only, the three-body forces contribution is quite small, the effect of the single particle potential is negligible and the three hole-line contribution is below 5% of the total energy and indeed vanishing small at the lowest densities. Despite the unitary limit is actually never reached, the total energy stays very close to one half of the free gas value throughout the considered density range. A rank one separable representation of the bare NN interaction, which reproduces the physical scattering length and effective range, gives results almost indistinguishable from the full Brueckner G-matrix calculations with a realistic force. The extension of the calculations below $k_F = 0.4$ fm$^{-1}$ does not indicate any pathological behavior of the neutron Equation of State.

## Key claim

The neutron matter equation of state (EOS) in the Fermi momentum range \(0.4 < k_F < 0.8\) fm\(^{-1}\) is very close to one half of the free Fermi gas value, even though the unitary limit (infinite scattering length) is strictly never reached; the condition \(1/|a| < k_F/\alpha < 1/r_0\) (with \(\alpha = 2(9\pi/4)^{1/3}\)) is satisfied in this range, placing the system "close" to the unitary limit.

## Method

The hole-line expansion of Bethe, Brueckner, and Goldstone (BBG) was applied to pure neutron matter using the Argonne v\(_{18}\) realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction. The Brueckner G-matrix was solved in the \(^1S_0\) channel, which dominates at low density, and the three hole-line contributions were summed via the Bethe-Fadeev equation. A rank-one separable representation of the bare NN interaction, reproducing the physical scattering length and effective range, was also constructed and compared to the full G-matrix calculations.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum range studied: \(k_F = 0.4\) to \(0.8\) fm\(^{-1}\), corresponding to densities between about 1/50 and 1/5 of saturation density.
- Three-body force contribution (Urbana model) was less than 0.01 MeV and therefore neglected.
- Total three hole-line contribution \(D_3\) at \(k_F = 0.4\) fm\(^{-1}\) was 0.023 MeV; at \(k_F = 0.8\) fm\(^{-1}\) it was 0.148 MeV, below 5% of the total energy.
- Neutron-neutron scattering length used: \(a = -18.5\) fm; effective range: \(r_0 = 2.7\) fm.
- In-medium scattering length \(a'\) approximately \(- \pi/(2k_F)\); in-medium effective range \(r_0' \approx r_0 - 4/(\pi k_F)\).
- The total energy stays very close to one half of the free gas value throughout the considered density range; Monte Carlo calculations for the unitary limit give a factor of approximately 0.44.

## Context

This work extends the BBG hole-line expansion to low-density neutron matter, complementing variational calculations by Panda et al. (2005) and finite-volume Green's function Monte Carlo calculations by Carlson et al. (2003), which also found the EOS close to half the free gas value. The results contradict the expectation that the unitary limit factor should be 0.44, as found in Monte Carlo studies by Carlson et al. (2003), Boro et al. (2005), and Bulgac et al. (2006) for schematic forces. The separable representation demonstrates that the EOS is determined solely by the scattering length and effective range, enabling reliable extension of calculations below \(k_F = 0.4\) fm\(^{-1}\) where the simple factor of 1/2 no longer holds.
