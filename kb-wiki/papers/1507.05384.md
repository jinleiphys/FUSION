# Constraining the symmetry energy content of nuclear matter from nuclear masses: a covariance analysis

> Elements of nuclear symmetry energy evaluated from different energy density functionals parametrized by fitting selective bulk properties of few representative nuclei are seen to vary widely. Those obtained from experimental data on nuclear masses across the periodic table, however, show that they are better constrained. A possible direction in reconciling this paradox may be gleaned from comparison of results obtained from use of the binding energies in the fitting protocol within a microscopic model with two sets of nuclei, one a representative standard set and another where very highly asymmetric nuclei are additionally included. A covariance analysis reveals that the additional fitting protocol reduces the uncertainties in the nuclear symmetry energy coefficient, its slope parameter as well as the neutron-skin thickness in $^{208}$Pb nucleus by $\sim 50\%$. The central values of these entities are also seen to be slightly reduced.

## Key claim

The authors show that the apparent paradox between tightly constrained symmetry energy from nuclear masses and widely varying values from microscopic mean-field models can be partially resolved by including binding energies of highly asymmetric nuclei (isospin asymmetry δ = 0.3) in the fitting protocol, which reduces uncertainties in the symmetry energy coefficient, its slope parameter, and neutron-skin thickness by approximately 50%.

## Method

The authors performed a covariance analysis within a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model, fitting binding energies and charge radii of a standard set of nuclei (model-I) and then adding binding energy differences for the highly asymmetric pairs (²⁴O-¹⁶O) and (³⁰Ne-¹⁸Ne) (model-II). The curvature matrix from χ² minimization was used to compute correlated errors and correlation coefficients for the model parameters and predicted observables, including the symmetry energy coefficient Cv⁰, its slope parameter L₀, and neutron-skin thickness Δrnp in ²⁰⁸Pb.

## Key numbers

- Uncertainty in Cv⁰ reduced from ±3.08 MeV (model-I) to ±1.51 MeV (model-II), a reduction of approximately 51%
- Uncertainty in L₀ reduced from ±17.08 MeV (model-I) to ±7.00 MeV (model-II), a reduction of approximately 59%
- Uncertainty in Δrnp for ²⁰⁸Pb reduced from ±0.065 fm (model-I) to ±0.030 fm (model-II), a reduction of approximately 54%
- Central value of Cv⁰ shifted from 32.03 MeV (model-I) to 31.69 MeV (model-II)
- Central value of L₀ shifted from 57.62 MeV (model-I) to 55.63 MeV (model-II)
- Correlation coefficients between gρ and η2ρ parameters and between L₀ and Δrnp were both approximately 0.95 for both models

## Context

This work addresses the discrepancy between symmetry energy constraints from macroscopic models like the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) of Moller (2012), which gave Cv⁰ = 32.5 ± 0.5 MeV and L₀ = 70 ± 15 MeV, and the wider variations seen in microscopic energy density functionals surveyed by Dutra (2012, 2014). The study extends prior work by Agrawal (2012, 2013) and Liu (2013) on correlating symmetry energy with neutron-skin thickness, and suggests that including highly asymmetric nuclei in fitting protocols can achieve constraints comparable to those from the SAMi Skyrme force of Roca-Maza (2012), which used pure neutron matter equation of state as pseudo-data. The authors note that further extension to other energy density functionals and inclusion of isovector giant dipole polarizability data for ²⁰⁸Pb, as studied by Reinhard (2010) and Roca-Maza (2013), would be valuable next steps.
