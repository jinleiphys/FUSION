# Half-Skyrmions, Tensor Forces and Symmetry Energy in Cold Dense Matter

> In a previous article, the 4D half-skyrmion (or 5D dyonic salt) structure of dense baryonic matter described in crystalline configuration in the large $N_c$ limit was shown to impact nontrivially on how anti-kaons behave in compressed nuclear matter with a possible implication on an "ice-9" phenomenon of deeply bound kaonic matter and condensed kaons in compact stars. We extend the analysis to make a further prediction on the scaling properties of hadrons that have a surprising effect on the nuclear tensor forces, the symmetry energy and hence on the phase structure at high density. We treat this problem relying on certain topological structure of chiral solitons. Combined with what can be deduced from hidden local symmetry for hadrons in dense medium and the "soft" dilatonic degree of freedom associated with the trace anomaly of QCD, we uncover a novel structure of chiral symmetry in the "supersoft" symmetry energy that can influence the structure of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the transition from skyrmion matter to half-skyrmion matter at a density \(n_{1/2} \sim (1.3-2)n_0\) (where \(n_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is normal nuclear matter density) causes an abrupt change in the scaling of the \(\rho\)-meson tensor force, which in turn produces a "supersoft" symmetry energy that turns over and then rises again, rather than the monotonic increase assumed in standard treatments.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor Skyrme model supplemented with a soft dilaton scalar field, placing skyrmions on an FCC crystal lattice and squeezing the system to simulate increasing density. They extract scaling relations for the nucleon mass, vector-meson mass, and the \(\rho\)-nucleon coupling strength in the half-skyrmion phase, then apply these scalings to the non-relativistic two-body tensor forces from one-pion and one-\(\rho\) exchange. The symmetry energy is computed via collective quantization of the skyrmion crystal, treating the whole matter as a single rotating system.

## Key numbers

* The half-skyrmion transition density is \(n_{1/2} \sim (1.3-2)n_0\) with \(n_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).
* The nucleon mass scaling in the half-skyrmion phase is constant: \(m_N^*/m_N \approx b\) with \(b \sim 0.6-0.8\) for \(n_{1/2} \lesssim n \lesssim n_c\).
* The critical density for chiral restoration (\(f_\pi^*=0\)) is \(n_c/n_0 \approx 4-25\) for dilaton mass \(m_\chi \approx 700-1200\) MeV.
* The symmetry energy from collective quantization shows a cusp at \(n_{1/2}\), computed with \(f_\pi=93\) MeV and \(1/e^2 \approx 0.03\) for two dilaton masses (600 and 700 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of half-skyrmion matter (Lee, Park, Rho, 2003) and the scaling relations of Brown and Rho (1991). It contradicts the naive expectation that tensor-force cancellation persists to high density, as assumed in the C14 dating analysis of Holt et al. (2008). The predicted "supersoft" symmetry energy, which turns over near \(n_0\) and then rises, is consistent with the FOPI/GSI data on \(\pi^-/\pi^+\) ratios discussed by Xu and Li (2010) and Baldo and Burgio (2010). The results enable predictions for neutron-star structure and can be tested at upcoming facilities such as RIB and FAIR/GSI.
