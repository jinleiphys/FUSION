# Four-Body Bound State Calculations in Three-Dimensional Approach

> The four-body bound state with two-body interactions is formulated in Three-Dimensional approach, a recently developed momentum space representation which greatly simplifies the numerical calculations of few-body systems without performing the partial wave decomposition. The obtained three-dimensional Faddeev-Yakubovsky integral equations are solved with two-body potentials. Results for four-body binding energies are in good agreement with achievements of the other methods.

## Key claim

The authors formulate the four-body bound state with two-body interactions in a Three-Dimensional (3D) momentum space representation of the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations, avoiding partial wave decomposition entirely; however, as a first step they neglect spin and isospin degrees of freedom and study only the four-boson bound state, not the full four-nucleon problem.

## Method

The coupled Faddeev-Yakubovsky integral equations for two independent components (one for the 3+1 partition and one for the 2+2 partition) are written directly in terms of vector Jacobi momenta and the angles between them, without partial wave expansion. The equations are solved numerically using spin-independent and spin-averaged two-body potentials, with the amplitudes depending on six variables: three momentum magnitudes and three angles. The matrix elements of permutation operators and the two-body t-matrix are evaluated in this 3D representation.

## Key numbers

- Four-body binding energies are reported as being in good agreement with results from other methods (no specific numerical binding energy values are given in the truncated text).
- The number of different spin, isospin and angular momentum combinations needed in modern partial-wave-based four-body calculations is stated as 1572 or more (citing references [42] and [43]).
- The four-body wave function contains twelve different 3+1 type chains and six 2+2 type chains, for a total of eighteen Faddeev-Yakubovsky components.

## Context

This work extends the Three-Dimensional approach previously developed by W. Gloeckle and collaborators for two- and three-body systems (Gloeckle et al., 2000-2005) to the four-body bound state problem. It contrasts with traditional partial-wave-based Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations in momentum space (e.g., those by Kamada and Gloeckle, 1990s) and in configuration space, which require many partial wave combinations. The paper enables future calculations of the four-nucleon bound state without partial wave decomposition, and the authors note that calculations including three-body interactions are currently underway.
