# Muonic molecules of charge Z >= 3: Coulombic properties and nuclear transitions.

> In this report we will discuss properties of and models for nuclear transitions in muonic molecules formed via collisions of muonic atoms of hydrogen isotopes with light nuclei like $L\!i$ and $B\!e$. Their importance for nuclear astrophysics and nuclear physics at super low energies is emphasized.

# Muonic molecules of charge Z >= 3: Coulombic properties and nuclear transitions

## Key claim

Muonic molecules formed from hydrogen isotopes and light nuclei like Li and Be (with Z >= 3) can support nuclear transitions at super-low energies, but these transitions are generally suppressed compared to analogous ones in He-based muonic molecules due to larger molecular size, except in special cases where threshold resonances or closely spaced two-body channel thresholds produce long-range nuclear interactions.

## Method

The authors used a hyperspherical adiabatic approach (surface function method) to solve the three-body Coulomb problem for systems of type hZμ, where h = p, d, or t and Z = Li or Be isotopes. They calculated eigenpotentials by expanding surface functions in a basis of hyperspherical harmonics and channel functions, then applied an extreme adiabatic approximation to obtain binding energies. For nuclear transition probabilities, they employed a four-body LCAO-type ansatz treating the initial and final states as linear combinations of channel wavefunctions, with the overlap integral S₃ (probability of heavy particles being at small distances) as the key uncertain parameter.

## Key numbers

- Threshold energy difference between d+⁷Be and p+⁸Be*(2⁺) channels: approximately 0.04 MeV
- Threshold energy difference between p+¹⁰Be and t+⁸Be(g.s.) channels: approximately 0.004 MeV
- Separation energy of the proton in ⁸B: 0.14 MeV
- Energy release Q in the transition p+¹⁰Beμ → t+⁸Beμ: approximately 4 keV
- Estimated transition rate P⁻ for d⁷Beμ → p⁸Beμ: 4.6 × 10¹¹ sec⁻¹
- Hartree energy unit for the muon (used in molecular potential fitting): H = 5626.5 eV
- Excited state energy in ¹⁰Be: 8.4774 MeV (coinciding with ⁸Be+2n threshold)
- Triton binding energy: 8.48 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies of muonic molecules beyond the well-studied dtμ system (Vesman mechanism, 1967) to heavier systems with Z ≥ 3, where earlier estimations by Arikawa et al., Belyaev et al., and Gershtein had suggested the existence of hLiμ and hBeμ resonances. The paper discusses how such systems could provide access to nuclear reaction data at eV-keV energies that are otherwise inaccessible in laboratory experiments, particularly relevant for astrophysical S-factor determinations such as the p+⁷Be → ⁸B+γ reaction crucial for solar neutrino flux calculations (Bahcall, 1989; Jones, 1992). The approach enables future experimental searches for muon-catalyzed fusion cycles analogous to the dt cycle but involving Li and Be isotopes, potentially addressing the primordial ⁷Li abundance problem (Maurice, 1984; Wagoner, 1973).
