# Vacuum polarization Contribution to the Astrophysical S-Factor

> We study the effect of vacuum polarization in nuclear reactions of astrophysical interest. This effect has the opposite sign compared to the screening by the atomic electrons. It is shown that vacuum polarization further increases the longstanding differences between the experimental data of astrophysical nuclear reactions at very low energies and the theoretical calculations which aim to include electron screening, albeit by a small amount.

## Key claim

Vacuum polarization increases the electromagnetic potential between two like charges, opposite in sign to atomic electron screening, but its contribution to the astrophysical S-factor never exceeds a few percent and is not large enough to counter the electron screening enhancement observed in low-energy nuclear reactions.

## Method

The authors calculated s-wave WKB penetrability factors through the Coulomb barrier including both the atomic screening correction (treated as a constant U_e in the adiabatic approximation) and the Uehling vacuum polarization potential. They computed the ratio of penetrabilities with atomic screening alone and with both atomic screening and vacuum polarization relative to the bare Coulomb penetrability for twelve nuclear reactions at their lowest measured experimental energies.

## Key numbers

- For the D(d,p)T reaction at E_min = 1.62 keV with U_e = 20 eV: P_{C+Scr}/P_C = 1.163, P_{C+Scr+Vac.Pol.}/P_C = 1.150
- For the ^3He(^3He,2p)^4He reaction at E_min = 25 keV with U_e = 292 eV: P_{C+Scr}/P_C = 1.196, P_{C+Scr+Vac.Pol.}/P_C = 1.159
- For the ^3He(d,p)^4He reaction at E_min = 5.88 keV with U_e = 119 eV: P_{C+Scr}/P_C = 1.331, P_{C+Scr+Vac.Pol.}/P_C = 1.303
- For the ^11B(p,alpha)^8Be reaction at E_min = 16.70 keV with U_e = 346 eV: P_{C+Scr}/P_C = 1.461, P_{C+Scr+Vac.Pol.}/P_C = 1.419
- The vacuum polarization potential uses the fine structure constant alpha = 1/137 and the electron Compton wavelength lambda_e = 386 fm
- The nuclear radius approximation used is R_n = 1.2 (A_1^{1/3} + A_2^{1/3}) fm

## Context

This work extends the study of vacuum polarization effects previously investigated by Baur, Rosel, and Trautmann (1977) for elastic scattering below the Coulomb barrier and by Assenbaum, Langanke, and Soff (1988) for subbarrier fusion reactions using the Uehling formalism. The paper shows that vacuum polarization cannot resolve the longstanding discrepancy between experimental data and theoretical calculations of electron screening, particularly for the ^3He(^3He,2p)^4He reaction where a mechanism reducing the screening enhancement seems needed. The results enable more precise extrapolations of measured S-factors to stellar energies by identifying vacuum polarization as one of several small contributions to the weak energy dependence of the S-factor.
