# Surface diffuseness anomaly in heavy-ion fusion potentials

> Recent high precision experimental data for heavy-ion fusion cross sections at energies in the vicinity of the Coulomb barrier systematically show that a strikingly large surface diffuseness parameter for a Woods-Saxon potential is required in order to fit the data. We discuss possible origins for this anomaly, including the effects of dissipation and the sensitivity of fusion cross sections to the choice of inter-nuclear potential. Our study suggests that the frozen density approximation, which is often used in analyses of heavy-ion reactions, may have to be re-examined for heavy-ion fusion.

# Surface diffuseness anomaly in heavy-ion fusion potentials

## Key claim

The paper argues that the systematically large surface diffuseness parameter (0.8–1.4 fm) required to fit heavy-ion fusion data with a Woods-Saxon potential, compared to the standard scattering value of ~0.63 fm, may originate either from deviations of the true inter-nuclear potential from the Woods-Saxon shape or from energy dissipation effects; the authors suggest that both explanations point toward a need to re-examine the frozen density approximation for fusion reactions.

## Method

The authors used the barrier penetration model with incoming wave boundary condition to compute fusion cross sections for the ¹⁶O + ²⁰⁸Pb system, comparing Woods-Saxon potentials with different diffuseness parameters against a double-folding potential (using the DDM3Y1-Paris nucleon-nucleon interaction) and a complementary error function potential. They also applied the Glas-Mosel critical distance model and the surface friction model of Fröbrich to assess whether energy dissipation could mimic the effect of a large diffuseness parameter.

## Key numbers

- Standard surface diffuseness parameter for Woods-Saxon potential in scattering analyses: ~0.63 fm
- Surface diffuseness parameters required to fit fusion data: ranging between 0.8 and 1.4 fm
- For ¹⁶O + ²⁰⁸Pb, a Woods-Saxon potential with a = 1.12 fm fits the fusion data well, while a = 0.63 fm significantly overestimates cross sections above the barrier
- Barrier parameters for ¹⁶O + ²⁰⁸Pb from parabolic approximation: R_b = 11.94 fm, V_b = 74.87 MeV, ħΩ = 5.00 MeV
- Glas-Mosel model fit parameters for ¹⁶O + ²⁰⁸Pb: R_c = 10.39 fm, V_c = 72.4 MeV
- Error function potential parameters used: V_0 = 220 MeV, r_0 = 0.92 fm, a = 2.9 fm

## Context

This work addresses the "surface diffuseness anomaly" identified in high-precision fusion measurements by Dasgupta et al. (1998), Leigh et al. (1995), and Morton et al. (1999), where fusion data systematically require larger diffuseness than the scattering value established by Christensen and Winther (1976) and Satchler (2001). The paper extends earlier work by Esbensen and Back (1996), who showed that coupled-channels calculations could resolve the anomaly for ¹⁶O + ¹⁴⁴Sm but not for other systems. The study enables future dynamical calculations that go beyond the frozen density approximation, which the authors identify as necessary to distinguish between potential-shape and dissipation effects.
