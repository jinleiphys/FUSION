# Disentangling Initial-State and Evolution Effects in Heavy-Ion Collisions Using EPOS and PHSD

> In this study we examine the impact of the initial stage and dynamical evolution on final-state observables in heavy-ion collisions. For this goal we develop a novel approach, EPOSir+PHSDe, which employs EPOS initial conditions as the starting point for parton and hadron evolution within the PHSD microscopic transport approach. By examining the space-time evolution of matter in this model and comparing to EPOS (which starts with an S-matrix approach for parallel scatterings for the initial conditions and uses a hydrodynamic evolution for the quark-gluon plasma stage with the UrQMD as afterburner) and PHSD (which starts with primary high energy $NN$ scattering realized via the LUND string model and continues with fully microscopic transport dynamics for strongly interacting partonic and hadronic matter), we identify the key differences in the final particle distributions among the three approaches. Our analysis focuses on rapidity, transverse momentum spectra, and flow harmonics $v_2$ for Au+Au collisions at the invariant energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. We find a dominant influence of dynamical evolution over the initial conditions on the final observables.

## Key claim

The authors find that the dynamical evolution of the quark-gluon plasma has a dominant influence on final-state observables (rapidity distributions, transverse momentum spectra, and flow harmonics) compared to the initial conditions, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific combination of models used: the novel EPOSir+PHSDe hybrid approach, which uses EPOS initial conditions with a rope procedure as input for PHSD evolution, enables this disentanglement only after introducing ropes to reduce the overproduction of prehadrons that would otherwise occur when using EPOS initial conditions directly with PHSD evolution.

## Method

The authors developed a hybrid model, EPOSir+PHSDe, which uses EPOS initial conditions (EPOSi) with an added rope procedure (EPOSir) to fuse overlapping string segments into ropes, then decays these ropes into clusters and subsequently into "rope core prehadrons" via microcanonical decay. These prehadrons, along with corona prehadrons from EPOS, are extrapolated back in time from the hyperbola of fixed proper time to a constant time slice and inserted into the PHSD microscopic transport framework, which then evolves the system using off-shell Kadanoff-Baym dynamics for partonic and hadronic phases, including a melting procedure for prehadrons in cells with energy density above the critical value of 0.5 GeV/fm³. By comparing EPOSir+PHSDe to full EPOS (same initial conditions, different evolution) and to full PHSD (different initial conditions, same evolution), the authors isolate the contributions of initial conditions versus dynamical evolution to final observables in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Au+Au collisions at invariant energy √sNN = 200 GeV are the system studied throughout
- EPOS uses shear viscosity to entropy ratio η/s = 0.08 and zero bulk viscosity for hydrodynamic evolution
- PHSD uses critical energy density εC = 0.5 GeV/fm³ for the melting of prehadrons into partons, based on lattice QCD
- Prehadron formation time in PHSD is τF = 0.8 fm/c, with tF = τF γ
- PHSD core prehadrons become deconfined into partons at a time of 0.064 fm/c after the start of PHSD evolution
- The momentum eccentricity εP for EPOS with η/s = 0.08 shows individual events reaching values beyond 0.7 for semiperipheral collisions at b = 7 fm
- EPOSi (without hydro) overestimates charged particle multiplicity dNch/dη by a large amount compared to BRAHMS data for central 0-5% collisions, while full EPOS with hydro reproduces the data reasonably well
- EPOSir (EPOSi with ropes) decreases the number of prehadrons per unit pseudorapidity compared to EPOSi for central 0-5% Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, averaged over 5000 events
- The PHSD grid cell size in the transverse plane is Δx = Δy = 1 fm, equal to the hadron size, and Δz = 1/γcom fm

## Context

This work extends the long-standing program of comparing and combining the EPOS model (Werner, 2023) and the PHSD model (Cassing, 2008-2021) for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. The EPOS approach uses an S-matrix multiple scattering formalism for initial conditions followed by hydrodynamic evolution with UrQMD afterburner, while PHSD uses the LUND string model (FRITIOF 7.02 and PYTHIA 6.4) for initial nucleon-nucleon scatterings followed by fully microscopic transport based on Kadanoff-Baym equations. The novel EPOSir+PHSDe hybrid enables future systematic studies to separate initial-state from evolution effects in observables such as flow harmonics and particle spectra, and can be extended to other collision energies and systems to test whether the dominance of evolution over initial conditions is a general feature of heavy-ion collisions.
