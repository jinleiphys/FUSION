# Evidence for non-hadronic interactions of charm degrees of freedom in heavy-ion collisions at relativistic energies

> Within the Hadron-String Dynamics (HSD) transport approach we study the suppression pattern of charmonia at RHIC with respect to centrality and rapidity employing various model concepts such as variants of the 'comover absorption' model or the 'charmonium melting' scenario. We find that especially the ratio of the forward to mid-rapidity nuclear modification factors of J/Psi (R_AA (forward) / R_AA (mid)) cannot be explained by the interactions with 'formed' comoving mesons or by the 'color screening mechanism' alone. Only when incorporating interactions of the c or c-bar quark with a pre-hadronic medium satisfactory results are obtained. A detailed comparison to the PHENIX data demonstrates that non-hadronic interactions are mandatory to describe the narrowing of the J/Psi rapidity distribution from p+p to central Au+Au collisions. The Psi' to J/Psi ratio is found to be crucial in disentangling the different charmonium absorption scenarios especially in the RHIC energy range. Furthermore, a comparison of the transport calculations to the statistical model of Gorenstein and Gazdzicki as well as the statistical hadronization model of Andronic et al. shows differences in the energy dependence as well as centrality dependence of the J/Psi to pion ratio which may be exploited experimentally to disentangle different concepts. We find additionally that the collective flow of charm in the HSD transport appears compatible with the data at SPS energies but substantially underestimates the data at top RHIC energies such that the large elliptic flow v_2 of charm seen experimentally has to be attributed to early interactions of non-hadronic degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

Non-hadronic interactions of charm quarks with a pre-hadronic medium are mandatory to describe the narrowing of the J/Psi rapidity distribution from p+p to central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, as the ratio of forward to mid-rapidity nuclear modification factors cannot be explained by interactions with formed comoving mesons or by color screening alone.

## Method

The authors employed the Hadron-String Dynamics (HSD) transport approach to study charmonium suppression in Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies, implementing three scenarios: (1) threshold melting, (2) dissociation by scattering on hadron-like correlators (comover scenario), and (3) additional scattering of charm with pre-hadrons considered as color neutral precursors of hadronic states. They compared the transport calculations to PHENIX data for J/Psi nuclear modification factors, rapidity distributions, and the Psi' to J/Psi ratio, as well as to statistical model predictions from Gorenstein and Gazdzicki and from Andronic et al.

## Key numbers

- Energy densities above 16 GeV/fm^3 are reached in the early overlap phase of central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, dropping below 1 GeV/fm^3 after about 6 fm/c in the center of the grid
- The c-cbar formation time tau_c is about 0.05 fm/c for a transverse mass of 4 GeV; the transient time for central Au+Au at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV is t_r approximately 0.13 fm/c
- The baryonic absorption cross sections used are: sigma_{c-cbar B} = 4.18 mb, sigma_{J/Psi B} = 4.18 mb, sigma_{chi_c B} = 4.18 mb, and sigma_{Psi' B} = 7.6 mb
- The comover model parameter |M_0|^2 is set to 0.18 fm^2/GeV^2, fixed by comparison to NA38 and NA50 data for S+U and Pb+Pb collisions
- The HSD calculation for d+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV uses average number of binary collisions <N_coll> = 7.6 +/- 0.3 according to the PHENIX estimate

## Context

This work extends previous HSD studies of charmonium suppression at SPS energies (Linnyk 2007, Bratkovskaya 2003, 2004) to RHIC energies, finding that hadronic comover interactions alone are insufficient to describe the data. The results contradict the statistical hadronization model of Andronic et al. and the statistical model of Gorenstein and Gazdzicki in their predictions for the J/Psi to pion ratio as a function of centrality and energy. The finding that HSD substantially underestimates the elliptic flow v_2 of charm at top RHIC energies indicates that the large observed v_2 must be attributed to early interactions of non-hadronic degrees of freedom, enabling future experimental discrimination between different charmonium absorption scenarios using the Psi' to J/Psi ratio.
