# Cooper-Mesons in the Color-Flavor-Locked Superconducting Phase of Dense QCD

> QCD superconductors in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase sustain excitations (``Cooper'' mesons) that can be described as pairs of particles or holes around a gapped Fermi surface. In weak coupling and to leading logarithm accuracy the masses, decay constants and form factors of the scalar, pseudoscalar, vector and axial-vector excitations, which explicitly are of finite size, can be calculated exactly. Furthermore, the constraints of this microscopic calculation on the effective-lagrangian description and the computation of the generalized triangle anomaly are discussed.

# Cooper-Mesons in the Color-Flavor-Locked Superconducting Phase of Dense QCD

## Key claim
In the weak-coupling limit and to leading logarithm accuracy, the masses, decay constants, and form factors of scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector excitations (Cooper mesons) in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase of dense QCD can be calculated exactly from a microscopic Bethe-Salpeter approach, but the results are conditional on the leading-logarithm approximation and the assumption of weak coupling, meaning that hidden gauge symmetry and vector meson dominance are not realized at this level of approximation.

## Method
The quark propagator in the CFL phase was constructed using the Nambu-Gorkov formalism with a gap function G(q) that satisfies a non-conventional BCS gap equation incorporating Debye-screened and Landau-damped gluon propagators. The Bethe-Salpeter equation for particle-particle and hole-hole excitations around the gapped Fermi surface was solved in the leading logarithm approximation to obtain the vertex functions, masses, and decay constants of the generalized mesons, and the triangle anomaly was computed directly from the microscopic quark-loop diagram.

## Context
This work extends the effective-lagrangian description of the CFL phase introduced by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999) by providing a microscopic calculation of the parameters that are inputs to such effective theories. It builds on the gap equation analysis of Son (1999) and the effective field theory framework of Leutwyler (1994) for nonrelativistic antiferromagnetic Goldstone modes. The results enable quantitative calculations of transport properties in dense QCD matter relevant to compact stars, and the finding that vector mesons are bound and degenerate in the CFL phase has implications for dilepton and neutrino emissivities in neutron star interiors.
