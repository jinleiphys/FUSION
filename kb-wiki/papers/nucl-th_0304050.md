# Unifying aspects of light- and heavy-systems

> Dyson-Schwinger equations furnish a Poincare' covariant framework within which to study hadrons. A particular feature is the existence of a nonperturbative, symmetry preserving truncation that enables the proof of exact results. Key to the DSE's efficacious application is their expression of the materially important momentum-dependent dressing of parton propagators at infrared length-scales, which is responsible for the magnitude of constituent-quark masses and the length-scale characterising confinement in bound states. A unified quantitative description of light- and heavy-quark systems is achieved by capitalising on these features.

## Key claim

Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs), when combined with a nonperturbative, symmetry-preserving truncation scheme, can provide a unified quantitative description of both light- and heavy-quark systems by capitalizing on the momentum-dependent dressing of parton propagators at infrared length-scales. The paper argues that the rainbow-ladder truncation is accurate to within approximately 12% for the dynamically generated quark mass, and that including just one additional gluon ladder correction yields 1% accuracy.

## Method

The author employs the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) in Euclidean space, focusing on the gap equation for the dressed-quark propagator and the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for meson bound states. A systematic, symmetry-preserving truncation scheme is used, based on a dressed-loop expansion of the quark-antiquark scattering kernel, which guarantees the preservation of vector and axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identities. The analysis uses a simplified momentum-independent interaction model (the Munczek-Nemirovsky model) to reduce integral equations to algebraic equations, enabling a closed-form solution for the gluon-ladder-dressed quark-gluon vertex and the corresponding vertex-consistent Bethe-Salpeter kernel.

## Key numbers

- The dynamically generated dressed-quark mass at zero momentum, \(M(0) = B(0)/A(0)\), calculated with the fully resummed gluon-ladder vertex is 1.117 (in units of the model's mass-scale \(\mathcal{G}\)), compared to 1.0 for the rainbow truncation (N=0), 1.105 for N=1, and 1.115 for N=2.
- The pion mass in the chiral limit (\(m=0\)) is exactly zero at all orders of truncation (N=0,1,2,\(\infty\)), demonstrating model-independent Goldstone boson behavior.
- For a current-quark mass \(m = 0.011\) (in units of \(\mathcal{G}\), corresponding to 11 MeV with \(\mathcal{G}=0.48\) GeV), the pion mass is 0.152 GeV at all truncation orders.
- The \(\rho\)-meson mass in the chiral limit (\(m=0\)) is 0.678 GeV for the rainbow-ladder truncation (N=0), 0.745 GeV for N=1, 0.754 GeV for N=2, and 0.754 GeV for the fully resummed vertex (N=\(\infty\)).
- With \(m=0.011\) (11 MeV), the \(\rho\)-meson mass is 0.695 GeV (N=0), 0.762 GeV (N=1), 0.770 GeV (N=2), and 0.770 GeV (N=\(\infty\)).
- The model mass-scale is set to \(\mathcal{G} = 0.48\) GeV, with the current-quark mass \(m = 0.023\,\mathcal{G} = 11\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to hadron physics, building on prior developments in continuum strong QCD methods by Roberts and collaborators (1990s) and the systematic truncation scheme of Munczek (1995) and Bender, Roberts, and von Smekal (1996). The paper demonstrates that the rainbow-ladder truncation, which is the leading-order term in the systematic expansion, provides a quantitatively reliable description of both light mesons (pion, rho) and can be naturally extended to heavy-quark systems. The results enable future applications to heavy-light mesons and the derivation of heavy-quark symmetry limits from the DSE framework, as well as connections to lattice-QCD simulations.
