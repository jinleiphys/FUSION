# Bayesian nonparametric inference of neutron star equation of state via neural network

> We develop a new nonparametric method to reconstruct the Equation of State (EoS) of Neutron Star with multimessenger data. As an universal function approximator, the Feed-Forward Neural Network (FFNN) with one hidden layer and a sigmoidal activation function can approximately fit any continuous function. Thus we are able to implement the nonparametric FFNN representation of the EoSs. This new representation is validated by its capabilities of fitting the theoretical EoSs and recovering the injected parameters. Then we adopt this nonparametric method to analyze the real data, including mass-tidal deformability measurement from the Binary Neutron Star (BNS) merger Gravitational Wave (GW) event GW170817 and mass-radius measurement of PSR J0030+0451 by {\it NICER}. We take the publicly available samples to construct the likelihood and use the nested sampling to obtain the posteriors of the parameters of FFNN according to the Bayesian theorem, which in turn can be translated to the posteriors of EoS parameters. Combining all these data, for a canonical 1.4 $M_\odot$ neutron star, we get the radius $R_{1.4}=11.83^{+1.25}_{-1.08}$ km and the tidal deformability $Λ_{1.4} = 323^{+334}_{-165}$ (90\% confidence interval).Furthermore, we find that in the high density region ($\geq 3ρ_{\rm sat}$), the 90\% lower limits of the $c_{\rm s}^2/c^2$ ($c_{\rm s}$ is the sound speed and $c$ is the velocity of light in the vacuum) are above $1/3$, which means that the so-called conformal limit (i.e., $c_{\rm s}^2/c^2<1/3$) is not always valid in the neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors develop a nonparametric method using a Feed-Forward Neural Network (FFNN) to represent the neutron star equation of state directly, rather than as an interpolation tool between observations and EoS parameters, and find that the conformal limit (c_s^2/c^2 < 1/3) is violated at high densities (≥ 3ρ_sat) at 90% confidence, though the model does not enforce convergence to the conformal limit at asymptotically high densities as some prior works do.

## Method

The authors represent the neutron star EoS using a FFNN with one hidden layer of 10 nodes and a sigmoidal activation function, taking log(pressure) as input and the auxiliary variable φ = log(c^2 dε/dp - 1) as output, which automatically enforces microscopic stability and causality. They perform Bayesian inference via nested sampling (PyMultiNest) using a likelihood that combines the GW170817 gravitational wave data (via interpolated likelihood), the mass-radius measurement of PSR J0030+0451 from NICER (via Gaussian KDE), the mass measurement of PSR J0740+6620, and nuclear physics constraints on pressure at ρ_sat and 1.85ρ_sat. The enthalpy-based formalism of Lindblom (2014) is used to solve the TOV and Regge-Wheeler equations to obtain mass, radius, and tidal deformability.

## Key numbers

- For a canonical 1.4 M⊙ neutron star, radius R_{1.4} = 11.83^{+1.25}_{-1.08} km (90% confidence interval)
- For a canonical 1.4 M⊙ neutron star, tidal deformability Λ_{1.4} = 323^{+334}_{-165} (90% confidence interval)
- The 90% lower limits of c_s^2/c^2 are above 1/3 in the high density region (≥ 3ρ_sat), indicating violation of the conformal limit
- Pressure at ρ_sat constrained to 3.12 × 10^33 dyn/cm^2 ≤ p(ρ_sat) ≤ 4.70 × 10^33 dyn/cm^2, and p(1.85ρ_sat) ≥ 1.21 × 10^34 dyn/cm^2 (nuclear constraints from prior literature)
- Absolute fitting errors |φ - φ_fit| are on the order of 10^{-4} to 10^{-1} for theoretical EoSs where φ is of order 0.1 to 10
- Relative errors |1 - Γ_fit/Γ| in calculating Γ(p) are as low as 0.1% to 10%

## Context

This work extends the nonparametric approach to neutron star EoS inference previously done via Gaussian processes (Landry et al. 2020; Essick et al. 2020) by using a FFNN representation that has fewer parameters (31) and enables efficient MCMC sampling, unlike the Monte Carlo integration required for GP methods. It differs from the machine learning approach of Fujimoto et al. (2018, 2020) who used FFNN as an interpolation tool between observations and EoS parameters with training data from parametric forms, whereas here the FFNN directly represents the EoS itself within a Bayesian framework. The results agree with joint multimessenger analyses by Dietrich et al. (2020), Raaijmakers et al. (2020), and others, and the finding that the conformal limit is violated supports the conjecture of Bedaque and Steiner (2015) that heavy neutron stars (~2 M⊙) require such violation.
