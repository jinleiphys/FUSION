# Lattice QCD spectroscopy for hadronic CP violation

> The interpretation of nuclear electric dipole moment (EDM) experiments is clouded by large theoretical uncertainties associated with nonperturbative matrix elements. In various beyond-the-Standard Model scenarios nuclear and diamagnetic atomic EDMs are expected to be dominated by CP-violating pion-nucleon interactions that arise from quark chromo-electric dipole moments. The corresponding CP-violating pion-nucleon coupling strengths are, however, poorly known. In this work we propose a strategy to calculate these couplings by using spectroscopic lattice QCD techniques. Instead of directly calculating the pion-nucleon coupling constants, a challenging task, we use chiral symmetry relations that link the pion-nucleon couplings to nucleon sigma terms and mass splittings that are significantly easier to calculate. In this work, we show that these relations are reliable up to next-to-next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion in both SU(2) and SU(3) chiral perturbation theory. We conclude with a brief discussion about practical details regarding the required lattice QCD calculations and the phenomenological impact of an improved understanding of CP-violating matrix elements.

## Key claim

The authors propose that CP-violating pion-nucleon coupling constants arising from quark chromo-electric dipole moments can be reliably extracted from lattice QCD calculations of nucleon sigma terms and mass splittings, provided the chiral symmetry relations are modified to be protected from next-to-leading-order and the dominant next-to-next-to-leading-order chiral corrections, contrary to earlier concerns that higher-order corrections would spoil such relations.

## Method

The authors use SU(2) and SU(3) chiral perturbation theory to derive modified relations linking the CP-violating pion-nucleon couplings (bar g_0 and bar g_1) to derivatives of the nucleon mass and mass splitting with respect to quark chromo-magnetic dipole moment couplings and quark masses. They demonstrate that these modified relations are preserved at next-to-leading order and survive the dominant next-to-next-to-leading-order corrections, unlike the leading-order tree-level relations which receive large corrections. The analysis involves computing loop diagrams and counterterms up to next-to-next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of vacuum condensates r is estimated by naive dimensional analysis as r ~ O(Lambda_chi^2) with Lambda_chi ~ 1 GeV, and a QCD sum rules result gives r ~ 0.4 GeV^2
- The physical pion decay constant is F_pi = 92.2 MeV
- A determination of the isoscalar CP-violating pion-nucleon coupling bar g_0(theta) from the QCD theta term with O(15%) uncertainty was previously achieved using lattice calculations of the isovector nucleon mass splitting
- The modified relations for bar g_0 and bar g_1 are shown to be reliable up to next-to-next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion in both SU(2) and SU(3) chiral perturbation theory

## Context

This work extends the chiral symmetry approach previously applied to the QCD theta term by Crewther (1979) and de Vries (2015), where bar g_0 was related to the neutron-proton mass splitting. It addresses concerns raised by Seng (2016) that higher-order chiral corrections strongly violate the tree-level relations for the quark chromo-electric dipole moment case. The proposed modified relations enable lattice QCD calculations of CP-violating pion-nucleon couplings using spectroscopic methods rather than direct three-point function calculations, which are substantially more difficult. This is motivated by the need to interpret nuclear and atomic electric dipole moment experiments, where these couplings often dominate over single-nucleon contributions.
