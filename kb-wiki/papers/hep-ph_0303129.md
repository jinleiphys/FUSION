# Covariant derivative expansion of Yang-Mills effective action at high temperatures

> Integrating out fast varying quantum fluctuations about Yang--Mills fields A_i and A_4, we arrive at the effective action for those fields at high temperatures. Assuming that the fields A_i and A_4 are slowly varying but that the amplitude of A_4 is arbitrary, we find a non-trivial effective gauge invariant action both in the electric and magnetic sectors. Our results can be used for studying correlation functions at high temperatures beyond the dimensional reduction approximation, as well as for estimating quantum weights of classical static configurations such as dyons.

## Key claim

The paper derives the 1-loop effective action for Yang-Mills fields at high temperatures by integrating out fast quantum fluctuations, assuming the spatial fields \(A_i\) and the temporal component \(A_4\) are slowly varying but allowing the amplitude of \(A_4\) to be arbitrarily large, not just small as in the dimensional reduction approximation.

## Method

The authors use the background field method, splitting the gluon fields into a static background and quantum fluctuations, then integrating out the fluctuations to obtain functional determinants for ghosts and gluons. They evaluate these determinants using a covariant derivative expansion in powers of spatial derivatives \(D_i\) while summing all powers of the \(A_4\) field amplitude, employing the Schwinger proper time representation and Pauli-Villars regularization. The calculation is performed explicitly for the \(SU(2)\) gauge group up to fourth order in covariant derivatives, yielding the functions multiplying the gauge-invariant structures \(E_i^2\), \((E_i A_4)^2\), \(B_i^2\), and \((B_i A_4)^2\).

## Key numbers

* The static potential is \(V(\nu) = \frac{(2\pi)^2}{3} \nu^2 (1-\nu)^2\) modulo 1, where \(\nu = \sqrt{A_4^a A_4^a} / (2\pi T)\).
* The electric sector function \(f_1\) is \(\frac{11}{48\pi^2}\left[2\log\frac{M}{4\pi T} - \psi(-\nu/2) - \psi(\nu/2) + \frac{20}{11\nu}\right]\), with \(\psi\) the digamma function and \(M\) the Pauli-Villars mass.
* The electric sector function \(f_2\) is \(\frac{11}{48\pi^2}\left[\psi(-\nu/2) + \psi(\nu/2) - \psi(\nu) - \psi(1-\nu) - \frac{20}{11\nu}\right]\).
* The magnetic sector functions \(h_1\) and \(h_2\) are also expressed through digamma functions (explicit forms given in the full text but truncated here).
* The running-coupling term contains \(\frac{11}{24\pi^2} \ln\left(\frac{T}{\Lambda} \text{const.}\right)\), with the constant determined to relate the Pauli-Villars and \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) schemes.

## Context

This work extends the known 1-loop static potential for \(A_4\) from Gross, Pisarski, and Yaffe (1981) and Weiss (1981) by computing the kinetic terms for both electric and magnetic sectors at all values of the Polyakov loop. It goes beyond the dimensional reduction approximation of Appelquist and Pisarski (1981) and agrees with partial results from Chapman (1994) and from Belyaev, Gockeler, and Kalloniatis (1996) for the special case \(A_i=0\). The results enable the study of correlation functions and quantum weights of semiclassical configurations such as dyons at temperatures where the Polyakov loop deviates significantly from its perturbative center-group values.
