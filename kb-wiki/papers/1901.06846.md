# Determination of Strength of Isoscalar Pairing Interaction by a Mathematical Identity in QRPA

> I propose a new method to determine the strength of the isoscalar proton-neutron pairing interaction by a mathematical identity derived in the quasiparticle random-phase approximation. This method is applied for a few nuclei possibly having the neutrinoless double-$β$ decay. Reduced half-life, the theoretical quantity necessary for determining the effective neutrino mass, is calculated for $^{48}$Ca.

## Key claim

The strength of the isoscalar proton-neutron pairing interaction can be determined by requiring that two different QRPA expressions for the same double-charge-change transition matrix element yield identical numerical values, under the presumption that all other interactions are already established.

## Method

The author calculates the nuclear matrix element for neutrinoless double-beta decay using two distinct QRPA formulations: one based on proton-neutron QRPA (pnQRPA) and the other on like-particle QRPA (lpQRPA). The equality of these two expressions imposes a constraint on the effective interactions, which is used to determine the isoscalar pairing interaction strength. The method is applied to four candidate nuclei for neutrinoless double-beta decay using the Skyrme SkM* interaction for particle-hole channels and contact volume pairing interactions.

## Key numbers

- The isoscalar pairing interaction strengths for the four nuclei studied range between -50.0 and -197.4 MeV fm^3.
- The like-particle pairing interaction strengths for the same nuclei range between -176.4 and -224.5 MeV fm^3.
- For 48Ca, assuming an effective neutrino mass of 10 meV, the predicted half-life for neutrinoless double-beta decay is 2 x 10^29 years.
- The age of the universe is given as approximately 13.8 x 10^9 years for comparison.

## Context

This work extends previous QRPA calculations of neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements, which had relied on indirect determinations of the isoscalar pairing interaction strength by reproducing experimental data not strongly sensitive to proton-neutron pairing correlations, as done by Simkovic (2013). The new method eliminates an important uncertainty in QRPA calculations of the reduced half-life, which is the theoretical quantity needed to extract the effective neutrino mass from experimental half-life measurements. The calculated reduced half-life for 48Ca is compared with results from other groups using the shell model, interacting boson model-2, and generator-coordinate method.
