# Exploring hyperon structure with electromagnetic transverse densities

> We explore the structure of the spin-1/2 flavor-octet baryons (hyperons) through their electromagnetic transverse densities. The transverse densities describe the distribution of charge and magnetization at fixed light-front time and enable a spatial representation of the baryons as relativistic systems. At peripheral distances b~1/M_pi the transverse densities are computed using a new method that combines chiral effective field theory and dispersion analysis. The peripheral isovector densities arise from two-pion exchange, which includes the rho-meson resonance through elastic unitarity. The isoscalar densities are estimated from vector meson exchange (omega, phi). We find that the "pion cloud" in the charged Sigma hyperons is comparable to the nucleon, while in the Xi it is suppressed. The Lambda-Sigma^0 transition density is pure isovector and represents a clear manifestiation of peripheral two-pion dynamics.

# Exploring hyperon structure with electromagnetic transverse densities

## Key claim
The peripheral isovector charge and magnetization densities of charged Sigma hyperons are comparable in magnitude to those of the nucleon, while those of Xi hyperons are substantially suppressed; these results depend on the isospin factors in the pi-BB' couplings and the relative contributions of intermediate octet and decuplet states in the chiral effective field theory Born graphs, and are computed with controlled accuracy only down to distances b ~ 1 fm.

## Method
The authors computed the isovector spectral functions of the SU(3) octet baryons on the two-pion cut using elastic unitarity, the N/D method, and dynamical input from chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) at leading order with relativistic octet and decuplet baryons, combined with the empirical timelike pion form factor from e+e- annihilation experiments. The isoscalar spectral functions were modeled by vector meson exchange (omega, phi) with couplings constrained by SU(3) symmetry and dispersive fits to nucleon form factor data. The peripheral transverse charge and magnetization densities were then evaluated through dispersive integrals of the spectral functions using modified Bessel functions.

## Key numbers
- The peripheral isovector densities are computed for distances b ~ 1/M_pi, corresponding to b ~ 1.4 fm
- The two-pion threshold is at t_thr = 4 M_pi^2, with the rho resonance at t ~ 30 M_pi^2
- The isovector densities decay approximately as ~ exp(-M_rho b) at b ~ 1 fm, with M_rho ~ 770 MeV
- The Lambda-Sigma^0 transition form factor is accessible experimentally through the Dalitz decay Sigma^0 -> Lambda e+e- at timelike momentum transfers 4 m_e^2 < t < (m_Sigma0 - m_Lambda)^2 = 0.006 GeV^2
- The spectral functions are constructed in the region 4 M_pi^2 < t < 1 GeV^2, which includes the rho meson resonance
- The isoscalar densities decay with the omega mass; isoscalar and isovector densities become comparable only at distances b < 2 fm
- The Lambda and Sigma^0 peripheral densities are an order of magnitude smaller than for other hyperons at b > 2 fm

## Context
This work extends the transverse density approach previously applied to nucleon structure (Miller 2007, Miller 2011) to the full SU(3) flavor-octet baryons, using a new method combining chiEFT and dispersion analysis developed by Alarcon (2017). The results enable model-independent predictions for hyperon peripheral structure that can be compared with lattice QCD calculations (Lin 2008, Shanahan 2014) and with experimental measurements of the Lambda-Sigma^0 transition form factor via Dalitz decay. The method also permits quark flavor decomposition of the transverse densities, showing the transition from pion cloud at b > 3 fm to a mean-field picture of valence quarks at b ~ 1 fm.
