# Microscopic calculations based on chiral two- and three-nucleon forces for proton- and $^{4}$He-nucleus scattering

> We investigate the effects of chiral three-nucleon force (3NF) on proton scattering at 65 MeV and $^{4}$He scattering at 72 MeV/nucleon from heavier targets, using the standard microscopic framework composed of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) method and the $g$-matrix folding model. For nuclear matter, the $g$ matrix is evaluated from chiral two-nucleon force (2NF) of N$^{3}$LO and chiral 3NF of NNLO by using the BHF method. Since the $g$ matrix thus obtained is numerical and nonlocal, an optimum local form is determined from the on-shell and near-on-shell components of $g$ matrix that are important for elastic scattering. For elastic scattering, the optical potentials are calculated by folding the local chiral $g$ matrix with projectile and target densities. This microscopic framework reproduces the experimental data without introducing any adjustable parameter. Chiral-3NF effects are small for proton scattering, but sizable for $^{4}$He scattering at middle angles where the data are available. Chiral 3NF, mainly in the 2$π$-exchange diagram, makes the folding potential less attractive and more absorptive for all the scattering.

## Key claim

Chiral three-nucleon force (3NF) effects are small for proton scattering at 65 MeV but sizable for 4He scattering at 72 MeV/nucleon at middle angles, with the 3NF making the folding potential less attractive and more absorptive; the paper claims this without introducing any adjustable parameter, but the results depend on the specific choice of low-energy constants (c1,c3,c4) = (-0.81,-3.4,3.4) GeV^-1 and (cD,cE) = (-4.381,-1.126) from chiral effective field theory.

## Method

The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations for symmetric nuclear matter using chiral two-nucleon force (2NF) at N3LO and chiral three-nucleon force (3NF) at NNLO, deriving an effective two-nucleon force by averaging the 3NF over the third nucleon in the Fermi sea. The resulting numerical and nonlocal g-matrix was then converted into a local 3-range Gaussian form by chi-squared fitting to the on-shell and near-on-shell components, and this local chiral g-matrix was folded with projectile and target densities to produce optical potentials for elastic scattering.

## Key numbers

- Proton scattering at 65 MeV from 40Ca, 58Ni, and 208Pb targets: chiral-3NF effects enhance the spin-orbit part of the optical potential by about 30 percent, visible in vector analyzing power at theta_cm approximately 60 degrees for 40Ca and 58Ni
- 4He scattering at 72 MeV/nucleon from 58Ni and 208Pb targets: chiral-3NF effects are sizable at middle angles theta_cm greater than or approximately 20 degrees where experimental data are available
- The 3-range Gaussian form for the central part of the g-matrix uses ranges of (0.4, 0.9, 2.5) fm
- Cutoff energy Lambda = 550 MeV used with form factor exp{-(q'/Lambda)^6 - (q/Lambda)^6} for both V12 and V12(3)

## Context

This work extends previous exploratory studies by Toyokawa (2014) and Minomo (2014) that simulated 3NF effects by modifying the Melbourne g-matrix with multiplicative factors, now providing full chiral g-matrices from chiral effective field theory. The results confirm that the Melbourne g-matrix approach without 3NFs works well for nucleon-nucleus scattering because it is governed by low-density surface regions, while for 4He scattering the 3NF effects become important at middle angles where far-side scattering dominates. The framework enables systematic microscopic calculations of optical potentials for heavier projectiles and targets without adjustable parameters, building on the density-approximation methods developed by Egashira (2014) and Toyokawa (2015) for 3,4He scattering.
