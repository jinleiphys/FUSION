# Flavor and Spin Structure of Octet Baryons at Large x

> The quark flavor and spin distributions in octet baryons are calculated both in the SU(6) quark spectator diquark model and in a perturbative QCD (pQCD) based model. It is shown that the $Λ$ has the most significant difference in flavor structure at large $x$ between the two models, though the flavor and spin structure of other baryons can also provide tests of different models. The Drell-Yan process for $Σ^{\pm}$ beams on isoscalar targets can be used to test different predictions concerning the valence quark flavor structure of the $Σ^{\pm}$.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that the Lambda baryon has the most significant difference in flavor structure at large x between the SU(6) quark spectator diquark model and a perturbative QCD based model, though the abstract glosses over the condition that this conclusion depends on the choice of SU(6) symmetry with a specific mixing angle theta = pi/4 and particular parameter choices for the pQCD model.

## Method

The authors extended the SU(6) quark spectator diquark model and a perturbative QCD based model from the nucleon and Lambda cases to all octet baryons, calculating unpolarized and polarized valence quark distributions. For the SU(6) model, they used the Brodsky-Huang-Lepage prescription for the light-cone momentum space wave function with specific quark and diquark masses. For the pQCD model, they adopted canonical forms for quark distributions with parameters constrained by SU(3) symmetry from hyperon decay data and deep inelastic scattering measurements.

## Key numbers

- The ratio (q2/q1) at x approaches 1 for the Lambda: in the diquark model it approaches 0, while in the pQCD model it approaches 1/2, representing the largest difference among all octet baryons
- For all other octet baryons besides Sigma0 and Lambda, (q2/q1) at x approaches 1: diquark model gives 0, pQCD model gives 1/5
- For Sigma0 with q1=u,d and q2=s, (q2/q1) at x approaches 1: diquark model gives 0, pQCD model gives 2/5
- For all octet baryons besides Lambda, (Delta q2/q2) at x approaches 1: diquark model gives -1/3, pQCD model gives 1
- The strange quark content ratio kappa = 2s_bar/(u_bar + d_bar) in the nucleon is in the range 0.373 to 0.57 from neutrino-induced charm production experiments
- The authors used kappa(x2) = 0.44 at x2 = 0.3 from CTEQ parameterizations for their Drell-Yan ratio predictions

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of nucleon and Lambda quark structure by Ma (1996) and the authors' own earlier work (MSY2, MSY3) to all octet baryons. The results are consistent with a calculation of octet baryon quark structure in the MIT bag model by Boros and Thomas (1999), though with some differences in detailed features. The paper enables experimental tests of different models through Drell-Yan processes using Sigma+ and Sigma- beams on isoscalar targets, and suggests that Xi- beams could also be used to measure valence quark distributions.
