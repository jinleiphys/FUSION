# Gluon imaging using azimuthal correlations in diffractive scattering at the Electron-Ion Collider

> We study coherent diffractive photon and vector meson production in electron-proton and electron-nucleus collisions within the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory. We show that electron-photon and electron-vector meson azimuthal angle correlations are sensitive to non-trivial spatial correlations in the gluon distribution of the target, and perform explicit calculations using spatially dependent McLerran-Venugopalan initial color charge configurations coupled to the numerical solution of small $x$ JIMWLK evolution equations. We compute the cross-section differentially in $Q^2$ and $|t|$ and find sizeable anisotropies in the electron-photon and electron-$\mathrm{J}/ψ$ azimuthal correlations ($v_{1,2} \approx 2 - 10 \%$) in electron-proton collisions for the kinematics of the future Electron-Ion Collider. In electron-gold collisions these modulations are found to be significantly smaller ($v_{1,2} <0.1 \%$). We also compute incoherent diffractive production where we find that the azimuthal correlations are sensitive to fluctuations of the gluon distribution in the target.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that azimuthal angle correlations between the scattered electron and produced photon or J/ψ in coherent diffractive scattering at the future Electron-Ion Collider are sensitive to non-trivial spatial correlations in the gluon distribution of the target, with sizeable anisotropies (v₁,₂ ≈ 2–10%) predicted for electron-proton collisions but significantly smaller modulations (v₁,₂ < 0.1%) for electron-gold collisions.

## Method

The authors compute coherent diffractive photon (DVCS) and J/ψ production cross-sections within the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory using the dipole picture, where the virtual photon fluctuates into a quark-antiquark pair that scatters eikonally with the target's dense gluon field. They employ spatially dependent McLerran-Venugopalan initial color charge configurations coupled to numerical solutions of the small-x JIMWLK evolution equations, and also use a simpler impact parameter dependent Golec-Biernat-Wüsthoff model with angular correlations as a baseline. The cross-section is computed differentially in Q² and |t|, and the azimuthal angle correlations between the electron and produced vector particle are extracted from the lepton-hadron tensor decomposition in the virtual photon polarization basis.

## Key numbers

- Azimuthal anisotropy coefficients v₁ and v₂ for electron-proton collisions: approximately 2–10% for both DVCS and J/ψ production at EIC kinematics
- Azimuthal anisotropy coefficients v₁ and v₂ for electron-gold collisions: less than 0.1%
- The cross-section is computed differentially in virtuality Q² and momentum transfer |t|, with |t| approximately equal to the squared transverse momentum transfer Δ²⊥

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of azimuthal correlations in DVCS within the dipole framework by Hatta et al. (2017), who first identified cos(φ) and cos(2φ) modulations generated by the elliptic gluon Wigner distribution. The calculations provide predictions for the future Electron-Ion Collider, enabling experimental access to the spatial gluon distribution of protons and nuclei through exclusive diffractive processes, complementing prior HERA measurements of exclusive vector meson production (Aktas et al., 2005; Chekanov et al., 2002) and DVCS (Adloff et al., 2001; Aktas et al., 2005). The results also demonstrate that incoherent diffractive production at moderate |t| is sensitive to event-by-event fluctuations of the spatial gluon distribution in the target.
