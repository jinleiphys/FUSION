# From nuclear matter to Neutron Stars

> Neutron stars are the most dense objects in the observable Universe and conventionally one uses nuclear theory to obtain the equation of state (EOS) of dense hadronic matter and the global properties of these stars. In this work, we review various aspects of nuclear matter within an effective Chiral model and interlink fundamental quantities both from nuclear saturation as well as vacuum properties and correlate it with the star properties.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that an effective chiral model can interlink nuclear saturation properties, vacuum quantities (such as the pion decay constant and sigma meson mass), and neutron star global properties, but the model's parameters are constrained by heavy-ion collision data to favor a nucleon effective mass ratio \(m^{\star}/m > 0.82\) and a specific parameter set with \(Y = 0.85\) and incompressibility \(K \approx 300\) MeV, rather than a single universal prediction.

## Method

The author constructs an effective chiral Lagrangian including the lowest-lying octet of baryons interacting via scalar (\(\sigma\)), vector (\(\omega\)), and isovector (\(\rho\)) mesons, with nonlinear terms in the scalar field. Parameters are fixed by requiring symmetric nuclear matter saturation at density \(\rho_0 = 0.153\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) and binding energy per nucleon \(B/A - m = -16.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\), for a range of nucleon effective mass ratios \(Y = m^{\star}/m = 0.75\) to \(0.90\). The equation of state (EOS) for beta-equilibrated matter is then computed self-consistently, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations for both nucleon-only and hyperon-rich compositions.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density fixed at \(\rho_0 = 0.153\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) and binding energy per nucleon at \(-16.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* Nuclear incompressibility \(K\) ranges from 163 MeV (set 16, \(Y=0.90\)) to 1098 MeV (set 1, \(Y=0.75\)); heavy-ion collision data constrain \(K\) to the range \(167-380\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* Scalar meson mass \(m_{\sigma}\) ranges from 339.9 MeV (set 16) to 691.4 MeV (set 1); the experimental estimate is \(513 \pm 32\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (Murakami 2002).
* Pion decay constant \(f_{\pi}\) ranges from 110.2 MeV (set 1) to 193.4 MeV (set 16); the experimental value is approximately 92.4 MeV.
* Maximum neutron star mass for nucleon-only matter ranges from \(1.71\ M_{\odot}\) (set 16) to \(2.49\ M_{\odot}\) (set 1); for hyperon-rich matter it ranges from \(1.37\ M_{\odot}\) to \(2.04\ M_{\odot}\).
* Radius of the maximum-mass star for nucleon-only matter ranges from 11.0 km (set 16) to 14.2 km (set 1); for hyperon-rich matter it ranges from 16.3 km to 20.8 km.
* Central density of the maximum-mass star for nucleon-only matter ranges from \(1.26 \times 10^{15}\ \mathrm{g\ cm^{-3}}\) to \(2.54 \times 10^{15}\ \mathrm{g\ cm^{-3}}\); for hyperon-rich matter it ranges from \(0.59 \times 10^{15}\ \mathrm{g\ cm^{-3}}\) to \(1.03 \times 10^{15}\ \mathrm{g\ cm^{-3}}\).
* The EOS with \(Y = 0.87\) and \(K = 231\ \mathrm{MeV}\) agrees completely with heavy-ion collision flow data for symmetric nuclear matter in the density range \(2 < \rho_B/\rho_0 < 4.6\).

## Context

This work reviews and extends the application of effective chiral models to nuclear matter and neutron stars, building on the framework of Quantum Hadrodynamics (Walecka 1974, Serot and Walecka 1986) and earlier chiral model studies (e.g., references ch01, ch02). It addresses the longstanding "Coester band" problem and the uncertainty in nuclear incompressibility (ranging from 180 to 800 MeV in the literature) and the poorly constrained sigma meson mass (400-1200 MeV from the Particle Data Group). The results enable future studies of rotating neutron stars and mixed-phase or quark matter interiors, as noted by the author's work in progress (Jha 2009).
