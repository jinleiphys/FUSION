# Low-energy Electro-weak Reactions

> Chiral effective field theory (EFT) provides a systematic and controlled approach to low-energy nuclear physics. Here, we use chiral EFT to calculate low-energy weak Gamow-Teller transitions. We put special emphasis on the role of two-body (2b) weak currents within the nucleus, and discuss their applications in predicting physical observables.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory enables parameter-free calculations of low-energy Gamow-Teller transitions, but the theory's predictive power depends on calibrating the unknown low-energy constant \(c_D\) (which connects two-body weak currents to three-nucleon forces) against the triton beta-decay half-life; the resulting predictions for other observables (e.g., \(^4\)He properties, muon capture on \(^3\)He, and \(^6\)He beta decay) then serve as tests of the framework.

## Method

The author uses chiral EFT to construct the nuclear axial current up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N\(^3\)LO), including one-body currents with form-factor corrections and two-body (2b) meson-exchange currents. For \(A=3\) systems, the Schrödinger equation is solved with a chiral N\(^3\)LO nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential and a next-to-next-to-leading order (N\(^2\)LO) three-nucleon (NNN) force; the unknown contact LEC \(c_D\) is fixed by reproducing the empirical Gamow-Teller matrix element extracted from triton beta decay. For \(^6\)He, the phenomenological JISP16 potential is used, and for muon capture on \(^3\)He, the Argonne \(v_{18}\) (AV18) plus Urbana IX (UIX) potentials are combined with the chiral current. The in-medium quenching of the axial coupling is studied via a normal-ordered 1b approximation in a Fermi-gas reference state.

## Key numbers

* Triton beta decay: the extracted LECs are \(c_D = -0.2 \pm 0.1\) and \(c_E = -0.205 \pm 0.015\) (Gazit).
* \(^3\)H ground-state energy with NN+NNN force: \(-8.473(4)\) MeV (experiment: \(-8.482\) MeV); point-proton radius: \(1.605(5)\) fm (experiment: \(1.60\) fm).
* \(^4\)He ground-state energy with NN+NNN force: \(-28.50(2)\) MeV (experiment: \(-28.296\) MeV); point-proton radius: \(1.461(2)\) fm (experiment: \(1.467(13)\) fm).
* Muon capture rate on \(^3\)He: predicted \(\Gamma = 1499 (2)_\Lambda (3)_{\rm{NM}} (5)_t (6)_{\mathrm{RC}}\) Hz, compared to the experimental rate \(1496(4)\) Hz.
* Induced pseudoscalar form factor from muon capture: \(g_P(q^2=-0.954 m_\mu^2) = 8.13 \pm 0.6\).
* Second-class current constraint: \(m_e F_S / F_V = (0.5 \pm 2) \times 10^{-4}\), consistent with CVC.
* \(^6\)He beta decay: theoretical Gamow-Teller matrix element \(|\rm{GT}(^6{\rm He})|_{\rm theo} = 2.198 \pm 0.007\); experimental value \(2.161 \pm 0.005\) (overprediction by about 1.7%).
* In-medium quenching: at nuclear saturation density (\(\rho \approx 0.10\)–\(0.12\) fm\(^{-3}\)), the effective axial coupling \(g_A^{\rm eff}\) is predicted to be between \(0.74\,g_A\) and \(0.96\,g_A\) depending on the calibration of \(c_D\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT program for nuclear forces and currents (Weinberg, van Kolck, Epelbaum, Machleidt, and others) to weak Gamow-Teller transitions, emphasizing the consistency between two-body axial currents and three-nucleon forces via the common LEC \(c_D\). It builds on prior calculations of the triton half-life (Gazit 2009) and muon capture on \(^3\)He (Gazit 2008) to constrain the weak structure of the nucleon. The in-medium quenching analysis addresses the long-standing puzzle of the “quenched” axial coupling in nuclei (Wildenthal 1983, QRPA/EDF studies) and provides a microscopic explanation via chiral 2b currents. The results enable parameter-free predictions for neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements (Javier 2011) and for WIMP-nucleus scattering (Javier 2012).
