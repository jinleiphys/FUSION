# Shape Coexistence and the Effective Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction

> The phenomenon of shape coexistence is discussed within the self-consistent Hartree-Fock method and the nuclear shell model. The occurrence of the coexisting configurations with different intrinsic shapes is traced back to the properties of the effective Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

Shape coexistence arises from the interplay between the average (liquid-drop) part of the nuclear energy and the shell correction, and the relative energies of coexisting states are strongly affected by model uncertainties such as treatment of pairing, surface tension, and level density, rather than by the intrinsic configurations themselves.

## Method

The authors applied the self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (HF) method with eight different Skyrme parameterizations (SkM*, SkT6, Zσ, SkP, SLy4, SkI1, SkI3, SkI4, SkO, and SkO') to compute potential energy surfaces as functions of quadrupole deformation β for nuclei in four shape-coexistence regions. They also employed the nuclear shell model with the Heyde formula to decompose the intruder-state energy into particle-hole, pairing, monopole, and quadrupole contributions. The HF calculations included zero-point energy corrections for rotational and vibrational motion using the Gaussian Overlap Approximation.

## Key numbers

- The surface energy coefficient a_s ranges from approximately 17 MeV (for "soft" interactions Zσ, SkO, SkO') to approximately 20 MeV (for "stiff" interactions SkT6, SLy4, SkP).
- For ³²Mg, the prolate minimum lies from 2 MeV to 4 MeV above the spherical ground state for most Skyrme forces, but is practically degenerate with the spherical minimum for SkM* and Zσ.
- The deformed configuration in ³⁰Ne lies approximately 1 MeV higher in energy than that in ³²Mg for all forces used.
- The effective nucleon mass varies from 1.0 (SkT6, SkP) to approximately 0.65 (SLy4, SkI1, SkI3, SkI4), with SkM* and Zσ around 0.8.
- The asymmetry energy ranges from 26 MeV (Zσ) to 38 MeV (SkI1), with most forces around 30–32 MeV.
- The incompressibility K for all selected forces is 210–250 MeV.
- For ³⁴Mg, the deformed intrinsic shape has β ranging from 0.3 to 0.4.
- The 2⁺ state in ³²Mg lies at 886 keV experimentally.

## Context

This work extends the mean-field and shell-model analyses of shape coexistence reviewed by Heyde (1983) and Wood (1992), and builds on the Strutinsky energy theorem (Strutinsky, 1968; Brack, 1972) to connect coexistence to effective Hamiltonian properties. The systematic comparison of ten Skyrme parameterizations across four mass regions (N≈20, N≈28, N≈Z≈40, and N≈56, Z≈40) enables future studies to identify which force properties (surface tension, effective mass, spin-orbit strength) most critically determine the excitation energies of intruder states, and highlights the need for improved pairing treatments in light nuclei.
