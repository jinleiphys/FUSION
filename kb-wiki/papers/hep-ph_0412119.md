# Universality of the EMC Effect

> Using effective field theory, we investigate nuclear modification of nucleon parton distributions (for example, the EMC effect). We show that the universality of the shape distortion in nuclear parton distributions (the factorisation of the Bjorken x and atomic number (A) dependence) is model independent and emerges naturally in effective field theory. We then extend our analysis to study the analogous nuclear modifications in isospin and spin dependent parton distributions and generalised parton distributions.

## Key claim

Using effective field theory, the authors show that the observed universality of the shape distortion in nuclear parton distributions (the factorisation of Bjorken x and atomic number A dependence) is model independent and emerges naturally from QCD symmetries and scale separation, provided that higher-order contributions (such as pionic and three-body operators) are suppressed to N³LO or beyond.

## Method

The authors match quark-level twist-two operators to hadronic operators in an effective field theory where nucleons are the relevant degrees of freedom, keeping the leading one-body and two-body operators. They then compute nuclear matrix elements of these operators to obtain moments of nuclear parton distributions, showing that the ratio of deviations for different moments is independent of A, which forces the x and A dependence of the nuclear modification to factorise. The analysis is extended to isospin-dependent, spin-dependent, and generalised parton distributions using the same operator matching procedure.

## Key numbers

- The EMC effect in iron shows a deviation from unity of up to 20% over the range 0.05 < x < 0.65.
- The two-body operator β_n (non-singlet in SU(4)) is suppressed relative to α_n by β_n/α_n = O(1/N_c²) ≈ 0.1, where N_c is the number of colors.
- The matrix element of (N†τN)² for an isoscalar state with atomic number A is smaller than that of (N†N)² by a factor of A.
- α₀ = 0 by charge conservation, and α₁ ≈ 0 from asymptotic relations and experimental data.
- Λ₀ is chosen as 1 fm⁻¹ as an arbitrary dimensionful parameter in the definition of G(A).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach previously applied to meson and single nucleon systems (AS, CJ, Detmold 2005) to the multi-nucleon case, providing a model-independent derivation of the factorisation observed in data from the European Muon Collaboration (Aubert 1983) and confirmed by many subsequent experiments (Arneodo 1992, Geesaman 1995, Piller 1999, Norton 2003). The analysis enables first-principles lattice QCD calculations of the shape distortion function g(x) through finite volume scaling of two-nucleon energy levels (Luscher 1986, Beane 2003, Detmold 2004), and predicts analogous factorisations in spin-dependent structure functions and generalised parton distributions that can be tested by future experiments such as MINERνA.
