# Violation of the Holographic Viscosity Bound in a Strongly Coupled Anisotropic Plasma

> We study the conductivity and shear viscosity tensors of a strongly coupled N=4 super-Yang-Mills plasma which is kept anisotropic by a theta parameter that depends linearly on one of the spatial dimensions. Its holographic dual is given by an anisotropic axion-dilaton-gravity background and has recently been proposed by Mateos and Trancanelli as a model for the pre-equilibrium stage of quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions. By applying the membrane paradigm which we also check by numerical evaluation of Kubo formula and lowest lying quasinormal modes, we find that the shear viscosity purely transverse to the direction of anisotropy saturates the holographic viscosity bound, whereas longitudinal shear viscosities are smaller, providing the first such example not involving higher-derivative theories of gravity and, more importantly, with fully known gauge-gravity correspondence.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity purely transverse to the direction of anisotropy saturates the holographic viscosity bound of ℏ/4π, while longitudinal shear viscosities are smaller, providing the first example of a violation of this bound that does not involve higher-derivative theories of gravity and has a fully known gauge-gravity correspondence.

## Method

The authors study a strongly coupled N=4 super-Yang-Mills plasma made anisotropic by a theta parameter that depends linearly on one spatial dimension, whose holographic dual is an anisotropic axion-dilaton-gravity background proposed by Mateos and Trancanelli. They apply the membrane paradigm to compute the shear viscosity tensor components, and verify their results by numerical evaluation of Kubo formulas and by calculating the lowest lying quasinormal modes of the relevant shear fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- The purely transverse shear viscosity η⊥ equals s/4π, saturating the holographic viscosity bound.
- The longitudinal shear viscosity η∥ = s/(4π ℋ(u_h)) < s/4π for a ≠ 0, where ℋ(u_h) > 1 at the anisotropic horizon.
- The ratio η∥/η⊥ is identical to σz/σ⊥, the ratio of DC conductivities along and transverse to the anisotropy direction.
- The diffusion constant Dxy = η⊥/(ε+P⊥) = 1/(4π T) for the purely transverse shear mode.
- The diffusion constant Dzx = η∥/(ε+P⊥) is given by an integral expression involving the metric functions ℬ, ℋ, and dilaton φ.

## Context

This work extends the holographic study of anisotropic plasmas initiated by Mateos and Trancanelli (2011), who provided the anisotropic axion-dilaton-gravity background used here. It contrasts with earlier results from p-wave superfluid models by Erdmenger et al. (2010) where nonuniversal shear viscosities were found above the bound, and with the universal saturation of the bound in isotropic Einstein gravity established by Buchel (2003) and Iqbal (2008). The results enable future studies of anisotropic hydrodynamic evolution with different shear viscosity components, and comparisons with weakly coupled anisotropic non-Abelian plasma calculations.
