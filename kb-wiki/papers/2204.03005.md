# Long-term 3D-MHD Simulations of Black Hole Accretion Disks formed in Neutron Star Mergers

> We examine the long-term evolution of accretion tori around black hole (BH) remnants of compact object mergers involving at least one neutron star, to better understand their contribution to kilonovae and the synthesis of r-process elements. To this end, we modify the unsplit magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) solver in FLASH4.5 to work in non-uniform three-dimensional spherical coordinates, enabling more efficient coverage of a large dynamic range in length scales while exploiting symmetries in the system. This modified code is used to perform BH accretion disk simulations that vary the initial magnetic field geometry and disk compactness, utilizing a physical equation of state, a neutrino leakage scheme for emission and absorption, and modeling the BH's gravity with a pseudo-Newtonian potential. Simulations run for long enough to achieve a radiatively-inefficient state in the disk. We find robust mass ejection with both poloidal and toroidal initial field geometries, and suppressed outflow at high disk compactness. With the included physics, we obtain bimodal velocity distributions that trace back to mass ejection by magnetic stresses at early times, and to thermal processes in the radiatively-inefficient state at late times. The electron fraction distribution of the disk outflow is broad in all models, and the ejecta geometry follows a characteristic hourglass shape. We test the effect of removing neutrino absorption or nuclear recombination with axisymmetric models, finding $\sim 50\%$ less mass ejection and more neutron-rich composition without neutrino absorption, and a subdominant contribution from nuclear recombination. Tests of the MHD and neutrino leakage implementations are included.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that long-term 3D-MHD simulations of black hole accretion disks formed in neutron star mergers produce robust mass ejection with both poloidal and toroidal initial magnetic field geometries, but outflow is suppressed at high disk compactness, and the electron fraction distribution of the outflow is broad in all models.

## Key numbers

- Mass ejection decreased by approximately 50% when neutrino absorption was removed in axisymmetric models, with more neutron-rich composition
- The average electron fraction of outflow increased by 5% when using the new diffusion time prescription compared to the old scheme
- Outflow quantity decreased by about 50% in the new leakage scheme compared to the old implementation
- The magnetized blast test used initial overpressure of approximately 500 erg cm^-3 and uniform magnetic field strength B0 = 10 G
- The torus test used a maximum density of 10 in code units, with orbital timescale of approximately 50 at initial circularization radius r_circ = 4.7 and minimum radius r = 3
- The 3D SANE torus used resolutions of Delta r_circ ~ 0.040, r_circ min(Delta theta) = 0.10, and Delta phi = 0.03 in code units

## Context

This work extends previous BH accretion disk simulations by Hawley (2000), Mignone (2007), and the authors' prior work (MSc) by implementing MHD in non-uniform spherical coordinates within FLASH4.5. The neutrino leakage scheme improves upon the implementations of FM13 and MF14 by adopting the diffusion time prescription from ILEAS2019, which brings results closer to Monte-Carlo transport methods like SedonuGR (Richers 2015). The simulations enable better understanding of kilonova contributions and r-process nucleosynthesis from post-merger accretion disks, with the bimodal velocity distributions tracing mass ejection by magnetic stresses at early times and thermal processes in the radiatively-inefficient state at late times.
