# Constraining the equation of state of neutron stars using multimessenger observations

> Neutron stars are the densest objects known in our visible universe. Properties of matter inside a neutron star are encoded in its equation of state, which has wide-ranging uncertainty from a theoretical perspective. With the current understanding of quantum chromodynamics, it is hard to determine the interactions of neutron star matter at such high densities. Also performing many body calculations is computationally intractable. Besides the constitution of the neutron star core is highly speculative -- it is not ruled out that it contains exotic matter like strange baryons, meson condensates, quark matter, etc. Although the matter inside the neutron star is extremely dense, but the temperature of this object is very cold in most of its life span. We cannot produce such dense but rather cold material in our laboratory. Since probing the physics of neutron star matter is inaccessible by our earth based experiments, we look for astrophysical observations of neutron stars. This thesis deals with the theoretical and computational techniques required to translate neutron star observables from astrophysical observations to its equation of state.

## Key claim
This thesis argues that multimessenger observations of neutron stars, combining gravitational-wave detections (e.g., GW170817), X-ray pulse-profile modeling (NICER), and radio pulsar timing, can jointly constrain the equation of state of dense nuclear matter, but the inferred EoS depends sensitively on assumptions about pressure anisotropy, crust elasticity, and the rotational state of the star.

## Method
The author develops a hybrid EoS parameterization that combines a parabolic expansion-based nuclear empirical model around saturation density with a generic 3-segment piecewise polytrope at higher densities. This parameterization is used in a Bayesian framework to simultaneously fit mass, radius, and tidal deformability measurements from multiple neutron star observations, including GW170817, GW190425, NICER data for PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, and radio mass measurements of massive pulsars. The tidal deformability of anisotropic stars is computed by solving the Tolman–Oppenheimer–Volkoff equations with the Bowers–Liang anisotropy model, and the effect of solid crusts is included via a fully general relativistic treatment with realistic shear moduli.

## Context
This work extends the Bayesian inference framework for neutron star EoS developed by Özel and collaborators (Özel 2015) and the tidal deformability formalism of Hinderer (2007), Damour (2009), and Binnington (2009). It directly builds on the multimessenger constraints from the LIGO/Virgo detection of GW170817 (Abbott 2017, 2018) and the NICER mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451 (Riley 2019, Miller 2019) and PSR J0740+6620 (Riley 2021, Miller 2021). The anisotropic pressure analysis generalizes the Bowers–Liang (1974) model, and the crustal shear study extends the work of Yagi and Yunes (2015) on universal relations. The results enable future third-generation gravitational-wave detectors to distinguish between isotropic and anisotropic EoS models and to test the nature of the mass-gap object GW190814.
