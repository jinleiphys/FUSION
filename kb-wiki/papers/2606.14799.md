# Experimental exploration of the QCD phase diagram

> The different phases of strongly interacting matter are governed by Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). At high temperature and/or density a deconfined, chirally symmetric phase of quarks and gluons is expected to govern the nature of strongly interacting matter, the so-called quark-gluon plasma. Here we review what is known about the existence of this exotic form of matter from an experimental point of view and confront the results with QCD predictions based on 'Lattice QCD', where QCD is discretized on a space-time lattice and solved numerically in a Monte Carlo approach. The form of the presentation is explicitly pedagogical but the results include even very recent developments. Our research is based on the interpretation of hadron production data from relativistic nucleus--nucleus collisions over a wide energy range, with the aim to make progress in the understanding of the QCD phase, and of phenomena like deconfinement and hadronization.

## Key claim

This pedagogical review argues that hadron production data from relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions, interpreted via the Statistical Hadronization Model (SHM), provide strong experimental evidence for the existence of a deconfined, chirally symmetric quark-gluon plasma (QGP) whose chemical freeze-out temperature saturates near the QCD pseudo-critical temperature, but the review hedges that the detailed production mechanism for loosely bound states like light nuclei remains an open question.

## Method

The authors compile and analyze hadron yield measurements from AGS, SPS, RHIC, and LHC experiments over a wide range of collision energies (from \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 4.8\) GeV to 2.76 TeV). They fit these data within the grand-canonical formulation of the Statistical Hadronization Model (hadron resonance gas) to extract the chemical freeze-out temperature \(T_{CF}\) and baryo-chemical potential \(\mu_B\) at each energy, using the complete hadron spectrum from the Particle Data Group and applying S-matrix treatments for pion-nucleon interactions where needed.

## Key numbers

* Initial energy density in central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV): \(\epsilon_{BJ} \approx 14.5\) GeV/fm\(^3\), more than a factor of 30 above the critical energy density of 0.4 GeV/fm\(^3\).
* Initial temperature estimated at LHC energy: \(T \approx 307\) MeV, assuming an ideal relativistic gas of quarks and gluons.
* Pseudo-critical temperature for the chiral crossover at vanishing \(\mu_B\) from Lattice QCD: \(156.5 \pm 1.5\) MeV (Bazavov 2018) and \(158.0 \pm 0.6\) MeV (Borsanyi 2020).
* Chemical freeze-out temperature from SHM fit to ALICE data for central Pb-Pb at LHC: \(T_{CF} = 156.6 \pm 1.7\) MeV, with \(\mu_B = 0.7 \pm 3.8\) MeV and volume \(V = 4175 \pm 380\) fm\(^3\).
* Improved precision on baryo-chemical potential at LHC: \(\mu_B = 0.7 \pm 0.45\) MeV (ALICE 2023).
* Limiting temperature from parametrization of energy dependence: \(T_{CF}^{lim} = 158.4 \pm 1.4\) MeV.
* Freeze-out temperature from light nuclei yields: \(T_{nuclei} = 159 \pm 5\) MeV.
* Energy density at AGS (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.8\) GeV): \(\epsilon_{BJ} \approx 1.9\) GeV/fm\(^3\), with estimated temperature \(T \approx 180\) MeV.
* Energy density at SPS (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17.2\) GeV): \(\epsilon_{BJ} \approx 3.5\) GeV/fm\(^3\), with estimated temperature \(T \approx 212\) MeV.
* Energy density at RHIC (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV): \(\epsilon_{BJ} \approx 5.5\) GeV/fm\(^3\), with estimated temperature \(T \approx 239\) MeV.
* Fractional energy loss of incident nucleons: approximately 86% from a rapidity shift of about two units.

## Context

This review extends the long-standing program of using the Statistical Hadronization Model to connect heavy-ion collision data to QCD phase boundary predictions from Lattice QCD, building on earlier work by Hagedorn (1965), Braun-Munzinger (1998, 2003), and Andronic (2008, 2017). It confronts experimental results from AGS, SPS, RHIC, and LHC with Lattice QCD calculations by Bazavov (2018) and Borsanyi (2020) on the pseudo-critical temperature, and discusses the open question of how loosely bound objects like deuterons (binding energy 2.2 MeV) are produced at temperatures near 157 MeV. The work enables future searches for a critical end point in the QCD phase diagram at \(\mu_B\) around 600 MeV, as discussed in recent reviews by Fischer (2026) and Braun-Munzinger (2026).
