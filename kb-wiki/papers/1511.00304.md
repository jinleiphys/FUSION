# The heat capacity of the neutron star inner crust within an extended NSE model

> Superfluidity in the crust is a key ingredient for the cooling properties of proto-neutron stars. Present theoretical calculations employ the quasi-particle mean-field Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory with temperature dependent occupation numbers for the quasi-particle states. Finite temperature stellar matter is characterized by a whole distribution of different nuclear species. We want to assess the importance of this distribution on the calculation of heat capacity in the inner crust. Following a recent work, the Wigner-Seitz cell is mapped into a model with cluster degrees of freedom. The finite temperature distribution is then given by a statistical collection of Wigner-Seitz cells. We additionally introduce pairing correlations in the local density BCS approximation both in the homogeneous unbound neutron component, and in the interface region between clusters and neutrons. The heat capacity is calculated in the different baryonic density conditions corresponding to the inner crust, and in a temperature range varying from 100 KeV to 2 MeV. We show that accounting for the cluster distribution has a small effect at intermediate densities, but it considerably affects the heat capacity both close to the outer crust and close to the core. We additionally show that it is very important to consider the temperature evolution of the proton fraction for a quantitatively reliable estimation of the heat capacity.

## Key claim

Accounting for the full statistical distribution of nuclear species (rather than a single representative nucleus) has a small effect on the heat capacity of the neutron star inner crust at intermediate densities, but considerably affects it both close to the outer crust and close to the core; additionally, the temperature evolution of the proton fraction must be included for a quantitatively reliable estimation of the heat capacity.

## Method

The authors map each Wigner-Seitz cell into a model with cluster degrees of freedom and treat the finite-temperature distribution as a statistical collection of such cells, using an extended Nuclear Statistical Equilibrium (NSE) model. Pairing correlations are introduced in the local density BCS approximation both in the homogeneous unbound neutron component and in the interface region between clusters and neutrons. The heat capacity is then calculated across the baryonic density conditions of the inner crust for temperatures from 100 keV to 2 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: 100 keV to 2 MeV.
- The pairing gap for pure neutron matter at zero temperature is taken from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations (Cao 2006), with the BCS gap equation solved at finite temperature using an energy cutoff of 16 MeV for the reduced chemical potential.
- The Coulomb parameter used in the cluster model is \(a_C = 0.69\) MeV.
- The pairing contribution to the cluster energy is evaluated as \(E_{pair} = \pm 12/\sqrt{A}\) MeV, where the sign depends on whether the nucleus is even-even or odd-odd.

## Context

This work extends the NSE model of Gulminelli et al. (2015) by adding pairing correlations, which were absent in that earlier semi-classical treatment. It contrasts with standard Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations (e.g., Fortin et al. 2010, Pastore et al. 2015) that assume a single representative nucleus per Wigner-Seitz cell and often neglect the temperature dependence of the proton fraction. The results enable future calculations of neutron star cooling curves that include both the cluster distribution and superfluid properties.
