# Tetramer Bound States in Heteronuclear Systems

> We calculate the universal spectrum of trimer and tetramer states in heteronuclear mixtures of ultracold atoms with different masses in the vicinity of the heavy-light dimer threshold. To extract the energies, we solve the three- and four-body problem for simple two- and three-body potentials tuned to the universal region using the Gaussian expansion method. We focus on the case of one light particle of mass $m$ and two or three heavy bosons of mass $M$ with resonant heavy-light interactions. We find that trimer and tetramer cross into the heavy-light dimer threshold at almost the same point and that as the mass ratio $M/m$ decreases, the distance between the thresholds for trimer and tetramer states becomes smaller. We also comment on the possibility of observing exotic three-body states consisting of a dimer and two atoms in this region and compare with previous work.

## Key claim

The distance between the thresholds where the trimer and tetramer states disappear into the heavy-light dimer threshold becomes smaller as the mass ratio \(M/m\) decreases, and for \(M/m = 7/6\) the two states almost disappear at the same point, a qualitatively new behavior not seen in identical boson systems.

## Method

The authors solved the three- and four-body Schrödinger equations for systems of one light particle (mass \(m\)) and two or three heavy bosons (mass \(M\)) using the Gaussian expansion method (GEM). They employed simple Gaussian two-body and three-body potentials tuned to the universal regime, with the heavy-light interaction resonant and the heavy-heavy interaction switched off. Energies were extracted by varying the two-body potential strength to scan the scattering length, while keeping the three-body repulsive force fixed at \(w_0/E_s \approx 9.6\) to ensure results were within the universal window.

## Key numbers

* For the mass ratio \(M/m = 133/6\) (Cs-Li), the ratio of the tetramer ground state energy to the trimer ground state energy \(\sqrt{E_4^{0,0}/E_3^0}\) is 1.51121 at \(w_0/E_s = 9.60\).
* For the mass ratio \(M/m = 133/6\), the ratio of the excited tetramer energy to the trimer ground state energy \(\sqrt{E_4^{0,1}/E_3^0}\) is 1.0110 at \(w_0/E_s = 9.60\).
* For the mass ratio \(M/m = 133/6\), the ratio of the lowest two trimer energies \(\sqrt{E_3^0/E_3^1}\) is 4.9023 at \(w_0/E_s = 9.60\).
* For the mass ratio \(M/m = 7/6\) (Li-Li), no excited tetramer state was found at any scattering length, including on the negative side.
* The difference in \(r_0/a\) between the trimer and tetramer threshold crossings decreases as \(M/m\) decreases, with the two thresholds almost coinciding for \(M/m = 7/6\).

## Context

This work extends the four-body calculations of Blume and Yan (2014) for heteronuclear systems to positive scattering lengths further from the unitary limit and systematically studies the mass ratio dependence. It confirms the finding of Wang et al. (2012) that for large mass ratios two tetramer states exist, but shows that for smaller mass ratios the excited tetramer disappears, consistent with the recent Na-Rb study by Wang et al. (2016) at a mass ratio of about 3.8. The results enable future experimental searches for tetramer states in heteronuclear ultracold mixtures, particularly in systems with mass ratios near unity where the trimer and tetramer thresholds nearly coincide.
