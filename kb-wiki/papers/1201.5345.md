# Chiral transition temperature and aspects of deconfinement in 2+1 flavor QCD with the HISQ/tree action

> We present results on the chiral transition temperature Tc in 2+1 flavor QCD extrapolated to the continuum limit and the physical light quark mass. The extrapolations are based on the data from simulations on lattices with temporal extent Ntau=6, 8 and 12 with the HISQ/tree and Ntau=8 and 12 with the asqtad action. The chiral transition is analyzed in terms of universal O(N) scaling functions. After performing simultaneous asqtad and HISQ/tree continuum extrapolation the chiral transition temperature is Tc=154 +/- 9 MeV. We also discuss the deconfinement aspects of the transition in terms of the renormalized Polyakov loop, fluctuations and correlations of several conserved charges and the trace anomaly.

# Chiral transition temperature and aspects of deconfinement in 2+1 flavor QCD with the HISQ/tree action

## Key claim
The chiral transition temperature in 2+1 flavor QCD at physical light quark mass is determined to be 154 ± 9 MeV after a simultaneous continuum extrapolation of asqtad and HISQ/tree action data, but this value depends on the assumption that O(N) universal scaling functions describe the chiral transition, and the analysis combines a statistical fit error of ±8 MeV with an additional ±1 MeV systematic error from lattice scale determination.

## Method
The chiral condensate and chiral susceptibility were computed on lattices with temporal extent Nτ = 6, 8, and 12 using the HISQ/tree action and Nτ = 8 and 12 using the asqtad action, covering temperatures from 130 to 440 MeV. Simultaneous fits to both observables were performed using O(N) scaling functions with a regular part including linear dependence in the light-to-strange quark mass ratio H = ml/ms and quadratic dependence in temperature, yielding pseudo-critical temperatures that were then extrapolated to the continuum limit via a combined 1/Nτ² fit constrained to a common intercept. Deconfinement aspects were studied through the renormalized Polyakov loop, fluctuations and correlations of conserved charges (light and strange quark number susceptibilities, electric charge-strangeness correlations), and the trace anomaly.

## Key numbers
- Chiral transition temperature: Tc = 154 ± 9 MeV (combined error from fit and lattice scale determination)
- Temperature range covered: T = 130–440 MeV for HISQ/tree (Nτ = 6, 8, 12) and T = 148–304 MeV for asqtad (Nτ = 8, 12)
- Light quark mass values: ml = ms/20 for asqtad and ml = ms/40 for HISQ/tree (chiral condensate analysis); ml = ms/20 for all other quantities with HISQ/tree
- Root-mean-squared pion mass on finest Nτ = 12 HISQ/tree lattice: approximately 200 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous HotQCD collaboration studies using p4 and asqtad actions (RBC-Bielefeld 2009, 2010) by applying O(N) scaling analysis to the HISQ/tree action with finer lattices, and provides a continuum-extrapolated chiral transition temperature that supersedes earlier estimates. The results enable comparisons with the Hadron Resonance Gas model (HRG) for fluctuations and correlations, and the trace anomaly parametrization (s95p-v1) combining HRG at low T with lattice data from HotQCD (2010) at high T; the Budapest-Wuppertal collaboration (2008, 2010) demonstrated that using the kaon decay constant fK to set the scale reduces cutoff effects in hadronic observables, a technique adopted here.
