# Solar Models and Astrophysical S-factors Constrained by Helioseismic Results and Updated Neutrino Fluxes

> The ratio of metal abundance to hydrogen abundance of the solar photosphere, $(Z/X)_{s}$, has been revised several times. Standard solar models, based on these revised solar abundances, are in disagreement with seismically inferred results. Recently, Magg et al. introduced a new value for $(Z/X)_{s}$, which is still in debate in the community. The solar abundance problem or solar modeling problem remains a topic of ongoing debate. We constructed rotating solar models in accordance with various abundance scales where the effects of convection overshoot and enhanced diffusion were included. Among these models, those utilizing Magg's abundance scale exhibit superior sound-speed and density profiles compared to models using other abundance scales. Additionally, they reproduce the observed frequency separation ratios $r_{02}$ and $r_{13}$. These models also match the seismically inferred surface helium abundance and convection zone depth within $1σ$ level. Furthermore, the calculated neutrino fluxes from these models agree with detected ones at the level of $1σ$. We found that neutrino fluxes and density profile are influenced by nuclear reactions, allowing us to use the combination of detected neutrino fluxes and seismically inferred density for diagnosing astrophysical $S$-factors. This diagnostic approach shows that $S_{11}$ may be underestimated by $2\%$, while $S_{33}$ may be overestimated by about $3\%$ in previous determinations. The $S$-factors favored by updated neutrino fluxes and helioseismic results can lead to significant improvements in solar models.

# Solar Models and Astrophysical S-factors Constrained by Helioseismic Results and Updated Neutrino Fluxes

## Key claim

Rotating solar models constructed with Magg et al.'s (2022) abundance scale can simultaneously match helioseismic constraints and detected neutrino fluxes at the 1σ level, but only if the astrophysical S-factors S₁₁ and S₃₃ are adjusted from previously accepted values, specifically, S₁₁ may be underestimated by 2% and S₃₃ may be overestimated by about 3% in prior determinations.

## Method

The authors constructed rotating and nonrotating solar models using the Yale Rotating Stellar Evolution Code with OPAL equation-of-state tables and OP opacity tables reconstructed with Magg et al. (2022) mixtures, incorporating convection overshoot, enhanced diffusion, and rotational mixing. They calibrated models to the present solar luminosity, radius, mass, and age, then compared predicted sound speed profiles, density profiles, frequency separation ratios (r₀₂ and r₁₃), surface helium abundance, convection zone depth, and neutrino fluxes against helioseismic inferences and detected neutrino fluxes from Borexino and other experiments. By systematically varying the nuclear cross-section factors S₁₁, S₃₃, S₃₄, and S₁₇, they diagnosed which S-factor values best reconcile the combined helioseismic and neutrino constraints.

## Key numbers

- Surface helium abundance of rotating model B92R: 0.2484, consistent with seismically inferred 0.2485 ± 0.0035 (Basu 2004)
- Convection zone base radius of B92R: 0.711 R☉, consistent with seismically inferred 0.713 ± 0.003 R☉ (Christensen-Dalsgaard 1991)
- Relative sound speed difference between Sun and B92R: less than 0.0031; density difference: less than 0.013
- χ²_{cₛ+ρ} for B92R: 289; for B89R: 215; for A11R: comparable to B89R
- S₁₁ may be underestimated by 2% relative to previous determinations
- S₃₃ may be overestimated by about 3% relative to previous determinations
- S₁₁ value favored by constraints: approximately 4.13 × 10⁻²² keV barns (an increase of about 3% from Adelberger et al. 2011 value of 4.01 × 10⁻²² keV barns)
- S₁₇ value favored by constraints: approximately 22.4 × 10⁻³ keV barns (an increase of about 8% from Adelberger et al. 2011)
- ⁷Be neutrino flux from B92R: 4.82 × 10⁹ cm⁻² s⁻¹, compared to detected (4.99 ± 0.11) × 10⁹ cm⁻² s⁻¹ (Borexino)
- Total CNO neutrino fluxes from B92R: 8.8 × 10⁸ cm⁻² s⁻¹; from A11R: 6.4 × 10⁸ cm⁻² s⁻¹; detected: 6.6⁺²·⁰₋₀·₉ × 10⁸ cm⁻² s⁻¹ (Borexino 2022)
- ⁸B neutrino flux from A11R: 5.02 × 10⁶ cm⁻² s⁻¹; from B89R: 5.65 × 10⁶ cm⁻² s⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate over the solar abundance problem, where standard solar models using revised low metal abundances (e.g., Asplund 2005, 2009; Caffau 2011) disagree with helioseismic results. The authors build on the recent abundance scale of Magg et al. (2022), which gives (Z/X)ₛ = 0.0225, and show that rotating models with enhanced diffusion can resolve the discrepancies that persist in standard models. The diagnostic approach combining helioseismic density profiles with detected neutrino fluxes from Borexino (2018, 2022) and Bergstrom (2016) enables new constraints on nuclear S-factors, particularly S₁₁ and S₃₃, which have been uncertain across determinations by Adelberger et al. (2011), Bahcall et al. (1988, 1992), and recent chiral effective field theory calculations by Acharya et al. (2016, 2023) and DeLeone et al. (2023). The results suggest that future experiments like the Jiangmen Underground Neutrino Observatory could further constrain CNO cycle S-factors.
