# Rigorous limits for hadronic and semi-leptonic CP-violating coupling constants from the electric dipole moment of $^{199}$Hg

> Relativistic many-body methods at different levels of approximations are employed to gain insights into the passage of the electron correlations from lower to higher levels in the accurate determination of the electric dipole polarizability ($α_d$) and electric dipole moment (EDM) due to the electron-nucleus tensor-pseudotensor (T-PT) and nuclear Schiff moment (NSM) interactions of the $^{199}$Hg ground state. Moreover, plausible reasons for the differences in the previous calculations are pointed out. Comparison between the calculated and experimental results of $α_d$ indicates that our EDM calculations are about 3\% accurate which in combination with the measured value of $^{199}$Hg EDM yield limits for the T-PT coupling constant $C_T < 2.31 \times 10^{-9}$ and the NSM $S<1.55 \times 10^{-12}|e|fm^3$. From this limit of $S$ and the latest nuclear structure calculation, we get improved limits for the strong CP violating parameter $θ_{QCD} < 2.12 \times 10^{-10}$ and the combined up and down quark chromo-EDMs $(-0.004 \tilde{d}_u + 0.020 \tilde{d}_d) < 1.15 \times 10^{-27} |e| cm$.

## Key claim
The paper claims improved limits on CP-violating coupling constants derived from the electric dipole moment (EDM) of $^{199}$Hg, but with the explicit hedge that the atomic EDM calculations are about 3% accurate based on comparison between calculated and experimental electric dipole polarizability ($\alpha_d$). The final limits depend on combining these atomic calculations with nuclear structure calculations that carry their own uncertainties.

## Method
The authors employed relativistic many-body methods at multiple levels of approximation, Dirac-Fock (DF), second- and third-order many-body perturbation theory (MBPT(2), MBPT(3)), random phase approximation (RPA), and coupled-cluster theory with single and double excitations (CCSD), to calculate the electric dipole polarizability and EDM contributions from the tensor-pseudotensor (T-PT) electron-nucleus interaction and the nuclear Schiff moment (NSM) interaction in the $^{199}$Hg ground state. They compared their results with previous calculations and with the experimental $\alpha_d$ value to validate accuracy, then combined their final CCSD EDM results with the measured $^{199}$Hg EDM upper limit to extract bounds on CP-violating parameters.

## Key numbers
- Electric dipole polarizability $\alpha_d$ from CCSD method: 34.98 $e a_0^3$, compared to experimental value 33.91(34) $e a_0^3$ (Goebel, year not specified)
- T-PT coupling constant limit: $C_T < 2.31 \times 10^{-9}$
- Nuclear Schiff moment limit: $S < 1.55 \times 10^{-12} |e| fm^3$
- Strong CP-violating parameter limit: $|\bar{\theta}| < 1.1 \times 10^{-9}$
- Combined up and down quark chromo-EDM limit: $|\tilde{d}_u - \tilde{d}_d| < 2.9 \times 10^{-26} |e| cm$
- Neutron EDM limit from this work: $d_n < 8.18 \times 10^{-26} |e| cm$
- Proton EDM limit from this work: $d_p < 7.75 \times 10^{-25} |e| cm$

## Context
This work extends previous atomic EDM calculations for $^{199}$Hg that showed large discrepancies, particularly between the PRCC method of Latha et al. (year not specified) and the CI+MBPT approach of Dzuba et al. (2009). The authors identify a phase factor problem in the PRCC calculations that explains some of these differences. Their improved atomic limits, combined with the nuclear structure calculations of Engel et al. (year not specified) and Dekens et al. (year not specified), yield more stringent bounds on the strong CP-violating parameter $\bar{\theta}$ and quark chromo-EDMs than previously known, though further reduction of nuclear calculation uncertainties could enable even tighter constraints.
