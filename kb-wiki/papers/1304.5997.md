# Can bimodality exist without phase transition?

> Here we present an explicit counterexample to the widely spread beliefs about an exclusive role of bimodality as the first order phase transition signal. On the basis of an exactly solvable statistical model generalizing the statistical multifragmentation model of nuclei we demonstrate that the bimodal nuclear fragment size distributions can naturally appear in infinite system without a phase transition. It appears at the supercritical temperatures due to the negative values of the surface tension coefficient. The developed statistical model corresponds to the compressible nuclear liquid with the tricritical endpoint located at one third of the normal nuclear density.

# Can bimodality exist without phase transition?

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that bimodal nuclear fragment size distributions can appear in an infinite system without a first-order phase transition, specifically at supercritical temperatures where the surface tension coefficient becomes negative. This directly contradicts the widely held belief that bimodality is an unambiguous signal of a first-order phase transition in finite but large systems.

## Method
The authors construct an exactly solvable constrained statistical multifragmentation model (CSMM) in the thermodynamic limit, generalizing the standard statistical multifragmentation model (SMM). They introduce a new parameterization of the liquid phase pressure to allow for a compressible nuclear liquid, and a temperature-dependent surface tension coefficient that becomes negative above a critical temperature T_cep = 18 MeV. The model is solved analytically in the grand canonical ensemble, and fragment size distributions are computed for the gaseous phase and the supercritical temperature region where no phase transition exists by construction.

## Key numbers
- Normal nuclear density: ρ₀ ≈ 0.17 fm⁻³ at T = 0 and zero pressure
- Nucleon mass: m ≈ 940 MeV
- Nucleon degeneracy factor: z₁ = 4
- Fisher topological exponent: τ ≈ 1.825
- Surface tension parameters: σ₀ = 18 MeV, T_cep = 18 MeV, ζ ≥ 1
- Binding energy per nucleon: W₀ = 16 MeV
- Liquid pressure parameterization constants: a₂ ≈ 1.233 × 10⁻² MeV⁻¹, a₄ ≈ 4.099 × 10⁻⁷ MeV⁻³
- Tricritical endpoint located at one third of the normal nuclear density
- Isotherms shown for T = 11, 16, 17, 18 MeV from bottom to top in the phase diagram

## Context
This work provides an explicit counterexample to the bimodality-phase transition connection argued by Hill, Chomaz (2003), and Gulminelli (2007), who identified each local maximum of a bimodal distribution with a pure phase. The model extends the statistical multifragmentation model of Bugaev (2000) by introducing a compressible nuclear liquid and a realistic surface tension parameterization based on the exact analytical solution of the partition function of surface deformations. The result enables a re-evaluation of bimodality as a phase transition signal in nuclear multifragmentation experiments and suggests that negative surface tension at supercritical temperatures can produce bimodal distributions without phase coexistence.
