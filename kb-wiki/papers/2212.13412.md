# On nuclear short-range correlations and the zero-energy eigenstates of the Schrodinger equation

> We present a systematic analysis of the nuclear 2 and 3-body short range correlations, and their relations to the zero-energy eigenstates of the Schrodinger equation. To this end we analyze the doublet and triplet Coupled-Cluster amplitudes in the high momentum limit, and show that they obey universal equations independent of the number of nucleons and their state. Furthermore, we find that these Coupled-Cluster amplitudes coincide with the zero-energy Bloch-Horowitz operator. These results illuminate the relations between the nuclear many-body theory and the generalized contact formalism, introduced to describe the nuclear 2-body short range correlations, and it might also be helpful for general Coupled-Cluster computations as the asymptotic part of the amplitudes is given and shown to be universal.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the asymptotic high-momentum behavior of two-body and three-body Coupled-Cluster amplitudes is governed by universal equations that coincide with the zero-energy Bloch-Horowitz operator, thereby placing the Generalized Contact Formalism on more rigorous theoretical footing, but this universality is limited in the sense that it depends on the specific nucleon-nucleon interaction potential used.

## Method

The authors analyze the Coupled-Cluster doublet (2-body) and triplet (3-body) amplitudes in the high-momentum limit, using a momentum basis with periodic boundary conditions for closed-shell systems. They systematically truncate the Coupled-Cluster equations by neglecting higher-order cluster operators and subleading commutator terms, then derive asymptotic equations for the two-body and three-body amplitudes. These asymptotic equations are solved formally and shown to be equivalent to the zero-energy Bloch-Horowitz operator for the two-body case, with an analogous relation derived for the three-body case.

## Key numbers

- The asymptotic form \( t^{ab}_{ij} \rightarrow -\frac{1}{E^{ab}_{ij}}V^{ab}_{ij} \) is valid only at extremely high momentum, larger than 10 fm\(^{-1}\), making it impractical for actual calculations (citing Zabolitzky, 1978).
- The Fermi momentum \( p_F \) defines the boundary between hole states (momentum less than \( p_F \)) and particle states (momentum greater than \( p_F \)) for closed-shell systems.
- The excitation energy \( E^{ab}_{ij} \) for two-body amplitudes is given by \( E^{ab}_{ij} = (\epsilon_a + \epsilon_b) - (\epsilon_i + \epsilon_j) \).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical foundations of the Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF) introduced by Weiss et al. (2014, 2015) for describing nuclear short-range correlations, which was previously verified numerically using ab-initio calculations. The paper connects Coupled-Cluster theory to the Bloch-Horowitz formalism (Bloch and Horowitz, 1958) and builds on earlier work by Amado (1976) and Zabolitzky (1978) on asymptotic momentum distributions. The results enable systematic inclusion of three-body short-range correlations beyond the leading two-body term in the asymptotic expansion of the nuclear many-body wave-function, and may improve practical Coupled-Cluster computations by providing universal asymptotic forms for the amplitudes.
