# Composite octet baryons in a relativistic mean field description of nuclear and neutron star matter

> We examine the properties of composite octet baryons in the nuclear medium and neutron star matter. The internal quark-diquark structure of the octet baryons and the equations of state of nuclear matter and neutron star matter in the mean field approximation are described by using the three-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model as an effective quark theory of QCD. After introducing our model, we first discuss the properties of single baryons and their effective meson exchange interactions in symmetric nuclear matter by using concepts of Fermi liquid theory. Several model independent implications of this description are derived, and illustrated by numerical results obtained in our model. Second, we extend the model description to high baryon densities, and investigate the equation of state of neutron star matter and the resulting star masses. We find that the so called hyperon puzzle persists also for the case of composite hadrons. To get more information on this point, we also investigate the role of 6-fermi and 8-fermi interactions, in addition to the standard 4-fermi interactions. The strengths of those higher order fermi interactions is determined so as not to spoil the saturation properties of nuclear matter. Among them, an interaction characterized by a product of four quark current operators plays a special role to stabilize the stars over a large region of central baryon densities, although it has little effect on the maximum star masses.

## Key claim

The hyperon puzzle, the difficulty of reproducing neutron stars of about two solar masses when hyperons are included, persists even when baryons are treated as composite quark-diquark objects rather than point-like particles, and an 8-fermi interaction characterized by a product of four quark current operators stabilizes stars over a large range of central densities but has little effect on the maximum star masses.

## Method

The authors use a three-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model as an effective quark theory of QCD, describing octet baryons as quark-diquark bound states and applying the mean field approximation to obtain the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter and neutron star matter. They extend the model to high baryon densities, include 6-fermi and 8-fermi interactions in addition to the standard 4-fermi interactions, and determine the strengths of these higher-order interactions so as not to spoil the saturation properties of nuclear matter. The effective baryon-baryon interactions are expressed using concepts of Fermi liquid theory, and neutron star masses are computed under the constraints of chemical equilibrium and charge neutrality.

## Key numbers

* The vacuum effective quark masses are determined from the NJL model; the strange quark mass remains constant in symmetric nuclear matter under 4-fermi interactions only.
* The 8-fermi interaction characterized by a product of four quark current operators stabilizes neutron stars over a large region of central baryon densities, but has little effect on the maximum star masses.
* The hyperon puzzle persists for composite hadrons, meaning the maximum neutron star masses remain below about 2 solar masses when hyperons are included, consistent with the known difficulty.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous NJL-based study of octet baryons in free space (Carrillo-Serrano, 2016) to the nuclear medium and neutron star matter, and generalizes their earlier flavor SU(2) work (Tanimoto, 2019) to include strangeness. It connects the quark-diquark description of baryons to the Fermi liquid theory of Landau and Migdal, and addresses the hyperon puzzle discussed in reviews by Chatterjee (2015) and Burgio (2021). The results enable future studies of how chiral symmetry restrictions on the interaction Lagrangian affect the equation of state, and motivate further investigation of higher-order Fermi interactions as a mechanism to stabilize neutron star matter.
