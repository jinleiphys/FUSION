# Role of phi decays for K- yields in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The production of strange mesons in collisions of Ar+KCl at a kinetic beam energy of 1.756 AGeV is studied within a transport model of Boltzmann-Ühling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) type. In particular, $ϕ, K^+$ and $K^-$ yields and spectra are compared to the data mesured recently by the HADES collaboration and the $ϕ$ yield measured previously by the FOPI collaboration. Our results are in agreement with these data thus presenting an interpretation of the subleading role of $ϕ$ decays into $K^-$'s and confirming the importance of the strangeness-exchange channels for $K^-$ production.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while the transport model reproduces the experimentally measured φ/K⁻ ratio within error bars, the φ decay channel contributes only about 14-18% of the final K⁻ yield, confirming that strangeness-exchange reactions (πY ↔ NK⁻ and BY ↔ NNK⁻) remain the dominant source of K⁻ production in these collisions.

## Method

The authors employed a Boltzmann-Ühling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport code to simulate Ar+KCl collisions at 1.756 AGeV kinetic beam energy, including 24 baryon resonances and mesons (π, η, ω, ρ, σ, φ, K⁺, K⁻) as well as hyperons (Λ, Σ). They compared calculated transverse-mass spectra, rapidity distributions, and multiplicities of K⁺, K⁻, and φ mesons against HADES and FOPI experimental data, and analyzed the time evolution, freeze-out conditions, centrality dependence, and contributions of individual production channels.

## Key numbers

- K⁺ multiplicity from simulation: 2.7 × 10⁻²; experimental value: (2.8 ± 0.4) × 10⁻² (HADES)
- K⁻ multiplicity from simulation: 7.8 × 10⁻⁴; experimental value: (7.1 ± 1.9) × 10⁻⁴ (HADES)
- φ multiplicity from simulation: 2.2 × 10⁻⁴; experimental value: (2.6 ± 0.8) × 10⁻⁴ (HADES)
- φ/K⁻ multiplicity ratio from simulation: 0.28; experimental value: 0.37 ± 0.13 (HADES)
- Contribution of φ → K⁺K⁻ decays to K⁻ yield: 14% (from simulation ratio) to 18% (from experimental ratio)
- Strangeness-exchange channel πY ↔ NK⁻ contributes 42% of final K⁻ yield; BY ↔ NNK⁻ contributes 32%
- φ production peaks at local baryon density of 1.75 n₀ (where n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³), with tails from 0.3 n₀ to 2.3 n₀
- Maximum central density reaches 2.4 n₀ at 7.5 fm/c
- Switching off in-medium potentials reduces K⁻ yield to about 60% of the default case
- Hard equation of state (κ = 380 MeV) reduces K⁻ yield by 11% and φ yield by 15% compared to soft EoS (κ = 215 MeV)

## Context

This work extends earlier transport model analyses by Hartnack (2001, 2003, 2005, 2007) that identified strangeness-exchange reactions as the dominant K⁻ production mechanism, but which did not include the φ decay channel. The paper directly addresses the question posed by Kampfer (2001) about the importance of φ feeding for K⁻ yields, using new HADES data (Agakishiev, 2009) and improved NN → NNφ cross sections from Kaptari (2004, 2008). The results enable future comparisons with multi-strange hadron dynamics (such as Ξ⁻) and provide a baseline for interpreting forthcoming HADES data from heavier collision systems.
