# Multiplicity Fluctuations in Au+Au Collisions at RHIC

> The preliminary data of the PHENIX collaboration for the scaled variances of charged hadron multiplicity fluctuations in Au+Au at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV are analyzed within the model of independent sources. We use the HSD transport model to calculate the participant number fluctuations and the number of charged hadrons per nucleon participant in different centrality bins. This combined picture leads to a good agreement with the PHENIX data and suggests that the measured multiplicity fluctuations result dominantly from participant number fluctuations. The role of centrality selection and acceptance is discussed separately.

## Key claim
The measured charged hadron multiplicity fluctuations in Au+Au collisions at RHIC are dominantly caused by fluctuations in the number of participant nucleons, not by intrinsic dynamical fluctuations from the hadron production process itself. This conclusion is conditional on the model of independent sources and the specific centrality selection via Beam-Beam Counters used by PHENIX.

## Method
The authors used the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) transport model to simulate Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV and define 5% centrality classes based on the Beam-Beam Counter (BBC) distribution, matching the PHENIX experimental selection. They calculated the scaled variance of participant number fluctuations (ω_P), the mean number of charged hadrons per participant (n_i), and the fraction of accepted particles (q_i) for each centrality bin. These quantities were inserted into the model of independent sources formula ω_i^acc = 1 - q_i + q_i ω_i^* + q_i n_i ω_P, using HSD nucleon-nucleon values for the single-source fluctuations ω_i^*, to predict the scaled variance of charged hadron fluctuations in the PHENIX acceptance.

## Key numbers
- The scaled variance of participant number fluctuations ω_P decreases with increasing mean participant number ⟨N_P⟩ across 5% centrality bins, while n_i (charged hadrons per participant) increases.
- The PHENIX detector accepts only 3 to 3.5% of charged particles, with pseudorapidity |η| < 0.26, azimuthal angle φ < 245°, and p_T range 0.2 to 2.0 GeV/c.
- The HSD nucleon-nucleon single-source scaled variances at √s = 200 GeV are ω_-^* = 3.0, ω_+^* = 2.7, and ω_ch^* = 5.7.
- The model predictions from Eq. (omega-acc) show good agreement with the PHENIX preliminary data for the scaled variance of charged particle fluctuations as a function of ⟨N_P⟩.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of multiplicity fluctuations from SPS fixed-target experiments (where projectile participant number could be fixed) to RHIC collider conditions, where centrality is defined by BBC and ZDC detectors. It builds on prior transport model studies by Konchakovski et al. (2006) and Gorenstein et al. (2006) that examined participant number fluctuations and their influence on hadron fluctuations. The results enable future searches for QCD critical point signals by showing that to isolate dynamical fluctuations, one must use most central collisions with ≤1% centrality selection and enlarge the mid-rapidity acceptance to about 10% to distinguish between statistical model predictions (ω_± < 1) and HSD transport model predictions (ω_± much larger than unity).
