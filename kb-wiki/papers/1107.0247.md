# Ekpyrosis and inflationary dynamics in heavy ion collisions: the role of quantum fluctuations

> We summarize recent significant progress in the development of a first-principles formalism to describe the formation and evolution of matter in very high energy heavy ion collisions. The key role of quantum fluctuations both before and after a collision is emphasized. Systematic computations are now feasible to address early time dynamics essential to quantifying properties of strongly interacting quark-gluon matter.

# Ekpyrosis and inflationary dynamics in heavy ion collisions: the role of quantum fluctuations

## Key claim
Quantum fluctuations play a dual essential role in heavy ion collisions: a dominant class drives the energy evolution of the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) nuclear states before the collision, while another class grows exponentially with the square root of proper time after the collision and likely isotropizes matter in the Glasma. The authors present a first-principles formalism that resums both leading logarithms in x and leading instabilities of order (α_S exp(2√(μ τ)))^n, but emphasize that the formalism is currently demonstrated for inclusive quantities and that explicit numerical implementation for QCD is outlined rather than fully executed.

## Method
The authors develop a factorization scheme that separates quantum fluctuations from the two incoming nuclei before the collision (justified by causality at leading-log x accuracy) and resums them via the JIMWLK renormalization group equation. After the collision, they treat the Glasma background field plus Gaussian-distributed quantum fluctuations whose variance is given by the small fluctuation propagator in the Glasma background as τ → 0^+, then solve the classical Yang-Mills equations repeatedly with these initial conditions. The resulting expression for inclusive quantities involves a path integral over the classical fields, the JIMWLK-evolved weight functionals for the nuclear sources, and a Gaussian ensemble of quantum fluctuations added to the classical initial conditions.

## Key numbers
- The color sources are strong, with ρ_{1,2}^a ∼ 1/g, where g ≪ 1 is the QCD coupling.
- Before the collision, nuclear ground states have occupation numbers O(1/g^2).
- Quantum fluctuations at next-to-leading order have relative strength g compared to the classical background.
- The leading instabilities grow as (α_S exp(2√(μ τ)))^n, with μ ∼ Q_S, where n denotes the order in perturbation theory beyond the classical leading order contribution.
- These instabilities become comparable to the background field at τ ∼ 1/Q_S.

## Context
This work extends the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory (Gelis 2010) and the JIMWLK renormalization group approach (Jalilian-Marian 1997, Iancu 2000) to include post-collision quantum fluctuations. The early-time dynamics bears close analogy to pre-heating dynamics in inflationary cosmology (Polarski 1995, Kofman 1997, Micha 2004), where quantum fluctuations enhanced by parametric resonance with the background inflaton field drive turbulent thermalization. The formalism enables systematic computation of early-time dynamics essential for quantifying properties of strongly interacting quark-gluon matter, including parton energy loss and sphaleron transitions, and connects to experimental measurements of spatial anisotropies in heavy ion data (Sorensen 2011).
