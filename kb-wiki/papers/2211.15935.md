# QRPA calculations for M1 transitions with the noniterative finite amplitude method and the application to neutron radiative capture cross sections

> We derive the equations of quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) based on the finite amplitude method (FAM) with the Hartree-Fock+BCS (HF+BCS) single-particle states, and calculate the magnetic dipole (M1) transition for deformed gadolinium isotopes. Our QRPA calculation shows both large spin-flip transitions in the 5 to 10 MeV excitation energy and the low energy orbital transition that would correspond to the M1 scissors mode observed in nuclear experiments. Then, we calculate neutron capture reactions based on the statistical Hauser-Feshbach theory with the photoabsorption cross sections given by QRPA. We find that the capture cross section is enhanced due to the contribution from the low energy M1 transition although the calculated capture cross section still underestimates the experimental data. This issue in the calculated capture cross section could be improved by uncertainties of low energy E1 transition neglected in our QRPA calculation.

# QRPA calculations for M1 transitions with the noniterative finite amplitude method and the application to neutron radiative capture cross sections

## Key claim

The authors derive QRPA equations using a noniterative finite amplitude method with HF+BCS single-particle states and calculate M1 transitions for deformed gadolinium isotopes. While the inclusion of low-energy M1 transitions enhances the calculated neutron capture cross section, the result still underestimates experimental data, which the authors attribute to neglected low-energy E1 transition uncertainties rather than deficiencies in the M1 calculation itself.

## Method

The authors derive QRPA matrices A and B through explicit linearization of residual interactions in the finite amplitude method framework, using HF+BCS single-particle states with Skyrme SLy4 parameters. They solve the QRPA equation for the M1 operator with the spurious rotational mode eliminated using symmetry operators, then feed the microscopically calculated photoabsorption cross sections for both E1 and M1 transitions into statistical Hauser-Feshbach theory calculations to obtain neutron capture cross sections.

## Key numbers

- The QRPA equation is solved from E = 62.5 keV to 20 MeV at every 62.5 keV with a fixed Lorentzian width gamma = 125 keV
- The configuration space is restricted to quasiparticle pairs with u_mu^2, v_nu^2 > 10^-2 and E_mu + E_nu < E_cut = 50 MeV
- The total M1 transition strength integrated from 5 MeV to 15 MeV is sum B(M1) = 29.4 mu_N^2 for ^156Gd
- The orbital contribution near 3.3 MeV (scissors mode) yields sum B(M1) = 4.9 mu_N^2 when integrated up to 4 MeV
- The spurious mode appears at 1.2 MeV in the M1 transition for ^156Gd

## Context

This work extends the noniterative FAM-RPA approach previously developed by Sasaki (2022) to include pairing correlations via QRPA, enabling M1 transition calculations for open-shell deformed nuclei. The authors build on prior QRPA calculations for M1 transitions in deformed nuclei by Vesely (2009), Nesterenko (2010), and Repko (2018), and apply the results to neutron capture calculations following the framework of Mumpower (2017) and Goriely (2018). The approach enables fully microscopic neutron capture cross section calculations without phenomenological giant resonance parameters, though the authors note that the overestimation of total M1 strength (compared to the typical ~11 mu_N^2 for heavy deformed nuclei reported by Richter (1995)) could be addressed by applying a spin g-factor quenching factor of 0.6-0.7 as proposed by Kruzic (2020).
