# Pairing Correlations and Thermodynamic Properties of Inner Crust Matter

> In this review paper we discuss the effects of pairing correlations on inner crust matter in the density region where nuclear clusters are supposed to coexist with nonlocalised neutrons. The pairing correlations are treated in the framework of the finite temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach and using zero range nuclear forces. After a short introduction and presentation of the formalism we discuss how the pairing correlations affect the structure of the inner crust matter, i.e., the proton to neutron ratio and the size of Wigner-Seitz cells. Then we show how the pairing correlations influence, though the specific heat of neutrons, the thermalization of the crust in the case of a rapid cooling scenario.

## Key claim

This review argues that pairing correlations significantly affect both the structure (proton-to-neutron ratio and Wigner-Seitz cell size) and the thermodynamic properties (specific heat and thermalization time) of inner crust matter, but the quantitative impact on cell structure is ambiguous because the absolute minima of the binding energy are very little pronounced, especially in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations.

## Method

The authors employ the finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach with zero-range Skyrme-type nuclear forces and three different pairing functionals (isoscalar weak ISW, isoscalar strong ISS, and isovector strong IVS) to describe spherical Wigner-Seitz cells containing nuclear clusters surrounded by nonlocalized neutrons and a uniform electron gas. The cell structure (neutron number N, proton number Z, and cell radius R_WS) is determined by minimizing the total energy under beta-equilibrium conditions, and the neutron specific heat is calculated from the temperature-dependent quasiparticle spectrum. The thermalization time of the inner crust is estimated using the relativistic heat diffusion equation in a rapid cooling scenario where the core cools via direct URCA processes.

## Key numbers

- The inner crust region considered spans baryonic densities from approximately 4.6 x 10^11 g cm^-3 (cell 10) to 7.9 x 10^13 g cm^-3 (cell 1), based on the Negele and Vautherin (1973) Wigner-Seitz cell structure.
- For the isoscalar strong (ISS) pairing scenario, the pairing strength is V_0 = -570 MeV fm^-3, and for the isoscalar weak (ISW) scenario, V_0 = -430 MeV fm^-3, with parameters alpha=0.45, eta=0.7, and rho_0=0.16 fm^-3.
- For the isovector strong (IVS) pairing functional, the parameters are V_0 = -703.86 MeV fm^-3, eta_s=0.7115, alpha_s=0.3865, eta_n=0.9727, and alpha_n=0.3906.
- In cell 6 (baryon density 2.6 x 10^12 g cm^-3), the HFB calculations predict 378 unbound neutrons out of a total of 460 neutrons and 40 protons.
- The quadrupole collective mode in cell 2 has a reduced transition probability B(E2) of 25 x 10^3 Weisskopf units, with more than 99% of the total quadrupole strength concentrated in a peak at about 3 MeV.
- The finite-size energy correction for the Wigner-Seitz approximation is parametrized as f(rho_{n_g}, R_WS) = 89.05 (rho_{n_g}/rho_0)^0.1425 R_WS^{-2}, where rho_0 is the saturation density.
- The specific heat calculations are performed for a temperature of 10^9 K, and the thermalization time evolution is shown for an initial temperature T_i = 500 keV.

## Context

This review extends the pioneering work of Negele and Vautherin (1973), who first calculated inner crust structure using Hartree-Fock without pairing, and the subsequent Hartree-Fock BCS studies by Baldo et al. (2005, 2006, 2007). The authors build on their own previous HFB calculations (Grill et al., 2011; Sandulescu et al., 2004a, 2004b, 2008) to systematically compare three pairing scenarios. The work enables more realistic modeling of neutron star crust cooling and thermal relaxation following X-ray transients, as studied by Shternin (2007), Brown and Cumming (2009), and Haensel (2007), though a systematic investigation of collective QRPA modes on thermodynamic properties is still missing.
