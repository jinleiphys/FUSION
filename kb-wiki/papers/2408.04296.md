# Spin polarization of $Λ$ hyperons along beam direction in p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=8.16$ TeV using hydrodynamic approaches

> We have implemented the 3+1 dimensional CLVisc hydrodynamics model with TRENTO-3D initial conditions to investigate the spin polarization of $Λ$ hyperons along the beam direction in p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 8.16$ TeV. Following our previous theoretical framework based on quantum kinetic theory, we consider three different scenarios: $Λ$ equilibrium, $s$ quark equilibrium, and iso-thermal equilibrium scenarios. We have computed the second Fourier sine coefficients of spin polarization along the beam direction, denoted as $\left\langle P_{z} \sin 2(ϕ_{p} - Ψ_{2}) \right\rangle$, with $ϕ_{p} - Ψ_{2}$ being the azimuthal angle relative to the second-order event plane $Ψ_{2}$, as functions of multiplicity, transverse momentum and pseudo-rapidity in the three scenarios. Additionally, we have also computed the spin polarization along the beam direction, $P_{z}$, as a function of the azimuthal angle. We find that the spin polarization induced by thermal vorticity always provides an opposite contribution compared to the shear-induced polarization in p+Pb collisions. The total spin polarization computed by the current hydrodynamic model disagrees with the data measured by LHC-CMS experiments. Our findings imply that other non-flow effects may play a crucial role in p+Pb collisions.

# Spin polarization of Λ hyperons along beam direction in p+Pb collisions at √sNN = 8.16 TeV using hydrodynamic approaches

## Key claim

The total spin polarization of Λ hyperons along the beam direction computed by the 3+1D CLVisc hydrodynamics model with TRENTO-3D initial conditions disagrees with LHC-CMS experimental data in p+Pb collisions at √sNN = 8.16 TeV, implying that non-flow effects not captured by thermal vorticity and shear-induced polarization may play a crucial role in small collision systems.

## Method

The authors implemented the 3+1D CLVisc hydrodynamics model with TRENTO-3D initial conditions to simulate p+Pb collisions at √sNN = 8.16 TeV, running 10^5 minimum bias events divided into five multiplicity intervals. They computed the second Fourier sine coefficient ⟨Pz sin 2(φp − Ψ2)⟩ and the azimuthal-angle-dependent spin polarization Pz using the modified Cooper-Frye formula based on quantum kinetic theory, considering three scenarios: Λ equilibrium, s quark equilibrium (with ms = 0.3 GeV), and iso-thermal equilibrium (dropping ∇T terms). The spin polarization was decomposed into contributions from the thermal-vorticity tensor and the thermal-shear tensor.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: √sNN = 8.16 TeV for p+Pb collisions
- Freeze-out temperature: Tf = 154 MeV
- s quark mass in the s quark equilibrium scenario: ms = 0.3 GeV
- Five multiplicity intervals used: [3,60), [60,120), [120,150), [150,185), [185,250) for charged particle multiplicity Nch with pT > 0.4 GeV and |η| < 2.4
- Average charged multiplicity in the highest interval: ⟨Nch⟩exp = 203.3 (experiment) vs. ⟨Nch⟩CLVisc = 204.2 (model)
- Average charged multiplicity in the lowest interval: ⟨Nch⟩exp = 40 (experiment) vs. ⟨Nch⟩CLVisc = 29.3 (model)
- Integration region for spin polarization: pT ∈ [0.8, 3.0] GeV, η ∈ [−2.4, 2.4], φ ∈ [0, 2π]
- At least 2000 minimum bias events per multiplicity interval for spin polarization calculations
- The total ⟨Pz sin 2(φp − Ψ2)⟩ is negative in all three scenarios except for low multiplicity in the iso-thermal equilibrium scenario
- In Λ and iso-thermal equilibrium scenarios, total ⟨Pz sin 2(φp − Ψ2)⟩ changes from positive to negative at pT ≈ 1.0 GeV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework previously applied to relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions (STAR Collaboration 2017, ALICE Collaboration 2021) to the small system of p+Pb collisions, where LHC-CMS experiments (Chenyan Li 2024 talk) recently measured spin polarization along the beam direction. The authors note that the CMS data show the second Fourier sine coefficient decreasing with multiplicity, contrasting with the trend of elliptic flow v2, which challenges the picture that spin polarization is induced by anisotropic flow. The study uses the same three scenarios (Λ equilibrium, s quark equilibrium, iso-thermal equilibrium) employed in prior works by Fu (2021), Becattini (2021), Yi (2021), and Wu (2022) for nucleus-nucleus collisions. The results enable future investigations of non-flow effects, electromagnetic field contributions, spin Hall effects from ∇(μ/T), and interaction corrections to the modified Cooper-Frye formula as discussed by Fang (2022, 2023) and Lin (2024).
