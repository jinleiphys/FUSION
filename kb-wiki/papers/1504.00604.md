# Quartic isospin asymmetry energy of nuclear matter from chiral pion-nucleon dynamics

> Based on a chiral approach to nuclear matter, we calculate the quartic term in the expansion of the equation of state of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter. The contributions to the quartic isospin asymmetry energy $A_4(k_f)$ arising from $1π$-exchange and chiral $2π$-exchange in nuclear matter are calculated analytically together with three-body terms involving virtual $Δ(1232)$-isobars. From these interaction terms one obtains at saturation density $ρ_0 = 0.16\,$fm$^{-3}$ the value $A_4(k_{f0})= 1.5\,$MeV, more than three times as large as the kinetic energy part. Moreover, iterated $1π$-exchange exhibits components for which the fourth derivative with the respect to the isospin asymmetry parameter $δ$ becomes singular at $δ=0$. The genuine presence of a non-analytical term $δ^4 \ln|δ|$ in the expansion of the energy per particle of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter is demonstrated by evaluating a s-wave contact interaction at second order.

## Key claim
The quartic isospin asymmetry energy of nuclear matter at saturation density is small (1.5 MeV), but the expansion of the energy per particle in the isospin asymmetry parameter δ contains a non-analytical term δ⁴ ln|δ| that must be included in future equation-of-state fits.

## Method
The author calculates contributions to the quartic isospin asymmetry energy A₄(k_f) from one-pion exchange, chiral two-pion exchange, and three-body terms involving virtual Δ(1232)-isobars within a chiral approach to nuclear matter. Analytical expressions are derived using expansion formulas for integrals over two Fermi spheres with different proton and neutron Fermi momenta. The presence of a non-analytical δ⁴ ln|δ| term is demonstrated by evaluating a s-wave contact interaction at second order in many-body perturbation theory.

## Key numbers
- A₄(k_f0) = 1.5 MeV at saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ (k_f0 = 263 MeV)
- Kinetic energy part A₄(k_f0)^(kin) = 0.464 MeV, using nucleon mass M = 939 MeV
- A₄(k_f0) is 3.2 times the free Fermi-gas kinetic energy part
- A₄(k_f0)^(fac) = -1.35 MeV (factorizable Fock contribution from iterated 1π-exchange)
- A₄(k_f0)^(F2) = 1.70 MeV (Fock exchange term without Pauli-blocking)
- Quadratic isospin asymmetry energy A₂(k_f0)^(emp) = 34 MeV used as empirical constraint
- Δ-nucleon mass splitting Δ = 293 MeV
- Pion mass m_π = 135 MeV, pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV, axial-vector coupling g_A = 1.3
- Short-distance parameters: B₅ = 0, B_{n,5} = -3.58

## Context
This work extends the chiral approach to nuclear matter developed by Kaiser (previous works, 2002-2012) which treats multi-pion exchange explicitly and adjusts short-distance parameters to bulk nuclear matter properties. It contradicts the recent result of Cai and Li (2015) who found A₄(ρ₀) = (7.2 ± 2.5) MeV, about 16 times the free Fermi gas prediction, while agreeing with the Darmstadt group (Drischler et al., 2014) who obtained A₄(ρ₀) = (1.0 ± 0.2) MeV from chiral low-momentum interactions. The demonstration of a non-analytical δ⁴ ln|δ| term enables improved fits of the equation of state for isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter and has implications for neutron star properties such as proton fraction at beta-equilibrium and the direct URCA process.
