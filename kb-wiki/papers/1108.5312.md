# Experimental Validation of the Largest Calculated Isospin-Symmetry-Breaking Effect in a Superallowed Fermi Decay

> A precision measurement of the gamma yields following the beta decay of 32Cl has determined its isobaric analogue branch to be (22.47^{+0.21}_{-0.19})%. Since it is an almost pure Fermi decay, we can also determine the amount of isospin-symmetry breaking in this superallowed transition. We find a very large value, delta_C=5.3(9)%, in agreement with a shell-model calculation. This result sets a benchmark for isospin-symmetry-breaking calculations and lends support for similarly-calculated, yet smaller, corrections that are currently applied to 0+ -> 0+ transitions for tests of the Standard Model.

## Key claim

The isospin-symmetry-breaking correction in the superallowed Fermi decay of 32Cl is measured to be delta_C = 5.3(9)%, the largest ever determined in such a transition, and agrees with a shell-model calculation of delta_C = 4.6(5)% within stated uncertainties. This validation supports the theoretical procedures used to calculate the smaller isospin-breaking corrections applied to 0+ -> 0+ transitions for Standard Model tests.

## Method

The experiment used the inverse-kinematic transfer reaction 1H(32S,n)32Cl with a 24.8 MeV/nucleon 32S beam on a LN2-cooled H2 gas target at the Cyclotron Institute at Texas A&M University. Reaction products were separated by the Momentum Achromatic Recoil Separator to produce a 91% pure, 20 MeV/nucleon 32Cl beam that was implanted in an aluminized-Mylar tape and moved to a shielded counting station, where beta-gamma coincident events were acquired using a 1.5-inch diameter, 1 mm-thick scintillator and a 70% HPGe detector. Relative gamma-ray yields were converted from peak areas using a photopeak efficiency calibration determined to +/-0.2% from 50-1400 keV and +/-0.4% from 1.4-3.5 MeV, with extrapolations above 3.5 MeV using the Cyltran and Penelope Monte Carlo codes.

## Key numbers

- Isobaric analogue branch for 32Cl decay: R = (22.47 +0.21/-0.19)%, with statistical uncertainty +/-0.13% and systematic uncertainty +0.16/-0.14%
- Experimental isospin-symmetry-breaking correction: delta_C = 5.3(9)%
- Theoretical shell-model isospin-symmetry-breaking correction: delta_C = 4.6(5)%, composed of delta_C1 = 3.75(45)% and delta_C2 = 0.85(3)%
- Partial half-life to the isobaric analogue state: t = 1.327(13) s
- Phase-space factor: f = 2411.6 +/- 2.3 +/- 0.3
- Experimental ft value: ft = 3200(30) s
- Decay energy: Q_EC = 12680.9(6) keV
- 32Cl half-life: t_1/2 = 298(1) ms
- Energy separation between the analogue 1+,T=1 state at 7002 keV and the nearby 1+,T=0 state at 7190 keV: 188.2 +/- 1.2 keV

## Context

This work extends the study of isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections in superallowed Fermi decays, which have previously been limited to cases where delta_C is smaller than about 2% (Hardy 2009). The 32Cl result provides a benchmark for theoretical methods proposed to calculate isospin-symmetry breaking in nuclei (Towner and Hardy 2008; Auerbach 2009; Liang 2009; Satula 2011; Miller 2008, 2009), and validates the shell-model approach currently applied to the 13 most precisely measured 0+ -> 0+ transitions used for tests of the Standard Model and determination of V_ud.
