# The pion quasiparticle in the low-temperature phase of QCD

> We investigate the properties of the pion quasiparticle in the low-temperature phase of two-flavor QCD on the lattice with support from chiral effective theory. We find that the pion quasiparticle mass is significantly reduced compared to its value in the vacuum, by contrast with the static screening mass, which increases with temperature. By a simple argument, near the chiral limit the two masses are expected to determine the quasiparticle dispersion relation. Analyzing two-point functions of the axial charge density at non-vanishing spatial momentum, we find that the predicted dispersion relation and the residue of the pion pole are simultaneously consistent with the lattice data at low momentum. The test, based on fits to the correlation functions, is confirmed by a second analysis using the Backus-Gilbert method.

## Key claim

The pion quasiparticle mass is significantly reduced compared to its vacuum value at temperatures near the QCD crossover, while the static screening mass increases; the authors test whether the parameter \(u\) determined from the ratio of quasiparticle mass to screening mass correctly predicts the momentum-dependent dispersion relation, with the caveat that the chiral effective theory predictions are only expected to hold at sufficiently small momenta.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with two degenerate dynamical light flavors using the Wilson plaquette action and O(a) improved Wilson fermions on a \(24 \times 64^3\) finite-temperature ensemble at \(T = 169(3)\) MeV and a \(128 \times 64^3\) zero-temperature ensemble with identical bare parameters. They extracted the screening pion mass and decay constant from static spatial correlators, determined the parameter \(u\) from time-dependent correlators at zero spatial momentum using two estimators, and then tested the predicted dispersion relation by analyzing the two-point function of the axial charge density at non-vanishing spatial momentum using both fit-based and Backus-Gilbert inversion methods.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Brandt 2014) of the pion at finite temperature in two-flavor lattice QCD, which performed a temperature scan on coarser \(16 \times 32^3\) ensembles; here they focus on a single temperature with higher statistics on a finer \(24 \times 64^3\) lattice. The results are consistent with predictions from thermal chiral effective theory (Pisarski 1996, Son 2001, Son 2002) and with two-loop results in zero-temperature chiral perturbation theory (Schenk 1993, Toublan 1997). The study enables future lattice calculations approaching the chiral limit for quantitative comparison with effective theory, and provides a method to test the dispersion relation and residue predictions simultaneously using Euclidean correlation functions at non-zero momentum.
