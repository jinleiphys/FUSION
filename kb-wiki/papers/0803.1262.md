# Dilepton production from a viscous QGP

> This work calculates the first correction to the leading order qq dilepton production rates due to shear viscosity in an expanding gas. The modified rates are integrated over the space-time history of a viscous hydrodynamic simulation of RHIC collisions. The net result is a {\em hardening} of $q_\perp$ spectrum with the magnitude of the correction increasing with invariant mass. We argue that a thermal description is reliable for invariant masses less than $M_{max}\approx(2τ_0 T_0^2)/(η/s)$. For reasonable values of the shear viscosity and thermalization time $M_{max}\approx 4.5$ GeV. Finally, the early emission from a viscous medium is compared to emission from a longitudinally free streaming plasma. Qualitative differences in $q_\perp$ spectrum are seen which could be used to extract information on the thermalization time, viscosity to entropy ratio and possibly the thermalization mechanism in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The first viscous correction to leading-order quark-antiquark dilepton production rates hardens the transverse momentum spectrum, with the correction magnitude increasing with invariant mass; however, a thermal description is only reliable for invariant masses below \(M_{max}\approx(2\tau_0 T_0^2)/(\eta/s)\), and above this threshold a kinetic description is required.

## Method

The authors calculated the first-order shear viscosity correction to the quark and antiquark distribution functions using the Grad moment expansion (coefficient \(C_1\approx0.97\) for massless fermions), then substituted these into the Born-approximation kinetic theory expression for dilepton emission from \(q\bar{q}\) annihilation. The modified rates were integrated over the space-time history of a 1+1 dimensional boost-invariant viscous hydrodynamic simulation of Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, using a second-order relaxation scheme (Ottinger and Grmela formalism) with initial conditions at \(\tau_0=0.2\) fm/c and a critical temperature \(T_c=0.170\) GeV.

## Key numbers

* For \(\eta/s=0.2\) and \(\tau_0=1\) fm/c, the maximum invariant mass for which a thermal description is reliable is \(M_{max}\approx 4.5\) GeV.
* The viscous correction to the distribution function uses the coefficient \(C_1=14\pi^4/1350\zeta(5)\approx 0.97\) for a massless fermion gas.
* The approximate condition for reliability of the thermal description is \(M_{max} \approx (2 \tau_0 T_0^2)/(\eta/s)\).
* For a simulation started at \(\tau_0=0.2\) fm/c with \(\eta/s=0.2\), the upper bound of the domain of hydrodynamics is at most \(M_{max}\approx 2.0\) GeV.
* Finite viscosity (\(\eta/s=0.2\)) causes an \(\approx 30\%\) increase in yield in the low mass region and \(\approx 50\%\) increase in the higher mass region, due to slower depletion of energy density at early times.

## Context

This work extends the program of viscous hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions, following the dissipative hydrodynamics studies of Dusling and Teaney (2007), Song and Heinz (2007), Baier and Romatschke (2007), and Romatschke (2007). It provides the first calculation of viscous corrections to dilepton observables, complementing earlier work on viscous corrections to hadron spectra by Teaney (2003). The results enable extraction of the shear viscosity to entropy ratio (\(\eta/s\)) and thermalization time from experimental dilepton transverse momentum spectra, and the comparison with a free-streaming plasma model (similar to the approach of Martinez and Strickland, 2007) offers a way to distinguish thermalization mechanisms.
