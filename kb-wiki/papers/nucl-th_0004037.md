# A number projected model with generalized pairing interaction

> A mean-field model with a generalized pairing interaction that accounts for neutron-proton pairing is presented. Both the BCS as well as number-projected solutions of the model are presented. For the latter case the Lipkin-Nogami projection technique was extended to encompass the case of non-separable proton-neutron systems. The influence of the projection on various pairing phases is discussed. In particular, it is shown that number-projection allows for mixing of different pairing phases but, simultanously, acts destructively on the proton-neutron correlations. The basic implications of proton-neutron pairing correlations on nuclear masses are discussed. It is shown that these correlations may provide a natural microscopic explanation of the Wigner energy lacking in mean-field models. A possible phase transition from isovector to isoscalar pairing condensate at high angular momenta is also discussed. In particular predictions for the dynamical moments of inertia for the superdeformed band in $^{88}$Ru are given.

## Key claim

Number projection via the Lipkin-Nogami technique allows mixing of isovector (T=1) and isoscalar (T=0) pairing phases in N=Z nuclei, which the unprojected generalized BCS (gBCS) mean-field does not permit; however, number projection simultaneously acts destructively on proton-neutron correlations.

## Method

The authors extended the Lipkin-Nogami (LN) number-projection technique to non-separable proton-neutron systems, solving the resulting HFB-like equations with a generalized pairing interaction separable in the particle-particle channel. The model uses a deformed Woods-Saxon single-particle potential, includes nuclear rotation via the cranking approximation, and imposes antilinear simplex symmetry as a self-consistent symmetry to simplify the Bogolyubov transformation. The pairing interaction is defined through generators that distinguish T=1 (αα̃) and T=0 (αα) np-pairing modes, with the T=0 strength scaled by a parameter x^(T=0) relative to the T=1 np-pairing strength.

## Key numbers

- The critical neutron/proton excess above which no collective np-pairing solution exists is |T_z^(crit)| ~ 2.
- For N=Z nuclei with isospin-symmetric coupling (G^(T=1)_pp = G^(T=1)_nn = G^(T=1)_np), the BCS solutions fall into three regimes: x^(T=0) < 1 (T=1 favored), x^(T=0) = 1 (degenerate), and x^(T=0) > 1 (pure T=0 np-pairing phase).
- The critical value x^(T=0)_crit for the onset of T=0 pairing in N≠Z nuclei increases sharply with increasing |T_z|.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier letter (Satula 1997) by providing detailed technical exposition of the Lipkin-Nogami projection for np-pairing and new numerical results. It addresses the long-standing difficulty of incorporating T=0 and T=1 pairing on equal footing in mean-field theory, following early efforts by Goswami (1964, 1965), Goodman (1968, 1970, 1972), and others reviewed by Goodman (1979). The model offers a microscopic explanation of the Wigner energy in N≈Z nuclei and predicts a phase transition from isovector to isoscalar pairing condensate at high angular momenta, with specific predictions for the dynamical moments of inertia in the superdeformed band of ⁸⁸Ru.
