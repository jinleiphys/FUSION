# Helicity Evolution at Small x: Summary of Recent Developments

> We construct small-$x$ evolution equations which can be used to calculate quark and anti-quark helicity TMDs and PDFs, along with the $g_1$ structure function. These evolution equations resum powers of $α_s \, \ln^2 (1/x)$ in the polarization-dependent evolution along with the powers of $α_s \, \ln (1/x)$ in the unpolarized evolution which includes saturation effects. The equations are written in an operator form in terms of polarization-dependent Wilson line-like operators. While the equations do not close in general, they become closed and self-contained systems of non-linear equations in the large-$N_c$ and large-$N_c \, \& \, N_f$ limits. We construct a numerical solution of the helicity evolution equations in the large-$N_c$ limit. Employing the extracted intercept, we are able to predict directly from theory the behavior of the quark helicity PDFs at small $x$, which should have important phenomenological consequences. We also give an estimate of how much of the proton's spin may be at small $x$ and what impact this has on the so-called "spin crisis." Based on JHEP 1601 (2016) 072 (arXiv:1511.06737), arXiv:1610.06197 and arXiv:1610.06188.

## Key claim

The authors derive small-$x$ evolution equations for quark and anti-quark helicity parton distribution functions (PDFs) and transverse momentum-dependent PDFs (TMDs) that resum powers of $\alpha_s \ln^2(1/x)$ in the polarization-dependent evolution, but only in the double-logarithmic approximation (DLA) where the unpolarized dipole $S$-matrix is set to 1; the equations do not systematically include all leading-logarithmic (LLA) terms of the pure helicity evolution.

## Method

The authors construct evolution equations for the polarized dipole amplitude $G_{10}(z)$ and a "neighbor dipole" amplitude $\Gamma_{02,21}(z')$ using polarization-dependent Wilson line-like operators in the eikonal approximation. They close the resulting hierarchy of equations in the large-$N_c$ limit and in the large-$N_c \& N_f$ limit, then numerically solve the large-$N_c$ system on a discretized grid in rescaled rapidity $\eta$ and transverse coordinate $s_{10}$ variables.

## Key numbers

- The small-$x$ intercept for the flavor-singlet quark helicity PDF is $\alpha_h = 2.31 \, \sqrt{\alpha_s N_c / (2\pi)}$, obtained from the numerical solution of the large-$N_c$ evolution equations.
- This intercept is about 35% smaller than the "pure glue" intercept of $3.66\sqrt{\alpha_s N_c/(2\pi)}$ obtained by Bartels, Ermolaev, and Ryskin (1996).
- For $\alpha_s \approx 0.25$, the intercept evaluates to $\alpha_h \approx 0.80$.
- The numerical solution was computed with grid parameters $\eta_{max} = 40$ and $\Delta\eta = 0.05$.

## Context

This work extends the small-$x$ helicity evolution framework beyond the leading-twist treatment of Bartels, Ermolaev, and Ryskin (1996) by resumming all twists at small $x$, identifying diagram contributions omitted in that earlier work. The derived intercept provides direct theoretical input for the small-$x$ behavior of $\Delta\Sigma(x,Q^2)$, which is needed to resolve uncertainties in global fits of helicity PDFs from groups such as DSSV (de Florian et al., 2014), JAM (Sato et al., 2016), and NNPDF (Nocera et al., 2014). The results enable future phenomenological studies of the quark contribution to the proton spin, particularly relevant for measurements at a future Electron-Ion Collider.
