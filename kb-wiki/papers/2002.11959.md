# Pole analysis on the hadron spectroscopy of $Λ_b\to J/Ψp K^-$

> In this paper we study the $J/Ψp$ spectroscopy in the process of $Λ_b\to J/Ψp K^-$. The final state interactions of coupled channel $J/Ψp$ ~-~ $\bar{D} Σ_c$~-~$\bar{D}^{*} Σ_c$ are constructed based on K-matrix with the Chew-Mandelstam function. We build the $Λ_b\to J/Ψp K^-$ amplitude according to the Au-Morgan-Pennington method. The event shape is fitted and the decay width of $Λ_b\to J/Ψp K^-$ is used to constrain the parameters, too. With the amplitudes we extract out the poles and their residues. Our amplitude and pole analysis suggest that the $P_c(4312)$ should be $\bar{D}Σ_c$ molecule, the $P_c(4440)$ could be an S-wave compact pentaquark state, and the structure around $P_c(4457)$ is caused by the cusp effect. The future experimental measurement of the decays of $Λ_b\to \bar{D}Σ_c K^-$ and $Λ_b\to \bar{D}^*Σ_c K^-$ would further help to study the nature of these resonances.

## Key claim

The amplitude and pole analysis suggests that the three structures observed by LHCb in the J/ψp spectrum have different origins: the Pc(4312) is a D̄Σc molecule, the Pc(4440) could be an S-wave compact pentaquark state, and the structure around Pc(4457) is caused by a cusp effect rather than a genuine resonance, though a molecular component cannot be entirely excluded.

## Method

The authors constructed a coupled-channel scattering amplitude for J/ψp, D̄Σc, and D̄*Σc using a K-matrix formalism with Chew-Mandelstam functions to enforce unitarity. They built the Λb → J/ψp K− decay amplitude using the Au-Morgan-Pennington (AMP) method to incorporate final-state interactions, then fitted the event shape and decay width to LHCb data. Poles were extracted by analytically continuing the T-matrix to unphysical Riemann sheets and classified using the pole counting rule.

## Key numbers

- Pc(4440) pole location: 4444.09 +2.53 −1.48 − i 3.10 +0.53 −1.33 MeV (Fit 2, RS III)
- Pc(4457) pole location: 4456.77 +3.10 −8.89 − i 7.77 +11.07 −4.41 MeV (Fit 2, RS VII)
- Predicted branching ratio for Λb0 → D̄Σc K−: (1.49 ± 0.26) × 10−4
- Predicted branching ratio for Λb0 → D̄*Σc K−: (0.30 ± 0.08) × 10−4
- Fit 2 χ2 per degree of freedom: 1.32

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel K-matrix analysis of Fernandez-Ramirez (2019) and the Lippmann-Schwinger approach of Du (2019) by applying the pole counting rule of Morgan (1992) to distinguish molecular from compact pentaquark states. The analysis enables future experimental measurements of Λb → D̄Σc K− and Λb → D̄*Σc K− decays to further discriminate the nature of these resonances.
