# Comparisons of electric charge and axial charge meson cloud distributions in the PCQM

> The meson cloud distributions in $r$-space are extracted from the nucleon electromagnetic and axial form factors which are derived in the perturbative chiral quark model. The theoretical results indicate that the electric charge and axial charge distributions of the three-quark core are the same, the magnetic charge distributions of the meson cloud and three-quark core are more or less in the same region and peak at distances of around 2 $\rm GeV^{-1}$, but the axial charge meson cloud distributes mainly inside the three-quark core.

## Key claim

The electric charge and axial charge distributions of the three-quark core are the same, while the axial charge meson cloud distributes mainly inside the three-quark core, in contrast to the magnetic charge meson cloud which peaks at distances around 2 GeV^{-1} and occupies a region similar to the three-quark core.

## Method

The authors extract meson cloud distributions in coordinate space (r-space) from nucleon electromagnetic and axial form factors previously calculated in the perturbative chiral quark model (PCQM). They separate the leading order (three-quark core) and loop (meson cloud) contributions to the form factors and perform inverse Fourier transformations to obtain the spatial distributions, then compare the magnetic charge and axial charge distributions from both contributions.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic charge distributions of both the meson cloud and three-quark core peak at distances of around 2 GeV^{-1}.
- The nucleon magnetic moment from the PCQM is μ_p = 2.735 ± 0.121.
- The nucleon axial charge from the PCQM is g_A^N = 1.301 ± 0.230.
- The meson cloud contributes 30%–40% to the total axial charge of octet baryons.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous PCQM studies of octet baryon electromagnetic and axial form factors (Liu 2014, Liu 2015) by extracting the spatial distributions of the meson cloud. The results for magnetic charge distributions are consistent with earlier determinations from chiral perturbation theory (Hammer 2004, MeiBner 2007) and the chiral soliton model (MeiBner 1988, Holzwarth 1996), which found confined pion cloud distributions peaking around 0.3 fm, rather than the long-range (~2 fm) pion cloud reported by Friedrich (2003) in a constituent quark model. The finding that axial charge meson cloud distributes mainly inside the three-quark core provides new insight into the spatial structure of the nucleon's axial properties.
