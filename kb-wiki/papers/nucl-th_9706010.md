# An Unified Approach To Pseudo Scalar Meson Photoproductions Off Nucleons In The Quark Model

> An unified approach to the pseudo scalar meson ($π, η$, and $K$) photoproduction off nucleons are presented. It begins with the low energy QCD Lagrangian, and the resonances in the s- and u- channels are treated in the framework of the quark model The duality hypothesis is imposed to limit the number of the t-channel exchanges. The CGLN amplitudes for each reaction are evaluated, which include both proton and neutron targets. The important role by the S-wave resonances in the second resonance region is discussed, it is particularly important for the $K, η$ and $η^\prime$ photoproductions.

# An Unified Approach To Pseudo Scalar Meson Photoproductions Off Nucleons In The Quark Model

## Key claim

The paper presents a unified quark-model approach to pion, eta, and kaon photoproduction off nucleons that combines the low-energy QCD Lagrangian with the duality hypothesis to limit t-channel exchanges, claiming that this reduces free parameters to essentially one per isospin channel (such as alpha_eta_NN or alpha_KN_Lambda and alpha_KN_Sigma), but the coupling constants for K and eta productions are still treated as free parameters at the present stage due to quark mass effects causing about 30 percent deviation from measured values.

## Method

The authors start from the chiral QCD Lagrangian of Manohar and Georgi, treating pseudoscalar mesons as Goldstone bosons, and evaluate s- and u-channel resonance contributions using the quark model with harmonic oscillator wavefunctions up to principal quantum number n=2 in the SU(6) x O(3) symmetry limit. The CGLN amplitudes for each reaction are derived from quark-level transition operators, separating center-of-mass motion from internal motions, and the duality hypothesis is imposed to exclude t-channel exchanges such as K*, K1, rho, and omega that would cause double counting with the complete set of s- and u-channel resonances. The seagull term generated by gauge transformation of the axial vector in the QCD Lagrangian produces the leading term in the low-energy theorem for threshold pion photoproduction.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant deviation from the Goldberg-Treiman relation is about 30 percent due to quark mass effects, while the Goldberg-Treiman relation is accurate within 5 percent for pion couplings.
- The harmonic oscillator form factor for the seagull term is F(k,q) = exp(-(k-q)^2 / (6 alpha^2)), where alpha is the oscillator strength.
- The s-channel resonance amplitude includes a form factor exp(-(k^2+q^2)/(6 alpha^2)) in the harmonic oscillator basis even in the real photon limit.
- For the resonance S11(1535), the branching ratios are set to x_pi = x_eta = 0.5, while x_pi = 1.0 for all other resonances as a first-order approximation.
- The u-channel resonance contributions include only excited states with n <= 2, which is the minimum number required for contributions from every term in the master equations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigations of threshold pion photoproduction (Li, 1994) and kaon and eta photoproductions (Li, 1995) by imposing the duality hypothesis of Dolen, Horn, and Schmid, which was not done in their earlier kaon study. The approach contrasts with traditional isobaric models such as those by J-C David et al. (1995) and the RPI group (Mukhopadhyay et al.), which treat meson-baryon interactions at the phenomenological level with no explicit QCD connection and have parameters related to the number of included resonances. The unified framework enables direct confrontation of photoproduction data with explicit quark and gluon degrees of freedom, and can be extended to heavy pseudoscalar meson photoproduction such as eta-prime.
