# S-wave DK interactions in the chiral SU(3) quark model

> The $DK$ interaction is relevant to the interpretation of the $D_{sJ}(2317)$. We dynamically investigate $S$-wave $DK$ interactions in the chiral SU(3) quark model by solving the resonating group method equation. The numerical results show an attraction between $D$ and $K$, which is from boson exchanges between light quarks. However, such an attraction is not strong enough to form a $DK$ molecule. Meanwhile, $S$ partial wave phase shifts of $DK$ elastic scattering are obtained. The case of $S$-wave $D^*K$ is rather similar to that of $DK$. To draw a definite conclusion whether a molecular state exists in $DK$ or $D^*K$ system, more details of dynamics should be considered in further study.

# S-wave DK interactions in the chiral SU(3) quark model

## Key claim
The chiral SU(3) quark model predicts an attractive interaction between D and K mesons from boson exchanges between light quarks, but this attraction is not strong enough to form a DK molecular bound state. The authors explicitly state that a definite conclusion on whether a molecular state exists in the DK or D*K system requires further study including additional dynamical effects.

## Method
The authors solved the resonating group method (RGM) equation for S-wave DK interactions in the chiral SU(3) quark model, where the quark-quark interaction includes confinement, one-gluon exchange, and pseudoscalar and scalar meson exchanges. The model parameters for light quark pairs were taken from previous work (Zhang et al., 2007) that successfully described baryon ground states, deuteron binding, and nucleon-nucleon scattering. The annihilation part of the q-anti-q interaction was neglected, and chiral field induced interactions between heavy and light quarks were not considered.

## Key numbers
- The up (down) quark mass was taken as m_u(d) = 313 MeV and the strange quark mass as m_s = 470 MeV.
- The harmonic-oscillator width parameter b_u = 0.50 fm.
- The coupling constant g_ch was determined from g^2_NNpi/4pi = 13.67, with the relation g^2_ch/4pi = (3/5)^2 * (g^2_NNpi/4pi) * (m^2_u / M^2_N).
- The phenomenological sigma meson mass was taken as m_sigma = 595 MeV.
- Three values of the charm quark mass were used: m_c = 1430 MeV, 1550 MeV, and 1870 MeV.
- Two cutoff values were used: Lambda = 1100 MeV and Lambda = 1500 MeV; with the larger cutoff, the attractive potential was 4-6 MeV stronger.
- The scalar meson mixing angle theta^s was tested at 0 degrees, 35.264 degrees, and -18 degrees; non-zero angles weakened the attraction.

## Context
This work extends the chiral SU(3) quark model, previously successful for light quark systems such as nucleon-nucleon scattering and hyperon-nucleon cross sections (Zhang et al., 2007), to the DK system relevant to the interpretation of the D_sJ(2317) meson discovered by BaBar (2003) and confirmed by CLEO (2003). The study was motivated by proposals that D_sJ(2317) might receive a large component of DK (Barnes et al., 2003) or arise from mixing between the 0+ c-anti-s state and the DK continuum (van Beveren and Rupp, 2003). The results indicate that additional dynamics, such as vector meson exchanges, chiral field interactions between heavy and light quarks, and s-channel annihilation, need to be included before a definitive conclusion on DK or D*K molecular states can be reached.
