# On the convergence of the chiral expansion for the baryon ground-state masses

> We study the chiral expansion of the baryon octet and decuplet masses in the isospin limit. It is illustrated that a chiral expansion of the one-loop contributions is rapidly converging up to quark masses that generously encompasses the mass of the physical strange quark. We express the successive orders in terms of physical meson and baryon masses. In addition, owing to specific correlations amongst the chiral moments, we suggest a reordering of terms that make the convergence properties more manifest. Explicit expressions up to chiral order five are derived for all baryon masses at the one-loop level. The baryon masses obtained do not depend on the renormalization scale. Our scheme is tested against QCD lattice data, where the low-energy parameters are systematically correlated by large-Nc sum rules. A reproduction of the baryon masses from PACS-CS, LHPC, HSC, NPLQCD, QCDSF-UKQCD and ETMC is achieved for ensembles with pion and kaon masses smaller than 600 MeV. Predictions for baryon masses on ensembles from CLS as well as all low-energy constants that enter the baryon masses at N3LO are made.

## Key claim

The chiral expansion of baryon octet and decuplet masses converges rapidly up to quark masses that generously encompass the physical strange quark mass, provided the successive orders are expressed in terms of physical meson and baryon masses and a reordering of terms based on correlations among chiral moments is applied.

## Method

The authors derived explicit one-loop expressions up to chiral order five for all baryon octet and decuplet masses using the relativistic chiral Lagrangian with baryon octet and decuplet fields, employing physical meson and baryon masses in the loop functions. They applied large-N_c sum rules to correlate the low-energy parameters, reducing the number of free parameters, and performed global fits to QCD lattice data from six different collaborations (PACS-CS, LHPC, HSC, NPLQCD, QCDSF-UKQCD, and ETMC) for ensembles with pion and kaon masses below 600 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order chiral symmetry breaking parameters extracted from physical baryon mass differences are: b_D ≈ 0.07 GeV^{-1}, b_F ≈ -0.21 GeV^{-1}, d_D ≈ -0.49 GeV^{-1}
- The leading-order baryon masses from the chiral Lagrangian are: M = M_N^{(0)} ≈ 800 MeV for the octet and M + Δ = M_Δ^{(0)} ≈ 1100 MeV for the decuplet
- The parameters b_0 and d_0 are estimated as: b_0 ≈ -0.39 GeV^{-1}, d_0 ≈ -0.11 GeV^{-1}
- At second order (Q^2), the physical baryon masses are reproduced with an uncertainty of 3.1 MeV for the octet states and 3.5 MeV for the decuplet states
- The fit covers more than 220 lattice data points from five different lattice groups (PACS-CS, LHPC, HSC, NPLQCD, QCDSF-UKQCD) plus ETMC data
- The analysis is restricted to ensembles with pion and kaon masses smaller than 600 MeV

## Context

This work extends the comprehensive analysis of Lutz (2014) by further developing the chiral extrapolation scheme to restore chiral constraints close to the chiral limit where m_π ≪ M_Δ - M_N. It addresses the long-standing controversy in the community (Bernard 1993, Banerjee 1994, Banerjee 1995, Lehnhart 2004, Semke 2005) about whether meaningful chiral expansion results can be obtained in the flavour SU(3) case. The new scheme enables predictions for baryon masses on ensembles from the CLS collaboration and provides all low-energy constants entering the baryon masses at N^3LO.
