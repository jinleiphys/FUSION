# Neutrino processes in the $K^0$ condensed phase of color flavor locked quark matter

> We study weak interactions involving Goldstone bosons in the neutral kaon condensed phase of color flavor locked quark matter. We calculate the rates for the dominant processes that contribute to the neutrino mean free p ath and to neutrino production. A light $K^+$ state, with a mass $\tilde{m}_{K^+} \propto (Δ/μ) (Δ/m_s)(m_d-m_u)$, where $μ$ and $Δ$ are the quark chemical potential and superconducting gap respectively, is shown to play an important role. We identify unique characteristics of weak interaction rates in this novel phase and discuss how they might influence neutrino emission in core collapse supernova and neutron stars.

## Key claim

In the neutral kaon condensed phase of color flavor locked (CFL) quark matter, a light $K^+$ state with mass $\tilde{m}_{K^+} \propto (\Delta/\mu) (\Delta/m_s)(m_d-m_u)$ plays an important role in weak interaction processes, and the rates for neutrino emission and absorption are dominated by reactions involving this $K^+$ mode, but only under the assumption that instanton-induced contributions to the $K^0$ mass are negligible (set to $\chi=0$ in this work).

## Method

The authors construct an effective chiral Lagrangian for the CFLK$^0$ phase, gauging it to obtain leading-order couplings of Goldstone bosons to weak currents. They compute neutrino mean free paths and emissivities for charged-current processes ($\nu_e \rightarrow e^- K^+$, $\bar{\nu}_e K^+ \rightarrow e^+$, and analogous reactions with $\pi^\pm$) and neutral-current processes ($\nu \rightarrow \nu \tilde{\pi}_0$, $\nu \rightarrow \nu \tilde{\eta}$) by evaluating phase-space integrals with thermal distribution functions for mesons, electrons, and neutrinos at temperatures $T=5$ MeV and $T=15$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- Quark chemical potential $\mu = 400$ MeV, superconducting gap $\Delta = 100$ MeV, strange quark mass $m_s = 150$ MeV, up quark mass $m_u = 3.75$ MeV, down quark mass $m_d = 7.5$ MeV
- Decay constant $f_\pi = 0.21 \mu = 84$ MeV; velocity factor $v = 1/\sqrt{3}$
- Neutrino mean free path for $\nu_e \rightarrow K^+ e^-$: $\lambda = 682$ m at $T=5$ MeV, $\lambda = 42$ m at $T=15$ MeV
- Neutrino mean free path for $\bar{\nu}_e K^+ \rightarrow e^+$: $\lambda = 848$ m at $T=5$ MeV, $\lambda = 43$ m at $T=15$ MeV
- Emissivity for $K^+ \rightarrow e^+ \nu_e$: $\dot{\epsilon} = 7.4 \times 10^{30}$ ergs/cm$^3$/s at $T=5$ MeV, $3.5 \times 10^{31}$ ergs/cm$^3$/s at $T=15$ MeV
- Emissivity for $K^+ e^- \rightarrow \nu_e$: $\dot{\epsilon} = 1.6 \times 10^{34}$ ergs/cm$^3$/s at $T=5$ MeV, $6.9 \times 10^{36}$ ergs/cm$^3$/s at $T=15$ MeV
- Emissivity for $\tilde{\pi}_0 \rightarrow \nu \bar{\nu}$: $\dot{\epsilon} = 1.4 \times 10^{35}$ ergs/cm$^3$/s at $T=5$ MeV, $1.1 \times 10^{36}$ ergs/cm$^3$/s at $T=15$ MeV
- Mixing angle between $\pi_0$ and $\eta$ in CFLK$^0$ phase: $\psi \simeq 25^\circ$ (compared to $\psi_{\rm CFL} \simeq 15^\circ$ in the CFL phase)
- Critical temperature for $K^0$ condensate melting estimated as $T_c \gsim 50$ MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of neutrino emission and propagation rates in the symmetric CFL phase by Reddy (2002) and Jaikumar (2002) to the less symmetric CFLK$^0$ phase, where neutral kaon condensation occurs due to the strange quark mass stress identified by Bedaque and Schafer (2001). The results show that the CFLK$^0$ phase has a specific heat roughly twice that of the CFL phase and that reactions involving the light $K^+$ mode dominate neutrino opacity, which could significantly affect neutrino emission in core-collapse supernovae and neutron star cooling. The authors note that the size of the superconducting gap and instanton-induced $\langle \bar{q} q \rangle$ at these densities remain poorly known, and a large instanton contribution would suppress $K^0$ condensation entirely.
