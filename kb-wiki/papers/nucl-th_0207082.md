# Nuclear absorption and anomalous J/psi suppression in Pb+Pb collisions

> We have studied the J/psi suppression in 158 GeV/c Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS. J/psi production is assumed to be a two step process, (i) formation of c bar{c} pair, which is accurately calculable in QCD and (ii) formation of J/psi meson from the c bar{c} pair, which can be conveniently parameterized. In a pA/AA collision, as the c bar{c} pair pass through the nuclear medium, it gains relative square momentum. As a result, some of the c bar{c} pairs can gain enough momentum to cross the threshold to become open charm meson, leading to suppression in pA/AA collisions. The model without any free parameter could describe the of NA50 data on centrality dependence of the ratio's; J/psi over Drell-Yan, J/psi over minimum bias and also the Drell-Yan over minimum bias. The model was used to predict J/psi suppression at RHIC energy. At RHIC energy, hard processes may be important. With hard processes included, J/psi's are strongly suppressed, in agreement with other model calculations. We also show that centrality dependence of J/psi over minimum bias ratio can be used to determine the fraction of hard processes in the collision.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the anomalous J/psi suppression observed in Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS can be explained entirely by conventional nuclear absorption (gain of relative square momentum by c-cbar pairs traversing the nuclear medium) without requiring a deconfined quark-gluon plasma scenario, and that even at RHIC energy the suppression patterns from nuclear absorption and from a QGP-based model may be indistinguishable.

## Method

The author models J/psi production as a two-step process: (i) perturbative QCD calculation of c-cbar pair production, and (ii) a parameterized transition probability for c-cbar pairs to form J/psi mesons. As c-cbar pairs traverse the nuclear medium, they gain relative square momentum at a rate epsilon^2 = 0.225 GeV^2/fm; pairs that gain enough momentum cross the open charm threshold and are lost from J/psi production. The model is applied to Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV/c by folding the suppression factor with Glauber-model nuclear geometry and a Gaussian E_T-b correlation, with parameters q=0.274 GeV and a=1.97 obtained from fitting the NA50 E_T distribution above 60 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Momentum gain per unit length: epsilon^2 = 0.225 GeV^2/fm, obtained from fitting total J/psi cross section data in pp, pA, and AA collisions
- Normalization factor sigma^{J/psi}/sigma^{DY} = 53.5 used for J/psi to Drell-Yan ratio calculations
- Average E_T loss in a J/psi or DY event: approximately 3 GeV less than a minimum bias event
- Parameters for E_T distribution fit: q = 0.274 GeV (average E_T per participant), a = 1.97 (dimensionless dispersion parameter)
- At RHIC energy, nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section assumed to be 41 mb (versus 32 mb at SPS)
- For RHIC predictions, E_T distribution scaled with q = 0.46 GeV; hard scattering fraction of 37% used in some calculations

## Context

This work extends the QCD-based model of Qiu, Vary, and Zhang (2002) for J/psi suppression via relative momentum gain, which the author previously applied to NA50 J/psi/DY data (Chaudhuri, 2002). The present paper adds analysis of J/psi/minimum-bias and DY/minimum-bias ratios, including the E_T loss effect identified by Capella, Kaidalov, and Sousa (2002). The model contradicts the interpretation that anomalous J/psi suppression necessarily signals deconfinement, as argued by the NA50 collaboration and by Blaizot, Dinh, and Ollitrault (2000). It enables predictions for RHIC that can be compared with future Au+Au data and suggests that the J/psi/MB ratio shape may be used to determine the fraction of hard processes in the collision.
