# Fusion of Borromean nucleus $^{9}$Be with $^{197}$Au target at near barrier energies

> To probe the role of the intrinsic structure of the projectile on sub-barrier fusion, measurement of fusion cross sections has been carried out in $^{9}$Be + $^{197}$Au system in the energy range E$_{c.m.}$/V$_B$ $\approx$ 0.82 to 1.16 using off-beam gamma counting method. Measured fusion excitation function has been analyzed in the framework of the coupled-channel approach using CCFULL code. It is observed that the coupled-channel calculations, including couplings to the inelastic state of the target and the first two states of the rotational band built on the ground state of the projectile, provide a very good description of the sub-barrier fusion data. At above barrier energies, the fusion cross section is found to be suppressed by $\approx$ 39(2)\% as compared to the coupled-channel prediction. A comparison of reduced excitation function of $^{9}$Be + $^{197}$Au with other $x$ + $^{197}$Au shows a larger enhancement for $^9$Be in the sub-barrier region amongst Z=2-5 weakly and tightly bound projectiles, which indicates the prominent role of the projectile deformation in addition to the weak binding.

# Fusion of Borromean nucleus \(^{9}\)Be with \(^{197}\)Au target at near barrier energies

## Key claim

The coupled-channel calculations including couplings to the inelastic state of the target and the first two states of the rotational band built on the ground state of the projectile provide a very good description of the sub-barrier fusion data, but at above-barrier energies the fusion cross section is suppressed by approximately 39(2)% relative to the coupled-channel prediction. Among Z=2-5 weakly and tightly bound projectiles on \(^{197}\)Au, \(^{9}\)Be shows the largest sub-barrier fusion enhancement, which indicates that projectile deformation plays a prominent role in addition to weak binding.

## Method

Fusion cross sections were measured for the \(^{9}\)Be + \(^{197}\)Au system in the energy range E\(_{c.m.}\)/V\(_B\) approximately 0.82 to 1.16 using the off-beam gamma counting method with HPGe detectors. The measured fusion excitation function was analyzed using the coupled-channel code CCFULL, including couplings to the inelastic excitation of the \(^{197}\)Au target and the first two excited states of the rotational band built on the ground state of \(^{9}\)Be. Evaporation residue cross sections for \(^{203}\)Bi (3n) and \(^{202}\)Bi (4n) were extracted, and the complete fusion cross section was obtained by correcting for unmeasured channels using the statistical model code PACE2 with an Ignatyuk level density parameter a = A/9 MeV\(^{-1}\).

## Key numbers

- Fusion suppression factor S = 0.61(2), corresponding to approximately 39(2)% suppression of complete fusion at above-barrier energies for \(^{9}\)Be + \(^{197}\)Au.
- Measurements were performed over E\(_{c.m.}\)/V\(_B\) from approximately 0.82 to 1.16, with V\(_B\) = 38.4 MeV for the system.
- Measured complete fusion cross sections range from 0.021 ± 0.004 mb at E\(_{c.m.}\) = 31.6 MeV to 367 ± 7 mb at E\(_{c.m.}\) = 44.7 MeV.
- The deformation parameter \(\beta_2\) = 1.3 for \(^{9}\)Be was used in the CCFULL calculations to describe the data.
- The ratio R of measured xn channels (3n+4n) to complete fusion cross section was 0.96 to 0.99 across the energy range, with maximum correction of approximately 4% at extreme energy points.

## Context

This work extends systematic fusion studies of weakly bound projectiles on \(^{197}\)Au targets, including \(^{6}\)He (reported by Yu et al.), \(^{8}\)He (reported by Lemasson et al.), and \(^{6,7}\)Li (reported by Chakraborty et al.), as well as the tightly bound \(^{11}\)B (reported by Shrivastava et al.) and \(^{4}\)He. The observed 39% suppression is consistent with the 25-40% range reported for \(^{9}\)Be on other heavy targets. The finding that \(^{9}\)Be shows the largest sub-barrier enhancement among Z=2-5 projectiles, even exceeding that of halo nuclei \(^{6,8}\)He, enables further investigation into the interplay between deformation and weak binding in fusion reactions.
