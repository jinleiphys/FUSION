# Simulation of Lindblad equations for quarkonium in the quark-gluon plasma

> We study the properties of the Lindbladian quantum mechanical evolution of quarkonia with non-Abelian charges (color-singlet and octet) in the quark-gluon plasma. We confirm that heavy quark recoils in the Lindblad equation correctly thermalize quarkonium colorful states within statistical errors from the simulation method. We also demonstrate that the Lindblad equation in the dipole limit can provide an efficient alternative method, which is applicable to a finite time evolution before thermalization and dramatically reduces the numerical cost. Our findings will serve as a foundation for large-scale simulation of quarkonium dynamics in the relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that the Lindblad equation for quarkonium with non-Abelian color charges (color-singlet and octet) correctly thermalizes to a Boltzmann distribution within statistical errors, but only when heavy quark recoil terms (proportional to 1/T) are included in the Lindblad operators; without these recoil terms, the system would not reach equilibrium.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the Lindblad master equation for the relative motion of a heavy quark-antiquark pair in the quark-gluon plasma using the Quantum State Diffusion (QSD) method, sampling thousands of random wave functions on a one-dimensional lattice with 255 points. The simulation modeled the color-singlet and octet channels with a screened Coulomb potential U(r) = -α/r exp(-m_D r) and a Gaussian noise kernel γ(r) = γ_0 exp(-r²/ℓ_corr²), with parameters α=0.225, m_D=2T, and γ_0=T/π. Two initial conditions were used: a singlet ground state (IC 1) and a pure octet wave packet (IC 2), and the evolution was tracked until steady state at t ≈ 24τ_eq.

## Key numbers

- Equilibration time τ_eq ≈ 236/M at T=0.1M (for bottomonium, M≈4.8 GeV gives τ_eq ≈ 49 fm/c)
- Decoherence time τ_dec ≈ 191/M at T=0.1M
- Ground state decay rate Γ_0 ≈ 1.71 × 10⁻³ M at T=0.1M
- Best-fit temperatures from Boltzmann distribution: T'/M = 0.0994 (IC 1, T=0.1M), T'/M = 0.1012 (IC 2, T=0.1M), T'/M = 0.2846 (T=0.3M)
- Lattice spacing Δx = 1/M with N_x = 255 points
- Cutoff parameter x_c = 1/M for regularizing the Coulomb singularity
- Wave packet size a = 0.2/√2 × 1/((4/3)Mα) for IC 2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Abelian (electromagnetic) simulation (Miura, 2019) to the non-Abelian QCD case with color-singlet and octet channels, confirming thermalization for the first time in this setting. The Lindblad equation used is derived from weak-coupling NRQCD (Akamatsu, 2014; Akamatsu, 2020) and is related to the pNRQCD-based Lindblad equations (Brambilla, 2016; Brambilla, 2017) that have been applied to phenomenological studies of bottomonium in heavy-ion collisions (Brambilla, 2020; Brambilla, 2021; Brambilla, 2022). The demonstration that the dipole approximation works for timescales relevant to heavy-ion collisions (τ_hic ≈ 250/M) supports the use of computationally cheaper dipole-approximated Lindblad equations for phenomenological simulations.
