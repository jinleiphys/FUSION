# Comprehensive study of compact stars with dark matter

> We present a comprehensive study of compact stars admixed with non-self annihilating self-interacting fermionic dark matter, delineating the dependence on the nuclear equation of state by considering the two limiting parametrized equations of state for neutron star matter obtained by smoothly matching the low-density chiral effective theory and the high-density perturbative QCD. These two parametrizations are the limiting cases of a wide variety of smooth equations of state, i.e. the softest and stiffest possible one without a phase transition, that generate masses and radii compatible with 2M$_\odot$ observations and the tidal constraint from GW170817. With an exhaustive analysis of the possible stable mass-radius configurations, we determine the quantity of dark matter contained in stars with masses and radii compatible with the aforementioned astrophysical constraints. We find that for dark particle masses of a few tenths of GeV, the dark core collapses and no stable solutions are found for the two limiting ordinary matter equations of state. For lower masses, the dark matter fraction is limited to 10%, being at most 1% for masses ranging from 0.1 to 10 GeV for the limiting soft nuclear equation of state. For the limiting stiff nuclear equation of state, the dark matter fraction can reach values of more than 10%, but the dark particle mass is being constrained to 0.3 GeV and 10 GeV for the weak self-interacting case and has to be at least 5 GeV for the strong self-interacting one. For dark particle masses of less than 0.1 GeV, stable neutron star configurations should have less than 1% of self-interacting dark matter to be compatible with the constraint of the tidal deformability from GW170817 for the two limiting ordinary matter equations of state studied.

## Key claim
For non-self-annihilating self-interacting fermionic dark matter, the maximum allowed dark matter fraction in neutron stars depends strongly on both the dark particle mass and the stiffness of the nuclear equation of state, with stable configurations only existing when the dark matter core does not collapse; the abstract's claim of "dark matter fraction limited to 10%" applies only under specific conditions of particle mass and equation of state choice.

## Method
The authors solved the two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations for ordinary matter (OM) and dark matter (DM) interacting only gravitationally, using two limiting parametrized equations of state for OM (EoSI and EoSII) from Kurkela (2014) that represent the softest and stiffest possible smooth equations of state without phase transitions. DM was modeled as a non-self-annihilating self-interacting Fermi gas with varying particle mass and interaction strength. Stability of mass-radius configurations was determined using the generalized criterion from Hippert (2022), which considers the coupled stability of both fluids via the eigenvalues of the particle-number variation matrix.

## Key numbers
- For dark particle masses of a few tenths of GeV, the dark core collapses and no stable solutions exist for either limiting OM equation of state.
- For the limiting soft nuclear equation of state (EoSI), the DM fraction is at most 1% for DM particle masses ranging from 0.1 to 10 GeV.
- For the limiting stiff nuclear equation of state (EoSII), the DM fraction can exceed 10%, but the DM particle mass is constrained to 0.3 GeV and 10 GeV for weak self-interaction, and must be at least 5 GeV for strong self-interaction.
- For DM particle masses less than 0.1 GeV, stable neutron star configurations must have less than 1% self-interacting DM to satisfy the GW170817 tidal deformability constraint (Lambda_1.4 < 720) for both limiting OM equations of state.
- For a 1.4 solar mass star with EoSI and DM interaction strength y_int=0.1, DM particle masses of 0.2 GeV and 0.4 GeV increase tidal deformability above the GW170817 upper limit of Lambda_1.4=720, while masses of 0.6 GeV and above keep Lambda_1.4 below 720.
- For EoSII without DM, the tidal deformability is Lambda_1.4 approximately 738, slightly above the GW170817 limit of 720.

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Tolos (2015) and Dengler (2021) by using two limiting parametrized equations of state from Kurkela (2014) that represent a wide range of possible nuclear matter behaviors, rather than a single specific model. It also improves upon the stability analysis of those earlier works by applying the coupled two-fluid stability criterion from Hippert (2022), which accounts for how one fluid's stability affects the other. The results enable future work to constrain dark matter properties by comparing predicted mass-radius and tidal deformability signatures with observations from NICER and gravitational-wave events like GW170817, and provide a framework for testing whether specific dark matter models are compatible with existing neutron star data.
