# Application of nonextensive statistics to particle and nuclear physics

> We present an overview of possible imprints of non-extensitivity in particle and nucler physics. Special emphasis is put on the intrinsic fluctuations present in the system under consideration as the possible source of nonextensivity. The possible connection of nonextensivity and the self organized criticality apparently being observed in some cosmic rays and hadronic experiments will also be discussed.

# Application of nonextensive statistics to particle and nuclear physics

## Key claim
Deviations from exponential distributions observed in cosmic ray data and multiparticle production can be explained by intrinsic temperature fluctuations within the system, which manifest as nonextensivity in Tsallis statistics; the nonextensivity parameter \(q\) is directly related to the relative variance of these fluctuations, \(\omega = q-1\), rather than being an ad-hoc fitting parameter.

## Method
The authors review cosmic ray cascade data (depth distributions from the Pamir lead chamber) and transverse momentum spectra from heavy ion collisions, fitting them to Tsallis-type power-law distributions \(\exp_q(-x/\lambda_0)\) instead of simple exponentials. They derive a formal connection between the nonextensivity parameter \(q\) and the relative variance \(\omega\) of fluctuating quantities (such as mean free path \(\lambda\) or temperature \(T\)), showing that \(q = 1 \pm \omega\) for \(q>1\) and \(q<1\) respectively. They also extend the analysis to nonexponential decays via random matrix theory and to self-organized criticality in cascade processes, including jet production in hadronic collisions.

## Key numbers
- In cosmic ray data from the Pamir lead chamber, the relative variance of the mean free path is \(\omega \geq 0.2\), corresponding to a fitted \(q = 1.3\) when using a Lévy-type formula.
- For transverse momentum distributions in heavy ion collisions, the deviation from Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics is very small: \(|q-1| \sim 0.015\), which nevertheless implies large relative temperature fluctuations \(\Delta T/T \simeq 0.12\).
- The total heat capacity \(C\) of the system is related to fluctuations by \(\sigma^2(\beta)/\langle\beta\rangle^2 = 1/C = \omega = q-1\).
- For multichannel decays with \(\nu\) channels, the nonextensivity parameter is \(q = 1 + 2/\nu\).
- In jet cross sections at \(\sqrt{s}=546\) GeV and 1800 GeV, the power-law exponent is \(\alpha = 5.01\) and 4.9 respectively, with smearing dispersion \(\delta = 0.74\) and 0.79.
- For atmospheric cascade energy spectra, the limiting value of \(q\) for large cascade generations (\(N \geq 6\)) is \(q \rightarrow 3/2\), giving \(\alpha \rightarrow 2\).
- In \(p-\bar{p}\) collisions, the mean multiplicity of neutral mesons and transverse mass spectra of pions follow a power law \(C \cdot x^{-P}\) with \(P \simeq 10.1\) and \(P \simeq 9.6\) respectively, corresponding to \(q = 1 + 1/P \sim 1.1\).

## Context
This review extends the authors' earlier work (Wilk and Wlodarczyk, 2000) connecting fluctuations to nonextensivity, and builds on Tsallis (1988) nonextensive entropy formalism. It contrasts with the standard Boltzmann-Gibbs approach and with alternative explanations such as collective flow in heavy ion collisions. The work enables experimental tests to distinguish between event-by-event temperature fluctuations (from different initial conditions) and intra-event temperature fluctuations (from intrinsic dynamics), which would provide direct measurement of the heat capacity of the produced hadronic system and potentially reveal phase transitions in Quark-Gluon Plasma formation. The analysis also unifies descriptions of jet production (Meng et al., 1996; JMRT) and cosmic ray cascades (RWW) under a common nonextensive framework.
