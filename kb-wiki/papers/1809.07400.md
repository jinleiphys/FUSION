# Bulk observables in the LHC 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions within the integrated HydroKinetic Model

> The paper is devoted to the description and prediction of various bulk observables in the Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV within the integrated hydrokinetic model (iHKM). Sensitivity of the results to the choice of the appropriate model parameter values is also investigated. It is found that changing of the relaxation time and the rate of thermalization, which characterize the pre-thermal stage of the matter evolution, as well as switching to another equation of state at the hydrodynamic stage and the corresponding hadronization temperature, does not destroy the results, if simultaneously one provides an appropriate adjusting of the initial time for the superdense matter formation and related maximal initial energy density.

# Bulk observables in the LHC 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions within the integrated HydroKinetic Model

## Key claim

The integrated hydrokinetic model (iHKM) can describe and predict bulk observables in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, but the model parameters (thermalization time, relaxation time, equation of state, and particlization temperature) can be varied over a range without destroying agreement with data, provided the maximal initial energy density and initial time are simultaneously re-adjusted. This means that these individual characteristics of matter evolution cannot be uniquely extracted from experimental results.

## Method

The iHKM simulates heavy-ion collisions through successive stages: formation of superdense matter, gradual thermalization described by relaxation time tau_rel and thermalization time tau_th, viscous relativistic hydrodynamic expansion using the Israel-Stewart formalism, particlization at temperature T_p, and a hadronic cascade stage simulated with the UrQMD model. The authors compare results for two different particlization temperatures (165 MeV with the Laine-Schroeder equation of state and 156 MeV with the HotQCD Collaboration equation of state) and vary tau_th between 1.0 and 1.5 fm/c and tau_rel between 0.25 and 0.6 fm/c, adjusting the maximal initial energy density epsilon_0(tau_0) to maintain agreement with experimental data on charged particle multiplicity and pion spectra.

## Key numbers

- Maximal initial energy density for T_p=165 MeV: epsilon_0=1067 GeV/fm^3 at initial time tau_0=0.1 fm/c
- Maximal initial energy density for T_p=156 MeV: epsilon_0=870 GeV/fm^3 at initial time tau_0=0.12 fm/c
- Parameter alpha (proportion between binary collisions and wounded nucleons): alpha=0.24, same as at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV
- Thermalization time tau_th=1.0 fm/c and relaxation time tau_rel=0.25 fm/c used for 5.02 TeV predictions
- For the 2.76 TeV comparison: epsilon_0=834 GeV/fm^3 for tau_th=1.0 fm/c and epsilon_0=681 GeV/fm^3 for tau_th=1.5 fm/c
- For the 2.76 TeV relaxation time comparison: epsilon_0=630 GeV/fm^3 for tau_rel=0.6 fm/c
- The K* yield in iHKM at 5.02 TeV is approximately twice the preliminary experimental value
- Up to 70% of direct K* resonances are lost due to scattering of daughter kaons and pions, while up to 50% of the initial number of direct K*'s can be recovered through recombination of K-pi pairs

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous iHKM studies at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV (Shapoval and Sinyukov, 2015-2017) to the higher LHC energy of 5.02 TeV, confirming that the same model parameters (alpha, Lambda, tau_th, tau_rel) that worked at the lower energy also work at the higher energy after adjusting epsilon_0 and tau_0. The results confirm the earlier observation from the authors' 2017 paper on particle number ratios that different particlization temperatures with corresponding equations of state can give equally good descriptions if epsilon_0 is re-adjusted. The paper enables predictions for pion and kaon interferometry radii and elliptic flow at 5.02 TeV that can be tested against future final ALICE data, and it highlights the need for further investigation of the K* yield discrepancy at this energy.
