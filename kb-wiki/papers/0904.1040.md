# Locating critical point of QCD phase transition basing on finite-size scaling

> It is argued that in relativistic heavy ion collisions, due to limited size of the formed matter, the reliable criterion of critical point is finite-size scaling, rather than non-monotonous behavior of observable. How to locate critical point by finite-size scaling is proposed. The data of $\pt$ correlation from RHIC/STAR are analyzed. Critical points are likely observed around $\sqrt s =62$ and 200 GeV. They could be, respectively, the transition of deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration predicted by lattice-QCD. Further confirmation with other observable and energies is suggested.

## Key claim

The authors argue that in relativistic heavy ion collisions, the finite size of the formed matter makes finite-size scaling a more reliable criterion for identifying a QCD critical point than the commonly sought non-monotonous behavior of observables; they claim to have likely observed two critical points around sqrt(s) = 62 and 200 GeV, but hedge that these could respectively correspond to the deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration transitions predicted by lattice-QCD, and that further confirmation with other observables and energies is needed.

## Method

The authors apply finite-size scaling analysis to published data on transverse momentum (pt) correlation from Au+Au collisions at RHIC/STAR at four incident energies (20, 62, 130, and 200 GeV) and six centralities. They treat collision energy as a proxy for temperature and define the relative system size L as sqrt(N_part)/sqrt(2N_A), where N_part is the number of participant nucleons. They search for a "fixed point" where the scaled observable P(sqrt(s),L) * L^{-a} becomes independent of system size L, and also examine the log-log linearity of ln P versus ln L as a cross-check.

## Key numbers

* Two fixed points were found: one at sqrt(s) = 62 GeV with exponent -a_{0,1} = 2.09, and one at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV with exponent -a_{0,2} = 2.08.
* The parabola fit parameter |c_2| (measuring deviation from straight-line behavior in ln P vs ln L) was 0.6 +/- 0.09 at sqrt(s) = 62 GeV and 0.77 +/- 0.1 at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, compared to 1.86 +/- 0.93 at 20 GeV and 1.56 +/- 0.41 at 130 GeV.
* The ratio |c_1/c_2| was 4.32 +/- 0.67 at 62 GeV and 2.74 +/- 0.1 at 200 GeV, versus 2.1 +/- 1.16 at 20 GeV and 2.20 +/- 0.63 at 130 GeV.
* The critical exponents of the normalized pt correlation (about 1.1) were smaller than those of the unnormalized pt correlation.

## Context

This work extends the finite-size scaling formalism developed by Fisher (1971), Brezin (1982), and Chen, Dohm, and Talapov (1996) to the analysis of heavy-ion collision data, arguing that the non-monotonous behavior approach (Stephanov, Rajagopal, and Shuryak, 1998-1999) has not yielded clear signals in data from RHIC and SPS. The finding of two critical points is interpreted as possibly corresponding to deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration transitions, which lattice-QCD (Aoki et al., 2006; Karsch, 2002) suggests may occur at different temperatures. The similar ratios of critical exponents at the two points are noted to be consistent with the 3-dimensional Ising model universality class (Garcia and Gonzalo, 2003) and the 3-dimensional O(4) model (Braun and Klein, 2008). The authors call for additional data at other observables and at intermediate collision energies to confirm the results.
