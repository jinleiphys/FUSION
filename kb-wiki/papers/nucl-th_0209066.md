# Meson mixing amplitudes in asymmetric nuclear matter

> Using a purely hadronic model, we study the charge-symmetry-breaking $ρ$-$ω$, $σ$-$δ$, $σ$-$ρ$ and $δ$-$ω$ mixing amplitudes in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. The basic assumption of the model is that the mixing amplitude is generated by nucleon and anti-nucleon loops and hence driven entirely by the difference between proton and neutron Fermi momenta and the proton-neutron mass difference. We find that the behavior of the mixing amplitude is very complicated in the spacelike region and quite sensitive to the proton fraction of nuclear medium and nuclear density. In particular, in neutron rich nuclei (like Pb) and/or neutron stars the mixing amplitudes become about 10 $\sim$ 100 times as large as those in typical mirror nuclei.

## Key claim

Using a purely hadronic model (Quantum Hadrodynamics), the authors find that charge-symmetry-breaking meson mixing amplitudes in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter are highly sensitive to the proton fraction and nuclear density, becoming about 10 to 100 times larger in neutron-rich nuclei (like Pb) and neutron stars than in typical mirror nuclei, but only under the assumption that the mixing is generated entirely by nucleon and anti-nucleon loops driven by the difference between proton and neutron Fermi momenta and the proton-neutron mass difference.

## Method

The authors employ Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) within the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) to calculate the polarization functions for ρ-ω, σ-δ, σ-ρ, and δ-ω mixing in asymmetric nuclear matter. The mixing amplitudes are computed from nucleon and anti-nucleon loop diagrams, with the difference between proton and neutron contributions giving the charge-symmetry-breaking effect; a monopole form factor with cutoff Λ = 1.5 GeV is introduced at each vertex in the spacelike region, and the coupling constants are fixed by fitting symmetric nuclear matter saturation properties, the empirical symmetry energy, and the measured ρ-ω mixing amplitude at the ω pole.

## Key numbers

- Saturation energy for symmetric nuclear matter: −15.75 MeV at saturation density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³
- Effective proton mass at ρ₀: M_p* = 679.36 MeV; effective neutron mass at ρ₀: M_n* = 680.65 MeV
- Nuclear incompressibility: K = 460 MeV
- Coupling constants: g_σ = 8.360, g_δ = 3.257, g_ω = 9.493, g_ρ = 3.455
- ρ-ω mixing amplitude at the ω pole used for calibration: ⟨ρ|H_em|ω⟩ = −4520 MeV²
- In neutron stars (ρ_B ≈ 3ρ₀, f_p ≈ 0.15), the ρ-ω mixing amplitude in the longitudinal mode and the σ-ρ mixing amplitude become about 100 times larger than those for f_p = 0.49
- In Pb (ρ_B = 0.15 fm⁻³, f_p = 0.4), the mixing amplitudes are about 5 to 10 times as large as those in matter with f_p = 0.49
- At high ρ_B and small f_p, the ratio of the ρ-ω mixing amplitude at the ω pole in asymmetric matter to that in vacuum becomes 30 to 40

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the momentum dependence of ρ-ω mixing (e.g., the off-shell analysis by Piekarewicz and Williams in the 1990s and the QCD sum-rule calculation by Hatsuda et al.) by examining the mixing amplitudes in the spacelike region as functions of nuclear density and proton fraction, which had not been done previously. The results are relevant to understanding the Okamoto-Nolen-Schiffer (ONS) anomaly in nuclei, the structure of heavy neutron-rich nuclei like Pb, and neutron star properties; however, the authors note that a complete determination of the charge-symmetry-breaking nuclear force also requires knowledge of the CSB meson-nucleon vertex, as emphasized by Cohen and Miller.
