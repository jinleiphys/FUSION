# T(r)opical Dyson-Schwinger Equations

> We provide a glimpse of recent progress in hadron physics made using QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations, reviewing: the notion of in-hadron condensates and a putative solution of a gross problem with the cosmological constant; the dynamical generation of quark anomalous chromo- and electro-magnetic moments, and their material impact upon the proton's electric/magnetic form factor ratio; a computation that simultaneously correlates the masses of meson and baryon ground- and excited-states; and a prediction for the x->1 value of the ratio of neutron/proton distribution functions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in-hadron condensates, rather than spacetime-independent vacuum condensates, are the correct interpretation in QCD due to confinement, and that this reinterpretation reduces the mismatch between the theoretical and observed cosmological constant by a factor of \(10^{46}\) or more.

## Method

The authors review recent progress in hadron physics using QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs), focusing on the dressed-quark propagator (gap equation) and a novel form of the Bethe-Salpeter equation that incorporates a fully dressed quark-gluon vertex. They also employ a symmetry-preserving regularisation of a vector times vector contact interaction to compute the light hadron spectrum, correlating meson and baryon ground- and excited-state masses within a single framework.

## Key numbers

* The mismatch between theory and observation for the cosmological constant is reduced by a factor of \(10^{46}\) when condensates are understood as in-hadron properties rather than spacetime-independent vacuum configurations.
* Dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) is responsible for 98% of a proton's mass.
* The computation of the light hadron spectrum produces a root-mean-square-relative-error per degree of freedom of \(\overline{\mbox{rms}}=13\%\).
* The ratio of neutron-to-proton structure functions at \(x=1\) is predicted to be \(F_2^n/F_2^p = 0.36 \pm 0.03\).
* In the nucleon's rest frame, only 37% of the total nucleon spin is contained within components of the Faddeev amplitude that possess zero quark orbital angular momentum.
* The dressed-quark anomalous magnetic moment is characterised by a \(Q^2=0\) strength \(\eta_{\rm em}=0.4\) in the calculation of the proton electric-to-magnetic form factor ratio.

## Context

This work extends the DSE framework, building on prior studies of the hadron spectrum (Holl 2005) and hadron form factors (Cloet 2008, Aznauryan 2009), and directly addresses the cosmological constant problem raised by Brodsky (2010). The results enable a unified description of the hadron spectrum, elastic and transition form factors, and parton distribution functions, and provide a foundation for understanding confinement and the origin of nuclear mass through DCSB.
