# Solitonic excitations in collisions of superfluid nuclei: a qualitatively new phenomenon distinct from the Josephson effect

> Recently, we have reported a novel role of pairing in low-energy heavy ion reactions at energies above the Coulomb barrier, which may have a detectable impact on reaction outcomes, such as the kinetic energy of fragments and the fusion cross section [arXiv:1611.10261, arXiv:1702.00069]. The phenomenon mimics the one studied experimentally with ultracold atomic gases, where two clouds of fermionic superfluids with different phases of the pairing fields are forced to merge, inducing various excitation modes of the pairing field. Although it originates from the phase difference of the pairing fields, the physics behind it is markedly different from the so-called Josephson effect. In this short contribution, we will briefly outline the results discussed in our recent papers and explain relations with the field of ultracold atomic gases.

# Solitonic excitations in collisions of superfluid nuclei: a qualitatively new phenomenon distinct from the Josephson effect

## Key claim
The authors report that collisions of superfluid nuclei at energies above the Coulomb barrier can produce a solitonic excitation (domain wall) in the pairing field that hinders energy dissipation and neck formation, leading to detectable changes in fragment kinetic energy and fusion probability. This effect originates from the relative phase difference of the pairing fields but is markedly different from the Josephson effect, as it occurs in the strong-coupling limit where condensates actually merge rather than weakly couple through a barrier.

## Method
The authors performed time-dependent superfluid local density approximation (TDSLDA) simulations for collisions of superfluid nuclei, including ⁴⁴Ca+⁴⁴Ca, ⁹⁰Zr+⁹⁰Zr, ⁸⁶Zr+¹²⁶Sn, and ²⁴⁰Pu+²⁴⁰Pu at various incident energies above the Coulomb barrier, using the FaNDF⁰ nuclear functional without spin-orbit coupling. They compared cases with relative phase difference Δφ=π (opposite phases) and Δφ=0 (same phase) of the pairing fields of the colliding nuclei.

## Key numbers
- Total kinetic energy (TKE) of outgoing fragments can be changed as large as 25 MeV in ²⁴⁰Pu+²⁴⁰Pu collisions.
- Fusion threshold energy can be affected almost 30 MeV in ⁹⁰Zr+⁹⁰Zr collisions.
- The ⁹⁰Zr+⁹⁰Zr reaction was simulated at E≃1.05V_Bass, where V_Bass is the phenomenological fusion barrier.
- Pairing effects were small in the lighter system ⁴⁴Ca+⁴⁴Ca due to strong tendency towards fusion.
- In ultracold atomic gas experiments, the soliton lives about 10 ms and decays into a vortex ring.

## Context
This work extends the application of TDSLDA, previously developed for ultracold atomic gases (e.g., Bulgac et al., various years) and nuclear systems (e.g., PRC 2011, PRL 2015, PRL 2016), to low-energy heavy ion reactions. It contradicts earlier expectations of a nuclear Josephson effect (Dietrich et al., 1970s; Sorensen, 1980s) which focused on sub-barrier multinucleon transfer via weak coupling through a barrier. The phenomenon is analogous to experiments with ultracold atomic gases at MIT (Ku et al., 2016) where merging two clouds with different phases produces solitonic excitations. The results enable experimental searches for detectable signatures of pairing dynamics in carefully selected nuclear systems, such as changes in fragment kinetic energy and fusion cross section.
