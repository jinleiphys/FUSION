# Proton-to-Alpha Branching Ratio in the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C fusion reaction at Astrophysical Energies

> The unique resonance features in the $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C fusion reaction lead to significant fluctuations in the branching ratio $R_{p/α}=σ_p/σ_α$, making it difficult to determine the $R_{p/α}$ at astrophysical energies. By combining Hauser--Feshbach statistical-model calculations with constraints from direct charged-particle and gamma-ray measurements, we investigate the energy dependence of the averaged $R_{p/α}$ and predict its behavior within the Gamow window. Owing to the strong energy dependence of $R_{p/α}$, the corresponding reaction-rate ratios, $\langle σv \rangle_p / \langle σv \rangle_α$, during core and shell carbon burning are determined to be 0.29, 0.45, and 0.52 at $T_9 = 0.5$, 1.0, and 1.2, respectively, significantly lower than the widely adopted CF88 constant value of 0.78. The implications of the revised $\langle σv \rangle_p / \langle σv \rangle_α$ ratio for stellar nucleosynthesis and white-dwarf evolution are also discussed.

## Key claim

By combining Hauser-Feshbach statistical-model calculations with constraints from direct charged-particle and gamma-ray measurements, the authors predict that the averaged proton-to-alpha branching ratio in the 12C+12C fusion reaction decreases significantly within the Gamow window, yielding reaction-rate ratios substantially lower than the widely adopted CF88 constant value of 0.78.

## Method

The authors normalized TALYS-based Hauser-Feshbach calculations to experimental cross-section ratios corrected for missing reaction channels using the prescription of Li et al. (2020). They employed a Monte Carlo resampling technique and Log-Normal distribution fitting to determine an optimal scaling factor f that adjusts the TALYS prediction to match the averaged experimental trend, using data from charged-particle measurements (Becker et al., Patterson et al.) and gamma-ray spectroscopy (Aguilera et al., Kettner et al., High and Cujec) within the energy window E_cm = 3.5–6.4 MeV. The calibrated model was then extrapolated to astrophysical energies within the Gamow window (E_cm ~ 1–3 MeV) to predict the energy-dependent branching ratio and corresponding reaction-rate ratios.

## Key numbers

- Global optimal scaling factor f = 0.698, with uncertainty band defined by e^{-δ} = 0.708 (lower) and e^{+δ} = 1.412 (upper), obtained from combined fit of charged-particle and gamma-ray datasets in the energy range E_cm = 3.5–6.4 MeV
- Reaction-rate ratios ⟨σv⟩_p / ⟨σv⟩_α determined to be 0.29 at T_9 = 0.5, 0.45 at T_9 = 1.0, and 0.52 at T_9 = 1.2, compared to the CF88 constant value of 0.78
- For charged-particle data alone (Becker et al., Patterson et al.) within E_cm = 3.5–6.4 MeV: scaling factor f = 0.690, δ = 0.265, with uncertainty bounds 0.767 (lower) and 1.303 (upper)
- For gamma-ray data alone (Aguilera et al., Kettner et al., High and Cujec) within E_cm = 3.7–6.4 MeV: scaling factor f = 0.715, with uncertainty bounds 0.689 (lower) and 1.452 (upper)
- Q-value for 12C(12C,α)20Ne reaction is 4.62 MeV, substantially larger than the 2.24 MeV for 12C(12C,p)23Na reaction

## Context

This work extends the statistical-model analysis of Li et al. (2020), who used TALYS to estimate branching ratios for unobserved channels in the 12C+12C fusion reaction and reconciled discrepancies among existing data sets. The results contradict the constant branching ratio assumption of the CF88 compilation by Caughlan and Fowler (1988), which assumed ⟨σv⟩_p / ⟨σv⟩_α ≈ 0.78 over most astrophysical temperatures. The predicted temperature-dependent ratios enable more reliable predictions of the 23Na/20Ne abundance ratio in massive stars, as studied by Pignatari et al. (2012), and have implications for white dwarf models as demonstrated by De Gerónimo et al. (2024), who showed that the branching ratio uncertainty affects the final chemical structure of super-asymptotic giant branch progenitors by up to ~17% in central 20Ne abundance.
