# Color Glass Condensate in Schwinger-Keldysh QCD

> Within the Schwinger-Keldysh representation of many-body QCD, it is shown that the leading quantum corrections to the strong classical color field is classical in the sense that the fluctuation field still obeys the classical Jacobi-field equation, while the quantum effects solely resides in the fluctuations of the initial field configurations. Within this context, a systematic derivation of the JIMWLK renormalization group equation is presented. A clear identification of the correct form of gauge transformation rules and the correct form of the matter-field Lagrangian in the Schwinger-Keldysh QCD is also presented.

## Key claim

Within the Schwinger-Keldysh representation of many-body QCD, the leading quantum corrections to the strong classical color field are shown to be classical in the sense that the fluctuation field still obeys the classical Jacobi-field equation, while quantum effects reside solely in the fluctuations of the initial field configurations; this holds at leading order because the difference-difference propagator vanishes identically, making the triple-eta terms contribute only at higher orders.

## Method

The author reformulates QCD using the Schwinger-Keldysh closed-time-path formalism in the Keldysh (r-a) representation, separating the gluon field into a common part A and a difference part eta. The classical Yang-Mills equation emerges naturally from the term linear in eta, while the triple-eta term provides quantum corrections. The strong classical field is separated from the fluctuation field, and power counting is performed with the strong source scaling as O(1/g) and the classical field as O(1/g). A systematic derivation of the JIMWLK renormalization group equation is presented using this formalism, along with a clear identification of the correct gauge transformation rules and the correct form of the matter-field Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- The strong external color current is O(1/g) and the classical Yang-Mills field is O(1/g), where g is the coupling constant with g << 1.
- The covariant derivative containing only the A field is O(1).
- Interaction terms involving 3 fields in both the classical and quantum parts of the Lagrangian are O(g), and interaction terms involving 4 fields are O(g^2).
- The triple-eta vertex contributes only at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) and beyond, since the difference-difference propagator vanishes identically.

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework originally developed by McLerran, Venugopalan, Ayala, and Jalilian-Marian (1993-1995) by providing a systematic derivation of the JIMWLK renormalization group equation within the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism. It builds upon the earlier introduction of the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism for CGC by Iancu, McLerran, and Leonidov (2000) and the subsequent diagrammatic approach developed by Gelis, Venugopalan, and collaborators (2006-2012). The paper resolves an old ambiguity in the CGC formulation regarding the gauge-invariant coupling between the retarded current and the gauge field, showing that neither the Tr J+ W nor the Tr J+ ln W forms are correct, and that the Schwinger-Keldysh QCD automatically yields the correct coupling. This work sets the stage for more elaborate NNLO calculations.
