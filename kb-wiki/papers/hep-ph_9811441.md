# Gauge Lattice Simulation of the soft QGP Dynamics in Ultra-Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> In a fully relativistic approach, a RLSM description of nuclei colliding at ultra-relativistic energies can be formulated within the framework of a classical transport theory. The valence quarks of the nucleons are described through collections of classical point-like particles moving in the continuum. They are coupled to soft gluon fields which are described through the Yang Mills equations on a gauge lattice. In a first step, we focus on the range of low-$p_t$ interactions. Results from numerical model simulations of pure gluonic nucleus-nucleus collisions on SU(2) gauge lattices in 3+1 dimensions are presented. They show an effect which we call the glue burst.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in pure gluonic collisions on an SU(2) gauge lattice, non-linear Yang-Mills dynamics can convert longitudinal energy flow into transverse energy flow after a characteristic time delay, an effect they call the "glue burst." This claim is hedged by the fact that the simulations use SU(2) rather than SU(3) gauge group, plane-wave initial conditions, and no valence quarks, so the result applies only to the soft gluon mean-field sector in a simplified model.

## Method

The authors simulate collisions of Gaussian wave packets of SU(2) gauge fields on a 3+1 dimensional lattice using the classical Yang-Mills equations in temporal gauge. They initialize two wave packets with opposite longitudinal momenta and different color polarizations, then evolve the link variables and electric fields via discretized equations of motion. The transverse and longitudinal energy densities and Poynting vector components are computed as functions of time and longitudinal position to track energy flow.

## Key numbers

- Lattice size: 10x10x2000 points for plane-wave collisions, with lattice spacing \(a_l = 0.1\,{\rm fm}\) and time step \(\Delta t = 0.01\,{\rm fm}\).
- Wave packet parameters: average momentum \(\overline k_3 = \pi/(2a_3)\), width \(\Delta k_3 = \pi/(100 a_3)\), coupling \(g=1\), and \(\sigma = 1\).
- Longitudinal energy density \(w_L^{(E)}\) grows exponentially by more than two orders of magnitude from time step \(t_{1000}\) to \(t_{1700}\) in the overlap region.
- By time step \(t_{10000}\), the longitudinal energy density has increased by five orders of magnitude relative to its initial value.
- Transverse energy flow density grows by three orders of magnitude from time step \(t_{6000}\) to \(t_{10000}\).
- The time delay before the glue burst scales with \(g^{-2}\).
- For finite transverse extent simulations: lattice 4x200x800, coupling \(g=6.0\), transverse width \(8\,{\rm fm}\) in y-direction with \(\Delta k_y = 1/(40a_2)\) and \(\lambda=8\).

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan (1994) idea of expanding around mean color fields rather than the empty QCD vacuum, and the parton cascade model of Geiger (1992). It is a first step toward combining a parton cascade with a mean-field description of color fields, as proposed in the authors' earlier work (BMP.98). The results enable future studies of how non-perturbative gluon mean-field dynamics contribute to transverse momentum generation in the early stages of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly for the soft (low-\(p_t\)) sector relevant to RHIC and LHC physics.
