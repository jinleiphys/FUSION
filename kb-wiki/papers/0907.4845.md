# Shock Treatment: Heavy Quark Energy Loss in a Novel AdS/CFT Geometry

> We first note the failures of traditional pQCD techniques as applied to high-pT heavy ion physics and the suggestion of examining the double ratio of charm to bottom nuclear modification factors to generically distinguish between these weak coupling ideas and the strong coupling ideas of AdS/CFT. In order to gain confidence in the use of AdS/CFT (and to increase the likelihood of falsifying it and/or pQCD) we extend its application to heavy quark energy loss in both thermal and nonthermal media by calculating the string drag in a shock metric.

## Key claim

The paper extends AdS/CFT calculations of heavy quark energy loss to nonthermal media by computing the string drag in a shock metric, finding that the energy loss form is independent of whether the medium is thermal or nonthermal; however, the result depends on the typical transverse momentum of the shock medium particles, and only when that scale is related to a temperature does it exactly reproduce the previous AdS/Schwarzschild results.

## Method

The author models a heavy quark in the fundamental representation as an open Nambu-Goto string terminating on a D7 brane at a finite radial coordinate in an asymptotic AdS$_5$ space, with the spacetime metric taken as that of a shock wave. By assuming a static solution ansatz for the string embedding in the shock metric and solving the equations of motion derived from the Nambu-Goto action, the drag force on the heavy quark is extracted from the momentum flow down the string. The parameter $\mu$ in the shock metric is related to the typical momentum $\Lambda$ of the medium particles by boosting the stress-energy tensor between the medium rest frame and the heavy quark rest frame.

## Key numbers

- Drag force in the heavy quark rest frame: $dp/dt = \sqrt{\lambda \mu} / (2\pi)$, where $\lambda$ is the 't Hooft coupling and $\mu$ is the shock metric parameter.
- Energy loss in the medium rest frame: $dp'/dt' = -(\sqrt{\lambda}/2) (\Lambda^2 / M_q) p'$, where $\Lambda$ is the typical momentum of medium particles and $M_q$ is the heavy quark mass.
- When the typical medium particle momentum is set to $\Lambda = \sqrt{\pi} T$, the result reproduces the black hole metric result: $dp'/dt' = -\pi \sqrt{\lambda} T^2 p' / (2 M_q)$.
- Speed limit from the static quark solution: $\gamma \le (4 M_q^2) / (\lambda T^2)$, identical to the limit from the black hole metric.

## Context

This work extends previous AdS/CFT calculations of heavy quark energy loss in a vacuum metric by Mikhailov (2003) and in a black hole metric by Gubser (2006) to the case of a shock metric, thereby generalizing the calculation to media with any isotropic distribution of momentum. The author notes that traditional pQCD techniques do not provide a quantitatively consistent picture of all known RHIC data, and that examining the double ratio of charm to bottom nuclear modification factors has been suggested as a way to falsify either pQCD or AdS/CFT approaches. This work enables more rigorous theoretical error estimates for AdS/CFT predictions, which are needed to test both formalisms against upcoming experimental heavy ion data.
