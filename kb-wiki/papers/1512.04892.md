# Using Wilson flow to study the SU(3) deconfinement transition

> We explore the use of Wilson flow to study the deconfinement transition in SU(3) gauge theory. We use the flowed Polyakov loop as a renormalized order parameter for the transition, and use it to renormalize the Polyakov loop. We also study the flow properties of the electric and magnetic gluon condensates, and demonstrate that the difference of the flowed operators shows rapid change across the transition point.

# Using Wilson flow to study the SU(3) deconfinement transition

## Key claim
Wilson flow can be used to construct a renormalized order parameter for the SU(3) deconfinement transition via the flowed Polyakov loop, but the perturbative renormalization using leading-order perturbation theory shows remnant dependence on flow time, particularly near the transition temperature, necessitating a nonperturbative matching approach for temperatures close to T_c.

## Method
The authors performed lattice simulations of SU(3) pure gauge theory at finite temperature using the Wilson action, with lattice spacings corresponding to N_t = 6, 8, and 10. They applied Wilson flow to smear gauge links to a physical scale sqrt(t), then constructed flowed Polyakov loops and electric/magnetic gluon condensate operators from the flowed configurations. The flowed Polyakov loop was used as a renormalized order parameter, and the renormalized Polyakov loop was extracted both through leading-order perturbative renormalization and through nonperturbative matching using a baseline value at 3 T_c.

## Key numbers
- The critical temperature satisfies T_c * sqrt(t_0) ≈ 0.25 in pure SU(3) gauge theory, where t_0 is defined by ⟨E(t)⟩ = 0.3.
- The Lambda parameter Λ_{MSbar}^{n_f=0} = 1.20(2) T_c, determined using two-loop perturbation theory from plaquette values.
- The renormalized Polyakov loop baseline value P_ren(3 T_c) = 1.0169(1) from prior work (Renpol1).
- The flowed Polyakov loop shows removal of lattice spacing dependence at flow times sqrt(t) T ≃ 0.05, with further suppression of finite-a corrections at sqrt(t) T ≃ 0.16.
- The renormalized gluon condensate from tau lepton decay analysis is quoted as 0.02–0.01 GeV^4, while a recent determination gives 0.009 ± 0.007 GeV^4.
- For SU(3) gauge theory, a recent lattice determination quotes ⟨G^2⟩ = 24.2 ± 8.0 Λ_{MSbar}^4.

## Context
This work extends the use of Wilson flow, originally developed for scale setting in lattice QCD (Luscher 2010, BMW collaboration), to finite-temperature studies of the deconfinement transition. It builds on prior approaches to renormalizing the Polyakov loop (Renpol, Renpol1) and on calculations of the renormalized Polyakov loop in perturbation theory (Mikko). The study of electric and magnetic gluon condensates connects to finite-temperature sum rule calculations (Lee) and to earlier lattice determinations of the gluon condensate from plaquette operators (Su3thermo). The flowed Polyakov loop approach was also considered by Petreczky, though with a different renormalization strategy. The results enable a simpler nonperturbative renormalization scheme for the Polyakov loop and demonstrate that the difference of flowed electric and magnetic condensates provides a sharp marker for the deconfinement transition, with potential applicability to QCD with fermions.
