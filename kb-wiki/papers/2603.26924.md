# Density screening effects in the NJL model: Chiral condensate, speed of sound, and the Critical End Point

> The phase diagram of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) remains a central topic in high-energy physics. At high temperature and low baryochemical potential, the chiral transition is experimentally observed and theoretically explored to be a smooth crossover, while at high densities, a first-order phase transition is theoretically expected in lack of direct experimental evidence. The search for the Critical End Point (CEP), where both regimes meet, is one of the main objectives of heavy-ion experiments at FAIR and NICA. In this work, we explore the QCD phase diagram structure using the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, incorporating medium screening effects through an effective coupling $G(T,μ)$ for $μ\gg T\sim 0$. We apply a consistent regularization scheme and Sommerfeld expansion to include low thermal and large density corrections in the gap equation. Our numerical analysis focuses on the behavior of the chiral condensate, the dynamical quark mass, and the speed of sound. We find that screening effects shift the posible position of the CEP and modify the nature of the chiral transition. These findings provide theoretical support for ongoing experimental searches and may have implications for the physics of compact stars.

## Key claim

The paper finds that incorporating medium screening effects through a density- and temperature-dependent effective coupling \(G(T,\mu)\) in the NJL model shifts the possible position of the Critical End Point (CEP) toward higher chemical potentials and modifies the nature of the chiral transition, but the authors note that a rigorous identification of the CEP requires simultaneous divergence of the quark-number susceptibility and a discontinuity in the dynamical mass, which they do not definitively demonstrate.

## Method

The authors employ the two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the Hartree (mean-field) approximation, solving the self-consistent gap equation for the dynamical quark mass. They incorporate medium screening effects through a phenomenological effective coupling \(G'(T,\mu) = G\,m'/m\), where \(m\) is the vacuum mass and \(m'\) is the mass in the medium, and use a Sommerfeld expansion to include low thermal and large density corrections in the regime \(\mu \gg T \sim 0\). The speed of sound \(c_s^2\) is computed from the thermodynamic potential using the expression \(c_s^2 = \left.\frac{\partial p}{\partial \varepsilon}\right|_{s}\), which at very low temperatures reduces to \(c_s^2 \approx \frac{n}{\mu} \left (\frac{\partial n}{\partial\mu} \right)^{-1}\).

## Key numbers

* The baryon number density \(n\) for the screened coupling \(G'\) shows a crossover towards negative values at \(\mu \approx 600\)–\(700\) MeV, indicating a more marked change in chiral restoration compared to the constant-coupling case.
* The speed of sound \(c_s^2\) approaches the conformal value of \(1/3\) at large chemical potentials for both constant and screened couplings, with the approach being monotonic and from below.
* The standard NJL model without screening typically places the CEP at \(\mu \approx 300\)–\(350\) MeV and \(T \approx 50\)–\(80\) MeV for two light flavors with a cutoff \(\Lambda\) in the range 590–650 MeV, while screening pushes the relevant structure toward higher chemical potentials.
* The density \(n\) for the constant coupling case decreases monotonically between approximately \(2.5 > n > 0\) in \(\mu \leq 1000\) MeV without crossing the negative axis.

## Context

This work extends standard NJL model studies (Buballa 2005, Klevansky 1992, Hatsuda 1994) by incorporating medium screening effects that weaken the scalar attraction at high density, which is physically motivated by Debye screening of gluon exchanges (Roberts 1994, Fischer 2009). The results are consistent with functional renormalization group studies (Schaefer 2007, Herbst 2011) that find a moderate shift of the CEP location when fluctuations beyond mean field are included, and the speed of sound behavior is compared to lattice QCD calculations at zero chemical potential (Borsanyi 2014, Bazavov 2012) and to PNJL model studies at finite density (Fukushima 2008, Schaefer 2007). The findings provide theoretical support for experimental searches at FAIR and NICA and may have implications for the equation of state of compact stars, where a softer equation of state in the transitional region is compatible with neutron-star constraints (Annala 2020, Tews 2018).
