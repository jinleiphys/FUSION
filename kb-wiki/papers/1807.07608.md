# Hydrodynamic modes in a magnetized chiral plasma with vorticity

> By making use of a covariant formulation of the chiral kinetic theory in the relaxation-time approximation, we derive the first-order dissipative hydrodynamics equations for a charged chiral plasma with background electromagnetic fields. We identify the global equilibrium state for a rotating chiral plasma confined to a cylindrical region with realistic boundary conditions. Then, by using linearized hydrodynamic equations, supplemented by the Maxwell equations, we study hydrodynamic modes of magnetized rotating chiral plasma in the regimes of high temperature and high density. We find that, in both regimes, dynamical electromagnetism has profound effects on the spectrum of propagating modes. In particular, there are only the sound and Alfvén waves in the regime of high temperature, and the plasmons and helicons at high density. We also show that the chiral magnetic wave is universally overdamped because of high electrical conductivity in plasma that causes an efficient screening of charge fluctuations. The physics implications of the main results are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The authors derive first-order dissipative hydrodynamics for a charged chiral plasma with background electromagnetic fields and find that, when dynamical electromagnetism is included self-consistently, the chiral magnetic wave is universally overdamped due to high electrical conductivity, contrary to predictions from models that treat electromagnetic fields as nondynamical backgrounds.

## Method

Starting from the covariant formulation of chiral kinetic theory in the relaxation-time approximation, the authors derive the complete set of first-order dissipative hydrodynamic equations for a charged chiral plasma, supplemented by the Maxwell equations. They identify the global equilibrium state for a uniformly rotating chiral plasma confined to a cylindrical region with realistic boundary conditions, assuming vanishing chiral chemical potential in equilibrium. Using linearized hydrodynamic equations, they study the spectrum of propagating hydrodynamic modes in the high-temperature and high-density regimes, analyzing the effects of dynamical electromagnetism on each mode.

## Key numbers

- Speed of sound in the ideal gas approximation: \(c_s = 1/\sqrt{3}\)
- Electrical conductivity in the relaxation-time approximation: \(\sigma_E = \tau e^2 \frac{3(\mu^2 + \mu_5^2) + \pi^2 T^2}{9\pi^2 \hbar^3}\)
- Chiral electric separation conductivity: \(\sigma_E^5 = \tau e^2 \frac{2\mu \mu_5}{3\pi^2 \hbar^3}\)
- Shear viscosity coefficient from the relaxation-time approximation: \(\frac{8\tau\epsilon}{15}\) (coefficient in the dissipative stress tensor \(\pi^{\mu\nu}\))
- Sound velocity in strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma: \(c_s^2 \approx 0.25\) to \(0.3\) for temperatures above the deconfinement transition, reaching within 10% of the ideal gas value at \(T \simeq 350~\text{MeV}\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous derivation of second-order dissipative hydrodynamics for neutral chiral plasmas (Gorbar, 2017) to the charged case with background electromagnetic fields, using the covariant chiral kinetic theory developed by Hidaka (2016, 2017). It contradicts earlier studies of hydrodynamic modes in magnetized chiral plasmas (Yamamoto, 2015; Abbasi, 2015; Kalaydzhyan, 2016; Abbasi, 2016; Hattori, 2017) that used nondynamical background field approximations and predicted exotic propagating modes; the present work shows that dynamical electromagnetism eliminates many of those modes. The results enable future studies of collective mode signatures in heavy-ion collisions, the early universe, and compact star astrophysics, where self-consistent electromagnetic treatment is essential.
