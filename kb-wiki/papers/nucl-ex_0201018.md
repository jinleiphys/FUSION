# Systematics of mid-rapidity K-/pi ratio in heavy-ion collisions

> It is observed that K-/pi in A+A and possibly p+p and pbar+p collisions follows an interesting systematic in omega, the pion transverse energy per unit of rapidity and transverse overlap area. The systematics show a linear increase of K-/pi with omega in the AGS and SPS energy regime and a saturation at RHIC energy. The systematics indicate that omega might be the relevant variable underlying K-/pi. At high energy, the omega variable is related to the gluon saturation scale in high density QCD, and perhaps to the initial energy density in the Bjorken picture.

# Systematics of mid-rapidity K-/pi ratio in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The mid-rapidity K-/pi ratio in heavy-ion collisions follows a systematic trend when plotted against ω, the pion transverse energy per unit of rapidity and transverse overlap area, showing a linear increase in the AGS and SPS energy regime and a saturation at RHIC energy. The paper presents this as an observation that ω might be the relevant variable underlying K-/pi, but emphasizes that the interpretation requires a leap of faith and that gluon saturation should be irrelevant at AGS energies even though the data follow the same systematics.

## Method
The author compiled mid-rapidity K-/pi data from AGS, SPS, and RHIC experiments for heavy-ion collisions across different collision systems, energies, and centralities, and plotted them against a newly defined experimental variable ω. This variable is constructed from the pion mean transverse mass, the mid-rapidity pion multiplicity density, and the transverse overlap area of the colliding nuclei, designed to approximate the gluon transverse area density and scale as Q_s^3. The same analysis was extended to p+p and pbar+p collisions using a proton radius of 0.8 fm from the MIT bag model.

## Key numbers
- K-/pi in central A+A collisions increases steadily with sqrt(s_NN) from AGS through SPS energies, while K+/pi reaches a maximum at sqrt(s_NN) ≈ 10 GeV and then drops at higher energies.
- Enhancement in K-/pi from elementary p+p to A+A collisions is about a factor of 2 and is similar at SPS and RHIC.
- The saturation scale Q_s at RHIC energies is on the order of 1–2 GeV/c.
- The proton size used for p+p and pbar+p ω calculation is r = 0.8 fm from the MIT bag model; a choice of r = 0.6–0.7 fm would bring the elementary data points on top of the A+A systematics.
- The ω variable is approximately equal to the Bjorken estimate of initial energy density times formation time.

## Context
This work extends the study of strangeness production as a probe of deconfined quark-gluon matter, following the suggestion by Rafelski that strangeness is sensitive to such a state. The paper addresses the complication that K+/pi is affected by net-baryon density, as noted in prior work on net-baryon density effects, and focuses on K-/pi instead. The ω variable is motivated by the gluon saturation picture from high density QCD, and the observed systematics enables future tests with low energy RHIC data that would overlap with existing SPS and RHIC data in ω. The author notes that a similar study in terms of participant density ρ_part can be found in Polish (2001).
