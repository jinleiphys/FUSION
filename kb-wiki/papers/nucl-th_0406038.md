# Chiral approach to nuclear matter: Role of two-pion exchange with virtual delta-isobar excitation

> We extend a recent three-loop calculation of nuclear matter in chiral perturbation theory by including the effects from two-pion exchange with single and double virtual $Δ(1232)$-isobar excitation. Regularization dependent short-range contributions from pion-loops are encoded in a few NN-contact coupling constants. The empirical saturation point of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter, $\bar E_0 = -16 $MeV, $ρ_0 = 0.16 $fm$^{-3}$, can be well reproduced by adjusting the strength of a two-body term linear in density (and weakening an emerging three-body term quadratic in density). The nuclear matter compressibility comes out as $K = 304 $MeV. The real single-particle potential $U(p,k_{f0})$ is substantially improved by the inclusion of the chiral $πNΔ$-dynamics: it grows now monotonically with the nucleon momentum $p$. The effective nucleon mass at the Fermi surface takes on a realistic value of $M^*(k_{f0})=0.88M$. As a consequence of these features, the critical temperature of the liquid-gas phase transition gets lowered to the value $T_c \simeq 15 $MeV. In this work we continue the complex-valued single-particle potential $U(p,k_f)+i W(p,k_f)$ into the region above the Fermi surface $p>k_f$. The effects of $2π$-exchange with virtual $Δ$-excitation on the nuclear energy density functional are also investigated. The effective nucleon mass associated with the kinetic energy density is $\widetilde M^*(ρ_0)= 0.64M$. Furthermore, we find that the isospin properties of nuclear matter get significantly improved by including the chiral $πNΔ$-dynamics.

## Key claim

By including two-pion exchange with virtual delta-isobar excitation in a three-loop chiral perturbation theory calculation, the empirical saturation point of nuclear matter can be reproduced, but only by adjusting the strength of a two-body term linear in density and weakening an emerging three-body term quadratic in density, with the three-body contact parameter set to \(\zeta = -3/4\).

## Method

The authors extended a previous three-loop chiral perturbation theory calculation of nuclear matter by incorporating two-pion exchange diagrams with single and double virtual \(\Delta(1232)\)-isobar excitation, evaluated using non-relativistic \(\Delta \leftrightarrow \pi N\) transition vertices and \(\Delta\)-propagators. Short-range contributions from pion-loop divergences were regularized via a twice-subtracted dispersion relation and encoded in two NN-contact coupling constants \(B_3\) and \(B_5\) (with \(B_5\) set to zero), plus a three-body contact term controlled by parameter \(\zeta\). The energy per particle was computed by summing Hartree and Fock contributions from the \(\Delta\)-driven two-pion exchange, three-body Pauli-blocking diagrams, and previously derived terms from one-pion exchange and iterated one-pion exchange.

## Key numbers

* Saturation energy \(\bar E_0 = -16.0\) MeV at saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.157\) fm\(^{-3}\) (Fermi momentum \(k_{f0} = 261.6\) MeV), achieved with \(B_3 = -7.99\), \(B_5 = 0\), and \(\zeta = -3/4\).
* Nuclear matter compressibility \(K = 304\) MeV.
* Effective nucleon mass at the Fermi surface \(M^*(k_{f0}) = 0.88M\) (with \(M = 939\) MeV).
* Effective nucleon mass associated with kinetic energy density \(\widetilde M^*(\rho_0) = 0.64M\).
* Critical temperature of the liquid-gas phase transition \(T_c \simeq 15\) MeV.
* Potential depth at zero momentum and saturation density \(U(0,k_{f0}) = -78.2\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the three-loop chiral perturbation theory calculations of nuclear matter by Kaiser et al. (2002) and Lutz et al. (2000), which included only nucleon intermediate states and produced unrealistic single-particle properties such as an effective nucleon mass \(M^*(k_{f0}) \simeq 3M\) and a critical temperature \(T_c \simeq 25.5\) MeV. The inclusion of virtual \(\Delta(1232)\)-isobar degrees of freedom, motivated by the comparable scales of the delta-nucleon mass splitting \(\Delta = 293\) MeV and the Fermi momentum \(k_{f0} \simeq 262\) MeV, eliminates the downward bending of the asymmetry energy and neutron matter energy per particle seen in previous chiral calculations, and enables realistic predictions for finite-temperature behavior and isospin properties of nuclear matter.
