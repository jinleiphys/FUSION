# $2π$ production in the Giessen coupled-channel model

> We present a coupled-channel Lagrangian approach (GiM) to describe the $πN \to πN$, $2πN$ scattering in the resonance energy region. The $2πN$ production has been significantly improved by using the isobar approximation with $σN$ and $πΔ(1232)$ in the intermediate state. The three-body unitarity is maintained up to interference pattern between the isobar subchannels. The scattering amplitudes are obtained as a solution of the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the $K$ matrix approximation. As a first application we perform a partial wave analysis of the $πN \to πN$, $π^0π^0 N$ reactions in the Roper resonance region. We obtain $R_{σN}(1440)=27^{+4}_{-9}$\,\% and $R_{σN}(1440)=12^{+5}_{-3}$\,\% for the $σN$ and $πΔ$ decay branching ratios of $N^*(1440)$ respectively. The extracted $πN$ inelasticities and reaction amplitudes are consistent with the results from other groups.

## Key claim

The paper presents a coupled-channel Lagrangian approach (GiM) that extends previous simplified treatments of the $2\pi N$ final state by explicitly including the isobar approximation with $\sigma N$ and $\pi \Delta(1232)$ intermediate states, maintaining three-body unitarity up to interference terms between the isobar subchannels, but the results are presented as a feasibility study restricted to the $\pi^0\pi^0 n$ channel in the first resonance region.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the $K$-matrix approximation for $\pi N \to \pi N$ and $\pi N \to 2\pi N$ scattering, reducing the three-body problem to two-body scattering equations for isobar production amplitudes. The interaction kernel is calculated from Lagrangian densities with derivative couplings for pseudoscalar vertices, and the $\sigma N$ and $\pi \Delta(1232)$ isobar subchannels are used to describe the $\pi^- p \to \pi^0\pi^0 n$ reaction. A partial wave analysis is performed assuming dominant $S_{11}$ and $P_{11}$ partial wave contributions in the isospin $I=1/2$ channel, limited to center-of-mass energies up to 1.46 GeV.

## Key numbers

- $R_{\sigma N}(1440) = 27^{+4}_{-9}\%$ for the $\sigma N$ decay branching ratio of $N^*(1440)$
- $R_{\pi \Delta}(1440) = 12^{+5}_{-3}\%$ for the $\pi \Delta(1232)$ decay branching ratio of $N^*(1440)$
- The analysis is limited to $\sqrt{s} = 1.46$ GeV as the upper energy boundary due to expected contributions from $N^*(1520)$ above that energy

## Context

This work extends the Giessen coupled-channel model (GiM) which previously treated the $2\pi N$ final state as a generic channel without explicit isobar structure, as done in earlier works by Feuster (1998), Penner (2002), and Shklyar (2004-2013). The results are compared to the single-energy solutions from Manley (1984) and to the multichannel analysis from the Kent group (Shrestha, 2012) and the BoGa group (Anisovich, 2011), which give different branching ratios for the Roper resonance decay modes. The approach enables direct analysis of $2\pi N$ experimental data and provides a framework for investigating cascade transitions like $N^{*\prime} \to \pi N^* \to \pi\pi N$, which could help resolve the long-standing controversy about the nature of the Roper resonance.
