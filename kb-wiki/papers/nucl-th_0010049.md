# Phases of QCD at High Baryon Density

> We review recent work on the phase structure of QCD at very high baryon density. We introduce the phenomenon of color superconductivity and discuss how the quark masses and chemical potentials determine the structure of the superfluid quark phase. We comment on the possibility of kaon condensation at very high baryon density and study the competition between superfluid, density wave, and chiral crystal phases at intermediate density.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the phase structure of QCD at high baryon density, arguing that color superconductivity is the dominant phenomenon at asymptotically high densities, but that at intermediate densities the competition between superfluid, density wave, and chiral crystal phases remains unresolved, with the chiral density wave state being "practically degenerate" with the BCS solution given the uncertainties in the calculation.

## Method

The authors use weak-coupling perturbative methods (ladder diagrams with one-gluon exchange) to solve the gap equation for color-superconducting phases with two and three flavors, and employ an effective Nambu-Jona-Lasinio-type description with instanton-generated forces to study the competition between particle-particle (BCS) and particle-hole (Overhauser) pairing at intermediate densities. For kaon condensation, they construct an effective Lagrangian for the pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons in the color-flavor-locked phase and minimize the thermodynamic potential under conditions of beta equilibrium and charge neutrality.

## Key numbers

* For two-flavor quark matter at densities near 5 times nuclear matter density, the superconducting gap is approximately 100 MeV, with condensation energy about -50 MeV/fm³.
* The critical temperature for the two-flavor color superconductor is given by T_c = 0.56 Δ₀.
* For p-wave pairing in one-flavor quark matter, the gap is expected to be less than 1 MeV, compared to the s-wave gap scale of 100 MeV.
* The kaon mass in the color-flavor-locked phase is approximately 5 MeV at μ = 500 MeV and approximately 1 MeV at μ = 1000 MeV.
* The critical strange quark mass for the transition from color-flavor-locked to two-flavor pairing satisfies Δ > m_s²/(2μ).
* The electron chemical potential in the absence of kaon condensation is μ ≈ m_s²/(4p_F) = π^(2/3)m_s²/(4ρ_B^(1/3)).

## Context

This review extends the earlier work on color superconductivity by Alford (1998), Rapp (1998), and the color-flavor-locked phase proposed by Alford (1999), and addresses the competition with chiral density waves originally suggested by Deryagin (1992) for large N_c. The results enable predictions for the possible phase structure inside compact stars, where densities reach several times nuclear matter density, and suggest that kaon condensation in the color-flavor-locked phase is likely even for moderate Fermi momenta around 500 MeV.
