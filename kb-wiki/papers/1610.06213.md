# Direct Photon Elliptic Flow at BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC)

> We use an event-by-event hydrodynamical description of the heavy-ion collision process with Glauber initial conditions to calculate the thermal emission of photons. The photon rates in the hadronic phase follow from a spectral function approach and a density expansion, while in the partonic phase they follow from the AMY perturbative rates. The calculated photon elliptic flows are lower than those reported recently by both the ALICE and PHENIX collaborations.

# Direct Photon Elliptic Flow at BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC)

## Key claim

The calculated photon elliptic flows from an event-by-event hydrodynamical model with Glauber initial conditions are lower than those reported by both the ALICE and PHENIX collaborations, but the authors note that viscous corrections to the photon rates were not included and that the hadronic photon rates used differ substantially from those employed by other groups.

## Method

The authors used an event-by-event relativistic hydrodynamic simulation with Glauber Monte Carlo initial conditions, tuned to reproduce measured charged particle multiplicities at fixed centralities for both RHIC (Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV) and LHC (Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV). Photon emission rates in the hadronic phase were computed using a chiral reduction scheme with spectral functions from e+e- annihilation and tau-decay data, expanded to one-pion and two-pion contributions, while partonic phase rates used the resummed AMY perturbative QCD rates with a running strong coupling constant. The photon elliptic flow was defined as the event-averaged correlation between the complex photon flow coefficient and the integrated pion flow coefficient.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC, the entropy per wounded nucleon was set to S_WN=15.5 with nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section sigma_inel^NN=40 mb; at LHC, S_WN=27.0 with sigma_inel^NN=64 mb.
- The ratio of normalization constants kappa_LHC/kappa_RHIC = 1.74, matching the ratio of multiplicities between the two colliding systems.
- The freeze-out temperature was set to T_FO=137 MeV for PHENIX and T_FO=131 MeV for ALICE, with critical temperature T_crit=190 MeV for switching between partonic and hadronic emission.
- The two-pion contribution to hadronic photon rates at T=100 MeV is about 3 times smaller at the highest point near threshold compared to the rates used by Rapp and collaborators.
- The prompt photon spectrum at LHC used parameters A=0.55269, B=0.48304, and n=2.6788 in the parametrization q0 d^3 sigma^pp/d^3 q = A (1+q_T^2/B)^{-n}.
- The simulated charged particle elliptic flows at RHIC are below experimental data especially at high transverse momentum; at LHC they are below empirical values at low p_T but reach empirical values near p_T ~ 3.5 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical analysis of direct photon anisotropies at RHIC and LHC using a hydrodynamical framework, complementing studies by the McGill group (Gale et al., 2015) and others (Rapp, 2013; Dusling and Zahed, 2010). The authors use hadronic photon rates from a chiral reduction scheme developed by Lee et al. (2014) that differ substantially from the diagrammatic approach of Rapp and collaborators, particularly in the two-pion contribution. The calculation develops computational machinery for future Beam Energy Scan studies at RHIC where hadronic rates become increasingly important, but the authors note that implementing viscous corrections in the chiral reduction scheme remains an open problem.
