# Symmetry Improved CJT Effective Action

> The formalism introduced by Cornwall, Jackiw and Tomboulis (CJT) provides a systematic approach to consistently resumming non-perturbative effects in Quantum Thermal Field Theory. One major limitation of the CJT effective action is that its loopwise expansion introduces residual violations of possible global symmetries, thus giving rise to massive Goldstone bosons in the spontaneously broken phase of the theory. In this paper we develop a novel symmetry-improved CJT formalism for consistently encoding global symmetries in a loopwise expansion. In our formalism, the extremal solutions of the fields and propagators to a loopwise truncated CJT effective action are subject to additional constraints given by the Ward identities due to global symmetries. By considering a simple O(2) scalar model, we show that, unlike other methods, our approach satisfies a number of important field-theoretic properties. In particular, we find that the Goldstone boson resulting from spontaneous symmetry breaking of O(2) is massless and the phase transition is a second order one, already in the Hartree-Fock approximation. After taking the sunset diagrams into account, we show how our approach properly describes the threshold properties of the massless Goldstone boson and the Higgs particle in the loops. Finally, assuming minimal modifications to the Hartree-Fock approximated CJT effective action, we calculate the corresponding symmetry-improved CJT effective potential and discuss the conditions for its uniqueness for scalar-field values away from its minimum.

## Key claim

The authors develop a symmetry-improved CJT (Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis) formalism that enforces Ward identities from global symmetries as constraints on the extremal solutions of the fields and propagators, thereby ensuring that the Goldstone boson remains massless and the phase transition is second order even at the Hartree-Fock approximation level, unlike standard truncations of the CJT effective action.

## Method

The authors impose the Ward identity constraint \(v \Delta^{-1,GG}(k=0) = 0\) on the loopwise-truncated CJT effective action, using a Lagrange multiplier method to enforce this condition alongside the usual extremization with respect to the propagator. They apply this formalism to a simple O(2) scalar model, first in the Hartree-Fock (double-bubble) approximation and then including sunset diagrams, renormalizing the theory with temperature-independent counter-terms in the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme using dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers

- In the Hartree-Fock approximation of the O(2) model, the Goldstone boson is massless and the phase transition is second order, in contrast to standard CJT truncations which yield a massive Goldstone boson and a first-order transition.
- The regulator introduced for functional differentiation is \(\eta m^2\), taken to zero as \(\eta \to 0\), with the Lagrange multiplier redefined as \(l_0 = \eta l / v\) kept fixed to a non-zero finite value.
- The theory is renormalized using the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme with \(T\)-independent counter-terms, following the approach of van Hees (2002), Blaizot (2003), and Berges (2005).

## Context

This work extends the CJT formalism (Cornwall, Jackiw, and Tomboulis, 1974) by addressing the long-standing problem of residual global symmetry violations in loopwise truncations, which previously gave rise to massive Goldstone bosons (Baym, 1975; Amelino-Camelia, 1997; Petropoulos, 2000; Lenaghan, 2000). Prior attempts to restore the Goldstone theorem, such as adding phenomenological terms (Ivanov, 2005; Ivanov, 2006), using external propagators (van Hees, 2002; Nemoto, 2005), or employing non-linear representations (Grahl, 2013), suffered from pathologies including first-order phase transitions, erroneous thresholds, or non-analyticities. The symmetry-improved formalism enables consistent non-perturbative studies of phase transitions and threshold properties in quantum thermal field theory, properly describing the decay of the Higgs particle into massless Goldstone bosons.
