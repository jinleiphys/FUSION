# A systematic study of J/psi suppression in cold nuclear matter

> Based on a Glauber model, a statistical analysis of all mid-rapidity J/psi hadroproduction and leptoproduction data on nuclear targets is carried out. This allows us to determine the J/psi-nucleon inelastic cross section, whose knowledge is crucial to interpret the J/psi suppression observed in heavy-ion collisions, at SPS and at RHIC. The values of sigma are extracted from each experiment. A clear tension between the different data sets is reported. The global fit of all data gives sigma=3.4+/-0.2 mb, which is significantly smaller than previous estimates. A similar value, sigma=3.5+/-0.2 mb, is obtained when the nDS nuclear parton densities are included in the analysis, although we emphasize that the present uncertainties on gluon (anti)shadowing do not allow for a precise determination of sigma. Finally, no significant energy dependence of the J/psi-N interaction is observed, unless strong nuclear modifications of the parton densities are assumed.

# A systematic study of J/psi suppression in cold nuclear matter

## Key claim

The J/psi-nucleon inelastic cross section extracted from a global fit of all mid-rapidity hadroproduction and leptoproduction data is sigma = 3.4 +/- 0.2 mb (or 3.5 +/- 0.2 mb when nDS nuclear parton densities are included), which is significantly smaller than previous estimates; however, the authors emphasize that present uncertainties on gluon (anti)shadowing do not allow for a precise determination of sigma, and a clear tension between different data sets is reported.

## Method

A Glauber model analysis was performed on all available mid-rapidity J/psi hadroproduction data (E537, NA3, NA38, NA50, E672, E866, HERA-B, PHENIX) and leptoproduction data (NMC) on nuclear targets, using a chi-squared minimization procedure that properly accounts for correlated systematic errors. The J/psi production cross section in nuclei was computed within the Colour Evaporation Model for hadroproduction and leading-order QCD for leptoproduction, with nuclear absorption described by a survival probability factor depending on the J/psi-nucleon inelastic cross section and the nuclear thickness function. The analysis was performed both with and without nuclear parton density corrections (using the nDS parametrization), and a global fit was carried out weighting each experiment by its chi-squared per degree of freedom.

## Key numbers

- Global fit of all data gives sigma = 3.4 +/- 0.2 mb (without nuclear PDF corrections) and sigma = 3.5 +/- 0.2 mb (with nDS nuclear parton densities)
- NA3 experiment yields sigma = 2.7 +/- 0.2 mb at sqrt(s) = 16.7-22.9 GeV for p and pi+/- projectiles on Pt targets
- NA50 experiment yields sigma = 4.7 +/- 0.5 mb at sqrt(s) = 27.4-29.1 GeV for p projectiles on Be, Al, Cu, Ag, W, Pb targets
- E537 experiment yields sigma = 6.6 +/- 1.1 mb at sqrt(s) = 15.3 GeV for pi- and anti-p projectiles on Be, Cu, W targets
- NA38 experiment yields sigma = 5.5 +/- 0.7 mb at sqrt(s) = 29.1 GeV for p projectiles on C, Al, Cu, W targets
- E866 experiment yields sigma = 2.7 +/- 0.9 mb at sqrt(s) = 38.1 GeV for p projectiles on Be, Fe, W targets
- NMC leptoproduction data gives an upper limit sigma <= 0.9 mb at sqrt(s) = 8-21 GeV for gamma* on C, Sn targets
- PHENIX experiment yields sigma = 3.5 +/- 3.0 mb at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV for p and d projectiles on p, Au targets
- HERA-B experiment yields sigma = 2.1 +/- 1.3 mb at sqrt(s) = 41.6 GeV for p projectiles on C, W targets
- E672 experiment yields sigma = 11.2 +/- 6.1 mb at sqrt(s) = 31.5 GeV for pi- projectiles on C, Al, Cu, Pb targets

## Context

This work extends the earlier Glauber analyses by Gerschel and Hufner (1993) and Kharzeev, Lourenco, Nardi and Satz (1996) by performing a systematic statistical analysis of all available mid-rapidity J/psi data on nuclear targets, including proper treatment of correlated systematic errors and nuclear parton density uncertainties. The extracted cross section provides a crucial baseline for interpreting J/psi suppression observed in heavy-ion collisions at SPS (NA38, NA50, NA60) and RHIC (PHENIX), enabling separation of cold nuclear matter effects from possible quark-gluon plasma signatures. The reported tension between different data sets, particularly the discrepancy between NA3/E866 results (sigma ~ 2.7 mb) and NA50/NA38/E537 results (sigma ~ 4.7-6.6 mb), calls for new measurements such as the upcoming NA60 p-A data to resolve the origin of these differences.
