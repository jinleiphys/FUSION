# Automated event generation for S-wave quarkonium and leptonium production in NRQCD and NRQED

> We present an extension of the MadGraph5_aMC@NLO framework that enables the automated calculation of leading-order cross sections for S-wave quarkonium and leptonium production within the non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) and non-relativistic QED (NRQED) factorisation formalisms. The framework has been validated against a variety of benchmark processes, demonstrating robustness and flexibility for phenomenological studies. A key advantage of this implementation is its seamless integration with existing MadGraph5_aMC@NLO features, allowing computations not only within the Standard Model but also in a wide range of Beyond the Standard Model or Effective Field Theory scenarios via a modified Universal Feynman Output (UFO) interface. Furthermore, the framework maintains compatibility with standard Monte Carlo event generators for parton showering and hadronisation. Through numerous examples, we highlight that theoretical studies of quarkonium processes require careful consideration: the impact of subleading contributions is often difficult to predict using simple counting arguments based solely on the hierarchy of couplings and velocity-scaling rules.

## Key claim

The paper presents an extension of the MadGraph5_aMC@NLO framework that enables automated leading-order cross section calculations for S-wave quarkonium and leptonium production, but only for S-wave states (not P-wave), and the authors caution that the impact of subleading contributions is often difficult to predict using simple counting arguments based solely on the hierarchy of couplings and velocity-scaling rules.

## Method

The authors implemented the non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) and non-relativistic QED (NRQED) factorisation formalisms within the MadGraph5_aMC@NLO framework by introducing a new "Boundstate" particle class and a modified Universal Feynman Output (UFO) model called sm_onia. The implementation uses covariant projection methods to isolate individual Fock states, applying colour, spin, and orbital angular momentum projectors to amplitudes for heavy-quark or lepton pair production, then combining the constituents into effective particles with the quantum numbers of the desired bound state. Physical quarkonium states are treated as collections of Fock states (analogous to multiparticles in MadGraph), with long-distance matrix elements (LDMEs) specified in an input card, while leptonium LDMEs are fixed by solving the Schrodinger equation with the Coulomb potential.

## Key numbers

- For charmonium states such as J/psi, a typical value is v^2 ~ 0.3 (the squared relative velocity of the constituent quarks)
- The colour-singlet LDME for J/psi(1|3S11) is 1.16 GeV^3
- The colour-octet LDME for J/psi(1|3S18) is 0.00902923 GeV^3
- The colour-octet LDME for J/psi(1|1S08) is 0.0146 GeV^3
- The colour-singlet LDME for Upsilon(1|3S11) is 9.28 GeV^3
- The leptonium LDME for the N-th radial excitation of an S-wave state is given by (2S+1) * alpha^3 / (pi * N^3) * (m_{l-} * m_{l'+} / (m_{l-} + m_{l'+}))^3

## Context

This work extends the capabilities of the MadGraph5_aMC@NLO framework to include quarkonium and leptonium production, building on prior dedicated tools such as MadOnia (Artoisenet 2007) and HelacOnia (Shao 2012, 2015) which were process-independent but had limitations and were not ported to the current MadGraph framework. The new implementation enables automated event generation for processes involving one or more S-wave quarkonia and/or leptonia, including compatibility with Beyond the Standard Model and Effective Field Theory scenarios via the UFO interface, and maintains compatibility with standard Monte Carlo event generators for parton showering and hadronisation. The authors note that no other publicly available tool currently provides similar functionality for leptonium processes, and they plan to extend the framework to NLO accuracy using an extended Frixione-Kunszt-Signer infrared-divergence subtraction formalism in the near term.
