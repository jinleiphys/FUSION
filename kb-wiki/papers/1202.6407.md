# Strangeness in the nucleon: what have we learned?

> We review the state of our knowledge concerning the contribution of strange quarks to various nucleon properties. In the case of the electric and magnetic form factors, the level of agreement between theory and experiment is very satisfactory and gives us considerable confidence in our capacity to make reliable calculations within non-perturbative QCD. In view of the importance of the scalar form factors to the detection of dark matter candidates such as neutralinos, we place a particular emphasis on the determination of the pi-N and strange quark sigma commutators.

# Strangeness in the nucleon: what have we learned?

## Key claim
The strange quark contribution to the nucleon mass (the strange sigma commutator, σ_s) is approximately 20–30 MeV, almost an order of magnitude smaller than the ~300 MeV value believed for two decades, with profound consequences for dark matter detection cross sections. The agreement between lattice QCD calculations and experimental parity-violating electron scattering data for the strange electric and magnetic form factors is now excellent, with theoretical accuracy exceeding experimental precision by almost an order of magnitude.

## Method
The authors review parity-violating electron scattering experiments at MIT-Bates, Mainz, and JLab combined with global analysis and radiative corrections to determine strange vector form factors. For the sigma commutators, they use finite-range regularization (FRR) chiral perturbation theory fitted to lattice QCD data for the octet baryon masses as a function of pion and kaon mass, extracting σ_πN and σ_s via the Feynman-Hellmann theorem. They also analyze the H-dibaryon mass using FRR to cross-validate the sigma commutator values.

## Key numbers
- Strange magnetic form factor: at most a few percent of the proton magnetic form factor at low Q²
- Strange electric radius: at most a few percent of the proton charge radius
- σ_s = 31 ± 15 ± 4 ± 2 MeV from Young and Thomas (2009)
- σ_πN = 47 ± 8 ± 1 ± 3 MeV from Young and Thomas (2009)
- y = 0.05 from Young and Thomas (2009)
- σ_s = 21 ± 7 MeV from Shanahan et al. (2011), described as the most reliable current determination
- σ_πN = 44 ± 5 MeV from Shanahan et al. (2011)
- y = 0.04 ± 0.02 from Shanahan et al. (2011)
- σ_s = 11 ± 13 +9/-3 MeV from Bali et al. (2011) at a light quark mass somewhat larger than physical
- y = 0.066 ± 0.011 ± 0.002 from the European Twisted Mass Collaboration (Dinter et al., 2011)
- H-dibaryon likely unbound by 13 ± 14 MeV from Shanahan et al. (2011)
- σ_0 = 36 ± 7 MeV from Borasoy (1996) using SU(3) analysis

## Context
This review extends the two-decade program of determining strange quark contributions to nucleon properties, beginning with the realization by McKeown (1989) that parity-violating electron scattering could constrain strange vector form factors. The work of Leinweber and Thomas (1999, 2004) developed indirect lattice techniques with chiral extrapolation that initially seemed to disagree with experiment but now show excellent agreement with direct lattice calculations from Doi et al. (2009). The dramatic downward revision of σ_s from ~300 MeV to ~20–30 MeV overturns the earlier SU(3) analysis of Borasoy (1996) and early lattice simulations, with major implications for neutralino dark matter cross sections in the CMSSM as explored by Giedt et al. (2009). The new values enable more reliable predictions for dark matter detection experiments and for studies of possible variations in fundamental constants as pursued by Flambaum et al. (2004).
