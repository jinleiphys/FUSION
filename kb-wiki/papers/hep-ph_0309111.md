# Chiral soliton models, large N_c consistency and the Theta^+ exotic baryon

> Predictions for a light collective $Θ^+$ baryon state (with strangeness +1) based on the collective quantization of chiral soliton models are shown to be inconsistent with large $N_c$ QCD. The lightest strangeness +1 state to emerge from the analysis has an excitation energy which at large $N_c$ scales as $N_c^0$ while collective quantization is legitimate only for excitations which go to zero as $N_c \to \infty$. This inconsistency strongly suggests that predictions for $Θ^+$ properties based on collective quantization of chiral solitons are not valid.

## Key claim
Predictions for a light collective Θ⁺ baryon (strangeness +1) based on collective quantization of chiral soliton models are inconsistent with large N_c QCD because the excitation energy of the lightest strangeness +1 state scales as N_c^0, while collective quantization is only valid for excitations that vanish as N_c → ∞.

## Method
The paper analyzes the SU(3) chiral soliton model treated with collective quantization, where a classical static hedgehog configuration in the SU(2) subspace is quantized semi-classically using an SU(3) generalization. The collective Hamiltonian depends on moments of inertia I₁ and I₂, with a quantization constraint J'_8 = -N_c B/(2√3) derived from the Witten-Wess-Zumino term. The author examines the N_c scaling of the mass splitting between the anti-decuplet (which contains the Θ⁺) and the octet by deriving the general expression for arbitrary N_c, rather than fixing N_c=3 at the outset.

## Key numbers
- The mass splitting between the anti-decuplet and octet for arbitrary N_c is M_{“10̅”} - M_{“8”} = (3 + N_c)/(4 I₂), which scales as N_c^0 at large N_c because I₂ scales as N_c.
- For N_c=3, the anti-decuplet-octet splitting is M_{10̅} - M_8 = 3/(2 I₂), which coincides with the general expression but hides the N_c scaling.
- The decuplet-octet splitting M_{“10”} - M_{“8”} = 3/(2 I₁) scales as 1/N_c, consistent with collective motion.
- The lowest-lying representation containing strangeness +1 at large N_c is (p,q) = (0, (N_c+3)/2) with J=1/2, denoted “10̅”.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of SU(3) chiral soliton quantization developed in the mid-1980s (SU3Quant, 1984) and directly challenges the predictions of Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997) for the Θ⁺ baryon. The argument uses large N_c consistency conditions from Dashen, Jenkins, and Manohar (1994) to show that the anti-decuplet states are not collective excitations, unlike the octet and decuplet which are consistent with large N_c scaling. The paper enables future work using linear response theory or scattering methods (e.g., kaon-baryon resonances) to describe exotic states, and predicts that if one light strangeness +1 resonance exists, others differing by order 1/N_c must also exist (Cohen and Lebed, 2003).
