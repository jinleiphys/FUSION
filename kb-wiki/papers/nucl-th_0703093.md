# Nuclear Saturation and Correlations

> The relation between nuclear saturation and NN-correlations is examined. Nucleons bound in a nucleus have a reduced effective mass due to the mean field. This results in off-energy-shell scatterings modifying the free-space NN-interaction by a dispersion correction. This is a major contribution to the density-dependence of the effective in-medium force and to saturation. Low-momentum effective interactions have been derived by renormalisation methods whereby correlations may be reduced by effectively cutting off high momentum components of the interaction. The effect of these cut-offs on dispersive corrections and on saturation is the main focus of this paper. The role of the tensor-force, its strength and its effect on correlations is of particular interest. The importance of the definition of the mean field in determining saturation as well as compressibility is also pointed out. With a cut-off below $\sim 2.6 fm^{-1}$ there is no saturation but at lower density the binding energy is still well approximated suggesting that such a force may be useful in nuclear structure calculations of (small) finite nuclei if saturation is not an issue. A separable interaction that fits experimental phase-shifts exactly by inverse scattering methods is used. Recent experiments measure short ranged correlations (SRC's) to be 0.23 for $^{56}Fe$. Other experiments have obtained a depletion of occupation-numbers in $^{208}Pb$ to be $\sim 0.2$. For nuclear matter with the separable interaction and a continuous spectrum we obtain the related quantity $κ$ to be 0.175 with the Bonn-B deuteron parameters, while Machleidt's gets $κ= 0.125$ for the Bonn-B potential and a continuous spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the dispersion correction arising from the reduced effective mass of nucleons in the mean field is a major contribution to the density-dependence of the effective in-medium force and to nuclear saturation, but this effect decreases as the momentum cutoff is lowered below approximately 3.0 fm^{-1}, and with a cutoff below approximately 2.6 fm^{-1} there is no saturation at all.

## Method

The authors performed Brueckner nuclear matter calculations using a separable NN interaction derived from inverse scattering that fits experimental phase shifts exactly. They computed the reaction matrix K as a function of starting energy, relative momentum, and center-of-mass momentum, using the continuous choice for the single-particle potential U(k). The effect of high-momentum cutoffs on the dispersion correction, the wound integral, and the depletion factor kappa was systematically studied by varying the cutoff momentum Lambda from 9.8 fm^{-1} down to 1.6 fm^{-1}.

## Key numbers

- For nuclear matter at normal density (k_F = 1.35 fm^{-1}) with the separable interaction and continuous spectrum, the total depletion factor kappa = 0.175 with Bonn-B deuteron parameters, compared to Machleidt's value of kappa = 0.125 for the Bonn-B potential with continuous spectrum
- With cutoff Lambda = 2.6 fm^{-1}, there is essentially no saturation; with Lambda = 2.0 fm^{-1}, even less evidence of saturation
- The dispersion correction for a specific case (P = 1.5 fm^{-1}, k = 0.45 fm^{-1}) is 1.7 MeV for Lambda = 9.8 fm^{-1} but only 0.10 MeV for Lambda = 2.0 fm^{-1}
- For the ^{3}S_{1} state at k_F = 1.35 fm^{-1}, kappa = 0.109 for Lambda = 9.8, decreasing to 0.062 for Lambda = 2.0
- For the ^{1}S_{0} state at k_F = 1.35 fm^{-1}, kappa = 0.016 for Lambda = 9.8, decreasing to 0.012 for Lambda = 2.0
- The SD defect wavefunction contributes kappa = 0.21 for k = 0, P = 0, Lambda = 9.8
- Recent experiments measure short-ranged correlations (SRCs) to be 0.23 for ^{56}Fe; other experiments obtain depletion of occupation numbers in ^{208}Pb to be approximately 0.2
- The kappa value varies with tensor strength: kappa = 0.196 for Bonn-C (P_D = 5.6), 0.175 for Bonn-B (P_D = 5.0), and 0.162 for Bonn-A (P_D = 4.4)
- At twice normal density (k_F = 1.7 fm^{-1}), the total kappa for Lambda = 9.8 is 0.172, nearly unchanged from normal density; for Lambda = 2.6, kappa decreases to 0.088, a reduction of approximately 40 percent

## Context

This work extends the long-standing investigation of nuclear saturation mechanisms, building on the Brueckner theory developed over 50 years ago and the Moszkowski-Scott separation method (1960). The paper directly addresses the modern approach of low-momentum effective interactions (V_{low-k}) and effective field theory (EFT), showing that cutting off high-momentum components below approximately 2.6 fm^{-1} eliminates saturation, consistent with results from Bozek (2006). The authors emphasize that the tensor force uncertainty, with deuteron D-state probability P_D ranging from 4 to 7 percent as seen in the Bonn-A, Bonn-B, and Bonn-C potentials of Machleidt (1989), remains a critical unresolved issue for understanding saturation. The results enable further work on spectral self-consistency and hole-hole ladder corrections in Green's function approaches, as pursued by Muther (2003) and Bozek (1999, 2002).
