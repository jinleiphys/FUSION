# Evolution of mechanism of parton energy loss with transverse momentum at RHIC and LHC in relativistic collision of heavy nuclei

> We analyze the suppression of particle production at large transverse momenta in ($0-5%$ most) central collisions of gold nuclei at $\sqrt{s_\textrm{NN}}=$ 200 GeV and lead nuclei at $\sqrt{s_{\textrm{NN}}}=$ 2.76 TeV. Full next-to-leading order radiative corrections at ${\cal{O}}(α_s^3)$, and nuclear effects like shadowing and parton energy loss are included. The parton energy loss is implemented in a simple multiple scattering model, where the partons lose an energy $ε=λ\times dE/dx$ per collision, where $λ$ is their mean free path. We take $ε=κE$ for a treatment which is suggestive of the Bethe Heitler (BH) mechanism of incoherent scatterings, $ε= \sqrt{αE}$ for LPM mechanism, and $ε=$ constant for a mechanism which suggests that the rate of energy loss ($dE/dx$) of the partons is proportional to total path length ($L$) of the parton in the plasma, as the formation time of the radiated gluon becomes much larger than $L$. We find that while the BH mechanism describes the nuclear modification factor $R_{\textrm{AA}}$ for $p_T \leq$ 5 GeV/$c$ (especially at RHIC energy), the LPM and more so the constant $dE/dx$ mechanism provides a good description at larger $p_T$. This confirms the earlier expectation that the energy loss mechanism for partons changes from BH to LPM for $p_T \ge λ<k_T^2>$, where $λ\approx$ 1 fm and $<k_T^2> \approx$ 1 GeV$^2$ is the average transverse kick-squared received by the parton per collision. The energy loss per collision at the $\sqrt{s_\textrm{NN}}$ =2.76 TeV is found to be about twice of that at 0.2 TeV.

# Evolution of mechanism of parton energy loss with transverse momentum at RHIC and LHC in relativistic collision of heavy nuclei

## Key claim

The mechanism of parton energy loss in central heavy-ion collisions changes from Bethe-Heitler (incoherent) to Landau-Pomeranchuk-Midgal (coherent) as transverse momentum increases beyond approximately 5 GeV/c, with the transition occurring at p_T approximately equal to lambda times the average transverse momentum squared per collision (about 5 GeV). The energy loss per collision at LHC energies (sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV) is about twice that at RHIC energies (sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV), though the extracted energy-loss coefficients are smaller than those implied by static-medium theoretical calculations.

## Method

The analysis uses next-to-leading order perturbative QCD at O(alpha_s^3) with CTEQ4M parton distribution functions and Binnewies-Kniehl-Kramer fragmentation functions to compute the baseline pp spectra. Nuclear effects are included via EKS98 shadowing and a multiple-scattering model where partons lose energy epsilon per collision over a mean free path lambda = 1 fm, with the nuclear fragmentation function modified to account for energy loss and gluon radiation. Three energy-loss prescriptions are tested: epsilon = kappa E (Bethe-Heitler), epsilon = sqrt(alpha E) (LPM), and epsilon = constant (coherent limit where dE/dx is proportional to path length L).

## Key numbers

- Transition momentum between BH and LPM regimes: p_T approximately 5 GeV/c, corresponding to E_LPM = lambda * <k_T^2> where lambda ≈ 1 fm and <k_T^2> ≈ 1 GeV^2
- At RHIC (sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, 0-5% central Au+Au): BH mechanism describes R_AA for 2.5 < p_T < 6 GeV/c with kappa ≈ 8%; LPM mechanism describes R_AA for 5 < p_T < 12 GeV/c with alpha = 0.07 GeV^(1/2)
- At LHC (sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, 0-5% central Pb+Pb): BH mechanism describes R_AA for 5 < p_T < 7 GeV/c; LPM mechanism describes R_AA for 7 < p_T < 14 GeV/c with alpha ≈ 0.25 GeV^(1/2); constant energy loss describes R_AA for 10 < p_T < 20 GeV/c
- Energy loss per collision at LHC is about twice that at RHIC
- R_AA at LHC reaches approximately 0.4 at p_T ≈ 20 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological multiple-scattering model of Wang (1998) and Jeon et al. (2003) to LHC energies, confirming the qualitative predictions of Baier et al. (1997) regarding the transition from Bethe-Heitler to LPM regimes as parton energy increases. The analysis uses PHENIX data for neutral pions at RHIC and ALICE data for charged hadrons at LHC. The results enable future studies of centrality dependence of the energy-loss mechanism and azimuthal anisotropy at large p_T, and provide a simple framework for interpreting jet-quenching measurements across different collision systems and energies.
