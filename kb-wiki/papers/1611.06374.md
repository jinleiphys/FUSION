# String point of view for heavy-light mesons

> An approximate rotational symmetry of a heavy-light meson is viewed from a string picture. Using a simple string configuration, we derive a formula, $(M-m_c)^2=πσL$, whose coefficient of the r.h.s. is just 1/2 of that of a light meson with two light quarks. A numerical plot is obtained for $D$ mesons of experimental data as well as several theoretical models, which shows good agreement with this formula.

# String point of view for heavy-light mesons

## Key claim
Heavy-light mesons with the same angular momentum L are approximately degenerate, and their masses (after subtracting the heavy quark mass) follow a linear relation with L: (M - m_c)^2 = πσL, where the coefficient is exactly half of that for light mesons with two light quarks. This holds as an approximate rotational symmetry in the heavy quark limit, supported by experimental data and model calculations.

## Method
The authors adopt Nambu's hadronic string picture, where a string consisting only of gluons connects quarks at its ends. For a heavy-light meson in the heavy quark limit, the heavy quark is fixed at one end while the light quark rotates at the speed of light. The mass M and angular momentum L are derived from integrals over the string length, assuming a simple rotating string configuration. The charm quark mass m_c is subtracted from the total meson mass to remove quark mass effects, yielding the relation (M - m_c)^2 = πσL.

## Key numbers
- For light mesons (two light quarks), the Chew-Frautschi formula gives M^2 = 2πσL, with a numerical fit M^2 = 1.103 L + ... from Afonin (2007).
- For heavy-light mesons, the derived formula is (M - m_c)^2 = πσL, where the coefficient is exactly 1/2 of that for light mesons.
- The mass gap between different multiplets with different L is nearly equal to Λ_QCD ~ 300 MeV.
- Experimental data for D mesons and B mesons, as well as model calculations by Godfrey and Isgur (1985), show good agreement with the linear relation (M - m_c)^2 = πσL.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous observation (Matsuki, 2016) that heavy-light meson spectra exhibit approximate rotational symmetry, by providing a physical string picture explanation. The derivation follows Nambu's (1974) hadronic string model and Afonin's (2007) derivation of the Chew-Frautschi formula for light mesons. The results support the interpretation of heavy-light mesons as rotating strings and enable further theoretical studies of heavy meson spectroscopy using string models.
