# On the Coefficients of a Hyperbolic Hydrodynamic Model

> Based on the Nakajima-Zubarev type nonequilibrium density operator, we derive a hyperbolic hydrodynamical equation. Microscopic Kubo-formulas for all coefficients in the hyperbolic hydrodynamics are obtained. Coefficients $α_{i}$'s and $β_{i}$'s in the Israel-Stewart equation are given as current-weighted correlation lengths which are to be calculated in statistical mechanics.

# On the Coefficients of a Hyperbolic Hydrodynamic Model

## Key claim

The authors derive microscopic Kubo-type formulas for all coefficients in the Israel-Stewart hyperbolic hydrodynamic equation, expressing the additional coefficients αᵢ and βᵢ as current-weighted correlation times and cross-current-weighted correlation distances that can be calculated from equilibrium statistical mechanics. However, the derivation is limited to linear order in the thermodynamic forces and assumes the validity of a gradient expansion where microscopic relaxation lengths are much smaller than macroscopic scales.

## Method

The authors start from the Nakajima-Zubarev nonequilibrium density operator and expand it around local equilibrium to linear order in thermodynamic forces. They perform a Taylor expansion of the thermodynamic forces (gradients of βUᵐ and βμ) around the position of the operator being evaluated, then decompose the resulting expressions for ⟨Tᵐⁿ⟩ and ⟨Jᵐ⟩ using projection operators and Curie's theorem for isotropic systems. By substituting the first-order Navier-Stokes relations into the second-order derivative terms, they obtain the Israel-Stewart form and identify the coefficients αᵢ and βᵢ as canonical correlation integrals.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity Kubo formula: ηₛ = β ⟨π|π⟩, where ⟨π|π⟩ = (1/5) ⟨⟨πᵐⁿ|πₘₙ⟩⟩
- Bulk viscosity Kubo formula: ηᵥ = β ⟨P|P'⟩
- Heat conductivity on E-frame: κ = ((⟨ε⟩ₗ + ⟨P⟩ₗ)/(⟨n⟩ₗ T))² ⟨I|I⟩
- Current-weighted correlation times: τ̄_I = ⟨I|t|I⟩/⟨I|I⟩, τ̄_s = ⟨π|t|π⟩/⟨π|π⟩, τ̄_v = ⟨P|t|P'⟩/⟨P|P'⟩
- Cross-current-weighted correlation distances: x̄_Is = ((⟨ε⟩ₗ+⟨P⟩ₗ)/(⟨n⟩ₗ T)) √(1/κ) √(β/ηₛ) ⟨I|x|π⟩, with analogous expressions for x̄_Iv and x̄_vI

## Context

This work extends the Kubo formula approach (Kubo 1957, Zubarev 1974) from Navier-Stokes coefficients to the second-order Israel-Stewart coefficients, which previously had only been calculated via the Grad 14-moment method from kinetic theory. The authors reference the phenomenological Israel-Stewart formulation (Israel and Stewart 1976, 1979) and earlier discussions of hydrodynamic applicability conditions by Iso et al. (IMN). The results enable future evaluation of these coefficients using Lattice QCD simulations or hadro-molecular calculations, providing a microscopic foundation for causal relativistic hydrodynamics used in heavy-ion collision phenomenology at RHIC and LHC.
