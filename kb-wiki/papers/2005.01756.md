# Sterile Neutrinos and the Global Reactor Antineutrino Dataset

> We present results from global fits to the available reactor antineutrino dataset, as of Fall 2019, to determine the global preference for a fourth, sterile neutrino. We have separately considered experiments that measure the integrated inverse-beta decay (IBD) rate from those that measure the energy spectrum of IBD events at one or more locations. The software used is the newly developed GLoBESfit tool set which is based on the publicly available GLoBES framework and will be released as open-source software.

## Key claim

The global reactor antineutrino dataset as of Fall 2019 shows a preference for a sterile neutrino with a significance that depends on which flux model (Huber-Mueller, summation method, or HKSS) is used to compute the expected event rates, and on whether rate-only or spectral-ratio data are included.

## Method

The authors performed global fits to the available reactor antineutrino dataset, separately analyzing experiments that measure the integrated inverse-beta decay (IBD) rate and those that measure the energy spectrum of IBD events at one or more locations. They used the newly developed GLoBESfit tool set, built on the GLoBES framework, to compute chi-squared functions over ranges of the sterile neutrino parameters sin²2θₑₑ and Δm²₄₁, incorporating systematic uncertainties from the Huber-Mueller flux predictions and considering alternative flux models (summation method and HKSS).

## Key numbers

- The effective active-sterile mixing angle is parameterized as sin²2θₑₑ = sin²2θ₁₄, where |Uₑ₄|² = sin²θ₁₄
- For the Daya Bay rate-evolution analysis, the 1230-day data set from An (2017) was used, considering the 6AD and 8AD periods
- Daya Bay detector target masses range from 19.891 × 10³ kg to 19.989 × 10³ kg
- Daya Bay detector total efficiencies range from 0.8013 to 0.9588
- Daya Bay effective baselines range from 563.4 m to 1639.7 m
- RENO near detector operating time is 1807.88 days with efficiency 60.3%; far detector operating time is 2193.04 days with efficiency 68.7%
- RENO near detector effective baseline is 0.4331 km; far detector effective baseline is 1.4469 km
- Double Chooz near detector operating time is 258.0 days with efficiency 85.47%; far detector operating time is 818.0 days with efficiency 86.78%
- The Markov Chain Monte Carlo analysis used 100 chains of walkers with randomized starting points, each taking 1500 steps with the first 500 ignored for burn-in

## Context

This work extends previous global fits to reactor antineutrino data searching for sterile neutrinos, using the most recent data from Daya Bay (An 2017), RENO (2018), Double Chooz (2019), DANSS (2018), NEOS (2016), and Bugey-3 (1994). The authors compare results using three different flux models: the standard Huber-Mueller predictions, the ab initio (summation method) fluxes, and the HKSS fluxes. The GLoBESfit software is released as open source, enabling future analyses of reactor antineutrino data for sterile neutrino searches and other new-physics scenarios.
