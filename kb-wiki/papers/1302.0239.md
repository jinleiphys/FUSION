# Boltzmann-Langevin One-Body dynamics for fermionic systems

> A full implementation of the Boltzmann-Langevin equation for fermionic systems is introduced in a transport model for dissipative collisions among heavy nuclei. Fluctuations are injected in phase space and not, like in more conventional approaches, as a projection on suitable subspaces. The advantage of this model is to be specifically adapted to describe processes characterised by instabilities, like the formation of fragments from a hot nuclear system, and by dissipation, like the transparency in nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a full implementation of the Boltzmann-Langevin equation for fermionic systems where fluctuations are injected directly in phase space, rather than as a projection on a reduced subspace; this approach is specifically adapted to describe processes characterised by instabilities and dissipation, but the authors note that the number of effective collisions is reduced with respect to the Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) approach because Pauli blocking is applied to extended distributions of test particles rather than to test-particle couples.

## Method

The authors construct the Boltzmann-Langevin One Body (BLOB) model using the same semiclassical isospin-dependent mean-field as the Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) model, with an incompressibility of 200 MeV and two forms of the symmetry energy (asy-stiff and asy-soft). The collision term is replaced by a stochastic Boltzmann-Langevin approach where nucleons are represented by agglomerates of test particles sharing the same volume in coordinate and momentum space, and the collision probability is weighted on the mean-free-path condition, the average relative momentum between clouds, and the nucleon-nucleon cross section divided by the number of test particles per nucleon. The effective-collision probability is further weighted on Pauli-blocking factors of the destination sites, taking into account the full extension of the initial distributions through a numerical optimisation procedure.

- System simulated: \(^{124}\)Sn\(+^{124}\)Sn at 30 \(A\)MeV and impact parameter \(b=0\).

## Context

This work extends the Bauer-Bertsch approach (Bauer 1987) for semiclassical test-particle-based transport models and contrasts with the Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) model of Guarnera (1997) and Chomaz (2004), where fluctuations are injected in a reduced subspace (coordinate space) rather than full phase space. The BLOB model enables the study of fragment formation from hot nuclear systems and transparency in nucleus-nucleus collisions, and the authors note that a similar comparison between SMF and AMD was done in Rizzo and Colonna (2008). The approach is promising for implementing isovector effects in collision dynamics and improving transport models for nuclear collisions.
