# The Surprising Transparency of the sQGP at LHC

> We present parameter-free predictions of the nuclear modification factor, R_{AA}^pi(p_T,s), of high p_T pions produced in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt{s}_{NN}=2.76 and 5.5 ATeV based on the WHDG/DGLV (radiative+elastic+geometric fluctuation) jet energy loss model. The initial quark gluon plasma (QGP) density at LHC is constrained from a rigorous statistical analysis of PHENIX/RHIC pi^0 quenching data at sqrt{s}_{NN}=0.2 ATeV and the charged particle multiplicity at ALICE/LHC at 2.76 ATeV. Our perturbative QCD tomographic theory predicts significant differences between jet quenching at RHIC and LHC energies, which are qualitatively consistent with the p_T-dependence and normalization---within the large systematic uncertainty---of the first charged hadron nuclear modification factor, R^{ch}_{AA}, data measured by ALICE. However, our constrained prediction of the central to peripheral pion modification, R^pi_{cp}(p_T), for which large systematic uncertainties associated with unmeasured p+p reference data cancel, is found to be over-quenched relative to the charged hadron ALICE R^{ch}_{cp} data in the range 5<p_T<20 GeV/c. The discrepancy challenges the two most basic jet tomographic assumptions: (1) that the energy loss scales linearly with the initial local comoving QGP density, rho_0, and (2) that ρ_0 \propto dN^{ch}(s,C)/dy is proportional to the observed global charged particle multiplicity per unit rapidity as a function of sqrt{s} and centrality class, C. Future LHC identified (h=pi,K,p) hadron R^h_{AA} data (together with precise p+p, p+Pb, and Z boson and direct photon Pb+Pb control data) are needed to assess if the QGP produced at LHC is indeed less opaque to jets than predicted by constrained extrapolations from RHIC.

## Key claim

The authors present parameter-free predictions of the pion nuclear modification factor at LHC energies based on the WHDG/DGLV jet energy loss model, but find that their constrained prediction over-quenches relative to the ALICE charged hadron data in the range 5 < p_T < 20 GeV/c, challenging the assumptions that energy loss scales linearly with initial local QGP density and that this density is proportional to the observed charged particle multiplicity.

## Method

The authors used the WHDG/DGLV model incorporating radiative plus elastic energy loss and geometric fluctuations to compute the nuclear modification factor R_AA^pi for pions in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)_NN = 2.76 and 5.5 ATeV. The initial QGP density at LHC was constrained by a statistical analysis of PHENIX/RHIC pi^0 quenching data at sqrt(s)_NN = 0.2 ATeV combined with ALICE/LHC charged particle multiplicity measurements at 2.76 ATeV, then extrapolated to LHC energies assuming the medium density scales with observed charged hadron multiplicity.

## Key numbers

- The ALICE charged particle rapidity density in 0-5% central collisions: dN_ch/deta = 1601 +/- 60, and 35 +/- 2 in 70-80% peripheral collisions
- The best fit value for gluon density at RHIC: dN_g/dy = 1400 (+200/-375) for fixed alpha_s = 0.3
- The scaled gluon density at LHC in 0-5% central collisions: dN_g/dy = 3000 (+500/-800)
- The charged particle multiplicity per participant increased by a factor of 2.2 from RHIC to LHC
- The discrepancy between WHDG predictions and ALICE R_cp data occurs in the range 5 < p_T < 20 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the WHDG jet energy loss model (Wicks, 2005) from RHIC to LHC energies, updating earlier 2007 LHC predictions (Abreu, 2007; Horowitz, 2007) by incorporating the new ALICE charged particle multiplicity data. The results challenge both perturbative QCD tomographic assumptions and AdS/CFT holographic approaches (Noronha, 2010) that predict energy loss increases monotonically with plasma density. Future LHC identified hadron data together with p+p, p+Pb, Z boson, and direct photon control measurements are needed to determine whether the QGP at LHC is less opaque than predicted by constrained extrapolations from RHIC.
