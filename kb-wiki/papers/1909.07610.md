# How the antisymmetrization affects a cluster-cluster interaction: two-cluster systems

> We study effects of the antisymmetrization on the potential energy of two-cluster systems. The object of the investigation is the lightest nuclei of p-shell with a dominant alpha-cluster channel. For this aim we construct matrix elements of two-cluster potential energy between cluster oscillator functions with and without full antisymmetrization. Eigenvalues and eigenfunctions of the potential energy matrix are studied in detail. Eigenfunctions of the potential energy operator are presented in oscillator, coordinate and momentum spaces. We demonstrate that the Pauli principle affects more strongly the eigenfunctions than the eigenvalues of the matrix and leads to the formation of resonance and trapped states.

## Key claim

The Pauli principle affects the eigenfunctions of the two-cluster potential energy operator more strongly than its eigenvalues, and this effect can lead to the formation of resonance and trapped states in light p-shell nuclei, even though the eigenvalues of the exact and folding (non-antisymmetrized) potential energy matrices remain very close to each other for most states.

## Method

The authors constructed matrix elements of the two-cluster potential energy operator between cluster oscillator functions, both with full antisymmetrization (exact treatment) and without it (folding approximation), for light p-shell nuclei with dominant alpha-cluster channels (⁵He, ⁵Li, ⁶Li, ⁷Li, ⁷Be, ⁸Be). They diagonalized these matrices to obtain eigenvalues and eigenfunctions, then analyzed the eigenfunctions in oscillator, coordinate, and momentum spaces. Three different nucleon-nucleon potentials were employed: Volkov N2 (VP), modified Hasegawa-Nagata (MHNP), and Minnesota (MP), with Coulomb forces treated exactly and spin-orbit forces neglected.

## Key numbers

- Oscillator length b values (in fm) for different nuclei and potentials: ⁵He/⁵Li: 1.38 (VP), 1.32 (MHNP), 1.28 (MP); ⁶Li: 1.46 (VP), 1.36 (MHNP), 1.31 (MP); ⁷Li/⁷Be: 1.44 (VP), 1.36 (MHNP), 1.35 (MP); ⁸Be: 1.38 (VP), 1.32 (MHNP), 1.28 (MP)
- The Pauli principle affects only cluster basis states with number of oscillator quanta N_osc ≤ 25, corresponding to an estimated range R_P ≈ b √((A₁+A₂)/(A₁A₂)) √(2N_osc+3) ≈ 10 fm
- For ⁵He, ⁵Li, ⁶Li, ⁷Li, and ⁷Be, eigenvalues of the norm kernel Λ_nL > 1 for normal parity states and Λ_nL < 1 for abnormal parity states
- For the 0⁺ state in ⁸Be = α+α, the Pauli principle annihilates two cluster functions (n=0, L=0 and n=1, L=0)
- The number of Pauli forbidden states n₀ is given by max((A-4-L)/2, 0) for normal parity states and max((A-3-L)/2, 0) for abnormal parity states

## Context

This work extends the method introduced by Lashko (2019) for analyzing potential energy matrices in two-cluster systems, applying it specifically to study how antisymmetrization affects the cluster-cluster interaction. It builds upon the algebraic version of the resonating group method (RGM) developed by Filippov and Okhrimenko (1981) and earlier studies of Pauli principle effects on the kinetic energy operator by Fujiwara et al. (2004, 2005) and Lashko et al. (2008, 2009), which showed that the kinetic energy modified by antisymmetrization can generate effective attraction or repulsion between clusters. The results enable a more complete understanding of how the Pauli principle creates resonance and trapped states in two-cluster systems, and provide a separable representation of the nonlocal inter-cluster interaction that can be used in future dynamical calculations.
