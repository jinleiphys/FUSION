# Center symmetry and Hagedorn spectrum

> This paper explores the conjecture that large Nc gauge theories have a Hagedorn spectrum, if, and only if, they are confining and posses an explicit or emergent center symmetry. Evidence in support of this conjecture is presented. Many classes of large Nc gauge theories are considered. In all cases, we find that theories for which there exists a strong plausibility argument for a Hagedorn spectrum at large Nc are also believed to be confining and possess either an explicit center symmetric or have a strong plausibility argument for the existence of an emergent center symmetry at large Nc . Conversely, all theories we considered which are believed not to have a Hagedorn spectrum at large Nc, either were known not to be confining or else were believed to lack an emergent center symmetry. This is consistent with expectations based on the conjecture.

## Key claim

The paper conjectures that a large \(N_c\) gauge theory has a Hagedorn spectrum if, and only if, it is confining and possesses an explicit or emergent center symmetry; the authors present case-by-case plausibility arguments rather than a rigorous proof, noting that the conjecture applies only in the standard 't Hooft large \(N_c\) limit and to ultraviolet-complete theories.

## Method

The authors survey a wide range of large \(N_c\) gauge theories in 1+1, 2+1, and 3+1 dimensions, classifying each according to whether it is confining, whether it has an explicit or emergent center symmetry, and whether there exist plausibility arguments for a Hagedorn spectrum. They compare theories known to have a Hagedorn spectrum (e.g., pure Yang-Mills, QCD with adjoint quarks) against those known not to have one (e.g., the 't Hooft model, the Veneziano limit, conformal theories) and check consistency with the presence or absence of center symmetry and confinement.

## Key numbers

- In the 't Hooft model (large \(N_c\) QCD in 1+1 dimensions with fundamental quarks), the meson spectrum obeys \(m_n^2 = g^2 \pi n\) for large integers \(n\), leading to \(N(m) \sim m^2\), a quadratic, not exponential, growth.
- For 1+1 QCD with massless adjoint quarks, the string tension vanishes as \(\sigma \sim m_q g\) (proportional to quark mass), and the spectrum does not exhibit Hagedorn behavior; with large \(N_f \gg 1\) the theory becomes effectively Abelian with a single meson mass \(m^2 = g^2 N_c N_f / \pi\).
- In the Veneziano limit (\(N_f/N_c\) fixed), meson and glueball widths scale as \(N_f/N_c\) and \((N_f/N_c)^2\) respectively, remaining of order unity, so hadron masses are not well-defined and no Hagedorn spectrum exists.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing connection between center symmetry and confinement indicators (Polyakov loop, area law for Wilson loops) to the Hagedorn spectrum, building on prior studies of the 't Hooft model ('t Hooft 1974), 1+1 QCD with adjoint quarks (Kogan 1995, Bhanot 1993, Kutasov 1993, Gross 1997), and emergent center symmetry (Cohen 2008). It enables a systematic classification of which large \(N_c\) gauge theories are expected to exhibit exponential growth in their hadron spectra, and it clarifies that the Hagedorn spectrum is not a universal feature of confinement but requires the additional condition of center symmetry.
