# The strength of the analog and Gamow-Teller giant resonances and hindrance of the 2νββ-decay rate

> An approach for describing the hindrance of the nuclear 2νββ-decay amplitude is proposed. The approach is based on a new formula obtained by a model-independent transformation of the initial expression for the amplitude. This formula takes explicitly into account the hindrance of the decay-amplitude due to the presence of the collective Gamow-Teller state. Calculations are performed within the simplest version of the approach. Calculated and experimental 2νββhalve-lives are compared for a wide range of nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the hindrance of the nuclear 2νββ-decay amplitude can be described in a model-independent way by explicitly accounting for the presence of the collective Gamow-Teller state (GTS), which exhausts about 70%–80% of the Gamow-Teller sum rule in intermediate and heavy mass nuclei; the approach bypasses the instability of QRPA calculations that are highly sensitive to the particle-particle interaction strength.

## Method

The authors transform the standard expression for the 2νββ-decay amplitude into a new formula (Eq. MG) that explicitly contains the energy of the collective Gamow-Teller state and an operator V_G^{(-)} derived from the time derivative of the transition operator. They then evaluate the Gamow-Teller amplitude using the BCS model for subsystems with strong nucleon pairing and the pair-vibration model for "magic ± two nucleons" subsystems, with the spin-orbit part of the nuclear mean field treated as the main source of Gamow-Teller strength redistribution from the GTS to its satellites.

## Key numbers

- The GTS exhausts about 70%–80% of the Gamow-Teller sum rule 3e_q^2(N−Z), where e_q ≈ 0.8 is an effective charge describing renormalization of the axial coupling constant.
- The IAS exhausts more than 95% of the Fermi sum rule N−Z.
- The strength parameter F_τ = 300 MeV fm^3 is chosen to describe experimental neutron and proton binding-energy differences for nuclei ⁴⁸Ca, ⁶⁸Ni, ¹³²Sn, ²⁰⁸Pb.
- The strength parameter F_{στ} = 255 MeV fm^3 is chosen following Migdal (1983).
- Calculated amplitudes M_GT agree with experimental amplitudes within a factor of 2–3 for all nuclei except Te isotopes.

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts by Beres (1990) and Rumyantsev (1996) that used perturbation theory to describe the hindrance of the 2νββ-decay amplitude. The approach is similar to the method originally proposed by Lane (1961) for Fermi-type excitations, which uses the approximate conservation of isospin symmetry to evaluate the small Fermi-strength of IAS satellites. The paper enables a more stable calculation of 2νββ-decay half-lives that avoids the sensitivity to the particle-particle interaction strength found in QRPA calculations by Vogel (1988) and Civitarese (1995), and suggests that the approach could be incorporated into QRPA in future work.
