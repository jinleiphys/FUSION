# Nucleon Gluon Distribution Function from 2+1+1-Flavor Lattice QCD

> The parton distribution functions (PDFs) provide process-independent information about the quarks and gluons inside hadrons. Although the gluon PDF can be obtained from a global fit to experimental data, it is not constrained well in the large-$x$ region. Theoretical gluon-PDF studies are much fewer than those of the quark PDFs. In this work, we present the first lattice-QCD results that access the $x$-dependence of the gluon unpolarized PDF of the nucleon. The lattice calculation is carried out with nucleon momenta up to 2.16 GeV, lattice spacing $a\approx0.12$ fm, with valence pion masses of 310 and 690 MeV. We use reduced Ioffe-time distributions to cancel the renormalization and implement a one-loop perturbative pseudo-PDF matching to the lightcone distribution. Our matrix element results in coordinate space are consistent with those obtained from the global PDF fits of CT18 NNLO and NNPDF3.1 NNLO. Our fitted gluon PDF extrapolated to the physical pion mass gives consistent results in the $x > 0.3$ region.

## Key claim
This work presents the first lattice-QCD calculation that accesses the $x$-dependence of the unpolarized gluon PDF of the nucleon, using the pseudo-PDF approach. The results are consistent with global fits (CT18 NNLO and NNPDF3.1 NNLO) in the $x > 0.3$ region, but the authors note that the gluon operator mixing with the quark singlet sector is neglected and that a naive extrapolation to the physical pion mass is used.

## Method
The calculation uses 2+1+1-flavor HISQ lattices with a lattice spacing of 0.12 fm and valence pion masses of 310 and 690 MeV. The gluon unpolarized PDF is extracted via the pseudo-PDF approach, using reduced Ioffe-time distributions (ITDs) to cancel renormalization, followed by a one-loop perturbative matching to the lightcone distribution. Nucleon momenta up to 2.16 GeV are used, and the ground-state matrix elements are obtained from two-state fits to two- and three-point correlators.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon momenta up to 2.16 GeV are used in the lattice calculation.
- The lattice spacing is $a \approx 0.12$ fm.
- Valence pion masses of 310 and 690 MeV are used for the calculation.
- The reduced ITDs are extrapolated to the physical pion mass of 135 MeV using a naive ansatz.
- The matching kernel uses a strong coupling $\alpha_s$ at scale $\mu$ with $N_c=3$ colors.

## Context
This work extends the pseudo-PDF methodology, previously applied to nucleon and pion valence PDFs (e.g., JLab/W&M '17, '19, '20; ETMC '20), to the gluon sector for the first time. It addresses the lack of theoretical constraints on the gluon PDF at large $x$, where experimental global fits are poorly constrained. This calculation enables future lattice studies to refine the gluon PDF using multiple lattice spacings, larger volumes, and nonperturbative renormalization to reduce systematic uncertainties.
