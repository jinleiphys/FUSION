# Rotating neutron stars with quark cores

> The rotating neutron star properties are studied with a phase transition to quark matter. The density-dependent relativistic mean-field model (DD-RMF) is employed to study the hadron matter, while the Vector-Enhanced Bag model (vBag) model is used to study the quark matter. The star matter properties like mass, radius,the moment of inertia, rotational frequency, Kerr parameter, and other important quantities are studied to see the effect on quark matter. The maximum mass of rotating neutron star with DD-LZ1 and DD-MEX parameter sets is found to be around 3$M_{\odot}$ for pure hadronic phase and decreases to a value around 2.6$M_{\odot}$ with phase transition to quark matter, which satisfies the recent GW190814 constraints. For DDV, DDVT, and DDVTD parameter sets, the maximum mass decreases to satisfy the 2$M_{\odot}$. The moment of inertia calculated for various DD-RMF parameter sets decreases with the increasing mass satisfying constraints from various measurements. Other important quantities calculated also vary with the bag constant and hence show that the presence of quarks inside neutron stars can also allow us to constraint these quantities to determine a proper EoS. Also, the theoretical study along with the accurate measurement of uniformly rotating neutron star properties may offer some valuable information concerning the high-density part of the equation of state.

# Rotating neutron stars with quark cores

## Key claim
The maximum mass of a rotating neutron star with DD-LZ1 and DD-MEX parameter sets decreases from around 3 solar masses in the pure hadronic phase to around 2.6 solar masses upon phase transition to quark matter, which satisfies the GW190814 constraint and implies that the secondary component of GW190814 could be a fast-rotating hybrid star. For the softer DDV, DDVT, and DDVTD parameter sets, the maximum mass decreases to satisfy the 2 solar mass limit.

## Method
The authors employed the density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DD-RMF) model for hadronic matter and the vector-enhanced bag (vBag) model for quark matter, using the Gibbs construction for global charge neutrality to model the hadron-quark mixed phase. Rotating neutron star properties were computed using the RNS code for stationary, axisymmetric space-times, solving Einstein's field equations for the rotating configuration. Five DD-RMF parameter sets were used: DD-LZ1, DD-MEX (stiff EoS group) and DDV, DDVT, DDVTD (soft EoS group), with three values of the effective bag constant (130, 145, and 160 MeV) for the quark phase.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass of rotating neutron star with DD-LZ1 and DD-MEX parameter sets: around 3 solar masses for pure hadronic phase, decreasing to around 2.6 solar masses with phase transition to quark matter
- For static neutron stars: DD-LZ1 maximum mass 2.55 solar masses with radius 12.30 km; DD-MEX maximum mass 2.57 solar masses with radius 12.46 km
- Static neutron star maximum masses for soft EoS sets: DDV 1.95 solar masses, DDVT 1.93 solar masses, DDVTD 1.85 solar masses
- Radii at canonical mass (1.4 solar masses): DDV 12.11 km, DDVT 11.40 km, DDVTD 11.33 km
- Mixed-phase density ranges for DD-LZ1: 2.56-4.23 rho_0 at B_eff^(1/4)=130 MeV; 2.73-4.95 rho_0 at 145 MeV; 3.04-5.43 rho_0 at 160 MeV
- For the generic DD-RMF parameter sets (not DD-LZ1/DD-MEX), mixed-phase starts at 2.47 rho_0 and extends to 4.03 rho_0 at B_eff^(1/4)=130 MeV

## Context
This work extends studies of hybrid stars with quark cores by using recent DD-RMF parameter sets (DD-LZ1, DD-MEX, DDV, DDVT, DDVTD) combined with the vBag model, building on prior work by Klaehn (2015) on the vBag model and by various groups on rotating neutron star properties since the late 1990s. The results address the nature of the secondary component of the GW190814 event observed by LIGO/Virgo (Abbott 2020), suggesting it could be a rapidly rotating hybrid star, and also satisfy constraints from pulsar mass measurements of PSR J1614-2230 (Demorest 2010), PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis 2013), and MSP J0740+6620 (Cromartie 2020). The study enables future constraints on the high-density equation of state through combined measurements of rotating neutron star properties.
