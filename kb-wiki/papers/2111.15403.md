# Quadrupole-octupole coupling and the evolution of collectivity in neutron-deficient Xe, Ba, Ce, and Nd isotopes

> The evolution of quadrupole and octupole collectivity in neutron-deficient Xe, Ba, Ce, and Nd nuclei near the "octupole magic" neutron number $N=56$ is investigated within the mapped $sdf$-IBM framework. Microscopic input is obtained via quadrupole and octupole constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations, based on the parametrization D1M of the Gogny energy density functional. Octupole-deformed mean-field ground states are predicted for Ba and Ce isotopes near $N=56$. Excitation energies of positive- and negative-parity states as well as electric transition rates are computed with wave functions resulting from the diagonalization of the mapped IBM Hamiltonian. The parameters of the Hamiltonian are determined via the mapping of the mean-field potential energy surfaces onto the expectation value of the Hamiltonian in the condensate state of the $s$, $d$, and $f$ bosons. Enhanced octupolarity is predicted for Xe, Ba, and Ce isotopes near $N=56$. The shape/phase transition from octupole-deformed to strongly quadrupole-deformed near $N=60$ is analyzed in detail.

## Key claim

The paper predicts enhanced octupole collectivity in neutron-deficient Xe, Ba, and Ce isotopes near the "octupole magic" neutron number N=56, but the claim is hedged by the fact that the mapped sdf-IBM framework does not include neutron-proton pairs, which are expected to be important in these N≈Z nuclei and may affect the quantitative predictions.

## Method

The authors performed axially-symmetric quadrupole (β₂) and octupole (β₃) constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny-D1M energy density functional to obtain mean-field potential energy surfaces for Xe, Ba, Ce, and Nd isotopes. These surfaces were then mapped onto the expectation value of an sdf-interacting boson model (IBM) Hamiltonian in a boson condensate state, with the Hamiltonian parameters determined by reproducing the topology of the mean-field surfaces near the global minimum. The resulting IBM Hamiltonian was diagonalized using the ARBMODEL code to compute excitation energies and electric transition rates.

## Key numbers

- Octupole-deformed mean-field ground states are predicted for ¹¹⁰Ba, ¹¹²Ba, ¹¹⁴Ba, and ¹¹⁴Ce near N=56
- The quadrupole-quadrupole boson interaction strength κ₂ ≈ -0.1 MeV was obtained from the mapping procedure
- The E2 boson charge e₂ = 0.107 e·b was fixed to reproduce experimental B(E2;2⁺₁→0⁺₁) rates for Xe nuclei
- The E1 and E3 boson effective charges were adopted as e₁ = 0.01 e·b¹/² and e₃ = 0.12 e·b³/² from previous studies
- The doubly-magic nucleus ¹⁰⁰Sn is taken as the inert core, giving boson number n = (A-100)/2 for each nucleus

## Context

This work extends the mapped sdf-IBM approach, previously applied to light actinides and rare-earth nuclei (Nomura 2013, 2014) and to neutron-rich Xe, Ba, Ce, and Nd isotopes (Nomura 2021), to the less-studied N≈Z≈56 region where octupole correlations are expected but experimental data are limited. The calculations complement earlier mean-field studies by Heenen (1994) and Cao (2020) by providing spectroscopic predictions beyond the mean-field level, and enable comparison with future experimental measurements using radioactive-ion beams.
