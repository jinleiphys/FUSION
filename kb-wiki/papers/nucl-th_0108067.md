# On the D-wave state component of the deuteron in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of light nuclei

> The D-wave state component of the neutron-proton bound state in the deuteron is calculated in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of light nuclei - the relativistically covariant quantum field theoretic approach to the description of low-energy nuclear forces. The theoretical value of the fraction of the D-wave state relative to the S-wave state is equal to eta_d = 0.0238. This agrees well with the phenomenological value eta_d = 0.0256(4) quoted by Kamionkowski and Bahcall (ApJ. 420, 884 (1994)).

## Key claim

The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of light nuclei, at leading order in the large \(N_C\) expansion and neglecting the deuteron binding energy relative to the nucleon mass, yields a D-wave to S-wave ratio \(\eta_d = 0.0238\), which agrees with the phenomenological value \(\eta_d = 0.0256 \pm 0.0004\) used by Kamionkowski and Bahcall (1994).

## Method

The authors used the nuclear Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NNJL) model, a relativistically covariant quantum field theory, to compute the amplitude for the transition n + p → D. They decomposed this amplitude into S-wave and D-wave neutron-proton densities using a relativistically covariant partial-wave analysis developed by Anisovich et al. (1998) for nucleon-nucleon scattering. The fraction \(\eta_d\) was derived from the relation between the vector (\(g_V\)) and tensor (\(g_T\)) coupling constants, which are connected at leading order in the large \(N_C\) expansion by \(g_T = \sqrt{3/8}\,g_V\), with \(g_V\) fixed by the deuteron electric quadrupole moment \(Q_D = 0.286\,\text{fm}^2\).

## Key numbers

- The theoretical D-wave to S-wave ratio for the deuteron is \(\eta_d = 0.0238\).
- The phenomenological value quoted by Kamionkowski and Bahcall (1994) is \(\eta_d = 0.0256 \pm 0.0004\).
- The deuteron electric quadrupole moment used to fix the vector coupling constant is \(Q_D = 0.286\,\text{fm}^2\).
- The nucleon mass used is \(M_N = 940\,\text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the NNJL model, previously applied to the \(\Delta\Delta\) component of the deuteron (Ivanov et al., 1998) and to low-energy electromagnetic and weak reactions, by calculating the D-wave component of the deuteron wave function. The result agrees with the phenomenological parameterization used by Kamionkowski and Bahcall (1994) for the astrophysical factor \(S_{\text{pp}}(0)\) in solar proton burning, and demonstrates that the NNJL model can describe low-energy tensor nuclear forces. This enables further applications of the NNJL model to nuclear structure and reactions where the D-wave component is relevant.
