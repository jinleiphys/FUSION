# Precise measurement of $α_K$ and $α_T$ for the 39.8-keV $E$3 transition in $^{103}$Rh: Test of internal-conversion theory

> Neutron-activated sources of $^{103}$Ru and $^{103}$Pd both share the isomeric first-excited state in $^{103}$Rh as a daughter product. From independent measurements of both decays, we have measured the $K$-shell and total internal conversion coefficients, $α_K$ and $α_T$, for the 39.8-keV $E$3 transition, which de-excites that state in $^{103}$Rh, to be 141.1(23) and 1428(13), respectively. When compared with Dirac-Fock calculations, our new results disagree with the version of the theory that ignores the $K$-shell atomic vacancy, which is consistent with our conclusion drawn from a series of measurements on high multipolarity transitions in nuclei with higher $Z$. Calculations that include the atomic vacancy indicate that the transition actually has a small $M$4 component with mixing ratio $δ$ = 0.023(5).

## Key claim

The measured K-shell and total internal conversion coefficients for the 39.8-keV E3 transition in 103Rh disagree with Dirac-Fock calculations that ignore the atomic vacancy left by the emitted electron, but agree with calculations that include the vacancy; the data also indicate a small M4 admixture with mixing ratio δ = 0.023(5).

## Method

The authors neutron-activated natural ruthenium and palladium targets to produce 103Ru and 103Pd sources, both of which decay to the same isomeric first-excited state in 103Rh. They recorded gamma-ray and K x-ray spectra with a precisely calibrated HPGe detector, extracted peak areas using least-squares fitting, and applied corrections for impurities, ruthenium fluorescence, Lorentzian line shapes, and self-attenuation. For the 103Ru decay, αK was obtained from the ratio of K x-ray to gamma-ray intensities using the known K-shell fluorescence yield, while αT was derived from the intensity balance of transitions feeding and depopulating the 39.8-keV level; the 103Pd decay provided a consistency check via a relation linking αK and αT.

## Key numbers

- αK for the 39.8-keV E3 transition in 103Rh: 141.1(23)
- αT for the 39.8-keV E3 transition in 103Rh: 1428(13)
- Mixing ratio δ (M4 component in the E3 transition): 0.023(5)
- K-shell fluorescence yield for rhodium (Z=45): 0.809(4), taken from the evaluated compilation of Schötzig (1996)
- Efficiency ratio εγ39.8/εK20.6 used in the αK determination: 1.118(18)
- Relative gamma-ray intensity of the 39.8-keV transition (normalized to Iγ497.1 = 100): 0.0752(12)
- Probability of K-shell electron capture in 103Pd decay: 0.8595(10)

## Context

This work is the ninth in a systematic series by the Texas A&M group measuring K-shell internal conversion coefficients for E3 and M4 transitions with ±2% or better precision, covering nuclei from Z=45 up to Z=78. The results support the version of Dirac-Fock theory that includes the atomic vacancy created by the emitted conversion electron, consistent with the group's earlier findings for higher-Z nuclei such as those reported in Nica et al. (2004, 2005, 2007, 2008, 2009, 2014, 2016, 2017a, 2017b) and Hardy et al. (2014). The measurement resolves longstanding discrepancies among six prior αK measurements (Lévy 1969, Pénétra 1970, Czyżewski 1975, Macias 1976, Van Hise 1979, Sánchez 1999) and two prior αT measurements (Czyżewski 1975, Van Hise 1979), none of which had sufficient precision or accounted for the near-coincidence of the 39.8-keV gamma ray with twice the rhodium Kα x-ray energy. The result enables a more stringent test of internal-conversion theory at low atomic number and provides precise input for nuclear structure evaluations of 103Rh.
