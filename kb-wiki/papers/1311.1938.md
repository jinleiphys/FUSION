# Electromagnetic excitation of nuclei and neutron evaporation in ultrarelativistic ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions

> We present a new approach for calculating electromagnetic excitation of nuclei as well as probabilities of emission and distributions of neutrons from decays of excited nuclear systems for ultrarelativistic, ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions. Excitation functions for $γ$ + Pb $\to$ Pb$^*$ are parametrized using physics-motivated components: excitation of giant resonances, quasi-deuteron absorption mechanism, excitation of nucleon resonances as well as high-energy dissociation of protons and neutrons. Neutron emission (up to 10 neutrons) from low-energy excitations of $^{208}$Pb is modelled in terms of the Hauser-Feshbach formalism. The probabilities of a given number of neutrons are calculated as a function of excitation energy in a Monte-Carlo code GEMINI. These functions are parametrized by smooth analytical functions. The results are compared to appropriate data for the $γ$ Pb $\to$ Pb$^*$ $\to$ k n reaction. As an example the approach is used for calculating electromagnetic excitation in UPC processes. Both single photon and double photon excitations are included and discussed. Topological cross sections with a given number of neutrons in forward and backward directions are calculated in the calculation at the LHC energies. Excitation functions are presented. The results of calculation are compared with SPS and more recent ALICE experimental data and good agreement is achieved.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new parametrization of photoabsorption on lead nuclei and neutron evaporation probabilities that achieves good agreement with world data on photonuclear reactions and with ALICE data on ultraperipheral collisions, but the authors note that the Hauser-Feshbach formalism assumes full equilibration of the nuclear system and that the agreement would be much worse if pre-equilibrium processes were included.

## Method

The authors parametrize the total photoabsorption cross section for gamma + Pb using four physics-motivated components: giant dipole resonance (GDR), quasi-deuteron absorption, nucleon resonances (primarily the Delta resonance), and high-energy nucleon dissociation via pomeron and reggeon exchange. Neutron emission probabilities for up to 10 neutrons from excited 208Pb are calculated using the Hauser-Feshbach formalism implemented in the Monte Carlo code GEMINI++, with the resulting probabilities parametrized as smooth analytical functions of excitation energy. These ingredients are combined with the Weizsacker-Williams photon flux to compute impact-parameter-dependent excitation probabilities and topological cross sections for single-photon, double-photon, and mutual excitations in ultraperipheral Pb-Pb collisions.

## Key numbers

* GDR parameters for 208Pb: resonance energy E_r = 13.373 MeV, width Gamma_r = 3.938 MeV, strength S_r = 1.33716, with sigma_TRK = 60*(NZ/A) mb MeV
* Gaussian parametrization of Delta resonance region: C_G = 23 barn MeV, sigma_G = 110 MeV, mu_G = 350 MeV
* High-energy photoabsorption cross section (E > 8 GeV): sigma_gammaA = (15.2 + 0.06 ln^2(E/omega_0)) mb with omega_0 = 80 GeV
* Single-nucleus single-photon excitation cross sections at gamma_cm = 3100 (LHC): (6-40) MeV region = 133.8 b, (40-2000) MeV region = 54.6 b, (2-80) GeV region = 18.7 b
* Single-nucleus single-photon cross sections at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV: 1 neutron = 82.888 b, 2 neutrons = 18.532 b, 3 neutrons = 2.856 b (with exponential factor)
* Ratio 2n/1n = 22%, in good agreement with ALICE result of 22.5 +/- 0.5 stat +/- 0.9 syst %
* Total mutual excitation cross section at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV: 2.02091 b (with exponential factor)
* Double-photon excitation cross section for one nucleus at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV: 8.823 b total

## Context

This work extends the approach of Vidovic et al. (1993) for electromagnetic excitation in ultraperipheral collisions by incorporating a multicomponent photoabsorption parametrization and Hauser-Feshbach neutron evaporation calculations from GEMINI++. The results are compared with experimental data from SPS (Golubeva et al., 2005) and ALICE (2012), and with calculations by Pshenichnov et al. (2011). The formalism enables calculation of topological cross sections for neutron multiplicities that can be used as input for triggering and background studies in exclusive processes such as AA -> AA J/Psi, AA -> AA rho^0, and AA -> AA lepton pairs at LHC energies.
