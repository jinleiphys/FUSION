# Semiclassical transport of hadrons with dynamical spectral functions in A+A collisions at SIS/AGS energies

> The transport of hadrons with dynamical spectral functions A_h(X,\vec{P},M^2) is studied for nucleus-nucleus collisions at SIS and AGS energies in comparison to the conventional quasi-particle limit and the available experimental data within the recently developed off-shell HSD transport approach. Similar to reactions at GANIL energies the off-shell effects show up in high momentum tails of the particle spectra, however, at SIS and AGS energies these modifications are found to be less pronounced than at lower energies due to the high lying excitations of the nucleons in the collision zone.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that off-shell transport effects in nucleus-nucleus collisions at SIS and AGS energies are less pronounced than at lower GANIL energies, contrary to what might be expected from simply scaling up the collision energy, because the high-lying excitations of nucleons in the collision zone suppress the off-shell modifications in high-momentum tails of particle spectra.

## Method

The authors extended the semiclassical HSD (Hadron-String-Dynamics) transport approach, derived from the Kadanoff-Baym equations, to include propagation of hadrons with dynamical spectral functions A_h(X,P,M^2) and momentum-dependent self energies. They implemented a test-particle representation with equations of motion for position, momentum, and invariant mass squared, and specified collision terms for binary reactions using Breit-Wigner cross sections with total widths that include both vacuum decay width and collisional broadening. The approach was applied to nucleus-nucleus collisions at SIS (approximately 1-2 A GeV) and AGS (approximately 10-15 A GeV) energies and compared to the conventional quasi-particle limit and experimental data.

## Key numbers

- At SIS and AGS energies, off-shell modifications to particle spectra are "less pronounced" than at GANIL energies (approximately 100 A MeV), due to high-lying nucleon excitations in the collision zone.
- The test-particle simulations used a Woods-Saxon potential with parameters R = 5 fm and a_0 = 0.6 fm, with test particles initialized at energy P_0 = 2.0 GeV and mean mass 1.0 GeV.
- The momentum-dependent trial potential used cutoff parameters Lambda_V = Lambda_W = 2.0 GeV, with strengths C_W = 0.6 GeV^2 for the imaginary part and C_V = -0.3 GeV^2 for the real part.
- The correction factor (1-C_(i))^(-1) in the equations of motion was found to produce differences in test-particle momenta that "cannot be distinguished within the resolution of Fig. 3."

## Context

This work extends the off-shell HSD transport approach previously developed by the same authors (Cass99) for GANIL energies to the higher energy regimes of SIS and AGS, where the approach is compared to conventional on-shell transport models such as BUU/VUU (Bertsch, CMMN, Niita, Wolf90), QMD (Aich, T1, T2), UrQMD (URQMD), ART (ART), ARC (Kah2), and HSD (CB99, Ehehalt). The study enables future investigations of whether off-shell effects become important again at even higher energies (e.g., SPS or RHIC) or whether the suppression observed here persists, and it highlights the need for fully microscopic off-shell matrix elements to replace the ad-hoc assumptions used for off-shell scattering cross sections.
