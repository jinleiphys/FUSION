# On the microscopic origin of the Interacting Boson Model

> A microscopic interpretation of the s and d bosons of the Interacting Boson Model is being suggested: the s, d bosons can be interpreted as symmetric pairs of harmonic oscillator quanta of the valence nuclear shell. Within this interpretation, the SU(3) limit of the Interacting Boson Model results to the Elliott SU(3) symmetry.

## Key claim

The s and d bosons of the Interacting Boson Model can be interpreted as symmetric pairs of harmonic oscillator quanta from the valence nuclear shell, with no approximations or special circumstances needed because quanta are bosons and pairs of quanta are also bosons. This interpretation directly connects the SU(3) limit of the Interacting Boson Model to the Elliott SU(3) symmetry of the Shell Model, addressing a long-standing gap in the microscopic justification of the IBM for deformed nuclei.

## Method

The author constructs the spatial U(3) algebra of a valence shell using spherical tensor operators u_m^dagger of degree l=1, which create harmonic oscillator quanta with angular momentum l=1 and projection m = -1,0,+1 when acting on the vacuum state. By showing that there exist exactly six types of cartesian symmetrized pairs of such quanta, which transform into spherical tensors of degree 0 and 2, the author identifies these as the s and d bosons of the Interacting Boson Model. The work uses the LS coupling scheme to separate the spatial part of the nuclear wave function from the spin-isospin part, and employs the proxy-SU(3) symmetry to handle spin-orbit split shells where harmonic oscillator shells with N and N+1 quanta mix.

## Key numbers

- The s,d nuclear shell lies among the harmonic oscillator magic numbers 8-20 and contains orbitals with N=2 number of quanta, with Omega = 6 spatial harmonic oscillator eigenstates.
- The U(3) irrep for two protons in the s,d shell occupying the same cartesian orbital is [4,0,0], giving the SU(3) irrep (lambda,mu) = (4,0).
- The spin-orbit splitting leads to shells among proton or neutron numbers 6-14, 14-28, 28-50, 50-82, 82-126, 126-182.
- The harmonic oscillator shells with N = 0,1,2,3,4,5,6 quanta lie among magic numbers 0-2, 2-8, 8-20, 20-40, 40-70, 70-112, 112-168 respectively.

## Context

This work extends the Elliott SU(3) symmetry (Elliott, 1958-1968) and the Interacting Boson Model of Arima and Iachello (1975-1979) by providing a microscopic interpretation of the s and d bosons that does not rely on the seniority-based Otsuka-Arima-Iachello mapping (Otsuka, 1978), which works for vibrational and gamma-unstable nuclei but not for deformed nuclei. The approach also differs from the Bonatsos-Klein-Li mapping (Bonatsos, 1984-1987) and the Ginocchio model (Ginocchio, 1979), which either require truncation in the boson space or are limited to specific single-j shells. This interpretation enables a parameter-free connection between the Shell Model SU(3) symmetry and the IBM SU(3) limit for deformed nuclei, and can be applied within the proxy-SU(3) scheme (Bonatsos, 2021) to handle realistic spin-orbit split shells.
