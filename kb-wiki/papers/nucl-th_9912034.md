# New Result for the Pion-Nucleon Sigma Term from an Updated VPI/GW Pion-Nucleon Partial-Wave and Dispersion Relation Analysis

> A new result for the nucleon sigma term from an updated pion-nucleon partial-wave and dispersion relation analysis of the Virginia Polytechnic Institute (now George Washington University) group is discussed. Using a method similar to that of Gasser, Leutwyler, Locher, and Sainio, we obtain Sigma=90 +/- 8 MeV (preliminary), in disagreement with the canonical result 64 +/- 8 MeV, but consistent with expectations based on new information on the $π$NN coupling constant, pionic atoms, and the Delta resonance width.

# New Result for the Pion-Nucleon Sigma Term from an Updated VPI/GW Pion-Nucleon Partial-Wave and Dispersion Relation Analysis

## Key claim

The pion-nucleon sigma term is found to be 90 ± 8 MeV (preliminary), which is about 27 MeV larger than the canonical result of 64 ± 8 MeV, but this large increase is expected based on new information on the πNN coupling constant, pionic atom data, and the Delta resonance width.

## Method

The authors performed an updated partial-wave analysis (solution "SM99") of pion-nucleon scattering data up to 2 GeV, constrained by forward C±(ω) and derivative E±(ω) dispersion relations, as well as fixed-t B±(ν,t) and C±(ν,t) dispersion relations satisfied to within ~1% up to ~800 MeV. The subthreshold coefficients d̄+00 and d̄+01 were extracted using the Gasser-Leutwyler-Locher-Sainio (GLLS) method from the forward C+ and E+ dispersion relations, with the πNN coupling constant f² and p-wave scattering volume a+1+ treated as free parameters determined by fit to data, while s-wave scattering lengths were taken from PSI pionic hydrogen results. A second independent determination of Σd came from the slope of fixed-t C+ dispersion relation subtraction constants as a function of momentum transfer t at t=0.

## Key numbers

- Sigma term Σ = 90 ± 8 MeV (preliminary), from the GLLS method using solution SM99
- Σd = 78 MeV from the GLLS method, compared to the canonical Σd ≃ 50 MeV from the Karlsruhe KA84 solution
- πNN coupling constant f² = 0.0759 ± 0.0004 (g²/4π = 13.72 ± 0.07), about 5% below the canonical value 0.079 used in KH80 and KA84
- Subthreshold coefficients from GLLS method: d̄+00 = -1.27 ± 0.03, d̄+01 = 1.27 ± 0.03 mπ⁻¹
- From the tangent of C̄+(0,t) subtraction constants: d̄+00 = -1.15 ± 0.03, d̄+01 = 1.23 ± 0.03, yielding Σd = 80 MeV
- ΔD = 12 MeV used as the O(t²) correction, taken from Gasser et al. (1988) and Sainio (1997)
- χ²/data point for SM99: (2, 2, 2.5) for (π⁺p, π⁻p, charge exchange), compared to (4, 5, 3.5) for KA84

## Context

This work extends the dispersion relation approach of Gasser, Leutwyler, Locher, and Sainio (1988) and Sainio (1997), who validated the method using the Karlsruhe KH80 and KA84 phase shifts but obtained Σ ≃ 62 MeV consistent with the canonical result of Koch (1982). The new result contradicts the canonical value 64 ± 8 MeV and is the first independent dispersion-relation-based sigma term analysis since the Karlsruhe group's work. The authors urge a new analysis based on the Karlsruhe methods applied to modern data to check these findings, and plan further study to explore systematic uncertainties and resolve small inconsistencies between the two extraction methods.
