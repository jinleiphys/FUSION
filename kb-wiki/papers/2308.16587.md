# Un-screened forces in Quark-Gluon Plasma?

> We study the correlator of temporal Wilson lines at non-zero temperature in 2+1 flavor lattice QCD with the aim to define the heavy quark-antiquark potential at non-zero temperature. For temperatures $153~{\rm MeV} \leq T \leq 352~{\rm MeV}$ the spectral representation of this correlator is consistent with a broadened peak in the spectral function, position or width of which then defines the real or imaginary parts of the heavy quark-antiquark potential at non-zero temperature, respectively. We find that the potential's real part is not screened contrary to the widely-held expectations. We comment on how this fact may modify the picture of quarkonium melting in the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

Contrary to the widely-held expectation that color screening in the quark-gluon plasma makes the heavy quark-antiquark interaction short-ranged, the real part of the heavy quark-antiquark potential is not screened for temperatures between 153 MeV and 352 MeV, as determined from lattice QCD calculations of temporal Wilson line correlators.

## Method

The authors performed 2+1 flavor lattice QCD calculations using the highly improved staggered quark (HISQ) action and Lüscher-Weisz gauge action on lattices with temporal extent Nτ = 16–36, corresponding to temperatures 153 MeV ≤ T ≤ 352 MeV, at three lattice spacings (a = 0.0280, 0.0404, and 0.0493 fm). They computed correlators of temporal Wilson lines in Coulomb gauge, applied gradient flow for noise reduction, and extracted the spectral function by fitting a model consisting of a cut Lorentzian peak (representing the dominant in-medium state) plus a low-energy contribution, with the high-energy part fixed from zero-temperature calculations.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: 153 MeV ≤ T ≤ 352 MeV
- Lattice spacings used: a = 0.0280 fm (β = 8.249), a = 0.0404 fm (β = 7.825), a = 0.0493 fm (β = 7.596)
- Light quark mass corresponding to mπ = 161 MeV in the continuum limit (for ml = ms/20)
- The low-energy contribution cr^low(T)/Ar(T) was found to be less than 5 × 10^{-4}
- For T > 500 MeV and rT < 0.4, the weak-coupling thermal correction to the real part of the potential is smaller than 0.1 T

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of the complex heavy quark-antiquark potential in quenched QCD (Rothkopf 2011, Bala 2019) and in 2+1 flavor QCD (Burnier 2015, Bala 2021), which used coarser lattices (Nτ = 12) and a single lattice spacing per temperature. The present results supersede earlier conclusions from Bayesian reconstruction methods applied to older lattice data (Burnier 2014) that had larger statistical errors and discretization artifacts. The finding that the real part is unscreened agrees qualitatively with weak-coupling effective field theory predictions (Brambilla 2008) for rT < 1, and the large imaginary part suggests that quarkonium melting occurs via rapid damping of the chromo-electric force rather than through static screening, challenging the original picture proposed by Matsui and Satz (1986).
