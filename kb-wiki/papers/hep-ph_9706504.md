# Signatures of Absorption Mechanisms for J/psi and psi' Production in High-Energy Heavy-Ion Collisions

> J/psi and psi' produced in high-energy heavy-ion collisions are absorbed by their collisions with nucleons and produced soft particles, leading to two distinct absorption mechanisms. The signature of absorption by produced soft particles, as revealed by psi' production data, consists of a gap and a change of the slope in going from the pA line to the nucleus-nucleus line when we make a semi-log plot of the survival probability as a function of the path length. Using this signature, we find from the J/psi production data in pA, O-Cu, O-U, and S-U collisions that the degree of J/psi absorption by produced soft particles is small and cannot account for the J/psi data in Pb-Pb collisions. The anomalous suppression of J/psi production in Pb-Pb collisions can be explained as due to the occurrence of a new phase of strong J/psi absorption, which sets in when the local energy density exceeds about 3.4 GeV/fm^3. To probe the chemical content of the new phase, we propose to study the abundance of open-charm mesons and charm hyperons which depends sensitively on the quark chemical potential.

## Key claim

The anomalous suppression of J/psi production in Pb-Pb collisions cannot be explained by absorption by nucleons or produced soft particles as constrained by pA, O-Cu, O-U, and S-U data; it requires a new phase of strong J/psi absorption that sets in when the local energy density exceeds about 3.4 GeV/fm^3, which may be the quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The author analyzes experimental J/psi and psi' production cross sections from NA3, NA38, NA51, and NA50 collaborations, plotting survival probabilities as functions of path length in semi-log plots to identify distinct absorption signatures. A microscopic absorption model using a row-on-row picture with straight-line space-time trajectories is employed, where the survival probability depends on collisions with nucleons (hard component) and produced soft particles (soft component), with rate constants extracted from fits to the data.

## Key numbers

- J/psi-nucleon absorption cross section from fits: 6.94 mb (no soft absorption) or 6.36 mb (with soft absorption)
- J/psi-gluon rate constant from fits: 0.049 c/fm (corresponding to J/psi-gluon cross section about 0.3 mb)
- J/psi-hadron rate constant from fits: 0.096 c/fm (corresponding to J/psi-hadron cross section about 0.68 mb)
- psi'-gluon and psi'-hadron rate constants from fits: 3 c/fm (corresponding to cross sections about 20 mb)
- Threshold number of baryon-baryon collisions for new phase: N_c = 4
- New phase rate constant: k_psi_x = 1 c/fm
- Threshold local energy density: about 3.4 GeV/fm^3
- Lattice QCD quark-gluon plasma energy density comparison: about 4.2 GeV/fm^3 with T_c about 0.2 GeV
- For mu_u = mu_d = 400 MeV at T = 200 MeV: u and d quark densities about 6 times s and anti-s densities, which are about 6 times anti-u and anti-d densities

## Context

This work extends the Gerschel and Hufner (1988) approach of separating hard absorption components via pA lines, and addresses the debate following the NA50 observation of a possible discontinuity in J/psi absorption in Pb-Pb collisions. The author argues against the comover absorption model proposed by Gavin (1996), Capella (1996), and Cassing (1996), instead supporting the quark-gluon plasma interpretation suggested by Wong (1996), Kharzeev (1996), and Blaizot (1996). The proposed measurement of open-charm meson and charm hyperon abundances provides a new experimental signature to probe the chemical content of the absorbing phase.
