# Study of a background reconstruction method for the measurement of D-meson azimuthal angular correlations

> We study experimental background reconstruction methods for the measurement of $D-\overline{D}$ correlation using a PYTHIA simulation. Like-sign and side-band background methods that are widely used in the experimental measurements of single $D$-meson production yields were deployed for the correlation background study. It is found that the like-sign method which well describes the combinatorial background of single $D^{0}$ meson yields fails to reproduce the correlated background in the $D^{0}-\overline{D^{0}}$ correlation measurement, while the side-band background method yields a good description of the background for both the single $D^0$ yields and of the correlated background of the $D^{0}-\overline{D^{0}}$ correlation measurement.

# Study of a background reconstruction method for the measurement of D-meson azimuthal angular correlations

## Key claim
The Like-Sign background method, which works well for reconstructing single D⁰ meson yields, fails to reproduce the correlated background in D⁰–D̅⁰ azimuthal correlation measurements because it introduces residual correlations from local or global charge conservation in the underlying event. The Side-Band method, however, yields a good description of the correlated background across the signal-to-background ratio regions investigated.

## Method
The authors used the PYTHIA 8.168 Monte Carlo event generator to simulate p+p collisions at √s = 500 GeV, with parameters tuned to reproduce STAR experimental data on inclusive c̅c production cross-section. They reconstructed D⁰ mesons by pairing kaon and pion candidates via the hadronic decay channel D⁰ → K⁻π⁺ (and charge conjugate), combining all kaons and pions at midrapidity (|η| ≤ 1) without distinguishing secondary decay vertices. They then compared the D⁰–D̅⁰ azimuthal correlation signals obtained using Like-Sign and Side-Band background subtraction methods against the true correlation signals directly from PYTHIA, and quantified the goodness of fit using relative difference estimators ΔP and ΔE.

## Key numbers
- PYTHIA simulation of p+p collisions at √s = 500 GeV with six billion minimum bias events generated
- Strong coupling constant αs = 0.15 and minimum invariant pT threshold for hard QCD processes = 1.5 GeV/c used in the PYTHIA tune
- Trigger and associated D-meson pT cuts set to > 1.0 GeV/c for the correlation analysis shown in Fig. 3
- Signal-to-background ratio of approximately 0.3 used for the correlation comparisons in Fig. 3
- Mis-identification effect from particle identification criteria evaluated via toy Monte Carlo simulation found to be less than 1%

## Context
This work extends studies of heavy flavor azimuthal correlations as a probe of charm-medium interaction dynamics in QCD, following theoretical predictions that radiative versus collisional energy loss affect angular correlations differently (intro3, intro4, 2018). The Like-Sign and Side-Band methods were previously deployed by experimentalists for single D-meson yield measurements (intro7, intro10), but this paper demonstrates their differing validity for correlation measurements. The results provide direct references for the upcoming sPHENIX experiment at RHIC, which will measure D–D̅ azimuthal correlations in p+p and Au+Au collisions through fully reconstructed D-meson hadronic decays.
