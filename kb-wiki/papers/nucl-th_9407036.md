# Flow of Nucleons and Fragments in 40Ar + 27Al Collisions Studied with AMD

> Collective transverse momentum flow of nucleons and fragments in intermediate energy 40Ar + 27Al collisions is calculated with the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD). The observed flow and its balance energy are reproduced very well by the calculation with the Gogny force which corresponds to the soft EOS of the nuclear matter. Especially the calculated absolute value of the fragment flow is larger than that of the nucleon flow in the negative flow region, which can be explained by the existence of two components of flow. In addition to many similarities, difference in the deuteron flow is found between 12C + 12C and 40Ar + 27Al collisions, and its origin is investigated by paying attention to the production mechanism of light fragments. We also investigate the dependence of the flow of nucleons and fragments on the stochastic collision cross section and the effective interaction, and conclude that the stiff EOS without momentum dependence of the mean field is not consistent to the experimental data.

## Key claim

The antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculation with the Gogny force (soft equation of state, momentum-dependent mean field) reproduces the observed collective transverse momentum flow and its balance energy in 40Ar + 27Al collisions, but the stiff equation of state without momentum dependence (SKG2 force) is inconsistent with the experimental data.

## Method

AMD simulations were performed for 40Ar + 27Al collisions at incident energies from 25 to 135 MeV/nucleon with a fixed impact parameter of 3 fm, using a Slater determinant of Gaussian wave packets with time evolution determined by the time-dependent variational principle and stochastic two-nucleon collisions. The flow was calculated using the definition ⟨wPx/A⟩ = Σk Ak sign(Pkz)Pkx/Ak / Σk Ak, and results were compared for two effective interactions: the Gogny force (incompressibility K = 228 MeV, soft EOS) and a Skyrme-type SKG2 force (incompressibility K = 373 MeV, stiff EOS, no momentum dependence). Statistical decays of excited fragments were treated with a statistical cascade code after the dynamical stage (t_sw = 150 fm/c, or 225 fm/c for energies below 30 MeV/nucleon).

## Key numbers

- Balance energy for nucleon flow: about 85 MeV/nucleon (for 40Ar + 27Al, b = 3 fm, Gogny force, 1.0σ cross section)
- Gogny force incompressibility: K = 228 MeV
- SKG2 force incompressibility: K = 373 MeV
- Incident energy range studied: 25 MeV ≤ E/A ≤ 135 MeV
- Gaussian width parameter: ν = 0.16 fm⁻²
- Ground state binding energy of 40Ar with Gogny force: 335 MeV (experimental: 344 MeV)
- Ground state binding energy of 27Al with Gogny force: 220 MeV (experimental: 225 MeV)
- Ground state deformation β of 27Al with Gogny force: 0.40 (prolate)
- At 45 MeV/nucleon, about 30% of dynamically produced deuterons in 40Ar + 27Al are composed of nucleons from different initial nuclei (P_PT ≈ 30%), compared to about 15% in 12C + 12C collisions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous AMD study of 12C + 12C collisions (Ono and Horiuchi, 1994) to a heavier system (40Ar + 27Al), finding that the fragment flow magnitude increases with fragment mass number in the negative flow region, a feature also observed experimentally in 40Ar + 45Sc collisions (Westfall et al.). The two-component interpretation (dynamically emitted nucleons with small flow vs. excited fragments with larger flow) explains the mass-number dependence. The study enables systematic determination of the nuclear equation of state by comparing fragment and nucleon flows across different systems, and shows that the stiff EOS without momentum dependence (similar to Skyrme VII) is ruled out by the data.
