# Regge Behavior of DIS Structure Functions

> Building on previous works of the mid 1960's, we construct an integral equation for forward elastic scattering (t=0) at arbitrary virtuality Q^2 and large s=W^2. This equation sums the ladder production of massless intermediate bosons to all orders, and the solution exhibits Regge behavior. The equation is used to study scattering in a simple chi^2 phi scalar theory, where it is solved appoximately and applied to the study of DIS at small x. We find that the model can naturally describe the quark distribution in both the large x region and the small x region dominated by Reggeon exchange.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an integral equation for forward elastic scattering at arbitrary virtuality \(Q^2\) and large \(s=W^2\) that sums ladder production of massless intermediate bosons to all orders, and shows that the solution exhibits Regge behavior; however, the model is limited to a simple scalar \(\chi^\dagger\chi\phi\) theory with a small coupling constant, and the \(Q^2\) evolution of structure functions is not discussed because \(Q^2\) is fixed at a large value.

## Method

The authors derived an integral equation for the imaginary part of the forward photon-quark scattering amplitude in a scalar \(\chi^\dagger\chi\phi\) theory, summing ladder diagrams to all orders in the \(t\)-channel. They solved this equation approximately in the small Bjorken \(x\) limit by assuming a Regge-like ansatz and imposing boundary conditions on the solution, then matched the Regge contribution to the pole term to obtain a solution valid for all \(x\). The structure functions for deep inelastic scattering (DIS) from a scalar bound state were then computed by convolving the off-shell quark scattering amplitude with a covariant bound state vertex function in the spectator formalism.

## Key numbers

* The leading Regge trajectory intercept is \(\ell_0 = \frac{1}{2}\left(\sqrt{1+4\lambda}-3\right)\) for \(\lambda < 2\), where \(\lambda = g^2/(16\pi^2 m^2)\) is the dimensionless coupling.
* The solution for the leading trajectory at small \(x_B\) is \(M_0(x_B,\kappa,Q^2) = c_0 (1/x_B)^{\ell_0} \left(m^2/(m^2+\kappa)\right)^{\ell_0+1}\), with the coefficient fixed as \(c_0 = 2\pi\lambda e^2/Q^2\).
* The structure function \(\nu W_2\) at large \(Q^2\) is given by \(\nu W_2(\nu,Q^2) = x S_0 \int_0^\infty dp_\perp^2/m^2 \int_x^1 dz/(1-z) \Psi^2(p^2) \left[ \delta(x/z-1) + \lambda (z/x)^{\ell_0} \left(m^2/(m^2-p^2)\right)^{\ell_0+1} \right]\), where \(S_0 = m^2 M_B e^2/(4\pi)\).

## Context

This work extends the mid-1960s studies of Regge behavior from ladder diagram summation (Bertocci, Simonov) to the specific context of DIS structure functions, building on earlier results that showed Regge behavior arises from infinite sums of ladder diagrams in the \(t\)-channel. The paper provides an explicit matching of the small-\(x\) Regge contribution to the large-\(x\) pole term in a solvable scalar model, which enables future applications to generalized parton distributions (GPDs) as noted by Radyushkin, Ji, and Diehl (2003). The approach contrasts with purely perturbative QCD treatments by incorporating nonperturbative summation of ladder diagrams needed for bound state formation and small-\(x\) physics.
