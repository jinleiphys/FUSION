# Mirror nuclei constraint in mass formula

> The macroscopic-microscopic mass formula is further improved by considering mirror nuclei constraint. The rms deviation with respect to 2149 measured nuclear masses is reduced to 0.441 MeV. The shell corrections, the deformations of nuclei, the neutron and proton drip lines, and the shell gaps are also investigated to test the model. The rms deviation of alpha-decay energies of 46 super-heavy nuclei is reduced to 0.263 MeV. The central position of the super-heavy island could lie around N=176~178 and Z=116~120 according to the shell corrections of nuclei.

## Key claim

The macroscopic-microscopic mass formula is improved by adding a term proportional to the isospin asymmetry times the shell correction of the mirror nucleus, which empirically enforces the mirror-nuclei constraint that the "nuclear part" of the binding energy should be nearly equal for mirror pairs. The improvement reduces the rms deviation for 2149 measured masses to 0.441 MeV, but the authors note that deviations remain large for nuclei near certain neutron numbers (e.g., N ≈ 18, 26, 40, 56, 64, 70, 80, 88), which they attribute to possible triaxial or tetrahedral deformations not included in the model.

## Method

The authors start from a previously published macroscopic-microscopic mass formula (WS model) based on a modified Bethe-Weizsäcker liquid-drop energy plus Strutinsky shell corrections. They modify the Coulomb energy form to Z²/A¹/³ [1 − Z⁻²/³] following the finite-range droplet model (FRDM), and they replace the microscopic shell correction ΔE with c₁ E_sh + |I| E_sh′, where E_sh′ is the shell energy of the mirror nucleus. The |I| E_sh′ term is intended to reduce the shell-correction difference between mirror nuclei and to account for isospin-symmetry-breaking effects. The β₆ deformation is also included. Parameters are refitted to the 2149 measured masses from AME2003.

## Key numbers

- RMS deviation for 2149 measured nuclear masses: 0.441 MeV (WS* model), compared to 0.516 MeV for the previous WS model and 0.656 MeV for FRDM.
- RMS deviation for 1988 neutron separation energies: 0.332 MeV (WS*), compared to 0.346 MeV (WS) and 0.399 MeV (FRDM).
- RMS deviation for α-decay energies of 46 super-heavy nuclei: 0.263 MeV (WS*), compared to 0.566 MeV (FRDM) and 0.284 MeV (WS).
- With an empirical tetrahedral-deformation correction Δ_T added, the mass RMS deviation further reduces to 0.417 MeV.
- Predicted central position of the super-heavy island: N = 176–178 and Z = 116–120, based on shell corrections.
- Shell correction for ²⁹⁴117: −5.77 MeV (WS*); experimental Q_α = 10.96(10) MeV, WS* Q_α = 11.32 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the macroscopic-microscopic mass formula approach of Möller et al. (1995, FRDM) and the authors' own WS model (Wang, 2010) by explicitly incorporating the mirror-nuclei constraint motivated by isospin symmetry. The improvement is most notable for neutron-rich and super-heavy nuclei, where the new term reduces shell-correction differences between mirror pairs. The predicted shell corrections for super-heavy nuclei are comparable to fission barriers calculated by Kowal et al. (2007), and the model reproduces shell gaps at magic numbers Z = 20, 28, 40, 50, 82 better than FRDM or HFB-17. The authors note that residual deviations near specific neutron numbers may require inclusion of triaxial or tetrahedral deformations, as discussed by Dudek et al. (2002).
