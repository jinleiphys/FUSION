# A Calculation of Baryon Diffusion Constant in Hot and Dense Hadronic Matter Based on an Event Generator URASiMA

> We evaluate thermodynamical quantities and transport coefficients of a dense and hot hadronic matter based on an event generator URASiMA (Ultra-Relativistic AA collision Simulator based on Multiple Scattering Algorithm). The statistical ensembles in equilibrium with fixed temperature and chemical potential are generated by imposing periodic boundary condition to the simulation of URASiMA, where energy density and baryon number density is conserved. Achievement of the thermal equilibrium and the chemical equilibrium are confirmed by the common value of slope parameter in the energy distributions and the saturation of the numbers of contained particles, respectively. By using the generated ensembles, we investigate the temperature dependence and the chemical potential dependence of the baryon diffusion constant of a dense and hot hadronic matter.

## Key claim

The authors compute the baryon diffusion constant in hot and dense hadronic matter using statistical ensembles generated by the event generator URASiMA, but note that the temperature range is limited to 100–200 MeV, which may explain why the temperature dependence of the diffusion constant is not clearly observed.

## Method

Statistical ensembles at fixed temperature and chemical potential were generated by placing hadrons in a periodic box and running the URASiMA event generator, which simulates hadronic 2-body interactions, resonance formation/decay, and multi-particle production processes with detailed balance restored for energies below 3 GeV. After thermalization (beyond 150 fm/c), the velocity autocorrelation function of baryons was computed from the ensembles, and the diffusion constant was extracted via the first fluctuation-dissipation theorem using the integral of the velocity correlation function, assuming exponential relaxation.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range of the simulation: 100 MeV to 200 MeV.
- Thermalization time: approximately 100 fm/c; ensembles taken after t = 150 fm/c.
- For a system temperature of 100 MeV, the suppression factor for processes violating detailed balance at sqrt(s) > 3 GeV is exp(-30), making their influence negligibly small.
- The diffusion constant D is obtained from the velocity correlation function as D = (1/3) <v(t)·v(t)> τ, where τ is the relaxation time, and the relativistic velocity β = p/E is used.

## Context

This work extends earlier thermodynamic studies of hot and dense hadronic matter using URASiMA by Sasaki and Miyamura (1999), and contrasts with results from the UrQMD event generator, which reported Hagedorn-type temperature saturation. The authors note that lattice QCD calculations of transport coefficients are very difficult below T_c and nearly impossible at finite density due to the sign problem, as discussed by Nakamura and the Glasgow group. This is the first calculation of a transport coefficient (baryon diffusion constant) in hot and dense hadronic matter based on an event generator, and it enables future evaluation of viscosities and heat conductivity via the same fluctuation-dissipation framework, as well as providing constraints for baryon diffusion in inhomogeneous big-bang nucleosynthesis scenarios.
