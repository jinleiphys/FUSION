# Nuclear Interactions: The Chiral Picture

> Chiral expansions of the two-pion exchange components of both two- and three-nucleon forces are reviewed and a discussion is made of the predicted pattern of hierarchies. The strength of the scalar-isoscalar central potential is found to be too large and to defy expectations from the symmetry. The causes of this effect can be understood by studying the nucleon scalar form factor.

## Key claim

The chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) hierarchy is violated in the scalar-isoscalar central potential \(V_C^+\), which is found to be about ten times larger than the isovector central potential \(V_C^-\) in regions of physical interest, defying the expected ordering where \(V_C^+\) begins at \(\mathcal{O}(q^3)\) and \(V_C^-\) at \(\mathcal{O}(q^2)\).

## Method

The paper reviews chiral expansions of the two-pion exchange (TPE) components of both two- and three-nucleon forces, using ChPT up to \(\mathcal{O}(q^4)\) for the two-body potential and up to \(\mathcal{O}(q^4)\) corrections for the three-body force. The two-body TPE potential is decomposed into three families of diagrams (I, II, III) based on their dynamical origin, and the three-body force is analyzed via its leading term and ChPT corrections involving parameter shifts and new mathematical functions. The nucleon scalar form factor is studied in configuration space to understand the origin of the hierarchy violation.

## Key numbers

- \(|V_C^+| \sim 10 \, |V_C^-|\) in regions of physical interest, defying the predicted chiral hierarchy where \(V_C^+\) begins at \(\mathcal{O}(q^3)\) and \(V_C^-\) at \(\mathcal{O}(q^2)\).
- For the \(V_C^+\) component, the ratio between \(\mathcal{O}(q^4)\) and \(\mathcal{O}(q^3)\) contributions is larger than 0.5 for distances smaller than 2.5 fm.
- The nucleon sigma-term is predicted to be between 43 MeV and 49 MeV, depending on the \(\pi N\Delta\) coupling constant, in agreement with the empirical value \(45 \pm 8\) MeV.
- The critical radius where the quark condensate interpretation breaks down is \(R \sim 0.6\) fm.
- ChPT corrections to the leading three-body force parameters \((a,b,c)\) produce changes smaller than 10%, and new parameters \(e_i\) are also typically about 10% of the leading ones.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory treatment of nuclear forces developed by the author's group (Robilotta and collaborators, 2005-2007) and others using both covariant and heavy-baryon formalisms. The hierarchy violation in the isoscalar central potential is traced to large low-energy constants (LECs) generated dynamically by delta intermediate states, and the analysis of the nucleon scalar form factor suggests that perturbation theory may not apply to isoscalar interactions at intermediate distances. The results enable further study of \(\mathcal{O}(q^4)\) corrections to three-nucleon forces, which may help explain effects such as the \(A_y\) puzzle.
