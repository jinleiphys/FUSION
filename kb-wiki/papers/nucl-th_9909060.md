# Nucleonic resonance excitations and "missing resonances" in the $ω$ meson photoproduction

> In this work, an improved quark model approach to the $ω$ meson photoproduction with an effective Lagrangian is reported. The {\it t}-channel {\it natural} parity exchange is consistently taken into account through the Pomeron exchange, while the {\it unnatural} parity exchange is described by the $π^0$ exchange. It shows that with very limited number of parameters, all the available experimental data in the low energy regime can be consistently accounted for in this framework. Effects from the intermediate nucleonic resonances are investigated in several polarization observables. The sensitivities of these observables to the resonance effects are probably the best way for identifying those so-called "missing resonances" in the baryon spectrum.

## Key claim
The paper claims that polarization observables in ω meson photoproduction are highly sensitive to intermediate nucleonic resonance effects, and that these observables may be the best way to identify "missing resonances" in the baryon spectrum, but this sensitivity is conditional on a reliable description of non-resonance contributions at forward angles.

## Method
The authors use an improved quark model approach with an effective Lagrangian for ω meson photoproduction. The t-channel natural parity exchange is described by Pomeron exchange (with parameter β₀ = 1.27), while unnatural parity exchange is described by π⁰ exchange (with parameter απ = 200 MeV). The s- and u-channel resonance contributions are included via a quark-vector-meson coupling effective Lagrangian with two parameters (a = -0.8 and b = -1.6) constrained by large-angle differential cross section data.

## Key numbers
- π⁰ exchange parameter απ = 200 MeV, the only parameter introduced for the unnatural parity exchange terms
- Pomeron coupling strength β₀ = 1.27, determined by experimental data at high energies
- Constituent quark mass mq = 330 MeV used in the effective Lagrangian
- Couplings g²πNN/4π = 14 and g²ωπγ = 3.315 employed for the πNN and ωπγ vertices
- Resonance parameters: S11(1535) mass 1535 MeV, width 150 MeV; D13(1520) mass 1520 MeV, width 120 MeV; P13(1720) mass 1720 MeV, width 150 MeV; F15(1680) mass 1680 MeV, width 130 MeV; P11(1440) mass 1440 MeV, width 350 MeV; P11(1710) mass 1710 MeV, width 100 MeV; P13(1900) mass 1900 MeV, width 250 MeV; F15(2000) mass 2000 MeV, width 250 MeV
- Photon energy threshold for ω meson photoproduction: Eγ = 1.12 GeV
- Resonance contributions dominate over π⁰ and Pomeron exchange from threshold to about Eγ = 1.5 GeV

## Context
This work extends earlier vector-meson-dominant model calculations by Friman and Soyeur (1990s) and the author's previous work (1998) by including Pomeron exchange, which significantly improves the description at photon energies above the resonance region (from threshold to about 2.2 GeV). The approach addresses the "missing resonances" problem, where the non-relativistic constituent quark model predicts a richer baryon spectrum than observed in πN → πN scatterings, suggesting these resonances may couple weakly to the πN channel. The framework enables searches for missing resonances in ωN decay channels and predicts specific signatures in beam and target polarization asymmetries that can be tested against data from CLAS and GRAAL experiments.
