# Relativistic chiral representation of the πN scattering amplitude

> We have analyzed pion-nucleon scattering using the manifestly relativistic covariant frameworks of Infrared Regularization (IR) and Extended-On-Mass-Shell (EOMS) up to O(q^3) in the chiral expansion, where q is a generic small momentum. We describe the low-energy phase shifts with a similar quality as previously achieved with Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory, being the EOMS description better than the IR one. The Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy is extracted from data of partial wave analyses using both schemes, obtaining an unacceptable large value for the case of IR due to the loop contribution. On the other hand, EOMS gives small values compatible with other phenomenological approaches. Finally, we have unitarized the amplitudes provided by both schemes to extend the range of our description obtaining a good agreement with the data up to energies of \sqrt{s}\approx 1.3 GeV for the EOMS scheme while IR can not go beyond energies of \sqrt{s}\approx 1.25 GeV due to the unphysical cut that this scheme introduces.

## Key claim

The Extended-On-Mass-Shell (EOMS) scheme provides a better description of low-energy pion-nucleon phase shifts than Infrared Regularization (IR) when both are used up to O(q^3) in chiral perturbation theory, and only EOMS yields a Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy compatible with phenomenological expectations (around 2-3%) once loop contributions are included.

## Method

The authors performed a chiral perturbation theory analysis of pion-nucleon scattering using two manifestly relativistic covariant frameworks: Infrared Regularization (IR) and Extended-On-Mass-Shell (EOMS), both truncated at O(q^3). They fitted the low-energy constants to phase shift data from the Karlsruhe (KA85) and GWU (WI08) partial wave analyses, using two fitting strategies for the P33 partial wave (standard chi-squared and effective range expansion). They then unitarized the amplitudes using a method that satisfies unitarity exactly, introducing a Castillejo-Dalitz-Dyson (CDD) pole to account for the Delta(1232) resonance in the P33 channel.

## Key numbers

- Perturbative fits reproduce experimental phase shifts up to energies of 1.14 GeV for most partial waves.
- Unitarized EOMS scheme describes phase shifts accurately up to sqrt(s) ≈ 1.3 GeV.
- Unitarized IR scheme cannot go beyond sqrt(s) ≈ 1.25 GeV due to an unphysical cut introduced by the IR scheme.
- The Goldberger-Treiman deviation from EOMS is Δ_GT = 0.015 ± 0.018, giving g_πN = 13.07 ± 0.23 and f^2 = 0.077 ± 0.003.
- For the IR scheme, the Goldberger-Treiman violation is 22% at renormalization scale μ = 1 GeV and 15% at μ = 0.5 GeV (for the KA85-1 fit).
- The unphysical cut in IR appears at s = 2(m^2 + M_π^2) ≳ 1.34^2 GeV^2, causing unitarity violation for s ≳ 1.34^2 GeV^2 and fast rising phase shifts for sqrt(s) ≳ 1.26 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HBChPT) studies by Fettes, Meissner, and Steininger, and improves upon previous IR calculations by Becher and Leutwyler and by Terschlusen and Entem, which had found worse phase shift descriptions and large Goldberger-Treiman violations. The EOMS scheme, developed by Fuchs, Gegelia, Japaridze, and Scherer, avoids the unphysical cuts and scale dependence problems of IR, enabling unitarized descriptions up to higher energies and providing a framework for testing chiral low-energy theorems more reliably.
