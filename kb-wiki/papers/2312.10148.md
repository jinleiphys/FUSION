# Which first order phase transitions to quark matter are possible in neutron stars?

> We examine which first order phase transitions are consistent with today's astrophysical constraints. In particular, we explore how a well-constrained mass-radius data point would restrict the admissible parameter space and to this end, we employ the most likely candidates of the recent NICER limits of PSR J0030+0451. To systematically vary the stiffness of the equation of state, we employ a parameterizable relativistic mean field equation of state, which is in compliance with results from chiral effective field theory. We model phase transitions via Maxwell constructions and parameterize them by means of the transitional pressure $p_{\rm trans}$ and the jump in energy density $Δε$. This provides us with a generic setup that allows for rather general conclusions to be drawn. We outline some regions in the $p_{\rm trans}$-$Δε$ parameter space that may allow for a phase transition identification in the near future. We also find that a strongly constrained data point, at either exceptionally large or small radii, would reduce the parameter space to such an extent that mass and radius become insufficient indicators of a phase transition.

## Key claim

The authors examine which first-order phase transitions to quark matter in neutron stars remain consistent with current astrophysical constraints, finding that a strongly constrained mass-radius data point at either exceptionally large or small radii would reduce the parameter space to such an extent that mass and radius become insufficient indicators of a phase transition.

## Method

The authors employ a parameterizable relativistic mean field equation of state with variable effective nucleon mass at saturation density (m*/m from 0.55 to 0.75), in compliance with chiral effective field theory results. They model phase transitions via Maxwell constructions parameterized by the transitional pressure p_trans and the jump in energy density Δε, using a maximally stiff constant speed of sound approach (c_QM = 1) for the quark phase. They then apply constraints from the 2 M_sun mass limit, NICER radius measurements of PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J0030+0451, and the GW170817 tidal deformability measurement.

## Key numbers

- Effective nucleon mass range studied: m*/m = 0.55 to 0.75, where smaller values correspond to stiffer equations of state
- Only equations of state with 0.65 ≤ m*/m < 0.75 are compatible with NICER, GW170817, and mass constraints without a phase transition
- The Seidov limit for destabilization at the phase transition is given by Δε/ε_trans = 1/2 + (3/2)(p_trans/ε_trans)
- The softest EoS (m*/m = 0.75) has a maximal mass barely above 2 M_sun, failing to meet the mass constraint of at least 2.09 M_sun from Romani (2022)
- The stiffest EoS (m*/m = 0.55) generates a maximal mass of nearly 2.8 M_sun
- For the stiffest EoS, the border between category III and IV corresponds to n ≃ 1.7 n_0, the smallest density where this border can lie
- The hypothetical larger estimate for PSR J0030+0451 gives mass 1.7^{+0.18}_{-0.19} M_sun with radius 14.44^{+0.88}_{-1.05} km
- The hypothetical smaller estimate for PSR J0030+0451 gives mass 1.4^{+0.13}_{-0.12} M_sun with radius 11.71^{+0.88}_{-0.83} km
- The least massive known neutron star mass M_min ≃ 1.174 ± 0.004 M_sun for pulsar J0453+1559 from Martinez (2015)

## Context

This work extends the categorization of twin star configurations from Christian (2017) by using known mass ranges of neutron stars as a basis, and reinforces the finding from Christian (2020) that twin stars require phase transitions at densities above about 1.7 n_0. The authors find that a phase transition can ease the tension between GW170817 and NICER radius constraints, but that a strongly constrained data point at either exceptionally large or small radii would make mass and radius insufficient indicators of a phase transition. The results are generic for first-order phase transitions or rapid crossovers, and the authors note that the larger and smaller estimates for PSR J0030+0451 from Vinciguerra (2023) are mutually exclusive with no equation of state containing both stars even with a phase transition.
