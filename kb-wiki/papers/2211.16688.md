# Neutron-skin values and matter and neutron radii determined from reaction cross sections of proton scattering on $^{12}$C, $^{40,48}$Ca, $^{58}$Ni, $^{208}$Pb

> Very lately, the PREX and the CREX collaboration present skin values, $r_{\rm skin}^{208}({\rm newPREX2}) =0.278 \pm 0.078\ {\rm (exp)} \pm 0.012\ {\rm (theor.)}\,{\rm fm}$ and $r_{\rm skin}^{48}=0.121 \pm 0.026\ {\rm (exp)} \pm 0.024\ {\rm (model)}$, respectively. We recently determined a neutron-skin value $r_{\rm skin}^{208}=0.278 \pm 0.035$fm from measured reaction cross sections $σ_{\rm R}({\rm exp})$ of p+$^{208}$Pb scattering in a range of incident energies $10 \lsim E_{\rm in} \lsim 100$ MeV where the chiral (Kyushu) $g$-matrix folding model is reliable for $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C scattering. The data $σ_{\rm R}({\rm exp})$ are available for proton scattering on $^{58}$Ni, $^{40,48}$Ca, $^{12}$C targets. Our first aim is to test the Kyushu $g$-matrix folding model for p+$^{208}$Pb scattering in $20 \lsim E_{\rm in} \lsim 180$ MeV. Our second aim is to determine skin values $r_{\rm skin}$ and matter and neutron radii, $r_{\rm m}$ and $r_{\rm n}$, for $^{208}$Pb, $^{58}$Ni, $^{40,48}$Ca, $^{12}$C from the $σ_{\rm R}({\rm exp})$. Our method is the Kyushu $g$-matrix folding model with the densities scaled from the D1S-GHFB+AMP densities, where D1S-GHFB+AMP stands for Gogny-D1S HFB (GHFB) with the angular momentum projection (AMP). As for proton scattering, we find that our model is reliable in $20 \lsim E_{\rm in} \lsim 180$ MeV. For $^{208}$Pb, the skin value deduced from $σ_{\rm R}({\rm exp})$ in $20 \lsim E_{\rm in} \lsim 180$ MeV is $r_{\rm skin}^{208}(σ_{\rm R})=0.299 \pm 0.020$ fm. Our results on $r_{\rm skin}$ are compared with the previous works. Our result $r_{\rm skin}^{208}(σ_{\rm R}) = 0.299 \pm 0.020$ fm agrees with $r_{\rm skin}^{208}({\rm PREX2}) = 0.283\pm 0.071$ fm. In addition, our result $r_{\rm skin}^{48}=0.103 \pm 0.022$ fm is consistent with the CREX value.

## Key claim

The authors determine neutron-skin values and matter/neutron radii for five nuclei from measured proton reaction cross sections using the Kyushu g-matrix folding model, finding that their result for 208Pb (0.299 ± 0.020 fm) agrees with the PREX2 value and their result for 48Ca (0.103 ± 0.022 fm) is consistent with the CREX value, though the abstract's claim of consistency glosses over that the 48Ca result is slightly lower than the CREX central value of 0.121 fm.

## Method

The authors used the Kyushu g-matrix folding model, which is a microscopic optical potential derived from chiral N3LO two-nucleon forces and NNLO three-nucleon forces via Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations, to compute reaction cross sections for proton scattering on 208Pb, 58Ni, 48Ca, 40Ca, and 12C targets. Proton and neutron densities were scaled from D1S-GHFB+AMP (Gogny-D1S Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov with angular momentum projection) densities, with the proton density scaled to match experimental charge radii from electron scattering and the neutron density scaled to reproduce measured reaction cross sections in the incident energy range 20 to 180 MeV.

## Key numbers

- 208Pb neutron-skin value from reaction cross sections: r_skin^208(sigma_R) = 0.299 ± 0.020 fm, with neutron radius r_n = 5.743 ± 0.020 fm and matter radius r_m = 5.627 ± 0.020 fm
- 48Ca neutron-skin value: r_skin^48 = 0.103 ± 0.022 fm, with neutron radius r_n = 3.488 ± 0.022 fm
- 58Ni matter radius: r_m = 3.711 ± 0.010 fm, with neutron-skin value r_skin = 0.055 ± 0.010 fm and neutron radius r_n = 3.740 ± 0.010 fm
- 208Pb PREX2 value used as reference: r_skin^208(PREX2) = 0.283 ± 0.071 fm
- 48Ca CREX value used as reference: r_skin^48(CREX) = 0.121 ± 0.050 fm (combining experimental and model uncertainties)
- Scaling factors for 208Pb: alpha_n = 1.015, alpha_p = 1.000; for 48Ca: alpha_n = 0.973, alpha_p = 0.982; for 12C: alpha_n = 0.942, alpha_p = 0.957

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous determination of the 208Pb neutron-skin value from reaction cross sections (Tagami 2020) by expanding the energy range to 20-180 MeV and applying the method to additional nuclei (58Ni, 48Ca, 40Ca, 12C). The results are compared with the PREX collaboration's parity-violating electron scattering measurements (Adhikari 2021) and the CREX collaboration's results (CREX 2022), as well as with E1 polarizability measurements (Tamii 2011 for 208Pb; Birkhan 2016 for 48Ca) and the GSI storage-ring measurement for 58Ni (Zamora 2017). The work enables improved constraints on the symmetry energy slope parameter L through the established correlation between neutron-skin thickness and L.
