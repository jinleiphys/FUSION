# Holography and off-center collisions of localized shock waves

> Using numerical holography, we study the collision, at non-zero impact parameter, of bounded, localized distributions of energy density chosen to mimic relativistic heavy ion collisions, in strongly coupled N = 4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory. Both longitudinal and transverse dynamics in the dual field theory are properly described. Using the gravitational description we solve 5D Einstein equations, without dimensionality reducing symmetry restrictions, to find the asymptotically anti-de Sitter spacetime geometry. Implications of our results on the understanding of early stages of heavy ion collisions, including the development of transverse radial flow, are discussed.

## Key claim

Using numerical holography, the authors report the first calculation of off-center collisions of bounded, localized energy distributions in strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory that does not impose unrealistic dimensionality-reducing symmetry restrictions, though the Gaussian transverse profiles used may suppress elliptic flow signals.

## Method

The authors construct initial data by combining metrics of gravitational shock waves moving at the speed of light in opposite directions in asymptotically anti-de Sitter spacetime, using Gaussian energy density profiles with longitudinal width w = 1/2 and transverse width R = 4. They numerically solve the full 5D Einstein equations in infalling Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates using spectral methods on a grid of size N_x = N_y = 39, N_z = 145, N_r = 40, with a fourth-order Runge-Kutta time integration and time step 0.005, evolving from t = -2 to t = 4. The SYM stress-energy tensor is extracted from the sub-leading metric coefficients g^{(4)}_{\mu\nu} via the holographic stress tensor formula.

## Key numbers

- Impact parameter b = 3/4 R = 3 in the x-direction (with R = 4)
- Longitudinal width w = 1/2, transverse width R = 4
- At t = 0, energy density and flux differ by only 8% and 15% respectively from a linear superposition of two unmodified shocks
- Equilibration time t_hydro ≈ 1.25, about 30% smaller than in previous planar shock studies
- At τ = 1.25 and ξ = 0, a fluid droplet with Δ < 0.15 forms with transverse radius x_⊥ ≲ 5.3
- At τ = 2 and ξ = 0, Δ < 0.15 for x_⊥ ≲ 8.6
- Maximum fluid 3-velocity at t = 4 is 0.64 (near receding maxima); maximum transverse velocity in x-y plane is 0.3
- At t = 4 and radius x_⊥ ≈ 5, transverse fluid velocity magnitude is 0.3
- Transverse plane anisotropy of energy density and fluid velocity at t = 4 is about 1%
- Longitudinal flow deviates from boost invariance at the 20% level or better in regions where ε > 0.6 max(ε)

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of heavy ion collisions that imposed boost invariance, planar shocks, or imploding shells (Chesler 2009, Chesler 2010, Casalderrey-Solana 2013, van der Schee 2013, Bantilan 2012, Bantilan 2014). The inclusion of full transverse dynamics reveals faster equilibration than in planar shock collisions (Chesler 2010, Chesler 2013), consistent with expectations from quasinormal mode spectra (Heller 2012, Heller 2013). The early-time transverse flow agrees remarkably well with the gradient-driven formula T^{0x} = -(t/2) ∂_x ε predicted by Vredevoogd (2008), enabling future studies with more realistic nuclear density profiles and shorter-distance fluctuations.
