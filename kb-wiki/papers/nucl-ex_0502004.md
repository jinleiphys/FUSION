# Structure of collective modes in transitional and deformed nuclei

> The collective structure of atomic nuclei intermediate between spherical and quadrupole deformed structure presents challenges to theoretical understanding. However, models have recently been proposed in terms of potentials which are soft with respect to the quadrupole deformation variable beta. To test these models, information is needed on low-spin states of transitional nuclei. The present work involves measurement of electromagnetic decay properties of low-spin states for nuclei in the A=100 (gamma-soft) and N=90 (axially symmetric) transition regions. Population in beta-decay and thermal neutron capture are used, and measurements are carried out using gamma-ray coincidence spectroscopy, fast electronic scintillation timing, and gamma-ray induced Doppler broadening techniques, in experiments at Yale, TRIUMF ISAC, and the ILL. To facilitate interpretation of these nuclei, a new approach is developed that simplifies the application of the geometric collective model by use of scaling properties. Solutions are also obtained for the E(5) Hamiltonian for finite well depth.

## Key claim

The E(5) critical-point symmetry model reproduces many characteristics of low-lying levels in transitional nuclei such as ¹⁰²Pd and ¹⁵²Sm, but significant deviations exist, including missing enhanced transitions, quantitative differences in B(E2) strengths, and the presence of intruder 0⁺ states, that indicate a slight preference for axial symmetry or the incipient formation of a γ band, rather than pure γ-soft structure.

## Method

Electromagnetic decay properties of low-spin states in nuclei from the A≈100 (γ-soft) and N≈90 (axially symmetric) transition regions were measured using γ-ray coincidence spectroscopy, fast electronic scintillation timing (FEST), and γ-ray induced Doppler broadening (GRID) techniques. Experiments were performed at Yale (YRAST Ball and SPEEDY arrays), TRIUMF ISAC, and the ILL (GAMS4 spectrometer), with population via β⁺/ε decay and thermal neutron capture. A new approach simplifying the geometric collective model through scaling properties was developed, and solutions for the E(5) Hamiltonian with finite well depth were obtained.

- ¹⁰²Pd: R₄/₂ = 2.29; E(5) prediction for E(0⁺₂)/E(2⁺₁) = 3.03, observed E(0⁺₁₆₅₈)/E(2⁺₅₅₆) = 2.98
- ¹⁰²Pd: B(E2; 4⁺₂₁₃₈→2⁺₁₅₃₄) = 46(11) W.u., in agreement with E(5) prediction of 51(4) W.u. based on B(E2; 2⁺₅₅₆→0⁺₀) = 32.6(23) W.u.
- ¹⁰²Pd: B(E2; 0⁺₁₆₅₈→2⁺₅₅₆) = 13.3 W.u., lower than E(5) prediction of 28(2) W.u.
- ¹⁰²Pd: 0⁺₁₅₉₃ isomer lifetime = 21(6) ns
- ¹⁵²Sm: B(E2; 2⁺₈₁₀→0⁺₆₈₅) = 111(28) W.u. (revised from prior 520(170) W.u.)
- ¹⁵²Sm: B(E2; 4⁺₁₀₂₃→2⁺₈₁₀) = 203(38) W.u.

## Context

This work extends the experimental testing of the E(5) critical-point symmetry model (Iachello, 2000) and the X(5) model (Iachello, 2001) for transitional nuclei. For ¹⁰²Pd, the results build on prior IBM studies (Stachel 1982, Bucurescu 1986, Pan 1998) and earlier β-decay work (Cornelis 1979, 1981), revealing that while E(5) describes many features, intruder 0⁺ states (previously identified in higher-N Pd isotopes by Wood 1992) complicate the interpretation. For ¹⁵²Sm, the measurements improve upon earlier β-decay data (Casten 1998) and provide new lifetime values (Klug 2000) and multipolarity information needed for X(5) comparisons. The results enable quantitative tests of the geometric collective model and critical-point symmetries, while identifying the need for models incorporating both γ-softness and incipient axial deformation.
