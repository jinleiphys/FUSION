# Quantum Information at the Electron-Ion Collider

> We investigate quantum-information-theoretic observables in electron-proton scattering at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). Our analysis focuses on entanglement and magic, two complementary indicators of non-classicality in quantum states. We show that while unpolarized and longitudinally polarized beams yield unentangled separable outcomes, transverse beam polarization enables the generation of entangled and non-stabilizer states. This result holds for both elastic and deep inelastic electron-proton scattering in QED. In the deep inelastic regime, the degree of quantum correlation is governed by the transversity parton distribution functions, providing a novel perspective on spin dynamics within QCD. These results establish the EIC as a promising environment for generating entangled and non-stabilizer states in high-energy physics, and they highlight opportunities for future lepton-hadron colliders to extend such studies into new kinematic domains.

## Key claim

The paper shows that nontrivial quantum correlations (entanglement and magic) in electron-proton scattering at the EIC arise only when both beams are transversely polarized; unpolarized or longitudinally polarized beams yield only separable, classically correlated final states, and the effect is governed by transversity parton distribution functions in the deep inelastic regime.

## Method

The authors computed the spin density matrix of the final-state electron-proton (or electron-quark) pair from helicity amplitudes for t-channel photon exchange, treating both elastic and deep inelastic scattering. For deep inelastic scattering, they incorporated the transversity parton distribution functions (PDFs) from the JAMDiFF collaboration to relate the transverse polarization of the initial proton to that of the struck quark, and evaluated concurrence and the second stabilizer Rényi entropy (SSRE) as functions of scattering angle, momentum transfer Q^2, and Bjorken x.

## Key numbers

- EIC beam energies: electron beams up to 18 GeV, proton beams up to 275 GeV per nucleon, with projected integrated luminosity of order 100 fb^{-1}
- For 100% transversely polarized beams in elastic ep scattering, the final state becomes a maximally entangled spin triplet at backward scattering (Q^2 ≈ s)
- In deep inelastic scattering at backward scattering (cosθ = -1), the concurrence of the eq pair is given by C = b^e_perp * b^q_perp, where b^e_perp and b^q_perp are the transverse polarization degrees of the electron and quark
- The effective transverse polarization fraction of quarks from a transversely polarized proton, α = (Σ_q e_q^2 h_{1,q}(x))/(Σ_q e_q^2 f_q(x)), maximizes at x around 0.6-0.8
- Concurrence of the eq pair from DIS at a sqrt(s) = 20 GeV EIC is shown as a function of Q^2 and x, reaching maximum in the backward-scattering region where Q^2 = x(20 GeV)^2

## Context

This work extends recent studies of quantum information in high-energy scattering, particularly entanglement in top quark pair production at the LHC (Afik 2020, ATLAS 2023, CMS 2024) and the first investigations of magic in fundamental interactions (White 2024, Robin 2024, Aoude 2025). It contrasts with prior EIC spin studies (Qi 2025) by formulating entanglement observables directly from the final-state eq pair using transversity PDFs, rather than relying on decay distributions. The results establish the EIC as a platform for generating entangled and non-stabilizer states in Standard Model processes, and motivate future lepton-hadron colliders such as the TeV-scale Muon synchronization Ion Collider (MuSIC) for extending quantum tomography to new kinematic regimes.
