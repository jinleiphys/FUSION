# Low-lying magnetic excitations of doubly-closed-shell nuclei and nucleon-nucleon effective interactions

> We have studied the low lying magnetic spectra of 12C, 16O, 40Ca, 48Ca and 208Pb nuclei within the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) theory, finding that the description of low-lying magnetic states of doubly-closed-shell nuclei imposes severe constraints on the spin and tensor terms of the nucleon-nucleon effective interaction. We have first made an investigation by using four phenomenological effective interactions and we have obtained good agreement with the experimental magnetic spectra, and, to a lesser extent, with the electron scattering responses. Then we have made self-consistent RPA calculations to test the validity of the finite-range D1 Gogny interaction. For all the nuclei under study we have found that this interaction inverts the energies of all the magnetic states forming isospin doublets.

## Key claim

The description of low-lying magnetic states in doubly-closed-shell nuclei imposes severe constraints on the spin and tensor terms of the nucleon-nucleon effective interaction, but the good agreement with experimental spectra obtained using four phenomenological interactions (with and without tensor terms, zero-range and finite-range) suggests that the parametrization captures general features necessary for describing magnetic excitations, while the self-consistent Gogny D1 interaction fails by inverting the energies of all magnetic states forming isospin doublets.

## Method

The authors solved Random Phase Approximation (RPA) equations for the low-lying magnetic spectra of 12C, 16O, 40Ca, 48Ca, and 208Pb using a discrete set of single-particle wave functions and energies. In the phenomenological approach, the mean-field basis was generated by a Woods-Saxon well, and four effective interactions were constructed: two zero-range (Landau-Migdal type, with and without tensor terms, labeled LM and LMtt) and two finite-range (based on the Argonne V18 potential, with and without tensor terms, labeled FR and FRtt). In the self-consistent approach, the Hartree-Fock basis and RPA residual interaction both used the Gogny D1 finite-range interaction, including both direct and exchange matrix elements.

## Key numbers

* For 12C, the calculated 1+ isoscalar state energies range from 13.87 MeV (FRtt) to 14.41 MeV (LM, LMtt), compared to the experimental value of 12.71 MeV; the isovector 1+ state energies range from 17.97 MeV (LMtt) to 18.17 MeV (FR), compared to the experimental 15.11 MeV.
* For 12C, the calculated 4- isoscalar state energies range from 17.75 MeV (FRtt) to 18.21 MeV (LM, LMtt), compared to the experimental 18.27 MeV; the isovector 4- state energies range from 19.49 MeV (FRtt) to 21.70 MeV (LM), compared to the experimental 19.50 MeV.
* For 16O, the calculated 4- isoscalar state energies range from 17.73 MeV (FRtt) to 18.15 MeV (LM, LMtt), compared to the experimental 17.79 MeV; the isovector 4- state energies range from 19.45 MeV (FRtt) to 21.41 MeV (LM), compared to the experimental 18.98 MeV.
* For 208Pb, the parameters of the zero-range interactions were adjusted to reproduce the 1+ states at 5.85 and 7.30 MeV and the 12- states at 6.43 and 7.08 MeV.
* The configuration space for 12C in the phenomenological calculation used 5 major harmonic oscillator shells (44 single-particle states); for 208Pb, 9 major shells for protons and 10 for neutrons (100 single-particle states) were used.
* In the self-consistent calculations, for 12C the computational box radius was 10 fm and the maximum particle energy cutoff was 50 MeV; for 208Pb these were 14 fm and 50 MeV, respectively.

## Context

This work extends systematic RPA studies of magnetic excitations in doubly-closed-shell nuclei, following earlier phenomenological work by Co' (1990) and Lallena (1993, 1996) that focused on single nuclei or limited sets of states. The authors test the Gogny D1 interaction (Gogny 1975, Blaizot 1977, Decharge 1980) in a self-consistent RPA framework and find it inverts the isospin doublet ordering, which contradicts the correct sequence obtained with the phenomenological interactions. This result indicates that the study of magnetic spectra is selective in constraining the spin and tensor terms of effective interactions, and the failure of the Gogny D1 interaction suggests the need for improved parametrizations of these terms in self-consistent approaches.
