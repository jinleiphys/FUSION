# Description of light clusters in relativistic nuclear models

> Light clusters are included in the equation of state of nuclear matter within the relativistic mean field theory. The effect of the cluster-meson coupling constants on the dissolution density is discussed. Theoretical and experimental constraints are used to fix the cluster-meson couplings. The relative light cluster fractions are calculated for asymmetric matter in chemical equilibrium at finite temperature. It is found that above T = 5 MeV deuterons and tritons are the clusters in larger abundances. The results do not depend strongly on the relativistic mean field interaction chosen.

# Description of light clusters in relativistic nuclear models

## Key claim

Light clusters (deuterons, tritons, helions, and alpha particles) can be included in the equation of state of nuclear matter within the relativistic mean field framework using point-like particles with meson couplings, and the dissolution density depends primarily on the isoscalar cluster-meson couplings rather than the specific relativistic mean field interaction chosen; however, the results are conditional on fixing the cluster-meson coupling constants to reproduce theoretical dissolution densities at zero temperature and experimental Mott points near T = 5 MeV.

## Method

The authors treat light clusters (d, t, h, alpha) as point-like particles without internal structure within the relativistic mean field approximation, interacting through exchange of sigma, omega, and rho meson fields. The cluster-meson coupling constants are parametrized as fractions of the nucleon-meson couplings (g_v^i = eta_i g_v, g_s^i = beta_i g_s, g_rho^i = delta_i g_rho), and the dissolution density for each cluster is determined from the crossing between the proton-neutron free energy and the free energy of matter with the largest possible fraction of that cluster. Three different parametrizations (NL3, FSU, IU-FSU) are used to test dependence on the underlying nuclear interaction.

## Key numbers

- Dissolution densities at T = 0 MeV in symmetric nuclear matter: triton 0.00183175 fm^-3, helion 0.00144835 fm^-3, alpha 0.00439226 fm^-3, deuteron 0.00044815 fm^-3
- Above T = 5 MeV, deuterons and tritons are the clusters in larger abundances
- For the FSU parametrization with g_s^i = 0, the fitted omega-cluster coupling ratios g_v^i/g_v are: triton 2.961, helion 3.283, deuteron 3.038, alpha 4.440
- For the NL3 parametrization with g_s^i = 0, the fitted omega-cluster coupling ratios g_v^i/g_v are: triton 3.357, helion 3.718, deuteron 3.371, alpha 4.919
- Saturation density for the three models: NL3 0.148 fm^-3, FSU 0.148 fm^-3, IU-FSU 0.155 fm^-3
- Binding energies of clusters in vacuum: triton 8.481798 MeV, helion 7.718043 MeV, alpha 28.29566 MeV, deuteron 2.224566 MeV

## Context

This work extends the generalized relativistic mean field model of Typel (2010), which included light clusters with density-dependent couplings and quantum statistical medium effects, by developing a simpler implementation using constant cluster-meson couplings within the non-linear Walecka model. The approach contrasts with the excluded volume prescriptions used by Lattimer and Swesty (1991) and Shen (1998) for alpha particle dissolution, and with the virial expansion approach of Horowitz and Schwenk (2006) for low-density nuclear matter. The results enable more realistic equations of state for core-collapse supernova simulations and protoneutron star cooling calculations, particularly at the low densities and moderate temperatures where cluster formation is energetically favorable.
