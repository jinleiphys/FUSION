# Unitary Fermi gas, epsilon expansion, and nonrelativistic conformal field theories

> We review theoretical aspects of unitary Fermi gas (UFG), which has been realized in ultracold atom experiments. We first introduce the epsilon expansion technique based on a systematic expansion in terms of the dimensionality of space. We apply this technique to compute the thermodynamic quantities, the quasiparticle spectrum, and the critical temperature of UFG. We then discuss consequences of the scale and conformal invariance of UFG. We prove a correspondence between primary operators in nonrelativistic conformal field theories and energy eigenstates in a harmonic potential. We use this correspondence to compute energies of fermions at unitarity in a harmonic potential. The scale and conformal invariance together with the general coordinate invariance constrains the properties of UFG. We show the vanishing bulk viscosities of UFG and derive the low-energy effective Lagrangian for the superfluid UFG. Finally we propose other systems exhibiting the nonrelativistic scaling and conformal symmetries that can be in principle realized in ultracold atom experiments.

# Unitary Fermi gas, epsilon expansion, and nonrelativistic conformal field theories

## Key claim

The unitary Fermi gas can be systematically studied via an epsilon expansion around four and two spatial dimensions, where the system becomes weakly interacting, and the results can be extrapolated to the physical case of three dimensions to obtain quantitative predictions for thermodynamic quantities, quasiparticle spectra, and critical temperature.

## Method

The authors extend the problem of fermions with zero-range interaction at infinite scattering length to arbitrary spatial dimension d, identifying d=4 and d=2 as noninteracting limits where the system becomes weakly interacting. Near d=4, the unitary Fermi gas is described as a weakly interacting gas of fermions and composite bosons with coupling g ~ (4-d)^(1/2); near d=2, it reduces to a weakly interacting Fermi gas with coupling proportional to (d-2). Feynman diagrams are organized by power counting in epsilon = 4-d or bar-epsilon = d-2, and results for d=3 are obtained by extrapolating the series expansions using Pade approximants and Borel-Pade interpolation.

## Key numbers

* Bertsch parameter xi (ground state energy ratio) at d=3 from NLO Pade interpolation: xi ≈ 0.391, 0.366, 0.373, spanning xi ≈ 0.377 ± 0.014
* Bertsch parameter xi at d=3 from NLO Borel-Pade interpolation: xi ≈ 0.391, 0.364, 0.378, spanning xi ≈ 0.378 ± 0.013
* Bertsch parameter xi at d=3 from NNLO Pade interpolation: xi ≈ 0.340, 0.372, 0.370, 0.357
* Leading-order expansion of xi near d=4: xi = (1/2) epsilon^(3/2) + (1/16) epsilon^(5/2) ln epsilon - 0.0246 epsilon^(5/2) + O(epsilon^(7/2))
* Leading-order expansion of xi near d=2: xi = 1 - bar-epsilon + O(bar-epsilon^2)
* NNLO expansion of xi near d=4: xi = (epsilon^(3/2)/2) exp(epsilon ln epsilon/(8-2 epsilon)) [1 - 0.04916 epsilon - 0.95961 epsilon^2 + O(epsilon^3)]
* NNLO expansion of xi near d=2: xi = 1 - bar-epsilon + 0.80685 bar-epsilon^2 + O(bar-epsilon^3)
* Naive extrapolation of NLO expansion to d=3: xi → 0.475
* Mean field approximation yields xi ≈ 0.591
* Monte Carlo simulation result for comparison: xi ≈ 0.40(1) (Zhang)

## Context

This work extends the BCS-BEC crossover picture (Eagles 1969, Leggett 1980, Nozieres and Schmitt-Rink 1985) by providing a systematic analytical approach to the unitary limit where standard perturbative expansion in |a k_F| fails. The epsilon expansion technique, first suggested by Nussinov and Nussinov (2006), is developed into a full field-theoretical framework using Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation and dimensional regularization. The results enable analytical computation of thermodynamic quantities, quasiparticle spectra, and critical temperature for the unitary Fermi gas, and the operator-state correspondence established for nonrelativistic conformal field theories allows exact computation of few-body energies in harmonic traps. The vanishing bulk viscosity and low-energy effective Lagrangian derived from scale and conformal invariance provide constraints that can be tested in ultracold atom experiments with 40K and 6Li.
