# K$^+$-nucleus quasielastic scattering

> K$^+$--nucleus quasielastic cross sections measured for a laboratory kaon beam momentum of 705 MeV/$c$ are presented for 3--momentum transfers of 300 and 500 MeV/$c$. The measured differential cross sections for C, Ca and Pb at 500 MeV/$c$ are used to deduce the effective number of nucleons participating in the scattering, which are compared with estimates based on the eikonal approximation. The long mean free path expected for K$^+$ mesons in nuclei is found. Double differential cross sections for C and Ca are compared to relativistic nuclear structure calculations.

## Key claim

The measured effective number of nucleons participating in K$^+$ quasielastic scattering is consistently about 30% larger than predictions from the eikonal approximation using standard K$^+$-N total cross sections from phase shift solutions, which contradicts the fact that multiple scattering theory underestimates K$^+$-nucleus elastic scattering data (which would require a larger K$^+$-N cross section).

## Method

K$^+$ quasielastic double differential cross sections were measured at a laboratory beam momentum of 705 MeV/$c$ for C, Ca, Pb, and D targets using the hypernuclear spectrometer system (Moby Dick) at Brookhaven National Laboratory. Data were taken at laboratory scattering angles of 24°, 33°, and 42°, corresponding to momentum transfers of 300, 400, and 500 MeV/$c$. The effective number of nucleons A$_{\rm eff}$ was extracted from the 500 MeV/$c$ data by integrating the double differential cross sections over the quasielastic region and dividing by the appropriate K$^+$-p and K$^+$-n differential cross sections, then compared to eikonal approximation estimates and to relativistic quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) calculations with and without random phase approximation (RPA) correlations.

## Key numbers

- K$^+$ mean free path in nuclear matter at 705 MeV/$c$ is about 4 fm, compared to about 2 fm for $\pi^+$ or protons at that momentum
- K$^+$-p and K$^+$-n differential cross sections at 42° taken as 2.00 mb/sr and 1.21 mb/sr, respectively
- K$^+$-p and K$^+$-n total cross sections used: 12.40 mb and 15.76 mb, respectively
- Measured differential cross section for C at 500 MeV/$c$: 13.6 $\pm$ 1.8 mb/sr, giving A$_{\rm eff}$ = 8.47 $\pm$ 1.1 (eikonal prediction A$_{\rm eff}^{\rm E}$ = 6.7)
- Measured differential cross section for Ca at 500 MeV/$c$: 33.7 $\pm$ 4.4 mb/sr, giving A$_{\rm eff}$ = 21.0 $\pm$ 2.7 (eikonal prediction A$_{\rm eff}^{\rm E}$ = 16.0)
- Measured differential cross section for Pb at 500 MeV/$c$: 69.5 $\pm$ 9.0 mb/sr, giving A$_{\rm eff}$ = 45.7 $\pm$ 5.9 (eikonal prediction A$_{\rm eff}^{\rm E}$ = 36.0)
- Ratio A$_{\rm eff}$/A$_{\rm eff}^{\rm E}$ = 1.27 $\pm$ 0.17 for C, 1.31 $\pm$ 0.17 for Ca, and 1.27 $\pm$ 0.17 for Pb
- Effective number of protons in Pb is 18.1 out of 82; for pion single charge exchange on Bi the effective number was 9.3 out of 83 (Ouyang et al., 1993)
- K$^+$ reaches a nuclear density in Pb which is 55% of the central density; for pion SCX only 25% of the central density is reached
- Reduced nucleon effective mass used in QHD calculations for Ca: M$^*$/M = 0.8576

## Context

This work extends studies of quasielastic scattering with hadronic probes, including pions (Peterson et al., 1992), to the K$^+$ meson, which has a weaker nucleon interaction and thus probes deeper into the nuclear interior. The results contradict earlier K$^+$-nucleus elastic scattering studies (Marlow et al., 1982; Siegel et al., 1984) and total cross section measurements (Krauss et al., 1992) where multiple scattering calculations systematically underestimate the data, whereas here the eikonal approximation overestimates the K$^+$-N cross section needed to describe quasielastic scattering. The data enable tests of relativistic QHD calculations with RPA correlations, and the measured A$_{\rm eff}$ values provide constraints for understanding the K$^+$-nucleus optical potential.
