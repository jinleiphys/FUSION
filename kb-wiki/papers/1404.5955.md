# Sterile neutrinos and right-handed currents in KATRIN

> Kurie-plot experiments allow for neutrino-mass measurements based on kinematics in an almost model-independent manner. A future tritium-based KATRIN-like experiment can be sensitive to light sterile neutrinos with masses below 18 keV, which are among the prime candidates for warm dark matter. Here we consider such keV neutrinos in left--right symmetric extensions, i.e. coupled to right-handed currents, which allow for an enhanced contribution to beta decay even for small active--sterile mixing, without violating astrophysical X-ray constraints. The modified spectral shape is in principle distinguishable from the standard contribution---especially for sterile neutrino masses below 9 keV, which can lead to a distinct peak. We compare the sensitivity to constraints from the LHC and neutrinoless double beta decay.

# Sterile neutrinos and right-handed currents in KATRIN

## Key claim

A future KATRIN-like experiment measuring the full tritium beta-decay spectrum could detect keV-scale sterile neutrinos coupled to right-handed currents, with the modified spectral shape being distinguishable from the standard active-sterile mixing case, especially for sterile neutrino masses below 9 keV, where a distinct peak can appear, even when active-sterile mixing is too small to be detected by astrophysical X-ray observations.

## Method

The authors derive the differential electron energy spectrum for tritium beta decay in a left-right symmetric model, including contributions from left-handed currents (standard), right-handed currents (via W_R exchange), and W_L-W_R mixing. They expand the modified Kurie function to second order in the small parameters (W_L/W_R mass ratio, left-right mixing angle ξ, and active-sterile mixing matrix elements S_ei) and analyze the shape function S(E) that describes the deviation from the standard beta spectrum due to a keV sterile neutrino. The expected sensitivity is estimated by translating the known KATRIN reach for active-sterile mixing (sin²θ_s ~ 10⁻⁶–10⁻⁸) into constraints on the combined left-right parameters.

## Key numbers

- KATRIN's expected sensitivity to active-sterile mixing: sin²θ_s = ε ≃ 10⁻⁶–10⁻⁸ for sterile neutrino masses 1 keV ≲ m_s ≲ 17 keV
- The Q-value for tritium beta decay is Q ≃ 18.6 keV, setting the maximum sterile neutrino mass accessible
- Sterile neutrinos with mass M ≤ E₀/2 ≃ 9.3 keV can produce a spectral peak in the electron spectrum S(E) under full constructive interference (b = a)
- The maximal enhancement factor from constructive LR interference is 3√3/4 ≃ 1.3 at the peak
- The parameter C = (g_V² - 3g_A²)/(g_V² + 3g_A²) ≃ -0.65 appears in the interference terms
- Current limits from kink-searches in nuclear beta decay are sin²θ_s < 10⁻³

## Context

This work extends the study of keV sterile neutrino detection in beta decay (Shrock 1980, de Vega 2011) by incorporating left-right symmetric models, where sterile neutrinos are no longer purely sterile but couple via right-handed currents. Prior work by Bonn (2007), Stephenson (2000), and Sejersen Riis (2011) studied right-handed current effects for active neutrinos in KATRIN, but never for keV sterile neutrinos. The analysis enables comparison of KATRIN's potential reach to constraints from the LHC and neutrinoless double beta decay, and is qualitatively applicable to other precise beta-spectrum experiments such as Project 8.
