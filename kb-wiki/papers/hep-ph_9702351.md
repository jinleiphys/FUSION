# Negative Parity Baryons in the QCD Sum Rule

> Masses and couplings of the negative parity excited baryons are studied in the QCD sum rule. Separation of the negative-parity spectrum is proposed and is applied to the flavor octet and singlet baryons. We find that the quark condensate is responsible for the mass splitting of the ground and the negative-parity excited states. This is expected from the chiral symmetry and supports the idea that the negative-parity baryon forms a parity doublet with the ground state. The meson-baryon coupling constants are also computed for the excited states in the QCD sum rule. It is found that the πNN^* coupling vanishes in the chiral limit.

## Key claim

The quark condensate is responsible for the mass splitting between positive-parity ground state baryons and negative-parity excited states, supporting the idea that these states form a parity doublet that would become degenerate if chiral symmetry were restored. The pi-N-N* coupling constant vanishes in the chiral limit.

## Method

The authors used QCD sum rules with an "old-fashioned" correlation function (retarded Green's function) in the rest frame to separate positive- and negative-parity baryon contributions, which are mixed in standard time-ordered correlation functions. They applied Borel sum rules to extract masses of flavor octet and singlet baryons, and computed meson-baryon coupling constants using two-point correlation functions between the vacuum and a one-meson state in the soft meson limit. The interpolating field parameter t was chosen as t=0.8 for negative-parity states and t=-1 (Ioffe's current) for positive-parity states.

## Key numbers

- Quark condensate determined as ⟨q̄q⟩ = (-0.244 GeV)^3
- Mixed condensate parameter m0 = 0.9 GeV (where m0^2 = ⟨q̄ g σ·G q⟩ / ⟨q̄q⟩)
- Strange quark mass ms = 0.1 GeV
- Flavor SU(3) breaking parameters: χ = ⟨s̄s⟩/⟨q̄q⟩ = 0.75, χ5 = ⟨s̄ g σ·G s⟩ / ⟨q̄ g σ·G q⟩ = 0.8
- Gluon condensate fixed to ⟨(αs/π) GG⟩ = (0.36 GeV)^4
- Calculated masses: N+ = 0.94 GeV, N- = 1.54 GeV (experiment: 1.535 GeV)
- Calculated masses: Λ+ = 1.12 GeV, Λ- = 1.55 GeV (experiment: 1.67 GeV)
- Calculated masses: Σ+ = 1.21 GeV, Σ- = 1.63 GeV (experiment: 1.62 GeV)
- Calculated masses: Ξ+ = 1.32 GeV, Ξ- = 1.63 GeV (experiment: unknown)
- Calculated masses: ΛS- = 1.31 GeV, ΛS+ = 2.94 GeV (experiment: ΛS- = 1.405 GeV)
- πNN coupling gπNN ~ 13, ηNN coupling gηNN ≤ 5
- πNN* coupling gπNN* ~ 1, ηNN* coupling gηNN* ~ 2

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to baryons pioneered by Ioffe (1981) and Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979) by developing a technique to separate positive- and negative-parity contributions that are mixed in standard correlation functions. The parity-doublet interpretation connects to the linear sigma model for baryons proposed by DeTar and Kunihiro (1989). The results for Λ(1405) improve upon earlier work by Liu (1993) by including continuum contributions, and the analysis of meson-baryon couplings follows the method of Shiomi and Hatsuda (1994) for the πNN coupling. The predicted masses for negative-parity hyperons and the reversed ordering of the flavor-singlet ΛS spectrum provide testable predictions for future experiments.
