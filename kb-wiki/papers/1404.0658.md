# Energy dependence of the parity-violating asymmetry of circularly polarized photons in $d\vecγ \to np$ in pionless effective field theory

> We calculate the energy dependence of the asymmetry in the cross sections for circularly polarized photons on an unpolarized deuteron target in $d\vecγ \to np$ in pionless effective field theory. By matching the parity-violating low-energy constants to different sets of corresponding model parameters we obtain estimates for the asymmetry. In addition we calculate two possible figures of merit for the asymmetry in order to assess the preferred photon energy at which to perform a possible future experiment at a high-intensity photon source.

## Key claim

The parity-violating asymmetry \(A^\gamma_L\) in the photodisintegration of the deuteron by circularly polarized photons is calculated in pionless effective field theory (EFT) to next-to-leading order (NLO), including its energy dependence; the authors emphasize that their numerical estimates are only order-of-magnitude because the parity-violating low-energy constants (LECs) are not yet determined from experiment, and the results depend on which set of model parameters is used to match those LECs.

## Method

The authors use pionless effective field theory (EFT) in the dibaryon formulation, including both the \(Z\)-parametrization and the resummed effective range expansion (ERE) for the parity-conserving sector. They calculate the parity-violating amplitudes \(V\), \(W\), \(U_1\), \(U_2\), and \(U_3\) up to NLO, and combine them with the parity-conserving isovector M1 (\(Y\)), isoscalar M1 (\(X\)), and isovector E1 (\(E1_v\)) amplitudes to obtain the asymmetry \(A^\gamma_L\) as a function of photon energy. The parity-violating LECs are matched to the Desplanques-Donoghue-Holstein (DDH) best values and reasonable ranges, as well as to other model estimates.

## Key numbers

* The deuteron binding momentum is \(\gamma_t = 45.7025\) MeV, and the \(^1S_0\) virtual bound state pole binding momentum is \(\gamma_s = -7.890\) MeV.
* The effective range about the deuteron pole is \(\rho_t = 1.764\) fm, and the effective range about zero momentum in the \(^1S_0\) channel is \(r_s = 2.73\) fm.
* The \(^1S_0\) scattering length is \(a_s = -23.714\) fm.
* The isoscalar nucleon magnetic dipole moment is \(\kappa_0 = 0.4399\), and the isovector magnetic dipole moment is \(\kappa_1 = 2.3529\).
* The experimental \(np\) capture cross section used to fit the LEC \(L_1\) is \(\sigma^{\text{expt}} = 334.2 \pm 0.5\) mb at a neutron velocity of 2200 m/s.
* Using the DDH best values, the asymmetry \(A^\gamma_L\) is found to be of order \(10^{-7}\); the authors recommend a photon energy \(\lesssim 2.3\) MeV for a future experiment.
* For comparison, a previous DDH-based calculation using the Argonne \(v_{18}\) potential gave \(A^\gamma_L = 2.53 \times 10^{-8}\) at photon energy \(\omega = 2.235\) MeV (Liu 2004, Schiavilla 2004).

## Context

This work extends the LO threshold calculations of the asymmetry in pionless EFT by Savage (2000), Schindler (2009), and Shin (2009) to NLO and to finite photon energies, providing the first systematic energy dependence in this framework. It contrasts with earlier meson-exchange model results (Oka 1983, Khriplovich 2000, Hyun 2004, Liu 2004, Schiavilla 2004) which showed strong sensitivity to the choice of parity-conserving potential (e.g., AV18 vs. CD-Bonn) and to the values of the parity-violating couplings. The results enable experimental planning for a measurement of \(A^\gamma_L\) at the High Intensity Gamma-Ray Source (HI\(\gamma\)S) at Triangle Universities Nuclear Laboratory, and they motivate future extraction of the parity-violating LECs from other observables to reduce the theoretical uncertainty.
