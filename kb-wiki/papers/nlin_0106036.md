# A semiquantal approach to finite systems of interacting particles

> A novel approach is suggested for the statistical description of quantum systems of interacting particles. The key point of this approach is that a typical eigenstate in the energy representation (shape of eigenstates, SE) has a well defined classical analog which can be easily obtained from the classical equations of motion. Therefore, the occupation numbers for single-particle states can be represented as a convolution of the classical SE with the quantum occupation number operator for non-interacting particles. The latter takes into account the wavefunctions symmetry and depends on the unperturbed energy spectrum only. As a result, the distribution of occupation numbers $n_s$ can be numerically found for a very large number of interacting particles. Using the model of interacting spins we demonstrate that this approach gives a correct description of $n_s$ even in a deep quantum region with few single-particle orbitals.

# A semiquantal approach to finite systems of interacting particles

## Key claim

The authors propose that the distribution of occupation numbers for single-particle states in a quantum system of interacting particles can be accurately computed by replacing the quantum shape of eigenstates (SE) with its classical analog, even in deep quantum regions with few single-particle orbitals, provided the classical dynamics is chaotic. The approach avoids diagonalization of huge Hamiltonian matrices and works for systems where the classical analog of the SE can be obtained from classical equations of motion.

## Method

The method expresses the occupation number distribution n_E(h) as a convolution of the shape of eigenstates W_E(E^0_j) with the quantum occupation number operator for non-interacting particles, averaged over degenerate unperturbed many-body states. The quantum SE is replaced by its classical analog, obtained by sampling the distribution of unperturbed energy H_0 on the constant total energy surface H=E from classical trajectories in the chaotic regime. The approach is tested on a model of N interacting 3-D spins with anisotropic Ising interaction (J_x = -J_y = J, J_z = 0 in the mean-field approximation), comparing quantum diagonalization results with the semiquantal approximation and classical data.

## Key numbers

- For N=3 interacting particles with m=5 (L=11 single-particle levels) at energy E=-2.75 with ΔE=0.15, the semiquantal approach reproduces the quantum occupation number distribution, while classical data disagree in this deep quantum region.
- For N=3 particles with m=13 (L=27 single-particle levels) at the same energy E=-2.75, the semiquantal and quantum results show "very nice correspondence."
- For N=10 particles with m=3 (L=7 single-particle levels) at E=-8 with ΔE=0.2, the semiquantal distribution is closely approximated by a Bose-Einstein distribution.
- For N=10 particles with m=13 (L=27 single-particle levels) at E=-6.5 with ΔE=0.2, the semiquantal distribution again matches the Bose-Einstein distribution.
- The exact quantum treatment for N=10 particles requires diagonalization of an 8008 x 8008 matrix, while the semiquantal approach requires "few minutes on a standard portable PC."

## Context

This work extends the statistical description of chaotic eigenstates developed by Flambaum, Izrailev, and Casati (1996, 1997) for systems with two-body random interaction, where it was shown that strong interaction leads to smooth occupation number distributions. The approach is applied to a dynamical spin model previously studied by Borgonovi and Izrailev (2000), which was found to be chaotic and exponentially unstable in a wide energy range. The method enables the study of systems with up to 1000 interacting particles occupying 10-20 single-particle levels, which would be computationally prohibitive for exact diagonalization, and demonstrates that the standard Bose-Einstein distribution can emerge in closed systems with strong enough interaction acting as an internal heat bath.
