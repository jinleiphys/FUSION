# Projector operators for the no-core shell model

## Key claim
The paper suggests projection operators for the no-core shell model (NCSM) that can extract spurious-free center-of-mass (CM) components and states with definite angular momentum, spin, and isospin from the basis of Slater determinants. However, the CM "quasi-projector" does not satisfy the standard projector property P² = P unless a normalization factor is applied, and the practical advantage depends on whether the user normalizes the resulting function.

## Method
The author constructs a CM quasi-projector as a product of operators \(\widetilde{Q}_\varkappa = H_{CM} - (\varkappa + 3/2)\hbar\Omega\) for \(\varkappa = 1\) to \(N\), where \(N\) is the maximal oscillator quanta in the model space. This product, when applied sequentially to a wave function, extracts the spurious-free component with \(0\hbar\Omega\) CM excitation, up to a constant factor \(D = (-1)^N N! (\hbar\Omega)^N\). The same polynomial-product technique is extended to construct projectors for definite orbital angular momentum \(L\), total angular momentum \(J\), total spin \(S\), and isospin \(T\), using operators like \(\widetilde{Q}_L = \hat{L}^2 - L(L+1)\) and products over all accessible quantum numbers.

## Key numbers
- The normalization constant for the CM quasi-projector is \(D = (-1)^N N! (\hbar\Omega)^N\), where \(N\) is the maximal CM-excitation quanta in the model space.
- The CM Hamiltonian eigenvalues are \((\varkappa + 3/2)\hbar\Omega\) for CM-excitation quanta \(\varkappa = 0, 1, \dots, N\).
- The normalization factor for the angular momentum projector is \(D^{L_{\max}}_L = \prod_{\kappa=0}^{L-1}[\kappa(\kappa+1)-L(L+1)] \times \prod_{\kappa=L+1}^{L_{\max}}[\kappa(\kappa+1)-L(L+1)]\), where \(L_{\max}\) is the maximal accessible orbital momentum in the given \(N\hbar\Omega\) model space.

## Context
This work extends the standard NCSM approach (Vary and collaborators, various years) which uses an auxiliary Hamiltonian with a \(\beta\widetilde{Q}_0\) term to suppress spurious CM-excited states. The proposed projectors offer an alternative to the conventional \(m\)-scheme basis that includes all possible \(J\), \(L\), and \(S\) values, potentially reducing the basis size. The angular momentum projector differs from prior polynomial expansions in SU(2) generators (FOS reference) by being a finite polynomial rather than an infinite series, making it more practical for shell model codes. The CM projector is claimed to be novel. The method enables direct diagonalization of the intrinsic Hamiltonian without the auxiliary term, though the author notes that the auxiliary term remains useful for numerical stability against round-off errors in Lanczos iterations.
