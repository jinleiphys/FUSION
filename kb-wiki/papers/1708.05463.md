# Quantifying finite-momentum effects in meson quasi-PDFs

> The recently proposed large momentum effective theory (LaMET) of Ji has led to a burst of activity among lattice practitioners to perform and control the first pioneering calculations of the quasi-PDFs of the nucleon. These calculations represent approximations to the standard PDFs defined as correlation functions of fields with lightlike separation, being instead correlations along a longitudinal direction of the operator $γ^z$; as such, they differ from standard PDFs by power-suppressed $1 \big/ p^2_z$ corrections, becoming exact in the limit $p_z \to \infty$. Investigating the systematics of this behavior thus becomes crucial to understanding the validity of LaMET calculations. While this has been done using models for the nucleon, an analogous dedicated study has not been carried out for the $π$ and $ρ$ quark distribution functions. Using a constituent quark model, a systematic calculation is performed to estimate the size and $x$ dependence of the finite-$p_z$ effects in these quasi-PDFs, finding them to be potentially tamer for lighter mesons than for the collinear quasi-PDFs of the nucleon.

## Key claim

Using a constituent quark model, the author finds that finite-momentum corrections to meson quasi-PDFs may be potentially tamer for lighter mesons (π and ρ) than for the collinear quasi-PDFs of the nucleon, with the caveat that this conclusion depends on the specific model parameters and the meson mass.

## Method

The author employs a relativistic constituent quark model with pseudoscalar (π) and vector (ρ) meson-quark couplings, computing both the exact light-front (LF) parton distribution functions and the LaMET quasi-PDFs at finite longitudinal momentum p_z. The model parameters (constituent quark masses and UV cutoff regulators Λ_M) are tuned to reproduce lattice QCD results for the first moments of the meson valence distributions from Best (1997). The quasi-PDFs are then evaluated at p_z = 1, 2, and 3 GeV and compared to the exact LF distributions to quantify the finite-momentum corrections.

## Key numbers

- π meson first moment: ⟨x⟩_π = 0.279(83), tuned to Λ_π = 0.0892 GeV
- ρ meson first moment: ⟨x⟩_ρ = 0.334(21), tuned to Λ_ρ = 0.2750 GeV
- Constituent quark masses: m = m_s = 0.33 GeV for π; effective mass m^eff = 0.6 GeV for ρ
- Hadronic masses: M_π = 0.139 GeV, M_ρ = 0.77 GeV
- At p_z = 2 GeV, the π quasi-PDF approximates the exact calculation to better than 10% for x ≲ 0.7, with ~20% precision until just beyond x = 0.8
- At p_z = 2 GeV, the ρ quasi-PDF fractional deviation |δ_ρ| ≲ 10% for x ≲ 0.75
- At p_z = 1 GeV, the π valence PDF should be accessible to ~20% precision for x ≲ 0.6
- The finite-p_z effect in the π quasi-PDF at x = 0.7 is δ_π ≲ 10% at p_z = 2 GeV, compared to a factor of ~2-3 effect for the nucleon unpolarized xu(x) distribution in the quark-diquark model of Gamberg (2014)

## Context

This work extends the analysis of finite-momentum corrections in LaMET calculations, which had previously been carried out for the nucleon using quark-diquark models by Gamberg (2014), to the π and ρ meson systems for the first time. The study is motivated by the large momentum effective theory (LaMET) of Ji (2013) and addresses the need to understand systematic uncertainties in lattice QCD calculations of meson PDFs, which are currently being pursued by multiple groups. The results suggest that lattice calculations of meson quasi-PDFs may achieve precise approximations at moderate boosts, enabling future lattice computations to impact hadronic structure phenomenology, particularly for the pion.
