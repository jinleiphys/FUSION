# Color screening in cold quark matter

> We compute---at finite quark chemical potentials---the color screening of cold quark matter at the one-loop level, comparing the normal, BCS-paired U(1)em (or Higgs) phase and a singlet phase with color-singlet condensate near the Fermi surface. The latter phase is computed using the example of two-color QCD with a color-singlet diquark condensate. In contrast to the normal and Higgs phases, neither electric nor magnetic screening masses appear in the singlet phase. The absence of a magnetic mass, within a perturbative framework, is a consequence of the proper treatment of gauge invariance. While at large momenta the gluon self-energies approach those in the normal phase, the medium contributions to the infrared region below a scale of the mass gap are substantially suppressed. Infrared gluons at low quark density in the singlet phase appear protected from medium effects, unless the quark-gluon vertices are significantly enhanced in the infrared.

## Key claim

The authors compute color screening in cold quark matter at one-loop level and find that, in a phase with a color-singlet condensate (the "singlet phase"), neither electric nor magnetic screening masses appear, in contrast to the normal and Higgs phases; this absence of screening in the singlet phase is conditional on proper treatment of gauge invariance and holds only within a perturbative framework, with the caveat that infrared gluons appear protected from medium effects unless quark-gluon vertices are significantly enhanced in the infrared.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop gluon polarization function (self-energy) in cold quark matter at finite quark chemical potential, using the Nambu-Gor'kov formalism to treat quark propagators with a diquark condensate. They compare three phases: normal (gapless) quark matter, a U(1)em BCS-paired (Higgs) phase, and a singlet phase with a color-singlet diquark condensate, using two-color QCD with two flavors as the example for the singlet phase. The calculations incorporate gauge invariance constraints via the Ward-Takahashi identity and use a minimally improved vertex to remove artificial contributions that would otherwise produce spurious screening masses.

## Key numbers

- Electric screening mass in normal phase: \( m_E^2 / g_s^2 = N_f \mu^2 / \pi^2 \simeq 0.2 \, \mu^2 \) for \( N_f = 2 \) (at \( k_0 = 0, \mathbf{k} \rightarrow 0 \))
- At low density \( \mu \sim (1.5-2.0) \Lambda_{\rm QCD} \sim (0.3-0.4) \) GeV, electric screening in normal quark matter gives \( m_E^2 / g_s^2 \sim (0.45-0.8) \, \Lambda_{\rm QCD}^2 \)
- In the Higgs phase: \( m_E^2 / g_s^2 \sim \mu^2 \) and \( m_M^2 / g_s^2 \sim \mu^2 \)
- In the singlet phase: \( m_E^2(k)/g_s^2 \sim \mathbf{k}^2 \times (\mu^2 / \Delta^2) + \cdots \) and \( m_M^2(k) \sim 0 \) at small \( \mathbf{k} \)

## Context

This work extends the study of screening in dense QCD matter beyond the hard-dense-loop (HDL) approximation used by Son (1998) for weak coupling, addressing the intermediate chemical potential regime \( \mu \sim \Lambda_{\rm QCD} \) relevant for neutron star physics. The authors compare their results for the singlet phase to the two-flavor color superconductor with residual SUc(2) symmetry studied by Rischke (2000), and to the asymptotic deconfinement scenario proposed by Rischke, Son, and Stephanov (2000). The singlet phase results also provide insights for inhomogeneous chiral condensate phases studied by Nickel (2009) and Kojo (2009), and the two-color QCD system enables comparison with lattice studies by Nakamura (1984), Kogut (2001), and Cotter (2012).
