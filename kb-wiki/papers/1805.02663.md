# Second-order Hydrodynamics in Next-to-Leading-Order QCD

> We compute the hydrodynamic relaxation times $τ_π$ and $τ_j$ for hot QCD at next-to-leading order in the coupling with kinetic theory. We show that certain dimensionless ratios of second-order to first-order transport coefficients obey bounds which apply whenever a kinetic theory description is possible; the computed values lie somewhat above these bounds. Strongly coupled theories with holographic duals strongly violate these bounds, highlighting their distance from a quasiparticle description.

## Key claim
The paper computes the second-order hydrodynamic relaxation times τ_π and τ_j for hot QCD at next-to-leading order (NLO) in the coupling, and shows that certain dimensionless ratios of second-order to first-order transport coefficients obey universal lower bounds (τ_π/(η/(ε+P)) ≥ 5 and τ_j/D_q ≥ 3) that hold for any kinetic theory description; the computed NLO values lie somewhat above these bounds, while strongly coupled holographic theories violate them by roughly a factor of two.

## Method
The authors use a linearized kinetic theory framework for QCD, solving the Boltzmann equation with a collision operator that includes both 2↔2 elastic scatterings and effective 1↔2 inelastic processes. They extend previous leading-order (LO) calculations of the collision operator to (almost) NLO by incorporating soft scattering corrections (δq̂), in-medium dispersion shifts, and subtractions for overlapping LO contributions, then solve for the linearized departure from equilibrium χ(p) using a variational method. The second-order coefficients τ_π and τ_j are obtained from inner products of χ with itself, using the relations η τ_π = (β/15)(χ,χ) and κ τ_j = (1/3)(χ,χ).

## Key numbers
- The dimensionless ratio τ_π/(η/(ε+P)) has a lower bound of 5 for any kinetic theory; the NLO QCD result at α_s ∼ 0.3 is approximately 7.5 (about 1.5 times the bound).
- The dimensionless ratio τ_j/D_q has a lower bound of 3 for any kinetic theory; the NLO QCD result at α_s ∼ 0.3 is approximately 4.5 (about 1.5 times the bound).
- For strongly coupled N=4 SYM theory with a holographic dual, τ_π/(η/(ε+P)) = 4 − 2 ln(2) ≈ 2.61, which is roughly half the kinetic theory minimum of 5.
- For strongly coupled N=4 SYM theory, τ_j/D_{U(1)} = π/2 ≈ 1.57, which is roughly half the kinetic theory minimum of 3.
- The LO results for both ratios decrease with increasing coupling toward the bounds, while the NLO results increase above the LO values for m_D/T > 0.5 (α_s ∼ 0.3).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous NLO calculations of shear viscosity and baryon-number diffusion (Ghiglieri, Moore, Teaney, 2018) to second-order transport coefficients, and builds on the LO determination of τ_π by York and Moore (2008) and the leading-log estimate of τ_j by Hong (2010). The universal bounds derived here contrast sharply with results from strongly coupled N=4 SYM theory via AdS/CFT (Baier et al., 2007; Bu, 2015), which violate the bounds by roughly a factor of two, demonstrating that such holographic theories are far from having a quasiparticle description. The results provide phenomenologically useful relations for heavy-ion collision simulations, showing that the dimensionless ratios vary by at most 40% across coupling strengths and between LO and NLO, making them robust inputs for hydrodynamic modeling.
