# Diquark-diquark correlations in the $^1S_0$ $ΛΛ$ potential

> We derive a $ΛΛ$ potential from a chiral constituent quark model that has been successful in describing one, two and three nonstrange baryon systems. The resulting interaction at low energy is attractive at all distances due to the $σ$ exchange term. The attraction allows for a slightly bound state just below the $ΛΛ$ threshold. No short-range repulsive core is found. We extract the diquark-diquark contribution that turns out to be the most attractive and probable at small distances. At large distances the asymptotic behavior of the $ΛΛ$ interaction provides a prediction for the $σΛΛ$ coupling constant.

# Diquark-diquark correlations in the $^1S_0$ $\Lambda\Lambda$ potential

## Key claim

Contrary to previous quark model results that found a repulsive core in the $\Lambda\Lambda$ interaction, the authors show that in a chiral constituent quark model tightly constrained by nonstrange baryon data, the $^1S_0$ $\Lambda\Lambda$ potential is attractive at all distances, with no short-range repulsive core. This attraction is driven by the sigma meson exchange, which dominates over the combined gluon, pion, and eta repulsion, but the resulting bound state is extremely shallow (0.022 MeV binding), not a tightly bound H-dibaryon.

## Method

The authors use a chiral constituent quark model (CCM) with confinement, one-gluon exchange, and pseudoscalar (pion, kaon, eta) plus scalar (sigma) meson exchanges between quarks, with parameters fixed from nonstrange baryon spectra and baryon-baryon data. The $\Lambda\Lambda$ potential in the $^1S_0$ channel is computed using the Born-Oppenheimer (adiabatic) approximation within the Resonating Group Method framework, and bound states are searched for via the Fredholm determinant method applied to the Lippmann-Schwinger equation. The diquark-diquark component of the wave function is isolated by projecting onto the $\phi^{MA}\chi^{MA}$ flavor-spin configuration of each $\Lambda$.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the $\Lambda\Lambda$ system: 0.022 MeV, just below the $\Lambda\Lambda$ threshold
- Ratio of sigma coupling constants: $g_{\sigma\Lambda\Lambda} / g_{\sigma NN} = 0.88$
- Strange quark mass: $m_s = 555$ MeV
- Light quark mass: $m_{u,d} = 313$ MeV
- Gluon coupling constant: $\alpha_s = 0.54$
- Chiral coupling constant: $g_{ch}^2 / 4\pi = 0.54$
- Sigma meson mass: $m_\sigma = 3.42$ fm$^{-1}$
- Pion mass: $m_\pi = 0.7$ fm$^{-1}$
- Kaon mass: $m_K = 2.51$ fm$^{-1}$
- Eta mass: $m_\eta = 2.77$ fm$^{-1}$
- Confinement strength: $a_c = 185.0$ MeV fm$^{-1}$
- At $R = 0.01$ fm, the gluon repulsion in $\Lambda\Lambda$ is about one third of that in $NN$, and the pion repulsion is about one fifth of that in $NN$
- Diquark-diquark probability increases from 0.25 at large separation to nearly 0.5 at very short interbaryonic distances
- Variation of $m_s$ by 150 MeV changes the potential by at most 15%; varying $\Lambda_{\eta,K}$ changes it by about 5%

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model (CCM) of Fernandez, Valcarce, Straub, and Faessler (1993) and Valcarce et al. (2005), which successfully describes nonstrange one-, two-, and three-baryon systems, to the strange baryon sector. It contradicts the prevailing conclusion from earlier quark approaches (reviewed by Sakai, Shimizu, and Yazaki, 2000) that the $\Lambda\Lambda$ interaction has a repulsive core, and also disagrees with models that predicted a tightly bound H-dibaryon (Jaffe, 1977). The result enables a consistent microscopic description of $\Lambda\Lambda$ hypernuclei using the same interaction for $NN$, $N\Lambda$, and $\Lambda\Lambda$ systems, and provides a prediction for the $\sigma\Lambda\Lambda$ coupling constant that can be tested against baryonic models such as those of Reuber, Holinde, and Speth (1994) and Maessen, Rijken, and de Swart (1989).
