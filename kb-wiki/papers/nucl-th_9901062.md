# Beyond Mean Field Confrontation of Different Models with High Transverse Momentum Proton Spectra

> Several models have been proposed to simulate heavy ion reactions beyond the mean field level. The lack of data in phase space regions which may be sensitive to different treatments of fluctuations made it difficult to judge these approaches. The recently published high energy proton spectra, measured in the reaction 94 AMeV Ar + Ta, allow for the first time for a comparison of the models with data. We find that these spectra are reproduced by Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) and Boltzmann Uehling Uhlenbeck (BUU) calculations. Models like Boltzmann Langevin (BL) in which additional fluctuations in momentum space are introduced overpredict the proton yield at very high energies. The BL approach has been successfully used to describe the recently measured very subthreshold kaon production assuming that the fluctuations provide the necessary energy to overcome the threshold in two body collisions. Our new findings suggest that the very subthreshold kaon production cannot be due to two body scattering and thus remains a open problem.

## Key claim

High-transverse-momentum proton spectra from 94 AMeV Ar+Ta collisions are reproduced by Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) and Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) calculations, but the Boltzmann-Langevin (BL) approach, which introduces additional fluctuations in momentum space, overpredicts the proton yield at very high energies; this suggests that the very subthreshold kaon production previously attributed to two-body scattering in BL models cannot be due to such scattering and remains an open problem.

## Method

The authors compared experimental proton spectra from the reaction 94 AMeV Ar+Ta with simulations from three transport models: QMD (an n-body molecular dynamics approach using Gaussian wave packets), BUU (a one-body mean-field theory with a collision term), and a specific realization of the Boltzmann-Langevin approach called BL_PM (which uses a projection method to inject quadrupole-dominated fluctuations in momentum space at each time step). For each model, they computed the double differential cross section dσ/dΩ dE for protons emitted at sideward angles (50°–100°) and compared the slopes and absolute yields to data in the kinetic energy range 175–350 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The apparent temperature extracted from the exponential slope of the BL_PM calculation is 76 MeV, while for BUU and QMD it is 22 MeV.
- The difference between BL_PM and the other models reaches 3 orders of magnitude at proton kinetic energy of 225 MeV.
- QMD yields an apparent temperature of order 17 MeV in the window 175–250 MeV, giving the best agreement with data.
- In QMD and BUU, no NN collisions occur with √s larger than 2.3 GeV; in BL_PM, collisions with √s above the kaon production threshold of 2.548 GeV are observed.
- For the reaction Ar+Ta at 92 AMeV, the measured kaon production cross section is σ_K+ = (2.9 ± 1.6) × 10⁻⁹ b; the BL_PM simulation (Q2+Q3 setup) yields a kaon cross section of 1.6 × 10⁻⁹ b, comparable to experiment.

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons of QMD and BUU by Aichelin et al. (1989) to a new phase-space region, high-transverse-momentum protons, made accessible by recent experimental data from Germain et al. (1997). The results contradict the interpretation of very subthreshold kaon production at 92 AMeV (Julien et al., 1991; Lecolley, 1995) as arising from two-body scattering in BL models (Belkacem et al., 1993), because the same BL fluctuations that produce kaons also overproduce high-energy protons. The findings point toward a collective kaon production mechanism not present in QMD or BUU, or toward strong in-medium modifications of strange particle properties, though calculations by Schaffner-Bielich et al. (1997) indicate the kaon production threshold increases in nuclear matter.
