# Heavy Hadrons in Dense Matter

> We study the behavior of dynamically-generated baryon resonances with heavy-quark content within a unitarized coupled-channel theory in matter that fulfills heavy-quark spin symmetry constraints. We analyze the implications for the formation of charmed mesic nuclei and the propagation of heavy mesons in heavy-ion collisions from RHIC to FAIR.

# Heavy Hadrons in Dense Matter

## Key claim
Dynamically-generated heavy baryon resonances with charm and bottom content, obtained from a unitarized coupled-channel theory incorporating heavy-quark spin symmetry, are well described as molecular states composed of s-wave meson-baryon pairs only when they are narrow resonances or bound states; wider states show smaller compositeness, indicating missing contributions in the model.

## Method
The authors employ a unitarized coupled-channel approach using an extended Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction as the kernel of the on-shell Bethe-Salpeter equation, with heavy-quark spin symmetry constraints incorporated to connect vector and pseudoscalar meson sectors. They compute scattering amplitudes for meson-baryon channels with heavy (charm/bottom), strange, and isospin content (H=1, S=0, I=0) to extract poles corresponding to Lambda states. The generalized Weinberg sum rule is used to quantify the compositeness of each resonance in terms of its coupling to the dominant meson-baryon channels.

## Key numbers
- The narrow Lambda_c(2595) state (J^P = 1/2^-) has mass 2619 MeV, width 1.2 MeV, and compositeness 1-Z = 0.878, with dominant couplings to D N (|g_i| = 3.49) and D* N (|g_i| = 5.64)
- The wide Lambda_c(2595) state (J^P = 1/2^-) has mass 2617 MeV, width 90 MeV, and compositeness 1-Z = 0.401, with dominant coupling to pi Sigma_c (|g_i| = 2.36)
- The Lambda_c(2625) state (J^P = 3/2^-) has mass 2667 MeV, width 55 MeV, and compositeness 1-Z = 0.365, with dominant coupling to pi Sigma_c* (|g_i| = 2.19)
- The Lambda_b(5912) bound state (J^P = 1/2^-) has mass 5878 MeV, width 0 MeV, and compositeness 1-Z = 0.956, with dominant couplings to anti-B N (|g_i| = 4.55) and anti-B* N (|g_i| = 7.70)
- The second Lambda_b(5912) state (J^P = 1/2^-) has mass 5949 MeV, width 0 MeV, and compositeness 1-Z = 0.865, with dominant coupling to pi Sigma_b (|g_i| = 1.31)
- The Lambda_b(5920) state (J^P = 3/2^-) has mass 5963 MeV, width 0 MeV, and compositeness 1-Z = 0.818, with dominant coupling to pi Sigma_b* (|g_i| = 1.54)

## Context
This work extends the unitarized coupled-channel approach with heavy-quark spin symmetry developed by Garcia-Recio et al. (2008, 2012, 2013, 2015) and Tolos et al. (2013) to study heavy baryon resonances in dense matter. The results contrast with earlier predictions by Tsushima et al. (1998) using a quark-meson coupling model, which found strongly bound D-meson states in nuclei; the present model predicts only weakly bound D^0-nucleus states with significant widths. The findings enable further studies of heavy-meson propagation in heavy-ion collisions from RHIC to FAIR energies, particularly through Fokker-Planck transport coefficients, and inform the search for charmed mesic nuclei at the PANDA experiment.
