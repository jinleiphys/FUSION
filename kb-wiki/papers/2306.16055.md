# Neutrino spectrum and energy loss rates due to weak processes on hot $^{56}$Fe in pre-supernova environment

> Applying TQRPA calculations of Gamow--Teller strength functions in hot nuclei, we compute the (anti)neutrino spectra and energy loss rates arising from weak processes on hot $^{56}$Fe under pre-supernova conditions. We use a realistic pre-supernova model calculated by the stellar evolution code MESA. Taking into account both charged and neutral current processes, we demonstrate that weak reactions with hot nuclei can produce high-energy (anti)neutrinos. We also show that, for hot nuclei, the energy loss via (anti)neutrino emission is significantly larger than that for nuclei in their ground state. It is found that the neutral current de-excitation via the $ν\barν$-pair emission is presumably a dominant source of antineutrinos. In accordance with other studies, we confirm that the so-called single-state approximation for neutrino spectra might fail under certain pre-supernova conditions. }

## Key claim
Using TQRPA calculations of Gamow-Teller strength functions in hot nuclei, the authors compute that weak reactions on hot $^{56}$Fe under pre-supernova conditions produce high-energy (anti)neutrinos and that the energy loss via (anti)neutrino emission is significantly larger than for ground-state nuclei, though the single-state approximation for neutrino spectra may fail under certain pre-supernova conditions.

## Method
The authors applied the Thermo-Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (TQRPA) to calculate Gamow-Teller strength functions for hot $^{56}$Fe, then used these to compute (anti)neutrino spectra and energy loss rates from both charged-current and neutral-current weak processes. The calculations were performed for a realistic pre-supernova model generated by the stellar evolution code MESA, considering the thermal population of nuclear excited states.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of weak processes in pre-supernova environments by applying TQRPA to hot $^{56}$Fe, a key nucleus in stellar evolution. It builds on earlier calculations of Gamow-Teller strength distributions in hot nuclei by the same group and others, and it confirms findings from other studies (e.g., those using the single-state approximation) that such approximations can be inadequate. The results enable more accurate modeling of neutrino emission and energy loss in core-collapse supernova simulations, particularly for the silicon-burning and oxygen-burning stages where $^{56}$Fe is abundant.
