# The proton magnetic radius: a new puzzle?

## Key claim
The authors argue that conflicting determinations of the proton magnetic radius, between dispersion-relation analyses, lattice QCD calculations, and direct electron-scattering experiments, constitute a new puzzle analogous to the earlier proton charge radius puzzle, but they hedge that the discrepancy is not yet resolved and that current lattice QCD precision is insufficient to settle the issue.

## Method
The authors compare the proton magnetic radius \(r_M\) extracted from dispersion-relation analyses of electron-proton scattering data (including time-like region data) with values from lattice QCD calculations at physical pion masses and with direct experimental fits from the A1 collaboration. They also examine the relationship between the Zemach radius \(r_Z\) and \(r_M\) using both generalized dipole form factors and dispersion-theoretical parametrizations fitted to PRad data, to assess whether atomic-physics measurements could provide an independent constraint.

## Key numbers
- Dispersion-relation analysis gives \(r_M = 0.849^{+0.003}_{-0.003}{}^{+0.001}_{-0.004}\) fm (Lin et al., 2021), where the first error is statistical and the second systematic.
- The A1 collaboration (Bernauer et al., 2010) reports \(r_M = 0.777(13)_{\rm stat.}(9)_{\rm syst.}(5)_{\rm model}(2)_{\rm group}\) fm.
- Lattice QCD from Mainz (Djukanovic et al., 2023) gives \(r_M = 0.8111(89)\) fm, roughly a \(4\sigma\) deviation from the dispersive value.
- The isovector magnetic radius from dispersion relations is \(r_M^V = 0.854(1)(3)\) fm; lattice results range from \(0.742(27)(--)\) fm (Cyprus, Alexandrou et al., 2022) to \(0.814(7)(5)\) fm (Mainz, Djukanovic et al., 2023).
- The dispersive Zemach radius is \(r_Z = 1.054^{+0.003}_{-0.002}{}^{+0.000}_{-0.001}\) fm, consistent within \(1\sigma\) with muonic hydrogen spectroscopy giving \(r_Z = 1.082(37)\) fm (Antognini et al., 2013).

## Context
This work extends the discussion of the proton radius puzzle, previously focused on the electric radius \(r_E\), to the magnetic radius \(r_M\), highlighting a new discrepancy between dispersion-relation analyses (Lin et al., 2021) and lattice QCD results (Djukanovic et al., 2023; Tsuji et al., 2023; Alexandrou et al., 2022). The authors note that the A1 collaboration's direct fit (Bernauer et al., 2010) yields a much smaller \(r_M\) but produces a magnetic form factor with a bump-dip structure inconsistent with unitarity and analyticity (Belushkin et al., 2006). They propose that a precise determination of the Zemach radius from atomic spectroscopy could provide an independent cross-check, and that future lattice QCD calculations with higher precision may resolve the conflict.
