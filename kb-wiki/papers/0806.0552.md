# Nuclear isospin mixing and elastic parity-violating electron scattering

> The influence of nuclear isospin mixing on parity-violating elastic electron scattering is studied for the even-even, N=Z nuclei 12C, 24Mg, 28Si, and 32S. Their ground-state wave functions have been obtained using a self-consistent axially-symmetric mean-field approximation with density-dependent effective two-body Skyrme interactions. Some differences from previous shell-model calculations appear for the isovector Coulomb form factors which play a role in determining the parity-violating asymmetry. To gain an understanding of how these differences arise, the results have been expanded in a spherical harmonic oscillator basis. Results are obtained not only within the plane-wave Born approximation, but also using the distorted-wave Born approximation for comparison with potential future experimental studies of parity-violating electron scattering. To this end, for each nucleus the focus is placed on kinematic ranges where the signal (isospin-mixing effects on the parity-violating asymmetry) and the experimental figure-of-merit are maximized. Strangeness contributions to the asymmetry are also briefly discussed, since they and the isospin mixing contributions may play comparable roles for the nuclei being studied at the low momentum transfers of interest in the present work.

# Nuclear isospin mixing and elastic parity-violating electron scattering

## Key claim

Isospin mixing in nuclear ground states of N=Z nuclei produces deviations in the parity-violating asymmetry that are characteristically at the level of a few percent for light nuclei and up to more than 10% for heavier nuclei, within momentum transfers up to about 1 fm⁻¹ where the experimental figure-of-merit is favorable. These effects are comparable in size to strangeness contributions at the low momentum transfers of interest, and must be accounted for in precision tests of the Standard Model and in determinations of neutron densities from parity-violating electron scattering.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent axially-symmetric Hartree-Fock calculations with Skyrme interactions (SLy4 and two other forces) including BCS pairing correlations, with the Coulomb interaction included non-perturbatively in the mean field to generate isospin-mixed ground states for ¹²C, ²⁴Mg, ²⁸Si, and ³²S. The deformed HF+BCS single-particle wave functions were expanded in a spherical harmonic oscillator basis of 11 major shells to compute the isoscalar and isovector Coulomb monopole form factors, and the parity-violating asymmetry was evaluated both in plane-wave Born approximation and using distorted-wave Born approximation with a partial-wave treatment of Coulomb distortion. The nucleon electromagnetic form factors were taken from the Höhler parametrization, and strangeness contributions were included using a standard parametrization with parameters ρₛ and μₛ.

## Key numbers

- The reference parity-violating asymmetry for a pure T=0 ground state is A⁰ = 3.22 × 10⁻⁶ |Q²| (with Q² in fm⁻²), corresponding to a_A β_V^(0) = 2 sin²θ_W = 0.46 within the Standard Model.
- The weak neutral current coupling constants used are: β_V^(p) = 0.04, β_V^(n) = -0.5, β_V^(0) = -0.46, β_V^(1) = 0.54, and β_V^(s) = -1.
- The figure-of-merit for parity-violating measurements typically peaks at momentum transfers around 0.5 fm⁻¹.
- Isospin-mixing effects on the asymmetry reach up to more than 10% for the heavier nuclei considered (³²S) at momentum transfers around 1 fm⁻¹.
- The unpolarized elastic cross sections calculated from HF+BCS densities show good agreement with experimental data up to scattering angles of 60° (momentum transfer around 2 fm⁻¹) for all four nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Donnelly, Dubach and Sick (1989), who first examined isospin-mixing effects on parity-violating electron scattering using a simple two-level shell-model approach with only one active shell in a spherical harmonic oscillator basis and without Coulomb distortion. The present calculation improves upon that work by using a self-consistent mean-field approach with Skyrme forces that includes Coulomb effects non-perturbatively, pairing correlations, deformation, spin-orbit corrections to the Coulomb monopole operators, modern electroweak nucleon form factors, and full treatment of Coulomb distortion of electron waves. The results are relevant for interpreting experiments such as HAPPEX-He at Jefferson Lab, which have achieved parity-violating asymmetry measurements at the 4% level, and for the PREX experiment on ²⁰⁸Pb, which aims for 3% precision in the asymmetry to determine the neutron radius. The work also enables future studies where isospin mixing and strangeness contributions must be disentangled to extract neutron densities from parity-violating electron scattering.
