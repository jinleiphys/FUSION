# Energy loss in high energy heavy ion collisions from the Hydro+Jet model

> We investigate the effect of energy loss of jets in high energy heavy ion collisions by using a full three-dimensional space-time evolution of a fluid combined with (mini-)jets that are explicitly evolved in space-time. In order to fit the pi^0 data for the Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_{NN}) = 130 GeV, the space-time averaged energy loss dE/dx(tau <= 3 fm/c) = 0.36 GeV/fm is extracted within the model. It is found that most energy loss occurs at the very early time less than 2 fm/c in the QGP phase and that energy loss in the mixed phase is negligible within our parameterization for jet energy loss. This is a consequence of strong expansion of the system.

## Key claim

The paper extracts a space-time averaged energy loss of 0.36 GeV/fm for jets in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV, but this value is conditional on averaging only over the first 3 fm/c of the collision evolution and depends on the specific parameterization of energy loss as proportional to local parton density.

## Method

The authors combine a full three-dimensional hydrodynamic model of the thermalized medium with (mini-)jets generated from PYTHIA 6.2 using CTEQ5 leading-order parton distribution functions and a K-factor of 2. Jets are explicitly propagated in space-time through the fluid, and their energy loss is modeled as dE/dx = epsilon * sigma * rho(tau, r), where the parton density rho is obtained from the hydrodynamic temperature evolution, with epsilon*sigma treated as a free parameter fit to PHENIX pi0 data.

## Key numbers

- Space-time averaged energy loss dE/dx(tau <= 3 fm/c) = 0.36 GeV/fm extracted for central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV
- Best fit energy loss parameter epsilon*sigma = 0.06 GeV fm^2 reproduces the PHENIX pi0 spectrum for central 10% collisions (b = 3.35 fm)
- Maximum initial energy density at tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c is 33.7 GeV/fm^3 for 5% central collisions
- Average parton density values: 11 fm^{-3} (tau <= 1.0 fm/c), 7.7 fm^{-3} (tau <= 2.0 fm/c), 6.0 fm^{-3} (tau <= 3.0 fm/c)
- Corresponding energy losses: dE/dx(tau <= 1.0 fm/c) ~ 0.66 GeV/fm, dE/dx(tau <= 2.0 fm/c) ~ 0.46 GeV/fm
- About 90% of jet quenching occurs within tau < 2 fm/c
- QGP phase lifetime is about 4 fm/c; mixed phase lasts until 9-10 fm/c
- Minimum momentum transfer p_T,min = 2.0 GeV/c; Gaussian k_T width <k_T^2> = 1 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends prior phenomenological studies by Wang (2001) and others that extracted space-time averaged energy losses of 0.25-0.3 GeV/fm using pQCD parton models without dynamical expansion effects. By incorporating full three-dimensional hydrodynamics from Hirano (2001, 2002), the authors demonstrate that most jet energy loss occurs in the pure QGP phase within the first 2 fm/c, with negligible contribution from the mixed phase, and that the extracted average energy loss is close to previous pQCD results because it effectively averages over this short early time window. The Hydro+Jet model enables future studies of higher p_T predictions and elliptic flow parameters.
