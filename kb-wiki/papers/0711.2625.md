# Generalized parton distributions and the structure of the nucleon

> Generalized parton distributions have been introduced in recent years as a suitable theoretical tool to study the structure of the nucleon. Unifying the concepts of parton distributions and hadronic form factors, they provide a comprehensive framework for describing the quark and gluon structure of the nucleon. In this review their formal properties and modeling are discussed, summarizing the most recent developments in the phenomenological description of these functions. The status of available data is also presented.

## Key claim

Generalized parton distributions (GPDs) unify the concepts of ordinary parton distributions and hadronic form factors into a comprehensive framework for describing the quark and gluon structure of the nucleon, but they do not represent a probability; instead, they give access to momentum correlations of partons in the nucleon through interference between amplitudes describing different parton configurations.

## Method

The paper is a review that discusses the formal properties of GPDs, including their definition as nondiagonal matrix elements of light-cone operators, their relation to deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and hard meson production, and their evolution equations. It summarizes various theoretical approaches for modeling GPDs, such as quark models, lattice QCD simulations, and the chiral quark-soliton model, and presents the status of experimental data from HERA, HERMES, CLAS, and Jefferson Lab.

## Key numbers

- The Bjorken variable \(x_B = Q^2/2p \cdot q\) (or \(x_B = Q^2/2M_N \nu\) for a nucleon at rest) defines the fractional longitudinal momentum of the struck parton in deep inelastic scattering.
- The momentum transfer squared \(Q^2 = -q^2 = \mathbf{q}^2 - \nu^2\) becomes very large in the Bjorken regime, where scaling occurs and structure functions become independent of \(Q^2\) at fixed \(x_B\).
- Three kinds of quark distributions are distinguished: the quark density \(q(x_B)\), the helicity distribution \(\Delta q(x_B)\), and the transversity \(\Delta_T q(x_B)\); the last decouples from inclusive DIS and cannot be measured in that process.
- The transverse separation of the quark-antiquark pair probed in GPD processes is of order \(1/Q\).

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on GPDs by Dittes, Muller, Robaschik, Geyer, and Horejsi (1988) and Muller, Robaschik, Geyer, Dittes, and Horejsi (1994), and the seminal papers by Ji (1997) and Radyushkin (1996) that connected GPDs to DVCS and the spin structure of the nucleon. It also builds on the factorization proof by Collins, Frankfurt, and Strikman (1997). The review enables future work on three-dimensional imaging of the nucleon via the transverse spatial distribution of partons, and on extracting the total angular momentum contribution of quarks to the nucleon spin.
