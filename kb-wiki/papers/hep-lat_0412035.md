# The critical end point of QCD

> We investigate the critical end point of QCD with two flavours of light dynamical quarks at finite lattice cutoff a=1/4T using a Taylor expansion of the baryon number susceptibility. We find a strong volume dependence of the position of the critical end point. In the large volume limit we obtain T^E/T_c \~ 0.95 and mu_B^E/T^E ~ 1.1, where T_c is the cross over temperature at zero chemical potential, and T^E and mu_B^E are the temperature and the baryon chemical potential at the critical end point. The small value of mu_B^E places it in the range of observability in energy scans at the RHIC.

## Key claim

The authors locate the QCD critical end point for two-flavor QCD using a Taylor expansion of the baryon number susceptibility, finding a strong volume dependence of its position; the quoted values \(T^E/T_c \approx 0.95\) and \(\mu_B^E/T^E \approx 1.1\) are a lower bound on the critical end point in the large volume limit, not a definitive determination.

## Method

The authors perform lattice QCD simulations with two flavors of light dynamical staggered quarks at a fixed lattice cutoff \(a = 1/4T\) (temporal extent \(N_t = 4\)), using a Taylor expansion of the baryon number susceptibility in powers of \(\mu_B/T\) to extrapolate from zero chemical potential. They compute the non-linear susceptibilities (NLS) up to 8th order via noisy trace evaluation and conjugate gradient matrix inversion, then analyze the radius of convergence of the series for \(\chi_B\) on lattices with spatial sizes \(N_s = 8, 10, 12, 16, 24\) to study finite volume effects.

## Key numbers

- The critical end point in the large volume limit is found at \(T^E/T_c \approx 0.95\) and \(\mu_B^E/T^E \approx 1.1\), where \(T_c\) is the crossover temperature at zero chemical potential.
- The quark mass ratio is \(m_\pi/m_\rho = 0.31 \pm 0.01\) at a lattice spacing where \(m_\rho/T_c = 5.4 \pm 0.2\).
- The spatial lattice sizes span \(N_s m_\pi \approx 3\) to \(10\), with strong "small" volume effects observed at the lower end and a more stable physical value for larger \(N_s\).
- The temperature scale is given in the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme, with simulations performed at \(T/T_c\) values ranging from \(0.75 \pm 0.02\) to \(2.15 \pm 0.10\) across multiple lattice volumes.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice estimates of the QCD critical end point for \(N_f = 2\) and \(N_f = 2+1\) flavors, which used reweighting (Fodor and Katz, 2002), analytic continuation from imaginary chemical potential (de Forcrand and Philipsen, 2002), and Taylor expansions (Allton et al., 2002). The authors use significantly larger lattices than previous studies with comparable quark masses, enabling the first systematic investigation of finite volume effects on the critical end point. The small value of \(\mu_B^E\) places it within reach of energy scans at RHIC, and the method could eventually be extended to determine critical exponents as computing resources allow continuum extrapolations.
