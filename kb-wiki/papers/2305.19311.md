# Renormalons in the energy-energy correlator

> The energy-energy correlator (EEC) is an observable of wide interest for collider physics and Standard Model measurements, due to both its simple theoretical description in terms of the energy-momentum tensor and its novel features for experimental studies. Significant progress has been made in both applications and higher-order perturbative predictions for the EEC. Here, we analyze the nature of the asymptotic perturbative series for the EEC by determining its analytic form in Borel space under the bubble-sum approximation. This result provides information on the leading and subleading nonperturbative power corrections through renormalon poles. We improve the perturbative convergence of the $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ series for the EEC by removing its leading renormalon using an R scheme, which is independent of the bubble-sum approximation. Using the leading R-scheme power correction determined by fits to thrust, we find good agreement with EEC OPAL data already at ${\mathcal O}(α_s^2)$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the leading renormalon in the energy-energy correlator (EEC) appears at $u=1/2$ in Borel space under the bubble-sum approximation, confirming the $1/Q$ power correction structure derived earlier by Korchemsky and collaborators, but the authors caution that this renormalon-based calculation does not account for potential ${\cal O}(1)$ hadron-mass corrections that could spoil the universality of power corrections between thrust and the EEC.

## Method

The authors computed the bubble-sum approximation for the EEC in $e^+e^-$ collisions by replacing the external gluon in leading-order diagrams with chains of fermion loops, then performing a Borel transform to identify renormalon poles on the positive real $u$-axis. They analytically evaluated the phase-space integrals for both $q\bar q$ and $qg/\bar q g$ contributions in Borel space, obtaining closed-form expressions involving hypergeometric functions. They then applied an R-scheme transformation to remove the leading $u=1/2$ renormalon from the $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ perturbative series, using the universality of the $\bar\Omega_1$ parameter between thrust and the EEC to fix the leading power correction from fits to thrust data.

## Key numbers

- The leading renormalon residue at $u=1/2$ is $\Res_{1/2} = -\frac{4 C_F}{\pi\beta_0}\frac{\mu e^{c/2}}{Q}\frac{1}{[z(1-z)]^{3/2}}$, giving an ambiguity $\Delta_{1/2} = -\frac{8 i C_F e^{5/6}}{\beta_0}\frac{1}{[z(1-z)]^{3/2}} \frac{\Lambda_{\rm QCD}}{Q}$ in the $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ perturbative series.
- The ambiguity in the hadronic parameter $\bar\Omega_1$ from the bubble-sum calculation is $\Delta_{1/2}(\bar\Omega_1) = \Lambda_{\rm QCD} \frac{16 i C_{F} e^{5/6}}{\beta_0}$, confirming cancellation with the EEC ambiguity.
- The R-scheme results at ${\cal O}(\alpha_s^2)$ show good agreement with EEC data from the OPAL experiment at LEP, using the leading R-scheme power correction determined by fits to thrust.

## Context

This work extends the renormalon analysis of Nason and collaborators (1995) for weighted integrals of the EEC to the full angular dependence, and provides an independent confirmation of the $1/\sin^3\chi$ power correction structure derived by Korchemsky and collaborators (1999) using operator methods. The bubble-sum calculation finds no renormalon corresponding to an ${\cal O}(1/Q^2)$ power correction. The R-scheme improvement follows the approach of Hoang and collaborators (2009) and Abbate and collaborators (2010), who applied similar methods to thrust and other event shapes. This enables improved perturbative predictions for the EEC that can be compared directly to experimental data from LEP and the LHC.
