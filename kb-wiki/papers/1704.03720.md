# Determination of the density and temperature dependence of the shear viscosity of a unitary Fermi gas based on hydrodynamic flow

> We determine the shear viscosity of the ultracold Fermi gas at unitarity in the normal phase using hydrodynamic expansion data. The analysis is based on a generalized fluid dynamic framework which ensures a smooth transition between the fluid dynamic core of the cloud and the ballistic corona. We use expansion data taken by Joseph et al. and measurements of the equation of state by Ku et al. We find that the shear viscosity to particle density ratio just above the critical temperature is $η/n|_{T_c}=0.41\pm 0.11$. We also obtain evidence that the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio has a minimum slightly above $T_c$ with $η/s |_{\it min}=0.50\pm 0.10$.

# Determination of the density and temperature dependence of the shear viscosity of a unitary Fermi gas based on hydrodynamic flow

## Key claim

The shear viscosity of the unitary Fermi gas in the normal phase has a non-trivial density dependence that is required to fit the experimental expansion data, and the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio exhibits a shallow minimum slightly above the critical temperature, though the authors can only weakly exclude a minimum at or below Tc at about 1 sigma confidence.

## Method

The authors analyzed elliptic flow expansion data from Joseph et al. (2014) using an anisotropic fluid dynamics framework that smoothly transitions between a fluid dynamic core and a ballistic corona, avoiding the need for an artificial critical radius. They performed fits to the aspect ratio data at fixed expansion time as a function of initial cloud energy, using a virial expansion of the shear viscosity in powers of the density, combined with a parametrization of the equation of state measured by Ku et al. (2011).

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to particle density ratio just above the critical temperature: eta/n at Tc = 0.41 plus or minus 0.11
- Minimum shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: eta/s at min = 0.50 plus or minus 0.10, located slightly above Tc
- Best fit coefficient for the leading temperature-dependent term: eta_0 = 0.265 plus or minus 0.02, agreeing to better than 1 percent with the kinetic theory prediction of 0.264
- First density correction coefficient: eta_2 = 0.060 plus or minus 0.02
- Second density correction coefficient: eta_3 = -(2 plus or minus 8) times 10 to the minus 4, consistent with zero within error bars
- Critical energy where superfluidity occurs at the trap center: E/(N E_F) = 0.70
- Critical temperature: Tc = 0.167(13) T_F_loc from Ku et al. (2011)

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic fluid dynamics method introduced by Bluhm et al. (2015) to lower temperatures and three-dimensional systems with no axial symmetry, using the same expansion data as Joseph et al. (2014) but without assuming a constant critical radius for the fluid core. The reconstruction disagrees with the lattice calculation of Wlazlowski et al. (2013) but agrees well near Tc with the T-matrix calculation of Enss et al. (2010). The results enable more reliable extraction of transport coefficients from expansion experiments and provide constraints for quasi-particle models of strongly correlated Fermi gases.
