# Massive neutron stars with small radii in relativistic mean-field models optimized to nuclear ground states

> We present an equation of state (EoS) for neutron stars using the relativistic mean-field model with isoscalar- and isovector-meson mixing. Taking into account the results of the neutron skin thickness, $R_{\rm skin}$, of $^{208}$Pb reported by the PREX collaboration, the dimensionless tidal deformability of a canonical neutron star observed from GW170817, and a $2.6$ $M_{\odot}$ compact star implied by the secondary component of GW190814, a new effective interaction is constructed so as to reproduce the saturation condition of nuclear matter and the ground-state properties of finite, closed-shell nuclei. We find that the neutron star EoS exhibits the rapid stiffening around twice the nuclear saturation density, which is caused by the soft nuclear symmetry energy, $E_{\rm sym}$. It is also noticeable that the thick $R_{\rm skin}$ from the PREX-2 experiment can be achieved with the small slope parameter of $E_{\rm sym}$ stemming from the isoscalar-meson mixing. Thus, we speculate that the secondary component of GW190814 is the heaviest neutron star ever discovered.

## Key claim

The authors present a new relativistic mean-field interaction (OMEG) that can simultaneously reproduce the thick neutron skin of 208Pb from PREX-2 (0.283 ± 0.071 fm), the small tidal deformability from GW170817, and a maximum neutron star mass reaching 2.6 solar masses, but only by invoking strong sigma-delta meson mixing that produces a soft symmetry energy with a small slope parameter L = 50 MeV, contrary to the large L values typically required to explain the PREX-2 result.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with nonlinear couplings including isoscalar- and isovector-meson mixing terms (σ²δ² and ω²ρ²). They fit the coupling constants to reproduce nuclear saturation properties, ground-state properties of closed-shell nuclei, the PREX-2 neutron skin thickness of 208Pb, the dimensionless tidal deformability Λ₁.₄ from GW170817, and the 2.6 M⊙ compact object from GW190814. The resulting equation of state is then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for neutron star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness of 208Pb: R_skin = 0.23 fm (experimental PREX-2 value: 0.283 ± 0.071 fm)
- Slope parameter of symmetry energy: L = 50 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation: E_sym = 34.55 MeV
- Saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: E₀ = -16.45 MeV
- Incompressibility: K₀ = 280.00 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass ratio at saturation: M_N*/M_N = 0.64
- Maximum neutron star mass exceeds 2.35 ± 0.17 M⊙ (PSR J0952-0607) and reaches 2.6 M⊙
- Canonical neutron star radius R₁.₄ lies around 12.5 km
- Tidal deformability Λ₁.₄ from GW170817: 190^{+390}_{-120}
- Speed of sound exceeds c_s ≈ 0.85 at central density of maximum mass

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous RMF model with meson mixing (Miyatsu 2022) by adjusting parameters to simultaneously satisfy the PREX-2 neutron skin measurement, GW170817 tidal deformability constraints, and the possibility of a 2.6 M⊙ neutron star from GW190814. It contrasts with models like BigApple (Fattoyev 2020) and NL3 that require larger L values, and with Bayesian analyses (Huth 2021) that constrain neutron star radii. The OMEG interaction enables future studies of whether the secondary component of GW190814 is the heaviest neutron star rather than the lightest black hole, and raises questions about the compatibility between PREX-2 and CREX results for 48Ca.
