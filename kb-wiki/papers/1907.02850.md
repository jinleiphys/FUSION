# Fluctuations of identified particle yields using the ν dyn variable at energies available at the BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

> We study the fluctuations of net-charge, net-pion, net-kaon and net-proton using $D$-measure and $ν_{dyn}$ variables in heavy-ion jet interaction generator (HIJING), ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD), and hadron resonance gas (HRG) model at different collision energies \sqsn. It has been observed that, the values of $D$ strongly dependent on $Δη$ in HIJING and UrQMD models and independent in HRG model. The diffusion coefficients ($σ$) of identified particles are estimated at various \sqsn. It is observed that, the $σ$ values are independent of collision energies but emphasizes the particle species dependence of diffusion coefficient in the QGP medium. This study provides a realistic baseline for comparison with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the fluctuation strength variable $\langle N_{\mathrm{ch}}\rangle \nu_{(\pm,\mathrm{dyn})}$ for net-charge, net-pion, net-kaon, and net-proton is independent of collision energy but shows clear particle species dependence across the HIJING, UrQMD, and HRG models; however, this energy independence is qualified by the observation that net-proton fluctuations show a small energy dependence in both HIJING and UrQMD models, and the HRG model shows a decrease in net-charge fluctuations with increasing collision energy.

## Method

The authors calculated the fluctuation variable $\nu_{(\pm,\mathrm{dyn})}$ for net-charge, net-pion, net-kaon, and net-proton using three models: the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model with resonance decay contributions, the HIJING event generator (V.1.37), and the UrQMD transport model (V.1.30). For HIJING and UrQMD, 0.2 million central (0-5%) Au+Au events were generated at each collision energy, with particles selected in the transverse momentum range 0.2 $\le p_T$ (GeV/$c$) $\le$ 5.0. The HRG calculations were performed within the same kinematic acceptance, considering charged hadrons with masses up to 2.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 19.6 GeV, $\langle N_{\mathrm{ch}}\rangle \nu_{(\pm,\mathrm{dyn})}$ values for net-charge, net-pion, net-kaon, and net-proton are shown as a function of $\Delta\eta$ for (0-5%) central Au+Au collisions in HRG, HIJING, and UrQMD models (Figure 1).
- The $\langle N_{\mathrm{ch}}\rangle \nu_{(\pm,\mathrm{dyn})}$ values for net-charge in the HRG model decrease with increasing collision energy, while for identified particles (net $\pi$, net $p$, net $K$) the values do not change as a function of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$.
- The $\langle N_{\mathrm{ch}}\rangle \nu_{(\pm,\mathrm{dyn})}$ values for net-charge, net-pion, and net-kaon are independent of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ in HIJING and UrQMD models, while net-proton values show small energy dependence in both models.
- The $\langle N_{\mathrm{ch}}\rangle \nu_{(\pm,\mathrm{dyn})}$ values for net-proton in the UrQMD model show a flattening trend at small $\Delta\eta$ and start decreasing as a function of $\Delta\eta$ at larger rapidity windows.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of net-charge fluctuations using the $D$ measure and $\nu_{(\pm,\mathrm{dyn})}$ variable (Jeon 2000, Asakawa 2000, Shuryak 2000) by focusing on identified particle species (pions, kaons, protons) rather than all inclusive charged particles as in Mishra (2017). The study provides a baseline from three different models (HRG, HIJING, UrQMD) for comparison with experimental data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, particularly for understanding particle species dependence of diffusion coefficients in the QGP medium and the role of resonance decays in fluctuation measurements.
