# Multi-neutron correlations in light nuclei via ab-initio lattice simulations

> The quest to understand multi-neutron systems has a long history, and recent experimental efforts aim to probe candidate four-neutron configurations in neutron-rich light nuclei such as ${}^8$He and ${}^7$H via quasi-free knockout reactions. However, the ground-state energies of the hydrogen isotopes ${}^6$H and ${}^7$H are not yet well constrained, with substantial discrepancies across experimental analyses and theoretical predictions. Using ab initio nuclear lattice effective field theory with an ensemble of 282 chiral two- and three-nucleon forces, we perform a Bayesian uncertainty-quantified analysis of the ground-state energies of ${}^6$H and ${}^7$H. The marginal posteriors suggest single-neutron separation energy $S_n({}^{7}\mathrm{H})=0.35^{+0.32}_{-0.32}$ MeV, which kinematically disfavors sequential decay via ${}^{6}\mathrm{H}+n$ and thereby makes multi-neutron emission channels comparatively more relevant. Intrinsic densities indicate triton- and $α$-like clusters in ${}^7$H and ${}^8$He, respectively. By computing two-body and reduced four-body correlation functions, we find that the valence neutrons in the surface region of these systems form compact dineutrons that predominantly organize into approximately symmetric dineutron-dineutron configurations, with only a small but non-negligible fraction assembling into more compact tetraneutron-like substructures. In ${}^7$H, these components account for roughly 95\% and 5\% of the sampled four-neutron configurations, respectively, and ${}^8$He exhibits a similar hierarchy. For these configurations, we also extract the corresponding spatial and angular correlation patterns among the nucleons. These results provide nuclear-structure insights into the debate surrounding four-neutron clusters and complement ongoing experimental searches for tetraneutron signatures in light nuclei.

## Key claim

The ground states of $^{7}$H and $^{8}$He are dominated (about 95%) by an approximately spatially symmetric, extended dineutron-dineutron geometry with large inter-pair separation and finite torsion, while a compact, twisted tetraneutron-like configuration with enhanced four-body correlations accounts for the remaining 5%, complicating experimental isolation of a genuinely compact tetraneutron signal.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) simulations using auxiliary-field Monte Carlo sampling on a periodic cubic lattice with side length L=12 and spatial lattice spacing a=1.32 fm. They employed an ensemble of 282 chiral two- and three-nucleon forces at N$^3$LO order, obtained through history matching, and used the pinhole method to sample spatial coordinates, spins, and isospins of all nucleons. Two-body and reduced four-body correlation functions were computed from the pinhole configurations to characterize multi-neutron geometries, with Bayesian posterior predictive distributions quantifying uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of $^{6}$H: $-4.94^{+0.14}_{-0.14}$ MeV (median and 1$\sigma$ credible interval)
- Ground-state energy of $^{7}$H: $-5.29^{+0.16}_{-0.16}$ MeV (median and 1$\sigma$ credible interval)
- Single-neutron separation energy $S_n(^{7}\mathrm{H}) = 0.35^{+0.32}_{-0.32}$ MeV
- $^{8}$He $0^+$ state energy: $-30.73(19)$ MeV from lattice, compared to experimental $-31.41(1)$ MeV (Tilley 2004)
- $^{8}$He $0_2^+$ state energy: $-24.99(72)$ MeV from lattice, compared to experimental $-24.74(6)$ MeV (Yang 2023)
- In $^{7}$H, extended dineutron-dineutron configurations account for roughly 95% of sampled four-neutron configurations, while compact tetraneutron-like configurations account for roughly 5%
- Spatial lattice spacing: $a \simeq 1.32$ fm, corresponding to momentum cutoff $\Lambda \simeq 471$ MeV
- Gaussian smearing width for nucleon coordinates: 0.84 fm

## Context

This work extends previous studies of multi-neutron correlations using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (Kanada-Enyo 2007, Aoyama 2009), microscopic cluster calculations (Itagaki 2008, Kobayashi 2013, Yamaguchi 2023, Nakagawa 2025), and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approaches (Hagino 2008) by providing the first ab initio calculation of four-body correlation functions in $^{7}$H and $^{8}$He. The results complement ongoing experimental searches for tetraneutron signatures in quasi-free knockout reactions (Duer 2022) and double charge-exchange reactions (Kisamori 2016), and suggest that symmetric dineutron-dineutron modes may complicate the isolation of compact tetraneutron signals in future experiments.
