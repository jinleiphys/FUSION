# Hydrodynamical analysis of flow at RHIC

> We use a hydrodynamical model to describe the evolution of the collision system at collision energies sqrt{s}=130 and 200 GeV. At lower sqrt{s}=130 GeV energy we compare the results obtained assuming fast or slow thermalization (thermalization times tau_0=0.6 and 4.1 fm/c, respectively) and show that slow thermalization fails to reproduce the observed anisotropy of particle distribution. At sqrt{s}=200 GeV collision energy our results show anisotropies similar to those observed at sqrt{s}=130 GeV.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a hydrodynamical model with fast thermalization (thermalization time tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c) can reproduce both the observed transverse momentum spectra and azimuthal anisotropy of particles at RHIC, while a model with slow thermalization (tau_0 = 4.1 fm/c) fails to generate sufficient anisotropy, but this conclusion depends on the specific choice of freeze-out temperature and the assumption that chemical and kinetic freeze-out occur at the same temperature.

## Method

The author uses a boost-invariant ideal fluid hydrodynamical model solved in two transverse dimensions, with two different equations of state: one with a phase transition at T_c = 165 MeV (EoS A) and a purely hadronic one (EoS H). For fast thermalization, the initial energy density is a scaled sum of participant and binary collision densities (mixing parameter x = 0.4) at tau_0 = 0.6 fm; for slow thermalization, the system undergoes free streaming from tau_i = 0.6 fm until tau_0 = 4.1 fm, after which the energy density distribution from free-streaming particles is taken as the thermal initial condition. The freeze-out temperature is fixed by fitting preliminary PHENIX pion and antiproton p_t-distributions in most central collisions at sqrt{s} = 130 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Thermalization times compared: tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c (fast) and tau_0 = 4.1 fm/c (slow) at sqrt{s} = 130 GeV.
- Freeze-out temperature for fast thermalization: T_f ≈ 140 MeV; for slow thermalization: T_f ≈ 100 MeV.
- Scaling factor for antiproton yield to T_ch = 165 MeV: factor 2 for tau_0 = 0.6 fm, factor 27 for tau_0 = 4.1 fm.
- At sqrt{s} = 200 GeV, charged particle multiplicity dN/dη = 650 ± 35 in 6% most central collisions (PHOBOS collaboration) used to fix parametrization.
- Average energy density at tau = 1 fm increases from 5.4 GeV/fm^3 (sqrt{s} = 130 GeV) to 6.8 GeV/fm^3 (sqrt{s} = 200 GeV).
- Maximum temperature at tau_0 = 0.6 fm increases from 355 MeV (sqrt{s} = 130 GeV) to 375 MeV (sqrt{s} = 200 GeV).
- Increase in particle multiplicity from sqrt{s} = 130 to 200 GeV is 14%, while E_t increases by ~18%.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier hydrodynamical analyses (Kolb 2001, Huovinen 2001, Kolb 2001b) by constraining the model to reproduce single-particle p_t-distributions from PHENIX (Velkovska 2001) and testing the consequences of a long thermalization time that begins in the hadronic phase. The results corroborate the earlier argument that fast thermalization is necessary to explain the observed large elliptic flow at RHIC, and provide the first hydrodynamical predictions at sqrt{s} = 200 GeV, showing only small changes in spectra and negligible change in differential v_2 compared to sqrt{s} = 130 GeV. The paper enables future comparisons with higher-energy data and motivates more careful analysis of systematic errors in experimental anisotropy measurements (Tang 2001).
