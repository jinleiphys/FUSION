# Microscopic Restoration of Proton-Neutron Mixed Symmetry in Weakly Collective Nuclei

> Starting from the microscopic low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction V{low k}, we present the first systematic shell model study of magnetic moments and magnetic dipole transition strengths of the basic low-energy one-quadrupole phonon excitations in nearly-spherical nuclei. Studying in particular the even-even N=52 isotones from 92Zr to 100Cd, we find the predicted evolution of the predominantly proton-neutron non-symmetric state reveals a restoration of collective proton-neutron mixed-symmetry structure near mid-shell. This provides the first explanation for the existence of pronounced collective mixed-symmetry structures in weakly-collective nuclei.

# Microscopic Restoration of Proton-Neutron Mixed Symmetry in Weakly Collective Nuclei

## Key claim

The paper provides the first microscopic explanation for the existence of pronounced collective mixed-symmetry structures in weakly collective nuclei, showing that the restoration of proton-neutron mixed symmetry near mid-shell originates from the approximate energy degeneracy of basic proton and neutron one-quadrupole phonon excitations, not from strong collective proton-neutron coupling as assumed in the interacting boson model.

## Method

Shell model calculations were performed using the low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction V_{low k} derived from the CD-Bonn potential with a cutoff of 2.1 fm^{-1}, with an effective interaction based on folded diagram methods including core polarization to second order. The calculations used an inert ^{88}Sr core with a proton model space of [2p_{1/2}, 1g_{9/2}] and neutron model space of [1g_{7/2}, 2d_{5/2}, 2d_{3/2}, 3s_{1/2}, 1h_{11/2}], studying the even-even N=52 isotones from ^{92}Zr to ^{100}Cd. The authors computed magnetic moments and magnetic dipole transition strengths for the two lowest 2^+ states, identifying the 2^+_II state as the mixed-symmetry state based on its strong M1 transition to the 2^+_I state.

## Key numbers

- B(M1; 2^+_II → 2^+_I) values show a pronounced parabolic behavior across the N=52 isotones, maximized at mid-shell (^{96}Ru)
- The difference in diagonal M1 matrix elements between proton and neutron configurations is μ_π - μ_ν ≈ 4.2 μ_N
- Calculated g-factors in ^{96}Ru for the 2^+_I and 2^+_II states are 0.42 and 0.48, respectively, close to the Z/A = 0.458 value expected for a fully collective state
- The 2^+_I state in ^{92}Zr has a predicted negative g-factor of -0.31, indicating significant neutron character
- Overlap between 2^+_I and 2^+_II wavefunctions is less than 0.1 in every case
- The 2^+_1 energy of N=50 isotones drops from 1509 keV in ^{92}Mo to 1394 keV in ^{98}Cd
- The 2^+_1 energy in ^{92}Zr is 934 keV, increasing to 1472 keV in ^{102}Sn

## Context

This work extends previous experimental observations of mixed-symmetry states in N=52 isotones, particularly in ^{94}Mo (Pietralla, 1999; Fransen, 2003) and neighboring nuclei (Werner, 2002; Fransen, 2005), as well as the first identification of mixed-symmetry states in an odd-mass nucleus ^{93}Nb (Orce, 2006). The calculations contradict the F-spin limit predictions of the interacting boson model (IBM-2) of Iachello (1984), which would predict local extrema for g-factors at mid-shell and a reduction of B(M1) from ^{94}Mo to ^{96}Ru. The results enable predictions of mixed-symmetry formation and structural evolution in other nuclear regions and provide parallel insight into analogous two-fluid quantum systems such as trapped Bose-Einstein condensates, metallic clusters, and elliptical quantum dots.
