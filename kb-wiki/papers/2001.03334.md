# New Ideas in Constraining Nuclear Forces

> In recent years, nuclear physics has benefited greatly from the development of powerful ab initio many-body methods and their combination with interactions from chiral effective field theory. With increasing computational power and continuous development of these methods, we are entering an era of precision nuclear physics. Indeed, uncertainties from nuclear Hamiltonians now dominate over uncertainties from many-body methods. This review summarizes the current status of, and future directions in, deriving and constraining nuclear Hamiltonians.

## Key claim

The review argues that uncertainties from nuclear Hamiltonians now dominate over uncertainties from many-body methods in ab initio nuclear calculations, but this conclusion is conditional on the assumption that many-body method uncertainties are well-controlled and that the attained numerical convergence across different approaches is reliable.

## Method

The authors summarize the current status of deriving and constraining nuclear Hamiltonians within chiral effective field theory (chiral EFT), reviewing different regularization schemes (nonlocal, semilocal, and local interactions) and power counting approaches. They examine three major sources of Hamiltonian uncertainty: truncation of the chiral expansion, uncertainty in low-energy constants (LECs), and dependence on regularization scheme and cutoff scale. The review synthesizes discussions from a 2018 workshop at ECT* that brought together specialists in Hamiltonian construction and many-body calculations.

## Key numbers

- The hard scale (breakdown scale) of chiral EFT is estimated to be approximately 500-600 MeV, based on Bayesian analysis of NN scattering data from Furnstahl (2015) and Melendez (2017)
- The Illinois-7 3N potential failed in neutron matter, producing a vanishing pressure above a density of approximately 0.1 fm^{-3}
- For the leading-order 3N forces, five LECs need to be determined, with two pure 3N couplings (c_D and c_E) that only appear in the 3N sector
- The density range between 1-2 n_sat is identified as crucial for neutron-star physics, where it remains unclear up to which densities chiral EFT interactions remain reliable

## Context

This review extends the line of work on chiral EFT interactions pioneered by Weinberg (1991), Ordonez (1992), and later developed by Entem and Machleidt (2003) and Epelbaum, Krebs, and Meissner (2014). It contrasts with phenomenological approaches such as the AV18 potential of Wiringa (1994) and CD-Bonn potential of Machleidt (2000), which were guided by quantitative reproduction of experimental data rather than systematic EFT expansion. The review enables future work on improved power counting schemes, matching to lattice QCD calculations, and developing statistical inference procedures for quantifying systematic EFT uncertainties in predictions of nuclear observables.
