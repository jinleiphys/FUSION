# Meson-Photon Transition Form Factors

> We present the results of our recent analysis of the meson-photon transition form factors $F_{Pγ}(Q^2)$ for the pseudoscalar mesons $P = π^0,η,η',η_c$, using the local-duality version of QCD sum rules.

## Key claim

The local-duality (LD) version of QCD sum rules, which approximates the effective continuum threshold by its asymptotic value, provides a reliable description of meson-photon transition form factors for pseudoscalar mesons at momentum transfers above a few GeV², but the BaBar data for the pion show an extreme violation of this local duality that is hard to understand in QCD.

## Method

The authors analyze the three-point correlation function of one axial and two vector currents (⟨AVV⟩) and, for charmonium, also the pseudoscalar-vector-vector (⟨PVV⟩) correlator. They apply QCD sum rules in the local-duality limit (Borel parameter τ=0), where the ground-state transition form factor is expressed as an integral of the perturbative QCD spectral density up to an effective continuum threshold s_eff(Q²). The effective threshold is fixed by matching the LD form factor to the asymptotic pQCD factorization formula at large Q², and the LD model assumes this threshold remains constant at finite Q².

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: f_π = 0.130 GeV
- For η_c, the LD model predicts F_{η_cγ}(0) = 0.067 GeV⁻¹ from the ⟨AVV⟩ correlator and F_{η_cγ}(0) = 0.086 GeV⁻¹ from the ⟨PVV⟩ correlator, compared to the experimental value F_{η_cγ}(0) = 0.08 ± 0.01 GeV⁻¹
- The η-η' mixing angle is φ ≈ 38°, with decay constants f_n ≈ 1.07 f_π and f_s ≈ 1.36 f_π
- For light pseudoscalars, the asymptotic effective threshold is s_eff(Q²→∞) = 4π² f_π²
- The BaBar data for the pion form factor show disagreement with the LD model at Q² as large as 40 GeV²
- For the Belle data, the LD regime appears to set in already at Q² ≥ 5–10 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses of transition form factors using local-duality sum rules (Balakireva et al. 2011, Lucha and Melikhov 2011) and applies the method to π⁰, η, η', and η_c mesons. The results are compared with experimental data from CELLO, CLEO, BaBar, and Belle collaborations. The paper highlights a tension between BaBar and Belle measurements of the pion transition form factor, and suggests that a small logarithmic rise in Q²F_{Pγ}(Q²) would require duality-violating terms in the spectral densities. The accuracy of the LD model for transition form factors has implications for the pion elastic form factor, which should be testable with the JLab CLAS12 upgrade.
