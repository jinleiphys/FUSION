# Double-Lambda hypernuclei within a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach

> We extend our Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) approach, which was used earlier to describe successfully the single-$Λ$ hypernuclei, to investigate the binding and the $ΛΛ$ bond energies of the double-$Λ$ hypernuclei. The nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) and $Λ$-nucleon ($ΛN$) interactions are taken from our previous study. For the $ΛΛ$ force several Skyrme-like potentials available in the literature have been used. We discuss the sensitivity of the calculated $ΛΛ$ binding and bond energies to the $ΛΛ$ and $ΛN$ force parameters. It is found that the existing $ΛΛ$ hypernuclear data do not allow to distinguish between various $ΛΛ$ force parameter sets used by us. However, they show some selectivity for a particular set of the $ΛN$ potential determined in our previous work.

## Key claim

The Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) model, previously successful for single-Lambda hypernuclei, can be extended to describe double-Lambda hypernuclei across a wide mass range; however, the existing experimental data on double-Lambda hypernuclei do not allow a distinction between the various Lambda-Lambda force parameter sets used, though they show some selectivity for a particular Lambda-nucleon potential set (HP-Lambda-2) from the authors' prior work.

## Method

The authors extended their Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) formalism, using the SLy4 parameter set for the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction and the HP-Lambda-2 set for the Lambda-nucleon (Lambda-N) interaction from their previous study. For the Lambda-Lambda (Lambda-Lambda) interaction, they employed five Skyrme-like parameter sets (S-Lambda-Lambda-1, S-Lambda-Lambda-2, S-Lambda-Lambda-3, S-Lambda-Lambda-1-prime, and S-Lambda-Lambda-3-prime) taken from the literature, which were fitted to different experimental bond energies of the 13-Lambda-Lambda-B ground state. They solved the coupled SHF equations self-consistently to calculate the two-Lambda binding energies (B-Lambda-Lambda) and the Lambda-Lambda bond energies (Delta-B-Lambda-Lambda) for double-Lambda hypernuclei ranging from mass 6 to mass 210.

## Key numbers

*   For 6-Lambda-Lambda-He, the calculated B-Lambda-Lambda values are 11.88 MeV (S-Lambda-Lambda-1), 9.25 MeV (S-Lambda-Lambda-2), and 7.60 MeV (S-Lambda-Lambda-3), compared to the experimental value of 6.91 +/- 0.16 MeV (NAGARA event).
*   For 10-Lambda-Lambda-Be, the calculated B-Lambda-Lambda values are 19.78 MeV (S-Lambda-Lambda-1), 18.34 MeV (S-Lambda-Lambda-2), and 15.19 MeV (S-Lambda-Lambda-3), compared to the experimental value of 14.94 +/- 0.13 MeV.
*   For 13-Lambda-Lambda-B, the calculated B-Lambda-Lambda values are 21.21 MeV (S-Lambda-Lambda-1), 20.26 MeV (S-Lambda-Lambda-2), and 17.76 MeV (S-Lambda-Lambda-3), compared to the experimental value of 23.30 +/- 0.70 MeV.
*   The calculated bond energy Delta-B-Lambda-Lambda for A=6 ranges from 2.36 MeV (S-Lambda-Lambda-1) to 6.64 MeV (S-Lambda-Lambda-3), with the experimental value being 3.82 +/- 1.72 MeV.
*   For A=13, the calculated bond energy Delta-B-Lambda-Lambda ranges from 0.69 MeV (S-Lambda-Lambda-1) to 2.32 MeV (S-Lambda-Lambda-3), with the experimental value being 0.6 +/- 0.8 MeV.
*   For heavier systems (A=210), the calculated bond energy Delta-B-Lambda-Lambda drops to 0.25 MeV (S-Lambda-Lambda-1), 0.42 MeV (S-Lambda-Lambda-2), and 0.48 MeV (S-Lambda-Lambda-3).

## Context

This work extends the SHF approach of Guleria et al. (2012) for single-Lambda hypernuclei to the double-Lambda case, building on prior mean-field studies such as the relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations of Lan et al. (1998) and Rufa et al. (1990). The results are compared with cluster model calculations by Hiyama et al. (2010) and shell model calculations by Gal et al. (2011), as well as quark mean-field model results by Hu et al. (2013). The study demonstrates that the SHF model can serve as a workable framework for double-Lambda hypernuclei across a wide mass range, but it also highlights that more experimental data, especially for heavier systems, are needed to constrain the Lambda-Lambda interaction.
