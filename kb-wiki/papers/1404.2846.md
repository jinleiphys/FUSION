# Pseudo-Critical Enhancement of Thermal Photons in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We compute the spectra and elliptic flow of thermal photons emitted in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions (URHICs) at RHIC and LHC. The thermal emission rates are taken from complete leading-order rates for the QGP and hadronic many-body calculations including baryons and antibaryons, as well as meson-exchange reactions (including Bremsstrahlung). We first update previous thermal fireball calculations by implementing a lattice-QCD based equation of state and extend them to compare to recent LHC data. We then scrutinize the space-time evolution of Au-Au collisions at RHIC by employing an ideal hydrodynamic model constrained by bulk- and multistrange-hadron spectra and elliptic flow, including a non-vanishing initial flow. We systematically compare the evolutions of temperature, radial flow, azimuthal anisotropy and four-volume, and exhibit the temperature profile of thermal photon radiation. Based on these insights, we put forward a scenario with a "pseudo-critical enhancement" of thermal emission rates, and investigate its impact on RHIC and LHC direct photon data.

# Pseudo-Critical Enhancement of Thermal Photons in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a "pseudo-critical enhancement" of thermal photon emission rates around the phase transition temperature (T_pc ≈ 170 MeV) could help resolve discrepancies between theoretical calculations and experimental direct-photon data at RHIC and LHC, but the authors hedge that this is a conjectured scenario rather than a definitive finding, and that quantitative differences remain between their fireball and hydrodynamic models.

## Method

The authors computed thermal photon spectra and elliptic flow using complete leading-order QGP emission rates and hadronic many-body calculations (including baryons, antibaryons, and meson-exchange reactions with Bremsstrahlung), integrated over space-time evolutions from two bulk-medium models: a schematic blast-wave fireball and 2+1-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics (AZHYDRO code). Both models used a lattice-QCD based equation of state (latPHG) matched to a hadron resonance gas with chemical freezeout at T_ch = 160 MeV, and were constrained by fits to bulk-hadron and multistrange-hadron spectra and elliptic flow data from RHIC Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 A GeV.

## Key numbers

- Pseudo-critical temperature: T_pc ≈ 170 MeV (from lattice-QCD based equation of state)
- Chemical freezeout temperature: T_ch = 160 MeV (for both RHIC and LHC energies)
- Kinetic freezeout temperature: T_fo ≈ 100 MeV for light hadrons, T_fo = 160 MeV for multistrange hadrons (phi, Omega)
- Central initial temperature in hydro model: T_0 = 398 MeV for 0-20% Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Thermalization time in hydro: tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c
- Initial radial flow surface value in hydro: approximately 0.13 c
- Effective temperature from PHENIX excess radiation: T_eff = 221 ± 19 (stat) ± 19 (syst) MeV
- Maximum direct-photon v_2 from fireball: approximately 5.7% (using PHENIX pp baseline)
- Maximum direct-photon v_2 from hydro: approximately 4.4% (using PHENIX pp baseline)
- Entropy reduction in updated fireball: approximately 20% compared to previous work (vanHees 2011)
- Charged-particle multiplicity at LHC: dN_ch/dy = 1040 over |y| < 0.75 for 0-40% Pb-Pb at sqrt(s) = 2.76 A TeV

## Context

This work extends previous thermal fireball calculations by vanHees (2011) that first identified the importance of the transition region for photon v_2, updating them with a lattice-QCD based equation of state and extending to LHC energies. The authors compare their results to ideal hydrodynamic calculations by He (2011) and find that both models underestimate the PHENIX direct-photon data at low q_T, though their hydro results show higher v_2 than other hydrodynamic calculations by Holopainen (2011), Dion (2011), and Shen (2013) due to faster build-up of elliptic flow from compact initial conditions and finite initial flow. The pseudo-critical enhancement scenario is motivated by the observation that the differential emission four-volume peaks around T_pc ≈ 170 MeV as a consequence of the rapid change in entropy density from the equation of state, and the authors suggest this could help resolve discrepancies with data if future work investigates enhanced emission rates in the transition region.
