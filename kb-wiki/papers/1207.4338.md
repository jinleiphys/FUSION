# Scalar-particle self-energy amplitudes and confinement in Minkowski space

> We analyze the analytic structure of the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) contribution to the self-energy amplitude for a scalar particle in a ϕ^2 χ-theory. To this end we derive dispersion relations in 1+1 and in 3+1 dimensional Minkowski space. The divergent loop integrals in 3+1 dimensions are regularized using dimensional regularization. We find that the CST dispersion relations exhibit, in addition to the usual right-hand branch cut, also a left-hand cut. The origin of this "spectator" left-hand cut can be understood in the context of scattering for a scalar ϕ^2 χ^2-type theory. If the interaction kernel contains a linear confining component, its contribution to the self-energy vanishes exactly.

## Key claim

The Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) self-energy amplitude for a scalar particle in a \(\phi^2\chi\) theory exhibits, in addition to the usual right-hand branch cut, a left-hand cut that originates from the negative-energy pole contribution of the light particle omitted in the spectator approximation; if the interaction kernel contains a linear confining component, its contribution to the self-energy vanishes exactly.

## Method

The authors derived dispersion relations for the scalar-particle self-energy in 1+1 and 3+1 dimensional Minkowski space, using the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) framework where the heavier particle in the loop is placed on its positive-energy mass shell. In 1+1 dimensions, analytic expressions for both the full Feynman and CST self-energy amplitudes were obtained and compared, while in 3+1 dimensions the divergent loop integrals were regularized using dimensional regularization. The analytic structure was analyzed by splitting the product of propagators via an algebraic identity and examining the resulting dispersion integrals.

## Key numbers

- In 1+1 dimensions, the full Feynman self-energy \(\Sigma(s)\) has a branch cut on the positive real axis from threshold \(s_+ = (M+m)^2\) to \(\infty\), with \(\mathrm{Im}\Sigma(s) = -\frac{1}{2\rho(s)}\theta(s-s_+)\) where \(\rho(s)=\sqrt{(s-s_+)(s-s_-)}\) and \(s_-=(M-m)^2\).
- The CST self-energy \(\Sigma_\text{CST}(s)\) in 1+1 dimensions has a right-hand cut from \(s_+\) to \(\infty\) and a left-hand cut from \(-\infty\) to \(s_-\), with \(\mathrm{Im}\Sigma_\text{CST}(s) = -\frac{1}{2\rho(s)}\left[\frac12\theta(-s)+ \theta(s)\theta(s_--s)+\theta(s-s_+)\right]\).
- The linear confining potential in momentum space is given by \(V_\mathrm L({\bm q}) = \lim_{\epsilon \rightarrow 0} \left[ V_\mathrm{A}({\bm q}) -(2\pi)^3\delta^{(3)}({\bm q})\int \frac{\mathrm d^3 q'}{(2\pi)^3} V_\mathrm A({\bm q}') \right]\) with \(V_{\mathrm A}({\bm q}) = - \frac{8\pi\sigma}{({\bm q}^2+\epsilon^2)^2}\).

## Context

This work extends the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) approach to meson structure, previously applied with constant dressed quark masses (Gross 1991, Gross 1994, Savkli 1999), by calculating the self-energy that yields a momentum-dependent mass function. The analysis of the analytic structure, including the left-hand cut, parallels methods used in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory by Becher and Leutwyler (1999). The results enable a consistent treatment of quark dressing and confinement in CST, necessary for studying chiral symmetry breaking and obtaining massless pion solutions in the chiral limit.
