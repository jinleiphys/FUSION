# Influence of momentum and charge conservation on azimuthally sensitive correlations

> Charge neutralisation procedure based on a Markov chain Monte Carlo algorithm is applied to a system of generated hadrons. The algorithm changes the charge of a randomly picked particle by shifting it within its isomultiplet. For baryons changes in both electric charge and baryon number are applied and the algorithm leads to charge and baryon number neutralisation. The procedure can thus be used to study the effects of the local charge and baryon number conservation. We attempt to study these together with the local momentum conservation and their effect on azimuthal correlator observable sensitive to local C and CP violation in quark-gluon plasma.

# Influence of momentum and charge conservation on azimuthally sensitive correlations

## Key claim
The paper shows that the azimuthal correlator γ, which is sensitive to local C and CP violation in quark-gluon plasma, receives a contribution from joint momentum, electric charge, and baryon number conservation that is independent of the combined charge of the hadron pair. However, the toy model only conserves charge globally, not locally, which may explain why it completely misses the correlation of like-sign pairs observed in experiment.

## Method
The authors generated hadrons using the Lorentz Invariant Phase Space generator REGGAE to enforce momentum conservation, then applied a Markov chain Monte Carlo algorithm to enforce charge and baryon number neutralisation by shifting particles within their isomultiplets (pions in the isotriplet, nucleons in the isodoublet). Elliptic flow was simulated by generating two samples of hadrons moving away from each other in the x-direction, and the azimuthal correlator γ was calculated for like-sign and unlike-sign particle pairs as a function of multiplicity.

## Key numbers
- The simulated integrated elliptic flow v₂ was adjusted to match experimental values; the histogram of integrated v₂ is shown in Figure 3 (no explicit numerical value given in the text).
- The azimuthal correlator γ was calculated for all pairs of charged particles, with like-sign (positive and negative separately) and unlike-sign pairs shown in Figure 4; no significant difference was found between like-sign and unlike-sign values.
- The observed signal from the toy model qualitatively agrees with the measured correlation of unlike-sign pairs from the STAR experiment, but completely misses the correlation of like-sign pairs.

## Context
This work extends separate studies by Bzdak (2012) and Pratt (2012) on the effects of local conservation of momentum, transverse momentum, and charge on the azimuthal correlator γ, which was originally proposed by Voloshin (2004) to detect the Chiral Magnetic Effect predicted by Kharzeev (2008). The results indicate that global conservation laws alone cannot reproduce the full experimental correlation pattern, motivating future inclusion of local charge conservation in the model.
