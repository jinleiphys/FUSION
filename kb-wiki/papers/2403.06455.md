# Neutrinoless double-$β$ decay and double Gamow-Teller transitions

> The neutrinoless double-$β$ ($0νββ$) decay and the double Gamow-Teller (DGT) transition are investigated with the state-of-the-art Relativistic Configuration-interaction Density functional theory. A strong linear correlation between the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) of the $0νββ$ decay and the DGT transition is demonstrated. This linear correlation is found to originate from the similarity of the leading-order term of the $0νββ$-decay operator and the DGT-transition one, as revealed by expanding the $0νββ$-decay operator in terms of the spherical harmonics. The present results provide a strong support to constrain the $0νββ$-decay NMEs through the double charge-exchange reactions.

## Key claim

A strong linear correlation (Pearson's correlation coefficient r = 0.977) exists between the nuclear matrix elements of neutrinoless double-beta decay and double Gamow-Teller transitions across ten relevant nuclei, but this correlation does not originate from the dominant short-range character of both transitions as previously proposed by Shimizu (2018); instead, it arises from the similarity of the leading-order term (L=0) of the 0νββ-decay operator and the DGT-transition operator.

## Method

The authors employed the Relativistic Configuration-interaction Density functional (ReCD) theory, which combines configuration-interaction shell model methods with relativistic density functional theory, to calculate nuclear matrix elements for both 0νββ decay and DGT transitions. They solved the triaxial relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov equation in a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis with 10 major shells, using two relativistic density functionals (PC-PK1 and PC-F1) and a finite-range separable pairing force with strength G = 728 MeV fm³. The 0νββ-decay operator was expanded in spherical harmonics to isolate the L=0 component, and the single-particle matrix elements of this leading-order term were compared analytically to those of the DGT-transition operator.

## Key numbers

- Pearson's correlation coefficient r = 0.977 between M^(DGT) and M^(0ν) scaled by A^(-1/3) for ten nuclei (⁴⁸Ca, ⁷⁶Ge, ⁸²Se, ⁹⁶Zr, ¹⁰⁰Mo, ¹¹⁶Cd, ¹²⁴Sn, ¹²⁸Te, ¹³⁰Te, ¹³⁶Xe)
- Pearson's correlation coefficient r = 0.999 between the leading-order term M^(0ν)_(L=0) and M^(DGT)
- Pearson's correlation coefficient r = 0.898 between M^(0ν)_(L=1) and M^(DGT)
- The AA coupling channel contributes more than 95% of the total 0νββ-decay NME
- A quasiparticle excitation energy cutoff E_cut = 5.0 MeV was used for the intrinsic configuration space

## Context

This work extends the investigation of the correlation between 0νββ decay and DGT transitions, previously studied by Shimizu (2018) using configuration-interaction shell model and nonrelativistic density functional theory, and by various groups using quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA), valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG), and in-medium generator coordinate method (IM-GCM). The present ReCD calculations contradict the earlier explanation by Shimizu (2018) that the correlation arises from short-range dominance of both transitions, instead demonstrating that the DGT transition is not short-range dominated. The results support using double charge-exchange reactions to constrain 0νββ-decay nuclear matrix elements for current and next-generation experiments.
