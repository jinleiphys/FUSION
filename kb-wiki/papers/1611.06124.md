# In-Medium Pion Valence Distribution Amplitude

> After a brief review of the quark-based model for nuclear matter, and some pion properties in medium presented in our previous works, we report new results for the pion valence wave function as well as the valence distribution amplitude in medium, which are presented in our recent article. We find that both the in-medium pion valence distribution and the in-medium pion valence wave function, are substantially modified at normal nuclear matter density, due to the reduction in the pion decay constant.

## Key claim

The in-medium pion valence wave function and valence distribution amplitude are substantially modified at normal nuclear matter density, but the authors note that the results are valid only up to around that density and cannot reliably discuss the chiral limit (vanishing effective light-quark masses) due to the simplicity of the light-front constituent quark model.

## Method

The authors combined a light-front constituent quark model for the pion with in-medium input calculated by the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which describes symmetric nuclear matter via self-consistent scalar-isoscalar sigma and vector-isoscalar omega mean fields coupled directly to confined quarks. The in-medium constituent light-quark properties were constrained by the saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter (binding energy 15.7 MeV at density 0.15 fm^-3), and the pion mass was assumed unchanged from its vacuum value up to normal nuclear matter density based on pionic-atom experiments.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear matter density (rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3), the effective constituent quark mass m_q* decreases from 220 MeV (vacuum) to 79.5 MeV.
- The in-medium pion decay constant f_pi* decreases from 93.1 MeV (vacuum) to 40.2 MeV at rho_0.
- The root-mean-square pion charge radius increases from 0.73 fm (vacuum) to 1.96 fm at rho_0.
- The valence component probability eta* increases from 0.782 (vacuum) to 0.930 at rho_0.
- The ratio of in-medium to vacuum quark condensate estimated via a Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner-like relation gives approximately 0.52 at rho_0, compared to 0.67 +/- 0.06 extracted from pionic-atom experiments at 0.17 fm^-3.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of pion properties in nuclear medium (deMelo 2014, pimedium2) by presenting new results for the in-medium pion valence distribution amplitude. The approach uses the quark-meson coupling model of Guichon (1988) and Saito (2007) to provide the in-medium input, and the light-front constituent quark model that was previously successful for vacuum pion and kaon properties (deMelo 2002, Yabusaki 2015). The results enable future studies of reactions involving pions in nuclear medium based on a constituent quark picture, and the authors note that the reduction in f_pi* is larger than that calculated by the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Vogl, Lutz).
