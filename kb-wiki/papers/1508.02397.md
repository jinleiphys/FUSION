# Employing Helicity Amplitudes for Resummation

> Many state-of-the-art QCD calculations for multileg processes use helicity amplitudes as their fundamental ingredients. We construct a simple and easy-to-use helicity operator basis in soft-collinear effective theory (SCET), for which the hard Wilson coefficients from matching QCD onto SCET are directly given in terms of color-ordered helicity amplitudes. Using this basis allows one to seamlessly combine fixed-order helicity amplitudes at any order they are known with a resummation of higher-order logarithmic corrections. In particular, the virtual loop amplitudes can be employed in factorization theorems to make predictions for exclusive jet cross sections without the use of numerical subtraction schemes to handle real-virtual infrared cancellations. We also discuss matching onto SCET in renormalization schemes with helicities in $4$- and $d$-dimensions. To demonstrate that our helicity operator basis is easy to use, we provide an explicit construction of the operator basis, as well as results for the hard matching coefficients, for $pp\to H + 0,1,2$ jets, $pp\to W/Z/γ+ 0,1,2$ jets, and $pp\to 2,3$ jets. These operator bases are completely crossing symmetric, so the results can easily be applied to processes with $e^+e^-$ and $e^-p$ collisions.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a helicity operator basis in soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) that allows hard Wilson coefficients from matching QCD onto SCET to be directly given in terms of color-ordered helicity amplitudes, enabling seamless combination of fixed-order helicity amplitudes at any known order with resummation of higher-order logarithmic corrections without numerical subtraction schemes for real-virtual infrared cancellations.

## Method

The authors construct SCET operators built from collinear quark and gluon fields of definite helicity, using the spinor-helicity formalism to define helicity-specific building blocks. They then perform matching calculations from QCD onto these SCET helicity operators for processes including pp → H + 0,1,2 jets, pp → W/Z/γ + 0,1,2 jets, and pp → 2,3 jets, where the Wilson coefficients are directly given by the infrared-finite parts of color-ordered QCD helicity amplitudes. The operator bases are constructed to be completely crossing symmetric, allowing application to e+e- and e-p collisions.

## Key numbers

- The paper provides explicit operator bases and hard matching coefficients for pp → H + 0,1,2 jets, pp → W/Z/γ + 0,1,2 jets, and pp → 2,3 jets
- The matching for V+2 parton and H+2 parton processes is noted as known to three loops, referencing Baikov (2009), Gehrmann (2010), and Abbate (2010)
- The matching for V+3 partons has been performed at both NLO and NNLO, referencing Becher (2009, 2011, 2012, 2013)
- The matching for H+3 partons has been performed at both NLO and NNLO, referencing Liu (2012), Jouttenus (2013), Huang (2014), and Becher (2014)
- The matching for all massless 2→2 processes has been obtained at NLO in Kelley (2010) and at NNLO in Broggio (2014)

## Context

This work extends the SCET matching framework to higher-multiplicity processes by introducing a helicity operator basis that simplifies what was previously a laborious construction of operator bases with explicit Lorentz indices and gamma matrices. It builds upon prior SCET matching calculations for V+2 parton and H+2 parton processes (Baikov 2009, Gehrmann 2010, Abbate 2010), V+3 partons (Becher 2009, 2011, 2012, 2013), H+3 partons (Liu 2012, Jouttenus 2013, Huang 2014, Becher 2014), and massless 2→2 processes (Kelley 2010, Broggio 2014). The formalism enables analytic resummation for exclusive jet cross sections at LHC processes with higher multiplicities, and can be used to improve Monte Carlo parton-shower descriptions as demonstrated in Alioli (2012, 2013, 2015).
