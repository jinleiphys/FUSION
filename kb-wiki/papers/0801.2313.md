# Surface effects in color superconducting strange-quark matter

> Surface effects in strange-quark matter play an important role for certain observables which have been proposed in order to identify strange stars, and color superconductivity can strongly modify these effects. We study the surface of color superconducting strange-quark matter by solving the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations for finite systems ("strangelets") within the MIT bag model, supplemented with a pairing interaction. Due to the bag-model boundary condition, the strange-quark density is suppressed at the surface. This leads to a positive surface charge, concentrated in a layer of ~1 fm below the surface, even in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase. However, since in the CFL phase all quarks are paired, this positive charge is compensated by a negative charge, which turns out to be situated in a layer of a few tens of fm below the surface, and the total charge of CFL strangelets is zero. We also study the surface and curvature contributions to the total energy. Due to the strong pairing, the energy as a function of the mass number is very well reproduced by a liquid-drop type formula with curvature term.

## Key claim

The authors show that in color-flavor locked (CFL) strangelets, the total electric charge strictly vanishes despite a positive surface charge layer (~1 fm thick) from suppressed strange-quark density, because this positive charge is exactly compensated by a negative charge distributed over a much thicker layer (tens of fm) below the surface, a result that contradicts earlier assumptions that CFL strangelets would retain a net positive surface charge.

## Method

The authors solved the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations for finite spherical systems ("strangelets") within the MIT bag model, supplemented with a four-point pairing interaction in the scalar color antitriplet channel. They imposed the bag-model boundary condition on the quark fields, included Coulomb interactions in the Hartree approximation, and iterated the self-consistency problem for the pairing fields, Coulomb potential, and quark wave functions until convergence. The bag radius was determined by minimizing the total energy (quark energy plus bag volume energy) for fixed baryon number, and β-stability was enforced by comparing energies of configurations with different flavor compositions.

## Key numbers

- For a strangelet with baryon number A=108 and no pairing (HΛ²=0), the energy per baryon is E/A=932.5 MeV (including 1.0 MeV Coulomb) and the bag radius R=4.36 fm.
- For the 2SC-like solution with HΛ²=1.5 and A=108, the pairing gap at the center is Δ₂(0)=32.9 MeV, with E/A=930.7 MeV and R=4.31 fm.
- For the CFL-type solution with HΛ²=1.75 and A=108 (Z=0), the gaps at center are Δ₂(0)=33.9 MeV, Δ₅(0)=37.0 MeV, Δ₇(0)=36.9 MeV, with E/A=934.8 MeV and R=4.17 fm.
- In infinite matter with HΛ²=1.75, the bag constant is B^{1/4}=153.97 MeV, baryon density ρ_B=0.367 fm^{-3}, and pairing gaps Δ₂=35.1 MeV, Δ₅=Δ₇=34.5 MeV.
- The positive surface charge layer has thickness ~1 fm, while the compensating negative charge layer extends over a few tens of fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of strangelet surface properties by Madsen (2001), who found a positive surface charge from the bag-model boundary condition but did not treat pairing self-consistently. The authors show that in the CFL phase, where all quarks are paired, the total charge vanishes, contradicting the earlier picture of a net positive surface charge that would require an electron cloud. This result is relevant for models of strange star crusts composed of strangelets in an electron gas (proposed by Jaikumar et al.) and for understanding the electric field at strange star surfaces (studied by Usov and Page). The findings enable more realistic calculations of surface tension and pasta-phase formation in strange quark matter.
