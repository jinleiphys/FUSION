# Impact of slow conversions on hybrid stars with sequential QCD phase transitions

> In this paper, we investigate the impact of the phase conversion speed on the properties of hybrid stars with sequential sharp QCD phase transitions. We consider that these hybrid stars possess an inner (outer) core of CFL (2SC) quark matter surrounded by hadronic matter. Assuming that the phase conversions can be slow or rapid, we analyze the dynamical stability of these objects. In particular, we present our predictions for the mass-radius profile and the fundamental eigenfrequencies of hybrid stars with sequential QCD first order phase transitions. Our results demonstrate that the usual stability criteria i.e. $\partial M/ \partial P_0 \geq 0$, is sufficient only if both conversions are rapid. Moreover, we show that if any of the interfaces has a slow conversion speed, the fundamental eigenfrequencies are significantly modified.

## Key claim

The usual stability criterion (∂M/∂P₀ ≥ 0) is sufficient for dynamical stability only when both sequential phase conversions are rapid; if either interface has a slow conversion speed, the fundamental eigenfrequencies are significantly modified and stable configurations can exist even where the usual criterion fails.

## Method

The authors model hybrid stars with two sequential first-order QCD phase transitions: hadronic matter to 2SC quark matter (outer core), then 2SC to CFL quark matter (inner core). They use a generalized constant-speed-of-sound (CSS) parametrization for quark matter and generalized piece-wise polytropes for nuclear matter, solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations and the Chandrasekhar pulsation equations with junction conditions from Pereira (2017) for both rapid and slow phase conversions. They compute over 4000 equations of state, selecting 8 representative models, and analyze the fundamental eigenfrequency ω² for four conversion-speed combinations: rapid-rapid, rapid-slow, slow-rapid, and slow-slow.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear saturation density is n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- The hadronic EOS uses a Sly4 crust and matches chiral effective field theory at 1.1 n₀.
- For the first set of quark matter parameters: s₁ = 0.2 (2SC phase) and s₂ = 0.33 (CFL phase); for the second set: s₁ = 0.33 and s₂ = 0.5.
- Transition pressures are varied over ranges: 10 ≤ P₁ ≤ 60 MeV fm⁻³ and 80 ≤ P₂ ≤ 600 MeV fm⁻³.
- Energy density jumps are varied: 80 ≤ Δε₁ ≤ 800 MeV fm⁻³ and 20 ≤ Δε₂ ≤ 800 MeV fm⁻³.
- The oscillation period of fluid elements near interfaces is of order ≈ 1–100 kHz, so rapid/slow conversions are characterized by timescales smaller/larger than ≈ 0.01–1 ms.
- All selected EOSs produce stellar configurations surpassing the two solar mass limit.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Lugones (2021) on slow-stable hybrid stars with a single phase transition to the case of sequential QCD phase transitions. It builds on the formalism of Pereira (2017) for junction conditions at sharp interfaces and on the CSS parametrization of Alford (2017) for sequential transitions. Previous studies by Alford (2017), Han (2018), and Rodriguez (2020) assumed a single conversion speed for both transitions; this paper shows that different combinations of slow and rapid conversions produce distinct stable branches, opening up new parameter space for quark matter in hybrid stars that can be tested with future gravitational-wave and NICER observations.
