# Nuclear response at zero and finite temperature

> We present some recent developments on the nuclear many-body problem, such as the treatment of high-order correlations and finite temperature in the description of in-medium two-nucleon propagators. In this work we discuss two-time propagators of the particle-hole type, which describe the response of finite nuclei to external probes without nucleon transfer. The general theory is formulated in terms of the equation of motion method for these propagators with the only input from the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction. The numerical implementation was performed on the basis of the effective mason-nucleon Lagrangian in order to study the energy-dependent kernels of different complexity. The finite-temperature extension of the theory with $ph\otimes phonon$ configurations is applied to a study of the multipole response of medium-mass nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper presents a novel synthesis of the equation of motion (EOM) method with quantum hadrodynamics to describe nuclear response, claiming that including higher-order configurations such as 2q⊗2phonon (two quasiparticles coupled to two phonons) can resolve the insufficient fragmentation and spreading width of the giant dipole resonance that remained in the earlier relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation (RQTBA). The claim is hedged by the fact that the numerical implementations are still based on an effective meson-nucleon interaction (NL3) rather than the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction, and only the first iteration (n=2) of the iterative procedure has been performed.

## Method

The authors formulate the nuclear response using the equation of motion method for two-time particle-hole propagators, deriving a Dyson equation with an interaction kernel split into instantaneous and time-dependent parts. They map the EOM kernel to the particle-vibration coupling (PVC) approach in the time blocking approximation (TBA) by introducing a second correlation function into the dynamical kernel, creating the EOM/RQTBA³ approach. The numerical implementation uses the effective NL3 meson-nucleon Lagrangian from quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) to approximate the static part of the kernel, while the finite-temperature extension adopts the thermal (imaginary-time) Green's function formalism with a soft blocking procedure.

## Key numbers

- Dipole photoabsorption cross sections in ⁴²Ca and ⁴⁸Ca were calculated using EOM/RQTBA³ and compared to evaluated experimental data from Erokhova (2003) and the National Nuclear Data Center (NNDC)
- The dipole strength distribution in ⁶⁸Ni was studied at various temperatures using finite-temperature relativistic RPA (FT-RRPA) and finite-temperature relativistic time blocking approximation (FT-RTBA)
- The temperature dependence of the spin-isospin response was investigated and found to be even more sensitive to low and moderate temperatures than the dipole response

## Context

This work extends the relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation (RQTBA) developed by Litvinova, Ring, and Tselyaev (2007-2013) and the non-relativistic (quasiparticle) time blocking approach by Tselyaev (1989). It builds on the phenomenological multiphonon approach of Soloviev (1992) and Ponomarev (2001) by providing a more consistent microscopic foundation linked to the bare interaction. The finite-temperature results have implications for astrophysical modeling of the r-process nucleosynthesis and core-collapse supernovae, as the enhanced low-lying dipole and spin-isospin strength distributions at astrophysical conditions may affect these processes.
