# Effects of neutron-skin thickness on direct hard photon emission from reactions induced by the neutron-rich projectile $^{50}$Ca

> Direct hard photon emissions from incoherent proton-neutron bremsstrahlung in collisions of the neutron-rich projectile $^{50}$Ca with $^{12}$C and $^{40}$Ca targets are simulated in the framework of the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model. By adjusting the diffuseness parameter of neutron density in the droplet model to obtain different neutron skin thicknesses for $^{50}$Ca, the effects of neutron skin thickness on direct hard photon emission are investigated via several probes. The results show that more direct hard photons are produced with increasing neutron skin thickness in peripheral collisions. Meanwhile, we find that the multiplicity yield ratio $R_{cp}(σ_γ)$ between central collisions and peripheral collisions as well as the rapidity dependence of multiplicity for direct hard photons are sensitive to neutron skin thickness. The results indicate that direct hard photon emission can be taken as an experimental observable to extract information on neutron skin thickness.

## Key claim

Direct hard photon emission from incoherent proton-neutron bremsstrahlung in peripheral collisions of the neutron-rich projectile ⁵⁰Ca can serve as an experimental observable to extract neutron skin thickness, but the sensitivity is strongest at incident energies around 120 MeV/nucleon and for specific observables such as the yield ratio between central and peripheral collisions.

## Method

The authors performed simulations using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model with in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections, embedding an incoherent proton-neutron bremsstrahlung channel for hard photon production. They varied the neutron skin thickness of the ⁵⁰Ca projectile by adjusting the diffuseness parameter of the neutron density distribution in the droplet model, using values f_n = 1.0, 1.2, 1.4, and 1.6. Collisions of ⁵⁰Ca with ¹²C and ⁴⁰Ca targets were simulated at incident energies from 40 to 150 MeV/nucleon, with central collisions defined as 0%-10% centrality and peripheral collisions as 80%-100% centrality.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness δ_np values for ⁵⁰Ca range from approximately 0.15 fm (f_n = 1.0) to about 0.45 fm (f_n = 1.6), as shown in the inset of Figure 1.
- The total yield of direct hard photons increases with incident energy from 40 to 150 MeV/nucleon for all projectile-target combinations.
- The yield ratio R_cp(σ_γ) between central (0%-10%) and peripheral (80%-100%) collisions shows a decreasing trend with increasing f_n, with the effect most pronounced for incident energies above 100 MeV/nucleon.
- At an incident energy of 120 MeV/nucleon, the rapidity distributions of direct hard photon multiplicity show increasing yield with larger neutron skin thickness, especially at midrapidity.

## Context

This work extends prior studies that proposed neutron-to-proton yield ratios (Sun 2011) and ³H-to-³He yield ratios (Dai 2014) as probes of neutron skin thickness, but addresses the advantage that hard photons are not disturbed by final-state interactions. The results suggest that direct hard photon observables could complement measurements from parity-violating electron scattering (PREX experiment for ²⁰⁸Pb, CREX experiment for ⁴⁸Ca) and provide a method applicable to short-lived neutron-rich isotopes at radioactive ion beam facilities such as RIBF at RIKEN, FRIB at MSU, and HIAF at Huizhou.
