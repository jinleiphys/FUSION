# Thermal Relaxation of Dark Matter Admixed Neutron Star

> Motivated by the various theoretical studies regarding the efficient capturing of dark matter by neutron stars, we explore the possible indirect effects of captured dark matter on the cooling mechanism of a neutron star. The equation of states for different configurations of dark matter admixed star at finite temperature is obtained using the relativistic mean-field formalism with the IOPB-I parameter set. We show that the variation in the dark matter momentum vastly modifies the neutrino emissivity through specific neutrino generating processes of the star. The specific heat and the thermal conductivity of a dark matter admixed star have also been investigated to explore the propagation of cooling waves in the interior of the star. The dependence of theoretical surface temperature cooling curves on the equation of state and chemical composition of the stellar matter has also been discussed along with the observational data of thermal radiation from various sources. We observed that the dark matter admixed canonical stars with $k_{f}^{\rm DM} > 0.04$ comply with the fast cooling scenario. Further, the metric for internal thermal relaxation epoch has also been calculated with different dark matter momentum and we deduced that increment of dark matter segment amplify the cooling and internal relaxation rates of the star.

## Key claim

The authors show that dark matter admixed canonical neutron stars with dark matter Fermi momentum \(k_{f}^{\rm DM} > 0.04\) GeV comply with the fast cooling scenario, but this conclusion depends on the specific choice of the IOPB-I relativistic mean-field parameter set and the assumption that dark matter is fermionic neutralinos with a fixed Higgs coupling of \(y = 0.07\).

## Method

The authors used temperature-dependent relativistic mean-field (RMF) formalism with the IOPB-I parameter set to compute the equation of state (EOS) for neutron star matter at \(10^{9}\) K, incorporating fermionic dark matter (neutralinos) via a Higgs-mediated nucleon-dark matter interaction. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain stellar structure, then solved the relativistic energy balance and heat transport equations (Thorne 1977) to compute neutrino emissivity from eight processes (including direct Urca, modified Urca, and various Bremsstrahlung channels), specific heat, thermal conductivity, and surface temperature cooling curves for canonical and maximum-mass stars with dark matter momentum varied from 0.00 to 0.06 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of the neutron star without dark matter (\(k_{f}^{\rm DM}=0.00\) GeV) at \(10^{9}\) K: \(2.130 M_{\odot}\) with radius \(11.987\) km
- Maximum mass with \(k_{f}^{\rm DM}=0.06\) GeV: \(1.604 M_{\odot}\) with radius \(8.688\) km
- Canonical star radius (\(R_{1.4}\)) without dark matter: \(13.497\) km; with \(k_{f}^{\rm DM}=0.06\) GeV: \(9.250\) km
- Dark matter momentum threshold for fast cooling in canonical stars: \(k_{f}^{\rm DM} > 0.04\) GeV
- Central temperature fixed at \(10^{9}\) K for all EOS calculations
- Dark matter density considered around one thousand times smaller than nuclear saturation density
- Yukawa coupling for nucleon-Higgs interaction set to \(0.001336\); neutralino-Higgs coupling fixed at \(0.07\)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dark matter effects on neutron star structure (Das et al. 2021, Lourenco et al. 2021) by adding finite-temperature thermal evolution and cooling dynamics, which were previously explored for standard neutron stars without dark matter by Yakovlev (1999) and Page (2006). The results enable comparison of theoretical surface temperature cooling curves with observational data from X-ray observatories (Chandra, XMM-Newton, NICER) and can constrain dark matter properties through neutron star thermal observations.
