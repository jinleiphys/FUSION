# Baryon Spectrum Analysis using Dirac's Covariant Constraint Dynamics

> The energy spectrum of the baryons is determined by treating each of them as a three-body system with the interacting forces coming from a set of two-body potentials that depend on both the distance between the quarks and the spin and orbital angular momentum coupling terms. Constraint dynamics is first reviewed for a relativistic two-body system in order to assemble the necessary two body framework for the three-body problem and then we review the different types of covariant two-body interactions involved in constraint dynamics, including vector and scalar, and solve the problem of energy eigenstates using constraint dynamics. The Two Body Dirac equations of constraint dynamics derived by Crater and Van Alstine, matched with the quasipotential formalism of Todorov as the underlying two-body formalism are used, as well as the three-body constraint formalism of Sazdjian to integrate the three two-body equations into a single relativistically covariant three body equation for the bound state energies. The results are analyzed and compared to experiment using a best fit method and several different algorithms, including a gradient approach, and Monte Carlo method.

## Key claim

The paper presents a relativistic quark model for baryon spectroscopy that extends the Two-Body Dirac equations (TBDE) of constraint dynamics, previously successful for mesons, to the three-body quark problem using Sazdjian's N-body formalism, with the caveat that the approach is limited to confined systems where questions of correct cluster decomposition do not need to be addressed.

## Method

The authors combine three relativistic formalisms: the Two Body Dirac equations of Crater and Van Alstine for two-body interactions, the Todorov quasipotential formalism to provide an invariant framework for vector and scalar dynamics, and Sazdjian's three-body constraint formalism to integrate three two-body equations into a single covariant three-body equation. A variational principle with Gaussian basis wavefunctions of total JM is used to solve the eigenvalue equation, with a recursion algorithm to handle the energy dependence of the effective Hamiltonian, and a chi-squared minimization Monte Carlo routine combined with a simplified gradient approach is employed for fitting the baryon spectrum.

## Key numbers

- The scalar confining potential is taken as S = 4πΛ²r/27, modified from the meson case by a factor of 1/2 due to the different color factor (−2α_s/3 for quark-quark versus −4α_s/3 for quark-antiquark interactions)
- The vector potential is A = −8π/[27r log(Ke² + B/(Λr)²)] + e₁e₂/(4πr), again with the color factor adjusted for quark-quark interactions
- The Richardson potential in momentum space is given as Ṽ(q) = −(16π/27) × 1/[q² ln(1 + q²/Λ²)]
- The two-body effective particle energy is ε_w = (w² − m₁² − m₂²)/2w and the relativistic reduced mass is m_w = m₁m₂/w

## Context

This work extends the relativistic constraint dynamics formalism previously applied successfully to meson spectroscopy by Crater et al. (2009) using the Two-Body Dirac equations, and compares results to the approach of Capstick and Isgur (1986) for baryon spectroscopy. The paper enables relativistic three-body calculations for baryons using the same interaction potentials that worked for mesons, with the scalar confining interaction treated as arising from effective potentials between constituent quarks rather than fundamental vertices, allowing the sign of the qq scalar potential to be chosen the same as the q\bar{q} scalar potential for phenomenological reasons.
