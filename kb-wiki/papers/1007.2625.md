# Universal Quantum Viscosity in a Unitary Fermi Gas

> A Fermi gas of atoms with resonant interactions is predicted to obey universal hydrodynamics, where the shear viscosity and other transport coefficients are universal functions of the density and temperature. At low temperatures, the viscosity has a universal quantum scale $\hbar n$ where $n$ is the density, while at high temperatures the natural scale is $p_T^3/\hbar^2$ where $p_T$ is the thermal momentum. We employ breathing mode damping to measure the shear viscosity at low temperature. At high temperature $T$, we employ anisotropic expansion of the cloud to find the viscosity, which exhibits precise $T^{3/2}$ scaling. In both experiments, universal hydrodynamic equations including friction and heating are used to extract the viscosity. We estimate the ratio of the shear viscosity to the entropy density and compare to that of a perfect fluid.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity of a unitary Fermi gas exhibits universal scaling with temperature, following a precise \(T^{3/2}\) dependence at high temperatures, and the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density \(\eta/s\) reaches a minimum near the superfluid transition temperature, approximately five times the conjectured string theory bound of \(\hbar/(4\pi k_B)\).

## Method

The shear viscosity was extracted from two distinct hydrodynamic experiments on a 50-50 mixture of the two lowest hyperfine states of \(^6\)Li, magnetically tuned to a broad Feshbach resonance. At high temperatures (initial energy per particle \(E\) between \(2.3\,E_F\) and \(4.6\,E_F\)), the anisotropic expansion of the cloud after release from a deep optical trap was measured via the time-dependent aspect ratio, and the viscosity was determined by fitting to universal hydrodynamic equations that include both the friction force and the heating rate. At low temperatures, the viscosity was obtained from the damping rate of the radial breathing mode, using raw cloud profiles from prior work (Kinast 2004).

## Key numbers

* The trap-averaged viscosity parameter \(\bar{\alpha}\) was determined for initial energies \(E\) from \(0.6\,E_F\) to \(4.6\,E_F\).
* At high temperatures, the viscosity coefficient obeys \(\alpha_0 = \alpha_{3/2}\, \theta_0^{3/2}\), where \(\theta_0\) is the reduced temperature at the trap center.
* The simple approximation \(\bar{\alpha} \simeq \alpha_0\) yields \(\alpha_{3/2} = 3.4 \pm 0.03\) (statistical error from the fit).
* A relaxation model gives \(\bar{\alpha} = 1.3\,\alpha_0\) at high \(T\), yielding \(\alpha_{3/2} = 2.6\).
* A variational Boltzmann calculation for a 50-50 mixture gives \(\alpha_{3/2} = 45\pi^{3/2}/(64\sqrt{2}) = 2.77\).
* The ratio \(\eta/s\) near the critical energy \(E_c/E_F = 0.7-0.8\) is about five times the string theory limit \(\hbar/(4\pi k_B)\).

## Context

This work extends measurements of universal transport in unitary Fermi gases beyond equilibrium thermodynamics, which had been studied in detail by Luo (2007), Jin (2007), and Salomon (2008). The results enable direct comparison of the \(\eta/s\) ratio to the string theory bound conjectured by Kovtun (2005), which was previously applied to the quark-gluon plasma produced in gold ion collisions. The measured viscosity coefficients can test predictions from kinetic theory by Bruun (2007) and from variational calculations, and the smooth joining of data from two independent methods validates the universal hydrodynamic description including both friction and heating.
