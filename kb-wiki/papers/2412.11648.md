# Isotopic Transparency in Central Xe+Sn Collisions at 100 MeV/nucleon

> A new method, based on comparing isotopic yield ratios measured at forward and sideward polar angles and on cross-bombarding heavy nuclei with different neutron-to-proton ratios, is used to quantify the stopping power of nuclear matter in heavy-ion collisions. For central collisions of isotopically separated $^{124,129}$Xe+$^{112,124}$Sn at 100~MeV/nucleon bombarding energy, measured with the 4$π$ multidetector INDRA at GSI, a moderate transparency is deduced for hydrogen isotopes, whereas for heavier fragmentation products with atomic number $Z \ge 3$ a high transparency exceeding 50\% is observed. An anomalously large transparency is found for alpha particles, and possible explanations are presented.

# Isotopic Transparency in Central Xe+Sn Collisions at 100 MeV/nucleon

## Key claim
The paper claims that a moderate transparency (20-45%) is observed for hydrogen isotopes in central Xe+Sn collisions at 100 MeV/nucleon, while heavier fragments with atomic number Z ≥ 3 exhibit high transparency exceeding 50%. However, the abstract glosses over that these results depend on the method of selecting central events (multiplicity versus transverse energy), and that an anomalously large transparency exceeding 100% is found specifically for alpha particles, which the authors attribute to secondary deexcitation or alpha clustering effects rather than simple nuclear stopping.

## Method
The authors used the INDRA 4π multidetector at GSI to measure isotopic yield ratios from four combinations of isotopically separated 124,129Xe projectiles on 112,124Sn targets at 100 MeV/nucleon bombarding energy. Central collisions were selected by accepting only the top 1% of events based on either charged-particle multiplicity (Mc) or total transverse kinetic energy of light charged particles (E⊥12). Isotopic transparency was quantified by comparing yield ratios measured at forward polar angles (θcm ≤ 34°) versus sideward angles (80° ≤ θcm ≤ 100°), using the exponential dependence of yield ratios on the total N/Z of the system at sideward angles as a baseline for full isospin equilibration.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of stopping power in heavy-ion collisions by the INDRA and FOPI collaborations, particularly the excitation function for stopping in Au+Au collisions reported by Andronic et al. (2006) and the systematic study by Lehaut et al. (2010) for mass-symmetric reactions. The new method based on isotopic yield ratios complements earlier approaches using the isotropy ratio Rp defined by Reisdorf et al. (2004) and the momentum anisotropy studies of Le Fèvre et al. (2004, 2007) for Xe+Sn and Au+Au systems. The results enable quantitative comparison with dynamical models such as quantum molecular dynamics approaches used by Zbiri et al. and with statistical models like the Quantum Statistical Model (Hahn et al., 1988) and Statistical Multifragmentation Model (Botvina et al., 1995), providing experimental constraints on the degree of isospin equilibration and the density of the emitting source at freeze-out.
