# 3D Imaging via Polarized Jet Fragmentation Functions and Quantum Simulation of the QCD Phase Diagram

> Understanding the interactions between elementary particles and mapping out the internal structure of the hadrons are of fundamental importance in high energy nuclear and particle physics. This thesis concentrates on the strong interaction, described by Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). We introduce a novel concept called "polarized jet fragmentation functions" and develop the associated theory framework known as QCD factorization which allows us to utilize jet substructure to probe spin dynamics of hadrons, especially nucleon's three-dimensional imaging. Furthermore, non-perturbative QCD studies, particularly of the QCD phase diagram, are important for understanding the properties of hadrons. The development of quantum computing and simulators can potentially improve the accuracy of finite-temperature simulations and allow researchers to explore extreme temperatures and densities in more detail. In this thesis, I present my work in two aspects of QCD studies: (1) investigating the nucleon structure using polarized jet fragmentation functions and (2) illustrating how to apply quantum computing techniques for studying phase diagram of a low energy QCD model. The first category investigates phenomena such as hadron production inside jets, spin asymmetries, etc., providing valuable insight into the behavior of quarks and gluons in hadrons. The second category provides potential applications of quantum computing in QCD and explores the non-perturbative nature of QCD.

## Key claim

The thesis introduces "polarized jet fragmentation functions" as a novel theoretical framework that extends QCD factorization to jet substructure, enabling three-dimensional imaging of nucleon spin dynamics, but this approach is currently limited to theoretical development and has not yet been experimentally validated, pending future facilities like the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC).

## Method

The author develops QCD factorization theorems for polarized jet fragmentation functions, which describe how partons fragment into jets while preserving spin information, and applies this formalism to processes such as proton-proton collisions and electron-jet production at the future EIC. Separately, the author implements a quantum algorithm to simulate the chiral phase diagram of the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model in 1+1 dimensions at finite temperature and finite chemical potential, as a proof-of-principle for overcoming the sign problem in lattice QCD.

## Key numbers

- Strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s \sim 0.1\) for momentum transfers \(Q > 100\) GeV, where perturbative QCD applies
- The QCD Landau pole scale \(\Lambda \sim 200\) MeV, below which perturbative calculations become unreliable
- Proton size \(R_p \sim 1\) fm, corresponding to the non-perturbative scale \(\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}} \sim 200\) MeV
- The 1-loop \(\beta\)-function coefficient \(\beta_0 = (33 - 2n_f)/(12\pi)\) with \(n_f = 6\) quark flavors, giving \(\beta_0 > 0\) and producing asymptotic freedom
- Structure functions \(F_2\) and \(F_L\) are known to \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^3)\) (N\(^3\)LO) in perturbative QCD
- PDFs and FFs are extracted at scales \(Q^2 = 10\) GeV\(^2\) and \(Q^2 = 10^4\) GeV\(^2\) as shown in the MSHT20 and deFlorian (2014) analyses

## Context

This work extends the established collinear QCD factorization framework, which has successfully described DIS structure functions (Particle Data Group 2022), single inclusive jet production at CMS (2021), and hadron production at ALICE (2012), by incorporating transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions (TMDs) into jet substructure observables. The quantum simulation component addresses the sign problem in lattice QCD at finite baryon density (Ratti 2021), using the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model as a low-energy proxy for full QCD, and enables future exploration of the QCD phase diagram including the quark-gluon plasma regime relevant to the early Universe and neutron stars.
