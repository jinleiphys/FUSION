# Point-form dynamics of quasistable states

> We present a field theoretical model of point-form dynamics which exhibits resonance scattering. In particular, we construct point-form Poincaré generators explicitly from field operators and show that in the vector spaces for the in-states and out-states (endowed with certain analyticity and topological properties suggested by the structure of the $S$-matrix) these operators integrate to furnish differentiable representations of the causal Poincaré semigroup, the semidirect product of the semigroup of spacetime translations into the forward lightcone and the group of Lorentz transformations. We also show that there exists a class of \emph{irreducible} representations of the Poincaré semigroup defined by a complex mass and a half-integer spin. The complex mass characterizing the representation naturally appears in the construction as the square root of the pole position of the propagator. These representations provide a description of resonances in the same vein as Wigner's unitary irreducible representations of the Poincaré group provide a description of stable particles.

## Key claim

The paper presents a quantum field theoretical model of point-form dynamics that exhibits resonance scattering, showing that the Poincaré generators constructed from field operators integrate to furnish differentiable representations of the causal Poincaré semigroup only when the in-state and out-state spaces are endowed with specific analyticity and topological properties suggested by the S-matrix structure, rather than for arbitrary state spaces.

## Method

The authors construct point-form Poincaré generators by integrating the energy-momentum tensor and Lorentz tensor density on a forward hyperboloid \(x^\mu x_\mu = \tau^2, x^0 > 0\) for a model with two massive scalar fields and an interaction Lagrangian without derivative coupling. They then solve the eigenvalue problem for the interacting momentum operators by constructing creation and annihilation operators for the interacting system and obtaining a new vacuum state different from the initial Fock vacuum. Finally, they construct rigged Hilbert spaces for resonance scattering and demonstrate that the generators integrate to representations of the causal Poincaré semigroup, defined as the semidirect product of the semigroup of spacetime translations into the forward lightcone and the Lorentz group.

## Key numbers

- The causal Poincaré semigroup is defined as \({\cal P}_+ = \{(\Lambda,a): \Lambda\in SO(3,1),\ a^0\geq0,\ a_\mu a^\mu\geq0\}\).
- The forward hyperboloid used as the integration surface is defined by \(x^\mu x_\mu = \tau^2\) with \(x^0 > 0\).
- The inner product on the forward hyperboloid is given by \((\psi,\phi)_\tau := i\int 2d^4x\,\delta(x^2-\tau^2)\theta(x^0)x^\mu(\psi^*(x)\partial_\mu\phi(x)-\phi(x)\partial_\mu\psi^*(x))\).
- The one-particle velocity eigenstates are normalized as \(\langle\bs{q},m|\bs{q'},m\rangle = 2q^0\delta(\bs{q}-\bs{q}')\).
- The commutation relations for creation and annihilation operators are \([\hat{a}(\bs{q},m),\hat{a}^\dagger(\bs{q}',m)] = 2q^0\delta(\bs{q}-\bs{q}')\).

## Context

This work synthesizes two previously separate approaches: the Bakamjian-Thomas construction developed by Bohm, Wickramasekara, and others (Bohm and Harshman, 1998; Bohm et al., 2001; Wickramasekara and Bohm, 2002) which emphasized point-form dynamics and semigroup integrability but lacked concrete field-theoretic realizations, and the explicit field-theoretic models of Antoniou, Gadella, and others (Antoniou et al., 1998; Antoniou et al., 1999) which constructed interaction-incorporating Poincaré algebras but did not achieve manifest Lorentz invariance of the analyticity properties needed for semigroup representations. The paper enables a consistent relativistic quantum field theory of resonances and decaying states, extending Wigner's characterization of stable particles by unitary irreducible representations of the Poincaré group to quasistable states via irreducible representations of the causal Poincaré semigroup defined by a complex mass and half-integer spin.
