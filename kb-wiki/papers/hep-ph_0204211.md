# Does parton saturation at high density explain hadron multiplicities at RHIC ?

> We discuss the recent claim that hadron multiplicities measured at RHIC energies are directly described in terms of gluon degrees of freedom fixed from the initial conditions of central heavy ion collisions. The argument is based on the parton saturation scenario expected to be valid at high parton densities and on the assumption of conserved gluon number. Alternatively we conjecture that "bottom-up" equilibration before hadronization modifies this picture, due to nonconservation of the number of gluons.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the "bottom-up" equilibration scenario, which includes gluon number-changing inelastic processes, provides a more convincing description of RHIC hadron multiplicities than the direct parton-hadron duality assumed by Kharzeev, Levin, and Nardi (KLN), but only if the undetermined parameters \(c\) and \(c_{eq}\) lie within a specific limited range.

## Method
The authors critically review the KLN approach, which assumes conserved gluon number and uses the McLerran-Venugopalan model to relate initial gluon density to final hadron multiplicity. They then derive the charged hadron multiplicity predicted by the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario, which includes gluon splitting (\(2 \leftrightarrow 3\) processes) that increase gluon number between initial and equilibration times. The resulting expression is compared to PHOBOS data for central Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 130\) GeV and \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) GeV, and the parameters \(c\) (initial gluon liberation constant) and \(c_{eq}\) (equilibration time constant) are constrained by consistency and by the requirement that the equilibration temperature exceed the deconfinement temperature.

## Key numbers
- For central Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 130\) GeV, the PHOBOS reference value for the scaled charged hadron multiplicity is \(\langle \frac{2}{N_{part}} \frac{d N_{ch}}{d\eta} \rangle = 3.24 \pm 0.1 (stat) \pm 0.25 (syst)\).
- In the KLN approach, agreement with data requires \(c \simeq 3\), whereas the numerical estimate from classical field simulations gives \(c \simeq 0.5\) for \(Q_s = 1\) GeV.
- In the "bottom-up" scenario, consistency constraints require \(c \le 1.5\) and \(c_{eq} \ge 1.5\).
- For the preferred values \(c \simeq 1\) and \(c_{eq} \simeq 2\), the "bottom-up" scenario yields for central collisions: ratio of final to initial gluon number \(R = 3\), equilibration temperature \(T_{eq} = 230\) MeV, and equilibration time \(\tau_{eq} = 3.6\) fm (for \(Q_s = 1\) GeV).
- The "bottom-up" prediction for the multiplicity at \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) GeV is \(\langle \frac{2}{N_{part}} \frac{dN_{ch}}{d\eta} \rangle = 3.84\), compared to the PHOBOS measurement of \(3.78 \pm 0.25 (syst)\).

## Context
This paper extends the debate on whether hadron multiplicities at RHIC are determined directly by initial-state parton saturation (as argued by Kharzeev, Levin, and Nardi (2001)) or are modified by subsequent thermalization. The authors contrast the KLN assumption of conserved gluon number with the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario of Baier, Mueller, Schiff, and Son (2001), which includes inelastic gluon branching. The work enables future calculations of the equilibration constant \(c_{eq}\) and more precise estimates of the gluon liberation constant \(c\) to resolve which picture is correct.
