# Subleading contributions to the three-nucleon contact interaction

> We obtain a minimal form of the two-derivative three-nucleon contact Lagrangian, by imposing all constraints deriving from discrete symmetries, Fierz identities and Poincare' covariance. The resulting interaction, depending on 13 unknown low-energy constants, leads to a three-nucleon potential which we give in a local form in configuration space. We also consider the leading (no-derivative) four-nucleon interaction and show that there exists only one independent operator.

## Key claim

The paper derives a minimal form of the two-derivative three-nucleon contact Lagrangian, reducing an initial list of 146 operators to 13 independent low-energy constants after imposing constraints from discrete symmetries, Fierz identities, and Poincare covariance; the abstract does not emphasize that 5 of the 18 operators surviving Fierz constraints are further eliminated by relativity requirements.

## Method

The authors enumerated all possible two-derivative three-nucleon contact operators compatible with rotational, isospin, parity, and time-reversal invariance, obtaining 146 operators. They applied Fierz rearrangement identities to reduce this set to 18 independent operators, then imposed Poincare covariance constraints to eliminate 5 additional operators, leaving 13. The resulting three-nucleon potential was expressed in local form in configuration space using a momentum cutoff depending only on momentum transfer, and the same Fierz methodology was applied to the leading four-nucleon contact Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- The initial list of two-derivative three-nucleon contact operators contained 146 operators (Table 1).
- After Fierz rearrangements, 128 linearly independent relations were found among the 3 × 146 relations, leaving 18 independent operators.
- Poincare covariance further reduced the independent operators from 18 to 13.
- The leading (no-derivative) four-nucleon contact Lagrangian, initially written with 16 operators, reduces to only 1 independent operator after Fierz rearrangements.
- The subleading three-nucleon contact potential depends on 13 unknown low-energy constants (E1 through E13).
- The leading four-nucleon contact potential is governed by a single low-energy constant F.

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory treatment of nuclear forces beyond the leading three-nucleon contact interaction (the E-term) used in calculations by Navratil and Gazit, and addresses the claim by Bedaque (2002) that only one subleading three-nucleon contact term is needed for renormalization. The authors argue that all symmetry-allowed terms must be included at a given order, not just those required by renormalization. The derived 13-parameter potential enables systematic fitting to polarization observables in nucleon-deuteron scattering where discrepancies exist (e.g., vector analyzing powers Ay and iT11), and the four-nucleon contact term can independently reproduce the 4He binding energy, allowing decoupling of three-nucleon and four-nucleon sectors.
