# Finite-temperature equation of state of polarized fermions at unitarity

> We study in a nonperturbative fashion the thermodynamics of a unitary Fermi gas over a wide range of temperatures and spin polarizations. To this end, we use the complex Langevin method, a first principles approach for strongly coupled systems. Specifically, we show results for the density equation of state, the magnetization, and the magnetic susceptibility. At zero polarization, our results agree well with state-of-the art results for the density equation of state and with experimental data. At finite polarization and low fugacity, our results are in excellent agreement with the third-order virial expansion. In the fully quantum mechanical regime close to the balanced limit, the critical temperature for superfluidity appears to depend only weakly on the spin polarization.

## Key claim
The complex Langevin method provides nonperturbative first-principles results for the thermodynamics of the unitary Fermi gas at finite spin polarization, but the authors caution that the method remains "under construction" and its reliability is supported by self-consistency checks and agreement with other methods in limiting cases rather than by rigorous mathematical proof.

## Method
The authors discretize the unitary Fermi gas on a (3+1)-dimensional spacetime lattice with spatial volume L³ (L = 7, 9, 11 lattice sites) and temporal extent Nτ = 160 steps, then apply a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to represent the interaction via an auxiliary field. To circumvent the sign problem that arises when the chemical potentials for spin-up and spin-down fermions differ, they use the complex Langevin method, which complexifies the auxiliary field and evolves it stochastically in the complex plane. They compute the density, magnetization, and magnetic susceptibility as functions of the average chemical potential βμ and the chemical potential difference βh.

## Key numbers
- Temperature fixed at β = 8.0, corresponding to thermal de Broglie wavelength λT ≈ 7.0 in lattice units.
- Chemical potential range: βμ ∈ [-3.0, 2.5]; asymmetry parameter range: βh ∈ [0.0, 2.0].
- Critical chemical potential for the balanced superfluid transition: (βμ)c ≈ 2.5, from prior work (Ku 2012).
- Third-order virial coefficients used: b2 = 3/(4√2) and b3 ≈ -0.29095; fourth-order coefficient for unpolarized gas: b4 = 0.078(18) from Yan (2016).
- At βh = 2.0 and the largest βμ values, the relative magnetization reaches approximately 25%.

## Context
This work extends prior nonperturbative studies of the unitary Fermi gas, which were largely restricted to the unpolarized (balanced) case due to the sign problem in conventional Monte Carlo methods. The authors previously validated the complex Langevin approach for one-dimensional spin- and mass-imbalanced systems (Loheac 2018, Rammelmüller 2017). Their results at zero polarization agree with experimental data from Ku (2012) and Van Houcke (2012), as well as with diagrammatic Monte Carlo calculations (Van Houcke 2012) and determinantal hybrid Monte Carlo (Drut 2012). The finite-polarization results enable future ab initio studies of the Chandrasekhar-Clogston limit and the dependence of the superfluid critical temperature on spin polarization.
