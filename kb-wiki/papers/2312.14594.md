# Thermalization and quark production in spatially homogeneous systems of gluons

> We first assemble a full set of the Boltzmann Equation in Diffusion Approximation (BEDA) for studying thermalization/hydrodynamization and quark production in out of equilibrium systems. We then discuss thermalization and the production of three flavors of massless quarks in spatially homogeneous systems initially filled only with gluons. A complete parametric understanding for thermalization and quark production is obtained for both initially very dense or dilute systems, which are complemented by detailed numerical simulations. For initial distributions more relevant for heavy-ion collisions, the complete thermal equilibration is found to be significantly delayed by considering quark production.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for initial gluon distributions relevant to heavy-ion collisions, the inclusion of quark production significantly delays complete thermal equilibration compared to a pure-gluon system, with the final stage of thermalization following a "top-down" pattern where gluons thermalize among themselves before quarks fill a Fermi-Dirac profile.

## Method

The authors assemble the Boltzmann Equation in Diffusion Approximation (BEDA) for gluons, quarks, and antiquarks, combining a Fokker-Planck-type \(2 \leftrightarrow 2\) kernel with a \(1 \leftrightarrow 2\) kernel computed in the deep LPM regime. They then solve this kinetic equation numerically for spatially homogeneous systems initially containing only gluons, studying both underpopulated (dilute) and overpopulated (dense) initial conditions, and track the time evolution of macroscopic quantities and distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- For the underpopulated scenario with initial gluon occupancy \(f_0 = 10^{-4}\), the soft gluon thermalization scale is \(p_{s,g} \equiv (\hat{q}_A m_D^4 t^2/2)^{1/5}\) and the soft quark thermalization scale is \(p_{s,q} \equiv [\alpha_s C_F\pi(\mathcal{I}_c+\bar{\mathcal{I}}_c)t]^{2/5}\hat{q}_F^{1/5}\).
- For the overpopulated scenario with initial gluon occupancy \(f_0 = 100\), the quark distribution exhibits self-similar scaling of the form \(F = F_s((Qt)^{1/7}p / Q)\).
- The gluon distribution at small momenta approximates \(f^g(p) \approx T_*/p\) for \(p \lesssim p_{s,g}\), and the quark distribution approximates \(f^q(p) \approx 1/(e^{-\mu_*/T_*} + 1)\) for \(p \lesssim p_{s,q}\).

## Context

This work extends the pure-gluon kinetic theory results of Barrera Cabodevila (2022) by including quark production via \(g \rightarrow q\bar{q}\) and \(gg \rightarrow q\bar{q}\) processes. It builds on the diffusion approximation framework developed by Mueller (1999) and Blaizot (2014) for the \(2 \leftrightarrow 2\) kernel, and the LPM-resummed \(1 \leftrightarrow 2\) kernel from Baier (1996) and Arnold (2008). The finding that gluons thermalize among themselves before quarks is consistent with the results of Kurkela (2018). The work enables future studies of thermalization in expanding systems more directly relevant to heavy-ion collision dynamics.
