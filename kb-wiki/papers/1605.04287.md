# Initial conditions for hydrodynamics from weakly coupled pre-equilibrium evolution

> We use effective kinetic theory, accurate at weak coupling, to simulate the pre-equilibrium evolution of transverse energy and flow perturbations in heavy-ion collisions. We provide a Green function which propagates the initial perturbations to the energy-momentum tensor at a time when hydrodynamics becomes applicable. With this map, the complete pre-thermal evolution from saturated nuclei to hydrodynamics can be modelled in a perturbatively controlled way.

## Key claim

The paper provides a perturbatively controlled Green function that propagates initial energy and flow perturbations from the earliest moments of a heavy-ion collision (around \(\tau \sim 1/Q_s\)) to a later time when viscous hydrodynamics becomes applicable, but only under the assumption of weak coupling (\(\alpha_s \ll 1\)) and for linearized perturbations on top of a translationally invariant Bjorken-expanding background.

## Method

The authors use effective kinetic theory (EKT) at leading order in the coupling \(\lambda = 4\pi \alpha_s N_c\) to simulate the pre-equilibrium evolution of gluonic matter in heavy-ion collisions. They split the distribution function into a boost-invariant, transversely uniform background and a linearized perturbation with a transverse wave number \(k_\perp\), then solve the coupled Boltzmann equations numerically for both the background and the perturbation. The initial condition at \(\tau_0 = 1/Q_s\) is taken from classical Yang-Mills simulations of the Color-Glass Condensate framework, and the response functions for energy and momentum perturbations are extracted at suggested hydrodynamic initialization times \(Q_s \tau = 10\) and \(Q_s \tau = 20\).

## Key numbers

- The asymptotic entropy per area per rapidity is \(\Lambda_s^2 = 1.95 \, Q_s^2\), implying the asymptotic gluon multiplicity is 2.33 times the input multiplicity at \(\tau_0\).
- At \(Q_s \tau = 10\) the entropy has reached 72% of its asymptotic value; at \(Q_s \tau = 20\) it has reached 82%.
- For \(\lambda = 10\) (corresponding to \(\alpha_s \approx 0.26\)), the shear viscosity is \(\eta/s = 0.62\) and the relaxation time is \(\tau_\pi = 5.1 \, \eta/sT\).
- The dispersion relation for sound modes deviates significantly from second-order hydrodynamics for wavenumbers \(k/T \gtrsim 0.4\).
- Setting \(Q_s \simeq 1.4 \, \text{GeV}\) reproduces the entropy required by hydrodynamic simulations at the LHC, giving an initialization time \(\tau_i \simeq 1.4 \, \text{fm}/c\) and a temperature \(T \simeq 430 \, \text{MeV}\) at \(Q_s \tau = 10\).
- The asymptotic relation between initial and final energy perturbations in the long-wavelength limit is \(\lim_{\tau \to \infty} \delta e(\tau)/e(\tau) = (8/9) \, \delta e(\tau_0)/e(\tau_0)\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Kurkela and collaborators (2015) which used EKT to monitor equilibration of a uniform plasma during Bjorken expansion, by now including transverse gradients that generate preflow. It provides a controlled perturbative alternative to models that either neglect prethermal evolution entirely (Niemi 2015) or use ad hoc prescriptions to regulate extreme initial conditions from the IP-glasma model (Schenke 2012). The Green function enables future hydrodynamic simulations to incorporate pre-equilibrium dynamics in a way that is independent of the initialization time, improving the empirical determination of QGP transport coefficients.
