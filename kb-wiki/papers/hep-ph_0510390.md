# In-Medium formation of J/Psi as a probe of charm quark thermalization

> Charmonium formation via charm quark in-medium recombination in heavy ion interactions at collider energies has the potential to probe some properties of the medium by utilizing the sensitivity of the recombination process to the momentum distribution of the quarks. We have examined the transverse momentum spectra of J/Psi, characterized by <p_T^2>, which result from the formation process in which the charm quark distributions are unchanged from their initial production in a pQCD process. This is contrasted with the case in which the charm quarks have completely come into thermal equilibrium with an expanding medium whose properties are determined by the spectra of produced light hadrons. We find that the resulting <p_T^2> of the formed J/Psi provide a distinct signature of the underlying charm quark spectra, and that signature is essentially independent of the detailed dynamics of the in-medium formation reaction. In addition, both of these signatures are sufficiently separated from the case in which no in-medium formation takes place. Finally, utilizing a model for the fraction of J/Psi which originate from in-medium formation, we predict the centrality behavior of these signatures.

## Key claim

The transverse momentum squared of J/Psi formed via in-medium charm quark recombination provides a distinct signature of the underlying charm quark momentum distribution, and this signature is essentially independent of the detailed dynamics of the formation reaction, provided the charm quarks have either retained their initial pQCD distribution or have fully thermalized.

## Method

The author calculated J/Psi transverse momentum spectra from in-medium formation using a Boltzmann equation with competing formation and dissociation reactions, starting from charm quark distributions either unchanged from NLO pQCD production (supplemented by an initial-state transverse momentum kick) or fully thermalized with parameters (T = 170 MeV, maximum transverse expansion rapidity = 0.5) determined by light hadron spectra. The formation probability was weighted using an operator-product motivated cross section for ccbar forming J/Psi with gluon emission, and the resulting <p_T^2> values were compared to those from initial "diagonal" ccbar pair hadronization and to thermal J/Psi spectra.

## Key numbers

- For minimum bias Au-Au collisions with no in-medium formation, the predicted <p_T^2> of J/Psi from initial ccbar pairs is approximately 6.3 GeV^2.
- For in-medium formation from pQCD charm quarks, the predicted <p_T^2> is approximately 3.6 GeV^2.
- For in-medium formation from thermalized charm quarks (T = 170 MeV, y_Tmax = 0.5), the predicted <p_T^2> is approximately 1.3 GeV^2.
- For J/Psi themselves obeying the thermal distribution, the predicted <p_T^2> is approximately 3.0 GeV^2.
- The initial-state transverse momentum broadening parameter extracted from d-Au data is lambda^2 = 0.35 +/- 0.14 GeV^2, based on an average of 5.4 inelastic interactions in minimum bias d-Au at RHIC.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of J/Psi production as a signature for color deconfinement (Matsui 1986) and in-medium recombination (Braun-Munzinger 2000, Thews 2000) by focusing on the transverse momentum spectrum rather than absolute yields, which are sensitive to model parameters. It contrasts with the approach of Batsouli (2002), who found that low-pT decay leptons from charmed hadrons could not differentiate thermal from pQCD charm distributions, and shows that J/Psi from in-medium formation can distinguish these cases. The results enable experimental tests at RHIC where a non-monotonic behavior of <p_T^2> with system size (falling below the d-Au value in Au-Au) would signal in-medium formation, with the absolute value correlated to the charm quark distribution in the medium.
