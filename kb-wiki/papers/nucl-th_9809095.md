# Renormalization schemes and the range of two-nucleon effective field theory

> The OS and PDS renormalization schemes for the effective field theory with nucleons and pions are investigated. We explain in detail how the renormalization is implemented using local counterterms. Fits to the NN scattering data are performed in the 1S0 and 3S1 channels for different values of mu_R. An error analysis indicates that the range of the theory with perturbative pions is consistent with 500 MeV.

## Key claim

The range of the two-nucleon effective field theory with perturbative pions is consistent with 500 MeV, based on an error analysis of nucleon-nucleon scattering data at next-to-leading order, and this range does not depend on the choice of renormalization scheme (OS or PDS) or the value of the renormalization point.

## Method

The authors performed fits to nucleon-nucleon scattering data in the 1S0 and 3S1 channels using both the OS (off-shell momentum subtraction) and PDS (power divergence subtraction) renormalization schemes, for different values of the renormalization scale μ_R. They implemented renormalization using local counterterms, computed beta functions for the four-nucleon operators in both schemes, and applied an error analysis to the phase shift δ rather than to p cot δ, following a method due to Lepage.

## Key numbers

- The large S-wave scattering lengths are 1/a(1S0) = -8.32 MeV and 1/a(3S1) = 36.4 MeV.
- The scale associated with potential pion exchange is approximately 300 MeV, derived from M g_A^2 / (8 π f^2) ~ (300 MeV)^{-1}, where M is the nucleon mass, g_A = 1.25 is the axial-vector coupling, and f = 130 MeV is the pion decay constant.
- The physical threshold for Δ resonance production in elastic nucleon scattering is p = 525 MeV; for S-wave channels coupling to ΔΔ intermediate states, the threshold is p = 740 MeV.
- The rho meson mass is m_ρ = 770 MeV, and the N*(1440) N intermediate state has a threshold of p = 685 MeV.
- The error analysis indicates that the range of the theory with perturbative pions is consistent with 500 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (KSW, 1998), who introduced the PDS scheme and estimated the range of the theory as Λ_π ~ 300 MeV based on the running of the coupling C_0(μ_R). It also addresses arguments by Gegelia (1998), Steele and Furnstahl (1998), and Cohen (1997) that the range might be as small as m_π. By introducing the OS scheme and showing that the power counting remains manifest for all μ_R > 1/a, the authors demonstrate that the range is not limited by the large-μ_R behavior of the couplings, and they provide a data-driven estimate of Λ_π ~ 500 MeV. This enables future higher-order calculations to obtain a more accurate determination of the effective theory's range.
