# Microscopic investigation of one- and two-proton decay from the excited states of 10C

> We present microscopic cluster model calculations for the 1p and 2p decays of the 0+2 and 2+2 states of 10C. With the R-matrix method, we have estimated the decay widths. The obtained 1p and 2p decay widths are in good agreement with the recent experimental data and support the validity of the di-proton approximation for the 2p decay of 10C(0+2). We also show the suppression of the 1p decay of the 10C(0+2) state due to the structure mismatch with the decay channel.

# Microscopic investigation of one- and two-proton decay from the excited states of 10C

## Key claim

The microscopic cluster model calculations, combined with the R-matrix method, reproduce the experimental 1p and 2p decay widths of the 0+2 and 2+2 states of 10C in order of magnitude, supporting the di-proton approximation for the 2p decay of 10C(0+2) only when the two-proton wave function is assumed to be a compact diproton with a fixed inter-proton distance of 0.5 fm; the authors caution that this two-body model may not apply to other 2p emitters such as 6Be.

## Method

The authors used the real-time evolution method combined with the generator coordinate method to obtain wave functions of 10C, 9B, and 8Be, with the Volkov No.2 central interaction and G3RS spin-orbit interaction. The reduced width amplitudes were calculated using the Laplace expansion method, and the partial decay widths were obtained via the R-matrix formalism by comparing the logarithmic derivatives of the RWA and the Coulomb function to determine the channel radius. For the 2p decay, the two-proton wave function was approximated by a single Slater determinant projected to Jπ=0+ with the two protons placed at a fixed distance of 0.5 fm, representing a compact diproton state.

## Key numbers

* 1p decay width of 10C(0+2) to 9B(3/2-)+p: 11 keV, compared to experimental upper limit of less than 10 keV (Charity 2022).
* 2p decay width of 10C(0+2) to 8Be(0+)+2p: 119 keV, compared to experimental value of 96(57) keV (NNDC).
* 1p decay width of 10C(2+2) to 9B(3/2-)+p: 473 keV, compared to experimental value of 250(46) keV (Charity 2022).
* 1p decay width of 9B(1/2+) to 8Be(0+)+p: 355 keV, compared to experimental value of 700(+270/-200) keV (Scholl 2011).
* Decay Q-value for 10C(0+2) to 9B(3/2-)+p: 1.21 MeV; to 8Be(0+)+2p: 1.40 MeV.
* Decay Q-value for 10C(2+2) to 9B(3/2-)+p: 1.37 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic cluster model approach, previously developed by Chiba (2017), Zhao (2021, 2022), and Taniguchi (2021) for calculating reduced width amplitudes, to the study of proton decays from excited states of 10C. It provides quantitative confirmation of the qualitative explanation by Fortune (2006) that the suppression of 1p decay from 10C(0+2) is due to structural mismatch between the valence proton configurations in 10C(0+2) and 9B(3/2-), and that the 2p decay can be described by a two-body di-proton model. The results enable future applications of the microscopic cluster model to three-body decay calculations, as noted by Grigorenko (2009), and suggest caution in applying the two-body approximation to other 2p emitters such as 6Be.
