# Neutrino absorption by hot nuclei in supernova environments

> Using the thermal quasiparticle random phase approximation, we study the process of neutrino and antineutrino capture on hot nuclei in supernova environments. For the sample nuclei $^{56}$Fe and $^{82}$Ge we perform a detailed analysis of thermal effects on the strength distribution of allowed Gamow-Teller transitions which dominate low-energy charged-current neutrino reactions. The finite temperature cross sections are calculated taking into account the contributions of both allowed and forbidden transitions. The enhancement of the low-energy cross sections is explained by considering thermal effects on the GT$_\pm$ strength. For $^{56}$Fe we compare the calculated finite-temperature cross sections with those obtained from large-scale shell-model calculations.

# Neutrino absorption by hot nuclei in supernova environments

## Key claim

The thermal quasiparticle random phase approximation (TQRPA) reveals that finite-temperature neutrino and antineutrino capture cross sections on hot nuclei in supernova environments are several times larger at low neutrino energies than those obtained from large-scale shell-model calculations, because the TQRPA accounts for thermally unblocked low-energy Gamow-Teller transitions that do not appear in shell-model approaches due to application of Brink's hypothesis.

## Method

The authors applied the proton-neutron thermal quasiparticle random phase approximation (TQRPA) based on thermo-field dynamics to compute temperature-dependent strength functions for allowed Gamow-Teller and forbidden transitions in the sample nuclei ⁵⁶Fe and ⁸²Ge. They used a phenomenological nuclear Hamiltonian consisting of Woods-Saxon potentials, BCS pairing interactions, and separable multipole and spin-multipole residual interactions in the particle-hole channel, solving the finite-temperature BCS equations and TQRPA secular equations to obtain phonon excitation spectra and transition probabilities. Cross sections for neutrino and antineutrino absorption were calculated using the Walecka-Donnelly formalism with Coulomb corrections via Fermi functions and effective momentum approximation, including Pauli blocking from the degenerate electron gas.

## Key numbers

- For ⁵⁶Fe, the ground-state reaction threshold is Q₋ = 4.56 MeV for νₑ absorption and Q₊ = 4.71 MeV for ν̅ₑ absorption
- For ⁸²Ge, the Q₋-value is negative at Q₋ = −4.71 MeV, allowing neutrino absorption at all energies, while the antineutrino threshold is Q₊ = 13.58 MeV
- The critical temperature for pairing phase transition is T_cr ≈ 0.8 MeV for ⁵⁶Fe and T_cr ≈ 0.6 MeV for ⁸²Ge, based on zero-temperature pairing gaps of Δ_p(n) = 1.57(1.36) MeV for ⁵⁶Fe and Δ_p(n) = 1.22(0.0) MeV for ⁸²Ge
- For ⁵⁶Fe(νₑ,e⁻) at E_ν = 10 MeV, the QRPA cross section is 2.39 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (with exponent −1), compared to 3.63 × 10⁻⁴² cm² from Skyrme QRPA and 6.61 × 10⁻⁴² cm² from the hybrid approach
- For ⁵⁶Fe(ν̅ₑ,e⁺) at E_ν = 10 MeV, the QRPA cross section is 4.92 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (with exponent −1), compared to 2.95 × 10⁻⁴² cm² from Skyrme QRPA and 0 from the hybrid approach
- For ⁵⁶Fe(νₑ,e⁻) at E_ν = 100 MeV, the QRPA cross section is 7.26 × 10⁻⁴² cm² (with exponent +3), compared to 6.60 × 10⁻⁴² cm² from Skyrme QRPA and 6.52 × 10⁻⁴² cm² from the hybrid approach

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous TQRPA studies of neutral-current inelastic neutrino scattering on ⁵⁴,⁵⁶Fe and ⁸²Ge (Dzhioev 2014, Dzhioev 2011) to charged-current neutrino and antineutrino absorption reactions relevant to core-collapse supernova environments. It contrasts with the large-scale shell-model approach developed for iron group nuclei by Sampaio (2001, 2002) and Juodagalvis (2005), which applies Brink's hypothesis assuming Gamow-Teller distributions on excited states are identical to those on the ground state, and with the hybrid approach of Kolbe (2003) and Toivanen (2001) that combines shell-model GT contributions with RPA for other multipoles. The TQRPA method avoids Brink's hypothesis and can be applied to nuclei beyond the pf-shell where shell-model diagonalization becomes computationally prohibitive, enabling future studies of neutrino-nucleus reactions for a wider range of nuclei in supernova simulations.
