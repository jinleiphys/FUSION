# Reaction mechanism of quasi-free knockout processes in exotic RI beam era

> The quasi-free nucleon knockout reaction has been revealed the single-particle nature of nuclei. Thanks to the advances in experimental techniques and reaction theory, various new aspects of nuclei are being revealed by knockout reactions. In this article, we review the basic concept of the quasi-free knockout reaction, and recent achievements in the SEASTAR project using the MINOS system. We also present our new findings on the low-energy nucleon knockout reaction and the $α$ knockout reaction. The combination of the (microscopic) structure theory, reaction theory and experiments will be the key to a complete understanding of the $α$ formation and its universality in the coming decades. Noble clusters, e.g., $d$, $t$, $^{3}$He, etc. are in the scope of the ONOKORO project. The implementation of the two (and more) nucleon correlation in the reaction theory is essential to connect the properties of such clusters and the reaction observables. A new framework, CDCCIA, is introduced for this purpose, which will also be applicable to the two-nucleon knockout reactions, e.g., $(p,3p)$, $(p,2pn)$, and $(p,p2n)$.

## Key claim

The paper reviews quasi-free knockout reactions as a probe of nuclear structure, but emphasizes that the interpretation of observables (such as spectroscopic factors and momentum distributions) depends critically on the reaction mechanism, beam energy, and separation energy of the knocked-out particle, with contributions from competing processes like transfer and inelastic decay becoming non-negligible at lower incident energies.

## Method

The authors review the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework for describing quasi-free nucleon and cluster knockout reactions, including its extension to fragile clusters via the newly introduced CDCCIA method. They analyze experimental data from the SEASTAR project at RIKEN RIBF using the MINOS detection system, comparing measured momentum distributions and cross sections with DWIA, Quantum Transfer-to-the-Continuum (QTC), and microscopic structure calculations (shell model, VS-IMSRG, SCGF, HFB, THSR, AMD).

## Key numbers

- $^{54}$Ca$(p,pn)^{53}$Ca reaction: longitudinal momentum distributions for ground state, 2220 keV and 1738 keV excited states were measured, supporting $p$-wave valence neutron and $N=34$ shell closure
- $^{14}$O$(p,2p)^{13}$N and $^{14}$O$(p,pn)^{13}$O reactions at $\sim 100$ MeV/nucleon: proton separation energy $S_p = 4.2$ MeV, neutron separation energy $S_n = 23.2$ MeV; the $(p,pn)$ channel shows strong asymmetry with long low-momentum tail and sharp high-momentum cut
- $^{8}$He$(p,p\alpha)4n$ reaction: four-neutron resonance observed at $2.37\pm0.38$(stat.)$\pm0.44$(sys.) MeV with width $1.75\pm0.22$(stat.)$\pm0.30$(sys.) MeV
- $^{112,116,120,124}$Sn$(p,p\alpha)$Cd reactions: experimental $\alpha$ knockout cross sections show isotope dependence consistent with theoretical prediction of $\alpha$ formation on nuclear surface
- $^{212}$Po: $\alpha$ decay lifetime about $0.3~\mu$s, while knockout reaction timescale is approximately $10^{-22}$ s ($\sim 30~\mathrm{fm}/c$)

## Context

This review extends the long history of quasi-free knockout studies dating back to Jacob (1966), Chant (1977, 1983), and the systematic cluster knockout work of Carey (1981, 1984). It summarizes recent achievements from the SEASTAR project at RIKEN RIBF using the MINOS system, and introduces the ONOKORO project for studying noble clusters (deuteron, triton, $^{3}$He). The new CDCCIA framework enables treatment of fragile clusters and two-nucleon knockout reactions such as $(p,3p)$, $(p,2pn)$, and $(p,p2n)$, which were previously inaccessible to standard DWIA. The work enables future systematic studies of $\alpha$ clustering across the nuclear chart and quantitative connection between cluster formation amplitudes and reaction observables.
