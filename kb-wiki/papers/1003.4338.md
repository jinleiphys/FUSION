# Spin 1/2^+, spin 3/2^+ and transition magnetic moments of low lying and charmed baryons

> Magnetic moments of the low lying and charmed spin 1/2^+ and spin 3/2^+ baryons have been calculated in the SU(4) chiral constituent quark model (\chiCQM) by including the contribution from c \bar c fluctuations. Explicit calculations have been carried out for the contribution coming from the valence quarks, "quark sea" polarizations and their orbital angular momentum. The implications of such a model have also been studied for magnetic moments of the low lying spin 3/2^+ \to 1/2^+ and 1/2^+ \to 1/2^+ transitions as well as the transitions involving charmed baryons. We are able to achieve an excellent agreement with data for the case of low lying spin 1/2^+ baryons and spin 3/2^+ baryons. For the spin 1/2^+ and spin 3/2^+ charmed baryon magnetic moments, our results are consistent with the predictions of the QCD sum rules, Light Cone sum rules and Spectral sum rules. For the cases where "light" quarks dominate in the valence structure, the sea and orbital contributions are found to be fairly significant however, they cancel in the right direction to give the correct magnitude of the total magnetic moment. On the other hand, when there is an excess of "heavy" quarks, the contribution of the "quark sea" is almost negligible. The effects of configuration mixing and quark masses have also been investigated.

## Key claim

The SU(4) chiral constituent quark model, including contributions from valence quarks, "quark sea" polarizations, and their orbital angular momentum, achieves excellent agreement with experimental data for low-lying spin 1/2+ and spin 3/2+ baryon magnetic moments without using any magnetic moments as inputs, while for charmed baryons the results are consistent with QCD sum rule predictions rather than experimental data.

## Method

The authors extended the SU(3) chiral constituent quark model to SU(4) by including c-cbar fluctuations from Goldstone boson emission, calculating magnetic moments as the sum of valence quark spin polarization contributions, "quark sea" spin polarization contributions, and orbital angular momentum contributions of the "quark sea" via the generalized Cheng-Li mechanism. They computed these contributions for all low-lying octet and decuplet baryons, single-charmed and double-charmed spin 1/2+ and spin 3/2+ baryons, and transition magnetic moments for spin 3/2+ to spin 1/2+ and spin 1/2+ to spin 1/2+ transitions including charmed baryon transitions. The symmetry breaking parameters were fixed by deep inelastic scattering data on spin polarization functions and flavor distribution functions, while the mixing angle was fixed by fitting the neutron charge radius.

## Key numbers

- Proton magnetic moment: 2.79 mu_N (perfect fit to experimental value)
- Sigma+ magnetic moment: 2.46 mu_N (perfect fit to experimental value)
- Xi0 magnetic moment: -1.25 mu_N (perfect fit to experimental value)
- Lambda magnetic moment: -0.61 mu_N (perfect fit to experimental value)
- Delta++ magnetic moment: 4.51 mu_N (within experimental range 3.7 to 7.5)
- Delta+ magnetic moment: 2.0 mu_N (agrees with experimental value 2.7+1.0-1.3 +/- 1.5 +/- 3)
- Omega- magnetic moment: -1.71 mu_N (agrees with experimental value -1.94 +/- 0.31)
- Proton spin polarizations: Delta u = 0.93, Delta d = -0.34, Delta s = -0.03, Delta c = -0.002
- Flavor distribution for proton: anti-u = 0.23, anti-d = 0.34, anti-s = 0.086, anti-c = 0.005
- Symmetry breaking parameters: a = 0.12, alpha = beta = 0.45, zeta = -0.21, gamma = 0.11
- Quark masses used: M_u = M_d = 0.33 GeV, M_s = 0.51 GeV, M_c = 1.70 GeV

## Context

This work extends the SU(3) chiral constituent quark model calculations of magnetic moments by Dahiya et al. to the SU(4) framework incorporating charm quark fluctuations, building on earlier SU(4) spin and flavor structure calculations by Song et al. and Dahiya et al. The results improve upon non-relativistic quark model predictions by Choudhury et al. and are consistent with QCD sum rule approaches by Wang et al., QCD Spectral sum rules, and Light Cone QCD sum rules by Aliev et al. The model enables future experimental tests of intrinsic charm contributions to baryon magnetic moments and provides predictions for charmed baryon magnetic moments where no experimental data currently exists.
