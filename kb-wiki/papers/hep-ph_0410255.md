# rho^0 mass in a hot hadron gas

> We study the behavior of the rho^0 vector mass during the hadronic phase of an ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collision at finite temperature. We show that scattering with the most abundant particles during this stage, namely, pions, kaons and nucleons, leads to an overall temperature dependent, decrease of the rho^0 intrinsic mass at rest, compared to its value in vacuum. The main contributions arise from s-channel scattering with pions through the formation of a_1 resonances as well as with nucleons through the formation of even parity, spin 3/2 [N(1720)] and 5/2 [$Δ$(1905)] nucleon resonances. We show that it is possible to achieve a shift in the intrinsic rho^0 mass of order - 40 MeV, when including the contributions of all the relevant mesons and baryons that take part in the scattering, for temperatures between chemical and kinetic freeze-out.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the intrinsic mass of the rho^0 meson at rest decreases with temperature in a hot hadron gas, with a possible shift of approximately -40 MeV when including scattering contributions from pions, kaons, and nucleons via s-channel resonance formation, but only for temperatures between chemical and kinetic freeze-out and only when both s- and t-channel contributions are included.

## Method

The author computed the one-loop thermal self-energy of the rho^0 meson using the imaginary-time formalism of thermal field theory, evaluating the real part of the self-energy at zero three-momentum. The calculation included interactions with pions, kaons, and nucleons via effective Lagrangians respecting current conservation and parity invariance, using generalized Rarita-Schwinger propagators for baryon resonances with spin 3/2 and 5/2. Coupling constants were determined by comparing theoretical branching ratios for resonance decay into the rho-N channel with experimentally measured values, folding with a relativistic Breit-Wigner spectral function for the rho meson.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum lifetime of the rho meson is approximately 1.3 fm, shorter than the lifetime of the system created in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
- The STAR collaboration reported a shift of approximately -40 MeV for the peak of the invariant mass distribution of rho^0 -> pi+ pi- in minimum bias p+p collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, and approximately -70 MeV in peripheral Au+Au collisions at the same energy.
- The paper claims a possible shift in the intrinsic rho^0 mass of order -40 MeV when including contributions from all relevant mesons and baryons, for temperatures between chemical and kinetic freeze-out.
- The coupling constants for baryon resonances were computed: f = 9.7 for N(1520) (3/2^-), f = 7 for N(1720) (3/2^+), f = 4.4 for Delta(1700) (3/2^-), and f = 12.9 for Delta(1905) (5/2^+), using a cutoff Lambda = 1 GeV.
- The rho-nucleon coupling constants were taken as f_{rho NN} = 2.63 and kappa = 6.1.
- The vacuum decay width of the rho meson used in the calculation was Gamma^{vac} = 150 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of temperature-driven modifications to rho meson properties from interactions with mesons (Rapp, year not specified) and with baryons studied in dense nuclear matter contexts (Teodorescu, Friman, Peters, years not specified). The author builds on a recent relativistic framework (Ayala, year not specified) that included baryon resonance interactions, and provides a full account of both s- and t-channel contributions from scattering off pions, kaons, and nucleons. The results enable interpretation of the STAR collaboration's observed shifts in the rho^0 invariant mass distribution in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies, and provide a systematic thermal field-theoretical calculation for the dilute, nearly baryon-free hadronic phase between chemical and kinetic freeze-out.
