# Bayesian study of relativistic open and hidden charm in anisotropic lattice QCD

> We present the first combined study of correlators and spectral properties of charmonium and open-charm-mesons at finite temperature using a fully relativistic lattice QCD approach. The QCD medium is captured by second generation anisotropic 24^3xN_t lattices from the FASTSUM collaboration, including 2+1 flavors of clover discretized quarks with m_pi~380 MeV. Two Bayesian methods are deployed to reconstruct the spectral functions, the recent BR method as well as the Maximum Entropy Method with Fourier basis. We take particular care to disentangle genuine in-medium effects from method artifacts with the help of the reconstructed correlator. Consistent with the direct inspection of correlators, we observe no significant in-medium modification for J/Psi and eta_c around the crossover, while the chi_c states on the other hand show clear changes around the transition. At the highest temperature, T=352 MeV, J/Psi and eta_c also exhibit discernible changes compared to the vacuum. For D mesons around $T_c$, no significant modifications are observed, but we find clear indications that no bound state survives at the highest temperature of T=352 MeV. Above T_c we discover a significant difference between D and D* mesons, the latter being much more strongly affected by the medium.

# Bayesian study of relativistic open and hidden charm in anisotropic lattice QCD

## Key claim

The paper presents the first combined study of temporal correlators and spectral functions for both charmonium and open-charm mesons at finite temperature using a fully relativistic lattice QCD approach, with the caveat that the pion mass is unphysically heavy at approximately 380 MeV and the pseudocritical temperature is determined to be 185(4) MeV. The authors observe no significant in-medium modification for J/Psi and eta_c around the crossover temperature, while chi_c states show clear changes around the transition, and at the highest temperature of 352 MeV both J/Psi and eta_c exhibit discernible changes compared to vacuum.

## Method

The study uses second generation anisotropic 24^3 x N_tau lattices from the FASTSUM collaboration with 2+1 flavors of clover discretized quarks and an anisotropy parameter of xi = a_s/a_tau = 3.5, with spatial lattice spacing a_s = 0.123 fm. Temperature is varied in a fixed scale manner by changing the number of points in the Euclidean time direction, with eight temperatures ranging from 44 MeV to 352 MeV. Two Bayesian methods are deployed to reconstruct spectral functions from Euclidean correlators: the BR method and the Maximum Entropy Method with Fourier basis, using the reconstructed correlator to disentangle genuine in-medium effects from method artifacts.

## Key numbers

- Pseudocritical temperature: T_c = 185(4) MeV, determined from the inflection point of the Polyakov loop
- Pion mass: m_pi approximately 380 MeV (unphysically heavy)
- Spatial lattice spacing: a_s = 0.123 fm
- Anisotropy parameter: xi = a_s/a_tau = 3.5
- Highest temperature studied: T = 352 MeV (1.90 T_c)
- Vacuum ground state masses from effective mass fits: eta_c = 2.960(6) GeV, J/Psi = 3.041(7) GeV, chi_c0 = 3.45(4) GeV, chi_c1 = 3.9(6) GeV, D = 1.88(1) GeV, D* = 1.99(1) GeV, D_s = 1.943(8) GeV, D*_s = 2.06(1) GeV
- In the pseudoscalar channel, thermal modifications of the correlator are less than 2% at all temperatures
- At the highest temperature of 352 MeV, clear indications that no D meson bound state survives

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of charmonium at finite temperature by Aarts et al. (2007) and Borsanyi et al. (2014) to include open charm mesons for the first time using temporal correlators, whereas prior open charm studies by Bazavov et al. (2014) and Mukherjee et al. (2015) used spatial correlators and cumulants. The results enable comparison with the statistical model of hadronization for charmonium yields from RHIC to LHC energies as discussed by Andronic et al. (2008), and provide a thermal baseline for understanding D meson flow measurements at the LHC by ALICE (2013, 2017). The reconstructed correlator ratios show qualitative differences from those of Ding et al. (2012), with an intermediate upward bend between T_c and 1.5 T_c not seen in that earlier work.
