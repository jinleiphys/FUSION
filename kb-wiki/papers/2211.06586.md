# Confinement-deconfinement transition in $SU(3)$-Higgs theory

> We study lattice cutoff effects on the confinement-deconfinement transition and the $Z_3$ symmetry in $SU(3)$-Higgs theory in $3+1$ dimensions. The Higgs in this study is a complex triplet with vanishing bare mass and quartic coupling. The lattice cutoff is regulated by varying the number of temporal lattice sites, $N_τ$. Our results show that the nature of the confinement-deconfinement transition depends on $N_τ$. For $N_τ=2$ the transition is found to be the end point of a first-order transition and is first order for $N_τ\ge 3$. The distributions of the Polyakov loop and other observables, sensitive to the $Z_3$ symmetry, show that the strength of $Z_3$ explicit breaking decreases with $N_τ$. Up to $T\simeq 2T_c$, the free energy difference between $Z_3$ states decreases with $N_τ$, suggesting the realization of $Z_3$ symmetry in the continuum limit.

## Key claim

The confinement-deconfinement transition in $SU(3)$-Higgs theory with vanishing bare Higgs mass and quartic coupling depends on the lattice cutoff, with the transition being an endpoint of a first-order transition for $N_\tau=2$ and first-order for $N_\tau\ge 3$; the $Z_3$ explicit breaking decreases with increasing $N_\tau$, suggesting realization of $Z_3$ symmetry in the continuum limit, contrary to strong-coupling and perturbative calculations that predicted maximal explicit breaking for these parameters.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of $SU(3)$-Higgs theory on $N_s^3 \times N_\tau$ lattices with $N_s \ge 4N_\tau$ for $N_\tau = 2, 3, 4, 8$, using a discretized action with hopping parameter $\kappa = 1/8$ (corresponding to vanishing bare Higgs mass and quartic coupling). They computed the Polyakov loop magnitude and phase distributions, the gauge-Higgs interaction term $S_K$, and the gauge action $S_g$, and performed finite-size scaling analysis using Binder cumulants and susceptibility of magnetization-like observables to determine the nature of the transition.

## Key numbers

- For $N_\tau=2$, the Binder cumulant $U_M$ near the critical point lies within 0.44--0.48 for spatial sizes $N_s = 12, 20, 30, 40$, consistent with the 3D Ising universality class value of approximately 0.47
- The critical exponent $\gamma/\nu$ determined from susceptibility of M-like observables for $N_\tau=2$ is approximately 1.973, compared to the 3D Ising value of approximately 1.968
- For $N_\tau=3$, the transition is first order at $\beta_g = 1.854$; for $N_\tau=4$, the transition is first order at $\beta_g = 1.904$
- The differences $\Delta S_K$ and $\Delta S_g$ between $\theta=0$ and $\theta=2\pi/3$ $Z_3$ states decrease exponentially with $N_\tau$ up to $T \simeq 2T_c$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $Z_2$ symmetry in $SU(2)$-Higgs theory by Biswal et al. (2016) to the $SU(3)$ case, where the Higgs is a complex triplet rather than a doublet. The results contradict expectations from strong-coupling calculations by Green et al. (1983) and perturbative loop calculations by Weiss (1981) and Biswal et al. (2018), which predicted maximal explicit $Z_N$ breaking for vanishing Higgs mass and quartic coupling. The findings enable future studies of $Z_3$ explicit breaking on the full phase diagram of $SU(3)$-Higgs theory including quartic interactions, and suggest that continuum-lattice simulations of the confinement-deconfinement transition in $SU(3)$ with fundamental matter may recover the $Z_3$ symmetry.
