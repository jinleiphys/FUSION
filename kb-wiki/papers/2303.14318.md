# Soft dipole resonance in $^8$C and its isospin symmetry with $^8$He

> We investigate the soft dipole resonance in the proton-rich nucleus $^8_6$C$_2$, which is a collective dipole oscillation of four valence protons against the $α$ core,and discuss the isospin symmetry with the mirror nucleus $^8_2$He$_6$. We use the $α+N+N+N+N$ five-body cluster model and many-body resonances are obtained using the complex-scaling method. The $1^-$ resonance of $^8_6$C$_2$ is confirmed at the excitation energy of 13 MeV with a large decay width of 24 MeV, and its structure is similar to the soft dipole resonance in $^8_2$He$_6$, such as the configuration mixing and the spatial properties. These results indicate a good isospin symmetry in the soft dipole resonances of two nuclei with a common collective excitation of multiproton and multineutron, while the ground states of two nuclei show different properties due to the Coulomb repulsion of valence protons in $^8_6$C$_2$, leading to the symmetry breaking. In conclusion, the appearance of the isospin symmetry differs depending on the states of $^8_6$C$_2$ and $^8_2$He$_6$.

## Key claim

The soft dipole resonance (SDR) in the proton-rich nucleus $^8$C is confirmed at an excitation energy of 13 MeV with a large decay width of 24 MeV, and its structure shows good isospin symmetry with the SDR in $^8$He, even though the ground states of the two mirror nuclei exhibit symmetry breaking due to Coulomb repulsion in $^8$C.

## Method

The authors used an $\alpha+N+N+N+N$ five-body cluster model within the cluster orbital shell model (COSM) to describe $^8$C and $^8$He, assuming an $\alpha$ cluster core. Many-body resonances were obtained using the complex-scaling method, which transforms coordinates by a scaling angle $\theta$ to expose resonance poles in the complex energy plane. The Hamiltonian included the $\alpha$-nucleon Kanada-Kaneko-Nagata-Nomoto potential, the Minnesota potential for nucleon-nucleon interactions, and Coulomb interactions for valence protons.

## Key numbers

- The $1^-$ resonance of $^8$C is found at an excitation energy of 12.65 MeV with a decay width of 24.04 MeV (resonance pole at 15.97 MeV from the $\alpha+p+p+p+p$ threshold).
- The SDR in $^8$He is found at an excitation energy of 14.02 MeV with a decay width of 21.12 MeV.
- The ground state of $^8$C is a $0^+$ resonance at 3.32 MeV above the $\alpha+p+p+p+p$ threshold with a decay width of 0.072 MeV, close to the experimental value of 3.449(30) MeV.
- The relative distance between the $\alpha$ core and four valence nucleons in the ground state is 2.05 fm for $^8$He and 2.36 fm for $^8$C; for the SDRs, these values become $2.67+0.84i$ fm and $2.62+1.15i$ fm, respectively.
- The estimated $\hbar\omega_R$ for the $\alpha$-$4N$ relative motion is 17.2 MeV for $^8$He and 13.0 MeV for $^8$C, compared to calculated excitation energies of 14.0 MeV and 12.7 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous predictions of the soft dipole resonance in $^8$He (Myo et al., 2022a, 2022b, 2023) to its mirror nucleus $^8$C, using the same five-body cluster model and complex-scaling method. The study enables future experimental searches for these resonances (Lehr et al., 2022) and opens the question of whether the isospin symmetry observed in the SDRs persists when $\alpha$ cluster excitation is allowed, which could couple the SDR with the giant dipole resonance.
