# Spectrum of resonance states in $^6$He. Experimental and theoretical analysis

> We explore the structure of resonance states in $^{6}$He by experimental and theoretical methods. We present the results of experimental investigations of the three-body continuous spectrum of $^{6}$He. For this aim, we use the reaction $^{3}$H$( α,pα)nn$, which is induced by the interaction of alpha-particles with a triton at the beam energy $E_α =$~67.2 MeV. The theoretical analysis of the resonance structure in $^{6}$He is carried out within the framework of a three-cluster microscopic model. The model exploits the hyperspherical harmonics to describe the intercluster dynamics. The set of new resonance states is discovered by the experimental and theoretical methods. The energy, width, and dominant decay channels of resonances are determined.The obtained results are compared in detail with the results of different theoretical models and experiments as well.

## Key claim

The paper claims to have discovered ten excited resonance states in \(^{6}\)He by combining experimental data from the \(^{3}\)H\((\alpha,p\alpha)nn\) reaction at \(E_\alpha = 67.2\) MeV with a three-cluster microscopic model, but the identification and parameters of these states depend on a fitting procedure that assumes a two-stage reaction mechanism and uses a Breit-Wigner decomposition, meaning the existence of some states is conditional on the validity of this model-dependent analysis.

## Method

The experiment used the four-body reaction \(^{3}\)H\((\alpha,p\alpha)nn\) induced by alpha-particles at 67.2 MeV on a triton target, detecting proton-alpha coincidences with two \(\Delta E-E\) telescopes. The coincidence events were selected using a Monte Carlo simulation to isolate those from the four-body reaction, and the resulting proton-energy spectrum was projected onto the excitation energy of \(^{6}\)He and fitted with a sum of Breit-Wigner terms plus a phase-space background. The theoretical analysis employed a microscopic three-cluster model based on the resonating group method combined with hyperspherical harmonics, using the Volkov and Minnesota nucleon-nucleon potentials to solve the Schrödinger equation for the three-cluster continuum and extract resonance energies and widths from eigenphase shifts of the S-matrix.

## Key numbers

- Ground state of \(^{6}\)He is bound by 0.936 MeV relative to the \(\alpha+n+n\) threshold (stated in introduction).
- Beam energy of alpha-particles: \(E_\alpha = 67.2 \pm 0.4\) MeV.
- Ten excited levels of \(^{6}\)He were extracted from the experiment; for example, level \(N_s=1\) at excitation energy \(E = 1.7 \pm 0.2\) MeV with width \(\Gamma = 0.65 \pm 0.15\) MeV; level \(N_s=2\) at \(E = 2.5 \pm 0.2\) MeV, \(\Gamma = 0.4 \pm 0.2\) MeV; level \(N_s=3\) at \(E = 3.1 \pm 0.3\) MeV, \(\Gamma = 0.4 \pm 0.2\) MeV; level \(N_s=10\) at \(E = 18.5 \pm 0.4\) MeV, \(\Gamma = 1.7 \pm 0.6\) MeV.
- For the theoretical model with the Volkov potential N2 (\(m=0.615\)), the calculated ground-state energy of \(^{6}\)He is \(-0.953\) MeV (compared to the experimental value \(-0.973\) MeV).
- Oscillator lengths used: \(b = 1.37\) fm for Volkov potentials and \(b = 1.285\) fm for the Minnesota potential.
- Maximal hypermomentum \(K_{\text{max}} = 14\) for positive parity states and \(K_{\text{max}} = 13\) for negative parity states; hyperradial excitations up to \(n_{\rho,\text{max}} = 70\) (equivalent to 140\(\hbar\Omega\) shell-model excitations).

## Context

This work extends earlier experimental studies of \(^{6}\)He resonances, notably the lower-energy measurement at \(E_\alpha = 27.2\) MeV by Mougeot et al. (2014) which detected two low-lying levels at 2.4 and 3 MeV, and the theoretical three-cluster model developed by Vasilevsky et al. (2001). The higher beam energy in the present experiment allowed exploration of the excitation spectrum up to about 20 MeV, revealing many new states. The results are compared with prior experimental data from the \(^{7}\)Li\((n,d)^{6}\)He reaction by Brady et al. (1977), the \(^{6}\)He\((p,p\alpha)\) reaction by Lapoux et al. (2001), and the \(^{7}\)Li\((t,\alpha)^{6}\)He reaction by Ajzenberg-Selove et al. (1954), as well as with the transfer reaction \(p(^{8}\text{He},t)\) by Mougeot et al. (2012). The theoretical model enables future studies of the nature of three-cluster resonances, including their dominant decay channels and geometric configurations.
