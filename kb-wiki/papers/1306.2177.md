# Production of strangeness -1 and -2 hypernuclei within a field theoretic model

> We present an overview of a fully covariant formulation for describing the production of strangeness -1 and -2 hypernuclei using probes of different kinds. This theory is based on an effective Lagrangian picture and it focuses on production amplitudes that are described via creation, propagation and decay into relevant channel of intermediate baryonic resonance states in the initial collision of the projectile with one of the target nucleons. The bound state nucleon and hyperon wave functions are obtained by solving the Dirac equation with appropriate scalar and vector potentials. Specific examples are discussed for reactions which are of interest to current and future experiments on the hypernuclear production.

## Key claim

A fully covariant effective Lagrangian model, which treats baryons as Dirac particles and includes intermediate baryonic resonance states, can describe the production of both strangeness -1 and -2 hypernuclei across hadronic and electromagnetic probes within a single theoretical framework, provided that the bound-state spinors are obtained from solving the Dirac equation with appropriate scalar and vector potentials.

## Method

The production amplitudes are calculated from Feynman diagrams where the projectile interacts with a bound target nucleon, exciting intermediate baryon or hyperon resonance states that propagate and decay into a kaon and a hyperon, which is then captured into a nuclear orbit. For strangeness -1 production, three nucleon resonances (N*(1650), N*(1710), N*(1720)) with appreciable branching ratios to the K+Λ channel are included; for strangeness -2 production via the (K-,K+) reaction, the intermediate channels are Λ, Σ hyperons and eight of their resonances with masses up to 2.0 GeV. The bound-state nucleon and hyperon wave functions are obtained by solving the Dirac equation with Woods-Saxon scalar and vector potentials, with depths searched to reproduce the experimental or predicted binding energies.

## Key numbers

- For the 0s1/2 Λ state in 12ΛB, the experimental binding energy is 11.37 MeV; the searched scalar and vector potential depths are Vs = -212.70 MeV and Vv = 171.78 MeV, giving Vs + Vv = -40.92 MeV for the Λ-nucleus interaction depth.
- For the 0s1/2 Ξ state in 12Ξ-Be, the binding energy predicted by the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model is 3.04 MeV; the searched potential depths are Vs = -112.11 MeV and Vv = 90.81 MeV, giving Vs + Vv = -21.30 MeV for the Ξ-nucleus interaction depth.
- The threshold for the 12C(γ,K+)12ΛB reaction is about 695 MeV; the momentum transfer at 10° kaon angle varies from approximately 2 fm-1 to 1.4 fm-1 for photon energies between 0.7 GeV and 1.2 GeV.
- The differential cross sections for 12C(γ,K+)12ΛB peak at photon energies around 850 MeV, about 200 MeV above the production threshold.
- The threshold beam momenta for the 12C(K-,K+)12Ξ-Be and 28Si(K-,K+)28Ξ-Mg reactions are about 0.761 GeV/c and 0.750 GeV/c, respectively.
- For 12Ξ-Be production, the cross sections peak at pK- around 1.0 GeV/c, about 0.3 GeV/c above threshold, with magnitudes in excess of 1 μb near the peak.
- The QMC model predicts for 28Ξ-Mg three bound Ξ- states: 1s1/2 (binding energy 8.982 MeV), 1p3/2 (4.079 MeV), and 1p1/2 (4.414 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous effective Lagrangian studies of (p,K+) reactions (Shyam 2004, 2006), (π+,K+) reactions (Ben10), and (γ,K+) reactions (Shyam 2008, 2009) to include strangeness -2 hypernuclear production via the (K-,K+) reaction, building on an elementary p(K-,K+)Ξ- study (Shyam 2011). The model enables predictions for experiments at the JPARC facility in Japan, which has an approved proposal to produce Ξ hypernuclei, and complements ongoing and future measurements at Jefferson Laboratory and MAMI-C for Λ hypernuclei. The approach contrasts with impulse approximation models (Ike94) and earlier potential models (Dov83), and the extracted Ξ-nucleus interaction depth of about -21 MeV is higher than the shallow potential of approximately -16 MeV conjectured from emulsion events in the E176 experiment (Aok93).
