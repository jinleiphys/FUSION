# Microscopic pairing in fission dynamics

> Nuclear fission can be modelled as a quantum tunneling process driven by the interplay between the nuclear binding energy and the collective inertia. Within the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin formalism, spontaneous fission half-lives can be obtained by minimizing the action integral in the multidimensional space of collective degrees of freedom. Hence, including the relevant collective variables is crucial for properly describing spontaneous fission probabilities. Pairing correlations play an essential role in this evaluation since the collective inertia decreases as the inverse of the square of the pairing gap, and, therefore, they should be considered as a relevant degree of freedom on the same footing as deformation parameters. In this work, we show that the spontaneous fission half-lives in fermium isotopes can be reproduced in a microscopic theory by considering the least-action fission path in a two-dimensional space with constraints on the quadrupole moment and pairing correlations. We consider two microscopic quantities as degrees of freedom associated with pairing: the pairing gap parameter $Δ$, and the particle number fluctuations $\langle ΔN^2 \rangle$. Least-action paths, computed using the Dijkstra algorithm, are compared with minimum-energy paths, highlighting the importance of pairing correlations as a dynamical degree of freedom.

## Key claim

Spontaneous fission half-lives in fermium isotopes can be reproduced in a microscopic theory by considering the least-action fission path in a two-dimensional space with constraints on the quadrupole moment and pairing correlations, but the agreement depends on the specific isotope and the choice of pairing coordinate (pairing gap parameter or particle number fluctuations), with some discrepancies remaining for the lightest isotope studied.

## Method

The authors used the self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory with the Gogny D1S nuclear interaction to compute potential energy surfaces and collective inertia tensors in a two-dimensional collective space defined by the axial quadrupole moment and a pairing degree of freedom (either the pairing gap parameter or the average particle number fluctuations). The least-action fission paths were determined using the Dijkstra algorithm on a regular grid of constrained HFB calculations, and spontaneous fission half-lives were computed within the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) formalism using the adiabatic time-dependent HFB framework with the perturbative cranking approximation for collective inertia.

## Key numbers

- For $^{262}$Rf, the static least-energy path gives $\log_{10} (T_{1/2}/\mathrm{s}) = 4.61$, while the least-action path gives $\log_{10} (T_{1/2}/\mathrm{s}) = -2.61$ with the pairing gap as coordinate and $\log_{10} (T_{1/2}/\mathrm{s}) = -1.27$ with particle number fluctuations, compared to the experimental value of $\log_{10} (T_{1/2}/\mathrm{s}) = -0.6$.
- In $^{262}$Rf, the $B_{22}$ component of the collective inertia is reduced by a factor of 20 along the least-action path compared to the static path.
- The experimental half-life of $^{242}$Fm is $T_{1/2} = 800\ \mu$s, and $^{258}$Fm has $T_{1/2} = 370\ \mu$s, while $^{252}$Fm has $T_{1/2} = 129$ years.
- The inner fission barrier height for $^{242-244}$Fm is approximately 8 MeV, with a narrow outer barrier of 1-2 MeV.
- For $^{246-250}$Fm isotopes, the inner and outer fission barriers reach heights of 10 MeV and 7 MeV, respectively.
- The half-lives are reduced by three to eight orders of magnitude when using the least-action path compared to the least-energy path across the fermium isotopic chain.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pairing correlations in fission dynamics, which were performed in macroscopic-microscopic models by Baran (1978), Staszczak (1985, 1989), and Lojewski (1999), and later in microscopic self-consistent calculations by Giuliani (2014) and others. The authors build on their own prior work (Zdeb, 2025) that established the equivalence of the pairing gap and particle number fluctuations as pairing coordinates for static fission paths. The results enable future studies of fission dynamics in three-dimensional collective spaces that include octupole deformation as an additional degree of freedom, and highlight the need to address limitations of the perturbative cranking approximation for collective inertia.
