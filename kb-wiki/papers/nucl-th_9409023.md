# Quantal Bifurcations in Rotational Spectra of Some Odd Atomic Nuclei

> To show the existence of precursor phenomena of the transition order$\ to$chaos in atomic nuclei a simple analysis has been made, based on a recent criterion proposed by Pavli\-chenkov. The basic idea is that nonlinear effects in rotational bands should lead to bifurcation, which qualitatively changes the spectrum for one of the nuclear excitation branches. Experimental data concerning the isotopes of Er, Hf, Yb, Os, Dy, W strongly support the above criterion.

# Quantal Bifurcations in Rotational Spectra of Some Odd Atomic Nuclei

## Key claim
Nonlinear effects in rotational bands, specifically centrifugal and Coriolis forces, should lead to bifurcation that qualitatively changes the spectrum for one nuclear excitation branch, and this bifurcation can serve as a precursor signature of the order-to-chaos transition in atomic nuclei. The authors claim only a "rough agreement" between calculated critical angular momentum values and those extracted from experimental data, using a crude model that does not aim to describe any particular nuclear spectrum in detail.

## Method
The authors applied the particle-rotor model with a Hamiltonian consisting of the rotational energy of the core and a quadrupole deformation term, using the Pavlichenkov criterion to identify bifurcation points in rotational bands. They solved BCS equations with a pure pairing force and Nilsson parametrization to estimate occupation probabilities, Fermi energy, and pairing gap, then calculated the critical angular momentum I_c from the relation I_c = j(1-p + sqrt(p^2 - 2p)) where p = QJ. Experimental energy levels for odd isotopes of Er, Hf, Yb, Os, Dy, and W were analyzed by plotting the quantity eta(I) = [E(I) - E(j) - A(I-j)^2] / [(2I+1)A] as a function of angular momentum I, with parameter A determined by linear regression from favoured band data.

## Key numbers
- Critical angular momentum I_c is defined by the formula I_c = j(1-p + sqrt(p^2 - 2p)), where p = QJ, with Q being the quadrupole deformation parameter and J the moment of inertia.
- The quadrupole splitting parameter Q is given by Q = [(N+3/2) * hbar * omega_0 * delta] / [2j(j+1)] * (u^2_j - v^2_j), where delta is the deformation parameter and j the single-particle angular momentum.
- The parameter A = 1/(2J) was determined by linear regression from experimental data of favoured bands.
- The paper reports a "rough agreement" between calculated I_c values (Th) and experimental I_c values (Exp) across the studied isotopes, but no specific numerical values for I_c are given in the truncated text.

## Context
This work extends the bifurcation criterion proposed by Pavlichenkov (1989, 1993) for rotational spectra, which was previously applied to Yb odd-N nuclei in Pavlichenkov (1988, 1989). The authors demonstrate that the same bifurcation behavior occurs in additional isotope chains including Er, Hf, Os, Dy, and W, supporting the idea that such bifurcations may be universal precursors of the order-to-chaos transition in nuclear systems as discussed by Bohigas and Weidenmuller (1988) and Aberg (1992). The analysis enables future studies to use bifurcation patterns as a model-independent signature of the transition from regular to chaotic nuclear motion, though the authors note that a more refined model would be needed for detailed spectroscopic description of individual nuclei.
