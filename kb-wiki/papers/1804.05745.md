# Further studies of multiplicity derivative in models of heavy ion collision at intermediate energies as a probe for phase transition

> In conjunction with models, the experimental observable total multiplicity can be used to check if the data contain the signature of phase transition and if it is first order. Two of the models reach similar conclusions. The third one is quite different.

# Further studies of multiplicity derivative in models of heavy ion collision at intermediate energies as a probe for phase transition

## Key claim

The appearance of a maximum in the derivative of total multiplicity with respect to energy (or temperature) is a signature of first order phase transition in heavy ion collisions, as demonstrated in the canonical thermodynamic model (CTM) and the lattice gas model; however, the percolation model, which exhibits no first order phase transition, shows no such maximum in the multiplicity derivative, confirming that the observable can distinguish between first order and continuous phase transitions.

## Method

The authors computed the total multiplicity M and its derivative with respect to the control parameter (bond breaking probability p_b for percolation, temperature T for CTM and lattice gas) for three models: a bond percolation model on a 6^3 lattice of nucleons, a canonical thermodynamic model (CTM) with liquid drop energies and Coulomb interactions, and a lattice gas model with nearest-neighbor interactions (v_np = -5.33 MeV, v_nn = v_pp = 0) on a cubic lattice with freeze-out volume larger than normal nuclear volume. For the lattice gas model, 50,000 Monte-Carlo events were generated at each temperature using the Metropolis algorithm, with cluster identification based on relative momenta and binding energies. The second moment m_2 was also computed for percolation and lattice gas models as a function of reduced multiplicity n = M/A.

## Key numbers

- Bond percolation model used a system of 6^3 = 216 nucleons; M ranges from 1 (p_b = 0) to 216 (p_b = 1).
- Lattice gas model used fragmenting system with Z = 82 and N = 126 (A = 208), with D^3 cubic boxes where D = 7 or 8, and each box has volume (1.0/0.16) fm^3.
- Lattice gas model interaction strengths: v_np = -5.33 MeV, v_nn = v_pp = 0.0 MeV.
- In the lattice gas model, the maximum of dM/dT occurs at the same temperature as the maximum of C_v (heat capacity), with C_v normalized by a factor of 1/10 for plotting on the same scale; dM/dT is given in units of MeV^{-1}.
- In the percolation model, the second moment function m_2(p_b) has a maximum at approximately p_b = 0.8 (equivalently p_s = 0.2).
- For the lattice gas model at D = 7 and D = 8, m_2 curves are plotted as a function of reduced multiplicity n for the system with Z = 82 and N = 126.

## Context

This work extends the previous study by Mallik et al. (2016) which used only the canonical thermodynamic model (CTM) to propose that the maximum in dM/dE signals first order phase transition. The present paper tests this claim against two additional models: the percolation model (Campi, Bauer), which represents a continuous phase transition, and the lattice gas model (Pan, Samaddar, Chomaz), which can exhibit first order transition. The results confirm that the multiplicity derivative distinguishes between first order (CTM and lattice gas show a maximum) and continuous (percolation shows no maximum) phase transitions, while the traditional second moment m_2 curves are similar across models and thus ambiguous. This enables experimental searches for the phase transition signal using the experimentally accessible observable dM/dE from central collision data at varying beam energies.
