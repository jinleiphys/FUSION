# Center symmetry and area laws

> SU($N_c$) gauge theories containing matter fields may be invariant under transformations of some subgroup of the $\mathbb{Z}_{N_c}$ center; the maximum such subgroup is $\mathbb{Z}_{p}$, with $p$ depending on $N_c$ and the representations of the various matter fields in the theory. Confining SU($N_c$) gauge theories in either 3+1 or 2+1 space-time dimensions and with matter fields in any representation have string tensions for representation $R$ given by $σ_R =σ_f \, \, \frac{p_R (p-p_R) \, \, g\left (p_R (p-p_R) \right )}{(p-1) \, \, g(p -1 )} $ with $p_R={n_R \, \rm mod}(p)$, where $σ_f $ is the string tension for the fundamental representation, $g$ is a positive finite function and $n_R$ is the n-ality of $R$. This implies that a necessary condition for a theory in this class to have an area law is invariance of the theory under a nontrivial subgroup of the center. Significantly, these results depend on $p$ regardless of the value of $N_c$.

# Center symmetry and area laws

## Key claim

For confining SU($N_c$) gauge theories in 3+1 or 2+1 dimensions with matter fields in any representation, the string tension for representation $R$ depends only on the maximum subgroup $\mathbb{Z}_p$ of the center under which the theory is invariant, rather than on $N_c$ itself; a necessary condition for any such theory to have an area law for the fundamental representation is invariance under a nontrivial subgroup of the center.

## Method

The paper analyzes the connection between center symmetry and area laws by studying the n-ality of matter fields and gluons in SU($N_c$) gauge theories. It proves two properties: (i) the maximum subgroup of the center under which the theory is invariant is $\mathbb{Z}_p$ with $p = \mathrm{gcd}(N_c, n_1, n_2, \ldots, n_m)$, where $n_i$ are the n-alities of the fields; (ii) fields in the theory can combine into a representation $R$ if and only if $n_R$ is an integer multiple of $p$. These properties are then used to derive the general form of the string tension and its dependence on $p$-ality rather than $N_c$.

## Key numbers

- The maximum center subgroup preserved by a theory is $\mathbb{Z}_p$ with $p = \mathrm{gcd}(N_c, n_1, n_2, \ldots, n_m)$, where $n_i$ are the n-alities of all fields in the theory.
- For theories with maximum center subgroup $\mathbb{Z}_2$ or $\mathbb{Z}_3$, all representations with nonzero string tension have the same string tension as the fundamental representation (ratio fixed to unity).
- For a theory with trivial center subgroup ($p=1$), the string tension vanishes for all representations, implying no area law for any Wilson loop.
- The string tension for representation $R$ is given by $\sigma_R = \sigma_f \, \frac{p_R(p-p_R) \, g(p_R(p-p_R))}{(p-1) \, g(p-1)}$, where $p_R = n_R \mod p$, $\sigma_f$ is the fundamental string tension, and $g$ is a positive finite function.

## Context

This work extends the classic connection between center symmetry and confinement established by 't Hooft (1979) through center vortices, and the k-string picture from Greensite (2003) and others (Kogut-Susskind, 1975; various lattice studies). It formally proves that the folklore linking area laws to center symmetry holds generally for confining SU($N_c$) gauge theories with matter, not just pure Yang-Mills. The result enables future lattice studies to test whether the function $g(x)$ is universal for theories with $p>3$, which would imply a sine law scaling as seen in supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory.
