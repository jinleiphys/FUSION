# Microscopic optical potential for exotic isotopes from chiral effective field theory

> We compute the isospin-asymmetry dependence of microscopic optical model potentials from realistic chiral two- and three-body interactions over a range of resolution scales $Λ\simeq 400-500$\,MeV. We show that at moderate projectile energies, $E_{\rm inv} = 110 - 200$\,MeV, the real isovector part of the optical potential changes sign, a phenomenon referred to as isospin inversion. We also extract the strength and energy dependence of the imaginary isovector optical potential and find no evidence for an analogous phenomenon over the range of energies, $E \leq 200$\,MeV, considered in the present work. Finally, we compute for the first time the leading corrections to the Lane parametrization for the isospin-asymmetry dependence of the optical potential and observe an enhanced importance at low scattering energies.

## Key claim

The real isovector part of the microscopic optical potential changes sign at moderate projectile energies (isospin inversion), predicted to occur in the range \(E_{\rm inv} = 155 \pm 45\) MeV, while the imaginary isovector part shows no evidence for a similar sign change up to 200 MeV. The leading corrections to the Lane parametrization (terms quadratic in isospin asymmetry) become important at low scattering energies but are consistent with zero above 100 MeV.

## Method

The authors computed the nucleon self-energy in asymmetric nuclear matter at second order in many-body perturbation theory, using chiral two- and three-nucleon forces with momentum-space cutoffs \(\Lambda \simeq 400-500\) MeV. The real and imaginary volume components of the optical potential were extracted for arbitrary isospin asymmetry \(\delta_{np} = (N-Z)/A\) at nuclear matter saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), with single-particle energies computed self-consistently. The linear and quadratic terms in \(\delta_{np}\) were obtained numerically from the exact isospin-asymmetry dependence of the self-energy.

## Key numbers

- Isospin inversion energy for the real isovector optical potential: \(E_{\rm inv} = 155 \pm 45\) MeV; the two low-momentum interactions alone give \(E_{\rm inv} = 120 \pm 10\) MeV.
- At threshold, the isovector real optical potential strength is \(U_I^{450}(0) = 32 \pm 6\) MeV for cutoff 450 MeV and \(U_I^{500}(0) = 31 \pm 6\) MeV for cutoff 500 MeV.
- At the isospin inversion energy, the uncertainty is \(U_I^{450}(E_{inv}) = 0 \pm 5\) MeV and \(U_I^{500}(E_{inv}) = 0 \pm 4\) MeV.
- The subleading quadratic term \(U_{II}\) contributes roughly \((15-20\) MeV\()\delta_{np}^2\) at low energies; above 100 MeV it is consistent with zero.
- The isovector imaginary optical potential strength is predicted to be \(W_I \simeq 8-12\) MeV.
- The empirical band for the real isovector potential is given by \(U_I = (28 \pm 6)\) MeV \(-\) \((0.15 \pm 0.05)E\).

## Context

This work extends microscopic optical potential calculations from chiral effective field theory (Holt et al., 2013; Toyokawa et al., 2015) to include isospin-asymmetry dependence, addressing the need for predictive reaction models for exotic neutron-rich isotopes at radioactive ion beam facilities. The results contradict earlier semi-microscopic mean-field models (such as RMF, Gogny, SKM*, and SkLya) and some microscopic calculations (Brueckner-Hartree-Fock and Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock) that disagreed on the energy dependence of the isovector components. The predicted isospin inversion energy and the strength of the imaginary isovector potential can be tested at next-generation radioactive beam facilities, and the finding that the Lane parametrization works well above 100 MeV supports extrapolations of phenomenological optical potentials (Becchetti and Greenlees, 1969; Varner et al., 1991; Koning and Delaroche, 2003) into neutron-rich regions.
