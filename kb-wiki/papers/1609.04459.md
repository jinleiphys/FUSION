# Anisotropic flow measurements in Pb--Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV with ALICE

> Anisotropic flow is a sensitive probe of the initial conditions and the transport properties of the Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) produced in heavy-ion collisions. In these proceedings, we present the first results of elliptic ($v_2$), triangular ($v_3$) and quadrangular flow ($v_4$) of charged particles in Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=$ 5.02 TeV with the ALICE detector. In addition, the comparison of experimental measurements to various theoretical calculations will be discussed. This provides a unique opportunity to test the validity of the hydrodynamic picture and discriminates between various possibilities for the temperature dependence of shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of the produced QGP.

## Key claim

The first measurements of elliptic ($v_2$), triangular ($v_3$), and quadrangular flow ($v_4$) of charged particles in Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=$ 5.02 TeV show a moderate increase compared to 2.76 TeV, consistent with hydrodynamic predictions that support a low value of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $\eta/s$ that does not change significantly between the two energies.

## Method

Anisotropic flow coefficients were measured using charged particles reconstructed with the ALICE Inner Tracking System and Time Projection Chamber (full azimuthal coverage, $|\eta|<0.8$) from approximately 140k minimum-bias Pb--Pb events recorded in November 2015. Both two-particle correlations with a pseudorapidity gap ($|\Delta\eta|>1$) and multi-particle cumulant methods ($v_n\{4\}$, $v_n\{6\}$, $v_n\{8\}$) were employed to suppress non-flow effects and to probe flow fluctuations. The results were compared to hydrodynamic calculations (Noronha-Hostler 2015, Niemi 2015) and to the AMPT model with string melting.

## Key numbers

- Maximum $v_2\{2, |\Delta\eta|>1\}$ of 0.104 $\pm$ 0.001 (stat.) $\pm$ 0.002 (syst.) in the 40--50% centrality class for $p_{\rm T}$-integrated flow (0.2 $< p_{\rm T} <$ 5.0 GeV/$c$).
- Increase from 2.76 TeV to 5.02 TeV over 0--50% centrality: (3.0$\pm$0.6)% for $v_2$, (4.3$\pm$1.4)% for $v_3$, and (10.2$\pm$3.8)% for $v_4$.
- Fully $p_{\rm T}$-integrated $v_2$ in 20--30% centrality increases by (4.9$\pm$1.9)% from $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=2.76$ TeV to 5.02 TeV.
- Multi-particle cumulant results $v_2\{4\}$, $v_2\{6\}$, $v_2\{8\}$ agree within 1% for the same centrality.

## Context

This work extends previous anisotropic flow measurements at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=2.76$ TeV by ALICE (ALICE 2016, Adam 2016) to the higher LHC energy of 5.02 TeV, providing the first such results at this energy. The observed moderate increase in flow coefficients and the compatibility with hydrodynamic calculations (Noronha-Hostler 2015, Niemi 2015) support a low $\eta/s$ for the QGP that does not change significantly with collision energy. The $p_{\rm T}$-differential results, which are roughly compatible with AMPT calculations, will enable future comparisons with hydrodynamic calculations to further constrain initial conditions, radial flow, and freeze-out conditions.
