# Measurement and analysis of the pp -> pp gamma reaction at 310 MeV

> The pp -> pp gamma reaction has been studied at a beam energy of 310 MeV by detecting both final protons at the PROMICE-WASA facility and identifying the photon through the resulting missing-mass peak. The photon angular distribution in the center-of-mass system and those of the proton-proton relative momentum with respect to the beam direction and to that of the recoil photon were determined reliably up to a final pp excitation energy of Epp ~ 30 MeV. Except for very small Epp values, the behavior of these distributions with excitation energy is well reproduced by a new refined model of the hard bremsstrahlung process. The model reproduces absolutely the total cross section and its energy dependence to within the experimental and theoretical uncertainties.

## Key claim

The paper reports that a new refined model of hard bremsstrahlung (the HN model) reproduces the measured photon angular distributions, proton-proton relative momentum distributions, and total cross section for the pp → ppγ reaction at 310 MeV to within experimental and theoretical uncertainties, except for very small final pp excitation energies (Epp) where significant cancellations between amplitudes occur.

## Method

The experiment was conducted at the PROMICE-WASA facility at Uppsala, using a stored 310 MeV proton beam from the CELSIUS ring incident on an internal hydrogen gas-jet target. Both final-state protons were detected in the forward detector system, and the photon was identified via the missing-mass peak in the pp → ppX reaction, yielding 58,026 kinematically well-defined events after cuts. The data were compared to a quantum field-theory-based model (HN) that couples the photon to a relativistic pp T-matrix derived from the Bonn potential, ensuring gauge invariance through the generalized Ward-Takahashi identity, with and without minimal inclusion of the Δ(1232) isobar contributions.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: Tp = 309.7 ± 0.3 MeV, determined from the maximum polar angle of protons from π0 production
- Total integrated luminosity: 340 nb⁻¹, derived from comparison with elastic pp scattering
- Total events: 66,521 ppγ candidates and 861,449 ppπ⁰ candidates in the missing-mass distribution before cuts
- Final event sample: 58,026 ppγ events after kinematic fitting and timing cuts
- Missing-mass peak width: σ(MX²) = 0.056 Mπ⁰², with events retained for |MX²/Mπ⁰²| < 0.137
- Systematic uncertainty: approximately 15% overall
- Data coverage: Epp up to approximately 30 MeV, with proton polar angles between about 3° and 22°
- Minimum proton energy for acceptance: 38 MeV (requiring penetration into second layer of forward trigger hodoscope)
- Acceptance: up to 60% at low Epp, decreasing at higher Epp and small θγ

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of pp → ppγ at Epp < 3 MeV (Johansson 2009) to Epp up to approximately 30 MeV, providing the most complete measurement of proton-proton bremsstrahlung at an energy far above threshold. The results are compared to the HN model (Haberzettl and Nakayama 2010), which successfully described earlier KVI data at 190 MeV (KVI02) and TRIUMF data at 280 MeV (Michaelian 1990), solving a long-standing discrepancy between experiment and earlier theory. The data enable testing of the role of Δ isobar contributions in the hard bremsstrahlung process and provide constraints for future coupled-channels approaches incorporating NN ⇌ ΔN dynamics.
