# Born-Oppenheimer Renormalization group for High Energy Scattering: CSS, DGLAP and all that

> In \cite{one}, we have introduced the Born-Oppenheimer (BO) renormalization group approach to high energy hadronic collisions and derived the BO approximation for the light cone wave function of a fast moving projectile hadron. In this second paper, we utilize this wave function to derive the BO evolution of partonic distributions in the hadron -- the gluon transverse momentum and integrated parton distributions (TMD and PDF respectively). The evolution equation for the TMD contains a linear and a nonlinear term. The linear term reproduces the Collins-Soper-Sterman (CSS) equation with a physical relation between the transverse and longitudinal resolution scales. We explain how this equivalence arises, even though the BO and CSS cascades are somewhat different in structures. The nonlinear term in the evolution has a very appealing physical meaning: it is a correction due to stimulated emission, which enhances emission of gluons (bosons) into states with a nonzero occupation. For the evolution of the PDF we again find a linear and nonlinear term. At not very small Bjorken $x$, the linear term recovers the DGLAP equation in the leading logarithmic approximation. At small $x$ however there are contributions from gluon splittings which are in the BFKL kinematics leading to a modification of the DGLAP equation. The nonlinear terms have the same physical origin as in the equation for the TMD -- the stimulated emission corrections. Interestingly the nonlinear corrections are the most important for the virtual terms, so that the net correction to the DGLAP is negative and mimics shadowing, although the physical origin of the nonlinearity is very different.

## Key claim

The Born-Oppenheimer (BO) renormalization group evolution of gluon TMDs and PDFs yields linear terms that reproduce the Collins-Soper-Sterman (CSS) and DGLAP equations respectively, but only under the condition that the transverse and longitudinal resolution scales are related by a specific physical relation; the nonlinear terms, which arise from stimulated emission of gluons (a Bose enhancement effect), produce corrections that mimic shadowing in the PDF evolution despite having a very different physical origin from standard GLR-MQ shadowing corrections.

## Method

The authors use the Born-Oppenheimer (BO) approximation for the light cone wave function of a fast-moving projectile hadron, previously derived in their companion paper, to compute the evolution of the gluon transverse momentum dependent distribution (TMD) and the integrated gluon parton distribution (PDF) as the frequency cutoff \(E\) (the resolution scale) is increased. The evolution is obtained by expanding the path-ordered exponential that generates the wave function to second order in the coupling, integrating over the newly opened phase space of "fast" gluon modes with frequencies between \(E\) and \(Ee^\Delta\), and extracting the change in the expectation value of the gluon number operator. The resulting equations contain both linear terms, which are compared to standard CSS and DGLAP equations, and nonlinear terms arising from the Bose statistics of gluons.

## Key numbers

* The linear term in the TMD evolution reproduces the CSS equation when the transverse and longitudinal resolution scales satisfy \(\ln \frac{\mu^2}{\mathbf{k}^2} = 2 \ln \frac{k^+}{\xi}\) in the doubly logarithmic approximation (with a slight modification for single logarithms).
* The nonlinear stimulated emission correction to the TMD evolution is \(1/N_c\) suppressed and further suppressed by a factor \(1/(Q^2 S_\perp)\) at high transverse resolution scale \(Q^2\), where \(S_\perp\) is the transverse area of the projectile.
* The linear term in the PDF evolution recovers the DGLAP equation in the leading logarithmic approximation for not very small Bjorken \(x\), with additional BFKL-like contributions at very small \(x\).
* The nonlinear correction to the DGLAP evolution is negative (mimicking shadowing) and is of the same order in \(\alpha_s\) as the linear term, unlike the GLR term which is suppressed by an additional power of \(\alpha_s\) but enhanced by \(\ln 1/x\).

## Context

This work extends the Born-Oppenheimer renormalization group approach introduced by the same authors in their companion paper (Duan, Kovner, Lublinsky, 2024) to the evolution of partonic distributions, providing a unified framework that connects to the established CSS equation (Collins, Soper, Sterman, 1985) and DGLAP equation (Dokshitzer, Gribov, Lipatov, Altarelli, Parisi, 1977). The nonlinear stimulated emission corrections are distinct from the GLR shadowing corrections (Gribov, Levin, Ryskin, 1983; Mueller, Qiu, 1986) in both physical origin and parametric dependence, and the TMD evolution differs from earlier CGC-based TMD discussions (Marquet, 2016; Altinoluk, 2018) which had the longitudinal resolution scale tied to the gluon's own longitudinal momentum. The results enable future derivation of the BFKL-type evolution of total cross sections within the BO framework, to be presented in a third paper of this series.
