# Self-consistent Skyrme QRPA for use in axially-symmetric nuclei of arbitrary mass

> We describe a new implementation of the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) in axially-symmetric deformed nuclei with Skyrme and volume-pairing energy-density functionals. After using a variety of tests to demonstrate the accuracy of the code in ^{24,26}Mg and ^{16}O, we report the first fully self-consistent application of the Skyrme QRPA to a heavy deformed nucleus, calculating strength distributions for several K^pi in ^{172}Yb. We present energy-weighted sums, properties of gamma-vibrational and low-energy K^pi=0^+ states, and the complete isovector E1 strength function. The QRPA calculation reproduces the properties of the low-lying 2^+ states as well or better than it typically does in spherical nuclei.

# Self-consistent Skyrme QRPA for use in axially-symmetric nuclei of arbitrary mass

## Key claim
The authors present the first fully self-consistent Skyrme QRPA calculation for a heavy deformed nucleus (¹⁷²Yb), but the results are preliminary and the code shows limitations in handling spurious states in heavy nuclei under current space-size restrictions, particularly in the isoscalar K^π = 1^+ channel.

## Method
The authors implement a fully self-consistent quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) in axially-symmetric deformed nuclei using Skyrme energy-density functionals with volume pairing. They construct the QRPA A and B matrices in the M-scheme using canonical-quasiparticle basis states from a cylindrical-box B-spline HFB code, diagonalize the matrices, and use the rigid-rotor approximation to calculate transition strengths. The code is parallelized and tested in light nuclei (¹⁶O, ²⁴Mg, ²⁶Mg) before application to ¹⁷²Yb.

## Key numbers
- In ²⁶Mg with SkP, spurious number-operator states appear at E = 0.008 MeV and 0.024 MeV, with negligible strength to all other K^π = 0^+ excitations
- In ²⁴Mg with SkP, the spurious rotational state in K^π = 1^+ appears at E = 0.045 MeV, with physical states having transition strengths four orders of magnitude smaller
- In ¹⁷²Yb with SkM*, the HFB calculation yields pairing gaps Δ_p = 1.248 MeV, Δ_n = 0.773 MeV, and quadrupole deformation β = 0.34
- Energy-weighted sums for ¹⁷²Yb agree with analytical sum rules to within about 2% except in the IS K^π = 1^+ channel; for example, IV E1 K^π = 1^- QRPA sum = 289.819 MeV fm² vs. analytical 285.764 MeV fm²
- The γ-vibrational K^π = 2^+ state in ¹⁷²Yb is calculated at E = 2.261 MeV (experimental: 1.466 MeV) with B(E2) = 0.041 e²b² (experimental: 0.0433 e²b²)
- The low-energy K^π = 0^+ state in ¹⁷²Yb is calculated at E = 1.390 MeV (experimental: 1.117 MeV) with B(E2) = 0.00495 e²b² (experimental: 0.0081 e²b²)

## Context
This work extends previous self-consistent Skyrme QRPA calculations, which had been limited to nuclei with A ≲ 40, to heavy deformed nuclei. It builds on spherical QRPA codes (Terasaki 2005, 2006) and deformed RPA approaches without pairing (Inakura 2009), as well as separable approximations to Skyrme-QRPA (Nesterenko 2006, Severyukhin 2008). The code enables systematic investigation of β and γ vibrations in rare-earth nuclei using modern density functionals, though the authors note that experimental pygmy resonances in the IV E1 strength may involve configurations beyond QRPA.
