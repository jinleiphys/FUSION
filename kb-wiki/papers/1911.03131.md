# Transport coefficients of hot and dense matter

> We present calculations for the shear viscosity of the hot and dense quark-gluon plasma (QGP) using the partonic scattering cross sections as a function of temperature $T$ and baryon chemical potential $μ_B$ from the dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) that is matched to reproduce the equation of state of the partonic system above the deconfinement temperature $T_c$ from lattice QCD. To this aim we calculate the collisional widths for the partonic degrees of freedom at finite $T$ and $μ_B$ in the time-like sector and conclude that the quasiparticle limit holds sufficiently well. Furthermore, the ratio of shear viscosity $η$ over entropy density $s$, i.e. $η/s$, is evaluated using these collisional widths and are compared to lQCD calculations for $μ_B$ = 0 as well. We find that the ratio $η/s$ is in agreement with the results of calculations within the original DQPM on the basis of the Kubo formalism. Furthermore, there is only a very modest change of $η/s$ with the baryon chemical $μ_B$ as a function of the scaled temperature $T/T_c(μ_B)$.

## Key claim

The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density (η/s) for the quark-gluon plasma shows only a very modest change with baryon chemical potential μB when considered as a function of the scaled temperature T/Tc(μB), and the quasiparticle limit (width much smaller than mass) holds sufficiently well in the dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) for the temperatures and chemical potentials studied.

## Method

The authors calculated the shear viscosity η of the quark-gluon plasma using the DQPM, which describes partonic propagators with complex self-energies. They computed the collisional widths (interaction rates) for partonic degrees of freedom at finite temperature T and baryon chemical potential μB using microscopic differential cross sections for parton scattering in the on-shell limit. The shear viscosity was then evaluated via the Kubo formalism and the relaxation-time approximation (RTA), and the ratio η/s was compared to lattice QCD (lQCD) results at μB = 0.

## Key numbers

- The ratio η/s is evaluated for μB in the range 0 to 0.45 GeV (450 MeV), where the transition between hadronic and quark-gluon plasma phases is assumed to be a smooth cross-over.
- The results for η/s at μB = 0 are compared to lQCD data for pure SU(3) gauge theory from Astrakhantsev (2017).
- The Kovtun-Son-Starinets (KSS) bound η/s = 1/(4π) is shown as a reference line.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of shear viscosity within the DQPM using the Kubo formalism (Soloveva et al., 2019) to include a microscopic calculation of interaction rates from partonic cross sections. It also extends lattice QCD results, which are only available at μB = 0, to finite baryon chemical potential. The results enable more realistic hydrodynamical simulations of heavy-ion collisions at moderate μB, such as those at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and future experiments at FAIR.
