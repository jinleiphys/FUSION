# Origin and properties of strong inter-nucleon interactions

> I start with a historical review of the attempts to construct theories for the origin of nuclear forces, for which I also summaries the most important properties. The review then shifts to its main focus, which is the chiral effective field theory approach to nuclear forces. I summarize the current status of this approach and discuss the most important open issues: the proper renormalization of the chiral two-nucleon potential and sub-leading three-nucleon forces.

## Key claim

The paper argues that chiral effective field theory (ChET) provides a systematic, order-by-order framework for nuclear forces that is firmly rooted in low-energy QCD, but two major open issues remain: the proper renormalization of the chiral two-nucleon potential and the need for sub-leading three-nucleon forces to resolve persistent discrepancies in few-nucleon observables.

## Method

The author reviews the historical development of nuclear force theories from Yukawa's meson exchange through one-boson-exchange models to modern chiral effective field theory. The chiral EFT approach constructs the most general Lagrangian consistent with the symmetries of low-energy QCD, particularly spontaneously broken chiral symmetry, and organizes contributions by a power counting expansion in (Q/Λχ) where Q ~ mπ and Λχ ~ 1 GeV. The nuclear force hierarchy emerges order by order: leading order (LO) gives one-pion exchange plus two contact terms, next-to-leading order (NLO) adds two-pion exchange and seven contact terms, next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) provides realistic intermediate-range attraction and the first nonvanishing three-nucleon forces, and N3LO includes two-loop two-pion exchange, three-pion exchange, and 15 new contact terms enabling quantitative description up to about 300 MeV laboratory energy.

## Key numbers

- The chiral symmetry breaking scale Λχ is approximately 1 GeV, with the soft scale Q ~ mπ ~ 140 MeV
- At N3LO, the χ²/datum for reproducing the 1999 np database below 290 MeV is 1.10 (2402 data points), compared to 20 at NNLO and 86 at NLO
- The χ²/datum for N3LO in the 0-100 MeV bin is 1.05 (1058 data), 1.08 in 100-190 MeV (501 data), and 1.15 in 190-290 MeV (843 data)
- The leading four-nucleon force at N3LO contributes approximately 0.1 MeV to the ⁴He binding energy (total 28.3 MeV)
- The wound integral in nuclear matter for a renormalized LO interaction with Λ → ∞ is about 40%, compared to 5-10% for conventional nuclear forces
- Cutoff plateaus of constant low χ² at NNLO are observed for cutoff parameters ranging from about 450 to 850 MeV

## Context

This review extends the meson-exchange models of the 1950s-1980s (Paris potential by Lacombe et al. 1980, Bonn potential by Machleidt et al. 1987) and the phenomenological JISP-16 potentials based on inverse scattering (Shirokov 2009) by providing a QCD-based effective field theory framework. The chiral EFT approach, pioneered by Weinberg (1979, 1991, 1992), has been applied successfully in few-nucleon reactions (Kalantar-Nayestanaki et al. 2012), structure of light and medium-mass nuclei (Navratil et al. 2007, Roth et al. 2012, Hergert et al. 2013), and nuclear matter (Hebeler et al. 2011, Coraggio et al. 2012). The open issues of proper renormalization (following the critique by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise 1996) and subleading three-nucleon forces are needed to resolve the Ay puzzle in nucleon-deuteron scattering and improve spectra of light nuclei.
