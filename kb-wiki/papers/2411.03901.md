# Hyperon Production in Bi+Bi Collisions at NICA and Angular Dependence of Hyperon Spin Polarization

> The strange baryon production in Bi+Bi collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=9.0$\,GeV is studied using the PHSD transport model. Hyperon and anti-hyperon yields, transverse momentum spectra, and rapidity spectra are calculated, their centrality dependence and the effect of rapidity and transverse momentum cuts are studied. The rapidity distributions for $\overline{Lambda}$, $Ξ$, $\overline{Xi}$ baryons are found to be systematically narrower than for $Λ$s. The $p_T$ slope parameters for anti-hyperons vary more with centrality than those for hyperons. Restricting the accepted rapidity range to $|y|<1$ increases the slope parameters by 13--30\,MeV depending on the centrality class and the hyperon mass. Hydrodynamic velocity and vorticity fields are calculated and the formation of two oppositely rotating vortex rings moving in opposite directions along the collision axis is found. The hyperon spin polarization induced by the medium vorticity within the thermodynamic approach is calculated and the dependence of the polarization on the transverse momentum and rapidity cuts and on the centrality selection is analyzed. The cuts have stronger effect on the polarization of $Λ$ and $Ξ$ hyperons than on the corresponding anti-hyperons. The polarization signal is maximal for the centrality class 60-70\%. We show that for the considered hyperon polarization mechanism the structure of the vorticity field makes an imprint on the polarization signal as a function of the azimuthal angle in the transverse momentum plane, $ϕ_H$, $\cosϕ_H=p_x/p_T$. For particles with positive longitudinal momentum, $p_z>0$, the polarization increases with $\cosϕ_H$, while for particles with $p_z<0$ it decreases.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in Bi+Bi collisions at NICA energies, the vortical mechanism of hyperon spin polarization produces a characteristic dependence of the polarization on the azimuthal angle in the transverse momentum plane, with opposite trends for particles with positive versus negative longitudinal momentum, but this result is conditional on the specific thermodynamic approach used and the particular structure of the vorticity field generated by the PHSD transport model.

## Method

The authors used the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport model to simulate Bi+Bi collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=9.0 GeV, generating about 2 million events. They calculated hydrodynamic velocity and vorticity fields from the microscopic transport by averaging the energy-momentum tensor over many events, then applied the thermodynamic approach of Becattini and collaborators to compute hyperon spin polarization from the local thermal vorticity. The centrality classes were defined using the charged particle multiplicity distribution in the pseudorapidity range |eta|<0.5, following a procedure similar to experimental methods.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN)=9.0 GeV for Bi+Bi collisions
- About 2x10^6 events generated with the PHSD model
- For the most central collisions (0-5% centrality class), yield ratios: M_Lambda : M_Xi : M_Omega = 1 : (9.10 +/- 0.02)x10^{-2} : (3.71 +/- 0.04)x10^{-3}; M_anti-Lambda : M_anti-Xi : M_anti-Omega = 1 : (2.09 +/- 0.02)x10^{-1} : (2.16 +/- 0.06)x10^{-2}
- Restricting the accepted rapidity range to |y|<1 increases the slope parameters by 13-30 MeV depending on the centrality class and hyperon mass
- The polarization signal is maximal for the centrality class 60-70%
- Slope parameters for Lambda and Xi hyperons decrease monotonously by about 15 MeV and 8 MeV respectively from most central to 40-50% centrality; anti-hyperons show variations of about 30 MeV with centrality
- For the rapidity distributions of Lambda hyperons in centrality class 0-5%: Delta y_Lambda = 0.72(1), sigma_Lambda = 0.74(2); for anti-Lambda: Delta y_anti-Lambda = 0.41(1), sigma_anti-Lambda = 0.49(1)

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the PHSD model for Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies (Tsegelnik 2023, Voronyuk 2023) to the Bi+Bi system at NICA energies, and demonstrates that the vortical mechanism of hyperon polarization produces a specific angular dependence that could be measured by the MPD detector at NICA. The results are compared with NA49 experimental data on Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=8.77 GeV (Alt 2008, Alt 2005), showing good agreement for hyperon yield ratios. The paper enables experimental searches for the predicted azimuthal angle dependence of hyperon polarization at NICA, which would test the thermodynamic vorticity mechanism against alternative polarization mechanisms proposed in the literature.
