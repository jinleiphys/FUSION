# QCD sum rules for D and B mesons in nuclear matter

> QCD sum rules for D and B mesons embedded in cold nuclear matter are evaluated. We quantify the mass splitting of D - D-bar and B - B-bar mesons as a function of the nuclear matter density; extrapolated to saturation density it is in the order of 60 and 130 MeV driven essentially by the condensates <q-adjoint q>, <q-adjoint g sigma G q> and <q-bar q>. The genuine chiral condensate <q-bar q>, amplified by heavy-quark masses, enters the Borel transformed sum rules for the mass splitting beyond linear density dependence. Including strange quark condensates reveals a umerically smaller and opposite effect for the Ds - Ds-bar mass splitting.

# QCD sum rules for D and B mesons in nuclear matter

## Key claim

The mass splitting between D and anti-D mesons (and B and anti-B mesons) in cold nuclear matter is predicted to be approximately 60 MeV and 130 MeV respectively at saturation density, but these values depend sensitively on the sign and magnitude of the odd mixed quark-gluon condensate ⟨q† g σ G q⟩, and the chiral condensate ⟨q̄ q⟩ enters the mass splitting only beyond linear order in density, amplified by the heavy-quark mass.

## Method

The authors evaluate QCD sum rules for the two-point correlation function of pseudoscalar heavy-light currents (j = i d̄ γ5 c for D⁺, and analogous currents for B mesons) in the rest frame of cold nuclear matter, using the operator product expansion up to mass dimension 5. They employ a pole-plus-continuum ansatz for the spectral function, separate the correlation function into even and odd parts under q₀ → −q₀, and solve the Borel-transformed sum rules for the mass splitting Δm and centroid mass m as functions of nuclear matter density, using tabulated vacuum and density-dependent condensate values.

## Key numbers

- Mass splitting 2Δm ≈ −60 MeV for D – anti-D mesons at nuclear saturation density n = 0.15 fm⁻³
- Mass splitting 2Δm ≈ −130 MeV for B – anti-B mesons at n = 0.15 fm⁻³
- Vacuum D meson mass reproduced as m ≈ 1.863 GeV using threshold s₀² = 6.0 GeV²
- Vacuum B meson mass reproduced as m ≈ 5.33 GeV using threshold s₀² = 40 GeV²
- For Dₛ – anti-Dₛ mesons at n = 0.15 fm⁻³ and strangeness content y = 0.5, mass splitting 2Δm ≈ +25 MeV (opposite sign to the non-strange case)
- Charmed quark pole mass m_c = 1.5 GeV; bottom quark pole mass m_b = 4.7 GeV
- Borel mass at the minimum for D mesons: M ≈ 0.95 GeV; for B mesons: M ≈ 1.67 GeV in vacuum, shifting to M ≈ 1.71 GeV at saturation density

## Context

This work extends QCD sum rule calculations for open-charm mesons in nuclear matter beyond earlier treatments such as Hayashigaki (which considered only the even part of the OPE up to mass dimension 4) and resolves discrepancies in Wilson coefficients for medium-specific condensates found in the literature (Aliev, Morath). The results enable predictions for the in-medium behavior of D and B mesons relevant to experiments at FAIR (CBM and PANDA collaborations), and complement hadronic model calculations by Tolos, Angel, and Lutz_charm as well as quark-meson coupling model studies by Tsushima. The finding that the chiral condensate enters the mass splitting only at order n² (amplified by heavy-quark mass) clarifies the role of chiral symmetry breaking in heavy-light meson systems.
