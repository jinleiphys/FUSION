# A chiral quark-soliton model with broken scale invariance for nuclear matter

> We present a model for describing nuclear matter at finite density based on quarks interacting with chiral fields, σand πand with vector mesons introduced as massive gauge fields. The chiral Lagrangian includes a logarithmic potential, associated with the breaking of scale invariance. We provide results for the soliton in vacuum and at finite density, using the Wigner-Seitz approximation. We show that the model can reach higher densities respect to the linear-σmodel and that the introduction of vector mesons allows to obtain saturation. This result was never obtained before in similar approaches.

## Key claim

The authors claim that including a logarithmic potential (associated with broken scale invariance) and vector mesons in a chiral quark-soliton model allows the soliton crystal to reach higher densities than the linear sigma model and, for the first time in such approaches, to obtain saturation of nuclear matter. The hedge is that the dilaton field is kept frozen at its vacuum value, which is justified only at low temperatures even at large densities.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral Lagrangian coupling quarks to sigma, pion, and vector meson fields (omega, rho, and axial-vector a1), with a logarithmic potential replacing the Mexican hat potential to incorporate broken scale invariance. They solve the mean-field equations for a single hedgehog soliton in vacuum and then apply the Wigner-Seitz approximation to model nuclear matter at finite density, imposing periodic boundary conditions on spherical cells of radius R and solving the coupled field equations self-consistently as R is decreased.

## Key numbers

- For the model without vector mesons (m_sigma=550 MeV, g_pi=5): soliton mass M=1176 MeV at mean-field level; isoscalar charge radius squared = (0.76 fm)^2; isovector magnetic moment mu_{I=1}=3.83 mu_N; axial coupling g_A=1.27; average pion number in unprojected state = 2.4
- For the model with vector mesons (m_sigma=1200 MeV, g_pi=3.9, g_omega=12, g_rho=4): soliton mass M=1329.5 MeV; isoscalar charge radius squared = (0.78 fm)^2; isovector magnetic moment mu_{I=1}=4.49 mu_N; axial coupling g_A=1.34; average pion number = 2.62
- Bare meson masses used: m_pi=139 MeV, m_rho=m_A=776 MeV, m_omega=782 MeV; sigma field masses tested: m_sigma=550 MeV and m_sigma=1200 MeV
- Quark-pion coupling g_pi varied from 3.9 to 5; g_rho fixed at 4; g_omega varied between 10 and 13
- Vacuum value sigma_0 = f_pi = 93 MeV; delta = 4/33 from QCD beta function
- Wigner-Seitz calculations started from R=4 fm (indistinguishable from vacuum) down to the smallest radius where self-consistent solutions exist; solutions without vector mesons showed significant field modification below R=2 fm

## Context

This work extends the hadronic model of Bonanno (2008) to a microscopic quark level, where fermions are treated as quarks and nucleons emerge as solitonic solutions, following the approach of Birse (1986) and Kahana (1984). It addresses the failure of the linear sigma model at densities around rho_0, as discussed by Furnstahl and Serot (1993), where the normal broken-chiral-symmetry solution becomes unstable to the Lee-Wick phase. The inclusion of vector mesons allows saturation, which was not achieved in prior soliton-crystal approaches such as those by Weber (1998) using the linear sigma model, and the logarithmic potential enables stable solitonic solutions at higher densities than previously possible.
