# Direct Evaluation of the Quark Content of Nucleons from Lattice QCD at the Physical Point

> We evaluate the light, strange and charm scalar content of the nucleon using one lattice QCD ensemble generated with two degenerate light quarks with mass fixed to their physical value. We use improved techniques to evaluate the disconnected quark loops to sufficient accuracy to determine the strange and charm nucleon $σ$-terms. in addition to the light quark content $σ_{πN}$. We find $σ_{πN}$=$37.2(2.6)(^{4.7}_{2.9})$ MeV $σ_{s}$=$41.1(8.2)(^{7.8}_{5.8})$ MeV and $σ_c$=$79(21)(^{12}_{\phantom{1}8})$ MeV, where the first error is statistical and the second is the systematic error due to the determination of the lattice spacing, the assessment of finite volume and residual excited state effects.

## Key claim
The paper directly evaluates the nucleon sigma terms from a single lattice QCD ensemble at the physical pion mass, finding σπN = 37.2 MeV, which is consistent with the Feynman-Hellmann approach but significantly lower than the value of 59.1(3.5) MeV obtained from Roy-Steiner analyses of pionic-atom data. The authors note that this tension implies a considerably smaller πN-scattering length than pionic-atom phenomenology suggests.

## Method
The authors compute nucleon three-point functions for scalar operators using one Nf=2 twisted mass fermion ensemble at maximal twist with a lattice spacing of a=0.093(1) fm. They evaluate both connected and disconnected quark loop contributions, using stochastic techniques with the one-end trick for the light quark disconnected loops and Osterwalder-Seiler doublets for strange and charm loops. Excited state contamination is controlled through three complementary approaches: plateau fits at multiple source-sink separations, two-state fits, and the summation method.

## Key numbers
- σπN = 37.2(2.6)(+4.7−2.9) MeV, where the first error is statistical and the second is systematic from lattice spacing, finite volume, and excited state effects
- σs = 41.1(8.2)(+7.8−5.8) MeV
- σc = 79(21)(+12−8) MeV
- fNud = 0.0399(28)(+40−21), fNs = 0.0440(88)(+72−51), fNc = 0.085(22)(+11−7) (dimensionless ratios σf/mN with mN=933(8) MeV)
- yN = 0.075(16)(+14−10) (the ratio 2⟨N|ss|N⟩/⟨N|uu+dd|N⟩)
- Lattice spacing a=0.093(1) fm determined from the nucleon mass
- Lattice size 48^3 × 96, with 1800 configurations used for fermion loops

## Context
This work extends direct lattice QCD evaluations of nucleon sigma terms (Bali 2012, Gong 2013, Yang 2015) by performing the calculation at the physical pion mass, eliminating chiral extrapolation uncertainties. The σπN result agrees with the Feynman-Hellmann analysis of Durr (2015) who found 38(3)(3) MeV, but disagrees with the phenomenological Roy-Steiner analysis of Hoferichter (2015) yielding 59.1(3.5) MeV. The results enable improved predictions for spin-independent WIMP-nucleon scattering cross sections in dark matter direct detection experiments.
