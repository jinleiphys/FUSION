# Perturbation solutions of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics for longitudinally expanding fireballs

> The solutions of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics for longitudinal expanding fireballs is investigated with the Navier-Stokes theory and Israel-Stewart theory. The energy and Euler conservation equations for the viscous fluid are derived in Rindler coordinates with the longitudinal expansion effect is small. Under the perturbation assumption, an analytical perturbation solution for the Navier-Stokes approximation and numerical solutions for the Israel-Stewart approximation are presented. The temperature evolution with both shear viscous effect and longitudinal acceleration effect in the longitudinal expanding framework are presented and specifically temperature profile shows symmetry Gaussian shape in the Rindler coordinates. In addition, in the presence of the longitudinal acceleration expanding effect, the results of the Israel-Stewart approximation are compared to the results from Bjorken and Navier-Stokes approximation, and it gives a good description than the Navier-Stokes theories results at the early stages of evolution.

# Perturbation solutions of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics for longitudinally expanding fireballs

## Key claim
The paper presents analytical perturbation solutions for first-order (Navier-Stokes) and numerical solutions for second-order (Israel-Stewart) relativistic viscous hydrodynamics for longitudinally expanding fireballs, but only under the condition that the longitudinal acceleration parameter λ* is small (0 < λ* << 1) and the space-time rapidity ηs is not very large (|ηs| << 5). The perturbation approach is valid only in the leading order accuracy of λ*.

## Method
The authors derive energy and Euler conservation equations for viscous fluids in Rindler coordinates, assuming a (1+1)-dimensional fluid flow embedded in (1+3)-dimensional space-time with a flow velocity field where the flow rapidity Ω depends only on space-time rapidity ηs. For the Navier-Stokes (first-order) approximation with relaxation time τπ = 0, they obtain an analytical perturbation solution by Taylor-expanding hyperbolic functions to leading order O(λ*). For the Israel-Stewart (second-order) approximation, they numerically solve the coupled differential equations using finite-difference methods with grid spacing Δτ = 0.05 fm and Δηs = 0.05, assuming a conformal equation of state ε = 3p and relaxation time τπ = (2 - ln2)/(2πT).

## Key numbers
- Longitudinal acceleration parameter λ* values used: 0.05 and 0.10
- Initial proper time τ0 = 0.2 fm/c
- Initial temperature T0(0.2, 0.0) = 0.65 GeV
- Shear viscosity ratio η/s = 1/4π (the AdS/CFT lower bound)
- Relaxation time τπ = (2 - ln2)/(2πT)
- Grid spacing: Δτ = 0.05 fm, Δηs = 0.05
- Reynolds number defined as R0^{-1} = Πd/(T0τ0), where Πd = 4η/(3s)
- Equation of state parameter κ = 3 (for ε = 3p)

## Context
This work extends the ideal hydrodynamic CNC solutions (Csörgő, Nagy, Csanád, 2006-2008) and CKCJ solutions (Csörgő, Kasza, Csanád, Jiang, 2018) for longitudinally expanding fireballs by including viscous corrections. The perturbation solutions recover the Bjorken solution (Hwa 1974, Bjorken 1982) when λ* = 0 and R0^{-1} = 0, and the first-order Bjorken viscous solution (Arnold-Moore-Yaffe 2004, Teaney 2003) when λ* = 0 and R0^{-1} ≠ 0. The results enable future phenomenological studies of the interplay between shear viscosity and longitudinal acceleration effects in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, and provide testbeds for numerical hydrodynamic codes such as CLvisc (Pang 2018).
