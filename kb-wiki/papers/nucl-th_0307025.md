# Relativistic short-range correlation effects on the pion dynamics in nuclear matter

> Replicated theoretical attempts of relativistic approaches to the pion self-energy in nuclear matter yield unphysical pion spectra. We demonstrate the crucial dependence of the calculated pion spectra on the correct relativistic accounting for the short-range correlation effects on the pion self-energy in the medium. To do this, we simulate the short-range interactions by phenomenological contact terms in the relativistic Lagrangian density, and derive the pion self-energy by carefully taking into account the relativistic kinematics. The obtained spectrum for the pion-like excitations in cold nuclear matter shows physically meaningful branches, in contrast to those obtained before by different authors by the use of simplified relativistic approaches to the short-range correlations.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the unphysical pion spectra (with group velocity exceeding the speed of light) obtained in previous relativistic calculations arise from incorrectly replacing \(-k^{2}\) with \(\omega^{2}-k^{2}\) in the non-relativistic expression for short-range correlations, rather than from a fully relativistic treatment of the kinematics.

## Method

The authors simulate short-range correlations by phenomenological contact terms in the relativistic Lagrangian density with Landau-Migdal parameters \(g_{NN}^{\prime}\), \(g_{N\Delta}^{\prime}\), and \(g_{\Delta\Delta}^{\prime}\). They derive the pion self-energy by solving the Dyson equations for the polarization tensors in the reference frame where \(K=(\omega,0,0,k)\), using the mean-field ground state for nucleons and neglecting vacuum contributions. The resulting self-energy is then used to solve the dispersion equation for pion-like excitations in cold symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density used: \(n_{0}=0.17\,\text{fm}^{-3}\)
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant: \(f=0.988\)
- Universal Landau-Migdal coupling used in one calculation: \(g^{\prime}=0.6\)
- Non-universal Landau-Migdal parameters from recent analysis: \(g_{NN}^{\prime}=0.6\), \(g_{N\Delta}^{\prime}=0.24\pm 0.10\), \(g_{\Delta\Delta}^{\prime}=0.6\)
- Effective nucleon masses tested: \(M^{\ast}=0.7\) and \(M^{\ast}=0.9\) (in units of the free nucleon mass)
- Relativistic accounting for short-range correlations increases the effective pion mass by about 20%

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic treatment of short-range correlations by Migdal (1971) and the relativistic approach by Lutz (1998), correcting the sign error in the latter's Eq. (9). The authors show that simplified relativistic approaches used by Mao, Herbst, Mornas, Dmitriev, and Xia produce unphysical pion spectra with infinite group velocity, while their fully relativistic treatment yields physically meaningful branches. This enables reliable calculations of pion dynamics in nuclear matter relevant for relativistic heavy-ion collisions and neutron star physics.
