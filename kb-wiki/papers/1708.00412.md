# "Luneburg-lens-like structural Pauli attractive core of the nuclear force at short distances" [arXiv:1703.09396]

> A recent paper [S. Ohkubo, Phys. Rev. C 95, 044002 (2017)] found that the measured 1S0 phase shifts can be reproduced using a deeply attractive nucleon-nucleon potential. We find that the deuteron would decay strongly via pion emission to the deeply bound state arising in this potential. There- fore the success of a deeply attractive potential in describing phase shifts must be regarded only as an interesting curiosity.

## Key claim

The deeply attractive nucleon-nucleon potential proposed by Ohkubo (2017) to reproduce measured 1S0 phase shifts necessarily produces a deeply bound state; if such a potential were real, the deuteron would decay strongly via pion emission with a lifetime of about 1.6 × 10^{-23} s, so the potential's phase-shift success must be regarded only as an interesting curiosity.

## Method

Miller uses a variational principle with a single-parameter trial wave function to show that the expectation value of the Hamiltonian for the Ohkubo potential is negative, proving a bound state must exist. He then computes the decay width of the deuteron to this deeply bound state via pion emission using first-order perturbation theory, evaluating the matrix element with the Argonne V18 deuteron wave function and the bound-state wave function from the Ohkubo potential.

## Key numbers

- The Ohkubo potential has a depth of 2125 MeV at r=0 and a half-width r0 of about 0.4 fm.
- The variational calculation yields a Hamiltonian expectation value bottoming out at about -620 MeV.
- Numerical solution of the Schroedinger equation gives a binding energy of about 640 MeV for the deeply bound state.
- The pion momentum in the decay is k = 728 MeV/c.
- The computed decay width is Gamma = 42 MeV.
- The corresponding deuteron lifetime is T = 1.6 × 10^{-23} s.

## Context

This work directly contradicts the proposal by Ohkubo (2017) that a deeply attractive nucleon-nucleon potential can describe scattering data, and extends earlier quark-model ideas by Neudatchin (1977) and Liberman (1977) about short-range nuclear forces. The result shows that such a potential would make the deuteron unstable, with drastic consequences for low-energy nuclear reactions including np → dγ and the pp → D e- ν̄_e reaction essential for solar energy production, and thus the potential cannot represent the real nuclear force.
