# Chiral-symmetry breaking and confinement in Minkowski space

> We present a model for the quark-antiquark interaction formulated in Minkowski space using the Covariant Spectator Theory. The quark propagators are dressed with the same kernel that describes the interaction between different quarks. By applying the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity we show that our model satisfies the Adler-zero constraint imposed by chiral symmetry. For this model, our Minkowski-space results of the dressed quark mass function are compared to lattice QCD data obtained in Euclidean space. The mass function is then used in the calculation of the electromagnetic pion form factor in relativistic impulse approximation, and the results are presented and compared with the experimental data from JLab.

## Key claim

The authors present a quark-antiquark interaction model in Minkowski space using Covariant Spectator Theory that satisfies chiral symmetry constraints, including the Adler zero condition for pion-pion scattering, provided the linear-confining kernel includes equal-weighted scalar and pseudoscalar components (lambda_S = lambda_P) and decouples from the chiral-limit pion equation.

## Method

The authors solve the Covariant Spectator Theory Dyson-Schwinger equation (CST-DSE) for the dressed quark propagator using a kernel that combines a linear-confining part with vector-axial-vector remainder terms. They apply the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity to enforce chiral symmetry and demonstrate that the linear-confining kernel decouples from the chiral-limit pion equation. The resulting dressed quark mass function is then used in a relativistic impulse approximation calculation of the electromagnetic pion form factor.

## Key numbers

- Dressed quark mass in the chiral limit: m_chi = 0.308 GeV, fixed by fit to lattice QCD data extrapolated to m_0 = 0
- Strong quark form factor mass parameter: M_g = 1.734 GeV, fixed by fit to lattice QCD data in the chiral limit
- Bare quark masses used for mass function calculations: m_0 = 0, 0.016 GeV, 0.032 GeV, 0.047 GeV, and 0.063 GeV
- Pion mass mu = 0.42 GeV gives the best fit to JLab experimental data for the electromagnetic pion form factor over the full spacelike Q^2 range
- Scaling relation at large Q^2: F_pi(Q^2, lambda*mu) approximately equals lambda^2 * F_pi(Q^2, mu) for Q^2 much greater than mu^2

## Context

This work extends previous Covariant Spectator Theory quark models (Gross 1991, Gross 1994, Savkli 1999) by incorporating a kernel that satisfies the Adler zero constraint for pion-pion scattering, going beyond earlier models that used non-chirally-symmetric spin structures. The Minkowski-space results for the dressed quark mass function are compared to Euclidean-space lattice QCD data (Bowman 2005), and the pion form factor is compared to JLab experimental data (PhysRevC.78.045203). This enables future calculations of transition form factors and hadronic contributions to light-by-light scattering for precision calculations of the muon anomalous magnetic moment.
