# Chiral kinetic theory from Landau level basis

> We derive a chiral kinetic theory with Landau level basis, which is valid for slow-varying magnetic field with arbitrary magnitude. We apply the new chiral kinetic theory to calculate the electric conductivity transverse to the magnetic field in a magnetized QED and QCD plasma. Under the lowest Landau level approximation and relaxation time approximation, we find the transverse conductivity approaches a constant in the large magnetic field limit and is inversely proportional to the relaxation time. We also obtain a frequency-dependent transverse conductivity in response to a time-dependent electric field. We find a high frequency enhancement in this conductivity.

## Key claim

The authors derive a chiral kinetic theory (CKT) using a Landau level basis that is valid for magnetic fields of arbitrary magnitude, provided the magnetic field varies slowly in spacetime. The abstract's claim of validity for "slow-varying magnetic field with arbitrary magnitude" glosses over the condition that the perturbation (electric field or gradient) must be much smaller than the background magnetic field, specifically \(f_{\mu\nu} \ll B\) and \(\partial_X \ll \sqrt{B}\).

## Method

The authors start from the Wigner function for right-handed chiral fermions and split the gauge potential into a constant background magnetic field \(A_\mu\) and a perturbation \(a_\mu\). They derive equations of motion for the Wigner function components \(F\) and \(j_i\) using a gradient expansion that keeps all orders in the background magnetic field but only first order in the perturbation. The resulting kinetic equations are formally identical to those from a free-particle basis but with a different power counting, and the zeroth-order solution is given by Landau level contributions rather than free particle contributions. The authors then apply the formalism to compute transverse electric conductivity using the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation and a relaxation time approximation for the collision term.

## Key numbers

* Transverse conductivity in the strong magnetic field limit for QED: \(\sigma_\perp \xrightarrow{eB \gg p_z^2} \frac{e^2}{8\pi^2 \tau} \left[1 + \frac{1}{eB}\left(\mu^2 + \frac{\pi^2 T^2}{3} - \frac{1}{2\tau^2}\right)\right]\), where \(\tau\) is the relaxation time.
* Hall conductivity in the strong magnetic field limit for QED: \(\sigma_H \xrightarrow{eB \gg p_z^2} -\frac{e^2 \mu}{4\pi^2}\), which is independent of \(\tau\).
* Transverse conductivity for QCD with \(N_c\) colors and \(N_f\) flavors in the strong field limit: \(\sigma_\perp \xrightarrow{eB \gg p_z^2} 2N_c \sum_f \frac{Q_f^2 e^2}{8\pi^2 \tau_\omega} \left[1 + \frac{1}{|Q_f| eB}\left(\mu_q^2 + \frac{\pi^2 T^2}{3} - \frac{1}{2\tau_\omega^2}\right)\right]\), with \(\tau_\omega = \tau/(1 - i\omega \tau)\).
* For QGP phenomenology, the authors estimate \(\tau T \simeq 0.3\) based on lattice results for conductivity from Ding (2010), and take \(eB \simeq m_\pi^2 - 10 m_\pi^2\) with initial temperature \(T = 350\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral kinetic theory framework beyond the weak-field expansion (which becomes singular when \(p^2 \sim \hbar B\)) by using a Landau level basis, building on earlier attempts for the lowest Landau level by Hattori (2016). The results for transverse conductivity differ qualitatively from the weak magnetic field case studied by Gorbar (2016), Chen (2016), and Hidaka (2017), where ordinary conductivity is isotropic and proportional to \(\tau\) while Hall conductivity depends on \(\tau\). In contrast, the authors find \(\sigma_\perp \sim 1/\tau\) in the strong field limit, and the Hall conductivity becomes independent of \(\tau\). The frequency-dependent extension shows a high-frequency enhancement of the transverse conductivity, similar to a holographic study by Li (2018). The results enable future calculations of transverse transport in magnetized quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy ion collisions, though the authors note that including higher Landau levels is necessary for a definitive answer.
