# Extracting many-body color charge correlators in the proton from exclusive DIS at large Bjorken x

> We construct a general QCD light front formalism to compute many-body color charge correlators in the proton. These form factors can be extracted from deeply inelastic scattering measurements of exclusive final states in analogy to electromagnetic form factors extracted in elastic electron scattering experiments. Particularly noteworthy is the potential to extract a novel Odderon form factor, either indirectly from exclusive $J/Ψ$ measurements, or directly from exclusive measurements of the $η_c$ or tensor mesons at large Bjorken x. Besides the intrinsic information conveyed by these color charge correlators on the spatio-temporal tomography at the sub-femtoscopic scale at large x, the corresponding cumulants extend the domain of validity of McLerran-Venugopalan type weight functionals from small x and large nuclei to nucleons and light nuclei at large $x$, as well as to non-zero momentum transfer. This may significantly reduce nonperturbative systematic uncertainties in the initial conditions for QCD evolution equations at small $x$ and could be of strong relevance for the phenomenology of present and future collider experiments.

## Key claim

The authors construct a general QCD light front formalism to compute many-body color charge correlators in the proton, arguing that these form factors can be extracted from exclusive deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) measurements at large Bjorken x, in close analogy to electromagnetic form factors extracted from elastic electron scattering. The hedge is that this approach is complementary to generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and transverse momentum distributions (TMDs), and that the extraction depends on the dominance of the three-quark valence Fock state at large x, which may not hold at lower x values.

## Method

The authors develop a Hamiltonian light front formalism in light cone gauge, focusing on the three valence quark Fock state of the proton at large Bjorken x. They compute expectation values of quadratic and cubic color charge density operators in the proton, expressed as matrix elements of light front Fock-space wave functions, and relate these to color charge form factors that can be extracted from exclusive DIS measurements of vector mesons (J/Ψ) and pseudoscalar or tensor mesons (ηc). The formalism extends the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model weight functionals from small x and large nuclei to nucleons at large x and non-zero momentum transfer.

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1993-1994) and the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory framework from small x and large nuclei to the large x regime in nucleons and light nuclei. It provides a first-principles approach to compute initial conditions for the Balitsky-JIMWLK and Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) evolution equations (Balitsky, 1995; Kovchegov, 1999), which currently rely on the MV model for large nuclei. The formalism enables extraction of a novel Odderon form factor from exclusive J/Ψ or ηc measurements, which is of topical interest following claims of Odderon exchange in proton-proton elastic scattering at LHC energies (TOTEM collaboration, 2017). The approach is complementary to lattice QCD computations of color charge form factors (Winter et al., 2017) and may reduce nonperturbative systematic uncertainties in small x QCD evolution initial conditions relevant for RHIC and LHC phenomenology.
