# Neutron stars and strange stars in the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model

> We investigate the equations of state for pure neutron matter and strange hadronic matter in $β$-equilibrium, including $Λ$, $Σ$ and $Ξ$ hyperons. The masses and radii of pure neutron stars and strange hadronic stars are obtained. For a pure neutron star, the maximum mass is about $1.8 M_{\mathrm{sun}}$, while for a strange hadronic star, the maximum mass is around $1.45 M_{\mathrm{sun}}$. The typical radii of pure neutron stars and strange hadronic stars are about 11.0-12.3 km and 10.7-11.7 km, respectively.

## Key claim

The maximum mass of a pure neutron star is about 1.8 solar masses, while for a strange hadronic star (including hyperons) the maximum mass is around 1.45 solar masses; however, the authors note that if heavy stars such as 4U 1820-30 with a mass of about 2.3 solar masses are confirmed, the strange hadronic star model would be ruled out if this model is a good description of Nature.

## Method

The authors used the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model, where quarks are confined within baryons by an effective potential and interact with scalar and vector mesons based on SU(3) chiral symmetry. They solved the equations of state for pure neutron matter and for strange hadronic matter in beta-equilibrium including the full baryon octet (nucleons, Lambda, Sigma, and Xi hyperons) at zero temperature. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation was then solved to obtain the mass-radius relations for both types of stars.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of a pure neutron star: about 1.8 solar masses, with a central density of 1.05 fm^-3 and a corresponding radius of 11.0 km.
- Maximum mass of a strange hadronic star: about 1.45 solar masses, with a central density of 1.0 fm^-3 and a corresponding radius of 10.9 km.
- For pure neutron stars with masses between 0.5 and 1.8 solar masses, radii are about 11.0-12.3 km.
- For strange hadronic stars with masses between 0.5 and 1.45 solar masses, radii are about 10.7-11.7 km.
- For central densities between 3 rho_0 and 6 rho_0 (where rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3 is the saturation density), pure neutron star masses are in the range 1.48-1.8 solar masses and strange hadronic star masses are in the range 1.23-1.45 solar masses.
- The six known double neutron-star binaries have masses in the range 1.36 +/- 0.08 solar masses (Thorsett and Heiselberg, cited in the paper).

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model, previously applied to nuclear matter, finite nuclei, and hypernuclei by Wang and collaborators (2003, 2004), to the study of neutron stars and strange hadronic stars. The results are compared with earlier relativistic mean field models (Walecka model, Serot 1992) and with observations of six double neutron-star binaries with masses around 1.36 solar masses (Thorsett and Heiselberg, cited in the paper). The paper enables future investigation of whether including a quark core in strange stars could increase the maximum mass to accommodate heavy stars like 4U 1820-30 with a mass of about 2.3 solar masses.
