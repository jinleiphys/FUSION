# Transverse Momentum Broadening and the Jet Quenching Parameter, Redux

> We use Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) to analyze the transverse momentum broadening, or diffusion in transverse momentum space, of an energetic parton propagating through quark-gluon plasma. Since we neglect the radiation of gluons from the energetic parton, we can only discuss momentum broadening, not parton energy loss. The interaction responsible for momentum broadening in the absence of radiation is that between the energetic (collinear) parton and the Glauber modes of the gluon fields in the medium. We derive the effective Lagrangian for this interaction, and we show that the probability for picking up transverse momentum k_\perp is given by the Fourier transform of the expectation value of two transversely separated light-like path-ordered Wilson lines. This yields a field theoretical definition of the jet quenching parameter \hat q, and shows that this can be interpreted as a diffusion constant. We close by revisiting the calculation of \hat q for the strongly coupled plasma of N=4 SYM theory, showing that previous calculations need some modifications that make them more straightforward and do not change the result.

## Key claim

The paper provides a field-theoretical definition of the jet quenching parameter \(\hat q\) as a diffusion constant derived from transverse momentum broadening in the absence of gluon radiation, but explicitly notes that neglecting radiation is not justified by any controlled approximation and that the calculation is limited to the \(Q \to \infty\) limit where Glauber gluon exchange dominates over soft gluon exchange.

## Method

The authors use Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) to analyze the propagation of an energetic on-shell parton through quark-gluon plasma, focusing on interactions with Glauber gluons whose momenta scale as \(Q(\lambda^2, \lambda^2, \lambda)\) with \(\lambda = T/Q \ll 1\). They apply the optical theorem to relate the probability distribution \(P(k_\perp)\) for transverse momentum acquired over a distance \(L\) to the imaginary part of forward scattering amplitudes, summing diagrams with arbitrarily many Glauber gluon insertions. The calculation yields \(P(k_\perp)\) as the Fourier transform of the thermal expectation value of two transversely separated light-like path-ordered Wilson lines, denoted \(\mathcal{W}(x_\perp)\).

## Key numbers

* The hard parton initial momentum is \(q_0 = (0, Q, 0)\) with \(Q \gg T\), defining the small parameter \(\lambda = T/Q \ll 1\).
* Glauber gluon momenta scale as \(p \sim Q (\lambda^2, \lambda^2, \lambda)\), and after interaction the collinear parton momentum is \(q \sim Q (\lambda^2, 1, \lambda)\).
* The jet quenching parameter is defined as \(\hat q \equiv \langle k_\perp^2 \rangle / L\), with the normalization \(\int d^2 k_\perp / (2\pi)^2 \, P(k_\perp) = 1\).
* For strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM theory, the authors revisit the calculation of \(\hat q\) and find that previous results from Liu, Rajagopal, and Wiedemann (2006) are unchanged despite subtleties in operator ordering.

## Context

This work extends the earlier SCET-based analysis of Idilbi and Majumder (2008) for transverse momentum broadening in deep inelastic scattering on large nuclei, and reproduces an expression for \(P(k_\perp)\) first obtained by Casalderrey-Solana and Salgado and by Liang, Wang, and Zhou using different methods. The field-theoretical definition of \(\hat q\) in terms of light-like Wilson lines enables systematic factorization and future inclusion of radiative processes, and the authors clarify that the operator ordering in the Wilson line expectation value differs from a standard Wilson loop, a subtlety that had not been previously noticed in this context.
