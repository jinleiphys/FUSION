# Near-Threshold Photoproduction of eta-mesons on Three-Nucleon Nuclei

> A microscopic few-body description of near-threshold coherent photoproduction of the eta meson on tritium and 3He targets is given. The photoproduction cross-section is calculated using the Finite Rank Approximation (FRA) of the nuclear Hamiltonian. The results indicate a strong final state interaction of the eta meson with the residual nucleus. Sensitivity of the results to the choice of the eta N T-matrix is investigated.

# Near-Threshold Photoproduction of eta-mesons on Three-Nucleon Nuclei

## Key claim

The coherent photoproduction cross section for eta mesons on tritium and helium-3 targets is strongly enhanced near threshold due to final state interaction, but the magnitude of this enhancement depends significantly on which version of the poorly-constrained eta-nucleon T-matrix is used, with differences between versions that reproduce the same scattering length and S11 resonance pole position.

## Method

The authors used the Finite Rank Approximation (FRA) of the nuclear Hamiltonian, which neglects the continuous spectrum and retains only the ground state of the three-nucleon system, to solve the few-body equations for eta-nucleus scattering. The photoproduction amplitude was obtained by treating the gamma-nucleon and eta-nucleon channels as coupled channels, then applying the principle of detailed balance from the radiative absorption reaction. Three different separable forms of the eta-nucleon T-matrix were constructed, all reproducing the same scattering length and S11 resonance parameters but differing in their treatment of two-body unitarity, while the nuclear bound state was obtained from the Integro-Differential Equation Approach (IDEA) with the Malfliet-Tjon potential.

## Key numbers

- The eta-nucleon scattering length used for versions I and II of the T-matrix was a_etaN = (0.75 + i 0.27) fm
- The S11 resonance parameters used were E0 = 1535 MeV - (m_N + m_eta) and Gamma = 150 MeV
- The range parameter for the Yamaguchi form factor was alpha = 3.316 fm^-1
- For version III-a, the resulting scattering length was a_etaN = (0.77 + i 0.26) fm; for version III-b, a_etaN = (0.79 + i 0.32) fm
- The cross sections were normalized to sigma_0 at E_gamma = 652 MeV, where the near-threshold enhancement dies out
- The ratio of neutron to proton photoproduction amplitudes was A = -0.84 +/- 0.15 from multipole analysis
- Variation of the off-shell parameter kappa between 1 fm^-1 and 10 fm^-1 produced less than 1% difference in the cross section curves

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous FRA calculations of low-energy eta scattering from three-nucleon systems (Rakityansky et al., 1995; 1996) to include photoproduction, and contrasts with the first-order optical potential approach of Tiator et al. which underestimated the near-threshold cross section. The results show that the final state interaction is crucial and that two-body unitarity in the eta-nucleon T-matrix significantly affects predictions, enabling future experimental determination of the neutron-to-proton ratio A for the photoproduction amplitude by measuring on helium-3 rather than tritium.
