# Eta and Eta' physics

> This talk describes the reasons why $η$ and $η^\prime$ decays are an interesting topic of study for both theory and experiment. The main part discusses the results of the recent calculation of $η\to3π$ at two-loop order in ChPT. Some puzzling aspects of the results compared to earlier dispersive calculations are highlighted. I also like to remind the reader of the use of $η$ and $η^\prime$ decays for studying the anomaly.

## Key claim

The recent two-loop (order p^6) Chiral Perturbation Theory calculation of η → 3π finds a 20–30% correction in amplitude, which is larger in magnitude than earlier dispersive estimates, but the predicted Dalitz plot parameter α for the neutral decay is consistently positive while dispersive calculations and an earlier chiral unitary approach give a negative value, indicating a puzzle.

## Method

The author performed a full two-loop (order p^6) Chiral Perturbation Theory calculation for η → 3π, generalizing methods developed for π⁰–η mixing to processes with mixing on more than one external leg. Input parameters were taken from the main order p⁶ fit (fit 10) of a prior work (Bijnens and collaborators, 2007), and the needed order p⁶ low-energy constants were determined by resonance exchange. The amplitude was computed along the Dalitz plot lines t = u and s = u, and the Dalitz plot parameters a, b, d, f for the charged decay and α for the neutral decay were extracted.

## Key numbers

* The lowest-order (tree-level) ChPT prediction for the η → 3π decay rate is 66 eV, compared to the experimental value of 295 ± 17 eV (PDG 2006).
* The order p⁴ calculation increased the predicted decay rate to about 150 eV, with about half of the enhancement coming from ππ rescattering and the other half from other effects like chiral logarithms.
* Dispersive calculations (Kambor, Wiesendanger, Wyler, 1996; Anisovich, Leutwyler, 1996) found a decay rate of about 220 eV.
* The new order p⁶ ChPT calculation finds a 20–30% correction in amplitude, larger in magnitude than the dispersive estimates.
* For the charged decay η → π⁺π⁻π⁰, the NNLO (order p⁶) central prediction gives A₀² = 538, a = −1.271, b = 0.394, d = 0.055, f = 0.025.
* For the neutral decay η → π⁰π⁰π⁰, the NNLO central prediction gives A̅₀² = 4790 and α = 0.013.
* The KLOE experiment measures α = −0.027 ± 0.004⁺⁰·⁰⁰⁴₋₀·₀₀₆ for the neutral decay, while the ChPT NNLO prediction gives α = +0.013.

## Context

This work extends the earlier order p⁴ ChPT calculation of η → 3π by Gasser and Leutwyler (1985) and the dispersive analyses of Kambor, Wiesendanger, and Wyler (1996) and Anisovich and Leutwyler (1996). The puzzling discrepancy between the sign of the Dalitz plot parameter α in the neutral decay (positive in ChPT, negative in dispersive calculations and experiment) highlights the need for further study. The results also enable improved determinations of the quark-mass ratios R = (mₛ − m̂)/(m_d − m_u) and Q² = R(mₛ + m_d)/(2m̂) from η → 3π decay.
