# Deconfinement transition for nonzero baryon density in the Field Correlator Method

> Deconfinement phase transition due to disappearance of confining colorelectric field correlators is described using nonperturbative equation of state. The resulting transition temperature $T_c(μ)$ at any chemical potential $μ$ is expressed in terms of the change of gluonic condensate $ΔG_2$ and absolute value of Polyakov loop $L_{fund} (T_c)$, known from lattice and analytic data, and is in good agreement with lattice data for $ΔG_2 \approx 0.0035 $ GeV$^4$. E.g. $T_c(0) =0.27; 0.19; 0.17$ GeV for $n_f=0,2,3$ respectively.

## Key claim

The deconfinement transition temperature \(T_c(\mu)\) as a function of baryon chemical potential can be expressed analytically using only the change in gluonic condensate \(\Delta G_2\) and the absolute value of the Polyakov loop \(L_{fund}(T_c)\), under the assumption that the vacuum fields are rigid and do not depend on \(T\) or \(\mu\) except at the phase boundary.

## Method

The authors use the Field Correlator Method (FCM) to describe the nonperturbative equation of state of the quark-gluon plasma in the Single Line Approximation (SLA), where the pressure of quarks and gluons is expressed through the Polyakov loop. The deconfinement transition is identified with the disappearance of the colorelectric field correlator \(D^E(x)\), and the transition temperature is obtained by equating the total pressure in the confined and deconfined phases, neglecting the hadron gas pressure in the first approximation.

## Key numbers

- The change in gluonic condensate across the transition is taken as \(\Delta G_2 \approx 0.00341\) GeV\(^4\) (approximately half the standard gluon condensate \(G_2^{st} \approx 0.012\) GeV\(^4\)).
- For \(\Delta G_2 = 0.00341\) GeV\(^4\), the transition temperature at zero chemical potential is \(T_c(0) = 0.27\) GeV for \(n_f=0\), \(0.19\) GeV for \(n_f=2\), and \(0.17\) GeV for \(n_f=3\).
- The critical chemical potential at zero temperature is \(\mu_c(0) = 0.63\) GeV for \(n_f=2\) and \(0.58\) GeV for \(n_f=3\).
- The curvature parameter in the expansion \(T_c(\mu) = T_c(0) (1 - C \cdot 9\mu^2/T_c^2(0))\) is \(C = 0.0110(3)\).
- The static potential at the transition temperature is fixed as \(V_1(\infty, T_c) = 0.50(5)\) GeV, giving \(\kappa = 0.25\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Field Correlator Method previously applied to nonzero temperature by Simonov and collaborators (2003, 2004) to include nonzero baryon density, and provides an analytic expression for the deconfinement curve that agrees with lattice data for \(\mu \lesssim 0.25\) GeV. The results are consistent with lattice calculations of the Polyakov loop and gluonic condensate, and the predicted \(T_c(0)\) values for different numbers of flavors match lattice reviews (e.g., Karsch 2006). The approach enables future inclusion of quark masses and studies of the phase transition near \(\mu_c(0)\), where strong quark-quark and three-quark interactions may become important.
