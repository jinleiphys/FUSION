# Monte Carlo simulation of the nuclear medium: Fermi gases, nuclei and the role of Pauli potentials

> The role of Pauli potentials in the semiclassical simulation of Fermi gases at low temperatures is investigated. An alternative Pauli potential to the usual bivariate Gaussian form by Dorso et al. is proposed. This new Pauli potential allows for a simultaneous good reproduction of not only the kinetic energy per particle but also the momentum distribution and the two-body correlation function. The reproduction of the binding energies in finite nuclei in the low and medium mass range is also analyzed. What is found is that given a reasonable short-range atractive nuclear interaction one can include correlation effects in a suitable chosen density dependent Pauli potential.

# Monte Carlo simulation of the nuclear medium: Fermi gases, nuclei and the role of Pauli potentials

## Key claim

A new density-dependent Pauli potential, composed of separate spatial and momentum exponential terms plus a smeared step function, can simultaneously reproduce the kinetic energy per particle, momentum distribution, and two-body correlation function of a low-temperature Fermi gas, whereas the standard bivariate Gaussian form by Dorso et al. fails to reproduce the "Fermi hole" at small distances. The reproduction of binding energies in low and medium mass nuclei requires that the Pauli potential parameters depend on density.

## Method

The author performed Metropolis Monte Carlo simulations of NVT systems with N=1000 fermions in a cubic box at reduced temperature τ = T/T_F = 0.05, using a Hamiltonian with kinetic energy and a Pauli effective potential that acts only between nucleons with identical spin and isospin. Two forms of the Pauli potential were compared: the bivariate Gaussian form by Dorso et al. (with parameters V_S=207 MeV, q_0=1.644 fm, p_0=120 MeV/c) and a new form with separate spatial exponential, momentum exponential, and a smeared Heaviside step function (V_q=13.517 MeV, V_p=1.260 MeV, V_Θ=3.560 MeV, q_0=0.66 fm, p_0=49.03 MeV/c, η=30). For finite nuclei, a simplified square-well nuclear potential (V_well=-3 MeV, width 2 fm) with a repulsive core (V_core=10 MeV, width 1 fm) and Coulomb interaction was used.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ_0 = 0.148 fm^{-3}
- Dorso et al. Pauli potential parameters at ρ_0 and τ=0.05: V_S=207 MeV, q_0=1.644 fm, p_0=120 MeV/c
- New Pauli potential parameters at ρ_0 and τ=0.05: V_q=13.517 MeV, V_p=1.260 MeV, V_Θ=3.560 MeV, q_0=0.66 fm, p_0=49.03 MeV/c, η=30
- Square-well nuclear potential depth V_well = -3 MeV with width 2 fm and core V_core = 10 MeV with width 1 fm
- Simulation performed with N=1000 particles at reduced temperature τ = 0.05

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical approach to fermionic many-body simulations pioneered by Wilets et al. and the Gaussian Pauli potential form introduced by Dorso et al. The new potential addresses a known deficiency in reproducing the two-body correlation function at small distances, which is important for simulations of nuclear pasta at densities 0.01ρ_0 to 0.5ρ_0 and temperatures of tens of MeV, as studied by Maruyama et al. The density-dependent parametrization enables application to finite nuclei and could improve dynamical descriptions of matter in supernova events and neutron stars.
