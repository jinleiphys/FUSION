# Two-nucleon emitters within a pseudostate approach

> A method to identify and characterize three-body resonances in a discrete basis is discussed in the context of two-nucleon emitters. For this purpose, a resonance operator is introduced and diagonalized in a basis of energy pseudostates within the hyperspherical formalism. Then, the energy and width of the resonance are obtained from its time dependence. The approach is illustrated for $^{16}$Be ($^{14}\text{Be}+n+n$).

## Key claim

The paper presents a method to identify and characterize three-body resonances in a discrete basis by diagonalizing a resonance operator and extracting the energy and width from the time dependence of the resulting state; the approach is illustrated for the specific case of the ground-state resonance of \({}^{16}\)Be, but the authors note that the extracted width depends on an extrapolation to \(x=0\) of a linear trend, which is a consequence of the discrete nature of the basis.

## Method

The authors introduce a resonance operator \(\widehat{M} = \widehat{H}^{-1/2}\widehat{V}\widehat{H}^{-1/2}\) and diagonalize it in a basis of energy pseudostates obtained within the hyperspherical formalism. The eigenstates of \(\widehat{M}\) corresponding to large negative eigenvalues are identified as resonances. The time-dependent amplitude of the selected eigenstate is then computed, and the resonance energy \(\varepsilon_r\) and width \(\Gamma\) are obtained by minimizing a quadratic deviation between this amplitude and an ideal Breit-Wigner form, with the final values extrapolated to the limit of a vanishing damping parameter \(x\).

## Key numbers

* The ground-state resonance of \({}^{16}\)Be(0\(^+\)) is found at \(\varepsilon_r = 1.341\) MeV with a width \(\Gamma = 0.160\) MeV, obtained by extrapolating the linear trend of the extracted parameters to \(x=0\).
* Convergence of the resonance energy is shown from \(\varepsilon_r = 1.403\) MeV at \(K_{max}=22\) to \(\varepsilon_r = 1.341\) MeV at \(K_{max}=30\), with \(\Gamma\) stable around 0.160 MeV for \(K_{max} \ge 24\).
* Convergence of the width is shown from \(\Gamma = 0.228\) MeV at \(N=8\) to \(\Gamma = 0.160\) MeV at \(N=20\), with \(\varepsilon_r\) converging to 1.341 MeV at \(N=24\).

## Context

This work extends the study of two-nucleon emitters beyond the driplines, following experimental observations of direct two-proton decays (Giovinazzo, 2002; Grigorenko, 2009) and two-neutron decays (Spyrou, 2012; Kohley, 2013). The method provides an alternative to the three-body eigenphase analysis within the hyperspherical \(R\)-matrix method used by Lovell (2017) to solve the three-body scattering problem, and the computed width for \({}^{16}\)Be is consistent with those results. The approach enables the identification and characterization of three-body resonances using a discrete basis without solving the full scattering problem, which can be applied to other two-nucleon emitters.
