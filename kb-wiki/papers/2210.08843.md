# Ab initio single-neutron spectroscopic overlaps in lithium isotopes

> We calculate single-neutron spectroscopic overlaps for lithium isotopes in the framework of the \textit{ab initio} symmetry-adapted no-core shell model. We report the associated neutron-nucleus asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) and spectroscopic factors (SFs) that are important ingredients in many reaction cross section calculations. While spectroscopic factors have been traditionally extracted from experimental cross sections, their sensitivity on the type of reactions, energy, and the underlying models point to the need for determining SF from first-principle structure considerations. As illustrative examples, we present $^6$Li+n, $^7$Li+n, and $^8$Li+n, and we show that the results are in a good agreement with those of other \textit{ab initio} methods, where available, including the quantum Monte Carlo approach. We compare ANCs and SFs to available experimentally deduced values, with a view toward expanding this study to heavier nuclei and to extracting inter-cluster effective interactions for input into analyses of existing and future experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that *ab initio* symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) calculations of single-neutron spectroscopic overlaps for lithium isotopes produce asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) and spectroscopic factors (SFs) that agree with other *ab initio* methods and available experimental data, but only when sufficiently large model spaces (up to Nmax=12) are used and the overlap tails are matched to exterior Whittaker functions at appropriately chosen channel radii.

## Method

The authors used the *ab initio* symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) with the NNLOopt chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction to calculate ground-state wavefunctions of Li-6, Li-7, Li-8, and Li-9 in harmonic oscillator bases with Nmax up to 12 and oscillator parameters hbar-omega of 10, 15, and 20 MeV. They computed single-neutron spectroscopic overlaps for the partitions Li-6+n, Li-7+n, and Li-8+n by evaluating reduced matrix elements of a neutron creation operator between the A-body and (A-1)-body eigenstates in the SU(3)-coupled basis, then matched the interior overlap functions to exterior Whittaker functions at channel radii to extract ANCs while preserving the spectroscopic factors.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state energies of Li-6, Li-7, and Li-8 from SA-NCSM with NNLOopt interaction, extrapolated to infinite model space, agree with experimental values within uncertainties (blue bands in Fig. 1 show extrapolated values with fitting and hbar-omega variance)
* For the Li-6+n overlap at Nmax=12 with hbar-omega=15 MeV, the tail coincides with the exact Whittaker function solution up to about 7 fm
* Spectroscopic factors for Li-6+n converge toward the experimentally deduced value from Jun et al. (2010) as model space increases (Fig. 2b)
* For the Li-7+n system, the experimentally deduced SF is from Schiffer et al. (1967), and the VMC result is from the QMC database (Fig. 3)
* For the Li-8+n system, the experimentally deduced SF is from Wuosmaa et al. (2005) (Fig. 4)
* Woods-Saxon potential depths fitted to reproduce experimental neutron separation energy of Li-7: V0=-71.95 MeV for p1/2 and V0=-61.31 MeV for p3/2, with R0=1.25A^(1/3) fm, a=0.65 fm diffuseness, and spin-orbit depth V_SO=6 MeV

## Context

This work extends the *ab initio* symmetry-adapted no-core shell method to single-nucleon spectroscopic observables, building on prior SA-NCSM studies of nuclear structure in light and intermediate-mass nuclei including Ne-20, Mg-21, Mg-22, Mg-28, Ne-32, and Ti-48 (Launey et al., 2021). The results are compared to quantum Monte Carlo calculations (Brida et al., 2011; Nollett and Wiringa, 2011) and no-core shell model calculations (Navratil, 2004), showing good agreement. The method enables future extraction of inter-cluster effective interactions for reaction studies and can be extended to medium-mass nuclei (A less than or about 50) and nuclei near drip lines.
