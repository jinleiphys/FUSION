# Isospin-breaking corrections in the pion-deuteron scattering length

> It is shown that isospin-breaking corrections to the pion-deuteron scattering length can be very large, because of the vanishing of the isospin-symmetric contribution to this scattering length at leading order in chiral perturbation theory. We further demonstrate that these corrections can explain the bulk of the discrepancy between the recent experimental data on pionic hydrogen and pionic deuterium. We also give the first determination of the electromagnetic low-energy constant f1.

## Key claim

Isospin-breaking corrections to the pion-deuteron scattering length can be very large (about 42% of the experimental value) because the isospin-symmetric contribution vanishes at leading order in chiral perturbation theory; these corrections can explain the bulk of the discrepancy between experimental data on pionic hydrogen and pionic deuterium.

## Method

The authors computed the leading-order (O(p²)) isospin-breaking correction to the πd scattering length using chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), including contributions from the electromagnetic low-energy constants (LECs) f₁ and f₂ and the strong LEC c₁. They then used this correction to re-analyze the combined experimental data on pionic hydrogen (energy shift and width) and pionic deuterium (energy shift) to extract the S-wave πN scattering lengths a⁺ and a⁻, and to determine the previously unknown electromagnetic LEC f₁.

## Key numbers

- Leading-order isospin-breaking correction to the πd scattering length: Δa_πd^LO = -(0.0110^{+0.0081}_{-0.0058}) M_π⁻¹, which is 42% of the experimental Re a_πd^exp = -(0.0261 ± 0.0005) M_π⁻¹
- Resulting πN scattering lengths after including isospin-breaking corrections: a⁺ = (0.0015 ± 0.0022) M_π⁻¹, a⁻ = (0.0852 ± 0.0018) M_π⁻¹
- First determination of the electromagnetic LEC: f₁ = -2.1^{+3.2}_{-2.2} GeV⁻¹
- Pionic hydrogen experimental values used: ε₁s = -7.120 ± 0.008 ± 0.009 eV, Γ₁s = 0.800 ± 0.030 eV
- Pionic deuterium experimental value: Re a_πd^exp = -(0.0261 ± 0.0005) M_π⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory treatment of isospin breaking in the πd system beyond earlier studies by Deloff (2001) and Doring (2004), which included only Coulomb and mass-difference effects but not the direct quark-photon coupling encoded in electromagnetic LECs. The results resolve a discrepancy that emerged when new experimental data from the Pionic Hydrogen Collaboration at PSI (Gotta, 2005) were combined with ChPT calculations of isospin-breaking corrections in pionic hydrogen by Lyubovitskij (2000) and Gasser (2002), which had left the LEC f₁ undetermined. The work enables a consistent combined analysis of pionic hydrogen and deuterium data to extract πN scattering lengths, and motivates improved measurements of the pionic deuterium energy shift as well as higher-order (O(p³)) calculations of isospin-breaking corrections.
