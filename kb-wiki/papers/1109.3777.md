# Influence of charge asymmetry and isospin dependent cross-section on nuclear stopping

> Using the isospin dependent quantum molecular dynamics model, we study the effect of charge asymmetry and isospin dependent cross-section on nuclear stopping and multiplicity of free nucleons and LMF's. Simulations were carried out for the reactions $^{124}X_{m}+^{124}X_{m}$, where m varies from 47 to 59 and for $^{40}Y_{n}+^{40}Y_{n}$, where n varies from 14 to 23. Our study shows that nuclear stopping as well as the production of LMF's depend strongly on the isospin of the cross-section.

## Key claim
Nuclear stopping and the production of light-mass fragments (LMFs) depend strongly on whether an isospin-dependent or isospin-independent nucleon-nucleon cross-section is used in the IQMD model, but the effect of charge asymmetry (varying the proton number at fixed mass) on these observables is very weak (less than 3%).

## Method
The authors performed simulations using the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model with a soft equation of state. They calculated the anisotropy ratio ⟨R⟩, the quadrupole moment ⟨1/Q_ZZ⟩, and the rapidity distribution of free nucleons and LMFs for two series of symmetric collisions: ⁴⁰X_m + ⁴⁰X_m (with proton number m from 14 to 23) and ¹²⁴Y_n + ¹²⁴Y_n (with proton number n from 47 to 59), all at an incident energy of 100 MeV/nucleon. They compared results using an isospin-dependent cross-section (σ_iso) and an isospin-independent cross-section (σ_noiso), and also compared their calculated anisotropy ratio for ¹²⁴Sn + ¹²⁴Sn with experimental data from the INDRA collaboration.

## Key numbers
- The difference between σ_iso and σ_noiso in the stopping observables at 100 MeV/nucleon is 8% for ⟨R⟩ and 26% for ⟨1/Q_ZZ⟩.
- The influence of charge asymmetry on the rapidity distribution is less than 3%, despite the charge of the colliding nuclei varying from 47 to 59 units at fixed mass 124.
- The theoretical anisotropy ratio ⟨R⟩ for the reaction ¹²⁴Sn₅₀ + ¹²⁴Sn₅₀, calculated with σ_iso reduced by 20%, is in good agreement with the experimental data from the INDRA collaboration.

## Context
This work extends the study of nuclear stopping in heavy-ion collisions by focusing on the separate roles of charge asymmetry and the isospin dependence of the nucleon-nucleon cross-section, building on earlier studies by Bauer (no year given), Peilert et al. (no year given), and Fen Fu et al. (no year given). The finding that stopping is sensitive to the isospin-dependent cross-section but insensitive to the symmetry potential supports the proposal by Jian-Ye Liu et al. (no year given) that nuclear stopping can be used as a probe of the isospin dependence of the in-medium cross-section. The comparison with INDRA data validates the model and suggests that a 20% reduction of the isospin-dependent cross-section is needed to reproduce experimental results, a choice motivated by prior work (refs. 18 and 19 in the paper).
