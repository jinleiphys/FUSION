# Rapidity dependence of nuclear coalescence: impact on cosmic ray antinuclei

> Upcoming studies at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) aim to extend the rapidity coverage in measurements of the production cross section of antinuclei ${\rm \bar d}$ and $\overline{^3\rm He}$. We illustrate the impact of such studies on cosmic ray (CR) flux predictions, important, in turn, for the interpretation of results from CR experiments. We show that, in terms of the rapidity effect, covering the range $|y|<1.5$ at the LHC should be sufficient for the astrophysical CR calculation. Important extrapolation remains in other aspects of the problem, notably $\sqrt{s}$.

## Key claim

Extending LHC rapidity coverage for antinuclei production to \(|y|<1.5\) would be sufficient for astrophysical cosmic ray calculations, but significant extrapolation remains in the center-of-mass energy \(\sqrt{s}\) dependence, since most cosmic ray antideuterons come from pp collisions at \(\sqrt{s}<30\) GeV while LHC collider mode operates at few TeV.

## Method

The author computes the cosmic ray flux of antiprotons, antideuterons, and antihelium-3 by integrating the Lorentz-invariant differential production cross section over primary proton energy and scattering angle, using a coalescence prescription with coalescence factors taken from ALICE pp data at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) TeV and 13 TeV. To assess the impact of limited rapidity coverage, the differential cross section is set to zero for kinematic regions outside a given rapidity cut \(|y|<Y\), and the resulting cosmic ray flux is compared to the full calculation. Cosmic ray propagation is treated using a rigidity-dependent mean column density calibrated to AMS-02 boron/carbon flux ratio data.

## Key numbers

- At rigidity \(\mathcal{R}\lesssim5\) GV, about 90% of the cosmic ray antideuteron and antihelium-3 flux predictions depend on extrapolation beyond current rapidity coverage.
- For cosmic rays at \(\mathcal{R}=10\) GV, only about 6%, 15%, and 30% of antiprotons, antideuterons, and antihelium-3 respectively come from initial states with \(\sqrt{s}>25\) GeV.
- The mean column density used for propagation is \(\mathcal{X}\approx10.5\left(\frac{\mathcal{R}}{10\ \mathrm{GV}}\right)^{-0.56}\left(1+\left(\frac{\mathcal{R}}{3\times10^3\ \mathrm{GV}}\right)^{0.6}\right)\ \mathrm{g/cm^2}\), valid for \(4\ \mathrm{GV}\lesssim\mathcal{R}\lesssim10^3\ \mathrm{GV}\).
- Current LHC antinuclei yields are only reported for \(|y|<0.5\) (ALICE 2019, 2020, 2021).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Blum (2017) which demonstrated the impact of \(p_t\) and \(\sqrt{s}\) coverage for antiproton, antideuteron, and antihelium-3 production but did not exhibit rapidity dependence. It also complements Korsmeier (2018) which discussed antiproton production kinematics including rapidity but did not consider antideuterons or antihelium-3. The results enable LHC experiments to prioritize rapidity coverage targets for upcoming measurements, while highlighting the need for fixed-target programs like AMBER (Seitz 2023) and NA61/SHINE (Marcinek 2022) to address the \(\sqrt{s}\) extrapolation issue.
