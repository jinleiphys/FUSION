# Heavy Quark Momentum Broadening in a Non-Abelian Plasma away from Thermal Equilibrium

> We perform classical-statistical real-time lattice simulations to compute real-time spectral functions and momentum broadening of quarks in the presence of strongly populated non-Abelian gauge fields. Based on a novel methodology to extract the momentum broadening for relativistic quarks, we find that the momentum distribution of quarks exhibit interesting non-perturbative features as a function of time due to correlated momentum kicks it receives from the medium, eventually going over to a diffusive regime. We extract the momentum diffusion coefficient for a mass range describing charm and bottom quarks and find sizeable discrepancies from the heavy quark limit.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first extraction of the heavy quark momentum diffusion coefficient for relativistic quarks in a non-equilibrium, highly occupied non-Abelian plasma, finding sizeable deviations from the infinite-mass (static) limit for charm- and bottom-quark mass ranges, which the abstract glosses over by not specifying that the deviations are most pronounced for masses corresponding to charm quarks and that the infinite-mass limit is only recovered for masses m/Q ≥ 6.

## Method

The authors perform classical-statistical real-time lattice simulations of SU(2) gauge fields evolved via the classical Yang-Mills equations on a 256³ lattice with spacing a_s = 0.5/Q, reaching a self-similar scaling regime at times Qt = 500–1500. Quark wavefunctions are then evolved quantum-mechanically in the background of these classical gauge fields using an O(a²)-improved Wilson-Dirac Hamiltonian, and the momentum distribution dN/d³q is obtained by projecting the evolved wavefunctions onto free Dirac spinors. The momentum diffusion coefficient κ is extracted from the late-time linear slope of the second moment ⟨q²⟩ of the momentum distribution.

## Key numbers

* For m/Q = 1.2 (charm-like), κ = 37.13(5) × 10⁻⁵ Q³ at Qt = 1500.
* For m/Q = 2.1 (charm-like), κ = 16.54(4) × 10⁻⁵ Q³ at Qt = 1500.
* For m/Q = 4.2 (bottom-like), κ = 8.64(2) × 10⁻⁵ Q³ at Qt = 1500.
* For m/Q = 6.0 (bottom-like), κ = 7.24(1) × 10⁻⁵ Q³ at Qt = 1500.
* For m/Q = 12.0, κ = 6.20(2) × 10⁻⁵ Q³ at Qt = 1500.
* In the infinite-mass limit (from color-electric field correlator), κ = 5.227(3) × 10⁻⁵ Q³.
* The medium-induced mass (m_eff − m_bare) is ~0.08 Q for light quarks, decreasing to less than a tenth of that value for m_bare = 2.1 Q.
* The damping factor γ falls from ~20% of the effective mass for light quarks to less than ~10% for heavier quarks.

## Context

This work extends classical-statistical lattice studies of heavy quark momentum broadening in non-equilibrium plasmas, previously performed only in the infinite-mass limit by Boguslavski et al. (2020), by treating quarks as relativistic particles via the Dirac equation. It also contrasts with perturbative QCD calculations of κ in thermal equilibrium by Laine (2009) and with lattice QCD results in equilibrium by Altenkort et al. (2023), Brambilla et al. (2020), and others, which find significantly lower κ values. The results enable more realistic modeling of heavy quark dynamics in the pre-equilibrium stages of heavy-ion collisions, where a highly occupied gluonic plasma is believed to exist, and suggest that significant momentum broadening and elliptic flow buildup for charm and bottom quarks may occur already in these early stages.
