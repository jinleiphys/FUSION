# Quantum computing for effective nuclear lattice model

> Nuclear lattice effective field theory has become an important framework for quantum many-body calculations in nuclear physics, yet its classical implementation remains increasingly challenging for more general interactions and larger systems. In this work, we develop a quantum-computing framework for a three-dimensional nuclear lattice model. We construct a variational quantum eigensolver framework and systematically compare the Jordan-Wigner and Gray code encodings. Our analysis shows that for the few-body systems considered here, Gray code combined with symmetry reduction yields a substantially more compact qubit representation. Based on this framework, we perform numerical studies for $^{2}\mathrm{H}$, $^{3}\mathrm{H}$, and $^{4}\mathrm{He}$ on finite lattices. The calculated ground-state energies exhibit a clear approach toward the corresponding experimental binding energies as the lattice size increases. These results provide a proof-of-principle foundation for future quantum simulations of nuclear many-body problems.

# Quantum computing for effective nuclear lattice model

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that for few-body nuclear systems (deuteron, triton, helium-4) on finite three-dimensional lattices, Gray code encoding combined with spatial symmetry reduction yields a substantially more compact qubit representation than Jordan-Wigner encoding, but this advantage is explicitly limited to the low-particle-number regime where the reduced Hilbert space remains small.

## Method
The authors constructed a three-dimensional cubic lattice Hamiltonian with an exact kinetic term and contact two-body and three-body interactions, using coupling constants fitted at lattice size L=6 to match experimental deuteron and triton binding energies. They implemented a variational quantum eigensolver (VQE) framework using a hardware-efficient ansatz with R_y gates, comparing Jordan-Wigner encoding (which requires 4L^3 qubits) against Gray code encoding applied to a symmetry-reduced Hilbert space constrained by translational invariance (zero-momentum sector) and cubic point group symmetry (A_1g irreducible representation). All variational protocols were emulated classically using the PennyLane framework with JAX-based automatic differentiation and Adam/L-BFGS-B optimizers.

## Key numbers
- Gray code encoding requires only 9 qubits at L=6 for n=3 particles, compared to 648 qubits for Jordan-Wigner encoding at the same lattice size
- Two-body coupling constant: c_2 = -143.202887 (dimensionless, fitted at L=6)
- Three-body coupling constant: c_3 = 107.232753 (dimensionless, fitted at L=6)
- Lattice spacing: a = 1.160747 fm
- Experimental binding energy benchmarks: deuteron ≈ -2.22 MeV, triton ≈ -8.48 MeV, helium-4 ≈ -28.30 MeV
- VQE optimization stopping accuracy: 10^{-6} MeV
- Lattice sizes studied: L = 2, 3, 4, 5, 6 (three-dimensional cubic lattice with periodic boundary conditions)

## Context
This work extends the nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) framework, which has been applied to ground states (PhysRevLett.104.142501, Elhatisari 2024), excited states (PhysRevLett.112.102501), and scattering observables (Nature 528-111), by introducing a quantum-computing approach to address the sign problem that limits classical quantum Monte Carlo methods. The study builds on prior quantum-algorithm work for nuclear systems, including the pioneering cloud quantum computation of the deuteron (PhysRevLett.120.210501) and shell-model studies (Perez-Obiol 2023, PhysRevC.105.064317), while being among the first to systematically investigate lattice formulations with quantum encodings. The results provide a proof-of-principle foundation for future quantum simulations of nuclear many-body problems, though the current framework is restricted to classically emulated VQE calculations of few-body systems with simple contact interactions.
