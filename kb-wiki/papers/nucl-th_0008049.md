# Valence-quark distributions in the pion

> We calculate the pion's valence-quark momentum-fraction probability distribution using a Dyson-Schwinger equation model. Valence-quarks with an active mass of 0.30 GeV carry 71% of the pion's momentum at a resolving scale q_0=0.54 GeV = 1/(0.37 fm). The shape of the calculated distribution is characteristic of a strongly bound system and, evolved from q_0 to q=2 GeV, it yields first, second and third moments in agreement with lattice and phenomenological estimates, and valence-quarks carrying 49% of the pion's momentum. However, pointwise there is a discrepancy between our calculated distribution and that hitherto inferred from parametrisations of extant pion-nucleon Drell-Yan data.

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger equation model yields a pion valence-quark distribution whose low moments agree with lattice and phenomenological estimates after evolution, but there is a pointwise discrepancy between the calculated distribution and the form inferred from parametrisations of extant pion-nucleon Drell-Yan data, which may be attributable to the restricted function space used in data parametrisations.

## Method

The authors calculate the pion's valence-quark momentum-fraction probability distribution using a phenomenological Dyson-Schwinger equation model with algebraic parametrisations of the dressed-quark propagator, pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, and dressed-quark-photon vertex. The distribution is obtained by evaluating the one-dimensional integral that yields the valence-quark distribution via the hadronic tensor for deep inelastic lepton-pion scattering, using the handbag diagrams in the Bjorken limit. The valence-quark mass is determined by requiring the distribution to satisfy the valence-quark number sum rule.

## Key numbers

- Valence-quark active mass: $\check M = 0.30$ GeV
- Resolving scale: $q_0 = 0.54$ GeV $= 1/(0.37$ fm)
- At $q_0$, valence-quarks carry 71% of the pion's momentum: $\int_0^1 dx\,x\,[u_v(x;q_0)+\bar d_v(x;q_0)] = 0.71$
- Second moment at $q_0$: $\langle x^2\rangle_{q_0} = 0.18$
- Third moment at $q_0$: $\langle x^3\rangle_{q_0} = 0.10$
- After evolution to $q=2$ GeV, valence-quarks carry 49% of the pion's momentum
- At $q=2$ GeV: $\langle x\rangle = 0.24$, $\langle x^2\rangle = 0.098$, $\langle x^3\rangle = 0.049$
- Euclidean constituent-quark mass: $M^E = 0.33$ GeV
- Calculated pion decay constant: $f_\pi = 0.090$ GeV (observed: 0.092 GeV)
- Calculated pion mass: $m_\pi = 0.139$ GeV (observed: 0.138 GeV)

## Context

This work extends Dyson-Schwinger equation studies previously applied to electron-nucleon and meson-nucleon form factors (Hecht et al., 2000; Martin et al., 2000) to the calculation of parton distribution functions. The calculation contrasts with Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model approaches (Toki et al., 1998; Arriola et al., 1999; Bentz et al., 1999) that yield a flat distribution $u_v(x)=\theta(x)\theta(1-x)$ as an artefact of momentum-independent Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, and with constituent quark models (Cotanch and Miller, 1998) and instanton-liquid models (Dorokhov, 1999). The results enable improved parametrisations of pion parton distributions and can be extended to nucleon targets using models like those of Martin et al. (2000) and Reinhard et al. (1999).
