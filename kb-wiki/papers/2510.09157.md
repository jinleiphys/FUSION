# Toroidal dipole mode in nuclei

> A short review on the toroidal dipole mode (TDM) in nuclei is done. The appearance of TDM in nuclei is justified. The experimental manifestation of TDM in (e,e') reaction in 58Ni is shortly reported. The relation of TDM and pygmy E1 resonance is discussed.

# Toroidal dipole mode in nuclei

## Key claim

The paper argues that the toroidal dipole mode (TDM) is a general feature of all atomic nuclei, and that low-energy electric dipole states previously assigned as magnetic (M1) in the nucleus 58Ni are actually isoscalar toroidal excitations. The authors hedge that while their Skyrme force SVmas10 reproduces the energy spectrum and transverse form factors well, it underestimates B(E1) values, suggesting that coupling with complex configurations may be needed to fully describe the states.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Quasiparticle Random-Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculations with various Skyrme forces for the spherical nucleus 58Ni. They computed the (e,e') cross-section in Plane-Wave Born Approximation (PWBA) and compared the squared transverse form factors (TFF) at large scattering angles (up to 178.5 degrees) with experimental data from TU Darmstadt. The toroidal nature of the states at 5-10 MeV was identified by analyzing their response to the transition toroidal operator and the distributions of convective current transition densities (CTD).

## Key numbers

* The dipole states in 58Ni were identified at 5-10 MeV, with six states found in early (e,e') experiments at TU Darmstadt.
* The QRPA calculation with SVmas10 gives B(E1)=6.2 x 10^{-5} e^2 fm^2 for the 8.26-MeV state, compared to the experimental value of 18.5 x 10^{-3} e^2 fm^2 for the neighboring observed 8.24-MeV state.
* The squared TFF for QRPA states at 8.26 and 9.85 MeV were compared with experimental data for states at 8.24 and 10.04 MeV, showing reasonable agreement.
* The scattering angles used in the experimental data ranged from 92.9 degrees to 164.9 degrees (Mettner data) and up to 178.5 degrees (Reitz data).
* The typical PDR energy region is given as approximately (50-60) A^{-1/3} MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical prediction of TDM in nuclei by Semenko (1981) and subsequent studies using density-functional theory and fluid-dynamical approaches, particularly the calculations for 208Pb by Repko et al. (2013). It directly addresses the puzzle from early (e,e') experiments by Mettner (1987) and Reitz, where dipole states in 58Ni were initially misassigned as M1 excitations due to their enhanced transverse form factor slope. The results enable reinterpretation of numerous early (e,e') data for low-energy magnetic states and open the way to explore electric nuclear excitations beyond the continuity equation, with implications for astrophysical applications and the equation of state. The paper also connects nuclear TDM studies to the broader field of toroidal excitations in solid-state physics, metamaterials, and metaphotonics.
