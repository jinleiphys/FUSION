# Model dependence of isospin sensitive observables at high densities

> Within two different frameworks of isospin-dependent transport model, i.e., Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU04) and Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport models, sensitive probes of nuclear symmetry energy are simulated and compared. It is shown that neutron to proton ratio of free nucleons, pi-/pi+ ratio as well as isospin-sensitive transverse and elliptic flows given by the two transport models with their "best settings", all have obvious differences. Discrepancy of numerical value of isospin-sensitive n/p ratio of free nucleon from the two models mainly originates from different symmetry potentials used and discrepancies of numerical value of charged pi-/pi+ ratio and isospin-sensitive flows mainly originate from different isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross sections. These demonstrations call for more detailed studies on the model inputs (i.e., the density- and momentum-dependent symmetry potential and the isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross section in medium) of isospin-dependent transport model used. The studies of model dependence of isospin sensitive observables can help nuclear physicists to pin down the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy through comparison between experiments and theoretical simulations scientifically.

# Model dependence of isospin sensitive observables at high densities

## Key claim
Even when two transport models (IBUU04 and UrQMD) are tuned to use nearly identical density-dependent symmetry energies, the resulting isospin-sensitive observables (n/p ratio, pi-/pi+ ratio, and nucleonic flows) still show large discrepancies because the underlying symmetry potentials and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections differ in their momentum and isospin dependence. These model uncertainties can be comparable to or larger than the effects of the symmetry energy itself on these probes.

## Method
The authors simulated central and semi-central collisions of 197Au + 197Au at 400 MeV/nucleon beam energy using two isospin-dependent transport models: the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU04) model and the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model. Both models were configured with their "best settings" and with symmetry energies chosen to be nearly identical (x=0 in IBUU04, gamma=0.5 in UrQMD). The n/p ratio of free nucleons, pi-/pi+ ratio, and neutron-proton differences in directed flow, transverse flow, and elliptic flow were compared between the two frameworks.

## Key numbers
- The n/p ratio of free nucleons from UrQMD is 14.3% larger than that from IBUU04 in central 197Au+197Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon.
- The pi-/pi+ ratio from UrQMD is 30% larger than that from IBUU04 in the same reaction system.
- The beam energy used for all simulations is 400 MeV/nucleon.
- The freeze-out time settings are 150 fm/c in UrQMD and 40 fm/c in IBUU04.
- The projectile rapidity y_b is 0.8935 for the 400 MeV/nucleon beam.
- The symmetry energy at normal density S_0 is set to 32 MeV in the UrQMD model.
- The Fermi kinetic energy at normal density epsilon_F is approximately 38 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier efforts to constrain the high-density symmetry energy using heavy-ion collisions, which had yielded contradictory conclusions: a soft symmetry energy was indicated by fitting FOPI data (2007) with the IBUU04 model (Xiao et al., 2009), while a stiff result was obtained using the LQMD model (Feng et al., 2010). The study directly compares two widely used transport frameworks (IBUU04 and UrQMD) to quantify model uncertainties, following calls for model-dependence studies by Cozma et al. (2013). The results demonstrate that before the symmetry energy can be reliably extracted, the momentum dependence of the symmetry potential and the isospin dependence of in-medium NN cross sections must be better constrained, and that probes such as eta meson production may be needed to overcome current model uncertainties.
