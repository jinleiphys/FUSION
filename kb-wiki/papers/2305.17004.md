# Cabibbo suppressed hyperon production off nuclei induced by antineutrinos

> In this work we study the production of $Σ$ and $Λ$ hyperons in strangeness changing $ΔS = -1$ charged current interactions of muon antineutrinos on nuclear targets. At the nucleon level, besides quasielastic scattering we consider the inelastic mechanism in which a pion is produced alongside the hyperon. Its relevance for antineutrinos with energies below 2 GeV is conveyed in integrated and differential cross sections. We observe that the distributions on the angle between the hyperon and the final lepton are clearly different for quasielastic and inelastic processes. Hyperon final state interactions, modeled with an intranuclear cascade, lead to a significant transfer from primary produced $Σ$'s into final $Λ$'s. They also cause considerable energy loss, which is apparent in hyperon energy distributions. We have investigated $Λ$ production off ${}^{40}$Ar in the conditions of the recently reported MicroBooNE measurement. We find that the $Λπ$ contribution, dominated by $Σ^*(1385)$ excitation, accounts for about one third of the cross section.

## Key claim

The authors find that inelastic hyperon-pion ($Y\pi$) production, dominated by $\Sigma^*(1385)$ resonance excitation, contributes substantially to strangeness-changing antineutrino-nucleus cross sections and accounts for about one third of the $\Lambda$ production cross section under MicroBooNE conditions, contrary to earlier studies that considered only quasielastic mechanisms.

## Method

The authors compute cross sections for $\Lambda$ and $\Sigma$ hyperon production off nuclear targets via charged-current $\bar{\nu}_\mu$ interactions, combining quasielastic (QE) scattering with inelastic $Y\pi$ channels where a pion is produced alongside the hyperon. The nuclear response is treated with a local Fermi gas model, and hyperon final state interactions (FSI) are simulated using a Monte Carlo intranuclear cascade that includes $\Sigma \rightarrow \Lambda$ conversion, energy loss, and a $\Lambda$ nuclear potential of depth 30 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The $\Lambda\pi$ contribution, dominated by $\Sigma^*(1385)$ excitation, accounts for about one third of the cross section in MicroBooNE conditions on ${}^{40}$Ar.
- For $\Lambda$ production on ${}^{16}$O and ${}^{40}$Ar, QE mechanisms are clearly larger than $Y\pi$ mechanisms across the examined energy range ($E_{\bar{\nu}} \lesssim 2$ GeV).
- For $\Sigma^+$ production, $\Sigma^+ \pi$ primary production becomes gradually dominant for $E_{\bar{\nu}} > 1.2$ GeV, since no QE $\Sigma^+$ production occurs on single nucleons.
- 55-70% of produced pions are absorbed at $E_{\bar{\nu}} = 1$ GeV and 30-50% at 2 GeV for targets including ${}^{12}$C, ${}^{16}$O, ${}^{40}$Ca, ${}^{40}$Ar, and ${}^{56}$Fe.
- If trapped $\Lambda$ hyperons in the mean field potential were counted, the $\Lambda$-production cross section would increase by about 20% in ${}^{16}$O and 30% in ${}^{40}$Ar.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of quasielastic hyperon production by Singh (2006) and Sobczyk (2019) by including the inelastic $Y\pi$ channel, which had not previously been studied in nuclei. The model is applied to the recent MicroBooNE measurement (2022) of $\bar{\nu}_\mu + {}^{40}\mathrm{Ar} \rightarrow \mu^+ + \Lambda + X$, where only five $\Lambda$ events were identified. The results enable improved interpretation of upcoming higher-statistics data from MicroBooNE and the SBND detector, and could help constrain $\bar{\nu}$ contamination in neutrino beams.
