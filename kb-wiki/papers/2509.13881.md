# Nonperturbative Heavy-Flavor Transport Approach for Hot QCD Matter

> The heavy charm and bottom quarks are unique probes of the transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and its hadronization in high-energy nuclear collisions. A key challenge in this context is to embed the interactions of the heavy quarks in the expanding medium compatible with the strong-coupling nature of the QGP, and thus to unravel the underlying microscopic mechanisms. In the present work we progress toward this goal by combining recent $T$-matrix interactions for elastic scattering with an effective transport implementation of gluon radiation, and apply these in a Langevin framework in a viscous hydrodynamic evolution. Hadronization of heavy quarks is evaluated using a modern recombination model with 4-momentum conservation, supplemented with fragmentation constrained by data in proton-proton collisions. Deploying this approach to charm-hadron observables in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC yields fair agreement with experiment while also identifying areas of further systematic improvement of the simulations and its current input.

## Key claim

The paper claims that combining nonperturbative heavy-quark T-matrix interactions (constrained by recent lattice-QCD data) with an effective transport implementation of gluon radiation within a Langevin framework yields fair agreement with charm-hadron observables in Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC, while also identifying areas for systematic improvement; the hedge is that no parameter was tuned specifically to the heavy-ion data, and the description is better for the VCP potential scenario whose spatial diffusion coefficient aligns better with lattice-QCD data.

## Method

The authors combined heavy-light T-matrix interactions (solved self-consistently with light-parton spectral functions reproducing the lattice-QCD equation of state) with a diffusion-induced radiation term in the LIDO Langevin framework, using a 2+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic evolution for the bulk medium. Hadronization was evaluated using the resonance recombination model (RRM) with 4-momentum conservation, supplemented by heavy-quark effective theory fragmentation functions constrained by data in proton-proton collisions. The charm-quark transport was simulated from the hydrodynamic thermalization time through the QGP, with initial conditions from FONLL spectra and Glauber binary-collision profiles including nuclear shadowing.

## Key numbers

* Charm-quark spatial diffusion coefficient: the VCP scenario yields values that agree better with lattice-QCD data from Altenkort (2023) and HotQCD (2025) at low temperatures, while the WLC scenario gives a weaker temperature dependence.
* Hadronization temperature: T_H = 160 MeV used for the RRM implementation on the hydrodynamic hypersurface.
* Light, strange, and charm quark masses: m_q = 0.3 GeV, m_s = 0.4 GeV, m_c = 1.5 GeV; meson width Gamma_M = 0.1 GeV.
* Strangeness suppression factor in pp collisions: gamma_s = 0.7 to reproduce D_s^+ yield.
* Nuclear shadowing: integrated suppression of 25% in semi-/central Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, with a variation range of 20-30% causing up to 5% variation in the maximum of R_AA.
* D_s^+/D^0 ratio: improved over previous work by He (2019), mostly due to revised branching ratios of excited D_s^* mesons (approximately 80% branching into DK above the DK threshold).

## Context

This work extends previous heavy-flavor transport approaches by Rapp (2018) and Zhao (2023) by synthesizing microscopic HQ T-matrix interactions from Liu (2017) and Tang (2023) with the Trento plus viscous-fluid dynamics bulk evolution from Shen (2014) and Bernhard (2019), and HF transport merging elastic diffusion and radiative interactions from Ke (2018). It improves upon earlier calculations by He (2019) by using T-matrix interactions with an underlying potential constrained by recent lattice-QCD data from Altenkort (2023) rather than the internal energy, and by refining the effective transport implementation of radiation with improved interference behavior for static media. The framework enables more stringent tests of the underlying theory determining the momentum and temperature dependencies of heavy-quark interactions, particularly through comparisons to D_s and Lambda_c hadron measurements from ALICE (2021).
