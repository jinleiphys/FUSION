# The Parton Orbital Angular Momentum: Status and Prospects

> Theoretical progress on the formulation and classification of the quark and gluon orbital angular momenta (OAM) is reviewed. Their relation to parton distributions and open questions and puzzles are discussed. We give a status report on the lattice calculation of the parton kinetic and canonical OAM and point out several strategies to calculate the quark and gluon canonical OAM on the lattice.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that a complete gauge-invariant decomposition of the proton spin into quark and gluon orbital angular momentum (OAM) contributions is feasible, but the precise form depends on how the system is probed, and there remains an intrinsic ambiguity in separating total OAM into quark and gluon parts, leading to essentially two complementary decompositions (kinetic and canonical).

## Method

The authors review the theoretical formulation of quark and gluon OAM in QCD, discussing both the kinetic (Ji) decomposition and the canonical (Jaffe-Manohar) decomposition, and their gauge-invariant extensions via the Chen et al. approach and the background field method. They examine how OAM contributions relate to generalized parton distributions (GPDs), transverse-momentum distributions (TMDs), and generalized transverse-momentum dependent distributions (GTMDs), and present a status report on lattice QCD calculations of parton OAM using both the Mellin moment approach and the large-momentum effective field theory (LaMET) approach.

## Key numbers

* Quark spin contribution to the proton spin is about 25% from deep-inelastic scattering experiments (Aidala 2012)
* Gluon polarization contributes about 35% to the proton spin budget (de Florian 2014, Nocera 2014)
* In the light-front constituent quark model (LFCQM), the total quark kinetic OAM is 0.126, with u-quark contribution 0.071 and d-quark contribution 0.055; the total quark canonical OAM from GTMDs is also 0.126, but with u-quark 0.169 and d-quark -0.042 (Lorce and Pasquini 2011)
* In the light-front chiral-quark soliton model (LFχQSM), the total quark kinetic OAM is 0.069, with u-quark -0.008 and d-quark 0.077; the total quark canonical OAM from GTMDs is also 0.069, but with u-quark 0.093 and d-quark -0.023 (Lorce and Pasquini 2011)

## Context

This review extends and critically examines the theoretical framework for parton OAM following the pioneering work of Ji (1996) on the kinetic decomposition and Jaffe and Manohar (1989) on the canonical decomposition, as well as the gauge-invariant decomposition approach of Chen et al. (2008, 2009). The paper discusses open puzzles, such as the difference between canonical and kinetic OAM in quark models where gluon degrees of freedom are absent, and clarifies that the interpretation of the integrand of the Ji relation as a density of parton angular momentum is not theoretically justified. The work enables future lattice calculations of GTMDs and OAM using LaMET, and highlights the need for further investigations of Lorentz covariance, equations of motion, regularization prescriptions, and surface terms.
