# Attractors for Flow Observables in 2+1D Bjorken Flow

> We examine the capabilities of second-order Israel-Stewart-type hydrodynamics to capture the early-time behaviour of the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions. We point out that at very early times, the dynamics of the fireball is governed by the local 0+1-D Bjorken flow attractor due to the rapid expansion along the longitudinal direction. Discrepancies between hydrodynamics and kinetic theory in this far-from-equilibrium regime leads to disagreement at the level of late-time observables, such as elliptic flow. We show that rescaling the initial energy-density profile for hydrodynamics accounts for such discrepancies, restoring agreement with kinetic theory for large opacities (small shear viscosity / large system size / high energy).

## Key claim

Rescaling the initial energy-density profile for second-order Israel-Stewart-type hydrodynamics can restore agreement with kinetic theory for late-time observables such as elliptic flow, but only for large opacities (small shear viscosity / large system size / high energy); the rescaling fails when transverse expansion interrupts equilibration before the system reaches the hydrodynamic attractor.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) using a lattice Boltzmann method, and the Müller-Israel-Stewart (MIS) viscous hydrodynamics equations using the vHLLE code, for 2+1D Bjorken flow with boost-invariant initial conditions. They initialized the system at very early times (τ₀ = 10⁻⁶ R) using a realistic transverse energy-density profile for 30-40% most central Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV, and compared the time evolution of the transverse-plane energy per rapidity and elliptic flow coefficient between the two approaches. They derived a rescaling formula for the hydrodynamic initial energy density based on the different early-time attractor exponents γ of RTA and MIS hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

- Total energy per unit rapidity of the initial profile: dE⁰_⟂/dη = 1280 GeV
- Effective radius of the initial profile: R = 2.78 fm
- Initial eccentricities: ε₂ = 0.42, ε₄ = 0.21, ε₆ = 0.09
- Early-time attractor exponent γ: γ = 4/9 ≈ 0.444 for RTA; γ = (√505 - 13)/18 ≈ 0.526 for MIS hydrodynamics
- Integration constant C_∞ for the energy attractor: C_∞ ≈ 0.88 for RTA; C_∞ ≈ 0.80 for MIS hydrodynamics
- The rescaling scheme brings hydrodynamics and RTA into agreement for 4πη/s ≲ 3
- Final-state observables were measured at τ = 4R

## Context

This work extends the study of hydrodynamic attractors initiated by Heller and Spaliński (2015) to 2+1D systems with transverse expansion, going beyond the 0+1D Bjorken flow attractor. It quantifies how discrepancies between MIS hydrodynamics and kinetic theory during the pre-equilibrium phase propagate to late-time observables, and provides a rescaling prescription to correct for these discrepancies. The results enable more reliable use of viscous hydrodynamics for modeling heavy-ion collisions at LHC energies, particularly for the regime of small shear viscosity where equilibration occurs before transverse expansion becomes dominant.
