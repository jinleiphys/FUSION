# Effects of anharmonic vibration on large-angle quasi-elastic scattering of 16O+144Sm

> We study the effects of double octupole and quadrupole phonon excitations in the 144Sm nucleus on quasi-elastic 16O+144Sm scattering at backward angles. To this end, we use the coupled-channels framework, taking into account explicitly the anharmonicities of the vibrations. We use the same coupling scheme as that previously employed to explain the experimental data of sub-barrier fusion cross sections for the same system. We show that the experimental data for the quasi-elastic cross sections are well reproduced in this way, although the quasi-elastic barrier distribution has a distinct high energy peak which is somewhat smeared in the experimental barrier distribution. We also discuss the effects of proton transfer on the quasi-elastic barrier distribution. Our study indicates that the fusion and quasi-elastic barrier distributions for this system cannot be accounted for simultaneously with the standard coupled-channels approach.

## Key claim

The experimental quasi-elastic cross sections for 16O+144Sm are well reproduced by coupled-channels calculations that include double anharmonic quadrupole and octupole phonon excitations in 144Sm, but the quasi-elastic barrier distribution still shows a distinct high-energy peak that is smeared in the experimental data, and this discrepancy cannot be resolved even by including proton transfer channels.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-channels calculations using a version of the code CCFULL, incorporating coupling to the 2+ and 3- single phonon states and the 2+⊗2+, 2+⊗3-, and 3-⊗3- double phonon states of 144Sm. Anharmonicities were included via matrix elements derived from the U(5) limit of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM), with nonzero χ parameters generating quadrupole moments. Proton transfer channels were added using a macroscopic form factor proportional to the derivative of the nuclear potential, with coupling strengths fitted to reproduce experimental transfer cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energies of 144Sm: ε2 = 1.61 MeV (quadrupole phonon) and ε3 = 1.81 MeV (octupole phonon)
- Nuclear potential parameters: radius parameter r0 = 1.1 fm, diffuseness a = 0.75 fm, depth V0 = 112 MeV
- Deformation parameters: β2 = 0.13 and β3 = 0.23
- Optimum Q-values for proton transfer: Qopt(1p) = -1.79 MeV and Qopt(2p) = 0.13 MeV
- Coupling strengths for proton transfer: Ftr = 0.12 fm (one proton) and 0.16 fm (two protons)
- Calculations performed at θc.m. = 170°

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channels analysis of Hagino et al. (1997) which showed that double anharmonic phonon excitations reproduce the fusion barrier distribution for 16O+144Sm, but had not been applied to quasi-elastic scattering. It contradicts the earlier suggestion by Timmers et al. (1995) that transfer channels might explain the difference between fusion and quasi-elastic barrier distributions. The study indicates that standard coupled-channels approaches cannot simultaneously account for both barrier distributions, pointing toward dynamical effects such as couplings to deep-inelastic scattering as a possible origin, and leaves open the need for calculations incorporating such effects.
