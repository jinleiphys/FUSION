# Gluon Transport Equation in the Small Angle Approximation and the Onset of Bose-Einstein Condensation

> In this paper, we study the evolution of a dense system of gluons, such as those produced in the early stages of ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. We describe the approach to thermal equilibrium using the small angle approximation for gluon scattering in a Boltzmann equation that includes the effects of Bose statistics. In the present study we ignore the effect of the longitudinal expansion, i.e., we restrict ourselves to spatially uniform systems, with spherically symmetric momentum distributions. Furthermore we take into account only elastic scattering, i.e., we neglect inelastic, number changing, processes. We solve the transport equation for various initial conditions that correspond to small or large initial gluon phase-space densities. For a small initial phase-space density, the system evolves towards thermal equilibrium, as expected. For a large enough initial phase-space density the equilibrium state contains a Bose condensate. We present numerical evidence that such over-populated systems reach the onset of Bose-Einstein condensation in a finite time. The approach to condensation is characterized by a scaling behavior that we briefly analyze.

## Key claim

The paper provides numerical evidence that over-populated gluon systems (with initial phase-space density exceeding a critical value) reach the onset of Bose-Einstein condensation in a finite time, but only within the idealized scenario of a static, uniform system with spherically symmetric momentum distributions and purely elastic scattering, the authors explicitly neglect longitudinal expansion, inelastic number-changing processes, and the dynamics of a fully developed condensate.

## Method

The authors solve a Fokker-Planck equation derived from the Boltzmann-Nordheim transport equation in the small-angle scattering approximation for gluons, including Bose statistical factors. They consider spatially uniform, isotropic systems with no longitudinal expansion, using only elastic gluon-gluon scattering. The evolution is studied numerically for various initial conditions based on a color-glass-condensate (CGC)-inspired momentum distribution, with a smooth cutoff to avoid numerical divergences, and the integrals \(I_a\) (diffusion coefficient) and \(I_b\) (proportional to Debye screening mass) are computed self-consistently at each time step.

## Key numbers

- The critical initial occupation factor for the CGC-inspired step-function distribution is \(f_0^c \approx 0.154\); for the smooth distribution used numerically, \(f_0^c = 0.1675\).
- The over-occupation parameter for the initial CGC distribution is \(n_0 \epsilon_0^{-3/4} = f_0^{1/4} \, 2^{5/4} / (3 \pi^{1/2})\); the equilibrium value for a massless Bose gas is \(n \epsilon^{-3/4}|_{SB} \approx 0.28\).
- For the under-occupied case with \(f_0 = 0.1\), the numerical computation conserves energy to better than \(0.05\%\) over the longest evolution.
- The effective temperature at initial time is \(T^* = (1+f_0) Q_s / 3\) for the CGC distribution.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of quark-gluon plasma thermalization using the small-angle approximation (Mueller, 1999; Bjoraker, 2000) by including Bose statistical factors that allow soft mode amplification and the possibility of Bose-Einstein condensation. It is closely related to studies of condensation kinetics in massive scalar fields (Semikoz, 1995) and non-relativistic atomic gases (Lacaze, 2001), but differs by considering massless gluons with ultra-relativistic dispersion and using the small-angle approximation appropriate for QCD. The results enable future work on the kinetics of systems with a fully developed condensate and motivate studies of whether transient condensates form in heavy-ion collisions despite inelastic processes (Huang, 2013).
