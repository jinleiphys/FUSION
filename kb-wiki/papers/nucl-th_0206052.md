# Mean $p_t$ fluctuations from 2- and 4-particle correlations

> Recent results on event-by-event mean transverse momentum, $\mpt$, fluctuations in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions are briefly reviewed. We conclude that the observed fluctuations are in a rough agreement with that expected for the independent superposition of nucleon-nucleon collisions. We further discuss the possibility of the use of the forth order cumulants of the particle transverse momentum distribution in order to access the fluctuations related to collective phenomena.

## Key claim

The observed event-by-event mean transverse momentum fluctuations in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions are in rough agreement with expectations from independent superposition of nucleon-nucleon collisions, but the use of fourth-order cumulants of the particle transverse momentum distribution could access fluctuations related to collective phenomena that are not visible in two-particle correlations.

## Method

The paper reviews measurements of dynamical fluctuations in mean transverse momentum using the covariance of particle transverse momenta, quantified by the dynamical variance σ²_d (or equivalently κ₂,ₚₜ), and the related Φₚₜ measure. It then proposes using fourth-order cumulants κ₄,ₚₜ, defined as the four-particle correlation minus three times the square of the two-particle correlation, to enhance the relative contribution of collective phenomena compared to cluster-based particle production.

## Key numbers

- For central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, STAR reports σ_d/⟨p_t⟩ = 1.2 ± 0.2% (preliminary, 6% most central collisions)
- For central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, PHENIX measurements yield σ_d/⟨p_t⟩ = 1.4 ± 0.9% (averaged over centralities)
- NA49 at CERN SPS (√s_NN = 17 GeV) reports Φₚₜ = 0.6 ± 1 MeV/c for 5% most central Pb+Pb collisions in rapidity region 4.0 < y < 5.5
- CERES at CERN SPS reports Φₚₜ = 7.8 ± 0.9 MeV/c in central Pb+Au collisions at central rapidity, corresponding to σ_d/⟨p_t⟩ ≈ 1.4% (with multiplicity ~130, ⟨p_t⟩ ≈ 420 MeV/c, σ_pt,inclusive ≈ 0.270 GeV/c)
- For pp collisions at ISR, σ_d/⟨p_t⟩ ≈ 12%, which rescales to σ_d/⟨p_t⟩ ≈ 0.8% in Au+Au when scaled by multiplicity density

## Context

This work reviews and extends the analysis of mean transverse momentum fluctuations previously studied using measures such as Φₚₜ (introduced by the author and collaborators) and Δσ_pt (used by STAR). The paper builds on earlier proposals for cumulant analysis of multiplicity fluctuations by Ina (year not given) and on successful applications of four-particle azimuthal cumulants for elliptic flow measurements by the STAR Collaboration (Ollitrault and collaborators, year not given). The proposed fourth-order cumulant technique enables future experiments to distinguish genuine collective phenomena from trivial correlations due to resonance decays, jets, or independent nucleon-nucleon collisions.
