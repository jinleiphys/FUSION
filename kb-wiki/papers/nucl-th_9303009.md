# The response of non-relativistic confined systems

> We study the non-relativistic response of a 'diquark' bound by confining forces, for which perturbation theory in the interaction fails. As non-perturbative alternatives we consider the Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith theory and a summation method. We show that, contrary to the case of singular repulsive forces, the GRS theory can generally be applied to confined systems. When expressed in the GRS-West kinematic variable y, the response has a standard asymptotic limit and calculable dominant corrections of orders 1/q,1/q. That theory therefore clearly demonstrates how constituents, confined before and after the absorption of the transferred momentum and energy, behave as asymptotically free particles. We compare the GRS results with those for a summation method for harmonic and square well confinement and also discuss the convergence of the GRS series for the response in powers of 1/q.

## Key claim

The Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith (GRS) theory, which fails for singular repulsive forces, can be applied to singular attractive (confining) forces, and the asymptotic limit of the response in the GRS-West variable \(y\) is the same as for free on-shell constituents, with calculable corrections of order \(1/q\) and \(1/q^2\).

## Method

The authors study the non-relativistic response (structure function) \(S(q,\omega)\) of a two-body "diquark" system bound by confining potentials, using the GRS series expansion in powers of \(1/q\) and a separate summation method that replaces the sum over discrete excited states by an integral. They compute the first three coefficient functions \(F_0(y), F_1(y), F_2(y)\) explicitly for harmonic oscillator and infinite square well confinement, and compare the results from both methods.

## Key numbers

- For harmonic confinement, the asymptotic limit of the reduced response is \(F_0(y) = (1/\sqrt{\pi\beta^2}) \exp(-y^2/\beta^2)\), where \(\beta = (m\omega_0/2)^{1/2}\) is the inverse length parameter.
- For harmonic confinement at \(y=0\), the leading correction is \((m/q)^2 F_2(0)/F_0(0) = -\beta^2/(6q^2)\).
- For the infinite square well at \(y=0\), the leading correction is \((m/q)^2 F_2(0)/F_0(0) = \pi^2(\pi^2-4)/(16 q^2 a^2)\), where \(a\) is the well half-width.
- For the square well, the asymptotic limit is \(F_0(y) = (\pi a/2) [\cos(ay)/\gamma(ay)]^2\) with \(\gamma(ay) = (\pi/2)^2 - (ay)^2\).

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic treatment of confined systems by Greenberg (1993), who studied harmonic oscillator confinement and found that the asymptotic response in the Bjorken variable vanishes unless the quark momentum fraction equals the mass ratio. The authors show that the GRS-West variable \(y\) is preferable to the non-relativistic Bjorken variable \(x\) because it permits a power series expansion in \(1/q\) and directly yields the single-particle momentum distribution. The results enable future studies of how confinement corrections approach the asymptotic free-particle limit, and contrast with the case of singular repulsive forces studied by Weinstein and Negele (1982) and Gurvitz, Rinat, and Rosenfelder (1989), where the asymptotic limit differs from the free-particle result.
