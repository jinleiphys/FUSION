# Magnetic Moment of The $Θ^+$ Pentaquark State

> We have calculated the magnetic moment of the recently observed $Θ^+$ pentaquark in the framework of the light cone QCD sum rules using the photon distribution amplitudes. We find that $μ_{Θ^+}=(0.12\pm 0.06) μ_N$, which is quite small. We also compare our result with predictions of other groups.

# Magnetic Moment of The $\Theta^+$ Pentaquark State

## Key claim
The magnetic moment of the $\Theta^+$ pentaquark is predicted to be quite small, $|\mu_{\Theta^+}| = (0.12 \pm 0.06) \mu_N$, but this result is an absolute value and assumes the pentaquark is an isoscalar with $J = 1/2$; no assumption is made about its parity, and the formalism picks out only the state with lowest mass without knowledge of its parity.

## Method
The authors employed light cone QCD sum rules (LCQSR) using photon distribution amplitudes to calculate the magnetic moment. They considered a correlator of interpolating currents for the $I=0$ pentaquark state in an external electromagnetic field, performed operator product expansion on the light cone, applied double Borel transformation to eliminate single-pole contributions, and subtracted continuum contributions using quark-hadron duality. The sum rule for the magnetic moment was divided by the previously derived mass sum rule to extract the combination $|F_1(0) + F_2(0)|$.

## Key numbers
- $|\mu_{\Theta^+}| = (0.12 \pm 0.06) \mu_N$ (in units of nucleon magneton)
- $|\mu_{\Theta^+}| = (0.20 \pm 0.10) \, e_{\Theta^+} / (2 m_0)$ (in units of $\Theta^+$ magneton)
- Central value obtained at Borel parameter $M^2 = 2.0$ GeV$^2$ and continuum threshold $s_0 = 4.0$ GeV$^2$
- The pentaquark mass used from previous work: $M_0 = (1.56 \pm 0.15)$ GeV, corresponding to $M^2 = 2$ GeV$^2$ and $s_0 = 4$ GeV$^2$

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous QCD sum rule analysis of the $\Theta^+$ pentaquark mass (Huang et al., 2003) to calculate its magnetic moment, a fundamental observable encoding quark structure and dynamics. The result is compared with predictions from other approaches: the chiral soliton model (Kim and Praszalowicz, 2003) gave $(0.2 \sim 0.3) \mu_N$; the diquark picture of Jaffe and Wilczek (2003) yielded $\kappa_{\Theta^+} = -0.7$ for $J^P = 1/2^+$ and $-0.2$ for $J^P = 1/2^-$; and a quark model calculation by Zhao (2003) gave $0.13 \, e/(2m_0)$. The small magnetic moment predicted across different models may help distinguish theoretical approaches once experimental extraction becomes possible with accumulated events.
