# Unified approach to nuclear densities from exotic atoms

> Parameters of nuclear density distributions are derived from least-squares fits to strong interaction observables in exotic atoms. Global analyses of antiprotonic and pionic atoms show reasonably good agreement between the two types of probes regarding the average behaviour of root-mean-square radii of the neutron distributions. # Unified approach to nuclear densities from exotic atoms

## Key claim
Global analyses of antiprotonic and pionic atoms show reasonably good agreement on the average root-mean-square radii of neutron distributions, but the apparent conflict regarding the shape of the neutron distribution is attributed to the different radial sensitivities of the two probes rather than to a genuine discrepancy.

## Method
Strong interaction level shifts and widths in exotic atoms were calculated using an optical potential of the generic tρ(r) form inserted into the appropriate wave equation. Neutron density distributions were parameterized as two-parameter Fermi (2pF) distributions with three shape options (skin, halo, and average), and a linear dependence of r_n - r_p on (N-Z)/A was employed. Global least-squares fits were performed to approximately 90 data points for antiprotonic atoms from the PS209 collaboration and approximately 100 data points for pionic atoms, with the proton densities considered known from charge distributions.

## Key numbers
- For antiprotonic atoms with halo shape: γ = 0.9 ± 0.1 fm (χ² = 196 for 90 data points)
- For antiprotonic atoms with average shape: γ = 1.25 ± 0.15 fm (χ² = 198 for 90 data points)
- For pionic atoms with skin shape: γ = 1.0 ± 0.1 fm (χ² = 173 for 100 data points)
- Best-fit finite-range potential for antiprotonic atoms yields a χ² per degree of freedom of about 2
- For antiprotonic atoms, the ratio of absorption on a neutron to absorption on a proton is 0.99 ± 0.07
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, r_n - r_p = 0.18 ± 0.02 fm
- The finite range of the antiproton-nucleon interaction is 1.1 ± 0.1 fm
- From previous work by Garcia-Recio et al. (1992), γ = 1.06 ± 0.34 fm for their semi-theoretical model and γ = 0.97 ± 0.12 fm for the phenomenological model of Meirav et al. (1989)

## Context
This work extends global analyses of neutron density distributions from exotic atoms, building on prior studies of antiprotonic atoms by Trzcinska et al. (2001), Jastrzebski et al. (2004), and Friedman et al. (2005), as well as pionic atom studies by Batty et al. (1989) and Garcia-Recio et al. (1992). The functional derivative method, introduced for kaonic atoms by Friedman (2007) and applied to pionic and antiprotonic atoms by Friedman and Gal (2007), reveals that pionic atoms are sensitive to densities around 50% of central density while antiprotonic atoms probe the extreme periphery below 10% of central density. This work enables a unified description of neutron rms radii across the periodic table with γ = 1.0 ± 0.1 fm and δ = -0.035 fm, resolving the apparent shape conflict as a consequence of different radial sensitivities rather than a physical disagreement.
