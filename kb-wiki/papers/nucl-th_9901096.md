# Tests and applications of self-consistent cranking in the interacting boson model

> The self-consistent cranking method is tested by comparing the cranking calculations in the interacting boson model with the exact results obtained from the SU(3) and O(6) dynamical symmetries and from numerical diagonalization. The method is used to study the spin dependence of shape variables in the $sd$ and $sdg$ boson models. When realistic sets of parameters are used, both models lead to similar results: axial shape is retained with increasing cranking frequency while fluctuations in the shape variable $γ$ are slightly reduced.

## Key claim

The self-consistent cranking method in the interacting boson model reproduces exact dynamical moment of inertia to within a few percent for realistic Hamiltonians, but generates spurious frequency-dependent terms in the shape variable gamma that become dominant at high frequencies, meaning the method is reliable for shape evolution only at low to mid frequencies.

## Method

The authors tested self-consistent cranking by comparing cranking calculations in the sd-interacting boson model with exact results from SU(3) and O(6) dynamical symmetries and from numerical diagonalization for N=10 bosons. They then applied the method to study the spin dependence of shape variables in both the sd and sdg boson models using realistic parameter sets, solving the cranking equation by minimizing the expectation value of H minus omega times L_x with respect to the boson mean fields.

## Key numbers

- In the SU(3) limit, the exact dynamical moment of inertia is 200/3 (approximately 66.7) in units of hbar^2/MeV, and the cranking result deviates by about 5 percent
- In the O(6) limit, the exact dynamical moment of inertia is 100 hbar^2/MeV, and the cranking result shows a maximum deviation of about 10 percent
- The cranking moment of inertia in the SU(3) limit follows the 1/N expansion: J_cr = J_ex (1 + 1/(2N) + 1/(7N^2))
- The cranking moment of inertia in the O(6) limit follows: J_cr = J_ex (1 - 1/N + 1/(2N^2))
- For the sdg-IBM calculations, the quadrupole coupling strength kappa_2 was set to -20 keV and the hexadecapole coupling ratio kappa_4/kappa_2 ranged from 0 to 0.4
- The g-boson energy was set to epsilon_g = 4 N kappa_2, approximately 1 MeV

## Context

This work extends the single previous application of self-consistent cranking to the interacting boson model by Schaaser and Brink (1984-1986), who studied only the moment of inertia. The paper addresses the need for methods to study triaxial effects at high spins, which could not be handled by the 1/N expansion technique of Kuyucak and Morrison (1988-1989) that assumed axial symmetry. The results enable future work on whether triaxial effects in the IBM require three-body interactions rather than g bosons.
