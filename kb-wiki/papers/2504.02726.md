# Constraining hot and cold nuclear matter properties from heavy-ion collisions and deep-inelastic scattering

> We perform a global analysis of deep-inelastic $e+p$ scattering data from HERA and transverse energy distributions in $p+p$ and $p+\mathrm{Pb}$ collisions, alongside charged hadron multiplicities in $\mathrm{Pb}+\mathrm{Pb}$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}} = 5.02\;\mathrm{TeV}$ from ALICE. Using a saturation-based initial state model grounded in high-energy QCD, we determine the early-time non-equilibrium shear viscosity to entropy density ratio $η/s$ of the quark-gluon plasma. Our results provide new insights into the early-time transport properties of nuclear matter under extreme conditions.

## Key claim
The paper determines the early-time non-equilibrium shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s of the quark-gluon plasma by performing a global analysis that combines deep-inelastic e+p scattering data from HERA with transverse energy distributions in p+p and p+Pb collisions and charged hadron multiplicities in Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, using a saturation-based initial state model grounded in high-energy QCD.

## Method
The authors performed a Bayesian fit of the Golec-Biernat–Wüsthoff dipole model parameters (Q_s,0 and λ) to HERA inclusive γ*+p reduced cross-section data in the kinematic range 2 GeV² ≤ Q² ≤ 22 GeV² and x ≤ 0.01, using a Gaussian process emulator trained on 1000 Latin hypercube design points. They then determined a K-factor of 1.93 ± 0.04 by matching the mean transverse energy per unit rapidity from the saturation model to ALICE measurements in p+p and p+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV. Finally, they extracted η/s by calibrating a multiplicity estimator [Eq. (25)] against full KøMPøST+MUSIC+iSS+SMASH simulations for 8 η/s values in the range [0.16, 0.72] and four hydrodynamization times τ_hydro ∈ {0.4, 0.6, 0.8, 1.0} fm, then fitting the calibrated estimator to the ALICE charged hadron multiplicity in central (0–2.5%) Pb+Pb collisions.

## Key numbers
- Q_s,0 = 0.393 GeV and λ = 0.219 from the maximum a posteriori probability fit to HERA DIS data, with uniform priors [0.1, 0.8] GeV for Q_s,0 and [0.1, 0.5] for λ
- Model uncertainty of 6.6% applied to DIS cross sections to achieve reduced χ² ≈ 1.02
- K = 1.93 ± 0.04, determined from mean transverse energy per unit rapidity ⟨dE⊥/dy⟩ = 6.80 ± 0.08 GeV for p+p and 23.2 ± 0.3 GeV for p+Pb at √sNN = 5.02 TeV
- 320 full event-by-event simulations for each of five parameter sets (⟨K⟩ plus four Gaussian samples) to calibrate the multiplicity estimator
- 2^16 initial-state configurations used for the Pb+Pb multiplicity estimator
- S/N_ch fixed to 7.5 and ν_eff = 40 in the multiplicity estimator

## Context
This work extends the saturation-based modeling approach of Golec-Biernat and Wüsthoff (1998) and the IP-Glasma model of Schenke et al. (2012) by performing a combined fit across cold (DIS) and hot (heavy-ion) nuclear matter observables. The pre-equilibrium entropy production follows the framework of Giacalone et al. (2019), and the calibration procedure uses the KøMPøST pre-equilibrium evolution of Kurkela et al. (2018) matched to MUSIC hydrodynamics via the equilibrium entropy-matching method of Borghini et al. (2024). The results enable a systematic extraction of early-time QGP transport properties without running full dynamical simulations for every parameter set, and provide a bridge between parton saturation physics and hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions.
