# A method to obtain bounds on the equation of state of cold nuclear matter from imaginary chemical potentials

> The sign problem in numerical calculations of the QCD Euclidean space path integral of QCD with a chemical potential vanishes if the chemical potential is imaginary. Moreover, calculations of the partition function with imaginary chemical potentials are equivalent to calculations with Lagrange multipliers enforcing the current density. At zero temperature, Lorentz boosts allow one to deduce properties of systems with both number density and current density from properties of systems with a current density alone; this allows both upper and lower bounds to be determined for the equation of state (EOS) in the form of energy density as a function of number density.

# A method to obtain bounds on the equation of state of cold nuclear matter from imaginary chemical potentials

## Key claim

The paper introduces a method to derive both upper and lower bounds on the equation of state (EOS) of cold nuclear matter, specifically the energy density as a function of baryon number density, from QCD calculations at imaginary chemical potential, which avoid the sign problem. The bounds are valid only at zero temperature and require extrapolation from sufficiently low temperatures, rather than providing the EOS itself.

## Method

The analysis uses the Euclidean-space QCD functional integral with a Lagrange multiplier that enforces a fixed baryon current density, which is equivalent to calculations with an imaginary chemical potential. At zero temperature, Lorentz boosts relate systems with both number density and current density to systems with current density alone, allowing the derivation of inequalities that bound the energy density. The bounds depend on a free parameter β (with -1 < β < 1), and sweeping through β yields upper and lower bounds across all densities; calculations at multiple values of the Euclidean chemical potential μ^E produce more stringent constraints.

## Key numbers

- The worst-case analysis yields bounds satisfying: T_zz^E(μ^E)/3 ≤ ε_lb(n) ≤ ε(n) ≤ ε_ub(n) ≤ 3 T_zz^E(μ^E), where n = n^E(μ^E), giving less than an order of magnitude between lower and upper bounds.
- For the classical φ^4 model studied as a test case, the geometric mean of the worst-case upper and lower bounds precisely yields the true zero-temperature EOS for all densities n.
- At asymptotically large densities in the φ^4 model, the energy density scales as ε_asy/(m^4 g^2) = (3/4) (n/(m^3 g^2))^(4/3), sharing asymptotic scaling with QCD.
- The special case β^2 = 1/2 gives n_β^l = n_β^u, yielding upper and lower bounds evaluated at the same density from a single n^E value.

## Context

This work extends the approach of QCD inequalities (Cohen, 2003) and phase-quenched evaluations (Moore, 2023; Abbott, 2024) that provided only upper bounds on the free energy, and often weak ones at low densities and temperatures. The new method provides both upper and lower bounds from imaginary chemical potential calculations, which have previously been used only for analytic continuation to real chemical potentials at high temperatures (de Forcrand, 2002; Guenther, 2020). The approach enables future lattice QCD studies at low temperatures without a sign problem, and the author suggests testing the method on tractable models including the massive Thirring/sine-Gordon model, the 't Hooft model, and the Gross-Neveu model, as well as on QCD with isospin chemical potential using chiral perturbation theory.
