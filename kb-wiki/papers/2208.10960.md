# Few nucleons scattering in pionless effective field theory

> We present a comprehensive theoretical study of low-energy few nucleon scattering for systems with $A\leq 4$. To this end, we utilize pionless effective field theory, which we employ at next-to-leading order. We show that at this level the theory yields accurate predictions for the low-energy scattering parameters in all studied channels. These predictions are on par with the best experimental evaluations and the available theoretical calculations. We confirm the recent observation that a four-body force is needed at next-to-leading-order and find that for nuclear systems it only appears in a single spin-isospin channel.

## Key claim

Pionless effective field theory at next-to-leading order yields accurate predictions for low-energy scattering parameters in all studied channels with A ≤ 4, but a four-body force is required at NLO and appears only in a single spin-isospin channel (S,I = (0,0)) for nuclear systems.

## Method

The authors solve the few-body Schrödinger equation using a correlated Gaussian basis with the stochastic variational method, applying a shallow harmonic oscillator trap to extract scattering parameters via the Busch formula. LO interactions are treated non-perturbatively, while NLO range corrections are included as a single perturbative insertion using first-order perturbation theory. Low-energy constants are fitted to reproduce experimental two-body scattering lengths and effective ranges, the triton binding energy, and the ⁴He binding energy, with cutoff variation studied over λ ∈ [1,10] fm⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends previous pionless EFT scattering studies, which were predominantly limited to A ≤ 3 systems (BedHamKol98, BedKol98, Van13), to include all s-wave scattering processes with A ≤ 4. The authors confirm the recent observation by Bazak, Kirscher, and Konig (2019) that a four-body force is needed at NLO for bosonic systems, and demonstrate its necessity in the nuclear S,I = (0,0) channel. The results enable systematic NLO predictions for few-nucleon scattering observables and provide a framework for testing universal relations such as the atom-dimer scattering length formula of Skorniakov and Ter-Martirosian (1957).
