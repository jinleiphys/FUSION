# Skyrmions and Fractional Quantum Hall Droplets Unified by Hidden Symmetries in Dense Matter

> A chain of connections in compressed baryonic matter, up-to-date glaringly missing in nuclear effective field theory, between intrinsic or emergent symmetries of QCD, mesons-gluons dualities, vector meson dominance and Chern-Simons fields has recently been revealed, presaging a possible new paradigm in nuclear theory. It indicates a ubiquitous role, thus far unexplored, of hidden symmetries -- flavor-local and scale -- permeating from dilute baryonic systems to normal nuclear matter and then to compact-star matter. Here I give a brief account of the possibly "indispensable" relevance of the $η^\prime$ singular ring, a.k.a. fractional quantum Hall (FQH) droplet, to the properties of the lowest-lying vector mesons $ω$ and $ρ$, relevant to dilepton production processes, argued to be Seiberg-dual to the gluons near the chiral restoration.

# Skyrmions and Fractional Quantum Hall Droplets Unified by Hidden Symmetries in Dense Matter

## Key claim

Hidden local symmetry (HLS) for vector mesons and hidden scale symmetry, when implemented together in a nuclear effective field theory, unify the seemingly distinct topological descriptions of baryons, skyrmions for two or more flavors and fractional quantum Hall (FQH) droplets for one flavor, and this unification is essential for correctly describing vector meson properties near chiral restoration, though the argument is "somewhat speculative" and relies on the controversial notion of a genuine dilaton with an infrared fixed point.

## Method

The author constructs a scale-invariant extension of the hidden local symmetry Lagrangian (χHLS) by introducing a conformal compensator field χ (the "genuine dilaton") that transforms linearly under scale transformations, with the dilaton decay constant f_χ serving as the order parameter. The homogeneous Wess-Zumino (hWZ) term of HLS, whose coefficients are fixed by vector meson dominance (VMD), is shown to encode the connection between skyrmion and FQH droplet configurations; by dialing baryon density rather than quark masses, the system can be driven from the skyrmion regime through a topology-change density near (3-4)n_0 toward the η′ singular ring where the FQH droplet emerges. The analysis uses the GnEFT framework, which incorporates intrinsic density dependence (IDD) from QCD matching, to track how the dilaton condensate f_χ^* evolves with density and eventually vanishes at the chiral restoration point.

- Nuclear matter density: n_0 ∼ 0.16 fm⁻³
- Topology-change density (hadron-quark continuity): n_hqc ∼ (3-4) n_0
- Vector manifestation density: n_VM ≳ 25 n_0
- Chiral restoration temperature: T_c ≈ 150 MeV
- Free-space axial coupling constant: g_A = 1.276; renormalized in-medium value: g_A^eff ≈ 1
- Nucleon mass in the parity-doubling regime: m_0 ∼ (0.6-0.9) m_N over compact-star densities ∼ (6-7) n_0
- Pseudo-conformal sound speed: v_pcs²/c² ≈ 1/3
- Neutron star mass: M ≈ 2 M_⊙
- Dilaton mass relation: m_σ² ∝ (α_IRs − α_s) β_QCD′ with α_IRs − α_s = O(m_K²/m_π²)
- Quantization condition from FQH droplet: c_3 + c_4 = 2, with individual coefficients c_3 = c_4 = 1 and c_1 = 2/3

## Context

This work extends the hidden local symmetry approach of Bando, Kugo, and Yamawaki (1985) and the vector manifestation idea of Harada and Yamawaki (2003) by incorporating scale symmetry via the genuine dilaton concept of Crewther (2020). It directly builds on the FQH droplet description of the one-flavor baryon proposed by Komargodski (2018) and the subsequent unification with skyrmions via domain walls by Karasik (2020, 2021) and Kitano (2021). The paper connects these formal developments to the GnEFT framework for dense matter (reviewed by Rho, 2021) and addresses the long-standing puzzle of the missing dropping ρ mass signal in dilepton experiments, arguing that the FQH droplet structure near chiral restoration supports the "needle-in-haystack" scenario of Harada et al. (2008) rather than ruling out vector manifestation. The Cheshire Cat Principle from chiral bag models (Nielsen et al., 1985) is invoked to reconcile the two topological pictures.
