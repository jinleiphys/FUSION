# Nuclear suppression of $¤Ľ$ meson yields with large $p_T$ at the RHIC and the LHC

> We calculate $¤Ľ$ meson transverse momentum spectra in p+p collisions as well as their nuclear suppressions in central A+A collisions both at the RHIC and the LHC in LO and NLO with the QCD-improved parton model. We have included the parton energy loss effect in hot/dense QCD medium with the effectively medium-modified $¤Ľ$ fragmentation functions in the higher-twist approach of jet quenching. The nuclear modification factors of $¤Ľ$ meson in central Au+Au collisions at the RHIC and central Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC are provided, and a nice agreement of our numerical results at NLO with the ALICE measurement is observed. Predictions of yield ratios of neutral mesons such as $¤Ľ/¤Ç^0$, $¤Ľ/╬Ě$ and $¤Ľ/¤ü^0$ at large $p_T$ in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are also presented for the first time.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that the nuclear modification factor $R_{\rm AA}$ of $\phi$ mesons in central Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, computed at next-to-leading order (NLO), agrees well with ALICE data, but their NLO prediction for $R_{\rm AA}$ in central Au+Au collisions at the RHIC undershoots the available PHENIX data in the limited $p_T$ region of 4ÔÇô7 GeV, which the authors attribute to the pQCD calculation being more applicable at larger $p_T$ and to the omission of non-perturbative mechanisms such as recombination.

## Method

The authors compute $\phi$ meson transverse momentum spectra in p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV and $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV at both LO and NLO using the QCD-improved parton model with CTEQ6M parton distribution functions and $\phi$ fragmentation functions derived from a broken SU(3) model. For A+A collisions, they incorporate parton energy loss via effectively medium-modified fragmentation functions in the higher-twist approach of jet quenching, using a 3+1D ideal hydrodynamics description for the medium evolution and the EPS09s parametrization for nuclear parton distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- Jet transport parameter $\hat{q}_0 = 1.20 \pm 0.30$ GeV$^2$/fm at $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm/c for central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV (RHIC), obtained by fitting to PHENIX $\pi^0$ data.
- Jet transport parameter $\hat{q}_0 = 2.2 \pm 0.5$ GeV$^2$/fm at $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm/c for central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV (LHC), obtained by fitting to ALICE and CMS charged hadron data.
- The ratio $\hat{q}_0^{\rm LHC} / \hat{q}_0^{\rm RHIC} \approx 1.83$, which is close to the ratio of transverse hadron densities ($2.2 \pm 0.23$) between the two collision systems.
- The NLO calculation of $R_{\rm AA}$ for $\phi$ in Pb+Pb at the LHC agrees with ALICE data over the $p_T$ range 4ÔÇô20 GeV.
- In p+p collisions at the RHIC, strange quark fragmentation contributes only 5ÔÇô10% of the total $\phi$ meson yield, with gluon fragmentation dominating across a wide $p_T$ range up to $\sim 20$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous NLO calculations of $\pi^0$, $\eta$, and $\rho^0$ suppression in heavy-ion collisions (Chen et al., 2010, 2011; Dai et al., 2015, 2017) to the $\phi$ meson, which is unique because its production is dominated by gluon fragmentation rather than light quark fragmentation. The study provides the first pQCD-based predictions for $\phi$ meson yields in A+A collisions at both RHIC and LHC energies, and demonstrates that yield ratios such as $\phi/\pi^0$, $\phi/\eta$, and $\phi/\rho^0$ in A+A collisions do not converge to their p+p values at high $p_T$, unlike the ratios among light-quark-dominated mesons ($\eta/\pi^0$, $\rho^0/\pi^0$). This enables a new probe of the color-charge sensitivity of jet quenching in the quark-gluon plasma.
