# Collective dynamics in heavy and light-ion collisions -- II) Determining the origin of collective behavior in high-energy collisions

> Exploiting the first measurements of the same ion species in OO collisons at RHIC and LHC, we propose an observable to distinguish whether collective behavior builds up through a hydrodynamic expansion of a strongly interacting QGP or few final state re-scatterings. Our procedure allows to disentangle the effects of the initial state geometry and the dynamical response mechanism on anisotropic flow. We validate its ability to discriminate between systems with different interaction rates using results from event-by-event simulations in kinetic theory.

# Collective dynamics in heavy and light-ion collisions, II) Determining the origin of collective behavior in high-energy collisions

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the observable W, defined as the ratio of logarithmic differences in flow cumulants and transverse energy between collisions of the same ion species at RHIC and LHC, can distinguish whether collective flow arises from hydrodynamic expansion of a strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma or from few final-state rescatterings, provided that the initial state geometry is sufficiently similar between the two collision energies.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event simulations using the Boltzmann equation in conformal relaxation time approximation (RTA) for oxygen-oxygen (OO) collisions at RHIC (200 GeV) and LHC (7 TeV), as well as for lead-lead (PbPb) and gold-gold (AuAu) collisions. Initial states were generated using the T R ENTo model with 6000 pre-generated nucleon configurations of 16O nuclei from quantum Monte Carlo calculations. Simulations were run at five values of shear viscosity (4π η/s = 0.3, 0.5, 1.0, 1.5, 3.0), plus ideal hydrodynamics and opacity-linearized limits, to validate that the observable W tracks the logarithmic derivative of the flow response curve κ(γ̂).

## Key numbers

- The first-order Pade approximation for the flow response uses κ_LO = 0.201 (linear opacity limit) and κ_id = 0.547 (ideal hydrodynamic limit)
- The second-order Pade fit parameters are a = 0.217(31) and b = 0.845(73)
- The observable W transitions from W = 0 (ideal hydrodynamics) to W = 1 (noninteracting limit)
- The inverse function approximating γ̂ from W is γ̂ ≈ 2.5 × (1 − W)^0.78 / W for 0.05 ≲ W ≲ 0.9
- The opacity range scanned across all systems was γ̂ ∼ 1.5–40
- The scaling factor between the universal κ(γ̂) curve from previous work and the current data is 0.93

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on the applicability of hydrodynamics in hadronic collisions (Ambrus 2022) and is accompanied by a companion paper (Ambrus 2024) providing further setup details. The observable builds on the observation by Bhalerao (2011) that ratios of cumulants can eliminate response coefficients, and on work by Giacalone (2016) showing equivalence of v2{6}/v2{4} and ε2{6}/ε2{4}. The approach exploits the first measurements of oxygen-oxygen collisions at both RHIC and LHC to disentangle initial state geometry from dynamical response, enabling direct experimental tests of whether small systems behave as ideal fluids or weakly interacting systems.
