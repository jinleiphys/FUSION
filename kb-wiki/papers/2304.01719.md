# Investigation of the cause of the discrepancies between calculated running sums for nuclear matrix elements of two-neutrino double-$β$ decay

> A qualitative difference in the running sum for the nuclear matrix element of the two-neutrino double-$β$ decay of $^{136}$Xe was found four years ago between quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) and shell model calculations. The former result has large increase and decrease with respect to the excitation energy of the intermediate state, and the latter one is an almost monotonically and mildly increasing function. My QRPA calculations independently of the above one do not have a remarkable decrease. This discrepancy is a serious problem affecting the reliability of calculations of the neutrinoless double-$β$ decay, and the cause was unknown. I perform several relevant test calculations and make an analytical consideration to find the cause, which is found to be in the strength of the attractive interactions. The possible major local decrease in the running sum is also explained analytically. The interactions of my QRPA calculation are appropriate in terms of the strength, thus the almost monotonic behavior is reasonable.

## Key claim

The cause of the qualitative discrepancy between running sums for the two-neutrino double-beta decay nuclear matrix element of 136Xe, where some calculations show large increase and decrease while others show nearly monotonic behavior, is found to be the strength of the attractive residual interactions, not the difference between quasiparticle random-phase approximation and shell model methods themselves.

## Method

The author performed test calculations using the quasiparticle random-phase approximation with Skyrme interactions (SkM* and SGII parameter sets) and contact pairing interactions, examining the effects of continuum single-particle states via different cylindrical box sizes (20, 10, and 8 fm), suppressing forward or backward amplitudes of high angular-momentum orbitals (0g9/2 and 0h9/2), and artificially enhancing the isoscalar proton-neutron pairing interaction strength. An analytical treatment using a separable interaction model in the random-phase approximation was also carried out to explain the sign inversion mechanism of the Gamow-Teller transition matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The maximum variation of the author's QRPA results when suppressing 0g9/2 and 0h9/2 amplitudes was only 5%, compared to a factor of 4.5 variation in the corresponding shell model calculations of Horoi (2013).
- With the isoscalar proton-neutron pairing interaction strength G^IS_pn = -280.0 MeVfm^3 (close to the QRPA breaking point), the nuclear matrix element was 61% of the normal result (G^IS_pn = -55.0 MeVfm^3).
- The binding energy per nucleon of 136Xe with SkM* was 8.415 MeV (experimental: 8.396 MeV), while with SGII it was 8.603 MeV; the SGII nuclear matrix element was 57% of the SkM* value.
- The GT- strength sum S_GT- was 85.145 and GT+ strength sum S_GT+ was 1.139 for the normal calculation; with G^IS_pn = -280.0 MeVfm^3, S_GT- = 84.725 and S_GT+ = 0.718 (a 37% reduction in GT+ strength).

## Context

This work addresses a discrepancy first reported by Ganioglu (2019) between the QRPA calculations of Simkovic (2018) and shell model calculations of Menendez (2009) for the 136Xe two-neutrino double-beta decay running sum. The author's own QRPA calculations (Terasaki, 2019) showed monotonic behavior, and additional shell model work by Horoi (2013) also found non-monotonic behavior when including 0g9/2 and 0h9/2 orbitals. The finding that interaction strength, not the choice of QRPA versus shell model, is the essential cause enables more reliable predictions for neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements, which are needed for determining the effective Majorana neutrino mass from future experimental data.
