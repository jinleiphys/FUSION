# Coupled-channels study of the $π^{-}p \to ηn$ process

> The reaction $π^{-}p \to ηn$ is investigated within a dynamical coupled-channels model of meson production reactions in the nucleon resonance region. The meson baryon channels included are $πN$, $ηN$, $πΔ$, $σN$, and $ρN$. The non-resonant meson-baryon interactions of the model are derived from a set of Lagrangians by using a unitary transformation method. One or two excited nucleon states in each of $S$, $P$, $D$, and $F$ partial waves are included to generate the resonant amplitudes. Data of $π^{-}p \to ηn$ reaction from threshold up to a total center-of-mass energy of about 2 GeV are satisfactorily reproduced and the roles played by the following nine nucleon resonances are investigated: $S_{11}(1535)$, $S_{11}(1650)$, $P_{11}(1440)$, $P_{11}(1710)$, $P_{13}(1720)$, $D_{13}(1520)$, $D_{13}(1700)$, $D_{15}(1675)$, and $F_{15}(1680)$. The reaction mechanism as well as the predicted $ηN$ scattering length are discussed.

## Key claim

The reaction $\pi^{-}p \to \eta n$ is satisfactorily reproduced from threshold up to about 2 GeV center-of-mass energy using a dynamical coupled-channels model, but the extracted $\eta N$ scattering length and the roles of individual resonances depend on how the $\rho N$ couplings to the $S_{11}$ resonances are treated, as demonstrated by the two models (A and B) which both fit the data but differ in those couplings.

## Method

The authors employed a dynamical coupled-channels model based on a Hamiltonian formulation, including the $\pi N$, $\eta N$, $\pi \Delta$, $\sigma N$, and $\rho N$ channels. Non-resonant interactions were derived from tree-level Lagrangians using a unitary transformation method, and one or two excited nucleon states in each of the $S$, $P$, $D$, and $F$ partial waves were included to generate resonant amplitudes. The model parameters were fitted to 294 differential cross-section data points for $\pi^{-}p \to \eta n$ from threshold up to $W \approx 2$ GeV, while fixing most parameters to values previously determined from $\pi N$ elastic scattering analysis by Julia-Diaz et al. (2007).

## Key numbers

- Reduced $\chi^2$ per data point: 2.03 for Model A, 1.94 for Model B, compared to 6.94 using parameters from Julia-Diaz et al. (2007) and 2.44 from the GWU/SAID group (Arndt et al., 2006)
- Predicted $\eta N$ scattering length from Model B: $a_{\eta N} = (0.30 + i 0.18)$ fm
- Bare mass of $S_{11}(1535)$: 1809 MeV (Model A) and 1808 MeV (Model B)
- Bare mass of $S_{11}(1650)$: 1901 MeV (Model A) and 1861 MeV (Model B)
- Data base includes 1508 differential and 98 total cross-section data points from 1964-1980, plus recent Crystal Ball data from Prakhov et al. (2005)
- Systematic uncertainties: 6% for Prakhov et al. data, 11.4% for Deinet et al. data, 10-14% for Richards et al. data

## Context

This work extends the dynamical coupled-channels model developed by Julia-Diaz et al. (2007) for $\pi N$ elastic scattering to the $\pi^{-}p \to \eta n$ reaction, providing a framework that differs from K-matrix approaches used by the Giessen Group (Feuster, 1998; Penner, 2002), the Bonn Group (Anisovich, 2005; Sarantsev, 2005), and the Zagreb Group (Batinic, 1995-1997). The results enable future coupled-channels studies of $\gamma p \to \eta p$ photoproduction, where understanding hadronic final-state interactions is essential for extracting resonance properties from electromagnetic data.
