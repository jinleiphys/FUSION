# Axion cooling of neutron stars. II. Beyond hadronic axions

> We study the axion cooling of neutron stars within the Dine-Fischler-Srednicki-Zhitnitsky (DFSZ) model, which allows for tree level coupling of electrons to the axion {and locks the Peccei-Quinn charges of fermions via an angle parameter}. This extends our previous study [Phys. Rev. D 93, 065044 (2016)] limited to hadronic models of axions. We explore the two-dimensional space of axion parameters within the DFSZ model by comparing the theoretical cooling models with the surface temperatures of a few stars with measured surface temperatures. It is found that axions masses $m_a\ge 0.06$ to 0.12 eV can be excluded by x-ray observations of thermal emission of neutron stars (in particular by those of Cas A), the precise limiting value depending on the angle parameter of the DFSZ model. It is also found that axion emission by electron bremsstrahlung in neutron star crusts is negligible except for the special case where neutron Peccei-Quinn charge is small enough, so that the coupling of neutrons to axions can be neglected.

## Key claim

Axion masses in the range \(m_a \ge 0.06\) to 0.12 eV can be excluded by X-ray observations of thermal emission from neutron stars, specifically the compact central object in Cassiopeia A, but the precise limiting value depends on the angle parameter \(\cos^2\beta\) of the DFSZ model and on the envelope composition (iron versus light elements).

## Method

The author performed numerical cooling simulations of 1.4 \(M_\odot\) neutron stars using the NSCool code, extended to include axion emission processes from both hadrons (nucleon bremsstrahlung and Cooper pair-breaking-formation) and electrons (electron bremsstrahlung in the crust) within the DFSZ model. The two-dimensional parameter space of the DFSZ model was explored by varying the axion decay constant \(f_a\) (four values: \(f_{a7} = 5, 10, 15, 20\)) and the PQ charge parameter \(\cos^2\beta\) (five values: 0.0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75, 1.0), and comparing the resulting cooling tracks to observed surface temperatures of three neutron stars (Cas A, PSR B0656+14, and Geminga) for both iron and light-element envelope compositions.

## Key numbers

* Axion masses \(m_a \ge 0.06\) to 0.12 eV are excluded by X-ray observations of thermal emission from neutron stars, with the precise limit depending on the DFSZ angle parameter \(\cos^2\beta\).
* For an iron envelope (\(\eta = 0\)), axion decay constant values \(f_{a7} = 5\) (corresponding to \(m_a \approx 0.12\) eV) exclude all \(\cos^2\beta\) values except \(\cos^2\beta = 0.0\); \(f_{a7} = 10\) (\(m_a \approx 0.06\) eV) excludes \(\cos^2\beta \ge 0.5\); \(f_{a7} = 15\) excludes \(\cos^2\beta \ge 0.75\); and \(f_{a7} = 20\) is consistent with all \(\cos^2\beta\) values.
* For a light-element envelope (\(\eta = 1\)), \(f_{a7} = 5\) excludes \(\cos^2\beta \ge 0.25\); \(f_{a7} = 10\) excludes \(\cos^2\beta = 1.0\); and \(f_{a7} = 15\) and 20 are consistent with all \(\cos^2\beta\) values.
* The Cas A central compact object has a surface temperature \(T = 2.0 \pm 0.18 \times 10^6\) K at an age of 320 years.
* PSR B0656+14 has a surface temperature \(T_w = (6.5 \pm 0.1) \times 10^5\) K at a characteristic age of \(1.1 \times 10^2\) kyr.
* Geminga has a surface temperature \(T_w = (5.0 \pm 0.1) \times 10^5\) K at a characteristic age of \(3.4 \times 10^2\) kyr.
* The axion mass is related to the decay constant by \(m_a = 0.6\ \text{eV} / f_{a7}\), where \(f_{a7} = f_a / (10^7\ \text{GeV})\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study (Sedrakian 2016) which was limited to the hadronic KSVZ axion model, by implementing the DFSZ model that allows tree-level coupling of axions to electrons. The results are complementary to studies of Cas A cooling by Leinson (2014) and Hamaguchi (2018), and to the analysis of the hot CCO HESS J1731-347 by Beznogov (2018). The findings enable future observational campaigns to further constrain axion properties, particularly through improved measurements of neutron star surface temperatures and through analysis of the putative fast cooling of Cas A.
