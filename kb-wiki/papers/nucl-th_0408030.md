# The $e^+e^-$ pair production in relativistic ions collision and its correspondence to electron scattering

> It is shown that the processes of electron(positron) scattering and $e^+e^-$ pair production in the Coulomb fields of two relativistic ions, can be expressed through the amplitudes of lepton scattering off the ion Coulomb field via Watson expansion. We obtained the compact expressions for these amplitudes valid in the high energy limit and discuss the crossing symmetry relations among the considered processes.

# The $e^+e^-$ pair production in relativistic ions collision and its correspondence to electron scattering

## Key claim
The authors show that the amplitudes for electron scattering and $e^+e^-$ pair production in the Coulomb fields of two relativistic ions can be expressed through lepton scattering amplitudes via a Watson expansion, but contrary to earlier claims, the crossing symmetry procedure cannot be used to derive the pair production amplitude from the electron scattering amplitude beyond the Born approximation due to discontinuities in the amplitude function.

## Method
The authors represent the amplitudes for electron scattering and lepton pair production in an arbitrary external electromagnetic field using a common function $T(x_2,x_1)$ that obeys an integral equation. For the two-center problem (two ions with charge numbers $Z_1, Z_2$), they expand $T$ as an infinite Watson series in terms of single-center amplitudes $T^{(1)}$ and $T^{(2)}$. They then solve for $T^{(1)}$ and $T^{(2)}$ in the high-energy limit where the Lorentz factor $\gamma = E/M \to \infty$, obtaining compact expressions using lightcone coordinates and impact parameters $\vec b_1, \vec b_2$ for the ions.

## Key numbers
- The Lorentz factor of colliding ions is taken to infinity: $\gamma = E/M \to \infty$, defining the high-energy limit used throughout.
- The fine structure constant is defined as $\alpha = e^2/(4\pi)$, which sets the coupling strength for the QED interactions considered.
- The single-center amplitude $T^{(1)}$ contains a term with an integral over $k_+$ from $-\infty$ to $\infty$, and $T^{(2)}$ contains an analogous integral over $k_-$ from $-\infty$ to $\infty$.

## Context
This work addresses the long-standing problem of accounting for final-state interactions in $e^+e^-$ pair production in the Coulomb fields of two relativistic ions, which has been investigated by many authors (references 1-12 in the paper). The authors specifically critique earlier results by SW, BM, and ERSG (2000s) that claimed a simple phase-factor solution for the amplitude, and they disagree with the conclusion by ERG and BGK (2000s) that crossing symmetry is violated beyond the Born approximation. Instead, they argue that the earlier works made two errors: omitting higher terms of the Watson expansion and incorrectly applying crossing symmetry. This work clarifies that the problem of final-state interaction to all orders in $\alpha$ remains unsolved even in QED, and it sets the stage for a deeper investigation that the authors promise to present elsewhere.
