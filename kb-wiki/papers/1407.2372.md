# Hadron mass scaling near the s-wave threshold

> The influence of a two-hadron threshold is studied for the hadron mass scaling with respect to some quantum chromodynamics parameters. A quantum mechanical model is introduced to describe the system with a one-body bare state coupled with a single elastic two-body scattering. The general behavior of the energy of the bound and resonance state near the two-body threshold for a local potential is derived from the expansion of the Jost function around the threshold. It is shown that the same scaling holds for the nonlocal potential induced by the coupling to a bare state. In p or higher partial waves, the scaling law of the stable bound state continues across the threshold describing the real part of the resonance energy. In contrast, the leading contribution of the scaling is forbidden by the nonperturbative dynamics near the s-wave threshold. As a consequence, the bound state energy is not continuously connected to the real part of the resonance energy. This universal behavior originates in the vanishing of the field renormalization constant of the zero-energy resonance in the s wave. A proof is given for the vanishing of the field renormalization constant, together with a detailed discussion.

## Key claim

The scaling of hadron mass near a two-body threshold depends critically on the partial wave: for \(p\) or higher partial waves, the bound state energy scales linearly with the variation of the bare mass and continues smoothly across the threshold as the real part of the resonance energy, whereas for the \(s\)-wave, the leading linear contribution is forbidden by the vanishing of the field renormalization constant at zero energy, resulting in a quadratic scaling and no continuous connection between bound state and resonance.

## Method

A quantum mechanical coupled-channel Hamiltonian is introduced, consisting of a one-body bare state coupled to a single elastic two-body scattering channel. The bound state channel is eliminated via the Feshbach method to obtain an effective nonlocal potential. The near-threshold behavior of the eigenenergy is derived by expanding the Jost function around zero momentum for a general local potential, and the same scaling is shown to hold for the nonlocal potential induced by the coupling to the bare state. The field renormalization constant \(Z\) is computed from the derivative of the self-energy, and its vanishing at the \(s\)-wave threshold is proven using the analytic properties of the self-energy integral, the effective range expansion, and the pole counting argument.

## Key numbers

* For \(l=0\) (s-wave), the binding energy scales as \(E_h = -\tilde{F}_0 \, \delta M^2\) for a small decrease of the bare mass \(\delta M\), where \(\tilde{F}_0 = F_0 / \bar{M}_0^2\) and \(F_0 = (\alpha_0')^2 / (2\mu \gamma_0^2)\).
* For \(l \neq 0\) (p-wave and higher), the binding energy scales as \(E_h = \tilde{F}_l \, \delta M\) for \(\delta M > 0\), and the real part of the resonance energy scales as \(\text{Re}\, E_h = \tilde{F}_l \, \delta M\) for \(\delta M < 0\), with \(\tilde{F}_l = F_l / \bar{M}_0\) and \(F_l = \alpha_l' / (2\mu \beta_l)\).
* For \(l \neq 0\), the imaginary part of the resonance energy scales as \(\text{Im}\, E_h \propto (\delta\lambda)^{l+1/2}\).
* The field renormalization constant at zero binding energy vanishes for the s-wave: \(Z(0) = 0\), as shown by the divergence of the derivative of the self-energy \(\Sigma'(-B) \propto g_0^2 \pi / (2\sqrt{B})\) in the limit \(B \to 0\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of near-threshold hadron dynamics, particularly the behavior of states like the \(H\) dibaryon which appears as a stable bound state in heavy quark mass lattice QCD simulations (Inoue 2010, Beane 2010) but becomes a resonance near the \(\Lambda\Lambda\) threshold at the physical point (Shanahan 2011). The scaling laws derived here contrast with naive expectations that the bound state energy scaling continues smoothly across the threshold; the s-wave case instead exhibits a universal quadratic scaling due to the vanishing of the field renormalization constant, a result related to the compositeness theorem of Weinberg (1965). The findings enable a model-independent connection between the hadron mass scaling in QCD parameters and the structure (elementary vs. composite) of near-threshold states, and clarify the role of the zero-energy resonance in the s-wave.
