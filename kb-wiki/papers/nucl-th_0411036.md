# Rescattering effects and two-step process in kaon photoproduction on the deuteron

> Kaon photoproduction on the deuteron is investigated by considering YN and KN rescattering and the two-step process gamma d --> pi NN --> KYN. A strong enhancement in the total cross section is found from the two-step process. YN rescattering has remarkable effects in the inclusive and exclusive cross section, while the effect of KN rescattering is much less important.

## Key claim
The two-step process γd → πNN → KYN produces a strong enhancement in the total cross section for kaon photoproduction on the deuteron, while YN rescattering has remarkable effects in inclusive and exclusive cross sections; however, the effect of KN rescattering is much less important and is described as negligible.

## Method
The authors solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the YN rescattering amplitude in a partial wave decomposition, using the elementary operator of Mart and Bennhold (2000) and the Bonn model deuteron wave function. The KN rescattering was evaluated directly using a rank-1 separable potential for V_KN, and the two-step πK process was calculated analogously to the KN rescattering. The exclusive cross section was computed in the deuteron rest frame with proper antisymmetrization.

## Key numbers
- Total cross section for γd → KYN is shown as a function of photon energy E_γ (figure only, no explicit numerical values given in text).
- Exclusive cross section for Λ-channels was calculated at kaon momentum 870 MeV/c and for Σ-channels at 810 MeV/c, at photon energy 1.3 GeV, as a function of hyperon angle θ'_Y.
- Inclusive cross section is shown as a function of kaon momentum p_K at photon energies 1.14 GeV and 1.3 GeV (figure only, no explicit numerical values given in text).

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations by Yamamura et al. (1999), who studied YN rescattering for K⁺ channels using the Nijmegen YN potential, by adding the two-step πK process and KN rescattering. It also relates to Li et al. (1992), who extracted elementary cross sections from deuteron targets for neutron channels, and to other recent calculations by Kerbikov (2001) and by Maxwell (2004) on YN rescattering and pion-mediated processes. The results enable more complete modeling of kaon photoproduction on deuterium targets for comparison with experimental data from facilities like ELSA and CEBAF.
