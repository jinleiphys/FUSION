# Wounded quarks in A+A, p+A, and p+p collisions

> We explore predictions of the wounded quark model for particle production and properties of the initial state formed in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. The approach is applied uniformly to A+A collisions in a wide collision energy range, as well as for p+A and p+p collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC). We find that generically the predictions from wounded quarks for such features as eccentricities or initial sizes are close (within 15\%) to predictions of the wounded nucleon model with an amended binary component. A larger difference is found for the size in p+Pb system, where the wounded quark model yields a smaller (more compact) initial fireball than the standard wounded nucleon model. The inclusion of subnucleonic degrees of freedom allows us to analyze p+p collisions in an analogous way, with predictions that can be used in further collective evolution. The approximate linear dependence of particle production in A+A collisions on the number of wounded quarks, as found in previous studies, makes the approach based on wounded quarks natural. Importantly, at the LHC energies we find approximate uniformity in particle production from wounded quarks, where at a given collision energy per nucleon pair similar production of initial entropy per source is needed to explain the particle production from p+p collisions up to A+A collisions. We also discuss the sensitivity of the wounded quark model predictions to distribution of quarks in nucleons, distribution of nucleons in nuclei, and to the quark-quark inelasticity profile in the impact parameter. In our procedure, the quark-quark inelasticity profile is chosen in such a way that the experiment-based parametrization of the proton-proton inelasticity profile is properly reproduced. The parameters of the overlaid multiplicity distribution is fixed from p+p and p+Pb data.

## Key claim

The wounded quark model provides an approximately uniform description of particle production from p+p to A+A collisions at LHC energies, with the ratio of charged hadron multiplicity to number of wounded quarks being roughly independent of centrality and colliding system, but this scaling is only approximate and shows deviations at RHIC energies (about 30% difference between p+p and A+A bands) and some centrality dependence even at LHC.

## Method

The authors implement a Monte Carlo Glauber model where three constituent quarks are distributed inside each nucleon according to an exponential radial distribution (Eq. 5), with quark-quark inelastic collisions described by a Gaussian profile in impact parameter (Eq. 6). The quark distribution parameters and quark-quark cross section are fitted at each collision energy to reproduce the experimental nucleon-nucleon total inelastic cross section and inelasticity profile from the Barger-Phillips parametrization. An overlaid negative binomial (or gamma) distribution is convolved with the wounded quark distribution to account for multiplicity fluctuations, with parameters fixed from p+p and p+Pb data.

## Key numbers

- Predictions from wounded quarks for eccentricities and initial sizes are within 15% of predictions from the wounded nucleon model with an amended binary component
- For p+Pb collisions, the wounded quark model yields a smaller (more compact) initial fireball than the standard wounded nucleon model
- At LHC energies, the average number of charged hadrons per wounded quark per unit pseudorapidity is roughly similar across p+p, p+A, and A+A systems
- At RHIC energy of 200 GeV, the p+p point for dNch/dη/QW is about 30% higher than the band for A+A collisions
- The wounded quark model gives larger ellipticity than wounded nucleons, with the effect reaching about 15% at peripheral collisions
- For the ratio ε3/ε2 in most central collisions, both models give approximately 1.1
- The κ parameter of the negative binomial distribution is 0.54 for the wounded quark model and 0.9 for the wounded nucleon model in p+Pb fits
- The Gaussian smearing parameter is σ=0.2 fm for wounded quarks and σ=0.4 fm for wounded nucleons

## Context

This work extends the wounded quark model approach of Eremin and Voloshin (2003) and the PHENIX Collaboration studies (Adler 2013, Adare 2015) by applying it uniformly to A+A, p+A, and p+p collisions, with careful fitting of quark distributions and inelasticity profiles to reproduce proton-proton scattering data. The results are compared to the standard wounded nucleon model with binary component (Kharzeev 2000) and to the quark-diquark model of Bialas (2006, 2007). The study enables more realistic initial conditions for hydrodynamic modeling of small systems (p+p and p+A) and addresses the long-standing problem of the knee structure in U+U collisions at RHIC.
