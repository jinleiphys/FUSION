# A Framework for Systematic Study of QCD Vacuum Structure I: Kolmogorov Entropy and the Principle of Chiral Ordering

> In this series of articles we describe a systematic approach to studying QCD vacuum structure using the methods of lattice gauge theory. Our framework incorporates four major components. (i) The recently established existence of space-time order at all scales (fundamental structure) observed directly in typical configurations of regularized path-integral ensembles. (ii) The notion of scale-dependent vacuum structure (effective structure) providing the means for representing and quantifying the influence of fluctuations at various scales on physical observables (phenomena). (iii) The unified description of gauge and fermionic aspects of the theory which facilitates a high level of space-time order in the path-integral ensembles. (iv) The strict ``Bottom-Up'' approach wherein the process of identifying the vacuum structure proceeds inductively, using the information from valid lattice QCD ensembles as the only input. In this work we first elaborate on the meaning of the notion of space-time order in a given configuration which is conceptually at the heart of the path-integral approach to vacuum structure. It is argued that the algorithmic complexity of binary strings associated with coarse-grained descriptions of the configuration provides a relevant quantitative measure. The corresponding ensemble averages define the ranking of different lattice theories at given cutoff by the degree of space-time order generated via their dynamics. We then introduce the set of local transformations of a configuration, chiral orderings, in which the transformed gauge connection represents an effective matrix phase acquired by chiral fermion when hopping over a given link. It is proposed that chiral orderings facilitate the evolution in the set of actions which increases the degree of space-time order while preserving the physical content of the theory, and should thus be used in the search for the fundamental QCD vacuum structure. The relation to renormalization group ideas is discussed, and the first step in general formulation of effective lattice QCD realizing the notion of scale-dependent vacuum structure is given.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that QCD vacuum structure should be studied via a "Bottom-Up" approach using lattice QCD ensembles as the sole input, rather than relying on ad hoc proposals for collective degrees of freedom. The key hedge is that this framework is presented as a conceptual proposal and set of conjectures, not as rigorously proven theorems, with the authors explicitly stating that most statements "might never be proved."

## Method

The author develops a framework incorporating four components: (1) the existence of space-time order at all scales observed in typical lattice configurations, (2) scale-dependent vacuum structure (effective structure) quantifying how fluctuations at various scales influence observables, (3) unified description of gauge and fermionic aspects, and (4) a strict "Bottom-Up" inductive approach using only information from valid lattice QCD ensembles. The paper introduces algorithmic complexity of binary strings from coarse-grained descriptions as a quantitative measure of space-time order, and defines "chiral orderings" as local transformations where the transformed gauge connection represents an effective matrix phase acquired by a chiral fermion when hopping over a given link.

## Key numbers

* No specific numerical results are presented in this paper; it is a conceptual and methodological framework paper.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (Horvath 2002, 2003, 2005) which observed space-time order in topological charge density configurations from lattice QCD, and contrasts with the "Top-Down" approach where collective degrees of freedom (instantons, magnetic monopoles, center vortices) are proposed a priori. The framework enables systematic identification of QCD vacuum structure without relying on ad hoc guesses, and connects to renormalization group ideas for defining scale-dependent effective structure. The paper is the first in a series intended to develop a general theoretical and practical framework for studying QCD vacuum structure.
