# The role of proton-antiproton regeneration in the late stages of heavy-ion collisions

> We investigate the long-standing question of the effect of proton-antiproton annihilation on the (anti-)proton yield, while respecting detailed balance for the 5-body back-reaction for the first time in a full microscopic description of the late stages of heavy-ion collisions. This is achieved by employing a stochastic collision criterion in a hadronic transport approach (SMASH), which allows to treat arbitrary multi-particle reactions. It is used to account for the regeneration of (anti-)protons via $5π\rightarrow p\bar{p}$. Our results show that a back-reaction happens for a fraction of 15-20\% of all annihilations. Within a viscous hybrid approach Au+Au/Pb+Pb collisions from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3$ GeV$-5.02$ TeV are investigated and the quoted fraction is independent of the beam energy or centrality of the collision. Taking the back-reaction into account results in regeneration of half of the (anti-)proton yield that is lost due to annihilations at midrapidity. We also find that, concerning the multiplicities, treating the back-reaction as a chain of 2-body reactions is equivalent to a single 5-to-2 reaction.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including the 5-body back-reaction $5\pi \rightarrow p\bar{p}$ via detailed balance in a hadronic transport approach regenerates 40-50% of the (anti-)proton yield lost to annihilation at midrapidity, but full regeneration is not achieved for any system studied. The fraction of annihilations that have a corresponding back-reaction is 15-20%, independent of beam energy or centrality.

## Method

The authors employ the SMASH-vHLLE hybrid approach, using SMASH for initial conditions and the hadronic afterburner, and vHLLE for 3+1D viscous hydrodynamics with shear viscosity $\eta/s=0.1$ and bulk viscosity $\zeta/s=0.05$. Proton-antiproton annihilation and regeneration are treated via two methods: a stochastic collision criterion that directly implements the $5 \leftrightarrow 2$ reaction, and a resonance treatment that models the process as a chain of 2-body reactions through intermediate resonances ($p\bar{p} \rightarrow h_1 \rho \rightarrow 5\pi$). The stochastic method uses the $\sigma_{2\rightarrow5}$ cross section equal to the $p\bar{p}$ inelastic cross section, assuming multi-pion production saturates the inelastic channel.

## Key numbers

- Back-reaction occurs for 15-20% of all $p\bar{p}$ annihilations, stable across all collision energies from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3$ GeV to 5.02 TeV and centralities 0-5%, 20-30%, and 40-50%
- At midrapidity ($|y|<0.5$), 40-50% of the (anti-)proton yield lost to annihilation is regenerated
- For the full $4\pi$ multiplicity, 20-30% of the $p\bar{p}$ pairs lost to annihilation are regenerated
- Systems studied: Au-Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=39$ GeV and 200 GeV; Pb-Pb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3$ GeV, 2.76 TeV, and 5.02 TeV
- The switching temperature for hydrodynamics corresponds to an energy density $\epsilon_{\mathrm{crit}}=0.5$ GeV/fm$^3$

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of baryon-antibaryon annihilation in heavy-ion collisions (Becattini 2012, Steinheimer 2012) that did not include the backward reaction, and addresses the debate on whether detailed balance significantly affects yields (Rapp 2000, Pan 2012). It improves upon prior transport calculations (Cassing 2001, Seifert 2017, Seifert 2018) that used only $3 \leftrightarrow 2$ reactions rather than the full $5 \leftrightarrow 2$ process. The results enable quantitative extraction of QGP transport coefficients (JETSCAPE 2020) that rely on proton yields as a basic bulk observable, and demonstrate that non-equilibrium effects in the hadronic afterburner prevent the proton yield from corresponding to a single chemical freeze-out temperature.
