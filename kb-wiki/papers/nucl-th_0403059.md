# Spin polarized states in nuclear matter with Skyrme effective interaction

> The possibility of appearance of spin polarized states in symmetric and strongly asymmetric nuclear matter is analyzed within the framework of a Fermi liquid theory with the Skyrme effective interaction. The zero temperature dependence of the neutron and proton spin polarization parameters as functions of density is found for SkM$^*$, SGII (symmetric case) and SLy4, SLy5 (strongly asymmetric case) effective forces. By comparing free energy densities, it is shown that in symmetric nuclear matter ferromagnetic spin state (parallel orientation of neutron and proton spins) is more preferable than antiferromagnetic one (antiparallel orientation of spins). Strongly asymmetric nuclear matter undergoes at some critical density a phase transition to the state with the oppositely directed spins of neutrons and protons while the state with the same direction of spins does not appear. In comparison with neutron matter, even small admixture of protons strongly decreases the threshold density of spin instability. It is clarified that protons become totally polarized within a very narrow density domain while the density profile of the neutron spin polarization parameter is characterized by the appearance of long tails near the transition density.

## Key claim

In symmetric nuclear matter, the ferromagnetic (parallel spin) state is thermodynamically preferred over the antiferromagnetic (antiparallel spin) state at all densities where both solutions exist, but this conclusion depends on the specific Skyrme parametrization used (SkM* or SGII). In strongly asymmetric nuclear matter, only the antiparallel spin ordering appears, and even a small proton admixture (as low as 5%) dramatically lowers the threshold density for spin instability compared to pure neutron matter.

## Method

The authors used a Fermi liquid theory with Skyrme effective interactions to derive self-consistent equations for the ferromagnetic and antiferromagnetic spin order parameters at zero temperature. They solved these equations numerically for symmetric nuclear matter using SkM* and SGII forces, and for strongly asymmetric nuclear matter (isospin asymmetry parameters α = 0.95, 0.9, 0.8) and neutron matter (α = 1) using SLy4 and SLy5 forces, determining the density dependence of neutron and proton spin polarization parameters and comparing free energy densities of different spin states.

## Key numbers

- For symmetric nuclear matter with SkM* force: FM order parameter appears at density ≈ 2ρ₀ (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³), total FM polarization at ≈ 2.7ρ₀; AFM order parameter appears at ≈ 3.3ρ₀, total AFM polarization at ≈ 4.5ρ₀
- For symmetric nuclear matter with SGII force: FM order parameter appears at ≈ 2.75ρ₀, total FM polarization at ≈ 3.9ρ₀; AFM order parameter appears at ≈ 5ρ₀, total AFM polarization at ≈ 7.2ρ₀
- For neutron matter with SLy4 potential: critical density for spin polarization ≈ 0.59 fm⁻³
- For neutron matter with SLy5 potential: critical density for spin polarization ≈ 0.58 fm⁻³
- For asymmetric nuclear matter with α = 0.95 using SLy4 and SLy5: spin polarized state arises at ≈ 0.38 fm⁻³; protons become totally polarized at ≈ 0.41 fm⁻³ (SLy4) and ≈ 0.40 fm⁻³ (SLy5); neutrons become totally polarized at ≈ 1.05 fm⁻³ (SLy4) and ≈ 1.02 fm⁻³ (SLy5)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of spin polarization in nuclear matter using Skyrme forces that calculated magnetic susceptibility to find instability thresholds (Vidana et al., 2001; Rios et al., 2003), by directly solving for the order parameters and comparing free energies of different spin configurations. The results contrast with calculations using realistic NN potentials: Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations with Nijmegen II and Reid93 potentials (Vidana et al., 2002) found no ferromagnetic transition in neutron matter up to 7ρ₀, and calculations with NSC97e potential (Vidana et al., 2003) predicted no spin polarization in asymmetric nuclear matter up to 1.2 fm⁻³. The findings enable further study of whether Skyrme forces overestimate spin instability compared to realistic interactions, and have implications for neutron star magnetic field generation and supernova neutrino transport.
