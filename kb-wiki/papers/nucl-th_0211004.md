# Two-proton emission induced by electron scattering

> We present a model to describe the emission of two protons in electron scattering experiments. The process is induced by one-body electromagnetic operators acting together with short-range correlations, and by two-body Delta currents. The model includes all the diagrams containing a single correlation function. The sensitivity of the cross section to the details of the correlation function is studied by using realistic and schematic correlations. Results for the 16O nucleus are presented.

## Key claim

The model shows that (e,e'2p) cross sections are sensitive to the details of short-range correlations (SRC), but this sensitivity is only accessible through quantitative comparison with experimental data; qualitative features such as the shape of angular distributions are not sensitive to SRC details, and the large theoretical uncertainties, especially from two-body Δ-current parameters, prevent these experiments from being the definitive tool for determining SRC characteristics.

## Method

The authors model two-proton emission from ¹⁶O induced by electron scattering using a correlated many-body state built from a Slater determinant multiplied by a product of two-body scalar correlation functions f(r). The transition matrix element is expanded to first order in h = 1 − f², producing two-point and three-point diagrams, and the calculation includes both one-body electromagnetic operators acting with short-range correlations and two-body Δ-currents. Cross sections are computed for various final states of the residual ¹⁴C nucleus, with the kinematics fixed except for the detection angle of one emitted proton.

## Key numbers

- Cross sections are calculated for the ¹⁶O(e,e'2p)¹⁴C process, with the residual nucleus in 0⁺ and 1⁺ states reached by emitting protons from the p levels of ¹⁶O.
- The V8 correlation function has a deeper minimum than the G and S3 correlations, and both V8 and S3 overshoot the asymptotic value of 1 in the region between r = 1 and 2 fm.
- Cross sections obtained with the gaussian correlation are larger than those from the other correlations.
- The 0⁺ states are rather insensitive to the two-body currents, while the 1⁺ state is dominated by the Δ excitation even at relatively low excitation energy.
- Lowering the minimum of a schematic box correlation does not produce a large effect; inserting a part that overshoots the asymptotic value reduces the cross section, as does reducing the size of the box.

## Context

This work extends a series of studies by the same group on short-range correlation effects in electromagnetically induced nuclear excitations, including inclusive electron scattering for discrete low-lying states (Mokeev et al., 2000) and the quasi-elastic peak (Co' et al., 2001), as well as one-nucleon emission by real photons (Anguiano et al., 2002) and electrons (Mokeev et al., 2001). The authors note that in those earlier calculations SRC effects were hidden by the large uncorrelated one-body operator contributions, so two-nucleon emission is investigated as a way to eliminate those contributions. The correlation functions G and S3 come from Fermi Hypernetted Chain calculations with semi-realistic interactions (Arias de Saavedra et al., 1996), while V8 is the scalar part of a state-dependent correlation from FHNC calculations with the V8' Argonne interaction plus three-body Urbana IX interaction (Fabrocini et al., 2000). The paper concludes that (e,e'2p) experiments should be considered one element of a puzzle, together with elastic, inclusive, and one-nucleon emission experiments, that the authors are trying to describe in a unique theoretical framework.
