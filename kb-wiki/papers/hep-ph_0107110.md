# Understanding $B\to D^{*-}N\bar{N}$ and Its Implications

> The CLEO Collaboration recently reported the observation of the $B^0\to D^{*-}p\bar{n}$ mode at a rate only a factor of 4--5 lower than $B^0\to D^{*-}ρ^+$ and $D^{*-}π^+$. We try to understand this with a factorization approach of current produced nucleon pairs. The baryon weak vector current form factors are related by isospin rotation to nucleon electromagnetic form factors. By using $G^{p,n}_M$ measured from $e^+e^-\to \bar{N}N$ and $p\bar{p}\to e^+e^-$ processes, assuming factorization of the $B^0\to D^{*-}$ transition and the $p\bar n$ pair production, we are able to account for up to 60% of the observed rate. The remainder is argued to arise from the axial current. The model is then applied to B decays to other mesons plus $p\bar{n}$ modes and $D^{*-}$ plus strange baryon pairs.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the observed branching fraction of \(B^0\to D^{*-}p\bar{n}\) can be understood via a factorization approach where the nucleon pair is directly produced by the weak current, with the vector current contribution accounting for 50%–60% of the measured rate and the remainder attributed to the axial current, rather than relying on a simple pole model with unreliable meson-nucleon couplings.

## Method

The authors factorize the three-body decay amplitude into the product of the \(B^0\to D^{*-}\) transition matrix element and the weak current matrix element for \(p\bar{n}\) pair production. They relate the weak vector form factors of the nucleon to the isovector electromagnetic form factors via isospin rotation, using experimental data on \(G_M^{p,n}\) from \(e^+e^-\to \bar{N}N\) and \(p\bar{p}\to e^+e^-\) processes. The vector current contribution to the branching fraction is computed by fitting the time-like magnetic form factor data with parameterizations constrained by perturbative QCD counting rules, and the axial current contribution is estimated by scaling from the vector case using a dipole fit to the space-like axial form factor.

## Key numbers

- Measured branching fraction: \(\text{Br}(B^0\to D^{*-}p\bar{n}) = (14.5^{+3.4}_{-3.0}\pm 2.7)\times 10^{-4}\) (CLEO Collaboration).
- Vector current contribution (light-front model, assuming \(|G_E|=|G_M|\)): \(\text{Br}_V^{\text{LF}} = (7.14^{+0.69}_{-0.65})\times 10^{-4} (a_1/0.74)^2\), accounting for about 50% of the observed rate.
- Vector current contribution (BSW model, assuming \(|G_E|=|G_M|\)): \(\text{Br}_V^{\text{BSW}} = (8.72^{+0.85}_{-0.79})\times 10^{-4} (a_1/0.86)^2\), accounting for about 60% of the observed rate.
- Vector current contribution (light-front model, assuming \(|G_E^n|=0\)): \(\text{Br}_V^{\text{LF}} = (8.96^{+1.02}_{-0.94})\times 10^{-4} (a_1/0.74)^2\).
- Vector current contribution (BSW model, assuming \(|G_E^n|=0\)): \(\text{Br}_V^{\text{BSW}} = (10.94^{+1.25}_{-1.15})\times 10^{-4} (a_1/0.86)^2\).
- Estimated axial-to-vector ratio at threshold: \(r^2(4m_N^2)\sim 0.35\), giving total branching fractions compatible with experiment (e.g., \(\text{Br}^{\text{BSW}}\sim 14.77\times 10^{-4}\) for the \(|G_E^n|=0\) case).
- The differential decay rate peaks at \(m_{p\bar{n}} \simeq 2.14\) GeV, near the \(p\bar{n}\) threshold of 1.88 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Dunietz (1998) that \(B\to D N\bar{N}X\) decays could be sizable and the observation by the CLEO Collaboration (Anderson et al., 2001) of the \(B^0\to D^{*-}p\bar{n}\) mode at a rate only a factor of 4–5 lower than the two-body modes \(B^0\to D^{*-}\rho^+\) and \(D^{*-}\pi^+\). The authors improve upon the simple pole model of Hou et al. (2001) by using a data-driven factorization approach that avoids unreliable time-like meson-nucleon couplings. The results enable future extraction of time-like nucleon axial form factors from \(B^0\to D^{*-}p\bar{n}\) data and motivate searches for charmless baryonic modes such as \(B\to \eta^\prime \Lambda \bar{p}\) and \(B\to \gamma \Lambda \bar{p}\), which could probe triple-product CP and T violation.
