# A novel explanation of charmonium-like structure in $e^+e^-\to Žł(2S)ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-$

> We first present a non-resonant description to charmonium-like structure $Y(4360)$ in the $Žł(2S)ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-$ invariant mass spectrum of the $e^+e^-\to Žł(2S)ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-$ process. The $Y(4360)$ structure is depicted well by the interference effect of the production amplitudes of $e^{+} e^{-} \to Žł(2S) ŽĆ^+ ŽĆ^-$ via the intermediate charmonia $Žł(4160)/Žł(4415)$ and direct $e^+e^-$ annihilation into $Žł(2S)ŽĆ^+ ŽĆ^-$. This fact shows that $Y(4360)$ is not a genuine resonance, which naturally explains why $Y(4360)$ was only reported in its hidden-charm decay channel $Žł(2S)ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-$ and was not observed in the exclusive open-charm decay channel or $R$-value scan.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the $Y(4360)$ structure observed in $e^+e^- \to \psi(2S)\pi^+\pi^-$ is not a genuine resonance, but rather an interference effect between the direct $e^+e^-$ annihilation into $\psi(2S)\pi^+\pi^-$ and the production via intermediate charmonia $\psi(4160)$ and $\psi(4415)$. This non-resonant explanation is presented as an extension of the same authors' previous work on $Y(4260)$ (Chen 2010), and the authors note that the same mechanism cannot describe the $Y(4660)$ structure also seen in the same data.

## Method

The authors model the $e^+e^- \to \psi(2S)\pi^+\pi^-$ cross section using two production amplitudes: a direct non-resonant amplitude parameterized by an exponential form factor, and amplitudes from intermediate $\psi(4160)$ and $\psi(4415)$ charmonia decaying via scalar $\sigma$ and $f_0(980)$ states into $\psi(2S)\pi^+\pi^-$. The total amplitude includes three fitted phase angles ($\phi_1$, $\phi_2$, $\phi_s$) to account for unknown hadronic loop effects, and the model is fitted to the BaBar and Belle experimental data for the $\psi(2S)\pi^+\pi^-$ invariant mass spectrum.

## Key numbers

- The fitted parameter $a$ (controlling the direct amplitude form factor) is $4.9248 \pm 0.4105$ GeV$^{-2}$.
- The fitted coupling constant $g_{NoR}$ for direct production is $0.0074 \pm 0.0009$ GeV$^{-1}$.
- The fitted phase angles are $\phi_1 = 2.6770 \pm 0.1260$ rad, $\phi_2 = 1.8509 \pm 0.3010$ rad, and $\phi_s = 0.0003 \pm 0.2467$ rad.
- The model reproduces the $Y(4360)$ structure in the $\psi(2S)\pi^+\pi^-$ invariant mass spectrum around $\sqrt{s} \approx 4.36$ GeV, but cannot describe the $Y(4660)$ structure.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous non-resonant explanation for $Y(4260)$ in $e^+e^- \to J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-$ (Chen 2010) to the $Y(4360)$ structure in $e^+e^- \to \psi(2S)\pi^+\pi^-$. It contradicts earlier interpretations of $Y(4360)$ as a conventional charmonium state (e.g., Ding 2007, Li and Chao 2009, Segovia 2008), a charmonium hybrid (Kalashnikova 2008), a $D^*\bar{D}_1$ molecular state (Close 2009, Close 2010), or a baryonium state (Qiao 2007). The paper enables future study of other charmonium-like structures in hidden-charm processes, particularly the $Y(4660)$ structure and the suggestive rise at 4260 MeV in $e^+e^- \to h_c\pi^+\pi^-$ reported by CLEO-c (Mitchell 2011).
