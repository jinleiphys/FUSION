# Strongly Coupled Plasma Liquids

> This paper intends to review some of the prominent properties of strongly coupled classical plasmas having in mind the possible link with the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions. Thermodynamic and transport properties of classical liquid-state one-component plasmas are described and features of collective excitations are presented.

## Key claim

This review argues that the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) created in heavy-ion collisions shares key properties with conventional strongly coupled classical electromagnetic plasmas, but the authors explicitly restrict their analysis to the one-component plasma (OCP) model and classical systems, noting that a full description of sQGP would require a multicomponent quantum plasma model.

## Method

The paper reviews thermodynamic and transport properties of strongly coupled classical one-component plasmas using both theoretical approaches (Hyper-Netted-Chain method, Quasi-Localized Charge Approximation) and molecular dynamics simulations employing the particle-particle particle-mesh (P3M) method for handling long-range Coulomb and Yukawa interactions. Transport coefficients are obtained from equilibrium simulations via Green-Kubo relations and from non-equilibrium molecular dynamics simulations, while collective excitations are studied through Fourier analysis of density and current fluctuation spectra.

## Key numbers

- Liquid phase in 3D Coulomb OCP exists for coupling parameter values Gamma ≤ 175, with crystallization into bcc lattice at Gamma ≈ 175
- In 2D, crystallization into hexagonal lattice occurs at Gamma ≈ 137
- Self-diffusion coefficient follows approximate relation D* = 2.95 Gamma^(-1.34) for Coulomb OCP
- Shear viscosity exhibits a minimum at Gamma ≈ 20 (Vieillefosse and Hansen) or Gamma ≈ 8 (Wallenborn and Baus)
- Longitudinal mode in Coulomb OCP peaks near plasma frequency ω₀ at k → 0; for Yukawa potential with κ > 0 the mode becomes quasi-acoustic with ω/ω₀ ∝ k^(1/2)
- Transverse mode exhibits acoustic dispersion with a low-k cutoff even for weak screening

## Context

This review extends the pioneering work of Brush, Sahlin and Teller (1966) and the systematic studies of Hansen and coworkers on one-component plasma properties, connecting them to the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma discovered at RHIC. The paper enables future work on multicomponent plasma models (such as ionic mixtures and charged particle bilayers) that would be needed for a more complete description of sQGP, while noting that large-scale quantum simulations remain a decade away.
