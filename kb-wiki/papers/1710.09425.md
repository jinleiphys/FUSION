# Decoding the phase structure of QCD via particle production at high energy

> Recent studies based on non-perturbative lattice Monte-Carlo solutions of Quantum Chromodynamics, the theory of strong interactions, demonstrated that at high temperature there is a phase change from confined hadronic matter to a deconfined quark-gluon plasma where quarks and gluons can travel distances largely exceeding the size of hadrons. The phase structure of such strongly interacting matter can be decoded via analysis of particle abundances in high energy nuclear collisions within the framework of the statistical hadronization approach. The results imply quark-hadron duality at and experimental delineation of the location of the phase boundary of strongly interacting matter.

## Key claim

The chemical freeze-out temperature extracted from statistical hadronization analysis of particle yields in high-energy nuclear collisions coincides with the pseudo-critical temperature from lattice QCD, implying that hadronization occurs at the QCD phase boundary and supporting a quark-hadron duality concept, but only for collision energies where the baryo-chemical potential is small (μB < 300 MeV) and the transition is a crossover rather than a first-order phase transition.

## Method

The authors performed a statistical hadronization analysis of measured hadron yields (rapidity densities dN/dy at midrapidity) from central heavy-ion collisions across multiple accelerator facilities (AGS, SPS, RHIC, LHC), fitting the thermal parameters temperature T_CF, baryo-chemical potential μB, and fireball volume V using a hadron resonance gas partition function that includes all known hadrons and resonances with an excluded volume correction (radius r0 = 0.3 fm). The resulting freeze-out points were compared directly to lattice QCD predictions for the pseudo-critical temperature as a function of μB, using recent LQCD results from Bazavov (2014) and Borsanyi (2012).

## Key numbers

- T_CF = 156.5 ± 1.5 MeV for central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energy (√s_NN = 2.76 TeV), with μB = 0.7 ± 3.8 MeV and V = 5280 ± 410 fm³
- LQCD pseudo-critical temperature at vanishing μB: 154 ± 9 MeV (Bazavov 2014) and 156 ± 9 MeV (Borsanyi 2010, 2013), in excellent agreement with T_CF
- T_nuclei = 159 ± 5 MeV extracted from light nuclei yields, consistent with T_CF
- Limiting temperature T_CF^lim = 158.4 ± 1.4 MeV from fit to the five highest-energy data points
- Energy density at deconfinement transition from LQCD: about 1 GeV/fm³
- Normal nuclear matter density: n0 = 0.16/fm³
- Deuteron binding energy: 2.2 MeV; hypertriton Λ separation energy: 130 ± 30 keV

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization approach pioneered by Cleymans (1992), Braun-Munzinger (1994, 2003), and Becattini (1995) by incorporating recent precision LHC data from the ALICE collaboration and making quantitative comparisons with lattice QCD results from Bazavov (2014) and Borsanyi (2010, 2013). The finding that T_CF equals T_c within errors supports the earlier proposal by Braun-Munzinger (1998) and Stock (1999) that chemical freeze-out occurs at hadronization, and enables future predictions of particle yields at even higher energies (e.g., Future Circular Collider) with estimated precision better than 15%. The results also constrain the location of the QCD critical end point, which remains an outstanding problem being addressed experimentally (Luo 2017) and theoretically (Karsch 2013).
