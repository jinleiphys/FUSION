# Novel mechanism for electric quadrupole moment generation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We present the spatial distributions of electromagnetic fields ($\bf E$ and $\bf B$) and electromagnetic anomaly $ \bf E \cdot B$ in Au+Au collisions at the RHIC energy $\sqrt{s}$=200 GeV based on a multi-phase transport model. A dipolar distribution of $\bf E \cdot B$ is observed in non-central collisions. We find that the coupling of the $\bf E \cdot B$ dipole and magnetic field $\bf B$ can induce an electric quadrupole moment which can further lead to the difference in elliptic flows between positive charged particles and negative charged particles through final interactions. The centrality dependence of the density of $\bf E \cdot B$ is similar to the trend of the slope parameter $r$ measured from the difference in elliptic flows between positive pions and negative pions by the STAR collaboration. Therefore, the novel mechanism for electric quadrupole moment generation can offer a new interpretation of the observed charge-dependent elliptic flow of pions, but without the formation of chiral magnetic wave.

## Key claim
The paper argues that a dipolar spatial distribution of the electromagnetic anomaly **E·B** in non-central heavy-ion collisions, when coupled with the magnetic field **B**, can generate an electric quadrupole moment that leads to charge-dependent elliptic flow of pions, offering an alternative to the chiral magnetic wave (CMW) interpretation. The authors hedge that this mechanism works "without the formation of chiral magnetic wave" and does not require a finite baryon density (μ_V ≠ 0), unlike the CMW mechanism.

## Method
The authors used the AMPT model (string-melting version) to simulate Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV. They calculated the electromagnetic fields **E** and **B** at the field point r = (0,0,0) at t = 0 using the Liénard-Wiechert potentials summed over all charged protons, then computed **E·B** on an event-by-event basis. The density of ⟨**E·B**⟩ in the transverse plane was obtained by averaging over events and dividing by the collision area, determined via four different methods for defining the nuclear radius.

## Key numbers
- The magnetic field magnitude in non-central collisions is estimated as eB ∼ m_π² ∼ 10¹⁸ to 10¹⁹ Gauss, based on energies available at RHIC and LHC.
- The spatial distributions of ⟨B_x⟩, ⟨E_x⟩, ⟨B_y⟩, and ⟨E_y⟩ are shown for impact parameters b = 0, 5, 10, and 12 fm in Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV.
- The zone-averaged density of ⟨**E·B**⟩ in the lower transverse plane (y < 0 fm) is compared with the slope parameter r measured by STAR as a function of N_part (number of participating nucleons).
- For single events at b = 10 fm, the **E·B** distribution remains negative in the positive-y half-plane and positive in the negative-y half-plane, with fluctuations.

## Context
This work extends the study of electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions by Deng (2012) and Zhao (2017) to include the spatial distribution of **E·B**. It directly addresses the interpretation of charge-dependent elliptic flow measurements by the STAR collaboration (Adamczyk 2015), which had been attributed to the chiral magnetic wave (CMW) proposed by Kharzeev (2010) and Burnier (2011). The paper offers a new mechanism that does not require the CMW, and suggests that the energy dependence of the slope parameter r could be tested in the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC to distinguish between the two mechanisms.
