# Stability of Matter-Antimatter Molecules

> We examine the stability of matter-antimatter molecules by reducing the four-body problem into a simpler two-body problem with residual interactions. We find that matter-antimatter molecules with constituents (m1+, m2-, m2bar+, m1bar-) possess bound states if their constituent mass ratio m1/m2 is greater than about 4. This stability condition suggests that the binding of matter-antimatter molecules is a rather common phenomenon. We evaluate the binding energies and eigenstates of matter-antimatter molecules (mu+ e-)-(e+ mu-), (pi+ e-)-(e+ pi-), (K+ e-)-(e+ K-), (p e-)-(e+ pbar), (p mu-)-(mu+ pbar), and (K+ mu-)-(mu+ K-), which satisfy the stability condition. We estimate the molecular annihilation lifetimes in their s states.

## Key claim

Matter-antimatter molecules of the form $(m_1^+, m_2^-, \bar{m}_2^+, \bar{m}_1^-)$ possess bound states only when the constituent mass ratio $m_1/m_2$ exceeds approximately 4, a condition the authors argue makes such binding "a rather common phenomenon" rather than a rare exception.

## Method

The authors reduce the four-body Coulomb problem to a simpler two-body problem by treating the system as two neutral atoms ($A$ and $\bar{A}$) interacting via residual Coulomb forces. They compute the interatomic potential as the sum of a direct potential $V_{\rm dir}$ (the leading-order Coulomb interaction between constituents of different atoms) and a polarization potential $V_{\rm pol}$ (arising from virtual electric dipole excitations of the atoms). The resulting Schrödinger equation for the relative motion of the two atoms is then solved to obtain molecular eigenstates and binding energies.

## Key numbers

* The stability condition for molecular binding is $m_1/m_2 > 4$ (dimensionless mass ratio threshold)
* For the $(p e^-)$-$(e^+ \bar{p})$ system: Bohr radius $a = 52946$ fm, atomic energy unit $\alpha^2 \mu = 27.20$ eV
* For the $(p \mu^-)$-$(\mu^+ \bar{p})$ system: Bohr radius $a = 284.7$ fm, atomic energy unit $\alpha^2 \mu = 5.057$ keV
* For the $(K^+ \mu^-)$-$(\mu^+ K^-)$ system: Bohr radius $a = 83.59$ fm, atomic energy unit $\alpha^2 \mu = 17.22$ keV
* For the $(K^+ e^-)$-$(e^+ K^-)$ system: Bohr radius $a = 52972$ fm, atomic energy unit $\alpha^2 \mu = 27.18$ eV
* For the $(\pi^+ e^-)$-$(e^+ \pi^-)$ system: Bohr radius $a = 53111$ fm, atomic energy unit $\alpha^2 \mu = 27.11$ eV
* For the $(\mu^+ e^-)$-$(e^+ \mu^-)$ system: Bohr radius $a = 53174$ fm, atomic energy unit $\alpha^2 \mu = 27.08$ eV

## Context

This work extends the long history of matter-antimatter molecule studies initiated by Wheeler (1946), who first proposed the $(e^+e^-)$-$(e^-e^+)$ molecule, and builds on the authors' prior method for molecular states in heavy quark mesons (Wong, 2004) and their earlier study of the $H\bar{H}$ system (Lee, 2008). The results provide binding energies, eigenstates, and annihilation lifetime estimates for six specific molecular systems that satisfy the stability condition, enabling future experimental searches at facilities such as RHIC and the LHC where large numbers of charged particles and antiparticles are produced in close proximity.
