# Variational Principle in the Algebra of Asymptotic Fields

> This paper proposes a variational principle for the solutions of quantum field theories in which the ``trial functions'' are chosen from the algebra of asymptotic fields, and illustrates this variational principle in simple cases.

## Key claim

This paper proposes a variational principle for quantum field theories where trial operators are chosen from the algebra of asymptotic fields (the Haag expansion), and claims that minimizing the absolute squares of the coefficients of non-diagonal terms in the Hamiltonian yields the exact solution in all sectors, but only under the assumption that no massless fields or particles are present.

## Method

The author formulates a variational principle in which the interacting field is expanded in normal-ordered products of asymptotic (in) fields, the Haag expansion, parametrized by a constant v and coefficient functions f^{(n)}. The Hamiltonian is expressed as a functional of these parameters, and the variational condition requires minimizing the sum of integrals of absolute squares of the coefficients of all non-diagonal operator terms (Eq. Q), with arbitrary positive weighting factors λ_n. The method is tested on free scalar and Dirac fields, the φ^4 model in 1+1 dimensions, and the derivative coupling model in 1+3 dimensions.

## Key numbers

- For the free scalar field test, the variational minimum occurs when the trial generalized free field reduces to a free field of mass m (the Lagrangian mass), with the continuous weight σ concentrated at κ² = m².
- For the φ^4 model in 1+1 dimensions, the gap equation relating physical mass m_v² to bare mass m_u² and cut-off Λ is: m_v² = m_u² + (λ/2)(v² + ⟨φ₀²⟩), where ⟨φ₀²⟩ = (1/4π)∫_{-Λ}^{Λ} dk/E(k).
- The renormalized gap equation using a finite mass M gives: m_v² = M² + (λ/2)v² + (λ/8π) ln(M²/m_v²).
- For the derivative coupling model, the solution that reduces the Hamiltonian to free field form is ψ(x) = :exp(ig φ₀(x)): ψ₀(x), with the field strength renormalization factor 1/Z₂ = lim_{x→y} exp(g² ⟨φ(x)φ(y)⟩).

## Context

This work extends the Haag expansion method (Haag, 1958) and the N-quantum approximation to a variational framework, contrasting with path integral methods (Peskin, Weinberg, Feynman, Wilson, Creutz) and the Tamm-Dancoff approximation (Tamm, 1945; Dancoff, 1950) which lose explicit Lorentz invariance or crossing symmetry. The φ^4 calculation reproduces results of Tiktopoulos (1997) in the lowest approximation but will differ in higher approximations, where the Haag expansion uses higher-degree normal-ordered products rather than Gaussian vacuum functionals. The method aims to enable calculations of bound states with soft photon clouds and eventually address nonabelian gauge theories where confined fields lack asymptotic fields.
