# Realistic Nuclear Wave functions and Heavy Ion Collisions

> We discuss the implications of recent experimental evidence of nuclear Short Range Correlations (SRCs) on the modeling of the wave function of complex nuclei. We perform a calculation of potential energy contributions of pp and pn pairs in nuclei, showing that the presence of strong tensor correlations produce a ratio between the twos of about 1/9, which strongly deviates from the combinatorial counting of the number of pairs. We also discuss implications for the production of Monte Carlo configuration for the simulation of nuclear reactions involving complex nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper argues that strong tensor correlations in nuclei cause the potential energy contributions from proton-proton (pp) and proton-neutron (pn) pairs to deviate dramatically from simple combinatorial counting, with pn pairs dominating at a ratio of about 1/9 relative to pp pairs, but this result is conditional on using a full state-dependent correlation model rather than central correlations only.

## Method

The authors calculated the state-dependent two-body density matrices for 16O and 40Ca using a cluster expansion method with realistic wave functions of the form ψ = Π f(rij) φ, where φ is an independent particle model wave function and f(rij) includes correlation operators. They then computed the contributions of pp and pn pairs to the potential energy using the Argonne AV8' potential, separating the contributions from central correlations versus full state-dependent correlations including tensor and spin-isospin operators.

## Key numbers

- For 16O with central correlations only: P(pp) = 23% and P(pn) = 53% of total potential energy, proportional to combinatorial fractions Z(Z-1)/A(A-1) and 2ZN/A(A-1) respectively
- For 16O with full state-dependent correlations: P(pp) = 8% and P(pn) = 83% of total potential energy
- For 16O with full correlations: P(I=0) = 74% and P(I=1) = 26%, where I is the total isospin of the pair
- The calculations for 40Ca give practically the same results as for 16O

## Context

This work extends experimental findings on Short Range Correlations from measurements by Egiyan (2006), Tang (2002), Piasetzky (2006), and Subedi (2008), which showed that about 90% of high momentum protons are correlated with a neutron. The authors build on the cluster expansion approach previously developed by the Perugia group (Alvioli, 2005) and extend a Monte Carlo code for nuclear configurations (Alvioli, 2009) to include state-dependent correlations, enabling more realistic simulations of heavy ion collisions within the Glauber framework.
