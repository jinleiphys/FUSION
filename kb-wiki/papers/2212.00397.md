# Fusion systematics for weakly bound nuclei using neutron flow and collective degrees of freedom

> A systematic analysis of the fusion cross sections around the Coulomb barrier energies with stable weakly bound ($^{6}$Li,$^{7}$Li,$^{9}$Be) and strongly bound $^{12}$C projectiles on various targets was performed by using neutron flow model and coupled channels approach. The analysis show that both the models are successful in explaining the near barrier fusion data. Further, it is also observed that the collective degrees of freedom as well as the neutron flow influence the near barrier fusion process involving weakly bound projectiles.

# Fusion systematics for weakly bound nuclei using neutron flow and collective degrees of freedom

## Key claim
Both the neutron flow (Stelson) model and the coupled channels approach successfully describe near-barrier fusion cross sections for stable weakly bound projectiles (⁶Li, ⁷Li, ⁹Be) and strongly bound ¹²C on various targets, but the data cannot distinguish which mechanism is more appropriate because for the Sm isotopes studied, both the neutron separation energy and collectivity decrease with increasing target mass number.

## Method
Complete fusion cross sections for ⁶Li, ⁷Li, ⁹Be, and ¹²C projectiles on targets ranging from ²⁸Si to ²⁰⁹Bi were analyzed using the Stelson neutron flow model, which parameterizes sub-barrier fusion via a threshold energy (T_exp) where free neutron flow begins, and using coupled channels calculations with the CCFULL code that include inelastic excitations of target and projectile states. The extracted quantities B - T_exp (barrier minus threshold) were correlated with the coupling strength F from coupled channels and with B - T_cal (barrier minus calculated threshold from neutron potentials), and linear fits were performed to compare the two models.

## Key numbers
- For ¹²C + ²⁰⁸Pb: B = 55.47 ± 0.69 MeV, T_exp = 54.17 ± 0.06 MeV, B - T_exp = 1.3 ± 0.50 MeV, F = 0.64 MeV
- For ⁶Li + ²⁰⁸Pb: B = 27.72 ± 1.07 MeV, T_exp = 26.58 ± 0.09 MeV, B - T_exp = 1.14 ± 1.1 MeV, F = 0.35 MeV
- For ⁷Li + ²⁰⁹Bi: B = 29.52 ± 0.67 MeV, T_exp = 26.56 ± 0.10 MeV, B - T_exp = 2.96 ± 0.68 MeV, F = 0.33 MeV
- For ⁹Be + ²⁰⁸Pb: B = 40.19 ± 0.23 MeV, T_exp = 36.69 ± 0.05 MeV, B - T_exp = 3.5 ± 0.24 MeV, F = 0.51 MeV
- For ¹²C + ¹⁵²Sm: B = 47.27 ± 0.92 MeV, T_exp = 42.0 ± 0.19 MeV, B - T_exp = 5.27 ± 0.94 MeV, F = 1.54 MeV
- For ⁹Be + ⁸⁹Y: B = 22.49 ± 0.46 MeV, T_exp = 20.77 ± 0.08 MeV, B - T_exp = 1.72 ± 0.47 MeV, F = 0.38 MeV
- A 20% change in fusion cross section changes the barrier radius by about 10% with negligible change in the fusion barrier
- Varying the diffuseness parameter from 0.65 to 0.8 fm changes T_cal values by around 1 MeV

## Context
This work extends the comparative analysis of the Stelson neutron flow model and coupled channels formalism previously applied to strongly bound projectiles like ¹⁶O (Kailas 1993) to weakly bound projectiles (⁶Li, ⁷Li, ⁹Be) and ¹²C. The results show that both models reproduce the systematic trends, consistent with earlier studies by Shapira (1993) and VinodKumar (1996) for strongly bound systems. The paper identifies that for ⁹Be projectiles, T_exp values are a few MeV higher than T_cal, an anomaly requiring further investigation, and suggests that future studies on isotopic chains where neutron separation energy and collectivity vary in opposite directions could distinguish which mechanism dominates.
