# Parameter-Free Solution of the Bohr Hamiltonian for Actinides Critical in the Octupole Mode

> An analytic, parameter-free (up to overall scale factors) solution of the Bohr Hamiltonian involving axially symmetric quadrupole and octupole deformations, as well as an infinite well potential, is obtained, after separating variables in a way reminiscent of the Variable Moment of Inertia (VMI) concept. Normalized spectra and B(EL) ratios are found to agree with experimental data for 226-Ra and 226-Th, the nuclei known to lie closest to the border between octupole deformation and octupole vibrations in the light actinide region.

# Parameter-Free Solution of the Bohr Hamiltonian for Actinides Critical in the Octupole Mode

## Key claim

The paper claims that an analytic, parameter-free (up to overall scale factors) solution of the Bohr Hamiltonian with axially symmetric quadrupole and octupole deformations and an infinite well potential yields normalized spectra and B(EL) ratios that agree with experimental data for 226Ra and 226Th, but only for these two nuclei which lie closest to the border between octupole deformation and octupole vibrations; the model does not produce odd-even staggering because the infinite barrier between positive and negative octupole deformation regions prevents it.

## Method

The authors solve the Bohr Hamiltonian by assuming only axially symmetric quadrupole (β₂) and octupole (β₃) deformations, introducing polar coordinates (β̃, φ) where φ determines the relative weight of quadrupole versus octupole deformation. They separate variables by approximating the L-dependent effective potential u_L(φ) as a harmonic oscillator around its minimum φ_L for each angular momentum L, using a Variable Moment of Inertia (VMI)-like minimization procedure, then solve the radial β̃ equation with an infinite square well potential leading to Bessel function solutions. The calculation is limited to the n=0 oscillator quantum number in the φ equation.

## Key numbers

- The ground state band spectrum and intraband B(E2) values of the present model are similar to those of the X(5) model (Iacob 2001), but the normalized position of the 0₂⁺ state is predicted at 12.569 in the present model versus 5.649 in X(5)
- The φ_L values for each L are determined by minimizing u_L(φ); for 226Ra the best agreement in the related AQOA model (Bonatsos 2004) occurs at φ₀ = 56°, for 226Th at φ₀ = 60°
- The model predictions for B(E1) branching ratios of 226Ra are compatible with experimental data from Wollersheim (1993) within experimental errors
- The model predictions for B(E1)/B(E2) ratios for 226Th are compatible with experimental data from Bizzeti (1998) within experimental errors, lying considerably lower than the BBS predictions

## Context

This work extends the critical point symmetry approach of E(5) and X(5) (Iacob 2001) by adding the octupole degree of freedom to describe low-lying negative parity states in light actinides, while leaving out the γ degree of freedom for tractability. The model is a parameter-free version of the earlier one-parameter Analytic Quadrupole Octupole Axially symmetric (AQOA) model (Bonatsos 2004), and its predictions are very similar to that model with φ₀ values of 56° for 226Ra and 60° for 226Th. The results enable systematic searches for X(5)-like nuclei in the light actinide region, where the 0₂⁺ state should appear at roughly twice the energy predicted by X(5), and the authors plan to apply the model to the A≈150 region where octupole deformation is also established.
