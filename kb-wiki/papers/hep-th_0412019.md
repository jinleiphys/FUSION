# New variational perturbation theory based on $q-$deformed oscillator

> A new variational perturbation theory is developed based on the $q-$deformed oscillator. It is shown that the new variational perturbation method provides 200 or 10 times better accuracy for the ground state energy of anharmonic oscillator than the Gaussian and the improved Gaussian approximation, respectively.

# New variational perturbation theory based on \(q-\)deformed oscillator

## Key claim

The new variational perturbation method based on the \(q-\)deformed oscillator provides 200 times better accuracy for the ground state energy of the anharmonic oscillator than the Gaussian approximation, and 10 times better accuracy than the post-Gaussian (second-order) approximation, **for weak coupling**. The maximal error of the present approximation is less than 0.8% across all coupling strengths.

## Method

The authors separate the anharmonic oscillator Hamiltonian into a \(q-\)deformed oscillator Hamiltonian \(\hat H_q\) and a perturbation term \(V_I'\), introducing two variational parameters \(\bar\Omega\) and \(\epsilon\). They compute the expectation value of the full Hamiltonian with respect to the \(q-\)deformed ground state up to \(O(\epsilon^2)\), then minimize this energy to obtain gap equations relating \(\xi\) and \(\epsilon\). The first-order perturbative correction is constructed by writing the Hamiltonian as a generalized deformed oscillator with creation and annihilation operators \(\hat a_{(1)}\) and \(\hat a_{(1)}^\dagger\) satisfying a deformed algebra, from which excited state energies are obtained via the recurrence relation \([n+1] = F([n])\).

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy error for \(\xi_0 = 0.06\): 0.00003% (present method), compared to 0.006% (Gaussian) and -0.0003% (post-Gaussian)
- Ground state energy error for \(\xi_0 = 0.6\): 0.0083% (present method), compared to 0.21% (Gaussian) and -0.037% (post-Gaussian)
- Ground state energy error for \(\xi_0 = 6\): 0.307% (present method), compared to 1.09% (Gaussian) and -0.37% (post-Gaussian)
- Ground state energy error for \(\xi_0 = 60\): 0.628% (present method), compared to 1.75% (Gaussian) and -0.68% (post-Gaussian)
- Ground state energy error for \(\xi_0 = 600\): 0.731% (present method), compared to 1.95% (Gaussian) and -0.79% (post-Gaussian)
- Asymptotic ground state energy for \(\xi_0 \rightarrow \infty\): \(0.370 \, \xi_0^{1/3}\) (present method), compared to \(0.375 \, \xi_0^{1/3}\) (Gaussian) and \(0.365 \, \xi_0^{1/3}\) (post-Gaussian)
- Maximum expansion parameter \(\epsilon \simeq 0.1717\) as \(\xi_0 \rightarrow \infty\), compared to Gaussian expansion parameter \(\xi_G = 1\) at the same limit

## Context

This work extends the variational perturbation theory based on the Gaussian approximation developed by Stevenson (1984), Jackiw (1974), and others, including the post-Gaussian (second-order) corrections of Lee (1996). The key advance is replacing the Gaussian trial wave function with a \(q-\)deformed oscillator basis, which naturally emerges from the first-order perturbative correction to the Gaussian approximation as shown by Bak (1998). The method enables systematic higher-order corrections through generalized deformed oscillator algebras and provides a framework for extending the approach to quantum field theory by expanding fields in Fourier modes where each mode acts as a generalized deformed oscillator.
