# Kinetic freeze-out temperature and transverse flow velocity in Au-Au collisions at RHIC-BES energies

> Based on the data-driven analysis, the mid-rapidity transverse momentum spectra of charged hadrons produced in central and peripheral gold-gold (Au-Au) collisions from the Beam Energy Scan (BES) program at the relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) are fitted by the blast-wave model with Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics. The model result are in agreement with the experimental data measured by the STAR Collaboration at the RHIC-BES energies. We observe that the kinetic freeze-out temperature, transverse flow velocity, mean transverse momentum, and initial temperature increase with the collision energy and with the event centrality.

## Key claim

The kinetic freeze-out temperature, transverse flow velocity, mean transverse momentum, and initial temperature all increase with both collision energy (from 7.7 to 39 GeV) and event centrality (from peripheral to central) in Au-Au collisions at RHIC-BES energies, but the authors note that there is entanglement in extracting these parameters and that different choices of fitting can produce opposite trends (e.g., decreasing temperature from peripheral to central collisions).

## Method

The authors fitted mid-rapidity transverse momentum spectra of charged hadrons (pi+, K+, p) measured by the STAR Collaboration using the blast-wave model with Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics, which describes the soft excitation component in the low-pT region (pT up to 2-3 GeV/c). The model assumes a self-similar flow profile with a radial position dependence beta(r) = beta_S (r/R)^(n0) where n0=2, and the transverse flow velocity is obtained as beta_T = 2 beta_S/(n0+2) = 0.5 beta_S. The fits were performed separately for each particle species, centrality class, and collision energy, with the extracted parameters then averaged by weighting the yields of different particle species.

## Key numbers

- For pi+ in 0-5% central Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV: T0 = 0.130 +/- 0.004 GeV, beta_T = 0.306 +/- 0.006
- For p in 0-5% central Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV: T0 = 0.134 +/- 0.004 GeV, beta_T = 0.340 +/- 0.005
- For pi+ in 0-5% central Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN)=39 GeV: T0 = 0.141 +/- 0.004 GeV, beta_T = 0.330 +/- 0.007
- For p in 0-5% central Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN)=39 GeV: T0 = 0.149 +/- 0.005 GeV, beta_T = 0.359 +/- 0.008
- For pi+ in 70-80% peripheral Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV: T0 = 0.115 +/- 0.005 GeV, beta_T = 0.269 +/- 0.007
- For p in 70-80% peripheral Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 GeV: T0 = 0.120 +/- 0.006 GeV, beta_T = 0.204 +/- 0.009
- For pi+ in 70-80% peripheral Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN)=39 GeV: T0 = 0.130 +/- 0.005 GeV, beta_T = 0.319 +/- 0.008
- For p in 70-80% peripheral Au-Au at sqrt(s_NN)=39 GeV: T0 = 0.127 +/- 0.004 GeV, beta_T = 0.274 +/- 0.007

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of kinetic freeze-out parameters in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, analyzing data from the STAR Collaboration (2014, 2017) across six collision energies from 7.7 to 39 GeV. The results are consistent with the general expectation that higher collision energies and more central collisions produce larger energy deposition and more violent expansion. The authors note that their finding of a positive correlation between T0 and beta_T differs from some previous studies (e.g., STAR Collaboration 2014, 2017) that reported a negative correlation or decreasing T0 from peripheral to central collisions, highlighting the model-dependence and entanglement in extracting these parameters. This work enables further investigation of the QCD phase diagram at RHIC-BES energies and comparison with results from other models such as the Tsallis statistics approach.
