# Elastic and radiative pi^+p scattering and properties of the Delta^{++} resonance

> We study the Delta^{++} contributions to elastic and radiative π^+p scattering within an effective Lagrangian model which incorporates the Delta, N, rho and sigma meson degrees of freedom. This model provides a description of the Delta resonance and its interactions that respects electromagnetic gauge invariance and invariance under contact transformations when finite width effects are incorporated. Following recent developments in the description of unstable gauge bosons, we use a complex mass scheme to introduce the finite width of the Delta^{++} without spoiling gauge invariance. The total cross section of elastic π^+p scattering, whose amplitude exhibits the resonant plus background structure of S-matrix theory, is used to fix the mass, width and strong coupling of the Δresonance. The differential cross section of elastic scattering is found in very good agreement with experimental data. The magnetic dipole moment of the Delta^{++}, μ_{Delta}, is left as the only adjustable parameter in radiative π^+p scattering. From a fit to the most sensitive configurations for photon emission in this process, we obtain μ_Δ = (6.14 +-0.51)e/2m_p, in agreement with predictions based on the SU(6) quark model.

## Key claim

The magnetic dipole moment of the Δ⁺⁺ resonance is determined from radiative π⁺p scattering using a full dynamical model that respects electromagnetic gauge invariance and invariance under contact transformations, yielding μ_Δ = (6.14 ± 0.51)e/2m_p, but this result depends on the complex mass scheme prescription for introducing the finite width of the Δ⁺⁺ without spoiling gauge invariance, and the authors note that their modified Born approximation neglects some unitarity-restoring contributions.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective Lagrangian model including Δ, N, ρ, and σ meson degrees of freedom, using a complex mass scheme (replacing m_Δ² → m_Δ² − i m_Δ Γ_Δ in all Feynman rules involving the Δ⁺⁺) to maintain gauge invariance with finite width effects. The mass, width, and strong coupling of the Δ resonance were fixed by fitting the total cross section of elastic π⁺p scattering in the laboratory kinetic energy range T_lab = 75–300 MeV, using experimental data from Pedroni et al. The magnetic dipole moment of the Δ⁺⁺ was then left as the only adjustable parameter in radiative π⁺p scattering, extracted from a fit to the most sensitive photon emission configurations.

## Key numbers

- Δ⁺⁺ mass: m_Δ = (1211.2 ± 0.?) MeV (value truncated in text; the fit included all intermediate states Δ⁺⁺, Δ⁰, N, ρ, and σ)
- Δ⁺⁺ magnetic dipole moment: μ_Δ = (6.14 ± 0.51) e/2m_p, obtained from radiative π⁺p scattering data
- The resonance region for π⁺p elastic scattering is centered around T_lab ≈ 175 MeV, corresponding to Δ⁺⁺ production
- The ρ meson mass and nucleon mass were taken from the Particle Data Group (1998 compilation); the σ meson mass was set to 650 MeV
- Coupling constants used: (g_ρ², g_πNN²)/4π = (2.9, 14); κ_ρ = 3.7

## Context

This work extends earlier determinations of the Δ⁺⁺ magnetic dipole moment by addressing inconsistencies in previous models, particularly the violation of gauge invariance and contact transformation invariance when finite width effects are introduced. The authors critique prior approaches including the relativistic models of Musa et al. and Wittman et al. (which used specific values of the arbitrary parameter A from contact transformations), the soft-photon approximation of Lin et al. (which ignored finite width effects and four-particle vertices), and the method of Pt et al. (which attempted to detach the Δ⁺⁺ decay from the full scattering amplitude). The complex mass scheme used here follows developments in the description of unstable gauge bosons at LEP and the ρ meson resonance by Beenakker et al. (1997). This work enables a consistent extraction of baryon resonance properties from combined elastic and radiative scattering data, and the authors note that their extracted μ_Δ value agrees with predictions based on the SU(6) quark model.
