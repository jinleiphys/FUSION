# Neural Posterior Estimation of Neutron Star Equations of State

> We present a simulation-based inference (SBI) framework to constrain the neutron star (NS) equation of state (EoS) from astrophysical observations of masses, radii and tidal deformabilities, using Neural posterior estimation (NPE) with Conditional Normalising Flows (CNF). To ensure that the model conforms with reality, physics-informed constraints are embedded directly into the training loss. This enables efficient, likelihood-free inference of full posterior distributions for key thermodynamic quantities-including pressure, squared speed of sound, and the trace anomaly-conditioned on observational data. Our models are trained on synthetic datasets generated from two agnostic EoS priors: polytropic parametrizations (PT) and gaussian process (GP) reconstructions. These datasets span various scenarios, including the presence or absence of tidal deformability information and observational noise. Across all settings, the method produces accurate and well-calibrated posteriors, with uncertainties reduced when tidal deformability constraints are included. Furthermore, we find that the behavior of normalized predictive dispersions is strongly correlated with the maximum central density inside NSs, suggesting that the model can indirectly infer this physically meaningful quantity. The approach generalizes well across EoS families and accurately reconstructs derivative quantities such as the polytropic index, demonstrating its robustness and potential for probing dense matter in NS cores.

## Key claim

Neural Posterior Estimation with Conditional Normalizing Flows can accurately recover full posterior distributions for neutron star equation of state quantities (pressure, squared speed of sound, trace anomaly) from synthetic mass-radius and tidal deformability observations, but the method's uncertainty at high densities is strongly correlated with the maximum central density inside neutron stars, meaning the model cannot reliably constrain the EoS above densities that are not probed by observations.

## Method

The authors trained Conditional Normalizing Flow models to approximate posterior distributions of EoS parameters conditioned on synthetic observational data, using a simulation-based inference approach that avoids explicit likelihood calculations. Training data were generated from two agnostic EoS priors: piecewise-polytropic parameterizations (PT) and Gaussian process reconstructions (GP), with four dataset variants that included or excluded tidal deformability information and observational noise. Physics-informed constraints enforcing monotonic pressure increase with density were embedded directly into the training loss function, and models were trained on 20 fixed baryon density points from 0.13 fm⁻³ to 1.28 fm⁻³.

## Key numbers

- Training dataset sizes: 36,391 samples for PT prior, 70,176 samples for GP prior (90/10 train-test split)
- Pressure evaluated at 20 fixed density points with spacing δn = 0.0605 fm⁻³, from n₁ = 0.13 fm⁻³ to n₂₀ = 1.28 fm⁻³
- Maximum noise for mass: σ_M = 0.1 M⊙ (approximately 5% of a 2 M⊙ neutron star)
- Maximum noise for radius: σ_R = 0.3 km
- Tidal deformability noise model: σ(1.4 M⊙) = 223.8, with mass-dependent function σ(M) = 121483.4 × exp(−5M + 0.37M²)
- Synthetic observations used 15 mass samples per EoS, divided into three mass ranges: [1.0, 1.4] M⊙, [1.4, 1.7] M⊙, and [1.7, M_max(EoS)] M⊙, with 5 samples per range
- Test set mass distribution: [6, 4, 5] samples across the three mass ranges, based on 122 pulsar observations from Rocha et al. (2023)

## Context

This work extends simulation-based inference approaches to neutron star EoS inference, building on prior applications of normalizing flows to neutron star physics by Brandes et al. (2024), Wouters et al. (2025), and Carvalho et al. (2024). The physics-informed loss function follows the approach introduced by DiClemente et al. (2025). The method is designed to handle future high-precision observations from missions such as STROBE-X and eXTP, as current data from NICER and GW170817 remain insufficient to fully constrain the high-density behavior of neutron star matter, with recent work by Huang et al. (2023) and Huxford et al. (2024) showing that present measurements primarily constrain the EoS at intermediate densities.
