# Shell model studies of the proton drip line nucleus $^{106}$Sb

> We present results of shell model calculations for the proton drip line nucleus $^{106}$Sb. The shell model calculations were performed based on an effective interaction for the $2s1d0g_{7/2}0h11_{11/2}$ shells employing modern models for the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The results are compared with the recently proposed experimental yrast states. A good agreement with experiment is found lending support to the experimental spin assignements.

## Key claim

Shell-model calculations using a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential reproduce the experimental yrast states of the proton drip line nucleus $^{106}$Sb, lending support to the experimental spin assignments, though the authors note that the experimental ground state could have spin $1^+$ rather than the tentatively assigned value, which would shift all experimental spin values by one unit.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for $^{106}$Sb using an effective two-body interaction derived for the $2s_{1/2}$, $1d_{5/2}$, $1d_{3/2}$, $0g_{7/2}$, and $0h_{11/2}$ valence orbits, with $^{100}$Sn as the closed core. The effective interaction was obtained from the CD-Bonn free nucleon-nucleon potential via a perturbative many-body scheme that first solves the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the $G$-matrix and then includes core-polarization and folded-diagram terms through third order in the $\hat{Q}$-box. The Lanczos algorithm was used to solve the shell-model eigenvalue problem, and effective proton and neutron charges were calculated from perturbative many-body methods through second order for use in $E2$ transition calculations.

## Key numbers

- Proton separation energy of $^{106}$Sb is approximately 400 keV.
- Harmonic oscillator energy used: $\hbar\Omega = 8.5$ MeV for $A=100$.
- Neutron single-particle energy differences relative to $1d_{5/2}$: $\varepsilon_{0g_{7/2}} - \varepsilon_{1d_{5/2}} = 0.2$ MeV, $\varepsilon_{1d_{3/2}} - \varepsilon_{1d_{5/2}} = 2.55$ MeV, $\varepsilon_{2s_{1/2}} - \varepsilon_{1d_{5/2}} = 2.45$ MeV, $\varepsilon_{0h_{11/2}} - \varepsilon_{1d_{5/2}} = 3.2$ MeV.
- Experimental $B(E2;4_1^+ \rightarrow 2_1^+)$ transition strength: $2.8(3)$ W.u.
- Microscopically calculated effective charges (diagonal contributions): proton $1d_{5/2}$ = 1.06e, $0g_{7/2}$ = 1.15e, $1d_{3/2}$ = 1.04e, $0h_{11/2}$ = 1.16e; neutron $1d_{5/2}$ = 0.53e, $0g_{7/2}$ = 0.72e, $1d_{3/2}$ = 0.52e, $0h_{11/2}$ = 0.51e.
- Effective charges deduced from fitting the $B(E2)$ transition: $e_n = 0.8-1.0e$ and $e_p = 1.4e \pm 0.2e$.
- Microscopically calculated effective charges: $e_n = 0.5-0.7e$ and $e_p = 1.1-1.2e$.

## Context

This work extends shell-model studies to the proton drip line region around $A=100$, following experimental identification of $^{106}$Sb by Sohler et al. (1999) and earlier studies of $^{100}$Sn and neighboring isotopes by Lipoglavsek et al. (1996), Gorska et al. (1997), and Grawe et al. (1997). The calculations use the same effective interaction approach previously applied successfully to tin isotopes by Holt et al. (1998), and the results enable comparison of microscopically derived effective charges with those extracted from $E2$ data in $^{102}$Sn and $^{104}$Sn, as well as with theoretical estimates by Sagawa et al. (1987) and Hamamoto and Sagawa (1997).
