# Kaon and Hyperon Form Factors in Kaon Electroproduction on the Nucleon

> The electromagnetic form factors of strange mesons and baryons are studied by means of kaon electroproduction on the nucleon. The response functions that are sensitive to the K0, Lambda, Sigma, and KK*γtransition form factors are systematically explored. The effects of these form factors on several response functions are discussed.

## Key claim

Kaon electroproduction on the nucleon can provide indirect information on the electromagnetic form factors of strange mesons (K0, KK*γ transition) and hyperons (Λ, Σ+), but unlike the charged pion and kaon case, this cannot be done model-independently using Chew-Low extrapolation; the main limitation is the uncertainty in the elementary production operator.

## Method

The authors systematically explored the 36 response functions for pseudoscalar meson electroproduction, using elementary models from Williams (1992) extended to the n(e,e'K0)Λ and p(e,e'K0)Σ+ channels, and computed the sensitivity of each response function to the K0, K+K*+γ, K0K*0γ, Λ, and Σ+ form factors. They employed two relativistic quark models (light-cone quark model and quark-meson vertex model) for the K0 form factor, vector meson dominance and chiral perturbation theory for comparison, a hybrid vector meson dominance model and a chiral quark-soliton model for hyperon form factors, and two different models for the KK*γ transition form factors.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal response function for n(e,e'K0)Λ calculated with the LCQ-model K0 form factor is almost 50% larger than the calculation with no K0 pole.
- The charged KK*γ transition form factor (K+K*+γ) displays a zero at q2 = -2.7 GeV2, indicating destructive interference between light and heavy quark contributions.
- Response functions involving triple polarization are about two orders of magnitude smaller than the other response functions.
- The zero in the charged transition form factor around q2 = -2.6 GeV2 is not visible in the response functions because they are already very small at that kinematics.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous report (Mart and Bennhold, 1997) and addresses the lack of experimental data for strange hadron form factors, which contrasts with the well-studied nucleon form factors and the Chew-Low extrapolation technique used for charged pion and kaon form factors (Chew and Low, 1959; Baker et al., 1993). The results enable future experimental proposals at TJNAF (now Jefferson Lab) using CLAS in Hall B, particularly for measuring the Λ form factor via double polarization response functions in p(e,e'K+)Λ, and for testing models of the K0, KK*γ, and Σ+ form factors once the elementary production operator uncertainties are reduced through precise kaon photoproduction experiments.
