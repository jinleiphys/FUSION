# Universal properties of infrared oscillator basis extrapolations

> Recent work has shown that a finite harmonic oscillator basis in nuclear many-body calculations effectively imposes a hard-wall boundary condition in coordinate space, motivating infrared extrapolation formulas for the energy and other observables. Here we further refine these formulas by studying two-body models and the deuteron. We accurately determine the box size as a function of the model space parameters, and compute scattering phase shifts in the harmonic oscillator basis. We show that the energy shift can be well approximated in terms of the asymptotic normalization coefficient and the bound-state momentum, discuss higher-order corrections for weakly bound systems, and illustrate this universal property using unitarily equivalent calculations of the deuteron.

## Key claim

The energy shift from truncating a harmonic oscillator basis can be well approximated by an exponential formula involving the asymptotic normalization coefficient and bound-state momentum, but the effective hard-wall radius is more accurately given by \(L_2 = \sqrt{2(N+3/2+2)}b\) rather than the previously used \(L_0\) or \(L'_0\), with corrections needed for weakly bound systems like the deuteron.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrödinger equation for two-body model potentials (square well, exponential, Gaussian, quartic) and for the deuteron using the Entem-Machleidt 500 MeV chiral EFT N\(^3\)LO potential, with harmonic oscillator basis truncations at various \(N\) and \(\hbar\Omega\) values. They compared energies from truncated basis calculations to exact solutions and to solutions with Dirichlet boundary conditions at candidate radii \(L_0\), \(L'_0\), and \(L_2\), and analytically derived the lowest eigenvalue of the momentum operator \(p^2\) in a finite oscillator basis to determine the correct box size. They also computed scattering phase shifts from positive-energy states in the oscillator basis using the extracted box size.

## Key numbers

- For a square well with \(V_0=4\) (dimensionless units, \(\hbar=\mu=R=1\)), the exact ground-state energy is \(-1.51\); the truncated basis (\(N=4\), \(\hbar\Omega=6\)) gives \(-1.33\), while Dirichlet energies at \(L_0\), \(L'_0\), and \(L_2\) are \(-0.97\), \(-1.21\), and \(-1.29\) respectively.
- For the deuteron with the N\(^3\)LO potential, the exact binding energy is \(-2.2246\) MeV and the exact radius squared is \(3.9006\) fm\(^2\).
- The lowest momentum eigenvalue \(\kappa_{\text{min}}\) for \(N=2\) is \(0.9586\) (dimensionless), while \(\pi/L_2 = 0.9472\) and \(\pi/L_0 = 1.1874\); for \(N=20\), \(\kappa_{\text{min}} = 0.4585\), \(\pi/L_2 = 0.4582\), and \(\pi/L_0 = 0.4791\).
- The analytical derivation yields \(\Delta = 2\) in the ansatz \(\kappa = \pi/\sqrt{4n+3+2\Delta}\), giving \(\kappa_{\text{min}} = \pi/L_2\) for \(n\gg 1\).

## Context

This work refines the infrared extrapolation framework introduced by Furnstahl et al. (2012) and Coon et al. (2012), which showed that a finite harmonic oscillator basis imposes an effective hard-wall boundary condition. The improved determination of the box radius \(L_2\) enables more accurate extrapolation formulas and allows computation of scattering phase shifts directly in the oscillator basis, analogous to Lüscher-type formulas used in lattice calculations (Lüscher, 1985; Lee, 2010; König, 2011; Pine, 2012). The universal expression for the coefficient \(A\) in terms of the asymptotic normalization coefficient and binding momentum connects to methods used in quantum chemistry (linear energy method of Djajaputra and Shertzer, 2000) and extends the approach to weakly bound systems where higher-order corrections are important.
