# Collectivity Embedded in Complex Spectra of Finite Interacting Fermi Systems: Nuclear Example

> The mechanism of collectivity coexisting with chaos in a finite system of strongly interacting fermions is investigated. The complex spectra are represented in the basis of two-particle two-hole states describing the nuclear double-charge exchange modes in $^{48}$Ca. An example of $J^π=0^-$ excitations shows that the residual interaction, which generically implies chaotic behavior, under certain specific and well identified conditions may create strong transitions, even much stronger than those corresponding to a pure mean-field picture. Such an effect results from correlations among the off-diagonal matrix elements, is connected with locally reduced density of states and a local minimum in the information entropy.

# Collectivity Embedded in Complex Spectra of Finite Interacting Fermi Systems: Nuclear Example

## Key claim

The residual interaction in a finite fermion system, which generically produces chaotic (GOE-like) behavior, can under specific conditions create strong collective transitions that exceed mean-field predictions, provided that correlations among off-diagonal matrix elements coincide with a locally reduced density of states and a local minimum in information entropy. This collectivity is not a generic property of complex spectra but requires a delicate matching of interaction strength to nonuniformities in the state distribution.

## Method

The authors diagonalize a realistic nuclear Hamiltonian in a basis of 2p2h (two-particle two-hole) states for the nucleus $^{48}$Ca, using a Woods-Saxon mean field and a density-dependent zero-range residual interaction. They compute the transition strength for a double-charge exchange (DCX) $J^\pi=0^-$ excitation built from two single-phonon operators (a dipole and a spin-quadrupole mode), and analyze the diagonal and off-diagonal contributions to the strength, the information entropy of eigenvectors, the spectral rigidity $\Delta_3$, and the distribution of off-diagonal matrix elements. The residual interaction strength is artificially scaled by a factor $g$ to explore the transition from mean-field to strongly mixed regimes.

## Key numbers

- The collective state appears at 51.1 MeV excitation energy in $^{48}$Ca, with a transition strength almost a factor of 2 larger than any unperturbed transition.
- About 95% of the collective transition strength originates from the off-diagonal component $S^{od}_{F_{\alpha}}(n)$.
- The model space contains N=2286 2p2h states for the $J^\pi=0^-$ DCX excitations.
- The collective state is shifted approximately 10 MeV higher in energy compared to the unperturbed transitions.
- For a residual interaction scaling factor $g=0.7$, the strongest transition (at 48.6 MeV) is another factor of 2 stronger than for $g=1$.
- The spectral rigidity $\Delta_3(L)$ for the 400-state interval covering the collective state (44.5-52.1 MeV, states n=751 to n=1150) shows larger deviations from GOE than the interval covering the first density maximum (35.2-44.5 MeV, states n=351 to n=750).
- The distribution of off-diagonal matrix elements follows $P(H)=a|H|^b\exp(-|H|/c)$ with parameters $a=676$, $b=-1.21$, $c=0.69$ (negative side) and $a=692$, $b=-1.22$, $c=0.81$ (positive side), showing asymmetry with more abundant positive matrix elements.
- The single-phonon 1p1h spaces contain 28 $J^\pi=1^-$ states and 25 $J^\pi=1^+$ states.

## Context

This work extends the study of chaos and collectivity in finite fermion systems beyond the 1p1h level, where the Brown-Bolsterli schematic model (Brown and Bolsterli, 1959) successfully explained giant resonances as collective edge effects. The authors build on their earlier work (Drozdz et al., 1990, 1994) showing that GOE fluctuations appear already in the 2p2h space, and here demonstrate that collectivity can survive in this chaotic environment under specific conditions. The results are relevant for understanding double-charge exchange reactions (Bilger et al., 1993; Kagarlis and Johnson, 1994) and more generally for mesoscopic systems where nonuniform level densities occur (Nazmitdinov and Heiss, 1997). The finding that collective states reside in regions of reduced chaos parallels the hypothesis that biological collectivity occurs at the border between chaos and regularity (Kauffman, 1993).
