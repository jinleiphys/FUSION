# Pion and kaon valence quark distribution functions from Dyson-Schwinger equations

> Using realistic quark propagators and meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes based on the Dyson-Schwinger equations, we calculate the pion and kaon's valence parton distribution functions (PDF) through the modified impulse approximation. The PDFs we obtained at hadronic scale have the purely valence characteristic and exhibit both dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking effects. A new calculation technique is introduced to determine the valence PDFs with precision. Through NLO DGLAP evolution, our result is compared with pion and kaon valence PDF data at experimental scale. Good agreement is found in the case of pion, while deviation emerges for kaon. We point out this situation can be resolved by incorporating gluon contributions into the mesons if the pion hosts more gluons than kaon nonperturbatively.

## Key claim
Using realistic Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) solutions for quark propagators and meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, the authors compute pion and kaon valence parton distribution functions (PDFs) at a hadronic scale and find good agreement with pion data after NLO DGLAP evolution, but a deviation for the kaon that they argue can be resolved if the pion contains more gluons than the kaon nonperturbatively.

## Method
The authors employ the modified impulse approximation to compute valence-quark PDFs from dressed quark propagators and meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes obtained with a DCSB-improved kernel beyond rainbow-ladder truncation. They introduce a new technique that determines the PDF point-wise by rewriting moment integrals into a form that directly yields the x-dependent distribution, avoiding reconstruction from a finite set of moments. The PDFs at hadronic scale are then evolved to experimental scales using NLO DGLAP evolution via the QCDNUM package.

## Key numbers
- Pion valence PDF at hadronic scale: $\langle x \rangle^\pi_u = 0.5$ (quark number sum rule gives $\langle x^0 \rangle^\pi_u = 1$)
- Pion PDF broadness: $\langle(2x-1)^2\rangle_u^\pi = 0.20$ (compared to 0.15 from algebraic model)
- Kaon PDF: $\langle x \rangle^K_{\bar{s}} = 0.55$, $\langle x \rangle^K_u = 0.45$ (10% asymmetry)
- Kaon PDF broadness: $\langle(2x-1)^2\rangle_u^K = 0.176$ (compared to 0.134 from algebraic model)
- Hadronic scale determined as $\mu_0 = 520$ MeV, producing $\langle x \rangle_v^\pi \sim 0.24$ at $\mu_2 = 2$ GeV, close to Drell-Yan data analysis $2\langle x \rangle_v^{\mu_2} = 0.47(2)$ and lattice simulation $2\langle x \rangle_v^{\mu_2} = 0.48(4)$
- Strong coupling constant set to $\alpha_s(1 \text{ GeV}) = 0.491$

## Context
This work extends DSE-based calculations of meson PDFs beyond simple algebraic models (Chang 2014, Chen 2016) by using realistic DCSB-improved kernel solutions for both pion and kaon. The results are compared with Drell-Yan experimental analyses (Conway 1989, Aicher 2010) and NJL model calculations (Hutauruk 2016). The authors note that the kaon PDF discrepancy at intermediate x could be resolved by incorporating nonperturbative gluon content, following the argument of Chen (2016) that pions host more gluons than kaons. This enables future nonperturbative DSE-BSE studies including $q\bar{q}g$ components in meson bound states.
