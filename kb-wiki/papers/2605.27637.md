# Projection operator onto spin-S eigenspaces of total and orbital angular momenta

> The Frobenius covariant is used to construct a projection operator onto the spin-S eigenspaces associated with the squares of the total and orbital angular momenta. The covariant admits two equivalent representations: as a polynomial in powers of the scalar product of the spin and orbital angular momentum operators, and as a finite expansion in terms of their respective polarization operators. A correspondence is established with Villars' angular momentum projection, used in nuclear structure studies.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a projection operator onto simultaneous eigenspaces of total and orbital angular momentum squares using the Frobenius covariant, establishing that this operator admits two equivalent representations: as a polynomial in the scalar product of spin and orbital angular momentum operators, and as a finite expansion in terms of their respective polarization operators. The abstract's claim of a "correspondence" with Villars' angular momentum projection is qualified by the paper's demonstration that the off-diagonal elements of Villars' operator can be expressed algebraically via the Frobenius covariant and ladder operators, rather than through numerical integration over the rotation group.

## Method

The author uses the Frobenius covariant, defined as a product over all possible total angular momentum values j excluding the target J, to construct the projection operator onto the spin-S eigenspaces of total and orbital angular momenta. The operator is then expanded in two forms: as powers of the scalar product S^mu L_mu, and as a sum over scalar products of spin polarization operators T_K(S) and orbital polarization operators T_K(L). The correspondence with Villars' angular momentum projection operator P^J_{MM'} is established by expressing its diagonal elements as the Frobenius covariant and its off-diagonal elements via ladder operators acting on the Frobenius covariant.

## Key numbers

- The Frobenius covariant F^LS_J is defined as a product over all j = |L-S|, ..., L+S excluding j=J, with denominator j(j+1)-J(J+1)
- The expansion in polarization operators involves a sum over K from 0 to 2*min(S,L), with a prefactor (2J+1) times a 6j symbol
- The summation over magnetic quantum numbers yields the factor (2L+1)/(4pi) times the Legendre polynomial P_L(nn')
- The normalization constant N_{JMM'} for off-diagonal Villars operators involves products over m in the interval [min(M',M-+1), max(M',M-+1)] with factors of sqrt((J-+m+1)(J-+m)/2)

## Context

This work extends the projection operator techniques used in nuclear shell models for restoring rotational symmetry, as described by Schmid (2004) and Raduta (2015), by providing algebraic expressions that avoid numerical integration over the rotation group. The formalism enables the decomposition of non-relativistic propagators for spin-S particles in spherically symmetric potentials into radial and angular parts, with applications to atomic electron capture, beta-decay processes studied by Simkovic (2021), and decays of trapped particles such as the Omega-minus hyperon (Krivoruchenko, 2008). The expansion in polarization operators follows the normalization conventions of Varshalovich (1988) and connects to the symmetric traceless tensor formalism of Applequist (1989) used in integer-spin particle propagators.
