# In-medium ion mass renormalization and lattice vibrations in the neutron star crust

> The inner crust of a neutron star consists of nuclei immersed in a superfluid neutron liquid. As these nuclei move through the fermionic medium they bring it into motion as well. As a result their mass is strongly renormalized and the spectrum of the ion lattice vibrations is significantly affected. Consequently we predict that the specific heat and the lattice thermal energy of the Coulomb crystal at these densities are noticeably modified.

# In-medium ion mass renormalization and lattice vibrations in the neutron star crust

## Key claim
The effective mass of ions moving through the superfluid neutron liquid in the inner crust of a neutron star is strongly renormalized, which significantly modifies the phonon spectrum and thermodynamic functions of the Coulomb crystal. This effect is derived under the assumption that the neutron gas is strongly paired at low temperatures (T ≲ 0.1 MeV), allowing a hydrodynamic approximation with an irrotational velocity field.

## Method
The author solves the Poisson equation for the velocity field of a spherical nucleus moving through a superfluid neutron medium, imposing boundary conditions of phase continuity and mass conservation at the nuclear surface. From the kinetic energy of the flow, an analytic expression for the renormalized ion mass is derived as a function of the density ratio γ = ρ_out/ρ_in, where ρ_in is the uniform nucleon density inside the nucleus and ρ_out is the neutron gas density outside. The renormalized plasma frequency and thermodynamic functions are then computed for a bcc Coulomb crystal using nuclear parameters from prior density functional calculations.

## Key numbers
- The inner crust densities considered range from 10¹¹ g/cm³ to 10¹⁴ g/cm³.
- The renormalized ion mass is given by M^ren = (4/3)πR³ m ρ_in × (1−γ)²/(2γ+1), where m is the nucleon mass and γ = ρ_out/ρ_in.
- The ratio of plasma frequencies is ω_p/ω_p^ren = |1−γ|/√(2γ+1), where ω_p^ren uses the renormalized ion mass.
- In the T→0 limit, the ratios of lattice thermal energy and specific heat are U^ren/U = C_v^ren/C_v = |1−γ|³/(2γ+1)^(3/2).
- The thermodynamic functions are evaluated at temperature T = 0.1 MeV.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of Coulomb crystals in neutron star crusts (e.g., BBP, NVB, DHM, DHA, MH, MBH) by incorporating the hydrodynamic effect of the superfluid neutron gas on ion motion. It builds on the recognition that at low temperatures the neutron gas is strongly paired, simplifying the problem compared to the normal Fermi gas case treated by Rosch (1999). The results enable recalculation of thermal and electric conductivities governed by electron-phonon scattering, particularly Umklapp processes, and motivate future consideration of shape vibrations of nuclei.
