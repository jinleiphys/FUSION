# The reaction $πN \to ππN$ at threshold in chiral perturbation theory

> In the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, we give thIn the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, we give the chiral expansion for the $πN \to ππN$ threshold amplitudes $D_1$ and $D_2$ to quadratic order in the pion mass. The theoretical results agree within one standard deviation with the empirical values. We also derive a relation between the two threshold amplitudes of the reaction $πN \to ππN$ and the $ππ$ S--wave scattering lengths, $a_0^0$ and $a_0^2$, respectively, to order ${\cal O}(M_π^2)$. We show that there are uncertainties mostly related to resonance excitation which make an accurate determination of the $ππ$ scattering length $a_0^0$ from the $ππN$ threshold amplitudes at present very difficult. The situation is different in the $ππ$ isospin two final state. Here, the chiral series converges and one finds $a_0^2 = -0.031 \pm 0.007$ consistent with the one--loop chiral perturbation theory prediction.

## Key claim

The authors compute the chiral expansion of the $\pi N \to \pi \pi N$ threshold amplitudes $D_1$ and $D_2$ to quadratic order in the pion mass (${\cal O}(M_\pi^2)$) using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, finding agreement within one standard deviation with empirical values; however, they show that uncertainties from resonance excitation make an accurate determination of the $\pi\pi$ scattering length $a_0^0$ from these threshold amplitudes very difficult, while the isospin-two channel yields a convergent chiral series giving $a_0^2 = -0.031 \pm 0.007$.

## Method

The authors performed a one-loop calculation in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT) to order ${\cal O}(q^3)$, corresponding to all corrections up to and including ${\cal O}(M_\pi^2)$ relative to the tree-level result. They computed the Born contributions, 36 distinct one-loop diagrams, and counterterm contributions from the chiral Lagrangians ${\cal L}_{\pi N}^{(2)}$, ${\cal L}_{\pi N}^{(3)}$, and ${\cal L}_{\pi\pi}^{(4)}$, using dimensional regularization and renormalizing masses, decay constants, and coupling constants to their physical values. The finite low-energy constants were estimated via resonance saturation.

## Key numbers

- $a_0^2 = -0.031 \pm 0.007$ extracted from the $\pi\pi$ isospin-two final state, consistent with the one-loop chiral perturbation theory prediction
- The leading-order tree-level amplitudes: $D_1 = \krig{g}_A/(8 F^3)$, $D_2 = -3\krig{g}_A/(8 F^3)$
- The Born contributions expanded in $\mu = M_\pi/m$: $D_1^{\rm Born} = \dfrac{\krig{g}_A}{8 F^3} \biggl[ 1+ \dfrac{7}{2}\mu - \mu^2 \biggl( \dfrac{1}{8}+\dfrac{3}{2}g_A^2 \biggr) \biggr]$, $D_2^{\rm Born} = \dfrac{\krig{g}_A}{8 F^3} \biggl[ -3- \dfrac{17}{2}\mu + \mu^2 \biggl( \dfrac{11}{8}+ 2 g_A^2 \biggr) \biggr]$
- The integral $I = \int_0^1 dx \dfrac{x}{\sqrt{(1-x)(1+3x)}} \arctan \dfrac{x}{\sqrt{(1-x)(1+3x)}} = 0.6456$ appearing in the one-loop results
- The Weinberg tree-level prediction: $a_0^0 = 7M_\pi^2 / (32 \pi F_\pi^2) = 0.16$ and $a_0^2 = (-2/7) a_0^0$
- The Gasser-Leutwyler one-loop prediction: $a_0^0 = 0.20 \pm 0.01$
- The strong pion-nucleon coupling constant $g_{\pi N} = 13.4$
- The pion decay constant $F_\pi \simeq 93$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier tree-level Olsson-Turner approach (Olsson and Turner, 1970s) and the improved low-energy representation of Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1990s) by computing all corrections up to ${\cal O}(M_\pi^2)$ in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, making it sensitive to the one-loop corrections to the $\pi\pi$ scattering lengths from Gasser and Leutwyler (1983, 1984). The results enable a more precise extraction of the $\pi\pi$ S-wave scattering lengths from threshold $\pi N \to \pi\pi N$ data, though the authors find that resonance uncertainties limit the accuracy for $a_0^0$ while the isospin-two channel $a_0^2$ is well determined. The calculation also provides a framework for future higher-order calculations and for resolving the discrepancy between the chiral prediction and data for the $\pi^- p \to \pi^0 \pi^0 n$ channel.
