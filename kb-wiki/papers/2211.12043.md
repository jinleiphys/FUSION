# Hybrid stars may have an inverted structure

> We propose a new stellar structure of compact stars, the ``cross stars" that consist of a hadronic matter core and a quark matter crust, with an inverted structure compared to the conventional hybrid stars. This distinct stellar structure naturally arises from the quark matter to hadronic matter transition associated with the chemical potential crossing, in the context of the quark matter hypothesis that either strange or up-down quark matter is the ground state of baryonic matter at low pressure. We find that the interplay between the hadronic matter and quark matter compositions of cross stars can help to reconcile the small radii constraints indicated by the LIGO/Virgo GW170817 event, the large radii constraints set for massive compact stars by recent NICER X-ray observations, and the recent observation of the most-massive pulsar PSR J0952-0607. This leaves more space open for the equation of states of both hadronic matter and quark matter.

# Hybrid stars may have an inverted structure

## Key claim

The authors propose that compact stars can exist with a hadronic matter core and a quark matter crust ("cross stars"), which is the inverted structure compared to conventional hybrid stars. This structure arises only under the quark matter hypothesis that either strange or up-down quark matter is the ground state of baryonic matter at low pressure, and requires that the chemical potentials of the two phases cross at some critical pressure within the stable branch of compact stars.

## Method

The authors model quark matter using a parametrized grand potential with an effective bag constant B and QCD correction parameter a_4, considering both up-down quark matter (udQM) and strange quark matter (SQM). They adopt the Maxwell construction for the phase transition and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equations with the APR equation of state as the representative benchmark for hadronic matter, using eleven hadronic models to check robustness. The mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities are computed and compared against constraints from GW170817, NICER observations, and pulsar mass measurements.

## Key numbers

- For udQM, the bag constant B is bounded by B ≤ 56.8 MeV/fm³ from requiring a_4_min ≤ 1; benchmark values used are B = 20, 35, 50 MeV/fm³
- For SQM, the bag constant is constrained to B ∈ [15.5, 82.2] MeV/fm³
- The critical pressure P_cr for the phase transition ranges from 5 to 96 MeV/fm³ for udQM and 7 to 93 MeV/fm³ for SQM with the APR hadronic EOS
- The energy density jump at the transition Δρ ranges from 83 to 111 MeV/fm³ for udQM and 59 to 175 MeV/fm³ for SQM
- The largest radius difference between twin star configurations reaches 4.3 km at M ~ 1.7 M⊙ for udQM and 3.1 km at M ~ 1.75 M⊙ for SQM
- The hadronic core in cross stars near maximum mass has a mass of ~0.1 M⊙ and width of ~1 km

## Context

This work extends the quark matter hypothesis of Bodmer (1971), Witten (1984), and Holdom (2017) by considering a phase transition from absolutely stable quark matter back to hadronic matter at higher pressure, which is the inverse of conventional hybrid star models (Alford 2013). The cross star structure helps reconcile the small radius constraints from GW170817 with the large radius constraints from NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, and the high mass of PSR J0952-0607. This opens more parameter space for both hadronic and quark matter equations of state, and could be distinguished from other compact stars through future radius measurements or gravitational wave asteroseismology.
