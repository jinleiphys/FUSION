# Lattice QCD and heavy ion collisions: a review of recent progress

> In the last few years, numerical simulations of QCD on the lattice have reached a new level of accuracy. A wide range of thermodynamic quantities is now available in the continuum limit and for physical quark masses. This allows a comparison with measurements from heavy ion collisions for the first time. Furthermore, calculations of dynamical quantities are also becoming available. The combined effort from first principles and experiment allows us to gain an unprecedented understanding of the properties of quark-gluon plasma. I will review the state-of-the-art results from lattice simulations and connect them to the experimental information from RHIC and the LHC.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD simulations have reached sufficient accuracy to provide continuum-extrapolated thermodynamic quantities at physical quark masses that can be compared to heavy-ion collision data for the first time, but the comparison is limited to small baryochemical potentials (μ_B/T ≤ 2) due to the fermion sign problem, and the QCD phase diagram at large net-baryon density remains vastly unexplored.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD simulations using staggered fermions (stout-improved and HISQ actions) on hyper-cubic lattices N_s³ × N_t, with thermodynamic quantities calculated from derivatives of the Euclidean path integral partition function. At finite baryochemical potential, the sign problem is circumvented through Taylor expansion around μ_B=0 (up to order (μ_B/T)⁶) and analytic continuation from imaginary chemical potential, with strangeness neutrality and electric charge conservation enforced to match experimental conditions.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature from chiral susceptibility: T_c = 154 ± 9 MeV (crossover, not a sharp phase transition)
- Curvature of the phase diagram at μ_B=0: κ = 0.020(4) for equal quark chemical potentials with m_s/m_{u,d}=20
- Taylor expansion coefficients c₂, c₄, c₆ for pressure are continuum-extrapolated; c₈ determined at two temperatures and N_t=8
- The Taylor series truncated at O(μ_B⁶) agrees with full holographic results up to μ_B/T ≈ 2.5
- Charm quark becomes a relevant degree of freedom at T ∼ 250 MeV
- Thermodynamic quantities remain far from the ideal gas (Stefan-Boltzmann) limit even at T ∼ 400 MeV
- Lattice results approach perturbative QCD (HTL and Electrostatic QCD) at T ∼ 2-3 T_c

## Context

This review extends the work of the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi et al., 2013) and the HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov et al., 2014), whose continuum-extrapolated equations of state at zero density agree with each other. The results enable Bayesian validation against RHIC and LHC data (Pratt et al., 2015), provide inputs for hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions, and constrain the QCD phase diagram at finite density for the Beam Energy Scan programs at RHIC, FAIR, and NICA. The review also discusses the need for higher-order Taylor coefficients to cover the full RHIC energy scan range, as demonstrated by holographic approaches (Critelli et al., 2017).
