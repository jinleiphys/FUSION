# Nuclear matter incompressibility coefficient in relativistic and nonrelativistic microscopic models

> We systematically analyze the recent claim that nonrelativistic and relativistic mean field (RMF) based random phase approximation (RPA) calculations for the centroid energy E_0 of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance yield for the nuclear matter incompressibility coefficient, K_{nm}, values which differ by about 20%. For an appropriate comparison with the RMF based RPA calculations, we obtain the parameters for the Skyrme force used in the nonrelativistic model by adopting the same procedure as employed in the determination of the NL3 parameter set of an effective Lagrangian used in the RMF model. Our investigation suggest that the discrepancy between the values of K_{nm} predicted by the relativistic and nonrelativistic models is significantly less than 20%.

# Nuclear matter incompressibility coefficient in relativistic and nonrelativistic microscopic models

## Key claim
The discrepancy between the nuclear matter incompressibility coefficient K_nm predicted by relativistic mean field (RMF) and nonrelativistic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock based RPA models is significantly less than 20%, contrary to earlier claims. The difference arises primarily from differences in the symmetry energy coefficient J and its slope L between the models, not from an inherent model dependence.

## Method
The authors generated new Skyrme interaction parameter sets (SK272 and SK255) by performing a least-squares fit to the same experimental data for nuclear binding energies, charge radii, and neutron radii that were used to determine the NL3 parameter set of the RMF effective Lagrangian. They then performed highly accurate Hartree-Fock based RPA calculations for the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) centroid energy in several nuclei, using a box size of 90 fm, a smearing parameter of 1 MeV, and a maximum particle-hole excitation energy above 500 MeV to ensure numerical accuracy within 0.1 MeV.

## Key numbers
- The NL3 parameter set yields K_nm = 271.76 MeV and J = 37.4 MeV.
- The SK272 parameter set (K_nm = 272 MeV) gives ISGMR centroid energies E_0 that are higher by 4-6% than those from the NL3 parameter set in RRPA calculations.
- Reducing K_nm by about 10% (to 255 MeV in the SK255 set) reproduces the RRPA results for NL3 within the uncertainty of experimental data.
- For the SGII interaction (K_nm approximately 215 MeV), the E_0 for 208Pb is within 0.3 MeV of the SK255 result, despite a 40 MeV difference in K_nm.
- An increase in J by 10% leads to an increase in K_nm by about 5% when requiring a fixed E_0.
- For 208Pb with SK272 and SK255, the isovector giant dipole resonance peak energy is 13.2 and 13.3 MeV respectively, compared to the experimental value of 13.3 ± 0.1 MeV.

## Context
This work addresses the long-standing discrepancy between nonrelativistic Skyrme-based calculations (which gave K_nm in the range 210-220 MeV, as in Colo (1992) and Hamamoto (1997)) and relativistic RPA calculations (which gave K_nm between 250-270 MeV, as in Ma (2001) and Vretenar (2003)). The authors show that when Skyrme parameters are fitted to the same data as the NL3 set (Lalazissis (1997)), the model dependence largely disappears. This enables a more reliable extraction of K_nm from ISGMR data and clarifies that the symmetry energy properties, not the relativistic versus nonrelativistic framework, drive the apparent discrepancy. The analysis extends earlier work by Piekarewicz (2002) that was restricted to 208Pb and used less constrained interaction parameters.
