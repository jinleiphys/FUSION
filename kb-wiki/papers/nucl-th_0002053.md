# Implications of Quark-Lepton Symmetry for Neutrino Masses and Oscillations

> We identify a plausible scenario based on quark-lepton symmetry which correlates long baseline oscillations with maximal mixing to sterile neutrinos. The implication for the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (SNO) is that the neutral current signal will be found to suffer the same suppression from the Standard Solar Model prediction as obtains for the charged current signal. Flavor mixing among active neutrinos is expected to occur on shorter baselines with smaller mixing amplitudes.

# Implications of Quark-Lepton Symmetry for Neutrino Masses and Oscillations

## Key claim
The paper argues that if quark-lepton symmetry holds and neutrino Majorana masses are much smaller than their Dirac masses, then long-baseline neutrino oscillations (such as solar and atmospheric) will be dominated by maximal mixing between active and sterile neutrinos, rather than between active flavors. The hedge is that this scenario depends on the assumption that sterile neutrino Majorana masses are small compared to Dirac masses, which contradicts the conventional see-saw mechanism.

## Method
The authors construct a theoretical scenario based on quark-lepton symmetry where each active neutrino has a sterile partner, forming a pseudo-Dirac bispinor. They assume the Dirac mass connecting active and sterile states is much larger than the sterile neutrino's Majorana mass, leading to nearly degenerate mass eigenstates with maximal mixing. They then apply this framework to existing solar and atmospheric neutrino oscillation data to derive predictions for the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (SNO) and other experiments.

## Key numbers
- The known Dirac fermion masses span a range of almost six orders of magnitude, while neutrino masses must be at least five to six orders of magnitude smaller still.
- The paper predicts that SNO will observe a reduction in the neutral current signal equal to that already found in the charged current signal (the specific numerical suppression is not given, as it depends on Standard Solar Model predictions).
- Flavor mixing among active neutrinos is expected to occur on shorter baselines with mixing amplitudes "much less than maximal" (no specific numerical values provided).

## Context
This work extends the pseudo-Dirac neutrino concept previously discussed by Wolfenstein and others, and contrasts with the conventional see-saw mechanism of Gell-Mann, Ramond, and Slansky which predicts large sterile neutrino masses. The scenario is consistent with the solar neutrino data from the Homestake and Kamiokande experiments, and with the Super-Kamiokande atmospheric neutrino results, though the authors note that active-to-sterile mixing for atmospheric neutrinos is not favored by current data. The paper enables a direct experimental test at SNO, where the neutral current measurement will distinguish between active-sterile and active-active oscillation scenarios.
