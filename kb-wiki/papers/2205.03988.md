# Azimuthal-sensitive three-dimensional HBT radius in Au-Au collisions at $E_{beam} = 1.23$$A$ GeV by the IQMD model

> We used an Isospin dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate Au + Au collisions at beam energy $E_{beam}$ = 1.23$A$ GeV, which corresponds to center of mass energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.4$ GeV. Firstly, we obtained reasonable rapidity and transverse mass spectra of $π^-$ and $π^+$ as well as "apparent" temperature parameters in comparison with the HADES data. Then by calculating three-dimensional Hanbury Brown and Twiss (HBT) radius of same charged pion-pairs, we obtained the square difference of outward radius and sideward radius, i.e. $R_{out}^{2}-R_{side}^{2}$, as well as the freeze-out volume ($V_{fo}$), which were basically consistent with the trend of HADES experimental results. The azimuthal dependence of the HBT radii was also calculated by a matrix method, and corrected by the rotation matrix. In addition, the eccentricities of the $xy$- and $zy$- planes of pion-pair emissions were also extracted for different pair transverse momentum and collision centrality, which show that the $xy$-eccentricity increases with pair transverse momentum as well as centrality but not for $zy$-eccentricity, indicating a more asymmetric transverse emission of particle-pairs with higher transverse momentum in off-central collisions.

## Key claim

The IQMD model reproduces the trend of HADES experimental results for three-dimensional HBT radii, $R_{out}^{2}-R_{side}^{2}$, and freeze-out volume in Au-Au collisions at $E_{beam}=1.23A$ GeV, but the model systematically overproduces pion yields by factors of approximately 0.54-0.58 relative to data, and the origin of this overshoot remains unclear.

## Method

The authors used the Isospin dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate 1,600,000 events of Au+Au collisions at $E_{beam}=1.23A$ GeV ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.4$ GeV). They calculated three-dimensional Hanbury Brown and Twiss (HBT) radii for same-charged pion pairs from the freeze-out phase-space data, using the Cartesian OSL coordinate system with event plane correction via the first-order event plane method. The azimuthal dependence of HBT radii was obtained through a spatial correlation tensor (S-matrix) method, and a rotation correction was applied to diagonalize the tensor and extract the tilt angle of the emission source.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: $E_{beam}=1.23A$ GeV, corresponding to $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.4$ GeV
- Ratio of experimental over model pion yields: $\frac{dn/dY_{HADES}}{dn/dY_{IQMD}}|_{\pi^{+}} = 0.54$, $\frac{dn/dY_{HADES}}{dn/dY_{IQMD}}|_{\pi^{-}} = 0.58$
- Apparent temperature parameters for $\pi^{+}$ in 0-10% centrality: $T_{1}^{IQMD}=52.83$ MeV, $T_{2}^{IQMD}=92.63$ MeV (HADES: $T_{1}=54$ MeV, $T_{2}=92$ MeV)
- Apparent temperature parameters for $\pi^{-}$ in 0-10% centrality: $T_{1}^{IQMD}=46.06$ MeV, $T_{2}^{IQMD}=89.96$ MeV (HADES: $T_{1}=46$ MeV, $T_{2}=91$ MeV)
- Spatial tilt angle of the reaction plane: $\theta_{s}=30.09^{\circ}$ for $\pi^{-}\pi^{-}$ pairs with mean transverse momentum 150-200 MeV/c in 10-30% centrality
- $R_{out}^{2}-R_{side}^{2}$ becomes negative for $m_t > 300$ MeV/c
- Wave packet width parameter $L=2.16$ fm$^2$ for Au+Au reactions
- In-medium correction factor $\eta=0.2$ for nucleon-nucleon cross section

## Context

This work extends HBT interferometry studies to the relatively low beam energy of 1.23$A$ GeV, where previous measurements from the HADES Collaboration (2020) provided experimental data for comparison. The study uses the IQMD model, which includes isospin degrees of freedom, to simulate collisions that produce pions primarily through $\Delta(1232)$ resonance decay. The azimuthal-sensitive HBT analysis using the matrix method follows the approach of previous works (e.g., HADES Collaboration, 2020) and enables extraction of the spatial tilt angle and eccentricities of the pion emission source, providing constraints on the dynamics of particle emission in off-central collisions at intermediate energies.
