# Nuclear physics uncertainties in neutrino-driven, neutron-rich supernova ejecta

> Neutrino-driven ejecta in core collapse supernovae (CCSNe) offer an interesting astrophysical scenario where lighter heavy elements between Sr and Ag can be synthesized. Previous studies emphasized the important role that ($α,n$) reactions play in the production of these elements, particularly in neutron-rich and alpha-rich environments. In this paper, we have investigated the sensitivity of elemental abundances to specific ($α,n$) reaction-rate uncertainties under different astrophysical conditions. Following a Monte Carlo nucleosynthesis study with over 36 representative astrophysical wind conditions, we have identified the most important reactions based on their impact on the final elemental abundances. Experimental studies of these reactions will reduce the nucleosynthesis uncertainties and make it possible to use observations to understand the origin of lighter heavy elements and the astrophysical conditions where they are formed.

## Key claim

The paper identifies the most important (α,n) reactions for the weak r-process in neutrino-driven supernova ejecta, but the authors hedge that the importance of specific reactions depends strongly on the astrophysical conditions (electron fraction, entropy, expansion timescale) and that the list of 45 key reactions is only valid for the "CPR2" group of trajectories where individual reaction rates have critical impact, not for all neutrino-driven ejecta conditions.

## Method

The authors selected 36 representative trajectories from a previous systematic study (Bliss et al. 2018) covering a broad range of neutrino-driven wind conditions (electron fractions 0.40-0.49, entropies 31-175 kB/nuc, expansion timescales 9.7-63.8 ms). For each trajectory, they performed over 10,000 Monte Carlo nucleosynthesis calculations using a reaction network, simultaneously varying 909 (α,n) reaction rates on nuclei from Fe (Z=26) to Rh (Z=45) within a factor of 10 uncertainty (log-normal distribution with σ=2.3). Key reactions were identified using Spearman's rank correlation coefficient between rate variation factors and final elemental abundances, combined with a requirement that the elemental abundance varies by at least a factor of 5 within 2-σ of the distribution.

## Key numbers

- 36 representative trajectories selected from the CPR2 group of the Bliss et al. (2018) systematic study, covering electron fractions Ye = 0.40-0.49, entropies 31-175 kB/nuc, and expansion timescales 9.7-63.8 ms
- 909 (α,n) reactions on stable and neutron-rich nuclei between Fe (Z=26) and Rh (Z=45) were varied simultaneously in each Monte Carlo run
- Over 10,000 network calculations were performed for each of the 36 trajectories
- Rate variation factors followed a log-normal distribution with σ = 2.3, corresponding to a factor of 10 uncertainty (0.1 to 10) with 68.3% probability
- 45 (α,n) reactions were identified as having an impact on elemental abundances between Z=36-47, satisfying |r_corr| ≥ 0.20 and an abundance variation of at least a factor of 5 within 2-σ of the average abundance
- The threshold for meaningful correlation was set at |r_corr| ≥ 0.20

## Context

This work extends the systematic nucleosynthesis study of Bliss et al. (2018), which classified neutrino-driven ejecta abundances into four patterns (NSE1, NSE2, CPR1, CPR2) based on neutron-to-seed and alpha-to-seed ratios at T=3 GK. Previous studies by Hoffman et al. (1996) and Wanajo (2006) emphasized that (α,n) reactions at N=50 are critical for the weak r-process. The paper enables future experimental measurements of the identified 45 reactions to reduce nucleosynthesis uncertainties, which will allow observations of elemental abundances in old stars (such as the Honda-like stars or r-limited stars) to constrain the astrophysical conditions in core-collapse supernova neutrino-driven winds.
