# First evidence for the J$>$1 components of the pygmy dipole resonance in neutron-rich nuclei

> Gamma ($γ$) decay shapes the synthesis of heavy elements in neutron-rich nuclear environments of neutron star mergers, supplying the Universe with heavy elements. The low-energy pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) influences nuclear reaction rates of the rapid nucleosynthesis through enhanced $γ$ transitions. However, since it is difficult to reproduce astrophysical conditions in laboratories, PDR was previously observed only in $J = 1$ spin states. Here we report the first experimental observation of $J > 1$ components of PDR, identified in the $β$-delayed $γ$ decay of the J$^π$ = 3$^{-}$ spin-parity isomer of $^{80}$Ga. The data analysis, combined with decay information and theoretical calculations allows the identification of resonant structures below the neutron emission threshold of the neutron-rich germanium $^{80}$Ge as J$^π = (2,3)^-$ components of the PDR built on the low-lying J$^π$ = 2$^+$ quadrupole state. Our findings extend the concept of PDR beyond dipole states, with implications for nuclear structure theory and experiment, as well as the element production in the cosmos.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first experimental observation of pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) components with spin-parity J>1, specifically J^π = (2,3)^- states in ^80Ge, identified via β-delayed γ decay of the J^π = 3^- isomer of ^80Ga, extending the PDR concept beyond the previously observed J=1 dipole states.

## Method

The experiment was performed at the ALTO ISOL facility, where a radioactive ^80g+mGa beam (containing both J^π = 3^- and J^π = 6^- isomers) was produced by photo-fission of a UC_x target with a 50-MeV electron beam. The β-delayed γ rays from ^80Ga decay were detected using a hybrid array consisting of a plastic scintillator for β-tagging, two HPGe detectors for high-resolution low-energy γ detection, and three PARIS clusters (phoswich detectors) for high-efficiency high-energy γ detection up to the Q_β value of 10.312(4) MeV. The data analysis combined γ-γ coincidence techniques, time-of-flight neutron-γ separation, and theoretical calculations within the relativistic equation of motion (REOM) method to identify resonant structures below the neutron emission threshold of ^80Ge.

## Key numbers

- Q_β value of ^80Ga: 10.312(4) MeV relative to the ground state of ^80Ge (Wang 2017)
- PARIS energy resolution: 112 keV at 7.18 MeV; detection efficiency: 0.30(2)% at 7.18 MeV under mode 3 (add-back within three clusters)
- HPGe energy resolution: 2.611 keV at 1083.6 keV
- ^80g+mGa beam intensity: ~10^4 particles per second
- β-tagging efficiency: ~70%
- High-energy γ rays identified with E1 multipolarities at energies: 3664.6, 3818.6, 3920.4, 4225.1, 4412.8, 4665.3, 4678.5, 5387.4, 7181, and 7337 keV
- Excited states identified at 7840 keV and 7996 keV with J^π = (2,3)^-, located below the neutron separation threshold S_n

## Context

This work extends previous PDR studies that were restricted to J=1 spin states observed in stable or near-stable nuclei using electromagnetic probes such as bremsstrahlung, Compton backscattering, and Coulomb excitation (Bracco 2019, Savran 2013). It builds on pioneering β-decay experiments by Scheck (2016), Gottardo (2017), and Mashtakov (2021) that demonstrated the effectiveness of β-decay for studying J=1 PDR states. The findings enable future experimental and theoretical studies of multicomponent PDR and its impact on r-process nucleosynthesis in neutron star mergers, where excited-state PDR components influence neutron capture and photodisintegration rates.
