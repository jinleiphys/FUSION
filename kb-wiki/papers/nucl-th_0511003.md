# An Effective Theory for the Four-Body System with Short-Range Interactions

> We discuss the application of an effective theory with contact interactions to the non-relativistic four-body system with short-range interactions. We have computed the binding energies of the 4He tetramer and the alpha-particle. A well-known linear correlation between three- and four-body binding energies can be understood as a result of the absence of a four-body force at leading order within this framework. Further, we give results for the binding energies of the four-boson system in two spatial dimensions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that an effective field theory with only contact interactions, requiring a three-body force at leading order but no four-body force, can describe four-body systems with large scattering length; the well-known linear correlation (Tjon-line) between three- and four-body binding energies follows directly from the absence of a four-body force at leading order.

## Method

The author solved the Yakubovsky equations for the four-body system using a regulated effective potential with contact interactions at leading order, with a separable two-body interaction and a three-body force whose coupling constant was fixed by the shallowest three-body bound state energy. The two-body coupling constant was matched to either the scattering length or the binding energy of the two-body system, and the cutoff dependence was analyzed to determine whether a four-body force was needed for renormalization.

## Key numbers

- $^4$He tetramer ground state binding energy: 492 mK (compared to 559.7 mK from Blume and Greene)
- $^4$He tetramer excited state binding energy: 128 mK (compared to 132.7 mK from Blume and Greene)
- $\alpha$-particle binding energy: 29.5 MeV (using triplet scattering length as input) or 26.9 MeV (using deuteron binding energy as input); experimental value is 29.0 ± 0.1 MeV
- Four-boson system in 2D ground state binding energy: 197.3(1) times the two-body binding energy $B_2$
- Four-boson system in 2D excited state binding energy: 25.5(1) times $B_2$
- Ratio $B_4^{(0)}/B_3^{(0)}$ in 2D: 11.94, compared to the asymptotic large-N prediction of 8.567

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach previously applied to three-body systems by Bedaque (1998) and Braaten (2004) to the four-body system, using the same contact-interaction framework that exhibits a limit cycle in the three-body sector. The results for the $^4$He tetramer agree with the microscopic calculations of Blume and Greene (2000) using the LM2M2 potential, and the $\alpha$-particle binding energy agrees with the experimental value to within 10%. The linear correlation (Tjon-line) between three- and four-body binding energies, long known in nuclear physics from phenomenological potential calculations by Nogga (2000) and chiral EFT calculations by Epelbaum (2002), is shown to be a universal consequence of the absence of a four-body force at leading order. The 2D results extend the asymptotic predictions for large N-boson droplets by Hammer (2004) to the N=4 case.
