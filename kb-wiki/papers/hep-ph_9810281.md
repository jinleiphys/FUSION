# Signatures of Quark-Gluon-Plasma formation in high energy heavy-ion collisions: A critical review

> A critical review on signatures of Quark-Gluon-Plasma formation is given and the current (1998) experimental status is discussed. After giving an introduction to the properties of QCD matter in both, equilibrium- and non-equilibrium theories, we focus on observables which may yield experimental evidence for QGP formation. For each individual observable the discussion is divided into three sections: first the connection between the respective observable and QGP formation in terms of the underlying theoretical concepts is given, then the relevant experimental results are reviewed and finally the current status concerning the interpretation of both, theory and experiment, is discussed. A comprehensive summary including an outlook towards RHIC is given in the final section.

## Key claim

This critical review argues that no single observable can unambiguously prove Quark-Gluon-Plasma (QGP) formation in high-energy heavy-ion collisions; instead, a strategy of collecting circumstantial evidence from multiple signals, measured simultaneously as excitation functions across varying beam energies, system sizes, and impact parameters, is required to identify anomalous behavior consistent with a phase transition.

## Method

The authors survey the theoretical foundations of QCD matter in both equilibrium (lattice gauge theory, perturbation theory) and non-equilibrium (parton cascades, hadronic transport, nuclear fluid dynamics) frameworks. For each proposed QGP observable, they present the underlying theoretical connection to deconfinement, review the relevant experimental results from AGS and SPS programs (as of 1998), and critically assess the current status of interpretation, including ambiguities and limitations.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature from quenched lattice QCD: T_C = 265^(+10)_(-5) MeV (no dynamical quarks)
- Critical temperature from lattice QCD with dynamical quarks at mu_B=0: T_C ~ 140 MeV
- Energy densities achievable at AGS and SPS: 0.5–10 GeV/fm^3
- Temperature range at AGS and SPS: 100–200 MeV
- At energy densities ~20 GeV/fm^3, temperature ~600 MeV and coupling constant g ~ 2
- Predicted thermalization time scale at RHIC: 0.3–0.5 fm/c
- Chemical equilibrium time scale: several fm/c
- Estimated SPS energy density at proper time tau_0 ~ 1 fm/c: epsilon ~ 1 to 5 GeV/fm^3
- Baryon densities at SPS: up to rho <= 1 fm^(-3)
- Extrapolated RHIC energy densities: up to 20 GeV/fm^3 at rho ~ 2 rho_0
- Entropy per baryon ratio at LHC: estimated 10^3 to 10^4 (early universe: 10^9)
- Liquid-gas phase transition temperature in nuclear matter: T ~ 5 MeV

## Context

This review synthesizes and critically evaluates the extensive body of work from the AGS and SPS experimental programs (1986–1998) and the theoretical developments in lattice QCD, transport models, and hydrodynamics. It extends earlier reviews by Shuryak (1980), McLerran (1986), and Stock (1986), among others, while highlighting unresolved issues such as the unknown order of the QCD phase transition at finite baryon density and the questionable applicability of perturbative QCD at experimentally accessible temperatures. The paper sets the stage for the upcoming RHIC experimental program by identifying which observables require simultaneous anomalous behavior to build a circumstantial case for QGP formation.
