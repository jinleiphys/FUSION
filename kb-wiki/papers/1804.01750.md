# Constituent-quark model with pionic contributions: electromagnetic $N\rightarrowΔ$ transition

> We report on ongoing work to determine the pion-cloud contribution to the electromagnetic $N\rightarrowΔ$ transition form factors. The starting point is an $SU(6)$ spin-flavor symmetric constituent-quark model with instantaneous confinement that is augmented by dynamical pions which couple directly to the quarks. This system is treated in a relativistically invariant way within the framework of point-form quantum mechanics using a multichannel formulation. The first step is to determine the electromagnetic form factors of the bare particles that consist only of three quarks. These form factors are basic ingredients for calculating the pion-cloud contributions. Already without the pion cloud, electromagnetic nucleon and $N\rightarrow Δ$ transition form factors compare reasonably well with the data. By inclusion of the pion-cloud contribution coming from the $π$-$N$ intermediate state the reproduction of the data is further improved.

## Key claim

The pion-cloud contribution to the electromagnetic \(N\rightarrow\Delta\) transition form factors is calculated within a constituent-quark model with dynamical pions, and while the inclusion of the \(\pi\)-\(N\) intermediate state improves agreement with data, the calculation is still incomplete and additional contributions from \(\pi\Delta_0\) intermediate states are expected to be needed, especially at low \(Q^2\).

## Method

The authors start from an \(SU(6)\) spin-flavor symmetric constituent-quark model with instantaneous confinement, augmented by dynamical pions coupling directly to quarks, and treat the system relativistically using point-form quantum mechanics with a multichannel formulation. They first determine the electromagnetic form factors of the bare three-quark particles (nucleon \(N_0\) and Delta \(\Delta_0\)), then calculate the one-photon-exchange amplitude for \(e^- p \rightarrow e^- \Delta^+\) scattering including the \(\pi\)-\(N\) intermediate state, and extract the transition form factors from the microscopic current in the infinite-momentum frame using three specific spin-matrix elements.

## Key numbers

* The constituent-quark mass is set to \(m_q = 0.263\) GeV, taken from the literature.
* The Gaussian range parameter for the three-quark wave function is \(\alpha = 2.645\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
* The pseudovector pion-quark coupling constant is \(f_{\pi q q} = 0.678\).
* The bare nucleon and Delta mass (degenerate) is \(m_0 = 1.67\) GeV.
* For \(Q^2 \gtrsim 0.5\) GeV\(^2\), predictions for \(G_M^*\) agree well with data; for vanishing \(Q^2\), the model underestimates the data by about \(25\%\).
* Pion-cloud effects are significant for \(G_E^*\) and the ratio \(R_{EM}\) for \(Q^2 \lesssim 1\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends prior constituent-quark model calculations of electromagnetic form factors by the same group (Biernat 2009, GomezRocha 2012, Biernat 2014) and addresses the role of the pion cloud in the \(N\rightarrow\Delta\) transition, which has been studied in other models such as those by Ramalho (2008) and Sanchis-Alepuz (2017). The results enable future inclusion of \(\pi\Delta_0\) intermediate states and more sophisticated wave functions (e.g., \(d\)-wave contributions as in Ramalho 2008) to improve agreement with experimental data at low \(Q^2\).
