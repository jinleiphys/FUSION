# Study of the nucleon-induced preequilibrium reactions in terms of the Quantum Molecular Dynamics

> The preequilibrium (nucleon-in, nucleon-out) angular distributions of $^{27}$Al, $^{58}$Ni and $^{90}$Zr have been analyzed in the energy region from 90 to 200 MeV in terms of the Quantum Moleculear Dynamics (QMD) theory. First, we show that the present approach can reproduce the measured (p,xp') and (p,xn) angular distributions leading to continuous final states without adjusing any parameters. Second, we show the results of the detailed study of the preequilibrium reaction processes; the step-wise contribution to the angular distribution, comparison with the quantum-mechanical Feshbach-Kerman-Koonin theory, the effects of momentum distribution and surface refraction/reflection to the quasifree scattering. Finally, the present method was used to assess the importance of multiple preequilibrium particle emission as a function of projectile energy up to 1 GeV.

## Key claim

The Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model can reproduce measured (p,xp') and (p,xn) angular distributions for 27Al, 58Ni, and 90Zr at 90 to 200 MeV without any parameter adjustment, resolving the backward-angle underestimation problem present in other semiclassical models and the forward/backward-angle problems in cascade models.

## Method

The authors represented each nucleon by a Gaussian wave packet in phase space and solved Newtonian equations of motion with a Skyrme-type effective N-N interaction plus Coulomb and symmetry energy terms, incorporating stochastic nucleon-nucleon collisions with Pauli blocking. They generated ground states of target nuclei using Woods-Saxon coordinate distributions and local Thomas-Fermi momentum distributions, then simulated 50,000 events per system to compute double-differential cross sections. The step number of each emitted nucleon was tracked by counting collisions: the projectile-target nucleon collision was step 1, and subsequent collisions incremented the step number as the sum of the colliding particles' step numbers plus one.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.168 fm⁻³, Skyrme parameters A = −124 MeV, B = 70.5 MeV, τ = 4/3, giving binding energy/nucleon of 16 MeV and compressibility of 237.7 MeV (soft equation of state)
- Width parameter L = 2 fm² for Gaussian wave packets, fixed to give stable ground states across a wide mass range
- Symmetry energy coefficient Cₛ = 25 MeV
- For 58Ni(p,xp') at 120 and 200 MeV, and 90Zr(p,xp') and (p,xn) at 160 MeV, QMD reproduced measured angular distributions from very forward to backward angles without the underestimation seen in other semiclassical models
- The 1-step QMD cross section for 90Zr(p,n) at 160 MeV showed no steep drop at 0 degrees, unlike uniform Fermi gas calculations, due to refraction effects
- Multiple preequilibrium emission (3rd, 4th, and 5th particles) contributed about 30% of the inclusive proton yield above 25 MeV at 500 MeV incident energy, and almost 50% at 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the QMD approach of Peilert (1992) and Niita (1995) to systematically study preequilibrium angular distributions and multiple preequilibrium emission, addressing open problems in semiclassical models such as the Geometry-Dependent Hybrid model of Blann (1984) and the cascade model of Bertini (1974), which underestimated backward and forward-angle cross sections respectively. The results are compared with the quantum-mechanical Feshbach-Kerman-Koonin (FKK) theory of Feshbach (1980), showing that QMD avoids the need to adjust the effective N-N cross section (V₀ parameter) that FKK requires depending on projectile, ejectile, target, and incident energy as noted by Watanabe (1995). The findings enable parameter-free predictions of nucleon-induced reactions up to 1 GeV, relevant to spallation neutron source design and the LAMPF measurements of vonach (1994, 1995).
