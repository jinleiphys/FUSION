# Electromagnetic fields from quantum sources in heavy-ion collisions

> We compute the electromagnetic field created by an ultrarelativistic charged particle in vacuum at distances comparable to the particle Compton wavelength. The wave function of the particle is governed by the Klein-Gordon equation, for a scalar particle, or the Dirac equation, for a spin-half particle. The produced electromagnetic field is essentially different in magnitude and direction from the Coulomb field, induced by a classical point charge, due to the quantum diffusion effect. Thus, a realistic computation of the electromagnetic field produced in heavy-ion collisions must be based upon the full quantum treatment of the valence quarks.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic field produced by an ultrarelativistic charged particle at distances comparable to its Compton wavelength is essentially different in magnitude and direction from the classical Coulomb field due to quantum diffusion of the particle wave function, meaning that realistic computations of electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions require a full quantum treatment of valence quarks rather than the classical point-particle approximation.

## Method

The authors model valence quarks as Gaussian wave packets with width parameter a = 1 fm, evolving the wave function according to the Klein-Gordon equation for scalar particles or the Dirac equation for spin-1/2 particles. They compute the charge and current densities from the wave functions, then calculate the retarded electromagnetic fields in the particle rest frame and Lorentz-transform them to the laboratory frame where the particle moves with velocity v along the z-axis.

## Key numbers

- Wave packet width parameter a = 1 fm used in all calculations
- Quark mass m = 0.3 GeV used in calculations
- Lorentz factor γ = 100 used in calculations
- At t = 0.25 fm, the magnitude of the magnetic field from quantum sources is an order of magnitude larger than the classical field
- The classical description breaks down at distances as large as 6 fm at γ = 100
- At large impact parameter b, the convective current contributes to the monopole term falling off as 1/b², while the leading spin current contribution starts with the dipole term falling off as 1/b³

## Context

This work extends the classical treatment of electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions by Kharzeev (2007), Skokov (2009), and Voronyuk (2011), who treated valence quarks as point particles. The authors show that the classical approximation is inadequate at distances relevant to heavy-ion collisions, and that quantum diffusion produces a sign-flip effect in the magnetic field that is absent in the classical calculation. This enables more realistic computations of electromagnetic field effects in heavy-ion collisions by incorporating the full quantum treatment of valence quarks.
