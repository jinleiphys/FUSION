# Renormalization of EFT for nucleon-nucleon scattering

> The renormalization of EFT for nucleon-nucleon scattering in nonperturbative regimes is investigated in a compact parametrization of the $T$-matrix. The key difference between perturbative and nonperturbative renormalization is clarified. The underlying theory perspective and the 'fixing' of the prescriptions for the $T$-matrix from physical boundary conditions are stressed.

## Key claim
The paper argues that in nonperturbative nucleon-nucleon scattering, conventional perturbative counter-term renormalization of the potential fails to remove divergences from the T-matrix; instead, the divergent quantity \(\mathcal{G}\) (which contains all loop iterations) must be renormalized separately, and the renormalization prescription must be "locked" with the potential to satisfy physical boundary conditions.

## Method
The author reformulates the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for nucleon-nucleon scattering into a compact nonperturbative parametrization \(T^{-1} = V^{-1} - \mathcal{G}\), where \(V\) is the potential and \(\mathcal{G}\) encodes all loop-generated rescattering effects. Using this form, the paper demonstrates analytically that introducing counter terms into \(V\) cannot simultaneously cancel divergences in both the numerator and denominator of \(\mathcal{G}\) for any nontrivial momentum-dependent potential, except in the simplest contact-interaction case. The analysis is then extended to argue that renormalization must instead be performed at the integral level or via "endogenous" counter terms that enter before the nonperturbative summation (e.g., in a Schrödinger equation approach).

## Key numbers
- The on-shell unitarity relation is given as \(T^{-1}_{os} = K^{-1}_{os} + i\mu \frac{\sqrt{2\mu E}}{2\pi}\), with \(\mu\) the reduced mass and \(E\) the center-of-mass energy.
- A primary example shows phase shifts for the \(^1D_2\) channel predicted using a single fitted parameter \(Re(\mathcal{G}_{l=2}) \approx g_{l=2;0}\) for energies \(50\ \text{MeV} \le E \le 200\ \text{MeV}\), with potentials at leading order (LO), next-to-leading order (NLO), and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO).
- The low-energy expansion of \(Re(\mathcal{G}_l(p))\) is given as a Padé or Taylor series: \(g_{l;0} + g_{l;1} p^2 + g_{l;2} p^4 + \cdots\), with coefficients to be determined from empirical phase shifts at low energies (e.g., \(E \leq 50\ \text{MeV}\)).

## Context
This work extends the ongoing debate on renormalization and power counting in chiral effective field theory for nuclear forces, following foundational work by Weinberg (1990s) and subsequent proposals by van Kolck (1999), Epelbaum et al. (2000), Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998), and Beane et al. (2002). The paper clarifies why exogenous counter-term subtraction fails in nonperturbative regimes, supporting the Schrödinger-equation approach of Beane et al. (2002) and the integral-subtraction methods of Gegelia (1999) and Nieves (2003). It also suggests a new semi-phenomenological strategy: fitting the low-energy coefficients of \(\mathcal{G}\) directly to empirical phase-shift data, which could test the convergence of chiral expansions without explicit loop calculations.
