# Consequences Of Fully Dressing Quark-Gluon Vertex Function With Two-Point Gluon Lines

> We extend recent studies of the effects of quark-gluon vertex dressing upon the solutions of the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator. A momentum delta function is used to represent the dominant infrared strength of the effective gluon propagator so that the resulting integral equations become algebraic. The quark-gluon vertex is constructed from the complete set of diagrams involving only 2-point gluon lines. The additional diagrams, including those with crossed gluon lines, are shown to make an important contribution to the DSE solutions for the quark propagator, because of their large color factors and the rapid growth in their number.

## Key claim

The paper extends previous studies of quark-gluon vertex dressing by including all possible diagrams constructed from two-point gluon lines (including crossed gluon diagrams), and finds that these additional contributions produce much larger corrections to the quark propagator than earlier ladder-rainbow or improved ladder-rainbow approximations, with no evident convergence as the number of gluon lines increases.

## Method

The authors employ the Munczek-Nemirovsky Ansatz for the gluon propagator, which uses a momentum delta function to represent the dominant infrared strength, reducing the Dyson-Schwinger integral equations to algebraic ones. They develop a recursive algorithm to generate all vertex diagrams with exactly n gluon lines constructed from two-point gluon functions, compute the Dirac algebra and color factors using computer-algebraic methods, and solve the quark gap equation self-consistently with the dressed vertex up to n=6 gluon lines. They also introduce a phenomenological parameter C to partially account for the effects of three-gluon coupling, and employ a Pade approximant to attempt resummation of the quark self-energy contributions.

## Key numbers

* The interaction mass scale is set to G = 1 GeV and the current quark mass is m = 0.0175 G = 17.5 MeV for the numerical solutions.
* For n=2 gluon lines, there are 4 diagrams in the full vertex scheme, compared to 1 in ladder-rainbow summed and 3 in the improved ladder-rainbow scheme.
* For n=3 gluon lines, there are 27 diagrams in the full vertex scheme, compared to 1 in ladder-rainbow summed and 6 in the improved ladder-rainbow scheme.
* For n=6 gluon lines, there are 38,232 diagrams in the full vertex scheme, compared to 1 in ladder-rainbow summed and 21 in the improved ladder-rainbow scheme.
* For n=7 gluon lines, the full vertex scheme includes approximately 5 x 10^5 diagrams, which the authors deemed infeasible to calculate.
* The color factor for the crossed gluon diagram (Fig. 1d) is (N_c^2 + 1)/(2N_c)^2, while the ladder-type diagrams (Figs. 1a, 1b, 1c) have color factor 1/(2N_c)^2.
* The phenomenological parameter C for simulating three-gluon coupling takes values in the range -1/8 to 1 for N_c = 3, with the authors exploring C = 0.5.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quark-gluon vertex dressing in Dyson-Schwinger equation approaches to QCD, specifically the ladder-rainbow truncation of Maris and Tandy (1999) and the improved ladder-rainbow summation of Matevosyan, Thomas, and Tandy (2006). The authors find that including crossed gluon diagrams (omitted in prior work) produces significantly larger corrections than the earlier improvements, and that convergence with respect to the number of gluon lines is not achieved even at n=6. The results suggest that a consistent treatment involving two-, three-, and four-point gluon functions as well as ghost contributions might be necessary for a convergent summation, and that the Munczek-Nemirovsky model's simplified gluon propagator may introduce artifacts that limit the reliability of the approach.
