# Coupled-channels study of Lambda K and Sigma K states in the chiral SU(3) quark model

> The $S$-wave $ΛK$ and $ΣK$ states with isospin $I=1/2$ are dynamically investigated within the framework of the chiral SU(3) quark model by solving a resonating group method (RGM) equation. The model parameters are taken from our previous work, which gave a good description of the energies of the baryon ground states, the binding energy of the deuteron, and the experimental data of the nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) and nucleon-hyperon ($NY$) scattering. Assumed not to give important contributions in the scattering processes, the s-channel quark-antiquark ($q\bar q$) annihilation interactions are not included as a first step. The results show a strong attraction between $Σ$ and $K$, which consequently results in a $ΣK$ quasi-bound state with about 17 MeV binding energy, unlike the case of $ΛK$ which is unbound. When the channel coupling of $ΛK$ and $ΣK$ is considered, a sharp resonance state near 1670 MeV with spin-parity $J^P=1/2^-$ is found. The narrow gap of the $ΛK$ and $ΣK$ thresholds, the strong attraction between $Σ$ and $K$, and the sizeable off-diagonal matrix elements of $ΛK$ and $ΣK$ are responsible for the appearance of this resonance.

# Coupled-channels study of Lambda K and Sigma K states in the chiral SU(3) quark model

## Key claim

The chiral SU(3) quark model, with s-channel quark-antiquark annihilation interactions omitted as a first step, predicts a sharp resonance near 1670 MeV with spin-parity J^P=1/2^- when channel coupling of Lambda K and Sigma K is considered; this resonance is argued to be a candidate for N*(1650) rather than N*(1535), though the authors caution that coupling to N-eta and N-pi channels must be included before a final identification can be made.

## Method

The authors solved the resonating group method (RGM) equation for S-wave Lambda K and Sigma K systems with isospin I=1/2 within the chiral SU(3) quark model, using model parameters previously fixed by baryon ground-state energies, deuteron binding energy, and nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-hyperon scattering data. One-channel calculations were performed for each system separately, followed by a two-channel coupled calculation including both Lambda K and Sigma K channels; the s-channel quark-antiquark annihilation interactions were omitted as a preliminary step.

## Key numbers

- Sigma K quasi-bound state binding energy: about 17 MeV, obtained from a one-channel RGM bound-state calculation
- Resonance mass from coupled-channel calculation: M approximately 1669 MeV
- Resonance width from coupled-channel calculation: Gamma approximately 5 MeV
- Mass difference between Lambda K and Sigma K thresholds: about 78 MeV
- Model parameters: up/down quark mass m_u=313 MeV, strange quark mass m_s=470 MeV, harmonic-oscillator width b_u=0.5 fm, sigma meson mass m_sigma=595 MeV, cutoff Lambda=1100 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) quark model, previously applied to nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon scattering by Zhang et al. (1997) and Dai et al. (2003), to baryon-meson systems. The finding of a strong Sigma-K attraction qualitatively agrees with the effective chiral Lagrangian calculations of Kaiser et al. (1995, 1997), who argued that S11(1535) is a quasi-bound Lambda K - Sigma K state. The present results suggest the resonance near 1670 MeV prefers to be N*(1650) rather than N*(1535), but the authors note that coupling to N-eta and N-pi channels (as studied by Schutz et al., 1998) and to a genuine three-quark component must be included to resolve the nature of the S11 resonances. The work also relates to the BES Collaboration's observation of a threshold enhancement in Lambda K invariant mass from J/psi decays.
