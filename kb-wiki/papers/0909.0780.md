# Thermalization through parton transport

> A radiative transport model is used to study kinetic equilibration during the early stage of a relativistic heavy ion collision. The parton system is found to be able to overcome expansion and move toward thermalization via parton collisions. Scaling behaviors show up in both the pressure anisotropy and the energy density evolutions. In particular, the pressure anisotropy evolution shows an approximate alpha_s scaling when radiative processes are included. It approaches an asymptotic time evolution on a time scale of 1 to 2 fm/c. The energy density evolution shows an asymptotic time evolution that decreases slower than the ideal hydro evolution. These observations indicate that partial thermalization can be achieved and viscosity is important for the evolution during the early longitudinal expansion stage of a relativistic heavy ion collision.

# Thermalization through parton transport

## Key claim
A radiative transport model including medium-dependent cross sections and parton-number-changing processes (2-to-3 and 3-to-2) shows that a parton system can overcome longitudinal expansion and move toward thermalization, but only partial thermalization is achieved; the pressure anisotropy evolution exhibits an approximate alpha_s scaling (rather than the alpha_s T_0 scaling seen with 2-to-2 collisions alone), and the energy density decreases slower than ideal hydrodynamic evolution, indicating that viscosity remains important.

## Method
The author used a radiative transport model based on the AMPT framework, incorporating pQCD 2-to-2 (gg to gg) cross sections regulated by a Debye screening mass that depends on local medium density, plus 2-to-3 (gg to ggg) cross sections taken as one-half of the 2-to-2 cross section following Xu and Greiner, with 3-to-2 rates determined by detailed balance. Simulations were performed for a box of 5x5x5 fm^3 with 2000 particles at 1 GeV temperature to study chemical equilibration, and for expanding systems with initial energy densities of 38 and 77 GeV/fm^3 (corresponding to initial temperatures 0.5 and 1.0 GeV) and coupling constants alpha_s = 0.3 and 0.6, starting from either isotropic or transverse initial distributions.

## Key numbers
- Initial energy densities used: 38 GeV/fm^3 (T_0 = 0.5 GeV) and 77 GeV/fm^3 (T_0 = 1.0 GeV)
- Coupling constants used: alpha_s = 0.3 and 0.6
- Pressure anisotropy approaches an asymptotic time evolution on a time scale of 1 to 2 fm/c
- In a box of 5x5x5 fm^3 with 2000 particles at T = 1 GeV and alpha_s = 0.4, the system reaches chemical and kinetic equilibrium by 3 fm/c
- Energy density evolution at late times follows 1/tau^{1.25} for alpha_s = 0.6, compared to 1/tau for free streaming and 1/tau^{1.33} for ideal hydrodynamics

## Context
This work extends the AMPT model studies of isotropization by Zhang (2008) and others (Xu 2007, Huovinen 2008, El 2009) by introducing medium-dependent cross sections and radiative (2-to-3 and 3-to-2) processes beyond the fixed 2-to-2 partonic cross sections used previously. The finding that the pressure anisotropy shows approximate alpha_s scaling rather than alpha_s T_0 scaling, and that the energy density falls slower than ideal hydro, quantifies the role of viscosity during the early longitudinal expansion stage and enables more realistic modeling of the approach to thermalization in relativistic heavy ion collisions.
