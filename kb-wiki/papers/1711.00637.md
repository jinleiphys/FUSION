# Investigating the quark flavor dependence of the chiral magnetic effect with a multiphase transport model

> Because the properties of the QCD phase transition and the chiral magnetic effect (CME) depend on the number of quark flavors ($N_{f}$) and quark mass, relativistic heavy-ion collisions provide a natural environment to investigate the flavor features if quark deconfinement occurs. We introduce an initial two-flavor or three-flavor dipole charge separation into a multiphase transport (AMPT) model to investigate the flavor dependence of the CME. By taking advantage of the recent ALICE data of charge azimuthal correlations with identified hadrons, we attempt to disentangle two-flavor and three-flavor CME scenarios in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV. We find that the experimental data show a certain potential to distinguish the two scenarios, therefore we further suggest to collect more data to clarify the possible flavor dependence in future experiments.

# Investigating the quark flavor dependence of the chiral magnetic effect with a multiphase transport model

## Key claim
The experimental data show a certain potential to distinguish two-flavor and three-flavor CME scenarios in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, but within current experimental uncertainties it is difficult to conclude which flavor scenario the ALICE data favor, and more statistics are required.

## Method
The authors introduced a centrality-dependent initial dipole charge separation into the AMPT model with string melting mechanism, assuming the separation percentage is proportional to the impact-parameter-dependent magnetic field from Deng (2012). They implemented both two-flavor (u and d quarks only) and three-flavor (u, d, and s quarks) charge separations by switching the transverse momentum components of upward- and downward-moving quarks and antiquarks, then compared the simulated charge azimuthal correlations with ALICE data for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV.

## Key numbers
- The relative CME current difference between two-flavor and three-flavor cases is 1/6, arising from trace coefficients of 5/9 for two-flavor CME and 2/3 for three-flavor CME.
- The initial charge separation is introduced around the mean formation time of partons, i.e. 2–3 fm/c in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV from the AMPT model.
- The original AMPT model without charge separation reproduces 60–70% magnitude of the CME observable γ in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, and an initial charge separation percentage of ~10% was needed to match STAR data in prior work (Ma 2011).
- The centrality bin of 30-50% was used for the kaon-hadron correlation analysis.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous AMPT-based CME study (Ma 2011) by introducing a centrality-dependent charge separation percentage proportional to the magnetic field from Deng (2012), and by considering both two-flavor and three-flavor scenarios. It responds to the ALICE measurement of identified hadron triggered charge azimuthal correlations (Onderwaater 2015), which enables access to flavor properties of the CME and avoids contamination from ρ meson decays (Zhao 2017). The results suggest that kaon-hadron correlations have potential to distinguish flavor scenarios, but higher statistics are needed to clarify the flavor dependence and further understand QCD phase transitions.
