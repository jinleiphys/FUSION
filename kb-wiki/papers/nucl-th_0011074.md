# Trouble in Asymptopia---the Hulthen Model on the Light Front

> We use light-front dynamics to calculate the electromagnetic form-factor for the Hulthen model of the deuteron. For small momentum transfer Q^2 < 5 GeV^2 the relativistic effects are quite small. For Q^2 = 11 GeV^2 there is about a 13% discrepancy between the relativistic and non-relativistic approaches. For asymptotically large momentum transfer, however, the light-front form factor, log Q^2 /Q^4, markedly differs from the non-relativistic version, 1/Q^4. This behavior is also present for any wave function, such as those obtained from realistic potential models, which can be represented as a sum of Yukawa functions. Furthermore, the asymptotic behavior is in disagreement with the Drell-Yan-West relation. We investigate precisely how to determine the asymptotic behavior and confront the problem underlying troublesome form factors on the light front.

## Key claim

The light-front electromagnetic form factor for the Hulthen deuteron model behaves as log(Q²)/Q⁴ at asymptotically large momentum transfer, which differs from the non-relativistic 1/Q⁴ behavior and violates the Drell-Yan-West relation; this asymptotic behavior arises because the series expansion in 1/Q does not converge uniformly in the light-front momentum fraction x, and the effect is present for any wave function representable as a sum of Yukawa functions.

## Method

The authors solved the Weinberg equation for two equal-mass nucleons interacting via the Hulthen potential, using an auxiliary operator p³ = (x - 1/2)√[(p_perp² + m²)/(x(1-x))] to map the light-front bound-state equation onto a Schrodinger-like form. They then computed the electromagnetic form factor F(Q²) from the light-front wave function using the standard integral over the light-front momentum fraction x and transverse momentum p_perp, and compared it to the non-relativistic limit obtained by taking m → ∞. The asymptotic behavior was extracted by introducing a cutoff λ in the x-integral to regularize the divergent end-point region, and the resulting functional form was verified against the exact numerical calculation.

## Key numbers

- For Q² = 11 GeV², there is approximately a 13% discrepancy between the relativistic and non-relativistic form factors
- The non-relativistic asymptotic limit is 0.080585 (GeV)⁴ / Q⁴
- The light-front root-mean-square deuteron radius is 1.9467 fm, compared to the non-relativistic value of 1.9395 fm (a 0.37% difference)
- The asymptotic form factor follows F(Q²) ~ (α + β log(Q/m)) / Q⁴ with empirically determined coefficients α = 0.039472 and β = 0.044930 at z = Q/m ≈ 400
- The coefficient β predicted from the regularization integral is 0.045020, differing by only 0.20% from the empirical value
- The Hulthen model parameters are a = 0.23161 fm⁻¹ and b = 1.3802 fm⁻¹

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts by Brodsky and Lepage (1980) and others to apply light-front dynamics to nuclear models, specifically following the approach of using quantum-mechanical potential models on the light front rather than full quantum field theory. The authors show that the asymptotic behavior they find also appears in the Coulomb potential model and in realistic deuteron wave functions parameterized as sums of Yukawa functions (as used by Machleidt, 1989). The result contradicts the Drell-Yan-West relation and is similar to the asymptotic behavior found for the Wick-Cutkosky model by Vinokurov (1972). The paper identifies that potentials behaving as 1/r at small distances cause logarithmic divergences in the asymptotic expansion, and notes that in QCD such logarithms are removed by evolution of the longitudinal momentum amplitude, but in these potential models no such higher-order corrections exist.
