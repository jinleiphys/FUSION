# Nonlinear coupling of flow harmonics: Hexagonal flow and beyond

> Higher Fourier harmonics of anisotropic flow ($v_4$ and beyond) get large contributions induced by elliptic and triangular flow through nonlinear response. We present a general framework of nonlinear hydrodynamic response which encompasses the existing one, and allows to take into account the mutual correlation between the nonlinear couplings affecting Fourier harmonics of any order. Using Large Hadron Collider data on Pb+Pb collisions at ~$\sqrt[]{s}=2.76$ TeV, we perform an application of our formalism to hexagonal flow, $v_6$, a coefficient affected by several nonlinear contributions which are of the same order of magnitude. We obtain the first experimental measure of the coefficient $χ_{624}$, which couples $v_6$ to $v_2$ and $v_4$. This is achieved by putting together the information from several analyses: event-plane correlations, symmetric cumulants, as well as new higher-order moments recently analyzed by the ALICE collaboration. The value of $χ_{624}$ extracted from data is in fair agreement with hydrodynamic calculations, although with large error bars, which would be dramatically reduced by a dedicated analysis. We argue that within our formalism the nonlinear structure of a given higher harmonic can be determined more accurately than the harmonic itself, and we emphasize potential applications to future measurements of $v_7$ and $v_8$.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general framework for isolating nonlinear hydrodynamic response contributions to higher-order flow harmonics that accounts for mutual correlations between nonlinear terms, which previous analyses had assumed to be independent. The authors obtain the first experimental measure of the coefficient χ₆₂₄ coupling v₆ to v₂ and v₄, though with large error bars that would be dramatically reduced by a dedicated analysis.

## Method

The authors decompose higher-order flow harmonics into linear combinations of products of lower-order harmonics plus a residual uncorrelated term, then solve a linear system of equations by inverting the correlation matrix of the nonlinear terms. For hexagonal flow v₆, they extract the matrix elements from existing ALICE and ATLAS data on Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV, combining event-plane correlations, symmetric cumulants, and higher-order moments. They compare the extracted response coefficients to hydrodynamic calculations using smooth initial density profiles with imposed deformations.

## Key numbers

- χ₆₂₄ extracted from data is the first experimental measure of this coefficient, coupling v₆ to v₂ and v₄; it is larger than χ₆₂ and χ₆₃ for all centralities
- The off-diagonal element Σ₁₃ in the correlation matrix is large due to strong correlation between v₂ and v₄; when rewritten in terms of U₄, off-diagonal elements become much smaller than diagonal elements
- χ₆₂ and χ₆₃ extracted with the full correlation matrix show flatter centrality dependence than previous ALICE results, improving agreement with hydrodynamics
- The nonlinear contribution from χ₆₂₄ is the largest of the three nonlinear contributions to v₆ for all centralities
- Hydrodynamic calculations with η/s = 1/4π show viscosity results in modest reduction of χ₆₂ and χ₆₂₄, with larger dependence for χ₆₃

## Context

This work extends the nonlinear hydrodynamic response formalism of Yan (2015) and Zhao (2017) by relaxing the assumption that nonlinear terms are pairwise uncorrelated, which was used in previous analyses by ALICE (2017) and Qian (2016). The framework enables more accurate determination of nonlinear structure for higher harmonics beyond v₆, specifically v₇ and v₈, where correlations between terms are expected to be more significant. The extracted response coefficients provide robust probes of hydrodynamic behavior because they are largely independent of initial-state uncertainties that plague traditional flow observables.
