# Fully strange tetra- and penta-quarks in a chiral quark model

> Motivated by the recently reported resonant structure $X(2300)$, a strong candidate for a fully strange tetraquark with positive parity, we perform a systematic study of fully strange tetra- and penta-quark systems within a chiral quark model. Low-lying $S$-wave configurations of the $ss\bar s\bar s$ and $ssss\bar s$ systems are investigated using the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) combined with the Complex Scaling Method (CSM), which allows for a unified treatment of bound, resonant, and scattering states. For tetraquarks, all possible configurations: meson-meson, diquark-antidiquark, and K-type structures, with complete color bases, are incorporated, while baryon-meson and diquark-diquark-antiquark configurations are considered for pentaquarks. Several weakly bound states and narrow resonances are identified in both sectors. In particular, a compact fully strange tetraquark with $J^P=1^+$ is found near $2.3\,\text{GeV}$, providing a natural interpretation of the $X(2300)$ resonance. Additional exotic states with dominant hidden-color and K-type components are predicted in the mass ranges $1.6-3.1$ GeV for tetraquarks and $2.6-3.2$ GeV for pentaquarks. The internal structure of these states is analyzed through their sizes, magnetic moments, and wave-function compositions, highlighting the essential role of channel coupling and exotic color configurations. Finally, promising two-body strong decay channels are proposed to facilitate future experimental searches.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a compact fully strange tetraquark with quantum numbers \(J^P=1^+\) exists near 2.3 GeV, providing a natural interpretation of the experimentally reported \(X(2300)\) resonance; however, this identification depends on the specific chiral quark model employed and the inclusion of all possible \(S\)-wave configurations, with the state emerging as a narrow resonance rather than a bound state.

## Method

The authors solved the complex-scaled Schrödinger equation for \(ss\bar s\bar s\) tetraquark and \(ssss\bar s\) pentaquark systems using the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) combined with the Complex Scaling Method (CSM) within a chiral constituent quark model. For tetraquarks, they incorporated meson-meson, diquark-antidiquark, and four K-type configurations with complete color bases, while for pentaquarks they considered baryon-meson and diquark-diquark-antiquark configurations. The Hamiltonian included color confinement, one-gluon exchange, and Goldstone-boson exchange interactions, with all model parameters taken from Vijande (2004).

## Key numbers

- A compact fully strange tetraquark with \(J^P=1^+\) is found near **2.3 GeV**, providing an interpretation of the \(X(2300)\) resonance.
- Additional exotic tetraquark states are predicted in the mass range **1.6–3.1 GeV**.
- Additional exotic pentaquark states are predicted in the mass range **2.6–3.2 GeV**.
- The theoretical mass of the \(\phi(1S)\) meson is **1011 MeV** (experimental: 1020 MeV); the \(\phi(2S)\) is **1720 MeV** (experimental: 1680 MeV).
- The theoretical mass of the \(\eta'(1S)\) meson is **828 MeV** (experimental: 958 MeV); the \(\eta'(2S)\) is **1639 MeV** (experimental: 1760 MeV).
- The theoretical mass of the \(\Omega(1S)\) baryon is **1634 MeV** (experimental: 1672 MeV); the \(\Omega(2S)\) is **2226 MeV** (no experimental value reported).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of fully strange exotic states such as the \(\phi(2170)\), which has been interpreted as a fully strange tetraquark with \(J^{PC}=1^{--}\) in QCD sum rules by Wang (2006) and Chen (2008) and in constituent quark models by Deng (2010) and Drenska (2008). The study also builds on QCD sum-rule calculations by Wan (2025) favoring a \(J^P=1^+\) interpretation for \(X(2300)\), and on effective model analyses by Cao (2025) and Liu (2026). The present work enables future experimental searches by proposing specific two-body strong decay channels for the predicted states, and it highlights the essential role of hidden-color and K-type configurations that were often neglected in prior studies.
