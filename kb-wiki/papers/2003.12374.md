# Three-body vs. dineutron approach to two-neutron radiative capture in $^{6}$He

> The low-energy behavior of the strength function for the $1^-$ soft dipole excitation in $^{6}$He is studied theoretically. Use of very large basis sizes and well-grounded extrapolation procedures allows to move to energies as small as 1 keV, at which the low-energy asymptotic behavior of the E1 strength function seems to be achieved. It is found that the low-energy behavior of the strength function is well described in the effective three-body "dynamical dineutron model". The astrophysical rate for the $α$+$n$+$n \rightarrow ^6$He+$γ$ is calculated. Comparison with the previous calculations is performed.

## Key claim
The low-energy behavior of the E1 strength function for the 1⁻ soft dipole excitation in ⁶He is well described by a dynamical dineutron model, but only when the n‑n final‑state interaction (virtual state in the spin‑singlet s‑wave channel) is included; the convergence of the full three‑body calculation becomes extremely slow below about 60–80 keV, requiring an exponential extrapolation to reach energies as low as 1 keV.

## Method
The authors solved the inhomogeneous three‑body Schrödinger equation for the E1 transition in ⁶He using a hyperspherical harmonic expansion with basis sizes up to K_max = 101. Because convergence degrades at low energies, they applied an exponential extrapolation procedure to obtain the strength function down to 1 keV. They also constructed a simplified “dynamical dineutron model” in the T‑Jacobi system, factorizing the three‑body Green’s function into a product of two‑body Green’s functions for the n‑n and core‑dineutron subsystems, and compared its predictions with the full three‑body results and with earlier calculations.

## Key numbers
- The full three‑body E1 strength function is converged down to E_T ≈ 60–80 keV with K_max = 101; at E_T = 1 keV, 95% convergence would require K_max ≈ 250.
- Switching off the n‑n final‑state interaction reduces the low‑energy E1 strength function by a factor of about 9 (i.e., the full result is ≈ 9 times larger).
- The asymptotic low‑energy behavior of the E1 strength function follows dB_E1/dE_T ∼ E_T³.
- The binding energy of ⁶He relative to the α+n+n threshold is E_b = 0.973 MeV.
- The dynamical dineutron model with V_y = 0 (no interaction in the Y coordinate) agrees with the full three‑body rate to within about 50% over the whole temperature range.
- At T ≈ 0.6 GK, the asymptotic rate expression (based on the E_T³ law) differs from the full three‑body result by only a factor of 2.

## Context
This work extends the authors’ previous studies of two‑proton radiative capture in ¹⁷Ne (Grigorenko, 2005; 2006; 2007; Parfenova, 2018) to the two‑neutron case, revealing a qualitative difference: for 2n capture the low‑energy dynamics is governed by the n‑n virtual state, whereas for 2p capture it is governed by resonances in the core‑p subsystem. The results contradict earlier calculations by Efros (1996), Bartlett (2006), Goerres (1995), and de Diego (2010, 2011, 2014), which are mutually inconsistent and, in the case of de Diego (2010), show an unphysical low‑energy enhancement of the strength function. The dynamical dineutron model provides a semi‑analytical tool for reliable extrapolation of experimental data to the low energies needed for astrophysical r‑process network calculations.
