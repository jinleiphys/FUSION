# Causality violation and the speed of sound of hot and dense quark matter in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model

> The Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model is widely used to study strong-interaction phenomena in vacuum and quark matter. Since the model is nonrenormalizable, one needs to work within a specific regularization scheme to obtain finite results. Here we show that a commonly used cutoff regularization scheme leads to unphysical results, such as superluminal speed of sound and wrong high-temperature behavior of the specific heat and other thermodynamical quantities. Such a troublesome feature of the cutoff regularization invalidates the model for temperature and baryon density values relevant to the phenomenology of heavy-ion collisions and compact stars. We show that the source of the problems stems from cutting off momentum modes in finite integrals depending on thermal distribution functions in the grand canonical potential. The problems go away when taking into account the full momentum range of those integrals. Explicit examples are worked out in the SU(2)-flavor version of the model.

## Key claim

The commonly used cutoff regularization scheme (TRS) in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model, which applies the same momentum cutoff to both divergent vacuum integrals and finite thermal integrals, produces unphysical results including superluminal speed of sound and incorrect high-temperature behavior of thermodynamic quantities; these problems are resolved when finite integrals depending on Fermi-Dirac distributions are integrated over the full momentum range without cutoff.

## Method

The authors computed the grand canonical potential, gap equation, entropy density, and quark number density for the SU(2)-flavor NJL model at finite temperature and chemical potential, comparing two regularization procedures: applying a three-momentum cutoff Lambda to both divergent and finite integrals (TRS), versus applying the cutoff only to divergent vacuum integrals while integrating finite thermal contributions over all momenta. They calculated the speed of sound, specific heat, trace anomaly, and conformal measure for both finite quark baryon chemical potential and finite chiral chemical potential scenarios.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff value used was Lambda = 587.9 MeV, with bare quark mass m0 = 5.6 MeV and coupling G = 2.44/Lambda^2, chosen to reproduce pion decay constant f_pi = 92.4 MeV, pion mass m_pi = 135 MeV, and chiral condensate <u_bar u> = (-240.8 MeV)^(1/3)
- With TRS (cutoff applied to finite integrals), causality violation (c_s^2 > 1) occurs for temperatures T greater than approximately 255 MeV at zero chemical potential
- With TRS, the conformal limit c_s^2 = 1/3 is violated for temperatures T greater than 170 MeV at zero chemical potential
- With TRS at zero temperature, the model can be safely used only for quark chemical potentials up to approximately 500 MeV, corresponding to baryon density rho_B approximately 7 rho_0 (where rho_0 = 0.16 fm^(-3) is nuclear saturation density)
- With TRS, the trace anomaly and conformal measure become negative for temperatures near T = 200 MeV for both mu_q and mu_5 cases
- Results with TRS and full integration start to deviate at temperatures close to T = 100 MeV for both trace anomaly and conformal measure

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Farias (2016) on regularization schemes in the NJL model, specifically the medium separation scheme, and addresses issues raised in works by Costa (2009, 2010) and Marty (2013) regarding the Stefan-Boltzmann limit. The paper demonstrates that the commonly used TRS approach, employed in numerous publications including Wang (2021), Wu (2020), Zhao (2019), and others, invalidates the NJL model for temperature and baryon density values relevant to heavy-ion collision phenomenology and compact star physics. The findings enable proper use of the NJL model for studying the QCD phase diagram, chiral symmetry restoration, and equations of state for neutron star and heavy-ion collision applications.
