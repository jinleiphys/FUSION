# Confronting generalized hidden local symmetry chiral model with the ALEPH data on the decay tau^- to pi^+ pi^- pi^- nu_tau

> Generalized Hidden Local Symmetry (GHLS) model is the chiral model of pseudoscalar, vector, and axial vector mesons and their interactions. It contains also the couplings of strongly interacting particles with electroweak gauge bosons. Here, GHLS model is confronted with the ALEPH data on the decay $τ^-\toπ^-π^-π^+ν_τ$. It is shown that the invariant mass spectrum of final pions in this decay calculated in GHLS framework with the single $a_1(1260)$ resonance disagrees with the experimental data at any reasonable number of free GHLS parameters. Two modifications of GHLS model based on inclusion of two additional heavier axial vector mesons are studied. One of them giving a good description of the ALEPH data, with all the parameters kept free is shown to result in very large $Γ_{a_1^\pm\toπ^\pmγ}$ partial width. The other scheme with the GHLS parameters fixed in a way that the universality is preserved and the observed central value of $Γ_{a_1^\pm\toπ^\pmγ}$ is reached, results in a good description of the three pion spectrum in $τ^-\toπ^+π^-π^-ν_τ$ decay.

## Key claim

The Generalized Hidden Local Symmetry (GHLS) chiral model with only a single \(a_1(1260)\) resonance cannot reproduce the ALEPH data on the \(\tau^-\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^-\nu_\tau\) decay, regardless of how the free parameters are varied; a good description requires the inclusion of two additional heavier axial vector mesons, but only if the GHLS parameters are constrained to preserve universality and match the measured radiative decay width \(\Gamma_{a_1^\pm\to\pi^\pm\gamma}\).

## Method

The authors computed the invariant mass spectrum of the three-pion final state in \(\tau^-\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^-\nu_\tau\) using the GHLS lagrangian, which includes pseudoscalar, vector, and axial vector mesons and their electroweak couplings. They first tested the model with a single \(a_1(1260)\) resonance, then extended it by adding two heavier axial vector mesons (\(a_1^\prime\) and \(a_1^{\prime\prime}\)) with amplitudes respecting the Adler condition. Fits to the ALEPH data were performed under three scenarios: unconstrained parameters, parameters fixed to preserve \(\rho\) universality (\(a=2\)), and parameters further constrained to reproduce the central value of \(\Gamma_{a_1^\pm\to\pi^\pm\gamma}=640\) keV.

## Key numbers

- The single-\(a_1\) fit with free parameters gave \(m_{a_1}\approx 1.54\) GeV, \(a\approx 1.75\), \(r\approx 1.05\), \(\beta\approx 0.84\), and \(\chi^2/N_{\rm d.o.f.}=690/112\), failing to reproduce the spectral shape.
- The unconstrained fit including \(a_1\), \(a_1^\prime\), and \(a_1^{\prime\prime}\) (variant A) yielded \(m_{a_1}=1.332\pm0.015\) GeV, \(a=1.665\pm0.011\), and \(\chi^2/N_{\rm d.o.f}=79/102\), but \(a\neq 2\) violates universality.
- The fit with \(a\equiv 2\) and \(\Gamma_{a_1^\pm\to\pi^\pm\gamma}\) fixed to 640 keV (variant C) gave \(m_{a_1}=1.368\pm0.006\) GeV, \(\chi^2/N_{\rm d.o.f}=45/102\), and a branching fraction \(B_{\tau^-\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^-\nu_\tau}=(8.97\pm0.13)\%\).
- The measured radiative width is \(\Gamma_{a_1^\pm\to\pi^\pm\gamma}=640\pm246\) keV (Zielinska, year not given in text).

## Context

This work extends earlier GHLS studies of four-pion processes (Achasov 2005, 2008a, 2008b) where the model failed to describe \(e^+e^-\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^+\pi^-\) data near 1 GeV without including \(\rho^\prime\) and \(\rho^{\prime\prime}\) resonances. It contradicts the simpler "canonical" parameter choice of Bando (1988) for the \(\tau\) decay channel. The results enable future tests of GHLS with heavier axial vector states, whose existence is supported by quark model predictions (Isgur 1985, 1987) and experimental evidence (Particle Data Group, Amelin, CLEO).
