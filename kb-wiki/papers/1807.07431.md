# Proton Isovector Helicity Distribution on the Lattice at Physical Pion Mass

> We present a state-of-the-art calculation of the isovector quark helicity Bjorken-$x$ distribution in the proton using lattice-QCD ensembles at the physical pion mass. We compute quasi-distributions at proton momenta $P_z \in \{2.2, 2.6, 3.0\}$~GeV on the lattice, and match them systematically to the physical parton distribution using large-momentum effective theory (LaMET). We reach an unprecedented precision through high statistics in simulations, large-momentum proton matrix elements, and control of excited-state contamination. The resulting distribution with combined statistical and systematic errors is in agreement with the latest phenomenological analysis of the spin-dependent experimental data; in particular, $Δ\bar{u}(x)>Δ\bar{d}(x)$.

## Key claim

The authors present a lattice-QCD calculation of the isovector quark helicity distribution at the physical pion mass that agrees with phenomenological global analyses (NNPDFpol1.1 and JAM17) within combined statistical and systematic errors, but the agreement is limited to the moderate-to-large x region; at small x (below 0.1) the calculation is limited by the accuracy of large-zPz data, and at x very close to 1 the calculation is in principle limited by finite lattice spacing effects at large proton momentum.

## Method

The authors compute bare isovector quark helicity quasi-distributions on a lattice with spacing 0.09 fm and box size 5.8 fm using clover valence fermions on 884 gauge configurations with 2+1+1 flavors of HISQ dynamical quarks at pion mass 135 MeV. They use Gaussian momentum smearing to achieve proton momenta of 2.2, 2.6, and 3.0 GeV, then renormalize the matrix elements in the RI/MOM scheme and match to the physical PDF in the MSbar scheme using one-loop large-momentum effective theory (LaMET) matching. Excited-state contamination is controlled through six source-sink separations (0.54 to 1.08 fm) with four different two-state fitting strategies.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing a = 0.09 fm, box size L ≈ 5.8 fm, with pion mass Mπ ≈ 135 MeV
* Proton momenta used: Pz ∈ {2.2, 2.6, 3.0} GeV, corresponding to n ∈ {10, 12, 14} in units of 2π/L
* Six source-sink separations: tsep ∈ {0.54, 0.72, 0.81, 0.90, 0.99, 1.08} fm with measurements {16, 32, 32, 64, 64, 128}k respectively
* Renormalization scales: μR ∈ {2.3, 3.7} GeV with pzR = 2.2 GeV as central value
* Final PDF presented at scale μ = 3 GeV, normalized by gA = 1.275
* Estimated systematic uncertainties: 8% from lattice spacing effects, 5% from finite volume effects

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier exploratory calculation (Lin et al., 2014) at pion mass 310 MeV with proton momentum up to 1.3 GeV, and improves upon the first physical-pion-mass helicity distribution results reported by the same group (Lin et al., 2017). It is consistent with phenomenological fits from NNPDFpol1.1 (Nocera et al., 2014) and JAM17 (Ethier et al., 2017) in the large-x region. The calculation demonstrates the potential of lattice QCD combined with LaMET to provide ab initio determinations of x-dependent parton distributions, and the authors note that future finer lattice spacings and higher proton momenta will enable pushing to smaller x in advance of experiments at the Electron-Ion Collider.
