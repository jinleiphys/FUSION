# $B_c^{\pm}$-$^{12}$C states and detailed study of momentum space method for $Υ$- and $η_b$-nucleus bound states

> We perform a detailed study of the $Υ$-, $η_b$-, and $B_c$-nucleus systems in momentum space to calculate the bound-state energies and the corresponding coordinate-space radial wave functions. The attractive strong potentials for the meson-nucleus systems are calculated from the Lorentz scalar mass modifications of these mesons in nuclear matter in the local density approximation in the nucleus. The downward shift of the meson masses may be regarded as a signature of partial restoration of chiral symmetry in a nuclear medium applied in the present study in an empirical sense, because the origin of the negative mass shift in this study is not directly related to the chiral symmetry mechanism. Furthermore, as an initial and realistic study, the $B_c^{\pm}$-$^{12}$C bound states are studied for the first time, with the effects of self-consistently calculated Coulomb potentials in $^{12}$C (when the $B_c^{\pm}$ mesons are absent).

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the $\Upsilon$, $\eta_b$, and $B_c$ mesons can form bound states with light nuclei ($^4$He and $^{12}$C) through attractive Lorentz scalar potentials derived from in-medium mass modifications, with the caveat that the negative mass shift is treated empirically and is not directly linked to chiral symmetry restoration mechanisms.

## Method

The authors calculate the in-medium mass shifts of $\Upsilon$, $\eta_b$, and $B_c$ mesons from one-loop self-energy diagrams involving intermediate-state hadrons ($BB$, $BB^*$, $B^*D$, and $BD^*$ loops), using a flavor SU(5) symmetric effective Lagrangian with coupling constants fixed by $\Upsilon$ decay data. The nuclear density-dependent mass shifts are converted into coordinate-space potentials via local density approximation using nuclear density distributions from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model for $^{12}$C and a parameterized density for $^4$He. The Klein-Gordon equation is solved in momentum space using a double spherical Bessel transform for partial wave decomposition and an inverse iteration method to find eigenvalues and eigenfunctions.

## Key numbers

* $\Upsilon$-$^4$He 1s bound state energy: -6.25 MeV for cutoff $\Lambda = 4000$ MeV
* $\Upsilon$-$^{12}$C 1s bound state energy: -15.26 MeV for $\Lambda = 4000$ MeV
* $\Upsilon$-$^{12}$C 1p bound state energy: -9.57 MeV for $\Lambda = 4000$ MeV
* $\eta_b$-$^4$He 1s bound state energy: -71.59 MeV for $\Lambda = 4000$ MeV

* $\eta_b$-$^{12}$C 1p bound state energy: -55.13 MeV for $\Lambda = 4000$ MeV
* $\eta_b$-$^{12}$C 1d bound state energy: -48.50 MeV for $\Lambda = 4000$ MeV
* $\eta_b$-$^{12}$C 2s bound state energy: -36.30 MeV for $\Lambda = 4000$ MeV

* $B_c$-$^4$He 1s bound state energy (no Coulomb): -76.67 MeV for $\Lambda = 4000$ MeV
* $B_c$-$^4$He 1p bound state energy (no Coulomb): -42.91 MeV for $\Lambda = 4000$ MeV

* $B_c$-$^{12}$C 1p bound state energy (no Coulomb): -52.88 MeV for $\Lambda = 4000$ MeV
* Coulomb potential alone for $B_c^\pm$-$^{12}$C 1s: approximately $\pm 1.4$ MeV for attractive and repulsive cases respectively

## Context

This work extends previous studies of bottomonium-nucleus bound states by Zeminiani, Cobos-Martinez, and Tsushima (2020-2022) and charmonium-nucleus systems proposed by Brodsky (1989) and studied by Krein (2010) and Tsushima (2011). The paper provides the first calculation of $B_c^\pm$-$^{12}$C bound states including self-consistently calculated Coulomb potentials within the QMC model framework of Guichon (1987, 1995) and Saito (1996). The momentum-space method developed here enables future extension to complex Hamiltonians including meson widths and imaginary potentials, which is difficult to implement in coordinate-space calculations.
