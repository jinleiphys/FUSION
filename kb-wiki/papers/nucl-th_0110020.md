# Geometric relation between centrality and the impact parameter in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We show, under general assumptions which are well satisfied in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, that the geometric relation of centrality c to the impact parameter b, namely c ~ pi b^2/sigma_inel, holds to a very high accuracy for all but most peripheral collisions. More precisely, if c(N) is the centrality of events with the multiplicity higer than N, then b is the value of the impact parameter for which the average multiplicity <n(b)> is equal to N. The corrections to this geometric formula are of the order (Delta n(b)/<n(b)>)^2, where Delta n(b) is the width of the multiplicity distribution at a given value of b, hence are very small. In other words, the centrality effectively measures the impact parameter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the geometric relation \(c \simeq \pi b^2 / \sigma_{\rm inel}\) between centrality \(c\) and impact parameter \(b\) holds to very high accuracy for all but the most peripheral collisions, provided the width of the multiplicity distribution at fixed impact parameter is much smaller than its mean. The corrections to this formula are of order \((\Delta n(b) / \bar{n}(b))^2\) and are therefore very small in practice.

## Method

The authors derive the relation analytically by starting from the definition of centrality as a cumulant of the multiplicity distribution, then taking the limit of an infinitely narrow multiplicity distribution at fixed impact parameter. They quantify the corrections from finite width using a Gaussian parametrization of the conditional multiplicity distribution and a Sommerfeld-type expansion. Numerical tests are performed for two models: a wounded-nucleon model and the optical-limit Glauber model for binary collisions, both applied to Au+Au reactions at RHIC energies.

## Key numbers

- For the wounded-nucleon model with \(\sigma = 40\) mb, the total inelastic nucleus-nucleus cross section is \(\sigma_{\rm inel} = 7.05\) b.
- The correction to the geometric relation at intermediate impact parameters is of order \(10^{-3}\).
- For the Gaussian-parametrized Glauber model with \(\beta = 4.6\) fm, the correction term is approximately \(-0.2/N\), which is very small at large \(N\).
- The geometric relation holds to high accuracy for impact parameters \(b < 14\) fm (wounded-nucleon model) and \(b < 13.5\) fm (Glauber model for binary collisions).

## Context

This work extends the geometric interpretation of centrality used in the Glauber model, as previously discussed by Vogt (1999), by providing a rigorous derivation and quantifying the corrections from finite multiplicity fluctuations. The result simplifies the comparison between theoretical calculations that use impact parameter as input and experimental data categorized by centrality, eliminating the need for explicit Glauber-model mapping in most cases. The paper enables direct use of the geometric relation for analyses of data from SPS and RHIC experiments, including those from PHOBOS, BRAHMS, PHENIX, and STAR collaborations.
