# Center-of-mass problem in truncated configuration interaction and coupled-cluster calculations

> The problem of center-of-mass (CM) contaminations in ab initio nuclear structure calculations using configuration interaction (CI) and coupled-cluster (CC) approaches is analyzed. A rigorous and quantitative scheme for diagnosing the CM contamination of intrinsic observables is proposed and applied to ground-state calculations for He-4 and O-16. The CI and CC calculations for O-16 based on model spaces defined via a truncation of the single-particle basis lead to sizable CM contaminations, while the importance-truncated no-core shell model based on the $N_{\max}\hbarΩ$ space is virtually free of CM contaminations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that truncated configuration interaction (CI) and coupled-cluster (CC) calculations for O-16 using single-particle basis truncations (e_max) produce sizable center-of-mass (CM) contaminations, while the importance-truncated no-core shell model (IT-NCSM) based on the N_max hbar-Omega space remains virtually free of such contaminations, with the caveat that the diagnostics rely on a finite beta parameter and the results depend on model-space size and oscillator frequency.

## Method

The authors propose a diagnostic scheme using a modified Hamiltonian H_beta = H_int + beta * H_cm, where H_cm is a harmonic-oscillator CM Hamiltonian, and monitor the change in the intrinsic energy Delta<H_int>_beta and the expectation value <H_cm>_beta as functions of beta. They apply this to ground-state calculations of He-4 and O-16 using importance-truncated CI with up to 4p4h excitations (IT-CI(4p4h)), CCSD, and CR-CC(2,3) methods, all based on the V_UCOM interaction derived from the Argonne V18 potential, using harmonic-oscillator single-particle bases truncated by e_max.

## Key numbers

- For He-4 with IT-CI(4p4h) at hbar-Omega=30 MeV and e_max=6: Delta<H_int>_beta=10 = 0.190 MeV, <H_cm>_beta=10 = 0.017 MeV
- For He-4 with CCSD at hbar-Omega=30 MeV and e_max=6: Delta<H_int>_beta=10 = 0.493 MeV, <H_cm>_beta=10 = 0.024 MeV
- For He-4 with CR-CC(2,3) at hbar-Omega=30 MeV and e_max=6: Delta<H_int>_beta=10 = 0.556 MeV, <H_cm>_beta=10 = 0.039 MeV
- For O-16 with IT-CI(4p4h) at hbar-Omega=38 MeV and e_max=8: Delta<H_int>_beta=10 = 2.41 MeV, <H_cm>_beta=10 = 0.30 MeV
- For O-16 with CCSD at hbar-Omega=38 MeV and e_max=6: Delta<H_int>_beta=10 = 3.31 MeV, <H_cm>_beta=10 = 0.28 MeV
- For O-16 with CR-CC(2,3) at hbar-Omega=38 MeV and e_max=6: Delta<H_int>_beta=10 = 1.70 MeV, <H_cm>_beta=10 = 0.47 MeV
- For O-16 with IT-NCSM(seq) at N_max=8 and hbar-Omega=30 MeV: Delta<H_int>_beta is below 150 keV for all beta values

## Context

This work extends the analysis of CM contamination from the valence-shell model, where the Gloeckner-Lawson prescription (1974) was used, to ab initio CI and CC methods for light nuclei. It provides a quantitative diagnostic that goes beyond the common practice of using <H_cm>_beta=0 alone, which the authors show can be misleading (e.g., He-4 CCSD at hbar-Omega=38 MeV and e_max=6 gives <H_cm>_beta=0 = 8.965 MeV but Delta<H_int>_beta=10 = 0.924 MeV). The results enable systematic assessment of CM contamination in future ab initio calculations for medium-mass nuclei and highlight the need for either using translationally invariant model spaces (like NCSM) or developing methods to remove CM contamination in truncated CI and CC approaches.
