# Bound State Solutions of the Relativistic Finite-difference Equation for the Ring-shaped Quesne Oscillator Potential

> We solve exactly the relativistic finite-difference equation for the quantum three-dimensional ring-shaped Quesne oscillator potential. Our investigation is based on a finite-difference version of relativistic quantum mechanics. So-called relativistic configurational r-space is a key concept here. We show that the radial wavefunctions and angular wavefunctions are expressed through the continuous dual Hahn polynomials and Jacobi polynomials, respectively. A discrete energy spectrum has been found. The radial wave functions and energy spectrum have the correct nonrelativistic limit. We also build a dynamical symmetry group SU (1, 1) for the radial part of the equation of motion, which allows us to find the energy spectrum purely algebraically.

# Bound State Solutions of the Relativistic Finite-difference Equation for the Ring-shaped Quesne Oscillator Potential

## Key claim

The relativistic finite-difference equation for the ring-shaped Quesne oscillator potential is exactly solvable, with the energy spectrum given by a discrete formula that has the correct nonrelativistic limit; however, the solution is subject to the condition that the "orbital" quantum number k satisfies an upper bound Λ_k ≤ (m_0 c^2 / 2ħω)^2, which imposes a relativistic constraint not present in the nonrelativistic case.

## Method

The authors solve the finite-difference relativistic wave equation (a generalization of the free Schrödinger equation in relativistic configurational r-space) for the ring-shaped Quesne oscillator potential V(r) = [½ m_0 ω² (r + iλ̄)² + α/(r² sin²ϑ)] e^{iλ̄∂_r}. The equation is separated into angular and radial parts; the angular equation is solved via Gegenbauer polynomials (Jacobi-type), and the radial finite-difference equation is solved by comparison with the equation for continuous dual Hahn polynomials. A dynamical symmetry group SU(1,1) is constructed for the radial equation, allowing algebraic determination of the energy spectrum.

## Key numbers

- Energy spectrum: E_{nk} = ħω(2n + μ_k + ν_k), where n = 0,1,2,... is the radial quantum number, and μ_k, ν_k are real parameters defined by μ_k = ½ + ½ √[1 + (2/ω₀²)(1 - √(1 - 4ω₀²Λ_k))] and ν_k = ½ + ½ √[1 + (2/ω₀²)(1 + √(1 - 4ω₀²Λ_k))], with ω₀ = ħω/(m_0 c²) and Λ_k = L(L+1) where L = k + |M|.
- Upper bound on orbital quantum number: Λ_k ≤ (m_0 c² / 2ħω)².
- Angular wave function expressed via Gegenbauer polynomials: F_{km}(cosϑ) = c_{km} (sinϑ)^{|M|} C_k^{(|M|+1/2)}(cosϑ), with normalization constant c_{km} = 2^{-|M|} √[(k+|M|+½) k! Γ(k+2|M|+1) / Γ(k+|M|+1)].
- Nonrelativistic limit yields: lim_{c→∞} λ̄^{μ_k} = L+1, and the radial wave function reduces to generalized Laguerre polynomials L_n^{L+1/2}(λ₀² r²) with λ₀ = √(m_0 ω/ħ).
- Numerical calculations used ħ = c = m₀ = Λ = 1 and ω₀ = 0.1.

## Context

This work extends the exactly solvable nonrelativistic ring-shaped Quesne oscillator potential (Quesne, 2002) to the finite-difference relativistic quantum mechanics framework developed by Nagiyev and collaborators. It generalizes the earlier relativistic treatment of the ring-shaped Coulomb potential (Nagiyev et al., 2015) to the oscillator case. The model provides an analytically tractable platform for studying relativistic bound states with non-central interactions, and the SU(1,1) dynamical symmetry group construction enables algebraic spectrum determination. The results enable further investigation of hidden symmetries and anisotropic quantum systems within relativistic finite-difference quantum mechanics.
