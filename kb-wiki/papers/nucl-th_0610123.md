# Ground state correlations and structure of odd spherical nuclei

> It is well known that the Pauli principle plays a substantial role at low energies because the phonon operators are not ideal boson operators. Calculating the exact commutators between the quasiparticle and phonon operators one can take into account the Pauli principle corrections. Besides the ground state correlations due to the quasiparticle interaction in the ground state influence the single particle fragmentation as well. In this paper, we generalize the basic QPM equations to account for both mentioned effects. As an illustration of our approach, calculations on the structure of the low-lying states in $^{131}$Ba have been performed.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the basic quasiparticle-phonon nuclear model (QPM) equations for odd spherical nuclei to simultaneously account for both Pauli principle corrections and ground state correlations (backward-going quasiparticle-phonon vertices), showing that the inclusion of backward amplitudes shifts the first solution to higher energies and reduces the gap between low-lying states, but the authors note that results for the transitional nucleus \(^{131}\)Ba may lack some accuracy because the wave function does not contain configurations to account for anharmonic effects.

## Method

The authors start from the QPM Hamiltonian with Woods-Saxon mean field, pairing, and separable multipole residual forces, then apply the equation of motion method to an excitation operator for odd nuclei that includes both forward-going (quasiparticle and quasiparticle\(\otimes\)phonon) and backward-going (time-conjugate) terms. Exact commutation relations between quasiparticle and phonon operators are used to derive a system of linear equations for the amplitudes, with the new quantities \(\mathcal{C}_J\) and \(\mathcal{T}_J\) encoding Pauli principle corrections and the vertices \(W\) representing ground state correlations. The system is solved in a diagonal approximation for \(\mathcal{C}_J\) and \(\mathcal{T}_J\), reducing it to a generalized eigenvalue problem, and applied to calculate the low-lying states of \(^{131}\)Ba.

## Key numbers

- The parameters of the Woods-Saxon potential for \(^{131}\)Ba are given: for neutrons (\(N=74\)), \(r_0 = 1.280\) fm, \(V_0 = 43.40\) MeV, \(\kappa = 0.413\) fm\(^{-2}\), \(\alpha = 1.613\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(G_{N,Z} = 0.124\) MeV; for protons (\(Z=53\)), \(r_0 = 1.240\) fm, \(V_0 = 59.72\) MeV, \(\kappa = 0.350\) fm\(^{-2}\), \(\alpha = 1.587\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(G_{N,Z} = 0.130\) MeV.
- For the \((d,p)\)-reaction spectroscopic factors: the experimentally measured values are 0.53 for the \(1/2^+\) state and 1.03 for the \(3/2^+\) state; the calculations give 0.46 and 1.36, respectively.
- The calculation is restricted to six neutron states: \(1/2^+\), \(3/2^+\), \(9/2^-\), \(11/2^-\), \(5/2^+\), \(7/2^+\).
- Phonons with multipolarities \(\lambda = 2, 3, 4, 5\) are included.

## Context

This work extends earlier QPM formulations for odd spherical nuclei by Khuong (1981) and Alikov (1981), which accounted for Pauli principle corrections but neglected ground state correlations, and by Sluys (1983), which included ground state correlations but without the full Pauli principle treatment. The new generalized equations enable more accurate descriptions of low-lying states in odd-mass nuclei far from magic numbers, with the authors noting that calculations for other Ba isotopes are in progress and that a self-consistent mean field with more realistic effective forces is desirable for further improvement.
