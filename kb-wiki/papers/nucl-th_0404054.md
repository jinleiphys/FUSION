# Cavitation and bubble collapse in hot asymmetric nuclear matter

> The dynamics of embryonic bubbles in overheated, viscous and non-Markovian nuclear matter is studied. It is shown that the memory and the Fermi surface distortions significantly affect the hinderance of bubble collapse and determine a characteristic oscillations of the bubble radius. These oscillations occur due to the additional elastic force induced by the memory integral.

# Cavitation and bubble collapse in hot asymmetric nuclear matter

## Key claim

Memory effects and Fermi surface distortions in hot nuclear matter significantly hinder bubble collapse and produce characteristic oscillations of the bubble radius, but only when the relaxation time is large enough such that ω_F τ ≳ 1; in the Markovian (zero-memory) limit these oscillations disappear and the collapse follows the classical Rayleigh-Plesset behavior.

## Method

The author derives a non-Markovian equation of motion for the bubble radius R(t) from Euler-like equations that include a pressure tensor arising from quadrupole distortion of the Fermi surface. The memory integral I(R;t) is obtained by coupling the kinetic equation for the phase-space distribution function to the displacement field of a spherical bubble, with the relaxation time τ due to interparticle collisions. The equation is solved numerically for the collapse phase starting from the critical radius R* at the top of the thermodynamic potential barrier, using parameters for infinite asymmetric nuclear matter with a neutron-rich vapor.

## Key numbers

- Critical radius R* = 8.2 fm for overheating temperature ΔT = 0.5 MeV, liquid temperature T₀ = 4 MeV, and asymmetry parameter X_liq = 0.1
- Initial collapse velocity Ṙ(0) = −2.9 × 10⁻³ c, derived from equipartition energy E_kin,0 = (T₀ + ΔT)/2 = 2.25 MeV
- Minimum bubble radius reached during collapse: R_min = 0.28 R* (≈ 2.3 fm) in the presence of noncondensable neutron vapor
- Collapse time in the non-viscous limit (τ → 0): t_clps,0 = 4 × 10⁻²² s
- Rayleigh collapsing time for comparison: t_clps,R = 6.1 × 10⁻²² s, using undercompression pressure ΔP ≈ 0.25 MeV/fm³
- Fermi energy ε_F = 37 MeV, surface tension at zero temperature σ(0) = 1.1 MeV·fm⁻², bulk density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Relaxation time parameter τ₀ = 850 fm/c in τ = τ₀/T²
- Critical temperature for infinite nuclear matter: T_crit = 14.6 MeV
- Parameter q ≈ 4.1 MeV·fm relating critical radius to overheating: R* = q/ΔT for symmetric matter

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of embryonic bubble formation in hot nuclear matter by Schulz, Voskresensky and Bondorf (1983), Blin et al. (1986, 1988), Bondorf, Donangelo and Sneppen (1988), and others, which focused on static or equilibrium properties. The author applies the non-Markovian Fermi-liquid formalism developed in Kolomietz, Radionov and Shlomo (2001) to the dynamical problem of bubble collapse, showing that memory effects produce oscillatory behavior absent in classical Newtonian liquids. The results enable future investigation of bubble dynamics in finite nuclei including Coulomb forces and surface effects, and suggest that characteristic gamma-quanta emission from oscillating bubbles could provide a measurable signature of the liquid-gas phase transition temperature in nuclear matter.
