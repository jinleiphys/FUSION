# Relativistic Hydrodynamic Attractors with Broken Symmetries: Non-Conformal and Non-Homogeneous

> Standard textbooks will state that hydrodynamics requires near-equilibrium to be applicable. Recently, however, out-of-equilibrium attractor solutions for hydrodynamics have been found in kinetic theory and holography in systems with a high degree of symmetry, suggesting the possibility of a genuine out-of-equilibrium formulation of hydrodynamics. This work demonstrates that attractor solutions also occur in non-conformal kinetic theory and spatially non-homogeneous systems, potentially having important implications for the interpretation of experimental data in heavy-ion and proton-proton collisions and relativistic fluid dynamics as a whole.

# Relativistic Hydrodynamic Attractors with Broken Symmetries: Non-Conformal and Non-Homogeneous

## Key claim
This work demonstrates that hydrodynamic attractor solutions exist not only in highly symmetric systems (conformal and spatially homogeneous), but also in non-conformal kinetic theory and spatially non-homogeneous systems; however, in the non-conformal case the attractor becomes an extended object (an envelope of curves) rather than a single curve, and in the non-homogeneous case the attractor is only partially known because numerical evolution slows down dramatically near a critical point.

## Method
The author studies two systems: (1) non-conformal kinetic theory using the Boltzmann BGK equation for a massive gas (particle mass m=1) undergoing one-dimensional boost-invariant Bjorken flow, solved numerically via an integral equation for energy-density evolution; and (2) conformal resummed BRSSS (rBRSSS) theory in 2+1 dimensions, solved numerically using the VH2+1 solver with optical Glauber model initial conditions for Au+Au collisions at impact parameter b=8 fm. For both systems, the author tracks the evolution of scaling variables A1 = D ln s / (nabla_lambda u^lambda) and A2 = (Tr T^{munu} + epsilon - 3P) / (zeta T) as functions of inverse gradient strength Gamma.

## Key numbers
- For the non-conformal kinetic theory attractor calculation with relaxation time tau_R = 0.4/T and initial proper time tau_0 = 0.1 (in mass units m=1), the slow-roll condition yields initial anisotropy parameter xi_0 approximately equal to 17.8.
- For the conformal non-homogeneous rBRSSS system with eta/s = 0.16 and tau_pi T = 4 eta/s, trajectories cluster near an apparent attractor at Gamma approximately equal to 16 and A1 approximately equal to -0.96, where evolution slows down dramatically.
- The conformal (zero-width) attractor from prior work is recovered in the limit Lambda_0 approaches infinity.

## Context
This work extends prior findings of hydrodynamic attractor solutions in conformal, spatially homogeneous systems by Heller and Spalinski (2015), Romatschke (2017), Spalinski (2017), and Strickland (2017). It addresses the open question of whether attractors survive when symmetries are broken, which is critical for applications to heavy-ion collisions. The existence of an attractor for the non-equilibrium equation of state (A2) eliminates the textbook requirement that hydrodynamics requires equilibrium, and the author notes that non-equilibrium equations of state are now routinely used in precision fits of hydrodynamic models to experimental data by Ryu and collaborators (2015) and Habich and collaborators (2015). The results suggest attractor solutions likely exist for QCD in relativistic ion collisions, potentially explaining hydrodynamic-like signatures observed in proton-proton collisions by Schukraft (2017) and possibly electron-positron collisions by Nagle and collaborators (2017).
