# Collective flow in event-by-event partonic transport plus hydrodynamics hybrid approach

> Complete evolution of the strongly interacting matter formed in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions is studied within a coupled Boltzmann and relativistic viscous hydrodynamics approach. For the initial nonequilibrium evolution phase, we employ the AMPT model that explicitly includes event-by-event fluctuations in the number and positions of the participating nucleons as well as of the produced partons with subsequent parton transport. The ensuing near-equilibrium evolution of quark-gluon and hadronic matter is modeled within the (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics. We probe the role of parton dynamics in generating and maintaining the spatial anisotropy in the preequilibrium phase. Substantial eccentricities epsilon_n are found to be generated in the event-by-event fluctuations in parton production from initial nucleon-nucleon collisions. For ultracentral heavy-ion collisions, the model is able to explain qualitatively the unexpected hierarchy of the harmonic flow coefficients v_n(p_T)(n=2-6) observed at LHC. We find that the results for v_n(p_T) are rather insensitive to the variation (within a range) of the time of switchover from AMPT parton transport to hydrodynamic evolution. The usual Grad and the recently proposed Chapman-Enskog-like (nonequilibrium) single-particle distribution functions are found to give very similar results for v_n(n=2-4). The model describes well both the RHIC and LHC data for v_n(p_T) at various centralities, with a constant shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of 0.08 and 0.12, respectively. The event-by-event distributions of v_{2,3} are in good agreement with the LHC data for midcentral collisions. The linear response relation v_n = k_n epsilon_n is found to be true for n=2,3, except at large values of epsilon_n, where a larger value of k_n is required, suggesting a small admixture of positive nonlinear response even for n=2,3.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a hybrid model combining AMPT parton transport with (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics can describe both RHIC and LHC data for harmonic flow coefficients v_n(p_T) (n=2-6) at various centralities, but the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio must be different at the two energies: 0.08 at RHIC and 0.12 at LHC. The model explains the unexpected hierarchy of v_n(p_T) in ultracentral collisions only qualitatively, not quantitatively.

## Method

The authors couple the AMPT (string-melting version) model for the preequilibrium partonic phase with the (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics code VISH2+1 for the near-equilibrium evolution of quark-gluon and hadronic matter. At a switchover time τ_sw = 0.4 fm/c, pointlike partons from AMPT are smeared with a 2D Gaussian distribution (σ = 0.8 fm) to initialize the hydrodynamic energy density, with a normalization factor N adjusted to reproduce central p_T spectra (1.38 at RHIC, 1.24 at LHC). The hydrodynamic evolution uses the s95p-PCE equation of state and continues until a decoupling temperature of 120 MeV, with hadronic spectra computed via the Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription using Grad's 14-moment approximation for the viscous correction δf.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 0.08 for RHIC Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 0.12 for LHC Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV
- Normalization factor N = 1.38 at RHIC and N = 1.24 at LHC
- Switchover time τ_sw = 0.4 fm/c (with insensitivity in window 0.3 < τ_sw ≤ 0.8 fm/c)
- Smearing parameter σ = 0.8 fm
- Decoupling temperature T_dec = 120 MeV
- Parton-parton elastic cross section of 1.5 mb in ZPC cascade
- Linear response coefficients: k_2 = 0.327 and k_3 = 0.236 for 20-25% centrality Pb+Pb at LHC
- Nucleus-nucleus total inelastic cross section σ = 784 fm^2 used for centrality conversion

## Context

This work extends previous studies that used smooth initial conditions from Glauber or color-glass-condensate models (Hirano 2005, Qiu 2011) by incorporating event-by-event fluctuations at both nucleonic and partonic levels along with preequilibrium parton dynamics. It builds on the AMPT model (Lin 2004) and the VISH2+1 viscous hydrodynamics code (Song 2007). The results enable more precise extraction of η/s by reducing uncertainties from initial-state modeling, and the finding of different η/s values at RHIC (0.08) versus LHC (0.12) suggests a temperature-dependent shear viscosity, consistent with earlier studies by Gale (2012) that used different fluctuating initial conditions.
