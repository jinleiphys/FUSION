# Electroweak hadron structure within a point-form approach

> We present a relativistic point-form approach for the calculation of electroweak form factors of few-body bound states. As an example, the transition form factors for the semileptonic weak decay $B\to D^*e\bar ν_e$ are discussed and it is sketched how they can be extracted unambiguously from the invariant transition amplitude that describes the process. It is shown how these form factors go over into one universal function, the Isgur-Wise function in the heavy-quark limit, $m_Q\to \infty$, and comparison with the available experimental data is made.

## Key claim

The paper presents a relativistic point-form approach for calculating electroweak form factors of few-body bound states, claiming that transition form factors for the semileptonic weak decay \(B\to D^*e\bar \nu_e\) can be extracted unambiguously from the invariant transition amplitude, but this extraction is only frame-independent and free of spurious covariants for decay processes (not for scattering), and the approach respects heavy-quark symmetry only in the limit \(m_Q\to\infty\).

## Method

The authors employ a point-form relativistic quantum mechanics based on the Bakamjian-Thomas construction, where the 4-momentum operator factorizes into an interacting mass operator and a free velocity operator. They formulate electron-meson scattering on a Hilbert space with \(e q\bar q\) and \(e q\bar q \gamma\) sectors, using velocity states as a basis, and extract the hadron current from the on-shell matrix elements of an optical potential that describes one-photon exchange. For the weak decay \(B\to D^*e\bar \nu_e\), they compute the transition current microscopically and decompose it covariantly to extract the form factors \(V\), \(A_0\), \(A_1\), and \(A_2\) using six non-vanishing spin matrix elements of the current.

## Key numbers

* Harmonic-oscillator wave function with oscillator parameter \(a = 0.55\) GeV used for numerical calculations of the \(B\to D^*\) transition form factors.
* Physical quark masses used: \(m_u = 0.25\) GeV, \(m_b = 4.8\) GeV, \(m_c = 1.6\) GeV.
* Heavy-quark symmetry is broken by about \(20\%\) when comparing the Isgur-Wise function with transition form factors obtained for physical heavy-quark masses.
* The central value \(|V_{cb}| = 0.0409\) (from the Particle Data Group 2012) is assumed for comparison with experimental data from Belle, CLEO, and BABAR.

## Context

This work extends the point-form approach previously applied to electromagnetic properties of spin-0 and spin-1 two-body bound states of equal-mass particles (Biernat 2009, 2010, 2011) to electroweak structure of mesons with unequal-mass constituents (GomezRocha 2012). The numerical results for the Isgur-Wise function agree with those from a front-form quark model (Cheng 1996), but discrepancies appear for finite heavy-quark masses, likely due to different roles of Z-graph (non-valence) contributions in the two approaches. The formalism enables future investigation of whether Z-graph contributions can restore equivalence between point-form and front-form calculations for decay processes, as was already found for electromagnetic form factors in the space-like momentum-transfer region.
