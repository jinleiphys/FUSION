# MARS Code Developments

> Recent developments in the physical model of 1 MeV to 100 TeV hadron and lepton interactions with nuclei and atoms are described. These include a new nuclear cross section library, a model for soft pion production, the cascade-exciton model, the dual parton model, deuteron-nucleus and neutrino-nucleus interaction models, detailed description of muon, pion, and antiproton absorption and a unified treatment of muon and charged hadron electromagnetic interactions with matter. New algorithms are implemented into the MARS13(98)} Monte Carlo code and benchmarked against experimental data. The code capabilities to simulate cascades and generate a variety of results in complex media have been also enhanced.

## Key claim

The MARS13(98) Monte Carlo code has been substantially extended with new physical models and algorithms covering hadron and lepton interactions from 1 MeV to 100 TeV, but the paper presents these developments as a summary of ongoing work rather than a final validation, noting that some models (e.g., elastic scattering at low energies) still require improvement.

## Method

New compilations and parameterizations of hadron-nucleon and hadron-nucleus cross sections were implemented, covering total, elastic, inelastic, and production cross sections from 1 MeV to 100 TeV using data from the Cascade-Exciton Model (CEM95), the Glauber multiple scattering theory, and the Dual Parton Model (DPMJET-2.3/2.4). A new phenomenological model for pion production in proton-nucleus interactions was developed for 5 GeV to 100 TeV, and specialized models were added for deuteron-nucleus collisions, neutrino-nucleus interactions, and the absorption of stopped pions, muons, and antiprotons. The electromagnetic treatment of muons and charged hadrons was unified with improved algorithms for bremsstrahlung, ionization, and delta-electron production.

## Key numbers

- Energy range covered: 1 MeV to 100 TeV for hadron and lepton interactions with nuclei and atoms
- For stopped pi- in hydrogen: 60% probability for charge exchange (pi- p -> pi0 n) producing two photons of 68.9 MeV each and a neutron with 0.4 MeV kinetic energy; 40% probability for radiative capture (pi- p -> n gamma) producing a photon of 129.4 MeV and a neutron with 8.9 MeV kinetic energy
- Stopped antiproton annihilation at rest produces pions with multiplicity of 4.3, normalized to twice the nucleon mass
- Muon bremsstrahlung total cross section and dE/dx calculated by new simplified algorithm agree within 1% with the exact formalism
- Benchmarking results shown for dose equivalent attenuation in concrete for 100 and 400 MeV neutron beams over six decades
- Calculations shown for neutrino energies from 100 MeV to 10 TeV for dose in tissue-equivalent phantom

## Context

This work extends the MARS code system developed over 24 years at Fermilab, which had already demonstrated reliable performance in applications at Fermilab, CERN, KEK, and other centers (Mokhov 1995, 1997). The new developments were driven by challenging applications at Fermilab and include implementation of the Cascade-Exciton Model (CEM95) which the 1994 International Code Comparison for Intermediate Energy Nuclear Data showed to have one of the best predictive powers for double differential cross sections. The code now enables simulation of cascades in complex media with improved treatment of mixtures and compounds, and includes an automatic interface to the MCNP code for low-energy neutron and photon transport.
