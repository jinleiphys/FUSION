# One-body Properties of Nuclear Matter with Off-shell Propagation

> Symmetric nuclear matter is studied in the self-consistent, in-medium $T$-matrix approach. One-body spectral function, optical potential, and scattering width are calculated. Properties of quasi-particle excitations at the Fermi surface are discussed. Dispersive self-energies are dominated by contributions from the $^1S_0$, $3S_1-^3D_1$, and $^3P_1$ partial waves.

## Key claim

Symmetric nuclear matter at normal density is at the limit of the region of \(^3S_1-^3D_1\) pairing instability; owing to the dressing of propagators in the gap equation the superfluid gap is very small, if not vanishing, depending on the details of the interaction in the deuteron channel.

## Method

The self-consistent, in-medium \(T\)-matrix approximation was solved iteratively with fully dressed propagators, using a separable parameterization of the Paris potential including \(J=0,1,2\) and \(^3D_3-^3G_3\) partial waves. The retarded \(T\)-matrix, self-energy, and spectral function were discretized on a uniform energy grid and evaluated using Fourier transforms for the energy integrals, with the quasi-particle peak separated from the smooth background at zero temperature. The chemical potential was adjusted at each iteration to maintain the density condition \(\rho = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).

## Key numbers

- Binding energy: \(-15.1\ \text{MeV}\) at normal density (\(\rho = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\)).
- Fermi energy: \(-21.4\ \text{MeV}\).
- Effective mass at the Fermi momentum: \(m^\star \simeq 0.85\,m\).
- \(k\)-mass at the Fermi momentum: \(m_k/m \simeq 0.62\).
- \(\omega\)-mass at the Fermi momentum: \(m_\omega/m \simeq 1.37\).
- Quasi-particle renormalization factor at the Fermi momentum: \(Z_{p_F} \simeq 0.73\).
- Depth of the single-particle potential: \(H_0 = -70\ \text{MeV}\) with nonlocality range \(\beta = 0.58\ \text{fm}\).
- Maximum on-shell scattering width: \(26\ \text{MeV}\).
- At \(T = 4\ \text{MeV}\), the real part of the single-particle potential shifts by less than \(1\ \text{MeV}\) from its zero-temperature value.

## Context

This work extends earlier Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations (Kohler, 1992; Baldo et al., 1992) by including full off-shell propagation and self-consistent dressing of propagators, which earlier \(T\)-matrix calculations (Vonderfecht, 1996; Alm, 1996; Dickhoff, 1999; Dewulf, 2000) had not fully achieved with realistic interactions and multiple partial waves. The results enable reliable predictions for the high-energy tails of spectral functions relevant to electron scattering experiments, and provide a baseline for studying pairing in nuclear matter with realistic interactions.
