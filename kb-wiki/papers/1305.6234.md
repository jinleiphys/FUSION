# Single-particle potential from resummed ladder diagrams

> A recent work on the resummation of fermionic in-medium ladder diagrams to all orders is extended by calculating the complex single-particle potential $U(p,k_f)+ i\,W(p,k_f)$ for momenta $p<k_f$ as well as $p>k_f$. The on-shell single-particle potential is constructed by means of a complex-valued in-medium loop that includes corrections from a test-particle of momentum $\vec p$ added to the filled Fermi sea. The single-particle potential $U(k_f,k_f)$ at the Fermi surface as obtained from the resummation of the combined particle and hole ladder diagrams is shown to satisfy the Hugenholtz-Van-Hove theorem. The perturbative contributions at various orders $a^n$ in the scattering length are deduced and checked against the known analytical results at order $a^1$ and $a^2$. The limit $a\to\infty$ is studied as a special case and a strong momentum dependence of the real (and imaginary) single-particle potential is found. This indicates an instability against a phase transition to a state with an empty shell inside the Fermi sphere such that the density gets reduced by about 5%. For comparison, the same analysis is performed for the resummed particle-particle ladder diagrams alone. In this truncation an instability for hole-excitations near the Fermi surface is found at strong coupling. For the set of particle-hole ring diagrams the single-particle potential is calculated as well. Furthermore, the resummation of in-medium ladder diagrams to all orders is studied for a two-dimensional Fermi gas with a short-range two-body contact-interaction.

# Single-particle potential from resummed ladder diagrams

## Key claim

The resummation of combined particle-particle and hole-hole ladder diagrams to all orders yields a complex single-particle potential whose on-shell value at the Fermi surface satisfies the Hugenholtz-Van-Hove theorem, but only when subtle delta-function terms arising from differentiating the discontinuity of the arctangent function at infinity are included; in the unitary limit (infinite scattering length), the strong momentum dependence of the real potential indicates an instability toward a Sarma phase with an empty shell inside the Fermi sphere, reducing the density by about 5%.

## Method

The complex single-particle potential U(p,k_f) + i W(p,k_f) is constructed by adding a test-particle of momentum p to the filled Fermi sea via a substitution in the occupation number, then computing the first-order change in the resummed interaction energy density derived from the all-orders summation of ladder diagrams. The calculation uses a complex-valued in-medium loop organized by medium-insertions, with separate treatment for momenta inside (p < k_f) and outside (p > k_f) the Fermi sphere, and the resulting double-integral representations are expanded perturbatively to check against known analytical results at orders a^1 and a^2.

## Key numbers

- The "normal" Bertsch parameter from the combined ladder resummation is ξ_n = 0.507, compared to ξ_n^(pp) ≈ 0.237 from particle-particle ladder diagrams only.
- In the unitary limit (a → ∞), the density reduction from the predicted Sarma phase instability is about 5%.
- The first-order Hartree-Fock potential is Φ_1(x) = 4/(3π) ≈ 0.42441, with zero imaginary part.
- Second-order boundary values: Φ_2(0) = 4/π^2 ≈ 0.40528, Φ_2(1) = 4(11 − 2 ln 2)/(15π^2) ≈ 0.25975, Ω_2(0) = 0.15915.
- Third-order boundary values: Φ_3(0) = 0.44788, Φ_3(1) = 0.20153, Ω_3(0) = 0.13406, with analytical value Ω_3(0) = 16[2√2 ln(1+√2) − 3 ln 2]/(5π^2).
- Fourth-order boundary values: Φ_4(0) = 0.42857, Φ_4(1) = 0.15744, Ω_4(0) = 0.07256.
- Fifth-order boundary values: Φ_5(0) = 0.38638, Φ_5(1) = 0.13314, Ω_5(0) = 0.02273.
- The slope of Φ_2 at x=1 is Φ_2'(1) = 16(1 − 7 ln 2)/(15π^2).
- The function R(s,κ) has zeros along a line starting at s_0=0, κ_0=0.83356 and ending at s_1=0.55243, κ_1=0.83356.
- The parameter ζ from the corrected integrand (including the delta-function term) is ζ = 0.1163.

## Context

This work extends the all-orders resummation of in-medium ladder diagrams previously developed by Kaiser (2013) for the interaction energy per particle to the complex single-particle potential, providing more detailed many-body information than the energy alone. The perturbative results at second order are checked against the classical work of Galitskii, and the low-density expansion up to order a^4 from Hammer and Steele is reproduced with improved numerical accuracy. The findings enable further studies of instabilities in strongly interacting Fermi systems, particularly the Sarma phase, and provide a benchmark for comparing with experimental extrapolations of the Bertsch parameter in ultracold ⁶Li gases at Feshbach resonances, where the normal-phase value ξ_n ≈ 0.45 from Zwierlein (2013) lies between the present result (0.507) and the pairing-dominated ground-state value (0.376).
