# A Note on the Eigenvalue Problem in the su(1,1)-Algebra

> Normalization constant in the eigenstate appearing in the eigenvalue problem of the su(1,1)-algebra is discussed. This normalization constant is expressed in terms of the Gauss' hypergeometric series which is not absolutely convergent. It is proved that this series is obtained as a certain limit of an absolutely convergent series, which was conjectured in the previous paper.

## Context

This work extends the eigenvalue problem for the su(1,1)-algebra previously investigated by the same authors in their paper (A) (Nishiyama et al., 2004) and in a reference cited as Ref. 2. The paper resolves a convergence issue with the normalization constant that was left as a conjecture in (A). The results enable the construction of normalized eigenstates for the full family of operators TМӮвӮҖ(Оё) across all Оё values, including the problematic region where the eigenvalue becomes pure imaginary, which is relevant for applications of the su(1,1) algebra in nuclear physics and other quantum systems.
