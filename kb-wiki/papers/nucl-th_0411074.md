# A Note on the Eigenvalue Problem in the su(1,1)-Algebra

> Normalization constant in the eigenstate appearing in the eigenvalue problem of the su(1,1)-algebra is discussed. This normalization constant is expressed in terms of the Gauss' hypergeometric series which is not absolutely convergent. It is proved that this series is obtained as a certain limit of an absolutely convergent series, which was conjectured in the previous paper.

# A Note on the Eigenvalue Problem in the su(1,1)-Algebra

## Key claim

The normalization constant for eigenstates of the su(1,1)-algebra, expressed as a Gauss hypergeometric series that is not absolutely convergent in the region π/4 ≤ |θ| ≤ π/2, is shown to be obtainable as the limit of an absolutely convergent series as a regularization parameter ε approaches zero, confirming a conjecture from the authors' previous paper (Nishiyama et al., 2004, referred to as (A)).

## Method

The authors construct a family of operators T̂₀(θ) and T̂±(θ) that interpolate between the standard su(1,1) generators at θ=0 and the operators at θ=±π/2. They solve the eigenvalue problem for T̂₀(θ) by finding eigenstates in an exponential form exp(w(θ)T̂₊)|t⟩, where w(θ) is determined from the commutation relations. For the region where the hypergeometric series for the normalization constant diverges (|w(θ)²|=1), they introduce a regularized set of operators T̃±,₀(θ) depending on a parameter ε, compute the absolutely convergent normalization constant for ε>0, and take the limit ε→0⁺.

## Key numbers

- The eigenvalue t(θ) = s(θ)√(cos²θ − sin²θ) · t, where s(θ) is a step function: s(θ)=+1 for −π/4 ≤ θ ≤ π/2 and s(θ)=−1 for −π/2 ≤ θ < −π/4
- The parameter w(θ)² = (cos²θ − s(θ)√(cos²θ−sin²θ)) / (cos²θ + s(θ)√(cos²θ−sin²θ))
- In the region 0 ≤ |θ| < π/4, w(θ)² is real with 0 ≤ w(θ)² < 1, and the normalization constant is N_{t(θ)} = (1−w(θ)²)^{−2t} for t>0
- At θ=±π/2, the regularized parameter w̃(±π/2)=±i√(1−ε) gives |w̃(±π/2)²| = 1−ε < 1 for ε>0, and N_{t̃(±π/2)} = (2−ε)^{−2t}
- The normalization constant for the full eigenstates is N_{t(θ),t₀(θ)} = (cos²θ−sin²θ)² · (t₀−t)!Γ(t₀+t)/Γ(2t)

## Context

This work extends the eigenvalue problem for the su(1,1)-algebra previously investigated by the same authors in their paper (A) (Nishiyama et al., 2004) and in a reference cited as Ref. 2. The paper resolves a convergence issue with the normalization constant that was left as a conjecture in (A). The results enable the construction of normalized eigenstates for the full family of operators T̂₀(θ) across all θ values, including the problematic region where the eigenvalue becomes pure imaginary, which is relevant for applications of the su(1,1) algebra in nuclear physics and other quantum systems.
