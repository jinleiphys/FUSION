# The effect of self interacting isoscalar-vector meson on finite nuclei and infinite nuclear matter

> A detailed study is made for the nucleon-nucleon interaction based on relativistic mean field theory in which the potential is explicitly expressed in terms of mass and the coupling constant of the meson fields. A unified treatment for self-coupling of isoscalar-scalar $σ-$, isoscalar-vector $ω$-mesons and their coupling constant are given with a complete analytic form. The present investigation is focused on the effect of self-interacting higher order $σ$ and $ω$ field on nuclear properties. An attempt is made to explain the collapsing stage of nucleon by higher order $ω$-field. Both infinite nuclear matter and the finite nuclear properties are included in the present study to observe the behaviour or sensitivity of this self interacting terms.

## Key claim

The self-interacting isoscalar-vector (ω) meson term, with coupling constant c₃, produces a strongly attractive potential at extremely short distances (~0.2 fm) that may explain the collapsing stage of nucleons during quark-gluon plasma formation, but the effect on finite nuclei properties like binding energy and compressibility is negligible for negative c₃ values despite producing a stiff equation of state in infinite nuclear matter.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic mean field (RMF) Lagrangian with linear σ, ω, and ρ meson couplings, then add non-linear self-coupling terms for the σ field (g₂, g₃) and the ω field (c₃). They derive analytic expressions for the nucleon-nucleon potential in coordinate space including these non-linear terms, using the NL3 parameter set with varying c₃ values from -2 to +2. The mean field equations are solved self-consistently to compute binding energies, giant monopole excitation energies, and compressibility for ⁴⁰Ca and ²⁰⁸Pb, as well as energy per particle and pressure density for symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- The linear RMF NN-potential depth is ~150 MeV, while the non-linear case gives ~50 MeV depth, making the non-linear result more reasonable for fitting NN-data.
- The non-linear ω-meson self-coupling term becomes active at ~0.2 fm, while the linear ω-term gives an infinite repulsive barrier at ~0.4 fm.
- The coupling constant c₃ is varied from -2 to +2, with the NL3 base value listed as 0.0 ± 0.6.
- The σ-meson masses used are: m_σ = 508.194 MeV, m_ω = 782.5 MeV, m_ρ = 763.0 MeV, with coupling constants g_σ = 8.31, g_ω = 13.18, g_ρ = 6.37, and non-linear σ parameters g₂ = -10.4307, g₃ = -28.8851.
- The incompressibility and excitation energy of finite nuclei do not change for negative c₃ values, but increase substantially after c₃ ~ 1.0.

## Context

This work extends the RMF approach of Boguta and Bodmer (1977) and Bodmer (1991) by adding the ω-meson self-coupling term (c₃) on top of the NL3 parameter set from Furnstahl et al. (1987, 1989). The ω⁴ term already exists in the FSU-Gold parameter set of Piekarewicz (2001, 2005), but here the authors systematically study its effect on both finite nuclei and infinite nuclear matter. The results connect to neutron star equation of state constraints from Demorest et al. (2010), which suggest NL3 needs a slightly softer EOS, while the G2 (E-RMF) model of Singh et al. (2013) demands a stiffer EOS. The short-range attractive component of the ω self-coupling provides a mechanism for nucleon collapse in heavy-ion collisions toward quark-gluon plasma formation.
