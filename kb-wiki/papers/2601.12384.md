# Signatures of QCD conductivities in heavy-ion collisions

> Dissipative processes are pivotal for understanding the hydrodynamic evolution of hot and dense QCD matter created in relativistic nuclear collisions. The interplay of multiple conserved charges -- net baryon, strangeness, and electric charge -- is of particular interest. We simulate the longitudinal hydrodynamic evolution with the three diffusion currents in a hydrodynamic model with a lattice-QCD-based equation of state, NEOS-4D, and estimate rapidity distributions including diffusive corrections to the phase-space distribution in the presence of multiple charges, which ensure charge conservation at particlization. We determine the response of particle yields at midrapidity to changes in the diagonal and off-diagonal conductivities. Inversely, we find that most components of the conductivity matrix can be constrained experimentally using identified particle multiplicities at different collision energies.

## Key claim

The authors show that most components of the conductivity matrix for baryon, electric charge, and strangeness diffusion can be constrained experimentally using identified particle multiplicities at different collision energies, but the actual sensitivity depends on the specific diagonal or off-diagonal component and the collision energy, with some components (e.g., \(\kappa_{BQ}\)) having only marginal effects for the chosen parameter values.

## Method

The authors simulate (1+1)-dimensional longitudinal hydrodynamic evolution of net baryon, electric charge, and strangeness in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 19.6\), 62.4, and 200 GeV, using a lattice-QCD-based equation of state (NEOS-4D) and including three diffusion currents with both diagonal and off-diagonal conductivities. They compute the off-equilibrium phase-space distribution corrections (\(\delta f\)) via Grad's moment method to ensure charge conservation at particlization via the Cooper-Frye prescription, and then construct response surfaces for hadronic yields (\(\pi^+\), \(\pi^-\), \(K^+\), \(K^-\), \(p\), \(\bar{p}\)) using linear response matrices, quadratic response tensors, and Gaussian process regression.

## Key numbers

- Diagonal conductivities set to \(\kappa_{BB}/T^2 = 0.05\), \(\kappa_{QQ}/T^2 = 0.02\), \(\kappa_{SS}/T^2 = 0.1\); off-diagonal set to \(\kappa_{BQ}/T^2 = 0.002\), \(\kappa_{BS}/T^2 = -0.02\), \(\kappa_{QS}/T^2 = 0.01\) for the demonstrative simulations.
- The \(\delta f\) correction leads to an approximately 1-2% correction to the integrated baryon number at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 19.6\) GeV and a 2-3% correction at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 200\) GeV.
- The difference in mean \(p_T\) due to diffusion currents is less than 0.1% for net baryons and less than 1% for net charges.
- The initial proper time \(\tau_0\) is 3.0 fm/\(c\) at 19.6 GeV, 1.5 fm/\(c\) at 62.4 GeV, and 1.0 fm/\(c\) at 200 GeV; the transverse area \(S\) is 200 fm\(^2\) for 19.6 and 62.4 GeV, and 250 fm\(^2\) for 200 GeV.
- The particlization hypersurface is defined by energy density \(e = 0.4\) GeV/fm\(^3\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of baryon diffusion in relativistic hydrodynamics (Monnai 2012, Denicol 2018, Li 2018) and multi-charge diffusion (Fotakis 2019, Pala 2025) by incorporating a full three-charge diffusion framework with off-diagonal conductivities and \(\delta f\) corrections, using a four-dimensional lattice-QCD-based equation of state (NEOS-4D, Monnai 2024). It enables future experimental constraints on the conductivity matrix from RHIC beam energy scan data, building on the response-surface methodology of Sangaline (2015) and the Gaussian process regression approach of Gong (2024).
