# A multichannel model for clusters of an $α$ and select $N=Z$ nuclei

> A multi-channel algebraic scattering (MCAS) method has been used to solve coupled sets of Lippmann-Schwinger equations for $α$+nucleus systems to find spectra of the compound systems. Low energy spectra for ${}^{12}$C, ${}^{16}$O, and ${}^{20}$Ne are found with the systems considered as the coupling of an $α$ particle with low-excitation states of the core nuclei, ${}^8$Be, ${}^{12}$C, and ${}^{16}$O, respectively. Collective models have been used to define the matrices of interacting potentials. Quadrupole (and octupole when relevant) deformation is allowed and taken to second order. The calculations also require a small monopole interaction to provide an extra energy gap commensurate with an effect of strong pairing forces. The results compare reasonably well with known spectra given the simple collective model prescriptions taken for the coupled-channel interactions. Improvement of those interaction specifics in the approach will give spectra and wave functions suitable for use in analyses of cross sections for $α$ scattering and capture by light-mass nuclei; reactions of great importance in nuclear astrophysics.

## Key claim

The multi-channel algebraic scattering (MCAS) method, using simple collective model prescriptions for coupled-channel interactions, can reproduce low-energy spectra of 12C, 16O, and 20Ne when treated as alpha-particle clusters with core nuclei 8Be, 12C, and 16O respectively, but the results are only "reasonably well" matched to known spectra and require improvement of interaction specifics before the approach can be reliably used for astrophysical cross-section analyses.

## Method

The authors solved coupled sets of Lippmann-Schwinger equations using the MCAS method for alpha+nucleus systems, treating the compound nuclei as an alpha particle coupled to low-excitation collective states of the core nuclei (8Be, 12C, and 16O). Collective rotational and vibrational models were used to define the matrices of interacting potentials, with quadrupole (and octupole where relevant) deformation included to second order, plus a small monopole interaction to account for pairing effects. Pauli blocking was handled using orthogonalizing pseudo-potentials (OPP) with variable strengths to achieve full blocking, hindrance, or allowance of specific orbits.

## Key numbers

- For the alpha+alpha test case (8Be): ground state (0+) resonance centroid at 0.095 MeV with width 6 eV (experimental: 0.092 MeV, 5.96 eV); first excited (2+) resonance centroid at 3.13 MeV with width 1.06 MeV (experimental: 3.03 MeV, 1.51 MeV)
- For 12C from alpha+8Be: ground state predicted at 5.92 MeV below threshold, corrected to 7.37 MeV below threshold with monopole term; Hoyle state (0+2) found at 0.24 MeV above threshold (experimental: 0.28 MeV) but with MCAS width 750 keV versus experimental width approximately 0.008 keV
- For 12C negative-parity states: 3- state at 1.81 MeV with width 9.2 keV (experimental: 2.30 MeV, 35 +/- 5 keV); 1- state at 2.04 MeV with width 1085 keV (experimental: 3.50 MeV, 315 +/- 25 keV)
- For 16O from alpha+12C: sub-threshold states were in "very good agreement" with known values after tuning, with the monopole term value of -3.5 MeV used to set the ground-to-first-excited-state splitting

## Context

This work extends the MCAS method, previously applied to nucleon-nucleus systems (Amos 2003) and spin-1/2 projectile systems (Canton 2006a), to treat alpha-particle projectiles on light N=Z nuclei. It builds on semi-algebraic cluster models (Yepez 2012, Fraser 2012) and generator coordinate method studies (Kobayashi and Enyo 2014) of alpha+12C clustering in 16O. The approach aims to enable future analyses of alpha scattering and capture cross sections relevant to nuclear astrophysics, particularly the triple-alpha process and CNO cycle reactions, but the current results show significant discrepancies in resonance widths and ordering of negative-parity states that require improved interaction prescriptions.
