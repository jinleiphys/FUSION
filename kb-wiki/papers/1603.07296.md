# $N=Z$ nuclei: A laboratory for neutron-proton collective mode

> The neutron-neutron and proton-proton pairing correlations have long been recognised to be the dominant many-body correlation beyond the nuclear mean field since the introduction of pairing mechanism by Bohr, Mottelson and Pines nearly 60 year ago. Nevertheless, few conclusion has been reached concerning the existence of analogous neutron-proton (np) pair correlated state. One can see a renaissance in np correlation studies in relation to the significant progress in radioactive ion beam facilities and detection techniques. The np pairs can couple isospin T = 1 (isovector) or 0 (isoscalar). In the isovector channel, the angular momentum zero component is expected to be the most importance one. On the other hand, as one may infer from the general properties of the np two-body interaction, in the isoscalar channel, both the np pairs with minimum (J=1) and maximum (J=2j) spin values can be important. In this contribution, we will discuss the possible evidence for np pair coupling from different perspective and analyze its influence on interesting phenomena including the Wigner effect and mass correlations in odd-odd nuclei. In particular, we will explain the spin-aligned pair coupling scheme and quartet coupling involving pairs with maximum (J=2j) spin values.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while neutron-proton (np) pairing correlations remain inconclusively established, there is evidence for specific np coupling schemes, particularly the spin-aligned pair coupling with maximum angular momentum (J=2j) and quartet coupling, that can explain phenomena such as the Wigner effect and mass correlations in odd-odd N=Z nuclei, though the authors caution that separating residual np pairing from mean-field effects remains a challenge.

## Method

The authors analyze empirical binding energy systematics using multiple odd-even staggering (OES) formulas (three-point, four-point, and five-point formulas) to extract pairing gaps and isolate the residual np interaction from mean-field contributions. They also examine the average np interaction extracted from double binding energy differences across even-even, odd-A, and odd-odd nuclei, and discuss the seniority coupling scheme and its extension to spin-aligned np pair coupling within the shell-model context for single-j configurations.

## Key numbers

- The empirical pairing gap extracted from the three-point formula for odd nuclei, Δ^(3)_C(N), shows a weak A dependence and can be reasonably fitted as a constant value; for heavy nuclei the residual contribution from the mean field is around -60 keV.
- The mean δ_np values (residual np interaction) extracted from experimental data are around 300 keV for all known nuclei, reducing to below 200 keV for available superheavy nuclei.
- The average np interaction V_pn in odd-odd N=Z nuclei is three times as large as the average values in N≠Z nuclei, while those in even-even N=Z nuclei and adjacent odd-A nuclei are roughly twice as large.
- The neutron pairing gap trend is approximated as Δ ≈ 12/A^(1/2) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing investigation of np pairing correlations that began with the introduction of nuclear pairing by Bohr, Mottelson, and Pines (1958) and the BCS theory. It builds on studies of the Wigner effect by Wigner (1941) and van Isacker, Warner, and Brenner (1995), and on the spin-aligned np pair coupling scheme proposed for 92Pd by Cederwall et al. (2011) and Qi et al. (2011). The paper enables future work on distinguishing correlated np pairs from uncorrelated ones in N=Z nuclei, and on constraining nuclear structure models using the local fluctuations of V_pn that large-scale Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations currently fail to explain (Stoitsov et al., 2007).
