# The Quark Structure of the Nucleon and Moat Regimes in Nuclear Matter

> We employ a model of nuclear structure that takes into account the quark substructure of the baryons to understand the behavior of static two-point correlation functions of meson fields in dense nuclear matter. We show that these correlation functions display ``moat'' regimes, where the inverse static correlation function is nonmonotonic in momentum space. This can have interesting consequences for the nature of nuclear matter, and it strengthens the possibility of a liquid-crystal phase for high densities.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including the quark substructure of nucleons via the Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model washes out the high-density inhomogeneous instabilities found in standard Relativistic Mean-Field (RMF) models, but the "moat regime" (nonmonotonic static correlation functions) remains robust across a wide range of densities and model parameterizations.

## Method

The authors perform a stability analysis of homogeneous symmetric nuclear matter by calculating the static dielectric function (SDF) within the Relativistic Random Phase Approximation (RRPA) at one-loop order, using the QMC model that incorporates the scalar polarizability of nucleons from their quark substructure. They compute the dressed meson propagators for scalar (sigma) and vector (omega) meson exchanges, diagonalize the propagator matrix, and identify instabilities where the SDF crosses zero and moat regimes where the SDF has a non-zero global minimum. The analysis is repeated for two parameter sets with sigma meson masses of 600 MeV and 700 MeV, and also for pure neutron matter.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: 0.16 fm^-3, binding energy: -15.9 MeV
- Scalar polarizability: d_sigma = 0.15 fm, corresponding to a bag radius of 0.8 fm
- Sigma meson masses used: 600 MeV (QMC600) and 700 MeV (QMC700); omega meson mass fixed at 783 MeV
- Coupling constants: QMC600 uses g_sigma = 9.02, g_omega = 8.42; QMC700 uses g_sigma = 10.52, g_omega = 8.41
- Moat regime wave number is roughly the same across models up to a density of 0.41 fm^-3
- Transverse instabilities in QMC model appear only at "unphysically high densities" beyond model applicability

## Context

This work extends the stability analysis of nuclear matter from standard RMF models (Lim 1989) to the QMC model that includes quark substructure, addressing a long-standing issue noted in the review by Serot (1991) that the composite structure of baryons must be considered. The results connect to studies of moat regimes in dense quark matter (Pisarski 2021, Fu 2019, Cao 2025) and to the possibility of liquid-crystal phases in nuclear matter (Migdal 1990). The finding that moat regimes are robust while instabilities are model-dependent is consistent with NJL model studies of quark matter (Buballa 2020, Pannullo 2022, Pannullo 2024). The work enables further investigation of liquid-crystal phases in dense nuclear matter and their consequences for compact stars, as recently explored by Kochankovski (2025).
