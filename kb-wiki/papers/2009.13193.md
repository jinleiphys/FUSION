# The origin of the spin-orbit and pseudospin-orbit splittings' evolution in neutron drop

> We found the same staggering pattern in the evolution of the spin-orbit (SO) and pseudospin-orbit (PSO) splittings in neutron drops obtained by the energy-density functional (EDF) theory using SAMi-T and SLy5 parameterizations, with and without the tensor terms respectively. This staggering evolution is similar to the results recently obtained by the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) theory with Bonn A potential, which was claimed to be a tensor effect. In this work, we present an intuitive but essential explanation on the origin of this staggering evolution in the Skyrme EDF theory. We found that for both the SO and PSO partners, the energy splittings mainly originate from the SO potential. The staggering evolution of both the SO and PSO splittings is mainly due to the drastic change of density while neutrons filling different single-particle orbits. The key to determine the specific staggering pattern is the strength of the neutron density gradient term and the SO density term in the SO potential, not necessarily the tensor force.

## Key claim

The staggering pattern in the evolution of spin-orbit (SO) and pseudospin-orbit (PSO) splittings in neutron drops, previously attributed to tensor effects in relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) calculations, can be reproduced and explained within Skyrme energy-density functional (EDF) theory without tensor terms, where the key driver is the drastic change of neutron density as neutrons fill different single-particle orbits.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations for neutron drops (systems of neutrons trapped by an external harmonic oscillator field with ℏω = 10 MeV) using the SLy5 parameterization (without tensor terms) and the SAMi-T parameterization (with tensor terms). They decomposed the single-particle energies into three components: contributions from wave-function derivatives, from the central Hartree-Fock and centrifugal potentials, and from the spin-orbit potential, to isolate the origin of the energy splittings between SO and PSO partner states.

## Key numbers

- The SLy5 parameterization uses W0 = 126.0 MeV fm^5, c = 1, and α = 80.2 MeV fm^5 for the spin-orbit form-factor; SAMi-T uses W0 = 130.0 MeV fm^5, c = 0.78, and α = 73.0 MeV fm^5.
- For the 1p SO splitting at N=20 with SLy5, the total splitting Δε = 5.241 MeV, dominated by the SO-potential contribution Δε3 = 5.233 MeV, while Δε1 = -0.203 MeV and Δε2 = 0.211 MeV nearly cancel.
- For the 1p SO splitting at N=28 with SLy5, the total splitting drops to Δε = 2.142 MeV, with Δε3 = 2.104 MeV.
- For the 1p̃ PSO splitting at N=20 with SLy5, the total splitting Δε = -4.253 MeV, with Δε3 = -4.639 MeV, while Δε1 = 20.976 MeV and Δε2 = -20.590 MeV nearly cancel.
- The SO splittings reach local minima at N=14 and N=28, and local maxima at N=20 and N=40 for both 1p and 1d partners.

## Context

This work extends the findings of Shen et al. (2018) who observed the staggering pattern in RBHF calculations with Bonn A potential and attributed it to tensor effects, leading to the development of the SAMi-T functional by Shen et al. (2019). The authors show that the same pattern appears in the traditional Skyrme EDF without tensor terms (SLy5), contradicting the tensor-force interpretation. This enables a simpler understanding of spin-orbit splitting evolution in neutron-rich systems and suggests that future functional development for neutron drops should focus on the density-gradient and spin-orbit density terms rather than tensor-force parametrizations.
