# Mass dependence of inclusive nuclear $ϕ$ photoproduction

> Based on a prior determination of the $ϕ$ selfenergy in a nuclear medium we perform a theoretical study of inclusive $ϕ$ photoproduction in nuclei, looking at the $A$ dependence of the cross sections for different $ϕ$ momenta. We find sizeable reductions in the nuclear cross sections with respect to the elementary one, using a $ϕ$ selfenergy which implies a width about six times the free one at normal nuclear density. The calculations are done to match the set up for an ongoing experiment at {\it SPring8/Osaka} which should provide valuable information on the renormalization of the $ϕ$ properties in nuclei.

# Mass dependence of inclusive nuclear φ photoproduction

## Key claim

The survival probability of φ mesons produced in nuclear photoproduction is significantly reduced (to ~0.7 for heavy nuclei) due to in-medium φ absorption, and this reduction depends on both the nuclear mass number A and the φ momentum; the effect is measurable in the ongoing SPring8/Osaka experiment, though the largest fraction of φ mesons emerge with momenta around 1500 MeV/c rather than at rest, requiring theoretical extrapolation from zero-momentum models.

## Method

The authors compute the inclusive nuclear φ photoproduction cross section using an eikonal approximation, where the survival probability of the φ meson exiting the nucleus is given by an exponential factor involving the imaginary part of the φ selfenergy integrated along the trajectory. They extend two existing models for the φ selfenergy at rest, the Oset-Ramos (OR) model and the Cabrera-Vicente (CV) model, to finite φ momenta up to 2 GeV, incorporating s-wave and p-wave kaon selfenergies, vertex corrections, and Pauli blocking effects on the final nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The φ width at normal nuclear density ρ₀ is about 22 MeV in the OR model and about 28 MeV in the CV model, compared to the free width of about 4.4 MeV (i.e., roughly six times the free width).
- The mass shift of the φ at ρ₀ is small, about −6 MeV.
- For φ momentum p = 1400 MeV and heavy nuclei, the ratio σ_A/(Aσ) (survival probability) is about 0.65.
- For ⁶⁴Cu, the survival probability ranges from about 0.8 at p = 2000 MeV to about 0.7 at p = 1000 MeV; for A ≈ 240, the survival probability at p = 1000 MeV is about 0.57.
- The Pauli blocking correction reduces the survival probability by about 0.1 for forward φ production at p = 2000 MeV, nearly constant across a wide range of A.
- The coherent contribution to the cross section is below 10% for photon energies below 2000 MeV and nuclei beyond A ∼ 12, and negligible beyond 4 degrees scattering angle.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of the φ selfenergy in nuclear matter by Oset and Ramos (2000) and Cabrera and Vicente (2003), which were performed for φ mesons at rest, to the finite momenta relevant for the ongoing φ photoproduction experiment at SPring8/Osaka (Ishikawa, 2003). The study enables experimental extraction of the in-medium φ width by comparing measured A-dependent survival probabilities with these theoretical predictions, complementing earlier proposals to study φ properties via π⁻ p → φ n reactions (Klingl, 1999) and via invariant mass spectra of K⁺K⁻ pairs (Bhalerao, 1997). The results also address concerns raised by Mosel (2002) that Coulomb interactions in heavy nuclei might obscure medium modifications in the K⁺K⁻ invariant mass distribution.
