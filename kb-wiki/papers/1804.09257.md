# Photons from thermalizing matter in heavy ion collisions

> We investigate the production of direct photons in heavy ion collisions within the modified bottom-up thermalization scenario, which we show to be related to the thermalizing Glasma framework. The dynamics of the parton system up to thermalization/equilibration can be described by two momentum scales, by means of which we express the photon invariant yield excluding the prompt pQCD contribution. We derive an analytic formula, which provides an estimate of photon production from thermalizing matter for a wide range of collision systems and energies. We compare the yield with that measured in the PHENIX Au+Au run04 and the combined run07+run10 data sets at root-s = 200GeV. We also make theory-data comparisons for Pb+Pb at 2760 GeV and d+Au at 200 GeV collision energies. Finally, we make predictions for the direct photon invariant yield from collisions of U+U, Cu+Au, $^3$He+Au at 200 GeV and Pb+Pb at 5020 GeV.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that direct photon production in heavy ion collisions can be described by a modified bottom-up thermalization scenario (mBU-Glasma), which is related to the thermalizing Glasma framework. The key hedge is that the derived analytic formula provides an estimate of photon production from thermalizing matter for a wide range of collision systems and energies, but the normalization constant must be fixed by fitting to experimental data in one centrality bin before applying it to other systems.

## Method

The authors derive an analytic expression for the direct photon invariant yield by expressing the dynamics of the parton system up to thermalization using two momentum scales, an ultraviolet scale Omega_UV and an infrared scale Omega_IR, within the modified bottom-up thermalization scenario. They integrate the photon production rate over space-time evolution assuming boost-invariant longitudinal expansion, obtaining a power-law dependence on transverse momentum p_T with exponent Delta that depends on the anisotropy parameter delta. The yield is then compared to PHENIX Au+Au data at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, ALICE Pb+Pb data at 2760 GeV, and PHENIX d+Au data at 200 GeV, with the normalization fixed either from one centrality bin or through global fitting.

## Key numbers

* The exponent Delta in the photon yield formula ranges from 3 to 9, corresponding to the anisotropy parameter delta ranging from 0 to 10/21.
* The photon invariant yield scales as (Q_s / p_T)^Delta, where Q_s is the saturation momentum scale, applicable in the range 1 GeV/c less than or approximately equal to p_T less than or approximately equal to 6 GeV/c.
* The yield formula includes a factor of N_part^(2/3) from the nuclear overlap area, where N_part is the number of participants.
* The smallest possible value for Omega_UV is taken to be of the order of the QCD transition temperature approximately 170 MeV.
* The saturation momentum Q_s is taken to be approximately 1 GeV at RHIC energies.
* The paper makes predictions for direct photon invariant yield from U+U, Cu+Au, ^3He+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV and Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 5020 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the thermalizing Glasma framework of Blaizot (2011) and Liao (2013) by connecting it to the modified bottom-up thermalization scenario of Mueller (2006). It provides an alternative to earlier photon production calculations based on spectral function approaches (Dusling, 2010; Kim, 2017), Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics transport (Bratkovskaya, 2008; Linnyk, 2015), and hydrodynamic simulations (DPSYJG, 2011; SPDJG, 2015). The results enable predictions for direct photon yields in future heavy ion collision systems at RHIC and LHC energies, including U+U, Cu+Au, ^3He+Au, and Pb+Pb at 5020 GeV.
