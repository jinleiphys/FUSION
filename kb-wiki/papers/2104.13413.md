# Spectral representation of the shear viscosity for local scalar QFTs at finite temperature

> In local scalar quantum field theories (QFTs) at finite temperature correlation functions are known to satisfy certain non-perturbative constraints, which for two-point functions in particular implies the existence of a generalisation of the standard Källén-Lehmann representation. In this work, we use these constraints in order to derive a spectral representation for the shear viscosity arising from the thermal asymptotic states, $η_{0}$. As an example, we calculate $η_{0}$ in $ϕ^{4}$ theory, establishing its leading behaviour in the small and large coupling regimes.

## Key claim

The paper derives a non-perturbative spectral representation for the shear viscosity arising from thermal asymptotic states, $\eta_{0}$, in local scalar quantum field theories at finite temperature, using the thermal spectral representation of the two-point commutator that generalizes the Källén-Lehmann representation. The authors prove that if the KMS condition (thermal equilibrium) holds, then $\eta_{0}$ is finite, and they establish that the model dependence of $\eta_{0}$ factorizes completely into the thermal spectral density $\widetilde{D}_{\beta}(\vec{u},s)$.

## Method

The authors start from the Kubo relation for shear viscosity $\eta$ in terms of the spectral function $\rho_{\pi\pi}(p_0)$ of the traceless energy-momentum tensor correlator. They substitute the general non-perturbative spectral representation of the thermal two-point commutator $\widetilde{C}_\beta(p_0,\vec{p})$, which involves the thermal spectral density $\widetilde{D}_\beta(\vec{u},s)$, into the expression for $\rho_{\pi\pi}(p_0)$ derived from asymptotic-state contributions. After performing angular integrals and applying the Kubo limit $p_0 \to 0$, they obtain a closed-form spectral representation for $\eta_0$ in terms of integrals of $\widetilde{D}_\beta$ multiplied by a fixed integral kernel involving Bose-Einstein distribution moments. As an explicit example, they calculate $\eta_0$ in $\phi^4$ theory by using the known form of the damping factor $\widetilde{D}_{m,\beta}(\vec{u})$ for that theory.

## Key numbers

- For the free scalar theory, the damping factor is $\widetilde{D}_{m,\beta}(\vec{u}) = (2\pi)^3 \delta^3(\vec{u})$, leading to a specific form of $\rho_{\pi\pi}(p_0)$.
- In $\phi^4$ theory, the leading behavior of $\eta_0$ in the small coupling regime is $\eta_0 \propto T^3 / \lambda^2$ (where $\lambda$ is the quartic coupling constant), and in the large coupling regime $\eta_0 \propto T^3 / \lambda^{1/2}$.
- The general representation for $\eta_0$ involves the dimensionless integrals $\mathcal{I}_N(R,a,b)$ defined as $\mathcal{I}_N(R,a,b) = \int_0^b d\hat{q} \, (\hat{q}-a)^N \, n\!\left(\sqrt{\hat{q}^2+R^2}\right)$, where $n$ is the Bose-Einstein distribution and $\hat{q}=|\vec{q}|/T$.
- The support of $\rho_{\pi\pi}^{(1)}(p_0)$ is $2\sqrt{\nu} \leq p_0 < \infty$, of $\rho_{\pi\pi}^{(2)}(p_0)$ is $-\infty < p_0 < \infty$, and of $\rho_{\pi\pi}^{(3)}(p_0)$ is $-\infty < p_0 < -2\sqrt{\nu}$, where $\sqrt{\nu}$ is the mass gap.

## Context

This work extends the axiomatic finite-temperature QFT framework developed by Bros, Buchholz, Ojima, and others (Bros 1992, Bros 1995, Bros 1998, Bros 1996) by applying the thermal spectral representation of the two-point function to transport coefficients. It provides a non-perturbative analytic foundation for shear viscosity calculations that complements perturbative approaches (Jeon 1994, Jeon 1995, Arnold 2000, Arnold 2003) and Euclidean-based methods like lattice QFT (Nakamura 2004, Meyer 2007) and functional renormalization group (Haas 2013, Christiansen 2014). The result enables future model-independent studies of transport in thermal scalar QFTs and establishes rigorous finiteness conditions for $\eta_0$ based solely on the KMS condition.
