# Subtractive renormalization of chiral effective theory NN potentials up to next-to-next-to-leading order

> We have developed a subtractive renormalization method with which we can evaluate nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering phase shifts produced by the NN potential obtained at leading, next-to-leading, and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in chiral effective theory ($χ$ET). In this method the low-energy constants associated with short-distance NN physics are eliminated from the Lippmann-Schwinger equation (LSE) for the NN t-matrix, in favor of physical observables. This allows us to straightforwardly compute scattering phase shifts for ultra-violet cutoffs of at least 10 GeV. We then perform detailed analyses of the maximum cutoff at which the use of a $χ$ET NN potential in the LSE makes sense.

## Key claim

The authors develop a subtractive renormalization method for chiral effective theory nucleon-nucleon potentials that eliminates the need for fine-tuning of low-energy constants, enabling calculations with ultraviolet cutoffs up to at least 10 GeV; however, they conclude that for cutoffs larger than approximately 1 GeV, the two-pion-exchange piece of the chiral potential should not be iterated in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation because it fails to satisfy all criteria for successful renormalization.

## Method

The authors construct the fully off-shell partial-wave t-matrix from the long-range part of the potential and the on-shell value of the t-matrix at zero energy, using a subtractive procedure that eliminates the unknown low-energy constants in favor of physical observables such as scattering lengths and phase shifts at a chosen energy. They apply this method to leading-order, next-to-leading-order, and next-to-next-to-leading-order chiral potentials in both S-waves and P-waves, using both dimensionally-regularized and spectral-function-regularized two-pion-exchange potentials, and consider both energy-dependent and momentum-dependent contact terms.

## Key numbers

* The subtractive renormalization method reproduces conventional fitting results for LO S-wave phase shifts within a relative difference of 2%.
* For the DR NNLO TPE with an energy-dependent contact term, the phase shifts first diverge at cutoff Λ ≈ 1 GeV for the singlet channel and Λ ≈ 1.2 GeV for the triplet channel.
* For the DR NNLO TPE in S-waves, the highest cutoff one can adopt in the LSE is Λ_c ∼ 1 GeV.
* For the SFR NNLO TPE, the critical cutoff Λ_c can be extended to 2 GeV before similar problems appear.
* The intrinsic cutoff for the SFR TPE is set to Λ̃ = 800 MeV.
* Input energies for the subtractive method are E* = 1.4 MeV for the singlet channel and E* = 10 MeV for the triplet channel.

## Context

This work extends the subtractive renormalization approach previously applied to LO potentials (NTvK05) up to NNLO in chiral effective theory, and addresses the long-standing debate about whether chiral potentials can be successfully iterated in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation at high cutoffs. The results contradict the assumption that cutoff independence alone guarantees successful renormalization, showing that renormalization-point independence is also necessary, and they enable systematic assessment of which chiral potentials can be treated non-perturbatively. The findings support the alternative approach of treating parts of the potential in perturbation theory, as advocated by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1996) and later works.
