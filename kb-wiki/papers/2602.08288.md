# Probing $α$ clustering in $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ at CSR energies using the Jet AA Microscopic Transport Model

> We investigate the sensitivity of low-energy nuclear collisions to intrinsic nuclear structure by studying the interplay between initial-state geometry and final-state observables in C+C and C+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.36$~GeV, relevant for experiments at the Cooling Storage Ring (CSR) facility in Lanzhou and forthcoming experiments at the High Intensity heavy-ion Accelerator Facility (HIAF) in Huizhou. Calculations are performed within the Jet AA Microscopic Transport Model (JAM) using Woods--Saxon and triangular $α$-clustered configurations for the $^{12}C$ nucleus. The initial geometry is characterized in terms of transverse size, compactness, eccentricities, and their ensemble-averaged fluctuations. We find that $α$ clustering leads to a more compact participant configuration than the Woods--Saxon case, while transverse-size and eccentricity fluctuations show only weak sensitivity to clustering. At this beam energy, radial observables remain sensitive to geometric compactness, with the ensemble-averaged proton mean transverse momentum $\langle p_T \rangle$ enhanced for $α$-clustered configurations, whereas pions show little sensitivity. The anisotropic response is examined using flow harmonic coefficients. We find an enhancement of the root-mean-square flow magnitudes, $v_n\{2\} = \sqrt{\langle v_n^2\rangle}$, for $α$-clustered configurations at large $N_{part}$, while the ensemble-averaged fluctuation strength of individual harmonics remains small. Symmetric cumulants of the initial-state eccentricities show sensitivity to clustering, whereas the corresponding ensemble-averaged correlations among final-state flow harmonics do not exhibit a comparably strong separation. These results indicate that radial observables and correlation-based flow measurements provide complementary probes of $α$ clustering in low-energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

Alpha clustering in 12C leads to a more compact initial participant configuration than a Woods-Saxon distribution, which enhances the proton mean transverse momentum in C+C and C+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.36 GeV, while pions show little sensitivity; however, the effect is conditional on the absence of strong hydrodynamic response at this beam energy, meaning that initial-state eccentricities and their correlations do not translate into significant modifications of final-state flow coefficients v_n.

## Method

The authors used the Jet AA Microscopic Transport Model (JAM version 2.58) in mean-field mode with the MD2 equation of state (nuclear incompressibility K=380 MeV) to simulate C+C and C+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.36 GeV. Two initial configurations for 12C were compared: a spherical Woods-Saxon distribution and a triangular alpha-clustered configuration with three alpha clusters at the vertices of an equilateral triangle (d_alpha-alpha=3.1 fm, sigma=0.8 fm), with the overall orientation randomized event-by-event. The time evolution was followed up to 100 fm/c with a time step of 1 fm/c, and initial-state geometry was characterized through transverse size, compactness, eccentricities, and their ensemble-averaged fluctuations.

## Key numbers

* The root-mean-square radius of the Woods-Saxon configuration of 12C is r_rms approximately 2.4 fm, while that of the alpha-clustered configuration is r_rms approximately 2.3 fm, a difference of less than 4%.
* The alpha-clustered configuration uses d_alpha-alpha=3.1 fm and sigma=0.8 fm for the Gaussian profile of nucleons within each cluster.
* The nuclear incompressibility used in the MD2 equation of state is K=380 MeV.
* The time evolution was followed up to 100 fm/c with a time step of 1 fm/c.
* In C+Pb collisions with C participants only, SC{epsilon_2,epsilon_3} for the Woods-Saxon configuration remains nearly constant around zero as a function of N_part, while the clustered configuration displays an increasingly negative correlation with increasing N_part.

## Context

This work extends the program of using nuclear collisions to probe intrinsic nuclear structure, following experimental signatures of deformation in U+U collisions by STAR (2024, 2025) and Xe+Xe collisions by ALICE (2018), CMS (2019), and ATLAS (2019). It specifically addresses whether alpha-clustering effects in light nuclei such as 12C survive the hadronic dynamics at lower collision energies, where baryon stopping and mean-field potentials dominate rather than hydrodynamic expansion. The results enable future experiments at the Cooling Storage Ring (CSR) in Lanzhou and the High Intensity heavy-ion Accelerator Facility (HIAF) in Huizhou to use proton mean transverse momentum and proton-pion pT differences as complementary probes to flow-based measurements for isolating clustering-induced modifications of initial geometry.
