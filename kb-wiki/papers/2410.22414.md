# Entanglement Entropy is Elastic Cross Section

> We present universal relations between entanglement entropy, which quantifies the quantum correlation between subsystems, and the elastic cross section, which is the primary observable for high energy particle scattering, by employing a careful formulation of wave packets for the incoming particles. For 2-to-2 elastic scattering with no initial entanglement and subdividing the system along particle labels, we show that both the Rényi and Tsallis entropies in the final states are directly proportional to the elastic cross section in unit of the transverse size for the initial wave packets, which is then interpreted as the elastic scattering probability. The relations do not depend on the underlying dynamics of the quantum field theory and are valid to all orders in coupling strengths. Furthermore, computing quantum correlations between momentum and non-kinematic data leads to entanglement entropies expressed as various semi-inclusive elastic cross sections. Our result gives rise to a novel ``area law'' for entanglement entropy in a two-body system.

## Key claim

For 2-to-2 elastic scattering with no initial entanglement, both the Rényi and Tsallis entropies in the final states are directly proportional to the elastic cross section in units of the transverse size of the initial wave packets, which is then interpreted as the elastic scattering probability; the relations do not depend on the underlying dynamics of the quantum field theory and are valid to all orders in coupling strengths, but only at leading order in the plane wave limit where the wave packet momentum spread is small compared to the momentum and the transverse size is large compared to the inverse longitudinal spread.

## Method

The authors formulate the initial scattering state using wave packets with finite characteristic sizes in both momentum and position space, rather than momentum eigenstates which correspond to plane waves with infinite spatial extent. They compute the Tsallis and Rényi entanglement entropies for the final state of 2-to-2 elastic scattering by expanding around the plane wave limit, using the S-matrix formalism and the optical theorem. The system is partitioned along particle labels (type-A and type-B particles) and also along kinematic versus non-kinematic (flavor) quantum numbers, with the final state density matrix projected onto the elastic two-particle subspace.

## Key numbers

- The transverse size of the position-space wave packet is \(L = 1/\delta_\tv\), where \(\delta_\tv\) is the transverse momentum width; the longitudinal momentum width is \(\dtp\), and in relativistic scattering \(\delta_\tv \ll \dtp\).
- The universal integral \(I_0(|\vec{k}|)\) scales as \(\delta_\tv^2 = 1/L^2\), making it the inverse of the characteristic transverse area of the position wave function.
- The plane wave limit conditions are: \(\dtp/|\vec{k}| \to 0\), \(1/(L\,\dtp) \ll 1\), and \(1/(L\,\dtp) \lesssim \dtp/|\vec{k}|\).
- The scattering probabilities satisfy \(\mathcal{P}_{\rm tot} = I_0(|\vec{k}|) [\sigma_{\rm tot} + \mathcal{O}(\dtp/|\vec{k}|)]\), \(\mathcal{P}_{\rm el} = I_0(|\vec{k}|) [\sigma_{\rm el} + \mathcal{O}(\dtp/|\vec{k}|)]\), and \(\mathcal{P}_{\rm inel} = I_0(|\vec{k}|) [\sigma_{\rm inel} + \mathcal{O}(\dtp/|\vec{k}|)]\).

## Context

This work extends prior perturbative calculations of entanglement entropy in particle scatterings under specific theories (Dvali 2014, Seki 2014, Peschanski 2016, Fan 2017, Beane 2018, Low 2021, and others) by providing a universal, non-perturbative relation valid to all orders in coupling. The result gives rise to a novel "area law" for entanglement entropy in a two-body system, analogous to area laws found in black hole physics (Bekenstein 1973, Hawking 1975) and quantum many-body systems (Srednicki 1993, Maldacena 1997, Ryu 2006, Eisert 2008). The direct proportionality of entropy to cross section, first pointed out in Low (2024), enables interpreting cross section simultaneously as an area and as a probability, and opens the possibility of computing entanglement entropies as various semi-inclusive elastic cross sections when considering partitions involving momentum and non-kinematic data.
