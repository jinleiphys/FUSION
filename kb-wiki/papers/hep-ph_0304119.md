# Nuclear Structure Functions at Small x from Inelastic Shadowing and Diffraction

> Nuclear structure functions at small x and small or moderate $Q^2$ are studied using the relation with diffraction on nucleons which arises from Gribov's Reggeon Calculus. A reasonable description of experimental data is obtained with no fitted parameters. A comparison with other models and predictions for future lepton-ion colliders are provided. Consequences for the reduction of multiplicities in nucleus-nucleus collisions at energies of RHIC and LHC are examined.

## Key claim

Nuclear structure functions at small x and small or moderate Q^2 can be described using the relation between diffraction on nucleons and nuclear shadowing that arises from Gribov's Reggeon Calculus, obtaining reasonable agreement with experimental data with no fitted parameters, but the model is explicitly limited to x < 0.01 and Q^2 < 10 GeV^2 and the description of Q^2 evolution is too slow at x ~ 0.01 due to the lack of DGLAP evolution.

## Method

The authors relate the diffractive deep inelastic scattering cross section on nucleons to the first (double-scattering) contribution to nuclear shadowing using Gribov's Reggeon Calculus and AGK cutting rules. The diffractive cross section is taken from a two-component dipole model (Capella et al., 2002) that separates short-distance (r < 0.2 fm) and long-distance (r > 0.2 fm) contributions with quasi-eikonal unitarization. Higher-order rescatterings are included via two unitarization schemes: a Schwimmer summation of fan diagrams and an eikonal unitarization, both constrained to reproduce the double-scattering result at first nontrivial order.

## Key numbers

- The model is valid for x < 0.01 and Q^2 < 10 GeV^2, including photoproduction.
- The diffractive slope parameter used is B = 6 GeV^{-2}, compared to experimental values of 7.2 ± 1.1 (stat.)^{+0.7}_{-0.9} (syst.) GeV^{-2} at <Q^2> = 8 GeV^2 from ZEUS.
- The Pomeron intercept used is Δ = α_P(0) - 1 = 0.2.
- The upper limit for the diffractive variable x_P is set to x_{Pmax} = 0.1.
- The lower integration limit for the diffractive mass is M^2_min = 4m_π^2 = 0.08 GeV^2.
- The separation scale between short and long distance components is r_0 = 0.2 fm.
- For light nuclei (A ≤ 20), the nuclear radius is given by R_A = 0.82 A^{1/3} + 0.58 fm, with R_N = 0.8 fm added in quadrature for form factor calculations.
- For the deuteron, the form factor is F_D(t) = e^{at} with a = 40 GeV^{-2}.
- For the triple Pomeron calculation of multiplicity reduction, parameters C = 0.31 fm^2 and Δ = 0.13 are used, with m_T = 0.4 GeV by default.
- Multiplicity reduction factors at b = 0 reach ~1/2 for AuAu at RHIC and ~1/3 for PbPb at LHC.
- At x = 10^{-5} and Q^2 = 3 GeV^2, different models for the ratio Pb/nucleon differ by up to a factor of 0.6.
- An increase of B from 6 to 7.2 GeV^{-2} produces an increase of shadowing for Pb of at most 10% at x = 10^{-7}.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Frankfurt and Strikman (1999) and Frankfurt, Guzey, and Strikman (2001) by using a more complete unitarized model for diffraction (Capella et al., 2002) that allows reliable extrapolation to smaller x and higher W^2, and by covering the full low Q^2 region rather than only Q^2 ≥ 4 GeV^2. The results enable predictions for future lepton-ion colliders and provide detailed multiplicity reduction factors for heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, confirming the estimates of Armesto, Capella, and Ferreiro (2002) and Armesto, Capella, Ferreiro, and Salgado (2002).
