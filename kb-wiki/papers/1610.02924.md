# The role of meson exchange currents in charged current (anti)neutrino-nucleus scattering

> We present our recent progress in the description of neutrino-nucleus interaction in the GeV region, of interest for ongoing and future oscillation experiments. In particular, we discuss the weak excitation of two-particle-two-hole states induced by meson exchange currents in a fully relativistic framework. We compare the results of our model with recent measurements of neutrino scattering cross sections, showing the crucial role played by two-nucleon knockout in the interpretation of the data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that two-particle-two-hole (2p2h) excitations induced by meson exchange currents (MEC) are essential for reproducing measured charged-current quasielastic (CCQE) neutrino and antineutrino cross sections, but the model's success is conditional on the specific kinematic regime (GeV region) and on prior validation against electron scattering data.

## Method

The authors compute the 2p2h hadronic tensor within a relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model, using tree-level one-pion-exchange MEC operators (seagull, pion-in-flight, pion-pole, and Delta-pole diagrams). They combine this 2p2h-MEC contribution with the SuSAv2 (Super-Scaling-Approach version 2) model for the quasielastic and Delta-resonance regions, forming the "SuSAv2-MEC" model. The model predictions are compared with flux-averaged differential cross section data from the MiniBooNE, MINERvA, and T2K experiments for neutrino and antineutrino scattering on carbon-12.

## Key numbers

- The 2p2h MEC contribution amounts to about 20-25% of the total response for neutrinos and 15-20% for antineutrinos in the MiniBooNE experiment (mean beam energy 0.788 GeV for neutrinos, 0.665 GeV for antineutrinos).
- For momentum transfers q = 800 and 1200 MeV/c, the 2p2h MEC strength at the peak maximum is around 1/2 of the 1p1h response in the T and T' channels.
- The pp/np ratio for neutrino-induced 2p2h final states is around 5-6 near the maximum of the cross section.
- The MINERvA mean beam energy is about 3.5 GeV for both neutrinos and antineutrinos.

## Context

This work extends the SuSAv2 model (Amaro 2004) by adding MEC-induced 2p2h contributions, following prior validation against electron scattering data (Megias 2016). It directly addresses the long-standing discrepancy between CCQE data from MiniBooNE, MINERvA, and T2K and models based only on one-body currents. The results enable future application to asymmetric nuclei like argon-40 (relevant for DUNE) and motivate extension to the full inelastic region beyond the Delta resonance.
