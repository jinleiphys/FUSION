# Dipole-dipole dispersion interactions between neutrons

> We investigate the long-range interactions between two neutrons utilizing recent data on the neutron static and dynamic electric and magnetic dipole polarizabilities. The resulting long-range potentials are used to make quantitative comparisons between the collisions of a neutron with a neutron and a neutron with a proton. We also assess the importance of the first pion production threshold and first excited state of the nucleon, the $Δ$-resonance ($J^π$ = + 3/2, I = 3/2). We found both dynamical effects to be quite relevant for distances r between ~ 50 fm up to ~$10^3$ fm in the nn system, the neutron-wall system and in the wall-neutron-wall system, reaching the expected asymptotic limit beyond that. Relevance of our findings to the confinement of ultra cold neutrons inside bottles is discussed.

# Dipole-dipole dispersion interactions between neutrons

## Key claim
The long-range Casimir-Polder interaction between two neutrons, and between a neutron and a wall, is significantly influenced by dynamical effects from pion production threshold and the Delta resonance for separation distances between approximately 50 fm and 10^3 fm, but the asymptotic static-limit form is only reached beyond 10^3 fm; the effect on ultracold neutron confinement in bottles is extremely small compared to the repulsive Fermi pseudopotential.

## Method
The authors parametrized the dynamic electric and magnetic dipole polarizabilities of the neutron using chiral effective field theory results from Lensky, McGovern, and Pascalutsa (2015), fitting analytic forms that incorporate pion production threshold behavior and the Delta resonance. They then computed the Casimir-Polder interaction potentials for neutron-neutron, neutron-wall, and wall-neutron-wall systems by integrating these dynamic polarizabilities over imaginary frequencies, and compared the results to the static-limit approximations. The effect on scattering lengths and cross sections was estimated using first-order perturbation theory with plane-wave neutron wavefunctions.

## Key numbers
- Static electric dipole polarizability of the neutron: α_n = (11.6 ± 1.5) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (PDG recommended value) and α_n = (12.5 ± 1.8) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (KosCamWis03 recommendation)
- Static magnetic dipole polarizability of the neutron: β_n = (3.7 ± 2.0) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (PDG) and β_n = (2.7 ∓ 1.8) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (KosCamWis03)
- Asymptotic neutron-neutron Casimir-Polder potential: V*_{CP,nn}(r) ≈ -0.49 × 10⁻³ (r/fm)⁻⁷ MeV for r → ∞
- Asymptotic neutron-proton Casimir-Polder potential: V*_{CP,pn}(r) ≈ 0.91 × 10⁻³ (r/fm)⁻⁴ [-1 + 0.40 (r/fm)⁻¹] MeV for r → ∞
- Ratio of CP-induced cross section corrections at 1 eV center-of-mass energy: Δσ_nn / Δσ_pn ≈ 1.36 × 10⁻⁵ (for R = 20 fm, q = 5 × 10⁻⁵ fm⁻¹)
- Neutron-neutron scattering length: a_nn = -18.9 ± 0.4 fm
- Neutron-proton scattering length: a_pn = -23.74 fm
- The magnetic dipole polarizability contribution decreases the neutron-neutron Casimir-Polder interaction strength by about 10%
- The static-limit wall-neutron-wall potential at the midpoint: V*_{CP,WnW}(0,L) = -11π³ α_n(0) / (90 α₀ L⁴)

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework of Feinberg and Sucher (1970) for retarded dispersion interactions between polarizable particles to the neutron-neutron system, using modern chiral effective field theory calculations of neutron polarizabilities from Lensky, McGovern, and Pascalutsa (2015) and Hildebrandt et al. (2003). It improves upon the earlier estimate by Arnold (1973), which used outdated polarizability values and lacked dynamic polarizabilities. The results enable quantitative assessment of whether Casimir-Polder interactions could affect ultracold neutron confinement in material bottles, where the dominant repulsive interaction is the Fermi pseudopotential (e.g., 252 neV for nickel walls, 54 neV for aluminum). The authors find the CP effect is negligible for practical neutron confinement, but the work provides a framework for future studies of long-range electromagnetic interactions in few-nucleon systems.
