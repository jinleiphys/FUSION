# Observables in Strongly Coupled Anisotropic Theories

> We review certain anisotropic gauge/gravity dualities, focusing more on a theory with space dependent axion term. Then we discuss and also present some new results for several observables: the static potential and force, the imaginary part of the static potential, the quark dipole in the plasma wind, the drag force and diffusion time, the jet quenching of heavy and light quarks, the energy loss of rotating quarks, the photon production and finally the violation of the holographic viscosity over entropy bound. The corresponding weakly coupled results are also discussed. Finally we investigate the bounds of the parameters of the current strongly coupled anisotropic theories attempting to match them with the observed quark-gluon plasma and report the problems appear.

## Key claim

The paper reviews and presents new results for several observables in strongly coupled anisotropic plasmas using gauge/gravity dualities, but concludes that quantitative predictions for the observed expanding quark-gluon plasma are not possible using these particular dualities due to certain bounds on their parameters.

## Method

The author studies two anisotropic supergravity backgrounds: a top-down type IIB solution with a space-dependent axion term (Background 1, from Mateos and Trancanelli 2011) and a stationary anisotropic solution with a singular metric derived from an anisotropic energy-momentum tensor (Background 2, from Janik and Witaszczyk 2008). For Background 1, analytical expansions are used for small anisotropy parameter a/T, while numerical solutions are employed for larger anisotropies. Observables are computed using the holographic dictionary: Wilson loops for static potentials, Nambu-Goto action for drag force and jet quenching, and fluctuation-dissipation relations for transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The anisotropic phase of the QGP lasts approximately 0.1 fm less than or similar to tau less than or similar to 0.3-2 fm after formation at tau approximately 0.1 fm
- For Background 1 at small anisotropy: temperature T = 1/(pi u_h) + a^2 u_h (5 log 2 - 2)/(48 pi) + O(a^4)
- Energy density: E = (3 pi^2 N_c^2 T^4)/8 + a^2 (N_c^2 T^2)/32 + O(a^4)
- Pressures: P_{x1 x2} = (pi^2 N_c^2 T^4)/8 + a^2 (N_c^2 T^2)/32 + O(a^4); P_{x3} = (pi^2 N_c^2 T^4)/8 - a^2 (N_c^2 T^2)/32 + O(a^4)
- Entropy density: s proportional to (pi^2 N_c^2 T^3)/2 + a^2 (N_c^2 T)/16 + O(a^4)
- For large anisotropy in Background 1: s proportional to N_c^2 a^{1/3} T^{8/3}
- Relation between weak-coupling anisotropy parameter xi and Background 1 parameter a: xi approximately (5 a^2)/(8 pi^2 T^2) for a much less than T
- Relation between xi and Background 2 parameter B: xi = (5/4)B + O(B^2) for small B

## Context

This work extends holographic studies of the quark-gluon plasma from equilibrium (reviewed in multiple references) to the anisotropic non-equilibrium phase that occurs in heavy-ion collisions. The author compares results from strongly coupled anisotropic models to weakly coupled calculations by Mrowczynski (2004), the quarkonium studies by Dumitru et al. (2009), and the jet quenching calculations by Romatschke and Strickland (2004). The paper enables future work by establishing that the current anisotropic holographic models cannot make quantitative contact with experimental QGP data due to parameter bounds, highlighting the need for improved time-dependent anisotropic dualities.
