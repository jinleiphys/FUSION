# JLab and J-PARC for the J/{\ensuremathψ} Production at the Threshold

> New threshold measurements for $γ~p\to p~J/ψ\to p~(μ^+μ^-)$ by 007 and $γ^\ast~p\to p~J/ψ\to p~(e^+e^-)$ by CLAS12 allow us to extend the previous phenomenological determination of the J/\ensuremathψ-proton scattering length, $α_{J/ψp}$, using GlueX threshold data for $γ~p\to p~J/ψ\to p~(e^+e^-)$. The agreement between all three J/\ensuremathψ data sets shows no indication of systematic differences between methodologies. Furthermore, perturbative QCD predictions support the phenomenological determination of heavy vector meson-nucleon scattering lengths. The role of the nucleon form factor, $F_N$, is discussed, and a possible correction to the phenomenological scattering length for heavy vector meson photoproduction is calculated using the pole form of $F_N$. Upcoming J-PARC threshold measurements of the reaction $π^-~p\to n~J/ψ\to n~(l^+l^-)$ will help to evaluate the possible role of heavy pentaquark, $P_c$, states in low-energy $J/ψ$ production and the effects caused by the nucleon form factors.

## Key claim

The paper extends the phenomenological determination of the J/ψ-proton scattering length using new threshold measurements from JLab experiments (007 and CLAS12) alongside previous GlueX data, finding agreement between all three data sets with no systematic differences; however, the extracted scattering length is much smaller than typical hadron sizes, which the authors attribute to the "young meson" effect where the recently produced charm-quark pair has not yet formed a full-sized vector meson, and they note that the nucleon form factor correction may enlarge the extracted value but still leaves it far below 1 fm.

## Method

The authors applied the vector meson dominance (VMD) model to relate near-threshold photoproduction total cross sections of γ p → J/ψ p to elastic J/ψ-proton scattering, fitting the total cross sections as a function of the J/ψ center-of-mass momentum q using a polynomial expansion in odd powers of q (σ_t = a q + b q³ + c q⁵). The linear coefficient a was then converted to the absolute value of the J/ψ-proton scattering length using the formula |α_{V N}|² = a [α/(12π) × M_V k_thr / Γ(V → e⁺ e⁻)], where α is the fine structure constant, M_V is the vector meson mass, k_thr is the photon threshold momentum, and Γ is the partial decay width to electron-positron pairs. The authors also calculated a correction to the phenomenological scattering length using a pole form of the nucleon form factor F_N(t) = 1/(1 - t/Λ²) with Λ² = 0.71 GeV².

## Key numbers

- J/ψ-proton scattering length from combined fit of GlueX high-luminosity, 007, and CLAS12 data: |α_{J/ψ p}| = 2.74 ± 0.27 mfm (millifermi)
- J/ψ-proton scattering length from GlueX high-luminosity data alone: |α_{J/ψ p}| = 2.89 ± 0.34 mfm
- J/ψ-proton scattering length from GlueX low-luminosity data: |α_{J/ψ p}| = 3.08 ± 0.55 mfm
- Threshold energy for J/ψ photoproduction on a fixed proton target: E = 8.2 GeV
- Momentum transfer at J/ψ threshold: t = -2.2 GeV²
- GlueX high-luminosity data: 320 pb⁻¹ luminosity, 2270 ± 58 J/ψ events
- 007 experiment: 8.68 × 10⁶ pb⁻¹ luminosity, 1041 ± 55 J/ψ events (muon mode)
- CLAS12 experiment: 149 pb⁻¹ luminosity, 780 ± 40 J/ψ events
- Corrected J/ψ-proton scattering length using pole form factor: 0.0113 ± 0.0011 fm
- φ-proton scattering length: 0.063 ± 0.010 fm
- Υ-proton scattering length (theoretical projection): 0.00051 ± 0.00003 fm

## Context

This work extends previous phenomenological analyses of vector meson-proton scattering lengths by Strakovsky et al. (2019, 2025) using GlueX data, now incorporating new threshold measurements from the 007 Collaboration (Duran 2022, 007 2026) and CLAS12 (Chatagnon 2026). The results are compared with theoretical calculations from the Nanjing University group (Tang 2025) who obtained |α_{J/ψ N}| = 6.34 mfm, the Bonn University group (Du 2020) who found |α^{J=1/2}| = 0.2-3.1 mfm and |α^{J=3/2}| = 0.2-3.0 mfm, and the Tsinghua University group (Wu 2024) who reported α_{J/ψ N} = -10 to -0.1 mfm via coupled-channel mechanisms. The paper also discusses the Lattice HAL Collaboration result (Lyu 2024) of α(²S_{1/2}) = 380 ± 40 mfm from elastic (c̄c)p scattering, which is much larger than the photoproduction values, highlighting the "young meson" puzzle. Upcoming J-PARC measurements of π⁻ p → n J/ψ will help evaluate the role of heavy pentaquark P_c states and nucleon form factor effects without relying on the VMD hypothesis.
