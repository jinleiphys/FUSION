# On eta-pi mixing close to the eta-Helium threshold

> A K-matrix formalism is used to relate the amplitudes for the three reactions pd --> 3He eta, pi- 3H --> 3He eta and pd --> 3H pi+. Free parameters are fitted to the available experimental data and an extrapolation below the eta 3He threshold is made to see the origin of the eta 3He threshold enhancement. The existence of a virtual -- and not a quasi-bound -- state finds support in the data. The K-matrix permits a discussion of eta-pi mixing. A mixing parameter of 0.010(5), i.e. a mixing angle theta =0.6(3) degrees, is extracted from a best fit to the very recent pd --> 3He pi0 reaction data.

# On eta-pi mixing close to the eta-Helium threshold

## Key claim

A K-matrix analysis of pd -> 3He eta, pi- 3H -> 3He eta, and pd -> 3H pi+ reactions supports the existence of a virtual state (not a quasi-bound state) in the eta-3He system near threshold, and extracts an eta-pi mixing parameter of 0.010(5) corresponding to a mixing angle of 0.6(3) degrees from a fit to recent pd -> 3He pi0 data. The mixing parameter is smaller than the 1.5(4) degrees extracted from the pi d -> eta NN reaction by Tippens (2001), and the result carries large uncertainty due to limited data.

## Method

A four-channel K-matrix formalism was applied to the S-wave two-body subsystems of the meson-3N system, coupling the pd, 3He eta, 3He pi0, and 3H pi+ channels under the assumption of isospin conservation. The K-matrix parameters were reduced from 10 to 5 relevant elements using isospin symmetry and an S(1535) resonance dominance model, then fitted to 17 experimental data points (8 from SATURNE for pd -> 3He eta, 4 each from COSY for 3H pi+ and 3He pi0, and 1 from Brookhaven for pi+ 3H -> 3He eta) using the Minuit minimization package.

## Key numbers

- K_{p,eta} = 0.0115(9) fm, the pd -> 3He eta transition K-matrix element
- K_{p,pi} = 0.00207(3) fm, the pd -> 3He pi0 transition K-matrix element
- K_{pi,eta} = 0.067(11) fm, the pi-eta coupling K-matrix element
- K_{eta,eta} = 4.24(29) + i 0.69(81) fm, the eta-3He diagonal K-matrix element
- A_{eta,eta} = 4.24(29) + i 0.72(81) fm, the eta-3He scattering length (large positive real part indicating a virtual state)
- |A_{p,eta}| = 0.013 fm, the modulus of the pd -> 3He eta transition length from SATURNE data
- |K_{pi,eta}| ≈ 0.07 fm, from Brookhaven pi+ 3H -> 3He eta data
- |A_{p,pi}| ≈ 0.00021 fm, crude estimate from pd -> 3H pi+ data
- omega = |K_{p,eta}/K_{p,pi}| = 5.54(50), the ratio of transition matrix elements
- psi = 4.14(27), the relative phase between K_{p,eta} and K_{p,pi}
- theta = 0.010(5) = 0.6(3) degrees, the eta-pi mixing parameter
- lambda = 1.04(5), the isospin violation correction factor for the charged-to-neutral pion ratio

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of eta-3He interactions at SATURNE by Mayer (1996) and theoretical work by Wilkin (1993), Wycech, Green and Niskanen (1995), and Belyaev et al. (1995) that suggested either virtual or quasi-bound states in the eta-3He system. The analysis uses new COSY data from Magiera and Machner (2000) and Hawranek et al. (2003) on pion production to constrain the K-matrix below the eta threshold. The extracted mixing angle of 0.6(3) degrees is smaller than the 1.5(4) degrees from the pi d -> eta NN reaction by Tippens (2001) but consistent with theoretical calculations by Piekarewicz (1993) and Leutwyler (1996) that yield 0.75-0.85 degrees. The work enables future extraction of the mixing parameter with improved precision, provided more detailed data are obtained in the proton laboratory momentum range 1.55-1.59 GeV.
