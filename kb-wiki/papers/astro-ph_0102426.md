# Shadows of Relic Neutrino Masses and Spectra on Highest Energy GZK Cosmic Rays

> The Ultra High Energy (UHE) neutrino scattering onto relic cosmic neutrinos in galactic and local halos offers an unique way to overcome GZK cut-off. The UHE nu secondary of UHE photo-pion decays may escape the GZK cut-off and travel on cosmic distances hitting local light relic neutrinos clustered in dark halos. The Z resonant production and the competitive W^+W^-, ZZ pair production define a characteristic imprint on hadronic consequent UHECR spectra. This imprint keeps memory both of the primary UHE nu spectra as well as of the possible relic neutrino masses values, energy spectra and relic densities. Such an hadronic showering imprint should reflect into spectra morphology of cosmic rays near and above GZK 10^{19}-10^{21}eV cut-off energies. ## Key claim

The paper proposes that ultra-high-energy (UHE) neutrinos from distant sources can scatter off relic cosmic neutrinos clustered in galactic and local dark halos, producing hadronic showers via Z resonance and W+W-/ZZ pair production that could explain cosmic rays above the GZK cutoff; the resulting spectral imprints depend on the specific values of relic neutrino masses, with the condition that the Z resonance requires fine-tuned neutrino masses around 0.4 eV to produce nucleons near observed GZK energies.

## Method

The authors compute cross sections for three interaction channels between incoming UHE neutrinos and relic neutrinos: s-channel Z resonance (nu nu_r -> Z -> hadrons), s-channel W+W- and ZZ pair production (nu nu_r -> W+W-, ZZ), and t-channel W exchange (nu_i nu_j -> l_i l_j). They apply Lorentz transformations to convert the center-of-mass particle spectra (assumed power-law with index alpha ~ 1.5 from Monte Carlo simulations) into laboratory-frame fluence spectra for secondary nucleons, photons, and electrons, then normalize these to the observed UHECR flux near GZK energies assuming a 1% interaction probability.

## Key numbers

- Z resonance peak cross section: <sigma_Z> = 4.2 x 10^-32 cm^2
- For relic neutrino mass m_nu = 0.4 eV, Z resonance occurs at incoming neutrino energy E_nu = 10^22 eV, producing protons at E_p = 2.2 x 10^20 eV with energy fluence dN/dE x E^2 = 1.2 eV
- For relic neutrino mass m_nu = 1.2 eV, Z resonance occurs at E_nu = 3.33 x 10^21 eV
- For relic neutrino masses m_nu1 = 0.1 eV and m_nu2 = 0.05 eV (non-degenerate), Z resonances occur at E_nu1 = 4 x 10^22 eV and E_nu2 = 8 x 10^22 eV
- Asymptotic cross section for W+W- and t-channel: sigma_asym ~ 108.5 pb
- ZZ pair production cross section formula: sigma_ZZ = (G^2 M_Z^2 / 4 pi) x y x (1 + y^2/4)/(1 - y/2) x {ln[2/y (1 - y/2 + sqrt(1-y))] - sqrt(1-y)} where y = 4 M_Z^2 / s, with G^2 M_Z^2 / 4 pi = 35.2 pb
- Energy peak for photons from Z decay: E_gamma = 9.5 x 10^19 eV with fluence 4.25 eV
- Energy peak for electrons from pion decay: E_e = 5 x 10^19 eV with fluence 2.4 eV
- Energy peak for prompt electrons: E_e = 5 x 10^21 eV with fluence 0.66 eV

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposals by Fargion and Salis (1997), Fargion, Mele, and Salis (1999), Weiler (1999), and Yoshida et al. (1998) that UHE neutrino scattering on relic neutrinos could overcome the GZK cutoff, which was originally identified by Greisen, Zatsepin, and Kuzmin (1966). The paper introduces for the first time the ZZ pair production channel as an additional mechanism and considers the spectral signatures of non-degenerate neutrino masses split near the Super-Kamiokande atmospheric neutrino anomaly value of Delta m ~ 0.1 eV. This framework enables future searches for spectral modulations in UHECR data above 10^19 eV that could reveal the mass hierarchy of relic neutrinos and distinguish between Z resonance, WW, and ZZ production channels.
