# Neutrino and antineutrino charged-current multi-nucleon cross sections revisited

> In this work we improve on several aspects of the computation of the (anti-)neutrino charged-current multi-nucleon cross section carried out in Phys.Rev.C 83 (2011) 045501 and Phys.Rev.C 102 (2020) 024601. Most importantly, we implement a consistent treatment of the nucleon self-energy in the $W^\pm N\to N'π$ amplitude entering the definition of the two-particle two-hole (2p2h) cross-section, and estimate the source of uncertainty of our model due to a simplified treatment of the $Δ$ self-energy. Our new predictions are around $20-40\%$ higher than previously. We show comparisons for the inclusive lepton double-differential cross sections, with no pions in the final state, measured by MiniBooNE on carbon and by T2K on carbon and oxygen. In all cases, we find an excellent reproduction of the experiments, and in particular, the neutrino MiniBooNE data is now well described without requiring a global $90\%$ re-scaling of the flux. In addition, we take the opportunity of this revision to discuss in detail several important issues of the calculation of the 2p2h cross section, delving into the microscopic dynamics of the multi-nucleon mechanisms. The improved treatment presented in this work provides realistic first-step emitted two-nucleon final state momentum configurations, beyond the approximation of phase-space distributions.

## Key claim

The improved treatment of nucleon self-energy in the $W^\pm N\to N'\pi$ amplitude leads to 2p2h cross sections that are 20-40% higher than previous predictions, and the neutrino MiniBooNE data is now well described without requiring a global 90% re-scaling of the flux, though the authors note that the largest source of theoretical uncertainty comes from the simplified treatment of the $\Delta$ self-energy, which they estimate by varying the effective real part of the $\Delta$ self-energy in the range $[-50\rho/\rho_0, +30 \rho/\rho_0]$ MeV.

## Method

The authors compute the (anti-)neutrino charged-current multi-nucleon (2p2h) cross section using a many-body field theory approach based on the local density approximation, where the $W$ boson self-energy in nuclear matter is expanded in terms of nucleon hole lines. The calculation employs an effective spin-isospin interaction between $NN$, $N\Delta$, and $\Delta\Delta$ states that includes pion and rho exchanges supplemented by short-range correlations and RPA collective polarization corrections, going beyond the simple one-pion exchange used in many other approaches. The $W^\pm N\to N'\pi$ amplitude is taken from a model that includes the $\Delta(1232)$ resonance and non-resonant background terms, with the $\Delta$ propagator using the pure spin-3/2 projector operator and consistent couplings.

## Key numbers

* New predictions for 2p2h cross sections are 20-40% higher than those obtained in the previous works of Phys.Rev.C 83 (2011) 045501 and Phys.Rev.C 102 (2020) 024601.
* The two-nucleon removal energy corrections used are approximately 27 MeV for carbon and 22 MeV for oxygen.
* The pion and rho form factor cutoffs are $\Lambda_\pi = 1200$ MeV and $\Lambda_\rho = 2500$ MeV, with meson masses $m_\pi = 139$ MeV and $m_\rho = 770$ MeV.
* The $\pi NN$ coupling constant is $f^2_{\pi NN}/4\pi = 0.08$, and the $\pi N\Delta$ coupling is $f^*_{\pi N\Delta} = 2.13 f_{\pi NN}$.
* The real part of the $\Delta$ self-energy in the medium is varied in the range $[-50\rho/\rho_0, +30 \rho/\rho_0]$ MeV to estimate systematic uncertainty.

## Context

This work extends the Valencia group's many-body framework previously applied to inclusive scattering of photons (Carrasco 1989), electrons (Gil 1997), and pions from nuclei (Salcedo 1987, Nieves 1993, 1991), and specifically improves on the neutrino 2p2h calculations of Nieves (2011) and Sobczyk (2020). The corrected treatment of nucleon self-energy resolves a theoretical inconsistency in those earlier works, and the new predictions enable more reliable modeling of the CC0$\pi$ cross section for neutrino oscillation experiments like MiniBooNE and T2K, as well as providing realistic first-step emitted two-nucleon final state momentum configurations beyond phase-space distributions.
