# Vortical waves in a quantum fluid with vector, axial, and helical charges. I. Non-dissipative transport

> Due to the spin-orbit coupling, Dirac fermions, submerged in a thermal bath with finite macroscopic vorticity, exhibit a spin polarisation along the direction parallel to the vorticity vector $\boldsymbolΩ$. Due to the symmetries of the Lagrangian for free massless Dirac particles, there are three independent and classically conserved currents corresponding to the vector, axial, and helical charges. The constitutive relations for the charge currents and the stress-energy tensor at thermal equilibrium, derived in the framework of quantum field theory at finite temperature, reveal vorticity-induced contributions that deviate from the perfect fluid form. In this paper, we consider the mode structure of the corresponding hydrodynamical theory and derive collective excitations associated with coherent fluctuations of all three charges. We show that the chirally imbalanced rotating fluid should possess non-reciprocal gapless waves that propagate with different velocities along and opposite to the vorticity vector. We also uncover a strictly unidirectional mode, which we call the Axial Vortical Wave, propagating in the background of the axial charge density. The emergence of this wave can be traced back to earlier studies of vortical chiral fluids in a hydrodynamic approach. We also point out an unexpected instability in the limit of degenerate matter and discuss possible solutions when helicity and axial charge non-conservation are taken into account.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a rotating fluid of massless Dirac fermions, when accounting for the independent helical degree of freedom (distinct from chirality), supports non-reciprocal gapless waves propagating with different velocities along and opposite to the vorticity vector, including a strictly unidirectional mode called the Axial Vortical Wave that propagates only in one direction relative to the vorticity depending on the sign of the axial chemical potential.

## Method

The authors start from the grand canonical ensemble description of free massless Dirac fermions in a rigidly rotating thermal bath, characterized by vector, axial, and helical chemical potentials. They compute the constitutive relations for charge currents and the stress-energy tensor at thermal equilibrium using quantum field theory at finite temperature, then perform a linear mode analysis of the resulting hydrodynamic equations in the Landau frame, working to first order in the angular velocity Omega and neglecting dissipative effects. The wave spectrum is derived by solving the coupled conservation equations for the three charge densities and the energy-momentum tensor, focusing on fluctuations propagating along the vorticity direction.

## Key numbers

- The classical thermodynamic pressure is given by P = -(T^4/pi^2) * sum_{sigma,lambda} Li_4(-exp(mu_{sigma,lambda}/T)), where Li_4 is the polylogarithm and mu_{sigma,lambda} = sigma*mu_V + 2*lambda*mu_A + 2*sigma*lambda*mu_H
- The vortical conductivities in the Landau frame are sigma^omega_ell = (1/2)*(partial^2 P)/(partial mu_ell partial mu_A) - (Q_ell Q_A)/(E+P)
- The vector tau^mu is of third order in Omega and can be neglected when working to linear order in Omega
- The shear-stress tensor pi^{munu}_L is quadratic with respect to Omega and is disregarded in the linear analysis

## Context

This work extends the existing literature on anomalous hydrodynamic modes in chiral fluids, particularly the Chiral Magnetic Wave (Kharzeev, 2010) and Chiral Vortical Wave (Jiang, 2015), by incorporating the helical degree of freedom as an independent conserved charge. The paper builds on the kinetic theory framework developed by Ambrus (2019) for helical vortical effects and generalizes the mode analysis of Gorbar (2017) to include helicity fluctuations. The results enable future experimental searches for these waves in heavy-ion collision experiments at facilities such as the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider, where the quark-gluon plasma is expected to possess significant vorticity.
