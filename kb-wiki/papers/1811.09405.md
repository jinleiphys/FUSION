# The problem of cluster separability in relativistic few-body systems

> An appropriate framework for dealing with hadron structure and hadronic physics in the few-GeV energy range is relativistic quantum mechanics. The Bakamjian-Thomas construction provides a systematic procedure for implementing interactions in a relativistic invariant way. It leads, however, to problems with cluster separability. It has been known for some time, due to Sokolov's pioneering work, that mass operators with correct cluster properties can be obtained through a series of unitary transformations making use of so-called packing operators. In the present contribution we sketch an explicit construction of packing operators for three-particle systems consisting of distinguishable, spinless particles.

# The problem of cluster separability in relativistic few-body systems

## Key claim
The Bakamjian-Thomas construction for relativistic three-particle systems fails to satisfy cluster separability, but this can be remedied by applying a series of unitary transformations using Sokolov's packing operators. The authors sketch an explicit construction of these packing operators for three-particle systems of distinguishable, spinless particles, though the practical applicability depends on the ability to calculate the packing operators.

## Method
The authors start with Bakamjian-Thomas (BT) type four-momentum operators for two-particle subsystems and add a third particle via tensor-product construction to form 2+1-body systems. They then identify unitary packing operators that transform the four-velocity operators of these 2+1-body systems into the free three-particle four-velocity operator, thereby converting the mass operators into BT-type form. The packing operators are further split into products of unitary operators depending on two-particle mass operators, and their action is computed using mixed velocity eigenstates to achieve a three-body mass operator with correct cluster properties.

## Key numbers
- The paper presents no explicit numerical results; it is a formal/methodological contribution.

## Context
This work extends Sokolov's (1978) formal solution to the cluster separability problem in relativistic quantum mechanics by providing an explicit construction of packing operators for three-particle systems. It builds on the Bakamjian-Thomas (1953) construction for implementing interactions in Poincaré generators and uses the point-form of relativistic dynamics as developed by Dirac (1949) and Klink (2018). The authors note that while the formal solution has been known for some time, cluster separability has been neglected in practical applications, which may be justified for weakly bound nuclear systems as discussed by Keister (2011) but requires thorough investigation for strong binding. This work enables numerical studies to clarify the role of cluster separability in relativistic three-body systems.
