# Partial Dynamical Symmetry as an Intermediate Symmetry Structure

> We introduce the notion of a partial dynamical symmetry for which a prescribed symmetry is neither exact nor completely broken. We survey the different types of partial dynamical symmetries and present empirical examples in each category.

## Key claim

Partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) is an intermediate symmetry structure where a prescribed symmetry is neither exact nor completely broken, allowing some (but not all) states to retain solvability, good quantum numbers, or symmetry-dictated structure, depending on the type of PDS.

## Method

The paper surveys three types of partial dynamical symmetry (type I: part of the states have all the dynamical symmetry; type II: all the states have part of the dynamical symmetry; type III: part of the states have part of the dynamical symmetry) using the interacting boson model (IBM) based on a U(6) spectrum generating algebra. For each type, Hamiltonians are constructed from boson-pair or boson-triplet operators that annihilate specific intrinsic states, ensuring that a subset of states (or selected quantum numbers) remain solvable while others become mixed. Empirical examples are presented for nuclei including 168Er, 196Pt, and 162Dy.

## Key numbers

* 168Er (N=16): SU(3) PDS type I calculation with parameters t0=2t2=4, λ1=13 keV reproduces experimental B(E2) branching ratios from the γ band; for example, the 2+γ → 0+g ratio is 64.27 (experiment 54.0) and 2+γ → 2+g ratio is 100.0 (experiment 100.0), normalized to the 2+γ → 2+g transition.
* 196Pt (N=6): O(6) dynamical symmetry predicts an anharmonicity of 1.71 for excited bands, compared to the empirical value of 1.30.
* 162Dy (N=15): O(6) PDS type II calculation (H1) with parameters κ0=8 keV, κ2=1.364 keV, λ1=8 keV and type III calculation (H2) with parameters h0=28.5 keV, h2=6.3 keV, λ2=13.45 keV both reproduce ground band B(E2) values; for example, the 2+K=0_1 → 0+K=0_1 transition is 107 (10-2 e2b2) in both calculations, matching the experimental 107(2).
* 162Dy: For H2 (type III), the K=0_2 band has O(6) composition: σ=N (85.50%), σ=N-2 (14.45%), σ=N-4 (0.05%), and is composed of 36.29% β, 63.68% γ2K=0, and 0.03% β2 modes.

## Context

This work extends the concept of dynamical symmetry developed by Iachello and colleagues in the interacting boson model (IBM) and spectrum generating algebras. It addresses the common situation where exact dynamical symmetry predictions are rarely fulfilled in realistic nuclei, requiring symmetry breaking. The paper provides a systematic classification of intermediate symmetry structures (PDS types I, II, III) with concrete nuclear examples (168Er for SU(3) PDS, 196Pt for O(6) PDS, 162Dy for O(6) PDS types II and III), and notes that PDS has also been applied to proton-neutron boson systems (IBM-2), bose-fermi systems (IBFM), fermionic systems (Escudero and Leviatan 2000; Rowe 2001), and molecular spectroscopy (Pinchen 1997). The approach enables future studies of mixed systems with coexisting regularity and chaos (Whelan 1993; Leviatan and Whelan 1996).
