# Small-$x$ Quark and Gluon Helicity Contributions to the Proton Spin Puzzle

> A small-$x$ helicity evolution has been derived in 2016-18 and received an important modification in 2022. This article discusses its general framework and summarizes the recent theoretical developments, including the asymptotic behaviors of helicity PDFs and $g_1$ structure function at small $x$. The latest fits to various polarized scattering data are also discussed. The results from this research program will provide important theoretical inputs for the future polarized small-$x$ measurements at the electron-ion collider (EIC).

## Key claim
The small-$x$ helicity evolution (KPSCTT) predicts asymptotic power-law growth of helicity parton distribution functions and the $g_1$ structure function, but the intercept values differ slightly from those obtained by the earlier Bartels-Ermolaev-Ryskin (BER) approach, with the discrepancy at large $N_c$ being about 0.003 in the intercept parameter and up to 3% for realistic numbers of light quark flavors.

## Method
The authors construct small-$x$ helicity evolution equations within the color glass condensate (CGC) framework using the shockwave formalism and the light-cone operator treatment (LCOT) method. The evolution resums double-logarithmic contributions $\alpha_s \ln^2(1/x)$ and is formulated in terms of polarized dipole amplitudes, including the newly discovered type-2 polarized Wilson line contribution. The equations close into linear integral systems in the large-$N_c$ limit (pure gluonic) and the large-$N_c\&N_f$ limit (including quarks), under the mean-field approximation and in the pre-saturation regime.

## Key numbers
- The small-$x$ asymptotics follow $g_1(x,Q^2) \sim \Delta\Sigma(x,Q^2) \sim \Delta G(x,Q^2) \sim (1/x)^{\alpha_h \sqrt{\alpha_s N_c/2\pi}}$ in the large-$N_c$ limit, with intercept $\alpha_h \simeq 3.661$ for KPSCTT and $\alpha_h \simeq 3.664$ for BER.
- For $N_f=2,3,4$ in the large-$N_c\&N_f$ limit, the intercept $\alpha_h^{(N_f)}$ differs from BER intercepts by up to 3%.
- A fit using the large-$N_c$ KPS evolution (without type-2 contributions) to 122 polarized DIS data points with $x<0.1$ and $Q^2 > m_{\text{charm}}^2$ achieved $\chi^2$ per degree of freedom of 1.01, compared to 1.07 in a prior fit by Sato et al. (2016).
- At $Q^2=10$ GeV$^2$, the quark helicity contribution to the proton spin is estimated to be negative 10-20% when the evolution starting point $x_0$ is near 0.1.

## Context
This work extends the Kovchegov-Sievert-Pitonyak (KPS) helicity evolution (2015-2018) by incorporating the type-2 polarized dipole amplitude discovered by Cougoulic et al. (2022), yielding the KPSCTT equation. It contradicts the earlier Bartels-Ermolaev-Ryskin (BER) approach (1996) in the precise numerical values of the small-$x$ intercepts, though the discrepancy is small. The results provide theoretical inputs for future polarized small-$x$ measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and enable improved global fits to polarized deep-inelastic scattering and semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering data.
