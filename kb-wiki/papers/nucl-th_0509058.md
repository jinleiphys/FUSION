# Entropy in the Thermal Model

> A brief review of the Hadron Gas model with reference to high energy heavy ion collisions is presented. The entropy dependence on temperature and baryonic chemical potential is numerically calculated, together with the entropy distribution between baryons and mesons. The theoretical entropy for a QGP with equivalent parameters is also calculated. It is shown that at low temperatures the dominant entropy contribution comes from baryons, while at high energies the dominant contribution comes from mesons. The turnover from baryon to meson domination occurs at T approx 140 MeV, which corresponds to an energy domain of the AGS at Brookhaven National Laboratory.

## Key claim

This paper presents a numerical calculation of entropy density in the Hadron Gas model, showing that the dominant entropy contribution shifts from baryons at low temperatures to mesons at high temperatures, with the turnover occurring at approximately T ≈ 140 MeV, which corresponds to the energy domain of the AGS at Brookhaven National Laboratory. The author notes that this result is based on a simplified model assuming charge independence (μ_Q = 0) and a static fireball approximation.

## Method

The author numerically integrates quantum-statistical expressions for number density and entropy density using Gauss-Laguerre quadrature with 15 points, validated against Simpson integration to within 5 ppm for number densities and 0.5% for entropy densities. The calculation sums over 358 hadrons composed of u, d, and s quarks with masses below 2.6 GeV, using a grand canonical ensemble with chemical potentials determined by conservation of baryon number, charge, and strangeness. The strangeness chemical potential μ_S is found iteratively via the bisection method for each point on the freeze-out curve, which relates temperature T and baryonic chemical potential μ_B from experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Turnover from baryon-dominated to meson-dominated entropy occurs at T ≈ 140 MeV, corresponding to the AGS energy domain at Brookhaven National Laboratory
- Lattice QCD suggests QGP formation occurs at temperatures of 150-200 MeV or densities of 5-10 ρ₀, where ρ₀ ≈ 3 × 10¹⁴ g/cm³
- Chemical freeze-out temperature at CERN SPS energies is T ≈ 170 MeV; thermal freeze-out occurs at T ≈ 130 MeV
- The ratio μ_S/μ_B approaches a limiting value of approximately 0.236 as μ_B → 0
- For a QGP, the required ratio would be μ_S/μ_B = 1/3
- The average energy per hadron at chemical freeze-out is approximately 1 GeV
- Baryonic chemical potential as a function of beam energy follows μ_B(s) ≈ a/(1 + √s/b) with a ≈ 1.27 GeV and b ≈ 4.3 GeV
- Typical values of the charge chemical potential are -10 MeV < μ_Q < -4 MeV depending on temperature
- For Pb+Pb and Au+Au collisions, the neutron-to-proton ratio is approximately N_p/N_n ≈ 0.66 and Z/A ≈ 0.40

## Context

This work reviews and extends the Hadron Gas thermal model as applied to high-energy heavy ion collisions, building on the freeze-out parameterization established by Cleymans and Redlich (2003) and the beam-energy dependence of μ_B from Cleymans (2003). The numerical calculation of entropy partitioning between baryons and mesons provides a tool for distinguishing hadronic from partonic phases in heavy ion collisions, with implications for interpreting data from RHIC and future LHC experiments where parameters are expected to approach μ_B ≈ 0⁺ and T ≈ 0.17 GeV.
