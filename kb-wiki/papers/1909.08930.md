# Gamow-Teller excitations at finite temperature: Competition between pairing and temperature effects

> The relativistic and nonrelativistic finite temperature proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (FT-PNQRPA) methods are developed to study the interplay of the pairing and temperature effects on the Gamow-Teller excitations in open-shell nuclei, as well as to explore the model dependence of the results by using two rather different frameworks for effective nuclear interactions. The Skyrme-type functional SkM* is employed in the nonrelativistic framework, while the density-dependent meson-exchange interaction DD-ME2 is implemented in the relativistic approach. Both the isoscalar and isovector pairing interactions are taken into account within the FT-PNQRPA. Model calculations show that below the critical temperatures the Gamow-Teller excitations display a sensitivity to both the finite temperature and pairing effects, and this demonstrates the necessity for implementing both in the theoretical framework. The established FT-PNQRPA opens perspectives for the future complete and consistent description of astrophysically relevant weak interaction processes in nuclei at finite temperature such as $β$-decays, electron capture, and neutrino-nucleus reactions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for open-shell nuclei below the critical temperature for pairing collapse, both finite temperature and pairing correlations (isoscalar and isovector) must be included simultaneously to correctly describe Gamow-Teller excitations, as the two effects compete and produce qualitatively different strength distributions than either effect alone.

## Method

The authors developed finite-temperature proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (FT-PNQRPA) methods in both nonrelativistic and relativistic frameworks, using the Skyrme-type functional SkM* for the nonrelativistic approach and the density-dependent meson-exchange interaction DD-ME2 for the relativistic approach. Ground-state properties were obtained from finite-temperature Hartree-Fock BCS (FT-HFBCS) or finite-temperature Hartree BCS (FT-HBCS) calculations, with isovector pairing adjusted to empirical pairing gaps and isoscalar pairing treated as a free parameter in the residual interaction. The FT-PNQRPA matrix was diagonalized to obtain GT- strength distributions for the open-shell nuclei 42Ca, 46Ti, and 118Sn at temperatures from zero up to the critical temperature.

## Key numbers

- For 42Ca without isoscalar pairing at T=0 MeV, the Gamow-Teller resonance (GTR) and low-energy peak were obtained at 17.13 and 8.73 MeV using SkM*, and at 15.85 and 8.57 MeV using DD-ME2.
- With the largest isoscalar pairing strength (V0is=726 MeV fm3 for SkM*, G0is=220 MeV for DD-ME2), the GTR and low-energy peaks shifted to 16.68 and 6.75 MeV (SkM*) and 13.44 and 6.36 MeV (DD-ME2).
- The critical temperature for neutron pairing in 42Ca was 0.89 MeV for SkM* and 0.9 MeV for DD-ME2.
- The experimental reduced GT- transition strength for the 0.61 MeV state in 42Ca is B(GT-)=2.173(47), with total strength below 12 MeV of B(GT-)=2.7(4).
- Adjusting isoscalar pairing to V0is=482 MeV fm3 (SkM*) or G0is=120 MeV (DD-ME2) placed the peak at 0.6 MeV, yielding total B(GT-) below 12 MeV of 2.96 (SkM*) and 2.36 (DD-ME2).

## Context

This work extends previous zero-temperature PNQRPA studies of Gamow-Teller excitations (e.g., Bai et al. 2013, Niu et al. 2015) and finite-temperature RPA studies that neglected pairing (e.g., Paar et al. 2007, Niu et al. 2009) by developing a framework that simultaneously incorporates both temperature and pairing correlations for open-shell nuclei. The FT-PNQRPA method enables future calculations of astrophysically relevant weak interaction processes such as beta-decays, electron capture, and neutrino-nucleus reactions at finite temperature, which are needed for stellar evolution models.
