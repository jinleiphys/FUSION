# Analysis of Light Neutrino Exchange and Short-Range Mechanisms in $0νββ$ Decay

> Neutrinoless double beta decay ($0νββ$) is a crucial test for lepton number violation. Observation of this process would have fundamental implications for neutrino physics, theories beyond the Standard Model and cosmology. Focussing on so called short-range operators of $0νββ$ and their potential interplay with the standard light Majorana neutrino exchange, we present the first complete calculation of the relevant nuclear matrix elements, performed within the interacting boson model (IBM-2). Furthermore, we calculate the relevant phase space factors using exact Dirac electron wavefunctions, taking into account the finite nuclear size and screening by the electron cloud. The obtained numerical results are presented together with up-to-date limits on the standard mass mechanism and effective $0νββ$ short-range operators in the IBM-2 framework. Finally, we interpret the limits in the particle physics scenarios incorporating heavy sterile neutrinos, Left-Right symmetry and R-parity violating supersymmetry.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first complete calculation of nuclear matrix elements for short-range operators in neutrinoless double beta decay within the interacting boson model (IBM-2), including their potential interplay with the standard light Majorana neutrino exchange mechanism, but the derived limits on effective couplings depend on the nuclear model used and the assumed values of nucleon form factors, with matrix elements from different nuclear approaches still varying by factors of approximately 2-3.

## Method

The authors performed a non-relativistic expansion and closure approximation to map the five classes of short-range quark current products from the effective Lagrangian onto nuclear matrix elements between initial and final 0+ nuclear states, using nucleon form factors parametrized in dipole form with charges from lattice QCD and experimental data. They evaluated all relevant nuclear matrix elements within the interacting boson model (IBM-2) framework and calculated phase space factors using exact Dirac electron wavefunctions that account for finite nuclear size and electron cloud screening. The resulting numerical matrix elements and phase space factors were combined with experimental half-life limits to set upper bounds on effective couplings for short-range operators, which were then interpreted in the context of heavy sterile neutrinos, Left-Right symmetry, and R-parity violating supersymmetry.

## Key numbers

- Current experimental limit on the 0νββ decay half-life in ⁷⁶Ge: T₁/₂ > 1.8 × 10²⁶ yr (90% CL), from Agostini et al. (2020)
- Limit on effective Majorana neutrino mass from this bound: |m_ββ| ≲ 79 – 180 meV at 90% CL for unquenched axial coupling g_A = 1.27
- Future experiments will probe |m_ββ| ≈ 20 meV, corresponding to the minimal value for inversely ordered neutrinos
- Nucleon form factor charges used: g_S = 1.0, g_P' = 349, g_V = 1.0, g_W = 3.7, g_A = 1.269, g_P = 231, g_T1 = 1.0, g_T2 = -3.3, g_T3 = 1.34
- Shape parameters for dipole form factors: m_V = 0.84 GeV, m_A = 1.09 GeV, pion mass m_π = 0.138 GeV
- Effective axial-vector charge used in nuclear calculations: g_A = 1.0 (quenched), with corresponding induced pseudoscalar charge g_P = 182

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analytic derivation of short-range operator nuclear matrix elements (Graf et al., 2018) by providing the first complete numerical evaluation within the IBM-2 framework, complementing earlier calculations using different nuclear structure approaches such as those by Barea et al. (2009, 2013, 2015), Simkovic et al. (2007, 2013), Suhonen et al. (1991, 2012), Caurier et al. (2007), Menendez et al. (2008), and Rodriguez et al. (2010). The results enable improved interpretation of experimental limits on 0νββ decay in terms of specific beyond-Standard-Model scenarios including heavy sterile neutrinos, Left-Right symmetric models, and R-parity violating supersymmetry, while the remaining factor-of-2-3 spread among different nuclear models motivates further refinement of nuclear structure calculations.
