# Chiral extrapolation of lattice moments of proton quark distributions

> We present the resolution of a long-standing discrepancy between the moments of parton distributions calculated from lattice QCD and their experimental values. We propose a simple extrapolation formula for the moments of the nonsinglet quark distribution u-d, as a function of quark mass, which embodies the general constraints imposed by the chiral symmetry of QCD. The inclusion of the leading nonanalytic behavior leads to an excellent description of both the lattice data and the experimental values of the moments.

## Key claim

The long-standing 50% discrepancy between lattice QCD moments of the nonsinglet quark distribution \(u-d\) and experimental values is resolved by including the leading nonanalytic chiral behavior \(m_\pi^2 \log m_\pi\) in the extrapolation formula, rather than using a naive linear extrapolation in quark mass.

## Method

The authors fit lattice QCD data for the first three nontrivial moments (\(n=1,2,3\)) of the \(u-d\) quark distribution, taken from quenched simulations at \(\beta=6.0\) and unquenched calculations at \(\beta=5.6\) (both with lattice spacing \(a = 0.1\) fm), using a formula \(\langle x^n \rangle_{u-d} = a_n + b_n m_\pi^2 + a_n c_{\rm LNA} m_\pi^2 \ln(m_\pi^2/(m_\pi^2 + \mu^2))\). The coefficient \(c_{\rm LNA} = -(3 g_A^2 + 1)/(4\pi f_\pi)^2\) is fixed from chiral perturbation theory, while \(a_n\), \(b_n\), and the cutoff mass \(\mu\) are fit parameters. The extrapolation is validated against a meson cloud model (cloudy bag model) before being applied to the lattice data.

## Key numbers

- The best fit to lattice data uses \(\mu = 550\) MeV, with \(\mu\) ranging from 400 to 700 MeV consistent within current errors.
- Fit parameters for the central curves: \(a_1=0.1427\), \(b_1=-0.0624\,{\rm GeV}^{-2}\); \(a_2=0.0459\), \(b_2=-0.0245\,{\rm GeV}^{-2}\); \(a_3=0.0184\), \(b_3=-0.00666\,{\rm GeV}^{-2}\).
- \(\chi^2\) per degree of freedom for the fits: 0.98 (\(n=1\)), 0.60 (\(n=2\)), 0.60 (\(n=3\)).
- Lattice data correspond to quark masses from 30 to 190 MeV (pion masses from over 300 MeV down to \(\sim 50\) MeV) at a momentum scale \(Q^2 \approx 4\) GeV\(^2\).
- A linear extrapolation overestimates experimental values by about 50% for all three moments.

## Context

This work resolves a decade-old discrepancy between lattice QCD calculations of parton distribution moments and experimental data, which had threatened the credibility of ab initio hadron structure calculations. It extends earlier chiral extrapolation studies of hadron masses (Leinweber et al., 2000), magnetic moments (Leinweber et al., 1999), and charge radii (Hackett-Jones et al., 2000) to the case of parton distribution moments. The result enables future lattice calculations to reliably predict hadron observables by working at moderately light pion masses (\(m_\pi^2 = 0.05\) GeV\(^2\)) and using the proposed chiral extrapolation, rather than requiring brute-force calculations at the physical pion mass.
