# The $^3$He(e, e$'$d)p Reaction in q$ω$-constant Kinematics

> The cross section for the $^3$He(e, e$'$d)p reaction has been measured as a function of the missing momentum $p_m$ in q$ω$ -constant kinematics at beam energies of 370 and 576 MeV for values of the three-momentum transfer $q$ of 412, 504 and 604 \mevc. The L(+TT), T and LT structure functions have been separated for $q$ = 412 and 504 \mevc. The data are compared to three-body Faddeev calculations, including meson-exchange currents (MEC), and to calculations based on a covariant diagrammatic expansion. The influence of final-state interactions and meson-exchange currents is discussed. The $p_m$-dependence of the data is reasonably well described by all calculations. However, the most advanced Faddeev calculations, which employ the AV18 nucleon-nucleon interaction and include MEC, overestimate the measured cross sections, especially the longitudinal part, and at the larger values of $q$. The diagrammatic approach gives a fair description of the cross section, but under(over)estimates the longitudinal (transverse) structure function.

## Key claim

The cross section for the $^3$He(e, e$'$d)p reaction is reasonably well described by all calculations, but the most advanced Faddeev calculations using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon interaction with meson-exchange currents overestimate the measured cross sections, especially the longitudinal part and at larger momentum transfer $q$, while the diagrammatic approach underestimates the longitudinal and overestimates the transverse structure function.

## Method

The $^3$He(e, e$'$d)p reaction was measured in $q\omega$-constant kinematics using electron beams of 370 and 576 MeV from the AmPS pulse-stretcher ring at NIKHEF, with scattered electrons detected by the QDD spectrometer and knocked-out deuterons by the QDQ spectrometer. Cross sections were measured as a function of missing momentum $p_m$ for three-momentum transfer $q$ values of 412, 504, and 604 MeV/$c$, and the L(+TT), T, and LT structure functions were separated for $q$ = 412 and 504 MeV/$c$ by comparing data at two beam energies and at deuteron angles both smaller and larger than the momentum transfer angle.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies: 370 and 576 MeV
- Three-momentum transfer values: 412, 504, and 604 MeV/$c$
- Missing momentum range: up to 210 MeV/$c$
- Centre-of-mass energies of the outgoing deuteron: 14.7 MeV (at $q$ = 412 MeV/$c$), 21.0 MeV (at $q$ = 504 MeV/$c$), and 31.1 MeV (at $q$ = 604 MeV/$c$)
- Systematic uncertainty: about 3% for cross sections and 4-5% for structure functions
- Real-to-accidental coincidence ratio: ranged from 15 to 1640 depending on kinematics
- Missing energy resolution: varied between 0.3 and 2.0 MeV depending on kinematics

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements of the $^3$He(e, e$'$d)p reaction in parallel kinematics by Spaltro (1998) to $q\omega$-constant kinematics, enabling separation of the longitudinal-transverse interference structure function for the first time in this reaction. The data are compared to three-body Faddeev calculations by Golak (1995) using Bonn-B and AV18 potentials, and to covariant diagrammatic calculations by Nagorny (1989, 1991, 1994), testing the treatment of final-state interactions and meson-exchange currents. The results enable further investigation of short-distance behavior of the proton-neutron pair in $^3$He and suggest the need for inclusion of three-nucleon forces and Sachs form factors in future calculations.
