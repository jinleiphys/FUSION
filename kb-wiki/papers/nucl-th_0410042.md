# Partial-Wave Analyses of all Proton-Proton and Neutron-Proton Data Below 500 MeV

> In 1993 the Nijmegen group published the results of energy-dependent partial-wave analyses (PWAs) of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering data for laboratory kinetic energies below Tlab=350 MeV (PWA93). In this talk some general aspects, but also the newest developments on the Nijmegen NN PWAs are reported. We have almost finished a new energy-dependent PWA and will discuss some typical aspects of this new PWA; where it differs from PWA93, but also what future developments might be, or should be.

# Partial-Wave Analyses of all Proton-Proton and Neutron-Proton Data Below 500 MeV

## Key claim
The Nijmegen group has nearly completed a new energy-dependent partial-wave analysis (PWA) of all proton-proton and neutron-proton scattering data up to 500 MeV laboratory kinetic energy, extending their earlier PWA93 which was limited to 350 MeV. The new analysis introduces inelasticities via complex boundary conditions above pion-production threshold and replaces the previous long-range interaction with a chiral two-pion exchange potential, but the authors caution that the work remains in an exploratory phase with finetuning still required.

## Method
The authors perform energy-dependent partial-wave analyses by solving the relativistic Schrodinger equation for each partial wave at distances r > b, using known long-range and intermediate-range strong and electromagnetic interactions, while introducing an energy-dependent boundary condition at r = b to parametrize unknown short-range physics phenomenologically. For energies above pion-production threshold, inelasticities are introduced in selected waves using a complex energy-dependent boundary condition. The long-range interaction previously based on a modified Nijm78 potential has been replaced by one-pion exchange and chiral two-pion exchange potentials.

## Key numbers
- The energy range of the PWA has been extended from 350 MeV to 500 MeV laboratory kinetic energy
- The current database includes approximately 5000 proton-proton and 5000 neutron-proton data points below 500 MeV
- The analysis uses approximately 10000 total nucleon-nucleon scattering data points

## Context
This work extends the Nijmegen group's earlier PWA93 (Rentmeester, Timmermans, de Swart, 1993) which covered data below 350 MeV. The new analysis enables separate determination of I=1 and I=0 neutron-proton phase parameters without input from proton-proton partial waves, allowing studies of charge-independence breaking in the I=1 nucleon-nucleon waves. The replacement of the Nijm78 potential with chiral two-pion exchange provides a more model-independent long-range interaction that could be systematically improved by including higher orders, and has already yielded better fits to data and determinations of low-energy coupling constants.
