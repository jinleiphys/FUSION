# Role of chiral two-body currents in $^6$Li magnetic properties in light of a new precision measurement with the relative self-absorption technique

> A direct measurement of the decay width of the excited $0^+_1$ state of $^6$Li using the relative self-absorption technique is reported. Our value of $Γ_{γ, 0^+_1 \to 1^+_1} = 8.17(14)_\mathrm{stat.}(11)_\mathrm{syst.} \mathrm{eV}$ provides sufficiently low experimental uncertainties to test modern theories of nuclear forces. The corresponding transition rate is compared to the results of ab initio calculations based on chiral effective field theory that take into account contributions to the magnetic dipole operator beyond leading order. This enables a precision test of the impact of two-body currents that enter at next-to-leading order.

## Key claim

The measured gamma-decay width of the 0+1 state of 6Li, 8.17(14)_stat.(11)_syst. eV, provides sufficiently low experimental uncertainties to test modern theories of nuclear forces, and when compared to ab initio calculations including two-body currents at next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory, shows excellent agreement, indicating the importance of two-body currents for a correct description of the 6Li nucleus.

## Method

The gamma-decay width of the 0+1 state of 6Li was measured using the relative self-absorption technique, a nuclear resonance fluorescence-based method that uses a normalization target to separate resonant and nonresonant processes. The experiment was performed at the Darmstadt High-Intensity Photon Setup (DHIPS) with bremsstrahlung photons from a 7.1(2) MeV electron beam on a copper radiator, using a lithium carbonate (Li2CO3) scattering target enriched to 95.00(1)% in 6Li and pure 11B normalization targets. On the theory side, ab initio calculations were performed using the importance-truncated no-core shell model (IT-NCSM) with chiral effective field theory interactions, including for the first time a consistent similarity renormalization group (SRG) transformation of the M1 operator and contributions from two-body currents at next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers

*   Gamma-decay width: Gamma_{gamma, 0+1 -> 1+1} = 8.17(+0.14/-0.13)_stat.(+0.10/-0.11)_syst. eV, corresponding to a B(M1; 0+ -> 1+) strength of 15.61(+0.27/-0.25)_stat.(+0.19/-0.21)_syst. mu_N^2.
*   The experimental self-absorption ratio R_exp = 0.5192(20) was obtained with a relative uncertainty of 0.39%.
*   The effective temperature of the Li2CO3 target material was determined to be T_eff = 411(11) K from density functional theory calculations.
*   The most complete theoretical calculation (Hamiltonian I with NLO SRG evolved operator) gave B(M1) = 15.74(12) mu_N^2 and mu = 0.8240(34) mu_N, compared to the experimental B(M1) = 15.61(33) mu_N^2 and the known ground-state magnetic moment mu = 0.82205667(26) mu_N.
*   The 0+1 state of 6Li has an excitation energy of 3562.88(10) keV and a half-life of about 80 attoseconds.

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of the B(M1) strength in 6Li, which were dominated by electron scattering results from Eigenbrod (1969) and Bergstrom (1975) that required model-dependent extrapolation to the photon point, by providing a direct measurement at the photon point with controlled systematic uncertainties. The theoretical calculations go beyond prior work by Calci (2016), Shin (2017), Parzuchowski (2017), and Binder (2018) by including consistent SRG evolution of the M1 operator and two-body currents from chiral effective field theory, and extend the approach of Pastore (2013) by using consistent chiral interactions rather than phenomenological potentials. The results enable a precision test of two-body currents in light nuclei and, combined with the high-precision 6He beta-decay rate measurement by Knecht (2012), offer a comprehensive test of electroweak interactions in the A=6 system.
