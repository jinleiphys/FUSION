# Projection after variation in the finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation

> The finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approximation often breaks symmetries of the underlying many-body Hamiltonian. Restricting the calculation of the HFB partition function to a subspace with good quantum numbers through projection after variation restores some of the correlations lost in breaking these symmetries, although effects of the broken symmetries such as sharp kinks at phase transitions remain. However, the most general projection after variation formula in the finite-temperature HFB approximation is limited by a sign ambiguity. Here, we extend the Pfaffian formula for the many-body traces of HFB density operators introduced by L. M. Robledo in Ref. [1] to eliminate this sign ambiguity and evaluate the more complicated many-body traces required in projection after variation in the most general HFB case. We validate our method through a proof-of-principle calculation of the particle-number-projected HFB thermal energy in a simple model.

# Projection after variation in the finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation

## Key claim

The paper extends Robledo's Pfaffian formula for many-body traces of HFB density operators to eliminate a sign ambiguity that previously limited the most general projection-after-variation (PAV) calculations in finite-temperature HFB, but only for cases where the HFB Hamiltonian has no simplifying degeneracies such as those from time-reversal symmetry.

## Method

The author expresses the symmetry-projected HFB partition function as an integral over group elements of many-body traces of the form Tr[ R(Omega) exp(-beta(H_HFB - mu N)) ]. These traces are rewritten as Tr[ exp(1/2 eta-dagger C eta) ] using the group property of fermion operators, where the matrix C is obtained from matrix multiplication of the symmetry generator matrices and the HFB Hamiltonian matrix. The Pfaffian formula of Robledo (2009) is then applied to evaluate these traces unambiguously by determining the phase factor of the Balian-Brezin decomposition through the expectation value in the particle vacuum state. The method is validated by comparing particle-number-projected thermal energies from the Pfaffian approach with explicit projection in a pairing model with cranking.

## Key numbers

- The model uses the f_{7/2} shell with 8 single-particle orbitals, pairing strength G = 1, half-filling (4 particles), and cranking frequencies omega/G ranging from 0 to 1.5.
- The pairing phase transition in the unprojected HFB occurs at T_c = 1 (in units of G) for zero cranking.
- For the test case with N_s = 8, the calculation of the PAV partition function and thermal energy for all 238 beta values takes approximately 1 second on a laptop (2.7 GHz Intel Core i5 with 8 GB RAM).
- The method scales as O(N_s^3) for matrix multiplication and Pfaffian evaluation per quadrature point, with at worst O(N_s^4) overall scaling for N_s quadrature points.

## Context

This work extends the Pfaffian approach of Robledo (2009) for unprojected HFB density operators to the more complicated traces required in PAV calculations, overcoming a sign ambiguity noted by Rossignoli (1993, 1994) and Tanabe (2005). The method enables PAV calculations in systems where no simplifying symmetries exist, such as odd-even and odd-odd nuclei where time-reversal symmetry is broken, or rotating nuclei with a cranking term as studied by Goodman (1974). This enables finite-temperature HFB studies of odd-mass nuclei with particle-number or angular-momentum PAV, and studies of nuclear response to rotations at finite temperature, though the author notes that PAV remains limited by residual effects of broken symmetries such as sharp kinks at phase transitions.
