# Anisotropic pressure induced by finite-size effects in SU(3) Yang-Mills theory

> We study the pressure anisotropy in anisotropic finite-size systems in SU(3) Yang-Mills theory at nonzero temperature. Lattice simulations are performed on lattices with anisotropic spatial volumes with periodic boundary conditions. The energy-momentum tensor defined through the gradient flow is used for the analysis of the stress tensor on the lattice. We find that a clear finite-size effect in the pressure anisotropy is observed only at a significantly shorter spatial extent compared with the free scalar theory, even when accounting for a rather large mass in the latter.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the pressure anisotropy induced by finite-size effects in SU(3) Yang-Mills theory is remarkably weaker than in a free scalar theory, with a clear deviation from isotropy appearing only at significantly shorter spatial extents than predicted by the free theory, even when accounting for a large scalar mass.

## Method

Lattice simulations of SU(3) Yang-Mills theory were performed on anisotropic spatial volumes with periodic boundary conditions above the critical temperature \(T_c\). The energy-momentum tensor was measured using the gradient flow method, with the flowed field operator defined through perturbative coefficients at two- and three-loop order. The \(t\to0\) limit was taken assuming a linear flow-time dependence, and results were obtained for two lattice spacings (\(N_\tau=12\) and \(16\)) without a full continuum extrapolation.

## Key numbers

- At \(L_xT=1.5\), the ratio \(P_x/P_z = 1\) within statistics for temperatures \(1.4 \le T/T_c \le 2.1\), showing no measurable anisotropy.
- At \(L_xT=1\), the ratio reaches \(P_x/P_z < -1\) for all temperatures studied, compared to \(P_x/P_z = -1\) in the massless free scalar theory.
- The free massless scalar theory shows a clear deviation from isotropy already at \(L_xT=2\), with the ratio crossing zero at \(L_xT \simeq 1.5\).
- For \(T/T_c = 1.12\), the SU(3) YM theory shows greater sensitivity to the periodic boundary condition than at higher temperatures, deviating from the trend observed for \(1.4 \le T/T_c \le 2.1\).
- The ratio \((P_x+\delta)/(P_z+\delta)\) with \(\delta=\Delta/4\) was computed up to \(T/T_c \simeq 25\) (at \(\beta=9.0\)), though temperatures above \(\beta=7.4\) are approximate.

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of Casimir-type effects in gauge theories, following simulations in 2+1 dimensional Yang-Mills theory (Karabali, 2018) and SU(2) gauge theory (Chernodub, 2018). It provides the first systematic lattice investigation of anisotropic stress in 3+1 dimensional SU(3) Yang-Mills theory using the gradient flow method, which enables direct measurement of the stress tensor components. The results are relevant for hydrodynamic modeling of small collision systems in heavy-ion physics, where finite-size corrections to the equation of state may become important, and for understanding the Debye screening scale in jet tomography calculations.
