# An Extension of the Fractional Parentage Expansion to Nonrelativistic and Relativistic $SU(3)_{f}$ Dibaryon Calculations

> The fractional parentage expansion method is extended from $SU(2)_{f}$ nonrelativistic to $SU(3)_{f}$ and relativistic dibaryon calculations. A transformation table between physical bases and symmetry bases for the $SU(3)_{f}$ dibaryon is provided. A program package has been written for dibaryon calculation based on the fractional parentage expansion method.

## Key claim

The fractional parentage expansion method, previously limited to nonrelativistic SU(2) flavor dibaryon calculations, is extended to SU(3) flavor and to relativistic Dirac quark cluster models, contingent on the availability of recently computed SU(mn) ⊃ SU(m) × SU(n) isoscalar factors for up to six particles.

## Method

The authors construct a unitary transformation between physical two-baryon cluster bases (with delocalized single-quark orbitals) and symmetry bases classified by the group chain SU(36) ⊃ SUˣ(2) × SU(18) ⊃ SUᶜ(3) × SU(6) ⊃ SUᶠ(3) × SUᵒ(2). They compute the transformation coefficients as products of isoscalar factors from Chen’s tables, then use fractional parentage expansions to reduce six-quark Hamiltonian matrix elements in the symmetry basis to sums of two-body matrix elements and four-quark overlaps. For the relativistic extension, the lowest Dirac spinor is factorized into a pseudo-orbital part and a Pauli spinor, preserving the same group-theoretic classification.

## Key numbers

- The transformation table covers hypercharge values Y = 2 down to Y = -4, with the Y = 2 part being a revised, phase-consistent version of Harvey’s table 11 (1981) for the I = J = 1 case.
- The H-particle wavefunction is given as a specific linear combination: |H⟩ = √(1/5)[√(3/8)|ΣΣ⟩ – √(4/8)|NΞ⟩ – √(1/8)|ΛΛ⟩] plus hidden-color terms with coefficients √(6/40), √(8/40), √(2/40), √(3/40), –√(4/40), –√(1/40), and –√(8/40).
- The adiabatic potential V_α(s) is computed for separations s from 0.1 fm to 3 fm.
- The number of interacting pairs in the six-quark system is (⁶₂) = 15; for one-body operators the factor is 6/2 = 3.

## Context

This work extends the fractional parentage method of Harvey (1981, 1988) and Chen (1983) from two-flavor to three-flavor (u,d,s) dibaryon calculations, and from nonrelativistic to relativistic quark models. It provides the group-theoretic machinery needed for systematic dibaryon searches in the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) of Wang, Wu, Teng, and Goldman (1992), which is constrained by N-N scattering data. The transformation tables and computer program enable future calculations of dibaryon candidates in the three-flavor world, addressing the long-standing discrepancy between theoretical predictions of quasi-stable dibaryons and the lack of experimental observation (except the deuteron).
