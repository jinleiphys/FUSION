# Giant dipole resonance in proton capture reactions using an extended quantum molecular dynamics model

> Proton capture reaction is an important process concerning the astrophysical origin of the elements. In present work, we focus on giant dipole resonance (GDR) in proton capture reactions, such as $^{11}$B$(p, γ)^{12}$C, $^{27}$Al$(p, γ)^{28}$Si, $^{39}$K$(p, γ)^{40}$Ca, and $^{67}$Co$(p, γ)^{68}$Ni in a framework of an extended quantum molecular dynamics model. The systematic properties of GDR parameters including the peak energy, the strength and full width at half maximum (FWHM) have been studied. The dependence of FWHM on temperature has also been discussed. Some comparisons with experimental data have been presented.

## Key claim

The extended quantum molecular dynamics (EQMD) model can reliably describe giant dipole resonance (GDR) properties in proton capture reactions for compound nuclei in the mass range A ~ 12 to 68, but the proton energies required in the calculation are generally larger than those in experimental data due to the frictional cooling method used in the ground-state initialization.

## Method

The authors used the EQMD model, which incorporates a Skyrme potential, Coulomb potential, symmetry potential, and Pauli potential with a dynamical variable width for Gaussian wave packets of each nucleon. They simulated proton capture reactions at zero impact parameter for four systems: 11B(p,γ)12C, 27Al(p,γ)28Si, 39K(p,γ)40Ca, and 67Co(p,γ)68Ni, running 15000 events per reaction with a time evolution of about 700 fm/c. The GDR gamma emission probability was obtained from the Fourier transform of the second time derivative of the dipole moment in the Goldhaber-Teller model, and the peak energy, strength, and full width at half maximum (FWHM) were extracted by Gaussian fitting to the gamma spectra.

## Key numbers

- For 27Al(p,γ)28Si at proton energy 50 MeV, the calculated GDR peak energy and FWHM match experimental data from Dowell (1983) with final-state energy 8.59 MeV.
- The peak energy of GDR in 27Al(p,γ)28Si centers at approximately 19 MeV and shows a decreasing trend with increasing proton energy.
- As proton incident energy increases, the peak energy decreasing rates are: 7.3% for 11B(p,γ)12C, 9.4% for 27Al(p,γ)28Si, 7.7% for 39K(p,γ)40Ca, and 0.5% for 67Co(p,γ)68Ni.
- The FWHM of GDR in 27Al(p,γ)28Si increases from approximately 8.5 MeV to 10 MeV with increasing incident energy.
- The temperature of the 67Co(p,γ)68Ni system remains below 1.5 MeV when proton incident energy increases from 20 MeV to 90 MeV.
- The GDR width remains almost unchanged for T < 1.5 MeV and increases sharply with T within 1.5 MeV < T < 3.5 MeV.
- For T > 4 MeV, the results support saturation of the FWHM at higher excitation.

## Context

This work extends the EQMD model, previously applied to GDR excited by direct boost (He 2014), to proton capture reactions as an alternative excitation method. The results are compared with experimental data from Dowell (1983) and with thermal shape fluctuation model calculations by Pandit (2012) for 120Sn. The study enables systematic investigation of GDR parameters across different mass regions and addresses the ongoing debate about whether the GDR width saturates at high temperature (T >= 4 MeV), supporting the saturation hypothesis against claims from pre-equilibrium gamma emission models.
