# Proton-Nucleus Collisions at the LHC: Scientific Opportunities and Requirements

> Proton-nucleus (p+A) collisions have long been recognized as a crucial component of the physics programme with nuclear beams at high energies, in particular for their reference role to interpret and understand nucleus-nucleus data as well as for their potential to elucidate the partonic structure of matter at low parton fractional momenta (small-x). Here, we summarize the main motivations that make a proton-nucleus run a decisive ingredient for a successful heavy-ion programme at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) and we present unique scientific opportunities arising from these collisions. We also review the status of ongoing discussions about operation plans for the p+A mode at the LHC.

## Key claim
Proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC are essential both as a baseline for interpreting nucleus-nucleus data and for studying partonic structure at low momentum fractions (small-x), but the feasibility of such runs depends on resolving accelerator challenges and the actual physics reach is conditional on achieving a minimum integrated luminosity of 0.1 pb⁻¹.

## Method
The paper reviews accelerator requirements for asymmetric beam collisions at the LHC, including injector chain modifications and beam dynamics for p+Pb and d+Pb modes. It surveys nuclear parton distribution function (nPDF) uncertainties using global fits (EPS09, HKN07, nDS) and evaluates benchmark hard processes (Drell-Yan, J/ψ, high-pT hadrons, heavy bosons) that could constrain nPDFs. The authors also assess experimental capabilities for small-x saturation studies, ultra-peripheral collisions, and cosmic-ray-related measurements.

## Key numbers
- Centre-of-mass energy per nucleon for p+Pb collisions: √sNN = 8.8 TeV (with proton beam at 7 TeV and Pb beam at 2.76 TeV per nucleon)
- Estimated peak luminosity for p+Pb: L = 1.5 × 10²⁹ cm⁻² s⁻¹
- Required integrated luminosity for feasibility of considered measurements: 0.1 pb⁻¹ (assuming 10⁶ s running time)
- Central rapidity shift for p+Pb: Δy = 0.46 (in direction of lighter ion)
- Centre-of-mass energy per nucleon for d+Pb collisions: √sNN = 6.22 TeV (with deuteron beam at 3.5 TeV per nucleon)
- Central rapidity shift for d+Pb: Δy = 0.12
- Pb bunch intensity assumed: 7 × 10⁷ ions per bunch
- Proton bunch intensity assumed: 1.15 × 10¹⁰ protons per bunch

## Context
This 2011 white paper extends earlier discussions of p+A at the LHC (Accardi et al., 2003; Bedjidian et al., 2003; Arleo et al., 2003) and builds on experience from RHIC deuteron-gold runs (2003, 2008) which provided decisive benchmarks for jet quenching and J/ψ suppression discoveries. The document enables planning for LHC p+Pb runs and complements future projects like the LHeC and EIC electron-nucleus colliders, while identifying open questions about injector upgrades for deuteron operation and the need for further beam dynamics studies.
