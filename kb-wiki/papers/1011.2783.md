# 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions serve a nearly perfect quark-gluon liquid

> The specific shear viscosity (eta/s)_QGP of a Quark-Gluon-Plasma (QGP) at temperatures T_c < T < 2T_c is extracted from the centrality dependence of the eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow measured in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Coupling viscous fluid dynamics for the QGP with a microscopic transport model for hadronic freeze-out we find that the eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow is a universal function of charged multiplicity per unit overlap area, (1/S)(dN_ch/dy), that depends only on the viscosity but not on the model used for computing the initial fireball eccentricity. Comparing with measurements we find 1 < (4pi)(eta/s)_QGP < 2.5 where the uncertainty range is dominated by model uncertainties for the eccentricity values used to normalize the measured elliptic flow.

## Key claim

The specific shear viscosity of the QGP at temperatures between T_c and 2T_c is constrained to the range 1 < 4π(η/s)_QGP < 2.5, where the uncertainty is dominated by model uncertainties for the initial fireball eccentricity, not by the dynamical evolution model.

## Method

The authors coupled the (2+1)-dimensional viscous fluid dynamics code VISH2+1 for the QGP phase with the microscopic hadronic transport model UrQMD for the hadronic freeze-out stage, switching at a temperature of 165 MeV. They computed the eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow v2/ε as a function of charged multiplicity per unit overlap area (1/S)(dN_ch/dy) for 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions, using Monte Carlo versions of the Glauber and fKLN models to generate initial entropy density distributions, and compared the resulting universal curves to STAR data for ⟨v2⟩ and v2{2}.

## Key numbers

- The specific shear viscosity of the QGP is constrained to 1 < 4π(η/s)_QGP < 2.5.
- The switching temperature from hydrodynamics to UrQMD is T_sw = 165 MeV.
- Parameter pairs (η/s, cτ0) that reproduce measured pion and proton pT-spectra include (0, 0.4 fm), (0.08, 0.6 fm), (0.16, 0.9 fm), and (0.24, 1.2 fm).
- The shear relaxation time τπ = 3η/(sT) is of order 0.2 fm/c.
- Early flow with τ0 = 0.4 fm/c for η/s = 0.08 increases v2/ε by about 5 percent.

## Context

This work extends earlier viscous hydrodynamic analyses by Romatschke (2007) and Song (2007) by using a hybrid model that eliminates the need for separate chemical and kinetic freeze-out parameters, which had been a major source of uncertainty in purely hydrodynamic approaches. The universal scaling of v2/ε with (1/S)(dN_ch/dy) enables a more robust extraction of the QGP shear viscosity, and the results confirm that the QGP behaves as a nearly perfect liquid with η/s close to the KSS bound of 1/(4π).
