# Evidence for rigid triaxial deformation in $^{76}$Ge from a model-independent analysis

> An extensive, model-independent analysis of the nature of triaxial deformation in $^{76}$Ge, a candidate for neutrinoless double-beta ($0νββ$) decay, was carried out following multi-step Coulomb excitation. Shape parameters deduced on the basis of a rotational-invariant sum-rule analysis provided considerable insight into the underlying collectivity of the ground-state and $γ$ bands. Both sequences were determined to be characterized by the same $β$ and $γ$ deformation parameter values. In addition, compelling evidence for low-spin, rigid triaxial deformation in $^{76}$Ge was obtained for the first time from the analysis of the statistical fluctuations of the quadrupole asymmetry deduced from the measured $E2$ matrix elements. These newly determined shape parameters are important input and constraints for calculations aimed at providing, with suitable accuracy, the nuclear matrix elements relevant to $0νββ$.

## Key claim

The paper provides evidence that the low-spin structure of $^{76}$Ge exhibits rigid triaxial deformation, with the ground-state and gamma bands characterized by the same beta and gamma deformation parameter values. This conclusion is drawn from a model-independent rotational-invariant sum-rule analysis of measured E2 matrix elements, specifically from the statistical fluctuations of the quadrupole asymmetry being close to zero, which indicates rigidity rather than softness in the gamma degree of freedom.

## Method

Multi-step Coulomb excitation of $^{76}$Ge beams on a $^{208}$Pb target was performed at three beam energies (291, 304, and 317 MeV) using the ATLAS accelerator at Argonne National Laboratory. De-excitation gamma rays were detected with the GRETINA tracking array in coincidence with scattered reaction products recorded by the CHICO2 position-sensitive detector array. The measured gamma-ray intensities were analyzed using the semi-classical coupled-channel Coulomb excitation code GOSIA to extract 103 E2 and M1 reduced matrix elements, which were then subjected to a rotational-invariant sum-rule analysis to determine deformation parameters and their statistical fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- The average quadrupole invariant value is approximately 0.30 e$^2$b$^2$, corresponding to an average quadrupole deformation of beta approximately 0.28.
- The average value of the quadrupole asymmetry invariant for the ground-state band is approximately 0.15, corresponding to a deformation parameter delta of approximately 27 degrees.
- The average value of the quadrupole asymmetry invariant for the gamma band is approximately 0.24, corresponding to a deformation parameter delta of approximately 25 degrees.
- The E2 matrix element for the $0_1^+ \rightarrow 2_1^+$ transition is 0.526(2) e b.
- The E2 matrix element for the $2_1^+ \rightarrow 4_1^+$ transition is 0.795(5) e b.

## Context

This work extends the earlier Coulomb excitation study of $^{76}$Ge by Toh et al. (2001), which had limited population of relevant states, and provides a more complete set of E2 matrix elements enabling model-independent characterization. The results confirm the interpretation of rigid triaxiality proposed by Toh et al. (2013) based on energy staggering patterns, and are consistent with calculations using the triaxial projected shell model (gamma approximately 30 degrees) and symmetry-conserving configuration mixing with the Gogny D1S interaction (gamma approximately 25 degrees). The findings directly impact calculations of nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay of $^{76}$Ge to $^{76}$Se, as theoretical approaches must now reproduce the parent nucleus as a rigid triaxial rotor while accounting for triaxiality in the daughter, which was recently studied by the same group (2019).
