# Interactions of two and three mesons including higher partial waves from lattice QCD

> We study two- and three-meson systems composed either of pions or kaons at maximal isospin using Monte Carlo simulations of lattice QCD. Utilizing the stochastic LapH method, we are able to determine hundreds of two- and three-particle energy levels, in nine different momentum frames, with high precision. We fit these levels using the relativistic finite-volume formalism based on a generic effective field theory in order to determine the parameters of the two- and three-particle K-matrices. We find that the statistical precision of our spectra is sufficient to probe not only the dominant $s$-wave interactions, but also those in $d$ waves. In particular, we determine for the first time a term in the three-particle K-matrix that contains two-particle $d$ waves. We use three $N_f=2+1$ CLS ensembles with pion masses of $200$, $280$, and $340\;$MeV. This allows us to study the chiral dependence of the scattering observables, and compare to the expectations of chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that lattice QCD data for two- and three-meson systems (pions or kaons at maximal isospin) have sufficient statistical precision to determine not only the dominant s-wave interactions but also d-wave contributions, including a first determination of a term in the three-particle K-matrix that contains two-particle d waves, using three ensembles with pion masses of 200, 280, and 340 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of lattice QCD on three \(N_f=2+1\) CLS ensembles with pion masses of 200, 280, and 340 MeV, using the stochastic LapH method to compute hundreds of two- and three-particle energy levels in nine different momentum frames. They fit these finite-volume spectra using the relativistic finite-volume formalism (RFT approach) based on a generic effective field theory, including both s-wave and d-wave interactions in the two-particle K-matrix and a threshold expansion for the three-particle K-matrix through quadratic order. The fits were performed globally to the combined two- and three-particle spectra, with the quantization conditions block-diagonalized by projecting onto irreducible representations of the little group for each total momentum.

## Key numbers

- Three ensembles used: N203 (\(M_\pi = 340\) MeV), N200 (\(M_\pi = 280\) MeV), D200 (\(M_\pi = 200\) MeV)
- Pion mass values: 200, 280, and 340 MeV
- For SU(2) ChPT validity, \(M_\pi^2/(4\pi F_\pi)^2\) ranges up to 0.075 on the ensembles
- The ratio \(M_\pi^2/M_K^2\) takes values {0.18, 0.38, 0.61} on D200, N200, and N203 ensembles respectively
- For SU(3) ChPT, \(M_K^2/(4\pi F_\pi)^2\) takes values {0.18, 0.15, 0.12} on the ensembles
- The range of validity for two-pion quantization condition is \(0 < E^* < 4 M_\pi\); for three pions it is \(M_\pi < E^* < 5 M_\pi\)
- For two kaons, the strict upper bound is \(2 M_K + M_\pi\); for three kaons, \(3 M_K + M_\pi\)
- Tree-level (LO) ChPT prediction for three-particle K-matrix: \(M^2 \cK_{\df,3}^{\iso,0} = 18 M^4/F^4 = 18 (16\pi M a_0)^2\) and \(M^2\cK_{\df,3}^{\iso,1} = 27 M^4/F^4 = 27 (16\pi M a_0)^2\)

## Context

This work extends the relativistic field theory (RFT) approach for three-particle quantization conditions, previously applied only with s waves in moving frames (Horz et al., 2020), to include d waves in moving frames for the first time. It builds on the formalism developed by Hansen and Sharpe (2014) and its extensions to d waves (Blanton et al., 2019) and moving frames (Horz et al., 2020). The results enable future lattice QCD studies of three-particle systems with higher partial waves, and allow comparison with chiral perturbation theory predictions for the chiral dependence of scattering observables, though the authors note that discretization effects remain uncontrolled and require multiple lattice spacings to demonstrate control.
