# Alpha Cluster Model of Atomic Nuclei

> Description of a nuclear system in its ground state and at low excitations based on the equation of state (EoS) around normal density is presented. In the expansion of the EoS around the saturation point additional spin polarization terms are taken into account. These terms, together with the standard symmetry term, are responsible for appearance of the $α$-like clusters in the ground state configurations of the N=Z even-even nuclei. At the nuclear surface these clusters can be identified as alpha particles. A correction for the surface effects is introduced for atomic nuclei. Taking into account an additional interaction between clusters the binding energies and sizes of the considered nuclei are very accurately described. The limits of the EoS parameters are established from the properties of the $α$, $^{3}$He and $t$ particles.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the ground state configurations of N=Z even-even nuclei contain alpha-like clusters that emerge naturally from an equation of state (EoS) expanded around saturation density, provided that additional spin polarization terms are included alongside the standard symmetry term; the authors hedge that this clustering description is valid only for the ground state and low excitations, and that the adopted parameter set is not unique, with other equally possible choices existing.

## Method

The authors model nuclear matter as a four-component fluid (protons and neutrons with spin up/down) using a 12-parameter cubic EoS expanded around saturation density, which includes terms for symmetric matter, isospin symmetry energy, and separate spin symmetry energies for neutrons and protons. They represent each nucleon by a Gaussian wave packet and minimize the average Hamiltonian (volume energy from the EoS, surface energy via a variance term, and Coulomb energy) to find ground state configurations. The parameters are constrained by fitting the binding energies and root-mean-square (rms) radii of the alpha particle, triton, helium-3, and deuteron, with the remaining degrees of freedom fixed by requiring consistency with heavier nuclei.

## Key numbers

* Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: \(\rho_0 = 0.185\) fm\(^{-3}\), which is higher than the commonly used range of 0.16–0.17 fm\(^{-3}\).
* Volume energy parameter: \(e_{00} = -16.79\) MeV.
* Compressibility of symmetric nuclear matter: \(K_0 = 270\) MeV, chosen within the experimental range of 250 ± 50 MeV.
* Surface tension parameter: \(\lambda = 0.4\) (dimensionless).
* Wigner (symmetry energy) constant: \(e_{I0} = 32\) MeV, within the range 32 ± 1 MeV.
* Slope of symmetry energy: \(L_I = 59.405\) MeV, within the range 59 ± 17 MeV.
* Curvature of symmetry energy: \(K_I = -1250\) MeV, consistent with negative experimental constraints within large error bars.
* Spin symmetry energy parameters for neutrons/protons: \(e_{ii0} = 82.37\) MeV, \(L_{ii} = 242.48\) MeV, \(K_{ii} = 19.909\) MeV.
* Mutual spin interaction parameters: \(e_{ij0} = -1.5\) MeV, \(L_{ij} = 182\) MeV, \(K_{ij} = 1210\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the alpha cluster model of Hafstad and Teller (1938) and the earlier approach of Sosin (2014) by incorporating a microscopic EoS with spin polarization terms and explicit cluster-cluster interactions. The model reproduces binding energies and rms radii of light charged particles (deuteron, triton, helium-3, alpha) exactly and describes the ground state properties of n-alpha nuclei up to Z=28, classifying configurations by the number of central alpha-like clusters. The approach enables future studies of exotic nuclei and neutron skin properties, though the authors note that more advanced parameter searches involving nuclei across the chart of nuclides are needed to refine the symmetry energy parameters.
