# Robust Nuclear Observables and Constraints on Random Interactions

> The predictions of the IBM two-body random ensemble are compared to empirical results on nuclei from Z=8 to 100. Heretofore unrecognized but robust empirical trends are identified and related both to the distribution of valence nucleon numbers and to the need for and applicability of specific, non-random interactions. Applications to expected trends in exotic nuclei are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that while global features of nuclear spectra (such as the prevalence of 0⁺ ground states and the overall E(4⁺₁) vs. E(2⁺₁) trajectory) can be reproduced by random interactions in the Interacting Boson Model, specific robust empirical features, particularly the absence of a peak at R₄/₂ = 2.0 and the presence of a peak at R₄/₂ ∼ 2.3 in nuclei with intermediate valence nucleon numbers, require constrained, non-random interactions.

## Method

The authors compared empirical R₄/₂ ≡ E(4⁺₁)/E(2⁺₁) values for nuclei from Z=8 to 100 with calculations from two IBM Hamiltonian ensembles: the full general Hamiltonian H_IBM (with two 1-body and seven 2-body interactions) and the focused ECQF Hamiltonian H_εκ = ε n_d − κ Q·Q. Random Gaussian interaction strengths were used, and the resulting R₄/₂ distributions were binned by boson number N_B (valence nucleon number N_val = 2N_B) and compared to empirical distributions from the same N_B ranges. Additional analysis examined the energies of gamma vibrational modes (E(2⁺₂)) in deformed rare earth nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Empirical R₄/₂ values show a peak at ∼2.3 for nuclei with 8–18 valence nucleons (N_B = 4–9), not at 2.0 as predicted by random ensembles.
- Random IBM calculations produce peaks at R₄/₂ ∼ 2.0 and ∼ 3.33, with no peak at 2.3 for any N_B range.
- For N_B = 7, R₄/₂ values of 2.2–2.4 require intermediate ε/κ values near 20–30.
- In deformed rare earth nuclei, empirical R₂/₂ ≡ E(2⁺₂)/E(2⁺₁) values show a broad bump centered at ∼15, while random H_IBM calculations with N_B = 16 show no peak above 2.
- Truncating the interaction space to ε/κ < 26 and using χ = −0.4 reproduces the empirical R₂/₂ peak at ∼15.

## Context

This work extends prior random-interaction studies in the Shell Model (Johnson et al., 1999) and IBM (Kusnezov et al., 1999) by providing the first explicit comparison of random-ensemble predictions with experimental data across the entire nuclear chart. It contradicts the assumption that random interactions alone can explain all robust nuclear features, showing that the empirical preference for R₄/₂ ∼ 2.3 reflects both the specific interactions and the locus of accessible nuclei. The results enable predictions of R₄/₂ distributions for exotic nuclei and suggest that measured R₄/₂ values can be used to infer shell structure or residual interactions in unmapped regions.
