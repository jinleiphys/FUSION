# Algebraic model of an oblate top

> We consider an algebraic treatment of a three-body system. In particular, we develop the formalism for a system of three identical objects and discuss an application to nonstrange baryon resonances which are interpreted as vibrational and rotational excitations of an oblate symmetric top. We derive closed expressions for a set of elementary form factors that appear in the calculation of both electromagnetic, strong and weak couplings of baryons.

## Key claim

The paper presents an algebraic treatment of a three-body system using a U(7) spectrum generating algebra, claiming that nonstrange baryon resonances can be interpreted as vibrational and rotational excitations of an oblate symmetric top. The hedge is that this interpretation holds specifically in the large N limit (infinitely large model space) and for one- and two-body interactions that commute with the operator F_2, with higher-order interactions potentially breaking the M_F symmetry that underpins the classification.

## Method

The authors develop a bosonic quantization method for the two relative Jacobi coordinates of a three-body system, introducing dipole bosons for each coordinate plus an auxiliary scalar boson under a total boson number conservation constraint, which generates a U(7) spectrum generating algebra. They construct a Hamiltonian invariant under the D_3 permutation group (isomorphic to S_3) containing at most two-body interactions, and analyze its intrinsic part in the large N limit to extract vibrational normal modes and rotational excitations. The wave functions are obtained by projection from intrinsic (coherent) states, and closed-form expressions for elementary form factors are derived using spherical Bessel functions in the large N limit.

## Key numbers

- The symmetric stretching mode frequency: epsilon_1 = 4 N xi_1 R^2
- The degenerate doublet (antisymmetric stretching and bending) frequency: epsilon_2 = 4 N xi_2 R^2/(1+R^2)
- The normalization factor X_D in the large N limit: X_D = N R sqrt(2)/(1+R^2)
- The r.m.s. deviation of the fit to nucleon and delta resonance spectra: 39 MeV
- The rotational energy formula: E_rot = kappa_1 L(L+1) - kappa_2 M_F^2, with M_F = |K +/- 2l|

## Context

This work extends the algebraic method previously applied to nuclear collective vibrations (Interacting Boson Model by Arima and Iachello) and molecular rovibrational states (vibron model) to the three-body problem with U(7) as the spectrum generating algebra. It builds on the earlier study by Bohm and Teese using SO(3,2) for a prolate symmetric top, and on the work of Bowler et al. in the harmonic oscillator quark model for baryon spectroscopy. The formalism enables calculation of electromagnetic, strong, and weak couplings of baryon resonances, with the elementary form factors serving as the backbone for such calculations in both molecular physics (X_3 molecules) and hadron physics (nonstrange qqq baryons).
