# Hadronic unquenching effects in the quark propagator

> We investigate hadronic unquenching effects in light quarks and mesons. Within the non-perturbative continuum framework of Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter equations we quantify the strength of the back reaction of the pion onto the quark-gluon interaction. To this end we add a Yang-Mills part of the interaction such that unquenched lattice results for various current quark masses are reproduced. We find considerable effects in the quark mass function at low momenta as well as for the chiral condensate. The quark wave function is less affected. The Gell--Mann-Oakes-Renner relation is valid to good accuracy up to pion masses of 400-500 MeV. As a byproduct of our investigation we verify the Coleman theorem, that chiral symmetry cannot be broken spontaneously when QCD is reduced to 1+1 dimensions.

## Key claim

The paper quantifies the back-reaction of pions onto the quark-gluon interaction within a Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter framework, finding that these hadronic unquenching effects are considerable in the quark mass function at low momenta and in the chiral condensate, while the quark wave function is less affected; the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation holds to good accuracy only up to pion masses of 400-500 MeV.

## Method

The authors constructed a truncation scheme for the Schwinger-Dyson equations of the gluon, ghost, and quark propagators combined with the Bethe-Salpeter equation for light mesons, adding a one-pion exchange diagram to the quark-gluon vertex alongside a Yang-Mills part modeled as an effective one-gluon exchange. They fixed the parameters of the Yang-Mills interaction to reproduce unquenched lattice results for the quark propagator at various current quark masses, then solved the coupled system of equations for the quark propagator and the pion Bethe-Salpeter vertex self-consistently.

## Key numbers

* Quark mass function: considerable effects found at low momenta, with the chiral condensate also significantly affected; the quark wave function is less affected.
* Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation: valid to good accuracy up to pion masses of 400-500 MeV.
* Pion decay constant: errors on the level of a few percent introduced by neglecting the H-pi dressing function in the Bethe-Salpeter vertex.
* In two dimensions, the pion back-reaction integral is infrared singular for any finite dressing function B(p^2), forcing the trivial solution B(p^2)=0 and supporting Coleman's theorem that continuous symmetries cannot be broken dynamically in 1+1 dimensions.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of unquenching effects in the Schwinger-Dyson framework, where quark loops in the gluon polarization were investigated by Fischer (2003, 2005), and complements the work of Watson and Cassing (2004) who solved a coupled system for the Bethe-Salpeter kernel and four-point function but neglected the back-reaction of hadronic resonances on the quark propagator. The approach also extends earlier NJL-model studies of pion effects by Dmitrasinovic (1995), Nikolov (1996), and Oertel (2000) to full QCD, and enables extrapolation of unquenched lattice results from large quark masses toward physical up/down quark values.
