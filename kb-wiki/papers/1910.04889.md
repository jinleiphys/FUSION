# The concept of induced surface and curvature tensions and a unified description of the gas of hard discs and hard spheres

> Mathematically rigorous derivation of the hadron matter equation of state within the induced surface and curvature tensions approach is worked out. Such an equation of state allows one to go beyond the Van der Waals approximation for the interaction potential of hard spheres. The compressibility of a single- and two-component hadron mixtures are found for two- and three-dimensional cases. The obtained results are compared to the well known one- and two-component equations of state of hard spheres and hard discs. The values of the model parameters which successfully reproduce the above-mentioned equations of state on different intervals of packing fractions are determined from fitting their compressibility factors. It is argued that after some modification the developed approach can be also used to describe the mixtures of gases of convex hard particles of different sizes and shapes.

## Key claim

The paper provides a mathematically rigorous derivation of the induced surface tension (IST) equation of state for hadron matter, extending it to include curvature tension (ISCT), and demonstrates that this approach can accurately describe the compressibility of hard-sphere and hard-disc gases beyond the Van der Waals approximation, but only when auxiliary parameters are introduced and fitted to known equations of state on specific intervals of packing fractions.

## Method

The authors start from the excluded volume of a mixture of Boltzmann particles with different hard-core radii, expressed using the Isihara-Hadwiger formula, and derive the grand canonical ensemble partition via a Laplace transform to the isobaric ensemble. They then obtain a system of self-consistent equations for pressure and the induced surface tension coefficient, and generalize it by introducing auxiliary parameters α_k and β_k (greater than unity) to account for higher virial coefficients and to ensure that the surface and curvature tensions vanish at high densities. The model parameters are determined by fitting the compressibility factor to the Carnahan-Starling equation of state for hard spheres (D=3) and the Barrio-Solana equation of state for hard discs (D=2) over various intervals of packing fraction.

## Key numbers

* For a one-component gas of hard spheres (nucleons with mass 938.9 MeV, degeneracy factor 4, temperature T=100 MeV), the IST equation of state with two parameters reproduces the Carnahan-Starling compressibility factor with a mean deviation of about 2 percent up to a packing fraction η ≃ 0.28–0.30.
* The Carnahan-Starling compressibility factor for hard spheres is Z_CS = (1+η+η^2−η^3)/(1−η)^3, where η = ρ V_1 is the packing fraction.
* The Van der Waals equation of state for hard spheres diverges at η = 0.25, while the IST approach extends the valid range to η ≃ 0.28–0.30.
* For hard discs, the IST equation of state is compared to the Barrio-Solana equation of state on intervals η ∈ [0; 0.2], [0; 0.3], and [0; 0.4].

## Context

This work extends the induced surface tension concept previously developed by Bugaev et al. (IST1, IST2, IST3) for hadronic and nuclear matter, which had reproduced the Carnahan-Starling equation of state only up to η ≃ 0.2 with a single parameter. The new derivation provides a mathematically rigorous foundation and introduces curvature tension, enabling accurate description of both hard-sphere and hard-disc gases up to higher packing fractions. The approach is intended to enable modeling of multicomponent systems with many particle sorts and shapes in arbitrary dimensions, with potential applications to dense hadronic matter and nuclear clusters.
