# Manifestation of quark effects in nuclei via bremsstrahlung analysis in the proton-nucleus scattering

> \textbf{Background} (1) The incoherent emission of photons is dominant comparing to coherent one in proton-nucleus scattering. The incoherent bremsstrahlung is very sensitive to the magnetic moments of nucleons in nuclei. (2) According to the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, the nucleon magnetic moments in nuclei are enhanced relative to those in vacuum, originating from the quark structure of nucleons. \textbf{Purpose} Investigate possibilities of observing quark effects in nuclei by the analysis of bremsstrahlung in nuclear reactions. \textbf{Methods} Analyse the bremsstrahlung cross sections with established model in proton-nucleus scattering, by extending with inclusion of in-medium modified nucleon magnetic moments in nuclei by the QMC model. \textbf{Results} (1) After calibrating the model without the quark effects for experimental data (TAPS Collaboration data for $p + \isotope[197]{Au}$), we calculate the cross sections and observe the slight difference between the spectra for models with and without quark effects. Such result is found for the first time, confirming possibilities of observing the quark effects in the spectra of bremsstrahlung. (2) As found, quark effects are not enough to be observed in middle and heavy nuclei, as they have dominant incoherent contributions. (3) \isotope[18]{C} has minimal incoherent contribution concerning other carbon isotopes, where the quark effects should be minimal. In ratios between the spectra for \isotope[18]{C} and \isotope[12]{C} with and without the quark effects the difference is clearly observed. \textbf{Conclusions} We establish the new physical observable for the quark effects in nuclei in the bremsstrahlung accompanied in the nuclear reactions, which can be measured. The present suggestion is for the first time in both theoretically and experimentally to study the quark effects in nuclei via the bremsstrahlung.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates for the first time that quark effects in nuclei, specifically the in-medium enhancement of nucleon magnetic moments predicted by the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, can be observed through bremsstrahlung photon spectra in proton-nucleus scattering, but only in carefully chosen light nuclei where the coherent and incoherent bremsstrahlung contributions are comparable.

## Method

The authors extended an established bremsstrahlung model for proton-nucleus scattering by incorporating in-medium modified nucleon magnetic moments calculated from the QMC model. They calibrated the model without quark effects against experimental TAPS Collaboration data for p + 197Au at proton beam energy Ep = 190 MeV, then calculated differential cross sections with and without the enhanced magnetic moments for nuclei ranging from 197Au down to carbon isotopes, analyzing the ratios of spectra to isolate the quark signal.

## Key numbers

- For 197Au at Ep = 190 MeV, the in-medium proton magnetic moment ratio is μp*/μp(0) = 1.078154 and neutron ratio is μn*/μn(0) = 1.077861, using average nucleon density ρ = 0.78286 ρ0 with ρ0 = 0.15 fm−3
- For 40Ca, the in-medium proton magnetic moment ratio is μp*/μp(0) = 0.1071 and neutron ratio is μn*/μn(0) = 1.066, using average nucleon density ρ = 0.71148 ρ0
- For 16O, the in-medium proton magnetic moment ratio is μp*/μp(0) = 1.06286 and neutron ratio is μn*/μn(0) = 1.05750, using average nucleon density ρ = 0.61242 ρ0
- For 12C, the in-medium proton magnetic moment ratio is μp*/μp(0) = 1.055091 and neutron ratio is μn*/μn(0) = 1.050417, using average nucleon density ρ = 0.53277 ρ0
- For 18C, the in-medium proton magnetic moment ratio is μp*/μp(0) = 1.054102 and neutron ratio is μn*/μn(0) = 1.049399, using average nucleon density ρ = 0.52137 ρ0
- A calibration constant c = 1.296296296 was required to reproduce experimental data for 197Au

## Context

This work extends the established bremsstrahlung formalism of Maydanyuk and Zhang (2015) and Maydanyuk (2023), which demonstrated that incoherent bremsstrahlung dominates over coherent emission by factors of 10^6–10^7 for heavy nuclei. The QMC model predictions for enhanced nucleon magnetic moments in nuclei, developed by Guichon (1987), Saito (2005), and others, are here applied to nuclear bremsstrahlung for the first time. The key finding is that the ratio of normalized cross sections between 12C and 18C provides a clean observable for quark effects, independent of the calibration constant, and this can be tested experimentally.
