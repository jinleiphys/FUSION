# Meson-exchange currents in quasielastic electron scattering in a generalized superscaling approach

> We present a model that incorporates the effect of two-body currents in quasielastic electron-nucleus scattering within the framework of a consistent superscaling formalism. This is achieved by defining an averaged single-nucleon hadronic tensor based on the 1p1h matrix element of the one-body current plus meson-exchange currents (MEC). The consistent treatment of one- and two-body currents in our model enables the calculation of exchange current effects in the kinematical region where the Fermi gas response is zero, but not the scaling function. The effect of MEC is consistently taken into account when extracting the phenomenological scaling function from electron scattering data. With this model, we investigate the effect of MEC on the response functions taking into account the effective mass of the nucleon, and examine the consequences it has on the inclusive $(e,e')$ cross section. We find that 1p1h MEC deplete the quasielastic transverse response, while they not alter significantly the scaling behavior of (e,e') data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that incorporating meson-exchange currents (MEC) into the 1p1h response within a generalized superscaling formalism depletes the quasielastic transverse response, while not significantly altering the scaling behavior of (e,e') data, provided the MEC are consistently included in the single-nucleon prefactor used to define the scaling function.

## Method

The authors define an effective single-nucleon hadronic tensor that sums the one-body current and the 1p1h matrix element of two-body meson-exchange currents (seagull, pionic, and Delta isobar currents) within a relativistic mean field (RMF) model of nuclear matter with an effective nucleon mass. They compute averaged single-nucleon responses by integrating over hole momenta with a smeared Fermi distribution (Fermi function with smearing parameter b = 50 MeV/c) to extend the averaging to the region |psi*| > 1, and then use these averaged responses to define a new prefactor for constructing a phenomenological scaling function from inclusive (e,e') data after subtracting a calculated 2p2h contribution.

## Key numbers

*   The effective mass used for carbon-12 is M* = 0.8, corresponding to m_N* = 0.8 * m_N.
*   The vector energy used for carbon-12 is E_v = 141 MeV.
*   The smearing parameter for the Fermi distribution used to extend the averaging procedure is b = 50 MeV/c.
*   The scaling function of nuclear matter in the RMF is given by f*(psi*) = (3/4)(1 - psi*^2) theta(1 - psi*^2).

## Context

This work extends the SuSAM* (superscaling with relativistic effective mass) model of Mariano et al. (2017) and Amaro et al. (2018) by consistently incorporating 1p1h meson-exchange currents, which had previously been omitted due to their violation of scaling properties in the Fermi gas model of Amaro et al. (2002). It enables a refined scaling analysis of inclusive electron scattering data that accounts for two-body current effects, providing a more accurate foundation for extrapolating to neutrino-nucleus cross sections needed for oscillation experiments.
