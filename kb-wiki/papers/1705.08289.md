# Regeneration of $K^0_{S}$ mesons

## Key claim
Previous calculations of $K^0_{S}$ regeneration used non-coupled equations of motion instead of the correct coupled equations, leading to results that differ radically from the exact solution. The author presents calculations based on both the exact solution of coupled equations and perturbation theory, showing they agree only in the limit $\Delta \Gamma t \gg 1$.

## Method
The author solves the coupled equations of motion for $K^0$ and $\bar{K}^0$ in a medium, including the off-diagonal mass term $\epsilon = (m_L - m_S)/2$ that was omitted in prior work. The exact solution for the probability $|K_S(t)|^2$ is derived and then compared with a perturbative calculation of the process $K^0_L \rightarrow K^0_S \rightarrow \pi\pi$ using diagram techniques. The connection between the two approaches is established by rewriting the perturbative width to show it contains the $K_L K_S$ transition width multiplied by the branching ratio for $K^0_S \rightarrow \pi\pi$.

## Key numbers
- $\epsilon = (m_L - m_S)/2 = \Delta m/2$ is the small off-diagonal mass parameter coupling $K^0$ and $\bar{K}^0$.
- For $\Delta \Gamma t \gg 1$, the exact probability $|K_S(t)|^2 \approx \frac{1}{4} e^{-\Gamma(K_L \rightarrow K_S)t} e^{-(\Gamma^a_{K^0} + \Gamma^d)t}$, where $\Gamma(K_L \rightarrow K_S) = \frac{\epsilon^2}{|V|^2} \Delta \Gamma$.
- The perturbative width for $K^0_L \rightarrow K^0_S \rightarrow \pi\pi$ is $\Gamma(K^0_L \rightarrow K^0_S \rightarrow \pi\pi) = \frac{\epsilon^2}{|V|^2} \Gamma_d(K^0_S \rightarrow \pi\pi)$.

## Context
This work corrects a fundamental defect in prior regeneration calculations by Good (1957) and others (references 1-7 in the paper), who used non-coupled equations of motion that omitted the off-diagonal mass term $\epsilon$. The approach extends the methodology used for $n\bar{n}$ transitions in matter (references 10-15) to the kaon system. The exact solution enables proper treatment of regeneration for any value of $\Delta \Gamma t$, while the perturbative approach provides a verification in the limit $\Delta \Gamma t \gg 1$. The author indicates a following paper will continue the analysis.
