# Masses of positive- and negative-parity hadron ground-states, including those with heavy quarks

> A symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector$\times$vector contact interaction is used to compute spectra of ground-state $J^P = 0^\pm, 1^\pm$ $(f\bar g)$ mesons, their partner diquark correlations, and $J^P=1/2^\pm, 3/2^\pm$ $(fgh)$ baryons, where $f,g,h \in \{u,d,s,c,b\}$. Results for the leptonic decay constants of all mesons are also obtained, including scalar and pseudovector states involving heavy quarks. The spectrum of baryons produced by this chiefly algebraic approach reproduces the 64 masses known empirically or computed using lattice-regularised quantum chromodynamics with an accuracy of 1.4(1.2)%. It also has the richness of states typical of constituent-quark models and predicts many baryon states that have not yet been observed. The study indicates that dynamical, nonpointlike diquark correlations play an important role in all baryons; and, typically, the lightest allowed diquark is the most important component of a baryon's Faddeev amplitude.

## Key claim

A symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector×vector contact interaction (CI) can reproduce the 64 known masses of ground-state baryons (including parity partners) with an accuracy of 1.4(1.2)%, but this agreement relies on introducing eight free parameters in the baryon sector (four to correct for omitted meson-cloud contributions) and separate spin-orbit correction factors for mesons and diquarks.

## Method

The authors used a symmetry-preserving contact interaction (CI) to solve Bethe-Salpeter equations for mesons and diquark correlations, and quark+diquark Faddeev equations for baryons, all within a rainbow-ladder truncation scheme. For scalar and pseudovector meson channels, multiplicative spin-orbit correction factors (g_SO^0+ = 0.32, g_SO^1+ = 0.25) were introduced to compensate for the CI's failure to generate sufficient spin-orbit repulsion. Diquark Bethe-Salpeter equations were obtained from meson equations by multiplying the kernel by 1/2, with an additional factor s_SO = 1.8 to weaken spin-orbit repulsion in diquarks relative to mesons.

## Key numbers

- Baryon spectrum accuracy: 1.4(1.2)% for 64 masses known empirically or from lattice QCD
- Meson mass mean relative-difference between CI predictions and experiment/lQCD: 5(6)% (excluding fitted entries)
- Meson leptonic decay constant mean-absolute-relative-difference: 15(11)%
- Parity partner splitting overall: 0.35(10) GeV
- Spin-orbit correction factors: g_SO^0+ = 0.32, g_SO^1+ = 0.25 for mesons; s_SO = 1.8 for diquarks
- b-c spectrum mass difference from equal spacing rules: d_M^bc = 3.18(4) GeV
- Scalar diquark masses: [ud]_0+ = 0.78 GeV, [us]_0+ = 0.93 GeV, [uc]_0+ = 2.15 GeV, [sc]_0+ = 2.26 GeV, [ub]_0+ = 5.51 GeV, [sb]_0+ = 5.60 GeV, [cb]_0+ = 6.48 GeV
- Pseudovector diquark masses: {uu}_1+ = 1.06 GeV, {us}_1+ = 1.16 GeV, {ss}_1+ = 1.26 GeV, {uc}_1+ = 2.24 GeV, {sc}_1+ = 2.34 GeV, {cc}_1+ = 3.30 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous CI studies of pseudoscalar and vector mesons and positive-parity baryons by Yin et al. (2019) and Gutierrez-Guerrero et al. (2019) to include scalar and pseudovector mesons and negative-parity baryons across all five quark flavours (u,d,s,c,b). It complements rainbow-ladder truncation calculations of low-lying SU(N_f=5) multiplets by Qin et al. (2018, 2019) and provides a largely algebraic alternative to lattice QCD spectrum calculations (Durr et al., 2008; Edwards et al., 2011; Bali et al., 2017). The results enable systematic predictions for unobserved baryon states and clarify the role of dynamical diquark correlations in baryon structure, while also revealing the weakening of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking with increasing current-quark mass.
