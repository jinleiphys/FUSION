# Chiral symmetry in matter

> Soft-pion theorems are used to show how chiral symmetry constrains the contributions of low-momentum pions to the quark condensate, the pion decay constant and hadron masses, all of which have been proposed as signals of partial restoration of chiral symmetry in matter. These have contributions of order T^2 for a pion gas or of order m_pi for cold nuclear matter, which have different coefficients in all three cases, showing that there are no simple relations between the changes to these quantities in matter. In particular, such contributions are absent from the masses of vector mesons and nucleons and so these masses cannot scale as any simple function of the quark condensate. More generally, pieces of the quark condensate that arise from low-momentum pions should not be associated with partial restoration of chiral symmetry.

# Chiral symmetry in matter

## Key claim

Low-momentum pions contribute to the quark condensate at order T² in a pion gas or order m_π in cold nuclear matter, but these contributions are absent from hadron masses; therefore hadron masses cannot scale as any simple function of the quark condensate, and changes in the condensate from low-momentum pions should not be interpreted as signals of partial chiral symmetry restoration.

## Method

Soft-pion theorems derived from the PCAC pion field are used to compute the scalar density of quarks in pions and nucleons, and to constrain the isoscalar pion-hadron scattering amplitude to be of order q² or m_π² in the chiral limit. For a warm pion gas, the Bose-Einstein distribution of pions is averaged to obtain the scalar density of pions ⟨½φ²⟩_T = T²/8, and this is combined with the pion matrix element of the scalar density to compute the change in the quark condensate to order T². For cold nuclear matter, chiral perturbation theory is used to identify the nonanalytic term of order m_π in the scalar density of pions in a nucleon, and Weinberg's power counting is applied to show the absence of a corresponding term of order m_π in the hadron-nucleon scattering amplitude.

## Key numbers

- Quark condensate in vacuum: ⟨ψ̅ψ⟩ ∼ −3 fm⁻³, from the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation with average quark mass m̅ ≃ 7 MeV.
- Pion-pion sigma commutator: σ_ππ = m_π/2 = 70 MeV, corresponding to an integrated scalar density of quarks in a pion of σ_ππ/m̅ ∼ 10.
- Pion-nucleon sigma commutator: σ_πN = 45 ± 7 MeV, corresponding to an integrated scalar density of quarks in a nucleon of ∼ 6.
- Change in quark condensate in a pion gas to order T²: ⟨ψ̅ψ⟩ ≃ ⟨0|ψ̅ψ|0⟩ (1 − T²/(8 f_π²)).
- Pion decay constant in a pion gas to order T²: f_π(T) = f_π (1 − T²/(12 f_π²)).
- Estimated ∼ 30% reduction in the quark condensate in nuclear matter at density ρ = 0.17 fm⁻³, from the additive estimate using the nucleon scalar density ρ_s.
- Nonanalytic term in the scalar density of pions in a nucleon: −(9/(16π)) (g_πNN/(2 M_N))² m_π + ⋯.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing study of chiral symmetry in a pion gas (references 2–12 in the paper) and applies the same lessons to cold nuclear matter (references 13–16). It contradicts the simple scaling picture suggested by mean-field approaches such as the linear sigma model and Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model, where hadron masses decrease in proportion to the quark condensate. The results show that pionic fluctuations must be included beyond the mean-field approximation, and that QCD sum rule analyses of hadrons in matter must carefully treat the cancellation of order-T² terms between the operator product expansion and the spectral representation, as shown by Eletsky and Ioffe (1990s) and others. The paper enables a more careful interpretation of experimental signals from relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and poses the open question of finding an order parameter with a more direct relation to hadron masses than the quark condensate.
