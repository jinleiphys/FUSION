# Effects of neutron-rich nuclei masses on symmetry energy

> We explore the impact of neutron-rich nuclei masses on the symmetry energy properties using the mass table evaluated by the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) model. First, using the semi-empirical mass formula with the DRHBc mass table, we investigate the symmetry energy at saturation density $ρ_0$, denoted as $S_0$, and the ratio of surface to volume contributions to the symmetry energy, $κ$. As a result, we obtain $S_0=27.85\,{\rm MeV}$ ($κ=1.38$) for $a_{\rm sym}(A) =S_0 (1 - κA^{-1/3})$ (Type I) and $S_0=32.66\,{\rm MeV}$ ($κ=3.15$) for $a_{\rm sym}(A) = S_0 (1 + κA^{-1/3} )^{-1}$ (Type II), which are lower than those obtained using the AME2020 mass table, $S_0=28.54\,{\rm MeV}$ ($κ=1.29$) for Type I and $S_0=33.81\,{\rm MeV}$ ($κ=3.04$) for Type II. Second, we further investigate the effect of these changes in $a_{\rm sym}(A)$ on the density-dependent symmetry energy by employing the empirical model of $S(ρ) = C_k(ρ/ρ_0)^{2/3} + C_1(ρ/ρ_0) + C_2(ρ/ρ_0)^γ$ and universal relation of $a_{\rm sym}(A=208) = S(ρ=0.1\,{\rm fm}^{-3})$. Compared to the experimental constraints, we find that $S_0$ and slope parameter $L$, determined by the DRHBc mass table with Type II, are more suitable to explain the constraints by heavy ion collisions and isobaric analog states than AME2020. We also discuss the neutron skin thickness derived from the $L$, comparing it with experimental measurements.

## Key claim

Using the DRHBc mass table (which includes more neutron-rich nuclei than the AME2020 experimental compilation) reduces the extracted symmetry energy at saturation density \(S_0\) compared to AME2020, and the Type II functional form for \(a_{\rm sym}(A)\) yields \(S_0\) and slope parameter \(L\) values that are more consistent with constraints from heavy-ion collisions and isobaric analog states, but only when the uncertainty in saturation density \(\rho_0\) is taken into account.

## Method

The authors extracted the symmetry energy coefficient of finite nuclei \(a_{\rm sym}(A)\) from binding energies in the DRHBc and AME2020 mass tables, after removing shell corrections (from KTUY05) and Wigner corrections. They fitted \(a_{\rm sym}(A)\) with two phenomenological forms: Type I (\(a_{\rm sym}=S_0(1-\kappa A^{-1/3})\)) and Type II (\(a_{\rm sym}=S_0(1+\kappa A^{-1/3})^{-1}\)). Using the universal relation \(a_{\rm sym}(A=208) = S(\rho=0.1\ {\rm fm}^{-3})\) and an empirical density-dependent symmetry energy model \(S(\rho) = C_k(\rho/\rho_0)^{2/3} + C_1(\rho/\rho_0) + C_2(\rho/\rho_0)^\gamma\) with fixed \(C_k=17.47\ {\rm MeV}\) and \(\gamma=1.52\), they determined \(C_1\), \(C_2\), \(S_0\), and the slope parameter \(L\) for each mass table and fitting form.

## Key numbers

- \(S_0 = 27.85\ {\rm MeV}\) (\(\kappa=1.38\)) for DRHBc Type I; \(S_0 = 32.66\ {\rm MeV}\) (\(\kappa=3.15\)) for DRHBc Type II.
- \(S_0 = 28.54\ {\rm MeV}\) (\(\kappa=1.29\)) for AME2020 Type I; \(S_0 = 33.81\ {\rm MeV}\) (\(\kappa=3.04\)) for AME2020 Type II.
- \(L = 52.5^{+13.8}_{-10.6}\ {\rm MeV}\) for DRHBc Type I; \(L = 106.7^{+21.1}_{-15.5}\ {\rm MeV}\) for DRHBc Type II (uncertainties from \(\rho_0 = 0.15 \pm 0.01\ {\rm fm}^{-3}\)).
- \(L = 48.4^{+13.6}_{-10.5}\ {\rm MeV}\) for AME2020 Type I; \(L = 107.2^{+21.6}_{-15.8}\ {\rm MeV}\) for AME2020 Type II.
- Neutron skin thickness \(\Delta R_{\rm np} = 0.178^{+0.019}_{-0.016}\ {\rm fm}\) (DRHBc Type I) and \(\Delta R_{\rm np} = 0.258^{+0.031}_{-0.023}\ {\rm fm}\) (DRHBc Type II), compared to PREX-2 measurement \(0.283 \pm 0.071\ {\rm fm}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of symmetry energy parameters by using the DRHBc mass table (DRHBcMassTable, 2022), which covers neutron-rich nuclei up to the neutron drip line, in contrast to the experimental AME2020 compilation (Wang, 2021). The results are compared to constraints from heavy-ion collisions (Tsang, 2008), isobaric analog states (Danielewicz, 2008), the finite-range droplet model (Moller, 2012), and neutron star observations (Steiner, 2011). The findings enable tighter constraints on the saturation density \(\rho_0\) and show that the Type II form with DRHBc masses is consistent with the PREX-2 neutron skin measurement and previous microscopic DRHBc calculations (Kim, 2021).
