# Time evolution formalism in the complex scaling method: Application to the E1 response of $^6$He

> Background: The complex scaling method (CSM) has been successfully used to describe many-body resonances as eigenvalues of the complex-scaled Hamiltonian in an appropriate $L^2$ basis representation. Its scope has subsequently been extended to many-body continuum states, strength functions, and scattering observables. However, a general framework that incorporates time evolution within the same CSM framework has not yet been established. Purpose: We formulate a time-evolution formalism as a natural extension of the CSM based on the extended completeness relation (ECR), and apply it to the electric dipole (E1) excitation of $^6$He in order to clarify how an initially correlated three-body configuration evolves into continuum states. Methods: Time evolution is described by a complex-scaled time-evolution operator represented with the ECR. The formalism is first tested in a simple two-body model through comparison with a direct numerical solution of the time-dependent Schrödinger equation. It is then applied to the E1 excitation of $^6$He in an $α+ n + n$ three-body model, and the density distributions are analyzed in different Jacobi coordinate systems. Results: The present formalism reproduces the wave-packet evolution obtained in the direct time-dependent calculation. In the application to $^6$He, the initial E1-excited state exhibits a correlated configuration and evolves into spatially extended continuum states. The time evolution of the density distributions indicates the coexistence of sequential decay through a core-neutron subsystem and direct breakup. Conclusions: The present formalism extends the scope of the CSM from spectral and scattering observables to real-time continuum dynamics, and provides a unified framework that connects initial-state correlations, continuum structure, and decay dynamics in weakly bound nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a time-evolution operator within the complex scaling method (CSM) using the extended completeness relation (ECR), enabling real-time continuum dynamics for the first time in this framework; the formalism is validated by reproducing a direct time-dependent Schrödinger equation solution in a two-body test case, but the three-body application to $^6$He is purely demonstrative and the density distributions are analyzed qualitatively rather than compared to experimental data.

## Method

The authors construct a spectral representation of the complex-scaled time-evolution operator by inserting the extended completeness relation (ECR) into $e^{-i\hat{H}^\theta t/\hbar}$, expanding it over the biorthogonal eigenstates of the complex-scaled Hamiltonian. They test the formalism on a two-body model with a Gaussian potential and a $p$-wave resonance at $E_r=0.377$ MeV, comparing the wave-packet evolution to a direct Crank-Nicolson solution of the time-dependent Schrödinger equation. They then apply the method to the E1 excitation of $^6$He described in an $\alpha+n+n$ three-body model using the orthogonality condition model (OCM) with the KKNN potential for $n$-$\alpha$ interactions and the Minnesota force for $n$-$n$ interactions, solving the complex-scaled Schrödinger equation via the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) with 25 basis functions per coordinate and partial waves up to $l_1,l_2 \le 3$.

## Key numbers

- Resonance energy $E_r = 0.377$ MeV and decay width $\Gamma = 0.342$ MeV for the $p$-wave resonance in the two-body test model with Gaussian potential parameters $V_0 = -25$ MeV and $\beta = 1/9$ fm$^{-2}$
- 60 Gaussian basis functions used in the two-body test, with range parameters $b_n$ in geometric progression from 0.01 fm to 60 fm
- Complex scaling angle $\theta = 10$ degrees used for the two-body test calculation
- Time evolution examined at $ct = 20$, $40$, and $80$ fm in the two-body test, and at $ct = 50$, $150$, and $300$ fm for the $^6$He three-body calculation
- 25 Gaussian basis functions employed for each coordinate in the $^6$He three-body calculation, with range parameters $b_n$ in geometric progression from 0.3 fm to 40 fm
- Complex scaling angle $\theta = 10$ degrees used for the $^6$He three-body calculation

## Context

This work extends the CSM framework, previously applied to resonance spectroscopy (Aguilar 1971, Balslev 1971), continuum responses via complex-scaled Green's functions (Myo 2001, Myo 2009), and scattering observables via the complex-scaled Lippmann-Schwinger equation (Kikuchi 2009, Kikuchi 2010), into the time domain for the first time. The formalism enables a unified description connecting initial-state correlations, continuum structure, and decay dynamics in weakly bound nuclei, and the $^6$He application builds on prior three-body OCM calculations (Kikuchi 2010) that reproduced ground-state properties. The time-dependent analysis of decay modes in few-body systems has been previously addressed for two-proton emission (Oishi 2014), but the present work provides a CSM-based alternative that treats bound, resonant, and continuum states on equal footing through the ECR.
