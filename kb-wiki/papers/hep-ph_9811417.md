# Minijet transverse energy production in the next-to-leading order in hadron and nuclear collisions

> The transverse energy flow generated by minijets in hadron and nuclear collisions into a given rapidity window in the central region is calculated in the next-to-leading (NLO) order in QCD at RHIC and LHC energies. The NLO transverse energy production in pp collisions cross sections are larger than the LO ones by the factors of K_{RHIC} ~ 1.9 and K_{LHC} ~ 2.1 at RHIC and LHC energies correspondingly. These results were then used to calculate transverse energy spectrum in nuclear collisions in a Glauber geometrical model. We show that accounting for NLO corrections in the elementary pp collisions leads to a substantial broadening of the E_{perp} distribution for the nuclear ones, while its form remains practically unchanged.

## Key claim

The next-to-leading order (NLO) QCD corrections to minijet transverse energy production in pp collisions are substantial, with K-factors of about 1.9 at RHIC and 2.1 at LHC energies, but the authors note that these corrections are computed within a leading-twist approximation that may conflict with unitarity at low transverse energies, especially at LHC energies for large rapidity windows.

## Method

The authors used the Monte Carlo code of Kunzst and Soper (1992) to compute the NLO cross section for transverse energy production into a central rapidity window (-0.5 ≤ y ≤ 0.5) in pp collisions, using MRSG(-) parton distribution functions. They then embedded these NLO pp cross sections into a Glauber geometrical model of nuclear collisions, approximating the transverse energy distribution at fixed impact parameter as a Gaussian and integrating over impact parameter to obtain the nuclear cross section.

## Key numbers

- NLO K-factor for pp collisions at RHIC energy (√s = 200 GeV): K_RHIC ~ 1.9
- NLO K-factor for pp collisions at LHC energy (√s = 5500 GeV): K_LHC ~ 2.1
- Integrated minijet production cross section σ(E₀) for cutoff E₀ = 4 GeV at RHIC (√s = 200 GeV): LO = 0.31 mb, NLO = 0.55 mb
- Integrated minijet production cross section σ(E₀) for cutoff E₀ = 4 GeV at LHC (√s = 5500 GeV): LO = 15.3 mb, NLO = 26.9 mb
- Limiting cutoff energy E₀* for unit central rapidity window at RHIC: LO = 1.1 GeV, NLO = 1.3 GeV
- Limiting cutoff energy E₀* for unit central rapidity window at LHC: LO = 2.5 GeV, NLO = 3.0 GeV
- Power-law exponent α for transverse energy spectrum at RHIC: LO = 4.91, NLO = 4.92
- Power-law exponent α for transverse energy spectrum at LHC: LO = 4.14, NLO = 4.24

## Context

This work extends the leading-order (LO) calculation of minijet transverse energy spectra in heavy ion collisions by Eskola, Kajantie, and collaborators (1994) to NLO in QCD, using the NLO jet code of Kunzst and Soper (1992) previously applied to Tevatron energies. The results confirm the "expected" K-factors used in earlier minijet reviews by Eskola (1997) and X.-N. Wang (1997), and show that NLO corrections broaden the transverse energy distribution in nuclear collisions, providing more favorable conditions for collective effects such as quark-gluon plasma formation. The authors identify the need for resummation of higher-order corrections and inclusion of nonlinear (eikonal unitarization) effects as the next necessary steps, referencing work by Jalilian-Marian, Kovchegov, McLerran, and others on quasiclassical and nonlinear approaches.
