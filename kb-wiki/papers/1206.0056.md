# The unrestricted Skyrme-tensor time-dependent Hartree-Fock and its application to the nuclear response from spherical to triaxial nuclei

> The nuclear time-dependent Hartree-Fock model formulated in the three-dimensional space,based on the full Skyrme energy density functional and complemented with the tensor force,is presented for the first time. Full self-consistency is achieved by the model. The application to the isovector giant dipole resonance is discussed in the linear limit, ranging from spherical nuclei (16O, 120Sn) to systems displaying axial or triaxial deformation (24Mg, 28Si, 178Os, 190W, 238U). Particular attention is paid to the spin-dependent terms from the central sector of the functional, recently included together with the tensor. They turn out to be capable of producing a qualitative change on the strength distribution in this channel. The effect on the deformation properties is also discussed. The quantitative effects on the linear response are small and, overall, the giant dipole energy remains unaffected. Calculations are compared to predictions from the (quasi)-particle random phase approximation and experimental data where available, finding good agreement.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first full implementation of the Skyrme energy density functional including tensor terms in a three-dimensional time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) model with no spatial symmetry restrictions, achieving full self-consistency. The authors find that while the newly included spin-dependent terms from the central sector can produce qualitative changes in the strength distribution of the isovector giant dipole resonance, the quantitative effects on the linear response are small and the giant dipole energy remains unaffected overall.

## Method
The authors implemented the full Skyrme-tensor energy density functional in a TDHF model formulated on a three-dimensional Cartesian grid with no symmetry restrictions, including all time-odd terms. They applied the model to study the isovector giant dipole resonance in the linear (small amplitude) limit by applying a delta-function boost operator to the ground state, evolving the system in time, and extracting the strength distribution via Fourier transform of the time-dependent dipole moment. Calculations were performed for spherical nuclei (16O, 120Sn), axially deformed nuclei (24Mg, 28Si, 178Os, 190W), and triaxial nuclei (238U).

## Key numbers
- 16O and 120Sn were used as spherical test cases for the isovector giant dipole resonance response
- 24Mg, 28Si, 178Os, 190W, and 238U were used as deformed test cases spanning axial and triaxial deformation
- The tensor force coupling constants are expressed in terms of isoscalar-isovector coefficients C_T^alpha, with A_alpha = C_0^alpha + C_1^alpha and B_alpha = C_0^alpha - C_1^alpha for each term alpha of the functional

## Context
This work extends previous TDHF calculations that included time-odd terms from modern Skyrme EDFs but omitted tensor terms, such as those by Nakatsukasa (2005) which studied linear response in light spherical and deformed nuclei while omitting some spin-dependent terms. It also complements spherical RPA calculations including tensor terms by Cao (2010) and Bai (2010), and addresses the lack of a self-consistent implementation of the Skyrme-tensor functional in deformed (quasi-)particle RPA. The model enables future applications to large-amplitude dynamics such as nucleus-nucleus collisions, fusion, and fission processes within the same formalism.
