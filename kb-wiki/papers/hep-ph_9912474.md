# The Thermal Renormalization Group for Fermions, Universality, and the Chiral Phase-Transition

> We formulate the thermal renormalization group, an implementation of the Wilsonian RG in the real-time (CTP) formulation of finite temperature field theory, for fermionic fields. Using a model with scalar and fermionic degrees of freedom which should describe the two-flavor chiral phase-transition, we discuss the mechanism behind fermion decoupling and universality at second order transitions. It turns out that an effective mass-like term in the fermion propagator which is due to thermal fluctuations and does not break chiral symmetry is necessary for fermion decoupling to work. This situation is in contrast to the high-temperature limit, where the dominance of scalar over fermionic degrees of freedom is due to the different behavior of the distribution functions. The mass-like contribution is the leading thermal effect in the fermionic sector and is missed if a derivative expansion of the fermionic propagator is performed. We also discuss results on the phase-transition of the model considered where we find good agreement with results from other methods.

## Key claim

The thermal renormalization group (TRG) for fermions requires an effective mass-like term in the fermion propagator, generated by thermal fluctuations without breaking chiral symmetry, for fermion decoupling to work at second-order phase transitions; this leading thermal effect is missed if a derivative expansion of the fermionic propagator is performed, in contrast to the high-temperature limit where scalar dominance arises from different distribution functions.

## Method

The authors formulate the TRG by imposing a Wilsonian cutoff only on the thermal parts of real-time (CTP) propagators, replacing the Fermi-Dirac distribution \(\tilde{N}(\omega_k)\) with a cutoff version \(\tilde{N}(\omega_k;\Lambda) = \theta(|\vec{k}|-\Lambda) \tilde{N}(\omega_k)\). They derive an exact flow equation for the scale-dependent effective action \(\Gamma_\Lambda\) and apply it to the chiral quark-meson model (two-flavor chiral fermions coupled to an \(O(4)\)-symmetric scalar sector via a Yukawa coupling). The flow equations are solved using a derivative expansion for the scalar sector (general effective potential plus wave-function renormalization) while keeping the Yukawa coupling in its original form, and incorporating the fermionic thermal mass from hard thermal loop contributions.

## Key numbers

* The Yukawa coupling in the realistic case is \(\bar{h}^2 \sim \mathcal{O}(10)\) (from matching to QCD via Euclidean RG methods, cited from Jungnickel and Wetterich).
* The critical exponents obtained from the TRG for the \(O(4)\)-symmetric scalar model (in the absence of fermions) are in much better agreement with known values than those from a simple polynomial approximation for the effective potential.
* The anomalous dimension \(\eta\) is found to be very small in all situations studied, justifying the neglect of imaginary contributions to two-point functions.

## Context

This work extends the thermal renormalization group (previously formulated for scalar theories by D'Attanasio and Pietroni (1997) and for gauge theories by the same authors) to include fermionic degrees of freedom. It addresses the chiral phase-transition of QCD with two massless flavors, modeled by the chiral quark-meson model studied by Jungnickel and Wetterich (1996) using Euclidean RG methods. The results agree well with other nonperturbative approaches and enable future studies of dynamical aspects of the phase-transition, such as the damping rate and critical slowing down, which were previously calculated for scalar \(\varphi^4\)-theory by Pietroni (1998).
