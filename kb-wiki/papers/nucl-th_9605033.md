# Nuclear halo effects in neutron-capture reactions of astrophysical interest

## Key claim

The halo effect in direct neutron-capture reactions to final states with a loosely bound neutron can change the energy dependence of the cross section from the normal low-energy behavior (1/v for s-wave, v³ for d-wave) to a v-behavior for projectile energies above the neutron separation energy. This effect was not taken into account in previous calculations of the ¹⁸O(n,γ)¹⁹O reaction, but it contributes only about 5% to the total cross section in the astrophysically relevant range and therefore does not significantly alter the reaction rate for this specific case.

## Method

The authors calculate direct-capture (DC) cross sections for electric dipole (E1) transitions using the radial overlap integral formalism, where the bound-state wave function for weakly bound final states is approximated by its asymptotic Hankel function form in the nuclear exterior. They apply this model to the ¹⁸O(n,γ)¹⁹O reaction, using spectroscopic factors extracted from the ¹⁸O(d,p)¹⁹O reaction for transitions to positive-parity final states, and adjusting the spectroscopic factor for the negative-parity 3/2⁻ state to match the experimental thermal cross section. The energy dependence of the cross section is derived analytically for s→p and d→p transitions by inserting the asymptotic forms of the Bessel and Hankel functions.

## Key numbers

- The 3/2⁻ state in ¹⁹O is bound by only 12 keV with respect to the neutron threshold.
- At thermal energies, the main contributions to the halo transition come from a region of about 75 fm (s-wave) and 160 fm (d-wave); at thermonuclear energies, about 45 fm (s-wave) and 85 fm (d-wave).
- The transition to the ¹⁹O(3.945 MeV) state contributes about 5% to the total cross section of ¹⁸O(n,γ)¹⁹O in the astrophysically relevant energy range of 10–250 keV.
- The change in energy dependence from normal behavior to halo behavior occurs at approximately 12 keV, equal to the neutron separation energy of the 3/2⁻ state.

## Context

This work extends the direct-capture model for neutron-rich nuclei previously applied to magic nuclei and the border of stability, as discussed in Oberhummer (1996). The calculations for the three main transitions to positive-parity final states follow the same approach as Gruber (1995) and Meissner (1996), but the authors show that previous publications on ¹⁸O(n,γ)¹⁹O incorrectly extrapolated the s-wave using a 1/v-behavior from the thermal cross section and neglected the d-wave contribution. The halo effect is expected to become more relevant for neutron-capture reactions further from stability, especially when approaching the neutron drip lines in α-process and r-process scenarios in supernovae and inhomogeneous big-bang models.
