# Di-hadron fragmentation and mapping of the nucleon structure

> The fragmentation of a colored parton directly into a pair of colorless hadrons is a non-perturbative mechanism that offers important insights into the nucleon structure. Di-hadron fragmentation functions can be extracted from semi-inclusive electron-positron annihilation data. They also appear in observables describing the semi-inclusive production of two hadrons in deep-inelastic scattering of leptons off nucleons or in hadron-hadron collisions. When a target nucleon is transversely polarized, a specific chiral-odd di-hadron fragmentation function can be used as the analyzer of the net density of transversely polarized quarks in a transversely polarized nucleon, the so-called transversity distribution. The latter can be extracted through suitable single-spin asymmetries in the framework of collinear factorization, thus in a much simpler framework with respect to the traditional one in single-hadron fragmentation. At subleading twist, the same chiral-odd di-hadron fragmentation function provides the cleanest access to the poorly known twist-3 parton distribution $e(x)$, which is intimately related to the mechanism of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in QCD. When sensitive to details of transverse momentum dynamics of partons, the di-hadron fragmentation functions for a longitudinally polarized quark can be connected to the longitudinal jet handedness to explore possible effects due to $CP-$violation of the QCD vacuum. In this review, we outline the formalism of di-hadron fragmentation functions, we discuss different observables where they appear and we present measurements and future worldwide plans.

## Key claim

Di-hadron fragmentation functions (DiFFs) provide a simpler alternative route to extract the transversity parton distribution function compared to single-hadron fragmentation, because the relevant single-spin asymmetry in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) is proportional to the product of transversity and a chiral-odd interference fragmentation function without convolution over parton transverse momenta, working in the framework of collinear factorization.

## Method

The authors review the formalism of di-hadron fragmentation functions (DiFFs), which describe the non-perturbative fragmentation of a colored parton into a pair of colorless hadrons. They discuss how the chiral-odd interference fragmentation function H_1^sphericalangle can be extracted from the Artru-Collins asymmetry in e+e- annihilation data (specifically from the BELLE experiment) and then used to extract the transversity distribution from target-spin asymmetries in two-hadron SIDIS off transversely polarized targets (HERMES and COMPASS experiments). The formalism is extended to subleading twist to access the twist-3 parton distribution e(x), and to transverse-momentum-dependent DiFFs for exploring longitudinal jet handedness.

## Key numbers

- HERMES measurement: average kinematics of <x> = 0.07, <y> = 0.64, <Q^2> = 2.35 GeV^2, <z> = 0.43, with average target polarization <S_T> = 0.74 for a 27.6 GeV e+- beam on a transversely polarized hydrogen target
- COMPASS measurement: 160-GeV muon beam on NH3 and 6LiD targets with <S_T> = 0.83 and <S_T> = 0.47 respectively, with cuts Q^2 > 1 GeV^2, W^2 > 25 GeV^2, and pair invariant mass M_h < 1.5 GeV
- BELLE measurement: Artru-Collins asymmetry for pi+ pi- pairs collected at cm energy around the Y(4S) resonance, with pions required to have minimal fractional energy z > 0.1, data collected in an 8x8 grid of (z, M_h) bins at Q^2 = 100 GeV^2
- The target-spin asymmetry A_SIDIS from HERMES was positive over the whole range, with a scale uncertainty of 8.1 percent from target polarization

## Context

This review extends the formalism of di-hadron fragmentation functions originally developed in jet calculus by Konishi (1978) and later formalized by Bianconi, Bacchetta, and collaborators (1999-2003). It contrasts the DiFF approach to transversity extraction with the traditional TMD-based Collins effect method used by Anselmino (2008, 2013), noting that DiFFs avoid the complications of TMD evolution. The work enables future extractions of the twist-3 parton distribution e(x) from beam-spin asymmetries and exploration of CP-violation effects in QCD through longitudinal jet handedness, with worldwide experimental plans at facilities such as JLab, COMPASS, and EIC.
