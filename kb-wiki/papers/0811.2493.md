# Electromagnetic interactions at RHIC and LHC

> At LHC energies the Lorentz factor will be 3400 for the Pb + Pb collisions and the electromagnetic interactions will play important roles. Cross sections for the electromagnetic particle productions are very large and can not be ignored for the lifetimes of the beams and background. In this article, we are going to study some of the electromagnetic processes at RHIC and LHC and show the cross section calculations of the electron-positron pair production with the giant dipole resonance of the ions.

# Electromagnetic interactions at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim

At LHC and RHIC energies, electromagnetic lepton pair production cross sections are large enough to affect beam lifetimes and backgrounds, but lowest-order QED calculations for electron-positron pair production accompanied by giant dipole resonance (GDR) excitation agree with experimental data only when higher-order QED effects are included; the author's Monte Carlo QED calculation without higher-order effects yields results systematically lower than the Hencken et al. calculation but still within the experimental error bars.

## Method

The author performed a lowest-order QED calculation of electron-positron pair production in ultra-peripheral heavy-ion collisions (impact parameter b > 2R) using a semi-classical approximation and Monte Carlo integration with 10 million points per variable. The probability for simultaneous GDR excitation was included as an independent factor using a 1/ρ² approximation with multi-photon unitarity restoration. The calculations were restricted to the STAR kinematic cuts (pair mass 140 MeV < M_ee < 265 MeV, pair rapidity |Y| < 1.15, transverse momentum p⊥ > 65 MeV) for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Free electron-positron pair production cross section: about 36 kbarn at RHIC energies (Au+Au) and about 227 kbarn at LHC energies (Pb+Pb)
- Bound-free pair production cross section: about 80 barns at RHIC energies (Au+Au) and about 200 barns at LHC energies (Pb+Pb)
- Bound-free pair production cross section for Pb+Pb at LHC: about 280 b
- Bound-free pair production cross section for Au+Au at RHIC: about 80 b
- Lorentz factor γ = 3400 for Pb+Pb at LHC; γ = 100 for Au+Au at RHIC
- Ratio E_max/E_crit: 1.6 at SPS, 160 at RHIC, 54,000 at LHC
- Total untagged cross section for Au+Au at RHIC with STAR restrictions: 0.322 barn
- Tagged cross sections for Au+Au at RHIC (ρ_min = 13, 14, 15 fm): author's Monte Carlo QED results are 1.98 mb, 1.50 mb, and 1.33 mb respectively; Hencken et al. results are 2.30 mb, 1.76 mb, and 1.43 mb respectively
- Baltz calculation including higher-order QED: 1.67 mb, in excellent agreement with STAR data of 1.65 ± 0.23 (stat) ± 0.30 (syst) mb
- Parameter ρ_0 = 1.35 λ_C without restrictions and 0.114 λ_C for STAR kinematic restrictions

## Context

This work extends calculations of electromagnetic lepton pair production in ultra-peripheral collisions, previously modeled via the equivalent-photon approximation by Williams (1934), by using a lowest-order QED Monte Carlo approach. The results are compared with the STAR Collaboration measurement at RHIC and with calculations by Hencken et al. and Baltz. The paper shows that higher-order QED effects, as included by Baltz, are necessary to fully reproduce the STAR data, and that the FAIR facility at GSI (planned for 2016) will enable further tests of QED in strong fields at intermediate relativistic energies (γ up to 20 in fixed-target mode).
