# Quark-Gluon Plasmas and Thermalization

> In these lectures, I will attempt a pedagogical and qualitative introduction to the theory of equilibrium and thermalization of quark-gluon plasmas. I assume only that the reader is familiar with quantum field theory at zero temperature and with QCD as the theory of the strong interactions. I focus on the limit of small alpha_s, which in principle should be relevant at extremely high temperature because of asymptotic freedom, and in any case provides a clean theoretical context in which to discuss a variety of phenomena. Topics discussed include the basic equilibrium formalism for finite-temperature quantum field theory, Debye screening, electric deconfinement, magnetic confinement, dimensional reduction, plasma waves, kinetic theory, hydrodynamic properties such as viscosity, the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect, thermalization in (arbitrarily high energy) heavy ion collisions, and QCD plasma instabilities.

## Key claim

These lectures provide a pedagogical introduction to equilibrium and thermalization of quark-gluon plasmas, focusing on the limit of small \(\alpha_{\mathrm{s}}\) (weak coupling) which is theoretically clean but may not be directly applicable to experimentally achievable plasmas such as those at RHIC, where the coupling is likely not small.

## Method

The paper develops the equilibrium formalism for finite-temperature quantum field theory using imaginary-time path integrals and Matsubara frequency sums, then applies it to static properties such as Debye screening, dimensional reduction to an effective three-dimensional theory, and the emergence of non-perturbative magnetic confinement at the scale \(g^2 T\). It then extends the discussion to real-time near-equilibrium physics via kinetic theory and hydrodynamic properties (viscosity), and concludes with far-from-equilibrium thermalization in heavy ion collisions, including the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect and QCD plasma instabilities.

## Key numbers

*   Free energy expansion: \(F = \# T^4 [1 + \# g^2 + \# g^3 + g^4(\# \ln g + \#) + \# g^5 + g^6(\# \ln g + \mathbf{\#}) + \cdots]\), where the last (boldface) coefficient is non-perturbative.
*   Inverse Debye screening length: \(\xi_{\mathrm{D}}^{-1} \simeq \# g T [1 + g(\# \ln g + \mathbf{\#})]\), where the last numerical coefficient is non-perturbative.
*   Shear viscosity of a QGP to leading order: \(\eta = \frac{\# T^3}{g^4 \ln g} \times f\left( \frac{1}{\ln g} \right)\).
*   Debye mass squared: \(m_{\mathrm{D}}^2 = (1 + \tfrac{1}{6} N_{\mathrm{f}}) g^2 T^2\), where \(N_{\mathrm{f}}\) is the number of quark flavors.
*   Energy density of an ideal quark-gluon plasma with massless \(u\), \(d\), and \(s\) quarks: \(\epsilon = 47.5 \times \frac{\pi^2}{30} T^4\).
*   For strongly-coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory in the large \(N_{\mathrm{c}}\) and infinite 't Hooft coupling limit: \(\epsilon = 0.75 \, \epsilon_{\mathrm{ideal}}\).
*   Scale of non-perturbative magnetic physics: \(p_{\mathrm{mag}} \sim g^2 T\).

## Context

These lectures extend the formalism of zero-temperature quantum field theory and QCD to finite temperature, building on prior work on the weak-coupling expansion of the free energy (Author F, year not given) and Debye screening (Author debye, year not given). The discussion of shear viscosity references a known leading-order result (Author shear, year not given), and the treatment of thermalization in heavy ion collisions complements the lectures by Francois Gelis (year not given) on the color glass condensate. The paper enables further first-principles calculations of transport coefficients and thermalization rates in the weak-coupling limit, while also highlighting the non-perturbative magnetic sector that requires lattice or classical field theory methods.
