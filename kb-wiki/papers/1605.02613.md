# Effective field theory for large logarithms in radiative corrections to electron proton scattering

> Radiative corrections to elastic electron-proton scattering are analyzed in effective field theory. A new factorization formula identifies all sources of large logarithms in the limit of large momentum transfer, $Q^2\gg m_e^2$. Explicit matching calculations are performed through two-loop order. A renormalization analysis in soft-collinear effective theory is performed to systematically compute and resum large logarithms. Implications for the extraction of charge radii and other observables from scattering data are discussed. The formalism may be applied to other lepton-nucleon scattering and $e^+e^-$ annihilation processes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that standard phenomenological prescriptions for radiative corrections in electron-proton scattering fail to capture subleading logarithms beginning at order α² log³(Q²/m²), and provides a systematic effective field theory framework to identify and resum all large logarithms in the limit Q² ≫ m².

## Method

The analysis uses soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) to separate physics at the hard scale (Q²) from physics at the soft scale (ΔE²) and the electron mass scale (m²). Explicit matching calculations between QED and the effective theory are performed through two-loop order in the static source limit (M → ∞), and a renormalization group analysis is carried out to resum large logarithms. The factorization formula separates the cross section into hard, jet, remainder, and soft functions, each depending on distinct physical scales.

## Key numbers

- For Q² ∼ GeV², log(Q²/m²) ≈ 15, indicating the size of large logarithms that must be controlled
- The proton charge radius extracted from the Mainz A1 dataset (2010) is r_E = 0.895(20) fm, while from other world data it is r_E = 0.916(24) fm; a naive average gives r_E = 0.904(15) fm
- The muonic hydrogen determination is r_E(μH) = 0.84087(39) fm, differing from the electron-scattering average by approximately 7σ
- At E = 1 GeV and ΔE = 5 MeV, the total radiative correction δ is computed at first and second order in α, with the second-order correction containing terms of order α²L³ that are not captured by simple exponentiation
- The two-loop mixed real-virtual correction δ_{eγ}^{(2)} equals δ_e^{(1)} δ_{eγ}^{(1)} in the static source limit

## Context

This work extends the heavy particle effective theory analysis of Yennie (1961) to the relativistic regime where v·v′ ≫ 1, building on the author's previous analysis with Lee and Arrington (2015) that revealed significant Q²-range dependence in extracted charge radii. The formalism enables systematic resummation of large logarithms that are missed by conventional radiative correction prescriptions, and can be applied to other lepton-nucleon scattering processes and e⁺e⁻ annihilation. The analysis clarifies conflicting definitions of Born form factors and charge radii in the literature, and provides a quantum field theoretical foundation for quantifying uncertainties due to radiative corrections in the proton radius puzzle.
