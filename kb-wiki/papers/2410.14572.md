# Constraining a relativistic mean field model using neutron star mass-radius measurements II: Hyperonic models

> We investigate whether measurements of the neutron star mass and radius or the tidal deformability can provide information about the presence of hyperons inside a neutron star. This is achieved by considering two inference models, with and without hyperons, based on a field-theoretical approach. While current observations do not distinguish between the two scenarios, we have shown that data simulating expected observations from future large area X-ray timing telescopes could provide some information through Bayes factors. Inference using simulated data generated from an EOS containing hyperons decisively favours the hyperonic model over the nucleonic model. However, a 2\% uncertainty in the mass and radius determination may not be sufficient to constrain the parameters of the model when only six neutron star mass-radius measurements are considered.

## Key claim

Current neutron star observations cannot distinguish between models with and without hyperons, but simulated data from future X-ray telescopes could provide decisive Bayesian evidence for hyperonic models, provided the mass-radius uncertainty is better than 2% and more than six measurements are available.

## Method

The authors constructed two relativistic mean field (RMF) models: a nucleonic-only model (FSU2R) and a hyperonic model (FSU2H) that includes Λ, Σ, and Ξ hyperons with couplings constrained by hypernuclear data and SU(3) symmetry relations. They performed Bayesian inference using current NICER mass-radius measurements for PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, plus gravitational wave tidal deformability data from GW170817 and GW190425, and then repeated the analysis with simulated future mass-radius measurements at 2% uncertainty for six neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of the constrained hyperonic prior: ~2.5 M⊙, compared to ~3.0 M⊙ for the constrained nucleonic prior
- Hyperon potentials at saturation density: UΛ(N)(n0) = −25 to −30 MeV, UΣ(N)(n0) = 10 to 40 MeV, UΞ(N)(n0) = −10 to −25 MeV
- ΛΛ bond energy reproduced: ΔBΛΛ(n0/5) = 0.67 MeV at density nΛ ≈ n0/5
- Simulated future data masses for hyperonic injection: [1.40, 1.60, 1.70, 1.80, 1.90, 1.94] M⊙; for nucleonic injection: [1.20, 1.40, 1.90, 2.00, 2.10, 2.20] M⊙
- Minimum allowed radius for hyperonic prior after nuclear constraints: 9.8 km

## Context

This work extends the nucleonic-only RMF study of Huang (2024) by adding hyperonic degrees of freedom, building on the FSU2R and FSU2H parameterizations of Tolos (2016, 2017). The authors show that current NICER and gravitational wave data cannot constrain hyperonic parameters, consistent with earlier meta-model studies by Raaijmakers (2021) and Annala (2023) that found composition is poorly constrained by existing observations. The simulated future scenarios demonstrate that next-generation X-ray telescopes like STROBEX or eXTP could potentially discriminate between nucleonic and hyperonic compositions using Bayes factors, though 2% uncertainty on only six mass-radius measurements may still be insufficient to fully constrain the hyperonic model parameters.
