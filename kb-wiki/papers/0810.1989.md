# The characterizing variable for critical point in momentum space

> The possible experimentally observable signal in momentum space for the critical point, which is free from the contamination of statistical fluctuations, is discussed. It is shown that the higher order scaled moment of transverse momentum can serve as an appropriate signal for this point, provided the transverse momentum distribution has a sudden change when energy increases passing through the critical point. A 2-D percolation model with a linear temperature gradient is constructed to check this suggestion. A sudden change of third order scaled moment of transverse momentum is successfully observed.

## Key claim

The third-order scaled moment of transverse momentum (or its reciprocal) can serve as a signal for the critical point in momentum space, provided the transverse momentum distribution undergoes a sudden change when collision energy increases through the critical point. This approach is based on the property that some quantities change from zero to non-zero at the critical point, rather than on large fluctuations, making it free from contamination by statistical fluctuations.

## Method

The authors constructed a 2-dimensional site-percolation model with a linear temperature gradient, where the center of a big circle is at 400 MeV and the fringe at 150 MeV. They calculated the transverse momentum of cells in clusters from a Boltzmann distribution assuming a pion mass of 135 MeV, then computed the first, second, and third order moments of the transverse momentum distribution as a function of the percolation parameter eta (which corresponds to energy density). The reciprocal of the scaled third-order moment, D3, was examined for its dependence on eta.

## Key numbers

- The percolation model uses a system size L = 500 (ratio of big circle radius to small circle radius).
- The critical value of the percolation parameter eta_c is approximately 1.12.
- The temperature gradient ranges from T_max = 400 MeV at the center to T_min = 150 MeV at the fringe.
- The cell mass is assumed to be the pion mass, 135 MeV.
- A sudden change in D3 is observed at eta approximately 1.12, corresponding to a temperature around 233 MeV where a peak appears in the cell temperature distribution.

## Context

This work extends the search for critical point signals in heavy-ion collisions beyond the fluctuation-based approach (e.g., Stephanov, Rajagopal, and Shuryak (1999)), which suffers from contamination by statistical fluctuations as noted by Voloshin, Koch, and Ritter (1999) and others. The authors propose that the higher-order scaled moments of transverse momentum, measured over the whole event sample rather than event-by-event, could be used in the energy scan programs at RHIC by STAR and PHENIX (Mitchell for PHENIX (2006); Sorensen for STAR (2006)). The approach enables locating the critical point region already in the first round of energy scans with large steps, after which finer scans can be concentrated in that region.
