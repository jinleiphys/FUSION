# A case in favor of the $N^*(1700)(3/2^-)$

> Using an interaction extracted from the local hidden gauge Lagrangians, which brings together vector and pseudoscalar mesons, and the coupled channels $ρN$ (s-wave), $πN$ (d-wave), $πΔ$ (s-wave) and $πΔ$ (d-wave), we look in the region of $\sqrt s =1400-1850$ MeV and we find two resonances dynamically generated by the interaction of these channels, which are naturally associated to the $N^*(1520) (3/2^-)$ and $N^*(1700) (3/2^-)$. The $N^*(1700) (3/2^-)$ appears neatly as a pole in the complex plane. The free parameters of the theory are chosen to fit the $πN$ (d-wave) data. Both the real and imaginary parts of the $πN$ amplitude vanish in our approach in the vicinity of this resonance, similarly to what happens in experimental determinations, what makes this signal very weak in this channel. This feature could explain why this resonance does not show up in some experimental analyses, but the situation is analogous to that of the $f_0(980)$ resonance, the second scalar meson after the $σ(f_0(500))$ in the $ππ$(d-wave) amplitude. The unitary coupled channel approach followed here, in connection with the experimental data, leads automatically to a pole in the 1700 MeV region and makes this second $3/2^-$ resonance unavoidable.

## Key claim

Using a unitary coupled-channel approach with interactions derived from local hidden gauge Lagrangians, the authors find that the $N^*(1700)(3/2^-)$ resonance appears unavoidably as a pole in the complex plane, even though its signal in the $\pi N$ (d-wave) channel is very weak because both the real and imaginary parts of the amplitude vanish near the resonance, which could explain why some experimental analyses do not see it.

## Method

The authors constructed a coupled-channel model including $\rho N$ (s-wave), $\pi N$ (d-wave), $\pi \Delta$ (s-wave), and $\pi \Delta$ (d-wave) channels, all with isospin $I=1/2$ and $J^P=3/2^-$. The interaction potentials were derived from local hidden gauge Lagrangians, and the Bethe-Salpeter equation was solved in a unitary approach with loop functions regularized using dimensional regularization. The free parameters (subtraction constants and d-wave transition strengths $\gamma_{ij}$) were fitted to the $\pi N$ (d-wave) partial wave amplitude data from the GWU analysis (Arndt 2006) in the energy range 1400-1800 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Two poles were found in the complex plane: one at $1467 + i83$ MeV associated with the $N^*(1520)(3/2^-)$ and one at $1665 + i78$ MeV associated with the $N^*(1700)(3/2^-)$.
- For the $N^*(1700)$, the branching ratios obtained were: $\rho N_{(s)} = 8.02\%$, $\pi \Delta_{(s)} = 4.51\%$, $\pi \Delta_{(d)} = 13.36\%$, and $\pi N_{(d)} = 28.54\%$.
- The coupling of the $N^*(1700)$ to the $\pi N$ (d-wave) channel was $|g_i| = 0.94$, compared to $|g_i| = 1.60$ for the $N^*(1520)$.
- The fit used a regularization scale $\mu = 630$ MeV, with subtraction constants: $\alpha_{\rho N(s)} = -1.57$, $\alpha_{\pi \Delta(s)} = -1.6$, $\alpha_{\pi \Delta(d)} = -4.2$, $\alpha_{\pi N(d)} = -4.2$, and d-wave parameters $\gamma_{33} = 0.041$, $\gamma_{34} = -0.0202$, $\gamma_{44} = 0.0050$.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical generation approach previously applied to the $N^*(1520)$ by Sarkar (2009) using chiral Lagrangians, and contrasts with the Juelich group analyses (Doering 2009, Roenchen 2012) where the $N^*(1520)$ was an input resonance rather than dynamically generated. The study provides theoretical support for the existence of the $N^*(1700)$, which has been questioned in analyses by the GWU group (Arndt 2006) and the MAID analysis, but is included by the Bonn-Gatchina group (Thoma 2008). The vanishing of the $\pi N$ amplitude near 1700 MeV is compared to the analogous situation of the $f_0(980)$ resonance in $\pi\pi$ scattering, where the $f_0(500)$ and $f_0(980)$ overlap and produce a zero in the amplitude.
