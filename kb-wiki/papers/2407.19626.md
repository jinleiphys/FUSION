# Formulating Schwinger--Dyson equations for QED propagators in Minkowski space

> The Schwinger--Dyson equations (SDEs) are coupled integral equations for the Green's functions of a quantum field theory (QFT). The SDE approach is the analytic nonperturbative method for solving strongly coupled QFTs. When applied to QCD, this approach, also based on the first principle, is the analytic alternative to lattice QCD. However, the SDEs for the n-point Green's functions involves (n+1)-point Green's functions (sometimes (n+2)-point functions as well). Therefore any practical method for solving this infinitely coupled system of equations requires a truncation scheme. When considering strongly coupled QED as a modeling of QCD, naive truncation schemes violate various principles of the gauge theory. These principles include gauge invariance, gauge covariance, and multiplicative renormalizability. The combination of dimensional regularization with the spectral representation of propagators results in a tractable formulation of a truncation scheme for the SDEs of QED propagators, which has the potential to preserve the aforementioned principles and renders solutions obtainable in the Minkowski space.

## Key claim

The paper claims that combining dimensional regularization with the spectral representation of propagators yields a tractable truncation scheme for the Schwinger-Dyson equations (SDEs) of QED propagators that can preserve gauge invariance, gauge covariance, and multiplicative renormalizability while allowing solutions to be obtained directly in Minkowski space, rather than Euclidean space. The hedge is that this approach has the *potential* to preserve these principles, and it is presented as a formulation rather than a complete solution.

## Method

The author formulates the SDEs for QED propagators using a spectral representation of the propagators combined with dimensional regularization in 4-2*epsilon* dimensions, working directly in Minkowski space without Wick rotation. The method involves evaluating loop integrals in Minkowski space using contour integration for the time component while applying dimensional regularization only to spatial dimensions, then expressing the resulting integrals as hypergeometric functions. The author develops a truncation scheme that maintains gauge covariance by constructing linear transforms that are exponent-preserving on the variable *z* = *p*^2/*s*, which allows the spectral representation to be applied consistently.

## Key numbers

- The loop integral *L*_{0*n*}(*Delta*, *epsilon*) is evaluated in Minkowski space and yields the result *i*(-1)^*n* / (4*pi*)^{*d*/2} * Gamma(*n* - *d*/2) / [Gamma(*n*) * Delta^{n-d/2}], which agrees with the standard Wick-rotated result from Peskin and Schroeder (1995).
- The more general integral *L*_{*mn*}(*Delta*, *epsilon*) = integral *d*underline{l} *l*^{2*m*} / (*l*^2 - *Delta* + *i*epsilon)^*n* is given by *i*(-1)^{*n*-*m*} / (4*pi*)^{*d*/2} * Gamma(*n* - *d*/2 - *m*) / [Gamma(*n*) * Delta^{n-d/2-m}] * product_{*m*'=1}^{*m*} (*d*/2 + *m*' - 1), for *m* >= 1 and *n* >= *m* + 1.
- The hypergeometric function expansions for the *epsilon* -> 0 limit yield: ~_2*F*_1(1-*epsilon*,3;3-*epsilon*;*z*) = -1/(*z*-1) + *epsilon*[ln(1-*z*)/(*z*-1) + (*z* + *z*^2/2 + ln(1-*z*))/(*z*^2(*z*-1))] + *O*(*epsilon*^1).
- The hypergeometric function expansion for ~_2*F*_1(1-*epsilon*,2;2-*epsilon*;*z*) = -1/(*z*-1) + *epsilon*[ln(1-*z*)/(*z*-1) + (*z* + ln(1-*z*))/(*z*(*z*-1))] + *O*(*epsilon*^1).

## Context

This work extends the nonperturbative SDE approach previously applied to QCD as an analytic alternative to lattice QCD, but focuses on strongly coupled QED as a modeling of QCD. The author addresses the problem that naive truncation schemes for SDEs violate gauge invariance, gauge covariance, and multiplicative renormalizability, which has been a known difficulty in the field. The formulation enables future work to solve for QED propagators directly in Minkowski space while preserving gauge principles, potentially providing a tractable method that could later be extended to QCD. The author references standard results from Peskin and Schroeder (1995) for loop integrals and from Abramowitz and Stegun (1964) for hypergeometric function identities.
