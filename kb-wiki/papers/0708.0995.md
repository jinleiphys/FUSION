# Fixed-axis polarization states: covariance and comparisons

> Addressing the recent criticisms of Kvinikhidze and Miller, we prove that the spectator wave functions and currents based on ``fixed-axis'' polarization states (previously introduced by us) are Lorentz covariant, and find an explicit connection between them and conventional direction-dependent polarization states. The discussion shows explicitly how it is possible to construct pure $S$-wave models of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The authors prove that the spectator wave functions and currents based on "fixed-axis" polarization states are Lorentz covariant, contrary to criticisms by Kvinikhidze and Miller (2007), and show that these states can be explicitly connected to conventional direction-dependent polarization states through a Wigner rotation, enabling the construction of pure S-wave models of the nucleon.

## Method

The authors construct fixed-axis polarization vectors for an axial vector diquark constituent of a nucleon bound state, defined initially in the nucleon rest frame and then boosted along the direction of the bound state three-momentum. They evaluate the spectator wave function and electromagnetic current in the relativistic impulse approximation, performing the diquark polarization sum in a collinear frame where initial and final nucleon momenta are aligned, then demonstrate covariance by showing the current transforms as a four-vector under Lorentz transformations. They also derive an explicit connection between fixed-axis and rotated polarization states using a manifestly covariant Lorentz transformation, relating the wave functions through a Wigner rotation in moving frames.

## Key numbers

- The fixed-axis polarization vectors in the nucleon rest frame are: ε₀(±) = ∓(1/√2)[0, 1, ±i, 0]ᵀ and ε₀(0) = [0, 0, 0, 1]ᵀ
- For a moving nucleon with four-momentum P = {ℰ_p, 0, 0, p}, the boosted longitudinal polarization is ε_p(0) = (1/M)[p, 0, 0, ℰ_p]ᵀ
- The polarization sum for equal masses yields D_{+-}^{μν}(P_-,q) = -g^{μν} + 2P^μP^ν/P^2 - P_+^μP_-^ν/M^2, where P = (P_+ + P_-)/2
- The non-zero wave function components in the nucleon rest frame are: ψ = φ × {1 for λ=0, λ_n=+1/2; -1 for λ=0, λ_n=-1/2; -√2 for λ=+1, λ_n=+1/2; √2 for λ=-1, λ_n=-1/2}

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous proposal of fixed-axis polarization states (Gross, Ramalho, and Pena, 2006) by providing a rigorous proof of covariance and addressing specific criticisms raised by Kvinikhidze and Miller (2007). The formalism enables construction of pure S-wave nucleon models that are simpler than conventional helicity-based approaches, and the explicit connection between fixed-axis and rotated polarization states clarifies how angular momentum components are handled in different bases. This enables future phenomenological studies of nucleon form factors and the γ*+N→Δ transition using orthogonal N and Δ wave functions.
