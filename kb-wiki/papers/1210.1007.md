# Charmed mesons in nuclei with heavy-quark spin symmetry

> We study the properties of charmed pseudoscalar and vector mesons in dense matter within a unitary meson-baryon coupled-channel model which incorporates heavy-quark spin symmetry. This is accomplished by extending the SU(3) Weinberg-Tomozawa Lagrangian to SU(8) spin-flavor symmetry and implementing a suitable flavor symmetry breaking. Several resonances with negative parity are generated dynamically by the s-wave interaction between pseudoscalar and vector meson multiplets with $1/2^+$ and $3/2^+$ baryons. Those states are then compared to experimental data as well as theoretical models. Next, Pauli-blocking effects and meson self-energies are introduced in a self-consistent manner to obtain the open-charm meson spectral functions in a dense nuclear environment. We finally discuss the formation of $D$-mesic nuclei.

## Key claim

The SU(8) spin-flavor symmetric model, which incorporates heavy-quark spin symmetry, generates dynamically several negative-parity baryon resonances in the charmed sector, and the in-medium spectral functions of open-charm mesons show a rich resonance-hole structure; however, the predicted D-mesic nuclei are only weakly bound for D⁰ and have significant widths, making experimental detection challenging.

## Method

The authors extend the SU(3) Weinberg-Tomozawa chiral Lagrangian to SU(8) spin-flavor symmetry and implement symmetry breaking via deformation of mass and decay constant parameters. They solve the on-shell Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels to obtain scattering amplitudes and identify dynamically-generated baryon resonances from the poles. For the nuclear medium, they incorporate Pauli blocking effects and meson self-energies self-consistently to compute the D and D* meson spectral functions, and then solve the Schrodinger equation in local density approximation using the resulting energy-dependent optical potential to study bound states in nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The Λc(2595) experimental resonance is identified with a pole at 2618.8 MeV (width not given as matching the experimental value because the three-body decay channel Λcππ is not included)
- A second broad Λc resonance is observed at 2617 MeV with large coupling to the Σcπ channel, forming a two-pole pattern analogous to the Λ(1405)
- A spin-3/2 Λc resonance is found at (2666.6 - i 26.7) MeV, assigned to the experimental Λc(2625)
- For the Σc sector (C=1, S=0, I=1, J=1/2), three resonances are obtained with masses 2571.5, 2622.7, and 2643.4 MeV and widths 0.8, 188.0, and 87.0 MeV respectively
- A spin-3/2 Σc bound state is found at 2568.4 MeV, considered the charmed counterpart of the Σ(1670)
- A second spin-3/2 Σc resonance is found at (2692.9 - i 33.5) MeV
- A bound state for the anti-D meson is found at 2805 MeV, almost at the anti-D N threshold

## Context

This work extends the SU(6) approach in the light sector (Garcia-Recio, 2005; Toki, 2007) to SU(8) spin-flavor symmetry for charm, building on earlier coupled-channel models for charmed meson-baryon interactions (Tolos, 2004; Lutz, 2003; Hofmann, 2005; Mizutani, 2006). The results contrast with earlier predictions from the quark-meson coupling model (Tsushima, 1999) which found stronger binding for D-mesic nuclei, and with the SU(4) t-vector meson exchange model (Tolos, 2007) which predicted very large widths. The findings enable future experimental searches at the PANDA facility at FAIR, though the authors note that reactions with antiprotons may have very low production rates and proton beam reactions, while difficult, seem more promising for trapping D⁰ in nuclei.
