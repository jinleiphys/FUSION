# Light meson decay in the c3p0 model

> Having its origin in a successful mapping technique, the Fock-Tani formalism, the corrected 3P0 model (C3P0) retains the basic aspects of the 3P0 predictions with the inclusion of bound-state corrections. Evaluation of the decay amplitudes has be performed for open-flavor strong decays in the light meson sector. The bound-state corrections introduce a fine-tuning for the former 3P0 model, in particular, the adjustment of the D/S ratios in $b_{1}\toωπ$, $a_{1}\toρπ$ and $h_1\toρπ$ decays.

## Key claim

The corrected 3P0 (C3P0) model, derived from the Fock-Tani formalism, reproduces the basic predictions of the standard 3P0 model for open-flavor strong decays of light mesons while introducing bound-state corrections that act as a fine-tuning, particularly improving the decay rate of rho to pi pi and the D/S ratio of a1 to rho pi.

## Method

The authors start from a microscopic quark-antiquark pair-creation Hamiltonian and apply the Fock-Tani unitary transformation to obtain an effective Hamiltonian for meson decay. The resulting C3P0 amplitude includes bound-state corrections through a kernel Delta that sums over the quantum numbers of the final-state mesons. Decay rates are computed for seven light 1S and 1P meson decays using harmonic oscillator wave functions with a scale parameter beta, and the model parameters gamma (pair production strength) and beta are adjusted by minimizing the chi-squared-like quantity R comparing theoretical to experimental decay rates.

## Key numbers

- Optimum parameters for the standard 3P0 model: gamma = 0.506, beta = 0.397 GeV, giving R = 0.559
- Optimum parameters for the C3P0 model: gamma = 0.535, beta = 0.387 GeV, giving R = 0.486
- rho to pi pi decay rate: experimental 149.4 MeV; 3P0 prediction 81 MeV; C3P0 prediction 111 MeV
- b1 to omega pi decay rate: experimental 142 MeV; both models predict 143 MeV
- b1 to omega pi D/S ratio: experimental 0.277(27); both models predict 0.288
- a1 to rho pi D/S ratio: experimental -0.108(16); 3P0 prediction -0.149; C3P0 prediction -0.121
- h1 to rho pi decay rate: experimental 360 MeV; 3P0 prediction 378 MeV; C3P0 prediction 374 MeV
- h1 to rho pi D/S ratio: 3P0 prediction 0.215; C3P0 prediction 0.214

## Context

This work extends the standard 3P0 model of Micu (1969) and Le Yaouanc et al. (1970s), as applied to light meson decays by Barnes et al. (1990s), by incorporating bound-state corrections derived from the Fock-Tani formalism previously developed for hadron-hadron scattering. The C3P0 model improves agreement with experiment for the rho to pi pi decay and the a1 D/S ratio, while preserving the b1 results. Future work could extend the approach to strange mesons, glueball candidates, and charmed mesons, and include the full meson octet in the bound-state kernel.
