# Sensitivity of reaction cross sections to halo nucleus density distributions

> In order to clear up the sensitivity of the nucleus--nucleus reaction cross sections $σ_R$ to the nuclear matter distributions in exotic halo nuclei, we have calculated the values of $σ_R$ for scattering of $^6$He, $^{11}$Li, and $^{19}$C nuclei on several nuclear targets at the energy of 0.8 GeV/nucleon. The calculations were performed in the "rigid target" approximation to the Glauber theory, different shapes of the nuclear density distributions in $^6$He, $^{11}$Li, and $^{19}$C being assumed.

## Key claim
The reaction cross sections for halo nuclei on nuclear targets retain a significant sensitivity to the radial shape of the nuclear density distribution, even when calculated with the more accurate "rigid target" approximation to Glauber theory, rather than the optical limit approximation which overestimates cross sections.

## Method
The authors calculated reaction cross sections for scattering of the halo nuclei $^6$He, $^{11}$Li, and $^{19}$C on several nuclear targets at 0.8 GeV/nucleon. They used the Glauber theory within the "rigid target" approximation, where the scattering amplitude of one nucleon on the target is calculated first and then used for the composite projectile. The projectile densities were modeled as a Gaussian core plus a 1p-shell harmonic oscillator-type halo, and calculations were performed for different assumed shapes of the nuclear density distributions.

## Key numbers
- Reaction cross sections were calculated for $^6$He, $^{11}$Li, and $^{19}$C projectiles on $^4$He, $^{12}$C, $^{28}$Si, $^{58}$Ni, and $^{208}$Pb targets.
- All calculations were performed at a fixed beam energy of 0.8 GeV/nucleon.
- The free nucleon-nucleon total cross section used in the profile function was $\sigma_{NN} = 41$ mb.
- The slope parameter of the nucleon-nucleon amplitude was set to $\beta_{NN} = 0$ fm$^2$, and the ratio of real to imaginary part $\epsilon_{NN}$ was set to 0.

## Context
This work extends the investigation of Bush et al. (2004), who used the optical limit approximation to Glauber theory and concluded that nucleus-nucleus cross sections are more sensitive to the nuclear periphery than proton-nucleus cross sections. The present authors note that the optical limit significantly overestimates reaction cross sections for halo nuclei, and they repeat the calculations using the more accurate rigid target approximation to test whether that conclusion holds. This enables a more reliable determination of nuclear matter radii and radial shapes of exotic nuclei from reaction cross section measurements.
