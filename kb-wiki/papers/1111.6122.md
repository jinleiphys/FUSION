# Strange quark matter:Business as usual or phase transition?

> We give an overview of some results presented at the Strange Quark Matter 2011 conference in Krakow, and interpret them in light of the search of the search for a QCD deconfinement phase transition in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The paper argues that while a clear scaling violation signature of the QCD deconfinement phase transition (as illustrated in a schematic figure) has not yet been conclusively observed in heavy ion collisions, there are "interesting hints" that such a signature is in sight, and the author remains optimistic that significant strides toward obtaining it were made at the SQM2011 conference.

## Method

The author reviews experimental results and theoretical arguments presented at the Strange Quark Matter 2011 conference, evaluating each from the perspective of how far it advances the goal of observing a deconfinement phase transition. The analysis covers global system characteristics (multiplicity, HBT radii), thermal model fits (equilibrium vs. non-equilibrium with phase space occupancy parameters), strangeness enhancement, quarkonium suppression, fluctuations and higher cumulants, elliptic flow, and other observables such as momentum correlations and jet suppression.

## Key numbers

- LHC has "conclusively established" that multiplicity per participant grows with energy as a power-law, but this increase is "only apparent at the LHC"
- At sqrt(s) = 39 GeV, quark coalescence behavior of v2 "starts to definitely break" as particle/antiparticle v2 difference grows
- ALICE data shows that soft physics in p-p is different from A-A even when event multiplicity is the same; source radii scale as ~(dN/dy)^(1/3) but with different scaling constants for p-p and A-A
- 10% variations of gamma_q (the phase space occupancy parameter), "well below the error in fits," could increase or decrease fluctuations by ~100%
- pQCD works "comparatively well" for R_AA of light quarks but "tends to miss v2 of these quarks for the same values of the opacity parameter"

## Context

This theory summary talk at SQM2011 extends the long-standing search for the QCD deconfinement phase transition through changes in the chemistry of heavy ion collision systems, historically focused on strangeness enhancement as proposed by Rafelski, Mueller, and Koch. The paper contrasts equilibrium thermal model fits (Cleymans) with non-equilibrium fits using gamma parameters (Rafelski, Petran), noting that no experimental observable can conclusively distinguish between them. It discusses lattice results from Gavai and Schmidt showing a cross-over at low chemical potential, and the expectation of a critical point at high chemical potential based on effective theories (Costa) and universality arguments (Redlich, Schmidt). The work enables future searches for the phase transition at lower energies (FAIR, NICA, SHINE, RHIC low energy) and in astrophysical contexts (supernovae, neutron stars via Sagert, strange matter via Labun and Steinheimer).
