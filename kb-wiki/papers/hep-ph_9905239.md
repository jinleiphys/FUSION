# From hard thermal loops to Langevin dynamics

> In hot non-Abelian gauge theories, processes characterized by the momentum scale $g^2 T$ (such as electroweak baryon number violation in the very early universe) are non-perturbative. An effective theory for the soft ($|\vec{p}|\sim g^2 T$) field modes is obtained by integrating out momenta larger than $g^2 T$. Starting from the hard thermal loop effective theory, which is the result of integrating out the scale $T$, it is shown how to integrate out the scale $gT$ in an expansion in the gauge coupling $g$. At leading order in $g$, one obtains Vlasov-Boltzmann equations for the soft field modes, which contain a Gaussian noise and a collision term. The 2-point function of the noise and the collision term are explicitly calculated in a leading logarithmic approximation. In this approximation the Boltzmann equation is solved. The resulting effective theory for the soft field modes is described by a Langevin equation. It determines the parametric form of the hot baryon number violation rate as $Γ= κg^{10} \log(1/g) T^4$, and it allows for a calculation of $κ$ on the lattice.

## Key claim

In hot non-Abelian gauge theories, the effective theory for soft field modes (with momenta of order \(g^2 T\)) can be reduced to a Langevin equation by integrating out both the hard scale \(T\) and the semi-hard scale \(gT\) in a leading logarithmic approximation, yielding the parametric form of the hot sphaleron rate as \(\Gamma = \kappa g^{10} \log(1/g) T^4\), but this result is only leading-order in \(g\) and leading-logarithmic, and the coefficient \(\kappa\) must be determined non-perturbatively on the lattice.

## Method

Starting from the hard thermal loop (HTL) effective theory, which integrates out the scale \(T\), the author separates the fields into soft (\(|\vec{p}| \sim g^2 T\)) and semi-hard (\(|\vec{k}| \sim gT\)) components using a separation scale \(\mu\) satisfying \(g^2 T \ll \mu \ll gT\). The semi-hard fields are then integrated out perturbatively by solving their linearized equations of motion (Vlasov-Boltzmann equations) in a formal expansion in powers of the soft fields and the gauge coupling \(g\). The resulting term \(\xi\) in the soft-field equations is expanded, and at leading order in \(g\) the two-point function of the noise and the collision term are calculated explicitly in a leading logarithmic approximation, which allows the Boltzmann equation to be solved and reduced to a Langevin equation for the soft gauge fields.

## Key numbers

* Hot sphaleron rate parametric form: \(\Gamma = \kappa g^{10} \log(1/g) T^4\), where \(\kappa\) is a non-perturbative coefficient to be computed on the lattice.
* Characteristic time scale for soft non-perturbative dynamics: \(t \sim 1/(g^4 T \log(1/g))\).
* Debye mass squared: \(\mmdebye = (1/3)(N + N_s/2 + n_f/4) g^2 T^2\) for an SU(\(N\)) gauge theory with \(n_f\) chiral fermions and \(N_s\) scalars in the fundamental representation.
* Separation scale condition: \(g^2 T \ll \mu \ll gT\).

## Context

This work extends the program of integrating out thermal scales in hot gauge theories, building on the hard thermal loop effective theory of Pisarski (1989) and the realization by Arnold, Son, and Yaffe (1997) that hard modes strongly affect soft gauge field dynamics via Landau damping. The paper improves upon earlier one-loop calculations in the HTL effective theory (Bodeker, 1998) by using a kinetic-equation formulation (Blaizot and Iancu, 1993; Nair, 1993) to systematically integrate out the scale \(gT\). The resulting Langevin equation enables non-perturbative lattice calculations of the hot sphaleron rate, as subsequently performed by Moore (1999), and provides a rigorous foundation for the color conductivity picture of Arnold, Son, and Yaffe (1999).
