# Three-body model of the d+A system in an antisymmetrised, translationally invariant many nucleon theory

> This paper concerns the theory of direct A(d,p)B reactions and how practical 3-body models of them are related to optical model potentials describing the interaction of the deuteron's constituents and the target A. A new definition of the nucleon optical potential is introduced which necessitates a careful examination of the boundary conditions involved in the associate coupled channels problem. The new definition turns out to be convenient for making a connection with the theory of the (d,p) reaction. The analysis is carried out within an antisymmetrised, translationally invariant formalism and provides a firm basis from a many-body point of view for the three-body force effects calculated in previous work by Dinmore, Timofeyk, Al-Khalili and Johnson.

# Three-body model of the d+A system in an antisymmetrised, translationally invariant many nucleon theory

## Key claim

The paper provides a formal many-body foundation for three-body models of A(d,p)B reactions by introducing a new definition of the nucleon optical potential that, within an antisymmetrised and translationally invariant framework, explicitly generates effective three-body force effects not captured by standard phenomenological nucleon optical potentials. The analysis shows that the standard three-body model Hamiltonian must be supplemented by these additional interaction terms, which the author identifies as the many-body origin of the three-body force effects previously calculated by Dinmore, Timofeyk, Al-Khalili and Johnson (2019).

## Method

The author derives coupled equations for the overlap functions of the n+p+A system by starting from the exact A+2 scattering wavefunction for a deuteron incident on an A-nucleon target in its ground state, defined within a Fock-space formalism. A new definition of the nucleon optical potential operator is introduced using one-body overlap functions and the concept of B-space, where a fictitious particle interacts with the target nucleons through an operator \(\hat{\mathcal{U}}\) that includes exchange effects from antisymmetrisation. These techniques are extended to the n+p+A system by introducing \(\bar{B}\)-space, where a fictitious particle with internal two-nucleon states (including the deuteron ground state and continuum states) interacts with the target through a folded interaction \(\bar{\mathcal{U}}\), and the resulting coupled equations are shown to reduce to a three-body model Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron ground state energy is given by \(E_{d_0} = \frac{\hbar^2 K_{d_0}^2}{4m} - \epsilon_{d_0}\), where \(\epsilon_{d_0}\) is the deuteron binding energy.
- For continuum two-nucleon states, the energy is \(E_d = \frac{\hbar^2 K_d^2}{4m} + \frac{\hbar^2 k_d^2}{m}\), with \(\epsilon_d = \frac{\hbar^2 k_d^2}{m}\) for \(d \neq d_0\) and \(\epsilon_d = -\epsilon_0\) for \(d = d_0\).
- The fictitious particle in \(\bar{B}\)-space has mass \(\frac{2Am}{(A+2)}\) and the target centre-of-mass kinetic energy term involves \(\frac{\hbar^2 K_d^2}{2Am}\).
- The nucleon optical potential in B-space is defined as \(\langle \mathbf{k} | \hat{V}_{\mathrm{opt}} | \mathbf{k}' \rangle = \langle \mathbf{k}, 0 | \hat{U}_{\mathrm{eff}} | \mathbf{k}', 0 \rangle\), where \(\hat{U}_{\mathrm{eff}}\) is the effective interaction operator given by \(\hat{U}_{\mathrm{eff}} = \hat{\mathcal{U}} + \hat{\mathcal{U}} Q_0 \frac{1}{(E + i\epsilon - \hat{T} - \hat{h}_A - Q_0 \hat{\mathcal{U}} Q_0)} Q_0 \hat{\mathcal{U}}\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous formal developments on the nucleon optical potential (Johnson 2017, Johnson 2019) to the three-body problem of deuteron-induced reactions, providing a many-body justification for the three-body force effects calculated by Dinmore, Timofeyk, Al-Khalili and Johnson (2019) using a non-antisymmetrised model. The formalism connects the effective interactions in three-body models of A(d,p)B reactions to the nucleon optical model operators defined through Feshbach's theory, and the analysis of boundary conditions for the coupled channels problem is essential for establishing this link. The results enable future work to incorporate antisymmetrisation and translation invariance consistently into practical three-body reaction calculations, and the author notes that for the A=2 case the coupled equations may need to be converted to Faddeev (1961) or AGS (1967) equations to properly take the scattering limit.
