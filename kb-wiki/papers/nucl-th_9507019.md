# Nuclear Shell Structure and Chaotic Dynamics in Hexadecapole Deformation

> The effect of an axially symmetric hexadecapole term is investigated in a strongly deformed quadrupole potential. While the system is nonintegrable and shows significant chaotic behaviour classically, the quantum mechanical treatment not only produces a general smoothing effect with regard to chaos but even yields a pronounced shell structure at certain hexadecapole strength parameter values for oblate and prolate deformation.

# Nuclear Shell Structure and Chaotic Dynamics in Hexadecapole Deformation

## Key claim
The addition of an axially symmetric hexadecapole term to a strongly deformed quadrupole potential can produce pronounced shell structure in both oblate and prolate systems, despite the system being nonintegrable and classically chaotic. This result is not obvious because the octupole term, by contrast, produces chaos without shell structure in the oblate case.

## Method
The authors investigated classical and quantum single-particle motion in an axially symmetric harmonic oscillator potential with a hexadecapole term, using cylindrical coordinates. They applied secular perturbation theory with the removal of resonances method (RRM) to find parameter values where the nonintegrable problem reduces effectively to an integrable two-dimensional oscillator, and performed a Strutinsky-type analysis of the quantum spectrum for angular momentum projections m from 0 to 15.

## Key numbers
- For oblate deformation with quadrupole parameter b=2/5 and hexadecapole strength λ=0.18, the winding number ω_ρ/ω_z = 1:2 applies for about two thirds of the ξ_z range.
- Shell structure in the oblate case occurs along λ ≈ 0.09, with level bunching for m=0 at λ ≈ 0.12 and for m=15 at λ ≈ 0.07.
- Stability islands in the oblate case appear at λ ≈ 0.25, 0.22, and 0.21, corresponding to particle numbers N (or Z) ≈ 56, 86, and 126 respectively.
- For prolate deformation with b=5/2 and λ=0.011, the winding number is 2:1, and magic numbers appear for N (or Z) ≈ 40, 80, 140.
- The winding number in the prolate case is independent of ξ_ρ for about 85% of its allowed range.

## Context
This work extends the earlier study of octupole deformation by Heiss, Nazmitdinov, and Radu (1994), which found shell structure only for prolate systems. The authors contrast the hexadecapole term with the octupole case, noting that even multipoles can restore original symmetry in oblate systems while odd multipoles cannot. The results enable experimental investigation of whether observed superdeformed shell patterns arise from plain quadrupole deformation, quadrupole plus octupole, or stronger quadrupole with hexadecapole admixture, as suggested by comparisons with predictions from Ragnarsson and Sheline (1984).
