# A correlated model for lambda-hypernuclei

> We study the properties of hypernuclei containing one lambda hyperon in the framework of the correlated basis function theory with Jastrow correlations. Fermi hypernetted chain integral equations are derived and used to evaluate energies and one-body densities of lambda hypernuclei having a doubly closed shell nucleonic core in the jj coupling scheme, from Carbon to Lead. We also study hypernuclei having the least bound neutron substituted by the lambda particle. The semi-realistic Afnan and Tang nucleon-nucleon potential and Bodmer and Usmani lambda-nucleon potential are adopted. The effect of many-body forces are considered by means either of a three body lambda-nucleon-nucleon potential of the Argonne type or of a density dependent modification of the lambda-nucleon interaction, fitted to reproduce the lambda binding energy in nuclear matter. While Jastrow correlations underestimate the attractive contribution of the three body $\la$ interaction, the density dependent potential provides a good description of the lambda binding energies over all the nuclear masses range, in spite of the relative simplicity of the model.

# A correlated model for lambda-hypernuclei

## Key claim
The density dependent modification of the lambda-nucleon interaction, fitted to reproduce the lambda binding energy in nuclear matter (30 MeV), provides a good description of lambda binding energies across the nuclear mass range from Carbon to Lead, despite the relative simplicity of the model. However, Jastrow correlations underestimate the attractive contribution of the three-body lambda-nucleon-nucleon interaction, making the explicit three-body potential too repulsive in this framework.

## Method
The authors extended the correlated basis function theory with Jastrow correlations and Fermi hypernetted chain (FHNC) integral equations to describe hypernuclei containing one lambda hyperon. They evaluated energies and one-body densities for lambda-hypernuclei with doubly closed shell nucleonic cores in the jj coupling scheme, using the semi-realistic Afnan and Tang S3 nucleon-nucleon potential and the Bodmer and Usmani lambda-nucleon potential, with many-body forces treated either via a three-body lambda-nucleon-nucleon potential of the Argonne type or a density dependent modification of the lambda-nucleon interaction.

## Key numbers
- Lambda binding energy in nuclear matter: 30.26 MeV, reproduced with density dependent potential parameter gamma = 2.2 fm^3
- Nuclear matter binding energy per nucleon with S3 potential at saturation density (0.16 fm^-3): 14.43 MeV (empirical value is 16 MeV)
- For 17-Lambda-O with two-body lambda-nucleon force only: B_Lambda = 27.5 +/- 2.0 MeV (VMC result from Usmani 1995), in qualitative agreement with the authors' value
- For 17-Lambda-O with three-body lambda-nucleon-nucleon force: B_Lambda = 6.81 MeV, showing the three-body potential is too repulsive with Jastrow correlations
- All calculations underbind the lambda in Carbon by approximately 30%

## Context
This work extends the FHNC approach previously developed for doubly closed shell nuclei in jj-coupling by Arias de Saavedra (1996) to systems containing a lambda hyperon as an impurity. It builds upon the FHNC equations for a lambda in infinite nuclear matter developed by Usmani (1980) and the variational Monte Carlo study of hypernuclei by Usmani (1995). The results enable systematic microscopic calculations of hypernuclear structure across the periodic table, though the authors note that tensor correlations would be needed to properly treat pion-exchange induced three-body forces.
