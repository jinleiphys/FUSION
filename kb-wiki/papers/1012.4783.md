# Dalgarno-Lewis Method Revisited

> Proving the existence of an operator that connects non-perturbed states to perturbed states, an alternative derivation of the Dalgarno-Lewis method is given. To illustrate that the Dalgarno-Lewis method is an apt tool for algebraic Hamiltonians, the method is applied to one class of such systems, namely deep three-dimensional potentials with positive parity.

# Dalgarno-Lewis Method Revisited

## Key claim

The paper proves that an operator exists which connects non-perturbed states to perturbed states in non-degenerate perturbation theory, and that this operator is the same for all states, a connection to the Dalgarno-Lewis method that the authors claim was not previously noted, despite extensive prior literature on the method.

## Method

The authors derive an operator $\hat{S}_n(\lambda)$ expressed as an exponential series in $\lambda$, with operators $\hat{F}$, $\hat{G}$, $\hat{\Gamma}$ determined order-by-order from commutator relations. They show that the Dalgarno-Lewis method emerges naturally from the operator formalism, and then apply it to a three-dimensional Hamiltonian with a deep potential expanded as a power series in $\mathbf{r}^2$, using the SU(1,1) Lie algebra of harmonic oscillator creation and annihilation operators to compute perturbative corrections algebraically.

## Key numbers

- The first-order energy correction is given by $\epsilon_n^{(1)} = \langle n | \hat{h} | n \rangle$, the standard expression from non-degenerate perturbation theory.
- The second-order correction is $\epsilon_n^{(2)} = -\frac{1}{2} \langle n| [\hat{F}, \hat{h}_{\rm ND}] | n \rangle$, where $\hat{h}_{\rm ND}$ is the non-diagonal part of the perturbation.
- The third-order correction is $\epsilon_n^{(3)} = \langle n | \frac{1}{2} (\hat{F}^2 \hat{h}_{\rm D} + \hat{h}_{\rm D} \hat{F}^2) - \hat{F}_n \hat{h} \hat{F}_n |n \rangle$.
- For the deep potential application, the energy eigenvalues are given by $\frac{E_m}{V_0} = -1 + \left( m+\frac{1}{2} \right) \sqrt{2} \delta + \left( C_2 - 3 m^2 \right) \frac{\beta}{\alpha^2} \delta^2 - \left( 7 m C_2 - 15 m^3 - \frac{3}{2} m \right) \sqrt{2} \frac{\beta^2 V_0}{\alpha^4} \delta^3 + \cdots$, where $\delta = \hbar \sqrt{\frac{\alpha}{\mu V_0}}$ is a dimensionless expansion parameter.
- The Casimir operator eigenvalue is $k = \frac{1}{2} \left( \ell + \frac{3}{2} \right)$, where $\ell$ is the orbital angular momentum quantum number.

## Context

This work extends the Dalgarno-Lewis method (Dalgarno and Lewis, original paper) by providing an operator-based derivation that had not been previously noted, despite extensive literature including Schwinger (unknown year), Mavromatis (unknown year), Nandi (unknown year), and Imbo (1983). The application to deep three-dimensional potentials with positive parity demonstrates that the method makes rotational bands manifest, confirming numerical predictions by Buck (unknown year) for deep Gaussian potentials. The algebraic SU(1,1) formulation enables systematic calculation of higher-order perturbative corrections for Hamiltonians with underlying algebraic structure, relevant to alpha-clustering in nuclei.
