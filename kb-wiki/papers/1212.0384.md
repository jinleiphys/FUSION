# New Skyrme energy density functional for a better description of the Gamow-Teller Resonance

> We present a new Skyrme energy density functional (EDF) named SAMi [Phys. Rev. C 86 031306(R)]. This interaction has been accurately calibrated to reproduce properties of doubly-magic nuclei and infinite nuclear matter. The novelties introduced in the model and fitting protocol of SAMi are crucial for a better description of the Gamow-Teller Resonance (GTR). Those are, on one side, the two-component spin-orbit potential needed for describing different proton high-angular momentum spin-orbit splitings and, on the other side, the careful description of the empirical hierarchy and positive values found in previous analysis of the spin (G_0) and spin-isospin (G_0^') Landau-Migdal parameters: 0 < G_0 < G_0^', a feature that many of available Skyrme forces fail to reproduce. When employed within the self-consistent Hartree-Fock plus Random Phase Approximation, SAMi produces results on ground and excited state nuclear properties that are in good agreement with experimental findings. This is true not only for the GTR, but also for the Spin Dipole Resonance (SDR) and the Isobaric Analog Resonance (IAR) as well as for the non charge-exchange Isoscalar Giant Monopole (ISGMR) and Isovector Giant Dipole (IVGDR) and Quadrupole Resonances (IVGQR).

# New Skyrme energy density functional for a better description of the Gamow-Teller Resonance

## Key claim
The new SAMi Skyrme energy density functional improves the description of the Gamow-Teller Resonance (GTR) in medium and heavy nuclei by incorporating a two-component spin-orbit potential and enforcing the empirical hierarchy 0 < G_0 < G_0' for the Landau-Migdal parameters, features that many existing Skyrme forces fail to reproduce. The improvement is demonstrated for doubly-magic spherical nuclei within the self-consistent Hartree-Fock plus Random Phase Approximation (HF+RPA) framework, without compromising the description of other nuclear observables.

## Method
The authors constructed a new Skyrme energy density functional named SAMi (Skyrme-Aizu-Milano) by performing a chi-squared minimization using the MINUIT package, fitting to binding energies and charge radii of doubly-magic nuclei (40,48Ca, 90Zr, 132Sn, 208Pb), proton spin-orbit splittings of 1g and 2f levels in 90Zr and 208Pb, fixed Landau-Migdal parameters (G_0=0.15, G_0'=0.35), and pseudo-data from variational calculations of uniform neutron matter. The functional includes a two-parameter spin-orbit potential (W_0 and W_0') and a central tensor (J^2) term, and was used in fully self-consistent HF+RPA calculations to compute charge-exchange and non-charge-exchange resonance properties.

## Key numbers
- GTR excitation energy in 48Ca: SAMi predicts 10.2 MeV for the high-energy peak, compared to experimental value 10.5 MeV; low-energy peak at 2.0 MeV (SAMi) versus 3.0 MeV (experiment)
- GTR in 90Zr: SAMi predicts excitation energy 15.5 MeV (experiment 15.8 ± 0.5 MeV) for the high-energy peak between 12-30 MeV, exhausting 70% of the Ikeda Sum Rule (experiment 57%)
- GTR in 208Pb: SAMi predicts excitation energy 19.3 MeV, matching the experimental value 19.2 ± 0.2 MeV within experimental accuracy
- Isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonance centroid energy in 208Pb: SAMi predicts 14.48 MeV (experiment 14.24 ± 0.11 MeV)
- Isovector Giant Dipole Resonance centroid energy in 208Pb: SAMi predicts 13.95 MeV (experiment 13.25 ± 0.10 MeV)
- Isovector Giant Quadrupole Resonance excitation energy in 208Pb: SAMi predicts 23.1 MeV (experiment 23.0 ± 0.2 MeV)
- Spin Dipole Resonance sum rule in 90Zr: SAMi predicts 150 fm^2 (experiment 148 ± 12 fm^2)
- Spin Dipole Resonance total integral in 208Pb: SAMi predicts 1224 fm^2 (experiment 1004 +24/-23 fm^2)

## Context
This work extends the Skyrme HF+RPA approach by addressing known deficiencies in spin-isospin properties, following earlier efforts with interactions such as SGII (Giai and Sagawa, 1981), SkO' (Reinhard and Flocard, 1999), and SLy5 (Chabanat et al., 1998). The SAMi functional improves upon these by incorporating a two-component spin-orbit potential (following Reinard and Flocard, 1995) and enforcing the empirical Landau-Migdal parameter hierarchy suggested by Wakasa et al. (2007). The results enable more reliable predictions of Gamow-Teller strength distributions relevant for electron-capture in core-collapse supernovae, neutrino-induced nucleosynthesis, and double-beta decay studies, while also providing a benchmark for future extensions to deformed and open-shell nuclei.
