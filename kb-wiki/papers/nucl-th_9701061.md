# Exact Q-Deformed Dyson Expansion for the Nuclear J=5/2 Shell

> The quon algebra, which interpolates between the Bose and Fermi algebras and depends on a free paramenter $q$, is used to generate a deformed Dyson boson expansion of the quadrupole operator. Then we obtain a quadrupole-quadrupole hamiltonian, for a single j-shell, in terms of this deformed bosonic operator. The hamiltonian is diagonalized and its eigenvalues are compared with the ones obtained from the fermionic quadrupole-quadrupole hamiltonian. The deformation parameter helps in achieving the correct energy levels, what cannot be encountered in practice with the usual non-deformed Dyson expansion.

# Exact Q-Deformed Dyson Expansion for the Nuclear J=5/2 Shell

## Key claim

The quon-algebra deformation of the Dyson boson expansion, applied to a quadrupole-quadrupole Hamiltonian in a single j=5/2 shell, yields energy levels that agree with the exact fermionic shell-model result much better than the usual non-deformed Dyson expansion, provided the deformation parameters q_s, q_d, and q_g are chosen appropriately; however, for the g-boson (J=4) a physically reasonable deformation parameter could not be obtained from the Pauli-principle norm-matching criterion at this low j value, so q_g was fixed to 1.

## Method

The authors constructed a Dyson boson expansion of the quadrupole operator for a single j=5/2 shell using q-deformed bosons (quons) that satisfy the commutation relation [x, x†]_q = 1, with separate deformation parameters q_s, q_d, and q_g for s (J=0), d (J=2), and g (J=4) bosons. The expansion coefficients A through J for the multipole operators Q^J_M were determined by requiring that the mapped operators satisfy the same commutation relations as the fermionic multipole operators, and these coefficients turned out to be independent of the q parameters. The resulting quadrupole-quadrupole Hamiltonian was then diagonalized in a basis of two-q-deformed-boson states for a system of four identical fermions, and the energy eigenvalues were compared with the exact shell-model diagonalization.

## Key numbers

- The Dyson expansion coefficients for the multipole operators (independent of q) are: A = 0.8165, B = -0.5832, C = 0.9091, D = 0.8207, E = 0.7559, F = 1.8516, G = -0.9091, H = -0.3350, J = -1.235.
- From the Pauli-principle norm-matching criterion for j=5/2, the estimated deformation parameters are q_s = 0.3 and q_d = 0.6; no reasonable value (between -1 and 1) was found for q_g.
- The best-fit deformation parameters (with q_g fixed to 1) are q_s = 0.2 and q_d = 0.4.
- The fermionic spectrum for four particles in a j=5/2 shell interacting via the quadrupole-quadrupole force contains exactly three states with angular momenta 0, 2, and 4.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous deformed Marumori-type boson mapping for a pairing interaction (Avancini et al., 1996) to the Dyson-type mapping for a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, using the quon algebra of Greenberg (1991, 1992) which interpolates between Bose and Fermi statistics. The usual non-deformed Dyson expansion (with all q = 1) gives very poor agreement with the exact shell-model result for this low-j case. The deformed mapping improves the agreement considerably, but the difficulty in fixing q_g for j=5/2 suggests that the method is more naturally suited to larger j shells; the authors note that for j=7/2 and above, reasonable values for all three q parameters can be obtained from the norm-matching criterion. This enables future systematic studies of deformed boson expansions in heavier single-j shells.
