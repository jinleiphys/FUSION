# Experimental evidence for 56Ni-core breaking from the low-spin structure of the N=Z nucleus 58Cu

> Low-spin states in the odd-odd N=Z nucleus 58Cu were investigated with the 58Ni(p,n gamma)58Cu fusion evaporation reaction at the FN-tandem accelerator in Cologne. Seventeen low spin states below 3.6 MeV and 17 new transitions were observed. Ten multipole mixing ratios and 17 gamma-branching ratios were determined for the first time. New detailed spectroscopic information on the 2+,2 state, the Isobaric Analogue State (IAS) of the 2+,1,T=1 state of 58Ni, makes 58Cu the heaviest odd-odd N=Z nucleus with known B(E2;2+,T=1 --> 0+,T=1) value. The 4^+ state at 2.751 MeV, observed here for the first time, is identified as the IAS of the 4+,1,T=1 state in 58Ni. The new data are compared to full pf-shell model calculations with the novel GXPF1 residual interaction and to calculations within a pf5/2 configurational space with a residual surface delta interaction. The role of the 56Ni core excitations for the low-spin structure in 58Cu is discussed.

# Experimental evidence for 56Ni-core breaking from the low-spin structure of the N=Z nucleus 58Cu

## Key claim
The low-spin structure of 58Cu provides experimental evidence for the breaking of the 56Ni doubly magic core, as demonstrated by the fact that full pf-shell model calculations including core excitations (using the GXPF1 interaction) reproduce electromagnetic transition strengths much better than calculations with an inert 56Ni core, particularly for isovector M1 transitions which are reduced by a factor of 5-10 and isoscalar B(E2) values which are enhanced by a factor of 4 when core excitations are included.

## Method
Low-spin states in 58Cu were populated via the 58Ni(p,nγ)58Cu fusion evaporation reaction at 14 MeV beam energy using the Cologne FN-Tandem accelerator, with γγ-coincidences, γγ-angular correlations, and γ-ray linear polarization measurements performed using the Cologne Osiris cube spectrometer equipped with five Compton-suppressed Ge-detectors and one Euroball Cluster detector used as a Compton polarimeter. The experimental data were compared to full pf-shell model calculations using the GXPF1 residual interaction (allowing up to 6-particle excitations from the f7/2 orbital) and to calculations within a restricted pf5/2 configurational space with a residual surface delta interaction (SDI) assuming an inert 56Ni core.

## Key numbers
- 17 low-spin states observed below 3.6 MeV, with 17 new γ transitions and 5 new levels identified
- 10 multipole mixing ratios and 17 γ-branching ratios determined for the first time
- The 2+2 state at 1653 keV identified as the Isobaric Analogue State (IAS) of the 2+1,T=1 state of 58Ni at 1454 keV; the 4+ state at 2751 keV identified as the IAS of the 4+1,T=1 state of 58Ni at 2460 keV
- Mean level deviations between experiment and theory: 41 keV for GXPF1 calculations versus 83 keV for SDI calculations with inert 56Ni core
- Summed B(M1) strength for the lowest two 1+ states: 5.7 μN2 in SDI calculations (with 56Ni core) versus 2.5 μN2 in GXPF1 calculations (including core excitations)
- B(M1; 1+2 → 0+1) values differ by a factor of 5 between the two calculations even with spin quenching of 0.7 for SDI
- The 2+2 → 1+1 M1 transition is suppressed by two to four orders of magnitude relative to other isovector M1 transitions, interpreted as a Q-phonon selection rule

## Context
This work extends the study of odd-odd N=Z nuclei in the pf-shell beyond the f7/2 region (where nuclei like 46V, 50Mn, and 54Co had been studied by Friessner (1999), Schmidt (2000), and Lisetskiy (2002)) into the mass region above 56Ni, where earlier attempts by Phillips (1968) and Bruge (1977) using an inert 56Ni core required substantial changes in single-particle energies and too large effective quadrupole charges. The new data enable testing of the GXPF1 interaction developed by Honma (2002) as a universal effective interaction for the whole pf-shell, and the identification of a proposed T=0 and T=1 doublet of 4+ states at approximately 2.7 MeV offers a path to study isospin breaking for nuclei along the N=Z line above 56Ni.
