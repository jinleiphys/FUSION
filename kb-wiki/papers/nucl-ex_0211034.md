# On possible existence of the dibaryon resonance $d^*_1$ and its role in the $npγ$ and $pdγ$ processes below the pion threshold

> We give reasons for the existence of the NN decoupled dibaryon resonance $d^*_1$(1956). Strong evidence for its presence has first been found in the energy spectrum of coincident photons emitted at $\pm 90^0$ from the $pp \to γγX$ process at 216 MeV measured by the DIB2$γ$ collaboration at JINR. As further experimental indications of the $d^*_1$(1956) existence we present those found in the available photon energy spectra of $npγ$, $pdγ$, and $pAγ$ reactions below the pion threshold. It is noted that serious discrepancies between the $npγ$ and $pdγ$ experimental cross sections and theoretical calculations can reasonably be explained by the fact that latter did not take into account the $d^*_1$ effect.

## Key claim
The paper argues that a narrow peak at 24 MeV and a broad structure between 45-75 MeV in the photon energy spectrum of the \(pp \to \gamma\gamma X\) reaction at 216 MeV constitute evidence for a previously unknown \(NN\) decoupled dibaryon resonance \(d^*_1(1956)\), and that similar structures observed in \(np\gamma\), \(pd\gamma\), and \(pA\gamma\) spectra can be explained by the same dibaryon mechanism, provided the calibration of the \(np\gamma\) detector is correct.

## Method
The author analyzed the energy spectrum of coincident photons emitted at \(\pm 90^\circ\) in the laboratory from the \(pp \to \gamma\gamma X\) reaction at 216 MeV, measured by the DIB2\(\gamma\) collaboration at JINR. Monte Carlo simulations were performed assuming the process \(pp \to \gamma d^*_1(1956) \to pp\gamma\gamma\) as the sole mechanism for the \(pp \to pp\gamma\gamma\) reaction. The author then compared existing experimental inclusive photon energy spectra from \(np\gamma\), \(pd\gamma\), and \(pA\gamma\) reactions below the pion threshold with theoretical calculations that did not include the dibaryon mechanism, and added Monte Carlo simulated contributions from the \(d^*_1(1956)\) resonance to those spectra.

## Key numbers
- The measured photon energy spectrum from \(pp \to \gamma\gamma X\) at 216 MeV shows a narrow peak at about 24 MeV with a statistical significance of 5.3\(\sigma\) and a width (FWHM) of about 8 MeV, compatible with experimental resolution.
- A broad peak in the same spectrum lies in the energy range 45-75 MeV with a statistical significance of 3.5\(\sigma\).
- The differential cross section for resonance production of two photons emitted symmetrically at \(\theta_{lab} = \pm 90^\circ\) from the process \(pp \to \gamma d^*_1 \to pp\gamma\gamma\) at 216 MeV was estimated to be about 9 nb/sr\(^2\).
- The total cross section for \(d^*_1(1956)\) production in the \(pp\gamma\gamma\) reaction at 216 MeV was estimated to be about 1.4 \(\mu\)b.
- The Monte Carlo calculation for the \(np\gamma\) reaction at 170\(\pm\)35 MeV used a \(d^*_1(1956)\) production cross section of 4.3 \(\mu\)b.
- The Monte Carlo calculation for the \(pd\gamma\) reaction at 195 MeV used a \(d^*_1(1956)\) production cross section of 9.4 \(\mu\)b.
- The Monte Carlo calculation for the \(p+C \to \gamma X\) reaction at 200 MeV used a \(d^*_1(1956)\) production cross section of 56.5 \(\mu\)b.

## Context
This work extends the search for \(NN\) decoupled dibaryon resonances reported by the DIB2\(\gamma\) collaboration at JINR (GerKh93, EGK95, PRC64) by reinterpreting existing data on \(np\gamma\), \(pd\gamma\), and \(pA\gamma\) reactions. The paper argues that serious discrepancies between experimental cross sections and theoretical calculations, such as those by Shafer and Jetter for \(np\gamma\) and by Nak92 for \(pd\gamma\), can be resolved by including the \(d^*_1(1956)\) dibaryon mechanism. The findings suggest that future calculations of photon production in \(NN\) and \(NA\) collisions below the pion threshold must account for this resonance to achieve agreement with data.
