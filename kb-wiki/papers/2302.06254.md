# Localization measures of parity adapted U($D$)-spin coherent states applied to the phase space analysis of the $D$-level Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model

> We study phase-space properties of critical, parity symmetric, $N$-quDit systems undergoing a quantum phase transition (QPT) in the thermodynamic $N\to\infty$ limit. The $D=3$ level (qutrit) Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick (LMG) model is eventually examined as a particular example. For this purpose, we consider U$(D)$-spin coherent states (DSCS), generalizing the standard $D=2$ atomic coherent states, to define the coherent state representation $Q_ψ$ (Husimi function) of a symmetric $N$-quDit state $|ψ>$ in the phase space $\mathbb CP^{D-1}$ (complex projective manifold). DSCS are good variational aproximations to the ground state of a $N$-quDit system, specially in the $N\to\infty$ limit, where the discrete parity symmetry $\mathbb{Z}_2^{D-1}$ is spontaneously broken. For finite $N$, parity can be restored by projecting DSCS onto $2^{D-1}$ different parity invariant subspaces, which define generalized ``Schrödinger cat states'' reproducing quite faithfully low-lying Hamiltonian eigenstates obtained by numerical diagonalization. Precursors of the QPT are then visualized for finite $N$ by plotting the Husimi function of these parity projected DSCS in phase space, together with their Husimi moments and Wehrl entropy, in the neighborhood of the critical points. These are good localization measures and markers of the QPT.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that parity-projected U($D$)-spin coherent states (generalized Schrodinger cat states) provide good variational approximations to low-lying Hamiltonian eigenstates of the $D$-level Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model for finite $N$, and that Husimi function moments and Wehrl entropy serve as effective localization measures and markers of quantum phase transitions, but only in the thermodynamic $N\to\infty$ limit where the discrete parity symmetry $\mathbb{Z}_2^{D-1}$ is spontaneously broken.

## Method

The authors generalize U($D$)-spin coherent states (DSCS) to define the Husimi function $Q_\psi(z)$ on the complex projective phase space $\mathbb{C}P^{D-1}$ for symmetric $N$-quDit systems. They project DSCS onto $2^{D-1}$ parity-invariant subspaces of the $\mathbb{Z}_2^{D-1}$ symmetry group to create parity-adapted cat states (DCATs), then compute Husimi moments and Wehrl entropy for these states. For the $D=3$ (qutrit) LMG model, they compare variational DCAT states to numerically diagonalized Hamiltonian eigenstates using fidelity, and visualize quantum phase transition precursors through Husimi function plots near critical points.

## Key numbers

* The Hilbert space dimension for indistinguishable atoms reduces from $D^N$ to $\binom{N+D-1}{D-1}$, the dimension of the fully symmetric irreducible representation of U($D$).
* The parity symmetry group generalizes from $\mathbb{Z}_2$ for $D=2$ to $\mathbb{Z}_2^{D-1}$ for general $D$, providing $2^{D-1}$ different parity invariant subspaces.
* The Hamiltonian density uses renormalized one-body interactions $\varepsilon \to \epsilon/N$ and two-body interactions $\lambda_2 \to -\lambda/[N(N-1)]$ to handle the thermodynamic $N\to\infty$ limit.
* The $D=3$ level (qutrit) LMG model is examined as a particular example, with energy measured in $\epsilon > 0$ units and the phase diagram discussed in terms of control parameter $\lambda$.

## Context

This work extends previous phase-space analyses of quantum phase transitions using Husimi functions and Wehrl entropy from the U(2) (two-level) Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model studied by Romera (2014) and others to general U($D$) symmetric multi-quDit systems. It generalizes the parity-adapted coherent state approach previously applied to the Dicke model of superradiance for two-level atoms (PhysRevA.85.053831, 2013) and three-level atoms (Castanos, 2018), as well as the U(3) vibron model (PhysRevA.86.032508, 2012). The work enables classification of excited states in $D$-level LMG models by comparing numerical eigenstates to variational ground states adapted to different parities, and provides a framework for identifying quantum phase transition order through localization measures in higher-dimensional phase spaces.
