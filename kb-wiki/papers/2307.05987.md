# Dispersion relations alone cannot guarantee causality

> We show that linear superpositions of plane waves involving a single-valued, covariantly stable dispersion relation $ω(k)$ always propagate outside the lightcone, unless $ω(k) =a+b k$. This implies that there is no notion of causality for individual dispersion relations, since no mathematical condition on the function $ω(k)$ (such as the front velocity or the asymptotic group velocity conditions) can serve as a sufficient condition for subluminal propagation in dispersive media. Instead, causality can only emerge from a careful cancellation that occurs when one superimposes all the excitation branches of a physical model. This is shown to happen automatically in local theories of matter that are covariantly stable. Hence, we find that the need for nonhydrodynamic modes in relativistic fluid mechanics is analogous to the need for antiparticles in relativistic quantum mechanics.

## Key claim

Linear superpositions of plane waves involving a single-valued, covariantly stable dispersion relation ω(k) always propagate outside the lightcone unless ω(k) = a + b k, meaning that no mathematical condition on the function ω(k) (such as front velocity or asymptotic group velocity conditions) can serve as a sufficient condition for subluminal propagation in dispersive media.

## Method

The authors analyze the propagation of linear perturbations constructed from a single dispersion branch ω(k) via Fourier superposition, and show that such single-branched wavepackets always have non-compact support at later times unless the dispersion relation is exactly linear. They then prove that if the underlying dynamics is governed by a local operator (a polynomial in spatial derivatives) and all eigenvalues obey the covariant stability bound Im ω(k) ≤ |Im k|, then the full multi-branch solution automatically propagates subluminally, with all superluminal tails cancelling out.

## Key numbers

- The front velocity condition v_f = lim_{k→∞} (Re ω)/k ∈ [-1,1] is shown to be insufficient for causality, as demonstrated by three counterexamples: the diffusion equation (ω = -i k²), the Euclidean wave equation (ω = ±i k), and the linearized Benjamin-Bona-Mahony equation (ω = k/(1+k²)), all of which have v_f = 0 yet are acausal.
- The covariant stability bound Im ω(k) ≤ |Im k| is derived as the physical requirement that a stable system must be stable in every inertial frame; violation would allow a boost with velocity v = Im k/Im ω to produce a growing Fourier mode.
- For hyperbolic first-order systems of the form ∂_t Ψ = -Ξ Ψ - M ∂_x Ψ, the covariant stability bound implies that the characteristic velocities (eigenvalues of M) coincide with the front velocities and lie in [-1,1].

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Hegerfeldt (1974) on the impossibility of localizing relativistic single-particle wavefunctions, showing that the same phenomenon applies to all collective excitations with a single-valued dispersion relation. The results explain why non-hydrodynamic modes are necessary for causality in relativistic fluid mechanics, analogous to the need for antiparticles in relativistic quantum mechanics, and contradict earlier attempts to use front velocity or asymptotic group velocity as sufficient causality criteria (Brillouin, 1960; Fox and Kuper, 1970; Krotscheck, 1978; Pu, 2010). The paper enables a rigorous causality criterion for local theories of matter that are covariantly stable, and shows that the BBM equation (Benjamin, 1972) is acausal because its dynamical operator is non-local.
