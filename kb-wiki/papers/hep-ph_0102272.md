# Projection Operator Approach to Langevin Equations in $ϕ^4$ Theory

> We apply the projection operator method (POM) to $ϕ^4$ theory and derive both quantum and semiclassical equations of motion for the soft modes. These equations have no time-convolution integral term, in sharp contrast with other well-known results obtained using the influence functional method (IFM) and the closed time path method (CTP). However, except for the fluctuation force field terms, these equations are similar to the corresponding equations obtained using IFM with the linear harmonic approximation, which was introduced to remove the time-convolution integral. The quantum equation of motion in POM can be regarded as a kind of quantum Langevin equation in which the fluctuation force field is given in terms of the operators of the hard modes. These operators are then replaced with c-numbers using a certain procedure to obtain a semiclassical Langevin equation. It is pointed out that there are significant differences between the fluctuation force fields introduced in this paper and those introduced in IFM. The arbitrariness of the definition of the fluctuation force field in IFM is also discussed.

## Key claim

The projection operator method (POM) yields quantum and semiclassical Langevin equations for the soft modes in \(\phi^4\) theory that contain no time-convolution integral term, unlike results from the influence functional method (IFM) and closed time path method (CTP); however, aside from the fluctuation force field terms, the POM equations are similar to those obtained from IFM with the linear harmonic approximation, and the fluctuation force fields in the two approaches differ significantly.

## Method

The authors applied the time-convolutionless (TCL) version of the projection operator method to \(\phi^4\) theory, dividing the field into soft modes (momenta below a cutoff \(\Lambda_I\)) and hard modes (momenta between \(\Lambda_I\) and an ultraviolet cutoff \(\Lambda\)). The hard modes were treated as a thermal bath at temperature \(T\) with initial density matrix \(\rho_E = e^{-\beta H_E}/Z\), and the projection operator was defined as \(P O = \text{Tr}_E[\rho_E O]\). The TCL equation was expanded to first order in the interaction Liouville operator \(L_I\), and the fluctuation-dissipation theorem of the second kind was used to fix the expansion order for the fluctuation term, yielding a quantum Langevin equation for the soft-mode field operator \(\hat{\phi}_<(\mathbf{x},t)\). A semiclassical Langevin equation was then obtained by replacing the operator-valued fluctuation force fields with c-number stochastic variables.

## Key numbers

* The hard-mode cutoff \(\Lambda_I\) must satisfy \(m \ll \Lambda_I \ll T\) to ensure a large separation of time scales between soft and hard modes, where \(m\) is the mass and \(T\) the temperature.
* The second-order correlation of the fluctuation force field \(\hat{f}_3\) is \(\langle \hat{f}_3(\mathbf{x}_1,t_1)\hat{f}_3(\mathbf{x}_2,t_2) \rangle = -\frac{i\lambda^2}{4} G_{\Lambda_I}(\mathbf{x}_1-\mathbf{x}_2,t_1-t_2)\), where \(G_{\Lambda_I}\) is the hard-mode propagator.
* The second-order correlation of \(\hat{f}_2\) is \(\langle \hat{f}_2(\mathbf{x}_1,t_1)\hat{f}_2(\mathbf{x}_2,t_2) \rangle = -\frac{\lambda^2}{2} G_{\Lambda_I}(\mathbf{x}_1-\mathbf{x}_2,t_1-t_2)^2\).
* The second-order correlation of \(\hat{f}_1\) is \(\langle \hat{f}_1(\mathbf{x}_1,t_1)\hat{f}_1(\mathbf{x}_2,t_2) \rangle = \frac{i\lambda^2}{36} [6 G_{\Lambda_I}(\mathbf{x}_1-\mathbf{x}_2,t_1-t_2)^3 + 9 G_{\Lambda_I}(\mathbf{x}_1-\mathbf{x}_2,t_1-t_2) G_{\Lambda_I}(0,0)^2]\).

## Context

This work extends the projection operator method (POM) of Hashitsume, Shibata, and Shingu (1977) and its improvement by Uchiyama and Shibata (1999) and Koide and Maruyama (2000) to quantum field theory, specifically \(\phi^4\) theory. It contrasts with the influence functional method (IFM) and closed time path method (CTP) used by Greiner, Muller, and others (1996), which produce Langevin equations with time-convolution integrals that must be removed via the quasi-instantaneous approximation or the linear harmonic approximation. The POM result is similar to the IFM result with the linear harmonic approximation but differs in the fluctuation force field structure, and the authors note that the arbitrariness in defining the fluctuation force field in IFM can partially eliminate these differences. This work enables the study of nonlinear Langevin equations in quantum field theory without memory terms, applicable to phenomena such as the time evolution of disoriented chiral condensates.
