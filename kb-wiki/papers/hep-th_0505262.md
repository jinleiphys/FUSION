# Cross-ladder effects in Bethe-Salpeter and Light-Front equations

> Bethe-Salpeter (BS) equation in Minkowski space for scalar particles is solved for a kernel given by a sum of ladder and cross-ladder exchanges. The solution of corresponding Light-Front (LF) equation, where we add the time-ordered stretched boxes, is also obtained. Cross-ladder contributions are found to be very large and attractive, whereas the influence of stretched boxes is negligible. Both approaches -- BS and LF -- give very close results.

## Key claim

Cross-ladder contributions to the Bethe-Salpeter and Light-Front equations are found to be very large and attractive, while the influence of stretched box diagrams is negligible (less than 2% of the binding energy); both approaches give very close results, though the Bethe-Salpeter equation is slightly more attractive than Light-Front dynamics for the same kernel.

## Method

The Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space for scalar particles with zero total angular momentum was solved by projecting onto the Light-Front plane and using the Nakanishi integral representation for the amplitude, avoiding Wick rotation. The corresponding Light-Front equation was solved with a kernel including ladder, cross-ladder, and stretched-box time-ordered diagrams, where the cross-ladder kernel was derived from the Feynman amplitude using Feynman parametrization and the Light-Front kernel was obtained via Weinberg rules. Both equations were solved numerically using a spline expansion of the solution, with the coupling constant eigenvalue extrapolated from a nearly linear behavior for fixed binding energy.

## Key numbers

- For binding energy B = 0.01 and exchange mass μ = 0.5 (constituent mass m = 1), the coupling constant α for BS ladder is 1.44, for BS ladder+cross-ladder is 1.21, for LF ladder is 1.46, for LF ladder+cross-ladder is 1.23, and for LF ladder+cross-ladder+stretched-box is 1.21.
- For binding energy B = 1.00 and exchange mass μ = 0.5, the coupling constant α for BS ladder is 6.71, for BS ladder+cross-ladder is 4.56, for LF ladder is 7.17, for LF ladder+cross-ladder is 4.97, and for LF ladder+cross-ladder+stretched-box is 4.91.
- For μ = 0.15 and α = 0.9, the binding energies obtained with BS equation are B_L ≈ 0.035, B_L+CL ≈ 0.06, and B_all (complete set of all irreducible diagrams from Nieuwenhuis and Tjon (1996)) ≈ 0.225.
- The numerical results were obtained with N ≈ 10 Gauss quadrature points in each integration variable, ensuring an accuracy of 1%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous method for solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space (Carbonell and Karmanov (2004)), which was previously applied only to ladder kernels, by incorporating cross-ladder exchanges for the first time in both Bethe-Salpeter and Light-Front frameworks. The results are compared to those of Nieuwenhuis and Tjon (1996), who included the full set of all irreducible cross-ladder graphs using the Feynman-Schwinger representation, showing that the cross-ladder graphs considered here represent only a small part of the total correction; the results are also compared to an estimate by Adam, Dorkin, and Tjon (1999) using a dispersion relation approach, where the present results are smaller by a factor of 3. The work enables future inclusion of higher-order stretched box contributions and comparison with nonrelativistic limits, where the cross-ladder and stretched box diagrams reduce but do not eliminate the discrepancy between relativistic and nonrelativistic results at small binding energies.
