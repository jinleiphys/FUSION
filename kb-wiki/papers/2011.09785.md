# Nuclear matter at finite temperature and static properties of proto-neutron star

> With the effective chiral model, the finite temperature properties of nuclear matter have been studied at different temperatures. For symmetric nuclear matter, I particularly focused on the possibility of liquid-gas phase transition at low temperature and density. The critical temperature obtained in this context, is consistent with the experimental and empirical findings. The free energy and entropy variations are also studied for different values of temperature. A few asymmetric nuclear matter properties like the equation of state and the speed of sound with respect to temperature are also examined. The work is also extended to obtain the equation of state of $β$ stable nuclear matter at finite temperature. For the neutrino free case, the various static proto-neutron star properties are computed for a wide range of temperature, relevant to proto-neutron stars. For all the values of temperature, the obtained estimates of maximum gravitational mass are found to be in good agreement with the observational constraints specified from massive pulsars like PSR J0348+0432 and PSR J0740+6620. The results of surface redshift for all the temperature also satisfy the maximum surface redshift constraints from EXO 07482-676, 1E 1207.4-5209 and RX J0720.4-3125.

# Nuclear matter at finite temperature and static properties of proto-neutron star

## Key claim

The effective chiral model, with temperature effects invoked only through the kinetic part of the interaction, successfully describes nuclear matter properties across different density regimes at finite temperature, yielding a critical temperature for the liquid-gas phase transition consistent with experimental bounds and producing proto-neutron star maximum gravitational masses that satisfy observational constraints from massive pulsars for all temperatures considered.

## Method

The author employs an effective chiral model based on chiral symmetry and relativistic mean field treatment, where nucleon and meson masses are generated dynamically through spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. Temperature effects are incorporated through the kinetic part of the interaction via nucleon and anti-nucleon distribution functions. The equation of state is computed for symmetric nuclear matter at low density to study the liquid-gas phase transition, and for asymmetric nuclear matter at high density with fixed asymmetry parameter α=0.7. For proto-neutron star properties, β-equilibrated neutrino-free matter is considered, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain static stellar configurations.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for liquid-gas phase transition: Tc = 16.54 MeV, with critical density ρc = 0.170 fm⁻³ and critical pressure Pc = 4.31 MeV fm⁻³
- Saturation density of the model: ρ₀ = 0.153 fm⁻³
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: B/A = -16.3 MeV
- Nuclear incompressibility: K = 303 MeV
- Symmetry energy coefficient: J = 32 MeV
- Slope parameter: L₀ = 87 MeV
- Nucleon effective mass ratio: m*/m = 0.85
- Scalar meson mass: mσ = 510 MeV
- Maximum gravitational mass results are compared with PSR J0348+0432 (M = 2.01 ± 0.04 M⊙) and PSR J0740+6620 (2.14⁺⁰·¹⁰₋₀·₀₉ M⊙ at 68.3% confidence)
- Surface redshift results are compared with constraints from EXO 07482-676 (Zs = 0.35), 1E 1207.4-5209 (Zs = 0.12-0.23), and RX J0720.4-3125 (Zs = 0.205⁺⁰·⁰⁰³₋₀·₀₀₆)

## Context

This work extends the finite-temperature study of Sahu (2004) using the same effective chiral model to more rigorously examine thermal properties across different density domains, including both low-density liquid-gas phase transition and high-density β-stable matter relevant to proto-neutron stars. The critical temperature obtained (16.54 MeV) falls within the experimentally determined range of 10-20 MeV reported by Sharma, Kaijpper, Yang, Elliott, Takatsuka, Holt, Karnaukhov, and Bharat, and is comparable to values from other theoretical models such as NL3 (14.60 MeV), G3 (15.37 MeV), IU-FSU (14.50 MeV), and Walecka (18.3 MeV). The results enable further investigation of proto-neutron star properties using this phenomenological model, which has previously been applied to study hybrid stars with hyperons and Δ baryons at zero temperature by Sen and collaborators.
