# Heavy Quark Spin Symmetry Violating Hadronic Transitions of Higher Charmonia

> In heavy quarkonia, hadronic transitions serve as an enlightened probe for the structure and help to establish the understanding of light quark coupling with a heavy degree of freedom. Moreover, in recent years, hadronic transitions revealed remarkable discoveries to identify the new conventional heavy quarkonia and extracting useful information about the so called "XYZ" exotic states. In this contribution, we present our predictions for heavy quark spin symmetry (HQSS) breaking hadronic transitions of higher $S$ and $D$ wave vector charmonia based on our recently proposed model (inspired by Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model) to create light meson(s) in heavy quarkonium transitions. We also suggest spectroscopic quantum numbers $(^{2S+1}L_J)$ for several observed charmoniumlike states. Our analysis indicates that the $Y(4360)$ is most likely to be a $3D$ dominant state.

## Key claim

The authors predict heavy quark spin symmetry (HQSS) violating hadronic transition widths for higher S- and D-wave vector charmonia using a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) inspired model, and argue that the Y(4360) is most likely a 3D-dominant charmonium state, but note that their prediction for Y(4660) as a pure 5S state lies slightly above the experimental upper limit, suggesting it may have a sizeable 4D component.

## Method

The authors model the coupling of light scalar and pseudoscalar mesons to charm quarks using an effective Lagrangian with scalar and pseudoscalar interactions inspired by the NJL model. They compute decay widths for processes such as Ψ → J/ψ η and Ψ → h_c η using simple harmonic oscillator (SHO) wavefunctions and an S-D mixing formalism with a mixing angle θ = 13°, fitting the overall coupling g from the ψ(2S) → J/ψ η decay. The model avoids the soft-gluon and low-momentum assumptions of QCD multipole expansion and heavy meson chiral Lagrangians, making it applicable to higher charmonia with large decay momenta.

## Key numbers

- Branching fraction B[ψ(3686) → J/ψ η] = (3.36 ± 0.05)%, compared to B[ψ(3686) → J/ψ ππ] = (34.49 ± 0.30)% for π⁺π⁻ and (18.17 ± 0.31)% for π⁰π⁰
- Upper limit B[ψ(4160) → h_c(1P) η] < 2 × 10⁻³
- Model parameters: m_c = 1.5 GeV, β = 0.40 GeV, g = 0.80, |θ| = 13°
- Ratio Γ(ψ(4160) → h_c(1P) η) / Γ(ψ(4160) → J/ψ η) = 7.887 × 10⁻²
- Ratio Γ(ψ(4415) → h_c(1P) η) / Γ(ψ(4415) → J/ψ η) = 6.736 × 10⁻²
- For Y(4360) as 3³D₁ with θ = 0°: predicted Γ(Y → J/ψ η) = 0.047 MeV; with θ = 13°: 0.016 MeV; with θ = 34°: 1.0 × 10⁻³ MeV; experimental upper limit < 0.963 MeV
- For Y(4660) as 5³S₁ with θ = 0°: predicted Γ(Y → J/ψ η) = 0.057 MeV; experimental upper limit < 0.046 MeV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of HQSS-violating hadronic transitions beyond the QCD multipole expansion formalism (Kuang, 2006) and heavy meson chiral Lagrangians (HMCL), which are limited to low-momentum transitions among lower charmonia. The authors also note that nonrelativistic effective field theory (NREFT) approaches (Guo, 2010) are not suitable for higher charmonia where decay momenta lie in the relativistic regime. The predictions provide guidance for ongoing experiments at Belle, BESIII, and forthcoming facilities (PANDA and BelleII) to identify missing higher vector charmonia and clarify the nature of XYZ exotic states.
