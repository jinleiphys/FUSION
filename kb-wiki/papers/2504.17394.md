# Study of QCD critical point with three-nucleon correlations in light nuclei yields ratios using PYTHIA8/Angantyr

> This study utilizes the PYTHIA8 Angantyr model to systematically investigate the effects of three nucleons correlation $C_{n^2p}$ on the light nuclei yield ratio $N_tN_p/N_d^2$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV. The analysis explores this property across different rapidity ranges, collision centralities, and collision energies, while also examining the roles of multi-parton interactions (MPI) and color reconnection (CR) mechanisms. The results show that the light nuclei yield ratio remains stable with changes in rapidity coverage and collision centrality but slightly increases with rising collision energy. The impact of CR on the light nuclei yield ratio depends entirely on the presence of MPI; when MPI is turned off, CR has no effect. Additionally, the three-nucleon correlation, enhances the light nuclei yield ratio in both central and peripheral collisions. However, the non-monotonic energy dependence observed in experiments, the peak at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = $20\sim30$ GeV reported by the STAR experiment, cannot be explained by the Angantyr model due to its lack of key mechanisms related to the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). Nevertheless, the Angantyr model serves as an important baseline for studying collision behaviors in the absence of QGP effects.

## Key claim

The PYTHIA8 Angantyr model, which lacks quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and critical phenomena mechanisms, cannot reproduce the non-monotonic energy dependence of the light nuclei yield ratio \(N_tN_p/N_d^2\) observed by STAR, specifically the peak at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 20\sim30\) GeV; however, the model serves as a baseline for collision behavior in the absence of QGP effects, and the three-nucleon correlation \(C_{n^2p}\) consistently enhances the yield ratio in both central and peripheral collisions.

## Method

The authors used the PYTHIA8 Angantyr model (version 8.308) to simulate Au+Au collisions at eight beam energies, generating approximately one million events per energy. They calculated the light nuclei yield ratio \(R = N_tN_p/N_d^2\) from the coalescence model, incorporating the three-nucleon correlation \(C_{n^2p}\) alongside the two-nucleon correlation \(C_{np}\) and relative neutron density fluctuation \(\Delta\rho_n\). The analysis varied rapidity coverage, collision centrality (defined by \(\sum E_T\) of charged particles in \(|\eta|<0.5\)), and model tunes that enable or disable multi-parton interactions (MPI) and color reconnection (CR).

## Key numbers

- The light nuclei yield ratio \(N_tN_p/N_d^2\) was studied at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4,\) and 200 GeV in Au+Au collisions.
- For 0-10% central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 14.5\) GeV, the yield ratio calculated with Eq. (lnr) (including \(C_{n^2p}\)) consistently exceeds the reference line \(1/2\sqrt{3}\).
- For 60-80% peripheral Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 39\) GeV, excluding \(C_{np}\) and \(C_{n^2p}\) leads to a significant increase in the light nuclei yield ratio.
- The light nuclei yield ratio shows a slight increase with rising collision energy across all model configurations, while excluding \(C_{np}\) and \(C_{n^2p}\) causes a decrease with energy.
- The STAR experiment reports a peak in the yield ratio at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 20\sim30\) GeV for 0-10% central Au+Au collisions, which the Angantyr model cannot reproduce.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous PYTHIA8 Angantyr study (Zhang et al., 2023) that only considered two-body neutron-proton correlations \(C_{np}\), by now incorporating the three-nucleon correlation \(C_{n^2p}\) which directly impacts triton production. The results contradict the non-monotonic energy dependence observed by the STAR experiment (Zhang, 2019; Zhang, 2020) and NA49 (Sun, 2017), which show a peak at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 20\sim30\) GeV attributed to QCD critical point effects. The Angantyr model provides a baseline for nucleon coalescence in the absence of QGP and critical phenomena, enabling future models that incorporate these mechanisms to isolate their contributions.
