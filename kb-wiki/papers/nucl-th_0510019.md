# Break-up fragments excitation and the freeze-out volume

> We investigate, in microcanonical multifragmentation models, the influence of the amount of energy dissipated in break-up fragments excitation on freeze-out volume determination. Assuming a limiting temperature decreasing with nuclear mass, we obtain for the Xe+Sn at 32 MeV/nucleon reaction [J. D. Frankland et al., Nucl. Phys. A689, 905 (2001); A689, 940 (2001)] a freeze-out volume almost half the one deduced using a constant limiting temperature.

# Break-up fragments excitation and the freeze-out volume

## Key claim
The freeze-out volume extracted from microcanonical multifragmentation models depends strongly on the assumed excitation energy of primary fragments; using a mass-dependent limiting temperature that decreases with nuclear mass yields a freeze-out volume almost half the value obtained with a constant limiting temperature, but the result is conditional on the specific form of the limiting temperature assumed.

## Method
The authors used the Microcanonical Multifragmentation Model (MMM) to simulate the multifragmentation of the Xe+Sn system at 32 MeV/nucleon, varying the limiting temperature parameter τ that controls the maximum excitation energy allowed for primary fragments. They compared three cases: τ = 9 MeV (constant), τ = 5 MeV (constant), and a mass-dependent τ(A) = 4.3 + 18.3/√A − 19.9/A MeV, fitting the resulting charge distributions and kinetic energy distributions to experimental data from Frankland et al. (2001) to determine the freeze-out volume and source parameters.

## Key numbers
- For the Xe+Sn reaction at 32 MeV/nucleon, using a constant limiting temperature τ = 9 MeV gave a freeze-out volume of 8–9 V₀ (where V₀ is normal nuclear matter density volume), as previously reported by the MMM group.
- With the mass-dependent limiting temperature τ(A), the newly identified source parameters were: (A=220, Z=88), E_ex = 5.1 MeV/nucleon, V = 5 V₀, E_flow = 1 MeV/nucleon, α = 1.8, a freeze-out volume reduction to about half the previous value.
- The mass-dependent τ(A) ranges from 8.5 MeV for A=5 to 5.5 MeV for A=200.
- For the test system (230, 94) with E_ex = 5.3 MeV/nucleon and V = 9 V₀, the total multiplicity changed from 32.5 (τ=9 MeV) to 34.0 (τ(A)) to 38.4 (τ=5 MeV), while average Coulomb energy increased from 390.61 MeV to 413.33 MeV to 406.96 MeV respectively.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Frankland et al. (2001) on the Xe+Sn reaction at 32 MeV/nucleon, which used the MMM model with a constant limiting temperature of 9 MeV to obtain a freeze-out volume of 8–9 V₀. The authors show that incorporating a mass-dependent limiting temperature, motivated by phenomenological analyses of caloric curve plateaus by Natowitz et al. and mean-field calculations by Baldo et al., dramatically reduces the extracted freeze-out volume. This result highlights the model-dependence of freeze-out volume determinations and calls for model-independent experimental measurements of primary fragment excitation energies, such as those from relative velocity correlations by Houdan et al. which gave 2–3 MeV/nucleon for primary fragments in central Xe+Sn collisions.
