# nEoS: Neutron Star Equation of State from hadron physics alone

> We contribute a publicly available set of tables and code to provide Equations of State (EoS) for matter at neutron star densities. Our EoSes are constrained only by input from hadron physics and fundamental principles, without feedback from neutron star observations, and so without relying on General Relativity. They can therefore be used to test General Relativity itself, as well as modified gravity theories, with neutron star observables, without logical circularity. We have adapted state of the art results from NN chiral potentials for the low--density limit, pQCD results for the asymptotically high-density EoS, and use monotonicity and causality as the only restrictions for intermediate densities, for the EoS sets to remain as model-independent as is feasible today.

## Key claim

The authors provide a set of neutron star equations of state (EoS) constrained only by hadron physics (chiral perturbation theory at low density and perturbative QCD at high density) and the fundamental principles of causality and monotonicity, explicitly avoiding any input from neutron star observations or General Relativity; this allows the EoS to be used for tests of General Relativity and modified gravity theories without logical circularity, but the resulting uncertainty bands are deliberately broad because no astrophysical constraints are imposed.

## Method

The authors collected EoS data from several contemporary chiral perturbation theory calculations (ranging from NLO to N4LO) for the low-density regime up to about 200 MeV/fm3, and used the pQCD parametrization of Kurkela et al. (2014) for the high-density regime above baryon chemical potential 2.6-2.8 GeV. For the intermediate density region, they generated random interpolating functions on a logarithmic grid in energy-density/pressure space, accepting only those that satisfy causality (speed of sound less than c) and monotonicity (speed of sound non-negative), using Von Neumann rejection. The low-density and high-density bands were also randomly sampled with random walks imposing the same basic conditions, and the entire procedure was repeated for multiple chiral potentials and cutoff choices to produce 18 sets of 1000 EoS each.

## Key numbers

- Low-density chiral perturbation theory is used only up to an energy density of order 200 MeV/fm3 (0.2 GeV4), with one exception extending to 375 MeV/fm3
- The pQCD matching occurs at baryon chemical potential of either 2.6 GeV or 2.8 GeV
- The grid for intermediate densities uses about 50 points along the energy density axis, logarithmically distributed between 2.6 x 10^2 MeV/fm3 and 4.3 x 10^5 MeV/fm3
- The total set comprises 18000 equations of state, organized in subsets of 1000 EoS each
- For one comparison, subset (1a) yields neutron star radius R = (12.8 +/- 2.1) km for a 1.4 solar mass star, while subset (4a) yields R = (9 +/- 1.8) km
- The highest mass claim referenced is 2.27 (+0.17/-0.15) solar masses from Linares et al. (2018)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kurkela et al. (2014) and Annala et al. (2017) by constructing EoS from hadron theory alone without any astrophysical constraints, making them suitable for testing General Relativity itself rather than assuming it. The authors provide publicly available EoS tables and code at a dedicated website, enabling numerical relativity practitioners to test modified gravity theories with neutron star observables without circular reasoning. The work contrasts with data-driven EoS determinations that implicitly assume General Relativity through the use of Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations, and it identifies several avenues for future improvement including lattice gauge theory input, finite temperature effects for mergers, and inclusion of the outer crust EoS.
