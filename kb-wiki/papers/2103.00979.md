# Resolution of the spin paradox in the Nilsson model

> In the well-known Nilsson diagrams, depicting the dependence of the nuclear single-particle energy levels on quadrupole deformation, a spin paradox appears {as the deformation sets in, leading from spherical shapes to prolate deformed shapes with cylindrical symmetry}. Bunches of levels corresponding to a spherical shell model orbital, sharing the same orbital angular momentum and the same total angular momentum, appear to correspond to Nilsson energy levels, labeled by asymptotic quantum numbers in cylindrical coordinates, some of which have spin up, while some others have spin down. Furthermore, for some orbitals the correspondence between spherical shell model quantum numbers and Nilsson asymptotic quantum numbers is not the same for protons and for neutrons. Introducing a new rule of correspondence between the two sets of quantum numbers, we show that the spin paradox is resolved and full agreement between the proton and neutron Nilsson diagrams is established. The form of the Nilsson diagrams as a function of the quadrupole deformation remains unchanged, the only difference between the new diagrams and the traditional ones being the mutual exchange of the Nilsson labels for certain pairs of single-particle energy levels.

# Resolution of the spin paradox in the Nilsson model

## Key claim

The spin paradox in Nilsson diagrams, where bunches of levels sharing the same orbital angular momentum and total angular momentum correspond to asymptotic levels with mixed spin projections, and where the correspondence differs between protons and neutrons, is resolved by introducing an arbitrary rule equating the radial quantum numbers ($n_r = n_\rho$) and the orbital angular momentum with the sum of cylindrical quantum numbers ($L = n_z + \Lambda$), without changing the Hamiltonian or the shape of the Nilsson diagrams.

## Method

The authors re-examine the correspondence between spherical shell model quantum numbers ($N, L, j, \Omega$) and asymptotic Nilsson quantum labels ($\Omega[N n_z \Lambda]$) in the modified harmonic oscillator with axially symmetric quadrupole deformation. They impose a new mapping rule derived from equating the isotropic harmonic oscillator energy expressions in cylindrical and spherical coordinates, setting $n_r = n_\rho$ and $L = n_z + \Lambda$. They then diagonalize the full Hamiltonian matrix of the modified harmonic oscillator for the $N=2$ and $N=3$ shells as a function of the quadrupole deformation parameter $\delta$, using the standard Nilsson parameters $\kappa$ and $\mu$, and compare the resulting level labels to the traditional Nilsson diagrams from Nilsson (1969) and Ragnarsson (1970).

## Key numbers

- For the $N=2$ shell: the labels 1/2[200+] and 1/2[211−] are interchanged compared to the standard Nilsson diagrams, using constants $\kappa = 0.105$ and $\mu = 0$ (valid for both protons and neutrons).
- For the $N=3$ shell: the labels 1/2[310+] and 1/2[321−] are interchanged, and the labels 3/2[301+] and 3/2[312−] are also interchanged, using constants $\kappa = 0.090$ and $\mu = 0.25$ (valid only for neutrons).
- In the neutron shell with $N=82$–126, the parameters used are $\kappa = 0.0635$ and $\mu = 0.422$; in the proton shell with $Z=82$–126, the parameters are $\kappa = 0.0575$ and $\mu = 0.652$, giving a 40% difference in the $\kappa\mu$ product (0.0268 for neutrons versus 0.0375 for protons).
- The deformation parameter $\delta$ is defined as $\delta = (\omega_\perp - \omega_z)/\omega_0$, with small deformations $\delta \lesssim 0.1$, moderate $0.1 \lesssim \delta \lesssim 0.3$, and large $\delta > 0.3$.

## Context

This work extends the standard Nilsson model introduced by Nilsson (1955) and detailed in Nilsson and Ragnarsson (1995) and Ragnarsson (1970), which describes single-particle energy levels in deformed nuclei using asymptotic quantum numbers in cylindrical coordinates. The spin paradox, where spherical orbitals like 1g7/2 correspond to a mixture of spin-up and spin-down Nilsson orbitals in the traditional neutron diagrams from Firestone (1996), is resolved by the new labeling rule. The resolution enables identical Nilsson diagrams for protons and neutrons, whereas previously separate tables were needed for each, and it maintains the same energy-level dependence on deformation as the traditional approach, with only certain pairs of Nilsson labels interchanged.
