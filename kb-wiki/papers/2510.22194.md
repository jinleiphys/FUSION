# Enhancement of Alpha Decay due to Medium Effects

> We study the effect of medium on radioactive alpha decay and other similar decays. The initial state in these type of decays is a quasi-bound state with energy greater than zero. Such a state has very large amplitude in the nuclear region and is exponentially suppressed at larger distances. The decay rate of such states is known to decrease rapidly with decrease in the Q-value. Here we study such a decay within a medium. We assume a simple spherically symmetric repulsive potential model for the medium. This models the cumulative effect of all nuclei in the medium which at short distances present repulsive Coulomb interaction. We find that as the Q-value becomes very small, the medium effects lead to a substantial enhancement in rate. In contrast, for large Q-values, the medium effects are negligible. We briefly comment on application to real systems and the experimental implications of this result.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for alpha decay (and similar decays) with a very small Q-value, the decay rate can be substantially enhanced by a repulsive medium potential, whereas for large Q-values the medium effects are negligible; the enhancement arises because the medium potential distorts the quasi-bound state wavefunction, causing a large amplitude to develop outside the nuclear region.

## Method

The authors model the combined nuclear and medium potential as a spherically symmetric piecewise-constant potential with a time-dependent repulsive barrier representing the medium. They construct a wave-packet initial state centered on the resonant energy of the quasi-bound state and solve the time-dependent Schrodinger equation numerically to compute the evolution of the wavefunction as the medium potential is turned on. The decay is quantified by the change in probability amplitude at nuclear distances versus large distances after the medium potential reaches its final value.

## Key numbers

* The nuclear well depth is set to \(V_0 = 50\) MeV, the barrier height to \(V_1 = 100\) in atomic units (a.u.), the nuclear length scale \(L_n = 0.9566 \times 10^{-4}\) a.u., and the barrier length scale \(L_b = 0.1\) a.u.
* The medium potential parameters are \(L_c = 1\) a.u., \(L_d = 2.5\) a.u., final medium potential height \(V_{2f} = 1\) a.u., and the time to reach full potential \(T = 1\).
* At \(t=0\) (no medium), the wavefunction amplitude at \(r > L_b\) is of order \(10^{-26}\); at \(t = T\) (medium potential on), the amplitude at nuclear distances is of order \(10^{-26}\) while the amplitude at larger distances is somewhat larger, indicating a drastic reduction in probability at nuclear distances over the short time \(T=1\).

## Context

This work extends studies of environmental effects on nuclear decay rates, such as the observed dependence of alpha decay on lattice compression (Sikdar 2022) and unexplained changes in beta decay rates for strongly ionized nuclei (PhysRevLett.77.5190). The authors propose that their mechanism may explain the production of elements with lower atomic number than the parent in electrolysis experiments with nickel and palladium thin films (Miley 1996, Srinivasan 2020). The results suggest that for very low Q-value decays, applying an external repulsive potential or embedding nuclei in a condensed medium could accelerate decay, with implications for nuclear physics and nuclear astrophysics.
