# Transition to meson-dominated matter at RHIC. Consequences for kaon flow

> Anisotropic flow of kaons and antikaons is studied in heavy-ion collisions at CERN SPS and BNL RHIC energies within the microscopic quark-gluon string model. In the midrapidity range the directed flow of kaons v_1 differs considerably from that of antikaons at SPS energy (E_{lab} = 160 AGeV), while at RHIC energy (\sqrt{s} = 130 AGeV) the excitation functions of both, kaon and antikaon, flows coincide within the statistical error bars. The change is attributed to formation of dense meson-dominated matter at RHIC, where the differences in interaction cross-sections of kaons and antikaons become unimportant. The time evolution of the kaon anisotropic flow is also investigated. The elliptic flow of these hadrons is found to develop at midrapidity at times 3 < t < 10 fm/c, which is much larger than the nuclear passing time t^{pass} = 0.12 fm/c. As a function of transverse momentum the elliptic flow increases almost linearly with rising p_t. It stops to rise at p_t > 1.5 GeV/c reaching the saturation value $v_2^K (p_t) \approx 10%$.

# Transition to meson-dominated matter at RHIC. Consequences for kaon flow

## Key claim
At RHIC energy (√s = 130 AGeV), the directed flow of kaons and antikaons coincides within statistical error bars, whereas at SPS energy (Elab = 160 AGeV) they differ considerably; this change is attributed to the formation of dense meson-dominated matter at RHIC where differences in interaction cross-sections between kaons and antikaons become unimportant, though the authors note that the QGSM model does not assume QGP formation and the predicted antiflow of kaons at midrapidity is an order of magnitude stronger than the UrQMD model prediction.

## Method
The authors employed the microscopic cascade quark-gluon string model (QGSM), based on Gribov-Regge theory with string phenomenology, to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energy (Elab = 160 AGeV) and Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy (√s = 130 AGeV). The model includes string fragmentation, resonance formation, hadron rescattering, and incorporates multiple subprocesses including quark annihilation, colour exchange, multi-Pomeron exchanges, and hard gluon-gluon scattering, but neglects mean fields and spectator evaporation. The directed flow coefficient v1 and elliptic flow coefficient v2 of kaons (K+ + K0) and antikaons (K- + anti-K0) were calculated as functions of rapidity, transverse momentum, and time evolution from early stages (t = 3 fm/c) through freeze-out (t ≥ 60 fm/c at SPS, t ≥ 100 fm/c at RHIC).

## Key numbers
- At SPS energy (Elab = 160 AGeV), the directed flow of kaons and antikaons differs considerably in the midrapidity range
- At RHIC energy (√s = 130 AGeV), the excitation functions of both kaon and antikaon flows coincide within statistical error bars
- The nuclear passing time at RHIC is t_pass = 0.12 fm/c
- The elliptic flow of kaons develops at midrapidity at times 3 < t < 10 fm/c
- The elliptic flow saturates at pt ≥ 1.5 GeV/c reaching v2^K(pt) ≈ 10%
- The elliptic flow rises linearly with pt in the interval 0.25 ≤ pt ≤ 1.5 GeV/c
- At SPS energy, the passing time for central heavy-ion collisions is 1.44 fm/c
- The predicted antiflow of kaons in the central rapidity range at RHIC is an order of magnitude stronger compared to the signal predicted by the UrQMD model

## Context
This work extends studies of anisotropic flow in heavy-ion collisions from SIS and AGS energies (where in-medium kaon potentials from effective chiral models cause different K+ and K- flow, as studied by FOPI, KAOS, and E895 collaborations) to SPS and RHIC energies. The QGSM had previously predicted nucleon directed flow at SPS (Amprl91) and described elliptic flow of charged particles at RHIC (plb01) measured by STAR, PHOBOS, and PHENIX. The results enable experimental tests of whether dense meson-dominated matter forms at RHIC, with the authors noting that deviations from QGSM predictions could indicate new physics such as QGP formation not included in the model. The study contrasts with UrQMD model predictions (BlSt00) which found very flat kaon flow at midrapidity at RHIC, and with hydrodynamic predictions (KSH99) which would show continued linear growth of elliptic flow above pt = 1.5 GeV/c.
