# Time dependent signatures of core-collapse supernova neutrinos at HALO

> We calculate the response of a lead-based detector, such as the Helium and Lead Observatory (HALO) or its planned upgrade HALO-1kt to a galactic core-collapse supernova. We pay particular attention to the time dependence of the reaction rates. All reaction rates decrease as the neutrino luminosity exponentially drops during the cooling period but the ratio of one-neutron (1n) to two-neutron (2n) event rates in HALO is independent of this overall decrease. Nevertheless, we find that this ratio still changes with time due to the changing character of neutrino flavor transformations with the evolving conditions in the supernova. In the case of inverted hierarchy, this is caused by the fact that the spectral splits become less and less sharp with the decreasing luminosity. In the case of normal hierarchy, it is caused by the passage of the shock wave through the Mikheyev-Smirnov-Wolfenstein resonance region. However, in both cases, we find that the change in the ratio of 1n to 2n event rates is limited to a few percent.

## Key claim

The ratio of one-neutron (1n) to two-neutron (2n) event rates in HALO changes with time during a core-collapse supernova due to evolving neutrino flavor transformations, but this change is limited to a few percent in both normal and inverted mass hierarchies.

## Method

The authors performed dynamical, time-dependent calculations of neutrino flavor evolution by explicitly solving the evolution equations from the proto-neutron star surface to the supernova surface, incorporating collective neutrino oscillations and Mikheyev-Smirnov-Wolfenstein (MSW) resonances. They used the neutrino bulb model with single-angle approximation and included both the exponentially decreasing neutrino luminosity during the cooling period and the propagating shock wave in the mantle. The response of a lead-based detector was calculated for four different neutrino energy distribution models using charged-current and neutral-current reactions on lead-208.

## Key numbers

- Initial neutrino luminosities: Lν_α = Lν̄_α = 3.18 × 10^52 erg/s, with exponential decay time τ = 3.0 s
- Total gravitational binding energy released: E_B = 5.72 × 10^53 erg
- For inverted hierarchy, the low split energy E_IH is around 7-9 MeV for models I-III and 14 MeV for model IV; the high split energy E_IH' ranges from 35 MeV (model I) to 60 MeV (model IV)
- Reaction threshold energies: CC1n at 9.76 MeV, CC2n at 17.86 MeV, NC1n at 7.37 MeV, NC2n at 14.11 MeV
- HALO time resolution: approximately 200 μs for neutron thermalization and capture
- Detector mass considered: 1 kiloton of lead target

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of HALO event rates by Vaananen (2011) and Vale (2015), which used manual modifications of neutrino energy spectra to mimic flavor evolution and ignored shock wave effects, and Bandyopadhyay (2016), which used time-dependent luminosities but ignored collective oscillations and shock waves. The present study provides the first fully dynamical, time-dependent calculation of neutrino flavor evolution for HALO, enabling extraction of time-dependent signatures that could help determine the neutrino mass hierarchy and probe supernova dynamics.
