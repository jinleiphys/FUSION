# Primordial $α + d \to {}^{6}{\rm Li} + γ$ reaction and second Lithium puzzle

> During the Big Bang, ${}^{6}{\rm Li}$ was synthesized via the ${}^{2}{\rm H}(α,γ){}^{6}{\rm Li}$ reaction. After almost 25 years of the failed attempts to measure the ${}^{2}{\rm H}(α,γ){}^{6}{\rm Li}$ reaction in the lab at the Big Bang energies, just recently the LUNA collaboration presented the first successful measurements at two different Big Bang energies [M. Anders {\it et al.}, Phys. Rev. Lett. {\bf 113}, 042501 (2014)]. In this paper we will discuss how to improve the accuracy of the direct experiment. To this end the photon's angular distribution is calculated in the potential model. It contains contributions from electric dipole and quadrupole transitions and their interference, which dramatically changes the photon's angular distribution. The calculated distributions at different Big Bang energies have a single peak at $\sim 50^{\circ}$. These calculations provide the best kinematic conditions to measure the ${}^{2}{\rm H}(α,γ){}^{6}{\rm Li}$ reaction. The expressions for the total cross section and astrophysical factor are also derived by integrating the differential cross section over the photon's solid angle. The LUNA data are in excellent agreement with our calculations using a potential approach combined with a well established asymptotic normalization coefficient for ${}^{6}{\rm Li} \to α+d$. Comparisons of the available experimental data for the $S_{24}$ astrophysical factor and different calculations are presented. The Big Bang lithium isotopic ratio ${}^{6}{\rm Li}/^{7}{\rm Li} = (1.5 \pm 0.3)\times 10^{-5}$ following from the LUNA data and the present analysis are discussed in the context of the disagreement between the observational data and the standard Big Bang model, which constitutes the second Lithium problem.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the photon angular distribution from the \(^{2}\mathrm{H}(\alpha,\gamma)^{6}\mathrm{Li}\) reaction at Big Bang energies has a single peak near \(50^{\circ}\), and that using this optimal kinematic condition can significantly reduce the uncertainty of direct measurements compared to the LUNA experiment, while the LUNA data themselves are in excellent agreement with the authors' potential-model calculations.

## Method

The authors calculate the photon angular distribution for the direct radiative capture reaction \(\alpha + d \to {}^{6}\mathrm{Li} + \gamma\) using a potential model that includes electric dipole (E1) and electric quadrupole (E2) transitions and their interference. The initial scattering wave function includes spin-orbit interaction, and the overlap function for the bound state of \({}^{6}\mathrm{Li}\) is expressed using an asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) for the virtual decay \({}^{6}\mathrm{Li} \to \alpha + d\). The differential cross section is derived via multipole expansion of the vector potential and Siegert's theorem, and the total cross section and astrophysical \(S_{24}\) factor are obtained by integrating over the photon solid angle.

## Key numbers

- The calculated photon angular distributions at Big Bang energies have a single peak at approximately \(50^{\circ}\).
- The Big Bang lithium isotopic ratio derived from the LUNA data and the present analysis is \({}^{6}\mathrm{Li}/^{7}\mathrm{Li} = (1.5 \pm 0.3) \times 10^{-5}\).
- The first resonance in the \({}^{6}\mathrm{Li}(3^{+})\) state occurs at a relative \(\alpha\)-d energy of \(E = 0.712\) MeV.
- An earlier upper limit for the astrophysical factor was \(S_{24}(53\ \mathrm{keV}) < 2.0 \times 10^{-7}\) MeV b (from Ceccil, year not given in text).
- The standard Big Bang prediction for \({}^{7}\mathrm{Li}/\mathrm{H}\) based on nine years of WMAP results is \(5.13 \times 10^{-10}\), and based on Planck results is \((4.56 - 5.34) \times 10^{-10}\) (Coc 2014).
- Observed \({}^{7}\mathrm{Li}/\mathrm{H}\) in metal-poor halo stars is \(1.58^{+0.35}_{-0.28} \times 10^{-10}\) (Sbordone, year not given in text).
- The average \({}^{6}\mathrm{Li}/^{7}\mathrm{Li}\) isotopic ratio reported in nine stars by Asplund (2006) was approximately \(5 \times 10^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends the direct measurement of the \(^{2}\mathrm{H}(\alpha,\gamma)^{6}\mathrm{Li}\) reaction recently performed by the LUNA collaboration (Anders 2014), which was the first successful measurement at Big Bang energies after 25 years of failed attempts. The paper provides a theoretical framework to optimize future experiments by calculating the photon angular distribution, which was not discussed in the LUNA work. The results are relevant to the second Lithium puzzle, the discrepancy between the observed \({}^{6}\mathrm{Li}/^{7}\mathrm{Li}\) ratio in metal-poor stars (Asplund 2006) and standard Big Bang nucleosynthesis predictions (Coc 2014), and the analysis by Lind (year not given in text) using 3D non-local thermodynamic equilibrium models suggests that the observational support for a significant non-standard \({}^{6}\mathrm{Li}\) source may be weakened.
