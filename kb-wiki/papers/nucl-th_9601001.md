# Quasifree Eta Photoproduction from Nuclei

> Quasifree $η$ photoproduction from nuclei is studied in the Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (DWIA). The elementary eta production operator contains Born terms, vector meson and nucleon resonance contributions and provides an excellent description of the recent low energy Mainz measurements on the nucleon. The resonance sector includes the $S_{11}(1535)$, $P_{11}(1440)$ and $D_{13}(1520)$ states whose couplings are fixed by independent electromagnetic and hadronic data. Different models for the $ηN$ t-matrix are used to construct a simple $ηA$ optical potential based on a $t ρ$-approximation. We find that the exclusive $A(γ,ηN)B$ process can be used to study medium modifications of the $N^*$ resonances, particularly if the photon asymmetry can be measured. The inclusive $A(γ, η)X$ reaction is compared to new data obtained on $^{12}C$, $^{40}Ca$, and is found to provide a clear distinction between different models for the $ηN$ t-matrix.

## Key claim

Quasifree eta photoproduction from nuclei, studied in the Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (DWIA), can be used to probe medium modifications of nucleon resonances (particularly the S11(1535) and D13(1520)) and to distinguish between different models of the eta-nucleon t-matrix, provided that the photon asymmetry can be measured for exclusive reactions and that inclusive data are compared with distorted-wave calculations.

## Method

The authors developed a DWIA model for the exclusive A(γ,ηN)B reaction and the inclusive A(γ,η)X reaction. The elementary eta photoproduction operator included Born terms, vector meson exchange, and contributions from the S11(1535), P11(1440), and D13(1520) resonances, with couplings fixed by independent electromagnetic and hadronic data. For the final-state interactions, two different eta-nucleus optical potentials were constructed using a tρ approximation: DW1 based on the coupled-channel approach of Bennhold and Tanabe (1991), and DW2 based on the three-channel analysis of Batinic et al. (1995). Harmonic oscillator wavefunctions were used for bound nucleons, and a non-relativistic global optical model was used for the outgoing nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit pseudoscalar eta-NN coupling constant: gηNN²/4π = 0.4, determined from comparison with Mainz data on the nucleon.
- Scattering length for DW2 model: aη = (0.876 + i 0.274) fm; effective range: rη = (-1.682 - i 0.139) fm.
- For the inclusive reaction on 12C and 40Ca at Eγ = 750 MeV, the plane wave calculation over-predicts the data by about a factor of 2; the DW1 calculation agrees reasonably well, while the DW2 calculation underestimates the data by about 20 to 30%.
- Cross sections as large as around 0.2 μb/MeV·sr² can result at eta energies around 100 MeV for forward angles (θη < 30°).
- Total cross sections were calculated at three photon energies: 700 MeV, 750 MeV, and 780 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of quasifree pion photoproduction from nuclei (Wright et al., 1987) and provides a theoretical framework for interpreting new high-quality inclusive data from the TAPS collaboration at Mainz on 12C and 40Ca (Krusche et al., 1995; Robig-Landau et al., 1995). The model enables future exclusive measurements to test whether the masses of nucleon resonances (especially S11(1535) and D13(1520)) are modified in the nuclear medium, and it distinguishes between competing eta-nucleon scattering models (Bennhold and Tanabe, 1991 vs. Batinic et al., 1995). The work also relates to the question of possible eta-nucleus bound states, suggested by the attractive real part of the eta-nucleus optical potential at low energies.
