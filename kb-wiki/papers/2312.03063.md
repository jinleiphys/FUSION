# Real-time correlators in 3+1D thermal lattice gauge theory

> We present the first direct ab-initio computation of unequal-time correlation functions in non-Abelian lattice gauge theory. We demonstrate non-trivial consistency relations among correlators, time-translation invariance, and agreement with Monte-Carlo results for thermal equilibrium in 3+1 dimensions by employing our stabilized complex Langevin method. Our work sets the stage to extract real-time observables, relevant to quark-gluon plasma physics within a first-principles real-time framework.

## Key claim

The authors present the first direct ab-initio computation of unequal-time correlation functions in non-Abelian lattice gauge theory in 3+1 dimensions, using a stabilized complex Langevin method. The claim is hedged by the fact that the simulations are performed for SU(2) gauge theory in a weakly coupled regime away from the true continuum limit, with the extrapolation to the Schwinger-Keldysh contour requiring a polynomial extrapolation in the tilt angle \(\alpha \to 0^+\).

## Method

The authors discretize SU(2) Yang-Mills theory on a complex time contour (Schwinger-Keldysh contour) with a tilt angle \(\alpha\) that regularizes the path integral, using a lattice of size \(N_t \times 16^3\) with Wilson action and bare coupling \(g=0.5\). They solve the complex Langevin equation with a field-independent kernel \(\Gamma_0 = |a_t|^2 / a_s^2\), \(\Gamma_i = 1\), combined with gauge cooling and an improved Runge-Kutta update step, generating configurations for multiple tilt angles \(\tan\alpha = 1/3\) down to \(1/96\). Observables are computed by resampling onto a common discretization and extrapolating to \(\alpha \to 0\) via cubic polynomial fits.

## Key numbers

- Lattice size: \(N_s^3 = 16^3\) spatial points, with temporal points \(N_t\) ranging from 50 to 64 depending on tilt angle.
- Bare coupling: \(g = 0.5\); inverse temperature: \(\beta = 1/T = 1\); maximal real-time extent: \(t_\mathrm{max} = 1.5 \beta\).
- Tilt angles simulated: \(\tan\alpha = 1/3, 1/6, 1/12, 1/24, 1/48, 1/96\).
- Langevin time step: \(\epsilon = 10^{-4}\); configurations sampled between Langevin times \(\theta = 10\) and \(\theta = 20\) with spacing \(\Delta\theta = 0.1\).
- Number of independent simulations: 100 to 1000; gauge cooling step size: \(\alpha_{\mathrm{GC}} = 0.05\).
- Anisotropy: \(a_s / |a_t| \approx 16\) along the contour; temporal lattice spacings \(\tilde a \approx 0.0625\) and \(a_\tau \approx 0.0625\).
- Polyakov loop expectation value: \(\langle P \rangle \approx 0.98\); critical coupling for phase transition: \(g_\mathrm{crit} \approx 2\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous revision of the complex Langevin equation for non-Abelian gauge theories on complex time contours (Boguslavski 2022) to the first computation of unequal-time correlation functions. It contrasts with prior approaches that rely on spectral reconstruction and analytic continuation from Euclidean lattice gauge theory (Asakawa 2000, Meyer 2007, Burnier 2014, Altenkort 2022), which form an ill-posed inverse problem, and with classical-statistical lattice simulations (Boguslavski 2018, 2020, 2021) that are justified only far from equilibrium at weak couplings. The results enable future direct extraction of transport coefficients and spectral functions for quark-gluon plasma physics, and could be extended to non-equilibrium gauge theories and to SU(3) with further stabilization.
