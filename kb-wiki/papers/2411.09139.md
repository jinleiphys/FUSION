# Probing the QCD Critical End Point with Finite-Size Scaling of Net-Baryon Cumulant Ratios

> Finite-size scaling (FSS) is applied to net-baryon cumulant ratios $C_2/C_1$, $C_3/C_2$, $C_4/C_2$, $C_3/C_1$, and $C_4/C_1$ measured in Au+Au collisions over the Beam Energy Scan Phase~I range $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$--$200$~GeV to constrain the location and universality class of the QCD critical end point (CEP). Although finite-size and finite-time effects suppress non-monotonic signatures in unscaled data, the FSS analysis reveals a collapse of measurements from different beam energies and centralities onto universal scaling functions. All cumulant ratios collapse under a single, common set of critical exponents and exhibit divergence patterns characteristic o 3D Ising critical behavior. The scaling results indicate a CEP at $\sqrt{s}_{\rm CEP}\approx33.0$~GeV, corresponding to $μ_{B,\rm CEP}\approx130$~MeV and $T_{\rm CEP}\approx158.5$~MeV. These findings demonstrate that finite-size scaling provides a robust, model-independent framework for accessing critical behavior in finite, dynamically evolving systems, where non-equilibrium baryon-number transport can enhance the experimental visibility of susceptibility-driven fluctuations without modifying the underlying universality class.

## Key claim

Finite-size scaling of net-baryon cumulant ratios measured in Au+Au collisions reveals a collapse of data from different beam energies and centralities onto universal scaling functions consistent with 3D Ising critical behavior, indicating a QCD critical end point at approximately 33.0 GeV collision energy; however, the analysis relies on the assumption that finite-size effects dominate over finite-time effects, and the scaling collapse does not require visible non-monotonicity in the unscaled data.

## Method

The analysis applies finite-size scaling to cumulant ratios C2/C1, C3/C2, C4/C2, C3/C1, and C4/C1 measured by STAR in Au+Au collisions over the Beam Energy Scan Phase I range (7.7 to 200 GeV) across multiple centrality intervals. Critical exponents from the 3D Ising universality class (nu=0.630, gamma=1.237, beta=0.326, Delta=1.563) are used to rescale the data, with the characteristic transverse size L obtained from Monte Carlo Glauber simulations. Two complementary scaling variables are constructed: a field-driven variable h based on 1/mu_B and a density-driven variable t based on sqrt(s), both referenced to the extracted CEP location.

## Key numbers

- CEP collision energy: sqrt(s)_CEP approximately 33.0 GeV
- CEP baryon chemical potential: mu_B,CEP approximately 130 MeV
- CEP temperature: T_CEP approximately 158.5 MeV
- Critical exponents used: nu=0.630, gamma=1.237, beta=0.326, Delta=1.563 (3D Ising universality class)
- Beam energy range analyzed: sqrt(s_NN)=7.7 to 200 GeV
- Systematic uncertainties in freeze-out mapping lead to shifts of order +/-5 MeV in T_CEP and +/-10 MeV in mu_B,CEP

## Context

This work extends the finite-size scaling framework previously applied to susceptibility-related proxy observables by Lacey (2014) directly to net-baryon cumulant ratios measured by the STAR collaboration (2021). The analysis addresses the challenge that finite-size and finite-time effects suppress non-monotonic signatures in unscaled data, which has limited earlier attempts to identify critical behavior through background-subtraction approaches. The results provide a model-independent constraint on the CEP location that can be tested against future measurements from the Beam Energy Scan Phase II and compared with lattice QCD calculations, which currently yield weak constraints at comparable mu_B values due to finite volume and analytic continuation limitations.
