# Strong-Isospin Violation in the Neutron-Proton Mass Difference from Fully-Dynamical Lattice QCD and PQQCD

> We determine the strong-isospin violating component of the neutron-proton mass difference from fully-dynamical lattice QCD and partially-quenched QCD calculations of the nucleon mass, constrained by partially-quenched chiral perturbation theory at one-loop level. The lattice calculations were performed with domain-wall valence quarks on MILC lattices with rooted staggered sea-quarks at a lattice spacing of b=0.125 fm, lattice spatial size of L=2.5 fm and pion masses ranging from m_pi ~ 290 MeV to ~ 350 MeV. At the physical value of the pion mass, we predict M_n - M_p |(d-u) = 2.26 +- 0.57 +- 0.42 +- 0.10 MeV where the first error is statistical, the second error is due to the uncertainty in the ratio of light-quark masses, eta=m_u/m_d, determined by MILC, and the third error is an estimate of the systematic due to chiral extrapolation.

## Key claim
The strong-isospin violating component of the neutron-proton mass difference is predicted to be 2.26 ± 0.57 ± 0.42 ± 0.10 MeV at the physical pion mass, consistent with the phenomenological estimate of 2.05 ± 0.30 MeV, but the result carries a systematic error from truncation of the chiral expansion and depends on the MILC determination of the light-quark mass ratio η = m_u/m_d.

## Method
The authors performed fully-dynamical lattice QCD and partially-quenched QCD (PQQCD) calculations of the nucleon mass using domain-wall valence quarks on MILC lattices with rooted staggered sea quarks at a lattice spacing of b = 0.125 fm and spatial size L = 2.5 fm. They computed proton mass splittings at pion masses between approximately 290 MeV and 350 MeV, then extrapolated to the physical pion mass using partially-quenched heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (PQHBχPT) at one-loop order, fitting the counterterms and coupling constants to the lattice data.

## Key numbers
- Predicted strong-isospin violating neutron-proton mass difference: M_n - M_p |^(d-u) = 2.26 ± 0.57 (stat) ± 0.42 (quark mass ratio) ± 0.10 (chiral extrapolation) MeV at the physical pion mass of 140 MeV.
- Tree-level (O(m_q)) extraction gave M_n - M_p |^(d-u) = 1.96 ± 0.92 ± 0.37 MeV.
- Lattice pion masses used: m_π ≈ 294.2 ± 1.7 MeV (V1,V1;V1), 326.2 ± 1.6 MeV (V1,V2;V1), 354.4 ± 1.4 MeV (V2,V2;V1) on the b m_l = 0.007 ensembles; and 291.0 ± 2.1 MeV (V1,V1;V2), 323.7 ± 1.0 MeV (V1,V2;V2), 352.9 ± 1.8 MeV (V2,V2;V2) on the b m_l = 0.010 ensembles.
- Light-quark mass ratio used: η = m_u/m_d = 0.43 ± 0.01 ± 0.08 from MILC (Aubin 2004).
- Fitted isospin-breaking coefficient at NLO: (1/3)(2ᾱ - β̄) = 0.229 ± 0.058 in lattice units.

## Context
This work provides the first lattice QCD calculation of the strong-isospin violating component of the neutron-proton mass difference, which had previously been estimated only phenomenologically (Gasser 1982) as 2.05 ± 0.30 MeV by subtracting the electromagnetic contribution from the experimental mass difference. The result is consistent with that estimate and demonstrates that partially-quenched lattice techniques can extract isospin-violating quantities from isospin-symmetric lattices. This enables future precise determinations of the electromagnetic contribution to the neutron-proton mass difference and opens the possibility of computing charge-symmetry breaking contributions to nucleon-nucleon scattering using similar methods.
