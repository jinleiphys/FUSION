# Towards a metallurgy of neutron star crusts

> In the standard picture of the crust of a neutron star, matter there is simple: a body-centered-cubic (bcc) lattice of nuclei immersed in an essentially uniform electron gas. We show that at densities above that for neutron drip ($\sim4\times10^11$) g cm$^{-3}$ or roughly one thousandth of nuclear matter density, the interstitial neutrons give rise to an attractive interaction between nuclei that renders the lattice unstable. We argue that the likely equilibrium structure is similar to that in displacive ferroelectric materials such as BaTiO$_3$. As a consequence, properties of matter in the inner crust are expected to be much richer than previously appreciated and we mention consequences for observable neutron star properties.

## Key claim

The paper argues that at densities above neutron drip (~4×10^11 g cm−3), interstitial neutrons create an attractive interaction between nuclei that destabilizes the standard body-centered-cubic (bcc) lattice, leading to a richer equilibrium structure analogous to displacive ferroelectrics such as BaTiO₃, rather than the simple uniform lattice previously assumed.

## Method

The authors derived the thermodynamic stability condition for a two-component system of neutrons and protons (with electrons ensuring charge neutrality) using second-order energy density variations. They calculated the effective proton-proton interaction at finite wave numbers by combining screened Coulomb interactions with bulk nuclear matter derivatives from Lattimer and Swesty's microscopic calculations, and then determined the critical wave number for instability and the growth rates of the softest mode including shear elastic constants.

## Key numbers

- Neutron drip density: ~4×10^11 g cm−3 (roughly one thousandth of nuclear matter density)
- The induced interaction magnitude is typically no more than 0.2-0.3 times the direct interaction for bulk disturbances
- Shear elastic constants for the bcc Coulomb lattice: c₁₁-c₁₂ ≈ 0.10 n_i Z²e²/a and c₄₄ ≈ 0.37 n_i Z²e²/a
- The ratio (c₁₁-c₁₂)/2c₄₄ for a Coulomb crystal is ~0.13 (compared to unity for an isotropic solid)
- Shear elastic constant contributions are typically of order Z^(2/3)α ~ 0.1 times other terms in the instability condition

## Context

This work extends the standard picture of neutron star crusts (Haensel 2007) by showing that interstitial neutrons act like a second component in a binary alloy, analogous to spinodal decomposition in metallic alloys (Sedrakian 2005). The predicted phase transition has consequences for neutron star cooling (Yakovlev 2001), glitch phenomena (Andersson 2012, Chamel 2013), crust breaking in gamma-ray burst precursors (Troja 2010, Tsang 2012), and gravitational wave mountains on accreting neutron stars (Horowitz and Kadau 2009).
