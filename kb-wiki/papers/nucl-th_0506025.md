# Detecting QGP with Charge Transfer Fluctuations

> In this study, we analyze the recently proposed charge transfer fluctuations within a finite pseudo-rapidity space. As the charge transfer fluctuation is a measure of the local charge correlation length, it is capable of detecting inhomogeneity in the hot and dense matter created by heavy ion collisions. We predict that going from peripheral to central collisions, the charge transfer fluctuations at midrapidity should decrease substantially while the charge transfer fluctuations at the edges of the observation window should decrease by a small amount. These are consequences of having a strongly inhomogeneous matter where the QGP component is concentrated around midrapidity. We also show how to constrain the values of the charge correlations lengths in both the hadronic phase and the QGP phase using the charge transfer fluctuations.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that charge transfer fluctuations within a finite pseudo-rapidity window can detect a localized quark-gluon plasma (QGP) component concentrated at midrapidity, provided the detector coverage is comparable to the QGP extent; the signal depends critically on the difference between charge correlation lengths in the QGP and hadronic phases, which is expected to be a factor of 2 to 4.

## Method

The authors model the system using a two-component neutral cluster model, where the joint probability density for charged decay products factorizes into a cluster rapidity distribution and an exponential decay function with a charge correlation length gamma. They compute the charge transfer fluctuation D_u(eta) within a finite pseudo-rapidity window |eta| <= 1.0, using a hadronic correlation length gamma_1 = 1.75 fixed from HIJING simulations, and vary the QGP correlation length gamma_2 between 0.001 and 1.0. The QGP fraction p and the width sigma_1 of the QGP component are constrained by requiring that the model reproduces the STAR net charge fluctuation measurement kappa(1.0) = 0.27 for central collisions.

## Key numbers

- The hadronic charge correlation length is gamma_1 = 1.75, determined from HIJING simulations of Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV.
- For central collisions, the net charge fluctuation measurement from STAR gives kappa(eta_o=1.0) = 0.27.
- The predicted value of kappa(eta=0.0) for central collisions is approximately 0.35, compared to approximately 0.45 for peripheral collisions (HIJING, UrQMD, RQMD values).
- The predicted value of kappa(eta=1.0) changes from approximately 0.30 for peripheral collisions to 0.27 for central collisions.
- The rms width xi of the QGP component in pseudo-rapidity is always larger than 1.24 (since xi > gamma_1/sqrt(2) = 1.24), exceeding the STAR rapidity window size eta_o = 1.0.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous proposal of charge transfer fluctuations as a local observable for QGP detection (Shi 2005) by considering a realistic finite detector acceptance (STAR, |eta| <= 1.0) and detector efficiency effects. It contrasts with net charge fluctuations (Asakawa 2000, Jeon 2000) and balance function width (Bass 2000), which are only sensitive to QGP when the QGP fraction is substantial across the whole observation window. The paper enables experimental tests at RHIC to determine the fraction and spatial extent of deconfined matter, potentially explaining why net charge fluctuations have not shown a strong QGP signal despite theoretical expectations.
