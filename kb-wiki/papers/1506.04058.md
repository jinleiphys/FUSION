# Degeneracies of particle and nuclear physics uncertainties in neutrinoless double beta decay

> Theoretical estimates for the half life of neutrinoless double beta decay in candidate nuclei are affected by both particle and nuclear physics uncertainties, which may complicate the interpretation of decay signals or limits. We study such uncertainties and their degeneracies in the following context: three nuclei of great interest for large-scale experiments (76-Ge, 130-Te, 136-Xe), two representative particle physics mechanisms (light and heavy Majorana neutrino exchange), and a large set of nuclear matrix elements (NME), computed within the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA). It turns out that the main theoretical uncertainties, associated with the effective axial coupling g_A and with the nucleon-nucleon potential, can be parametrized in terms of NME rescaling factors, up to small residuals. From this parametrization, the following QRPA features emerge: (1) the NME dependence on g_A is milder than quadratic; (2) in each of the two mechanisms, the relevant lepton number violating parameter is largely degenerate with the NME rescaling factors; and (3) the light and heavy neutrino exchange mechanisms are basically degenerate in the above three nuclei. We comment on the challenging theoretical and experimental improvements required to reduce such particle and nuclear physics uncertainties and their degeneracies.

# Degeneracies of particle and nuclear physics uncertainties in neutrinoless double beta decay

## Key claim

The paper shows that within the QRPA framework, the main theoretical uncertainties in neutrinoless double beta decay half-life estimates, associated with the effective axial coupling g_A and the nucleon-nucleon potential, can be parametrized as NME rescaling factors, leading to a near-complete degeneracy between light and heavy Majorana neutrino exchange mechanisms in the three nuclei studied (76-Ge, 130-Te, 136-Xe), provided the g_A dependence of the NME is milder than quadratic due to the stabilizing role of 2νββ calibration.

## Method

The authors calculated 108 nuclear matrix elements (NME) within the QRPA with partial restoration of isospin symmetry, for three nuclei (76-Ge, 130-Te, 136-Xe) and two mechanisms (light and heavy Majorana neutrino exchange), using three values of g_A (0.80, 1.00, 1.27), two nucleon-nucleon potentials (CD-Bonn and Argonne), and three single-particle space sizes (small, intermediate, large). The g_pp parameter was fixed for each calculation by requiring that the theoretical 2νββ half-life equals the experimental value in each nucleus. The NME spread was then parametrized empirically in terms of a central value plus systematic biases from g_A variations and potential choice, plus residual uncertainties. Prospective half-life data were generated assuming a measured 76-Ge half-life of 10^26 years with ±20% experimental uncertainty, and a χ² analysis was performed to quantify degeneracies.

## Key numbers

- The phase-space factors G_i used are: 2.21×10⁻²⁶ y⁻¹ eV⁻² for 76-Ge, 1.33×10⁻²⁵ y⁻¹ eV⁻² for 130-Te, and 1.36×10⁻²⁵ y⁻¹ eV⁻² for 136-Xe (Table I)
- Current experimental lower limits on half-lives at 90% C.L.: 76-Ge > 3.0×10²⁵ y (GERDA+IGEX+HdM), 130-Te > 4.0×10²⁴ y (CUORE-0+CUORICINO), 136-Xe > 3.4×10²⁵ y (KamLAND-Zen+EXO-200) (Table I)
- Three g_A values used: 1.27, 1.00, and 0.80
- For the light mechanism (L), the empirical parametrization gives: α^L = 0.458, β^L = 0.021, σ^L = 0.032 (Table II)
- For the heavy mechanism (H), the empirical parametrization gives: α^H = 0.544, β^H = 0.089, σ^H = 0.025 (Table II)
- The β^j parameter (sensitivity to nucleon-nucleon potential) is about four times larger for the H mechanism (0.089) than for the L mechanism (0.021)
- From the fit to prospective data, the rescaled LNV parameter is: μ̄^L_c ± δ^L = -0.772 ± 0.029 for the L mechanism, and μ̄^H_c ± δ^H = -2.572 ± 0.027 for the H mechanism
- The total uncertainty on m_ββ for g_A in [0.80, 1.27] amounts to a factor of ~2, and for M_ββ to a factor of ~3
- The χ²_min for distinguishing L and H mechanisms is ~1.1, corresponding to a ~1σ difference

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Faessler et al. (2008, 2009) and Rotunno (2013) on the degeneracy between nuclear model uncertainties and lepton number violating parameters in 0νββ decay, and by Suhonen (2013) and Barea et al. (2013, 2015) on g_A quenching effects. The paper sharpens earlier findings by providing a quantitative parametrization of QRPA uncertainties including g_A < 1 cases, and demonstrates that the two mechanisms (light and heavy neutrino exchange) are phenomenologically indistinguishable at the ~1σ level in the three nuclei studied. The results highlight the need for dedicated theoretical and experimental studies to reduce g_A uncertainties and improve understanding of the nucleon-nucleon potential, as well as the importance of multi-isotope data to partially lift these degeneracies.
