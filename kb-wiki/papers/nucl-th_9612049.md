# Exclusive diffractive processes and the quark substructure of mesons

> Exclusive diffractive processes on the nucleon are investigated within a model in which the quark-nucleon interaction is mediated by Pomeron exchange and the quark substructure of mesons is described within a framework based on the Dyson-Schwinger equations of QCD. The model quark-nucleon interaction has four parameters which are completely determined by high-energy $πN$ and $K N$ elastic scattering data. The model is then used to predict vector-meson electroproduction observables. The obtained $ρ$- and $ϕ$-meson electroproduction cross sections are in excellent agreement with experimental data. The predicted $q^2$ dependence of $J/ψ$-meson electroproduction also agrees with experimental data. It is shown that confined-quark dynamics play a central role in determining the behavior of the diffractive, vector-meson electroproduction cross section. In particular, the onset of the asymptotic $1/q^4$ behavior of the cross section is determined by a momentum scale that is set by the current-quark masses of the quark and antiquark inside the vector meson. This is the origin of the striking differences between the $q^2$ dependence of $ρ$-, $ϕ$- and $J/ψ$-meson electroproduction cross sections observed in recent experiments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed differences in the $q^2$ dependence of $\rho$-, $\phi$- and $J/\psi$-meson electroproduction cross sections arise from the momentum scale set by the current-quark masses of the quark and antiquark inside the vector meson, and that confined-quark dynamics (rather than perturbative methods) play a central role in determining the behavior of diffractive vector-meson electroproduction cross sections, without requiring any quark-Pomeron form factor.

## Method

The authors construct a model where the quark-nucleon interaction is mediated by Pomeron exchange, using a Regge-like parameterization with a nucleon isoscalar electromagnetic form factor. The quark substructure of mesons is described using confined-quark propagators and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes obtained from phenomenological studies of the Dyson-Schwinger equations of QCD, which exhibit dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and confinement. The four parameters of the quark-nucleon Pomeron-exchange interaction ($\alpha_0$, $\alpha_1$, $\beta_u$, $\beta_s$) are completely determined by fitting to high-energy $\pi N$ and $K N$ elastic scattering data, and the model is then applied to predict vector-meson electroproduction observables without introducing any additional quark-Pomeron form factors.

## Key numbers

- $\alpha_0 = 0.10$ (taken from analysis of $pp$ and $\bar{p}p$ total cross section data, where the best fit value was $0.096^{+0.012}_{-0.009}$)
- $t_0 = 0.7$ GeV$^2$ (parameter in the nucleon isoscalar EM form factor $F_1(t)$)
- $\lambda = 0.566$ GeV (momentum scale used in the algebraic confined-quark propagator)
- Effective $u$-quark mass $M^u_Q \approx 330$ MeV and effective $s$-quark mass $M^s_Q \approx 490$ MeV (defined as the solution of $k^2 - B_f(k^2)/A_f(k^2) = 0$)
- $u,d$ quark bare mass $m_f = 5.1$ MeV; $s$ quark bare mass $m_f = 127.5$ MeV (from the confined-quark propagator parameters)
- The asymptotic behavior of the cross section follows $1/q^4$ for large $q^2$

## Context

This work extends the Donnachie-Landshoff (1984, 1987) Pomeron-exchange model by incorporating nonperturbative quark dynamics from Dyson-Schwinger equation studies, specifically using the confined-quark propagator and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes from Burden et al. (which successfully described $\pi$- and $K$-meson electromagnetic form factors). Unlike previous approaches by Laget and by Donnachie and Landshoff that required flavor-dependent quark-Pomeron form factors to fit data, this model obtains agreement with $\rho$-, $\phi$- and $J/\psi$-meson electroproduction data within a single framework. The work enables further investigation of whether Pomeron exchange can be interpreted as multiple-gluon exchange in QCD, and provides predictions for the ratio of longitudinal to transverse vector-meson electroproduction cross sections that differ from other models.
