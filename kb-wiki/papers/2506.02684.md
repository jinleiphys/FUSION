# Deformed magic numbers at $N=$178 and $Z=$120, 124 in the 112 $\leq N \leq $ 190 superheavy region from Skyrme mean-field calculations

> Background: Various motivations for exploration of superheavy region revolve around the question on whether 126 is a spherical proton magic number, as is the case for neutrons. In exploring this region, identification of nuclei with relatively longer half-life as compared to its neighbours is crucial for experimental studies. Such information is provided from theoretical predictions, which are however, heavily dependent on the theoretical model used and observable quantities under investigation. Purpose: Limiting ourselves to the Skyrme Hartree-Fock-plus-Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer approach, we aimed to analyse the appearance of a nuclear region with relatively high stability associated with emergence of spherical and deformed magic numbers in the region of $170 \le N \le 190$ ($112 \le Z \le 130$) based on various observables. Methods: Three Skyrme parametrizations namely the SkM* frequently employed for fission calculations, and the SLy5 and SLy4 commonly used for superheavy region, are considered to provide comparisons within the Skyrme mean-field approach. We evaluated the variation of electric quadrupole deformation ($β_{20}$), binding energy per nucleon ($BE/A$), two-nucleon separation energy differential ($δS2_{q}$), alpha-decay energy ($Q_α$) and alpha-decay half-lives ($T_{1/2}$). Conclusion: Our analyses suggest that neutron number $N = 178$ is candidate for deformed magic number around proton number $114 \le Z \le 118$. For protons, $Z = 120$ and $124$ appears to be a candidate for deformed magic number at around $N = 172 \sim 178$. Both sets of deformed magic numbers appear at oblate ground-state deformation.

# Deformed magic numbers at N=178 and Z=120, 124 in the superheavy region from Skyrme mean-field calculations

## Key claim

The authors suggest that neutron number N=178 is a candidate deformed magic number for proton numbers Z=114 to Z=118, and proton numbers Z=120 and Z=124 are candidates for deformed magic numbers around neutron numbers N=172 to N=178, but these conclusions are limited to the Skyrme Hartree-Fock-plus-Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer approach and depend on the specific Skyrme parametrization used (SkM*, SLy5, SLy4), with all identified deformed magic numbers appearing at oblate ground-state deformation.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme Hartree-Fock-plus-Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (SHF+BCS) calculations using three Skyrme parametrizations (SkM*, SLy5, and SLy4) for nuclei in the region 112 ≤ Z ≤ 130 and 170 ≤ N ≤ 190, with the single-particle wavefunction expanded on a harmonic oscillator basis of size N0=18. They evaluated the electric quadrupole deformation (β20), binding energy per nucleon (BE/A), two-nucleon separation energy differential (δS2q), alpha-decay energy (Qα), and alpha-decay half-lives (T1/2) using multiple empirical formulas including Viola-Seaborg, Parkhomenko-Sobiczewski, Royer, modified Royer, Brown, and modified Brown formulas.

## Key numbers

- Neutron number N=178 is identified as a deformed magic number candidate for proton numbers 114 ≤ Z ≤ 118, appearing at oblate ground-state deformation
- Proton numbers Z=120 and Z=124 are identified as deformed magic number candidates at around N=172 to N=178, appearing at oblate ground-state deformation
- The SkM* parametrization predicts Z=120 as deformed magic around N=172 with oblate shape, and Z=124 around N=176 with slightly oblate shape
- The SLy5 parametrization predicts Z=120 as deformed magic around N=176, and Z=124 around N=180
- The SLy4 parametrization predicts Z=114 around N=174 with prolate shape, Z=120 with N=174, and Z=122 with N=172 with oblate shape
- Basis size convergence test on 294Og shows Qα values: SkM* gives 12.17 MeV at N0=18 and 12.22 MeV at N0=22; SLy5 gives 11.40 MeV at N0=18 and 11.41 MeV at N0=22
- Averaged pairing strengths: SkM* Gn=14.79 MeV, Gp=13.72 MeV; SLy5 and SLy4 Gn=16.72 MeV, Gp=14.25 MeV
- Binding energy per nucleon differences between the authors' HF+BCS results and HFB results from Karim (2019) are on average about 14 keV for SkM* and SLy4, and about 25 keV for SLy5

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical predictions of magic and sub-magic numbers in the superheavy region, which have been heavily model-dependent and often provide conflicting results. Prior studies using various approaches such as Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (Rutz 1997, Bender 1999, Kruppa 2000), Relativistic Mean-Field (Patra 1999, Li 2014), and macroscopic-microscopic models (Wang 2015, Cui 2018) have predicted various candidates including Z=114, 120, 126 and N=172, 178, 184. The authors' cross-analysis of multiple observables (separation energies, single-particle levels, Qα values, and half-lives) enables identification of deformed magic numbers at N=178 and Z=120, 124 specifically at oblate deformation, which could guide experimental searches for relatively stable superheavy nuclei that serve as "stops" in the synthesis path toward Z=126.
