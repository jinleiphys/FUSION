# Parametrized Equation of State for QCD from 3D Ising Model

> The only first principle knowledge of the QCD equation of state at finite baryonic density is given from Lattice QCD as a Taylor expansion around $μ_B = 0$. The coefficients of such an expansion are currently available up to order ${\cal O}(μ_B^6)$. The expected critical behavior of QCD is in the same static universality class as the 3D Ising model. By means of a suitable parametrization for the scaling equation of state of 3D Ising and a parametrized map to connect to QCD, we present an equation of state matching first principle Lattice QCD calculations, which spans the values of baryonic densities explored in the BES-II program, and includes the correct scaling behavior in the proximity of the critical point. This EoS can serve as an important ingredient for the fluid dynamical simulations of heavy ion collisions at BES energies needed as a basis for the calculation of observables. Future comparisons between such calculations and BES-II data can constrain the parameters in the EoS -- including the parameters that describe the location of the critical point. This contribution reports on work done within the Fluctuations/Equation of State working group of the BEST Collaboration.

# Parametrized Equation of State for QCD from 3D Ising Model

## Key claim
The authors present a parametrized QCD equation of state that exactly matches lattice QCD results at zero baryochemical potential while incorporating critical behavior in the 3D Ising universality class, but the construction relies on a specific choice of parameters (including a critical point location at μ_BC = 350 MeV and T_C ≈ 143.2 MeV) that must be constrained by future comparisons with BES-II experimental data.

## Method
The authors use a parametric representation of the 3D Ising model scaling equation of state near its critical point, then apply a linear transformation with six parameters to map the Ising variables (magnetic field h, reduced temperature r) onto QCD coordinates (T, μ_B). The critical contribution to the QCD pressure is extracted from lattice QCD Taylor expansion coefficients up to O(μ_B^6), and the full pressure is reconstructed by adding this critical part to a regular part defined to match lattice results at μ_B = 0, with lattice data extended to low temperatures using the Hadron Resonance Gas model.

## Key numbers
- Critical point location set to μ_BC = 350 MeV and T_C ≈ 143.2 MeV, with the crossover transition temperature at μ_B = 0 given as T_0 ≈ 155 MeV and curvature κ ≈ -0.0149 from Bellwied (2015)
- 3D Ising critical exponents used with parametrization parameters a = -0.76201 and b = 0.00804 for the function h̃(θ), with θ bounded by |θ| ≤ θ_0 ≈ 1.154
- The mapping parameters are set to w = 1, ρ = 2, with angles α_1 ≈ 3.85° and α_2 ≈ 93.85°
- The parametrization of lattice/HRG data covers the temperature range T = 5–500 MeV using 5th-order polynomial ratios in inverse temperature
- The full pressure is shown for T = 50–500 MeV and μ_B = 0–450 MeV, up to order O(μ_B^4)

## Context
This work extends the approach of Nonaka (2004) and Guida (1996) for mapping the 3D Ising model onto QCD, and builds on lattice QCD Taylor expansion coefficients from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Bellwied 2015) and the HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov 2017). The resulting equation of state is designed for use in hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions within the BEST Collaboration framework, enabling future comparisons with BES-II data to constrain the location of the QCD critical point. The parametric nature allows systematic variation of the critical point location and other parameters, though the current choice of parameters is not unique and must satisfy thermodynamic consistency requirements.
