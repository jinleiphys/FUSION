# Onset of the Limit Cycle and Universal Three-Body Parameter in Efimov Physics

> The Efimov effect is the only experimentally realized universal phenomenon that exhibits the renormalization-group limit cycle with the three-body parameter parametrizing a family of universality classes. Recent experiments in ultracold atoms have unexpectedly revealed that the three-body parameter itself is universal when measured in units of an effective range. By performing an exact functional renormalization-group analysis with various finite-range interaction potentials, we demonstrate that the onset of the renormalization-group flow into the limit cycle is universal, regardless of short-range details, which connects the missing link between the two universalities of the Efimov physics. A close connection between the topological property of the limit cycle and few-body physics is also delineated.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the onset of the renormalization-group flow into the limit cycle is universal regardless of short-range details of the interaction potential, and that this onset point coincides with the universal three-body parameter, thereby connecting the discrete scale invariance of the Efimov spectrum with the experimentally observed universality of the three-body parameter measured in units of the effective range.

## Method

The authors performed an exact functional renormalization-group (FRG) analysis for identical bosons at the unitarity limit using a separable-potential model that reproduces the low-energy pair correlations of four different finite-range interaction potentials: Gaussian, van der Waals, Yukawa, and infinite square-well. They derived and numerically solved the exact RG flow equation for the dimensionless three-body coupling constant, defined as a rescaled particle-dimer scattering amplitude, and identified the onset of the limit-cycle behavior as the RG scale at which the first divergence of the coupling constant occurs.

## Key numbers

* The universal onset point of the limit cycle is found at \(kr_{\mathrm{eff}} = 0.49(4)\), which agrees with the universal three-body parameter \(\kappa^* r_{\mathrm{eff}} = 0.49\).
* Using the universal relation between \(\kappa^*\) and \(a_-\), the authors obtain \(a_- = -4.3\, r_{\mathrm{eff}}\).
* The universal scaling factor relating successive Efimov states is \(e^{2\pi/s_0} \simeq 22.7^2\), with \(s_0 \simeq 1.00624\).

## Context

This work extends previous RG analyses of the Efimov effect, which were limited to the zero-range model (e.g., Bedaque et al. (1999) and others) and treated the three-body parameter as an input, by incorporating finite-range interactions that are essential for understanding the universality of the three-body parameter. The results are consistent with experimental findings in ultracold atoms (e.g., Berninger et al. (2011), Wild et al. (2012), Roy et al. (2013)) and with numerical calculations by Wang et al. (2012) and Naidon et al. (2014). The work enables a deeper connection between the geometrical properties of the RG limit cycle and universal few-body physics, and suggests that the number of four-body bound states associated with each Efimov state may be understood as a topological winding number.
