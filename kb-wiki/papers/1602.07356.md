# Nuclear structure theory of the heaviest nuclei

> The current status of the application of covariant density functional theory to the description of actinides and superheavy nuclei is reviewed. The achievements and open problems are discussed.

# Nuclear structure theory of the heaviest nuclei

## Key claim
Covariant density functional theory (CDFT) provides a reasonable description of single-particle properties, fission barriers, and rotational excitations in actinides and superheavy nuclei, but the theoretical uncertainties in single-particle energies and shell-gap sizes are large enough to significantly affect the reliability of predictions for superheavy nuclei, especially because the shell gaps in that region are relatively small.

## Method
The authors review systematic calculations using ten covariant energy density functionals (CEDFs) including NL3*, DD-ME2, DD-MEδ, and DD-PC1, applied within relativistic mean field (RMF) and relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) frameworks. Single-particle spectra are computed for spherical superheavy nuclei, while fission barriers are evaluated in RMF+BCS and RHB calculations with axial, triaxial, and octupole degrees of freedom. Rotational properties of even-even and odd-mass actinides are studied using the cranked RHB plus Lipkin-Nogami (CRHB+LN) approach.

## Key numbers
- The Z=120 and N=172 shell gaps are especially pronounced in ²⁹²120; the Z=120 and N=184 gaps are reduced in ³⁰⁴120.
- Inner fission barrier heights in superheavy nuclei differ by up to 6 MeV between different models (macroscopic+microscopic, Skyrme DFT, and CDFT).
- RHB results for inner fission barrier heights are higher than RMF+BCS results by roughly 1 MeV.
- The peak in Qα curves at N=164 (reflecting the deformed N=162 shell gap) is seen in experimental data for Rf, Sg, Hs, and Ds isotopic chains.
- The Z=120, N≥174 nuclei are predicted spherical in NL3* but oblate in DD-PC1, contradicting expectations from spherical shell gaps.
- Rotational upbendings in even-even A≥242 nuclei are well described; similar upbendings predicted in lighter nuclei are not seen experimentally.

## Context
This review extends the systematic benchmarking of CDFT against actinide data (Afanasjev and Agbemava, 2014) to superheavy nuclei, and compares predictions with macroscopic+microscopic methods (Möller, 2009; Kowal, 2010) and Skyrme DFT (SkM*). The work identifies that available experimental data cannot distinguish between different model predictions for the center of the island of stability. The analysis enables future configuration assignments in odd-mass light superheavy nuclei (A∼255, Z≥102) based on rotational fingerprints, but notes that no CDFT studies of spontaneous fission half-lives exist yet, unlike in non-relativistic DFT.
