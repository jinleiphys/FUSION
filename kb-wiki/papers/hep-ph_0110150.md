# Color superconducting quark matter in compact stars

> When nuclear matter reaches a high enough density, we expect that the nucleons will overlap so much as to lose their separate identities, and merge into quark matter. In this talk I will review some theoretical expectations and speculations about quark matter, focusing on the phenomenon of quark pair condensation (color superconductivity) and its application to compact stars.

# Color superconducting quark matter in compact stars

## Key claim

Color superconductivity is expected to occur in quark matter at densities somewhat above nuclear density, but the observable consequences for compact stars remain speculative because the transition region between nuclear matter and quark matter could take the form of either a mixed phase or a sharp interface, depending on the QCD-scale surface tension, which is currently unknown.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical patterns of quark pair condensation (color superconductivity) in QCD at high density and low temperature, analyzing the symmetry breaking patterns for two-flavor (2SC) and three-flavor (color-flavor locked, CFL) cases, as well as the intermediate case with a massive strange quark. It then applies these results to compact star phenomenology by constructing phase diagrams in the chemical potential plane, comparing the free energies of mixed phases versus sharp interfaces, and calculating latent heat release during the nuclear-to-quark matter transition.

## Key numbers

- Quark pair condensate gap magnitude in the 2SC phase: around 100 MeV, roughly independent of the cutoff
- Strange quark mass: ~100 MeV; up and down quark masses: less than or equal to 10 MeV
- Compact star masses: close to 1.4 solar masses, with radii of order 10 km
- For the CFL phase, the neutrality line is at mu_e = 0
- The sharp interface between nuclear matter and CFL quark matter occurs at mu = 365 MeV
- If the QCD-scale surface tension sigma_QCD is about 10 MeV/fm^2, much of the mixed phase survives; for sigma_QCD greater than about 40 MeV/fm^2, the mixed phase is not favored for any mu
- The influence of color superconductivity on the equation of state is an O((Delta/mu)^2) effect, a few percent

## Context

This review extends the earlier work on color superconductivity from the 1970s (Barrois, Bailin and Love) and the revived interest in the late 1990s (Alford, Rajagopal, Wilczek; Rapp, Schafer, Shuryak). It discusses the quark-hadron continuity conjecture of Schafer and Wilczek (1999), which suggests the CFL phase may connect continuously to hypernuclear matter. The paper enables future work on compact star observables such as neutrino mean free paths, glitch mechanisms from crystalline superconductivity, and gravitational wave signatures from neutron star mergers.
