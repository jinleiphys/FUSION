# Time Scales in Many-Body Fast Neutrino Flavor Conversion

> Time scales associated with many-body fast neutrino flavor conversions in core-collapse supernova are explored in the context of an effective two-flavor model with axial symmetry. We present a preliminary study of time scales obtained from a linear stability analysis and from the distributions of Loschmidt echo crossing times (intimately connected to dynamical phase transitions in non-equilibrium systems) determined by time evolution with the exact many-body Hamiltonian. Starting from a tensor-product initial state describing systems of $N$ neutrinos, with $N/2$ electron-type and $N/2$ heavy-type, with uniform angular distributions, the Loschmidt echo crossing times, $t_{\mathcal{L}_{\times}}$, are found to exhibit two distinct time scales that are exponentially separated. The second peak structure at longer times, effectively absent for $N=4$, develops with increasing $N$. When re-scaled in terms of $\log t_{\mathcal{L}_{\times}}$, the distributions are found to become increasingly well described by the sum of two stable distributions. The distribution of Loschmidt echo crossing times differs somewhat from the results of the (numerical) linear stability analysis, which exhibits a peak at finite frequency and a second peak consistent with zero frequency. The exact analysis suggests that the zero-frequency instability manifests itself as a modest flavor-conversion time scale.

# Time Scales in Many-Body Fast Neutrino Flavor Conversion

## Key claim
The Loschmidt echo crossing times in a many-body neutrino system with axial symmetry exhibit two distinct time scales that are exponentially separated, with the second peak structure at longer times developing only for systems larger than N=4. The exact many-body analysis suggests that the zero-frequency instability identified in linear stability analysis manifests as a modest flavor-conversion time scale, differing from the linear stability analysis which shows a peak at finite frequency and a second peak consistent with zero frequency.

## Method
The authors performed exact many-body time evolution of N-neutrino systems (N=4 to N=14) using the Hamiltonian in Eq. (3) with axial symmetry, starting from a tensor-product initial state with N/2 electron-type and N/2 heavy-type neutrinos with uniform angular distributions. They computed Loschmidt echo crossing times from the time evolution and compared these with eigenvalues obtained from a linear stability analysis of the mean-field equations, using 10.5k independent random angular configurations per system size. For systems with N ≤ 10, exact evolution was performed using Mathematica; for larger systems, matrix product state simulations with Trotterization via the Time Evolving Block Decimation method were used.

## Key numbers
- For N=14 systems, two classes of unstable modes were identified with growth rates of either Γ ≈ 10⁻⁴–10⁻⁵ μ or Γ ≈ 10⁻⁹–10⁻¹⁰ μ from linear stability analysis
- The fraction of stable eigenvalues (with imaginary component Γ=0) decreases exponentially with system size
- Maximum evolution time interval of 50 (2N/μ) was used, with a Trotter step size of Δt = 0.05 (2N/μ)
- All distributions were formed from 10.5k independent samples of the coupling matrix J_ij for each system size
- The histogram bin width used was δw_× = 0.100 for the log-transformed crossing times

## Context
This work extends previous analyses by Roggero et al. (2021, 2022) that connected unstable collective modes to Dynamical Phase Transitions in simpler angular geometries, now generalized to the full multi-angle regime. The study uses the axially symmetric Hamiltonian model commonly employed in fast-flavor conversion studies (Padilla et al., 2021; Fiorillo et al., 2023) and confirms that this model is integrable as shown by Fiorillo et al. (2023). The results enable future characterization of how the two distinct time scales observed in the many-body treatment relate to the zero-frequency and finite-frequency instabilities identified in mean-field linear stability analyses.
