# A Complete Version of the Glauber Theory for Elementary Atom - Target Atom Scattering and Its Approximations

> A general formalism of the Glauber theory for elementary atom (EA) - target atom (TA) scattering is developed. A second-order approximation of its complete version is considered in the framework of the optical-model perturbative approach. A `potential' approximation of a second-order optical model is formulated neglecting the excitation effects of the TA. Its accuracy is evaluated within the second-order approximation for the complete version of the Glauber EA-TA scattering theory.

# A Complete Version of the Glauber Theory for Elementary Atom – Target Atom Scattering and Its Approximations

## Key claim

The authors develop a complete Glauber theory formalism for elementary atom (EA) – target atom (TA) scattering that accounts for all possible excitations of both the EA and TA in intermediate and final states, and show that for the DIRAC experiment's dimesoatom (DMA) – TA interactions, the coherent sector can be accurately described by a simplified version neglecting intermediate TA excitations, while the incoherent sector can be described by the Born approximation.

## Method

The authors formulate the full Glauber profile function for EA–TA scattering by introducing configuration spaces for the EA wave functions and the TA constituent wave functions, with a phase-shift function that accounts for the Coulomb interaction between EA constituents and TA electrons. They then expand the optical-model phase-shift function in a perturbative series, retaining terms up to second order, and express the first-order term Φ₁ and second-order term Φ₂ in terms of one-particle and two-particle TA form factors and correlation functions. The accuracy of the "potential" approximation (which neglects all TA excitations) is evaluated by comparing the full second-order expressions for coherent and incoherent total cross sections with their approximate forms.

## Key numbers

- The relative correction to the DMA–TA coherent interaction cross section from intermediate incoherent effects is of order Z³α⁴ ⟨r²⟩_EA / ⟨r²⟩_TA ln(⟨r²⟩_TA / ⟨r²⟩_EA) ≪ 1, where α is the fine structure constant and Z is the TA nuclear charge.
- The relative accuracy of the Born approximation for incoherent interactions is of order Zα² ⟨r²⟩_EA / ⟨r²⟩_TA ln(⟨r²⟩_TA / ⟨r²⟩_EA).
- The relative accuracy of the "potential" approximation for elastic scattering is (1/Z) ⟨s²⟩ / R²₊ ln(R²₊ / ⟨s²⟩), where ⟨s²⟩ is the mean squared EA size and R₊ is a characteristic TA radius.
- For the DIRAC experiment, the required accuracy for pionium (A₂π) excitation and ionization cross sections is 1% or better for target atom charge Z > 60.
- The declared accuracy in determining the A₂π lifetime using the "potential" approximation is 10%.

## Context

This work extends the conventional Glauber theory, originally developed for high-energy nuclear and particle physics (Glauber, 1959), to the scattering of relativistic hydrogenlike elementary atoms (such as pionium A₂π, A_πK, A_KK) by ordinary target atoms. It generalizes the eikonal approach of Tarasov and Heim (2000s) that neglected all TA excitations, and provides a rigorous framework for evaluating the incoherent scattering contributions that were previously estimated using the first-order Born approximation by Basel (2000s) and Pak (2000s). The formalism enables the DIRAC experiment at CERN to achieve the required 1% accuracy in cross sections for extracting dimesoatom lifetimes, and is applicable to a wide range of EA–TA systems including positronium (A₂e), muonium, and hadronic atoms.
