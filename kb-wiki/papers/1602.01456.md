# Coherent photoproduction of vector mesons in heavy ion ultraperipheral collisions: Update for run 2 at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> We make predictions for the cross sections of coherent photoproduction of $ρ$, $ϕ$, $J/ψ$, $ψ(2S)$, and $Υ(1S)$ mesons in Pb-Pb ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs) at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV in the kinematics of run 2 at the Large Hadron Collider extending the approaches successfully describing the available Pb-Pb UPC data at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV. Our results illustrate the important roles of hadronic fluctuations of the photon and inelastic nuclear shadowing in photoproduction of light vector mesons on nuclei and the large leading twist nuclear gluon shadowing in photoproduction of quarkonia on nuclei. We show that the ratio of $ψ(2S)$ and $J/ψ$ photoproduction cross sections in Pb-Pb UPCs is largely determined by the ratio of these cross sections on the proton. We also argue that UPCs with electromagnetic excitations of the colliding ions followed by the forward neutron emission allows one to significantly increase the range of photon energies accessed in vector meson photoproduction on nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors predict cross sections for coherent photoproduction of five vector mesons in Pb-Pb ultraperipheral collisions at the LHC run 2 energy of \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV, extending approaches that successfully described data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV. The predictions explicitly incorporate hadronic fluctuations of the photon and inelastic nuclear shadowing for light mesons, and leading twist nuclear gluon shadowing for quarkonia, with the caveat that the ratio of \(\psi(2S)\) to \(J/\psi\) cross sections is largely determined by the corresponding ratio on the proton.

## Method

The cross sections are calculated using the standard UPC formalism: the rapidity-dependent cross section is the sum of two terms, each being the product of a photon flux from one ion and the photoproduction cross section on the other ion. For light vector mesons (\(\rho\), \(\phi\)), the \(\gamma A \to V A\) cross section is computed using a modified vector meson dominance (mVMD) model combined with the Gribov–Glauber (GG) model of nuclear shadowing, where the photon fluctuates into eigenstates of the scattering operator with a distribution \(P_V(\sigma)\). For heavy vector mesons (\(J/\psi\), \(\psi(2S)\), \(\Upsilon(1S)\)), the \(\gamma A \to V A\) cross section is computed using leading-order (LO) perturbative QCD for charmonia and next-to-leading order (NLO) pQCD for bottomia, with nuclear gluon distributions from the leading twist nuclear shadowing model and EPS09 nuclear parton distribution functions. The photon flux includes the probability of no strong interaction via the Glauber model, and additional Coulomb excitations leading to forward neutron emission are treated by modifying the flux with probabilities \(P_i(b)\) for channels 0n0n, 0nXn, and XnXn.

## Key numbers

- Pb-Pb UPCs at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV: predictions for coherent photoproduction of \(\rho\), \(\phi\), \(J/\psi\), \(\psi(2S)\), and \(\Upsilon(1S)\) mesons.
- For \(\psi(2S)\) at central rapidity: \(d\sigma_{Pb Pb \to \psi(2S) Pb Pb}(y=0)/dy = 0.61\)–\(0.89\) mb, where the spread is from theoretical uncertainty in the leading twist nuclear shadowing model.
- Ratio of \(\psi(2S)\) to \(J/\psi\) cross sections at \(y \approx 0\): \(d\sigma_{\psi(2S)}/d\sigma_{J/\psi} = 0.17\)–\(0.20\), with lower and upper limits corresponding to \(\mu^2 = 3\) GeV\(^2\) and \(\mu^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\), respectively.
- For \(\psi(2S)\) photoproduction on the proton: \(R = \sigma_{\gamma p \to \psi(2S) p}/\sigma_{\gamma p \to J/\psi p} = 0.166\, (W_{\gamma p}/90\ {\rm GeV})^{\Delta\delta}\) with \(\Delta\delta = 0.24 \pm 0.17\) from HERA data (Adloff 2002).
- For \(J/\psi\) photoproduction on nuclei: the resolution scale \(\mu^2 \approx 3\) GeV\(^2\) is determined phenomenologically from HERA and LHCb data.
- For \(\Upsilon(1S)\): the resolution scale \(\mu^2 \approx M_{\Upsilon}^2/4 = 22.4\) GeV\(^2\) in the non-relativistic limit; NLO pQCD is required to describe the \(W_{\gamma p}\) dependence of \(\sigma_{\gamma p \to \Upsilon p}\) data from ZEUS, H1, and LHCb.
- The ALICE measurement at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV gave \(d\sigma^{\rm coh}_{\psi(2S)} = 0.83 \pm 0.19\) mb (stat + syst), and the ratio \(d\sigma^{\rm coh}_{\psi(2S)}/dy)/(d\sigma^{\rm coh}_{J/\psi}/dy) = 0.34^{+0.08}_{-0.07}\) (stat + syst), which disagrees with the authors' predictions of 0.38–0.56 mb and 0.15–0.16, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the approaches that successfully described Pb-Pb UPC data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV from the ALICE Collaboration (Adam 2015, Abbas 2013, Abelev 2012) to the run 2 energy of 5.02 TeV. For light mesons, it builds on the mVMD-GG model of Frankfurt (2015) and the two-state GVMD model of Frankfurt (2002, 2002) that described fixed-target and RHIC data. For heavy mesons, it uses LO pQCD for charmonia (Ryskin 1992, Guzey 2013) and NLO pQCD for bottomia (Jones 2013, Guzey 2013), with nuclear gluon shadowing from the leading twist model (Frankfurt 2011) and EPS09 nuclear PDFs (Eskola 2009). The predictions enable tests of inelastic nuclear shadowing in \(\rho\) and \(\phi\) photoproduction, constraints on gluon shadowing down to \(x \approx 5 \times 10^{-4}\) for \(\mu^2 = 2\)–\(20\) GeV\(^2\), and resolution of the disagreement between theory and ALICE data on \(\psi(2S)\) suppression at 2.76 TeV (Adam 2015).
