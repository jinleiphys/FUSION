# Hot Nuclear Matter in the Quark Meson Coupling Model with Dilatons

> We study hot nuclear matter in an explicit quark model based on a mean field description of nonoverlapping nucleon bags bound by the self-consistent exchange of scalar and vector mesons as well as the glueball field. The glueball exchange as well as a realization of the broken scale invariance of quantum chromodynamics is achieved through the introduction of a dilaton field. The calculations also take into account the medium-dependence of the bag constant. The effective potential with dilatons is applied to nuclear matter. The nucleon properties at finite temperature as calculated here are found to be appreciably different from cold nuclear matter. The introduction of the dilaton potential improves the shape of the saturation curve at T=0 and is found to affect hot nuclear matter significantly.

## Key claim

The inclusion of a dilaton field to realize broken scale invariance in the Quark Meson Coupling model improves the shape of the nuclear matter saturation curve at zero temperature and significantly alters the properties of hot nuclear matter, but the results depend on the chosen vacuum energy density of the dilaton field, taken here as epsilon_vac = (250 MeV)^4.

## Method

The authors employ a mean-field description of nonoverlapping nucleon bags (quark bags) coupled self-consistently to scalar and vector meson fields and to a dilaton (glueball) field that implements the broken scale invariance of QCD. The bag constant is treated as medium-dependent, and the effective potential including the dilaton is applied to symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature. Calculations are performed for several temperatures (cold, 50, 150, 200, and 240 MeV) as a function of baryon density rho_B, and the resulting dilaton field expectation value chi = phi/phi_0, effective nucleon mass M*_N, nucleon bag radius R, and the sigma (scalar) field are extracted.

## Key numbers

* The vacuum energy density of the dilaton field is taken as epsilon_vac = (250 MeV)^4 (used in Figures 1-4) and also (200 MeV)^4 (used in Fig. 5(a)).
* The dilaton field ratio chi = phi/phi_0 is plotted for baryon densities from 0 to 0.5 fm^{-3} at temperatures of cold, 50, 150, 200, and 240 MeV (Figure 1 and Fig. 5(a)).
* The effective nucleon mass M*_N (in MeV) is shown as a function of baryon density rho_B (fm^{-3}) for the same temperature set, with values ranging from approximately 1000 MeV at low density down to below 500 MeV at high density (Figure 2).
* The nucleon bag radius R (in fm) is plotted versus baryon density for the same temperatures, increasing from about 0.5 fm at low density to above 1.0 fm at high density (Figure 3).
* The scalar (sigma) field (in MeV) is shown as a function of baryon density, rising from near 0 MeV at zero density to above 60 MeV at the highest densities (Figure 4).

## Context

This work extends the Quark Meson Coupling (QMC) model, which describes nuclear matter in terms of nucleon bags exchanging mesons, by incorporating a dilaton field to account for the broken scale invariance of QCD. The approach contrasts with earlier QMC calculations that omitted the dilaton, and it shows that the dilaton potential improves the saturation curve at T=0 and has a significant impact on the temperature dependence of nucleon properties. The results enable future studies of hot nuclear matter and possibly neutron star matter within a quark-based framework that respects QCD scale anomaly.
