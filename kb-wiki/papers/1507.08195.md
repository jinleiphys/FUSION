# Rapidity dependence in holographic heavy ion collisions

> We present an attempt to closely mimic the initial stage of heavy ion collisions within holography, assuming a decoupling of longitudinal and transverse dynamics in the very early stage. We subsequently evolve the obtained initial state using state-of-the-art hydrodynamic simulations, and compare results to experimental data. We present results for charged hadron pseudo-rapidity spectra and directed and elliptic flow as functions of pseudo-rapidity for $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\,{\rm GeV}$ Au-Au and $2.76\,{\rm TeV}$ Pb-Pb collisions. The directed flow interestingly turns out to be quite sensitive to the viscosity. The results can explain qualitative features of the collisions, but the rapidity spectra in our current model is narrower than the experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors construct initial conditions for heavy-ion collisions by combining holographic calculations of the longitudinal and transverse dynamics under the assumption that these dynamics decouple in the very early stage (first 0.1-0.2 fm/c), then evolve them with viscous hydrodynamics; the resulting rapidity spectra are narrower than experimental data, and the directed flow is highly sensitive to the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio.

## Method

The authors model the initial stage of heavy-ion collisions using holography in N=4 super-Yang-Mills theory, assuming decoupling of longitudinal and transverse dynamics for the first 0.1 fm/c (LHC) or 0.2 fm/c (RHIC). They construct the energy density from the longitudinal holographic profile using Eq. (1) and the transverse flow velocity from Eq. (2) (v_i = -0.33 tau (partial_i e)/e), then initialize the viscous hydrodynamic simulation Music with these profiles at proper time tau_ini. The fluid is evolved with constant shear viscosity to entropy density ratios and converted to particles at a freeze-out temperature of 150 MeV using the Cooper-Frye formalism.

## Key numbers

- Initial proper time tau_ini = 0.1 fm/c for LHC (2.76 TeV Pb-Pb) and 0.2 fm/c for RHIC (200 GeV Au-Au) collisions
- Energy density rescaling factor of 20 for LHC and 6 for RHIC to match charged hadron multiplicities at mid-rapidity
- Freeze-out temperature of 150 MeV used for converting fluid to particles
- Impact parameter of 1 fm for central collisions and 8 fm for approximately 35% centrality collisions
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratios tested: eta/s = 0, 0.08, 0.12, and 0.2
- Initial rapidity profile width of 0.9 from holography (Casalderrey-Solana 2013), which widens substantially through hydrodynamic evolution

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of QGP thermalization including homogeneous and boost-invariant settings (Heller 2012, Chesler 2009, Heller 2011), planar shock wave collisions (Chesler 2010, Casalderrey-Solana 2013), and transverse expansion (van der Schee 2012, Habich 2014). It combines these lessons to construct initial conditions for the Music hydrodynamic simulation (Schenke 2010, 2011), enabling comparison of rapidity-dependent observables like directed flow to experimental data from STAR, ALICE, and CMS. The approach enables future studies including event-by-event fluctuations and finite coupling corrections to improve quantitative agreement with data.
