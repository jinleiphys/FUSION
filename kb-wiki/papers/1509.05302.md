# Production of antimatter $^{5,6}$Li nuclei in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV

> Combining the covariant coalescence model and a blast-wave-like analytical parametrization for (anti-)nucleon phase-space freezeout configuration, we explore light (anti-)nucleus production in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV. Using the nucleon freezeout configuration (denoted by FO1) determined from the measured spectra of protons (p), deutrons (d) and $^{3}$He, we find the predicted yield of $^{4}$He is significantly smaller than the experimental data. We show this disagreement can be removed by using a nucleon freezeout configuration (denoted by FO2) in which the nucleons are assumed to freeze out earlier than those in FO1 to effectively consider the effect of large binding energy value of $^{4}$He. Assuming the binding energy effect also exists for the production of $^5\text{Li}$, $^5\overline{\text{Li}}$, $^6\text{Li}$ and $^6\overline{\text{Li}}$ due to their similar binding energy values as $^{4}$He, we find the yields of these heavier (anti-)nuclei can be enhanced by a factor of about one order, implying that although the stable (anti-)$^6$Li nucleus is unlikely to be observed, the unstable (anti-)$^5$Li nucleus could be produced in observable abundance in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV where it may be identified through the p-$^4\text{He}$ ($\overline{\text{p}}$-$^4\overline{\text{He}}$) invariant mass spectrum. The future experimental measurement on (anti-)$^5\text{Li}$ would be very useful to understand the production mechanism of heavier antimatter.

# Production of antimatter \(^{5,6}\)Li nuclei in central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV

## Key claim
The enhancement of \(^{4}\)He yield over the exponential reduction trend seen in lighter nuclei can be explained by its large binding energy causing earlier freezeout; assuming the same binding energy effect applies to \(^{5}\)Li and \(^{6}\)Li, their yields are enhanced by about one order of magnitude, making the unstable \(^{5}\)Li potentially observable through the p-\(^{4}\)He invariant mass spectrum, although the stable \(^{6}\)Li remains unlikely to be detected.

## Method
The authors combined a covariant coalescence model with a blast-wave-like analytical parametrization of the (anti-)nucleon phase-space freezeout configuration. They first fitted the measured spectra of protons, deuterons, and \(^{3}\)He to extract a baseline freezeout configuration (FO1), then introduced a second configuration (FO2) with reduced transverse size and proper time to mimic the earlier freezeout expected for tightly bound \(^{4}\)He. The same FO2 configuration was applied to predict yields of \(^{5}\)Li and \(^{6}\)Li.

## Key numbers
- Midrapidity yield ratio \(^{5}\)Li/\(^{4}\)He: \(1.32 \times 10^{-3}\) (FO1, no binding effect) vs. \(11.7 \times 10^{-3}\) (FO2, with binding effect), an enhancement factor of about 8.77.
- Midrapidity yield ratio \(^{6}\)Li/\(^{4}\)He: \(1.67 \times 10^{-6}\) (FO1) vs. \(26.4 \times 10^{-6}\) (FO2), an enhancement factor of about 15.9.
- Midrapidity yield ratio \(^{5}\overline{\text{Li}}\)/\(^{4}\overline{\text{He}}\): \(0.99 \times 10^{-3}\) (FO1) vs. \(8.74 \times 10^{-3}\) (FO2), enhancement factor about 8.82.
- Midrapidity yield ratio \(^{6}\overline{\text{Li}}\)/\(^{4}\overline{\text{He}}\): \(0.94 \times 10^{-6}\) (FO1) vs. \(14.9 \times 10^{-6}\) (FO2), enhancement factor about 15.8.
- FO1 parameters: \(T = 111.6\) MeV, \(\rho_0 = 0.98\), \(R_0 = 15.6\) fm, \(\tau_0 = 10.55\) fm/c, \(\Delta\tau = 3.5\) fm/c, \(\xi_p = 10.45\), \(\xi_{\overline{p}} = 7.84\).
- FO2 parameters: \(T = 111.6\) MeV, \(\rho_0 = 0.98\), \(R_0 = 12.3\) fm, \(\tau_0 = 8.3\) fm/c, \(\Delta\tau = 3.5\) fm/c, \(\xi_p = 21.4\), \(\xi_{\overline{p}} = 16.04\).
- Binding energies: \(^{4}\)He = 28.296 MeV, \(^{5}\)Li = 26.330 MeV, \(^{6}\)Li = 31.994 MeV.
- \(^{5}\)Li half-life: about \(370 \times 10^{-24}\) s (111 fm/c).

## Context
This work extends the study of light antimatter production in heavy-ion collisions, following the STAR collaboration's observation of \(^{4}\overline{\text{He}}\) (Abelev et al., 2011) and the exponential reduction trend noted for p, d, and \(^{3}\)He yields. Previous coalescence model calculations (Xue et al., 2012) also underestimated the \(^{4}\)He yield. The authors propose a binding-energy-based freezeout modification to resolve this discrepancy, and they predict that the unstable \(^{5}\)Li may be observable at RHIC, offering a test of the production mechanism. Future measurement of \(^{5}\)Li would help distinguish between the binding energy effect and alternative mechanisms such as direct production from the correlated vacuum (Greiner et al., 1996).
