# Left-handed nuclei

> The orientation of the angular momentum vector with respect to the triaxial density distribution selects a left-handed or right-handed system principal axes. This breaking of chiral symmetry manifests itself as pairs of nearly identical $ΔI=1$-bands. The chiral structures combine high-j particles and high-j holes with a triaxial rotor. Tilted axis cranking calculations predict the existence of such configurations in different mass regions. There is experimental evidence in odd-odd nuclei around mass 134. The quantized motion of the angular momentum vector between the left- and right-handed configurations, which causes the splitting between the chiral sister bands, can be classified as tunneling (chiral rotors) or oscillation (chiral vibrators).

## Key claim

The paper claims that rotating triaxial nuclei can exhibit chirality, manifesting as pairs of nearly identical ΔI=1 rotational bands, but notes that the chiral symmetry breaking is weaker than in molecules because substantial tunneling between left- and right-handed configurations occurs, with the splitting between chiral sister bands classified as either tunneling (chiral rotors) or oscillation (chiral vibrators).

## Method

The authors performed Tilted Axis Cranking (TAC) calculations using a mean-field approximation to the two-body Routhian, allowing all orientations of the density distribution with respect to the rotational axis. They also studied the particle-rotor model with one high-j proton and one high-j neutron hole coupled to a triaxial rotor, analyzing classical orbits of the angular momentum vector on the intersection of the angular momentum sphere and energy spheroid. Self-consistent chiral solutions were found for specific nuclei, particularly 134Pr, using the hybrid version of TAC.

## Key numbers

- The critical angular momentum for the transition from chiral vibration to chiral rotation in the particle-rotor model is J_crit = 4√2/3 × j, which for h11/2 particles (j=5.5) gives J_crit = 10.3
- The static energy barrier between left- and right-handed sectors is in the order of 100 keV for J ≈ 15
- TAC calculations for 134Pr show maximal triaxiality of γ ≈ 30°
- TAC barriers between left- and right-handed sectors are in the order of 50-100 keV
- In 135Nd, a pair of negative parity bands come as close as 60 keV
- The chiral vibration frequency is less than 300 keV
- For 134Pr, the chiral character appears only for I > 15

## Context

This work extends the concept of chirality from molecular physics and particle physics to nuclear physics, building on the earlier suggestion by Meng and Frauendorf that rotation of triaxial nuclei may attain chiral character. The paper extends the Tilted Axis Cranking model developed by Frauendorf (2001) and applies it to predict chiral configurations in multiple mass regions. The experimental evidence discussed includes work on N=75 isotones by various groups (Bark, Starosta, Hecht, Hartley, all early 2000s), and the paper enables future microscopic calculations of tunneling between chiral configurations, which the authors note they have not yet been able to perform.
