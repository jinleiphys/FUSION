# The reactions $pp\to pΛK^+$ and $pp\to pΣ^0K^+$ near their thresholds

> The reactions $pp\to pΛK^+$ and $pp\to pΣ^0K^+$ are studied near their thresholds. The strangeness production process is described by the $π$- and $K$ exchange mechanisms. Effects from the final state interaction in the hyperon-nucleon system are taken into account rigorously. It is found that the $Λ$ production is dominated by $K$ exchange whereas $K$- as well as $π$ exchange play an important role for the $Σ^0$ case. It is shown that the experimentally observed strong suppression of $Σ^0$ production compared to $Λ$ production at the same excess energy can be explained by a destructive interference between $π$ and $K$ exchange in the reaction $pp\to pΣ^0K^+$. Implications of such an interference on the reaction $pp\to nΣ^+K^+$ are pointed out.

## Key claim

The experimentally observed strong suppression of Σ⁰ production compared to Λ production at the same excess energy (a factor of about 25) cannot be explained by final state interaction effects alone; instead, the authors conclude that a destructive interference between π-exchange and K-exchange mechanisms in the reaction pp → pΣ⁰K⁺ is the likely origin, though this conclusion depends on the assumption that π and K exchange are the dominant production mechanisms.

## Method

The authors describe strangeness production via π-exchange and K-exchange mechanisms and treat the final state interaction in the hyperon-nucleon system rigorously using the distorted wave Born approximation. They employ two microscopic YN interaction models (the Jülich model and the Nijmegen NSC97f model) and compute total cross sections near threshold, considering only s-waves for the outgoing particles and the lowest partial waves (³P₀ and ³P₁) for the initial pp system. The elementary amplitudes for KN scattering and πN → KY reactions are approximated using scattering lengths and on-shell threshold amplitudes, and the initial state interaction between the protons is neglected.

## Key numbers

- The experimental cross section ratio σ(pp → pΛK⁺) / σ(pp → pΣ⁰K⁺) at the same excess energy is about 25.
- At excess energies of 13.2 MeV (Λ) and 13.0 MeV (Σ⁰), the Jülich model with K-exchange alone yields a Born cross section ratio of about 16; after including diagonal FSI and ΣN ↔ ΛN conversion, the ratio increases further.
- For π-exchange alone, the Jülich model Born cross section ratio is 0.9.
- For the Jülich model with destructive interference between π and K exchange (the "K−π" case), the predicted cross section ratio σΛ/σΣ⁰ is in rough agreement with experiment; the absolute cross sections overestimate data by roughly a factor of 4 (normalized by factors 0.25 for Λ and 0.30 for Σ⁰).
- For the reaction pp → nΣ⁺K⁺ at 13 MeV excess energy, the Jülich model predicts cross sections of 86 nb ("K+π" case) and 229 nb ("K−π" case).
- The KN s-wave scattering lengths used are a⁰ = −0.038 fm and a¹ = −0.304 fm.
- The πN → KY threshold amplitudes used: f(π⁰p → K⁺Λ) = (−0.06 + i0.48) × 10⁻¹ fm, f(π⁰p → K⁺Σ⁰) = (0.45 − i0.35) × 10⁻¹ fm, f(π⁺p → K⁺Σ⁺) = (−0.11 + i0.21) × 10⁻¹ fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier model calculations of associated strangeness production in pp collisions (Laget, Li, Tsumura, Sibirtsev, Shyam) by focusing on threshold energies and rigorously including final state interactions using microscopic YN potentials from the Jülich group (Holzenkamp 1989) and Nijmegen group (NSC97 potentials). The study was motivated by the COSY-TOF measurement (Sewerin 1999) showing a factor-of-25 suppression of Σ⁰ relative to Λ production, which contradicted the factor-of-2.5 ratio seen at higher energies (Flammino 1986, Vigdor 1991). The results enable future measurements of the σΣ⁺/σΣ⁰ ratio near threshold at facilities like COSY to test the interference mechanism, and call for investigations of additional production mechanisms such as vector meson (ρ, K*) exchange.
