# Borromean structures in medium-heavy nuclei

> Borromean nuclear cluster structures are expected at the corresponding driplines. We locate the regions in the nuclear chart with the most promising constituents, it being protons and alpha-particles and investigate in details the properties of the possible borromean two-alpha systems in medium heavy nuclei. We find in all cases that the alpha-particles are located at the surface of the core-nucleus as dictated by Coulomb and centrifugal barriers. The two lowest three-body bound states resemble a slightly contracted $^{8}\text{Be}$ nucleus outside the core. The next two excited states have more complex structures but with strong components of linear configurations with the core in the middle. Alpha-removal cross sections would be enhanced with specific signatures for these two different types of structures. The even-even borromean two-alpha nucleus, $^{142}$Ba, is specifically investigated and predicted to have $^{134}\text{Te}-α-α$ structure in its ground state and low-lying spectrum.

## Key claim

Borromean two-alpha structures in medium-heavy nuclei are predicted to exist, with the alpha particles located at the surface of the core nucleus due to Coulomb and centrifugal barriers; the two lowest three-body bound states resemble a slightly contracted ⁸Be nucleus outside the core, while the next two excited states have strong components of linear configurations with the core in the middle.

## Method

The authors used the adiabatic hyperspherical expansion method to solve the three-body problem for a core plus two alpha particles, treating all particles as point-like with effective Woods-Saxon potentials for the alpha-core interaction and the Ali-Bodmer potential for the alpha-alpha interaction. The alpha-core potential depth was tuned to produce slightly unbound two-body subsystems, and the calculations were performed for the test system ¹⁴⁸Nd (¹⁴⁰Ba + α + α) and then for the Borromean candidate ¹⁴²Ba (¹³⁴Te + α + α). The angular part of the Faddeev equations was solved for fixed hyperradius ρ, producing effective adiabatic potentials, and the coupled hyperradial equations were then solved to obtain bound state energies and wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The alpha-alpha potential reproduces the measured ⁸Be resonance energy of 0.091 MeV, with a calculated root-mean-square radius of 5.95 fm.
- For the ¹⁴⁸Nd (¹⁴⁰Ba + α + α) system with V₀ = 28.8 MeV, four bound 0⁺ states are found at energies -4.9, -3.7, -2.6, and -0.8 MeV, with the ground state having an average α-α distance of 4.8 fm and an average α-core distance of 6.9 fm.
- For the same system, the 2⁺ ground state is at -4.5 MeV with average α-α distance 4.7 fm and α-core distance 7.0 fm; the 4⁺ ground state is at -3.5 MeV with average α-α distance 4.9 fm and α-core distance 7.0 fm.
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters used are: diffuseness a = 0.65 fm, radius R = 9.1 fm for A=140 (with r₀ = 1.16 fm, rπ = 1.4 fm, Rα = 1.7 fm), and Coulomb cutoff radius RC = 7.4 fm for core charge Z=56.

## Context

This work extends the study of Borromean nuclear cluster structures, previously confined mostly to light nuclei (reviewed by Riisager (2013) and Freer (2007)), into the medium-heavy region near the alpha-dripline. The authors use the liquid drop model to locate promising regions on the nuclear chart where two-alpha Borromean systems may occur, identifying ¹⁴⁰Ba, ¹⁴⁴Ce, and ¹⁴⁸Nd as candidate cores. The specific prediction for ¹⁴²Ba having a ¹³⁴Te-α-α structure in its ground state and low-lying spectrum provides a concrete target for future experimental searches via alpha-removal cross sections, which would show enhanced signatures with distinct patterns for the two types of structures (contracted ⁸Be-like versus linear core-in-the-middle configurations).
