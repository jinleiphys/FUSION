# Magicity of neutron-rich isotopes within relativistic self-consistent approaches

> The formation of new shell gaps in intermediate mass neutron-rich nuclei is investigated within the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory, and the role of the Lorentz pseudo-vector and tensor interactions is analyzed. Based on the Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation, we discuss in detail the role played by the different terms of the Lorentz pseudo-vector and tensor interactions in the appearing of the $N=16$, 32 and 34 shell gaps. The nuclei $^{24}$O, $^{48}$Si and $^{52,54}$Ca are predicted with a large shell gap and zero ($^{24}$O, $^{52}$Ca) or almost zero ($^{48}$Si, $^{54}$Ca) pairing gap, making them candidates for new magic numbers in exotic nuclei. We find from our analysis that the Lorentz pseudo-vector and tensor interactions induce very specific evolutions of single-particle energies, which could clearly sign their presence and reveal the need for relativistic approaches with exchange interactions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Lorentz pseudo-vector (pion) and tensor (rho-meson) interactions in relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory are essential for predicting new magic numbers in neutron-rich nuclei, but the authors hedge that the effect of these interactions is not reducible to the rank-2 tensor force alone; the central component of these Lorentz couplings plays a dominant or comparable role depending on the shell gap, and the origin of the N=32 and N=34 shell gaps is fundamentally different.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) calculations using the PKA1 effective Lagrangian (which includes both pi-pseudo-vector and rho-tensor couplings) and compared results with the PKO3 Lagrangian (which includes only the pi-pseudo-vector coupling) and with two relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) models (DD-ME2 and DD-ME-delta) that contain no tensor terms. The Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation was used to decompose the Lorentz pseudo-vector and tensor interactions into their central and rank-2 tensor components, and the evolution of single-particle energies and pairing gaps was analyzed along isotopic chains (Ca isotopes) and isotonic chains (N=16, 32, 34) from stability to the drip line.

## Key numbers

- The energy difference between nu2p_{1/2} and nu2p_{3/2} states at N=32 in Ca is 2.72 MeV for RHFB-PKA1, compared to 1.69 MeV for PKO3 and 1.76 MeV for DD-ME2 (Table 1).
- The energy difference between nu1f_{5/2} and nu2p_{1/2} states at N=34 in Ca is 2.45 MeV for RHFB-PKA1, compared to 0.77 MeV for PKO3 and 1.21 MeV for DD-ME2 (Table 1).
- The energy difference between nu1f_{5/2} and nu2p_{1/2} states at N=34 in Si is 4.05 MeV for RHFB-PKA1, compared to 2.72 MeV for PKO3 and 3.18 MeV for DD-ME2 (Table 1).
- The nu2p spin-orbit splitting enhancement from Ni to Ca along N=32 is about 1.2 MeV for RHFB-PKA1, about 0.5 MeV for RHFB-PKO3, and about 0.2 MeV for RHB-DD-ME2 and RHB-DD-ME-delta (Section 3.2).
- The experimental single-particle gap between nu1d_{3/2} and nu2s_{1/2} in O-24 is 4.86(13) MeV (Hoffman 2008), and RHFB-PKA1 predicts a value close to this, while PKO3, DD-ME2, and DD-ME-delta predict values well below the data (Section 3.3).
- The coupling between nu2p_{1/2} and nu2p_{3/2} states at N=32 is reduced by about 40% compared to values at N=30 and 34 in the RHFB-PKA1 calculation (Section 3.1).
- The central term of the Lorentz PV and T couplings contributes 70% of the increase in the nu2p spin-orbit splitting from Ni to Ca, while the rank-2 tensor contributes only 30% (Section 3.2).

## Context

This work extends previous non-relativistic studies of tensor interactions in Skyrme (Grasso 2014, Yuksel 2014) and Gogny (Anguiano 2012) forces, as well as M3Y-type interactions (Nakada 2008, Nakada 2010), by providing a fully relativistic treatment that includes both the central and tensor components of the Lorentz pseudo-vector and tensor couplings. The paper contradicts the assumption that the rank-2 tensor force alone drives shell evolution, showing instead that the central term from the same Lorentz vertices is often dominant. The results enable future experimental searches for new magic numbers in neutron-rich nuclei such as Si-48, Ca-52, Ca-54, and O-24, and provide a benchmark for comparing relativistic and non-relativistic approaches to tensor interactions in medium-mass exotic nuclei.
