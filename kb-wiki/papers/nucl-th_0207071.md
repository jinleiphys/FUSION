# Stability of strangelet at finite temperature

> Using the quark mass density- and temperature dependent model, we have studied the thermodynamical properties and the stability of strangelet at finite temperature. The temperature, charge and strangeness dependences on the stability of strangelet are investigated. We find that the stable strangelets are only occured in the high strangeness and high negative charge region.

## Key claim

Stable strangelets at finite temperature exist only in the region of high strangeness and high negative charge, extending the zero-temperature prediction of Greiner et al. (1997) to finite temperature using the quark mass density- and temperature-dependent (QMDTD) model rather than the MIT bag model.

## Method

The authors employed the quark mass density- and temperature-dependent (QMDTD) model, in which the vacuum energy density B(T) is a function of temperature with a critical temperature T_c = 170 MeV, and quark masses depend on both baryon number density and temperature. They calculated the free energy per baryon for strangelets with baryon numbers A=5 and A=10 at temperatures T=20 MeV and T=50 MeV, then tested stability against strong decay (single baryon emission conserving strangeness) and weak decay (nonleptonic decays with ΔS=±1) using free energy balance conditions.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for quark deconfinement: T_c = 170 MeV
- Bag constant at zero temperature: B_0 = 170 MeV fm^{-3}
- Current strange quark mass: m_{s0} = 150 MeV
- For A=5, T=50 MeV: only 4 out of 128 strangelets are completely stable (Z=-3,S=12), (Z=-4,S=13), (Z=-4,S=14), (Z=-5,S=15)
- Minimal strangeness S_c for metastability: S_c = 5 for A=5 at T=50 MeV; S_c = 10 for A=10 at T=50 MeV
- Critical strangeness fraction f_sc = 1 at T=50 MeV (independent of baryon number); f_sc = 2 at T=20 MeV
- Maximal charge Z_m = 1 for A=5 at T=50 MeV; Z_m = 6 for A=10 at T=50 MeV; Z_m = -3 for A=5 at T=20 MeV
- Stable radius decreases from 1.64 fm to 1.615 fm when strangeness changes from 2 to 9 (A=5, Z=1, T=50 MeV)
- Stable radius increases from 1.64 fm to 1.69 fm when charge rises from 1 to 8 (A=5, S=2, T=50 MeV)
- Free energy per baryon for S=3, Z=-2, A=5: F/A = 1064.9 MeV at T=20 MeV; F/A = 898.9 MeV at T=50 MeV

## Context

This work extends the stability analysis of strangelets from zero temperature (using the MIT bag model by Jaffe and co-workers, and by Greiner et al. (1997)) to finite temperature using the QMDTD model, which allows quark confinement to weaken with temperature unlike the permanent confinement of the MIT bag model. The results support the prediction of Greiner et al. (1997) that stable strangelets are highly negatively charged and have high strangeness, and show that higher temperature favors strangelet stability. This enables more realistic predictions for strangelet detection in relativistic heavy ion collision experiments where quark-gluon plasma forms at high temperature.
