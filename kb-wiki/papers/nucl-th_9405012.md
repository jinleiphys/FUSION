# Many-Body Currents and the Strange-Quark Content of 4he

> Meson-exchange current (MEC) contributions to the parity-violating (PV) asymmetry for elastic scattering of polarized electrons from $^4$He are calculated over a range of momentum transfer using Monte Carlo methods and a variational $^4$He ground state wavefunction. The results indicate that MEC's generate a negligible contribution to the asymmetry at low-$|\qv|$, where a determination of the nucleon's mean square strangeness radius could be carried out at CEBAF. At larger values of momentum transfer -- beyond the first diffraction minimum -- two-body corrections from the $ρ$-$π$ \lq\lq strangeness charge" operator enter the asymmetry at a potentially observable level, even in the limit of vanishing strange-quark matrix elements of the nucleon. For purposes of constraining the nucleon's strangeness electric form factor, theoretical uncertainties associated with these MEC contributions do not appear to impose serious limitations.

## Key claim

Meson-exchange current (MEC) contributions to the parity-violating asymmetry for elastic scattering of polarized electrons from 4He are negligible at low momentum transfer (where a determination of the nucleon's mean square strangeness radius could be carried out at CEBAF), but at momentum transfers beyond the first diffraction minimum, two-body corrections from the rho-pi "strangeness charge" operator enter the asymmetry at a potentially observable level even if the nucleon's strange-quark matrix elements vanish.

## Method

The authors calculated the parity-violating asymmetry for elastic scattering of polarized electrons from 4He over a range of momentum transfer using Monte Carlo methods with a variational 4He ground state wavefunction obtained from realistic interactions. They computed matrix elements of one-body and two-body (meson-exchange current) charge operators, including the isoscalar electromagnetic current and the strange-quark current, and evaluated the Coulomb multipole operators for the 4He ground state.

## Key numbers

- The 4He strangeness radius is two orders of magnitude more sensitive to the nucleon's strangeness radius than to two-body contributions.
- At higher momentum transfer, the rho-pi strangeness transition charge operator generates nearly a 15% contribution to the asymmetry at the kinematics of the experiment.
- The dipole form factor parameterization uses lambda_V^D = 4.97, lambda_n = 5.6, mu_p = 2.79, and mu_n = -1.91.
- The Sachs electric and magnetic form factors are defined with tau = q^2/(4 m_N^2) for elastic scattering in the Breit frame.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation (Musolf, Schiavilla, and Donnelly, 1994) which used a simple 4He shell model wavefunction and phenomenological two-body correlation function, now employing a variational wavefunction from realistic interactions and computing the asymmetry over the full momentum transfer range relevant to CEBAF experiments. The results enable interpretation of approved CEBAF experiments (experiments 24 and 25) that aim to constrain the nucleon's strangeness electric form factor, and show that theoretical uncertainties from MEC contributions do not impose serious limitations on such constraints. The only other observable with significant sensitivity to the rho-pi MEC is the B form factor of the deuteron (as noted in prior work).
