# Quark mass function from a OGE-type interaction in Minkowski space

> We present results for the quark mass function in Minkowski space calculated from an interaction kernel that consists of an effective one-gluon-exchange and a constant interaction. We analyze the gauge dependence of our results and compare them in the spacelike region to the available lattice QCD data.

## Key claim
The quark mass function calculated from a one-gluon-exchange (OGE) plus constant interaction kernel in Minkowski space, using the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST), is gauge-independent on the mass shell and shows very weak gauge dependence in the spacelike region, but the choice of gluon dressing functions introduces a discontinuity at p²=0 that vanishes only in Yennie gauge (ξ=3).

## Method
The authors solve the Dyson equation for the dressed quark propagator within the Covariant Spectator Theory, using an interaction kernel that combines an effective OGE interaction (with a regularized gluon propagator of finite mass M_g=0.6 GeV) and a constant interaction. The self-energy is evaluated in the quark rest frame, with loop momenta integrated by taking residues at the quark propagator poles. The model parameters are calibrated by requiring that the effective OGE strength ηα_s^r = 0.5 and by fitting the spacelike mass function to lattice QCD data from Bowman (2005) and Oliveira (2018).

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark mass m = 0.3 GeV (chosen value in the chiral limit m_0=0)
- Gluon mass parameter M_g = 0.6 GeV (fixed value for the dressed gluon propagator)
- Effective OGE strength ηα_s^r = 0.5 (maintained regardless of Λ_g choice)
- Scale parameter Λ_g values: 0.9 GeV for ξ=0 (Landau gauge), 0.6 GeV for ξ=1 (Feynman gauge), 0.45 GeV for ξ=3 (Yennie gauge)
- In the chiral limit with OGE kernel alone, Λ_g ≲ 2 GeV leads to unnaturally large α_s^r

## Context
This work extends the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) formalism, previously applied to mesons by Gross (1991), Biernat (2014), and Leitão (2017), by calculating the quark mass function from a consistent interaction kernel that includes both OGE and constant contributions. The results enable future meson calculations where the quark dressing and quark-quark interaction are fully consistent, and the spacelike comparison to lattice QCD data from Bowman (2005) and Oliveira (2018) provides a calibration benchmark. The gauge dependence analysis identifies Yennie gauge as the preferred gauge for avoiding discontinuities at p²=0.
