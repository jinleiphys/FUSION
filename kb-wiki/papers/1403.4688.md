# Isospin nonconserving interaction in the T=1 analogue states of the mass-70 region

> Mirror energy differences (MED) and triplet energy differences (TED) in the T=1 analogue states are important probes of isospin-symmetry breaking. Inspired by the recent spectroscopic data of 66Se, we investigate these quantities for A=66-78 nuclei with large-scale shell-model calculations. For the first time, we find clear evidences suggesting that the isospin nonconserving (INC) nuclear force has a significant effect for the upper fp shell region. Detailed analysis shows that in addition to the INC force, the electromagnetic spin-orbit interaction plays an important role for the large, negative MED in A=66 and 70 and the multipole Coulomb term contributes to the negative TED in all the T=1 triplet nuclei. The INC force and its strength needed to reproduce the experimental data are compared with those from the G-matrix calculation using the modern charge-dependent nucleon-nucleon forces.

## Key claim

For the first time, clear evidence is found that the isospin nonconserving (INC) nuclear force has a significant effect in the upper fp shell region (A=66-78), contrary to previous suggestions that only Coulomb and electromagnetic spin-orbit interactions were responsible for isospin-symmetry breaking in this mass region.

## Method

Large-scale shell-model calculations were performed in the pf5/2g9/2 valence space using the JUN45 effective interaction, with the SS method employed for diagonalization. The multipole Coulomb term, monopole Coulomb term, and electromagnetic spin-orbit single-particle energy shift were included, and a phenomenological INC interaction with J=0 pairing terms for pp, nn, and pn channels was added with isotensor strength β^(J=0)_(2) = 100 keV and isovector strength β^(J=0)_(1) = 300 keV to reproduce experimental CED, MED, and TED data for A=66.

## Key numbers

- Isotensor strength β^(J=0)_(2) = 100 keV and isovector strength β^(J=0)_(1) = 300 keV for the J=0 INC pairing interaction, chosen to reproduce A=66 data
- G-matrix isotensor strengths for modern charge-dependent forces: 225 keV (N3LO), 330 keV (CD-Bonn), and 621 keV (AV18), all two to six times larger than the phenomenological value of 100 keV
- For A=67 mirror pair 67As/67Se, recalculated MED with INC forces gave -45.2 keV for the 3/2- state and -30.8 keV for the 7/2- state, compared to experimental values of -43 keV and -50 keV respectively (improvement from previous results of -15.4 keV and -72.2 keV without INC)
- J=2 INC pairing calculation with β^(J=2)_(2) = β^(J=2)_(1) = -200 keV failed to describe MED for J=2 and TED for J=4, though it reproduced MED data for J=6

## Context

This work extends previous studies of mirror energy differences (MED) and triplet energy differences (TED) from the f7/2 shell region (reviewed by Bentley (2007)) and the upper sd shell (Vedora (2007)) into the upper fp shell region above N=Z=28. It contradicts earlier findings for the upper fp shell (Kaneko (2012), Ekman (2005), Andersson (2005)) which suggested that INC interactions were not needed, and instead shows that INC forces are essential to explain the new A=66 data from Ruotsalainen (2013) and Obertelli (2011). The results enable predictions for MED and TED in A=70, 74, and 78 nuclei, and raise the open question of why modern charge-dependent nucleon-nucleon forces (N3LO, CD-Bonn, AV18) cannot reproduce the phenomenological INC strengths, suggesting three-nucleon forces from chiral effective field theory as a possible solution.
