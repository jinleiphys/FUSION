# Collective and chaotic motion in self-bound many-body systems

> We investigate the interplay of collective and chaotic motion in a classical self-bound N-body system with two-body interactions. This system displays a hierarchy of three well separated time scales that govern the onset of chaos, damping of collective motion and equilibration. Comparison with a mean-field problem shows that damping is mainly due to dephasing. The Lyapunov exponent, damping and equilibration rates depend mildly on the system size N.

# Collective and chaotic motion in self-bound many-body systems

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a classical self-bound N-body system with two-body surface interactions exhibits a clear hierarchy of three well-separated time scales for the onset of chaos, damping of collective motion, and equilibration; however, the damping of the collective dipole mode is primarily due to dephasing rather than two-body collisions, and can be accurately captured by a mean-field model of non-interacting particles.

## Method

The author studies a classical Hamiltonian system of N particles in two dimensions with a hard-core two-body interaction (infinite repulsion at contact distance a, zero otherwise), corresponding to particles moving freely within a convex billiard in 2N-dimensional configuration space. The Lyapunov exponent is computed using the tangent map method, and the time evolution of the N-body system is performed by organizing collision times in a partially ordered tree with computational effort O(N ln N). Initial conditions for collective motion corresponding to the giant dipole resonance are prepared by drawing momenta from biased distributions (protons moving against neutrons), and the time evolution of the dipole moment D and the moments of the single-particle energy distribution are tracked to extract damping rates, periods, and equilibration rates.

## Key numbers

- Lyapunov exponent λ for N=3: 0.963 ± 0.004 (units of ħ/ma²), based on 70,000 trajectories
- Lyapunov exponent λ for N=500: 1.75 ± 0.02 (units of ħ/ma²)
- Damping rate γ for N=100: 0.39 ± 0.05 (units of ħ/ma²)
- Damping rate γ for N=500: 0.37 ± 0.04 (units of ħ/ma²)
- Period τ for all N from 100 to 500: 1.77 ± 0.01 to 1.77 ± 0.05 (units of ma²/ħ)
- Equilibration rate α for N=100: 0.21 (units of ħ/ma²)
- Equilibration rate α for N=500: 0.10 (units of ħ/ma²)
- Lyapunov exponent λ for collective mode at N=220: 1.61 ± 0.03 (units of ħ/ma²)

## Context

This work extends earlier classical models of nuclear equilibration and damping by Koonin and Randrup (1977), Blocki et al. (1978), and others, which typically coupled a moving wall to independent particles and lacked two-body forces and rotational symmetry. The model improves upon Bauer et al. (1994) by including a two-body surface interaction and constructing the collective mode from single-particle coordinates. The results enable a semi-quantitative comparison with experimental data on giant dipole resonances in heavy nuclei, showing that the damping width scales as γ ∝ N^{-1/3} and the frequency as ω ∝ N^{-1/3}, consistent with measurements by Speth and van der Woude (1981). The finding that damping is dominated by dephasing rather than two-body collisions suggests that mean-field approaches may be sufficient for describing collective damping, while equilibration requires explicit two-body interactions.
