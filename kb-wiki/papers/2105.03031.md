# Coulomb screening effect on the Hoyle state energy in thermal plasmas

> The first excited $J^π=0^+$ state of $^{12}$C, the so-called Hoyle state, plays an essential role in a triple-$α$ ($^4$He) reaction, which is a main contributor to the synthesis of $^{12}$C in a burning star. We investigate the Coulomb screening effects on the energy shift of the Hoyle state in a thermal plasma environment using precise three-$α$ model calculations. The Coulomb screening effect between $α$ clusters are taken into account within the Debye-Hückel approximation. To generalize our study, we utilize two standard $α$-cluster models, which treat the Pauli principle between the $α$ particles differently. We find that the energy shifts do not depend on these models and follow a simple estimation in the zero-size limit of the Hoyle state when the Coulomb screening length is as large as a value typical of such a plasma consisting of electrons and $α$ particles.

## Key claim

The energy shift of the Hoyle state due to Coulomb screening in a thermal plasma is independent of the choice of α-cluster model (shallow potential model or orthogonality condition model) and follows the simple zero-size-limit estimate \(12e^2/\lambda_D\) when the Debye screening length is much larger than the root-mean-square pair distance of the Hoyle state (approximately 6–8 fm), which holds for the plasma conditions typical of normal stars.

## Method

The authors solved the three-α Schrödinger equation using the stochastic variational method with fully symmetrized correlated Gaussian basis functions, incorporating the Coulomb screening effect via the Debye-Hückel approximation (screened Coulomb potential \(V^{\rm Coul}_{ij}(C) = (4e^2/r_{ij})\exp(-Cr_{ij})\)). They compared three different α-cluster models: two shallow potential models (Set 1 with only attraction; Set 2 with attraction plus short-range repulsion) and the orthogonality condition model (OCM), which treats the Pauli principle by projecting out Pauli-forbidden states with a large prefactor (10⁵ MeV).

## Key numbers

- The Q-value shift \(\Delta Q\) of the Hoyle state coincides with the simple estimate \(12e^2C = 12e^2/\lambda_D\) for Coulomb screening factors \(C \lesssim 0.2\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- For the plasma environment considered, temperature \(T \approx 10^8\) K and density \(\rho \approx 10^3\)–\(10^6\) g cm\(^{-3}\) give Debye screening lengths of order \(10^3\)–\(10^4\) fm (\(C = 10^{-3}\)–\(10^{-4}\) fm\(^{-1}\)).
- The root-mean-square pair distance \(d\) of the screened Hoyle states is approximately 6–8 fm for different models; at \(C = 0.1\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(d = 6\)–7 fm.
- At \(C = 0.01\) fm\(^{-1}\) (screening length 100 fm), the pair density tails are not much affected; at \(C = 0.1\) fm\(^{-1}\) (screening length 10 fm), the pair density at \(r \gtrsim 10\) fm is reduced; at \(C = 1\) fm\(^{-1}\) (screening length 1 fm), the Coulomb interaction is almost completely screened.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Phyu et al., 2020) by adding the orthogonality condition model (OCM) to test model dependence, following earlier OCM applications by Kato and Kato (2005, 2007) and comparisons by Pinilla et al. (2011) and Arai (2018). The results confirm that the energy shift is universal for weak screening as in normal stars, and the authors suggest future work on three-α systems in cold neutron matter, where the effective α-particle mass may change (Nakano et al., 2020).
