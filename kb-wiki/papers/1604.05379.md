## Key claim

The ratio of $\Lambda$ production with and without a beam proton diquark remnant follows a simple universal function $r = [\kappa/(y_p - y)]^i$ of the rapidity difference, valid over four orders of magnitude, but the fit quality depends on which experimental datasets are included, with significant tension between experiments leading to systematic uncertainties in the parameters.

## Method

The author decomposed measured $\Lambda$ and $\bar{\Lambda}$ rapidity distributions from proton scattering experiments into contributions from production mechanisms that either contain a diquark remnant of the beam proton (mechanism $\alpha$) or do not contain any beam remnant (mechanism $\beta$). By selecting events at rapidities where contributions from the second beam can be neglected, the ratio $r = n_{\Lambda\alpha1}/n_\beta$ was extracted from the measured $\bar{\Lambda}/\Lambda$ ratio $f$ using $r = 1/f - 1$, and then fitted with a power-law function of the rapidity loss $y_p - y$.

## Key numbers

- The universal function parameters from the preferred fit (omitting R-603 and R-607 data) are $\kappa = 2.86 \pm 0.03 \pm 0.07$ and $i = 4.39 \pm 0.06 \pm 0.15$, where the first uncertainty is statistical and the second systematic.
- The fit is valid over four orders of magnitude, from $r \approx 0.01$ to $r \approx 100$.
- The fit including all data gives $\kappa = 2.79 \pm 0.03$ and $i = 4.54 \pm 0.08$ with $\chi^2 = 637$ for 121 degrees of freedom.
- Fitting only the E8 Pb data where mechanism $\alpha$ dominates gives $\kappa = 2.93 \pm 0.15$ and $i = 4.06 \pm 0.30$ with $\chi^2 = 10.6$ for 13 degrees of freedom.
- Fitting all data with $y_p - y > 2.8$ where mechanism $\beta$ dominates gives $\kappa = 2.94 \pm 0.10$ and $i = 4.23 \pm 0.25$ with $\chi^2 = 37$ for 32 degrees of freedom.

## Context

This work extends the observation by LHCb and others (2014) that the $\bar{\Lambda}/\Lambda$ production ratio in proton scattering is a universal function of rapidity loss, independent of center-of-mass energy from 0.024 to 7 TeV, target type, and transverse momentum. The author provides a physical interpretation in terms of two competing production mechanisms and shows that the ratio of these mechanisms follows a simple power law, which should also apply to other baryons containing a beam diquark remnant such as $\Lambda_c$, $\Lambda_b$, and $\Sigma^+$.
