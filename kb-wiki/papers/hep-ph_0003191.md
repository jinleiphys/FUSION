# Signatures of the Induced $Θ$ vacuum state in Heavy Ion Collisions

> It was argued recently that, in general, an arbitrary induced theta vacuum state would be created in the heavy ion collisions, similar to the creation of the disoriented chiral condensatewith an arbitrary isospin direction. It should be a large domain with a wrong theta orientation which will mimic the physics of the world when the fundamental theta is non-zero. I suggest a few simple observables which can (hopefully) be measured on an event by event basis at RHIC, and which uniquely determine whether the induced theta vacuum state is created.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a large domain with a wrong induced theta vacuum orientation may be formed temporarily in heavy ion collisions due to non-equilibrium dynamics after the QCD phase transition, mimicking the physics of a world where the fundamental theta parameter is non-zero. The author acknowledges that such a stable theta vacuum state cannot be produced in heavy ion collisions, but expects that locally, for a short period of time, such a domain may be formed.

## Method

The author uses an effective chiral Lagrangian approach that reproduces the anomalous conformal and chiral Ward identities of QCD, with the effective potential for arbitrary theta given in terms of the unitary matrix U describing the phases of the chiral condensate. The properties of pseudo-Goldstone bosons in the theta vacuum background are derived by parametrizing the chiral matrix and calculating the mass matrix for pions, eta, and eta-prime mesons. The decay rates for eta to pi pi and eta-prime to pi pi in the theta vacuum are computed using PCAC techniques and low-energy theorems relating the topological charge density to chiral condensates.

## Key numbers

* The eta meson decay rate in the theta vacuum is estimated as Gamma(eta to pi pi) ~ 0.5 MeV times (sin(theta/2))^2, which would essentially determine the full width of the eta meson in the theta non-zero world.
* The eta-prime meson decay rate in the theta vacuum is estimated as Gamma(eta-prime to pi pi) ~ 2 MeV times (sin(theta/2))^2, which is almost an order of magnitude larger than the full width of the eta-prime meson in the theta=0 world (Gamma(eta-prime) ~ 0.2 MeV).
* The pion mass in the theta vacuum is given by m_pi^2 = (4/f_pi^2) (M_q |cos(theta/2)|), where f_pi ~ 133 MeV, and could be as low as 95 MeV for a certain value of theta rather than 135 MeV.
* The damping constant used in numerical simulations of the chiral field evolution is gamma ~ Lambda_QCD ~ 200 MeV.
* The expected lifetime of the induced theta vacuum domain is tau ~ (10-100 F) (Fermi).

## Context

This work extends the earlier proposal by Buckley, Fodor, and Zhitnitsky (1999) that induced theta vacuum states could be created in heavy ion collisions, similar to the creation of disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) with arbitrary isospin direction as discussed by Rajagopal (1995), Bjorken, Kowalski, and McLerran (1993), and Boyanovsky (1998). The author suggests observables that avoid the problem of final state interactions mimicking true CP violation, which was noted by Peccei as a difficulty with the CP-odd correlation signals proposed by Pisarski (1999). The work enables experimental searches at RHIC for signatures of CP violation in heavy ion collisions that would indicate the formation of a new ground state of matter.
