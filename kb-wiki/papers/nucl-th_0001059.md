# alpha-nucleus potentials for the neutron-deficient p nuclei

> alpha-nucleus potentials are one important ingredient for the understanding of the nucleosynthesis of heavy neutron-deficient p nuclei in the astrophysical gamma-process where these p nuclei are produced by a series of (gamma,n), (gamma,p), and (gamma,alpha) reactions. I present an improved alpha-nucleus potential at the astrophysically relevant sub-Coulomb energies which is derived from the analysis of alpha decay data and from a previously established systematic behavior of double-folding potentials.

## Key claim

An improved alpha-nucleus potential for the astrophysically relevant sub-Coulomb energy region (about 5 to 12 MeV) is derived from double-folding analysis of alpha decay data, but the potential is only well-defined for the real part; the imaginary part remains unconstrained by experiment at very low energies.

## Method

Double-folding potentials were calculated using the DDM3Y effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, with nuclear densities taken from experimental charge density distributions. The strength parameter lambda of the folding potential was adjusted to reproduce the alpha decay energy of each alpha emitter, and the semi-classical model of Gurvitz and Kalbermann (1987) was used to calculate decay widths and preformation factors P from the ratio of calculated to experimental half-lives.

## Key numbers

- Volume integrals J_R for the real part of the potential range from about 285 to 330 MeV fm^3 for nuclei with A >= 140, with a recommended interpolation formula: J_R(E) = 350 MeV fm^3 x exp[-(E-30 MeV)^2/(75 MeV)^2], valid from very low energies up to about 40 MeV
- The energy dependence of J_R at low energies is Delta J_R/Delta E approximately 1.7 MeV fm^3/MeV
- The uncertainty of an interpolated J_R value is significantly smaller than 10 MeV fm^3 (about 3%) in the interesting energy range around 10 MeV
- Preformation factors P range from 0.62% for Po-210 to 64.6% for Be-8, with local maxima around magic neutron numbers N=82 and N=126
- For the system Po-212 = Pb-208 + alpha, the calculated half-life is 9.20 x 10^-9 s compared to the experimental (2.99 +/- 0.02) x 10^-7 s, giving P = 3.08%
- For the system Gd-148 = Sm-144 + alpha, the calculated half-life is 6.20 y compared to the experimental 74.6 +/- 3.0 y, giving P = 8.31%

## Context

This work extends the systematic double-folding potential studies of Atzrott et al. (1996) from higher scattering energies down to the sub-Coulomb region relevant for the astrophysical gamma-process. The derived potential addresses the need for improved alpha-nucleus potentials highlighted by Rauscher (1998) and Grama and Goriely (1998), and provides an alternative to the specially shaped cosh potential of Buck et al. (1993) which gave preformation factors of unity but could not reproduce precision scattering data. The results enable more reliable calculation of (gamma,alpha) reaction rates for p-nuclei nucleosynthesis, particularly for the branching nucleus Gd-148 which affects the Sm-146/Sm-144 chronometer ratio.
