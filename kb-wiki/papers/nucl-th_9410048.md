# Auxiliary potential in no-core shell-model calculations

> The Lee-Suzuki iteration method is used to include the folded diagrams in the calculation of the two-body effective interaction $v^{(2)}_{\rm eff}$ between two nucleons in a no-core model space. This effective interaction still depends upon the choice of single-particle basis utilized in the shell-model calculation. Using a harmonic-oscillator single-particle basis and the Reid-soft-core {\it NN} potential, we find that $v^{(2)}_{\rm eff}$ overbinds $^4\mbox{He}$ in 0, 2, and $4\hbarΩ$ model spaces. As the size of the model space increases, the amount of overbinding decreases significantly. This problem of overbinding in small model spaces is due to neglecting effective three- and four-body forces. Contributions of effective many-body forces are suppressed by using the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock single-particle Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The Lee-Suzuki iteration method yields a starting-energy-independent two-body effective interaction \(v^{(2)}_{\rm eff}\) for no-core shell-model calculations, but this interaction still overbinds \(^4\)He in small harmonic-oscillator model spaces (0, 2, and \(4\hbar\Omega\)) because effective three- and four-body forces are neglected; the overbinding can be suppressed by introducing a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) auxiliary single-particle potential.

## Method

The authors used the Lee-Suzuki iteration method to sum folded diagrams to all orders, producing a starting-energy-independent two-body effective interaction \(v^{(2)}_{\rm eff}\) from the Reid-soft-core \(NN\) potential. They performed no-core shell-model diagonalizations for \(^4\)He in three harmonic-oscillator model spaces (\(0\hbar\Omega\), \(2\hbar\Omega\), and \(4\hbar\Omega\)) with \(\hbar\Omega\) ranging from 10 to 28 MeV. They then introduced a BHF auxiliary single-particle potential for the active states within the model space to suppress the contributions of effective many-body forces.

## Key numbers

- In the \(0\hbar\Omega\) model space, the ground-state energy of \(^4\)He ranges from -33.9 MeV (at \(\hbar\Omega=10\) MeV) to a minimum of -43.3 MeV (at \(\hbar\Omega=22\) MeV), overbinding the experimental value of -28.3 MeV by 5.6 to 15.0 MeV.
- In the \(2\hbar\Omega\) model space, the lowest ground-state energy is -33.6 MeV (at \(\hbar\Omega=16\) MeV), still overbinding the experimental value.
- In the \(4\hbar\Omega\) model space, the lowest ground-state energy is -31.9 MeV (at \(\hbar\Omega=14\)-16 MeV), overbinding the experimental value by 3.6 MeV and the Green's function Monte Carlo result of -24.6 MeV by 7.3 MeV.
- With the BHF auxiliary potential in the \(0\hbar\Omega\) model space, the minimal ground-state energy is -17.9 MeV (at \(\hbar\Omega=16\) MeV), which is above the exact result.
- The "average curvature" parameter \(C_a\) decreases from 0.61 for \(N=0\) to 0.39 for \(N=2\) and 0.27 for \(N=4\).

## Context

This work extends the no-core approach of Zheng, Vary, and Barrett (1993) and the studies of effective many-body forces by Muther, Polls, and collaborators to realistic \(NN\) potentials, specifically the Reid-soft-core potential. It demonstrates that the overbinding problem in small model spaces, previously noted by Zheng et al. (1993), arises from neglected effective three- and four-body forces, and that introducing a BHF auxiliary potential can significantly reduce this overbinding. The results enable future studies to search for an optimal auxiliary potential that minimizes the effects of many-body forces in no-core shell-model calculations.
