# Modification of the pi-omega-rho vertex in nuclear medium and its influence on the dilepton production rate in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> In this PhD thesis we analyze the medium modification of the pi-omega-rho coupling and subsequently discuss its possible influence on the dilepton production rate in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The first part of the thesis is devoted to the medium modifications of the Dalitz decays from the omega and rho mesons in nuclear matter. We use the relativistic field theory formalism describing modifications of the coupling by one-loop Feynman diagrams with nucleons and Delta(1232) isobars. We consider cases when the decaying particle is at rest or at motion with respect to the medium. We investigate the dependence of medium effects on the dilepton invariant mass and observe a sizeable increase of the effective coupling constants compared to their vacuum values. The effect grows with density. In the second part of the thesis we apply our model to evaluate the influence of the medium effects on the dilepton production from the Dalitz decays. In the calculations of the dilepton spectrum we use the model of the hydrodynamic expansion of the fire cylinder. In our comparison to the CERES dilepton data we take into account the experimental cuts. The medium modifications enhance the effect of the Dalitz decays. It is worth to point out that in the medium the rho decay due to the isospin degeneracy becomes as important as the omega decay. The main new and original results of the thesis are: 1. Significant in-medium increase of the pi-omega-rho coupling constant. 2. Large enhancement of the Dalitz decays from the omega and rho mesons in the range 0.2-0.6 GeV of the dilepton invariant mass.

## Key claim

The in-medium modification of the pi-omega-rho vertex leads to a sizeable increase of the effective coupling constants compared to their vacuum values, with the effect growing with density; however, the resulting enhancement of Dalitz decays alone falls short of describing the CERES dilepton data, suggesting that other medium effects (such as dropping masses or broadening widths) are also needed.

## Method

The author uses relativistic field theory with one-loop Feynman diagrams incorporating nucleons and Delta(1232) isobars to compute the in-medium modification of the pi-omega-rho coupling for the Dalitz decays omega -> pi0 gamma* and rho^a -> pi^a gamma*. The analysis is performed in the leading baryon density approximation at zero temperature, considering both the case where the decaying particle is at rest and where it moves with respect to the medium. The in-medium-modified coupling is then applied to calculate dilepton production rates using the vector dominance model and a hydrodynamic expansion model of the fire cylinder, with the CERES experimental acceptance cuts included.

## Key numbers

* The pi-omega-rho coupling constant increases by about a factor of 2 for the omega -> pi0 gamma* decay in nuclear medium.
* The pi-omega-rho coupling constant increases by about a factor of 5 for the rho^a -> pi^a gamma* decay in nuclear medium.
* Large enhancement of the Dalitz decays from omega and rho mesons is observed in the range 0.2-0.6 GeV of the dilepton invariant mass.
* The quark condensate at nuclear matter saturation density (rho_N = 0.17 fm^{-3}) is roughly 25-50% smaller than its vacuum value, depending on the precise value of the sigma term (about 45 MeV).
* The gluon condensate decreases by around 5% at nuclear matter saturation density.
* From QCD sum rules, m_rho* / m_rho is approximately 1 - 0.18 (rho_N / rho_0), and m_phi* / m_phi is approximately 1 - 0.15a (rho_N / rho_0) with a = 0.17.
* From QCD sum rule reanalysis, m_rho* / m_rho = 0.78 +/- 0.08 at normal nuclear matter density.

## Context

This PhD thesis extends the line of work on in-medium modifications of vector meson properties, which includes the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis (Brown and Rho, 1991), QCD sum rule calculations by Hatsuda and Lee (1992), and hadronic model approaches by various groups. The work specifically focuses on the pi-omega-rho vertex modification, which had not been previously studied in detail, and applies it to the long-standing puzzle of low-mass dilepton enhancement observed by the CERES and HELIOS collaborations at CERN SPS. The results enable future combined analyses where this vertex modification is included alongside other medium effects such as dropping masses and broadening widths to potentially resolve the discrepancy between theoretical predictions and experimental data.
