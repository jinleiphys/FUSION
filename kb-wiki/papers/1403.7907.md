# Probing the $XYZ$ states through radiative decays

> In this work, we have adopted the spin rearrangement scheme in the heavy quark limit and extensively investigated three classes of the radiative decays: $\mathfrak{M}\to (b\bar{b})+γ$, $(b\bar{b})\to \mathfrak{M}+γ$, $ \mathfrak{M} \to \mathfrak{M}^\prime+γ$, corresponding to the electromagnetic transitions between one molecular state and bottomonium, one bottomonium and molecular state, and two molecular states respectively. We also extend the same formalism to study the radiative decays of the molecular states with hidden charm. We have derived some model independent ratios when the initial or final states belong to the same spin flavor multiplet. Future experimental measurement of these ratios will test the molecular picture and explore the underlying structures of the $XYZ$ states.

## Key claim

The paper derives model-independent ratios for radiative decays between molecular states and bottomonia (or charmonia) using heavy quark spin symmetry, but these ratios are only valid when the initial or final states belong to the same spin-flavor multiplet and do not include phase space contributions; the analysis does not depend on whether the system is a bound molecular state or a mixture of molecule and two-meson continuum.

## Method

The authors employed the spin rearrangement scheme in the heavy quark limit, decomposing the total angular momentum of molecular states (composed of S-wave and P-wave bottom mesons) into heavy quark spin and light spin components using 6-j and 9-j symbols. They then calculated the reduced matrix elements for three classes of radiative transitions: molecular state to bottomonium plus photon, bottomonium to molecular state plus photon, and molecular state to another molecular state plus photon, assuming conservation of heavy and light spins. The formalism was extended to hidden-charm molecular states by the same spin recoupling method.

## Context

This work extends the spin rearrangement approach previously applied by Voloshin (2011) to radiative transitions of $\Upsilon(5S)$ to hidden-bottom isovector molecular resonances, now including systems with P-wave bottom mesons. The derived ratios can test the molecular interpretation of $XYZ$ states such as $X(3872)$, $Y(4260)$, and $Y(4360)$ when compared with future experimental measurements, complementing dynamical calculations using the one-boson exchange model or the quark-pion interaction model of Tornqvist (1993).
