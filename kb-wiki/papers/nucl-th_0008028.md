# Quark Models of Baryon Masses and Decays

> The application of quark models to the spectra and strong and electromagnetic couplings of baryons is reviewed. This review focuses on calculations which attempt a global description of the masses and decay properties of baryons, although recent developments in applying large N_c QCD and lattice QCD to the baryon spectrum are described. After outlining the conventional one-gluon-exchange picture, models which consider extensions to this approach are contrasted with dynamical quark models based on Goldstone-boson exchange and an algebraic collective-excitation approach. The spectra and electromagnetic and strong couplings that result from these models are compared with the quantities extracted from the data and each other, and the impact of various model assumptions on these properties is emphasized. Prospects for the resolution of the important issues raised by these comparisons are discussed.

## Key claim

This review argues that quark potential models remain an indispensable tool for understanding baryon spectroscopy, despite the promise of lattice QCD and large Nc QCD, because they provide the only current framework for a global yet detailed description of baryon masses and decay properties across many states.

## Method

The authors review and compare several classes of quark models for baryons: the conventional one-gluon-exchange picture (exemplified by the nonrelativistic Isgur-Karl model and the MIT bag model), dynamical models based on Goldstone-boson exchange, and an algebraic collective-excitation approach. They compare the predicted spectra, electromagnetic couplings, and strong decay widths from these models against each other and against experimental data extracted from pion-nucleon and kaon-nucleon scattering analyses, emphasizing how different model assumptions affect the results.

## Key numbers

- The Gell-Mann–Okubo mass formula (M_Sigma + 3 M_Lambda)/2 = M_N + M_Xi holds to about 1% for the J^P = 1/2^+ octet baryons.
- The equal spacing rule for the J^P = 3/2^+ decuplet gives charge-averaged mass splittings of 153, 149, and 139 MeV between successive states (Sigma* minus Delta, Xi* minus Sigma*, Omega minus Xi*).
- The SU(6) relation M_Sigma* minus M_Sigma = M_Xi* minus M_Xi gives experimental differences of 192 and 215 MeV, respectively.
- The Isgur-Karl model predicts a hyperfine splitting of about 75 MeV (delta/4) between the unperturbed level and the split N* and Delta* negative-parity states.
- Quenched lattice QCD results from the CP-PACS collaboration show systematic deviations from experimental masses, with the nucleon mass fit to an error of at most -6.6% when the strange-quark mass is fixed by fitting to the phi meson.

## Context

This review extends the earlier comprehensive review by Hey and Kelly (1983) on baryon physics in the quark model, covering developments since that time. It contrasts the one-gluon-exchange approach of De Rujula, Georgi, and Glashow (1975) and the detailed Isgur-Karl model (1977-1982) with alternative approaches such as Goldstone-boson exchange models and the algebraic collective-excitation method. The work enables future comparisons between quark model predictions and emerging lattice QCD results, and provides a framework for interpreting whether newly discovered baryon states are three-quark states, hybrid baryons with excited glue, or some admixture.
