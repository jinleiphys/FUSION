# Nuclear Molecular Halo: Threshold Effect or Soft Dipole in 11Li?

> The observation of large E1 strength near threshold in the electromagnetic dissociation of 11Li poses a fundamental question: Is the large E1 strength due to the threshold or is it due to a low lying E1 state? Such molecular cluster states were observed in 18O and in several nuclei near the drip line. We discuss the nature of the "threshold effect" as well as review the situation in Molecular (and Particle Physics) where such Molecular States are observed near the dissociation limit. We suggest that the situation in 11Li is reminiscent of the argon-benzene molecule where the argon atom is loosely bound by a polarization (van der Waals) mechanism and thus leads to a very extended object lying near the dissociation limit. Such states are also suggested to dominate the structure of mesons [a_0(980), f_0(975)] and baryons [Lambda(1405)] with proposed Kaon molecular structure (e.g. by Dalitz) near threshold. The inspection of such states throughout Physics allows us to gain insight into this phenomenon and suggest that a new collective Molecular Dipole Degree of Freedom plays a major role in the structure of hadrons (halo nuclei, mesons and baryons), and that quantitative tools such as the E1 Molecular Sum Rule are useful for elucidating the nature of the observed low lying E1 strength in halo nuclei.

# Nuclear Molecular Halo: Threshold Effect or Soft Dipole in 11Li?

## Key claim
The large E1 strength observed near threshold in the electromagnetic dissociation of 11Li is consistent with a low-lying soft dipole molecular state rather than a purely kinematic threshold effect, because the peak at approximately 1.2 MeV occurs at four times the two-neutron separation energy (Q = 300 keV), whereas a pure threshold effect would peak at 2Q.

## Method
The paper analyzes the photodisintegration cross section of 11Li using the standard parameterization of neutron capture cross sections expanded in powers of neutron velocity (the 1/v law and higher-order terms). It compares the measured E1 strength from the GSI electromagnetic dissociation experiment to the predictions of this parameterization, and also evaluates the molecular sum rule derived by Alhassid, Gai and Bertsch (1990) for cluster states, applying it to the 11Li system as a 2n + 9Li molecular configuration.

## Key numbers
- The two-neutron separation energy in 11Li is Q = 300 keV; the observed E1 peak lies at approximately 1.2 MeV, which is 4Q, not 2Q as would be expected from a pure threshold effect.
- The dipole strength at approximately 1.2 MeV in 11Li exhausts 20% of the two-neutron molecular sum rule.
- The total E1 strength integrated up to 5 MeV exhausts 100% of the two-neutron molecular sum rule.
- For comparison, molecular cluster states in 18O were measured to exhaust 13% of the molecular sum rule for B(E1) and 23% for B(E2).

## Context
This work extends the molecular cluster state concept previously established in 18O by Gai (1983, 1989, 1991) to halo nuclei near the drip line. The paper draws an analogy to molecular physics (the argon-benzene van der Waals molecule) and to particle physics, where Dalitz proposed the Lambda(1405) as an NKbar molecule and Weinstein and Isgur (1982) proposed the a0(980) and f0(975) as KKbar molecules. The analysis challenges the "nuclear shakeoff mechanism" proposed by the MSU group (Karataglidis et al., 1997) as an alternative explanation for the observed strength, and calls for more precise data to distinguish between the threshold effect and soft dipole interpretations.
