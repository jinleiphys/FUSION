# $θ$-dependence of the lightest meson resonances in QCD

> We derive the pion mass and the elastic pion-pion scattering amplitude in the QCD theta-vacuum up to next-to-leading order in chiral perturbation theory. Using the modified inverse amplitude method, we study the theta-dependence of the mass and width of the light scalar meson sigma(500) and the vector meson rho(770).

# θ-dependence of the lightest meson resonances in QCD

## Key claim

The pion-pion scattering amplitude up to next-to-leading order in the QCD θ-vacuum takes exactly the same form as the well-known amplitude at θ=0, with the only change being the replacement of the leading-order pion mass squared at θ=0 by its θ-dependent counterpart; this property does not hold at higher orders, where CP-violating three-pion vertices would appear.

## Method

The authors derived the charged and neutral pion masses and the elastic ππ scattering amplitude in the QCD θ-vacuum up to next-to-leading order in chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), including strong isospin breaking. Using the modified inverse amplitude method (mIAM) to unitarize the NLO amplitude, they searched for poles in the complex s-plane of the unitarized partial waves to extract the θ-dependent masses and widths of the σ(500) and ρ(770) resonances.

## Key numbers

- Leading-order pion mass squared in the θ-vacuum: M̊²(θ) = 2B m̄ cos(θ/2) √(1 + ε² tan²(θ/2)), where m̄ = (m_u+m_d)/2 and ε = (m_d-m_u)/(2m̄)
- At θ=0, the σ pole position: √s_σ = (443.1 − i 217.4) MeV
- At θ=0, the ρ pole position: √s_ρ = (751.9 − i 75.4) MeV
- Low-energy constants used at scale μ=770 MeV: l₁ʳ = (−3.7 ± 0.2)×10⁻³, l₂ʳ = (5.0 ± 0.4)×10⁻³
- Physical input values: M_π = 138.04 MeV (isospin averaged), F_π = 92.21 MeV, yielding M = 139.46 MeV and F = 86.43 MeV from standard ChPT one-loop expressions

## Context

This work extends previous studies of QCD at non-zero θ, including the leading-order pion mass calculation by Brower (2003) and the investigation of QCD at θ∼π by Smilga (1998) and Tytgat (1999). The authors note that the NLO ππ amplitude exhibits an accidental symmetry reminiscent of the Kaplan-Manohar transformation (1986). The results enable precision modeling of nuclear forces under variations of fundamental parameters, as used in extracting limits on Higgs vacuum expectation value variations from Big Bang nucleosynthesis (Berengut, 2013).
