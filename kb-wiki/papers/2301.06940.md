# Accessing Compton Form Factors at the Electron Ion Collider in China: An Impact study on $\textbf{Im} {\mathcal E}$

> We estimate the impact of asymmetry measurements of Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS) with transversely polarized proton beam taken at a future Electron Ion Collider in China (EicC) on the extraction of Compton Form Factors (CFFs). The CFFs extracted from an analysis based on artificial neural-network approach are reweighted by means of pseudo-data generated in the expected kinematic region of EicC. We find a remarkable improvement in the extraction of CFF $\textbf{Im} {\mathcal E}$, especially at the range of parton momentum fraction $x \sim$ 0.01, thus hinting for a future experimental probe of the parton orbital angular momentum. This work casts a glance at a practical implementation of Bayesian reweighting method on CFFs' impact study.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that asymmetry measurements of Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS) with a transversely polarized proton beam at the future Electron Ion Collider in China (EicC) can significantly improve the extraction of the Compton Form Factor (CFF) Im E, particularly in the sea quark region around parton momentum fraction x ~ 0.01, but only when the integrated luminosity is reduced to 0.12 fb^-1 to keep the Bayesian reweighting procedure reliable.

## Method

The authors generated pseudo-data for the transversely polarized proton beam-spin asymmetry A_UT with sin(phi-phi_s)cos(phi) modulation using the MILOU Monte Carlo generator within the EicC kinematic region (3.5 GeV electron beam on 20 GeV proton beam). They then applied a Bayesian reweighting technique to an ensemble of 101 CFF replicas produced by the PARTONS framework using artificial neural networks, updating the weights of each replica based on the chi-squared compatibility between the pseudo-data and the replica predictions.

## Key numbers

- EicC design parameters: 3.5 GeV polarized electron beam with 80% polarization colliding with 20 GeV polarized proton beam with 70% polarization at instantaneous luminosity of 2 x 10^33 cm^-2 s^-1
- Integrated luminosity assumed for pseudo-data generation: 50.0 fb^-1, corresponding to approximately 290 days of data taking
- The reweighting procedure was performed with a reduced integrated luminosity of 0.12 fb^-1 to maintain reliability
- The kinematic coverage spans 1.0 GeV^2 < Q^2 < 30.0 GeV^2 with 27 kinematic bins in the (x_B, Q^2) plane and 69 bins in the (x_B, Q^2, -t) plane
- The t-slope parameter used in MILOU was 5.45 GeV^-2
- The current integrated luminosity of A_UT in the sea quark region estimated by PARTONS is only around 0.01 fb^-1

## Context

This work extends the global extraction efforts of CFFs using neural network approaches by Kumericki (2011) and the PARTONS framework by Berthou (2015) and Moutarde (2019), applying Bayesian reweighting methods previously developed by the NNPDF collaboration (Ball 2010, 2011). The study demonstrates that EicC measurements can probe the GPD E, which is crucial for accessing parton orbital angular momentum through Ji's sum rule (1996), in the sea quark region that is complementary to the valence region accessible at JLab 12 GeV and the gluon-focused program at the EIC at Brookhaven National Laboratory.
