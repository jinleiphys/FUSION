# Three-body scattering problem in the fixed center approximation: the case of attraction

> We study the scattering of a light particle on a bound pair of heavy particles (e.g., the deuteron) within the fixed center approximation in the case of light-heavy attraction, solving the integral equation for the three-body Green's function both in the coordinate and in the momentum space. The results for the three-body scattering amplitude appear to be ambiguous -- they depend on a single real parameter. This parameter may be fixed by a three-body input, e.g., the three-body scattering length. We also solve the integral equation for the three-body Green function in the momentum space, introducing a finite cut-off. We show that all three approaches are equivalent. We also discuss how our approach to the problem matches with the introduction of three-body contact interaction as done by other authors.

# Three-body scattering problem in the fixed center approximation: the case of attraction

## Key claim

The three-body scattering amplitude for a light particle scattering on a bound pair of heavy particles in the fixed center approximation with light-heavy attraction is ambiguous, depending on a single real parameter (denoted B or b) that must be fixed by a three-body input such as the three-body scattering length. The periodic dependence of the solution on the momentum-space cut-off Lambda is a direct consequence of this ambiguity, not a separate regularization artifact.

## Method

The authors solved the integral equation for the three-body Green's function in the fixed center approximation both in coordinate space and in momentum space, for the case of attractive light-heavy interactions (pion-nucleon attraction with a > 0). In coordinate space, the divergent integral kernel 1/(r-a) was replaced by a principal value plus a delta-function term with an undetermined dimensionless parameter B. In momentum space, the integral equation for the function R(p,p') was solved analytically with infinite upper integration limit, yielding a sum of a particular solution of the inhomogeneous equation and a general solution of the homogeneous equation proportional to sin(ap) sin(ap'). The same equation was then solved numerically with a finite cut-off Lambda, and the numerical solutions were compared to the analytical expressions.

## Key numbers

- The two-body scattering length used in the model is a = 0.005 MeV^{-1}
- The critical cut-off values for a = 0.005 MeV^{-1} are: Lambda_cr^{(1)} = 385 MeV, Lambda_cr^{(2)} = 1000 MeV, Lambda_cr^{(3)} = 1615 MeV
- For Lambda = 700 MeV (midway between first and second critical values), the fitted parameter b = 0.07
- For Lambda = 3800 MeV (midway between critical values), the fitted parameter b = 0.20, with Lambda_cr^{(7)} = 4075 MeV
- For Lambda = 1050 MeV (close to Lambda_cr^{(2)} = 1000 MeV), the fitted parameter b = -3.05, with the nearest critical value at 1615 MeV
- The asymptotic expression for the particular solution at p >> p' is R_In^{(as)}(p,p') = 4 pi^2 cos(pa) sin(p'a)

## Context

This work extends the fixed center approximation treatment of three-body scattering, originally formulated by Foldy (1945) for pion-deuteron scattering and by Bruckner (1953), to the case of attractive interactions where the standard multiple scattering series becomes divergent. The authors show that their approach is equivalent to introducing a three-body contact interaction as done by Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999) in the context of the Skornyakov-Ter-Martirosyan equation, but clarify that the cut-off dependence is not eliminated by such an interaction but rather reparameterized. The results enable a consistent treatment of systems such as pionic deuterium and kaonic deuterium where the three-body scattering length must be used as input to fix the free parameter, after which the energy dependence of the three-body s-wave scattering phase can be predicted.
