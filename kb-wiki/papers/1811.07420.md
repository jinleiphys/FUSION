# Possible signals of two QCD phase transitions at NICA-FAIR energies

> The chemical freeze-out irregularities found with the most advanced hadron resonance gas model and possible signals of two QCD phase transitions are discussed. We found that the center-of-mass collision energy range of tricritical endpoint of QCD phase diagram is [9; 9.2] GeV which is consistent both with QCD inspired exactly solvable model and with experimental findings.

## Key claim

The paper argues that two distinct QCD phase transitions, chiral symmetry restoration (CSR) and deconfinement, can be identified in heavy-ion collision data, and that the tricritical endpoint (3CEP) where they meet lies in the narrow center-of-mass energy range \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \in [9; 9.2]\) GeV, a claim hedged by the fact that this range is derived by combining a theoretical model prediction with experimental signals and is not directly measured.

## Method

The authors used a multicomponent hadron resonance gas model (MHRGM) with 4 or 5 hard-core radii for different hadron species to fit hadronic multiplicity ratios from AGS, SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies, achieving a fit quality of \(\chi^2/dof \simeq 1.08\). They then analyzed the collision energy dependence of chemical freeze-out (CFO) parameters, temperature, pressure, baryonic charge density, entropy per baryon, pion number per baryon, and trace anomaly, to identify irregularities (quasi-plateaus and peaks) that they interpret as signals of two phase transitions. The equation of state of the matter created between the two baryonic density maxima was extracted and compared to a MIT-Bag-like form to determine effective degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- The tricritical endpoint (3CEP) collision energy range is found to be \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \in [9; 9.2]\) GeV, consistent with both a QCD-inspired exactly solvable model and experimental findings.
- The CFO temperature jumps sharply when collision energy increases from \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\simeq 4.3\) GeV to \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\simeq 4.9\) GeV; over this same range the CFO pressure increases by a factor of 6.
- A huge maximum of baryonic charge density occurs at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\simeq 4.9\) GeV.
- Two sets of correlated quasi-plateaus in entropy per baryon, total pion number per baryon, and thermal pion number per baryon are located at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \simeq 3.8-4.9\) GeV and \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \simeq 7.6-9.2\) GeV.
- Two sharp peaks of baryonic charge density and two peaks of trace anomaly \(\delta = (\epsilon - 3p)/T^4\) are observed at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=4.9\) GeV and \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=9.2\) GeV.
- The extracted equation of state for the phase between the two density maxima has coefficients \(A_0 \simeq 2.53 \times 10^{-5}~\text{MeV}^{-3}\text{fm}^{-3}\), \(A_2 \simeq 1.51 \times 10^{-6}~\text{MeV}^{-3}\text{fm}^{-3}\), \(A_4 \simeq 1.001 \times 10^{-9}~\text{MeV}^{-3}\text{fm}^{-3}\), and bag constant \(B \simeq 9488~\text{MeV}~\text{fm}^{-3}\).
- From these coefficients, the total effective degrees of freedom are \(N_{dof}^{tot} \simeq 1770\), with fermionic \(N_{f}^{eff} \simeq 141\) and bosonic \(N_{b}^{eff} \simeq 1523\).
- The CEP temperature from an exactly solvable model of QGP bags with surface tension is \(T_{cep} \in [148.4; 157.4]\) MeV, which combined with CFO data gives a 3CEP energy range of \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \in [9; 17.3]\) GeV before narrowing to \([9; 9.2]\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the findings of the Kiev and Giessen groups (Bugaev et al., 2015-2018; various references), who independently concluded that CSR occurs at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\simeq 4-5\) GeV and deconfinement at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\simeq 9-10\) GeV, with CSR being a first-order transition and deconfinement second-order or weak first-order. It contradicts the interpretation of the "Kink," "Strangeness Horn," and "Step" irregularities by the model of Ref. [KAB_Horn] (likely Rafelski and collaborators, 1980s-1990s), which the authors argue used an oversimplified hadronic equation of state with only 30 degrees of freedom and a deconfinement temperature above 200 MeV, inconsistent with modern lattice QCD. The paper also disagrees with the claim by Ko et al. (2018) that the enhancement of light nuclei fluctuations at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\simeq 8.8\) GeV signals a critical endpoint (CEP), instead arguing it signals a tricritical endpoint (3CEP). The results enable experimental searches at NICA and FAIR to target the narrow energy window around 9-9.2 GeV for the 3CEP and to distinguish between CSR and deconfinement signals.
