# Probing mixed-spin pairing in heavy nuclei

> The nature of the nuclear pairing condensate is an active topic of investigation, especially as regards its neutron-proton versus identical-particle character, which manifests as the difference between spin-singlet and spin-triplet pairing. In this work, we probe the recently proposed mixed-spin pairing condensates, using a phenomenological Hamiltonian and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory along with the gradient method. In addition to improving the solution of the many-body problem, we have calculated a series of physical quantities and examined the robustness of the mixed-spin pairing state as the input Hamiltonian is modified. Overall, we find that even though the mixed-spin correlation energy is suppressed in comparison to earlier work, the new pairing behavior persists. We also discuss the possibility of directly probing the mixed-spin pairing phase.

# Probing mixed-spin pairing in heavy nuclei

## Key claim

Even though the mixed-spin correlation energy is suppressed when the Hartree-Fock Γ field is explicitly included in the calculations (compared to earlier work that dropped this term), the mixed-spin pairing behavior persists and remains below the proton dripline, suggesting experimental relevance.

## Method

The authors used a phenomenological Hamiltonian with a Woods-Saxon one-body potential and a contact two-body interaction, solved via Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory using the gradient method with up to 8 constraining fields. They explicitly included the Γ (Hartree-Fock) field that was omitted in prior work by Bertsch and Luo (2010) and Gezerlis, Bertsch, and Luo (2011), and calculated correlation energies, particle-number fluctuations, and pairing gaps for heavy nuclei with neutron numbers between 50 and 75.

## Key numbers

- Correlation energy for $^{132}_{60}$Nd (spin-singlet): approximately 6.5 MeV with Γ included, compared to approximately 8 MeV without Γ
- Correlation energy for $^{132}_{66}$Dy (spin-triplet): approximately 4 MeV with Γ included, compared to approximately 11 MeV without Γ
- Correlation energy for $^{132}_{64}$Gd (mixed-spin): approximately 4 MeV with Γ included, compared to approximately 7 MeV without Γ
- Particle-number fluctuations never exceed 4 particles (or 3%) across the studied nuclei
- Default pairing strengths: $v_s = 300$ MeV for spin-singlet, $v_t = 450$ MeV for spin-triplet (ratio approximately 1.5)
- Default spin-orbit strength: $V_{SO} = 33$ MeV fm$^2$; Woods-Saxon depth: $V_{WS} = -50$ MeV; diffuseness: $a = 0.67$ fm

## Context

This work extends the studies of Bertsch and Luo (2010) and Gezerlis, Bertsch, and Luo (2011), which first proposed mixed-spin pairing in heavy nuclei but omitted the Hartree-Fock Γ field. By including this term, the authors find that while correlation energies are reduced, the mixed-spin and spin-triplet pairing regions persist below the proton dripline, enabling future experimental searches for these exotic pairing phases. The results also connect to broader investigations of neutron-proton pairing reviewed by Frauendorf and Macchiavelli (2014).
