# A Cautionary Tale: The Coulomb Modified ANC for the $\mathbf{1/2^+_2}$ State in $^\mathbf{17}$O

> We discuss the impact of the uncertainty ($\pm 8$ keV) in the excitation energy of the astrophysically important 6.356 MeV $1/2^+_2$ state of $^{17}$O on the precision with which the Coulomb reduced ANC ($\widetilde{C}$) for the $\left<^{17}\mathrm{O}(1/2^+_2) \mid \protect{^{13}\mathrm{C}} + α\right>$ overlap can be extracted from direct reaction data. We find a linear dependence of $\widetilde{C}^2$ on the binding energy, the value extracted varying by a factor of 4 over the range $E_{\mathrm{ex}} = 6.356$ -- $6.348$ MeV. This represents an intrinsic limit on the precision with which $\widetilde{C}^2$ can be determined which cannot be improved unless or until the uncertainty in $E_{\mathrm{ex}}$ is reduced.

## Key claim

The uncertainty of ±8 keV in the excitation energy of the 6.356 MeV 1/2⁺₂ state of ¹⁷O imposes an intrinsic limit of approximately a factor of 4 on the precision with which the Coulomb reduced ANC (C̃²) for the ⟨¹⁷O(1/2⁺₂) | ¹³C + α⟩ overlap can be extracted from direct reaction data, which cannot be improved unless the excitation energy uncertainty is reduced.

## Method

The authors re-analyzed two existing transfer reaction data sets probing the ⟨¹⁷O(1/2⁺₂) | ¹³C + α⟩ overlap: the 3.57 MeV ¹³C(⁶Li,d)¹⁷O data of Avila (2015) and the 45 MeV ¹³C(¹¹B,⁷Li)¹⁷O data of Mezhevych (2017). They performed DWBA (and CCBA for the second reaction) calculations using Fresco, binding the transferred α particle in a Woods-Saxon well with radius parameter r₀ varied from 1.25 to 2.50 fm, and for each r₀ varied the binding energy ε from 2.69 keV to 10.69 keV (corresponding to the lower limit of the excitation energy uncertainty). Calculations were normalized to data by minimizing χ².

## Key numbers

- The excitation energy of the ¹⁷O 1/2⁺₂ state is 6.356 MeV, with a stated uncertainty of ±8 keV (Tilley 1993).
- The state lies approximately 3 keV below the α emission threshold.
- The binding energy ε was varied from 2.69 keV to 10.69 keV, corresponding to the lower limit of the excitation energy uncertainty.
- C̃² varies by approximately a factor of 4 over the range E_ex = 6.356 to 6.348 MeV.
- The variation of C̃² as a function of ε is linear over this range.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANC) for near-threshold states, building on the formalism of Mukhamedzhanov (2012) and prior analyses of the ¹⁷O 1/2⁺₂ state by Avila (2015) and Mezhevych (2017). The ¹³C(α,n)¹⁶O reaction is considered the main neutron source for the s process in AGB stars (Iben 1983), and the 6.356 MeV state makes an important contribution to the astrophysical S factor. The results caution that for near-threshold levels, excitation energy uncertainties can significantly impact ANC extraction, and suggest that similar sensitivity tests should be performed on a case-by-case basis; Ogata (2012) noted a related effect from mass uncertainties in eikonal reaction theory.
