# In medium effects on the $ϕ$ meson

> The temperature dependence of the $ϕ$ meson mass and its decay width ($ϕ\rightarrow K \bar{K}$)have been studied from an effective non-linear chiral Lagrangian in $SU(3)$. Effective mass has been obtained from the pole position of the full propagator. The width has also been calculated. It has been found that the mass decreases with temperature very slowly whereas the decay width increases quite sharply. Possible consequence on the QGP signals is discussed.

# In medium effects on the $\phi$ meson

## Key claim
The $\phi$ meson mass decreases very slowly with temperature and always remains above the two-kaon threshold, contrary to earlier QCD sum rule predictions that the mass would drop below twice the kaon mass above $T = 150$ MeV. The decay width increases sharply with temperature, reaching about $21$ MeV, because the kaon mass also decreases with temperature, a self-consistency effect neglected in prior work.

## Method
The authors used a non-linear chiral Lagrangian in $SU(3)$ within the Hidden Gauge Symmetry Approach (HGSA), with the parameter $a=2$ fixed by Vector Meson Dominance. The temperature-dependent $\phi$ meson mass was obtained from the pole position of the full propagator by solving Dyson's equation with the self-energy calculated at one-loop order including $K\bar{K}$ and $\rho\pi$ loops. The decay width $\phi \rightarrow K\bar{K}$ was computed from the imaginary part of the polarization function, and the $\phi$ and $K$ meson masses were treated self-consistently at finite temperature.

## Key numbers
- At $T = 150$ MeV (most central collisions), the $\phi$ meson mass decreases to about $1016$ MeV, in agreement with AGS experimental data.
- The decay width $\phi \rightarrow K\bar{K}$ increases from $3.7$ MeV at zero temperature to about $21$ MeV at finite temperature.
- The QCD sum rule calculation of Ko and collaborators (1995) predicted the $\phi$ mass would fall below twice the kaon mass above $T = 150$ MeV, which the present work contradicts.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of the temperature dependence of the kaon mass in the non-linear sigma model with broken $SU(3)_V$ (Bhattacharyya et al., 1995). It directly contradicts the QCD sum rule results of Asakawa and Ko (1994) and Ko and Siebert (1995), who predicted a secondary $\phi$ peak in dilepton spectra due to the $\phi$ mass dropping below the $K\bar{K}$ threshold. The present results show that the primary $\phi$ peak remains observable in the dilepton spectrum from the hadronic sector, and that self-consistent treatment of both $\phi$ and $K$ masses is essential for reliable in-medium predictions.
