# Vacuum Contributions in a Chiral Effective Lagrangian for Nuclei

> A relativistic hadronic model for nuclear matter and finite nuclei, which incorporates nonlinear chiral symmetry and broken scale invariance, is presented and applied at the one-baryon-loop level to finite nuclei. The model contains an effective light scalar field that is responsible for the mid-range nucleon--nucleon attraction and which has anomalous scaling behavior. One-loop vacuum contributions in this background scalar field at finite density are constrained by low-energy theorems that reflect the broken scale invariance of quantum chromodynamics. A mean-field energy functional for nuclear matter and nuclei is derived that contains small powers of the fields and their derivatives, and the validity of this truncation is discussed. Good fits to the bulk properties of finite nuclei and single-particle spectra are obtained.

## Key claim

The paper presents a relativistic hadronic model for nuclear matter and finite nuclei that incorporates nonlinear chiral symmetry and broken scale invariance, but the authors explicitly hedge that the model's validity depends on the assumption that mean fields dominate the dynamics and that expansions in powers of the fields and their derivatives can be truncated at low order, with the condition that these approximations are justified only for densities not much higher than nuclear matter equilibrium density.

## Method

The authors construct a chirally invariant effective Lagrangian containing nucleons, pions, a light scalar field (with anomalous scaling dimension d) representing mid-range nucleon-nucleon attraction, a heavy scalar glueball field, and omega vector mesons. They integrate out the heavy glueball field by iteratively solving its equation of motion, expand the resulting Lagrangian in powers of the light scalar field and its derivatives, and constrain the scalar potential using low-energy theorems from broken scale invariance in QCD. The one-baryon-loop vacuum contributions are absorbed into renormalized coupling constants, and the model is applied at the Hartree (mean-field) level to finite nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The light scalar mass is given by \( m_{\rm s}^2 = 4H_{\rm q}/(d^2 S_0^2) \), where \( H_{\rm q} \) is the quark contribution to the trace anomaly and \( S_0 \) is the vacuum expectation value of the light scalar field.
- The pion-decay constant is \( f_{\pi} = 93 \) MeV.
- The axial coupling constant is \( g_{\rm \scriptscriptstyle A} = 1.23 \).
- The heavy glueball mass is roughly 1.6–1.8 GeV from lattice QCD calculations.
- The ratio of mean fields (or nucleon self-energies) to the free nucleon mass is roughly \( 1/4 \) to \( 1/3 \) at nuclear matter equilibrium density.
- The gradient of the mean scalar field satisfies \( |\nabla\phi / \phi| \lesssim 100 \) MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Furnstahl, Serot, and collaborators (1994) that found generic failures of linear sigma model generalizations with "Mexican hat" potentials for nuclear phenomenology, and by the Minnesota group (Heide, 1994) who obtained improved results by abandoning such potentials. The paper builds a different effective model using nonlinear chiral symmetry and broken scale invariance constraints, enabling a systematic treatment of vacuum loop contributions in relativistic mean-field models of finite nuclei while leaving open the question of going beyond one-loop order systematically.
