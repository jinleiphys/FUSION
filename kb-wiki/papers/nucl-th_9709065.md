# Periodic-Orbit Bifurcation and Shell Structure at Exotic Deformation

> By means of periodic orbit theory and deformed cavity model, we have investigated semiclassical origin of superdeformed shell structure and also of reflection-asymmetric deformed shapes. Systematic analysis of quantum-classical correspondence reveals that bifurcation of equatorial orbits into three-dimensional ones play predominant role in the formation of these shell structures.

## Key claim

The paper argues that bifurcations of planar equatorial orbits into three-dimensional periodic orbits are the semiclassical origin of superdeformed and hyperdeformed shell structures in prolate cavities, and that similar bifurcations from equatorial-plane orbits explain shell structure in reflection-asymmetric (pear-shaped) cavities, but the authors note that the relative contributions of different periodic orbits depend strongly on deformation, unlike the qualitative argument of Strutinsky et al.

## Method

The authors used the deformed cavity model with Dirichlet boundary conditions, solving the Schrödinger equation numerically to obtain single-particle eigenvalues as functions of deformation parameters. They performed Fourier transforms of the quantum level densities with respect to wave number to obtain length spectra, and compared these to classical periodic orbits systematically searched using the monodromy method. Bifurcation points of equatorial-plane orbits were determined by the condition that the trace of the reduced monodromy matrix equals 2, following the condition derived by Balian and Bloch.

## Context

This work extends the periodic-orbit theory approach of Strutinsky et al. and the bifurcation analysis of Nishioka et al. (who studied spheroidal cavities) to both superdeformed and hyperdeformed shapes as well as reflection-asymmetric cavities. The results confirm the qualitative argument of Strutinsky et al. for prolate normal and superdeformations, but reveal a strong deformation dependence of relative periodic-orbit contributions that was not previously appreciated. The findings for reflection-asymmetric shapes appear consistent with realistic calculations by Frauendorf and Pashkevich for sodium cluster shapes, and the analysis enables identification of which specific periodic orbits dominate shell structure at exotic deformations, potentially guiding searches for new deformed nuclear and cluster configurations.
