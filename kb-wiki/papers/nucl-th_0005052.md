# How to Renormalize the Gap Equation in High Density QCD

> We discuss two technical issues related to the gap equation in high-density QCD: i) how to obtain the asymptotic solution with well controlled approximations, and ii) the renormalization of four-quark operators in the high-density effective field theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that previous analytical determinations of the superconducting gap in high-density QCD suffered from two flaws: improper regularization/renormalization of the gap equation and an uncontrolled approximation that converted the integral equation into a differential equation. The authors provide a corrected asymptotic solution that confirms Son’s (1999) original result for the leading exponential behavior of the gap, but with an additional prefactor contribution from a four-quark counterterm that is expected to be of order one.

## Method

The authors start from the high-density effective field theory for QCD, where fermions live on the two-dimensional Fermi surface and interact through four-dimensional gluons and contact operators. They regulate the gap equation with a sharp cutoff in the perpendicular momentum, perform the perpendicular and parallel momentum integrations, and define a renormalized gap equation by requiring that the gap be independent of the cutoff, which forces the four-fermi counterterm to run with the cutoff scale. They then find the exact asymptotic solution of the resulting integral equation without making the usual approximation of replacing the logarithm by step functions, instead using an ansatz of the form Δ(p) = p^z and solving the resulting transcendental indicial equation. The same analysis is repeated using dimensional regularization with minimal subtraction.

## Key numbers

- The gap at asymptotically high density is Δ = [2^10 √2 π^4 μ] / [N_f^(5/2) g^5] × exp(D(2μ, M)/2) × exp(-3π^2/(√2 g)), where μ is the chemical potential, N_f is the number of flavors, g is the QCD coupling, and D is the counterterm
- The coupling constant in the gap equation is C = g/(3√2 π)
- The Debye screening mass is M_d^2 = N_f g^2 μ^2 / (2π^2)
- The scale M_Λ⊥ = 4(Λ⊥)^6 / (π M_d^5) for cutoff regularization
- The scale M_2μ = 2^10 √2 π^4 μ / (N_f^(5/2) g^5) when the cutoff is set to 2μ
- The indicial equation for the asymptotic solution is z = -(π/2) C^2 cot(πz/2), which for small z gives z = ± iC
- The asymptotic solution is Δ(p) = Δ(Δ) sin(C log(M/p)) with Δ = M exp(-π/(2C))

## Context

This work extends the original analysis of Son (1999) on color superconductivity in high-density QCD by correcting two technical flaws in previous treatments: the improper regularization of the gap equation and the uncontrolled approximation of replacing the logarithm by step functions. The authors show that their exact asymptotic solution confirms Son’s result for the leading exponential behavior, while also demonstrating that the four-quark counterterm runs in a manner similar to counterterms in effective field theory treatments of the three-body problem in nuclear physics (Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck, 1998-1999). The paper enables future work to systematically include next-to-leading order corrections to the gap.
