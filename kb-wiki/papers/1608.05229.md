# Semihadronic and hadronic decays of $Žü^0$ and $Žē$ mesons coherently photoproduced in the isoscalar nuclei

> The interference of the $Žü^0$ and $Žē$ mesons has been studied in the hadronic and leptonic decay channels, i.e., dipion and dilepton decay channels respectively, but this interference is not studied yet in the semihadronic or semileptonic decay channel, e.g., $V \to ŽĆ^0╬│$. $V$ denotes either $Žü^0$ or $Žē$ meson. To look for the quoted interference in the $ŽĆ^0╬│$ decay channel as well as the contribution of $Žü$ meson to the cross section, the correlated $ŽĆ^0╬│$ invariant mass distribution spectra are calculated in the photonuclear reaction in the multi-GeV region. It is assumed that these bosons arise in the final state due to the decay of $Žü^0$ and $Žē$ mesons which are photoproduced coherently in the isoscalar nucleus. The elementary reaction in the nucleus is considered to proceed as $╬│N \to VN$; $V \to ŽĆ^0╬│$. The forward propagation of the $Žü$ and $Žē$ mesons and the near forward emission of pion are considered, so that they can be described by the eikonal form. The meson nucleus interactions are evaluated using $t\varrho$ approximation. Replacing the decay vertex $V \to ŽĆ^0╬│$ by $V \to ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-$ in the above formalism, it is also used to study the $Žü-Žē$ interference in the $ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^-$ decay channel.

# Semihadronic and hadronic decays of $\rho^0$ and $\omega$ mesons coherently photoproduced in the isoscalar nuclei

## Key claim

The $\rho-\omega$ interference, previously studied only in hadronic (dipion) and leptonic (dilepton) decay channels, is predicted to be observable in the semihadronic $\pi^0\gamma$ decay channel of coherently photoproduced vector mesons on isoscalar nuclei, provided one looks several hundred MeV away from the $\omega$ meson peak region where the $\rho$ meson contribution becomes comparable to that of the $\omega$ meson.

## Method

The author calculates the correlated $\pi^0\gamma$ and $\pi^+\pi^-$ invariant mass distribution spectra for the photonuclear reaction $\gamma A \to VA$; $V \to ab$ (where $ab$ is either $\pi^0\gamma$ or $\pi^+\pi^-$) in the multi-GeV region, assuming forward production of vector mesons and near-forward pion emission described by eikonal propagators. The meson-nucleus optical potentials are evaluated using the $t\varrho$ approximation, and the elementary reaction is taken as $\gamma N \to VN$ with vector meson dominance (VDM) relating the photoproduction amplitude to the meson-nucleon scattering amplitude. The formalism is applied to isoscalar nuclei $^{12}$C and $^{40}$Ca at beam energies of 3 and 5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Pole masses used: $m_{\rho^0}=775.26$ MeV and $m_{\omega}=782.65$ MeV.
- At $E_\gamma=3$ GeV on $^{12}$C, the peak cross section in the $\pi^0\gamma$ channel is increased by $\approx 12\%$ due to inclusion of the $\rho^0$ meson contribution.
- At $m\sim 0.64$ GeV and $m\sim 0.92$ GeV on $^{12}$C at 3 GeV, the cross section is increased by $\sim 100\%$ due to $\rho-\omega$ interference, and by a factor of $\sim 3.5$ due to the $\rho$ meson alone.
- For $^{12}$C at $E_\gamma=5$ GeV, the cross section at $m=0.64$ GeV rises from 47.3 $\mu$b/(GeV sr) to 0.12 mb/(GeV sr) compared to 3 GeV; at $m=0.92$ GeV it rises from 59.52 $\mu$b/(GeV sr) to 0.29 mb/(GeV sr).
- For $^{40}$Ca at 5 GeV, the cross section is 1.15 mb/(GeV sr) at $m=0.64$ GeV and 1.91 mb/(GeV sr) at $m=0.92$ GeV.
- The amplitude ratio $\xi$ for $^{12}$C at 3 GeV is $\simeq 1.17 \times 10^{-1}$ with relative phase $\phi = 61^0$; at 5 GeV $\xi = 1.24 \times 10^{-1}$ and $\phi = 63^0$; for $^{40}$Ca at 5 GeV $\xi = 1.28 \times 10^{-1}$ and $\phi \approx 61^0$.
- For the $\pi^+\pi^-$ channel on $^{12}$C at $k_V=6.4$ GeV, $\xi = 8.66 \times 10^{-2}$ and $\phi \approx 65^0$.
- $\Gamma_{\omega \to \pi^+\pi^-}(m_\omega) = 0.13$ MeV, compared to $\Gamma_{\rho^0 \to \pi^+\pi^-}(m_{\rho^0}) \sim 149$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of $\rho-\omega$ interference from the well-established hadronic ($\pi^+\pi^-$) and leptonic ($e^+e^-$) decay channels, previously investigated in photonuclear reactions by Alvensleben et al. (1970) and Biggs et al. (1970), to the semihadronic $\pi^0\gamma$ channel. The author notes that prior work by Das (2013) studied $\omega$ meson modification in nuclei via $\pi^0\gamma$ emission but neglected the $\rho$ meson contribution, which this paper shows is negligible only in the $\omega$ peak region but becomes significant away from it. The formalism also reproduces the $\pi^+\pi^-$ data from Alvensleben et al. (1970) and demonstrates that the sharp peak at the $\omega$ mass in the dipion spectrum arises from $\rho-\omega$ interference rather than direct $\omega$ decay. This enables future experimental searches for $\rho-\omega$ interference in the $\pi^0\gamma$ channel at facilities such as JLab, particularly using heavy isoscalar nuclei and higher beam energies where the cross section is measurable.
