# Complex energy approaches for calculating isobaric analogue states

> Two methods the complex energy shell model (CXSM) and the complex scaling (CS) approach were used for calculating isobaric analog resonances (IAR) in the Lane model. The IAR parameters calculated by the CXSM and the CS methods were checked against the parameters extracted from the direct numerical solution of the coupled channel Lane equations (CC). The agreement with the CC results was generally better than 1 keV for both methods and for each partial waves concerned. Similarities and differences of the CXSM and the CS methods are discussed. CXSM offers a direct way to study the configurations of the IAR wave function in contrast to the CS method.

## Key claim

The complex energy shell model (CXSM) and complex scaling (CS) methods both reproduce isobaric analogue resonance (IAR) parameters extracted from direct numerical solution of the coupled-channel Lane equations to within 1 keV, but only when the integration contours and basis parameters are carefully optimized; the agreement holds for each partial wave studied in the p+208Pb system.

## Method

The authors solved the Lane equations for the p+208Pb system using three approaches: (1) direct numerical integration of the coupled-channel equations (CC) with a fourth-order Runge-Kutta method, (2) diagonalization of the Hamiltonian in a Berggren basis (CXSM) that includes bound states, decaying resonances, and discretized scattering states along complex contours, and (3) complex scaling (CS) of the Lane equations with a Laguerre mesh basis. For the CS method, the standard sharp-edge Coulomb potential was replaced by a dilatation-analytic error-function form to satisfy mathematical requirements. IAR parameters were extracted from the CC S-matrix by fitting a one-pole formula with a linear background phase shift.

## Key numbers

* For the g9/2 partial wave with the standard Coulomb potential: CXSM gives Er = 14.954 MeV, Γ = 0.046 MeV; CC gives Er = 14.954 MeV, Γ = 0.047 MeV.
* For the i11/2 partial wave with the standard Coulomb potential: CXSM gives Er = 15.526 MeV, Γ = 0.003 MeV; CC gives Er = 15.526 MeV, Γ = 0.003 MeV.
* For the g9/2 partial wave with the dilatation-analytic Coulomb potential: CXSM gives Er = 14.933 MeV, Γ = 0.041 MeV; CS gives Er = 14.933 MeV, Γ = 0.041 MeV; CC gives Er = 14.934 MeV, Γ = 0.042 MeV.
* The single-particle neutron 1g9/2 bound state energy is (-22.878, 0.0000) MeV; the 2g9/2 neutron bound state is at (-4.060, 0.0000) MeV.
* The single-particle proton 1g9/2 bound state energy with the standard Coulomb potential is (-11.894, 0.0000) MeV; the 2g9/2 proton resonance is at (7.674, -6.2 × 10^-4) MeV.
* The Coulomb energy difference Δc used in the Lane equations is 18.9 MeV.
* The Woods-Saxon potential parameters: r0 = 1.19 fm, a = 0.75 fm, Vso = 11.6 MeV, nucleon potential depth 56.4 MeV, symmetry potential strength 0.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of complex-energy methods (Berggren basis and complex scaling) to the coupled-channel Lane model for isobaric analogue resonances, building on earlier developments of the complex energy shell model (CXSM) by the same group and the Gamow Shell Model by Michel et al. (2003, 2007). The results enable future use of these methods for analyzing experimental data on exotic neutron-rich nuclei, where IAR studies have been suggested as a surrogate for direct measurements of systems such as 11Li, 14Be, 7He, and 9He (Ter-Akopian 1997, Rogachev 2003, 2004). The paper also demonstrates that the CXSM offers a direct way to study the wave function configuration of the IAR, in contrast to the CS method.
