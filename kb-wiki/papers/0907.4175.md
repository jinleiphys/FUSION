# Influence of the nucleon-nucleon collision geometry on the determination of the nuclear modification factor for nucleon-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions

> The influence of the underlying nucleon-nucleon collision geometry on evaluations of the nuclear overlap function ($T_{AB}$) and number of binary collisions ($N_{coll}$) is studied. A narrowing of the spatial distribution of the hard-partons with large light-cone fraction $x$ in nucleons leads to a downward correction for $N_{coll}$ and $T_{AB}$, which in turn, results in an upward correction for the nuclear modification factor $R_{AB}$. The size of this correction is estimated for several experimentally motivated nucleon-nucleon overlap functions for hard-partons. It is found to be significant in peripheral nucleus-nucleus and nucleon-nucleus collisions, and are much larger at the LHC energy of $\sqrt{s}=5.5$ TeV than for the RHIC energy of $\sqrt{s}$=200 GeV. The implications for experimental measurements are also discussed.

## Key claim

The standard Glauber calculation of the nuclear overlap function \(T_{AB}\) and number of binary collisions \(N_{coll}\) systematically overestimates these quantities for peripheral collisions because it uses a uniform nucleon-nucleon overlap function based on soft partons, whereas hard-scattering processes involve a narrower spatial distribution of hard partons; this leads to an upward correction to the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AB}\) that is significant in peripheral nucleus-nucleus and nucleon-nucleus collisions, especially at LHC energies.

## Method

The author used a Monte Carlo Glauber approach to calculate \(N_{coll}\) and \(T_{AB}\) for Au-Au and deuteron-Au collisions, comparing the standard flat nucleon-nucleon overlap function (a step function with radius \(r_n = \sqrt{\sigma_{nn}^{inel}/\pi}/2\)) against four narrower hard-scattering overlap functions: a step function with half the radius, a truncated Gaussian profile from PYTHIA, a hard-sphere nucleon overlap, and a dipole formula for gluon spatial distribution derived from \(J/\Psi\) photo-production data. The ratio of \(N_{coll}^{hs}\) (using the hard-scattering overlap) to the nominal \(N_{coll}\) was evaluated as a function of impact parameter, number of participants \(N_{part}\), and centrality bins for both RHIC (\(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, \(\sigma_{nn}^{inel}=42\) mb) and LHC (\(\sqrt{s}=5.5\) TeV, \(\sigma_{nn}^{inel}=72\) mb) energies.

## Key numbers

* For Au-Au collisions at RHIC energy (\(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, \(\sigma_{nn}^{inel}=42\) mb), the downward correction to \(N_{coll}\) for the 70-80% centrality bin is about 5% for the Gaussian overlap function and about 15% for the hard-sphere overlap function.
* The bias for Au-Au collisions at LHC energy (\(\sqrt{s}=5.5\) TeV, \(\sigma_{nn}^{inel}=72\) mb) is about 60% larger than at RHIC energy.
* For deuteron-Au collisions at LHC energy, the correction factor for the 60-100% centrality range (where \(\langle N_{part}\rangle \approx 4\)) can be as large as 15%.
* The bias crosses zero at \(N_{part} \sim 150\) (or \(b_{AuAu} \sim 8\) fm) and becomes slightly positive (less than a few percent) for more central collisions.

## Context

This work extends the standard Glauber model formalism (Miller et al., 2007) by incorporating the fact that the transverse spatial distribution of hard partons (with large light-cone fraction \(x\)) is narrower than that of soft partons, as established by Frankfurt, Strikman, and Weiss (2004, 2005) and Weiss (2009). The bias was partially considered earlier by the PHENIX Collaboration using a Gaussian profile to estimate systematic errors on \(N_{coll}\), but that study was motivated by uncertainty in the nucleon matter distribution rather than the narrowing of hard-parton spatial distributions. The results imply that proper interpretation of the nuclear modification factor in peripheral p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC requires using the full Wong formula (Wong, 1984) with a hard-parton-specific nucleon-nucleon overlap function.
