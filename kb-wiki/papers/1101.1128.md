# Tracing Remnants of the Baryon Vector Current Anomaly in Neutron Radiative Beta Decay

> Harvey, Hill, and Hill have found that unexpected interactions involving the nucleon, photon, and weak gauge bosons at low energies emerge from gauging the axial anomaly of QCD under the full electroweak symmetry of the Standard Model (SM). In this contribution we consider how such interactions can be isolated through a triple-product momentum correlation in neutron radiative beta-decay. The correlation is both parity- and naively time-reversal-odd, so that it vanishes in the Standard Model save for effects induced by final-state interactions (FSI). Nevertheless, the correlation can be generated by sources of CP violation beyond the Standard Model, and such couplings, being spin-independent, are not constrained by the nonobservation of the neutron electric dipole moment (EDM). We consider the sorts of limits on its strength which can be determined at existing and anticipated facilities, as well as the size of induced correlations from known FSI. We also briefly consider the possibility of nuclear beta-decay studies as well as the prospects for muon-induced reaction studies.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a triple-product momentum correlation in neutron radiative beta-decay can isolate the pseudo-Chern-Simons term arising from the baryon vector current anomaly, but the correlation vanishes in the Standard Model except for final-state interaction effects; its observation would require sources of CP violation beyond the Standard Model, and such couplings are not constrained by neutron electric dipole moment limits because they are spin-independent.

## Method

The authors compute the interference term between the Standard Model V-A amplitude and the pseudo-Chern-Simons contact interaction in the transition matrix element for neutron radiative beta-decay, working to leading recoil order and linear order in the coupling constant. They define an asymmetry based on the sign of the triple product eta = (l_e x l_nu) dot k, where l_e, l_nu, and k are the electron, antineutrino, and photon momenta respectively. They also estimate the statistical sensitivity achievable at existing and planned neutron facilities, and evaluate the size of induced correlations from Standard Model final-state interactions using Cutkosky rules.

## Key numbers

* Asymmetry for photon energy threshold 0.035 MeV: A = -2.60 x 10^-3 Im(xi_bar) (MeV^-2), with branching ratio 1.83 x 10^-3
* Asymmetry for photon energy threshold 0.3 MeV: A = -1.34 x 10^-2 Im(xi_bar) (MeV^-2), with branching ratio 8.62 x 10^-5
* Maximum photon energy in neutron radiative beta-decay: 0.782 MeV
* Vector-meson dominance estimate for charged-current coupling: xi_bar_rho approximately 4.6 x 10^-7 MeV^-2
* Vector-meson dominance estimate for neutral-current coupling: xi_bar_omega approximately 4.1 x 10^-6 MeV^-2
* Statistical error on asymmetry achievable in one week at NIST with 0.3 MeV threshold: approximately 7 x 10^-3
* Projected statistical reach with planned New Guide Hall at NIST: approximately 1 x 10^-3
* Energy release in 19Ne decay: approximately 2.7 MeV
* 19Ne lifetime: 17.2 seconds

## Context

This work extends the theoretical findings of Harvey, Hill, and Hill (2007, 2008) who discovered that gauging the axial anomaly of QCD under the full electroweak symmetry produces unexpected low-energy contact interactions of pseudo-Chern-Simons form. The paper proposes neutron radiative beta-decay as the simplest system to test for these interactions, and also discusses nuclear beta-decay studies at rare isotope facilities like FRIB and muon-induced reactions as complementary probes. The analysis enables experimental searches for new CP-violating physics that would be inaccessible through neutron electric dipole moment measurements.
