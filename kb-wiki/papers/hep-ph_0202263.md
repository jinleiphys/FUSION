# Renormalization of self-consistent Schwinger-Dyson equations at finite temperature

> We show that Dyson resummation schemes based on Baym's $Φ$-derivable approximations can be renormalized with counter term structures solely defined on the vacuum level. First applications to the self-consistent solution of the sunset self-energy in $ϕ^4$-theory are presented.

# Renormalization of self-consistent Schwinger-Dyson equations at finite temperature

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that Dyson resummation schemes based on Baym's Φ-derivable approximations can be renormalized using counter term structures defined solely at the vacuum level, provided one uses the real-time formalism and BPHZ renormalization to isolate divergent vacuum subdiagrams from temperature-dependent parts. The claim is conditional on the systematic expansion around the self-consistent vacuum solution and the use of the Bethe-Salpeter ladder equation to render the finite-temperature self-energy finite.

## Method
The authors split the self-energy and propagator into three parts: a renormalized self-consistent vacuum part, a part linear in the deviation from the vacuum propagator, and a remainder. The linear part is expressed via a four-point Bethe-Salpeter kernel Λ, which is renormalized through a sweep-up/sweep-down procedure that subtracts divergences using differences of the vacuum four-point vertex. The finite-temperature self-energy is then computed by substituting the renormalized Bethe-Salpeter function into the linear integral equation, while the remainder is finite after subtracting explicit vacuum subdivergences. First numerical applications are performed for the self-consistent sunset self-energy in φ⁴-theory on a 200 × 200 momentum lattice.

## Key numbers
- The self-consistent self-energy was calculated for λ = 30, m = 140 MeV, and T = 250 MeV, shown as functions of p₀ for various 3-momenta.
- The vacuum threshold for decay into three particles is at p₀² − p⃗² ≥ 9M².
- Numerical calculations were performed on a 200 × 200 lattice in the (p₀, |p⃗|)-plane.
- The in-medium effective mass M and spectral width Γ are defined via the dispersion relation [p₀² − m² − Re Σ(p₀, p⃗)]_{p=(M,0⃗)} = 0 and Γ = −Im Σ(p)/p₀|_{p=(M,0⃗)}.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous renormalization formalism (van Hees and Knoll, 2001) to numerical implementation, demonstrating that the method works for self-consistent approximations derived from the 2PI effective action. It builds on Baym's Φ-derivable approximations (Baym, 1962) and the BPHZ renormalization scheme, and addresses the long-standing problem of whether self-consistent Dyson resummations can be renormalized with temperature-independent counter terms. The results enable future applications to renormalized Kadanoff-Baym equations for non-equilibrium quantum transport, and to QCD or hadronic matter at finite temperature and baryon density, though the authors note that gauge symmetries and Ward-Takahashi identities remain problematic for self-consistent schemes beyond the one-point level.
