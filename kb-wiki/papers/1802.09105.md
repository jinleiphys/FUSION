# Configurational entropy and $ρ$ and $ϕ$ mesons production in QCD

> In the present work the electroproduction for diffractive $ρ$ and $ϕ$ mesons by considering AdS/QCD correspondence and Color Glass Condensate (CGC) approximation are studied with respect to the associated dipole cross section, whose parameters are studied and analysed in the framework of the configurational entropy. Our results suggest different quantum states of the nuclear matter, showing that the extremal points of the nuclear configurational entropy is able to reflect a true description of the $ρ$ and $ϕ$ mesons production, using current data concerning light quark masses. During the computations parameters, obtained in fitting procedure, coincide to the experimental within 0.1 %.

## Key claim

The configurational entropy's extremal points can be used to derive the polarization parameter λ for diffractive ρ and φ meson production cross sections, reproducing the values obtained from fitting to 2015 HERA data within 0.1% error, but the method also reveals a second possible minimum for φ production that could correspond to an alternative parameter set sensitive to quark mass variations.

## Method

The author computed the nuclear configurational entropy from the spatial Fourier transform of the diffractive ρ and φ meson production cross sections, using the dipole cross section from the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) model combined with holographic light-front meson wavefunctions from AdS/QCD. For each meson, three of the four adjustable parameters (σ₀, x₀, γₛ) were fixed to the best-fit values from Ahmady (2015), while the polarization parameter λ was varied freely to locate the minima of the configurational entropy.

## Key numbers

- For ρ meson production, the minimum of the nuclear configurational entropy occurs at λ = 0.206, matching the value from Fit C of Ahmady (2015) where σ₀ = 29.9 mb, γₛ = 0.724, x₀ = 6.33 × 10⁻⁶, with effective quark masses m_{u,d} = [0.046, 0.14] GeV and χ²/d.o.f = 1.07.
- For φ meson production, the deepest minimum of the configurational entropy occurs at λ = 0.222, matching the value from Fit B of Ahmady (2015) where σ₀ = 24.9 mb, γₛ = 0.722, x₀ = 1.80 × 10⁻⁵, with effective quark masses m_{u,d}, mₛ = [0.046, 0.14] GeV and χ²/d.o.f = 1.02.
- The calculation error is less than 0.1% compared with experimental data from Ahmady (2015).
- The diffractive slope parameter uses B_D = 14N (1 GeV²/(Q² + M_V²))^0.2 + N, with N ≈ 0.55 GeV⁻².

## Context

This work extends the configurational entropy framework previously applied to nuclear and particle physics by Karapetyan (2016, 2017) and to meson production by Bernardini (2016) and Braga (2017), now specifically to diffractive vector meson electroproduction. The results confirm the parameter fits from Ahmady (2015) based on 2015 HERA data, and suggest that configurational entropy minima can serve as a predictive tool for determining the polarization parameter λ, potentially reducing the need for multi-parameter fitting. The second minimum found for φ production opens the possibility of alternative parameter sets that could be tested against future experimental data.
