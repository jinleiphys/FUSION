# Radiative transitions between $0^{-+}$ and $1^{--}$ heavy quarkonia on the light front

> We present calculations of radiative transitions between vector and pseudoscalar quarkonia in the light-front Hamiltonian approach. The valence sector light-front wavefunctions of heavy quarkonia are obtained from the Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) approach in a holographic basis. We study the transition form factor with both the traditional "good current" $J^+$ and the transverse current $\vec J_\perp$ (in particular, $J^R=J^x+i J^y$). This allows us to investigate the role of rotational symmetry by considering vector mesons with different magnetic projections ($m_j=0,\pm 1$). We use the $m_j=0$ state of the vector meson to obtain the transition form factor, since this procedure employs the dominant spin components of the light-front wavefunctions and is more robust in practical calculations. While the $m_j=\pm 1$ states are also examined, transition form factors depend on subdominant components of the light-front wavefunctions and are less robust. Transitions between states below the open-flavor thresholds are computed, including those for excited states. Comparisons are made with the experimental measurements as well as with Lattice QCD and quark model results. In addition, we apply the transverse current to calculate the decay constant of vector mesons where we obtain consistent results using either $m_j=0$ or $m_j=1$ light-front wavefunctions. This consistency provides evidence for features of rotational symmetry within the model.

## Key claim

The paper calculates radiative transition form factors between vector and pseudoscalar heavy quarkonia using light-front wavefunctions from the Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) approach, but the authors qualify that the most robust results come from using the transverse current with the \(m_j=0\) state of the vector meson, since this procedure employs the dominant spin components of the light-front wavefunctions, while results from \(m_j=\pm 1\) states depend on subdominant components and are less robust.

## Method

The authors obtain valence-sector light-front wavefunctions for heavy quarkonia by diagonalizing an effective Hamiltonian in a basis representation using BLFQ with a holographic basis, which includes transverse confinement from light-front holography, longitudinal confinement, and one-gluon exchange with a running coupling. They compute the M1 transition form factor \(V(Q^2)\) using both the "good current" \(J^+\) and the transverse current \(\vec J_\perp\) (specifically \(J^R = J^x + i J^y\)), extracting the form factor from the \(m_j=0\) state of the vector meson via the transverse current as the primary method. The transition form factors are evaluated at finite basis truncations \(N_{\max}=L_{\max}=8, 16, 24, 32\) and extrapolated to the complete basis limit using a second-order polynomial in \(1/N_{\max}\).

## Key numbers

- For allowed transitions (\(nS \to nS + \gamma\)), the transition form factor at zero momentum transfer \(\hat V(0)\) is approximately 2, close to the nonrelativistic limit.
- The extrapolations of \(\hat V(0)\) using three different fitting functions agree to within 1% of each other.
- The mass spectrum from the BLFQ model agrees with PDG data with an r.m.s. mass deviation of 30 to 40 MeV for states below the open flavor thresholds.
- For the \(J/\psi(1S) \to \eta_c(1S) + \gamma\) transition, the extrapolation uncertainty is given by the difference between the result at \(N_{\max}=L_{\max}=32\) and the extrapolated value.

## Context

This work extends the BLFQ model previously developed by some of the same authors (Li et al., 2017) for heavy quarkonia, which provided mass spectra, decay constants, radii, distribution amplitudes, and parton distributions, by now calculating radiative transitions. The results are compared with experimental measurements, lattice QCD calculations, and quark model results (such as those from Eichten et al., 2007, and Brambilla et al., 2005). The calculations enable predictions for radiative transitions that have not yet been measured experimentally, and the consistency of vector meson decay constants obtained from different magnetic projections provides evidence for features of rotational symmetry within the model.
