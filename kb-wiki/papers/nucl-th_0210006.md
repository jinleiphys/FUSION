# Shell model in the complex energy plane and two-particle resonances

> An implementation of the shell-model to the complex energy plane is presented. The representation used in the method consists of bound single-particle states, Gamow resonances and scattering waves on the complex energy plane. Two-particle resonances are evaluated and their structure in terms of the single-particle degreees of freedom are analysed. It is found that two-particle resonances are mainly built upon bound states and Gamow resonances, but the contribution of the scattering states is also important.

# Shell model in the complex energy plane and two-particle resonances

## Key claim
Two-particle resonances are mainly built upon bound states and Gamow resonances, but the contribution of scattering states is also important; contrary to intuitive assumptions, the narrowest single-particle resonance does not always dominate the formation of narrow two-particle resonances, and increasing the interaction strength can make some resonances wider rather than narrower due to the non-Hermitian Berggren metric.

## Method
The authors implement a shell-model formalism in the complex energy plane using the Berggren representation, which consists of bound single-particle states, Gamow resonances, and discretized complex-energy scattering states. Two-particle resonances are evaluated by solving the dispersion relation for a separable two-body interaction (derivative of Woods-Saxon potential) without matrix diagonalization, using a rectangular contour in the complex energy plane to isolate physically relevant two-particle states from the non-resonant continuum background.

## Key numbers
- For the two-neutron case in ⁷⁸Ni (WS1 potential), the shell N=50 shows a gap of about 3.6 MeV between the lowest particle state (1d₅/₂) and the highest hole state (0g₉/₂)
- The rectangular contour for two-neutron calculations used a=0.5 MeV, b=9 MeV, c=-4 MeV, d=20 MeV, with 10 Gaussian points per MeV on contour lines (except 5 points per MeV on the last segment), yielding n_g=225 scattering states
- Convergence to six digits in evaluated quantities required resonances to lie at least 300 keV from the contour borders
- For the two-neutron case with no bound single-particle states (WS2, V₀=37 MeV), the contour used a=0.1 MeV, b=13 MeV, c=-6 MeV, d=26 MeV
- For two-proton resonances in ¹⁰²Te, the contour used a=0.1 MeV, b=19 MeV, c=-1 MeV, d=26 MeV, with n_g=298 scattering states per partial wave
- The ground state of ⁸⁰Ni was assumed to lie below 2ε(1d₅/₂), with a maximum correlation energy gap of 2.527 MeV corresponding to G_α=0.0028 MeV
- For the (0h₁₁/₂)² two-neutron state at G_α=0.0020 MeV, neglecting the continuum (n_g=0) gave real part errors of 1.2 MeV and unphysical positive imaginary parts

## Context
This work extends the Berggren representation approach (Berggren 1968, Romo 1968) from one-particle to two-particle resonances, building on earlier developments by Liotta and collaborators. It provides a method to isolate physical two-particle resonant states from the continuous background, which was outlined schematically in Rol (year not specified in text). The formalism enables microscopic calculations of two-particle resonances in nuclei near drip lines, including halo nuclei (Hansen and collaborators, various years), and can treat cases where the Fermi level is unbound, relevant for nuclei far from beta stability. The approach contrasts with standard shell-model calculations that ignore the continuum (Blomqvist and collaborators, various years) and with cluster decay calculations that use bound representations (Preparata and collaborators, various years).
