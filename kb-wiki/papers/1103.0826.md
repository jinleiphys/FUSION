# Diquarks: a QCD sum rule perspective

> We propose a phenomenological QCD sum rule with an explicit diquark field to investigate the essential ingredients inside the hadrons. Introducing the mass (m_ϕ) and the condensate (ϕ^2) for the diquark field as parameters in the model, we find that the sum rule works well for Lambda, Lambda_c and Lambda_b. This implies that these Lambda baryons can be represented by a diquark and a quark configuration. We also find that there is a duality relation among the parameters (m_ϕ, ϕ^2), for which the sum rule is equally good. In the limit when ϕ^2 =0 we find m_ϕ=0.4 GeV, which can be thought as the constituent diquark mass.

## Key claim

The authors propose a phenomenological QCD sum rule that explicitly includes a diquark field, and find that the masses of the Lambda, Lambda_c, and Lambda_b baryons can be reproduced, implying these baryons can be represented by a diquark and a quark configuration. However, the sum rule works only for these specific Lambda baryons and fails for the nucleon, and there exists a duality relation between the diquark mass and diquark condensate parameters such that multiple parameter pairs yield equally good sum rules.

## Method

The authors introduce an explicit scalar-diquark field phi with mass m_phi and condensate <phi^2> into the QCD Lagrangian, then calculate the correlation function for the Lambda_h current J_Lambda = phi^dagger h using the operator product expansion (OPE) to leading order in alpha_s up to dimension-6 condensates. They apply Borel-transformed dispersion relations to extract the Lambda_h mass from the sum rule, treating m_phi and <phi^2> as free parameters to be determined by requiring Borel stability. The sum rule is evaluated for Lambda (h = s), Lambda_c (h = c), and Lambda_b (h = b) using conventional values for quark masses and condensates from the literature.

## Key numbers

- For the Lambda baryon with diquark mass m_phi = 0.2 GeV, a stable Borel curve reproducing the physical Lambda mass is obtained with diquark condensate <phi^2> = -(0.04 GeV)^2 (corresponding to scaling factor lambda = -0.5).
- The Borel window for the Lambda sum rule is between M^2 = 1.3 GeV^2 and M^2 = 2.2 GeV^2.
- In the limit where the diquark condensate <phi^2> = 0, the diquark mass required is m_phi = 0.4 GeV, interpreted as the constituent diquark mass.
- For Lambda_c and Lambda_b, the same diquark parameter sets that work for Lambda reproduce the observed masses, with continuum thresholds s_0 = 7 GeV^2 and 40 GeV^2 respectively.
- Input parameters include: gluon condensate <(alpha/pi) G^2> = (0.33 GeV)^4, strange quark condensate <sbar s> = -0.8 (0.23 GeV)^3, strange quark mass m_s = 0.12 GeV, charm quark mass m_c = 1.47 GeV, bottom quark mass m_b = 4.6 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to baryons by introducing an explicit diquark degree of freedom, building on the long-standing idea of diquark correlations in hadron physics dating back to Ida and Lichtenberg (1960s) and Jaffe (2004). The method is applied to the Lambda baryon family, where the color-spin interaction between the heavy quark and the light quarks is suppressed, allowing the diquark correlation to remain strong. The results enable future studies of other hadronic configurations where diquark correlations are expected to be important, such as hadrons with large angular momentum as discussed by Selem and Wilczek (2006), and exotic configurations with heavy quarks as studied by Lipkin (1987) and Lee et al. (2007, 2009). The authors note that the sum rule fails for the nucleon and for the sigma meson, consistent with the expectation that the diquark description is only valid in configurations where the diquark correlation remains intact.
