# Two-photon exchange corrections in elastic electron-proton scattering

> We apply a subtracted dispersion relation (DR) formalism with the aim to improve predictions for the two-photon exchange (TPE) corrections to elastic electron-proton scattering observables at small momentum transfers. We study the formalism on the elastic TPE contribution in comparison with existing data for unpolarized cross sections. We extend the general formalism of TPE to elastic scattering with massive lepton and perform a numerical estimate of the muon-proton scattering at low momentum transfer in view of the upcoming muon-proton scattering experiment (MUSE). We study the influence of the double-virtual Compton scattering (VVCS) subtraction function on the unpolarized lepton-proton scattering cross-section. We show that the resulting TPE correction is negligible in the electron-proton scattering and smaller than planned uncertainties of the MUSE experiment for the subtraction functions evaluated in chiral perturbation theory.

## Key claim
The two-photon exchange (TPE) correction to elastic electron-proton scattering at small momentum transfers is negligible for electron-proton scattering and smaller than the planned uncertainties of the MUSE experiment for muon-proton scattering, provided the subtraction functions are evaluated in chiral perturbation theory.

## Method
The author applies a subtracted dispersion relation (DR) formalism to the elastic TPE contribution, comparing it with existing unpolarized cross-section data and with a hadronic model that assumes an on-shell form of the off-shell photon-proton vertex. The formalism is extended to massive leptons for muon-proton scattering, and the influence of the double-virtual Compton scattering (VVCS) subtraction function on the unpolarized cross-section is studied.

## Key numbers
- TPE correction for electron-proton scattering is negligible at small momentum transfers (no specific numerical value given for the correction itself, but it is stated to be smaller than experimental uncertainties).
- For muon-proton scattering at MUSE kinematics, the TPE correction from the elastic intermediate state is of the same order of magnitude as the expected uncertainty of the MUSE experiment (no explicit numerical value for the correction provided).
- The VVCS subtraction function contribution to TPE correction in muon-proton scattering at beam momentum p = 153 MeV is shown in comparison with the leading elastic contribution (Feshbach term); the effect from the enhanced subtraction function is of the same order as the expected MUSE uncertainty or slightly below it, while chiral perturbation theory predictions are "few orders of magnitude smaller."
- The subtracted DR predictions are shown for Q² = 0.05 GeV² and Q² = 1 GeV², with three subtraction points ε₀ = 0.2, 0.5, 0.8.

## Context
This work extends the dispersion relation approach of Borisyuk (2008) and compares it with the hadronic model of Blunden (2005) for TPE evaluation. It addresses the proton radius puzzle, where TPE estimates at small momentum transfer can reduce uncertainties in electron-proton scattering data. The results are directly relevant to the upcoming MUSE experiment (Gilman, 2013) and build on prior work by Gorchtein (2004), Carlson (2011), and Miller (2012) regarding the VVCS subtraction function.
