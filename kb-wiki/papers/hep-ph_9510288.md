# Are We Really Measuring the $ρ$-Value?

> The ``normal'' analysis of $\pbar p$ and $pp$ elastic scattering uses a `spinless' Coulomb amplitude, \ie a Rutherford amplitude ($2\sqrtπα/t$) multiplied by a Coulomb form factor $G^2(t)$, an {\em ansatz} that pretends that the nucleon does not have any magnetic scattering. In this note, we investigate the role of the anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon, $κ\approx 1.79$. Given the method of analysis currently used by most published experiments, we conclude that the current experimentally inferred values of $ρ$ for $\pbar p$ should be {\em systemeatically lowered} by $\approx 0.005$---0.0100 and, correspondingly, the $ρ$ values for $pp$ should be {\em systematically raised} by the same amount. We discuss the theoretical uncertainties and a method of experimentally minimizing them.

## Key claim

The standard "spinless" Coulomb amplitude used in analyses of antiproton-proton and proton-proton elastic scattering neglects the nucleon's anomalous magnetic moment (κ ≈ 1.79), and correcting for this effect systematically shifts the experimentally inferred ρ-values by approximately 0.005–0.010, with the sign of the shift depending on the particle–antiparticle system.

## Method

The author derives the full one-photon-exchange electromagnetic scattering amplitude including the anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon, expands the resulting differential cross section for small momentum transfer |t|, and compares it to the conventional "spinless" Rutherford amplitude. Using the published UA4/2 data at √s = 541 GeV and the E710 data at √s = 1.8 TeV, the author imposes equality of the integrated cross sections over the experimental t-intervals to compute the shift in ρ without refitting the raw data. A toy nuclear model with a nuclear "anomalous magnetic moment" κ_N is also introduced to estimate the theoretical uncertainty from unknown nuclear spin-flip contributions.

## Key numbers

- The anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon is κ ≈ 1.79.
- For the UA4/2 experiment (√s = 541 GeV, t-interval 0.00075 ≤ |t| ≤ 0.12 GeV², published ρ = 0.135 ± 0.015), the exact numerical shift in ρ is Δρ_UA4 = −0.0105.
- For the E710 experiment (√s = 1.8 TeV, t_min = 0.00075 GeV², t_max = 0.077 GeV², ρ = 0.134 ± 0.069), the exact numerical shift is Δρ_E710 = −0.004.
- The shift in ρ for UA4/2 is comparable to the quoted total error of ±0.015 and larger than the statistical error of ±0.007.
- For the toy nuclear model with κ_N = 0, the shift in ρ is reduced to approximately −0.006.
- The effective form factor parameter a' = 3.674, compared to the standard a = 5.6338 (from Λ² = 0.71 GeV²).

## Context

This work extends the standard analysis of elastic scattering used by experiments such as UA4/2 (1995) and E710, which employed the "spinless" Coulomb amplitude as described by the Bethe–Cahn phase formalism (Cahn, 1982). The author's conclusion that ρ-values for antiproton-proton scattering should be systematically lowered and those for proton-proton scattering raised contradicts the earlier analysis by Buttimore (no year given), which did not account for the experimental t-interval. The paper enables future experiments to reduce theoretical uncertainties by splitting data into two distinct t-regions and using an effective form factor G_eff(t) with Λ'² ≈ 1.0 GeV² instead of the standard dipole form factor.
