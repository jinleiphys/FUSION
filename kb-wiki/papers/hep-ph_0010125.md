# Weak and Electromagnetic Interactions of Hyperons: a Chiral Approach

> A range of issues in the field of weak and electromagnetic interactions of hyperons is presented from the perspective of (broken) chiral symmetry, together with an assessment of where important challenges remain.

## Key claim

The paper argues that chiral perturbation theory (broken chiral symmetry) provides a coherent framework for understanding weak and electromagnetic interactions of hyperons, but acknowledges that many basic questions remain unresolved and that the approach is limited to energy-momenta much less than 1 GeV.

## Method

The author reviews the application of effective field theory and chiral perturbation theory to hyperon physics, beginning with an introduction to spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and Goldstone's theorem in QCD. The formalism is developed by constructing effective Lagrangians that respect the chiral symmetry of QCD, with the pseudoscalar mesons (pi, K, eta) treated as would-be Goldstone bosons whose masses arise from explicit chiral symmetry breaking via quark masses. The approach is then extended to include baryons and their weak and electromagnetic interactions.

## Key numbers

- The neutral and charged hyperons discovered by Rochester and Butler had lower mass limits of 770 +/- 200 m_e and 980 +/- 150 m_e respectively
- The Gell-Mann-Okubo mass relation 3 m_eta^2 + m_pi^2 - 4 m_K^2 = 0 is well-satisfied experimentally
- The effective field theory is expected to be valid for energies and momenta much less than 1 GeV, where the heavy scale is set by M_N, m_rho, etc. at approximately 1 GeV
- The pion mass is about 140 MeV, while the nearest 1/2- resonance to the 1/2+ nucleon lies nearly 600 MeV higher in energy

## Context

This review extends the chiral symmetry approach previously applied to meson systems (developed over the preceding fifteen years) to the more complex case of hyperon weak and electromagnetic interactions. The work builds upon the foundational understanding of SU(3) symmetry from the 1960s and the development of chiral perturbation theory by authors such as Gasser and Leutwyler. The paper omits discussion of strong interactions and polarization issues in hyperon production, focusing instead on what chiral symmetry can reveal about weak and electromagnetic processes, while identifying where important challenges remain for future theoretical and experimental work.
