# Improved method for partial-wave decomposition of two-pion exchange three-nucleon force

> In this article an efficient method to calculate the matrix elements of three-nucleon force is presented. The new method is improved version of partial-wave decomposition presented in Few-Body Syst. 22 (1997) 107, which simplifies expression to be evaluated and permits to reduce computational effort as much as six times. Proposed method naturally applies to Faddeev-type calculations but also can be used by any method relaying on partial-wave decomposition.

## Key claim

The paper presents an improved partial-wave decomposition method for two-pion exchange three-nucleon forces that reduces computational effort by up to a factor of six compared to the prior method of Huber (1997), by requiring only one basis transformation operation instead of two and by avoiding the inclusion of unphysical symmetric intermediate states.

## Method

The author reformulates the Faddeev equations to redistribute the three-nucleon force terms, then derives new expressions for the matrix elements of the three operator terms (C1, C3, and C4) that appear in the two-pion exchange three-nucleon force. For the C4 term, the operator is rewritten using the identity relating the spin of the spectator nucleon to the total spin of the three-nucleon system, which eliminates contributions from unphysical states. The resulting expressions require only a single particle-basis transformation between physical antisymmetric states, whereas the prior method of Huber (1997) required two such transformations involving both physical and unphysical states.

## Key numbers

- Computational effort reduction: the new method permits evaluation of two-pion exchange three-nucleon force terms "as much as six times faster" than the prior partial-wave decomposition of Huber (1997)
- The basis transformation operation coupling unphysical and physical states in both bra and ket is "numerically at least four times heavier" than one coupling only physical states
- The basis transformation transforming a combination of unphysical and physical ket states into a physical bra state is "numerically at least two times heavier" than one coupling only physical states

## Context

This work extends the partial-wave decomposition technique for two-pion exchange three-nucleon forces introduced by Huber (1997), which required two basis transformation operations and the inclusion of unphysical symmetric states. The improved method applies naturally to Faddeev-type calculations and can be used by any method relying on partial-wave decomposition. The author notes that the technique can be applied to evaluate any momentum or coordinate space three-nucleon force of the form V_ijk = sum over cyclic permutations of V_ij V_jk, and that a variation of this technique may be possible for more complex forces such as the Illinois force containing three-pion ring terms.
