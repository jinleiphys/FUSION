# Analysis of OPAL Bose-Einstein Correlation at $Z^0$-pole by the second conventional formula

> We can obtain a second conventional formula (${\rm CF_{II}}$) with two components by extending the conventional formula ${\rm CF_{I}}$ with one component for Bose-Einstein Correlation (BEC). We used ${\rm CF_{II}}$ to analyze the BEC at $Z^0$-pole as part of the OPAL collaboration. $R_1({\rm G})=0.91\pm 0.03$ fm ($λ_1=0.61\pm 0.03$) and $R_2({\rm G})=2.57\pm 0.44$ fm ($λ_1=0.35\pm 0.09$) are the estimated interaction region, where $λ_1$ and $λ_2$ are the degrees of coherence, and G is the Gaussian distribution, respectively. Long range correlation (LRC) is also studied.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a "second conventional formula" (CF\(_{II}\)) with two Gaussian components to describe Bose-Einstein correlations at the \(Z^0\)-pole, claiming that this two-source model (reflecting 2-jet and 3-jet events) yields a better fit to OPAL data than the standard single-component formula, but the improvement is conditional on the specific choice of long-range correlation (LRC) function and on constraints applied to the fit parameters.

## Method

The authors extended the standard one-component Bose-Einstein correlation formula (CF\(_{I}\)) to a two-component form (CF\(_{II}\)) containing two exchange functions \(E_{\rm BE_1}\) and \(E_{\rm BE_2}\) with associated source radii \(R_1\), \(R_2\) and coherence parameters \(\lambda_1\), \(\lambda_2\). They fitted this CF\(_{II}\) multiplied by various long-range correlation (LRC) functions to the OPAL collaboration's published BEC data at the \(Z^0\)-pole, using Gaussian and exponential forms for the exchange functions and polynomial or power-series forms for the LRC, with constraints derived from the earlier CF\(_{I}\) fit results.

## Key numbers

* \(R_1({\rm G}) = 0.91 \pm 0.03\) fm and \(R_2({\rm G}) = 2.57 \pm 0.44\) fm from the abstract (Gaussian exchange functions).
* \(\lambda_1 = 0.61 \pm 0.03\) and \(\lambda_2 = 0.35 \pm 0.09\) from the abstract (degrees of coherence).
* In the CF\(_{II}\) fit with \(\lambda_1 + \lambda_2 \le 1.0\) constraint: \(R_1 = 0.925 \pm 0.048\) fm, \(R_2 = 1.926 \pm 0.277\) fm, \(\lambda_1 = 0.840 \pm 0.050\), \(\lambda_2 = 0.160 \pm 0.024\), \(\chi^2/{\rm ndf} = 104.9/59\) (\(p = 0.022\%\)).
* In the CF\(_{II}\) fit without the \(\lambda_1+\lambda_2\) bound but assuming \(\lambda_1+\lambda_2'=1.0\): \(R_1 = 0.931 \pm 0.009\) fm, \(R_2 = 2.506 \pm 0.339\) fm, \(\lambda_1 = 0.841 \pm 0.010\), \(\lambda_2 = 0.521 \pm 0.159\), \(\chi^2/{\rm ndf} = 98.7/59\) (\(p = 0.091\%\)).
* Using the alternating power-series LRC (LRC\(_{\rm (a.p.s.)}\)): \(R_1 = 0.895 \pm 0.017\) fm, \(R_2 = 2.393 \pm 0.512\) fm, \(\lambda_1 = 0.498 \pm 0.016\), \(\lambda_2 = 0.223 \pm 0.075\), \(\chi^2/{\rm ndf} = 86.3/59\) (\(p = 1.2\%\)).
* After correcting for \(\pi\)-\(K\) contamination using \(f(Q)=0.81-0.07Q\): \(R_1 = 0.912 \pm 0.031\) fm, \(R_2 = 2.574 \pm 0.435\) fm, \(\lambda_1 = 0.605 \pm 0.026\), \(\lambda_2 = 0.348 \pm 0.087\), \(\chi^2/{\rm ndf} = 85.6/59\) (\(p = 1.34\%\)).
* The ratio of 3-jet to all-jet events extracted from the LRC\(_{\rm (a.p.s.)}\) fit is \(14\%\), compared to the OPAL empirical value of \(17.4 \pm 0.7\%\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses of CMS Bose-Einstein correlations at 0.9 TeV and 7 TeV (Biyajima 2018, Mizoguchi 2019, Biyajima 2019) where the single-component CF\(_{I}\) gave large \(\chi^2/{\rm ndf}\) values. It applies the two-component CF\(_{II}\) formalism to the OPAL \(Z^0\)-pole data originally analyzed by Acton (1991) using CF\(_{I}\). The paper enables future application of the two-source model to other BEC datasets, particularly CMS data at higher energies, and provides a framework for separating resonance contributions (from \(\eta'\) and \(\eta\) decay chains) from the genuine Bose-Einstein signal.
