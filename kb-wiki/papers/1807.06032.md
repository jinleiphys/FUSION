# Multiparticle correlations and collectivity in small systems from the initial state

> We report on recent progress in understanding multiparticle correlations in small systems from the initial state. First, we consider a proof-of-principle parton model, which we use to demonstrate that many of the multiparticle correlations observed in light-heavy ion collisions, often ascribed to hydrodynamic collectivity, can be qualitatively reproduced in an initial state model. Then, we study the two-particle harmonics $v_2$ and $v_3$ for p/d/$^3$He+Au collisions at RHIC using the dilute-dense Color Glass Condensate Effective Field Theory framework. We show that this provides a viable alternative explanation to hydrodynamics, and elaborate on how such modeling can be improved.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that multiparticle correlations in small collision systems (p/d/³He+Au), often attributed to hydrodynamic collectivity from a quark-gluon plasma, can be qualitatively and even quantitatively reproduced by purely initial-state models within the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory, without requiring final-state hydrodynamic evolution.

## Method
The authors first develop a proof-of-principle parton model where eikonal quarks scatter coherently off a dense gluonic target, described by Wilson lines in the McLerran-Venugopalan model, to compute multiparticle cumulants and Fourier harmonics v_n{m}. They then perform a more realistic event-by-event calculation of two-particle v_2 and v_3 for p/d/³He+Au collisions at RHIC using the dilute-dense CGC framework, where initial color charge densities are determined analogously to the IP-Glasma model and all free parameters are constrained by fitting the STAR d+Au multiplicity distribution.

## Key numbers
- The parton model uses a fixed Gaussian width B_p = 4 GeV⁻² for the projectile quark Wigner function.
- For the parton model, p_⟂^max = 3 GeV is used when calculating Fourier harmonics as a function of Q_s,T².
- The dilute-dense gluon calculation reproduces the ordering of v_2 and v_3 for p/d/³He+Au collisions at RHIC as measured by PHENIX (Aidala 2018), with v_2 described well and v_3 overestimated for p+Au and d+Au.
- The multiplicity distribution from the dilute-dense model is a convolution of negative binomial distributions, constrained by STAR d+Au data (Abelev 2008).

## Context
This work extends the Glasma graph approximation (Dumitru 2008) by including coherent multiple scattering, which is shown to be essential for generating a finite v_2{4} from the initial state. It directly challenges the interpretation of the PHENIX system scan (Aidala 2018) as uniquely requiring hydrodynamic response to initial geometry. The results enable future comparisons with classical Yang-Mills dynamics calculations and motivate inclusion of fragmentation effects (Schenke 2016) and quantification of differences between initial-state and hydrodynamic frameworks.
