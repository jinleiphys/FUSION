# Nuclear structure of 178Hf related to the spin-16, 31-year isomer

> The projected shell model is used to study the multi-quasiparticle and collective excitations of 178Hf. With an axially symmetric basis, the spin-16 isomer at 2.4 MeV appears to be well separated in energy/spin space from other configurations. However, projected energy surface calculations suggest that 178Hf has significant softness to axially asymmetric shapes, which can strongly modify the level distribution. The implications for photodeexitation of the isomer are discussed.

## Key claim

The projected shell model (PSM) calculations show that the spin-16, 2.4 MeV isomer in 178Hf is well separated in energy from other configurations, suggesting that a series of external excitations via gateway states may be necessary to trigger isomeric decay; however, the nucleus exhibits significant softness to axially asymmetric (gamma) shapes, which could strongly modify the level distribution and potentially enhance excitation of gateway states.

## Method

The authors used the projected shell model (PSM) with an axially symmetric multi-quasiparticle basis built from deformed Nilsson plus BCS calculations, including up to 4-quasiparticle configurations in three major shells for neutrons and protons. They diagonalized a Hamiltonian containing quadrupole-quadrupole, monopole-pairing, and quadrupole-pairing interactions after angular-momentum projection, and extended the PSM to compute projected energy surfaces as functions of both quadrupole deformation epsilon2 and triaxial deformation gamma.

## Key numbers

- The spin-16 isomer in 178Hf has an excitation energy of 2.4 MeV and a half-life of 31 years.
- The deformation parameters used were epsilon2 = 0.251 and epsilon4 = 0.056.
- The 16+ isomeric band is separated from other bands by an energy gap of nearly 500 keV.
- The projected energy surfaces show that energies remain almost constant for gamma values up to about 15 degrees, indicating gamma softness.
- The monopole-pairing force constants were given by G_M = [20.12 -/+ 13.13 (N-Z)/A] A^{-1} for neutrons and protons respectively.
- The quadrupole-pairing strength parameter G_Q was set to 0.16 times G_M.

## Context

This work extends the projected shell model approach to study high-spin isomers and potential gateway states for triggered gamma-ray emission, following earlier experimental reports of triggering the 178Hf isomer with low-energy photons (Collins 1999, 2000, 2002) that were later refuted (Ahmad 2001, 2003). The calculations contrast with the case of the spin-9, 75 keV isomer in 180Ta where a threshold triggering energy of 1.01 MeV was determined (Belic 1999) and intermediate states identified (Walker 2001). The finding of gamma softness in 178Hf connects to discussions of critical point nuclei (Iachello 2000) and prior studies of gamma deformation for high-K bandheads in 178W and 182Os (Xu 1998) and hafnium isotopes (Xu 2000), but now suggests that explicit inclusion of the gamma degree of freedom in the PSM is needed to properly describe multi-quasiparticle states and guide experiments on triggered isomer decay.
