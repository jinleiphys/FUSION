# What does the rho-meson do? In-medium mass shift scenarios versus hadronic model calculations

> The NA60 experiment has studied low-mass muon pair production in In-In collisions at $158 {\rm AGeV}$ with unprecedented precision. With these results there is hope that the in-medium modifications of the vector meson spectral function can be constrained more thoroughly than before. We investigate in particular what can be learned about collisional broadening by a hot and dense medium and what constrains the experimental results put on in-medium mass shift scenarios. The data show a clear indication of considerable in-medium broadening effects but disfavor mass shift scenarios where the $ρ$-meson mass scales with the square root of the chiral condensate. Scaling scenarios which predict at finite density a dropping of the $ρ$-meson mass that is stronger than that of the quark condensate are clearly ruled out since they are also accompanied by a sharpening of the spectral function.

# What does the rho-meson do? In-medium mass shift scenarios versus hadronic model calculations

## Key claim

The NA60 dimuon data for 158 AGeV In-In collisions disfavor mass shift scenarios where the rho-meson mass scales with the square root of the chiral condensate (a=1/2), and clearly rule out scaling scenarios where the mass drop is stronger than that of the quark condensate (a >> 1), because such scenarios are accompanied by a sharpening rather than broadening of the spectral function. The data instead favor hadronic model calculations that predict substantial in-medium broadening with only small mass shifts.

## Method

The authors constructed a dynamical fireball evolution model for 158 AGeV In-In collisions by scaling a previously validated Pb-Pb evolution model using the ratio of charged particle rapidity densities (dNch/deta) between the two systems and nuclear overlap calculations. They then calculated dilepton spectra using different in-medium rho-meson spectral functions: three hadronic model calculations (a chiral model, a Walecka model, and a self-consistent pi-rho model using a Phi-functional approach) and a schematic mass scaling model based on Brown-Rho scaling with QCD sum rule constraints on the correlation between mass shift and width. The calculations were compared with NA60 data after acceptance folding, with the mass scaling models compared only to the low pT (pT < 0.5 GeV) data since they assume the rho-meson at rest relative to the thermal bath.

## Key numbers

- The fireball evolution model yields a peak temperature of about 250 MeV, a lifetime of approximately 7.5 fm/c, and a top transverse flow velocity of 0.5c at decoupling.
- The ratio of charged particle rapidity densities between semi-central In-In and 30% peripheral Pb-Au collisions is dNch^{In-In}/dNch^{Pb-Pb} = 0.68.
- The decoupling temperature for In-In collisions was tentatively chosen as T_F = 130 MeV.
- In the Pisarski linear sigma model calculation, the rho-meson and a1-meson masses become degenerate at T_c resulting in m_rho = m_a1 = 962 MeV under vector meson dominance, or m_rho = m_a1 = 629 MeV without vector meson dominance.
- The mass scaling model uses the relation m*_rho / m_rho = (langle q qbar rangle* / langle q qbar rangle)^a with a=1/2 as the primary case studied.

## Context

This work extends earlier dilepton studies by the CERES and HELIOS collaborations (Masera 1995, Agakishiev 1995, 1998, Lenkeit 1999) which observed enhanced low-mass dilepton production but lacked the mass resolution to distinguish between broadening and mass shift scenarios. The NA60 data now provide sufficient precision to test the Brown-Rho scaling conjecture (Brown 1991, 1995, 2001, 2002) against hadronic many-body calculations (Rapp 1999, Alam 1999, Gale 2003) that predict broadening without strong mass shifts. The results enable future work to focus on non-perturbative broadening mechanisms, as the self-consistent pi-rho model's success despite neglecting baryon effects suggests the generic shape and 3-momentum dependence of the spectral function may be more important than previously assumed.
