# Nonlinear Schrodinger Equation Solitons on Quantum Droplets

> Irrotational ow of a spherical thin liquid layer surrounding a rigid core is described using the defocusing nonlinear Schrodinger equation. Accordingly, azimuthal moving nonlinear waves are modeled by periodic dark solitons expressed by elliptic functions. In the quantum regime the algebraic Bethe ansatz is used in order to capture the energy levels of such motions, which we expect to be relevant for the dynamics of the nuclear clusters in deformed heavy nuclei surface modeled by quantum liquid drops. In order to validate the model we match our theoretical energy spectra with experimental results on energy, angular momentum and parity for alpha particle clustering nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that azimuthal nonlinear waves on a spherical thin liquid layer surrounding a rigid core can be modeled by periodic dark solitons of the defocusing nonlinear Schrodinger equation, and that the quantized energy levels from the algebraic Bethe ansatz for this system can be matched to experimental alpha-cluster spectra in nuclei, but only with the caveat that the fit requires three adjustable parameters (c, N, and an energy rescaling factor E0) and that the parameter c varies systematically with parity and angular momentum.

## Method

The authors start from the Euler and Laplace equations for irrotational flow of an incompressible fluid in a thin spherical shell of thickness h around a rigid core of radius R0-h, with h/R0 much less than 1. They perform an asymptotic expansion in the small thickness parameter and in a slow modulation parameter to derive a defocusing nonlinear Schrodinger equation for the azimuthal envelope of surface perturbations. They then quantize this classical field theory using the algebraic Bethe ansatz, obtaining Bethe equations whose solutions lambda_j yield energy eigenvalues E_N = sum lambda_j^2, and fit these theoretical spectra to experimental alpha-cluster resonance energies by adjusting the interaction parameter c, the rapidity number N, and an overall energy scale.

## Key numbers

* The dispersion relation for linear waves is omega^2 = (epsilon sigma)/(rho R0^3 sin^2 theta) (2k^2 - k^4/sin^2 theta), where epsilon = h/R0 is the small thickness parameter.
* The phase velocity is v = epsilon^{1/2}/(R0 sin theta) sqrt(2 sigma/(rho R0)).
* The measured moment of inertia for alpha + ^{28}Si elastic scattering is I = 3.3 +/- 0.2 hbar^2/MeV, compared to a rigid rotor value of approximately twice that.
* The measured moment of inertia for alpha + ^{36}Ar elastic scattering is I = 3.8 +/- 0.3 hbar^2/MeV, while an alpha particle orbiting a non-interacting ^{36}Ar core would give I = 9.3 hbar^2/MeV.
* The theoretical energy spectra are fitted to experimental data for nuclei with atomic masses ranging from 20 to 212.
* In the large-c limit, the Bethe equations reduce to exp(2 pi i lambda_j) = +/- 1, giving equidistant energy lines with lambda_j = 1/2.

## Context

This work extends the classical liquid drop model for nuclear surfaces, previously treated with KdV cnoidal waves by Carstea and Ludu (prior work cited as "prl" and "book"), into the quantum regime using the algebraic Bethe ansatz for the defocusing nonlinear Schrodinger equation. The model aims to explain alpha-clustering phenomena in nuclei ranging from light (^{12}C, ^{20}Ne) to heavy (^{212}Po) systems, addressing anomalies such as the moment of inertia discrepancy in alpha-daughter quasimolecular rotational bands where measured moments of inertia are roughly half the rigid rotor values. The approach connects to Bose-Einstein condensation models of alpha clusters (2018bec) and to earlier nonlinear collective models for cluster radioactivity (first, gherghescu), but claims the advantage of being already quantized without requiring shell corrections.
