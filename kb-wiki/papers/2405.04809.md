# Collective-subspace requantization for sub-barrier fusion reactions: Inertial functions for collective motions

> The adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method is used to determine the optimum reaction path and to calculate the potential and the inertial functions of the reaction model. The properties of the inertial functions are investigated with the ASCC method, in comparison with those of the cranking formulae. In addition, the properties of the pair rotation are investigated in the BCS pair model. The moments of inertia for rotation in both the real and the gauge spaces may decrease as the deformation develops.

## Key claim

The adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method provides inertial functions for sub-barrier fusion reactions that correctly reproduce asymptotic values (total mass, reduced mass, reduced moment of inertia), while the cranking formulae fail to reproduce these values because they neglect residual dynamical effects, particularly those restoring Galilean invariance.

## Method

The authors solve the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) equations, which consist of a moving-frame mean-field equation and a moving-frame random-phase approximation (MF-RPA) equation, to determine the collective subspace (reaction path) for sub-barrier fusion. The inertial functions for the collective Hamiltonian are calculated from the ASCC solution, and the moments of inertia for rotational modes are obtained from zero-frequency solutions of the MF-RPA using the strength function method. The ASCC results are compared with the non-perturbative and perturbative cranking formulae, and the properties of pair rotation are analyzed using a BCS pair model.

## Key numbers

- The ASCC inertial functions reproduce the total mass \(Am\) for translational motion, the reduced mass \(\mu_{\text{red}} = A_1 A_2 m/(A_1+A_2)\) for relative motion at large \(r\) (\(r \gg R_{\text{touch}}\)), and the reduced moment of inertia \(\mathscr{I}_{\text{red}} \equiv \mu_{\text{red}} r^2\) at large \(r\).
- The cranking formulae approximately produce the total effective mass \(A\langle m^* \rangle\) for translational motion, the reduced effective mass \(\mu^*_{\text{red}} = A_1 A_2 \langle m^* \rangle/(A_1+A_2)\) at large \(r\), and moments of inertia \(\mu^*_{\text{red}} r^2\) at large \(r\), indicating their failure.
- The moment of inertia \(\mathscr{I}(r)\) decreases as the relative distance \(r\) decreases (or as deformation \(\beta\) increases): \(d\mathscr{I}/dr < 0\) and \(d\mathscr{I}/d\beta < 0\).
- For pair rotation, the moment of inertia \(\mathscr{I}_{\text{pair}}\) decreases as the pairing gap \(\Delta\) increases: \(\partial\mathscr{I}_{\text{pair}}/\partial\Delta < 0\), with the analytical BCS result \(\partial\mathscr{I}_{\text{pair}}/\partial\Delta = -\rho(\lambda) I(\Lambda/\Delta)/\Delta\) where \(I(\eta) = \eta/(\eta^2+1)^{3/2} > 0\).

## Context

This work extends the ASCC method of Matsuo et al. (2000) and Hinohara et al. (2007, 2009) to sub-barrier fusion reactions, improving upon the five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian (5DCH) method and the cranking formula of Inglis (1954) and Kerman (1961), which fail to reproduce correct asymptotic inertial masses. The results enable microscopic calculation of fusion cross sections below the Coulomb barrier, and the finding that moments of inertia decrease with deformation (contrary to naive intuition) suggests a transition from rigid-body to irrotational-flow behavior. The pair-rotation analysis extends the work of Brink and Broglia (2005) on Galilean invariance breaking by the pair field.
