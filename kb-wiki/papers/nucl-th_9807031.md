# Critical analysis of quark-meson coupling models for nuclear matter and finite nuclei

> Three versions of the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model are applied to describe properties of nuclear matter and finite nuclei. The models differ in the treatment of the bag constant and in terms of nonlinear scalar self-interactions. As a consequence opposite predictions for the medium modifications of the internal nucleon structure arise. After calibrating the model parameters at equilibrium nuclear matter density, binding energies, charge radii, single-particle spectra and density distributions of spherical nuclei are analyzed and compared with QHD calculations. For the models which predict a decreasing size of the nucleon in the nuclear environment, unrealistic features of the nuclear shapes arise.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that once calibrated to reproduce the same equilibrium nuclear matter properties, the QMCII and MQMC models yield nearly identical results for nuclear matter and finite nuclei, despite making opposite predictions for the medium modification of the nucleon size; however, the gradient coupling inherent to the bag model leads to significant differences in nuclear surface properties, and the QMCI model fails to reproduce realistic nuclear phenomenology because it cannot be calibrated to produce sufficiently small effective nucleon masses.

## Method

The authors applied three versions of the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, QMCI (original, fixed bag constant), QMCII (fixed bag constant with cubic and quartic scalar self-interactions), and MQMC (density-dependent bag constant), to infinite nuclear matter and spherical finite nuclei using a relativistic mean-field framework. They calibrated the model parameters to reproduce the same empirical nuclear matter saturation properties (density, binding energy, symmetry energy, compression modulus, and effective mass) and then solved the coupled Dirac and meson field equations self-consistently for finite nuclei, comparing the resulting binding energies, charge radii, single-particle spectra, and density distributions with experimental data and with QHD calculations.

## Key numbers

- At equilibrium nuclear matter density, the QMCI model predicts an effective nucleon mass of M*/M = 0.80 and a compression modulus of K0 = 280 MeV.
- The MQMC model predicts the bag radius increases by roughly 25% of its free space value at equilibrium density.
- The maximal density for QMCI is approximately 4.92 times the saturation density; for QMCII it is approximately 1.23 times the saturation density; for MQMC it is approximately 3.38 times the saturation density.
- At nuclear matter equilibrium, the gradient coupling in the QMCII model is 6 to 7 times bigger than in the MQMC model.
- To reproduce the experimental charge radius of 40Ca, the scalar meson mass had to be increased by 10% for MQMC and by 30% for QMCII.
- The QMCII model overestimates binding energies of light nuclei by up to approximately 6% for 16O; the QMCI model underestimates binding energies by 6% to 9%.

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling approach of Guichon (1988) and the modified QMC model of Jin and Jennings (1996a, 1996b) by systematically comparing three versions against each other and against the well-established quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) framework of Serot and Walecka (1997). The analysis shows that the QMCII and MQMC models, when properly calibrated, reproduce the nonlinear scalar self-interactions essential for successful hadronic phenomenology as established by Reinhard (1986), Furnstahl (1989, 1993), and others. The key finding, that the gradient coupling predicted by the bag model produces unrealistic nuclear shapes for models where the nucleon shrinks in the medium, enables future work to discriminate between competing predictions for medium modifications of nucleon structure by comparing against precise nuclear density measurements.
