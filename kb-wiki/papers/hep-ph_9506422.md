# Magnetic Moments of the SU(3) Octet Baryons in the semibosonized SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio Model

> We investigate the magnetic moments of the SU(3) octet baryons in the framework of the $SU(3)$ semibosonized Nambu--Jona--Lasinio model. The rotational $1/N_c$ corrections and strange quark mass in linear order are taken into account. We derive general relations between magnetic moments of the SU(3) octet baryons, based on the symmetry of our model. These relations indicate that higher order corrections such as $O(m_s/N_c)$ and $O(m^{2}_{s})$ are relatively small. The magnetic moments of the octet baryons predicted by our model are quantitatively in a good agreement with experimental results within about 15$\%$.

# Magnetic Moments of the SU(3) Octet Baryons in the semibosonized SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio Model

## Key claim
The semibosonized SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model reproduces the magnetic moments of the SU(3) octet baryons within about 15% of experimental values, but this accuracy represents the upper limit achievable by any model with "hedgehog symmetry" due to unavoidable systematic errors from neglected O(m_s/N_c) and O(m_s^2) corrections.

## Method
The authors compute magnetic moments of the SU(3) octet baryons using the semibosonized SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, treating baryons as N_c valence quarks coupled to a polarized Dirac sea bound by a chiral background field in the Hartree approximation. They include rotational 1/N_c corrections and strange quark mass corrections to linear order, using the Kahana-Ripka method to evaluate the double sums over eigenstates of the quark Hamiltonian in the soliton field. The only free parameter is the constituent quark mass M, fixed to 420 MeV by the mass splitting of the SU(3) baryons.

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark mass M = 420 MeV, fixed by SU(3) baryon mass splitting
- Rotational 1/N_c corrections contribute approximately 50% to the magnetic moments of all octet members
- The relation μ_Σ^0 = 1/2 (μ_Σ^+ + μ_Σ^-) is trivially fulfilled with no corrections in 1/N_c or m_s
- The relation for μ_Λ gives: empirical value -(0.613 ± 0.004) versus predicted -(0.402 ± 0.10) (in nuclear magnetons)
- The relation for μ_Σ^0Λ gives: empirical value -(1.61 ± 0.08) versus predicted -(1.48 ± 0.03) (in nuclear magnetons)
- The relation neglecting 1/N_c corrections (μ_Ξ^0 relation) gives: empirical value -(1.250 ± 0.015) versus predicted -(4.8 ± 0.2) (in nuclear magnetons)
- Overall agreement with experimental data within about 15%

## Context
This work extends the SU(3) version of the semibosonized NJL model previously applied to baryon mass splittings by Blotz et al. (1995) and Weigel et al. (1995), and to axial constants. The authors derive general relations between hyperon magnetic moments based on hedgehog symmetry, showing that relations which cancel 1/N_c corrections (first three relations) are satisfied to O(m_s/N_c) accuracy, while the relation neglecting 1/N_c corrections fails badly. This demonstrates that 1/N_c corrections are numerically large while O(m_s/N_c) corrections are relatively small. The model is compared to the Skyrme model, noting that the NJL model achieves the same algebraic structure as the Skyrme model with vector mesons but without introducing additional parameters. The results enable a systematic understanding of the accuracy limits of hedgehog-type models for baryon magnetic moments.
