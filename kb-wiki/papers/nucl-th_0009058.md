# Effective Field Theory with Two and Three Nucleons

> Progress in the Effective Field Theory of two and three nucleon systems is sketched, concentrating on the low energy version in which pions are integrated out as explicit degrees of freedom. Examples given are calculations of deuteron Compton scattering, three body forces and the triton, and nd partial waves.

## Key claim

The paper argues that Effective Field Theory (EFT) provides a systematic, model-independent framework for two- and three-nucleon systems, but the power counting in the three-body sector requires an unusual renormalization that promotes the three-body force to leading order in the triton channel, contrary to naive dimensional analysis which would place it at higher order.

## Method

The author reviews the construction of an EFT for nucleons where pions are either integrated out (ENT with no pions) or treated perturbatively (KSW counting), and compares this to the Weinberg approach where the potential is power-counted and then iterated via a Schrodinger equation. For the three-body system, the leading-order Faddeev equation is solved numerically, and the ultraviolet behavior of the half-off-shell amplitude is analyzed via Mellin transformation to determine the scaling of three-body forces.

## Key numbers

- The scattering lengths in the S-wave channels are: 1/a(^1S0) = -8.3 MeV, 1/a(^3S1) = 36 MeV
- The deuteron binding energy is B = 2.225 MeV, giving a typical binding momentum gamma = sqrt(M B) approximately 46 MeV
- The expansion parameter in most applications is approximately 1/3, so NLO calculations are expected to be accurate to about 10% and N2LO to about 4%
- In the doublet S-wave (triton) channel, the Mellin transform yields s0 = +/- 1.0062 i, indicating two linearly independent solutions
- The triton binding energy B3 and three-body scattering length a3^(1/2) lie on a curve known as the Phillips line

## Context

This work extends the EFT approach of Weinberg (1990) and the KSW counting scheme of Kaplan, Savage and Wise (1998) to three-nucleon systems. The surprising result that the three-body force must be promoted to leading order in the triton channel was discovered by Bedaque, Hammer and van Kolck (1999). This explains why potential models that accurately describe NN scattering can vary significantly in their predictions of the triton binding energy, while all lying on the Phillips line. The approach enables systematic calculations of three-nucleon observables with controlled theoretical uncertainties, as demonstrated in deuteron Compton scattering calculations by Chen, Springer, Savage and Griesshammer (2000).
