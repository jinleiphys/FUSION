# In-Medium Charmonium Production in Proton-Nucleus Collisions

> We study charmonium production in proton-nucleus ($p$-A) collisions focusing on final-state effects caused by the formation of an expanding medium. Toward this end, we utilize a rate equation approach within a fireball model as previously employed for a wide range of heavy-ion collisions, adapted to the small systems in $p$-A collisions. The initial geometry of the fireball is taken from a Monte-Carlo event generator where initial anisotropies are caused by fluctuations. We calculate the centrality and transverse-momentum dependent nuclear modification factor ($R_{p{\rm A}}$) as well as elliptic flow ($v_2$) for both $J/ψ$ and $ψ(2S)$ and compare them to experimental data from RHIC and the LHC. While the $R_{p{\rm A}}$s show an overall fair agreement with most of the data, the large $v_2$ values observed in $p$-Pb collisions at the LHC cannot be accounted for in our approach. While the former finding generally supports the formation of a near thermalized QCD medium in small systems, the discrepancy in the $v_2$ suggests that its large observed values are unlikely to be due to the final-state collectivity of the fireball alone.

## Key claim

The authors find that while their fireball rate-equation approach can reproduce the nuclear modification factor ($R_{pA}$) for charmonia in proton-nucleus collisions, supporting the formation of a near-thermalized QCD medium in small systems, the model cannot account for the large elliptic flow ($v_2$) values observed in $p$-Pb collisions at the LHC, suggesting that the observed $v_2$ is unlikely to originate from final-state collectivity of the fireball alone.

## Method

The authors employ a kinetic rate-equation approach within an anisotropically expanding fireball model, adapted from heavy-ion collisions to small $p$-A systems, to compute charmonium production including both suppression and regeneration contributions. The initial fireball geometry is taken from a Monte-Carlo Glauber event generator with fluctuations, and the time evolution is constructed through an elliptic expansion with conserved total entropy matched to measured charged-hadron multiplicities. The transverse-momentum dependent nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow are calculated by solving a Boltzmann equation for the primordial component and using an anisotropic blastwave description for the regeneration component, with cold-nuclear-matter effects implemented via EPS09 parton shadowing and Gaussian $p_T$ broadening.

## Key numbers

- Initial transverse area for $p$-Pb collisions: $A_\perp^{p{\rm Pb}} = 7.8$ fm$^2$
- Initial eccentricity from Monte-Carlo Glauber: $e = 0.2$
- Fireball transverse accelerations: $a_x = 0.34$/fm and $a_y = 0.13$/fm, giving average $a_T \sim 0.24$/fm
- Charm-quark thermal relaxation time increased from 4 to 15 fm/$c$ for $p$-Pb collisions
- Charmonium formation times assumed in range 1-2 fm
- $J/\psi$ dissociation temperatures: $T_c$ for $\psi(2S)$, 1.3$T_c$ for $\chi_c$, and 2$T_c$ for $J/\psi$
- $J/\psi$ over $c\bar{c}$ ratio fixed at 0.58%
- $J/\psi$ $v_2$ predicted up to $\sim$2% in high-multiplicity $p$-Pb collisions at 8.16 TeV
- $\psi(2S)$ $v_2$ predicted up to $\sim$5% in high-multiplicity $p$-Pb collisions at 8.16 TeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous rate-equation approach for $d$-Au collisions at RHIC (Du 2015) to $p$-Pb collisions at the LHC, including calculations of $p_T$ spectra, rapidity dependencies, and elliptic flow. The approach builds on earlier transport models developed for heavy-ion collisions (Grandchamp 2003, Zhao 2010) and competes with comover-based explanations for $\psi(2S)$ suppression (Ferreiro 2014). The discrepancy between the model's small predicted $J/\psi$ $v_2$ and the larger experimental values from ALICE and CMS indicates that final-state interactions in the fireball cannot explain the observed elliptic flow, pointing toward initial-state effects or other mechanisms as the dominant source.
