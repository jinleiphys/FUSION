# Improving the accuracy of neutrino energy reconstruction in charged-current quasielastic scattering off nuclear targets

> We report the results of a theoretical study of quasielastic electron and neutrino interactions with carbon. Our approach takes into account the effects of final-state interactions between the struck nucleon and the residual nucleus, neglected in the impulse approximation, through a generalization of the spectral function formalism. The calculated electron-scattering cross sections turn out to be in very good agreement with the available data over a broad kinematical region. The impact of nuclear effects on the reconstruction of neutrino energy in charged-current quasielastic processes is also studied, and the results of our approach are compared to the predictions of the relativistic Fermi gas model, routinely employed in most Monte Carlo simulations. Finally, we discuss the limitations of the existing procedure for energy reconstruction and propose a new, improved, one. At energy ~600 MeV, we observe a sizable difference between neutrino and antineutrino scattering, important for the measurements of charge-parity symmetry violation. Our analysis suggests that a reliable determination of neutrino energy can only be obtained from models validated by a systematic comparison to the available electron-scattering data.

# Improving the accuracy of neutrino energy reconstruction in charged-current quasielastic scattering off nuclear targets

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a spectral function approach incorporating final-state interactions through a generalized formalism and the real part of the optical potential can accurately reproduce electron-carbon scattering data across a broad kinematic range, and that this validated model reveals significant limitations in the standard relativistic Fermi gas model used for neutrino energy reconstruction. The claim is conditional: reliable neutrino energy determination requires models validated by systematic comparison to electron-scattering data, and the proposed improvement depends on using separation energies of 19 MeV for neutrinos and 6 MeV for antineutrinos at 600 MeV true energy.

## Method

The authors employ a convolution scheme that generalizes the impulse approximation by folding the cross section with a function describing final-state interactions between the struck nucleon and the residual nucleus, using the correlated Glauber approximation. The hole spectral function of carbon is obtained from the local-density approximation combining experimental shell-model information with many-body calculations of nuclear matter, while the particle spectral function accounts for Pauli blocking in either a step-function or local-density approximation. The real part of the Dirac phenomenological optical potential from Cooper et al. is included to modify the struck nucleon's energy spectrum, and the effective momentum approximation accounts for Coulomb distortion of charged leptons.

## Key numbers

- The real part of the proton optical potential for carbon is -33.3, -22.0, and -11.8 MeV in the kinematic setups of Figs. 2a, 2e, and 2i respectively
- The RFG model uses Fermi momentum 221 MeV and separation energy 25 MeV, determined from a fit to data at one kinematic point
- At neutrino energy ~600 MeV, the most probable antineutrino energy is lower by 14 MeV at cosθ=0.97 and 17 MeV at cosθ=0.87 compared to neutrino energy for the same muon kinematics
- The separation energy required to peak the reconstructed energy distribution at true energy 600 MeV is 19 MeV for νμ and 6 MeV for ν̄μ
- For the RFG model with separation energy 34 MeV instead of 25 MeV, the maxima of the probability distribution shift by ~9 MeV
- The Coulomb correction for carbon is estimated to be 3.5 MeV
- The giant dipole resonance in carbon has width Γc.m. = 3.2 MeV at excitation energy Ex = 22.6 MeV
- The difference between separation energies for neutrinos and antineutrinos is 13 MeV, compared to 4 MeV assumed in MiniBooNE and MINERνA analyses
- At true energies between 400 and 1000 MeV, the reconstructed energy maxima differ from true values by no more than 2 MeV when using the optimized separation energies

## Context

This work extends the spectral function approach developed by Benhar and collaborators (Benhar, 2005; Benhar and Meloni, 2009) for neutrino-oxygen interactions to carbon targets, and directly addresses the limitations of the relativistic Fermi gas model routinely employed in Monte Carlo simulations by experiments such as T2K and NOνA. The authors compare their results to prior work by Leitner and Mosel (2010) using the GiBUU transport model, Martini et al. (2012) on multinucleon effects, Nieves et al. (2012) on unfolding distortions, and Lalakulich et al. (2012) on two-nucleon knockout contributions, as well as Coloma and Huber (2013) on biases in oscillation parameter determination. The validated approach enables more accurate neutrino energy reconstruction for oscillation studies, though the authors note that two-body current contributions and meson-exchange currents, which produce the high-energy tail of the quasielastic peak, are not fully included and require further development.
