# Emergence of High-Order Deformation in Rotating Transfermium Nuclei: A Microscopic Understanding

> The rotational properties of the transfermium nuclei are investigated in the full deformation space by implementing a shell-model-like approach in the cranking covariant density functional theory on a three-dimensional lattice, where the pairing correlations, deformations, and moments of inertia are treated in a microscopic and self-consistent way. The kinematic and dynamic moments of inertia of the rotational bands observed in the transfermium nuclei $^{252}$No, $^{254}$No, $^{254}$Rf, and $^{256}$Rf are well reproduced without any adjustable parameters using a well-determined universal density functional. It is found for the first time that the emergence of the octupole deformation should be responsible for the significantly different rotational behavior observed in $^{252}$No and $^{254}$No. The present results provide a microscopic solution to the long-standing puzzle on the rotational behavior in No isotopes, and highlight the risk of investigating only the hexacontetrapole ($β_{60}$) deformation effects in rotating transfermium nuclei without considering the octupole deformation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the significantly different rotational behavior observed in the two neighboring isotopes $^{252}$No and $^{254}$No at high angular momenta originates from the emergence of octupole deformation in $^{252}$No, not from hexacontetrapole ($\beta_{60}$) deformation effects as previously speculated. This conclusion is conditional on the use of a fully microscopic, parameter-free approach that treats pairing correlations, deformations, and moments of inertia self-consistently in the full deformation space.

## Method

The authors implemented a shell-model-like approach (SLAP) within cranking covariant density functional theory (CDFT) on a three-dimensional lattice, using the point-coupling density functional PC-PK1 without any adjustable parameters. The Dirac equation for nucleons was solved in a 3D lattice space with mesh sizes of 1 fm and 26 grid points along each axis, and the cranking many-body Hamiltonian with pairing correlations was diagonalized in a many-particle configuration space of 1000 dimensions for both neutrons and protons, with pairing strengths of 0.25 MeV (neutrons) and 0.30 MeV (protons).

## Key numbers

- Ground-state binding energies for $^{252}$No, $^{254}$No, $^{254}$Rf, and $^{256}$Rf were reproduced within 1.30 MeV of experimental data (Wang 2021); calculated values: 1871.3 MeV, 1885.6 MeV, 1876.7 MeV, and 1892.0 MeV respectively.
- Quadrupole deformations $\beta_{20}$ for all four nuclei are close to 0.30 (0.296 for $^{252}$No, 0.297 for $^{254}$No, 0.300 for $^{254}$Rf, 0.302 for $^{256}$Rf).
- Octupole deformation $\beta_{30}$ for $^{252}$No ground state is 0.0042, while for $^{254}$No it is 0.0004; $\beta_{32}$ is 0.0128 for $^{252}$No and 0.0000 for $^{254}$No.
- The experimental dynamic moments of inertia of $^{252}$No exhibit an abrupt upbending at rotational frequency $\hbar\omega \approx 0.15$ MeV, while those of $^{254}$No are much smoother.
- The octupole deformations $\beta_{30}$ and $\beta_{32}$ for $^{252}$No sharply increase at $\hbar\omega \approx 0.15$ MeV, corresponding to the upbending point.

## Context

This work provides a microscopic solution to a long-standing puzzle regarding the different rotational behavior of $^{252}$No and $^{254}$No, which previous studies using the total Routhian surface method (Liu 2012) and the particle-number-conserving cranked shell model (Zhang 2013) had attributed to hexacontetrapole ($\beta_{60}$) deformation effects, but those methods neglected reflection-asymmetric deformations and required parameter fitting. The present results extend cranking CDFT calculations (Ren 2019, Zhang 2022) by incorporating exact particle-number-conserving pairing via SLAP, and they enable future investigations of rotating transfermium nuclei that must include octupole deformation degrees of freedom rather than focusing solely on $\beta_{60}$ deformation.
