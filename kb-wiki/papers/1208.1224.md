# Toward parton equilibration with improved parton interaction matrix elements

> The Quark-Gluon Plasma can be produced in high energy heavy ion collisions and how it equilibrates is important for the extraction of the properties of strongly interacting matter. A radiative transport model can be used to reveal interesting characteristics of Quark-Gluon Plasma thermalization. For example, screened parton interactions always lead to partial pressure isotropization. Systems with different initial pressure anisotropies evolve toward the same asymptotic evolution. In particular, radiative processes are crucial for the chemical equilibration of the system. Matrix elements under the soft and collinear approximation for these processes, as first derived by Gunion and Bertsch, are widely used. A different approach is to start with the exact matrix elements for the two to three and its inverse processes. General features of this approach will be reviewed and the results will be compared with the Gunion-Bertsch results. We will comment on the possible implications of the exact matrix element approach on Quark-Gluon Plasma thermalization.

# Toward parton equilibration with improved parton interaction matrix elements

## Key claim
Radiative transport with exact matrix elements for 2→3 and 3→2 gluonic processes yields cross sections and reaction integrals that differ substantially from the widely used Gunion-Bertsch approximation, implying that elastic collisions may be more important for QGP thermalization than previously thought and that the specific shear viscosity may be much larger than the conjectured quantum limit.

## Method
The author simulates a gluon system in a box using perturbative QCD cross sections regulated by a Debye screening mass, with 2→2 cross sections proportional to α_s² and 2→3 cross sections initially taken as 50% of the 2→2 cross section for exploratory studies. Exact matrix elements for gg→ggg and its inverse are then computed using a symmetric form of the squared matrix element with Debye screening mass regularization, and the resulting cross sections, Dalitz plots, and reaction integrals are compared to those from the Gunion-Bertsch formula.

## Key numbers
- For α_s=0.47, μ²=10 fm⁻², and s=4 GeV²: σ₂₂=0.312 fm², σ₂₃=0.0523 fm², giving σ₂₃/σ₂₂=0.168 (much smaller than 50%)
- For α_s=0.3, μ²=6.38 fm⁻², and s=4 GeV²: σ₂₂=0.199 fm², σ₂₃=0.0504 fm², giving σ₂₃/σ₂₂≈0.253
- For the 3→2 process with α_s=0.47 and μ²=10 fm⁻²: reaction integral I₃₂=6.84 fm² when one incoming particle is soft, and I₃₂=4.85 fm² when all three incoming particles have about the same energy
- The Gunion-Bertsch result can exceed the exact matrix element by up to 50% in certain kinematic regions when regulated with screening mass

## Context
This work extends the radiative transport model introduced by Xu and Greiner (2004), who first applied stochastic methods to relativistic transport with particle number changing processes and used the Gunion-Bertsch formula for 2→3 matrix elements. The exact matrix element approach follows earlier work by Gottschalk (1979) and Berends (1981) on the full 2→3 gluonic amplitude. The finding that exact cross sections are much smaller than Gunion-Bertsch estimates suggests that specific shear viscosity calculations by Xu and Greiner (2007) may need revision, and would be in qualitative agreement with calculations by Chen et al. (2009, 2010) based on exact matrix elements. The results enable future explicit calculations of transport coefficients and thermalization dynamics using improved inelastic matrix elements.
