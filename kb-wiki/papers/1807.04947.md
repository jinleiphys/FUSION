# Role of breakup processes in deuteron-induced spallation reactions at 100-200 MeV/nucleon

> Use of deuteron-induced spallation reactions at intermediate energies has recently been proposed for transmutation of several long-lived fission products (LLFPs). In the design study of a transmutation system using a deuteron primary beam, accurate cross section data of deuteron-induced reactions on the LLFPs are indispensable. Reliable model predictions play an important role in completing the necessary cross section data since currently available experimental data are very limited. Under the circumstances, we have been developing a code system dedicated for deuteron-induced reactions, which is called DEURACS. Aiming to predict the production cross sections of residual nuclei, the purpose of the present work is to clarify a role of deuteron breakup processes in deuteron-induced spallation reactions at intermediate energies. Isotopic production cross sections of residual nuclei in the deuteron-induced reactions on 93Zr and 107Pd at 100-200 MeV/nucleon are analyzed using DEURACS, in which the breakup processes are explicitly taken into account. The calculated cross sections are decomposed into individual components corresponding to the absorption of either neutron or proton in the incident deuteron, or the deuteron itself. The calculated cross sections reproduced the experimental data well over a wide mass number range of residual nuclei. From a component-by-component analysis, it was found that the components of nucleon absorption have the significant contributions to the production of residual nuclei. Consideration of the breakup processes is essential to predict the production cross sections of residual nuclei in deuteron-induced reactions. The framework of DEURACS is applicable to deuteron-induced spallation reactions at intermediate energies.

## Key claim

The deuteron breakup processes are essential for accurately predicting production cross sections of residual nuclei in deuteron-induced spallation reactions at 100-200 MeV/nucleon, with the nucleon absorption components after non-elastic breakup making significant contributions that are comparable to or exceed those from direct deuteron absorption.

## Method

The authors used the DEURACS code system, which combines the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) theory and the Glauber model for breakup processes with the CCONE code for pre-equilibrium and compound nucleus processes. The calculated cross sections were decomposed into three components corresponding to absorption of the deuteron itself, absorption of the proton after non-elastic breakup, and absorption of the neutron after non-elastic breakup. The calculations were performed for deuteron-induced reactions on 93Zr and 107Pd targets at incident energies of 100-200 MeV/nucleon and compared with experimental data from inverse kinematics measurements.

## Key numbers

- For the 93Zr+d reaction at 105 MeV/nucleon, DEURACS reproduced experimental isotopic production cross sections over a wide mass number range, while CCONE (without breakup) underestimated production of isotopes with A>90 and overestimated those with A<90.
- For the 107Pd+d reaction at 118 MeV/nucleon and 196 MeV/nucleon, DEURACS calculations showed overall good agreement with experimental data from Wang et al.
- The elastic breakup cross section on 93Zr at 56 MeV total kinetic energy was calculated as 181.14 mb, comparable to 172.69 mb calculated by Neoh et al. for 90Zr.
- The neutron removal cross section on 93Zr at 56 MeV was calculated as 610 mb, compared to 590 mb from Neoh et al. for 90Zr.
- The deuteron total reaction cross section on 107Pd from the MWO formula and the AC optical potential calculation agreed up to 100 MeV/nucleon but differed by about a factor of 1.4 at 200 MeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the DEURACS code system, previously validated for double-differential cross sections of (d,xn) and (d,xp) reactions up to 50 MeV/nucleon, to spallation reactions at higher energies (100-200 MeV/nucleon). It builds on experimental studies by Kawase et al. and Wang et al. who measured proton- and deuteron-induced spallation on 93Zr and 107Pd in inverse kinematics. The approach differs from that of Avrigeanu et al., who used an empirical formula for breakup processes applicable only below 80 MeV. The findings enable more reliable predictions of cross section data for transmutation system design using deuteron primary beams, and provide the first validation of the Minomo-Washiyama-Ogata formula for deuteron total reaction cross sections above 100 MeV/nucleon.
