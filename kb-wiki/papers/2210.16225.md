# Is the "RG-invariant EFT'' for few-nucleon systems cutoff independent?

> We consider nucleon-nucleon scattering using the formulation of chiral effective field theory which is claimed to be renormalization group invariant. The cornerstone of this framework is the existence of a well-defined infinite-cutoff limit for the scattering amplitude at each order of the expansion, which should not depend on a particular regulator form. Focusing on the $^3P_0$ partial wave as a representative example, we show that this requirement can in general not be fulfilled beyond the leading order, in spite of the perturbative treatment of subleading contributions to the amplitude. Several previous studies along these lines, including the next-to-leading order calculation by Long and Yang [Phys. Rev. C84, 057001 (2011)] and a toy model example with singular long-range potentials by Long and van Kolck [Annals Phys. 323, 1304-1323 (2008)], are critically reviewed and scrutinized in detail.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the "RG-invariant" chiral EFT framework for nucleon-nucleon scattering cannot generally achieve a cutoff-independent NLO amplitude in the infinite-cutoff limit, despite perturbative treatment of subleading contributions, because the oscillatory nature of the LO wave function near the origin (caused by singular attractive potentials) prevents consistent renormalization beyond leading order.

## Method

The authors analyze NN scattering in the \(^3P_0\) partial wave using the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the LO amplitude and distorted-wave Born approximation for the NLO amplitude, employing both non-local and local regulators (smooth power-like and sharp cutoff forms). They examine the cutoff dependence of the NLO amplitude by varying the cutoff parameters \(\Lambda_0\) (for LO) and \(\Lambda_2\) (for NLO) independently, using an \(N/D\) dispersive representation to identify "exceptional" cutoff values where the determinant of the contact-term amplitude matrix vanishes, and support this with numerical calculations using a modified two-pion-exchange potential.

## Key numbers

- The LO amplitude is renormalized at a laboratory energy of \(T_\text{lab} = 50\) MeV, corresponding to on-shell momentum \(p_0\).
- The pion mass is set to \(M_\pi = 138.04\) MeV, the nucleon mass to \(m_N = 938.9\) MeV, the pion decay constant to \(F_\pi = 92.1\) MeV, and the axial coupling to \(g_A = 1.29\).
- The cutoff values are varied up to \(\Lambda \simeq 5\) GeV in the context of prior work by Long and Yang (2011).
- The width of "exceptional" cutoff regions scales as \(\delta \Lambda \sim \bar C_0^{1/\bar\alpha} / \bar\Lambda^{\beta/\bar\alpha}\), with \(\bar\alpha = 1\) or \(2\) typically.

## Context

This work critically reexamines the infinite-cutoff (RG-invariant) scheme for chiral EFT, extending earlier critiques by Epelbaum et al. (2009, 2018, 2020, 2021) regarding issues like spurious bound states and repulsive interactions. It specifically scrutinizes the NLO calculations by Long and Yang (2011) and the toy model with singular long-range potentials by Long and van Kolck (2008), demonstrating that the claimed cutoff independence fails due to unavoidable "exceptional" cutoff values where the contact-term amplitude vanishes at the renormalization point but not elsewhere. This challenges the feasibility of the RG-invariant approach for few-nucleon systems beyond LO and supports the finite-cutoff scheme as a more practical alternative.
