# Color Transparency

> Color transparency is the vanishing of nuclear initial or final state interactions involving specific reactions. The reasons for believing that color transparency might be a natural consequence of QCD are reviewed. The main impetus for this talk is recent experimental progress, and this is reviewed briefly.

## Key claim

Color transparency is the vanishing of nuclear initial or final state interactions in specific high-momentum-transfer exclusive reactions, but it is an expected rather than certain consequence of QCD; the observation requires both a point-like configuration (PLC) formed in a coherent process and sufficiently high energy to avoid PLC expansion before escaping the nucleus.

## Method

The paper reviews the three theoretical elements required for color transparency: PLC formation in high-momentum-transfer exclusive reactions, cancellation of gluon emission amplitudes for color-neutral small-size objects (with interaction cross section proportional to the square of the transverse separation), and PLC expansion dynamics governed by time-dilated hadronic evolution. Experimental results from (p,pp) reactions at BNL, (e,e'p) at SLAC, coherent di-jet production from pions at Fermilab, and (e,e',π⁺) and (e,e',ρ⁰) at Jefferson Lab are compared against predictions of transparency ratios.

## Key numbers

- The PLC expansion time for a protonic PLC of momentum P is t ≈ 2P/ΔM², where ΔM² = 0.7 GeV² is a reasonable numerical value.
- The effective cross section as a function of distance z traversed uses a coherence length l_c ≡ 2P/ΔM².
- Theoretical analyses suggest small configurations can be produced for momentum transfers as low as about 1 or 2 (GeV/c)².
- The Fermilab di-jet experiment showed a Pt to C target ratio seven times larger than predicted from Glauber theory.
- The JLab (e,e',π⁺) experiment detected a significant rise of T(A) as Q² ranges between 1 and 5 (GeV/c)².
- The JLab (e,e',ρ⁰) experiment detected a significant rise of T(A) as Q² ranges between 1 and 2.5 (GeV/c)².

## Context

This talk reviews the color transparency phenomenon following earlier reviews by Frankfurt (1992), Frankfurt (1994), Jain (1995), and Miller (2007), with the main impetus being recent experimental progress at Jefferson Lab. The work extends theoretical understanding of PLC formation and expansion, with the Fermilab di-jet experiment (Frankfurt 1993) providing the clearest observation at high energy, while the JLab results for pion and rho meson electroproduction show encouraging signs at medium energies. The existence of PLCs has implications for the EMC effect and QCD factorization proofs (Collins 1996).
