# Up- and down-quark contributions to the nucleon electromagnetic form factors at low $Q^2$

> The spatial distribution of charge and magnetization within the nucleon (proton and neutron) is encoded in the elastic electromagnetic form factors $G_E^{(p,n)}$ and $G_M^{(p,n)}$. These form factors have been precisely measured utilizing elastic electron scattering, and the combination of proton and neutron form factors allows for the separation of the up- and down-quark contributions to the nucleon form factors. We expand on our original analyses and extract the up- and down-quark contributions to the nucleon electromagnetic form factors from worldwide data with an emphasis on precise new data covering the low-momentum region, which is sensitive to the large-scale structure of the nucleon. From these, we construct the flavor-separated Dirac and Pauli form factors and their ratios, and compare the results to recent extractions and theoretical calculations and models.

# Up- and down-quark contributions to the nucleon electromagnetic form factors at low $Q^2$

## Key claim
The up- and down-quark contributions to the nucleon electromagnetic form factors are extracted from worldwide data with an emphasis on precise new data covering the low-momentum region, and the results show that the down-quark contributions fall more rapidly than the up-quark contributions at high $Q^2$ with respect to the previously reported $1/Q^4$ scaling behavior, though this behavior is sensitive to the parametrization of $G_E^n$ at $Q^2 > 2$ (GeV/c)$^2$.

## Method
The authors performed a flavor separation of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors using combined cross section and polarization measurements of elastic electron-proton scattering, incorporating two-photon exchange (TPE) corrections based on a parametrization linear in $\varepsilon$ that vanishes in the limit $\varepsilon \rightarrow 1$. They included new $G_M^n$ data from CLAS, performed a new global fit to $G_M^n$, and used a parametrization of $R_n$ to construct $G_E^n$, accounting for uncertainties from all form factor measurements. The flavor-separated Dirac ($F_1^{(u,d)}$) and Pauli ($F_2^{(u,d)}$) form factors and their ratios were then constructed and compared to previous extractions (CJRW, VAMZ, AMT) and theoretical models (DSE, PC-RCQM, GBE-RCQM, GPD).

## Key numbers
- $\kappa_u = \mu_u - 2 = 1.67$ and $\kappa_d = \mu_d - 1 = -2.03$: the $Q^2 = 0$ limits of $F_2^{u,d}$, used to scale the ratios $\kappa_u^{-1} F_2^u /F_1^u$ and $\kappa_d^{-1} F_2^d /F_1^d$.
- The down-quark contributions $F_1^d$ and $F_2^d$ exhibited $1/Q^4$ scaling behavior above $Q^2 = 1.0$ (GeV/c)$^2$ in the CJRW analysis, but the present results suggest a faster falloff than this scaling.
- The ratio $F_2^u /F_1^u$ falls rapidly up to $Q^2 \simeq 1$ (GeV/c)$^2$ where it starts to decrease slowly.
- The ratio $\kappa_d^{-1} F_2^d /F_1^d$ increases slowly at low $Q^2$ and becomes almost constant for $Q^2 > 1$ (GeV/c)$^2$.
- Neutron form factor ratio $R_n = \mu_n G_E^n/G_M^n$ measurements extend up to 3.4 GeV$^2$ (Riordan et al., 2010).

## Context
This work extends the original CJRW analysis (CJRW, 2011) by incorporating TPE corrections, additional uncertainties, and updated form factor data including precise low-momentum data from Bernauer et al. (2014). The results are compared to the VAMZ parametrization (Venkat et al., 2011), the AMT extraction (Arrington et al., 2007), and theoretical calculations including a Dyson-Schwinger equation calculation (Cloet et al., 2009), a pion-cloud relativistic constituent quark model (Cloet et al., 2012), a Goldstone-boson exchange relativistic constituent quark model (Rohrmoser et al., 2011), and generalized parton distribution calculations (Gonzalez et al., 2013). The high-$Q^2$ measurements of $G_E^n$ planned at Jefferson Lab after the 12 GeV upgrade are identified as critical to pin down the behavior of the flavor-separated ratios and to examine theoretical models.
