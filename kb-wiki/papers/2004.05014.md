# Emergence of a pseudogap in the BCS-BEC crossover

> Strongly correlated Fermi systems with pairing interactions become superfluid below a critical temperature $T_c$. The extent to which such pairing correlations alter the behavior of the liquid at temperatures $T > T_c$ is a subtle issue that remains an area of debate, in particular regarding the appearance of the so-called pseudogap in the BCS-BEC crossover of unpolarized spin-$1/2$ nonrelativistic matter. To shed light on this, we extract several quantities of crucial importance at and around the unitary limit, namely: the odd-even staggering of the total energy, the spin susceptibility, the pairing correlation function, the condensate fraction, and the critical temperature $T_c$, using a non-perturbative, constrained-ensemble quantum Monte Carlo algorithm.

## Key claim

The authors find that pseudogap signatures (suppressed spin susceptibility and finite even-odd pairing gap above the critical temperature) diminish smoothly as the system approaches unitarity from the BEC side, but they conclude it is premature to exclude unitarity from the pseudogap regime because all characteristic temperatures \(T^*\) exceed the critical temperature \(T_c\) at all scattering lengths studied (\(0.0 \le 1/(k_F a) \le 0.3\)).

## Method

The authors performed auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFQMC) lattice simulations with constrained ensembles using particle-projection methods, employing a cubic momentum cutoff rather than a spherical cutoff to avoid breaking Galilean invariance at lower order. They simulated unpolarized spin-\(1/2\) nonrelativistic fermions on a cubic lattice of size \(L = N_x \ell\) with lattice spacing \(\ell = 1\) in lattice units, extracting the condensate fraction, spin susceptibility, even-odd pairing gap, energy equation of state, and critical temperature at and around the unitary limit.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature at unitarity: \(T_c / \varepsilon_F = 0.167(13)\), consistent with the experimental result of Ku (2012).
- Condensate fraction at \(1/(k_F a) = 0.2\): zero-temperature estimate \(\alpha(T = 0) \approx 0.65\) from Astrakharchik (2005).
- Spin susceptibility measurements performed for couplings \(0.1 \le 1/(k_F a) \le 0.3\), described as the first QMC measurements of \(\chi_S\) away from unitarity.
- Even-odd pairing gap studied for couplings \(0.0 \le 1/(k_F a) \le 0.3\), with a plateau detected above \(T_c\) at \(1/(k_F a) \approx 0.3\) where \(na^3 \sim 1\).
- Temperature-dependent Bertsch parameter \(\xi(T/\varepsilon_F, 1/(k_F a))\) extracted at four different scattering lengths, approaching zero-temperature reference values at unitarity.

## Context

This work extends previous AFQMC studies of the BCS-BEC crossover by Bulgac (2008) and Jensen (2020), addressing the ongoing debate about whether a pseudogap exists at the unitary limit. The authors compare their results to experimental measurements by Ku (2012) and Sanner (2011), as well as theoretical calculations by Magierski (2011), Wlazlowski (2013), and Enss (2012). The use of a cubic momentum cutoff (rather than spherical) is argued to resolve discrepancies with earlier AFQMC results. The study enables future work on more refined extrapolations to the limits of zero effective range, infinite volume, and zero density.
