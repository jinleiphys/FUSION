# Few-body structures in the mirror nuclei, $^{11}$O and $^{11}$Li

> We investigate the dripline mirror nuclei, $^{11}$Li and $^{11}$O, located on the neutron and proton dripline, respectively. We calculate the lowest four states, $3/2^-$, $1/2^+$, $3/2^+$ and $5/2^+$, built on double occupancy in the nuclear $s_{1/2}$ and $p_{1/2}$ valence single-particle states. We use the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method to solve the three-body problem for a frozen nuclear core surrounded by two identical nucleons. The four analogue states in $^{11}$O are obtained with precisely the same interactions as used for the four states in $^{11}$Li, except for addition of the Coulomb interaction from the charge of the substituted valence protons. Surprisingly the four energies deviate from each other only by less than a few hundred keV. Any of them could then turn out to be the ground state, due to the uncertainty related to the angular momentum and parity dependence of the three-body potential. Still, our calculations marginally favor the $1/2^+$ state. The structures of these four states in $^{11}$O deviate substantially from the analogue states in the mirror, $^{11}$Li.

## Key claim

The four lowest states ($3/2^-$, $1/2^+$, $3/2^+$, and $5/2^+$) in $^{11}$O have energies that deviate from each other by less than a few hundred keV, meaning any of them could be the ground state due to uncertainty in the angular momentum and parity dependence of the three-body potential; the calculations marginally favor the $1/2^+$ state, contrary to previous assignments of $3/2^-$ as the ground state.

## Method

The authors used the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method to solve the three-body problem for a frozen nuclear core ($^9$Li or $^9$C) surrounded by two identical nucleons (two neutrons for $^{11}$Li, two protons for $^{11}$O). The four analogue states in $^{11}$O were computed with precisely the same nuclear interactions as for $^{11}$Li, with the addition of the Coulomb interaction from the charge of the substituted valence protons. Resonances were obtained via the complex scaling method, and the core-nucleon interaction used a Gaussian potential with spin-orbit and spin-spin terms, with parameters from Mor19.

## Key numbers

- The $^{11}$Li ground state ($3/2^-$) has a computed energy of $-0.37$ MeV (bound), fitted to the experimental two-neutron separation energy of $369.15(65)$ keV from Smi08.
- The $^{11}$Li positive-parity resonances are at $0.39-i0.15$ MeV ($1/2^+$), $0.35-i0.09$ MeV ($3/2^+$), and $0.47-i0.22$ MeV ($5/2^+$), with widths of 0.30, 0.18, and 0.44 MeV respectively.
- The $^{11}$O $3/2^-$ ground-state resonance is at $4.74$ MeV with width $2.75$ MeV; the $1/2^+$ state is at $3.77$ MeV with width $2.74$ MeV; the $3/2^+$ state is at $3.79$ MeV with width $2.84$ MeV; the $5/2^+$ state is at $4.16$ MeV with width $3.38$ MeV.
- The $^{10}$Li $2^-$ virtual state is at $-0.020$ MeV; the $1^+$ resonance is at $0.32$ MeV with width $0.19$ MeV; the $2^+$ resonance is at $0.58$ MeV with width $0.49$ MeV.
- The $^{10}$N $2^-$ resonance is at $1.74$ MeV with width $3.94$ MeV; the $1^+$ resonance is at $2.62$ MeV with width $1.68$ MeV; the $2^+$ resonance is at $2.89$ MeV with width $2.21$ MeV.
- The Coulomb shift $\Delta_c^{(1)}$ for the $3/2^-$ state is $4.93$ MeV; for $1/2^+$ it is $2.69-i0.77$ MeV; for $3/2^+$ it is $3.41-i0.86$ MeV; for $5/2^+$ it is $3.89-i1.20$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of dripline mirror nuclei triggered by the recent experiments on $^{11}$O by Web19, following earlier theoretical work on $^{10}$N by Hoo17 and Lep02. It provides a complete description of all four low-lying states ($3/2^-$, $1/2^+$, $3/2^+$, $5/2^+$) built on the $s_{1/2}$ and $p_{1/2}$ valence single-particle states, whereas previous calculations (e.g., Cha12, For19) considered only the $3/2^-$ state, and Wan19 additionally considered the $5/2^+$ state. The results enable experimental identification of the ground state in $^{11}$O and demonstrate that the Thomas-Ehrman shift can be strongly state-dependent for halo nuclei, potentially altering the level ordering compared to the mirror $^{11}$Li.
