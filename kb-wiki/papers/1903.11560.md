# Emergence of a complete heavy-quark spin symmetry multiplet: seven molecular pentaquarks in light of the latest LHCb analysis

> A recent analysis by the LHCb collaboration suggests the existence of three narrow pentaquark-like states --- the $P_c(4312)$,$P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ --- instead of just one in the previous analysis (the $P_c(4450)$). The closeness of the $P_c(4312)$ to the $\bar{D} ╬ú_c$ threshold and the $P_c(4440)$/$P_c(4457)$ to the $\bar{D}^* ╬ú_c$ one suggests a molecular interpretation of these resonances. We show that these three pentaquark-like resonances can be naturally accommodated in a contact-range effective field theory description that incorporates heavy-quark spin symmetry. This description leads to the prediction of all the seven possible S-wave heavy antimeson-baryon molecules (that is, there should be four additional molecular pentaquarks in addition to the $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$), providing the first example of a heavy-quark spin symmetry molecular multiplet that is complete. If this is confirmed, it will not only give us an impressive example of the application of heavy-quark symmetries and effective field theories in hadron physics: it will also uncover a clear and powerful ordering principle for the molecular spectrum, reminiscent of the SU(3)-flavor multiplets to which the light hadron spectrum conforms.

## Key claim

The three pentaquark-like states recently reported by LHCb, the Pc(4312), Pc(4440) and Pc(4457), can be naturally described as S-wave heavy antimeson-baryon molecules within a contact-range effective field theory that incorporates heavy-quark spin symmetry, and this description predicts the existence of four additional molecular pentaquarks, forming the first complete heavy-quark spin symmetry multiplet of hadronic molecules. The authors note that the identification of the Pc(4440) and Pc(4457) with specific spin-parity assignments (J^P = 1/2^- versus 3/2^-) is ambiguous, though their calculations show a mild preference for scenario A (Pc(4440) as 1/2^- and Pc(4457) as 3/2^-).

## Method

The authors construct a contact-range effective field theory (EFT) potential for S-wave heavy antimeson-baryon (D-bar Sigma_c, D-bar Sigma_c*, D-bar* Sigma_c, D-bar* Sigma_c*) interactions, which under heavy-quark spin symmetry reduces to two unknown coupling constants (C_a and C_b). They fix these couplings by requiring the potential to reproduce the masses of the Pc(4440) and Pc(4457) as bound states near the D-bar* Sigma_c threshold, considering two possible spin-parity assignments (scenarios A and B). The potential is renormalized using a Gaussian separable regulator with cutoff Lambda = 0.5ÔÇô1.0 GeV, and the bound-state solutions are obtained from the Lippmann-Schwinger equation; regulator independence is checked with a delta-shell potential in coordinate space (R_c = 0.5ÔÇô1.0 fm).

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of hidden-charm pentaquarks, previously suggested for the Pc(4380) and Pc(4450) discovered by LHCb in 2015 (Aaij 2015), to the three new states reported in the updated LHCb analysis (Aaij 2019). The authors build on their earlier derivation of heavy-quark spin symmetry constraints for heavy antimeson-baryon systems (Liu 2018) and show that, unlike the incomplete multiplet observed for heavy meson-antimeson molecules (e.g., the X(3872) and its potential partners), the seven S-wave D-bar(*) Sigma_c(*) molecules can form a complete HQSS multiplet. The predictions are consistent with the one-boson-exchange models of Chen (2019) and He (2019) (which correspond to scenario A) and differ from the ordering predicted by Karliner (2015) (scenario B). The work enables experimental searches for the four predicted missing pentaquarks, the D-bar Sigma_c* molecule near 4370ÔÇô4380 MeV and the three D-bar* Sigma_c* molecules in the 4500ÔÇô4525 MeV range, and establishes heavy-quark spin symmetry as an ordering principle for molecular spectra analogous to SU(3)-flavor multiplets in light hadron spectroscopy.
