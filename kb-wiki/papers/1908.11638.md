# $\bf ^{12}{C} + {}^{16}{O}$ molecular resonances at deep sub-barrier energy

> The existence of $^{12}{\rm C} + {}^{16}{\rm O}$ molecular resonances at sub-barrier energy has been a significant problem in nuclear astrophysics because they strongly affect the $^{12}{\rm C} + {}^{16}{\rm O}$ fusion reaction rate in type Ia supernovae and heavy stars. However, experimental surveys have been limited to 4~MeV and cannot access the deep sub-barrier energy due to a very small fusion cross section. Here we predict a couple of resonances with $J^π=0^+$, $2^+$, and $4^+$ in the deep sub-barrier energy based on the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics calculation that reproduces the known resonances and low-lying spectrum of $^{28}{\rm Si}$.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the existence of a couple of molecular resonances with \(J^\pi = 0^+\), \(2^+\), and \(4^+\) at deep sub-barrier energies (below the Coulomb barrier of approximately 10 MeV) based on antisymmetrized molecular dynamics calculations, but notes that these predictions depend on the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction (Gogny D1S) and the model space used, and that the number and energies of such resonances have varied significantly across different theoretical models.

## Method

The authors performed antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations using the Gogny D1S effective interaction, with constraints on both quadrupole deformation and inter-cluster distance between \(^{12}\mathrm{C}\) and \(^{16}\mathrm{O}\) clusters and between \(\alpha\) and \(^{24}\mathrm{Mg}\) clusters. After energy variation, the basis wave functions were projected to eigenstates of angular momentum and superposed using the generator coordinate method (GCM) to diagonalize the nuclear Hamiltonian. The reduced width amplitude (RWA) for \(^{12}\mathrm{C}+^{16}\mathrm{O}\) clustering was calculated to identify molecular states among the excited states of \(^{28}\mathrm{Si}\).

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb barrier height for the \(^{12}\mathrm{C}+^{16}\mathrm{O}\) system was estimated as approximately 10 MeV above the \(^{12}\mathrm{C}+^{16}\mathrm{O}\) threshold.
- The MR2 band is built on a \(0^+\) state at 1.8 MeV above the \(^{12}\mathrm{C}+^{16}\mathrm{O}\) threshold (deep sub-barrier).
- The MR1 band \(0^+\) states lie at 5 to 10 MeV above threshold, with RWA peaking at inter-cluster distance \(a = 6\)–7 fm.
- The MR2 band RWA peaks at a shorter distance of \(a = 4.5\) fm.
- \(B(E2)\) strengths between \(J^\pi \leq 6^+\) states in the MR1 band are on the order of \(10^3~\mathrm{e}^2\mathrm{fm}^4\).
- Experimentally observed resonances at 3.9–4.0 and 4.4 MeV from low-energy \(^{12}\mathrm{C}+^{16}\mathrm{O}\) fusion reactions (Fang et al., 2017) coincide reasonably with the \(2^+\) and \(4^+\) states of the calculated MR2 band.

## Context

This work extends earlier AMD calculations that successfully described the low-lying states and superdeformed band of \(^{28}\mathrm{Si}\) (PhysRevC.80.044316) to highly excited states, aiming to identify \(^{12}\mathrm{C}+^{16}\mathrm{O}\) molecular resonances of astrophysical interest. It addresses the long-standing uncertainty in theoretical predictions, where earlier resonating group method calculations by Baye et al. (1976, 1977) and orthogonality condition model calculations by Kato et al. (1985) gave different numbers and energies of molecular states. The predicted deep sub-barrier MR2 band lies within the Gamow window for carbon burning and is expected to impact the \(^{12}\mathrm{C}+^{16}\mathrm{O}\) fusion reaction rate in type Ia supernovae and heavy stars, affecting element abundances in the \(14 \lesssim Z \lesssim 20\) region.
