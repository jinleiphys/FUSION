# Interaction between Interpenetrating Charge Clouds and Collision of High-Energy Particles

> Interaction between two interpenetrating spherically symmetric charge distributions has been calculated. Limited range terms appear in addition to the Coulomb potential. Its strength increases and range decreases with reducing sizes of the interacting particles. Between two hydrogen atoms it yields the Morse Potential. Soft core potentials are obtained between pairs of nucleons. It has been shown that when high-energy particles approach one another the potential between them increases with increasing relative speed. They are not likely to disintegrate on impact. A possible way of smashing particles by 3-body collisions is indicated. PACS no. 11.90.+t 12.40.-y

# Interaction between Interpenetrating Charge Clouds and Collision of High-Energy Particles

## Key claim
The interaction between two interpenetrating spherically symmetric charge distributions produces short-range terms in addition to the Coulomb potential, and this classical overlap interaction can yield potentials that resemble the Morse potential for hydrogen atoms and soft-core nuclear potentials for nucleons, provided the charge clouds retain their identities during overlap. The author suggests that this mechanism may account for the strong nuclear force, but notes that the numbers used for nucleon charge distributions are not sacrosanct and were chosen to demonstrate feasibility.

## Method
The author calculates the electrostatic interaction energy between two overlapping spherically symmetric charge distributions by integrating the potential of one distribution against the charge density of the other. For hydrogen atoms, exponential charge densities from ground-state wavefunctions are used; for nucleons, a two-component charge density (negative core plus positive outer cloud) is parameterized with rms radii and charge magnitudes, and the resulting potential is compared to the Reid soft-core potential. Relativistic contraction is modeled by reducing the rms radii of the charge distributions to simulate high-energy collisions.

## Key numbers
- For two hydrogen atoms with equal charge distributions, the interaction energy at total overlap is V(0) = q_A q_B (5√3)/(8 r̅), where r̅ is the rms radius.
- The rms radius of a nucleon is smaller than that of an atom by a factor of 10^5, making the potential between nucleons of order MeV compared to eV between atoms.
- For the proton charge distribution, the negative core has rms radius r̅_0 = 0.005 fm and charge q_0 = -32 e_P; the positive outer cloud has rms radius r̅_1 = 0.8 fm and charge q_1 = +33 e_P.
- The peak of the p-p potential at R=0 for a proton at rest is about 4 × 10^5 MeV.
- For high-energy collisions, rms radii are reduced: at 20% reduction, r̅_0 = 0.004 fm and r̅_1 = 0.64 fm; at 40% reduction, r̅_0 = 0.003 fm and r̅_1 = 0.48 fm.

## Context
This work extends classical electrostatic calculations to overlapping charge distributions, yielding short-range potentials that the author connects to the Morse potential for molecular binding (Slater, year not given) and to the Reid soft-core nucleon-nucleon potential (Reid, year not given). The author argues that the charge-independent nature of nuclear force can be explained if protons and neutrons have similar two-component charge structures with large internal charges (32-33 units) that nearly cancel. The paper suggests that relativistic contraction at high energies makes particles harder to disintegrate in direct collisions, and proposes that three-body collisions (a stationary "soft" proton caught between two high-speed nuclei) may be a viable method for smashing protons, though the outcome depends on the strength of gluonic binding.
