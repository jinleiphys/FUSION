# Including resummation in the NLO BK equation

> We include a resummation of large transverse momentum logarithms in the next-to-leading order (NLO) Balitsky-Kovchegov equation. The resummed evolution equation is shown to be stable, the evolution speed being significantly reduced by NLO corrections. The contributions from NLO terms that are not enhanced by large logarithms are found to be numerically important close to phenomenologically relevant initial conditions. We numerically determine the value for the constant in the resummed logarithm that includes a maximal part of the full NLO terms in the resummation.

## Key claim

The resummation of large transverse momentum logarithms stabilizes the NLO Balitsky-Kovchegov equation, but the finite (non-logarithmically-enhanced) NLO terms are numerically important near phenomenologically relevant initial conditions; the constant \(C_\textnormal{sub}=0.65\) in the resummed logarithm is determined to minimize the contribution of these finite NLO terms.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the NLO BK equation in the large-\(N_c\) limit, including a resummation of double and single collinear logarithms via the replacement of the perturbative expansion with Bessel-function and exponential factors, following the procedure of Iancu et al. (2015). They subtract the \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)\) part of the resummed single-log term to avoid double counting with the full NLO kernel \(K_2\), and separate the evolution into LO (running coupling), resummation, and finite NLO contributions. The running coupling is implemented via the "Balitsky" prescription for the LO term and a parent-dipole prescription for NLO terms, frozen to \(\alpha_s(r\to\infty)=0.76\) in the infrared.

## Key numbers

- The subtraction constant that minimizes the finite NLO terms is \(C_\textnormal{sub}=0.65\).
- The infrared coupling is frozen to \(\alpha_s(r\to\infty)=0.76\).
- The LO DGLAP anomalous dimension resummed in the single transverse log is \(A_1 = 11/12\).
- The initial saturation scale \(\frac{Q_{s,0}}{\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}}\) and anomalous dimension \(\gamma\) are tunable parameters; at LO phenomenology prefers \(\gamma\gtrsim 1\) at \(y=0\), evolving toward \(\gamma\sim 0.8\).

## Context

This work extends the numerical solution of the NLO BK equation from Lappi and Mäntysaari (2015, 2016) by including the resummation of double and single transverse logarithms developed by Iancu et al. (2015). It goes beyond the earlier resummation-only calculation of Iancu et al. (2015), which set \(C_\textnormal{sub}=1\) and neglected the finite NLO terms, by consistently including those terms and determining the optimal subtraction constant. The results enable future fits to DIS data and applications to particle production in proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions.
