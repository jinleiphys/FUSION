# Cause of the charge radius isotope shift at the \emph{N}=126 shell gap

> We discuss the mechanism causing the `kink' in the charge radius isotope shift at the N=126 shell closure. The occupation of the 1$i_{11/2}$ neutron orbital is the decisive factor for reproducing the experimentally observed kink. We investigate whether this orbital is occupied or not by different Skyrme effective interactions as neutrons are added above the shell closure. Our results demonstrate that several factors can cause an appreciable occupation of the 1$i_{11/2}$ neutron orbital, including the magnitude of the spin-orbit field, and the isoscalar effective mass of the Skyrme interaction. The symmetry energy of the effective interaction has little influence upon its ability to reproduce the kink.

# Cause of the charge radius isotope shift at the *N*=126 shell gap

## Key claim

The occupation of the neutron 1*i*<sub>11/2</sub> orbital is the decisive factor for reproducing the experimentally observed kink in the charge radius isotope shift at the *N*=126 shell closure, but several factors within the Skyrme effective interaction can cause this orbital to be preferentially occupied, including the magnitude of the spin-orbit field and the isoscalar effective mass, while the symmetry energy has little influence.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus BCS pairing calculations for lead isotopes from *A*=200 to *A*=214, using a range of Skyrme effective interactions. They computed the charge radius isotope shift δ⟨*r*<sup>2</sup><sub>ch</sub>⟩ relative to <sup>208</sup>Pb and defined a ratio *R*<sub>kink</sub> as the gradient of the isotope shift above *A*=208 divided by the gradient below *A*=208. They then correlated *R*<sub>kink</sub> with the BCS occupation probabilities of the neutron 1*i*<sub>11/2</sub> and 2*g*<sub>9/2</sub> orbitals, and with nuclear matter properties of each Skyrme interaction.

## Key numbers

- The experimental ratio *R*<sub>kink</sub> (gradient above *A*=208 divided by gradient below *A*=208) is 2.04(5).
- Successful Skyrme interactions are defined as those producing *R*<sub>kink</sub> within approximately 10% of the experimental value.
- The neutron 2*g*<sub>9/2</sub> and 1*i*<sub>11/2</sub> orbitals are separated by approximately 1 MeV experimentally, with the 2*g*<sub>9/2</sub> more deeply bound.
- Skyrme interactions with a smaller integrated spin-orbit field tend to reproduce the kink, with SkI5 being a notable exception that reproduces the kink despite a spin-orbit field magnitude similar to unsuccessful interactions.

## Context

This work extends the explanation proposed in Goddard et al. (2013) that the 1*i*<sub>11/2</sub> neutron orbital occupation causes the kink, in contrast to earlier explanations by Reinhard and Sharma that emphasized the 2*g*<sub>9/2</sub> orbital radius or the role of ground-state quadrupole correlations by Brown. The results demonstrate that the spin-orbit field and isoscalar effective mass are the relevant Skyrme parameters, while the symmetry energy shows no correlation with kink reproduction. This enables future work to include tensor forces (as noted by Zalewski et al.) and beyond-mean-field effects to further refine the mechanism.
