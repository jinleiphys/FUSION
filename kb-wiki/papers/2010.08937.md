# Vorticity and Spin Polarization in Heavy Ion Collisions: Transport Models

> Heavy ion collisions generate strong fluid vorticty in the produced hot quark-gluon matter which could in turn induce measurable spin polarization of hadrons. We review recent progress on the vorticity formation and spin polarization in heavy ion collisions with transport models. We present an introduction to the fluid vorticity in non-relativistic and relativistic hydrodynamics and address various properties of the vorticity formed in heavy ion collisions. We discuss the spin polarization in a vortical fluid using the Wigner function formalism in which we derive the freeze-out formula for the spin polarization. Finally we give a brief overview of recent theoretical results for both the global and local spin polarization of $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ hyperons.

## Key claim

This review paper summarizes progress on vorticity formation and spin polarization in heavy ion collisions using transport models, but it does not present new original calculations; instead it synthesizes existing theoretical results and describes the formalism (Wigner function, freeze-out formula) that connects fluid vorticity to measurable spin polarization of hyperons, with the caveat that the spin polarization formula (Eq. for average spin per particle) is derived under the assumption of global equilibrium and then generalized to a hydrodynamic freeze-out hypersurface.

## Method

The authors review the definitions of four types of relativistic vorticity (kinematic, temperature, enthalpy, and thermal) and derive the spin polarization of spin-1/2 fermions in a vortical fluid using the covariant Wigner function formalism with a semi-classical expansion in powers of Planck constant. They describe how transport models (such as AMPT and HIJING) extract the fluid velocity and temperature fields from particle phase-space information via coarse-graining or Gaussian smearing, and then compute the vorticity field. The average spin per particle on a freeze-out hypersurface is given by an integral formula involving the thermal vorticity tensor and the Fermi-Dirac distribution.

## Key numbers

* Global spin polarization of Λ and anti-Λ hyperons measured by STAR in Au+Au collisions: about 2% at √sNN = 7.7 GeV, decreasing to about 0.3% at √sNN = 200 GeV, and almost vanishing at LHC energies (ALICE in Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV).
* Kinematic vorticity magnitude at mid-rapidity: about 10^20 s^{-1} at impact parameter b = 10 fm and √s = 200 GeV, surpassing the vorticity of any other known fluids.
* Kinematic vorticity at mid-rapidity decreases as collision energy √s increases, despite the total angular momentum of the colliding nuclei growing with energy.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on vorticity and spin polarization in heavy ion collisions that began with the prediction of global polarization from orbital angular momentum (Liang 2004, Voloshin 2004) and the subsequent measurements by STAR (2017) and ALICE (2019). It summarizes results from transport-model calculations (Jiang 2016, Deng 2016, Li 2017, Wei 2018, Shi 2017) that reproduce the measured global polarization, and it contrasts these with hydrodynamic-model approaches (ECHO-QGP, PICR, CLVisc). The paper enables future work on local spin polarization patterns and spin-spin correlations by providing the formal connection between the thermal vorticity and the freeze-out spin polarization formula.
