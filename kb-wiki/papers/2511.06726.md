# Jacobi Coordinates on Hyper-tori and Geometric Factors in the Volume Dependencies

> We derive the volume dependence of bound states from a cluster-cluster picture with nucleon degrees of freedom. To achieve this, we demonstrate how to construct Jacobi coordinates on the lattice under the periodic boundary. A constant factor called ``Geometric factor'' appears in the generalization from point-like particles to clusters. We validate our derivation using many-body calculations, specifically, we find this factor to be essential in extracting asymptotic normalization constants from lattice calculations of \isotope[16]{O} ground state.

## Key claim

The paper derives a constant "geometric factor" that appears when generalizing volume-dependence formulas for bound states from point-like two-body systems to cluster-cluster configurations with nucleon degrees of freedom; this factor is not a measurable physical quantity but an artifact of choosing nucleons rather than clusters as the intrinsic degrees of freedom, and it can be geometrically increasing with the mass numbers of clusters.

## Method

The author first demonstrates the geometric factor in a simple mean-field picture with Dirichlet boundary conditions (harmonic oscillator bases), showing that the volume correction accumulates as a factor equal to the number of particles. The paper then constructs Jacobi coordinates on hyper-tori under periodic boundary conditions through an iterative transformation that shifts copies of triangular regions, preserving translational invariance while removing center-of-mass ambiguity. Finally, the volume dependence for bound states on cubic lattices is derived by applying a surface-integral technique to the cluster-cluster wavefunction ansatz, using the Jacobi lattice construction to handle the relative coordinates between clusters.

## Key numbers

* For the single-particle basis with Dirichlet boundary, the energy shift is \(\Delta E_L = A \times \frac{\kappa C_0^2}{m}\exp(-2 \kappa L)\), where \(A\) is the number of particles and the factor \(A\) counts the faces of a hypercube in the \(A\)-dimensional configuration space.
* For the two-body system on a periodic lattice with Coulomb interaction, the leading volume dependence is \(\Delta E_{\textrm{TB},L} = -\frac{3 C_0^2}{\mu L} [W_{-\bar\eta,\frac{1}{2}}(\kappa L)]^2\), where \(\mu\) is the reduced mass, \(\kappa\) the binding momentum, \(\bar\eta\) the Sommerfeld parameter, and \(C_0\) the asymptotic normalization constant for angular momentum \(l=0\).
* The geometric factor for a cluster \(C = (C_{p\uparrow},C_{p\downarrow},C_{n\uparrow},C_{n\downarrow})\) in a nucleus \(A = (A_{p\uparrow},A_{p\downarrow},A_{n\uparrow},A_{n\downarrow})\) is given by \(G_{A,C} = \binom{A_{p\uparrow}}{C_{p\uparrow}}\binom{A_{p\downarrow}}{C_{p\downarrow}}\binom{A_{n\uparrow}}{C_{n\uparrow}}\binom{A_{n\downarrow}}{C_{n\downarrow}}\).

## Context

This work extends the volume-dependence formalism initiated by Luscher (1985-1991) for two-body systems on periodic lattices, and the generalization to cluster configurations by Konig (2017). The paper also connects to the BREW-type formulation by Busch (1998) and Luu (2010) for harmonic oscillator bases, and to the exponential cutoff dependence derived by Furnstahl (2013) and More (2013). The geometric factor is essential for extracting asymptotic normalization constants from lattice calculations, which are needed for astrophysical reaction rates where peripheral interactions are too low to measure at relevant energies. The Jacobi lattice construction enables future work on few-body algorithms such as the DVR method and improves understanding of volume dependence for clustering systems.
