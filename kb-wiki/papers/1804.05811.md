# From cluster structures to nuclear molecules: the role of nodal structure of the single-particle wave functions

> The nodal structure of the density distributions of the single-particle states occupied in rod-shaped, hyper- and megadeformed structures of non-rotating and rotating $N\sim Z$ nuclei has been investigated in detail. The single-particle states with the Nilsson quantum numbers of the $[NN0]1/2$ (with $N$ from 0 to 5) and $[N,N-1,1]Ω$ (with $N$ from 1 to 3 and $Ω=1/2$, 3/2) types are considered. These states are building blocks of extremely deformed shapes in the nuclei with mass numbers $A \leq 50$. Because of (near)axial symmetry and large elongation of such structures, the wave functions of the single-particle states occupied are dominated by a single basis state in cylindrical basis. This basis state defines the nodal structure of the single-particle density distribution. The nodal structure of the single-particle density distributions allows to understand in a relatively simple way the necessary conditions for $α$-clusterization and the suppression of the $α$-clusterization with the increase of mass number. It also explains in a natural way the coexistence of ellipsoidal mean-field type structures and nuclear molecules at similar excitation energies and the features of particle-hole excitations connecting these two types of the structures. Our analysis of the nodal structure of the single-particle density distributions does not support the existence of quantum liquid phase for the deformations and nuclei under study.

## Key claim

The nodal structure of single-particle density distributions, dominated by a single basis state in cylindrical coordinates due to extreme prolate deformation and near-axial symmetry, provides a simple explanation for the necessary conditions for alpha-clusterization and its suppression with increasing mass number, as well as the coexistence of ellipsoidal mean-field structures and nuclear molecules at similar excitation energies. The analysis does not support the existence of a quantum liquid phase for the deformations and nuclei under study (N=Z nuclei with mass numbers A less than or equal to 50).

## Method

The authors performed systematic cranked relativistic mean field (CRMF) calculations using the NL3* covariant energy density functional, solving the equations in an anisotropic three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis in Cartesian coordinates with truncation up to N_F=14 fermionic shells and N_B=20 bosonic shells. They analyzed the single-particle density distributions and their nodal structure for occupied states in rod-shaped, hyperdeformed, and megadeformed configurations of non-rotating and rotating N=Z nuclei, specifically focusing on 12C, 28Si, and 40Ca, considering Nilsson states of the [NN0]1/2 and [N,N-1,1]Omega types.

## Key numbers

- The rod-shaped configuration in 12C was studied at rotational frequency Omega_x = 3.2 MeV, corresponding to spin I = 8.77 hbar.
- The [000]1/2 state in 12C has wave function structure 96.0% |000,1/2> at Omega_x = 0.0 MeV, decreasing to 92.5% |000,1/2> at Omega_x = 3.2 MeV.
- The [220]1/2 state in 12C has wave function structure 89.7% |220,1/2> at Omega_x = 0.0 MeV, decreasing to 72.3% |220,1/2> at Omega_x = 3.2 MeV.
- In the megadeformed [42,42] configuration of 40Ca, the [000]1/2 state has 92.7% |000,1/2> at Omega_x = 0.0 MeV and 90.4% |000,1/2> at Omega_x = 1.8 MeV (I = 25 hbar).
- The [440]1/2 state (r=-i) in 40Ca shows the largest fragmentation, with dominant component decreasing from 77.4% |440,1/2> at Omega_x = 0.0 MeV to 36.0% |440,1/2> at Omega_x = 1.8 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the systematic studies of cluster and extremely deformed structures in light nuclei performed within the density functional theory framework by Afanasjev and collaborators (2016, 2017), specifically addressing the gap in understanding how single-particle states contribute to clusterization. The analysis builds on earlier discussions of single-particle density contributions in 8Be and 20Ne by Ebran et al. (2012, 2014), and provides a microscopic explanation for the transition from localized clusters to more uniform density distributions with increasing mass number, contradicting the conclusion of Ebran et al. (2013) that a quantum liquid phase emerges for nuclei with A approximately 30. The results enable future studies of particle-hole excitations that connect ellipsoidal mean-field structures to nuclear molecules in the A=28-50 mass region.
