# The Sea of Quarks and Antiquarks in the Nucleon: a Review

> The quark and gluon structure of the proton has been under intense experimental and theoretical investigation for five decades. Even for the distributions of the well-studied valence quarks, challenges such as the value of the down quark to up quark ratio at high fractional momenta remain. Much of the sea of quark-antiquark pairs emerges from the splitting of gluons and is well described by perturbative evolution in quantum chromodynamics. However, experiments confirm that there is a non-perturbative component to the sea that is not well understood and hitherto has been difficult to calculate with ab initio non-perturbative methods. This non-perturbative structure shows up, perhaps most directly, in the flavor dependence of the sea antiquark distributions. While some of the general trends can be reproduced by models, there are features of the data that do not seem to be well described. This article discusses the experimental situation, the status of calculations and models, and the directions where these studies will progress in the near future.

## Key claim

While perturbative QCD evolution from gluon splitting describes the sea quark distributions at low x and high energy scales, experiments confirm a non-perturbative component to the sea that is not well understood and has been difficult to calculate with ab initio non-perturbative methods; this non-perturbative structure appears most directly in the flavor dependence of the sea antiquark distributions, and some features of the data are not well described by current models.

## Method

The authors review experimental measurements of sea quark and antiquark distributions in the nucleon using deep inelastic scattering (DIS) and Drell-Yan reactions, along with global parton distribution function (PDF) fits from CTEQ, MRST, and NNPDF collaborations. They also examine spin-dependent parton distributions from polarized DIS and semi-inclusive DIS, and discuss strange quark distributions extracted from neutrino DIS with dimuon final states and from W boson production at colliders.

## Key numbers

- NMC measured the Gottfried sum integral to be 0.240 ± 0.016 (later updated to 0.235 ± 0.026), showing that $\bar{d} > \bar{u}$
- NA51 found $\bar{u}/\bar{d} (\langle x \rangle = 0.18) = 0.51 \pm 0.04 \pm 0.05$ at leading order
- NUSEA measured $\int_{0.015}^{0.35} dx [\bar{d}(x) - \bar{u}(x)] = 0.080 \pm 0.011$ at an average scale of 54 GeV$^2$, and extrapolating to the integral from 0 to 1 gave 0.118 ± 0.012
- ATLAS measured $(s + \bar{s})/(2\bar{d}) = 1.19 \pm 0.07 (\text{exp.}) \pm 0.02 (\text{mod}) {}^{+0.02}_{-0.10} (\text{par})$ at x ~ 0.023 and scale 1.9 GeV$^2$
- NuTeV found strange sea suppression parameters $\kappa_{\nu} = 0.44 \pm 0.06 \pm 0.04$ and $\kappa_{\nu}^- = 0.45 \pm 0.08 \pm 0.07$ for the strange and anti-strange distributions relative to the non-strange sea

## Context

This review extends the line of work on nucleon parton structure that began with the parton model and QCD evolution, and specifically addresses the experimental discovery by the New Muon Collaboration (1991) that $\bar{d} > \bar{u}$, contradicting the earlier assumption of flavor-symmetric sea distributions used in global fits before 1989. The review discusses how the non-perturbative flavor asymmetry of the sea, confirmed by NUSEA and HERMES, cannot be generated by perturbative QCD evolution and requires non-perturbative mechanisms such as pion-nucleon fluctuations or chiral soliton models. Future progress will come from improved lattice QCD calculations, higher precision Drell-Yan measurements, and better understanding of nuclear corrections in deuterium and heavy targets.
