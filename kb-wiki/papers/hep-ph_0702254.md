# Density fluctuations in the presence of spinodal instabilities

> Density fluctuations resulting from spinodal decomposition in a non-equilibrium first-order chiral phase transition are explored. We show that such instabilities generate divergent fluctuations of conserved charges along the isothermal spinodal lines appearing in the coexistence region. Thus, divergent density fluctuations could be a signal not only for the critical end point but also for the first order phase transition expected in strongly interacting matter. We also compute the mean-field critical exponent at the spinodal lines. Our analysis is performed in the mean-field approximation to the NJL model formulated at finite temperature and density. However, our main conclusions are expected to be generic and model independent.

# Density fluctuations in the presence of spinodal instabilities

## Key claim

Divergent fluctuations of conserved charges (baryon number, electric charge) are not only a signal for the QCD critical end point but also appear along the isothermal spinodal lines of a first-order chiral phase transition in non-equilibrium systems. The authors stress that this result is obtained in the mean-field approximation to the NJL model, but they argue the conclusion is generic and model independent because it follows from a thermodynamic relation.

## Method

The authors compute the net quark number susceptibility χ_μμ = ∂n_q/∂μ in the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with two quark flavors and three colors, using the mean-field approximation at finite temperature and chemical potential. They locate the isothermal and isentropic spinodal lines in the (T, n_q) plane by finding where (∂P/∂V)_T = 0 and (∂P/∂V)_S = 0, respectively, and then evaluate the susceptibility along paths crossing these lines. The model parameters (coupling G_S Λ² = 2.44, cutoff Λ = 587.9 MeV, current quark masses m_u = m_d = 5.6 MeV) are fixed to reproduce vacuum pion properties.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant: G_S Λ² = 2.44, with three-momentum cutoff Λ = 587.9 MeV.
- Current quark masses: m_u = m_d = 5.6 MeV.
- Characteristic growth time for unstable density fluctuations: τ ∼ 1–10 fm, estimated for a typical wave vector q = 100–300 MeV using results from Heiselberg (1995).
- Mean-field critical exponent at the isothermal spinodal line: γ = 1/2, with χ_μμ ∼ (μ − μ_c)^{−γ}.
- Mean-field critical exponent at the critical end point: γ = 2/3.

## Context

This work extends the study of charge fluctuations as signals for the QCD phase diagram, previously focused on the critical end point by Stephanov (1998) and Hatta and Ikeda (2003). The authors show that spinodal instabilities, discussed in the context of the nuclear liquid-gas transition by Randrup (1995) and Heiselberg (1995), also produce divergent fluctuations in the chiral transition. The results enable experimental searches for first-order phase transition signals over a broader range of beam energies in heavy-ion collisions than the narrow critical region around the CEP, and they motivate kinetic calculations along the lines of Berdnikov and Rajagopal (2000) for quantitative predictions.
