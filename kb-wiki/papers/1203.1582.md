# Cluster structures in $^{11}$B

> Structures of excited states in $^{11}$B are investigated with a method of $ő≤$-$ő≥$ constraint antisymmetrized molecular dynamics in combination with the generator coordinate method. Various excited states with developed cluster core structures are suggested in positive- and negative-parity states. For negative-parity states, we suggest a band with a $2őĪ+t$ cluster structure. This band starts from the $3/2^{-}_{3}$ state and can correspond to the experimental band observed recently. In positive-parity states, two $őĪ$ core cluster structures with surrounding nucleons are found. A $K^ŌÄ=1/2^+$ band is suggested to be constructed from a remarkably developed cluster structure with a large prolate deformation. We discuss features of the cluster structure in association with molecular orbital structures of $^{10}$Be.

## Key claim

The authors suggest that the $3/2^{-}_{3}$ state in $^{11}$B has a dilute $2\alpha + t$ cluster structure analogous to the $0^{+}_{2}$ state in $^{12}$C, based on similar GCM amplitude distributions on the $\beta$-$\gamma$ plane, though they note that the calculated excitation energies are 2.5-4.5 MeV higher than experimental values for the proposed negative-parity band.

## Method

The authors used $\beta$-$\gamma$ constraint antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with variational calculations at 196 mesh points on the $\beta$-$\gamma$ plane, followed by generator coordinate method (GCM) superposition of parity and total-angular-momentum projected wave functions. They employed the Volkov No.2 interaction for the central force and the G3RS interaction for the spin-orbit force, with a width parameter $\nu = 0.235$ fm$^{-2}$ for single-particle Gaussian wave packets.

## Key numbers

- The minimum point of the negative-parity energy surface is at $(\beta \cos \gamma, \beta \sin \gamma) = (0.13, 0.13)$, shifting to $(0.33, 0.13)$ after $3/2^{-}$ angular-momentum projection
- The minimum point of the positive-parity energy surface is at $(\beta \cos \gamma, \beta \sin \gamma) = (0.45, 0.00)$, shifting to $(0.60, 0.09)$ after $5/2^{+}$ angular-momentum projection
- The $3/2^{-}_{1}$ state has 87% overlap with the energy minimum state at $(\beta \cos \gamma, \beta \sin \gamma) = (0.33, 0.13)$ in the $3/2^{-}$ energy surface
- Calculated $B(E2)$ for $5/2^{-}_{1} \rightarrow 3/2^{-}_{1}$ is 9.2 $e^{2}$fm$^{4}$ (experiment: $14 \pm 3$ $e^{2}$fm$^{4}$)
- Calculated isoscalar monopole strength $B(E0;IS)$ for $3/2^{-}_{1} \rightarrow 3/2^{-}_{3}$ is 150 fm$^{4}$ (experiment: $96 \pm 16$ fm$^{4}$)
- Calculated root-mean-square radius for $3/2^{-}_{1}$ is 2.29 fm (experiment: $2.09 \pm 0.12$ fm); for $3/2^{-}_{3}$ it is 2.65 fm
- $E2$ transition strengths between $3/2^{-}_{3}$, $5/2^{-}_{3}$, $7/2^{-}_{3}$, and $9/2^{-}_{3}$ states are 20-30 $e^{2}$fm$^{4}$
- For the $K^{\pi}=1/2^{+}$ band, $B(E2)$ for $11/2^{+}_{2} \rightarrow 7/2^{+}_{4}$ is 154.8 $e^{2}$fm$^{4}$ and for $5/2^{+}_{3} \rightarrow 1/2^{+}_{1}$ is 48.6 $e^{2}$fm$^{4}$

## Context

This work extends earlier AMD studies of cluster structures in $^{11}$B by En'yo (2007) and addresses the controversial relationship between the $^{11}$B($3/2^{-}_{3}$) state and the $^{12}$C($0^{+}_{2}$) Hoyle state, which was questioned by Yamada (2010). The calculations support the earlier suggestion by En'yo (2007) and Kawabata (2007) that these states are analogous, based on similar GCM amplitude distributions. The results also connect to molecular orbital descriptions of $^{10}$Be developed by Seya (1981), Okabe (1979), and Itagaki (2000), showing that positive-parity states in $^{11}$B can be understood in terms of $2\alpha$ core structures with valence nucleons occupying $\pi_{3/2}$ and $\sigma_{1/2}$ molecular orbitals. The work enables future theoretical calculations of partial decay widths for the proposed cluster band and further studies of the relationship between cluster structures in $^{11}$B and $^{12}$C.
