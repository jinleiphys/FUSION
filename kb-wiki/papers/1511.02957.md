# Generator coordinate method for hypernuclear spectroscopy with a covariant density functional

> We apply the generator coordinate method (GCM) to single-$Λ$ hypernuclei in order to discuss the spectra of hypernuclear low-lying states. To this end, we use the same relativistic point-coupling energy functional both for the mean-field and the beyond-mean-field calculations. This relativistic GCM approach provides a unified description of low-lying states in ordinary nuclei and in hypernuclei, and is thus suitable for studying the $Λ$ impurity effect. We carry out an illustrative calculation for the low-lying spectrum of $^{21}_Λ$Ne, in which the interplay between the hypernuclear collective excitations and the single-particle excitations of the unpaired $Λ$ hyperon is taken into account in a full microscopic manner.

## Key claim

The generator coordinate method (GCM) applied to the whole hypernucleus (not just the core) provides a unified beyond-mean-field description of low-lying states in ordinary nuclei and hypernuclei, but the adiabatic approximation is used (different hyperon orbitals are not mixed in the total wave function) and the calculation is restricted to axially deformed reference states.

## Method

The authors generate a set of hypernuclear mean-field reference states with different quadrupole deformation β using deformation-constrained relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations with the PC-F1 force for nucleon-nucleon and PCY-S2 force for nucleon-Λ interactions. These states are projected onto good particle number and angular momentum, then superposed via the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation to obtain the low-lying states of the hypernucleus. An illustrative calculation is performed for ²¹_ΛNe, placing the Λ hyperon in the four lowest single-particle orbitals (Ω^π = 1/2⁺₁, 1/2⁻₁, 3/2⁻₁, and 1/2⁻₂).

## Key numbers

- The energy difference between the positive-parity (s-orbital) and negative-parity (p-orbital) Λ configurations at β=0 is about 10.4 MeV, consistent with 2/3 of ħω = 41A⁻¹/³ MeV for nucleons.
- The Λ binding energy B_Λ of ²¹_ΛNe (energy difference between 0⁺₁ of ²⁰Ne and 1/2⁺₁ of ²¹_ΛNe) is 14.11 MeV, slightly smaller than the mean-field result of 14.27 MeV.
- The energy difference between the 1/2⁻ and 3/2⁻ states in the K^π = 1/2⁻ band is 270 keV with the GCM calculation, compared to less than 40 keV with the microscopic particle-rotor model.
- Energy splittings in the K^π = 1/2⁺ band doublets are 41.5 keV (3/2⁺, 5/2⁺), 71.2 keV (7/2⁺, 9/2⁺), and 53.8 keV (11/2⁺, 13/2⁺).
- The E2 transition strength for 3/2⁺ → 1/2⁺ in ²¹_ΛNe is smaller than the E2 strength for 2⁺ → 0⁺ in ²⁰Ne by 13.37%.
- The energy minimum for the K^π = 1/2⁺ configuration appears at β = 0.62 after projection, compared to β = 0.49 in the mean-field curve; the projected 0⁺ of ²⁰Ne has a minimum at β = 0.65.
- The energy minimum for the J^π = 1/2⁻ configuration appears at β = 0.69.

## Context

This work extends beyond-mean-field methods for hypernuclear spectroscopy, going beyond the angular-momentum-projected Skyrme density functional calculation of Cui (2015) which did not include configuration mixing. It is complementary to the microscopic particle-rotor model developed by Mei, Hagino, and Yao (2014, 2015), where GCM is applied only to the core nucleus; here all nucleons and the hyperon are treated on the same footing. The approach enables future studies of hypernuclear shape fluctuations and the polarization effect of the Λ hyperon on the nuclear core, and can be extended to include triaxial and octupole deformations.
