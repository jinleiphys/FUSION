# Thermal Equilibration of 176-Lu via K-Mixing

> In astrophysical environments, the long-lived (\T_1/2 = 37.6 Gy) ground state of 176-Lu can communicate with a short-lived (T_1/2 = 3.664 h) isomeric level through thermal excitations. Thus, the lifetime of 176-Lu in an astrophysical environment can be quite different than in the laboratory. We examine the possibility that the rate of equilibration can be enhanced via K-mixing of two levels near E_x = 725 keV and estimate the relevant gamma-decay rates. We use this result to illustrate the effect of K-mixing on the effective stellar half-life. We also present a network calculation that includes the equilibrating transitions allowed by K-mixing. Even a small amount of K-mixing will ensure that 176-Lu reaches at least a quasi-equilibrium during an s-process triggered by the 22-Ne neutron source.

# Thermal Equilibration of 176-Lu via K-Mixing

## Key claim

Even a small amount of K-mixing between two 7- states near 725 keV (with mixing matrix elements as small as 1-6.6 eV, corresponding to mixing fractions of 3.74 × 10⁻⁴% to 0.015%) is sufficient to ensure that 176-Lu reaches at least a quasi-equilibrium during an s-process triggered by the 22-Ne neutron source, potentially at lower temperatures than previously thought possible via the previously identified 838.6-keV mediating state.

## Method

The authors calculated γ-decay rates for K-mixed transitions between states in the ground-state and isomer bands of 176-Lu using a semi-empirical approach based on the intrinsic quadrupole moment Q₀ = 7.35(4) e b and gyromagnetic ratios from deformed Woods-Saxon wave functions. They solved coupled differential equations describing the time evolution of the nuclear states under stellar conditions, reducing the system to a two-level approximation for the ground-state and isomer clusters, and performed post-processing network calculations for a 3 M☉ AGB star during the thermal-pulse phase.

## Key numbers

- Ground state half-life: 37.6 Gy; isomer half-life: 3.664 h
- Excitation energies of the two 7- states proposed to mix: 724.7 keV (Kπ = 0-) and 725.2 keV (Kπ = 6-), with energy difference ΔEx = 517 eV
- For the (76) E2 transition: B(E2) = 1.734 e b², implying Tγ₁/₂(76) = 16.7/α² ps
- For the (53) M1 transition: B(M1) = 0.298 μN², Tγ₁/₂(M1) = 31.5 ps, with mixing ratio δ(53) = 0.3 giving Tγ₁/₂(53) = 28.9/α² ps
- For maximal mixing (V = 258.5 eV, β² = 50%): Tγ₁/₂(56) = 33.4 ps and Tγ₁/₂(73) = 57.8 ps
- For minimal mixing (V = 1 eV, β² = 3.74 × 10⁻⁴%): Tγ₁/₂(56) = 4.47 × 10⁶ ps and Tγ₁/₂(73) = 7.73 × 10⁶ ps
- Equilibration time between clusters at T₉ = 0.25 and β² = 9.40 × 10⁻³%: approximately 10⁵ s
- Full thermal equilibrium achieved for T₉ ≳ 0.22-0.30 for the K-mixing scenario, compared to T₉ ≈ 0.29-0.32 via the 838.6-keV state
- Solar system Hf/Lu ratio adopted: 7.67(18)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Klay (1991) and Lesko (1991) who showed that 176-Lu can reach quasi-equilibrium through a mediating state at 838.6 keV, and by Kondev and McGoram who identified K-mixing at higher excitation energies in 176-Lu. The paper demonstrates that K-mixing between states at approximately 725 keV provides a more efficient equilibration path than previously considered, operating at lower temperatures than the 838.6-keV route. This mechanism is expected to operate in other s-process nuclei with K-isomers, such as 180-Ta, and enables more accurate modeling of the s-process branching at A = 176, though the small predicted γ-ray branching ratios complicate experimental verification and the use of 176-Lu as an s-process thermometer.
