# Splitting of the chiral critical point and realization of solitonic pion condensate driven by isospin density

> We study the influence of the isospin asymmetry on the phase structure of strongly interacting quark matter near the tricritical point (TCP) using a generalized Ginzburg-Landau approach. The effect has proven to be so drastic, not only bringing about the shift of the location of TCP, but resulting in a rich fine structure at the vicinity of TCP. In particular, we find that an arbitrary small perturbation due to isospin density lifts the degeneracy of TCP making it split into four independent multicritical points. Accordingly, the homogeneous pion condensate and its solitonic counterpart come to occupy large domains in the Ginzburg-Landau coupling space.

# Splitting of the chiral critical point and realization of solitonic pion condensate driven by isospin density

## Key claim
Using a generalized Ginzburg-Landau approach, an arbitrarily small isospin chemical potential perturbation lifts the degeneracy of the tricritical point (TCP) in strongly interacting quark matter, splitting it into four independent multicritical points; this result is derived under the assumption that quark loops dominate near the TCP, which is justified only if the TCP is located at large fugacity region.

## Method
The authors constructed a Ginzburg-Landau (GL) functional for the chiral four-vector order parameter (sigma and pion fields) up to sixth order in the order parameter and its spatial derivatives, incorporating isospin asymmetry as a perturbative expansion in the isospin chemical potential μI. They derived universal relations among the GL couplings by assuming quark-loop dominance near the TCP, reducing the parameter space to three independent parameters: α₂⁰, α₄⁰, and μI². The phase diagram was analyzed for homogeneous condensates analytically, and for inhomogeneous (solitonic) condensates by solving the Euler-Lagrange equation analytically for one-dimensional structures or using a variational chiral spiral ansatz.

## Key numbers
- TCP shifts from (α₂=0, α₄=0) to TCP′ at (α₂=0, α₄=-μI²) when isospin density is introduced.
- The second-order phase boundary between Wigner and pion condensed phases is given by α₂ = μI²α₄/2.
- The second-order critical line connecting TCP′ to point Q is α₂ = (3/8)(α₄+μI²)².
- The second-order critical line linking TCP′ to point R is α₂ = (5/36)(α₄+μI²)².
- Point Q is located at (α₂=3μI⁴/32, α₄=-3μI²/2) and classified as a Lifshitz bicritical point.
- Point R is located at (α₂=0.21μI⁴, α₄=-2.22μI²) and classified as a critical (end) point.
- For the homogeneous case with nonzero quark mass h, the CEP is located at (α₂=2.28h⁴/⁵, α₄=-2.25h²/⁵).

## Context
This work extends the Ginzburg-Landau analysis of the QCD phase diagram near the tricritical point, previously studied by Nickel (2009) who showed that the TCP is replaced by a Lifshitz point when inhomogeneous chiral condensates are considered. The authors incorporate isospin asymmetry, which had been studied in model-based approaches by Klein (2003) but not systematically within a GL framework. The results enable future investigation of how these pion condensed phases near TCP may connect to pion condensates in nuclear matter at zero temperature (Migdal, 1990) and to analogous phenomena in ultracold atomic gases (Maeda, 2012), as well as potential experimental signatures in heavy ion collisions and proto-neutron star cooling.
