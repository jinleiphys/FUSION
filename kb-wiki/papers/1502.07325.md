# Dimension-5 CP-odd operators: QCD mixing and renormalization

> We study the off-shell mixing and renormalization of flavor-diagonal dimension-5 T- and P-odd operators involving quarks, gluons, and photons, including quark electric dipole and chromo-electric dipole operators. We present the renormalization matrix to one-loop in the $\bar{\rm MS}$ scheme. We also provide a definition of the quark chromo-electric dipole operator in a regularization-independent momentum-subtraction scheme suitable for non-perturbative lattice calculations and present the matching coefficients with the $\bar{\rm MS}$ scheme to one-loop in perturbation theory, using both the naive dimensional regularization and 't Hooft-Veltman prescriptions for $γ_5$.

## Key claim

The paper studies the off-shell mixing and renormalization of flavor-diagonal dimension-5 T- and P-odd operators (quark electric dipole and chromo-electric dipole operators) at one-loop order in the MSbar scheme, but the abstract glosses over the fact that the vacuum alignment procedure, which eliminates pion tadpoles via chiral rotations, is required to define the physical CP-violating combinations, and that the results depend on whether the Peccei-Quinn mechanism is operative.

## Method

The authors constructed a basis of dimension-5 CP-odd operators (quark CEDM, quark EDM, and lower-dimensional operators they mix with) and computed the full one-loop renormalization matrix in the MSbar scheme using both naive dimensional regularization and the 't Hooft-Veltman prescription for gamma5. They then defined a regularization-independent momentum-subtraction (RI/MOM) scheme suitable for non-perturbative lattice calculations and computed the one-loop matching coefficients between the RI/MOM and MSbar schemes. The calculation used off-shell matrix elements with quarks and gluons as external states in a fixed gauge, with BRST symmetry constraints applied to restrict mixing with gauge-dependent operators.

## Key numbers

* The renormalization matrix for the dimension-5 CP-odd operator basis is presented to one-loop order in the MSbar scheme at O(alpha_s)
* The matching coefficients between the RI/MOM scheme and the MSbar scheme are computed to one-loop in perturbation theory at O(alpha_s)
* The vacuum-aligned effective Lagrangian involves the reduced quark mass m_* = (m_s m_d m_u) / (m_s (m_u + m_d) + m_u m_d), which appears in the shifted theta parameter and the CEDM operator combinations
* The induced theta parameter in the presence of the Peccei-Quinn mechanism is given by theta_ind = (r/2) Tr[M^{-1} [d_CE]], where r is the ratio of matrix elements (Delta^1_S - Delta^1_V)/(Delta^0_S - Delta^0_V)

## Context

This work extends the program of lattice QCD calculations of CP-violating hadronic matrix elements initiated by Bhattacharya et al. (2012, 2013, 2014) for nucleon EDMs and T-odd pion-nucleon couplings. It provides the necessary renormalization and matching framework to connect lattice-computed matrix elements of dimension-5 CP-odd operators to the MSbar scheme used in phenomenological analyses. The results are compared to related work by Constantinou et al. (2015) on the renormalization of the strangeness-changing chromo-magnetic quark operator. This enables systematically improvable lattice QCD calculations of nucleon EDMs induced by BSM CP-violating sources, which previously relied on model-dependent estimates from QCD sum rules (Pospelov 1999, 2000) and Dyson-Schwinger equations (Pitschmann 2012, 2014) that differ by up to an order of magnitude.
