# Non-flow effects in correlation between harmonic flow and transverse momentum in nuclear collisions

> A large anti-correlation signal between elliptic flow $v_2$ and average transverse momentum $[p_{\mathrm{T}}]$ was recently measured in small collision systems, consistent with a final-state hydrodynamic response to the initial geometry. This negative $v_2$-$[p_{\mathrm{T}}]$ correlation was predicted to change to positive correlation for events with very small charged particle multiplicity $N_{\mathrm{ch}}$ due to initial-state momentum anisotropies of the gluon saturation effects. However, the role of non-flow correlations is expected to be important in these systems, which is not yet studied. We estimate the non-flow effects in $pp$, $p$Pb and peripheral PbPb collisions using {\tt Pythia} and {\tt Hijing} models, and compare them with the experimental data. We show that the non-flow effects are largely suppressed using the rapidity-separated subevent cumulant method (details of the cumulant framework are also provided). The magnitude of the residual non-flow is much less than the experimental observation in the higher $N_{\mathrm{ch}}$ region, supporting the final-state response interpretation. In the very low $N_{\mathrm{ch}}$ region, however, the sign and magnitude of the residual non-flow depend on the model details. Therefore, it is unclear at this moment whether the sign change of $v_2$-$[p_{\mathrm{T}}]$ can serve as evidence for initial state momentum anisotropies predicted by the gluon saturation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the sign change of the $v_2$-$[p_{\mathrm{T}}]$ correlation at very low charged particle multiplicity, which was predicted as evidence for initial-state gluon saturation effects, cannot be unambiguously interpreted because the residual non-flow contributions in that region are model-dependent and can be either positive or negative depending on the event generator used.

## Method

The authors used the Pythia8 (A2 tune) and Hijing v1.37 event generators to simulate pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV, pPb and peripheral PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=5.02$ TeV, which contain only non-flow correlations from jets, dijets, and resonance decays but no genuine long-range collective flow. They calculated the covariance $\cov{n} \equiv \langle v_n^2 [p_{\mathrm{T}}] \rangle - \langle v_n^2 \rangle \langle [p_{\mathrm{T}}] \rangle$ and the Pearson correlation coefficient $\rho(v_n^2,[p_{\mathrm{T}}])$ using standard, two-subevent, and three-subevent cumulant methods with pseudorapidity gaps between subevents to suppress short-range correlations.

## Key numbers

* In the $0.2 < p_{\mathrm{T}} < 2$ GeV range and $\nch > 12$ region, the residual non-flow contribution to $|\rho(v_2^2,[p_{\mathrm{T}}])|$ is $< 0.02$ in pPb and PbPb collisions and $< 0.05$ in pp collisions.
* In the higher $p_{\mathrm{T}}$ range ($0.5 < p_{\mathrm{T}} < 2$ GeV) and $\nch > 20$ region, $|\rho(v_2^2,[p_{\mathrm{T}}])|$ from non-flow is $\lesssim 0.02$ in PbPb and $\lesssim 0.06$ in pPb collisions, but reaches $\sim 0.1$ to $0.2$ in pp collisions.
* The non-flow contribution to $\cov{2}$ in pPb collisions at $\nch \sim 20$ reaches 30-40% of the experimental values for $0.5 < p_{\mathrm{T}} < 2$ GeV.
* The values of $v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\}$ used for variance estimation are in the range 0.71-0.74 as a function of $\nch$, leading to a 28% reduction in $\var{2}$.

## Context

This work extends the study of $v_n$-$[p_{\mathrm{T}}]$ correlations proposed by Giacalone et al. (2020) as a discriminator between initial-state gluon saturation effects and final-state hydrodynamic response. The paper directly addresses the role of non-flow correlations, which were not studied in the original proposal. The results show that the sign change predicted by the CGC-hydro model for pPb collisions may be obscured by model-dependent non-flow contributions, and that the three-subevent method (Jia et al., 2017) suppresses but does not eliminate these effects. The findings complicate the interpretation of ATLAS data (Aad et al., 2019; ATLAS, 2021) and call for further quantitative model investigations before the sign change can serve as evidence for gluon saturation.
