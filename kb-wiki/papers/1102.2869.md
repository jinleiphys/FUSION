# Quark Matter In Massive Compact Stars

> The recent observation of the pulsar PSR J1614-2230 with a mass of 1.97 +/- 0.04 M_sun gives a strong constraint on the quark and nuclear matter equations of state (EoS). We explore the parameter ranges for a parameterized EoS for quark stars. We find that strange stars, made of absolutely stable strange quark matter, comply with the new constraint only if effects from the strong coupling constant and color-superconductivity are taken into account. Hybrid stars, compact stars with a quark matter core and an hadronic outer layer, can be as massive as 2 M_sun, but only for a significantly limited range of parameters. We demonstrate that the appearance of quark matter in massive stars depends crucially on the stiffness of the nuclear matter EoS. We show that the masses of hybrid stars stay below the ones of hadronic and pure quark stars, due to the softening of the EoS at the quark-hadron phase transition.

## Key claim

The new mass limit of PSR J1614-2230 (1.97 ± 0.04 M☉) does not rule out quark matter in compact stars, but it provides tight bounds on its properties; strange stars can comply only if effects from the strong coupling constant and color-superconductivity are included, and hybrid stars can reach 2 M☉ only for a significantly limited range of parameters.

## Method

The authors model quark matter with a modified bag model equation of state (EoS) that includes an effective bag constant B_eff, a parameter a_4 for first-order QCD corrections (strong coupling constant effects), and a pairing gap Δ for color-superconductivity in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase. For hybrid stars, they combine this quark EoS with two relativistic mean-field (RMF) hadronic EoSs (TM1 and NL3) and model the quark-hadron phase transition using both the Maxwell construction (large surface tension) and the Gibbs construction (negligible surface tension). They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain maximum masses and map the allowed parameter space against the constraints from the existence of atomic nuclei, absolute stability of strange quark matter, Keplerian rotation, and the mass of PSR J1614-2230.

## Key numbers

- PSR J1614-2230 mass: 1.97 ± 0.04 M☉ (Demorest 2010).
- For unpaired strange quark stars with a_4 = 1 (no QCD corrections), the 2-flavor line requires M_max ≲ 1.92 M☉, ruled out by the new mass limit within its 1σ error.
- The maximum allowed mass for unpaired strange stars in the allowed parameter region is 2.54 M☉ at a_4 ≈ 0.53 and B_eff^(1/4) ≈ 123.7 MeV.
- For color-superconducting strange stars with a_4 = 1.0, m_s = 100 MeV, and Δ = 100 MeV, the largest mass allowed is 2.34 M☉ at B_eff^(1/4) = 145 MeV.
- For hybrid stars with the TM1 hadronic EoS (maximum neutron star mass 2.2 M☉) and the NL3 hadronic EoS (maximum neutron star mass 2.78 M☉), hybrid star maximum masses experience a minimum around n_crit ~ 0.2 fm^(-3).
- Hybrid stars with a pure quark core and mass ≥ 1.93 M☉ are obtained only for a_4 ≲ 0.6.

## Context

This work extends the studies of Ozel (2010) and Lattimer (2010) on the implications of the PSR J1614-2230 mass measurement for quark and hybrid stars, but provides a more systematic analysis of the full parameter range of the quark bag model. Contrary to Lattimer (2010), the authors find that a quark-hadron mixed phase plays a major role in supporting high-mass hybrid stars and that pairing (color-superconductivity) helps increase the maximum mass. The results enable future comparisons with other effective QCD models such as Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) and PNJL models, and motivate further observational constraints from cooling, r-modes, gravitational wave signals, and heavy-ion collision experiments.
