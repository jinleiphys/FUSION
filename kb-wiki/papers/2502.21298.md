# The quantum Newton's bucket: Active and passive rotations in quantum theory

> Motivated both by classical physics problems associated with ``Newton's bucket'' and recent developments related to QCD in rotating frames of reference relevant to heavy ion collisions, we discuss the difference between ``active'' and ``passive'' rotations in quantum systems. We examine some relevant potentials and give general symmetry arguments to give criteria where such rotations give the same results. We close with a discussion of how this can be translated to quantum field theory.

## Key claim

The paper argues that active rotations (where the Hamiltonian includes a rotation term) and passive rotations (where the detector rotates around a stationary system) are equivalent in quantum theory only when all generators of the rotation group commute with the Hamiltonian, a condition that is inherently quantum and related to the uncertainty principle; this equivalence fails for systems with cylindrical symmetry even when they would be equivalent classically.

## Method

The authors compare active and passive rotations by computing the time evolution of the density matrix for several quantum systems: a particle in a Coulombic potential (spherical symmetry), a particle in a position-varying magnetic field in two dimensions, a particle in rotating cylindrical wells, and a Coulombic potential in a 2D rotating cylindrical well. For each system, they derive the density matrix under active rotation (adding a term -ω·J to the Hamiltonian) and under passive rotation (applying a rotation operator U(t) to the density matrix), then compare the resulting expressions to determine equivalence. They also discuss general symmetry arguments using group theory and the Wigner-Eckart theorem, and extend the discussion to quantum field theory and linearized general relativity.

## Key numbers

- For the Coulombic potential in spherical symmetry, the energy eigenvalues are given by H_{M_i} = -α²m/(2ħ²n²) - ħω_i M_i, and the active and passive rotations produce density matrices differing only by a sign in the exponential, showing equivalence.
- For the position-varying magnetic field B(r) = (Ω₁ + Ω₂/r + Ω₃/r²)ẑ, the active rotation energy eigenvalues contain terms depending on quantum numbers n and m_s in a way that cannot be reproduced by a passive rotation, leading to non-equivalence.
- For a rotating cylindrical well of radius R with constant depth U₀, the slow rotation (R < 1/Ω) energy eigenvalues are E_{Mnk_z} = -y²_{Mn}/(2mR²) - Mλ/R + k_z²/(2m), and the rapid rotation (R > 1/ω) eigenvalues are E_{Mak_z} = (ω²x²_{Ma} + k_z²)/(2m) - U₀ - Mω; both cases yield density matrices different from the passive rotation case, showing non-equivalence.
- For the Coulombic potential in a cylindrical well, the energy eigenvalues are E_{n',M} = -α²m/(2[n'(ω)-1/2]²) - Mω, with n' depending on ω, again leading to non-equivalence with passive rotation.

## Context

This work extends the classical "Newton's bucket" thought experiment into quantum theory, motivated by recent studies of QCD in rotating reference frames relevant to heavy ion collisions (Lisabec, Voloshin, Qun). The authors build on the rigid body approximation used in works such as Buzzegoli (2023) and show that this approximation fails in quantum systems where commutators between generators are non-negligible. The paper connects to the Bohr-Van Leeuwen theorem and to the work of Feynman on linearized general relativity, showing that the equivalence principle ensures active and passive rotations coincide in gravity. The results enable future investigation of whether active and passive rotations are equivalent in full quantum field theory and quantum gravity, particularly for systems away from global equilibrium.
