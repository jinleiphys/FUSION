# Fusion at deep subbarrier energies: potential inversion revisited

> For a single potential barrier, the barrier penetrability can be inverted based on the WKB approximation to yield the barrier thickness. We apply this method to heavy-ion fusion reactions at energies well below the Coulomb barrier and directly determine the inter-nucleus potential between the colliding nuclei. To this end, we assume that fusion cross sections at deep subbarrier energies are governed by the lowest barrier in the barrier distribution. The inverted inter-nucleus potentials for the $^{16}$O +$^{144}$Sm and $^{16}$O +$^{208}$Pb reactions show that they are much thicker than phenomenological potentials. We discuss a consequence of such thick potential by fitting the inverted potentials with the Bass function.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the inter-nucleus potential for heavy-ion fusion reactions at deep subbarrier energies is much thicker than phenomenological Woods-Saxon potentials, but only under the assumption that fusion cross sections at those energies are governed solely by the lowest adiabatic barrier in the barrier distribution.

## Method

The authors applied a WKB-based potential inversion formula to experimental fusion cross sections for the 16O+144Sm and 16O+208Pb systems, extracting the barrier thickness from the penetrability. They assumed that at deep subbarrier energies only the lowest eigen-barrier contributes, corrected for its weight factor, and estimated the outer turning point using a Woods-Saxon potential with parameters adjusted to reproduce the barrier height from the barrier distribution peak. The inverted potentials were then fitted with the Bass function to test consistency with coupled-channels calculations.

## Key numbers

- For the 16O+208Pb system, the deviation of the inverted potential from the phenomenological Woods-Saxon potential starts at around E = 70.4 MeV.
- The Bass function parameters used were A = 0.05 MeV^-1 (modified from the original 0.03 MeV^-1), B = 0.0061 MeV^-1, d1 = 3.3 fm, and d2 = 0.65 fm.
- The Woods-Saxon potential with surface diffuseness a = 1.0 fm has a thickness similar to the Bass potential for the 16O+208Pb system, compared to a = 0.65 fm for the standard Woods-Saxon potential.

## Context

This work extends the earlier potential inversion analysis by Balantekin et al. (1983), which yielded unphysical multi-valued potentials because it neglected channel coupling effects. The present study explicitly accounts for the barrier distribution arising from channel coupling, applying inversion only to the lowest eigen-barrier. The results are consistent with the steep fall-off of fusion cross sections observed by Jiang et al. and Dasgupta et al. (2006), and suggest that the deviation from Woods-Saxon shape may be related to the one-body adiabatic potential energy surface, as considered by Ichikawa et al. (2007) using the Krappe-Nix-Sierk potential. The paper enables future work to distinguish whether the thick potential is intrinsic or mocks up dynamical effects such as energy and angular momentum dissipation.
