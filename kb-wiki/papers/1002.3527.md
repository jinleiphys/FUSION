# 13th International Conference on Elastic and Diffractive Scattering (Blois Workshop) - Moving Forward into the LHC Era

> Proceedings of the 13th International Conference on Elastic and Diffractive Scattering (Blois Workshop) - Moving Forward into the LHC Era

## Key claim

This is a conference proceedings volume, not a single research paper; it collects 70 talks from the 13th International Conference on Elastic and Diffractive Scattering (Blois Workshop) held at CERN in 2009, with the stated aim of moving forward into the LHC era by reviewing the status of QCD studies in hadronic interactions at high energy.

## Method

The proceedings compile write-ups of most of the 70 talks presented across eight sessions convened by experts, covering: total proton-proton cross section; elastic pp scattering; inelastic diffractive scattering in electron-proton, pp and heavy-ion collisions; central exclusive production; photon-induced processes; forward physics and low-x QCD; and cosmic-ray physics and related studies at the LHC. The volume includes individual contributions from experimental collaborations (CDF, ATLAS, CMS, LHCb, ALICE, TOTEM, H1, ZEUS, HERMES, PHENIX, STAR, Belle, BaBar) and theoretical groups.

## Key numbers

Review-type paper: no original numerics.

## Context

This proceedings volume extends the series of Blois Workshops on elastic and diffractive scattering that began in 1985, following the previous edition EDS'07 at DESY. It provides a snapshot of the field just before the LHC began operations, enabling future comparisons between LHC data and predictions from models of total cross sections, diffraction, and forward physics. The volume includes a panel discussion on what can be learned from LHC experiments and a summary session covering theoretical aspects (Soffer) and experimental highlights (Dainton).
