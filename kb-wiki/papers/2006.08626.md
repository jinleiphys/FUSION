# Forward doubly-virtual Compton scattering off the nucleon in chiral perturbation theory: II. Spin polarizabilities and moments of polarized structure functions

> We examine the polarized doubly-virtual Compton scattering (VVCS) off the nucleon using chiral perturbation theory ($χ$PT). The polarized VVCS contains a wealth of information on the spin structure of the nucleon which is relevant to the calculation of the two-photon-exchange effects in atomic spectroscopy and electron scattering. We report on a complete next-to-leading-order (NLO) calculation of the polarized VVCS amplitudes $S_1(ν, Q^2)$ and $S_2(ν, Q^2)$, and the corresponding polarized spin structure functions $g_1(x, Q^2)$ and $g_2(x,Q^2)$. Our results for the moments of polarized structure functions, partially related to different spin polarizabilities, are compared to other theoretical predictions and "data-driven" evaluations, as well as to the recent Jefferson Lab measurements. By expanding the results in powers of the inverse nucleon mass, we reproduce the known "heavy-baryon" expressions. This serves as a check of our calculation, as well as demonstrates the differences between the manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon $χ$PT (B$χ$PT) and heavy-baryon (HB$χ$PT) frameworks.

## Key claim

The paper presents a complete next-to-leading-order (NLO) calculation of polarized doubly-virtual Compton scattering (VVCS) off the nucleon using manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (BχPT), finding that the inclusion of a dipole form factor for the magnetic γ*NΔ transition is essential to reproduce the Q² dependence of the forward spin polarizability γ₀(Q²), and that the results differ significantly from previous covariant BχPT calculations due to differences in power counting and renormalization of πΔ-loop contributions.

## Method

The authors computed the polarized VVCS amplitudes S₁(ν,Q²) and S₂(ν,Q²) and the corresponding spin structure functions g₁(x,Q²) and g₂(x,Q²) at NLO in BχPT using the δ-counting scheme, including πN loops, Δ-exchange with Coulomb-quadrupole (C2) coupling, and πΔ loops. The non-Born amplitudes were expanded at low energies to extract generalized spin polarizabilities γ₀(Q²) and δLT(Q²), and the results were compared to experimental data from Jefferson Lab, the MAID model, and previous chiral perturbation theory calculations including heavy-baryon (HBχPT) and infrared regularization (IR) approaches.

## Key numbers

* Proton forward spin polarizability at Q²=0: γ₀p = -0.93(92) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴, composed of πN-loop contribution 2.01, Δ-exchange -2.84, and πΔ-loop -0.10 (all in units of 10⁻⁴ fm⁴)
* Neutron forward spin polarizability at Q²=0: γ₀n = 0.03(92) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴, composed of πN-loop contribution 2.98, Δ-exchange -2.84, and πΔ-loop -0.10 (all in units of 10⁻⁴ fm⁴)
* Slope of proton forward spin polarizability at Q²=0: dγ₀p/dQ²|Q²=0 = -0.22(4) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁶, with πN-loop -0.33, Δ-exchange +0.11, πΔ-loop +0.01
* Slope of neutron forward spin polarizability at Q²=0: dγ₀n/dQ²|Q²=0 = -0.61(7) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁶, with πN-loop -0.73, Δ-exchange +0.11, πΔ-loop +0.01
* GDH collaboration experimental value for proton: γ₀p = -1.00(8)(12) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴ at the real-photon point
* Dipole form factor cutoff parameter for magnetic coupling: Λ² = 0.71 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous NLO BχPT calculation of unpolarized VVCS (Alarcon 2020) to the polarized case, improving upon their earlier calculation (Lensky 2014) by including the Coulomb-quadrupole C2 N→Δ transition, correct elastic form-factor contributions, and improved error estimates accounting for cancellations between chiral orders. The results differ significantly from the covariant BχPT calculation of Bernard (2012), which used small-scale expansion rather than δ-counting and did not include the dipole form factor for the magnetic γ*NΔ coupling. The calculation reproduces the heavy-baryon χPT results of Kao (2002, 2003) when expanded in inverse nucleon mass, but shows that HBχPT fails to describe the Q² dependence of polarizabilities. The results are relevant for two-photon exchange corrections to hyperfine structure in hydrogen and muonic hydrogen spectroscopy.
