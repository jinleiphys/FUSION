# Kaon Thresholds and Two-Flavor Chiral Expansions for Hyperons

> Two-flavor chiral expansions provide a useful perturbative framework to study hadron properties. Such expansions should exhibit marked improvement over the conventional three-flavor chiral expansion. Although one can theoretically formulate two-flavor theories for the various hyperon multiplets, the nearness of kaon thresholds can seriously undermine the effectiveness of the perturbative expansion in practice. We investigate the importance of virtual kaon thresholds on hyperon properties, specifically their masses and isovector axial charges. Using a three-flavor expansion that includes SU(3) breaking effects, we uncover the underlying expansion parameter governing the description of virtual kaon thresholds. For spin-half hyperons, this expansion parameter is quite small. Consequently virtual kaon contributions are well described in the two-flavor theory by terms analytic in the pion mass-squared. For spin three-half hyperons, however, one is closer to the kaon production threshold, and the expansion parameter is not as small. Breakdown of SU(2) chiral perturbation theory is shown to arise from a pole in the expansion parameter associated with the kaon threshold. Estimating higher-order corrections to the expansion parameter is necessary to ascertain whether the two-flavor theory of spin three-half hyperons remains perturbative. We find that, despite higher-order corrections, there is a useful perturbative expansion for the masses and isovector axial charges of both spin-half and spin three-half hyperons.

# Kaon Thresholds and Two-Flavor Chiral Expansions for Hyperons

## Key claim

Two-flavor chiral perturbation theory provides a useful perturbative framework for hyperon masses and isovector axial charges, but only because the expansion parameter governing virtual kaon threshold effects is small for spin-half hyperons and remains manageable for spin three-half hyperons despite the nearness of kaon production thresholds; the breakdown of SU(2) chiral perturbation theory arises from a pole in the expansion parameter associated with the kaon threshold, yet higher-order corrections still permit a useful perturbative expansion for both spin-half and spin three-half hyperons.

## Method

The authors used SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory including SU(3) breaking effects to analyze kaon loop contributions (sunset diagrams) to hyperon masses and isovector axial charges. They expanded the kaon mass about the SU(2) chiral limit and derived the expansion parameter epsilon_{BB'} = (1/2 C m_pi^2) / ([m_K^{SU(2)}]^2 - delta_{BB'}^2) that governs the description of virtual kaon thresholds in the two-flavor theory. They then compared the full non-analytic functions for sunset diagrams with successive approximations analytic in the pion mass-squared to test convergence.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of strange to average light quark mass is m_s / m_hat ~ 25, motivating the SU(2) expansion with epsilon_{SU(2)} = m_hat / m_s ~ 1/25.
- The kaon mass in the SU(2) chiral limit is m_K^{SU(2)} = 0.486(5) GeV, estimated using the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation.
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale is Lambda_chi ~ 1.1 GeV.
- Positive Delta S = -1 baryon mass splittings (in GeV): delta_{N Sigma*} = 0.45, delta_{Lambda Xi*} = 0.42, delta_{Xi Omega} = 0.36, delta_{Sigma Xi*} = 0.34, delta_{N Sigma} = 0.25, delta_{Lambda Xi} = 0.20, delta_{N Lambda} = 0.18, delta_{Delta Sigma*} = 0.15, delta_{Sigma* Xi*} = 0.15, delta_{Xi* Omega} = 0.14, delta_{Sigma Xi} = 0.12.
- Positive Delta S = 1 baryon mass splittings (in GeV): delta_{Lambda Delta} = 0.12, delta_{Xi Sigma*} = 0.07, delta_{Sigma Delta} = 0.04.
- The physical kaon mass is m_K = 0.50 GeV.
- Expansion parameters epsilon_{BB'} for Delta S = -1 transitions range from epsilon_{N Sigma*} = 0.24 down to epsilon_{Sigma Xi} = 0.04; for Delta S = 1 transitions all are epsilon = 0.04.
- The generic SU(3) expansion parameter is epsilon_{SU(3)} = m_eta / M^{SU(3)} ~ 0.5.
- For the Sigma* baryon, the SU(2) expansion of the axial charge sunset diagram becomes unreliable past m_pi ~ 0.3 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts to formulate two-flavor chiral perturbation theory for hyperons (Tiburzi 2008, Jiang 2009) by quantitatively assessing the impact of virtual kaon thresholds. It builds on the SU(2) treatment of pions (Gasser 1983), nucleons (Bernard 1995), and deltas (Hemmert 1997), as well as recent SU(2) studies of kaon properties (Roessl 1999, Allton 2008, Flynn 2008, Bijnens 2009). The results justify the SU(2) approach for hyperon masses and axial charges used in lattice QCD applications, where interpolation in the strange quark mass is now possible. The work enables future lattice QCD studies of hyperon resonance properties and clarifies that the efficacy of the two-flavor expansion is likely observable-dependent, requiring further investigation for other low-energy hyperon observables.
