# Alpha clustering in $^{28}$Si probed through the identification of high-lying $0^+$ states

> Aspects of nuclear structure in alpha-conjugate nuclei have long been associated with alpha clustering, including the existence of superdeformed bands. In this paper, an alpha-particle inelastic scattering experiment investigating the location of $0^+$ states in $^{28}$Si is reported in an attempt to locate possible cluster states. The results are compared to a semi-microscopic model which shows good agreement with the data, and supports the assignment of a newly discovered $0^+$ state as the band-head of a previously observed superdeformed band in $^{28}$Si

## Key claim

The paper reports the identification of a new 0+ state at 9.71 MeV in 28Si, which is a strong candidate for the band-head of a previously observed superdeformed band, though the authors note that experimental confirmation of this assignment is not possible in this experiment and the state lies about 400 keV higher than a simple linear extrapolation from known band members would predict.

## Method

Alpha-particle inelastic scattering at 200 MeV was performed on a natSi target at the iThemba Laboratory using the K600 magnetic spectrometer, covering scattering angles from 0 degrees to 6 degrees. Scattered particles were momentum-analysed and detected using multiwire drift chambers and plastic scintillators, with excitation energies from 6 to 14 MeV studied. Differential cross sections were extracted and compared to DWBA calculations using a folding-model optical potential with adjusted radial parameters, and the results were compared to predictions from the semi-microscopic algebraic quartet model using the multichannel dynamical symmetry (MUSY).

## Key numbers

- New 0+ state identified at 9.71(2) MeV excitation energy in 28Si, with differential cross section 2.42(5) mb/sr at theta_lab < 2 degrees
- 0+ state at 10.81(3) MeV with differential cross section 2.23(4) mb/sr at theta_lab < 2 degrees
- 0+ state at 11.14(2) MeV with differential cross section 5.79(7) mb/sr at theta_lab < 2 degrees
- Unresolved 0+ strength at 12.99(2) MeV with differential cross section 4.51(11) mb/sr at theta_lab < 2 degrees
- Energy resolution of 80 keV FWHM for the 0-degree experiment and 65 keV FWHM for the small-angle experiment
- The 9.71-MeV state is approximately 400 keV higher than expected from linear extrapolation of the superdeformed band
- The MUSY Hamiltonian parameters: a = -0.133 MeV, b = 0.000444 MeV, d = 1.003 MeV, with hbar-omega = 12.11 MeV from systematics

## Context

This work extends previous studies of superdeformed bands in alpha-conjugate nuclei such as 40Ca (PhysRevLett.87.222501, 2001) and 36Ar (PhysRevLett.85.2693, 2000) to the lighter system 28Si, where superdeformed bands had been suggested but the 0+ band-head was unknown. The results are compared to the semi-microscopic algebraic quartet model with multichannel dynamical symmetry (MUSY) developed by Cseh (2016), which predicts six 0+ states in the excitation region studied, in good agreement with the four distinct states plus unresolved strength observed. The identification of the 9.71-MeV state as the superdeformed band-head enables future studies of the full rotational band and tests of cluster configurations such as 24Mg+alpha, 12C+16O, and 20Ne+2alpha predicted by Kimura and others.
