# Relaxation of the chiral imbalance in dense matter of a neutron star

> Using the quantum field theory methods, we calculate the helicity flip of an electron scattering off protons in dense matter of a neutron star. The influence of the electroweak interaction between electrons and background nucleons on the helicity flip is examined. We also derive the kinetic equation for the chiral imbalance. The derived kinetic equation is compared with the results obtained by other authors.

## Key claim

The helicity flip rate in electron-proton collisions within neutron star matter is proportional to the chiral imbalance \(\mu_5\) itself, not to \(\mu_5 + V_5\) (where \(V_5\) is the electroweak potential difference between left and right electrons), contrary to a recent claim by Sigl and Leite (2016). This result holds under the approximations of elastic scattering and ultrarelativistic, highly degenerate electrons.

## Method

The total probability for an electron to flip its helicity in an electron-proton (\(ep\)) collision was computed using quantum field theory, starting from the matrix element for electromagnetic \(ep\) scattering with polarized electrons and unpolarized protons. The calculation was performed both for vacuum electron spinors and for exact solutions of the Dirac equation that include the electroweak interaction of electrons with background nucleons (neutrons and protons). The phase-space integrals were evaluated assuming highly degenerate, ultrarelativistic electrons and nonrelativistic, degenerate protons at small nonzero temperature, with the plasma frequency used to regularize infrared divergences. The resulting total probability was then used to derive a kinetic equation for the chiral imbalance \(\mu_5 = (\mu_R - \mu_L)/2\).

## Key numbers

- The helicity flip rate coefficient is \(\Gamma_f = \frac{\alpha_{\mathrm{em}}^2}{\pi} \left[ \ln\left( \frac{48\pi}{\alpha_{\mathrm{em}}} \right) - 4 \right] \left( \frac{m}{\mu_e} \right)^2 \left( \frac{M}{\mu_e} \right) T\), where \(\alpha_{\mathrm{em}}\) is the fine structure constant, \(m\) and \(M\) are the electron and proton masses, \(\mu_e\) is the mean electron chemical potential, and \(T\) is the temperature.
- The Coulomb cut-off parameter \(L^2 = 3\pi/\alpha_{\mathrm{em}} \approx 1291 \gg 1\), leading to the logarithmic factor \(\ln(16 L^2) - 4\) in the total probability.
- The minimal scattering angle for the helicity flip process is \(\chi_\mathrm{min} \sim \alpha_{\mathrm{em}}/48\pi = 4.8 \times 10^{-5}\), and the angle at which inelastic effects become comparable to the electroweak contribution is \(\chi_5 = 3.2 \times 10^{-4}\), satisfying \(\chi_5 \gg \chi_\mathrm{min}\).
- The mean electron energy in the neutron star is \(\langle E \rangle \sim 10^2\ \text{MeV}\), and the proton Fermi momentum is \(p_{\mathrm{F}_p} \sim 10^2\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work completes the quantum field theory foundation for the model of magnetic field generation in magnetars originally proposed by Dvornikov and Semikoz (2015a, 2015b, 2015c), which relied on the parity-violating electron-nucleon electroweak interaction. The present calculation corrects the earlier estimate of the helicity flip rate \(\Gamma_f\) from those papers, which had been based on classical plasma physics and on the results of Kelly (1973) for unpolarized electrons, yielding a different temperature dependence. The result disagrees with the claim of Sigl and Leite (2016) that the kinetic equation for the chiral imbalance should contain \(\mu_5 + V_5\) rather than \(\mu_5\) alone. The derived \(\Gamma_f\) is linear in temperature \(T\) (for degenerate protons), in contrast to the temperature-independent result of Grabowska, Kaplan, and Reddy (2015) who assumed nondegenerate protons, which is appropriate for the early stages of neutron star evolution but not for the thermally relaxed stars (\(t \gtrsim 10^2\ \text{yr}\)) considered here.
