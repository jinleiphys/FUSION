# Asymmetric nuclear matter and neutron-skin in extended relativistic mean field model

> The density dependence of the symmetry energy, instrumental in understanding the behaviour of the asymmetric nuclear matter, is investigated within the extended relativistic mean field (ERMF) model which includes the contributions from the self and mixed interaction terms for the scalar-isoscalar ($σ$), vector-isoscalar ($ω$) and vector-isovector ($ρ$) mesons upto the quartic order. Each of the 26 different parameterizations of the ERMF model employed are compatible with the bulk properties of the finite nuclei. The behaviour of the symmetry energy for several parameter sets are found to be consistent with the empirical constraints on them as extracted from the analyses of the isospin diffusion data. The neutron-skin thickness in the $^{208}$Pb nucleus for these parameter sets of the ERMF model lie in the range of $\sim 0.20 - 0.24$ fm which is in harmony with the ones predicted by the Skyrme Hartree-Fock model. We also investigate the role of various mixed interaction terms which are crucial for the density dependence of the symmetry energy.

# Asymmetric nuclear matter and neutron-skin in extended relativistic mean field model

## Key claim

The density dependence of the symmetry energy for several parameter sets of the extended relativistic mean field (ERMF) model is consistent with empirical constraints from isospin diffusion data, but only for parameter sets that yield a neutron-skin thickness in 208Pb of approximately 0.20–0.24 fm; the paper does not claim that all 26 parameterizations satisfy the constraints, only that a subset does.

## Method

The authors employed 26 different parameterizations of the ERMF model, which includes self and mixed interaction terms for the σ, ω, and ρ mesons up to quartic order. Each parameterization was fitted to experimental binding energies and charge radii of closed-shell finite nuclei. The symmetry energy, its slope L, curvature K_asy, and the incompressibility coefficients K_0 and K_sat,2 were computed at saturation density and compared against empirical constraints derived from isospin diffusion data analyses. The role of cubic and quartic order mixed interaction terms involving the ρ meson was investigated by generating two families of interactions (F1 and F2) with different combinations of coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-skin thickness ΔR in 208Pb for the 21 BSR parameter sets ranges from 0.16 to 0.28 fm.
- The ERMF model predicts ΔR ≈ 0.20–0.24 fm as consistent with empirical constraints from isospin diffusion data.
- Empirical constraints used: E_sym = 30 ± 5 MeV, L = 88 ± 25 MeV, K_asy = −500 ± 50 MeV, K_0 = 240 ± 20 MeV, K_sat,2 = −370 ± 120 MeV.
- Only five parameter sets (BSR4, BSR11, BSR12, BSR18, BSR19) with ΔR = 0.22–0.24 fm obey all empirical constraints simultaneously.
- RMS errors on total binding energy for BSR parameter sets are 1.5–1.8 MeV; RMS errors on charge radii are 0.025–0.04 fm.
- For the F1 family with ΔR = 0.22 fm and η_ρ = η_1ρ = η_2ρ = 0, RMS errors increase to δB = 4.8 MeV and δr_ch = 0.05 fm.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of symmetry energy density dependence using Skyrme Hartree-Fock models (Chen 2005) and conventional RMF models (Chen 2007), which found that only a few parameter sets satisfied empirical constraints from isospin diffusion data. It directly addresses the inadequacy found in earlier ERMF studies (Sulaksono 2009), where parameter sets yielded either too low or too high neutron-skin thickness. The results enable more reliable predictions for neutron-skin thickness in 208Pb and constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy at subnormal densities, while noting that quartic order mixed interaction terms may be important for behavior at supranormal densities, which remains largely unknown.
