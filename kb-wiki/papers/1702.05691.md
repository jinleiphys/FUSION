# Oscillation Modes of Strange Quark Stars with a Strangelet Crust

> We study the non-radial oscillation modes of strange quark stars with a homogeneous core and a crust made of strangelets. Using a 2-component equation of state (core+crust) for strange quark stars that can produce stars as heavy as 2 solar masses, we identify the high-frequency l=2 spheroidal (f, p) in Newtonian gravity, using the Cowling approximation. The results are compared to the case of homogeneous compact stars such as polytropic neutron stars, as well as bare strange stars. We find that the strangelet crust only increases very slightly the frequency of the spheroidal modes, and that Newtonian gravity overestimates the mode frequencies of the strange star, as is the case for neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper finds that adding a strangelet crust to a strange quark star increases the frequency of spheroidal oscillation modes only very slightly (a few Hz for the f-mode, about 2% for the p-mode), and that the Newtonian approximation used in the calculation overestimates these mode frequencies compared to a full general relativistic treatment.

## Method

The authors model a strange quark star with a homogeneous core described by a thermodynamic Bag model equation of state (EOS) with O(m_s^4) corrections and a crust made of a globally neutral mixed phase of strangelets and electrons. They solve the non-radial oscillation equations in Newtonian gravity using the Cowling approximation (neglecting perturbations in the gravitational potential) for l=2 spheroidal (f and p) modes, connecting the crust and core eigenfunctions through continuity conditions at the crust-core interface.

## Key numbers

- f-mode frequencies for homogeneous strange quark stars lie in the 4-5 kHz range for masses M ~ (1.2-2) solar masses
- p1-mode frequencies for homogeneous strange quark stars lie in the 25-35 kHz range for masses M ~ (1.2-2) solar masses
- For a 2-component strange star with a strangelet crust, the crust thickness is on the order of 10^4 cm (no more than 1% of the stellar radius)
- The strangelet crust modifies the p-mode frequency by about 2% relative to the bare strange star case
- For a polytropic neutron star model with M=1.4 solar masses and R=10 km, the f-mode frequencies range from 3.59 kHz (n=1.0 polytrope) to 5.11 kHz (n=2.0 polytrope)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of oscillation modes in compact stars, particularly the work of Lugones and Vasquez-Flores (2013) who studied spheroidal modes for hadronic, self-bound, and hybrid matter stars using general relativistic fluid equations. The key difference is that this paper considers a heterogeneous crust made of strangelets (following the Gibbs construction of Jaikumar (2005) and Alford (2006)) rather than homogeneous quark phases or mixed phases of quarks and hadronic matter. The results enable future comparisons with full general relativistic calculations (currently underway) that will yield damping times and mode amplitudes for generating gravitational wave signal templates from pulsating strange stars.
