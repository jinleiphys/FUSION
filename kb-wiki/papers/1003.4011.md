# Symmetry energy in the structure and in reactions

> Efforts to extract information on magnitude and density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy are discussed. The utilized data include those on mass dependence of the excitation energies to the isobaric analog states of ground states, as well as data on the diffusion of isospin in heavy-ion reactions. Results following from different observables are compared.

# Symmetry energy in the structure and in reactions

## Key claim

The paper argues that the nuclear symmetry energy and its density dependence can be extracted from both nuclear structure data (excitation energies to isobaric analog states) and heavy-ion reaction data (isospin diffusion), but that results from different observables do not yet show consistency; systematic errors in each methodology must be understood before convergence can be achieved.

## Method

The author analyzes the mass dependence of symmetry coefficients derived from excitation energies to isobaric analog states (IAS) of ground states, using shell corrections from Koura et al. (2005) and IAS data from Antony et al. (1997). The inverse symmetry coefficient is fitted as a function of A^{-1/3} to extract volume and surface symmetry coefficients, which are then correlated to the slope parameter L of the symmetry energy. Additionally, transport calculations using the ImQMD model are compared to data on isospin equilibration from Sn + Sn collisions at 50 MeV/nucleon from Liu et al. (2007), using a parameterized form of the symmetry energy S(rho) = 12.3 MeV (rho/rho_0)^{2/3} + 17.6 MeV (rho/rho_0)^{gamma_i}.

## Key numbers

- Volume symmetry coefficient from IAS analysis: a_a^V = (31.5-33.5) MeV
- Surface symmetry coefficient from IAS analysis: a_a^S = (9.5-12) MeV
- Slope parameter L estimated from IAS analysis: L ~ 95 MeV
- Symmetry coefficient values drop to ~10 MeV at low mass number A and rise to ~23 MeV in heavy nuclei
- In transport calculations for Sn + Sn collisions at 50 MeV/nucleon, chi^2 minimization yields gamma_i ~ 0.7 for the density dependence of symmetry energy S(rho) = 12.3 MeV (rho/rho_0)^{2/3} + 17.6 MeV (rho/rho_0)^{gamma_i}

## Context

This work extends efforts to constrain the nuclear symmetry energy using both structure and reaction observables, building on prior work by Danielewicz (2003) on Thomas-Fermi calculations and by Danielewicz (2009) on correlations between symmetry energy parameters. The paper compares results from IAS analysis with those from heavy-ion reactions (Tsang et al., 2009) and collective excitations, finding that while some convergence exists, consistency has not yet been achieved. The analysis enables future work by identifying the need to filter Skyrme interactions that exhibit unphysical features (such as long-wavelength instabilities or excessive nonlocality) before reliable curvature corrections and firmer conclusions on a_a^V, a_a^S, and L can be established.
