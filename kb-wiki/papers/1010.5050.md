# Relativistic mean field study of the properties of Z=117 nucleus and the decay chains of $^{293,294}$117 isotopes

> We have calculated the binding energy, root-mean-square radius and quadrupole deformation parameter for the recently synthesized superheavy element Z=117, using the axially deformed relativistic mean field (RMF) model. The calculation is extended to various isotopes of Z=117 element, strarting from A=286 till A=310. We predict almost spherical structures in the ground state for almost all the isotopes. A shape transition appears at about A=292 from prolate to a oblate shape structures of Z=117 nucleus in our mean field approach. The most stable isotope (largest binding energy per nucleon) is found to be the $^{288}$117 nucleus. Also, the Q-value of $α$-decay $Q_α$ and the half-lives $T_α$ are calculated for the $α$-decay chains of $^{293}$117 and $^{294}$117, supporting the magic numbers at N=172 and/ or 184.

# Relativistic mean field study of the properties of Z=117 nucleus and the decay chains of 293,294117 isotopes

## Key claim

The axially deformed relativistic mean field (RMF) model with the NL3 parameter set predicts that the most stable isotope of element Z=117 is 288117 (with N=171), and that a shape transition from prolate to oblate occurs at approximately A=292, but the authors note that the ground state and first excited state are nearly degenerate for many isotopes, meaning the ground state assignment could change with small variations in input parameters such as pairing strength.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic mean field equations by expanding Dirac spinors and boson fields in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis with an initial deformation β0, using the NL3 parameter set and constant-gap BCS pairing. Binding energies, quadrupole deformation parameters β2, root-mean-square matter and charge radii, and two-neutron separation energies were calculated for the isotopic chain 286-310117. The Qα values for α-decay were obtained from the binding energy difference between parent and daughter nuclei, and half-lives Tα were estimated using the Viola-Seaborg phenomenological formula.

## Key numbers

- The most stable isotope (largest binding energy per nucleon) is 288117, with a total binding energy of 2052.586 MeV from RMF(NL3), compared to 2047.09 MeV from the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM).
- For 288117, the quadrupole deformation parameter β2 = 0.018 (nearly spherical) from RMF(NL3), while FRDM gives β2 = 0.080.
- The shape transition from prolate to oblate occurs at A=292 in RMF(NL3), where β2 = -0.017, while FRDM gives β2 = 0.072 (prolate) for the same nucleus.
- For the α-decay chain of 293117, the RMF(NL3) Qα value for the parent 293117 is 11.480 MeV with log10 Tα(s) = -2.71, compared to the experimental Qα = 11.03 MeV and log10 Tα(s) = -1.60.
- For the α-decay chain of 294117, the RMF(NL3) Qα value for the parent 294117 is 10.763 MeV with log10 Tα(s) = -0.906, compared to the experimental Qα = 10.81 MeV and log10 Tα(s) = -1.03.
- The two-neutron separation energy S2n for 288117 is 14.836 MeV from RMF(NL3) and 15.16 MeV from FRDM.
- The pairing energy Epair decreases by approximately 15% from A=286 to A=310, from 14.698 MeV for 288117 to 11.538 MeV for 310117.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean field studies of superheavy elements previously performed by Patra et al. (various years) and others using the NL3 parameter set, which reproduces properties of both stable and exotic nuclei. The calculations are motivated by the recent synthesis of Z=117 at Flerov Laboratory (Oganessian, 2010) via the reaction 48Ca + 249Bk → 297117, which decays into 293117 and 294117. The results support the predicted magic numbers at N=172 and/or 184, consistent with earlier predictions by Rutz et al. (1997), Gupta et al. (1997), and Patra et al. (various years). The comparison with Finite Range Droplet Model calculations by Moller et al. (1997) shows general agreement, though with some differences in deformation assignments. The work enables further experimental searches for the island of stability and provides predictions for α-decay chains that can be tested in future heavy-ion fusion experiments.
