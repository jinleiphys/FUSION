# Densities and energies of nuclei in dilute matter

> We explore the ground-state properties of nuclear clusters embedded in a gas of nucleons with the help of Skyrme-Hartree-Fock microscopic calculations. Two alternative representations of clusters are introduced, namely coordinate-space and energy-space clusters. We parameterize their density profiles in spherical symmetry in terms of basic properties of the energy density functionals used and propose an analytical, Woods-Saxon density profile whose parameters depend, not only on the composition of the cluster, but also of the nucleon gas. We study the clusters' energies with the help of the local-density approximation, validated through our microscopic results. We find that the volume energies of coordinate-space clusters are determined by the saturation properties of matter, while the surface energies are strongly affected by the presence of the gas. We conclude that both the density profiles and the cluster energies are strongly affected by the gas and discuss implications for the nuclear EoS and related perspectives. Our study provides a simple, but microscopically motivated modeling of the energetics of clusterized matter at subsaturation densities, for direct use in consequential applications of astrophysical interest.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that both the density profiles and energies of nuclear clusters embedded in a dilute nucleon gas are strongly modified by the gas, and that these in-medium effects can be captured by an analytical Woods-Saxon parameterization whose parameters depend on the gas properties as well as the cluster composition, with the caveat that the results are obtained within the Hartree-Fock approximation using the SLy4 Skyrme functional and neglecting pairing correlations.

## Method

The authors performed spherical Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations in Wigner-Seitz cells of radius 35 fm with mixed boundary conditions, studying isotopic chains from Z=20 (Ca) to Z=126 (Pb) with neutron numbers up to approximately 3000. They introduced two alternative representations of clusters: coordinate-space clusters (r-clusters), where the cluster and gas are separated in space, and energy-space clusters (e-clusters), where bound and resonant states define the cluster while the gas penetrates the cluster interior. The microscopic density profiles were fitted with Woods-Saxon functions, and the cluster energies were analyzed using the local-density approximation (LDA) validated against the microscopic results.

## Key numbers

* Wigner-Seitz cell radius of 35 fm was used for all microscopic calculations
* Gas densities up to approximately 0.02 fm^{-3} were studied for neutron-rich clusters
* The surface diffuseness of r-clusters is typically 1-2 fm
* The analytical model reproduces Woods-Saxon fit parameters with accuracy of roughly 5% or better
* Differences between treating neutron distributions independently versus as the difference of total and proton densities are less than 1%

## Context

This work extends earlier single-nucleus approximation (SNA) approaches to stellar matter equations of state, such as those by Lattimer and Swesty (1991) and Shen et al. (1998), by providing microscopically motivated in-medium modifications of cluster properties. It also goes beyond recent nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) implementations by Hempel et al. (2010), Raduta et al. (2010), and Gulminelli et al. (2012) that treat cluster-gas interactions via excluded-volume approaches with free-nucleus binding energies. The analytical model enables direct use of in-medium modified cluster self-energies in finite-temperature equation of state modeling for astrophysical applications, particularly for neutron star crust and supernova matter.
