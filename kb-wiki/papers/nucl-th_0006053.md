# Poincare' Covariant Current Operator and Elastic Electron-Deuteron Scattering in the Front-form Hamiltonian Dynamics

> The deuteron electromagnetic form factors, $A(Q^2)$ and $B(Q^2)$, and the tensor polarization $T_{20}(Q^2)$, are unambiguously calculated within the front-form relativistic Hamiltonian dynamics, by using a novel current, built up from one-body terms, which fulfills Poincaré, parity and time reversal covariance, together with Hermiticity and the continuity equation. A simultaneous description of the experimental data for the three deuteron form factors is achieved up to $Q^2 < 0.4 (GeV/c)^2$. At higher momentum transfer, different nucleon-nucleon interactions strongly affect $A(Q^2)$, $B(Q^2)$, and $T_{20}(Q^2)$ and the effects of the interactions can be related to $S$-state kinetic energy in the deuteron. Different nucleon form factor models have huge effects on $A(Q^2)$, smaller effects on $B(Q^2)$ and essentially none on $T_{20}(Q^2)$.

## Key claim

The authors unambiguously calculate the deuteron electromagnetic form factors \(A(Q^2)\), \(B(Q^2)\), and \(T_{20}(Q^2)\) within front-form Hamiltonian dynamics using a novel current operator built from one-body terms that satisfies Poincaré, parity, and time reversal covariance, Hermiticity, and current conservation; a simultaneous description of experimental data is achieved only up to \(Q^2 < 0.4\ (\text{GeV}/c)^2\), while at higher momentum transfer the results become strongly dependent on the choice of nucleon-nucleon interaction and nucleon form factor models.

## Method

The authors construct a Poincaré covariant current operator for the deuteron by starting from a free one-body current in a special Breit frame where the initial and final total three-momenta are directed along the spin quantization axis, then applying a Hermiticity-preserving symmetrization that introduces implicit two-body terms via the front-form spin operator. The deuteron internal wave function is obtained by solving a mass equation that reduces to a nonrelativistic Schrödinger equation in momentum representation, using standard nucleon-nucleon potentials (e.g., from the Nijmegen group). The three independent matrix elements of the current in this Breit frame are expressed in terms of the free current matrix elements, from which the charge, quadrupole, and magnetic form factors \(G_C\), \(G_Q\), and \(G_M\) are extracted, and then \(A(Q^2)\), \(B(Q^2)\), and \(T_{20}(Q^2)\) are computed.

## Key numbers

* The simultaneous description of experimental data for \(A(Q^2)\), \(B(Q^2)\), and \(T_{20}(Q^2)\) is achieved up to \(Q^2 < 0.4\ (\text{GeV}/c)^2\).
* At \(Q^2 = 0\), the theoretical deuteron magnetic moment \(\mu_d\) agrees with experiment to within \(0.5\%\) and the quadrupole moment \(Q_d\) to within \(2\%\), using interactions that reproduce the experimental asymptotic normalization ratio \(\eta = A_D/A_S\).
* The deuteron magnetic moment in nuclear magnetons is given by \(\mu_d = \frac{m_p}{(\sqrt{2} m_d)} \lim_{Q \rightarrow 0} \frac{1}{Q} [\mathcal{J}^x_{1,0} - \mathcal{J}^x_{0,1}]\).
* The deuteron quadrupole moment is \(Q_d = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{m_d} \lim_{Q \rightarrow 0} \frac{1}{Q^2} [\mathcal{J}^+_{0,0} - \mathcal{J}^+_{1,1}]\).
* The form factor \(A(Q^2)\) has been measured up to \(Q^2 = 6\ (\text{GeV}/c)^2\) (JLABA, JLABC).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous front-form Hamiltonian dynamics approach (Lev, Pace, and Salme, 1999), which successfully resolved the longstanding discrepancy between theoretical and experimental deuteron magnetic and quadrupole moments at \(Q^2 = 0\). It contrasts with earlier front-form calculations (e.g., Kondratyuk, 1980; Chung, Coester, and Keister, 1988; Grach and Kondratyuk, 1984) that used the \(q^+ = 0\) frame and suffered from ambiguities due to the "angular condition" not being satisfied by the free current. The present unambiguous method enables systematic investigation of the sensitivity of deuteron form factors to different nucleon-nucleon interactions (e.g., Nijmegen potentials) and nucleon electromagnetic form factor models, and may allow extraction of information on the neutron charge form factor from elastic electron-deuteron scattering.
