# Unitarity and Entropy Change in Exclusive Quark Combination Models

> Entropy change in exclusive quark combination models is not an isolated problem. Contrary to adding and tuning some parameters to the relevant model(s) to fix the entropy, we show that it relates to the most general principles. Unitarity of the combination model is demonstrated to play the central rôle that guarantees the non-decrease of the entropy in the exclusive combination process.

# Wiki Page: Unitarity and Entropy Change in Exclusive Quark Combination Models

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that unitarity of the quark combination model, rather than any ad-hoc parameter tuning, guarantees the non-decrease of entropy in the exclusive combination process for a colour-singlet constituent quark system, provided all quarks are combined into hadrons with energy and momentum conserved.

## Method
The authors formulate unitarity of the quark combination model by introducing a unitary time-evolution operator U that describes the transition from a colour-singlet constituent quark state to hadron states, with the condition that the quark and hadron states form two complete sets of bases of the same Hilbert space. They then analyze entropy change using the von Neumann entropy formula S = -tr(ρ ln ρ) and show that a uniform unitary transformation on the density matrix leaves the entropy invariant. The argument is illustrated with the Shandong Group Quark Combination Model (SDQCM), which arranges quarks along the rapidity axis and combines nearest quarks step by step into mesons or baryons.

## Key numbers
- The step number for Au+Au central reactions at 200 GeV is more than 4500 steps, providing a large ensemble for cancellation of energy fluctuations in individual combination steps.
- The SDQCM employs a non-saturation potential to compensate for energy-momentum conservation during the combination process, with constituent quark masses as tunable parameters.

## Context
This work extends the formal understanding of quark combination models (QCM), which were first proposed in the early 1970s by Anisovich (1972) and Bjorken to describe multi-production in high energy collisions. The paper addresses a specific difficulty encountered when QCM is applied to central gold-gold collisions at RHIC, where the large proton/pion ratio and constituent quark number scaling of elliptic flow are naturally explained by quark combination. The authors show that concerns about entropy decrease in the combination process are resolved by unitarity, without needing extra inputs such as volume or temperature changes that were introduced in earlier bag model treatments by Song and Wang (2009). This enables consistent extrapolation of entropy-energy relations from the hadron system back to the constituent quark system, which is crucial for tracking the thermal state of quark-gluon matter.
