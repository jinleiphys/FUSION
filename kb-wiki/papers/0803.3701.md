# A beyond mean field analysis of the shape transition in the Neodymium isotopes

> The beyond mean field approximation combined with the Gogny interaction is used to analyze the spherical to prolate deformed shape transition in the Neodymium isotopic chain. The vibrator as well as the rotor limits are nicely reproduced while the transitional region is only qualitatively described probably due to the lack of triaxial correlations in the calculations. Our results do not support the interpretation of $^{150}$Nd as a critical point nucleus and question the interpretation of shape changes as nuclear shape phase transitions.

# A beyond mean field analysis of the shape transition in the Neodymium isotopes

## Key claim

The spherical-to-prolate shape transition in the Neodymium isotopic chain is only qualitatively described by beyond-mean-field calculations with the Gogny interaction, and the results do not support the interpretation of 150Nd as a critical point nucleus. The authors question whether nuclear shape changes should be interpreted as nuclear shape phase transitions at all, because the microscopic potentials that produce X(5)-like spectra do not satisfy the required condition of coexisting and degenerate spherical and deformed minima.

## Method

The authors performed beyond-mean-field calculations using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach with the Gogny D1S force. They first generated particle-number-projected potential energy surfaces in the (beta, gamma) plane for 144-154Nd, then carried out generator coordinate method (GCM) calculations along the axial quadrupole degree of freedom with particle number and angular momentum projection. Configuration mixing was performed using the Hill-Wheeler equation, but triaxial degrees of freedom were not included in the final GCM calculations due to computational limitations.

## Key numbers

- The ratio R4/2 = E(4+)/E(2+) varies from approximately 2 (vibrational limit, 144Nd) to approximately 3.33 (rotational limit, 152-154Nd) across the isotopic chain.
- In the IBM description, the critical point of a first-order phase transition occurs for a control parameter zeta = 0.02547, where spherical and prolate deformed minima are degenerate; this critical condition exists only in the extremely narrow range 0.0251 <= zeta <= 0.0255.
- The nucleus 148Nd is identified as the most transitional nucleus in the calculations, not 150Nd as claimed in the X(5) interpretation.
- For 146Nd, calculations including both prolate and oblate configurations yield spectra close to the vibrational limit, while calculations restricted to prolate configurations alone yield spectra close to the rotational limit.

## Context

This work extends the beyond-mean-field study of shape transitions in the A ~ 150 region, following the first such study by Niksic and collaborators (2007) using relativistic mean field theory, which concentrated on 148-150-152Nd. The present calculations cover a wider range (144-154Nd) and explicitly analyze the role of triaxial shapes. The results contradict the interpretation of 150Nd as a critical point nucleus proposed by Iachello (2001) and supported by Casten and collaborators (2001) and Krucken and collaborators (2002). The authors argue that X(5)-type spectra can arise from potentials very different from the critical potential required by Iachello's theory, and that the neglect of triaxial and octupole degrees of freedom in previous analyses may be significant. Earlier Gogny-force calculations by Garrote and collaborators (1998) had shown that Nd isotopes are soft against octupole deformation and could reproduce negative parity states.
