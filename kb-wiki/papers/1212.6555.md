# Turbulence-Induced Instabilities in EP and QGP

> Polarization properties of turbulent stochastically inhomogeneous ultrarelativistic QED plasma are studied. It is shown that the sign of nonlinear turbulent Landau damping corresponds to an instability of the spacelike modes and, for sufficiently large turbulent fields, to an actual instability of a system.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the sign of the nonlinear turbulent Landau damping in ultrarelativistic QED plasma corresponds to an instability of spacelike modes, and for sufficiently large turbulent fields, to an actual instability of the system; however, the authors note that because the perturbative expansion requires turbulent fields to be small, the result for the onset of instability at parametrically large fields should be considered only a qualitative indication.

## Method

The authors study polarization properties of a weakly turbulent ultrarelativistic QED plasma using a collisionless Vlasov kinetic equation with stochastic Gaussian turbulent fields characterized by a two-point correlator with Gaussian spatial and temporal decay. They compute the polarization tensor to first order in the regular field and second order in the turbulent fields, expanding in gradients of the turbulent correlation scale, and extract the leading turbulent modifications to the imaginary part (Landau damping) of both transverse and longitudinal polarization components.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic contribution to the transverse turbulent polarization, Im[χ_T^(1)(x)], changes sign at x* ≈ 0.43 in the spacelike domain (x < 1), where x = ω/|k|.
- The Debye mass is given by m_D^2 = e^2 T^2 / 3.
- The gradient expansion parameter is (|k|l), where l ≡ √2 (τ a) / √(τ² + a²) with τ and a the temporal and spatial correlation lengths of the turbulent fields.

## Context

This work extends the study of turbulent QCD plasma as a candidate for describing primordial quark-gluon matter in the weak coupling regime, following the calculation of anomalously low shear viscosity in turbulent QGP by Asakawa, Bass, and Muller (2006). The authors restrict the present analysis to the Abelian case for simplicity, noting that the non-Abelian generalization yields identical expressions up to trivial color factors. The results enable further investigation of how turbulent stochastic inhomogeneity, analogous to stochastic transition radiation, can drive secondary instabilities in relativistic plasmas.
