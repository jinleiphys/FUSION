# Flavourful hadronic physics

> We review theoretical approaches to form factors that arise in heavy-meson decays and are hadronic expressions of non-perturbative QCD. After motivating their origin in QCD factorisation, we retrace their evolution from quark-model calculations to non-perturbative QCD techniques with an emphasis on formulations of truncated heavy-light amplitudes based upon Dyson-Schwinger equations. We compare model predictions exemplarily for the B\toπtransition form factor and discuss new results for the g_{D*Dπ} coupling in the hadronic D* decay.

# Flavourful hadronic physics

## Key claim

The paper reviews theoretical approaches to heavy-meson decay form factors, arguing that while factorisation theorems separate hard from soft physics, a reliable evaluation of the non-perturbative soft contributions remains notoriously difficult and currently requires modelling with as much input from non-perturbative QCD as possible. The authors emphasise that the running quark mass from Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) quark propagators is crucial to include confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking effects, but consistent solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for D and B mesons within the DSE formalism remain an unfinished task.

## Method

The authors compare several theoretical approaches to heavy-to-light transition form factors: relativistic quark models, light-cone sum rules (LCSR), lattice-QCD simulations, and Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) methods. They present the DSE framework in detail, describing the dressed quark propagator obtained from the gap equation, the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes for mesons, and the heavy-light transition amplitude expressed as a triangle diagram. They also compute the strong coupling \(g_{D^*D\pi}\) from the hadronic \(D^*\) decay using the DSE approach with dressed quark propagators and the pion's Bethe-Salpeter amplitude.

## Key numbers

- \(F_+^{B\to\pi}(0)\) values from different models range from 0.217 (Ref. Ebert 2006) to 0.29 (Ref. Melikhov 2001), with LCSR predictions of \(0.25\pm0.05\) (Ref. Khodjamirian 2006) and \(0.26\pm0.03\) (Ref. Ball 2004)
- At \(q^2=20\) GeV\(^2\), \(F_+^{B\to\pi}\) values range from 1.40 (Ref. Ebert 2006) to 1.83 (Ref. Khodjamirian 2006)
- Experimental coupling \(g_{D^*D\pi} = 17.9 \pm 0.3 \pm 1.9\) (Anastassov 2001)
- DSE calculation yields \(g_{D^*D\pi} = 21\) (El-Bennich 2009), about 16% larger than the experimental value
- Lattice simulation with \(n_f=2\) yields \(g_{D^*D\pi} = 20 \pm 2\) (Becirevic 2009)
- Current lattice results for \(B\to\pi\) transitions are obtained at large squared momentum transfer, \(q^2 \simeq 16\) GeV\(^2\)

## Context

This review extends earlier work on heavy-meson decay form factors by comparing quark-model calculations (Ebert 2006, Melikhov 2001, Lu 2007), light-cone sum rules (Khodjamirian 2006, Ball 2004), and lattice-QCD simulations with DSE-based approaches (Ivanov 2007). The paper builds on the QCD factorisation framework (Beneke 1999), perturbative QCD (Keum 2000), and soft-collinear effective theory (Bauer 2000) to argue that precise form factor calculations require non-perturbative input that captures confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. The DSE calculation of \(g_{D^*D\pi}\) agrees with recent lattice results (Becirevic 2009) and enables future work toward consistent Bethe-Salpeter solutions for heavy mesons, which would reduce model dependence in transition amplitudes.
