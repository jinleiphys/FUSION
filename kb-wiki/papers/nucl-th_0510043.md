# QGP and Modified Jet Fragmentation

> Recent progresses in the study of jet modification in hot medium and their consequences in high-energy heavy-ion collisions are reviewed. In particular, I will discuss energy loss for propagating heavy quarks and the resulting modified fragmentation function. Medium modification of the parton fragmentation function due to quark recombination are formulated within finite temperature field theory and their implication on the search for deconfined quark-gluon plasma is also discussed

## Key claim

This review argues that jet quenching in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, observed through suppression of high-$p_T$ hadron spectra, disappearance of back-to-back correlations, and azimuthal anisotropy, can be consistently described by parton energy loss proportional to the gluon density of the medium; the initial gluon density in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV is estimated to be about 30 times higher than in cold Au nuclei, assuming the theoretical result that parton energy loss is proportional to the gluon density.

## Method

The paper reviews leading-order pQCD parton model calculations incorporating modified parton fragmentation functions due to parton energy loss, with the average energy loss given by $\langle \Delta E \rangle \approx \pi C_a C_A \alpha_s^3 \int_{\tau_0}^{R_A} d\tau \rho(\tau) (\tau-\tau_0) \ln(2E/\tau\mu^2)$. The formalism is extended to heavy quark energy loss, dihadron fragmentation functions with DGLAP evolution, and a field-theoretical formulation of quark recombination in a thermal medium where vacuum expectation values are replaced by thermal averages.

## Key numbers

- Initial gluon density in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV is about 30 times higher than in cold Au nuclei
- Charm quark mass set at $M=1.5$ GeV in numerical calculations of heavy quark energy loss
- Energy loss for a gluon is 9/4 times larger than for a quark (non-Abelian color factor)
- Nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ for neutral pions at fixed $p_T=6$ GeV in central Au+Au collisions calculated as a function of $\sqrt{s}$ from 20 AGeV to 5500 AGeV
- Change of slope in $R_{AA}$ energy dependence around $\sqrt{s}=1300$ GeV
- At intermediate $p_T=2-4$ GeV/$c$, baryon-to-meson ratio increases by about a factor of 5 over the value in $p+p$ collisions

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical estimates of parton energy loss by Gyulassy-Wang (GP,WG), Baier-Dokshitzer-Mueller-Peigne-Schiff (BDMPS), Zakharov (Zh), and others, and applies them to experimental observations from PHENIX and STAR at RHIC. The review enables further tests of the parton energy loss picture through heavy quark tagging, energy dependence of hadron suppression, and dihadron correlations, and provides a field-theoretical foundation for quark recombination models such as those by Hwa-Yang (2002) and Fries et al. (2003).
