# A Class of Exactly Solvable Pairing Models

> We present three classes of exactly solvable models for fermion and boson systems, based on the pairing interaction. These models are solvable in any dimension. As an example we show the first results for fermion interacting with repulsive pairing forces in a two dimensional square lattice. Inspite of the repulsive pairing force the exact results show attractive pair correlations.

# A Class of Exactly Solvable Pairing Models

## Key claim

The paper presents three families of exactly solvable pairing models (rational, trigonometric, and hyperbolic) for fermion and boson systems that are solvable in any dimension, not just one dimension. Despite using a repulsive pairing force in a two-dimensional square lattice, the exact solutions reveal attractive pair correlations, though the authors present these as preliminary results and note that the interaction energy density shows a tendency toward saturation consistent with vanishing in the thermodynamic limit.

## Method

The authors construct integrable Hamiltonians from linear combinations of commuting operators \(R_l\) built from the generators of SU(2) (for fermions) or SU(1,1) (for bosons), imposing a Gaudin-type condition on the coupling matrices \(X_{ll'}\) and \(Y_{ll'}\). They then propose a generalized Richardson ansatz for the eigenstates as product pair wavefunctions, leading to coupled nonlinear equations for the pair energies \(e_\alpha\) that must be solved for each model. For the rational model in a 2D square lattice, they solve these equations numerically for a 6x6 lattice at half filling with a repulsive pairing interaction.

## Context

This work extends the exact solution of the pairing model originally solved by Richardson (1963-1968) and later shown to be integrable by Cambiaggio, Rivas, and Saraceno (1997). The rational model reproduces the standard pairing Hamiltonian, while the trigonometric and hyperbolic models provide new integrable families. The authors connect their results to the work of Krishnamurthy and Shastry (2000), who found quasi-long-range order in the thermodynamic limit of the repulsive pairing model in 2D, and note that these models may be useful for studying features of high-Tc superconductivity. The hyperbolic model was independently proposed by Amico, Di Lorenzo, and Osterloh for ultrasmall superconducting grains.
