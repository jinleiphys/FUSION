# Self-Consistent Quasi-Particle RPA for the Description of Superfluid Fermi Systems

> Self-Consistent Quasi-Particle RPA (SCQRPA) is for the first time applied to a more level pairing case. Various filling situations and values for the coupling constant are considered. Very encouraging results in comparison with the exact solution of the model are obtained. The nature of the low lying mode in SCQRPA is identified. The strong reduction of the number fluctuation in SCQRPA vs BCS is pointed out. The transition from superfluidity to the normal fluid case is carefully investigated.

## Key claim

Self-Consistent Quasi-Particle RPA (SCQRPA) is applied for the first time to a two-level pairing model with arbitrary degeneracy and filling, and the authors find that the theory gives "very promising results" when compared to the exact solution of the model, but only within the limitations of the specific model and the truncation scheme used for the quasi-particle number operator expansion.

## Method

The authors start from the two-level pairing model Hamiltonian with equal degeneracy 2Ω and single-particle energy splitting ε, transform it to a quasi-particle basis via a Bogoliubov transformation, and derive the SCQRPA equations by minimizing a mean excitation energy with respect to the RPA amplitudes. The Bogoliubov amplitudes u_j and v_j are determined from the minimization of the ground-state energy using the correlated RPA ground-state rather than the BCS ground-state, leading to renormalized single-particle energies and interactions that couple back to the RPA amplitudes. The quasi-particle occupation numbers and their variances are expressed through an expansion in the pair operators P†_j and P_j, truncated at second order, and all expectation values are computed self-consistently in the RPA vacuum.

## Key numbers

- For the symmetric case N=2Ω, the standard QRPA yields a Goldstone mode Ω^{QRPA}_1 = 0 and a second mode Ω^{QRPA}_2 = sqrt(4Δ² - 2Δ²/Ω), where Δ = sqrt(g²Ω² - ξ²/4) and ξ = 2εΩ/(2Ω-1).
- In the BCS solution for the symmetric case, the chemical potential is μ = -g/2.
- The gap equation in the BCS theory for the symmetric case gives Δ = sqrt(g²Ω² - ξ²/4).
- The Bogoliubov amplitudes for the symmetric case are u²_1 = v²_{-1} = (1/2)(1 + ξ/(2gΩ)) and v²_1 = u²_{-1} = (1/2)(1 - ξ/(2gΩ)).

## Context

This work extends the Self-Consistent RPA (SCRPA) approach previously applied to the many-level pairing model in the non-superfluid phase by Dukelsky and Schuck (2001) and others, now developing the formalism for the fully developed superfluid regime. The SCQRPA is derived from a variational principle close to a Raleigh-Ritz variational theory, going beyond mean-field BCS theory by including correlations and quantum fluctuations self-consistently. The approach is tested on the exactly solvable two-level pairing model, and the authors aim to ultimately apply this microscopic method to calculate pairing properties of realistic Fermi systems starting from the bare force, addressing the problem of strongly reduced pairing gaps found in more sophisticated approaches such as correlated basis function methods and self-consistent T-matrix approaches.
