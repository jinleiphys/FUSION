# Fission dynamics, dissipation and clustering at finite temperature

> The saddle-to-scission dynamics of the induced fission process is explored using a microscopic finite-temperature model based on time-dependent nuclear density functional theory (TDDFT), that allows to follow the evolution of local temperature along fission trajectories. Starting from a temperature that corresponds to the experimental excitation energy of the compound system, the model propagates the nucleons along isentropic paths toward scission. For the four illustrative cases of induced fission of $^{240}$Pu, $^{234}$U, $^{244}$Cm, and $^{250}$Cf, characteristic fission trajectories are considered, and the partition of the total energy into various kinetic and potential energy contributions at scission is analyzed, with special emphasis on the energy dissipated along the fission path and the prescission kinetic energy. The model is also applied to the dynamics of neck formation and rupture, characterized by the formation of few-nucleon clusters in the low-density region between the nascent fragments.

## Key claim

The paper develops a finite-temperature extension of time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) that can model the evolution of local temperature along fission trajectories, but the present implementation cannot describe the dynamical treatment of pairing correlations and therefore only applies to cases where pairing correlations essentially vanish (temperatures above approximately 0.6 MeV).

## Method

The authors perform time-dependent covariant density functional theory calculations starting from initial states beyond the outer fission barrier, with initial temperatures corresponding to experimental excitation energies of the compound system. At each time step, the local temperature and chemical potential are adjusted to conserve total energy and particle number, while the nucleons are propagated along isentropic paths toward scission. The model uses the point-coupling relativistic energy density functional PC-PK1 on a three-dimensional lattice with mesh spacing 1.0 fm and box size 20×20×60 fm³, with a time step of 6.67×10⁻⁴ zs.

## Context

This work extends previous TDDFT studies of fission dynamics at zero temperature (Ren 2022, Ren 2022) and finite-temperature TDGCM+GOA calculations (Zhao 2019, Zhao 2019) by introducing a microscopic model that can track local temperature changes along fission trajectories. The approach enables analysis of energy dissipation and heating during the saddle-to-scission phase, which is not possible in the TDGCM+GOA framework that assumes constant temperature. The results demonstrate the importance of one-body dissipation mechanisms included in TDDFT, in contrast to approaches that consider only collective degrees of freedom. The model also allows study of neck formation and the dynamical synthesis of light clusters in the low-density region between nascent fragments, building on earlier work by Negele (1978) on pairing correlations in fission.
