# A puzzle of the pion-photon transition form factor - resolved?

> By means of QCD sum rules in local-duality limit, we analyze the behaviour of the form factors for the transitions of a real and a virtual photon to some pseudoscalar meson as functions of the involved momentum transfer. Except for the findings of BaBar for the neutral-pion form factor, the experimental data for all these transition form factors are compatible with saturation for large momentum transfer predicted by pQCD factorization. For light pseudoscalar mesons, saturation is observed already at relatively small momentum transfer, whereas for the eta-c meson it sets in only at larger momentum transfer. A recent measurement of the neutral-pion transition form factor by Belle seems to resolve this disturbing puzzle as its outcome is compatible with both saturation for relatively small momentum transfer and the behaviour of the eta and eta' transition form factors at large momentum transfer.

## Key claim

The BaBar collaboration's measurement of the neutral-pion transition form factor, which showed a violation of the expected saturation at large momentum transfer, may be resolved by the Belle collaboration's data, which is compatible with saturation for \(Q^2 \ge 10\)–\(15\;\text{GeV}^2\) and with the large-\(Q^2\) behaviour of the \(\eta\) and \(\eta'\) transition form factors.

## Method

The authors analyze the \(\gamma\gamma^* \to P\) transition form factors for pseudoscalar mesons \(P = \pi^0, \eta, \eta', \eta_c\) using QCD sum rules in the local-duality (LD) limit, where the Borel parameter is taken to zero. The nonperturbative effects are encoded in an effective continuum threshold \(s_{\text{eff}}(Q^2)\), which is fixed by requiring consistency with pQCD factorization at asymptotically large \(Q^2\). The form factors are then computed from the spectral integral of the perturbative QCD spectral density, truncated at this effective threshold.

## Key numbers

- For the \(\eta_c\) meson, the LD model yields \(F_{\eta_c\gamma}(Q^2=0) = 0.067\;\text{GeV}^{-1}\) from the \(\langle AVV\rangle\) correlator and \(0.086\;\text{GeV}^{-1}\) from the \(\langle PVV\rangle\) correlator, compared to the measured value \(0.08 \pm 0.01\;\text{GeV}^{-1}\).
- For light pseudoscalar mesons (\(\pi^0, \eta, \eta'\)), saturation of the form factor is observed at relatively small \(Q^2 \ge 10\)–\(15\;\text{GeV}^2\).
- For the \(\eta_c\) meson, saturation sets in only at larger \(Q^2 \ge 200\)–\(300\;\text{GeV}^2\).
- The Belle \(\pi^0\) transition form-factor data agrees with the onset of the LD regime already in the range \(Q^2 \ge 5\)–\(10\;\text{GeV}^2\).
- The charged-pion decay constant used is \(f_\pi = 130\;\text{MeV}\).
- The \(\eta\)–\(\eta'\) mixing angle is \(\phi \approx 38^\circ\), with decay constants \(f_n \approx 1.07 f_\pi\) and \(f_s \approx 1.36 f_\pi\).

## Context

This work extends the local-duality QCD sum-rule approach previously applied to meson–photon transitions by the same authors (Balakireva, Lucha, Melikhov, 2011) and others (Lucha, Melikhov, 2011). It directly addresses the puzzle created by the BaBar collaboration's measurement of the \(\pi^0\) form factor, which disagreed with the \(\eta\) and \(\eta'\) form factors and with pQCD factorization predictions. The Belle collaboration's more recent data appears to resolve this discrepancy, and the results enable further tests of local duality, including predictions for the charged-pion elastic form factor that could be verified by CLAS12 after the JLab 12 GeV upgrade.
