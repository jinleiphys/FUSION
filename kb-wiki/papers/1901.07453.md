# Orbital Angular Momentum at Small $x$

> We determine the small Bjorken $x$ asymptotics of the quark and gluon orbital angular momentum (OAM) distributions in the proton in the double-logarithmic approximation (DLA), which resums powers of $α_s \ln^2 (1/x)$ with $α_s$ the strong coupling constant. Starting with the operator definitions for the quark and gluon OAM, we simplify them at small $x$, relating them, respectively, to the polarized dipole amplitudes for the quark and gluon helicities defined in our earlier works. Using the small-$x$ evolution equations derived for these polarized dipole amplitudes earlier we arrive at the following small-$x$ asymptotics of the quark and gluon OAM distributions in the large-$N_c$ limit: \begin{align} L_{q + \bar{q}} (x, Q^2) = - ΔΣ(x, Q^2) \sim \left(\frac{1}{x}\right)^{\frac{4}{\sqrt{3}} \, \sqrt{\frac{α_s \, N_c}{2 π}} }, \ \ \ \ \ L_G (x, Q^2) \sim ΔG (x, Q^2) \sim \left(\frac{1}{x}\right)^{\frac{13}{4 \sqrt{3}} \, \sqrt{\frac{α_s \, N_c}{2 π}}} . \end{align}

## Key claim

The small-\(x\) asymptotics of the quark and gluon orbital angular momentum (OAM) distributions in the proton are determined in the double-logarithmic approximation (DLA), which resums powers of \(\alpha_s \ln^2 (1/x)\), and the results hold specifically in the large-\(N_c\) limit.

## Method

The author starts from the operator definitions of quark and gluon OAM expressed via Wigner distributions, simplifies them at small \(x\) using the shock-wave approach and Brodsky-Lepage spinors, and relates the OAM distributions to the first impact-parameter moments of the polarized dipole amplitudes previously defined for quark and gluon helicities. The small-\(x\) evolution equations for these polarized dipole amplitudes, derived in earlier works, are then used to construct and solve the evolution equations for the impact-parameter moments, yielding the asymptotic power behavior.

## Key numbers

* Quark OAM distribution: \(L_{q + \bar{q}} (x, Q^2) \sim \left(\frac{1}{x}\right)^{\frac{4}{\sqrt{3}} \, \sqrt{\frac{\alpha_s \, N_c}{2 \pi}}}\) in the large-\(N_c\) DLA limit.
* Gluon OAM distribution: \(L_G (x, Q^2) \sim \left(\frac{1}{x}\right)^{\frac{13}{4 \sqrt{3}} \, \sqrt{\frac{\alpha_s \, N_c}{2 \pi}}}\) in the large-\(N_c\) DLA limit.
* The quark OAM distribution is equal in magnitude to the negative of the quark helicity distribution: \(L_{q + \bar{q}} (x, Q^2) = - \Delta\Sigma(x, Q^2)\) at this order.
* The gluon OAM distribution has the same small-\(x\) asymptotics as the gluon helicity distribution: \(L_G (x, Q^2) \sim \Delta G (x, Q^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the small-\(x\) DLA resummation program for helicity distributions (Kovchegov 2015-2018) to the orbital angular momentum components of the proton spin, which are required by the Jaffe-Manohar sum rule. It goes beyond the one-loop \(Q^2\) evolution equations for OAM solved by Hatta (2018), which resum \(\alpha_s \ln Q^2\) rather than the leading \(\alpha_s \ln^2 (1/x)\) terms. The results enable theoretical constraints on the small-\(x\) contribution to the proton spin sum rule and provide predictions for future measurements of OAM distributions at small \(x\).
