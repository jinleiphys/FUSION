# Quark mass difference effects in hadronic Fermi matrix elements from first principles

> It was recently estimated that the strong isospin-symmetry breaking (ISB) corrections to the Fermi matrix element in free neutron decay could be of the order $10^{-4}$, one order of magnitude larger than the na\"ıve estimate based on the Behrends-Sirlin-Ademollo-Gatto theorem. To investigate this claim, we derive a general expression of the leading ISB correction to hadronic Fermi matrix elements, which takes the form of a four-point correlation function in lattice gauge theory and is straightforward to compute from first principles. Our formalism paves the way for the first determination of such correction in the neutron sector with fully-controlled theory uncertainties.

## Key claim

The strong isospin-symmetry breaking (ISB) correction to the Fermi matrix element in free neutron decay could be of order 10⁻⁴, one order of magnitude larger than the naive estimate based on the Behrends-Sirlin-Ademollo-Gatto theorem, due to coherent enhancement from excited state contributions that avoids cancellation between electromagnetic and quark mass difference effects. The authors derive a formalism to compute this correction from first principles using lattice QCD, but defer the actual lattice calculation to future work.

## Method

The authors split the full Hamiltonian into an isospin-symmetric part H₀ and an ISB perturbation V, then use Brillouin-Wigner perturbation theory to derive an expression for the leading O(V²) correction to the Fermi matrix element. They re-express this correction in terms of a four-point correlation function involving two isotriplet scalar operators and a Green's function 1/(H₀−ζ) in the isospin-symmetric system, which is directly calculable on the lattice. The generating function F(ζ) and its derivative at the hadron mass are obtained from integrals of the four-point correlation function over Euclidean time.

## Key numbers

- The strong ISB correction to the neutron Fermi matrix element is estimated at δ_N ∼ 4 × 10⁻⁴ (from Crawford (2022), after averaging over three models)
- A ∼20% precision on the non-perturbative calculation of F'_N(m_N⁰) would keep the theory uncertainty of M_F² below 10⁻⁴
- For pion beta decay, chiral perturbation theory predicts δ_π ≈ 1 × 10⁻⁵, using QCD parameters from the FLAG review (F₀ = 80.3(6.0) MeV, m_u = 2.27(9) MeV, m_d = 4.67(9) MeV, m_s/ˆm = 27.42(12))
- The naive power-counting estimate based on (m_u−m_d)²/Λ_QCD² gives ∼10⁻⁵

## Context

This work extends the method developed by Seng (2023) for nuclear beta decays to the hadron sector without the model assumptions present in that earlier work. It directly addresses the claim by Guichon (2011) and Crawford (2022) that strong ISB corrections in neutron decay could be an order of magnitude larger than the BSAG theorem estimate from Behrends and Sirlin (1960) and Ademollo and Gatto (1964). The formalism enables the first first-principles lattice QCD determination of this correction in the neutron sector, which is needed for precision tests of the Standard Model through V_ud extraction from neutron and nuclear beta decays. The approach could also be generalized to study the K₀ → π⁻ transition form factor f_+^{Kπ}(0) to improve V_us extraction from semileptonic kaon decays.
