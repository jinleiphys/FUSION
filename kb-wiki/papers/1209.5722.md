# Phase space factors for double-$β$ decay

> A complete and improved calculation of phase space factors (PSF) for $2νββ$ and $0νββ$ decay is presented. The calculation makes use of exact Dirac wave functions with finite nuclear size and electron screening and includes life-times, single and summed electron spectra, and angular electron correlations.

## Key claim

The paper provides a complete and improved calculation of phase space factors for both two-neutrino and neutrinoless double-beta decay by using exact Dirac wave functions with finite nuclear size and electron screening, correcting the approximations used in prior work by Tomoda (1991) that evaluated electron wave functions at the nuclear radius without screening.

## Method

The authors solved the radial Dirac equations using the RADIAL subroutine package with a Coulomb potential modified by a uniform nuclear charge distribution of radius \(R = r_0 A^{1/3}\) with \(r_0 = 1.2\) fm, and included electron screening via the Thomas-Fermi approximation solved by the Majorana method. Electron wave functions were evaluated at the nuclear radius (approximation I) for all nuclei, and the resulting phase space factors \(G_{2\nu}^{(0)}\), \(G_{2\nu}^{(1)}\), and \(G_{0\nu}^{(0)}\) were computed by integrating the differential decay rates over electron and neutrino energies for 18 candidate nuclei for \(\beta^-\beta^-\) decay.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{76}\)Ge \(2\nu\beta\beta\) decay: \(G_{2\nu}^{(0)} = 48.17 \times 10^{-21}\) y\(^{-1}\) and \(G_{2\nu}^{(1)} = -26.97 \times 10^{-21}\) y\(^{-1}\) with \(Q_{\beta\beta} = 2.039061(7)\) MeV and \(\tilde{A} = 9.411\) MeV.
- For \(^{136}\)Xe \(2\nu\beta\beta\) decay: \(G_{2\nu}^{(0)} = 1433 \times 10^{-21}\) y\(^{-1}\) and \(G_{2\nu}^{(1)} = -927.2 \times 10^{-21}\) y\(^{-1}\) with \(Q_{\beta\beta} = 2.45783(37)\) MeV and \(\tilde{A} = 13.06\) MeV.
- For \(^{150}\)Nd \(2\nu\beta\beta\) decay: \(G_{2\nu}^{(0)} = 36430 \times 10^{-21}\) y\(^{-1}\) and \(G_{2\nu}^{(1)} = -26860 \times 10^{-21}\) y\(^{-1}\) with \(Q_{\beta\beta} = 3.37138(20)\) MeV and \(\tilde{A} = 13.72\) MeV.
- For \(^{48}\)Ca \(2\nu\beta\beta\) decay: \(G_{2\nu}^{(0)} = 15550 \times 10^{-21}\) y\(^{-1}\) and \(G_{2\nu}^{(1)} = -11930 \times 10^{-21}\) y\(^{-1}\) with \(Q_{\beta\beta} = 4.27226(404)\) MeV and \(\tilde{A} = 7.717\) MeV.
- For \(^{130}\)Te \(2\nu\beta\beta\) decay: \(G_{2\nu}^{(0)} = 1529 \times 10^{-21}\) y\(^{-1}\) and \(G_{2\nu}^{(1)} = -993.9 \times 10^{-21}\) y\(^{-1}\) with \(Q_{\beta\beta} = 2.52697(23)\) MeV and \(\tilde{A} = 13.27\) MeV.
- For \(^{100}\)Mo under the single state dominance hypothesis: \(G_{2\nu}^{(0)} = 4134 \times 10^{-21}\) y\(^{-1}\) and \(G_{2\nu}^{(1)} = -2762 \times 10^{-21}\) y\(^{-1}\) with \(\tilde{A}_{\rm SSD} = 1.685\) MeV.
- The dependence of \(G_{2\nu}^{(0)}\) on the closure energy \(\tilde{A}\) is mild (less than 1%) except very close to threshold where it reaches about 7%.

## Context

This work extends the earlier phase space factor calculations of Tomoda (1991) by including finite nuclear size and electron screening, which are particularly important for heavy nuclei where \(\alpha Z\) is large. The results enable more accurate extraction of nuclear matrix elements from experimental half-lives for experiments such as EXO (searching for \(^{136}\)Xe decay) and NEMO (searching for \(^{82}\)Se decay), and provide the necessary input for interpreting both \(2\nu\beta\beta\) and \(0\nu\beta\beta\) decay measurements in the context of neutrino mass determination.
