# Universal geometrical scaling for hadronic interactions

> It is shown that defining a suitable saturation momentum $Q_s$, the $p_T$ distributions of pp and AA collisions for any centrality and energy depend only on $τ=p^2_T/Q_s^2$ for $p_T<Q_s$. For different projectiles, targets and centralities, the corresponding $τ$-lines present small differences for $τ<1$. For $τ>1$, the higher the energy or the larger the size of the participant nuclei, the larger suppression present the respective spectra. The integrated spectrum gives a fraction of the hard multiplicity in the range from 9% for pp at 0.9 TeV to 2% for Pb-Pb central collisions at 2.76 TeV.

# Universal geometrical scaling for hadronic interactions

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the transverse momentum spectra of charged particles in both pp and AA collisions, across different centralities and energies, scale onto a universal curve when expressed as a function of the scaling variable τ = p_T²/Q_s², provided the nuclear saturation momentum Q_s^A is defined with an energy-dependent exponent β(s) that accounts for energy conservation in string formation.

## Method
The authors define a nuclear saturation momentum Q_s^A using a parametrization based on string percolation theory, incorporating the number of participant nucleons N_A, the nuclear mass number A, and an energy-dependent function β(s). They then plot experimental charged-particle multiplicity data per participant from RHIC (Cu-Cu and Au-Au at 62.4 and 200 GeV) and LHC (Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV, p-Pb at 5.02 TeV, and pp at 0.9, 2.36, and 7 TeV) as a function of τ, and examine the collapse of the data onto a single curve for τ < 1. The hard and soft multiplicity fractions are computed by integrating the fitted p_T distributions above and below Q_s.

## Key numbers
- λ = 0.27 (the exponent in the proton saturation momentum definition Q_s^p); a slightly worse scaling was obtained with λ = 0.30
- Q_0 = 1 GeV (reference scale for saturation momentum)
- √s_0 = 245 GeV (energy scale of the energy conservation effect in β(s))
- Hard multiplicity fraction ranges from 9% for pp at 0.9 TeV to 2% for Pb-Pb central collisions (0-5%) at 2.76 TeV
- For pp collisions, hard fraction varies weakly from 9% at 0.9 TeV to 8% at 7 TeV
- For Au-Au 0-6% central at 62.4 GeV, hard fraction is 7%; for Au-Au 0-6% central at 200 GeV, hard fraction is 4%
- For Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV, hard fraction is 2% for both peripheral (40-50%) and central (0-5%) collisions
- For p-Pb at 5.02 TeV, hard fraction is 7%
- For Cu-Cu 0-6% central at 62.4 GeV, hard fraction is 7%; at 200 GeV, hard fraction is 6%
- Ratios of AA to pp spectra vary between 0.5 and 1 in the range 0.2 < τ < 1

## Context
This work extends the geometrical scaling previously demonstrated for pp collisions (ref1, ref2) to AA collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, using a saturation momentum parametrization derived from string percolation theory (ref3, ref4). The scaling is related to the color glass condensate framework (ref15, ref16) and the percolation of strings model (ref17, ref18), where the saturation momentum plays a role analogous to the square root of the string density. The observed decrease in hard multiplicity fraction with increasing energy and nuclear size contradicts naive expectations but agrees with ALICE sphericity data (ref13, ref14) and with the color glass condensate prediction that larger saturation momentum leaves less room for hard collisions. The scaling violations for τ > 1 follow the hierarchy expected from jet quenching, with larger suppression for heavier nuclei.
