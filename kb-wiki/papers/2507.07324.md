# Chiral Cherenkov radiation in the presence of a time-dependent chiral chemical potential

> The photon production process $f\to f+γ$ in the presence of a time-dependent chiral chemical potential $μ_5$ is studied. The validity of the adiabatic approximation is demonstrated in the ultra-relativistic limit. Analytical expressions for the photon spectrum are derived for two models of the chiral magnetic conductivity $b_0\propto μ_5$: (i) $b_0(t)= A_1+B_1\tanh(t/τ)$ and (ii) $b_0(t)= A_2(t/τ)/(1+t^2/τ^2)$. It is known that for constant $b_0$, photon emission may exhibit a resonance for one photon polarization depending on the sign of $b_0$. It is shown that in model (i), up to two resonances may occur, depending on the sign of the asymptotic values $b_0(\infty)$ and $b_0(-\infty)$. No resonances are observed in model (ii). Numerical calculations are performed using parameters relevant to quark-gluon plasma, and the universality of the results is discussed.

# Chiral Cherenkov radiation in the presence of a time-dependent chiral chemical potential

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that when the chiral chemical potential (and thus the chiral magnetic conductivity $b_0$) varies in time, the photon spectrum from chiral Cherenkov radiation can exhibit up to two resonances for a given polarization (in the tanh model), or no resonances at all (in the pulse model), whereas the constant-$b_0$ case produces only a single resonance. The adiabatic approximation is shown to be valid for ultra-relativistic fermions, and the qualitative structure of the spectrum is argued to be universal for any model where $b_0$ is finite at either remote past or future.

## Method

The photon production process $f \to f + \gamma$ is studied in a chiral medium with time-dependent chiral magnetic conductivity $b_0(t) \propto \mu_5(t)$. The electromagnetic field amplitude $a(t)$ satisfies $\ddot a + \Omega^2(t) a = 0$ with $\Omega^2 = k^2 - \lambda b_0(t) k$, where $\lambda = \pm 1$ denotes circular polarization. The photon radiation probability is computed via perturbation theory, requiring the Fourier transform $\tilde a(E - E')$ of the field amplitude. Two analytical models for $b_0(t)$ are solved: (i) $b_0(t) = A_1 + B_1 \tanh(t/\tau)$, describing a transition between constant asymptotic values, and (ii) $b_0(t) = A_2 (t/\tau) / (1 + t^2/\tau^2)$, describing a finite-duration chirality pulse. The adiabatic approximation is employed in the ultra-relativistic limit $k \gg b_0$, yielding explicit expressions for the photon spectrum.

## Key numbers

- Chiral magnetic conductivity $b_0$ estimated in the range $10$–$100$ MeV for quark-gluon plasma (citing Kharzeev (2009), Gynther (2010), Yee (2009), Li (2016)).
- Transition time parameter $\tau$ taken as $25$ GeV$^{-1} \approx 5$ fm/c, with typical range $1$–$10$ fm based on sphaleron transition rates (citing Arnold (1996), Moore (2010)).
- Incident fermion energy $E = 20$ GeV, mass $m = 0.3$ GeV, unit charge used in numerical calculations.
- For model (i) with $A_1 = B_1 = 10$ MeV, resonances appear at $k_\bot$ values satisfying $k_\bot^2 = \lambda x(1-x)E(A_1 \mp B_1) - m^2 x^2$, with the condition $0 < x \le \lambda E (A_1 \mp B_1) / [\lambda E (A_1 \mp B_1) + m^2]$.
- For model (i) with $A_1 = 15$ MeV, $B_1 = 5$ MeV, two distinct resonances appear in the right-handed photon spectrum because both asymptotic values $b_0(\infty)$ and $b_0(-\infty)$ are positive.
- For model (ii) with $A_2 = 10$ MeV and $\tau = 25$ GeV$^{-1}$, no resonances are observed; the spectrum is independent of photon polarization.
- The adiabatic approximation condition $k_z^2 \gg |b_0|/\tau$ implies conservatively $k \gg 100$ MeV for the parameter ranges considered.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of chiral Cherenkov radiation at constant $b_0$ by Tuchin (2018) and Huang (2018), which showed that only one polarization exhibits a resonance depending on the sign of $b_0$. The time-dependent treatment is motivated by the evolution of chiral chemical potential in quark-gluon plasma due to sphaleron transitions (Arnold 1996, Moore 2010), chirality relaxation (Hongo 2022), and the inverse chiral cascade (Joyce 1997, Boyarsky 2011, Hirono 2015, Tuchin 2014). The results enable phenomenological predictions for parton energy loss and jet structure in heavy-ion collisions, and the formalism is also applicable to photon radiation from cosmic rays in axion fields (Saveliev 2024) or electrons in Weyl semimetals (Hansen 2024).
