# Real-time chiral dynamics at finite temperature from quantum simulation

> In this study, we explore the real-time dynamics of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) at a finite temperature in the (1+1)-dimensional QED, the massive Schwinger model. By introducing a chiral chemical potential $μ_5$ through a quench process, we drive the system out of equilibrium and analyze the induced vector currents and their evolution over time. The Hamiltonian is modified to include the time-dependent chiral chemical potential, thus allowing the investigation of the CME within a quantum computing framework. We employ the quantum imaginary time evolution (QITE) algorithm to study the thermal states, and utilize the Suzuki-Trotter decomposition for the real-time evolution. This study provides insights into the quantum simulation capabilities for modeling the CME and offers a pathway for studying chiral dynamics in low-dimensional quantum field theories.

## Key claim

The paper claims that under a rapid quench (as opposed to a slow variation of external fields), the chiral magnetic effect (CME) current develops a dependence on temperature that has not been evaluated before from first principles, and that this temperature dependence can be computed using quantum simulation methods in the massive Schwinger model.

## Method

The authors study the (1+1)-dimensional massive Schwinger model (QED in one spatial dimension) using a quench protocol where a time-dependent chiral chemical potential is introduced by rotating the theta angle as theta = -2 mu_5 t. They employ the quantum imaginary time evolution (QITE) algorithm to prepare thermal states on a lattice with N=4 qubits, then use second-order Suzuki-Trotter decomposition for real-time evolution of the system after the quench. The lattice Hamiltonian is expressed in spin representation via the Jordan-Wigner transformation, and simulations are performed using the Qiskit statevector simulator, with results compared to exact diagonalization for validation.

## Key numbers

* Thermal states were prepared for inverse temperatures from beta/a = 0 (aT = infinity) to beta/a = 4.0 (aT = 0.25) for three masses ma = 0.25, 0.5, 4.0 at coupling g = a^{-1}, using a trotter step size Delta beta = 0.01
* Fidelity between QITE-prepared thermal states and exact density matrices was between 0.999 and 1.0 for all masses and temperatures studied
* Real-time evolution used step sizes dt/a = 0.1 for light masses (ma = 0.25, 1.0) and dt/a = 0.02 for heavy mass (ma = 4.0)
* Exact diagonalization was performed on increasing lattice sizes from N=4 to N=10 qubits at two lattice spacings a = 1.0 GeV^{-1} and a = 0.5 GeV^{-1}, with chiral chemical potential mu_5 a = 0.1
* At stronger coupling g = 4a^{-1}, oscillations become faster but thermal damping sets in at approximately the same temperature as at g = a^{-1}

## Context

This work extends previous quantum simulation studies of the (1+1)-dimensional Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model by Czajka (2022) and the authors' own prior work on the Schwinger model at zero temperature by Kharzeev (2020) and Ikeda (2023). The paper addresses the theoretical prediction by Fukushima (2008) and PhysRevD.80.034028 that the CME current should be temperature-independent under slow variations, but can develop temperature dependence under rapid quenches due to contributions from excited fermion modes. The results enable future continuum extrapolation of finite-temperature CME dynamics and provide a pathway for studying chiral dynamics in low-dimensional quantum field theories on quantum hardware.
