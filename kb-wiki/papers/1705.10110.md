# Gluo-dissociation of Heavy Quarkonium in the Quark-Gluon Plasma Revisited

> Using an effective Hamiltonian derived from the QCD multipole expansion, we calculate the cross sections of gluo-dissociation of heavy quarkonia in the quark-gluon plasma, by including both the chromo-electric dipole ($E_1$) as well as the chromo-magnetic dipole ($M_1$) transition mechanisms. While the former allows to reproduce the results from operator-product-expansion calculations in the Coulomb approximation, the latter as a novel contribution is shown to be significant at low energies close to the threshold. Using thus obtained cross sections, we further carry out a full calculation of the gluo-dissociation rates for various charmonia and bottomonia within a non-relativitic in-medium potential model. The $M_1$ contribution turns out to be most prominent for the $J/ψ$ and accounts for $\sim 10\%-25\%$ of the total ($E1+M1$) dissociation rate at temperatures close to the transition temperature.

# Gluo-dissociation of Heavy Quarkonium in the Quark-Gluon Plasma Revisited

## Key claim
The chromo-magnetic dipole (M1) transition mechanism for gluo-dissociation of heavy quarkonia in the quark-gluon plasma, considered here for the first time, is shown to be significant at low gluon energies close to the scattering threshold; the M1 contribution accounts for approximately 10%–25% of the total (E1+M1) dissociation rate for the J/psi at temperatures close to the pseudo-critical temperature T_pc ~ 170 MeV.

## Method
The authors derive gluo-dissociation cross sections using an effective Hamiltonian from QCD multipole expansion, including both chromo-electric dipole (E1) and chromo-magnetic dipole (M1) transition mechanisms. They solve the Schrodinger equation with a temperature-dependent screened Cornell potential (parameters alpha=0.471, sigma=0.192 GeV^2, quark masses m_c=1.320 GeV, m_b=4.746 GeV) to obtain wave functions and binding energies for various charmonia and bottomonia, then compute cross sections and fold them with thermal gluon distributions (Bose-Einstein, with thermal gluon mass m_g(T)=sqrt(3/4)gT, g=2.3) to obtain dissociation rates.

## Key numbers
- M1 contribution to the J/psi dissociation rate accounts for ~10%–25% of the total (E1+M1) rate at temperatures close to T_pc ~ 170 MeV
- Cornell potential parameters: alpha=0.471, sigma=0.192 GeV^2; charm quark mass m_c=1.320 GeV, bottom quark mass m_b=4.746 GeV
- Debye screening mass functional form: m_D/T = -4.058 + 6.32*(T/T_c - 0.885)^0.1035, with T_c = 172.5 MeV
- Thermal gluon mass: m_g(T) = sqrt(3/4)gT with fixed g=2.3 for N_f=3 light flavors and N_c=3 colors
- The E1 cross section from full potential differs substantially (~50% for J/psi) from the "mixed" result using Coulomb potential with realistic binding energies

## Context
This work extends the seminal perturbative analysis of gluo-dissociation by Peskin (1979) using operator-product-expansion methods, and the QCD multipole expansion framework of Yan (1980). It goes beyond the usual Coulomb approximation by using a full in-medium potential model parameterized by Karsch (1987) with temperature-dependent screening. The results provide input for phenomenological studies of heavy quarkonium transport in the QGP, such as those by Zhao (2010) and Zhou (2014), and complement effective field theory approaches by Brambilla (2008, 2013) that distinguish gluo-dissociation (leading order) from quasi-free processes (next-to-leading order). The calculated rates are intended for use in fireball evolution models where the system spends significant time near T_c due to equation-of-state softening.
