# Empirical Determination of the Kaon Distribution Amplitude

> We propose a data-driven approach to extract the Kaon leading-twist distribution amplitude (DA) from empirical information on the ratio of the neutral-to-charged kaon electromagnetic form factors, $\mathcal{R}_K$. Our study employs a two-parameter representation of the DA at $ζ=2$ GeV, designed to capture the expected broadening and asymmetry of the distribution, as well as the soft endpoint behavior predicted by quantum chromodynamics (QCD). Our leading-order analysis of the latest experimental measurements of $\mathcal{R}_K$ reveals that the extracted DA exhibits a somewhat significant skewness, with the first symmetric moment approximately $\langle 1-2x \rangle_K= 0.082(7)$. On the other hand, the brodaness and general shape of the produced distributions show a reasonable consistency with contemporaty lattice and continuum QCD analyses. These findings highlight the importance of accurately determining the profile of the DA, especially the skewness and its relation to $SU_F(3)$ flavor symmetry breaking, as well as the inclusion of higher-order effects in the hard-scattering kernels for analyzing data at experimentally accessible scales.

# Empirical Determination of the Kaon Distribution Amplitude

## Key claim
The authors propose a data-driven extraction of the kaon leading-twist distribution amplitude (DA) from experimental measurements of the neutral-to-charged kaon electromagnetic form factor ratio, finding that at leading-order in perturbative QCD the extracted DA exhibits a somewhat significant skewness (first symmetric moment 0.082(7)), but caution that incorporating higher-order corrections in the hard-scattering kernels is expected to reduce this skewness and yield a more symmetric kaon DA.

## Method
The authors employ a two-parameter logarithmic representation of the kaon DA at a resolution scale of 2 GeV, designed to capture broadening, asymmetry, and the correct QCD endpoint behavior. They randomly scan the parameter space within physically motivated ranges informed by lattice QCD and continuum Schwinger methods, compute the neutral-to-charged kaon electromagnetic form factor ratio at leading-order using hard-scattering formulae with LO evolution, and retain parameter pairs yielding chi-squared per degree of freedom less than 2 when compared to BESIII experimental data in the momentum transfer range 12 to 25 GeV^2.

## Key numbers
- First symmetric moment of the kaon DA: ⟨1-2x⟩_K = 0.082(7) at ζ = 2 GeV
- Second moment: ⟨ξ²⟩_K = 0.239(9) at ζ = 2 GeV
- Peak position of the kaon DA: x^max = 0.37(1), reflecting a 25% deviation from the symmetric limit x=0.5
- Inverse moment ⟨1/x⟩_K = 4.03(18) at ζ = 2 GeV
- Inverse moment ⟨1/ȳ⟩_K = 2.89(11) at ζ = 2 GeV

## Context
This work extends data-driven approaches to meson distribution amplitudes, which have been extensively studied for the pion (Chang 2013, Cui 2020, Mikhailov 2016, Stefanis 2020) and for heavy quarkonia (Blossier 2024, Ding 2015, Serna 2020, Xu 2025). The analysis uses the recent BESIII measurement (2023) of the neutral-to-charged kaon form factor ratio, which aligns with an earlier measurement at 17.4 GeV² by Seth (2013). The results are compared with lattice QCD determinations (LatticeParton 2022, Zhang 2020, RQCD 2019) and continuum Schwinger method calculations using both rainbow-ladder (Shi 2014) and dynamical-beyond (Cui 2020) kernels. The work establishes a foundation for incorporating next-to-leading and next-to-next-to-leading order corrections (Chen 2023, Chen 2024) to the hard-scattering kernels, which are expected to reduce the extracted skewness and further isolate the role of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking versus explicit SU_F(3) flavor symmetry breaking in shaping the kaon wavefunction.
