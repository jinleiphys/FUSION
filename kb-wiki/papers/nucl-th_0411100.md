# Superscaling, Scaling Functions and Nucleon Momentum Distributions in Nuclei

> The scaling functions $f(ψ')$ and $F(y)$ from the $ψ'$- and $y$-scaling analyses of inclusive electron scattering from nuclei are explored within the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM). In addition to the CDFM formulation in which the local density distribution is used, we introduce a new equivalent formulation of the CDFM based on the one-body nucleon momentum distribution (NMD). Special attention is paid to the different ways in which the excitation energy of the residual system is taken into account in $y$- and $ψ'$-scaling. Both functions, $f(ψ')$ and $F(y)$, are calculated using different NMD's and are compared with the experimental data for a wide range of nuclei. The good description of the data for $y < 0$ and $ψ' < 0$ (including $ψ'< -1$) makes it possible to show the sensitivity of the calculated scaling functions to the peculiarities of the NMD's in different regions of momenta. It is concluded that the existing data on the $ψ'$- and $y$-scaling are informative for the NMD's at momenta not larger than $2.0 ÷2.5$ fm$^{-1}$. The CDFM allows us to study simultaneously on the same footing the role of both basic quantities, the momentum and density distributions, for the description of scaling and superscaling phenomena in nuclei.

## Key claim

The coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) can describe the experimental scaling functions \(f(\psi')\) and \(F(y)\) for negative scaling variables (including \(\psi' < -1\)) across a wide range of nuclei, but the existing data are informative for nucleon momentum distributions only at momenta not larger than \(2.0 \div 2.5\) fm\(^{-1}\).

## Method

The authors used the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) to calculate the \(\psi'\)-scaling function \(f(\psi')\) and the \(y\)-scaling function \(F(y)\) for inclusive electron scattering from nuclei. They introduced a new equivalent formulation of the CDFM based on the one-body nucleon momentum distribution (NMD) in addition to the existing formulation using the local density distribution. The scaling functions were computed as superpositions of relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) scaling functions weighted by the CDFM weight functions derived from either experimental charge densities or theoretical NMDs, and compared with experimental data for nuclei from \(^4\)He to \(^{197}\)Au.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{56}\)Fe, the parameters of the NMD extracted from \(y\)-scaling analysis are: \(b = 1.1838\) fm, \(C_1 = 0.30\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(C_2 = 0.11838\) fm, \(\alpha = 0.710\) fm\(^{-1}\), and \(a = 0.908\) fm.
- The Fermi momentum \(k_F\) in the RFG model ranges from 1.115 fm\(^{-1}\) for \(^{12}\)C to 1.216 fm\(^{-1}\) for \(^{197}\)Au.
- The CDFM calculations of \(f(\psi')\) were performed at transfer momentum \(q = 1560\) MeV/c for \(^4\)He, \(^{12}\)C, \(\(^{27}\)Al, and \(^{197}\)Au.
- The half-radius and diffuseness parameters for the symmetrized Fermi-type density of \(^4\)He are \(R_{1/2} = 1.710\) fm and \(b = 0.290\) fm; for \(^{12}\)C they are \(R_{1/2} = 2.470\) fm and \(b = 0.420\) fm; for \(^{27}\)Al they are \(R_{1/2} = 3.070\) fm and \(b = 0.519\) fm; for \(^{197}\)Au they are \(R_{1/2} = 6.419\) fm and \(b = 1.0\) fm.
- The existing data on \(\psi'\)- and \(y\)-scaling are informative for NMDs at momenta not larger than \(2.0 \div 2.5\) fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the CDFM approach to superscaling previously applied by Antonov et al. (2004) by adding calculations at \(q = 1560\) MeV/c and by explicitly relating the \(\psi'\)-scaling function to the nucleon momentum distribution. It also defines the \(y\)-scaling function \(F(y)\) within the CDFM and compares results using NMDs from the CDFM, from the \(y\)-scaling studies of Ciofi degli Atti and collaborators (1999, 1997), and from a light-front dynamics method. The study enables simultaneous analysis of both density and momentum distributions in scaling phenomena and provides a framework to extract NMD information from scaling data, while also showing that the RFG model fails for \(\psi' \leq -1\) where experimental data show nonzero scaling functions.
