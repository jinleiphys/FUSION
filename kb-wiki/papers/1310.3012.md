# The influence of magnetic field on the pion superfluidity and phase structure in the NJL model

> The influence of the magnetic field on the pion superfluidity and the phase structure is analyzed in the framework of the two-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio(NJL) model. To do this, we first derive the thermodynamic potential from the Lagrangian density of the NJL model in the mean field approximation. Using this thermodynamic potential, we get the gap equation of the chiral condensate and the pion condensate. The effect of external magnetic field on the pion condensate is not simple promotion or suppression, which we will discuss in detail in the paper. It is shown that the tricritical point on the pion superfluidity phase transition line moves to the space with smaller isospin chemical potential and higher temperature when the external magnetic field becomes stronger. The influence of external magnetic field on the chiral condensate is also studied.

## Key claim

The effect of an external magnetic field on pion condensation is not simply promotional or suppressive; it depends on the value of the isospin chemical potential, with suppression observed at low isospin chemical potential (e.g., 100 MeV) and promotion at high isospin chemical potential (e.g., 400 MeV).

## Method

The authors used the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field approximation, deriving the thermodynamic potential at finite temperature, finite isospin chemical potential, and in the presence of a constant external magnetic field. They solved the coupled gap equations for the chiral condensate sigma and the pion condensate pi numerically, using a hard momentum cutoff and summing over Landau levels. The phase diagrams were constructed by locating where the pion condensate vanishes as a function of temperature, isospin chemical potential, and magnetic field strength.

## Key numbers

- At zero magnetic field and T=100 MeV, the critical isospin chemical potential for pion superfluidity onset is approximately mu_I^c = m_pi/2 ≈ 70 MeV.
- At T=100 MeV, the maximum pion condensate gap is 1.1 sigma_0 at eB=0, and increases to 1.5 sigma_0 at eB=30 m_pi^2.
- At eB=0, the tricritical point in the T-mu_I plane is approximately at (T, mu_I) = (456, 130) MeV.
- The magnetic field magnitudes studied include eB = 0, 20 m_pi^2, and 30 m_pi^2, with m_pi = 139 MeV.
- The model parameters used are: m_0 = 5 MeV, Lambda = 650.7 MeV, G = 5.01 GeV^{-2}, and |<bar{psi}_u psi_u>|^{1/3} = -250 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior NJL model studies of pion superfluidity at finite isospin chemical potential (He et al., no year given) by adding an external magnetic field, and complements studies of magnetic field effects on chiral condensation (Klimenko and Gusynin, no year given) and on color superconductivity (Noronha et al., Paulucci et al., Ferrer et al., no years given). The results enable further investigation of QCD phase structure under the combined conditions of strong magnetic fields and isospin asymmetry, relevant for heavy-ion collisions and compact stars.
