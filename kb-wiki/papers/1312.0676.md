# Dilepton emission in high-energy heavy-ion collisions with viscous hydrodynamics

> The invariant mass spectrum and the elliptic flow of lepton pairs produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC are studied with viscous hydrodynamics. The effects of viscous corrections on dilepton observables are explored. The lepton pairs originating from charm quarks evolving in the viscous background are seen to be a good probe of quark energy loss and gain, as quantified by the dilepton spectrum and by the dilepton elliptic flow.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that viscous corrections to dilepton emission rates are negligible for hadronic-phase dileptons but become significant for QGP-phase dileptons at intermediate invariant masses, with the caveat that the hadronic-phase viscous corrections are derived using a simplified formalism where the proportionality constant $C_a$ is set to 1 for all particle species.

## Method

The authors use the 3+1D relativistic viscous hydrodynamics code MUSIC with $\eta/s = 1/4\pi$ to simulate Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}=200$ A GeV. They compute dilepton production from three sources: Born approximation for QGP thermal emission, vector meson dominance model for hadronic thermal emission (including $\rho$, $\omega$, $\phi$ mesons), and charm quark decays evolved via relativistic Langevin dynamics with spatial diffusion coefficient $D_c = 3/(2\pi T)$. Viscous corrections to the thermal rates are implemented by modifying the equilibrium distribution functions with a term proportional to the shear stress tensor $\pi^{\mu\nu}$.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio used is $\eta/s = 1/4\pi$
- The initial energy density in the viscous case is 90% of the inviscid case value to account for entropy buildup
- The crossover temperature from hadronic to QGP degrees of freedom is centered at approximately 175 MeV
- For the QGP Born rate, the proportionality constant for the Fermi-Dirac distribution correction is $C_q = 7\pi^4/(675\zeta(5)) \approx 0.97$
- Viscous effects on the bulk evolution in the hadronic phase raise the dilepton yield by approximately 60% at momenta from 3 to 4 GeV at invariant mass $M = m_\rho$
- The matching between low-energy and high-energy forward scattering amplitudes occurs at $E_V - m_V \sim 4$ GeV for pions and $E_V - m_V \sim 1$ GeV for nucleons
- Charm quarks decay semi-leptonically into an electron with a branching fraction of approximately 10%

## Context

This work extends the viscous hydrodynamic treatment of electromagnetic probes previously applied to real photons by Dion et al. (2011) and others, now applied to dilepton observables. The hadronic rates build on the many-body approach of Rapp (1999, 2009) and the forward scattering amplitude formalism of Eletsky et al. (2001). The charm quark treatment uses the Langevin dynamics framework of Moore and Teaney (2004). The results enable quantitative extraction of the shear viscosity coefficient from dilepton measurements at RHIC, complementing similar efforts using hadronic observables by Gale et al. (2013).
