# Three-body scattering and quantization conditions from $S$ matrix unitarity

> Two methodologies have been presented in the literature which connect relativistic three-particle scattering amplitudes with lattice QCD spectra -- the ``relativistic effective field theory'' approach and the ``finite-volume unitarity'' method. While both methods have been shown to be equivalent in various works, it has not been shown how to arrive at the relativistic effective field theory results directly from $S$ matrix unitarity. In this work, we provide a simple proof of the relativistic effective field theory form of the scattering equations directly from unitarity. Motivated by the finite-volume unitarity approach, we then postulate a set of quantization conditions which relate the finite-volume energy spectra to the $K$ matrices which drive the short-distance physics in the scattering equations, obtaining all previously known results for three identical particles. This work also presents new relations which provide a new pathway to generalize the results to arbitrary systems.

## Key claim

This work provides a direct proof that the relativistic effective field theory (RFT) form of three-body scattering equations can be derived solely from S matrix unitarity, without requiring the Feynman diagram summation used in the original RFT approach. The derivation is conditional on restricting the total energy to the elastic region above three-particle threshold and below the first inelastic threshold, where only three-particle intermediate states can go on-shell.

## Method

The author starts from the S matrix unitarity constraint \(T - T^\dagger = i T^\dagger T\) for three-to-three scattering of identical spinless particles, and constructs on-shell representations of the scattering amplitude in a pair-spectator basis where two particles are coupled to definite angular momentum \(\ell\) while the third is a spectator. By isolating the known singularities associated with on-shell two-particle subprocesses and particle exchange, the unitarity constraint is satisfied by a linear integral equation driven by an unknown real K matrix that encodes short-distance physics. The resulting infinite-volume scattering equations are then embedded in a finite cubic volume with periodic boundary conditions, replacing continuous integrals over spectator momenta with discrete sums over quantized momenta, yielding quantization conditions whose determinant vanishes at the finite-volume energy eigenvalues.

## Key numbers

- The total energy is restricted to the kinematic domain \((3m)^2 \le s < (5m)^2\) where only three-particle states can go on-shell, with \(s = P^2\) being the invariant mass squared of the three-particle system.
- The pair invariant mass \(\sigma_k = (P - k)^2\) takes values \((2m)^2 \le \sigma_k \le (\sqrt{s} - m)^2\) in a physical scattering process.
- The relative momentum of the pair in its center-of-momentum frame is \(q_k^\star = \sqrt{\sigma_k/4 - m^2}\).
- Quantized momenta in the finite cubic volume are given by \(\mathbf{k} = (2\pi/L)\mathbf{n}\) where \(\mathbf{n} \in \mathbb{Z}^3\).

## Context

This work unifies two previously separate approaches for connecting three-particle scattering amplitudes to lattice QCD spectra: the relativistic effective field theory (RFT) method developed by Hansen and Sharpe (2014-2015) and the finite-volume unitarity (FVU) method developed by Mai, Doring, and collaborators (2017-2018). While earlier work by Jackura (2019) and Blanton (2020) showed the equivalence of the two frameworks by fixing the three-to-three amplitude as the observable, this paper provides the first direct derivation of the RFT equations from unitarity alone. The new integral equation and quantization condition presented here enable a more direct computational approach for numerical applications and provide a pathway to generalize the results to systems with arbitrary numbers of incoming and outgoing particles.
