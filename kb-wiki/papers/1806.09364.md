# Weak pion production off the nucleon in covariant chiral perturbation theory

> Weak pion production off the nucleon at low energies has been systematically investigated in manifestly relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory with explicit inclusion of the $Δ$(1232) resonance. Most of the involved low-energy constants have been previously determined in other processes such as pion-nucleon elastic scattering and electromagnetic pion production off the nucleon. For numerical estimates, the few remaining constants are set to be of natural size. As a result, the total cross sections for single pion production on neutrons and protons, induced either by neutrino or antineutrino, are predicted. Our results are consistent with the scarce existing experimental data except in the $ν_μn\to μ^-nπ^+$ channel, where higher-order contributions might still be significant. The $Δ$ resonance mechanisms lead to sizeable contributions in all channels, especially in $ν_μp\to μ^- pπ^+$, even though the considered energies are close to the production threshold. The present study provides a well founded low-energy benchmark for phenomenological models aimed at the description of weak pion production processes in the broad kinematic range of interest for current and future neutrino-oscillation experiments.

## Key claim

The authors present the first systematic study of weak pion production off nucleons up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in covariant chiral perturbation theory with explicit inclusion of the Δ(1232) resonance, but the predictive power is limited to energies near threshold (invariant mass of the final πN system ≤ 1.18 GeV, well below the Δ-resonance peak), and the few remaining undetermined low-energy constants are set to natural size rather than fitted to data.

## Method

The hadronic transition amplitude for charged-current weak pion production was calculated in manifestly relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory using the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme, with power counting following the δ-counting rule where the Δ-nucleon mass difference is treated as order p^(1/2). Tree-level diagrams up to O(p^3) were computed alongside 89 one-loop diagrams generated from topologies with nucleon, pion, and Δ(1232) propagators, using low-energy constants previously determined from pion-nucleon elastic scattering and electromagnetic pion production. The total cross sections for single pion production on neutrons and protons induced by neutrinos and antineutrinos were then predicted.

## Key numbers

- The energy range studied extends from the production threshold E_ν^(thr.) ≈ 276.5 MeV (for μ leptons) to E_ν^(max) ≈ 415 MeV, corresponding to Q^2 < 0.02 GeV^2 and pion momentum < 0.18 GeV
- The invariant mass of the final πN system W ≤ 1.18 GeV, well below the Δ(1232) resonance peak at approximately 1.232 GeV
- The Δ-nucleon mass difference δ = m_Δ - m_N ≈ 300 MeV is used as an expansion parameter in the δ-counting scheme

## Context

This work extends the systematic application of chiral perturbation theory to weak pion production, which had previously been limited to leading-order tree-level amplitudes and a few low-energy theorems with one-loop corrections in the heavy-baryon formalism by Bernard et al. (1993). The results provide a low-energy benchmark for phenomenological models such as the HNV model of Hernandez et al. (2007), which achieved agreement with bubble chamber data but introduced tensions in the N-Δ(1232) axial coupling C_5^A relative to the Goldberger-Treiman value. The present ChPT calculation fulfills perturbative unitarity and Watson's theorem by construction, unlike most phenomenological models that require ad hoc form factors, and can serve as a foundation for improving neutrino-oscillation analyses that currently assume 20-30% errors for single-pion production.
