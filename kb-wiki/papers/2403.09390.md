# Curvature of the chiral phase transition line from the magnetic equation of state of (2+1)-flavor QCD

> We analyze the dependence of the chiral phase transition temperature on baryon number and strangeness chemical potentials by calculating the leading order curvature coefficients in the light and strange quark flavor basis as well as in the conserved charge ($B, S$) basis. Making use of scaling properties of the magnetic equation of state (MEoS) and including diagonal as well as off-diagonal contributions in the expansion of the energy-like scaling variable that enters the parametrization of the MEoS, allows to explore the variation of $T_c(μ_B,μ_S) = T_c ( 1 - (κ_2^B \hatμ_B^2 + κ_2^S \hatμ_S^2 + 2κ_{11}^{BS} \hatμ_B \hatμ_S))$ along different lines in the $(μ_B,μ_S)$ plane. On lattices with fixed cut-off in units of temperature, $aT=1/8$, we find $κ_2^B=0.015(1)$, $κ_2^S=0.0124(5)$ and $κ_{11}^{BS}=-0.0050(7)$. We show that the chemical potential dependence along the line of vanishing strangeness chemical potential is about 10\% larger than along the strangeness neutral line. The latter differs only by about $3\%$ from the curvature on a line of vanishing strange quark chemical potential, $μ_s=0$. We also show that close to the chiral limit the strange quark mass contributes like an energy-like variable in scaling relations for pseudo-critical temperatures. The chiral phase transition temperature decreases with decreasing strange quark mass, $T_c(m_s)= T_c(m_s^{\rm phy}) (1 - 0.097(2) (m_s-m_s^{\rm phys})/m_s^{\rm phy}+{\cal O}((Δm_s)^2)$.

## Key claim

The chiral phase transition temperature in (2+1)-flavor QCD depends on baryon number and strangeness chemical potentials through leading-order curvature coefficients, which the authors determine on lattices with a fixed cutoff (aT=1/8) rather than in the continuum limit; the results are therefore subject to cutoff effects that have not yet been extrapolated away.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with the HISQ action on Nτ=8 lattices at five light-to-strange quark mass ratios (H=1/20, 1/27, 1/40, 1/80, 1/160) and extracted the chiral phase transition temperature and curvature coefficients by fitting the magnetic equation of state (MEoS) to the renormalized order parameter M and its derivatives, using scaling functions of the 3d O(2) universality class. They computed diagonal and off-diagonal curvature coefficients in both the quark flavor basis (μℓ, μs) and the conserved charge basis (μB, μS) via Taylor expansion of the free energy at vanishing chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- κ₂ᴮ = 0.015(1) (curvature coefficient for baryon chemical potential in the conserved charge basis, on Nτ=8 lattices)
- κ₂ˢ = 0.0124(5) (curvature coefficient for strangeness chemical potential in the conserved charge basis)
- κ₁₁ᴮˢ = -0.0050(7) (off-diagonal curvature coefficient coupling baryon and strangeness chemical potentials)
- The chemical potential dependence along the line of vanishing strangeness chemical potential is about 10% larger than along the strangeness neutral line
- The curvature along the strangeness neutral line differs by only about 3% from the curvature on the line μs=0
- The chiral phase transition temperature depends on the strange quark mass as Tc(ms) = Tc(ms^phys) (1 - 0.097(2) (ms - ms^phys)/ms^phys + O((Δms)²))
- The chiral phase transition temperature at vanishing chemical potentials on Nτ=8 lattices is Tc = 143.8(2) MeV (from a fit to H=1/40 and 1/80 data in the temperature interval [140:148] MeV)

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD determinations of the curvature of the pseudo-critical line (Bazavov 2018, Borsanyi 2020) by computing both diagonal and off-diagonal curvature coefficients in the conserved charge basis and by analyzing the chiral limit using scaling properties of the magnetic equation of state. The results enable improved constraints on the location of the critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram, as the chiral phase transition temperature provides an upper bound for the temperature at the critical endpoint (Karsch 2019). The study also shows that the strange quark mass acts as an energy-like variable in scaling relations near the chiral limit, which will inform future continuum-extrapolated calculations.
