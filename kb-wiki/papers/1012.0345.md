# Dissipative processes in superfluid neutron stars

> We present some results about a novel damping mechanism of r-mode oscillations in neutron stars due to processes that change the number of protons, neutrons and electrons. Deviations from equilibrium of the number densities of the various species lead to the appearance in the Euler equations of the system of a dissipative mechanism, the so-called rocket effect. The evolution of the r-mode oscillations of a rotating neutron star are influenced by the rocket effect and we present estimates of the corresponding damping timescales. In the description of the system we employ a two-fluid model, with one fluid consisting of all the charged components locked together by the electromagnetic interaction, while the second fluid consists of superfluid neutrons. Both components can oscillate however the rocket effect can only efficiently damp the countermoving r-mode oscillations, with the two fluids oscillating out of phase. In our analysis we include the mutual friction dissipative process between the neutron superfluid and the charged component. We neglect the interaction between the two r-mode oscillations as well as effects related with the crust of the star. Moreover, we use a simplified model of neutron star assuming a uniform mass distribution.

## Key claim

The paper presents a novel damping mechanism for r-mode oscillations in superfluid neutron stars, the "rocket effect", which arises from processes that change the number of protons, neutrons, and electrons (such as direct Urca processes), but this mechanism can only efficiently damp the countermoving (superfluid) r-mode oscillations where the two fluids oscillate out of phase, not the standard comoving r-modes.

## Method

The authors employ a two-fluid model of a neutron star, with one fluid consisting of all charged components (protons and electrons locked together by electromagnetic interaction) and the second fluid consisting of superfluid neutrons. They linearize the hydrodynamical equations around an equilibrium configuration of a uniformly rotating neutron star with constant angular velocity, assuming a simplified toy-model star with uniform density, no crust, and uniform mass distribution. The damping timescale from the rocket term is computed by assuming that changes in particle number densities arise from out-of-equilibrium direct Urca processes, and the results are compared with the growth timescale from gravitational wave radiation to determine the instability window.

## Key numbers

- The toy-model neutron star has uniform density ρ = 2.5 ρ₀, where ρ₀ = 2.8 × 10¹⁴ g cm⁻³ is the nuclear saturation density
- The star radius is R = 10 km and mass M ≈ 1.47 M⊙
- The critical proton fraction for direct Urca processes is x_p^c = 1/9
- The typical timescale of gravitational wave emission is of the order of tens of seconds
- The damping time associated with the rocket term is computed for temperature T = 10⁹ K and critical superfluid temperature T_c = 10¹⁰ K
- Mutual friction is evaluated for entrainment parameter values ε = 0.0002 and ε = 0.002

## Context

This work extends the study of r-mode oscillations in superfluid neutron stars by introducing a dissipative mechanism not previously considered in this context, the rocket effect, which arises from non-conservation of individual particle species numbers. The analysis builds on the two-fluid model of Prix (2002) and the r-mode stability analysis of Haskell (2009), and it specifically considers direct Urca processes as identified by Lattimer (1991) for densities exceeding nuclear saturation density. The results enable more precise constraints on neutron star structure and rotation frequencies by showing that the rocket effect significantly reduces the instability window for superfluid r-modes compared to calculations considering only shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and mutual friction.
