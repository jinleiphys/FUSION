# Quasideuteron configurations in 46V and 58Cu

> The data on low spin states in the odd-odd nuclei 46V and 58Cu investigated with the 46Ti(p,ngamma)46V, 32S(16O,pn)46V and 58Ni(p,ngamma)58Cu reactions at the FN-TANDEM accelerator in Cologne are reported. The states containing large quasideuteron components are identified from the strong isovector M1 transitions, from shell model calculations and from experimental data for low-lying states.

# Quasideuteron configurations in 46V and 58Cu

## Key claim

Strong isovector M1 transitions between low-lying T=0 and T=1 states in odd-odd N=Z nuclei can be used to identify quasideuteron configurations, but the strength of these transitions is fragmented in 46V due to coupling to a collective deformed core and in 58Cu due to mixing with other non-collective two-nucleon configurations, rather than appearing as a single strong transition as in lighter nuclei.

## Method

Low-spin states in 46V were populated via 46Ti(p,nγ)46V and 32S(16O,pn)46V reactions, and in 58Cu via 58Ni(p,nγ)58Cu, all using the FN-TANDEM accelerator at Cologne. Gamma-ray singles and γγ-coincidence spectra were measured with Compton-suppressed germanium detectors; lifetimes were extracted from the data and absolute B(M1) and B(E2) values or lower limits were determined for 12 transitions in 46V. Shell model calculations in the full pf-shell (KB3 and FPD6 interactions) for 46V and in a restricted p3/2, f5/2, p1/2 model space with Surface Delta Interaction for 58Cu were compared to the experimental results.

## Key numbers

- In 46V, the experimental B(M1; 0⁺₁ → 1⁺₁) is ≥2.31 μN², compared to a shell model (KB3) value of 3.80 μN²; the estimated total strength concentrated in this transition is 6(2) μN².
- In 46V, the experimental B(E2; 2⁺₁ → 0⁺₁) is 137(35) e²fm⁴, corresponding to a deformation β ≈ 0.23 for the K=0, T=1 band.
- In 46V, the experimental B(E2; 4⁺₂ → 2⁺₁) lower limit is ≥169 e²fm⁴, consistent with the predicted value of 185(47) e²fm⁴ from the measured 2⁺₁ → 0⁺₁ strength.
- In 46V, the experimental B(M1; 3⁺₂ → 2⁺₁) is 1.98(71) μN² (shell model KB3: 1.25 μN²).
- In 58Cu, the lifetime of the 1⁺₂ state is τ = 114(29) fs, giving B(M1; 0⁺₁ → 1⁺₂) = 2.5(5) μN².
- In 58Cu, the B(E2; 2⁺₂ → 0⁺₁) value is comparable to the B(E2; 2⁺₁ → 0⁺₁) = 130(7) e²fm⁴ for the isospin triplet partner 58Ni.
- The hypothetical deuteron with a bound Jπ=0⁺, T=1 state would have B(M1; 0⁺ → 1⁺) = 16 μN²; for one proton and one neutron in the f7/2 orbital the predicted strength is 18 μN²; for the p3/2 orbital the total M1 0⁺₁ → 1⁺ strength is 13 μN² (or 7 μN² with quenched spin g-factors by a factor of 0.7).

## Context

This work extends the study of quasideuteron configurations previously identified in lighter odd-odd N=Z nuclei such as 6Li, 18F, 42Sc (where a single strong M1 transition is observed) and in 10B, 22Na, 26Al (where fragmentation occurs due to collectivity) as reported by Lisetskiy (1999) and Lisetskiy (2000). The present results for 46V and 58Cu demonstrate two distinct fragmentation mechanisms: strong coupling to a deformed core in 46V (with deformation β ≈ 0.23 for both K=0 and K=3 bands) and mixing with closely lying orbitals in the near-spherical 58Cu. The findings enable future accurate lifetime measurements in other odd-odd N=Z nuclei to further clarify the structure of quasideuteron states and the competition between T=0 and T=1 pn interactions.
