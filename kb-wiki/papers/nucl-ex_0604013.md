# Lowest Q^2 Measurement of the gamma*p -> Delta Reaction: Probing the Pionic Contribution

> To determine nonspherical angular momentum amplitudes in hadrons at long ranges (low Q^2), data were taken for the p(\vec{e},e'p)π^0 reaction in the Delta region at Q^2=0.060 (GeV/c)^2 utilizing the magnetic spectrometers of the A1 Collaboration at MAMI. The results for the dominant transition magnetic dipole amplitude and the quadrupole to dipole ratios at W=1232 MeV are: M_{1+}^{3/2} = (40.33 +/- 0.63_{stat+syst} +/- 0.61_{model}) (10^{-3}/m_{π^+}),Re(E_{1+}^{3/2}/M_{1+}^{3/2}) = (-2.28 +/- 0.29_{stat+syst} +/- 0.20_{model})%, and Re(S_{1+}^{3/2}/M_{1+}^{3/2}) = (-4.81 +/- 0.27_{stat+syst} +/- 0.26_{model})%. These disagree with predictions of constituent quark models but are in reasonable agreement with lattice calculations with non-linear (chiral) pion mass extrapolations, with chiral effective field theory, and with dynamical models with pion cloud effects. These results confirm the dominance, and general Q^2 variation, of the pionic contribution at large distances.

## Key claim

At the lowest four-momentum transfer squared ever probed in modern electroproduction of the Delta resonance (Q^2 = 0.060 (GeV/c)^2), the measured quadrupole-to-dipole ratios disagree with constituent quark model predictions but agree with chiral effective field theory and dynamical models that include pion cloud effects, confirming the dominance of the pionic contribution at large distances.

## Method

The A1 Collaboration at MAMI measured the p(e,e'p)pi^0 reaction using two magnetic spectrometers with a longitudinally polarized 855 MeV electron beam on a liquid hydrogen target. Sequential measurements were taken at W=1221 MeV and Q^2=0.060 (GeV/c)^2 at three pion center-of-mass angles (143, 156, and 180 degrees), with the proton arm moved through three phi* settings for non-parallel kinematics. The five-fold differential cross sections were decomposed into partial cross sections (sigma_T, sigma_L, sigma_LT, sigma_TT, sigma_LT'), and the three resonant multipoles (M_{1+}^{3/2}, E_{1+}^{3/2}, S_{1+}^{3/2}) were extracted by fitting four reaction models (SAID, MAID 2003, Sato-Lee, DMT) to the data.

## Key numbers

- M_{1+}^{3/2} = (40.33 +/- 0.63_{stat+syst} +/- 0.61_{model}) (10^{-3}/m_{pi^+}) at W=1232 MeV and Q^2=0.060 (GeV/c)^2
- Re(E_{1+}^{3/2}/M_{1+}^{3/2}) = (-2.28 +/- 0.29_{stat+syst} +/- 0.20_{model})% (the electric quadrupole to dipole ratio, EMR)
- Re(S_{1+}^{3/2}/M_{1+}^{3/2}) = (-4.81 +/- 0.27_{stat+syst} +/- 0.26_{model})% (the Coulomb quadrupole to dipole ratio, CMR)
- Beam polarization was approximately 75%, beam energy had absolute uncertainty of +/- 160 keV with 30 keV spread (FWHM)
- Overall systematic error for cross sections was 3 to 4%, verified by elastic electron-proton scattering agreement at the 3% level

## Context

This work extends previous electroproduction measurements of the gamma* N -> Delta transition at JLab (Joo, Frolov) for Q^2 from 0.4 to 4.0 (GeV/c)^2, at Bates (Warren, Mertz, Kunz, Sparveris) at Q^2=0.127 (GeV/c)^2, and at Mainz (Pospischil, Bartsch, Elsner) at Q^2=0.127 and 0.20 (GeV/c)^2, by probing the lowest Q^2 value yet achieved. The results disagree with constituent quark models (Capstick and Karl, hypercentral quark model) which underpredict the M1 amplitude by about 30% and underestimate the EMR and CMR by an order of magnitude, but agree with chiral effective field theory calculations (Pascalutsa and Vanderhaeghen, Gail and Hemmert) and dynamical models with pion cloud effects (Sato-Lee, DMT). The measurement enables tests of the next order in chiral perturbation theory and motivates lattice QCD calculations with lighter pion masses.
