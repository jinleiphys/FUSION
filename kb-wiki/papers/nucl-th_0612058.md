# Equation of State for Hot and Dense Matter: $σ$-$ω$-$ρ$ Model with Scaled Hadron Masses and Couplings

> The proposed earlier relativistic mean-field model with hadron masses and coupling constants depending on the $σ$-meson field is generalized to finite temperatures. Within this approach we simulate the in-medium behavior of the hadron masses motivated by the Brown-Rho scaling. The high-lying baryon resonances and boson excitations as well as excitations of the $σ$, $ω$ and $ρ$ fields interacting via mean fields are incorporated into this scheme. Thermodynamic properties of hot and dense hadronic matter are elaborated with the constructed equation of state. Even at zero baryon density, effective masses of $σ$-$ω$-$ρ$-$N$ excitations abruptly drop down for $T\gsim 170$ MeV and reach zero at a critical temperature $T=T_{cσ}\sim 210$ MeV. Below $T_{cσ}$ (at $T\sim 190$ MeV) the specific heat gets a peak like at crossover. We demonstrate that our EoS can be matched with that computed on the lattice for high temperatures provided the baryon resonance couplings with nucleon are partially suppressed. In this case the quark liquid would masquerade as the hadron one. The model is applied to description of heavy ion collisions in a broad collision energy range. It might be especially helpful for studying phase diagram in the region near possible phase transitions.

## Key claim

The paper extends the zero-temperature KVOR relativistic mean-field model to finite temperatures by incorporating hadron masses and couplings that scale with the σ-meson field, motivated by Brown-Rho scaling. The authors claim that even at zero baryon density, effective masses of σ-ω-ρ-N excitations drop abruptly above T ≈ 170 MeV and reach zero at a critical temperature T_cσ ≈ 210 MeV, with a specific heat peak at T ≈ 190 MeV resembling a crossover, but they note that matching lattice QCD results requires partial suppression of baryon resonance couplings with nucleons.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model where hadron masses and coupling constants depend on the σ-meson field through a universal scaling function Φ = 1 − f, with f = g_σN χ_σ σ/m_N. They include baryons (N, Δ, Λ, Σ, Ξ, Σ*, Ξ*, Ω), meson excitations (σ, ω, ρ), and (quasi)Goldstone bosons (π, K, η) plus higher-mass partners (K*, η', φ), all treated in the quasiparticle approximation. The energy density is minimized with respect to the σ, ω, and ρ mean fields to obtain the equation of state at finite temperature and baryon density, with boson excitations treated perturbatively.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for σ-ω-ρ-N effective masses reaching zero: T_cσ ≈ 210 MeV
- Onset of abrupt effective mass drop: T ≳ 170 MeV
- Specific heat peak temperature (crossover-like): T ≈ 190 MeV
- Pion decay constant used: f_π = 93 MeV
- ηN sigma term estimated from scattering data: Σ_ηN ≈ 280 ± 130 MeV
- ηN coupling constant: κ_ηN ≈ 0.40 ± 0.08 fm
- Vacuum Δ-isobar mass width: Γ_Δ^max ≈ 115 MeV

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature KVOR model (Khvorostukhin, Toneev, Voskresensky, 2004) to finite temperatures, which was shown to satisfy most constraints from atomic nuclei, neutron stars, and heavy-ion collisions among phenomenological models tested by the "Army" collaboration (2005). The model implements Brown-Rho scaling (Brown and Rho, 1991) where hadron masses and couplings decrease with density, in contrast to other RMF models that use constant or increasing effective meson masses (e.g., HP, FST). The resulting equation of state can be matched to lattice QCD calculations at high temperatures and is intended for studying the QCD phase diagram near possible phase transitions, particularly for applications to heavy-ion collisions at FAIR, RHIC low-energy campaign, and the NICA collider.
