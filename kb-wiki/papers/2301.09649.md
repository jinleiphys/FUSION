# Understanding Jet Charge

> The jet charge is an old observable that has proven uniquely useful for discrimination of jets initiated by different flavors of light quarks, for example. In this Letter, we propose an approach to understanding the jet charge by establishing simple, robust assumptions that hold to good approximation non-perturbatively, such as isospin conservation and large particle multiplicity in the jets, forgoing any attempt at a perturbative analysis. From these assumptions, the jet charge distribution with fixed particle multiplicity takes the form of a Gaussian by the central limit theorem and whose mean and variance are related to fractional-power moments of single particle energy distributions. These results make several concrete predictions for the scaling of the jet charge with the multiplicity, explaining many of the results already in the literature, and new results we validate in Monte Carlo simulation.

## Key claim

The jet charge distribution, conditioned on fixed particle multiplicity, takes a Gaussian form whose mean and variance are related to fractional-power moments of single-particle energy distributions, based solely on non-perturbative assumptions (isospin conservation, large multiplicity, identical independent particle production) without any perturbative QCD input.

## Method

The authors assume that hadrons in a jet are produced through identical, independent processes with large multiplicity, that only pions (pi+, pi-, pi0) are present, and that SU(2) isospin is exact. From these assumptions, the central limit theorem gives a Gaussian jet charge distribution. The mean and variance are expressed in terms of moments of the single-particle energy fraction distribution p(z|N), which is expanded about its mean value 1/N. The resulting expressions predict scaling of the jet charge variance with particle multiplicity N and the exponent kappa. These predictions are validated using Pythia 8.303 simulations of pp collisions at 5.02 TeV with anti-kT jets of radius R=0.4 and transverse momentum above 120 GeV.

## Key numbers

* Jet charge variance for inclusive jets: sigma_kappa^2 = (2/3) N^(1-2kappa) (1 + kappa(2kappa-1) sigma_z^2 N^2 + ...), where sigma_z^2 is the variance of the single-particle energy fraction distribution
* For up quark jets, mean jet charge <Q_kappa>_u = (2/3) <z^kappa>; for down quark jets, <Q_kappa>_d = -(1/3) <z^kappa>
* The discrimination power metric eta(kappa) = (<Q_kappa>_u - <Q_kappa>_d)^2 / sigma_kappa^2 = (1/(2/3)N) (1 - kappa^2 sigma_z^2 N^2 + ...)
* Simulated events: pp collisions at 5.02 TeV center-of-mass energy, jets with R=0.4, transverse momentum pT > 120 GeV, charged particle multiplicity fixed to Nch=12 for the mode of the distribution

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of jet charge beyond perturbative approaches pioneered by Waalewijn (2012) and Krohn (2012), who developed non-linear evolution equations for the jet charge distribution. It also relates to machine learning studies by Lee (2022) showing jet charge provides the sole separation power for up versus down quark discrimination. The results explain previously observed features, such as why kappa=0.5 gives reduced dependence on jet energy (Waalewijn 2012, Krohn 2012), and predict that binning jet charge measurements by charged particle multiplicity will reveal scaling properties and improve discrimination performance at the LHC and future colliders like the Electron-Ion Collider.
