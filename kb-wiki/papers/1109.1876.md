# Resonance Contribution to Radiative Neutron Capture on Lithium-7

> Using halo effective field theory, we provide a model-independent calculation of the radiative neutron capture on lithium-7 over an energy range where the contribution from the 3+ resonance becomes important. One finds that a satisfactory description of the capture reaction, in the present single-particle approximation, suggests the use of a resonance width about three times larger than the experimental value. We also present power counting arguments that establish a hierarchy for electromagnetic one- and two-body currents.

## Key claim
Using halo effective field theory, the paper provides a model-independent calculation of radiative neutron capture on lithium-7 that includes the 3+ resonance contribution, but finds that a satisfactory description of the capture reaction in the single-particle approximation requires using a resonance width about three times larger than the experimental value.

## Method
The authors construct a halo effective field theory with neutron and lithium-7 core fields, treating the core as inert and structureless at leading order. They match the EFT couplings to effective range expansion parameters for s-wave and p-wave elastic scattering, using the known resonance energy and width to determine the 3+ resonance parameters. The capture cross section is calculated from E1 transitions from initial s-wave states to the 2+ ground state and 1+ excited state, plus M1 transitions from the 3+ resonance to the ground state, with two-body current contributions included.

## Key numbers
- s-wave scattering lengths: a0^(2) = -3.63 ± 0.05 fm for the 5S2 channel, a0^(1) = 0.87 ± 0.07 fm for the 3S1 channel
- Binding momenta: γ(2+) ≈ 57.8 MeV for the 2+ ground state, γ(1+) ≈ 41.6 MeV for the 1+ excited state
- Thermal capture cross sections: σ(2+) = 40.56 mb to the ground state, σ(1+) = 4.80 mb to the excited state (Lynn 1991)
- Effective range from thermal fit: r1(2+) = -1.47 fm^-1 for the 2+ state, r1(1+) ≈ -1.93 fm^-1 for the 1+ state
- Resonance parameters (experimental central values): Er = 0.222 MeV, Γr = 0.031 MeV in the center-of-mass frame
- From experimental resonance parameters: (|1/a1(3)|^1/3, r1(3)) = (46.38, -547.1) MeV
- From Huang's Woods-Saxon synthetic data: Er = 0.228 MeV, Γr = 0.115 MeV, giving (|1/a1(3)|^1/3, r1(3)) = (30.69, -154.3) MeV
- Branching ratio for E1 capture to the 1+ excited state: 0.106 (Lynn 1991)

## Context
This work extends the earlier halo EFT calculation by Rupak (2011) that considered only the leading E1 transition from s-wave continuum to the p-wave 8Li ground state. The present paper explicitly includes capture to the first excited state of 8Li, the contribution from the low-lying p-wave resonance at approximately 0.22 MeV, and elaborates on the power counting for two-body currents. The formalism sets the stage for handling the more weakly bound p-7Be mirror system, which is crucial for determining the 7Be(p,γ)8B reaction rate that governs the solar neutrino flux from 8B decay detected by SNO and Super-K.
