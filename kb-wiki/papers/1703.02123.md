# Combining symmetry collective states with coupled cluster theory: Lessons from the Agassi model Hamiltonian

> The failures of single-reference coupled cluster for strongly correlated many-body systems is flagged at the mean-field level by the spontaneous breaking of one or more physical symmetries of the Hamiltonian. Restoring the symmetry of the mean-field determinant by projection reveals that coupled cluster fails because it factorizes high-order excitation amplitudes incorrectly. However, symmetry-projected mean-field wave functions do not account sufficiently for dynamic (or weak) correlation. Here we pursue a merger of symmetry projection and coupled cluster theory, following previous work along these lines that utilized the simple Lipkin model system as a testbed [J. Chem. Phys. 146, 054110 (2017)]. We generalize the concept of a symmetry-projected mean-field wave function to the concept of a symmetry projected state, in which the factorization of high-order excitation amplitudes in terms of low-order ones is guided by symmetry projection and is not exponential, and combine them with coupled cluster theory in order to model the ground state of the Agassi Hamiltonian. This model has two separate channels of correlation and two separate physical symmetries which are broken under strong correlation. We show how the combination of symmetry collective states and coupled cluster is effective in obtaining correlation energies and order parameters of the Agassi model throughout its phase diagram.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that combining symmetry-projected states (symmetry collective states) with coupled cluster theory can accurately model the ground state of the Agassi Hamiltonian throughout its phase diagram, including regions where single-reference coupled cluster fails due to spontaneous symmetry breaking, but the success is conditional on the specific choice of symmetry-breaking operators included in the wave function ansatz.

## Method

The authors apply projected restricted coupled cluster (PRCC) and projected quasirestricted coupled cluster (PQCC) methods to the Agassi model Hamiltonian, which combines Lipkin-type and pairing-type correlation channels. The PRCC wave function uses a symmetry-adapted cluster operator T acting on a projected Hartree-Fock (PHF) reference, while PQCC additionally includes higher-order symmetry-breaking excitations Q that couple the two correlation channels. The T and Q amplitudes are determined either variationally by minimizing the energy expectation value or through a similarity-transformed approach standard in coupled cluster theory.

## Key numbers

- For the Agassi model with j=20, RCCD (restricted coupled cluster with doubles) fails to yield real solutions for correlation strengths x > 1.1, where x is the larger of the dimensionless parameters chi or Sigma_0
- Mean-field parity symmetry breaking occurs for chi = V(2j-1)/epsilon > 1, and mean-field number symmetry breaking occurs for Sigma_0 = [g(2j-1)+V]/epsilon > 1
- The PHF wave function is exact in the strong attractive pairing limit (Sigma_0 approaching infinity and Sigma_0 much greater than chi) and in the simultaneous thermodynamic and strong Lipkin correlation limit (chi approaching infinity, chi much greater than Sigma_0, and j approaching infinity)
- The Hilbert space of the Agassi model grows as O(n^2) with system size, allowing exact diagonalization for systems of up to hundreds of particles

## Context

This work extends previous developments on the Lipkin model (Wahlen 2017) to the more complex Agassi model, which has two separate physical symmetries (parity and particle number) that break at the mean-field level under strong correlation. The Agassi model is a schematic version of the pairing-plus-quadrupole model (Bes 1969) used in nuclear physics to describe deformed superconducting nuclei. The methods developed here lay the foundation for future application to realistic finite many-body systems, including molecules exhibiting mean-field spin symmetry breaking (Qiu 2017) and systems requiring particle number restoration in quasiparticle coupled cluster (Duguet 2016).
