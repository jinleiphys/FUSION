# Shell Model Analysis of the Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay of $^{48}$Ca

> The neutrinoless double beta ($0νββ$) decay process could provide crucial information to determine the absolute scale of the neutrino masses, and it is the only one that can establish whether neutrino is a Dirac or a Majorana particle. A key ingredient for extracting the absolute neutrino masses from $0νββ$ decay experiments is a precise knowledge of the nuclear matrix elements (NME) describing the half-life of this process. We developed a new shell model approach for computing the $0νββ$ decay NME, and we used it to analyze the $0νββ$ mode of $^{48}$Ca. The dependence of the NME on the short range correlations parameters, on the average energy of the intermediate states, on the finite-size cutoff parameters, and on the effective interaction used for the many-body calculations is discussed.

# Shell Model Analysis of the Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay of $^{48}$Ca

## Key claim

The nuclear matrix element (NME) for the $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay of $^{48}$Ca shows only moderate variation (approximately 20% spread) across five different shell-model effective interactions and three short-range correlation parametrizations, with an overall average NME of 0.86; however, the authors estimate the error due to the effects studied to be about 18%, and note that eliminating the Miller-Spencer short-range correlation parametrization would significantly increase this average value.

## Method

The authors developed a new shell-model approach for computing the $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay NME using the closure approximation, calculating two-body matrix elements from neutrino potentials that include higher-order terms in the nucleon currents, finite-size effects via dipole form factors with cutoff parameters $\Lambda_V=850$ MeV and $\Lambda_A=1086$ MeV, and short-range correlations parametrized from Miller-Spencer, CD-Bonn, and AV18 nucleon-nucleon interactions. They applied this method to the $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay of $^{48}$Ca using five different effective interactions (GXPF1, GXPF1A, KB3, KB3G, and FPD6) in the $pf$-shell model space, and analyzed the dependence of the NME on the average energy of intermediate states, finite-size cutoff parameters, and short-range correlation parameters.

## Key numbers

* Overall average NME for $^{48}$Ca $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay across all effective interactions and SRC parametrizations: 0.86, with an estimated error of about 18%
* For the GXPF1A interaction with $\left< E \right> = 7.72$ MeV and no SRC: $M^{0\nu}_{GT} = 0.556$, $M^{0\nu}_F = -0.219$, $M^{0\nu}_T = -0.015$, total $M^{0\nu} = 0.711$
* For the GXPF1A interaction with CD-Bonn SRC: $M^{0\nu}_{GT} = 0.688$, $M^{0\nu}_F = -0.222$, $M^{0\nu}_T = -0.014$, total $M^{0\nu} = 0.845$
* For the GXPF1A interaction with AV18 SRC: $M^{0\nu}_{GT} = 0.634$, $M^{0\nu}_F = -0.204$, $M^{0\nu}_T = -0.014$, total $M^{0\nu} = 0.779$
* For the GXPF1A interaction with Miller-Spencer SRC: $M^{0\nu}_{GT} = 0.465$, $M^{0\nu}_F = -0.141$, $M^{0\nu}_T = -0.014$, total $M^{0\nu} = 0.570$
* $Q_{\beta\beta}$-value of $^{48}$Ca: 4.271 MeV
* Natural abundance of $^{48}$Ca: 0.187%
* Phase-space factor $G^{0\nu}_1 = 6.5 \times 10^{-14}$ yr$^{-1}$
* A future measurement of the $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay half-life of $10^{26}$ yr could detect a neutrino mass $\left< m_{\beta\beta} \right>$ of about $230 \pm 45$ meV
* Variation of NME with $\left< E \right>$ from 2.5 MeV to 12.5 MeV: less than 5%
* Variation of NME with $\Lambda_A$ while $\Lambda_V$ fixed at 850 MeV: within 5%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous shell-model analysis of the two-neutrino double beta decay of $^{48}$Ca (Horoi, Stoica, and Brown, 2007) to the neutrinoless mode, incorporating recent developments in short-range correlations from Simkovic et al. (2009) and Engel and Hagen (2009) that showed the NME may not be as strongly suppressed as previously thought. The results are relevant for the CANDLES and CARVEL experiments searching for $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay of $^{48}$Ca, and the analysis suggests that the shell-model approach, which successfully predicted the $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay half-life of $^{48}$Ca (without adjustable parameters beyond the Gamow-Teller quenching factor from spectroscopy), can provide reliable NME for the $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay. The authors note that the NME for $^{48}$Ca is a factor of 4-5 smaller than those of other $\beta\beta$ emitters such as $^{76}$Ge and $^{82}$Se, as previously reported by Retamosa, Caurier, and Nowacki (1995) and Poves (2007).
