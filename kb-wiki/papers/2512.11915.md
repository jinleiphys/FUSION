# Dynamical study of hidden-strange pentaquarks as analogs of the hidden-charm states

> Motivated by the recent BESIII experiment~\cite{BESIII:2024muk} searching for hidden-strange exotic hadrons, we perform a systematic theoretical study of the hidden-strange pentaquark system within the framework of the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) and the resonating group method (RGM). Our results demonstrate that the channel coupling effect plays a decisive role in the formation of bound and resonance states. It not only significantly enhances the short-range attraction but also induces essential attractive contributions from pion exchange. We predict three bound states with masses of $1759$ MeV, $2000$ MeV, and $2407$ MeV. Furthermore, we report the existence of a hidden-strange pentaquark resonance state, $ΞK^{\ast}$, with quantum numbers $I(J^{P})=0(1/2^{-})$. This resonance is identified in the $S$-wave scattering phase shifts of the $Λη_{s}$ and $Λϕ$ open channels, with a predicted mass in the range of $2204\text{--}2208$ MeV. By accounting for both the scattering width from channel coupling and the intrinsic decay width of the constituent $K^{\ast}$, the total decay width is estimated to be $55\text{--}63$ MeV. These theoretical predictions provide important guidance for future experimental searches for such exotic states at facilities like BESIII.

## Key claim

The authors predict that channel coupling effects are decisive for forming hidden-strange pentaquark bound and resonance states in the $nnss\bar{s}$ system, with three bound states and one resonance state identified, but these predictions depend on the specific quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) parameters and the restriction to $S$-wave channels only.

## Method

The authors performed dynamical calculations within the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) solved by the resonating group method (RGM), considering $S$-wave channels with isospin $I=0$ and $I=1$ and quantum numbers $J^{P}=1/2^{-}$, $3/2^{-}$, and $5/2^{-}$. They first computed effective potentials for each hadron-pair channel, then carried out single-channel and coupled-channel bound-state calculations, and finally analyzed scattering phase shifts to identify resonance states and estimate their decay widths.

## Key numbers

* Three bound states predicted with masses of 1759 MeV, 2000 MeV, and 2407 MeV for the $I=0$ system, corresponding to $J^{P}=1/2^{-}$, $3/2^{-}$, and $5/2^{-}$ respectively
* A resonance state $\Xi K^{\ast}$ with quantum numbers $I(J^{P})=0(1/2^{-})$ predicted with mass in the range 2204--2208 MeV
* Total decay width of the resonance estimated to be 55--63 MeV, accounting for both scattering width from channel coupling and intrinsic decay width of the constituent $K^{\ast}$
* In the $I=0$ $J^{P}=1/2^{-}$ system, the $\Xi K$ channel shows a binding energy of -2 MeV (mass 1867 MeV) and the $\Xi K^{\ast}$ channel shows a binding energy of -5 MeV (mass 2261 MeV) in single-channel calculations
* In the $I=0$ $J^{P}=5/2^{-}$ system, the $\Xi^{\ast} K^{\ast}$ channel shows a binding energy of -18 MeV (mass 2370 MeV) in single-channel calculation

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QDCSM studies of hidden-strange pentaquarks (Gao 2017, Huang 2018) and is motivated by the recent BESIII experiment (BESIII 2024) searching for hidden-strange exotic hadrons in $\psi(3686)\rightarrow \Sigma^{0}\bar{\Sigma}^{0}\phi$ decays. The study draws analogy from the hidden-charm pentaquark family discovered by LHCb (LHCb 2015, 2019, 2021, 2022), particularly the $P_{cs}$ states, to predict analogous hidden-strange $P_{s}^{'}$ states. A recent QCD sum rule study (Ben 2025) suggested a possible $P_{s}'$ state with $J^{P}=3/2^{-}$, but this work provides the first comprehensive dynamical calculation with coupled channels for the $nnss\bar{s}$ system, offering predictions that can guide experimental searches at facilities like BESIII.
