# Effects of the $γ-$rays Scattered Backward by Metals on the Nuclear Energy Level Width

> By placing a ${}^{133}Cs$ $γ$-ray source embedded in a solid at the center of a platinum (gold) cylinder, we try to change the width of the 81-keV level. Our results show a narrowed energy level and, equivalently, a prolonged lifetime. With a 0.5-mm-thick, 5-cm-long, 2-mm-diameter platinum cylinder, we obtain a width narrower by $6.1 % $ at $4.2 \: K$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that ratios of off-diagonal to diagonal quark number susceptibilities (such as \(C_{BS}\), \(C_{QS}\), and the Wroblewski parameter \(\lambda_s\)) are "robust" in quenched lattice QCD, meaning they are almost independent of lattice spacing (within \(\sim 5\%\)) and have very mild dependence on sea quark mass, for temperatures \(T_c < T \le 2T_c\). The hedge is that this robustness is demonstrated only in the quenched approximation (no dynamical quarks), and the authors note that unquenching may change individual susceptibilities near \(T_c\) but argue the ratios themselves may still be robust.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations using the standard Wilson gauge action and unimproved staggered fermions, on lattices with temporal extents \(N_\tau = 4, 8, 10, 12\) and spatial sizes \(N_s = 2N_\tau + 2\), for temperatures \(T/T_c = 1.1, 1.25, 1.5, 2.0\). They computed flavour-diagonal and flavour-off-diagonal quark number susceptibilities at zero chemical potential, using bare valence quark masses \(m_q/T_c = 0.1\) for light \(u,d\) flavours and \(m_q/T_c = 1.0\) for the strange flavour, and extrapolated the results to the continuum limit by fitting linear functions in \(1/N_\tau^2\) (for diagonal susceptibilities) or constants (for off-diagonal susceptibilities).

## Key numbers

*   At \(T = 1.1 T_c\), the continuum-extrapolated diagonal susceptibility for light quarks is \(\chi_u/T^2 = 0.79(1)\) and for strange quarks is \(\chi_s/T^2 = 0.33(1)\).
*   At \(T = 2.0 T_c\), the continuum-extrapolated diagonal susceptibility for light quarks is \(\chi_u/T^2 = 0.86(2)\) and for strange quarks is \(\chi_s/T^2 = 0.70(2)\).
*   The off-diagonal susceptibility \(\chi_{ud}/T^2\) at \(T = 1.1 T_c\) is \(-4(4) \times 10^{-3}\) in the continuum limit.
*   The off-diagonal susceptibility \(\chi_{us}/T^2\) at \(T = 1.1 T_c\) is \(-6(4) \times 10^{-3}\) in the continuum limit.
*   The Wroblewski parameter \(\lambda_s = \chi_s/\chi_u\) is constant within \(\sim 5\%\) across lattice spacings for all temperatures studied; its continuum value at \(T = 1.1 T_c\) is approximately \(0.42\) (from figure, consistent with the value \(\lambda_s \approx 0.43\) extracted from RHIC data).
*   The ratios \(C_{BS}\) and \(C_{QS}\) are within \(\sim 5\%\) of their ideal gas values (1) for all temperatures \(1.1 T_c \le T \le 2 T_c\).

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD studies of conserved charge correlations by Gavai et al. (2004) and Koch et al. (2005) to the continuum limit using quenched simulations with larger temporal extents (\(N_\tau = 12\)), confirming that the ratios \(C_{BS}\), \(C_{QS}\), and the Wroblewski parameter are robust against lattice spacing artifacts. The results contradict the predictions of the bound-state model of Shuryak and Zahed (2004), which predicted \(C_{BS} \approx 0.62\) at \(1.5 T_c\) and much smaller values near \(T_c\); instead, the lattice values remain close to the ideal quark-gas limit, supporting a quasi-particle picture of the Quark-Gluon Plasma. This enables reliable comparison with experimental data from RHIC and the LHC, and provides a benchmark for future unquenched (dynamical) simulations.
