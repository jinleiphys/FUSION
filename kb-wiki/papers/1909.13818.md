# Unified Description of Polarized and Unpolarized Quark Distributions in the Proton

> We propose a unified new approach to describe polarized and unpolarized quark distributions in the proton based on the gauge-gravity correspondence, light-front holography, and the generalized Veneziano model. We find that the spin-dependent quark distributions are uniquely determined in terms of the unpolarized distributions by chirality separation without the introduction of additional free parameters. The predictions are consistent with existing experimental data and agree with perturbative QCD constraints at large longitudinal momentum $x$. In particular, we predict the sign reversal of the polarized down-quark distribution in the proton at $x=0.8\pm0.03$, a key property of nucleon substructure which will be tested very soon in upcoming experiments.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a unified approach that determines polarized quark distributions directly from unpolarized ones using chirality separation, without introducing additional free parameters. The key hedge is that the sign reversal of the polarized down-quark distribution at \(x=0.8\pm0.03\) is predicted robustly only in the large-\(x\) region dominated by valence states, while individual flavor predictions at lower \(x\) have larger uncertainties due to poorly constrained sea quark contributions.

## Method

The authors combine light-front holographic QCD (LFHQCD) with the generalized Veneziano model to derive parton distribution functions from the analytic structure of nucleon form factors. They relate the spin-non-flip Dirac form factor \(F_1(t)\) and the axial form factor \(F_A(t)\) through a sign flip in the chiral-odd component, which yields a direct relation between unpolarized PDFs \(q(x)\) and polarized PDFs \(\Delta q(x)\) for each twist-\(\tau\) Fock state. The coefficients are fixed by matching to the nucleon electromagnetic form factor data from Ye (2017) and the first moment of unpolarized valence quark distributions, with the isovector axial charge \(g_A\) providing an additional constraint on sea quark contributions.

## Key numbers

* The universal mass scale in LFHQCD is \(\kappa = 0.534\ \mathrm{GeV}\), fixed by the \(\rho/\omega\) trajectory.
* The sign reversal of the polarized down-quark distribution in the proton is predicted at \(x = 0.80 \pm 0.03\), with the uncertainty including variation among three coefficient sets and the scale \(Q\) from \(\sqrt{3}\) to \(\sqrt{5}\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
* The isovector axial charge evaluated with minimal sea contribution gives \(g_{A,\mathrm{min}} = 0.867\) (case I), \(0.879\) (case II), and \(0.881\) (case III), compared to the experimental value \(g_A = 1.2732(23)\) from Tanabashi (2018).
* The helicity asymmetry \(\Delta q(x)/q(x)\) approaches 1 as \(x\to1\) with a power behavior \((1-x)^2\), consistent with the pQCD prediction of Farrar (1975) and Brodsky (1994).
* The matching scale for PDF evolution is \(Q = 1.06\ \mathrm{GeV}\), determined from the strong coupling constant study of Deur (2016).

## Context

This work extends the LFHQCD framework previously applied to unpolarized PDFs and GPDs by de Teramond (2018) to include spin-dependent distributions, addressing the long-standing puzzle that global pQCD analyses (de Florian 2008, Nocera 2014) and Dyson-Schwinger equation calculations (Roberts 2013) favor negative \(\Delta d/d\) at large \(x\) despite the pQCD helicity retention prediction. The predicted sign reversal at \(x=0.8\pm0.03\) will be tested by upcoming Jefferson Lab experiments (E12-06-110 and E12-06-122), and the analytic relation between unpolarized and polarized distributions can be implemented in global analyses such as those of Leader (2001) and Bourrely (2001).
