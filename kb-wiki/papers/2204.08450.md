# Comment on "Scaling properties of background- and chiral-magnetically-driven charge separation in heavy ion collisions at $\sqrt{s}_{\rm NN}=200$ GeV (arXiv:2203.10029)"

> In a recent preprint arXiv:2203.10029, the authors argue for a multiplicity scaling of the squared inverse width of the $R_{Ψ_{2}}$ distribution to claim a positive signal for the chiral magnetic effect from STAR's isobar data, in stark contradiction to the conclusion reached by the STAR Collaboration. This Comment points out the fallacy of their arguments and reinforces the STAR conclusion.

## Key claim

The authors of arXiv:2203.10029 incorrectly argue that the squared inverse width of the R(ΔS) distribution should be scaled by multiplicity (Nch) to claim a positive chiral magnetic effect signal from STAR's isobar data, but this scaling is spurious because the correct background scaling factor is the elliptic flow anisotropy v2, and applying the proper v2 scaling reinforces the STAR conclusion that no CME is observed.

## Method

The Comment demonstrates algebraically that 1/σR² ≈ Nch Δγ, and since Δγ is approximately inversely proportional to Nch, 1/σR² has no explicit Nch dependence on first order. Instead, 1/σR² explicitly depends on v2 because Δγ is proportional to v2, and the Comment shows that v2 varies with multiplicity, creating a spurious correlation between 1/σR² and 1/Nch. The Comment then compares the v2 versus 1/Nch trends in Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions from the STAR isobar data to show that the changes in v2 and 1/Nch between the two systems are in opposite directions.

## Key numbers

- The multiplicity in Ru+Ru is larger than in Zr+Zr by approximately 4.4% in the 20-50% centrality range.
- The measured isobar ratios of Δγ/v2 are all above the inverse-multiplicity baseline by approximately 1%.
- The baseline for the isobar ratio of 1/σR² is approximately 1.02, as stated in the STAR isobar paper.
- Scaling the 1/σR² isobar ratio down by the v2 ratio between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr moves it down by approximately -2%, further below unity.

## Context

This Comment directly contradicts the analysis of Lacey et al. (2022) in arXiv:2203.10029, who claimed that multiplicity scaling of 1/σR² yields a positive CME signal from the STAR isobar data (STAR Collaboration, 2021). The Comment reinforces the STAR conclusion that no CME is observed, and extends prior work by Feng (2020) and Choudhury (2021) demonstrating that v2 is the driving physics underlying the 1/σR² background. The Comment enables future analyses to correctly use v2 scaling rather than Nch scaling when comparing isobar systems for CME searches.
