# The Origin of the Difference between Multiplicities in e+e- Annihilation and Heavy Ion Collisions

> Multiplicities in e+e- annihilation and relativistic heavy ion collisions show remarkable similarities at high energies. A thermal-statistical model is proposed to explain the differences which occur mainly at low beam energies. Two different calculations are performed, one using an approximate thermodynamic relationship, the other using a full thermal model code. The results are in qualitative agreement, suggesting that the interplay of baryon density and temperature tends to systematically suppress the total multiplicity at lower beam energies.

## Key claim

The difference between charged particle multiplicities in e+e- annihilation and heavy ion collisions at low beam energies can be explained by the suppression of entropy due to the presence of a conserved baryon number, quantified by the ratio of baryochemical potential to temperature (μ_B/3T), rather than by a simple pion suppression mechanism.

## Method

The authors compare total charged particle multiplicities from e+e- annihilation and Au+Au collisions as a function of center-of-mass energy, using the PHOBOS data set. They perform two calculations: one using an approximate thermodynamic relationship ΔN_ch = μ_B/(3T) derived from entropy considerations, and another using a full thermal-statistical model code (THERMUS) that computes entropy density as a function of temperature and baryochemical potential along the freezeout curve.

## Key numbers

- The multiplicity per participant pair in A+A collisions agrees with that measured in e+e- within 10% over a large range in √s and √s_NN
- The thermal model gives S/N_ch = 7.2 for the entropy per charged particle after strong decays, compared to the simple estimate of αβ = 6
- The parametrization of baryochemical potential used is μ_B(√s) = 1.2735/(1 + 0.2576√s)
- The Thermal II parametrization of freezeout temperature is T(μ_B) = 0.16446 - 0.11196μ_B^2 - 0.139139μ_B^4 + 0.0684637μ_B^6 (in GeV)
- The e+e- data are divided by the Landau-Fermi expression N_ch = 2.2 s^{1/4}
- The quantity s/T^3 is constant over a large range in center-of-mass energies down to √s_NN = 5 GeV, diverging only at very low energies

## Context

This work extends the thermal-statistical approach started by Fermi (1950), Landau, and Hagedorn, and builds on the PHOBOS results from Back (2003) showing scaling of multiplicity with participant number. The authors use freezeout parameter systematics from Cleymans and Redlich (1998) and the Thermal II parametrization from Steinberg. The work contrasts with the pion suppression concept discussed by Gazdzicki (1997), as the authors focus on suppression of total entropy rather than just pions, and includes all available meson and baryon resonances rather than just delta resonances. This enables a unified thermodynamic description of particle production across e+e- and heavy ion systems.
