# Comparison of $\bar{\hbox{N}}\hbox{N}$ optical models

> We compare the strong part of the $\bar{\hbox{N}}\hbox{N}$ interaction obtained by the Nijmegen partial wave analysis and the results of some of the most popular $\bar{\hbox{N}}\hbox{N}$ optical potentials in configuration space. We have found severe discrepancies in most of the partial waves, especially above $p_{Lab}$=400 MeV/c where the partial wave analysis displays a resonant-like structure in the $^{31}$S$_0$ and $^{33}$P$_0$ waves. Some theoretical difficulties to interpret this behaviour in terms of dynamical resonances are pointed pout and an alternative explanation is suggested. A much better stability is observed in the low energy parameters, apart from some discrepancies due to the presence of near-threshold quasi-bound states in particular waves. Large deviations have also been found between the corresponding potentials, at short and medium-range ($r\gtrsim 1$ fm) distances.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that while several popular antinucleon-nucleon (N̄N) optical models in configuration space produce similar integrated cross sections, they exhibit severe discrepancies at the partial-wave level when compared to the Nijmegen partial wave analysis (NPWA), particularly above pLab = 400 MeV/c where the NPWA shows resonant-like structures in the ³¹S₀ and ³³P₀ waves that the optical models fail to reproduce.

## Method

The authors computed the S-matrix for the Paris 2009, Dover-Richard (DR1 and DR2), and Kohno-Weise (KW) optical models in the energy range 0 < pLab < 1000 MeV/c for each partial wave state, solving the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation in configuration space with the original model parameters. They extracted complex phase shifts, inelasticity parameters, and mixing parameters for uncoupled and tensor-coupled states, then compared these with the strong N̄N phase shifts from the Nijmegen partial wave analysis (NPWA) and, for low-energy parameters, with the Jülich potential.

## Key numbers

- The NPWA displays a resonant-like structure in the ³¹S₀ and ³³P₀ waves above pLab = 400 MeV/c, which none of the optical models reproduce
- The Paris 2009 potential has a near-threshold quasi-bound state in the ¹¹S₀ channel at E = -10.2 - 23.2i MeV (determined by the authors), while the original Paris publication gave E = -4.8 - 26i MeV
- The DR2 model has a quasi-bound state in the same channel at E = -138 - 320i MeV, much deeper and leaving no trace in the scattering region
- The n̄n channel opens at p̄p center-of-mass energy E ≥ 2.5866 MeV, corresponding to q = 0.2497 fm⁻¹ and pLab = 98.54 MeV/c
- The Paris potential uses a cut-off radius rc = 0.84 fm or rc = 1.0 fm, with approximately 30 total parameters and χ²/datum ≈ 5
- The DR models use rc = 0.8 fm with only 4 parameters controlling the annihilation potential W, achieving χ²/data ≈ 0.5 for DR1
- The KW model was adjusted to reproduce p̄p cross sections in the region 200 < pLab < 700 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the comparison of N̄N optical models beyond the protonium level shifts and scattering lengths studied previously by Carbonell et al. (1989) and Carbonell et al. (1992), providing the first systematic comparison at non-zero energies. The authors find that the Paris, Dover-Richard, and Kohno-Weise models, despite using very different potentials and parameter counts, produce similar integrated cross sections but disagree dramatically at the partial-wave level. The Jülich potential (Haidenbauer, 2017), which was adjusted to reproduce the NPWA phase shifts, is noted as the most complete and accurate N̄N description but is formulated in momentum space, making it difficult to implement for studies of peripheral hydrogenic orbits in p̄-A systems needed for the PUMA research project.
