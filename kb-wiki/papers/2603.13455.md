# Strangeness neutrality and the QCD phase diagram

> We map out the phase structure of $N_f=2+1$ flavour QCD at strangeness neutrality with functional QCD. We find a critical end point at $(T_{\rm CEP},μ_{B,{\rm CEP}})|_{n_S=0} = (92, 696)$\,MeV. The computation is done with the functional renormalisation group, and we systematically improve on previous works, hence reducing the systematic error significantly. Our results pass relevant QCD benchmarks: they agree well with and corroborate the QCD phase structure from functional QCD results at vanishing strangeness chemical potential. Moreover, they agree well with lattice QCD results at vanishing chemical potential. Specifically, the ratio of the second order curvature coefficient $κ_2$ agrees with that obtained from lattice computations, $κ_2(n_S=0)/κ_2(μ_S=0)=0.897(20)$.

## Key claim

The paper maps the QCD phase diagram at strangeness neutrality (n_S=0) using functional renormalization group methods, finding a critical end point at (T_CEP, mu_B_CEP) = (92, 696) MeV, but this result assumes the dominance of scalar-pseudoscalar chiral dynamics and the absence of novel emergent dynamics at finite density.

## Method

The authors solve the functional renormalization group flow equations for N_f=2+1 flavor QCD with emergent composite fields for the scalar-pseudoscalar nonet, including open strange mesons such as kaons and the eta-meson. The strangeness neutrality condition n_S=0 is enforced by determining the strangeness chemical potential mu_S0(T,mu_B) that yields zero strangeness density at each point in the phase diagram. The chiral crossover temperature is extracted from the peak of the thermal susceptibility of the renormalized light chiral condensate.

## Key numbers

- Critical end point at strangeness neutrality: (T_CEP, mu_B_CEP) = (92, 696) MeV
- Critical end point at mu_S=0 in the same setup: (T_CEP, mu_B_CEP) = (102, 644) MeV
- Chiral crossover temperature at mu_B=0: T_c = 157.5(5) MeV
- Curvature coefficient kappa_2 at n_S=0: 0.0130(2)
- Curvature coefficient kappa_2 at mu_S=0: 0.0145(2)
- Ratio kappa_2(n_S=0)/kappa_2(mu_S=0) = 0.897(20), compared to lattice ratio 0.893(35) from Ding (2024)

## Context

This work extends previous functional QCD studies of the phase diagram at mu_S=0 by Fu (2019), Pawlowski (2025), and others, which found CEP locations near (107, 635) MeV and (102, 644) MeV respectively. The strangeness neutrality condition is the relevant constraint for heavy-ion collisions, and the results agree quantitatively with lattice QCD benchmarks at vanishing chemical potential from the HotQCD collaboration (2018) and the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (2020). The computation enables predictions for the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC and future experiments like CBM and CEE+, providing guidance for the search of the critical end point in heavy-ion collisions.
