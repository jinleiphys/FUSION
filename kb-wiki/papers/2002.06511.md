# ${}_{ΛΛ}^{5}$H and ${}_{ΛΛ}^{5}$He Hypernuclei reexamined in Halo/Cluster Effective Theory

> The J=1/2 iso-doublet double-$Λ$-hypernuclei, namely, ${}_{ΛΛ}^{5}$H and ${}_{ΛΛ}^{5}$He, are examined as the three-body cluster states, $ΛΛt$ ($t\equiv {}^3$H or triton) and $ΛΛh$ ($h\equiv {}^3$He or helion), respectively, in a model independent framework utilizing pionless halo effective theory. Both singlet and triplet states of the constituent $ΛT$ ($T\equiv t,h$) sub-system are used in the elastic channel for the study of $_Λ^4$H$-Λ$ and $_Λ^4$He$-Λ$ scattering processes. A prototypical leading order investigation using a sharp momentum cut-off regulator, irrespective of the type of the elastic channel chosen, yields almost identical cut-off dependence of the three-body binding energy or the double-$Λ$-separation energy ($B_{ΛΛ}$) is obtained for the mirror partners, evidently suggesting good isospin symmetry in these three-body systems. Subsequently, upon normalization of our solutions to the integral equation with respect to a single pair of input data from an {\it ab initio} potential model analysis for each mirror hypernuclei, yields $B_{ΛΛ}$ which agrees fairly well with various erstwhile regulator independent potential models for our choice of the cut-off regulator, $\sim 200$ MeV. This is either consistent with pionless effective theory or with its slightly augmented version with a hard scale around $2m_π$, where low-energy $Λ$-$Λ$ interactions dominated by $ππ$ or $σ$-meson exchange. Finally, to demonstrate the predictability of our effective theory, we present preliminary estimates of the S-wave $ΛΛT$ three-body scattering lengths and the $Λ$-separation energies.

## Key claim

The double-Λ hypernuclei ${}_{ΛΛ}^{5}$H and ${}_{ΛΛ}^{5}$He can be described as three-body cluster states (ΛΛt and ΛΛh) within pionless halo effective theory at leading order, and their binding energies show good isospin symmetry; however, the predictions depend on normalizing to a single input from an ab initio potential model analysis due to the current lack of experimental data for these systems.

## Method

The authors treat ${}_{ΛΛ}^{5}$H and ${}_{ΛΛ}^{5}$He as three-body systems composed of two Λ hyperons and a core (triton for ${}_{ΛΛ}^{5}$H, helion for ${}_{ΛΛ}^{5}$He) using pionless effective field theory at leading order. They construct coupled integral equations for S-wave scattering amplitudes with both spin-singlet and spin-triplet ΛT (T = t, h) dimer fields as elastic channels, introduce a sharp momentum cutoff regulator Λc, and solve the equations in both bound-state and scattering domains. The three-body contact interactions are fixed by renormalization group limit cycle behavior, and the solutions are normalized using a single pair of input data from the ab initio stochastic variational method analysis of Nemura et al. (2004).

## Key numbers

- The experimental Λ-separation energies for the mirror hypernuclei are: ${}_Λ^4$H ground state (J^P=0^+) ${\mathcal B}_Λ[0^+] = 2.157 \pm 0.077$ MeV, ${}_Λ^4$He ground state ${\mathcal B}_Λ[0^+] = 2.39 \pm 0.05$ MeV; first excited states (J^P=1^+) are $1.067 \pm 0.08$ MeV for ${}_Λ^4$H and $0.984 \pm 0.05$ MeV for ${}_Λ^4$He.
- The cutoff regulator is chosen at Λc ∼ 200 MeV, consistent with pionless effective theory or its augmented version with hard scale ΛH ≳ 2mπ.
- For the benchmark value aΛΛ = −0.80 fm, the predicted Λ-separation energies are ${\mathcal B}_Λ({}_{ΛΛ}^{5}{\rm H}) = 2.295$ MeV and ${\mathcal B}_Λ({}_{ΛΛ}^{5}{\rm He}) = 2.212$ MeV.
- The accepted range for the double-Λ scattering length is −1.92 fm ≲ aΛΛ ≲ −0.5 fm, constrained by RHIC data from the STAR Collaboration (Adamczyk et al., 2014).

## Context

This work extends the pionless effective field theory approach previously applied to few-nucleon systems and single-Λ hypernuclei by Contessi et al. (2019) to the specific case of the ${}_{ΛΛ}^{5}$H and ${}_{ΛΛ}^{5}$He iso-doublet, using a halo/cluster formalism rather than a full ab initio many-body calculation. The analysis provides model-independent universal correlations between the double-Λ separation energy and the ΛΛ scattering length, enabling preliminary estimates of three-body scattering lengths and Λ-separation energies that can be tested against future experimental data from relativistic heavy-ion collisions or emulsion experiments.
