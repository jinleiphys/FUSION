# Phase structure of strong interaction matter from Functional QCD

> In this contribution to the Encyclopedia of Nuclear Physics, we aim to provide a pedagogical introduction to the functional approach to QCD at finite temperature and chemical potential. We briefly outline the general framework and address its complementarity to other first-principle approaches to non-perturbative QCD. We discuss selected results obtained with Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE) and the functional renormalisation group (fRG) in the context of a general physics perspective on the QCD phase diagram. This article is specifically aimed at students and non-practitioners of functional methods alike and may serve as a short guide to further literature.

## Key claim

This pedagogical review argues that functional QCD (Dyson-Schwinger equations and the functional renormalisation group) is currently the only first-principles framework that can perform direct non-perturbative computations at high baryon chemical potential, but the authors caution that systematic error control within truncation schemes remains under development and that predictions for the critical end point or other high-density phenomena cannot yet be considered definitive.

## Method

The authors present the theoretical foundations of functional QCD at finite temperature and chemical potential, deriving the grand potential from the one-particle irreducible effective action and expanding it in terms of full correlation functions. They contrast the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) tower, which involves one-loop and two-loop exact integral equations for n-point functions, with the functional renormalisation group (fRG) tower, which involves one-loop exact integral-differential flow equations, and discuss how both require truncations of the infinite hierarchy of vertices. They then describe how order parameters such as the chiral condensate and its susceptibilities are extracted from the dressed quark propagator, and how the chiral crossover line is parameterised via a curvature expansion in baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers

* The pseudo-critical temperature for the chiral transition at zero chemical potential is \(T_c \approx 157\) MeV with an error margin below two MeV, from lattice QCD results cited by the authors (Bazavov 2018, Borsanyi 2020).
* The curvature of the chiral crossover line is parameterised as \(T_c(\mu_B)/T_c = 1 - \kappa_2 (\mu_B/T_c)^2 - \kappa_4 (\mu_B/T_c)^4 - \cdots\), with an estimated upper bound \(\kappa \lesssim 0.0145\) derived from the nuclear liquid-gas transition at \(\mu_B^{(\mathrm{lg})} \approx 923\) MeV.
* The chiral crossover line from lattice QCD is reliable in the regime \(\mu_B/T \lesssim 3\), while emergent density-dependent dynamics is expected in the regime \(\mu_B/T \gtrsim 4.5\).

## Context

This review extends the pedagogical and technical overviews of functional QCD found in earlier reviews by Fischer (2018), Dupuis (2020), Fu (2022), Rennecke (2025), Huber (2025), and Fischer (2026). It complements lattice QCD results at zero and imaginary chemical potential (Aoki 2006, Borsanyi 2010, Bazavov 2011, Bhattacharya 2014, Bazavov 2018, Borsanyi 2020) by providing a framework for direct computations at real baryon chemical potential where lattice methods suffer from the sign problem. The work enables future quantitative studies of the critical end point, moat regimes, inhomogeneous phases, and colour-superconducting phases at high density, and connects functional predictions to freeze-out data from heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC, LHC, FAIR, and HIAF.
