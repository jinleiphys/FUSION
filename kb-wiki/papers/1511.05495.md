# Consistent analysis of one-nucleon spectroscopic factors involving weakly- and strongly-bound nucleons

> There is a considerable interest in understanding the dependence of one-nucleon removal cross sections on the asymmetry of the neutron $S_n$ and proton $S_p$ separation energies, following a large amount of experimental data and theoretical analyses in a framework of sudden and eikonal approximations of the reaction dynamics. These theoretical calculations involve both the single-particle cross section and the shell-model description of the projectile initial state and final states of the reaction residues. The configuration mixing in shell-model description of nuclear states depends on the proximity of one-nucleon decay threshold but does it depend sensitively on $S_n - S_p$? To answer this question, we use the shell model embedded in the continuum to investigate the dependence of one-nucleon spectroscopic factors on the asymmetry of $S_n$ and $S_p$ for mirror nuclei $^{24}$Si, $^{24}$Ne and $^{28}$S, $^{28}$Mg and for a series of neon isotopes ($20 \leq A \leq 28$).

## Key claim

The paper shows that one-nucleon spectroscopic factors calculated in the shell model embedded in the continuum (SMEC) are only weakly correlated with the asymmetry of neutron and proton separation energies (ΔS = S_n − S_p or S_p − S_n), contradicting the strong ΔS dependence previously inferred from the ratio of experimental to theoretical one-nucleon removal cross sections. The authors conclude that the explanation of that cross-section ratio’s ΔS dependence does not reside in the behavior of the spectroscopic factors themselves.

## Method

The authors use the shell model embedded in the continuum (SMEC), a modern version of the continuum shell model, to compute one-nucleon spectroscopic factors for the ground states of mirror nuclei ²⁴Si/²⁴Ne and ²⁸S/²⁸Mg, and for a chain of neon isotopes (20 ≤ A ≤ 28). The Hilbert space is divided into two orthogonal subspaces (0 and 1 particle in the scattering continuum), and the effective Hamiltonian includes coupling to 18 lowest decay channels (9 proton, 9 neutron) via a Wigner-Bartlett interaction. Spectroscopic factors from SMEC are compared to those from the standard shell model (SM) using the USD interaction, with the ratio R_SF = S_SMEC / S_SM evaluated as a function of ΔS.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁴Si with α = 0.73, the ratio R_SF for the proton (neutron) d₅/₂ spectroscopic factor is 0.974 (0.957) at the experimental separation energies.
- For ²⁸S with α = 0.73, the ratio R_SF for the proton s₁/₂ (neutron d₅/₂) spectroscopic factor is 0.848 (0.975) at the experimental separation energies.
- In ²⁴Si, 92.7% of the proton d₅/₂ SM spectroscopic strength and 91.9% of the neutron d₅/₂ SM spectroscopic strength are in the ground state.
- In ²⁸S, only 44% of the proton s₁/₂ SM spectroscopic strength is in the ground state, while 97.6% of the neutron d₅/₂ SM spectroscopic strength is in the ground state.
- The ΔS variation of R_SF in ²⁴Si and ²⁴Ne for α = 0.73 does not exceed 10%.
- The experimental ratio R_σ for one-proton (one-neutron) removal from ²⁴Si is ∼0.8 ± 0.04 (∼0.39 ± 0.04); for ²⁸S it is ∼0.92 ± 0.07 (∼0.31 ± 0.025).

## Context

This work extends the discussion of the ΔS dependence of one-nucleon removal cross sections reported by Gade et al. (2008) and Gade et al. (2014), who found a striking dependence of the ratio R_σ = σ_exp/σ_th on the asymmetry of neutron and proton separation energies. The authors contrast their results with the strong ΔS dependence of spectroscopic factors found by Hagen et al. (2012) using coupled-cluster calculations for oxygen isotopes, and with the weak ΔS dependence observed by Flavigny et al. (2013) using transfer reactions. The study enables a more consistent separation of structure and reaction-dynamics effects in intermediate-energy knockout reactions, and suggests that the empirical normalization of theoretical cross sections should not be attributed to spectroscopic factor systematics.
