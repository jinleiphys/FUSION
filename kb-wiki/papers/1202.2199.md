# Thermalization component model of multiplicity distributions of charged hadrons at BNL($E^{lab}_{NN}=2-11.6GeV/c$), the CERN($E^{lab}_{NN}=20-200GeV/c$), and the BNL ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=19.6-200 GeV$)

> We find that collective flow model which can successfully analyze charged particle distributions at AGS and lower SPS (less than 20Gev/n). but fails to analyze that of at RHIC. The tails of distribution of charged particle at RHIC has a jump from the collective flow model calculation as the energy increases. Thermalization Component Model is presented based on collective flow to study the multiplicity distributions at RHIC in this paper. It is realized that the region of phase space of collective flow can reflect that of thermalization region. By comparing the contributions of particle productions from thermalization region at different energies and different centralities, we can deepen our study on the feature of collective movement at RHIC. Keywords: Thermalization, Thermalization component model

## Key claim

The Collective Flow Model (CFM) successfully describes charged particle distributions at AGS and lower SPS energies (below 20 GeV/nucleon) but fails at RHIC energies, where the tails of the distributions show a symmetric jump away from the CFM calculation as energy increases; the Thermalization Component Model (TCM) is presented as an extension that accounts for this discrepancy by compartmentalizing phase space into thermalized and non-thermalized regions.

## Method

The authors developed the Thermalization Component Model (TCM) by combining a two-dimensional collective flow description for the central rapidity region with Gaussian distributions for the target and projectile fragmentation regions. They fitted the model to experimental charged hadron pseudo-rapidity distributions from AGS (E^{lab}_{NN}=2-11.6 GeV), SPS (E^{lab}_{NN}=20-200 GeV), and RHIC (sqrt(s_{NN})=19.6-200 GeV) for Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions, using chi-squared minimization to determine best-fit parameters including the flow rapidity extent eta_0 and the positions of fragmentation sources y_{1,2}.

## Key numbers

- At SPS energies, the percentage of charged hadrons from the thermalization region decreases from 94.13% at E^{lab}_{NN}=30 GeV to 83.52% at E^{lab}_{NN}=158 GeV (Table 1).
- At RHIC energies, the thermalization percentage decreases from 77.99% at sqrt(s_{NN})=19.6 GeV to 73.38% at sqrt(s_{NN})=200 GeV (Table 1).
- The collective flow rapidity extent eta_0 follows a linear relation with ln(sqrt(s_{NN})): eta_0 = 0.40 ln(sqrt(s_{NN})) + 0.71, fitted from SPS and RHIC data (Eq. 7).
- For Au+Au at sqrt(s_{NN})=200 GeV, the thermalization region contributes 73.38% of particles (n_3=3629 out of total 4949) (Table 1).

## Context

This work extends the Collective Flow Model of Schnedermann et al. (1990s), which successfully described AGS and lower SPS data, to higher energies where it fails. The TCM approach is similar to the three-Gaussian fits used by the PHOBOS Collaboration (Back et al., 2000s) and the three-component Fokker-Planck approach of Wolschin (2000s), but grounds the central component in collective flow theory rather than a purely empirical Gaussian. The model enables predictions for the thermalization extent at LHC energies and provides a framework for studying how thermalization depends on collision energy, centrality, and system size.
