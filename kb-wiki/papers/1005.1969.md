# Lattice QCD and Hydro/Cascade Model of Heavy Ion Collisions

> We report here on a recent lattice study of the QCD transition region at finite temperature and zero chemical potential using domain wall fermions (DWF). We also present a parameterization of the QCD equation of state obtained from lattice QCD that is suitable for use in hydrodynamics studies of heavy ion collisions. Finally, we show preliminary results from a multi-stage hydrodynamics/hadron cascade model of a heavy ion collision, in an attempt to understand how well the experimental data (e.g. particle spectra, elliptic flow, and HBT radii) can constrain the inputs (e.g. initial temperature, freezeout temperature, shear viscosity, equation of state) of the theoretical model.

## Key claim
The paper reports a lattice QCD study of the QCD crossover temperature using domain wall fermions, but the result carries significant uncertainties from small spatial volumes, unphysical light quark masses (m_pi = 300 MeV near the susceptibility peak), and simulations not performed on a line of constant quark mass, so only a wide range of 150 MeV < T < 190 MeV can be quoted.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations at 11 temperatures between 120 MeV and 220 MeV at zero chemical potential using domain wall fermions on a temporal lattice size N_t = 8. They calculated the disconnected chiral susceptibility and subtracted chiral condensate to locate the chiral transition, and electric charge and isospin susceptibilities for the deconfinement transition. Separately, they constructed a parameterization of the QCD equation of state from lattice data and implemented a multi-stage model combining optical Glauber initial conditions, 2-d viscous hydrodynamics in the Israel-Stewart formalism, Cooper-Frye freezeout, and the UrQMD hadronic cascade for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV per nucleon pair.

## Key numbers
- The disconnected chiral susceptibility shows a clear peak at T ≈ 170 MeV, corresponding to bare coupling beta = 6/g_0^2 = 2.03
- The crossover temperature is constrained only to the range 150 MeV < T < 190 MeV due to systematic uncertainties
- Lattice spacings used range from 0.11 fm to 0.21 fm, with spatial volumes from (1.7 fm)^3 up to (3.1 fm)^3
- The light quark masses correspond to m_pi = 300 MeV near the susceptibility peak
- Fit parameters for the interaction measure I(T) parameterization: alpha_1 = 0.26867, alpha_2 = 0.00345, alpha_3 = 0.50120, alpha_4 = 0.18529, alpha_5 = 15.17516
- Model parameters explored for Au+Au at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV: impact parameter b = 4.4 fm, N_part = 270, initial temperatures T_i = 250, 300, 350 MeV, eta/s = 0.08, 0.16, 0.24, freezeout temperature T_f = 150 MeV

## Context
This work extends recent high-precision lattice QCD studies of the QCD transition using staggered fermions (Aoki 2006, Aoki 2009, Cheng 2006) by applying domain wall fermions to test the robustness of those Tc results and better understand chiral symmetry's role in the crossover. The equation of state parameterization is designed for use in hydrodynamic models, and the multi-stage hydrodynamics/cascade model aims to systematically constrain model inputs (initial temperature, shear viscosity, freezeout temperature, equation of state) by comparing to experimental data from PHENIX on particle spectra, elliptic flow, and HBT radii. The model builds on prior work by Romatschke (2008) on viscous hydrodynamics and the UrQMD cascade code (Bleicher 1999, Bass 1998), and the authors plan a systematic exploration of parameter space to determine which inputs best match experimental results across different systems, energies, and centralities.
