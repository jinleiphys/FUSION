# Isospin Dependence in the Odd-Even Staggering of Nuclear Binding Energies

> The FRS-ESR facility at GSI provides unique conditions for precision measurements of large areas on the nuclear mass surface in a single experiment. Values for masses of 604 neutron-deficient nuclides (30<=Z<=92) were obtained with a typical uncertainty of 30 microunits. The masses of 114 nuclides were determined for the first time. The odd-even staggering (OES) of nuclear masses was systematically investigated for isotopic chains between the proton shell closures at Z=50 and Z=82. The results were compared with predictions of modern nuclear models. The comparison revealed that the measured trend of OES is not reproduced by the theories fitted to masses only. The spectral pairing gaps extracted from models adjusted to both masses, and density related observables of nuclei agree better with the experimental data.

## Key claim
The odd-even staggering (OES) of nuclear binding energies increases systematically toward the proton drip-line for all even-Z isotopic chains between Z=50 and Z=82, but this general trend is not reproduced by modern nuclear mass models fitted only to binding energies; models adjusted to both masses and density-related observables agree better with the experimental data.

## Method
The experiment used the FRS-ESR facility at GSI to measure masses of neutron-deficient nuclides via Schottky Mass Spectrometry (SMS). Projectile fragmentation of a 600-900 MeV/u 209Bi beam on beryllium targets produced exotic nuclei, which were separated in-flight by the FRS, injected into the ESR, and cooled to a velocity spread of δv/v ≈ 5×10⁻⁷. Masses were determined from revolution frequencies of stored ions, achieving a mass resolving power above 2×10⁶ (FWHM). Neutron and proton pairing-gap energies were extracted from measured masses using five-point finite-difference formulae, and compared with predictions from the Finite-Range Liquid-Drop Model (FRLDM), Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) plus BCS/HFB models, and relativistic mean-field (RMF) models.

## Key numbers
- Masses of 604 neutron-deficient nuclides (30 ≤ Z ≤ 92) were obtained with a typical uncertainty of 30 μu.
- Masses of 114 nuclides were determined for the first time.
- 582 different nuclides were observed in the frequency spectra; 117 were used for calibration.
- The velocity spread after electron cooling was δv/v ≈ 5×10⁻⁷.
- The mass resolving power was more than 2×10⁶ (FWHM).
- In the readjusted FRLDM, the best-fit pairing strength constants were g0p = g0n = 20.80 MeV and g1p = −g1n = 22.40 MeV.
- With the new pairing description, the σrms value for prediction of nuclear binding energies between Z=50 and Z=82 improved by about 25%.

## Context
This work extends earlier observations of increasing pairing gaps toward the proton drip-line for a small number of Dy-Hg isotopes (Alkhazov, 1983) to a systematic study of all even-Z isotopic chains from tin to lead. The results contradict predictions of mass models fitted only to binding energies, such as the original FRLDM (Moeller, 1995) and several Skyrme-HFB models (MSk7, BSk1-BSk7), which fail to reproduce the observed isospin dependence. In contrast, RMF models (NL-Z2, NL3, PC-F1) and SHF forces (SkI3, SkI4) adjusted to both masses and density-related observables reproduce the general trend, demonstrating the need for improved understanding of pairing in exotic nuclei and enabling more reliable predictions for astrophysical r-process modeling and future measurements at the FAIR project.
