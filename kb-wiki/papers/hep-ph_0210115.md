# Calculating the pion decay constant from α_s(M_Z)

> We revisit the analysis of the improved ladder Schwinger-Dyson (SD) equation for the dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in QCD with emphasizing the importance of the scale ambiguity. Previous calculation done so far naively used one-loop MSbar coupling in the improved ladder SD equation without examining the scale ambiguity. As a result, the calculated pion decay constant f_πwas less than a half of its experimental value f_π=92.4MeV once the QCD scale is fixed from the high energy coupling α_s(M_Z). In order to settle the ambiguity in a proper manner, we adopt here in the present paper the next-to-leading-order effective coupling instead of a naive use of the MSbar coupling. The pion decay constant f_πis then calculated from high energy QCD coupling strength α_s(M_Z)=0.1172 \pm 0.0020. Within the Higashijima-Miransky approximation, we obtain f_π=85--106MeV depending on the value of α_s(M_Z) which agrees well with the experimentally observed value f_π=92.4MeV. The validity of the improved ladder SD equation is therefore ascertained more firmly than considered before.

## Key claim

By replacing the naively used one-loop \(\overline{\rm MS}\) coupling with a next-to-leading-order effective coupling derived from the background field method, the improved ladder Schwinger-Dyson equation yields a pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 85\)–\(106\ \mathrm{MeV}\) from the high-energy QCD coupling \(\alpha_s^{\overline{\rm MS}}(M_Z)=0.1172 \pm 0.0020\), resolving the previous discrepancy where \(f_\pi\) was less than half the experimental value \(92.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Method

The authors solve the improved ladder Schwinger-Dyson equation for dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in QCD, using the Higashijima-Miransky approximation for the angular integral of the kernel. Instead of the one-loop \(\overline{\rm MS}\) coupling with the renormalization scale set to the gluon momentum \(\sqrt{z}\), they adopt a next-to-leading-order effective coupling defined via the background field method, which eliminates the scale ambiguity. The running of the effective coupling is computed numerically using the two-loop \(\overline{\rm MS}\) renormalization group equation with the boundary condition \(\alpha_s^{\overline{\rm MS}}(m_b)=0.2197\) (corresponding to \(\alpha_s^{\overline{\rm MS}}(M_Z)=0.1172\)), and the coupling is infrared-regularized with a smooth interpolation. The pion decay constant is then evaluated using the Pagels-Stokar formula.

## Key numbers

* \(f_\pi = 85\)–\(106\ \mathrm{MeV}\) obtained from the improved ladder SD equation with the effective coupling, using \(\alpha_s^{\overline{\rm MS}}(M_Z)=0.1172 \pm 0.0020\) as input, compared to the experimental value \(f_\pi = 92.4\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The \(\overline{\rm MS}\) coupling at the bottom quark mass is \(\alpha_s^{\overline{\rm MS}}(m_b) = 0.2197 \pm 0.0075\) (NNLO) or \(0.2188 \pm 0.0074\) (NLO), with \(m_b = 4.3\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
* The previous naive use of the one-loop \(\overline{\rm MS}\) coupling gave \(f_\pi\) of order \(30\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for the same high-energy input.

## Context

This work extends the improved ladder Schwinger-Dyson analysis of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in QCD, previously studied by Aoki (1990), Jain (1991), and Kugo (1992), which had failed to reproduce the experimental \(f_\pi\) from the high-energy coupling. By introducing an effective coupling via the background field method (Abbott, 1981), similar in spirit to the pinch technique of Cornwall and Papavassiliou (1989), the authors resolve the scale ambiguity and restore agreement with experiment. This enables more reliable connections between high-energy QCD parameters and low-energy observables, particularly for studies of QCD under extreme conditions (hot and/or dense QCD).
