# Critical Points in the QCD Phase Diagram with Two Flavors of Quarks

> We employ the linear sigma model to study the chiral dynamics of two flavors of quarks at finite temperature and density. Our calculations include thermal fluctuations of both the bosonic and fermionic fields. In particular, we determine the phase diagram in the plane of temperature and baryon chemical potential as a function of the pion mass. An interesting phase structure occurs that results in zero, one, or two critical points depending on the value of the vacuum pion mass.

## Key claim

The linear sigma model coupled to two flavors of quarks predicts that the QCD phase diagram can contain zero, one, or two critical points depending on the value of the vacuum pion mass, with the unconventional two-critical-point scenario appearing for pion masses below approximately 35 MeV.

## Method

The authors used the linear sigma model with two flavors of quarks, including thermal fluctuations of both bosonic (meson) and fermionic (quark) fields. They integrated out the quark degrees of freedom to obtain an effective mesonic Lagrangian, then decomposed the scalar field into a condensate plus fluctuations and solved the resulting self-consistent equations numerically for the condensate and quasiparticle masses. The vacuum pion mass was scanned from 0 to 350 MeV while fixing the pion decay constant at 92.4 MeV, the sigma mass at 700 MeV, and the quark mass at 313 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For vacuum pion mass of 321 MeV, the critical point sits on the T = 0 axis; for vacuum pion mass above 321 MeV, there is no phase transition at all.
- For vacuum pion mass of 35 MeV, two critical points appear: one at (mu_c1 ≈ 725 MeV, T_c1 ≈ 92 MeV) and another at (mu_c2 ≈ 240 MeV, T_c2 ≈ 137 MeV).
- The vacuum pion mass was varied from 0 to m_sigma/2 = 350 MeV, with the sigma mass fixed at 700 MeV.
- The pion decay constant was fixed at f_pi = 92.4 MeV and the quark mass at m_q = 313 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies using the linear sigma model (Scavenius et al., 2001; Fraser et al., various; Carter et al., 1997, 2000; Mocsy, 2004) by including thermal fluctuations of both meson and fermion fields and scanning a wide range of vacuum pion masses. The two-critical-point phase diagram is described as unconventional and, to the authors' knowledge, the first time such a result has appeared in a model calculation. The results are intended for comparison with lattice gauge theory at different quark masses and may inform experimental searches at facilities such as FAIR and RHIC.
