# Azimuthal distributions of radial momentum and velocity in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Azimuthal distributions of radial (transverse) momentum, mean radial momentum, and mean radial velocity of final state particles are suggested for relativistic heavy ion collisions. Using transport model AMPT with string melting, these distributions for Au + Au collisions at 200 GeV are presented and studied. It is demonstrated that the distribution of total radial momentum is more sensitive to the anisotropic expansion, as the anisotropies of final state particles and their associated transverse momentums are both counted in the measure. The mean radial velocity distribution is compared with the radial °ow velocity. The thermal motion contributes an isotropic constant to mean radial velocity.

## Key claim
The azimuthal distribution of total radial (transverse) momentum is a more sensitive measure of anisotropic expansion than the conventional multiplicity distribution, because it simultaneously accounts for both the anisotropy in particle number and the anisotropy in their associated transverse momenta. The mean radial velocity distribution of final-state particles can be decomposed into an isotropic constant from thermal motion plus an anisotropic component that approximates the radial flow velocity.

## Method
The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model with string melting to generate approximately 1.6 million minimum-bias events for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. They computed azimuthal distributions of total radial momentum, mean radial momentum, and mean radial velocity of final-state particles within rapidity range y in [-5,5], and extracted the elliptic flow parameter v2 from Fourier expansions of each distribution. The mean radial velocity distributions were fitted to the form V0 + Va cos(2phi) and compared with the anisotropic blast-wave model parameterization beta = beta0 + beta_a cos(2phi).

## Key numbers
- For Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the anisotropy parameter v2 from radial momentum distribution is the largest among the three observables at all centralities, larger than v2 from multiplicity distribution and v2 from mean radial momentum distribution.
- The v2 parameter from both radial momentum and multiplicity distributions increases with pt for pt < 2 GeV/c, and slightly decreases for pt > 2 GeV/c.
- From fitting the spectra of pion, proton and kaon from AMPT string melting, the blast-wave flow velocity parameters are beta = 0.35 + 0.04 cos(2phi).
- The anisotropic component Va of mean radial velocity is approximately 0.01, compared to beta_a approximately 0.04 from blast-wave fitting.
- In central (0-5%) collisions, the mean radial velocity becomes approximately an isotropic constant, with zero azimuthal gradient.

## Context
This work extends the conventional study of anisotropic flow, which has focused on multiplicity distributions and their Fourier coefficients v_n(N) as established by Voloshin, Zhang (1996) and Danielewicz, Odyniec (1985). The authors propose new observables that combine multiplicity and momentum information, addressing a gap noted in prior work. The results enable direct comparison between experimentally measurable mean radial velocity and the radial flow velocity parameter used in blast-wave models (Oh, Lin, Ko, 2009; Huovinen, Kolb, Heinz, 2001), potentially allowing extraction of flow velocity without relying on spectrum fitting. The centrality dependence of the mean radial velocity gradient provides a test for viscous hydrodynamic predictions (Song, Heinz, 2008).
