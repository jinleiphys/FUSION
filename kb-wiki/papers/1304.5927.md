# Deformation and cluster structures in $^{12}$C studied with configuration mixing using Skyrme interactions

> We report an investigation of the structure of $^{12}$C nucleus employing a newly developed configuration-mixing method. In the three-dimensional coordinate-space representation, we generate a number of Slater determinants with various correlated structures using the imaginary-time algorithm. We then diagonalize a many-body Hamiltonian with the Skyrme interaction in the space spanned by the Slater determinants with parity and angular momentum projections. Our calculation reasonably describes the ground and excited states of $^{12}$C nucleus, both for shell-model-like and cluster-like states. The excitation energies and transition strengths of the ground-state rotational band are well reproduced. Negative parity excited states, $1_1^-$, $2_1^-$, and $3_1^-$, are also reasonably described. The second and third $0^+$ states, $0_2^+$ and $0_3^+$, appear at around 8.8 MeV and 15 MeV, respectively. The $0_2^+$ state shows a structure consistent with former results of the α-cluster models, however, the calculated radius of the $0_2^+$ state is smaller than those calculations. The three-α linear-chain configuration dominates in the $0_3^+$ state.

## Key claim

The paper reports that a configuration-mixing method using Skyrme interactions can reasonably describe both shell-model-like and cluster-like states in \(^{12}\)C, but the calculated radius of the \(0_2^+\) (Hoyle) state is smaller than that obtained from previous \(\alpha\)-cluster models, and the excitation energy of the \(0_2^+\) state is slightly overestimated by about 1 MeV relative to experiment.

## Method

The authors generate a set of Slater determinants (SDs) with various correlated structures using the imaginary-time method in a three-dimensional coordinate-space representation, starting from random Gaussian wave packets. They then perform parity and angular momentum projections on these SDs and diagonalize the many-body Hamiltonian with the SLy4 Skyrme interaction in the space of projected SDs, applying a screening procedure to remove overcomplete basis functions. To assess convergence, they prepare ten independent sets of 50 SDs each and compute statistical averages and standard deviations of energies, transition strengths, and radii.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state binding energy: \(95.3 \pm 0.2\) MeV (correlation energy \(4.7 \pm 0.2\) MeV), compared to the measured value of 92.2 MeV.
- Excitation energy of the \(0_2^+\) (Hoyle) state: approximately 8.8 MeV; the \(0_3^+\) state appears at around 15 MeV.
- \(B(E2; 2_1^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+)\): \(8.6 \pm 0.2\) \(e^2\text{fm}^4\) (experiment: \(7.6 \pm 0.4\) \(e^2\text{fm}^4\)).
- \(B(E2; 0_2^+ \rightarrow 2_1^+)\): \(13.6 \pm 1.2\) \(e^2\text{fm}^4\) (experiment: \(13 \pm 2\) \(e^2\text{fm}^4\)).
- \(B(E3; 3_1^- \rightarrow 0_1^+)\): \(77 \pm 4\) \(e^2\text{fm}^6\) (experiment: \(107 \pm 14\) \(e^2\text{fm}^6\)).
- \(M(E0; 0_1^+ \rightarrow 0_2^+)\): \(4.5 \pm 0.2\) \(e\text{fm}^2\) (experiment: \(5.4 \pm 0.2\) \(e\text{fm}^2\)).

## Context

This work extends the configuration-mixing method previously reported by Shinohara (2006) to the study of \(^{12}\)C, using the Skyrme interaction without empirical parameters specific to this nucleus. It contrasts with microscopic cluster models such as the generator coordinate method (GCM) by Uegaki (1979) and the resonating group method (RGM) by Kamimura (1981), which assume \(\alpha\)-clusters from the outset and do not include spin-orbit interaction, leading to too low excitation energies for the ground-state rotational band. The results enable future ab-initio descriptions of cluster states by demonstrating that long-range correlations can be captured with a Skyrme Hamiltonian, and they provide a benchmark for comparing with no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations by Navratil (2003) and lattice calculations.
