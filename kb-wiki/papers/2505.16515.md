# Seniority-two valence-shell building blocks of the octupole phonon

> The relative ordering of $J^-$ levels of multiplets resulting from two-body excitations, which include a $J^π=3^-$ state that can contribute to the octupole phonon, are investigated in a simplistic shell-model approach. To calculate the relative level ordering, harmonic oscillator wavefunctions and a residual $δ$ interaction are used. The simplistic approach confirms for the particle-particle channel, the often stated preference of the $Δj=3, Δl =3$ subshell combination over the $Δj=3, Δl =1$ subshell combination through an enhanced energy gain. Furthermore, it is shown that, in the particle-hole channel, the gain is less pronounced for the $Δj=3, Δl =3$ subshell configuration. In combination with the overall structure of an oscillator shell, these results explain the comparatively low excitation energy for 3$^-_1$ excitations observed for the octupole-soft proton and neutron numbers predicted by various models.

## Key claim

The relative ordering of negative-parity levels in seniority-two valence-shell excitations, including the 3⁻ state that contributes to the octupole phonon, is investigated using a simplistic shell-model approach with harmonic oscillator wavefunctions and a residual delta interaction; the authors explicitly note that this approach neglects higher-seniority excitations and cross-oscillator-shell contributions, and that the spherical approach should be treated with care for nuclei that may adopt quadrupole-deformed shapes.

## Method

The authors calculated two-body matrix elements for particle-particle and particle-hole configurations using harmonic oscillator wavefunctions and a residual delta interaction, decomposing the energy shift into an empirical strength constant, a Slater integral of the radial wavefunctions, and an angular part determined by 3-j symbols. They focused exclusively on the two subshell combinations that can couple to a 3⁻ state within a single oscillator shell: the Δj=3, Δl=3 configuration [1(N+1)ₙ₊₃/₂, 2(N-2)ₙ₋₃/₂] and the Δj=3, Δl=1 configuration [1(N+1)ₙ₊₃/₂, 1Nₙ₋₁/₂], for oscillator shells N=3 through N=6.

## Key numbers

- For the Δj=3, Δl=3 configuration in the particle-particle channel, the angular part A for J=3 ranges from 40/21 (≈1.905) for N=3 to 1120/286 (≈3.916) for N=6.
- For the Δj=3, Δl=1 configuration in the particle-particle channel, the angular part A for J=3 ranges from 40/21 (≈1.905) for N=3 to 1120/286 (≈3.916) for N=6.
- The Slater integral F_R for the Δj=3, Δl=3 configuration at A=144 (N=4) is 0.42 × 10⁻³ fm⁻³, while for the Δj=3, Δl=1 configuration at the same mass it is 0.68 × 10⁻³ fm⁻³.
- The Slater integral F_R for the Δj=3, Δl=3 configuration at A=222 (N=5) is 0.27 × 10⁻³ fm⁻³, while for the Δj=3, Δl=1 configuration at the same mass it is 0.46 × 10⁻³ fm⁻³.
- The inverse oscillator length b is given as b = 41 A⁻¹/³ mc² / [2(ℏc)²] fm⁻², with values ranging from 0.121 fm⁻² at A=68 to 0.082 fm⁻² at A=222.

## Context

This work extends the general two-body matrix element calculations of Schiffer (1976) by focusing specifically on the valence-shell subshell combinations that contribute to the octupole phonon, using the most simplistic approach possible for maximum clarity. The results explain why the 3⁻₁ excitation energy is lowest at octupole-soft proton and neutron numbers (Z=34, 56, 88 and N=34, 56, 88, 134) as predicted by Butler and others, and the authors note that future work should investigate how the occupancy of identical orbitals for N_ν ≈ N_π nuclei affects electric dipole moments compared to the N_ν = N_π+1 case.
