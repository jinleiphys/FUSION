# Number of constituent quark scaling of elliptic flows in high multiplicity p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 5.02 TeV

> We briefly summarize our recent study on the number of constituent quark (NCQ) scaling of hadron elliptic flows in high multiplicity p-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 5.02 TeV. With the inclusion of hadron production via the quark coalescence model at intermediate $p_T$, the viscous hydrodynamics at low $p_T$, and jet fragmentation at high $p_T$, our $Hydro-Coal-Frag$ model provides a nice description of the $p_T$-spectra and differential elliptic flow $v_2(p_T)$ of pions, kaons and protons over the $p_T$ range from 0 to 6 GeV. Our results demonstrate that including the quark coalescence is essential for reproducing the observed approximate NCQ scaling of hadron $v_2$ at intermediate $p_T$ in experiments, indicating strongly the existence of partonic degrees of freedom and the formation of quark-gluon plasma in high multiplicity p--Pb collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim

Including hadron production via quark coalescence at intermediate transverse momentum is essential for reproducing the observed approximate number of constituent quark (NCQ) scaling of hadron elliptic flow in high multiplicity p-Pb collisions, which strongly indicates the existence of partonic degrees of freedom and the formation of quark-gluon plasma in these small collision systems.

## Method

The authors used a hybrid model called Hydro-Coal-Frag that combines three hadron production mechanisms: low transverse momentum hadrons from viscous hydrodynamics (VISH2+1), intermediate transverse momentum hadrons from quark coalescence (combining thermal partons from hydrodynamics with hard partons from the LBT energy loss model), and high transverse momentum hadrons from jet fragmentation (PYTHIA8). The two tunable parameters pT1 and pT2 were fixed by fitting the pT-spectra of pions, kaons, and protons as well as the p/pi yield ratio. All produced hadrons were then fed into the UrQMD model for hadronic rescattering until kinetic freeze-out.

## Key numbers

- Collision system: p-Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, 0-20% centrality
- The model describes pT-spectra and v2(pT) over the pT range from 0 to 6 GeV
- Tunable parameters: pT1 = 1.6 GeV (threshold for thermal partons from hydrodynamics), pT2 = 2.6 GeV (threshold for hard partons from LBT)
- The NCQ scaling is approximately reproduced in the range 1.4 < pT/n < 2.2 GeV
- The Hydro-Frag model (without coalescence) underestimates the magnitude of v2(pT)/n by about 50%

## Context

This work extends the study of NCQ scaling, previously established as evidence for quark-gluon plasma in large heavy-ion collision systems, to small collision systems such as p-Pb at the LHC. It builds on prior experimental measurements from ALICE, CMS, and ATLAS collaborations, and on theoretical work using the color glass condensate model (Dusling, 2015) and hydrodynamic models (Song, 2017). The results enable further investigation into whether partonic degrees of freedom exist in small collision systems and help disentangle the origin of collectivity in high multiplicity p-Pb collisions.
