# Ordered bicontinuous double-diamond morphology in subsaturation nuclear matter

> We propose to identify the new "intermediate" morphology in subsaturation nuclear matter observed in a recent quantum molecular dynamics simulation with the ordered bicontinuous double-diamond structure known in block copolymers. We estimate its energy density by incorporating the normalized area-volume relation given in a literature into the nuclear liquid drop model. The resulting energy density is higher than the other five known morphologies.

# Ordered bicontinuous double-diamond morphology in subsaturation nuclear matter

## Key claim

The "intermediate" morphology observed in quantum molecular dynamics simulations of subsaturation nuclear matter, characterized by negative Euler characteristic and a highly connected spongelike shape, is identified with the ordered bicontinuous double-diamond (OBDD) structure known from block copolymer physics. However, the estimated energy density of this OBDD phase is 25–30 keV/fm³ higher than that of the slab morphology, meaning it is not the ground state at any composition, consistent with the block copolymer case.

## Method

The author incorporates the normalized area-volume relation for the OBDD structure from Anderson et al. (1990) into the nuclear liquid drop model of Ravenhall et al. (1983). The surface energy density is computed from the area-volume relation using a cubic cell with lattice parameter X, and the Coulomb energy is determined by assuming the equilibrium condition E_S = 2E_C that holds for the five known morphologies. The bulk energy and electron energy are taken as morphology-independent, and the parameter set (x = 0.3, E_0 = -11.4 MeV, K = 291 MeV, n_s = 0.147 fm⁻³, σ = 0.74 MeV/fm²) from Ravenhall et al. (1983) is adopted.

## Key numbers

- The estimated energy density of the OBDD structure is 25–30 keV/fm³ higher than that of the slab morphology.
- The saturation density used is n_s = 0.147 fm⁻³.
- The surface tension is σ = 0.74 MeV/fm².
- The binding energy per baryon is E_0 = -11.4 MeV.
- The incompressibility is K = 291 MeV.
- The proton fraction is fixed at x = 0.3.
- The density range considered is 0.1 n_s to n_s (subsaturation nuclear matter).

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear pasta phases in subsaturation nuclear matter, where Ravenhall et al. (1983) and Hashimoto et al. (1984) first predicted non-spherical morphologies (sphere, cylinder, slab, tube, bubble). Watanabe et al. (2003) observed a new "intermediate" morphology in QMD simulations with negative Euler characteristic. The identification with the OBDD structure, known from block copolymer experiments by Thomas et al. (1986) and Hasegawa et al. (1987), provides a geometric interpretation. The result that OBDD is not the ground state is consistent with free-energy calculations in block copolymers by Anderson et al. (1990), Likos et al. (1995), and Olmsted et al. (1995). A note added in proof mentions that the bicontinuous double-gyroid structure (three-fold junctions) is favored in block copolymers, but distinguishing these mathematically idealized surfaces in the nuclear case would be difficult.
