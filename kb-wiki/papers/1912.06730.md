# A new approach to search for free neutron-antineutron oscillations using coherent neutron propagation in gas

> Coherent forward neutron propagation in gas is discussed as a new approach to search for neutron-antineutron oscillations ($ n-\bar{n}$), which violate both $B$ and $B-L$ conservation. We show that one can increase the probability of neutron - antineutron transitions to essentially the free neutron oscillation rate in the presence of a nonzero external magnetic field by tuning the density of an appropriate mixture of gases so that the neutron optical potential of the gas cancels that from an external magnetic field.

## Key claim

By tuning the density of a mixture of gases (such as parahydrogen and helium-4) so that the neutron optical potential of the gas cancels the magnetic potential from an external field, one can increase the probability of neutron-antineutron transitions to essentially the free oscillation rate even in the presence of a nonzero external magnetic field, provided the field is sufficiently uniform.

## Method

The authors model neutron-antineutron oscillations in a gas medium using a mixing matrix that includes the neutron and antineutron Fermi potentials (derived from coherent forward scattering lengths) and the magnetic interaction energy. They show that the parameter suppressing oscillations, S = (2μ_n·B - V_n + V_anti-n), can be set to zero by adjusting three experimental parameters: the density of parahydrogen, the density of helium-4, and the value of the residual magnetic field. The analysis uses the Lindblad formalism to account for decoherence from antineutron absorption in the gas, and shows that for practical observation times the suppression from decoherence is below 1%.

## Key numbers

- The best existing free neutron-antineutron oscillation limit from the ILL experiment is τ_n-anti-n > 0.86×10^8 s, using a neutron current of 1.25×10^11 n/s and average observation time of ~0.1 s.
- The neutron optical potential for oxygen at atmospheric pressure is V_n = 7.5×10^-11 eV; for comparison, the magnetic energy for B = 10^-9 T is |μ_n·B| = 6×10^-17 eV.
- Neutron coherent scattering lengths used: for 16O, b_nO = 5.8 fm; for hydrogen, b_nH = -3.7 fm; for 4He, b_nHe = 3.3 fm.
- The predicted antineutron scattering length follows b_anti-nA = 1.54 A^1/3, compared to b_nA = 1.35 A^1/3 for neutrons.
- For parahydrogen gas at 100 μbar pressure over 100 meters, neutron attenuation is below 1%.
- The decoherent damping reduces the oscillation probability by less than 1% compared to the undamped case for the proposed operating conditions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous proposal (Nesvizhevsky et al., 2018) to use coherent neutron reflection from mirrors for neutron-antineutron searches, now applying coherent forward scattering in gas instead. The idea shares similarities with proposals for neutron-mirror neutron oscillations (Berezhiani, 2019) and with the MSW mechanism for neutrino oscillations in matter (Wolfenstein, 1978; Mikheyev and Smirnov, 1985). The approach relaxes the most severe technical requirement of past free neutron experiments (Baldo et al., 1994) by allowing operation in a much larger but uniform magnetic field, reducing the need for extensive magnetic shielding. The practical implementation requires theoretical understanding of low-energy antineutron-nucleus interactions comparable to that needed for the mirror reflection approach.
