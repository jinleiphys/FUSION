# Effects of medium-induced $ρ-ω$ meson mixing on the equation of state in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter

> We reexamine effects of the $ρ-ω$ meson mixing mediated by nucleon polarizations on the symmetry energy in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter. Taking into account the rearrangement term neglected in previous studies by others, we evaluate the $ρ-ω$ mixing angle in a novel way within the Relativistic Mean-Field Models with and without chiral limits. It is found that the symmetry energy is significantly softened at high densities contrary to the finding in earlier studies. As the first step of going beyond the lowest-order calculations, we also solve the RPA equation for the $ρ-ω$ mixing. In this case, it is found that the symmetry energy is not only significantly softened by the $ρ-ω$ mixing at supra-saturation densities, similar to the lowest-order $ρ-ω$ mixing, but interestingly also softened at subsaturation densities. In addition, the softening of the symmetry energy at subsaturation densities can be partly suppressed by the nonlinear self-interaction of the $σ$ meson.

## Key claim
The authors reexamine the medium-induced ρ-ω meson mixing in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter and find that, contrary to earlier studies (e.g., Kistner et al. (1997) and Isayev et al. (2000)), the symmetry energy is significantly softened at high densities when the previously neglected rearrangement term is included; at subsaturation densities, the softening occurs only when the random phase approximation (RPA) is used, and it can be partly suppressed by nonlinear σ meson self-interactions.

## Method
The authors use relativistic mean-field (RMF) models with and without chiral limits (the SLC and NL3 parameter sets, respectively), incorporating the Brown-Rho (BR) scaling for density-dependent meson masses and coupling constants. They compute the ρ-ω mixing angle by matching the total energy density including the lowest-order nucleon polarization diagram, and also solve the RPA (Dyson) equation to include higher-order nucleon loop contributions. The rearrangement term in the pressure, required for thermodynamic consistency, is explicitly included.

## Key numbers
- The mixing angle θ reaches a maximum magnitude of about 40° in pure neutron matter at very low densities, well below 45°, avoiding the unphysical imaginary ρ meson mass seen in earlier studies.
- At saturation density (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³), the symmetry energy is fixed to 31.6 MeV for both the CSLC and CNL3 models.
- In npe matter at β equilibrium (asymmetry α ≈ 0.92), the ρ and ω meson masses at saturation density are 0.846 and 0.9 times their respective masses in symmetric matter for the CSLC model.
- At α = 0.2 (approximately the average isospin asymmetry for ²⁰⁸Pb), the scaled masses at saturation density are 0.878 and 0.87 for the ρ and ω mesons, corresponding to a splitting of about 17 MeV.
- The dimensionless mixing parameter γ varies from near zero in symmetric matter to about 0.2 in highly isospin-asymmetric matter at low densities.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of ρ-ω mixing in asymmetric nuclear matter by Kistner et al. (1997) and Isayev et al. (2000), which neglected the rearrangement term and found a sharp stiffening of the symmetry energy at supra-normal densities with a mixing angle near 45°. The authors show that including the rearrangement term avoids causality violation and instead produces a significant softening of the symmetry energy. The results also connect to the Brown-Rho scaling scenario for meson mass dropping and to experimental constraints on the symmetry energy from heavy-ion reactions (Tsang et al. (2004), Chen et al. (2005), Li et al. (2005)). The work enables further studies of the symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities, relevant for neutron star physics and future radioactive beam experiments.
