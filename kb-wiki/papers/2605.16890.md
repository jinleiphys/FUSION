# A self-consistent spectral framework for inclusive non-elastic breakup, with the Trojan Horse method as the sub-Coulomb resonant limit

> The Trojan Horse Method (THM) extracts low-energy charged-particle resonance strengths through a plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) reduction of a three-body transfer matrix element. The Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) inclusive non-elastic breakup framework has not been brought to the sub-Coulomb astrophysical regime where THM operates. I introduce a diagonal isolated-pole spectral ansatz for the absorptive participant-target optical potential with three explicit validity conditions, two closed by $R$-matrix tabulations and the third a model-dependent continuum-decoupling diagnostic. The IAV inclusive cross section reduces in the isolated-resonance limit to a sum of per-pole distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) pole cross sections weighted by channel branching ratios. The factorized PWIA-THM resonance-strength formula is then identified as a non-perturbative reduction of the per-pole DWBA cross section under four approximations (plane-wave substitution on the entrance and exit distorted waves, zero-range or surface-localized treatment of the spectator-participant interaction, on-shell evaluation of the binary subreaction vertex, and post-form remnant neglect), not a multiplicative correction factor. The per-pole DWBA pole cross section is the natural extraction quantity for sub-Coulomb resonance-strength analysis.

## Key claim

The Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) inclusive non-elastic breakup framework, previously applied only at intermediate and high energies, is brought to the sub-Coulomb astrophysical regime of the Trojan Horse Method (THM) through a diagonal isolated-pole spectral ansatz for the absorptive participant-target optical potential, with three explicit validity conditions; the factorized PWIA-THM resonance-strength formula is then identified as a non-perturbative reduction of the per-pole DWBA pole cross section under four specific approximations, not as a multiplicative correction factor.

## Method

The author introduces a diagonal isolated-pole spectral ansatz for the absorptive part of the participant-target optical potential, formulated through three validity conditions: (A1) diagonality of the absorptive potential on the resonance-state pole basis, (A2) decoupling from the non-resonant continuum of the participant-target subsystem, and (A3) pole isolation with half-width small against nearest-neighbor spacing. Within this ansatz, the IAV inclusive non-elastic breakup cross section reduces in the isolated-resonance limit to a sum of per-pole distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) pole cross sections weighted by Lorentzian profiles. A three-layer Feshbach decomposition resolves the width dictionary relating the spectral pole half-width to the total and non-elastic decay widths. The factorized PWIA-THM resonance-strength formula is then derived as a non-perturbative reduction of the per-pole DWBA cross section under four approximations: plane-wave substitution on entrance and exit distorted waves, zero-range or surface-localized treatment of the spectator-participant interaction, on-shell evaluation of the binary subreaction vertex, and post-form remnant neglect.

## Key numbers

- The spectral pole half-width is identified as \(\xi_n^\text{IAV} = \Gamma_n^\text{non-el}/2 \simeq \Gamma_n^\text{tot}/2\) through the three-layer Feshbach decomposition, with the second equality holding in the sub-Coulomb limit where the participant-channel partial width \(\Gamma_x^n \ll \Gamma_n^\text{non-el}\).
- The off-diagonal matrix elements of the absorptive potential satisfy the Cauchy-Schwarz bound \(|W_{nm}|^2 \leq \xi_n^\text{IAV}\,\xi_m^\text{IAV}\), giving the controlling ratio \(|W_{nm}|/|E_n-E_m| \leq \sqrt{\xi_n^\text{IAV}\,\xi_m^\text{IAV}}/|E_n-E_m|\) for condition (A1).
- A recent THM measurement of the 11 keV resonance strength in the \(^{19}\text{F}(p,\alpha\gamma)^{16}\text{O}\) reaction disagrees by approximately a factor of six in the partial decay strength with the \(R\)-matrix evaluation using JUNA direct measurements above \(\approx 70\) keV.

## Context

This work extends the IAV inclusive non-elastic breakup framework (Ichimura, Austern, Vincent 1985; Lei and Moro 2018) to the sub-Coulomb astrophysical regime where the Trojan Horse Method operates, which has not been previously assessed in a controlled framework. The paper addresses the quantitative tension in the \(^{19}\text{F}(p,\alpha\gamma)^{16}\text{O}\) reaction rate, where a recent THM measurement (Su 2025) disagrees with \(R\)-matrix evaluations using JUNA direct measurements (Zhang 2021, 2022; Chen 2024; Liu 2025). The framework enables sub-Coulomb resonance-strength analysis using the per-pole DWBA pole cross section as the natural extraction quantity, rather than a multiplicative correction factor on top of the PWIA-THM expression, and provides explicit validity conditions testable from \(R\)-matrix tabulations and continuum models.
