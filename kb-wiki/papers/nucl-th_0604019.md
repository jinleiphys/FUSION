# Effects of spin-orbit interaction on nuclear response and neutrino mean free path

> The effects of the spin-orbit component of the particle-hole interaction on nuclear response functions and neutrino mean free path are examined. A complete treatment of the full Skyrme interaction in the case of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter is given. Numerical results for neutron matter are discussed. It is shown that the effects of the spin-orbit interaction remain small, even at momentum transfer larger than the Fermi momentum. The neutrino mean free paths are marginally affected.

## Key claim

The spin-orbit component of the Skyrme particle-hole interaction couples the S=0 and S=1 spin channels in nuclear response functions, but its effects on the dynamical structure functions and neutrino mean free paths remain small (a few percent) except when the system approaches an instability region in the S=1 channel, where even a small modification can produce large changes.

## Method

The authors derived the full RPA response function for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter including the zero-range spin-orbit term of the Skyrme interaction, which couples the S=0 and S=1 spin channels through a modified complex interaction parameter \(\widetilde{W}_1^{(\alpha)}(q,\omega,T)\). They solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the correlated particle-hole Green's function and computed the dynamical structure functions and neutrino mean free paths for neutron matter using the SLy230b Skyrme force at various densities, temperatures, and momentum transfers.

## Key numbers

- The spin-orbit interaction modifies the neutrino mean free path by only 1% at T=10 MeV, 3-4% at T=20 MeV, and 5-10% at T=30 MeV for densities \(\rho_0\) and \(2\rho_0\) (Table II).
- At T=10 MeV and \(\rho=\rho_0\), the HF mean free path is 45.1 m; the RPA ratio with spin-orbit is 1.03, without spin-orbit is 1.04.
- At T=30 MeV and \(\rho=2\rho_0\), the RPA ratio with spin-orbit is 0.27, without spin-orbit is 0.38.
- The spin-orbit effect becomes dramatic near an instability in the S=1 channel at \(\rho \simeq \rho_0\) and \(q \simeq 2\) fm\(^{-1}\) for the SLy230b force.
- The interaction parameter \(W_2^{S=0}=163.55\) MeV.fm\(^5\) and \(W_2^{S=1}=-163.55\) MeV.fm\(^5\) for SLy230b.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Garcia-Recio et al. (1992) for RPA response functions with Skyrme interactions by including the previously ignored two-body spin-orbit component, and builds on the neutron matter calculations of Navarro et al. (1999). The results show that spin-orbit effects are generally negligible for neutrino mean free paths in neutron stars, but the coupling between spin channels can become important near instabilities characteristic of Skyrme-type interactions (Margueron et al., 2002). The formalism enables future studies of finite-range spin-orbit forces and applications to asymmetric nuclear matter.
