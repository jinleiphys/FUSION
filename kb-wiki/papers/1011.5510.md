# Klein-Gordon Equation in Hydrodynamical Form

> We follow and modify the Feshbach-Villars formalism by separating the Klein-Gordon equation into two coupled time-dependent Schroedinger equations for particle and antiparticle wave function components with positive probability densities. We find that the equation of motion for the probability densities is in the form of relativistic hydrodynamics where various forces have their classical counterparts, with the additional element of the quantum stress tensor that depends on the derivatives of the amplitude of the wave function. We derive the equation of motion for the Wigner function and we find that its approximate classical weak-field limit coincides with the equation of motion for the distribution function in the collisionless kinetic theory.

## Key claim

The Klein-Gordon equation can be separated into two coupled time-dependent Schrödinger equations for particle and antiparticle wave function components with positive probability densities, and the resulting equation of motion for these probability densities takes the form of relativistic hydrodynamics, but only in the simplified case where particle-antiparticle pair production is suppressed and the interaction strength is not large compared to the rest mass.

## Method

The author modifies the Feshbach-Villars formalism by introducing a new definition of the auxiliary wave function ψ₄ and a complex conjugation in the definition of the antiparticle component χ₋, so that both particle and antiparticle Schrödinger equations contain kinetic energy operators with the same sign. The Klein-Gordon equation is then separated into two coupled time-dependent Schrödinger equations for χ₊ and χ₋, and the equations of continuity and Euler equations for the probability densities are derived. The Wigner function is also constructed from these wave functions, and its equation of motion is compared to the collisionless kinetic theory limit.

## Key numbers

- The quantum stress tensor is given by p_{± ij}^{(q)} = −(ℏ²/4m)∇²φ₊² δ_{ij} + (ℏ²/m)∇ᵢφ₊ ∇ⱼφ₊, where ℏ is Planck's constant divided by 2π and m is the particle mass.
- The conserved particle number for a single-particle state is n_particle = ∫ d³r (E − e_ν A⁰)/m (|χ₊|² − |χ₋|²), which takes the value n_ν = ±1 for a particle or antiparticle state.
- For a density-dependent two-body interaction v_s(r, r₂) = [a₂ + a₃ n((r + r₂)/2)] δ(r − r₂), the mean-field stress tensor contribution is p_{ij}^{(v)} = ½[a₂ + 2a₃ n(r)] n² δ_{ij}.

## Context

This work extends the Feshbach-Villars formalism (Feshbach and Villars, 1958) by modifying the definitions of the auxiliary wave function and antiparticle component to make relativistic properties more apparent and to facilitate a hydrodynamical description. It generalizes the earlier non-relativistic hydrodynamical formulation of the Schrödinger equation (Wong, 1976; Wong, 1977) to the Klein-Gordon equation, and connects to the relativistic hydrodynamics used in heavy-ion collisions (Landau, 1953; Bjorken, 1983; Baym et al., 1983; Ollitrault, 1992). The work enables future studies of relativistic hydrodynamics for strongly interacting quantum systems where quantum stress tensors and mean-field interactions dominate over thermal effects, particularly for systems such as the quark-gluon plasma near the transition temperature T_c.
