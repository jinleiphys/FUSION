# Large-scale shell-model study of two-neutrino double-beta decay of $^{82}$Se, $^{94}$Zr, $^{108}$Cd, $^{124}$Sn, $^{128}$Te, $^{130}$Te, $^{136}$Xe, and $^{150}$Nd

> Large-scale shell-model calculations have been performed for the study of two neutrino double-beta ($2νββ$) decay in $^{82}$Se, $^{94}$Zr, $^{108}$Cd, $^{124}$Sn, $^{128}$Te, $^{130}$Te, $^{136}$Xe, and $^{150}$Nd. We have employed JUN45 interaction to calculate the nuclear matrix element (NME) for $2νββ$ decay in $^{82}$Se. In the case of $^{94}$Zr, the glekpn effective interaction is used. For $^{108}$Cd, we have used a realistic effective interaction derived through the G-matrix approach. In the case of $^{124}$Sn, $^{128,130}$Te and $^{136}$Xe, the sn100pn effective interaction is employed. For $^{150}$Nd, we have used KHHE effective interaction based on holes in a $^{208}$Pb core. We have extracted the half-lives of these nuclei for the $2νββ$ decay with the help of calculated NME. Our results are consistent with the available experimental half-lives. The variation of cumulative $2νββ$ NME with respect to the excitation energy of the intermediate $1^+$ states is also shown, and in all cases, it is ensured that their values are almost saturated. In the present work we have calculated more intermediate $1^+$ states as much as possible in comparison to results available in the literature.

## Key claim

Large-scale shell-model calculations using five different effective interactions (JUN45, glekpn, G-matrix, sn100pn, KHHE) produce two-neutrino double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements and half-lives for eight nuclei that are consistent with available experimental data, but the agreement is qualified by the use of effective axial-vector coupling constants (\(g_A^{eff}\)) that are adjusted per nucleus and by the limited model spaces that may not fully capture contributions from the Gamow-Teller giant resonance region.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations using the NuShellX code with five different effective interactions tailored to each mass region: JUN45 for \(^{82}\)Se, glekpn for \(^{94}\)Zr, a G-matrix derived interaction for \(^{108}\)Cd, sn100pn for \(^{124}\)Sn, \(^{128}\)Te, \(^{130}\)Te, and \(^{136}\)Xe, and KHHE for \(^{150}\)Nd. They computed the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for \(2\nu\beta\beta\) decay by summing over intermediate \(1^+\) states in the daughter nuclei, using the standard expression involving Gamow-Teller transition matrix elements and energy denominators, and extracted half-lives using phase-space factors from the literature and effective \(g_A\) values.

## Key numbers

- \(^{82}\)Se: calculated NME \(|M_{2\nu}| = 0.1713\), extracted half-life \(0.68 \times 10^{20}\) yr, compared to experimental average \(0.87^{+0.02}_{-0.01} \times 10^{20}\) yr (Barabash).
- \(^{94}\)Zr: calculated NME \(|M_{2\nu}| = 0.0611\), extracted half-life \(9.08 \times 10^{23}\) yr, within experimental range \((0.31 \sim 66) \times 10^{23}\) yr (Tretyak).
- \(^{108}\)Cd: calculated NME \(|M_{2\nu}| = 0.1729\), extracted half-life \(1.19 \times 10^{27}\) yr, compared to experimental lower limit \(> 4.1 \times 10^{17}\) yr (Danevich) and range \((3.939 \sim 9.959) \times 10^{27}\) yr (Raina).
- \(^{124}\)Sn: calculated NME \(|M_{2\nu}| = 0.0369\), extracted half-life \(1.43 \times 10^{21}\) yr (no experimental value available).
- \(^{128}\)Te: calculated NME \(|M_{2\nu}| = 0.0255\), extracted half-life \(6.82 \times 10^{24}\) yr, compared to experimental \((2.25 \pm 0.09) \times 10^{24}\) yr (Barabash).
- \(^{130}\)Te: calculated NME \(|M_{2\nu}| = 0.0516\), extracted half-life \(9.78 \times 10^{20}\) yr, compared to experimental \((7.91 \pm 0.21) \times 10^{20}\) yr (Barabash).
- \(^{136}\)Xe: calculated NME \(|M_{2\nu}| = 0.0748\), extracted half-life \(1.27 \times 10^{21}\) yr, compared to experimental \((2.18 \pm 0.05) \times 10^{21}\) yr (Barabash).
- \(^{150}\)Nd: calculated NME \(|M_{2\nu}| = 0.0691\), extracted half-life \(6.05 \times 10^{18}\) yr, compared to experimental \((9.34 \pm 0.65) \times 10^{18}\) yr (Barabash).

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of \(2\nu\beta\beta\) decay by calculating more intermediate \(1^+\) states than most prior calculations, achieving saturation of the cumulative NME for several nuclei. The results are compared with earlier calculations using QRPA (Simkovic, Suhonen), PHFB (Raina), DSM (Sahu), IBM (Nomura), and other shell-model calculations (Caurier, Horoi). The good agreement with experimental half-lives for six of the eight nuclei suggests the same interactions and model spaces can be used for future calculations of neutrinoless double-beta decay NMEs, which would help constrain neutrino mass bounds.
