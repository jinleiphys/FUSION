# Critique of a Pion Exchange Model for Interquark Forces

> I describe four serious defects of a widely discussed pion exchange model for interquark forces: it doesn't solve the "spin-orbit problem" as advertised, it fails to describe the internal structure of baryon resonances, it leads to disastrous conclusions when extended to mesons, and it is not reasonably connected to the physics of heavy-light systems.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Glozman-Riska pion-exchange (OPE) model for interquark forces is ruled out by four serious defects, but the central hedge is that while the model is unsustainable in its extreme version, some elements of meson-exchange physics surely play a role in baryons, and the dominance of one-gluon-exchange (OGE) over OPE must have a dynamical origin rather than a general principle.

## Method

The author accepts the Glozman-Riska OPE model at face value and critiques it against four criteria: the spin-orbit problem in baryons, the internal wave functions of baryon resonances, the extension to mesons, and the connection to heavy-light systems. The analysis compares OPE predictions to those of the standard OGE-based Isgur-Karl model and to experimental data on baryon and meson spectra, decay amplitudes, and mixing angles, using heavy quark symmetry and the 1/N_c expansion as theoretical frameworks.

## Key numbers

- The spin-orbit matrix element extracted from isovector mesons is -3 ± 20 MeV, much smaller than OGE would produce alone.
- A fit to heavy-light meson data gives an OGE spin-orbit matrix element of +240 MeV and a Thomas precession spin-orbit matrix element from confinement of -200 MeV, nearly cancelling.
- The Isgur-Karl model predicts the N*1/2- mixing angle θ = -arctan((√5-1)/2) ≈ -32°, matching the empirical value of ≈ -35°, while the OPE model predicts θ = ±13°.
- The Isgur-Karl model predicts the N*3/2- mixing angle θ = arctan(√10/(14+√206)) ≈ +6°, matching the empirical value of ≈ +10°, while the OPE model predicts θ = ±8°.
- The Λ(1405)1/2- mass is 1405 MeV, 85 MeV below the Isgur-Karl model prediction of 1490 MeV.
- The center-of-gravity of Λc(2627)3/2- and Λc(2594)1/2- is 330 MeV above Λc(2285), compared to 365 MeV for the Λ(1520) and Λ(1405) above Λ(1115).
- The predicted splitting of the (ΛQ3/2-, ΛQ1/2-) multiplet using 1/m_Q scaling gives 110 MeV in the Λ sector, compared to the observed 115 MeV.
- The OZI-violating amplitude A_OZI from kaon exchange would be of order 200 MeV (the Σ*-Σ splitting), but the observed A_OZI for vector mesons is only about 10 MeV.
- The K*-K splitting predicted from 1/m_Q scaling of the D*-D splitting (141 MeV) is 460 MeV, close to the actual splitting of 400 MeV.

## Context

This paper extends the critique of the Glozman-Riska OPE model (Glozman and Riska, 1996) by comparing it to the standard OGE-based quark model of De Rujula, Georgi, and Glashow (1975), applied to baryons most extensively by Isgur and Karl (1978). The work contradicts claims that OPE solves the baryon spin-orbit problem and enables a clearer understanding of why OGE must dominate residual interquark forces in both mesons and baryons, while suggesting that future studies of duality violation in Z-graphs could clarify the role of meson-exchange effects.
