# Strong decays of multi-strangeness baryon resonances in the quark model

> Decay properties of multi-strangeness $Ξ$ and $Ω$ baryon resonances are investigated within the constituent quark model by including relativistic corrections. The strong decay widths of various $Ξ$ and $Ω$ resonances are computed and tested for internal quark configurations to pin down their spin-parity ($J^P$) quantum numbers and internal structures. We found that the Roper-like resonances in the multi-strangeness sector have large decay widths bearing resemblances to their siblings in light and heavy baryon sectors. In addition, we obtained the decay ratio for the $Ω(2012)$ as $Γ{(Ω\to Ξ\bar{K} π)} / Γ{(Ω\to Ξ\bar{K})}=4.5\%$, which is consistent with the experimental data when $J^P=3/2^-$ is assigned. This observation holds the possibility that the $Ω(2012)$ could naturally be explained as a three-quark state in the quark model. The decays of other low-lying $Ξ$ and $Ω$ resonances are systematically analyzed, which would be useful to unveil the structure of these resonances in future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $\Omega(2012)$ resonance can be naturally explained as a three-quark state in the constituent quark model when assigned $J^P=3/2^-$, based on the computed decay ratio $\Gamma{(\Omega\to \Xi \bar{K} \pi)} / \Gamma{(\Omega\to \Xi \bar{K})}=4.5\%$ being consistent with experimental data, but this conclusion depends on the inclusion of relativistic corrections and the specific choice of quark model parameters.

## Method

The authors used a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with a harmonic oscillator potential and SU(3) flavor symmetry, computing strong decay widths for one-pseudoscalar-meson emission decays of $\Xi$ and $\Omega$ baryon resonances. They employed the axial-vector quark-meson coupling interaction and included relativistic corrections up to $O(1/m^2)$ via the Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation, which were found to be particularly important for Roper-like states where leading-order terms are suppressed. The model parameters (constituent quark mass $m$ and spring constant $k$) were fixed to reproduce baryon core radii from lattice calculations, with $m=0.417$ GeV for $\Xi$ baryons and $m=0.450$ GeV for $\Omega$ baryons.

## Key numbers

- Decay ratio for $\Omega(2012)$: $\Gamma{(\Omega\to \Xi \bar{K} \pi)} / \Gamma{(\Omega\to \Xi \bar{K})}=4.5\%$, consistent with experimental data when $J^P=3/2^-$ is assigned
- Predicted decay width of $\Xi(1530)^0$: $\Gamma_{\rm NR+RC}=10.7$ MeV, compared to experimental value $9.1 \pm 0.5$ MeV (PDG 2020)
- Predicted total decay width of $\Xi(1690)^0$ with $\ket{70,{}^{2}8,1,1,\tfrac{1}{2}^-}$ assignment: $\Gamma_{\rm NR+RC}=35.4$ MeV, compatible with experimental upper limit $<30$ MeV
- Predicted total decay width of $\Xi(1620)^0$ with $\ket{70,{}^{4}8,1,1,\tfrac{1}{2}^-}$ assignment: $\Gamma_{\rm NR+RC}=34.6$ MeV, compared to experimental $40\pm15$ MeV
- Constituent quark mass parameters: $m=0.417$ GeV for $\Xi$ baryons, $m=0.450$ GeV for $\Omega$ baryons
- Harmonic oscillator parameters for $\Xi$ baryons: $(\omega_\lambda, a_\lambda, a_\rho) = (0.379, 0.325, 0.281)$ GeV; for $\Omega$ baryons: $(\omega_\lambda, a_\lambda, a_\rho) = (0.365, 0.331, 0.287)$ GeV
- Spring constant: $k = 0.02$ GeV$^3$
- Decay constants: $f_\pi=93$ MeV, $f_K=111$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model analysis of baryon resonances to the multi-strangeness sector, following the authors' previous work on relativistic corrections in heavy baryon decays (Arifi, Suenaga, Hosaka 2021). The study addresses the recent experimental interest in $\Xi$ and $\Omega$ resonances triggered by discoveries at Belle, BESIII, and LHCb collaborations, and aims to resolve the debate over whether the $\Omega(2012)$ is a hadronic molecule (as proposed by several groups around 2018-2020) or a standard three-quark state. The results provide systematic predictions for decay patterns of low-lying $\Xi$ and $\Omega$ resonances that can be tested in future experiments at facilities such as the J-PARC hadron facility, PANDA, and the Kaon Long Facility.
