# Production of gluons in the classical field model for heavy ion collisions

> The initial stages of relativistic heavy ion collisions are studied numerically in the framework of a 2+1 dimensional classical Yang-Mills theory. We calculate the energy and number densities and momentum spectra of the produced gluons. The model is also applied to non central collisions. The numerical results are discussed in the light of RHIC measurements of energy and multiplicity and other theoretical calculations. Some problems of the present approach are pointed out.

# Production of gluons in the classical field model for heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The classical Yang-Mills model can reproduce the observed RHIC multiplicity with a saturation scale Λ_s ≈ 2 GeV, but the resulting average energy per particle (1.5 GeV) is well within the bound from early thermalization, while a competing calculation by Krasnitz, Nara, and Venugopalan gives 3.1 GeV per particle, which is very hard to reconcile with that bound and may indicate that RHIC energies are not in the domain of validity of the classical field model.

## Method

The paper solves the 2+1 dimensional classical Yang-Mills equations on a transverse lattice using a Hamiltonian formalism with boost-invariant fields, starting from the McLerran-Venugopalan model where each nucleus is represented by static random color sources with a Gaussian distribution. The initial conditions at proper time τ = 0 are obtained by matching the pure gauge solutions of the two nuclei on the light cone, and the system is then evolved in proper time using lattice equations of motion. Gluon number spectra are extracted from equal-time field correlators in Coulomb gauge using a free lattice dispersion relation.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless energy density parameter f_E = 0.227 ± 0.003 and number density parameter f_N = 0.289 ± 0.003 for √(g⁴ μ² π R_A²) = 120 (with μ = 0.49 GeV, g = 2, π R_A² = 148 fm²)
- The initial multiplicity per unit rapidity dN_init/dη = 0.29 g² π R_A² μ², giving μ = 0.48 GeV or Λ_s = 1.9 GeV when matched to dN_init/dη ≈ 1000
- The initial transverse energy per unit rapidity dE_init/dη = 0.23 g⁴ π R_A² μ³ = 1.5 GeV × dN_init/dη
- The observed final transverse energy at RHIC is dE_final/dη ≈ 600 GeV, with total multiplicity dN_final/dη ≈ 1000
- The phase space density f(k) is of order one only up to momenta of a fraction of g² μ, meaning the assumption of high occupation numbers is only marginally satisfied

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan model (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1994) for nucleus-nucleus collisions first studied numerically by Krasnitz, McLerran, and collaborators (Krasnitz et al., 2001). The results differ from those of Krasnitz, Nara, and Venugopalan (2003) by approximately a factor of two in the energy density f_E, attributed to a different normalization of SU(3) generators. The paper also explores finite nuclei with a saturation-inspired modification of the source correlation function, similar to the color neutrality approach of Krasnitz, Nara, and Venugopalan (2003) and Lappi and McLerran (2003). The work enables future incorporation of the JIMWLK renormalization group equation and extension to 3+1 dimensional models for studying thermalization.
