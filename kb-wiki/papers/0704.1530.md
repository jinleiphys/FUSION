# A study of the $p d \to p d η$ reaction

> A study of the $p d \to p d η$ reaction in the energy range where the recent data from Uppsala are available, is done in the two-step model of $η$ production including the final state interaction. The $η-d$ final state interaction is incorporated through the solution of the Lippmann Schwinger equation using an elastic scattering matrix element, $T_{ηd \to ηd}$, which is required to be half off-shell. It is written in a factorized form, with an off-shell form factor multiplying an on-shell part given by an effective range expansion up to the fourth power in momentum. The parameters of this expansion have been taken from an existing recent relativistic Faddeev equation solution for the $ηNN$ system corresponding to different $η-N$ scattering amplitudes. Calculations have also been done using few body equations within a finite rank approximation (FRA) to generate $T_{ηd \to ηd}$. The $p-d$ final state interaction is included in the spirit of the Watson-Migdal prescription by multiplying the matrix element by the inverse of the Jost function. The $η-d$ interaction is found to be dominant in the region of small invariant $η-d$ mass, $M_{ηd}$. The $p-d$ interaction enhances the cross section in the whole region of $M_{ηd}$, but is larger for large $M_{ηd}$. We find nearly isotropic angular distributions of the proton and the deuteron in the final state. All the above observations are in agreement with data. The production mechanism for the entire range of the existing data on the $p d \to p d η$ reaction seems to be dominated by the two-step model of $η$ production.

## Key claim

The two-step model of η production, where the η meson is produced via an intermediate pion (p p → d π⁺ followed by π⁺ N → η N), dominates the p d → p d η reaction across the entire measured energy range, contrary to earlier work by Tengblad et al. (2004) which found comparable contributions from a direct one-step mechanism at high energies.

## Method

The production amplitude was calculated using a two-step mechanism involving an intermediate pion, with the T-matrix for p p → d π⁺ taken from the SAID partial-wave analysis and the T-matrix for π⁺ n → η p from a coupled-channel formalism including πN, ηN, and πΔ channels. The η-d final state interaction was incorporated through a half-off-shell T-matrix, described either by a factorized form with an effective range expansion (up to fourth power in momentum) using parameters from a relativistic Faddeev equation solution for the ηNN system, or by solving few-body equations within a finite rank approximation. The p-d final state interaction was included via the Watson-Migdal prescription using the inverse Jost function for both doublet and quadruplet spin states, including Coulomb effects.

## Key numbers

- The momentum transfer in the one-step (direct) process is approximately 840 MeV/c near threshold and remains around 600 MeV/c even at the highest beam energy of 1096 MeV
- The one-step contribution to the total cross section is about four orders of magnitude smaller than the two-step contribution at threshold, and remains two orders of magnitude smaller at the highest available beam energy (T_p = 1096 MeV)
- The η-N scattering lengths used in the calculations range from (0.42, 0.34) fm to (1.07, 0.26) fm
- The effective range expansion parameters for η-d scattering were taken from a relativistic Faddeev equation calculation corresponding to η-N scattering lengths of 0.42 + i0.34 fm, 0.75 + i0.27 fm, and 1.07 + i0.26 fm
- The p-d effective range parameters used are a_Q = 11.88 fm, b_Q = 2.63 fm, a_D = 2.73 fm, and b_D = 2.27 fm, determined from fits to p-d elastic scattering phase shifts up to relative momentum around 200 MeV/c
- The separation energy of ³He into p-d is taken as 5.48 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier studies of the p d → ³He η reaction (Upadhyay et al., 2005) to the p d → p d η channel, using the same two-step production model and final state interaction treatments. It directly contradicts the findings of Tengblad et al. (2004), who found comparable contributions from direct and two-step mechanisms at high energies, but did not include final state interactions and used approximate kinematics. The results enable improved understanding of the η-N interaction through analysis of η-d final state interactions, and the authors note that the η-d effective range parameters from the relativistic Faddeev equation calculation of Garcilazo et al. (2003) with a_ηN = (1.07, 0.26) fm give the best agreement with data.
