# A Lee-Yang--inspired functional with a density--dependent neutron-neutron scattering length

> Inspired by the low--density Lee-Yang expansion for the energy of a dilute Fermi gas of density $ρ$ and momentum $k_F$, we introduce here a Skyrme--type functional that contains only $s$-wave terms and provides, at the mean--field level, (i) a satisfactory equation of state for neutron matter from extremely low densities up to densities close to the equilibrium point, and (ii) a good--quality equation of state for symmetric matter at density scales around the saturation point. This is achieved by using a density--dependent neutron-neutron scattering length $a(ρ$) which satisfies the low--density limit (for Fermi momenta going to zero) and has a density dependence tuned in such a way that the low--density constraint $|a(ρ) k_F| \le 1$ is satisfied at all density scales.

## Key claim

The authors introduce a Skyrme-type functional with a density-dependent neutron-neutron scattering length \(a(\rho)\) that, at the mean-field level, provides a satisfactory equation of state for neutron matter from extremely low densities up to densities close to the equilibrium point of symmetric matter, and a good-quality equation of state for symmetric matter around saturation, but only by imposing the low-density constraint \(|a(\rho) k_F| \le 1\) at all density scales and by treating the effective range as a free parameter to recover missing attractive contributions.

## Method

The authors construct a Skyrme functional containing only \(s\)-wave terms (parameters \(t_0, t_1, t_3\) and \(x_0, x_1, x_3\)) whose mean-field equation of state for neutron matter reproduces the first four terms of the Lee-Yang expansion. They impose a density-dependent scattering length \(a(\rho)\) that equals \(-18.9\) fm for Fermi momenta up to \(k_F^{\text{max}} \sim 0.05\) fm\(^{-1}\) (density \(\sim 4 \times 10^{-6}\) fm\(^{-3}\)), and beyond that is tuned so that \(|k_F a(\rho)| \le 1\) holds. The parameters \(t_0, t_3, t_1\) are adjusted to give a satisfactory symmetric matter equation of state, while the effective range \(r_s\) is treated as a free parameter (set to \(-4.5\) fm) to include the \(k_F^5\) terms and provide the missing attractive contribution.

## Key numbers

- The free neutron-neutron \(s\)-wave scattering length is \(-18.9\) fm.
- The maximum Fermi momentum for which the free scattering length is used is \(k_F^{\text{max}} \sim 0.05\) fm\(^{-1}\), corresponding to a maximum density \(\sim 4 \times 10^{-6}\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- For the simplified \(t_0-t_3\) model, the adjusted parameters are \(t_0 = -1803.93\) MeV fm\(^3\) and \(t_3 = 12911.00\) MeV fm\(^4\), giving a saturation density of 0.16 fm\(^{-3}\) with an energy per particle of \(-16.04\) MeV.
- For the full \(t_0-t_1-t_3\) model with density-dependent scattering length, the adjusted parameters are \(t_0 = -1818\) MeV fm\(^3\), \(t_3 = 12970\) MeV fm\(^4\), and \(t_1 = 15\) MeV fm\(^5\).
- The effective range used to obtain an acceptable neutron matter equation of state is \(r_s = -4.5\) fm for the case \(|k_F a(k_F)| = 1\).
- At \(-a k_F \sim 6\), the energy divided by the free gas energy is \(\sim 0.6\), comparable to Quantum Monte Carlo results.

## Context

This work extends the Lee-Yang low-density expansion (Lee and Yang, 1950s) and the YGLO hybrid functional (Grasso, Lacroix, and Yang, 2016) by introducing a simpler Skyrme-type functional without resumed expressions. It bridges effective field theory descriptions of dilute Fermi gases with energy-density-functional theories used in nuclear many-body problems. The functional enables applications to neutron-rich systems at very low densities as well as isospin-symmetric and asymmetric systems at ordinary nuclear density scales, and shows strong analogy with ultracold atomic gases where the scattering length is tuned by an applied magnetic field.
