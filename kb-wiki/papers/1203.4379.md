# Consistent description of nuclear charge radii and electric monopole transitions

> A systematic study of energy spectra throughout the rare-earth region (even-even nuclei from $_{58}$Ce to $_{74}$W) is carried out in the framework of the interacting boson model (IBM), leading to an accurate description of the spherical-to-deformed shape transition in the different isotopic chains. The resulting IBM Hamiltonians are then used for the calculation of nuclear charge radii (including isotope and isomer shifts) and electric monopole transitions with consistent operators for the two observables. The main conclusion of this study is that an IBM description of charge radii and electric monopole transitions is possible for most of the nuclei considered but that it breaks down in the tungsten isotopes. It is suggested that this failure is related to hexadecapole deformation.

## Key claim

The interacting boson model (IBM) can provide a consistent description of nuclear charge radii and electric monopole (E0) transitions for most even-even rare-earth nuclei from Ce to W, but this description breaks down in the tungsten isotopes, a failure the authors suggest is related to hexadecapole deformation.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic study of energy spectra for even-even nuclei from Ce to W using the interacting boson model (IBM-1), fitting a general one- and two-body Hamiltonian to ground-state bands and excited K^π=0^+ and K^π=2^+ bands up to J^π=10^+. They then used the resulting IBM Hamiltonians to calculate charge radii (including isotope and isomer shifts) and electric monopole transition strengths, employing consistent effective operators for both observables based on the assumption that the effective nucleon charges are the same for charge radius and E0 transition operators.

## Key numbers

- The rms deviation σ for energy fits across 78 nuclei and 846 excited levels is typically of order 100 keV, ranging from 81 keV (Ce) to 124 keV (Nd).
- The parameter η in the charge radius operator is kept constant at 0.50 fm^2 for all isotopes, with |α| values varying per isotope series from 0.10 fm^2 (Hf) to 0.26 fm^2 (Sm).
- The largest isotope shift peaks occur for 152-150Sm, 154-152Gd, and 156-154Dy (N=90 to N=88 differences).
- Calculated isomer shifts for 152Sm range from 12 to 37 × 10^{-3} fm^2 depending on the theoretical model, compared to experimental values of 12 to 49.6 × 10^{-3} fm^2 from multiple measurements.

## Context

This work extends the systematic IBM description of rare-earth nuclei previously carried out by Garcia-Ramos et al. (2003) and McCutchan et al. (2004), and tests the correlation between charge radii and E0 transitions proposed by Wood et al. (1999) in the context of configuration mixing. The study enables a consistent collective interpretation of excited 0^+ states in deformed nuclei, addressing the long-standing controversy about whether such states correspond to β-vibrational bands or arise from other mechanisms such as pairing isomers or shape coexistence.
