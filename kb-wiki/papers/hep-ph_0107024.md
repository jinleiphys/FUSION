# Effective gluon interactions in the Colour Superconductive Phase of two flavor QCD

> The gluon self-energies and dispersion laws in the color superconducting phase of QCD with two massless flavors are calculated using the effective theory near the Fermi surface. These quantities are calculated at zero temperature for all the eight gluons, those of the remaining SU(2) color group and those corresponding to the broken generators. The construction of the effective interaction is completed with the one loop calculation of the three- and four-point gluon interactions.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the gluon self-energies, dispersion laws, and three- and four-point gluon interactions in the two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase of QCD, using an effective theory near the Fermi surface at zero temperature. The abstract's claim of completing the effective interaction is hedged by the fact that the gradient expansion approximation used for the dispersion laws overestimates the rest masses of gluons 4-7 by about 40-50% compared to the exact numerical result, and the effective description is limited to energies below the gap parameter Δ.

## Method

The authors use an effective two-dimensional field theory near the Fermi surface, where quark momenta are decomposed into a chemical potential term and a residual momentum, and negative-energy states are integrated out. They compute the one-loop gluon polarization tensor Π^{μν}_{ab}(p) from the two diagrams in Fig. 1 (a sunset and a tadpole) for all eight gluons, distinguishing between the unbroken SU(2) gluons (indices 1-3), the broken gluons (indices 4-7), and the eighth gluon. The calculation is performed in the low-momentum limit using a gradient expansion, with infrared divergences controlled by a Matsubara frequency sum at finite temperature followed by the T→0 limit. The three- and four-point gluon vertices are then computed from one-loop quark-loop diagrams (Figs. 2 and 3), with the results expressed in terms of integrals over Fermi velocity directions and color structure constants.

## Key numbers

- Debye mass squared for gluons 4-7: Π^{00}(0) = (3/2) m²_g, where m²_g = μ² g² / (3 π²)
- Meissner mass squared for gluons 4-7: -Π^{ij}(0) = (1/2) m²_g
- Debye mass squared for gluon 8: Π^{00}(0) = 3 m²_g
- Meissner mass squared for gluon 8: -Π^{ij}(0) = (1/3) m²_g
- Rest mass for gluons 4-7 (exact numerical result): m_R = 0.894 Δ
- Rest mass for gluons 4-7 (gradient expansion approximation): m^R = √2 Δ ≈ 1.414 Δ
- Rest mass for gluon 8 spatial modes: m^R = m_M = √(μ² g² / (9 π²)), of order gμ
- Rest mass for gluon 8 temporal mode: m^R = 3 Δ
- Dielectric constant for unbroken SU(2) gluons: ε = k + 1, with k = g² μ² / (18 π² Δ²)
- Gluon speed for unbroken SU(2) gluons: v = 1/√(ε λ) ∝ Δ / (g μ)

## Context

This work extends the effective theory near the Fermi surface, previously applied to the Color-Flavor Locking (CFL) phase by Casalbuoni et al. (2000) and to the crystalline phase by Casalbuoni et al. (2001), to the two-flavor 2SC phase. The results confirm and extend earlier calculations by Rischke (2000) and Son (1999) for the gluon masses and dispersion laws, and by Shovkovy (2000) for the gradient expansion approximation's overestimate of masses. The complete one-loop effective gluon interactions enable future studies of gluon dynamics and transport properties in the 2SC phase, and the authors note that the longitudinal and transverse modes of the eighth gluon, with their large rest mass of order gμ, are decoupled from low-energy physics below the gap Δ.
