# Operator Evolution via the Similarity Renormalization Group I: The Deuteron

> Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) flow equations can be used to unitarily soften nuclear Hamiltonians by decoupling high-energy intermediate state contributions to low-energy observables while maintaining the natural hierarchy of many-body forces. Analogous flow equations can be used to consistently evolve operators so that observables are unchanged if no approximations are made. The question in practice is whether the advantages of a softer Hamiltonian and less correlated wave functions might be offset by complications in approximating and applying other operators. Here we examine the properties of SRG-evolved operators, focusing in this paper on applications to the deuteron but leading toward methods for few-body systems. We find the advantageous features generally carry over to other operators with additional simplifications in some cases from factorization of the unitary transformation operator.

## Key claim

The SRG evolution of operators, when applied consistently with the Hamiltonian evolution, preserves all matrix elements exactly, and the advantageous decoupling and smoothness seen for the Hamiltonian generally carry over to other operators, with the additional simplification that the unitary transformation operator can factorize under certain kinematic conditions.

## Method

The authors solve the SRG flow equation for the Hamiltonian in a discretized partial-wave momentum basis using the generator \(G_s = T_{\text{rel}}\), which suppresses off-diagonal momentum-space matrix elements. Rather than solving the operator flow equation directly, they construct the unitary transformation \(U_s\) from the eigenvectors of the evolved and unevolved Hamiltonians via \(U_s = \sum_\alpha |\psi_\alpha(s)\rangle\langle\psi_\alpha(0)|\) and then apply \(\mathcal{O}_s = U_s \mathcal{O}_{s=0} U_s^\dagger\) as a simple matrix product. They test decoupling by evolving the Hamiltonian and operators in the full momentum space, truncating the model space to a cutoff \(\Lambda\), and comparing expectation values.

## Key numbers

* The SRG flow parameter \(\lambda = s^{-1/4}\) is used; evolution is shown from \(\lambda = 15\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) to \(1.5\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) for AV18 and from \(\lambda = 6\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) to \(1.5\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) for N\(^3\)LO (500 MeV).
* For the momentum occupation operator at \(q = 0.34\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) in the deuteron, the spike at \(\lambda = 1.5\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) contains approximately 96% of the full expectation value.
* Decoupling tests show that when the SRG evolution \(\lambda\) is below the truncation cutoff \(\Lambda = 2.5\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) (for AV18) or \(\Lambda = 2.0\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) (for N\(^3\)LO), the momentum distribution expectation values are reproduced for all momenta, even outside the truncated space.

## Context

This work extends earlier SRG studies that focused on binding energies and phase shifts (Bogner 2007, Jurgenson 2009) and a limited analysis of the deuteron momentum distribution (Bogner 2007). It also builds on prior examinations of decoupling for long-distance operators like rms radius, quadrupole moment, and \(1/r\) (Jurgenson 2007), which only used bare operators. The paper enables future calculations of SRG-evolved operators in few-body systems and addresses whether the induced many-body hierarchy from chiral effective field theory is maintained by the SRG evolution.
