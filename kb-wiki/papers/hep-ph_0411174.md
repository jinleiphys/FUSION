# Evolution of the parton dihadron fragmentation functions

> Quark and gluon parton dihadron fragmentation functions and their evolution are studied in the process of e+e- annihilation. We provide definitions of such dihadron fragmentation functions in terms of parton matrix elements and derive the momentum sum rules and their connection to single hadron fragmentation functions. We parameterize results from the Lund Monte Carlo model JETSET as the initial conditions for the parton dihadron fragmentation functions at the scale Q_0^2=2 GeV^2. The evolution equations for the quark and gluon fragmentation functions are solved numerically and the results at different higher scales Q^2 agree well with JETSET results. The importance of the input from the single fragmentation functions is pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that dihadron fragmentation functions for both quarks and gluons can be defined within perturbative QCD and evolved via DGLAP equations, with the numerical evolution results agreeing well with the Lund Monte Carlo model JETSET, provided that the initial conditions are parameterized from JETSET at the scale Q_0^2 = 2 GeV^2 rather than from a simple product ansatz.

## Method

The authors define quark and gluon dihadron fragmentation functions in terms of parton matrix elements using the cut-vertex formalism, derive momentum sum rules connecting them to single hadron fragmentation functions, and obtain DGLAP evolution equations that include new mixed contributions where the two detected hadrons emerge from two separate partons after a splitting. They extract initial conditions for the dihadron fragmentation functions from the Lund Monte Carlo model JETSET at Q_0^2 = 2 GeV^2 using three million dijet events at sqrt(s) = 20 GeV for quarks and three-jet events for gluons, then solve the evolution equations numerically and compare the results at higher scales Q^2 to JETSET output.

## Key numbers

- Initial scale for parameterization: Q_0^2 = 2 GeV^2
- Center-of-mass energy for JETSET event generation: sqrt(s) = 20 GeV
- Number of simulated dijet events for quark fragmentation extraction: 3 million events distributed equally over three flavors
- Number of flavors assumed in gluon evolution: n_f = 3
- The momentum sum rule for dihadron fragmentation functions yields: sum over h1,h2 of integral dz1 dz2 (z1+z2)/2 D_{q,g}^{h1 h2}(z1,z2) = ⟨N(N-1)⟩/⟨N⟩, where ⟨N⟩ is the mean multiplicity and ⟨N(N-1)⟩ is the second cumulant moment of the multiplicity distribution

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Majumder and Wang, 2004) which defined dihadron fragmentation functions and derived evolution for the non-singlet quark case, now incorporating singlet quark and gluon dihadron fragmentation functions relevant to medium modification studies in heavy-ion and deep inelastic scattering collisions. The formalism enables systematic study of dihadron correlations measured at RHIC (Adler et al., 2003; Adler et al., 2004) and HERMES (Ding, 2004), where same-side correlations show minimal nuclear dependence despite significant suppression of single inclusive spectra (Airapetian et al., 2000), and provides the vacuum baseline needed before extending to medium-modified evolution.
