# Effects of $N(2000){5/2}^+$ on $γp \to K^+ Λ(1405)$

> The photoproduction reaction of $γp \to K^+Λ(1405)$ is investigated based on an effective Lagrangian approach at the tree-level approximation with the purpose of understanding the reaction mechanism and extracting the resonance contents and the associated resonance parameters in this reaction. Apart from the $t$-channel $K$ and $K^\ast$ exchanges, $s$-channel nucleon ($N$) exchange, $u$-channel $Σ$, $Λ$, and $Λ(1405)$ exchanges, and generalized contact term, the exchanges of a minimum number of $N$ resonances in the $s$ channel are taken into account in constructing the reaction amplitudes to describe the experimental data. It is found that by introducing the $N(2000){5/2}^+$ resonance exchange in the $s$ channel, one can reproduce the most recent differential cross-section data from the CLAS Collaboration quite well. Further analysis shows that the cross sections of $γp \to K^+Λ(1405)$ at high energies are dominated by the $t$-channel $K$ exchange, while the contributions from the $s$-channel $N$ and $N(2000){5/2}^+$ exchanges are rather significant to the cross sections in the near-threshold energy region. Predictions for the beam and target asymmetries for $γp \to K^+Λ(1405)$ are given.

# Effects of $N(2000){5/2}^+$ on $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda(1405)$

## Key claim

By introducing only the $N(2000){5/2}^+$ resonance exchange in the $s$ channel, the authors reproduce the CLAS differential cross-section data for $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda(1405)$ quite well, but the fit yields a $\chi^2/{\rm ND}=3.44$ over 158 data points, indicating that the description is not perfect and small deviations remain, particularly around $\cos\theta\approx 0$ at $W=2100$ MeV.

## Method

The authors employ an effective Lagrangian approach at tree level, constructing amplitudes from $t$-channel $K$ and $K^\ast$ exchanges, $s$-channel nucleon ($N$) and $N(2000){5/2}^+$ resonance exchanges, $u$-channel $\Sigma$, $\Lambda$, and $\Lambda(1405)$ exchanges, and a generalized contact current that ensures gauge invariance via the Ward-Takahashi identity. The $N(2000){5/2}^+$ resonance mass, width, and coupling constant products are treated as free fit parameters, while other coupling constants are fixed from SU(3) flavor symmetry or prior determinations.

## Key numbers

- Fitted mass of $N(2000){5/2}^+$: $M_R = 1913 \pm 7$ MeV
- Fitted width of $N(2000){5/2}^+$: $\Gamma_R = 471 \pm 81$ MeV
- Fitted coupling $g_{\Lambda^\ast NK} = 2.43 \pm 0.06$
- Fitted coupling $g_{\Lambda^\ast NK^\ast} = 2.04 \pm 0.57$
- Fitted cutoff for $s$-channel nucleon exchange: $\Lambda_N = 1641 \pm 18$ MeV
- Fitted cutoff for $t$-channel $K$ and $K^\ast$ exchanges: $\Lambda_{K^\ast,K} = 1261 \pm 19$ MeV
- Fit quality: $\chi^2/{\rm ND} = 3.44$ for 158 data points
- The $N(2000){5/2}^+$ resonance exchange is responsible for the bump structure in total cross sections around $W \approx 2.1$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Kim (2013) on $\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda(1405)$ photoproduction, which used five nucleon resonances but showed discrepancies in differential cross sections below $W=2.3$ GeV. The present study achieves a better description of the near-threshold region using only one resonance, the $N(2000){5/2}^+$, which is rated as a two-star resonance by the Particle Data Group. The results enable future extraction of resonance parameters from spin observables (beam and target asymmetries) and motivate investigation of coupled-channel effects from intermediate $K^\ast \Sigma$ states, as previously considered via triangle singularity mechanisms by Wang (2013).
