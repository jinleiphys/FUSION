# Symmetry breaking and quark-hadron duality in structure functions

> We identify conditions under which a summation over nucleon resonances can yield, via quark-hadron duality, parton model results for electromagnetic and neutrino structure functions at large x. While a summation over the lowest even and odd parity multiplets is sufficient to achieve duality in the symmetric quark model, a suppression of transitions to specific final states is required for more realistic cases incorporating SU(6) breaking. We outline several scenarios consistent with duality, discuss their implications for the high Q^2 behavior of transition form factors, and illustrate how they can expose the patterns in the flavor-spin dependence of inter-quark forces.

# Symmetry breaking and quark-hadron duality in structure functions

## Key claim
Quark-hadron duality between resonance sums and parton model results for structure functions at large x can survive SU(6) symmetry breaking, but only if the pattern of suppression in the initial state wave function matches that in the final state resonance transitions. The specific suppression of spin-3/2 quark configurations or helicity-3/2 contributions is required to avoid t-channel exotics and maintain consistency between electromagnetic and neutrino observables.

## Method
The authors compute structure function ratios (F1^n/F1^p, F1^{νp}/F1^{νn}) and polarization asymmetries (A1^p, A1^n, A1^{νp}, A1^{νn}) by summing over N* resonances in the 56+ and 70- SU(6) multiplets, with transition strengths parameterized by mixing angles θ_s (for spin-1/2 vs spin-3/2 suppression) or θ_h (for helicity-1/2 vs helicity-3/2 suppression). They fit the x-dependence of these mixing angles to existing SLAC data on the neutron-to-proton structure function ratio R^{np}, then predict the resulting polarization asymmetries and compare with data from SLAC, SMC, and HERMES.

## Key numbers
- SU(6) symmetric predictions: R^{np} = 2/3, A1^p = 5/9, A1^n = 0 for electromagnetic scattering; R^ν = 1/2, A1^{νp} = -1/3, A1^{νn} = 2/3 for neutrino scattering
- Spin-1/2 dominance limit (θ_s → 0): R^{np} = 6/19 ≈ 0.316, A1^p = 1, A1^n = 1 for electromagnetic; R^ν = 1/14 ≈ 0.071, A1^{νp} = 1, A1^{νn} = 1 for neutrino
- Helicity-1/2 dominance limit (θ_h → 0): R^{np} = 3/7 ≈ 0.429, A1^p = 1, A1^n = 1 for electromagnetic; R^ν = 1/5 = 0.200, A1^{νp} = 1, A1^{νn} = 1 for neutrino
- Suppression of Δ states alone gives R^{np} = 10/19 ≈ 0.526, A1^p = 1, A1^n = 2/5 = 0.400
- Magnetic dominance expected by Q^2 ≈ 0.5 GeV^2 in specific models; helicity-1/2 to helicity-3/2 amplitude ratio for D13 resonance consistent with zero beyond Q^2 ≈ 2 GeV^2 (Burkert)

## Context
This work extends the earlier SU(6) symmetric quark model duality analyses of Close, Gilman, Karl, and others (1970s) and the explicit model demonstration by Close and Isgur that summing over 56+ and 70- multiplets yields parton model results. It addresses the challenge posed by modern precision data from Jefferson Lab and SLAC showing strong SU(6) breaking at large x, where F2^n/F2^p ≪ 2/3. The paper provides a framework for testing whether the x → 1 behavior of N* transition form factors (measurable at Jefferson Lab) matches the pattern required by duality, which would reveal the flavor-spin dependence of inter-quark forces. The results enable predictions for neutrino structure function ratios that can be tested with future high-intensity neutrino beams (NuMI).
