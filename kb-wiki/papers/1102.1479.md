# Recursive calculation of matrix elements for the generalized seniority shell model

> A recursive calculational scheme is developed for matrix elements in the generalized seniority scheme for the nuclear shell model. Recurrence relations are derived which permit straightforward and efficient computation of matrix elements of one-body and two-body operators and basis state overlaps.

## Key claim

The paper develops a systematic recursive scheme for computing matrix elements and overlaps in the generalized seniority shell model, enabling calculations for states of generalized seniority up to at least v=3 (and implicitly higher), whereas prior methods were practically limited to v≤4 and became cumbersome beyond v=2.

## Method

Recurrence relations are derived using angular-momentum coupled commutator methods (Wick's theorem in coupled form), where matrix elements of one-body operators and overlaps for states of generalized seniority v and pair number N are expressed in terms of commutators that yield matrix elements and overlaps involving lower values of v and/or N. The explicit recurrence relations for a specific generalized seniority are obtained by repeated application of a commutator product rule, and matrix elements of two-body operators are computed directly from these one-body operator matrix elements and overlaps.

## Key numbers

- The recurrence network allows calculation of one-body operator matrix elements for v≤4 and overlaps for v≤5, as required for two-body Hamiltonian matrix elements between states of v=3.
- The seed values for the recurrence, the norms of zero-seniority states, are given by the formula: OLAPzero(N) = (N!)^2 * sum over partitions M of N into D terms M_c of [product over c of alpha_c^(2M_c) * binomial(Omega_c, M_c)], where Omega_c = (2c+1)/2 is the pair degeneracy of level c.
- The collective S pair is defined as S^dagger = sum_c alpha_c * (hat{c}/2) * A^dagger(cc0), with normalization condition sum_c (2c+1)*alpha_c^2 = sum_c (2c+1).

## Context

This work extends the generalized seniority or broken pair approximation (Talmi 1971, Shlomo 1972, Gambhir 1969, Allaart 1988) by providing a systematic method that overcomes the limitations of the combinatorial approach (Pittel 1982) which became cumbersome with increasing seniority, and the hybrid commutator method (Frank 1982) which was only practical for v≤2. The recurrence scheme enables microscopic derivation of interacting boson model (IBM) and interacting boson fermion model (IBFM) Hamiltonian parameters and transition operators (Otsuka 1978, Pittel 1982, Iachello 1987), particularly for applications to neutrinoless double beta decay (Barea 2009) where phenomenological calibration is not viable.
