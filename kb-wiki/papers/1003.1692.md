# On heavy-quarkonia suppression by final-state multiple scatterings in most central Au+Au collisions at RHIC

> We study heavy-quarkonia suppression under final-state multiple scatterings in most central Au~+~Au collisions at RHIC energy. We first calculate the survival probability of a heavy quarkonium under multiple scattering in Bjorken's expanding QGP at large $N_c$. Then, we calculate the rapidity dependence of the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ for heavy-quarkonia production by considering final-state multiple scatterings in most central Au~+~Au collisions in a simplified model. In our formula a constant $P_0$ is also introduced to estimate the possible cold nuclear effects. By fitting the data for $J/Ψ$ production in most central Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$~GeV at RHIC, we find that the transportation coefficient $\hat{q}_0\simeq(0.33-0.95)~\mbox{GeV}^2$/fm, and, accordingly, the energy density at $τ_0$ is $ε_0\simeq(1.39-5.62)~\mbox{GeV}/\mbox{fm}^3$ in perturbative thermal QCD. A better understanding of cold nuclear effects is essential for us to get a more accurate analysis. The small values of the transportation coefficient $\hat{q}_0$ in our estimate are in sharp contrast with those obtained by the analysis of high-$p_T$ hadron spectra in Ref. [31].

## Key claim

The paper argues that the small transportation coefficient $\hat{q}_0 \simeq (0.33-0.95)~\mbox{GeV}^2$/fm extracted from $J/\Psi$ suppression data is in sharp contrast with values exceeding $5~\mbox{GeV}^2$/fm obtained from high-$p_T$ hadron spectra, but this estimate depends on a constant $P_0$ introduced to account for cold nuclear effects, and a better understanding of those cold nuclear effects is essential for a more accurate analysis.

## Method

The authors calculated the survival probability of a heavy quarkonium under multiple scattering in Bjorken's expanding quark-gluon plasma (QGP) at large $N_c$, using a Gaussian wave packet approximation for the quarkonium overlap function. They then computed the rapidity dependence of the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ for heavy-quarkonia production by considering final-state multiple scatterings in most central Au+Au collisions, using a simplified Glauber-model geometry with a Woods-Saxon nuclear density profile and fitting the $J/\Psi$ data to a constant $R_{AA}=0.286$ due to the boost invariance of the background medium.

## Key numbers

* Transportation coefficient: $\hat{q}_0 \simeq (0.33-0.95)~\mbox{GeV}^2$/fm, extracted from fitting $J/\Psi$ production in most central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$~GeV at RHIC.
* Energy density at thermalization time $\tau_0$: $\epsilon_0 \simeq (1.39-5.62)~\mbox{GeV}/\mbox{fm}^3$ in perturbative thermal QCD, corresponding to the same fit.
* Best fit for $J/\Psi$ $R_{AA}$ versus rapidity $y$ in most central Au+Au collisions: $R_{AA}=0.286$ with $\chi^2=9.4$.
* $J/\Psi$ properties used: mass 3.10~GeV, binding energy $E_B=0.64$~GeV, formation time $\tau_B=0.31$~fm, size $a_B=0.5$~fm.
* $\Upsilon$ properties: mass 9.46~GeV, binding energy $E_B=1.10$~GeV, formation time $\tau_B=0.18$~fm, size $a_B=0.28$~fm.
* Average transverse momentum squared for $J/\Psi$ in $p+p$ collisions: $\langle p_T^2 \rangle \simeq 3.75~\mbox{GeV}^2$.
* Critical temperature for deconfinement: $T_c \simeq 190$~MeV, from lattice simulation.
* Thermalization time range considered: $\tau_0 = 0.5-1.5$~fm.

## Context

This work extends the study of heavy-quarkonia suppression as a probe of QGP properties, originally proposed by Matsui and Satz (1986), and addresses the puzzle from PHENIX data (Adare et al., 2006) that $J/\Psi$ suppression at RHIC is smaller than expected from local medium density estimates. The extracted transportation coefficient $\hat{q}_0$ is much smaller than values from high-$p_T$ hadron spectra analysis by Eskola et al. (2004) and also smaller than the estimate of 1.6-2.0~GeV$^2$/fm by Arleo et al. (2006), highlighting a persistent puzzle. The approach enables future extraction of thermodynamic properties at thermalization time using $\Upsilon$ production at LHC, where recombination effects are small and the short formation time ($\tau_B=0.18$~fm) allows probing earlier stages of the medium.
