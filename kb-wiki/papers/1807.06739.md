# From in-Medium Color Forces to Transport Properties of QGP

> A thermodynamic quantum many-body $T$-matrix approach is employed to study the spectral and transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma at moderate temperatures where nonperturbative effects are essential. For the partonic two-body interaction we utilize a QCD-inspired Hamiltonian whose color forces are motivated by the heavy-quark (HQ) limit including remnants of the confining force, and augmented by relativistic corrections. We solve the in-medium parton propagators and $T$-matrices selfconsistently and resum the skeleton diagrams for the equation of state (EoS) to all orders thereby accounting for the dynamical formation of two-body bound states. Two types solutions for the in-medium potential are found in when fitting to lattice-QCD data for the EoS, HQ free energy and quarkonium correlators: a weakly-coupled scenario (WCS) with a (real part of the) potential close to the free energy, resulting in moderately broadened spectral functions and weak bound states near $T_c$, and a strongly-coupled scenario (SCS), with a much stronger potential which produces large imaginary parts ("melting" the parton spectral functions) and generates strong bound states near $T_c$. We calculate pertinent transport coefficients (specific shear viscosity and HQ diffusion coefficient) and argue that their constraints from heavy-ion phenomenology unambiguously favor the strongly-coupled scenario.

## Key claim

The paper argues that phenomenological constraints from heavy-ion collisions on transport coefficients (specific shear viscosity and heavy-quark diffusion coefficient) unambiguously favor the strongly-coupled scenario (SCS) over the weakly-coupled scenario (WCS), even though both scenarios are consistent with lattice-QCD data for the equation of state, heavy-quark free energy, and quarkonium correlators.

## Method

The authors employ a thermodynamic quantum many-body T-matrix approach using a QCD-inspired Hamiltonian with color forces motivated by the heavy-quark limit, including remnants of the confining force and relativistic corrections. They solve the in-medium parton propagators and T-matrices selfconsistently and resum the skeleton diagrams for the equation of state to all orders, accounting for dynamical formation of two-body bound states. Two types of solutions for the in-medium potential are found when fitting to lattice-QCD data: a weakly-coupled scenario (WCS) with a potential close to the free energy, and a strongly-coupled scenario (SCS) with a much stronger potential producing large imaginary parts.

## Key numbers

* Specific shear viscosity (eta/s) in the SCS is about twice the conjectured lower bound (Kovtun, 2004), while in the WCS it is another factor of about 2 larger at low temperature.
* Heavy-quark spatial diffusion coefficient (D_s) in the SCS is about twice the thermal wavelength, while in the WCS it is up to another factor of 5 larger.
* The ratio D_s(2 pi T) over 4 pi eta/s is near 1 in the strongly coupled limit (Gubser, 2006) and 5/2 in a perturbative system (Danielewicz, 1984); the WCS realizes 5/2 at all temperatures, while the SCS realizes near 1 at low temperatures.
* The Luttinger-Ward functional (Phi) contribution to the pressure accounts for more than 50 percent close to T_c in the SCS, while remaining small at all temperatures in the WCS.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous T-matrix calculations (Liu, 2016; Liu, 2017) by connecting microscopic parton spectral functions to transport coefficients within a unified nonperturbative framework. The results contradict the Bayesian analysis of lattice-QCD Wilson line/loop data (Burnier, 2014), which extracted a potential and imaginary parts close to the WCS. The findings enable using transport coefficients from heavy-ion phenomenology to discriminate between microscopic scenarios of the quark-gluon plasma, with the SCS implying a long-range remnant of the confining potential that generates strong-coupling behavior for long-wavelength excitations.
