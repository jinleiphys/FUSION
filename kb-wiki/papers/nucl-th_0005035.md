# A New Coupling Potential for the Scattering of Deformed Light Heavy-Ions

> This letter introduces a new coupling potential to explain the experimental data over wide energy ranges for a number of systems. Within the coupled-channels formalism, this letter first shows the limitations of the standard coupled-channels theory in the case where one of the nuclei in the reaction is strongly deformed and then, demonstrates that a global solution to the problems of light heavy-ion reactions such as $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C, $^{16}$O+$^{28}$Si and $^{12}$C+$^{24}$Mg can be found using a new second-derivative coupling potential in the coupled-channels formalism. This new approach consistently improves the agreement with the experimental data for the elastic and inelastic scattering data as well as for their excitation functions using constant or slightly energy-dependent parameters.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new second-derivative coupling potential that consistently improves agreement with experimental data for elastic and inelastic scattering of deformed light heavy-ion systems over wide energy ranges, using constant or only slightly energy-dependent parameters, whereas the standard coupled-channels theory fails to provide a global solution.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-channels calculations for the \(^{12}\text{C}+^{12}\text{C}\), \(^{16}\text{O}+^{28}\text{Si}\), and \(^{12}\text{C}+^{24}\text{Mg}\) systems, first using the standard first-derivative coupling potential derived from a deformed optical potential with a Woods-Saxon shape. They then replaced the coupling potential with a new second-derivative form (the second derivative of a Woods-Saxon shape) while keeping all other potential parameters fixed, and compared the resulting angular distributions and excitation functions to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^{12}\text{C}+^{12}\text{C}\) system, data were analyzed from \(E_{\text{Lab}} = 20.0\) MeV to 126.7 MeV, including elastic, single-\(2^{+}\), and mutual-\(2^{+}\) inelastic scattering.
- For the \(^{16}\text{O}+^{28}\text{Si}\) system, data were analyzed from \(E_{\text{Lab}} = 29.0\) to 142.5 MeV, including elastic and single-\(2^{+}\) states.
- For the \(^{12}\text{C}+^{24}\text{Mg}\) system, data were analyzed from \(E_{\text{Lab}} = 16.0\) to 24.0 MeV.
- In the standard coupled-channels model for \(^{12}\text{C}+^{12}\text{C}\), the mutual-\(2^{+}\) state magnitude was underestimated by a factor of 3 to 10.

## Context

This work extends earlier coupled-channels studies of light heavy-ion reactions that had failed to provide consistent fits over wide energy ranges, such as those by Bra82, Sci97, and Sto79. The new coupling potential resolves long-standing problems including anomalous large angle scattering, oscillatory structure near the Coulomb barrier, and the out-of-phase problem between theory and experiment, which previous ad-hoc models (e.g., Kobos and Satchler (1984) for \(^{16}\text{O}+^{28}\text{Si}\)) could only address with artificial potential adjustments. The approach enables simultaneous description of elastic and inelastic scattering data and excitation functions for multiple systems, and suggests that the coupling potential need not share the same energy dependence as the central potential.
