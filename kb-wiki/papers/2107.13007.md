# Measurement of Bell-type inequalities and quantum entanglement from $Λ$-hyperon spin correlations at high energy colliders

> Spin correlations of $Λ$-hyperons embedded in the QCD strings formed in high energy collider experiments provide unique insight into their locality and entanglement features. We show from general considerations that while the Clauser-Horne-Shimony-Holt inequality is less stringent for such states, they provide a benchmark for quantum-to-classical transitions induced by varying i) the associated hadron multiplicity, ii) the spin of nucleons, iii) the separation in rapidity between pairs, and iv) the kinematic regimes accessed. These studies also enable the extraction of quantitative measures of quantum entanglement. We first explore such questions within a simple model of a QCD string composed of singlets of two partial distinguishable fermion flavors and compare analytical results to those obtained on quantum hardware. We further discuss a class of spin Hamiltonians that model the dynamics of $Λ$ spin correlations. Prospects for extracting quantum features of QCD strings from hyperon measurements at current and future colliders are outlined.

## Key claim

The authors show that while the Clauser-Horne-Shimony-Holt (CHSH) inequality is less stringent for mixed states of Lambda-hyperon pairs produced in QCD strings than for pure states, the modified inequality and related entanglement measures can still serve as a benchmark for quantum-to-classical transitions induced by varying hadron multiplicity, nucleon spin, rapidity separation, and kinematic regimes.

## Method

The authors derive a theorem showing that symmetric, rotationally invariant two-particle correlation functions for spin-1/2 hyperons imply the density matrix is diagonal in the Bell basis, which leads to a modified one-parameter CHSH inequality for mixed states. They compute spin correlations analytically in a toy model of a QCD string composed of singlet pairs of two partially distinguishable fermion flavors (strange and light quarks), implement quantum circuits for this model on IBM's QISKIT simulator and ibmq_16_melbourne quantum hardware, and discuss spin Hamiltonians (Anderson model, Kondo model, RKKY model) that capture the dynamics of Lambda spin correlations in QCD strings.

## Key numbers

- The weak decay parameter for Lambda hyperons is alpha = 0.750 +/- 0.010, updated from the previous value of 0.642.
- Entanglement fidelity criterion requires the coefficient of cos(theta_ab) in the correlation function to be greater than 1/2 for the state to be entangled.
- The Peres-Horodecki PPT criterion for Werner states shows entanglement for p > 1/3, which is less restrictive than the fidelity criterion of 1/2.
- For the toy model with N=8 particles, a=1 s\bar{s} singlet, b=2 mixed singlets, the correlation function coefficient is a/(a+b/2)^2 = 1/4.

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Tornqvist (1980) to use Lambda-hyperon spin correlations as a test of local hidden variable theory, adapting it to the many-body mixed states produced in QCD strings at high energy colliders. The authors connect their framework to the study of long-range rapidity correlations (the "ridge" effect) in QCD and to quantum-to-classical transitions observed in ultracold atomic gases (Berges 2014, Prufer 2018). The results enable experimental searches for quantum entanglement in Lambda-hyperon pairs at current facilities (RHIC, LHC) and future ones (Electron-Ion Collider), providing a baseline since no current Monte Carlo generators implement parton-level spin entanglement.
