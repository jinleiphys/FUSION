# Predictions of chiral perturbation theory for Compton scattering off protons

> We compute the Compton scattering off the nucleons in the framework of manifestly covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (B$χ$PT). The results for observables differ substantially from the corresponding calculations in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB$χ$PT), most appreciably in the forward kinematics. We verify that the covariant $p^3$ result fulfills the forward-Compton-scattering sum rules. We also explore the effect of the $Δ$(1232) resonance. We find that the substantial effect of the $Δ$-excitation on the nucleon polarizabilities can naturally be accommodated in the manifestly covariant calculation.

## Key claim

The manifestly covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (BχPT) calculation at order p³ yields predictions for nucleon polarizabilities that differ substantially from heavy-baryon χPT (HBχPT) results, particularly in forward kinematics; the authors argue that the Δ(1232) resonance contribution can be accommodated more naturally in the covariant framework than in the heavy-baryon approach.

## Method

The authors computed Compton scattering off nucleons using manifestly covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (BχPT) at orders p³ and p⁴/Δ, where Δ ≈ 300 MeV is the Δ(1232) resonance excitation energy. They evaluated one-loop diagrams using the leading-order chiral Lagrangian for the nucleon after a field redefinition, and included Δ(1232) Born contributions with γN→Δ couplings (g_M = 2.95, g_E = -1.0) from pion-photoproduction analyses and h_A = 2.85 from the Δ→πN decay width. The on-mass-shell renormalization scheme was adopted with standard parameters (g_A = 1.267, f_π = 0.0924 GeV, m_π = 0.139 GeV, M_N = 0.9383 GeV, κ_p = 1.79).

## Key numbers

- Electric polarizability of the proton α^(p) = 6.8 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ at BχPT O(p³); α^(p) = 10.8 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ at BχPT O(p⁴/Δ); compared to PDG value 12.0 ± 0.6 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Magnetic polarizability of the proton β^(p) = -1.8 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ at BχPT O(p³); β^(p) = 2.9 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ at BχPT O(p⁴/Δ); compared to PDG value 1.9 ± 0.5 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Δ(1232) resonance excitation energy Δ = M_Δ - M_N ≈ 0.3 GeV
- Pion decay constant f_π = 0.0924 GeV, pion mass m_π = 0.139 GeV, nucleon mass M_N = 0.9383 GeV
- Axial coupling constant g_A = 1.267, anomalous magnetic moment of proton κ_p = 1.79
- γN→Δ couplings: g_M = 2.95, g_E = -1.0; h_A = 2.85 corresponding to Δ→πN decay width of 115 MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier BχPT calculation of polarizabilities by Bernard et al. (1991) and contrasts with the heavy-baryon χPT (HBχPT) calculations by Beane et al. (2004) and others, which had shown better agreement with experiment at leading order. The authors demonstrate that the covariant p³ result satisfies forward-Compton-scattering sum rules, unlike HBχPT, and that the Δ(1232) contribution can be naturally included in BχPT, resolving the tension between the two frameworks. This enables consistent treatment of Compton scattering and pion photoproduction data within a single covariant chiral effective field theory.
