# Hadronic observables from Au+Au collisions at s(NN)**(1/2)=200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at s(NN)**(1/2)=5.5 TeV from a simple kinematic model

> A simple kinematic model based on superposition of p+p collisions, relativistic geometry and final-state hadronic rescattering is used to calculate various hadronic observables in s(NN)**(1/2) = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions and s(NN)**(1/2) = 5.5 TeV Pb+Pb collisions. The model calculations are compared with experimental results from several s(NN)**(1/2) = 200 GeV Au+Au collision studies. If a short hadronization time is assumed in the model, it is found that this model describes the trends of the observables from these experiments surprisingly well considering the model's simplicity. This also gives more credibility to the model predictions presented for s(NN)**(1/2) = 5.5 TeV Pb+Pb collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a simple kinematic model based on superposition of PYTHIA-generated p+p collisions, relativistic geometry, and final-state hadronic rescattering can describe the trends of many hadronic observables from Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV surprisingly well, but only if a short hadronization proper time of 0.1 fm/c is assumed; the model provides qualitative rather than quantitative agreement for most observables.

## Method

The model superposes PYTHIA-generated p+p collisions within the nuclear overlap geometry of colliding Au or Pb nuclei, with the number of superposed collisions determined by the impact parameter. Hadrons from each p+p collision are assigned initial space-time positions using a proper-time hadronization picture with tau = 0.1 fm/c, followed by a semi-classical Monte Carlo hadronic rescattering calculation with binary collisions among pions, kaons, nucleons, lambdas, and resonances. A single 87K event minimum-bias run was used for Au+Au at 200 GeV and an 800-event run for Pb+Pb at 5.5 TeV, with centrality windows defined by multiplicity cuts matching experimental conditions.

## Key numbers

- Hadronization proper time tau = 0.1 fm/c, set to match results from a Tevatron HBT study
- For Au+Au at 200 GeV, the model rapidity distributions agree with PHOBOS data at the 10-15% level near mid-rapidity
- For high pT in minimum bias Au+Au, the model under-predicts the PHENIX absolute scale by up to a factor of four at some points
- For V2 vs. pT in 0-5% centrality Au+Au, the model is systematically higher than STAR data by about 0.01-0.02 over the entire pT range below 2 GeV/c
- For two-pion HBT in 0-5% centrality Au+Au, the model under-predicts the magnitude of Rside by about 20% compared to STAR
- For two-pion HBT in 40-80% centrality Au+Au, the model under-predicts Rside by about 30%
- For two-pion HBT vs. kT in 0-5% centrality, the model under-predicts Rside by about 24% and over-predicts the lambda parameter by about 30% (model lambda = 0.61, constant in kT)
- For RAA vs. pT, the model peak occurs at about 1.3 GeV/c while the PHENIX peak occurs at about 2.3 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Humanic (2006a, 1998a, 2006b) that used a similar hadronic rescattering model but with thermal parameterizations for initial kinematics, no jets, and only qualitative comparisons at fixed impact parameter. The present model improves on those by using PYTHIA-generated p+p collisions (including jets), allowing transverse expansion in the initial geometry, running in minimum-bias mode for quantitative centrality comparisons, and correcting an error in the inelastic scattering algorithm that previously limited calculations to pT below 2 GeV/c. The model enables predictions for LHC-energy Pb+Pb collisions at 5.5 TeV and provides a baseline for testing whether hadronic degrees of freedom with a very short hadronization time can describe heavy-ion observables, challenging the prevailing view that partonic degrees of freedom are required at early times.
