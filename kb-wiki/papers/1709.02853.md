# Kaon transverse charge density from space- and timelike data

> We used the world data on the kaon form factor to extract the transverse kaon charge density using a dispersion integral of the imaginary part of the kaon form factor in the timelike region. Our analysis includes recent data from $e^+e^-$ annihiliation measurements extending the kinematic reach of the data into the region of high momentum transfers conjugate to the region of short transverse distances. To calculate the transverse density we created a superset of both timelike and spacelike data and developed an empirical parameterization of the kaon form factor. The spacelike set includes two new data points we extracted from existing cross section data. We estimate the uncertainty on the resulting transverse density to be 5\% at $b$=0.025 fm and significantly better at large distances. New kaon data planned with the 12 GeV Jefferson Lab may have a significant impact on the charge density at distances of $b<$ 0.1fm.

## Key claim

The authors extract the transverse kaon charge density from a dispersion integral of the imaginary part of the kaon form factor in the timelike region, using a new empirical parameterization that includes recent high-energy data from CLEO and CMD-2; the extraction relies on the assumption that the form factor parameterization, which includes five rho resonances and uses Gounaris-Sakurai Breit-Wigner functions for the rho resonances, provides a valid analytic continuation into the spacelike region.

## Method

The authors compiled a superset of world data on the charged and neutral kaon form factor from both spacelike (elastic K-e scattering and electroproduction) and timelike (e+e- annihilation) measurements, including two new spacelike data points extracted from existing cross section data. They developed an empirical parameterization based on a vector meson dominance model with Breit-Wigner functions for rho, omega, and phi resonances and their radial excitations, fitting the parameters to all data simultaneously. The transverse charge density was then obtained by substituting the dispersion relation for the form factor into the two-dimensional Fourier transform, using the imaginary part of the fitted form factor weighted by a modified Bessel function.

## Key numbers

- Mean charge radius of the kaon: <r_K^2> = 0.34 ± 0.05 fm^2, extracted from elastic K-e scattering at spacelike momentum transfers up to Q^2 = 0.10 GeV^2
- Timelike kaon form factor data available up to Q^2 = 17.4 GeV^2 (center of mass energy sqrt(s) = 4.2 GeV) from e+e- annihilation measurements
- Estimated uncertainty on the resulting transverse density: 5% at b = 0.025 fm, with significantly better precision at larger distances
- Best fit (Model 8) achieved chi^2/dof = 290/267 for the complete data set including spacelike and timelike data
- Fitted phi meson mass: 1019.3 ± 0.02 MeV; fitted phi meson width: 4.23 ± 0.04 MeV
- Fitted fourth rho resonance mass: 1294 ± 16 MeV; fitted fourth rho resonance width: 174 ± 60 MeV

## Context

This work extends the method previously applied to the pion by Miller (2011) and to the proton by Miller (2010) for extracting transverse charge densities from timelike form factor data using dispersion relations. The analysis improves upon the parameterization of Bruch et al. (2004), which under-predicted new high-precision data from Pedlar (2005) and Seth (2012) by three sigma, by adding additional rho resonances and using Gounaris-Sakurai Breit-Wigner functions. The results enable comparisons of the kaon transverse charge density with those of the proton, pion, and neutron, and provide a baseline for assessing the impact of future measurements planned with the 12 GeV Jefferson Lab, which may significantly constrain the charge density at distances b < 0.1 fm.
