# Universal Relations with Dynamical Tides

> Observations of neutron stars and the precise measurement of their macroscopic properties have provided valuable insights into fundamental physics, both by constraining the behavior of nuclear matter under extreme conditions and by enabling tests of general relativity in the strong-field regime. In this context, equation-of-state-insensitive or ``quasi-universal'' relations between key observables, such as the compactness, dimensionless static tidal deformability, and moment of inertia, play a crucial role in connecting different measurable observables while minimizing uncertainties due to the yet unknown equation-of-state. In this work, we identify new quasi-universal relations between the static, dimensionless tidal deformability ($Λ^{(0)}$) and its leading-order dynamical correction ($Λ^{(2)}$), as well as between $Λ^{(0)}$ and a combination of these parameters ($\sqrt{ Λ^{(0)}/Λ^{(2)}}\equiv Mω_*$), obtained from the small-frequency expansion of the relativistic tidal response. We test these relations across a representative set of 59 equations of state, finding that the equation-of-state dependence does not exceed $\sim$5\% for the $Λ^{(0)}$--$Λ^{(2)}$ relation and $\sim2.8\%$ for the $Λ^{(0)}$--$Mω_*$ relation. This indicates a high degree of universality and offers a simplified framework for incorporating dynamical tidal effects into gravitational-wave modeling. Furthermore, we compare the dynamical tidal response against different recent strategies (a Taylor expansion and a one-mode approximation) to model the dynamical tide. We find that both models are capable of capturing the frequency-dependent behavior of the dynamical tidal deformability, with the one-mode approximation agreeing better with the dynamical response than the Taylor expansion in most of the parameter space.

# Universal Relations with Dynamical Tides

## Key claim

The paper identifies new quasi-universal (equation-of-state-insensitive) relations between the static dimensionless tidal deformability Λ⁽⁰⁾ and its leading-order dynamical correction Λ⁽²⁾, and between Λ⁽⁰⁾ and the combination √(Λ⁽⁰⁾/Λ⁽²⁾) ≡ Mω_*, with the EOS dependence not exceeding ~5% and ~2.8% respectively, but these relations are derived from the small-frequency expansion of the relativistic tidal response and tested only across a representative set of 59 equations of state, not proven for all possible nuclear matter models.

## Method

The authors compute the frequency-dependent tidal deformability of neutron stars by solving linearized Einstein equations for time-harmonic perturbations (proportional to e^(iωt)) on a spherically symmetric background, using the relativistic framework of Hegade et al. (2024). They expand the dynamical Love number k₂(ω) to second order in (Mω)² to obtain the static term Λ⁽⁰⁾ and the leading dynamical correction Λ⁽²⁾, then compare this Taylor expansion and a one-mode approximation Λ^(OM)(ω) = Λ⁽⁰⁾/(1 − ω²/ω_*²) against the full dynamical tidal response Λ^(Dyn)(ω) across frequencies up to 1.5 kHz. The quasi-universal relations are tested by computing Λ⁽⁰⁾ and Λ⁽²⁾ for 59 equations of state, including 35 piecewise polytropes from Read (2008) and 24 phenomenological tabulated EOSs from Tan (2021).

## Key numbers

- EOS dependence of the Λ⁽⁰⁾–Λ⁽²⁾ relation does not exceed ~5% across 59 equations of state
- EOS dependence of the Λ⁽⁰⁾–Mω_* relation does not exceed ~2.8% across the same set
- For the SLy EOS with a 1.4 M⊙ star, the effective frequency f_* ≡ ω_*/2π is ~1.8 kHz, while the f-mode frequency for the same star is ~1.7 kHz
- The time scale T_* = 2π/ω_* is ~0.6 ms for a 1.4 M⊙ star with the SLy EOS
- The Taylor expansion reproduces the dynamical tide within 5% for GW frequencies up to ~750 Hz across all compactnesses considered
- The Taylor expansion error remains below 10% for frequencies up to ~1000 Hz
- The one-mode approximation reproduces the dynamical tide within 5% for GW frequencies up to ~1250 Hz
- The one-mode approximation error remains below 10% for frequencies up to ~1400 Hz
- The static tidal deformability can differ from the dynamical one by over 100% for low-compactness stars at frequencies significantly below contact (~2 kHz for a 1.4 M⊙ star)

## Context

This work extends the quasi-universal relations program, such as the I-Love-Q relations of Yagi (2013) and the binary-Love relations of Yagi (2015, 2016), from static tidal deformability to dynamical tidal effects. It builds on the relativistic dynamical tide framework of Pitre and Poisson (2023), which provided the first systematic relativistic treatment via small-frequency expansion, and the frequency-domain approach of Hegade et al. (2024), which captures full frequency-dependent fluid dynamics including g-mode resonances. The one-mode approximation compared here is commonly used in effective-one-body waveform models from Steinhoff (2016). The new quasi-universal relations enable dynamical tidal effects to be modeled with effectively a single independent parameter, simplifying incorporation into gravitational-wave parameter estimation without introducing additional degrees of freedom.
