# Search for narrow six-quark states in the reactions $γd\to πγnn$

> We study the reactions $γd\toπγNN$ with the aim to search for six-quark states, the decay of which into two nucleons is forbidden by the Pauli exclusion principle. These states with the masses $M<2m_N+m_π$ should mainly decay by emitting a photon. This is a new class of metastable six-quark states with the decay widths $<< 1$keV. The recent experiment at Proton Linear Accelerator of INR (Moscow) suggests a possibility of the existence of such states with masses 1904, 1926, and 1942 MeV. The proposed experiment at MAMI-C could provide an unique opportunity to observe such dibaryon states in mass region up to 2000 MeV and determine their masses and quantum numbers.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that six-quark states (supernarrow dibaryons or SNDs) with masses below the pion production threshold (M < 2m_N + m_pi) exist and decay primarily by photon emission, but the evidence for the three candidate masses observed at INR (1904, 1926, 1942 MeV) remains inconclusive because the LEGS data had limited resolution and the MAMI data had poor statistics.

## Method

The authors calculated cross sections for SND production in the reactions gamma d -> pi+ gamma nn, gamma d -> pi- gamma pp, and gamma d -> pi0 gamma pn using a model where the SND is produced from a six-quark component of the deuteron with probability eta, and decays through the virtual 31S0 NN state. They performed GEANT3 Monte Carlo simulations of the proposed experiment at MAMI-C using the Crystal Ball/TAPS spectrometer, a multi-wire proportional chamber, and PID detectors with a tagged photon beam of 250-800 MeV, simulating 500 hours of beam time.

## Key numbers

- Three candidate SND masses observed at INR: 1904 +/- 2 MeV, 1926 +/- 2 MeV, and 1942 +/- 2 MeV, with widths equal to the experimental resolution (~5 MeV) and statistical significances of 6.0, 7.0, and 6.3 standard deviations respectively
- Decay widths for D(1,1+) range from 0.51 eV at M=1.90 GeV to 109 eV at M=2.013 GeV; for D(1,1-) from 0.13 eV at M=1.90 GeV to 27 eV at M=2.013 GeV (at eta=0.01)
- Expected total cross sections for D(1,1-) photoproduction in gamma d -> pi+ D: 10-32 nb; for gamma d -> pi- D: up to 45 nb at M=1904 MeV
- Expected yields for 500 hours beam time: 170-240 events for pi+ channel, 2000-2360 events for pi- channel, 530-650 events for pi0 channel
- Expected mass resolution sigma_M: 3.8-7.0 MeV depending on channel and mass
- Predicted asymmetry R ~ 0.7 for D(1,1-) at MAMI with linearly polarized photons

## Context

This work extends the search for supernarrow dibaryons (SNDs) first observed by Fil'kov et al. at INR (Konobeevsky et al., 2003; Fil'kov et al., 2004) and supported by LEGS data (2008), but contradicts null results from Tamii et al. (2006) at RCNC who set upper limits below 1 microbarn/sr. The proposed MAMI-C experiment would improve on the LEGS measurement by using the Crystal Ball/TAPS detector system with better resolution and statistics, and could also test the existence of exotic baryon states reported by Tatischeff et al. (2006) at masses 1004, 1044, and 1094 MeV.
