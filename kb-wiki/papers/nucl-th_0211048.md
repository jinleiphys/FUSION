# Skyrme Hartree-Fock Calculations for the Alpha Decay Q Values of Super-Heavy Nuclei

> Hartree-Fock calculations with the SKX Skyrme interaction are carried out to obtain alpha-decay Q values for deformed nuclei above $^{208}$Pb assuming axial symmetry. The results for even-even nuclei are compared with experiment and with previous calculations. Predictions are made for alpha-decay Q values and half-lives of even-even super-heavy nuclei. The results are also compared for the recently discovered odd-even chain starting at Z=112 and N=165.

# Skyrme Hartree-Fock Calculations for the Alpha Decay Q Values of Super-Heavy Nuclei

## Key claim
The SKX Skyrme interaction, when used in axially-symmetric deformed Hartree-Fock calculations, reproduces experimental alpha-decay Q values for super-heavy nuclei to within a few hundred keV rms deviation, with the notable exception of a dip in theoretical Q values around N=152 that is not present in experiment. The results are remarkably similar to those from the independently determined SLy4 and MSk7 Skyrme interactions, despite differences in how the parameter sets were constrained.

## Method
The authors solved the Schrödinger equation for single-particle wave functions in coordinate space using a spherical basis for the angular part, with wave functions expanded in vector-spherical harmonics. Binding energies were calculated from the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock energy density integrated over spatial coordinates, with pairing treated in the BCS approach using pairing strengths G=1.9/√A MeV for protons and G=1.2/√A MeV for neutrons, and with center-of-mass and rotational corrections applied. Alpha-decay Q values were obtained from the binding energy difference Q_α = BE(A-4,Z-2) + BE(4,2) - BE(A,Z), using the experimental value of 28.30 MeV for the alpha particle binding energy.

## Key numbers
- Pairing strength: G=1.9/√A MeV for protons and G=1.2/√A MeV for neutrons, obtained from a fit to experimental pairing gaps of N=146 isotones and Z=92 isotopes
- Radial grid step size: h=0.2 fm inside an interval [0,R] with maximum radius R=(1.25A^{1/3}+12) fm
- Center-of-mass correction: E_cm = (3/4)(45A^{-1/3} - 25A^{-2/3}) MeV
- The largest deviation for SKX is for the Q value of ²⁵⁶No at N=154
- Half-life formula: log₁₀[T₁/₂/s] = 9.54(Z-2)^{0.6}/√(Q_α/MeV) - 51.37, giving agreement to within about a factor of three
- The proton Fermi energy becomes positive for the nucleus with N=188, predicted to be proton-unbound in the SKX parametrization
- An island of relative stability starts at N=164 where half-lives for Z≈53 remain at the msec level or longer until N≈174

## Context
This work extends the deformed Hartree-Fock approach of Cwiok et al. (1999) using the SLy4 interaction and of Goriely et al. (2001) using the MSk7 interaction, by applying the SKX Skyrme interaction which was explicitly constrained to reproduce observed single-particle levels in ²⁰⁸Pb (Brown 1998). The results are compared with the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) of Möller et al. (1995), which shows similar agreement in the region where data are available but diverges for extrapolation to heavier nuclei. The calculations enable predictions for alpha-decay Q values and half-lives up to the proton drip line at N=184 and Z=126, which are important for designing experiments to identify super-heavy elements.
