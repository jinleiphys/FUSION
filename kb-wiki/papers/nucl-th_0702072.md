# Universality of Decay out of Superdeformed Bands in the 190 Mass Region

> Superdeformed nuclei in the 190 mass region exhibit a striking universality in their decay-out profiles. We show that this universality can be explained in the two-level model of superdeformed decay as related to a strong separation of energy scales: a higher scale related to the nuclear interactions, and a lower scale caused by electromagnetic decay. Furthermore, we present the results of the two-level model for all decays for which sufficient data are known, including statistical extraction of the matrix element for tunneling through the potential barrier.

## Key claim

The universality of decay-out profiles in superdeformed nuclei of the 190 mass region arises because the branching ratio in the two-level model becomes insensitive to parameter variations once both conditions \(\Gamma_N \gtrsim \Gamma_S\) and \(V \gtrsim V_c\) are satisfied, causing the decay profiles to converge to a common shape. This explanation depends on the assumption that the relationship between nuclear spin and barrier shape is reasonably consistent from nucleus to nucleus.

## Method

The authors apply the two-level model of superdeformed decay, which keeps only one level in each well of the double-well potential: the decaying superdeformed (SD) level and the nearest-energy normally deformed (ND) level, connected by a tunneling matrix element \(V\). The branching ratios \(F_S\) and \(F_N\) are derived from the Green's function of the system, incorporating electromagnetic decay widths \(\Gamma_S\) and \(\Gamma_N\) and the detuning \(\Delta = \varepsilon_N - \varepsilon_S\). For each decay where sufficient data exist, the authors extract the tunneling rate \(\Gamma^\downarrow\) and then use a statistical approach based on the Wigner surmise for ND level spacings to obtain the probability distribution \(\mathcal{P}(V)\) and its mean \(\langle V\rangle\), as well as the critical tunneling matrix element \(\langle V_c\rangle\).

- For \({}^{192}\)Hg(10): \(F_S = 0.08\), \(\Gamma_S = 0.050\) meV, \(\Gamma_N = 0.733\) meV, \(D_N = 89\) eV, \(\Gamma^\downarrow = 2.7\) meV, \(\langle V\rangle = 41\) eV, \(\langle V_c\rangle = 5.6\) eV.
- For \({}^{192}\)Pb(14): \(F_S = 0.98\), \(\Gamma_S = 0.266\) meV, \(\Gamma_N = 0.201\) meV, \(D_N = 1,258\) eV, \(\Gamma^\downarrow = 0.0056\) meV, \(\langle V\rangle = 34\) eV, \(\langle V_c\rangle = 237\) eV.
- For \({}^{192}\)Pb(12): \(F_S = 0.66\), \(\Gamma_S = 0.132\) meV, \(\Gamma_N = 0.200\) meV, \(D_N = 1,272\) eV, \(\Gamma^\downarrow = 0.10\) meV, \(\langle V\rangle = 170\) eV, \(\langle V_c\rangle = 201\) eV.
- For \({}^{194}\)Hg(12) from Khoo et al. (1996) et al.: \(F_S = 0.58\), \(\Gamma_S = 0.097\) meV, \(\Gamma_N = 4.8\) meV, \(D_N = 16.3\) eV, \(\Gamma^\downarrow = 0.071\) meV, \(\langle V\rangle = 0.49\) eV, \(\langle V_c\rangle = 0.58\) eV.
- For \({}^{194}\)Pb(10) from Willsau et al. (1993) et al.: \(F_S = 0.90\), \(\Gamma_S = 0.045\) meV, \(\Gamma_N = 0.08\) meV, \(D_N = 21,700\) eV, \(\Gamma^\downarrow = 0.0053\) meV, \(\langle V\rangle = 1100\) eV, \(\langle V_c\rangle = 3300\) eV.

- In the 190 mass region, parameters relating to the potential double-well (\(D_N\) and \(V\)) are 10's to 1000's of eV, while electromagnetic decay parameters are fractions of meV.
- The standard deviation of \(\mathcal{P}(V)\) relative to its mean is \(\sigma_V / \langle V\rangle \approx 84\%\).

## Context

This work extends the two-level model of superdeformed decay developed by Stafford and Barrett (SB) and earlier work by Vigezzi et al. (1990) and Khoo et al. (1993), who established that electromagnetic widths are much smaller than inter-level spacings. The paper directly addresses the universality of decay profiles first demonstrated experimentally by Wilson et al. (2005) in the 190 mass region, contrasting it with mere abruptness noted earlier by Shimizu et al. (1993). The results enable future theoretical work connecting the angular momentum dependence of the tunneling matrix element \(V\) to the detailed shape of the barrier between the two wells, which would link the theoretical universality shown here to the experimental universality observed in spin-aligned decay profiles.
