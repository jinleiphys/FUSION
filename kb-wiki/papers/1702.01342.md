# Cooling of neutron stars with stiff stellar matter

> Recent evidence for high masses ($\sim 2~ M_\odot$) pulsars PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348-0432 requires neutron star matter to have a stiff equation of state (EoS). The thermal evolution of compact stars (CS) with stiff hadronic EoS necessitates the application of the "nuclear medium cooling" scenario with a selection of appropriate proton gap profiles together with in-medium effects (like pion softening) on cooling mechanisms in order to achieve a satisfactory explanation of all existing observational data for the temperature-age relation of CS. Here we focus on two examples from \cite{Grigorian:2016leu} for a stiff hadronic EoS without (DD2 EoS) and with (DD2vex) excluded volume correction.

# Cooling of neutron stars with stiff stellar matter

## Key claim
The "nuclear medium cooling" scenario, which includes in-medium effects such as pion softening and density-dependent proton pairing gaps, can explain all existing observational data for the temperature-age relation of compact stars when using stiff hadronic equations of state (DD2 and DD2vex), provided that different sources have different masses. The authors qualify that a satisfactory explanation requires appropriate selection of proton gap profiles together with in-medium effects, and that the resulting cooling curves are sensitive to the value and density dependence of the pp pairing gap and the effective pion gap.

## Method
The authors performed cooling simulations for neutron star sequences using two stiff hadronic equations of state: the DD2 EoS (without excluded volume correction) and the DD2vex EoS (with excluded volume correction). They computed the thermal evolution of compact stars by incorporating medium-modified Urca (MMU) processes with pion softening effects and pair-breaking-formation (PBF) processes, using various parametrizations for the effective pion gap and different models for the 1S0 proton pairing gaps and 3P2 neutron pairing gaps.

## Key numbers
- The nuclear saturation density is n0 = 0.16 fm^-3, used as reference density for pion softening effects.
- The maximum mass for the HDD EoS is Mmax = 2.06 M_sun, which is above the measured mass of PSR J0348+0432 at M = 2.01 ± 0.04 M_sun.
- For the DD2 EoS with n_c^pi = 3 n0 and EEHOr pp pairing model, Cas A ACIS-S data are explained by a neutron star mass of M = 1.950 M_sun, and HRC-S data by M = 1.980 M_sun.
- For the DD2 EoS with n_c^pi = 2.5 n0 and CCYms pp pairing model, Cas A ACIS-S data are explained by M = 1.757 M_sun, and HRC-S data by M = 1.774 M_sun.
- For the DD2vex EoS with n_c^pi = 2 n0 and BCLL pp pairing model, Cas A ACIS-S data are explained by M = 1.970 M_sun, and HRC-S data by M = 1.740 M_sun.
- The 3P2 neutron pairing gap computed by Schwenk (2003) is less than or equal to 10 keV, which the authors favor for their overall fit.

## Context
This work extends the "nuclear medium cooling" scenario developed in previous works by Grigorian, Blaschke, and Voskresensky (2005, 2013) to stiff hadronic equations of state required by high-mass pulsar observations (PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348-0432). It contrasts with the "minimal cooling paradigm" used by Elshamouty (2013) and Ho (2014), which does not incorporate in-medium effects in the weak interaction vertices and assumes suppressed proton PBF emissivity. The results enable future investigation of hybrid star cooling and high-mass twin stars, which could provide evidence for a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram as discussed by Benic (2014) and Alvarez-Castillo (2016).
