# Three-potential formalism for the three-body scattering problem with attractive Coulomb interactions

> A three-body scattering process in the presence of Coulomb interaction can be decomposed formally into a two-body single channel, a two-body multichannel and a genuine three-body scattering. The corresponding integral equations are coupled Lippmann-Schwinger and Faddeev-Merkuriev integral equations. We solve them by applying the Coulomb-Sturmian separable expansion method. We present elastic scattering and reaction cross sections of the $e^++H$ system both below and above the $H(n=2)$ threshold. We found excellent agreements with previous calculations in most cases.

## Key claim

The paper presents a generalization of the three-potential formalism to treat three-body scattering with both repulsive and attractive Coulomb interactions, using Merkuriev's splitting of the Coulomb potential in three-body configuration space to ensure the resulting Faddeev-Merkuriev integral equations have compact kernels; the method is tested on the e⁺+H system below the three-body breakup threshold, and the authors report "excellent agreements with previous calculations in most cases," implying some deviations exist.

## Method

The authors decompose the three-body scattering process into a two-body single-channel scattering, a two-body multichannel scattering, and a genuine three-body scattering, described by coupled Lippmann-Schwinger and Faddeev-Merkuriev integral equations. They apply Merkuriev's splitting of the Coulomb potential into short-range and long-range parts in the three-body configuration space, using a splitting function ζ(x,y) with parameters x⁰, y⁰ > 0 and ν > 2. The integral equations are solved by representing all operators on a finite subset of Coulomb-Sturmian (CS) basis functions, which provides a separable expansion of the short-range potentials and allows analytic calculation of the Coulomb Green's operator matrix elements via a continued fraction technique.

## Key numbers

- Elastic scattering and reaction cross sections of the e⁺+H system are presented both below and above the H(n=2) threshold (no specific numerical cross section values given in the truncated text).
- The splitting function parameters satisfy x⁰, y⁰ > 0 and ν > 2, with the specific functional form ζ(x,y) = 2 / {1 + [(x/x⁰)^ν / (1 + y/y⁰)]}.
- The Merkuriev formalism contains Noble's formalism in the limit y⁰ → ∞.
- The CS basis functions are defined with a fixed parameter b and quantum numbers n = 0,1,2,...; the three-body basis uses a direct product of CS states with angular momenta l and λ for Jacobi coordinates x and y.
- The continued fraction tail w satisfies w = u/(d+w) with solution w± = (b ± ik)²/(b²+k²), where k is the wave number.

## Context

This work extends the three-potential formalism previously developed for repulsive Coulomb interactions (Papp, 1997) to include attractive Coulomb interactions by incorporating Merkuriev's splitting method (Merkuriev, 1980). The approach builds on Noble's original formulation (Noble, 1967) for the nuclear three-body Coulomb problem and the Faddeev-Merkuriev theory developed into configuration-space differential equations (Faddeev and Merkuriev, 1993). The method enables practical solution of the integral equation form of the three-body Coulomb problem without requiring asymptotic boundary conditions, and the authors note that the CS separable expansion method had previously been tested on bound-state problems (Papp, 1997), p-d scattering below breakup (Papp, 1997), three-α resonances (Papp, 1997), and atomic bound states with attractive Coulomb interactions (Papp, 1997).
