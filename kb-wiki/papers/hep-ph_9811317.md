# Light-front Hamiltonian field theory. Towards a relativistic description of bound states

> My thesis contains an introduction to Light-front Hamiltonian field theory and updates of published articles: - "Equivalence of Covariant and Light-Front Perturbation Theory" (hep-ph/9702311 and hep-ph/9806365, N. C. J. Schoonderwoerd and B. L. G. Bakker, published in Phys.Rev.D) and - "Entanglement of Fock-space expansion and covariance in light-front Hamiltonian field theory" (hep-ph/9806365, N. C. J. Schoonderwoerd, B. L. G. Bakker and V.A. Karmanov, accepted for publication in Phys.Rev.C.)

## Key claim

The thesis demonstrates that light-front Hamiltonian dynamics provides a covariant framework for bound-state treatment and that higher Fock-state contributions are typically much smaller than in instant-form dynamics, but the work does not solve the eigenvalue problem or write down an interacting Hamiltonian; instead it establishes equivalence between light-front and covariant perturbation theory and quantifies the suppression of higher-order diagrams.

## Method

The author proves equivalence of light-front time-ordered perturbation theory and covariant perturbation theory by algebraic integration over the light-front energy \(k^-\) of covariant Feynman diagrams, resolving technical issues with ill-defined \(k^-\) integrals and transverse divergences in the Yukawa model. For the Fock-space analysis, the author compares contributions from one-boson-exchange diagrams to two-boson-exchange diagrams in scalar particle interactions, using masses and momenta relevant for the deuteron, to assess the accuracy of the ladder approximation.

## Key numbers

- The fermion self-energy functions \(F_1(q^2)\) and \(F_2(q^2)\) are given in closed form; for \(\mu = 0\), \(F_1(q^2)/(\pi^2 i) = 1/4 + m^2/(2q^2) - (1/2 - m^4/(2q^4)) \log((m^2 - q^2)/m^2)\) and \(F_2(q^2)/(\pi^2 i) = 1 - (1 - m^2/q^2) \log((m^2 - q^2)/m^2)\).
- The divergent parts of the structure functions satisfy \(f^{2-}_1 = - f^{2-}_2 = \pi i \, m/p^+\), verifying a cancellation condition.
- The stability group on the light-front has seven kinematic generators, compared to six in instant-form dynamics, with only three dynamical operators (\(P^-\), \(M^{-1}\), \(M^{-2}\)) versus four in instant-form.

## Context

This work extends the light-front quantization program initiated by Dirac (1949) and developed by Chang and Ma (1969) and Kogut and Soper (1970), addressing the claim by Burkardt and Langnau (1991) that rotational invariance is broken for S-matrix elements in the Yukawa model. The thesis provides the mathematical foundation for using light-front perturbation theory as a covariant framework and supports the viability of the ladder approximation for bound-state calculations, particularly for the deuteron, by showing that higher Fock-state contributions are numerically suppressed compared to instant-form dynamics.
