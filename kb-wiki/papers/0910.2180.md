# Shear viscosity from kaon condensation in color-flavor-locked quark matter

> We calculate the kaonic contribution to the shear viscosity of quark matter in the kaon-condensed color-flavor locked phase (CFL-K0). This contribution comes from a light pseudo-Goldstone boson which arises from the spontaneous breaking of the flavor symmetry by the kaon condensate. The other contribution, from the exactly massless superfluid 'phonon", has been calculated previously. We specialize to a particular form of the interaction lagrangian, parameterized by a single coupling. We find that if we make reasonable guesses for the values of the parameters of the effective theory, the kaons have a much smaller shear viscosity than the superfluid phonons, but also a much shorter mean free path, so they could easily provide the dominant contribution to the shear viscosity of CFL-K0 quark matter in a neutron star in the temperature range 0.01 to 1 MeV (10^8 to 10^10 K).

## Key claim

The kaonic contribution to the shear viscosity of CFL-K0 quark matter is much smaller than that from superfluid phonons, but kaons have a much shorter mean free path, so they could provide the dominant contribution to the shear viscosity in a neutron star in the temperature range 0.01 to 1 MeV (10^8 to 10^10 K), assuming reasonable guesses for the parameters of the effective theory.

## Method

The authors calculated the shear viscosity from Goldstone kaons in the kaon-condensed color-flavor-locked phase (CFL-K0) using kinetic theory and the Boltzmann equation for 2-to-2 scattering. They specialized to a particular form of the interaction Lagrangian with a single coupling constant C, derived from higher-derivative terms in the effective chiral Lagrangian for the meson field. The collision integral was evaluated numerically using the Vegas Monte Carlo algorithm, with the minimum-exponent parameter n = -1 in the polynomial expansion of the deviation function to achieve rapid convergence.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity from Goldstone kaons scales as η ∝ T^(-5), with the constant of proportionality having mass dimension 8.
- For typical parameter values (m_K = 4 MeV, f_π = 100 MeV, Δ = 100 MeV, C = 1), the Goldstone kaons become non-hydrodynamic at T ≲ 0.03 MeV, while phonons become non-hydrodynamic at T ≲ 1 MeV.
- The dimensionless parameter u = 3g^2/λ = 96 C sin^2(φ) (μ_K/Δ)^2 determines which scattering process dominates; when u ≪ 1 the contact term dominates, when u ≫ 1 the particle-exchange process dominates.
- The shear viscosity depends on the Goldstone kaon speed ν raised to the 11th power in the leading-order behavior when ν ≪ 1.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of transport properties in CFL quark matter, specifically the shear viscosity from superfluid phonons calculated by Manuel (2004) and Rupak (2007). The authors previously studied the bulk viscosity (Alford 2007, 2008), specific heat (Alford 2007), and thermal conductivity (Braby 2009) of CFL phases. The results enable comparison of kaonic and phononic contributions to shear viscosity in neutron star cores, which is relevant for understanding r-mode damping and the possibility of rapidly rotating neutron stars containing CFL-K0 quark matter.
