# Baryon density dependence of viscosities of the quark-gluon plasma at hadronization

> The $ϕ$ meson and $Ω$ baryon provide unique probes of the properties of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) at hadronization in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Using the quark recombination model with the quark phase-space information parameterized in a viscous blastwave, we perform Bayesian inference of the shear and bulk viscosities of the QGP at hadronization with a temperature of $T\sim 160$ MeV by analyzing the $ϕ$ and $Ω$ data in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=$ 19.6-200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=$ 2.76 TeV, corresponding to a baryon chemical potential variation from $μ_B\approx 0$ (at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}= 2.76$ TeV) to $200$ MeV (at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}= 19.6$ GeV). We find that the shear viscosity to enthalpy ratio $ηT/(ε+P)$ of the QGP at hadronization decreases as $μ_B$ increases, with $ηT/(ε+P)\approx 0.18$ at $μ_B=0$ and $ηT/(ε+P)\approx 0.08$ at $μ_B=200$ MeV, while the corresponding specific bulk viscosity is essentially constant with $ζT/(ε+ P)=0.02\sim 0.04$ for $μ_B<200$ MeV. Our results suggest that the QGP at hadronization ($T\sim 160$ MeV) with finite baryon density is more close to perfect fluid than that with zero baryon density.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to enthalpy ratio of the quark-gluon plasma at hadronization (temperature ~160 MeV) decreases as baryon chemical potential increases, while the specific bulk viscosity remains essentially constant; this suggests the QGP at finite baryon density is more close to a perfect fluid than at zero baryon density, but the results are obtained under the assumptions of the viscous blastwave parameterization and the quark recombination model.

## Method

The authors used the quark recombination model with quark phase-space distributions parameterized in a viscous blastwave, incorporating shear and bulk viscous corrections via Grad's method and the 14-moment approximation respectively. They performed Bayesian inference using the MADAI package with a Gaussian process emulator and Markov Chain Monte Carlo, fitting the transverse-momentum spectra and elliptic flow v2 of phi mesons and Omega baryons from Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=19.6-200 GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV. The temperature and baryon chemical potentials at hadronization were taken from the chemical freeze-out values of Andronic (2017), with values ranging from mu_B=0 at 2.76 TeV to mu_B=200 MeV at 19.6 GeV.

## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity to enthalpy ratio at hadronization: eta T/(epsilon+P) ≈ 0.18 at mu_B=0 (Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV) and eta T/(epsilon+P) ≈ 0.08 at mu_B=200 MeV (Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=19.6 GeV).
* Specific bulk viscosity at hadronization: zeta T/(epsilon+P)=0.02-0.04 for mu_B<200 MeV, essentially constant within uncertainties.
* For Au+Au at 19.6 GeV: eta T/(epsilon+P)=0.076 (+0.030/-0.029) and zeta T/(epsilon+P)=0.035 (+0.017/-0.016) at 68.3% confidence level.
* For Au+Au at 62.4 GeV: eta T/(epsilon+P)=0.15 ± 0.026 and zeta T/(epsilon+P)=0.037 ± 0.016 at 68.3% confidence level.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Yang (2022) from baryon-free QGP to finite baryon density, using phi and Omega observables which carry direct information from hadronization due to their small hadronic interaction cross sections as noted by Shor (1984). The results are consistent with the most recent Bayesian analyses finding eta/s ≈ 0.16 at T=160 MeV for baryon-free QGP from Nijs (2022) and Heffernan (2023), and the trend of decreasing shear viscosity with increasing mu_B aligns with predictions from the Chapman-Enskog theory of Denicol (2013), the hadron resonance gas model of Kadam (2015), and the holographic model of Grefa (2022). The findings provide reference for parameterizing viscosities in hydrodynamic simulations of baryon-rich QGP and for exploring transport properties in binary neutron star mergers where quark matter may exist.
