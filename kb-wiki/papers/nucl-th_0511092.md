The cooperative processes can be induced and enhanced by low energy external fields. # New Cooperative Mechanisms of Low Energy Nuclear Reactions Using Superlow Energy External Fields

## Key claim

The authors propose that low energy nuclear reactions (LENR) can occur through cooperative processes involving the entire system of nuclei, atoms, and condensed matter, with thresholds lower than those for free constituents, and that these processes can be induced and enhanced by low energy external fields, but this mechanism depends on the system being open and the classical reproducibility principle being reconsidered for LENR experiments.

## Method

The authors derive classical Hamiltonian mechanics for the hydrogen atom, showing that the Bohr quantization condition emerges from requiring minimality of the classical Hamiltonian combined with the second Kepler law, without quantum postulates. They then extend this reasoning to argue that proton and electron motions are synchronized with equal frequency, impulse, and de Broglie wavelength, making the hydrogen atom a nondecomposable system, and apply this cooperative framework to weak interaction processes (neutron decay and electron capture by protons) in condensed matter under external fields.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of electron kinetic energy to proton kinetic energy in a hydrogen atom is $4.46 \times 10^{-4}$.
- The invariant of motion $\mu f = \mu v r = 6.582118 \times 10^{-22}$ MeV·s, which equals $\hbar$.
- The rest mass difference between neutron and (proton+electron) is $0.7823$ MeV/$c^2$, converted to kinetic energy in neutron decay.
- The threshold energy for electron capture by free protons is $0.7823$ MeV; for the reaction $^{14}_{7}N + e^{-} \rightarrow ^{14}_{7}C + \nu_e$, the threshold is $T_k(e) = 156.41$ keV.
- The threshold for $^{32}_{16}S + e^{-} \rightarrow ^{32}_{15}P + \nu_e$ is $1.710$ MeV; for $^{33}_{16}S + e^{-} \rightarrow ^{33}_{15}P + \nu_e$, the threshold is $0.240$ MeV.
- The total number of neutrons produced by one typical lightning discharge is estimated as $2.5 \times 10^{10}$.
- For $^{58}_{28}Ni(68.27\%) + e^{-} \rightarrow ^{58}_{27}Co(70.78\ \text{days}) + \nu_e$, the threshold $Q_1 = 0.37766$ keV, compared to $Q = 0.7823$ MeV for free protons (ratio $Q_1/Q_2 \approx 2$).
- For $^{58}_{27}Co(70.78\ \text{days}) + e^{-} \rightarrow ^{58}_{26}Fe(0.28\%) + \nu_e$, the emitted energy $Q_2 = 2.30408$ MeV.
- For double electron capture $^{58}_{28}Ni(68.27\%) + 2e^{-} \rightarrow ^{58}_{26}Fe(0.28\%) + 2\nu_e$, the emitted energy $Q_3 = 1.92642$ MeV.

## Context

This paper extends the authors' earlier work on the universal resonance synchronization principle (Gareev, earlier work) and reviews of LENR stimulation mechanisms (Gareev, earlier work). It contradicts the assumption that nuclear and atomic processes can be treated independently, arguing instead for a unified treatment of the whole system. The work enables experimental verification through detection of specific isotopes like $^{58}_{27}Co$ and enriched $^{58}_{26}Fe$ from discharge experiments, and it suggests that radiocarbon dating is unreliable due to natural transmutation by lightning, citing Libby (1973) for radiocarbon production and Selvaraj (1970) for radiophosphorus production by thunderstorms.
