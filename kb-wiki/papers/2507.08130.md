# Chiral and deconfinement transitions in spin-polarized quark matter

> We investigate the influence of spin polarization in strongly interacting matter by introducing a finite spin potential, $μ_Σ$, which effectively controls the spin density of the system without requiring rotation or specific boundary conditions. Inspired by recent lattice QCD simulations that incorporated such a potential, we implement this approach within an effective QCD framework. Our results show that increasing spin polarization leads to a simultaneous decrease in both the chiral and deconfinement restoration temperatures. The resulting phase structure is qualitatively consistent with lattice findings, and notably, we observe the emergence of a first-order chiral phase transition at low temperature. These results suggest that spin-polarized environments can significantly impact the QCD phase diagram and offer a controlled route for studying spin effects in hot and dense matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that increasing spin polarization (via a finite spin potential μ_Σ) simultaneously decreases both the chiral and deconfinement restoration temperatures in strongly interacting matter, and that a first-order chiral phase transition emerges at low temperature. The hedge is that these results are obtained within an effective QCD model (the entangled Polyakov–Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model) in the mean-field approximation, and the qualitative agreement with lattice QCD is noted while the quantitative curvature differs.

## Method

The authors introduce a finite spin potential μ_Σ into the SU(2) entangled Polyakov–Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (EPNJL) model in the mean-field approximation, with the spin potential coupled to the quark spin operator in the z-direction. They solve the gap equations for the constituent quark mass M and the Polyakov loop expectation values l and l̄ at finite temperature T and zero baryon chemical potential, using the logarithmic parametrization for the Polyakov loop potential. Pseudocritical temperatures for chiral and deconfinement transitions are extracted from the peaks of the chiral and Polyakov loop susceptibilities as functions of T for various μ_Σ values.

## Key numbers

- The zero-temperature parameters used: Λ = 587.9 MeV, GΛ² = 2.44, m_c = 5.6 MeV (Buballa 2003).
- The Polyakov loop potential uses T₀ = 212 MeV, with parameters a₀ = 3.51, a₁ = -2.47, a₂ = 15.2, b₃ = -1.75.
- The entanglement parameters are α₁ = α₂ = 0.2 (Sakai 2010).
- At T = 0, a first-order phase transition occurs at μ_Σ ≈ 0.472 GeV.
- The critical end point (CEP) is located at (T_CEP, μ_Σ_CEP) = (0.091 GeV, 0.47226 GeV).
- The curvature κ from the quadratic fit T_pc(μ_Σ)/T_pc(0) = 1 − κ(μ_Σ/T_pc(0))² is κ ≈ 0.03 for both chiral and deconfinement transitions.
- The lattice QCD result (Braguta 2025) for the same curvature is κ ≈ 0.06 for M_ps/M_v = 0.175.

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD study by Braguta (2025) that introduced a finite spin potential μ_Σ in two-flavor Monte Carlo simulations, by implementing the same concept in an effective model to access real values of μ_Σ without the sign problem. The EPNJL model (Sakai 2010) is used to compare with lattice results and to predict the phase diagram at higher μ_Σ values that lattice QCD cannot yet reach. The results enable future studies of spin-polarized quark matter in compact objects and provide a controlled route for investigating spin effects in hot and dense matter, with planned extensions beyond mean-field and to renormalized theories.
