# Toward a reliable description of ${(p,pN)}$ reactions in the distorted-wave impulse approximation

> Background: Proton-induced nucleon knockout $(p,pN)$ reactions have been successfully used to study the single-particle nature of stable nuclei in normal kinematics with the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework. Recently, these reactions have been applied to rare-isotope beams at intermediate energies in inverse kinematics to study the quenching of spectroscopic factors. Purpose: Our goal is to investigate the effects of various corrections and uncertainties within the standard DWIA formalism on the $(p,pN)$ cross sections. The consistency of the extracted reduction factors between DWIA and other methods is also evaluated. Method: We analyze the $(p,2p)$ and $(p,pn)$ reactions data measured at the R$^3$B/LAND setup at GSI for carbon, nitrogen, and oxygen isotopes in the incident energy range of 300--450 MeV/u. Cross sections and reduction factors are calculated by using the DWIA method. The transverse momentum distribution of the $^{12}$C($p$,$2p$)$^{11}$B reaction is also investigated. Results: We have found that including the nonlocality corrections and the Møller factor affects the cross sections considerably. The proton-neutron asymmetry dependence of reduction factors extracted by the DWIA calculation is very weak and consistent with those given by other reaction methods and \textit{ab initio} structure calculations. Conclusions: The results found in this work provide a detailed investigation of the DWIA method for $(p,pN)$ reactions at intermediate energies. They also suggest that some higher-order effects, which is essential for an accurate cross-section description at large recoil momentum, is missing in the current DWIA and other reaction models.

## Key claim

The reduction factors extracted from DWIA analysis of GSI \((p,pN)\) data show a very weak dependence on proton-neutron asymmetry, consistent with other reaction models and *ab initio* calculations, but the absolute values overestimate those from \((e,e'p)\) experiments because higher-order effects missing in current DWIA and other reaction models become important at the large recoil momenta sampled by the semi-inclusive GSI data.

## Method

The authors analyzed semi-inclusive \((p,2p)\) and \((p,pn)\) data for carbon, nitrogen, and oxygen isotopes measured at the R\(^3\)B/LAND setup at GSI (beam energies 300–450 MeV/u) using the standard partial-wave distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) formalism. They calculated cross sections and reduction factors including nonlocality corrections (Perey factor for bound states, Darwin factor for scattering waves), the Møller factor for relativistic transformation of the \(pN\) cross section, and energy-dependent Dirac optical potentials (EDAD2 parameter set). The transverse momentum distribution of the \(^{12}\)C\((p,2p)^{11}\)B reaction was also computed and compared to data.

## Key numbers

* Reduction factor for \(^{12}\)C\((p,2p)^{11}\)B: \(R_s = 0.87(16)\) at 398 MeV/u beam energy.
* Reduction factor for \(^{16}\)O\((p,2p)^{15}\)N: \(R_s = 0.97(15)\) at 451 MeV/u.
* Linear fit to \(\Delta S\) dependence: \(R_s = 0.947(36) - 2.6(27) \times 10^{-3} \Delta S\) with reduced \(\chi^2/N = 0.74\).
* The Møller factor contributes 18%–26% to the reduction factor for the 300–450 MeV/u energy range.
* Neglecting nonlocality corrections changes the reduction factor for \(^{12}\)C\((p,2p)^{11}\)B from 0.87 to 0.77.
* Theoretical uncertainty from different Dirac optical potential sets is at most 10%; from different on-shell energy prescriptions for the \(pN\) cross section, 2% for \((p,2p)\) and 8% for \((p,pn)\).

## Context

This work extends the partial-wave DWIA analysis of \((p,pN)\) reactions in inverse kinematics (Yoshida 2018) to all published GSI R\(^3\)B/LAND data (Panin 2016, Atar 2018, Fèbre 2018, Holl 2019). It systematically evaluates corrections (nonlocality, Møller factor, energy-dependent optical potentials) that were not fully included in prior eikonal DWIA (Atar 2018), Faddeev-AGS (Fèbre 2018), or transfer-to-the-continuum (Gómez-Ramos 2018) analyses of the same data. The weak asymmetry dependence of reduction factors agrees with *ab initio* coupled-cluster (Jensen 2011) and self-consistent Green’s function (Cipollone 2015) calculations, contradicting the steeper trend seen in nucleon removal on composite targets (Gade 2008, Tostevin 2014). The results indicate that higher-order reaction mechanisms are needed to describe semi-inclusive data at large recoil momentum, motivating future restricted-kinematics measurements or inclusion of multistep processes in reaction models.
