# Formation of eta-mesic nuclei by (pi,N) reaction and N^*(1535) in medium

> We calculate formation spectra of eta-nucleus systems in (pi,N) reactions with nuclear targets, which can be performed at existing and/or forthcoming facilities, including J-PARC, in order to investigate eta-nucleus interactions. Based on the N^*(1535) dominance in the eta N system, eta-mesic nuclei are suitable systems for study of in-medium properties of the N^*(1535) baryon resonance, such as reduction of the mass difference of N and N^* in nuclear medium, which affects level structure of the eta and N^*-hole modes. We find that clear information on the in-medium N^*- and eta-nucleus interactions can be obtained through the formation spectra of the eta-mesic nuclei. We also discuss the experimental feasibilities by showing several spectra of (pi,N) reactions calculated with possible experimental settings. Coincident measurements of pi N pairs from the N^* decays in nuclei help us to reduce backgrounds.

## Key claim

The paper claims that clear information on the in-medium properties of the N*(1535) resonance and the eta-nucleus interaction can be obtained through formation spectra of eta-mesic nuclei produced via (pi,N) reactions, but only if the mass gap reduction between N and N*(1535) in nuclear medium is sufficiently large (e.g., 20% at normal nuclear density) to cause level crossing between eta and N*-hole modes.

## Method

The authors calculated formation spectra of eta-nucleus systems in (pi+,p) reactions on nuclear targets using the Green's function method with the impulse approximation, assuming N*(1535) dominance for the eta-N system. They employed two distinct chiral models for the in-medium N*(1535) properties: the chiral doublet model (where N* is a chiral partner of the nucleon, with density-dependent mass gap reduction parameterized by C=0.2) and the chiral unitary model (where N* is a dynamically generated resonance with negligible medium mass shift). The incident pion kinetic energies were set to 820 MeV and 650 MeV to satisfy recoilless kinematics at the eta threshold and at omega_eta = m_eta - 50 MeV, respectively, using eikonal approximation for distortion effects.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant g_eta for the s-wave eta NN* vertex is determined to be g_eta = 2.0 to reproduce the in-vacuum partial width Gamma_{N* -> eta N} = 75 MeV
- The mass gap reduction parameter C ranges from 0.1 to 0.3 empirically, with C=0.2 used for the chiral doublet model calculations (20% mass gap reduction at normal nuclear density rho_0)
- Level crossing between eta and N*-hole modes occurs around rho = 0.4 rho_0 when the mass gap is reduced by 20% at normal nuclear density
- At omega = m_eta - 100 MeV, the real part of the eta optical potential is about 140 MeV attractive, while at the eta threshold omega = m_eta it is 55 MeV repulsive at r=0 (chiral doublet model)
- Incident pion kinetic energies: T_pi = 820 MeV (recoilless at eta threshold) and T_pi = 650 MeV (recoilless at omega_eta = m_eta - 50 MeV)
- Elementary cross sections: (d sigma/d Omega)_{n(pi+,p)eta}^{lab} = 2.4 mb/sr for T_pi = 650 MeV and 0.64 mb/sr for T_pi = 820 MeV
- The level difference between N*-hole and eta modes without medium effects is only 50 MeV, which is 10% of the N-N* mass gap

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of eta-mesic nuclei by Haider and Liu (1986) and the authors' own prior works (PRC66, PRC68, Nagahiro 2005) on (d,He-3) and (gamma,p) formation reactions, now applying the (pi,N) reaction with recoilless kinematics achievable at J-PARC. It contrasts two chiral models for N*(1535): the chiral doublet model (DeTar 1988, Jido 2001) predicting significant mass gap reduction, and the chiral unitary model (Waas and Weise 1997, Inoue and Oset 2002) predicting negligible medium effects. The paper enables experimental searches for eta-mesic nuclei at J-PARC with specific beam energies and proposes coincident measurement of pi N pairs from N* decays to reduce backgrounds, addressing why the earlier experiment by Chrien (1988) using (pi+,p) with finite momentum transfer failed to observe the predicted narrow states.
