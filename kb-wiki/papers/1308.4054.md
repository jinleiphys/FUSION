# Calculations of the cross sections for synthesis of new {293-296}118 isotopes in {249-252}Cf(48Ca,xn) reactions

> A project of using a target consisting of the mixture of (249-252)Cf isotopes to be bombarded with the 48Ca beam, aimed to synthesize new isotopes of the heaviest known element Z = 118, is under way at the FLNR in Dubna. In the present work excitation functions for all the reactions: 249Cf(48Ca,xn)(297-x)118, 250Cf(48Ca,xn)(298-x)118, 251Cf(48Ca,xn)(299-x)118 and 252Cf(48Ca,xn)(300-x)118 have been calculated in the framework of the fusion-by-diffusion model, assuming fission barriers, ground-state masses and shell effects of the superheavy nuclei predicted by Kowal et al. Energy dependence of the effective cross sections for the synthesis of selected new isotopes: (293)118, (294)118, (295)118 and (296)118 is predicted for the particular isotopic composition of the Cf target prepared for the Dubna experiment.

## Key claim

The fusion-by-diffusion model predicts that using a mixed-isotope target of 249-252Cf with a 48Ca beam can synthesize new isotopes of element 118, with the largest weighted cross section of about 1 pb expected for 295118 at Ec.m. ≈ 208 MeV, though the predictions depend on theoretical fission barriers and shell effects from Kowal et al. and the specific isotopic composition of the target.

## Method

Excitation functions for the 249Cf(48Ca,xn)297-x118, 250Cf(48Ca,xn)298-x118, 251Cf(48Ca,xn)299-x118, and 252Cf(48Ca,xn)300-x118 reactions were calculated using the fusion-by-diffusion (FBD) model, which factorizes the evaporation-residue cross section into capture cross section, fusion probability, and survival probability. The fusion probability was derived from the Smoluchowski diffusion equation with the injection point as the only adjustable parameter, while the survival probability was computed using the statistical model with Weisskopf neutron widths and transition-state fission widths, incorporating level densities from Reisdorf (1981) and shell effects from Ignatyuk (1975). Fission barriers, ground-state masses, and shell effects for superheavy nuclei were taken from the Warsaw macroscopic-microscopic model of Kowal et al. (2010).

## Key numbers

- The weighted cross section for synthesis of 295118 is predicted to be about 1 pb at Ec.m. ≈ 208 MeV.
- The weighted cross section for synthesis of 294118 is predicted to be about 0.2–0.3 pb at Ec.m. = 208–216 MeV.
- The weighted cross section for synthesis of 293118 is predicted to be about 0.2 pb at Ec.m. = 216–218 MeV.
- For the 250Cf(48Ca,3n)295118 reaction, the cross section is about 3 pb at Ec.m. ≈ 208 MeV.
- The 249Cf target used in the earlier synthesis of 294118 had >98% purity; the mixed target composition is 42.31% 249Cf, 21.76% 250Cf, 35.64% 251Cf, and 0.29% 252Cf.
- The experimental cross section for 249Cf(48Ca,3n)294118 was evaluated at Ec.m. ≈ 210 MeV, with an upper limit at Ec.m. ≈ 205 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the fusion-by-diffusion model calculations previously applied to hot fusion reactions for elements Z = 114–118 by Siwek-Wilczynska et al. (2012), using the same theoretical fission barriers from Kowal et al. (2010) and the injection point systematics from that earlier study. It provides predictions for an ongoing experiment at FLNR in Dubna using a mixed Cf target, complementing earlier DNS model calculations by Mandaglio et al. for the same reactions. The results enable experimental planning for synthesizing new isotopes 293118, 295118, and 296118, with 295118 expected to have a longer half-life than the already detected 294118, and 293118 potentially providing cross-checks of the known boundary of superheavy nuclides through its decay chain.
