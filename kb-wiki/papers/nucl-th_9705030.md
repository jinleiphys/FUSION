# The Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction in a Chiral Constituent Quark Model

> We study the short-range nucleon-nucleon interaction in a chiral constituent quark model by diagonalizing a Hamiltonian comprising a linear confinement and a Goldstone boson exchange interaction between quarks. The six-quark harmonic oscillator basis contains up to two excitation quanta. We show that the highly dominant configuration is $\mid s^4p^2[42]_O [51]_{FS}>$ due to its specific flavour-spin symmetry. Using the Born-Oppenheimer approximation we find a strong effective repulsion at zero separation between nucleons in both $^3S_1$ and $^1S_0$ channels. The symmetry structure of the highly dominant configuration implies the existence of a node in the S-wave relative motion wave function at short distances. The amplitude of the oscillation of the wave function at short range will be however strongly suppressed. We discuss the mechanism leading to the effective short-range repulsion within the chiral constituent quark model as compared to that related with the one-gluon exchange interaction.

# The Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction in a Chiral Constituent Quark Model

## Key claim

The short-range part of the Goldstone boson exchange (GBE) interaction between constituent quarks, which already produces correct baryon spectra, also induces a strong effective repulsion between nucleons at zero separation in both the \(^3S_1\) and \(^1S_0\) channels. This repulsion arises from the specific flavour-spin symmetry of the GBE interaction rather than from the one-gluon exchange (OGE) mechanism used in previous models.

## Method

The authors diagonalized a six-quark Hamiltonian containing a linear confinement term and a Goldstone boson exchange interaction (including \(\pi\), \(K\), \(\eta\), and \(\eta'\) exchanges) in a harmonic oscillator basis with up to two excitation quanta. Using the Born-Oppenheimer (adiabatic) approximation, they calculated the effective internucleon potential at zero separation as the difference between the lowest six-quark eigenvalue and twice the nucleon mass computed in the same model. The basis included five configurations: \(|s^6 [6]_O [33]_{FS}\rangle\), \(|s^4p^2 [42]_O [33]_{FS}\rangle\), \(|s^4p^2 [42]_O [51]_{FS}\rangle\), \(|s^4p^2 [42]_O [411]_{FS}\rangle\), and a mixed \(|s^52s/s^4p^2 [6]_O [33]_{FS}\rangle\) state.

## Key numbers

- Effective repulsion height at zero separation: 0.830 GeV in the \(^3S_1\) channel and 1.356 GeV in the \(^1S_0\) channel.
- The configuration \(|s^4p^2 [42]_O [51]_{FS}\rangle\) has 93% probability in the ground state eigenvector for both \(SI=10\) and \(SI=01\) sectors.
- The \(\Delta-N\) splitting used to determine the coupling constant \(C_{\chi}\) is 293 MeV, giving \(C_{\chi} = 29.3\) MeV.
- Harmonic oscillator parameter for the nucleon: \(\beta = 0.437\) fm, yielding a nucleon mass of 969.6 MeV from the Hamiltonian.
- Constituent quark mass: \(m = 340\) MeV; confinement strength: \(C = 0.474\) fm\(^{-2}\); pion mass: \(\mu_{\pi} = 139\) MeV; eta mass: \(\mu_{\eta} = 547\) MeV; eta-prime mass: \(\mu_{\eta'} = 958\) MeV.
- When the GBE interaction is removed, the effective potential becomes attractive at \(-0.197\) GeV, demonstrating that GBE contributes approximately 1 GeV of repulsion.

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model developed by Glozman and Riska (1996), which successfully described baryon spectra using Goldstone boson exchange instead of one-gluon exchange. Previous studies of short-range NN repulsion (Oka and Yazaki, 1984; Myhrer and Wroldsen, 1988; Shimizu, 1989) attributed the repulsion to the colour-magnetic part of OGE combined with quark interchanges, while hybrid models (Kohno et al., 1991; Zhang et al., 1994; Fernandez et al., 1996) added meson exchange for long-range attraction but kept OGE for short-range repulsion. The present paper shows that GBE alone can produce the short-range repulsion, and that the resulting six-quark wave function is dominated by a configuration with \([51]_{FS}\) symmetry, making the standard single-channel resonating group method (RGM) ansatz inadequate for this model. This enables future dynamical calculations of NN phase shifts and wave functions that include the full six-quark structure at short distances.
