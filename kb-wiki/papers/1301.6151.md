# Propagation of uncertainties in the Skyrme energy-density-functional model

> Parameters of nuclear energy-density-functionals (EDFs) are always derived by an optimization to experimental data. For the minima of appropriately defined penalty functions, a statistical sensitivity analysis provides the uncertainties of the EDF parameters. To quantify theoretical errors of observables given by the model, we studied the propagation of uncertainties within the UNEDF0 Skyrme-EDF approach. We found that typically the standard errors rapidly increase towards neutron rich nuclei. This can be linked to large uncertainties of the isovector coupling constants of the currently used EDFs.

## Key claim

The standard errors of observables calculated with the UNEDF0 Skyrme-EDF model increase rapidly towards neutron-rich nuclei, which is linked to large uncertainties in the isovector coupling constants of the functional; however, the analysis is performed with a spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov solver, so the calculated uncertainties may lack deformation effects for nuclei that are actually deformed.

## Method

The authors used the UNEDF0 parameterization of the Skyrme energy-density-functional within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach, solving the HFB equations in a spherical harmonic-oscillator basis with the HOSPHE code. They computed the statistical standard deviation of observables from the covariance matrix of the UNEDF0 parameters (taken from Kortelainen et al., 2010) using a Taylor expansion, with derivatives calculated via finite differences and Richardson extrapolation up to order m=2. Ground-state properties were determined for all even-even semi-magic nuclei with N=20, 28, 50, 82, and 126 and Z=20, 28, 50, and 82 extending between the two-proton and two-neutron driplines.

## Key numbers

* Standard error of the binding energy of 208Pb: 0.41 MeV, compared to a fit residual of 4.07 MeV.
* Standard error of the proton rms radius in 208Pb: 0.006 fm.
* Standard error of the neutron rms radius in 208Pb: 0.062 fm.
* Standard error of the point-neutron density in 208Pb in the region R<4 fm: 0.0035 fm^-3 or less.
* Standard error squared of the binding energy in 208Pb: 0.17 MeV^2.
* Standard error squared of the two-neutron separation energy in 208Pb: 0.02 MeV^2.
* Standard error squared of the neutron rms radius in 208Pb: 3.8 x 10^-3 fm^2.
* Standard error squared of the proton rms radius in 208Pb: 4.1 x 10^-5 fm^2.
* Root-mean-square of standard errors for proton single-particle energies in 208Pb: 0.196 MeV.
* Root-mean-square of standard errors for neutron single-particle energies in 208Pb: 0.176 MeV.
* Standard errors of binding energies reach about 10 MeV near the neutron drip line.
* Standard errors of two-proton separation energies in heavy nuclei: below 0.5 MeV in the experimentally known region.
* Standard errors of two-neutron separation energies in heavy nuclei: below 0.25 MeV in the experimentally known region.

## Context

This work extends the statistical sensitivity analysis performed for the UNEDF0 parameter optimization by Kortelainen et al. (2010) to study how parameter uncertainties propagate to calculated observables. The results are directly relevant to assessing the predictive power of current Skyrme energy-density-functionals, particularly for neutron-rich nuclei that will be explored at radioactive-beam facilities. The finding that standard errors increase rapidly towards the neutron drip line, driven by poorly constrained isovector coupling constants, motivates future work to include more data constraining the isovector sector and to test the spherical approximation against deformed calculations as noted by Kortelainen et al. (2013).
