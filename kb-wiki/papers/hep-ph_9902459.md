# Electromagnetic Form Factors of Meson Transitions

> Within the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to modeling QCD for meson physics, we present new results for $g_{ρππ}$ and the coupling constants and form factors for the transitions $γ^\ast πρ$ and $γ^\ast π^0 γ$. We discuss the role of the sub-dominant covariants of the $π$ Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and investigate the asymptotic behavior of the $γ^\ast π^0 γ$ form factor.

## Key claim

Within the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to modeling QCD for meson physics, the paper presents new results for coupling constants and form factors that are parameter-free predictions based on a truncated QCD framework fitted to a few key low-energy observables; however, the results for \(g_{\rho\pi\pi}\) overestimate the empirical value by 21%, and the \(\gamma^\ast \pi^0 \gamma\) form factor significantly overestimates experimental data in the infrared region, suggesting deficiencies in the Ball-Chiu ansatz for the dressed photon-quark vertex at low momenta.

## Method

The authors employ the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) approach with a ladder/rainbow truncation for the quark-gluon interaction, using an effective gluon 2-point function in Landau gauge that interpolates between 1-loop pQCD in the UV and a phenomenological enhancement in the IR. Meson bound states are generated via the Bethe-Salpeter (BS) equation, with the pion BS amplitude including sub-dominant pseudovector covariants \(F_\pi\) and \(G_\pi\) beyond the leading \(\gamma_5\) term. The dressed quark propagator is represented by an analytic parametrization of numerical DSE solutions, and the dressed photon-quark vertex is modeled using the Ball-Chiu ansatz which satisfies the Ward-Takahashi identity.

## Key numbers

- \(g_{\rho\pi\pi} = 7.32\) (compared to experimental value 6.05), overestimating the empirical \(\rho\rightarrow\pi\pi\) decay width by 21%
- \(g_{\gamma \pi \rho} = 0.45\) from the separable model (compared to empirical value \(0.54 \pm 0.03\) from \(\rho\) decay); with more realistic \(\pi\) amplitudes, \(g_{\gamma \pi \rho} = 0.708\)
- \(g_{\pi\gamma\gamma} = 0.4996\) at the physical pion mass (chiral limit anomalous decay gives \(g^{\small 0}_{\pi\gamma\gamma}=1/2\), accounting for the \(7.7\;\text{eV}\) width)
- Pion transition radius from impulse approximation: \(\sim 0.48\;\text{fm}\) (compared to data suggesting \(\sim 0.65\;\text{fm}\))
- Asymptotic limit of \(Q^2 F(Q^2)\) for \(\gamma^\ast \pi^0 \gamma\): \(\frac{16}{3}\pi^2 f_\pi^2 + \mathcal{O}(1/Q)\), compared to pQCD factorization limit of \(8\pi^2 f_\pi^2\)

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to non-perturbative QCD modeling of hadrons, building on prior studies by Maris and Roberts (1997) for pion and kaon observables and by Roberts (1996) and Bicudo et al. (1996) for charge radii and form factors. The \(\gamma^\ast \pi\rho\) form factor results are softer than the vector meson dominance (VDM) prediction and are intended for application to deuteron electromagnetic form factors in the \(Q^2 \approx 2-6\;\text{GeV}^2\) range, where previous work by Van Orden et al. (1995) showed strong sensitivity to this meson-exchange current. The \(\gamma^\ast \pi^0 \gamma\) form factor study updates earlier work by Frank et al. (1995) and clarifies the slow approach to asymptotic behavior, suggesting that the Ball-Chiu ansatz for the dressed photon-quark vertex may have deficiencies at infrared and intermediate momenta that warrant further investigation through Bethe-Salpeter solutions for the vector vertex.
