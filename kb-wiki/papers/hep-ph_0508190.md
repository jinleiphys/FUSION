# Meson Supercurrent State in High Density QCD

> We study the effect of a non-zero strange quark mass on the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase of high density quark matter. We have previously shown that for a strange quark mass $m_s\sim m_u^{1/3}Δ^{2/3}$ the CFL state becomes unstable toward the formation of a neutral kaon condensate. Recently, several authors discovered that for $m_s\sim (2Δp_F)^{1/2}$ the CFL state contains gapless fermions, and that the gapless modes lead to an instability in current-current correlation functions. Using an effective theory of the CFL state we demonstrate that this instability is resolved by the formation of an inhomogeneous meson condensate, analogous to Migdal's p-wave pion condensate. This state has a non-zero meson current which is canceled by a backflow of gapless fermions.

## Key claim

The homogeneous kaon-condensed CFL phase becomes unstable toward the formation of an inhomogeneous meson supercurrent state when the effective strange quark chemical potential approaches the threshold for gapless fermion modes, but the precise onset chemical potential depends sensitively on the values of low-energy constants in the effective theory.

## Method

The author uses an effective field theory of the CFL phase containing Goldstone boson fields and baryon fields, with low-energy constants computed in weak-coupling perturbation theory. The vacuum energy is calculated as a function of a spatially varying kaon current by computing the fermion spectrum in the presence of the current, integrating over gapless fermion modes near the Fermi surface, and minimizing the resulting energy functional.

## Key numbers

- The critical strange quark mass for the CFL-to-kaon condensate transition is $m_s^{\rm crit}\sim m_u^{1/3}\Delta^{2/3}$
- Gapless fermion modes appear in the kaon condensed CFL phase at $\mu_s=4\Delta/3$ (compared to $\mu_s=\Delta$ in ordinary CFL)
- In the case $D=F=0$, the kaon condensed ground state becomes unstable for $(\Delta - \mu_s/2) < 0.46\Delta$
- In the realistic case $D=F=1/2$ with weak-coupling constants, the instability occurs for $(\Delta - 3\mu_s/4) < 0.007\Delta$
- The homogeneous gapless kaon condensed phase becomes stable again at $(\Delta-3\mu_s/4) < -0.05$
- The numerical coefficient $a$ in the dimensionless current variable is $a=13.9$ for $D=F=0$ and $a=0.43$ for $D=F=1/2$
- The function $f_h(x)$ develops a non-trivial minimum for $h_1 < h < h_2$ with $h_1\simeq -0.067$ and $h_2\simeq 0.502$

## Context

This work extends the study of the CFL phase under non-zero strange quark mass, following the discovery by Alford (2003) and Ruster (2004) that gapless fermion modes appear in the CFL spectrum, and the observation by Huang (2004) and Casalbuoni (2004) that these modes cause instabilities in current-current correlation functions. The proposed meson supercurrent state is analogous to Migdal's p-wave pion condensate in nuclear matter and to the Larkin-Ovchinnikov-Fulde-Ferrell (LOFF) phase studied in QCD by Alford (2000). The result suggests a sequence of phases as density decreases: CFL phase, s-wave kaon condensate, and p-wave kaon condensate, with open questions about the exact nature of the ground state, the role of electric charge neutrality, and whether the supercurrent phase evolves into a LOFF-type state at lower densities.
