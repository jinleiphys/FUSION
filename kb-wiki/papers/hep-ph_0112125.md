# Inverting a Supernova: Neutrino Mixing, Temperatures and Binding Energy

> We show that the temperatures of the emergent non-electron neutrinos and the binding energy released by a galactic Type II supernova are determinable, assuming the Large Mixing Angle (LMA) solution is correct, from observations at the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (SNO) and at Super-Kamiokande (SK). If the neutrino mass hierarchy is inverted, either a lower or upper bound can be placed on the neutrino mixing angle $θ_{13}$, and the hierarchy can be deduced for adiabatic transitions. For the normal hierarchy, neither can $θ_{13}$ be constrained nor can the hierarchy be determined. Our conclusions are qualitatively unchanged for the proposed Hyper-Kamiokande detector.

## Key claim

Assuming the Large Mixing Angle (LMA) solution is correct, the temperatures of emergent non-electron neutrinos and the binding energy released by a galactic Type II supernova are determinable from observations at SNO and SK, but only if the neutrino mass hierarchy is inverted can a bound be placed on the mixing angle θ₁₃ and the hierarchy deduced; for the normal hierarchy, neither θ₁₃ can be constrained nor the hierarchy determined.

## Method

The authors simulated neutrino energy spectra for a typical supernova at 10 kpc distance, generating data for the 32 kton SK detector and the 1.4 kton light water and 1 kton heavy water tanks at SNO, considering only inverse beta decay and charged-current reactions on deuterium and oxygen. They performed a χ²-analysis freely varying the binding energy E_b, the mean energies ⟨E_νₑ⟩, ⟨E_ν̄ₑ⟩, ⟨E_νₓ⟩, and sin²2θ₁₃, while fixing solar and atmospheric oscillation parameters and allowing the luminosity ratio L_νₑ/L_νₓ to vary between 1 and 2. Four datasets were simulated for each hierarchy type at two values of sin²2θ₁₃ corresponding to adiabatic (P_H=0) and non-adiabatic (P_H=1) oscillation transitions.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy range for a 15 M⊙ progenitor: E_b = 1.5–4.5 × 10⁵³ ergs, with a typical value of 3 × 10⁵³ ergs used in simulations
- Mean energy ratios: ⟨E_νₑ⟩ : ⟨E_ν̄ₑ⟩ : ⟨E_νₓ⟩ :: 0.85 : 1 : 1.1, with ⟨E_ν̄ₑ⟩ = 15 MeV as the reference
- Neutrino oscillation parameters fixed in analysis: sin²2θ₁₂ = 0.81, Δm²₂₁ = 5.6 × 10⁻⁵ eV², |Δm²₃₁| = 3 × 10⁻³ eV²
- CHOOZ bound: sin²2θ₁₃ ≤ 0.1 at 95% C.L.
- For the inverted hierarchy with non-adiabatic transitions, an upper bound on sin²2θ₁₃ can be placed; for adiabatic transitions with ⟨E_ν̄ₑ⟩ restricted to 10.5–19 MeV and ⟨E_νₓ⟩/⟨E_ν̄ₑ⟩ > 0.7, a lower bound on sin²2θ₁₃ is obtained
- The hierarchy can be deduced if sin²2θ₁₃ ≳ 10⁻³ for the inverted hierarchy; for sin²2θ₁₃ smaller than ≈ 10⁻⁴, the hierarchies are indistinguishable

## Context

This work extends the analysis of supernova neutrino signals by Dighe and collaborators, who developed the survival probability formalism for neutrino oscillations in supernovae, and by Minakata and Nunokawa, who studied hierarchy determination. The paper assumes that solar and atmospheric oscillation parameters will be known to within 10% from upcoming experiments like KamLAND and MINOS, and shows that a galactic supernova can probe sin²2θ₁₃ values more than two orders of magnitude smaller than long-baseline experiments using conventional neutrino beams. The results enable future detectors like Hyper-Kamiokande and UNO to determine supernova astrophysical parameters and potentially identify the neutrino mass hierarchy, though the normal hierarchy remains indistinguishable regardless of detector size.
