# Resonant Relaxation in Electroweak Baryogenesis

> We compute the leading, chiral charge-changing relaxation term in the quantum transport equations that govern electroweak baryogenesis using the closed time path formulation of non-equilibrium quantum field theory. We show that the relaxation transport coefficients may be resonantly enhanced under appropriate conditions on electroweak model parameters and that such enhancements can mitigate the impact of similar enhancements in the CP-violating source terms. We also develop a power counting in the time and energy scales entering electroweak baryogenesis and include effects through second order in ratios $ε$ of the small and large scales. We illustrate the implications of the resonantly enhanced ${\cal O}(ε^2)$ terms using the Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model, focusing on the interplay between the requirements of baryogenesis and constraints obtained from collider studies, precision electroweak data, and electric dipole moment searches.

## Key claim

The authors compute the leading chiral charge-changing relaxation term in quantum transport equations for electroweak baryogenesis using the closed time path (CTP) formalism, and find that under the same conditions that produce resonant enhancements of CP-violating sources, the relaxation transport coefficient is also resonantly enhanced, partially mitigating the impact of the enhanced sources on the final baryon asymmetry.

## Method

The authors apply the CTP formulation of non-equilibrium quantum field theory to derive the quantum transport equations governing chiral charge densities during electroweak baryogenesis, developing a power counting in the ratios epsilon of small to large physical scales (decoherence time, plasma time, and intrinsic quasiparticle time scales) and truncating at second order in epsilon. They compute both the CP-violating source terms and the relaxation transport coefficients for the Minimal Supersymmetric Standard Model (MSSM), using finite-temperature and finite-density Green's functions with equilibrium distribution functions, and solve the resulting coupled diffusion equations for the baryon density.

## Key numbers

* The observed baryon asymmetry of the Universe is Y_B = (7.3 ± 2.5) × 10^{-11} from Big Bang Nucleosynthesis and (9.2 ± 1.1) × 10^{-11} from WMAP, at 95% confidence level
* The CP-violating sources in the MSSM can experience resonant enhancements of order 10^3 over their strength estimated in previous treatments
* The baryon yield scales as Y_B ~ S^{CPV} / sqrt(Gamma_bar), where S^{CPV} is the CP-violating source and Gamma_bar is the relaxation transport coefficient
* The CP-violating phases in the MSSM must be unnaturally small (∼ 10^{-2}) to satisfy electric dipole moment constraints

## Context

This work extends the CTP-based treatment of CP-violating sources in electroweak baryogenesis by Riotto (1998) and Carena et al. (2000, 2002), who found resonant enhancements of sources that could allow successful baryogenesis with smaller CP-violating phases than previously believed. The authors show that the relaxation terms, which were previously estimated using conventional quantum transport theory by Huet and Nelson (1995) and others, receive similar resonant enhancements that counteract the enhanced sources. This work enables a more systematic comparison between baryogenesis requirements and constraints from collider studies, precision electroweak data, and electric dipole moment searches, though the authors note that additional transport coefficients considered sub-leading in previous work may also be enhanced under different conditions.
