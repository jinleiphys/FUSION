# Total Gluon Helicity Contribution to the Proton Spin from Lattice QCD

> We report a state-of-the-art lattice QCD calculation of the total gluon helicity contribution to the proton spin, $ΔG$. The calculation is done on ensembles with three different lattice spacings $a=\{0.08, 0.09, 0.11\}$ fm. By employing distillation and momentum smearing for proton external states, we extract the bare matrix elements of the topological current $K^μ$ using 5-HYP smeared Coulomb gauge fixing configurations. Furthermore, we apply a non-perturbative $\mathrm{RI/MOM}$ renormalization scheme augmented by the Cluster Decomposition Error Reduction (CDER) technique to determine the renormalization constants of $K^μ$. The results obtained from different components $K^{t,i}$ (with $i$ being the direction of proton momentum or polarization) are consistent with Lorentz covariance within uncertainties. After extrapolating to the continuum limit, $ΔG$ is found to be $ΔG = 0.231(17)^{\mathrm{sta.}}(44)^{\mathrm{sym.}}$ at the $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ scale $μ^2=10\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$, which constitutes approximately $46(9)\%$ of the proton spin.

## Key claim

The total gluon helicity contribution to the proton spin is found to be ΔG = 0.231(17)sta.(44)sym. at the MSbar scale μ² = 10 GeV², which constitutes approximately 46(9)% of the proton spin; this result rules out a negative gluon helicity contribution and is obtained after extrapolating to the continuum and infinite momentum limits using lattice QCD with three lattice spacings.

## Method

The calculation uses n_f = 2+1 flavor full QCD ensembles at three lattice spacings a = {0.08, 0.09, 0.11} fm generated by CLQCD. The bare matrix elements of the topological current K^μ are extracted from proton three-point functions using distillation and momentum smearing techniques, with 5-HYP smeared Coulomb gauge fixing. Nonperturbative renormalization is performed in the RI/MOM scheme augmented by the Cluster Decomposition Error Reduction (CDER) technique, followed by conversion to the MSbar scheme using three-loop conversion factors and renormalization group evolution to μ² = 10 GeV².

## Key numbers

- ΔG = 0.231(17)sta.(44)sym. at the MSbar scale μ² = 10 GeV², corresponding to approximately 46(9)% of the proton spin
- Three lattice spacings used: a = 0.07753(03)(45) fm, 0.08973(20)(53) fm, and 0.10524(05)(62) fm
- Pion masses of the ensembles: 292.3(1.0) MeV (C24P29), 224.1(1.2) MeV (C48P23), 287.3(2.5) MeV (E32P29), and 300.4(1.2) MeV (F32P30)
- RI/MOM renormalization scale window used for extrapolation: μ_RI ∈ [3.0, 5.0] GeV, corresponding to a²p² ∈ [2.6, 7.1] for ensemble C48P23
- Proton momenta up to p = 0.98 GeV used in the analysis

## Context

This work extends the exploratory calculation by the χQCD collaboration (Yang 2016) which obtained ΔG = 0.251(47)(16) using one-loop lattice perturbative renormalization, and addresses the need for proper nonperturbative renormalization that was previously challenging due to Coulomb gauge fixing. The calculation implements the topological current approach proposed by Pang (2024) which resolves inconsistencies between LaMET factorization for ΔG and Δg(x), and enables future first-principles calculations of quark and gluon orbital angular momentum for a complete decomposition of the proton spin.
