# First extraction of transversity from data on lepton-hadron scattering and hadronic collisions

> We present the first extraction of the transversity distribution based on the global analysis of pion-pair production in deep-inelastic scattering and in proton-proton collisions with one transversely polarized proton. The extraction relies on the knowledge of di-hadron fragmentation functions, which are taken from the analysis of electron-positron annihilation data. For the first time, the chiral-odd transversity is extracted from a global analysis similar to what is usually done for the chiral-even spin-averaged and helicity distributions. The knowledge of transversity is important among other things for detecting possible signals of new physics in high-precision low-energy experiments.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first extraction of the transversity parton distribution function (PDF) from a global analysis that simultaneously fits data from semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS), electron-positron annihilation, and proton-proton collisions, but the extraction is limited to the valence components of transversity and relies on the assumption that di-hadron fragmentation functions (DiFFs) are independently determined from electron-positron data.

## Method

The author performs a global fit of the chiral-odd transversity PDF \(h_1\) at leading twist using collinear factorization, analyzing data for semi-inclusive production of \((\pi^+ \pi^-)\) pairs. The fit uses SIDIS data from HERMES (proton target) and COMPASS (proton and deuteron targets), electron-positron annihilation data from BELLE to determine the di-hadron fragmentation function \(H_1^{\sphericalangle}\), and proton-proton collision data from STAR with one transversely polarized proton. The transversity is parametrized at the starting scale \(Q_0^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\) using a functional form that automatically satisfies the Soffer bound, with 10 free parameters for the valence up and down quark components, and the statistical uncertainty is studied at the 90% confidence level using a bootstrap method with 600 replicas.

## Key numbers

- The global fit yields a final \(\chi^2\) per degree of freedom equal to \(1.76 \pm 0.11\) with 10 free parameters.
- The transversity \(x h_1\) is displayed at \(Q^2 = 2.4\) GeV\(^2\) for valence up and down quarks.
- The tensor charges \(\delta u\) and \(\delta d\) are evaluated at \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\) and 90% confidence level.
- Including COMPASS deuteron pseudodata reduces the global \(\chi^2\) per degree of freedom to \(1.32 \pm 0.09\) and increases precision on the down quark tensor charge by almost a factor of 2 in the \(x\)-range covered by pseudodata.
- For the up quark, including COMPASS pseudodata yields an almost 20% increase in precision in the \(x\)-range of pseudodata.

## Context

This work extends previous extractions of transversity that used only SIDIS and electron-positron data, such as those by Bacchetta et al. (2012) and Radici et al. (2015), by including for the first time proton-proton collision data from the STAR collaboration. The results are compared with other phenomenological extractions based on the Collins effect by Kang et al. (2015) and Anselmino et al. (2013), as well as with lattice QCD calculations from the PNDME and ETMC collaborations. The paper finds agreement among phenomenological extractions but a trend of disagreement with lattice results for the up quark tensor charge, suggesting the need for further work to resolve what might be a "transverse spin puzzle."
