# A unique spinodal region in asymmetric nuclear matter

> Asymmetric nuclear matter at sub-saturation densities is shown to present only one type of instabilities. The associated order parameter is dominated by the isoscalar density and so the transition is of liquid-gas type. The instability goes in the direction of a restoration of the isospin symmetry leading to a fractionation phenomenon. These conclusions are model independent since they can be related to the general form of the asymmetry energy. They are illustrated using density functional approaches.

## Key claim

Asymmetric nuclear matter at sub-saturation densities exhibits only one type of spinodal instability, not two separate mechanical and chemical instabilities as previously argued; the instability is always dominated by isoscalar density fluctuations, making the transition a liquid-gas type regardless of asymmetry.

## Method

The authors performed a stability analysis of asymmetric nuclear matter by diagonalizing the curvature matrix of the free energy density with respect to baryon density and asymmetry density, computing its eigenvalues and eigenvectors. They then illustrated the general formalism using several Skyrme forces (SLy230a, SGII, SIII) and Gogny forces (D1, D1S, D1P) at both zero and finite temperature, extracting sound velocities and instability directions from the curvature eigenvalues.

## Key numbers

- The asymmetry energy curvature \(\mathcal{F}_{33}\) is always positive below saturation density, with the potential energy parameterized as \(\mathcal{V}(\rho_1,\rho_3)=\mathcal{V}_0(\rho_1)\rho_1^2+\mathcal{V}_1(\rho_1)\rho_3^2\) where \(\mathcal{V}_0/\mathcal{V}_1\sim -3\) and \(\mathcal{V}_0<0\) from Brueckner calculations (Vidana 2002).
- For the SLy230a interaction, the spinodal region shrinks with increasing temperature up to the critical temperature (5 to 10 MeV) where it is reduced to the symmetric nuclear matter critical point.
- The sound velocity in the spinodal region is larger than \(0.06c\) for almost all the spinodal zone for SLy230b and D1P interactions, with contour lines shown at \(i0.09c\), \(i0.06c\), \(i0.03c\), and 0 (the spinodal border).
- The instability direction angle with the isoscalar axis, normalized by the angle between the constant proton fraction line and the isoscalar axis, lies between 0 and 1 for all tested models (D1P, SGII, SLy230a) at various proton fractions \(y\).

## Context

This work contradicts the earlier claim by Muller and Serot (1995) and Bao et al. (1997) that asymmetric nuclear matter has separate chemical and mechanical instability regions with two distinct order parameters. The authors show that the chemical and mechanical stability conditions should not be considered separately but combined into the determinant of the curvature matrix, and that the instability direction is always isoscalar-like due to the attractive proton-neutron interaction. This enables a unified description of spinodal decomposition in asymmetric nuclear matter and provides a framework to constrain the isospin dependence of effective forces through observations of spinodal decomposition, building on the fractionation phenomenon discussed by Baran et al. (1998) and observed experimentally by Xu et al. (2000).
