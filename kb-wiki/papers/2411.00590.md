# Unlocking "imprints" of conserved charges in the initial state of heavy-ion collisions

> Hydrodynamic approaches to modeling relativistic high-energy heavy-ion collisions are based on the conservation of energy and momentum. However, the medium formed in these collisions also carries additional conserved quantities, including baryon number (B), strangeness (S), and electric charge (Q). In this Letter, we propose a new set of anisotropic flow observables designed to be exclusively sensitive to the effects of conserved BSQ charge fluctuations, providing insight into the initial state. Using the recently developed hydrodynamic framework \iccing{}+\ccake{}, we show that these new observables provide a measurable effect of initial BSQ charge fluctuations (ranging up to $\sim $10\%), which can be tested by experiments.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that new anisotropic flow observables using identified particles (PID) can isolate the effects of initial-state baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge (BSQ) fluctuations arising from gluon splitting into quark-antiquark pairs, with effects ranging up to approximately 10% in certain correlators. The hedge is that these effects are only visible when using two or more particles-of-interest (POI) in the flow analysis, not in standard single-POI measurements.

## Method
The authors used the TRENTo initial condition model to generate energy density profiles, then applied the ICCING framework to simulate gluon splitting into quark-antiquark pairs with tracked flavor, producing fluctuating BSQ charge densities. These initial conditions were evolved using the 2+1 relativistic viscous hydrodynamic code CCAKE with a lattice QCD equation of state, followed by freeze-out at fixed energy density 0.276 GeV/fm^3 and hadron decays, for 6,000 Pb-Pb events at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV across 0-60% centrality.

## Key numbers
- BSQ fluctuation effects on flow observables range up to approximately 10% for the proposed 4-particle correlator f_n(+,-)
- For Omega baryons, the 4-particle correlator shows deviations from unity of 10-20% (strongest for triangular flow v_3)
- For protons and Lambdas, the event-plane correlation shows 2-6% effects
- For cascades (Xi), the 4-particle correlator shows 5-10% effects
- The flow magnitude fluctuation observable v_n^+{2}+v_n^-{2}-2v_n^h{2} is extremely small, less than 1% across all centralities
- Freeze-out energy density was set to epsilon_FO = 0.276 GeV/fm^3 (corresponding to T_FO = 150 MeV at vanishing densities)
- Analysis used kinematic cuts of 0.2 < p_T < 3.0 GeV/c matching ALICE data

## Context
This work extends the ICCING+CCAKE framework developed by Carzon (2019) and Plumberg (2024) for simulating BSQ charge fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions. It builds on prior studies of nucleonic substructure by Petersen (2010), Schenke (2014), and Noronha-Hostler (2015), which did not consider flavor and color at the sub-nucleonic level. The new observables are designed to be testable in future high-luminosity LHC runs and could also serve as a background for studies of the chiral magnetic effect. The authors note that transport coefficients were assumed constant across chemical potentials, and future work should explore the role of BSQ diffusion and the equation of state at finite baryon densities.
