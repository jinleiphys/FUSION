# Dynamical approach to heavy-ion induced fission using actinide target nuclei at energies around the Coulomb barrier

> In order to describe heavy-ion fusion reactions around the Coulomb barrier with an actinide target nucleus, we propose a model which combines the coupled-channels approach and a fluctuation-dissipation model for dynamical calculations. This model takes into account couplings to the collective states of the interacting nuclei in the penetration of the Coulomb barrier and the subsequent dynamical evolution of a nuclear shape from the contact configuration. In the fluctuation-dissipation model with a Langevin equation, the effect of nuclear orientation at the initial impact on the prolately deformed target nucleus is considered. Fusion-fission, quasi-fission and deep quasi-fission are separated as different Langevin trajectories on the potential energy surface. Using this model, we analyze the experimental data for the mass distribution of fission fragments (MDFF) in the reactions of $^{34,36}$S+$^{238}$U and $^{30}$Si+$^{238}$U at several incident energies around the Coulomb barrier. We find that the time scale in the quasi-fission as well as the deformation of fission fragments at the scission point are different between the $^{30}$Si+$^{238}$U and $^{36}$S+$^{238}$U systems, causing different mass asymmetries of the quasi-fission.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a model that combines coupled-channels calculations with a Langevin-based fluctuation-dissipation dynamics to describe heavy-ion fusion and fission around the Coulomb barrier, but the key hedge is that the model approximates all orientation angles of the deformed target by introducing an effective charge-center distance at the contact point, rather than fully treating the angular dependence of the adiabatic potential energy surface.

## Method

The authors combine a coupled-channels approach (using the sudden approximation and the code CCFULL) for the barrier-penetration phase with a three-dimensional Langevin equation (coordinates \(z\), \(\delta\), \(\alpha\)) for the subsequent shape evolution from the contact point to scission. The nuclear shape is described by a two-center parametrization with a time-dependent neck parameter \(\epsilon\) that relaxes from \(\epsilon=1\) (diabatic) to \(\epsilon=0.35\) (adiabatic) with a relaxation time \(\tau_{\epsilon}=10^{-20}\) s. Fusion-fission, quasi-fission, and deep quasi-fission are distinguished by whether the Langevin trajectory enters a predefined compact-shape region (the "fusion box") on the adiabatic potential energy surface.

## Key numbers

- For the \(^{36}\text{S}+^{238}\text{U}\) reaction at the Bass barrier (158.8 MeV), the compound-nucleus formation probability for \(\ell=0\) and \(\theta=0\) is \(P_{\text{CN}}=0.03\).
- The standard deviation of the fusion-fission mass distribution for \(^{36}\text{S}+^{238}\text{U}\) at \(E_{\text{c.m.}}=176.0\) MeV is \(\sigma_m=37.2\) u.
- For the \(^{30}\text{Si}+^{238}\text{U}\) reaction, the quasi-fission mass asymmetry is \(A_{\text{H}}/A_{\text{L}} \sim 178/90\), compared to \(204/68\) for \(^{34}\text{S}+^{238}\text{U}\) and \(200/74\) for \(^{36}\text{S}+^{238}\text{U}\).
- The Bass barrier for \(^{30}\text{Si}+^{238}\text{U}\) is 141.1 MeV; for \(^{34}\text{S}+^{238}\text{U}\) it is 161.1 MeV; for \(^{36}\text{S}+^{238}\text{U}\) it is 158.8 MeV.
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters used are \(V_0=105.0\) MeV, \(R=10.92\) fm, \(R_T=7.44\) fm, and \(a_{\text{WS}}=0.75\) fm.
- The static deformation parameters for \(^{238}\text{U}\) are \(\beta_2=0.275\) and \(\beta_4=0.05\) in the coupled-channels calculation; the static deformation used in the dynamical calculation is \(\beta_2=0.215\).

## Context

This work extends the unified model of Zagrebaev et al. (2005, 2007) by incorporating orientation-dependent contact distances for deformed actinide targets, enabling calculations below the Coulomb barrier for the first time. It analyzes experimental mass distributions of fission fragments measured by the JAEA group (Nishio et al., 2008, 2010a, 2010b) for \(^{30}\text{Si}+^{238}\text{U}\) and \(^{34,36}\text{S}+^{238}\text{U}\). The results enable future predictions of fusion probabilities for superheavy-element synthesis using hot fusion reactions with actinide targets, and highlight that mass-symmetric fission fragments can arise from deep quasi-fission rather than compound-nucleus fission.
