# Mass and axial charge of heavy baryons

> We investigate the antitriplet and sextet heavy baryon systems with $J^P= \frac{1}{2}^+, \frac{3}{2}^+$ in the framework of the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. We first calculate the chiral corrections to the heavy baryon mass from the SU(3) flavor breaking effect up to $O(p^3)$. Then we extend the same formalism to calculate the chiral corrections to the axial charges of the heavy baryons in the isospin symmetry limit.

## Key claim

The authors calculate chiral corrections to heavy baryon masses up to \(O(p^3)\) and axial charges in the isospin symmetry limit using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT), finding that loop contributions are suppressed when the mass differences \(\delta\) between baryon representations are included, and that the extracted low-energy constants are consistent with prior estimates from the Gell-Mann-Okubo relation.

## Method

The authors employ heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) to compute one-loop chiral corrections to the self-energies of antitriplet and sextet heavy baryons with \(J^P = \frac{1}{2}^+, \frac{3}{2}^+\), including both type-I loops (intermediate and external baryons with same spin) and type-II loops (different spin), as well as tree-level counterterms from the \(O(p^2)\) SU(3) flavor-breaking Lagrangian and QED Lagrangian. They then extend the same formalism to calculate chiral corrections to the axial charges \(g_1\) through \(g_6\) at \(O(p^2)\) from vertex corrections and wave function renormalization, working in the heavy quark limit and using the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme.

## Key numbers

- Average masses used as inputs: \(M_{\bar3} = 2286.46\) MeV, \(M_6 = 2454.02\) MeV, \(M_{6^*} = 2518.4\) MeV (from Nakamura:2010zzi)
- Mass difference parameters: \(\delta_1 = 126.52\) MeV, \(\delta_2 = 67.03\) MeV (isospin symmetry limit)
- Pion decay constant: \(F_0 \approx 92.4\) MeV
- Axial coupling constant from quark model: \(g_1 = 0.98\) (from Liu:2012uw, Liu:2012sw)
- Extracted low-energy constants (Fit 4, \(\delta \neq 0\), both loop types): \(b_1' = -2.05\), \(c_1' = -6.44 \pm 4.8 \times 10^{-3}\); \(b_6' = -2.37\), \(c_6' = -2.87 \pm 0.7 \times 10^{-3}\); \(b_5' = -1.95\), \(c_5' = -6.50 \pm 9.9 \times 10^{-3}\)
- Predicted mass of \(\Xi_c^0\) (Fit 4): \(2476.66 \pm 0.65\) MeV (compared to experimental \(2470.88^{+0.34}_{-0.80}\) MeV)
- Decay width predictions: \(\Gamma_{\Sigma_c^{++}} = 2.60\) MeV (experimental \(2.23\) MeV); \(\Gamma_{\Sigma_c^{*++}} = 8.10\) MeV (experimental \(14.9\) MeV)

## Context

This work extends the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory framework previously applied to nucleon octet axial currents (Jenkins:1990jv, Jenkins:1991es, Zhu:2000zf, Zhu:2002tn) to the antitriplet and sextet heavy baryon systems containing a single charm or bottom quark. The mass calculations build on earlier SU(3) flavor-breaking analyses of heavy baryon scattering lengths (Liu:2012uw, Liu:2012sw) and the mass splitting study of spin-1/2 charmed baryons (Guo:2008ns), now including both spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 baryons with full \(O(p^3)\) chiral corrections. The results provide chiral extrapolation formulas useful for lattice QCD simulations of heavy baryon properties.
