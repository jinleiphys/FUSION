# Gamow-Teller response in deformed even and odd neutron-rich Zr and Mo isotopes

> Beta-decay properties of neutron-rich Zr and Mo isotopes are investigated within a microscopic theoretical approach based on the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation. The underlying mean field is described self-consistently from deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations with pairing correlations. Residual separable particle-hole and particle-particle forces are also included in the formalism. The structural evolution in these isotopic chains including both even and odd isotopes is analyzed in terms of the equilibrium deformed shapes. Gamow-Teller strength distributions, beta-decay half-lives, and beta-delayed neutron-emission probabilities are studied, stressing their relevance to describe the path of the nucleosynthesis rapid neutron capture process.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) based on self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations with pairing correlations can reproduce the beta-decay properties of neutron-rich Zr and Mo isotopes, but the agreement with experimental half-lives depends on the assumed equilibrium deformation shape (prolate, oblate, or spherical) and on the use of experimental versus theoretical Q-beta values, with the ground-state shape predictions sometimes at variance with experimental electromagnetic data.

## Method

The authors performed constrained deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations with the SLy4 interaction and BCS pairing to obtain potential energy curves and equilibrium shapes for even-even and odd-A Zr and Mo isotopes. They then applied a deformed proton-neutron QRPA with separable residual particle-hole (strength chi_ph_GT = 0.15 MeV) and particle-particle (strength kappa_pp_GT = 0.03 MeV) interactions to compute Gamow-Teller strength distributions, beta-decay half-lives, and beta-delayed neutron-emission probabilities, using experimental Q-beta and S_n values from Audi (2012) when available and theoretical values otherwise.

## Key numbers

- Pairing gaps for protons and neutrons are roughly around 1 MeV, determined from odd-even mass differences using a symmetric five-term formula.
- The coupling strengths used are chi_ph_GT = 0.15 MeV for the particle-hole channel and kappa_pp_GT = 0.03 MeV for the particle-particle channel.
- The quenching factor applied is (g_A/g_V)_eff = 0.77 (g_A/g_V)_free, with D = 6200 s in the half-life formula.
- For Zr isotopes, ground states are located at prolate deformations with beta approximately 0.4; oblate minima occur at beta approximately -0.2.
- In Mo isotopes, light isotopes (104,106Mo) show practically degenerate oblate and prolate shapes; heavier isotopes have oblate ground states at beta approximately -0.2 with prolate excited states below 1 MeV.
- The GT strength in odd-A isotopes is displaced to higher energies by about 2-3 MeV relative to even-even isotopes, corresponding to roughly twice the neutron pairing gap.
- Experimental GT strength data are available only below 1 MeV for 106,108Mo (Jokinen), 110Mo (Wang 2004), and 100,102,104Zr (Rinta 2007).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Sarriguren and Pereira) on even-even 100-110Zr and 104-114Mo isotopes by including odd-A isotopes and more exotic species (112-116Zr and 116-120Mo), using updated Q-beta and S_n values from Audi (2012). The deformed QRPA approach builds on earlier formalisms developed by Moller, Homma, and Hirata, and the results are compared with macroscopic-microscopic methods (Skalski 1997, FRDM), relativistic mean fields (Lalazissis), and nonrelativistic calculations with Skyrme (Bonche 1985) and Gogny (Hilaire) interactions. The study enables improved theoretical support for future half-life measurements at RIKEN and provides GT strength distributions needed for astrophysical r-process simulations under stellar conditions, where phase factors differ from terrestrial ones (Langanke).
