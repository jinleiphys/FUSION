# Accessing proton number fluctuations in limited regions of phase space

> In heavy ion collisions particle distributions fluctuate from event to event. It is interesting to study local fluctuations of a specific particle specie, e.g. baryons, in the transverse plane. Fluctuations of the harmonic flow provide an integrated measure of such fluctuations. An alternative approach is to study proton number fluctuations measured in a relatively narrow azimuthal angle window. Due to the transverse flow, particles registered in a narrow azimuthal angle window are emitted predominantly from a region of the fireball located at the same azimuthal angle. Similarly, it is informative to measure the covariance of the number of protons emitted in two opposite windows of azimuthal angles. The main difficulty lies in distinguishing effects of local baryon density fluctuations in the transverse plane from global particle density and collective flow fluctuations. These global volume and flow fluctuations can be estimated using some reference particles, e.g. charged mesons. Reliable estimates of such fluctuations can be constructed both for the second factorial cumulant of the proton number in an azimuthal angle window and for the covariance between two such windows. The simultaneous measurement of both quantities provides a sensitive probe of local baryon number fluctuations superimposed on top of a fluctuating fireball background density.

# Accessing proton number fluctuations in limited regions of phase space

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring the second factorial cumulant of proton number in a narrow azimuthal angle window, together with the covariance of proton numbers in two opposite windows, can reveal local baryon density fluctuations in the transverse plane, provided that global volume and flow fluctuations are estimated using reference particles (charged mesons). The method works only if the correlation length of baryon fluctuations is much smaller than the transverse size of the fireball, and the excess of the proton cumulant over the volume fluctuation baseline is interpreted as evidence for short-range baryon fluctuations.

## Method

The author performs 2+1-dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the MUSIC code with shear viscosity η/s = 0.08, with initial entropy and baryon density distributions generated via a Monte-Carlo Glauber model. The initial baryon density includes an additional random factor Γ_i sampled from a gamma distribution with mean one and width σ_B, allowing controlled variation of local baryon fluctuations. For each parameter set, 600 initial conditions are generated and evolved, with final-state particles produced using the ISS code with oversampling by a factor 20000 to wash out nonflow correlations. The second factorial cumulant of proton number in an azimuthal window and the covariance between opposite windows are compared to estimates of volume fluctuation contributions derived from charged meson distributions using a Gaussian model for the joint emission volumes.

## Key numbers

- The simulations are performed for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 19.6 GeV in the 0–5% centrality class.
- The initial entropy distribution uses parameters α = 0.18 and σ_w = 0.5 fm.
- The strength of baryon density fluctuations is varied using σ_B values up to 0.5.
- The second factorial cumulant of proton number in an azimuthal window of width Δφ = π/4 shows a clear excess over the volume fluctuation estimate when σ_B = 0.5 (dotted line versus dash-dotted line in Fig. 3).
- The ratio of the second factorial cumulant to the volume fluctuation estimate increases with σ_B for protons with 0 < p_T < 3 GeV (solid line) and for protons with 0.8 GeV < p_T < 3 GeV (dashed line) in Fig. 5.
- The covariance of proton numbers in opposite azimuthal windows remains approximately constant as σ_B increases, while the second factorial cumulant grows, causing the dynamical correlation coefficient to decrease (Fig. 9).

## Context

This work extends the study of baryon number fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, which are expected to increase near the QCD critical point (Stephanov 2008). Previous measurements of proton cumulants in rapidity windows (STAR 2021) have shown no dependence on rapidity window width up to |Δy| < 0.5, motivating the need for transverse-plane measurements. The method builds on the idea of using narrow azimuthal windows to restrict the emission region (Neff 2024), and provides a practical way to separate local baryon fluctuations from global volume and flow fluctuations using reference particles. The approach enables future experimental searches for critical fluctuations in limited regions of the fireball, complementing harmonic flow fluctuation measurements.
