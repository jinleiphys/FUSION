# Least action description of spontaneous fission in fermium and nobelium nuclei based on the Gogny energy density functional

> The systematic of the spontaneous fission half-lives for the nuclei $^{242-262}$Fm and $^{250-260}$No is analyzed, within a least action scheme, with the parametrization D1M of the Gogny energy density functional. The properties of the dynamic (least action) fission paths are analyzed and compared to those of the static (minimal energy) ones. The constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation is used to compute deformed mean-field configurations, zero-point quantum corrections and collective inertias. It is shown that a cumbersome full variational search of the least action fission path, within the space of HFB states, might not be required if the relevant degrees of freedom are taken into account in the minimization of the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin action. The action is minimized in terms of pairing fluctuations that explore the pairing content of the HFB states along the fission paths of the considered nuclei. It is found that, for a given shape, the minimum of the action in fermium and nobelium nuclei corresponds to a value of the pairing fluctuations larger than the one associated with the minimal energy solution for the same shape. The reduction of the action, via larger pairing correlations, has a significant impact on the predicted spontaneous fission half-lives improving their comparison with the experiment by several orders of magnitude.

## Key claim

The authors show that minimizing the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin action with respect to pairing fluctuations (particle number fluctuation \(\langle \Delta \hat{N}^2 \rangle\)) rather than using the minimal-energy (static) path significantly reduces the computed spontaneous fission half-lives for fermium and nobelium isotopes, improving agreement with experiment by several orders of magnitude; however, this improvement depends on treating the pairing fluctuation as an explicit dynamical variable alongside the quadrupole deformation.

## Method

The authors used the constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approximation with the Gogny-D1M energy density functional to compute deformed mean-field configurations, zero-point quantum corrections, and collective inertias for nuclei \(^{242-262}\)Fm and \(^{250-260}\)No. For each quadrupole moment \(Q_{20}\) along the static fission path, they performed additional HFB calculations with constraints on the particle number fluctuation \(\langle \Delta \hat{N}^2 \rangle\), increasing it from the self-consistent value until the minimum of the WKB action was reached, using both the Adiabatic Time Dependent HFB (ATDHFB) and Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) collective inertias.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{252}\)No, the static GCM and ATDHFB schemes gave \(\log_{10} t_{SF} = 9.3878\) and \(\log_{10} t_{SF} = 11.6662\) (half-life in seconds), while the dynamic least action approach gave \(\log_{10} t_{SF} = 3.1819\) (GCM) and \(\log_{10} t_{SF} = 3.9838\) (ATDHFB), compared to the experimental value.
- For \(^{252}\)No with \(E_0 = 1.0\) MeV, the dynamic half-lives were \(\log_{10} t_{SF} = 1.4302\) s (GCM) and \(\log_{10} t_{SF} = 2.1485\) s (ATDHFB).
- For \(^{252}\)No at \(Q_{20} = 44\) b, the GCM action integrand minimum was \(3.7799 \times 10^{-3}\) \(\hbar\) at \(\langle \Delta \hat{N}^2 \rangle = 26\), compared to \(4.8757 \times 10^{-3}\) \(\hbar\) at the self-consistent value \(\langle \Delta \hat{N}^2 \rangle_{self} = 14\); the ATDHFB minimum was \(4.3781 \times 10^{-3}\) \(\hbar\) at \(\langle \Delta \hat{N}^2 \rangle = 27\) versus \(5.9091 \times 10^{-3}\) \(\hbar\) at \(\langle \Delta \hat{N}^2 \rangle_{self} = 14\).
- For \(^{252}\)No, the static path inner barrier height was 10.75 MeV, while the dynamic ATDHFB inner barrier was 18.09 MeV and the dynamic GCM inner barrier was 15.38 MeV; the outer barrier heights were 4.23 MeV (static), 5.53 MeV (ATDHFB), and 4.94 MeV (GCM).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous least action study of uranium isotopes (Rodriguez-Guzman and Robledo, 2017) to the super-heavy fermium and nobelium chains, where experimental spontaneous fission half-lives show a characteristic bell-shaped dependence on neutron number. The results contradict the traditional assumption that static (minimum energy) paths are sufficient for fission lifetime calculations, and instead support earlier suggestions by Moretto (1970s) that pairing fluctuations play a key role in fission dynamics. The approach enables systematic microscopic predictions for spontaneous fission half-lives in super-heavy nuclei beyond Oganesson, and the authors suggest extending the framework to time-dependent methods for fission fragment mass distributions.
