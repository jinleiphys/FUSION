# Field Theoretic Approach to Interacting Two Body Tunneling

> Two body tunneling problems are hard to treat analytically due to the incompatibility between tunneling and perturbation theory. The lack of classical solutions of the Euclidean Lagrangian of continuous systems further thwarts semi-classical expansions. To develop an analytic theory which provides insight on interacting two-particle tunneling, we use new results to derive the Bethe-Salpeter equation of a tunneling field theory with Yukawa coupling. We show that in the one plus one dimensional case a closed form solution in the instantaneous positive-energy regime is permitted. We then compute the scattering amplitude by perturbing on interparticle interaction and recover the Lippmann-Schwinger equation to confirm physical consistency and relevancy.

## Key claim

The paper derives a Bethe-Salpeter equation for two-body tunneling in a Yukawa-coupled field theory, showing that in 1+1 dimensions a closed-form solution exists under the instantaneous positive-energy (Salpeter) approximation, but only when the interparticle interaction is weak relative to the barrier coupling (|g/eaV₀| ≪ 1) or in the non-relativistic regime.

## Method

The authors start from a field Lagrangian with a tunneling field ψ coupled to a meson field φ via Yukawa coupling g, and a static barrier potential u(x) = aV₀δ(x₃). They use the resummed tunneling propagator from Zielinski (2024b) to construct the four-point correlation function by resumming all ladder diagrams, yielding a Bethe-Salpeter equation. After reducing to 1+1 dimensions, applying the instantaneous approximation, and imposing the positive-energy (Salpeter) condition, they obtain an integral equation solvable via generalized Wiener-Hopf techniques in Laplace space, with the small-meson-mass approximation μ' → 0 to invert the shift operator.

## Key numbers

- The ladder approximation is justified for |g/eaV₀| ≪ 1, or for large μ, or in the non-relativistic regime.
- In the Born approximation, the interaction strength ratio g/eaV₀ = O(10⁻³) and larger causes interaction to make low-momentum tunneling dominate.
- The meson propagator is D_φ(q) = i/(q² − μ² + iε), with μ the meson mass.
- The barrier coupling constant is eaV₀, appearing in the resummed propagator via the function f(p_μ) = √(p₀² − p₁² − p₂² − m²).
- The shift operator approximation requires μ' small, where μ' = √(μ² + iε), to recover the Coulomb interaction in the non-relativistic reduction.

## Context

This work extends the field-theoretic tunneling formalism developed by Zielinski (2024) for single-particle S-matrices to the two-body case, using the resummed tunneling propagator from Zielinski (2024b). The Bethe-Salpeter approach follows the one-boson-exchange treatment originally by Wick (1954) and Cutkosky (1954), with the key difference that the propagator Γ contains barrier-dependent factors. The instantaneous positive-energy reduction is a canonical approximation related to the Salpeter equation and quasipotential reductions such as Blankenbecler–Sugar (1966), Thompson (1970), and Gross (1969). The formalism enables analytic treatment of two-body tunneling problems that previously lacked analytical approaches beyond mean-field approximations, with applications to two-proton decay and other nuclear tunneling phenomena.
