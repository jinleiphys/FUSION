# Defining the Underlying-Event Activity in the Presence of Heavy-Flavour Processes in Proton-Proton Collisions at LHC Energies

> We present a systematic analysis of heavy-flavour production in the underlying event in connection to a leading hard process in pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 13 TeV, using the PYTHIA 8 Monte Carlo event generator. We compare results from events selected by triggering on the leading hadron, as well as those triggered with reconstructed jets. We show that the kinematics of heavy-flavour fragmentation complicates the characterisation of the underlying event, and the usual method which uses the leading charged final-state hadron as a trigger may wash away the connection between the leading process and the heavy-flavour particle created in association with that. Events triggered with light or heavy-flavour jets, however, retain this connection and bring more direct information on the underlying heavy-flavour production process, but may also import unwanted sensitivity to gluon radiation. The methods outlined in the current work provide means to verify model calculations for light and heavy-flavour production in the jet and the underlying event in great details.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the standard method of defining the underlying event by triggering on the leading charged final-state hadron can obscure the connection between the hard process and associated heavy-flavour production in pp collisions at 13 TeV. Using reconstructed jets as triggers (both light and heavy-flavour) preserves this connection but introduces sensitivity to gluon radiation, meaning the choice of trigger definition fundamentally alters the characterisation of the underlying-event activity.

## Method
The authors performed a systematic Monte Carlo study using PYTHIA 8 to simulate proton-proton collisions at a centre-of-mass energy of 13 TeV. They compared two event-selection approaches: events triggered by the leading charged hadron and events triggered by reconstructed jets, analysing the underlying-event activity in the presence of heavy-flavour (charm and beauty) production. The analysis examined how the kinematics of heavy-flavour fragmentation affects the measured underlying-event observables.

## Key numbers
- The analysis was performed at a centre-of-mass energy of sqrt(s) = 13 TeV using the PYTHIA 8 event generator.
- The study compared underlying-event activity between hadron-triggered and jet-triggered event selections for heavy-flavour production processes.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the underlying event in proton-proton collisions by specifically addressing the complications introduced by heavy-flavour production, which had not been systematically treated in earlier analyses. The findings enable more precise comparisons between Monte Carlo model calculations and experimental data from the LHC, particularly for separating heavy-flavour production in the jet from that in the underlying event. The methods outlined provide a framework for verifying model calculations of both light and heavy-flavour production in jet and underlying-event measurements.
