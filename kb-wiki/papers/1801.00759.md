# On the clustering properties of produced particles in high-energy $pp$ collisions

> Minijets provide useful information on parton interactions in the low transverse-momentum (low-$p_T$) region. Because minijets produce clusters, we study the clustering properties of produced particles in high-energy $pp$ collisions as a first step to identify minijets. We develop an algorithm to find clusters by using the k-means clustering method, in conjunction with a k-number (cluster number) selection principle in the space of pseudorapidity and azimuthal angles. We test the clustering algorithm using events generated by PYTHIA 8.1, for $pp$ collision at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV. We find that clustering of low-$p_T$ hadrons occurs in high multiplicity events. However similar clustering properties are also present for particles produced randomly in a finite pseudorapidity and azimuthal angle space. To distinguish the dynamics from random generations of events, it is necessary to examine the correlation between particles and between clusters. We find that the correlations between clusters may provide a useful tool to distinguish the underlying dynamics of the reaction mechanism.

## Key claim

The authors develop a k-means clustering algorithm to identify minijet clusters in high-energy proton-proton collisions, but caution that similar clustering properties appear for randomly generated particles in the same finite pseudorapidity-azimuthal angle space, meaning cluster correlations rather than cluster presence alone are needed to distinguish dynamical from random clustering.

## Method

The authors apply the k-means clustering method in the pseudorapidity (η) and azimuthal angle (φ) plane, supplemented by a k-number selection principle that chooses the cluster number K yielding the fewest data points outside circles of radius R=0.6 around cluster centers. They test this algorithm on minimum-bias events generated by PYTHIA 8.1 for pp collisions at √s=200 GeV, accepting primary charged particles with |η|≤1 and pT≥0.15 GeV/c, and compare the clustering properties to those of randomly distributed particles in the same phase space.

## Key numbers

- The cluster radius is set to R=0.6 in the (η,φ) plane, based on a Gaussian two-particle distribution with σφ≈0.5 and half-width at half-maximum of 0.6
- The Gaussian width parameter σφ0=0.5 and mass scale ma=1.1 GeV from Eq. (2) for the minijet structure
- PYTHIA 8.1 events are generated at √s=200 GeV with acceptance |η|≤1
- The pT threshold for particle inclusion is pT≥0.15 GeV/c
- The diffuseness parameter a=0.1 is used for the continuous outside-points counting function

## Context

This work extends the study of minijet production in the low-pT region, following evidence from Wong (2012, 2015) that hard-scattering processes dominate down to a few tenths of GeV/c, and from STAR Collaboration (2005-2009) two-hadron correlation measurements showing jetlike structures at √s=200 GeV. The algorithm is intended as a first step toward identifying multiple minijets and mini-dijets from multiple parton interactions, which would enable event-by-event studies of the initial dynamics in pp collisions and provide information on the initial conditions for nucleus-nucleus collisions.
