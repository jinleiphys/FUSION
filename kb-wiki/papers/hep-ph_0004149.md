# Charge asymmetry due to the soft pion at high energy and its effect on the sea quark

> Using the soft pion theorem in the inclusive reactions, we estimate the soft pion contribution to the structure functions $F_2$ and $g_1$ in the nucleon, and show that it is an indispensable part in these structure functions in the small $x$ region. This contribution is asymmetric for the soft $π^+$ and $π^-$ due to pole terms in the soft pion limit, hence it is a remnant of the spontaneous chiral symmetry breakings. Then we show that the modified strange sea quark where the soft pion contribution is added to the distribution determined by the ansatz (strange-sea)=(up-sea + down-sea)/4 satisfies the mean charge sum rule for the sea quarks.

# Charge asymmetry due to the soft pion at high energy and its effect on the sea quark

## Key claim

The soft pion contribution to nucleon structure functions is an indispensable part in the small x region, and adding this contribution to the strange sea quark distribution obtained from the ansatz (strange-sea) = (up-sea + down-sea)/4 satisfies the mean charge sum rule for the sea quarks. The charge asymmetry of the soft pion contribution is a remnant of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, not a violation of charge symmetry.

## Method

The author applies the soft pion theorem to inclusive current-hadron reactions at high energy, using the PCAC relation and light-cone current algebra at an initial Q² = 4 GeV². The hadronic tensor in the soft pion limit is classified into terms corresponding to pion emission from the initial nucleon, final nucleon, and null-plane commutator terms, with contributions from graphs involving the nucleon pole and axial-vector current matrix elements. The structure functions F₂ and g₁ are related to the soft pion production cross sections, and the phase space for soft pions is constrained by experimental conditions on transverse momentum (|k⊥| ≤ b mπ with b near 1) and Feynman scaling variable (|xF| ≤ c with c near 0.1), using nucleon multiplicity parameterized as ⟨n⟩ = a logₑ s + 1 with a = 0.2.

## Key numbers

- The soft pion contribution to the Gottfried sum is found to be −0.04 to −0.02.
- The theoretical value for the charge asymmetry F⁺ − F⁻ is roughly 0.15 to 0.18 with a weak x_B dependence, compared to the experimental value 0.28 ± 0.05 in the region 0 < x_F < 0.1 from the Harvard-Cornell group.
- The numerical integration of the mean charge sum rule left-hand side using the ansatz (strange-sea) = (up-sea + down-sea)/4 gives 0.26 from x = 1×10⁻³ to 1 and 0.60 from x = 1×10⁻⁴ to 1; adding the soft pion contribution changes these values to 0.18 from x = 1×10⁻³ to 1 and 0.27 from x = 1×10⁻⁴ to 1.
- The soft pion contribution to the structure function g₁ becomes large below x = 1×10⁻³, but the contribution to the Bjorken sum rule and Ellis-Jaffe sum rule from above x = 1×10⁻⁴ is negligible small.

## Context

This work extends the soft pion theorem approach of Sakai (1970s) and the author's previous work (Koretune 1999) on the Gottfried sum to the strange sea quark distribution and spin-dependent structure functions. It addresses the discrepancy between the mean charge sum rule for sea quarks (Koretune 1998) and standard parameterizations such as CTEQ4M, which fail to satisfy this sum rule. The modified strange sea quark distribution proposed here may resolve the small charge symmetry violation effects reported by the Adelaide group (1998) and provides a dynamical mechanism for the flavor asymmetry at medium and high energy that mesonic cloud models (reviewed in 1990s) cannot cover.
