# N$^{\mathbf{3}}$LL + $\mathcal{O}(α_s^2)$ predictions of lepton-jet azimuthal angular distribution in deep-inelastic scattering

> We present an analysis of lepton-jet azimuthal decorrelation in deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (N$^{3}$LL) accuracy, combined with fixed-order corrections at $\mathcal{O}(α_s^2)$. In this study, jets are defined in the lab frame using the anti-$k_T$ clustering algorithm and the winner-take-all recombination scheme. The N$^{3}$LL resummation results are derived from the transverse-momentum dependent factorization formula within the soft-collinear effective theory, while the $\mathcal{O}(α_s^2)$ fixed-order matching distribution is calculated using the {\tt NLOJET++} event generator. The azimuthal decorrelation between the jet and electron serves as a critical probe of the three-dimensional structure of the nucleon. Our numerical predictions provide a robust framework for precision studies of QCD and the nucleon's internal structure through jet observables in DIS. These results are particularly significant for analyses involving jets in HERA data and the forthcoming electron-ion collider experiments.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first predictions of lepton-jet azimuthal angular decorrelation in deep-inelastic scattering at N\(^{3}\)LL + \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)\) accuracy, but the two-loop constant terms in the jet function required for this accuracy were determined numerically using external event generators (Event2 and NLOJET++) rather than from an analytic calculation.

## Method

The authors derived the N\(^{3}\)LL resummation formula from a transverse-momentum dependent (TMD) factorization theorem within soft-collinear effective theory (SCET), using jets defined in the lab frame with the anti-\(k_T\) clustering algorithm and the winner-take-all (WTA) recombination scheme. The \(\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)\) fixed-order matching distribution was computed using the NLOJET++ event generator, and the two-loop constant terms in the jet function were extracted numerically by comparing NLOJET++ results for back-to-back dijet production in \(e^+e^-\) collisions with the singular distribution from the fixed-order expansion of the resummation formula. The matched cross section was obtained using an additive matching scheme with a transition function to switch from resummed to fixed-order predictions at large azimuthal angles.

## Key numbers

* EIC kinematics: \(E_e = 18\) GeV, \(E_p = 275\) GeV, \(p_T > 15\) GeV, \(|y_J| < 1\)
* HERA kinematics: \(E_e = 27.5\) GeV, \(E_p = 920\) GeV, \(p_T > 10\) GeV, \(Q^2 > 150\) GeV\(^2\), \(-1 < y_J < 2.5\)
* Jet radius \(R = 1\) for the anti-\(k_T\) algorithm with WTA recombination
* Transition function parameter \(r = 0.16\) for EIC and \(r = 0.18\) for HERA
* Non-perturbative Sudakov parameters: \(g_1 = 0.106\) GeV\(^2\), \(g_2 = 0.84\), \(Q_0^2 = 2.4\) GeV\(^2\)
* \(b_{\rm max} = 1.5\) GeV\(^{-1}\) for the \(b_*\)-prescription
* Matching corrections exceed 20% for \(\delta\phi \geq 0.3\) at EIC, and are around 10% for \(\delta\phi < 0.4\) at HERA

## Context

This work extends the NNLL resummation of lepton-jet azimuthal angular distributions in the lab frame previously performed by Fang et al. (2023) for both \(ep\) and \(eA\) collisions, and complements N\(^{3}\)LL resummation studies of the transverse momentum imbalance in the Breit frame by Gutierrez-Reyes et al. (2018, 2019). The predictions provide a robust framework for precision QCD studies and three-dimensional nucleon imaging using jet observables, particularly relevant for analyzing HERA data from the H1 (2021) and ZEUS (2024) collaborations, and for the forthcoming Electron-Ion Collider experiments.
