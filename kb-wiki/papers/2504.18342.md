# Probing Quantum Phenomena through Photoproduction in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Photoproduction in ultra-peripheral relativistic heavy-ion collisions displays many unique features, often involving quantum mechanical coherence and two-source interference between photon emission from the two ions. We review the recent experimental results from RHIC and the LHC and theoretical studies of coherent vector meson photoproduction, emphasizing the quantum mechanical aspects of the interactions and the entanglement between the final state particles. These studies enrich our understanding of non-local realism, underscore the critical role of the polarization of the photon source, quantum interference and nuclear effect on the gluon distribution. It paves a way for quantitatively probing the quantum nature of these high-energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

This review argues that coherent vector meson photoproduction in ultra-peripheral relativistic heavy-ion collisions provides a unique femto-scale environment for probing quantum mechanical phenomena, including double-slit interference and entanglement-enabled intensity interferometry (E²I²), but the authors hedge that these processes have "several distinct differences" from traditional quantum optics experiments, particularly because they involve distinguishable particles (π⁺ and π⁻) from two sources of ρ⁰ mesons that decay at a distance.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental results from RHIC and the LHC alongside theoretical studies of coherent vector meson photoproduction, focusing on the quantum mechanical aspects of the interactions and the entanglement between final state particles. The authors compare photoproduction in heavy-ion collisions to classical double-slit experiments, Hanbury-Brown and Twiss (HBT) intensity interferometry, and the recently proposed E²I² phenomenon, emphasizing the role of photon polarization, quantum interference, and nuclear effects on gluon distributions. The review synthesizes worldwide experimental data on vector meson photoproduction cross sections and t-distribution slopes as functions of the photon-proton center-of-mass energy W_γp.

## Key numbers

- Maximum photon energy ω_max for gold nuclei at RHIC can reach 3 GeV, while for lead nuclei at LHC it can reach 80 GeV (lab frame)
- For light vector mesons (ρ, ω, φ), cross sections increase slowly with energy roughly as W_γp^0.22, attributed to photon-Pomeron reactions
- For ρ⁰ and ω mesons, an additional component at lower energies exhibits behavior approximately W_γp^-1.2 for ρ⁰ and W_γp^-2.0 for ω, due to photon-Reggeon interactions
- For J/ψ photoproduction, the cross section roughly follows W_γp^(0.7-0.8)
- Nuclear radius scaling of R = (1.12 ± 0.02)A^(1/3) was extracted from DESY data, with R_Au = 6.45 ± 0.27 fm and R_U = 6.90 ± 0.14 fm; a Cornell experiment obtained R_Au = 6.74 ± 0.06 fm
- The fine-structure constant α ≈ 1/137 appears in the photon-vector meson coupling coefficient C_V ~ √α

## Context

This review extends the line of work on quantum foundations in high-energy nuclear physics, building on the STAR Collaboration (2022) and ALICE Collaboration (2024) observations of spin interference patterns from ρ⁰ photoproduction, which the authors describe as "the first instances of Fermi-scale single-particle double-slit interference experiments." The work connects to earlier photoproduction measurements at DESY (Alvensleben, 1970) and Cornell that extracted nuclear radii, and to the theoretical framework of E²I² proposed by Cotler (2021). The review enables future quantitative probing of quantum nature in high-energy nuclear collisions and suggests applications for nuclear tomography through entanglement-enhanced intensity interferometry.
