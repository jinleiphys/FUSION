# Nuclear matrix elements of neutrinoless double beta decay with improved short-range correlations

> Nuclear matrix elements of the neutrinoless double beta decays of 96Zr, 100Mo, 116Cd, 128Te, 130Te and 136Xe are calculated for the light-neutrino exchange mechanism by using the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) with a realistic nucleon-nucleon force. The g_pp parameter of the pnQRPA is fixed by the data on the two-neutrino double beta decays and single beta decays. The finite size of a nucleon, the higher-order terms of nucleonic weak currents, and the nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations (s.r.c) are taken into account. The s.r.c. are computed by the traditional Jastrow method and by the more advanced unitary correlation operator method (UCOM). Comparison of the results obtained by the two methods is carried out. The UCOM computed matrix elements turn out to be considerably larger than the Jastrow computed ones. This result is important for the assessment of the neutrino-mass sensitivity of the present and future double beta experiments.

# Nuclear matrix elements of neutrinoless double beta decay with improved short-range correlations

## Key claim

The unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) for short-range correlations yields 0νββ nuclear matrix elements that are considerably larger than those obtained with the traditional Jastrow method, because the Jastrow procedure overestimates the reduction by cutting out the r ≤ 1 fm part of the relative two-particle wave function. The UCOM reduces matrix element magnitudes by only 4%–16%, whereas Jastrow reduces them by 25%–40%.

## Method

The authors used the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) with a realistic Bonn-A G-matrix nucleon-nucleon force in single-particle model spaces that included orbitals up to 0h11/2 and 0h9/2 for 96Zr and 100Mo, and up to 2p-1f-0h for the heavier nuclei. The g_pp parameter was fixed by experimental two-neutrino double beta decay matrix elements from Rodin et al. (2006), with cross-checks from single beta decay data where available, and short-range correlations were implemented via both the Jastrow function (with parameters a=1.1 fm², b=0.68 fm²) and the unitary correlation operator method using Bonn-A parameters from Neff and Feldmeier (2003).

## Key numbers

- UCOM reduces the magnitude of M^(0ν) by 4%–16% compared to the bare matrix element, while Jastrow reduces it by 25%–40%
- For 96Zr: M^(0ν) = −3.521 (UCOM) vs −2.454 (Jastrow) at g_pp = 1.085 with g_A = 1.25
- For 100Mo: M^(0ν) = −4.113 (UCOM) vs −2.914 (Jastrow) at g_pp = 1.08 with g_A = 1.25
- For 136Xe: M^(0ν) = −3.317 (UCOM) vs −2.537 (Jastrow) at g_pp = 0.74 with g_A = 1.25
- Predicted half-life for 100Mo: 3.8 × 10²³ yr/(⟨m_ν⟩ [eV])² at g_pp = 1.09, g_A = 1.25
- Predicted half-life for 130Te: 3.5 × 10²³ yr/(⟨m_ν⟩ [eV])² at g_pp = 0.90, g_A = 1.25
- Predicted half-life for 136Xe: 1.1 × 10²⁴ yr/(⟨m_ν⟩ [eV])² at g_pp = 0.74, g_A = 1.00
- The 2νββ matrix element for 96Zr is 0.026–0.112, yielding g_pp = 1.06–1.11

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous UCOM-based calculations for ⁴⁸Ca, ⁷⁶Ge, and ⁸²Se (Kortelainen and Suhonen, 2007) to six additional nuclei of experimental interest. The results disagree with the earlier calculations of Rodin et al. (2006) and Simkovic et al. (1999), but the authors note that Rodin et al. (2007) subsequently corrected a coding error, bringing their Jastrow-corrected results into agreement. The larger UCOM-corrected matrix elements imply shorter predicted 0νββ half-lives for a given effective neutrino mass, which directly affects sensitivity estimates for experiments such as NEMO 3, CUORICINO, and future planned detectors.
