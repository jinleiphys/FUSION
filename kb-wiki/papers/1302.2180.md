# Quarkonia in the Quark Gluon Plasma

> In this paper we review recent progress towards understanding the nature of quarkonia in the quark-gluon plasma. We review the theory necessary to understand the melting of bound states due to color-screening, including lattice results for the heavy quark potential, lattice results on the correlation functions related to the relevant spectral functions, and the emergence of a complex-valued potential in high-temperature quantum chromodynamics. We close with a brief survey of phenomenological models of quarkonium suppression in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

This review argues that the simple picture of quarkonium melting in the quark-gluon plasma due solely to color screening is insufficient; the actual situation involves additional complications including Landau damping, a poorly understood non-perturbative intermediate temperature region between 160 and 300 MeV, and cold nuclear matter effects that must be disentangled from hot-medium effects.

## Method

The authors review theoretical methods for studying quarkonia in the QGP, including lattice QCD calculations of the heavy quark potential, Polyakov loops, and correlation functions, as well as effective field theory derivations of a complex-valued heavy quark potential. They also survey phenomenological models of quarkonium suppression in relativistic heavy ion collisions, covering both static spectral functions and dynamical evolution in an expanding medium.

## Key numbers

- The chiral symmetry restoration temperature (referred to as the QCD critical temperature T_c) is 154(9) MeV from lattice studies.
- The pure gauge SU(N) deconfinement transition temperature is 292(2) MeV, obtained from T/√σ = 0.629(3) assuming √σ = 465 MeV.
- At temperatures of approximately 300 MeV and above, the entropy density from lattice studies shows an approximately 20% deviation from the entropy density of a non-interacting gas of quarks and gluons.
- The leading order Debye mass is m_D = √(N/3) g T √(1 + N_f/(2N)), where N_f is the number of light quark flavors.

## Context

This review extends and synthesizes work following the seminal proposal by Matsui and Satz (1986) that quarkonium melting signals deconfinement and QGP production in heavy ion collisions. It addresses controversies in lattice QCD results for spectral functions and dissociation temperatures, and discusses how potential models derived from QCD (rather than phenomenological constructions) have been developed in recent years. The review enables a more nuanced interpretation of experimental data from SPS, RHIC, and LHC on charmonium and bottomonium suppression, particularly for the J/ψ and Υ ground states.
