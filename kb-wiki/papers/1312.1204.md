# Frequency dependence of the Chiral Vortical Effect

> We study the frequency dependence of all the chiral vortical and magnetic conductivities for a relativistic gas of free chiral fermions and for a strongly coupled conformal field theory with holographic dual in four dimensions. Both systems have gauge and gravitational anomalies, and we compute their contribution to the conductivities. The chiral vortical conductivities and the chiral magnetic conductivity in the energy current show a frequency dependence in the form of a delta centered at zero frequency. This highly discontinuous behavior is a natural consequence of the Ward identities that include the energy momentum tensor. We discuss the physical interpretation of this result and its possible implications for the quark gluon plasma as created in heavy ion collisions. In the Appendix we discuss why the chiral magnetic effect seems to vanish in the consistent current for a particular implementation of the axial chemical potential.

## Key claim

The chiral vortical conductivities and the chiral magnetic conductivity in the energy current exhibit a highly discontinuous frequency dependence in the form of a delta function centered at zero frequency, meaning they are nonzero only at exactly zero frequency and vanish at any finite frequency when momentum is taken to zero, which is a natural consequence of the Ward identities that include the energy-momentum tensor.

## Method

The authors computed the frequency and momentum dependence of all chiral vortical and magnetic conductivities (electric, axial, and energy currents) using Kubo formulas for two systems: a relativistic gas of free chiral fermions at weak coupling (via one-loop Matsubara frequency sums) and a strongly coupled conformal field theory with a holographic dual in four dimensions (via numerical evaluation of the holographic model). Both systems incorporate gauge and mixed gauge-gravitational anomalies, and the conductivities are extracted from retarded correlators of the form \(\langle J_A^i J_B^j \rangle\) with appropriate tensor structures.

## Key numbers

- The DC chiral vortical conductivities at zero frequency and momentum are: \(\sigma_{e,(0)}^\cV = \frac{1}{2\pi^2} \mu\mu_5\), \(\sigma_{5,(0)}^\cV = \frac{1}{4\pi^2} \left( \mu^2 + \mu_5^2 + \frac{\pi^2}{3} T^2 \right)\), and \(\sigma_{\epsilon,(0)}^\cV = \frac{\mu_5}{6\pi^2} \left( 3\mu^2 + \mu_5^2 + \pi^2 T^2 \right)\).
- The DC chiral magnetic conductivities at zero frequency and momentum are: \(\sigma_{e,(0)}^\cB = \frac{1}{2\pi^2} \mu_5\), \(\sigma_{5,(0)}^\cB = \frac{1}{2\pi^2} \mu\), and \(\sigma_{\epsilon,(0)}^\cB = \frac{1}{2\pi^2} \mu\mu_5\).
- At high temperature (\(T/\mu_s \gg 1\)), the imaginary part of the chiral magnetic conductivity \(\Im[\sigma_e^\cB(\omega,0)]\) has a single peak at \(\omega \simeq 5.406 \, T\).
- The pressure of the free fermion gas is \(P = \frac{1}{12\pi^2} \left[ \mu^4 + 6\mu^2\mu_5^2 + \mu_5^4 + 2\pi^2 T^2 (\mu^2 + \mu_5^2) + \frac{7}{15}\pi^4 T^4 \right]\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) by Kharzeev (2009) and the chiral vortical effect (CVE) by Landsteiner (2011) and Amado (2011), which computed frequency dependence only for pure gauge anomalies or at zero frequency. The inclusion of mixed gauge-gravitational anomalies is crucial because the gravitational anomaly contribution can be a leading term in heavy ion collisions where temperature is much higher than chemical potential. The results enable more realistic modeling of the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy ion collisions, where finite lifetime and size require knowledge of the full frequency and momentum dependence of anomalous transport.
