# The quasi-normal modes of relativistic Fokker-Planck kinetic theory

> Employing the well-known unitary equivalence between Fokker-Planck operators and Schrödinger Hamiltonians, we compute the quasi-normal-mode spectrum of ultrarelativistic kinetic theories with momentum-space diffusion. We show that the collision operator reduces to a Dirac-delta Schrödinger problem in one spatial dimension, and to a Coulomb Schrödinger operator with hydrogenic spectrum in three dimensions. Finite spatial wavenumber appears as a perturbation of the associated quantum potential. The hydrodynamic mode is found to obey exact Fick-type diffusion at all real wavenumbers, whereas relativistic kinematics generically produces a continuous ballistic band in the non-hydrodynamic sector, a feature absent in the Newtonian regime.

## Key claim

The quasi-normal-mode spectrum of ultrarelativistic Fokker-Planck kinetic theory can be computed exactly by exploiting the unitary equivalence between the Fokker-Planck collision operator and a Schrodinger Hamiltonian, but the abstract's claim of a full computation is qualified by the fact that the spectrum depends qualitatively on the number of spatial dimensions: in one dimension the collision operator maps to a Dirac-delta Schrodinger problem, while in three dimensions it maps to a Coulomb Schrodinger operator with a hydrogenic spectrum, and finite spatial wavenumber appears only as a perturbation of the associated quantum potential.

## Method

The author linearizes the Boltzmann equation around equilibrium for a gas of (quasi)particles undergoing Fokker-Planck diffusion in momentum space, with collision operator I Phi = -nu partial_j (partial^j Phi + beta v^j Phi). By seeking plane-wave solutions Phi = Phi(p) exp(i k x - i omega t) and applying the transformation Phi = sqrt((2 pi)^D f_eq) psi, the eigenvalue problem is recast as a time-independent Schrodinger equation with effective potential V_chi(p) = beta^2 v^j v_j / 8 - (beta/4) partial_j v^j + chi v^1, where chi = i k/(2 nu) and E = i omega/(2 nu). The spectrum is then analyzed separately for massless particles in one and three spatial dimensions by solving the resulting Schrodinger problem with the appropriate boundary and matching conditions.

## Key numbers

* For nonrelativistic particles in D dimensions, the quasi-normal spectrum is discrete: i omega_N = (nu beta / m) N + k^2 / (beta^2 nu), with N in natural numbers, and the hydrodynamic mode has diffusion coefficient mathfrak{D} = (beta^2 nu)^{-1}.
* In one-dimensional ultrarelativistic theory, the hydrodynamic mode obeys omega = -i k^2 / (beta^2 nu), and the nonhydrodynamic continuum for real k is given by omega in k - i [beta^2 nu / 4, +infinity) union -k - i [beta^2 nu / 4, +infinity).
* For imaginary wavenumber k in iR in one dimension, the hydrodynamic mode exists only for |ik| < beta^2 nu / 2, and the nonhydrodynamic continuum is bounded below by beta^2 nu / 4 - |ik|.

## Context

This work extends the spectral analysis of relativistic kinetic theories beyond the Relaxation-Time Approximation (RTA) studied by Romatschke (2015), Bajec (2024), and Brants (2024), and beyond weakly coupled lambda phi^4 theory studied by Moore (2018) and Gavassino (2024). The author places ultrarelativistic Fokker-Planck kinetics on the same conceptual footing as the well-known harmonic-oscillator structure of nonrelativistic Ornstein-Uhlenbeck dynamics (Risken, Pavliotis). The results enable future computation of quasi-normal modes for Fokker-Planck models relevant to early-time gluon dynamics in heavy-ion collisions (Mueller 1999, Blaizot 2013).
