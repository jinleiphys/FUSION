# Remarks on N N -> N N pi beyond leading order

> In recent years a two-scale expansion was established to study reactions of the type N N -> N N pi within chiral perturbation theory. Then the diagrams of some subclasses that are invariant under the choice of the pion field no longer appear at the same chiral order. In this letter we show that the proposed expansion still leads to well defined results. We also discuss the appropriate choice of the heavy baryon propagator.

## Key claim

The two-scale expansion scheme for \(NN\to NN\pi\) in chiral perturbation theory, where the expansion parameter is \(\chi_{\rm prod}=\sqrt{m_\pi/M}\), is consistent with pion-field independence to all orders, despite the fact that diagrams invariant under the choice of the pion field no longer appear at the same chiral order in this scheme.

## Method

The authors evaluated the \(\alpha\)-dependent (pion-field dependent) contributions to the one-loop diagrams (a) through (d) of Fig. 1 for the reaction \(NN\to NN\pi\) within heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT), using the two-scale power counting where loop momenta scale as \(p_{\rm thr}=\sqrt{M m_\pi}\) or \(m_\pi\) depending on the diagram. They explicitly computed the sum of diagrams (a) and (b) at NLO and NNLO, and compared the order at which the \(\alpha\)-dependent terms appear to the order at which diagrams (c) and (d) start to contribute, using the appropriate heavy baryon propagator with the on-shell condition \(p_0 = \vec{p}^2/(2M)\).

## Key numbers

* The two-scale expansion parameter is \(\chi_{\rm prod} = \sqrt{m_\pi/M}\), where \(m_\pi\) is the pion mass and \(M\) the nucleon mass; the threshold momentum for pion production is \(p_{\rm thr} = \sqrt{M m_\pi}\).
* Leading order (LO) scales as \(\mathcal{O}(\chi_{\rm prod}^1)\) and subleading orders N\(^n\)LO scale as \(\mathcal{O}(\chi_{\rm prod}^{n+1})\).
* The \(\alpha\)-dependent terms from the sum of diagrams (a) and (b) at NLO are suppressed by a factor \((m_\pi/p_{\rm thr})^3 = \chi_{\rm prod}^3\) relative to the leading loops, meaning they start to contribute only at N\(^4\)LO.
* For all standard gauge choices, the parameter \(\alpha\) satisfies \(|\alpha| \le 1/4\).

## Context

This work extends the two-scale expansion scheme developed by Hanhart, Wirzba, and collaborators (2004-2006) for pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions, which was introduced to handle the large momentum transfer typical of meson production. It addresses a potential inconsistency raised by the observation in Novel (2005) that the sum of certain loop diagrams is independent of the pion field choice, while in the two-scale scheme those diagrams contribute at different chiral orders (NLO vs N\(^4\)LO). The authors show the scheme remains consistent, and their analysis of the heavy baryon propagator clarifies that the on-shell residual energy of the external nucleon cancels against the kinetic recoil term, resolving a subtlety in the application of HBChPT rules. This enables further systematic calculations of \(NN\to NN\pi\) to higher orders in the two-scale expansion.
