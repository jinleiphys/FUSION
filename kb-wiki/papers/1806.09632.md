# Universal magnetoresponse in QCD and $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM

> Using recent lattice data on the thermodynamics of QCD in the presence of a background magnetic field, we show that the ratio of transverse to longitudinal pressure exhibits, to good accuracy, a simple scaling behavior over a wide range of temperature and magnetic field, essentially depending only on the ratio $T/\sqrt B$. We compare this QCD response to the corresponding magnetoresponse in maximally supersymmetric Yang Mills theory. Given suitable calibrations defining the comparison, we find excellent agreement. This may be viewed as a further test of the applicability of holographic models for hot QCD.

## Key claim

The ratio of transverse to longitudinal pressure in QCD with a background magnetic field exhibits near-universal scaling behavior depending only on the ratio \(T/\sqrt{B}\), and this QCD magnetoresponse agrees with that of \(\mathcal{N}=4\) supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory to within lattice errors when a suitable charge rescaling factor is chosen, provided the comparison is made at a common renormalization point along a specific valley in parameter space.

## Method

The authors used lattice QCD data from Bali et al. (2014) for 2+1 flavor QCD with physical quark masses at various temperatures and magnetic field strengths, computing the transverse-to-longitudinal pressure ratio \(p_T/p_L\) at a renormalization point \(\mu = \sqrt{c_T T^2 + c_B |B|}\). They compared this ratio to the corresponding ratio in \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM theory, computed holographically via magnetic black brane solutions of 5D Einstein-Maxwell theory, after rescaling the SYM magnetic field by a factor \(\xi\) to account for differing field content.

## Key numbers

- The QCD pressure ratio \(p_T/p_L\) collapses onto a single curve when plotted as a function of \(T/\sqrt{B}\) over a wide range of temperatures and magnetic fields, with deviations possibly present only at the lowest temperatures and highest magnetic fields where error bars grow.
- The deviation from universality measure \(D\) is minimized at \((c_T, c_\Lambda) = (0.70, 0.46)\) for the renormalization point parameterization, with \(D \lesssim 1\) indicating universality within lattice errors.
- For vanishing \(c_\Lambda\), the minimum of \(D\) defines a valley along \(c_B = 0.087 - 0.084\,c_T\) in the renormalization point parameter space.
- The high-temperature matching procedure gives a charge rescaling factor \(\xi = \sqrt{19/3} \approx 2.5\) for comparing QCD and SYM.
- The comparison uses lattice QCD data only for temperatures above 150 MeV, excluding the hadronic phase.

## Context

This work extends the study of magnetoresponse in QCD using lattice data from Bali et al. (2013, 2014) and compares it to holographic calculations for \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM by D'Hoker and Kraus (2009) and Fuini et al. (2015). The near-universal scaling of the pressure anisotropy ratio in QCD, despite QCD being neither conformal nor supersymmetric, provides further evidence for the applicability of holographic models to hot QCD dynamics. The results enable future investigations of how other non-conformal deformations affect the robustness of holographic predictions for strongly coupled plasmas.
