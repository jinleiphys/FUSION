# Real time evolution of non-Gaussian cumulants in the QCD critical regime

> We derive a coupled set of equations that describe the non-equilibrium evolution of cumulants of critical fluctuations for space-time trajectories on the cross-over side of the QCD phase diagram. In particular, novel expressions are obtained for the non-equilibrium evolution of non-Gaussian Skewness and Kurtosis cumulants. Utilizing a simple model of the space-time evolution of a heavy- ion collision, we demonstrate that, depending on the relaxation rate of critical fluctuations, Skewness and Kurtosis can differ significantly in magnitude as well as in sign from equilibrium expectations. Memory effects are important and shown to persist even for trajectories that skirt the edge of the critical regime. We use phenomenologically motivated parameterizations of freeze-out curves, and of the beam energy dependence of the net baryon chemical potential, to explore the implications of our model study for the critical point search in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper derives a coupled set of first-order differential equations for the non-equilibrium evolution of the first four cumulants (mean, variance, skewness, kurtosis) of critical fluctuations in the QCD critical regime, applicable specifically to space-time trajectories on the crossover side of the phase diagram. The authors demonstrate that memory effects cause skewness and kurtosis to differ significantly in magnitude and sign from equilibrium expectations, even for trajectories that do not pass very close to the critical point.

## Method

The authors start from the Fokker-Planck equation describing the relaxation of the probability distribution of the zero-momentum mode of the critical field (the chiral order parameter) near the QCD critical point, which lies in the 3-D Ising universality class (model H). They expand the effective action to fourth order in the field and derive evolution equations for the cumulants by taking moments of the Fokker-Planck equation, keeping only leading-order terms in the small parameter ε = √(ξ³/V), where ξ is the correlation length and V the volume. The resulting closed system of coupled first-order differential equations is then solved numerically using a simple model of the space-time evolution of a heavy-ion collision, with phenomenologically motivated freeze-out curves and beam energy dependence of the net baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The variance (Gaussian cumulant) scales as ξ², the skewness scales as ξ^(9/2), and the kurtosis scales as ξ⁷ in equilibrium.
- The effective relaxation time τ_eff scales as ξ^z, where z is the dynamic scaling exponent characteristic of model H.
- The small expansion parameter ε = √(ξ³/V) satisfies ε ≪ 1 in the scaling regime where L_micr ≪ ξ ≪ L.
- The equilibrium kurtosis is negative when the critical point is approached from the crossover side and positive when approached from the first-order transition side.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of critical dynamics off-equilibrium, which focused primarily on Gaussian fluctuations, such as the quasi-stationary dynamics studied by Berdnikov and Rajagopal (1999) using a model equation for the relaxation of the correlation length, and hydrodynamic approaches by Nonaka and Asakawa (2004). The paper addresses the gap in knowledge of non-Gaussian fluctuations by providing the first systematic treatment of the real-time evolution of skewness and kurtosis cumulants near the QCD critical point. The results enable more realistic interpretations of the Beam Energy Scan (BES) at RHIC, suggesting that non-equilibrium memory effects must be accounted for when comparing experimental fluctuation measurements to equilibrium lattice QCD calculations.
