# Baryon electric dipole moments from strong CP violation

> The electric dipole form factors and moments of the ground state baryons are calculated in chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order. We show that the baryon electric dipole form factors at this order depend only on two combinations of low-energy constants. We also derive various relations that are free of unknown low-energy constants. We use recent lattice QCD data to calculate all baryon EDMs. In particular, we find d_n = -2.9\pm 0.9 and d_p = 1.1\pm 1.1 in units of 10^{-16} e θ_0 cm. Finite volume corrections to the moments are also worked out. We show that for a precision extraction from lattice QCD data, the next-to-leading order terms have to be accounted for.

## Key claim

The baryon electric dipole form factors at next-to-leading order in chiral perturbation theory depend on only two combinations of low-energy constants, and the formally suppressed next-to-leading order corrections are sizeable and even dominate for some baryons due to large cancellations at leading order.

## Method

The authors constructed the most general U(3)_L × U(3)_R chiral effective Lagrangian for mesons and baryons including the CP-violating θ-term, performed vacuum alignment to account for the shifted vacuum when θ₀ is non-zero, and calculated the electric dipole form factors of all ground-state octet baryons at next-to-leading order. They evaluated one-loop diagrams with meson-baryon intermediate states using infrared regularization, renormalized the divergences into the low-energy constants w₁₃' and w₁₄', and used recent lattice QCD data at M_π = 530 MeV to determine the two unknown combinations of low-energy constants.

## Key numbers

- Neutron EDM at physical pion mass: d_n = -2.9 ± 0.9 in units of 10⁻¹⁶ e θ₀ cm
- Proton EDM at physical pion mass: d_p = 1.1 ± 1.1 in units of 10⁻¹⁶ e θ₀ cm
- Determined low-energy constant combination w_a(1 GeV) = (-0.01 ± 0.02) GeV⁻¹ from lattice neutron EDM data at M_π = 530 MeV
- Determined low-energy constant combination w_b(1 GeV) = (-0.40 ± 0.05) GeV⁻¹ from lattice proton EDM data at M_π = 530 MeV
- Loop contributions at physical pion mass: d_n^loop = -3.1 ± 0.8, d_p^loop = 5.6 ± 1.0 - 6.1(β·GeV), d_Λ^loop = -2.6 ± 0.4, d_Σ⁰^loop = 0.8 ± 0.4, d_Ξ⁰^loop = -3.6 ± 0.8 (all in 10⁻¹⁶ e θ₀ cm)
- Used D = 0.804, F = 0.463, b_D = 0.068 GeV⁻¹, b_F = -0.209 GeV⁻¹ from fitting baryon mass differences at order δ²
- Used V₀⁽²⁾ = -5 × 10⁻⁴ GeV⁴ and V₃⁽¹⁾ = 3.5 × 10⁻⁴ GeV² from analysis of η-η' mixing

## Context

This work extends the studies of Ottnad et al. (2009) from the nucleon to the full baryon octet and makes contact with recent lattice QCD studies, building on earlier chiral perturbation theory calculations by Borasoy (2000) for the neutron and Λ EDMs and by Hockings and van Kolck (2005) for the nucleon electric dipole form factor at leading one-loop accuracy. The results enable precision extraction of baryon EDMs from lattice QCD data by providing the correct next-to-leading order extrapolation formulas including finite volume corrections, and allow constraints on the QCD vacuum angle θ₀ when combined with experimental limits such as the neutron EDM bound from Baker (2006).
