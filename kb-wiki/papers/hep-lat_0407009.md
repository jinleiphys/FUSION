# A nucleon in a tiny box

> We use Chiral Perturbation Theory to compute the nucleon mass-shift due to finite volume and temperature effects. Our results are valid up to next-to-leading order in the "\eps-regime" (mL ~ mβ<< 1) as well as in the "p-regime" (mL ~ mβ>> 1). Based on the two leading orders, we discuss the convergence of the expansion as a function of the lattice size and quark masses. This result can be used to extrapolate lattice results obtained from lattice sizes smaller than the pion cloud, avoiding the numerical simulation of physics under theoretical control. An extraction of the low-energy coefficient c_3 of the chiral Lagrangean from lattice simulations at small volumes and a ``magic'' ratio β=1.22262 L might be possible.

## Key claim

The paper uses Chiral Perturbation Theory to compute the nucleon mass shift due to finite volume and temperature effects, valid in both the epsilon-regime (mL much less than 1) and the p-regime (mL much greater than 1), with the caveat that convergence depends sensitively on the values of low-energy constants and is only reliable when the Delta(1232) resonance is included as an explicit degree of freedom.

## Method

The authors compute the nucleon mass shift in a finite box of size beta times L^3 using heavy baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory with explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom, treating pion zero modes non-perturbatively via integration over the SU(2) group manifold. They evaluate one-loop diagrams at leading order (epsilon^3) and next-to-leading order (epsilon^4) in the epsilon-regime power counting, where 1/L and 1/beta scale as epsilon and the pion mass scales as epsilon^2. The calculation uses the effective pion mass obtained from zero-mode integration and includes contributions from the low-energy constants c_1, c_2, and c_3.

## Key numbers

* Leading order mass shift: delta M^(3) = (1/(f^2 L^3)) * (3g_A^2/8 + 2g_NDelta^2/3) with g_A = 1.267 and g_NDelta = 1.00 +/- 0.08
* Next-to-leading order mass shift formula (Eq. M_strict): delta M^(3+4) = (1/(f^2 L^3)) * (3g_A^2/8 + 2g_NDelta^2/3) * (1 - tau(beta/L)/(ML)) - (3c_2)/(f^2 L^4) * (tau(beta/L) - L/beta) + (3c_3)/(f^2 beta L^3) - 2m^2 c_1 * (X'(m^2 f^2 beta L^3/2)/X(m^2 f^2 beta L^3/2) - 2)
* Magic ratio beta/L = 1.22262 eliminates the c_2 contribution to the mass shift
* Low-energy constants from fit 2 dagger: c_1 = -0.35 +/- 0.09/GeV, c_2 = -1.49 +/- 0.67/GeV, c_3 = 0.93 +/- 0.87/GeV
* Low-energy constants from Buettiker fit: c_1 = -0.81 +/- 0.15/GeV, c_2 = 2.99 +/- 0.77/GeV, c_3 = -4.70 +/- 0.95/GeV
* Gamma_0 = 1.675 (sum over nonzero integer vectors of 1/j^4 divided by pi^2)
* Pion decay constant f = 92.4 MeV

## Context

This work extends the epsilon-regime analysis of Gasser and Leutwyler (1987) from the meson sector to the one-baryon sector, providing a framework to extrapolate lattice QCD results from box sizes smaller than the pion cloud. The results enable extraction of the low-energy constant c_3 from lattice simulations at the magic ratio beta = 1.22262 L, where the poorly determined c_2 contribution cancels. The paper also discusses a subtlety in combining the imaginary time formalism with heavy baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory, confirming the correct analytic continuation using the real time formalism.
