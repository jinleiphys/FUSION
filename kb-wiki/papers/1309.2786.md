# Mixture of anisotropic fluids

> The recently introduced approach describing coupled quark and gluon anisotropic fluids is generalized to include explicitly the transitions between quarks and gluons. We study the effects of such processes on the thermalization rate of anisotropic systems. We find that the quark-gluon transitions may enhance the overall thermalization rate in the cases where the initial momentum anisotropies correspond to mixed oblate-prolate or prolate configurations. On the other hand, no effect on the thermalization rate is found in the case of oblate configurations. The observed regularities are connected with the late-time behavior of the analyzed systems which is described either by the exponential decay or the power law.

## Key claim
Quark-gluon transition processes can enhance the overall thermalization rate of anisotropic quark-gluon systems only when the initial momentum anisotropies correspond to mixed oblate-prolate or prolate configurations; no enhancement is found for oblate configurations, and the late-time behavior is governed either by exponential decay or power law depending on the initial configuration.

## Method
The authors generalize a previously introduced coupled quark and gluon anisotropic fluid model by adding explicit quark-gluon transition terms to the kinetic equations in the relaxation-time approximation. They solve the resulting system of equations for one-dimensional, boost-invariant expansion using the Romatschke-Strickland form for the anisotropic distribution functions, with equal relaxation times for quarks and gluons and zero baryon number. The time evolution of the anisotropy parameters, transverse-momentum scale, and effective temperature is obtained numerically for various initial anisotropy configurations.

## Key numbers
- Degeneracy factors: \(g_q = 24\) (quarks) and \(g_g = 16\) (gluons), giving ratio \(r = g_g/g_q = 2/3\)
- Initial time: \(\tau_0 = 0.1\) fm/c for all numerical calculations
- Initial conditions tested: \(\xi_g(\tau_0)=100, \xi_q(\tau_0)=0\) (oblate gluons, isotropic quarks); \(\xi_g(\tau_0)=100, \xi_q(\tau_0)=10\) (oblate both); \(\xi_g(\tau_0)=100, \xi_q(\tau_0)=-0.99\) (oblate gluons, prolate quarks); \(\xi_g(\tau_0)=-0.10, \xi_q(\tau_0)=-0.99\) (prolate both)
- The exponential decay timescale parameter is \(\tau_{\rm eq}/(1+2\delta)\) where \(\delta = \tau_{\rm eq}/\tau_{\rm tr}\), with the constraint \(0 \leq \delta \leq 2\) from the condition \(\tau_{\rm eq} < \frac{r}{1-r} \tau_{\rm tr}\)

## Context
This work extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics framework for coupled quark and gluon fluids introduced by Florkowski (2012) by adding explicit quark-gluon transition terms. The approach builds on the Romatschke-Strickland form for anisotropic distribution functions (Romatschke, 2003) and the relaxation-time approximation kinetic equations. The results show that for prolate or mixed oblate-prolate initial configurations, the quark-gluon transitions accelerate thermalization, which connects to the fast thermalization problem in heavy-ion collisions studied via AdS/CFT by Mateos (2011) and others. The next step would be a more general study of the connection between exponential solutions and fast thermalization.
