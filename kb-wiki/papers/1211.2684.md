# Present status of isobar models for elementary kaon photoproduction

> Some basic properties of isobar models are demonstrated and discussed on examples of the Saclay-Lyon and Kaon-MAID models. Predictions of these models are compared with experimental data for various processes and kinematical regions. Results of the isobar models are also compared with the Regge-isobar hybrid model, Regge-plus-resonance.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that different isobar models (Saclay-Lyon and Kaon-MAID) can fit experimental data equally well despite having fundamentally different dynamics, particularly in how they handle the reduction of Born-term contributions and the role of the $K_1$ exchange, but that data on $K^0$ photoproduction on deuteron can discriminate between them.

## Method

The authors compare the Saclay-Lyon (SLA) and Kaon-MAID (KM) isobar models, which both include Born diagrams and exchanges of $K^*(890)$ and $K_1(1270)$ resonances but differ in their choice of $s$- and $u$-channel resonances and in their treatment of hadronic structure (point-like in SLA versus dipole-type hadronic form factors in KM). They also compare these isobar models with the Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) hybrid model, which combines Regge trajectories for the non-resonant part with nucleon resonance exchanges for the resonant part. Predictions are compared with experimental data for $p(\gamma,K^+)\Lambda$, $n(\gamma,K^0)\Lambda$, and $d(\gamma,K^0)\Lambda p$ reactions across various energies.

## Key numbers

* The SLA and KM models provide reasonable results for photon energies below 2.2 GeV.
* In the $d(\gamma,K^0)\Lambda p$ reaction, the SLA model fits the shape of the momentum distribution with $\chi^2/n.d.f.$ = 0.64, while the KM2 version gives $\chi^2/n.d.f.$ = 1.75.
* The RPR model describes data up to $E_\gamma^{lab}\approx 16$ GeV.
* The SLAC data at W = 3.99 GeV show a suppression of the cross section at zero angle, while at W = 2.24 GeV this suppression is not observed.
* The electroproduction data point E94-107 at $Q^2$ = 0.07 (GeV/c)$^2$ suggests a decreasing angular dependence.
* The MAMI data at energy W = 1.75 GeV suggest a smooth $Q^2$ dependence, contrary to the original KM model prediction.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of isobar models (Saclay-Lyon A from SLA (1990s) and Kaon-MAID from KM (2000s)) with the Regge-plus-resonance approach (RPR-2007 from RPR (2007) and RPR-2011 from Lesley (2011)). The paper shows that $K^0$ photoproduction on deuteron can test model dynamics, and that recent MAMI data at small $Q^2$ constrain the longitudinal couplings in isobar models. The results are important for reliable predictions of cross sections in electroproduction of hypernuclei, where the elementary production amplitude is a necessary input.
