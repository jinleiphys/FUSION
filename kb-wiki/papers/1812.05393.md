# A new pQCD based Monte Carlo event generator for jets in the quark-gluon plasma

> A main difficulty in understanding the dynamics of jets produced in the high-density environment of ultrarelativistic heavy ion collision, is to provide a unified description for the two sources of radiation that are a priori expected: the "vacuum-like" emissions responsible for the parton shower from large virtualities down to the hadronisation scale and the "medium-induced" emissions responsible for the energy loss by the jet. In the recent paper arXiv:1801.09703, we demonstrated that these two mechanisms can be factorized from each other within a controlled, "double-logarithmic" approximation in perturbative QCD. In this proceeding we recall the main features of the jet evolution in a dense QCD medium. We emphasize that the in-medium parton showers differ from those in the vacuum in two crucial aspects: their phase-space is reduced and the first emission outside the medium can violate angular ordering. Based on this factorized picture, which is Markovian, we have recently developed a Monte Carlo event generator which includes both vacuum-like and medium-induced emissions and goes beyond the double logarithmic approximation. We here present our first results for the fragmentation function and the energy loss by the jet.

# A new pQCD based Monte Carlo event generator for jets in the quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim

Within a double-logarithmic approximation (DLA) of perturbative QCD, vacuum-like emissions and medium-induced radiations can be factorized from each other, enabling a Markovian Monte Carlo event generator that goes beyond the DLA; however, the factorization is rigorously justified only at DLA, and the generator's results for fragmentation functions and energy loss are preliminary.

## Method

The authors constructed a Monte Carlo event generator with two modules: a vacuum-like shower module implementing angular-ordered DGLAP splitting probabilities with energy conservation and running coupling, and a medium-induced shower module using BDMPS-Z branching rates with a fixed strong coupling constant. The generator first evolves the hard partons through the in-medium vacuum-like shower, then feeds all resulting partons into the medium-induced cascade, and finally evolves the out-of-medium partons through a vacuum shower until hadronisation. Jets are reconstructed using the anti-kT algorithm with R=0.4 from di-jet events at LHC sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

- The medium is characterized by two parameters: the distance L covered by the jet inside the medium and the jet quenching parameter hat-q, related to transverse momentum broadening via <k_perp^2> = hat-q * Delta-t.
- The critical angle for color decoherence of the last antenna is theta_c = 2 / sqrt(hat-q * L^3).
- The medium-induced branching rate for a gluon splitting uses tau_br(z) = (1/alpha_s) * sqrt( z(1-z) x E / hat-q_eff(z) ), with hat-q_eff(z) = hat-q * (1 - z(1-z)).
- The nuclear modification factor R_AA^jet and the fragmentation function ratio R_D(z) are shown for jets with p_T,jet >= 200 GeV at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV, for several values of hat-q and L.
- The energy spectrum of medium-induced shower particles from a leading gluon with E_gluon = 200 GeV shows a peak near omega = 200 GeV (leading particle remnant) and a broad peak around the characteristic scale Q_s = sqrt(hat-q * L).

## Context

This work extends the double-logarithmic approximation framework established by the same authors in Caucal (2018) for jet evolution in a QGP, building on the BDMPS-Z formalism for medium-induced radiation (Baier 1996, Zakharov 1996, Wiedemann 2000) and on multiple-branching treatments (Blaizot 2013, Blaizot 2014, Kurkela 2014, Escobedo 2016). The generator enables first-principles pQCD-based comparisons to LHC data on jet suppression (Khachatryan 2016, Aaboud 2018) and fragmentation functions (Chatrchyan 2014, Aaboud 2018), with planned extensions to include hadronisation and more realistic medium geometry.
