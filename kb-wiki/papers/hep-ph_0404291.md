# Low Energy Tests of the Weak Interaction

> The study of low energy weak interactions of light quarks and leptons continues to provide important insights into both the Standard Model as well as the physics that may lie beyond it. We review the status and future prospects for low energy electroweak physics. Recent important experimental and theoretical developments are discussed and open theoretical issues are highlighted. Particular attention is paid to neutrino physics, searches for permanent electric dipole moments, neutral current tests of the running of the weak mixing angle, weak decays, and muon physics. We argue that the broad range of such studies provides an important complement to high energy collider searches for physics beyond the Standard Model. The use of low energy weak interactions to probe novel aspects of hadron structure is also discussed.

## Key claim

Low energy weak interaction tests provide an important complement to high energy collider searches for physics beyond the Standard Model, but the experimental picture of the low scale running of the weak mixing angle is not entirely settled, unlike the well-tested running of QED and QCD couplings.

## Method

The authors review the status and future prospects for low energy electroweak physics, covering neutrino physics, searches for permanent electric dipole moments, neutral current tests of the running of the weak mixing angle, weak decays, and muon physics. They present a global fit to precision data using the FORTRAN package GAPP, comparing measurements at the Z pole, from deep inelastic neutrino scattering, parity-violating Moller scattering, and atomic parity violation with Standard Model predictions.

## Key numbers

- Weak mixing angle running: only four types of experiments have tested sin^2(theta_W) below the Z pole with high precision: cesium APV, deep-inelastic neutrino-nucleus scattering, PV Moller scattering, and PV elastic electron-proton scattering
- Higgs vacuum expectation value: v = 174.104 +/- 0.001 GeV (from Fermi constant)
- Global fit Higgs mass: M_H = 85^{+49}_{-32} GeV
- Direct Higgs search limit: M_H >= 114.4 GeV (95% CL)
- Number of active neutrinos from Z pole: N_nu = 2.986 +/- 0.007 (2 sigma deviation)
- Top quark mass: m_t = 174.3 +/- 5.1 GeV (Tevatron)
- W boson mass from LEP: M_W = 80.447 +/- 0.042 GeV
- W boson mass from Tevatron + UA2: M_W = 80.454 +/- 0.059 GeV
- Z boson mass: M_Z = 91.1876 +/- 0.0021 GeV
- Total Z width: Gamma_Z = 2.4952 +/- 0.0023 GeV
- Cosmological constant: Lambda_C = (2.3 +/- 0.2 meV)^4
- QCD theta-term limit: theta < 10^(-10) from electric dipole moment of Hg-199

## Context

This review extends the understanding of electroweak precision tests established at LEP 1, SLC, LEP 2, and the Tevatron Run I, and addresses open questions in the Standard Model including the hierarchy problem, the origin of CP violation, and the nature of neutrino mass. The work enables future low energy experiments such as Qweak (PV elastic electron-proton scattering) and e-D deep inelastic scattering to further test the running of the weak mixing angle, and complements high energy searches for supersymmetry and other new physics at colliders.
