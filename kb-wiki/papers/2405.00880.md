# $U_A(1)$ symmetry breaking quark interactions from vacuum polarization

> By considering the one loop background field method for a quark-antiquark interaction, mediated by one (non perturbative) gluon exchange, sixth order quark effective interactions are derived and investigated in the limit of zero momentum transfer for large quark and/or gluon effective masses. They extend fourth order quark interactions worked out in previous works of the author. These interactions break $U_A(1)$ symmetry and may be either momentum independent or dependent. Part of these interactions vanish in the limit of massless quarks, and several other -- involving vector and/or axial quark currents -- survive. In the local limit of the resulting interactions, some phenomenological implications are presented, which correspond to corrections to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. By means of the auxiliary field method, the local interactions give rise to three meson interactions whose values are compared to phenomenological values found in the literature. Contributions for meson-mixing parameters are calculated and compared to available results.

## Key claim

Sixth-order quark effective interactions that break $U_A(1)$ symmetry are derived from one-loop vacuum polarization with a dressed gluon exchange, extending the author's previous fourth-order interactions; these interactions vanish in the massless quark limit only partially, with vector and axial current terms surviving, and they provide corrections to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model rather than replacing it.

## Method

The authors start from a global color model with a dressed gluon propagator, perform a Fierz transformation to obtain color-singlet quark currents, and apply the background field method to integrate out the quark fields at one loop. A large quark mass expansion (equivalent to a large effective gluon mass expansion) of the resulting quark determinant yields sixth-order quark interactions in the local limit with zero momentum transfer, with coupling constants computed using a transverse gluon propagator from Schwinger-Dyson equations. The auxiliary field method is then used to convert these quark interactions into three-meson couplings for scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial meson nonets.

## Key numbers

- The NJL coupling constant normalization yields $G_0 = 10$ GeV$^{-2}$.
- For the flavor channel $T_{118}$, the scalar coupling constant $G_{sb,s} = 2.9$ GeV$^{-5}$ and the pseudoscalar coupling constant $G_{sb,ps} = 86.7$ GeV$^{-5}$.
- For the same channel, the derivative couplings are $G_{d1} = 59.3$ GeV$^{-6}$ and $G_{d2} = 221.7$ GeV$^{-6}$.
- The anomalous coupling $G_{sva,sb} = -459.7$ GeV$^{-7}$ for $T_{118}$.
- The mean-field corrections to the NJL pseudoscalar coupling constant are $G_{6,PS}^{11} = -9.4$ GeV$^{-2}$, compared to $G_0 = 10$ GeV$^{-2}$.
- The mixing parameter $G_{08}^{ps}$ ranges from $0.04$ to $0.14$ GeV$^{-2}$ for the pseudoscalar channel, comparable to values from the author's prior work (Braghin, 2021) which gave $G_{08}^{ps} \sim 1.04$ to $2.39$ GeV$^{-2}$.
- Constituent quark masses used are $M_u = M_d = M_s = 0.391$ GeV, with chiral condensates $langle \bar{u} u \rangle = langle \bar{d} d \rangle = (-0.240)^3$ GeV$^3$ and $langle \bar{s} s \rangle = (-0.290)^3$ GeV$^3$.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous derivations of fourth-order quark interactions from vacuum polarization (Braghin, 2014; Braghin, 2016; Braghin, 2021) to sixth order, providing a microscopic origin for $U_A(1)$ breaking beyond the instanton-induced 't Hooft interaction used in NJL models (t Hooft, 1976; Klevansky, 1992). The derived interactions have the same flavor structure as the 't Hooft interaction for flavor U(3) but differ for other flavor groups, and their numerical values are smaller than typical 't Hooft couplings ($\kappa \sim -100$ to $-2500$ GeV$^{-5}$). The results enable improved modeling of meson mixing and three-meson decays in low-energy QCD effective theories, particularly for the $\eta-\eta'$ system and vector-axial meson dynamics relevant to heavy-ion collision phenomenology.
