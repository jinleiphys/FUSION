# Chiral and trace anomalies in Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering II: QCD factorization and beyond

> We extend the discussion of the recently discovered 'anomaly poles' in QCD Compton scattering. We perform the complete one-loop calculation of the Compton amplitude using momentum transfer $t$ as the regulator of collinear divergences. In the gluon channel, we confirm the presence of poles $1/t$ in both the real and imaginary parts of the amplitude. In the quark channel, we find unexpected infrared single $1/ε$ and double $1/ε^2$ poles. We then perform the one-loop calculation of the leading-twist quark generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for quark and gluon external states with the same regulators and find that all these singular terms can be systematically absorbed into the GPDs, showing that QCD factorization is restored to this order. Having established this, we discuss the fate of the $1/t$ poles. We argue that they become the nonperturbative building blocks of GPDs that encode the chiral and trace anomalies of QCD, in a way consistent with the known constraints these anomalies impose on the nucleon axial and gravitational form factors. The scope of research on GPDs can therefore be expanded to address the manifestation and implications of quantum anomalies in high-energy exclusive processes.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the "anomaly poles" (1/t singularities) discovered in the one-loop QCD Compton amplitude do not violate the QCD factorization theorem, because they can be systematically absorbed into the twist-two generalized parton distributions (GPDs) as part of an infrared subtraction procedure, provided one uses momentum transfer t as a regulator of collinear divergences rather than dimensional regularization alone.

## Method

The authors performed a complete one-loop calculation of the QCD Compton scattering amplitude for both gluon-initiated and quark-initiated subprocesses, using nonzero momentum transfer t = (P₂-P₁)² as the regulator of collinear divergences while working in d = 4-2ε dimensions to handle ultraviolet divergences. They then computed the one-loop corrections to the leading-twist quark GPDs for free quark and gluon external states with the same regulators, and verified that all singular terms (1/t poles, 1/ε and 1/ε² infrared poles, and ln(Q²/-t) logarithms) can be systematically absorbed into the GPDs. The calculations were performed using the Mathematica package Package-X, with the HVBM scheme for γ₅ in the polarized sector.

## Key numbers

- Color factors: C_F = 4/3 and T_R = 1/2 appear in the one-loop coefficient functions
- The gluon channel exhibits 1/t poles in both the real and imaginary parts of the amplitude, with coefficients A(x,x_B,ξ) and Ã(x,x_B,ξ) given by equations (a1) and (a2)
- The quark channel contains unexpected infrared double poles 1/ε²_IR and single poles 1/ε_IR in the coefficient functions δC₁^q and δC̃₁^q
- The logarithm ln(Q²/(-l²)) replaces the -1/ε_IR pole of the usual dimensional regularization calculation
- The anomaly pole coefficients can be rewritten with leading-order kernels C₀ and C̃₀ factored out, involving kernels K(x,ξ) = x(1-x)/(1-ξ²) and L(x,ξ) = x(ξ-x)/(1-ξ²) for the unpolarized case, and K̃(x,ξ) = 4(1-x)/(1-ξ²) and L̃(x,ξ) = 4(ξ-x)/(1-ξ²) for the polarized case

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous discovery of anomaly poles in QCD Compton scattering (Bhattacharya, Hatta, Vogelsang, 2022) by completing the full one-loop calculation including the real part of the amplitude and the quark channel. It builds on earlier suggestions by Tarasov and collaborators (2020, 2021) regarding anomaly poles in polarized deep inelastic scattering, and on the general QCD factorization framework established by Collins (1998) and Ji (1997). The results enable the study of how chiral and trace anomalies of QCD manifest in GPDs and their constraints on nucleon axial and gravitational form factors, potentially connecting GPD phenomenology to fundamental issues of chiral symmetry breaking and hadron mass generation.
