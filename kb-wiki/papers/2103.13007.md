# Squeezed spectra of bosons and antibosons with different in-medium masses

> We study the influence of the in-medium mass difference between boson and antiboson on their spectra. The in-medium mass difference may lead to a difference between the transverse momentum spectra of boson and antiboson. This effect increases with the increasing in-medium mass difference between boson and antiboson. The difference between the transverse momentum spectra of boson and antiboson increases with the increasing expanding velocity of the source and decreases with the increasing transverse momentum in large transverse mass region (mT > 1:6 GeV). The interactions between the hadron and the medium may increase with the increasing temperature of the medium and the higher freeze-out temperature may lead to a larger mass difference between boson and antiboson, and may give rise to a larger difference between the transverse momentum spectra of boson and antiboson for higher freeze-out temperature.

# Squeezed spectra of bosons and antibosons with different in-medium masses

## Key claim
The in-medium mass difference between a boson and its antiboson leads to a difference in their transverse momentum spectra, but this difference is only significant in the large transverse mass region (mT > 1.6 GeV) and depends on the expanding velocity of the source; the effect is conditional on the energy split parameter delta being nonzero and on the freeze-out temperature being sufficiently high to generate a mass difference.

## Method
The authors derived single-particle momentum distributions for bosons and antibosons using a Bogoliubov transformation that relates free-particle creation and annihilation operators to quasiparticle operators in the medium, incorporating an energy split parameter delta between the boson and antiboson. They then computed the transverse momentum spectra for Kaon and D meson systems using a hydrodynamic source model with a Gaussian spatial distribution, sudden freeze-out assumption, and an expanding velocity profile, comparing the resulting spectra to experimental data from the PHENIX Collaboration for central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers
- For Kaon at freeze-out temperature T_f = 150 MeV, mass-shift delta m values of -5 MeV and -10 MeV were used, based on a hadronic medium value of about -10 MeV at 150 MeV from MFFK (2004).
- The source radius R was taken as 5 fm throughout the calculations.
- The velocity parameter u was taken as 0.6 for comparison with PHENIX central Au+Au collision data at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.
- The energy split delta values studied were 10 MeV and 20 MeV for Kaon and anti-Kaon spectra.
- For D meson, mass-shift delta m values of -3 MeV and -5 MeV were used at T_f = 150 MeV.
- The difference between boson and antiboson spectra decreases with increasing transverse momentum in the region mT > 1.6 GeV, and is nearly independent of expanding velocity for kT < 1.5 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of squeezing effects on boson spectra, previously applied to phi meson by Zhang (2020) where the antiparticle is identical, to the case of charged bosons and antibosons with different in-medium masses. It builds on the theoretical framework of squeezed back-to-back correlations developed by Asa and Cso (1996), Asa, Cso, and Gyu (1999), and Padula (2006), and on the Bogoliubov transformation approach of XPZ (2019). The results enable more accurate calculations of net-charge multiplicity distributions used to probe QCD phase transition and critical point signatures, as discussed by LAdamczyk and Xluo, by accounting for the in-medium mass difference between particles and antiparticles.
