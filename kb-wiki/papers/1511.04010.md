# q-Gamow States as continuous linear functionals on analytical test functions

> We define here q-Gamow states corresponding to Tsallis' q-statistics. We compute for them their norm, mean energy value an the q-analogue of the Breit-Wigner distribution (a q-Breit-Wigner).

## Key claim

The paper defines q-Gamow states as continuous linear functionals on analytical test functions, using the q-exponential from Tsallis statistics to generalize standard Gamow states; the authors compute the norm, mean energy, and q-Breit-Wigner distribution, with all results reducing to the conventional Gamow-state results in the limit q → 1.

## Method

The authors replace the ordinary exponential in the definition of a Gamow state with the q-exponential e_q(x) = [1 + (1-q)x]^{1/(1-q)} to construct q-Gamow states. They compute the norm of these states by evaluating integrals involving the q-exponential and hypergeometric functions, then derive the mean energy value using the q-deformed Hamiltonian action H φ_q(x) = (p^2/2m)[φ_q(x)]^q. Finally, they obtain the q-Breit-Wigner distribution by computing the scalar product between a q-Gamow state and a free-particle state, using gamma functions and asymptotic expansions.

## Key numbers

- The q-exponential parameter q ≈ 1.15 is cited as the value that fits LHC transverse momentum distributions over 14 logarithmic decades, as reported in prior experimental work.
- The norm of a q-Gamow state is given by <ψ_qG|ψ_qG> = [A(q,p)]^2, where A(q,p) involves the hypergeometric function F(1/2, (5-q)/(2(q-1)); (3+q)/(2(q-1)); [Re(p)]^2/|p|^2) and the prefactor ℏ √[2(q+1)] / [(5-q)|p|].
- In the limit q → 1, the norm squared reduces to ℏ / [2|Im(p)|], matching the standard Gamow-state norm.
- The mean energy <H>_q is defined via the symmetric combination of left and right actions, and in the limit q → 1 it becomes <H> = Re(p^2)/(2m).
- The q-Breit-Wigner distribution |<φ|φ_qG>|^2 is expressed in terms of the incomplete gamma function Γ[(3-q)/(1-q), √[2(q+1)] k / ((1-q)p)] and an exponential factor, with the standard Breit-Wigner form |Im(p)| / {π[(Re(p)-k)^2 + Im(p)^2]} recovered as q → 1.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior interpretation of Gamow states as Sebastiao e Silva ultradistributions in a Rigged Hilbert Space framework (Plastino and Rocca, 2015) by incorporating Tsallis q-statistics, which has been widely used by LHC experiments to fit transverse momentum distributions with q ≈ 1.15. The paper enables the description of complex-energy decay states in nonextensive statistical mechanics, providing a q-deformed analogue of the Breit-Wigner resonance shape that may be applied to high-energy hadronization data where thermal equilibrium is not reached.
