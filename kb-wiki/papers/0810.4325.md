# Universal Flow in the First Stage of Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> In the first moments of a relativistic heavy ion collision explosive collective flow begins to grow before the matter has yet equilibrated. Here it is found that as long as the stress-energy tensor is traceless, early flow is independent of whether the matter is composed of fields or particles, equilibrated or not, or whether the stress-energy tensor is isotropic. This eliminates much of the uncertainty in modeling early stages of a collision.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that during the first ~1 fm/c of a relativistic heavy ion collision, the collective flow (defined as \(T_{0x}/T_{00}\)) is universal across a wide class of models, provided the stress-energy tensor is traceless, longitudinal flow is boost-invariant, and the anisotropy of the spatial stress-energy components depends only on proper time \(\tau\) and not on transverse coordinates. This universality holds even though the models have very different transverse pressures and degrees of thermalization.

## Method

The authors derived analytic expressions for the early-time evolution of the momentum current \(T_{0x}/T_{00}\) from conservation laws of the stress-energy tensor, including longitudinal expansion. They then solved three simple models exactly: ideal hydrodynamics of an ultra-relativistic gas (\(P=\epsilon/3\)), coherent non-interacting electromagnetic fields (longitudinal electric fields from receding capacitor plates), and incoherent electromagnetic fields from a random ensemble of point charges receding at \(\pm c\). Each model was initialized with the same Gaussian transverse energy density profile (radii \(R_x=R_y=3\) fm) and zero collective velocity, and the evolution was computed numerically for times up to 1.0 fm/\(c\).

## Key numbers

- The universal linear approximation for the flow ratio is \(T_{0x}/T_{00} \approx x\tau/(2R_x^2)\), valid for small proper time \(\tau\).
- For the three models, \(T_{0x}/T_{00}\) shows differences of only a few percent by \(\tau = 1.0\) fm/\(c\).
- The collective velocity \(u_x\) differs between models: for ideal hydrodynamics (\(\kappa=1/3\)) it is largest, while for coherent fields (\(\kappa=1\)) it is smallest, opposite to the trend expected without longitudinal expansion.
- The elliptic anisotropy \(\epsilon_p\) for the incoherent field model remains exactly zero for all times, while for the coherent field model it grows quadratically in time.
- The quadratic approximation for elliptic anisotropy in ideal hydrodynamics is \(\epsilon_p^{(\text{hydro})} \approx (9\tau^2/32)(1/R_x^2 - 1/R_y^2)\) for \(R_x=2\) fm, \(R_y=3\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of early-time dynamics in relativistic heavy ion collisions, which had been highly uncertain due to competing descriptions based on classical QCD fields (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1993; Teaney, 2002) or partonic models (Bass, 2004). The result eliminates much of the need for detailed modeling of the pre-equilibrium stage when only the evolution of the stress-energy tensor at later times is of interest. It enables the initiation of viscous hydrodynamics at \(\tau \approx 1\) fm/\(c\) with known flow fields, isolating the later hydrodynamic evolution from contentious issues about the pre-equilibrium stage. However, the authors note that microscopic differences (quark/gluon densities, kinetic temperatures) remain important for other observables such as electromagnetic probes, jet quenching, and charge balance functions.
