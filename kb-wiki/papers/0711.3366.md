# Anisotropic flow in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC from the quark gluon string model with parton rearrangement

> We present predictions for the pseudorapidity dependence of the azimuthal anisotropy parameters v_1 and v_2 of baryons and inclusive charged hadrons in Pb+Pb collisions at a LHC energy of sqrt(s) = 5.5 TeV applying a microscopic transport model, namely the quark gluon string model (QGSM) which has been recently extended for parton rearrangement and fusion processes. Pb+Pb collisions with impact parameters b = 2.3 fm and b = 8 fm have been simulated in order to investigate additionally the difference between central and semiperipheral configurations. In contrast to v_1(eta) at RHIC, the directed flow of charged hadrons shows a small normal flow alignment. The elliptic flow v_2(eta) turns out to be rather similar in shape for RHIC and LHC conditions, the magnitude however increases about 10-20 % at the LHC, leading to the conclusion that the hydrodynamical limit will be reached.

## Key claim

The quark gluon string model with parton rearrangement predicts that the elliptic flow \(v_2\) of charged hadrons in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC will increase by about 10-20% compared to RHIC, indicating that the hydrodynamical limit will be "quasi reached" at LHC energies, whereas at RHIC it was reached only to a level of approximately 70-80%.

## Method

The authors used the Monte-Carlo version of the quark gluon string model (QGSM), a microscopic transport model based on Gribov-Regge theory with string phenomenology, which was extended to include locally density dependent parton rearrangement and fusion processes. They simulated Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.5\) TeV for two impact parameters: \(b = 2.3\) fm (central, corresponding to the mean impact parameter of the 5% most central collisions) and \(b = 8\) fm (semiperipheral). The pseudorapidity dependence of the first and second harmonic Fourier coefficients \(v_1\) and \(v_2\) of the azimuthal particle distribution was extracted for inclusive charged hadrons and for baryons.

## Key numbers

- For semiperipheral collisions (\(b = 8\) fm), the maximum elliptic flow of charged hadrons at midrapidity is \(v_2(\eta=0) = 6.7\%\); this is about 10-20% greater than the value of \(5.9 \pm 0.2\%\) obtained from a similar QGSM simulation at RHIC with the same number of participants.
- The number of charged hadrons at midrapidity for central collisions (\(b = 2.3\) fm) is \(dN_{ch}/d\eta\,|_{\eta=0}= 3813\); for semiperipheral collisions (\(b = 8\) fm) it is \(dN_{ch}/d\eta\,|_{\eta=0}= 953\).
- The ratio \(v_4/v_2^2\) for charged hadrons (averaged over \(0.15 < p_t < 2.0\) GeV and \(|\eta| < 4\)) is approximately \(0.76 \pm 0.24\) at LHC, compared to about 1.2 at RHIC and \(1.29 \pm 0.27\) from a similar analysis at top RHIC energy.
- The directed flow \(v_1\) of charged hadrons is less than 1.5% in a broad pseudorapidity range for semiperipheral collisions, and less than 2% overall.
- The annihilation probability for parton rearrangement processes was extrapolated to LHC using \(P_a(\sqrt{s_{NN}})=0.04\,\sqrt{s_{NN}/s_{NN}^{RHIC}}^\lambda\) with \(\lambda=0.288\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QGSM study of Au+Au collisions at RHIC (Bleibel 2006) to LHC energies, and provides microscopic transport predictions that can be compared to hydrodynamical calculations (Hirano 2007) and scaling arguments (Borghini 2007) which also predict increased elliptic flow, as well as to a parton transport approach (Molnar 2007) which predicts significantly smaller \(v_2\). The results suggest that the strongly coupled quark gluon plasma created at LHC will be even closer to the ideal hydrodynamical limit than at RHIC, where the limit was reached only to 70-80% (Hirano 2007, Drescher 2007). The predictions can be tested by the ALICE experiment at the LHC.
