# Charged particle elliptic flow in p+p collisions at LHC energies in a transport model PACIAE

> The parton and hadron cascade model PACIAE based on PYTHIA was used to investigate the charged particle elliptic flow in minimum bias pp collisions at the LHC energies. The strings were distributed randomly in the transverse ellipsoid of the pp collision system with major axis of $R$ (proton radius) and minor axis of $R(1-ξ)$ before parton rescattering. The charged particle elliptic flow as a function of the random number $ξ$ and transverse momentum $p_T$ were investigated. The calculated $v_2/ε$ as a function of reaction energy increases monotonously with increasing reaction energy up to $\sqrt{s}\sim$7 TeV and then turns to saturation. With the parton-parton cross section enlarges three times in parton rescattering, the rapidity integrated charged particle elliptic flow may reach 0.025 at $p_T \sim$2 GeV/c in the minimum bias pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}$=7 TeV.

## Key claim
The authors show that a non-zero elliptic flow in minimum bias pp collisions at LHC energies can be generated by introducing a random spatial fluctuation parameter ξ that controls the initial transverse eccentricity of the string distribution, but the resulting v₂ values are small (around 0.025 at most) and only reach measurable levels when the parton-parton cross section is artificially enlarged by a factor of three.

## Method
The PACIAE transport model, based on PYTHIA with added parton and hadron rescattering stages, was used to simulate minimum bias pp collisions. Before parton rescattering, strings were distributed randomly in a transverse ellipsoid with major axis R (proton radius) and minor axis R(1-ξ), where ξ is a random number controlling the initial spatial eccentricity. Parton rescattering was simulated using 2→2 LO-pQCD cross sections with a K factor of 3 and a color screening mass μ=0.63 GeV, and the resulting partonic matter was hadronized via the Lund string fragmentation model.

## Key numbers
- Charged particle elliptic flow v₂ from default PYTHIA was almost zero, and from default PACIAE was around 0.0012 at √s=14 TeV.
- With ξ=0.9 and three times enlarged parton-parton cross section, the rapidity integrated charged particle elliptic flow reached 0.025 at p_T ~2 GeV/c in minimum bias pp collisions at √s=7 TeV.
- Increasing the parton-parton cross section by a factor of three increased the integrated v₂ by nearly 60% at p_T ~2 GeV/c at √s=14 TeV.
- The scaled elliptic flow v₂/ε increased monotonically with collision energy up to √s ~7 TeV and then saturated.

## Context
This work extends the idea of initial-state fluctuations generating elliptic flow in small systems, previously proposed by Wiedemann (2009) and Chaudhuri (2009) using hydrodynamic models, and by Avsar (2010) using a dipole cascade model who predicted v₂ of 6-7% at √s=7 TeV. The PACIAE model produces much smaller v₂ values than those hydrodynamic predictions, suggesting that parton rescattering alone may not generate sufficient collective flow in pp collisions, and that the initial eccentricity from fluctuations must be combined with enhanced parton interactions to approach observable levels.
