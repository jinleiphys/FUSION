# From a few to many electrons in quantum dots under strong magnetic fields: Properties of rotating electron molecules with multiple rings

> Using the method of breaking of circular symmetry and of subsequent symmetry restoration via projection techiques, we present calculations for the ground-state energies and excitation spectra of N-electron parabolic quantum dots in strong magnetic fields in the medium-size range 10 <= N <= 30. The physical picture suggested by our calculations is that of finite rotating electron molecules (REMs) comprising multiple rings, with the rings rotating independently of each other. An analytic expression for the energetics of such non-rigid multi-ring REMs is derived; it is applicable to arbitrary sizes given the corresponding equilibrium configuration of classical point charges. We show that the rotating electron molecules have a non-rigid (non-classical) rotational inertia exhibiting simultaneous crystalline correlations and liquid-like (non-rigidity) characteristics. This mixed phase appears in high magnetic fields and contrasts with the picture of a classical rigid Wigner crystal in the lowest Landau level.

## Key claim

The paper claims that rotating electron molecules (REMs) in parabolic quantum dots under strong magnetic fields exhibit a non-rigid (non-classical) rotational inertia with simultaneous crystalline correlations and liquid-like characteristics, contrasting with the picture of a classical rigid Wigner crystal in the lowest Landau level. This claim is hedged by the condition that the effect of the external confinement on mixing with higher Landau levels is non-negligible for maximum density droplet (MDD) ground states feasible in currently available experimental quantum dots.

## Method

The authors used a two-step method of successive hierarchical approximations: first, a static electron molecule (SEM) was described by an unrestricted Hartree-Fock (UHF) determinant that violates circular symmetry, using displaced Gaussian orbitals for electrons localized at equilibrium positions of classical point charges in a parabolic confinement. Second, the broken circular symmetry was restored via projection techniques to produce rotating electron molecule (REM) states with good total angular momenta, allowing calculations for N-electron parabolic quantum dots in the range 10 ≤ N ≤ 30 under strong magnetic fields. The method was validated by comparing REM results with exact diagonalization (EXD) calculations for smaller N (N=3, 4, and 6) and with the commonly used lowest-Landau-level approximation.

## Key numbers

- For N=4 electrons, REM ground-state energies agreed well with EXD calculations in the range 2 T < B < 15 T, while the commonly used LLL approximation overestimated energies by as much as 5.5% at B ~ 4 T.
- For N=6 electrons in the lowest Landau level, REM yrast-band interaction energies showed relative error smaller than 0.3% compared to EXD, decreasing steadily for larger L values (e.g., 0.33% at L=140, 0.21% at L=200).
- For N=9 electrons in a (2,7) double-ring configuration, the REM ground-state energies approached the classical equilibrium energy of 16.75 meV (4.088 E0) as B → ∞.
- For N=9 electrons, the magic angular momenta ranged from L_m=36 (MDD at B=5 T) to L_m=208 (at B=25 T), with decomposition {k1=0, k2=0} to {k1=2, k2=24}.

## Context

This work extends previous exact diagonalization studies that were restricted to N ≤ 10 electrons (e.g., Maksym, Yannouleas, and Landman) by introducing a method capable of handling medium-size quantum dots with 10 ≤ N ≤ 30. The paper contradicts the common assumption that the lowest-Landau-level approximation is sufficient for describing MDD ground states in experimental quantum dots, showing that mixing with higher Landau levels is non-negligible. The findings enable future investigation of multi-ring rotating electron molecules and their supersolid-like characteristics, building on the classical polygonal-ring structures identified by Kong et al.
