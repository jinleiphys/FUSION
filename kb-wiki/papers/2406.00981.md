# Understanding the nature of the $Δ(1600)$ resonance

> We present a coupled-channel analysis of the $ J^P = 3/2^+ Δ$-baryon spectrum, based in the framework of Hamiltonian Effective Field Theory (HEFT). We construct a Hamiltonian which mixes quark model-like single-particle states and two-particle meson-baryon channels, and constrain this via experimentally measured $ πN \to πN $ scattering observables. In the same vein as Lüscher's approach, we then connect this infinite-volume inspired Hamiltonian with finite-volume lattice QCD results. Drawing on lattice correlation-matrix eigenvectors identifying the $ 1s $ and $ 2s $ states in the finite-volume $ Δ(3/2^+) $ spectrum, and utilising the HEFT eigenvectors describing the composition of the energy eigenstates, we resolve the structure of these states and their relation to the $ Δ(1600) $ resonance. We find the dominant contributions to this resonance come from strong rescattering in the $ πN $ and $ πΔ$ channels. This contrasts the long-held view of a dominant quark model-like core for the $ Δ(1600) $. Further discussion of other contemporary lattice results for the $ Δ$ spectrum and $ πN $ scattering states is also presented.

## Key claim

The dominant contributions to the Δ(1600) resonance come from strong rescattering in the πN and πΔ channels, rather than from a dominant quark model-like core as had been long assumed. This conclusion is conditional on the Hamiltonian Effective Field Theory (HEFT) framework's ability to simultaneously describe both infinite-volume πN scattering data and finite-volume lattice QCD results for the Δ(3/2⁺) spectrum.

## Method

The authors constructed a Hamiltonian that mixes quark model-like single-particle bare baryon states with two-particle meson-baryon channels (πN and πΔ), using momentum-dependent couplings motivated by chiral perturbation theory and regulated with dipole and tripole form factors. They constrained the Hamiltonian parameters by fitting to experimental πN → πN scattering phase shifts and inelasticities from the George Washington University SAID partial wave analysis database, then extended the Hamiltonian to finite volume with periodic boundary conditions and unphysical quark masses via linear interpolation of hadron masses in m_π². The finite-volume Hamiltonian eigenvalues and eigenvectors were compared directly with lattice QCD results from Hockley (2023) that identified the 1s and 2s states in the Δ(3/2⁺) spectrum, using eigenvector overlap probabilities to match bare basis states to lattice states.

## Key numbers

- The first excitation (2s state) in the CSSM lattice QCD results lies at approximately 2.15 GeV, far above the energy region typically associated with the Δ(1600) resonance.
- The pion decay constant used is f_π = 92.4 MeV.
- The regulator cutoff for p-wave channels uses a dipole form with exponent (l_α+3)/2 = 2, while for f-wave channels the exponent is (l_α+3)/2 = 3 (tripole form factor).
- The minimum regulator value used to determine the maximum momentum index is u_min = 0.01, following previous HEFT analyses.
- The degeneracy function C_3(2) = 12, counting the number of ways to produce n=2 from the sum of three squared integers.

## Context

This work extends the HEFT methodology previously applied to the Roper resonance N*(1440) by Leinweber (2015), Kiratidis (2015, 2016), and Liu (2016), and to the Λ(1405) by Hall (2014) and Liu (2016, 2023). It builds on an initial case study of the Δ(1600) by Abell (2021) that suggested the resonance is more than a predominant 3-quark radial excitation of the Δ(1232), contrasting with Dyson-Schwinger model results by Burkert (2017) and Liu (2022). The work enables future lattice QCD calculations to be guided by HEFT predictions for the finite-volume spectrum at various lattice sizes, and allows comparison with recent πN energy level results from Andersen (2017), Morningstar (2021), and Alexandrou (2023), as well as other lattice QCD results using single-hadron operators by Bulava (2010) and Khan (2020).
