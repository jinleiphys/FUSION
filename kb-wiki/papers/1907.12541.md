# Can We Observe Jet $P_T$-broadening in Heavy-Ion Collisions at the LHC?

> We investigate the effect of PT-broadening on jet substructure observables in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC. As an example, we focus on the opening angle of the two branches that satisfy the soft drop grooming condition in a highly energetic jet. The medium modification of the angular distribution can provide important information on the jet transport properties of hot QCD matter. In addition, we take into account a change of the overall fraction of quark and gluon jets in heavy-ion collisions. We comment on the comparison to a recent measurement from the ALICE Collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether jet $P_T$-broadening from multiple scatterings in the quark-gluon plasma can be observed in the opening angle distribution of soft-drop groomed jets at the LHC, but finds that the dominant effect is actually a narrowing of the distribution due to a shift toward quark jets in heavy-ion collisions, with $P_T$-broadening producing an opposite broadening effect that is currently difficult to isolate given experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential cross section in the groomed radius $\theta_g = R_g/R$ for inclusive jets in proton-proton collisions at leading-logarithmic accuracy with fixed coupling, including a Gaussian nonperturbative hadronization model parameterized by $Q_0^2$. For heavy-ion collisions, they modify the vacuum calculation in two ways: (1) they replace the vacuum quark/gluon fractions with in-medium fractions extracted from a global analysis of inclusive jet production in PbPb collisions, and (2) they add a $P_T$-broadening term $\langle \hat q L \rangle$ to the nonperturbative transverse momentum parameter, modeling incoherent multiple scatterings of the two groomed branches with the medium.

## Key numbers

- The nonperturbative hadronization parameter is set to $Q_0^2 = 1.54$ GeV$^2$ for both $p_T$ intervals studied (85-115 GeV and 200-250 GeV) in proton-proton collisions at 7 TeV.
- For the $P_T$-broadening illustration, a typical value $\langle \hat q L \rangle = 5$ GeV$^2$ is used, with the function $f(z_{\rm cut},\beta)=1$.
- The ALICE kinematics used for heavy-ion comparisons are $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV, jet $p_T = 80-120$ GeV, $|\eta| < 0.5$, jet radius $R=0.4$, and soft drop parameters $z_{\rm cut}=0.1$, $\beta=0$.
- The ALICE data classify events into three $\theta_g$ bins: $\theta_g < 0.25$ ($\Delta R_{12} < 0.1$), $\theta_g > 0.25$ ($\Delta R_{12} > 0.1$), and $\theta_g > 0.5$ ($\Delta R_{12} > 0.2$), with roughly half of heavy-ion events in the $\theta_g > 0.5$ bin.

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS approach to jet quenching (Baier et al., 1990s) by applying $P_T$-broadening concepts to jet substructure observables, specifically the soft-drop groomed radius. It builds on the CMS open data analysis of the $\theta_g$ distribution (Tripathee et al., 2017) and the extraction of in-medium quark/gluon fractions from inclusive jet data (Qiu et al., 2019). The results are compared to ALICE measurements of groomed jet observables (Acharya et al., 2019) and the Jewel event generator (Zapp et al., 2012). The paper enables future measurements of the nuclear modification factor with different grooming parameters and the potential extraction of the transport coefficient $\hat q$ from jet substructure data.
