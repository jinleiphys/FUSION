# Description of multinucleon transfer mechanism for ${}^{48} \mathrm{Ca}+{ }^{244} \mathrm{Pu}$ and ${ }^{86} \mathrm{Kr}+{}^{198} \mathrm{Pt}$ reactions in quantal transport approach

> This work aims to show that the quantal diffusion approach based on the stochastic mean field (SMF) theory is capable of explaining the reaction dynamics observed in MNT reactions. Primary product mass distributions in $^{48}$Ca+$^{244}$Pu reaction at E$_\text{c.m.}= $ 203.2 MeV and $^{86}$Kr+$^{198}$Pt reaction at E$_\text{c.m.} = $ 324.2 MeV are calculated and compared with the available experimental data.

# Description of multinucleon transfer mechanism for ⁴⁸Ca+²⁴⁴Pu and ⁸⁶Kr+¹⁹⁸Pt reactions in quantal transport approach

## Key claim
The quantal diffusion approach based on stochastic mean field (SMF) theory can explain multinucleon transfer (MNT) reaction dynamics without any adjustable parameters other than the standard parameters of the Skyrme energy density functional, but only for tip orientations of the deformed target nuclei (²⁴⁴Pu and ¹⁹⁸Pt) because side orientations did not satisfy the experimental angular and TKE-mass region conditions.

## Method
Time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations using the SLy4d Skyrme energy density functional were performed for both reactions across a range of initial angular momenta to obtain mean values of fragment mass, charge, scattering angles, and total kinetic energies. Quantal diffusion coefficients for neutron and proton transfer were computed from the TDHF single-particle wave functions using the SMF approach, and coupled differential equations were solved to determine neutron, proton, and mixed dispersions. Primary mass distributions from MNT were constructed as Gaussian probability distributions weighted by angular momentum, while contributions from fusion-fission in the mass-symmetric region were estimated using the statistical deexcitation code GEMINI++.

## Key numbers
- ⁴⁸Ca+²⁴⁴Pu reaction at E_c.m. = 203.2 MeV: initial angular momentum range 44ℏ ≤ ℓ_i ≤ 70ℏ; mean reaction time ≈ 6 zs; mass variance σ_AA ranges from 14.2 (ℓ_i = 70ℏ) to 19.2 (ℓ_i = 64ℏ)
- ⁸⁶Kr+¹⁹⁸Pt reaction at E_c.m. = 324.2 MeV: initial angular momentum range 60ℏ ≤ ℓ_i ≤ 120ℏ; mean reaction time ≈ 3 zs; mass variance σ_AA ranges from 9.2 (ℓ_i = 120ℏ) to 16.7 (ℓ_i = 60ℏ)
- Iso-scalar reduced curvature parameter β = 0.004 for ⁴⁸Ca+²⁴⁴Pu and β = 0.003 for ⁸⁶Kr+¹⁹⁸Pt; iso-vector reduced curvature parameter α = 0.156 for ⁴⁸Ca+²⁴⁴Pu and α = 0.176 for ⁸⁶Kr+¹⁹⁸Pt
- Normalization constants: η^MNT = 202 and η^FF = 17 for ⁴⁸Ca+²⁴⁴Pu; η^MNT = 225 and η^FF = 4 for ⁸⁶Kr+¹⁹⁸Pt
- Fusion-fission contribution to total yield: ≈ 11% for ⁴⁸Ca+²⁴⁴Pu (mass interval 126 ≤ A ≤ 166); ≈ 3% for ⁸⁶Kr+¹⁹⁸Pt (mass interval 122 ≤ A ≤ 162)

## Context
This work extends the SMF approach previously applied to other heavy-ion systems by Ayik (2008) and collaborators (Yilmaz 2018, Ayik 2019, Sekizawa 2020) to asymmetric MNT reactions involving deformed nuclei. It addresses the limitation of the time-dependent random phase approximation (TDRPA) method developed by Balian and Veneroni (1985, 1992), which can describe charge and mass dispersions only for symmetric systems. The results enable quantitative predictions for synthesizing new neutron-rich nuclei via MNT reactions and are compared directly with experimental data from Kozulin (2019) and Sen (2022).
