The nuclear suppression of the production rate due to initial and final state interactions is calculated and a novel two-step color exchange process is proposed, which is able to explain the data.

# Inelastic J/Ψ Photoproduction off Nuclei: Gluon Enhancement or Double Color Exchange?

## Key claim

The nuclear enhancement observed in inelastic J/Ψ photoproduction off heavy nuclei should not be interpreted as evidence for increased gluon density in nuclei; instead, a novel two-step color exchange process, combined with nuclear suppression from initial and final state interactions, can explain the data without invoking modified gluon distributions.

## Method

The authors calculate the nuclear suppression factor for inelastic J/Ψ photoproduction using Glauber's eikonal approximation for final state interactions, including both coherent and incoherent contributions from elastic and inelastic rescattering. They then propose a new mechanism where a photon produces a color-octet c-cbar pair on one nucleon, which propagates through nuclear matter losing energy via hadronization, and then converts to a color-singlet J/Ψ via a second color-exchange interaction with another nucleon. The triple-Regge formalism with Pomeron exchange is used to describe the elementary cross section on a free nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The experimental ratio of cross sections per nucleon on tin to carbon exceeds one by about 10%
- The E772 experiment for J/Ψ production in proton-nucleus collisions shows a suppression parameterized by A^(-ε) where ε = 0.08
- The total J/Ψ-nucleon cross section is approximately σ^{ΨN}_{tot} ≈ 5 mb
- The effective absorption cross section for charmonium in nuclear matter is about 60% of σ^{ΨN}_{in} due to inelastic shadowing corrections
- The elastic photoproduction cross section is taken as σ(γp → J/Ψ p) = 20 nb (middle value between EMC measurement of 12 nb and NMC result of 27 nb)
- The inelastic slope parameter for J/Ψ-p collisions is fixed at B^{Ψp}_{in} = 0.45 GeV^{-2} (between EMC value 0.58 ± 0.07 GeV^{-2} and NMC value 0.23 ± 0.02 GeV^{-2})
- The energy loss per unit length for the color-octet c-cbar pair is taken as κ = 5 GeV/fm
- The radiation time for gluon emission in the color-singlet model is about 1 fm in the NMC energy range

## Context

This work extends the analysis of J/Ψ photoproduction by the EMC collaboration (1990s) and NMC collaboration (1990s), who attributed the observed nuclear enhancement to increased gluon density in heavy nuclei. The authors argue against this interpretation by showing that the expected 20% suppression from initial/final state interactions (as seen in the E772 experiment for proton-nucleus collisions) must be accounted for, leaving a 30% discrepancy that cannot be explained by a few percent gluon enhancement. The proposed double color exchange mechanism provides an alternative explanation and enables future studies of color transparency effects and the space-time development of charmonium production in nuclear matter, with further details to be presented in a subsequent publication (Kopeliovich and Zamolodchikov, forthcoming).
