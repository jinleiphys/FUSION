# Towards order-by-order calculations of the nuclear and neutron matter equations of state in chiral effective field theory

> We calculate the nuclear and neutron matter equations of state from microscopic nuclear forces at different orders in chiral effective field theory and with varying momentum-space cutoff scales. We focus attention on how the order-by-order convergence depends on the choice of resolution scale and the implications for theoretical uncertainty estimates on the isospin asymmetry energy. Specifically we study the equations of state using consistent NLO and N2LO (next-to-next-to-leading order) chiral potentials where the low-energy constants cD and cE associated with contact vertices in the N2LO chiral three-nucleon force are fitted to reproduce the binding energies of 3H and 3He as well as the beta-decay lifetime of 3H. At these low orders in the chiral expansion there is little sign of convergence, while an exploratory study employing the N3LO two-nucleon force together with the N2LO three-nucleon force give first indications for (slow) convergence with low-cutoff potentials and poor convergence with higher-cutoff potentials. The consistent NLO and N2LO potentials described in the present work provide the basis for estimating theoretical uncertainties associated with the order-by-order convergence of nuclear many-body calculations in chiral effective field theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that order-by-order convergence of the nuclear and neutron matter equations of state in chiral effective field theory is not yet achieved at NLO and N2LO, and that the spread from varying the momentum-space cutoff between 450 and 600 MeV does not provide a reliable estimate of theoretical uncertainty at a given chiral order.

## Method

The authors computed the energy per particle of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using the nonperturbative particle-particle ladder approximation (Brueckner-Hartree-Fock) with chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials at NLO, N2LO, and N3LO, combined with consistent N2LO three-nucleon forces (3NFs). The low-energy constants cD and cE of the N2LO 3NF were fitted to reproduce the binding energies of 3H and 3He and the beta-decay lifetime of 3H. The density-dependent NN interaction derived from the chiral 3NF was used to incorporate three-body forces in the many-body calculation.

## Key numbers

*   The N3LO calculation of the symmetry energy slope parameter L at saturation density gives L = 39.5 +17.2 -13.6 MeV.
*   The N2LO calculation of the symmetry energy slope parameter L gives L = 76.9 +16.1 -31.2 MeV.
*   At N3LO, an uncertainty of about 8 MeV remains in the energy per particle of symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density.
*   The uncertainty from missing three-body force topologies in the density-dependent NN interaction is estimated as Delta E/A less than or equal to 1 MeV at saturation density.
*   The low-energy constants cD and cE for the N2LO 3NF with a 450 MeV cutoff are -0.326 and -0.149, respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the nuclear matter equation of state using chiral effective field theory, such as those by Coraggio (2014) and Krueger (2013), by providing consistent NLO and N2LO potentials with three-body forces fit to A=3 observables. The results highlight that the common practice of using cutoff variation as the sole uncertainty estimate is insufficient, and that the difference between predictions at consecutive chiral orders is a more reliable measure. The potentials presented enable future systematic studies of theoretical uncertainties in nuclear many-body calculations, which are needed to improve constraints on the symmetry energy from laboratory and astrophysical measurements.
