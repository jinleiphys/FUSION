# Neutrinoless double beta decay and direct searches for neutrino mass

> Study of the neutrinoless double beta decay and searches for the manifestation of the neutrino mass in ordinary beta decay are the main sources of information about the absolute neutrino mass scale, and the only practical source of information about the charge conjugation properties of the neutrinos. Thus, these studies have a unique role in the plans for better understanding of the whole fast expanding field of neutrino physics.

## Key claim

Neutrinoless double beta decay and direct kinematic searches for neutrino mass in ordinary beta decay are the main sources of information about the absolute neutrino mass scale and the only practical source of information about whether neutrinos are Majorana or Dirac particles; however, the kinematic beta-decay measurements are model-independent while neutrinoless double beta decay results depend on nuclear matrix element calculations and the assumption that the neutrino is a Majorana particle.

## Method

The paper is a working group report from the APS Multidivisional Neutrino Study that reviews the theoretical and experimental status of neutrinoless double beta decay and direct neutrino mass searches. It summarizes the connections between neutrinoless double beta decay, beta decay kinematics, neutrino oscillations, and cosmological constraints on neutrino mass. The report surveys existing experimental results from tritium beta decay experiments (Mainz and Troitsk) and describes the design and projected sensitivity of next-generation experiments including KATRIN, as well as proposed double beta decay experiments.

## Key numbers

- Mainz tritium beta decay result: m_nu_e^2 = -1.2 ± 2.2 ± 2.1 eV^2, yielding limit m_nu_e < 2.2 eV (95% CL)
- Troitsk tritium beta decay result: m_nu_e^2 = -2.3 ± 2.5 ± 2.0 eV^2, yielding limit m_nu_e < 2.1 eV (95% CL)
- KATRIN projected sensitivity: 0.20 eV (90% CL) after three years of running, with ability to observe a neutrino mass of 0.35 eV at 5 sigma significance
- Current microcalorimeter limit using Re-187: m(nu_e) < 15 eV, with energy resolution ΔE ~ 5 eV for short-term measurements
- Muon neutrino mass limit from pion decay: m(nu_mu) < 190 keV (90% confidence)
- Tau neutrino mass limit from tau decay: m(nu_tau) < 18.2 MeV (95% confidence)

## Context

This report was produced by a working group of the APS Multidivisional Neutrino Study (2004) and extends the reviews of Elliott and Vogel (2002) and McKeown and Vogel (2004). It synthesizes the connections between oscillation measurements (Super-Kamiokande, SNO, KamLAND, CHOOZ), cosmological constraints from WMAP, and laboratory searches for absolute neutrino mass. The report enables a coordinated strategy for next-generation experiments, showing that combining results from beta decay, neutrinoless double beta decay, and cosmology will be essential for determining the absolute neutrino mass scale and the Majorana or Dirac nature of neutrinos.
