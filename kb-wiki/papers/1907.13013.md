# Acceleration of the Sn Equations with Highly Anisotropic Scattering using the Fokker-Planck Equation

> The discrete ordinates method can model forward-peaked transport problems accurately. However, convergence of discrete ordinates solution can become arbitrarily slow upon use of standard iterative procedures like source iteration and GMRES. Standard zeroth and first moment-based acceleration methods like nonlinear diffusion acceleration and diffusion synthetic acceleration are ineffective in accelerating such problems because these methods do not correct higher order Legendre-moments of angular flux. We explore the idea of using Fokker-Planck as a preconditioner to accelerate forward-peaked transport problems in this paper.

## Key claim

The paper proposes using the Fokker-Planck equation as a preconditioner (Fokker-Planck Synthetic Acceleration, FPSA) to accelerate the iterative solution of the discrete ordinates (Sn) equations for forward-peaked transport problems, where standard zeroth and first moment-based acceleration methods like diffusion synthetic acceleration (DSA) and nonlinear diffusion acceleration (NDA) fail because they do not correct higher-order Legendre moments of the angular flux.

## Method

The authors develop FPSA by using the Fokker-Planck operator as the low-order approximation in a synthetic acceleration framework for the steady-state, monoenergetic, one-dimensional Cartesian Sn equations. They discretize the angular Laplacian term of the Fokker-Planck equation using two approaches: weighted finite difference (WFD) and moment preserving discretization (MPD). The method is analyzed using both angularly-continuous (Pl-based) Fourier analysis and angularly-discrete Fourier analysis to determine the spectral radius of the iteration matrix, and the theoretical predictions are compared with numerical results for three forward-peaked scattering kernels.

## Key numbers

* The angular multigrid method of Morel and Manteuffel (1991) achieved a maximum spectral radius of 0.6 in 1D for problems with high anisotropy.
* The Fokker-Planck synthetic acceleration is shown to be a special case of Pl acceleration when the scattering cross-section moments satisfy the relation sigma_{s,l} = sigma_{s,0} - ((sigma_{s,0} - sigma_{s,1})/2) * l(l+1).
* When the scattering cross-section moments satisfy the above relation and the Sn-PL equivalence condition (N = L+1) holds, FPSA converges in one iteration.

## Context

This work extends the line of research on accelerating Sn solutions for forward-peaked transport problems, building on the stability analysis of Pl acceleration by Valougeorgis, Williams, and Larsen (1988) and the angular multigrid method of Morel and Manteuffel (1991). The paper introduces a new synthetic acceleration approach based on the asymptotic Fokker-Planck limit of the Boltzmann equation (Pomraning, 1992), which enables the correction of higher-order Legendre moments that are not addressed by standard DSA (Alcouffe, 1977) or NDA (Smith et al., 2011). This work enables more efficient solution of transport problems with highly anisotropic scattering encountered in plasma physics, radiation shielding, medical physics, and astrophysics.
