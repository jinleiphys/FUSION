# Flows and polarization of early photons with magnetic field at strong coupling

> Recent experimental results from RHIC and LHC on hard photon emission rates in heavy-ion collisions indicate a large azimuthal asymmetry of photon emission rate parameterized by the elliptic flow v_2. Motivated by a recent proposal that the early magnetic field created by two colliding heavy-ions may be responsible for this large azimuthal asymmetry of photon emission rate, we compute the azimuthal dependence of the photon emission rate from a strongly coupled finite temperature plasma with magnetic field in the framework of gauge/gravity correspondence. We also propose and compute a new observable, "in/out-plane polarization asymmetry", constructed from the polarization dependence of the photon emission rates. We observe that both the azimuthal and polarization asymmetry of photon emissions are strongly affected by the triangle anomaly (chiral anomaly) for low frequency regime below 1 GeV.

## Key claim

The paper computes the azimuthal dependence of photon emission rates from a strongly coupled finite-temperature plasma in a constant magnetic field using gauge/gravity duality, finding that both the azimuthal asymmetry (elliptic flow) and a newly proposed "in/out-plane polarization asymmetry" are strongly affected by the triangle (chiral) anomaly for photon frequencies below 1 GeV, but the results assume a static plasma and constant magnetic field, requiring integration over the realistic time history of heavy-ion collisions under an additional assumption of adiabaticity.

## Method

The authors use the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic model, which is dual to large-Nc QCD with massless chiral quarks in the quenched approximation, to compute retarded Green's functions of the vector current in the presence of a constant background magnetic field. They solve the 5-dimensional equations of motion derived from the D8/anti-D8 brane action, including the Chern-Simons term that encodes the chiral anomaly, with incoming boundary conditions at the black hole horizon and extract the vector-vector retarded Green's function from the near-boundary asymptotics of the gauge field solutions. The photon emission rates are then obtained from the imaginary part of these retarded Green's functions using the Kubo formula.

## Key numbers

* The magnetic field strength is estimated to be as large as eB = 10^17 Gauss ~ m_pi^2.
* At transverse momentum p_T = 2 GeV, the observed elliptic flow of direct photons is as large as v_2 = 0.25.
* The chiral anomaly strongly affects both azimuthal and polarization asymmetry for photon frequencies below 1 GeV.
* The model parameters are fixed by fitting to the pion decay constant and rho meson mass with N_c = 3, giving C = 0.0211 and R^3 = 1.44 in units of GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier holographic computations of photon emission rates without magnetic field by Caron-Huot (2006) and Mateos (2007), and previous studies of magnetic field effects on photon emissions in the weak coupling regime by Tuchin (2010, 2012) and Basar (2012). The results indicate that the azimuthal dependence of photon emission rates is more complicated than a simple elliptic flow pattern, necessitating study of higher moments such as the quadrupole flow v_4, and the authors predict an interesting pattern of the ratio v_4/(v_2)^2 as a function of frequency that could be tested experimentally.
