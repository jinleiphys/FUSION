# Pion-Nucleon Scattering in Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory combined with the ${ 1/N_c}$ Expansion

> This work implements the combined BChPT and 1/Nc expansions for pion-nucleon elastic scattering. The effective theory is based on the baryon sector dynamical spin-flavor SU(4) symmetry emergent in the large Nc limit, whose breaking is controlled by the $1/N_c$ expansion. The non-commutativity of the chiral and 1/Nc expansions in unitarity corrections (loops) requires a linking of both expansions. As it was shown in the case of baryon masses and currents, the natural linking is the $ξ$-expansion, in which $O(p) = O(1/Nc ) = O(ξ)A$. The spin-flavor symmetry requires that the ground state baryons span an SU(4) symmetric irreducible representation which implies that in particular $N$ and $Δ$ are active degrees of freedom in the effective theory. The scattering amplitude is expanded to the next-to-next-to leading order in the $ξ$ expansion, corresponding to the one-loop contributions with the LO Lagrangian. The results are given for generic $N_c$ in order to demonstrate the consistency of the framework. The spin-flavor symmetry plays a central role in maintaining the consistency of the effective theory with respect to the $1/N_c$ expansion. This consistency manifests itself in an improvement in the convergence of the low energy expansion with respect to the case of the ordinary BChPT without an explicit dynamical $Δ$, which is known to be inconsistent with the constraints of $N_c$ scaling. Fits to the $πN \to πN$ S, P and D partial wave amplitudes from the SAID data base are finally used to test the framework and to determine the energy range of its applicability.

# Pion-Nucleon Scattering in Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory combined with the 1/N_c Expansion

## Key claim

The combined BChPT × 1/N_c framework (ξ-expansion) applied to πN elastic scattering at next-to-next-to-leading order (one-loop level) improves the convergence of the low-energy expansion compared to ordinary BChPT without an explicit dynamical Δ, which is known to be inconsistent with N_c scaling constraints; the spin-flavor SU(4) symmetry is central to maintaining consistency with the 1/N_c expansion, and fits to SAID partial wave amplitudes determine the energy range of applicability up to a pion CM momentum of 350 MeV (√s = 1.38 GeV).

## Method

The authors implement the ξ-expansion linking chiral and 1/N_c orders (O(p) = O(1/N_c) = O(ξ)) for πB → πB' scattering at NNLO, using non-relativistic baryon fields in the ground-state spin-flavor SU(4) multiplet (including both N and Δ as active degrees of freedom). They calculate one-loop diagrams organized by powers of the axial coupling gA, perform renormalization at generic N_c via counterterm Lagrangians projected in t-channel (J,I) spin-flavor operators, and fit the resulting real parts of S, P, and D partial wave amplitudes (plus the P33 imaginary part) to SAID single-energy solutions up to k = 350 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The LO axial coupling gA is related to the nucleon gA = 1.267 by (5/6) gA = gA at N_c = 3.
- The Δ decay width at NNLO is given by Γ_Δ = (g_{ANΔ}^2 m_N) / (6π (16 F_π)^2 m_Δ^4) λ_K(m_Δ^2, m_N^2, M_π^2)^{3/2}, where λ_K is the Källén phase space function.
- Fits to SAID data are performed up to a maximum pion CM momentum of 350 MeV, corresponding to √s = 1.38 GeV.
- The Δ-N mass difference at NNLO (N_c = 3) includes loop corrections with terms proportional to C_HF, M_π^2, and (M_π^2 - C_HF^2)^{3/2} arctan(C_HF/√(M_π^2 - C_HF^2)).
- The LO baryon mass formula includes a term -2 c_1 N_c M_π^2/Λ, where the σ term is O(N_c).
- The UV divergent piece of the baryon mass is O(N_c^0) and driven by the mass splitting term, while non-analytic contributions to the mass are O(N_c).

## Context

This work extends the combined BChPT × 1/N_c framework previously applied to baryon masses and axial currents by Calle Cordon and Goity (2012) and Fernando and Goity (2017) to the case of π-baryon scattering. The approach addresses the known poor convergence of ordinary BChPT with only spin-1/2 baryons (Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc, 1988; Jenkins and Manohar, 1991) by including the Δ as required by the emergent spin-flavor SU(4) symmetry in the large N_c limit (Dashen, Jenkins, and Manohar, 1993, 1994). The results enable systematic tests of the ξ-expansion against high-precision πN scattering data from the SAID database (Arndt et al., 2006) and provide a framework for extending baryon chiral perturbation theory to higher energies while maintaining consistency with 1/N_c scaling constraints.
