# Electroweak nuclear response at moderate momentum transfer

> We discuss the convergence of the expansion of the nuclear electroweak current in powers of |k|/M, where M is the nucleon mass and k denotes either the momentum transfer or the momentum of the struck nucleon. We have computed the electron and neutrino scattering cross sections off uniform nuclear matter at equilibrium density using correlated wave functions and the cluster expansion formalism. The results of our work suggest that the proposed approach provides accurate estimates of the measured electron scattering cross sections. On the other hand, the description of the current based on the widely used leading-order approximation does not appear to be adequate, even at momentum transfer as low as 300 MeV.

## Key claim

The widely used leading-order (LO) approximation for the nonrelativistic reduction of the nuclear electroweak current fails at momentum transfers above approximately 300 MeV, but including next-to-leading-order (NLO) terms yields accurate estimates of measured electron scattering cross sections at momentum transfers up to at least 500 MeV.

## Method

The authors computed electron and neutrino scattering cross sections off uniform nuclear matter at equilibrium density using correlated wave functions and the cluster expansion formalism at the two-body cluster level. They systematically compared the exact calculation of the nuclear current with the leading-order (LO) and next-to-leading-order (NLO) approximations obtained by expanding the current in powers of momentum over nucleon mass, for momentum transfers up to approximately 600 MeV.

## Key numbers

- At momentum transfer of 537 MeV (beam energy 2.02 GeV, scattering angle 15 degrees), the NLO and exact calculations accurately reproduce the extrapolated nuclear matter electron scattering data in the quasielastic peak region, while the LO approximation fails.
- For electron scattering at fixed 30-degree angle with beam energies 600-1000 MeV, corresponding to momentum transfer 300-500 MeV, the LO approximation shows sizable deviations from the exact result even at the lowest momentum transfer of 300 MeV.
- For muon neutrino scattering at beam energies 200-600 MeV, the LO approximation is accurate at 200 MeV (momentum transfer 170-300 MeV), but corrections become sizable as neutrino energy increases.
- The nuclear matter density used is 0.16 fm^{-3}, corresponding to Fermi momentum k_F = 1.33 fm^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation of impulse approximation breakdown for scalar probes (Ankowski and Benhar, 2010) to the full electroweak current. It contradicts the widespread use of the leading-order nonrelativistic current approximation in correlated-basis function calculations of nuclear matter response, as employed by Fabrocini and Fantoni (various years). The approach enables extension to finite nuclei (C, O, Ca) using techniques already developed for ground-state properties by the authors and collaborators, and provides a framework for including meson-exchange currents needed to address discrepancies with MiniBooNE neutrino data.
