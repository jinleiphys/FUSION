# Elimination of influence of neutron-skin size difference of initial colliding nuclei in Pb+Pb collisions

> Within an isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model using as an input nucleon density profiles from Hartree-Fock calculations based on a modified Skyrme-like (MSL) model, we study how to eliminate the influence of neutron-skin size difference of initial colliding nuclei in probing the nuclear symmetry energy. Within the current experimental uncertainty range of neutron-skin size of $^{208}$Pb, the Pb+Pb collisions are performed in semicentral and peripheral collisions with impact parameters of 5 and 9fm and at beam energies from 50 MeV/nucleon to 1000 MeV/nucleon, respectively. It is shown that combination of neutron and proton collective flows, i.e., neutron-proton differential elliptic flow, neutron-proton elliptic flow difference, neutron-proton differential transverse flow and neutron-proton transverse flow difference, can effectively eliminate the effects of neutron-skin size difference and thus can be as useful sensitive observables in probing nuclear matter symmetry energy in heavy-ion collisions. Moreover, the combined neutron-proton stopping power including the neutron-proton differential stopping power and neutron-proton stopping power difference can also eliminate the effects of neutron-skin size difference and shows some sensitivities to symmetry energy especially at the lower beam energy.

# Elimination of influence of neutron-skin size difference of initial colliding nuclei in Pb+Pb collisions

## Key claim
Combined neutron-proton collective flow observables (differential elliptic flow, elliptic flow difference, differential transverse flow, and transverse flow difference) and combined neutron-proton stopping power can effectively eliminate the influence of neutron-skin size differences between initial colliding nuclei while retaining sensitivity to the nuclear symmetry energy, particularly at lower beam energies around 50 MeV/nucleon. The claim is conditional: the elimination works "especially at the lower beam energy" and the observables show "some sensitivities" rather than strong sensitivity across all energies.

## Method
The study uses the isospin- and momentum-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU11) transport model with nucleon density profiles from Hartree-Fock calculations based on the modified Skyrme-like (MSL) model. Pb+Pb collisions are simulated at impact parameters of 5 fm (semicentral) and 9 fm (peripheral) with beam energies from 50 to 1000 MeV/nucleon, using neutron-skin thicknesses of 0.1 fm and 0.3 fm for 208Pb to represent the current experimental uncertainty range. Two forms of symmetry energy are compared: a soft symmetry energy (x=1, slope parameter L=16.4 MeV) and a stiff symmetry energy (x=0, L=62.1 MeV), with 4x10^5 events per case to ensure statistical precision.

## Key numbers
- Beam energies studied: 50, 200, 400, and 1000 MeV/nucleon
- Impact parameters: 5 fm (semicentral) and 9 fm (peripheral)
- Neutron-skin thicknesses used: 0.1 fm and 0.3 fm for 208Pb
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: 30.5 MeV at rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3
- Slope parameter L for soft symmetry energy (x=1): 16.4 MeV
- Slope parameter L for stiff symmetry energy (x=0): 62.1 MeV
- Saturation density: 0.16 fm^-3
- Binding energy of symmetric nuclear matter: -16 MeV
- Incompressibility: 212 MeV
- Experimental neutron-skin thickness range cited: 0.11 +/- 0.06 fm from pi+ -Pb scattering to 0.33 (+0.16/-0.18) fm from PREX-1

## Context
This work extends the author's previous study (Wei 2013) which showed that the charged pion ratio is sensitive to both symmetry energy and neutron-skin size, particularly in peripheral collisions. The paper addresses the problem identified by Ditoro (2005) and Bao (2008) that many proposed symmetry energy observables suffer from contamination by other poorly known physical effects. The combined flow observables build on the neutron-proton differential transverse flow concept proposed by Li (2000) and the neutron-proton elliptic flow difference from Ditoro (2005, 2010). The results enable future experimental measurements at facilities like those using the FOPI detector to extract cleaner constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy, especially at lower beam energies where the combined observables show maximal sensitivity.
