# Geometric bias and centrality dependence of jet quenching in high-energy nuclear collisions

> Jet quenching provides a valuable measure of the opacity of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) produced in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. However, substantial suppression of charged hadron spectra is observed in highly peripheral collisions, despite the expectation of negligible jet-QGP interactions in this regime. To address this, we develop a HIJING-based initial condition model that accounts for the impact parameter dependence of both inelastic nucleon-nucleon (NN) collisions and the number of hard partonic scatterings per inelastic NN collision. This dependence introduces a geometric bias effect on the jet yield within a given centrality class of nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions, suppressing the high transverse momentum hadron spectrum in peripheral collisions due to dilute nucleon overlap at large AA impact parameters. By combining this improved initial condition model with a linear Boltzmann transport model for jet-QGP interactions, we obtain a satisfactory description of the centrality dependence of charged hadron suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=5.02$ TeV.

## Key claim
The substantial suppression of charged hadron spectra observed in highly peripheral Pb+Pb collisions can be explained primarily by a geometric bias effect on the initial jet yield, rather than by jet interactions with the quark-gluon plasma. This bias arises because, within a given centrality class, events with a larger impact parameter have a reduced number of hard partonic scatterings, suppressing the high-transverse-momentum hadron spectrum even when jet-QGP interactions are negligible.

## Method
The authors developed a HIJING-based initial condition model that includes the impact-parameter dependence of both the number of inelastic nucleon-nucleon (NN) collisions and the number of hard partonic scatterings per inelastic NN collision. This model was coupled with a linear Boltzmann transport model (LBT) to simulate jet-QGP interactions. The combined framework was used to compute the nuclear modification factor (R_AA) for charged hadrons in Pb+Pb collisions at a center-of-mass energy of 5.02 TeV per nucleon pair.

## Key numbers
*   The model provides a satisfactory description of the centrality dependence of charged hadron suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV.
*   The geometric bias effect is shown to suppress the high-transverse-momentum hadron spectrum in peripheral collisions due to dilute nucleon overlap at large AA impact parameters.

## Context
This work addresses the long-standing puzzle of significant jet quenching signals observed in peripheral heavy-ion collisions, where the QGP medium is expected to be very small or absent. It extends the standard HIJING and LBT framework by incorporating a more detailed geometric bias, contradicting the assumption that all suppression in peripheral collisions must come from final-state energy loss. This enables a more accurate extraction of QGP transport coefficients from the centrality dependence of jet quenching data, as it separates initial-state geometric effects from final-state medium effects.
