# Schwinger's proper time and worldline holographic renormalisation

> Worldline holography states that within the framework of the worldline approach to quantum field theory, sources of a quantum field theory over Mink$_4$ naturally form a field theory over AdS$_5$ {\sl to all orders} in the elementary fields and in the sources of arbitrary spin. (Such correspondences are also available for other pairs of spacetimes, not only Mink$_4\leftrightarrow\mathrm{AdS}_5$.) Schwinger's proper time of the worldline formalism is automatically grouped with the physical four spacetime dimensions into an AdS$_5$ geometry. We show that the worldline holographic effective action in general and the proper-time profiles of the sources in particular solve a renormalisation group equation and, reversely, can be defined as solution to the latter. This fact also ensures regulator independence.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the worldline holographic effective action and the proper-time profiles of the sources solve a renormalisation group equation and can be defined as its solution, which ensures regulator independence. This holds to all orders in the elementary fields and in sources of arbitrary spin, with the hedge that the AdS$_5$ geometry is self-consistent and the proper-time regularisation corresponds to the UV-brane regularisation in holography.

## Method

The authors start from the worldline representation of the generating functional for a quantum field theory over Minkowski space, where Schwinger's proper time naturally becomes the fifth dimension of an AdS$_5$ geometry. They then impose independence of the effective action from the ultraviolet proper-time regulator $\varepsilon$, which leads to a Wilson-Polchinsky renormalisation condition. This condition is solved by reconstructing the full five-dimensional field theory with AdS isometries, where the sources acquire a fifth-dimensional profile determined by the renormalisation group equation. The method is applied explicitly to the free case of scalar QED at one loop and to general higher-spin sources.

## Key numbers

* For scalar QED with $N_f \times N_c$ scalar quarks, the coefficient for the quadratic term in the field strength is $\#_{2,2} = \frac{1}{2!} \frac{1}{6} \frac{2 N_f N_c}{(4\pi)^2}$.
* The one-loop beta-function coefficient obtained from the holographic renormalisation is $\beta_1 = \frac{N_f N_c}{48\pi^2}$ for scalar QED.
* For higher-spin sources of rank $L$, the characteristic exponents for the small-$T$ behaviour of the field are $\alpha = 1$ and $\alpha = 1-L$, obtained from the equation $-\alpha(\alpha-2+L)+L-1=0$.

## Context

This work extends the worldline holography program initiated by Dietrich and collaborators (2013-2015), which demonstrated that a quantum field theory over Mink$_4$ naturally reorganises into a field theory for its sources over AdS$_5$. It provides a first-principles derivation of the renormalisation group flow in this holographic setting, connecting to the Wilson-Polchinsky renormalisation condition (Wilson 1971). The results enable a systematic understanding of how bottom-up AdS/QCD models (Karch 2006, Erlich 2005) arise from quantum field theory, and the method applies to sources of arbitrary spin, including the Frønsdal equation for higher-spin fields in AdS.
