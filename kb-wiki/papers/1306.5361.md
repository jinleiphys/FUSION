# Shear viscosity of neutron-rich nucleonic matter near its liquid-gas phase transition

> Within a relaxation time approach using free nucleon-nucleon cross sections modified by the in-medium nucleon masses that are determined from an isospin- and momentum-dependent effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, we investigate the specific shear viscosity ($η/s$) of neutron-rich nucleonic matter near its liquid-gas phase transition. It is found that as the nucleonic matter is heated at fixed pressure or compressed at fixed temperature, its specific shear viscosity shows a valley shape in the temperature or density dependence, with the minimum located at the boundary of the phase transition. Moreover, the value of $η/s$ drops suddenly at the first-order liquid-gas phase transition temperature, reaching as low as $4\sim5$ times the KSS bound of $\hbar/4π$. However, it varies smoothly for the second-order liquid-gas phase transition. Effects of the isospin degree of freedom and the nuclear symmetry energy on the value of $η/s$ are also discussed.

## Key claim

The specific shear viscosity of neutron-rich nucleonic matter shows a valley shape in its temperature or density dependence even without a phase transition, but the minimum is located at the phase transition boundary; the value drops suddenly at a first-order liquid-gas phase transition but varies smoothly for a second-order transition, reaching as low as 4 to 5 times the KSS bound of ℏ/4π.

## Method

The authors used a relaxation time approach with free nucleon-nucleon cross sections modified by in-medium nucleon masses from an isospin- and momentum-dependent (MDI) interaction. They constructed the liquid-gas phase coexistence region via Gibbs conditions (equal chemical potentials, pressure, and temperature) and computed the shear viscosity separately for liquid and gas phases, then averaged by volume fraction in the mixed phase. The symmetry energy was varied using two parameter sets (x=0 for stiffer, x=1 for softer) to study its effects.

## Key numbers

- The specific shear viscosity η/s reaches as low as 4 to 5 times the KSS bound of ℏ/4π at the first-order liquid-gas phase transition temperature.
- The MDI interaction is fitted to a binding energy of -16 MeV and incompressibility of 212 MeV at saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- The symmetry energy at saturation density is fixed to E_sym(ρ₀) = 31.6 MeV, with slope parameters L ≈ 60 MeV (x=0, stiffer) and L ≈ 15 MeV (x=1, softer).
- The liquid-gas phase transition is studied at fixed pressure P = 0.1 MeV/fm³ and at fixed temperature T = 10 MeV for various isospin asymmetries (δ = 0, 0.5, 1).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of specific shear viscosity at the hadron-quark phase transition (Cse06, Lac07) and at the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition (Pal10, Zho12a, Zho12b), and addresses the speculation by Che07 that the behavior of η/s depends on the order of the transition. The results are expected to help interpret signatures of the liquid-gas phase transition in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions with rare isotopes, and to clarify the role of the nuclear symmetry energy in transport properties of neutron-rich matter.
