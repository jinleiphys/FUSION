# Jet multiplicities as the QGP thermometer

> It is proposed to use the energy behavior of mean multiplicities of jets propagating in a nuclear medium as the thermometer of this medium during the collision phases. The qualitative effects are demonstrated in the framework of the fixed coupling QCD with account of jet quenching.

# Jet multiplicities as the QGP thermometer

## Key claim

The energy dependence of mean multiplicities of jets propagating through nuclear matter can serve as a thermometer for the quark-gluon plasma, provided the coupling strength is treated as temperature-dependent and the enhancement of soft gluon emission (jet quenching) is accounted for by a nuclear QCD factor \(N_s\). The authors demonstrate this qualitatively within fixed-coupling QCD, concluding that the effective temperature is above \(3 T_c\) and close to \(6 T_c\) for the chosen parameter range, but note that the exact power \(\gamma\) of multiplicity growth must ultimately be determined from experimental data.

## Method

The authors solve the QCD integrodifferential equations for the generating functions of jet multiplicities, assuming a fixed (energy-independent) but temperature-dependent coupling strength \(\alpha_S\). They modify the standard QCD splitting kernels by introducing a nuclear factor \(N_s\) in the soft infrared terms to account for enhanced soft gluon emission due to rescattering in the deconfined medium. The equations are reduced to algebraic form using a scaling ansatz \(\langle n \rangle \propto \exp(\gamma y)\), yielding relations between the power \(\gamma\), the coupling \(\alpha_S\), and the nuclear factor \(N_s\).

## Key numbers

- For \(\gamma = 0.5\) and the estimated range \(1.6 < N_s < 1.8\) (from fits in Bwie (2005)), the effective coupling strength lies between \(\alpha_S = 0.14\) and \(\alpha_S = 0.122\).
- For \(\gamma = 0.4\), the coupling strength would be in the range \(0.07 < \alpha_S < 0.08\) for the same \(N_s\) interval.
- For \(\gamma = 0.6\), the coupling strength would be in the range \(0.22 < \alpha_S < 0.25\) for the same \(N_s\) interval.
- The ratio of gluon to quark jet multiplicities \(r\) is approximately 1.68 for \(\gamma = 0.6\), 1.82 for \(\gamma = 0.5\), and 1.95 for \(\gamma = 0.4\) in the region \(1.6 < N_s < 1.8\).
- The asymptotic value of \(r\) for \(N_s \to \infty\) is 2.25.

## Context

This work extends the QCD formalism for jet multiplicities developed for \(e^+e^-\) annihilation (Dremin (1994), Dokshitzer et al. (1991)) to the nuclear medium by incorporating temperature-dependent coupling from lattice QCD results (Kaczmarek et al. (2004), Karsch et al. (2001), Petreczky et al. (2004), Sorensen (2005)) and jet quenching effects modeled via modified splitting kernels (Bwie (2005)). The approach enables using measured jet multiplicity growth as a thermometer for the QGP, with the authors noting that direct insertion of lattice \(\alpha_S\) values into the QCD equations (relaxing the fixed-coupling assumption) is work in progress, and that higher-rank factorial moments and their oscillations will be studied elsewhere.
