# Holographic compact stars meet gravitational wave constraints

> We investigate a simple holographic model for cold and dense deconfined QCD matter consisting of three quark flavors. Varying the single free parameter of the model and utilizing a Chiral Effective Theory equation of state (EoS) for nuclear matter, we find four different compact star solutions: traditional neutron stars, strange quark stars, as well as two non-standard solutions we refer to as hybrid stars of the second and third kind (HS2 and HS3). The HS2s are composed of a nuclear matter core and a crust made of stable strange quark matter, while the HS3s have both a quark mantle and a nuclear crust on top of a nuclear matter core. For all types of stars constructed, we determine not only their mass-radius relations, but also tidal deformabilities, Love numbers, as well as moments of inertia and the mass distribution. We find that there exists a range of parameter values in our model, for which the novel hybrid stars have properties in very good agreement with all existing bounds on the stationary properties of compact stars. In particular, the tidal deformabilities of these solutions are smaller than those of ordinary neutron stars of the same mass, implying that they provide an excellent fit to the recent gravitational wave data GW170817 of LIGO and Virgo. The assumptions underlying the viability of the different star types, in particular those corresponding to absolutely stable quark matter, are finally discussed at some length.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a simple holographic model for deconfined three-flavor quark matter, when combined with a Chiral Effective Theory nuclear matter equation of state and with the single free parameter (the quark mass \(m_0\)) allowed to vary, can produce novel hybrid star solutions whose tidal deformabilities are smaller than those of ordinary neutron stars of the same mass, providing an excellent fit to the gravitational wave data GW170817, but only under the nontrivial assumption that three-flavor quark matter is absolutely stable relative to two-flavor quark matter.

## Method

The authors constructed equations of state for cold, dense compact star matter by combining the "medium stiffness" Chiral Effective Theory nuclear matter equation of state from Hebeler (2013) with a holographic quark matter equation of state derived from a probe D7 brane in an \(AdS_5 \times S^5\) background, treating the quark mass \(m_0\) as a free parameter. For each value of \(m_0\), the phase with lower free energy (higher pressure) was selected at each chemical potential, allowing for multiple first-order phase transitions. The resulting equations of state were then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute mass-radius relations, tidal deformabilities, Love numbers, moments of inertia, and mass distributions for the resulting compact star configurations.

## Key numbers

* For \(m_0 \gtrsim 313.1\) MeV, nuclear matter is dominant at low densities with a single first-order transition to quark matter at higher density (standard hybrid stars).
* For \(310.0\ \text{MeV} \lesssim m_0 \lesssim 313.1\) MeV, there are three first-order transitions between nuclear and quark matter phases as density increases.
* For \(261.4\ \text{MeV} \lesssim m_0 \lesssim 310.0\) MeV, quark matter is favored at both lowest and highest densities, with an intermediate density interval where nuclear matter pressure is larger.
* For \(m_0 \lesssim 261.4\) MeV, quark matter pressure is larger at all densities (pure strange quark stars).
* The tidal deformability of a 1.4 solar mass star is minimized not by ordinary neutron stars but by an HS2 solution (nuclear matter core with a quark matter crust).
* The transitions between phases occur at baryon densities between approximately \(13-22\) times the nuclear saturation density \(n_s\) at high chemical potential and between approximately \(3\times 10^{-3}\) to \(0.15\ n_s\) at low chemical potential, where \(n_s \approx 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the holographic approach to compact stars from Hoyos (2016), where the quark mass was fixed to make the quark matter pressure vanish at the same chemical potential as nuclear matter, yielding no stable hybrid stars. By relaxing that assumption, the authors discover new hybrid star types (HS2 and HS3) that were not present in that earlier analysis. The results enable direct comparison with gravitational wave observations from LIGO and Virgo (GW170817), showing that these novel hybrid stars can satisfy all existing observational bounds on compact star properties, particularly through their reduced tidal deformabilities relative to ordinary neutron stars.
