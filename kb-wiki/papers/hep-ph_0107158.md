# Constructing Parton Convolution in Effective Field Theory

> Parton convolution models have been used extensively in describing the sea quarks in the nucleon and explaining quark distributions in nuclei (the EMC effect). From effective field theory point of view, we construct the parton convolution formalism which has been the underlying conception of all convolution models. We explain the significance of scheme and scale dependence of the auxiliary quantities such as the pion distributions in a nucleon. As an application, we calculate the complete leading nonanalytic chiral contribution to the isovector component of the nucleon sea.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a rigorous effective field theory (EFT) framework for parton convolution models, showing that the conventional convolution picture emerges as a particular truncation of a systematic chiral expansion. The authors emphasize that auxiliary quantities such as pion distributions in a nucleon are scheme- and scale-dependent, meaning they cannot be defined in an absolute sense.

## Method

The authors match twist-two quark operators onto hadronic operators in chiral perturbation theory, using the heavy-baryon formalism with dimensional regularization. They express the moments of parton distributions as matrix elements of these hadronic operators, then reconstruct the full x-dependent distributions via inverse Mellin transforms. The formalism is applied to calculate the complete leading nonanalytic chiral contribution to the isovector component of the nucleon sea, including both pion-cloud and nucleon-operator contributions.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 93 MeV
- Leading nonanalytic contribution to the isovector sea asymmetry: int_0^1 (dbar - ubar) dx = (3g_A^2 + 1) / [2(4 pi f_pi)^2] * m_pi^2 * log(m_pi^2 / m_N^2) + ...
- For n odd, the moments A_n of the isovector pion distribution in the proton are: A_n = (-1)^((n-1)/2) * ((n+3)/(n+1)) * (3g_A^2 + delta_{n1}) * m_N^2 / [4(4 pi f_pi)^2] * (m_pi/m_N)^(n+1) * log(m_pi^2 / m_N^2) + ...
- For n even, A_n = 0
- The isospin charge counting gives a_1 = 2 for the first moment of the u-d distribution in the pi^+ meson in the chiral limit

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to parton distributions pioneered by Chen and Ji (2001) and Arndt and Savage (2001), providing a formal justification for the Sullivan process approach of Thomas (1983) and the later calculation by Thomas, Melnitchouk, and Steffens (2000). The authors show that their result agrees with Thomas et al. (2000) in the limit g_A = 1, but note that the sign of the leading chiral contribution to the isovector sea asymmetry is opposite to experimental data from Amaudraz et al. (1991) and Hawker et al. (1998), indicating the importance of other contributions. The formalism enables systematic higher-order corrections through chiral power counting, which was not possible in conventional convolution models.
