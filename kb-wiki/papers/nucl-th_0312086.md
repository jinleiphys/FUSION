# Three-body structure of the low-lying $^{17}$Ne-states

> The Borromean nucleus $^{17}$Ne ($^{15}$O$ + p + p$) is investigated by using the hyperspheric adiabatic expansion for a a three-body system. The measured size of $^{15}$O and the low-lying resonances of $^{16}$F ($^{15}$O$ + p$) are first used as constraints to determine both central and spin-dependent two-body interactions. Then, the ground state structure of $^{17}$Ne is found to be an almost equal mixture of $s^2$ and $d^2$ proton-$^{15}$O relative states, the two lowest excited states have about 80% of $sd$-mixed components, and for the next two excited three-body states the proton-$^{15}$O relative s-states do not contribute. The spatial extension is as in ordinary nuclei. The widths of the resonances are estimated by the WKB transmission through the adiabatic potentials and found in agreement with the established experimental limits. We compare with experimental information and previous works.

## Key claim

The ground state of $^{17}$Ne is an almost equal mixture of $s^2$ and $d^2$ proton-$^{15}$O relative states, but this conclusion depends on the Jacobi coordinate set used: in the proton-proton Jacobi set the $s$-wave contribution dominates at 89%, while in the core-proton Jacobi sets the $s$ and $d$ contributions are roughly 45% and 50% respectively, with the interference between $s$ and $d$ waves playing a negligible role.

## Method

The authors used the hyperspheric adiabatic expansion method to solve the three-body Faddeev equations for $^{17}$Ne as a $^{15}$O + p + p system. They first constrained the proton-$^{15}$O two-body interactions by reproducing the measured low-lying resonances of $^{16}$F and the mirror $^{16}$N, using both Woods-Saxon and Gaussian form factors with central, spin-spin, and spin-orbit terms. Bound states were obtained from exponentially decaying radial solutions, while resonance states were computed using complex scaling of the hyperradius to obtain exponentially decaying resonance wavefunctions, with widths estimated via WKB transmission through the adiabatic potentials.

## Key numbers

- Ground state two-proton separation energy: experimental value is -0.94 MeV; computed value without three-body force is -0.79 MeV (150 keV underbound)
- Excitation energies of the four lowest resonance states: 1.288 ± 0.008 MeV ($3/2^-$), 1.764 ± 0.012 MeV ($5/2^-$), 2.997 ± 0.011 MeV ($7/2^-$), and 3.548 ± 0.020 MeV ($9/2^-$)
- The lowest adiabatic potential accounts for more than 75% of the probability in all states: 88.5% ($1/2^-$), 90.7% ($3/2^-$), 77.2% ($5/2^-$), 97.5% ($7/2^-$), 91.9% ($9/2^-$)
- Experimental lower limit on the lifetime of the $3/2^-$ state is 26 ps, corresponding to an upper width limit of $2.5 \times 10^{-11}$ MeV
- The $s$-wave contribution in the proton-proton Jacobi set for the ground state is 89.0%, with $d$-waves contributing 3.8%
- The $^{15}$O core rms charge radius used is 2.65 fm, with a Coulomb range parameter $b_c$ = 2.16 fm

## Context

This work extends the hyperspheric adiabatic expansion method, previously well-tested on neutron dripline Borromean halos like $^{6}$He and $^{11}$Li (Nielsen et al., 2001), to the proton-rich Borromean nucleus $^{17}$Ne. The authors address inconsistencies in prior calculations of $^{17}$Ne structure (Fortier et al., 2001; Timofeyuk et al., 1996; Nakamura et al., 1998; Miljanic et al., 1997; Grigorenko et al., 2003) by properly including the non-zero spin of the $^{15}$O core and using spin-dependent two-body interactions constrained by $^{16}$F and $^{16}$N spectra. The results enable more reliable predictions for direct two-proton decay widths from the excited resonance states, which are promising candidates for such decays (Grigorenko et al., 1998; Chromik et al., 2002).
