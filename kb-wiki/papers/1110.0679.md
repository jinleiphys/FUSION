# Analysis of gamma-ray production in neutral-current neutrino-oxygen interactions at energies above 200 MeV

> It has long been recognized that observation of gamma rays originating from nuclear deexcitation can be exploited to identify neutral-current neutrino-nucleus interactions in water-Cherenkov detectors. We report the results of a calculation of the neutrino- and antineutrino-induced gamma-ray production cross section for oxygen target. Our analysis is focused on the kinematical region of neutrino energy larger than ~200 MeV, in which single-nucleon knockout is known to be the dominant reaction mechanism. The numerical results have been obtained using for the first time a realistic model of the target spectral function, extensively tested against electron-nucleus scattering data. We find that at neutrino energy 600 MeV the fraction of neutral-current interactions leading to emission of gamma-rays of energy larger than 6 MeV is ~41%, and that the contribution of the p_3/2 state is overwhelming.

## Key claim
At a neutrino energy of 600 MeV, approximately 41% of neutral-current interactions on oxygen lead to emission of gamma rays with energy above 6 MeV, with the contribution from the p3/2 shell state being dominant. This result is obtained using a realistic spectral function model for the first time, and the fraction is largely independent of the axial mass parameter.

## Method
The authors computed the neutral-current quasielastic neutrino-oxygen cross section using a realistic spectral function model of the oxygen nucleus, which incorporates nucleon-nucleon correlations and has been tested against electron-nucleus scattering data. The gamma-ray production cross section was then obtained by multiplying the cross section for each hole state (p1/2, p3/2, s1/2) by its branching ratio for deexcitation via gamma emission with energy above 6 MeV, taken from experimental data. The calculation covers neutrino and antineutrino interactions in the energy region above 200 MeV where single-nucleon knockout dominates.

## Key numbers
- At neutrino energy 600 MeV, the fraction of neutral-current interactions leading to gamma rays of energy larger than 6 MeV is approximately 41%.
- The p3/2 spectroscopic strength is 0.703, the p1/2 strength is 0.632, and the s1/2 strength is 0.422.
- The branching ratio for gamma emission from the p3/2 hole state is 100%, from the s1/2 state is 16 ± 1%, and from the p1/2 state is 0%.
- At neutrino energy 0.5 GeV (1.0 GeV), the average of neutrino and antineutrino cross sections exceeds the result of Kolbe (2002) by a factor of approximately 10 (15).
- The s1/2 contribution to the gamma-ray production cross section remains less than 4.5% for neutrino energies up to 5 GeV.
- The neutrino-induced gamma-production cross section reaches its maximum at approximately 1.9 GeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations by Kolbe (2002) and others that considered gamma-ray production from neutral-current neutrino-oxygen interactions at energies up to 500 MeV, but focused on inelastic excitation of nuclear resonances rather than quasielastic knockout. The use of a realistic spectral function model, validated against electron scattering data from Saclay (1993), NIKHEF-K (1997), and Jefferson Lab (2005), provides a more accurate treatment of nucleon momentum distributions and correlations. The results enable improved identification of neutral-current events in water-Cherenkov detectors like Super-Kamiokande, where gamma rays above the 5 MeV observational threshold can provide an alternative signal to neutron detection for tagging neutral-current interactions.
