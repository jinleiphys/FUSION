# Femtoscopic study of the $S=-1$ meson-baryon interaction: $K^-p$, $π^-Λ$ and $K^+Ξ^-$ correlations

> We study the femtoscopic correlation functions of meson-baryon pairs in the strangeness $S=-1$ sector, employing unitarized s-wave scattering amplitudes derived from the chiral Lagrangian up to next-to-leading order. For the first time, we deliver predictions on the $π^-Λ$ and $K^+Ξ^-$ correlation functions which are feasible to be measured at the Large Hadron Collider. We also demonstrate that the employed model is perfectly capable of reproducing the $K^-p$ correlation function data measured by the same collaboration, without the need to modify the coupling strength to the $\bar{K}^0n$ channel, as has been recently suggested. In all cases, the effects of the source size on the correlation are tested. In addition, we present detailed analysis of the different coupled-channel contributions, together with the quantification of the relative relevance of the different terms in the interaction. These calculations require the knowledge of the so-called production weights, for which we present two novel methods to compute them.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that two existing unitarized chiral perturbation theory models (Oset-Ramos and BCN), which were constrained to scattering data, can reproduce the measured $K^-p$ correlation function from ALICE without modifying the coupling strength to the $\bar{K}^0n$ channel, contrary to a recent suggestion by the ALICE collaboration (2022).

## Method

The authors compute femtoscopic correlation functions for $K^-p$, $\pi^-\Lambda$, and $K^+\Xi^-$ pairs using the generalized Koonin-Pratt formula with s-wave scattering amplitudes from unitarized chiral perturbation theory up to next-to-leading order. They employ two benchmark models: the Oset-Ramos model (Weinberg-Tomozawa term only, 7 free parameters) and the BCN model (full WT+Born+NLO kernel, 16 free parameters), both regularized via dimensional regularization. The Coulomb interaction is added separately to the strong amplitude for charged pairs, and production weights are computed using two novel methods described in an appendix.

## Key numbers

- Source sizes for $K^-p$ CF: $R_{\bar{K}N} = 1.08 \pm 0.18$ fm and $R_{\pi\Sigma,\pi\Lambda} = 1.23 \pm 0.21$ fm, taken from ALICE (2022)
- For $\pi^-\Lambda$ and $K^+\Xi^-$ CFs, three source sizes are tested: $R = 1.0$, $1.25$, and $1.5$ fm
- Production weight for $\bar{K}^0n$ in $K^-p$ CF: $0.90^{+0.09}_{-0.10}$ (new VLC method), compared to $0.97 \pm 0.20$ from ALICE (2022)
- Production weight for $\pi^0\Lambda$ in $K^-p$ CF: $1.47^{+0.16}_{-0.14}$ (new method), compared to $1.96 \pm 0.93$ from ALICE (2022)
- The cutoff for the wave function integral is fixed to $q_{\rm max} = 800$ MeV/c
- The second $\Lambda(1405)$ pole is located around $1420$ MeV with a width of approximately $40$ MeV across models

## Context

This work extends the femtoscopic analysis of the $S=-1$ meson-baryon interaction beyond the $K^-p$ channel studied by ALICE (2019, 2022) and Kamiya et al. (2019), providing first predictions for $\pi^-\Lambda$ and $K^+\Xi^-$ correlation functions measurable at the LHC. The BCN model (Feijoo 2018) previously reproduced scattering data including $K^-p \to \eta\Lambda$ and $K\Xi$ channels, and the present study tests its validity in the femtoscopic regime. The results enable future comparisons with ongoing ALICE measurements to constrain the subthreshold $\bar{K}N$ interaction and the lower-mass pole of the $\Lambda(1405)$, which remains poorly determined compared to the well-pinned upper pole.
