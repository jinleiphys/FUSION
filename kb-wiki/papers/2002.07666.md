# Green's functions for non-classical transport with general anisotropic scattering

> In non-classical linear transport the chord length distribution between collisions is non-exponential and attenuation does not respect Beer's law. Generalized radiative transfer (GRT) extends the classical theory to account for such two-point correlation between collisions and neglects all higher order correlations. For this form of transport, we derive the exact time-independent Green's functions for the isotropic point source in infinite 3D homogeneous media with general anisotropic scattering. Green's functions for both collision rate density, which characterizes absorption and reaction rates in the system, and radiance/flux, which characterizes displacement of radiation/particles, are solved in Fourier space. We validate the derivations using gamma random flights to produce the first anisotropic scattering benchmark solutions for the generalized linear Boltzmann equation. For gamma-4 flights with linearly anisotropic scattering and gamma-6 flights with Rayleigh scattering the collision rate density is found explicitly in real space as a sum of diffusion modes.

## Key claim

The paper derives exact time-independent Green's functions for the generalized linear Boltzmann equation with general anisotropic scattering in infinite 3D homogeneous media, but only for the specific case of generalized radiative transfer (GRT) that accounts for two-point correlations between collisions while neglecting all higher-order correlations.

## Method

The author extends Grosjean's 1951 Fourier transform approach for random flights to non-exponential free-path length distributions with anisotropic scattering, solving for both collision rate density and radiance/fluence in Fourier space. The method involves solving linear systems for spherical harmonic expansion functions \(h^{(l)}(u)\) that incorporate the free-path length distribution \(p_c(s)\), the phase function expansion coefficients \(A_l\), and the single-scattering albedo \(c\). The derived Fourier-space solutions are validated using Monte Carlo simulations of gamma random flights with linearly-anisotropic and Rayleigh scattering phase functions.

## Context

This work extends the Fourier transform approach for Green's functions from classical exponential transport (Davison 1947, Grosjean 1951, Paasschens 1997) to non-classical transport with general anisotropic scattering, building on prior GRT developments by Larsen (2011), Davis (2006), and d'Eon (2014, 2019). The derived Green's functions provide benchmark solutions for validating numerical codes for the generalized linear Boltzmann equation, and the explicit real-space solutions for gamma-4 and gamma-6 flights with anisotropic scattering enable direct comparison with diffusion approximations.
