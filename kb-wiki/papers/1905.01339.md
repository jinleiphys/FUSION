# The Giant Pairing Vibration in Heavy Nuclei: Present Status and Future Studies

> The Giant Pairing Vibration, a two-nucleon collective mode originating from the second shell above the Fermi surface, has long been predicted and expected to be strongly populated in two-nucleon transfer reactions with cross sections similar to those of the normal Pairing Vibration. Recent experiments have provided evidence for this mode in $^{14,15}$C but, despite sensitive studies, it has not been definitively identified either in Sn or Pb nuclei where pairing correlations are known to play a crucial role near their ground states. In this paper we review the basic theoretical concepts of this "elusive" state and the status of experimental searches in heavy nuclei. We discuss the hindrance effects due to Q-value mismatch and the use of weakly-bound projectiles as a way to overcome the limitations of the (p,t) and (t,p) reactions. We also discuss the role of the continuum and conclude with some possible future developments.

# The Giant Pairing Vibration in Heavy Nuclei: Present Status and Future Studies

## Key claim

The Giant Pairing Vibration (GPV), a two-nucleon collective mode predicted to have cross sections similar to the normal Pairing Vibration, has been observed in light nuclei (14,15C) but remains undetected in heavy nuclei like Sn and Pb despite sensitive searches; the authors argue this elusiveness likely results from a combination of Q-value mismatch in conventional transfer reactions and the dissolution of the GPV into the continuum due to width acquired from low orbital angular momentum single-particle states.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework of the GPV using Random Phase Approximation (RPA) and Tamm-Dancoff (TDA) calculations applied to a pairing Hamiltonian, and surveys experimental searches using (p,t) reactions at various proton energies (35, 50, and 60 MeV) at iThemba LABS and LNS Catania, as well as (6He,alpha) reactions at GANIL and TRIUMF. The authors analyze Q-value effects on cross sections using DWBA calculations with collective form factors, and examine continuum effects using the Berggren representation in the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers

- The GPV is predicted to lie at an energy of approximately 65/A^(1/3) MeV, corresponding to roughly 12 MeV in Pb and 14 MeV in Sn isotopes
- In the degenerate-level limit, the GPV cross section scales as approximately A^(2/3) times the single-particle cross section
- For the 208Pb(6He,alpha)210Pb reaction, the survey map identifies 6He and 11Li beams as cases where the transfer cross section to the GPV is predicted to be larger than to the ground state
- The (p,t) experiment on 118Sn at 50 MeV with zero-degree measurement set an upper limit on the GPV cross section between 0.13 and 0.19 mb over the spectrometer angular acceptance of +/- 2 degrees
- The (p,t) experiment at 35 MeV on 120Sn found a fitted width for a possible GPV bump of 1.5 +/- 0.4 MeV
- The 208Pb(6He,alpha) experiment at GANIL used a 20 MeV/A beam with intensity of 10^7 particles per second

## Context

This review extends the theoretical prediction of the GPV by Broglia and Bes (1977) and connects to the recent observation of GPV states in 14,15C by Cappuzzello and Cavallaro (2015) using heavy-ion-induced two-neutron transfer reactions. The paper addresses the longstanding puzzle of why the GPV has not been observed in Sn and Pb nuclei despite extensive searches by Crawley (1980s) and Mouginot (2000s), and discusses the role of continuum effects first studied in the Berggren representation by Dussel (2009). The work enables future experimental searches using weakly-bound projectiles and highlights the need to understand the fundamental differences between particle-hole and particle-particle damping mechanisms, as emphasized by Bortignon and Broglia (2019).
