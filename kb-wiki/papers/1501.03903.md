# Possible observables for the chiral electric separation effect in Cu + Au collisions

> The quark-gluon plasma (QGP) generated in relativistic heavy-ion collisions could be locally parity-odd. In parity-odd QGP, the electric field may induce a chiral current which is called the chiral electric separation effect (CESE). We propose two possible observables for CESE in Cu + Au collisions: The first one is the correlation $ζ_{αβ}=\langle \cos[2(ϕ_α+ϕ_β-2Ψ_{\rm RP})]\rangle$; the second one is the charge-dependent event-plane angle $Ψ^{q}_2$ with $q=\pm$ being charge. Nonzero $Δζ=ζ_{opp}-ζ_{same}$ and $ΔΨ=\langle|Ψ_2^+-Ψ_2^-|\rangle$ may signal the CESE in Cu + Au collisions. Within a multiphase transport model, we study how the final state interaction affects these observables. We find that the correlation $γ_{αβ}=\langle\cos(ϕ_α+ϕ_β-Ψ_{\rm RP})\rangle$ is sensitive to the out-of-plane charge separation caused by the chiral magnetic effect and to the in-plane charge separation caused by the in-plane electric field, but it is not sensitive to the CESE. On the other hand, $Δζ$ and $ΔΨ$ are sensitive to the CESE. Therefore, we suggest that the future experiments measure the above observables in Cu+Au collisions in order to disentangle different chiral and charge separation mechanisms.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the correlation difference Δζ = ζ_opp − ζ_same and the charge-dependent event-plane angle difference ΔΨ = ⟨|Ψ₂⁺ − Ψ₂⁻|⟩ are sensitive to the chiral electric separation effect (CESE) in Cu+Au collisions, while the previously studied correlation γ_αβ is not. These claims are based on simulations within a multiphase transport (AMPT) model with specific initial charge separation configurations, and the authors hedge that experimental identification of the Au and Cu sides requires reconstruction of the first harmonic event plane.

## Method
The authors used the AMPT model with string melting to simulate Cu+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV. They introduced six different initial charge distributions into the model's initial condition: normal (no anomalous effect), out-of-plane dipole (mimicking the chiral magnetic effect, CME), in-plane dipole (mimicking in-plane charge separation from the electric field, in-plane CSE), and combinations including a quadrupolar component (mimicking CESE plus CME plus in-plane CSE). The initial charge separation percentage was taken as f = 1.56b/fm, calibrated to Cu+Cu data. The final-state hadronic observables were then extracted after parton cascade and hadronization, with hadronic rescatterings switched off to ensure charge conservation.

## Key numbers
- The initial charge separation percentage was set as f = 1.56b/fm, where b is the impact parameter, calibrated to fit Cu+Cu experimental data at √s_NN = 200 GeV.
- For the CESE+CME+in-plane CSE configuration, the quadrupolar component used a switching percentage of f% for quarks with p_y > 0 and 0.5f% for quarks with p_y < 0.
- The Δζ observable showed an increasing trend from central to peripheral Cu+Au collisions, with a clear signal in the most peripheral centrality bin for the CESE+CME+in-plane CSE case.
- The ΔΨ observable showed a linear dependence on centrality for cases including CESE, with nonzero values across the whole centrality window; cases without CESE gave ΔΨ consistent with zero.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of chiral effects in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and chiral separation effect (CSE), which were studied in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions. The authors build on the proposal by Huang (2013) that Cu+Au collisions provide a strong in-plane electric field suitable for detecting the chiral electric separation effect (CESE). The study uses the AMPT model, previously applied by Ma (2011) and Shou (2014) to describe charge azimuthal correlations in Au+Au collisions. The results enable future experimental measurements at RHIC to disentangle CESE from CME and in-plane CSE by using the proposed Δζ and ΔΨ observables.
