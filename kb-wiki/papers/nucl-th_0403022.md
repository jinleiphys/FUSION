# On the usefulness of the spectral function concept

> The usefulness of the spectral function S in the process 3He(e,e'N) has been investigated in a kinematical regime constrained by the conditions that the three-nucleon center-of-mass energy is less than 150 MeV and the magnitude of the three-momentum transfer smaller than 600 MeV/c. Results based on a full treatment of the final state interaction are compared to the spectral function approximation. In the case of proton knockout in the direction of the photon kinematical conditions have been identified where both response functions, RL and RT, can be well approximated by S. These conditions occur for certain low missing momenta and missing energies but not in all cases. So care is required. In case of neutron knockout only RT is a candidate for an approximate treatment by S. In the case of RL the concept of using S is not valid in the studied kinematical regime. This does not exclude the possibility that beyond that regime it might be useful. Possible applications using S for the extraction of electromagnetic form factors of the nucleons are pointed out.

## Key claim

The spectral function S can provide a good approximation to the full response functions RL and RT for proton knockout from 3He only under specific kinematical conditions of low missing momenta and missing energies, and only at the higher end of the momentum transfer Q within the studied regime; for neutron knockout, only RT is a candidate for such an approximation, while RL cannot be approximated by S in the studied kinematical domain.

## Method

The authors performed non-relativistic calculations of the 3He(e,e'N) reaction in parallel kinematics, solving the full three-nucleon Faddeev equations with the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential to include complete final state interactions. They compared the resulting longitudinal (RL) and transverse (RT) response functions to the spectral function approximation (FSI23), which includes only the final state interaction between the two spectator nucleons, for a grid of missing momentum k and missing energy E values within a kinematical domain restricted to three-nucleon center-of-mass energy below 150 MeV and three-momentum transfer Q below 600 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- Kinematical regime: three-nucleon center-of-mass energy E3Nc.m. ≤ 150 MeV and three-momentum transfer |Q| ≤ 600 MeV/c
- For proton knockout, the spectral function approximation works for (k,E) pairs such as (0.1 fm^-1, 5 MeV), (0.25 fm^-1, 5 MeV), (0.25 fm^-1, 20 MeV), (0.5 fm^-1, 20 MeV), and (0.5 fm^-1, 40 MeV) at higher Q values
- For fixed electron kinematics, good agreement between S and full results occurs at the upper end of ejected proton energy E1 for (ω=100 MeV, Q=400 MeV/c), (ω=100 MeV, Q=500 MeV/c), and (ω=150 MeV, Q=600 MeV/c)
- Counterexamples where the spectral function approximation fails include (ω=100 MeV, Q=200 MeV/c) and (ω=200 MeV, Q=300 MeV/c)

## Context

This work extends the long-standing investigation of the spectral function concept in electron scattering on 3He, introduced by Dieperink and collaborators (1975, 1976) and extensively studied by Ciofi degli Atti and collaborators (1978-2002) and Sauer and collaborators (1983-1994). Previous studies had never included the full final state interaction among all three nucleons. The authors provide the first full treatment of final state interactions in this context, showing that the spectral function approximation is only valid in a limited corner of the kinematical domain, which enables future experimental validation of the theoretical predictions in that region and identification of where full dynamics must be considered.
