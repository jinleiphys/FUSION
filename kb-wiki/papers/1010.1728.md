# Nucleon-Nucleon interaction, charge symmetry breaking and renormalization

> We study the interplay between charge symmetry breaking and renormalization in the NN system for s-waves. We find a set of universality relations which disentangle explicitly the known long distance dynamics from low energy parameters and extend them to the Coulomb case. We analyze within such an approach the One-Boson-Exchange potential and the theoretical conditions which allow to relate the proton-neutron, proton-proton and neutron-neutron scattering observables without the introduction of extra new parameters and providing good phenomenological success.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that charge symmetry breaking (CSB) in the nucleon-nucleon system can be described without introducing extra short-distance parameters by imposing a renormalization condition that fixes the scattering length as an independent input, thereby disentangling known long-distance dynamics from short-distance physics.

## Method

The authors solve the reduced Schrödinger equation for s-waves using a One-Boson-Exchange (OBE) potential, replacing the standard regularity condition at the origin with an energy-independent boundary condition derived from the zero-energy wave function. This boundary condition is fixed by the scattering length, which is treated as an independent input rather than computed from the potential, and the limit of zero cut-off radius is taken to achieve regulator independence. The method is extended to proton-proton scattering by including the Coulomb potential and defining Coulomb-modified regular and irregular solutions.

## Key numbers

- The np scattering length is taken as input: α₀ = -23.74 fm (the experimental value for the np system).
- The effective range from the renormalized fit is r₀ = 2.672 fm, compared to r₀ = 2.678 fm and r₀ = 2.679 fm from the two regular solutions.
- The proton Bohr radius is a_B = 56.62 fm, derived from a_B = 2 / M_p α with α ≈ 1/137.
- The nn scattering length from a recent measurement of the π-d capture reaction is a_nn = -18.69(4) fm when corrected for magnetic interactions.

## Context

This work extends the renormalization approach previously applied to the charge-independent NN system by Cordon and Arriola (2008, 2009) to include charge symmetry breaking and Coulomb interactions. It contrasts with traditional OBE models such as the Bonn potential (Machleidt, 1987) and CD-Bonn (Machleidt, 2000), which compute scattering lengths from the potential and require fine-tuning, and with Effective Field Theory approaches (Kong, 1998; Epelbaum, 2005) that treat scattering lengths as completely unrelated. The derived universality relations enable connecting np, nn, and pp scattering observables without introducing new parameters, and the method can be applied to determine the Gamow-Teller matrix element for pp fusion from the np scattering length.
