# Charmonia Production in Hot QCD Matter and Electromagnetic Fields

> Both hot QCD matter and extremely strong electromagnetic fields are generated in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We employ the transport model and the equivalent photon approximation (EPA) to study charmonium hadroproduction and photoproduction in nucleus-nucleus collisions, respectively. In photoproduction, quasi-real photons may interact with the whole nucleus or individual nucleons, which is called the coherent and incoherent processes, respectively. The typical momentum of charmonium produced in two processes is located in $p_T\lesssim 1/R_A$ and $p_T\lesssim 1/R_N$, where $R_A$ and $R_N$ are the radii of nucleus and the nucleon. Both kinds of photoproduction and also hadroproduction are considered to calculate charmonium production in different transverse momentum bins, rapidity bins, and collision centralities, incorporating modifications from hot QCD matter and initial cold nuclear matter effects. Our calculations explain experimental data about charmonium nuclear modification factors and the production cross-section in ultra-peripheral collisions. Charmonium nuclear modification is far above the unit at extremely low $p_T$ ($p_T < 0.1$ GeV/c) in peripheral collisions with centrality 70-90\%, attributed to coherent photoproduction.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the observed enhancement of the charmonium nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) far above unity at very low transverse momentum (\(p_T < 0.1\) GeV/c) in peripheral Pb-Pb collisions (centrality 70-90%) is attributed to coherent photoproduction, but this conclusion is conditional on the assumption that hot QCD matter modifications to photoproduced charmonium are negligible.

## Method
The authors combine a Boltzmann-type transport model for charmonium hadroproduction (including primordial production, regeneration, and non-prompt production from B-hadron decay) with the equivalent photon approximation (EPA) for coherent and incoherent photoproduction. The photoproduction cross-sections are calculated using nuclear and nucleon form factors derived from a Woods-Saxon distribution, and the hadroproduction is evolved in a 2+1 dimensional hydrodynamic medium with initial conditions from the Glauber model and cold nuclear matter effects (shadowing via EPS09). The inclusive nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) is computed by summing all contributions across transverse momentum bins, rapidity bins, and collision centralities.

## Key numbers
- In peripheral collisions (centrality 70-90%) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV Pb-Pb, the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) is enhanced to around 10 at \(p_T < 0.2\) GeV/c due to photoproduction.
- The typical transverse momentum for coherent photoproduction is \(p_T \lesssim 1/R_A\) and for incoherent photoproduction is \(p_T \lesssim 1/R_N\), where \(R_A\) and \(R_N\) are the nuclear and nucleon radii, respectively; the transition occurs around \(p_T \approx 0.1\) GeV/c.
- The initial maximal temperature of the QGP in central collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV is \(T_0 = 510\) MeV at mid-rapidity and 450 MeV in forward rapidity, with \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/c.
- The shadowing factor for \(J/\psi\) in forward rapidity at 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb is taken as 0.8, and for \(c\bar{c}\) pairs as 1.0.
- The charm pair production cross-section is taken as \(d\sigma_{pp}^{c\bar{c}}/dy = 220 \times d\sigma_{pp}^{J/\psi}/dy\) at 5.02 TeV.
- The parameters for the photon-proton cross-section at 5.02 TeV are \(b_V = 4.0\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(X = 0.00406\) \(\mu\)b, and \(\epsilon = 0.65\); for 200 GeV Au-Au, \(X = 0.0015\) \(\mu\)b and \(\epsilon = 0.68\).

## Context
This work extends the study of charmonium production in heavy-ion collisions by simultaneously including hadroproduction (as modeled in transport approaches by Du (2017) and Zhou (2014)) and photoproduction (using the EPA method applied by Klein (1999) and Shi (2017)) from ultra-peripheral to central collisions. It explains the low-\(p_T\) enhancement of \(R_{AA}\) observed by the ALICE Collaboration (Shatat 2024) in peripheral Pb-Pb collisions, which cannot be accounted for by hadroproduction alone. The results enable a more complete understanding of electromagnetic field effects in heavy-ion collisions and provide a baseline for future studies of vector meson photoproduction in the presence of hot QCD matter.
