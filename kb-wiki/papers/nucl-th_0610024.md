# Overcoming Fragility

> We examine the sensitivity and surface bias of convolved DGLV radiative and elastic loss in a realistic geometry including Bjorken expansion. We find that this more faithful treatment of the medium density is not a priori reproducible via fixed length approximations, and neither the fragility nor the surface emission of BDMPS-based models is seen.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the previously claimed "fragility" and surface emission of BDMPS-based jet quenching models are artifacts of oversimplified geometry (hard cylinder or Woods-Saxon without Bjorken expansion), and that a more faithful treatment using realistic diffuse nuclear densities with Bjorken expansion restores sensitivity to medium density changes.

## Method

The authors used the WHDG model, which convolves DGLV radiative energy loss with elastic energy loss, and implemented a realistic geometry using a Woods-Saxon participant density from Glauber profiles with one-dimensional Bjorken expansion. They defined an effective path length for each parton as a line integral through the participant density field, then computed the distribution of path lengths and the resulting nuclear modification factor R_AA for pions at midrapidity in most-central Au+Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- The entropy-constrained medium density corresponds to dN_g/dy ~ 1000, which yields R_AA predictions consistent with data
- A factor of two increase in dN_g/dy beyond 1000 oversuppresses R_AA to values inconsistent with data
- The characteristic widths of surviving parton spatial distributions range from Δx ≈ 3-6 fm, indicating probes sample significant portions of the medium rather than only the surface
- For p_T = 15 GeV jets, the survival probability distributions for gluon, up, charm, and bottom quarks show a hierarchy of flavor-dependent effective path lengths

## Context

This work directly counters the pessimistic conclusions of earlier BDMPS-based studies (the "oldmodels" referenced in the text) that claimed jet tomography is impossible at RHIC and the LHC due to fragility and surface emission. The authors note that those earlier works used hard cylinder geometry without Bjorken expansion (first reference) or Woods-Saxon density without expansion (later references). The results are consistent with other non-fragile models using GLV-type radiative loss (Vitev, 2005) and higher-twist energy loss (Renk, 2006). The paper enables future work to relax the fixed coupling constant approximation and to test the model against the full set of high-p_T observables for tomographic medium probing.
