# Partonic Effects in Heavy Ion Collisions at RHIC

> Effects of partonic interactions in heavy ion collisions at RHIC are studied in a multiphase transport model (AMPT) that includes both initial partonic and final hadronic interactions.It is found that a large parton scattering cross section is needed to understand the measured elliptic flow of pions and two-pion correlation function.

## Key claim

Partonic interactions are essential for explaining the observed elliptic flow and two-pion correlation functions at RHIC, but only if the initial strings are converted to partons (string melting) and a large parton-parton scattering cross section of 6-10 mb is used; the default AMPT model without string melting produces elliptic flow much smaller than measured.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model, which combines initial conditions from HIJING with Zhang's Parton Cascade (ZPC) for partonic elastic scatterings and the ART (A Relativistic Transport) model for hadronic interactions. They extended the model with a "string melting" mechanism that converts excited strings into constituent quarks in high-energy-density regions, allowing partonic dynamics to include the energy originally stored in strings. The model parameters were first constrained by fitting data from Pb+Pb collisions at SPS at sqrt(s)=17A GeV, then applied to Au+Au collisions at RHIC at sqrt(s)=56A and 130A GeV.

## Key numbers

- A parton scattering cross section of 6-10 mb is needed to account for the observed dependence of charged particle elliptic flow on total charged particle multiplicity and transverse momentum in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=130A GeV
- To reproduce the measured one-dimensional correlation functions from STAR, a parton scattering cross section of about 10 mb is required in the extended AMPT model with string melting
- The ratios of anti-proton to proton and K+/pi+ in the absence of final-state hadronic interactions are 0.80 and 0.13, respectively, compared to 0.66 and 0.18 from the default AMPT model
- Turning off the partonic cascade leads to less than about 5% change in final charged particle yields in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=130A GeV
- The emission duration Delta-tau for midrapidity pions with 125 < p_T < 225 MeV/c lasts more than 25 fm/c at low transverse mass
- Source radii R_out, R_side, and R_long from the emission function are between 10 and 25 fm/c at low transverse mass, while fitted Gaussian radii are a factor of two to three smaller
- The ratio R_out/R_side has a value of about 1.2 for most values of m_T

## Context

This work extends the AMPT model developed by the same group (Ko, Lin, Pal) to include string melting, building on earlier studies of elliptic flow in parton cascade models (Zhang, 1999) and HBT interferometry (Pratt, 1986; Bertsch, 1988; Rischke, 1996). The results contradict the default HIJING model predictions (Wang, 1991) for rapidity distributions and show that hadronic observables like charged particle yields are insensitive to partonic interactions, while elliptic flow and pion correlation functions are sensitive probes. The work enables future studies of parton distribution dependence, inelastic partonic scatterings, and hadronization mechanisms to further test quark-gluon plasma signals at RHIC.
