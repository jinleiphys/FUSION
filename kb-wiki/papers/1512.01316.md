# Spin Polarized Photons from Axially Charged Plasma at Weak Coupling: Complete Leading Order

> In the presence of (approximately conserved) axial charge in the QCD plasma at finite temperature, the emitted photons are spin-aligned, which is a unique P- and CP-odd signature of axial charge in the photon emission observables. We compute this "P-odd photon emission rate" in weak coupling regime at high temperature limit to complete leading order in the QCD coupling constant: the leading log as well as the constant under the log. As in the P-even total emission rate in the literature, the computation of P-odd emission rate at leading order consists of three parts: 1) Compton and Pair Annihilation processes with hard momentum exchange, 2) soft t- and u-channel contributions with Hard Thermal Loop re-summation, 3) Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) re-summation of collinear Bremstrahlung and Pair Annihilation. We present analytical and numerical evaluations of these contributions to our P-odd photon emission rate observable.

## Key claim

The paper computes the P-odd photon emission rate from a QCD plasma with an approximately conserved axial charge at complete leading order in the QCD coupling constant, including both the leading log and the constant under the log, under the assumption that the axial charge relaxation rate (proportional to \(\alpha_s^5\) or \(\alpha_s (m_q/T)^2\)) is much slower than the photon emission rate (proportional to \(\alpha_{\rm EM}\alpha_s\)) so that a constant axial chemical potential can be assumed.

## Method

The authors compute the P-odd photon emission rate \(d\Gamma^{\rm odd}/d^3\bm k\) (the difference between rates for positive and negative photon helicity) for hard photons with momentum comparable to temperature \(T\), using weak-coupling thermal field theory in the high-temperature limit. The calculation follows the established decomposition for the P-even total emission rate into three parts: (1) hard Compton scattering and pair annihilation processes with momentum exchange of order \(T\), (2) soft t- and u-channel contributions regulated by Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) resummation, and (3) Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) resummation for collinear bremsstrahlung and pair annihilation. The authors treat a right-handed Weyl fermion with chemical potential \(\mu = \mu_A\) and then double the result for the left-handed fermion (with \(\mu = -\mu_A\)), up to the modification of the Debye mass \(m_D^2\) in the gluon HTL self-energy.

## Key numbers

* The P-odd photon emission rate scales as \(d\Gamma^{\rm odd}/d^3\bm k \sim \alpha_{\rm EM} \alpha_s (\log(1/\alpha_s) + c)\), where \(\alpha_s = g^2/(4\pi)\) is the QCD coupling constant and \(c\) is the constant under the log.
* The relaxation rate of axial charge via sphaleron transitions is \(\tau_R^{-1} \sim \alpha_s^5 \log(1/\alpha_s) T\), while the photon emission rate for hard momenta is \(d\Gamma/d^3 k \sim \alpha_{\rm EM} \alpha_s \log(1/\alpha_s) T\).
* The small-frequency limit of the imaginary part of the chiral magnetic conductivity at zero spatial momentum is \({\rm Im}\sigma_\chi(\omega,\bm 0) = -\xi^{\rm QCD}_5 \omega + \mathcal{O}(\omega^3)\), with \(\xi^{\rm QCD}_5 = -2.003 / (g^4 \log(1/g)) \cdot (\mu_A / T)\).
* For two-flavor QCD, the full result for the P-odd emission rate is multiplied by the charge factor \(Q_u^2 + Q_d^2 = 5/9\) relative to the result for a quark with electromagnetic charge \(Q = +1\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous classification of P- and CP-odd observables in photon and di-lepton emission rates (Mamo and Yee, 2013) by providing the complete leading-order computation of the photon spin asymmetry \(A_{\pm\gamma}\) in the presence of axial charge. The calculation builds on the established framework for the P-even total photon emission rate (Baier et al., 1991; Kapusta et al., 1991; Arnold et al., 2001) and on the computation of the small-frequency limit of the chiral magnetic conductivity (Jimenez-Alba et al., 2015). The results enable quantitative predictions for event-by-event measurements of photon spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions, where axial charge fluctuations arise from topological color field configurations or thermal sphaleron transitions.
