# Isospin composition of fission barriers

> We employ a microscopic method to study how isospin affect the fission potential of $^{240}$Pu. Our approach uses constrained Hartree-Fock theory (CHF) which allows us to separately investigate the isoscalar and isovector properties of the nuclear energy density functional (EDF). By analyzing the isoscalar and isovector components of the EDF along the fission path we can assess the isovector contribution to fission barriers. We study this effect for the fully adiabatic path to scission. The isovector component of the fission potential is found to increase in magnitude as the nucleus evolves towards scission, exemplifying the importance of stringent constraints on the isovector sector of the nuclear EDF for reliable predictions of fission properties.

## Key claim

The isovector component of the nuclear energy density functional (EDF) makes a substantial contribution to the fission potential of ²⁴⁰Pu, increasing in magnitude as the nucleus evolves toward scission, but the resulting total barrier heights show significant deviations from experimental values, particularly in the depth of the inner minimum.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock (CHF) calculations for ²⁴⁰Pu using the SLy4d Skyrme EDF with BCS pairing, decomposing the total energy into isoscalar and isovector components along the adiabatic fission path. The calculations used a three-dimensional solver with no symmetry restrictions (the VU-TDHF code) and a quadrupole constraint in steps of 100 fm² from the ground state (907 fm²) up to 12000 fm², allowing the system to find minimum energy configurations including triaxial and octupole deformations.

## Key numbers

- Inner fission barrier height: total potential gives 6.0 MeV, isoscalar-only gives 6.6 MeV; experimental value is approximately 6.05 MeV
- First minimum depth: total potential gives 0.82 MeV, isoscalar-only gives 2.2 MeV; experimental value is approximately 2.8 MeV
- Outer fission barrier height: total potential gives 3.9 MeV, isoscalar-only gives 6.0 MeV; experimental value is approximately 5.15 MeV
- Final fragment average mass and charge: light fragment A=107.71, Z=43.28; heavy fragment A=132.29, Z=50.72
- Ground state quadrupole deformation: 907 fm²; final separated fragments at R=17.7 fm
- Transition to octupole deformation occurs near q₂₀ = 4500 fm²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous microscopic decomposition of the EDF into isoscalar and isovector components, which was applied to fusion barriers in Godbey (2017), to the case of fission. The results show that the isovector contribution lowers the fission barrier throughout the path, similar to its role in N/Z equilibration during fusion described by Simenel (2020). The study enables future work on potential energy surfaces for multimodal fission and highlights that observables sensitive to scission dynamics, such as total kinetic energy, may provide stronger constraints on the isovector sector of the EDF than barrier heights alone.
