# Two-nucleon problem in semi-relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory

> We consider a symmetry-preserving approach to the nucleon-nucleon scattering problem in the framework of the higher-derivative formulation of baryon chiral perturbation theory. Within this framework the leading-order amplitude is calculated by solving renormalizable equations and corrections are taken into account perturbatively.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a symmetry-preserving, higher-derivative formulation of baryon chiral perturbation theory can produce a renormalizable leading-order nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude, with all higher-order corrections taken perturbatively, thereby avoiding the "consistency problems" previously claimed to plague Weinberg's program. The key qualifier is that the approach relies on a regulating function in the nucleon propagator that improves its ultraviolet behavior, and the renormalization scheme must be chosen appropriately (e.g., with a parameter L around 750 MeV for best leading-order description).

## Method

The authors introduce a regulating function F(L², p²) into the nucleon propagator to improve its UV behavior, then expand the scattering amplitude T, potential V, and two-nucleon propagator G order by order in small parameters (pion mass and small momenta). The leading-order amplitude T₀ is obtained by solving a renormalizable Lippmann-Schwinger-type equation in the center-of-mass frame, where the effective potential V₀ has a milder UV behavior than the standard potential. Higher-order corrections (NLO, NNLO, etc.) are then calculated perturbatively using T₀ as an effective NN vertex, with all divergences canceled by counter-terms present at the given order.

## Key numbers

- The best description of phase shifts at leading order is obtained for L ≈ 750 MeV (the regulating parameter in the nucleon propagator).
- The paper shows np phase shifts for the ³P₀ partial wave for L values ranging from 750 MeV to 500,000 MeV, compared to Nijmegen partial-wave analysis data.
- The leading-order equation in the center-of-mass frame involves an integral over d³k/(2π)³ with a propagator factor 1/(p² − k² + iε).

## Context

This work extends the higher-derivative formulation of baryon chiral perturbation theory (Djukanovic 2004) to the two-nucleon problem, directly addressing the "consistency problem" raised against Weinberg's original program (Weinberg 1990). It contrasts with the cutoff EFT approach advocated by Epelbaum (2005) and Lepage (1997), which treats the consistency problem as irrelevant. The approach enables systematic, order-by-order calculations of nucleon-nucleon scattering without requiring an infinite number of higher-order counter-terms, and can be extended to higher partial waves where the angular momentum barrier screens singular behavior.
