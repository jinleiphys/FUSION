# Long Range Structure of the Nucleon

> The long range structure of the nucleon is discussed starting from the old model of a quark bag with a pion cloud (``cloudy bag'') carrying on to the more recent ideas of the parton model of the nucleon. On the basis of the most recent measurements of the form factors at MAMI, JLab and MIT quantitative results for nucleon charge densities are presented within both non-relativistic and relativistic frameworks.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the long-range structure of the nucleon, particularly the contribution of the pion cloud to charge densities, can be extracted from electromagnetic form factors, but this interpretation is fundamentally limited by relativistic effects including Zitterbewegung and the inability to define a unique rest-frame density for a relativistic quantum system.

## Method

The authors review non-relativistic Fourier transform methods to extract three-dimensional charge densities from elastic electron scattering form factors, then introduce the light-front formalism using the Drell-Yan frame (q⁺ = 0) to define two-dimensional transverse quark charge densities via the positive-definite operator J⁺ = J⁰ + J³. They apply these methods to recent proton form factor data from MAMI (Bernauer et al., 2010) and neutron form factor data from MIT-Bates and Jefferson Laboratory, using empirical parametrizations to compute transverse charge densities for both unpolarized and transversely polarized nucleons.

## Key numbers

- Proton electric charge radius: √⟨r²⟩ = (0.879 ± 0.005_stat ± 0.004_syst ± 0.005_model) fm from MAMI (Bernauer et al., 2010)
- Earlier Stanford measurement gave √⟨r²⟩ = (0.805 ± 0.011) fm (Hand, 1963)
- Earlier Mainz measurement gave √⟨r²⟩ = (0.862 ± 0.012) fm (Simon, 1980)
- Muonic hydrogen Lamb shift measurement gave √⟨r²⟩ = (0.840 ± 0.001) fm (Pohl, 2010)
- Dipole form scale parameter Λ_D = 0.843 GeV, approximately equal to the ρ meson mass
- Confinement radius estimated from antiproton-proton annihilation: approximately 0.8 fm
- Pion Compton wavelength: approximately 1.4 fm
- Zitterbewegung resolution limit for nucleon: λ_lim ≈ 0.66 fm
- Structure observed in form factors at Q² ≈ 0.15 GeV²
- Neutron anomalous magnetic moment: μ_N = -1.91

## Context

This review extends the cloudy bag model and the earlier phenomenological analysis of Friedrich and Walcher (2003) which suggested a bump structure in nucleon form factors attributable to the pion cloud. The new high-precision MAMI data (Bernauer et al., 2010) show a larger proton charge radius than predicted by dispersion relations (Drechsel, 2007), while the inclusion of new MIT-Bates (Geis, 2008) and JLab (Riordan, 2010) neutron data makes the previously claimed pion-cloud bump disappear. The work enables future studies of generalized parton distributions and light-front wave functions to image quark charge densities, though the authors note that the five-standard-deviation discrepancy between electronic and muonic hydrogen measurements of the proton radius requires further theoretical work on the muonic Lamb shift calculations.
