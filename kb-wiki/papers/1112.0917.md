# Chirally motivated $\bar{K}N$ amplitudes for in-medium applications

> A new fit of a chirally motivated coupled-channels model for meson-baryon interactions is presented including the recent SIDDHARTA data on the 1s level characteristics of kaonic hydrogen. The kaon-nucleon amplitudes generated by the model are fully consistent with our earlier studies. We argue that a sharp increase of the real part of the in-medium $K^{-}p$ amplitude at subthreshold energies provides a link between the shallow $\bar{K}$-nuclear optical potentials obtained microscopically from threshold $\bar{K}N$ interactions and the phenomenological deep ones deduced from kaonic atoms data. The impact on the $A$-dependence of the $Λ$-hypernuclear formation rates measured in reactions with stopped kaons is discussed too.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the sharp increase of the real part of the in-medium $K^{-}p$ amplitude at subthreshold energies provides a link between shallow $\bar{K}$-nuclear optical potentials obtained microscopically from threshold $\bar{K}N$ interactions and the phenomenological deep ones deduced from kaonic atoms data, but this link depends on the specific energy shift from threshold to subthreshold energies rather than on the particular chiral model used.

## Method

The authors performed new fits of a chirally motivated coupled-channels separable-potential model for meson-baryon interactions, including the recent SIDDHARTA data on kaonic hydrogen 1s level characteristics. They considered both a leading-order (LO) Tomozawa-Weinberg interaction (TW1 model) and a model including next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections (NLO30 model), fitting to 15 experimental data points (10 cross sections, 3 branching ratios, 2 kaonic hydrogen characteristics) with 1 free parameter for TW1 and 7 free parameters for NLO30. The in-medium amplitudes were computed self-consistently including Pauli blocking and meson/baryon self-energies in the intermediate state Green's function.

## Key numbers

- Kaonic hydrogen 1s level shift: TW1 model gives 323 eV, NLO30 model gives 310 eV, experimental value is 283 +/- 42 eV
- Kaonic hydrogen 1s level width: TW1 model gives 659 eV, NLO30 model gives 607 eV, experimental value is 541 +/- 111 eV
- Threshold branching ratios: TW1 model gives gamma = 2.36, R_c = 0.636, R_n = 0.183; NLO30 model gives gamma = 2.37, R_c = 0.660, R_n = 0.191; experimental values are gamma = 2.36 +/- 0.04, R_c = 0.664 +/- 0.011, R_n = 0.189 +/- 0.015
- Chi-squared per data point: TW1 model gives 3.6, NLO30 model gives 0.61, CS30 model gives 0.78 when fitted to SIDDHARTA data
- Inverse range parameters for NLO30 model: alpha_piLambda = 297 MeV, alpha_piSigma = 491 MeV, alpha_KbarN = 700 MeV
- I=0 S-matrix pole positions for NLO30 model: z_1 = (1355, -86) MeV, z_2 = (1418, -44) MeV
- The subthreshold increase of in-medium K^-p attraction occurs at energies about 30 MeV below the KbarN threshold

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier studies (Cieply and Smejkal, 2010) by incorporating new SIDDHARTA data on kaonic hydrogen (2011) and adopting an alternate NLO formulation following Fettes (2000). The results are consistent with the earlier CS30 model fitted to DEAR data (2005) and with the IHW model (2011). The paper enables improved calculations of kaonic atom bound states and Lambda-hypernuclear formation rates in (K_stop-, pi-) reactions measured by the FINUDA collaboration (2011), showing that energy-dependent branching ratios significantly alter the A-dependence of production rates compared to constant threshold values.
