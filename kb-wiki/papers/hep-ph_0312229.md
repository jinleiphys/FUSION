# Spin asymmetries in gamma + N --> K*bar + Theta^+

> The photoproduction processes of the exotic $Θ^+(1540)$ baryon and the $\bar{K}^*$ meson from the nucleon targets, i.e., $γn \to K^{*-} Θ^+$ and $γp \to \bar{K}^{*0} Θ^+$, are investigated in a hadronic model. We consider $K$ and $K^*$ exchanges as well as the $s$ and $u$ channel nucleon and $Θ$ terms. Various spin asymmetries together with cross sections are first computed in order to study the production mechanisms and the parity of the $Θ^+(1540)$ baryon. Within the uncertainties arising from the model-dependence of the production mechanisms and several coupling constants, we find that some target-recoil double spin asymmetries, $C^{\rm TR}_{xx'}$ and $C^{\rm TR}_{xz'}$, are sensitive to the parity of $Θ^+$. In addition, the parity asymmetry of this reaction on the neutron target, which can be obtained by analyzing $K^*$ decay distribution, is found to be useful to estimate the $K^* N Θ$ coupling.

## Key claim

Within the uncertainties of the hadronic model and several coupling constants, the target-recoil double spin asymmetries \(C^{\rm TR}_{xx'}\) and \(C^{\rm TR}_{xz'}\) in the \(\gamma N \to \overline{K}^* \Theta^+\) reaction are sensitive to the parity of the \(\Theta^+(1540)\) baryon, and the parity asymmetry on the neutron target can be used to estimate the \(K^* N \Theta\) coupling.

## Method

The authors computed tree-level Feynman diagrams for \(\gamma n \to K^{*-} \Theta^+\) and \(\gamma p \to \bar{K}^{*0} \Theta^+\) including \(K\) and \(K^*\) exchanges in the \(t\)-channel, and nucleon and \(\Theta\) terms in the \(s\)- and \(u\)-channels. Effective Lagrangians derived from SU(3) symmetry were used with coupling constants fixed from the \(\Theta^+\) decay width (assumed 1 MeV) and quark model ratios for \(g_{K^* N \Theta}/g_{KN\Theta}\). Form factors with a cutoff \(\Lambda = 1.8\) GeV were applied, and the results were restricted to forward scattering angles (\(\theta < 90^\circ\)) to minimize uncertainties from neglected higher resonances.

## Key numbers

- \(\Theta^+(1540)\) decay width assumed as \(\Gamma(\Theta) = 1\) MeV, yielding \(g_{KN\Theta} = 0.984\) for even-parity \(\Theta^+\) and \(g_{KN\Theta} = 0.137\) for odd-parity \(\Theta^+\)
- Ratio \(g_{K^* N \Theta}/g_{KN\Theta} = \sqrt{3}\) for even-parity \(\Theta^+\) and \(1/\sqrt{3}\) for odd-parity \(\Theta^+\)
- \(K^* K \gamma\) couplings: \(g^c_{K^*K\gamma} = 0.254\) GeV\(^{-1}\) for charged decay, \(g^0_{K^*K\gamma} = -0.388\) GeV\(^{-1}\) for neutral decay
- Cutoff parameter \(\Lambda = 1.8\) GeV used for form factors
- Cross sections for odd-parity \(\Theta^+\) production are much smaller than those for even-parity \(\Theta^+\) production
- Differential cross sections computed at photon energy \(E_\gamma = 2.8\) GeV and 3.0 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of \(\Theta^+\) photoproduction by Oh, Kim, and Lee (2003) that considered \(\gamma N \to \overline{K} \Theta^+\) reactions, now including the \(\overline{K}^*\) final state to access additional spin observables. The calculations are based on the SU(3) symmetric Lagrangian for baryon antidecuplet interactions developed by Oh, Kim, and Lee (2003). The results are intended to guide experiments at Jefferson Lab and SPring-8 in determining the parity of the \(\Theta^+(1540)\), which remains contested between even-parity predictions from soliton models and diquark models (Karliner and Lipkin 2003; Jaffe and Wilczek 2003) and odd-parity predictions from quark potential models and QCD sum rules (Zhu 2003).
