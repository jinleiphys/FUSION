# Isotropization and hydrodynamization in weakly coupled heavy-ion collisions

> We numerically solve 2+1D effective kinetic theory of weak coupling QCD under longitudinal expansion relevant for early stages of heavy-ion collisions. We find agreement with viscous hydrodynamics and classical Yang-Mills simulations in the regimes where they are applicable. By choosing initial conditions that are motivated by color-glass-condensate framework we find that for Q=2GeV and $α_s$=0.3 the system is approximately described by viscous hydrodynamics well before $τ\lesssim 1.0$ fm/c.

## Key claim

For a realistic coupling of α_s ≈ 0.3 (λ = 10) and initial conditions motivated by the color-glass-condensate framework with Q_s = 2 GeV, the system is approximately described by viscous hydrodynamics well before τ ≲ 1.0 fm/c, even though the ratio of transverse to longitudinal pressures is still as large as P_T/P_L ≈ 5 at that time.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the 2+1D effective kinetic theory (EKT) of weakly coupled QCD under longitudinal expansion, using the Boltzmann equation with effective 2↔2 scattering and 1↔2 splitting terms to leading order in the 't Hooft coupling λ. They discretize the gluon distribution function on a 2D grid in momentum magnitude and angular direction, with at least 100 points in p and 250 in x_p, and use Monte Carlo integration for the collision integrals. Screening is treated isotropically using a Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) inspired prescription, and the system is initialized at Q_s τ = 1 with an anisotropic distribution parametrized by ξ = 4 or 10, matching the comoving energy density from classical Yang-Mills simulations.

## Key numbers

- For λ = 10 (α_s ≈ 0.3) and Q_s = 2 GeV, the system is described by viscous hydrodynamics to better than 10% accuracy in energy density after Q_s τ ∼ 5.0 (τ ∼ 0.5 fm/c).
- For λ = 10, even first-order hydrodynamics describes the evolution from Q_s τ ∼ 10 (τ ∼ 1 fm/c for Q_s = 2.0 GeV) where P_T/P_L ≈ 5.
- For λ = 1, hydrodynamical behavior is reached only at Q_s τ ∼ 2000.
- The classical scaling solution (λ = 0) is reached after Q_s τ ∼ 15, with distribution scaling as f(p_z, p_⊥, τ) = (Q_s τ)^{-2/3} f_S((Q_s τ)^{1/3} p_z, p_⊥).
- The gluon liberation coefficient from Lappi (2011) is c ≈ 1.25 and mean transverse momentum ⟨p_T⟩ ≈ 1.8 Q_s, extracted at Q_s τ = 12 from 2D classical Yang-Mills simulation.

## Context

This work bridges the gap between classical Yang-Mills simulations of the early over-occupied gluon stage and viscous hydrodynamics at later times, extending the proof-of-principle studies by Kurkela (2012), York (2014), and Kurkela (2014) from isotropic non-expanding settings to a boost-invariant 2+1D expanding geometry. It confirms the parametric picture of bottom-up thermalization by Baier (2000) but finds that the predicted scaling Q_s τ ∼ λ^{-13/5} sets in only at significantly smaller couplings than λ = 1. The results demonstrate that the robustness of hydrodynamics under large anisotropy, previously shown at strong coupling by Chesler and Yaffe (2010) using AdS/CFT, also holds in the weak coupling picture extrapolated to intermediate couplings relevant for heavy-ion collisions.
