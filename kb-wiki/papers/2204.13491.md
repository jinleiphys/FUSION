# First concurrent extraction of the leading-order scalar and spin proton polarizabilities

> We performed the first simultaneous extraction of the six leading-order proton polarizabilities. We reached this milestone thanks to both new high-quality experimental data and an innovative bootstrap-base fitting method. These new results provide a self-consistent and fundamental benchmark for all future theoretical and experimental polarizability estimates.

## Key claim

The authors performed the first simultaneous extraction of all six leading-order static proton polarizabilities without fixing any of them to theoretical predictions or external constraints, using a bootstrap-based fitting method combined with fixed-t subtracted dispersion relations and three different partial-wave analyses to evaluate model dependence.

## Method

The authors fitted 388 data points from 25 datasets of unpolarized differential cross sections and polarization asymmetries (Sigma_2x, Sigma_2z, Sigma_3) for real Compton scattering on the proton, using a bootstrap-based Monte Carlo method that generates N=10^4 replicas of the experimental database. The theoretical framework employed fixed-t subtracted dispersion relations with pion photoproduction amplitudes from three different partial-wave analyses (BnGa-2019, MAID-2021, SAID-MA19) as input. The LARA and LEGS unpolarized cross section datasets were excluded due to inconsistency, while LEGS asymmetry data were retained.

## Key numbers

- alpha_E1 + beta_M1 = (15.1 +/- 0.7 (fit) +/- 0.1 (model)) x 10^-4 fm^3
- alpha_E1 - beta_M1 = (10.3 +/- 1.2 (fit) +/- 0.2 (model)) x 10^-4 fm^3
- alpha_E1 = (12.7 +/- 0.8 (fit) +/- 0.1 (model)) x 10^-4 fm^3
- beta_M1 = (2.4 +/- 0.6 (fit) +/- 0.1 (model)) x 10^-4 fm^3
- gamma_E1E1 = (-3.0 +/- 0.6 (fit) +/- 0.4 (model)) x 10^-4 fm^4
- gamma_M1M1 = (3.7 +/- 0.5 (fit) +/- 0.1 (model)) x 10^-4 fm^4
- gamma_E1M2 = (-1.2 +/- 1.0 (fit) +/- 0.3 (model)) x 10^-4 fm^4
- gamma_M1E2 = (2.0 +/- 0.7 (fit) +/- 0.4 (model)) x 10^-4 fm^4
- Reduced chi-squared of the fit: chi^2_red = 1.13
- Fit p-value = 24%

## Context

This work extends previous extractions by Pasquini et al. (2019) and the A2 Collaboration (2019, 2021) that had to fix some polarizabilities to theoretical values from heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (McGovern et al., 2012) or baryon chiral perturbation theory (Lensky et al., 2014), or to PDG values. The new results provide a self-consistent benchmark for all future theoretical and experimental polarizability estimates, though the authors note that correlations remain between gamma_M1M1 and gamma_pi (rho ~ 0.80), indicating the need for further dedicated measurements.
