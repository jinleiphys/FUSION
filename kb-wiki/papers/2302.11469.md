# Frequencies of $f$- and $p$-oscillation modes in cold and hot compact stars

> A large collection of equations of state (EOSs) built within the covariant density functional (CDF) theory of hadronic matter and allowing for density dependent (DD) couplings is employed to study polar $f$- and $p$- oscillations of cold and hot compact stars. Correlations between oscillation frequencies of cold purely nucleonic neutron stars (NSs), their global parameters as well as properties of nuclear matter (NM) are investigated by considering a set of models from Beznogov and Raduta, [Phys.~Rev.~C 107, 045803 (2023)], where a number of constraints on the saturation properties of NM, pure neutron matter (PNM) and the lower bound of the maximal NS mass were imposed within a Bayesian framework. The roles of finite temperature and exotic particle degrees of freedom, e.g., hyperons, $Δ$-resonances, anti-kaon condensates or a hadron to quark phase transition, are addressed by employing a family of models publicly available on \textsc{CompOSE} and assuming idealized profiles of temperature or entropy per baryon and charge fraction. We find that finite temperature effects reduce the oscillation frequencies of nucleonic stars while the opposite effect is obtained for stars with exotic particle degrees of freedom. When the $Γ$-law is employed to build finite temperature EOSs, errors in estimating oscillation modes frequencies are of the order of 10\% to 30\%, depending on the mass. Throughout this work the Cowling approximation is used.

## Key claim

The paper finds that finite temperature effects reduce the oscillation frequencies of purely nucleonic stars, while the opposite effect (increased frequencies) occurs for stars containing exotic particle degrees of freedom such as hyperons, Delta resonances, anti-kaon condensates, or quark matter. However, these results are obtained under the Cowling approximation and using idealized profiles of temperature or entropy per baryon and charge fraction, not realistic proto-neutron star cooling simulations.

## Method

The authors computed polar f-mode and p-mode oscillation frequencies for cold and hot compact stars by solving the linearized perturbation equations within the Cowling approximation. They employed a large set of equations of state built within covariant density functional theory with density-dependent couplings, including the DDB* Bayesian family for cold nucleonic stars and multiple DD2-based models from the CompOSE database for hot stars with various exotic degrees of freedom. Correlations between oscillation frequencies and nuclear matter properties were analyzed using Kendall rank correlation coefficients for the DDB* family.

## Key numbers

- For cold purely nucleonic neutron stars, f-mode frequencies range between approximately 1 kHz and 3 kHz, while p1-mode frequencies range between approximately 4 kHz and 8 kHz
- When the Gamma-law is used to build finite temperature equations of state, errors in estimating oscillation mode frequencies are of the order of 10% to 30%, depending on the stellar mass
- The frequency range of cold beta-equilibrated hypernuclear stars computed within linearized general relativity is 1.47 kHz ≤ ν_f ≤ 2.45 kHz
- For the DDB* family, the median saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter is 0.154 fm^{-3} with 68% confidence interval +0.0047/-0.0048
- The median Dirac effective mass of nucleons at saturation for DDB* is 0.662 m_n with 68% confidence interval +0.039/-0.042
- The median symmetry energy slope L for DDB* is 44.6 MeV with 68% confidence interval +6/-5.7 MeV
- For the Gamma-law approximation at (S/A=2, Y_Q=0.2), radius uncertainties are 23%, 13%, and 9% for models with M/M_sun = 1, 1.4, and 2 respectively

## Context

This work extends the systematic investigation of nuclear saturation parameter effects on f-mode frequencies by Jaiswal (2021) and the study of correlations between nuclear matter properties and oscillation modes by Kunjipurayil (2022). It also builds upon studies of hyperon effects on f-mode oscillations by Pradhan (2021, 2022) and proto-neutron star oscillation studies by Ferrari (2003), Burgio (2011), and Camelio (2017). The use of the DDB* Bayesian family from Beznogov and Raduta (2023) allows for statistically meaningful correlation analysis, while the controlled comparison of models with identical nucleonic interactions enables discrimination between thermal and composition effects on oscillation frequencies.
