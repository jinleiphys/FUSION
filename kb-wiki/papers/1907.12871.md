# Dipole evolution: perspectives for collectivity and $γ^*$A collisions

> The transverse, spatial structure of protons is an area revealing fundamental properties of matter, and provides key input for deeper understanding of emerging collective phenomena in high energy collisions of protons, as well as collisions of heavy ions. In this paper eccentricities and eccentricity fluctuations are predicted using the dipole formulation of BFKL evolution. Furthermore, first steps are taken towards generation of fully exclusive final states of $γ^*$A collisions, by assessing the importance of colour fluctuations in the initial state. Such steps are crucial for the preparation of event generators for a future electron-ion collider. Due to the connection between an impact parameter picture of the proton structure, and cross sections of ep and pp collisions, the model parameters can be fully determined by fits to such quantities, leaving results as real predictions of the model.

## Key claim

The paper predicts eccentricities and eccentricity fluctuations in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions using the dipole formulation of BFKL evolution, with the hedge that these predictions are made possible because model parameters are fully determined by fits to total and semi-inclusive cross sections of ep and pp collisions, leaving the eccentricity results as genuine predictions rather than fits to flow data.

## Method

The authors implement a Monte Carlo version of Mueller's dipole model, evolving an initial state (a single dipole for virtual photons or three dipoles in an equilateral triangle for protons) in impact parameter space and rapidity according to the BFKL splitting kernel. After evolution, dipole-dipole interactions are calculated via two-gluon exchange, and the unitarized scattering amplitude is obtained by exponentiating the sum of individual dipole-dipole cross sections, resumming multi-Pomeron exchanges. Energy-momentum conservation, recoil effects, and a confinement scale (implemented as a fictitious gluon mass) are included as corrections beyond leading order, while saturation effects in the cascade itself are not treated.

## Key numbers

- The dipole-dipole cross section is proportional to alpha_s^2, with the explicit form f_ij = (alpha_s^2/2) log^2[(r_13 r_24)/(r_14 r_23)] in the large-N_c limit.
- The confinement scale is implemented by replacing 1/p_g^2 in the Coulomb propagator with 1/(p_g^2 + M_g^2), where M_g is a fictitious gluon mass.
- For parton-level eccentricity calculations, contributions are weighted by a factor pT/(pT + p_perp_min) with p_perp_min = 0.1 GeV to ensure infrared safety.
- The photon wave function includes the three lightest (massless) quarks, with longitudinal and transverse components given by modified Bessel functions K_0 and K_1.

## Context

This work extends the Mueller dipole model approach previously implemented in the Oedipus code (Salam, 1996) and the DIPSY code (Avsar et al., 2005; Flensburg, 2011), as well as a private implementation by Kovalenko et al. (2012). It differs from the DIPSY approach by not including the swing mechanism for sub-leading N_c effects in the cascade, instead including only minimal sub-leading corrections (energy-momentum conservation and confinement). The implementation within the Pythia event generator framework enables future public availability and integration with the Angantyr framework (Bierlich et al., 2016, 2018) for generating exclusive final states of gamma*A collisions, which is crucial for preparing event generators for a future electron-ion collider.
