# Virtual photon fragmentation functions

> We introduce operator definitions for virtual photon fragmentation functions, which are needed for reliable calculations of Drell-Yan transverse momentum ($Q_T$) distributions when $Q_T$ is much larger than the invariant mass $Q$. We derive the evolution equations for these fragmentation functions. We calculate the leading order evolution kernels for partons to fragment into a unpolarized as well as a polarized virtual photon. We find that fragmentation functions to a longitudinally polarized virtual photon are most important at small $z$, and the fragmentation functions to a transversely polarized virtual photon dominate the large $z$ region. We discuss the implications of this finding to the J/$ψ$ mesons' polarization at large transverse momentum.

## Key claim

The authors introduce operator definitions for virtual photon fragmentation functions that are needed for reliable calculations of Drell-Yan transverse momentum distributions when $Q_T$ is much larger than the invariant mass $Q$, and they derive the corresponding evolution equations. The abstract glosses over the condition that these fragmentation functions are only well-defined and physically meaningful when the virtual photon decays into a completely measured physical state, and that they are fully perturbative only when $Q \gg \Lambda_{\rm QCD}$.

## Method

The authors derive cut-vertex and operator definitions for parton-to-virtual-photon fragmentation functions in the light-cone gauge, then derive renormalization group evolution equations for these functions. They calculate the leading order evolution kernels for both unpolarized and polarized (transverse and longitudinal) virtual photon fragmentation functions, and solve the evolution equations numerically in $z$-space using boundary conditions that require $\mu_F^2 \geq Q^2/z$.

## Key numbers

- Quark-to-virtual-photon fragmentation functions are plotted at fragmentation scales $\mu_F = 10$ GeV and $\mu_F = 50$ GeV for virtual photon invariant mass $Q = 5$ GeV, with quark fractional charge $e_q = 2/3$
- At $Q = 1.5$ GeV and $\mu_F = 50$ GeV, QCD evolved fragmentation functions show enhanced contributions compared to $Q = 5$ GeV, particularly in the small $z$ region
- Gluon-to-virtual-photon fragmentation functions at $Q = 5$ GeV are about two orders of magnitude smaller than quark-to-virtual-photon fragmentation functions at $\mu_F = 10$ GeV, and about one order of magnitude smaller at $\mu_F = 50$ GeV
- The lowest order quark-to-longitudinally-polarized-virtual-photon fragmentation function contains a factor $2(1-z)/z$ multiplied by $(1 - Q^2/(z\mu_F^2))$

## Context

This work extends the concept of real photon fragmentation functions (Owens 1987, Aurenche 1999) to virtual photons, motivated by the proposal of Berger, Gordon, and Klasen (1999) that Drell-Yan $Q_T$ distributions provide an advantageous source of gluon distribution information free from the isolation complications of prompt photon production. The authors note that the virtual photon polarization measurements at high $Q_T$ could serve as a reference process to test models of J/$\psi$ formation, particularly in light of CDF data on J/$\psi$ polarization that appear inconsistent with various production models. The results enable resummation of large logarithms $\ln(Q_T^2/Q^2)$ in Drell-Yan production at high $Q_T$.
