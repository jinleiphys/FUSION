# Proton and Neutron Elastic Scattering on He Targets from $\textit{Ab Initio}$ SA-NCSM Optical Potentials

> We construct and discuss $\textit{ab initio}$ nucleon-nucleus optical potentials at low energies for $^{3,4,6}$He targets. In this work, we use the $\textit{ab initio}$ SA-NCSM/GF approach that combines the $\textit{ab initio}$ symmetry-adapted no-core shell model with the Green's function technique to construct optical potentials, and extend this formulation to proton scattering and targets with nonzero spin. We show that these optical potentials reproduce experimental differential cross sections and phase shifts for proton and neutron elastic scattering remarkably well. The $\textit{ab initio}$ SA-NCSM/GF approach provides nonlocal, energy dependent and dispersive optical potentials, suitable for the astrophysically relevant regime of low energies and for exotic nuclei, where experiments are difficult and data is often unavailable.

## Key claim

The SA-NCSM/GF approach, extended to proton scattering and targets with nonzero spin, produces ab initio optical potentials that reproduce experimental differential cross sections and phase shifts for elastic scattering on He isotopes, with the caveat that the calculations use extrapolated energies from finite model spaces and a range of harmonic oscillator parameters to estimate uncertainties.

## Method

The authors combine the symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) with the Green's function technique to construct nonlocal, energy-dependent optical potentials for nucleon-nucleus elastic scattering. They calculate the Green's function from one-body overlaps between the A-body target ground state and the (A±1)-body eigenstates, then invert it to obtain the inter-cluster nuclear potential, which is used in a coordinate-space Schrödinger equation solved via the R-matrix approach. The calculations use the NNLO_opt chiral potential and extrapolate ground-state energies to the infinite model space limit across a range of harmonic oscillator energy parameters.

## Key numbers

- For the ²P₃/₂ phase shift in ⁴He(p,p)⁴He at ħΩ = 12 MeV, the threshold energy agrees with experiment to within 70 keV.
- The ⁷He lowest resonance (3/2⁻) lies only 445 keV above the single neutron threshold, and the calculated peak location in neutron-⁶He elastic scattering closely agrees with this resonance energy.
- Differential cross sections are calculated for projectile laboratory kinetic energies from 2 to 16.4 MeV for ⁴He(n,n)⁴He, ⁴He(p,p)⁴He, and ³He(p,p)³He.
- The ⁴He(p,p)⁴He optical potentials show almost no energy dependence for ECM < 12 MeV across all partial waves studied (S₁/₂, P₁/₂, P₃/₂, D₃/₂, D₅/₂, F₅/₂, F₇/₂, G₇/₂).

## Context

This work extends the SA-NCSM/GF framework previously applied to neutron scattering on ⁴He (Burrows 2024) to proton scattering and targets with nonzero spin, including the open-shell halo nucleus ⁶He. The results agree with earlier NCSM/RGM calculations (Quaglioni 2009) for most phase shifts in ⁴He(p,p)⁴He, while improving the ²P₃/₂ phase shift that was underestimated in that earlier work. The approach enables ab initio optical potentials for exotic nuclei where experimental data is unavailable, relevant for astrophysical reaction rates and rare isotope beam experiments.
