# Analytical approach to the Bose polaron problem in one dimension

> We discuss the ground state properties of a one-dimensional bosonic system doped with an impurity (the so-called Bose polaron problem). We introduce a formalism that allows us to calculate analytically the thermodynamic zero-temperature properties of this system with weak and moderate boson-boson interaction strengths for any boson-impurity interaction. Our approach is validated by comparing to exact quantum Monte Carlo calculations. In addition, we test the method in finite size systems using numerical results based upon the similarity renormalization group. We argue that the introduced approach provides a simple analytical tool for studies of strongly interacting impurity problems in one dimension.

## Key claim

The authors introduce an analytical mean-field formalism, based on a Gross-Pitaevskii equation in the impurity rest frame, that can calculate the ground-state energy and structural properties of a one-dimensional Bose polaron for weak to moderate boson-boson interactions and any boson-impurity interaction strength, but the approach is limited to systems where the effective interaction parameter γ/κ ≲ 1.

## Method

The authors transform the many-body Schrödinger equation into a frame where all distances are measured from the impurity (analogous to a Lee-Low-Pines transformation), then apply a product ansatz for the bosonic wavefunction to derive a real Gross-Pitaevskii equation (GPE) for the boson field. They solve this GPE analytically using Jacobi elliptic functions in the thermodynamic limit and for finite particle numbers, obtaining closed-form expressions for the impurity energy and contact parameter. The analytical results are validated by comparison with quantum Monte Carlo calculations from Giorgini (2016) and with numerical solutions from a similarity renormalization group flow equation method.

## Key numbers

- For small boson-impurity coupling c, the impurity energy is ε ≃ cρ (first-order perturbation result), valid when c sets the smallest energy scale.
- For large c, the impurity energy approaches ε ≃ ρ² √(16γ/(9κ)), where γ = g/ρ is the dimensionless boson-boson interaction strength and κ = m/(1+m) is the reduced mass.
- The contact parameter C = tanh²(d), where d = (1/2) asinh((2ρ/c)√(γ/κ)), ranges from 1 at c=0 to 0 at 1/c=0.
- The overlap S at 1/c=0 reaches a minimum value of e^{(2-4ln2)/√(γκ)}.
- For finite N with impenetrable impurity (1/c=0) and equal masses, the energy converges to the thermodynamic value ε∞ = ρ²√(16γ/(9κ)) as N increases.

## Context

This work extends the analytical understanding of the Bose polaron problem in one dimension, complementing exact solutions available for identical masses (McGuire, 1965) and infinitely heavy impurities (Giorgini, 2016). The approach fills the gap between these exactly solvable limits and provides a simple analytical tool for studying strongly interacting impurity problems, enabling future investigations of attractive boson-impurity interactions, time-dependent dynamics after quenches, and extensions to higher spatial dimensions.
