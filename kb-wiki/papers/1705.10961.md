# Taking into Account the Centre-of-Mass Correlations in the Cross Sections for Elastic Scattering of Intermediate Energy Protons on the Exotic Nuclei $^6$He and $^8$He

> We calculate the differential cross sections for proton elastic scattering on the exotic halo nuclei $^6$He and $^8$He at energies around $\sim$0.7 GeV at the momentum transfers squared up to 0.30 (GeV/$c$)$^2$ and investigate the influence of the nucleon centre-of-mass correlations on the calculated cross sections. In particular, we show that the approximate account of the centre-of-mass correlations used previously considerably overestimates the cross sections at high values of the momentum transfer.

# Taking into Account the Centre-of-Mass Correlations in the Cross Sections for Elastic Scattering of Intermediate Energy Protons on the Exotic Nuclei $^6$He and $^8$He

## Key claim

The approximate correction factor for centre-of-mass correlations used in prior analyses of proton elastic scattering on $^6$He and $^8$He (e.g., Alkhazov et al. (2002), Dobrovolsky et al. (2006), Ilieva et al. (2012), and Le Xuan Chung et al. (2015)) significantly overestimates the calculated differential cross sections at momentum transfers squared $|t| > \sim 0.10$ (GeV/$c$)$^2$, particularly in the region of the first diffraction minimum and second diffraction maximum, compared to an exact treatment of the centre-of-mass correlations.

## Method

The authors calculate differential cross sections for proton elastic scattering on $^6$He and $^8$He using the Glauber multiple-scattering theory, comparing three treatments of centre-of-mass correlations: neglecting them entirely, including them via an approximate multiplicative correction factor $H_0(\mathbf{q}) = \exp[\mathbf{q}^2 R_m^2 / 6(A-1)]$, and including them exactly by treating the nucleus as separate core and halo clusters with Gaussian density distributions and applying correction factors to each cluster's scattering amplitude before combining them. The calculations use nuclear radii and proton-nucleon scattering parameters taken from Le Xuan Chung et al. (2015), with the internal core size fixed to that of $^4$He ($R^*_c = 1.46$ fm) from Lu et al. (2013).

## Key numbers

- Proton energy: 717 MeV for $p$-$^6$He scattering; 674 MeV for $p$-$^8$He scattering.
- Momentum transfer range studied: $\sim 0 < |t| < 0.30$ (GeV/$c$)$^2$.
- Nuclear rms radii used (from Le Xuan Chung et al. (2015)): for $^6$He, $R_c = 1.96$ fm and $R_h = 3.30$ fm; for $^8$He, $R_c = 1.81$ fm and $R_h = 3.12$ fm.
- Internal core size $R^*_c = 1.46$ fm (from Lu et al. (2013)).
- Proton-proton scattering parameters for $p$-$^6$He: $\sigma_{pp} = 44.6$ mb, $\beta_{pp} = 0.20$ fm$^2$, $\epsilon_{pp} = 0.069$; for $p$-$^8$He: $\sigma_{pp} = 41.9$ mb, $\beta_{pp} = 0.20$ fm$^2$, $\epsilon_{pp} = 0.129$.
- Proton-neutron scattering parameters for $p$-$^6$He: $\sigma_{pn} = 37.7$ mb, $\beta_{pn} = 0.24$ fm$^2$, $\epsilon_{pn} = -0.307$; for $p$-$^8$He: $\sigma_{pn} = 37.4$ mb, $\beta_{pn} = 0.24$ fm$^2$, $\epsilon_{pn} = -0.283$.

## Context

This work extends the Glauber-theory analyses of proton elastic scattering on $^6$He and $^8$He previously performed by Neumaier et al. (2002), Alkhazov et al. (2002), Dobrovolsky et al. (2006), Ilieva et al. (2012), and Le Xuan Chung et al. (2015), which used an approximate centre-of-mass correction factor. The authors demonstrate that this approximation is inadequate for light halo nuclei at high momentum transfers, and that an exact treatment of centre-of-mass correlations is necessary for reliable extraction of nuclear core and halo radii. The work motivates the need for new experimental data of better quality at high $|t|$ values and for theoretical analyses with accurate centre-of-mass correlations to determine the core sizes and radial shapes of these exotic nuclei.
