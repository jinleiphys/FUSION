# Parity and Time Reversal Invariance Violation in Neutron-Nucleus Scattering

> Parity violating (PV) as well as parity and time-reversal invariance violating (PTRIV) effects are enhanced a million times in neutron reactions near p-wave compound resonances. We present the calculation of such effects using a statistical theory based on the properties of chaotic eigenstates and discuss a possibility to extract the strength constants of PTRIV interactions from the experimental data, including nucleon-nucleon and pion-nucleon CP-violating interactions, the QCD theta-term and the quark chromo-EDM. PV effects have random sign for all target nuclei except for 232Th, where PV effects of a positive sign have been observed for ten statistically significant p-wave resonances, with energy smaller than 250 eV. This may be an indication of a possible regular (non-chaotic) contribution to PV effects. We link this regular effect to the doublets of opposite parity states in the rotation spectra of nuclei with an octupole deformation and suggest other target nuclei where this hypothesis may be tested. We also discuss a permanent sign contribution produced by doorway states. An estimate of the ratio of PTRIV effects to PV effects is presented. Although a polarised target is not needed for the measurement of PV effects, for the interpretation of the results, it may be convenient to do both PV and PTRIV experiments with a polarised target.

## Key claim

Parity-violating (PV) effects in neutron-nucleus scattering near p-wave compound resonances exhibit random sign for all target nuclei except ²³²Th, where ten statistically significant p-wave resonances below 250 eV all show positive sign, suggesting a possible regular (non-chaotic) contribution that may be linked to octupole deformation doublets rather than purely statistical fluctuations.

## Method

The authors develop a statistical theory based on chaotic eigenstate properties to calculate root-mean-squared matrix elements of PV and parity-and-time-reversal-invariance-violating (PTRIV) interactions in neutron-nucleus scattering. They derive general expressions for the forward scattering amplitude including parity violation, incorporating the mixing of s-wave and p-wave compound resonances via the weak interaction, and present the angular coefficients for both polarised and unpolarised targets. The theory is applied to analyse the permanent sign effect observed in ²³²Th, examining contributions from octupole deformation doublets and doorway states.

## Key numbers

- PV effects are enhanced by a factor of approximately 10⁶ in neutron reactions near p-wave compound resonances, arising from dynamical enhancement (factor ~10³ from small energy intervals between opposite parity states) and kinematic enhancement (factor ~10³ from the ratio √(Γ_sⁿ/Γ_pⁿ))
- Ten statistically significant p-wave resonances in ²³²Th with energies below 250 eV all show positive sign PV effects
- The interval between compound resonances with the same spin and parity is d ≈ 20 eV
- The spreading width of doorway states is Γ_d ∼ 100 keV, with distance between doorway states D ∼ 300 keV
- Energy splitting of opposite parity doublet states in nuclei with octupole deformation typically ranges from 25 keV to 400 keV in the ground state
- In nuclei with zero spin and octupole deformation, the negative parity rotational band is separated from the positive parity band by roughly 0.1–1 MeV
- The typical excitation energy of octupole vibration in most nuclei is ∼ 2.5–3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the statistical theory of chaotic compound states developed by Flambaum and collaborators (1993–1997) to calculate PV and PTRIV matrix elements, building on the prediction by Sushkov (1980) and Flambaum (1984) of million-fold enhancement of PV effects in neutron reactions. The paper addresses the anomalous constant-sign PV effect in ²³²Th first reported by experiments at Dubna and later reviewed by Mitchell (2001), proposing octupole deformation doublets as an explanation alternative to doorway state mechanisms suggested by Hussein (1995) and Feshbach (1996). The results enable extraction of strength constants for nucleon-nucleon and pion-nucleon CP-violating interactions, the QCD theta-term, and quark chromo-EDM from future PTRIV experiments, particularly if measurements focus on nuclei with octupole deformation where a permanent sign effect may reduce the number of resonances needed for analysis.
