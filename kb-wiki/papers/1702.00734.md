# Lambda-Nucleon and Sigma-Nucleon interactions from lattice QCD with physical masses

> We present our recent study on baryon-baryon ($BB$) interactions from lattice QCD with almost physical quark masses corresponding to $(m_π,m_K)\approx(146,525)$ MeV and large volume $(La)^4=(96a)^4\approx$ (8.1 fm)$^4$. In order to perform a comprehensive study of $BB$ interactions based on lattice QCD calculation with almost physical masses and to make better use of such large scale computer resources, a large number of $BB$ interactions from $NN$ to $ΞΞ$ are calculated simultaneously. In this report, we focus on the strangeness $S=-1$ channels of the hyperon interactions by means of HAL QCD method. The coupled-channel HAL QCD method is briefly outlined. The snapshots of central and tensor potentials in $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1-^3D_1$ channels are presented for $ΛN$, $ΣN$ (both the isospin $I=1/2, 3/2$) and their coupled-channel systems.

## Key claim

The paper presents preliminary lattice QCD results for $\Lambda N$ and $\Sigma N$ potentials at nearly physical quark masses, but the potentials are obtained at earlier time slices ($t-t_0=5-12$) than the ideal regime ($t-t_0 \gg 1/m_\pi \sim 1.4$ fm), making these snapshots preliminary rather than final quantitative results.

## Method

The authors employ the HAL QCD method, which extracts baryon-baryon potentials from the equal-time Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave function measured on lattice QCD configurations. They use $N_f=2+1$ gauge configurations on a $96^4$ lattice with the Iwasaki gauge action and nonperturbatively $O(a)$ improved Wilson quarks at $(m_\pi,m_K)\approx(146,525)$ MeV. The central and tensor potentials in the $^1S_0$ and $^3S_1-^3D_1$ channels are obtained by solving the leading-order Schroedinger equation with the normalized four-point correlation functions, using 52 wall sources on 207 gauge configurations.

## Key numbers

- Pion and kaon masses: $(m_\pi,m_K)\approx(146,525)$ MeV
- Lattice volume: $(La)^4=(96a)^4\approx(8.1\ \text{fm})^4$ with lattice spacing $a\approx 0.085$ fm
- Gauge coupling: $\beta=1.82$ with clover coefficient $c_{sw}=1.11$
- Quark hopping parameters: $(\kappa_{ud},\kappa_s)=(0.126117,0.124790)$
- Statistics: 207 gauge configurations at every 10 trajectories, 52 wall sources (about half of possible 96), bin size 23 for jackknife error estimation
- Time slices used for potential extraction: $t-t_0=5-12$ (preliminary; ideal regime requires $t-t_0 \gg 1/m_\pi \sim 1.4$ fm)

## Context

This work extends earlier HAL QCD studies reported at LATTICE 2008, LATTICE 2009, and LATTICE 2011 (Nemura 2012) which used heavier quark masses and smaller lattice volumes. It is part of a comprehensive program (Doi, Ishii, Sasaki 2016) calculating all baryon-baryon interactions from $NN$ to $\Xi\Xi$ simultaneously. The results enable future studies of hypernuclear structure and hyperonic equations of state for neutron stars, particularly addressing the repulsive nature of $\Sigma N$ interactions suggested by experimental observations of only a four-body $\Sigma$-hypernucleus ($^4_\Sigma$He).
