# Evading the sign problem in the mean-field approximation through Lefschetz-thimble path integral

> The fermion sign problem appearing in the mean-field approximation is considered, and the systematic computational scheme of the free energy is devised by using the Lefschetz-thimble method. We show that the Lefschetz-thimble method respects the reflection symmetry, which makes physical quantities manifestly real at any order of approximations using complex saddle points. The formula is demonstrated through the Airy integral as an example, and its application to the Polyakov-loop effective model of dense QCD is discussed in detail.

# Evading the sign problem in the mean-field approximation through Lefschetz-thimble path integral

## Key claim

The Lefschetz-thimble decomposition of the path integral manifestly respects the antilinear reflection symmetry (charge conjugation combined with complex conjugation, or CK symmetry) of dense QCD, which ensures that the partition function and physical observables remain real at any order of approximation using complex saddle points, even when the classical action is complex. The authors claim this solves the sign problem appearing in the mean-field approximation, but only under the condition that the coupling constant is generic so that Stokes phenomenon does not occur.

## Method

The authors complexify the field variables and apply Picard-Lefschetz theory to decompose the original oscillatory integration over real fields into a sum of convergent integrals over Lefschetz thimbles, each associated with a saddle point in the complexified space. They show that the antilinear map K (extending the reflection symmetry L to complex variables) pairs thimbles into invariant sets, and they derive a decomposition formula that makes the reality of the partition function manifest. The method is demonstrated on the Airy integral and applied to the Polyakov-loop effective model of heavy dense QCD using the mean-field approximation with complex saddle points.

## Key numbers

- For the Airy integral with a > 0, the saddle-point approximation gives Z_Airy(a) ≈ (a^{-1/4} / (2√π)) exp(-(2/3) a^{3/2}), which is the asymptotic formula as a → +∞.
- For the Airy integral with a < 0, the saddle-point approximation gives Z_Airy(a) ≈ (|a|^{-1/4} / √π) cos((2|a|^{3/2}/3) - π/4), which is the asymptotic formula as a → -∞.
- In the heavy-quark model with h = 0.1 and μ = 2, the CK-symmetric saddle point z* contributes to the partition function, as demonstrated by solving the flow equation.
- In the limit μ → +∞, the saddle-point solutions approach z_1* ≈ 3 e^{-μ/2} / (2√h) and z_2* ≈ -i e^{-μ} / (8h), converging to the perturbative vacuum (0,0).
- The difference between Polyakov loop expectation values ⟨ℓ_3̅⟩ - ⟨ℓ_3⟩ is positive and given by (2/3)(sinh(2i z_2*) - 2 cos(z_1*) sinh(i z_2*)).

## Context

This work extends the application of Picard-Lefschetz theory to path integrals (Witten 2010, Cristoforetti 2012-2014, Tanizaki 2014) and unifies it with the observation that CK-symmetric complex saddle points give real free energies in Polyakov-loop models (Nishimura 2014). The paper provides a rigorous mathematical foundation for earlier empirical proposals to circumvent the sign problem in the mean-field approximation (Dumitru 2005, Fukushima 2006, Ratti 2005, Roessner 2006). The result enables systematic perturbative improvements beyond the saddle-point approximation for dense QCD effective models, including Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models and potentially lattice QCD simulations. The authors note that positivity of the partition function is not guaranteed when multiple saddle points contribute, which may be relevant for studying phase transitions.
