# Fragment Formation and Phase Transitions in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We investigate the thermodynamical properties of nuclear matter in heavy ion collisions, in particular with respect to questions of thermodynamical instability, phase transitions and fragmentation. For this we analyze results of relativistic transport calculations of Au+Au collisons at intermediate energies, separately for spectator and participant matter. On one hand, we determine local thermodynamical variables from the analysis of the local momentum distribution; on the other, we analyze fragment energy spectra in a blast model scenario. We find that the spectator represents an instable, equilibrized fragmenting source, while in the participant no such common source can be identified. Our results compare well with experimental determinations of temperatures and flow velocities.

## Key claim

The spectator region in heavy ion collisions represents a well-equilibrated, instable fragmenting source that enters a region of spinodal instability, while in the participant region fragments are not formed in a common equilibrated freeze-out configuration and slope temperatures must be interpreted with caution.

## Method

Relativistic Boltzmann-Nordheim-Vlasov transport calculations were performed for Au+Au collisions at intermediate energies using the nonlinear parametrization NL2 for the self energies. Local thermodynamic variables (temperature, density, pressure) were extracted by fitting the local momentum distribution to covariant hot Fermi-Dirac distributions, while fragment kinetic energy spectra were analyzed using the Siemens-Rassmussen blast model to extract slope temperatures and flow velocities. Fragment formation was modeled using a phase space coalescence algorithm with parameters fitted to reproduce observed mass distributions.

## Key numbers

- Spectator matter enters spinodal instability at about 45 fm/c, with densities of approximately 0.4-0.5 times normal nuclear density (ρ₀) and local temperatures of 5-6 MeV
- For spectator nucleons, the slope temperature T_slope = (7.3 ± 3.5) MeV, close to the local temperature T_loc = 5-6 MeV
- For spectator fragments with A_f ≥ 3, slope temperatures saturate around T_slope ≈ 17 MeV
- In the participant region at 600 A MeV, nucleon freeze-out occurs at about 35-40 fm/c with local temperature T_loc ≈ 15 MeV and density near normal nuclear density
- Central Au+Au collisions at 600 A MeV show a well-developed radial flow pattern after about 20 fm/c

## Context

This work extends theoretical investigations of the nuclear matter equation of state in the expansion phase, following earlier studies of high-density behavior by Blaettel (1993) and Gaitanos (1999). The results for spectator matter agree with experimental findings from the ALADIN collaboration (1998, 1999) based on isotope thermometers and fragment kinetic energy spectra. The difference between slope temperatures and local temperatures is interpreted using the Goldhaber model (1974) and Bauer's work on Fermi motion contributions. For participant matter, the mass-dependent slope temperatures qualitatively match experimental observations from the EOS (1995) and FOPI (1997) collaborations, and similar calculations in the QMD model by Aichelin. This work enables future investigations using the Boltzmann-Langevin approach to include physical fluctuations in transport calculations of fragmentation phenomena.
