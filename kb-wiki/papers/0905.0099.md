# Light Nuclei of Even Mass Number in the Skyrme Model

> We consider the semiclassical rigid-body quantization of Skyrmion solutions of mass numbers B = 4, 6, 8, 10 and 12. We determine the allowed quantum states for each Skyrmion, and find that they often match the observed states of nuclei. The spin and isospin inertia tensors of these Skyrmions are accurately calculated for the first time, and are used to determine the excitation energies of the quantum states. We calculate the energy level splittings, using a suitably chosen parameter set for each mass number. We find good qualitative and encouraging quantitative agreement with experiment. In particular, the rotational bands of beryllium-8 and carbon-12, along with isospin 1 triplets and isospin 2 quintets, are especially well reproduced. We also predict the existence of states that have not yet been observed, and make predictions for the unknown quantum numbers of some observed states.

## Key claim
The Skyrme model, when semiclassically quantized with accurately calculated spin and isospin inertia tensors, reproduces the observed nuclear spectra of even-mass-number light nuclei (B=4,6,8,10,12) with good qualitative and encouraging quantitative agreement, but requires separate parameter sets for each mass number and overpredicts some excitation energies.

## Method
The authors numerically relaxed Skyrmion field configurations for baryon numbers B=4, 6, 8, 10 and 12 at various dimensionless pion masses m, then computed their static energies, charge radii, and inertia tensors. They applied semiclassical rigid-body quantization using collective coordinates for spatial rotations and isorotations, imposing Finkelstein-Rubinstein constraints from the discrete symmetries of each Skyrmion to determine allowed quantum states. For each B, they calibrated the model by matching the calculated mean charge radius to the experimental radius of the zero-isospin nucleus, and set the classical energy scale so that the Skyrmion mass equals the nuclear mass.

## Key numbers
- B=4 (helium-4): ground state 0+ at 0.0 MeV; 2- isotriplet predicted at 43.2 MeV (experimental average 23.7 MeV); 4+ state predicted at 59.5 MeV
- B=6 (lithium-6): ground state 1+ at 1.6 MeV; 3+ state predicted at 9.7 MeV (overpredicted by factor ~5); lowest isospin-1 state 0+ at 11.8 MeV
- B=8 (beryllium-8): rotational band 2+ at 3.0 MeV (experimental 3.0 MeV) and 4+ at 10.2 MeV (experimental 11.4 MeV); lowest isospin-1 state 0- at 13.0 MeV; lowest isospin-2 state 0+ at 38.0 MeV (experimental 27.3 MeV)
- B=10 (boron-10): ground state incorrectly predicted as 1+ at 1.1 MeV (experimental ground state is 3+); 0+ isospin-1 state at 10.4 MeV
- B=12 (carbon-12): rotational band 2+ at 4.4 MeV (experimental 4.4 MeV); 4+ at 14.4 MeV (experimental 14.4 MeV); Hoyle state 0+ at 7.7 MeV (experimental 7.7 MeV)

## Context
This work extends earlier Skyrme model quantizations by Battye, Manton, and collaborators (2005-2007) that used approximate Skyrmion solutions based on the rational map ansatz, by recalculating all solutions with a consistent numerical scheme and accurately computing inertia tensors for the first time. The paper builds on the alpha-particle clustering picture of nuclei, where B=4 cubic Skyrmions appear as substructures within larger Skyrmions, matching the observation that alpha-particles exist as stable substructures inside heavier nuclei. The results enable predictions of unknown quantum numbers for observed states and suggest refinements such as including vibrational degrees of freedom to address discrepancies like the missing 1+ states in mass-8 nuclei.
