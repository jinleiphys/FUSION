# Chiral restoration temperature at finite spin density in QCD

> We investigate the impact of a uniform spin density on the critical temperature of the chiral phase transition in finite-temperature QCD in the scope of the linear sigma model. We demonstrate that at a finite spin potential $μ_Σ$, corresponding to a finite spin density, the predictive power of the model is challenged by an ambiguity associated with a contribution of the vacuum renormalization term to the free energy. Eliminating the regularization freedom through comparison with recent low-$μ_Σ$ lattice data, we extend the phase diagram of QCD at finite spin density to regions inaccessible to lattice simulations. We show that, as the spin potential increases, the temperature of the chiral crossover transition diminishes and the chiral crossover turns into a first-order transition at a second-order critical end-point $(T,μ_Σ)_\mathrm{CEP}\simeq (0.142,0.098)$ GeV. With increasing spin potential, the critical temperature touches the zero-temperature axis at $μ_Σ = 0.310$ GeV, implying that the chiral symmetry is restored at higher potentials at any temperature.

## Key claim
The chiral crossover temperature in QCD decreases with increasing spin potential μ_Σ, and the crossover turns into a first-order transition at a second-order critical end-point; however, the predictive power of the linear sigma model is challenged by an ambiguity in the vacuum renormalization term, which is resolved by matching to low-μ_Σ lattice data.

## Method
The authors study the linear sigma model coupled to quarks (LSM_q) in the mean-field approximation, incorporating a finite spin density via a canonical spin operator term μ_Σ ψ^† (n_Σ · Σ) ψ in the quark Lagrangian. They derive the spin-dependent quark dispersion relation, compute the thermodynamic potential including zero-point and thermal contributions, and perform vacuum renormalization using two schemes: a naive scheme that makes the renormalized potential spin-independent, and a spin-dependent scheme with a free parameter ℓ that is fixed by comparison to lattice data from Braguta (2025). The phase diagram is then mapped by solving the saddle-point equations for the σ field.

## Key numbers
- Critical end-point: (T, μ_Σ)_CEP ≃ (0.142, 0.098) GeV
- Spin potential at which the critical temperature reaches zero: μ_Σ = 0.310 GeV
- Pion decay constant in vacuum: f_π = 93 MeV
- Pion mass in vacuum: m_π = 138 MeV
- Sigma meson mass in vacuum: m_σ = 600 MeV
- Constituent quark mass: m_q = 307 MeV, giving Yukawa coupling g ≃ 3.3

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD results from Braguta (2025), which showed that finite spin density decreases chiral and deconfinement temperatures but was limited to low spin potentials due to the sign problem. The authors use the LSM_q model, which is renormalizable, to explore regions inaccessible to lattice simulations. The study contrasts with the conserved helicity operator approach of Chernodub (2020) and highlights that the non-conservation of the canonical spin tensor leads to a nonlinear dispersion relation and μ_Σ-dependent zero-point free energy, unlike baryon chemical potential. The results enable predictions for the QCD phase diagram at arbitrary spin densities relevant to spin-polarized quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions.
