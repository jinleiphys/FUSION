# Exploring the Nuclear Shape Phase Transition in Ultra-Relativistic $^{129}$Xe+$^{129}$Xe Collisions at the LHC

> The shape phase transition for certain isotope or isotone chains, associated with the quantum phase transition of finite nuclei, is an intriguing phenomenon in nuclear physics. A notable case is the Xe isotope chain, where the structure transits from a $γ$-soft rotor to a spherical vibrator, with the second-order shape phase transition occurring in the vicinity of $^{128-130}$Xe. In this letter, we focus on investigating the $γ$-soft deformation of $^{129}$Xe associated with the second-order shape phase transition by constructing novel correlators for ultra-relativistic $^{129}$Xe+$^{129}$Xe collisions. In particular, our iEBE-VISHNU model calculations show that the $v_2^2-[p_T]$ correlation $ρ_{2}$ and the mean transverse momentum fluctuation $Γ_{p_T}$, which were previously interpreted as the evidence for the rigid triaxial deformation of $^{129}$Xe, can also be well explained by the $γ$-soft deformation of $^{129}$Xe. We also propose two novel correlators $ρ_{4,2}$ and $ρ_{2,4}$, which carry non-trivial higher-order correlations and show unique capabilities to distinguish between the $γ$-soft and the rigid triaxial deformation of $^{129}$Xe in $^{129}$Xe+$^{129}$Xe collisions at the LHC. The present study also provides a novel way to explore the second-order shape phase transition of finite nuclei with ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the previously claimed evidence for rigid triaxial deformation of $^{129}$Xe from the measured $\rho_2$ and $\Gamma_{p_T}$ correlators in ultra-relativistic $^{129}$Xe+$^{129}$Xe collisions can also be fully explained by a $\gamma$-soft deformation of $^{129}$Xe, which is associated with a second-order shape phase transition in the Xe isotope chain. The authors propose that higher-order 6-particle correlators $\rho_{4,2}$ and $\rho_{2,4}$ can distinguish between these two scenarios.

## Method

The authors used the iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model, which combines the TRENTo initial condition generator with (2+1)-dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (VISH2+1) and the UrQMD hadron cascade, to simulate ultra-relativistic $^{129}$Xe+$^{129}$Xe collisions at the LHC. For the $\gamma$-soft deformation scenario, the triaxial deformation parameter $\gamma$ was sampled from a flat distribution over $0^\circ \le \gamma \le 60^\circ$ with a fixed quadrupole deformation $\beta_2 = 0.17$, while for the rigid triaxial case, $\gamma$ was frozen at $30^\circ$ with the same $\beta_2$. The authors calculated 3-particle correlators ($\rho_2$ and $\Gamma_{p_T}$) from the full hydrodynamic simulations and constructed new 6-particle correlators ($\rho_{4,2}$ and $\rho_{2,4}$) from the initial-state TRENTo model, using analytic expressions from a liquid-drop model to interpret the $\gamma$ dependence.

## Key numbers

- The quadrupole deformation parameter $\beta_2$ of $^{129}$Xe was set to 0.17, based on predicted values for $^{128,130}$Xe ranging from 0.169 to 0.192 (Raman 2001, Elekes 2015).
- For the $\gamma$-soft deformation, $\gamma$ was sampled uniformly over $0^\circ \le \gamma \le 60^\circ$; for rigid triaxial deformation, $\gamma$ was fixed at $30^\circ$.
- The nuclear half-width radius $R_0$ was set to 5.36 fm and the nuclear diffusion parameter $a_0$ to 0.59 fm.
- The nucleon width parameter $w$ in TRENTo was set to 0.5 fm.
- For $\rho_{4,2}$ with $\beta_2 = 0.17$, the enhancement ratio $R(\rho_{4,2})$ for $\gamma$-soft relative to rigid triaxial deformation increases to about 50% in 0.2% ultra-central collisions.
- With $\beta_2 = 0.20$, the enhancement of $R(\rho_{4,2})$ can increase to about 100% in 0.2% ultra-central collisions.
- For $\rho_{2,4}$ with $\beta_2 = 0.17$, a small suppression within 2% is observed when changing from rigid triaxial to $\gamma$-soft deformation.

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear shape phase transitions from low-energy nuclear structure physics to ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. It directly addresses the claim by Bally (2021) that measured $\rho_2$ correlations in $^{129}$Xe+$^{129}$Xe collisions at the LHC provide evidence for rigid triaxial deformation of $^{129}$Xe with $\gamma = 27^\circ$. The authors show that the same data can be explained by a $\gamma$-soft deformation consistent with the E(5) critical point symmetry proposed by Iachello (2001) and the second-order shape phase transition in the Xe isotope chain near $^{128-130}$Xe identified by Casten (1985) and Clark (2004). The new 6-particle correlators $\rho_{4,2}$ and $\rho_{2,4}$ are proposed to resolve this ambiguity, enabling future experimental measurements at the LHC to distinguish between $\gamma$-soft and rigid triaxial deformation and thereby probe the second-order shape phase transition of finite nuclei.
