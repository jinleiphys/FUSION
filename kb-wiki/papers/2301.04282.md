# Speed of sound in QCD matter at finite temperature and density]{Speed of sound in QCD matter at finite temperature and density

> The speed of sound in QCD matter at finite temperature and density is investigated within the Polyakov loop improved Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model. The spinodal structure associated with the chiral first-order chiral phase transition is considered to describe the continuous variation of the speed of sound. The behaviors of the squared sound speed in different phases, including the stable, metastable and unstable phases, are derived. The relation between speed of sound and QCD phase transitions is systematically explored. In particular, the boundary of vanishing sound velocity is derived in the temperature-density phase diagram, and the region where the sound wave equation being broken is pointed out. Some interesting features of speed of sound under different definitions are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the squared speed of sound in QCD matter can become negative within the spinodal region of the first-order chiral phase transition, and that the boundary of vanishing sound velocity lies inside the mechanically unstable phase, meaning sound waves cannot propagate there; this result is derived within the Polyakov loop improved Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model and depends on the specific constraint condition (e.g., constant entropy per baryon, constant baryon density, or constant entropy) used to define the sound speed.

## Method

The authors use the 2+1 flavor PNJL model in the mean-field approximation to compute the thermodynamic potential, pressure, energy density, entropy density, and baryon number density as functions of temperature T and baryon density ρ_B. They derive analytic formulas for the squared speed of sound under five different constraint conditions (constant s/ρ_B, constant s, constant ρ_B, constant T, and constant μ_B) in terms of partial derivatives of entropy density, baryon density, chemical potential, and temperature. The phase diagram including the first-order chiral transition line, spinodal lines, and chiral crossover is determined from the model, and the sound speed is evaluated across the stable, metastable, and unstable phases.

## Key numbers

- The squared speed of sound c²_{s/ρ_B} approaches 1/3 at high temperature or very high density, the value of a noninteracting gas.
- For isentropic trajectories with s/ρ_B < 5.9, there exist two inflection points where (∂p/∂ε)_{s/ρ_B} changes sign, corresponding to vanishing sound velocity.
- The Polyakov-loop potential parameters used are a₀ = 3.51, a₁ = -2.47, a₂ = 15.2, b₃ = -1.75, with T₀ = 210 MeV.
- The model parameters are Λ = 602.3 MeV, GΛ² = 1.835, KΛ⁵ = 12.36, m_{u,d} = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 140.7 MeV, fitted to f_π = 92.4 MeV, M_π = 135.0 MeV, m_K = 497.7 MeV, and m_η = 957.8 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (He et al., 2022) which only considered sound speed in the stable phase, by now including the metastable and unstable phases and the spinodal structure associated with the first-order chiral phase transition. It builds on prior calculations of sound speed in the PNJL model (Motta et al., 2018; Ghosh et al., 2006; Marty et al., 2013; Deb et al., 2016; Saha et al., 2018; Zhao et al., 2020) and lattice QCD (Aoki et al., 2006; Borsanyi et al., 2014; Bazavov et al., 2014; Philipsen et al., 2013; Borsanyi et al., 2020), which focused mainly on high temperature and small chemical potential. The results enable future studies of the gravitational wave signal from cosmic QCD phase transitions, neutron star matter equations of state, and the bulk viscosity of strongly interacting matter, and suggest that the speed of sound extracted from charged particle multiplicity in heavy-ion collisions (Gardim et al., 2020; Sahu et al., 2021; Biswas et al., 2020) could be used to diagnose QCD phase structure in beam energy scan experiments.
