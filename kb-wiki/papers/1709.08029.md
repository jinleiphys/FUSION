# Quest for consistent modelling of statistical decay of the compound nucleus

> A statistical model description of heavy ion induced fusion-fission reactions is presented where shell effects, collective enhancement of level density, tilting away effect of compound nuclear spin and dissipation are included. It is shown that the inclusion of all these effects provides a consistent picture of fission where fission hindrance is required to explain the experimental values of both pre-scission neutron multiplicities and evaporation residue cross-sections in contrast to some of the earlier works where a fission hindrance is required for pre-scission neutrons but a fission enhancement for evaporation residue cross-sections.

## Key claim
The paper shows that including shell effects, collective enhancement of level density, the tilting-away (K-orientation) effect of compound nuclear spin, and dissipation in a statistical model provides a consistent picture of fission where fission hindrance is required to explain both pre-scission neutron multiplicities and evaporation residue cross-sections. This resolves an apparent contradiction in earlier works where fission hindrance was needed for pre-scission neutrons but fission enhancement was required for evaporation residue cross-sections.

## Method
The authors performed statistical model calculations for seven heavy-ion induced fusion-fission reactions covering a fissility range of 0.6 to 0.763. They sequentially incorporated shell corrections to the fission barrier and level density parameter (using the Ignatyuk formalism), collective enhancement of level density (CELD) following Zagrebaev et al., the K-orientation effect on fission width, and finally nuclear dissipation via the Kramers-modified fission width with a single adjustable reduced dissipation coefficient β. A Monte Carlo sampling tracked the compound nucleus evolution in small time steps until fission or evaporation residue formation.

## Key numbers
- The damping parameter for shell effects in level density was set to E_D = 18.5 MeV, taken from analysis of s-wave neutron resonances (Reisdorf, 1981).
- The critical energy for damping of collectivity was E_cr = 40 MeV with ΔE = 10 MeV (Junghans et al.).
- The rotational enhancement factor K_rot = τ_⟂ T/ħ², and vibrational enhancement factor K_vib = exp(0.055 × A^(2/3) × T^(4/3)).
- The reduced dissipation coefficient β was treated as the only adjustable parameter, with values in the range (1–20)×10²¹ s⁻¹ reported in the literature; the authors used β = 1×10²¹ s⁻¹ for the system ¹⁹F+¹⁸¹Ta (CN ²⁰⁰Pb).
- For lighter systems (A_CN < 200), experimental ν_pre values were reproduced with β = 2×10²¹ s⁻¹.
- Pre-saddle dissipation strength was found to be β = (1–3)×10²¹ s⁻¹ over a broad range of excitation energies for A_CN ≥ 200 nuclei.

## Context
This work extends statistical model analyses of fusion-fission reactions by Hinde (1986), Newton (1988), and others that required separate parameter adjustments for neutron multiplicities and cross-sections. It builds on prior inclusion of shell effects, CELD, and K-orientation by Yanez et al. (PRL 112) for pre- and post-scission multiplicities, and by Mayorov et al. (PRC 92) for super-heavy nuclei survival. The study enables a consistent framework where only one adjustable parameter (dissipation strength) is needed, and suggests that a hybrid approach coupling statistical models with Langevin dynamics in the saddle-to-scission region could improve descriptions for heavy systems, as noted by Vanin (1999).
