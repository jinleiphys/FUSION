# Towards QCD-assisted hydrodynamics for heavy-ion collision phenomenology

> Heavy-ion collisions are well described by a dynamical evolution with a long hydrodynamical phase. In this phase the properties of the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma are reflected in the equation of state (EoS) and the transport coefficients, most prominently by the shear and bulk viscosity over entropy density ratios $η$/s(T) and $ζ$/s(T), respectively. While the EoS is by now known to a high accuracy, the transport coefficients and in particular their temperature and density dependence are not well known from first-principle computations yet, as well as the possible influence they can have once used in hydrodynamical simulations. In this work, the most recent QCD-based parameters are provided as input to the MUSIC framework. A ratio $η$/s(T) computed with a QCD based approach is used for the first time \cite{Haas:2013hpa,Christiansen:2014ypa}. The IP-Glasma model is used to describe the initial energy density distribution, and UrQMD for the dilute hadronic phase. Simulations are performed for Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV, for different centrality intervals. The resulting kinematic distributions of the particles produced in the collisions are compared to data from the LHC, for several experimental observables. The high precision of the experimental results and the broad variety of observables considered allow to critically verify the quality of the description based on first-principle input to the hydrodynamic evolution.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a shear viscosity over entropy density ratio η/s(T) computed from a first-principles QCD-based functional diagrammatical approach can be used as input to hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions, but the absolute normalization of η/s(T) carries a systematic uncertainty that requires a temperature-independent shift d ∈ [-0.06, 0] to bring the computed flow coefficients into agreement with experimental data.

## Method

The authors used the IP-Glasma model for initial energy density distributions, the MUSIC framework for relativistic viscous hydrodynamics with both shear and bulk viscosities, and UrQMD for the dilute hadronic phase after particlization at T_switch = 145 MeV. They employed a pure-glue η/s(T) from a functional diagrammatic QCD approach parameterized as η/s(T) = a/α_s,HQ^γ(c T/T_c) + b/(T/T_c)^δ with a=0.15, b=0.14, c=0.66, γ=1.6, δ=5.1, and a ζ/s(T) parametrization with a maximum of approximately 0.3 at T=180 MeV. Simulations were performed for Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV across centrality intervals 0-5%, 5-10%, 10-20%, 20-30%, and 30-40%.

## Key numbers

- η/s(T) minimum value of 0.14 at T_min ≈ 1.26 T_c, where T_c ≈ 155 MeV
- Shift parameter d ∈ [-0.06, 0] applied to η/s(T) to test systematic uncertainty, covering the AdS/CFT bound value of 0.08
- ζ/s(T) maximum of approximately 0.3 at T = 180 MeV, converging to 0.03 in the low-temperature hadronic gas limit
- Switching temperature between hydrodynamics and UrQMD set to T_switch = 145 MeV
- η/s(T_switch) ≈ 0.27 in the simulation, while UrQMD has (η/s)(T_switch) ≈ 1
- Flow harmonics v_n{2} computed within 0.2 < p_T < 5.0 GeV and |η| < 0.8 with pseudorapidity gap |Δη| > 1.0

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic modeling efforts (Gale 2013, deSouza 2015, Luzum 2008, Teaney 2009) by incorporating for the first time a QCD-based η/s(T) from the functional diagrammatic approach of Haas (2013) and Christiansen (2014), rather than using Bayesian-inferred parametrizations (Bernhard 2016) or simple constant values. The results enable future work on computing bulk viscosity and relaxation time from the same diagrammatic approach, and the authors note that a threefold approach combining diagrammatic computations, lattice methods via the Kubo formula, and direct real-time functional methods is being pursued to reduce systematic uncertainties in transport coefficients.
