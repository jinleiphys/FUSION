# Rescuing the nonjet (NJ) azimuth quadrupole from the flow narrative

> According to the flow narrative commonly applied to high-energy nuclear collisions a cylindrical-quadrupole component of 1D azimuth angular correlations is conventionally denoted by quantity $v_2$ and interpreted to represent elliptic flow. Jet angular correlations may also contribute to $v_2$ data as "nonflow" depending on the method used to calculate $v_2$, but 2D graphical methods are available to insure accurate separation. The nonjet (NJ) quadrupole has various properties inconsistent with a flow interpretation, including the observation that NJ quadrupole centrality variation in A-A collisions has no relation to strongly-varying jet modification ("jet quenching") in those collisions commonly attributed to jet interaction with a flowing dense medium. In this presentation I describe isolation of quadrupole spectra from pt-differential $v_2(p_t)$ data from the RHIC and LHC. I demonstrate that quadrupole spectra have characteristics very different from the single-particle spectra for most hadrons, that quadrupole spectra indicate a common boosted hadron source for a small minority of hadrons that "carry" the NJ quadrupole structure, that the narrow source-boost distribution is characteristic of an expanding thin cylindrical shell (strongly contradicting hydro descriptions), and that in the boost frame a single universal quadrupole spectrum (Lévy distribution) on transverse mass $m_t$ accurately describes data for several hadron species scaled according to their statistical-model abundances. The quadrupole spectrum shape changes very little from RHIC to LHC energies. Taken in combination those characteristics strongly suggest a unique {\em nonflow} (and nonjet) QCD mechanism for the NJ quadrupole conventionally represented by $v_2$.

# Nonjet (NJ) Azimuth Quadrupole from High-Energy Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim

The nonjet (NJ) azimuth quadrupole component conventionally denoted by $v_2$ and interpreted as elliptic flow is actually a nonflow (and nonjet) QCD mechanism, based on evidence that quadrupole spectra are very different from single-particle spectra, indicate a common boosted hadron source for a small minority of hadrons, and show a narrow source-boost distribution characteristic of an expanding thin cylindrical shell rather than hydrodynamic flow.

## Method

Quadrupole spectra were isolated from $p_t$-differential $v_2(p_t,b)$ data from RHIC (200 GeV Au-Au collisions) and LHC (2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions) by multiplying $v_2(p_t)$ by the single-particle spectrum $\bar \rho_0(p_t)$, then applying transformations including multiplication by $p_t(\text{lab})/p_t(\text{boost})$, shifting from lab frame transverse rapidity $y_t$ to boost frame $y_t'$, and transforming to boost-frame transverse mass $m_t'$ with the appropriate Jacobian. The resulting quadrupole spectra were compared across hadron species after rescaling by statistical-model abundances relative to pions.

## Key numbers

- Source boost value $\Delta y_{t0} \approx 0.6$ inferred from 200 GeV Au-Au data, consistent across three hadron species (pions, kaons, protons/Lambdas)
- Quadrupole spectrum slope parameter $T_2 = 92$ MeV with Levy exponent $n_2 = 14$ for 200 GeV Au-Au, compared to single-particle spectrum values $T_0 = 145$ MeV and $n_0 = 12$
- Quadrupole spectrum slope parameter $T_2 = 94$ MeV with Levy exponent $n_2 = 12$ for 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb, compared to pp single-particle spectrum soft component values $T_0 = 145$ MeV and $n_0 = 8.8$
- 200 GeV quadrupole spectrum data multiplied by factor 2.5 (expected from energy scaling of $v_2$ and $\bar \rho_0$) matches LHC data
- Fragment $p_t$ spectrum from 91 GeV LEP dijets exhibits $T \approx 90$ MeV, similar to the quadrupole spectrum slope

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analysis of quadrupole spectra from 200 GeV Au-Au collisions (Trainor, 2015) and directly contradicts the flow narrative based on elliptic flow interpretations of $v_2$ data. The results show no evidence for radial flow or Hubble expansion of a bulk medium, consistent with earlier findings that the blast-wave model responds to jet contributions rather than radial flow (Trainor, 2010), and that $v_2$ systematics are uncorrelated with jet quenching (STAR collaboration, 2005). The quadrupole spectrum shape changes very little from RHIC to LHC energies, and the similarity to LEP dijet fragment spectra suggests a QCD mechanism analogous to QED antenna radiation rather than hydrodynamic flow.
