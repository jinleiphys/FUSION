# New data on $0^+$ states in $^{158}$Gd

> Excited states in the deformed nucleus $^{158}$Gd have been studied in the (p,t) reaction by using the Munich Tandem and Q3D spectrograph. 30 new excited 0$^+$ states (three tentative) have been assigned up to the 4.3 MeV excitation energy. The total number of 34 excited 0$^+$ states (four tentatively assigned) in a deformed nucleus, close to a complete level scheme, offers a new opportunity to test nuclear models and obtain more information on the structure of these special states.

## Key claim
The paper reports the identification of 30 new excited 0+ states in 158Gd via the (p,t) reaction, bringing the total to 34 excited 0+ states below 4.3 MeV. However, four of these assignments are tentative, and the authors note that the previously reported 0+ assignments for five states by Lesher et al. (2002) are not confirmed, with some reassigned as 2+ or 4+ states.

## Method
The 160Gd(p,t)158Gd reaction was studied using a 22 MeV proton beam from the Munich Tandem accelerator, with triton spectra measured at eight laboratory angles from 5° to 40° using the Q3D magnetic spectrograph. Angular distributions were compared with distorted wave Born approximation calculations using the CHUCK3 code, and 0+ states were identified by their characteristic angular distributions. Two separate experiments were performed to resolve calibration discrepancies, using 154Gd(p,t)152Gd and 172Yb(p,t)170Yb reactions for energy calibration.

## Key numbers
- 30 new excited 0+ states (three tentative) assigned up to 4.3 MeV excitation energy
- Total of 34 excited 0+ states (four tentatively assigned) in 158Gd
- Triton spectra resolution of 4-7 keV FWHM (first experiment) and 8-9 keV FWHM (second experiment)
- Ground state (p,t) cross section at 5°: 1320 ± 12 μb
- Second excited 0+ state at 1452.3 keV: cross section 389 ± 6 μb (about 29% of ground state cross section)
- First excited 0+ state at 1196.1 keV: cross section 3.1 ± 0.4 μb (about 0.2% of ground state cross section)
- 230 levels identified in the 5° spectrum, with 162 levels identified at all eight angles
- Target enrichment: 98.10% 160Gd, with impurities of 158Gd (0.99%), 156Gd (0.33%), and 157Gd (0.44%)

## Context
This work extends the study of 0+ states in deformed nuclei beyond the 3 MeV limit of most previous studies, following the first observation of multiple 0+ states in 158Gd by Lesher et al. (2002) who reported 13 excited 0+ states. The results challenge both the interacting boson model (IBM) and quasiparticle phonon model (QPM), which had been applied to 158Gd by Zamfir et al. (2002) and Lo Iudice et al. (2004) respectively. The complete or nearly complete sequence of 0+ states enables statistical analysis of collective spectra and investigation of K-symmetry breaking, as well as providing a stringent test for nuclear models of 0+ excitations.
