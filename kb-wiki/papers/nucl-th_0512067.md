# Single and double pi^{-}/pi^{+} ratios in heavy-ion reactions as probes of the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy

> Based on an isospin- and momentum-dependent hadronic transport model IBUU04, effects of the nuclear symmetry energy on the single and double pi^{-}/pi^{+}ratios in central reactions of ^{132}Sn+^{124}Sn and ^{112}Sn+^{112}Sn at a beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon are studied. It is found that around the Coulomb peak of the single pi^{-}/pi^{+} ratio the double pi^{-}/pi^{+} ratio taken from the two isotopic reactions retains about the same sensitivity to the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy. Because the double pi^{-}/pi^{+}ratio can reduce significantly the systematic errors, it is thus a more effective probe for the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The double \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratio, formed by taking the ratio of the single \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratio from the neutron-rich system \(^{132}\mathrm{Sn}+^{124}\mathrm{Sn}\) over that from the neutron-deficient system \(^{112}\mathrm{Sn}+^{112}\mathrm{Sn}\), retains about the same sensitivity to the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy as the single ratio around its Coulomb peak, while significantly reducing systematic errors.

## Method

The authors used the isospin- and momentum-dependent hadronic transport model IBUU04 to simulate central (\(b=1\) fm) reactions of \(^{132}\mathrm{Sn}+^{124}\mathrm{Sn}\) and \(^{112}\mathrm{Sn}+^{112}\mathrm{Sn}\) at a beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon. They employed a momentum-dependent single nucleon potential (MDI) with two parameterizations (\(x=0\) for a soft symmetry energy and \(x=-1\) for a stiff symmetry energy) to mimic different density dependences of the nuclear symmetry energy. The kinetic energy distributions of the single and double \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratios were calculated and compared, including a calculation with the Coulomb potentials turned off to isolate the Coulomb peak.

## Key numbers

* The single \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratio exhibits a Coulomb peak at a pion kinetic energy of about 45 MeV for all cases considered.
* With the Coulomb potentials turned off in the \(^{132}\mathrm{Sn}+^{124}\mathrm{Sn}\) reaction, the single \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratio becomes approximately a constant of about 2.4, consistent with the \(\Delta\) resonance model expectation of \((N/Z)^2 \approx 2.43\) for that system.
* The symmetry energy at normal nuclear matter density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is set to about 32 MeV, and the incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter is set to 211 MeV.
* The soft (\(x=0\)) and stiff (\(x=-1\)) symmetry energies have a slight difference at low densities but a large difference at high densities (about \(2\rho_0\)), where most pions are produced.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies showing that the single \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratio in neutron-rich heavy-ion collisions is a useful probe of the high-density symmetry energy (Li et al., 2002; Gai et al., 2004; Li et al., 2005). It follows the approach of a recent theoretical investigation of the double neutron/proton ratio for pre-equilibrium nucleons (Li et al., 2005). The double \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratio is proposed as a more effective probe because it reduces systematic errors, and it enables future experimental extraction of the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy, which is crucial for understanding neutron star structure and proto-neutron star evolution.
