# Relativistic Mean Field Description of Nuclear Collective Rotation -The Superdeformed Rotational Bands in the A$\sim$60 Mass Region-

> Relativistic Mean Field Theory is applied to the description of rotating nuclei. Since the previous formulation of Munich group was based on a special relativistic transformation property of the spinor fields, we reformulate in a fully covariant manner using tetrad formalism. The numerical calculations are performed for 3 zinc isotopes, including the newly discovered superdeformed band in $^{62}$Zn which is the first experimental observation in this mass region.

# Relativistic Mean Field Description of Nuclear Collective Rotation – The Superdeformed Rotational Bands in the A∼60 Mass Region

## Key claim

The authors reformulate Relativistic Mean Field Theory (RMFT) for rotating nuclei in a fully covariant manner using tetrad formalism, obtaining equations of motion that are identical to those of the earlier Munich group formulation. When applied to the newly discovered superdeformed band in ⁶²Zn, the calculated dynamical moments of inertia are somewhat too small compared to experimental values, though the authors caution that only limited experimental data exist for a decisive conclusion.

## Method

The authors apply RMFT combined with the cranking assumption, reformulated covariantly using tetrad formalism from general relativity to correctly handle the transformation of spinor fields in a rotating (non-inertial) frame. They solve the resulting equations self-consistently for three zinc isotopes (⁶⁰Zn, ⁶²Zn, ⁶⁴Zn) using the NL-SH parameter set, calculating excitation energies and dynamical moments of inertia for superdeformed rotational bands.

## Key numbers

- The extracted β₂ value for the experimentally observed superdeformed band in ⁶²Zn is 0.45.
- The superdeformed band in ⁶²Zn becomes yrast at I ≥ 24 (experimental observation).
- The lowest superdeformed states become yrast at I ≃ 16–24 in all three zinc isotopes studied.
- For ⁶⁰Zn, the superdeformed minimum was predicted by Ragnarsson to become yrast at I = 22.

## Context

This work extends the RMFT cranking approach previously developed by the Munich group (Koepf and Ring, 1989; Koepf and Ring, 1993) by providing a fully covariant justification using tetrad formalism, confirming that the earlier formulation yielded correct equations despite its use of Lorentz transformations in a non-inertial frame. The calculations are motivated by the recent experimental discovery of the first superdeformed band in the A∼60 mass region by the McMaster group (Svensson et al., 1997) in ⁶²Zn, and they compare with prior theoretical predictions by Ragnarsson (1990) for ⁶⁰Zn. The results call for more systematic experimental and theoretical investigation to reach a definite conclusion on superdeformed bands in this mass region.
