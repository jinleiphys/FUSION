# Resolving kinematic ambiguities in QCD predictions for Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering

> The existing QCD predictions for the Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS) depend on the convention used for the skewedness parameter and on the reference frame used to define helicity amplitudes. These ambiquities are formally power-suppressed but numerically significant. They are cancelled by finite-$t$ and target mass corrections that have been calculated recently to the $1/Q^2$ accuracy. It turns out that these corrections can be minimized, at least for unpolarized observables, by choosing a specific reference frame where longitudinal directions are defined by the photon momenta.

## Key claim

The existing leading-twist (LT) QCD predictions for Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS) are intrinsically ambiguous because they depend on the convention used for the skewedness parameter and on the reference frame used to define helicity amplitudes; these ambiguities are formally power-suppressed but numerically significant, and they are cancelled by finite-\(t\) and target mass corrections calculated to \(1/Q^2\) accuracy.

## Method

The authors computed the DVCS tensor to twist-four accuracy, including contributions from descendants of leading-twist operators, and compared two LT approximations (LT\(_{\rm KM}\) and LT\(_{\rm BMP}\)) against the full calculation including kinematic power corrections. They used the GK GPD model to generate numerical predictions for unpolarized cross sections, electron helicity-dependent cross section differences, and transverse target spin asymmetries, comparing these to data from the HALL A and HERMES collaborations.

## Key numbers

- For \(-t/Q^2 \simeq 1/4\), the kinematic factor \(\varkappa/2 \sim 1/3\).
- The BMP skewedness parameter is approximated as \(\xi_{\rm BMP} \approx (1+t/Q^2)\xi_{\rm KM}\) for \(x_B \le 0.4\).
- The unpolarized cross section comparison used data from HALL A at \(t = -0.33\) GeV\(^2\).
- Transverse target spin asymmetries were compared to HERMES data (Airapetian, 2008).

## Context

This work extends the recent calculations of kinematic power corrections by Braun, Manashov, and Pirnay (2012) and resolves ambiguities in earlier LT frameworks such as the Belitsky, Mueller, and Ji (BMJ) reference frame (Belitsky, 2001; Belitsky, 2010; Belitsky, 2012) and the KM convention used by Kumericki and Mueller (2009, 2013). The results enable future global DVCS fits that incorporate both kinematic higher-twist corrections and perturbative next-to-leading order corrections, moving beyond the LT approximations that have been standard in phenomenological studies.
