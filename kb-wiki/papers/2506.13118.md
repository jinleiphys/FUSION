# Evolution of energy density fluctuations in the presence of a magnetic field

> In this proceeding, we study the evolution of energy density fluctuations in the presence of a static and uniform magnetic field. By numerically solving the relativistic Boltzmann-Vlasov equation within the relaxation time approximation and performing the momentum mode analysis of different wavelength fluctuations, we show that the magnetic field increases the damping of mode oscillations. This causes a qualitative change in the fluctuations present in the system at the timescale required to achieve a local equilibrium state.

# Evolution of energy density fluctuations in the presence of a magnetic field

## Key claim
The authors show that a static and uniform magnetic field increases the damping of energy density fluctuation mode oscillations in a relativistic charged particle system, causing a qualitative change in fluctuations at the timescale required to achieve local equilibrium. This effect is demonstrated specifically for fluctuations evolving in the direction transverse to the magnetic field, with the damping enhancement arising from the Lorentz force.

## Method
The authors numerically solve the relativistic Boltzmann-Vlasov equation within the relaxation time approximation (RTA) for a system of charged particles and antiparticles in a static and uniform magnetic field oriented along the y-direction. They perform a momentum mode analysis by Fourier transforming the energy density fluctuations from configuration space to momentum space, and fit the time evolution of the most dominant mode to a damped harmonic oscillator function to extract the damping coefficient and decay timescale for different mode momenta and magnetic field strengths.

## Key numbers
- The dimensionless magnetic field parameter is defined as β₀ = |qB| τ_c / m₀, where τ_c is the relaxation time and m₀ is the particle mass.
- The time evolution of the dominant mode is fitted to Im[Π(κ'_p, t')] = α₀ cos(ω t' - φ) exp(-γ_m t'), where γ_m is the dimensionless damping coefficient.
- The decay timescale γ_m⁻¹ is fitted with a power law scaling γ_m⁻¹ = s₀ (κ_p τ_c)^(-s₁) + s₂.
- The ultraviolet momentum cutoff κ_c τ_c is obtained by setting γ_m⁻¹ = 1 in the power law scaling, which defines the wavelength cutoff λ_c* = λ_c/τ_c below which inhomogeneities are suppressed.
- The fractional change of wavelength cutoff δλ_c*(β₀) = [λ_c*(β₀) - λ_c*(0)] / λ_c*(0) increases as a function of β₀.
- Specific mode momenta studied include κ_p τ_c = 0.88 (low momentum) and κ_p τ_c = 2.0 (high momentum).
- Time evolution is shown at t/τ_c = 0 and t/τ_c = 3.0.

## Context
This work extends studies of pre-equilibrium dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where strong magnetic fields are generated in non-central collisions (Larry, ourMHD) and can survive longer in the highly conducting quark-gluon plasma (Ranjita, Tuchin, KharzMHD). The authors build on prior work using the Boltzmann-Vlasov equation in RTA (Rischke, Ashu, bgk, Anderson, PaulR) and on hydrodynamic simulations of initial state fluctuations (hydroSim, Schenke12). The results enable improved modeling of initial conditions for hydrodynamic evolution of the quark-gluon plasma, particularly for the generation of collective flow harmonics v_n(p_T) (Bhalerao 2014, Giacalone 2017, Saumia, ourRev, ehr), by showing that magnetic fields modify the coarse-grained length scale and suppress short-wavelength fluctuations in the reaction plane.
