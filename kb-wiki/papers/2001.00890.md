# The calculation of the neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix element within the realistic shell model

> We approach the calculation of the nuclear matrix element of the neutrinoless double-beta decay process, considering the light-neutrino-exchange channel, by way of the realistic shell model. To this end, we start from a realistic nucleon-nucleon potential and then derive the effective shell-model Hamiltonian and neutrinoless double-beta decay operator within the many-body perturbation theory. We focus on investigating the perturbative properties of the effective shell-model operator of such a decay process, aiming to establish the degree of reliability of our predictions. The contributions of the so-called short-range correlations and of the correction of Pauli-principle violations to the effective shell-model operator, the latter introduced in many-valence nucleon systems, are also taken into account. The subjects of our study are a few candidates to the neutrinoless double-beta decay detection, in a mass interval ranging from A=48 up to A=136, whose spin- and spin-isospin-dependent decay properties we have studied in previous works. Our results will be finally compared with shell-model calculations for the same set of nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the renormalization of the neutrinoless double-beta decay operator within the realistic shell model, derived consistently from a realistic nucleon-nucleon potential via many-body perturbation theory, significantly changes the nuclear matrix element values; the authors find that this renormalization has a less relevant effect than what was previously observed for effective single-body Gamow-Teller transitions in two-neutrino double-beta decay.

## Method

The authors start from the high-precision CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, renormalize its high-momentum components using the Vlow-k approach with a cutoff of 2.6 fm^-1, and then derive the effective shell-model Hamiltonian and the effective neutrinoless double-beta decay operator via the Q-box-plus-folded-diagram method within many-body perturbation theory, including diagrams up to third order. The effective operator expansion includes the chi_0, chi_1, and chi_2 terms, and the authors also incorporate second-order three-body diagrams to correct for Pauli-principle violations in many-valence nucleon systems, as well as short-range correlations treated consistently through the Vlow-k unitary transformation. The calculations are performed within the closure approximation for the neutrino potential, and the nuclear matrix elements are computed for five candidate nuclei using the NATHAN shell-model code.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff for the Vlow-k renormalization is Lambda = 2.6 fm^-1.
- The maximum unperturbed excitation energy for intermediate states in the Q-box expansion is E_max = N_max * hbar*omega with N_max = 18.
- Harmonic oscillator parameters are hbar*omega = 11, 10, 8 MeV for model spaces above ^40Ca, ^56Ni, and ^100Sn cores, respectively.
- The free axial coupling constant used is g_A^free = 1.2723.
- The cutoff parameters for the dipole form factors are Lambda_V = 850 MeV and Lambda_A = 1086 MeV.
- The parameter R in the neutrino potential is R = 1.2 A^(1/3) fm.
- For ^48Ca decay, the tensor component contribution can reach 10% of the Gamow-Teller contribution; for ^76Ge, ^82Se, ^130Te, and ^136Xe, the tensor component is less than 1% of the dominant Gamow-Teller one.
- The chi_3 contribution to the effective operator expansion is estimated to be at least one order of magnitude smaller than the chi_2 contribution.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous realistic shell-model studies of two-neutrino double-beta decay matrix elements and Gamow-Teller strength distributions for the same set of nuclei (Coraggio 2017, Coraggio 2019). It contrasts with earlier shell-model calculations of neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements by Wu (1985), Song (1991), and Holt (2013), which used different treatments of short-range correlations and different perturbative expansions; in particular, Holt (2013) employed a chiral N^3LO potential and included effective operator contributions up to third order but used phenomenological shell-model Hamiltonians rather than consistently derived ones. The results are compared with those of the Madrid-Strasbourg group (Menendez 2009), which used empirically tuned shell-model Hamiltonians, and the general agreement serves as a benchmark for the quality of the realistic shell-model wave functions. The work enables more reliable predictions of neutrinoless double-beta decay half-lives by providing nuclear matrix elements derived from a consistent microscopic framework, which can be combined with experimental half-life limits to extract constraints on the effective neutrino mass.
