# Multiplicity in pp and AA collisions: the same power law from energy-momentum constraints in string production

> We show that the dependence of the charged particle multiplicity on the centre-of-mass energy of the collision is, in the String Percolation Model, driven by the same power law behavior in both proton-proton and nucleus- nucleus collisions. The observed different growths are a result of energy- momentum constraints that limit the number of formed strings at low en- ergy. Based on the very good description of the existing data, we provide predictions for future high energy LHC runs.

# Multiplicity in pp and AA collisions: the same power law from energy-momentum constraints in string production

## Key claim

The charged particle multiplicity in both proton-proton and nucleus-nucleus collisions follows the same underlying power-law dependence on centre-of-mass energy within the String Percolation Model, but only after accounting for energy-momentum conservation constraints that reduce the effective number of sea strings at low energy. The observed different growth rates (AA scaling as s^0.15 versus pp scaling as s^0.11) are therefore not a fundamental difference but a consequence of finite-energy corrections that persist up to fairly high energies (approximately 500 GeV).

## Method

The authors model mid-rapidity charged particle multiplicity using the String Percolation Model, where particle production is proportional to the number of colour strings formed in the collision, modified by a colour reduction factor F(η^t) that accounts for string overlap and fusion. Energy-momentum conservation is implemented by replacing the Glauber-model combinatorial factor N_A^(4/3) with N_A^(1+α(√s)), where α(√s) = (1/3)[1 - 1/(1+ln(√s/s0+1))], so that the wounded-nucleon limit is recovered at low energy and the full Glauber limit at high energy. A global fit to 116 data points (19 pp points from √s=53 to 7000 GeV and 97 AA points from √s=19.6 to 2760 GeV for AuAu, CuCu, and PbPb at various centralities) determines three parameters: normalization κ, power λ, and threshold scale √s0.

## Key numbers

- pp multiplicity scales as s^0.11; AA multiplicity scales as s^0.15 (from experimental data cited in the introduction)
- Best-fit parameters: κ = 0.63 ± 0.01, λ = 0.201 ± 0.003, √s0 = 245 ± 29 GeV
- Predicted pp charged multiplicity per participant at √s = 8 TeV: 6.10 ± 0.14; at √s = 10 TeV: 6.50 ± 0.16; at √s = 14 TeV: 7.14 ± 0.18
- Predicted PbPb charged multiplicity per participant at √s = 3.2 TeV: 8.69 ± 0.14; at √s = 3.9 TeV: 9.22 ± 0.15; at √s = 5.5 TeV: 10.1 ± 0.2
- The asymptotic high-energy approximation (√s ≳ 500 GeV) agrees within 5% of the full expression

## Context

This work extends the String Percolation Model (SPM) framework previously developed by Amelin (1993), Dias de Deus (2007), Brogueira (2007, 2009), and Bautista (2009, 2010, 2011) to provide a unified description of multiplicity in both pp and AA collisions. It addresses the discrepancy noted by Aamodt (2010) that AA collisions show a stronger energy dependence than pp collisions, which had been discussed by Levin (2011), Baier (2011), Lappi (2011), Rezaeian (2011), Tribedy (2011), and Capella (2011). The approach is closely related to Colour Glass Condensate and Glasma flux tube descriptions (Lappi 2006, Kharzeev 2004, Albacete 2005, 2010). The results enable predictions for future LHC runs at √s = 8, 10, and 14 TeV for pp and √s = 3.2, 3.9, and 5.5 TeV for PbPb, and can be adapted to asymmetric proton-nucleus collisions.
