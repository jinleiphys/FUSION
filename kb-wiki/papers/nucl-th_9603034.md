# The Dynamical Structure of the Delta-Resonance and its Effect on Two- and Three-Nucleon Systems

> The pion-nucleon interaction in the P33 partial wave is assumed to proceed simultaneously through the excitation of the Delta-isobar and through a phenomenologically introduced non-resonant background potential. The introduction of the background potential allows a more realistic parameterization of the pion-nucleon-Delta vertex compared with the previously used one without background. It also modifies the propagation of the Delta-isobar in the nuclear medium and gives rise to novel effective nucleon-Delta interactions. Their consequences on predictions for observables in the two-nucleon system at intermediate energies and in the three-nucleon bound state are studied.

# The Dynamical Structure of the Delta-Resonance and its Effect on Two- and Three-Nucleon Systems

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two different parameterizations of the P33 pion-nucleon resonance, one with a vanishing non-resonant background potential and one with a non-vanishing background, both reproduce the experimental P33 phase shifts with comparable quality, yet they yield dramatically different bare Delta-isobar masses and self-energy corrections; however, the resulting effects on observables in two- and three-nucleon systems are less spectacular than expected from those differences, and the inclusion of the background potential often worsens agreement with experimental data.

## Method

The authors model the P33 pion-nucleon interaction using a Hamiltonian with nucleon, Delta-isobar, and pion degrees of freedom, where the Delta-isobar is a fictitious baryon with fixed real mass and vanishing width, and the physical resonance emerges from coupling between the Delta-isobar and pion-nucleon states. Two parameterizations are compared: one (P) with zero pion-nucleon background potential and a small cutoff mass (Lambda = 288 MeV/c), and one (KB) with a separable non-resonant background potential and a larger cutoff mass (Lambda = 859 MeV/c). The effects on two-nucleon observables above pion threshold are computed using a coupled-channel approach that solves for NN, pi-d, and pi-NN processes, while the three-nucleon bound state is treated via exact Faddeev calculations for the zeroth-order result and first-order perturbation theory for the background contributions.

## Key numbers

- Bare Delta-isobar mass m_Delta^0 = 1311 MeV/c^2 for the parameterization without background (Pa); m_Delta^0 = 1801 MeV/c^2 for the parameterization with background (KB)
- Cutoff mass Lambda = 287.9 MeV/c for (P) and (Pa); Lambda = 859.36 MeV/c for (KB)
- Effective coupling f^2/(4 pi) = 0.306 (in units of 1/(hbar c)^3) for both parameterizations
- Triton binding energy E_T = -7.849 MeV for parameterization (P); E_T = -7.731 MeV for (KB) in zeroth order; E_T = -7.730 MeV for (KB) in first order
- Delta-isobar probability in trinucleon wave function P_Delta = 1.71% for (P); P_Delta = 1.16% for (KB)
- Pion probability in trinucleon wave function P_pi = 0.06% for (P); P_pi = 0.195% for (KB)
- Binding energy correction of two-baryon nature Delta E_2 = 0.456 MeV for (P); Delta E_2 = 0.372 MeV for (KB) in first order
- Binding energy correction of three-baryon nature Delta E_3 = -0.924 MeV for (P); Delta E_3 = -0.721 MeV for (KB) in first order
- Chi^2/N = 1.7 for (Pa) fit; Chi^2/N = 0.8 for (KB) fit; Chi^2/N = 10.0 for original (P) fit

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Dreissigacker et al., 1982; Popping et al., 1987; Pena et al., 1992, 1993) that used a Hamiltonian with Delta-isobar and pion degrees of freedom but assumed a vanishing pion-nucleon background potential in the P33 partial wave. The introduction of a non-vanishing background potential allows a more realistic parameterization of the pion-nucleon-Delta vertex with a cutoff mass of order 1 GeV, comparable to that used in realistic one-boson exchange two-nucleon potentials. The results enable future work to differentiate between the two parameterizations through studies of electromagnetic pion production and Compton scattering on the nucleon, and they call for an overall fit of the Hamiltonian including two-baryon potentials to data from two-nucleon scattering and unitarily coupled processes with one pion.
