# Local Thermal and Chemical Equilibration and the Equation of State in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> Thermodynamical variables and their time evolution are studied for central relativistic heavy ion collisions from 10.7 to 160 AGeV in the microscopic Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics model (UrQMD). The UrQMD model exhibits drastic deviations from equilibrium during the early high density phase of the collision. Local thermal and chemical equilibration of the hadronic matter seems to be established only at later stages of the quasi- isentropic expansion in the central reaction cell with volume 125 fm$^{3}$. distributions at all collision energies for $t\geq 10 fm/c$ with a unique Baryon energy spectra in this cell are approximately reproduced by Boltzmann rapidly dropping temperature. At these times the equation of state has a simple form: $P \cong (0.12-0.15) ε$. At 160 AGeV the strong deviation from chemical equilibrium is found for mesons, especially for pions, even at the late stage of the reaction. The final enhancement of pions is supported by experimental data.

## Key claim
Local thermal and chemical equilibrium in the central cell of relativistic heavy ion collisions simulated with UrQMD is established only at late times (t ~ 10 fm/c), not during the early high-density phase; at SPS energies, even at late stages, strong deviations from chemical equilibrium persist for mesons, especially pions, despite thermal equilibration of baryons.

## Method
The authors used the Ultrarelativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model to simulate central A+A collisions at 10.7, 40, and 160 AGeV. They extracted energy density, baryon density, and strangeness density from a central cell (volume 125 fm³) at each time step, then fed these into a Statistical Model of Ideal Hadron Gas (SM) with the same 55 baryon and 32 meson species to solve for temperature T, baryochemical potential μ_B, and strange chemical potential μ_S. They compared the cell results to equilibrated infinite-matter UrQMD box calculations and to Boltzmann fits of particle spectra.

## Key numbers
- Energy density in the central cell reaches up to 27 GeV/fm³ at the beginning of Pb+Pb collisions at 160 AGeV.
- At late stages (t = 10–20 fm/c), the energy density ε ≈ 0.6 GeV/fm³ for all reactions studied.
- The equation of state at these late times takes the form P ≅ (0.12–0.15) ε.
- Baryon energy spectra in the cell are reproduced by Boltzmann distributions for t ≥ 10 fm/c at all collision energies.
- At 160 AGeV, more than 70% of the total energy in the cell is deposited in the mesonic sector; at 10.7 AGeV, about 70% is carried by baryons.
- The entropy per baryon s/ρ_B is nearly constant at 30 (SPS) and 15 (AGS) during t = 10–18 fm/c.
- The maximal temperature in the cell approaches the critical temperature T_c = 200 MeV predicted by the MIT bag model.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Bravina (1998) for Au+Au collisions at AGS energy (10.7 AGeV) to SPS energies (40 and 160 AGeV) using the same UrQMD framework. It contradicts the implicit assumption of local thermal equilibrium (LTE) used in macroscopic hydrodynamic models (Landau, 1953; Strottman, 1979) and thermal models (Fermi, 1950; Hagedorn, 1965) that adopt LTE as an ad hoc assumption. The finding that pions remain out of chemical equilibrium even at late stages, with yields enhanced by a factor of two relative to the ideal gas SM, points to the need for mechanisms such as an effective chemical potential for pions (as discussed by various authors, 1998) and highlights that the high-energy-density region near the expected quark-gluon plasma phase boundary deserves further study.
