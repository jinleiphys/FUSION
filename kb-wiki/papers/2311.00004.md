# Double parton scattering versus jet quenching

> A novel observable, the double nuclear modification factor, is proposed to probe simultaneously the initial and final state effects in nucleus-nucleus collisions. An interesting competition between the combinatorial enhancement in the double parton scattering and the suppression due to parton energy loss can be observed in the production rate of two hard particles. In particular, the production of $J/ψ$ mesons in association with a $W$ boson is not suppressed but is enhanced in the region of moderate transverse momenta, unlike the case of unassociated (inclusive) $J/ψ$ production. At the same time, in the region of high enough transverse momenta the nuclear modification factor for associated $J/ψ+W$ production converges to that of unassociated $J/ψ$.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a double nuclear modification factor \(R_{\rm AA}(ab)\) as a novel observable that can simultaneously probe initial-state effects (combinatorial enhancement from double parton scattering, DPS) and final-state effects (suppression from parton energy loss/jet quenching) in nucleus-nucleus collisions, with the hedge that the enhancement is only visible at moderate transverse momenta and converges to the single-particle suppression at high transverse momenta.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for the single-parton scattering (SPS) and double-parton scattering (DPS) cross sections in nucleus-nucleus collisions, scaling the nucleon-nucleon cross sections by powers of the atomic number \(A\) and nuclear thickness functions. They introduce a \(K\)-factor defined as the ratio of SPS to DPS cross sections for associated production, and combine it with the measured nuclear modification factor \(R_{\rm AA}(J/\psi)\) and a combinatorial enhancement factor \(C\) (which depends on \(A\) and the effective DPS cross section \(\sigma_{\rm eff, pp}=15\) mb) to predict \(R_{\rm AA}(J/\psi, W)\) via Eq. (Rjw). The \(K\)-factor for \(J/\psi+W\) production is extracted from ATLAS data (Aad 2015) as a function of \(J/\psi\) transverse momentum.

## Key numbers

* The combinatorial enhancement factor \(C\) for minimum-bias Pb-Pb collisions (\(A=208\)) is \(C \sim 215\); for \(A=40\) it is \(C \sim 27\).
* The effective DPS cross section used throughout is \(\sigma_{\rm eff, pp}=15\) mb.
* The measured \(R_{\rm AA}(J/\psi)\) is \(\simeq 0.5\) at low transverse momenta (\(p_T \simeq 2\) GeV/\(c\)) and \(\simeq 0.3\) for \(p_T > 5\) GeV/\(c\).
* The \(K\)-factor for \(J/\psi+W\) production varies from \(\sim 1.4\) at \(p_T \in [8.5,10]\) GeV/\(c\) to \(\sim 374\) at \(p_T \in [60,150]\) GeV/\(c\).
* The predicted double nuclear modification factor for \(J/\psi+W\) at \(p_T \in [8.5,10]\) GeV/\(c\) is \(R_{\rm AA}(J/\psi, W) \simeq 30\), compared to \(R_{\rm AA}(J/\psi) \simeq 0.3\) for unassociated \(J/\psi\).

## Context

This work extends the study of double parton scattering in nucleus-nucleus collisions (reviewed by d'Enterria 2017) by proposing a new observable that combines DPS enhancement with jet quenching, which is traditionally characterized by the single-particle nuclear modification factor \(R_{\rm AA}\). The specific example of \(J/\psi+W\) production builds on ATLAS measurements (Aad 2015) and prior theoretical work on DPS in heavy-ion collisions (d'Enterria 2013). The results enable future experimental measurements of the double nuclear modification factor across various particle species and centrality classes, potentially revealing the interplay between initial-state partonic correlations and final-state medium effects.
