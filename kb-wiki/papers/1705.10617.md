# The nuclear configurational entropy impact parameter dependence in the Color-Glass Condensate

> The impact parameter (b) dependence on the saturation scale, in the framework of the Color Glass Condensate (b-CGC) dipole model, is investigated from the configurational point of view. During the calculations and analysis of the quantum nuclear states, the critical points of stability in the configurational entropy setup are computed, matching the experimental parameters that define the onset of the quantum regime in the b-CGC in the literature with very good accuracy. This new approach is crucial and important for understanding the stability of quantum systems in study of deep inelastic scattering processes.

## Key claim
The impact parameter dependence of the saturation scale in the b-CGC dipole model can be derived from the minima of the nuclear configurational entropy, matching the experimental parameter that defines the onset of the quantum regime in the b-CGC with an accuracy of approximately 1.23%.

## Method
The author computed the spatial Fourier transform of the total deep inelastic cross section using the b-CGC dipole amplitude, then calculated the modal fraction and the configurational entropy from the cross section. The critical points (minima) of the configurational entropy were evaluated numerically for three values of impact parameter (b = 0, 0.404, and 0.693 GeV^-1) at two values of the Bjorken variable (x = 10^-4 and x = 10^-5), using the fitted parameters from the b-CGC model.

## Key numbers
- For x = 10^-4: the configurational entropy minimum occurs at c = 4.100 for b = 0, at c = 4.050 for b = 0.404 GeV^-1, and at c = 4.120 for b = 0.693 GeV^-1.
- For x = 10^-5: the configurational entropy minimum occurs at c = 4.080 for b = 0, at c = 4.040 for b = 0.404 GeV^-1, and at c = 4.120 for b = 0.693 GeV^-1.
- The best result corresponds to impact parameter b = 0.404 GeV^-1, yielding the fitted parameter c = 4.01, matching the value in Carvalho (year not given in text).
- The uncertainties of the calculation are upper bounded by approximately 1.23%.

## Context
This work extends the application of nuclear configurational entropy, previously used for mesons and scalar glueballs by Bernardini (2016) and for Bose-Einstein condensates by Casadio (2016), to the b-CGC dipole model for deep inelastic scattering. The results match the onset of the quantum regime in the CGC found by Carvalho (year not given in text) and by Karapetyan (2017) in prior work. This approach enables a new method for determining the impact parameter dependence of the saturation scale and understanding stability of quantum systems in DIS processes.
