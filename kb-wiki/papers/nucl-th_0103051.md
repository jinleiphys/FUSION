# Chiral Symmetry in Charmonium - Pion Cross Section

> We perform a non-perturbative calculation of the $J/Ψ-π$ cross section using a $SU(2)\times SU(2)$ effective Lagrangian. Our results differ from those of previous calculations, specially in the description of vertices involving pions.

## Key claim
The two alternative forms of the $\pi DD^*$ interaction Lagrangian found in the literature are not equivalent for processes involving a virtual $D^*$ meson exchange; the form that breaks chiral $SU(2)\times SU(2)$ symmetry yields a much larger cross section than the chiral-symmetric form, especially near threshold.

## Method
The authors performed a non-perturbative calculation of the $J/\Psi-\pi$ cross section using an $SU(2)\times SU(2)$ effective Lagrangian for the process $\pi+J/\psi\rightarrow D+D^*$ with $D^*$ meson exchange. They compared two forms of the $\pi DD^*$ interaction Lagrangian from the literature, derived the equations of motion for the $D^*$ meson, and computed the differential cross section including isospin factors, using coupling constants $g_{\pi DD^*}=8.8$ and $g_{\psi D^*D^*}=7.64$ from prior work.

## Key numbers
- At $\sqrt{s}=4$ GeV, the chiral Lagrangian ${\cal L}_{\pi DD^*}^{(II)}$ gives $\sigma\sim3.5$ mb, while the non-chiral ${\cal L}_{\pi DD^*}^{(I)}$ gives $\sigma\sim11.5$ mb for $\pi+J/\psi\rightarrow D+D^*$.
- Prior calculations using $D$ meson exchange only gave a cross section of order $2.5$ mb at $\sqrt{s}=5$ GeV (Matinyan and Muller, 1998).
- Prior calculations including $D^*$ meson exchange and four-point couplings gave a cross section of around $30$ mb at $\sqrt{s}=5$ GeV (Haglin, 2000; Haga, 2000).
- Prior calculations including anomalous parity interactions gave cross sections of about $100$ mb at $\sqrt{s}=5$ GeV for $\pi+J/\psi\rightarrow D+D^*$ and about $60$ mb at $\sqrt{s}=5$ GeV for $\pi+J/\psi\rightarrow \eta_c\rho$ (Oset, 2000; Haga, 2000).

## Context
This work extends the line of effective Lagrangian calculations for charmonium-hadron cross sections pioneered by Matinyan and Muller (1998) and further developed by three other groups (Oset, 2000; Haglin, 2000; Haga, 2000; Linko, 2000; Ldk, 2000). The authors show that previous calculations using $SU(4)\times SU(4)$ symmetry as a starting point are inconsistent because they break chiral $SU(2)\times SU(2)$ symmetry when empirical masses are used, leading to numerically important differences. This work enables more reliable non-perturbative calculations of charmonium dissociation in the fireball phase of heavy-ion collisions, where $\sqrt{s} \le 5$ GeV.
