# Stability and instability of a hot and dilute nuclear droplet. II. Dissipative isoscalar modes

> Results on dissipative isoscalar modes of a hot and dilute nuclear droplet are presented. As compared to the adiabatic limit (part I), realistic dissipation yields a substantial reduction of the growth rates for all unstable modes, while the area of spinodal instability in the ($\varrho$,T)-plane remains unchanged. The qualitative features of multifragmentation through spinodal decomposition as obtained in the adiabatic limit are not significantly affected by dissipation.

## Key claim

Realistic dissipation (finite relaxation time \(\tau\)) reduces the growth rates of unstable isoscalar modes by factors of 1/2 to 1/4 compared to the adiabatic limit, but the spinodal instability region in the \((\varrho,T)\)-plane remains unchanged, and the qualitative features of multifragmentation via spinodal decomposition survive.

## Method

The authors extend their earlier adiabatic collective model (Part I) by including a finite relaxation time \(\tau\) for two-body collisions, which characterizes the decay of deformations of the local Fermi sphere. They solve the third-order secular equation for the eigenmode energies by diagonalizing a nonsymmetric real matrix, using the mass tensor \(\mathbb{B}\), the adiabatic stiffness tensor \(\overline{\mathbb{C}}\), and the difference \(\mathbb{C}'\) between diabatic and adiabatic stiffness tensors. The relaxation time is evaluated from Fermi-liquid theory, yielding \(\hbar/\tau \approx T^2/3\) MeV in the spinodal region, and the calculations are performed for gold-like (\(A=197\)) and tin-like (\(A=100\)) droplets with both soft (SkM\(^*\)) and stiff equations of state.

## Key numbers

* Growth rates of unstable bulk and surface modes are reduced from their adiabatic values by factors of 1/2 to 1/4 in the region of interest (around \(T \approx 3\)–5 MeV, \(\varrho \approx 0.25\varrho_{eq}\)).
* The relaxation rate is typically \(\hbar/\tau \approx T^2/3\) MeV in the spinodal region, with the dimensionless constant \(\eta = 0.36\) from \(\sigma = 40\) mb and \(\varrho_{eq} = 0.14\) fm\(^{-3}\); in numerical calculations \(\eta = 0.25\) is used.
* For a gold-like droplet at \(T = 3\) MeV, the growth rates of unstable compressional quadrupole modes (\(l=2\)) as a function of density show no crossings of different radial nodes \(n=0,1,2,3\), unlike the adiabatic limit.
* Reducing the relaxation time by a factor of 2 (to \(\eta = 0.12\)) increases bulk instability growth rates by roughly 30%, while surface instabilities remain almost unchanged.
* The adiabatic spinodal line is not affected by dissipation; the diabatic spinodal for bulk modes lies well inside the adiabatic spinodal, while no diabatic spinodal exists for surface modes.

## Context

This work extends the adiabatic (thermodynamic) limit of Part I (Nörenberg, Papp, Rozmej, 2002) by incorporating realistic dissipation via two-body collisions, using the diabatic approach to dissipative collective motion. It contrasts with the unrealistic instantaneous equilibration (\(\tau = 0\)) assumed in the adiabatic limit, and shows that while growth rates are substantially reduced, the spinodal region and qualitative features of multifragmentation remain robust. The results are consistent with those quoted in Kiderlen and Schuck (1997) and provide a basis for interpreting experimental evidence of spinodal decomposition in reactions such as \(^{155}\)Gd(36 MeV/u)+U and \(^{129}\)Xe(32 MeV/u)+Sn, as reported by Frankland (2001) and Borderie (2001).
