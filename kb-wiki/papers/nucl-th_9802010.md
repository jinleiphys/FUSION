# Pion Electroproduction and Pion-induced Dileptonproduction on the Nucleon

> By introducing electromagnetic formfactors in the spacelike region we extend an effective, gauge invariant Lagrangian model considering Born terms, baryon resonances up to 1.7 GeV and vector meson contributions (Rho, Omega) to calculate electroproduction of Pi-mesons. This model forms the basis for predictions of pion induced dilepton production on the nucleon. Therefore, the implemented formfactors are constructed in such a way that their analytic continuation into the timelike region is possible. It is shown that the seagull term and the N*(1520) resonance play a dominant role in the dilepton production.

## Key claim

By extending a gauge-invariant Lagrangian model for photoproduction to virtual photons with electromagnetic formfactors that can be analytically continued from the spacelike to the timelike region, the authors show that the seagull (Kroll-Rudermann) term and the N*(1520) resonance dominate the pion-induced dilepton production cross section at a center-of-mass energy of 1.75 GeV, though the model's agreement with electroproduction data worsens at higher energies.

## Method

The authors start from an effective Lagrangian model containing Born terms, baryon resonances up to 1.7 GeV, and vector meson contributions (rho, omega) previously fitted to photoproduction data. They introduce electromagnetic formfactors using a vector-meson-dominance (VMD) motivated scheme with an intrinsic dipole-like cutoff function and rho-omega mixing, then fix the additional parameters by fitting to pion electroproduction data including the Delta(1232) region and higher energies. The same hadronic tensor is then used via detailed balance to make parameter-free predictions for pion-induced dilepton production on the nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The rho-omega mixing parameter magnitude is |epsilon| ≈ 0.0315 with phase φ ≈ 100.5°.
- The omega meson width is taken as constant Γ_ω = 8.43 MeV.
- The omega-pi-rho coupling constant is g_{ωπρ} ≈ 3, extracted from the three-pion decay of the omega or from superconvergent sum rules.
- The maximum invariant dilepton mass accessible at center-of-mass energies up to 1.7 GeV is m_{e+e-} ≈ 0.780 GeV.
- The threshold for omega production in the pion-induced reaction is m + m_ω ≈ 1.72 GeV, with calculations performed at a CMS energy of 1.75 GeV.

## Context

This work extends a photoproduction model by Feuster and Mosel (1997) to virtual photons, enabling predictions for the half-offshell reaction of pion-induced dilepton production. The reaction is relevant for experiments planned at GSI Darmstadt with the HADES spectrometer, where dileptons serve as probes for in-medium modifications of vector meson masses in nuclei, as discussed by Weidmann and collaborators. The model enables parameter-free calculations of dilepton production on the nucleon, which are necessary as a baseline for interpreting pion-induced reactions on nuclear targets.
