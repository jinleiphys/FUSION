# Three-body model of $^{6}$He with non-local halo effective field theory potentials

> We study the $^6$He Borromean nucleus in coordinate representation within a three-body model with two-body potentials derived from cluster effective field theory (EFT). These potentials are originally developed in momentum space and Fourier transformed to provide non-local potentials in configuration space. We use hyperspherical coordinates in combination with the Lagrange-mesh technique to compute the ground state energy, root mean square radius and the E1 strength distribution of $^6$He. We also introduce a three-body interaction to eliminate dependencies on the cutoff parameter of the two-body potentials on the ground state energy. The E1 strength distribution exhibits a low lying resonance as expected. However it is strongly influenced by the choice of the three-body EFT interaction.

# Three-body model of $^6$He with non-local halo effective field theory potentials

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that non-local two-body potentials derived from cluster effective field theory (EFT) can be Fourier transformed to configuration space and used in a three-body hyperspherical model to compute ground state properties and the E1 strength distribution of the Borromean nucleus \(^6\)He. However, the E1 strength distribution is strongly influenced by the choice of the three-body EFT interaction, and the ground state energy requires a three-body force to eliminate cutoff dependencies of the two-body potentials.

## Method

The authors use hyperspherical coordinates combined with the Lagrange-mesh technique to solve the three-body Schrödinger equation for \(^6\)He, described as an alpha core plus two neutrons. Two-body cluster EFT potentials for the \(S_0\) \(n-n\) channel and the \(S_{1/2}\) and \(P_{3/2}\) \(\alpha-n\) channels are derived in momentum space at leading order, then Fourier transformed to obtain non-local potentials in configuration space. A projection technique is extended to non-local potentials to remove forbidden states arising from the \(S\)-wave \(\alpha-n\) potential. A Gaussian three-body interaction is introduced to reproduce the experimental ground state energy and remove cutoff dependencies. The E1 strength distribution is computed using a pseudostate approach with a Gaussian smoothing kernel matching the experimental energy resolution.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of \(^6\)He: \(-0.979\) MeV, close to the experimental value \(-0.97546(23)\) MeV (BBC12), obtained with three-body force parameters \(V_{03} = -15.2\) MeV and \(\rho_0 = 5\) fm.
- Root mean square radius of \(^6\)He: 2.81 fm, compared to the experimental value 2.49(4) fm (SNK21), using the experimental \(^4\)He rms radius of 1.463(6) fm (Si08).
- Forbidden state energy of the \(S\)-wave \(\alpha-n\) EFT potential: \(-13.45\) MeV for cutoff \(\Lambda = 400\) MeV with \(m=2\), comparable to the value 12.38 MeV from the Kanada et al. local potential (KKN79).
- Convergence of the E1 strength distribution achieved at \(K_{\text{max}} = 13\) and with \(N = 60\) Lagrange-Laguerre basis functions, yielding 525 pseudostates.
- E1 strength distributions differ at most 10% in the peak region when varying the exponent \(m\) of the regularization function.

## Context

This work extends the cluster EFT approach of Jin et al. (2014), who solved the three-body problem in momentum space using Faddeev equations, by implementing the same EFT potentials in configuration space where Coulomb interactions can be handled more naturally. The non-local potentials are derived from the effective range expansion following the framework of Furnstahl et al. (2020, 2022) and Chen et al. (2024). The results are compared to three-body calculations with phenomenological local potentials, such as those of Descouvemont et al. (2003) and Bacca et al. (2009), and show good agreement for the E1 strength distribution. The strong sensitivity to the three-body cutoff parameter indicates that higher-order three-body EFT interactions may be needed for predictive calculations of electromagnetic observables.
