# Kinetic freeze-out temperature and flow velocity extracted from transverse momentum spectra of final-state light flavor particles produced in collisions at RHIC and LHC

> The transverse momentum spectra of final-state light flavor particles produced in proton-proton (p-p), copper-copper (Cu-Cu), gold-gold (Au-Au), lead-lead (Pb-Pb), and proton-lead (p-Pb) collisions for different centralities at relativistic heavy ion collider (RHIC) and large hadron collider (LHC) energies are studied in the framework of a multisource thermal model. The experimental data measured by the STAR, CMS, and ALICE Collaborations are consistent with the results calculated by the multi-component Erlang distribution and Tsallis Statistics. The effective temperature and real temperature (kinetic freeze-out temperature) of interacting system at the stage of kinetic freeze-out, the mean transverse flow velocity and mean flow velocity of particles, and the relationships between them are extracted. The dependences of effective temperature and mean (transverse) momentum on rest mass, moving mass, centrality, and center-of-mass energy, and the dependences of kinetic freeze-out temperature and mean (transverse) flow velocity on centrality, center-of-mass energy, and system size are obtained.

## Key claim

The kinetic freeze-out temperature extracted from the Tsallis distribution is approximately independent of center-of-mass energy, event centrality, and system size within error range, with an average value of (0.071 ± 0.007) GeV, which is lower than values obtained from the Boltzmann distribution in previous work and from the blast-wave model.

## Method

The authors fitted transverse momentum (p_T) spectra of light flavor hadrons (pions, kaons, protons, phi, Lambda, Xi, Omega) from p-p, Cu-Cu, Au-Au, Pb-Pb, and p-Pb collisions at RHIC and LHC energies (0.2–7 TeV) using multi-component Erlang distributions and Tsallis statistics within a multisource thermal model. From the linear correlations between effective temperature T and rest mass m_0, the intercept T_0 was extracted as the kinetic freeze-out temperature, while mean transverse flow velocity ⟨u_T⟩ and mean flow velocity ⟨u⟩ were obtained from linear correlations between mean transverse momentum ⟨p_T⟩ and mean moving mass m̅, and between mean momentum ⟨p⟩ and m̅, respectively.

## Key numbers

- Mean kinetic freeze-out temperature T_0 = (0.071 ± 0.007) GeV from the Tsallis distribution, compared to (0.137 ± 0.007) GeV from the Boltzmann distribution in previous work and 0.177 GeV from an exponential shape of transverse mass spectrum
- Mean transverse flow velocity ⟨u_T⟩ ranges from 0.339c to 0.522c extracted from ⟨p_T⟩–m̅ correlations
- Mean flow velocity ⟨u⟩ ranges from 0.532c to 0.820c extracted from ⟨p⟩–m̅ correlations
- The relation ⟨u⟩ = (π/2)⟨u_T⟩ holds with almost zero χ² based on isotropic emission assumption
- The relation k' = (1/2)⟨u_T⟩² with χ²/dof = 1.472 connects the slope from T–m̅ correlation to mean transverse flow velocity
- The relation k = (1/3)k_T² with χ²/dof = 1.546 connects slopes from T–m_0 and ⟨p_T⟩–m_0 correlations
- Effective temperature T increases with rest mass and centrality; entropy index q decreases with rest mass and centrality, and increases with center-of-mass energy

## Context

This work extends the multisource thermal model approach of Liu (2013) to systematically extract kinetic freeze-out temperature and flow velocities across multiple collision systems and energies, complementing the blast-wave model of Schnedermann (1993) and thermal/statistical model analyses. The extracted T_0 = 0.071 GeV is lower than the chemical freeze-out temperature of 0.156 GeV from particle ratios and the QCD critical point prediction of 0.170 GeV, and is close to the 0.098 GeV from the blast-wave model. The method enables systematic comparison of freeze-out conditions across system sizes from p-p to Pb-Pb collisions and across energies from RHIC to LHC.
