# Universal Expression for the Lowest Excitation Energy of Natural Parity Even Multipole States

> We present a new expression for the energy of the lowest collective states in even-even nuclei throughout the entire periodic table. Our empirical formula is extremely valid and holds universally for all of the natural parity even multipole states. This formula depends only on the mass number and the valence nucleon numbers with six parameters. These parameters are determined easily and unambiguously from the data for each multipole state. We discuss the validity of our empirical formula by comparing our results with those of other studies and also by estimating the average and the dispersion of the logarithmic errors of the calculated excitation energies with respect to the measured ones.

# Universal Expression for the Lowest Excitation Energy of Natural Parity Even Multipole States

## Key claim
The authors present a six-parameter empirical formula that reproduces the lowest excitation energies of natural parity even multipole states (2_1^+ through 10_1^+) across the entire periodic table of even-even nuclei, but the formula describes overall trends rather than exact predictions, with the average logarithmic error being practically zero while individual deviations can be substantial (dispersion sigma up to 0.353 for 2_1^+ states).

## Method
The authors fit a six-parameter formula E_x = alpha * A^(-gamma) + beta_p * exp(-lambda_p * N_p) + beta_n * exp(-lambda_n * N_n) to measured excitation energies of the first 2_1^+, 4_1^+, 6_1^+, 8_1^+, and 10_1^+ states in even-even nuclei, where A is the mass number and N_p, N_n are valence proton and neutron numbers. Parameters were determined by minimizing the chi-squared value of the logarithmic error R_E = log(E_x_cal / E_x_exp) for each multipole state separately, using data from the Raman compilation (2001) for 2_1^+ states and the Table of Isotopes (8th edition) for higher multipole states. The authors compared their results with those from the S and D nucleon pair approximation calculations by Jia et al. (2007) for 48 nuclides in the Sn, Te, Ba, and Ce isotopic chains.

## Key numbers
- For the 2_1^+ state: 557 data points fitted with parameters alpha = 68.37 MeV, gamma = 1.34, beta_p = 0.83 MeV, beta_n = 1.17 MeV, lambda_p = 0.42, lambda_n = 0.28, yielding chi^2 = 0.126
- For the 4_1^+ state: 430 data points fitted with alpha = 268.04 MeV, gamma = 1.38, beta_p = 1.21 MeV, beta_n = 1.68 MeV, lambda_p = 0.33, lambda_n = 0.23, chi^2 = 0.071
- For the 6_1^+ state: 375 data points fitted with alpha = 598.17 MeV, gamma = 1.38, beta_p = 1.40 MeV, beta_n = 1.64 MeV, lambda_p = 0.31, lambda_n = 0.18, chi^2 = 0.069
- For the 8_1^+ state: 309 data points fitted with alpha = 1438.59 MeV, gamma = 1.45, beta_p = 1.34 MeV, beta_n = 1.50 MeV, lambda_p = 0.26, lambda_n = 0.15, chi^2 = 0.053
- For the 10_1^+ state: 265 data points fitted with alpha = 2316.85 MeV, gamma = 1.47, beta_p = 1.36 MeV, beta_n = 1.65 MeV, lambda_p = 0.21, lambda_n = 0.14, chi^2 = 0.034
- The six-parameter formula (Case IV) reduces chi^2 by 17% for 2_1^+, 14% for 4_1^+, 8% for 6_1^+, 4% for 8_1^+, and 3% for 10_1^+ compared to the four-parameter version (Case I)
- For the 48-nuclide comparison with Jia et al.: total chi^2 = 0.079 (Jia) versus 0.070 (this work); average logarithmic error R_bar = 0.200 (Jia) versus 0.073 (this work)
- Dispersion sigma of logarithmic error across all data: 0.353 (2_1^+), 0.265 (4_1^+), 0.260 (6_1^+), 0.227 (8_1^+), 0.183 (10_1^+)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous four-parameter empirical formula for 2_1^+ states (Ha and Cha, 2007) to all natural parity even multipole states up to 10_1^+. The formula builds on the N_pN_n scheme originally noted by Hamamoto (1965) and developed by Casten and Zamfir (1996) and Casten (1985), which attributes the simple pattern of collective observables when plotted against the product of valence nucleon numbers to the active role of valence proton-neutron interactions. The results are comparable in predictive power to the S and D nucleon pair approximation calculations by Jia, Zhang, and Zhao (2007), though the empirical formula maintains similar chi^2 values across all multipolarities while the pair approximation degrades for higher spins. This enables rapid estimation of lowest excitation energies for even-even nuclei without detailed nuclear structure calculations, and the systematic parameter values across multipole states may guide microscopic theories of nuclear collectivity.
