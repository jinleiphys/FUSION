# Quarkyonic matter and hadron-quark crossover from an ultracold atom perspective

> The dense matter equation of state is of great interest due to the recent development of astrophysical observations for neutron stars. A rapid increase in pressure indicates a continuous crossover from a hadron phase to a quark phase without any phase transitions, yet its microscopic mechanism remains elusive. Recently, a peak in the speed of sound and a baryon momentum-shell structure, which are predicted from a quarkyonic matter picture, have been regarded as key features of the hadron-quark crossover. In this work, we explore a field-theoretical framework to describe the hadron-quark crossover, drawing an analogy with the Bose-Einstein condensate to Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BEC-BCS) crossover established in ultracold atomic experiments. Strikingly, a peak in the speed of sound and the baryon momentum-shell structure can simultaneously be explained by the tripling fluctuation effect arising from a different context of quantum many-body physics. We demonstrate these properties in a simplified model and provide a microscopic derivation of the quarkyonic matter model within our field-theoretical framework.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the characteristic features of quarkyonic matter, a peak in the speed of sound and a baryon momentum-shell structure, can be explained by tripling fluctuation effects (analogous to pairing fluctuations in the BEC-BCS crossover) rather than requiring a genuine phase transition, but this demonstration is performed in a simplified non-relativistic 1D model with a short-range three-body interaction, not in full QCD.

## Method
The authors employ a field-theoretical framework based on the phase-shift representation of N-body clustering fluctuations, extending the Nozieres–Schmitt-Rink approach for the BEC-BCS crossover to three-body (tripling) fluctuations. They compute the thermodynamic potential and fermion number density from the dressed three-body propagator in a non-relativistic 1D model of three-color fermions with a short-range attractive three-body interaction, then extract the momentum distributions and speed of sound. The connection to relativistic quarkyonic matter is made by generalizing the phase-shift approach to a 3D relativistic system with constituent quarks and non-relativistic baryons, showing that the resulting baryon number density reproduces the phenomenological quarkyonic matter model of McLerran and Reddy (2019).

## Key numbers
- Three-body binding energy: \(\mathcal{B} = \frac{\Lambda^2}{m} e^{\frac{2\sqrt{3}\pi}{mV_3}}\), where \(\Lambda\) is the ultraviolet momentum cutoff and \(V_3\) is the three-body coupling strength.
- Width of the baryonic momentum shell at \(T \rightarrow 0\): \(\Delta_{\rm B} = \frac{M_{\rm B}\mathcal{B}}{3k_{\rm F}} + O(M_{\rm B}^2\mathcal{B}^2/k_{\rm F}^3)\), where \(M_{\rm B}=3m\) is the baryon mass and \(k_{\rm F}=\sqrt{2m\mu}\) is the Fermi momentum.
- Temperature used in numerical demonstrations: \(T = 0.1\mathcal{B}\), \(0.125\mathcal{B}\), and \(0.5\mathcal{B}\).
- In the relativistic extension, the net baryon number density is given by \(\frac{\rho}{N_{\rm c}} \simeq \frac{2k_{\rm FQ}^3}{3\pi^2} + \frac{2k_{\rm FB}^3}{3\pi^2} - \frac{2(k_{\rm FB}-\Delta_{\rm B})^3}{3\pi^2}\), where \(k_{\rm FQ}=\sqrt{\mu^2-m_{\rm Q}^2}\) and \(k_{\rm FB}=\sqrt{2M_{\rm B}(N_{\rm c}\mu - N_{\rm c}m_{\rm Q} + \mathcal{B})}\).

## Context
This work extends the quarkyonic matter picture originally proposed by McLerran and Pisarski (2007) and the hadron-quark crossover EOS formulated by McLerran and Reddy (2019), which introduced a phenomenological baryonic momentum shell and peaked speed of sound. The authors provide a microscopic derivation of that phenomenological model using tripling fluctuation theory, drawing an analogy with the BEC-BCS crossover in ultracold Fermi gases (Nozieres and Schmitt-Rink, 1985). This framework enables future finite-temperature studies relevant to binary neutron-star mergers and core-collapse supernovae, and it offers a basis for improving quarkyonic matter models such as the extended relativistic mean-field model (Xia et al., 2018) and the extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model.
