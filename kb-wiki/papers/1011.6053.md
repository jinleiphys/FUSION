# Effects of Momentum Conservation and Flow on Angular Correlations at RHIC

> Correlations of azimuthal angles observed at RHIC have gained great attention due to the prospect of identifying fluctuations of parity-odd regions in the field sector of QCD. Whereas the observable of interest related to parity fluctuations involves subtracting opposite-sign from same-sign correlations, the STAR collaboration reported the same-sign and opposite-sign correlations separately. It is shown here how momentum conservation combined with collective elliptic flow contributes significantly to this class of correlations, though not to the difference between the opposite- and same-sign observables. The effects are modeled with a crude simulation of a pion gas. Though the simulation reproduces the scale of the correlation, the centrality dependence is found to be sufficiently different in character to suggest additional considerations beyond those present in the pion gas simulation presented here.

## Key claim

Momentum conservation combined with collective elliptic flow contributes significantly to same-sign and opposite-sign azimuthal correlations measured at RHIC, but does not affect the difference between them (the parity-odd observable). The authors' crude pion gas simulation reproduces the scale of the correlations but fails to match the centrality dependence, suggesting that additional physics beyond the simple model is required.

## Method

The authors derive a momentum sum rule linking the weighted correlation \(\gamma'_{\rm pbal} = \gamma'_{\rm ss} + \gamma'_{\rm os}/2\) to elliptic flow and multiplicity, accounting for finite detector acceptance and neutral particles. They then construct a pion gas cascade with fixed s-wave cross sections (15 or 30 mb), initial thermal momenta at \(T_0 = 250\) MeV, Bjorken longitudinal expansion, and local charge conservation enforced at the initial time \(\tau_0 = 1.0\) fm/\(c\). The system is evolved with straight-line trajectories punctuated by isotropic collisions, and the resulting correlations are compared to STAR data for both full acceptance and a STAR-like acceptance (pseudo-rapidity \(|\eta| < 1\), \(p_t\) between 200 MeV/\(c\) and 2 GeV/\(c\)).

## Key numbers

- For full acceptance with \(\sigma = 15\) mb and radii \(R_x = 1.8, R_y = 2.5\) fm: \(v_2 = 0.0677\), \(M\gamma'_{\rm pbal}/v'_2 = -0.985\), \(M\gamma_{\rm pbal}/v_2 = -1.901\)
- For STAR acceptance with \(\sigma = 15\) mb and same radii: \(v_2 = 0.0910\), \(M\gamma'_{\rm pbal}/v'_2 = -0.817\), \(M\gamma_{\rm pbal}/v_2 = -1.324\)
- For STAR acceptance with \(\sigma = 30\) mb and radii \(R_x = 1.5, R_y = 3.0\) fm: \(v_2 = 0.204\), \(M\gamma'_{\rm pbal}/v'_2 = -0.847\), \(M\gamma_{\rm pbal}/v_2 = -1.407\)
- For STAR acceptance with \(\sigma = 15\) mb and smaller system (\(R_x = 0.9, R_y = 1.25\) fm, 250 particles): \(v_2 = 0.0667\), \(M\gamma'_{\rm pbal}/v'_2 = -0.763\), \(M\gamma_{\rm pbal}/v_2 = -1.1354\)
- The experimental ratio \(M\gamma_{\rm pbal}/v_2\) from STAR varies by a factor of three from peripheral to central collisions, while the cascade model yields values near 1.3 with only 10% variation across all parameter changes

## Context

This work extends the theoretical discussion of momentum conservation effects on angular correlations initiated by Pratt (2010) and Bzdak (2010), and addresses the STAR collaboration's measurements of same-sign and opposite-sign correlations (2009). The authors show that the combination \(\gamma_{\rm pbal} = \gamma_{\rm ss} + \gamma_{\rm os}/2\) best isolates momentum conservation effects, complementing the parity-odd observable \(\gamma_{\rm qbal}\) which is dominated by charge conservation overlaid on elliptic flow as shown by Schlichting (2010). The failure of the simple pion gas model to reproduce the centrality dependence of the experimental ratio enables future work to incorporate more realistic dynamics, such as viscous hydrodynamics or more detailed hadronic transport, to extract information about the shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma.
