# Low energy peripheral scaling in Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering and uncertainty quantification

> We analyze the peripheral structure of the nucleon-nucleon interaction for LAB energies below 350 MeV. To this end we transform the scattering matrix into the impact parameter representation by analyzing the scaled phase shifts $(L+1/2) δ_{JLS} (p)$ and the scaled mixing parameters $(L+1/2)ε_{JLS}(p)$ in terms of the impact parameter $b=(L+1/2)/p$. According to the eikonal approximation, at large angular momentum $L$ these functions should become an universal function of $b$, {\it independent} on $L$. This allows to discuss in a rather transparent way the role of statistical and systematic uncertainties in the different long range components of the two-body potential. Implications for peripheral waves obtained in chiral perturbation theory interactions to fifth order (N5LO) or from the large body of NN data considered in the SAID partial wave analysis are also drawn from comparing them with other phenomenological high-quality interactions, constructed to fit scattering data as well. We find that both N5LO and SAID peripheral waves disagree more than $5 σ$ with the Granada-2013 statistical analysis, more than $ 2 σ$ with the 6 statistically equivalent potentials fitting the Granada-2013 database and about $1 σ$ with the historical set of 13 high-quality potentials developed since the 1993 Nijmegen analysis.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, for nucleon-nucleon scattering below 350 MeV laboratory energy, scaled phase shifts and mixing parameters become a universal function of the impact parameter at high angular momentum, but this scaling holds only within the uncertainties of the long-range potential; both the N5LO chiral perturbation theory and SAID partial wave analysis peripheral waves disagree with the Granada-2013 statistical analysis by more than 5 sigma, with the 6 statistically equivalent Granada potentials by more than 2 sigma, and with the historical set of 13 high-quality potentials by about 1 sigma.

## Method

The authors transformed the nucleon-nucleon scattering matrix into the impact parameter representation by analyzing scaled phase shifts (L+1/2)δ_JLS(p) and scaled mixing parameters (L+1/2)ε_JLS(p) as functions of the impact parameter b=(L+1/2)/p, following the eikonal approximation which predicts these functions become independent of L at large angular momentum. They compared peripheral waves from chiral perturbation theory at fifth order (N5LO) and from the SAID partial wave analysis against 13 high-quality phenomenological potentials (Nijmegen PWA, Nijm I, NijmII, Reid93, AV18, CD Bonn, Spectator, and six Granada potentials) that share the same charge-dependent one-pion exchange tail, quantifying discrepancies using standardized deviations.

## Key numbers

- The analysis covers laboratory energies below 350 MeV, corresponding to a maximum center-of-mass momentum p_max ~ sqrt(M m_π)
- The Granada-2013 database contains 6713 data points with normalizations in the LAB energy range between 1 eV (np) and 338 KeV (pp) up to 350 MeV for both np and pp
- The elementariness radius for one-pion exchange is r_c = 3.0 fm; for one-plus-two-pion exchange it is r'_c = 1.8 fm
- For L=4 (G waves), the maximum LAB energy not intruding below r_c=1.8 fm is 518.3 MeV; for r_c=3 fm it is 186.6 MeV
- For L=5 (H waves), the maximum LAB energy not intruding below r_c=1.8 fm is 774.2 MeV; for r_c=3 fm it is 278.7 MeV
- For L=6 (I waves), the maximum LAB energy not intruding below r_c=1.8 fm is 1081.4 MeV; for r_c=3 fm it is 389.3 MeV
- The most accurate determination of the pion-nucleon coupling constant from the Granada-2013 database yields f^2=0.0763(1) with χ^2/ν=1.025

## Context

This work extends the long tradition of peripheral wave analysis in nucleon-nucleon scattering dating back to Okun (1959) and Signell (1959), and builds upon the high-quality potential developments since the Nijmegen analysis (Stoks, 1993). The authors use the Granada-2013 database and the 6 Granada potentials (DS-OPE, DS-χTPE, SOG-OPE, SOG-χTPE, DS-ΔBO, SOG-ΔBO) along with the Nijmegen PWA, Nijm I, NijmII, Reid93, AV18, CD Bonn, and Spectator potentials. The peripheral plot method enables quantitative comparison of peripheral waves from chiral perturbation theory and SAID analyses against statistically well-founded potentials, providing a tool to identify which phase shifts behave as outliers and to separate statistical from systematic uncertainties in the long-range components of the two-body potential.
