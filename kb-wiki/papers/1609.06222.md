# Collisional broadening of angular correlations in a multiphase transport model

> Systematic comparisons of jetlike correlation data to radiative and collisional energy loss model calculations are essential to extract transport properties of the quark-gluon medium created in relativistic heavy ion collisions. This paper presents a transport study of collisional broadening of jetlike correlations, by following parton-parton collision history in a multiphase transport (AMPT) model. The correlation shape is studied as a function of the number of parton-parton collisions suffered by a high transverse momentum probe parton ($N_{\rm coll}$) and the azimuth of the probe relative to the reaction plane ($ϕ_{\rm fin.}^{\rm probe}$). Correlation is found to broaden with increasing $N_{\rm coll}$ and $ϕ_{\rm fin.}^{\rm probe}$ from in- to out-of-plane direction. This study provides a transport model reference for future jet-medium interaction studies.

## Key claim

Collisional energy loss alone, as implemented in the AMPT parton transport model, broadens jetlike angular correlations; the broadening increases with the number of parton-parton collisions suffered by a high-transverse-momentum probe parton and with the probe's azimuthal angle from in-plane to out-of-plane direction, but the paper notes that AMPT does not contain jets and that the correlations studied are with respect to a single initially energetic parton, not a true dijet.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of AMPT (v2.26t5) to simulate 10^5 Au+Au collisions at fixed impact parameter b = 8 fm at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. They traced the collision history of initial partons with transverse momentum pT > 3 GeV/c (defined as probe partons) and identified which medium partons interacted with each probe. They studied the azimuthal angular correlation between final-state medium partons (with 1 < pT_med < 2 GeV/c) and final-state probe partons, fitting the delta-phi distributions to a Gaussian plus constant pedestal to extract the width sigma.

## Key numbers

* The reduction factor of high-pT partons from initial to final state is approximately 3-4, and this factor is approximately pT independent.
* The angular dispersion of the probe parton direction from initial to final state is approximately 0.21*sqrt(N_coll), corresponding to about 12 degrees per collision.
* The total parton scattering cross section used in the simulation is sigma = 3 mb, with strong coupling constant alpha_s = 0.33 and Debye screening mass mu_D = 2.265/fm.
* The simulation used 10^5 Au+Au events at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV with impact parameter b = 8 fm.

## Context

This work provides a transport model reference for jet-medium interaction studies, extending prior collisional energy loss studies (Mustafa 2003, Djordjevic 2006, Adil 2006, Qin 2007, Peigne 2008, Shin 2010, Berrehrah 2014) that focused on the amount of energy loss rather than on angular correlations. The results are relevant to experimental studies of jetlike correlations at low-to-intermediate pT (Adams 2005, Adler 2005, Adare 2008, Aggarwal 2010, Agakishiev 2010, Agakishiev 2014) and to recent work showing that jetlike correlation width increases with collision centrality (Jiang 2015). The paper notes that AMPT contains only elastic collisional energy loss and cannot study radiative effects, and suggests future work embedding Pythia dijets into AMPT to study both jet partners.
