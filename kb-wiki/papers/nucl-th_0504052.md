# Self-consistent relativistic random phase approximation with vacuum polarization

> We present a theoretical formulation for the description of nuclear excitations within the framework of relativistic random-phase approximation whereby the vacuum polarization arising from nucleon-antinucleon fields is duly accounted for. The vacuum contribution to Lagrangian is explicitly described as extra new terms of interacting mesons by means of the derivative expansion of the effective action. It is shown that the self-consistent calculation yields zero eigenvalue for the spurious isoscalar-dipole state and also conserves the vector-current density.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including vacuum polarization via a derivative-expansion method in the relativistic random-phase approximation (RRPA) yields a self-consistent description of nuclear excitations, achieving zero excitation energy for the spurious isoscalar-dipole state and conserving the vector-current density, results that previous RRPA calculations with vacuum polarization (using local-density approximation) had failed to obtain.

## Method

The authors start from the Walecka sigma-omega model and incorporate vacuum polarization by expanding the one-baryon-loop effective action in derivatives of the meson fields, keeping the first three terms (a potential term and two wave-function renormalization terms). They solve the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) equations for the ground state of finite nuclei, including these vacuum corrections, and then use the resulting mean fields to construct the meson propagators and the polarization function for the RRPA equation. The RRPA equation is solved for the isoscalar-dipole response in O-16 and Ca-40, and the transition charge and current densities are checked against the conservation law.

## Key numbers

* The parameter set used for the RHA calculation: m_sigma = 458.0 MeV, m_omega = 814.0 MeV, g_sigma = 7.10, g_2 = 24.09 fm^{-1}, g_3 = -15.99, g_omega = 8.85.
* Coulomb responses for the isoscalar-dipole mode are shown at momentum transfers q = 237 MeV (for O-16) and q = 118 MeV (for Ca-40).
* The spurious isoscalar-dipole state appears at zero excitation energy in both O-16 and Ca-40.
* For the lowest positive-energy isoscalar-dipole state in O-16, the excitation energy is 12.94 MeV; the transition current is shown to be sufficiently conserved (the violation Delta-rho is small, as depicted in Fig. 2).

## Context

This work extends earlier RRPA calculations that used the local-density approximation for vacuum polarization (e.g., Ichii et al. (1987), Wehrberger et al. (1987), Horikawa and Toki (1988), Furnstahl (1989)), which violated self-consistency and failed to shift the spurious isoscalar-dipole state to zero energy. The present method builds on the derivative-expansion approach of Aitchison et al. (1984), Chanfray et al. (1985), and Ohta et al. (1985), and on the RHA treatment of finite nuclei by Haga et al. (2004). It enables fully consistent RHA+RRPA calculations for excited states, including excitations from negative-energy to positive-energy states, and opens the door to testing extended models (e.g., with surface pion condensation or omega tensor coupling) for a wide variety of nuclear observables.
