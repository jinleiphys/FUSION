# Legendre Analysis of Hadronic Reactions

> Expansions over Legendre functions are suggested as a model-independent way of compact presentation of modern precise and high-statistics data for two-hadron reactions. Some properties of the expansions are described.

# Legendre Analysis of Hadronic Reactions

## Key claim

Legendre expansions provide a model-independent method for compact presentation of high-statistics two-hadron reaction data, but the series is formally infinite and must be truncated in practice because higher coefficients have errors exceeding their fitted values. The approach works for both unpolarized cross sections and polarization variables, though polarization requires associated Legendre functions or Wigner d-harmonics rather than simple Legendre polynomials.

## Method

The authors propose expanding unpolarized differential cross sections for two-hadron reactions into a series of Legendre polynomials in z = cosθ, where θ is the center-of-mass polar angle, with energy-dependent coefficients A_J(E). For polarization variables multiplied by the unpolarized cross section, they use associated Legendre functions P_J^2(z) or Wigner d-harmonics instead. The expansion coefficients are truncated at a finite J determined by when experimental uncertainties exceed the fitted coefficient values.

## Key numbers

- For photoproduction reactions γp → ηp and γp → π⁰p, the Legendre expansion approach was illustrated using data from Refs. [CBC] and [A2] respectively (no specific coefficient values given in this paper).

## Context

This paper extends the author's previous work on Legendre analysis of hadronic reactions, with examples drawn from photoproduction data from the CLAS, CBELSA/TAPS (CBC), and A2 collaborations. The approach is presented as an alternative to traditional multi-panel figures showing angular distributions at fixed energies or excitation functions at fixed angles, which the author argues are bulky and non-visual for whole data-sets. The paper states that a more detailed discussion with proofs and physical examples will appear in a separate paper (Azimov, 2016, unpublished).
