# Towards an automated tool to evaluate the impact of the nuclear modification of the gluon density on quarkonium, D and B meson production in proton-nucleus collisions

> We propose a simple and model-independent procedure to account for the impact of the nuclear modification of the gluon density as encoded in nuclear collinear PDF sets on two-to-two partonic hard processes in proton-nucleus collisions. This applies to a good approximation to quarkonium, D and B meson production, generically referred to H. Our procedure consists in parametrising the square of the parton scattering amplitude, A_{gg -> H X} and constraining it from the proton-proton data. Doing so, we have been able to compute the corresponding nuclear modification factors for J/psi, Upsilon and D^0 as a function of y and P_T at sqrt(s_NN)=5 and 8 TeV in the kinematics of the various LHC experiments in a model independent way. It is of course justified since the most important ingredient in such evaluations is the probability of each kinematical configuration. Our computations for D mesons can also be extended to B meson production. To further illustrate the potentiality of the tool, we provide --for the first time-- predictions for the nuclear modification factor for eta_c production in pPb collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim
The authors propose a simple, model-independent procedure to evaluate the impact of nuclear modifications of the gluon density (as encoded in nuclear PDF sets) on quarkonium, D and B meson production in proton-nucleus collisions, but they explicitly restrict the analysis to gluon-induced 2-to-2 partonic processes and caution that other cold nuclear matter effects (nuclear absorption, energy loss, saturation) are not included, so the procedure gives only the nPDF contribution, not a full prediction.

## Method
The authors parametrize the squared parton scattering amplitude for the 2-to-2 process gg → HX using a functional form with four parameters (λ, κ, ⟨P_T⟩, n), then fit these parameters to proton-proton cross-section data for J/ψ, Υ(1S), η_c, and D^0 from LHC experiments. They then fold the same parametrized amplitude with one proton PDF and one nuclear PDF (EPS09LO, EPS09NLO, or nCTEQ15) to compute pPb cross sections and nuclear modification factors R_pPb at √s_NN = 5.02 and 8 TeV, using the HELAC-Onia framework and LHAPDF libraries.

## Key numbers
- J/ψ fit parameters (CT14NLO, LHCb data): λ = 0.296 ± 0.118, κ = 0.558, ⟨P_T⟩ = 4.5 (fixed), n = 2 (fixed)
- J/ψ fit parameters (CT14NLO, ATLAS & CMS data): λ = 0.378, κ = 0.743 ± 0.0395, ⟨P_T⟩ = 4.5 (fixed), n = 2 (fixed)
- Υ(1S) fit parameters (CT14NLO): λ = 0.768, κ = 0.0841 ± 0.0271, ⟨P_T⟩ = 13.5 (fixed), n = 2 (fixed)
- η_c fit parameters (CT14NLO): λ = 0.558, κ = 0.398, ⟨P_T⟩ = 4.5 (fixed), n = 2 (fixed)
- D^0 fit parameters (CT14NLO): λ = 2.29, κ = 1.11, ⟨P_T⟩ = 0.88, n = 2 (fixed)
- All pPb results computed at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV (and 8 TeV for some predictions)
- Nuclear modification factor R_pPb defined as dσ_{pPb} / (A dσ_{pp}) with A = 208 for lead

## Context
This work extends the approach of Ferreiro et al. (2008-2009) who developed the Jin Monte Carlo code for quarkonium production with exact kinematics and nuclear geometry, but the present tool is simpler, focuses only on nPDF effects with 2-to-2 kinematics, and can use any nPDF set from LHAPDF5/6. It enables model-independent comparisons of nPDF predictions with LHC pPb data for J/ψ, Υ, D^0, and provides first predictions for η_c production in pPb collisions. The procedure is complementary to full Glauber-based approaches and could eventually be merged with them.
