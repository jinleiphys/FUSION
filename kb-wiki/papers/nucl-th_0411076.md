# Jet quenching of massive quarks in a nuclear medium

> Utilizing the generalized factorization of twist-4 processes, we derive the modified heavy quark fragmentation function after considering the gluon radiation induced by multiple scattering in DIS. It is found that the mass effects of heavy quark may reduce the gluon formation time and change the medium size dependence of heavy quark energy loss. The radiative energy loss is also significantly suppressed relative to a light quark due to the dead-cone effect.

## Key claim

The paper derives the modified heavy quark fragmentation function in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) using twist-4 generalized factorization, finding that heavy quark mass effects reduce the gluon formation time and change the medium size dependence of energy loss from quadratic to linear under certain kinematic conditions, and that radiative energy loss is significantly suppressed relative to light quarks due to the dead-cone effect.

## Method

The authors consider charm quark production via charge-current interaction in DIS off a large nucleus, evaluating 23 cut diagrams for double scattering processes using the generalized factorization of twist-4 parton correlation functions. They derive modified splitting functions and an effective heavy quark fragmentation function that incorporates medium-induced gluon radiation, then compute the fractional energy loss carried by the radiated gluon as a function of nuclear size, momentum transfer \(Q^2\), and Bjorken variable \(x_B\).

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass \(M = 1.5\) GeV is used in numerical calculations.
- Heavy quark energy loss scales as \(\langle\Delta z_g^Q\rangle \sim C_A \frac{\widetilde{C} \alpha_s^2}{N_c} \frac{x_B}{x_A Q^2}\) (linear in nuclear size \(R_A\)) when \(x_B/Q^2 \gg x_A/M^2\) (small quark energy or small \(Q^2\)).
- Heavy quark energy loss scales as \(\langle\Delta z_g^Q\rangle \sim C_A \frac{\widetilde{C} \alpha_s^2}{N_c} \frac{x_B}{x_A^2 Q^2}\) (quadratic in nuclear size \(R_A\)) when \(x_B/Q^2 \ll x_A/M^2\) (large quark energy or large \(Q^2\)).
- The gluon formation time for heavy quark radiation is \(\tau_f = \frac{2z(1-z)q^-}{\ell_T^2 + (1-z)^2 M^2}\), which is shorter than for light quarks.

## Context

This work extends the twist-4 factorization approach previously applied to light quark energy loss in nuclei (Wang and Zhang, 2003) to the case of massive quarks, addressing the few existing studies on heavy quark energy loss (Kharzeev, 1996; Gyulassy and Wang, 1994; Dokshitzer and Kharzeev, 2001). The results can be extended to hot and dense media, enabling predictions for heavy quark production and suppression in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
