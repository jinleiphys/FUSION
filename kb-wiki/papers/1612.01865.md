# Mixing of charged and neutral Bose condensates at nonzero temperature and magnetic field

> It is expected that in the interior of compact stars a proton superconductor coexists with and couples to a neutron superfluid. Starting from a field-theoretical model for two complex scalar fields - one of which is electrically charged - we derive a Ginzburg-Landau potential which includes entrainment between the two fluids and temperature effects from thermal excitations of the two scalar fields and the gauge field. The Ginzburg-Landau description is then used for an analysis of the phase structure in the presence of an external magnetic field. In particular, we study the effect of the superfluid on the flux tube phase by computing the various critical magnetic fields and deriving an approximation for the flux tube interaction. As a result, we point out differences to the naive expectations from an isolated superconductor, for instance the existence of a first-order flux tube onset, resulting in a more complicated phase structure in the region between type-I and type-II superconductivity.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a system where a charged Bose condensate (superconductor) coexists with a neutral Bose condensate (superfluid), the transition between type-I and type-II superconductivity does not occur at the usual Ginzburg-Landau parameter value of κ² = 1/2, and the three critical magnetic fields H_c, H_c1, and H_c2 do not intersect at a single point, leading to a first-order flux tube onset and a more complicated phase structure in the transition region.

## Method

Starting from a relativistic field-theoretical model for two complex scalar fields (one charged, one neutral) with entrainment coupling, the authors derive a Ginzburg-Landau potential including temperature effects from thermal excitations of both scalar fields and the gauge field. They compute the three critical magnetic fields (H_c, H_c1, H_c2) and derive an analytical expression for the flux tube interaction at large separations using asymptotic solutions of the linearized equations of motion, then determine the phase transition points by minimizing the Gibbs free energy of a flux tube lattice.

## Key numbers

- The elementary charge is set to q = 2e ≃ 0.171 (Gaussian units), corresponding to a proton Cooper pair
- Chemical potentials are set to μ₁ = 1.5m and μ₂ = 1.8m, with m ≡ m₁ = m₂
- The cross-coupling values used are h = -0.1 and h = +0.1, with the constraint |h| < √(λ₁λ₂) for boundedness
- The second-order flux tube onset becomes discontinuous at α ≃ 0.292 for the h < 0 parameter set
- For the h < 0 case, temperature T = 2.43m is used; for h > 0, T = 3.5m
- The thermal mass coefficients include terms such as (2λ₁ - h + 6πq²)/6 for the charged field and (2λ₂ - h)/6 for the neutral field

## Context

This work extends previous non-relativistic approaches to coupled superfluid-superconductor systems (Alpar, Langer, Sauls 1984; Alford 2007) by including temperature dependence and entrainment effects in a Ginzburg-Landau framework. It builds on the calculation of flux tube interaction in single-component superconductors (Kramer 1971) and extends the work of Buckley (2003) on two-component systems. The results enable future full numerical evaluations of the flux tube phase diagram for neutron star interiors, where proton superconductivity coexists with neutron superfluidity, and may also apply to two-component Bose-Einstein condensates in synthetic magnetic fields or two-band superconductors.
