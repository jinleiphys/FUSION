# Mass--dependence of the Lambda hypernuclear decay widths

> Two different approaches have been employed for the evaluation of the decay widths of Lambda-hypernuclei (ranging from $^5_Λ$He to $^{208}_Λ$Pb) with the polarization propagator method. In ref.[1], the two-nucleon stimulated non-mesonic decay, $ΛNN\to NNN$, has been parameterized phenomenologically by means of data on the pion-nucleus optical potential. The other approach [2] consisted in a fully microscopic description of the non-mesonic decays through the first order approximation of the so-called bosonic-loop-expansion. Both calculations reproduce, with approximately the same accuracy, the experimental decay rates for the whole range of mass numbers considered.

## Key claim

Both a phenomenological parameterization (using pion-nucleus optical potential data) and a fully microscopic one-boson-loop calculation reproduce experimental Lambda hypernuclear decay rates with approximately the same accuracy across the mass range from 5He to 208Pb, but the two approaches treat the Landau parameters g' and g'_Lambda differently despite using the same numerical value for g'.

## Method

The author employed the polarization propagator method to compute the Lambda self-energy, whose imaginary part gives the decay width. In the phenomenological approach (ref. Ga1), the two-nucleon stimulated non-mesonic decay (Lambda NN -> NNN) was parameterized using data on the pion-nucleus optical potential from pionic atoms, with the short-range correlation parameters g' and g'_Lambda fixed to reproduce the non-mesonic decay rate of 12C. In the microscopic approach (ref. Ga2), a fully microscopic one-boson-loop approximation was used, with calculations performed in nuclear matter and then applied to finite nuclei by assigning average Fermi momenta to three mass regions (medium-light, medium, and heavy).

## Key numbers

- The two-body induced decay width (Gamma_2) is about 15% of the total width and is rather independent of hypernuclear dimension, with the exception of 5He.
- For the phenomenological calculation, the Landau parameters were fixed to g' = 0.8 and g'_Lambda = 0.4 to reproduce the non-mesonic decay rate of 12C.
- For the microscopic calculation, hypernuclei were classified into three mass regions: medium-light (A ~ 10) with average Fermi momentum 1.1 fm^-1; medium (A ~ 30-60) with 1.2 fm^-1; heavy (A >= 200) with 1.36 fm^-1.
- In the microscopic one-boson-loop calculation, the experimental data for all three mass regions are reproduced when g' = 0.8 (with g'_Lambda fixed to 0.4).

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical approaches to Lambda hypernuclear decay by comparing two distinct methods within the polarization propagator framework: a phenomenological parameterization based on pion-nucleus optical potential data (ref. Ga1) and a microscopic one-boson-loop expansion (ref. Ga2). The results are consistent with experimental data from Sz91, Ar93, No95, Ku98, Pa00, and Sa00, and with shell model calculations by Os93 and Mo94. The study demonstrates that using averaged Fermi momenta to simulate Lambda decay in finite nuclei is reliable, and that both phenomenological and microscopic approaches can successfully describe the saturation of the Lambda N -> NN interaction in nuclei.
