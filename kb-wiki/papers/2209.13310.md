# Elastic properties of nuclear pasta in a fully three-dimensional geometry

> Realistic estimations on the elastic properties of neutron star matter are carried out with a large strain ($\varepsilon \lesssim 0.5$) in the framework of relativistic-mean-field model with Thomas-Fermi approximation, where various crystalline configurations are considered in a fully three-dimensional geometry with reflection symmetry. Our calculation confirms the validity of assuming Coulomb crystals for the droplet phase above neutron drip density, which nonetheless does not work at large densities since the elastic constants are found to be decreasing after reaching their peaks. Similarly, the analytic formulae derived in the incompressible liquid-drop model give excellent description for the rod phase at small densities, which overestimates the elastic constants at larger densities. For slabs, due to the negligence on the variations of their thicknesses, the analytic formulae from liquid-drop model agree qualitatively but not quantitatively with our numerical estimations. By fitting to the numerical results, these analytic formulae are improved by introducing dampening factors. The impacts of nuclear symmetry energy are examined adopting two parameter sets, corresponding to the slope of symmetry energy $L = 41.34$ and 89.39 MeV. Even with the uncertainties caused by the anisotropy in polycrystallines, the elastic properties of neutron star matter obtained with $L = 41.34$ and 89.39 MeV are distinctively different, results in detectable differences in various neutron star activities.

## Key claim
The paper provides realistic estimations of elastic constants for nuclear pasta phases (droplets, rods, slabs) in neutron star crusts using a fully three-dimensional relativistic-mean-field model, finding that standard analytic formulae from Coulomb crystal and incompressible liquid-drop models work only at low densities and must be modified with dampening factors at higher densities. The authors show that the slope of symmetry energy L = 41.34 MeV versus 89.39 MeV produces elastic constants differing by almost an order of magnitude, which could cause detectable differences in neutron star oscillations and other activities.

## Method
The authors performed self-consistent Thomas-Fermi calculations within the relativistic-mean-field (RMF) model in a fully three-dimensional geometry with reflection symmetry, solving Klein-Gordon equations via fast cosine transformations with grid spacing less than 0.35 fm to achieve energy per baryon accuracy within approximately eV. Volume-preserving deformations (D1 through D4) were applied to unit cells containing droplet, rod, and slab phases, and elastic constants were extracted from the quadratic dependence of energy density on strain by fitting polynomial expansions to the numerical energy variations. The beta-stability condition was maintained throughout, and two parameter sets for the isovector channel corresponding to symmetry energy slopes L = 89.39 MeV (Set 0) and L = 41.34 MeV (Set 1) were used to examine symmetry energy impacts.

## Key numbers
* For BCC droplet phase: c11 - c12 = 0.04908 mu0 and c44 = 0.1827 mu0 from Monte Carlo simulations, where mu0 = alpha n_d Z^2 / R_W with alpha = 1/137
* For FCC droplet phase: c11 - c12 = 0.04132 mu0 and c44 = 0.1852 mu0 from Monte Carlo simulations
* The Bain barrier between BCC and FCC lattices occurs at epsilon_B approximately 0.1, with FCC achieved at epsilon_FCC = 2 - 2^(5/6) approximately 0.2182
* For droplet phase with L = 41.34 MeV, elastic constants are almost ten times larger than those with L = 89.39 MeV
* The core-crust transition density is n_t = 0.061 fm^-3 for Set 0 (L = 89.39 MeV), while deformed nuclei form at 0.069 less than n_b less than 0.089 fm^-3 for Set 1 (L = 41.34 MeV)
* For rods in honeycomb configuration: c11 - c12 = 2c44 = 2E_C * 10^(2.1(u^2 - 0.3)) and c11 + c12 = 3E_C from liquid-drop model
* For slabs: c11 = 6E_C from liquid-drop model
* Dampening factors introduced: for BCC droplets, c44 = 0.1705 mu0' / 10^(10u^8); for rods, c11 + c12 = 3E_C / 10^(3u^8); for slabs, c11 = 6E_C * 10^(0.55u - 10u^8 - 0.19)

## Context
This work extends previous elastic property calculations for neutron star crusts that used Monte Carlo simulations for Coulomb crystals (Ogata 1990) and incompressible liquid-drop models for rods and slabs (Pethick 1998, Pethick 2020), which neglected free neutrons, strong interactions, charge screening, and finite nuclear sizes. The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star asteroseismology, including quasiperiodic oscillations in magnetars (Sotani 2012) and short gamma ray burst precursors from binary systems (Tsang 2012), by providing density-dependent elastic constants that account for symmetry energy effects and the softening of pasta phases at high densities.
