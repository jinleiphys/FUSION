# Magnetic-field Manipulation of Chemical Bonding in Artificial Molecules

> The effect of orbital magnetism on the chemical bonding of lateral, two-dimensional artificial molecules is studied in the case of a 2e double quantum dot (artificial molecular hydrogen). It is found that a perpendicular magnetic field reduces the coupling (tunneling) between the individual dots and, for sufficiently high values, it leads to complete dissociation of the artificial molecule. The method used is building on Lowdin's work on Projection Operators in Quantum Chemistry; it is a spin-and-space unrestricted Hartree-Fock method in conjunction with the companion step of the restoration of spin and space symmetries via Projection Techniques (when such symmetries are broken). This method is able to describe the full range of couplings in two-dimensional double quantum dots, from the strong-coupling regime exhibiting delocalized molecular orbitals to the weak-coupling and dissociation regimes associated with a Generalized Valence Bond combination of atomic-type orbitals localized on the individual dots.

## Key claim
A perpendicular magnetic field reduces the interdot tunneling coupling in a two-electron double quantum dot (artificial molecular hydrogen), and for sufficiently high field values it induces complete dissociation of the artificial molecule; this dissociation is correctly described only by methods that break spatial symmetry and restore it via projection, while the molecular orbital (RHF) approximation fails to capture the dissociation limit.

## Method
The authors employed a spin-and-space unrestricted Hartree-Fock (sS-UHF) method, which relaxes both double-occupancy and spatial symmetry constraints, using a two-center-oscillator (TCO) confining potential with an interdot-distance-adjustable basis of at least 70 spatial TCO molecular orbitals. They then applied Lowdin's spin-projection technique to restore the broken spin and space symmetries, constructing a Generalized Valence Bond (GVB) wave function that is a linear superposition of two Slater determinants. The singlet-triplet energy difference was computed as a function of magnetic field for three values of the dielectric constant (κ = 45, 25, and 12.9 for GaAs).

## Key numbers
- For κ = 45, Vb = 0, and B = 0 T: RHF total energy = 13.68 meV, sS-UHF total energy = 12.83 meV (energy gain of 0.85 meV), triplet energy = 13.01 meV.
- For κ = 45, Vb = 0, at B = 9 T: RHF Δε = 0.93 meV, while sS-UHF and GVB Δε converge toward zero (dissociation).
- For κ = 45, Vb = 3.71 meV, at B = 9 T: RHF Δε = 1.21 meV.
- For κ = 12.9 (GaAs), g* = -0.44: the Zeeman linear dependence is γ ≈ 0.026 meV/T for Sz = +1.
- All calculations used m* = 0.067 me (GaAs effective mass), ħω0 = 5 meV, interdot distance d = 30 nm, and magnetic fields from B = 0 to B = 9 T.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous zero-field study (Yannouleas and Landman, 2000) of coupling and dissociation in lateral quantum dot molecules, where they showed that the interplay between Molecular Orbital (MO) and Valence Bond (VB) descriptions determines the nature of the chemical bond. The paper contrasts with Hubbard-type methods (Burkard et al., 1999; Husa et al., 2000) that use only separated-dot orbitals and fail for small dielectric constants or at zero field, and it notes that spin-density functional calculations (Nagano et al., 1998; Wensauer et al., 2000) also fail to describe dissociation due to the underlying MO picture. The results enable magnetic-field control of interdot coupling in quantum dot molecules, which has been suggested as a mechanism for two-qubit logic gates in quantum computing.
