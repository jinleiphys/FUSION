# Energetics, forces, and quantized conductance in jellium modeled metallic nanowires

> Energetics and quantized conductance in jellium modeled nanowires are investigated using the local density functional based shell correction method, extending our previous study of uniform in shape wires [C. Yannouleas and U. Landman, J. Phys. Chem. B 101, 5780 (1997)] to wires containing a variable shaped constricted region. The energetics of the wire (sodium) as a function of the length of the volume conserving, adiabatically shaped constriction leads to formation of self selecting magic wire configurations. The variations in the energy result in oscillations in the force required to elongate the wire and are directly correlated with the stepwise variations of the conductance of the nanowire in units of 2e^2/h. The oscillatory patterns in the energetics and forces, and the correlated stepwise variation in the conductance are shown, numerically and through a semiclassical analysis, to be dominated by the quantized spectrum of the transverse states at the narrowmost part of the constriction in the wire.

## Key claim

The oscillatory patterns in energetics, elongation force, and stepwise conductance in jellium-modeled sodium nanowires with variable-shaped constrictions are dominated by the quantized spectrum of transverse electronic states at the narrowmost part of the constriction, with the shell-correction amplitudes being over an order of magnitude smaller than in uniform wires due to the varying subband spectrum along the constriction.

## Method

The authors used a local-density-approximation (LDA) based shell correction method (SCM) to calculate the total energy of jellium-modeled sodium nanowires containing a parabolic, volume-conserving constricted region. The smooth energy contribution was obtained from variational extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) calculations, while the oscillatory shell-correction term was computed from the single-particle eigenvalues of the ETF potential. The elongation force was derived as the derivative of total energy with respect to constriction length, and conductance was evaluated using the Landauer formula in the adiabatic approximation with unit transmission coefficients for modes at the narrowmost cross-section.

## Key numbers

- Initial constriction length \(L_0 = 80\) a.u. and radius \(R_0 = 25\) a.u. for sodium nanowires (Wigner-Seitz radius \(r_s = 4\) a.u.)
- Exchange-correlation contribution to the ETF potential was approximately \(-5.4\) eV, dominant over the electrostatic contribution
- Minimal cross-sectional radius at \(\Delta L/L_0 = 1.125\) was \(a_0 = 12.62\) a.u.; breakage occurs at \(L = 5 L_0\) when \(a_0 = 0\)
- Total elongation force magnitudes were in the nanonewton range, comparable to measured values for gold nanowires
- Shell-correction amplitudes in variable-shaped wires were over an order of magnitude smaller than in uniform wires

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of uniform-in-shape jellium nanowires (Yannouleas and Landman, 1997) to wires with variable-shaped constricted regions, demonstrating that self-selecting magic wire configurations and correlated force oscillations and conductance steps persist. The LDA-based shell correction method, originally developed for metal clusters (Yannouleas and Landman, 1994-1997), provides a framework linking nanowire phenomena to cluster physics. The results enable future studies of how surface energy and material properties affect the number of stable wire configurations, and can be compared to atomistic simulations (Landman et al., 1990-1996) and experimental simultaneous force-conductance measurements (Rubio et al., 1996; Stalder and Dürig, 1996).
