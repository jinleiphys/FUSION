# Empirical Determination of Threshold Partial Wave Amplitudes in $p p \to p p ω$

> Using the model independent irreducible tensor approach to $ω$ production in $pp$ collisions, we show theoretically that, it is advantageous to measure experimentally the polarization of $ω$, in addition to the proposed experimental study employing a polarized beam and a polarized target.

## Key claim

The paper shows theoretically that measuring the polarization of the ω meson in addition to the proposed polarized-beam and polarized-target experiments on pp → ppω is sufficient to determine the leading threshold partial wave amplitudes f₁, f₂, and f₃ empirically without any discrete ambiguities, except for an overall phase.

## Method

The authors apply a model-independent irreducible tensor formalism to the reaction pp → ppω, expressing the transition matrix in terms of irreducible tensor amplitudes. At threshold, they restrict the final-state orbital angular momentum of the two-proton system to l_f = 0 and consider ω orbital angular momenta l = 0 and 1, reducing the problem to three threshold partial wave amplitudes (f₁, f₂, f₃) corresponding to specific initial and final partial wave states. They derive expressions for the unpolarized differential cross-section and the Fano statistical tensors (vector and tensor polarizations of the ω) in terms of these amplitudes, and show how measurements of these observables allow extraction of the magnitudes and relative phases.

## Key numbers

- The total cross-sections for pp → ppω have been measured at five c.m. energies in the range 3.8 MeV to 30 MeV above threshold (Hebbeker, 2000).
- Total and differential cross-sections were measured at 92 and 173 MeV above threshold (Abdul, 2003).
- The unpolarized differential cross-section at threshold is given by d³σ₀/(dW dΩ) = (1/192π²)[a₀ + (9/10)a₂ cos²θ], where a₀ = [|f₁|² + 3|f₂ + (1/√10)f₃|²] and a₂ = [|f₃|² - 2√10 Re(f₂f₃*)].
- The vector analyzing power difference A^B_Z - A^T_Z = (1/16π²)√(1/6) Im[f₁(f₂ + (1/√10)f₃)*] sinθ.
- The spin correlation A_Z = (i/32π²)√(1/3) Re[f₁(f₂ + (1/√10)f₃)*] sinθ.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent irreducible tensor approach previously applied to pp → ppπ⁰ (Ramachandran, 2001) and to the analysis of NN → NΔ transitions (Ray, 2003). It addresses the difficulty noted by Moskal (2004) and Hanhart (2004) that extracting specific information from data is often challenging. The paper enables complete empirical determination of the three leading threshold amplitudes for pp → ppω, which is relevant to the "missing resonance problem" (Bars, 2003) and to studies of OZI rule violations in heavy meson production (Amsler, 1998).
