# Quantal Nucleon Diffusion I: Central Collisions of Symmetric Nuclei

> Quantal diffusion mechanism of nucleon exchange is studied in the central collisions of several symmetric heavy-ion collisions in the framework of the Stochastic Mean-Field (SMF) approach. Since at bombarding energies below the fusion barrier, di-nuclear structure is maintained, it is possible to describe nucleon exchange as a diffusion process familiar from deep-inelastic collisions. Quantal diffusion coefficients, including memory effects, for proton and neutron exchanges are extracted microscopically employing the SMF approach. The quantal calculations of neutron and proton variances are compared with the semi-classical results.

## Key claim

The paper derives a fully quantal expression for nucleon diffusion coefficients in heavy-ion collisions from the Stochastic Mean-Field (SMF) approach, including memory effects and Pauli blocking, and shows that these quantal calculations require less numerical effort than their semi-classical counterparts despite providing more accurate results.

## Method

The authors employed the Stochastic Mean-Field (SMF) approach to study nucleon exchange in central collisions of symmetric nuclei at bombarding energies slightly below the fusion barrier, where a di-nuclear structure is maintained. They extracted quantal diffusion coefficients for proton and neutron transfers by evaluating the correlation function of stochastic drift coefficients using a diabatic approximation and closure relation, which eliminated the need to evolve a complete set of particle states. The calculations were performed in a three-dimensional Cartesian geometry using the Skyrme SLy4 interaction with no symmetry assumptions, and the resulting variances were compared with semi-classical calculations obtained via Wigner transformation of the quantal expression.

## Context

This work extends the Stochastic Mean-Field (SMF) approach developed by Ayik (2008) and collaborators to provide a fully quantal description of nucleon diffusion, improving upon earlier semi-classical treatments such as the nucleon exchange transport model of Randrup (1979). The quantal diffusion coefficients include memory effects and exact Pauli blocking, which were treated approximately in prior semi-classical calculations by Yilmaz (2011, 2014) and Ayik (2015a). The results enable more accurate predictions of charge and mass distributions in quasi-fission reactions and the production of neutron-rich nuclei, building on TDHF studies of quasifission by Umar (2015a, 2015c) and Oberacker (2014).
