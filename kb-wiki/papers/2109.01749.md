# Vector interactions inhibit quark-hadron mixed phases in neutron stars

> We investigate the surface tension $σ$ and the curvature energy $γ$ of quark matter drops in the MIT bag model with vector interactions. Finite size corrections to the density of states are implemented by using the multiple reflection expansion (MRE) formalism. We find that $σ$ and $γ$ are strongly enhanced by new terms arising from vector interactions. With respect to the noninteracting case they are increased by a large factor, which can be as high as $\sim 10$ when the vector coupling constant $g$ varies within the range used in the literature. This behavior may have major consequences for the hadron-quark mixed phase speculated to exist at neutron star (NS) interiors, which may be totally suppressed or have its extension substantially reduced.

# Vector interactions inhibit quark-hadron mixed phases in neutron stars

## Key claim

The surface tension and curvature energy of quark matter drops in the MIT bag model are strongly enhanced by repulsive vector interactions, with enhancement factors up to ~10 relative to the noninteracting case; this large increase may totally suppress or substantially reduce the density range over which a hadron-quark mixed phase can exist in neutron star interiors.

## Method

The authors employ the MIT bag model with vector interactions introduced via a vector-isoscalar meson field in the mean field approximation, using a universal coupling constant for all three quark flavors. Finite size effects for spherical quark droplets are incorporated through the multiple reflection expansion (MRE) formalism, which yields density of states corrections proportional to surface area and curvature. The surface tension and curvature energy are computed by separating the grand thermodynamic potential into volume, surface, and curvature contributions, with new vector-interaction terms arising from the coupling between volume, surface, and curvature number densities.

## Key numbers

- Surface tension in the noninteracting MIT bag model: σ ≈ 2–25 MeV fm⁻²; with vector interactions (G_V up to 1 fm²) these values increase by a factor up to ~10 for n_B = 10 n₀
- Curvature energy in the noninteracting MIT bag model: γ ≈ 2–12 MeV fm⁻¹; similarly enhanced by vector interactions
- Baryon densities considered: 2 n₀, 4 n₀, 6 n₀, 8 n₀, and 10 n₀, where n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ (nuclear saturation density)
- Vector coupling constant range: G_V from 0 to 1 fm², corresponding to the range used in the literature
- The ratio γC/(σS) = 2γ/(Rσ) is approximately 10% for droplet radius 10 fm and ~50% for radius 3 fm
- Coulomb potential φ at the droplet boundary is negative, taking values between 0 and −50 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of surface tension and curvature energy in the MIT bag model (Lugones 2016, 2018, 2020) and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Lugones 2013) by incorporating repulsive vector interactions, which have become important for explaining large observed neutron star masses (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013, Cromartie 2019, Riley 2021, Miller 2021). The results contradict the assumption that surface tension values below ~30 MeV/fm² (typical of most effective model calculations) are sufficient for mixed phase formation, and instead suggest that vector interactions may force a sharp hadron-quark interface in hybrid stars. The findings enable more realistic assessments of mixed phase stability in neutron star interiors, particularly for cold deleptonized neutron stars, hot lepton-rich proto-neutron stars, and postmerger neutron star remnants.
