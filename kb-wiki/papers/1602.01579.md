# Isospin properties of quark matter from a 3-flavor NJL model

> We have studied the properties of hot and dense quark matter based on the 3-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model as well as its Polyakov-loop extension (pNJL) with scalar-isovector and vector-isovector couplings. Provided a considerable large isospin asymmetry or isospin chemical potential, isospin splittings of constituent mass, chiral phase transition boundary, and critical point for $u$ and $d$ quarks can be observed for positive isovector coupling constants but are suppressed for negative ones. The quark matter symmetry energy decreases with the increasing isovector coupling constant, and is mostly enhanced in the pNJL model than in the NJL model. A positive scalar-isovector coupling constant is more likely to lead to an unstable isospin asymmetric quark matter. The isovector coupling has been further found to affect particle fractions as well as the equation of state in hybrid stars. Possible effects on the isospin properties of quark matter have also been discussed if the strangeness sector is further broken among the flavor symmetry.

## Key claim

The isospin splitting of chiral phase transition boundaries and critical points for u and d quarks becomes considerable only at a fixed large isospin chemical potential of μI = −30 MeV, while at the small isospin asymmetry δ = 0.198 typical of Au+Au collisions the QCD phase diagram is almost insensitive to isovector couplings.

## Method

The authors employed a 3-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model and its Polyakov-loop extension (pNJL) with scalar-isovector (GIS) and vector-isovector (GIV) coupling terms added to the Lagrangian. They solved the mean-field gap equations for quark condensates and constituent masses, and the thermodynamic potential, self-consistently at finite temperature and chemical potential to map the chiral phase diagram and compute the quark matter symmetry energy. The model parameters included current quark masses mu = md = 3.6 MeV, ms = 87 MeV, GSΛ² = 3.6, KΛ⁵ = 8.9, and a cutoff Λ = 750 MeV, with GV set to zero.

## Key numbers

- The critical point for chiral phase transition at isospin asymmetry δ = 0.198 in the NJL model without isovector couplings is at μ = 354 MeV, T = 45 MeV.
- The critical point in the pNJL model at δ = 0.198 without isovector couplings is at μ = 347 MeV, T = 93 MeV.
- The isospin asymmetry in central Au+Au collisions is δ = 0.198, derived from N = 118 and Z = 79 for gold.
- The scalar-isovector coupling strength that produces maximum separation of u and d quark phase boundaries is GIS = 0.14 GS.
- The quark matter symmetry energy decreases with increasing isovector coupling constant; for RIV = 0.5 the decrease is linear, and for RIS = 0.14 the decrease is strongest at intermediate densities.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the QCD phase diagram using NJL and pNJL models (Fukushima 2008, Carignano 2010, Bratovic 2013) by introducing scalar-isovector and vector-isovector couplings to explore isospin asymmetry effects. It builds on earlier observations of isospin splitting of chiral transitions (Toublan 2003, Frank 2003, Zhang 2014) and connects to the nuclear symmetry energy literature (Baran 2005, Steiner 2005, Lattimer 2007, Tsang 2011, Horowitz 2014). The results enable future transport model simulations of neutron-rich heavy-ion collisions at RHIC Beam-Energy Scan and FAIR-CBM energies, and inform models of hybrid stars with quark cores.
