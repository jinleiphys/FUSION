# Neutrino nucleus reactions at high energies within the GiBUU model

> The GiBUU model, which implements all reaction channels relevant at medium neutrino energy, is used to investigate the neutrino and antineutrino scattering on iron. Results for integrated cross sections are compared with NOMAD and MINOS data. It is shown, that final state interaction can noticeably change the spectra of the outgoing hadrons. Predictions for the Miner$ν$a experiment are made for pion spectra, averaged over NuMI neutrino and antineutrino fluxes.

# Neutrino nucleus reactions at high energies within the GiBUU model

## Key claim

The GiBUU transport model, using default parameters without fine-tuning, reproduces the energy dependence of inclusive neutrino-iron cross sections measured by NOMAD and MINOS, but the agreement for antineutrino data is good only above 25 GeV; below that energy the calculation lies above MINOS data and below IHEP-JINR data, with the overall agreement between calculation and data being better than the agreement of the data with each other.

## Method

The GiBUU transport model implements quasielastic scattering, resonance production, background processes, and deep inelastic scattering (via PYTHIA) for neutrino and antineutrino interactions on iron. Final state interactions are modeled by solving the semi-classical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation, which describes the dynamical evolution of phase space densities under mean-field potentials and collision terms. The transition between resonance and DIS regimes is handled by smoothly switching off resonance contributions above W = 2.05 GeV and switching on DIS contributions above W = 1.6 GeV, with default parameters W_RES-1 = 2.0 GeV, W_RES-2 = 2.05 GeV, W_DIS-1 = 1.6 GeV, and W_DIS-2 = 1.65 GeV.

## Key numbers

- World average total cross section per energy for an isoscalar target: sigma_tot/E_nu (neutrino) = 0.667 +/- 0.014 x 10^-38 cm^2/GeV; sigma_tot/E_nu (antineutrino) = 0.334 +/- 0.008 x 10^-38 cm^2/GeV
- NOMAD inclusive neutrino cross section measured for E_nu > 4.6 GeV with accuracy at least 4%
- MINOS neutrino cross section measured for E_nu > 3.48 GeV and antineutrino cross section for E_antinu > 6.07 GeV
- Different prescriptions for treating nuclear effects in DIS within GiBUU produce a 5-7% difference in results
- For dominant pion channels, final state interactions decrease the cross section at pion kinetic energies T_pi > 0.2 GeV and increase it at T_pi < 0.15 GeV

## Context

This work extends the GiBUU transport model, previously applied to nucleon-, nucleus-, pion-, and electron-induced reactions, to neutrino-nucleus scattering at medium and high energies. It compares with integrated cross section data from NOMAD and MINOS, and provides predictions for the upcoming Miner_nu_a experiment on iron targets using NuMI neutrino and antineutrino fluxes. The treatment of nuclear effects in high-energy neutrino reactions remains controversial, with different approaches (nuclear parton distributions from Eskola (1998), Fermi motion prescriptions) giving variations of 5-7%; the paper notes that no other event generator accounts for nuclear corrections in high-energy neutrino reactions. The results enable predictions for pion spectra and other final-state hadrons that will be testable by Miner_nu_a, which will measure on multiple nuclear targets (Plastic, Iron, Lead, Carbon, Water, liquid Helium).
