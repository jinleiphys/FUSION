# Narrow resonances in the continuum of the unbound nucleus $^{15}$F

> The structure of the unbound $^{15}$F nucleus is investigated using the inverse kinematics resonant scattering of a radioactive $^{14}$O beam impinging on a CH$_2$ target. The analysis of $^{1}$H($^{14}$O,p)$^{14}$O and $^{1}$H($^{14}$O,2p)$^{13}$N reactions allowed the confirmation of the previously observed narrow $1/2^{-}$ resonance, near the two-proton decay threshold, and the identification of two new narrow 5/2$^{-}$ and 3/2$^{-}$ resonances. The newly observed levels decay by 1p emission to the ground of $^{14}$O, and by sequential 2p emission to the ground state (g.s.) of $^{13}$N via the $1^-$ resonance of $^{14}$O. Gamow shell model (GSM) analysis of the experimental data suggests that the wave functions of the 5/2$^{-}$ and 3/2$^{-}$ resonances may be collectivized by the continuum coupling to nearby 2p- and 1p- decay channels. The observed excitation function $^{1}$H($^{14}$O,p)$^{14}$O and resonance spectrum in $^{15}$F are well reproduced in the unified framework of the GSM.

## Key claim
The paper reports the first clear observation of two new narrow resonances (5/2- and 3/2-) in the unbound nucleus 15F, more than 3 MeV above the Coulomb barrier; however, the 3/2- resonance is surprisingly 12.5 times narrower than predicted by mirror-nucleus arguments from Fortune and Sherr (2007, 2011) that do not consider continuum coupling.

## Method
The experiment used inverse kinematics resonant scattering of a radioactive 14O beam (7.42-7.64 MeV/u) from the SPIRAL1 facility at GANIL impinging on CH2 targets, employing the thick-target technique. Excitation functions for the 1H(14O,p)14O and 1H(14O,2p)13N reactions were measured using the MUST2 detector array, the VAMOS magnetic spectrometer, and the AGATA gamma-ray spectrometer, with R-matrix analysis performed using the AZURE2 code. Theoretical interpretation was carried out using the Gamow Shell Model in the coupled-channel representation (GSMCC) with a model space consisting of a 12C core and valence protons in the p and sd shells.

## Key numbers
- 5/2- resonance: Er = 5.93(10) MeV, Gamma(0_1+) = 3(2) keV, Gamma(1_-1) = 0.3(1) keV
- 3/2- resonance: Er = 6.33(13) MeV, Gamma(0_1+) = 28(13) keV, Gamma(1_-1) = 2.2(6) keV
- 1/2- resonance (confirmed): Er = 4.88(21) MeV, Gamma = 30(15) keV
- 5/2+ resonance: Er = 2.81(12) MeV, Gamma = 251(26) keV
- 3/2- resonance width is 12.5 times narrower than the prediction from Fortune and Sherr (2011)
- For the 3/2- resonance, the l=0 partial width is less than 1x10^-3 keV (less than 1 eV)
- Center-of-mass energy resolution: sigma(Er) = 50(5) keV for elastic channel, sigma(Er) = 300(20) keV for inelastic channel

## Context
This work extends the previous observation of the narrow 1/2- resonance in 15F by de Oliveira Santos et al. (2016) and confirms predictions by Canton et al. (2006) and Fortune and Sherr (2007, 2011) of three unusually narrow resonances in this unbound nucleus. The new resonances provide experimental evidence for the collectivization of wave functions by continuum coupling to nearby 2p- and 1p-decay channels, a mechanism previously discussed by Okolowicz et al. (2012, 2013). The results enable further tests of the Gamow Shell Model as a unified framework for describing both structure and reactions in open quantum systems near the dripline.
