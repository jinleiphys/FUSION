# Ground state properties of exotic nuclei near Z=40 in the relativistic mean-field theory,

> Study of the ground-state properties of Kr, Sr and Zr isotopes has been performed in the framework of the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory using the recently proposed relativistic parameter set NL-SH. It is shown that the RMF theory provides an unified and excellent description of the binding energies, isotope shifts and deformation properties of nuclei over a large range of isospin in the Z=40 region. It is observed that the RMF theory with the force NL-SH is able to describe the anomalous kinks in isotope shifts in Kr and Sr nuclei, the problem which has hitherto remained unresolved. This is in contrast with the density-dependent Skyrme Hartree-Fock approach which does not reproduce the behaviour of the isotope shifts about shell closure. On the Zr chain we predict that the isotope shifts exhibit a trend similar to that of the Kr and Sr nuclei. The RMF theory also predicts shape coexistence in heavy Sr isotopes. Several dramatic shape transitions in the isotopic chains are shown to be a general feature of nuclei in this region. A comparison of the properties with the available mass models shows that the results of the RMF theory are generally in accord with the predictions of the finite-range droplet model.

# Ground state properties of exotic nuclei near Z=40 in the relativistic mean-field theory

## Key claim
The relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with the NL-SH force reproduces the anomalous kinks in isotope shifts of Kr and Sr nuclei across the N=50 shell closure, a problem that had remained unresolved in the density-dependent Skyrme Hartree-Fock approach. The authors claim this is the first time a microscopic theory has been able to describe these empirical isotope shift data, but note that the RMF theory also predicts a similar trend for Zr isotopes where no empirical data yet exist.

## Method
The authors performed relativistic Hartree calculations for even-even isotopes of Kr (Z=36, A=72-100), Sr (Z=38, A=70-110), and Zr (Z=40, A=80-110) using the NL-SH parameter set in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis with 12 oscillator shells. Pairing was included via the BCS formalism with constant pairing gaps taken from empirical particle separation energies, and the center-of-mass energy correction was applied using a harmonic oscillator zero-point energy.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy deviations from empirical values are at most 3-4 MeV for light Kr isotopes, corresponding to a maximum discrepancy of 0.5%
- The asymmetry energy of NL-SH is 36 MeV, compared to 44 MeV for NL1 and the empirical value of about 33 MeV
- For Sr isotopes near A=98, the prolate shape is lower than the oblate shape by only about 300 keV; for heavier Sr isotopes the prolate minimum is 600-800 keV below the oblate minimum
- The magic nuclei $^{86}$Kr and $^{88}$Sr have the smallest rms charge radii in their respective chains, even when compared to nuclei ten mass numbers lower
- The RMF theory predicts binding energies within 1-2 MeV of the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) and extended Thomas-Fermi with Strutinsky Integral (ETF-SI) predictions

## Context
This work extends the RMF theory with the NL-SH force, previously successful for Pb isotope shifts (Sharma 1993), to the Z=40 region where earlier RMF calculations using NL1 (Maharana 1992, Shei 1993) had predicted spherical shapes for many Sr and Zr isotopes in contradiction with empirical deformed shapes. The paper contrasts with the density-dependent Skyrme Hartree-Fock approach (Bonche 1985, Tajima 1993), which gave only a nearly linear response for isotope shifts and could not reproduce the kink about shell closure. The results enable predictions for Zr isotope shifts where measurements do not yet exist, and demonstrate that the isospin dependence of the spin-orbit interaction arising naturally in RMF theory is not contained in the Skyrme approach.
