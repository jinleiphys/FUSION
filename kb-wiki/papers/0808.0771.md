# Electromagnetic Productions of KLambda and KSigma on the Nucleons

> We briefly review the progress and problems in the electromagnetic production of KLambda on the nucleon. The problem of the data discrepancy in this channel as well as the corresponding physics consequence are highlighted. We also discuss the effect of the new beam-recoil polarization data Cx and Cz on our analysis. For this purpose we use the isobar model Kaon-Maid and a recent multipoles model that can describe recent experimental data. We also present a new multipoles model for the KSigma channels to complete our analysis.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the lack of mutual consistency between the SAPHIR and CLAS datasets for $K\Lambda$ photoproduction leads to significantly different extracted resonance parameters and conclusions about "missing resonances," and that the new CLAS beam-recoil polarization data $C_x$ and $C_z$ select specific resonances as important, but the paper does not claim to have fully resolved these discrepancies.

## Method

The author uses two phenomenological models: the isobar model Kaon-Maid (based on tree-level Feynman diagrams with background terms and four nucleon resonances) and a multipole model (using Breit-Wigner resonance forms). Both models are fitted to combinations of SAPHIR, CLAS, and LEPS data for $K\Lambda$ and $K\Sigma$ channels. For $K\Sigma$ channels, the resonance properties are constrained using PDG values, and a new multipole model is presented.

## Key numbers

- The statistical parameter $|z_1|$ for Kaon-Maid fitted to SAPHIR data is 11.7, while for CLAS data it is 1.41, indicating Kaon-Maid is more consistent with CLAS data.
- A global scaling factor $s = 1.13$ for the full SAPHIR dataset and $s = 1.15$ for forward-angle SAPHIR data would improve agreement between the two datasets, smaller than the previously suggested factor of $\sim 4/3$.
- The extracted mass of the missing $D_{13}$ resonance from Kaon-Maid using SAPHIR data is $1.938 \pm 0.004$ GeV and using CLAS data is $1.927 \pm 0.003$ GeV; the extracted widths are $0.233 \pm 0.008$ GeV and $0.570 \pm 0.019$ GeV, respectively.
- The extracted mass of the missing $D_{13}$ resonance from the multipole model using SAPHIR data is $1.936 \pm 0.010$ GeV and using CLAS data is $1.915 \pm 0.004$ GeV; the extracted widths are $0.301 \pm 0.022$ GeV and $0.165 \pm 0.008$ GeV, respectively.
- The total number of data points used in the $K\Sigma$ fitting database is 2816.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of kaon photoproduction by Mart (1999) and Mart (2006) using the Kaon-Maid and multipole models, and directly addresses the data discrepancy problem between the SAPHIR and CLAS collaborations highlighted by Bydzovsky (2006). The inclusion of the new CLAS beam-recoil polarization data $C_x$ and $C_z$ from Bradford (2006) allows the author to identify which resonances are selected by these data, corroborating findings by Anisovich (2007). The new multipole model for $K\Sigma$ channels aims to resolve the long-standing problem noted by Mart (1995) that models reproducing $K^+\Sigma^0$ data overestimate charged $\Sigma$ data by almost two orders of magnitude, and enables future predictions for the unmeasured $K^0\Sigma^0$ channel.
