# Magnetic dipole operator from chiral effective field theory for many-body expansion methods

> Many-body approaches for atomic nuclei generally rely on a basis expansion of the nuclear states, interactions, and current operators. In this work, we derive the representation of the magnetic dipole operator in plane-wave and harmonic-oscillator basis states, as needed for Faddeev calculations of few-body systems or many-body calculations within, e.g., the no-core shell model, the in-medium renormalization group, coupled-cluster theory, or the nuclear shell model. We focus in particular on the next-to-leading-order two-body contributions derived from chiral effective field theory. We provide detailed benchmarks and also comparisons with quantum Monte Carlo results for three-body systems. The derived operator matrix elements represent the basic input for studying magnetic properties of atomic nuclei based on chiral effective field theory.

## Key claim

The paper derives the representation of the magnetic dipole operator in plane-wave and harmonic-oscillator basis states for use in many-body expansion methods, focusing on next-to-leading-order two-body contributions from chiral effective field theory. The authors validate their expressions by comparing trinucleon magnetic moments obtained from magnetic form factors in Faddeev calculations against those from the magnetic dipole operator used in Jacobi no-core shell-model calculations, finding agreement within the uncertainties of the chiral expansion.

## Method

The authors derived the one-body (leading-order) and two-body (next-to-leading-order) magnetic dipole operator matrix elements in both momentum-space partial-wave bases and harmonic-oscillator bases, using chiral effective field theory currents. They computed trinucleon magnetic moments via two independent routes: (1) evaluating the magnetic form factor at zero momentum transfer from the current operator in a momentum-space basis using Faddeev calculations, and (2) directly evaluating the magnetic dipole operator in a harmonic-oscillator basis within the Jacobi no-core shell model. The two-body operator contributions include both the intrinsic term and the Sachs term, the latter arising from the center-of-mass dependence of the two-body current.

## Key numbers

- The isoscalar and isovector nucleon magnetic form factors at zero momentum transfer are \(G_M^S(0) = 0.880\ \mu_N\) and \(G_M^V(0) = 4.706\ \mu_N\), where \(\mu_N = e\hbar / 2m_{\rm proton}\) is the nuclear magneton.
- The axial coupling used is \(g_A = 1.27\), the pion decay constant is \(F_\pi = 92.3\ \text{MeV}\), and the averaged pion mass is \(m_\pi = 138.039\ \text{MeV}\).
- The breakdown scale of the chiral expansion is \(\Lambda_{\rm b} = 500-600\ \text{MeV}\), with the typical momentum scale \(Q\) of order the pion mass \(m_\pi\).

## Context

This work extends previous chiral EFT studies of electromagnetic currents in few-body systems (Pastore et al., 2008-2013; Piarulli et al., 2013) by providing operator matrix elements suitable for many-body expansion methods applied to medium-mass nuclei. It enables calculations of magnetic properties of nuclei using methods such as the no-core shell model, in-medium similarity renormalization group, coupled-cluster theory, and the nuclear shell model, where previous studies of heavier nuclei had been limited to one-body currents only. The paper builds on the observation from studies of light nuclei (Pastore et al., 2013; Gayer et al., 2020) that two-body current contributions beyond leading order are crucial for improving agreement with experimental magnetic properties.
