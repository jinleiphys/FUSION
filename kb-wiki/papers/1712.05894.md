# The moment of inertia of the neutron star PSR J0348+0432 and its proto neutron star

> The difference of the moment of inertia of the neutron star PSR J0348 + 0432 and that of its proto neutron star is studied in the framework of the relativistic mean field theory considering baryon octet. The temperature of the proto neutron star PSR J0348+0432 is chosen as T=5 MeV. The calculations show that the central baryon number density of the proto neutron star PSR J0348+0432 is in the range 0.623$\sim$0.813 fm$^{-3}$, decreased by 2$\sim$7\% compared to that of the neutron star PSR J0348+0432. The radius of the proto neutron star PSR J0348+0432 is in the range 13.101$\sim$12.419 km, increased by 1$\sim$2\% compared to that of the neutron star PSR J0348+0432. The moment of inertia of the proto neutron star PSR J0348+0432 is in the range 1.939$\times$10$^{45}$$\sim$1.638$\times$10$^{45}$ g.cm$^{2}$, increased by about 2$\sim$7\% compared to that of the neutron star PSR J0348+0432.

## Key claim
For the massive neutron star PSR J0348+0432, the moment of inertia of its proto neutron star at a temperature of T=5 MeV is increased by about 2 to 7 percent compared to that of the cold neutron star, based on calculations using the relativistic mean field theory with baryon octet and a specific set of hyperon coupling parameters (No.40).

## Method
The relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with baryon octet was used to compute the equation of state for both the cold neutron star and the proto neutron star at T=5 MeV. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation and the moment-of-inertia equation were solved to obtain the mass, radius, and moment of inertia for the star PSR J0348+0432. The nucleon coupling constants were taken from the GL85 set, and hyperon coupling constants were selected from 40 parameter sets based on hyperon well depths and SU(6) symmetry, with parameter set No.40 chosen as it reproduces the observed mass of the star.

## Key numbers
- Central baryon number density of the proto neutron star PSR J0348+0432: 0.623 to 0.813 fm^-3, decreased by 2 to 7 percent compared to the cold neutron star (0.633 to 0.870 fm^-3).
- Radius of the proto neutron star PSR J0348+0432: 13.101 to 12.419 km, increased by 1 to 2 percent compared to the cold neutron star (12.957 to 12.143 km).
- Moment of inertia of the proto neutron star PSR J0348+0432: 1.939 x 10^45 to 1.638 x 10^45 g.cm^2, increased by about 2 to 7 percent compared to the cold neutron star (1.906 x 10^45 to 1.537 x 10^45 g.cm^2).
- The maximum mass of the cold neutron star PSR J0348+0432 for parameter No.40 is M=2.052 M_sun.

## Context
This work extends the study of massive neutron stars like PSR J0348+0432, previously observed by Antoniadis (2013), and PSR J1614-2230, observed by Demorest (2010), into the proto neutron star phase, which is important for understanding stellar evolution. The results for the cold neutron star radius are consistent with the range reported by Steiner (2015). The calculations enable future comparisons with observations of proto neutron star cooling and rotation, though the short lifetime of the proto neutron star (temperature dropping from 30 MeV to 5 MeV within about 20 seconds for a 1.4 M_sun star, as noted by Burrows (1986)) makes direct observation difficult.
