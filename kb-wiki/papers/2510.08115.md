# Probing Strange Dark Matter through $f$-mode Oscillations of Neutron Stars with Hyperons and Quark Matter

> We investigate the impact of a hypothetical bosonic dark matter (DM) candidate, the sexaquark, on the fundamental ($f$-mode) oscillations of neutron stars (NSs). By varying the DM particle mass and considering different core compositions including hypernuclear matter, sexaquark DM, and deconfined quark matter (QM), we construct hybrid equations of state (EOS) with a smooth hadron--quark crossover that remain consistent with current astrophysical constraints on mass, radius, and tidal deformability. Our analysis shows that the presence of these exotic components systematically alters quasi-universal $f$-mode relations. In particular, relations involving $f$--$\sqrt{M/R^{3}}$, $(R^{4}/M^{3}τ)(C)$, $ωM(C)$, require higher-order polynomial fits compared to standard studies. Quadratic forms remain sufficient for $f$--$\sqrt{M/R^{3}}$ and $ωM(C)$, while damping-time relations such as $(R^{4}/M^{3}τ)(C)$ demand higher-order corrections to capture their curvature. For $f(Λ)$, a cubic fit provides a satisfactory description. Within this extended framework the relations remain tight and effectively composition independent. These results suggest that precise $f$-mode measurements with future gravitational-wave detectors could provide clear signatures of DM and other exotic matter in NS interiors.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the presence of sexaquark dark matter, hyperons, and deconfined quark matter in neutron star cores systematically alters quasi-universal relations for f-mode oscillations, but these relations remain tight and effectively composition-independent, meaning that precise f-mode measurements with future gravitational-wave detectors could provide signatures of dark matter and exotic matter in neutron star interiors, provided that higher-order polynomial fits are used for some relations.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid equations of state using a smooth hadron-quark crossover (Replacement Interpolation Construction method) between a hadronic phase described by the DD2Y-T relativistic mean-field model (including nucleons, hyperons, and bosonic sexaquark dark matter) and a quark phase described by the nonlocal Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. They perform fully general-relativistic calculations of f-mode oscillation frequencies and damping times for these dark-matter-admixed hybrid stars, varying the sexaquark mass and coupling parameter while ensuring consistency with astrophysical constraints on mass, radius, and tidal deformability.

## Key numbers

- Sexaquark dark matter vacuum mass range considered: m_S = 1890-1950 MeV, with the favored range from Bayesian analysis being m_S = 1890-1900 MeV (fully consistent with all current observational constraints)
- Coupling parameter x (controlling density dependence of sexaquark mass) varied in the range 0.03 to 0.10
- f-mode frequencies for neutron stars typically span between 1.3 and 2.8 kHz
- Tidal deformability constraint from GW170817 for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: 70 <= Lambda_1.4 <= 580
- Nuclear saturation density n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dark matter in neutron stars by Shahrbaf (2022, 2024) and Thakur (2023, 2024, 2025) by incorporating full general-relativistic f-mode calculations (rather than the Cowling approximation used in some prior work) and by considering a hybrid star scenario with a smooth hadron-quark crossover. The study builds on the sexaquark dark matter candidate proposed by Farrar (2017, 2022) and uses the DD2Y-T relativistic mean-field model from Typel (2009). The results enable future gravitational-wave detectors such as the Einstein Telescope and Cosmic Explorer to potentially distinguish between standard neutron star compositions and those containing dark matter or deconfined quark matter through f-mode asteroseismology.
