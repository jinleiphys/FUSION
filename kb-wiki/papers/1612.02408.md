# Study of chiral vortical and magnetic effects in the anomalous transport model

> We extend our recent study of chiral magnetic effect in relativistic heavy ion collisions based on an anomalous transport model by including also the chiral vortical effect. We find that although vorticities in the chirally restored quark matter, which result from the large angular momentum in non-central collisions, can generate an axial charge dipole moment in the transverse plane of a heavy ion collision, it does not produce a difference in the eccentricities of negatively and positively charged particles. As a result, including the chiral vortical effect alone cannot lead to a splitting between the elliptic flows of negatively and positively charged particles. On the other hand, negatively and positively charged particles do develop a splitting in their elliptic flows if the effect due to a strong and long-lived magnetic field is also included. However, to have a positive slope in the dependence of the elliptic flow splitting on the charge asymmetry of the quark matter, as seen in experiments, requires the neglect of the effect of the Lorentz force. In this case, an elliptic flow splitting appears even at vanishing charge asymmetry.

# Study of chiral vortical and magnetic effects in the anomalous transport model

## Key claim

The chiral vortical effect (CVE) alone cannot produce a splitting between the elliptic flows of negatively and positively charged particles in relativistic heavy ion collisions, even though it generates an axial charge dipole moment; a positive slope in the charge-asymmetry dependence of elliptic flow splitting, as seen in experiments, requires including both magnetic and vorticity fields while neglecting the Lorentz force, but this also produces a nonzero splitting at zero charge asymmetry that is larger than experimental values.

## Method

The authors extended their previous anomalous transport model for massless quarks and antiquarks by adding chiral equations of motion that include both magnetic field and vorticity terms, with the vorticity modeled as a spatially uniform external field with a time dependence based on the AMPT model for Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV and 30-40% centrality. They simulated the time evolution of partonic matter using the chiral equations of motion with chirality-changing scattering between quarks and antiquarks of the same chirality, then used a duality ansatz to relabel partons as pions below the phase transition temperature of 150 MeV and evolved the pionic matter until kinetic freeze-out at 120 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Initial magnetic field strength: eB₀ = 7 mπ², with lifetime τ_B = 6 fm/c
- Initial vorticity: ω₀ = 0.1 fm⁻¹, with lifetime τ_ω = 2.7 fm/c
- Central density of quarks and antiquarks: ρ₀ = 13 fm⁻³
- Fireball radius: R = 3.5 fm, surface thickness a = 0.5 fm, spatial anisotropy parameter c = 1.5
- Phase transition temperature: T_C = 150 MeV; kinetic freeze-out temperature: T_f = 120 MeV
- Integrated v₂ of pions with 0.15 ≤ p_T ≤ 0.5 GeV/c: 0.036 in all three cases (CVE, CVE+CME, CVE+CME+LF)
- Slope parameter for elliptic flow difference vs. charge asymmetry without Lorentz force: 0.0108; with Lorentz force: -0.0139
- Intercept at zero charge asymmetry without Lorentz force: 4.9 × 10⁻³
- Axial charge current from vorticity at T = 0.3 GeV, μ = μ₅ = 0, ω = 0.02 GeV: 0.0375 fm⁻³ (compared to 0.02 fm⁻³ from magnetic field alone in prior work)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) in relativistic heavy ion collisions (Sun and Ko, Phys. Rev. C 94, 045204, 2016) by adding the chiral vortical effect (CVE). It contradicts the schematic expectation from the chiral vortical wave (CVW) argument of Jiang et al. (Phys. Rev. D 92, 071501, 2015) that CVE alone would produce elliptic flow splitting. The results show that reproducing the experimental elliptic flow splitting observed at RHIC requires both magnetic and vorticity fields while neglecting the Lorentz force, but this remains inconsistent with full dynamics. The large axial charge dipole moment generated by CVE may be relevant to the observed polarization of lambda hyperons at RHIC, which the authors plan to study quantitatively in future work.
