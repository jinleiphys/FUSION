# Nonequilibrium Quantum-Field Dynamics and Off-Shell Transport for $ϕ^4$-theory in 2+1 dimensions

> We solve the Kadanoff-Baym equations for nonequilibrium initial configurations of the $ϕ^4$-theory in 2+1 dimensions and compare to explicit solutions of generalized transport equations for the same theory. The latter transport equations are derived from the Kadanoff-Baym equation in a first order gradient expansion in phase space and explicitly retain the off-shell dynamics as inherent in the time-dependent spectral functions. The solutions of these equations compare very well with the exact solutions of the full Kadanoff-Baym equations with respect to the occupation numbers of the individual modes, the spectral evolution as well as the chemical equilibration process. Furthermore, the proper equilibrium off-shell distribution is reached for large times contrary to the quasiparticle Boltzmann limit. We additionally present a direct comparison of the solution of the generalized transport equations in the Kadanoff-Baym and Botermans-Malfliet form; both solutions are found to agree very well with each other.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that generalized off-shell transport equations derived from a first-order gradient expansion of the Kadanoff-Baym equations reproduce the full quantum time evolution of nonequilibrium \(\phi^4\)-theory in 2+1 dimensions with high accuracy, including the proper equilibrium off-shell distribution at large times, which the quasiparticle Boltzmann limit fails to reach.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the full Kadanoff-Baym equations for spatially homogeneous nonequilibrium initial configurations of \(\phi^4\)-theory in 2+1 dimensions within the three-loop approximation of the two-particle-irreducible (2PI) effective action, which includes a local tadpole self-energy and a non-local sunset self-energy. They then derived and solved generalized transport equations in Wigner space using a first-order gradient expansion, retaining off-shell dynamics through time-dependent spectral functions, and compared these solutions to the full Kadanoff-Baym results as well as to the on-shell quasiparticle Boltzmann limit. Additionally, they compared two formulations of the generalized transport equations: the Kadanoff-Baym form and the Botermans-Malfliet form.

## Key numbers

- The spectral function \(\bar{A}\) in the first-order gradient approximation takes a Breit-Wigner form: \(\bar{A} = \bar{\Gamma} / [\bar{M}^2 + \bar{\Gamma}^2/4]\), where \(\bar{M} = p_0^2 - \vec{p}^2 - m^2 - \bar{\Sigma}^{\delta} - Re\bar{\Sigma}^{R}\) and \(\bar{\Gamma}\) is the width from the imaginary part of the retarded self-energy.
- The non-local sunset self-energy in coordinate space is given by \(\Sigma^{\gtrless}(x_1,x_2) = -(\lambda^2/6) [G^{\gtrless}(x_1,x_2)]^3\), where \(\lambda\) is the coupling constant.
- The local tadpole self-energy is \(\Sigma^{\delta}(x) = (\lambda/2) i G^{<}(x,x)\), generating an effective mass for the field quanta.

## Context

This work extends previous numerical studies of Kadanoff-Baym equations for nonrelativistic systems by Danielewicz (1984) and for relativistic systems by Greiner and collaborators, providing the first direct comparison of full Kadanoff-Baym solutions with generalized off-shell transport equations for a strongly interacting field theory in 2+1 dimensions. The 2+1 dimensional case is chosen because it supercedes the artificial structure of 1+1 dimensional \(\phi^4\)-theory (where on-shell scattering is collinear and does not lead to equilibration) while avoiding the more severe renormalization complications of 3+1 dimensions. The results enable validation of off-shell transport schemes used in simulations of heavy-ion collisions, particularly for broad resonances like the \(\rho\) meson, and open the door to extending such comparisons to non-abelian gauge theories like QCD.
