# Medium Effects in DIS from Polarized Nuclear Targets

> The behavior of the nucleon structure functions in lepton nuclei deep inelastic scattering, both polarized and unpolarized, due to nuclear structure effects is reanalyzed. The study is performed in two schemes: an x-rescaling approach, and one in which there is an increase of sea quark components in the in medium nucleon, related to the low energy N-N interaction. In view of a recent interesting experimental proposal to study the behavior of the proton spin structure functions in nuclei we proceed to compare these approaches in an effort to enlighten the possible phenomenological interest of such difficult experiment.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a proposed experiment measuring the polarized structure function ratio \(R_{pol}=g_1^A/A g_1^p\) on a polarized \({}^{7}\)Li target could reveal whether spin-spin interactions in nuclei modify the scaling parameter \(\eta\) beyond the unpolarized value, but the authors hedge that this is not a conventional "average" EMC effect because the target is essentially a single polarized proton in a nuclear medium.

## Method

The authors compare two phenomenological schemes for nuclear medium effects in deep inelastic scattering: an \(x\)-rescaling approach (parameter \(\eta\)) and a modified sea scheme (MSS) with two parameters (\(\delta\) for rescaling, \(\sigma\) for sea enhancement). They apply both schemes to the unpolarized structure function \(F_2\) for \({}^{7}\)Li and deuterium at fixed \(Q^2 = 10\) GeV\(^2\), using parton distributions from Martin et al. (2009), then extend the same parameterizations to the polarized structure function \(g_1\) using fits from de Florian et al. (2008, 2009). The polarized ratio \(R_{pol}\) is defined as \(g_1^p(x^*)/g_1^p(x)\) in the \(x\)-rescaling approach, and analogously in the MSS.

## Key numbers

* For \({}^{7}\)Li in the \(x\)-rescaling approach, the parameter \(\eta = 1.011 \pm 0.002\) (extracted from a linear extrapolation of fits to several nuclei).
* For \({}^{7}\)Li in the MSS approach, the parameters are \(\delta = 1.016 \pm 0.001\) and \(\sigma = 1\) (fitted to reproduce the \(x\)-rescaling ratio in the EMC region).
* For deuterium in the MSS approach, the parameters are \(\delta = 1.005\) and \(\sigma = 1\).
* The analysis is performed at fixed \(Q^2 = 10\) GeV\(^2\).
* The polarized ratio \(R_{pol}\) is shown to be extremely sensitive to \(\eta\), with a band spanning from the unpolarized \(\eta = 1.011\) up to \(\eta = 1.02\).

## Context

This work extends the unpolarized EMC effect analysis of Garcia Canal (1984) and the pion content approach of Epele (1994) and de Florian (1993) to the polarized case, motivated by a recent experimental proposal (Joo, 2014) to measure the polarized structure function on \({}^{7}\)Li. The authors compare their results with two detailed model calculations: one by Cloet (2005, 2006) using a quark-diquark bound state in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, and another by Smith (2005) using the chiral quark-soliton model with explicit sea quarks. The paper enables future experimental discrimination between these competing theoretical descriptions of nuclear medium effects on spin structure.
