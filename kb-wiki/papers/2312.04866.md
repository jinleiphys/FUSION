# Understanding oscillating features of the timelike nucleon electromagnetic form factors within the extending vector meson dominance model

> We investigate the nonmonotonic behavior observed in the time-like nucleon electromagnetic form factors. Using a phenomenological extending vector meson dominance model, where the ground states $ρ$ and $ω$ and their excited states $ρ(2D)$, $ω(3D)$, and $ω(5S)$ are taken into account, we have successfully reproduced the cross sections of $e^+ e^- \to p \bar{p}$ and $e^+ e^- \to n \bar{n}$ reactions. Furthermore, we have derived the nucleon electromagnetic form factors in the time-like region, and it is found that the so-called periodic behaviour of the nucleon effective form factors is not confirmed. However, there are indeed nonmonotonic structures in the line shape of nucleon effective form factors, which can be naturally reproduced by considering the contributions from the low-lying excited vector states.

# Understanding oscillating features of the timelike nucleon electromagnetic form factors within the extending vector meson dominance model

## Key claim

Using an extending vector meson dominance model that includes the ground states ρ and ω plus the excited states ρ(2D), ω(3D), and ω(5S), the authors reproduce the cross sections of e+e- → p pbar and e+e- → n nbar reactions, but find that the so-called periodic behavior of the nucleon effective form factors reported by BESIII (2021) is not confirmed; instead, nonmonotonic structures arise naturally from the low-lying excited vector state contributions.

## Method

The authors employ a phenomenological vector meson dominance (VMD) model with a dipole intrinsic form factor g(s) = 1/(1 − γ s)^2, where γ = 1.41 GeV^−2, and parameterize the Dirac and Pauli form factors F1 and F2 for proton and neutron in the time-like region as sums over Breit-Wigner propagators for the ρ, ω, ρ(2D), ω(3D), and ω(5S) mesons. They perform a χ^2 fit to 224 experimental data points from BESIII, BaBar, CMD-3, and SND collaborations, covering center-of-mass energies from the kinematic reaction threshold up to 3.2 GeV, including total cross sections, electromagnetic form factors |GE| and |GM|, and the ratio |GE/GM|.

## Key numbers

* The ρ(2D) meson is assigned a mass of 2040 MeV and width of 202 MeV; the ω(3D) meson has mass 2283 MeV and width 94 MeV; the ω(5S) meson has mass 2422 MeV and width 69 MeV (Table 1).
* The fitted parameters yield χ^2/d.o.f = 1.6 for the global fit to 224 data points.
* The dipole form parameter γ = 1.41 GeV^−2 is used for the intrinsic form factor g(s).
* The ground state masses used are m_ρ = 775.26 MeV, Γ_ρ = 147.4 MeV, m_ω = 782.66 MeV, Γ_ω = 8.68 MeV.
* The fitted global factor c_0 for the dipole effective form factor G_D = c_0/(1−γ s)^2 is c_0 = 5.54 ± 0.02 for proton and c_0 = 4.08 ± 0.04 for neutron.
* The ratio R = σ(e+e- → p pbar)/σ(e+e- → n nbar) is found to be larger than 1 at all measured energies, consistent with BESIII (2021) results and contrasting with the earlier FENICE (1998) experiment.

## Context

This work extends the VMD approach of Iachello (1972, 2004) and Bijker (2004) for baryon electromagnetic form factors, and directly addresses the claim of periodic oscillatory behavior in nucleon effective form factors reported by BESIII (2021) and SND (2022). The authors show that the nonmonotonic structures can be explained by the interference of low-lying excited vector states rather than requiring a periodic functional form. The study enables future experimental tests of the predicted relative phase ΔΦ between GE and GM, particularly the spin polarization at 2.28 GeV and 2.40 GeV, and provides a framework for using e+e- → N Nbar data to improve knowledge of poorly known excited vector states above 2 GeV.
