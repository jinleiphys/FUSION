# Effects of nuclear medium and nonisoscalarity in extracting $sin^2θ_W$ using Paschos-Wolfenstein relation

> We study the nuclear medium effects and nonisoscalarity correction in the extraction of weak mixing angle sin$^2θ_W$ using Paschos-Wolfenstein (PW) relation. The calculations are performed for the iron nucleus. The results are discussed along with the experimental result inferred by NuTeV collaboration. The nuclear medium effects like Fermi motion, binding, shadowing and antishadowing corrections and pion and rho meson cloud contributions have been taken into account. Calculations have been performed in the local density approximation using a relativistic nuclear spectral function which includes nucleon correlations. These studies may be useful for the ongoing MINER$ν$A experiment as well as for the proposed NuSOnG experiment.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that nuclear medium effects and nonisoscalarity corrections in iron (N=30, Z=26) shift the extracted value of sin²θ_W from the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation, but these corrections alone are insufficient to fully resolve the NuTeV anomaly (the 3-standard-deviation discrepancy between the NuTeV result of 0.2277±0.0004 and the global fit value of 0.2227±0.0004).

## Method

The authors compute neutrino-nucleus deep inelastic scattering cross sections for iron using a relativistic nuclear spectral function in the local density approximation, including Fermi motion, binding, nucleon correlations, pion and rho meson cloud contributions, target mass corrections, and shadowing effects. They separate the nuclear hadronic tensor into proton and neutron contributions for the nonisoscalar target, expand the spectral function around the isoscalar condition in powers of (N-Z)/V, and evaluate the Paschos-Wolfenstein ratio using CTEQ6.6 parton distribution functions for the free nucleon structure functions.

## Key numbers

- NuTeV experimental values: R^ν_exp = 0.3916 ± 0.0007 and R^ν̄_exp = 0.4050 ± 0.0016 for iron
- NuTeV reported sin²θ_W = 0.2277 ± 0.0004, which is 3 standard deviations above the global fit value of sin²θ_W = 0.2227 ± 0.0004 (Abbaneo 2001)
- Iron nucleus has N=30 neutrons and Z=26 protons, giving nonisoscalarity parameter δ = N-Z = 4
- The study covers neutrino energies in the range relevant to the NuTeV experiment and the MINERνA experiment (1-20 GeV)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of nuclear medium effects on electromagnetic and weak structure functions (Haider et al. 2009, 2011, 2012) to the specific problem of the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation. It builds on the theoretical framework of Kulagin (2002), who investigated nuclear medium effects on the PW ratio and found small but significant isoscalar corrections, and Kumano (2002), who suggested valence quark distribution differences may contribute to the anomaly. The results are intended to inform ongoing analysis by the MINERνA experiment and the proposed NuSOnG experiment, and complement the work of Thomas (2010) who proposed charge symmetry violation and the isovector EMC effect as possible explanations for the NuTeV anomaly.
