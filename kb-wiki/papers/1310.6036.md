# Hydrodynamics of charge fluctuations and balance functions

> We apply stochastic hydrodynamics to the study of charge density fluctuations in QCD matter undergoing Bjorken expansion. We find that the charge density correlations are given by a time integral over the history of the system, with the dominant contribution coming from the QCD crossover region where the change of susceptibility per entropy, chi T/s, is most significant. We study the rapidity and azimuthal angle dependence of the resulting charge balance function using a simple analytic model of heavy-ion collision evolution. Our results are in agreement with experimental measurements, indicating that hydrodynamic fluctuations contribute significantly to the measured charge correlations in high energy heavy-ion collisions. The sensitivity of the balance function to the value of the charge diffusion coefficient D allows us to estimate the typical value of this coefficient in the crossover region to be rather small, of the order of 1/(2pi T), characteristic of a strongly coupled plasma.

## Key claim

The paper argues that hydrodynamic fluctuations, modeled via stochastic hydrodynamics, contribute significantly to measured charge correlations in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, with the dominant contribution arising from the QCD crossover region where the ratio \(\chi T/s\) changes most rapidly, rather than from the entire expansion history.

## Method

The authors linearize stochastic hydrodynamic equations around a boost-invariant Bjorken expansion background at zero net charge density, solving analytically for charge density fluctuations sourced by local noise currents. They use lattice QCD data from Borsanyi et al. (2010, 2011) for the temperature dependence of entropy density and charge susceptibility, and incorporate a sudden transverse expansion approximation with a blast-wave model to account for radial flow and Cooper-Frye freezeout to translate position-space correlations into momentum-space observables.

## Key numbers

* The charge diffusion coefficient \(D\) in the crossover region is estimated to be of order \(1/(2\pi T)\), characteristic of a strongly coupled plasma.
* For an ideal massless QGP with three flavors, \(\chi T/s = 6/(19\pi^2)\); for a massless pion gas, \(\chi T/s = 5/(2\pi^2)\), illustrating a roughly 16-fold increase in \(s/T^3\) versus a 2-fold increase in \(\chi/T^2\) across the crossover.
* The kinetic freezeout temperature is taken as \(T_{\rm kf} \approx 100\) MeV, with the corresponding hydrodynamic freezeout temperature \(T_{\rm hf} \approx 130\) MeV and pion chemical potential \(\mu_\pi \sim 80\) MeV, following Teaney (2002).

## Context

This work extends the stochastic hydrodynamics framework of Kapusta et al. (2011) by including charge diffusion and focusing on electric charge fluctuations at zero mean density, whereas that earlier work studied baryon number fluctuations at nonzero baryon density without diffusion. The results enable quantitative comparison with experimental balance function measurements from RHIC and LHC, and the sensitivity to the diffusion coefficient \(D\) provides a method to extract transport properties of the QCD crossover region. The approach complements initial-state fluctuation and jet-based explanations of charge correlations, and the authors note that initial-state charge fluctuations are suppressed in ultra-relativistic collisions due to the dominance of gluons.
