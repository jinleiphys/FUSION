# Moments of Nucleon Generalized Parton Distributions in Lattice QCD

> Calculation of moments of generalized parton distributions in lattice QCD requires more powerful techniques than those previously used to calculate moments of structure functions. Hence, we present a novel approach that exploits the full information content from a given lattice configuration by measuring an overdetermined set of lattice observables to provide maximal statistical constraints on the generalized form factors at a given virtuality, t. In an exploratory investigation using unquenched QCD configurations at intermediate sea quark masses, we demonstrate that our new technique is superior to conventional methods and leads to reliable numerical signals for the n=2 flavor singlet generalized form factors up to 3 GeV^2. The contribution from connected diagrams in the flavor singlet sector to the total quark angular momentum is measured to an accuracy of the order of one percent.

## Key claim

The authors present a novel lattice QCD technique that exploits an overdetermined set of lattice observables to provide maximal statistical constraints on generalized form factors (GFFs) at a given virtuality \(t\), demonstrating in an exploratory study at a single heavy pion mass (896 MeV) that the method yields reliable signals for the \(n=2\) flavor singlet GFFs up to 3 GeV\(^2\) and measures the connected-diagram contribution to total quark angular momentum to approximately one percent accuracy.

## Method

The method constructs an overdetermined system of linear equations by measuring multiple lattice representations of the same continuum twist-two operator and multiple momentum combinations at fixed virtuality \(t\), then solves the system via singular value decomposition (SVD) with jackknife error analysis. The lattice matrix elements are extracted from ratios of three-point to two-point correlation functions using smeared sources and sinks on unquenched (SESAM) Wilson configurations at \(\beta=5.6\) with \(\kappa_{\text{sea}}=\kappa_{\text{val}}=0.1560\), and are renormalized to the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme at \(\mu^2=4\) GeV\(^2\) using one-loop perturbative matching.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: \(m_\pi = 896(6)\) MeV, from lattice spacing \(a^{-1}=2.01\) GeV and \((am)_\pi = 0.446(3)\)
- Lattice: \(16^3 \times 32\) at \(\beta=5.6\)
- Reliable numerical signals obtained for \(n=2\) flavor singlet GFFs up to \(t = 3\) GeV\(^2\)
- Connected-diagram contribution to total quark angular momentum measured to an accuracy of order one percent

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD calculations of forward parton distribution moments (e.g., Gockeler (1996), Best (1997), Dolgov (2002)) to the more challenging case of generalized parton distributions, where the additional momentum transfer \(\Delta\) introduces extra statistical noise. The new overdetermined-observable technique is superior to conventional methods that select a single row of the matrix equation or take linear combinations of two equations, as was done previously for electromagnetic form factors. This enables future lattice calculations to provide first-principles input for extracting the full \(x\), \(\xi\), and \(t\) dependence of GPDs from experiments like deeply virtual Compton scattering, complementing phenomenological determinations that necessarily rely on uncontrolled assumptions.
