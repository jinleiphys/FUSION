# Relativistic hydrodynamics with spinodal decomposition

> We introduce the equations of relativistic hydrodynamics that incorporate phase separation via spinodal decomposition. These equations consider surface effects between the two phases and are applicable for simulating intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions and binary neutron star mergers, where a first-order phase transition is expected. We solve these equations in the context of Bjorken flow, which offers the relevant geometric framework for ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a covariant relativistic hydrodynamic framework that incorporates spinodal decomposition and surface effects between phases, but the numerical demonstration is limited to Bjorken flow with the simplifying assumption of boost-invariant energy density (violation less than 3%) and a sinusoidal baryon density perturbation.

## Method

The authors generalize the Cahn-Hilliard non-relativistic spinodal decomposition theory to a relativistic covariant form by constructing a stress-energy tensor and baryon current that include surface energy terms proportional to a coefficient K and gradients of the baryon density. They then reduce the conservation equations to a system of coupled partial differential equations in Bjorken coordinates (proper time τ and rapidity ξ) and solve them numerically using an equation of state that embeds a first-order phase transition curve with a critical point onto a background matched to lattice QCD at zero baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The surface energy coefficient is set to K = 0 and K = 5 × 10⁻⁵ MeV⁻⁴ to compare the effect of surface terms.
- The evolution starts at proper time τ = 10 fm with a constant energy density and sinusoidal baryon density distribution.
- The violation of boost invariance in energy density was found to be less than 3%.
- Results are shown at τ = 10 fm, 10.5 fm, 11.5 fm, 12.5 fm, and 13.5 fm for baryon density evolution.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic hydrodynamic treatment of first-order phase transitions by incorporating spinodal decomposition, building on prior studies of phase transitions in nuclear collisions by Randrup (2009, 2010) and on the authors' own earlier framework (Kapusta 2024). The approach is designed for application to intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions at RHIC's Beam Energy Scan and upcoming FAIR experiments, as well as binary neutron star mergers, where the QCD phase diagram at large baryon chemical potential is expected to exhibit a first-order phase transition terminating at a critical point (Fukushima 2010).
