# Two-level interacting boson models beyond the mean field

> The phase diagram of two-level boson Hamiltonians, including the Interacting Boson Model (IBM), is studied beyond the standard mean field approximation using the Holstein-Primakoff mapping. The limitations of the usual intrinsic state (mean field) formalism concerning finite-size effects are pointed out. The analytic results are compared to numerics obtained from exact diagonalizations. Excitation energies and occupation numbers are studied in different model space regions (Casten triangle for IBM) and especially at the critical points.

## Key claim

The standard mean-field (intrinsic state) formalism for the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) is only correct at leading order in a 1/N expansion, and its 1/N corrections are generally incorrect; the Holstein-Primakoff mapping provides a systematic expansion that yields proper finite-size corrections to excitation energies and occupation numbers, particularly at critical points where mean-field predictions fail.

## Method

The authors apply a Holstein-Primakoff transformation to eliminate the s-boson degree of freedom, then perform a shift transformation to handle both spherical (λμ=0) and deformed (λ0≠0) phases, retaining terms up to order N^0 (i.e., including N, N^(1/2), and N^0 contributions). A Bogoliubov transformation diagonalizes the resulting quadratic Hamiltonian separately for each μ mode (K=0, ±1, ±2 for L=2), and the analytical results are compared to exact numerical diagonalizations for the L=0 case (up to N=5000 bosons) and the L=2 (IBM) case (up to N=100 bosons).

## Key numbers

- For L=2 with χ=-√7/2 (SU(3) limit), the critical point occurs at x_c = 9/11 ≈ 0.818.
- For L=2 with χ=0, the second-order phase transition occurs at x_c = 4/5 = 0.8.
- The spinodal line for the SU(3) case (χ=-√7/2) gives x ≈ 0.820361.
- Numerical calculations for L=2 (IBM) are performed with N=100 bosons; for L=0, calculations use N=5000 bosons.
- In the spherical phase, the one-phonon excitation energy is given by Ξ(x)^(1/2) = [x(5x-4)]^(1/2), and the ground-state number of L-bosons is ⟨n_L⟩_gs = (2L+1)/2 × [(3x-2)/Ξ(x) - 1] + O(1/N).

## Context

This work extends the mean-field phase diagram studies of the IBM (Cejnar 2000, Jolie 2002, Arias 2003) by providing analytic finite-size corrections beyond the leading order, which the intrinsic state formalism (Ginocchio 1980, Dieperink 1980) cannot correctly compute. The method enables systematic study of quantum phase transitions in finite bosonic systems, particularly at critical points where 1/N corrections become singular, and is applicable to any two-level boson model (including the vibron model for L=1).
