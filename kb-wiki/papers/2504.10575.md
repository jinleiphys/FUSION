# Sakai-Sugimoto Model in an Off-Shell: Chiral Lagrangian to All Orders

> The Sakai-Sugimoto holographic model is famous for implementing the approximate chiral symmetry of QCD and reproducing the Chiral Lagrangian in a top-down approach. In this manuscript, we revisit the model in a formalism that is somewhat different from the original work by Sakai and Sugimoto: We start by identifying boundary degrees of freedom and splitting the bulk equations of motion into dynamical ones and constraints. The former are then solved to all orders in derivatives of the boundary fields. The constraints are left unsolved, leaving the dynamical degrees of freedom off-shell. This approach enables us to systematically derive the effective action of the boundary theory. The derived effective action is very rich in physics: it contains an $U(N_f)$ multiplet of massless pseudoscalars interacting (via trilinear and higher terms) with towers of massive (axial-)vector mesons. In contrast to the previous studies, our effective action is non-local. The original Chiral Lagrangian is recovered as its local expansion in small $π$-meson momenta (derivative expansion). We particularly zoom in on the values of four derivative terms couplings, the low energy constants, and compare those with the ones reported in the literature.

## Key claim

The authors derive an off-shell effective action for the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic model that is non-local and contains all orders in derivatives of the boundary fields, recovering the Chiral Lagrangian only as a local expansion in small pion momenta; the bare low energy constants (LECs) are distinguished from effective LECs, and the results for \(N_f=2,3\) agree with Hoyos (2022) but are now extended to arbitrary \(N_f\).

## Method

The bulk gauge field \(A_M\) is split into dynamical equations and constraints after fixing the axial gauge \(A_z=0\). The dynamical equations are solved perturbatively to all orders in derivatives of the boundary fields using a weak-field expansion in an auxiliary parameter \(\varepsilon\), keeping terms up to \(\varepsilon^3\) to obtain quartic interactions. The constraints are left unsolved, leaving the pions (\(\Sigma\) matrix and \(\eta'\)-meson) and towers of massive (axial-)vector mesons \(V_{\mu,n}\) as dynamical degrees of freedom in the resulting non-local effective action.

## Key numbers

* The pion decay constant \(f_\pi\) appears in the expansion \(\Sigma = \exp(2i\pi^a T^a / f_\pi)\) and in the boundary condition \(B_\mu^a(x) = -2i\,\text{Tr}[\Sigma^{-1}\partial_\mu\Sigma T^a]\).
* The Kaluza-Klein scale \(\mkk\) is defined as \(\mkk \equiv \frac{3}{2} \frac{U_{\text{KK}}^{1/2}}{R^{3/2}}\) and sets the mass of the lowest vector mode.
* The bare LECs obey \(L_2^{(l)} = -L_1^{(l)}\) from the leading-order DBI expansion, while the non-linear contributions satisfy \(L_2^{(nl)} = 2 L_1^{(nl)}\).
* The parametric dependence of the LECs is \(L_{1,2}^{(l)} = \mathcal{O}(N_c \lambda_{\text{YM}})\) and \(L_{1,2}^{(nl)} = \mathcal{O}(N_c / \lambda_{\text{YM}})\), where \(\lambda_{\text{YM}}\) is the 't Hooft constant.

## Context

This work extends the original Sakai-Sugimoto model (Sakai 2004, 2005) by using an off-shell formalism previously developed in the fluid/gravity correspondence (Bu 2014, Crossley 2015). It goes beyond the leading-order DBI expansion of Sakai (2004) and incorporates the non-linear effects computed by Hoyos (2022) for \(N_f=2\), while generalizing to arbitrary \(N_f\) and including the abelian \(\eta'\)-meson sector. The derived effective action enables systematic computation of meson spectra, \(\pi\pi\)-scattering amplitudes, and interactions such as \(\omega \to \rho\pi\) and \(\rho(1450) \to [a_1(1260) + \pi]_{P\text{-wave}}\), and clarifies the distinction between bare and effective LECs in the presence of vector mesons.
