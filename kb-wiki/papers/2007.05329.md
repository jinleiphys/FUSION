# On the nature of near-threshold bound and virtual states

> Physical states are characterised uniquely by their pole positions and the corresponding residues. Accordingly, in those parameters also the nature of the states should be encoded. For bound states (poles on the real $s$-axis below the lowest threshold on the physical sheet) there is an established criterion formulated originally by Weinberg in the 1960s, which allows one to estimate the amount of compact and molecular components in a given state. We demonstrate in this paper that this criterion can be straightforwardly extended to shallow virtual states (poles on the real $s$-axis below the lowest threshold on the unphysical sheet) which should be classified as molecular. We argue that predominantly non-molecular or compact states exist either as bound states or as resonances (poles on the unphysical sheet off the real energy axis) but not as virtual states. We also discuss the limitations of the mentioned classification scheme.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Weinberg's compositeness criterion for bound states can be extended to shallow virtual states (poles on the real axis below threshold on the unphysical sheet), showing that such states must be classified as molecular; however, the authors also note that the classification scheme has limitations and that the extension relies on neglecting range corrections of order \(\gamma/\beta\).

## Method

The authors use a nonrelativistic formalism with a Hamiltonian coupling a bare compact state to a two-hadron \(S\)-wave continuum, deriving the field renormalization constant \(Z\) from the self-energy and matching to the effective range expansion (ERE) to relate \(Z\) to the scattering length \(a\) and effective range \(r\). They then analyze pole trajectories in the complex momentum plane as a function of the scattering length, assuming the effective range remains constant, to classify states as bound, virtual, or resonant based on the sign and magnitude of \(a\) and \(r\).

## Key numbers

- For the \(^1S_0\) channel in nucleon-nucleon scattering: scattering length \(a = 23.7\ \text{fm}\) and effective range \(r = 2.7\ \text{fm}\), yielding \(\bar{X}_A = 0.9\) (molecular).
- For lattice NN scattering at \(m_\pi = 450\ \text{MeV}\): in the \(^3S_1\) channel, \(a_{\text{NLO}} = -2.234\ \text{fm}\) and \(r_{\text{NLO}} = 1.07\ \text{fm}\); in the \(^1S_0\) channel, \(a_{\text{NLO}} = -2.501\ \text{fm}\) and \(r_{\text{NLO}} = 1.25\ \text{fm}\); both give \(\bar{X}_A \approx 0.7\).
- For the deuteron: \(a = -5.41\ \text{fm}\) and \(r = +1.75\ \text{fm}\), giving \(\bar{X}_A = 0.8\) (molecular).

## Context

This work extends the Weinberg compositeness criterion (Weinberg, 1960s) originally formulated for bound states to virtual states, filling a gap left by prior generalizations that focused on resonances (Hyodo, 2013; Kamiya, 2016). The results enable model-independent classification of near-threshold states in heavy meson spectroscopy (e.g., \(D_{s0}^*(2317)\), \(X(3872)\), \(Z_b\) states) and provide a tool for analyzing lattice QCD results, such as the NN scattering data from NPLQCD (Orginos, 2015) and the \(DK\) system studied by Liu (2012).
