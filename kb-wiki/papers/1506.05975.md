# Subtraction method in the second random--phase approximation: first applications with a Skyrme energy functional

> We make use of a subtraction procedure, introduced to overcome double--counting problems in beyond--mean--field theories, in the second random--phase--approximation (SRPA) for the first time. This procedure guarantees the stability of SRPA (so that all excitation energies are real). We show that the method fits perfectly into nuclear density--functional theory. We illustrate applications to the monopole and quadrupole response and to low--lying $0^+$ and $2^+$ states in the nucleus $^{16}$O. We show that the subtraction procedure leads to: (i) results that are weakly cutoff dependent; (ii) a considerable reduction of the SRPA downwards shift with respect to the random--phase approximation (RPA) spectra (systematically found in all previous applications). This implementation of the SRPA model will allow a reliable analysis of the effects of 2 particle--2 hole configurations ($2p2h$) on the excitation spectra of medium--mass and heavy nuclei.

## Key claim

The subtraction procedure, applied to the second random-phase approximation (SRPA) for the first time, guarantees real excitation energies and reduces the systematic downward shift of SRPA spectra relative to RPA spectra, but only when the full $2p2h$ space is used coherently in both the SRPA matrices and the correction terms; the method fits perfectly into nuclear density-functional theory.

## Method

The authors implement the Tselyaev subtraction method within the SRPA framework using the Skyrme energy functional SGII for the nucleus $^{16}$O. They construct energy-dependent RPA-like matrices $A_{11'}(\omega)$ and $B_{11'}(\omega)$ that include $1p1h$-$2p2h$ coupling, then subtract the static parts $E_{11'}(0)$ and $F_{11'}(0)$ to obtain subtracted matrices $A^S_{11'}(\omega)$ and $B^S_{11'}(\omega)$, which are then transformed back into energy-independent matrix form. Calculations are performed both with full inversion of the $A_{22'}$ matrix (SSRPA$_F$) and with a diagonal approximation (SSRPA$_D$) where off-diagonal terms of $A_{22'}$ are discarded in the correction terms.

## Key numbers

* Isoscalar monopole response in $^{16}$O: $2p2h$ cutoff at 70 MeV leads to about 5000 $2p2h$ configurations; subtraction shifts the SRPA spectrum upward, with the effect increasing with cutoff energy.
* Isoscalar quadrupole response in $^{16}$O: $2p2h$ cutoff at 50 MeV leads to about 5000 $2p2h$ configurations; similar upward shift observed.
* Low-lying $0^+$ state energy in SSRPA$_D$ with cutoffs at 70, 80, and 90 MeV: 6.26 MeV, 6.13 MeV, and 5.96 MeV respectively, a difference of only 5% between highest and lowest.
* For comparison with experiment, monopole EWSR fractions multiplied by 0.48 and quadrupole EWSR fractions multiplied by 0.53, because only 48% of $E0$ EWSR and 53% of $E2$ EWSR were measured in the experimental reference.

## Context

This work extends the subtraction method previously applied by Tselyaev (2007) to particle-vibration coupling models (both non-relativistic and relativistic time-blocking approximation) into the SRPA framework for the first time. It addresses the systematic downward shift of SRPA spectra relative to RPA that was identified in earlier large-scale SRPA calculations by Gambacurta et al. (several papers) and Papakonstantinou (2014). The method enables reliable analysis of $2p2h$ configuration effects on excitation spectra of medium-mass and heavy nuclei within density-functional theory, avoiding the double-counting problems that arise when mixing EDFs with explicit Hamiltonian many-body theory.
