# Simple Formula for High-Energy Gluon Bremsstrahlung in a Finite, Expanding Medium

> Previous authors have considered the problem of the medium effects on single gluon bremsstrahlung associated with producing a high-energy particle in a finite, time-dependent QCD plasma. Working to leading logarithmic order, I show that the result for the bremsstrahlung gluon spectrum can be cast into a remarkably simple form in the general case. I similarly analyze the process of pair production. Also, I comment on the radius of convergence of the opacity expansion in cases where the leading-log approximation holds, showing that the opacity expansion does not converge when the thickness of the plasma is greater than roughly the bremsstrahlung formation time. Additionally, as a special bonus -- available for a limited time only while supplies last! -- I summarize translations between the notation used by a few of the groups who have worked on this and related problems.

## Key claim

The author shows that the leading-logarithmic result for the medium-induced single gluon bremsstrahlung spectrum in a finite, time-dependent QCD plasma can be reduced to a remarkably simple form involving a single differential equation, generalizing the earlier brick-medium result of Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, and Schiff (BDMS) to arbitrary non-uniform, time-dependent media. The result is valid only to leading logarithmic order and in three spatial dimensions, and the author notes that for sufficiently small medium thickness the average medium effect is instead dominated by atypical single-scattering events.

## Method

The author starts from the Zakharov/BDMPS formalism for the LPM effect in QCD, which expresses the bremsstrahlung spectrum in terms of a two-dimensional quantum mechanical Green's function with a time-dependent Hamiltonian. Working in the leading-log (harmonic oscillator) approximation, where the elastic scattering rate is replaced by its small-impact-parameter limit proportional to the jet quenching parameter \(\hat q(t)\), the author derives that the double integral over the Green's function can be evaluated analytically for arbitrary time dependence of \(\hat q(t)\). The final result is expressed in terms of a solution \(c(t)\) to a simple second-order differential equation \(d^2c/dt^2 = -\omega_0^2(t)\,c(t)\) with boundary condition \(c(t)\to 1\) as \(t\to\infty\), where \(\omega_0^2(t)\) depends on the local value of \(\hat q(t)\) along the particle's trajectory.

## Key numbers

* For a uniform brick medium of length \(L\), the spectrum is \(\omega \frac{d}{d\omega}(I-I_{\rm vac}) = \frac{\alpha}{\pi} x P_{s\to g}(x) \ln|\cos(\omega_0 L)|\), where \(\omega_0^2 = -i [(1-x)C_A + x^2 C_s] \hat q / [2x(1-x)E]\).
* The large-\(L\) limit of the brick result gives \(\ln|\cos(\omega_0 L)| \simeq |\omega_0|L/\sqrt{2} - \ln 2\), with the bulk rate \(\omega \frac{d\Gamma_{\rm bulk}}{d\omega} = \frac{\alpha}{\pi\sqrt{2}} x P_{s\to g}(x) |\omega_0|\).
* The leading-log value of \(\hat q\) for a weakly-coupled thermal QCD plasma is \(\hat q \simeq \alpha T m_D^2 \ln(Q_\perp^2/m_D^2)\) when \(Q_\perp \lesssim T\), and \(\hat q \simeq \alpha T m_D^2 \ln(T^2/m_D^2) + 4\pi \alpha^2 \mathcal{N} \ln(Q_\perp^2/T^2)\) otherwise.
* The Debye mass is \(m_D^2 = (1 + \frac16 N_f) g^2 T^2\) and the weighted number density is \(\mathcal{N} = \frac{\zeta(3)}{\zeta(2)} (1 + \frac14 N_f) T^3\).
* The harmonic oscillator approximation is valid when \(L \gg L_{\rm cr}/[\ln(Q_\perp^2/m_D^2)]^{1/2}\), where the infinite-medium formation time is \(L_{\rm cr} \sim (E_i / C_{R_i} \hat q)^{1/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the leading-log brick-medium result of Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, and Schiff (BDMS) from 1997 to the general case of non-uniform, time-dependent media such as an expanding quark-gluon plasma. The author notes that BDMS had previously found a less simple result for expanding media involving a double integral of a complicated function. The paper also comments on the radius of convergence of the opacity expansion, showing it does not converge when the plasma thickness exceeds roughly the bremsstrahlung formation time, and provides a translation table between notations used by different groups (BDMS, Zakharov, AMY). The result enables simpler numerical or analytic calculations of medium-induced gluon radiation in realistic expanding plasmas produced in heavy-ion collisions.
