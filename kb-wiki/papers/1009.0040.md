# Color Superfluid and Trionic State of Attractive Three-Component Lattice Fermionic Atoms at Finite Temperatures

> We investigate the finite-temperature properties of attractive three-component (colors) fermionic atoms in optical lattices using a self-energy functional approach. As the strength of the attractive interaction increases in the low temperature region, a second-order transition occurs from a Fermi liquid to a color superfluid (CSF). In the strong attractive region, a first-order transition occurs from a CSF to a trionic state. In the high temperature region, a crossover between a Fermi liquid and a trionic state is observed with increasing the strength of the attractive interaction. The crossover region for fixed temperature is almost independent of filling.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, for attractive three-component fermionic atoms in optical lattices at finite temperatures, the crossover region between a Fermi liquid and a trionic state occurs at nearly the same interaction strength regardless of filling, and that the phase diagram features a second-order Fermi-liquid-to-color-superfluid transition and a first-order color-superfluid-to-trionic transition, but these results are obtained within a self-energy functional approach that neglects spatial fluctuations, which may alter the low-temperature behavior in finite-dimensional systems.

## Method

The authors used a self-energy functional approach (SFA) based on the Luttinger-Ward variational method, applying a two-site Anderson impurity model as the reference system to study the three-component attractive Hubbard model on a Bethe lattice with a semicircular density of states. They calculated the color superfluid (CSF) order parameter, quasiparticle weight, entropy, trion number, and local susceptibility of trion-number fluctuations as functions of interaction strength and temperature, determining phase boundaries by comparing grand potentials of different phases.

## Key numbers

- The first-order CSF-to-trion quantum phase transition occurs at \(U/t = -1.94\) at zero temperature and half filling.
- The CSF order parameter \(\Phi\) vanishes discontinuously at \(U/t = -1.94\) at \(T=0\).
- The quasiparticle weight \(Z\) drops discontinuously to zero at the same \(U/t = -1.94\) at \(T=0\).
- The trion number \(N_t\) jumps to approximately 0.5 at the critical \(U/t = -1.94\) at half filling and \(T=0\).
- The crossover region between Fermi liquid and trionic state appears at \(|U|/t \sim 2\) at half filling, corresponding to the trionic excitation gap (\(2|U|\)) becoming comparable to the bandwidth (\(4t\)).
- For fixed temperature \(T/t = 0.08\), the crossover region appears in \(1.6 < |U|/t < 1.7\) irrespective of filling for \(0.1 < n < 0.9\).
- The entropy in the trionic state at half filling approaches \(S/L \sim \ln 2 \sim 0.693\).
- The local susceptibility of trion-number fluctuations \(\chi\) diverges as \(|U|/t\) approaches 1.94 from below at \(T=0\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous zero-temperature study (Inaba, 2010) to finite temperatures and complements studies of one-dimensional systems (Capponi, 2008; Molina, 2009; Azaria, 2009; Kantian, 2009) where the CSF is absent due to strong quantum fluctuations. The results are relevant for experiments on three-component fermionic atoms in optical lattices (Ottenstein, 2008; Huckans, 2009; Nakajima, 2006) and provide a phase diagram that could guide detection of the trionic state and adiabatic cooling via the Pomeranchuk effect (Richardson, 1963; Werner, 2005). The paper notes that spatial fluctuations, neglected here, may lead to a trionic density-wave state at low temperatures near half filling, as suggested by effective hopping and repulsion estimates (Rapp, 2007).
