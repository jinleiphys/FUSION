# Late Hadronization and Matter Formed at RHIC: Vector Manifestation, Brown-Rho Scaling and Hadronic Freedom

> Recent developments in our description of RHIC and related heavy-ion phenomena in terms of hidden local symmetry theories are reviewed with a focus on the novel nearly massless states in the vicinity of -- both below and above -- the chiral restoration temperature T_c. We present complementary and intuitive ways to understand both Harada-Yamawaki's vector manifestation structure and Brown-Rho scaling -- which are closely related -- in terms of "melting" of soft glues observed in lattice calculations and join the massless modes that arise in the vector manifestation (in the chiral limit) just below T_c to tightly bound massless states above T_c. This phenomenon may be interpreted in terms of the Beg-Shei theorem. It is suggested that hidden local symmetry theories arise naturally in holographic dual QCD from string theory, and a clear understanding of what really happens near the critical point could come from a deeper understanding of the dual bulk theory. Other matters discussed are the relation between Brown-Rho scaling and Landau Fermi-liquid fixed point parameters at the equilibrium density, its implications for "low-mass dileptons" produced in heavy-ion collisions, the reconstruction of vector mesons in peripheral collisions, the pion velocity in the vicinity of the chiral transition point, kaon condensation viewed from the VM fixed point, nuclear physics with Brown-Rho scaling, and the generic feature of dropping masses at the RGE fixed points in generalized hidden local symmetry theories.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the vector manifestation (VM) fixed point of hidden local symmetry theory, in which vector meson masses and couplings vanish as the chiral restoration temperature \(T_c\) is approached from below, can be understood intuitively through the "melting" of soft gluons observed in lattice calculations, and that this picture connects continuously to tightly bound massless states above \(T_c\) via the Bég-Shei theorem.

## Method

The authors reinterpret the Harada-Yamawaki hidden local symmetry renormalization group results using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model as an effective theory, backed by lattice gauge simulation data on the gluon condensate as a function of temperature. They estimate the soft glue contribution by integrating the energy difference between massive constituent quarks and massless current quarks up to a cutoff \(\Lambda = 660\) MeV, and compare this to the drop in the gluon condensate \(G^2(T)\) from lattice data. They also discuss the generalized hidden local symmetry (GHLS) framework that incorporates the \(a_1\) meson, identifying three possible fixed point scenarios (GL-type, VM-type, and hybrid-type) characterized by different ratios of \(\rho\) to \(a_1\) masses and different predictions for the \(\gamma\pi\pi\) coupling near chiral restoration.

## Key numbers

* The gluon condensate at \(T=0\) is \(G^2(0) \approx 0.012\) GeV\(^4\), dropping to \(G^2(T_c) \approx 0.006\) GeV\(^4\) at the chiral restoration temperature \(T_c = 170\) MeV, with no further melting between \(T_c\) and \(1.4 T_c\).
* The NJL model parameters giving \(T_c = 170\) MeV are a cutoff \(\Lambda = 660\) MeV and coupling \(G\Lambda^2 = 4.3\).
* The soft glue contribution estimated from constituent quarks of mass \(m_Q = 320\) MeV gives B(soft glue) \(\sim 0.5 \times 0.012\) GeV\(^4\), roughly half the total glue; a constituent quark mass \(m_Q \sim 370\) MeV reproduces the lattice drop of slightly more than half.
* The \(\rho \rightarrow 2\pi\) decay width vanishes as \(\Gamma(\rho\rightarrow 2\pi) \propto (\langle\bar{q}q\rangle_T / \langle\bar{q}q\rangle_0)^2\) just below \(T_c\).
* In the GHLS framework, three fixed points are identified: GL-type with \(M_\rho^2/M_{a_1}^2 \rightarrow 1\); VM-type with \(M_\rho^2/M_{a_1}^2 \rightarrow 0\); and hybrid-type with \(M_\rho^2/M_{a_1}^2 \rightarrow 1/3\), predicting \(g_{\gamma\pi\pi} \rightarrow 0\), \(1/2\), and \(1/3\) respectively.

## Context

This review extends the Brown-Rho scaling proposal (Brown and Rho, 1991) by connecting it to the Harada-Yamawaki vector manifestation fixed point (Harada and Yamawaki, 2001) and to lattice results on the gluon condensate (Miller, 2000). It builds on earlier work by Lee (1991) and Deng showing that about half the glue melts at \(T_c\) in unquenched QCD, and on the NJL model calculations of Bernard, Meissner and Zahed (1987). The paper enables future lattice and experimental tests of whether the VM fixed point, the GL-type fixed point, or the hybrid-type fixed point is realized in nature at high temperature, high density, or with many flavors, and suggests that holographic dual QCD from string theory (Sakai and Sugimoto, 2005) provides a natural framework for understanding the infinite tower of vector mesons and the emergence of hidden local symmetry.
