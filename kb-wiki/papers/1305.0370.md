# Non-Gaussian Fluctuation and Non-Markovian Effect in the Nuclear Fusion Process: Langevin Dynamics Emerging from Quantum Molecular Dynamics Simulations

> Macroscopic parameters as well as precise information on the random force characterizing the Langevin type description of the nuclear fusion process around the Coulomb barrier are extracted from the microscopic dynamics of individual nucleons by exploiting the numerical simulation of the improved quantum molecular dynamics. It turns out that the dissipation dynamics of the relative motion between two fusing nuclei is caused by a non-Gaussian distribution of the random force. We find that the friction coefficient as well as the time correlation function of the random force takes particularly large values in a region a little bit inside of the Coulomb barrier. A clear non-Markovian effect is observed in the time correlation function of the random force. It is further shown that an emergent dynamics of the fusion process can be described by the generalized Langevin equation with memory effects by appropriately incorporating the microscopic information of individual nucleons through the random force and its time correlation function.

## Key claim

The dissipation dynamics of the relative motion between two fusing nuclei is caused by a non-Gaussian distribution of the random force, and a clear non-Markovian effect is observed in the time correlation function of the random force; the authors qualify that the macroscopic dynamics described by a one-dimensional Langevin equation is not appropriate in the region where the non-Gaussian distribution appears, indicating a necessity of introducing a new macroscopic degree of freedom.

## Method

The authors performed improved quantum molecular dynamics (ImQMD) simulations of head-on collisions of 90Zr+90Zr at an incident energy of 195 MeV, with 10,000 collision events. From these microscopic simulations, they extracted the random force, friction coefficient, and time correlation functions of the random force to construct a generalized Langevin equation with memory effects for the relative motion between the two fusing nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The random force at the initial distance R0=30 fm shows a Gaussian distribution with full width at half maximum (FWHM) Γ ≈ 0.1 MeV/fm.
- At R ≈ 18 fm, the random force has a Gaussian distribution with Γ ≈ 0.5 MeV/fm.
- In Region 3 (just inside the barrier top to the fusing phase), the random force distribution has a Gaussian shape with Γ ≈ 15 MeV/fm, almost two orders of magnitude larger than in Region 1.
- The incident energy used in the simulations is E = 195 MeV.
- The effective temperature from the non-Markovian treatment (T_non-Markov^sym) shows consistent features with √(E_intr/a) in Region 3, while the Markovian effective temperature (T_Markov^sym) is smaller by an order of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to extract macroscopic transport parameters from microscopic nuclear dynamics, including time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations by Washiyama (2009) and Ayik (2009), and earlier improved quantum molecular dynamics studies by Wang (2002, 2004). The paper identifies for the first time non-Gaussian fluctuations and non-Markovian effects in the nuclear fusion process around the Coulomb barrier, and shows that a generalized Langevin equation with memory effects can describe the emergent macroscopic dynamics. The results enable future studies of the dependence of these effects on incident energy, impact parameter, and reaction system, and raise the open question of whether the new macroscopic degree of freedom indicated by the non-Gaussian fluctuations is related to neck formation.
