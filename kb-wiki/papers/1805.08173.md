# Backward single-pion production in the $\boldsymbol{pd\rightarrow {}^3\textrm{He}\,π^0}$ reaction with WASA-at-COSY

> New data on the production of single neutral pions in the $pd\rightarrow{}^3\textrm{He}\,π^0$ reaction are presented. For fifteen proton beam momenta between $p_p=1.60\;\textrm{GeV}/c$ and $p_p=1.74\;\textrm{GeV}/c$, differential cross sections are determined over a large fraction of the backward hemisphere. Since the only previous systematic measurements of single-pion production at these energies were made in collinear kinematics, the present work constitutes a significant extension of the current knowledge on this reaction. Even this far above the production threshold, significant changes are found in the behaviour of the angular distributions over small intervals in beam momentum.

## Key claim

The paper presents new differential cross sections for the $pd\rightarrow {}^3\textrm{He}\,\pi^0$ reaction over a large fraction of the backward hemisphere at fifteen proton beam momenta between 1.60 and 1.74 GeV/$c$, significantly extending the previous database which only contained systematic measurements in collinear kinematics. The authors note that even far above the production threshold, significant changes occur in the angular distributions over small intervals in beam momentum, but they hedge that the interpretation of these structures (particularly their coincidence with the $\eta$ production threshold) requires further theoretical work.

## Method

The experiment was performed at the WASA facility at COSY, where proton beams collided with frozen deuterium pellets. The $^3$He ejectiles emitted near the forward direction were identified and reconstructed using the WASA Forward Detector, which measures energy loss, polar and azimuthal angles. A missing-mass analysis was performed on the $^3$He four-momenta to extract the angular distributions of single-pion production, with background from two-pion production and nuclear breakup of $^3$He subtracted using a fit to the final state momentum spectra. The acceptance and reconstruction efficiency were determined iteratively using Monte Carlo simulations weighted by polynomial fits to the angular distributions.

## Key numbers

- Fifteen proton beam momenta between $p_p = 1.60$ GeV/$c$ and $p_p = 1.74$ GeV/$c$ were used, with a momentum resolution of $\Delta p/p \approx 10^{-3}$.
- The excess energy for $\pi^0$ production ranges from $Q_{\pi^0} = 426$ MeV to $Q_{\pi^0} = 494$ MeV.
- The polar scattering angle resolution is $\Delta\vartheta \approx 0.2^\circ$.
- The angle-dependent acceptance $A(\cos\vartheta_{\pi^0}^*)$ is on average about 40%.
- The minimum in the differential cross section moves from $\cos\vartheta_{\pi^0}^* \approx -1.1$ at $p_p = 1.62$ GeV/$c$ to $\cos\vartheta_{\pi^0}^* \approx -0.93$ at $p_p = 1.74$ GeV/$c$.
- The maximum in the angular distribution remains near $\cos\vartheta_{\pi^0}^* \approx -0.61$ across all beam momenta.
- The statistical uncertainty of the luminosity determination is about 2%, with an additional systematic normalisation uncertainty of approximately 16%.

## Context

This work extends the only previous systematic measurements of single-pion production in $pd$ fusion at these energies, which were made in collinear kinematics by Berthet (1985) and Kerboul (1986) at SATURNE using the SPES4 spectrometer. The new angular distributions enable future experiments to normalise cross sections for other meson production reactions (such as $\eta$ and $\pi^0\pi^0$ production) without relying on extrapolation to collinear kinematics, as was previously done by Adlarson (2014). The observed structures near the $\eta$ production threshold may be related to the anomalously strong $pd\rightarrow {}^3\textrm{He}\,\eta$ amplitude near threshold, possibly indicating a quasibound $^3_\eta$He state as suggested by Wilkin (1993), but the authors note that a two-step mechanism via $\eta$ production followed by $\eta{}^3\textrm{He}\rightarrow {}^3\textrm{He}\,\pi^0$ appears too small to explain the backward minima based on data from Peng (1989).
