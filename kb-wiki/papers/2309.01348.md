# $ΛΛ$ Interaction in a Nuclear Density Functional Theory and Hyperon Puzzle of the Neutron Star

> A Skyrme-type effective potential is determined to describe the interaction between $Λ$ hyperons in nuclear medium. Experimental data of the binding energies of the double-$Λ$ ($ΛΛ$) nuclei with mass numbers $A=10$--$13$ are used to fit the parameters of the $ΛΛ$ interaction. As a result of the fitting, we obtain eight different sets of the $ΛΛ$ interaction parameters, which reproduces the input data within 5\% deviation from the experimental data on average. The eight $ΛΛ$ interactions are plugged in the calculation of the heavier $ΛΛ$ nuclei and the neutron star equation of state to explore the issue of hyperon puzzle. We found that the $ΛΛ$ interaction, specifically, p-wave interaction makes the equation of state stiff enough that the maximum mass of the neutron star can be as large as, or above $2\;M_\odot$.

## Key claim

The authors claim that including a p-wave component in the Lambda-Lambda interaction, fitted to binding energies of double-Lambda hypernuclei with mass numbers A=10-13, can stiffen the neutron star equation of state enough to support maximum masses at or above 2 solar masses, potentially resolving the hyperon puzzle. However, this conclusion is hedged by the fact that only four of the eight fitted parameter sets produce maximum masses above 2 solar masses, and two sets give maximum masses slightly below this threshold.

## Method

The authors used a Skyrme-type effective potential within the KIDS (Korea-IBS-Daegu-SKKU) density functional framework, fitting four parameters (a0, a1, a2, a3) of the Lambda-Lambda interaction to experimental binding energies of five double-Lambda hypernuclei: 10_LambdaLambda_Be, 11_LambdaLambda_Be, 12_LambdaLambda_Be, 12_LambdaLambda_B, and 13_LambdaLambda_B. They performed separate fits for two underlying nuclear models (KIDS0 and KIDS-D) that give different Lambda hyperon threshold densities in neutron star matter, and selected eight parameter sets (four per model) that reproduce the input data within 5% mean deviation. These interactions were then used to compute the neutron star equation of state under beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were integrated to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- The eight fitted parameter sets reproduce experimental binding energies with mean deviations ranging from 2.857% to 4.509% across the five double-Lambda hypernuclei.
- For the KIDS0-LL2 model, the maximum neutron star mass reaches 2.0 solar masses; for KIDS-D-LL2, the maximum mass is 1.95 solar masses.
- The threshold density for Lambda creation is 0.7 fm^-3 in the KIDS0 model and 0.5 fm^-3 in the KIDS-D model.
- For the LL2 parameter sets, the Lambda fraction exceeds 30% in the neutron star core, while for LL1 and LL4 sets, the Lambda fraction does not exceed 10%.
- Binding energy differences between LL1 and LL4 interactions in the KIDS-D model are 5, 8, 7, and 4 MeV for 18_LambdaLambda_O, 42_LambdaLambda_Ca, 92_LambdaLambda_Zr, and 210_LambdaLambda_Pb, respectively.
- The KIDS0 and KIDS-D models (nucleons only) satisfy the NICER constraint R_1.4 = 11.8-13.1 km for the radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star.

## Context

This work extends the KIDS density functional framework, previously applied to single-Lambda hypernuclei by the same group (Choi et al., 2023), to include Lambda-Lambda interactions. It addresses the hyperon puzzle, where previous calculations showed that Lambda hyperon creation softens the equation of state too much to support 2 solar mass neutron stars (Demorest et al., 2010; Antoniadis et al., 2013). Earlier Skyrme-type Lambda-Lambda interactions (Lanskoy and Yamamoto, 1998; Minami et al., 2011) could only determine two parameters due to limited data, but the availability of new double-Lambda hypernuclei measurements now allows determination of all four parameters including p-wave and density-dependent terms. The results enable predictions for heavier double-Lambda nuclei such as 42_LambdaLambda_Ca and 92_LambdaLambda_Zr, which could be tested in future experiments to constrain many-body Lambda-Lambda interactions.
