# Photon radiation by relatively slowly rotating fermions in magnetic field

> We study the electromagnetic radiation by a fermion carrying an electric charge $q$ embedded in a medium rotating with constant angular velocity $\bfΩ$ parallel or anti-parallel to an external constant magnetic field $\bf B$. We assume that the rotation is "relatively slow"; namely, that the angular velocity $Ω$ is much smaller than the inverse magnetic length $\sqrt{qB}$. In practice, such angular velocity can be extremely high. The fermion motion is a superposition of two circular motions: one due to its rigid rotation caused by forces exerted by the medium, another due to the external magnetic field. We derive an exact analytical expression for the spectral rate and the total intensity of this type of synchrotron radiation. Our numerical calculations indicate very high sensitivity of the radiation to the angular velocity of rotation. We show that the radiation intensity is strongly enhanced if $q\bf B$ and $\bf Ω$ point in the opposite directions and is suppressed otherwise.

## Key claim

The paper derives an exact analytical expression for synchrotron radiation from a charged fermion in a rotating medium, but only under the condition that the angular velocity Ω is much smaller than the inverse magnetic length √(qB); this "relatively slow" rotation allows the authors to ignore boundary conditions at the causal radius r = 1/Ω, which would otherwise dominate the dynamics.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation in a rotating reference frame with a constant magnetic field along the rotation axis, using cylindrical coordinates and the symmetric gauge. They quantize the fermion field with eigenstates of total angular momentum and either helicity or spin magnetic moment, then compute the photon emission rate using the Chandrasekhar-Kendall basis for the photon field. The radiation intensity is obtained from the transition amplitude between fermion states, integrated over final photon and fermion quantum numbers.

## Key numbers

- The condition for "relatively slow" rotation is Ω ≪ √(qB); for the quark-gluon plasma at typical collision energies, √(qB) ~ 10^13–10^17 eV^2 and Ω ~ 10^9 eV, placing the system in Region II where this approximation holds.
- The classical synchrotron radiation intensity in the high-energy limit is W_cl = (q^2/4π) × 2(qB)^2 E^2 / (3M^4) (cgs units, Eq. d35).
- For a classical particle with qB = -1, L = 1, M = 1, Ω = ±10^(-5), the ratio ⟨W⟩/W_cl diverges near E ~ 4 when qB and Ω are anti-parallel, indicating breakdown of the classical model due to radiation reaction.
- The synchrotron frequency for a non-relativistic proton in a 1 Gs magnetic field is ω_B(P) ~ 10^4 s^(-1); a dental drill rotates at ~13 kHz ≈ 10^4 s^(-1), comparable to this frequency.

## Context

This work extends the classical and quantum theory of synchrotron radiation (Schott 1912; Sokolov and Ternov) to systems that rotate as a whole, such as the quark-gluon plasma produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where both intense magnetic fields (Kharzeev 2007; Skokov 2009) and large vorticity (Csernai 2013; Becattini 2015) are present. The authors previously published a letter outlining the method (Buzzegoli 2022), and this paper provides the full derivation and new numerical results. The results enable calculation of synchrotron radiation from rotating astrophysical objects and from the quark-gluon plasma, where the radiation may serve as a diagnostic tool.
