# Relation Between Chiral Susceptibility and Solutions of Gap Equation in Nambu--Jona-Lasinio Model

> We study the solutions of the gap equation, the thermodynamic potential and the chiral susceptibility in and beyond the chiral limit at finite chemical potential in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. We give an explicit relation between the chiral susceptibility and the thermodynamic potential in the NJL model. We find that the chiral susceptibility is a quantity being able to represent the furcation of the solutions of the gap equation and the concavo-convexity of the thermodynamic potential in NJL model. It indicates that the chiral susceptibility can identify the stable state and the possibility of the chiral phase transition in NJL model.

## Key claim

The chiral susceptibility can identify the furcation points of the gap equation solutions and the concavo-convexity changes of the thermodynamic potential in the NJL model, but its singularities do not correspond to the critical chemical potential for the chiral phase transition itself; rather they mark where possible states appear or disappear.

## Method

The authors solved the NJL model gap equation at zero temperature and finite chemical potential under the Hartree (random phase) approximation, both in the chiral limit (m=0) and with a finite current quark mass (m=5.6 MeV). They computed the chiral susceptibility as the derivative of the constituent quark mass with respect to the current quark mass, and compared its singularities with the furcation points of the gap equation solutions and the concavo-convexity of the thermodynamic potential.

## Key numbers

- At zero chemical potential in the chiral limit, the gap equation has three solutions: M = ±387.92 MeV (Nambu solutions) and M = 0 MeV (Wigner solution).
- In the chiral limit, a trifurcation of the Wigner solution occurs at μ = 335.59 MeV.
- In the chiral limit, the constant Nambu solutions exist only for μ < 387.92 MeV; at μ = 397.23 MeV the two positive solutions coalesce (bifurcation).
- Beyond the chiral limit (m = 5.6 MeV), at μ = 0 MeV the three solutions are M = 399.44 MeV, -11.64 MeV, and -375.89 MeV.
- Beyond the chiral limit, a bifurcation appears at μ = 361.08 MeV (M = 112.39 MeV), and the negative solutions coalesce at (μ, M) = (385.20, -355.37) MeV.
- The critical chemical potential for the chiral phase transition in the chiral limit is μ = 368.00 MeV (where the three minima of the thermodynamic potential have equal depth), and beyond the chiral limit it is μ = 382 MeV.
- The interaction parameters used are Λ = 587.90 MeV and GΛ² = 2.44.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of chiral susceptibility from the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach (Zhao et al., 2006) to the NJL model, providing an explicit analytical relation χ = 1/(G ∂²Ω/∂M²) that was not previously given. The results show that while chiral susceptibility singularities mark furcation points of the gap equation, they do not directly give the phase transition critical chemical potential, which must be determined from the thermodynamic potential. This enables using chiral susceptibility as a diagnostic tool in more complex frameworks like Dyson-Schwinger equations where the thermodynamic potential is difficult to compute explicitly.
