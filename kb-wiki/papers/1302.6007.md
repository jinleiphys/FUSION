# Anti-analog giant dipole resonances and the neutron skin of nuclei

> We examine a method to determine the neutron-skin thickness of nuclei using data on the charge-exchange anti-analog giant dipole resonance (AGDR). Calculations performed using the relativistic proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (RQRPA) reproduce the isotopic trend of the excitation energies of the AGDR, as well as that of the spin-flip giant dipole resonances (IVSGDR), in comparison to available data for the even-even isotopes 112-124Sn. It is shown that the excitation energies of the AGDR, obtained using a set of density-dependent effective interactions which span a range of the symmetry energy at saturation density, supplemented with the experimental values, provide a stringent constraint on the value of the neutron-skin thickness. For 124Sn, in particular, we determine the value DRpn=0.21(5) fm. The result of the present study shows that a measurement of the excitation energy of the AGDR in (p,n) reactions using rare-isotope beams in inverse kinematics, provides a valuable method for the determination of the neutron-skin thickness in exotic nuclei.

## Key claim

The excitation energy of the anti-analog giant dipole resonance (AGDR), measured relative to the isobaric analog state (IAS) in charge-exchange (p,n) reactions, provides a stringent constraint on the neutron-skin thickness of nuclei, but the method requires correcting for the energy shift caused by mixing with the isovector spin giant dipole resonance (IVSGDR) at the bombarding energy used.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) plus proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pn-RQRPA) calculations for even-even 112-124Sn isotopes, using a family of density-dependent meson-exchange effective interactions with systematically varied symmetry energy at saturation (30, 32, 34, 36, and 38 MeV, plus DD-ME2 at 32.3 MeV). They computed the centroid energies of the AGDR and IVSGDR relative to the IAS and the ground-state neutron-skin thickness for each interaction. For 124Sn, they corrected the experimental AGDR energy from Sterrenburg et al. (1980) by 0.33 MeV to compensate for IVSGDR mixing, then compared the theoretical AGDR energy versus neutron-skin thickness curve to the corrected experimental value to extract the neutron-skin thickness.

## Key numbers

- For 124Sn, the neutron-skin thickness determined from the AGDR method is ΔRpn = 0.21 ± 0.05 fm.
- The experimental AGDR energy relative to IAS for 124Sn from Sterrenburg et al. (1980) is 10.60 ± 0.20 MeV, corrected to 10.93 ± 0.20 MeV after accounting for a 0.33 MeV shift from IVSGDR mixing.
- The theoretical AGDR energies were calculated using effective interactions with symmetry energy at saturation a4 = 30, 32, 34, 36, and 38 MeV, plus DD-ME2 (a4 = 32.3 MeV).
- The DD-ME2 interaction predicts the electric dipole polarizability of 208Pb as αD = 20.8 fm3, compared to the measured value of (20.1 ± 0.6) fm3.
- The IVSGDR width for 124Sn is 9 MeV and the AGDR width is 3.6 MeV, with an energy difference Δ = 2.3 MeV between the two resonances.

## Context

This work extends the approach of using giant resonances to determine neutron-skin thickness, previously applied to the IVSGDR by Krasznahorkay et al. (2004) and the Gamow-Teller resonance by Vretenar et al. (2003), and introduces a self-consistent microscopic framework as an alternative to sum-rule-based analyses. The method is motivated by the schematic model of Krmpotic (1983) showing sensitivity of AGDR energy to neutron-skin thickness, and the results agree with prior determinations from antiproton absorption by Trzcinska et al. (2001), proton scattering by Terashima et al. (2008), and pygmy dipole resonance studies by Klimkiewicz et al. (2007). The successful test on stable 124Sn enables future application to exotic neutron-rich nuclei using rare-isotope beams in inverse kinematics, supported by recent advances in neutron time-of-flight spectrometers.
