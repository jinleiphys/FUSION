# Pion distribution amplitude from lattice-QCD

> A method is explained through which a pointwise accurate approximation to the pion's valence-quark distribution amplitude (PDA) may be obtained from a limited number of moments. In connection with the single nontrivial moment accessible in contemporary simulations of lattice-regularised quantum chromodynamics (QCD), the method yields a PDA that is a broad concave function whose pointwise form agrees with that predicted by Dyson-Schwinger equation analyses of the pion. Under leading-order evolution, the PDA remains broad to energy scales in excess of 100 GeV, a feature which signals persistence of the influence of dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. Consequently, the asymptotic distribution, ϕ_π^asy(x), is a poor approximation to the pion's PDA at all such scales that are either currently accessible or foreseeable in experiments on pion elastic and transition form factors. Thus, related expectations based on ϕ_π^asy(x) should be revised.

## Key claim

A pointwise accurate approximation to the pion's valence-quark distribution amplitude (PDA) can be obtained from a single nontrivial lattice-QCD moment, provided the reconstruction uses a functional form that respects the expected concave, single-peaked shape at x=1/2, rather than the conventional Gegenbauer expansion of order α=3/2 which forces a double-humped shape from limited data.

## Method

The authors reconstruct the pion PDA from the single lattice-QCD moment ⟨(2x-1)²⟩ = 0.27±0.04 at ζ=2 GeV (Braun 2006) by fitting a one-parameter Ansatz φ_π(x) = N_α x^{α-1/2}(1-x)^{α-1/2}, where α is determined by the moment. This Ansatz acknowledges that the PDA should be a symmetric concave function peaking at x=1/2, consistent with the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude peaking at zero relative momentum. The resulting PDA is then reprojected onto the standard Gegenbauer-α=3/2 basis to obtain all higher conformal moments, enabling leading-order evolution to any scale.

## Key numbers

- Lattice-QCD moment at ζ=2 GeV: ⟨(2x-1)²⟩ = 0.27 ± 0.04 (Braun 2006)
- Extracted exponent from lattice moment: α_- = 0.35^{+0.32}_{-0.24} (range 0.11 to 0.67) at ζ=2 GeV
- Gegenbauer coefficient a₂^{3/2}(τ₂) = 0.20 ± 0.12 at ζ=2 GeV
- Gegenbauer coefficient a₄^{3/2}(τ₂) = 0.093 ± 0.064 at ζ=2 GeV
- Gegenbauer coefficient a₆^{3/2}(τ₂) = 0.055 ± 0.041 at ζ=2 GeV
- PDA midpoint value φ_π(x=1/2; τ₂) = 1.20^{+0.16}_{-0.13}
- After leading-order evolution to ζ=10 GeV: α_- = 0.51^{+0.25}_{-0.20} (range 0.31 to 0.76)
- a₂^{3/2} falls to less than 10% of its ζ=2 GeV value only for ζ ≳ 100 GeV

## Context

This work reconciles the lattice-QCD result of Braun (2006) with the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) computation of Chang (2013), showing both approaches yield a broad concave PDA at ζ=2 GeV rather than the double-humped distribution previously inferred from the same lattice moment. The method enables extraction of all higher Gegenbauer coefficients from a single moment, allowing evolution calculations that demonstrate the asymptotic form φ_π^{asy}(x)=6x(1-x) remains a poor approximation even at scales above 100 GeV. This contradicts the assumptions underlying the experimental analysis of Aitala (2000) and implies that leading-order, leading-twist predictions using the asymptotic PDA are misleading for interpreting contemporary pion form factor experiments.
