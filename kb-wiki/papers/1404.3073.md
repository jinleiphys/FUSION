# A model study of the chiral phase diagram of QCD

> In this paper we study the chiral phase transition of QCD at finite temperature and density by using the rank-2 confining separable gluon propagator model in the framework of Dyson-Schwinger Equations. The critical end point is located at (T_CEP, μ_CEP) = (69 MeV, 270.3 MeV). It is also found that the first order phase transition might not end at one point, but experiences a two-phase coexisting meta-stable state. A comparison with the results in the previous literature is given.

## Key claim

Using a rank-2 confining separable gluon propagator model within the Dyson-Schwinger Equations framework, the authors locate the critical end point (CEP) at (T_CEP, mu_CEP) = (69 MeV, 270.3 MeV) and find that the first-order phase transition may not end at a single point but instead exhibits a two-phase coexisting meta-stable state, a result that is strongly model-dependent and yields a ratio T_CEP/mu_CEP = 0.255, much smaller than the ratio of approximately 1 given by lattice QCD and more sophisticated DSE models.

## Method

The authors solve the quark Dyson-Schwinger gap equation in the rainbow approximation using a rank-2 confining separable gluon propagator model extended to finite temperature and finite chemical potential, with the gluon propagator form kept unchanged from the zero-chemical-potential case. They compute the chiral susceptibility defined as the derivative of the B-function at zero momentum and lowest Matsubara frequency with respect to current quark mass, and also calculate the bag constant via the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) approximation to determine the stable phase when Nambu and Wigner solutions coexist. The model parameters are fixed from previous work: Lambda_0 = 0.638 GeV, Lambda_1/Lambda_0 = 1.21, D_0*Lambda_0^2 = 260.0, D_1*Lambda_1^4 = 130.0, and degenerate light quark mass m = 5.3 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Critical end point located at (T_CEP, mu_CEP) = (69 MeV, 270.3 MeV).
- Ratio T_CEP/mu_CEP = 0.255.
- For T = 37 MeV, the Nambu solution endpoint is at mu_N = 326.6 MeV and the Wigner solution endpoint is at mu_W = 285.2 MeV, with the first-order transition occurring at mu_c = 320.4 MeV based on the bag constant.
- At zero chemical potential, the chiral susceptibility peaks at T = 136 MeV, indicating a crossover transition.
- For T = 80 MeV, the peak positions of the two chiral susceptibility definitions are at mu = 258.4 MeV (lowest-frequency definition) and mu = 258.5 MeV (full-frequency definition).

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger Equation approach to the QCD phase diagram using a rank-2 confining separable gluon propagator model, following earlier studies by Qin et al. (2011) and Fischer et al. (2011) who also reported two-phase coexisting domains. The model yields a CEP at a much lower T/mu ratio than lattice QCD results by Fodor and Katz (2002) and Gavai and Gupta (2005), as well as NJL model calculations by Fu et al. (2008) and Costa et al. (2008), highlighting the strong model dependence of such predictions. The results enable further investigation of meta-stable states and phase coexistence in the QCD phase diagram using simplified but analytically tractable models.
