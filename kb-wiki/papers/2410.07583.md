# Relativistic spin hydrodynamics revisited with general rotation by entropy-current analysis

> We revisit the canonical formulation of spin hydrodynamics for Dirac fermions with a general thermal vorticity. The orders of the general thermal vorticity and the corresponding spin variables are considered independently from those of the conventional hydrodynamic variables and their perturbative gradients. Assuming a totally antisymmetric spin current of Dirac fermions, the entropy-current analysis with a general spin potential indicates that the constitutive relations of the stress-energy tensor have to involve spin variables, particularly those linked to boost symmetry, to adhere to the entropy principle. In the presence of the degree of freedom associated with boost symmetry, we choose the constitutive relations of the canonical formulation to be connected to those of the phenomenological formulation through pseudogauge transformation. Subsequently, a linear-mode analysis is conducted using the resulting spin hydrodynamic equations. It is observed that the spin and hydrodynamic modes in this canonical formulation display different characteristics compared to those in the phenomenological formulation up to the second order of gradient.

## Key claim

The authors show that in relativistic spin hydrodynamics for Dirac fermions with a general thermal vorticity (where the spin potential is not required to be orthogonal to the fluid four-velocity), the entropy principle cannot be satisfied unless the stress-energy tensor's constitutive relations include spin variables linked to boost symmetry, contrary to earlier canonical formulations that omitted these boost degrees of freedom.

## Method

The authors perform an entropy-current analysis on the canonical formulation of spin hydrodynamics for Dirac fermions, assuming a totally antisymmetric spin current. They treat the orders of thermal vorticity and spin variables independently from conventional hydrodynamic gradients, allowing application across a broad range of vorticity intensities. The constitutive relations of the canonical formulation are then connected to those of the phenomenological formulation through a pseudogauge transformation, and a linear-mode analysis is conducted on the resulting spin hydrodynamic equations.

## Key numbers

- The spin density $\mathcal{R}^{\mu\nu} \equiv -u_\alpha S^{\alpha\mu\nu}$ contains exactly three independent components associated with spatial rotation symmetry, while the remaining three components linked to boost symmetry are absent in the totally antisymmetric spin tensor $S^{\mu\nu\alpha}$.
- The spin tensor decomposition yields $\mathcal{S}^{\mu\nu} = \epsilon^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} \mathcal{R}_\rho u_\sigma + 2u^{[\mu}\mathcal{B}^{\nu]}$, where $\mathcal{R}_\rho$ captures all three independent components of the rotation part and $\mathcal{B}^\mu$ contains the three independent components related to boost symmetry.
- In the power counting scheme, $\epsilon \sim p \sim \beta \sim u^\mu \sim O(\partial^0)$, $\mathcal{S}^{\mu\nu} \sim O(\delta)$, and $\omega_{\mu\nu} \sim \varpi_{\mu\nu} \sim O(\varpi)$, with $\varpi_{\mu\nu} - \beta\omega_{\mu\nu} \sim O(\partial)$, where $O(\delta)$ could be $O(\hbar\varpi)$ if spin susceptibility is $O(\hbar\partial^0)$.

## Context

This work extends the entropy-current analysis approach introduced by Hattori et al. (2019) for the phenomenological formulation of spin hydrodynamics, and the canonical formulations by Hongo et al. (2021) and Cao et al. (2022) which omitted boost symmetry degrees of freedom. It builds upon the canonical formulation by Daher et al. (2022) that incorporates boost variables via pseudogauge transformation. The paper enables a consistent treatment of spin hydrodynamics across a broad range of thermal vorticity intensities, which is relevant for understanding the "sign problem" in local spin polarization observed in heavy-ion collisions at STAR (2017, 2018, 2020) and ALICE (2019, 2022).
