# Wilson Fermion Determinant in Lattice QCD

> We present a formula for reducing the rank of Wilson fermions from $4 N_c N_x N_y N_z N_t$ to $4 N_c N_x N_y N_z$ keeping the value of its determinant. We analyse eigenvalues of a reduced matrix and coefficients $C_n$ in the fugacity expansion of the fermion determinant $\sum_n C_n (\exp(μ/T))^n$, which play an important role in the canonical formulation, using lattice QCD configurations on a $4^4$ lattice. Numerically, $\log |C_n|$ varies as $N_x N_y N_z$, and goes easily over the standard numerical range; We give a simple cure for that. The phase of $C_n$ correlates with the distribution of the Polyakov loop in the complex plain. These results lay the groundwork for future finite density calculations in lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The paper presents a reduction formula that compresses the Wilson fermion determinant from rank \(4 N_c N_x N_y N_z N_t\) to rank \(4 N_c N_x N_y N_z\) while preserving the exact determinant value, but the numerical demonstration is limited to a small \(4^4\) lattice and the authors caution that the results "are not to be regarded as physical, due to the small lattice size."

## Method

The authors express the Wilson fermion matrix in a time-plane block matrix form and use projection operators \(r_\pm = (r \pm \gamma_4)/2\) to construct a permutation matrix \(P = c_a r_- + c_b r_+ V z^{-1}\). Multiplying the fermion matrix by \(P\) allows the determinant to be evaluated analytically in the time direction, reducing the problem to computing the determinant of a reduced matrix \(Q = (\alpha_1^{-1} \beta_1) \cdots (\alpha_{N_t}^{-1} \beta_{N_t})\) of rank \(N_{\rm red} = 4 N_c N_x N_y N_z\). The eigenvalues of \(Q\) are then used to express the full fermion determinant as a fugacity expansion \(\det \Delta(\mu) = \sum_n C_n (e^{\mu/T})^n\).

## Key numbers

- The rank reduction is from \(4 N_c N_x N_y N_z N_t\) to \(4 N_c N_x N_y N_z\), making the determinant computation \(1/N_t^3\) less time-consuming.
- On a \(4^4\) lattice, \(\log |c_n|\) reaches values up to \(10^{900}\), far exceeding the double-precision range of about \(10^{-308}\) to \(10^{308}\).
- For \(\beta = 1.85\), the zero-chemical-potential determinant values on four configurations are \(3.0957 \times 10^{-19}\), \(2.0921 \times 10^{-21}\), \(2.2560 \times 10^{-21}\), and \(5.1115 \times 10^{-18}\).
- For \(\beta = 2.0\), the zero-chemical-potential determinant values on four configurations are \(8.7586 \times 10^{-12}\), \(3.0329 \times 10^{-12}\), \(1.1159 \times 10^{-12}\), and \(1.2578 \times 10^{-12}\).
- The eigenvalues of the reduced matrix split into two groups: approximately half with \(|\lambda| \gtrsim 5\) and half with \(|\lambda| \lesssim 0.5\), with a gap containing no eigenvalues.

## Context

This work extends the reduction method previously developed for staggered fermions by Gibbs (1986) and Hasenfratz and Toussaint (1992) to Wilson fermions, which are free from the fourth-root problem that plagues staggered fermion simulations. The method builds on an approach by Borici (2004) tested only in a Schwinger model, and provides a practical tool for finite-density lattice QCD calculations using the canonical formulation, reweighting method, or density-of-state method. The results enable future realistic finite-density simulations with Wilson fermions on larger lattices, though the present demonstration is restricted to a \(4^4\) lattice.
