# Isoscalar and isovector neutron-proton pairing

> Neutron-proton (np-) pairing is expected to play an important role in the N Z nuclei. In general, it can have isovector and isoscalar character. The existence of isovector np-pairing is well established. On the contrary, it is still debated whether there is an isoscalar np-pairing. The review of the situation with these two types of pairing with special emphasis on the isoscalar one is presented. It is concluded that there are no substantial evidences for the existence of isoscalar np-pairing.

## Key claim

The paper concludes that there are no substantial evidences for the existence of isoscalar neutron-proton pairing, while isovector neutron-proton pairing is well established; however, the possibility of isoscalar pairing cannot be completely ruled out due to limitations of existing theoretical tools.

## Method

The paper is a review that systematically compares experimental data from non-rotating and rotating N≈Z nuclei with theoretical calculations from mean-field (MF) and density functional theory (DFT) frameworks, including cranked Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB), cranked Relativistic Mean Field (CRMF), and cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky (CNS) approaches. The analysis examines physical observables such as binding energies, moments of inertia, band crossing frequencies, deformation properties, configuration mixing, and terminating states to search for signatures of isoscalar neutron-proton pair condensate.

## Key numbers

- The isovector pairing gap is around 1.8 MeV (from Fig. 4 in Ref. MFC.00), while calculated isoscalar pairing gap in finite nuclei with Z=N=35 is of the order of 3 MeV (from Ref. GSMLSS.01).
- The scaling factor x0^{t=0} = G_np^{t=0}/G_np^{t=1} required to fit experimental Wigner energies is approximately 1.13 and 1.30 for BCS and BCSLN models in the fp shell, and approximately 1.25 in the BCSLN model in the A~76 mass region.
- In symmetric nuclear matter, the isoscalar pairing gap from realistic forces is 3 times larger than the isovector one (from Ref. GSMLSS.01).
- No systematic underestimate of moments of inertia (which could indicate t=0 np-pair field) was identified in N≈Z nuclei above band crossings, where unpaired CRMF calculations reproduce experimental data.

## Context

This review extends the debate on neutron-proton pairing in N≈Z nuclei, following earlier work by Goodman (2001), Satula and Wyss (1997, 2000), and Frauendorf and Sheikh (1999). It contrasts with claims that isoscalar pairing might explain Wigner energies (Satula and Wyss, 2000) and instead supports alternative explanations by Neergard (2002, 2003) and Bentley and Frauendorf (2012) that do not require isoscalar pairing. The paper enables future work by identifying that systematic comparison between isovector mean-field theory and experiment shows no need for isoscalar pairing, while noting that microscopic theories give no clear guidance on the strength to use for isoscalar pairing in MF/DFT models.
