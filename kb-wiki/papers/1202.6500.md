# Neutron star equilibrium configurations within a fully relativistic theory with strong, weak, electromagnetic, and gravitational interactions

> We formulate the equations of equilibrium of neutron stars taking into account strong, weak, electromagnetic, and gravitational interactions within the framework of general relativity. The nuclear interactions are described by the exchange of the sigma, omega, and rho virtual mesons. The equilibrium conditions are given by our recently developed theoretical framework based on the Einstein-Maxwell-Thomas-Fermi equations along with the constancy of the general relativistic Fermi energies of particles, the "Klein potentials", throughout the configuration. The equations are solved numerically in the case of zero temperatures and for selected parametrization of the nuclear models. The solutions lead to a new structure of the star: a positively charged core at supranuclear densities surrounded by an electronic distribution of thickness $\sim \hbar/(m_e c)$ of opposite charge, as well as a neutral crust at lower densities. Inside the core there is a Coulomb potential well of depth $\sim m_πc^2/e$. The constancy of the Klein potentials in the transition from the core to the crust, impose the presence of an overcritical electric field $\sim (m_π/m_e)^2 E_c$, the critical field being $E_c=m^2_e c^3/(e \hbar)$. The electron chemical potential and the density decrease, in the boundary interface, until values $μ^{\rm crust}_e < μ^{\rm core}_e$ and $ρ_{\rm crust}<ρ_{\rm core}$. For each central density, an entire family of core-crust interface boundaries and, correspondingly, an entire family of crusts with different mass and thickness, exist. The configuration with $ρ_{\rm crust}=ρ_{\rm drip}\sim 4.3\times 10^{11}$ g/cm$^3$ separates neutron stars with and without inner crust. We present here the novel neutron star mass-radius for the case $ρ_{\rm crust}=ρ_{\rm drip}$ and compare and contrast it with the one obtained from the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff treatment.

## Key claim

The paper claims that neutron star equilibrium configurations, when consistently treated with the Einstein-Maxwell-Thomas-Fermi equations and the constancy of general relativistic Fermi energies (Klein potentials), necessarily develop a positively charged core surrounded by an electron layer of thickness approximately the electron Compton wavelength, an overcritical electric field, and a Coulomb potential well, contradicting the standard assumption of local charge neutrality used in the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) treatment. The hedge is that this new structure is demonstrated for zero temperature and for selected nuclear model parametrizations, and the results are presented specifically for the case where the crust density equals the neutron drip density.

## Method

The authors formulated and solved numerically the full set of Einstein-Maxwell-Thomas-Fermi equations for a non-rotating, spherically symmetric neutron star composed of neutrons, protons, and electrons in beta-equilibrium, with strong interactions modeled by the exchange of sigma, omega, and rho virtual mesons in the mean-field approximation. The core equations were integrated from a given central density with regularity conditions at the origin, then matched through a core-crust transition layer (solved in three subregions with different characteristic length scales) to a neutral crust described either by a uniform approximation or the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland (BPS) equation of state, enforcing the constancy of the Klein potentials throughout the entire configuration.

## Key numbers

*   The electron distribution surrounding the positively charged core has a thickness of approximately the electron Compton wavelength, given as \(\sim \hbar/(m_e c) \sim 10^2 \hbar/(m_\pi c)\).
*   Inside the core, there is a Coulomb potential well of depth \(\sim m_\pi c^2/e\).
*   An overcritical electric field is present, of magnitude \(\sim (m_\pi/m_e)^2 E_c\), where the critical field is \(E_c = m_e^2 c^3/(e \hbar)\).
*   The configuration with \(\rho_{\rm crust} = \rho_{\rm drip} \sim 4.3 \times 10^{11}\) g/cm\(^3\) separates neutron stars with and without an inner crust.
*   Nuclear density is given as \(\rho_{\rm nuc} \sim 2.7 \times 10^{14}\) g/cm\(^3\).
*   The core-crust transition layer involves two scale lengths: the nuclear interaction scale \(\sim \lambda_\pi = \hbar/(m_\pi c) \sim 1.5\) fm and the electron screening scale \(\sim \lambda_e = \hbar/(m_e c) \sim 100\) fm.
*   The sigma-meson Compton wavelength is \(\lambda_\sigma = \hbar/(m_\sigma c) \sim 0.4\) fm for the NL3 model.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous theoretical framework (Belvedere et al. 2011, 2012) that proved the impossibility of imposing local charge neutrality in a self-gravitating system of degenerate neutrons, protons, and electrons, superseding the traditional TOV equations (Tolman 1939, Oppenheimer and Volkoff 1939). It builds on the concept of Klein potentials (Klein 1949) and the general relativistic Thomas-Fermi equations, and uses nuclear model parametrizations (NL3, NL-SH, TM1, TM2) fitted to experimental nuclear properties (Lalazissis 1997, Sharma 1993, Sugahara 1994, Hirata 1995). The results enable future studies of neutron star configurations with inner crusts (where \(\rho_{\rm crust} > \rho_{\rm drip}\)) and provide a new mass-radius relation that can be compared against the standard TOV treatment.
