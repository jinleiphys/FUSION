# Relativistic Quantum Dynamics of Many-Body Systems

> Relativistic quantum dynamics requires a unitary representation of the Poincare group on the Hilbert space of states. The dynamics of many-body systems must satisfy cluster separability requirements. In this paper we formulate an abstract framework of four dimensional Euclidean Green functions that can be used to construct relativistic quantum dynamics of N-particle systems consistent with these requirements. This approach should be useful in bridging the gap between few-body dynamics based on phenomenological mass operators and on quantum field theory.

# Relativistic Quantum Dynamics of Many-Body Systems

## Key claim

The paper formulates an abstract framework of four-dimensional Euclidean Green functions that can be used to construct relativistic quantum dynamics of N-particle systems consistent with cluster separability requirements, but the authors explicitly limit this exploratory study to systems with a fixed number of particles (e.g., nucleons) and note that there are no theorems defining minimal many-body interactions.

## Method

The authors construct an auxiliary Hilbert space with a unitary representation of the four-dimensional Euclidean group E(4) and a self-adjoint unitary involution operator Θ that provides a Poincaré-invariant indefinite inner product. Physical states are defined as equivalence classes of vectors in this auxiliary space using a Euclidean-invariant Green operator G that specifies a positive semi-definite inner product. Interactions are introduced by perturbing the free Green operator via G⁻¹ = G₀⁻¹ + U, where U is an E(4)-invariant, pseudo-Hermitean operator. For many-body systems, cluster separability is realized using formal annihilation and creation operators with connected Green functions that vanish when points separate into widely separated clusters.

## Key numbers

- For a spin-zero single particle, the Green function is G(sx - sx') = (1/2π)⁴ ∫ d⁴sp exp[isp(sx - sx')] / (sp² + m²), with ω(p) = √(m² + p²)
- For a spin-1/2 single particle, the Green function is G(sx - sy) = (1/2π)⁴ ∫ d⁴sp exp[isp·(sx - sy)] (sp·γ_e + m) / (sp² + m²)
- The inner product for spin-zero functions with support restricted to positive sx⁰ is (f, G f) = ∫ d³p / (2ω(p)) |f̃(p)|²
- The two-body interaction example includes scalar (V_S), pseudoscalar (V_P), tensor (V_T), vector (V_V), and axial-vector (V_A) terms

## Context

This work extends the Bakamjian-Thomas approach (Bakamjian, 1953) for constructing Poincaré generators by modifying the mass operator, and addresses the principal difficulty of realizing cluster separability in that approach, which was previously treated by recursive construction of many-body interactions (Cluster2). The framework connects to the Osterwalder-Schrader axioms (Osterwalder, 1973) establishing equivalence between Wightman axioms and Euclidean Green functions (Schwinger functions). The approach should enable bridging the gap between few-body dynamics based on phenomenological mass operators and quantum field theory, though the authors note the absence of finite-dimensional unitary representations of the Lorentz group as a central feature motivating their Euclidean formulation.
