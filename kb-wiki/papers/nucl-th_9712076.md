# Intermediate mass dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies

> Through the analysis of HELIOS-3 data on dilepton spectra, we demonstrate the importance of secondary processes for the dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions in the intermediate mass region. We find that, while the dilepton spectra in proton-induced reactions can be nicely explained by the decay of primary vector mesons, charmed hadrons, and initial Drell-Yan processes, the strong enhancement seen in the heavy-ion data comes mainly from the secondary meson-meson interactions which are unique to heavy-ion collisions.

# Intermediate mass dilepton production in heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies

## Key claim
Secondary meson-meson interactions, particularly the πa₁ → l⁺l⁻ process, are the dominant source of the observed dilepton enhancement in the intermediate mass region (1–2.5 GeV) for central S+W collisions at SPS energies, while the data do not require invoking quark-gluon plasma formation in sulfur-induced reactions.

## Method
The authors used a relativistic transport model to calculate dimuon spectra from secondary hadronic processes (ππ, πρ, πω, πa₁, KK̄, KK̄* + c.c.) in central S+W collisions at SPS energies, incorporating HELIOS-3 detector acceptances, mass resolution, and normalization. They compared the sum of these secondary contributions plus "background" sources (primary vector meson decays, charmed hadron decays, and initial Drell-Yan processes) directly against the HELIOS-3 experimental data.

## Key numbers
- The intermediate mass region studied spans from 1 to 2.5 GeV invariant mass.
- The πa₁ → l⁺l⁻ process is identified as "by far the most important source" for dimuon yields in this mass region.
- The ππ, πρ, and KK̄ contributions are important around 1 GeV invariant mass.
- Below 1.1 GeV and especially from 0.4 to 0.6 GeV, agreement with experimental data is "much better" when a dropping meson mass scenario is introduced.
- Excluding the ρ(1700) resonance from the πa₁ form factor reduces the πa₁ contribution and yields "slightly better" agreement with HELIOS-3 data.

## Context
This work extends prior thermal rate calculations by Gale (1994), Song (1994), Haglin (1995), and Kim (1996) that identified relevant hadronic processes for dilepton production, and applies them to actual heavy-ion collision data from the HELIOS-3 collaboration. The study shows that the intermediate-mass dilepton enhancement observed by HELIOS-3 and NA38/NA50 in central S+W and S+U collisions can be explained by secondary hadronic processes without requiring QGP formation, consistent with conclusions from J/ψ physics. The results enable future work at higher energies (e.g., RHIC) by providing a baseline for hadronic background subtraction needed to identify dilepton signals from the quark-gluon plasma phase.
