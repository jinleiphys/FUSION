# Anisotropic flow fluctuation as a possible signature of clustered nuclear geometry in O-O collisions at the Large Hadron Collider

> Nuclei having $4n$ number of nucleons are theorized to possess clusters of $α$ particles ($^4$He nucleus). The Oxygen nucleus ($^{16}$O) is a doubly magic nucleus, where the presence of an $α$-clustered nuclear structure grants additional nuclear stability. In this study, we exploit the anisotropic flow coefficients to discern the effects of an $α$-clustered nuclear geometry with respect to a Woods-Saxon nuclear distribution in O--O collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=7$ TeV using a hybrid of IP-Glasma + MUSIC + iSS + UrQMD models. In addition, we use the multi-particle cumulants method to measure anisotropic flow coefficients, such as elliptic flow ($v_{2}$) and triangular flow ($v_{3}$), as a function of multiplicity class. Anisotropic flow fluctuations, which are expected to be larger in small collision systems, are also studied for the first time in O--O collisions. It is found that an $α$-clustered nuclear distribution gives rise to an enhanced value of $v_{2}$ and $v_3$ for the low-multiplicity events. Consequently, a rise in $v_3/v_2$ is also observed for the 0--10\% multiplicity class. Further, for $α$-clustered O--O collisions, fluctuations of $v_{2}$ are larger for the highest multiplicity events, which decrease as the final-state multiplicity decreases. In contrast, for a Woods-Saxon $^{16}$O nucleus, $v_{2}$ fluctuations show an opposite behavior with decreasing multiplicity. When confronted with experimental data, this study may reveal the importance of the nuclear density profile on the discussed observables and provide physics validation for the hybrid model discussed in this work.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an alpha-clustered nuclear geometry in oxygen-16 nuclei produces enhanced elliptic flow (v2) and triangular flow (v3) in low-multiplicity O-O collisions at 7 TeV, and that the multiplicity dependence of v2 fluctuations differs qualitatively between clustered and Woods-Saxon distributions. The hedge is that these signatures are model-dependent predictions from a specific hybrid framework (IP-Glasma + MUSIC + iSS + UrQMD) that require confrontation with experimental data for validation.

## Method

The authors generated O-O collision events at sqrt(s_NN) = 7 TeV using a hybrid model chain: IP-Glasma for initial conditions (with nucleon positions from either Woods-Saxon or alpha-clustered tetrahedral geometry), MUSIC for (2+1)-dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamics with eta/s = 0.12, iSS for particle sampling via Cooper-Frye formalism, and UrQMD for hadronic cascade. They computed anisotropic flow coefficients v2 and v3 using two-particle Q-cumulants with a pseudorapidity gap of |Delta-eta| > 1.0 and four-particle cumulants, then extracted mean flow and flow fluctuations as functions of multiplicity class defined by V0M detector acceptance.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 7 TeV for O-O collisions
- Woods-Saxon parameters for oxygen-16: r0 = 2.608 fm, a = 0.513 fm, w = -0.051
- Alpha-cluster tetrahedron side length: 3.42 fm, giving RMS radius of 2.699 fm for oxygen-16
- Alpha-particle Woods-Saxon parameters: r0 = 0.964 fm, w = 0.517, a = 0.322 fm, giving RMS radius of 1.676 fm
- Switching energy density for hydrodynamics to particles: e_sw = 0.18 GeV/fm^3
- Thermalization time: tau_0 = 0.4 fm
- Lattice size L = 14 with lattice spacing a = 0.02 fm for IP-Glasma
- Pseudorapidity acceptance for reference flow particles: |eta| < 0.8
- Transverse momentum range for reference flow particles: 0.2 < p_T < 4.0 GeV/c
- 3500 IP-Glasma + MUSIC events, each sampled 200 times via iSS, yielding 700K minimum bias events total

## Context

This work extends prior studies of alpha-clustered nuclear geometry effects in O-O collisions using the AMPT model by Behera et al. (2023) and hydrodynamic calculations by Bozek (2014) for C-Au collisions with three-alpha clusters. It is the first study to report event-by-event anisotropic flow fluctuations from a hydrodynamically expanding system for O-O collisions, comparing clustered versus Woods-Saxon nuclear density profiles. The results enable experimental tests at the LHC and RHIC, which have plans to inject oxygen beams, and could provide physics validation for the hybrid IP-Glasma + MUSIC + iSS + UrQMD framework in small collision systems.
