# Approach to meson-baryon femtoscopy using effective field theories

> We calculate several femtoscopy correlation functions in the strangeness sectors $S=0$ and $S=-2$ for meson-baryon interactions. We combine the amplitudes of chiral perturbation theory at leading order with the TROY (T-matrix-based Routine for HadrOn femtoscopY) framework. We consider the correlation function for the $π^+$p and $π^-$p channels, which are currently under analysis by the ALICE collaboration at the LHC. Furthermore, we will show that analogous interactions can be used to reproduce the results for $K_S Λ$ correlation functions obtained by ALICE collaboration in PbPb collisions.

# Approach to meson-baryon femtoscopy using effective field theories

## Key claim
The authors calculate femtoscopy correlation functions for meson-baryon systems using leading-order chiral perturbation theory combined with the TROY framework, but note that their results for the π⁺p and π⁻p channels are only reliable after experimental filtering of πN pairs originating from the Δ resonance, which they do not include in their model.

## Method
The authors compute the T-matrix for meson-baryon interactions by solving a Bethe-Salpeter equation using s-wave contact potentials from baryon chiral perturbation theory at leading order (Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction), regulated with a Gaussian form factor. They then reconstruct the s-wave projection of the meson-baryon wave function and insert it into the Koonin-Pratt formula to obtain the correlation function, including Coulomb interactions for charged channels. The source function is taken as a spherically symmetric Gaussian with radius R depending on the collision system.

## Key numbers
- Cutoff Λ = 1125 MeV used for the S=0 sector, chosen to reproduce the N(1535) resonance position from Sun (2019)
- Cutoff Λ = 770 MeV used for the S=-2 sector, taken as the ρ meson mass
- Gaussian source radius R = 1 fm used for π⁻p and π⁺p correlation functions, representing typical pp collisions
- Gaussian source radius R = 3.61(44)(30) fm used for K_SΛ correlation function to match ALICE PbPb mid-peripheral collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV
- Gaussian radius range R ∈ (0.8, 1.4) fm used for K_SΛ predictions in pp collisions

## Context
This work extends femtoscopy studies of meson-baryon systems using effective field theories, building on the Koonin-Pratt formalism described by Lisa (2005) and Fabbietti (2021). The authors apply their framework to channels currently under analysis by the ALICE collaboration (π⁺p and π⁻p) and reproduce the K_SΛ correlation function results from ALICE in PbPb collisions reported by the LambdaKAlice collaboration. The approach enables future inclusion of p-wave resonances such as the Δ and Ξ(1680), as well as extension to other meson-baryon channels and adjustment of production weights.
