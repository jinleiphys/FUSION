# From Quarks and Gluons to Hadrons: Chiral Symmetry Breaking in Dynamical QCD

> We present an analysis of the dynamics of two-flavour QCD in the vacuum. Special attention is payed to the transition from the high energy quark-gluon regime to the low energy regime governed by hadron dynamics. This is done within a functional renormalisation group approach to QCD amended by dynamical hadronisation techniques. The latter allow us to describe conveniently the transition from the perturbative high-energy regime to the nonperturbative low-energy limit without suffering from a fine-tuning of model parameters. In the present work, we apply these techniques to two-flavour QCD with physical quark masses and show how the dynamics of the dominant low-energy degrees of freedom emerge from the underlying quark-gluon dynamics.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that dynamical hadronisation techniques within a functional renormalisation group framework can describe the transition from perturbative quark-gluon dynamics to nonperturbative hadronic degrees of freedom in two-flavour QCD with physical quark masses, without fine-tuning model parameters. The key hedge is that the analysis is restricted to the scalar-pseudoscalar mesonic channel (pions and sigma meson) under the assumption of maximal U(1)_A breaking, and that the truncation aims for semi-quantitative rather than full quantitative precision.

## Method

The authors employ a functional renormalisation group (FRG) approach to two-flavour QCD in four Euclidean dimensions at zero temperature and density, using the Wetterich equation with four-dimensional Litim regulators. They extend the effective action to include scale-dependent quark, gluon, ghost, and meson (pion and sigma) fields, with the mesonic sector described by an O(4)-symmetric potential V_k(rho) and a field-dependent Yukawa coupling h_k(phi^2). Dynamical hadronisation is implemented by choosing the hadronisation function dot A_k such that the flow of the four-quark coupling lambda_{q,k} vanishes identically, thereby storing all multi-quark correlations in the Yukawa coupling flow; the hadronisation function dot B_k is set to zero for simplicity.

## Key numbers

- The initial cutoff scale Lambda is taken as a large perturbative energy scale where the effective action reduces to the classical QCD action with only the strong coupling and current quark masses as free parameters.
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale k_chi is defined by the condition v_1 = 0 (curvature mass of the mesonic field vanishes), which signals a resonant four-quark interaction.
- The constituent quark mass is given by m_{q,k}^2 = (1/2) h_k^2(rho_{0,k}) rho_{0,k}, where rho_{0,k} is the physical minimum of the meson potential.
- The pion and sigma masses are m_{pi,k}^2 = V'(rho_{0,k}) and m_{sigma,k}^2 = V'(rho_{0,k}) + 2 rho_{0,k} V''(rho_{0,k}), respectively.
- The current quark mass m_q^UV is related to the symmetry breaking source c via m_q^UV = (h_Lambda / (2 v_{1,Lambda})) c.

## Context

This work extends previous functional renormalisation group studies by Braun (2008, 2009) and a quantitative quenched QCD study by Mitter (2014), and is part of the fQCD collaboration aiming at a quantitative FRG framework for QCD. The dynamical hadronisation technique was introduced by Gies (2001, 2002), Pawlowski (2005), and Floerchinger (2009), and is here applied for the first time to dynamical two-flavour QCD with physical quark masses. The results enable a unified description of QCD dynamics across all scales without manual bosonisation scale fixing, and are relevant for low-energy effective models and studies of QCD at high densities or with many flavours.
