# Nuclear modification factor in Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions using Boltzmann transport equation

> We investigate the nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) of identified particles as a function of transverse momentum ($p_{\rm T}$) in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=$ 2.76 and 5.02 TeV, as well as p-Pb collision at 5.02 TeV in the framework of Boltzmann transport equation with relaxation time approximation. In this framework, the initial distribution of particles is chosen as the Tsallis distribution and the local equilibrium distribution as the Boltzmann-Gibbs blast-wave distribution. The non-extensive parameter $q_{pp}$, the Tsallis temperature $T_{pp}$ and the equilibrium temperature $T_{eq}$ are set to be in common for all particles, while the ratio of kinetic freeze-out time to relaxation time $t_{f}/τ$ is different for different particles when we performed a combined fit to the $R_{AA}$ spectra of different particles at a given centrality. We observe that the fitted curves describe the spectra well up to $p_{\rm T}\approx$ 3 GeV/c. $q_{pp}$ and $T_{eq}$ ($t_{f}/τ$) decrease (increases) with centrality nonlinearly, while $T_{pp}$ is almost independent of centrality. The dependence of the rate at which $q_{pp}$, $T_{eq}$ or $t_{f}/τ$ changes with centrality on the energy and the size of the colliding system is discussed.

## Key claim
The Boltzmann transport equation with relaxation time approximation, using a Tsallis initial distribution and a Boltzmann-Gibbs blast-wave equilibrium distribution, can describe the nuclear modification factor spectra of identified particles up to about 3 GeV/c in Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions, but only when a combined fit is performed across particle species with common parameters for the non-extensive parameter, Tsallis temperature, and equilibrium temperature, while allowing the ratio of kinetic freeze-out time to relaxation time to differ per particle.

## Method
The authors solved the Boltzmann transport equation under the relaxation time approximation, taking the initial particle distribution as the thermodynamically consistent Tsallis distribution and the local equilibrium distribution as the Boltzmann-Gibbs blast-wave function. They performed combined least-chi-squared fits to the nuclear modification factor spectra of pions, kaons, protons, Lambda, K*0, and phi mesons across multiple centrality classes in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV, and to pions, kaons, and protons in p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV. The non-extensive parameter q_pp, Tsallis temperature T_pp, and equilibrium temperature T_eq were set common for all particles, while the ratio t_f/tau was allowed to differ per particle species.

## Key numbers
- In Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, the non-extensive parameter q_pp decreases with centrality following a power law with exponent b = -0.030 ± 0.005
- The Tsallis temperature T_pp is approximately 60.6 ± 3.3 MeV across centralities in Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV
- The equilibrium temperature T_eq decreases with centrality with exponent b = 0.290 ± 0.012 in Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV
- For pions in Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV, t_f/tau ranges from 2.213 ± 0.161 at 0-5% centrality to 0.851 ± 0.073 at 60-80% centrality
- For kaons in Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV, t_f/tau ranges from 1.731 ± 0.033 at 0-5% centrality to 0.658 ± 0.021 at 60-80% centrality
- For protons in Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV, t_f/tau ranges from 2.809 ± 0.053 at 0-5% centrality to 1.180 ± 0.044 at 60-80% centrality
- In Pb-Pb at 5.02 TeV, the rate at which ln q_pp changes with ln(N_part) is -0.022 ± 0.004
- The chi-squared per degree of freedom for the combined fit at 0-5% centrality in Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV is 37.975/116

## Context
This work extends the approach of Sahoo et al. (2015) and Sahoo et al. (2016), who previously applied the Boltzmann transport equation framework to individual fits of nuclear modification factor spectra in central collisions. The present study performs combined fits across multiple particle species and extends the analysis to non-central Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV, as well as p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV. The results enable systematic comparison of how the non-extensive parameter, equilibrium temperature, and relaxation time scale with centrality, collision energy, and system size, providing insight into the degree of deviation from equilibrium in the initial state and the time scale for thermalization.
