# Observing Non-Gaussian Sources in Heavy-Ion Reactions

> We examine the possibility of extracting non-Gaussian sources from two-particle correlations in heavy-ion reactions. Non-Gaussian sources have been predicted in a variety of model calculations and may have been seen in various like-meson pair correlations. As a tool for this investigation, we have developed an improved imaging method that relies on a Basis spline expansion of the source functions with an improved implementation of constraints. We examine under what conditions this improved method can distinguish between Gaussian and non-Gaussian sources. Finally, we investigate pion, kaon, and proton sources from the p-Pb reaction at 450 GeV/nucleon and from the S-Pb reaction at 200 GeV/nucleon studied by the NA44 experiment. Both the pion and kaon sources from the S-Pb correlations seem to exhibit a Gaussian core with an extended, non-Gaussian halo. We also find evidence for a scaling of the source widths with particle mass in the sources from the p-Pb reaction.

## Key claim

Non-Gaussian source functions can be extracted from two-particle correlation data using an improved imaging method based on Basis spline expansions, but the ability to distinguish Gaussian from non-Gaussian sources depends on the degree of the splines, the knot placement, and the use of equality constraints; the method reveals that both pion and kaon sources from S-Pb collisions at 200 GeV/nucleon exhibit a Gaussian core with an extended non-Gaussian halo, while p-Pb sources at 450 GeV/nucleon show evidence for scaling of source widths with particle mass.

## Method

The authors developed an improved imaging method that inverts the linear integral equation relating the two-particle correlation function to the source function by expanding the source in a Basis spline (b-spline) basis, which provides direct control over continuity of the source. They tested the method on model Gaussian and dipole-shaped sources using proton correlations with the REID93 nucleon-nucleon potential, then applied it to angle-averaged pion, kaon, and proton correlation data from the NA44 experiment for p-Pb reactions at 450 GeV/nucleon and S-Pb reactions at 200 GeV/nucleon. The inversion was stabilized using equality constraints (e.g., zero slope at the origin) and optimized knot placement to minimize source error.

## Key numbers

- For the Fourier transform estimate of resolution, if the correlation dies off around q ≈ 80 MeV/c, the smallest resolvable structure is Δr ≈ 8 fm.
- In tests with proton correlations binned in 6 MeV/c q-bins, the imaging region was set to r_max = 45 fm, roughly half the naive Fourier estimate of 103 fm.
- Using 11 box-spline coefficients gave χ² = 91 for a Gaussian source (R_G = 4.5 fm) and χ² = 90 for a dipole source (R_D = 3.5 fm), compared to χ² = 76 and 77 respectively with 14 coefficients.
- With 7 third-degree b-splines and equally spaced knots, the Gaussian source reconstruction gave χ² = 93 and the dipole source gave χ² = 93.
- Using the S'(r→0)=0 equality constraint with 9 third-degree b-spline coefficients and optimal knots, the Gaussian source reconstruction gave χ² = 93 and the dipole source gave χ² = 89.

## Context

This work extends the imaging approach of Brown and Danielewicz (1999) by replacing the box-spline basis with higher-degree b-splines to enforce continuity, and by implementing equality constraints and optimized knot placement to stabilize the ill-posed inversion. The method enables direct comparison of proton, kaon, and pion sources from the same reactions, which was previously difficult because different particles have different final-state interactions; the authors find that p-Pb source widths scale with particle mass as expected from Lund-type string phenomenology. The results contradict the assumption that sources are purely Gaussian and open the possibility of extracting detailed source shapes from existing NA44 data and future heavy-ion correlation measurements.
