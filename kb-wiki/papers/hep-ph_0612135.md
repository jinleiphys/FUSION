# Formation of Gapless Phases of $K^0$ Condensed Color-Flavor Locked Superconducting Quark Matter

> Electric and color neutral solutions, and the critical conditions for the formation of gapless color superconductors, are investigated in $K^0$ condensed color-flavor locked quark matter for nonzero strange quark mass. We show that as the strange quark mass increases, gapless modes for up-strange quark pairing occur first, followed by down-strange quark pairing. The behavior of the gaps, the dispersion relations, and the thermodynamic potential are all found as functions of the strange quark mass on the basis of a Nambu Jona-Lasinio type model. To a high degree of accuracy, they are presented as relatively simple elementary functions. This allows for easy computation for any reasonable range of baryon chemical potential and strange quark mass.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in $K^0$ condensed color-flavor locked (CFL$K^0$) quark matter, gapless modes appear first in the up-strange (channel II) pairing when the effective strange quark chemical potential $\mu_S = m_s^2/2\mu$ reaches $4/3$ of the gap $\Delta_{\rm CFL}$, rather than at $\mu_S = \Delta_0$ as in ordinary CFL matter; this delay is a direct consequence of the different electric/color neutral solution in the kaon-condensed phase.

## Method

The authors use a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type model with a hard momentum cutoff to compute the thermodynamic potential for CFL$K^0$ matter, incorporating electric and color neutrality conditions via chemical potentials $\mu_e$, $\mu_3$, and $\mu_8$. They derive analytic dispersion relations for the three pairing channels (I: $bd-gs$, II: $bu-rs$, III: $gu-rd$) in the CFL$K^0$ phase, then construct the gapless phase (gCFL$K^0$) by adding the contribution of gapless modes to the free energy and solving the coupled gap equations and neutrality conditions as functions of $\mu_S$.

## Key numbers

- The condition for $K^0$ condensation to occur (ignoring up and down quark masses) is $m_s < 3.034 (m_u \Delta^2)^{1/3} = 44.4 \,{\rm MeV} \times [(m_u/5\,{\rm MeV})(\Delta/25\,{\rm MeV})^2]^{1/3}$.
- The pion decay constant in CFL matter is $f_\pi^2 = (21 - 8\ln 2)/(36\pi^2) \mu^2 \approx (0.2086 \mu)^2$.
- The thermodynamic potential difference between neutral CFL$K^0$ and unpaired matter is $\Omega^{\rm neutral}_{{\rm CFL}K^0} = \Omega^{\rm neutral}_{\rm unpaired} + (3\mu^2/4\pi^2)(\mu_S^2 - 4\Delta^2) - (1/2) f_\pi^2 \mu_S^2$, giving the pairing condition $0.4224 \mu_S < \Delta$ compared to $0.5 \mu_S < \Delta$ for CFL.
- Channel II goes gapless when $\mu_S = (4/3) \Delta_{\rm CFL}(\mu_S) \approx 1.206 \Delta_0$; for $\Delta_0 = 25$ MeV and $\mu = 500$ MeV this occurs at $\mu_S \approx 30.143$ MeV.
- In the CFL phase with $m_s \neq 0$, the first gapless mode appears when $\mu_S = \Delta \approx 23.1$ MeV (using $\Delta_0 = 25$ MeV, $\mu = 500$ MeV, $\Lambda = 800$ MeV).

## Context

This work extends the study of gapless color superconductors by Alford, Kouvaris, and Rajagopal (2005) from ordinary CFL matter to the CFL$K^0$ phase, where neutral kaon condensation occurs as predicted by Schafer (2000) and Kryjevski (2003). The paper confirms the delay of gapless mode formation found by Kryjevski and Yamada (2005) and Forbes (2005) using effective theories, and provides analytic approximations for the gaps and chemical potentials that avoid full numerical minimization. The results enable further investigation of whether the chromomagnetic instability found in gCFL by Casalbuoni et al. (2005) and others might be partially or totally removed in the gCFL$K^0$ phase.
