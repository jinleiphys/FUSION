# Study of triangular flow $v_3$ in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions with a multiphase transport model

> We studied the relation between the initial geometry anisotropy and the anisotropic flow in a multiphase transport model (AMPT) for both Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV. It is found that unlike the elliptic flow $v_2$, little centrality dependence of the triangular flow $v_3$ is observed. After removing the initial geometry effect, $v_3/ε_3$ increases with the transverse particle density, which is similar to $v_2/ε_2$. The transverse momentum ($p_T$) dependence of $v_3$ from identified particles is qualitatively similar to the $p_T$ dependence of $v_2$.

## Key claim

Unlike elliptic flow \(v_2\), triangular flow \(v_3\) shows little centrality or system-size dependence in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV; however, the linear relation between \(v_3\) and the initial triangular eccentricity \(\varepsilon_3\) breaks down for \(\varepsilon_3 > 0.17\), where \(v_3\) decreases, in contrast to ideal hydrodynamic predictions.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model with a parton cross section of 3 mb, generating about 8 million Au+Au events and 19 million Cu+Cu events at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV. The event-plane method was applied: charged particles with \(p_T \leq 2\) GeV/\(c\) and \(|\eta| > 2\) were used to reconstruct the event plane, while particles with \(|\eta| < 1\) were used for the \(v_n\) measurement, with an \(\eta\) gap to reduce auto-correlations. The observed \(v_n\) were corrected by the corresponding event-plane resolution.

## Key numbers

* Triangular flow \(v_3\) shows little centrality dependence in both Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, in contrast to the strong centrality dependence of \(v_2\).
* In Au+Au collisions, \(v_3\) increases with \(\varepsilon_3\) up to \(\varepsilon_3 \approx 0.17\), then decreases for larger \(\varepsilon_3\); this decreasing trend is not seen in ideal hydrodynamic calculations.
* The ratio \(v_3/\varepsilon_3\) increases with transverse particle density and is consistent between Au+Au and Cu+Cu systems, but remains smaller than \(v_2/\varepsilon_2\) at all densities.
* At low \(p_T\) (below about 1.5 GeV/\(c\)), lighter particles (pions) have larger \(v_3\) than heavier particles (kaons, protons, lambdas), showing a mass-ordering pattern; at intermediate \(p_T\) (above 1.5 GeV/\(c\)), baryons and mesons separate into two groups.

## Context

This work extends the study of triangular flow \(v_3\) from hydrodynamic models (e.g., Alver and Roland (2010) and the ideal hydrodynamic calculations of Heinz and collaborators) to the AMPT transport model, and directly compares \(v_3\) with \(v_2\) in the same framework. It also provides a baseline for interpreting experimental \(v_3\) results from STAR (e.g., the preliminary results of Yadav) and for testing viscous hydrodynamic predictions, since the observed deviation from linear \(v_3\)-\(\varepsilon_3\) scaling at high \(\varepsilon_3\) suggests that converting efficiency from initial geometry to final flow depends on the collision centrality and system size.
