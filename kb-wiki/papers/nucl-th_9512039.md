# Nuclear Mass Dependence of Chaotic Dynamics in Ginocchio Model

> The chaotic dynamics in nuclear collective motion is studied in the framework of a schematic shell model which has only monopole and quadrupole degrees of freedom. The model is shown to reproduce the experimentally observed global trend toward less chaotic motion in heavier nuclei. The relation between current approach and the earlier studies with bosonic models is discussed.

## Key claim

The Ginocchio model, a schematic fermionic shell model with only monopole and quadrupole degrees of freedom, reproduces the experimentally observed global trend toward less chaotic (more regular) motion in heavier nuclei, but only when the shell degeneracy parameter Ω is varied to represent different mass regions; the trend is attributed to the Pauli-blocking effect hindering the realization of dynamical symmetry at smaller Ω (lighter nuclei).

## Method

The authors constructed a Hamiltonian for the Ginocchio model with proton and neutron degrees of freedom, consisting of pairing interactions between like particles and a quadrupole interaction between protons and neutrons, with a single parameter x controlling the transition from vibrational (x=0) to rotational (x=1) limits. They computed the nearest-neighbor level spacing distribution P(s) for various shell degeneracies Ω and numbers of fermion pairs, and quantified the degree of chaos using the Brody parameter β. The calculations were performed for fixed pair numbers Nπ=5 and Nν=4, with Ω values chosen to represent different nuclear mass regions.

## Key numbers

- For the nucleus ¹⁵⁰Nd (Ωπ=11, Ων=22, Nπ=5, Nν=4), the level spacing distribution transitions from Poisson-like at x=0.1 to Wigner-like at x=0.4, and remains Wigner-like up to x=1 (the rotational limit).
- With x=1 fixed and Ω=Ωπ=Ων varied as 10, 20, 40, 80, Poisson statistics (as in the boson limit) is recovered only at very large Ω (Ω=80).

## Context

This work extends the study of chaotic dynamics in nuclear collective motion from the interacting boson model (IBM) of Arima and Iachello (1970s) to a fermionic model, addressing the limitation that bosonic models are approximations to underlying fermionic degrees of freedom. It directly confronts the experimental compilation by Shriner, Mitchell and von Egidy (1990s) showing that level spacing distributions vary from Wigner-like (chaotic) in lighter nuclei to Poisson-like (regular) in heavier nuclei. The results enable future studies of spin effects reported by Abul-Magd and Weidenmüller (1990s) and more detailed comparisons with experimental nuclear level statistics across mass regions.
