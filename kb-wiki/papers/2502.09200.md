# PSR J0952-0607: Probing the Stiffest Equations of State and r-Mode Suppression Mechanisms

> We analyze PSR J0952-0607, the most massive and fastest spinning neutron star observed to date, to refine constraints on the neutron star equation of state (EoS) and investigate its robustness against r-mode instabilities. With a mass of \( 2.35 \pm 0.17 \, M_{\odot} \) and a spin frequency of 709.2 Hz, PSR J0952-0607 provides a unique opportunity to examine the effects of rapid rotation on the structure of a neutron star. Using a Bayesian framework, we incorporate the rotationally corrected mass of PSR J0952-0607, alongside PSR J0740+6620's static mass measurement, to constrain the EoS. Our findings demonstrate that neglecting rotational effects leads to biases in the inferred EoS, while including the neutron star spin produces tighter constraints on pressure-density and mass-radius relations. Additionally, we explore the r-mode instability window for PSR J0952-0607 under the assumption of both rigid and elastic crust models and find that a rigid crust allows a higher stable temperature range, whereas an elastic crust places the star within the instability window under certain thermal insulation conditions.

# PSR J0952-0607: Probing the Stiffest Equations of State and r-Mode Suppression Mechanisms

## Key claim

Including the rapid spin of PSR J0952-0607 (709.2 Hz) in Bayesian inference of the neutron star equation of state produces tighter constraints on pressure-density and mass-radius relations than using its mass alone, while neglecting rotational effects biases the inferred EoS toward lower pressures and radii at high densities. The r-mode instability analysis shows that a rigid crust allows a higher stable temperature range for this pulsar, whereas an elastic crust places the star within the instability window under certain thermal insulation conditions.

## Method

The authors employed a piecewise polytropic EoS parameterization with four parameters (log p1, Gamma1, Gamma2, Gamma3) and used nested sampling (Pymultinest) to perform Bayesian inference. They constructed likelihood functions from the mass measurement of PSR J0740+6620 (2.08 +/- 0.07 solar masses) and the mass and spin measurements of PSR J0952-0607 (2.35 +/- 0.17 solar masses, 709.2 Hz), applying rotational corrections via empirical formulae from Konstantinou (2022) that relate rotating to non-rotating mass and radius. The r-mode instability window was computed for each inferred EoS under both rigid and elastic crust assumptions, using shear viscosity models for neutron-neutron and electron-electron scattering.

## Key numbers

- PSR J0952-0607 mass: 2.35 +/- 0.17 solar masses; spin frequency: 709.2 Hz (period 1.41 ms)
- PSR J0740+6620 mass: 2.08 +/- 0.07 solar masses; spin frequency: 340 Hz
- With spin included, median radius for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: 11.65 (+2.65, -1.08) km (90% CI)
- With spin included, median radius for a 2.08 solar mass neutron star: 11.65 (+2.28, -1.10) km (90% CI)
- With spin included, median maximum mass of nonrotating neutron stars: 2.45 (+0.42, -0.27) solar masses (90% CI)
- Inferred nonrotating (TOV) mass of PSR J0952-0607: 2.10 (+0.25, -0.24) solar masses (90% CI)
- Without spin, median radius for 1.4 solar mass: 11.25 (+2.98, -1.47) km; maximum mass: 2.40 (+0.33, -0.25) solar masses
- With only PSR J0740+6620, median radius for 1.4 solar mass: 10.98 (+3.19, -1.56) km; maximum mass: 2.26 (+0.49, -0.21) solar masses
- Surface temperature range from Ho (2019): 3.4 x 10^5 K to 4.9 x 10^5 K (without envelope effects)

## Context

This work extends the line of Bayesian EoS inference from pulsar mass measurements, building on prior constraints from PSR J0740+6620 (Cromartie 2019, Fonseca 2021) and the discovery of PSR J0952-0607 (Bassa 2017, Romani 2022). The authors explicitly exclude constraints from gravitational wave events (LIGO/Virgo 2017, 2018, 2020) and NICER mass-radius measurements (Riley 2019, Miller 2019, Riley 2021, Miller 2021, Choudhury 2024) to isolate the impact of these two pulsars. The r-mode analysis connects to the debate about the nature of the GW190814 secondary component (Biswas 2020), where the absence of spin measurement limited tests of r-mode suppression. The results enable future combined analyses incorporating additional observational constraints and highlight the need to account for rotational effects when using fast-spinning pulsars for EoS inference.
