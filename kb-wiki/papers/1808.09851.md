# Two particle azimuthal harmonics in pA collisions

> We compute two-particle production in p+A collisions and extract azimuthal harmonics, using the dilute-dense formalism in the Color Glass Condensate framework. The multiple scatterings of the partons inside the projectile proton on the dense gluons inside the target nucleus are expressed in terms of Wilson lines. They generate interesting correlations, which can be partly responsible for the signals of collectivity measured at RHIC and at the LHC. Most notably, while gluon Wilson loops yield vanishing odd harmonics, quark Wilson loops can generate sizable odd harmonics for two particle correlations. By taking both quark and gluon channels into account, we find that the overall second and third harmonics lie rather close to the recent PHENIX data at RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper shows that within the dilute-dense Color Glass Condensate framework, odd azimuthal harmonics (such as v₃) in pA collisions can only arise from quark-quark channels, while gluon-gluon and quark-gluon channels produce only even harmonics; the overall second and third harmonics lie close to PHENIX data, but this agreement depends on the specific model assumptions including the use of valence quarks and the neglect of higher-order contributions.

## Method

The authors compute two-particle production in pA collisions using the dilute-dense formalism in the Color Glass Condensate framework, expressing multiple scatterings of projectile partons on target gluons in terms of Wilson lines. They evaluate the color averages of dipole and quadrupole correlators using the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model, and extract azimuthal harmonics vₙ{2} from two-particle correlations for quark-quark, quark-gluon, and gluon-gluon channels separately. The projectile partons are modeled with Gaussian Wigner distributions for impact parameter and intrinsic transverse momentum, and the calculation is performed at leading order in the dilute-dense factorization with sub-leading N_c contributions included.

## Key numbers

- The second harmonic v₂{2} from the quark-quark channel shows a falloff approximately proportional to ln(Q_s² B_p)/(Q_s² B_p) in the large Q_s² B_p region.
- For n ≥ 3, the large-Q_s asymptotic behavior of κₙ{2} in the quark-quark channel is given by (1/N_c²) × [n²/((n-2)(n+2))] × (1/(Q_s² B_p)).
- In the small Q_s limit, κₙ{2} for n ≥ 2 scales as (4/N_c²) × ((n/2)!²/n!) × (Q_s²/(2Δ²))ⁿ, with corrections of order Q_s²/Δ² and Q_s² B_p.
- The numerical values for vₙ from the quark-quark channel roughly agree with results in Dusling (2017) after setting Δ² B_p = 1.

## Context

This work extends the model calculations of Lappi (2015), Dusling (2017), and others by considering all possible combinations of incoming partons (quarks and gluons) and providing analytical understanding of azimuthal harmonics in the color dipole interpretation. It confirms the finding of Kovchegov (2012) and Dusling (2017) that two-gluon productions yield only even harmonics, while odd harmonics require quark channels. The results enable comparison with PHENIX data at RHIC and provide a baseline for distinguishing initial-state CGC effects from final-state hydrodynamic collective behavior in small systems such as pA and pp collisions.
