# Investigating the fission dynamics of the following neutron shell closed nuclei within a stochastic dynamical approach: 210Po, 212Rn, and 213Fr

> Dissipative dynamics of nuclear fission is a well confirmed phenomenon described either by a Kramers-modified statistical model or by a dynamical model employing the Langevin equation. Though dynamical models as well as statistical models incorporating fission delay are found to explain the measured fission observables in many studies, it nonetheless shows conflicting results for shell closed nuclei in the mass region 200. Analysis of recent data for neutron shell closed nuclei in excitation energy range 40$-$80 MeV failed to arrive at a satisfactory description of the data and attributed the mismatch to shell effects and/or entrance channel effects, without reaching a definite conclusion. In the present work we show that a well established stochastic dynamical code simultaneously reproduces the available data of pre-scission neutron multiplicities, fission and evaporation residue excitation functions for neutron shell closed nuclei $^{210}$Po and $^{212}$Rn and their isotopes $^{206}$Po and $^{214,216}$Rn without the need for including any extra shell or entrance channel effects. The calculations are performed by using a phenomenological universal friction form factor with no ad-hoc adjustment of model parameters. However, we note significant deviation, beyond experimental errors, in some cases of Fr isotopes.

## Key claim
The authors show that a 1D Langevin dynamical model with a universal, deformation-dependent reduced friction parameter (no ad-hoc adjustments) simultaneously reproduces pre-scission neutron multiplicities, fission cross-sections, and evaporation residue cross-sections for the neutron-shell-closed nuclei 210Po and 212Rn and their isotopes 206Po and 214,216Rn, without needing extra shell or entrance channel effects. However, significant deviation beyond experimental errors remains for some Fr isotopes (213,215,217Fr), particularly in pre-scission neutron multiplicities above approximately 50 MeV excitation energy and in evaporation residue cross-sections at high excitation energies.

## Method
The authors used a combined dynamical and statistical model based on a one-dimensional overdamped Langevin equation, with the driving force derived from nuclear entropy (free energy plus a thermodynamical correction from a deformation-dependent level density parameter). The fission dynamics were evolved from a ground-state configuration using a "funny-hills" shape parameterization for symmetric fission, with light-particle evaporation (n, p, alpha) coupled via a Monte Carlo procedure at each Langevin time step; after a stationary flux over the barrier was reached, the decay was modelled by an entropy-dependent statistical fission width. Calculations used a universal friction form factor (reduced dissipation coefficient) with no parameter adjustment, and 10^7 Langevin trajectories were run for each system, with results weighted over a fusion spin distribution from the surface friction model.

## Key numbers
- Pre-scission neutron multiplicities, fission cross-sections, and evaporation residue cross-sections were computed for 206,210Po, 212,214,216Rn, and 213,215,217Fr over an excitation energy range of approximately 40 to 80 MeV.
- For 210Po formed via 12C+198Pt and 18O+192Os, the model reproduced data up to 80 MeV excitation energy without microscopic shell corrections.
- For 212Rn and its isotopes 214,216Rn, the model successfully described pre-scission neutron multiplicities and fission excitation functions.
- For 213,215,217Fr, the model under-predicted pre-scission neutron multiplicities above approximately 50 MeV excitation energy, with the deviation increasing with energy.
- The calculated fusion cross-sections for Fr and Rn nuclei agreed with measured fusion data, supporting the validity of the calculations.
- The strength of the dissipation form factor used was consistent with values from the literature: approximately (4.5 ± 0.5) × 10^21 s^-1 (Schmitt 2007) and 2 × 10^21 s^-1 (Jurado 2004) for pre-saddle dissipation, and 1 to 6 × 10^21 s^-1 from a time-dependent Hartree-Fock + BCS study (Qiang 2021).

## Context
This work extends stochastic dynamical studies of fission (e.g., Frobrich 1998, Nadtochy 2002, Karpov, Schmitt 2014) to neutron-shell-closed nuclei in the A approximately 200 region, where previous statistical-model analyses (e.g., Golda 2013, Sandal 2013, Singh 2012) had reported conflicting results requiring shell corrections or entrance-channel effects. The present unified framework with a universal friction form factor resolves the discrepancies for Po and Rn isotopes, but the persistent deviations for Fr isotopes (especially in pre-scission neutron multiplicities and evaporation residue cross-sections) highlight the need for additional experimental measurements to rule out possible bias in evaporation residue transmission efficiency, and for further theoretical investigation of the role of entrance-channel dynamics or temperature-dependent dissipation in Fr systems.
