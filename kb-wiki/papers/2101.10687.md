# Nuclear matter distributions in the neutron-rich carbon isotopes $^{14-17}$C from intermediate-energy proton elastic scattering in inverse kinematics

> The absolute differential cross sections for small-angle proton elastic scattering off the nuclei $^{12,14-17}$C have been measured in inverse kinematics at energies near 700 MeV/u at GSI Darmstadt. The hydrogen-filled ionization chamber IKAR served simultaneously as a gas target and a detector for the recoil protons. The projectile scattering angles were measured with multi-wire tracking detectors. The radial nuclear matter density distributions and the root-mean-square nuclear matter radii were deduced from the measured cross sections using the Glauber multiple-scattering theory. A possible neutron halo structure in $^{15}$C, $^{16}$C and $^{17}$C is discussed. The obtained data show evidence for a halo structure in the $^{15}$C nucleus.

## Key claim
The measured differential cross sections for proton elastic scattering off $^{14-17}$C, analyzed with the Glauber multiple-scattering theory, provide evidence for a neutron halo structure in $^{15}$C, while the data for $^{16}$C and $^{17}$C are consistent with a neutron skin or a less pronounced halo, subject to the model-dependent parameterization of the nuclear density distributions.

## Method
Absolute differential cross sections for small-angle proton elastic scattering on $^{12,14-17}$C were measured in inverse kinematics at beam energies near 700 MeV/u at GSI Darmstadt. The hydrogen-filled ionization chamber IKAR served simultaneously as a gas target and a detector for recoil protons, while multi-wire proportional chambers measured projectile scattering angles. The radial nuclear matter density distributions and root-mean-square matter radii were deduced by fitting the measured cross sections with the Glauber multiple-scattering theory using four phenomenological density parameterizations (SF, GH, GG, GO).

## Key numbers
- Matter radius $R_{\rm m}$ for $^{12}$C: $2.34 \pm 0.05$ fm (weighted mean from SF and GH parameterizations).
- Matter radius $R_{\rm m}$ for $^{14}$C: $2.42 \pm 0.05$ fm (weighted mean from SF and GH parameterizations).
- Matter radius $R_{\rm m}$ for $^{15}$C: $2.59 \pm 0.05$ fm (averaged over SF, GH, GG, GO parameterizations).
- Matter radius $R_{\rm m}$ for $^{16}$C: $2.70 \pm 0.06$ fm (weighted mean from GH, SF, GG, GO parameterizations).
- Matter radius $R_{\rm m}$ for $^{17}$C: $2.68 \pm 0.05$ fm (averaged over SF, GH, GG, GO parameterizations).
- Valence neutron distribution radius $R_{\rm v}$ for $^{15}$C: $4.36 \pm 0.38$ fm (from GG and GO parameterizations, core $^{14}$C).
- Valence neutron distribution radius $R_{\rm v}$ for $^{17}$C: $4.05 \pm 0.47$ fm (from GG and GO parameterizations, core $^{16}$C).
- Neutron skin thickness $\delta_{\rm np}$ for $^{15}$C: $0.36 \pm 0.09$ fm; for $^{16}$C: $0.46 \pm 0.10$ fm; for $^{17}$C: $0.39 \pm 0.09$ fm (derived using proton radii from Kanungo (2016) and Angeli (2013)).

## Context
This work extends the PNPI-GSI collaboration's program of proton elastic scattering in inverse kinematics on exotic nuclei, previously applied to $^6$He, $^8$He, $^{11}$Li, $^{12}$Be, $^{14}$Be, and others (Alkhazov et al., 2002; Dobrovolsky et al., 2006; Ilieva et al., 2012). The results for $^{15-17}$C are compared with matter radii derived from interaction cross sections by Kanungo et al. (2016) and with theoretical predictions by Fortune (2018) and Abu-Ibrahim (2008). The data provide direct experimental constraints on the radial matter distributions of neutron-rich carbon isotopes, enabling refined tests of nuclear structure models for halo and skin phenomena near the neutron drip line.
