# EM vs Weak Structure Functions in DIS processes

> We obatin the ratio $F_i^A/F_i^{D}$(i=2,3, A=Be, C, Fe, Pb; D=Deuteron) in the case of weak and electromagnetic nuclear structure functions. For this, relativistic nuclear spectral function which incorporate the effects of Fermi motion, binding and nucleon correlations is used. We also consider the pion and rho meson cloud contributions and shadowing and antishadowing effects.

# EM vs Weak Structure Functions in DIS processes

## Key claim
The ratio of nuclear to deuteron structure functions for weak interactions (F₂ and F₃) differs qualitatively from the electromagnetic case, contrary to many phenomenological studies, and these differences are significant for neutrino experiments like MINERνA.

## Method
The authors compute nuclear structure functions using a relativistic nuclear spectral function that incorporates Fermi motion, binding, and nucleon correlations, derived from Lehmann's representation and nuclear many-body theory with local density approximation. Pion and rho meson cloud contributions are included via a many-body field theoretical approach, and shadowing/antishadowing effects are treated following Kulagin and Petti. Target mass corrections are applied following Schienbein, and results are compared at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO).

## Key numbers
- Base result (Fermi motion, binding, correlations) for weak structure functions in carbon decreases by 4–6% from the free case at low x.
- Including meson cloud contributions changes results by 15–17% at x = 0.08 and by 5–6% at x = 0.35.
- Difference between full model and results without shadowing/antishadowing is 3–4% at low x and low Q².
- NLO full model results reduce by 3–9% from LO results at x = 0.08 over the studied Q² range; at mid x the reduction is 3–5%; at high x NLO full results reduce by 14–24% from NLO base results.
- Ratios R(x, Q²) = 12F₂^{Pb} / 208F₂^{C} and 56F₂^{Pb} / 208F₂^{Fe} are presented at Q² = 5 GeV².

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of electromagnetic structure functions (Sajjad 2011) and weak structure functions (PRC 84, PRC 85) by systematically comparing electromagnetic and weak nuclear effects across multiple targets (Be, C, Fe, Pb). The results are compared with JLab data (Seely 2009) and SLAC data (Gomez 1994) for the electromagnetic case, and with phenomenological parametrizations from Hirai et al. and Eskola et al. for the weak case, finding significant differences. The calculations are intended to support the MINERνA experiment's study of nucleon dynamics in nuclear medium using weak structure functions.
