# Transmutation of $^{16}$O and $^{20}$Ne at the Large Hadron Collider

> In July 2025 the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) will collide $^{16}$O$^{16}$O and $^{20}$Ne$^{20}$Ne isotopes in a quest to understand the physics of ultrarelativistic light ion collisions. One particular feature is that there are many smaller isotopes with the exact same charge over mass ratio that potentially can be produced and contaminate the beam composition. Using the Trajectum framework together with the GEMINI code we provide an estimate of the production cross-section and its consequences. A potential benefit could be the interesting measurement of the multiplicity and mean transverse momentum of $^{16}$O$^{4}$He collisions.

## Key claim

The paper estimates that during the LHC's July 2025 light-ion run, a small fraction of the primary oxygen or neon beams will transmute into smaller isotopes (predominantly helium and deuterons) that can continue circulating in the beam; the authors hedge that the exact production cross-section depends strongly on the uncertain nucleon momenta in the fragmentation region, with a factor-of-two systematic uncertainty.

## Method

The authors used the Trajectum framework, which combines a modified T-RENTo initial condition model with relativistic hydrodynamics, and extended it to track spectator nucleons after the collision. Spectators were clustered based on spatial and momentum proximity, then fed into the GEMINI code to simulate the decay of excited isotopes into ground-state isotopes with lifetimes greater than one second. The model parameters were loosely motivated by describing the PbPb zero-degree calorimeter signal, with the nucleon-nucleon cross-section set to 68.3 mb and the spectator cross-section to 39.1 mb.

## Key numbers

- Total hadronic cross-section for OO collisions: about 1.42 b; for NeNe collisions: about 1.85 b
- The production of helium and other isotopes is enhanced by about 14% more than a naive scaling with the cross-section would give
- The circulating isotope production cross-sections are dominated by He-4; the ratio of deuteron plus helium production to the total hadronic cross-section is about (2.108 + 2.219) / 1420 = 0.3%
- Cross-sections for mixed collisions: OO-He-4 is 0.810 b, OO-deuteron is 0.696 b
- After about 12 hours, only about half of the oxygen will be left in the beam
- The optics are foreseen to only allow a momentum deviation of 0.034% of the canonical momentum for isotopes to keep circulating
- Halving the Fermi momentum and the maximum cluster momentum difference approximately doubles the amount of most circulating isotopes

## Context

This work extends the study of ultrarelativistic light ion collisions at the LHC, which aim to understand relativistic hydrodynamics at its limits and jet-medium interactions in small droplets of quark-gluon plasma, building on prior work by Brewer (2021), Grosse-Oetringhaus (2024), and Giacalone (2024) on the shapes of oxygen and neon isotopes. The transmutation effect is a potential contamination for the primary OO and NeNe collisions, but also presents an opportunity to study O-He-4 and Ne-He-4 collisions simultaneously, similar to the SMOG system at LHCb for fixed-target collisions. The paper notes significant uncertainty around the fragmentation region, with the Abrasion-Ablation Monte Carlo for Colliders (AAMMC) model by Svetlichnyi (2023) predicting significantly smaller nucleon momenta than the Fermi-momentum-based approach used here.
