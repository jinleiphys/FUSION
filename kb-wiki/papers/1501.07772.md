# Stability of magnetized strange quark matter in the MIT bag model with the density dependent bag pressure

> The stability of magnetized strange quark matter (MSQM) is studied in the MIT bag model with the density dependent bag pressure. In the consistent thermodynamic description of MSQM, the quark chemical potentials, the total thermodynamic potential and the anisotropic pressure acquire the corresponding additional term proportional to the density derivative of the bag pressure. The model parameter space is determined, for which MSQM is absolutely stable, i.e., its energy per baryon is less than that of the most stable $^{56}$Fe nucleus under the zero external pressure and vanishing temperature. It is shown that there exists the magnetic field strength $H_{u\,max}$ at which the upper bound $B_\infty^u$ on the asymptotic bag pressure $B_\infty\equiv B(\varrho_B\gg \varrho_0$) ($\varrho_0$ being the nuclear saturation density) from the absolute stability window vanishes. The value of this field, \hbox{$H_{u\,max}\sim$$(1$--$3)\cdot10^{18}$}~G, represents the upper bound on the magnetic field strength, which can be reached in a strongly magnetized strange quark star. It is clarified how the absolute stability window and upper bound on the magnetic field strength are affected by varying the parameters in the Gaussian parametrization for the density dependence of the bag pressure.

## Key claim

The paper claims that there exists an upper bound on the magnetic field strength, \(H_{u\,max} \sim (1\text{--}3)\cdot10^{18}\) G, beyond which magnetized strange quark matter (MSQM) cannot be absolutely stable; this bound depends on the parameters of the Gaussian parametrization for the density-dependent bag pressure and vanishes when the upper bound on the asymptotic bag pressure \(B_\infty^u\) becomes negative.

## Method

The author studies MSQM at zero temperature under charge neutrality and chemical equilibrium, using the MIT bag model extended with a density-dependent bag pressure \(B(\varrho_B)\) given by a Gaussian parametrization. The consistent thermodynamic description introduces an additional term proportional to \(\partial B/\partial\varrho_B\) in the quark chemical potentials, thermodynamic potential, and anisotropic pressures. The absolute stability window is determined by requiring that the energy per baryon of MSQM be less than that of \(^{56}\)Fe (930 MeV) and that two-flavor quark matter be less stable, under the equilibrium conditions of vanishing longitudinal and transverse pressures.

## Key numbers

* The maximum upper bound on the asymptotic bag pressure at \(H=0\) is \(B^{u}_{\infty,\,max} \approx 74.9\) MeV/fm\(^3\) for \(\Delta B_0 = 0\), independent of \(\beta\).
* The upper bound \(B_\infty^u\) vanishes at \(H_{u\,max} \approx 3.1\times 10^{18}\) G for \(\Delta B_0 = 0\) at any \(\beta\); for \(\Delta B_0 = 75\) MeV/fm\(^3\), \(H_{u\,max} \approx 1.1\times 10^{18}\) G at \(\beta=0.01\) and \(H_{u\,max} \approx 3\times 10^{18}\) G at \(\beta=0.17\).
* The maximum lower bound on the asymptotic bag pressure at \(H=0\) is \(B^{l}_{\infty,\,max} \approx 56.5\) MeV/fm\(^3\) for \(\Delta B_0 = 0\), independent of \(\beta\).
* The lower bound \(B_\infty^l\) vanishes at \(H_{l\,0} \approx 2.7\times 10^{18}\) G for \(\Delta B_0 = 0\) at any \(\beta\).
* At \(H=0\), the minimum upper bound on baryon density is \(\varrho_{B\,min}^u \approx 2.1\varrho_0\) for \(\Delta B_0 = 0\), independent of \(\beta\); the minimum lower bound is \(\varrho_{B\,min}^{\,l} \approx 1.4\varrho_0\) for \(\Delta B_0 = 0\), independent of \(\beta\).

## Context

This work extends the study of magnetized strange quark matter in the MIT bag model by incorporating a density-dependent bag pressure, following the approach of Burgio (2002) to reconcile constraints from heavy-ion experiments and neutron star observations. It builds on prior analyses of pressure anisotropy in magnetized matter by Kh (1997), FIKPS (2005), and Isayev (2011, 2012), and uses the thermodynamic consistency formalism of Xia (2014). The obtained upper bound on the magnetic field strength, \(H_{u\,max} \sim (1\text{--}3)\cdot10^{18}\) G, is similar to the estimate for maximum average magnetic fields in neutron stars composed of strange baryonic matter by Broderick (2002). The results enable further studies of strongly magnetized strange quark stars, including effects of perturbative quark interactions and nonuniform magnetic fields.
