# On the possible mixing of the electron capture and the positron emission channels in nuclear decay

> On the basis of the idea of mixing (interaction) between the electron capture and the positron emission channels in the β^+ decay in the cases when both channels are energetically allowed, we attempt to explain oscillations of the K-capture rates that were possibly seen in the recent experiment.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that oscillations of K-capture rates observed in one-electron ions of \(^{140}\)Pr and \(^{142}\)Pm may arise from mixing (interaction) between the electron capture and positron emission channels in \(\beta^+\) decay, rather than from neutrino oscillations; the effect is predicted to be strongly suppressed in neutral atoms or two-electron ions due to the Pauli principle.

## Method

The author develops a two-level phenomenological model analogous to neutrino oscillations, where the states \(|\mathrm{EC}\rangle\) and \(|\beta^+\rangle\) mix via a weak interaction, producing time-dependent transition rates. Using experimental oscillation amplitudes and periods from one-electron ion data, the mixing angle \(\vartheta\) and energy splitting \(\Delta\) are extracted for \(^{142}\)Pm and \(^{140}\)Pr. Microscopic estimates of the relevant weak-interaction matrix elements are then performed using contact interactions mediated by \(Z\) and \(W\) bosons, with plane waves for continuum leptons and non-relativistic hydrogenic 1s electron wave functions.

## Key numbers

* For one-electron \(^{142}\)Pm: \(w^0_{\mathrm{EC}}/w^0_{\beta^+} \approx 0.12\); the EC rate oscillates as \(w_{\mathrm{EC}}(t) = w^0_{\mathrm{EC}}[1 + 7.33\sin^2(2\vartheta)\sin^2(\Delta t/\hbar)]\).
* For one-electron \(^{140}\)Pr: \(w^0_{\mathrm{EC}}/w^0_{\beta^+} \approx 0.41\); the EC rate oscillates as \(w_{\mathrm{EC}}(t) = w^0_{\mathrm{EC}}[1 + 1.44\sin^2(2\vartheta)\sin^2(\Delta t/\hbar)]\).
* Extracted mixing parameters: for \(^{142}\)Pm, \(\vartheta \approx 8^\circ\), \(\Delta = \pm 0.407(13)\times 10^{-15}\) eV; for \(^{140}\)Pr, \(\vartheta \approx 16^\circ\), \(\Delta = \pm 0.410(5)\times 10^{-15}\) eV.
* Extracted off-diagonal matrix element: \(V_{\mathrm{EC},\beta^+} = 0.11\times 10^{-15}\) eV for \(^{142}\)Pm and \(0.22\times 10^{-15}\) eV for \(^{140}\)Pr.
* Microscopic estimate for \(^{142}\)Pm gives \(V_{\mathrm{EC},\beta^+} \approx 0.056\times 10^{-15}\) eV and \(V_{\beta^+,\beta^+} \approx 1.4\times 10^{-15}\) eV.
* For neutral \(^{140}\)Pr, the predicted ratio \(N(\mathrm{EC}(1s))/N(\beta^+) = 0.87\) is compared to the experimental average \(0.846(75)\).
* For neutral \(^{142}\)Pm, the predicted increase in \(N(\mathrm{EC})/N(\beta^+)\) is about 16% relative to the standard calculation, compared to the experimental value \(0.297(45)\) versus the theoretical \(0.257\).

## Context

This work extends the discussion following the experimental observation of time-dependent oscillations in K-capture rates of one-electron ions by Litvinov (2008) and Kienle (2008), which had been previously interpreted via neutrino oscillations by Ivanov (2008). The author proposes an alternative mechanism based on standard weak-interaction physics rather than neutrino mass differences, noting that the observed energy splitting \(\Delta \sim 10^{-16}\) eV is 14-15 orders of magnitude smaller than the mass differences relevant to solar and atmospheric neutrino oscillations. The paper predicts that the oscillations should be strongly suppressed in two-electron ions or neutral atoms, a claim supported by the later experiment of Vetter (2008) on neutral \(^{142}\)Pm, and suggests that the most direct test would be to observe time-antiphase oscillations in the positron emission branch of one-electron \(^{140}\)Pr.
