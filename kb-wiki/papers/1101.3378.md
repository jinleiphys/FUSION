# Light flavor asymmetry of nucleon sea

> The light flavor antiquark distributions of the nucleon sea are calculated in the effective chiral quark model and compared with experimental results. The contributions of the flavor-symmetric sea-quark distributions and the nuclear EMC effect are taken into account to obtain the ratio of Drell-Yan cross sections $σ^{\mathrm{pD}}/2σ^{\mathrm{pp}}$, which can match well with the results measured in the FermiLab E866/NuSea experiment. The calculated results also match the measured $\bar{d}(x)-\bar{u}(x)$ from different experiments, but unmatch the behavior of $\bar{d}(x)/\bar{u}(x)$ derived indirectly from the measurable quantity $σ^{\mathrm{pD}}/2σ^{\mathrm{pp}}$ by the FermiLab E866/NuSea Collaboration at large $x$. We suggest to measure again $\bar{d}(x)/\bar{u}(x)$ at large $x$ from precision experiments with careful experimental data treatment. We also propose an alternative procedure for experimental data treatment.

## Key claim

The effective chiral quark model, when including flavor-symmetric sea-quark contributions and the nuclear EMC effect, can reproduce the measured Drell-Yan cross-section ratio σ^(pD)/2σ^(pp) and the difference \(\bar{d}(x)-\bar{u}(x)\) from the FermiLab E866/NuSea experiment, but it cannot reproduce the ratio \(\bar{d}(x)/\bar{u}(x)\) derived indirectly by the E866/NuSea Collaboration at large \(x\); the authors therefore suggest that the ratio \(\bar{d}(x)/\bar{u}(x)\) at large \(x\) should be remeasured with careful data treatment and propose an alternative procedure for extracting it.

## Method

The authors calculate light-flavor antiquark distributions in the nucleon sea using the effective chiral quark model, with constituent quark distributions taken from either the constituent quark model or the light-cone quark-spectator-diquark model as inputs. They convolute these with splitting functions for Goldstone boson fluctuations and include additional flavor-symmetric sea contributions (estimated from the CTEQ4 parametrization) and the nuclear EMC effect via a \(Q^2\)-rescaling model to compute the Drell-Yan cross-section ratio \(\sigma^{\mathrm{pD}}/2\sigma^{\mathrm{pp}}\) and the antiquark distributions \(\bar{d}(x)-\bar{u}(x)\) and \(\bar{d}(x)/\bar{u}(x)\).

## Key numbers

- Gottfried sum from NMC (1994): \(\int_{0}^{1}[F_{2}^{\mathrm{p}}(x)-F_{2}^{\mathrm{n}}(x)]\mathrm{d}x/x = 0.235 \pm 0.026\).
- Integrated flavor asymmetry from NMC (1994): \(\int_{0}^{1}[\bar{d}^{\mathrm{p}}(x)-\bar{u}^{\mathrm{p}}(x)]\mathrm{d}x = 0.148 \pm 0.039\).
- Drell-Yan asymmetry from NA51: \(A_{\mathrm{DY}} = -0.09 \pm 0.02 \pm 0.025\) at \(\langle x \rangle = 0.18\), giving \(\bar{u}^{\mathrm{p}}/\bar{d}^{\mathrm{p}} = 0.51 \pm 0.04 \pm 0.05\).
- Integrated flavor asymmetry from E866/NuSea: \(\int_{0}^{1}[\bar{d}(x)-\bar{u}(x)]\mathrm{d}x = 0.118 \pm 0.012\).
- Cutoff parameter in the chiral quark model: \(\Lambda_{\pi} = \Lambda_{K} = 1500\) MeV, yielding \(\langle P_{\pi} \rangle = 0.149\), \(\langle P_{K} \rangle = 0.085\), \(\langle P_{\eta} \rangle = 0.063\), and renormalization constant \(Z = 0.682\).
- Constituent quark masses: \(m_u = m_d = 330\) MeV, \(m_s = 480\) MeV; pion masses \(m_{\pi^{\pm}} = m_{\pi^{0}} = 140\) MeV; kaon masses \(m_{K^{+}} = m_{K^{0}} = 495\) MeV.
- EMC effect rescaling factor: \(\xi = 1.1\) (and \(\xi = 1.5\) for comparison) at \(Q \approx 12.5\) GeV/c, applied for \(x > 0.22\).

## Context

This work extends the effective chiral quark model approach of Eichten, Hinchliffe, and Quigg (1992) and Wakamatsu (1992) to explain the nucleon sea flavor asymmetry, and it directly addresses the unexpected \(\bar{d}(x)/\bar{u}(x) < 1\) behavior at large \(x\) reported by the FermiLab E866/NuSea Collaboration (1998). The authors show that while their model matches the directly measured cross-section ratio and \(\bar{d}(x)-\bar{u}(x)\), the derived \(\bar{d}(x)/\bar{u}(x)\) from E866/NuSea may be affected by the nuclear EMC effect in deuterium, which the collaboration had assumed negligible. This work enables future precision experiments to remeasure \(\bar{d}(x)/\bar{u}(x)\) at large \(x\) with careful treatment of nuclear effects, and it provides an alternative data-analysis procedure.
