# Neutrinoless double beta decay with light sterile neutrinos: the contact terms

> We study neutrinoless double-beta decay in extensions of the Standard Model that include $n$ right-handed neutrino singlets, with masses $m_s$ below the GeV scale. Generalizing recently developed matching methods, we determine the $m_s$ dependence of the short-range $nn \to pp$ couplings that appear to leading order in the chiral effective field theory description of neutrinoless double beta decay. We focus on two scenarios, corresponding to the minimal $ν$SM and left-right symmetric models. We illustrate the impact of our new results in the case of the $ν$SM, showing a significant impact on the neutrinoless double-beta decay half-life when $m_s$ is in the 200-800 MeV range.

## Key claim

The paper determines the dependence of short-range $nn \to pp$ contact couplings on sterile neutrino masses $m_s$ below the GeV scale, showing that these couplings have a polynomial dependence on $m_s^2$ and produce a significant impact on neutrinoless double-beta decay half-lives when $m_s$ is in the 200-800 MeV range, but only for scenarios where the sterile neutrino mass is below the chiral symmetry breaking scale $\Lambda_\chi \sim 1$ GeV.

## Method

The authors generalize the Cottingham approach matching method from Cirigliano (2020, 2021) to compute the sterile-neutrino-mass dependence of the leading-order contact term ${\cal C}_1(m_s)$ in chiral effective field theory. They split the neutrino momentum integral into low-momentum ($|\vec{k}| < \Lambda$) and high-momentum ($|\vec{k}| > \Lambda$) regions, using chiral EFT and nucleon form factors for the low region and the operator product expansion for the high region, then enforce a matching condition between the full theory amplitude and the chiral EFT amplitude for the $nn \to pp e^- e^-$ process. The analysis covers both the minimal $\nu$SM scenario (left-left currents) and left-right symmetric models (left-right currents).

## Key numbers

- Sterile neutrino mass range studied: $m_s$ below the GeV scale, with significant impact on half-life when $m_s$ is in the 200-800 MeV range
- The contact coupling expansion: ${\cal C}_1(m_s) = {\cal C}_1^{(0)} + {\cal C}_1^{(2)} m_s^2 + \mathcal{O}(m_s^4)$, with coefficients expected to scale as $c_1^{(n)} = \mathcal{O}(F_\pi^{-2} \Lambda_\chi^{-n})$ from naive dimensional analysis
- The NLO correction parameter: $\dNLO \equiv 8C_2/(m_N C^2)$ with $C_2/C^2 \simeq 0.38$ from Kaplan (1996)
- The high-momentum region contribution involves the unknown matrix element $\bar{g}_1^{NN}$, estimated in the range $[-10, +10]$
- The low-momentum region uses form factor combination $g_{\rm full}(0) = (1 + 2g_A^2)$ at leading order

## Context

This work extends the matching methodology developed by Cirigliano (2020, 2021) for the massless neutrino case to include sterile neutrinos with masses below the GeV scale, building on the EFT framework for neutrinoless double-beta decay established by Dekens (2020, 2023, 2024). The results enable more accurate predictions for neutrinoless double-beta decay rates in the $\nu$SM and left-right symmetric models, particularly for sterile neutrino masses in the 200-800 MeV range where the contact term contribution becomes significant. The approach complements lattice QCD calculations by Nicholson (2018) and Detmold (2022) that determine hadronic matrix elements for heavier sterile neutrinos.
