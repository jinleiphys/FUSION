# Near-Threshold Production of $η$-Mesons

> It is shown that the striking energy variation in the $p\, d \rightarrow \mbox{$^{\, 3}{\rm He}$}\, η$ cross section near threshold is probably due to a final state interaction associated with a large (complex) $η-\mbox{$^{3}{\rm He}$}$ scattering length. The consequences of this hypothesis are studied for the production of the meson in the $η-\mbox{$^{4}{\rm He}$}$ and $η-\mbox{$^{7}{\rm Be}$}$ channels.

# Near-Threshold Production of η-Mesons

## Key claim

The striking energy variation in the pd → ³He η cross section near threshold is probably due to a final-state interaction associated with a large (complex) η-³He scattering length, though the analysis is simplified and the numerical agreement may be somewhat fortuitous given corrections to the lowest-order optical potential.

## Method

The author extracted the squared amplitude |f|² from measured cross sections by removing the kinematic phase-space factor, then modeled the near-threshold energy dependence using a final-state interaction enhancement factor derived from a constant K-matrix approximation (f ≈ f_B / (1 - ipa)). An η-nucleon scattering length was first determined from π⁻p → ηn data using unitarity constraints, then an η-³He optical potential was constructed from the ηN scattering length and a Gaussian nuclear density to compute the η-³He scattering length and predict the energy dependence of the pd → ³He η cross section.

## Key numbers

- The squared amplitude |f_η|² decreases by over a factor of three between threshold and an η center-of-mass momentum of p_η = 70 MeV/c, corresponding to a beam energy change of less than 10 MeV.
- The η-nucleon scattering length extracted from π⁻p → ηn data is a(ηN) = (0.55 ± 0.20 + i0.30) fm.
- The η-³He scattering length derived from the optical potential is a(η³He) = (-2.31 + i2.57) fm.
- The η-⁴He scattering length predicted from the same optical potential is (-2.00 + i0.97) fm.
- The decrease in |f|² between p_η = 0.1 and 0.4 fm⁻¹ is expected to be about 2.8 for ³He but only 1.9 for ⁴He.
- The η-⁷Be scattering length predicted from the optical potential is (-2.92 + i1.21) fm.
- A lower bound on the imaginary part of the ηN scattering length from threshold π⁻p → ηn data gives Im[a(ηN)] ≥ (0.28 ± 0.04) fm.

## Context

This work extends the final-state interaction approach previously applied to π⁰ production (Germond and Wilkin, 1990) to the η case, where the dominant S-wave N*(1535) resonance produces a strong near-threshold enhancement. The analysis contradicts the spectator-nucleon model of Laget and Lecolley (1988), which predicted little energy dependence near threshold, and suggests that perturbative treatments of η-nucleon rescattering are inadequate. The results enable predictions for coherent η production on ⁴He and ⁷Be targets, and motivate further measurements of the π⁻³He → η³H reaction closer to threshold than the data of Peng (1989).
