# Near-Threshold Production of $О·$-Mesons

> It is shown that the striking energy variation in the $p\, d \rightarrow \mbox{$^{\, 3}{\rm He}$}\, О·$ cross section near threshold is probably due to a final state interaction associated with a large (complex) $О·-\mbox{$^{3}{\rm He}$}$ scattering length. The consequences of this hypothesis are studied for the production of the meson in the $О·-\mbox{$^{4}{\rm He}$}$ and $О·-\mbox{$^{7}{\rm Be}$}$ channels.

## Context

This work extends the final-state interaction approach previously applied to ПҖвҒ° production (Germond and Wilkin, 1990) to the О· case, where the dominant S-wave N*(1535) resonance produces a strong near-threshold enhancement. The analysis contradicts the spectator-nucleon model of Laget and Lecolley (1988), which predicted little energy dependence near threshold, and suggests that perturbative treatments of О·-nucleon rescattering are inadequate. The results enable predictions for coherent О· production on вҒҙHe and вҒ·Be targets, and motivate further measurements of the ПҖвҒ»ВіHe вҶ’ О·ВіH reaction closer to threshold than the data of Peng (1989).
