# Quasielastic lepton scattering and back-to-back nucleons in the short-time approximation

> Understanding quasielastic electron- and neutrino-scattering from nuclei has taken on new urgency with current and planned neutrino oscillation experiments, and with electron scattering experiments measuring specific final states, such as those involving nucleon pairs in ``back-to-back'' configurations. Accurate many-body methods are available for calculating the response of light ($A \leq 12$) nuclei to electromagnetic and weak probes, but they are computationally intensive and only applicable to the inclusive response. In the present work we introduce a novel approach, based on realistic models of nuclear interactions and currents, to evaluate the short-time (high-energy) inclusive and exclusive response of nuclei. The approach accounts reliably for crucial two-nucleon dynamics, including correlations and currents, and provides information on back-to-back nucleons observed in electron and neutrino scattering experiments. We demonstrate that in the quasielastic regime and at moderate momentum transfers both initial- and final-state correlations, and two-nucleon currents are important for a quantitatively successful description of the inclusive response and final state nucleons. Finally, the approach can be extended to include relativistic---kinematical and dynamical---effects, at least approximately in the two-nucleon sector, and to describe the response in the resonance-excitation region.

## Key claim

The short-time approximation (STA) introduced in this work accounts for both initial- and final-state correlations and two-nucleon currents in quasielastic lepton scattering, but the authors explicitly hedge that the approach is valid only at high energy transfer and moderate-to-high momentum transfer, and that it will not correctly describe low-lying excitations or collective behavior like giant resonances.

## Method

The authors evaluate the real-time current-current correlator for short times by retaining the full ground state and current operators, but approximating final-state interactions at the two-nucleon level, specifically, only those affecting pairs involved at the electromagnetic interaction vertex. The approach uses realistic Argonne \(v_{18}\) two-nucleon and Urbana-IX three-nucleon interactions with associated one- and two-nucleon electroweak currents, and requires only knowledge of the ground state, making it applicable to heavier nuclei amenable to auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) calculations.

## Key numbers

- The central density of atomic nuclei is \(\sim 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), corresponding to a Fermi momentum \(k_F\) of \(\sim 1.35\) fm\(^{-1}\) or 270 MeV/c.
- The nearest-neighbor distance distribution for nucleons at nuclear matter density peaks at about 1.1 fm, similar to the range of one-pion-exchange.
- The relevant relative pair momentum corresponding to inter-nucleon separations of \(\sim 1\) fm is \(\pi / r_{ij} \sim 500\) MeV/c.
- Two-nucleon processes give \(\sim 30\%\) contributions to the electromagnetic transverse response at moderate momentum transfers (2–4 fm\(^{-1}\)).
- At an imaginary time \(\tau = 0.01\) MeV\(^{-1}\), nucleons have propagated only about 1.1 fm; at \(\tau = 0.02\) MeV\(^{-1}\), the equivalent distance is about 1.6 fm, determining the high-energy response at \(E \gtrsim 50\) MeV.
- Electron scattering experiments find roughly 20% probability for nucleons to be above the Fermi momentum \(k_F\).

## Context

This work extends the Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations of Carlson (2001), Lovato (2013, 2015, 2016), and others, which accurately compute inclusive electroweak response functions for light nuclei (\(A \leq 12\)) but are computationally intensive and limited to inclusive channels. The STA provides a computationally cheaper alternative that can also access exclusive channels involving back-to-back nucleon pairs, and can be extended to heavier nuclei via AFDMC and to include relativistic effects and the resonance-excitation region. The approach contrasts with plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) methods based on momentum distributions or spectral functions, which cannot preserve full sum rules because two-nucleon currents are ignored.
