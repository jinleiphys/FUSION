# Exact Solutions of the Dirac Equation for Modified Coulombic Potentials

> Exact solutions are found to the Dirac equation for a combination of Lorentz scalar and vector Coulombic potentials with additional non-Coulombic parts. An appropriate linear combination of Lorentz scalar and vector non-Coulombic potentials, with the scalar part dominating, can be chosen to give exact analytic Dirac wave functions. The method works for the ground state or for the lowest orbital state with l=j-{1/2}, for any j.

# Exact Solutions of the Dirac Equation for Modified Coulombic Potentials

## Key claim

Exact analytic Dirac wave functions exist for a combination of Lorentz scalar and vector Coulombic potentials plus additional non-Coulombic potentials, but only when the vector and scalar non-Coulombic parts satisfy the specific relation V(r) = -(E/m) S(r) and the scalar part dominates. The method is restricted to the ground state or the lowest orbital state with l = j - 1/2 for any j, and does not work for radially excited states.

## Method

The authors start from the Dirac equation with combined Coulombic (λ/r and η/r) and non-Coulombic (V(r) and S(r)) potentials, then impose an ansatz relating the upper and lower two-component spinors: v(r) = iγ (σ·r̂) u(r). This reduces the Dirac equation to two first-order ordinary differential equations for u(r), which are equated term-by-term to yield algebraic constraints linking γ, the Coulombic couplings, and the bound state energy E. The same procedure is extended to orbitally excited states with l = j - 1/2 by using the ansatz g(r) = γ f(r) for the radial functions.

## Key numbers

- Bound state energy is given by E = m (1 - γ²)/(1 + γ²) = m (bλ - η)/(λ - bη), where b = ±√(1 - λ² + η²)
- The constant a = γ(m + E) = ±√(m² - E²)
- Bound state energies are restricted to the range -m < E < m
- The Coulombic couplings must satisfy 1 - λ² + η² ≥ 0 for real solutions
- For the "normal" class, λ + η > 0 is required
- For orbitally excited states (l = j - 1/2), the same formulas apply with the replacements λ → λ/κ and η → η/κ, where κ = j + 1/2

## Context

This work extends the method of Franklin (1999), which found exact solutions for a Coulomb plus linear confining potential, to arbitrary combinations of Lorentz scalar and vector Coulombic potentials with general non-Coulombic parts. The approach also builds on saddle-point techniques used for the Dirac equation (1999) and Breit equation (1999). The results enable construction of exact analytic wave functions for a wide class of potentials, including power-law forms S(r) = μ(s+1) r^s, and allow three unusual sub-classes of solutions including cases where the non-Coulombic potential dominates at the origin or diverges negatively at infinity.
