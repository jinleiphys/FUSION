# The mass radius of the proton

> The mass radius is a fundamental property of the proton that so far has not been determined from experiment. Here we show that the mass radius of the proton can be rigorously defined through the formfactor of the trace of the energy-momentum tensor (EMT) of QCD in the weak gravitational field approximation, as appropriate for this problem. We then demonstrate that the scale anomaly of QCD enables the extraction of the formfactor of the trace of the EMT from the data on threshold photoproduction of $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ quarkonia, and use the recent GlueX Collaboration data to extract the r.m.s. mass radius of the proton ${\rm R_m = 0.55 \pm 0.03 \ fm}$. The extracted mass radius is significantly smaller than the charge radius of the proton ${\rm R_C = 0.8409 \pm 0.0004 \ fm}$. We attribute this difference to the interplay of asymptotic freedom and spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry in QCD, and outline future measurements needed to determine the mass radius more precisely.

# The mass radius of the proton

## Key claim
The root-mean-square mass radius of the proton can be extracted from threshold photoproduction data on J/ψ and Υ quarkonia using the scale anomaly of QCD, yielding a value significantly smaller than the charge radius; this difference is attributed to the interplay of asymptotic freedom and spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, but the extraction relies on the assumption that the scalar gluon operator dominates over tensor and derivative-suppressed contributions near threshold.

## Method
The author defines the mass radius through the formfactor of the trace of the energy-momentum tensor (EMT) in the weak gravitational field approximation, showing that the distribution of mass and the distribution of the trace of the EMT coincide in the non-relativistic limit. Using the scale anomaly of QCD, the formfactor of the trace of the EMT is related to the matrix element of the scalar gluon operator, which is extracted from the differential cross section of J/ψ photoproduction near threshold. The GlueX Collaboration data at photon lab energy 10.72 GeV are fitted with a dipole parameterization of the scalar gravitational formfactor, with the short-distance coefficient c₂ treated as a free parameter.

## Key numbers
- Extracted mass radius of the proton: R_m = 0.55 ± 0.03 fm
- Extracted scalar gravitational formfactor mass parameter: m_s = 1.24 ± 0.07 GeV
- Charge radius of the proton (for comparison): R_C = 0.8409 ± 0.0004 fm
- Short-distance coefficient from fit: |c₂|² = 0.043 ± 0.006 fm⁴, corresponding to c̄c pair size ≈ 0.1 fm
- Threshold minimal momentum transfer for J/ψ: t_min ≈ -2.23 GeV² ≈ -(1.5 GeV)²
- Charm quark mass: m_c ≈ 1.25 GeV, giving 4m_c² ≈ 6.25 GeV² as the upper bound of the kinematic region
- J/ψ mass: M_ψ ≈ 3.097 GeV; proton mass: M ≈ 0.938 GeV
- Adjusted r² of the fit: 0.99

## Context
This work provides the first experimental extraction of the proton mass radius, a fundamental property previously undetermined from experiment. It extends the proposal by Kharzeev (1995, 1998) that matrix elements of the scalar gluon operator can be extracted from heavy quarkonium photoproduction near threshold, and uses the recent GlueX Collaboration data (2019). The result is consistent with a lattice QCD evaluation by Shanahan and Detmold (2019) that found m_s = 1.13 ± 0.06 GeV for the gluon formfactor. The approach contrasts with vector meson dominance models and perturbative two-gluon exchange approaches that relate to gluon structure functions, since the scalar gravitational formfactor originates from the trace operator rather than traceless gluon operators. Future measurements at lower energies (E_γ ≤ 9.4 GeV) could reduce the theoretical uncertainty from derivative-suppressed operators to below 5%.
