# Meson production in proton-proton scattering within an extended linear sigma model

> We study the production of non-strange mesons in proton-proton scattering reactions within an effective model of mesons and baryons with global chiral symmetry. The model includes the nucleon ($N$) and its chiral partner ($N^{\ast}$) and their couplings to the $N_{f}=2$ multiplets of (pseudo-)scalar and (axial-)vector mesons. Results for the production of $ω$ and $η$ mesons are presented.

# Meson production in proton-proton scattering within an extended linear sigma model

## Key claim

The extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM) with nucleon and its chiral partner can describe the production of omega and eta mesons in proton-proton scattering, but the model overestimates the cross sections at higher energies and for eta production even at threshold, indicating that additional mechanisms such as three-meson vertices or vertex form factors are needed.

## Method

The authors use an effective chiral model (eLSM) with global chiral symmetry, including nucleon (N) and its chiral partner (N*) coupled to Nf=2 multiplets of (pseudo)scalar and (axial-)vector mesons. They compute total cross sections for pp → ppX (X = ω or η) using Feynman diagrams with 36 diagrams per current (9 exchange mesons, t- and u-channels, early and late emission), considering either only the nucleonic current or the coherent sum of nucleonic and resonant currents.

## Key numbers

- For pp → ppω: calculations using only the nucleonic current underestimate data points near the production threshold; including the resonant contribution leads to significant improvement of the description near threshold
- For pp → ppω: at higher energies, both nucleonic-only and resonant calculations tend to overestimate the data points
- For pp → ppη: the difference between nucleonic and resonant calculations is not significant; both curves overestimate the data considerably even at threshold

## Context

This work extends the eLSM framework previously developed by Susanna et al. (2014), Denis et al. (2014), and others, applying it to meson production in nucleon-nucleon scattering for the first time. The results enable further refinement of the model by including three-meson vertices and vertex form factors, which are expected to improve agreement with experimental data from Balestra et al. (referenced in the paper) at higher energies.
