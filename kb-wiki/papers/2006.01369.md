# Quantum Simulation of Nuclear Inelastic Scattering

> We present a time-dependent quantum algorithm for nuclear inelastic scattering in the time-dependent basis function on qubits approach. This algorithm aims to quantum simulate a subset of the nuclear inelastic scattering problems that are of physical interest, in which the internal degrees of freedom of the reaction system are excited by time-dependent external interactions. We expect that our algorithm will enable an exponential speedup in simulating the dynamics of the subset of the inelastic scattering problems, which would also be advantageous for the applications to more complicated scattering problems. For a demonstration problem, we solve for the Coulomb excitation of the deuteron, where the quantum simulations are performed with IBM Qiskit.

## Key claim

The authors present a time-dependent quantum algorithm (tBFQ) for simulating a subset of nuclear inelastic scattering problems where internal degrees of freedom are excited by time-dependent external interactions, and they expect an exponential speedup over classical algorithms for this subset, but only under the assumptions that the interaction can be factorized into time-dependent scalar coefficients and time-independent operators, and that the number of such interaction terms is small.

## Method

The authors discretize the scattering time into short steps and apply first-order Trotterization to decompose the full time-evolution operator into a product of time-independent unitaries for each step. Within the basis representation, they diagonalize the reference Hamiltonian and each time-independent interaction operator separately, then construct the time-evolution operator for each step as a product of a time-independent diagonal unitary (from the reference Hamiltonian) and a sequence of unitaries of the form \(U_i U_{i,d}(t_k) U_i^\dagger\) for each interaction term, where \(U_i\) is the fixed transformation to the eigenbasis of the interaction operator and \(U_{i,d}(t_k)\) is a diagonal unitary whose entries depend explicitly on the scattering time through the scalar coefficient \(a_i(t_k)\). The complete circuit is built by concatenating these time-step modules, with the time-dependent diagonal gates updated by simply changing the scattering-time parameter while the circuit structure remains fixed.

## Key numbers

* The algorithm requires \(\lceil \log_2 N_d \rceil\) qubits to represent a basis space of dimension \(N_d\).
* The first-order Trotterization accuracy is up to \((\Delta t)^2\), requiring \(||\hat{H}(t_i)|| \cdot \Delta t \ll 1\) for all times \(t_i\).
* The complete circuit size is bounded by \(poly(\log_2 N_d, T, D_{\max}, 1/\Delta t, 1/\epsilon)\), where \(T = t_f - t_0\) is the total scattering duration, \(D_{\max}\) is the maximal sparsity of the transformation unitaries, and \(\epsilon\) is the simulation precision.
* Classical algorithms for the same initial-value problem have complexity approximated by \((N_d)^2 \times (T/\Delta t)\) (matrix-vector multiplications per time step times number of steps).

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent basis function (tBF) approach previously demonstrated on classical computers for Coulomb excitation of the deuteron (Du 2018, Du 2017) and successfully applied to reproduce experimental results (Yin 2019). The tBFQ algorithm aims to enable quantum simulations of nuclear inelastic scattering problems that would be intractable on classical computers, and the authors note the treatment is adaptable for atomic and molecular quantum scattering processes (Bates 2012). The approach contrasts with lattice representations (Kogut 1979) and particle number representations (Ortiz 2002, McArdle 2020) by using a basis representation that can incorporate both bound and scattering channels of nuclear systems.
