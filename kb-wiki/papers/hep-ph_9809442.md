# J/ψSuppression in Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> We propose a model for the production and suppression of J/ψmesons in high-energy hadronic collisions. We factorized the process into a production of the $c\bar{c}$ pairs of relative momentum $k=k_c-k_{\bar{c}}$ convoluted with a transition probability from the produced $c\bar{c}$ pairs into the observed J/$ψ$ mesons. As the produced $c\bar{c}$ pairs exit the nuclear matter, the multiple scattering between the colored $c$ and $\bar{c}$ and the nuclear medium increases the square of the relative momentum between the $c$ and $\bar{c}, q^2=-k^2$, such that some of the $c\bar{c}$ pairs gain enough invariant mass to transmute into open charm states. With only one parameter, the amount of energy gained by the produced $c\bar{c}$ pair per unit length in the nuclear medium, our model can fit all observed J/ψsuppression data including recent NA50 data from Pb-Pb collisions.

# J/ψ Suppression in Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

## Key claim

The observed J/ψ suppression in nucleus-nucleus collisions, including the strong suppression seen by NA50 in Pb-Pb collisions at SPS energies, can be explained by a single-parameter model where multiple scattering of the colored c and cbar pair in nuclear matter increases their relative momentum squared, pushing some pairs above the open charm threshold. The model claims to fit all observed suppression data, but the authors note that the separation of multiple scattering from J/ψ formation is only justified when the J/ψ formation length exceeds the effective medium length.

## Method

The authors factorize the total J/ψ production cross section into a perturbatively calculable part for c-cbar pair production convoluted with a transition probability F(q^2) for a color-averaged c-cbar pair of relative momentum squared q^2 to evolve into a physical J/ψ meson. They propose three parameterizations for this transition probability: a constant (Color Evaporation Model), a Gaussian (Color-Singlet Model), and a power-law (related to Color-Octet Model). For nucleus-nucleus collisions, the effect of multiple scattering is represented by shifting q^2 to q^2 + ε^2 L(A,B), where ε^2 is the squared relative momentum gained per unit length of nuclear medium and L(A,B) is the effective nuclear medium length.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass set to m_c = 1.50 GeV; open charm threshold mass set to m' = 1.869 GeV
- ε^2 estimated from di-jet data in pion-nucleus collisions: 0.2-0.5 GeV^2 per unit length of nuclear medium
- For the Gaussian parameterization, the effective absorption cross section is σ_abs ~ 5.9 mb, matching the value obtained by NA50 (1997)
- Best-fit ε^2 values: 0.45 GeV^2/fm (constant model), 0.29 GeV^2/fm (Gaussian model), 0.25 GeV^2/fm (power-law model)
- Normalization factor f_J/ψ: 0.248 (constant), 0.470 (Gaussian), 0.485 (power-law)
- α_F parameter: 0 (constant), 1.2 GeV (Gaussian), 1.0 (power-law)

## Context

This work extends the mechanism proposed by Benesh, Qiu and Vary (1994) for J/ψ suppression in hadron-nucleus collisions to nucleus-nucleus collisions, specifically addressing the stronger suppression observed by the NA50 Collaboration (1997) in Pb-Pb collisions at SPS energies. The model contrasts with the conventional Glauber theory approach, which the authors argue (citing Kharzeev, 1997) cannot generate enough suppression for heavy nucleus-nucleus collisions. The power-law parameterization, which the authors prefer, predicts more suppression than the Gaussian parameterization (equivalent to Glauber theory) and enables distinguishing between different J/ψ production mechanisms through their suppression patterns. The work defers detailed treatment of collider energies to a future publication.
