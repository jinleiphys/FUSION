# Finite-amplitude method: An extension to the covariant density functionals

> The finite-amplitude method (FAM) is one of the most promising methods for optimizing the computational performance of the random-phase approximation (RPA) calculations in deformed nuclei. In this report, we will mainly focus on our recent progress in the self-consistent relativistic RPA established by using the FAM. It is found that the effects of Dirac sea can be taken into account implicitly in the coordinate-space representation and the rearrangement terms due to the density-dependent couplings can be treated without extra computational costs.

## Key claim

The finite-amplitude method (FAM) can be extended to covariant density functionals for self-consistent relativistic random-phase approximation (RPA) calculations, with the effects of the Dirac sea taken into account implicitly in the coordinate-space representation and the rearrangement terms due to density-dependent couplings treated without extra computational costs.

## Method

The authors implement both an iterative finite-amplitude method (i-FAM) and a matrix finite-amplitude method (m-FAM) within the point-coupling relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory, restricted to spherical nuclei. The FAM avoids explicit construction of the residual interaction matrices by using finite differences to evaluate the induced mean-field changes from small density deviations, solving the linear response equation iteratively in coordinate space. The radial FAM equations for monopole responses are solved as standard linear algebraic equations of the form \(\mathcal{A}\vec x = \vec b\), where the Hamiltonian is recalculated for each set of forward and backward amplitudes \(\{X_i(r)\}\) and \(\{Y_i(r)\}\).

## Key numbers

- The isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) in \(^{208}\)Pb is calculated using the density-dependent point-coupling RMF parametrization DD-PC1, with the experimental centroid energy from Youngblood (2004) used as a reference.
- The Dirac sea shows profound effects on the centroid energy of the ISGMR in \(^{208}\)Pb; the experimental data is reproduced only when the Dirac sea is taken into account.
- The i-FAM results for ISGMR in \(^{208}\)Pb are exactly on top of the m-FAM results that include the Dirac sea, confirming the equivalence of the two schemes.
- The rearrangement effects on the centroid energies of ISGMR in \(^{208}\)Pb are also substantial, as shown by comparing m-FAM calculations with and without rearrangement terms.

## Context

This work extends the finite-amplitude method, originally proposed by Nakatsukasa (2007) for non-relativistic Skyrme energy density functionals, to the covariant density functional theory (CDFT) framework. It addresses the computational challenge of conventional RPA calculations in deformed nuclei, where the number of particle-hole configurations becomes huge, and builds upon prior self-consistent relativistic RPA work by Liang (2008) that was restricted to spherical symmetry. The method enables future fully self-consistent deformed relativistic (Q)RPA calculations, which were previously only developed by Pena Arteaga (2008) in the relativistic framework.
