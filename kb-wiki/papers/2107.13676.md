# Possible studies of gluon transversity in the spin-1 deuteron at hadron-accelerator facilities

> Chiral-odd gluon transversity distribution could shed light on a new aspect of hadron physics. Although we had much progress recently on quark transversity distributions, there is no experimental measurement on the gluon transversity. The gluon trasversity does not exist in the spin-1/2 nucleons and it exists in the spin-1 deuteron. Therefore, it could probe new hadron physics in the deuteron beyond the basic bound system of a proton and a neutron because the nucleons cannot contribute directly. Here, we explain that the gluon transversity can be measured at hadron accelerator facilities, such as Fermilab and NICA, in addition to charged-lepton scattering measurements at lepton accelerator facilities by showing cross sections of the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process as an example.

## Key claim

The gluon transversity distribution, which is unique to spin-1 hadrons like the deuteron and cannot exist in spin-1/2 nucleons, can be measured at hadron-accelerator facilities such as Fermilab and NICA via the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process, provided that the linear-polarization asymmetry of the deuteron is used to isolate the signal from other tensor-polarization contributions.

## Method

The authors derived the theoretical formalism for the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process \(p+d \to \mu^+\mu^-+X\) with a linearly-polarized deuteron target. They calculated the differential cross section for the linear-polarization asymmetry \(d\sigma(E_x)-d\sigma(E_y)\) by integrating over the momentum fraction of partons in the unpolarized proton, using the CTEQ14 unpolarized PDFs for the proton and the NNPDF1.1 gluon transversity distribution (with the bold assumption that the longitudinally-polarized gluon distribution equals the gluon transversity). The cross section and spin asymmetry were evaluated for Fermilab kinematics with a 120 GeV proton beam.

## Key numbers

* The cross section for the linear-polarization asymmetry \(d\sigma(E_x)-d\sigma(E_y)\) was calculated for the Fermilab kinematics with \(p_p = 120\) GeV, taking \(\phi=0\), \(y=0.5\), and \(q_T=0.5\) or 1.0 GeV as a function of the dimuon-mass squared \(M_{\mu\mu}^2\).
* The spin asymmetry \(d\sigma(E_x-E_y)/d\sigma(E_x+E_y)\) is typically a few percent for the considered kinematics.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for gluon transversity studies, which previously existed only for charged-lepton scattering at JLab (JLab-gluon-trans), to hadron-accelerator facilities. The formalism enables independent and complementary measurements at Fermilab (Fermilab-E1039 experiment) and NICA, in addition to the planned JLab measurements. The authors note that the deuteron is a weak bound system of a proton and a neutron, but nucleons cannot contribute directly to the gluon transversity, so a finite experimental signal would indicate new hadron physics beyond the simple nucleonic picture.
