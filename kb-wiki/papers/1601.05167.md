# Beyond Wigner's isobaric multiplet mass equation: Effect of charge-symmetry-breaking interaction and Coulomb polarization

> The quadratic form of the isobaric multiplet mass equation (IMME), which was originally suggested by Wigner and has been generally regarded as valid, is seriously questioned by recent high-precision nuclear mass measurements. The usual resolution to this problem is to add empirically the cubic and quartic $T_z$-terms to characterize the deviations from the IMME, but finding the origin of these terms remains an unsolved difficulty. Based on a strategy beyond the Wigner's first-order perturbation, we derive explicitly the cubic and quartic $T_z$-terms. These terms are shown to be generated by the effective charge-symmetry breaking and charge-independent breaking interactions in nuclear medium combined with the Coulomb polarization effect. Calculations for the $sd$- and lower $fp$-shells explore a systematical emergence of the cubic $T_z$-term, suggesting a general deviation from the original IMME. Intriguingly, the magnitude of the deviation exhibits an oscillation-like behavior with mass number, modulated by the shell effect.

## Key claim

The cubic and quartic \(T_z\)-terms that empirically characterize deviations from Wigner's quadratic isobaric multiplet mass equation (IMME) are derived microscopically from the combined effect of charge-symmetry-breaking (CSB) and charge-independence-breaking (CIB) interactions in nuclear medium together with Coulomb polarization, going beyond the first-order perturbation approximation on which the original IMME is based.

## Method

The authors employ the generalized IMME (GIMME) framework, which separates Coulomb and nuclear contributions to the mass excess. The CSB and CIB interactions are extracted using the microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach with bare AV18 and AV14 interactions. Nucleonic density distributions are computed within a Skyrme energy-density functional approach (SLy4 interaction). The \(d\) and \(e\) coefficients for \(T=3/2\) quartets and \(T=2\) quintets are derived from integrals involving density-dependent symmetry energies and core density differences between members of the isobaric multiplets.

## Key numbers

- For \(T=3/2\) quartets across the \(sd\) and lower \(fp\) shells, calculated \(d\) values exhibit an oscillation-like behavior with a minimum of approximately \(-6\) keV and a maximum of approximately \(7\) keV, with extrema occurring across magic numbers 8, 20, and 28.
- For the \(A=21\) quartet, the calculated \(d\) value is \(3.4\) keV, compared to the experimental value of \(6.7(13)\) keV; shell-model calculations with USDA and USDB Hamiltonians give \(d=-0.3\) keV and \(0.3\) keV, respectively.
- For the \(A=53\) quartet, the initially reported experimental \(d=39(11)\) keV was later reduced and now agrees with the calculated prediction.
- For \(T=2\) quintets, calculated \(d\) values do not exceed 2 keV; for \(A=20\), the calculated \(d=1.7\) keV agrees with the later experimental measurement that revalidated the IMME for this quintet.
- Calculated and experimental \(e\) values for \(T=2\) quintets are compatible with zero.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previously proposed GIMME framework (Dong 2017) to explain the origin of higher-order terms in the IMME, which had been questioned by recent high-precision mass measurements (e.g., Mass20, Mass53). Prior shell-model calculations using isospin-nonconserving Hamiltonians (Brown 2005, Kaneko 2013, Kaneko 2014) and valence-space calculations from chiral effective field theory could not reproduce the experimental \(d\) values. The results show that the magnitude of deviation from the quadratic IMME is governed by shell effects, enabling predictions of where strong violations are expected and guiding future precision mass measurements.
