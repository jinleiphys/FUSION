# Probing the hadron-quark mixed phase at high isospin and baryon density - Sensitive observables

> We discuss the isospin effect on the possible phase transition from hadronic to quark matter at high baryon density and finite temperatures. The two-Equation of State (Two-EoS) model is adopted to describe the hadron-quark phase transition in dense matter formed in heavy-ion collisions. For the hadron sector we use Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) effective models, already tested on heavy ion collision (HIC). For the quark phase we consider various effective models, the MIT-Bag static picture, the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) approach with chiral dynamics and finally the NJL coupled to the Polyakov-loop field (PNJL), which includes both chiral and (de)confinement dynamics. The idea is to extract mixed phase properties which appear robust with respect to the model differences. In particular we focus on the phase transitions of isospin asymmetric matter, with two main results: i) an earlier transition to a mixed hadron-quark phase, at lower baryon density/chemical potential with respect to symmetric matter; ii) an "Isospin Distillation" to the quark component of the mixed phase, with predicted effects on the final hadron production. Possible observation signals are suggested to probe in heavy-ion collision experiments at intermediate energies, in the range of the NICA program.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, the hadron-to-quark phase transition should occur at lower baryon densities than in symmetric matter, and that the quark component of the mixed phase will become enriched in isospin (more neutron-rich), but these predictions depend on the assumption that quark effective models lack the repulsive isovector interaction terms present in the hadron sector.

## Method

The authors employ a Two-Equation of State (Two-EoS) model to describe the hadron-quark phase transition in dense matter formed in heavy-ion collisions. For the hadron sector they use Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) effective models already tested on heavy ion collisions; for the quark sector they compare the MIT-Bag model, the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) approach, and the NJL coupled to the Polyakov-loop field (PNJL). They apply Gibbs conditions (thermal, chemical, and mechanical equilibrium) to construct the mixed phase and compute the evolution of asymmetry parameters in each phase as a function of quark fraction.

## Key numbers

- The onset of the mixed phase in asymmetric matter (global asymmetry parameter α=0.2) moves from 6.5 down to 4.5 times nuclear saturation density ρ₀, compared to symmetric matter.
- In the NJL model with only chiral dynamics, no physical solution exists for temperatures above approximately 80 MeV; with the Hadron-PNJL model this temperature is enhanced to about 166 MeV.
- The expected Critical-End-Point in the Hadron-PNJL scheme appears at a quark chemical potential μ_q = μ_B/3 ≈ 200 MeV.
- In uranium-uranium collisions at 1 AGeV with semicentral impact parameter b=7 fm, the simulation shows baryon density around 3-4 times ρ₀, temperature 50-60 MeV, energy density 500 MeV fm⁻³, and proton fraction between 0.35 and 0.40.
- The best region to observe the mixed phase is at temperatures T ∼ 50-100 MeV, chemical potentials μ_B ∼ 1200-1800 MeV (densities ρ ∼ 3-6 ρ₀), accessible at beam energies 3-10 AGeV (√s_NN ∼ 3-5 GeV).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the hadron-quark phase transition in neutron stars (Glendenning 1992, Burgio 2002, Shao 2010, 2011) and in heavy-ion collisions (Di Toro 2006, 2009, Liu 2011) by focusing specifically on isospin effects at intermediate energies accessible to the NICA program. The paper proposes that the "Isospin Distillation" effect, where the quark phase becomes more neutron-rich than the coexisting hadron phase, can be probed through observables such as elliptic flow, n_q-scaling of particle flows, and ratios of neutron-rich to neutron-poor hadrons (n/p, ³H/³He, π⁻/π⁺, K⁰/K⁺) at high transverse momentum. The results also constrain vector interaction strengths in quark models (ratio R_V = G_V/G in the range 0.1-0.3) needed to support massive hybrid neutron stars (Shao 2012, 2013).
