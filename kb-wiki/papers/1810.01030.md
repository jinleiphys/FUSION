# Simulating the Chiral Magnetic Wave in a Box System

> The chiral magnetic wave from the interplay between the chiral magnetic effect and the chiral separation effect is simulated in a box system with the periodic boundary condition based on the chiral kinetic equations of motion. Simulation results are compared with available limits from theoretical derivations, and effects of the temperature, the magnetic field, and the specific shear viscosity on the key properties of the chiral magnetic wave are discussed. Our study serves as a baseline for further simulations of chiral anomalies in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The chiral magnetic wave (CMW) arising from the interplay of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) and the chiral separation effect (CSE) is simulated in a controlled cubic box with periodic boundary conditions, but unavoidable momentum-space truncations cause the simulated CME and CSE currents to be underestimated by about 25–50% compared to the theoretical limit, so the phase velocity of the CMW is also systematically lower than the analytic prediction.

## Method

Massless spin-1/2 particles with charge numbers +1 and -1 are initialized in a cubic box (side length 10 fm) with an isotropic Fermi-Dirac momentum distribution and a sinusoidal density profile along the magnetic field direction. The particles evolve under the chiral kinetic equations of motion (including Berry curvature and phase-space modification factor sqrt(G)) in a uniform, constant magnetic field, with elastic scatterings treated via a stochastic method using an isotropic cross section determined from the specific shear viscosity. The time evolution of the right-handed and left-handed particle densities is fitted to the analytic CMW solution to extract the phase velocity and the diffusion constant.

## Key numbers

- Box side length: 2l = 10 fm.
- Magnetic field strength: eB_y = 0.5 GeV/fm (in the main CMW study).
- Temperature: T = 0.3 GeV (in the main CMW study).
- Specific shear viscosity: eta/s = 0.08 hbar (in the main CMW study).
- Initial chiral asymmetry: A_c = (rho_R - rho_L)/n = 0.1.
- Wave number of the CMW: beta = pi/l.
- Time step: Delta t = 0.01 fm/c.
- With truncation k_min = 0.3 GeV/c, the simulated CME and CSE currents are only about half of the theoretical limit; with truncation 0.3 < sqrt(G) < 1.7, the underestimation is about 25–50%.
- Phase velocity from Eq. (v_p_t): v_p = (3 hbar e B) / (2 pi^2 T^2).
- Cross section range from specific shear viscosity: sigma_22 in (1.74, 4.36) hbar^2 / T^2, where sigma_22 is in fm^2 and T is in GeV.

## Context

This work extends the box-simulation methodology previously used to study gluon thermalization (stochastic method), specific shear viscosity of hadron gas (Dem09), and equilibrium properties of nucleons (Zha18) to the chiral anomaly domain. It provides a calibrated baseline for future anomalous transport simulations in realistic heavy-ion collision geometries, complementing hydrodynamic approaches (Hydro1, Hydro2, Liao) and earlier transport studies using blast-wave initial conditions (sun1, sun2). The results enable quantitative comparison with the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD calculation (K_CMW, ss) for the magnetic-field and temperature dependence of the CMW phase velocity and diffusion constant.
