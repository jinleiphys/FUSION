# Nucleon Effective E-Mass in Neutron-Rich Matter from the Migdal-Luttinger Jump

> The well-known Migdal-Luttinger theorem states that the jump of the single-nucleon momentum distribution at the Fermi surface is equal to the inverse of the nucleon effective E-mass. Recent experiments studying short-range correlations (SRC) in nuclei using electron-nucleus scatterings at the Jefferson National Laboratory (JLAB) together with model calculations constrained significantly the Migdal-Luttinger jump at saturation density of nuclear matter. We show that the corresponding nucleon effective E-mass is consequently constrained to $M_0^{\ast,\rm{E}}/M\approx2.22\pm0.35$ in symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) and the E-mass of neutrons is smaller than that of protons in neutron-rich matter. Moreover, the average depletion of the nucleon Fermi sea increases (decreases) approximately linearly with the isospin asymmetry $δ$ according to $κ_{\rm{p}/\rm{n}}\approx 0.21\pm0.06 \pm (0.19\pm0.08)δ$ for protons (neutrons). These results will help improve our knowledge about the space-time non-locality of the single-nucleon potential in neutron-rich nucleonic matter useful in both nuclear physics and astrophysics.

## Key claim

Using the Migdal-Luttinger theorem, the nucleon effective E-mass in symmetric nuclear matter is constrained to approximately 2.22 plus or minus 0.35 based on recent JLAB experiments and model calculations of short-range correlations, though the result depends on the largely uncertain parameter characterizing the isospin-dependence of the momentum distribution near the Fermi surface.

## Method

The authors parameterized the single-nucleon momentum distribution in cold asymmetric nuclear matter with a piecewise function: a constant plus quadratic momentum-dependent depletion below the Fermi momentum, and a C/k^4 high-momentum tail above it. They extracted the parameters from experimental constraints on the high-momentum tail fraction from JLAB electron-nucleus scattering data and from microscopic many-body theory predictions for pure neutron matter, then applied the Migdal-Luttinger theorem relating the discontinuity at the Fermi surface to the inverse of the nucleon E-mass.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon effective E-mass in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density: M_0^*,E/M ≈ 2.22 ± 0.35
- Average depletion of the Fermi sea in symmetric nuclear matter: κ ≈ 0.21 ± 0.06
- Proton depletion as function of isospin asymmetry δ: κ_p ≈ 0.21 ± 0.06 + (0.19 ± 0.08)δ
- Neutron depletion as function of isospin asymmetry δ: κ_n ≈ 0.21 ± 0.06 - (0.19 ± 0.08)δ
- High-momentum tail amplitude in symmetric nuclear matter: C_0 ≈ 0.161 ± 0.015
- High-momentum tail fraction in symmetric nuclear matter: x_HMT_SNM = 28% ± 4%
- High-momentum tail fraction in pure neutron matter: x_HMT_PNM = 1.5% ± 0.5%
- Neutron-proton depletion splitting coefficient: κ_n - κ_p ≈ (-0.37 ± 0.16)δ

## Context

This work extends the application of the Migdal-Luttinger theorem (Migdal 1957, Luttinger 1960) to extract the nucleon E-mass from experimentally constrained momentum distributions, complementing earlier theoretical predictions using methods such as correlated basis functions (Krotscheck 1981, Jackson 1982), Green's function methods (Blaizot 1981), and Dirac-Brueckner approaches (Jong 1991). The results provide empirical constraints on the space-time non-locality of the single-nucleon potential in neutron-rich matter, which is relevant for improving nuclear many-body theories and for applications to neutron star cooling and transport properties.
