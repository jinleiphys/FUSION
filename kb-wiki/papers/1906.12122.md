# Nuclear effective field theory: status and perspectives

> The nuclear physics landscape has been redesigned as a sequence of effective field theories (EFTs) connected to the Standard Model through symmetries and lattice simulations of Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD). EFTs in this sequence are expansions around different low-energy limits of QCD, each with its own characteristics, scales, and ranges of applicability regarding energy and number of nucleons. We review each of the three main nuclear EFTs -- Chiral, Pionless, Halo/Cluster -- highlighting their similarities, differences, and connections. In doing so, we survey the structural properties and reactions of nuclei that have been derived from the ab initio solution of the few- and many-body problem built upon EFT input.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the three main nuclear effective field theories (Chiral, Pionless, and Halo/Cluster) as a sequence of EFTs connected to QCD through symmetries, each with its own range of applicability regarding momentum scale and pion mass, but the authors emphasize conceptual similarities and differences rather than phenomenological successes, and note that the exact breakdown values of momentum and pion mass for Chiral EFT are not well known.

## Method

The authors survey the theoretical foundations of nuclear EFTs, starting from QCD symmetries and the construction of effective Lagrangians constrained only by those symmetries, with low-energy constants encoding short-distance QCD dynamics. They describe how each EFT (Chiral, Pionless, Halo/Cluster) is formulated as an expansion in Q/M_hi and M_lo/M_hi, where the potential is defined as the sum of irreducible diagrams and the full T-matrix is obtained by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger or Schrodinger equation. The review covers renormalization-group invariance, power counting, and the treatment of reducible diagrams that distinguish A=1 from A>=2 processes.

## Key numbers

* The nucleon mass m_N is approximately 940 MeV, setting the scale of nonperturbative QCD phenomena at M_QCD ~ 1 GeV.
* The pion mass m_pi is approximately 140 MeV at the physical point, with a pion mass splitting delta m_pi^2 = m_pi+/-^2 - m_pi0^2 of about (36 MeV)^2.
* The neutron-proton mass difference delta m_N = m_n - m_p is approximately 1.3 MeV.
* The common quark mass bar-m = (m_u + m_d)/2 is approximately 5 MeV, with relative mass splitting epsilon = (m_d - m_u)/(m_u + m_d) approximately 1/3.
* For Chiral EFT, the scale M_NN is approximately 270 MeV at the physical pion mass, below which pions are expected to be perturbative.
* Nuclear saturation at physical pion mass gives B_A/A approximately 10 MeV and nuclear radii R_A ~ R_0 A^{1/3} with R_0 approximately 1.2 fm.
* For A=0 (Chiral Perturbation Theory), the breakdown scale M_hi is less than or equal to 500 MeV.

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on Chiral EFT by Weinberg (1990-1992), Pionless EFT by Bedaque, van Kolck, Kaplan, and others (1997-1998), and Halo/Cluster EFT by Bertulani, Bedaque, and collaborators (2002-2003). It connects these three EFTs into a unified framework spanning from QCD scales down to halo nuclei near the driplines, and discusses recent extensions to deformed heavy nuclei initiated by Papenbrock (2010). The paper enables systematic error estimation in nuclear structure calculations and provides a bridge between lattice QCD simulations and nuclear observables.
