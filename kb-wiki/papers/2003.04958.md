# Virial coefficients of trapped and un-trapped three-component fermions with three-body forces in arbitrary spatial dimensions

> Using a coarse temporal lattice approximation, we calculate the first few terms of the virial expansion of a three-species fermion system with a three-body contact interaction in $d$ spatial dimensions, both in homogeneous space as well as in a harmonic trapping potential of frequency $ω$. Using the three-body problem to renormalize, we report analytic results for the change in the fourth- and fifth-order virial coefficients $Δb_4$ and $Δb_5$ as functions of $Δb_3$. Additionally, we argue that in the $ω\to 0$ limit the relationship $b_n^\text{T} = n^{-d/2} b_n$ holds between the trapped (T) and homogeneous coefficients for arbitrary temperature and coupling strength (not merely in scale-invariant regimes). Finally, we point out an exact, universal (coupling- and frequency-independent) relationship between $Δb_3^\text{T}$ in 1D with three-body forces and $Δb_2^\text{T}$ in 2D with two-body forces.

## Key claim

Using a coarse temporal lattice approximation at leading order (LO-SCLA), the authors report analytic results for the fourth- and fifth-order virial coefficients Δb₄ and Δb₅ as functions of Δb₃ for a three-species fermion system with a three-body contact interaction, but these results are approximate and valid primarily at weak coupling where the approximation performs best.

## Method

The authors implement a leading-order semiclassical lattice approximation (LO-SCLA) by taking a Trotter-Suzuki factorization of the Boltzmann weight and keeping only the leading term, which is equivalent to setting the commutator [T, V] = 0. They calculate the canonical partition functions Q_{MNL} for systems of up to five particles (three flavors) in d spatial dimensions, both in homogeneous space and in a harmonic trapping potential of frequency ω. The three-body problem is used to renormalize the theory, with Δb₃ serving as the renormalized dimensionless coupling constant.

## Key numbers

- Δb₄ = -3 / 2^(d/2) × Δb₃ for the homogeneous system in d dimensions
- Δb₅ = 3 × (1/2^d + 1/3^(d/2)) × Δb₃ for the homogeneous system in d dimensions
- In the βω → 0 limit, Δb₃^T = 3^(-d/2) × Δb₃, Δb₄^T = 4^(-d/2) × Δb₄, and Δb₅^T = 5^(-d/2) × Δb₅
- For the harmonically trapped case in 1D with three-body forces and 2D with two-body forces: Δb₃^T = (2/3) × Δb₂^(T,2b2D), valid for all couplings and all values of βω
- Δb₃^T = (C/λ_T^(2d)) × (1/3^(d/2+1)) × (βω/sinh(βω))^d for the trapped system

## Context

This work extends the LO-SCLA approach previously applied to two-component Fermi gases by Drut et al. (2018) and to harmonically trapped systems by Hou and Drut (2019) to the case of three-component fermions with three-body contact interactions. The authors establish a universal relationship between trapped and homogeneous virial coefficients (b_n^T = n^(-d/2) b_n in the ω → 0 limit) that they argue holds for arbitrary temperature and coupling strength, not merely in scale-invariant regimes as previously shown by Liu (2013) and others. The work also connects to the exact relationship between the 1D three-body problem and the 2D two-body problem pointed out by Drut et al. (2018), enabling future calculations of thermodynamics and Tan's contact for these systems across dimensions.
