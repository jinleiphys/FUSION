# Lepton-Proton Two-Photon Exchange in Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We use heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to evaluate the two-photon exchange corrections to the low-energy elastic lepton-proton scattering at next-to-leading order accuracy, i.e., ${\mathcal O}(α, M^{-1})$, including a non-zero lepton mass. We consider the elastic proton intermediate state in the two-photon exchange together in the soft photon approximation. The infrared singular contributions are projected out using dimensional regularization. The resulting infrared singularity-free two-photon exchange contribution is in good numerical agreement with existing predictions based on standard diagrammatic soft photon approximation evaluations.

## Key claim

The authors use heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) to evaluate two-photon exchange (TPE) corrections to elastic lepton-proton scattering at next-to-leading order (NLO) accuracy, i.e., O(α, M⁻¹), including a non-zero lepton mass. The resulting infrared singularity-free TPE contribution shows good numerical agreement with existing predictions based on standard diagrammatic soft photon approximation evaluations, but only for the elastic proton intermediate state and within the soft photon approximation.

## Method

The authors employed heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory to analytically evaluate the TPE box, cross-box, and seagull Feynman diagrams at O(e⁴) for elastic lepton-proton scattering. They used dimensional regularization to project out infrared singular contributions from the TPE box diagrams, and applied the soft photon approximation as defined by Mo and Tsai (1968), setting soft photon momenta to zero in both numerator and denominator of the loop integrands. The calculations were performed in the laboratory frame with kinematics matching the proposed MUSE experiment at PSI, using incident lepton momenta of 115, 153, and 210 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- The MUSE experiment will measure elastic μ±p and e±p cross sections at momentum transfers as low as |Q²| ~ 0.002 to 0.08 (GeV/c)², with a projected accuracy of less than 1%
- For electron scattering at incident momentum 0.115 GeV/c, |Q²| ranges from 0.0016 (GeV/c)² at θ=20° to 0.027 (GeV/c)² at θ=100°
- For electron scattering at incident momentum 0.210 GeV/c, |Q²| ranges from 0.0052 (GeV/c)² at θ=20° to 0.082 (GeV/c)² at θ=100°
- For muon scattering at incident momentum 0.115 GeV/c, |Q²| ranges from 0.0016 (GeV/c)² at θ=20° to 0.026 (GeV/c)² at θ=100°
- For muon scattering at incident momentum 0.210 GeV/c, |Q²| ranges from 0.0052 (GeV/c)² at θ=20° to 0.080 (GeV/c)² at θ=100°
- The proton mass used is M = 938.28 MeV

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of two-photon exchange corrections to elastic lepton-proton scattering by applying heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, providing a systematic, model-independent framework for low-energy evaluations. It builds upon earlier radiative correction analyses by Tsai (1961), Mo and Tsai (1968), and Maximon and Tjon (2000), which used phenomenological form factors, and is directly relevant to the MUSE experiment at PSI, which aims to resolve the proton radius puzzle by measuring elastic μ±p and e±p cross sections simultaneously. The HBχPT approach enables controlled uncertainty estimates through its power counting scheme and is particularly suited for the low-Q² regime (Q/Λχ ≪ 1) where the MUSE experiment will operate.
