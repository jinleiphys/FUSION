# Thermal Broadening of Bottomonia: Lattice Non-Relativistic QCD with Extended Operators

> We present lattice non-relativistic QCD calculations of bottomonium correlation functions at temperatures $T \simeq 150-350$ MeV. The correlation functions were computed using extended bottomonium operators, and on background gauge-field configurations for 2+1-flavor QCD having physical kaon and nearly-physical pion masses. We analyzed these correlation functions based on simple theoretically-motivated parameterizations of the corresponding spectral functions. The results of our analyses are compatible with significant in-medium thermal broadening of the ground state S- and P-wave bottomonia.

## Key claim

The results are compatible with significant in-medium thermal broadening of ground state S- and P-wave bottomonia, based on analyses of lattice correlation functions using simple theoretically-motivated parameterizations of spectral functions, but the authors cannot claim that the effective masses approach free theory values at high temperatures due to cutoff effects.

## Method

The authors performed lattice non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) calculations of bottomonium correlation functions on 2+1-flavor gauge configurations with physical kaon and nearly-physical pion masses, using extended (Gaussian-smeared) bottomonium operators in addition to point sources. They analyzed the correlation functions by constructing effective masses and fitting zero-temperature subtracted correlators with a parameterization of the in-medium spectral function that includes a Breit-Wigner (Lorentzian) form with a low-energy cutoff.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: T ≃ 150–350 MeV (specifically 151.1, 172.9, 199.3, 251.0, 273.1, and 333.5 MeV)
- Lattice spacings used: a = 0.0493–0.1088 fm
- Pion mass: 161 MeV in the continuum limit
- Chiral cross-over temperature: T_c = 156.5 ± 1.5 MeV
- Root mean square size of the meson source: r_RMS ≈ 0.21 fm for all lattice spacings
- Hyperfine splitting (Υ – η_b mass difference) at the finest lattice spacing (a = 0.0493 fm): 51.2(1.6) MeV, compared to PDG value of 61.3(2.3) MeV
- χ_b0 – η_b mass difference at a = 0.0493 fm: 459(11) MeV, compared to PDG value of 460.5(2.4) MeV
- χ_b1 – η_b mass difference at a = 0.0493 fm: 487(11) MeV, compared to PDG value of 493.8(2.3) MeV
- χ_b2 – η_b mass difference at a = 0.0493 fm: 505(11) MeV, compared to PDG value of 513.21(2.3) MeV
- h_b – η_b mass difference at a = 0.0493 fm: 495(11) MeV, compared to PDG value of 500.3(2.4) MeV

## Context

This work extends previous lattice NRQCD studies of bottomonium at non-zero temperature (Aarts et al., 2010–2014; Kim et al., 2014, 2018) by using extended (smeared) meson operators rather than point-like operators, which provide better projection onto ground states and greater sensitivity to in-medium modifications. The results are interpreted in analogy with static quark–anti-quark meson studies (Bazavov et al., 2014; Petreczky et al., 2017) where Hard Thermal Loop perturbation theory (Burnier et al., 2013) predicts a complex potential leading to thermal broadening; this work enables future quantitative extraction of bottomonium thermal widths and masses from lattice data using more sophisticated spectral function reconstruction methods.
