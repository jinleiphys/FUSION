# Equation of State and Collective Dynamics

> This talk summarizes the present status of a program to quantitatively relate data from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) on collective expansion flow to the Equation of State (EOS) of hot and dense strongly interacting matter, including the quark-gluon plasma and the quark-hadron phase transition. The limits reached with the present state of the art and the next steps required to make further progress will both be discussed.

# Equation of State and Collective Dynamics

## Key claim

The collective flow patterns observed at RHIC provide strong evidence that the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) created in these collisions behaves as an almost ideal fluid, but this ideal fluid behavior is only clearly manifest at RHIC because the initial energy densities and QGP lifetimes there are large enough to avoid dilution by the highly viscous hadron resonance gas that dominates expansion at lower energies.

## Method

The author solves the relativistic Euler equations for ideal fluid dynamics under the assumption of boost-invariant longitudinal expansion, using initial conditions (thermalization time ≤0.6 fm/c, initial entropy density 110 fm⁻³, initial central temperature ≈360 MeV ≈ 2Tc) and freeze-out conditions fixed by fitting pion and proton spectra from central Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 A GeV. The initial entropy density profile for all impact parameters is calculated from collision geometry using the Glauber model, and all hadron spectra for non-central collisions (including elliptic flow v₂) are then parameter-free predictions. The calculations are compared to RHIC data on transverse momentum spectra, elliptic flow, and azimuthally sensitive HBT correlations.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for QCD phase transition: Tc = 173 ± 15 MeV (from lattice QCD)
- Critical energy density: ec ≈ 0.7 GeV/fm³
- Initial thermalization time: τ₀ ≤ 0.6 fm/c
- Initial entropy density at fireball center: s₀ = 110 fm⁻³
- Initial maximal energy density: e₀ ≈ 30 GeV/fm³
- Initial central fireball temperature: T₀ ≈ 360 MeV ≈ 2 Tc
- Freeze-out energy density: edec = 0.075 GeV/fm³
- Speed of sound in QGP phase (T > 2Tc): cs² ≈ 1/3
- Speed of sound at softest point near Tc: cs² ≈ 1/20
- Speed of sound in hadron resonance gas: cs² ≈ 0.15
- Spatial eccentricity of time-integrated hydrodynamic pion emission function at b=7 fm: ⟨εx⟩ = 0.14 (56% of initial value εx(τ₀)=0.25)
- STAR measured spatial eccentricity at corresponding impact parameter: ⟨εx⟩ = 0.11 ± 0.035 (45±15% of initial deformation)
- Normalized sound attenuation length limit from Teaney: Γs/τ = (4/3Tτ)(η/s) cannot be much larger than about 0.1
- Lower bound conjecture for viscosity: η/s = ħ/(4π)
- Hydrodynamic model fails for initial energy densities less than about 10 GeV/fm³

## Context

This talk extends the program of relating RHIC collective flow data to the QCD equation of state, building on lattice QCD results from Karsch and Laermann (2004) and earlier hydrodynamic predictions by Kolb, Sorge, and Heinz (2000) that the elliptic flow excitation function should show a non-monotonic beam energy dependence due to the softening of the EOS at the phase transition. The work contrasts with perturbative QGP interpretations of lattice data, showing that the strongly coupled QGP behaves as an almost ideal fluid. The results enable a systematic program to quantitatively extract the EOS, thermalization time, and transport properties of QGP and hot hadronic matter, requiring viscous relativistic hydrodynamic codes and hybrid hydro-hadro simulations as pioneered by Teaney (2001).
