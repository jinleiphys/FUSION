# The (3+1)D structure of the dilute Glasma

> We study the (3+1)D structure of the Glasma in the dilute approximation, which allows us to describe the longitudinal dynamics that arise from the three-dimensional nuclear structure. We employ a nuclear model with tunable longitudinal and transverse fluctuation scales that generalizes the McLerran-Venugopalan model. We discuss the longitudinal profiles of the energy-momentum tensor and the transverse structure of the local rest frame energy density.

## Key claim
The dilute (3+1)D Glasma framework, expanded to leading order in color charge densities, allows calculation of the Glasma field strength tensor with fully general longitudinal dependence, but the transverse structure is governed by the infrared regulator mass rather than a saturation scale, and the Milne frame produces unphysical enhancement of the energy-momentum tensor components at large rapidities.

## Method
The authors solve classical Yang-Mills equations in covariant gauge using a dilute expansion in color charge densities \(\rho_A^m \rho_B^n\) to leading order \(m=n=1\), reducing the problem to an inhomogeneous wave equation for the Glasma field strength tensor \(f^{\mu\nu}\). They employ a three-dimensional generalization of the McLerran-Venugopalan model with tunable longitudinal correlation scale \(\xi\) and transverse infrared regulator \(m\), computing the field strength tensor numerically via Monte Carlo integration on a grid in proper time \(\tau\), spacetime rapidity \(\eta_s\), and transverse coordinates \(\mathbf{x}\).

## Key numbers
- Infrared regulator \(m = 0.2\) GeV sets the size of fluctuating transverse domains to \(\sim 1/m\) in the single-nucleus fields.
- Ultraviolet regulator \(\Lambda_\mathrm{UV} = 10\) GeV chosen for the lattice spacing.
- Results shown at proper time \(\tau = 0.4\) fm/c for Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 200\) GeV (RHIC energy).
- Longitudinal correlation scale parameter \(\xi \leq \sqrt{2}R/\gamma\), where \(\gamma = \sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}/(2m_u)\) depends on collider energy and nucleon mass.

## Context
This work extends the boost-invariant Glasma description (Lappi, 2006) by incorporating three-dimensional nuclear structure, generalizing the McLerran-Venugopalan model (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1993) with finite longitudinal extent and tunable correlation scales. The dilute approximation enables event-by-event calculations of the full (3+1)D field strength tensor, in contrast to non-perturbative approaches like IP-Glasma (Schenke et al., 2012) which develop a saturation scale \(Q_s\). The results suggest that Milne coordinates are inadequate for initial conditions with extended longitudinal structure, motivating development of alternative coordinate frameworks for heavy-ion collision simulations.
