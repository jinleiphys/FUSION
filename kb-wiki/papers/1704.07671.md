# Determination of Quark-Gluon-Plasma Parameters from a Global Bayesian Analysis

> The quality of data taken at RHIC and LHC as well as the success and sophistication of computational models for the description of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions have advanced to a level that allows for the quantitative extraction of the transport properties of the Quark-Gluon-Plasma. However, the complexity of this task as well as the computational effort associated with it can only be overcome by developing novel methodologies: in this paper we outline such an analysis based on Bayesian Statistics and systematically compare an event-by-event heavy-ion collision model to data from the Large Hadron Collider. We simultaneously probe multiple model parameters including fundamental quark-gluon plasma properties such as the temperature-dependence of the specific shear viscosity $η/s$, calibrate the model to optimally reproduce experimental data, and extract quantitative constraints for all parameters simultaneously. The method is universal and easily extensible to other data and collision models.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Bayesian model-to-data analysis can simultaneously extract multiple Quark-Gluon-Plasma (QGP) parameters from heavy-ion collision data, but the results are conditional on the specific model assumptions and the limited temperature range over which the data provide constraints; the extracted temperature dependence of the specific shear viscosity is most reliable below approximately 225 MeV.

## Method

The authors used the VISHNU event-by-event heavy-ion collision model, which combines relativistic viscous fluid dynamics with a microscopic hadronic afterburner (UrQMD), with 12 input parameters encoding initial conditions, shear and bulk viscosities, thermalization time, and transition temperature. They calibrated the model to ALICE multiplicity and flow data at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV beam energy by evaluating the model at 300 design points selected via a Latin hypercube algorithm, training Gaussian process emulators on the computed observables, and then performing Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampling to extract posterior probability distributions for all parameters simultaneously.

## Key numbers

- The TRENTO entropy deposition parameter p has a posterior distribution peaked at essentially zero with approximate 90% uncertainty of ±0.1, implying initial entropy deposition is roughly proportional to the geometric mean of participant nuclear thickness functions.
- The minimum value of the specific shear viscosity (η/s) at the transition temperature Tc = 0.154 GeV has a narrow posterior peak at 0.06, below the KSS bound of 1/4π ≈ 0.08 but consistent with it within 90% uncertainty.
- Zero η/s is excluded at the 90% level, and zero slope of the temperature dependence is also excluded at the 90% level, confirming a non-constant η/s.
- The calibrated model systematically overpredicts the mean transverse momentum of anti-protons by 10%.

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian model-to-data comparison approach pioneered by the MADAI collaboration (Petersen 2010, Novak 2013, Sangaline 2015, Bernhard 2015) to simultaneously constrain multiple QGP properties including the temperature dependence of shear viscosity. The analysis confirms that the TRENTO model with p ≈ 0 produces the correct ratio between initial state ellipticity and triangularity, consistent with earlier work by Moreland (2014), and places the EKRT and IP-Glasma initial condition models (Niemi 2015, Schenke 2012) within the peak of the posterior distribution while the KLN and wounded nucleon models fall outside. The framework enables future quantitative extractions of QGP properties including the QCD equation of state, sub-nucleonic structure, bulk viscosity, and heavy-quark transport coefficients.
