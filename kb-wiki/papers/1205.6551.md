# Structure of $^{10}_־›$Be and $^{10}_־›$B hypernuclei studied with the four-body cluster model

> The structure of the isodoublet hypernuclei, $^{10}_־›$B and $^{10}_־›$Be within the framework of an $־±+־±+־›+N$ four-body cluster model is studied. Interactions between the constituent subunits are determined so as to reproduce reasonably well the observed low-energy properties of the $־±־±$, $־±N$, $־±־›$, $־±־±־›$ and $־±־±N$ subsystems. Furthermore, the two-body $־›N$ interaction is adjusted so as to reproduce the $0^+$-$1^+$ splitting of $^4_־›$H. The $־›$ binding energies of $^{10}_־›$B and $^{10}_־›$Be are 8.76 MeV and 8.94 MeV, respectively. The energy splitting of the $1^-$-$2^-$ levels in $^{10}_־›$B is 0.08 MeV, which does not contradict the experimental report in BNL-E930. An even-state $־›N$ charge symmetry breaking (CSB) interaction determined from the A=4 systems works repulsively by +0.1 MeV (attractively by -0.1 MeV) in $^{10}_־›$Be ($^{10}_־›$B). We discuss a possibility that an odd-state CSB interaction improves the fitting to the experimental data of A=10 double $־›$ hypernuclei.

## Key claim

The calculated $1^-$-$2^-$ energy splitting in $^{10}_{\Lambda}$B is 0.08 MeV, which is less than the 100 keV detection threshold of the BNL-E930 experiment, supporting the interpretation that the absence of observed $\gamma$-ray transition is due to the splitting being too small rather than the $2^-$ state being the ground state.

## Method

The authors employed an $\alpha+\alpha+\Lambda+N$ four-body cluster model using the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) to solve the Schrֳ¶dinger equation for $^{10}_{\Lambda}$Be and $^{10}_{\Lambda}$B. Interactions between constituent subunits ($\alpha\alpha$, $\alpha N$, $\alpha\Lambda$, $\alpha\alpha\Lambda$, $\alpha\alpha N$) were determined to reproduce observed low-energy properties of these subsystems, and the two-body $\Lambda N$ interaction was adjusted to reproduce the $0^+$-$1^+$ splitting of $^4_{\Lambda}$H. The Pauli principle between nucleons in $\alpha$ clusters and the valence nucleon was treated using the orthogonality condition model (OCM).

## Key numbers

- $\Lambda$ binding energy of $^{10}_{\Lambda}$B: 8.76 MeV (experimental value: $8.89 \pm 0.12$ MeV)
- $\Lambda$ binding energy of $^{10}_{\Lambda}$Be: 8.94 MeV (experimental value: $9.11 \pm 0.22$ MeV)
- Energy splitting of the $1^-$-$2^-$ levels in $^{10}_{\Lambda}$B: 0.08 MeV
- Even-state $\Lambda N$ charge symmetry breaking (CSB) interaction works repulsively by $+0.1$ MeV in $^{10}_{\Lambda}$Be and attractively by $-0.1$ MeV in $^{10}_{\Lambda}$B
- $0^+$-$1^+$ splitting in $^{10}_{\Lambda}$Be: 0.2 MeV (final calculated value)
- $3^-$-$2^-_2$ doublet splitting in $^{10}_{\Lambda}$Be: 0.05 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous cluster-model studies of $s$- and $p$-shell hypernuclei, including $^4_{\Lambda}$H, $^7_{\Lambda}$Li, $^9_{\Lambda}$Be, and $^{13}_{\Lambda}$C (Hiyama 2000, 2006), to the $A=10$ isodoublet system. The results address the unresolved $1^-$-$2^-$ splitting in $^{10}_{\Lambda}$B from the BNL-E930 experiment, which shell-model calculations by Millener (2006, 2010) had difficulty reproducing with splitting values of 120 keV or 34 keV. The study also investigates charge symmetry breaking effects in $p$-shell hypernuclei, following earlier work on $A=7$ and $A=8$ systems (Hiyama 2006), and suggests that an odd-state CSB interaction may be needed to improve agreement with experimental data for $A=10$ double $\Lambda$ hypernuclei.
