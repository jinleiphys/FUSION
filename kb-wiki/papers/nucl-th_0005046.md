# Phi decay in nuclei

> We have studied the decay of the ϕmeson in nuclear matter by taking into account the renormalization of the K and \bar{K} mesons in the medium. We separate the contribution of the total width into different reaction channels, K\bar{K} and KYh, the latter ones associated with the ϕN \to K^+ Y reactions. We find a total width at normal nuclear matter density of about 22 MeV, considerably larger than the free one.

## Key claim

The total width of the phi meson in nuclear matter at normal nuclear density is about 22 MeV, considerably larger than the free width of 4.4 MeV, but this result depends on using a selfconsistent treatment of the kaon selfenergy and including p-wave contributions to all orders in density; the width is about a factor of two smaller than earlier estimates by Klinuc and Klilett.

## Method

The authors evaluated the phi meson selfenergy in nuclear matter by dressing the kaon and antikaon propagators with in-medium selfenergies. The K+ selfenergy was taken from a simple t-rho approximation, while the K- selfenergy included both an s-wave part from a selfconsistent coupled-channel chiral unitary approach (from Angels) and a p-wave part from Lambda h, Sigma h, and Sigma* h excitations. The phi width was then computed from the imaginary part of its selfenergy, separating contributions from K anti-K decay and from K Y h channels (phi N to K+ Y reactions), with vertex corrections required by gauge invariance included via an effective substitution in the p-wave selfenergy.

## Key numbers

* Total phi width at normal nuclear matter density (rho_0 = 0.17 fm^-3): 22 MeV, compared to the free width of 4.4 MeV.
* Free phi width for K+ K- decay: 2.18 MeV, requiring coupling constant g_phi = 4.57.
* The 3 pi decay channel accounts for only 3% of the in-medium width at rho_0 and was neglected.
* The K+ selfenergy in the t-rho approximation: Pi_{K+} = 0.13 m_K^2 (rho/rho_0).
* Vertex corrections increase the phi width by an additional 3 MeV at the phi mass (M_phi = 1019.413 MeV).
* Relativistic recoil corrections reduce the width by about 5 MeV at the phi mass.
* Omitting the dipole form factor raises the width to about 30 MeV; using a monopole form factor with Lambda = 1.3 GeV gives about 26 MeV.
* Theoretical uncertainty band for the phi width at normal density: 22-28 MeV.
* Using f = f_pi instead of f = 1.15 f_pi raises the width only to 25 MeV.
* The s-wave contribution at rho_0 is reduced by a factor of 2.5 relative to a linear extrapolation from low densities; the p-wave contribution is reduced by about 30%.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of phi meson properties in nuclei by Klinuc and Klilett, which predicted a very small mass shift and a substantially increased width, but the present calculation yields a width about a factor of two smaller due to the use of a selfconsistent antikaon selfenergy (following Lutz and Angels) and inclusion of p-wave contributions to all orders in density. The results are relevant for interpreting phi production experiments in gamma-nucleus or pion-nucleus collisions at facilities like Spring8/Osaka or GSI, and for analyses of dilepton production in heavy ion collisions around the phi energy.
