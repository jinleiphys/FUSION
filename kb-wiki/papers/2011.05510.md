# Strangeness $S = -3$ and $-4$ baryon-baryon interactions in relativistic chiral effective field theory

> The strangeness $S=-3$ and $-4$ baryon-baryon interactions are investigated in the relativistic chiral effective field theory at leading order. First, the potentials are derived from the $S=-1$ sector assuming that the corresponding low-energy constants are related to each other via SU(3) flavor symmetry. The comparison with the state-of-the-art lattice QCD simulations, show, however, that SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking effects can not be neglected. In order to take into account these effects, we redetermine two sets of low-energy constants by fitting to the lattice QCD data in the $ΞΣ$ and $ΞΞ$ channels respectively. The fitting results demonstrate that the lattice QCD $S$-waves phase shifts for both channels can be described rather well. Without any additional free low-energy constants, the predicted phase shifts for the ${}^3D_1$ channel and the mixing angle $\varepsilon_1$ are also in qualitative agreement with the lattice QCD data for the $S=-3$ channel, while the results for the $S=-4$ channel remain to be checked by future lattice QCD simulations. With the so-obtained low-energy constants, the $S$-wave scattering lengths and effective ranges are calculated for these two channels at the physical point. Finally, in combination with the $S=0$ and $-2$ results obtained in our previous works, we study the evolution of the irreducible representation $27$ in the baryon-baryon interactions as a function of increasing strangeness. It is shown that the attraction decreases dramatically as strangeness increases from $S=0$ to $S=-2$, but then remains relatively stable until $S=-4$. The results indicate that the existence of bound states in the $ΞΣ$ and $ΞΞ$ channels is rather unlikely.

## Key claim

The authors find that SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking is significant when relating the strangeness \(S=-1\) hyperon-nucleon interactions to the \(S=-3\) and \(-4\) sectors, and that after refitting low-energy constants to lattice QCD data, the predicted phase shifts for the \(^3D_1\) channel and mixing angle \(\varepsilon_1\) are only in qualitative agreement for the \(S=-3\) channel, while the \(S=-4\) results await future lattice QCD verification.

## Method

The authors employed relativistic chiral effective field theory at leading order, constructing baryon-baryon potentials from four-baryon contact terms and one-pseudoscalar-meson exchange terms. They first derived the \(S=-3\) and \(-4\) interactions from the \(S=-1\) sector using SU(3) flavor symmetry, then refitted two sets of low-energy constants directly to lattice QCD phase shift data from the HAL QCD Collaboration for the \(\Xi\Sigma\) and \(\Xi\Xi\) channels. The coupled-channel Kadyshevsky equation was solved with an exponential regulator for cutoff values between 550 and 700 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The \(\Xi\Sigma\) (\(I=3/2\)) singlet \(S\)-wave scattering length \(a_s^{\Xi\Sigma} = -1.02\) fm (cutoff 550-700 MeV), with effective range \(r_s^{\Xi\Sigma} = 0.88\) to 0.94 fm.
- The \(\Xi\Sigma\) (\(I=3/2\)) triplet \(S\)-wave scattering length \(a_t^{\Xi\Sigma} = -1.58\) to \(-1.62\) fm, with effective range \(r_t^{\Xi\Sigma} = 2.10\) to 2.17 fm.
- The \(\Xi\Xi\) (\(I=1\)) singlet \(S\)-wave scattering length \(a_s^{\Xi\Xi} = -0.46\) fm (cutoff 550-700 MeV), with effective range \(r_s^{\Xi\Xi} = 7.17\) to 7.24 fm.
- The \(\Xi\Xi\) (\(I=0\)) triplet \(S\)-wave scattering length \(a_t^{\Xi\Xi} = 0.16\) to 0.17 fm, with effective range \(r_t^{\Xi\Xi} = 8.12\) to 11.07 fm.
- The lattice QCD simulations used hadron masses: \(m_\pi = 146\) MeV, \(m_K = 525\) MeV, \(M_\Xi = 1356\) MeV, \(M_\Sigma = 1222\) MeV, and average octet baryon mass \(M_B = 1179\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic chiral EFT studies of the \(S=0\), \(-1\), and \(-2\) baryon-baryon systems (Ren et al., 2018; Kai-Wen et al., 2018; Jing et al., 2018) to the \(S=-3\) and \(-4\) sectors. It contradicts the predictions of the Bonn-Julich group (Haidenbauer et al., 2010) and the Nijmegen group (Stoks et al., 1999) by showing that strict SU(3) symmetry is insufficient and that SU(3) breaking must be included. The results enable future lattice QCD simulations to test the predicted \(^3D_1\) phase shifts and mixing angles for the \(\Xi\Xi\) system, and they indicate that bound states in the \(\Xi\Sigma\) and \(\Xi\Xi\) channels are unlikely.
