# Neutron contribution to nuclear DVCS asymmetries

> Using a simple model for nuclear GPDs, we study the role of the neutron contribution to nuclear DVCS observables. As an example, we use the beam-spin asymmetry A_{LU}^A measured in coherent and incoherent DVCS on a wide range of nuclear targets in the HERMES and JLab kinematics. We find that at small values of the momentum transfer t, A_{LU}^A is dominated by the coherent-enriched contribution, which enhances A_{LU}^A compared to the free proton asymmetry A_{LU}^p, A_{LU}^A(ϕ)/A_{LU}^p(ϕ)=1.8-2.2. At large values of t, the nuclear asymmetry is dominated by the incoherent contribution and A_{LU}^A/(ϕ)A_{LU}^p(ϕ)=0.66-0.74. The deviation of A_{LU}^A(ϕ)/A_{LU}^p(ϕ) from unity at large t is a result of the neutron contribution, which gives a possibility to constain neutron GPDs in incoherent nuclear DVCS. A similar trend is expected for other DVCS asymmetries.

## Key claim

Using a simple model for nuclear generalized parton distributions (GPDs), the neutron contribution to nuclear deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) beam-spin asymmetries is shown to be significant at large momentum transfer t, causing the ratio of nuclear to free proton asymmetries to fall below unity (0.66-0.74) rather than approaching unity as previously assumed when neutron contributions were neglected.

## Method

The author constructed nuclear GPDs using a convolution model where the nucleus consists of A uncorrelated nucleons (Z protons and N neutrons), with nuclear GPDs expressed as a sum of proton and neutron GPDs weighted by the nuclear form factor. The beam-spin asymmetry A_LU^A was computed for coherent-enriched and incoherent contributions using an interpolating formula that sums over final nuclear states, with nucleon GPDs taken from the dual parameterization with J_u=J_d=0. Calculations were performed for 4He, 14N, 20Ne, 84Kr, and 131Xe nuclei in HERMES kinematics (x_B=0.065, Q^2=1.7 GeV^2) and for 4He in JLab kinematics (E=6 GeV, x_B=0.15, Q^2=1.5 GeV^2).

## Key numbers

- At small momentum transfer t, the ratio A_LU^A(phi)/A_LU^p(phi) = 1.8-2.2 for the nuclei studied, dominated by the coherent-enriched contribution
- At large momentum transfer t, the ratio A_LU^A(phi)/A_LU^p(phi) = 0.66-0.74, depending on the target nucleus, due to the neutron contribution
- For 4He, 14N, 20Ne, and 84Kr at t=-0.018 GeV^2, A_LU^A(phi)/A_LU^p(phi) ≈ 1.65
- For 131Xe at t=-0.018 GeV^2, A_LU^A(phi)/A_LU^p(phi) = 1.23
- The coherent-enriched contribution scales as (A-1)/(Z-1); the purely coherent contribution scales as A/Z
- Calculations used x_B=0.065, Q^2=1.7 GeV^2 for HERMES kinematics and x_B=0.15, Q^2=1.5 GeV^2 for JLab kinematics

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses by Guzey (2003) and Kirchner (2003) that predicted nuclear DVCS asymmetries without including neutron contributions, where the ratio was expected to approach unity at large t. The present study shows that including neutrons produces a significant deviation below unity, making incoherent nuclear DVCS a sensitive tool to constrain neutron GPDs. The results are relevant for ongoing HERMES measurements and proposed JLab experiments using the BoNuS recoil detector, which could measure purely coherent DVCS on 4He and probe modifications of nucleon GPDs in nuclear medium.
