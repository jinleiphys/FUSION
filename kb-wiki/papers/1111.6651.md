# Local Multiplicity Fluctuations as a Signature of Critical Hadronization at LHC

> In central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC the multiplicity of particles produced is so high that it should become feasible to examine the nature of transition from the deconfined quark-gluon state to the confined hadron state by methods that rely on the availability of high multiplicity events. We consider four classes of the transition process ranging from critical behavior to totally random behavior, depending on whether or not there is clustering of quarks and on whether or not there is contraction of dense clusters due to confinement. Fluctuations of bin multiplicities in each event are quantified, and then the event-by-event fluctuations of spatial patterns are analyzed. A sequence of measures are proposed and are shown to be effective in capturing the essence of the differences among the classes of simulated events. It is demonstrated that a specific index has a low value for critical transition but a larger value if the hadronization process is random.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a specific erraticity index, \(\mu_q\), can distinguish between critical and non-critical hadronization scenarios in high-multiplicity Pb-Pb collisions at LHC, but only if the analysis is performed on narrow transverse momentum intervals and uses event-by-event fluctuations of spatial patterns rather than simple bin-averaged factorial moments. The authors hedge that this is a feasibility study under "optimal scenario" conditions, and that the actual occurrence of critical behavior at LHC is "not assured on theoretical grounds."

## Method

The authors simulate four classes of quark-to-hadron transition in central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC, varying two binary conditions: whether initial quark configurations exhibit clustering (power-law cluster size distribution with exponent \(\gamma\)) and whether confinement-driven contraction of dense bins is applied between time steps. For each class, they generate approximately \(10^6\) events of pions in \((\eta,\phi,p_T)\) space using a recombination model for pion formation, then compute horizontal factorial moments \(F_q^e(M)\) per event and vertical moments \(C_{p,q}(M)\) across events. The erraticity index \(\mu_q\) is extracted from the slope of \(\psi_q(p)\) versus \(p\) in the range \(1 \le p \le 2\).

## Key numbers

- Total charged multiplicity in central Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV is around \(6\times 10^3\) (from ALICE data cited in the paper).
- The cluster size distribution exponent \(\gamma = 2\) is used for the critical and pseudo-critical cases, while \(\gamma = 5\) is used for the quasi-critical and non-critical cases.
- The contraction parameter \(\sigma_1 = 0.1\) is used for the Gaussian width of dense-bin clusters after contraction.
- The inverse slope of the thermal \(p_T\) distribution decreases by 0.02 GeV per time step, starting from \(T = 0.4\) GeV.
- The distance threshold for \(q\bar{q}\) coalescence into a pion is \(d_0 = 0.03\) in the critical and quasi-critical cases.
- The analysis uses \(\Delta p_T = 0.1\) GeV/c intervals around \(p_T = 1\) GeV/c, with \(M\) varying from 8 to 70 bins per side.
- The exponent \(\nu\) in the relation \(\beta_q = (q-1)^\nu\) is found to be 1.136, 1.171, and 1.315 for \(\gamma = 1.5, 2.0, 2.5\) respectively in the clustering model.

## Context

This work extends the study of critical phenomena in heavy-ion collisions from the search for the QCD critical point at lower energies (where high baryon density is expected) to the very high energy regime of LHC where deconfinement-to-confinement transition may occur. It builds on prior intermittency analysis using factorial moments (Bialas and Peschanski, 1986) and the Ginzburg-Landau description of second-order phase transitions (Hwa and Nazirov, 1992), as well as the erraticity formalism for event-by-event fluctuations (Hwa, 1993). The paper enables experimental searches at LHC for signatures of critical hadronization using local multiplicity fluctuations in narrow \(p_T\) bins, a method that requires the high multiplicities available at LHC but has not yet been applied to existing data.
