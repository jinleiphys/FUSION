# Scaling properties of the $Δγ$ correlator and their implication for detection of the chiral magnetic effect in heavy-ion collisions

> The scaling properties of the $Δγ$ correlator, inferred from the Anomalous Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) model, are used to investigate a possible chiral-magnetically-driven (CME) charge separation in $p$+Au, $d$+Au, Ru+Ru, Zr+Zr, and Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt s_{\mathrm{NN}}=200$ GeV, and in $p$+Pb ($\sqrt s_{\mathrm{NN}}=5.02$ TeV) and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt s_{\mathrm{NN}}=5.02$ and $2.76$ TeV. The results indicate that the values of the quotient $Δγ/v_2$ with the elliptic flow coefficient $v_2$ for $p$+Au, $d$+Au, $p$+Pb, and Pb+Pb collisions, scale as $1/{\rm N_{ch}}$ consistent with background-driven charge separation. By contrast, the $Δγ/v_2$ values for Ru+Ru, Zr+Zr, and Au+Au collisions show scaling violations consistent with the presence of background plus a CME-driven contribution. Quantifying this CME-driven component indicates that in mid-central collisions, the fraction of the measured $Δγ/v_2$ attributable to the CME is approximately 27\% for Au+Au and roughly a factor of two smaller for Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr, which show similar magnitudes.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the quotient Δγ/v₂ for p+Au, d+Au, p+Pb, and Pb+Pb collisions scales as 1/Nch, consistent with background-driven charge separation, while for Ru+Ru, Zr+Zr, and Au+Au collisions the scaling violations indicate a non-negligible chiral magnetic effect (CME) contribution. The hedge is that the CME-driven fraction is extracted by assuming the background can be estimated from peripheral and central collisions using the 1/Nch scaling pattern, and that the AVFD model benchmarks are used to validate this interpretation.

## Method

The authors used the Anomalous Viscous Fluid Dynamics (AVFD) model to simulate charge separation from both CME and background sources in heavy-ion collisions, employing Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions, VISHNU hydrodynamic evolution, and a URQMD hadron cascade. They then analyzed published experimental data for Δγ and v₂ from RHIC (p+Au, d+Au, Ru+Ru, Zr+Zr, Au+Au at √sNN=200 GeV) and LHC (p+Pb at √sNN=5.02 TeV, Pb+Pb at √sNN=5.02 and 2.76 TeV) to test whether Δγ/v₂ scales as 1/Nch. For systems showing scaling violations, the background contribution was estimated by extrapolating the 1/Nch scaling from peripheral and central collisions, and the CME-driven fraction fCME was computed as the fractional deviation of the measured Δγ/v₂ from this background estimate.

## Key numbers

- In mid-central Au+Au collisions at √sNN=200 GeV, the fraction of the measured Δγ/v₂ attributable to the CME is approximately 27%.
- For Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at √sNN=200 GeV, the CME-driven fraction is roughly a factor of two smaller than for Au+Au, with similar magnitudes for the two isobars.
- The expected signal difference between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr from the fCME magnitudes is estimated to be only about 1.3%.
- In AVFD simulations for Au+Au at √sNN=200 GeV with n₅/s=0.1 and LCC=33%, the fCME value for 30-40% central collisions is approximately 30%.

## Context

This work extends the search for the chiral magnetic effect by using scaling properties of the Δγ correlator, building on prior experimental measurements from STAR (2013, 2014, 2019, 2021) at RHIC and ALICE and CMS (2016, 2017, 2020) at the LHC. The approach leverages the AVFD model framework developed by Shi (2017) and Jiang (2016) to separate CME signal from background, which has been a longstanding challenge since the correlator was proposed by Voloshin (2004). The results enable quantitative extraction of the CME-driven component in large systems and suggest that the Δγ correlator may be insensitive to the small signal difference between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr isobars, informing future isobar comparison analyses.
