# Heavy Flavor Results at RHIC - A Comparative Overview

> I review the latest heavy flavor measurements at RHIC experiments. Measurements from RHIC together with preliminary results from LHC offer us an opportunity to systematically study the sQGP medium properties. In the end, I will outlook a prospective future on precision heavy flavor measurements with detector upgrades at RHIC.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that heavy flavor measurements at RHIC, combined with preliminary LHC results, provide a systematic way to study strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) properties, but many conclusions remain qualitative and require further precision measurements and theoretical work to become quantitative.

## Method

The paper surveys recent heavy flavor results from the PHENIX and STAR experiments at RHIC, focusing on charm hadron reconstruction via hadronic decays (D0 and D*+ mesons), J/ψ production measurements, and Υ(1S+2S+3S) measurements in p+p and Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV. Results are compared to pQCD FONLL calculations, Blast-Wave model predictions, and theoretical models of quarkonium dissociation and regeneration, as well as to LHC data from ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS at higher energies.

## Key numbers

- STAR D0 and D*+ production cross sections in p+p at √s = 200 GeV are consistent with the upper bound of FONLL pQCD calculations over a wide pT range
- STAR total charm cross section per nucleon-nucleon collision shows approximate Nbin scaling from p+p to central Au+Au collisions
- D0 RAA in 0-80% minimum bias Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV covers pT up to ~5 GeV/c, with values consistent with unity but showing a hint of modification
- J/ψ RAA in Au+Au collisions: low pT (2-5 GeV/c) data consistent with PHENIX published results; high pT (>5 GeV/c) data show systematically higher RAA than low pT
- In peripheral Au+Au collisions, high pT J/ψ RAA is consistent with no suppression; in central collisions, J/ψ are still significantly suppressed
- J/ψ elliptic flow (v2) in 20-60% Au+Au collisions shows no sizable v2 for pT > 2 GeV/c, disfavoring coalescence production from thermalized charm quarks
- Bottom decay contribution to inclusive J/ψ is 10-25% for pT around 4-10 GeV/c at RHIC
- Υ(1S+2S+3S) RAA in central Au+Au collisions is ~3σ below unity, favoring the scenario where only Υ(1S) survives and all excited bottomonium states melt

## Context

This review extends the heavy flavor program at RHIC that began with earlier PHENIX measurements of J/ψ suppression and non-photonic electron production, and now includes STAR results from detector upgrades enabling high pT J/ψ and Υ measurements. The results are compared to LHC data from ALICE (2011), ATLAS (2011), and CMS (2011) at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, showing consistent trends of suppression and regeneration effects. The paper enables future precision measurements with planned detector upgrades (PHENIX VTX and FVTX, STAR HFT and MTD) that will address open questions about charm-medium interactions, bottomonium production as a clean probe of color screening, and heavy quark correlations.
