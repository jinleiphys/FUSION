# Spatial structure of the modified Coulomb potential in a superstrong magnetic field

> The modification of the Coulomb potential due to the enhancement of loop corrections in a superstrong magnetic field is studied numerically. We calculate the modified potential with high precision and obtain the pattern of equipotential lines. The results confirm the general features known from previous studies, however we emphasize some differences in potential structure that can be important for problems with spatially distributed charges.

## Key claim
The equipotential lines of the modified Coulomb potential in a superstrong magnetic field are not ellipses at mid-range distances (z ≲ 1/m), as previously assumed, but instead become "eye-shaped," meaning the potential diminishes with transverse distance ρ faster than earlier analytical models predicted. This result is numerically precise but is limited to the one-loop, lowest-Landau-level contribution to vacuum polarization.

## Method
The authors numerically evaluated the integral expression for the modified Coulomb potential Φ(ρ,z) in cylindrical coordinates, subtracting the unmodified Coulomb potential e/r to regularize the singularity at k⊥=0, k∥=0. They performed the k⊥ integration using the GNU Scientific Library and the k∥ Fourier transform using the FFTW package, achieving absolute errors below 10⁻⁷ (m·e) for B = 10⁴ B₀ and below 10⁻⁶ (m·e) for B = 10⁵ B₀.

## Key numbers
- For B = 10⁴ B₀, the analytical estimate ΔΦᵃⁿᵃˡʸᵗ(0,0)/m ≈ 4.793, while the numerical value from exact integration is 4.41692858.
- For B = 10⁴ B₀, the final numerical absolute error is less than 10⁻⁷ (m·e) for any ρ and z.
- For B = 10⁵ B₀, the final numerical absolute error is less than 10⁻⁶ (m·e) for any ρ and z.
- The critical magnetic field scale is B₀ ≡ m²/e ≈ 4.4 × 10¹³ G, and the study considers B = 10⁴ B₀ and B = 10⁵ B₀.

## Context
This work extends the analytical results of Shabad and Usov (1970s) and the interpolation formula of Vysotsky (2010), which provided expressions for Φ(0,z) and Φ(ρ,0) but assumed elliptic equipotential lines everywhere. The numerical calculation reveals that at mid-range distances (z ≲ 1/m) the equipotential lines are "eye-shaped" rather than elliptic, which is important for problems with spatially distributed charges, such as calculating energy levels of electrons in the field of a finite-size nucleus in a superstrong magnetic field.
