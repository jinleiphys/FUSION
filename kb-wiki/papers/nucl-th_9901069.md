# Deltaron Dibaryon Structure in Chiral SU(3) Quark Model

> We discuss the structure of Deltaron dibaryon in the chiral SU(3) quark model. The energy of Deltaron is obtained by considering the coupling of the $ΔΔ$ and $CC$ (hidden color) channels. The effects of various parameters on the Deltaron mass are also studied. It is shown that the mass of Deltaron is lower than the mass of $ΔΔ$ but higher than the mass of $ΔN π$.

# Deltaron Dibaryon Structure in Chiral SU(3) Quark Model

## Key claim

The Deltaron dibaryon (S=3, J^π=3^+, T=0) is bound relative to the ΔΔ threshold by several tens of MeV, but its mass remains above the ΔNπ threshold, meaning it is not a narrow-width, deeply bound state. The authors conclude that no deeply bound Deltaron dibaryon with narrow width exists, contrary to earlier claims of 320–390 MeV binding energy from the quark delocalization model of Wang et al. (1995).

## Method

The authors solve a coupled-channel Resonating Group Method (RGM) calculation including ΔΔ and hidden-color (CC) channels, with L=0 and L=2 orbital states mixed via tensor forces. The quark-quark interaction includes one-gluon-exchange (OGE), a quadratic confinement potential, and chiral field couplings from the chiral SU(3) quark model (pseudoscalar and scalar meson exchange). Model parameters are fixed by reproducing nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts and hyperon-nucleon cross sections from earlier work by Zhang et al. (1997). Forbidden states due to Pauli blocking are eliminated via an off-shell transformation.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy B = 37.3 MeV for the full OGE+SU(3) calculation with ΔΔ+CC channels and L=0+L=2 mixing (B = -(E_Deltaron - 2M_Δ))
- Root-mean-square radius R̅ = 0.92 fm for the same full calculation
- Binding energy B = 79.7 MeV for OGE+π,σ (chiral SU(2)) with ΔΔ+CC and L=0+L=2
- Binding energy B = 42.0 MeV for OGE only with ΔΔ+CC and L=0+L=2
- With error-function-like confinement (color screening length 2.0 fm), binding energy B = 38.1 MeV for OGE+SU(3) with ΔΔ+CC and L=0+L=2
- Largest binding found: B = 113.4 MeV for OGE+π,σ with b_N=0.60 fm and m_σ=550 MeV
- Baryon size parameter b_N = 0.505 fm (standard) or 0.60 fm (alternative); σ meson mass m_σ = 625 MeV (standard) or 550 MeV (limit case)

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) quark model, previously successful for nucleon-nucleon scattering and hyperon-nucleon cross sections (Zhang et al., 1997), to the Deltaron six-quark system. It contradicts the deeply bound Deltaron (binding 320–390 MeV) predicted by Wang et al. (1995) using the quark delocalization model, which the authors criticize for using non-unique confinement potentials between quarks in the same versus different baryon orbits. The calculation also follows earlier work by Yazaki (1987) who found the ΔΔ (S=3,T=0) state attractive using OGE and confinement only. The results enable experimental searches to focus on a Deltaron with mass above ΔNπ threshold, implying a broad width and challenging detection.
