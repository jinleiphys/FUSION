# Exotics and all that

> This invited contribution summarizes some of the more important aspects of exotics. We review theoretical expectations for exotic and nonexotic hybrid mesons, and briefly discuss the leading experimental candidate for an exotic, the pi_1(1600).

# Exotics and all that

## Key claim

The paper reviews theoretical expectations for exotic and nonexotic hybrid mesons, concluding that while the $\pi_1(1600)$ is a well-established exotic resonance, its identification as a hybrid meson faces two serious problems: a 300–400 MeV mass discrepancy with flux-tube and lattice gauge theory (LGT) predictions of approximately 1.9–2.0 GeV, and reported branching fractions that are inconsistent with the flux-tube model prediction that S+P modes (such as $b_1\pi$) should dominate over $\rho\pi$ and $\eta'\pi$.

## Method

The paper surveys theoretical models of hybrid mesons, the bag model, constituent-gluon models, and the flux-tube model, and compares their predictions for the mass spectrum, quantum numbers, and decay properties of exotic $J^{PC}$ states. It also reviews results from QCD sum rules and LGT (including quenched approximation studies by MILC, UKQCD, and Luo & Mei) and examines experimental data from the VES and E852 collaborations on the $\pi_1(1600)$ exotic candidate in the $b_1\pi$, $\eta'\pi$, and $\rho\pi$ decay channels.

## Key numbers

- Bag model predicts the exotic $1^{-+}$ mass at $M(1^{-+}) \approx 1.5$ GeV, with a total multiplet splitting of approximately 500 MeV.
- Flux-tube model (Isgur, Kokoski and Paton) finds the lightest hybrid multiplet at 1.9(1) GeV; a subsequent Hamiltonian Monte Carlo study finds 1.8–1.9 GeV.
- LGT results place the $1^{-+}$ exotic at about 2.0 GeV; the $0^{+-}$ and $2^{+-}$ may lie somewhat higher.
- LGT (NRQCD) predicts the $1^{-+}$ $b\bar b$ hybrid near 11.0 GeV and the $1^{-+}$ charmonium hybrid just below 4.4 GeV.
- $\pi_1(1600)$ mass: VES reports 1.61(2) GeV (all modes); E852 reports $1.597 \pm 0.010 {+0.045\atop -0.010}$ GeV ($\eta'\pi$) and $1.593 \pm 0.008 {+0.029\atop -0.047}$ GeV ($\rho\pi$).
- $\pi_1(1600)$ width: VES reports 0.29(3) GeV (all modes); E852 reports $0.340 \pm 0.040 \pm 0.050$ GeV ($\eta'\pi$) and $0.168 \pm 0.020 {+0.150\atop -0.012}$ GeV ($\rho\pi$).
- Relative branching fractions (VES): $\Gamma(b_1\pi) \equiv 1$, $\Gamma(\eta'\pi) = 1.0 \pm 0.3$, $\Gamma(\rho\pi) = 1.6 \pm 0.4$.
- Flux-tube model predicts hybrid baryon states starting with degenerate $(1/2^+ N)^2$ and $(3/2^+ N)^2$ at 1870(100) MeV.

## Context

This invited review extends the theoretical and experimental discussion of exotic mesons, building on prior work in the bag model (BagH), flux-tube model (Isgur, Kokoski and Paton 1985), and LGT studies reviewed by McNeile (2002) and Bali (2001). The paper highlights the tension between the $\pi_1(1600)$ mass of approximately 1.6 GeV and the flux-tube/LGT prediction of approximately 1.9–2.0 GeV, and notes that the observed branching fractions contradict the flux-tube S+P selection rule. Future experimental studies at the GlueX photoproduction facility at Jefferson Laboratory are identified as a key next step for testing theoretical models of exotic meson decays.
