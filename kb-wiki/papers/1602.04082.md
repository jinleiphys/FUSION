# On the phase-shift parameterization and ANC extraction from elastic-scattering data

> We develop a method to parameterize elastic-scattering phase-shifts for charged nuclei, based on Padﾃｩ expansions of a simplified effective-range function. The method is potential independent and the input is reduced to experimental phase shifts and bound-state energies. It allows a simple calculation of resonance properties and of asymptotic normalization constants (ANCs) of subthreshold bound states. We analyze the $1^-$ and $2^+$ phase shifts of the $^{12}$C$+ﾎｱ$ system and extract the ANCs of the corresponding bound states. For the $1^-$ state, a factor-3 improvement with respect to the best value available today is obtained, with a factor-10 improvement in reach. For the $2^+$ state, no improvement is obtained due to relatively larger error bars on the experimental phase shifts.

# On the phase-shift parameterization and ANC extraction from elastic-scattering data

## Key claim
The authors develop a potential-independent method based on Padﾃｩ expansions of a simplified effective-range function $\Delta_l$ to parameterize elastic-scattering phase shifts and extract asymptotic normalization constants (ANCs) of subthreshold bound states. For the $1^-$ state of the $^{12}\text{C}+\alpha$ system, they achieve a factor-3 improvement in ANC accuracy compared to the best available value, but for the $2^+$ state no improvement is obtained due to larger error bars on the experimental phase shifts.

## Method
The method uses the function $\Delta_l = (2\pi/a_N) \cot\delta_l / (e^{2\pi\eta}-1)$, which is expanded as a Padﾃｩ approximant incorporating known zeros (from bound-state energies and phase-shift zeros) and poles (from phase-shift infinities). The Padﾃｩ parameters are fitted to experimental phase-shift data via a two-step procedure: first a linear-algebra system provides initial coefficients, then a least-square minimization is performed on randomized data. The ANC is then computed analytically from the derivative of $\Delta_l^c$ at the bound-state energy using Eq. (ANC), without requiring a channel radius or nuclear potential.

## Key numbers
- For the $^{12}\text{C}+\alpha$ $1^-$ state at bound-state energy $E_B = -0.045$ MeV: extracted ANC = $220.5(6.5) \times 10^{12}$ fm$^{-1/2}$, compared to the previous best value $208(20) \times 10^{12}$ fm$^{-1/2}$ from Ref. PRL83 (factor-3 improvement in accuracy).
- For the $^{12}\text{C}+\alpha$ $2^+$ state at $E_B = -244.85$ keV: the [3/3] fit gives ANC = $110(11) \times 10^3$ fm$^{-1/2}$ and the [3/4] fit gives $131(15) \times 10^3$ fm$^{-1/2}$, both compatible with the previous value $114(10) \times 10^3$ fm$^{-1/2}$ from Ref. PRL83.
- For the $1^-$ resonance: resonance energy $2.3657(4)$ MeV and width $351(2)$ keV from Padﾃｩ approximants.
- For a test on a $^{12}\text{C}+\alpha$ $d$-wave potential with known ANC $138.4 \times 10^5$ fm$^{-1/2}$, the method yields $138.2 \times 10^5$ fm$^{-1/2}$ from a [2,4] fit of [1-10] MeV phase shifts.
- The approximate ANC formula Eq. (ANC_a) gives $130.5 \times 10^5$ fm$^{-1/2}$ (6% error) for the same test potential, compared to 11% error from Eq. (19) of Ref. PRC81.

## Context
This work extends the effective-range expansion approach by using Padﾃｩ approximants of the simplified function $\Delta_l$, avoiding the weak sensitivity of the standard effective-range function $K_l$ noted by Orlov (2016) and the complications of the R-matrix method (RPP71) and modified K-matrix expansions (Brune 1996, Humblet 1998). For the $^{12}\text{C}+\alpha$ system, the method improves on the R-matrix phase-shift analyses of Refs. PRL88 and PRC79, and on the ANC extraction from transfer reactions (PRL83) and beta-delayed alpha emission (Azuma 1994). The approach enables simpler and more accurate ANC extraction from elastic-scattering data alone, and the authors plan to extend it to coupled channels and capture reactions.
