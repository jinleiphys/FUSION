# Continuum coupled cluster expansion

> We review the basics of the coupled-cluster expansion formalism for numerical solutions of the many-body problem, and we outline the principles of an approach directed towards an adequate inclusion of continuum effects in the associated single-energy spectrum. We illustrate our findings by considering the simple case of a single-particle quantum mechanics problem.

## Key claim

The paper outlines a reformulation of the coupled-cluster expansion (CCE) that aims to include continuum effects without artificial discretization of the single-particle spectrum, but the actual implementation still requires a discretization on a grid for orthonormalization of continuum basis states, and the method is illustrated only for the trivial one-body case.

## Method

The author reviews the standard CCE formalism for the nuclear many-body problem and proposes constructing a complete single-particle basis that includes both discrete bound states and continuum states, where the continuum states are orthogonalized against the discrete set. The CCE equations are then rewritten in normal-ordered form so that the Schrödinger equation becomes a set of integral equations for cluster-correlation amplitudes \(S_n\) and the energy \(E\), with the goal of solving for one eigenstate at a time without explicit matrix diagonalization. The approach is demonstrated for the \(A=1\) single-particle quantum mechanics problem, where the cluster-correlation operator contains only 1-particle–1-hole correlations.

## Key numbers

* The benchmark for a \(^4\)He-like nucleus using the Argonne v\(_8'\) interaction showed that binding energies calculated by seven exact many-body formalisms agreed within 0.5%.
* For finite nuclei with \(A>4\), the no-core shell model (NCSM) and CCE are the only methods that can directly compare results with Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) for the same Hamiltonian.
* The present CCE implementation requires a large \(50 \hbar \Omega\) configuration space, leading to significant storage problems and lengthy execution time.

## Context

This work extends the CCE approach previously applied to \(^{16}\)O by Heisenberg and Mihaila (2000–2002) using realistic Argonne v\(_{14}\) and v\(_{18}\) interactions with Urbana IX three-body forces, which showed good agreement with experimental charge form factor data when meson-exchange currents were included. The proposed continuum CCE (c-CCE) aims to overcome limitations of the standard CCE that arise from discretizing the continuum part of the self-consistent mean-field Hamiltonian, and it seeks to enable calculations for arbitrary nuclei beyond \(A=16\) using a four-cluster truncation. The author suggests that solving the CCE equations with Monte Carlo techniques, similar to GFMC but with smaller cluster sizes, could provide accurate nuclear structure results for medium to heavy nuclei.
