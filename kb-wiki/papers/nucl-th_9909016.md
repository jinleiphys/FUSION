# Elusive Neutrons from Nuclei in Effective Field Theory

> We review recent computations of neutral pion photoproduction and Compton scattering on the deuteron in baryon chiral perturbation theory. Progress in extracting the neutron electric dipole amplitude, which is relevant in neutral pion photoproduction, and the neutron polarizabilities, which are relevant in Compton scattering, is discussed.

# Elusive Neutrons from Nuclei in Effective Field Theory

## Key claim
Neutral pion photoproduction and Compton scattering on the deuteron, computed in baryon chiral perturbation theory (χPT) using the Weinberg power-counting scheme, can extract neutron properties such as the electric dipole amplitude and polarizabilities, but only if the theoretical errors from neglected higher-order terms are under control; a full O(Q⁴) calculation is necessary before conclusions can be drawn from data at photon energies of order the pion mass.

## Method
The author reviews computations of neutral pion photoproduction and Compton scattering on the deuteron using baryon chiral perturbation theory (χPT) with the Weinberg formulation, where χPT is used to calculate an irreducible scattering kernel (to O(Q³) for Compton scattering) which is then sewn to external deuteron wavefunctions taken from the Argonne V18 potential. For photoproduction, the electric dipole amplitude E_d is computed at O(Q⁴) including three-body corrections. For Compton scattering, the differential cross section is computed at O(Q²) and O(Q³) for photon laboratory energies of 49, 69, and 95 MeV, with all contributions fixed in terms of known parameters (m_π, g_A, M, f_π) and no unknown counterterms appearing at O(Q³).

## Key numbers
- Electric dipole amplitude for neutral pion photoproduction on the deuteron: E_d = (−1.8 ± 0.2) × 10⁻³/m_π⁺ at O(Q⁴) in χPT, compared to the experimental value E_d_exp = (−1.45 ± 0.09) × 10⁻³/m_π⁺ from SAL
- Proton electric dipole amplitude: E_0+^{π⁰p} = −1.16 × 10⁻³/m_π⁺ (χPT prediction) and empirical value (−1.31 ± 0.08) × 10⁻³/m_π⁺ from MAMI
- Neutron electric dipole amplitude: E_0+^{π⁰n} = +2.13 × 10⁻³/m_π⁺ (χPT prediction), a factor of four larger than the proton cross section
- O(Q³) χPT predictions for nucleon polarizabilities: α_p = α_n = 12.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³, β_p = β_n = 1.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (using g_A = 1.26, f_π = 93 MeV)
- Experimental proton polarizabilities: α_p + β_p = 13.23 ± 0.86⁺⁰·²⁰₋₀·₄₉ × 10⁻⁴ fm³, α_p − β_p = 10.11 ± 1.74⁺¹·²²₋₀·₈₆ × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- At 95 MeV, the O(Q³) correction to the differential cross section is approximately 50%, while the contribution to the amplitude is about 25%
- The Weinberg power-counting is valid in the region m_π²/M ≪ Q ≪ Λ_χ, where Λ_χ ∼ 4πf_π

## Context
This paper reviews work extending the application of baryon chiral perturbation theory from single-nucleon processes to nuclear targets, specifically the deuteron, in order to extract neutron properties that are inaccessible from free-neutron targets. The photoproduction calculation (Beane, Lee, van Kolck 1995; Beane, Bernard, Lee, Meißner, van Kolck 1997) confirms the large χPT prediction for the neutron electric dipole amplitude, which models miss due to neglecting large chiral loop effects. For Compton scattering, the O(Q³) Weinberg formulation calculation (Beane, Malheiro, Phillips, van Kolck 1999) is compared to the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) formulation calculation (Chen, Griesshammer, Savage, Springer 1998), which is valid for lower energies; the two calculations show qualitative agreement but differ in detail, with the Weinberg calculation incorporating the full single-nucleon amplitude rather than its polarizability approximation. The work enables future extraction of neutron polarizabilities from coherent deuteron Compton scattering data at Saskatoon (energies 84.2–104.5 MeV) once a full O(Q⁴) calculation is completed, and highlights the need to include the Δ-resonance as a fundamental degree of freedom to extend the effective theory's validity to higher energies.
