# Magnetic Moments of Baryons with a Single Heavy Quark

> We calculate the magnetic moments of heavy baryons with a single heavy quark in the bound-state approach. In this approach the heavy baryons is considered as a heavy meson bound in the field of a light baryon. The light baryon field is represented as a soliton excitation of the light pseudoscalar and vector meson fields. For these calculations we adopt a model that is both chirally invariant and consistent with the heavy quark spin symmetry. We gauge the model action with respect to photon field in order to extract the electromagnetic current operator and obtain the magnetic moments by computing pertinent matrix elements of this operator between the bound state wavefunctions. We compare our predictions for the magnetic moments with results of alternative approaches for the description of heavy baryon properties.

## Key claim

The magnetic moments of heavy baryons with a single heavy quark are computed in a bound-state approach where the heavy baryon is treated as a heavy meson bound in the field of a light baryon soliton, with the results depending on an undetermined parameter α that must be constrained by comparison to experimental mass differences.

## Method

The authors construct a chirally invariant Lagrangian for light mesons (pions, ρ, ω) and a heavy meson Lagrangian consistent with heavy quark spin symmetry, then gauge the total action with respect to the photon field to extract the electromagnetic current operator. They obtain the soliton profiles for the light meson fields, solve for the heavy meson bound-state wavefunctions in the P-wave channel, and quantize the combined system using collective coordinates (cranking) to generate baryon states with good spin and isospin. Magnetic moments are computed as matrix elements of the electromagnetic current operator between these quantized heavy baryon states.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy ω^C (charm) ranges from 615 MeV (α = -0.3) to 376 MeV (α = 0.8); ω^B (bottom) ranges from 872 MeV to 602 MeV over the same α range.
- Hyperfine parameter χ^C ranges from 0.165 (α = -0.3) to 0.069 (α = 0.8); χ^B ranges from 0.060 to 0.031.
- Σ_C – Λ_C mass difference: computed values range from 167 MeV (α = -0.3) to 186 MeV (α = 0.8); experimental value is 168 MeV.
- Σ_C* – Λ_C mass difference: computed values range from 216 MeV (α = -0.3) to 207 MeV (α = 0.8); experimental value is 235 MeV.
- Nucleon – Λ_C mass difference: computed values range from -1187 MeV (α = -0.3) to -1419 MeV (α = 0.8); experimental value is -1344 MeV.
- Λ_B – Λ_C mass difference: computed values range from 3164 MeV (α = -0.3) to 3191 MeV (α = 0.8); experimental value is 3339 ± 9 MeV.
- Moment of inertia α² = 5.01 GeV⁻¹ for the chosen parameter set (g = 5.6, h̃ = 0.3, g̃_VVφ = 1.8, κ = 1.2).

## Context

This work extends the bound-state soliton approach previously applied to heavy baryon mass splittings (Scholl and Weigel, 1995) by computing static electromagnetic properties, specifically magnetic moments. The model builds on the chirally invariant Lagrangian with light vector mesons developed by Jain, Johnson, and Schechter (1988) and the heavy meson Lagrangian of Scholl and Weigel (1993). The results enable comparison with alternative approaches to heavy baryon structure and provide predictions that can be tested against future experimental measurements of heavy baryon magnetic moments.
