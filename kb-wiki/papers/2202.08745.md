# Pure gauge theories and spatial periodicity

> Properties of pure gauge theories in thermal equilibrium as calculated via standard functional integral treatments are mathematically identical to ground state properties of a theory with spatially-periodic boundary conditions imposed on the gauge fields. Such a theory has states that have no analog in a theory in which only physical observables associated with gauge-invariant operators are required to be periodic, rather than the gauge fields themselves; these states are in topological sectors that do not exist in the unconstrained theory. The topology arises because the boundary conditions in the functional integral are gauge invariant on a cylinder but not in the unconstrained theory.

# Pure gauge theories and spatial periodicity

## Key claim
Properties of pure gauge theories in thermal equilibrium, as calculated via standard functional integral treatments, are mathematically identical to ground state properties of a theory with spatially-periodic boundary conditions imposed on the gauge fields themselves, but this equivalence only holds for gauge-invariant operators, and the spatially-compactified theory contains topological sectors with no analog in a theory where only physical observables are required to be periodic.

## Method
The paper considers SU(3) pure gauge theory on a spatial cylinder, where the z-direction is compactified with periodicity β = 1/T, and constructs the spatial analog of the Polyakov loop and spatial center transformations. It then compares this theory to a "periodically-constrained large system" (PCLS) in which all gauge-invariant operators are constrained to be periodic with period β, but the gauge fields themselves are not required to be periodic. The analysis uses both Hamiltonian and functional integral formulations, including a lattice-regularized functional integral construction to make the distinction rigorous.

## Key numbers
- The spatial periodicity is β = 1/T, where T is the temperature of the associated thermal system.
- For SU(3), the spatial center symmetry has three eigenvalues labeled by n = 0, 1, 2, where eigenvalues are exp(i 2π n/3).
- The string tension σ is extracted from the correlator of spatial Polyakov loops as σ = lim_{τ→∞} [-log(C_{L_S^* L_S}(x,y,τ;β))] / (β τ).
- The string tension relates to the lowest-lying excited state energy E_1 via σ(T) = T E_1.

## Context
This work extends the long-standing use of the Polyakov loop and center symmetry as signatures of confinement and deconfinement in thermal Yang-Mills theory, following foundational work by Polyakov (1978) and McLerran (1981). It addresses conceptual issues raised by Smilga (1993) about whether a single Polyakov loop can acquire an expectation value, and connects to recent developments in quantum computing for gauge theories (Stryker 2018, Alexandru 2019, Lamm 2020, Cohen 2021). The paper demonstrates that the spatial cylinder formulation introduces topological sectors absent in the original theory, and that quantities like the string tension provide only indirect information about the periodically-constrained system, not direct observables. A forthcoming publication will explore the physical significance of the Polyakov loop and its correlators for real-time dynamics.
