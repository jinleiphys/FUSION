# Dileptons Get Nearly "Blind" to Mass-Scaling Effects In Hot and/or Dense Matter

> Based on BHHRS and further sharpened by discussions with Gerry Brown that I had in October 2008, we arrive at the present assessment of the dilepton saga, namely, that dileptons become "blind" to changes in the vacuum structure of chiral symmetry (such as, e.g., BR scaling at high temperature and/or at high density and hence are {\em not} an appropriate probe for a signal for partial or complete chiral restoration, contrary to what has been widely believed. There, however, are a variety of indirect indications that the scaling notion is qualitatively, if not quantitatively, valid and should work in various low-energy nuclear phenomena, and it is fair to conclude that while there is no direct evidence for the scaling notion, there is {\em none against} it either, in a strong disagreement with what was claimed in CERN Courier November 2009. I will touch briefly on certain observables that could give a clear-cut litmus signal for the vacuum structure of chiral symmetry modified by temperature and/or density.

# Dileptons Get Nearly "Blind" to Mass-Scaling Effects In Hot and/or Dense Matter

## Key claim
Dileptons produced in heavy-ion collisions are nearly blind to changes in the vacuum structure of chiral symmetry (such as Brown-Rho scaling) at high temperature and/or high density, and therefore are not an appropriate probe for partial or complete chiral restoration, contrary to widespread belief. However, the paper asserts that while there is no direct evidence for the scaling notion, there is none against it either, in strong disagreement with claims made in CERN Courier November 2009.

## Method
The paper presents a theoretical analysis within hidden local symmetry with vector manifestation (HLS/VM), considering the temperature evolution from the chiral transition temperature T_c down to the flash temperature T_flash, where approximately 90% of on-shell masses are recovered. The author argues that in the "hadronic freedom" regime between T_c and T_flash, BR-scaled sharp low-mass dileptons are strongly suppressed by HLS/VM, while below T_flash, conventional many-body hadronic processes dominate and produce results indistinguishable from non-scaling models. The analysis compares the HLS/VM scenario with conventional many-body approaches (nonVM) that assume vector dominance throughout the entire evolution.

## Key numbers
- The ratio m_rho*/m_rho approximately equals f_pi*/f_pi, which is approximately 1 - kappa (n/n_0), where kappa is between 0.1 and 0.2 at normal nuclear matter density n_0.
- At T_flash, approximately 90% of on-shell masses are recovered.
- The FOPI/GSI data constrain the nuclear symmetry energy S only up to about 1.3 n_0.
- The g*/m_rho* ratio is approximately constant for densities n greater than n_flash, where n_flash is a bit above the maximum density measured by FOPI data.
- In holographic QCD, N-body forces are suppressed as V_N ~ O(N_c/lambda^(N-1)), where N_c/lambda ~ O(10^(-1)) in the hadronic world.

## Context
This work extends the BHHRS analysis and discussions with Gerry Brown from October 2008, challenging the consensus in the heavy-ion community that dilepton spectra from NA60 and other experiments rule out mass scaling of hadrons. The paper contrasts the HLS/VM approach with conventional many-body hadronic approaches (nonVM) that fit parameters to on-shell data. It connects to prior work by Holt et al. on C-14 dating, by Song on rearrangement terms for thermodynamic consistency, and by Son and Stephanov on pion velocity predictions. The paper suggests that measuring the pion velocity v_pi at or near T_c could provide a smoking-gun test: v_pi approximately 0 would kill HLS/VM and BR scaling, while v_pi approximately 1 would support them.
