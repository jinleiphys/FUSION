# Critical point multiplicities and multiplicity fluctuations in heavy ion collisions

> An exactly solvable model of nuclear fragmentation is shown to lead to a simple connection between the critical point multiplicity $\langle m \rangle_{c}$ and the critical point exponent $τ$ recently reported on in the EOS collaboration. The importance of multiplicity fluctuations on critical point behavior is also discussed.

# Critical point multiplicities and multiplicity fluctuations in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The paper derives a simple analytic relation between the critical multiplicity ⟨m⟩_c and the critical exponent τ using an exactly solvable statistical model of nuclear fragmentation, and shows that the EOS collaboration's independently measured values of ⟨m⟩_c = 26 ± 1 and τ = 2.14 ± 0.06 are consistent with this relation, though the model predicts τ = 2.262 ± 0.013 from the same multiplicity.

## Method
The authors use an exactly solvable statistical model of hadronic matter with a Gibbs weight proportional to x_k = x/k^τ, where x is a tuning parameter related to volume, temperature, binding energy, and level spacing. In the grand canonical limit for large mass number A, they derive the relation ⟨m⟩_c / A = ζ(τ) / ζ(τ-1) from the partition function recursion relations, and analyze multiplicity fluctuations ⟨m²⟩ − ⟨m⟩² to identify the critical point as a cusp in the infinite A limit.

## Key numbers
- EOS collaboration reported ⟨m⟩_c = 26 ± 1 and τ = 2.14 ± 0.06 for fragmentation of gold nuclei using percolation model analysis.
- The model in this paper gives τ = 2.262 ± 0.013 when ⟨m⟩_c = 26 ± 1 is used as input.
- The critical point occurs at A = x_c g_{τ-1}(1) where g_{τ-1}(1) is finite only if τ > 2.
- The slope discontinuity in multiplicity fluctuations at the critical point is given by −2 A x_c.

## Context
This work extends the percolation model analysis of the EOS collaboration (Gilkes 1994) and Campi's (1986) percolation predictions for nuclear fragmentation critical behavior. The exactly solvable model parallels Feynman's approach to the λ transition in liquid helium and Bose condensation, where cluster size in fragmentation corresponds to cycle length in the symmetric group decomposition (Chase 1994). The relation between ⟨m⟩_c and τ enables a consistency check between different models of nuclear fragmentation critical phenomena and highlights the role of multiplicity fluctuations as a signature of the phase transition.
