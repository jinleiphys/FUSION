# The electron-nucleon cross section in $(e,e'p)$ reactions

> We examine commonly used approaches to deal with the scattering of electrons from a bound nucleon. Several prescriptions are shown to be related by gauge transformations. Nevertheless, due to current non-conservation, they yield different results. These differences reflect the size of the uncertainty that persists in the interpretation of $(e,e'p)$ experiments.

# The electron-nucleon cross section in $(e,e'p)$ reactions

## Key claim

The commonly used "conserved current" (cc) prescriptions for the $(e,e'p)$ reaction, which are related by gauge transformations, yield different results when applied to off-shell nucleons because the approximate nuclear current is not conserved; these differences reflect the size of the uncertainty that persists in the interpretation of $(e,e'p)$ experiments, and the ambiguities cannot be dismissed even when predictions among some currently used prescriptions are in close agreement.

## Method

The authors examine the de Forest (1983) prescription and two alternative methods for restoring current conservation in the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) for $(e,e'p)$ reactions. They show that replacing the longitudinal current component by the charge density (Coulomb gauge), replacing the charge density by the longitudinal current (Weyl gauge), or subtracting a term proportional to $q_\mu$ (Landau gauge) correspond to evaluating the same non-conserved current in different gauges. They then compute the electron-nucleon cross section for each prescription using two different forms of the free on-shell current (the "cc1" and "cc2" forms) and compare the results for kinematics relevant to the Makins et al. (1994) experiment.

## Key numbers

- At $Q^2 = 1.04$ GeV$^2$, $|\vec q| = 1.2$ GeV, with missing energy 47 MeV and missing momentum up to 250 MeV/c, the spread among different prescriptions relative to $\sigma_{cc2}$ exceeds $\pm 5\%$.
- When the outgoing proton momentum is reduced by 10% to access larger missing energy (approximately 140 MeV), the largest difference between cross sections grows to more than $\pm 10\%$.
- At higher energy, $Q^2 = 6.8$ GeV$^2$, $|\vec q| = 4.5$ GeV, with missing energy 9 MeV, the differences between cross sections are typically around $1\%$.
- At the same high energy but with outgoing proton momentum reduced by 3% to reach missing energy 137 MeV, the spread among prescriptions grows to about $5\%$.
- The off-shell parameter $\Delta\omega$ ranges from 47 to 80 MeV in the first kinematics and from 148 to 180 MeV in the larger missing-energy cases.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Naus, Pollock, Koch and Oelfke (1990) by explicitly connecting different current-conserving prescriptions to specific gauge choices and by quantifying the resulting uncertainties for kinematics of the Makins et al. (1994) color transparency experiment. The paper shows that the widely used de Forest (1983) "cc2" prescription is not uniquely determined, and that variations among prescriptions grow with the degree of off-shellness. The results indicate that until a fully consistent theoretical description of the $(e,e'p)$ reaction is achieved, the interpretation of experiments seeking subtle medium effects such as color transparency must account for these ambiguities.
