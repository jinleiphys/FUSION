# A new solution for effective interaction

> A new method is given for the model-space effective interaction. Introducing a new operator in place of the Q-box in the Krenciglowa-Kuo (KK) method, we derive a new equation for the effective interaction. This equation can be viewed as an extension of the KK method. We show that this equation can be solved both in iterative and non-iterative ways. We observe that the iteration procedure brings about fast acceleration of convergence compared to the KK approach. We also find that the non-iterative calculation reproduces successfully any set of the true eigenvalues of the original Hamiltonian. This non-iterative calculation can be made regardless of the magnitudes of the overlaps with the model space and the energy differences between the unperturbed energy and the eigenvalues to be solved.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new operator, the Z-box, which replaces the Q-box in the Krenciglowa-Kuo (KK) method to derive an extended equation for the effective interaction that can reproduce *any* set of true eigenvalues of the original Hamiltonian, regardless of the magnitudes of the overlaps with the model space or the energy differences between the unperturbed energy and the eigenvalues.

## Method

The authors define the Z-box operator in terms of the Q-box and its energy derivative, then derive a new eigenvalue equation for the model-space effective interaction. They solve this equation both iteratively (by replacing the Q-box with the Z-box in the KK iteration scheme) and non-iteratively (by finding crossing points between the functions \(F_k(E)\) and \(y=E\) using methods such as bisection). The method is tested on a schematic 4-dimensional model Hamiltonian with a 2-dimensional model space, originally introduced by Hoffmann et al.

## Key numbers

- For the model Hamiltonian with coupling strength \(x=0.05\), the exact eigenvalue of the lowest-lying state is 0.8904504858869942.
- After 3 iterations, the EKK method converges to 0.89045048588699 (14 decimal places correct), while the KK method after 3 iterations gives only 0.8904 (4 decimal places correct).
- After 7 iterations, the KK method reaches 0.8904504858 (10 decimal places correct), while the EKK method has already converged to more than 15 decimal places by iteration 4.
- The model uses a 4-dimensional entire Hilbert space (\(n=4\)) and a 2-dimensional model space (\(d=2\)), with unperturbed energy \(E_0=1\).

## Context

This work extends the Krenciglowa-Kuo (KK) method (Krenciglowa and Kuo, 1975) and the Lee-Suzuki (LS) method (Lee and Suzuki, 1980) for constructing effective interactions in truncated model spaces. The KK method only yields eigenvalues for states with largest P-space overlaps, while the LS method reproduces eigenvalues closest to a chosen unperturbed energy; both suffer from convergence difficulties and pole problems. The new Z-box approach removes these limitations, enabling stable calculation of any set of true eigenvalues and offering faster convergence than the KK method, with potential applications to many-body problems.
