# The isoscalar monopole resonance of the alpha particle: a prism to nuclear Hamiltonians

> We present an ab-initio study of the isoscalar monopole excitations of 4He using different realistic nuclear interactions, including modern effective field theory potentials. In particular we concentrate on the transition form factor $F_{\cal M}$ to the narrow $0^+$ resonance close to threshold. F_M exhibits a strong potential model dependence, and can serve as a kind of prism to distinguish among different nuclear force models. Comparing to the measurements obtained from inelastic electron scattering off 4He, one finds that the state-of-the-art theoretical transition form factors are at variance with experimental data, especially in the case of effective field theory potentials. We discuss some possible reasons for such discrepancy, which still remains a puzzle.

## Key claim

The isoscalar monopole transition form factor of the alpha particle shows a dramatic sensitivity to the nuclear Hamiltonian, and state-of-the-art theoretical calculations, particularly those using chiral effective field theory potentials, are at variance with experimental data; the discrepancy remains a puzzle.

## Method

The authors performed ab-initio calculations of the isoscalar monopole strength of 4He using the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method combined with the Effective Interaction Hyperspherical Harmonics (EIHH) approach. They diagonalized the Hamiltonian on a square integrable hyperspherical harmonics basis, accelerated by the Suzuki-Lee unitary transformation, using two different realistic Hamiltonians: the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential plus the UIX three-nucleon force, and a chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon potential plus a N2LO three-nucleon force. The resonance transition form factor was extracted by separating the resonant and non-resonant background contributions from the total monopole strength.

## Key numbers

- The experimental resonance energy of the 4He isoscalar monopole is E_R^exp = -8.20 ± 0.05 MeV, with a width of 270 ± 50 keV.
- The calculated resonance energies are E_R = -7.40(20) MeV for AV18+UIX and E_R = -7.50(30) MeV for N3LO+N2LO, both overestimating the experimental value by about 700 keV.
- The charge radius from AV18+UIX is 1.432(2) fm, while for N3LO+N2LO it is 1.464(2) fm, compared to the experimental value of 1.463(6) fm.
- The ground state energy of 4He with the N3LO+N2LO potential (3NF from Navratil 2007) is -28.357(7) MeV, and with the 3NF from Gazit 2009 it is -28.507(7) MeV; the experimental value is -28.30 MeV.
- For the N3LO+N2LO potential, the background reduction of the resonance strength varies between 13% at q = 0.25 fm^-1 and 22% at q = 2 fm^-1.

## Context

This work extends ab-initio studies of light nuclear systems using realistic potential models, as reviewed by Leidemann and Orlandini (2012), and directly compares with experimental data from Frosch et al. (1965), Walcher (1970), and Kobschall et al. (1983). The authors find that the isoscalar monopole transition form factor is far more sensitive to the Hamiltonian than the elastic form factor, which shows similar results across models. This sensitivity enables the observable to serve as a stringent test for nuclear force models, and the discrepancy with experiment, especially for chiral potentials, motivates further experimental confirmation and theoretical analysis of sum rules, transition densities, and three-nucleon force effects.
