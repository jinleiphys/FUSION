# The tensor-optimized high-momentum antisymmetrized molecular dynamics with bare interaction and its application in ${}^{4}$He nucleus

> We formulate the "tensor-optimized high-momentum antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (TO-HMAMD)" framework for ab initio calculations of nuclei by hybridizing the tensor-optimized (TO-) and high-momentum (HM-) AMD approaches. This hybrid approach has advantages in both analytical simplicity and numerical efficiency comparing with other AMD-based methods which treat the bare interaction, especially for heavier nuclear systems. In this work, the $s$-shell nucleus $^{4}$He is calculated with TO-HMAMD by including up to double product of nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) correlations, described by using high-momentum pairs and spatial correlation functions of nucleons. The total energy and radius of $^{4}$He nucleus are well reproduced using the AV8$^\prime$ interaction. The spin-isospin channel dependence is also discussed for $NN$-correlations, which are found to be mostly contributed in the even-state channels, especially the triplet-even channel. Analyses of the analytical formation and numerical results suggest that TO-HMAMD could be a promising framework for general nuclear systems.

## Key claim

The TO-HMAMD framework hybridizes the tensor-optimized (TOAMD) and high-momentum (HMAMD) approaches to balance analytical simplicity and numerical efficiency for ab initio nuclear calculations, and reproduces the total energy and radius of ⁴He using the AV8′ bare interaction when including up to double products of nucleon-nucleon correlations.

## Method

The authors construct TO-HMAMD wave functions by combining AMD basis states with high-momentum pairs (imaginary shifts of Gaussian centroids) and TOAMD correlation functions \(F_D\) (tensor) and \(F_S\) (short-range) in operator form. For ⁴He, they include single and double products of NN-correlations, superpose bases with different spin-isospin channels and Gaussian range parameters, and solve the Hill-Wheeler equation after angular momentum projection to \(J^\pi=0^+\).

## Key numbers

- Total energy of ⁴He: converged to the double TOAMD result using AV8′ interaction with Gaussian range parameter \(\nu=0.20\) fm\(^{-2}\).
- Number of diagrams for two-body operator \(V\) in TO-HMAMD: 16, compared to 412 in double TOAMD and 1 in double HMAMD.
- Number of bases in TO-HMAMD: 1813, compared to an estimated 13824 for double HMAMD and 1369 for double TOAMD.
- Number of mesh points for angular momentum projection in TO-HMAMD for ⁴He(0⁺): 14, compared to 8000 for double HMAMD.
- Magnitudes of imaginary shift \(|\bm{D}|\) used in HMAMD bases: ranging from 1 fm to 12 fm with 1 fm interval for single HMAMD calculations.

## Context

This work extends the TOAMD method (Myo 2015, 2017a-d) and HMAMD method (Myo 2017e, 2018; Isaka 2018), which were previously applied to s-shell nuclei like ³H and ⁴He. The hybrid TO-HMAMD approach reduces the analytical complexity of double TOAMD (which requires 412 diagram types) while improving numerical efficiency over double HMAMD (which requires many more bases and angular momentum projection points). The authors demonstrate convergence with double TOAMD results for ⁴He and suggest the method is promising for extension to p-shell nuclei.
