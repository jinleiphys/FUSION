# Deep learning assisted jet tomography for the study of Mach cones in QGP

> Mach cones are expected to form in the expanding quark-gluon plasma (QGP) when energetic quarks and gluons (called jets) traverse the hot medium at a velocity faster than the speed of sound in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. The shape of the Mach cone and the associated diffusion wake are sensitive to the initial jet production location and the jet propagation direction relative to the radial flow because of the distortion by the collective expansion of the QGP and large density gradient. The shape of jet-induced Mach cones and their distortions in heavy-ion collisions provide a unique and direct probe of the dynamical evolution and the equation of state of QGP. However, it is difficult to identify the Mach cone and the diffusion wake in current experimental measurements of final hadron distributions because they are averaged over all possible initial jet production locations and propagation directions. To overcome this difficulty, we develop a deep learning assisted jet tomography which uses the full information of the final hadrons from jets to localize the initial jet production positions. This method can help to constrain the initial regions of jet production in heavy-ion collisions and enable a differential study of Mach-cones with different jet path length and orientation relative to the radial flow of the QGP in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors develop a deep learning assisted jet tomography that uses final-state hadron momenta to localize initial jet production positions in heavy-ion collisions, enabling a differential study of Mach cones and diffusion wakes that would otherwise be washed out by averaging over all possible production locations and propagation directions.

## Method

The authors train a point-cloud deep neural network on simulated data from the CoLBT-hydro model, which couples the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model for jet parton propagation with 3+1D CLVisc viscous hydrodynamics for the QGP medium evolution. The network takes as input the four-momenta of hadrons inside the jet cone (with pT > 2 GeV/c) plus global gamma-jet event information, and is trained to predict the initial jet production coordinates (x, y) in the transverse plane. The trained network is then used to classify events by predicted production position, and the corresponding gamma-hadron correlations are examined for Mach cone signals.

## Key numbers

* The training and validation root-mean-square-error (RMSE) on CoLBT-hydro data is approximately 2.4 fm (from a loss of 0.06, corresponding to sqrt(6) = 2.4 fm).
* The testing error on pseudo-data from the LIDO transport model is 0.049, corresponding to an RMSE of 2.2 fm.
* Hadrons from jets are selected with pT > 2 GeV/c for training to reduce soft hadron background.
* The prompt photon transverse momentum range is pT_gamma in (200, 250) GeV, with |eta_gamma| < 0.32; jets have pT_jet > 100 GeV, |eta_jet| < 0.75, and cone size R = 0.4.
* Soft charged hadrons for correlation studies are restricted to pT_h in (1.0, 2.0) GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior attempts at jet tomography including longitudinal tomography using gamma/Z0-jet energy asymmetry (Zhang, 2009) and transverse or gradient tomography using transverse asymmetry (He, 2020; Fu, 2022), as well as a 2D jet tomography combining both approaches (Yang, 2021; Yang, 2022). It also builds on earlier deep neural network work for estimating hadron-triggered jet energy loss (Du, 2020; Du, 2021). The method enables "jet-position engineering" to isolate Mach cone signals that are otherwise obscured in event-averaged measurements, and the authors cross-check model dependence by testing a CoLBT-trained network on LIDO model pseudo-data.
