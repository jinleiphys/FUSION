# Attractive $N$-$ϕ$ Interaction and Two-Pion Tail from Lattice QCD near Physical Point

> First results on the interaction between the $ϕ$-meson and the nucleon ($N$) are presented based on the ($2+1$)-flavor lattice QCD simulations with nearly physical quark masses. Using the HAL QCD method, the spacetime correlation of the $N$-$ϕ$ system in the spin 3/2 channel is converted into the $N$-$ϕ$ scattering phase shift through the interaction potential. The $N$-$ϕ$ potential appears to be a combination of a short-range attractive core and a long-range attractive tail. The latter is found to be consistent with the two-pion exchange (TPE) obtained from the interaction between a color-dipole and the nucleon. The resultant scattering length and effective range for $m_π=$ 146.4 MeV are $ a^{(3/2)}_0=-1.43(23)_{\rm stat.}\left(^{+36}_{-06}\right)_{\rm syst.} {\rm fm}$ and $ r^{(3/2)}_{\rm eff}=2.36(10)_{\rm stat.}\left(^{+02}_{-48}\right)_{\rm syst.} {\rm fm}$, respectively. The magnitude of the scattering length is shown to have nontrivial dependence of $m_π$ and is sensitive to the existence of the long-range tail from TPE.

## Key claim

Using lattice QCD with nearly physical quark masses, the N-phi interaction in the spin 3/2 channel is found to be attractive at all distances, consisting of a short-range attractive core and a long-range attractive tail consistent with two-pion exchange (TPE); the abstract's claim of "first results" is qualified by the fact that the calculation is performed at a pion mass of 146.4 MeV (about 6% above the physical value) and only in the highest-spin channel where coupling to open channels is kinematically suppressed.

## Method

The HAL QCD method was used to convert the spacetime correlation function of the N-phi system in the ^4S_{3/2} channel into an interaction potential via a derivative expansion of the nonlocal potential, keeping only the leading-order central term. The potential was extracted from (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations on a 96^4 lattice with volume (8.1 fm)^3, using 200 gauge configurations with 128,000 total measurements, and the resulting potential was fitted to functional forms including a TPE tail term proportional to m_pi^4 * exp(-2 m_pi r)/r^2. The scattering phase shift was obtained by solving the Klein-Gordon equation with the fitted potential in infinite volume.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_pi = 146.4(4) MeV (lattice value, about 6% above physical 138.0 MeV)
- Scattering length in ^4S_{3/2} channel: a_0^{(3/2)} = -1.43(23)_stat.(+36/-06)_syst. fm at m_pi = 146.4 MeV
- Effective range in ^4S_{3/2} channel: r_eff^{(3/2)} = 2.36(10)_stat.(+02/-48)_syst. fm at m_pi = 146.4 MeV
- Model-dependent extrapolation to physical pion mass gives a_0^{(3/2)} approximately -1.25 fm and r_eff^{(3/2)} approximately 2.49 fm
- The spatial effective energy E_eff(r) shows a plateau at 2 m_pi = 292.8 MeV for r > 1.0 fm, confirming the TPE tail
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.0846 fm (a^{-1} = 2333 MeV), lattice volume L^4 = 96^4 corresponding to La = 8.1 fm

## Context

This work presents the first lattice QCD calculation of the N-phi interaction, extending the HAL QCD method previously applied to baryon-baryon systems (Gongyo 2018, Iritani 2019, Sasaki 2020, Lyu 2021) to a meson-baryon system. The results contradict earlier QCD sum rule calculations (Koike 1997, Klingl 1997) that gave much smaller scattering lengths, likely because those approaches could not capture the long-range TPE contribution. The scattering length magnitude is comparable to the spin-averaged value from ALICE Collaboration femtoscopic measurements (ALICE 2021), though the effective range is about three times smaller. This work enables future coupled-channel analyses of the ^2S_{1/2} and ^4S_{3/2} channels at the physical point, and provides a first-principles foundation for studying s\bar{s} interactions with nucleons relevant to phi meson production in nuclear matter experiments (Spring8-LEPS, KEK-PS E325, JLab-CLAS, COSY-ANKE).
