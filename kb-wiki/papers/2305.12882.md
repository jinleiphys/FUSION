# On the screening condition in the core of neutron stars

> Earlier, the screening condition in neutron star core has been formulated as equality of velocities of superconducting protons and the electrons $\mathbf{v}_p=\mathbf{u}_e$ at wavenumbers $q\llλ^{-1}$ ($λ$ is the London penetration depth) and has been used to derive the force exerted by the electrons on a moving flux tube. By calculating the current-current response, I find that $\mathbf{v}_p\neq\mathbf{u}_e$ for $l^{-1}<q\llλ^{-1}$ ($l$ is the electron mean free path). I show that at typical realistic parameters the electric field induced by a moving (relative to the electrons) flux tube is not screened by the electron currents. The implication is that the existing picture of the momentum exchange between the electrons and the flux tubes must be reassessed.

# On the screening condition in the core of neutron stars

## Key claim

The screening condition (equality of superconducting proton and electron velocities, **v**_p = **u**_e) assumed in prior derivations of the electron-flux tube force does not hold for realistic neutron-star parameters at wavenumbers between the inverse electron mean free path and the inverse London penetration depth (l⁻¹ < q ≪ λ⁻¹). The electric field induced by a flux tube moving relative to the electrons is not screened by electron currents under typical conditions, requiring reassessment of the existing momentum-exchange picture.

## Method

The author calculates the transverse current-current response of relativistic, quantum-degenerate electrons in the outer core of a neutron star using the Jancovici dielectric function modified by the Conti-Vignale relaxation-time approximation to include electron collisions. The proton supercurrent is treated as a given external current, and the induced electron current is computed via the dielectric function to evaluate the screening coefficient ζ = (1 − ε_t)/ε_t, which measures the effectiveness of current-current screening. The analysis covers the particle-hole regime (q > l⁻¹) with slow frequencies (ω ≪ ν), where ν is the total electron collision rate from scattering off normal protons in flux tubes and off magnetic field lines.

## Key numbers

- Flux tube separation distance: d_v = 143.8 (10¹⁵ Oe / H)^(1/2) fm, for magnetic field H = 10¹⁵ Oe.
- London penetration depth: λ(0) ≈ 80 fm, using proton fraction x_p = 0.05 and baryon density n = 0.16 fm⁻³.
- Proton coherence length: ξ(0) ≈ 7.2 (1 MeV / Δ_p) fm, where Δ_p is the proton superconducting energy gap.
- Electron Fermi wavenumber: k_e ≈ 0.6187 fm⁻¹.
- Electron collision rate with normal protons: ν₁ ≈ 7.898 × 10¹¹ s⁻¹ for H = 10¹⁵ Oe and Δ_p = 1 MeV.
- Electron collision rate with magnetic flux tubes: ν₂ ≈ α × 2.343 × 10¹⁹ s⁻¹, with α ∼ 10⁻², for H = 10¹⁵ Oe and Δ_p = 1 MeV.
- Electron mean free path range: between ∼10⁶ fm (from ν₂) and ∼10¹¹ fm (from ν₁), for H = 10¹⁵ Oe and Δ_p = 1 MeV.
- Dynamical frequencies considered: ω = 10, 10², 10³, and 10⁴ Hz, all much smaller than ν under realistic conditions.
- For ν = 3 × 10¹⁸ Hz and ω between 0 and 10⁴ Hz, screening fails at all length scales shorter than the electron mean free path (q > ν/c ≈ 10⁸ cm⁻¹).

## Context

This work extends the microscopic treatment of electron-flux tube interactions in neutron-star cores, which began with Alpar, Langer and Sauls (1984) and was later generalized by Ruderman, Zhu and Chen (1998) using a hydrodynamic form of electrical conductivity. The screening condition **v**_p = **u**_e was a basic assumption in Gusakov (2018) and Sourie and Chamel (2020) for deriving the force on flux tubes and studying superfluid neutron vortices. The present calculation shows that at realistic parameters (d_v ∼ λ, not d_v ≫ λ as assumed in Gusakov (2018)), the electrons are in the particle-hole regime rather than the hydrodynamic regime, so the screening condition fails. This opens the question of how to correctly compute the rate of momentum exchange between electrons and the flux tube lattice in superconducting nuclear matter.
