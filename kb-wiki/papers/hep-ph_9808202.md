# The Quark Orbital Angular Momentum in a Light-Cone Representation

> We perform an analysis of the quark angular momentum in a light-cone representation by taking into account the effect due to the Melosh-Wigner rotation and find that there is a relativistic correction factor connecting the quark orbital angular momentum with the quark model spin distribution: $L_q(x)={<M_L(x)>}Δq_{QM}(x)$. The quark orbital angular momentum $L_q(x)$ and the quark helicity distribution $Δq(x)$ are connected to the quark model spin distribution $Δq_{QM}(x)$ by a relation: $\frac{1}{2}Δq(x)+ L_q(x)=\frac{1}{2}Δq_{QM}(x)$, which means that one can decompose the quark model spin contribution $Δq_{QM}(x)$ by a quark helicity term $Δq(x)$ {\it plus} an orbital angular momentum term $L_q(x)$. There is also a new relation connecting the quark orbital angular momentum with the measurable quark helicity distribution and transversity distribution ($δq(x)$): $Δq(x)+L_q(x)=δq(x)$, from which we may have new sum rules connecting the quark orbital angular momentum with the nucleon axial and tensor charges.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the quark orbital angular momentum $L_q(x)$ is connected to the quark model spin distribution $\Delta q_{QM}(x)$ via a Melosh-Wigner rotation factor, and that the quark helicity distribution $\Delta q(x)$ plus the orbital angular momentum $L_q(x)$ equals the transversity distribution $\delta q(x)$, providing a new relation among three measurable quantities. The authors hedge that these relations are derived in a three-quark valence model without higher Fock states, and are expected to be approximately valid up to a momentum scale $Q_0^2 \approx 1-5$ GeV$^2$.

## Method

The authors performed a light-cone analysis of quark angular momentum by applying the orbital angular momentum operator directly to light-cone spin state wavefunctions, incorporating the Melosh-Wigner rotation that relates light-cone spin states to instant-form spin states. They derived a new Melosh-Wigner rotation factor $M_L(x,\mathbf{k}_\perp) = \mathbf{k}_\perp^2 / [(k^+ + m)^2 + \mathbf{k}_\perp^2]$ for the orbital angular momentum, and used it to connect $L_q(x)$ to the quark model spin distribution. The relations were then applied in a light-cone SU(6) quark-spectator model to calculate $x$-dependent distributions for valence $u$ and $d$ quarks, and new sum rules for the integrated quark orbital angular momentum were proposed using measured quantities from polarized deep inelastic scattering.

## Key numbers

- The Melosh-Wigner rotation factor for orbital angular momentum is $M_L(x,\mathbf{k}_\perp) = \mathbf{k}_\perp^2 / [(k^+ + m)^2 + \mathbf{k}_\perp^2]$, derived from the operator ${\hat L}_q = -i(k_1 \partial/\partial k_2 - k_2 \partial/\partial k_1)$.
- Using E143 data at $\langle Q^2 \rangle = 3$ GeV$^2$ ($\Gamma^p = 0.127$, $\Gamma^n = -0.037$) and $g_A/g_V = 6(\Gamma^p - \Gamma^n)$, the isovector and isoscalar orbital angular momenta are both estimated as $L_u - L_d = 0.09 \to 0.34$ and $L_u + L_d = 0.09 \to 0.34$.
- Using SMC data at $\langle Q^2 \rangle = 10$ GeV$^2$ ($\Gamma^p = 0.136$, $\Gamma^n = -0.063$), the estimates become $L_u - L_d = -0.01 \to 0.24$ and $L_u + L_d = 0.12 \to 0.37$.
- Using $g_A/g_V = 1.2573$ from neutron beta decay, the isovector component is $L_u - L_d = -0.05 \to 0.20$.
- Combining all results with data uncertainties of 0.05, the final ranges are $L_u - L_d = -0.10 \to 0.39$ and $L_u + L_d = 0.04 \to 0.42$.
- From lattice QCD results for axial charge $\Delta \Sigma = 0.18 \pm 0.10$ and tensor charge $\delta \Sigma = 0.562 \pm 0.088$, the total quark orbital angular momentum is $\Sigma L_q = \delta \Sigma - \Delta \Sigma = 0.38 \pm 0.13$, implying $76 \pm 26\%$ of the proton spin is due to quark relativistic orbital motions.

## Context

This work extends the light-cone analysis of the proton spin structure by Ma (1991), Brodsky (1994), and Schmidt (1997), who previously derived Melosh-Wigner rotation factors for the quark helicity and transversity distributions. The paper provides a unified framework connecting the quark orbital angular momentum to the quark model spin distribution, and proposes new sum rules that relate $L_q$ to measurable axial and tensor charges from polarized deep inelastic scattering experiments (EMC, SMC, E142, E143). The results enable tests of the relativistic quark model picture of the proton spin, where the small quark helicity sum observed in polarized DIS is not in conflict with the quark model because the missing spin is compensated by relativistic orbital angular momentum.
