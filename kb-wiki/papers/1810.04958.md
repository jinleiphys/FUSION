# Determination of the photodisintegration reaction rates involving charged particles: systematical calculations and proposed measurements based on Extreme Light Infrastructure - Nuclear Physics (ELI-NP)

> Photodisintegration reaction rates involving charged particles are of relevance to the p-process nucleosynthesis that aims at explaining the production of the stable neutron-deficient nuclides heavier than iron. In this study, the cross sections and astrophysical rates of (g,p) and (g,a) reactions for about 3000 target nuclei with 10<Z<100 ranging from stable to proton dripline nuclei are computed. To study the sensitivity of the calculations to the optical model potentials (OMPs), both the phenomenological Woods-Saxon and the microscopic folding OMPs are taken into account. The systematic comparisons show that the reaction rates, especially for the (g,a) reaction, are dramatically influenced by the OMPs. Thus the better determination of the OMP is crucial to reduce the uncertainties of the photodisintegration reaction rates involving charged particles. Meanwhile, a gamma-beam facility at ELI-NP is being developed, which will open new opportunities to experimentally study the photodisintegration reactions of astrophysics interest. Considering both the important reactions identified by the nucleosynthesis studies and the purpose of complementing the experimental results for the reactions involving p-nuclei, the measurements of six (g,p) and eight (g,a) reactions based on the gamma-beam facility at ELI-NP and the ELISSA detector for the charged particles detection are proposed, and the GEANT4 simulations are correspondingly performed. The minimum required energies of the gamma-beam to measure these reactions are estimated. It is shown that the direct measurements of these photonuclear reactions within the Gamow windows at T_9=2.5 for p-process are fairly feasible and promising at ELI-NP. The expected experimental results will be used to constrain the OMPs of the charged particles, which can eventually reduce the uncertainties of the reaction rates for the p-process nucleosynthesis.

## Key claim

The photodisintegration reaction rates for (γ,p) and especially (γ,α) reactions are dramatically influenced by the choice of optical model potential (OMP), with differences reaching one order of magnitude for many nuclei; the authors propose that direct measurements at the ELI-NP gamma-beam facility can constrain these OMPs and reduce the uncertainties in p-process nucleosynthesis calculations.

## Method

The authors performed systematic Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations (including pre-equilibrium contributions via the exciton model) using the TALYS 1.8 code for (γ,p) and (γ,α) reactions on about 3000 target nuclei with atomic numbers 10 ≤ Z ≤ 100, from stable to the proton dripline. For each reaction type, two sets of optical model potentials were compared: for (γ,p) reactions, the phenomenological Woods-Saxon (Koning-Delaroche) and the microscopic JLMB folding OMPs; for (γ,α) reactions, the phenomenological Woods-Saxon and the microscopic M3Y double-folding OMPs. The authors then performed GEANT4 simulations of six (γ,p) and eight (γ,α) reactions using the calculated cross sections and the specifications of the ELI-NP gamma-beam facility and the ELISSA silicon strip detector array.

## Key numbers

*   Cross sections and astrophysical rates were computed for about 3000 target nuclei with 10 ≤ Z ≤ 100, from stable to proton dripline nuclei.
*   The gamma-beam facility at ELI-NP is specified to have a spectral density of 10^4 photons/s/eV, energies up to 19.5 MeV, and a bandwidth of 0.5%.
*   The Gamow window for the p-process is considered at a typical temperature of T_9 = 2.5 (2.5 × 10^9 K).
*   The ELISSA detector prototype achieved an energy resolution of less than 1% and a position resolution of 1 mm.
*   The simulation used a target structure consisting of a 10 μm target foil and a 0.266 μm Carbon backing.

## Context

This work extends systematic calculations of photodisintegration reaction rates for the p-process, which aims to explain the production of stable neutron-deficient nuclides heavier than iron. The study builds on prior Hauser-Feshbach calculations (cal2004) and sensitivity studies using Monte Carlo procedures (MNRAS2016, MNRAS2018) that identified key reactions for p-nuclei abundance. The proposed experiments at the ELI-NP facility, using the ELISSA detector developed jointly by ELI-NP and INFN-Laboratori Nazionali del Sud, are intended to provide direct measurements that can constrain the optical model potentials for charged particles, thereby reducing the dominant source of uncertainty in the theoretical reaction rates for p-process nucleosynthesis.
