# Stability analysis of non-thermal fixed points in longitudinally expanding kinetic theory

> We use the Hamiltonian formulation of kinetic theory to perform a stability analysis of non-thermal fixed points in a non-Abelian plasma. We construct a perturbative expansion of the Fokker-Planck collision kernel in an adiabatic approximation and show that the (next-to-)leading order solutions reproduce the known non-thermal fixed point scaling exponents. Working at next-to-leading order, we derive the stability equations for scaling exponents and find the relaxation rate to the non-thermal fixed point. This approach provides the basis for an understanding of the prescaling phenomena observed in QCD kinetic theory and non-relativistic Bose gas systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the non-thermal fixed point in a Fokker-Planck toy model of longitudinally expanding Yang-Mills plasma is stable under small perturbations, with the relaxation rate of scaling exponents to the fixed point derived at next-to-leading order in an adiabatic approximation.

## Method

The authors employ the Hamiltonian formulation of kinetic theory, recasting the Boltzmann equation with a Fokker-Planck collision kernel into an infinite set of ordinary differential equations for moments of the distribution function. They apply an adiabatic approximation, treating the time-dependent momentum diffusion parameter as slowly varying, and construct a perturbative expansion of the collision kernel to derive flow equations for the time-dependent scaling exponents. The analysis is performed at next-to-leading order in a small expansion parameter Δ(τ) that decays at large times near the fixed point.

## Key numbers

- The fixed point scaling exponents are α* = -2/3, β* = 0, γ* = 1/3, corresponding to the "bottom-up" thermalization scenario of Baier (2000)
- The fixed point value of the auxiliary parameter v is v* = -1/2
- The relaxation eigenvalues are λ₁ = -2 and λ₂ = -4/3, with eigenvectors h₁ = (-4/9, 1)ᵀ and h₂ = (0, 1)ᵀ
- The asymptotic value of v(τ → ∞) is given by v = (1 + 2α* - 2β* - γ*)/(3 + 2α* - 2β* - γ*)

## Context

This work extends the prescaling concept proposed by Schmied (2018) and Mazeliauskas (2018), who observed that before fully developed scaling, systems may exhibit dynamics governed by fixed-point scaling functions with time-dependent exponents. The analysis provides a theoretical foundation for understanding prescaling phenomena observed in QCD kinetic theory and non-relativistic Bose gas systems, and lays groundwork for studying scaling in more complex systems such as full QCD kinetic theory.
