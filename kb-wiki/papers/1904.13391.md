# Meson-meson scattering in two-dimensional QCD

> We extend the formalism pioneered by Callan, Coote and Gross to investigate the meson-meson scattering within the framework of 't Hooft model, i.e., the two-dimensional QCD in the $N_c\to \infty$ limit. We derive the analytic expressions for various two-body meson-meson scattering amplitudes, concentrating on those quark diagrams which may be identified as the meson-meson contact interaction vertex in the context of the mesonic effective lagrangian in $1/N_c$ expansion. We also carry out a detailed numerical study for the meson-meson scattering for various quark flavors, and observe the near-threshold enhancement in some channels. This may be viewed as the hint of the existence of the tetra-quark state below two-meson threshold.

## Key claim

The authors extend the Callan-Coote-Gross formalism to compute meson-meson scattering amplitudes in the 't Hooft model (two-dimensional QCD in the \(N_c\to\infty\) limit), focusing on contact interaction diagrams that may accommodate tetraquark states. They observe near-threshold enhancement in some scattering channels, which they interpret as a hint of tetraquark states below the two-meson threshold, but they do not find any Breit-Wigner resonance, contradicting an earlier claim by Batiz, Pena and Stadler (2003).

## Method

The authors work within the 't Hooft model using light-cone gauge, where the dressed quark propagator and Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states are solved in the large-\(N_c\) limit. They derive analytic expressions for the contact interaction part of the meson-meson scattering amplitude at order \(1/N_c\) by summing planar box diagrams and diagrams with one additional gluon exchange, considering all possible flavor structures (one-flavor, two-flavor, three-flavor, and four-flavor scatterings). The amplitudes are computed by integrating over the light-cone momentum fraction after performing the \(k_+\) integral, and the infrared regulator \(\rho\) is taken to zero after verifying cancellation of divergent terms.

## Key numbers

* Bare quark masses chosen to mimic realistic QCD spectrum: \(m_u = 15.3\ \text{MeV}\), \(m_d = 30.6\ \text{MeV}\), \(m_s = 254\ \text{MeV}\), \(m_c = 1.44\ \text{GeV}\), \(m_b = 4.61\ \text{GeV}\).
* All dimensional quantities expressed in units of \(\sqrt{2\lambda} = 340\ \text{MeV}\), where \(\lambda = g_s^2 N_c / (4\pi)\).
* Near-threshold enhancement observed in the contact interaction amplitude for one-flavor scattering \(A(c\bar c)+B(c\bar c)\to C(c\bar c)+D(c\bar c)\), two-flavor scattering \(A(c\bar s)+B(c\bar s)\to C(c\bar s)+D(c\bar s)\), and three-flavor scattering \(A(c\bar u)+B(c\bar d)\to C(c\bar u)+D(c\bar d)\).
* No near-threshold enhancement observed in the four-flavor channel \(A(c\bar d)+B(b\bar s)\to C(b\bar d)+D(c\bar s)\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Callan, Coote and Gross (1975) for meson decay amplitudes to the case of meson-meson scattering in the 't Hooft model. It directly contradicts the earlier analysis by Batiz, Pena and Stadler (2003), who claimed to find a Breit-Wigner resonance interpreted as a sigma-like tetraquark state; the present authors argue that Batiz et al. neglected important one-gluon exchange diagrams and used an infrared regulator that masked the gauge dependence of their results. The study is motivated by Weinberg's (2013) argument that narrow tetraquark states are not incompatible with large-\(N_c\) QCD, and the near-threshold enhancement observed here may guide searches for tetraquark candidates in realistic four-dimensional QCD.
