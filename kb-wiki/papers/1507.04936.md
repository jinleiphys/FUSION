# Nucleon and pion structure with lattice QCD simulations at physical value of the pion mass

> We present results on the nucleon scalar, axial and tensor charges as well as on the momentum fraction, and the helicity and transversity moments. The pion momentum fraction is also presented. The computation of these key observables is carried out using lattice QCD simulations at a physical value of the pion mass. The evaluation is based on gauge configurations generated with two degenerate sea quarks of twisted mass fermions with a clover term. We investigate excited states contributions with the nucleon quantum numbers by analyzing three sink-source time separations. We find that, for the scalar charge, excited states contribute significantly and to a less degree to the nucleon momentum fraction and helicity moment. Our analysis yields a value for the nucleon axial charge agrees with the experimental value and we predict a value of 1.027(62) in the $\overline{\text{MS}}$ scheme at 2 GeV for the isovector nucleon tensor charge directly at the physical point. The pion momentum fraction is found to be $\langle x\rangle_{u-d}^{π^\pm}=0.214(15)(^{+12}_{-9})$ in the $\overline{\rm MS}$ at 2 GeV.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first lattice QCD evaluation of six nucleon and pion structure observables directly at the physical pion mass, avoiding chiral extrapolations; however, the results for isoscalar quantities and individual up- and down-quark contributions exclude disconnected diagrams, which the authors note contribute about 7-10% to the isoscalar axial and scalar charges based on a separate higher-mass study.

## Method
The authors used lattice QCD with two degenerate flavors of twisted mass fermions including a clover term, on gauge configurations at a single lattice spacing of 0.093 fm with pion masses down to 131 MeV. They computed nucleon three-point functions at zero momentum transfer using sequential inversions through the sink, analyzing three sink-source time separations to control excited-state contamination via plateau, summation, and two-state fit methods. For the pion momentum fraction, they used stochastic time-slice sources and extracted the matrix element at a single sink-source separation of half the temporal lattice extent.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon isovector tensor charge: \( g_T^{u-d} = 1.027(62) \) in the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme at 2 GeV, computed directly at the physical pion mass
- Pion isovector momentum fraction: \( \langle x \rangle_{u-d}^{\pi^\pm} = 0.214(15)(^{+12}_{-9}) \) in the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme at 2 GeV
- Lattice spacing: \( a = 0.093(1) \) fm, determined using the nucleon mass
- Physical pion mass ensemble: \( m_\pi = 0.1312(13) \) GeV on a \( 48^3 \times 96 \) lattice with spatial extent \( L = 4.46 \) fm

## Context
This work extends the methodology developed by the same collaboration in earlier studies (Alexandrou 2010, 2011, 2013) and builds on the ensemble generation described in Abdel-Rehim (2015) for meson masses and decay constants. The direct computation at the physical pion mass avoids the systematic uncertainties of chiral extrapolations that plagued earlier lattice calculations, and the results for the axial charge agree with the experimental value, establishing the method for predicting the tensor charge and moments that are relevant for beyond-Standard-Model searches in neutron decay and dark matter detection experiments.
