# Strong decays of the latest LHCb pentaquark candidates in hadronic molecule pictures

> We investigate the observed pentaquark candidates $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ from the latest LHCb measurement, as well as four possible spin partners in the $\bar{D}^{(*)}Σ_c^*$ system predicted from the heavy quark spin symmetry with the hadronic molecule scenarios. Similar to the previous calculation on $P_c(4380)$ and $P_c(4450)$, the partial widths of all the allowed decay channels for these $P_c$ states are estimated with the effective Lagrangian method. The cutoff dependence of our numerical results are also presented. Comparing with the experimental widths, our results show that $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$ and $P_c(4457)$ can be described well with the spin-parity-$1/2^-$-$\bar{D}Σ_c$, $1/2^-$-$\bar{D}^*Σ_c$ and $3/2^-$-$\bar{D}^*Σ_c$ molecule pictures, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the three LHCb pentaquark candidates \(P_c(4312)\), \(P_c(4440)\), and \(P_c(4457)\) can be described well as \(S\)-wave hadronic molecules with specific spin-parity assignments, but this conclusion depends on the choice of cutoff parameters and form factors used in the effective Lagrangian calculation.

## Method

The authors use an effective Lagrangian approach with the \(L\)-\(S\) coupling scheme to compute partial decay widths of the \(P_c\) states, treating them as pure \(\bar{D}^{(*)}\Sigma_c^{(*)}\) hadronic molecules. Two-body decays are calculated via triangle diagrams with one meson exchange, and three-body decays through the finite width of \(\Sigma_c^*\) are also considered. The effective couplings between the \(P_c\) states and their constituent meson-baryon pairs are estimated using the compositeness condition, and the cutoff parameters \(\Lambda_0\) and \(\Lambda_1\) are varied between 0.6 and 1.4 GeV to study the dependence of the results.

## Key numbers

- \(P_c(4312)\): mass \(4311.9 \pm 0.7^{+6.8}_{-0.6}\) MeV, width \(9.8 \pm 2.7^{+3.7}_{-4.5}\) MeV (from LHCb data, Table in Introduction).
- \(P_c(4440)\): mass \(4440.3 \pm 1.3^{+4.1}_{-4.7}\) MeV, width \(20.6 \pm 4.9^{+8.7}_{-10.1}\) MeV.
- \(P_c(4457)\): mass \(4457.3 \pm 0.6^{+4.1}_{-1.7}\) MeV, width \(6.4 \pm 2.0^{+5.7}_{-1.9}\) MeV.
- With \(\Lambda_0 = 1.0\) GeV and \(\Lambda_1 = 0.6\) GeV using form factor set \((f_1, f_3)\), the total width of \(P_c(4312)\) as a \(1/2^-\) \(\bar{D}\Sigma_c\) molecule is 3.9 MeV; \(P_c(4440)\) as \(1/2^-\) \(\bar{D}^*\Sigma_c\) gives 23.7 MeV; \(P_c(4457)\) as \(3/2^-\) \(\bar{D}^*\Sigma_c\) gives 14.7 MeV (Table III).
- With the same cutoffs but form factor set \((f_2, f_3)\), the total width of \(P_c(4312)\) is 13.2 MeV; \(P_c(4440)\) as \(1/2^-\) gives 22.4 MeV; \(P_c(4457)\) as \(3/2^-\) gives 17.9 MeV (Table IV).
- The binding energies used are approximately 10 MeV for \(P_c(4312)\), 20 MeV for \(P_c(4440)\), and 5 MeV for \(P_c(4457)\) relative to their respective thresholds.

## Context

This work extends previous molecular interpretations of the earlier LHCb pentaquarks \(P_c(4380)\) and \(P_c(4450)\) by Lin and Zou (2017) to the newly observed states from LHCb (Aaij et al., 2019). It also investigates four additional predicted \(\bar{D}^{(*)}\Sigma_c^*\) molecules from heavy quark spin symmetry, following predictions by Xiao et al. (2013) and Liu et al. (2019). The results enable future experimental determination of the spin-parity of these states by comparing predicted branching ratios, particularly the \(\bar{D}\Sigma_c\) to \(\bar{D}\Sigma_c^*\) ratio and the \(\eta_c p\) to \(J/\psi p\) ratio, which differ significantly between the \(1/2^-\) and \(3/2^-\) assignments.
