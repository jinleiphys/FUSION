# Relating eccentricity fluctuations to density fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions

> The magnitude of anisotropic flow in a nucleus-nucleus collision is determined by the energy density field, $ρ(x,y,z)$, created right after the collision occurs. Specifically, elliptic flow, $v_2$, and triangular flow, $v_3$, are proportional to the anisotropy coefficients $\varepsilon_2$ and $\varepsilon_3$, which are functionals of $ρ$. We express the mean and the variance of $\varepsilon_2$ and $\varepsilon_3$ as a function of the 1- and 2-point functions of $ρ$. These results generalize results obtained previously, that were valid only for central collisions, or only for identical point-like sources. We apply them to the color glass condensate effective theory, using the recently derived expression of the 2-point function.

## Key claim

The paper derives general expressions for the mean and variance of the initial anisotropy coefficients ε₂ and ε₃ in terms of the 1- and 2-point functions of the energy density field, generalizing previous results that were valid only for central collisions or for identical point-like sources; the new terms beyond the leading contribution are significant only for large impact parameters, where they produce a modest reduction of anisotropic flow fluctuations.

## Method

The authors perform a perturbative expansion of ε₂ and ε₃ to second order in the density fluctuation δρ, then compute statistical averages over events at fixed impact parameter. They express the resulting 2-point averages in terms of the 1-point function ⟨ρ(s)⟩ and the 2-point function S(s₁,s₂), assuming short-range correlations. The formalism is applied to the color glass condensate (CGC) effective theory using the 1- and 2-point functions evaluated by Albacete et al. (2018), and the results are compared to ALICE data for Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale at the center of the nucleus: Q_s0 = 1.06 GeV, which gives a good fit to LHC data.
- Hydrodynamic response coefficients used: κ₂ = 0.237 and κ₃ = 0.195, taken from Giacalone et al. (2019).
- Infrared cutoff m = 0.14 GeV (pion mass) used in CGC calculations.
- For CGC at b = 10 fm, the fixed-energy corrections to ⟨ε₂⟩ and σ² are 8×10⁻⁴ and −3.5×10⁻⁵, respectively, and smaller at smaller impact parameters.
- Nuclear cross section for Pb+Pb: σ_PbPb = 764 fm², used to define centrality as πb²/σ_PbPb.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Blaizot et al. (2014), which was valid only for central collisions (b=0), and generalizes results for identical point-like sources from Bhalerao et al. (2006) and Bhalerao et al. (2011) to arbitrary 2-point functions. The results validate the approximation used in Giacalone et al. (2019), where only the leading terms were considered, and enable more precise calculations of initial-state anisotropies in non-central collisions for any model of initial conditions, including the CGC effective theory.
