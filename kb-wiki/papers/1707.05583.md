# Multinucleon transfer dynamics in heavy-ion collisions near Coulomb barrier energies

> The multinucleon transfer reactions near barrier energies has been investigated with a multistep model based on the dinuclear system (DNS) concept, in which the capture of two colliding nuclei, the transfer dynamics and the de-excitation process of primary fragments are described by the analytical formula, the diffusion theory and the statistical model, respectively. The nucleon transfer takes place after forming the DNS and is coupled to the dissipation of relative motion energy and angular momentum by solving a set of microscopically derived master equations within the potential energy surface. Specific reactions of $^{40,48}$Ca+$^{124}$Sn, $^{40}$Ca ($^{40}$Ar, $^{58}$Ni)+$^{232}$Th, $^{40}$Ca ($^{58}$Ni)+$^{238}$U and $^{40,48}$Ca ($^{58}$Ni) +$^{248}$Cm near barrier energies are investigated. It is found that the fragments are produced by the multinucleon transfer reactions with the maximal yields along the $β$-stability line. The isospin relaxation is particularly significant in the process of fragment formation. The incident energy dependence of heavy target-like fragments in the reaction of $^{58}$Ni+$^{248}$Cm is analyzed thoroughly.

## Key claim

The multinucleon transfer (MNT) reaction mechanism near Coulomb barrier energies can be described by a multistep dinuclear system (DNS) model that couples nucleon transfer to the dissipation of relative motion energy and angular momentum; the model reproduces available experimental data, but the yields of calcium and potassium isotopes in the \(^{40}\)Ca+\(^{124}\)Sn reaction are underestimated because the model loses quasielastic scattering.

## Method

The authors model MNT reactions in three stages: capture of the two colliding nuclei (described by an analytical formula with a barrier distribution), nucleon transfer dynamics (solved via microscopically derived master equations on the potential energy surface, coupled to dissipation of relative motion and angular momentum), and de-excitation of primary fragments (treated with a statistical model based on Weisskopf evaporation theory, including competition with binary fission). The transition probability in the master equations considers only one-nucleon transfer steps (\(Z' = Z \pm 1\), \(N' = N \pm 1\)), and the interaction time is obtained from the deflection function method.

## Context

This work extends the DNS model, previously used by Feng et al. (2006, 2009, 2010) to describe superheavy nucleus formation, to the multinucleon transfer regime near the Coulomb barrier. It builds on experimental studies of damped collisions of actinide nuclei at GSI (Hildenbrand et al. 1977; Schadel et al. 1978; Kratz et al. 2013) and recent MNT experiments at Dubna (Kozulin et al. 2012), Argonne (Barrett et al. 2015), and GANIL (Walsh et al. 2015). The results enable planning of experiments at the Heavy-Ion Accelerator Research Facility in Lanzhou (HIRFL) to produce neutron-rich heavy nuclei, and suggest that the incident energy should be selected close to the Coulomb barrier for optimal fragment survival.
