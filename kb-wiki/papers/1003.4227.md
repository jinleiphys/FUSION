# Generalised mass formula for non-strange, strange and multiply-strange nuclear systems

> A simultaneous description of non-strange nuclei, hypernuclei and multiply-strange nuclear systems is provided by a single mass formula which is shown to be useful for estimating binding energies of nuclear systems over a wide mass range, including the light mass nuclei. It not only provides a good fit to the existing experimental data on hyperon-separation energies but also reproduces results of the relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations. In addition, it can provide the Lambda($Λ$), Cascade-0($Ξ^0$) and Cascade-minus ($Ξ^-$) drip lines. The existence of a range of bound pure-hyperonic systems without any neutron and proton is suggested among which $6Λ$, $9Ξ^0Ξ^0$, $10Ξ^-Ξ^-$, $1\Lambda7Ξ^0$, $1Λ8Ξ^-$, $1Ξ^09 Ξ^-$, $1Ξ^- 8Ξ^0$ and $2Λ+3Ξ^0+3Ξ^-$ represent the lightest species. In agreement with the RMF predictions, this generalized mass formula also predicts the nucleus $_{2Ξ^0 2Λ}{^8He}$ to be bound. An exotic $_{2Ξ^0 2Ξ^- 2Λ}{^{10}n}$ nucleus is also found to be bound. This new mass formula can be used in astrophysics for strange stellar objects, as well as in high energy physics for estimating the strangeness production yield in nucleus-nucleus or, nucleon-nucleon collisions.

## Key claim
A single generalized mass formula can simultaneously describe non-strange nuclei, hypernuclei, and multiply-strange nuclear systems, reproducing both experimental hyperon-separation energies and relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations, but only after introducing a new hyperon-number asymmetry parameter \(a_Y = 0.02\) that was not present in earlier formulations.

## Method
The author extends a previous mass formula for non-strange and singly-strange hypernuclei (Samanta, 2006) by adding a hyperon-number asymmetry term analogous to the neutron-proton asymmetry term in the Bethe-Weizsäcker formula. The new term depends on the squared difference between the total hyperon number and the proton and neutron numbers, scaled by a single fitted parameter \(a_Y = 0.02\). The formula is then compared to experimental hyperon separation energies and to RMF calculations of Schaffner et al. (1994) for multiply-strange systems, including pure hyperonic matter.

## Key numbers
- The hyperon-number asymmetry parameter is set to \(a_Y = 0.02\) to reproduce both experimental data and RMF (Model 2) results.
- The lightest bound pure hyperonic system without neutrons or protons is \(2\Lambda+3\Xi^0+3\Xi^-\) with binding energy per nucleon \(E_B/A = -0.603\) MeV.
- The minimum number of \(\Lambda\) hyperons that form a bound pure hyperonic system is 6\(\Lambda\); the maximum is 304\(\Lambda\).
- For pure \(\Xi^0\) systems, the minimum bound is \(9\Xi^0\) (\(E_B/A = -1.57\) MeV) and the maximum is \(748\Xi^0\) (\(E_B/A = -0.002\) MeV).
- For pure \(\Xi^-\) systems, the minimum bound is \(10\Xi^-\) (\(E_B/A = -0.63\) MeV) and the maximum is \(155\Xi^-\) (\(E_B/A = -0.01\) MeV).
- The exotic nucleus \(_{2\Xi^0 2\Xi^- 2\Lambda}{^{10}n}\) is bound with \(E_B/A = -5.98\) MeV.
- The nucleus \(_{2\Xi^0 2\Lambda}{^8He}\) is bound with \(E_B/A = -4.51\) MeV, in agreement with RMF predictions.

## Context
This work extends the hypernuclear mass formula of Samanta (2006) to multiply-strange systems, building on the generalized Bethe-Weizsäcker approach first proposed by Dover and Gal (1993) and later updated by Balberg et al. (1994) to match RMF calculations of Schaffner et al. (1994). The new formula reproduces both experimental data and RMF results, including those with hyperon-hyperon (YY) interactions (Model 2), and can be used in astrophysics for strange stellar objects and in high-energy physics to estimate strangeness production yields in nucleus-nucleus or nucleon-nucleon collisions. It also provides hyperon drip lines for cores such as \(^{56}Ni\), \(^{208}Pb\), and \(^{310}G\), enabling predictions for upcoming experiments at JLab, J-PARC, GSI, and LHC.
