# Width effects of broad new resonances in loop observables and application to $(g-2)_μ$

> In the phenomenology of strong interactions most physical states acquire a substantial width, and thus can only be defined in a model-independent way by pole positions and residues of the $S$-matrix. This information is incorporated in the Källén-Lehmann representation, whose spectral function characterizes the shape of the resonance and can be constrained by the dominant decay channels. Here, we argue that similar effects become important whenever beyond-the-Standard-Model particles possess a sizable decay width - as possible for instance in cases with a large branching fraction to a dark sector or strongly coupled scenarios - and show how their widths can be incorporated in the calculation of loop observables. As an application, we consider the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, including both the direct effect of new physics and the possible indirect impact of a broad light $Z'$ on $e^+e^-\to\text{hadrons}$ cross sections. Throughout, we provide results for a general spectral function and its reconstruction from the one-loop imaginary part, where the latter captures the leading two-loop effects.

## Key claim

The paper argues that when beyond-the-Standard-Model particles have a sizable decay width (e.g., from decays to a dark sector or in strongly coupled scenarios), their width effects must be incorporated into loop observables via the Källén-Lehmann spectral representation to avoid unphysical imaginary parts; the authors show that for moderate widths the spectral function can be reconstructed from the one-loop self-energy, capturing the leading two-loop effects.

## Method

The authors match the Källén-Lehmann representation to the Dyson series for scalar, fermion, and spin-1 particles, deriving spectral functions from the imaginary part of the one-loop self-energy with energy-dependent widths constrained by dominant decay channels. They then apply this formalism to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, computing both the direct BSM contribution and the indirect effect of a broad light Z' on the e+e- → hadrons cross section used in the data-driven HVP evaluation.

## Key numbers

- The ρ(770) resonance has Γρ/Mρ ≃ 20%, cited as an example of a moderate width where the KL representation is phenomenologically successful
- The current discrepancy between experiment and SM prediction for aμ is at the 4.2σ level
- For a Z' with MZ' = 1 GeV, the relative suppression of the aμ contribution is shown as a function of ΓZ'/MZ' (up to ~40% for Γ/M ~ 0.5 with n=1)
- For a Z' with MZ' = 0.8 GeV, ΓZ' = 0.2 GeV, and εZ' = 0.02, the shift Δaμ from BSM effects in HVP is shown as a function of the decay product mass M1 (up to ~5 × 10^-11 for r=3, n=1)
- The π0γ contribution calculation using an asymptotic expansion misses the correct value by a factor of 10

## Context

This work extends the use of Källén-Lehmann representations from hadronic phenomenology (where they improve Breit-Wigner parameterizations for resonances like the ρ(770), as done by Lomon (2012), Moussallam (2013), and Zanke (2021)) to BSM physics with broad new particles. It enables consistent calculation of loop observables for models with large widths, such as Lμ-Lτ Z' models used to address the aμ anomaly (Gninenko 2001, Baek 2001, Altmannshofer 2014) or dark matter portal scenarios, and provides a framework to assess whether broad Z' solutions to the HVP tension (preliminarily excluded by DiLuzio (2021) using constant-width propagators) could be revived with proper width treatment.
