# Potential roots of the deep sub-barrier heavy-ion fusion hindrance phenomenon

> We analyse the origin of the unexpected deep sub-barrier heavy-ion fusion hindrance in 64Ni+100Mo and 28Si+64Ni recations. Our analysis is based on the improved coupled-channels approach, implemented by means of the finite element method. With the aid of the Woods-Saxon potential the experimental cross sections and the S-factors of these reactions are remarkably well reproduced. We found that the account on the non-diagonal matrix elements of the coupling matrix, traditionally neglected in the conventional coupled-channels approaches in setting the left boundary conditions inside the potential pocket, and its minimal value are crucially important for the interpretation experimental data. Within our approach we found a good agreement with the experimental data for the S-factor of the fusion reaction 12C+12C, which has no a pronounced maximum for this system.

## Key claim

The deep sub-barrier heavy-ion fusion hindrance phenomenon can be reproduced using a simple Woods-Saxon potential without a repulsive core, provided that the coupled-channels equations are solved with an improved incoming wave boundary condition that properly accounts for the non-diagonal matrix elements of the coupling matrix at the left boundary inside the potential pocket.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled-channels equations using the finite element method (KANTBP code) with improved incoming wave boundary conditions at the minimum of the potential pocket. At the left boundary, they diagonalize the coupling matrix to obtain the correct linear independent solutions, rather than neglecting the off-diagonal matrix elements as in conventional approaches. The Woods-Saxon potential parameters are fitted to reproduce experimental fusion cross sections and astrophysical S-factors for the reactions 64Ni+100Mo, 28Si+64Ni, and 12C+12C.

## Key numbers

- For 64Ni+100Mo: Woods-Saxon parameters V0 = 79.938 MeV, a0 = 0.686 fm, R0 = 10.190 fm; potential barrier VB = 136.993 MeV; potential pocket minimum VP = 119.344 MeV
- For 28Si+64Ni: Woods-Saxon parameters V0 = 62.707 MeV, a0 = 1.014 fm, R0 = 7.354 fm; potential barrier VB = 52.697 MeV; potential pocket minimum VP = 43.027 MeV
- For 12C+12C: Woods-Saxon parameters V0 = 34.252 MeV, R0 = 3.865 fm, a0 = 0.952 fm; potential pocket minimum about -7 MeV
- Scaling parameters eta0 for the astrophysical S-factor: 105.74 for 64Ni+100Mo, 75.23 for 28Si+64Ni, and 41.25 for 12C+12C
- The sum of tunneling and reflection probabilities from the KANTBP code is 1 minus approximately 10^{-10}, providing a stringent test of calculation validity

## Context

This work extends the improved coupled-channels approach developed in Wen (2020) and Vinitsky (2020) to explain the deep sub-barrier fusion hindrance phenomenon first observed in precise measurements by Jiang (2002). It contradicts the prevailing view that conventional coupled-channels calculations with a Woods-Saxon potential are insufficient to reproduce experimental data, as stated by Jiang (2004), Montagnoli (2017), and Back (2014). The approach contrasts with alternative explanations such as the hybrid potential method of Misicu (2006) and Hagino (2018), the adiabatic approximation of Ichikawa (2009), and the density-constrained frozen Hartree-Fock method of Simenel (2017). The results for 12C+12C are similar to those of the coupled-channels theory with M3Y plus repulsive core potential in Esbensen (2011), and the empirical trends for the hindrance factor follow the systematics discussed by Jiang (2006) regarding the product Z1Z2*sqrt(mu).
