# Remarks on Strong CP-Violating Lagrangians

> Owing to a different treatment of the vacuum alignment, the strong CP-violating Lagrangian obtained by Di Vecchia, Veneziano and Witten (DVW) 3 decades ago do not look quite the same as the one originally derived by Baluni at the quark or hadron level. We show that they are consistent with each other and emphasize that, within the DVW approach, the $θG\tilde G$ term is not entirely removed away after the vacuum is rotated from the CP-odd state to the CP-even one; strong CP violation resides not only in the quark mass terms but also in the residual topological sector. While the latter relies on the Dashen's theorem, the latter is based on the minimization of a potential energy with respect to the phase of the quark condensate. Contrary to some claims, it is necessary to include the SU(3)-singlet $η_0$ tadpole contribution for strong CP-odd effects induced by the Baluni-type Lagrangian to ensure that strong CP violation vanishes in the zero axial anomaly limit.

## Key claim

The strong CP-violating Lagrangians derived by Di Vecchia, Veneziano and Witten (DVW) and by Baluni are consistent with each other, but within the DVW approach the sum of the phases of the quark condensate is not exactly equal to the theta parameter; a small calculable deviation remains, so strong CP violation resides both in the quark mass terms and in a residual topological sector.

## Method

The author compares two approaches for vacuum alignment in QCD: the Baluni-Crewther method using Dashen's theorem (minimizing the vacuum expectation value of the mass term with respect to SU(n) rotations) and the DVW method (minimizing the full potential energy including the topological charge density). The equivalence of the two minimization conditions is shown analytically, and the residual topological contribution is quantified. The chiral Lagrangian formalism in the leading 1/N_c expansion is used to derive the meson-level CP-odd operators and to compute the CP-violating pion-nucleon coupling as a test case.

## Key numbers

- The parameter a (mass squared of eta_0 in the chiral limit) is approximately 0.73 GeV^2, and v (characterizing spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking) is approximately 1.6 GeV.
- The residual deviation bar_theta = theta - sum(phi_i) is approximately 2 x 10^(-2) theta numerically.
- The pion decay constant f_pi = 132 MeV.
- The CP-violating pion-nucleon coupling bar_g_{pi NN} is given by 2*sqrt(2)*theta*(bar_m/f_pi)*(m_Xi - m_Sigma)/(2m_s - m_u - m_d)*(1 - I), where I involves the eta-eta' mixing and vanishes in the zero anomaly limit (a -> 0) ensuring bar_g_{pi NN} -> 0.

## Context

This work clarifies and reconciles two classic approaches to strong CP violation in QCD: the quark-level Lagrangian of Baluni (1979) and the chiral Lagrangian approach of Di Vecchia, Veneziano, and Witten (1980-1981). It extends the analysis of Crewther (1980) on vacuum alignment constraints and addresses a claim in the literature about the role of the eta_0 tadpole. The result enables consistent use of either Lagrangian in calculations of CP-odd observables such as the neutron electric dipole moment, and it notes that P- and CP-violating metastable domains in heavy ion collisions at RHIC provide a new experimental avenue to probe the theta vacuum.
