# $Δ$ resonances in Ca+Ca, Ni+Ni and Au+Au reactions from 1 AGeV to 2 AGeV: Consistency between yields, mass shifts and decoupling temperatures

> The Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport approach is used to calculate $Δ$(1232) yields in Ca+Ca, Ni+Ni and Au+Au collisions between 1 AGeV and 2 AGeV. We compare and validate two different methods to extract the yields of $Δ$(1232) resonances in such low energy nuclear collisions: Firstly, the $π^-$ spectra at low $p_\mathrm{T}$ are used to infer the $Δ$(1232) yield in A+A collisions, a method employed by the GSI/FOPI experiment. Secondly, we employ the invariant mass method used by the HADES collaboration, which has recently reported data in the $Δ^{++}\rightarrowπ^++p$ channel. We show that both methods are compatible with each other and with the theoretical calculations, indicating that the new HADES results are compatible with the previous FOPI measurements. Then we use the $Δ/nucleon$ ratio to extract the kinetic decoupling temperatures of the $Δ$(1232) resonances. We find that the extracted temperatures are consistent with the predicted mass shift of the $Δ$ resonance and the freeze-out parameters estimated from complementary studies (blast wave fits, coarse graining).

## Key claim

The paper shows that two different experimental methods for extracting Delta(1232) resonance yields in heavy-ion collisions (the low-pT pion method used by FOPI and the invariant mass method used by HADES) are mutually consistent when applied within the UrQMD transport model, and that the extracted decoupling temperatures are consistent with predicted mass shifts of the Delta resonance, but only for the specific energy range of 1 to 2 AGeV and for the specific systems studied.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD v3.4) transport model in cascade mode to simulate central collisions of Ca+Ca, Ni+Ni, and Au+Au at beam energies between 1.06 and 1.93 AGeV. They extracted Delta(1232) yields using two methods: (1) the low-pT pion method, where pions with pT below 0.3 GeV are taken as proxies for Delta decay products and scaled by an isospin factor from the isobar model, and (2) microscopic tracking of each decaying Delta resonance and its daughter particles to determine which Deltas are reconstructable. The Delta/nucleon ratios were then used in a thermal model in Boltzmann approximation to extract kinetic decoupling temperatures.

## Key numbers

- Delta resonance fraction increases from 10% to 20% when beam energy increases from 1 AGeV to 2 AGeV across all systems studied
- For Ni+Ni at 1.06 AGeV: decoupling temperature T = 74 MeV (microscopic UrQMD), 72 MeV (low-pT method), compared to FOPI value of 75 +/- 5 MeV
- For Ni+Ni at 1.45 AGeV: T = 85 MeV (microscopic), 82 MeV (low-pT), compared to FOPI value of 80 +/- 7 MeV
- For Ni+Ni at 1.93 AGeV: T = 95 MeV (microscopic), 93 MeV (low-pT), compared to FOPI value of 89 +/- 9 MeV
- For Au+Au at 1.23 AGeV: T = 76 MeV (microscopic), 72 MeV (low-pT); predicted mass shift of -55 MeV
- For Ca+Ca at 1.76 AGeV: T = 92 MeV (microscopic), 91 MeV (low-pT); predicted mass shift of -45 MeV
- The isobar scaling factors used: 2.84 for Ni+Ni, 2.24 for Au+Au, 3.0 for Ca+Ca
- For HADES Au+Au data extraction: n(pi-) = 17.1 +/- 0.9, <pi- low/pi- all> = 0.86, participants = 303 +/- 12

## Context

This work extends the FOPI collaboration's measurements (Hong 1997) of Delta yields in Ni+Ni collisions using the low-pT pion method and connects them to recent HADES collaboration data (Kornakov 2019, Adamczewski-Musch 2020) on Delta resonances in Au+Au and Ca+Ca systems. The extracted temperatures confirm previous estimates by Reichert (2019) linking the Delta spectral function to freeze-out temperature and predicted mass shifts of -47 MeV at 81 MeV temperature. The results enable future comparisons with thermal model predictions and support the use of either experimental method for Delta yield extraction in the GSI energy regime.
