# Anisotropic hydrodynamics with boost-non-invariant expansion

> We establish the anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) equations based on a boost-non-invariant longitudinally expanding system. Good consistency is found in the comparison between the aHydro results with those from the Boltzmann equation under relaxation time approximation. We also obtain corresponding attractor solution and observe the time delay of convergence when increasing the absolute value of the rapidity. We finally show that the rapidity dependence is solved by introducing the redefined attractor variables to compensate the time delay effect.

# Anisotropic hydrodynamics with boost-non-invariant expansion

## Key claim

The anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) equations based on the Romatschke-Strickland form can be extended to boost-non-invariant longitudinally expanding systems, but only for the symmetric case (a=1) where the system returns to symmetric forward-backward rapidity; for general asymmetry parameter a, the equations of motion lead to contradictory cross-derivative relations and cannot be simultaneously satisfied.

## Method

The authors construct aHydro equations in a comoving frame derived from an analytical solution with non-trivial rapidity dependence (Shi 2022), using Milne coordinates with proper-time τ and rapidity η. They take the Romatschke-Strickland ansatz for the distribution function, characterized by an anisotropy parameter ξ and energy scale Λ, and derive evolution equations from energy conservation and the longitudinal-transverse difference of third-order tensor moments. The aHydro results are compared with formal solutions of the Boltzmann equation under relaxation time approximation (RTA) for the symmetric case a=1, using identical initial RS-type distributions.

## Key numbers

- The asymmetry parameter a is constrained to the range (1/√2, √2), with a=1 corresponding to the symmetric forward-backward rapidity case
- For long-time asymptotic behavior, the temperature scales as ℛ^(1/4)Λ = c₀/τ̂^(1/3) - 2η̄/(3τ̂) and the anisotropy parameter ξ = 10η̄/c₀ τ̂^(-2/3) + 200η̄²/(3c₀²) τ̂^(-4/3)
- The shear-viscosity-to-entropy ratio used in comparisons is η̄ = 5/(4π)
- Initial parameters for the main comparison are Λ₀ = 1/τᵢ and ξ₀ = 1
- Convergence to the attractor solution at different rapidity η shows a factor of approximately 3 difference in convergence time
- The time constant t₀ = 0.01 τᵢ is used for introducing rapidity dependence in the attractor study

## Context

This work extends previous boost-invariant aHydro studies (Strickland 2017, Strickland 2018) and attractor solutions found for Müller-Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics (Heller 2011, Heller 2013, Heller 2015) to systems with longitudinal structure. The authors build upon an analytical solution with non-trivial rapidity dependence (Shi 2022) and demonstrate that aHydro remains a good approximation to the RTA Boltzmann equation even when boost-invariance is relaxed. The redefined attractor variables introduced here (Chen 2024) compensate for the rapidity-dependent convergence time, showing universal behavior before isotropization that occurs earlier than convergence with Müller-Israel-Stewart attractor and Navier-Stokes solutions. This enables more realistic modeling of heavy-ion collisions where non-trivial rapidity distributions are present.
