# Deconfinement of neutron star matter within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> We study the deconfinement transition of hadronic matter into quark matter under neutron star conditions assuming color and flavor conservation during the transition. We use a two-phase description. For the hadronic phase we use different parameterizations of a non-linear Walecka model which includes the whole baryon octet. For the quark matter phase we use an SU(3)_f Nambu-Jona-Lasinio effective model including color superconductivity. Deconfinement is considered to be a first order phase transition that conserves color and flavor. It gives a short-lived transitory colorless-quark-phase that is not in beta-equilibrium, and decays to a stable configuration in tau ~ tau_{weak} ~ 10^{-8} s. However, in spite of being very short lived, the transition to this intermediate phase determines the onset of the transition inside neutron stars. We find the transition free-energy density for temperatures typical of neutron star interiors. We also find the critical mass above which compact stars should contain a quark core and below which they are safe with respect to a sudden transition to quark matter. Rather independently on the stiffness of the hadronic equation of state (EOS) we find that the critical mass of hadronic stars (without trapped neutrinos) is in the range of ~ 1.5 - 1.8 solar masses. This is in coincidence with previous results obtained within the MIT Bag model.

## Key claim

The deconfinement transition from hadronic matter to quark matter in neutron stars proceeds through a short-lived intermediate colorless quark phase that is not in beta-equilibrium, and this intermediate phase determines the onset of the transition even though it decays to a stable configuration on weak interaction timescales (tau ~ 10^{-8} s).

## Method

The authors use a two-phase description with a non-linear Walecka model (including the full baryon octet) for the hadronic phase and an SU(3)_f Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) effective model including color superconductivity for the quark phase. They impose color and flavor conservation during the first-order phase transition, requiring that the just-deconfined quark matter has the same flavor composition as the beta-stable hadronic matter from which it originates. The Gibbs criteria (equal pressure, Gibbs free energy per baryon, and temperature) determine the transition conditions, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are integrated to find the critical stellar mass for quark core formation.

## Key numbers

* Critical mass of hadronic stars (without trapped neutrinos) is in the range ~ 1.5 - 1.8 solar masses, rather independently of the stiffness of the hadronic equation of state.
* For NJL parameter set 1, the critical mass range is 1.65-1.80 M_sun across three hadronic EOS parameterizations (GM1, GM4, GM5).
* For NJL parameter set 2, the critical mass range is 1.45-1.60 M_sun.
* The maximum masses of pure hadronic stars for the three parameterizations are: GM1 = 1.78 M_sun, GM4 = 2.2 M_sun, GM5 = 2.35 M_sun.
* The intermediate colorless quark phase decays to a stable configuration in tau ~ tau_weak ~ 10^{-8} s.
* The NJL model parameters include: set 1 with m_u,d = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 112.0 MeV, Lambda = 602.3 MeV, G*Lambda^2 = 4.638; set 2 with m_u,d = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 110.05 MeV, Lambda = 631.4 MeV, G*Lambda^2 = 4.370; both with H/G = 3/4.

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of the deconfinement transition that used the MIT Bag model (Lugones 2005, Bombaci 2007) by employing the NJL model for the quark phase, which provides a more realistic treatment of chiral symmetry breaking and color superconductivity. The results for the critical mass (1.5-1.8 M_sun) coincide with those obtained within the MIT Bag model for hybrid stars. The study is relevant for neutron stars older than about 1 minute, when trapped neutrinos have left the system, and enables future work incorporating finite-size effects (surface tension and curvature) in the nucleation of quark bubbles within the NJL framework.
