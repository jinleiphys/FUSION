# Effect of nuclear saturation parameters on possible maximum mass of neutron stars

> In order to systematically examine the possible maximum mass of neutron stars, which is one of the important properties characterizing the physics in high-density region, I construct neutron star models by adopting phenomenological equations of state with various values of nuclear saturation parameters for low-density region, which are connected to the equation of state for high-density region characterized by the possible maximum sound velocity in medium. I derive an empirical formula for the possible maximum mass of neutron star. If massive neutron stars are observed, it could be possible to get a constraint on the possible maximum sound velocity for high-density region.

## Key claim

The possible maximum mass of a neutron star can be expressed as an empirical linear function of the nuclear saturation parameter η = (K₀ L²)^(1/3) and the maximum sound velocity in the high-density region, v_s^max = √α. The abstract glosses over the condition that this result depends on connecting the low-density equation of state (EOS) to the high-density EOS at a fixed transition density of 2ρ₀, and that the linear fits are valid only for the specific range of parameters considered.

## Method

The author constructs neutron star models by integrating the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation using a piecewise EOS. For densities below 2ρ₀, the phenomenological Oyamatsu-Iida (OI-EOS) is used, which is parameterized by the incompressibility K₀ and the slope parameter L of the nuclear symmetry energy, combined into the auxiliary parameter η = (K₀ L²)^(1/3). For densities above 2ρ₀, a general EOS of the form p = α(ρ - ρ_t) + p_t is adopted, where α = (v_s^max)² and ρ_t = 2ρ₀. The maximum mass and corresponding radius are then computed for various combinations of η (ranging from 50.6 to 197.3 MeV) and α (ranging from 1/3 to 1), and the results are fitted to linear functions of η.

## Key numbers

- The plausible range for η determined from terrestrial nuclear experiments is 55.5 ≤ η ≤ 120 MeV, derived from fiducial values of 30 ≤ L ≤ 80 MeV and K₀ = 230 ± 40 MeV.
- The observed masses of PSR J1614-2230 (1.97 ± 0.04 M_⊙) and PSR J0348+0432 (2.01 ± 0.04 M_⊙) are used as constraints.
- To explain the lower limit of 1.97 M_⊙ for PSR J0348+0432 with α = 1/3, η must be larger than ~100 MeV, which leads to L ≳ 66 MeV with K₀ = 230 MeV.
- To explain the candidate massive neutron star J1748-2021B (M = 2.74 ± 0.21 M_⊙), the parameter α must be at least ~0.57, corresponding to v_s^max ≳ 0.75c.
- The linear fitting coefficients for the maximum mass M_max/M_⊙ = a₁ + a₂(η/1 MeV) are given as functions of α: a₁(α) = -0.356/α + 2.445 + 0.767α, and a₂(α) = (0.806/α + 1.098 - 0.393α) × 10⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the classic result of Hartle (1978), who derived a maximum mass of ~3.2 M_⊙ using the stiffest causal EOS connected at a higher transition density, and the more recent conjecture by Bedaque and Steiner (2015) that the sound velocity should be less than c/√3, which yields a maximum mass of ~2 M_⊙. The paper systematically shows how the maximum mass depends on both the low-density saturation parameters (via η) and the high-density sound speed, enabling future observations of massive neutron stars to constrain the maximum sound velocity in dense matter. The OI-EOS used for the low-density region was developed by Oyamatsu and Iida (2003, 2007).
