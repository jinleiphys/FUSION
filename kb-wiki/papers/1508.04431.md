# A self-consistent study of magnetic field effects on hybrid stars

> In this work we study the effects of strong magnetic fields on hybrid stars by using a full general-relativity approach, solving the coupled Maxwell-Einstein equation in a self-consistent way. The magnetic field is assumed to be axi-symmetric and poloidal. We take into consideration the anisotropy of the energy-momentum tensor due to the magnetic field, magnetic field effects on equation of state, the interaction between matter and the magnetic field (magnetization), and the anomalous magnetic moment of the hadrons. The equation of state used is an extended hadronic and quark SU(3) non-linear realization of the sigma model that describes magnetized hybrid stars containing nucleons, hyperons and quarks. According to our results, the effects of the magnetization and the magnetic field on the EoS do not play an important role on global properties of these stars. On the other hand, the magnetic field causes the central density in these objects to be reduced, inducing major changes in the populated degrees of freedom and, potentially, converting a hybrid star into a hadronic star.

# A self-consistent study of magnetic field effects on hybrid stars

## Key claim
The magnetic field and magnetization do not significantly alter the global properties (mass, radius) of hybrid stars, but the magnetic field reduces the central density enough to change the particle composition, potentially converting a hybrid star (containing nucleons, hyperons, and quarks) into a purely hadronic star. This conversion direction is opposite to the temporal evolution, as magnetic field decay over time would allow a hadronic star to become a hybrid one.

## Method
The authors solved the coupled Maxwell-Einstein equations in full general relativity using an axisymmetric, poloidal magnetic field configuration, implemented via the Lorene C++ numerical relativity library. They employed an extended SU(3) non-linear realization of the sigma model that includes hadrons (nucleons and hyperons) and quarks, with the Polyakov loop as the deconfinement order parameter, and incorporated the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) for all hadrons, Landau quantization for charged particles, and magnetization effects in the energy-momentum tensor.

## Key numbers
- Surface magnetic fields in magnetars: up to 10^14 - 10^15 G (from observations of Anomalous X-ray Pulsars and Soft-Gamma-Ray-Repeaters)
- Central magnetic fields in neutron stars can reach up to 10^18-20 G (from virial theorem arguments)
- For a magnetic dipole moment of μ = 3.5 × 10^32 Am^2, the maximum gravitational mass is reduced from 2.49 M⊙ (without magnetization) to 2.46 M⊙ (with magnetization)
- Nuclear saturation density: ~2.7 × 10^14 g/cm^3
- The magnetization strength in this work reaches about 10 times the value in Chatterjee (2014)

## Context
This work extends the self-consistent general-relativistic treatment of magnetized stars from Bonazzola (1993) and Bocquet (1995), and specifically follows the formalism of Chatterjee (2014) who studied magnetized quark stars using a CFL equation of state. Unlike Chatterjee (2014), who found negligible magnetization effects due to a stiff quark EoS, this work uses a more complex hybrid EoS with hadrons and quarks that produces magnetization about 10 times larger, yet still finds only small changes to global stellar properties. The key new result is that magnetic fields can qualitatively alter the internal composition by reducing central density, which enables future studies of how magnetic field decay over time could transform hadronic stars into hybrid stars.
