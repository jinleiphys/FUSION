# A practical method in calculating one loop quantum fluctuations to the energy of the non-topological soliton

> I have used a practical method to calculate the one-loop quantum correction to the energy of the non-topological soliton in Friedberg-Lee model. The quantum effects which come from the quarks of the Dirac sea scattering with the soliton bag are calculated by a summation of the discrete and continuum energy spectrum of the Dirac equation in the background field of soliton. The phase shift of the continuum spectrum is numerically calculated in an efficient way and all the divergences are removed by the same renormalization procedure.

## Key claim

The one-loop quantum correction to the energy of the non-topological soliton in the Friedberg-Lee model, calculated using a practical phase-shift method, is so large (approximately 8.26 fm\(^{-1}\)) that it exceeds the classical soliton energy (approximately 6.4 fm\(^{-1}\)), suggesting that the quantum fluctuations do not support the existence of the soliton in this model under the chosen parameter set.

## Method

The author computes the one-loop vacuum correction from quark fields in a static, spherically symmetric soliton background field by summing the discrete and continuum energy spectrum of the Dirac equation. The continuum contribution is obtained by numerically solving a Riccati-type equation for the scattering phase shift \(\delta_l(k)\) for each angular momentum channel \(l\), subtracting the first and second Born approximations to render the momentum integral finite. The subtracted Born terms are added back via their diagrammatic representation and renormalized using on-shell mass and coupling constant renormalization, yielding a finite correction term \(\Gamma_2\) computed from the Fourier transform of the soliton field.

## Key numbers

- Classical soliton energy: \(E_{cl} \approx 6.4\ \text{fm}^{-1} \approx 1262\ \text{MeV}\) for parameters \(a=17.7\ \text{fm}^{-2}\), \(b=-1457.4\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), \(c=20000\), \(g=12.16\).
- Energy contributions from continuum spectrum for each angular momentum: \(E^{(0)} = -4.26\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), \(E^{(1)} = -0.53\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), \(E^{(2)} = -0.3\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), \(E^{(3)} = -0.19\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).
- Renormalized finite energy part: \(\Gamma_2 = -0.42\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).
- Total renormalized quantum correction energy: \(E^{ren}_{vac} \sim 8.26\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).
- Bound state energy: \(E_1 = 1.6\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) (occupied by valence quarks, not contributing to correction).
- Sigma meson mass used in renormalization: \(m_\sigma = 550\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the phase-shift method for calculating one-loop quantum corrections, originally developed by Farhi, Graham, Haagensen, and Jaffe (1992) for Higgs-like models in the electroweak sector, to the Friedberg-Lee model of strong interactions. It contrasts with earlier approaches based on derivative expansion methods (e.g., by various authors in the 1980s and 1990s) which faced nontrivial renormalization challenges. The result enables future studies of quantum fluctuations in other QCD effective models, such as chiral soliton models, and at finite temperature to investigate soliton behavior during the deconfinement phase transition.
