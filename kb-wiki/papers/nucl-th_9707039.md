# Hybrid stars : Spin polarised nuclear matter and density dependent quark masses

> The possibility of formation of a droplet phase (DP) inside a star and its consequences on the structural properties of the star are investigated. For nuclear matter (NM), an equation of state (EOS) based on finite range, momentum and density dependent interaction, and which predicts that neutron matter undergoes ferromagnetic transition at densities realisable inside the neutron star is employed. An EOS for quark matter (QM) with density dependent quark masses, the so-called effective mass model, is constructed by correctly treating the quark chemical potentials. It is then found that a droplet phase consisting of strange quark matter and unpolarised nuclear matter sandwiched between a core of polarised nuclear matter and a crust containing unpolarised nuclear matter exists. Moreover, we could explain the mass and surface magnetic field satisfactorily, and as well allow, due to the presence of a droplet phase, the direct URCA process to happen.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether a droplet phase (mixed phase of quark matter and nuclear matter) can exist inside hybrid stars when nuclear matter is allowed to undergo a ferromagnetic transition at high densities, and finds that such a phase is possible only when the nuclear matter core is spin-polarised, with the droplet phase consisting of strange quark matter and unpolarised nuclear matter sandwiched between a polarised core and an unpolarised crust.

## Method

The authors constructed an equation of state for nuclear matter using a finite-range, momentum- and density-dependent (FRMDD) interaction that explicitly includes spin-isospin degrees of freedom, which predicts a ferromagnetic transition in neutron matter at around four times nuclear saturation density. For quark matter, they employed an effective mass model with density-dependent quark masses, correctly including the rearrangement energy term in the quark chemical potentials that was omitted in prior studies. They then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to determine hybrid star structure, applying Gibbs criteria for phase equilibrium between nuclear and quark matter with two conserved charges (baryon number and electric charge).

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density: $\rho_o \simeq 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$
- Ferromagnetic transition in pure neutron matter occurs at $\rho \sim 4\rho_0$
- Volume energy of symmetric nuclear matter: $E_V = -16.1\ \mathrm{MeV}$
- Symmetry (isospin) energy: $E_X = 33.4\ \mathrm{MeV}$
- Spin symmetry energy: $E_Y = 15\ \mathrm{MeV}$
- Spin-isospin symmetry energy: $E_Z = 36.5\ \mathrm{MeV}$
- Nuclear matter incompressibility from FRMDD interaction: $K = 240\ \mathrm{MeV}$
- Normal nuclear matter saturation density for FRMDD interaction: $\rho_0 = 0.1533\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$
- Surface energy coefficient: $a_S = 18.0\ \mathrm{MeV}$
- Effective mass model parameters used for flavour symmetric SQM: $n = 2/3$, $C = 85\ \mathrm{MeV\ fm}^{-2}$

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior study (Uma Maheswari et al., 1997) which first demonstrated that pure neutron matter undergoes ferromagnetic transition at $\rho \sim 4\rho_0$ using the FRMDD interaction, and applies this to the question of droplet phase formation in hybrid stars. The paper corrects earlier treatments of the effective mass model for quark matter by Chakrabarty et al. and Benvenuto et al., which had incorrectly omitted the rearrangement energy term in quark chemical potentials, leading to inconsistencies with the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem. The study enables future investigations of neutron star cooling via the direct URCA process, which becomes possible in the droplet phase due to increased proton fractions.
