# Fluctuations in jet momentum as an energy loss probe

> We investigate the measurement of the distribution of away-side jet $p_T$ as a way to probe the energy loss mechanism in heavy ion collisions, and constrain the properties of the medium created in heavy ion collisions. We define an observable related to the fluctuation of the energy deposited in the medium, and show that competing models of parton energy loss give differing values and scaling of this observable. We also argue that the scaling of this observable with system size can be related to the medium's partonic density. We then give a qualitative discussion of how the measurement we suggest can be performed and used to determine the parton energy loss mechanism and possibly the system's partonic density.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measuring the mean and event-by-event fluctuation of the ratio of away-side to near-side jet transverse momentum (z = P_T^away / P_T^near) can distinguish between the GLV ("thin plasma") and BDMPS/AMY ("continuous absorption") models of parton energy loss, because the two approaches predict qualitatively different scaling of these observables with system size.

## Method

The authors define an observable z = P_T^away / P_T^near, where P_T^away and P_T^near are the total hard transverse momenta within a jet cone of angle δθ on the away and near sides of a triggered dijet event. They calculate the probability density function of parton momentum after energy loss using the GLV formalism to first order in opacity and the AMY formalism via a rate equation from finite-temperature QCD. They then simulate the observable using the HIJING event generator for p-p, d-Au, and Au-Au collisions to determine suitable trigger momenta and jet cone angles.

## Key numbers

- For the AMY formalism, the system size at which the variance of z saturates to approximately 0.5 is ~2 fm for T=540 MeV, ~4 fm for T=360 MeV, and ~6 fm for T=180 MeV.
- HIJING simulations show that for jet cone angles less than 0.6 radians and trigger momenta p_Tmin below 4.5 GeV, the mean and variance of z converge independently of the precise jet cone angle.
- A ~30% shift is observed between the mean z in Au-Au and d-Au collisions in HIJING, attributed to the Cronin effect.
- Turning off mini-jets in HIJING results in a ~50% difference between the variance of z in Au-Au and d-Au collisions.

## Context

This work extends the debate between the GLV approach (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev, 2000-2002) and the BDMPS/AMY approach (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, Schiff, 1997; Arnold, Moore, Yaffe, 2002) for describing parton energy loss in heavy ion collisions. The authors argue that previous measurements of average jet suppression at RHIC could not distinguish between these models, and their proposed observable of momentum fluctuations could resolve this ambiguity. The measurement would enable quantitative determination of the medium's initial partonic density or temperature, removing the density/size ambiguity inherent in interpreting average jet suppression measurements.
