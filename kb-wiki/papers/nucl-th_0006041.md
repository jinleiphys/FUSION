# Antiproton-deuteron annihilation at low energies

> Recent experimental studies of the antiproton-deuteron system at low energies have shown that the imaginary part of the antiproton-deuteron scattering length is smaller than the antiproton-proton one. Two- and three-body systems with strong annihilation are investigated and a mechanism explaining this unexpected relation between the imaginary parts of the scattering lengths is proposed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a mechanism to explain the unexpected experimental observation that the imaginary part of the antiproton-deuteron scattering length is smaller than that of the antiproton-proton system, contradicting the naive geometrical picture where annihilation should increase with nuclear size. The explanation relies on two points: the imaginary part of the scattering length is primarily determined by the diffuseness of the exponential tail of the annihilation potential (not the nuclear size), and near-threshold resonances in the two-body antinucleon-nucleon system enhance the imaginary part more strongly in the antiproton-proton case than in the antiproton-deuteron case.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations in coordinate space for the three-body antiproton-deuteron system, using a Woods-Saxon optical potential for the antinucleon-nucleon interaction and the Malfliet-Tjon potential for the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The antinucleon-nucleon potential was chosen with a real part range larger than the imaginary part range, reproducing features of the Kohno-Weise potential, and the scattering length was extracted from the asymptotic form of the Faddeev component. The calculation was performed for pure annihilation (no real potential) and for cases where the real part produced near-threshold S-wave poles.

## Key numbers

- Imaginary part of the antiproton-proton scattering length (from experiment): Im a_sc(pp) = -[0.69 ± 0.01 (stat) ± 0.03 (sys)] fm
- Imaginary part of the antiproton-deuteron scattering length (from experiment): Im a_sc(pD) = -[0.62 ± 0.02 (stat) ± 0.05 (sys)] fm
- Imaginary part of the antiproton-helium scattering length (from experiment): Im a_sc(p^4He) = -[0.36 ± 0.03 (stat) +0.19/-0.11 (sys)] fm
- Imaginary part of the strong scattering length for antiproton-proton (Coulomb corrected): Im a_s(pp) = -(0.86 ± 0.04) fm
- Imaginary part of the strong scattering length for antiproton-deuteron (Coulomb corrected): Im a_s(pD) = -(0.80 ± 0.06) fm
- For the resonance case with V=80 MeV and no annihilation, the S-wave pole position in k-plane was k_b = i31.7 MeV/c; with W=50 MeV, k_b = -17.3 + i32.2 MeV/c; with W=500 MeV, k_b = -130.9 + i72.9 MeV/c
- For the excited state case with V=500 MeV and no annihilation, the loosely bound state had k_b = i36.5 MeV/c; with W=50 MeV, k_b = -11 + i37.8 MeV/c; with W=500 MeV, k_b = -46 + i80.3 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends prior three-body calculations of the antiproton-deuteron system by Wycech (1985), Lomon and Tjon, and Richard, which had already found that the antiproton-deuteron atomic width was smaller than the antiproton-proton width but used separable interactions or gave results differing from experiment. The paper provides a mechanism based on the diffuseness of the annihilation potential and the presence of near-threshold S-wave poles in the antinucleon-nucleon interaction, which are also present in the Kohno-Weise potential (Kohno and Weise, 1986) and in separable potentials used by Lomon and Tjon. The results enable predictions that low-energy antineutron-nucleus annihilation cross-sections should be approximately equal for all nuclei, and motivate further experiments at the antiproton decelerator facility.
