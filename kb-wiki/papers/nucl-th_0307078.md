# Continuum QRPA response for deformed neutron-rich nuclei

> We discuss properties of the quadrupole collective excitation of the deformed neutron-rich nucleus $^{38}$Mg within the framework of quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA). We first solve the coupled-channels equations to obtain the single-particle levels, and construct the ground state by treating the pairing correlations in the BCS approximation. We then solve the QRPA equation using the response function formalism, by including the continuum spectra with the box dicscretization method. We show that the collectivity of the gamma vibration (the lowest $K^π=2^+$ mode) is significantly enhanced if protons and neutrons have different deformations. We also discuss an attempt towards full continuum QRPA calculations for deformed nuclei.

## Key claim

The collectivity (strength and low excitation energy) of the gamma vibration (the lowest \(K^\pi=2^+\) mode) in the deformed neutron-rich nucleus \(^{38}\)Mg is significantly enhanced when protons and neutrons have different deformation parameters, an effect that is additional to the simple change in the average deformation.

## Method

The authors first solved coupled-channels equations for a deformed Woods-Saxon potential to obtain single-particle levels, then treated pairing correlations in the BCS approximation. They solved the QRPA equation using a response function formalism in coordinate space with a zero-range Skyrme residual interaction (SIII parameter set), discretizing the continuum states by placing the nucleus in a spherical box of 15 fm. The particle-hole and particle-particle channels were decoupled by the BCS approximation, and the residual interaction was renormalized so that the \(K^\pi=1^+\) Goldstone mode appeared at zero excitation energy.

## Key numbers

*   For \(^{38}\)Mg, the proton deformation parameter \(\beta_p = 0.33\) and the neutron deformation parameter \(\beta_n = 0.28\), a difference of 0.05, taken from HFB calculations by Terasaki et al.
*   The proton Fermi energy \(\lambda_p = -24.4\) MeV and the neutron Fermi energy \(\lambda_n = -0.56\) MeV.
*   The average pairing gap for protons \(\bar{\Delta}_p = 0.0\) MeV and for neutrons \(\bar{\Delta}_n = 1.50\) MeV.
*   The renormalization factors for the residual interaction were 1.00075 for \(K^\pi=0^-\), 0.95585 for \(K^\pi=1^-\), and 1.0300 for \(K^\pi=1^+\).
*   The isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (GQR) was found in the energy region 10–20 MeV for both \(^{24}\)Mg and \(^{38}\)Mg.

## Context

This work extends the QRPA framework to deformed, neutron-rich nuclei by using a computationally efficient response function method with the BCS approximation, avoiding the more demanding HFB-based continuum QRPA which had been limited to spherical systems (Matsuo, 2001; Khan, 2002). The finding that different proton and neutron deformations enhance the low-lying isoscalar quadrupole strength confirms a suggestion by Terasaki et al. (1997) that a low-lying isovector quadrupole mode may appear under such conditions. The formalism enables future full continuum QRPA calculations for deformed superfluid nuclei by combining the coupled-channels method for single-particle resonances with the Green function approach.
