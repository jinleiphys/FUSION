# Probe Chiral Magnetic Effect with Signed Balance Function

> In this paper a pair of observables are proposed as alternative ways, by examining the fluctuation of net momentum-ordering of charged pairs, to study the charge separation induced by the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) in relativistic heavy ion collisions. They are, the out-of-plane to in-plane ratio of fluctuation of the difference between signed balance functions measured in pair's rest frame, and the ratio of it to similar measurement made in the laboratory frame. Both observables have been studied with simulations including flow-related backgrounds, and for the first time, backgrounds that are related to resonance's global spin alignment. The two observables have similar positive responses to signal, and opposite, limited responses to identifiable backgrounds arising from resonance flow and spin alignment. Both observables have also been tested with two realistic models, namely, a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model and the anomalous-viscous fluid dynamics (AVFD) model. These two observables, when cross examined, will provide useful insights in the study of CME-induced charge separation.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the ratio of the out-of-plane to in-plane fluctuation of the difference between signed balance functions measured in the pair's rest frame ($r_{\mathrm{rest}}$), and its ratio to the same measurement in the laboratory frame ($R_{B}$), can together distinguish CME-induced charge separation from backgrounds arising from resonance flow and global spin alignment, because the two observables respond positively to signal but in opposite directions to these identifiable backgrounds.

## Method

The signed balance function approach counts the net momentum-ordering of charged particle pairs along the out-of-plane ($y$) and in-plane ($x$) directions, computing the event-by-event difference $\Delta B$ between signed balance functions for positive and negative particles. The width of the $\Delta B$ distribution is characterized by $r = \sigma_{\Delta B_y} / \sigma_{\Delta B_x}$, calculated in both the laboratory frame ($r_{\mathrm{lab}}$) and the pair's rest frame ($r_{\mathrm{rest}}$), with the ratio $R_{B} \equiv r_{\mathrm{rest}} / r_{\mathrm{lab}}$ serving as the second observable. The method was tested with toy model simulations incorporating primordial pions and $\rho$ resonances with various flow ($v_2$, $v_3$) and global spin alignment ($\rho_{00}$) parameters, as well as with the AMPT and AVFD realistic models.

## Key numbers

- For a simple case with CME signal only and no backgrounds, $r_{\mathrm{rest}}$ and $r_{\mathrm{lab}}$ increase with $a_1$; at $a_1 = 2\%$, $r_{\mathrm{rest}}$ and $r_{\mathrm{lab}}$ deviate from unity, while $R_{B}$ shows a deviation about an order of magnitude smaller.
- In simulations with resonance $v_2$ as the only background ($a_1 = 0$), $r_{\mathrm{rest}}$ increases with increasing resonance $v_2$, while $R_{B}$ decreases with increasing resonance $v_2$, showing opposite trends.
- For resonance global spin alignment ($\rho_{00}$) with no flow and $a_1 = 0$, $r_{\mathrm{rest}}$ and $R_{B}$ deviate from unity in opposite directions when $\rho_{00} \neq 1/3$; this pattern holds for all transverse spectra shapes considered.
- In simulations with $p_T$-dependent $v_2$ and $v_3$ for both primordial pions and $\rho$ resonances, with default parameters for 30-40% central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, $r_{\mathrm{rest}}$ and $R_{B}$ are on opposite sides of unity when $a_1 = 0$, largely due to finite $\rho$ resonance $v_2$.
- For the case with realistic flow and $\rho_{00}$ variation, $r_{\mathrm{rest}}$ and $R_{B}$ again change in opposite directions when responding to $\rho_{00}$ change, with the crossing point where both ratios equal unity depending on $a_1$.

## Context

This work extends the signed balance function approach previously proposed by Ye (2018) for studying magnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions, applying it specifically to the CME search. It addresses the long-standing challenge in CME searches, as reviewed by Kharzeev (2015), that backgrounds from elliptic flow of resonances (studied by Bzdak (2012), Pratt (2010), Schlichting (2010), Wang (2009, 2016), and Feng (2018)) can mimic CME signals. The paper introduces for the first time the consideration of resonance global spin alignment as a background, and demonstrates that the cross-examination of $r_{\mathrm{rest}}$ and $R_{B}$ can help disentangle signal from these backgrounds, enabling more robust CME searches in ongoing analyses such as the STAR isobaric collision data.
