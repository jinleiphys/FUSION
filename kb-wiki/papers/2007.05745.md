# Momentum dependence of light nuclei production in pp, p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> We study the momentum dependence of the production of light nuclei in high energy collisions in the nucleon coalescence/recombination mechanism. We derive formulas of the momentum distributions of deuterons ($d$) and helions ($^3$He). We obtain the analytic expressions of the coalescence factor $B_A$'s ($B_2$ for $d$ and $B_3$ for $^3$He) as functions of the collision system size and the momentum. We apply the deduced results to p-p, p-Pb and Pb-Pb collisions to naturally explain the interesting behaviors of $B_A$ observed in experiments at the CERN Large Hadron Collider.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytic expressions for the coalescence factors \(B_2\) and \(B_3\) as functions of collision system size and momentum, showing that applying the coalescence criterion in the rest frame of the forming light nuclei (rather than the laboratory frame) naturally explains the observed growth of \(B_A\) with transverse momentum in Pb-Pb collisions and the nearly constant behavior in p-p and p-Pb collisions at the LHC.

## Method

The authors start from the nucleon coalescence/recombination mechanism and derive general formulas for deuteron and helion momentum distributions. They assume factorization of coordinate and momentum dependencies, model the nucleon coordinate distribution as a Gaussian with effective source radius \(R_f\), and approximate the momentum-space kernel functions using delta functions justified by the small Gaussian width parameters. The resulting analytic expressions for \(B_2\) and \(B_3\) are then applied to p-p, p-Pb, and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies.

## Key numbers

* Deuteron root-mean-square radius \(R_d = 2.1421\) fm; helion root-mean-square radius \(R_{^3\text{He}} = 1.9661\) fm.
* Deuteron mass \(m_d = 1.875\) GeV; helion mass \(m_{^3\text{He}} = 2.815\) GeV.
* Spin degeneracy factors: \(g_d = 3/4\) for deuterons, \(g_{^3\text{He}} = 1/4\) for helions.
* Width parameter \(C = 4\) used in the nucleon coordinate distribution, following a prior work (fr2017acta).

## Context

This work extends prior coalescence models (e.g., 1BA1994PRL, 2BA1998PLB, 3BA1999PRC, 6BA2019PRC) by providing analytic expressions for the momentum dependence of \(B_A\) that can be directly compared to recent ALICE data. The key advance is the use of instantaneous coalescence in the nucleon-pair rest frame, which introduces a Lorentz contraction effect that explains the different \(p_T\) dependencies observed across collision systems. The results enable systematic studies of freeze-out properties and system-size effects in light nuclei production at the LHC.
