# Weak interaction rates for Kr and Sr waiting-point nuclei under rp-process conditions

> Weak interaction rates are studied in neutron deficient Kr and Sr waiting-point isotopes in ranges of densities and temperatures relevant for the rp-process. The nuclear structure is described within a microscopic model (deformed QRPA) that reproduces not only the half-lives but also the Gamow-Teller strength distributions recently measured. The various sensitivities of the decay rates to both density and temperature are discussed. Continuum electron capture is shown to contribute significantly to the weak rates at rp-process conditions.

## Key claim

The weak decay rates of the waiting-point nuclei \(^{72,74}\)Kr and \(^{76,78}\)Sr under rp-process conditions are calculated using a deformed QRPA model that reproduces both the measured Gamow-Teller strength distributions and terrestrial half-lives; the abstract's claim that continuum electron capture contributes significantly is qualified by the finding that this contribution becomes dominant only at densities above \(\log_{10}(\rho) \sim 6.5\) or at sufficiently high temperatures, while at lower densities and temperatures the \(\beta^+\) rate remains larger.

## Method

The nuclear structure was described using a deformed quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) built on a self-consistent Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculation with the SLy4 force and BCS pairing. Weak decay rates were computed as a function of temperature and density by summing over thermally populated parent states (up to 1 MeV excitation) and integrating phase-space factors that include both positron emission and continuum electron capture, with electrons treated as a degenerate Fermi-Dirac plasma. The Gamow-Teller strength distributions and half-lives from the model were first validated against experimental data for the four isotopes before applying the formalism to stellar conditions.

## Key numbers

- The most relevant rp-process conditions are \(T = 1.5\) GK and \(\rho = 10^6\) g cm\(^{-3}\).
- At \(T = 1.5\) GK, the population of the lowest excited state (\(E_{2^+} = 261\) keV in \(^{76}\)Sr) is about 12%.
- For densities larger than \(\log_{10}(\rho) \sim 6.5\), continuum electron capture becomes dominant at any temperature for \(^{72}\)Kr.
- The half-lives of Kr and Sr isotopes decrease with increasing temperature at fixed \(\rho = 10^6\) g cm\(^{-3}\), with the decrease becoming significant only beyond \(T = 3\) GK, outside the rp-process range.
- The effective axial-vector coupling ratio used is \((g_A/g_V)_{\rm eff} = 0.74 (g_A/g_V)_{\rm bare}\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier QRPA calculations of Sarriguren (2008) that reproduced experimental GT strength distributions and half-lives for these nuclei, and applies them for the first time to stellar rp-process conditions. It contradicts the earlier neglect of continuum electron capture in rp-process rate evaluations by Schatz (1998), showing that cEC becomes comparable to \(\beta^+\) decay at peak conditions and dominant at slightly higher densities and temperatures. The results enable more accurate network calculations for X-ray burst nucleosynthesis, particularly for the time scales set by these waiting-point nuclei.
