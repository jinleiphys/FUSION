# Reply to the "Comment on `Effect of density and nucleon-nucleon potential on the fusion cross section within the relativistic mean field formalism'"

> In reply to the Comment made by M. V. Chushnyakova et al. on our paper [Phys. Rev. C 101, 044603 (2020)], we argue that the calculations, results and conclusions of our paper remain valid. We have shown here the calculations for one reaction using the deformed densities and the R3Y nucleon-nucleon potential obtained within the relativistic mean-field (RMF) formalism. Suitable clarications and justifications are given to address all the points raised in the Comment.

## Key claim

The authors defend their original paper (Bhuyan et al., Phys. Rev. C 101, 044603, 2020) against a Comment by Chushnyakova et al., arguing that the original calculations, results, and conclusions remain valid despite the Comment's criticism about the use of spherical densities for target nuclei. They acknowledge that spherical densities were used as a simplifying first step in implementing the relativistic mean-field (RMF) approach for fusion studies.

## Method

The authors present new calculations for the 48Ca+238U reaction using deformed RMF densities and the relativistic R3Y nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential, with the quadrupole deformation parameter β2=0.283 for 238U obtained from the axially deformed RMF formalism with the NL3* parameter set. The capture cross-section is computed using the ℓ-summed Wong model, and results are compared with experimental data and with calculations using the Reid version of the M3Y NN potential folded with both spherical and deformed RMF densities.

## Key numbers

- For 48Ca+238U at center-of-mass energy 181 MeV: experimental cross-section 0.0248 mb; spherical R3Y-NL3* gives 9.133×10⁻⁵ mb; deformed R3Y-NL3* gives 0.0294 mb; deformed M3Y-Reid gives 0.0077 mb
- At 186 MeV: experimental 3.04 mb; spherical R3Y-NL3* 3.652 mb; deformed R3Y-NL3* 3.698 mb; deformed M3Y-Reid 1.864 mb
- At 193 MeV: experimental 40.2 mb; spherical R3Y-NL3* 41.202 mb; deformed R3Y-NL3* 42.844 mb; deformed M3Y-Reid 24.315 mb
- At 201 MeV: experimental 107 mb; spherical R3Y-NL3* 105.93 mb; deformed R3Y-NL3* 105.93 mb; deformed M3Y-Reid 103.686 mb
- The ratio σ_def/σ_exp for R3Y-NL3* ranges from 0.990 (at 201 MeV) to 1.217 (at 186 MeV), showing improved agreement with experiment when deformation is included

## Context

This reply addresses the Comment by Chushnyakova et al. (2022) on the original paper by Bhuyan et al. (2020), which introduced the RMF approach for fusion studies. The work extends previous studies by incorporating quadrupole deformations of the target nucleus within the same RMF formalism, and the authors note that a more detailed study including additional nuclear shape degrees of freedom is in progress. The results show that the R3Y NN potential gives better agreement with experimental data than the Reid M3Y potential, and that including deformation improves the match, especially at lower barrier energies.
