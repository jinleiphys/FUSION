# The character and prevalence of third minima in actinide fission barriers

> The double-humped structure of many actinide fission barriers is well established both experimentally and theoretically. There is also evidence, both experimental and theoretical, that some actinide nuclei have barriers with a third minimum, outside the second, fission-isomeric minimum. We perform a large-scale, systematic calculation of actinide fission barriers to identify which actinide nuclei exhibit third minima. We find that only a relatively few nuclei accessible to experiment exhibit third minima in their barriers, approximately nuclei with proton number $Z$ in the range $88 \leq Z \leq 94$ and nucleon number $A$ in the range $230 \leq A \leq 236 $. We find that the third minimum is less than 1 MeV deep for light Th and U isotopes. This is consistent with some previous experimental and theoretical results, but differs from some others. We discuss possible origins of these incompatible results and what are the most realistic predictions of where third minima are observable.

## Key claim

## Method

The authors used a macroscopic-microscopic model with a folded-Yukawa single-particle potential to calculate fission potential-energy surfaces for 5254 heavy nuclei, with each nucleus evaluated for more than 5 million shapes as a function of five deformation parameters: elongation (quadrupole moment), neck radius, left and right nascent fragment spheroidal deformations, and nascent fragment mass asymmetry. They identified third minima by immersion techniques, determining all minima deeper than 0.2 MeV and the saddles between all pairwise combinations of minima.

## Key numbers

- Third minima occur only for nuclei with proton number Z in the range 88 to 94 and nucleon number A in the range 230 to 236
- The third minimum is less than 1 MeV deep for light Th and U isotopes
- For 236Ra, the shell-plus-pairing energy is -3.39 MeV at the fission-isomeric minimum, -1.724 MeV at the second saddle, -5.63 MeV at the third minimum, and -7.39 MeV at the third saddle
- For 232U, the calculated shell-plus-pairing correction is -4.85 MeV at the third barrier peak and -2.59 MeV at the second peak
- The isotope sequences 228Th to 236Th and 228U to 236U are identified as the best candidates for observable third minima

## Context

This work extends earlier macroscopic-microscopic calculations by Moller (1972, 1974) and Bengtsson (1987) that found third minima in limited deformation spaces, and contradicts results from Cwiok (1994) and Ter-Akopian (1996) who reported 4-MeV deep third minima for light Th and U isotopes. The authors argue that their results are consistent with recent Woods-Saxon macroscopic-microscopic calculations by Kowal (2012) that used immersion methods, and suggest that experimental analyses by Csige (2009, 2012) for 232U may have been influenced by incorrect theoretical expectations. The work enables more reliable predictions of which actinide nuclei are likely to show experimental signatures of third minima in fission barriers.
