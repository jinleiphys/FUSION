# Perturbative Pions in Deuteron Compton Scattering and Three Body Physics

> 2 pages stone-age LaTeX2 with 2 figures, using sprocl.sty and epsfig (4 .eps figures). Abstract of a talk held at the Workshop Chiral Dynamics 2000: Theory and Experiment, TJNL (Newport News,USA) 17th -- 22nd July 2000; to be published in the Proceedings

## Key claim
Elastic deuteron Compton scattering can be described to next-to-leading order (NLO) in an effective field theory (EFT) with perturbative pions, yielding a parameter-free prediction with an accuracy of about 10%. The same perturbative-pion approach applied to three-body nd scattering shows convergence and agrees with experiment, but pionic corrections are numerically weaker than their formal NLO order would suggest.

## Method
The author uses the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) power-counting scheme, in which nucleons interact at leading order via a two-nucleon contact interaction without derivatives, and pion exchange is treated as a perturbation at NLO. For deuteron Compton scattering, the cross section is computed to NLO including the pion graphs that dominate the nucleon electric polarisability. For the three-body sector, a comparative study of nd scattering in the spin quartet S-wave is performed between the theory with explicit perturbative pions and the theory with pions integrated out, carried out to NLO and NNLO respectively.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron Compton scattering cross section calculated to NLO with an accuracy of 10% (no free parameters).
- Three-body nd scattering calculation performed for centre-of-mass momenta up to 300 MeV (E_cm approximately 70 MeV).
- NNLO calculation in the three-body sector carries an uncertainty of approximately 4%.
- Incident photon energies for Compton scattering shown: 49 MeV and 69 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) EFT approach (Kaplan, Savage, Wise, 1998) to deuteron Compton scattering and three-body nd scattering, demonstrating that perturbative pions can successfully describe these systems. The deuteron Compton results confirm the heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBchiPT) value for the nucleon electric polarisability alpha_E. For the three-body sector, the calculation agrees with experimental data and potential model calculations, and extends to a higher, previously untested momentum regime above the deuteron breakup point. The work enables future extraction of deuteron scalar and tensor electric and magnetic polarisabilities, and provides a systematic framework for comparing theories with and without explicit pions.
