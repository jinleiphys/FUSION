# Strange hadron yields and ratios in heavy ion collisions at RHIC energy

> Recent experimental data support the presence of quark coalescence in heavy ion collisions at RHIC energies. Hadronization of quark matter and hadron formation in heavy ion collisions can be described by the coalescence process, and measured data are reproduced successfully. On the other hand, the theoretical coalescence calculations are based on a non-relativistic description. Here we investigate the robustness of the coalescence description, using different wave-function overlap during hadron formation.

# Strange hadron yields and ratios in heavy ion collisions at RHIC energy

## Key claim

The coalescence model for hadronization in heavy ion collisions is robust against variations in the choice of quark and hadron wave functions (plain wave, gaussian, or hydrogen-like), provided the wave function combination is fixed; the resulting particle ratios vary by only 10–15% across different wave function setups, even though the extracted strange-to-light quark ratio varies by 100–150%.

## Method

The authors calculate quantum-mechanical coalescence cross sections for meson and baryon formation using a Yukawa potential with screening mass m_g = 0.8 GeV, considering three wave function choices for quarks (plain wave, gaussian) and three for prehadrons (plain wave, hydrogen-like, gaussian), yielding 18 combinations. They then compute thermally averaged coalescence rates using both non-relativistic Boltzmann and relativistic Jüttner momentum distributions for quarks with masses m_q = 0.3 GeV and m_s = 0.5 GeV, and use the measured Φ/K* ratio from RHIC to fix the strange-to-light quark ratio N_s/N_q, from which other hadron ratios are predicted.

## Key numbers

- Quark masses used: m_q = 0.3 GeV, m_s = 0.5 GeV, "verified in the analysis of the lattice data close to the quark-hadron phase transition"
- Screening mass of Yukawa potential: m_g = 0.8 GeV at T ≈ T_c
- Measured Φ/K* ratio used as input: 0.60 ± 0.15 in central Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 AGeV (RHIC)
- Extracted N_s/N_q values range from 0.201 (G+G → H with Jüttner distribution) to 0.503 (G+G → G with Boltzmann distribution), a 100–150% difference
- Predicted K*/ρ^0 ratios across all wave function setups: range 0.593–0.712
- Predicted Σ*/Δ ratios: range 0.790–0.931
- Predicted Ξ*/Σ ratios: range 0.681–0.819
- Predicted Ω/Ξ* ratios: range 0.614–0.757

## Context

This work extends the ALCOR model (which used plain wave + plain wave → hydrogen-like wave functions) and the MICOR model (which used plain wave + gaussian → hydrogen-like) by systematically testing all combinations of wave function choices. The authors show that the insensitivity of particle ratios to temperature and gluon mass supports the applicability of coalescence models to cross-over phase transitions, where no sharp transition temperature exists. The results enable future analyses connecting conservation laws to the quantum-mechanical description of quark coalescence, with further work in progress.
