# Superfluid phases of quark matter. III. Supercurrents and vortices

> We study, within Ginzburg-Landau theory, the responses of three-flavor superfluid quark-gluon plasmas to external magnetic fields and rotation, in both the color-flavor locked and isoscalar color-antitriplet diquark phases near the critical temperature. Fields are incorporated in the gradient energy arising from long wavelength distortions of the condensate, via covariant derivatives to satisfy local gauge symmetries associated with color and electric charge. Magnetic vortex formation, in response to external magnetic fields, is possible only in the isoscalar phase; in the color-flavor locked phase, external magnetic fields are incompletely screened by the Meissner effect. On the other hand, rotation of the superfluid produces vortices in the color-flavor locked phase; in the isoscalar phase, it produces a London gluon-photon mixed field. We estimate the coherence and Meissner lengths and critical magnetic fields for the two phases.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, near the critical temperature, the color-flavor locked phase of three-flavor quark matter cannot form magnetic vortices in response to external magnetic fields (only partial Meissner screening occurs), while the isoscalar color-antitriplet phase can form magnetic vortices; conversely, rotation produces vortices in the color-flavor locked phase but only a London gluon-photon mixed field in the isoscalar phase.

## Method

The authors extended the Ginzburg-Landau theory developed in earlier work (Iida and Baym, Paper I) by adding gradient energy terms for long-wavelength distortions of the condensate, incorporating external magnetic fields and rotation via covariant derivatives to satisfy local color and electromagnetic gauge symmetries. They derived the supercurrent densities from extremization of the free energy with respect to gluonic and photonic gauge fields, and calculated the coherence lengths, penetration depths, and critical magnetic fields for both the color-flavor locked and isoscalar phases in the weak-coupling limit near the critical temperature.

## Key numbers

- Coherence length for color-flavor locked phase: \(\xi_{\rm CFL} \simeq 0.26 \left(\frac{100 {\rm ~MeV}}{T_c}\right) \left(1-\frac{T}{T_c}\right)^{-1/2} {\rm ~fm}\)
- Penetration depth for color-flavor locked phase: \(\lambda_{\rm CFL} \simeq 2.1 \left(\frac{1}{g_3}\right) \left(\frac{300 {\rm ~MeV}}{\mu/3}\right) \left(1-\frac{T}{T_c}\right)^{-1/2} {\rm ~fm}\)
- Critical magnetic field for color-flavor locked phase: \(H_c \simeq 1.8 \times10^{19} g_3 \left(\frac{T_c}{100 {\rm ~MeV}}\right) \left(\frac{\mu/3}{300 {\rm ~MeV}}\right) \left(1-\frac{T}{T_c}\right) {\rm ~G}\)
- Stiffness parameter in weak coupling: \(K_T = \frac{7\zeta(3)n_b}{16\pi^2 T_c^2 \mu}\), where \(\zeta(3)=1.202\ldots\)

## Context

This work extends the Ginzburg-Landau analysis of superfluid quark matter from earlier studies by Bailin and Love and by Blaschke and Sedrakian, which treated only the isoscalar channel, to encompass both color-flavor locked and isoscalar condensates under external magnetic fields and rotation. The results enable predictions for the magnetic field evolution and rotational dynamics of neutron stars containing quark matter, including the possibility that color-flavor locked phase vortices could play a role in pulsar glitches (as discussed by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek), while isoscalar phase magnetic vortices could affect the star's magnetic field structure.
