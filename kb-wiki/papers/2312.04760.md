# Far-off-equilibrium early-stage dynamics in high-energy nuclear collisions

> We explore the far-off-equilibrium aspects of the (1+1)-dimensional early-stage evolution of a weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma using kinetic theory and hydrodynamics. For a large set of far-off-equilibrium initial conditions the system exhibits a peculiar phenomenon where its total equilibrium entropy decreases with time. Using a non-equilibrium definition of entropy based on Boltzmann's H-function, we demonstrate how this apparently anomalous behavior is consistent with the second law of thermodynamics. We also use the H-function to formulate `maximum-entropy' hydrodynamics, a far-off-equilibrium macroscopic theory that can describe both free-streaming and near-equilibrium regimes of quark-gluon plasma in a single framework.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for certain far-off-equilibrium initial conditions in a (1+1)-dimensional weakly-coupled quark-gluon plasma undergoing Bjorken expansion, the total equilibrium entropy can decrease with time, but this is consistent with the second law of thermodynamics when using a non-equilibrium entropy definition based on Boltzmann's H-function.

## Method

The authors solved the Boltzmann equation in a relaxation-time approximation for a weakly interacting gas of quarks, antiquarks, and gluons undergoing boost-invariant Bjorken expansion, using Romatschke-Strickland distributions to initialize extreme far-off-equilibrium conditions. They then formulated a macroscopic "maximum-entropy" hydrodynamics (ME-hydro) by reconstructing the kinetic distribution from the energy-momentum tensor using the maximum entropy principle, truncating the infinite hierarchy of momentum moments. The ME-hydro framework was tested against kinetic theory in both Bjorken and Gubser flow geometries.

## Key numbers

- The early-stage far-off-equilibrium dynamics is modeled until approximately 1 fm/c, based on prior work by Kurkela (2018) and Liyanage (2022).
- In Bjorken flow, the energy-momentum tensor takes the form diag(e, P_T, P_T, P_L) with transverse pressure P_T and longitudinal pressure P_L.
- The non-equilibrium entropy density is computed from Boltzmann's H-function using the formula s = -Σ_i ∫_{p_i} (u·p_i) [f^i ln f^i - (1 + a_i f^i)/a_i ln(1 + a_i f^i)], where a_q = -1 and a_g = 1.
- In Gubser flow, the evolution equations are expressed in terms of de-Sitter "time" ρ for two independent dimensionless variables (ê, P̂_T).

## Context

This work extends the study of far-off-equilibrium dynamics in quark-gluon plasma beyond the near-equilibrium regime typically described by Navier-Stokes hydrodynamics. It builds on the kinetic theory approach of Kurkela (2018) and Liyanage (2022), and the maximum entropy principle applied to hydrodynamics by Everett (2021). The paper enables a single macroscopic framework (ME-hydro) that can describe both the free-streaming and near-equilibrium regimes, outperforming Chapman-Enskog hydrodynamics (Chattopadhyay, 2018) in capturing the late-time transverse free-streaming regime of Gubser flow, while performing comparably to anisotropic hydrodynamics (Martinez, 2010; Martinez, 2017).
