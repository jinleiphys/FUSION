# $D^*DŽĆ$ and $B^*BŽĆ$ form factors from QCD Sum Rules

> The $H^*HŽĆ$ form factor for H = B and D mesons is evaluated in a QCD sum rule calculation. We study the Borel sum rule for the three point function of two pseudoscalar and one vector meson currents up to order four in the operator product expansion. The double Borel transform is performed with respect to the heavy meson momenta. We discuss the momentum dependence of the form factors and two different approaches to extract the $H^*HŽĆ$ coupling constant.

# $D^*D\pi$ and $B^*B\pi$ form factors from QCD Sum Rules

## Key claim
The $H^*H\pi$ coupling constants for $H = D$ and $H = B$ are extracted from QCD sum rules using a three-point function approach with double Borel transformation, yielding values that are systematically lower than those from light cone sum rules but consistent with other QCD sum rule methods. The results depend on the choice of continuum thresholds and parametrization of the form factor, with the $D^*D\pi$ coupling showing better agreement between two independent sum rule methods than the $B^*B\pi$ coupling.

## Method
The authors evaluate the three-point correlation function of two pseudoscalar and one vector meson currents up to dimension four in the operator product expansion, including the perturbative diagram and gluon condensate. A double Borel transform is performed with respect to the heavy meson momenta, and the sum rule is studied for the $p_\mu$ invariant structure. Two approaches are used to extract the coupling constant: extrapolation of the momentum-dependent form factor to $Q^2 = 0$ using Gaussian and other parametrizations, and a $Q^2 = 0$ sum rule with a single Borel transformation that includes a parameter $A$ to account for pole-continuum transitions.

## Key numbers
- $g_{D^*D\pi} = 5.7 \pm 0.4$ (final result, combining variations in continuum thresholds, parametrizations, and sum rule methods)
- $g_{B^*B\pi} = 14.5 \pm 3.9$ (final result, with same error sources)
- From the $Q^2 = 0$ sum rule method alone: $g_{D^*D\pi} \simeq 5.4$ and $g_{B^*B\pi} \simeq 10.6$ (for $\Delta_s = \Delta_u = 0.5$ GeV)
- From form factor fits with $\Delta_s = \Delta_u = 0.5$ GeV: $g_{D^*D\pi} = 5.3$ (monopole-like fit) and $5.7$ (Gaussian fit); $g_{B^*B\pi} = 14.7$ (monopole-like fit) and $17.2$ (Gaussian fit)
- Derived $D^*$ decay width: $\Gamma({D^*}^- \rightarrow \overline{D^0}\pi^-) = 6.3 \pm 0.9$ keV
- Derived coupling $g$ (for infinitely heavy fields): $g = 0.13 - 0.23$
- Input parameters: $m_c = 1.5$ GeV, $m_b = 4.7$ GeV, $m_D = 1.87$ GeV, $m_{D^*} = 2.01$ GeV, $m_B = 5.28$ GeV, $m_{B^*} = 5.33$ GeV, $f_\pi = 131.5$ MeV, $\langle \bar{q}q \rangle = -(0.23)^3$ GeV$^3$, $\langle g^2 G^2 \rangle = 0.5$ GeV$^4$

## Context
This work extends previous QCD sum rule calculations of the $H^*H\pi$ couplings, which had shown discrepancies of almost a factor of two between different approaches. The results are compared with the two-point function plus soft pion techniques of Colangelo et al. (1994) and the light cone sum rules of Belyaev et al. (1995) and Khodjamirian et al. (1999). The authors find fair agreement with the double momentum sum rule of Deandrea et al. (1998), while light cone sum rules give systematically larger values. The extracted coupling $g$ in the range 0.13-0.23 supports the QCD sum rule consensus that this coupling is small, in contrast to quark model predictions that give $1/3 \leq g \leq 1$. The results enable improved calculations of charmonium absorption by pions in heavy ion collisions, relevant for quark-gluon plasma signatures.
