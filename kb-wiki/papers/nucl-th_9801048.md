# Consequences of covariant kaon dynamics in heavy ion collisions

> The influence of the chiral mean field on the kaon dynamics in heavy ion reactions is investigated. Inside the nuclear medium the kaons are described as dressed quasi-particles carrying effective masses and momenta. A momentum dependent part of the interaction which resembles a Lorentz force originates from spatial components of the vector field and provides an important contribution to the in-medium kaon dynamics. This contribution is found to counterbalance the influence of the vector potential on the $K^+$ in-plane flow to a strong extent. Thus it appears to be difficult to restrict the in-medium potential from the analysis of the corresponding transverse flow.

## Key claim

The momentum-dependent Lorentz force arising from the spatial components of the vector field counterbalances the repulsive vector potential's effect on K+ in-plane flow to such an extent that it becomes difficult to restrict the in-medium kaon potential from the analysis of transverse flow data.

## Method

The authors extended a covariant quasi-particle framework, previously used for nucleons in relativistic transport theories, to describe kaons in heavy ion collisions. They derived equations of motion from the chiral Lagrangian in mean field approximation, incorporating effective masses and momenta, and applied three different parameterizations of the kaon mean field (MF, MF2, and a chiral perturbation theory parameterization) to simulate Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 A.GeV within the Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (RQMD) approach.

## Key numbers

- The mean field (MF) parameterization uses a kaon-nucleon Sigma term of 350 MeV; the MF2 parameterization uses 450 MeV with a rescaled pion decay constant f*_pi^2 = 0.6 f_pi^2.
- The vector potential V_0 at nuclear density 0.16 fm^-3 is 57 MeV for MF, 79 MeV for the chiral perturbation theory parameterization, and 95 MeV for MF2.
- The reaction studied is Ni+Ni at 1.93 A.GeV incident energy, with a semi-central impact parameter of b=3 fm for the illustrative calculations.
- The transverse momentum cut used for comparison with FOPI data is P_T / m_K > 0.5.

## Context

This work extends the covariant quasi-particle treatment of nucleons in relativistic mean fields (Serot 1988, Fuchs 1996) to kaons, building on the mean field approximation of Kaplan and Nelson (1986) and the kaon flow studies of Li and Ko (1995). It contradicts the expectation that K+ flow can straightforwardly constrain the in-medium potential, as the Lorentz force term cancels the vector potential's effect. The results suggest that future studies should examine radial flow phenomena (Wang 1998) and include explicit momentum dependence from p-wave contributions in the kaon-nucleon interaction.
