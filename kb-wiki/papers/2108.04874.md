# Temperature dependence of the properties of open heavy-flavor mesons

> We address the modification of open heavy-flavor mesons in a hot medium of light mesons within an effective theory approach consistent with chiral and heavy-quark spin-flavor symmetries and the use of the imaginary time formalism to introduce the non-zero temperature effects to the theory. The unitarized scattering amplitudes, the ground-state self-energies and the corresponding spectral functions are calculated self-consistently. We use the thermal ground-state spectral functions obtained with this methodology to further calculate 1) open-charm meson Euclidean correlators, and 2) off-shell transport coefficients in the hadronic phase.

## Key claim
The paper claims that open heavy-flavor mesons in a hot medium of light mesons acquire a width and a mass drop of some tens of MeV at T=150 MeV, but this result is conditional on a self-consistent iterative calculation using an effective theory that incorporates chiral and heavy-quark spin-flavor symmetries, and the thermal modifications are only computed below the deconfining transition temperature T_c.

## Method
The authors use an effective Lagrangian consistent with chiral and heavy-quark spin-flavor symmetries to describe s-wave scattering of open heavy-flavor mesons (D, D_s) off light mesons (π, K, K̅, η) in a thermal bath. They unitarize the scattering amplitudes by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels, and introduce finite-temperature effects via the imaginary-time formalism, dressing the heavy meson in the loop with its spectral function. The self-energy is obtained by closing the light-meson line in the corresponding T-matrix element, and self-consistency is achieved by iterating the equations for the two-body propagator, spectral function, and self-energy.

## Key numbers
- Mass decrease of open heavy-flavor ground states of some tens of MeV at T=150 MeV (exact value not specified in text).
- Width increase of open heavy-flavor ground states of some tens of MeV at T=150 MeV (exact value not specified in text).
- The spatial diffusion coefficient 2π T D_s(T) is computed below T_c and compared to lattice QCD results around T_c (exact numerical value not given in text).

## Context
This work extends the effective theory approach of Montana et al. (2020) for open heavy-flavor mesons at zero temperature to finite temperature, and compares results to lattice QCD calculations by Kelly et al. (2018). The computed Euclidean correlators and transport coefficients enable direct comparison with lattice QCD data and provide input for modeling heavy-flavor meson propagation in heavy-ion collisions, with the off-shell transport coefficients allowing smooth matching to lattice QCD results around the deconfining transition temperature.
