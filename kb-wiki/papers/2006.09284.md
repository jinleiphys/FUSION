# Pseudothermalization of the quark-gluon plasma

> In this proceedings contribution I review recent work in kinetic theory which demonstrates that, for system undergoing Bjorken expansion, there exists an attractor in all moments of the one-particle distribution function. I discuss how this attractor emerges in both exact solutions obtained in relaxation time approximation (RTA) and the effective kinetic theory approach to high-temperature quantum chromodynamics (QCD). The QCD effective kinetic theory collisional kernel used includes both elastic (2 <-> 2) and LPM-resummed inelastic (2 <-> 1) contributions. The results obtained indicate that a pseudothermal attractor exists in both RTA and QCD kinetic theory and that their respective attractors can be extended to early times when the system is far from equilibrium. Finally, I discuss how knowledge of the QCD effective kinetic theory attractor can be used to assess different hydrodynamic freeze-out prescriptions used in heavy-ion phenomenology. The results obtained show that improved freeze-out prescriptions such as anisotropic hydrodynamics perform better in conditions corresponding to those generated in high-multiplicity pA and pp collisions, e.g. short lifetime and high inverse Reynolds number.

# Pseudothermalization of the quark-gluon plasma

## Key claim

For a system undergoing Bjorken expansion, there exists an attractor in all moments of the one-particle distribution function, not just the energy-momentum tensor, and this attractor can be extended to early times when the system is far from equilibrium. The claim is hedged by the fact that the attractor is demonstrated in both relaxation time approximation (RTA) and QCD effective kinetic theory (EKT), but the QCD EKT results are obtained for a gluonic plasma only, and the convergence of moments with n=0 is slower than for higher moments.

## Method

The author reviews exact solutions to the RTA Boltzmann equation for a boost-invariant, transversally homogeneous Bjorken-expanding system, obtaining a closed integral equation for the effective temperature T(tau) and then solving for all moments of the distribution function. For QCD, the author uses a numerical implementation of the Arnold-Moore-Yaffe effective kinetic theory that includes both elastic (2 to 2) and LPM-resummed inelastic (2 to 1) contributions, discretizing the gluonic distribution function on a 250 x 2000 grid in momentum magnitude p and angular variable x = cos(theta). The evolution is computed for two classes of initial conditions: spheroidally deformed thermal (RS) and over-occupied CGC-type initial conditions, with varying initial momentum-space anisotropy and initialization time.

## Key numbers

- Convergence to the attractor occurs by approximately w-bar = tau/tau_eq = 2 for all moments with n > 1, where tau_eq = 5 eta-bar / T is the relaxation time.
- The grid used for EKT numerical solution has 250 points in p and 2000 points in x = cos(theta).
- Equilibrium typical momenta: <p>^10_eq is approximately 2.7 T, <p>^01_eq = <p>^20_eq is approximately 3.83 T, <p>^21_eq is approximately 5.95 T, and <p>^33_eq is approximately 11 T.
- The instantaneous interaction time used for scaling is tau_R(tau) = 4 pi eta-bar / T(tau).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of hydrodynamization and non-equilibrium attractors by Chesler and Yaffe (2009) and Heller and collaborators (2015-2019), which focused on the energy-momentum tensor, to all moments of the distribution function. The first attempts to address this question were presented by Strickland (2018) and Strickland (2019) using RTA. The QCD EKT results reported here from Almaalol, Kurkela, and Tantary (2020) establish the existence of an early-time attractor in full QCD kinetic theory. This enables assessment of different hydrodynamic freeze-out prescriptions used in heavy-ion phenomenology, showing that anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) performs better than linearized viscous hydrodynamics prescriptions in far-from-equilibrium conditions relevant to high-multiplicity pA and pp collisions.
