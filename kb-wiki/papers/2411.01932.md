# Enhancing Bayesian parameter estimation by adapting to multiple energy scales in RHIC and LHC heavy-ion collisions

> Improved constraints on current model parameters in a heavy-ion collision model are established using the latest measurements from three distinct collision systems. Various observables are utilized from Au--Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=200$~GeV and Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=5.02$~TeV and $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=2.76$~TeV. Additionally, the calibration of centrality is now carried out separately for all parametrizations. The inclusion of an Au--Au collision system with an order of magnitude lower beam energy, along with separate centrality calibration, suggests a preference for smaller values of nucleon width, minimum volume per nucleon, and free-streaming time. The results with the acquired \textit{maximum a posteriori} parameters show improved agreement with the data for the second-order flow coefficient, identified particle yields, and mean transverse momenta. This work contributes to a more comprehensive understanding of heavy-ion collision dynamics and sets the stage for future improvements in theoretical modeling and experimental measurements.

## Key claim

Including Au--Au collisions at 200 GeV alongside Pb--Pb collisions at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV, combined with separate centrality calibration for each parametrization, shifts the preferred values of nucleon width, minimum volume per nucleon, and free-streaming time toward the lower boundaries of their prior ranges, while the extracted shear and bulk viscosity coefficients show higher values with larger uncertainties compared to previous two-energy analyses.

## Method

A multi-stage heavy-ion collision model (TRENTo initial conditions, free-streaming pre-equilibrium, VISH2+1 viscous hydrodynamics, and UrQMD hadronic afterburner) with 16 free parameters was calibrated via Bayesian inference using a Gaussian process emulator trained on 500 Latin hypercube design points, each generating 3-5 million events per collision energy. Experimental observables including charged-particle yields, identified particle mean transverse momenta, anisotropic flow coefficients v2-v5, nonlinear flow coefficients, normalized symmetric cumulants, and symmetry-plane correlations were included from Au--Au at 200 GeV and Pb--Pb at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV, with centrality calibrated individually for each parameter set rather than using a fixed reference.

## Key numbers

* MAP value for nucleon width w = 0.6692 fm (at the lower boundary of prior range 0.6692-1.2428 fm)
* MAP value for minimum volume per nucleon d_min^3 = 0.7026 fm^3 (below the prior range lower bound of 0.889^3 fm^3)
* MAP value for free-streaming time tau_fs = 0.4576 fm/c (prior range 0.03-1.5 fm/c)
* MAP value for minimum shear viscosity eta/s(T_c) = 0.1578 (prior range 0-0.2)
* MAP value for maximum bulk viscosity (zeta/s)_max = 0.0746 (prior range 0-0.1)
* MAP value for switching/particlization temperature T_switch = 0.1469 GeV (prior range 0.135-0.165 GeV)
* Event statistics: 3x10^6 events at 5.02 TeV, 5x10^6 at 2.76 TeV, 4x10^6 at 200 GeV per design point
* Pb--Pb identified particle mean p_T predictions agree with data within 5%

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian calibration framework of Bernhard (2019) and Parkkila (2021) by adding Au--Au collisions at RHIC energy (200 GeV) to the existing LHC Pb--Pb data at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV, probing the model's ability to simultaneously describe systems differing by an order of magnitude in beam energy. The separate centrality calibration per parametrization reduces bias present in previous analyses that used a fixed reference parametrization. The results enable future studies to incorporate additional observables such as normalized asymmetric cumulants and unbiased symmetry-plane correlations, which show strong sensitivity to transport coefficients and switching temperature.
