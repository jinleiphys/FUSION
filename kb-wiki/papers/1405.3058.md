# More about the $B$ and $D$ mesons in nuclear matter

> We calculate the shifts in decay constants of the pseudoscalar $B$ and $D$ mesons in nuclear medium in the frame work of QCD sum rules. We write those shifts in terms of the $B-N$ and $D-N$ scattering lengths and an extra phenomenological parameter entered to calculations. Computing an appreciate forward scattering correlation function, we derive the QCD sum rules for the $B-N$ and $D-N$ scattering lengths and the extra phenomenological parameter in terms of various operators in nuclear medium. We numerically find the values of the shifts in the decay constants compared to their vacuum values. Using the sum rules obtained, we also determine the shifts in the masses of these particles due to nuclear matter and compare the results obtained with the previous predictions in the literature.

## Key claim

The shifts in the decay constants of pseudoscalar \(B\) and \(D\) mesons in nuclear matter are negative and are expressed in terms of the \(B-N\) and \(D-N\) scattering lengths plus an extra phenomenological parameter; the numerical results depend on the chosen Borel mass and continuum threshold working regions, with the shift in the \(B\) meson decay constant being roughly ten times larger than that of the \(D\) meson.

## Method

The authors used QCD sum rules in the linear density approximation for nuclear matter, computing a forward scattering correlation function for the \(B\) and \(D\) mesons interacting with a nucleon. They derived sum rules for the \(B-N\) and \(D-N\) scattering lengths and an extra phenomenological parameter by equating the hadronic and operator product expansion (OPE) sides of the correlation function, including contributions from various nuclear medium condensates. Borel transformation and continuum subtraction were applied to suppress higher-state contributions, and the parameters \(a\) and \(b\) were solved simultaneously from the sum rules and their derivatives with respect to \(1/M^2\).

## Key numbers

* Shift in the decay constant of the \(B\) meson: \(\delta f_B = -0.023 \pm 0.007\) GeV.
* Shift in the decay constant of the \(D\) meson: \(\delta f_D = -0.002 \pm 0.001\) GeV.
* Working region for the Borel mass parameter \(M^2\) in the \(B\) channel: \(25~\text{GeV}^2 \leq M^2 \leq 40~\text{GeV}^2\).
* Working region for the Borel mass parameter \(M^2\) in the \(D\) channel: \(4~\text{GeV}^2 \leq M^2 \leq 8~\text{GeV}^2\).
* Working region for the continuum threshold \(s_0\) in the \(B\) channel: \(34~\text{GeV}^2 \leq s_0 \leq 38~\text{GeV}^2\).
* Working region for the continuum threshold \(s_0\) in the \(D\) channel: \(5.6~\text{GeV}^2 \leq s_0 \leq 6.4~\text{GeV}^2\).
* Saturation nuclear matter density used: \(\rho_N^{\text{sat}} = (0.11)^3~\text{GeV}^3\).

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule studies of in-medium mass shifts for \(D\) and \(B\) mesons by Hayashigaki (2000) and Hilger (2009) to also include the shifts in the decay constants of these pseudoscalar mesons in nuclear matter. The results provide input for analyzing heavy ion collision experiments and for understanding the strong interactions of \(B\) and \(D\) mesons with nucleons, including their electromagnetic structures and decays such as \(B\) into charmed \(D\) mesons. The computed decay constant shifts can be used in future studies of meson properties at facilities like FAIR, CBM, and PANDA.
