# Meson-baryon scattering in resummed baryon chiral perturbation theory using time-ordered perturbation theory

> Integral equations for meson-baryon scattering amplitudes are obtained by utilizing time-ordered perturbation theory for a manifestly Lorentz-invariant formulation of baryon chiral perturbation theory. Effective potentials are defined as sums of two-particle irreducible contributions of time-ordered diagrams and the scattering amplitudes are obtained as solutions of integral equations. Ultraviolet renormalizability is achieved by solving integral equations for the leading order amplitude and including higher order corrections perturbatively. As an application of the developed formalism, pion-nucleon scattering is considered.

# Meson-baryon scattering in resummed baryon chiral perturbation theory using time-ordered perturbation theory

## Key claim

The authors develop a manifestly Lorentz-invariant formulation of baryon chiral perturbation theory using time-ordered perturbation theory that yields renormalizable integral equations for meson-baryon scattering amplitudes, provided that the leading-order amplitude is solved exactly and higher-order corrections are included perturbatively. The approach is applied to pion-nucleon scattering, but the authors caution that the $P_{33}$-wave cannot be described properly without explicitly including the $\Delta(1232)$ resonance as a dynamical degree of freedom.

## Method

The authors define effective meson-baryon potentials as sums of two-particle irreducible time-ordered perturbation theory diagrams derived from a SU(3) effective Lagrangian that includes pseudoscalar mesons, baryons, and vector mesons (with vector mesons included to improve ultraviolet behavior). The scattering amplitudes are obtained by solving coupled-channel integral equations in the partial wave basis, where the leading-order equation is solved exactly and higher-order corrections are treated perturbatively. For the pion-nucleon application, subtractive renormalization is employed to remove ultraviolet divergences, with the subtracted propagator chosen to match the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order pion-nucleon potential includes contributions from the axial coupling $g_A$, the pion decay constant $F_\pi$, the nucleon mass $m_N$, and the $\rho$-meson mass $M_\rho$, with the KSFR relation $M_\rho^2 = 2 g^2 F_\pi^2$ used to relate the $\rho$-meson mass to the coupling $g$.
- The two-body Green function for intermediate meson-baryon states is given by $G^{MB}(E) = \frac{1}{2 \omega_M \omega_B} \frac{-m_B}{E - \omega_M - \omega_B + i\epsilon}$, where $\omega_I = (\vec{q}^2 + m_I^2)^{1/2}$.
- The subtraction scheme replaces the pion-nucleon propagator $G(E)$ with $G_S(E) = G(E) - G(m_N)$, where $m_N$ is the physical nucleon mass, corresponding to taking into account contributions of an infinite number of pion-nucleon counterterms.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach, which has been used to describe meson-baryon scattering and interpret resonances like the $\Lambda(1405)$ as molecular states (Oller 2000, Jido 2003), by providing a manifestly renormalizable framework based on time-ordered perturbation theory. It addresses shortcomings of previous unitarization methods such as the Bethe-Salpeter equation with on-shell factorization (Oset 1997, Oller 1998) and the inverse amplitude method (Gomez Nicola 1999), which introduce cutoff dependence or violate chiral counting rules. The formalism enables future applications to SU(3) meson-baryon scattering with strangeness, including coupled-channel treatments of the $\Lambda(1405)$ region, and can be extended to include the spin-3/2 decuplet (e.g., the $\Delta(1232)$) as explicit degrees of freedom.
