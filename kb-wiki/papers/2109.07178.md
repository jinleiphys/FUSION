# Systematic study of two-proton radioactivity half-lives within the two-potential approach with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock

> In this work, we systematically study the two-proton($2p$) radioactivity half-lives using the two-potential approach while the nuclear potential is obtained by using Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach with the Skyrme effective interaction of {SLy8}. For true $2p$ radioactivity($Q_{2p}$ $>$ 0 and $Q_p$ $< $0, where the $Q_p$ and $Q_{2p}$ are the released energy of the one-proton and two-proton radioactivity), the standard deviation between the experimental half-lives and our theoretical calculations is {0.701}. In addition, we extend this model to predict the half-lives of 15 possible $2p$ radioactivity candidates with $Q_{2p}$ $>$ 0 taken from the evaluated atomic mass table AME2016. The calculated results indicate that a clear linear relationship between the logarithmic $2p$ radioactivity half-lives $\rm{log}_{10}T_{1/2}$ and coulomb parameters [ ($Z_{d}^{0.8}$+$l^{0.25}$)$Q_{2p}^{-1/2}$] considered the effect of orbital angular momentum proposed by Liu $et$ $al$ [Chin. Phys. C \textbf{45}, 024108 (2021)] is also existed. For comparison, the generalized liquid drop model(GLDM), the effective liquid drop model(ELDM) and Gamow-like model are also used. Our predicted results are consistent with the ones obtained by the other models.

## Key claim

The two-potential approach combined with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (TPA-SHF) using the SLy8 Skyrme interaction can reproduce experimental two-proton radioactivity half-lives with a standard deviation of 0.701, but only for true 2p radioactivity cases where the one-proton emission is energetically forbidden (Qp less than 0) and the two-proton emission is allowed (Q2p greater than 0).

## Method

The authors calculated two-proton radioactivity half-lives using the two-potential approach, where the nuclear potential between the emitted two protons and the daughter nucleus was obtained from the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method with the SLy8 Skyrme effective interaction, assuming the two protons share momentum equally and the nuclear potential is twice that of a single proton. The spectroscopic factor S2p was included using the cluster overlap approximation with a fixed parameter chi-squared of 0.0143. The model was applied to nuclei with atomic numbers between 4 and 36, and the results were compared with the generalized liquid drop model (GLDM), effective liquid drop model (ELDM), and Gamow-like model.

## Key numbers

- Standard deviation sigma = 0.701 between experimental and calculated logarithmic half-lives for true 2p radioactivity emitters using the TPA-SHF model
- For comparison, sigma values for other models: GLDM = 0.852, ELDM = 0.531, Gamow-like = 0.844
- Predicted half-lives for 15 candidate nuclei with Q2p greater than 0 from AME2016, including 49Ni (log10 T1/2 = 11.05 s) and 60Ge (log10 T1/2 = 12.09 s) as the longest-lived predictions
- Experimental half-lives reproduced include 19Mg (log10 T1/2 exp = -11.40 s, calculated = -11.00 s), 45Fe (multiple Q2p values from 1.100 to 1.210 MeV), 48Ni (Q2p = 1.350 and 1.290 MeV), 54Zn (Q2p = 1.480 and 1.280 MeV), and 67Kr (Q2p = 1.690 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the two-potential approach with Skyrme-Hartree-Fock, previously applied to proton radioactivity by the same group, to two-proton radioactivity for the first time. It builds on the diproton model and barrier penetration framework shared with alpha decay and cluster radioactivity, and compares directly with the generalized liquid drop model of Cui et al. (2020), the effective liquid drop model of Goncalves et al. (2017), and the Gamow-like model. The predicted half-lives for 15 candidates from AME2016 enable future experimental searches at radioactive beam facilities, and the linear relationship with the Coulomb parameter proposed by Liu et al. (2021) is confirmed for all four models tested.
