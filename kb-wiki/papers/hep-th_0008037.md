# Coarse graining and renormalization

> Formulating the QFT's as coarse grained 'low' energy sectors of a postulated complete quantum theory of everything with the 'high' energy modes integrated out or 'clustering' into 'low' energy objects, we can evaluate the Feynman amplitudes by solving a series of natural differential equations which automatically dissolves the necessity of infinity subtraction and the associated subtleties. This new strategy has direct implications to the scheme dependence problem.

# Coarse graining and renormalization

## Key claim
If one postulates the existence of a complete quantum theory of everything (QTOE) whose high-energy modes are integrated out, then Feynman amplitudes can be evaluated by solving differential equations that eliminate the need for infinity subtraction, provided the ordering condition sup{Λ_QFT, μ̄} ≪ inf{σ} holds, where σ are the parameters characterizing the high-energy modes and μ̄ is the clustering threshold scale.

## Method
The author defines a coarse-grained vacuum functional by applying a limit operation L_{σ} (taking σ → ∞) to a QTOE-defined generating functional, where the order of functional integration and the limit cannot be trivially exchanged. For each one-loop divergent diagram, a theorem is proved: applying (∂_p)^{ω_Γ+1} to the QTOE amplitude (with ω_Γ the superficial divergence degree) yields a convergent integral of the conventional integrand, so the amplitude can be recovered up to a polynomial of momenta and masses of power ω_Γ. The integration constants (finite but undetermined) must be fixed by symmetries, sum rules, or experimental data, paralleling renormalization conditions.

## Key numbers
- The ordering condition requires sup{Λ_QFT, μ̄} ≪ inf{σ}, where Λ_QFT is a general dimensional parameter (momenta or masses) in the QFT under consideration, μ̄ is the clustering threshold scale, and σ are the high-energy mode parameters.
- The superficial divergence degree ω_Γ determines the power of the differential operator (∂_p)^{ω_Γ+1} applied to the amplitude, and the resulting polynomial of momenta and masses has power ω_Γ.
- The renormalization group equation (Eq. 11) takes the standard form {μ ∂_μ − Σ δ̄_g ḡ ∂_{ḡ} − δ̄_{Γ^{(n)}}} Γ^{(n)}((p), (ḡ); {μ, [c̄⁰]}) = 0, with μ = t μ̄ and t such that max[μ] ≪ inf{σ}.

## Context
This paper extends the Wilsonian renormalization group approach (Wilson and Kogut, 1974) by refining the coarse-graining picture: high-energy modes are physically suppressed by a clustering mechanism in a postulated QTOE rather than cut off by hand. The work addresses the scheme dependence problem discussed by Stevenson (1981), Grunberg (1984), and Brodsky, Lepage, and Mackenzie (1983), and aims to remove the conceptual subtleties of infinite bare parameters and subtraction. The approach enables a reinterpretation of the renormalization group equation as a decoupling theorem for underlying high-energy modes, and it points toward a unified framework where quantization, coarse graining, and renormalization are coherently organized.
