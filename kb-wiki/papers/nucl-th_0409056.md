# Deformations in N=14 isotones

> Systematic analysis of deformations in neutron-rich N=14 isotones was done based on the method of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. The property of the shape coexistence in $^{28}$Si, which is known to have the oblate ground state and the prolate excited states, was successfully described. The results suggest that the shape coexistence may occur also in neutron-rich N=14 nuclei as well as $^{28}$Si. It was found that the oblate neutron shapes are favored because of the spin-orbit force in most of N=14 isotones. $Q$ moments and $E2$ transition strengths in the neutron-rich nuclei were discussed in relation to the intrinsic deformations, and a possible difference between the proton and neutron deformations in $^{24}$Ne was proposed.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that shape coexistence (oblate and prolate states) may occur in neutron-rich N=14 isotones beyond the well-known case of 28Si, but the actual occurrence and ordering of these shapes depends sensitively on the strength of the spin-orbit force and on the proton number of the specific nucleus.

## Method

The author performed systematic antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations for the N=14 isotones 19B, 20C, 22O, 24Ne, and 28Si. Energy variation was performed for parity-projected intrinsic states, followed by total-angular-momentum projection and diagonalization of the Hamiltonian and norm matrices to obtain rotational bands. Two different strengths of the spin-orbit force (u_ls = 900 MeV and u_ls = 2800 MeV) were tested using the MV1 central force with Majorana parameter m = 0.62.

## Key numbers

- In 28Si with u_ls = 2800 MeV, the calculated B(E2;2+ → 0+) for the oblate ground band is 9.1 Weisskopf units (w.u.), compared to the experimental value of 12.9 ± 0.5 w.u.
- The calculated B(E2;4+ → 2+) for the prolate band in 28Si is 40.0 w.u., compared to the experimental sum of 43.5 (+11.6, -8.7) w.u. for transitions into 2+(7.381) and 2+(7.417)
- For 24Ne, the calculated B(E2;2+ → 0+) in the 'oblate' ground band is 9.2 w.u., compared to the experimental value of 6.8 ± 2.9 w.u.
- For 24Si (evaluated assuming mirror symmetry with 24Ne), the calculated B(E2;2+ → 0+) in the 'oblate' band is 9.4 w.u., compared to the experimental value of 4.6 ± 1.4 w.u.
- The expectation value of the spin-orbit force in the oblate state of 28Si is -23.9 MeV, while in the prolate state it is -2.0 MeV (with u_ls = 2800 MeV)
- The calculated electric quadrupole moment Q(2+_1) for 28Si is 132 e^2fm^4, compared to the experimental value of 160(3) e^2fm^4
- The calculated magnetic dipole moment μ(2+_1) for 28Si is 1.03 μ_N, compared to the experimental value of 1.12(18) μ_N

## Context

This work extends previous AMD studies of B isotopes (Kanada-En'yo 2003) and earlier Hartree-Fock calculations on 28Si (Gupta 1967), while addressing the failure of most 7α-cluster model calculations (Bauhoff 1982, Zhang 1994) to reproduce the correct ordering of oblate and prolate states in 28Si due to omission of the spin-orbit force. The results enable predictions for the ground-state deformations of neutron-rich N=14 nuclei and suggest that the recently measured B(E2) in 24Si (Kanno 2004) can be explained by a difference between proton and neutron deformations in 24Ne, rather than by mirror symmetry expectations.
