# Mixing angle of $K_1$ axial vector mesons

> Analyses of various experimental measurements all indicate that the mixing angle $θ_{K_1}$ of $K_1(1270)$ and $K_1(1400)$ is in the vicinity of $33^\circ$ or $57^\circ$. However, whether $θ_{K_1}$ is greater or less than $45^\circ$ is still quite controversial. For example, there were two very recent studies of the strong decays of $K_1$ mesons. One group claimed that $θ_{K_1}\approx 60^\circ$, while the other group obtained $θ_{K_1}=(33.6\pm4.3)^\circ$. Since the determination of the mixing angles $α_{^3\!P_1}$ and $α_{^1\!P_1}$ with the former (latter) being the mixing angle of $f_1(1285)$ ($h_1(1170)$) and $f_1(1420)$ ($h_1(1380)$) in the flavor basis through mass relations depend on $θ_{K_1}$, we show that $θ_{K_1}\approx 57^\circ$ is ruled out as it leads to a too large deviation from ideal mixing in the $^1\!P_1$ sector, inconsistent with the lattice calculation of $α_{^1\!P_1}$ and the observation of strong decays of $h_1(1170)$ and $h_1(1380)$. We find that for $θ_{K_1}\approx (28-30)^\circ$, the corresponding $α_{^3\!P_1}$ and $α_{^1\!P_1}$ agree well with all lattice and phenomenological analyses. This again reinforces the statement that $θ_{K_1}\sim 33^\circ$ is much more favored than $57^\circ$.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the mixing angle \(\theta_{K_1}\) of \(K_1(1270)\) and \(K_1(1400)\) is near \(33^\circ\) rather than \(57^\circ\), because the \(57^\circ\) solution leads to a deviation from ideal mixing in the \(^1P_1\) sector that is inconsistent with lattice calculations and observed strong decay patterns of \(h_1(1170)\) and \(h_1(1380)\).

## Method

The author uses Gell-Mann–Okubo mass relations to compute the mixing angles \(\alpha_{^3P_1}\) (for \(f_1(1285)\)–\(f_1(1420)\)) and \(\alpha_{^1P_1}\) (for \(h_1(1170)\)–\(h_1(1380)\)) in the flavor basis as functions of \(\theta_{K_1}\). By comparing the resulting \(\alpha_{^1P_1}\) and \(\alpha_{^3P_1}\) values with lattice QCD results from the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration and with phenomenological analyses of radiative and strong decays, the author identifies which \(\theta_{K_1}\) values are consistent with all constraints.

## Key numbers

* \(\theta_{K_1} \approx 57^\circ\) yields \(\alpha_{^1P_1} = -53^\circ\), which is inconsistent with the lattice result \(\alpha_{^1P_1} = \pm(3 \pm 1)^\circ\) from the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration (Dudek 2011).
* \(\theta_{K_1} \approx (28-30)^\circ\) gives \(\alpha_{^1P_1} = -3.8^\circ\) to \(-2.4^\circ\) and \(\alpha_{^3P_1} = -18.9^\circ\) to \(-23.5^\circ\), agreeing with lattice and phenomenological analyses.
* Radiative decays of \(f_1(1285)\) yield \(\alpha_{^3P_1} = \pm(15.8^{+4.5}_{-4.6})^\circ\) (Yang 2012); an updated lattice calculation gives \(\alpha_{^3P_1} = \pm(27 \pm 2)^\circ\) (Dudek 2013); LHCb study of \(B_{d,s} \to J/\psi f_1(1285)\) gives \(\alpha_{^3P_1} = \pm(24.0^{+3.1+0.6}_{-2.6-0.8})^\circ\).
* For \(\theta_{K_1} = 57^\circ\), the quark content becomes \(h_1(1170) = 0.60 n\bar n - 0.80 s\bar s\) and \(h_1(1380) = 0.80 n\bar n + 0.60 s\bar s\), contradicting the observed decay modes.

## Context

This work extends earlier determinations of the \(K_1\) mixing angle by Suzuki (1993), Goldman (1993), and Cheng (1997), which all found two possible solutions near \(33^\circ\) or \(57^\circ\). It directly addresses the recent controversy between Kou (2013), who claimed \(\theta_{K_1} \approx 60^\circ\) from a \(^3P_0\) quark-pair-creation model, and Giacosa (2013), who obtained \(\theta_{K_1} = (33.6 \pm 4.3)^\circ\) from a phenomenological Lagrangian. By using lattice QCD results from Dudek (2011, 2013) and LHCb data on \(B_{d,s} \to J/\psi f_1(1285)\), the paper resolves the ambiguity and enables more reliable predictions for \(K_1\) decays in \(B\) and \(\tau\) physics.
