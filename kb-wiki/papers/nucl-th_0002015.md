# A phenomenological analysis of antiproton interactions at low energies

> We present an optical potential analysis of the antiproton-proton interactions at low energies. Our optical potential is purely phenomenological, and has been parametrized on data recently obtained by the Obelix Collaboration at momenta below 180 MeV/c. It reasonably fits annihilation and elastic data below 600 MeV/c, and allows us for an evaluation of the elastic cross section and rho-parameter down to zero kinetic energy. Moreover we show that the mechanism that depresses antiproton-nucleus annihilation cross sections at low energies is present in antiproton-proton interactions too.

# A phenomenological analysis of antiproton interactions at low energies

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a single energy-independent optical potential, with parameters fine-tuned on Obelix Collaboration antiproton-proton annihilation data below 180 MeV/c, can simultaneously fit annihilation and elastic data up to 600 MeV/c and predict the rho-parameter down to zero kinetic energy. The authors further claim that the "inversion" mechanism, where a very strong annihilation potential produces anomalously small reaction cross sections at low energies, which was previously observed in antiproton-nucleus systems, is also present in antiproton-proton interactions.

## Method
The authors constructed a purely phenomenological optical potential with a Woods-Saxon shape, including real and imaginary strengths, radii, and a common diffuseness, plus a Coulomb potential from a spherical charge distribution. They fine-tuned the potential parameters to fit antiproton-proton annihilation cross section data from the Obelix Collaboration at laboratory momenta between 30 and 180 MeV/c, and then tested the same potential against elastic scattering data at higher momenta (181, 287, and 505 MeV/c) from earlier work by Bruckner et al. (1986). The potential was used to calculate the rho-parameter (ratio of real to imaginary part of the forward scattering amplitude) across the full momentum range and to compare charged and neutral projectile cross sections.

## Key numbers
- Real potential strength: -46 MeV; imaginary potential strength: -8000 MeV
- Real potential radius: 1.89 fm; imaginary potential radius: 0.41 fm; common diffuseness: 0.2 fm
- Coulomb charge distribution radius: 1.25 fm
- The real and imaginary parts of the scattering length alpha are both approximately 0.7 to 0.8 fm, with positive sign, giving rho(0) approximately -1
- The "inversion point" k_inv where S-wave dominance produces the inversion effect is approximately 200 MeV/c
- At 100 MeV/c, P-wave contributions are 1% of the total strong elastic cross section and 10% of the annihilation cross section
- With Coulomb effects included, the ratio sigma_el/sigma_a approaches approximately 1/6 as momentum goes to zero; without charge, sigma_el/sigma_a approaches 0
- The fit on annihilation data is very good below 300 MeV/c and good within 10% at 600 MeV/c

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological optical potential analysis of antiproton-proton interactions originally performed by Bruckner et al. (1986), who fitted elastic differential cross sections at 181, 287, and 505 MeV/c but left a wide range of uncertainty in the potential parameters. The authors use new low-momentum annihilation data from the Obelix Collaboration (down to 30 MeV/c) to constrain the potential more precisely. The paper connects this analysis to the broader puzzle of antiproton-nucleus annihilation cross sections, where the "inversion" effect (repulsion-dominated low-energy interaction) had been previously discussed by the same authors and by Wycech et al. in their analysis of antiprotonic deuterium. The results enable predictions of elastic cross sections and the rho-parameter in momentum regions where no experimental data yet exist, and provide a foundation for future energy-dependent optical potential studies of antiproton-nucleus systems.
