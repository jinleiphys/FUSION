# Infrared regularization with vector mesons and baryons

> We extend the method of infrared regularization to spin-1 fields coupled to baryons. As an application, we discuss the axial form factor of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper extends the method of infrared regularization to Feynman diagrams containing both baryons and spin-1 (vector) mesons, provided the vector mesons appear only as internal lines with small momenta far from their mass shell and the baryon line runs through the diagram undergoing only soft interactions.

## Method

The authors generalize the infrared regularization scheme of Becher and Leutwyler (1999) to loop integrals with three propagators: one pion, one nucleon, and one vector meson. They expand the loop integral in the small momentum transfer variable, extract the infrared singular part proportional to d-dependent powers of the pion mass from each coefficient, and resum the series. The method is applied to the triangle graph contributing to the axial form factor of the nucleon, using an explicit rho resonance field.

## Key numbers

* The fundamental scalar loop integral for pion-nucleon system, I_MB, has leading term (1/(16π))(M/m) as d→4, where M is the pion mass and m is the nucleon mass.
* For the vector meson-pion loop integral I_MV at zero external momentum, the infrared singular part is I_MV^IR(0) = (1/(M²-M_V²))∫(d^d l/(2π)^d)(i/(l²-M²)), where M_V is the vector meson mass.
* The ratio γ = M_V²/m² is approximately 2/3 for the rho resonance in the real world.
* The small parameters used are α = M²/m² ≪ 1, β = k²/m² ≪ 1, and γ = M_V²/m² ∼ O(1), where k is the momentum transfer.

## Context

This work extends the infrared regularization scheme of Becher and Leutwyler (1999) from the pion-nucleon sector to include explicit vector meson fields, building on the extension to meson resonances with pions by Bruns and Meißner (2004). The method enables systematic calculation of observables where vector and axial-vector mesons play important roles, such as the electroweak form factors of the nucleon, including the π-ρ loop contribution to strangeness form factors studied by Meißner (1997) and Hammer (1997). The approach complements the regularization scheme used in Mainz (2003) and can be applied to test resonance saturation of low-energy coefficients in chiral perturbation theory.
