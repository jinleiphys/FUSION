# Simultaneous description of $β$ decay and low-lying structure of neutron-rich even- and odd-mass Rh and Pd nuclei

> The low-energy structure and $β$ decay properties of neutron-rich even- and odd-mass Pd and Rh nuclei are studied using a mapping framework based on the nuclear density functional theory and the particle-boson coupling scheme. Constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using the Gogny-D1M energy density functional are performed to obtain microscopic inputs to determine the interacting-boson Hamiltonian employed to describe the even-even core Pd nuclei. The mean-field calculations also provide single-particle energies for the odd systems, which are used to determine essential ingredients of the particle-boson interactions for the odd-nucleon systems, and of the Gamow-Teller and Fermi transition operators. The potential energy surfaces obtained for even-even Pd isotopes as well as the spectroscopic properties for the even- and odd-mass systems suggest a transition from prolate deformed to $γ$-unstable and to nearly-spherical shapes. The predicted $β$ decay $\log{ft}$ values are shown to be sensitive to the details of the wave functions for the parent and daughter nuclei, and therefore serve as a stringent test of the employed theoretical approach.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a theoretical framework combining nuclear density functional theory (using the Gogny-D1M energy density functional) with the interacting boson-fermion model can simultaneously describe low-energy collective excitations and beta decay properties of neutron-rich Pd and Rh isotopes, but the predicted log ft values are sensitive to the details of the wave functions for parent and daughter nuclei, serving as a stringent test of the approach rather than a definitive prediction.

## Method

Constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations using the Gogny-D1M energy density functional were performed for even-even Pd isotopes to obtain potential energy surfaces, which were then mapped onto the interacting boson model-2 Hamiltonian. Single-particle energies and occupation probabilities from the same mean-field calculations were used to determine the particle-boson interactions for odd-mass systems and the Gamow-Teller and Fermi transition operators within the interacting boson-fermion-fermion model-2 framework.

## Key numbers

- The ratio R4/2 of 4+1 to 2+1 excitation energies in the mapped IBM-2 approach is 2.96 for 104Pd, 2.86 for 106Pd, and 2.69 for 108Pd, compared with experimental ratios of 2.38, 2.40, and 2.41 respectively
- The derived kappa values for the mapped IBM-2 are approximately 1.4 times larger in magnitude than for the phenomenological IBM-2
- The neutron number range studied is N = 58-78 for Pd isotopes, with neutron boson numbers N_nu varying from 2 to 8 for 104-124Pd
- The proton boson number is fixed at N_pi = 2 for all studied nuclei
- The empirical pairing gap of 12 A^{-1/2} was used in BCS calculations
- The free nucleon values g_V = 1 and g_A = 1.27 were used for beta decay calculations
- The numeric constant K = 6163 s was used in the ft value formula

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous method applied to the A approximately 130 region (Nomura 2020) and to As and Ge nuclei in the A approximately 70-80 region (Nomura 2022) using relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations. The study focuses on neutron-rich A approximately 100-120 nuclei that are relevant for r-process nucleosynthesis and potential neutrinoless double-beta decay candidates such as 110Pd and 110Cd. The results are compared with conventional phenomenological IBM calculations from Van Isacker (1980) and with experimental data from the RIBF facility at RIKEN and NSCL at MSU.
