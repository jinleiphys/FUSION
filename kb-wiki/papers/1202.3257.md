# Dimensional regularization applied to nuclear matter with a zero--range interaction

> We apply the dimensional regularization procedure to treat an ultraviolet divergence occurring in the framework of the nuclear many-body problem. We consider the second--order correction (beyond the mean-field approximation) to the equation of state of nuclear matter with a zero-range effective interaction. The unphysical ultraviolet divergence that is generated at second order by the zero range of the interaction is removed by the regularization technique and the regularized equation of state (mean-field + second-order contributions) is adjusted to a reference equation of state. The main practical advantage of this procedure, with respect to a cutoff regularization, is to provide a unique set of parameters for the adjusted effective interaction. This occurs because the regularized second-order correction does not contain any cutoff dependence. The encouraging results found in this work indicate that such an elegant technique to generate regularized effective interactions is likely to be applied in future to finite nuclei in the framework of beyond mean-field models.

## Key claim

Dimensional regularization can remove the ultraviolet divergence that appears in the second-order correction to the nuclear matter equation of state when a zero-range Skyrme interaction is used, but the procedure only yields a usable parameter set for the full Skyrme interaction (not the simplified t0-t3 model) and requires careful additional constraints to produce a physically reasonable symmetry energy.

## Method

The authors applied dimensional regularization to the second-order perturbative correction to the equation of state of nuclear matter, replacing the dimension of divergent integrals with a continuous variable d and introducing an auxiliary scale mu. After regularization, they performed a minimal subtraction and then fitted the regularized mean-field plus second-order equation of state to the SLy5 mean-field reference equation of state by minimizing a chi-squared over 15 density points between 0.02 and 0.30 fm^-3, keeping all nine Skyrme parameters free.

## Key numbers

- The regularized second-order correction for the t0-t3 model in symmetric matter yields a finite term proportional to (-11+2 ln 2)/105 times a density-dependent coefficient.
- For symmetric matter with the full Skyrme interaction, the refitted incompressibility at saturation density was 229.5 MeV (without symmetry energy constraint) and 228.5 MeV (with symmetry energy constraint).
- The symmetry energy obtained from the symmetric-matter-only fit without constraint was 1643.3 MeV, far from acceptable values; with constraint it became 32.03 MeV.
- For the simultaneous fit of symmetric and pure neutron matter, the incompressibility at saturation was 252.18 MeV and the symmetry energy was -7.5 MeV (unacceptable); adding a symmetry energy constraint gave 32.7 MeV for symmetry energy and 310.74 MeV for incompressibility.
- For the simultaneous fit of symmetric and asymmetric matter (delta=0.5), the incompressibility at saturation was 233.8 MeV and the symmetry energy was 24.9 MeV; with an additional symmetry energy constraint the values were 233.9 MeV and 32.00 MeV respectively.
- The chi-squared values for the symmetric matter fit were 3.5 x 10^-7 (without constraint) and 4.6 x 10^-3 (with constraint); for the symmetric-plus-asymmetric fit the chi-squared values were 1.337 and 1.341 respectively.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous cutoff-regularization studies (Moghrabi 2010, Moghrabi 2012) by applying dimensional regularization, a technique from quantum field theory (t'Hooft and Veltman 1972, Bollini and Giambiagi 1972), to the nuclear many-body problem with zero-range Skyrme interactions. The dimensional regularization had previously been applied to dilute Fermi systems in effective field theory (Hammer and Furnstahl 2000, Kaiser 2011). The key advantage over cutoff regularization is that dimensional regularization provides a unique set of parameters independent of a cutoff, whereas the earlier cutoff approach required a different parameter set for each cutoff value. The results open the possibility of constructing regularized Skyrme-type effective interactions for use in beyond-mean-field models of finite nuclei.
