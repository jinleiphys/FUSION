# Shell-model interactions from chiral effective field theory

> We construct valence-space Hamiltonians for use in shell-model calculations, where the residual two-body interaction is based on symmetry principles and the low-momentum expansion from chiral effective field theory. In addition to the usual free-space contact interactions, we also include novel center-of-mass--dependent operators that arise due to the Galilean invariance breaking by in-medium effects. We fitted the low-energy constants to 441 ground- and excited-state energies in the sd shell and obtained a root-mean-square derivation of 1.8 MeV at leading order and of 0.5 MeV at next-to-leading order, with natural low-energy constants in all cases. The developed chiral shell-model interactions enable order-by-order uncertainty estimates and show promising predictions for neutron-rich isotopes beyond the fitted data set.

## Key claim

The authors construct valence-space shell-model Hamiltonians where the residual two-body interaction is based on chiral effective field theory, including novel center-of-mass-dependent operators that arise from broken Galilean invariance in the medium, and they fit the low-energy constants to 441 ground- and excited-state energies in the sd shell, achieving a root-mean-square deviation of 0.5 MeV at next-to-leading order with natural low-energy constants in all cases.

## Method

The authors start from chiral effective field theory contact interactions at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO), including both standard free-space operators and new center-of-mass-dependent operators that account for the broken Galilean invariance in the valence space due to the presence of the core. They transform these momentum-space interactions to antisymmetrized two-body harmonic-oscillator matrix elements in the sd shell, using an oscillator length corresponding to hbar-omega = 13.53 MeV to reproduce the radius of the 16O core, and fit the low-energy constants to 441 experimental states using chi-squared minimization with the shell-model code ANTOINE.

## Key numbers

* Root-mean-square deviation from experiment for the full data set: 1.78 MeV at LO (2 LECs), 0.718 MeV at NLO (9 LECs), and 0.510 MeV at NLO_vs (14 LECs, including center-of-mass-dependent operators)
* The naive cutoff estimate from basis truncation in the sd shell is Lambda_naive approximately 300 MeV, with a more sophisticated estimate of Lambda_HO = 375 MeV
* The oscillator frequency is set to hbar-omega = 13.53 MeV to reproduce the radius of the 16O core
* The theoretical uncertainty for ground-state energies at NLO is calculated using Q = m_pi / Lambda_b with Lambda_b = 375 MeV
* The average splitting between sd-shell orbitals used for excitation energy uncertainty estimates is E^av_sd = 3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological universal sd-shell (USD) interactions of Brown and Richter (2006) by constructing valence-space Hamiltonians from chiral effective field theory, which enables order-by-order uncertainty estimates that phenomenological fits cannot provide. The inclusion of center-of-mass-dependent operators, which have been explored in the context of Fermi liquid theory by Schwenk and collaborators (2003), is novel for shell-model interactions and addresses the broken Galilean invariance in the valence space. The chiral shell-model interactions enable predictions for neutron-rich isotopes beyond the fitted data set and provide a framework for systematic improvement to higher orders in the chiral expansion.
