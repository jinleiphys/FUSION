# Nuclear Physics Around the Unitarity Limit

> We argue that many features of the structure of nuclei emerge from a strictly perturbative expansion around the unitarity limit, where the two-nucleon S waves have bound states at zero energy. In this limit, the gross features of states in the nuclear chart are correlated to only one dimensionful parameter, which is related to the breaking of scale invariance to a discrete scaling symmetry and set by the triton binding energy. Observables are moved to their physical values by small, perturbative corrections, much like in descriptions of the fine structure of atomic spectra. We provide evidence in favor of the conjecture that light, and possibly heavier, nuclei are bound weakly enough to be insensitive to the details of the interactions but strongly enough to be insensitive to the exact size of the two-nucleon system.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the gross features of nuclear structure emerge from a strictly perturbative expansion around the unitarity limit (where two-nucleon S-wave scattering lengths are infinite), with physical observables recovered by adding small, perturbative corrections, provided that the typical binding momentum Q_A of the A-nucleon system satisfies 1/a_{s,t} < Q_A < 1/R.

## Method

The authors use Pionless Effective Field Theory (EFT) with contact interactions between nonrelativistic nucleons, taking the unitarity limit (infinite scattering lengths in both S-wave channels) as the leading-order (LO) input. They treat the physical finite scattering lengths, Coulomb interactions, isospin-breaking effects, and range corrections as first-order perturbations (NLO) around this limit, solving the three- and four-nucleon systems using Faddeev and Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations with separable regulators.

## Key numbers

- Two-nucleon scattering lengths: a_t ≈ 5.4 fm (triplet), a_s ≈ -23.7 fm (singlet); deuteron binding energy B_D ≈ 2.224 MeV; virtual state binding B_{NN*} ≈ 0.068 MeV
- Triton binding energy used to fix the three-body parameter Λ_*; at full unitarity LO, Λ_* = 234 MeV (compared to 175 MeV for standard Pionless EFT and 168 MeV for singlet-only unitarity)
- Three-nucleon binding momenta Q_3 ≈ 70 MeV, satisfying 1/a_{s,t} ≲ 45 MeV < Q_3 < 140 MeV (breakdown scale)
- Four-boson unitarity ratios: B_4/B_3 ≈ 4.611 (ground state), B_{4*}/B_3 ≈ 1.002 (excited state)
- For the alpha particle at unitarity LO: (B_α/B_T)^LO = 4.66 (ground state), (B_{α*}/B_T)^LO = 1.002 (excited state)
- At NLO with zero effective ranges: (B_α)^{NLO(r=0)} = 29.5 ± 8.7 MeV; ratio (B_α/B_T)^{NLO(r=0)} ≈ 3.48 compared to experimental 3.34
- Triton-helion splitting at NLO: (B_T - B_H)^{NLO} ≈ 0.92 ± 0.18 MeV, compared to experimental 0.764 MeV
- Discrete scaling factor exp(2π/s_0) ≈ 515, with s_0 ≈ 1.00624

## Context

This work extends the qualitative discussion of nuclear proximity to the unitarity limit by providing quantitative evidence for A ≤ 4 systems, building on the Pionless EFT framework of Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1998-1999) and the three-body calculations of König et al. (2015). It contrasts with traditional nuclear force constructions that treat finite scattering lengths nonperturbatively at leading order, and with potential-model studies by Kievsky et al. (2015) that map correlations as the unitarity limit is approached. The results enable systematic perturbative calculations for heavier nuclei (e.g., 16O, 6Li) and nuclear matter, and suggest connections to atomic and molecular physics near Feshbach resonances.
