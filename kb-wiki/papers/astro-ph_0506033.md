# Rates of Neutrino Absorption on Nucleons and the Reverse Processes in Strong Magnetic Fields

> The rates of electron neutrino capture on neutron, electron anti-neutrino capture on proton, and their reverse processes are important for understanding the production of heavy elements in the supernova environment above the protoneutron star. Observations and theoretical considerations suggest that some protoneutron stars may be born with strong magnetic fields. We develop a numerical method to calculate the above rates in supernova environments with magnetic fields up to 10^16 G. This method is accurate to the order of one over nucleon mass. We show that our results have the correct behavior in the limit of high neutrino energy or small magnetic field. Based on comparison of our results with various approximations, we recommend efficient estimates of the above rates for use in models of supernova nucleosynthesis in the presence of strong magnetic fields.

## Key claim

The paper develops a numerical method to calculate neutrino absorption and emission rates on nucleons in supernova environments with magnetic fields up to 10^16 G, accurate to order 1/m_N (including nucleon recoil and weak magnetism), which is required for the ~1% accuracy needed to determine the neutron-to-proton ratio for heavy element nucleosynthesis.

## Method

The authors use Landau wave functions for electrons, positrons, and protons in a uniform magnetic field, combined with the effective four-fermion Lagrangian for weak interactions including nucleon form factors f=1, g=1.26, and f2=3.7 for weak magnetism. They derive cross sections for neutrino absorption (ν_e + n → e^- + p and anti-ν_e + p → e^+ + n) and differential reaction rates for the reverse emission processes, averaging over thermal distributions of nucleons and e± with appropriate degeneracy factors for Landau levels.

## Key numbers

- Energy scale of magnetic field: sqrt(eB) = 7.69 (B/10^16 G)^(1/2) MeV
- Magnetic field considered: B ~ 10^16 G, with upper limit B ~ 10^18 G from gravitational binding energy
- Nucleon spin polarization at B ~ 10^16 G and T ~ 1 MeV: χ = μB/T = 3.15 × 10^(-2) (μ/μ_N) (B/10^16 G) (MeV/T)
- Proton Landau level spacing and anomalous magnetic moment contribution at B ~ 10^16 G: eB/m_p ~ μ̃_p B ~ 60 keV
- Typical neutrino energy: E_ν ~ 10 MeV; typical temperature: T ~ 1 MeV
- Neutrino absorption rate per nucleon: λ_νN = 49.7 (L_ν/10^51 erg s^(-1)) (10 MeV/⟨E_ν⟩) (⟨σ_νN⟩/10^(-41) cm^2) (10 km/R)^2 s^(-1)
- Cross section to O(1) in zero magnetic field: σ_νN^(0) = (G_F^2 cos^2 θ_C / π) E_e^(0) sqrt((E_e^(0))^2 - m_e^2)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation (Duan 2004) that assumed infinite nucleon mass (O(1) accuracy) by including O(1/m_N) corrections from nucleon recoil and weak magnetism, which were known to be important for supernova conditions from Horowitz (2001). Prior calculations by Leinson (1998), Roulet (1998), Lai (1998), Arras (1998), Gvozdev (1999), Chandra (2002), Bhattacharya (2002), and Shinkevich (2004) also used various approximations without full O(1/m_N) corrections. The results enable accurate modeling of the neutron-to-proton ratio in supernova nucleosynthesis for protoneutron stars with strong magnetic fields, building on the framework of Bethe (1985) for supernova dynamics and Qian (1996) for nucleosynthesis conditions.
