# Hypernuclei and in-medium chiral dynamics

> A recently introduced relativistic nuclear energy density functional, constrained by features of low-energy QCD, is extended to describe the structure of hypernuclei. The density-dependent mean field and the spin-orbit potential of a $Λ$-hyperon in a nucleus, are consistently calculated using the SU(3) extension of in-medium chiral perturbation theory. The leading long-range $ΛN$ interaction arises from kaon-exchange and $2π$-exchange with a $Σ$-hyperon in the intermediate state. Scalar and vector mean fields, originating from in-medium changes of the quark condensates, produce a sizeable {\em short-range} spin-orbit interaction. The model, when applied to oxygen as a test case, provides a natural explanation for the smallness of the effective $Λ$ spin-orbit potential: an almost complete cancellation between the background contributions (scalar and vector) and the long-range terms generated by two-pion exchange.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the extremely small spin-orbit splitting observed in Λ-hypernuclei arises from an almost complete cancellation between the short-range spin-orbit force from scalar and vector background mean-fields (originating from in-medium changes of quark condensates) and a long-range, "wrong-sign" spin-orbit term generated by two-pion exchange with an intermediate Σ-hyperon, a mechanism that is model-independent within SU(3) chiral dynamics.

## Method

The authors extend a relativistic nuclear energy density functional, previously constrained by low-energy QCD features, to include a single Λ-hyperon. The density-dependent Λ-nucleon couplings are calculated using the SU(3) extension of in-medium chiral perturbation theory, incorporating kaon-exchange Fock terms and two-pion exchange with a Σ-hyperon in intermediate states, including Pauli blocking effects. The resulting coupled Dirac equations for nucleons and the Λ-hyperon are solved self-consistently for the test case of ¹⁷ΛO, and the chiral SU(3) spin-orbit correction from two-pion exchange is evaluated in first-order perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- The empirical depth of the Λ central nuclear potential is VcΛ ≃ 32 ± 2 MeV, about 2/3 of the nucleon potential depth.
- The empirical Λ-nucleus spin-orbit strength is VlsΛ ≃ 4 ± 2 MeV, an order of magnitude smaller than the nucleon value (VlsN ∼ 20 MeV).
- The observed spin-orbit splitting between (p1/2)Λ and (p3/2)Λ orbits in ¹³ΛC is E(1/2⁻) – E(3/2⁻) = 152 ± 54 ± 36 keV.
- The strength of the model-independent Λ spin-orbit term from chiral SU(3) two-pion exchange is UlsΛ(kf(0)) ≃ –15 MeV·fm² at kf(0) ≃ 1.36 fm⁻¹, opposite in sign to the nucleon spin-orbit interaction.
- The Σ–Λ mass splitting is MΣ – MΛ = 77.5 MeV.
- The calculated Λ s-state energy in ¹⁷ΛO is slightly too deep compared to the empirical value for ¹⁶ΛO: εΛs = –12.42 ± 0.05 MeV.
- For a reduction factor χ in the range 0.4–0.5 (from QCD sum rules), the calculated spin-orbit splitting δΛ in ¹⁷ΛO falls within the empirical estimate of –0.8 MeV ≤ δΛ ≤ 0.2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the in-medium chiral effective field theory approach previously applied to nuclear matter and finite nuclei by Kaiser (2001), Fritsch (2004), and Finelli (2003, 2005) to include the strangeness degree of freedom, building on the calculation of the Λ self-energy in nuclear matter by Kaiser (2004). The model provides a microscopic explanation for the long-standing puzzle of the weak Λ spin-orbit force, which had been treated phenomenologically by Bando (1990) and measured with high precision by the E929 BNL experiment (Kohri, 2001). The framework enables systematic calculations for heavier hypernuclei and clarifies why the analogous cancellation does not occur for nucleons, where three-body spin-orbit forces from Δ-isobar excitations (Kaiser, 2003) neutralize the effect.
