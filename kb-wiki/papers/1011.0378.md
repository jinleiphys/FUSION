# Predictions of hadronic observables in Pb+Pb collisions at \surd sNN = 2.76 TeV from a hadronic rescattering model

> A kinematic model based on the superposition of p+p collisions, relativistic geometry and final-state hadronic rescattering is used to predict various hadronic observables in \surd sNN = 2.76 TeV Pb+Pb collisions. Predictions for rapidity and transverse momentum distributions, elliptic flow, and two-boson femtoscopy are presented. A short proper time for hadronization is assumed. Previous calculations using this model which were performed for \surd sNN = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions were shown to describe reasonably well the trends of observables in experiments carried out at that energy, giving the present predictions for Pb+Pb at higher energy some degree of credibility.

## Key claim

The paper presents predictions for hadronic observables in Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV using a model that assumes only hadronic degrees of freedom and a short proper time for hadronization (τ = 0.1 fm/c), explicitly labeling these as "limiting case scenario" predictions that would be contradicted if exotic phenomena (such as partonic degrees of freedom) are present.

## Method

The model superposes PYTHIA-generated p+p collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV within the collision geometry of Pb nuclei, with hadronization occurring at a fixed proper time τ = 0.1 fm/c from a thin uniform disk of radius 1 fm. Final-state hadronic rescattering is simulated via a semi-classical Monte Carlo calculation with isospin-averaged cross sections for π, K, N, Λ, and resonances (ρ, ω, η, η′, φ, Δ, K*), using binary collisions and relativistic kinematics. A 3200-event minimum-bias run was generated with impact parameters following dσ/db ∝ b, and observables were calculated with appropriate centrality and kinematic cuts.

## Key numbers

- Mid-rapidity dn/dη for charged particles in 0–5% centrality Pb+Pb collisions: 1174 (minimum bias: 323)
- The 0–5% centrality charged particle pT distribution for pT > 5 GeV/c is predicted to be about two orders of magnitude larger at LHC compared with RHIC
- Elliptic flow V2 for minimum bias peaks at about 0.14 near pT = 2.5 GeV/c
- Two-pion HBT Rlong for 0–5% centrality at ⟨kT⟩ = 0.15 GeV/c: 8.25 fm; at ⟨kT⟩ = 0.76 GeV/c: 2.53 fm
- Two-pion HBT Rside for 0–5% centrality at ⟨kT⟩ = 0.15 GeV/c: 5.33 fm; at ⟨kT⟩ = 0.76 GeV/c: 3.63 fm
- Two-pion HBT Rout for 0–5% centrality at ⟨kT⟩ = 0.15 GeV/c: 6.40 fm; at ⟨kT⟩ = 0.76 GeV/c: 3.67 fm
- Two-kaon HBT radius parameters for 0–5% centrality with 0.10 < kT < 1.00 GeV/c (⟨kT⟩ = 0.39 GeV/c): Rside = 3.60 ± 0.24 fm, Rout = 4.47 ± 0.16 fm, Rlong = 4.51 ± 0.35 fm
- Two-pion HBT Rlong predicted to be about 25% larger than in RHIC Au+Au collisions

## Context

This work extends the author's previous hadronic rescattering model calculations for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV (Humanic, 2008) to the higher LHC energy of √sNN = 2.76 TeV, where the earlier model described the trends of RHIC observables reasonably well. The predictions are intended as a baseline for upcoming LHC Pb+Pb data, with the explicit caveat that disagreement would indicate the presence of partonic degrees of freedom or other exotic phenomena. The model uses PYTHIA for p+p generation and a hadronization picture similar to that applied to e+e- collisions by Csorgo and collaborators.
