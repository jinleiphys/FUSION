# Comparison of Form Factors Calculated with Different Expressions for the Boost Transformation

> The effect of different boost expressions is considered for the calculation of the ground-state form factor of a two-body system made of scalar particles interacting via the exchange of a scalar boson. The aim is to provide an uncertainty range on methods employed in implementing these effects as well as an insight on their relevance when an ``exact'' calculation is possible. Using a wave function corresponding to a mass operator that has the appropriate properties to construct the generators of the Poincaré algebra in the framework of relativistic quantum mechanics, form factors are calculated using the boost transformations pertinent to the instant, front and point forms of this approach. Moderately and strongly bound systems are considered with masses of the exchanged boson taken as zero, 0.15 times the constituent mass $m$, and infinity. In the first and last cases, a comparison with ``exact'' calculations is made (Wick-Cutkosky model and Feynman triangle diagram). Results with a Galilean boost are also given. Momentum transfers up to $Q^2=100 m^2$ are considered. Emphasis is put on the contribution of the single-particle current, as usually done. It is found that the present point-form calculations of form factors strongly deviate from all the other ones, requiring large contributions from two-body currents. Different implementations of the point-form approach, where the role of these two-body currents would be less important, are sketched.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the point-form approach to calculating form factors of a two-body scalar system, using only the single-particle current, produces results that strongly deviate from both "exact" field-theory calculations and from instant-form and front-form results, requiring large contributions from two-body currents that are not needed in the other forms.

## Method

The authors construct a mass operator for a two-body system of scalar particles exchanging a scalar boson, using the Bakamjian-Thomas construction to ensure Poincaré invariance. They then calculate ground-state form factors using boost transformations appropriate to the instant, front, and point forms of relativistic quantum mechanics, comparing these results to "exact" calculations from the Wick-Cutkosky model (for zero-mass exchanged boson) and the Feynman triangle diagram (for infinite-mass exchanged boson). The calculations are performed for moderately bound systems (total mass M approximately 1.6 m) and strongly bound systems (M = 0.1 m), with exchanged boson masses of zero, 0.15 times the constituent mass m, and infinity.

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons between relativistic quantum mechanics approaches and field-theory calculations, notably the work by Coester and Ostebee (1975) on deuteron form factors and by Glöckle and Hamme (1992) on Lorentz-contraction effects. The study directly addresses the surprising results from Klink (1998) and subsequent applications by Allen (2000) and Wagenbrunn (2000), where point-form calculations showed an increased charge radius and rapid fall-off compared to expectations. The paper enables a systematic understanding of how different boost implementations affect form factor calculations and clarifies the role of two-body currents in restoring equivalence between different forms of relativistic quantum mechanics.
