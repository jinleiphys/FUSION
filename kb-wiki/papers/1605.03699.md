# Three nucleon forces in nuclear matter in the QCD sum rules

> We calculate the single-particle nucleon characteristics in symmetric nuclear matter with inclusion of the 3N interactions. The contribution of the 3N forces to the nucleon self energies are expressed in terms of the nonlocal scalar condensate (d=3) and of the configuration of the two four-quark condensates (d=6) in which two diquark operators act on two different nucleons of the matter. The most important part of the contribution of the four-quark condensate is calculated in a model-independent way. We employed a relativistic quark model of nucleon for calculation of the rest part. The density dependence of the vector and scalar nucleon self energies and of the single-particle potential energy are obtained.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the contribution of three-nucleon (3N) forces to nucleon self-energies in symmetric nuclear matter using QCD sum rules, finding that at saturation density the 3N forces reduce the effective mass by about 25 MeV and subtract 37 MeV from the vector self-energy, with the most important contributions expressed in terms of the observable pion-nucleon sigma term rather than requiring free parameters.

## Method

The authors extend the finite-density QCD sum rules approach beyond the gas approximation by including in-medium condensates that involve two nucleons of the matter simultaneously. The scalar condensate beyond the gas approximation is obtained from Chiral Perturbation Theory calculations of the pion cloud, while the four-quark condensate contributions where two diquark operators act on two different nucleons are computed partly in a model-independent way (scalar-scalar, vector-vector, and vector-scalar channels) and partly using a relativistic quark model of the nucleon (axial, tensor, and axial-tensor channels). The sum rules equations are then solved to extract the vector and scalar nucleon self-energies and the single-particle potential energy.

## Key numbers

- At saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, the 3N forces diminish the effective mass m* by about 25 MeV (with cutoff μ = 882 MeV)
- The 3N forces subtract 37 MeV from the vector self-energy Σ_V at ρ₀ (uncertainty about 1 MeV from cutoff variation)
- The effective mass reduction varies between 45 MeV and 13 MeV as the cutoff μ varies between 600 and 1200 MeV
- The 4N interactions are expected to add about 100 MeV to m* and provide only small change to Σ_V
- The scalar nucleon matrix element κ_N ≈ 8 for σ_N = 45 MeV
- The relativistic quark model parameters: R = 0.6 fm, β = 0.39, giving J_A = 0.75 and J_T = 0.88
- The dimensionless gas approximation parameters for symmetric matter: A_g = -0.10, B_g = 1.80, C_g = -0.88

## Context

This work extends the finite-density QCD sum rules approach beyond the gas approximation used in earlier studies (reviewed in Drukarev and Ryskin, 2010), where each condensate was treated as the sum of vacuum value plus nucleon density times the nucleon expectation value, corresponding to only two-nucleon forces. The nonlinear scalar condensate contribution is extracted from Chiral Perturbation Theory calculations by Kaiser et al. (2007) that included both 2N and 3N forces between matter nucleons. The results enable future calculations of nuclear matter properties with explicit three-nucleon forces expressed in terms of QCD condensates and the observable sigma term, and provide estimates for the magnitude of four-nucleon force contributions.
