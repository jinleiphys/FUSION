# Diabatic Mean-Field Description of Rotational Bands in Terms of the Selfconsistent Collective Coordinate Method

> Diabatic description of rotational bands provides a clear-cut picture for understanding the back-bending phenomena, where the internal structure of the yrast band changes dramatically as a function of angular momentum. A microscopic framework to obtain the diabatic bands within the mean-field approximation is presented by making use of the selfconsistent collective coordinate method. Applying the framework, both the ground state rotational bands and the Stockholm bands are studied systematically for the rare-earth deformed nuclei. An overall agreement has been achieved between the calculated and observed rotational spectra. It is also shown that the inclusion of the double-stretched quadrupole-pairing interaction is crucial to obtain an overall agreement for the even-odd mass differences and the rotational spectra simultaneously.

# Diabatic Mean-Field Description of Rotational Bands in Terms of the Selfconsistent Collective Coordinate Method

## Key claim

The selfconsistent collective coordinate (SCC) method can provide reliable diabatic rotational bands within the mean-field approximation, eliminating the unphysical mixing of bands at the same rotational frequency that occurs in the conventional adiabatic cranking model. The inclusion of the double-stretched quadrupole-pairing interaction is crucial to simultaneously reproduce even-odd mass differences and rotational spectra, while non-stretched and single-stretched forms fail to achieve comparable agreement.

## Method

The authors apply the SCC method to construct diabatic rotational bands by solving the selfconsistent cranking model equations using a rotational frequency expansion rather than an angular momentum expansion. The residual interaction is of pairing-plus-quadrupole type, with the quadrupole-pairing interaction tested in three variants (non-stretched, single-stretched, and double-stretched) and the particle-hole quadrupole interaction taken in the double-stretched form. Parameters for the monopole-pairing strength (controlled by a smoothed pairing gap parameter d) and quadrupole-pairing strength (parameter g₂) are determined by minimizing root-mean-square deviations of neutron even-odd mass differences and Harris moments of inertia for 83 rare-earth nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit parameters for the double-stretched quadrupole-pairing interaction: d = 14 MeV and g₂ = 30, yielding root-mean-square deviations X_rms(E_ν^(e-o), J₀) = (0.115, 0.136)
- For comparison, the non-stretched case with d = 12 MeV and g₂ = 0 gives X_rms(E_ν^(e-o), J₀) = (0.265, 0.204); the single-stretched case with d = 12 MeV and g₂ = 16 gives (0.192, 0.214)
- The monopole-pairing force strength is determined via the smoothed pairing gap formula with cutoff energy Λ = 1.2 ħω₀, where ħω₀ = 41.0/A^(1/3) MeV
- The selfconsistent quadrupole force strength is κ₂^self = (4π/3)(ħω₀)/(A R₀² b₀²) with R₀ = 1.2 A^(1/3) fm and b₀² = ħ/(Mω₀)
- The double-stretched quadrupole-pairing interaction with the same parameters (d = 14 MeV, g₂ = 30) applied to the actinide region reproduces well-deformed nuclei results, though light actinides with N ≈ 88-90 show discrepancies suggesting octupole correlations are important

## Context

This work extends the SCC method previously applied to ground-state rotational bands (g-bands) by Matsuyanagi, Terasaki, and collaborators (TMS91, Tera92, Tera94), who used angular momentum expansion and RPA normal mode analysis. The present study instead uses rotational frequency expansion, deriving the diabatic cranking model that connects to the cranked shell model approach of Bengtsson and Frauendorf (1979). The paper systematically tests three forms of quadrupole-pairing interaction (non-stretched, single-stretched, double-stretched) following earlier work by Hamamoto (1974), Diebel (1984), and Garrett (1982), and demonstrates that only the double-stretched form, consistent with the selfconsistent condition that ⟨Q₂K⟩ = 0 at equilibrium, can simultaneously fit even-odd mass differences and moments of inertia. The results enable systematic construction of diabatic g-bands and Stockholm bands (s-bands) for rare-earth deformed nuclei, allowing comparison with experimental rotational spectra across the region.
