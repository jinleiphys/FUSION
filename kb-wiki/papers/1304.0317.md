# Four-body structure of neutron-rich hypernucleus $^6_Î›$H

> The structure of heavy hyperhydrogen $^6_Î›$H is studied within the framework of a $tnnÎ›$ four-body cluster model. Interactions among the constituent subunits are determined so as to reproduce reasonably well the observed low-energy properties of the $tn, tÎ›$ and $tnn$ subsystems. # Four-body structure of neutron-rich hypernucleus $^6_\Lambda$H

## Key claim

The ground state of $^6_\Lambda$H is obtained as a resonant state, not a bound state, when the energy and width of the core nucleus $^5$H are reproduced within the experimental error bar; a bound state of $^6_\Lambda$H can only be generated if the $^5$H resonance energy is artificially lowered below the lower edge of the observed error band.

## Method

The authors employed a $tnn\Lambda$ four-body cluster model using the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) with nine sets of Jacobian coordinates. The $^5$H core nucleus was treated as a $tnn$ three-body system using the complex scaling method (CSM) to handle its broad resonant state, while the $^6_\Lambda$H ground state was analyzed using the stabilization (real-scaling) method. Interactions among subunits were determined by reproducing low-energy properties of the $tn$, $t\Lambda$, and $tnn$ subsystems, with a phenomenological $tnn$ three-body force introduced to match the observed $^5$H resonance.

## Key numbers

- $^5$H ground state resonance: $E_r = 1.7 \pm 0.3$ MeV, $\Gamma = 1.9 \pm 0.4$ MeV (experimental data, with respect to $tnn$ three-body breakup threshold)
- With $V_{3b} = -45$ MeV: $^5$H resonance at $E_r = 1.60$ MeV, $\Gamma = 2.44$ MeV; $^6_\Lambda$H ground state at $E = -0.87$ MeV ($\Gamma = 0.23$ MeV), $B_\Lambda = 2.47$ MeV
- With $V_{3b} = -65$ MeV: $^5$H resonance at $E_r = 1.17$ MeV, $\Gamma = 0.91$ MeV; $^6_\Lambda$H bound state at $E = -2.07$ MeV, $B_\Lambda = 3.24$ MeV (consistent with FINUDA data $B_\Lambda = 4.0 \pm 1.1$ MeV within error)
- With $V_{3b} = -73$ MeV: $^5$H resonance at $E_r = 0.73$ MeV, $\Gamma = 0.50$ MeV; $^6_\Lambda$H bound state at $E = -3.02$ MeV, $B_\Lambda = 3.75$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron-rich hypernuclei by Hiyama et al. (2009) on $^6_\Lambda$He, $^7_\Lambda$He, $^7_\Lambda$Li, and $^7_\Lambda$Be using $\alpha$ cluster models, and responds to the FINUDA observation of $^6_\Lambda$H as a bound state with $B_\Lambda = 4.0 \pm 1.1$ MeV. The results contradict earlier shell-model predictions by Dalitz (reported $B_\Lambda = 4.2$ MeV) and Millener ($B_\Lambda = 4.28 \pm 0.10$ MeV), as well as the larger binding energy of 5.8 MeV from Akaishi et al., because those calculations did not explicitly treat $^5$H as a broad three-body resonant state. The work enables future coupled-channel calculations including $tnn\Lambda$ and $t(^3\text{He})NN\Sigma$ channels to determine the role of $\Lambda N-\Sigma N$ coupling, and provides a baseline for interpreting the ongoing J-PARC E-10 experiment searching for $^6_\Lambda$H.
