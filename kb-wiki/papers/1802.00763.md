# Photon or meson formation in $J/ψ$ decays into $p {\overline{p}}$

> The measurements of the $J/ψ\rightarrow γp \overline{p}$ decays by the BES Collaboration indicate an enhancement at the $p \overline{p}$ threshold which, however, is not present in the J/$ψ$ decays into $ωp \overline{p}$ and into $πp \overline{p}$. Here, two processes for describing the decays $J/ψ\rightarrow \mathcal{B} p \overline{p}$ where $\mathcal{B} = γ, ω$ are presented in some detail and the cases $\mathcal{B} =ϕ, π$ are briefly touched on. The first one, applied not only to the radiative decay to reproduce the threshold peak but also to the $ωp \overline{p}$ decay channel to improve the description of the spectrum, postulates a direct emission of the boson before the baryon pair is formed. The second process assumes that the boson $\mathcal{B}$ is emitted from the baryon pair following the $J/ψ$ decay and includes for the decays into $γp \overline{p}$ a final state nucleon-antinucleon interaction based on the Paris $N \overline{N}$ potential. The reproduction of the $p \overline{p}$ distribution in the $J/ψ\rightarrow ωp \overline{p}$ decays needs a final state interaction involving a $N(2050)\ 3/2^-$ resonance. The photon- and meson-emission rates are reproduced in a semi-quantitative way.

## Key claim

The near-threshold enhancement in the $J/\psi \to \gamma p \overline{p}$ decay observed by BES can be reproduced by a model combining two processes: direct photon emission before baryon formation (which generates the threshold peak via final-state interactions) and photon emission from the baryon currents after the $p\overline{p}$ pair is formed; the absence of a similar threshold peak in $J/\psi \to \omega p \overline{p}$ and $J/\psi \to \pi p \overline{p}$ decays is explained by the dominance of different partial waves and the need for a $N(2050)\ 3/2^-$ resonance in the $\omega$ channel.

## Method

The authors developed two complementary mechanisms for $J/\psi \to \mathcal{B} p \overline{p}$ decays ($\mathcal{B} = \gamma, \omega, \phi, \pi$). The first mechanism (direct emission) assumes the boson is emitted before the baryon pair forms, with the $p\overline{p}$ final-state interaction described by the Paris $N\overline{N}$ potential; this generates the threshold peak in the radiative decay. The second mechanism (baryon current emission) assumes the boson is emitted from the baryon pair after $J/\psi$ decay into a $^3S_1$ $N\overline{N}$ state, with the source function parametrized as a Gaussian of radius $R_0$. For the $\omega p \overline{p}$ channel, the $p\overline{p}$ spectrum required inclusion of a $N(2050)\ 3/2^-$ resonance in the final-state interaction.

## Key numbers

- Source radius $R_0 = 0.28$ fm for the initial $N\overline{N}$ pair formation
- Paris potential generates a quasi-bound $^{11}S_0$ state at 4.8 MeV below threshold with 52 MeV width
- Experimental branching fraction for $J/\psi \to p \overline{p} \gamma$: $3.8(1.0) \times 10^{-4}$
- Experimental branching fraction for $J/\psi \to p \overline{p} \omega$: $9.8(1.0) \times 10^{-4}$
- Experimental branching fraction for $J/\psi \to p \overline{p} \pi^0$: $1.19(0.08) \times 10^{-3}$
- Experimental branching fraction for $J/\psi \to p \overline{p} \phi$: $5.23(0.34) \times 10^{-5}$
- Experimental branching fraction for $J/\psi \to p \overline{p}$: $2.120(0.029) \times 10^{-3}$
- Direct emission model produces a broad peak at $M_{p\overline{p}} \simeq 2130$ MeV and a narrow threshold peak
- Parameter $\beta = 0.175$ fm$^{3/2}$ gives best fit to the $M_{p\overline{p}}$ spectrum data
- The limiting source radius $R_f$ varies from 0.28 fm to approximately 0.61 fm as $M_{p\overline{p}}$ varies from 2.90 to 2.60 GeV/$c^2$

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier model (Loiseau and Wycech, 2005) that first suggested the $^{11}S_0$ partial wave dominance in $J/\psi \to \gamma p \overline{p}$ decays and the role of a quasi-bound $N\overline{N}$ state. It contrasts with the Bonn-Julich group results (Haidenbauer et al., 2006) whose potential does not generate a bound state in the $^1S_0$ wave, and with later chiral effective field theory work (Kang et al., 2015) that claims an isospin-1 bound state. The model enables interpretation of the $X(1835)$ meson observed by BES in $J/\psi \to \gamma \pi^+ \pi^- \eta'$ as arising from the same $^{11}S_0$ quasi-bound $N\overline{N}$ state, and provides a framework for understanding the puzzling absence of threshold enhancement in $\psi' \to \gamma p \overline{p}$ decays.
