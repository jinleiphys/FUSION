# The Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model: A brief review and some recent results

> A brief review of the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model is given, which is a top-down holographic model of low-energy QCD with chiral quarks derived from type-IIA superstring theory. The main predictions of the model, in particular concerning meson spectra, the gluon condensate, the QCD string tension, the mass of the $η'$ and of baryons are discussed and compared quantitatively with available experimental and/or lattice results. Then some recent results of potential interest to the physics program at the future FAIR facility are presented: The spectrum of glueballs and their decay rates into pions, and the phase diagram of QCD at finite temperature, density, and magnetic field strength.

## Key claim

The Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, a top-down holographic model of low-energy QCD derived from type-IIA superstring theory, reproduces many qualitative and often quantitative features of real QCD despite having almost no free parameters, but its predictions are limited to the regime of large 't Hooft coupling and large number of colors, and corrections needed to approach real QCD at finite N_c and λ are largely unknown.

## Method

The model starts from type-IIA supergravity dual to a 5-dimensional supersymmetric gauge theory, then performs dimensional reduction on a circle with supersymmetry-breaking boundary conditions to obtain nonsupersymmetric pure-glue Yang-Mills theory in 3+1 dimensions. Chiral quarks are introduced by adding probe D8 and anti-D8 branes that intersect the color D4-branes, with the global flavor symmetry U(N_f)_L × U(N_f)_R realized as a local gauge symmetry on the flavor branes that is spontaneously broken to the diagonal subgroup when the branes join in the bulk. The low-energy effective action on the D8-branes yields a chiral Lagrangian for Nambu-Goldstone bosons, vector and axial vector mesons, the Wess-Zumino-Witten term, and a Skyrme term, with only two free parameters: a Kaluza-Klein mass scale M_KK and the 't Hooft coupling λ at that scale.

## Key numbers

- M_KK = 949 MeV, κ = 7.45 × 10^{-3}, and λ̄ ≡ g²N_c/2 ≈ 16.63, obtained by fitting m_ρ ≈ 776 MeV and f_π ≈ 92.4 MeV
- String tension: √σ = 0.6262 M_KK ≈ 594 MeV (from the standard parameter set)
- Gluon condensate: C⁴ = 0.0126 GeV⁴ (with λ̄ = 16.63 and N_c = 3), coinciding with the SVZ sum rule value of 0.012 GeV⁴
- ρ meson mass: m_ρ² = λ₁ M_KK² with λ₁ = 0.669; a₁(1260) mass ratio: m²_{a₁}/m²_ρ ≈ 2.344 (experimental ≈ 2.52); ρ(1450) mass ratio: m²_{ρ(1450)}/m²_ρ ≈ 4.29 (experimental ≈ 3.57)
- η' mass: m_{η'} = (1/(3√3 π)) √(N_f/N_c) (g²N_c/2) M_KK
- Baryon mass (leading order): m_{D4} = 8π²κ M_KK = (1/(27π)) (g²N_c/2) N_c M_KK; with correction: M_B = [1/(27π)(g²N_c/2) + √(2/15)] N_c M_KK ≈ 1598 MeV (standard parameters)
- Skyrme model baryon mass: M_N ≈ 920 MeV (from effective Skyrme action with standard parameters)
- m_ρ/√σ ≈ 1.306 (standard parameters); large-N_c lattice gives 1.504(50)
- Alternative fit to lattice m_ρ/√σ gives λ̄ ≈ 12.55(80), √σ ≈ 515 MeV, α_s(M_KK) ≈ 0.66, f_π ≈ 80.3 MeV

## Context

This paper reviews the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, which extends Witten's 1998 model of low-energy QCD through type-IIA supergravity (Witten, 1998) by adding flavor D8-branes as proposed by Sakai and Sugimoto (2004, 2005). The model provides a top-down holographic approach that contrasts with bottom-up phenomenological models and offers predictions for meson spectra, glueballs, baryons, and the QCD phase diagram that can be compared with lattice results such as those from Lucini (2010) for glueballs and Bali (2013) for mesons at large N_c. The results are of potential interest to the physics program at the future FAIR facility, particularly for glueball decay rates into pions and the phase diagram at finite temperature, density, and magnetic field strength, where lattice QCD remains unreliable.
