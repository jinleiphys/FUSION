# Spin-Isospin Properties of $N=Z$ Odd-Odd Nuclei from a Core+$pn$ Three-Body Model including Core Excitations

> For $N=Z$ odd-odd nuclei, a three-body model assuming two valence particles and an inert core can provide an understanding of pairing correlations in the ground state and spin-isospin excitations. However, since residual core-nucleon interactions can have a significant impact on these quantities, the inclusion of core excitations in the model is essential for useful calculation to be performed. The effect of core excitations must be included in order to gain a detailed understanding of both the ground state and spin-isospin properties of these systems. To this end, we include the vibrational excitation of the core nucleus in our model. We solve the three-body core-nucleon-nucleon problem including core vibrational states to obtain the nuclear ground state as well as spin-isospin excitations. The spin-isospin excitations are examined from the point of view of SU(4) multiplets. By including the effect of core excitation, several experimental quantities of $N=Z$ odd-odd nuclei are better described, and the root mean square distances between proton and neutron and that between the center of mass of proton and neutron and core nucleus increase. Large $B$($M1$) and $B$(GT) observed for $^{18}$F and $^{40}$Ca were explained in terms of the SU(4) symmetry. The core nucleus is meaningfully broken by the residual core-nucleon interactions, and various quantities concerning spin-isospin excitations as well as the ground state become consistent with experimental data. Including the core excitation in the three-body model is thus important for a more detailed understanding of nuclear structure.

## Key claim

By including core vibrational excitations in a core+pn three-body model, the description of ground-state and spin-isospin properties of N=Z odd-odd nuclei is significantly improved compared to the inert-core approximation, but the improvement is conditional on the specific nucleus and observable, with some quantities (e.g., B(GT) for ⁵⁸Cu) remaining overestimated even after including core vibrations.

## Method

The authors solved a three-body core-nucleon-nucleon problem in which the core nucleus is allowed to undergo vibrational excitations described by random-phase-approximation (RPA) with the SkM* Skyrme force. The valence nucleons interact via a zero-range pairing interaction separated into spin-singlet and spin-triplet components, and the core-nucleon interaction includes both a static Woods-Saxon potential and a particle-vibration coupling term derived from the Skyrme energy density functional. The Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a basis of two-body valence wave functions coupled to core ground and excited vibrational states (natural parity states from L=0 to 5 below 30 MeV), and the resulting wave functions were used to compute magnetic moments, M1 transitions, and Gamow-Teller strengths for six N=Z odd-odd nuclei: ¹⁴N, ¹⁸F, ³⁰P, ³⁴Cl, ⁴²Sc, and ⁵⁸Cu.

## Key numbers

* Energy difference ΔE = E(1⁺₁) − E(0⁺₁) for ¹⁴N: 0.48 MeV (vibrational core) vs. 0.05 MeV (inert core) vs. 2.31 MeV (experiment)
* Energy difference ΔE for ¹⁸F: 1.04 MeV (both vibrational and inert core) vs. 1.04 MeV (experiment)
* Energy difference ΔE for ⁵⁸Cu: 0.08 MeV (vibrational core) vs. 0.68 MeV (inert core) vs. 0.20 MeV (experiment)
* Magnetic moment μ for ¹⁴N: 0.406 μ_N (vibrational core) vs. 0.379 μ_N (inert core) vs. 0.404 μ_N (experiment)
* Magnetic moment μ for ⁵⁸Cu: 0.432 μ_N (vibrational core) vs. 0.283 μ_N (inert core) vs. 0.52(8) μ_N (experiment)
* B(M1) for ¹⁸F: 16.1 μ_N² (vibrational core) vs. 18.1 μ_N² (inert core) vs. 19.71(3.47) μ_N² (experiment)
* B(M1) for ⁴²Sc: 5.44 μ_N² (vibrational core) vs. 6.79 μ_N² (inert core) vs. 6.16(265) μ_N² (experiment)
* B(GT) for ¹⁸O→¹⁸F first 1⁺ state: 4.16 g_A²/4π (vibrational core) vs. 4.96 g_A²/4π (inert core) vs. 3.11±0.03 g_A²/4π (experiment)
* B(GT) for ⁴²Ca→⁴²Sc first 1⁺ state: 3.08 g_A²/4π (vibrational core) vs. 3.60 g_A²/4π (inert core) vs. 2.16±0.15 g_A²/4π (experiment)
* B(GT) for ⁵⁸Ni→⁵⁸Cu first 1⁺ state: 1.424 g_A²/4π (vibrational core) vs. 0.194 g_A²/4π (inert core) vs. 0.155±0.010 g_A²/4π (experiment)
* Ratio of cumulative B(GT) at E=12 MeV for vibrational core to inert core: approximately 0.80 for ¹⁸F, ³⁰P, and ⁴²Sc
* Quenching factor to reproduce experimental B(GT) for first 1⁺ state of ⁴²Sc: (0.84±0.03)² for vibrational core, (0.77±0.03)² for inert core

## Context

This work extends the inert-core three-body model of Tanimura (2014, 2016) for N=Z odd-odd nuclei by incorporating core vibrational excitations via particle-vibration coupling, following the formalism of Bohr and Mottelson and earlier applications by Sagawa and Brown (1984) and Hamamoto (1996). While particle-vibration coupling had been studied in two-body systems and even-even nuclei, this work applies it for the first time to the three-body core+pn system. The inclusion of core vibrations improves agreement with experimental data for magnetic moments, energy splittings, and M1/GT transition strengths, and demonstrates that the SU(4) symmetry picture of Wigner (1937) remains useful for understanding strong spin-isospin transitions in ¹⁸F and ⁴⁰Ca even when core excitations are included. The results enable future studies of quenching and damping mechanisms in spin-isospin excitations beyond the standard RPA framework, and suggest that coupling to rotational excitations should be included for deformed cores like ¹²C and ²⁸Si.
