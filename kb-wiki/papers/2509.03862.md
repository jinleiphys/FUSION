# Stability analysis of two-fluid neutron stars featuring twin star and ultradense configurations

> We perform a detailed analysis of radial oscillations to discuss dynamical stability in two-fluid neutron stars composed of ordinary nuclear matter and a gravitationally coupled dark matter component. Using a fully relativistic two-fluid formalism, we solve the eigenvalue problem for a coupled system of equations with small-amplitude radial perturbations and derive the critical line corresponding to stability boundaries. We also compare these stability boundary lines obtained from the radial perturbations with those obtained from a generalized turning-point criterion based on extremization of mass and particle numbers, and find that the two methods agree to within better than $1\%$ across the parameter space explored. We consider both mirror dark matter and self-interacting fermionic dark matter models, and examine how microphysical properties$-$such as nuclear equations of state, dark matter mass, and vector coupling strength$-$reshape the topology of the stability boundary and gravitational mass contours. Our results reveal the emergence of ultra-dense and compact stars, with nuclear central densities exceeding single-fluid instability thresholds by factors of two or more, and the appearance of twin-star configurations with identical masses but distinct radii and internal fluid compositions. These findings have direct implications for the interpretation of neutron star observables and motivate future studies involving phase transitions, density discontinuities, or additional interactions in multi-component stellar systems. In particular, the emergence of exotic stable configurations beyond conventional stability limits underscores the need to reassess standard criteria in light of multi-fluid dynamics, with significant consequences for multimessenger probes of dense matter$-$including gravitational wave signals, mass-radius constraints, and post-merger remnants.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that two-fluid neutron stars (ordinary nuclear matter plus gravitationally coupled dark matter) can remain dynamically stable at nuclear central densities exceeding single-fluid instability thresholds by factors of two or more, and can produce twin-star configurations with identical masses but distinct radii and internal compositions. The authors find that stability boundaries from radial perturbation analysis and from a generalized turning-point criterion agree to within better than 1% across the parameter space explored.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for equilibrium configurations, then derive and numerically integrate a system of coupled second-order differential equations for small-amplitude radial perturbations of both fluids, treating the problem as an eigenvalue problem for the squared eigenfrequency ω². They implement a shooting method with boundary conditions at the stellar center, the inner fluid interface, and the outer stellar surface to determine the fundamental mode frequency, and compare these results with a generalized turning-point criterion based on simultaneous extremization of total mass and individual particle numbers of both fluids. The analysis covers both mirror dark matter (same equation of state as nuclear matter) and self-interacting fermionic dark matter models, using three nuclear equations of state: QMC-RMF4, DD2, and QHC21-BT.

## Key numbers

- QMC-RMF4 single-fluid maximum mass: M = 2.206 M⊙, radius R = 10.95 km, central energy density ≈ 1290 MeV/fm³
- DD2 single-fluid maximum mass: M = 2.427 M⊙, radius R = 11.92 km, central energy density ≈ 1090 MeV/fm³
- QHC21-BT single-fluid maximum mass: M = 2.200 M⊙, radius R = 11.06 km, central energy density ≈ 1234 MeV/fm³
- Stability boundary agreement between radial perturbation and generalized turning-point methods: better than 1% across the parameter space
- Nuclear central densities in two-fluid configurations can exceed single-fluid instability thresholds by factors of two or more while remaining stable

## Context

This work extends prior studies of dark matter admixed neutron stars (PhysRevD.103.043009, PhysRevD.111.043050) by performing the first full radial oscillation analysis for two-fluid systems with only gravitational coupling, rather than relying solely on the generalized turning-point method. The authors also identify low-density stability boundaries in the two-fluid parameter space for the first time. The results enable future studies of phase transitions, density discontinuities, and additional interactions in multi-component stellar systems, with implications for interpreting gravitational wave signals, mass-radius constraints, and post-merger remnants from multimessenger observations.
