# ${\barν}_l$ induced pion production from nuclei at $\sim$1 GeV

> We have studied charged current $\barν_l$ induced one pion production from $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O nuclear targets at MiniBooNE and atmospheric antineutrino energies. The calculations have been done for the incoherent pion production process as well as for the pions coming from the hyperons in the quasielastic production of $Λ$ and $Σ$. The calculations are done in the local density approximation. For the inelastic processes the calculations have been done in the $Δ$ dominance model and we take into account the effect of Pauli blocking, Fermi motion of the nucleon and renormalization of $Δ$ properties in the nuclear medium. The effect of final state interaction(FSI) of pions is also taken into account. For the hyperon production, the nuclear medium effects due to Fermi motion and FSI effects due to hyperon-nucleon scattering have been taken into account. These results may be quite useful in the analysis of SciBooNE, MicroBooNE, MINER$ν$A, and ArgoNeuT experiments when the pion analysis is done by using antineutrino beams.

## Key claim

Charged-current antineutrino-induced one-pion production from nuclei at around 1 GeV receives a significant contribution from pions produced via hyperon (Λ, Σ) decay, which can partially compensate for Cabibbo suppression because pions from Δ excitation are strongly reduced by nuclear medium effects and final-state interactions.

## Method

The authors calculated incoherent one-pion production on ¹²C and ¹⁶O targets using a Δ-dominance model for inelastic processes, with nuclear medium effects (Pauli blocking, Fermi motion, Δ self-energy renormalization) and final-state interactions of pions treated via Monte Carlo simulation. For hyperon production via quasielastic ΔS=1 processes, they included Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, and hyperon-nucleon final-state interactions (charge exchange and quasielastic scattering), but not pion final-state interactions because hyperons are assumed to decay outside the nucleus. All calculations were performed in the local density approximation.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear medium effects reduce the Δ-excitation cross section by 32%–40% for antineutrino energies 0.4 < E_ν̄_μ < 1.2 GeV; including pion absorption gives a total reduction of 40%–50%.
- Λ production threshold is at E_ν̄ ≈ 250 MeV; Σ⁻ and Σ⁰ production thresholds are at E_ν̄ ≈ 325 MeV.
- At E_ν̄ = 1 GeV, π⁻ from hyperon excitation contributes about 20% of total π⁻ production; π⁰ from hyperon excitation contributes about 30% of total π⁰ production.
- In the peak region of the Q² distribution (folded with MiniBooNE flux), π⁻ from hyperon excitations is about 40% of π⁻ from Δ excitation; π⁰ from hyperon excitations is about 75% of π⁰ from Δ excitation.
- For lepton energy distributions folded with atmospheric antineutrino flux, π⁻ from hyperon excitations is about 45% of π⁻ from Δ excitation; π⁰ from Δ and hyperon excitations contribute almost equally.

## Context

This work extends the Δ-dominance model previously applied to neutrino-induced pion production by Singh (1998) and to antineutrino-induced pion production by Athar, Ahmad, and Singh (2007), by adding the contribution from hyperon production channels first discussed qualitatively by Singh and Vacas (2006). The results are intended for use in analyzing data from SciBooNE, MicroBooNE, MINERνA, and ArgoNeuT experiments operating with antineutrino beams, where hyperon-mediated pions may constitute a non-negligible background or signal component.
