# A new molecular dynamics calculation and its application to the spectra of light and strange baryons

> A new approach based on antisymmetrized molecular dynamics is proposed to correctly take account of the many-body correlation. We applied it to the spectra of low-lying, light and strange baryons. The inclusion of the quark-quark correlation is vital to predict the precise spectra, and the semi-relativistic kinematics is also important to correct the level ordering. The baryon spectra calculated by the present method is as precise as the Faddeev calculation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Jacobi-coordinate-basis antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (JAMD) method can reproduce baryon spectra with precision comparable to the Faddeev calculation, but only when quark-quark correlation is included and semi-relativistic kinematics is used to correct the level ordering.

## Method

The authors applied the JAMD method, which expresses the spatial wavefunction in terms of gaussian functions with Jacobi coordinates and uses the generator coordinate method (GCM) to incorporate many-body correlations, to calculate the spectra of SU(3) octet and decuplet baryons. They employed both non-relativistic (NR) and semi-relativistic (SR) constituent quark models with a hybrid potential consisting of one-gluon exchange (OGE), meson-exchange (pi, K, eta, sigma), and confining potentials. The wavefunction was optimized using the frictional cooling method, and two variants were considered: JAMD-I (single optimized wavefunction) and JAMD-II (superposition of wavefunctions with different width parameters).

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass: 939 MeV (reproduced exactly by tuning C3q parameter)
- N-Delta mass difference: N(939 MeV) to Delta(1232-1236 MeV), used to fix alpha_s
- N-Omega mass difference: N(939 MeV) to Omega(1670-1673 MeV), used to fix strange quark mass
- N(1/2^-) mass: 1409 MeV (NR JAMD-II) vs 1411 MeV (Faddeev) vs 1553 MeV (experiment)
- N*(1/2^+) Roper resonance: 1423 MeV (NR JAMD-II) vs 1435 MeV (Faddeev) vs 1440 MeV (experiment)
- Delta*(3/2^+): 1589 MeV (NR JAMD-II) vs 1601 MeV (SR JAMD-II) vs 1600 MeV (experiment)
- Sigma(1/2^+): 1246 MeV (NR JAMD-II) vs 1214 MeV (SR JAMD-II) vs 1193 MeV (experiment)
- Lambda(1/2^+): 1135 MeV (NR JAMD-II) vs 1120 MeV (SR JAMD-II) vs 1116 MeV (experiment)
- Omega(3/2^+): 1673 MeV (NR JAMD-II) vs 1670 MeV (SR JAMD-II) vs 1672 MeV (experiment)
- Strange quark mass: 554 MeV (NR JAMD-II) to 525 MeV (SR JAMD-II)

## Context

This work extends the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) method, previously successful for nuclear structure calculations by Enyo and others, to few-quark systems by introducing Jacobi coordinates to handle correlations that standard AMD misses. The results are compared directly with the Faddeev equation calculations of the Salamanca group (ESP3, 2005), showing that JAMD-II achieves comparable precision. The method is intended to enable calculations for multiquark systems (pentaquarks, tetraquarks) where Faddeev approaches become impractical.
