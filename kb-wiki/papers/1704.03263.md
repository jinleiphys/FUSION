# Pion radiative weak decay from the instanton vacuum

## Key claim

The vector and axial-vector form factors for pion radiative weak decays computed from the gauged effective chiral action of the instanton vacuum in the large $N_c$ limit agree with experimental data only when nonlocal contributions from gauging the action are included; these nonlocal terms enhance the vector form factor by about 20% while reducing the axial-vector form factor by almost 30%.

## Method

The authors computed the three weak form factors ($F_V$, $F_A$, $R_A$) for the decays $\pi^+\to e^+\nu_e\gamma$ and $\pi^+\to e^+\nu_e e^+e^-$ by evaluating three-point correlation functions derived from the gauged effective chiral action (E$\chi$A) from the instanton vacuum. The calculation used a momentum-dependent dynamical quark mass from the instanton zero-mode solution, with the average instanton size $\bar{\rho}=0.33$ fm and constituent quark mass $M_0=350$ MeV as the only parameters, and included both local and nonlocal contributions from five Feynman diagrams to satisfy Ward-Takahashi identities.

## Key numbers

- $F_V(0) = 0.0271$ (instanton vacuum) and $F_V(0) = 0.0269$ (dipole parametrization), compared to experimental value $0.0258(17)$ from PIBETA Collaboration (Bychkov 2008)
- $F_A(0) = 0.0132$ (instanton vacuum) and $F_A(0) = 0.0116$ (dipole parametrization), compared to experimental value $0.0117(17)$ from PIBETA Collaboration (Bychkov 2008)
- $R_A(0) = 0.0462$ (instanton vacuum) and $R_A(0) = 0.0459$ (dipole parametrization), compared to experimental value $0.059^{+0.009}_{-0.008}$ from SINDRUM Collaboration (Egli 1989)
- Slope parameter $a_V = 0.0287$ (instanton vacuum) and $a_V = 0.0280$ (dipole), compared to experimental $a_V = 0.10(6)$ from PIBETA Collaboration (Bychkov 2008)
- Slope parameter $a_A = 0.0192$ (instanton vacuum) and $a_A = 0.0193$ (dipole)
- Nonlocal contributions enhance the vector form factor by about 20% and reduce the axial-vector form factor by about 30%
- Low-energy constants: $L_9^r = 5.43$, $L_{10}^r = -3.88$, $L_9^r + L_{10}^r = 1.55$ (instanton vacuum)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pion weak form factors using the instanton vacuum approach, which had successfully described other pion properties such as the electromagnetic form factor and charge radius (Nam 2007). The results are compared with chiral perturbation theory (Unterdorfer and Pichl 2008), which gave $F_V(0)=0.0262(5)$ and $F_A(0)=0.0106(36)$, the nonlocal Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (GomezDumm 2012), and the NJL model (Courtoy and Noguera 2007). The calculation enables improved predictions for the momentum-transfer dependence of all three form factors, which can be tested in future precision measurements of $\pi^+\to e^+\nu_e\gamma$ and $\pi^+\to e^+\nu_e e^+e^-$ decays.
