# Effects of T=0 two body matrix elements on M1 and Gamow-Teller transitions: isospin decomposition

> We perform calculations for M1 transitions and allowed Gamow Teller (GT) transitions in the even-even Titanium isotopes - $^{44}$Ti, $^{46}$Ti, and $^{48}$Ti. We first do calculations with the FPD6 interaction. Then to study the effect of T=0 matrix elements on the M1 and GT rates we introduce a second interaction in which all the T=0 matrix elements are set equal to zero and a third in which all the T=0 matrix elements are set to a constant. For the latter two interactions the T=1 matrix elements are the same as for FPD6. We are thus able to study the effects of the fluctuating T=0 matrix elements on M1 and GT rates.

## Key claim

The fluctuating T=0 two-body matrix elements of the FPD6 interaction have a significant effect on M1 and Gamow-Teller transition strengths in even-even Titanium isotopes, with the orbital B(M1) strength being enhanced when T=0 matrix elements are reintroduced, while the spin B(M1) strength is generally quenched; however, the authors note that for many channels the average T=0 effect (interaction B) captures most of the enhancement, suggesting that fluctuations and T=0 pairing are not always the dominant factor.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for M1 and Gamow-Teller transitions in $^{44}$Ti, $^{46}$Ti, and $^{48}$Ti using three variants of the FPD6 interaction: Interaction A with all T=0 matrix elements set to zero, Interaction B with all T=0 matrix elements set to a constant, and Interaction C (the unmodified FPD6). The T=1 matrix elements were kept identical to FPD6 in all three interactions. Calculations were carried out in model spaces ranging from a single-j shell (t=0, $f_{7/2}$ only) up to t=4 (full fp-shell) for $^{44}$Ti, and up to t=2 for $^{46}$Ti and $^{48}$Ti, where t is the number of nucleons excited from the $f_{7/2}$ shell.

## Key numbers

- For $^{44}$Ti J=0 T=0 $\rightarrow$ J=1 T=1 at t=4: spin B(M1) decreases from 9.296 $\mu_n^2$ (interaction A) to 3.267 $\mu_n^2$ (interaction C); orbital B(M1) increases from 1.121 $\mu_n^2$ to 2.144 $\mu_n^2$.
- For $^{46}$Ti J=0 T=1 $\rightarrow$ J=1 T=1 at t=2: orbital B(M1) increases from 0.1850 $\mu_n^2$ (interaction A) to 0.7095 $\mu_n^2$ (interaction C), nearly a factor of four.
- For $^{46}$Ti J=0 T=1 $\rightarrow$ J=1 T=2 at t=2: spin B(M1) decreases from 3.941 $\mu_n^2$ (interaction A) to 1.717 $\mu_n^2$ (interaction C); orbital B(M1) increases from 0.3846 $\mu_n^2$ to 0.5138 $\mu_n^2$.
- For $^{48}$Ti J=0 T=2 $\rightarrow$ J=1 T=2 at t=2: orbital B(M1) increases from 0.1931 $\mu_n^2$ (interaction A) to 0.5816 $\mu_n^2$ (interaction C).
- For $^{48}$Ti J=0 T=2 $\rightarrow$ J=1 T=3 at t=2: orbital B(M1) increases from 0.1555 $\mu_n^2$ (interaction A) to 0.2719 $\mu_n^2$ (interaction C).
- For $^{46}$Ti GT transition to $^{46}$Sc T=2 at t=2: strength decreases from 4.666 (interaction A) to 2.033 (interaction C), more than a factor of two reduction.
- For $^{44}$Ti GT transition to $^{44}$Sc T=1 at t=4: strength decreases from 5.503 (interaction A) to 1.934 (interaction C).
- The ratio B(GT)$_{^{46}Ti\rightarrow ^{46}Sc(T=2)}$ / B(GT)$_{^{46}Ti\rightarrow ^{46}V(T=2)}$ = 6, and for $^{48}$Ti the corresponding ratio is 15.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Robinson and Zamick, earlier references) on the effects of T=0 two-body matrix elements on energy levels in Titanium isotopes, now focusing on transition strengths. The results show that M1 and GT transitions are more sensitive to T=0 matrix elements than the energy spectra, which were only slightly affected. The finding that orbital B(M1) enhancement is largely captured by an average T=0 interaction (interaction B) suggests that for most channels the effect is not dominated by T=0 pairing fluctuations, though the GT channel to T-1 final states shows a different behavior where pairing effects appear important. The work enables future studies of isospin decomposition in transition strengths and provides a framework for understanding the role of T=0 interactions in nuclear collectivity, particularly the scissors mode.
