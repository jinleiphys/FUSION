# Chiral Dynamics: Theory and Experiment -- A Tribute to Aron Bernstein

> 16 pages, 7 figures, 1 table, opening talk 10th Workshop on Chiral Dynamics - Theory and Experiment (CD2021), Beijing, September 2021 (online)

## Key claim
This tribute paper argues that Aron Bernstein's work on threshold pion photoproduction and the neutral pion lifetime was central to establishing chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) as a quantitative tool for low-energy QCD, but the abstract's celebratory tone glosses over the fact that the low-energy theorem (LET) for neutral pion photoproduction was initially incorrect and had to be revised by including a previously neglected triangle diagram contribution at order μ².

## Method
The paper reviews Bernstein's contributions by tracing the historical interplay between theory and experiment in two main areas: (1) threshold γp → π⁰p, where baryon CHPT calculations at one-loop (O(p³)) and two-loop (O(p⁴)) order were compared with precision measurements from MAMI and the A2 collaboration; (2) the π⁰ → 2γ decay width, where CHPT with dynamical photons and 1/Nc expansions were used to compute corrections to the leading-order anomaly prediction, compared with PrimEx experimental results.

## Key numbers
- The original low-energy theorem gave E_{0+,thr} = -2.3 × 10⁻³/M_π; the corrected LET includes a new term (m_p²/(16F_π²))μ² that is numerically sizable.
- The π⁺n threshold lies 6.76 MeV above the π⁰p threshold.
- The leading-order anomaly prediction for the π⁰ width is Γ^{anom}_{π⁰γγ} = 7.76 eV.
- Ananthanarayan and Moussallam (2002) predicted Γ_{π⁰γγ} = 8.06 ± 0.02 ± 0.06 eV; Goity, Bernstein and Holstein (2002) predicted 8.10 ± 0.08 eV.
- PrimEx (2011) measured Γ_{π⁰γγ} = 7.82 ± 0.14 ± 0.17 eV (2.8% accuracy).
- PrimEx-II (2020) achieved Γ_{π⁰γγ} = 7.80 ± 0.06 ± 0.11 eV (1.5% accuracy).
- The CHPT prediction for the neutron S-wave amplitude is E_{0+}^{π⁰n} = 2.1 (canonical units), compared to |E_{0+}^{π⁰p}| ≈ 1.2.
- The deuteron threshold amplitude prediction is E_d^{th} = -1.8 ± 0.2, consistent with the reanalyzed Saclay value E_d^{exp} = -1.7 ± 0.2.
- The upper energy limit of heavy baryon CHPT for neutral pion photoproduction was determined to be E_γ^{max} = 170 MeV.

## Context
This paper reviews and celebrates the work of Aron Bernstein, who co-founded the "Chiral Dynamics – Theory and Experiment" workshop series (first held at MIT in 1994) and was instrumental in the PrimEx experiment at Jefferson Lab. The work extends earlier low-energy theorems by De Baenst (1970) and Vainshtein (1972), and the corrected LET was established by Bernard, Gasser, Kaiser and Meißner (1991) using baryon CHPT. The results enabled precise tests of chiral dynamics in pion photoproduction and the QCD chiral anomaly, and the paper issues an experimental challenge to measure γn → π⁰n on light nuclei (³He) to test the CHPT prediction for the neutron S-wave amplitude.
