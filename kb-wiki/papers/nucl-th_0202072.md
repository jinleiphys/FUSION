# Anisotropic flow from AGS to RHIC

> The recent results on anisotropic flow in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions along with recent methodical developments and achievements in the understanding of the phenomena, are reviewed. The emphasis is given to the elliptic flow results.

# Anisotropic flow from AGS to RHIC

## Key claim
The paper reviews anisotropic flow results, emphasizing elliptic flow, and argues that even at RHIC energies the elliptic flow continues to rise with particle density, which could challenge a purely hydrodynamic interpretation if the trend persists at full RHIC energy.

## Method
The paper reviews experimental measurements, analysis methods (including two-particle correlations, scalar-product method, and multi-particle cumulants), and theoretical models (blast-wave model, parton cascade, hydrodynamics, Color Glass Condensate) for anisotropic flow in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions from AGS to RHIC energies. It discusses how non-flow contributions are estimated and removed using cumulant techniques, and compares flow results across collision energies and centralities.

## Key numbers
- The non-flow contribution to the correlation between subevent flow angles was estimated as $\tilde{g}=0.05$ in STAR elliptic flow measurements.
- Using 4-particle cumulants, the ratio of flow from standard 2-particle correlation method to 4-particle cumulants can be as large as $5^{1/4} \approx 1.5$ for a flat $v$ distribution from $v=0$ to $v=v_{\rm max}$.
- At RHIC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130$ GeV), STAR results on elliptic flow from 4-particle cumulants show no non-flow effects; possible systematic errors in $v_2/\epsilon$ are of order 10-20% due to uncertainties in centrality measurements.
- Particle density increases by about 15% from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=130$ GeV to $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV.
- Parton cascade calculations used transport opacities corresponding to 35 and 13 times higher gluon density than given by the HIJING model, assuming 1 mb gluon transport cross section.

## Context
This review extends earlier work on anisotropic flow from AGS and SPS energies to the first RHIC results, building on the foundational observation by Ollitrault (1992) that elliptic flow is sensitive to early-time evolution. It discusses the low-density limit (Heiselberg and collaborators, 1999) versus the hydro limit (Kolb and collaborators, 2000; Shuryak, 1999), and the possibility of a phase transition signature via "kinks" in $v_2/\epsilon$ (Sorge, 1999). The paper enables future measurements at full RHIC energy to distinguish between hydrodynamic and transport descriptions, and highlights the need to account for flow fluctuations in high-precision analyses.
