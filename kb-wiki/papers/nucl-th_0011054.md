# Comment on "Complete One-Loop Analysis of the Nucleon's Spin Polarisabilities"

> We comment on the paper of Gellas et al, nucl-th/0002027, in which heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory results for the spin polarisabilities of the nucleon are given. That paper uses a definition which is different from the one used in papers by us, hep-ph/9908526, hep-ph/9909442 and hep-ph/0002133. In this comment we show that it is the latter definition, and not that of Gellas et al, that should be compared with the values of the polarisabilities extracted from dispersion relation analyses.

# Comment on "Complete One-Loop Analysis of the Nucleon's Spin Polarisabilities"

## Key claim

The spin polarisabilities calculated by Gellas et al. (2000) using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) employ a definition that excludes certain one-particle-reducible diagrams from the polarisability contributions, whereas the definition used by Ji, McGovern, Birse and collaborators is the one that should be compared with values extracted from dispersion relation analyses.

## Method

The authors compare two different definitions of nucleon spin polarisabilities arising from the treatment of the nucleon pole contribution in Compton scattering amplitudes. In fixed-t dispersion relations, the six independent Compton amplitudes are split into a pole piece (depending only on on-shell nucleon charge, mass and magnetic moment) and a non-pole piece obtained from an integral over the imaginary part above the πN cut; polarisabilities are defined as the lowest terms in the energy expansion of this non-pole piece. The disagreement concerns whether the ω³ terms from the diagram where a photon couples to a pion loop on the nucleon (Fig. 1) are included in the polarisabilities or assigned to the pole contribution.

## Key numbers

- The leading term from the disputed diagram (Fig. 1) contributes to renormalisation of the anomalous magnetic moment at leading order (LO) in the photon energy expansion ω.
- The disagreement specifically concerns the treatment of the ω³ terms from Fig. 1.
- For forward scattering, the polarisability γ₀ can be directly related to the spin-dependent total inelastic cross section via a GDH-style dispersion relation, and the pole contribution beyond the low-energy theorem vanishes for forward and backward angles.

## Context

This comment addresses a definitional dispute between two groups working on nucleon spin polarisabilities in HBχPT. Gellas, Hemmert and Meißner (2000) proposed excluding one-particle-reducible graphs from the polarisability definition, while Ji, Kao and Osborne (2000) and Kumar, McGovern and Birse (1999, 2000) used the standard definition matching dispersion relation analyses by Babusci et al. (1998) and Drechsel et al. (1998, 2000). The authors argue that the Gellas et al. definition violates Lorentz invariance to the order in 1/m_N they are working and has not been demonstrated to be gauge invariant. The comment notes that significant discrepancies remain between NLO HBχPT calculations and dispersion relation analyses, but that NLO corrections are of the same magnitude as LO values, so the chiral series has not yet converged.
