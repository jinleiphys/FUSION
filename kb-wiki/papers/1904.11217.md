# Causal hydrodynamic fluctuations in non-static and inhomogeneous backgrounds

> To integrate hydrodynamic fluctuations, namely thermal fluctuations of hydrodynamics, into dynamical models of high-energy nuclear collisions based on relativistic hydrodynamics, the property of the hydrodynamic fluctuations given by the fluctuation-dissipation relation should be carefully investigated. The fluctuation-dissipation relation for causal dissipative hydrodynamics with the finite relaxation time is naturally given in the integral form of the constitutive equation by the linear-response theory. While, the differential form of the constitutive equation is commonly used in analytic investigations and dynamical calculations for practical reasons. We give the fluctuation-dissipation relation for the general linear-response differential form and discuss the restrictions to the structure of the differential form, which comes from the causality and the positive semi-definiteness of the noise autocorrelation, and also the relation of those restrictions to the cutoff scale of the hydrodynamic fluctuations. We also give the fluctuation-dissipation relation for the integral form in non-static and inhomogeneous background by introducing new tensors, the pathline projectors. We find new modification terms to the fluctuation-dissipation relation for the differential form in non-static and inhomogeneous background which are particularly important in dynamical models to describe rapidly expanding systems.

## Key claim

The fluctuation-dissipation relation (FDR) for causal dissipative hydrodynamics in non-static and inhomogeneous backgrounds acquires new modification terms beyond the global-equilibrium form, which are particularly important for dynamical models of rapidly expanding systems such as those created in high-energy nuclear collisions.

## Method

The author derives the FDR for both the integral and differential forms of the constitutive equation in causal dissipative hydrodynamics, using linear-response theory. New tensors called "pathline projectors" are introduced to handle the tensor structure of the memory function for shear stress and diffusion currents in non-static and inhomogeneous backgrounds, preserving the transversality of dissipative currents to the flow velocity. The restrictions on the differential form structure arising from causality and positive semi-definiteness of the noise autocorrelation are analyzed, along with their relation to the cutoff scale of hydrodynamic fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- The relaxation time matrix is given as \(\tau_R = \mathrm{diag}(\tau_\Pi, \tau_\pi, \tau_{ij})\) for bulk pressure, shear stress, and diffusion currents respectively.
- The Onsager coefficient matrix is \(\kappa = \mathrm{diag}(\zeta, 2\eta\Delta^{\mu\nu\alpha\beta}, -\kappa_{ij}\Delta^{\mu\alpha})\) where \(\zeta\) is bulk viscosity, \(\eta\) is shear viscosity, and \(\kappa_{ij}\) are diffusion coefficients.
- The metric signature is \(g_{\mu\nu} = \mathrm{diag}(1, -1, -1, -1)\) with natural units \(c = k_B = 1\).

## Context

This work extends the FDR treatment from global equilibrium (as in Landau-Lifshitz fluctuating hydrodynamics and the first-order theory of Calzetta (1997) and Kapusta (2011)) to causal second-order hydrodynamics in non-static and inhomogeneous backgrounds. It addresses the problem that the naive global-equilibrium FDR used in existing dynamical models (e.g., Murase (2013), Young (2013)) is insufficient for rapidly expanding systems like those at RHIC and LHC. The results enable proper implementation of hydrodynamic fluctuations in fully non-linear dynamical simulations of high-energy nuclear collisions, which is essential for determining transport properties of the quark-gluon plasma and searching for the QCD critical point.
