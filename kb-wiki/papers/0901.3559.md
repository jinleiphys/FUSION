# Isovector EMC effect explains the NuTeV anomaly

> A neutron or proton excess in nuclei leads to an isovector-vector mean-field which, through its coupling to the quarks in a bound nucleon, implies a shift in the quark distributions with respect to the Bjorken scaling variable. We show that this result leads to an additional correction to the NuTeV measurement of sin^2(Theta_W). The sign of this correction is largely model independent and acts to reduce their result. Explicit calculation within a covariant and confining Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model predicts that this vector field correction accounts for approximately two-thirds of the NuTeV anomaly. We are therefore led to offer a new interpretation of the NuTeV measurement, namely, that it is further evidence for the medium modification of the bound nucleon wavefunction.

## Key claim
The isovector-vector mean field (ρ⁰) in nuclei with neutron excess shifts quark momentum from u- to d-quarks, producing a model-independent negative correction to the Paschos-Wolfenstein ratio that reduces the NuTeV measurement of sin²Θ_W. Explicit calculation within a covariant Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model predicts this vector field correction accounts for approximately two-thirds of the NuTeV anomaly, with the remainder explained by charge symmetry violation.

## Method
The authors use a Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with proper-time regularization to self-consistently compute in-medium quark distributions in asymmetric nuclear matter. The scalar and vector mean fields (σ, ω, ρ⁰) couple to quarks in bound nucleons, modifying their internal structure; the ρ⁰ field strength is fixed by the empirical symmetry energy of nuclear matter (a₄ = 32 MeV). Quark distributions are evolved from the model scale Q₀² = 0.16 GeV² to experimental Q², and the Paschos-Wolfenstein ratio is evaluated for an iron target with 5.74% neutron excess.

## Key numbers
- The total isoscalarity correction to the Paschos-Wolfenstein ratio for iron is ΔR_PW = −0.0139, broken into: naive correction −0.0107, Fermi motion correction −0.0004, and ρ⁰ field correction −0.0028.
- The combined medium correction (−0.0032) plus charge symmetry violation correction (−0.0017) gives ΔR_PW^(medium) + ΔR_PW^(CSV) = −0.0049, which accounts for the NuTeV anomaly.
- The NuTeV measured value of sin²Θ_W = 0.2277 ± 0.0013(stat.) ± 0.0009(syst.) differs from the world average of 0.2227 ± 0.0004 by three sigma.
- The ρ⁰ coupling constant is G_ρ = 14.2 GeV⁻², determined from the symmetry energy a₄ = 32 MeV.
- The model scale is Q₀² = 0.16 GeV².

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous NJL model studies of the EMC effect in symmetric nuclear matter (Cloet 2006, Cloet 2005) to include the isovector-vector ρ⁰ mean field, which had not been explored in relation to the NuTeV anomaly. It contradicts interpretations that the NuTeV anomaly requires physics beyond the Standard Model (Davidson 2001) and instead supports the view that bound nucleon wavefunctions are modified by the nuclear medium. The charge symmetry violation correction from quark mass differences (Londergan 2003) explains approximately one-third of the anomaly, while this work shows the ρ⁰ field correction accounts for the remaining two-thirds. The results enable experimental searches for flavour-dependent EMC effects at Jefferson Lab.
