# Energies and radii of light nuclei around unitarity

> Light nuclei fall within a regime of universal physics governed by the fact that the two-nucleon scattering lengths are large compared to the typical nuclear interaction range set by one-pion exchange. This places nuclear physics near the so-called unitarity limit in which the scattering lengths are exactly infinite. Effective field theory provides a powerful theoretical framework to capture this separation of scales in a systematic way. It is shown here that the nuclear force can be constructed as a perturbative expansion around the unitarity limit and that this expansion has good convergence properties for both the binding energies of $A=3,4$ nuclei as well as for the radii of these states.

## Key claim
The nuclear force can be constructed as a perturbative expansion around the unitarity limit (where two-nucleon scattering lengths are exactly infinite), and this expansion shows good convergence properties for binding energies and radii of A=3 and A=4 nuclei, provided one counts the inverse scattering length and binding momentum as separate small scales.

## Method
The author constructs a Pionless Effective Field Theory (EFT) where the leading-order (LO) interaction is set at the unitarity limit (infinite scattering lengths), with finite-scattering-length and range corrections treated perturbatively at next-to-leading order (NLO) and beyond. The three- and four-body systems are solved using Faddeev and Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations with separable Gaussian regulators, where the three-body force at LO and NLO is fixed by the triton binding energy, and charge radii are computed from the resulting wavefunctions.

## Key numbers
- Two-nucleon scattering lengths: \(a_t \simeq 5.4\ \mathrm{fm}\) (triplet, \(^3S_1\) channel) and \(a_s \simeq -23.7\ \mathrm{fm}\) (singlet, \(^1S_0\) channel), compared to the one-pion exchange range \(R \sim M_\pi^{-1} \simeq 1.4\ \mathrm{fm}\)
- Deuteron binding energy: \(B_D \simeq 2.224\ \mathrm{MeV}\), with the unitarity-limit estimate \(1/(M_N a_t^2) \approx 1.41\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Virtual state in the singlet channel: \(B_{NN^*} \simeq 0.068\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Ratio of four-body to three-body binding energies at unitarity: \(B_4/B_3 \simeq 4.611\) (ground state) and \(B_{4^*}/B_3 \simeq 1.002\) (excited state)
- Physical ratio for \(^4\mathrm{He}\) relative to \(^3\mathrm{He}\): \(B_\alpha/B_H \simeq 3.66\), with \(B_H \simeq 7.72\ \mathrm{MeV}\); excited state ratio \(B_{\alpha^*}/B_H \simeq 1.05\)

## Context
This work extends the "unitarity expansion" approach previously developed by the author (Konig 2016) for binding energies to also include charge radii of light nuclei. It builds on the observation that nuclear physics sits near the unitarity limit, a universality class shared with cold atomic gases (where Feshbach resonances tune scattering lengths) and certain hadronic molecules (Braaten 2003). The Efimov effect (Efimov 1970) predicts an infinite tower of three-body states at unitarity; Bedaque (1998, 1999) showed the triton can be interpreted as the single remaining bound state of such a tower for physical scattering lengths. The four-body universal ratios (Hammer 2006, Deltuva 2010) provide benchmarks: the closeness of physical \(^4\mathrm{He}\) ratios to unitarity values motivates the perturbative expansion. This work enables systematic calculation of few-nucleon observables with controlled theoretical uncertainties, connecting the unitarity expansion to the broader landscape of nuclear EFTs.
