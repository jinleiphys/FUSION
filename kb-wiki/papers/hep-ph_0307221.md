# Meson Structure Functions in Valon Model

> Parton distributions in a {\it{valon}} in the next-to-leading order is used to determine the patron distributions in pion and kaon. The validity of the valon model is tested and shown that the partonic content of the valon is universal and independent of the valon type. We have evaluated the valon distribution in pion and kaon, and in particular it is shown that the results are in good agreement with the experimental data on pion structure in a wide range of $x=[10^{-4},1]$

## Key claim

The valon model, using next-to-leading order parton distributions in a valon, can determine the parton distributions in pion and kaon with the claim that the partonic content of the valon is universal and independent of the valon type, but the paper acknowledges that the kaon structure remains uncertain due to lack of accurate or compatible experimental data.

## Method

The author used the valon model, where mesons are treated as bound states of two valons, each with a partonic structure calculated in QCD at next-to-leading order. The valon distribution in the pion was determined by fitting to Drell-Yan valence distribution data from the E615 collaboration at Q² = 25 GeV² using a χ² minimization procedure. For the kaon, the valon distribution was obtained by fitting to data on the ratio R = xū_K⁻ / xū_π⁻ at large x from Badier et al. (1980), maintaining the same Q₀² and Λ values as for the pion.

## Key numbers

- χ² per degree of freedom = 1.2 for the pion valence distribution fit at Q² = 25 GeV², yielding μ_m = ν_m = 0.01
- Valence quark sum rule integrals give 0.9994, 1.002, and 1.008 at Q² = 3, 10, and 20 GeV² respectively
- First two moments of pion valence quark distribution at Q² = 49 GeV²: 2⟨xq_valence_π⟩ = 0.378 and 2⟨x²q_valence_π⟩ = 0.151
- For the kaon fit, μ_k = 0.13 and ν_k = 0.28 with χ² = 0.646 per degree of freedom at Q² = 25 GeV²
- Average momentum fractions carried by Ū and S valons in K⁻ are 0.469 and 0.531 respectively
- Starting scale Q₀² = 0.47 GeV² with Λ_QCD = 0.22 GeV used for the pion fit

## Context

This work extends the valon model approach previously applied to the proton by Arash (2001) and applied to the pion at low x by Arash (2003), now covering the full x range [10⁻⁴, 1] and including kaon structure. The results are compared with the SMRS parameterization of Sutton et al. (1992) and the GRS parameterization of Gluck, Reya, and Schienbein (1999), showing that the valon model agrees with the pion flux normalization of ZEUS data while GRS agrees with the additive quark model normalization. The work enables determination of sea quark distributions in mesons that are experimentally inaccessible, particularly for the kaon's strange valence and sea components.
