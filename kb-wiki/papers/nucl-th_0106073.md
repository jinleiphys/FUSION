# Multistrange baryon production in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Using a multiphase transport model, we study the production of multistrange baryons from the hadronic matter formed in relativistic heavy ion collisions. The mechanism we introduce is the strangeness-exchange reactions between antikaons and hyperons. We find that these reactions contribute significantly to the production of multistrange baryons in heavy ion collisions at SPS energies, which has been found to be appreciably enhanced. We have also made predictions for multistrange baryon production in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and found a similar enhancement.

## Key claim

Strangeness-exchange reactions between antikaons and hyperons can account for most of the observed enhancement of multistrange baryons in heavy ion collisions at SPS energies, without requiring a quark-gluon plasma; however, the model still underpredicts the Omega yield by a factor of two compared to data.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model, which combines HIJING initial conditions, Zhang's Parton Cascade (ZPC) for partonic evolution, Lund string fragmentation, and a relativistic transport model (ART) for hadronic dynamics. They introduced strangeness-exchange reactions (anti-K + hyperon -> cascade + pion, and anti-K + cascade -> Omega + pion) perturbatively, assuming cross sections equal to the empirically known anti-K N -> Sigma pi cross sections, with inverse reactions treated via detailed balance.

## Key numbers

- In central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energy sqrt(s) = 17A GeV (impact parameter b <= 3 fm), the dynamical evolution leads to about 30% increase in Lambda production and 70% increase in Xi- production relative to the initial HIJING yield.
- The Omega- production in the AMPT model at SPS is a factor of two smaller compared to the WA97 experimental data.
- For Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy sqrt(s) = 130A GeV, the abundance of multistrange particles is increased by about a factor of two compared to SPS.
- The inverse slope parameter for nonstrange hadrons at SPS follows T = T_fo + m <beta_t>^2 with freeze-out temperature T_fo approximately 145 MeV and average transverse flow <beta_t> approximately 0.39c.
- At RHIC, the freeze-out temperature is T_fo approximately 155 MeV and average collective velocity <beta_t> approximately 0.43c.
- The proton slope parameter at SPS is T = 0.29 GeV, with a contribution of about 0.16 GeV from HIJING and a further increase of about 0.03 GeV from mean-field effects.

## Context

This work extends earlier transport model studies (URQMD) that failed to account for the factor-of-three enhancement of multistrange baryons observed by the WA97 Collaboration at SPS, and follows a suggestion by Vance (2000) that strangeness-exchange reactions could be important. The results show that hadronic mechanisms alone can produce most of the observed enhancement, challenging the interpretation of strangeness enhancement as a unique quark-gluon plasma signal. The paper also provides predictions for RHIC energies that are consistent with thermal model fits by Braun-Munzinger et al. (2001) and with preliminary PHENIX data.
