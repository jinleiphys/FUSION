# Gluonic Transversity from Lattice QCD

> We present an exploratory study of the gluonic structure of the $ϕ$ meson using lattice QCD (LQCD). This includes the first investigation of gluonic transversity via the leading moment of the twist-two double-helicity-flip gluonic structure function $Δ(x,Q^2)$. This structure function only exists for targets of spin $J\ge1$ and does not mix with quark distributions at leading twist, thereby providing a particularly clean probe of gluonic degrees of freedom. We also explore the gluonic analogue of the Soffer bound which relates the helicity flip and non-flip gluonic distributions, finding it to be saturated at the level of 80%. This work sets the stage for more complex LQCD studies of gluonic structure in the nucleon and in light nuclei where $Δ(x,Q^2)$ is an 'exotic glue' observable probing gluons in a nucleus not associated with individual nucleons.

## Key claim

This exploratory lattice QCD study presents the first calculation of the gluonic transversity structure function Δ(x,Q²) in any hadron, specifically the φ meson, and finds that the gluonic analogue of the Soffer bound is saturated at approximately 80% for the first moment, though this result is subject to caveats regarding renormalisation, the continuum limit, and the use of unphysical light quark masses.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations on an ensemble of 24³×64 isotropic gauge-field configurations with Nf=2+1 flavours of dynamical quarks, a lattice spacing of 0.1167(16) fm, and a pion mass of 450(5) MeV. They constructed Euclidean-space gluonic operators with appropriate H(4) symmetry properties to extract the reduced matrix element A₂ for the n=2 twist-two double-helicity-flip operator, and also computed the matrix elements B₂,₁ and B₂,₂ for the spin-independent gluon distribution, using ratios of two-point and three-point correlation functions with Wilson flow applied to the gluon operator links.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.1167(16) fm, with lattice dimensions L³×T = 24³×64 corresponding to L = 2.801(29) fm and T = 7.469(77) fm
- Pion mass: mπ = 450(5) MeV; kaon mass: mK = 596(6) MeV; φ meson mass: mφ = 1040(3) MeV
- Reduced matrix element A₂ ∼ 0.23(2)(5), where the first uncertainty is statistical and the second is an estimate of renormalisation effects (20% assigned)
- The gluonic analogue of the Soffer bound is saturated at the level of 80% for the first moment in the φ meson

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of unpolarised gluonic structure in the pion and nucleon by Meyer (2007) and Alexandrou (2013) to the novel observable of gluonic transversity, which was first proposed theoretically by Jaffe (1989) for spin J≥1 targets. The only prior information on Δ(x,Q²) came from a rudimentary bag model calculation of its first moment in the Δ baryon by Sather (1990) and a related model of its x-dependence in the deuteron by Nzar (1992). This study enables future lattice QCD calculations of gluonic transversity in the nucleon (via off-forward parton distributions) and in light nuclei, where Δ(x,Q²) serves as an "exotic glue" observable probing gluons not associated with individual nucleons, with potential experimental access at Jefferson Lab and the proposed Electron-Ion Collider.
