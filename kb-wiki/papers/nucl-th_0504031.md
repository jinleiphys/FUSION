# Influence of $NN$-rescattering effect on the photon asymmetry of $d(\vecγ,π^-)pp$ reaction

> The influence of final-state $NN$-rescattering on the beam asymmetry $Σ$ for linearly polarized photons in $π^-$ photoproduction on the deuteron in the energy range from $π$-threshold through the $Δ$(1232)-resonance has been investigated. Numerical results for this spin observable are predicted and compared with recent experimental data from the LEGS Spin collaboration. Final-state $NN$-rescattering is found to be quite important and leads to a better agreement with existing experimental data. Furthermore, the differences with other theoretical models have been discussed.

## Key claim

The inclusion of final-state nucleon-nucleon rescattering leads to a better, but still not fully satisfactory, description of the photon asymmetry data from the LEGS Spin collaboration; the effect is small (about 10% in the peak region) and arises mainly from interference with the impulse approximation amplitude, not from the rescattering amplitude alone.

## Method

The transition matrix for the reaction \(d(\vec{\gamma},\pi^-)pp\) was computed as the sum of an impulse approximation term and a term for final-state nucleon-nucleon rescattering, using the elementary photoproduction operator of Schmidt et al. (1996) and the Paris potential deuteron wave function. The half-off-shell nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitude was taken from a separable representation of the Paris potential, including all partial waves with total angular momentum \(J \le 3\). The photon asymmetry \(\Sigma\) was then evaluated from the differential cross section for linearly polarized photons and unpolarized deuterons.

## Key numbers

* At photon lab-energy \(\omega_{\gamma}=270\) MeV, inclusion of nucleon-nucleon rescattering yields an about 10% effect on the photon asymmetry in the region of the peak position.
* The photon asymmetry \(\Sigma\) is dominantly negative in the energy range from \(\pi\)-threshold to 500 MeV, with small positive values found at \(\omega_{\gamma}=200\) MeV and 500 MeV.
* The asymmetry vanishes at \(\theta_{\pi}=0^{\circ}\) for all energies studied, but does not vanish at \(180^{\circ}\).
* At extreme forward and backward pion angles, the nucleon-nucleon rescattering effect is "quite small, almost completely negligible."

## Context

This work extends earlier impulse-approximation calculations of the photon asymmetry by Lee (2004) and Darwish (2004c), which could not describe the preliminary LEGS Spin collaboration data. It also complements the author's previous study of the helicity structure of the same reaction (Darwish, 2004d), where nucleon-nucleon rescattering was found to be much less important for the polarized cross-section difference than for the unpolarized one. The results enable future improvements such as a complete three-body treatment of the final \(\pi NN\) system and independent evaluations using effective field theory.
