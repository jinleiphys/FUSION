# Statistical production of antikaon nuclear bound states in heavy ion collisions

> Recently it was conjectured that the strongly attractive antikaon--nucleon potential can result in the formation of antikaon nuclear bound states. We discuss the formation of such states as possible residues in heavy ion collisions. In this context, we calculate the excitation functions of single- and double-$K^-$ clusters in terms of the statistical thermal model. We show that, if such objects indeed exist, then, in heavy ion collisions, the single-$K^-$ clusters are most abundantly produced at present SIS energies, while the double-$K^-$ clusters show a pronounced maximum yield in the energy domain of the future accelerator at GSI. This is a direct consequence of: i) the baryonic dominance in low energy heavy ion collisions and the large baryonic content of the antikaonic bound states; ii) the strong energy dependence of strangeness production at low energies. The production yields of double-strange clusters is compared with that of double strange baryons. It is shown that at SIS energy there is a linear scaling relation of the $Ξ^-/K^+$ with $K^+/p$ yields ratio.

# Statistical production of antikaon nuclear bound states in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

If antikaon nuclear bound states exist, single-K⁻ clusters are most abundantly produced at present SIS energies, while double-K⁻ clusters show a pronounced maximum yield in the energy domain of the future accelerator at GSI, but this conclusion depends on the assumption that such states can survive the hot, dense medium of heavy ion collisions, despite theoretical concerns that finite-momentum effects and medium modifications may suppress or wash out the attractive antikaon potential.

## Method

The authors used the statistical hadron resonance gas model with exact canonical strangeness conservation to calculate production yields of single- and double-K⁻ nuclear clusters at chemical freezeout in heavy ion collisions. The thermal parameters (temperature T and baryon chemical potential μ_B) were taken from the phenomenological freezeout condition of fixed energy per particle ⟨E⟩/⟨N⟩ = 1 GeV, and the physical properties of the predicted K⁻ clusters (mass, binding energy, width, quantum numbers) were taken from prior theoretical calculations by Akaishi and collaborators. Yields were normalized to Λ and Ξ⁻ yields to remove dependence on the fireball volume.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au at 1 AGeV (T ≈ 52 MeV, μ_B ≈ 822 MeV), the calculated yield per Λ for pK⁻ is 0.399 × 10⁻², for ppK⁻ is 0.476 × 10⁻³, for pppK⁻ is 0.309 × 10⁻², and for pnnK⁻ is 0.713 × 10⁻²
- For Ni+Ni at 1.8 AGeV (T ≈ 70 MeV, μ_B ≈ 750 MeV), the calculated yield per Λ for pK⁻ is 0.142 × 10⁻¹, for ppK⁻ is 0.129 × 10⁻², for pppK⁻ is 0.362 × 10⁻², and for pnnK⁻ is 0.443 × 10⁻²
- The predicted K⁻ clusters have binding energies E_K ranging from 27 MeV (pK⁻, Λ(1405)) to 191 MeV (pnnK⁻) and widths Γ_K from 13 to 61 MeV
- Central nuclear densities of predicted K⁻ clusters exceed normal nuclear density by 4–9 times, with values up to 2.97 fm⁻³ for ppnK⁻K⁻
- The yield of double-K⁻ clusters is more than three orders of magnitude less abundant than Ξ⁻ yield at SIS energies, but increases by about three orders of magnitude up to √s ≈ 5 GeV

## Context

This work extends the statistical thermal model approach previously applied to strange particle production at SIS energies by Braun-Munzinger et al. (1999) and Cleymans et al. (1999), applying it to the newly conjectured antikaon nuclear bound states proposed by Akaishi and Yamazaki (2002). The paper addresses the experimental programs at SIS (FOPI collaboration) and the future FAIR facility (CBM experiment), providing quantitative predictions for the production yields of these exotic states. The authors also derive a linear scaling relation of Ξ⁻/K⁺ with K⁺/p ratios at SIS energies, which follows from detailed balance in strangeness-exchange reactions and provides a method to infer cascade yields from kaon and proton measurements.
