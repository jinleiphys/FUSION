# Regularization Prescription for the Mixing Between Nonlocal Gluon and Quark Operators

> It is well-known that in the study of mixing between nonlocal gluon and quark bilinear operators there exists an ambiguity when relating coordinate space and momentum space results, which can be conveniently resolved through Mellin moments matching in both spaces. In this work, we show that this ambiguity is due to the lack of a proper regularization prescription of the singularity that arises when the separation between the gluon/quark fields approaches zero. We then demonstrate that dimensional regularization resolves this issue and yields consistent results in both coordinate and momentum space. This prescription is also compatible with lattice extractions of parton distributions from nonlocal operators.

## Key claim

The ambiguity in relating coordinate-space and momentum-space results for the mixing between nonlocal gluon and quark bilinear operators arises from the lack of a proper regularization prescription for the singularity when the separation between the gluon/quark fields approaches zero; dimensional regularization resolves this issue and yields consistent results in both spaces, provided one works in \(d=4-2\epsilon\) dimensions and takes the local limit appropriately.

## Method

The authors analyze the mixing of nonlocal gluon and flavor-singlet quark operators in both light-front (LF) and quasi-light-front (quasi-LF) formulations, focusing on the gluon-in-quark channel where the mixing kernel contains a factor \(1/{\bf z_{12}}\) that is singular as the field separation \({\bf z_{12}}\to 0\). They compare the traditional operator product expansion (OPE) approach with direct coordinate-space factorization, and show that matching Mellin moments of the kernels in coordinate and momentum space is insufficient to remove the ambiguity. They then introduce dimensional regularization (DR) in \(d=4-2\epsilon\) dimensions, replacing logarithms like \(\ln({\bf z_{12}^2}\mu^2/4e^{-2\gamma_E})\) with \((({\bf z_{12}^2}\mu^2/4e^{-2\gamma_E})^\epsilon - 1)/\epsilon\) and treating the \(1/{\bf z_{12}}\) pole via the factor \(({\bf z_{12}^2})^\epsilon/{\bf z_{12}}\) in the Fourier transform, and verify that the resulting momentum-space matching kernels reproduce the correct local limits and are consistent with direct momentum-space calculations after convolution with parton distributions.

## Key numbers

- For the unpolarized case, the local limit of the gluon correlator in DR gives \(\langle P|O_{g,u}(0,0)|P\rangle = -\frac{8i a_s C_F}{3\epsilon_{\text{UV}}}\langle P|O_q^{-,1}(0,0)|P\rangle\), where \(a_s = \alpha_s/(4\pi)\) and \(C_F\) is the Casimir; this matches the mixing of gluon and quark average momentum.
- For the polarized case, the local limit yields \(0 = 0\) at one loop, consistent with the oddness of \(O_{g,h}(z_{12},0)\) in \({\bf z_{12}}\) and the vanishing first moment of the evolution kernel \(\int_0^1 d\alpha\, C_{gq,h}^{\text{evol},{\rm s}}(\alpha)=0\) at one loop.
- The one-loop matching kernels for quasi-PDFs and quasi-GPDs are given explicitly in terms of sign functions and logarithms, e.g., for unpolarized quasi-PDFs: \(\mathbb{C}_{gq}^{\text{FT,DR},{\rm u}}(x,y,P_z/\mu) = -4a_s C_F\{\text{sgn}(x-y)\frac{(x-y)^2+y^2}{2y^2}\ln\frac{4P_z^2(x-y)^2}{\mu^2} - \text{sgn}(x)\frac{x(x-2y)}{2y^2}\ln\frac{4P_z^2x^2}{\mu^2} + \text{sgn}(x-y)\frac{4x-3y}{2y} - \text{sgn}(x)\frac{x}{y}\}\).

## Context

This work extends the long-standing study of mixing between flavor-singlet quark and gluon operators in QCD evolution and factorization, where an ambiguity in Fourier transforming the coordinate-space kernel to momentum space was noted by Belitsky (2005) and Yao (2022). Prior approaches, such as matching Mellin moments (Belitsky 2005, Yao 2022) or imposing physical constraints on integration limits (Radyushkin 1997), were insufficient to uniquely fix the result. The dimensional regularization prescription proposed here resolves the ambiguity and is compatible with lattice extractions of parton distributions from nonlocal operators (Cichy 2018, Ji 2020, Constantinou 2020). This enables consistent calculations of gluon quasi-PDFs and quasi-GPDs from lattice QCD and clarifies that superficial differences between coordinate-space and momentum-space kernels (e.g., those in Ma 2022, Wang 2017) vanish after convolution with parton distributions.
