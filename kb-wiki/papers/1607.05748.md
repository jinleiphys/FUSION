# On light baryons and their excitations

> We study ground states and excitations of light octet and decuplet baryons within the framework of Dyson-Schwinger and Faddeev equations. We improve upon similar approaches by explicitly taking into account the momentum-dependent dynamics of the quark-gluon interaction that leads to dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. We perform calculations in both the three-body Faddeev framework and the quark-diquark approximation in order to assess the impact of the latter on the spectrum. Our results indicate that both approaches agree well with each other. The resulting spectra furthermore agree one-to-one with experiment, provided well-known deficiencies of the rainbow-ladder approximation are compensated for. We also discuss the mass evolution of the Roper and the excited Delta with varying pion mass and analyse the internal structure in terms of their partial wave decompositions.

## Key claim

The authors show that both the three-body Faddeev framework and the quark-diquark approximation produce baryon spectra that agree well with each other and with experiment, but only for the "good" channels (nucleon \(J^P=1/2^+\) and \(\Delta\) \(J^P=3/2^+\)); the agreement is conditional on compensating for known deficiencies of the rainbow-ladder truncation, which otherwise yields masses that are too low for parity partners and other channels.

## Method

The authors solved the covariant three-body Faddeev equation and the quark-diquark Bethe-Salpeter equation for light octet and decuplet baryons, using a momentum-dependent effective quark-gluon interaction (Maris-Tandy model) that preserves chiral symmetry via the axial Ward-Takahashi identity. They first determined the dressed quark propagator from its Dyson-Schwinger equation, then computed diquark Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes and propagators for scalar, axialvector, pseudoscalar, and vector diquarks, and finally solved the bound-state equations for baryons with quantum numbers \(J^P=1/2^\pm\) and \(3/2^\pm\). The three-body Faddeev equation was solved using an Arnoldi algorithm for eigenvalue extraction, while the quark-diquark equation allowed a full QR decomposition to access the complete spectrum below about 2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Mass of the first radial excitation of the nucleon (Roper) from the three-body framework: \(m_{N^*} = 1.45(5)\) GeV, close to the experimental \(N(1440)\).
- Mass of the first radial excitation of the \(\Delta\) from the three-body framework: \(m_{\Delta^*} = 1.49(6)\) GeV, agreeing with the lower edge of the PDG range for the \(\Delta(1600)\).
- Mass of the Roper from the quark-diquark approach: \(m_{N^*} = 1.50(9)\) GeV.
- Mass of the excited \(\Delta\) from the quark-diquark approach: \(m_{\Delta^*} = 1.73(12)\) GeV.
- The parity partner of the nucleon is underestimated by 20% in rainbow-ladder, leading to the wrong level ordering between the Roper and the \(N(1535)\).
- The factor \(c \approx 0.35\) was used to adjust the interaction strength in "bad" meson and diquark channels, gauged by the vector/axialvector parity partner splitting in the meson sector.

## Context

This work extends previous Dyson-Schwinger and Faddeev equation studies of baryons (Eichmann 2009, 2011; Sanchis-Alepuz 2011, 2013) by including excited states and systematically comparing the three-body Faddeev framework with the quark-diquark approximation. It also builds on earlier quark-diquark studies using momentum-independent interactions (Roberts 2011; Chen 2012) and momentum-dependent model ansatze (Segovia 2015). The results confirm that the quark-diquark approximation captures the essential dynamics of the three-body system for the channels studied, and they enable future work to incorporate beyond-rainbow-ladder corrections to improve the description of parity partners and other states where the vector-vector interaction misrepresents spin-dependent forces.
