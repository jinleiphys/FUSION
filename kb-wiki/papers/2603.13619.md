# Computing neutrino cross sections from Euclidian responses

> Energy integrated neutrino cross sections are integrals of nuclear responses weighted with kinematic prefactors. We decompose the prefactors into a limited set of functions of energy transfer and show the relevant integrals are the moments of the responses, and integrals weighted with $1/(a+ω)^n$ with $n\leq 2$. These can be directly obtained from the Euclidean response, avoiding the need for inversion of the Laplace transform. As a proof of concept we study the procedure with toy-model responses for the quasielastic peak. We show that the different contributions can be straightforwardly organized in terms of relative importance, and how flux-averaged cross sections can be obtained. Using a realistic model for the response and numerical uncertainty we show that it is feasible to obtain the required integrals from the Euclidean response, with large uncertainties only for the third moment. Due to kinematic restrictions, the integrals contain contributions from the unphysical region for neutrino scattering, coming from high-momentum nucleons. We show that (in the absence of two-body currents) robust corrections for this contamination are obtained from the single-nucleon momentum distribution. These results present an opportunity to compute certain neutrino cross sections with ab-initio methods with controlled uncertainties.

# Computing neutrino cross sections from Euclidean responses

## Key claim

Energy-integrated neutrino cross sections at fixed momentum transfer can be expressed as a small set of energy-weighted integrals of nuclear responses, specifically moments and integrals weighted with $1/(a+\omega)^n$ for $n \leq 2$, which can be extracted directly from Euclidean (imaginary-time) responses without inverting the Laplace transform, provided that contamination from the unphysical region ($\omega > q$) is corrected using the single-nucleon momentum distribution and that two-body currents are absent.

## Method

The kinematic prefactors in the charged-current cross section for massless leptons are decomposed into a linear combination of at most five functions of energy transfer $\omega$: powers $\omega^n$ for $n \in \{0,1,2\}$ and inverse powers $1/(E_f+\omega)^n$ for $n \in \{1,2\}$. The corresponding weighted integrals of the nuclear responses are then obtained directly from the Euclidean response $E_i(\tau,q)$: the moments are given by derivatives at $\tau=0$, and the $E_f$-dependent integrals are computed as $\int_0^\infty d\tau \, E_i(\tau) e^{-E_f\tau}$ and $\int_0^\infty d\tau \, E_i(\tau) \tau e^{-E_f\tau}$. The method is validated with a Gaussian toy-model response for the quasielastic peak and then tested with realistic plane-wave impulse approximation responses using a Variational Monte Carlo spectral function for $^{12}$C, including numerical uncertainties representative of Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations.

## Key numbers

- For fixed incoming energy at $q=300$ MeV, including contributions up to the second moment reproduces the total cross section with accuracy better than 5% over the whole energy range; at $q=600$ MeV the accuracy is better than 10%.
- For the energy-averaged cross section at $q=500$ MeV, the contribution from the second moment is negligible due to $(q/2M)^2$ and $(q/2M)^4$ suppression and partial cancellation between $LL$ and $T$ contributions.
- For the MiniBooNE flux-averaged cross section at $q=500$ MeV, discarding the $\omega^2$ terms leads to a discrepancy smaller than 5% with the full result.
- The third moment of the response is obtained with approximately 3–5% accuracy from the Euclidean response, while the fourth moment is not required if $\omega^2$ terms are dropped.

## Context

This work provides a complementary perspective to the standard approach of inverting Laplace transforms (e.g., maximum-entropy methods used in GFMC calculations by Lovato (2016) and Lovato (2017)) or Lorentz Integral Transforms (LIT) used in coupled-cluster and hyperspherical-harmonics approaches by Efros (1994) and Leidemann (2015). Recent machine-learning-based inversion methods by Raghavan (2021, 2024) and Chebyshev-polynomial reconstructions by Sobczyk (2021) address the ill-posed inversion problem, but the present work avoids inversion entirely for integrated observables. The method enables direct computation of certain neutrino cross sections from ab-initio Euclidean responses with controlled uncertainty propagation, provided two-body currents are absent and corrections for the unphysical region ($\omega > q$) are applied using the single-nucleon momentum distribution.
