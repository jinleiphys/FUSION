# Pairing correlations in nuclei on the neutron-drip line

> Paring correlations in weakly bound nuclei on the edge of neutron drip line is studied by using a three-body model. A density-dependent contact interaction is employed to calculate the ground state of halo nuclei $^{6}$He and $^{11}$Li, as well as a skin nucleus $^{24}$O. Dipole excitations in these nuclei are also studied within the same model. We point out that the di-neutron type correlation plays a dominant role in the halo nuclei $^{6}$He and $^{11}$Li having the coupled spin of the two neutrons $S$=0, while the correlation similar to the BCS type is important in $^{24}$O. Contributions of the spin $S$=1 and S=0 configurations are separately discussed in the low energy dipole excitations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that di-neutron type correlations (coupled spin S=0) dominate in the halo nuclei ⁶He and ¹¹Li, while a BCS-type correlation is more important in the skin nucleus ²⁴O; however, this distinction depends on the specific two-neutron separation energies and the dominant single-particle configurations of each nucleus.

## Method

The authors used a three-body model (core plus two valence neutrons) with a density-dependent contact delta interaction between the valence neutrons and a Woods-Saxon potential for the core-neutron interaction. The Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a discretized single-particle basis with an energy cutoff, and the interaction parameters were adjusted to reproduce the known two-neutron separation energy of each nucleus. The ground-state two-particle density and the dipole response (B(E1) strength) were calculated and decomposed into S=0 and S=1 spin components in the LS-coupling scheme.

## Key numbers

- For ⁶He: two-neutron separation energy S₂ₙ = 0.975 MeV; mean square distance between valence neutrons ⟨r_nn²⟩ = 21.3 fm²; mean square distance of center of mass from core ⟨r_c-2n²⟩ = 13.2 fm²; dominant configuration (p₃/₂)² at 83.0% fraction.
- For ¹¹Li: S₂ₙ = 0.295 MeV; ⟨r_nn²⟩ = 41.4 fm²; ⟨r_c-2n²⟩ = 26.3 fm²; dominant configuration (p₁/₂)² at 59.1% fraction.
- For ²⁴O: S₂ₙ = 6.452 MeV; ⟨r_nn²⟩ = 35.2 fm²; ⟨r_c-2n²⟩ = 10.97 fm²; dominant configuration (s₁/₂)² at 93.6% fraction.
- Total B(E1) strength: 1.31 e² fm² for ⁶He, 1.76 e² fm² for ¹¹Li, and 0.97 e² fm² for ²⁴O.
- Peak energies in B(E1) distribution: 1.55 MeV for ⁶He, 0.66 MeV for ¹¹Li, and 4.78 MeV for ²⁴O.
- Expectation value of angle between valence neutrons: 66.33 degrees for ⁶He, 65.29 degrees for ¹¹Li, and 82.37 degrees for ²⁴O.
- Calculated rms radius for ²⁴O: 4.45 fm, compared to 4.65 fm for the bound 2s₁/₂ state.

## Context

This work extends the three-body model with density-dependent delta interaction developed by Bertsch and Esbensen (1991, 1992, 1993) to study pairing correlations in neutron-drip-line nuclei. It contrasts the di-neutron correlation in borromean halo nuclei (⁶He and ¹¹Li) with the BCS-type correlation in the skin nucleus ²⁴O, and compares the calculated dipole response to recent Coulomb breakup experiments for ¹¹Li by Nakamura and collaborators (2005). The results enable further experimental studies of dipole excitations in ²⁴O to test the predicted difference in correlation types.
