# Collective effects in microscopic transport models

> We give a reminder on the major inputs of microscopic hadronic transport models and on the physics aims when describing various aspects of relativistic heavy ion collisions at SPS energies. We then first stress that the situation of particle ratios being reproduced by a statistical description does not necessarily mean a clear hint for the existence of a fully isotropic momentum distribution at hadrochemical freeze-out. Second, a short discussion on the status of strangeness production is given. Third we demonstrate the importance of a new collective mechanism for producing (strange) antibaryons within a hadronic description, which guarantees sufficiently fast chemical equilibration.

# Collective effects in microscopic transport models

## Key claim
The successful reproduction of particle ratios by a statistical (thermal) model does not necessarily prove the existence of a fully isotropic momentum distribution at hadrochemical freeze-out; a generalized statistical distribution allowing for momentum anisotropy can fit the same ratios equally well, with the largest deviation being only about 6% for the p/π+ ratio in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS.

## Method
The paper reviews microscopic hadronic transport models (RQMD, UrQMD, HSD, RBUU, QGSM) and their application to relativistic heavy ion collisions at SPS energies. It then presents a generalized statistical density operator that includes a momentum anisotropy parameter v, and calculates particle ratios from this distribution for comparison with standard thermal fits. Finally, it performs rate calculations for an expanding hadronic resonance gas to study the production of (strange) antibaryons via multi-mesonic fusion reactions required by detailed balance.

## Key numbers
- The largest deviation in particle ratios when varying the velocity parameter v from 0 to 0.4 c is about 6% for the p/π+ ratio in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS.
- The chemical equilibration time for kaons in a static box exceeds 40 fm/c for all equilibrium energy densities up to 2-3 GeV/fm³.
- At AGS to SPS energies, the K+/π+ ratio starts at approximately 6-8% after primary string fragmentation before hadronic rescattering.
- Strangeness production is underestimated by approximately 30% compared to explicit kaon data at lower bombarding energies in the HSD model.
- For the antihyperon production, a rapid expansion can increase the final yield by a factor of 2 to 3 compared to a slow expansion.
- The calculated ratios from the multi-mesonic fusion model give Ξ̅⁺/(Λ̅ + Σ̅⁰) ≈ 0.22 and Ω̅⁺/Ξ̅⁺ ≈ 0.35, in good agreement with WA97 data (0.188 ± 0.016 and 0.281 ± 0.053 respectively).
- The absolute yield of Λ̅ at midrapidity from WA97 is about 1.8 ± 0.2 and from NA49 about 1.5 in one unit of rapidity.
- The net baryon rapidity distribution dNB/dy ≈ 80 gives NΛ̅/NB ≈ 0.0094, corresponding to S/A slightly larger than 40.

## Context
This work extends the long-standing debate between statistical/thermal models (Braun-Munzinger et al., 1996) and microscopic transport approaches regarding the degree of thermalization in heavy ion collisions. It directly addresses the paradox that thermal models successfully describe particle ratios while transport models do not support complete local momentum equilibration (Bravina et al., 2000; Bass et al., 2000). The new collective mechanism for antibaryon production via multi-mesonic fusion resolves the previous difficulties in describing antihyperon yields within hadronic transport schemes (Sorge, 1995; Soff, 1998) and demonstrates that chemical saturation of rare antibaryons can occur in a hadronic gas without requiring a quark-gluon plasma. This enables future implementation of multi-particle interactions in transport codes, with initial steps by Cassing (2002) showing promising results for antiproton production at AGS and SPS energies.
