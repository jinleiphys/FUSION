# Centrality and Transverse momentum dependencies of Hadrons in Xe+Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.44$ TeV from a multi-phase transport model

> In this paper, we study and predict the charged-particle pseudorapidity multiplicity density ($\frac{dN_{ch}}{dη}$), transverse momentum spectra of identified particles and their ratios in relativistic heavy ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), using the string-melting version of a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model with improved quark coalescence method. Results of the charged-particle pseudorapidity multiplicity density from AMPT model calculations for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV are compared with the experimental data. Good agreements are generally found between theoretical calculations and experimental data. We predict $\frac{dN_{ch}}{dη}$ for Xe+Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.44$ TeV at different centralities, and $p_T$ spectra of charged pions, kaons and protons, and their ratios $K/π$ and $p/π$ in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV and Xe+Xe collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.44$ TeV that are being studied at LHC. The $p_T$ spectra of identified particles in Pb+Pb collisions from the improved AMPT model are compared and found to be consistent with results from the iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model with TRENTo initial condition.

## Key claim

The improved string-melting AMPT model with a new quark coalescence parameter \(r_{BM}=0.61\) can describe charged-particle pseudorapidity multiplicity density and transverse momentum spectra in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, and is used to predict the same observables for Xe+Xe collisions at 5.44 TeV, with the caveat that the model overestimates the \(p/\pi\) ratio for peripheral (70-80%) collisions above 1.5 GeV/c.

## Method

The authors used the string-melting version of the AMPT model (Ampt-v1.31t1-v2.31t1) with an improved quark coalescence method that removes the forced conservation of baryon, antibaryon, and meson numbers per event while conserving net-baryon and net-strangeness numbers. A coalescence parameter \(r_{BM}=0.61\) controls the relative probability of a quark forming a baryon versus a meson. The Lund string fragmentation parameters were set to \(a=0.2\) and \(b=0.15\) GeV\(^{-2}\), the QCD coupling constant \(\alpha_s=0.33\), and the screening mass \(\mu=3.2\) fm\(^{-1}\) giving a parton scattering cross section of 1.5 mb; hadronic interactions were terminated at 200 fm/c.

## Key numbers

* Charged-particle pseudorapidity multiplicity density \(dN_{ch}/d\eta\) for Pb+Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV shows good agreement with ALICE data for eight centrality classes, except slightly lower at forward pseudorapidity \(\eta>4\).
* For Xe+Xe collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.44\) TeV, impact parameter ranges 0-2.87 fm and 5.72-7.01 fm correspond to centralities 0-5% and 20-30%, respectively.
* The \(p/\pi\) ratio for Pb+Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV is overestimated by the model above 1.5 GeV/c for the 70-80% centrality class.
* Transverse momentum spectra of charged pions, kaons, and protons from the AMPT model for Pb+Pb at 5.02 TeV are consistent with results from the iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model with TRENTo initial condition.

## Context

This work extends the improved AMPT model validation from Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV and Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV (He, 2017) to higher LHC energies (5.02 TeV Pb+Pb and 5.44 TeV Xe+Xe). The predictions for Xe+Xe collisions, which have a mass number roughly halfway between proton and lead, provide a test of the model's predictive power against upcoming ALICE data. The results are compared with the iEBE-VISHNU hybrid model (Zhao, 2017) and prior ALICE measurements (Adam, 2015; Adam, 2016; Acharya, 2018).
