# Functional renormalization of QCD in $1 + 1$ dimensions: four-fermion interactions from quark-gluon dynamics

> Quantum Chromodynamics in two spacetime dimensions is investigated with the Functional Renormalization Group. We use a functional formulation with covariant gauge fixing and derive Renormalization Group flow equations for the gauge coupling, quark mass and an algebraically complete set of local fermion-fermion interaction vertices. The flow, based on a convenient Callan-Symanzik-type regularization, shows the expected behavior for a super-renormalizable theory in the ultraviolet regime and leads to a strongly coupled regime in the infrared. Through a detailed discussion of symmetry implications, and variations in the gauge group and flavor numbers, the analysis sets the stage for a more detailed investigation of the bound state spectrum in future work.

## Key claim

The authors derive functional renormalization group flow equations for QCD in two spacetime dimensions, showing that a Callan-Symanzik-type regulator works in this setting and that the theory flows from a super-renormalizable ultraviolet regime to a strongly coupled infrared regime, but they explicitly state that this work only sets the stage for future investigation of the bound state spectrum and does not yet resolve the infrared dynamics fully.

## Method

The authors use the Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) with a background-field method and covariant gauge fixing to derive flow equations for the gauge coupling, quark mass, and an algebraically complete set of local four-fermion interaction vertices in two-dimensional QCD. They employ a Callan-Symanzik-type regularization scheme and analyze the flow for varying numbers of colors and flavors, including the limits of infinite color number (the 't Hooft model) and infinite flavor number, while discussing symmetry constraints from gauge invariance, BRST symmetry, and the Coleman-Mermin-Wagner-Hohenberg-Berezinskii theorem.

## Key numbers

* The gauge coupling has energy dimension [g] = E in two dimensions (from the relation [g] = E^{(4-d)/2} with d=2), making the theory super-renormalizable.
* The fermion field has energy dimension [psi] = E^{1/2} in two dimensions (from [psi] = E^{(d-1)/2} with d=2).
* The gauge field has energy dimension [A] = E in two dimensions.
* The normalization constant for the SU(Nc) generators is C(r) = 1/2 for the fundamental representation and C(r) = Nc for the adjoint representation.

## Context

This work presents the first FRG study of two-dimensional QCD, extending prior work on the 't Hooft model (t Hooft, 1974) which solved the theory analytically in the limit of infinite colors and one flavor, and connecting to studies of two-dimensional QCD at finite color and flavor numbers using non-Abelian bosonization techniques (Abdalla, 1991; Konik, 2021; Lajer, 2021). The analysis enables future application of dynamical partial bosonization techniques to resolve bound state formation in more detail than currently feasible in four-dimensional QCD, and provides a foundation for connecting FRG results to the 't Hooft solution and to quantum simulation approaches (Farrell, 2022; Ciavarella, 2023).
