# The Be7(p,gamma)B8 cross section and the properties of Be7

> We study the nonresonant part of the $^7$Be($p,γ$)$^8$B reaction using a three-cluster resonating group model that is variationally converged and virtually complete in the $^4$He+$^3$He+$p$ model space. The importance of using adequate nucleon-nucleon interaction is demonstrated. We find that the low-energy astrophysical $S$-factor is linearly correlated with the quadrupole moment of $^7$Be. A range of parameters is found where the most important $^7$Be and $^7$Li properties are reproduced simultaneously; the corresponding $S$-factor at $E_{\rm cm}=20$ keV is $24.6-26.1$ eV$\cdot$b.

## Key claim

The low-energy astrophysical S-factor for the Be7(p,gamma)B8 reaction is linearly correlated with the quadrupole moment of Be7, and the authors predict S17 in the range 24.6-26.1 eV*b at E_cm=20 keV, but this result depends on using the Minnesota effective nucleon-nucleon interaction and on simultaneously reproducing properties of Be7, Li7, and B8.

## Method

The authors performed a microscopic three-cluster resonating group model calculation in the He4+He3+p model space, using the variational Siegert method for the B8 bound state and the variational Kohn-Hulthén method for scattering states. They calculated the E1 capture cross section into the B8 ground state in perturbation theory, adjusting the exchange mixture parameter to reproduce the experimental B8 binding energy (137 keV) and the spin-orbit force strength to match the Be7 3/2- and 1/2- state splitting.

## Key numbers

- S17 at E_cm=20 keV: 24.6-26.1 eV*b (range from restricted and full model space calculations)
- Be7 quadrupole moment predicted: between -6.0 e*fm2 and -6.9 e*fm2
- B8 quadrupole moment calculated: 7.45 e*fm2 (experimental value: 6.83 +/- 0.21 e*fm2)
- Li7 quadrupole moment calculated: -4.10 e*fm2 (experimental value: -4.05 +/- 0.08 e*fm2)
- Be7 binding energy relative to He4+He3: underbound by 200 keV in the full calculation
- B8 binding energy: 137 keV (reproduced exactly by parameter adjustment)

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic cluster calculations of the Be7(p,gamma)B8 reaction by Descouvemont and Baye (1983, 1988, 1994) and Johnson et al. (1992), using a more complete three-cluster model space with six basis states for Be7 rather than one or two. The predicted S17 range does not support the smaller values (16.5-17 eV*b) suggested by Mukhamedzhanov and Timofeyuk (1990), Zubarev (1994), and Barker and Spear (1986), but is consistent with direct capture experimental results (22+/-2 eV*b and 24+/-2 eV*b). The linear correlation between S17 and the Be7 quadrupole moment suggests that a precise measurement of the Be7 quadrupole moment or radius could test the self-consistency of these predictions.
