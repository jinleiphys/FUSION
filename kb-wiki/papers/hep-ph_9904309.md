# Reply to ``Comment on `Majoron emitting neutrinoless double beta decay in the electroweak chiral gauge extensions' ''

> We demonstrate that in the process of deducing the constraint on the electroweak mixing angle $θ_{W}$ in our paper, we have indeed been working with three mass scales while implementing (331) model.

# Reply to "Comment on 'Majoron emitting neutrinoless double beta decay in the electroweak chiral gauge extensions'"

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that their original paper (Pisano and Sharma, 1998) did in fact use three distinct mass scales for scalar vacuum expectation values when deriving the constraint on the electroweak mixing angle $\theta_W$ in the SU(3)$_C$ ⊗ SU(3)$_L$ ⊗ U(1)$_X$ (331) model, contrary to the implication in the Comment by Montero et al. that only two scales were used. A typographical error in the original paper made it appear as if only two mass scales were employed, and the authors correct this by providing the intended text.

## Method

The authors re-examine the symmetry breaking hierarchy $G_{331} \rightarrow G_{321} \rightarrow$ SU(3)$_C$ ⊗ U(1)$_{\rm em}$ in the 331 model, using a scalar sector composed of SU(3) triplets ($\eta$, $\rho$, $\chi$) and a symmetric sextet ($S$) with specified vacuum expectation values (VEVs). They compute the masses of the charged and neutral gauge bosons from the VEVs $v_\eta$, $v_\rho$, $v_{\sigma_1}$, $v_{\sigma_2}$, and $v_\chi$, then apply the approximation $v_\eta \approx v_\rho \approx \sqrt{2} v_{\sigma_2} \equiv v_1$ to derive expressions for $M_W$ and $M_Z$, and use the experimental $\rho$-parameter constraint to bound $v_{\sigma_1}$. The constraint on $\sin^2\theta_W$ is obtained by taking the limit $v_{\sigma_1}=0$ and $v_2 \gg v_1$ and comparing the 331 model mass ratio $M_Z^2/M_W^2$ with the standard model result.

## Key numbers

- $\rho = 0.9998 \pm 0.0008$ (experimental constraint on the $\rho$-parameter)
- $\sqrt{r} = v_{\sigma_1}/v_1 = 0.0173$, derived from the $\rho$-parameter constraint using the relation $0.9998 = (1 + \frac{2}{3}r)/(1 + \frac{4}{3}r)$
- $v_1^2 = (246/\sqrt{6})^2$ GeV$^2 \approx (100)^2$ GeV$^2$, the chosen scale for $v_1$
- $v_{\sigma_1} \leq 1.73$ GeV, the upper bound on the sextet VEV for $v_1 \sim 100$ GeV
- $\sin^2\theta_W < 1/4$, the upper bound on the electroweak mixing angle from the 331 model, with a Landau pole at $\sin^2\theta_W = 1/4$
- $v_\chi \equiv v_2$ on the TeV scale for the choice $v_1 \sim 100$ GeV, the scale needed to keep new gauge bosons sufficiently heavy

## Context

This reply addresses the Comment by Montero, de S. Pires, and Pleitez (1999), who pointed out that a majoron-like scheme in the 331 model requires at least three different mass scales for scalar vacuum expectation values. The authors clarify that their original work (Pisano and Sharma, 1998) on majoron-emitting neutrinoless double beta decay in electroweak chiral gauge extensions did employ three scales: $v_1 \sim 100$ GeV (related to SU(3)$_L$ ⊗ U(1)$_X$ symmetry breaking), $v_2$ on the TeV scale (to keep new gauge bosons heavy), and $v_{\sigma_1} \leq 1.73$ GeV (much smaller than $v_1$, consistent with the $\rho$ parameter). The existence of three scales has no bearing on the double beta decay related features discussed in the original paper. The work builds on the 331 model framework developed by Foot, Hernandez, Pisano, and Pleitez (1993).
