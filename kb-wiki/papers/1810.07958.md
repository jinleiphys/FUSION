# On the Existence of $N^*(890)$ Resonance in $S_{11}$ Channel of $πN$ Scatterings

> Low-energy partial-wave $πN$ scattering data is reexamined with the help of the production representation of partial-wave $S$ matrix, where branch cuts and poles are thoroughly under consideration. The left-hand cut contribution to the phase shift is determined, with controlled systematic error estimates, by using the results of $\mathcal{O}(p^3)$ chiral perturbative amplitudes obtained in the extended-on-mass-shell scheme. In $S_{11}$ and $P_{11}$ channels, severe discrepancies are observed between the phase shift data and the sum of all known contributions. Statistically satisfactory fits to the data can only be achieved by adding extra poles in the two channels. We find that a $S_{11}$ resonance pole locates at $\sqrt{z_{r}}=(0.895\pm0.081)-(0.164\pm0.023)i$ GeV, on the complex $s$-plane. On the other hand, a $P_{11}$ virtual pole, as an accompanying partner of the nucleon bound-state pole, locates at $\sqrt{z_{v}}=(0.966\pm0.018)$ GeV, slightly above the nucleon pole on the real axis below threshold. Physical origin of the two newly established poles is explored to the best of our knowledge. It is emphasized that the $\mathcal{O}(p^3)$ calculation greatly improves the fit quality comparing with the previous $\mathcal{O}(p^2)$ one.

## Key claim

The paper claims the existence of a previously unknown resonance, \(N^*(890)\), in the \(S_{11}\) channel of \(\pi N\) scattering, and a virtual pole in the \(P_{11}\) channel, based on a reanalysis of low-energy partial-wave data. The claim is hedged by the condition that statistically satisfactory fits to the phase shift data can only be achieved by adding these extra poles, and that the \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) chiral perturbation theory calculation greatly improves the fit quality compared to the previous \(\mathcal{O}(p^2)\) one.

## Method

The authors reexamined low-energy partial-wave \(\pi N\) scattering data using the Peking University (PKU) production representation of the partial-wave \(S\) matrix, which explicitly accounts for branch cuts and poles. The left-hand cut contribution to the phase shift was determined using \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) baryon chiral perturbation theory amplitudes in the extended-on-mass-shell scheme, with controlled systematic error estimates. By fitting the sum of known contributions (including the left-hand cuts and known poles) to the phase shift data, discrepancies in the \(S_{11}\) and \(P_{11}\) channels were identified, which could only be resolved by introducing additional poles in the \(S\) matrix.

## Key numbers

- The \(S_{11}\) resonance pole is located at \(\sqrt{z_{r}} = (0.895 \pm 0.081) - (0.164 \pm 0.023)i\) GeV on the complex \(s\)-plane.
- The \(P_{11}\) virtual pole is located at \(\sqrt{z_{v}} = (0.966 \pm 0.018)\) GeV, slightly above the nucleon pole on the real axis below threshold.
- The \(R\) parameters (interaction range of the \(\pi N\) potential) for the \(S_{11}\) and \(P_{11}\) channels are \(0.56\ m_{\pi}^{-1}\) and \(0.52\ m_{\pi}^{-1}\), respectively.

## Context

This work extends the earlier \(\mathcal{O}(p^2)\) analysis by Wang, Yao, and Zheng (2017), which suggested the existence of these hidden poles but could not achieve a good \(\chi^2\) fit for all channels. By incorporating the full structure of the left-hand cuts at \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) level, the present study provides crucial evidence supporting the existence of the \(P_{11}\) virtual pole (an accompanying partner of the nucleon bound state) and the subthreshold \(S_{11}\) resonance \(N^*(890)\). The results open a new window for studies in \(\pi N\) phenomenology and offer a natural explanation for the low-energy behavior of the phase shifts in these channels.
