# Nonflow contribution to Dihadron Azimuthal Correlations in 200 GeV/c Au+Au Collisions

> Dihadron azimuthal correlations in 200 GeV/c Au+Au have been simulated by a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model. Contribution from jet-medium interaction to correlation function is obtained by subtracting the combined harmonic flow background from the raw dihadron correlation function. Signals in centralities of 0-10%, 20-40% and 50-80% are compared in three associated transverse momentum ($p_{T}^{assoc}$) bins: 0.2-0.8 GeV/c, 0.8-1.4 GeV/c and 1.4-2.0 GeV/c. An obvious medium modification impact can be seen from the signal shape change and relative jet contribution in the above events, which shows different behaviors between central and peripheral events, and among different $p_{T}^{assoc}$ ranges in central events. More detailed $p_{T}^{assoc}$ dependence of the derived nonflow contribution is studied in central 0-10% events, in which a strong $p_{T}^{assoc}$ dependence of RMS width is observed. We also calculated that relative jet contributions in peripheral and central collisions in the above mentioned cases.

## Key claim

The paper uses the AMPT model to simulate dihadron azimuthal correlations in 200 GeV/c Au+Au collisions and isolates the nonflow (jet-medium) contribution by subtracting a combined harmonic flow background, but explicitly notes that the true background lies between two bounding formulas (upper and lower limits) rather than being uniquely determined.

## Method

The authors simulate 200 GeV/c Au+Au collisions using the string-melting version of the AMPT model with hadronic rescattering turned off. They construct raw dihadron azimuthal correlations between trigger particles (p_T > 2.5 GeV/c) and associated particles (p_T < 2.5 GeV/c) within |η| < 1.0. Harmonic flow coefficients v_n up to fifth order are computed from initial spatial geometry using the event plane method to minimize nonflow contamination. Two background formulas are used as upper and lower bounds: formula (1) multiplies v_n^trig and v_n^assoc before event averaging (including nonflow), while formula (4) multiplies event-averaged values (excluding flow fluctuations). The jet-medium signal is obtained by subtracting each background from the raw correlation.

## Key numbers

- Trigger particle p_T cut: > 2.5 GeV/c; associated particle p_T bins: 0.2-0.8, 0.8-1.4, and 1.4-2.0 GeV/c
- Centrality bins studied: 0-10%, 20-40%, and 50-80%
- Near-side jet relative contribution in 0-10% central events: 0.7%-1.8% for p_T^assoc 0.2-0.8 GeV/c, 1.1%-3.5% for 0.8-1.4 GeV/c, 1.6%-5.7% for 1.4-2.0 GeV/c (ranges from upper/lower background bounds)
- Away-side jet relative contribution in 0-10% central events: 1.3%-3.1% for p_T^assoc 0.2-0.8 GeV/c, 1.5%-4.4% for 0.8-1.4 GeV/c, 0.5%-3.4% for 1.4-2.0 GeV/c
- Away-side jet relative contribution in 0-10% central events is less than 5% overall
- For the most peripheral 50-80% centrality, away-side jet relative contribution reaches 8.2%-16.2% in the highest p_T^assoc bin (1.4-2.0 GeV/c)
- ZYAM normalization adjustment differs from theoretical normalization by less than 3%

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dihadron correlations at RHIC (Ko, 2008; GL, 2011) that showed odd harmonic flows like v_3 from initial geometry fluctuations can contribute to the away-side double peak structure. The paper uses the AMPT model (Lin et al., 2005) to systematically study the p_T^assoc and centrality dependence of the nonflow contribution, providing upper and lower bounds rather than a single background value. The results enable more precise experimental extraction of jet-medium interaction signals by quantifying the small (less than 5% in central collisions) jet contribution that must be separated from the dominant harmonic flow background.
