# Deuteron yields near the QCD phase transition

> We investigate the influence of QCD phase transition and critical fluctuations of the critical end point (CEP) on the deuteron yield within the functional renormalization group (fRG) approach, by using the nucleon coalescence model and a low energy effective field theory of quarks and mesons. It is found that the two-point baryon density correlation function is enhanced in a narrow region radiated from the CEP along the phase boundary. The deuteron yield arising from the two-point baryon correlation is small compared to the leading-order contribution, which is attributed to the fact that in the regime of low collision energy, i.e., the region of large baryon chemical potential, the freeze-out curves deviate from the critical region, resulting in that the enhancement of the deuteron yield stemming from the critical fluctuations near the CEP is mild.

## Key claim

The deuteron yield arising from the two-point baryon density correlation near the QCD critical end point (CEP) is small compared to the leading-order coalescence contribution, because at low collision energies (large baryon chemical potential) the chemical freeze-out curves bend away from the critical region, making the critical-fluctuation enhancement of the deuteron yield only mild.

## Method

The authors combine the nucleon coalescence model with a two-flavor quark-meson low-energy effective field theory solved via the functional renormalization group (fRG) approach. They compute the equal-time two-point baryon density correlation function from its flow equation, decompose it into a one-particle-irreducible vector-vector vertex and a sigma-propagator-mediated term, and then evaluate the deuteron yield contribution from this correlation using the coalescence model formulas. The calculations are performed on the QCD phase boundary and on three representative chemical freeze-out curves from the literature.

## Key numbers

* The CEP in this low-energy effective theory is located at baryon chemical potential \(\mu_{B_{\mathrm{CEP}}} = 643\) MeV and temperature \(T_{\mathrm{CEP}} = 98\) MeV.
* The deuteron width parameter is \(\sigma_d = \sqrt{3/4} \, r_d \approx 2.26\) fm, where \(r_d\) is the deuteron root-mean-square radius.
* The ratio \(N_d/N_p\) as a function of collision energy \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) shows a turnover between \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\) and 10 GeV.
* The enhancement factor from resonance decays converting chemical to kinetic proton yields is taken as \(R = 3\) for all collision energies.
* The two-point baryon density correlation at vanishing momentum, \(C_2(\tau=0,\bm{p}=0)\), is compared to the quadratic baryon number fluctuation \(\chi_2^B\) from earlier fRG work (Fu 2015), showing qualitative consistency.

## Context

This work extends the study of critical fluctuations near the QCD critical end point from conventional conserved-charge fluctuations (e.g., net-baryon number, as in Fu 2015, Fu 2021, STAR 2020-2025) to the production of light nuclei, specifically deuterons. It builds on the nucleon coalescence model of Sato (1981), Mrowczynski (1987), Scheibl (1998), and Chen (2003), and on the relation between deuteron yields and baryon density correlations developed by Sun (2020). The fRG treatment of the QCD phase diagram follows the approach of Fu (2019, 2023) and Gao (2020). The results enable future experimental searches for CEP signals using deuteron-to-proton ratios as a complementary observable to net-proton cumulants, particularly in the Beam Energy Scan program.
