# Nucleon partonic spin structure to be explored by the unpolarized Drell-Yan program of COMPASS experiment at CERN

> The observation of the violation of Lam-Tung relation in the $πN$ Drell-Yan process triggered many theoretical speculations. The TMD Boer-Mulders functions characterizing the correlation of transverse momentum and transverse spin for partons in unpolarized hadrons could nicely account for the violation. The COMPASS experiment at CERN will measure the angular distributions of dimuons from the unpolarized Drell-Yan process over a wide kinematic region and study the beam particle dependence. Significant statistics is expected from a successful run in 2015 which will bring further understanding of the origin of the violation of Lam-Tung relation and of the partonic transverse spin structure of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The violation of the Lam-Tung relation in the πN Drell-Yan process can be interpreted by TMD Boer-Mulders functions, but a comprehensive measurement with different beam types (π±, p, K±, p̄) over a wide kinematic range is required to distinguish this explanation from other proposed mechanisms such as QCD chromo-magnetic effects or Glauber gluon effects.

## Method

The COMPASS experiment at CERN uses a 190-GeV secondary hadron beam (97% π−, 2.5% K−, 1% p̄ for negative charge; 75% p, 24% π+ for positive charge) directed at nuclear targets (NH3, Al, W) inside a hadron absorber. A two-stage spectrometer with 40% overall acceptance detects dimuon pairs from the Drell-Yan process, and a CEDAR detector identifies the incident beam particle type. The angular distributions of decay muons are analyzed to extract the parameters λ, μ, and ν as functions of the dimuon transverse momentum pT.

## Key numbers

- Expected statistics for unpolarized high-mass (4 < Mμμ < 9 GeV) Drell-Yan events in a 140-day beam time: 800k for π-induced, 10k for K-induced, and 6k for p̄-induced events
- The π− beam flux is 108/s
- The COMPASS experiment supersedes the past E615 experiment by one order of magnitude in overall acceptance of 40%
- About 6000 J/ψ events were reconstructed from a 3-day test run in 2009
- The negatively-charged hadron beam at 190 GeV/c is composed of 97% π−, 2.5% K−, and 1% p̄; the positively-charged beam is 75% p and 24% π+

## Context

This work extends the measurements of Lam-Tung relation violation first observed by NA3 (1980s) and confirmed with better statistics by NA10 and E615 experiments in πN Drell-Yan processes. The COMPASS program will increase existing statistics of π, K, and p̄-induced Drell-Yan by a factor of 10 compared to prior experiments (NA3, NA10, E537, E615). The measurement enables discrimination between the Boer-Mulders function interpretation, the QCD chromo-magnetic effect proposed by Brandenburg et al., and the Glauber gluon mechanism, and complements the COMPASS polarized Drell-Yan program studying Sivers functions.
