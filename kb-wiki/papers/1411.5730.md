# Extension of time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations

> An extension of the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory (ETDHFB) which includes higher-order effects such as screening of the pairing correlation is proposed. ETDHFB is applied to a fermion system trapped in a harmonic potential to test its feasability by comparison with the exact solution. With the use of perturbative expressions for the pairing tensor and the two-body density matrix derived from ETDHFB, the screening effect is investigated for atomic fermion systems and isotopes of tin nuclei. It is found that the screening effect on the pairing correlation is not significant.

## Key claim

The proposed extension of time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory (ETDHFB) includes higher-order effects such as screening of the pairing correlation, but the screening effect on the pairing correlation is found to be not significant when applied to trapped fermion systems and tin isotopes.

## Method

The authors formulate ETDHFB using a truncation scheme similar to time-dependent density-matrix theory (TDDM), where higher-order reduced density matrices are approximated by lower-order ones to close the BBGKY hierarchy. They derive perturbative expressions for the pairing tensor and two-body correlation matrix from the ETDHFB equations, then apply these to a six-fermion system in a harmonic potential for comparison with exact diagonalization, and to isotopes of tin nuclei using a contact interaction.

## Key numbers

- For the trapped six-fermion system with harmonic oscillator frequency ω, the total energy is calculated as a function of χ = |g|/ħωξ³ (where ξ is the oscillator length); χ = 5 corresponds to |g| ≈ 400 MeV·fm³, similar to the strength of commonly used pairing interactions for nuclei.
- The adiabatic method for obtaining the ETDHFB ground state uses T = 4 × 2π/ω to suppress oscillating components.
- For the trapped fermions at χ = 5, the pairing energy in ETDHFB is slightly increased from the HFB value, while the perturbative approach with self-energy correction gives a slight decrease of the pairing energy.
- The correlation energy calculated from the 2p-2h/2h-2p excitation term (Eq. pertc) gives a significant contribution to the total energy, in agreement with results for the pairing Hamiltonian.

## Context

This work extends the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory to include higher-order effects, building on the TDDM approach developed by Wang and Cassing (1990s) and Gong and Tohyama (1990s) for normal-fluid systems. The screening effect studied here relates to the Gorkov-Melik-Barkhudarov correction, which has been shown to suppress pairing in neutron matter (Schulze 2001, Cao 2006, Baldo 2008) and dilute Fermi gases (Gorkov 1961, Heiselberg 2000, Tanizaki 2013), while studies for finite nuclei treating medium polarization as low-lying vibrations give opposite results (Barranco 1999). The ETDHFB approach enables a consistent microscopic treatment of various higher-order effects on the same footing, which previous contradictory results suggested was necessary.
