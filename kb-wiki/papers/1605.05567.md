# Structure and decay pattern of linear-chain state in 14C

> The linear-chain states of $^{14}$C are theoretically investigated by using the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. The calculated excitation energies and the $α$ decay widths of the linear-chain states were compared with the observed data reported by the recent experiments. The properties of the positive-parity linear-chain states reasonably agree with the observation, that convinces us of the linear-chain formation in the positive-parity states. On the other hand, in the negative-parity states, it is found that the linear-chain configuration is fragmented into many states and do not form a single rotational band. As a further evidence of the linear-chain formation, we focus on the $α$ decay pattern. It is shown that the linear-chain states decay to the excited states of daughter nucleus $^{10}{\rm Be}$ as well as to the ground state, while other cluster states dominantly decay into the ground state. Hence, we regard that this characteristic decay pattern is a strong signature of the linear-chain formation.

## Key claim

The positive-parity linear-chain states in 14C are convincingly identified by comparing calculated excitation energies and α decay widths with recent experimental resonances, but the negative-parity linear-chain configuration is fragmented into many states and does not form a single rotational band, so the paper does not support linear-chain formation in negative parity.

## Method

The authors performed antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations using the Gogny D1S effective interaction, which reproduces threshold energies in 14C within 1 MeV error. They applied constraint variational calculations with quadrupole deformation parameters β and γ as generator coordinates, followed by generator coordinate method (GCM) calculations to obtain the eigenstates of total angular momentum. The α decay widths were estimated from the GCM wave functions using the reduced width amplitude (RWA) approximated by the overlap with Brink-Bloch wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The ground state binding energy is calculated as -106.3 MeV, close to the observed value of -105.3 MeV
- The π-bond linear-chain band head (0+4 state) lies at 14.64 MeV excitation energy, close to the α threshold
- The σ-bond linear-chain band head (0+7 state) lies at 22.16 MeV excitation energy, about 9.18 MeV above the α threshold
- In-band B(E2) strengths for the π-bond linear chain: 165.5 e²fm⁴ (2+5→0+4), 257.4 e²fm⁴ (4+5→2+5), and 276.5 e²fm⁴ (6+2→4+5)
- In-band B(E2) strengths for the σ-bond linear chain: 441.9 e²fm⁴ (2+10→0+7) and 655.9 e²fm⁴ (4+11→2+10)
- The π-bond linear-chain 0+4 state has 0.87 overlap with the intrinsic linear-chain configuration shown in Fig. 3(c)
- The σ-bond linear-chain 0+7 state has 0.99 overlap with its intrinsic configuration shown in Fig. 3(d)

## Context

This work extends prior AMD calculations by Suhara et al. (2010) that predicted linear-chain states in 14C, but uses the Gogny D1S interaction instead of the Volkov interaction to better reproduce threshold energies. The calculated positive-parity π-bond linear-chain band agrees with resonances observed in 4He+10Be resonant scattering by Freer et al. (2014) and Fritsch et al. (2016), while the candidate reported by von Oertzen et al. (2004) at about 5 MeV lower energy is reassigned to the triangular band. The characteristic decay pattern where linear-chain states decay to excited states of 10Be as well as its ground state, unlike other cluster states that decay dominantly to the ground state, is proposed as a strong signature for experimental confirmation of linear-chain formation.
