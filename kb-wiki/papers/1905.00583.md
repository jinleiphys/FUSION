# Investigate the pentaquark resonance in the $NK$ system

> A dynamical calculation of pentaquark systems with quark contents $uudd\bar{s}$ is performed in the framework of quark delocalization color screening model with the help of resonating group method. The effective potentials between baryon and meson clusters are given, and the possible bound states or resonances are investigated. The single calculations show that the $NK^{*}$ with $I=0, J^{P}=\frac{1}{2}^{-}$, $ΔK^{*}$ with $I=1, J^{P}=\frac{1}{2}^{-}$, and $ΔK^{*}$ with $I=2, J^{P}=\frac{3}{2}^{-}$ are all bound, but they all turns into scattering states by coupling with the corresponding open channels. A possible resonance state $ΔK^{*}$ with $I=1, J^{P}=\frac{5}{2}^{-}$ is proposed. The mass is around $2110.5$ MeV, and the decay modes are $NK$ in $D$-wave or $NKππ$ in $P$-waves.

# Investigate the pentaquark resonance in the $NK$ system

## Key claim
A possible resonance state $\Delta K^{*}$ with $I=1, J^{P}=\frac{5}{2}^{-}$ is proposed, with mass around 2110.5 MeV, but the authors note that the present calculation only considers $S$-wave channels and that coupling to the $D$-wave $\Delta K$ channel through tensor interaction is expected to turn this bound state into a resonance with a decay width of several MeV.

## Method
The authors performed dynamical calculations of pentaquark systems with quark content $uudd\bar{s}$ using the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) combined with the resonating group method (RGM). Effective potentials between baryon and meson clusters were calculated for all channels with $I=0,1,2$ and $J^{P}=\frac{1}{2}^{-},\frac{3}{2}^{-},\frac{5}{2}^{-}$, followed by bound state calculations for single channels and with channel coupling. Scattering phase shifts of open channels were then computed via RGM to identify resonance states.

## Key numbers
- $NK^{*}$ with $I=0, J^{P}=\frac{1}{2}^{-}$: single-channel binding energy of -62.3 MeV (mass 1768.7 MeV), but becomes a scattering state after channel coupling.
- $\Delta K^{*}$ with $I=1, J^{P}=\frac{1}{2}^{-}$: single-channel binding energy of -68.1 MeV (mass 2055.9 MeV), but becomes a scattering state after channel coupling.
- $\Delta K^{*}$ with $I=2, J^{P}=\frac{3}{2}^{-}$: single-channel binding energy of -10.2 MeV (mass 2113.8 MeV), but becomes a scattering state after channel coupling.
- $\Delta K^{*}$ with $I=1, J^{P}=\frac{5}{2}^{-}$: single-channel binding energy of -13.5 MeV, with proposed mass around 2110.5 MeV; this is the only state that remains bound after channel coupling in the $S$-wave calculation.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of hidden-strange pentaquarks using the QDCSM, following earlier work on $N\phi$ bound states by Gao et al. (2001) and on $\Sigma K$, $\Sigma K^{*}$, and $\Sigma^{*} K^{*}$ molecular pentaquarks by the same group (Huang et al., referenced as pc_like). The study also connects to investigations of the $NK$ system by Hiyama (using the Isgur-Karl quark model) and by Barnes and Swanson (using the quark-Born-diagram method), where no resonance was found in the $NK$ scattering phase shifts. The proposed $\Delta K^{*}$ state with $I=1, J^{P}=\frac{5}{2}^{-}$ at 2110.5 MeV provides a candidate for future experimental searches in the strange sector, analogous to the hidden-charm pentaquarks $P_{c}^{+}(4380)$ and $P_{c}^{+}(4450)$ observed by LHCb. The authors identify the next step as including $D$-wave $\Delta K$ channel coupling via tensor interaction to obtain a realistic decay width.
