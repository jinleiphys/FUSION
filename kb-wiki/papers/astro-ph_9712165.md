# The influence of medium effects on the gross structure of hybrid stars

> We investigate the influence of medium effects on the structure of hybrid stars, i.e. neutron stars possessing a quark matter core. We found that medium effects reduce the extent of a pure quark matter phase in the interior of a hybrid star significantly in favor of a mixed phase of quark and hadronic matter. Over a wide range of the strong coupling constant - which parameterizes the influence of medium effects - quark matter is able to exist at least in a mixed phase in the interior of neutron stars.

## Key claim

Medium effects in quark matter, parameterized by the strong coupling constant, significantly reduce the extent of a pure quark matter phase inside hybrid stars in favor of a mixed phase of quark and hadronic matter, but over a wide range of the coupling constant (up to approximately g=3) quark matter can still exist at least in a mixed phase in the interior of neutron stars.

## Method

The authors constructed equations of state for hybrid stars by combining a hadronic phase described by the nonlinear Walecka model (including nucleons, hyperons, electrons, and muons) with a quark phase described by the effective mass bag model, where quarks acquire density-dependent effective masses from the hard dense loop approximation. The phase transition between hadronic and quark matter was constructed using Glendenning's Gibbs condition for mechanical and chemical equilibrium, allowing for a mixed phase with global charge neutrality. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation was then integrated to obtain mass-radius relations for non-rotating hybrid stars.

## Key numbers

- Bag constant chosen as B^(1/4) = 165 MeV (B ≈ 96 MeV/fm^3)
- For the free case (g=0), the energy per baryon of strange quark matter is (E/A)_QP ≈ 990 MeV, above the energy per nucleon in ^56Fe
- The onset of the phase transition from hadronic to mixed phase shifts from ε ≈ ε_0 (ε_0 = 140 MeV/fm^3) at g=0 to ε ≈ 2 ε_0 at g=3
- The onset of the pure quark phase shifts from ε ≈ 4 ε_0 at g=0 to ε ≈ 15 ε_0 at g=3
- For a canonical 1.4 M_⊙ star, the pure quark core radius shrinks from R ≈ 6 km at g=0 to R ≈ 3 km at g=1.5, and vanishes completely at g=2
- The limiting mass increases from approximately 1.4 M_⊙ at g=0 to approximately 1.55 M_⊙ at g=2
- The running coupling constant yields g ≈ 2.6-2.3 for Λ = 100 MeV and g ≈ 3.1-2.9 for Λ = 300 MeV over the relevant chemical potential range (μ ≈ 300-500 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the effective mass bag model for strange quark matter developed by Schertler (1997) by applying it to the structure of hybrid stars. It builds on the pioneering work of Glendenning (1992) on mixed phases in neutron stars and the relativistic mean-field models for hadronic matter used by Glendenning (1982) and others. The results show that medium effects transform pure quark cores into mixed phase cores over a wide range of coupling constants, which has implications for observable signatures such as pulsar timing structure proposed by Glendenning, Pei, and Weber (1997). Future work is needed to study the dependence on the hadronic equation of state and the bag constant.
