# Core momentum distribution in two-neutron halo nuclei

> The core momentum distribution of a weakly-bound neutron-neutron-core exotic nucleus is computed within a renormalized zero-range three-body model, with interactions in the s-wave channel. The halo wave-function in momentum space is obtained by using as inputs the two-body scattering lengths and the two-neutron separation energy. The core momentum densities are computed for $^{11}$Li, $^{14}$Be $^{20}$C and $^{22}$C. The model describes the experimental data for $^{11}$Li, $^{14}$Be and to some extend $^{20}$C. The recoil momentum distribution of the $^{20}$C from the breakup of $^{22}$C nucleus is computed for different two-neutron separation energies, and from the comparison with recent experimental data the two-neutron separation energy is estimated in the range $100\lesssim S_{2n}\lesssim 400$ KeV. The recoil momentum distribution depends weakly on the neutron-$^{20}$C scattering length, while the matter radius is strongly sensitive to it. The expected universality of the momentum distribution width is verified by also considering excited states for the system.

# Core momentum distribution in two-neutron halo nuclei

## Key claim
The renormalized zero-range three-body model, using only two-body scattering lengths and the two-neutron separation energy as inputs, can describe the core recoil momentum distributions for $^{11}$Li and $^{14}$Be, and to some extent $^{20}$C; for $^{22}$C, the model constrains the two-neutron separation energy to the range $100 \lesssim S_{2n} \lesssim 400$ keV, but this estimate depends on the poorly-known neutron-$^{20}$C scattering length and the matter radius, and the authors suggest that existing matter radius and virtual-state energy data may be overestimated.

## Method
The authors solved coupled subtracted Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian integral equations for the spectator functions $\chi_{nn}(q)$ and $\chi_{nc}(q)$ within a renormalized zero-range three-body model for the $n-n-c$ system, with $s$-wave interactions only. The core momentum distribution $n(q_c)$ was obtained from the three-body wave function in momentum space, using as inputs the two-body scattering lengths (or virtual-state energies) and the two-neutron separation energy $S_{2n}$. The model was applied to $^{11}$Li, $^{14}$Be, $^{20}$C, and $^{22}$C, with the theoretical distributions folded with experimental resolution and added to a wide Gaussian component to account for inner halo contributions.

## Key numbers
- For $^{11}$Li: the narrow component of the core momentum distribution has $\sigma = 22$ MeV/c (computed), compared to the experimental narrow component $\sigma = 21(3)$ MeV/c from Tanihata et al. (1996); the wide experimental component has $\sigma = 80$ MeV/c.
- For $^{14}$Be: the experimental full width at half maximum (FWHM) is $92.7 \pm 2.7$ MeV/c from Zahar et al. (1993), with $S_{2n} = 1.337$ MeV.
- For $^{22}$C: the two-neutron separation energy is estimated in the range $100 \lesssim S_{2n} \lesssim 400$ keV from comparison with the experimental core recoil momentum distribution of Kobayashi et al. (2012).
- For $^{22}$C: the root-mean-square matter radius is $5.4 \pm 0.9$ fm from Tanaka et al. (2010); the model predicts matter radii between 3.5 and 4.5 fm for $S_{2n}$ in the range 100-400 keV, depending on the $n-^{20}$C virtual-state energy.
- The $n-n$ singlet virtual-state energy is fixed at 143 keV throughout.
- For $^{20}$C: $S_{2n} = 3.5$ MeV and the one-neutron separation energy of $^{19}$C is $S_{1n} = 580$ keV.

## Context
This work extends the renormalized zero-range three-body model previously applied to halo nuclei by Frederico et al. (2012) and Yamashita et al. (2004, 2013), and tests the universality of the scaling function for the momentum distribution width in the Efimov limit. The analysis of $^{22}$C addresses the tension between the matter radius measurement of Tanaka et al. (2010) and the systematics-based $S_{2n}$ value of 0.42 $\pm$ 0.94 MeV from Audi et al. (2003), as well as the mass measurement of Gaudefroy et al. (2012) giving $S_{2n} = -0.14(46)$ MeV. The results enable future constraints on the $n-^{20}$C scattering length and $S_{2n}$ from combined momentum distribution and matter radius data, and suggest that independent new data on $S_{2n}$ for $^{22}$C are needed to resolve the current tension.
