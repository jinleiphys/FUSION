# Observation of Spin-Dependent Charge Symmetry Breaking in $Î›N$ Interaction: Gamma-Ray Spectroscopy of $^4_{Î›}$He

> The energy spacing between the ground-state spin doublet of $^4_Î›$He(1$^+$,0$^+$) was determined to be $1406 \pm 2 \pm 2$ keV, by measuring $Îł$ rays for the $1^+ \to 0^+$ transition with a high efficiency germanium detector array in coincidence with the $^4$He$(K^-,Ď€^-)$ $^4_Î›$He reaction at J-PARC. In comparison to the corresponding energy spacing in the mirror hypernucleus $^4_Î›$H, the present result clearly indicates the existence of charge symmetry breaking (CSB) in $Î›N$ interaction. It is also found that the CSB effect is large in the $0^+$ ground state but is by one order of magnitude smaller in the $1^+$ excited state, demonstrating that the $Î›N$ CSB interaction has spin dependence.

## Key claim
The energy spacing between the ground-state spin doublet of $^4_{\Lambda}$He($1^+,0^+$) was measured to be $1406 \pm 2 \pm 2$ keV, which when compared to the corresponding spacing in the mirror hypernucleus $^4_{\Lambda}$H ($1.09 \pm 0.02$ MeV) confirms charge symmetry breaking in the $\Lambda N$ interaction. The CSB effect is large in the $0^+$ ground state but smaller by one order of magnitude in the $1^+$ excited state, demonstrating that the $\Lambda N$ CSB interaction has spin dependence.

## Method
The $^4_{\Lambda}$He($1^+$) excited state was produced via the $^4$He$(K^-,\pi^-)$ reaction at J-PARC using a 1.5 GeV/$c$ kaon beam incident on a liquid $^4$He target. Gamma rays from the $1^+ \to 0^+$ transition were detected with the Hyperball-J germanium detector array in coincidence with the reaction products measured by the beam line spectrometer and the Superconducting Kaon Spectrometer (SKS). An event-by-event Doppler correction was applied using the measured recoil momentum of $^4_{\Lambda}$He to compensate for the Doppler broadening caused by the hypernucleus recoil velocity of $\beta = 0.03$--0.10.

## Key numbers
- $^4_{\Lambda}$He($1^+ \to 0^+$) gamma-ray transition energy: $1406 \pm 2$ (stat.) $\pm 2$ (syst.) keV
- $^4_{\Lambda}$He($1^+$) excitation energy: $1.406 \pm 0.002 \pm 0.002$ MeV (after adding 0.2 keV nuclear recoil correction)
- $^4_{\Lambda}$H($1^+,0^+$) energy spacing (weighted average of three previous measurements): $1.09 \pm 0.02$ MeV
- $\Delta B_\Lambda(0^+)$ = $B_\Lambda$($^4_{\Lambda}$He($0^+$)) $-$ $B_\Lambda$($^4_{\Lambda}$H($0^+$)) = $0.35 \pm 0.05$ MeV (from past emulsion data)
- $\Delta B_\Lambda(1^+)$ = $B_\Lambda$($^4_{\Lambda}$He($1^+$)) $-$ $B_\Lambda$($^4_{\Lambda}$H($1^+$)) = $0.03 \pm 0.05$ MeV
- $B_\Lambda$($^4_{\Lambda}$He($1^+$)) = $0.98 \pm 0.03$ MeV (combining present result with emulsion data)
- $B_\Lambda$($^4_{\Lambda}$H($1^+$)) = $0.95 \pm 0.04$ MeV (from emulsion data and gamma-ray data)
- Gamma-ray yield: $95 \pm 13$ counts
- Energy resolution: 5 keV (FWHM) at 1.4 MeV
- Total absolute photo-peak efficiency of Hyperball-J: $\sim 4\%$ for 1-MeV gamma rays

## Context
This work resolves a long-standing puzzle in hypernuclear physics regarding charge symmetry breaking in the $\Lambda N$ interaction. Previous emulsion experiments (Juric et al.) had reported a $B_\Lambda$ difference of $0.35 \pm 0.05$ MeV between $^4_{\Lambda}$He and $^4_{\Lambda}$H ground states, but theoretical calculations using models such as NSC97e (Nogga et al.) could only produce $\Delta B_\Lambda(0^+) \sim 70$ keV. The present result supersedes the only previous $^4_{\Lambda}$He gamma-ray measurement (which claimed $1.15 \pm 0.04$ MeV) and, combined with the $^4_{\Lambda}$H data, shows that the CSB effect is strongly spin-dependent. This finding supports the interpretation that $\Lambda$-$\Sigma$ mixing is responsible for the CSB effect, as the $1^+$ state receives a much smaller energy shift from $\Lambda$-$\Sigma$ mixing than the $0^+$ state, consistent with calculations by Gal using a central-force $\Lambda N$-$\Sigma N$ interaction.
