# Directed flow of $Λ$ in high-energy heavy-ion collisions and $Λ$ potential in dense nuclear matter

> We investigate the sensitivity of the $Λ$ directed flow to the $Λ$ potential in mid-central Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\approx3.0$--$30$ GeV. The $Λ$ potential obtained from the chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) is used in a microscopic transport model, a vector version of relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (RQMDv). We find that the density-dependent $Λ$ potentials, obtained from the $χ$EFT assuming weak momentum dependence of the potential, reproduce the rapidity and the beam-energy dependence of the $Λ$ directed flow measured by the STAR collaboration in the Beam Energy Scan program. Although the $Λ$ directed flow is insensitive to the density dependence of the potential, it is susceptible to the momentum dependence. We also show that a hydrodynamics picture based on the blast-wave model predicts a similarity of the proton, $Λ$, and $Ξ$ directed flows, but the directed flow of $Ω$ baryons slightly deviates from other baryons. We also show that the quark coalescence predicts different rapidity dependence of the directed flows for hyperons. These investigations suggest that measurements of a wide range of the rapidity dependence of the directed flow of hyperons may provide important information about the properties of hot and dense matter created in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The density-dependent Lambda potentials from chiral effective field theory (chiEFT), assuming weak momentum dependence, reproduce the rapidity and beam-energy dependence of Lambda directed flow measured by STAR, but the directed flow is insensitive to the density dependence of the potential while being susceptible to the momentum dependence.

## Method

The authors implemented Lambda potentials from chiEFT in a Lorentz-vector version of relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (RQMDv) within the JAM event generator, simulating mid-central Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies from 3.0 to 30 GeV per nucleon pair. They compared three types of momentum-dependent potentials (MD1, MD2, MD3) and two density-dependent potentials (GKW2 with two-body interactions only, GKW3 with three-body interactions included) against STAR data for directed flow as a function of rapidity and beam energy.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy range: sqrt(s_NN) = 3.0 to 30 GeV for Au+Au collisions
- Impact parameter range: 4.6 < b < 9.4 fm for midcentral collisions
- Saturation density rho_0 = 0.168 fm^-3 used for density normalization
- Ultraviolet momentum cutoff lambda = 500 MeV/c in the chiEFT calculation
- Optical potential at normal density and zero momentum: U_opt(rho_0, p=0) = -30 MeV for MD3 parameter set
- Optical potential at normal density and p=1 fm^-1: U_opt(rho_0, p=1 fm^-1) = -23.4 MeV for MD3
- Range parameter mu = 3.23 fm^-1 for MD1 and MD2 potentials; mu = 1.124 fm^-1 for MD3
- Taylor coefficient J_Lambda (potential at normal density): -33.83 MeV (GKW2), -29.55 MeV (GKW3)
- Taylor coefficient L_Lambda: -1.825 MeV (GKW2), 12.34 MeV (GKW3)
- Taylor coefficient K_Lambda: 356.0 MeV (GKW2), 504.8 MeV (GKW3)

## Context

This work extends the study of directed flow in heavy-ion collisions from protons to Lambda hyperons, building on prior transport model calculations by Nara et al. (JAMRQMDv, 2020) that explained proton directed flow with a purely hadronic equation of state. The authors test the strong repulsive Lambda potential predicted by chiEFT (Gerber, Kaiser, and Weise, 2020) that may resolve the hyperon puzzle in neutron-star physics, where hyperons soften the equation of state and make it difficult to explain two-solar-mass neutron stars. The results enable future measurements of hyperon directed flow over a wide rapidity range to constrain the momentum dependence of Lambda potentials in dense nuclear matter.
