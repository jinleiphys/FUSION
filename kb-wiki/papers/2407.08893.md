# Polarized Dipole Scattering Amplitudes meet the Valence Quark Model

> The recently revised small-$x$ helicity evolution, resumming the double-logarithmic factor, $α_s\ln^2(1/x)$, allows for the study of helicity distributions of quarks and gluons at small Bjorken $x$, corresponding to high center-of-mass energy. In this work, we calculate the moderate-$x$ initial conditions in the regime, $α_s \ln^2(1/x)\sim 1$, for the small-$x$ helicity evolution using a light-front valence quark model of the proton, which provides additional physical information about the target. The perturbative emission and absorption of a gluon by the valence quarks are also included. The results, given in Eqs. (35), provide a new set of initial conditions with a significantly reduced number of free parameters than conventional models. Consequently, the predictive power of small-$x$ helicity evolution is expected to improve once the initial conditions from this work are incorporated.

## Key claim

The authors calculate moderate-$x$ initial conditions for small-$x$ helicity evolution using a light-front valence quark model of the proton, reducing the number of free parameters from 24 in conventional models to 3-9, but caution that the resulting polarized dipole amplitudes are valid only at moderate $x$ and should not be directly related to parton helicity distributions via relations derived under the assumption of small $x$.

## Method

The authors model the proton as a bound state of three valence quarks using a light-front wave function with factorized momentum and spin-flavor components, including a Gaussian momentum-space wave function (the Schlumpf harmonic oscillator model). They evaluate the expectation values of polarized dipole operators (type-1 quark exchange, type-1 gluon exchange, and type-2 gluon exchange) by expanding the eikonal Wilson lines to leading order and including perturbative corrections from gluon emission and absorption at order $\mathcal{O}(\alpha_s^2)$. The calculation is performed in the mean-field large-$N_c$ and large-$N_f$ (Veneziano) limit, with explicit $N_c=3$ used for the valence quark state.

## Key numbers

- The proton model uses $\beta = 0.55$ GeV and $M = 0.26$ GeV to achieve a realistic proton charge radius of about 0.75 fm
- The initial conditions are calculated at $x \sim 0.1$, where the resummation parameter $\alpha_s \ln^2(1/x) \sim 1$
- The number of free parameters is reduced from 24 in the conventional JAM analysis (Adamiak 2023) to 3-9 depending on model flexibility
- The conventional JAM analysis achieved $\chi^2$ of 1.03 per data point using 226 data points from polarized DIS and SIDIS measurements at $0.005 \leq x \leq 0.1$ and $1.69 \text{ GeV}^2 \leq Q^2 \leq 10.4 \text{ GeV}^2$

## Context

This work extends the small-$x$ helicity evolution framework developed by Cougoulic et al. (2022) and Kovchegov et al. (2015-2021) by providing physically motivated initial conditions from a valence quark model, replacing the 24-parameter phenomenological model used in the JAM global analysis by Adamiak et al. (2023). The approach builds on previous unpolarized dipole-proton scattering calculations using the same valence quark model by Dumitru et al. (2018-2023), and differs from earlier valence quark model calculations for gluon helicity PDFs by Chen (2006) and Pasquini (2008) by focusing on the polarized dipole operators relevant to small-$x$ helicity evolution. The results enable improved predictive power for small-$x$ helicity evolution, particularly given the limited current experimental data, and will benefit from future Electron-Ion Collider measurements.
