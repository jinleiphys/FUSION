# Resolving the $R_{\rm pA}$ and $v_2$ puzzle of $D^0$ mesons in $p-$Pb collisions at the LHC

> It has been a challenge to understand the experimental data on both the nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow of $D^0$ mesons in $p-$Pb collisions at LHC energies. In this work, we study these collisions with an improved multi-phase transport model. By including the Cronin effect (i.e., transverse momentum broadening) and independent fragmentation for charm quarks, we provide the first simultaneous description of the $D^0$ meson $R_{\rm pA}$ and $v_2$ data at $p_{\rm T} \leq 8$ GeV$/c$. The model also reasonably describes the $D^0$ meson $p_{\rm T}$ spectra and the low-$p_{\rm T}$ charged hadron spectra, $R_{\rm pA}$ and $v_2$. Our results show that both parton interactions and the Cronin effect are important for the $D^0$ meson $R_{\rm pA}$, while parton interactions are mostly responsible for the $D^0$ meson $v_2$. It is thus essential to include the Cronin effect for the simultaneous description of the $D^0$ meson $R_{\rm pA}$ and $v_2$. This work implies that the Cronin effect could also be important for heavy hadrons in large systems.

## Key claim
By including the Cronin effect (transverse momentum broadening) and independent fragmentation for charm quarks in an improved multi-phase transport (AMPT) model, the authors provide the first simultaneous description of both the nuclear modification factor \(R_{\rm pA}\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\) of \(D^0\) mesons in \(p\)-Pb collisions at LHC energies for \(p_{\rm T} \leq 8\) GeV\(/c\), resolving a long-standing puzzle.

## Method
The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT model with several improvements: modern parton distribution functions, impact parameter-dependent nuclear shadowing, and local nuclear scaling of input parameters. Charm quarks were extracted from the HIJING model before string melting and subjected to parton cascade with separate scattering cross sections for light quarks (0.5 mb) and heavy-light quark interactions (1.5 mb). The Cronin effect was implemented by adding a transverse momentum kick to each \(c\bar{c}\) pair sampled from a two-dimensional Gaussian with width parameter \(w = w_0 \sqrt{1 + (n_{\rm coll} - i) \delta}\), where \(\delta = 5.0\) was determined from \(D^0\) meson \(R_{\rm pA}\) data. Independent fragmentation was added as an alternative hadronization process for heavy quarks when coalescence was unsuitable.

## Key numbers
- \(D^0\) meson \(R_{\rm pA}\) and \(v_2\) simultaneously described for \(p_{\rm T} \leq 8\) GeV\(/c\) in \(p\)-Pb collisions at LHC energies.
- Default light quark scattering cross section \(\sigma_{\rm LQ} = 0.5\) mb; heavy quark-light quark cross section \(\sigma_{\rm HQ} = 1.5\) mb.
- Cronin effect strength parameter \(\delta = 5.0\) (default), determined from \(D^0\) meson \(R_{\rm pA}\) data.
- For \(p+p\) collisions, the Gaussian width \(w = 0.375\) GeV\(/c\).
- For minimum bias \(p\)-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, the AMPT model gives \(\langle k_{\rm T}^2 \rangle = 3.27\) GeV\(^2\) for charm quarks, compared to 2.50 GeV\(^2\) from the HVQMNR code.
- For \(p+p\) collisions at 5.02 TeV, the AMPT model gives \(\langle k_{\rm T}^2 \rangle = 0.04\) GeV\(^2\), compared to 1.46 GeV\(^2\) from the HVQMNR code.

## Context
This work extends previous efforts that could describe either the heavy meson \(R_{\rm pA}\) (e.g., the POWLANG model of Beraudo (2015), pQCD calculations of Fujii (2013) and Kang (2014)) or the \(v_2\) (e.g., the color glass condensate framework of Zhang (2019, 2020)), but not both simultaneously. The study enables future investigations of the Cronin effect in larger collision systems (e.g., Pb-Pb) and suggests that its inclusion may affect extracted values of charm quark transport coefficients in models that currently describe \(D\) meson \(R_{\rm AA}\) and \(v_2\) (e.g., Song (2015), Cao (2016), Scardina (2017), He (2019)).
