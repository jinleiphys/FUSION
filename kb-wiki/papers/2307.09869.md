# Spatial imaging of proton via leading-twist non-skewed GPDs with basis light-front quantization

> The internal image of the proton is unveiled by examining the generalized parton distributions (GPDs) at zero skewness, within the basis light-front quantized environment. Several distributions emerge when a quark is sampled with different currents depending upon the helicity arrangements of the active quark and the proton target. We investigate six of the eight leading-twist proton GPDs of the valence quarks, the helicity conserving distributions $(H, E, \tilde{H})$ and the helicity non-conserving $(H_T,E_T,\tilde{H}_T)$ distributions at skewness set to zero ($ζ=0$). We consider purely transverse momentum transfer and, hence, obtain results describe only the proton's two-dimensional structure in the transverse plane. We present the Mellin moments of these distribution functions, where the first moment produces a form factor and the second Mellin moments help extract the information on partonic contributions to the hadronic angular momentum. We compare our results for the Mellin moments with those from lattice QCD and other approaches where available. We also present the GPDs in transverse position space.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first calculation of six leading-twist proton generalized parton distributions (GPDs) at zero skewness using basis light-front quantization (BLFQ) within the valence Fock sector, but the results are obtained from an effective QCD-inspired Hamiltonian rather than from first-principles QCD, and the approach is limited to purely transverse momentum transfer, describing only the proton's two-dimensional structure in the transverse plane.

## Method

The authors solve the light-front Hamiltonian eigenvalue problem for the proton using BLFQ with a truncated basis, restricting to the valence three-quark (qqq) Fock sector. The effective Hamiltonian includes kinetic energy, a confining potential derived from soft-wall light-front holographic QCD and phenomenological modeling, and one-gluon exchange interactions with a fixed coupling constant. The resulting light-front wave functions are used to compute the overlap representations of six leading-twist GPDs (H, E, H̃, H_T, E_T, H̃_T) at zero skewness (ζ=0) for u and d valence quarks, with basis truncation parameters N_max=10 and K=16.5.

## Key numbers

- Model parameters: quark masses m_q/K.E. = 0.3 GeV and m_q/OGE = 0.2 GeV; confinement strength κ = 0.34 GeV; coupling constant α_s = 1.1 ± 0.1; harmonic oscillator scale parameter b = 0.6 GeV
- Model scale: μ²_0 = 0.195 ± 0.020 GeV²
- Basis truncation parameters: N_max = 10 (transverse) and K = 16.5 (longitudinal)
- The GPDs are computed for momentum transfer squared -t ranging from 0 to several GeV², with distributions shown as functions of longitudinal momentum fraction x and -t

## Context

This work extends previous BLFQ studies of the proton that examined electromagnetic and axial-vector form factors (Mondal 2019, Xu 2021), unpolarized and helicity-dependent parton distribution functions (Mondal 2019, Xu 2021), and angular momentum distributions (Liu 2022) by now including the chiral-odd (transversity) GPDs H_T, E_T, and H̃_T alongside the chiral-even GPDs H, E, and H̃. The results are compared with lattice QCD calculations (Bhattacharya 2022, Alexandrou 2021, 2022) and Dyson-Schwinger equation approaches (Chen 2022, Xu 2015), and the computed GPDs can be used to extract information on partonic contributions to hadronic angular momentum and to provide input for upcoming experiments at the Electron-Ion Collider and the 12 GeV upgrade at Jefferson Lab.
