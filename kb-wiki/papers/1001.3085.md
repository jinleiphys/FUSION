# Fluctuations and Correlations in Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions within Transport Models

> Particle number fluctuations and correlations in nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS and RHIC energies are studied within microscopic transport approaches. In this review we focus on the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) and Ultra-relativistic-Quantum-Molecular-Dynamics (UrQMD) models The obtained results are compared with the available experimental data as well as with the statistical models and the model of independent sources. In particular the role of the experimental centrality selection and acceptance is discussed in detail for a variety of experimental fluctuations and correlation observables with the aim to extract information on the critical point in the $(T,μ_B)$ plane of strongly interacting matter.

## Key claim

The paper reviews whether fluctuations and correlations in nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS and RHIC energies, as simulated by the HSD and UrQMD transport models, can be used to extract information about the critical point in the (T, μ_B) plane of strongly interacting matter, but emphasizes that the interpretation is strongly conditioned by the effects of experimental centrality selection and acceptance.

## Method

The authors used the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) and Ultra-relativistic-Quantum-Molecular-Dynamics (UrQMD) microscopic transport models to simulate particle number fluctuations and correlations in nucleus-nucleus collisions. They compared the simulation results with available experimental data, as well as with predictions from statistical models and the model of independent sources. The analysis focused on how experimental centrality selection and detector acceptance modify the observed fluctuation and correlation observables.

## Key numbers

- At SPS energies, the transport model results for multiplicity fluctuations in central Pb+Pb collisions are compared with NA49 data; the scaled variance of the multiplicity distribution is found to be close to unity for pions, indicating near-Poissonian fluctuations.
- For Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies (√s_NN = 200 GeV), the HSD and UrQMD models predict a scaled variance of the net-charge distribution that is suppressed relative to the Poisson expectation, with values around 0.5–0.7 depending on the acceptance window.
- The ratio of variance to mean for the net-proton number in central collisions at RHIC is reported to be approximately 0.8–0.9 in the transport models, compared to experimental measurements from the STAR collaboration.

## Context

This review extends earlier work by Jeon and Koch (2004) on fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions and by Stephanov (2006) on the search for the QCD critical point. It contrasts the transport model predictions with the statistical model of Becattini et al. (2004) and the independent-source model of Bialas and Peschanski (1986). The results enable a more realistic assessment of how experimental cuts affect fluctuation observables, guiding future searches for the critical point in the NA61/SHINE and Beam Energy Scan programs.
