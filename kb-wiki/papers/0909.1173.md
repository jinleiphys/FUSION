# On the Crustal Matter of Magnetars

> We have investigated some of the properties of dense sub-nuclear matter at the crustal region (both the outer crust and the inner crust region) of a magnetar. The relativistic version of Thomas-Fermi (TF) model is used in presence of strong quantizing magnetic field for the outer crust matter. The compressed matter in the outer crust, which is a crystal of metallic iron, is replaced by a regular array of spherically symmetric Wigner-Seitz (WS) cells. In the inner crust region, a mixture of iron and heavier neutron rich nuclei along with electrons and free neutrons has been considered. Conventional Harrison-Wheeler (HW) and Bethe-Baym-Pethick (BBP) equation of states are used for the nuclear mass formula. A lot of significant changes in the characteristic properties of dense crustal matter, both at the outer crust and the inner crust, have been observed.

# On the Crustal Matter of Magnetars

## Key claim
The properties of dense sub-nuclear matter in both the outer and inner crust of magnetars change significantly in the presence of strong quantizing magnetic fields, but the effect is primarily limited to the electron component; the authors assume that bound protons, bound neutrons, and free neutrons (above neutron drip density) are not affected by the quantizing magnetic field, and the free neutrons are treated as a normal fluid rather than a superfluid.

## Method
The authors used a relativistic Thomas-Fermi model with a strong quantizing magnetic field to describe the outer crust as a regular array of spherically symmetric Wigner-Seitz cells containing metallic iron. For the inner crust, they considered a mixture of iron and heavier neutron-rich nuclei along with electrons and free neutrons, employing the conventional Harrison-Wheeler (HW) and Bethe-Baym-Pethick (BBP) equations of state for the nuclear mass formula, with the magnetic field affecting only the electron component.

## Key numbers
- The surface magnetic field of magnetars is observed to be greater than or equal to 10^15 G.
- The nuclear radius parameter used is r_0 = 1.12 fm.
- For the outer crust, the scaled WS cell surface radius x_s decreases from 30.67 MeV^-1 at B = B_c^(e) to 1.78 MeV^-1 at B = 10^3 × B_c^(e).
- The WS cell radius r_s follows the power law r_s = 1.704 × (B/B_c^(e))^(-0.91) Å.
- The scaled WS cell surface radius x_s follows the power law x_s = 29.43 × (B/B_c^(e))^(-0.41) MeV^-1.
- For B = B_c^(e), the maximum Landau quantum number near the nuclear surface starts at 124; for B = 10 × B_c^(e), it starts at 15; for B = 50 × B_c^(e), it starts at 2; for B = 100 × B_c^(e), it starts at 1.
- Beyond B = 100 × B_c^(e) (which is greater than 10^15 G), the upper limit of the Landau quantum number becomes identically zero at all points inside the WS cell.
- The inner crust density range in the conventional picture is approximately 10^9 to 10^12 g/cm^3.
- The neutron drip density is approximately 4 × 10^11 g/cm^3.
- For a typical neutron star, the inner crust width is about 0.5-0.6 km and the outer crust width is about 0.2-0.4 km.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous relativistic Thomas-Fermi formalism (Nag and Chakrabarty, 2009) to the crustal matter of magnetars, building on earlier studies of strong magnetic field effects on neutron star matter and quark matter. The investigation uses the conventional Harrison-Wheeler and Bethe-Baym-Pethick equations of state, which are standard in neutron star physics, and shows that strong magnetic fields compress Wigner-Seitz cells (analogous to magnetostriction) and increase the minimum mass number of nuclei required for mechanical stability in the inner crust. The results enable future studies of neutron superfluidity in the inner crust under strong magnetic fields, which the authors note will require treatment of the neutron's anomalous magnetic moment.
