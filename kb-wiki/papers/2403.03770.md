# An assessment of $\mathbfΥ$-states above $\mathbf{B\bar B}$-threshold using a constituent-quark-model based meson-meson coupled-channels framework

> The $Υ(10753)$ state has been recently observed by the Belle and Belle~II collaborations with enough global significance to motivate an assessment of the high-energy spectrum usually predicted by any reasonable \emph{naïve} quark model. In the framework of a constituent quark model which satisfactorily describes a wide range of properties of conventional hadrons containing heavy quarks, the quark-antiquark and meson-meson degrees of freedom have been incorporated with the goal of elucidating the influence of open-bottom meson-meson thresholds into the $Υ$ states whose masses are within the energy range of the $Υ(10753)$'s mass. It is well known that such effects could be relevant enough as to generate dynamically new states and thus provide a plausible explanation of the nature of the $Υ(10753)$ state. In particular, we have performed a coupled-channels calculation in which the bare states $Υ(4S)$, $Υ(3D)$, $Υ(5S)$ and $Υ(4D)$ are considered together with the threshold channels $B\bar{B}$, $B\bar{B}^\ast$, $B^\ast \bar{B}^\ast$, $B_s\bar{B}_s$, $B_s\bar{B}_s^\ast$ and $B_s^\ast \bar{B}_s^\ast$. Among the results we have described, the following conclusions are of particular interest: (i) a richer complex spectrum is gained when thresholds are present and bare bound states are sufficiently non-relativistic; (ii) those poles obtained in the complex energy plane do not have to appear as simple peaks in the relevant cross sections; and (iii) the $Υ(10750)$ candidate is interpreted as a dressed hadronic resonance whose structure is an equally mixture of a conventional $b\bar b$ state and $B^\ast \bar B^\ast$ molecule.

## Key claim

The Υ(10753) state cannot be explained as a conventional bottomonium system and is instead interpreted as a dressed hadronic resonance whose structure is an equally mixture of a conventional b\(\bar b\) state and a B*\(\bar B\)* molecule, based on a coupled-channels calculation that includes the bare states Υ(4S), Υ(3D), Υ(5S) and Υ(4D) together with six open-bottom meson-meson threshold channels.

## Method

The authors performed a coupled-channels calculation within a constituent quark model framework, solving the Schrödinger equation via the Gaussian Expansion Method for the quark-antiquark sector and using the Resonating Group Method to derive effective meson-meson interactions from the microscopic quark-(anti)quark potentials. The coupling between the b\(\bar b\) and meson-meson sectors was implemented using the \(^3P_0\) transition operator for light quark-antiquark pair creation, with the strength parameter \(\gamma=0.205\) fixed from previous global fits to meson strong decays. The resulting coupled Lippmann-Schwinger equations were solved to find poles in the complex energy plane and to compute cross sections for \(e^+e^- \to AB\) processes through the extended Van Royen-Weisskopf formalism.

## Key numbers

- The bare state masses predicted by the constituent quark model for the relevant states are: Υ(4S) at 10607 MeV, Υ(3D) at 10653 MeV, Υ(5S) at 10818 MeV, and Υ(4D) at 10853 MeV.
- The experimentally measured mass of the Υ(10753) state from Belle is M = (10752.7 ± 5.9\(^{+0.7}_{-1.1}\)) MeV with width Γ = (35.5\(^{+17.6}_{-11.3}\)\(^{+3.9}_{-3.3}\)) MeV, and from Belle II is M = (10753 ± 6) MeV with Γ = (36\(^{+18}_{-12}\)) MeV.
- The lowest open-bottom meson-meson threshold is B\(\bar B\) at 10558.8 MeV; other thresholds include B\(\bar B\)* at 10604.1 MeV, B*\(\bar B\)* at 10649.4 MeV, B_s\(\bar B_s\) at 10733.8 MeV, B_s\(\bar B_s\)* at 10782.7 MeV, and B_s*\(\bar B_s\)* at 10831.6 MeV.
- The \(^3P_0\) vertex form factor cutoff is \(\Lambda = 0.84\) GeV, with no fine-tuning of parameters employed.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model of Vijande (2004) and Segovia (2013, 2016), which successfully describes conventional heavy-quark hadrons, to include coupled-channels effects from open-bottom meson-meson thresholds. The study addresses the recent experimental observations of the Υ(10753) state by Belle (2019) and Belle II (2022), which cannot be accommodated by any naive quark model as a conventional bottomonium state. The results enable a dynamical interpretation of the Υ(10753) as a mixed conventional-molecular state, and the formalism can be applied to other XYZ mesons in the bottomonium sector, including the charged Z_b(10610) and Z_b(10650) states previously studied by Ortega (2021).
