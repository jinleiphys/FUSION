# Effect of Quark Dimension Reduction on Goldstone Mode in Magnetic Field

## Key claim
The quark dimension reduction in a strong magnetic field causes not only the magnetic catalysis effect on chiral symmetry restoration but also a sudden jump of the neutral pion mass at the Mott transition temperature, where the pion transitions from a bound state to a resonant state. This mass jump is a direct consequence of the infrared divergence in the polarization function at the two-quark threshold, which arises from the reduced dimensionality of quark momentum space in a magnetic field.

## Method
The authors use the Pauli-Villars regularized Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with two quark flavors in an external magnetic field. Mesons are treated as quantum fluctuations above the mean field using the random phase approximation (RPA), with the Leung-Ritus-Wang method employed to handle the quark propagator in the magnetic field. The pole masses of the neutral pion (Goldstone mode) and sigma meson (Higgs mode) are obtained from the pole of the meson propagator at zero three-momentum, and the coupling and decay constants are computed from the residue at the pole.

## Key numbers
- At zero magnetic field, the critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration is T_c = 157 MeV.
- At eB/m_pi^2 = 10, the critical temperature increases to T_c = 164 MeV; at eB/m_pi^2 = 20, T_c = 180 MeV.
- The Mott transition temperature T_m (where the pion mass jumps) is 167 MeV at eB/m_pi^2 = 0, 160 MeV at eB/m_pi^2 = 10, and 147 MeV at eB/m_pi^2 = 20.
- The crossing point where T_c equals T_m occurs at eB/m_pi^2 = 5.
- The model parameters are fixed in vacuum: coupling constant G = 3.44 GeV^{-2}, Pauli-Villars cutoff Lambda = 1127 MeV, and current quark mass m_0 = 5 MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of neutral mesons in the magnetized NJL model using methods such as four-momentum independent meson polarizations (NJL2), magnetic field independent regularization (MFIR), derivative expansion (Ritus5, Ritus6), and Phi-derivable approaches (Phi). The results are consistent with chiral perturbation theory (Hadron1) and lattice QCD simulations (L1, L2, L3) regarding the magnetic field dependence of meson masses in vacuum. The paper enables further investigation of possible sudden enhancement of neutral pions in relativistic heavy ion collisions where strong magnetic fields are created, and it clarifies the distinction between mass jumps from the Mott transition and those from a first-order chiral phase transition at finite baryon chemical potential.
