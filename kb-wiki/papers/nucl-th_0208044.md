# Elimination of Rotational Degrees of Freedom in Expansion Methods for Three Nucleons

> Euler angles determining rotations of a system as a whole are conveniently separated in three-particle basis functions. Analytic integration of matrix elements over Euler angles is done in a general form. Results for the Euler angle integrated matrix elements of a realistic NN interaction are listed. The partial wave decomposition of correlated three-body states is considered.

# Elimination of Rotational Degrees of Freedom in Expansion Methods for Three Nucleons

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Euler angles representing overall rotations of a three-nucleon system can be analytically separated and integrated out of matrix elements, reducing six-dimensional integrals to three-dimensional ones, provided the integrands are non-singular in the exceptional sub-areas where the body reference frame cannot be defined (collinear vectors, or u=0, or v=0).

## Method

The author uses Jacobi coordinates for three nucleons and defines a body reference frame with the z-axis along vector u and the x-axis in the u-v plane, then expands spatial basis functions F_LM(u,v) in terms of Wigner D-functions multiplied by body-frame functions depending only on scalar variables u, v, and t = (unit u)·(unit v). Analytic integration over Euler angles is performed using D-function orthogonality relations, and the resulting expressions are applied to matrix elements of realistic NN interaction operators including l², k², spin-orbit, and tensor operators S₁₂, S₁₂^ll, and S₁₂^kk. The partial wave decomposition of correlated three-body states is also treated via the same Euler angle separation.

## Key numbers

- The volume element transforms as dτ = dudv = dω dτ_int, with dτ_int = u²v² du dv dt, where dω = dα sinβ dβ dγ.
- Overlap-type integrals reduce to: ∫ dτ G*_L'M' Ô_u,v,t F_LM = δ_LL' δ_MM' 8π²(2L+1)⁻¹ ∫ dτ_int Σ_{M''=-L}^L G_LM''(u,v,t) Ô_u,v,t F_LM''(u,v,t).
- For the tensor operator N_μ^(2) (related to S₁₂), the body-frame value is N_μ^(2) = 2δ_μ0, leading to reduced matrix elements of the form (F_L' || V(r) N^(2) || G_L) = 16π²(2L'+1)⁻¹/² ∫ dτ_int V(r) Σ_M C_{LM20}^{L'M} F_L'M G_LM.
- The final closed-form expression for the f-coefficient in the L and K tensor matrix elements is f(L₁,L₂,M₁,M₂,μ₁,μ₂) = (6/5)¹/² (-1)^{μ₁+M₂} C^{2,M₁-M₂}_{L₁M₁L₂,-M₂} C^{2,μ₁-μ₂}_{1μ₁1,-μ₂}.

## Context

This work extends the approach of eliminating rotational degrees of freedom previously used by several groups (Delves, Kievsky et al., Efros) in solving the three-nucleon Schrödinger equation and the method of integral transforms (Efros 1985). The techniques are substantially simpler than those of Danilin (2000) for the same matrix elements. The formalism enables efficient calculation of matrix elements for realistic NN interactions, NNN forces, and reaction problems using regular quadratures, and is applicable beyond the three-nucleon problem to other three-particle systems.
