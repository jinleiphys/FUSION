# Revisit the isospin violating decays of $X(3872)$

> In this work, we revisit the isospin violating decays of $X(3872)$ in a coupled-channel effective field theory. In the molecular scheme, the $X(3872)$ is interpreted as the bound state of $\bar{D}^{*0}D^0/\bar{D}^0D^{*0}$ and $D^{*-}D^+/D^-D^{*+}$ channels. In a cutoff-independent formalism, we relate the coupling constants of $X(3872)$ with the two channels to the molecular wave function. The isospin violating decays of $X(3872)$ are obtained by two equivalent approaches, which amend some deficiencies about this issue in literature. In the quantum field theory approach, the isospin violating decays arise from the coupling constants of $X(3872)$ to two di-meson channels. In the quantum mechanics approach, the isospin violating is attributed to wave functions at the origin. We illustrate that how to cure the insufficient results in literature. Within the comprehensive analysis, we bridge the isospin violating decays of $X(3872)$ to its inner structure. Our results show that the proportion of the neutral channel in $X(3872)$ is over $80\%$. As a by-product, we calculate the strong decay width of $X(3872)\to \bar{D}^0 D^0π^0$ and radiative one $X(3872)\to \bar{D}^0 D^0γ$. The strong decay width and radiative decay width are about 30 keV and 10 keV, respectively, for the binding energy from $-300$ keV to $-50$ keV.

## Key claim

The isospin violating decays of $X(3872)$ arise from the coupling constants of the state to its two di-meson channels (neutral and charged), not from the mass difference in propagators as previously claimed; the proportion of the neutral channel in $X(3872)$ is over 80%, and the strong and radiative decay widths are about 30 keV and 10 keV respectively for binding energies between -300 keV and -50 keV.

## Method

The authors used a coupled-channel effective field theory with a hard cutoff regulator to solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equations for the neutral ($\bar{D}^{*0}D^0/\bar{D}^0D^{*0}$) and charged ($D^{*-}D^+/D^-D^{*+}$) di-meson channels. They introduced cutoff-independent parameters $b_{ij}$ to eliminate the regulator dependence, relating the coupling constants $g_1$ and $g_2$ of $X(3872)$ to the two channels to the molecular wave function via a mixing angle $\theta$. The isospin violating decays were then computed in two equivalent approaches: a quantum field theory approach using the residues of the $T$-matrix, and a quantum mechanics approach using wave functions at the origin.

## Key numbers

* The mass difference between the charged threshold ($D^{*-}D^+/D^-D^{*+}$) and the neutral threshold ($\bar{D}^{*0}D^0/\bar{D}^0D^{*0}$) is about 8 MeV.
* The proportion of the neutral channel in $X(3872)$ is over 80%, with the mixing angle $\theta$ in the range $(0,\pi/4)$ and $\tan\theta = c_2/c_1$ in the range $(0,1/2)$.
* The strong decay width of $X(3872)\to \bar{D}^0 D^0\pi^0$ is about 30 keV for binding energies from -300 keV to -50 keV.
* The radiative decay width of $X(3872)\to \bar{D}^0 D^0\gamma$ is about 10 keV for binding energies from -300 keV to -50 keV.
* The ratio $R_2$ (from the sequential decay of $\omega$ and $\rho$) is approximately 0.147, with an alternative value of 0.087 from Braaten (2005).
* The coupling constant $g_\pi$ for the $D^{*+}\to D^0\pi^+$ decay is extracted as 11.25.
* The total width of the $D^{*0}$ meson is taken as 60 keV.

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of $X(3872)$ as a bound state of $\bar{D}^{*0}D^0/\bar{D}^0D^{*0}$ and $D^{*-}D^+/D^-D^{*+}$, building on the coupled-channel effective field theory framework of Cohen (2004) and Braaten (2005). It corrects deficiencies in prior approaches: Gamermann (2009) assumed equal coupling constants $g_1=g_2$ and relied on cutoff-dependent propagator differences, while Li (2012) used only the coefficients of different channels rather than wave functions at the origin. The results agree with Wu (2021) who found the neutral component weight to be (83-88)% in a cutoff-dependent scheme. The approach enables improved calculations of isospin violation in other molecular candidates such as $P_c(4457)$, where the method of Gamermann (2009) was previously applied.
