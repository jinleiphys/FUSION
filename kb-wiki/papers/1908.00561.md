# Chiral kinetic theory from the on-shell effective theory: derivation of collision terms

> We show that the on-shell effective theory (OSEFT) is the quantum field theory counterpart of a Foldy-Wouthuysen diagonalization of relativistic quantum mechanics for massless fermions. Thus, it is free of the Zitterbewegung oscillations that would yield an ill-defined meaning to the semiclassical transport approach at short distances if derived from the pure Dirac picture. We present a detailed derivation of the collision terms in the chiral kinetic theory using the OSEFT. Collision integrals are derived up to order 1/E, where E is the energy of an on-shell fermion. At this order, the collision terms depends on the spin tensor of the fermion, and in the presence of chiral imbalance, it describes how a massless fermion of a given helicity interacts differently with the transverse photons of different circular polarization. In order to back up our results, we check that they allow us to reproduce the fermion decay rate in an ultradegenerate plasma with a chiral imbalance computed directly from QED.

## Key claim

The on-shell effective theory (OSEFT) is shown to be equivalent to a Foldy-Wouthuysen diagonalization of the Dirac Hamiltonian for massless fermions, meaning it is free of Zitterbewegung oscillations that would otherwise make a semiclassical transport approach ill-defined at short distances if derived from the pure Dirac picture.

## Method

The authors derive the OSEFT Lagrangian order by order via successive Foldy-Wouthuysen transformations of the QED Lagrangian for massless fermions, assuming the fermion energy E is the hard scale larger than electromagnetic fields and their gradients. They then use the Kadanoff-Baym equations in the real-time formalism of thermal field theory to derive collision terms for the chiral kinetic theory, applying a Wigner transform and gradient expansion to the OSEFT two-point functions up to order 1/E.

## Key numbers

- Collision integrals are derived up to order 1/E, where E is the energy of an on-shell fermion.
- The OSEFT Lagrangian is derived up to order 1/E^2, matching the Lagrangian used in Carignano (2018) for the collisionless chiral kinetic theory.
- The fermion decay rate in an ultradegenerate plasma with chiral imbalance computed from QED is reproduced using the OSEFT results for an isotropic plasma.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous derivation of chiral transport theory from the OSEFT (Manuel 2014, Carignano 2018) by providing a detailed derivation of collision terms. It contrasts with derivations from the Dirac picture (Hidaka 2016) which still contain Zitterbewegung effects, and shows that the OSEFT approach reproduces the fermion decay rate computed directly from QED. The side-jump phenomenon in chiral kinetic theory (Chen 2014, Chen 2015) is explained as a consequence of reparametrization invariance of the OSEFT, and the finite spatial extent of fermions in the Foldy-Wouthuysen picture resolves apparent paradoxes in binary collision trajectories.
