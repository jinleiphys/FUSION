# Spectral Statistics in Large Shell Model Calculations

> The spectral statistics of low--lying states of $fp$ shell nuclei are studied by performing large shell--model calculations with a realistic nuclear interaction. For $Ca$ isotopes, we find deviations from the predictions of the random--matrix theory which suggest that some spherical nuclei are not as chaotic in nature as the conventional view assumes.

# Spectral Statistics in Large Shell Model Calculations

## Key claim

Low-lying states of calcium isotopes in the fp shell show deviations from Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) predictions toward regularity, suggesting that some spherical nuclei are not as chaotic as the conventional view assumes, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific energy window (up to 4-5 MeV above the yrast line) and the limited number of level spacings available for statistical analysis.

## Method

Large shell-model calculations were performed in the (p1/2, p3/2, f5/2, f7/2) model space assuming a 40Ca inert core, using a minimally modified Kuo-Brown interaction and the Lanczos algorithm via the code ANTOINE. Energy spectra for total angular momentum J=0 to J=9 and fixed total isospin T were calculated for A=46-50 nuclei with various proton-neutron configurations. The nearest-neighbor spacing distribution P(s) of unfolded energy levels was computed and fitted to the Brody distribution, which interpolates between Poisson (omega=0, regular) and GOE (omega=1, chaotic) limits.

## Key numbers

- Brody parameter omega for 46V: 0.97 (up to 4 MeV) and 0.97 (up to 5 MeV), indicating near-chaotic behavior
- Brody parameter omega for 46Ti: 1.09 (up to 4 MeV) and 0.97 (up to 5 MeV), consistent with GOE predictions
- Brody parameter omega for 46Ca: 0.57 (up to 4 MeV) and 0.57 (up to 5 MeV), showing deviation from chaos toward regularity
- Brody parameter omega for 48Ca: 0.64 (up to 4 MeV) and 0.50 (up to 5 MeV)
- Brody parameter omega for 50Ca: 0.56 (up to 4 MeV) and 0.65 (up to 5 MeV)
- Combined analysis of 46Ca+48Ca+50Ca used 266 level spacings up to 5 MeV above the yrast line
- Combined analysis of 46V+46Ti used 236 level spacings up to 5 MeV above the yrast line
- For 46V, 96 levels were available up to 4 MeV and 159 levels up to 5 MeV above the yrast line for J=0-9

## Context

This work extends the findings of Bae et al. (1992), who observed regular features in heavy single-closed-shell nuclei 212Rn and 212Pb, to the lighter calcium isotopes in the fp shell. It contrasts with earlier shell-model studies in the sd shell by Ormand and Broglia (1992) that found GOE-like distributions, and with the prevailing view from experimental resonance data that spherical nuclei generally show chaotic spectra. The results suggest that single-closed-shell nuclei with only valence neutrons (or only valence protons) exhibit less chaotic behavior due to weaker two-body matrix elements compared to nuclei with both valence protons and neutrons. The analysis of experimental energy levels in 116Sn by Raman et al. (1991) yielded an intermediate Brody parameter omega=0.51+/-0.19, consistent with these theoretical findings for calcium isotopes.
