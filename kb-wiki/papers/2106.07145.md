# Interplay between pairing and triaxial shape degrees of freedom in Os and Pt nuclei

> The effect of coupling between pairing and quadrupole triaxial shape vibrations on the low-energy collective states of $γ$-soft nuclei is investigated using a model based on the framework of nuclear energy density functionals (EDFs). Employing a constrained self-consistent mean-field (SCMF) method that uses universal EDFs and pairing interactions, potential energy surfaces of characteristic $γ$-soft Os and Pt nuclei with $A\approx190$ are calculated as functions of the pairing and triaxial quadrupole deformations. Collective spectroscopic properties are computed using a number-nonconserving interacting boson model (IBM) Hamiltonian, with parameters determined by mapping the SCMF energy surface onto the expectation value of the Hamiltonian in the boson condensate state. It is shown that, by simultaneously considering both the shape and pairing collective degrees of freedom, the EDF-based IBM successfully reproduces data on collective structures based on low-energy $0^{+}$ states, as well as $γ$-vibrational bands.

## Key claim

By simultaneously including both pairing and triaxial quadrupole shape degrees of freedom in an energy-density-functional-based interacting boson model, the authors successfully reproduce experimental data on low-energy \(0^{+}\) states and \(\gamma\)-vibrational bands in \(\gamma\)-soft Os and Pt nuclei, though the mapping is only performed in the vicinity of the global minimum and the IBM energy surfaces are considerably softer than the self-consistent mean-field surfaces.

## Method

Constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations using the relativistic mean-field plus BCS method with the PC-PK1 and DD-PC1 energy density functionals and a separable pairing force were performed to construct three-dimensional potential energy surfaces as functions of the triaxial quadrupole deformations \((\beta,\gamma)\) and the pairing deformation \(\alpha\). The parameters of a boson-number-nonconserving interacting boson model Hamiltonian, which includes up to three-body boson terms and a monopole-pair transfer operator, were determined by mapping the self-consistent mean-field energy surface onto the expectation value of the Hamiltonian in the boson condensate state. The mapped Hamiltonian was then diagonalized in a Hilbert space consisting of three subspaces with boson numbers \(n_0-1\), \(n_0\), and \(n_0+1\).

## Key numbers

* Global minimum deformation parameters for \(^{188}\)Os with PC-PK1: \(\alpha = 15\), \(\beta = 0.20\), \(\gamma = 30^\circ\); with DD-PC1: \(\alpha = 9\), \(\beta = 0.25\), \(\gamma = 0^\circ\).
* Global minimum deformation parameters for \(^{194}\)Pt with PC-PK1: \(\alpha = 15\), \(\beta = 0.15\), \(\gamma = 42^\circ\); with DD-PC1: \(\alpha = 12\), \(\beta = 0.15\), \(\gamma = 36^\circ\).
* The mixing strength \(\theta\) is constant at 360 keV for all Os and Pt nuclei.
* The Inglis-Belyaev moment of inertia must be increased by 40% for both Xe and Os nuclei with both functionals.
* The parameter \(\chi\) in the quadrupole operator is very small, e.g., \(-0.02\) for \(^{190,192}\)Os and \(0.03\) for \(^{194,196}\)Pt with PC-PK1.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Xiang 2020, Nomura 2020, Nomura 2021) that introduced pairing vibrations into the quadrupole collective model and IBM for deformed rare-earth nuclei, and earlier IBM studies of the \(A\approx190\) region using the DD-PC1 functional (Nomura 2011, Nomura 2012) and Gogny functionals (Nomura 2011). It addresses the long-standing problem of simultaneously treating pairing and triaxial quadrupole deformations in realistic applications, which had previously been limited to axially symmetric shapes. The method enables future studies of shape coexistence and the coupling of pairing and triaxial degrees of freedom in other mass regions.
