# N-particle irreducible actions for stochastic fluids

> We construct one- and two-particle irreducible (1PI and 2PI) effective actions for the stochastic fluid dynamics of a conserved density undergoing diffusive motion. We compute the 1PI action at one-loop order, and the 2PI action in two-loop approximation. We derive a set of Schwinger-Dyson equations, and regularize the resulting equations using Pauli-Villars fields. We numerically solve the Schwinger-Dyson equations for a non-critical fluid. We find that higher-loop effects summed by the Schwinger-Dyson renormalize the non-linear coupling. We also find indications of a diffuson-cascade, the appearance $n$-loop corrections with smaller and smaller exponential suppression.

## Key claim

The authors construct one- and two-particle irreducible (1PI and 2PI) effective actions for stochastic fluid dynamics of a conserved density undergoing diffusive motion, and numerically solve the resulting Schwinger-Dyson equations for a non-critical fluid; they find that higher-loop effects renormalize the non-linear coupling and observe indications of a "diffuson-cascade" where n-loop corrections exhibit smaller exponential suppression, but note that the asymptotic long-time behavior is difficult to establish definitively.

## Method

The authors compute the 1PI effective action at one-loop order and the 2PI effective action in two-loop approximation for a stochastic diffusion equation with a cubic non-linearity. They derive Schwinger-Dyson equations for the self-energies in a mixed time-momentum representation, regularize short-time singularities using Pauli-Villars fields with two auxiliary fields, and solve the coupled integral equations numerically by fixed-point iteration starting from the free propagators.

## Key numbers

- The critical coupling for the cubic non-linearity is found to be $\lambda_{3,c}\simeq 2.58$ for Pauli-Villars parameters $\alpha_{1,2}=(4.0,5.0)$.
- The one-loop self-energy contains terms of order $t^{-5/2}$ and $t^{-3/2}$ in the short-time limit, requiring regularization.
- For $g_3=O(1)$, fluctuations with wave number $k=\xi^{-1}$ become non-perturbative for $t\gsim 2\tau\log(2/\alpha)\sim 12\tau$, where $\alpha=1/(64\sqrt{2}\pi^{3/2})$.
- Using estimates $\xi\simeq 1.2$ fm and $\tau=1.8$ fm from heavy ion collisions, non-perturbative effects set in for $t\gsim 20$ fm.
- The numerical solutions use a short-time cutoff $t_c=0.2$ and parameters $D=1$, $\lambda_3=2.5$.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative approach to effective actions for fluid dynamics developed by Chao and Schaefer (2020) to a non-perturbative framework based on n-particle irreducible effective actions, as reviewed by Berges (2004) and Calzetta (2008). The results are relevant to the study of hydrodynamic fluctuations near the conjectured critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram, where dynamical critical scaling predicts relaxation times scaling as $\tau\sim \xi^z$ (Hohenberg and Halperin, 1977). The method provides a systematic way to check approximations from mode coupling theory (Kawasaki, 1970) and the epsilon expansion, and can be extended to critical dynamics in model B and model H universality classes, as well as to functional renormalization group approaches (Canet, 2007).
