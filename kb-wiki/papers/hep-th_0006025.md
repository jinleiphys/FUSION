# Supersymmetric Nonlinear Sigma Models

> Supersymmetric nonlinear sigma models are formulated as gauge theories. Auxiliary chiral superfields are introduced to impose supersymmetric constraints of F-type. Target manifolds defined by F-type constraints are always non-compact. In order to obtain nonlinear sigma models on compact manifolds, we have to introduce gauge symmetry to eliminate the degrees of freedom in non-compact directions. All supersymmetric nonlinear sigma models defined on the hermitian symmetric spaces are successfully formulated as gauge theories.

## Key claim
All supersymmetric nonlinear sigma models defined on hermitian symmetric spaces can be formulated as gauge theories with auxiliary fields, but the target manifolds defined by F-type constraints alone are always non-compact; gauge symmetry must be introduced to eliminate the non-compact degrees of freedom and obtain models on compact manifolds.

## Method
The authors introduce auxiliary chiral superfields to impose supersymmetric F-type constraints, then add a local gauge symmetry (e.g., U(1) for CP^{N-1} or U(N) for Grassmann manifolds) whose gauge field acts as an auxiliary superfield. The gauge symmetry is complexified (e.g., U(1)^C) and the Fayet-Iliopoulos D-term is used to generate the Kähler potential via elimination of the auxiliary gauge field. For hermitian symmetric spaces beyond the projective and Grassmann cases, quadratic F-term constraints (e.g., φ·φ=0) are imposed with Lagrange multiplier superfields, reducing the global symmetry from SU(N) to O(N) or from SU(2N) to SO(2N) or Sp(N).

## Key numbers
- The O(N) supersymmetric nonlinear sigma model in 2D has bosonic field A taking values on S^{N-1} with radius √N/g.
- The CP^{N-1} model is formulated with N chiral superfields φ_i in the fundamental representation of SU(N) and a U(1) gauge symmetry.
- The Grassmann manifold SU(N)/SU(N-M)×U(M) is obtained with M copies of fundamental representations and U(M) gauge symmetry, yielding Kähler potential K = c log det(1_M + φ†φ).
- The quadratic surface Q^{N-2}(C) = SO(N)/SO(N-2)×U(1) is obtained from the F-term constraint φ·φ=0 with N chiral superfields, giving Kähler potential K = log(1 + Σ x_i*x_i + (1/4) Σ x_i*^2 x_j^2) for i,j=1,...,N-2.
- The SO(2N)/U(N) model uses a 2N×N matrix chiral superfield Φ with U(N) gauge symmetry and constraint Φ^T Φ = 0.
- The Sp(N)/U(N) model uses the same field with constraint Φ^T J Φ = 0 where J is the 2N×2N symplectic matrix.

## Context
This work extends the auxiliary field formulation of the CP^{N-1} model (a standard example of a supersymmetric nonlinear sigma model on a compact Kähler manifold) to all hermitian symmetric spaces, including Grassmann manifolds, quadratic surfaces, and the exceptional cases E_6/SO(10)×U(1) and E_7/E_6×U(1). The approach contrasts with the standard nonlinear realization method of Callan, Coleman, Wess and Zumino (1969) and the Kähler potential formulation of Zumino (1979) and Bagger and Witten (1982). The quantum Legendre transform property proven by the authors (Higashijima and Nitta, 2000) shows that the auxiliary field formulation is unique despite the apparent freedom to choose arbitrary functions in the Lagrangian. This enables nonperturbative studies of these models using large-N expansion techniques analogous to those used for the O(N) model.
