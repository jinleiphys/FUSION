# Nucleation of antikaon condensed matter in proto neutron stars

> A first order phase transition from nuclear matter to antikaon condensed matter may proceed through thermal nucleation of a critical droplet of antikaon condensed matter during the early evolution of proto neutron stars (PNS). Droplets of new phase having radii larger than a critical radius would survive and grow, if the latent heat is transported from the droplet surface to the metastable phase. We investigate the effect of shear viscosity on the thermal nucleation time of the droplets of antikaon condensed matter. In this connection we particularly study the contribution of neutrinos in the shear viscosity and nucleation in PNS.

## Key claim
Thermal nucleation of antikaon condensed matter in proto neutron stars (PNS) is possible only if the surface tension is at least 30 MeV fm\(^{-2}\), a higher threshold than in deleptonised neutron stars, because neutrino-trapped matter has larger shear viscosity which raises the temperature and can melt the condensate.

## Method
The authors apply Langer’s homogeneous nucleation theory to a first-order phase transition from charge-neutral, beta-equilibrated nuclear matter to K\(^{-}\) condensed matter in a neutrino-trapped PNS. They construct the equation of state at finite temperature using the relativistic mean field model with GM1 parameter set and a K\(^{-}\) potential depth of -160 MeV. Shear viscosity is calculated from neutrino-nucleon scattering using the coupled Boltzmann transport equation, and the thermal nucleation time is obtained from the nucleation rate prefactor that includes shear viscosity.

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity decreases with rising temperature for both deleptonised NS matter and neutrino-trapped PNS matter (lepton fraction \(Y_L = 0.4\)).
- For PNS, nucleation may not occur for surface tension < 30 MeV fm\(^{-2}\) because the droplet radius becomes less than the correlation length (\(\xi \sim 5\) fm).
- For NS, nucleation is possible for surface tension < 20 MeV fm\(^{-2}\).
- For PNS at baryon density \(n_b = 3.30 n_0\) and surface tension \(\sigma = 35\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\), the thermal nucleation time calculated with shear viscosity is several orders of magnitude shorter than the \(T^4\) approximation.
- Nucleation of antikaon condensates in PNS is possible if \(\tau_{th} < \tau_{cooling} (\sim 100\) s), which occurs for \(\sigma \geq 30\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of antikaon condensation nucleation in deleptonised neutron stars by Norsen and Reddy and by the authors themselves, which did not include neutrino contributions. It also builds on earlier calculations of shear viscosity in NS matter by Banik and Nandi. The results show that neutrino trapping in PNS raises the minimum surface tension required for nucleation compared to the deleptonised case, and that including shear viscosity in the nucleation prefactor is essential for accurate nucleation time estimates. This enables more realistic modeling of phase transitions in the early evolution of proto neutron stars.
