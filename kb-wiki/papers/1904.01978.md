# Constraining the Hadron-Quark Phase Transition Chemical Potential via Astronomical Observation

> We investigate the chemical potential and baryon number density of the hadron-quark phase transition in neutron star matter. The hadron matter is described with relativistic mean field theory, and the quark matter is described with the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach of QCD. In order to study the first-order phase transition, we develop the sound speed interpolation scheme to construct the equation of state in the middle density region where the hadron phase and quark phase coexist. The phase transition chemical potential is constrained with the maximum mass, the tidal deformability and the radius of neutrons stars. And the most probable value of the phase transition chemical potential is found.

## Key claim

The authors constrain the baryon chemical potential range for the hadron-quark phase transition in neutron star matter by using astronomical observations (maximum mass, tidal deformability, and radius) as calibration quantities, rather than relying on purely theoretical predictions which remain inconsistent across different non-perturbative QCD approaches.

## Method

The hadron matter is described with the TW-99 relativistic mean field (RMF) model including the baryon octet, while the quark matter is described using the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) approach with a Gaussian effective interaction and rainbow approximation. To model the first-order phase transition, the authors develop a sound speed interpolation scheme to construct the equation of state in the coexistence region, where the speed of sound is discontinuous at the boundaries. They then calculate neutron star observables (mass, radius, tidal deformability) for a large set of beginning and ending chemical potentials of the phase transition region, using the observed maximum mass around 2 solar masses and the tidal deformability constraint from GW170817 to exclude inappropriate constructions.

## Key numbers

- The hyperons begin to appear at baryon chemical potential μ = 1.03 GeV, corresponding to baryon density ρ_B = 1.90 ρ_sat (where ρ_sat is the nuclear saturation density)
- The TW-99 model parameters include: meson masses m_σ = 550 MeV, m_ω = 783 MeV, m_ρ = 763 MeV; nucleon coupling constants at saturation g_σN = 10.72854, g_ωN = 13.29015, g_ρN = 7.32196
- The DSE model uses parameters ω = 0.5 GeV and D = 1.0 GeV² for the effective interaction, with α = 2 controlling the approach to asymptotic freedom
- The bag constant in the DSE approach is set to B_DS = 90 MeV/fm³
- The current mass of the s quark is taken as 115 MeV (by fitting the kaon mass in vacuum)
- The tidal deformability constraint from GW170817 is Λ_1.4 < 800 with 90% credence level for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star

## Context

This work extends the 3-window construction approach for hybrid star equations of state (Masuda 2013, Kojo 2015, 2016) by using the speed of sound discontinuity as a signature of first-order phase transitions, rather than assigning the coexistence region artificially. The authors address the problem that different non-perturbative QCD approaches (DSE calculations by Qin (2011), Gao (2016), Fischer (2018); lattice QCD by Ding (2018); effective models) give quite distinct results for the baryon chemical potential at the critical endpoint and the phase coexistence region at zero temperature. The method enables constraining the phase transition chemical potential using multi-messenger astronomy data (mass measurements of 2 solar mass neutron stars by Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013), and gravitational wave observations from GW170817 by Abbott (2017)).
