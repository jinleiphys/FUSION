# Quantal Effects on Spinodal Instabilities in Charge Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

> Quantal effects on growth of spinodal instabilities in charge asymmetric nuclear matter are investigated in the framework of a stochastic mean field approach. Due to quantal effects, in both symmetric and asymmetric matter, dominant unstable modes shift towards longer wavelengths and modes with wave numbers larger than the Fermi momentum are strongly suppressed. As a result of quantum statistical effects, in particular at lower temperatures, magnitude of density fluctuations grows larger than those calculated in semi-classical approximation.

## Key claim

Quantal effects in spinodal instabilities of charge-asymmetric nuclear matter cause dominant unstable modes to shift toward longer wavelengths and suppress modes with wave numbers larger than the Fermi momentum; the magnitude of density fluctuations grows larger than semi-classical calculations predict, particularly at lower temperatures, but the effect is conditional on the density and temperature regime.

## Method

The authors employed a stochastic mean-field approach in the linear response regime, solving the linearized time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) equations for small-amplitude fluctuations of neutron and proton density matrices around an equilibrium state. Growth rates of unstable collective modes were determined from the roots of the quantal susceptibility (dispersion relation), using a Skyrme-type effective interaction with parameters fitted to saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter. Spectral intensities of density correlation functions and density correlation functions were computed analytically by retaining only the growing and decaying collective poles, with initial correlations treated as Gaussian random numbers according to the quantal fluctuation-dissipation theorem.

## Key numbers

- At initial density \(n = 0.2 n_0\) and asymmetry \(I = 0.0\) at \(T = 5\) MeV, quantal calculations confine unstable modes to wavelengths \(\lambda \approx 8\)–\(10\) fm, compared to \(\lambda \approx 7\) fm in semi-classical calculations.
- The maximum of the dispersion relation is reduced by about a factor \(3/4\) in quantal calculations relative to semi-classical calculations.
- At density \(n = 0.4 n_0\) and asymmetry \(I = 0.5\) at \(T = 1\) MeV, the magnitude of fluctuations in the most unstable range is nearly doubled in quantal calculations compared to semi-classical calculations.
- Correlation length of density fluctuations is about \(3.5\) fm at density \(n = 0.4 n_0\) and about \(3.0\) fm at density \(n = 0.2 n_0\).
- The Skyrme parameters used: \(A = -356.8\) MeV, \(B = +303.9\) MeV, \(\alpha = 1/6\), \(D = +130.0\) MeV fm\(^5\), \(D' = +34\) MeV fm\(^5\); saturation density \(n_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), binding energy \(\varepsilon_0 = 15.7\) MeV/nucleon, compressibility modulus \(K = 201\) MeV, surface energy coefficient \(a_{\text{surf}} = 18.6\) MeV, symmetry energy coefficient \(a_{\text{sym}} = 28.0\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations of spinodal instabilities in symmetric and charge-asymmetric nuclear matter, which were mostly carried out using semi-classical Boltzmann-Langevin type stochastic transport models (e.g., the work cited as R8). The authors previously proposed a stochastic mean-field approach (R7) that incorporates one-body dissipation and fluctuation mechanisms consistent with the quantal fluctuation-dissipation theorem. The present study quantifies how quantum statistical effects modify growth rates, dominant wavelengths, and density correlation functions compared to semi-classical approximations, and it enables more realistic microscopic simulations of spinodal decomposition in expanding nuclear systems, including induced fission and heavy-ion fusion near barrier energies.
