# The complete experiment for photoproduction of pseudoscalar mesons in a truncated partial wave analysis

> The complete experiment problem in the truncated partial wave analysis of pseudoscalar meson photoproduction with suppressed t-channel exchanges is investigated. The focus is set to ambiguities of the group S observables with the unpolarized differential cross section, $σ_0$, and the three single-spin observables, $Σ$, $T$ and $P$. For this purpose, the approach and formalism already worked out by Omelaenko in 1981 is revisited in this work. A numerical study using multipoles of the PWA solution MAID2007 shows how only one additional double polarization observable can resolve all ambiguities. Therefore, the possibility emerges to perform a complete experiment with only five observables.

## Key claim
The paper revisits Omelaenko's 1981 formalism to show that for pseudoscalar meson photoproduction with suppressed t-channel exchanges, a complete truncated partial wave analysis can be performed with only five observables (the group S observables plus one additional double polarization observable), rather than the eight previously thought necessary. This result is conditional on the truncation of the partial wave expansion at a finite maximum angular momentum ℓ_max.

## Method
The authors reformulate the multipole expansion of CGLN amplitudes into transversity amplitudes expressed as polynomials in t = tan(θ/2), then factor these polynomials into products of linear factors with complex roots. They analyze the discrete ambiguities arising from the group S observables (unpolarized cross section σ₀, photon beam asymmetry Σ, target asymmetry T, and recoil polarization P) by studying transformations of the polynomial roots that leave these observables invariant. A numerical study using multipoles from the MAID2007 partial wave solution is performed to test which double polarization observables can resolve the remaining discrete ambiguity.

## Key numbers
- 4L complex multipoles (with L = ℓ_max) are reduced to 8L - 1 independent real parameters after accounting for the undetermined overall phase
- The polynomial A′₂L(t) and B′₂L(t) have order 2L, with 4L + 2 expansion coefficients that reduce to 4L independent complex coefficients
- For ℓ_max = 1 (S+P waves), the formalism yields 2L = 2 roots per polynomial, giving 8L - 1 = 7 independent real parameters
- The numerical study using MAID2007 multipoles demonstrates that one additional double polarization observable (e.g., F, G, C_x', O_x', C_z', or O_z') resolves all ambiguities, while measuring E or H would not suffice

## Context
This work extends the formalism of Omelaenko (1981) for truncated partial wave analysis, which itself was based on Gersten's (1969) ambiguity studies in πN scattering. The paper revisits and clarifies Omelaenko's approach, which had not gained much attention, and applies it to modern partial wave analyses including MAID, SAID, and BnGa. This enables complete experiment analyses for pion and eta photoproduction without requiring recoil polarization measurements (since the single recoil polarization P can be measured in beam-target double polarization experiments), and provides a practical path forward for amplitude analysis at facilities like Mainz, Bonn, and JLab.
