# Effects of a multi-quark interaction on color superconducting phase transition in an extended NJL model

> We study the interplay of the chiral and the color superconducting phase transitions in an extended Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model with a multi-quark interaction that produces the nonlinear chiral-diquark coupling. We observe that this nonlinear coupling adds up coherently with the ω^2 interaction to produce the chiral-color superconductivity coexistence phase or cancel each other depending on its sign. We discuss that large coexistence region in the phase diagram is consistent with the quark-diquark picture for the nucleon whereas its smallness is the prerequisite for the applicability of the Ginzburg-Landau approach.

## Key claim

The size of the chiral-color superconductivity coexistence region in the QCD phase diagram is sensitive to the sign of the multi-quark (eight-quark) coupling constant \(d_{2,2}\) that generates a nonlinear \(\sigma^2\Delta^2\) interaction; a positive sign produces a large coexistence region consistent with the quark-diquark picture of the nucleon, while a negative sign shrinks the coexistence region and makes the Ginzburg-Landau approach applicable.

## Method

The authors extended the standard two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model by including a multi-quark interaction term that couples the chiral condensate \(\sigma\) and the diquark condensate \(\Delta\) via a \(\sigma^2\Delta^2\) interaction, in addition to the usual four-quark scalar, vector, and diquark channels. They applied the mean field approximation to derive the thermodynamic potential and gap equations for \(\sigma\), \(\omega\) (vector mean field), and \(\Delta\), then solved these equations numerically to map the phase diagram in the temperature-chemical potential (\(T\)-\(\mu\)) plane for both positive and negative signs of the \(d_{2,2}\) coupling.

## Key numbers

* The four-quark scalar coupling constant is \(g_{2,0} = 5.498\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\), the vector coupling is \(g_{0,2} = 0.2 g_{2,0}\), and the diquark coupling is \(d_{0,2} = 0.6 g_{2,0}\).
* The eight-quark coupling strength is set to \(|d_{2,2}| \sigma_0^2 = 0.2 G_{\Delta}\), where \(\sigma_0 = -0.03023\ \text{GeV}\) is the vacuum chiral condensate.
* The current quark mass is \(m_0 = 0.0055\ \text{GeV}\) and the three-momentum cutoff is \(\Lambda = 0.6315\ \text{GeV}\).
* With positive \(d_{2,2}\), the \(\sigma^2\Delta^2\) interaction shifts the color superconducting transition to lower density, producing a coexistence phase; with negative \(d_{2,2}\), the \(\omega^2\) and \(\sigma^2\Delta^2\) interactions cancel, making the coexistence region very small.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Kashiwa et al. (2007) that examined the effects of \(\sigma^2\omega^2\) and \(\sigma^4\) interactions on the chiral transition, and builds on the analysis of Berges and Rajagopal (1998) who first considered competition between chiral and diquark condensates. The results show that the sign of the eight-quark coupling determines whether the Ginzburg-Landau approach of Hatsuda et al. (2006) is applicable (negative sign) or whether the quark-diquark picture of the nucleon is favored (positive sign), motivating future work to determine the density dependence of the diquark interaction more precisely.
