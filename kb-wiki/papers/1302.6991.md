# Exact momentum fluctuations of an accelerated quark in N=4 super Yang-Mills

> In this work we consider a heavy quark moving with constant proper acceleration in the vacuum of any four dimensional conformal field theory. We argue that the two-point function of its momentum fluctuations is exactly captured by the Bremsstrahlung function that gives the total radiated power. For the particular case of N=4 SU(N) SYM this function is exactly known, so in this case we obtain an explicit expression for the momentum diffusion coefficient, and check that various limits of this result are reproduced by probe computations in AdS_5. Finally, we evaluate this transport coefficient for a heavy quark accelerated in the vacuum of N=4 SU(3) SYM, and comment on possible lessons of our results for the study of heavy quarks traversing the super Yang-Mills plasma.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the two-point function of momentum fluctuations for a heavy quark moving with constant proper acceleration in any four-dimensional conformal field theory is exactly captured by the Bremsstrahlung function that gives the total radiated power, with the qualification that this relation is claimed to be exact for any value of the coupling constant, any gauge group, and any representation of the gauge group.

## Method

The authors use conformal symmetry to relate the two-point function of displacement operators on a static world-line to that on a hyperbolic (constant proper acceleration) world-line via a special conformal transformation. They then compute the Fourier transform of this two-point function to extract the momentum diffusion coefficient, expressing it in terms of the Bremsstrahlung function and the Unruh temperature. For the specific case of N=4 SU(N) super Yang-Mills, they use the exact expression for the Bremsstrahlung function derived from localization techniques, and verify their result against AdS/CFT computations for both fundamental and symmetric representations using string and D3-brane probes.

## Key numbers

- The Bremsstrahlung function for N=4 U(N) SYM is given by B_U(N)(lambda,N) = (lambda/(16 pi^2 N)) * (L_{N-1}^2(-lambda/(4N)) + L_{N-2}^2(-lambda/(4N))) / (L_{N-1}^1(-lambda/(4N))), where L_n^alpha are generalized Laguerre polynomials.
- For SU(N) SYM, B_SU(N) = B_U(N) - lambda/(16 pi^2 N^2).
- The momentum diffusion coefficient is kappa = 16 pi^3 B(lambda,N) T^3, where T = a/(2 pi) is the Unruh temperature.
- In the large lambda, planar limit, kappa approaches 4 pi sqrt(lambda) T^3.
- For a probe in the k-symmetric representation, the D3-brane computation gives kappa = 4 pi k sqrt(lambda) sqrt(1 + k^2 lambda/(16 N^2)) T^3.
- The coefficient tilde gamma for the two-point function on a static world-line is tilde gamma = 3 sqrt(lambda)/pi^2 in the supergravity regime.

## Context

This work extends recent exact relations among observables for probes in conformal field theories established by Correa (2012) and Fiol (2012), who showed that the Bremsstrahlung function and the displacement operator two-point function coefficient are related by tilde gamma = 12 B for any CFT. The paper provides the first exact expression for the momentum diffusion coefficient of an accelerated quark in N=4 SYM at finite coupling and finite N, which was previously only known in the large N, large 't Hooft coupling limit from AdS/CFT computations by Xiao (2008) and Caceres (2010). The results enable potential estimates of momentum fluctuations for heavy quarks in the finite-temperature N=4 SYM plasma, which has been studied extensively via AdS/CFT by Herzog (2006), Gubser (2006), and Casalderrey-Solana (2006, 2007) as a model for quark-gluon plasma.
