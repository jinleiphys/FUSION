# Astrophysical S-factor for $^{16}$O+$^{16}$O within the adiabatic molecular picture

> The astrophysical S-factor for $^{16}$O + $^{16}$O is investigated within the adiabatic molecular picture. It very well explains the available experimental data. The collective radial mass causes a pronounced resonant structure in the S-factor excitation function, providing a motivation for measuring the $^{16}$O + $^{16}$O fusion cross section at deep sub-barrier energies.

## Key claim

The adiabatic molecular picture, using a two-center shell model based on realistic Woods-Saxon potentials and a cranking collective mass, very well explains the available experimental S-factor data for 16O+16O. The claim is conditional on the inclusion of neck formation in the potential and the resonant structure from the radial mass; the authors note that pairing correlations, neglected in the calculation, may somewhat reduce the magnitude of the predicted resonant bump.

## Method

The authors constructed an adiabatic collective potential energy surface V(R) using Strutinsky's macroscopic-microscopic method with a two-center shell model (TCSM) based on Woods-Saxon potentials, and calculated the radial-dependent collective mass parameter M(R) with the cranking mass formula. The macroscopic part of the potential came from the finite-range liquid drop model, while microscopic shell corrections were calculated with a Lanczos-based method. The radial Schrodinger equation was solved exactly with the modified Numerov method and ingoing wave boundary condition to compute the fusion cross section, including only even partial waves due to the identity of the interacting nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The s-wave molecular adiabatic potential has a barrier radius R_b = 8.4 fm.
- The experimental Q-value of the reaction is 16.54 MeV.
- At incident energies below 4 MeV, the S-factor is suppressed by a factor of five compared to that arising from a constant reduced mass.
- The cranking mass produces a local maximum in the S-factor around 4.5 MeV center-of-mass energy.
- The S-factor reduction from the cranking mass starts around 7-8 MeV energy region.
- Experimental data were binned into ΔE = 0.5 MeV energy intervals for presentation.

## Context

This work extends the adiabatic molecular picture, originally developed by the Frankfurt school (Greiner and collaborators) for heavy-ion physics, to the 16O+16O fusion reaction. It contrasts with phenomenological parametrizations that Jiang (2007) showed produce huge uncertainties in the S-factor extrapolation, and with the Fermionic Molecular Dynamics approach of Neff (2008) which does not reproduce the data as well. The results motivate new measurements of the 16O+16O fusion cross section at deep sub-barrier energies around 4-5 MeV to verify the predicted resonant structure, and the authors state that work is in progress for understanding molecular resonance structures in the challenging 12C+12C system.
