# Investigation of Nuclear Modification Factor from RHIC to LHC energies using Boltzmann Transport equation in conjunction with q-Weibull distribution

> The study of nuclear modification factor is crucial in advancing our knowledge of the hot and dense nuclear matter created during high energy heavy-ion collision. In this direction, we have developed a theoretical model for the nuclear modification factor using the Boltzmann Transport equation in relaxation time approximation with the q-Weibull distribution as the final state distribution and studied the experimental data of nuclear modification factor of charged hadrons as well as identified particles at various energies ranging from 7.7 GeV measured at RHIC upto the maximum value of 5.44 TeV studied in LHC. We observed a good agreement between the model and the experimental data as can be quantified using the $χ^2$/NDF values. We have also studied the mass dependence of different fit parameters that appears in the theoretical model and observe a linear mass dependence of some parameters.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a theoretical model for the nuclear modification factor, derived from the Boltzmann Transport Equation in relaxation time approximation with the q-Weibull distribution as the final state distribution, shows good agreement with experimental data across a wide range of collision energies (7.7 GeV to 5.44 TeV) and particle species, but this agreement is qualified by the model's applicability being limited to transverse momenta up to 8 GeV/c, beyond which the impact of suppression on the spectra starts to diminish.

## Method

The authors derived a master equation for the nuclear modification factor (R_AA) from the Boltzmann Transport Equation in relaxation time approximation, using the Boltzmann-Gibbs distribution as the equilibrium distribution and the q-Weibull distribution as the final state distribution. They then fitted this equation to experimental R_AA data for charged hadrons from AuAu, PbPb, and XeXe collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, as well as for identified hadrons (pions, kaons, protons, K*(892)^0, phi, and J/psi) in PbPb collisions at 2.76 and 5.02 TeV, using the ROOT data analysis framework with MINUIT for fitting and chi-squared per degree of freedom to quantify goodness of fit.

## Context

This work extends prior phenomenological studies of the nuclear modification factor using the Boltzmann Transport Equation, such as those by Tripathy (2017), Tripathy (2016), Singh (2023), Qiao (2020), and Moriggi (2022), but differs by expressing the master equation in terms of the final state distribution rather than the initial state distribution. The choice of the q-Weibull distribution for the final state builds on its demonstrated success in fitting transverse momentum spectra over a broader range than Tsallis, Blast-Wave, or Tsallis Blast-Wave models, as shown by Dash (2018) and Gupta (2023). The results enable systematic studies of mass-dependent freeze-out dynamics and the interplay of jet quenching, Cronin enhancement, and radial flow across collision energies from RHIC to LHC.
