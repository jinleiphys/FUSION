# Guiding center hydrodynamics with spin

> We build upon the recently formulated guiding-center kinetic theory for guiding-center plasma by incorporating spin degrees of freedom in the presence of electromagnetic fields. This approach yields a streamlined set of equations for guiding-center ideal hydrodynamics with spin--fewer than those in traditional spin hydrodynamics--owing to a restriction on motion perpendicular to the magnetic field. We propose that this framework offers a promising tool to comprehending how matter behaves under the influence of intense magnetic fields, such as spin polarization effects observed in experimental measurements.

# Guiding center hydrodynamics with spin

## Key claim

The paper extends the recently formulated guiding-center kinetic theory (Son 2024) to include spin degrees of freedom, yielding a streamlined set of ideal spin hydrodynamic equations with fewer equations than traditional spin hydrodynamics, but only under the condition that the magnetic field dominates over the electric field ($B_* \gg E_*$) and motion perpendicular to the magnetic field is restricted.

## Method

The author starts from the covariant guiding-center kinetic theory for a plasma in a strong magnetic field, where the cyclotron radius is smaller than the mean free path, and incorporates spin-1/2 degrees of freedom via a single-particle spin-dependent distribution function expanded to linear order in the spin polarization tensor $\omega_{\mu\nu}$. The equilibrium distribution functions are used to compute moments giving the charge current, energy-momentum tensor, and spin tensor, with the phase-space measure restricted to enforce momentum parallel to the magnetic field while retaining averaged perpendicular momentum contributions through an effective mass $\tilde m^2 = m^2 + \overline{p_\perp^2}$. The constitutive relations are derived for the longitudinal and transverse components of the charge current and energy-momentum tensor, and the spin tensor is computed explicitly, with entropy conservation verified at leading order.

## Key numbers

- Effective mass defined as $\tilde m^2 = m^2 + \overline{p_\perp^2}$, where $\overline{p_\perp^2}$ is the gyromotion-averaged squared perpendicular momentum
- Longitudinal number density: $n_0 = \frac{B_*}{(2\pi)^2} \, \tilde{m} \, K_1(\tilde{z})$ where $\tilde{z} = \tilde{m}/T$ and $K_1$ is the modified Bessel function
- Energy density: $\epsilon_0 = \frac{B_*}{(2\pi)^2}\tilde{m}\,T \left(\tilde z K_0(\tilde z)+K_1 (\tilde z)\right)$
- Longitudinal pressure: $P_0 = \frac{B_*}{(2\pi)^2}\tilde m \, T\, K_1(\tilde z)$, satisfying $P_0 = n_0 T$
- Transverse pressure: $P_\perp = 2 \cosh(\xi)\frac{B_*}{(2\pi)^2}(\tilde m^2 - m^2)K_0(\tilde z)$, related to magnetization by $P_\perp = -M B_*$
- Spin tensor leading term: $S^{\lambda,\mu\nu}_1 = \frac{P}{4} \beta^\lambda \, \omega^{\mu\nu}$ where $\beta^\mu = u^\mu/T$

## Context

This work builds directly on the guiding-center kinetic theory formulated by Son (2024) and extends it by incorporating spin degrees of freedom using the spin-dependent distribution function approach of Florkowski et al. (2018). It contrasts with traditional spin hydrodynamics frameworks (Florkowski 2017, Florkowski 2018, Bhadury 2020) which contain more equations due to unrestricted perpendicular motion. The framework is motivated by the unresolved puzzle of spin polarization in hyperons observed at RHIC (STAR 2017, STAR 2019) and ALICE (2019), and by the need to understand how intense electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions couple to spin degrees of freedom. The work enables future extensions to dissipative effects, nonlinear spin feedback, and coupling to dynamical Maxwell fields.
