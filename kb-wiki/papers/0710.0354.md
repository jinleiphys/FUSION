# The QCD Equation of State with almost Physical Quark Masses

> We present results on the equation of state in QCD with two light quark flavors and a heavier strange quark. Calculations with improved staggered fermions have been performed on lattices with temporal extent Nt =4 and 6 on a line of constant physics with almost physical quark mass values; the pion mass is about 220 MeV, and the strange quark mass is adjusted to its physical value. High statistics results on large lattices are obtained for bulk thermodynamic observables, i.e. pressure, energy and entropy density, at vanishing quark chemical potential for a wide range of temperatures, 140 MeV < T < 800 MeV. We present a detailed discussion of finite cut-off effects which become particularly significant for temperatures larger than about twice the transition temperature. At these high temperatures we also performed calculations of the trace anomaly on lattices with temporal extent Nt=8. Furthermore, we have performed an extensive analysis of zero temperature observables including the light and strange quark condensates and the static quark potential at zero temperature. These are used to set the temperature scale for thermodynamic observables and to calculate renormalized observables that are sensitive to deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration and become order parameters in the infinite and zero quark mass limits, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper presents lattice QCD calculations of the equation of state with two light and one heavier strange quark at almost physical quark masses (pion mass about 220 MeV), but the results are obtained on relatively coarse lattices with temporal extent Nτ = 4 and 6, and the authors acknowledge that finite cut-off effects become "particularly significant" for temperatures larger than about twice the transition temperature, requiring additional Nτ = 8 calculations at high temperatures to control these discretization errors.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations using a tree-level Symanzik-improved gauge action and an improved staggered fermion action (p4fat3 action) on lattices with temporal extent Nτ = 4 and 6, covering temperatures from 140 MeV to 800 MeV. They computed the trace anomaly (ε-3p)/T⁴ from the difference between finite-temperature and zero-temperature expectation values of the gauge action density and quark condensates, then obtained the pressure by integrating the trace anomaly over temperature, and derived the energy and entropy densities from standard thermodynamic relations.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: approximately 220 MeV (almost physical, with strange quark mass set to its physical value)
- Temperature range covered: 140 MeV ≤ T ≤ 800 MeV
- Lattice temporal extents used: Nτ = 4, 6, and additionally Nτ = 8 at high temperatures (T ≳ 350 MeV)
- Trace anomaly (ε-3p)/T⁴ values on Nτ = 4 lattices range from 0.54(29) at β=3.150 to 0.188(21) at β=3.920
- Pressure p/T⁴ values on Nτ = 4 lattices range from 0.0639 at β=3.150 to 4.7156 at β=3.920
- Trace anomaly (ε-3p)/T⁴ values on Nτ = 6 lattices range from 0.51(25) at β=3.335 to 0.599(78) at β=4.080
- Pressure p/T⁴ values on Nτ = 6 lattices range from 0.0480 at β=3.335 to 4.7085 at β=4.080
- Static quark potential parameter r₀/a ranges from 1.467(72) at β=3.150 to 10.39(23) at β=4.080
- Light quark condensate ⟨ψ̄ψ⟩_{l,τ} on Nτ = 4 lattices ranges from 0.28165(22) at β=3.150 to 0.00154411(10) at β=3.920

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of the QCD equation of state using improved staggered fermion actions, particularly the p4-action (Peikert, 2001) and the asqtad action (MILC Collaboration, 2007), by using almost physical quark masses (pion mass ~220 MeV) rather than heavier quarks. The results enable more reliable comparisons with heavy ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC, and provide a benchmark for perturbative calculations at high temperatures (Kapusta, 1979; Zhai, 1995; Arnold, 2000; Vuorinen, 2003; Kajantie, 2003) and hadron resonance gas models (Redlich, 2004). The paper also provides the temperature scale and renormalized observables needed for future continuum extrapolations with finer lattices.
