# Electromagnetic field and the chiral magnetic effect in the quark-gluon plasma

> Time evolution of electromagnetic field created in heavy-ion collisions strongly depends on the electromagnetic response of the quark-gluon plasma, which can be described by the Ohmic and chiral conductivities. The later is intimately related to the Chiral Magnetic Effect. I argue that a solution to the classical Maxwell equations at finite chiral conductivity is unstable due to the soft modes $k<σ_χ$ that grow exponentially with time. In the kinematical region relevant for the relativistic heavy-ion collisions, I derive analytical expressions for the magnetic field of a point charge. I show that finite chiral conductivity causes oscillations of magnetic field at early times.

## Key claim
The paper argues that a finite chiral conductivity σ_χ in the quark-gluon plasma causes an instability in the classical Maxwell equations for soft modes with wave number k < σ_χ, which grow exponentially with time; however, for realistic heavy-ion collision conditions this instability is negligible because the plasma size is much smaller than 1/σ_χ.

## Method
The author solves the Maxwell-Chern-Simons equations in a conducting medium with constant Ohmic conductivity σ and constant chiral conductivity σ_χ, first for free electromagnetic waves to derive the dispersion relation, then for the field of a point charge moving at ultra-relativistic speed. Analytical expressions for the magnetic field components are obtained in the diffusion approximation (ω ≪ σ v² γ², ω ≪ vγ k⊥), which applies to the entire QGP lifetime for γ ≈ 100.

## Key numbers
- Electrical conductivity of the QGP at critical temperature: σ = 5.8 MeV (from Ding (2010), Aarts (2007), Amato (2013), Cassing (2013)).
- For a heavy-ion collision at γ = 100, the diffusion approximation applies for x₋ ≫ 1/(σ v² γ²) ≈ 3·10⁻³ fm and x₋ ≫ b/(vγ) ≈ 0.1 fm for b ≈ 10 fm.
- The smallest radius of a spherical magnetic knot solution is R₀ ≈ κ₀/σ_χ with κ₀ ≈ 4.5; for realistic σ_χ this gives R₀ ≈ 200 fm, much larger than the QGP transverse size R_A ≈ 6–10 fm.
- The maximal growth rate of unstable modes is (√(σ² + σ_χ²) − σ)/2, occurring at k = σ_χ/2.
- The magnetic field components B_r and B_z are given by explicit formulas (Eqs. g34 and g38) that contain the factor exp(−b²σ/(4x₋)) and oscillatory terms sin(b²σ_χ/(4x₋)) and cos(b²σ_χ/(4x₋)).

## Context
This work extends the analytical treatment of electromagnetic fields in the QGP by Tuchin (2010, 2013) and Gursoy (2014), which considered only Ohmic conductivity, to include the chiral magnetic effect via a constant chiral conductivity σ_χ. The model follows the approach of McLerran (2013) and Chernodub (2010) for incorporating the chiral anomaly. The results enable future magneto-hydrodynamical simulations to account for the oscillatory behavior of magnetic field at early times, which may weaken magnetic-field-dependent phenomena such as charge separation (Kharzeev 2007) and B-dependent elliptic flow (Tuchin 2011, Mohapatra 2011).
