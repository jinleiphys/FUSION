# Baryon photo-decay amplitudes at the pole

> We derive relations for baryon photo-decay amplitudes both for the Breit-Wigner and the pole positions. With an updated SAID partial wave analysis, technically similar to the earliest Virginia Tech analysis of photoproduction data, we compare photo-decay amplitudes at both resonance positions for a few selected nucleon resonances. Comparisons are made and a qualitative similarity, seen between the pole and Breit-Wigner values extracted by the Bonn-Gatchina group, is confirmed in the present study.

## Key claim

The authors confirm that pole and Breit-Wigner photo-decay amplitudes for selected nucleon resonances are qualitatively similar in modulus, as previously reported by the Bonn-Gatchina group, but note that the pole extraction is more reliable in cases where the Breit-Wigner determination is problematic.

## Method

The authors derive relations connecting multipole residues to photo-decay amplitudes at both the Breit-Wigner and pole positions. They apply these relations using an updated SAID partial wave analysis of pion photoproduction data, based on a simple K-matrix approach originally used in the earliest Virginia Tech analysis (Workman et al., 1990), fitting data from threshold to a lab photon energy of 2 GeV. The multipole residues are extracted from known pi N pole positions and residues, with the energy-dependent pre-factors evaluated at the pole position.

## Key numbers

* Delta(1232) 3/2+: Breit-Wigner A_{1/2} = -141 +/- 3, A_{3/2} = -258 +/- 5 (units 10^{-3} x GeV^{-1/2}); pole A_{1/2} = -135 +/- 5 [-17 deg], A_{3/2} = -255 +/- 5 [-5 deg]
* N(1440) 1/2+: Breit-Wigner A_{1/2} = -60 +/- 2; pole A_{1/2} = -65 +/- 5 [-37 deg]
* N(1520) 3/2-: Breit-Wigner A_{1/2} = -19 +/- 2, A_{3/2} = +153 +/- 3; pole A_{1/2} = -22 +/- 3 [-10 deg], A_{3/2} = +156 +/- 6 [+11 deg]
* N(1535) 1/2-: Breit-Wigner A_{1/2} = +92 +/- 5; pole A_{1/2} = +77 +/- 5 [+5 deg]
* N(1650) 1/2-: Breit-Wigner A_{1/2} = +35 +/- 5; pole A_{1/2} = +36 +/- 3 [-16 deg]
* For the N(1440), the modulus of the elastic residue shifted from 108 MeV (1990) to 38 MeV (present); for the N(1535), from 54 MeV to 16 MeV

## Context

This work extends the early SAID photoproduction analysis (Workman et al., 1990) by comparing Breit-Wigner and pole extractions of photo-decay amplitudes, confirming the qualitative similarity reported by the Bonn-Gatchina group (2012). The results enable more reliable extraction of resonance parameters, particularly for states like the N(1650) where Breit-Wigner fits to the underlying pi N amplitude produce unreliable width and elasticity values. The pole determination also shows better stability for the E2/M1 ratio of the Delta(1232) compared to Breit-Wigner fits, as noted in earlier studies (Hanstein et al., 1998; Workman et al., 1999).
