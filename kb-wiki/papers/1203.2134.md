# Suppression, persistence and reentrance of superfluidity in overflowing nuclear systems

> Based on a microscopic description of superfluidity in overflowing nuclear systems, it is shown that continuum coupling plays an important role in the suppression, the persistence and the reentrance of pairing. In such systems, the structure of the drip-line nucleus determines the suppression and the persistence of superfluidity. The reentrance of pairing with increasing temperature leads to additional critical temperatures between the normal and superfluid phases.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the suppression or persistence of superfluidity in overflowing nuclear systems depends on whether the drip-line nucleus is magic (suppression) or non-magic (persistence), and that a reentrance of pairing with increasing temperature can occur when resonance states are present but too high in energy to be occupied at zero temperature.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations in coordinate representation for eight isotopic chains (Ni, Kr, Sr, Zr, Mo, Ru, Sn, Te) using the Skyrme SLy4 interaction in the mean-field channel and the ISS pairing force. All bound states were considered with angular momentum up to J_max=27/2, using a box radius of 26.7 fm with mixed Dirichlet-Von Neumann boundary conditions to describe the continuum. Finite-temperature HFB calculations were then employed to study pairing reentrance in selected Sn isotopes.

## Key numbers

- The box radius used for calculations was R_box=26.7 fm
- For the group A1 isotopes (Ni, Zr, Mo, Ru), the total occupation number of resonance states N_res for drip-line nuclei ranges from 2.0 (Ni) to 8.0 (Mo)
- For the group A2 isotopes (Kr, Sr, Sn, Te), the resonance occupation number N_res is 0.0 for all drip-line nuclei
- For the overflowing system 180Sn, three critical temperatures were identified: T_c1 ~ 11 keV (superfluid to normal transition in the neutron gas), T_c2 ~ 300 keV (reappearance of superfluidity), and T_c3 ~ 1 MeV (final vanishing of superfluidity)
- The critical temperature for normal superfluidity follows T_c = 0.57 * Delta(T=0)

## Context

This work extends the earlier prediction of pairing suppression in overflowing Z=50 systems by Grasso (2008) and the generic quenching prediction by Schuck (2011), which was limited to s-wave (l=0) single-particle states. The present HFB calculations include higher angular momentum states and continuum coupling, showing that suppression only occurs when continuum coupling is quenched by shell structure (magic drip-line nuclei). The predicted reentrance of superfluidity with temperature is based on a novel mechanism involving resonance states, distinct from previously known reentrance mechanisms in odd-nuclei (Balian, 1999), rotating nuclei (Dean, 2010), or asymmetric cold atom gases (Castorina, 2005; Levin, 2006). The results have implications for understanding the transition between inner and outer crusts in neutron stars, where cooling temperatures of 10 to 500 keV coincide with the predicted reentrance critical temperatures.
