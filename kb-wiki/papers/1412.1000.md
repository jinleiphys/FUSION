# The Non-Classical Boltzmann Equation, and Diffusion-Based Approximations to the Boltzmann Equation

> We show that several diffusion-based approximations (classical diffusion or SP1, SP2, SP3) to the linear Boltzmann equation can (for an infinite, homogeneous medium) be represented exactly by a non-classical transport equation. As a consequence, we indicate a method to solve diffusion-based approximations to the Boltzmann equation via Monte Carlo, with only statistical errors - no truncation errors.

## Key claim

Several diffusion-based approximations (classical diffusion, SP1, SP2, SP3) to the linear Boltzmann equation can be represented exactly by a non-classical transport equation, but only for an infinite, homogeneous medium with isotropic scattering and under the conditions that the scattering ratio is less than one and the source and flux vanish at infinity.

## Method

The authors convert the non-classical Boltzmann equation, which includes path-length-dependent total cross sections, into an integral equation for the scalar flux. They then derive the Green's functions for the classical diffusion, SP2, and SP3 approximations to the standard Boltzmann equation and convert those into integral equations of the same form. By matching the integral equations, they determine the specific path-length-dependent total cross section and path-length distribution function that make the non-classical transport equation reproduce each diffusion-based approximation.

## Key numbers

- For classical diffusion: the path-length distribution is \(p(s) = 3\Sigma_t^2 s e^{-\sqrt{3}\Sigma_t s}\), giving a mean free path \(\bar s = 2/(\sqrt{3}\Sigma_t)\).
- For SP2: the path-length distribution is \(p(s) = \frac{5}{9}\Lambda^2 s e^{-\Lambda s} + \frac{4}{9}\delta(s)\) with \(\Lambda = \sqrt{5/3}\,\Sigma_t\), giving a mean free path \(\bar s = \sqrt{20/27}\,(1/\Sigma_t)\).
- For SP3: the path-length distribution is \(p(s) = \Sigma_t^2 s (A^+ e^{-\Sigma_t \lambda^+ s} + A^- e^{-\Sigma_t \lambda^- s})\) with \(\lambda^+ = 2.941340\), \(\lambda^- = 1.161256\), \(A^+ = 5.642025\), and \(A^- = 0.469086\).

## Context

This work extends the non-classical transport theory introduced by Larsen (2007) and developed further by Larsen (2011) and Frank (2010) for inhomogeneous random media with correlated scattering centers. It also connects to the SPN hierarchy of approximations to transport theory, originally derived by Gelbard (1960, 1961, 1962) and later justified theoretically by Larsen, Morel, and McGhee (1993) and Tomasevic and Larsen (1996). The results enable solving diffusion-based approximations to the Boltzmann equation via Monte Carlo methods with only statistical errors and no truncation errors, which the authors state has not been done before.
