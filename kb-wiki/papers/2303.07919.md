# Model dependence of the number of participant nucleons and observable consequences in heavy-ion collisions

> The centrality determination and the estimated fluctuations of number of participant nucleons $N_{part}$ in Au-Au collisions at 1.23 $A$GeV beam kinetic energy suffers from severe model dependencies. Comparing the Glauber Monte Carlo (MC) and UrQMD transport models, it is shown that $N_{part}$ is a strongly model dependant quantity. In addition, for any given centrality class, Glauber MC and UrQMD predicts drastically different $N_{part}$ distributions. The impact parameter $b$ and the number of charged particles $N_{ch}$ on the other hand are much more correlated and give an almost model independent centrality estimator. It is suggested that the total baryon number balance, from integrated rapidity distributions, can be used instead of $N_{part}$ in experiments. Preliminary HADES data show significant differences to both, UrQMD simulations and STAR data in this respect.

## Key claim

The number of participant nucleons \(N_{part}\) in Au-Au collisions at 1.23 \(A\)GeV is a strongly model-dependent quantity, with Glauber Monte Carlo and UrQMD transport models predicting drastically different \(N_{part}\) distributions for any given centrality class, even when the charged-particle multiplicity distributions are fitted to match.

## Method

The authors compared the Glauber Monte Carlo model, which treats collisions as instantaneous independent inelastic binary scatterings of nucleons on straight trajectories, with the cascade version of the UrQMD transport model (version 3.5), which propagates hadrons and includes both elastic and inelastic nucleon-nucleon interactions. For Au-Au collisions at 1.23 \(A\)GeV, they generated \(7.5 \times 10^{5}\) events per model and fitted the Glauber MC to the UrQMD charged-track multiplicity distribution within the HADES detector acceptance (18 to 85 degrees polar angle) using a Negative Binomial Distribution. They then compared the impact parameter and \(N_{part}\) distributions for centrality classes defined either by the Glauber fit or directly from UrQMD.

## Key numbers

- The Glauber MC uses \(\sigma_{inel}^{NN} = 23.8\) mb for the collision criterion; the total proton-proton cross section used by UrQMD at 1.23 \(A\)GeV is 47.8 mb.
- For the 0-40% centrality class, the UrQMD \(N_{part}\) distribution peaks at around 385 participants, while the Glauber MC \(N_{part}\) distribution peaks at around 100 participants, a factor of about 4 lower.
- The mean number of participants \(\langle N_{part} \rangle\) from Glauber MC is smaller by up to a factor of approximately 2 than the \(\langle N_{part} \rangle\) from UrQMD for corresponding centrality classes.
- The standard deviation of the impact parameter \(\sigma_b\) differs by only about 0.1 fm between the three scenarios (Glauber MC, UrQMD, and Glauber centrality cuts applied to UrQMD).
- The Glauber MC \(N_{part}\) distributions have the largest variance (\(\sigma_{N_{part}}\) about 30) for most central collisions (5-10% centrality), while UrQMD \(N_{part}\) distributions have the largest variance for mid-central collisions (about 40-45% centrality).

## Context

This work extends the critique of Glauber MC assumptions at low beam energies, where elastic and inelastic cross sections are comparable, building on earlier studies showing spectator disintegration in fluid dynamics (Baumgardt 1975) and strong spectator-system interactions in transport models (Reichert 2023). The findings directly impact the HADES experiment at SIS-18 and the STAR experiment at RHIC at \(\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=3\) GeV, where Glauber MC-based centrality selection introduces large uncertainties in higher-order proton cumulant analyses (HADES 2020, STAR 2022). The paper suggests using total baryon number balance from integrated rapidity distributions as an alternative to \(N_{part}\) for experimental centrality determination.
