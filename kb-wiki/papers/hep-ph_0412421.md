# Electromagnetic N->Delta transition and neutron form factors

> The C2/M1 ratio of the electromagnetic N->Delta(1232) transition, which is important for determining the geometric shape of the nucleon, is shown to be related to the neutron elastic form factor ratio G_C^n/G_M^n. The proposed relation holds with good accuracy for the entire range of momentum transfers where data are available.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the ratio of charge quadrupole to magnetic dipole form factors in the electromagnetic N to Delta(1232) transition (C2/M1 ratio) follows the same curve as the ratio of elastic neutron charge to magnetic form factors (G_C^n/G_M^n) across the entire range of momentum transfers where data exist, with the relation holding within experimental uncertainties of order 10 percent from third-order SU(6) symmetry breaking.

## Method

The author derived an SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry relation connecting the N to Delta charge quadrupole transition form factor G_{C2}^{N to Delta} to the elastic neutron charge form factor G_C^n, and combined it with the known relation between the magnetic dipole transition form factor G_{M1}^{N to Delta} and the neutron magnetic form factor G_M^n. The resulting expression for the C2/M1 ratio was then compared with experimental data from pion-electroproduction experiments and with elastic neutron form factor measurements from double-polarization experiments across the range Q^2 = 0 to 4 GeV^2.

## Key numbers

- At Q^2 = 0, the predicted C2/M1 ratio is -0.031, compared with experimental values of -0.030(03) from Blanpied (2001) and -0.025(02) from Beck (1997)
- At Q^2 = 0.15 GeV^2, the calculated ratio R_n(exp) from neutron form factor data is -0.050(11), compared with C2/M1(exp) of -0.055(04) from Gothe (2001)
- At Q^2 = 1.45 GeV^2, R_n(exp) is -0.077(07) from Madey (2003), compared with C2/M1(exp) of -0.077(16) from Joo (2002)
- The asymptotic Q^2 to infinity limit of the C2/M1 ratio is predicted to be -0.061
- The static limit relation gives C2/M1(0) = -(M_Delta^2 - M_N^2)/(2 M_Delta) * (M_N/12) * (r_n^2 / mu_n) = -0.031

## Context

This work extends the known SU(6) relation between the N to Delta magnetic dipole transition form factor and the neutron magnetic form factor, originally derived by Beg (1964), to include a new relation connecting the charge quadrupole transition form factor to the neutron charge form factor. The paper enables using elastic neutron form factor data to extract information about the geometric shape of the nucleon, and conversely allows determination of the elastic neutron charge form factor from pion-electroproduction data on the N to Delta transition. The results are consistent with perturbative QCD predictions for the asymptotic behavior of the C2/M1 ratio from Idilbi (2004).
