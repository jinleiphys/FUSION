# Transverse Enhancement Model and MiniBooNE Charge Current Quasi-Elastic Neutrino Scattering Data

> Recently proposed Transverse Enhancement Model of nuclear effects in Charge Current Quasi-Elastic neutrino scattering [A. Bodek, H. S. Budd, and M. E. Christy, Eur. Phys. J. C{\bf 71} (2011) 1726] is confronted with the MiniBooNE high statistics experimental data. It is shown that the {\it effective} large axial mass model leads to better agreement with the data.

# Transverse Enhancement Model and MiniBooNE Charge Current Quasi-Elastic Neutrino Scattering Data

## Key claim
The Transverse Enhancement Model (TEM) of nuclear effects in charge current quasi-elastic (CCQE) neutrino scattering, when confronted with MiniBooNE high-statistics data, yields larger chi-squared values than the effective large axial mass model (ELAMM) with M_A = 1.35 GeV, indicating that ELAMM provides better agreement with the data. The hedge is that the comparison is based on shape-only fits with a 10.7% normalization uncertainty, and the TEM may still be relevant for describing multinucleon ejection effects in hadronic final states.

## Method
The author used the NuWro Monte Carlo event generator to produce three samples of 10^6 events each, comparing predictions from three models: (A) ELAMM with M_A = 1.35 GeV and a Fermi Gas (FG) model with Fermi momentum p_F = 220 MeV and binding energy B = 34 MeV; (B) TEM with standard axial mass M_A = 1.014 GeV, implemented in two variants, one without Fermi motion and with Pauli blocking via the NEUGEN Q^2-dependent reduction function, and another (TEM-FG) with Fermi motion and Pauli blocking via the FG model. A chi-squared statistical test with a 10.7% normalization error was applied to both the two-dimensional (2D) double differential cross section in muon kinetic energy and scattering angle (137 bins) and the Q^2_QE differential cross section (17 bins), including a low momentum transfer cut at q_cut = 400 MeV/c.

## Key numbers
- ELAMM 2D fit: λ = 1.03, χ²_min = 34.1 for 136 degrees of freedom (DOF), giving χ²/DOF ≈ 0.25.
- TEM (no Fermi motion) 2D fit: λ = 1.03, χ²_min = 196.2 for 136 DOF.
- TEM-FG 2D fit: λ = 1.135, χ²_min = 133.3 for 136 DOF.
- ELAMM Q² fit: λ = 1.075, χ²_min = 15.8 for 16 DOF.
- TEM Q² fit: λ = 1.015, χ²_min = 22.3 for 16 DOF.
- TEM-FG Q² fit: λ = 1.08, χ²_min = 44.0 for 16 DOF.
- With low momentum cut q_cut = 400 MeV/c, 2D χ²_min values reduced to 170.8 (TEM), 98.7 (TEM-FG), and 23.3 (ELAMM), with λ increased by 2–3%.
- Three selected 2D bins (T_μ in 500–600 MeV, cosθ_μ in -0.1 to 0.2) contributed 9.5, 7.2, and 5.5 to TEM χ²_min; these bins correspond to Q²_QE bins of (1.2, 1.5) GeV² and (1.5, 2) GeV² with cross sections 5.42×10⁻⁴⁸ cm² and 1.44×10⁻⁴⁷ cm²/MeV² respectively.

## Context
This work tests the Transverse Enhancement Model proposed by Bodek, Budd, and Christy (2011), which rescales magnetic nucleon form factors to describe nuclear effects in CCQE scattering, against the MiniBooNE high-statistics CCQE data. The paper extends earlier work by Martini, Ericson, Chanfray, and Marteau (the MEChM model) and by Nieves et al. (2011), who evaluated multinucleon ejection contributions and obtained a fitted axial mass of M_A = 1.077 ± 0.027 GeV with a normalization scale λ = 0.917 ± 0.029. The results enable future work to focus on providing predictions for hadronic final states in multinucleon ejection, which both ELAMM and TEM describe effectively but without microscopic detail.
