# Effect of $f(R,T)$ theory of gravity on the properties of strange quark stars

> In this study, we investigate strange quark stars within the framework of modified $f(R,T)$ gravity, where $R$ represents the Ricci scalar and $T$ denotes the trace of the energy-momentum tensor, specifically defined as $ f(R,T) = R + 2χT $. The equation of state is obtained with the different forms of the MIT bag model and quark mass model with medium effects and self-consistent thermodynamical treatment. We find that negative values of $χ$ significantly increase both the mass and radius of the quark star. The inclusion of $χ$ helps to satisfy recent the astrophysical constraints on the mass-radius relationship. We have also constrained the values of $χ$ for each EoS, based on the observed maximum mass and corresponding radius, demonstrating that the inclusion of this parameter helps to address the challenges posed by both the GW190814 event and NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451. We also observe that the inclusion of $f(R,T)$ gravity leads to an increase in both the maximum mass, by about $ (0.23- 0.27)~M_{\odot}$, and the corresponding radius, by approximately (1.5-2.0)~\text{km}, depending on the chosen equation of state.

## Key claim
Negative values of the coupling parameter χ in the f(R,T) = R + 2χT gravity theory significantly increase both the maximum mass and radius of strange quark stars, with the increase in maximum mass being about (0.23-0.27) solar masses and the corresponding radius increase being approximately (1.5-2.0) km, depending on the chosen equation of state. The inclusion of χ helps satisfy astrophysical constraints from the GW190814 event and NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451, but the specific χ values that work depend on which quark matter equation of state is used.

## Method
The authors solved the modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations derived from f(R,T) gravity with f(R,T) = R + 2χT, using six different quark matter equations of state: four variants of the MIT bag model (constant bag pressure, constant bag pressure with vector interactions, chemical potential-dependent bag pressure, and density-dependent bag pressure) and two variants of the quark mass model (chemical potential-dependent and density-dependent quark masses). All equations of state included medium effects, vector interactions, and self-consistent thermodynamic treatment, with model parameters constrained by the Bodmer-Witten stability conjecture. The mass-radius relations were computed for various χ values and compared against observational constraints from HESS J1731-347, GW170817, GW190814, PSR J0030+0451, and PSR J0740+6620.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass increase when χ changes from 0 to -2.5: (0.23-0.27) solar masses, depending on the equation of state
- Corresponding radius increase: (1.5-2.0) km, depending on the equation of state
- Central energy density at which maximum mass is reached: (400-600) MeV fm^{-3}
- Quark masses used: m_u = 2.0 MeV, m_d = 4.67 MeV, m_s = 93.4 MeV
- For the MIT bag model without vector interactions, bag pressure set to 60 MeV fm^{-3}
- For the vector MIT bag model: B = 65 MeV fm^{-3}, G_V = 0.2 fm^{-2}
- For the chemical potential-dependent bag model: B_0 = 50 MeV fm^{-3}, B_as = 30 MeV fm^{-3}, β_μ = 20, G_V = 0.2 fm^{-2}
- For the density-dependent bag model: B_0 = 60 MeV fm^{-3}, B_as = 20 MeV fm^{-3}, β_ρ = 0.1, G_V = 0.25 fm^{-2}
- For the chemical potential-dependent quark mass model: α_μ = 20, g_0 = 1.0, B_0 = 50 MeV fm^{-3}, G_V = 0.2 fm^{-2}
- For the density-dependent quark mass model: sqrt(C) = 150 MeV, a = 1.0, G_V = 0.2 fm^{-2}

## Context
This work extends previous studies of strange quark stars in f(R,T) gravity by using realistic equations of state with proper conservation laws, medium effects, and self-consistent thermodynamic treatment, whereas earlier work used simple MIT bag models without charge neutrality and chemical equilibrium conditions. The study addresses challenges posed by the GW190814 event (a compact object with mass 2.50-2.67 solar masses) and NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451, which are difficult to satisfy with standard general relativity and simple quark matter models. The results enable future work to constrain the f(R,T) coupling parameter χ for different quark matter equations of state and to identify which models best satisfy multiple observational constraints simultaneously.
