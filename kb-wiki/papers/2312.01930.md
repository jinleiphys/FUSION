# Solving the left-hand cut problem in lattice QCD: $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ from finite volume energy levels

> We discuss a novel effective-field-theory-based approach for extracting two-body scattering information from finite volume energies, serving as an alternative to Lüscher's method. By explicitly incorporating one-pion exchange, we overcome the challenging left-hand cut problem in Lüscher's method and can handle finite volume energy levels both below and above the left-hand cut. Applied to the lattice data for $DD^*$ scattering at a pion mass of 280 MeV, as an illustrative example, our results reveal the significant impact of the one-pion exchange on P-wave and S-wave phase shifts. The pole position of the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ state, extracted from the finite-volume energy levels at this pion mass while taking into account left-hand cut effects, range corrections and partial-wave mixing, is consistent with a near-threshold resonance. This study demonstrates, for the first time, that two-body scattering information can be reliably extracted from lattice spectra including the left-hand cut.

# Solving the left-hand cut problem in lattice QCD: $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ from finite volume energy levels

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that an effective-field-theory-based approach explicitly incorporating one-pion exchange can reliably extract two-body scattering information from finite volume energy levels including the left-hand cut region, which Lüscher's method cannot handle. The extracted pole position of the $T_{cc}(3875)^+$ state at a pion mass of 280 MeV is consistent with a near-threshold resonance, but with the caveat that the OPE shifts the pole into the complex plane, making it a below-threshold resonance with 85% probability within 1-sigma uncertainty.

## Method
The authors construct a chiral effective field theory Hamiltonian up to O(Q^2) that includes one-pion exchange and contact interactions in the relevant partial waves (^3S_1 and ^3P_0). They solve the finite-volume eigenvalue problem using a plane wave basis, which naturally accounts for partial-wave mixing from cubic box symmetry breaking, and fit the low-energy constants to the nine lowest-lying lattice energy levels from Padmanath (2022) in the irreps T_1^+(0), A_1^-(0), and A_2(1). The resulting Hamiltonian is then used to calculate infinite-volume scattering amplitudes and extract phase shifts and pole positions.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass: m_pi = 280 MeV; lattice spacing a = 0.08636 fm
- D meson mass: M_D = 1927 MeV; D* meson mass: M_D* = 2049 MeV
- Two spatial lattice sizes: L = 2.07 fm and L = 2.76 fm
- Left-hand cut branch point: (p_lhc^{1pi})^2 = -(126 MeV)^2, corresponding to (p_lhc/E_DD*)^2 = -0.001
- Pion decay constant at this pion mass: f_pi = 105.3 MeV
- Coupling constant: g = 0.517 +/- 0.015
- Fit 2 (with OPE) scattering length in ^3S_1: a = 1.46 +/- 0.57 fm
- Fit 2 effective range in ^3S_1: r = 0.096 +/- 0.53 fm
- Fit 2 T_cc pole position relative to DD* threshold: delta m = (-6.6 +/- 1.5) - i(4.0 +/- 3.7) MeV
- Fit 2 chi^2/dof = 2.95/6

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Padmanath (2022), who used Lüscher's method and effective range expansion to extract DD* phase shifts from lattice data, but whose results were questioned by Du (2023) regarding the importance of the one-pion exchange left-hand cut. The approach provides an alternative to the modified Lüscher formula proposed by Raposo (2023), which had no practical implementation to lattice data. The method enables future analyses of nucleon-nucleon scattering, hyperon-nucleon scattering, and other hadronic systems where left-hand cut effects are significant, and can directly extract the OPE strength g/f_pi from lattice data if energy level uncertainties are substantially reduced.
