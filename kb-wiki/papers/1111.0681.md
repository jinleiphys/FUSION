# Chiral Symmetry Breaking, Trace Anomaly and Baryons in Hot and Dense Matter

> We propose an effective chiral Lagrangian with a chiral scalar introduced as a dilaton associated with broken conformal symmetry and responsible for the trace anomaly in QCD and discuss the properties of hadronic matter at high density and temperature. As the "dilaton limit" is taken, which drives a system from nuclear matter density to near chiral restoration density, a linear sigma model emerges from the highly non-linear structure. A striking prediction is that as the dilaton limit is approached, the omega-nucleon interaction gets strongly suppressed at high density. This is shown to be a firm statement at the quantum level protected by an infrared fixed point of the renormalization group equations derived in chiral perturbation theory.

# Chiral Symmetry Breaking, Trace Anomaly and Baryons in Hot and Dense Matter

## Key claim
The omega-nucleon interaction gets strongly suppressed at high density as the "dilaton limit" is approached, and this suppression is a firm statement at the quantum level protected by an infrared fixed point of the renormalization group equations derived in chiral perturbation theory. The claim is conditional on the system being driven from nuclear matter density to near chiral restoration density, where a linear sigma model emerges from the highly non-linear structure.

## Method
The author constructs an effective chiral Lagrangian with a chiral scalar introduced as a dilaton associated with broken conformal symmetry, implementing the QCD trace anomaly. The hidden local symmetric (HLS) Lagrangian is linearized by taking the "dilaton limit" (kappa = g_A = g_V = 1), which transforms the non-linear chiral Lagrangian into a linear sigma model. The analysis is performed for two alternative chirality assignments to nucleons (the "naive" assignment and the mirror assignment), and quantum loop corrections are evaluated using chiral perturbation theory with HLS and heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers
- The chiral invariant mass m_0 is estimated as 210 MeV, derived from the gluon condensate at pseudo-critical temperature T_ch ~ 170 MeV, where the condensate is approximately 1/2 of its zero-temperature value.
- The omega-nucleon coupling squared g_VN^2 = (g * (1 - g_V))^2 goes to zero as g_V approaches 1 in the dilaton limit.
- The ratio g_VN^2 / m_V^2, which governs nuclear forces, scales as (1 - g_V)^2.
- The neutron star mass of 1.97 +/- 0.04 solar masses is referenced as a constraint that the softened equation of state could potentially satisfy.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Beane and van Kolck (1994) on the dilaton limit and builds on the hidden local symmetry framework of Bando et al. (1985). It connects to the half-skyrmion phase identified by Lee and Rho (2009) and the vector manifestation fixed point of Harada and Yamawaki (2003). The paper enables future calculations of the equation of state for compact star matter, particularly investigating how the suppression of short-range repulsion and symmetry energy could soften the equation of state at high density without requiring exotic matter such as kaon condensation or strange quark matter.
