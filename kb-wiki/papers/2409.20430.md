# Breakdown of collinear factorisation in the photoproduction of a $ π^{0}γ$ pair with large invariant mass

> We identify a $ 2 \to 3 $ exclusive process, where collinear factorisation is broken, namely the exclusive photoproduction of a $ π^{0}γ$ pair with large invariant mass. This occurs because the process suffers from gluon exchanges trapped in the Glauber region. Using an explicit example, we show that the Glauber gluon, which is exchanged between a collinear spectator parton from the nucleon sector and a soft spectator parton from the outgoing pion, has both of its lightcone plus and minus components pinched. Therefore, it cannot be deformed to collinear/soft regions, as is often the case for processes that do factorise. We further confirm the leading power behaviour of the identified Glauber region, highlighting that this is the case although it relies on extracting a soft parton from the outgoing pion. We stress that the Glauber pinch for this process is of the leading power, due to the possibility of having two-gluon exchanges between the collinear nucleon sector and hard partonic scattering sub-process. In fact, the Glauber gluon that we identify is one of these two active gluons, and therefore, its effects are observed already at leading order. A direct consequence of our work is that collinear factorisation breaks in the same way for other $ 2 \to 3 $ exclusive processes, where two-gluon exchanges in the $ t $-channel are possible, like in the exclusive production of a photon pair from $ π^{0} N $ collisions. However, we highlight that in cases where such two-gluon exchanges do not exist, like in the exclusive $ π^{\pm}γ$ photoproduction, the Glauber exchanges that we discuss here do not occur, and hence they do not suffer from factorisation breaking effects.

## Key claim

Collinear factorisation breaks for the exclusive photoproduction of a π⁰γ pair with large invariant mass because a Glauber gluon exchanged between a collinear spectator parton from the nucleon and a soft spectator parton from the outgoing pion has both lightcone components pinched, and this Glauber pinch contributes at leading power, not just at subleading order.

## Method

The authors perform a Libby-Sterman analysis to classify pinch-singular surfaces (PSSs) for the 2→3 process γ + N → γ' + N' + π⁰, working in a lightcone coordinate system where Δ and p_π define the n̄ and n directions. They then examine an explicit two-loop Feynman diagram (in Feynman gauge) to determine whether the loop momenta l and k are forced into a Glauber scaling region and to compare the power counting of that region against the standard collinear region.

## Key numbers

* The hard scale Q is defined as Q ∼ |q'_⊥|, |p_{π,⊥}|, with the dimensionless parameter λ ∼ {√|t|, m_π, m_N, Λ_QCD}/Q, and factorisation is expected as λ → 0.
* The collinear region (l ∼ (1, λ², λ)Q, k ∼ (λ², 1, λ)Q) yields a leading power of λ¹ for the amplitude.
* The Glauber region (l ∼ (λ, λ², λ)Q, k ∼ (λ, λ, λ)Q) also yields a leading power of λ¹, matching the collinear region.
* The Glauber gluon scaling identified is the "n̄-collinear-to-soft Glauber" scaling, k_g ∼ (λ, λ², λ)Q.

## Context

This work extends the study of collinear factorisation for 2→3 exclusive processes, which had been assumed valid based on calculations for di-photon photoproduction (Grocholski 2021, Grocholski 2022) and a general proof for a family of such processes (Qiu 2022). The authors show that the π⁰γ channel suffers from a leading-power Glauber pinch that breaks factorisation, a mechanism distinct from the endpoint/breakpoint divergences seen in simpler processes like DVCS. This result implies that other 2→3 processes with two-gluon t-channel exchanges, such as exclusive diphoton production from π⁰N collisions, also break collinear factorisation, while processes with only quark exchange (e.g., exclusive π±γ photoproduction) remain safe.
