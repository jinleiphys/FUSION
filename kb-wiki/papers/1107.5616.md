# Two-neutron correlations in microscopic wave functions of He-6, He-8 and C-12

> Two-neutron densities obtained from microscopic wave functions of $^6$He and $^8$He are investigated to reveal di-neutron correlations. In particular, the comparison of the two-neutron density with the product of one-neutron densities is useful for a quantitative discussion of di-neutron correlations. The calculations show that the S=0 spatial two-neutron correlation increases at the surface of $^6$He$(0^+_1)$ and $^8$He$(0^+_2)$. The enhancement is remarkable in the $^6$He$(0^+_1)$ ground state but not as prominent in the $^8$He$(0^+_1)$ ground state. Configuration mixing of many Slater determinants is essential to describe the di-neutron correlations. Two-neutron densities in $^{12}$C wave functions with $α$-cluster structures are also studied.

## Key claim

The paper shows that di-neutron (spin-zero) spatial correlations are enhanced at the nuclear surface of $^6$He$(0^+_1)$ and $^8$He$(0^+_2)$, but the enhancement is "remarkable" in the $^6$He ground state and "not as prominent" in the $^8$He$(0^+_1)$ ground state, with the qualification that configuration mixing of many Slater determinants is essential to describe these correlations.

## Method

The authors used antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) to construct microscopic many-body wave functions for $^6$He and $^8$He as linear combinations of 72 parity- and total-angular-momentum projected Slater determinants (multiconfiguration AMD). They calculated the two-neutron density $\rho^{(2)}_{nn}(R,r)$ and its spin-zero ($S=0$) and spin-one ($S=1$) components as functions of the pair center-of-mass distance $R$ from the nuclear center and the relative distance $r$ between the two neutrons. For comparison, they prepared reference states (PAV: projection after variation with a single Slater determinant) and sample states (PAV-ls0: no spin-orbit force) to isolate non-trivial correlations from trivial ones due to the Pauli principle and angular momentum coupling.

## Key numbers

- The one-body neutron density of the $^6$He$(0^+_1)$ MC state shows a low-density tail extending beyond 3.6 fm (the 1% level of central density for the PAV reference state), while the $S=0$ two-neutron c.m. density of the MC state is "very large" at $R > 3$ fm.
- For $^8$He$(0^+_1)$, the $S=0$ two-neutron c.m. density of the MC state at $R \sim 4$ fm is larger by a factor of 100 than that of the PAV reference state.
- The one-body density of the uncorrelated $^6$He PAV state drops to 1% of central density around 3.6 fm, while the two-body c.m. density does so at about 2.3 fm; for $^8$He the difference is about 1.3 fm.
- The AMD calculations used the interaction parameter set "m56-ls2000" (MV1 case(3) central force with $m=0.56$, $b=h=0.15$ and G3RS spin-orbit force with $u_I = -u_{II} = 2000$ MeV).

## Context

This work extends previous AMD studies of $^6$He and $^8$He structures by Kanada-En'yo (2007) and provides a quantitative method to identify di-neutron correlations in fully antisymmetrized microscopic wave functions, going beyond semi-microscopic three-body models (e.g., Hansen 1987, Bertsch 1991, Zhukov 1993, Esbensen 1997) that treat valence neutrons separately from core neutrons. The two-neutron density analysis enables future studies of how di-neutron correlations manifest in other neutron-rich nuclei and in $^{12}$C with alpha-cluster structures, and it clarifies the distinction between trivial correlations (Pauli principle, angular momentum coupling) and non-trivial correlations induced by the nuclear interaction.
