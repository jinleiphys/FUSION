# Survival Probability of Unstable States in Coupled-Channels -- nonexponential decay of "threshold-cusp"

> We investigate the survival probability of unstable states, the time-dependence of an initial state, in coupled channels. First, we extend the formulation of the survival probability from single channel to coupled channels (two channels). We derive an exact general expression of the two-channel survival probability using uniformization, a method which makes the coupled-channel S matrix single-valued, and the Mittag-Leffler expansion, i.e. a pole expansion. Second, we calculate the time dependence of the two-channel survival probability by employing the derived expression. It is the minimal distance between the pole and the physical region in the complex energy plane, not the imaginary part of the pole energy, which determines not only the energy spectrum of the Green's function but also the survival probability. The survival probability of the "threshold-cusp" caused by a pole on the unusual complex-energy Riemann sheet is shown to decay, not grow in time though the imaginary part of the pole energy is positive. We also show that the decay of the "threshold-cusp" is non-exponential. Thus, the "threshold-cusp" is shown to be a new type of unstable mode, which is found only in coupled channels.

# Survival Probability of Unstable States in Coupled-Channels -- nonexponential decay of "threshold-cusp"

## Key claim

The survival probability of a "threshold-cusp" state, caused by a pole on the unusual complex-energy Riemann sheet [tb]+ where the imaginary part of the pole energy is positive, decays in time rather than growing, and this decay is non-exponential for all times. The minimal distance between the pole and the physical region in the complex energy plane, not the imaginary part of the pole energy, determines both the energy spectrum and the time dependence of the survival probability.

## Method

The authors extend the formulation of survival probability from single-channel to two-channel scattering by deriving an exact general expression using uniformization (a method that makes the coupled-channel S matrix single-valued via the variable z = k1 + k2) and the Mittag-Leffler (pole) expansion of the Green's function. They then calculate the time dependence of the two-channel survival probability by evaluating the pole contributions analytically for single poles and numerically for a Flatte-type model with four poles, examining cases corresponding to a lower-channel resonance, a threshold cusp, and an upper-channel resonance.

## Key numbers

- For the threshold cusp case B: pole position in uniformization variable z_n = 0.9293 - 0.0707 i, complex energy e_n = 0.9992 + 0.0107 i, minimal distance d^e_n = 0.0222, located on sheet [tb]+.
- For the lower-channel resonance case A: z_n = 0.7281 + 0.5290 i, e_n = 0.6580 - 0.1009 i, d^e_n = 0.1083, on sheet [bt]-.
- For the upper-channel resonance case C: z_n = 1.6 - 0.1 i, e_n = 1.234 - 0.0679 i, d^e_n = 0.0685, on sheet [bb]-.
- The width of the spectrum peak for threshold cusp case B is approximately Gamma approx 4|e_n - 1| approx 2 d^e_n = 0.0444.
- The characteristic time scale for threshold cusp decay is proportional to |e_n - 1|^{-1}.
- At large times, the survival probability decreases by a power of t^{-3} (inverse-power decay law).
- For the Flatte model example: m = 1.1, gamma_1 = 0.1, gamma_2 = 0.9, with pole positions z_1 = 0.861 - 0.087 i, z_2 = 0.707 - 0.913 i, z_3 = -z_1*, z_4 = -z_2*.

## Context

This work extends the study of survival probability from single-channel systems (where non-exponential behavior at short and long times has been known since Khalfin (1958) and Fonda (1978)) to coupled-channel systems. It builds on the uniformized Mittag-Leffler expansion method developed by Ramirez Jimenez (2018), Yamada (2021), and earlier work by Newton (1958) and Kato (1965). The paper directly addresses the nature of near-threshold peaks observed in hadronic processes (reviewed by Chen (2016), Esposito (2017), and Guo (2018)), which are candidates for exotic hadrons. The finding that threshold-cusp states constitute a new class of unstable mode found only in coupled channels enables future experimental searches for non-exponential decay in materials with multiple decay channels, analogous to the luminescence decay experiments of Rothe (2006).
