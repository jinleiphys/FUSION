# Generalized parton distributions and strong forces inside nucleons and nuclei

> We argue that generalized parton distributions (GPDs), accessible in hard exclusive processes, carry information about the spatial distribution of forces experienced by quarks and gluons inside hadrons. This way the measurements of hard exclusive processes open a possibility for direct "measurements" of strong forces in different parts of nucleons and nuclei. Also such studies open a venue for addressing questions of the properties of the quark (gluon) matter inside hadrons and nuclei. We give a simple example of relations between GPDs and properties of "nuclear matter" in finite nuclei.

## Key claim

Generalized parton distributions (GPDs), accessible in hard exclusive processes, carry information about the spatial distribution of forces experienced by quarks and gluons inside hadrons, specifically through the form factors of the energy-momentum tensor. The paper argues that the form factor \(d(t)\) is related to the spatial distribution of shear forces and pressure inside hadrons, and that this can be studied via the D-term contribution to deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) amplitudes.

## Method

The author derives relations between the lowest Mellin moments of GPDs and the form factors of the QCD energy-momentum tensor (EMT) for quarks and gluons, specifically \(M_2(t)\), \(J(t)\), and \(d(t)\). These form factors are then interpreted in the Breit frame as spatial distributions of energy, angular momentum, and shear forces (stress tensor) inside the nucleon. As an illustrative example, the author applies a liquid-drop model of a large nucleus with sharp edges and a finite skin width to estimate the value of \(d(0)\) from nuclear surface tension and radius parameters.

## Key numbers

*   In the chiral quark soliton model at a low normalization point \(\mu \approx 0.6\) GeV, the quark form factor is estimated as \(d^Q(0) \approx -4.0\).
*   For a large nucleus modeled as a liquid drop, the total form factor is estimated as \(d(0) \approx -0.2\ A^{7/3}\left(1+3.8/A^{2/3}\right)\), using nuclear surface tension \(\gamma \approx 1\ \text{MeV/fm}^2\) and Hofstadter's nuclear radius \(R = 1.12\ A^{1/3}\ \text{fm}\) with skin width \(a \approx 0.54\ \text{fm}\).
*   The contribution of the D-term to the real part of the DVCS amplitude is predicted to grow with atomic number as \(A^{4/3}\), compared to the impulse approximation behavior \(\sim A\).

## Context

This work extends the interpretation of GPDs beyond parton distributions in the transverse plane (as discussed by Burkardt, Ralston, and Diehl) to the mechanical properties of hadrons, specifically the spatial distribution of forces. It builds on the formalism of Ji (1997) relating GPD moments to EMT form factors and on the D-term introduced by Polyakov and Weiss (1999). The paper enables experimental studies of strong forces inside nucleons and nuclei via hard exclusive processes like DVCS, particularly through beam charge asymmetry measurements on nuclear targets, and suggests that such measurements can probe properties of quark-gluon matter analogous to nuclear matter properties described by the Weizsacker formula and Hofstadter's electron scattering experiments.
