# Scattering states in Bethe-Salpeter equation

> 7 pages, 3 figures. Presented at the XXI International Baldin Seminar on High Energy Physics Problems, September 10-15, 2012, JINR, Dubna, Russia. To be published in the online journal Proceedings of Science - PoS(Baldin ISHEPP XXI)027 (2012)

## Key claim

The authors compute the off-mass-shell scattering amplitude satisfying the Bethe-Salpeter equation for spinless particles in Minkowski space with the ladder kernel for the first time, but only for the S-wave partial wave and for a specific one-boson exchange model.

## Method

The inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space is solved directly by carefully treating four sources of singularities: constituent propagator poles (handled via principal value decomposition and subtraction), exchange propagator poles (turned into logarithmic singularities after partial wave decomposition, integrated by splitting intervals), singularities in the Born term, and singularities in the amplitude itself (circumvented by factoring the amplitude as \(F_0 = K_0 f_0\) where \(f_0\) is smooth). The resulting non-singular equation for \(f_0\) is solved numerically, then the off-shell amplitude \(F_0\) is restored.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies from the homogeneous equation coincide with previous calculations within four-digit accuracy.
- Phase shifts from the Minkowski-space solution and from a Wick-rotated Euclidean equation coincide within 3-4 digits.
- For constituent mass \(m=1\), exchange mass \(\mu=0.5\), and coupling \(\alpha=0.5\), the phase shift from the Bethe-Salpeter equation is compared to the non-relativistic Schrödinger result (shown in Figure 1, left panel).
- For \(\alpha=1.2\) with \(\mu=0.5\) and \(m=1\), a bound state exists and the phase shift starts at 180 degrees (Levinson theorem); the inelastic threshold for meson creation is at incident momentum \(p^{thresh}=0.75\).
- The second threshold for creation of two mesons opens at \(p_s=1.118\).
- The scattering length \(a_0\) becomes infinite at \(\alpha\approx 1\) for the Bethe-Salpeter equation and at \(\alpha\approx 0.8\) for the Schrödinger equation (for \(\mu=0.5\)).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous method for bound-state Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes in Minkowski space (Karmanov and Carbonell, 2006; 2008) to scattering states, which had not been obtained before for a field-theoretic kernel. It contrasts with earlier Euclidean-space solutions (Tjon, 1975) that could not provide off-shell amplitudes needed for electromagnetic form factors, and with separable-kernel calculations (Burov and collaborators). The computed off-shell amplitude enables calculation of transition form factors (e.g., \(\gamma^* d \to np\)) and serves as input for three-body Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev equations.
