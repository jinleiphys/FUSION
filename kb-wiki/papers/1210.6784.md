# The QCD Critical Point : marching towards continuum

> We present results of our simulations of QCD with two light dynamical quarks on a 32^3 X 8 lattice at a current quark mass tuned to have the Goldstone pion mass of about 230 MeV. Employing the Taylor expansion method we proposed earlier, we estimate the radius of convergence of the series for the baryonic susceptibility by using terms up to eighth order. Together with our earlier results, corresponding to the same physical parameters but on coarser lattices at respectively 1.33 times and twice the lattice cut-off (a), we were able to attempt a march towards the continuum limit.

## Key claim

The authors estimate the radius of convergence of the Taylor expansion for baryonic susceptibility using terms up to eighth order on a \(32^3 \times 8\) lattice, and find that results for \(N_t = 8\) are in very good agreement with earlier \(N_t = 6\) results, suggesting the continuum limit is in sight and the critical point estimate is robust, but with larger error bands on \(T_E\) than previously reported.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with two flavors of staggered fermions at a quark mass corresponding to a Goldstone pion mass of about 230 MeV, on a \(32^3 \times 8\) lattice. They employed the Taylor expansion method to compute baryonic susceptibilities up to eighth order in the baryon chemical potential, using stochastic estimators requiring 20 inversions of the fermion matrix on about 1000 vectors per measurement. The radius of convergence was estimated using both the ratio method and the nth root method, and results were compared with earlier simulations on coarser \(N_t = 4\) and \(N_t = 6\) lattices.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: about 230 MeV (from the Goldstone pion, with \(m_\pi/m_\rho = 0.31 \pm 0.01\) based on MILC collaboration results)
- Lattice size: \(32^3 \times 8\) with \(m/T_c = 0.1\) fixed
- Simulation temperature range: \(0.90 \le T/T_c \le 2.01\)
- On \(N_t = 6\) lattice, the critical endpoint coordinates were \(T^E/T_c = 0.94 \pm 0.01\) and \(\mu_B^E/T^E = 1.8 \pm 0.1\)
- On \(N_t = 8\) lattice, the critical temperature \(T_E\) has a larger error band than the \(N_t = 6\) result, as shown in the phase diagram

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier lattice QCD studies of the QCD critical point using the Taylor expansion method on coarser lattices with \(N_t = 4\) (Datta, Gavai, Gupta, earlier work) and \(N_t = 6\) (Datta, Gavai, Gupta, earlier work). The results are compared with those from the Budapest-Wuppertal group (Fodor and Katz, 2004) who used a two-parameter reweighting method on \(N_t = 4\) lattices. The agreement between \(N_t = 8\) and \(N_t = 6\) results suggests that continuum extrapolation is becoming feasible, and the authors note that a new lattice action with correct chiral symmetries at nonzero chemical potential has recently been proposed (Gavai and Sharma, 2012) for future comparison.
