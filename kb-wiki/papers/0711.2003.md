# Plasma Electromagnetic Fluctuations as an Initial Value Problem

> Fluctuations of electric and magnetic fields in the collisionless plasma are found as a solution of the initial value linearized problem. The plasma initial state is on average stationary and homogeneous. When the state is stable, the initial fluctuations decay exponentially and in the long time limit a stationary spectrum of fluctuations is established. For the equilibrium plasma it reproduces the spectrum obtained from the fluctuation-dissipation relation. Fluctuations in the unstable two-stream system are also discussed.

## Key claim

Electromagnetic fluctuations in collisionless plasma are solved as an initial value problem, showing that for stable plasmas the initial fluctuations decay exponentially and a stationary fluctuation spectrum emerges at long times, while for unstable systems (e.g., two-stream) the memory of initial fluctuations is not lost because unstable modes grow exponentially.

## Method

The linearized Vlasov transport equation and Maxwell equations are solved using a one-sided Fourier transformation in time, with the initial state taken as on average stationary and homogeneous. The electric and magnetic fields are expressed in terms of the initial values of the distribution function and fields, and the initial fluctuations of the distribution function are identified with those of a classical system of noninteracting particles. The correlation functions of the fields are then computed by substituting these initial correlations and taking the long-time limit for stable plasmas.

## Key numbers

- For equilibrium plasma, the fluctuation spectrum of the longitudinal electric field is given by ⟨E^i E^i⟩_{ω k} = 8π (T/ω) (Im ε_L(ω,k) / |ε_L(ω,k)|^2), where T is the temperature and ε_L is the longitudinal dielectric function.
- The imaginary part of the longitudinal dielectric function in equilibrium is Im ε_L(ω,k) = (4π^2 e^2 ω)/(T k^2) ∫ d^3p/(2π)^3 δ(ω - k·v) f^0(p).
- The initial correlation function for the distribution function is ⟨δf_0(k1,p1) δf_0(k2,p2)⟩ = (2π)^3 δ^(3)(p2 - p1) (2π)^3 δ^(3)(k2 + k1) f^0(p1).

## Context

This work extends the method of Rostoker (1961) and Klimontovich and Silin (1962), as exposed in the handbook by Lifshitz and Pitaevskii (1981), to compute the full fluctuation spectra of electric and magnetic fields in collisionless plasma, including the challenging transverse components. The author notes that analytic computation of magnetic field fluctuations had not been published previously. The results for equilibrium plasma reproduce the spectrum obtained from the fluctuation-dissipation relation, and the method is intended to set the stage for nonequilibrium calculations, particularly for the quark-gluon plasma produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, which is discussed in Mrowczynski (2005) and Asakawa (2006).
