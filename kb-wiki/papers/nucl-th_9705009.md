# Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of nuclei with A \leq 7

> We report quantum Monte Carlo calculations of ground and low-lying excited states for nuclei with A \leq 7 using a realistic Hamiltonian containing the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon potentials. A detailed description of the Green's function Monte Carlo algorithm for systems with state-dependent potentials is given and a number of tests of its convergence and accuracy are performed. We find that the Hamiltonian being used results in ground states of both 6Li and 7Li that are stable against breakup into subclusters, but somewhat underbound compared to experiment. We also have results for 6He, 7He, and their isobaric analogs. The known excitation spectra of all these nuclei are reproduced reasonably well and we predict a number of excited states in 6He and 7He. We also present spin-polarized one-body and several different two-body density distributions. These are the first microscopic calculations that directly produce nuclear shell structure from realistic interactions that fit NN scattering data.

## Key claim

The Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations using the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana IX three-nucleon potentials produce ground states of 6Li and 7Li that are stable against breakup into subclusters, but the Hamiltonian systematically underbinds these nuclei compared to experimental binding energies.

## Method

The authors performed variational Monte Carlo (VMC) calculations to construct trial wave functions using correlated operators with LS coupling for p-shell nuclei, then used these as input for Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) propagations that project out the exact lowest energy state for given quantum numbers. The GFMC propagator was constructed with a simplified isoscalar Hamiltonian (H') containing only eight operator terms, with the difference (H-H') treated perturbatively. Calculations were carried out for A=6 nuclei (6He, 6Li, 6Be) and A=7 nuclei (7He, 7Li, 7Be, 7B), including all observed low-lying excitations and predictions for unobserved states.

## Key numbers

- 4He is overbound by approximately 2 MeV when using the simplified isoscalar Hamiltonian v'ij compared to the full Argonne v18 interaction
- The repulsive U0 term in the three-nucleon potential was increased by approximately 30% in H' to compensate for the overbinding tendency of the simplified two-body interaction
- VMC energies lie approximately 3 MeV above GFMC results for A=6 nuclei and approximately 4.5 MeV above GFMC results for A=7 nuclei
- The Argonne v18 potential fits 1787 pp and 2514 np scattering data points in the range 0-350 MeV with a chi-squared per datum of 1.09
- The Urbana IX three-nucleon potential parameters are A2π = -0.0293 MeV and U0 = 0.0048 MeV
- The electromagnetic contribution of vγ(np) to the deuteron binding energy is approximately 0.02 MeV, or 1% of the total binding energy
- The 6He ground state decays by beta-minus emission with a mean life of 807 ms
- The first excited state of 6He has a width of approximately 110 keV
- The 6Li (3+;0) first excited state has a width of 24 keV
- The 6Li (0+;1) second excited state has a width of only 8 eV
- The 6Be ground state has a width of 92 keV
- The 7He ground state is 0.44 MeV above the threshold for breakup into 6He+n with a width of 160 keV
- The isobaric analogs in A=7 have widths of 260, 320, and 1200 keV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous GFMC calculations reported in Pudliner et al. (1995) for A=6 nuclei, now including improved algorithms with an exact two-body propagator and better trial functions, plus the first detailed studies of A=7 nuclei. The calculations directly produce nuclear shell structure from realistic interactions that fit NN scattering data, in contrast to shell-model studies using G-matrices derived from NN interactions (ZBVHS95) or cluster-cluster models using combinations of NN and alpha-N potentials (KPRVR95). The results enable future microscopic calculations of light p-shell nuclei with realistic interactions, though the authors note that the method is not yet sensitive enough to accurately calculate quadrupole moments and asymptotic D/S ratios.
