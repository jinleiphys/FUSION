# Centrality-dependent forward $J/ψ$ production in high energy proton-nucleus collisions

> Forward $J/ψ$ production and suppression in high energy proton-nucleus collisions can be an important probe of gluon saturation. In an earlier work we studied this process in the Color Glass Condensate framework and showed that using the Glauber approach to extrapolate the dipole cross section of a proton to a nucleus leads to results closer to experimental data than previous calculations in this framework. Here we investigate the centrality dependence of the nuclear suppression in this model and show a comparison of our results with recent LHC data.

## Key claim

The authors show that using the optical Glauber model to relate the dipole cross section of a nucleus to that of a proton yields a centrality dependence of forward J/ψ suppression that is much stronger than observed in recent ALICE data, despite the model's success for minimum-bias collisions.

## Method

The cross section for c-cbar pair production in the dilute-dense limit of the Color Glass Condensate is computed, with hadronization into J/ψ mesons described by the color evaporation model. The dipole cross section for a proton is obtained from the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov equation with MV-e initial conditions fitted to HERA data, and generalized to a lead nucleus using the optical Glauber model with a Woods-Saxon distribution. Centrality classes are mapped to fixed impact parameters by matching the optical-Glauber number of binary collisions to the average values estimated by ALICE.

## Key numbers

- The MV-e fit parameters from HERA DIS data: Q_s0^2 = 0.060 GeV^2, C^2 = 7.2, e_c = 18.9, and sigma_0/2 = 16.36 mb.
- Woods-Saxon parameters: d = 0.54 fm, R_A = (1.12 A^{1/3} - 0.86 A^{-1/3}) fm.
- For the 2–10% centrality class: optical-Glauber ⟨N_coll⟩ = 14.7 versus ALICE ⟨N_coll⟩ = 11.7 ± 1.2 ± 0.9.
- For the 80–100% centrality class: optical-Glauber ⟨N_coll⟩ = 1.5 versus ALICE ⟨N_coll⟩ = 2.1 ± 0.1 ± 0.2.
- The charm quark mass is varied between 1.2 and 1.5 GeV in the uncertainty band.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier minimum-bias study (Ducloué, Lappi, Mäntysaari, 2015) which found that the optical Glauber approach gives results closer to experimental data than previous CGC calculations by Fujii et al. (2013). The present calculation shows that the centrality dependence predicted by this model is too strong compared to ALICE data (Adam et al., 2015), suggesting that fluctuations in the number of binary collisions must be included for a consistent comparison. The results motivate future work incorporating impact-parameter distributions rather than fixed values.
