# Consistent nonadditive approach and nuclear equation of state

> By using a $q$-calculus, the Walecka many-body field theory was studied in the context of the Tsallis framework. The most important aspect of the application of the $q$-calculus to the nonadditive formulation of QHD-I is that it naturally emerges as a thermodynamically consistent theory.

## Key claim

By applying the q-calculus to the Walecka (QHD-I) model, the authors derive a thermodynamically consistent nonadditive nuclear equation of state from first principles of quantum statistical mechanics, without the additional assumptions required in earlier approaches. The consistency emerges naturally from the formalism, but the nonadditive effects only appear at finite temperature and vanish in the zero-temperature limit.

## Method

The authors start from the QHD-I Lagrangian in the mean-field approximation and introduce nonadditive effects by replacing the standard Boltzmann-Gibbs evolution operator with a q-exponential operator, then performing a Wick rotation to obtain a generalized grand canonical partition function. Using the q-calculus product and logarithm, they derive expressions for pressure, energy density, baryon number density, and scalar density that depend on powers of the nonadditive distribution functions n^q_k(T,nu) and bar-n^q_k(T,nu). The effective nucleon mass M* is then determined self-consistently from the scalar density equation.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constants used: (g_sigma/m_sigma)^2 = 11.798 fm^2 and (g_omega/m_omega)^2 = 8.653 fm^2, fixed to give binding energy E_bind = -15.75 MeV and Fermi momentum k_F = 1.42 fm^{-1} at T=0
- For q=1, the critical temperature for the liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear matter is T_c = 20.16 MeV
- For q_c = 1.3, the critical temperature shifts to T_c = 20.65 MeV (near the convergence limit of the integrals)
- At zero baryon density with coupling C_S^2 = 365, a first-order phase transition occurs around T ~ 185 MeV for q=1, with a sudden drop in effective mass and a peak in specific heat
- For q=1.02 at zero baryon density, the specific heat becomes single-valued with a smooth peak around T ~ 180 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier nonadditive formulations of the QHD-I equation of state by Santos, Pereira, Silva, and Alcaniz (2013, 2014) that used a simple substitution of the Fermi-Dirac distribution with a nonadditive distribution, which suffered from thermodynamical inconsistency. The q-calculus approach, developed by Borges (2004) and Nivanen (2003), provides a first-principles derivation that resolves this inconsistency. The results connect to studies of proto-neutron star properties by Lavagno (2011) and to investigations of the QHD-I phase structure at zero baryon density by Theis, Stoecker, and Polonyi (1983). The formalism is general enough to be applied to any quantum system described by a partition function of the same form.
