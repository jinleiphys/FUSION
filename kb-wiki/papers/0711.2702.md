# Ab initio no-core shell model calculations for light nuclei

> An overview of the ab initio no-core shell model is presented. Recent results for light nuclei obtained with the chiral two-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions are highlighted. Cross section calculations of capture reactions important for astrophysics are discussed. The extension of the ab initio no-core shell model to the description of nuclear reactions by the resonating group method technique is outlined.

## Key claim

The ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) can reproduce light-nuclei properties using realistic two-nucleon (NN) and three-nucleon (NNN) interactions, but the convergence to exact results depends critically on constructing effective interactions via unitary transformations; the method is not variational when effective interactions are used, and the three-body effective interaction approximation is essential for p-shell nuclei with NNN interactions in model spaces up to Nmax=6 or 8.

## Method

The NCSM treats all A nucleons as active in a harmonic oscillator (HO) basis truncated by a maximum total HO energy (Nmax). To accelerate convergence, a Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation derives a hermitian effective interaction from the original realistic NN or NN+NNN potentials by solving the two-nucleon or three-nucleon problem exactly in the full space and projecting onto the model space. For systems with A>4, the calculation uses a Slater-determinant HO basis (m-scheme) with the effective interaction transformed from Jacobi coordinates, and the center-of-mass motion is removed by requiring the CM to be in the 0ħΩ state.

## Key numbers

- ³H ground-state energy with NN interaction only: -7.852(5) MeV (converged), compared to experimental -8.482 MeV
- ³H ground-state energy with NN+NNN interaction: -8.473(5) MeV, close to experiment
- ⁴He ground-state energy with NN interaction only: -25.39(1) MeV
- ⁴He ground-state energy with NN+NNN interaction: -28.34(2) MeV, compared to experimental -28.296 MeV
- NCSM calculations for ⁴He performed in basis spaces up to Nmax=20
- p-shell calculations with NNN interactions are presently feasible in model spaces up to Nmax=6 or Nmax=8

## Context

This work extends the NCSM approach (Navratil and Barrett 2000) to include chiral effective field theory NN and NNN interactions, building on prior few-body methods such as the Faddeev method (Faddeev 1960) for three nucleons and the Green's function Monte Carlo method (GFMC) for systems up to A=12. The results enable future applications to nuclear reactions by coupling the NCSM with the resonating group method, and the demonstrated convergence with three-body effective interactions makes calculations for p-shell nuclei (up to A=16) feasible where bare interactions would converge too slowly.
