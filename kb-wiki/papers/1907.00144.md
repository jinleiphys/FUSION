# Compact $sssc\bar{c}$ pentaquark states predicted by a quark model

> Several compact $sssc\bar c$ pentaquark resonances are predicted in a potential quark model. The Hamiltonian is the best available one, which reproduces the masses of the low-lying charmed and strange hadrons well. Full five-body calculations are carried out by the use of the Gaussian expansion method, and the relevant baryon-meson thresholds are taken into account explicitly. Employing the real scaling method, we predict four sharp resonances, $J^P=1/2^-$ ($E=5180$ MeV, $Γ=20$ MeV), $5/2^-$ (5645 MeV, 30 MeV), $5/2^-$ (5670 MeV, 50 MeV), and $1/2^+$ (5360 MeV, 80 MeV). These are the candidates of compact pentaquark resonance states from the current best quark model, which should be confirmed either by experiments or lattice QCD calculations.

## Key claim

The paper predicts four sharp compact pentaquark resonances in the \(sssc\bar{c}\) system using a potential quark model, but the authors explicitly state that these states should be confirmed either by experiments or lattice QCD calculations, and note that the model cannot describe meson exchange interactions between hadrons, meaning molecular-type resonances may not appear in their calculation.

## Method

The authors solved the full five-body Schrödinger equation using the Gaussian Expansion Method (GEM) with approximately 40,000 basis functions for most spin-parity channels. They employed the real scaling (stabilization) method to distinguish genuine compact resonances from discretized scattering states by scaling the range parameters of the Gaussian basis functions along the inter-cluster coordinates. The Hamiltonian used was the Semay and Silvestre-Brac (SSB) potential with the AP1 parameter set, which reproduces the masses of low-lying charmed and strange hadrons within 15 MeV.

## Key numbers

- \(J^P = 1/2^-\) resonance at \(E = 5180\) MeV with width \(\Gamma = 20\) MeV
- \(J^P = 5/2^-\) resonance at \(E = 5645\) MeV with width \(\Gamma = 30\) MeV
- \(J^P = 5/2^-\) resonance at \(E = 5670\) MeV with width \(\Gamma = 50\) MeV
- \(J^P = 1/2^+\) resonance at \(E = 5360\) MeV with width \(\Gamma = 80\) MeV
- For the AL1 parameter set, a sharp \(1/2^-\) resonance appears at 5220 MeV with a width of 25 MeV, compared to 5180 MeV with 20 MeV for AP1
- The peak position of the \(c\bar{c}\) correlation function for the \(1/2^-\) resonance is at about 0.25 fm, and the \(ss\) peak lies at about 0.85 fm

## Context

This work extends previous five-body calculations by Hiyama and Hosaka (2018) on the \(P_c\) (\(uudc\bar{c}\)) pentaquark system, where narrow states were found at significantly higher energies than the experimentally observed LHCb pentaquarks. The \(sssc\bar{c}\) system is chosen because the absence of light \(u,d\) quarks suppresses coupling to pions and meson exchange, making it more amenable to both quark model calculations and future lattice QCD comparisons. The results provide candidates for compact pentaquark states that could be observed in bottom quark decay processes such as \(\Xi_b^0 \rightarrow (sssc\bar{c}) + K^+\) or in high-energy heavy ion collisions, and the authors note that a related lattice QCD study is currently underway.
