# The fate of nonlinear perturbations near the QCD critical point

> The impact of the QCD critical point on the propagation of nonlinear waves has been studied. The effects have been investigated within the scope of second-order causal dissipative hydrodynamics by incorporating the critical point into the equation of state, and the scaling behaviour of transport coefficients and of thermodynamic response functions. Near the critical point, the nonlinear waves are found to be significantly damped which may result in the disappearance of the Mach cone effects of the away side jet. Such damping may lead to enhancement in the fluctuations of elliptic and higher flow coefficients. Therefore, the disappearance of Mach cone effects and the enhancement of fluctuations in flow harmonics in the event-by-event analysis may be considered as signals of the critical endpoint.

## Key claim

Near the QCD critical point, nonlinear perturbations in the quark-gluon plasma are significantly damped, which may cause the disappearance of Mach cone effects from away-side jets and enhance fluctuations in elliptic and higher flow coefficients; the authors emphasize that this damping occurs even though nonlinear waves normally survive dissipative effects away from the critical point.

## Method

The authors derived nonlinear wave equations for perturbations of energy density within second-order Israel-Stewart causal dissipative hydrodynamics, incorporating shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and thermal conductivity. They used the Reductive Perturbative Method with stretched coordinates to obtain a first-order equation (similar to Navier-Stokes) and a second-order equation containing Israel-Stewart relaxation and coupling effects. The critical point was included via an equation of state constructed following the 3D Ising model universality class (Asakawa 2009, Parotto 2018) and via scaling behavior of transport coefficients and thermodynamic response functions (Kunihiro 2012, Kapusta 2012).

## Key numbers

- The critical endpoint is assumed located at (T_c, mu_c) = (154, 367) MeV.
- Away from the critical point, at mu = 400 MeV and T = 185 MeV, nonlinear perturbations survive with reduced amplitude despite dissipative effects.
- Near the critical point, at mu = 367 MeV and T = 158 MeV, nonlinear perturbations are substantially dissipated.
- Initial perturbation profile parameters used: A_1 = 0.6, B_1 = 1, A_2 = 0.4, B_2 = 1 for the sech-squared profile.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of linear perturbations near the QCD critical point (Kunihiro 2012, Hasanujjaman 2019) to the nonlinear regime, and contrasts with earlier nonlinear wave studies in QGP by Fogaca (2010, 2012) that used only shear viscosity without bulk viscosity or thermal conductivity and without a critical point. The results suggest that the disappearance of Mach cone effects in two-particle correlations and enhanced event-by-event fluctuations of flow harmonics could serve as experimental signals for the critical endpoint in beam energy scan programs.
