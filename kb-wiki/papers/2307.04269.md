# Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis within the Scale Invariant Vacuum Paradigm

> The Scale Invariant Vacuum (SIV) paradigm is applied to the Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis using the known analytic expressions for the expansion factor $a$ and the plasma temperature $T$ as functions of the SIV time $τ$ since the Big-Bang when $a(τ=0)=0$. The results are compared to the known standard BBNS model as calculated with the PRIMAT code. Potential SIV-guided deviations from the local statistical equilibrium are explored. Overall, we find that smaller than usual baryon and non-zero dark matter content, by a factor of three to five times reduction, result in compatible to the standard reproduction of the light elements abundances.

# Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis within the Scale Invariant Vacuum Paradigm

## Key claim

The Scale Invariant Vacuum (SIV) paradigm can reproduce the observed light element abundances from Big-Bang Nucleosynthesis, but only if the baryon and dark matter content are reduced by a factor of three to five compared to standard values, and only when the SIV time parametrization and expansion factor are used without modification of nuclear reaction rates.

## Method

The authors applied the analytic SIV expressions for the expansion factor a(τ) and temperature T(τ) to the PRIMAT nucleosynthesis code, replacing the standard FLRW integration with SIV analytic functions. They compared the SIV-guided results to standard PRIMAT runs using the same reaction network with decoupled neutrinos and without QED dipole corrections, and explored potential deviations from local statistical equilibrium by rescaling reaction rates with a forward rescale factor FRF = λ, where λ is the SIV scale factor.

## Key numbers

- Baryon density parameter Ω_b = 1.6% and total matter density Ω_m = 5.9% (compared to standard Ω_b = 4.9% and Ω_m = 31%) produce the best fit to ^4He and D/H abundances within the SIV model
- The SIV time ticks about 1.008 times faster than PRIMAT time at early moments after the Big-Bang, with synchronization at low temperatures
- For the SIV model with Ω_m = 0.999 (λ = 1), the product T × a(T) coincides with PRIMAT's function at low temperatures (T < 1 GK)
- The SIV constant τ(a)/a² for Ω_m → 1 goes to 3τ₀/(4√(v_eq(Ω_m=1)))
- The fit quality metric √(χ_ε²) = 6.1 for the SIV model with fitted Ω_b and Ω_m, compared to 6.8 for standard PRIMAT

## Context

This work extends the SIV paradigm previously applied to galaxy rotation curves, cluster dynamics, and density fluctuation growth (Maeder 2017c, Maeder 2018, MaedGueor 2019) to the well-tested BBNS phenomenon. The authors use the PRIMAT code (PRIMAT 2021) as their standard comparison, and their results suggest that within SIV, dark matter content is less than 3 times the baryon matter content (unlike the standard ratio where dark matter exceeds baryon matter by more than 5 times). The study enables future investigation of whether the SIV framework can simultaneously account for BBNS, cosmic microwave background observations (Durrer 2008, Maeder 2017b), and large-scale structure without requiring the standard dark matter abundance.
