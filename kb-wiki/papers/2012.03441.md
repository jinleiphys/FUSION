# Parton energy loss in a hard-soft factorized approach

> An energetic parton travelling through a quark-gluon plasma loses energy via occasional hard scatterings and frequent softer interactions. Whether or not these interactions admit a perturbative description, the effect of the soft interactions can be factorized and encoded in a small number of transport coefficients. In this work, we present a hard-soft factorized parton energy loss model which combines a stochastic description of soft interactions and rate-based modelling of hard scatterings. We introduce a scale to estimate the regime of validity of the stochastic description, allowing for a better understanding of the model's applicability at small and large coupling. We study the energy and fermion-number cascade of energetic partons as an application of the model.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first numerical implementation of a hard-soft factorized parton energy loss model, where soft interactions are described stochastically via Langevin dynamics and hard interactions are treated with rate-based approaches; the model's validity depends on a separation scale \(\mu\) that must be chosen appropriately, and the authors find that the dependence on this scale is moderate in most cases.

## Method

The authors reformulate the Boltzmann collision kernel for an energetic parton in a quark-gluon plasma by dividing \(2\leftrightarrow 2\) elastic and \(1\leftrightarrow 2\) inelastic processes into hard and soft components based on cutoffs in momentum transfer (\(\mu_{\tilde{q}_\perp}\)), energy transfer (\(\Lambda\)), and radiated energy (\(\mu_\omega\)). Soft interactions are modeled stochastically using a Langevin equation with drag and diffusion coefficients calculated perturbatively, while hard interactions are treated with rate-based matrix elements (vacuum matrix elements for elastic scattering and AMY integral equations for inelastic radiation). The model is implemented within the JETSCAPE framework and tested for a static medium in the weak-coupling regime.

## Key numbers

* The cutoff for energetic partons is set to \(p_{\text{cut}} = 2\) GeV throughout the work.
* The soft-hard cutoff \(\mu_\omega\) for inelastic interactions is limited to \(\mu_\omega \lesssim T\), where \(T\) is the medium temperature.
* The cutoff \(\Lambda\) for distinguishing splitting-like processes from large-angle scattering is chosen as \(\Lambda = \min(\sqrt{3pT}, p_{\text{cut}})\).
* The transverse momentum diffusion coefficient for soft \(2\leftrightarrow 2\) interactions is given by \(\hat{q}_{\rm soft}^{2 \leftrightarrow 2} = \frac{g^2 C_R T m_D^2}{4\pi} \ln \left[1+\left(\frac{\mu_{\tilde{q}_\perp}}{m_D}\right)^2\right]\), where \(m_D^2 \equiv g^2T^2(N_c/3+N_f/6)\).
* The longitudinal diffusion coefficient for soft \(1\leftrightarrow 2\) interactions at leading order is \(\hat{q}^{1\leftrightarrow 2}_{L, \textrm{soft}} = \frac{(2-\ln2)g^4C_RC_AT^2\mu_\omega}{4\pi^3}\).
* The conversion rate for \(q \rightarrow g\) is \(\Gamma_{q\rightarrow g}^{\rm conv}(p) = \frac{g^2C_F m_\infty^2}{16\pi p} \log\left[1 + \frac{\mu_{\tilde q_\perp}^2}{m_{\infty}^2} \right]\), with \(m_{\infty}^2 = g^2 C_F T^2/4\).

## Context

This work extends the hard-soft factorization formalism proposed by Ghiglieri et al. (2015) for parton energy loss in a weakly-coupled QGP, which was shown to be equivalent to a fully rate-based treatment. The numerical implementation builds on the effective kinetic theory approach of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2002) and is integrated into the JETSCAPE framework (Putzschke et al., 2019) for full heavy-ion collision simulations. The model enables future extraction of non-perturbative transport coefficients from lattice QCD calculations (Moore et al., 2019) or Bayesian fits to experimental data, and can be extended to next-to-leading order in future work.
