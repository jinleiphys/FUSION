# A Reanalysis of Single Photon Data at CERN SPS

> We reanalyze the WA98 single photon data at CERN SPS by incorporating several recent developments in the study of prompt and thermal photon production from relativistic heavy ion collisions. Isospin and shadowing corrected NLO pQCD, along with an optimized scale for factorization, fragmentation and renormalization are considered for prompt photon production. Photons from thermal medium are estimated by considering a boost invariant azimuthally anisotropic hydrodynamic expansion of the plasma along with a well tested equation of state and initial conditions. A quantitative explanation of the data is obtained by combining $κ\times$ prompt with thermal photons, where $κ$ is an overall scaling factor. We show that, elliptic flow of thermal photons can play a crucial role to distinguish between the `with' and `without' phase transition scenarios at SPS energy.

# A Reanalysis of Single Photon Data at CERN SPS

## Key claim
A quantitative description of the WA98 single photon data at CERN SPS is obtained by combining thermal photons with prompt photons multiplied by a scaling factor κ, where κ ranges from 2.7 to 5.9 depending on the assumed thermalization time τ₀; the elliptic flow of thermal photons can distinguish between scenarios with and without a quark-gluon plasma phase transition at SPS energies.

## Method
Prompt photon production was calculated using isospin and shadowing corrected NLO pQCD with an optimized scale of p_T/2 for factorization, fragmentation, and renormalization at √s_NN = 17.3 GeV. Thermal photon production was estimated using boost-invariant azimuthally anisotropic ideal hydrodynamics with a first-order phase transition equation of state at T_c ≈ 164 MeV, considering five different thermalization times τ₀ from 0.2 to 1.0 fm/c in steps of 0.2 fm/c. The total photon yield was obtained by adding thermal photons to prompt photons scaled by a factor κ, normalized at p_T = 2.55 GeV/c.

## Key numbers
- Prompt photon production is about 17% of the total yield measured by WA98 at √s_NN = 17.3 GeV for Pb+Pb collisions
- Scaling factor κ = 2.7 for τ₀ = 0.2 fm/c, κ = 4.9 for τ₀ = 0.4 fm/c, κ = 5.4 for τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c, κ = 5.7 for τ₀ = 0.8 fm/c, and κ = 5.9 for τ₀ = 1.0 fm/c, all normalized at p_T = 2.55 GeV/c
- Fragmentation photons are about 30% of those produced from Compton plus annihilation processes in p+p collisions at √s = 19.4 GeV
- Transition temperature T_c ≈ 164 MeV for the first-order phase transition from plasma to hadronic phase
- Freeze-out energy density of about 0.075 GeV/fm³ used for thermal photon calculations

## Context
This work reanalyzes the WA98 single photon data (WA98 Collaboration, 2000) by incorporating recent NLO pQCD developments for prompt photons and improved thermal photon calculations with hydrodynamics. Earlier studies such as WA80 (WA80 Collaboration) had provided only upper limits for direct photon production. The analysis extends previous work by Chatterjee, Srivastava, and Jeon (2009) and shows that thermal photon elliptic flow can distinguish between scenarios with and without a phase transition, unlike hadronic observables which remain unaffected by the choice of τ₀. This enables future experimental measurements of photon v₂ at SPS energies to determine whether a quark-gluon plasma was formed.
