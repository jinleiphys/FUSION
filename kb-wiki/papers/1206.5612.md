# Semileptonic decays of spin-1/2 doubly charmed baryons

> We evaluate exclusive semileptonic decays of ground-state spin-1/2 doubly heavy charmed baryons. The decays are driven by a $c\to s,d$ transition at the quark level. Our form factors are consistent with Heavy Quark Symmetry constraints. The latter are valid in the limit of infinitely heavy quark mass at zero recoil.

## Key claim

The authors compute form factors for semileptonic decays of spin-1/2 doubly charmed baryons that are consistent with Heavy Quark Spin Symmetry (HQSS) constraints, but only near the zero recoil point and in the limit of infinitely heavy quark mass; at the actual charm quark mass, deviations from the HQSS relations are at the level of about 10%.

## Method

The authors evaluate exclusive semileptonic decays of ground-state spin-1/2 doubly charmed baryons (\(\Xi_{cc}\) and \(\Omega_{cc}\)) to single charmed baryons, driven by a \(c\to s\) or \(c\to d\) weak transition at the quark level. They parameterize the hadronic matrix elements in terms of six form factors for \(1/2\to 1/2\) transitions and eight form factors for \(1/2\to 3/2\) transitions, and then impose the constraints from Heavy Quark Spin Symmetry near zero recoil. The decay widths are computed using the standard electroweak formalism with CKM matrix elements \(|V_{cs}|=0.97345\) and \(|V_{cd}|=0.2252\) and the Fermi constant \(G_F = 1.16637(1)\times 10^{-11}\) MeV\(^{-2}\).

## Key numbers

- For \(\Xi_{cc}^{++}\to\Xi'^+_c e^+\nu_e\) (c→s): decay width = 0.146 ps\(^{-1}\)
- For \(\Xi_{cc}^{++}\to\Xi^+_c e^+\nu_e\) (c→s): decay width = 8.75 × 10\(^{-2}\) ps\(^{-1}\)
- For \(\Xi_{cc}^{++}\to\Xi^{*\,+}_c e^+\nu_e\) (c→s): decay width = 3.20 × 10\(^{-2}\) ps\(^{-1}\)
- For \(\Xi_{cc}^{++}\to\Lambda^+_c e^+\nu_e\) (c→d): decay width = 4.86 × 10\(^{-3}\) ps\(^{-1}\)
- For \(\Omega_{cc}^{+}\to\Omega^{0}_c e^+\nu_e\) (c→s): decay width = 0.282 ps\(^{-1}\)
- For \(\Omega_{cc}^{+}\to\Omega^{*\,0}_c e^+\nu_e\) (c→s): decay width = 5.77 × 10\(^{-2}\) ps\(^{-1}\)
- HQSS relations are satisfied to within about 10% near zero recoil for the actual charm quark mass

## Context

This work extends the application of Heavy Quark Spin Symmetry to doubly charmed baryons, following earlier studies of \(\Xi_{bc}\) and \(\Xi_{bb}\) baryons by Hernandez (2007). The results are compared with previous calculations by Faessler (2001) using a relativistic three-quark model and by Kiselev (2001) using heavy quark effective theory and QCD sum rules, finding fair agreement. The calculations enable predictions for exclusive semileptonic decay widths of \(\Xi_{cc}\) and \(\Omega_{cc}\) baryons that can be tested by future experiments at facilities such as LHCb.
