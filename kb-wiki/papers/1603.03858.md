# Determination of a dineutron correlation in Borromean nuclei via a quasi-free knockout ($p,pn$) reaction?

> To discuss the dineutron correlation in the ground state, the quasi-free neutron knockout reaction on $^6$He is investigated.In the present work, the momentum distribution of the two emitted neutrons is calculated with the $α$~+~$n$~+~$n$ three-body model and a simple reaction model to assess the effects of the knockout process via the $^5$He resonance and the target dependence in the momentum distribution. A clear signature of the dineutron correlation can be obtained by choosing the kinematical condition so the process via the $^5$He resonance is excluded because the $^5$He resonance drastically changes the momentum distribution. Using the proton target is important in the quantitative discussion on the dineutron correlation by the knockout reaction. In addition to theoretical arguments, a possible experimental setup to determine the dineutron correlation via the quasi-free knockout reaction is considered.

## Key claim

A clear signature of the dineutron correlation in the ground state of Borromean nuclei can be obtained from the quasi-free knockout reaction only by choosing a kinematical condition that excludes the process via the $^5$He resonance, because that resonance drastically changes the momentum distribution and suppresses the asymmetry that reveals the dineutron correlation.

## Method

The authors calculated the momentum distribution of two emitted neutrons from the quasi-free ($p,pn$) knockout reaction on $^6$He at 250 MeV/nucleon using an $\alpha$ + $n$ + $n$ three-body model solved with the orthogonality condition model (OCM) and a simple reaction model with a zero-range $p$-$n$ interaction. They included the $\alpha$-$n$ final-state interaction to account for the knockout process via the $^5$He resonance and used eikonal damping factors to estimate absorption by the target nucleus, comparing results for proton and $^{12}$C targets.

## Key numbers

- Two-neutron separation energy of $^6$He: 0.975 MeV (calculated) vs. 0.973 MeV (experimental)
- Matter radius of $^6$He: 2.46 fm (calculated) vs. 2.48 $\pm$ 0.03 fm (experimental)
- Charge radius of $^6$He: 2.04 fm (calculated) vs. 2.068(11) fm (experimental)
- Ground-state partial wave probability: $P((p_{3/2})^2)$ = 87.5%, $P((s_{1/2})^2)$ = 7.9%, $P((p_{1/2})^2)$ = 3.6%
- Damping factor at the origin for a proton target: 0.57; for a $^{12}$C target: 0.06
- For $k_{\alpha\text{-}n}$ = 0.4 fm$^{-1}$, the peak strength for $^{12}$C target is 7.37 times smaller than the no-absorption case, while for the proton target it is 1.72 times smaller
- Total cross section for the ($p,pn$) reaction on $^6$He: $2 \times 10^2$ $\mu$b; with a 50 keV gate on $\alpha$-$n$ relative energy, the cross section is on the order of 1 $\mu$b

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of dineutron correlations in Borromean nuclei such as $^6$He and $^{11}$Li, which have been investigated using Coulomb breakup reactions (Aumann 1999, Nakamura 2006) and the cluster sum rule (Esbensen 1992). The authors show that Coulomb breakup has limitations due to strong final-state interactions and core excitation effects (Kikuchi 2013), and that the one-neutron knockout reaction on $^{11}$Li and $^{14}$Be (Simon 2007) did not directly address the dineutron correlation. This work enables a more quantitative extraction of the dineutron correlation by using a proton target and gating on the $\alpha$-$n$ relative momentum to exclude the $^5$He resonance contribution.
