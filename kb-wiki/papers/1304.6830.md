# Multi-dimensional potential energy surfaces and non-axial octupole correlations in actinide and transfermium nuclei from relativistic mean field models

> We have developed multi-dimensional constrained covariant density functional theories (MDC-CDFT) for finite nuclei in which the shape degrees of freedom β_{λμ} with even μ, e.g., β_{20}, β_{22}, β_{30}, β_{32}, β_{40}, etc., can be described simultaneously. The functional can be one of the following four forms: the meson exchange or point-coupling nucleon interactions combined with the non-linear or density-dependent couplings. For the pp channel, either the BCS approach or the Bogoliubov transformation is implemented. The MDC-CDFTs with the BCS approach for the pairing (in the following labelled as MDC-RMF models with RMF standing for "relativistic mean field") have been applied to investigate multi-dimensional potential energy surfaces and the non-axial octupole $Y_{32}$-correlations in N=150 isotones. In this contribution we present briefly the formalism of MDC-RMF models and some results from these models. The potential energy surfaces with and without triaxial deformations are compared and it is found that the triaxiality plays an important role upon the second fission barriers of actinide nuclei. In the study of Y_{32}-correlations in N=150 isotones, it is found that, for 248Cf and 250Fm, β_{32} > 0.03 and the energy is lowered by the β_{32} distortion by more than 300 keV; while for 246Cm and 252No, the pocket with respect to β_{32} is quite shallow.

## Key claim

The authors find that triaxial (non-axial) deformations lower the second fission barriers of actinide nuclei by 0.5 to 1 MeV, an effect that can only be observed when both axial and reflection symmetries are simultaneously broken; for the N=150 isotones 248Cf and 250Fm, the non-axial octupole β32 distortion exceeds 0.03 and lowers the energy by more than 300 keV, but the pocket is shallow enough that static non-axial octupole deformation cannot be firmly concluded.

## Method

The authors developed multi-dimensional constrained relativistic mean field (MDC-RMF) models that simultaneously include shape degrees of freedom βλμ with even μ (β20, β22, β30, β32, β40, etc.) under V4 symmetry. They solved the Dirac equation in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis, implemented either BCS or Bogoliubov pairing, and performed one-, two-, and three-dimensional constraint calculations to map potential energy surfaces. The models were applied to actinide nuclei (e.g., 240Pu, 234U) using five different parameter sets (PC-PK1, NL3*, NL-Z2, DD-ME2, DD-PC1) and to N=150 isotones (246Cm, 248Cf, 250Fm, 252No) to study β32 correlations.

## Key numbers

- For 240Pu, triaxial deformation reduces the inner fission barrier height by more than 2 MeV compared to axial calculations, bringing it closer to the empirical value from Capote (2009).
- For 234U, the triaxiality lowers the second barrier by about 1 MeV for the NL-Z2 parameter set and by about 300 keV for the DD-PC1 parameter set.
- For even-even actinide nuclei, triaxiality lowers the outer fission barrier by 0.5 to 1 MeV, accounting for about 10 to 20% of the barrier height.
- For 248Cf, β32 = 0.037 and the energy gain from β32 distortion (Edepth) is 0.351 MeV.
- For 250Fm, β32 = 0.034 and Edepth = 0.328 MeV.
- For 252No, β32 = 0.025 and Edepth = 0.104 MeV.
- For 246Cm, β32 = 0.020 and Edepth = 34 keV.
- The ground state axial-quadrupole deformation for 248Cf and 250Fm is β20 ≈ 0.3.

## Context

This work extends the covariant density functional theory framework by breaking both axial and reflection symmetries simultaneously, building on prior constrained RMF calculations for fission barriers (e.g., Abusara (2010), Afanasjev (2013)). The finding that triaxiality lowers the outer barrier by 0.5–1 MeV is new and improves agreement with empirical barrier heights from Capote (2009). The study of β32 correlations in N=150 isotones follows the analysis of Chen (2008) and Jolos (2011) and is consistent with experimental observation that the 2− state in 248Cf is the lowest among these nuclei (Robinson, 2008). The results enable more accurate predictions of fission barriers for actinide and superheavy nuclei.
