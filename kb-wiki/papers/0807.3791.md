# Low energy theorem for virtual Compton scattering and generalized sum rules of the nucleon

> We formulate the low energy theorem for virtual Compton scattering off a nucleon and examine its consequences for generalized nucleon polarizabilites. As a result of a new, model independent definition of the low energy limit for VVCS reaction, all generalized sum rules of the nucleon have continuous limit for real photons and obtain contributions from the $t$-channel that were not included previously.

## Key claim

The low energy expansion for virtual Compton scattering (VVCS) must be defined around the point \(\nu = 0, q \cdot q' = 0\) (which corresponds to \(s = u = M^2\)) rather than around \(\nu = 0, t = 0\) as in prior work; this choice yields a model-independent separation of Born and non-Born contributions and ensures that all generalized sum rules have a continuous limit for real photons and receive previously omitted \(t\)-channel contributions.

## Method

The authors formulate a low energy theorem (LET) for symmetric VVCS by expanding the twelve invariant amplitudes in the Mandelstam variables \(\nu\), \(t\), and \(Q^2\) around the point \(\nu = 0, q \cdot q' = 0\) (i.e., \(t = -2Q^2\)). They separate the Born contributions (nucleon exchange in \(s\) and \(u\) channels, given in terms of physical elastic form factors) from the non-Born contributions (parametrized by generalized polarizabilities). Using fixed-\(t\) dispersion relations in \(\nu\) and subtracted dispersion relations in \(t\), they connect the forward (\(t=0\)) amplitudes to the low-energy point and derive sum rules for the generalized polarizabilities.

## Key numbers

- The spin-independent Born amplitudes at the low-energy point are \(\hat f^B_1(Q^2) = -e^2 F_P^2 / (4M^3)\) and \(\hat f^B_2(Q^2) = -e^2 F_D^2 / (4M^3)\), where \(F_D\) and \(F_P\) are the Dirac and Pauli elastic form factors of the nucleon.
- The low-energy expansion of the spin-independent amplitudes yields \(\hat f_1 = -e^2 F_P^2/(4M^3) - 4\pi\beta(Q^2) + O(\nu^2)\) and \(\hat f_2 = -e^2 F_D^2/(4M^3) + [4\pi\nu^2/(4M^2+2Q^2)][\alpha(Q^2)+\beta(Q^2)] + O(\nu^4)\), where \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) are generalized electric and magnetic polarizabilities.
- The generalized GDH sum rule at finite \(Q^2\) includes a \(t\)-channel contribution: \(-e^2 F_P^2(Q^2)/(8M^3) = (e^2/(MQ^2)) \int_0^{x_\pi} dx\, g_1(x,Q^2) - (2Q^2/\pi) \int dt' \, {\rm Im}\hat f_7(0,t',Q^2) / [t'(t'+2Q^2)]\), with \(x_\pi = Q^2/(Q^2+m_\pi^2+2Mm_\pi) < 1\).
- The generalized Baldin sum rule acquires a factor \((1+Q^2/2M^2)\) relative to the prior generalization by Drechsel et al. (2000).

## Context

This work extends the low energy theorem for real Compton scattering (Low, 1954) and the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule (Gerasimov, 1965; Drell and Hearn, 1966) to the virtual photon case. It corrects the formulation of generalized sum rules by Drechsel et al. (2000), which used an expansion around \(\nu=0, t=0\) and led to a discontinuity at the real photon point and a missing \(t\)-channel contribution. The new sum rules enable a consistent extraction of generalized nucleon polarizabilities from electron scattering data and provide a framework for interpreting the transition of the GDH integral from negative values at \(Q^2=0\) to small positive values at high \(Q^2\), as studied experimentally at JLab.
