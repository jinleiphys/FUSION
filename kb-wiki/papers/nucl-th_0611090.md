# Relativistic Dynamics of Non-ideal Fluids: Viscous and heat-conducting fluids I. General Aspects and 3+1 Formulation for Nuclear Collisions

> Relativistic non-ideal fluid dynamics is formulated in 3+1 space--time dimensions. The equations governing dissipative relativistic hydrodynamics are given in terms of the time and the 3-space quantities which correspond to those familiar from non-relativistic physics. Dissipation is accounted for by applying the causal theory of relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics. As a special case we consider a fluid without viscous/heat couplings in the causal system of transport/relaxation equations. For the study of physical systems we consider pure (1+1)-dimensional expansion in planar geometry, (1+1)-dimensional spherically symmetric ({\em fireball}) expansion, (1+1)-dimensional cylindrically symmetric expansion and a (2+1)-dimensional expansion with cylindrical symmetry in the transverse plane ({\em firebarell} expansion). The transport/relaxation equations are given in terms of the spatial components of the dissipative fluxes, since these are not independent. The choice for the independent components is analogous to the non-relativistic equations.

## Key claim

This paper provides a complete 3+1 space-time formulation of causal (Muller-Israel-Stewart) relativistic dissipative fluid dynamics for nuclear collisions, but the explicit transport equations are given only in the weak coupling limit where viscous/heat couplings are neglected and thermodynamic gradients are assumed small.

## Method

The covariant conservation equations for net charge and energy-momentum, together with the causal relaxation equations for bulk pressure, heat flux, and shear stress tensor, are decomposed into time and three-space components using the 3+1 formalism. The dissipative fluxes are expressed in terms of the ordinary three-velocity and lab-frame space and time derivatives, making the system suitable for numerical solution. The transport equations are simplified to the relativistic Maxwell-Cattaneo form by setting the viscous/heat coupling coefficients to zero and dropping terms involving thermodynamic gradients and acceleration-dissipative flux couplings.

## Key numbers

- The system comprises 14 independent dynamical variables: n, epsilon, u^mu, Pi, q^mu, pi^{munu} (1+1+3+1+3+5 fields)

## Context

This work extends the 3+1 formulation previously developed for relativistic ideal fluids (see DHR and references therein) to dissipative fluids using the causal theory of Muller (1967), Israel and Stewart (1979), which remedies the causality and stability problems of the earlier Eckart (1940) and Landau-Lifshitz theories. The formulation enables numerical simulations of dissipative relativistic nuclear collisions, building on recent applications of causal fluid dynamics to RHIC data (Muronga 2002, Heinz and collaborators). The companion paper II (Muronga, same year) provides the microscopic kinetic-theory derivation and explicit expressions for the transport coefficients and second-order coefficients alpha_i and beta_i.
