# Comment on "Evaluation of the Pion-Nucleon Sigma Term from CHAOS Data"

> The value of $59 \pm 12$ MeV for the pion-nucleon ($πN$) $Σ$ term, which Stahov, Clement, and Wagner recently extracted from the differential cross sections (DCSs) of the CHAOS Collaboration, does not match well the expectation of an enhanced (more positive) isoscalar component in the $πN$ interaction at low energies, which the rest of the modern (meson-factory) data favour. However, we have already demonstrated that the angular distribution of the CHAOS $π^+ p$ DCSs is not compatible in shape with the rest of the modern low-energy $π^+ p$ data. This problem must be addressed and resolved by the CHAOS Collaboration prior to the extrapolation of their partial-wave amplitudes into the unphysical region.

## Key claim

The value of 59 ± 12 MeV for the pion-nucleon Σ term extracted from CHAOS differential cross sections by Stahov, Clement, and Wagner does not match the expectation of an enhanced isoscalar component favored by other modern meson-factory data, but this discrepancy is secondary to the fact that the CHAOS π⁺p angular distributions are incompatible in shape with the rest of the modern low-energy π⁺p data.

## Method

The authors performed an exclusive partial-wave analysis of the CHAOS π⁺p and π⁻p differential cross sections, applying the same analysis criteria previously used on other low-energy πN measurements. They first used standard low-energy parameterizations of s- and p-wave K-matrix elements, then attempted to fit the ETH model (which includes t-channel σ- and ρ-exchange graphs and s- and u-channel baryon contributions below 2 GeV) to the combined CHAOS elastic-scattering database. They also compared the CHAOS data to predictions from their own recent partial-wave analysis to test normalization and shape compatibility.

## Key numbers

- The Σ term value extracted by Stahov, Clement, and Wagner from CHAOS data is 59 ± 12 MeV.
- The initial CHAOS database contained 546 data points; eleven outliers were removed, leaving 535 degrees of freedom for the ETH model fit.
- The SAID solution yields a Σ term of 79 ± 7 MeV, which the authors consider not unreasonable.
- Koch's canonical value for the Σ term is about 60 MeV.

## Context

This comment extends the authors' prior work (Matsinos and Rasche, 2013) demonstrating that the CHAOS π⁺p differential cross sections are incompatible in shape with other modern low-energy πN data. The paper contradicts the claim by Stahov, Clement, and Wagner that the CHAOS data can be reliably used to extract the Σ term, and it supports the SAID solution (Arndt et al.) result of 79 ± 7 MeV as more consistent with the broader modern dataset. The work enables future analyses by clarifying that the CHAOS data should not be included in sensitive low-energy analyses until the shape incompatibility is resolved by the CHAOS Collaboration.
