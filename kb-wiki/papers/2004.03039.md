# High-Energy Phase Diagrams with Charge and Isospin Axes under Heavy-Ion Collision and Stellar Conditions

> We investigate the phase transition from hadron to quark matter in the general case without the assumption of chemical equilibrium. The effects of net strangeness on charge and isospin fractions, chemical potentials, and temperature are studied in the context of the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model that incorporates chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement. The extent to which these quantities are probed during deconfinement in conditions expected to exist in protoneutron stars, binary neutron-star mergers, and heavy-ion collisions is analyzed via the construction of 3-dimensional phase diagrams.

## Key claim

The paper investigates the hadron-to-quark matter phase transition without assuming chemical equilibrium, constructing 3-dimensional phase diagrams that show how the deconfinement coexistence region depends on charge fraction or isospin fraction across a range of temperatures and baryon chemical potentials, but only up to 160 MeV (below the model's critical point at 167 MeV) to keep the discussion general and avoid the special feature of a critical point.

## Method

The authors use the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model, a nonlinear realization of the SU(3) sigma model that includes both hadronic and quark degrees of freedom with chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement. They construct 3-dimensional phase diagrams by varying the charged or isospin chemical potential at fixed temperature and charge/isospin fraction, determining the deconfinement coexistence region from a discontinuity in the deconfinement order parameter Phi. For non-strange matter, the strange chemical potential is numerically determined to enforce zero net strangeness; for strange matter, no constraint on net strangeness is applied (mu_S = 0).

## Key numbers

- The CMF model predicts a critical point for the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition of isospin symmetric matter at T_c = 16.4 MeV, mu_B,c = 910 MeV
- The deconfinement first-order phase transition line starts at T = 167 MeV for zero-strangeness isospin-symmetric matter and terminates on the zero-temperature axis at four times nuclear saturation density
- Nuclear saturation properties used for fitting: density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3, binding energy B/A = -16 MeV, compressibility K = 300 MeV, symmetry energy E_sym = 30 MeV with slope L = 88 MeV
- Hyperon potentials: U_Lambda = -28.00 MeV, U_Sigma = 5 MeV, U_Xi = -18 MeV
- Bare masses: 150 MeV for nucleons, 354.91 MeV for hyperons, 5 MeV for up and down quarks, 150 MeV for strange quarks
- Phase diagrams are shown for temperatures from 0 to 160 MeV and charge fractions from 0 to 0.5

## Context

This work extends previous studies of QCD phase diagrams that were typically restricted to two dimensions (temperature vs. baryon chemical potential or temperature vs. isospin chemical potential), and addresses the need to understand which plane of the phase diagram is probed in different experiments as noted by Monnai (2019). The CMF model used here was previously applied to neutron and protoneutron stars by Roark (2018) and Dexheimer (2018), and the authors note that lattice QCD results at finite isospin chemical potential (Brandt 2016, 2017, 2018) are not afflicted by the sign problem, while finite baryon chemical potential calculations remain challenging. The work enables construction of equation of state tables for the CompOSE repository and provides a framework for comparing conditions in heavy-ion collisions, protoneutron stars, and binary neutron-star mergers.
