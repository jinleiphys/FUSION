# Cranking in isospace - towards a consistent mean-field description of N=Z nuclei

> Excitation spectra of T=0,1,2 states in even-even (e-e) and odd-odd (o-o) N=Z nuclei are analyzed within a mean-field based model involving isovector and isoscalar pairing interactions and the iso-cranking formalism applied to restore approximately isospin symmetry. It is shown that T=0 states in o-o and T=1 states in e-e nuclei correspond to two-quasiparticle, time-reversal symmetry breaking excitations since their angular momenta are different than zero. On the other hand the lowest T=2 states in e-e and T=1 states in o-o nuclei, which both are similar in structure to their even-even isobaric analogue states, are described as e-e type vaccua excited (iso-cranked) in isospace. It appears that in all cases isoscalar pairing plays a crucial role in restoring the proper value of the inertia parameter in isospace.

# Cranking in isospace - towards a consistent mean-field description of N=Z nuclei

## Key claim

The paper shows that a mean-field model incorporating both isovector (t=1) and isoscalar (t=0) pairing interactions, combined with approximate isospin restoration via the iso-cranking formalism, can consistently describe the excitation energies of T=0, 1, and 2 states in even-even and odd-odd N=Z nuclei, provided that isoscalar pairing is enforced to be strong enough to reproduce the empirical Wigner energy. The authors explicitly state that the isoscalar pairing strength is fitted to the Wigner energy, and that it is "not at all settled whether it is due to a static pairing effect."

## Method

The authors use a Woods-Saxon single-particle potential with fixed deformation (beta_2 = 0.05) plus schematic isovector (t=1) and isoscalar (t=0) pairing interactions. The isovector pairing strength G^{t=1}(A) is determined using the average gap method, while the isoscalar strength G^{t=0}(A) is fitted to reproduce the empirical Wigner energy strength W(A) ~ 47/A MeV. The Bogoliubov transformation is solved using the Lipkin-Nogami approximate number projection technique, allowing mixing of t=1 and t=0 pairing phases. Isospin symmetry is approximately restored by applying the isospin cranking formalism to generate T=0, 1, and 2 states in N=Z nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The Wigner energy is parametrized as E_W = W(A)|N-Z| + d(A) delta_{NZ} pi_{pn}, where W(A) ~ 47/A MeV.
- For T=2 states in even-even N=Z nuclei, calculations including only t=1 pairing give excitation energies "far below the empirical data" (no specific numerical values given for this discrepancy).
- The single-particle model yields an inertia parameter a = delta_e (the average level spacing at the Fermi energy) for both even-T and odd-T bands, with the T=1 to T=0 energy shift equal to delta_e.
- In the low-frequency limit with isovector pairing, the kinematic iso-moment of inertia is approximately 0.80/delta_e for Delta = 0.5 delta_e, 0.96/delta_e for Delta = delta_e, and 0.90/delta_e for Delta = 2 delta_e.
- The symmetry energy from self-consistent mean-field models yields E_sym(A,T) ~ (1/2) a_sym T^2/A, providing only T^2/[T(T+1)] fraction of the empirical symmetry energy, where empirical data are consistent with T(T+lambda) dependence with lambda ~ 1.25 (citing Jan65).
- The T=2 states in even-even nuclei are computed by iso-cranking the e-e vacuum to T_x = sqrt(6).
- The T=1 states in even-even nuclei are computed as 2qp configurations iso-cranked to T_x = 1.
- The T=1 states in odd-odd nuclei are computed as e-e-like vacua iso-cranked to T_x = sqrt(2).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Satula 2000a, Satula 2000b) on the role of isoscalar pairing in N=Z nuclei. It addresses the long-standing problem of the Wigner energy, which traditional mass models (Myers 1966, ETFSI, Satula 1997as) fail to reproduce microscopically within the mean-field approach. The paper contrasts its approach with the "congruence energy" mechanism of Myers (1997) and with spherical Skyrme-HFB calculations that show no Wigner energy effect. The results enable a consistent mean-field description of isobaric excitations in N=Z nuclei and challenge the argument by the Berkeley group (Macchiavelli 2000) that near-degeneracy of T=1 and T=0 states rules out isoscalar pairing.
