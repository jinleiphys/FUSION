# Return of the EMC Effect: Finite Nuclei

> A light front formalism for deep inelastic lepton scattering from finite nuclei is developed. In particular, the nucleon plus momentum distribution and a finite system analog of the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem are presented. Using a relativistic mean field model, numerical results for the plus momentum distribution and ratio of bound to free nucleon structure functions for Oxygen, Calcium and Lead are given. We show that we can incorporate light front physics with excellent accuracy while using easily computed equal time wavefunctions. Assuming nucleon structure is not modified in-medium we find that the calculations are not consistent with the binding effect apparent in the data not only in the magnitude of the effect, but in the dependence on the number of nucleons.

## Key claim

Using a relativistic mean field model and assuming nucleon structure is not modified in-medium, the calculated nuclear structure function ratios for Oxygen, Calcium, and Lead are not consistent with the EMC effect data, failing both in the magnitude of the effect and in the dependence on the number of nucleons.

## Method

The authors developed a light front formalism for deep inelastic lepton scattering from finite nuclei, deriving the nucleon plus momentum distribution from the covariant Green's function using a Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD-I) Lagrangian. They computed the plus momentum distribution numerically from relativistic self-consistent Hartree wave functions for Oxygen-16, Calcium-40, and Lead-208, then used a convolution formula with a free nucleon structure function parameterization to obtain the nuclear structure function ratio.

## Key numbers

* The plus momentum distribution peaks near y=1 for all nuclei studied, as required by the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem analog derived for finite systems.
* For Oxygen-16, the harmonic oscillator model gave an oscillator length b=1.6 fm fit to the root mean square radius of 2.7 fm, with a scalar potential v0 approximately 50 MeV.
* The ratio function R(x) at x approximately 0.72 shows a minimum that becomes less pronounced as A increases, contrary to the experimental trend where R(x approximately 0.72) scales as A^{-1/3}.
* Scalar mesons carry a small fraction of plus momentum (delta y approximately 0.005) in finite nuclei, which vanishes as A approaches infinity.
* The effective nucleon mass in nuclear matter within QHD-I is approximately 0.56 times the free nucleon mass.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous infinite nuclear matter result (Miller and Smith, 2002) to finite nuclei, where they found that relativistic mean field theory with unmodified nucleon structure produces no appreciable binding effect. The results contradict the standard binding effect explanation for the EMC effect observed by the European Muon Collaboration (Aubert, 1983) and studied in reviews by Arneodo (1994), Geesaman, Saito, and Thomas (1995), and Piller and Weise (2000). The failure suggests that explanations beyond conventional nucleon-meson dynamics are needed, such as the quark-meson coupling model (Saito, 1994), the chiral quark soliton model reviewed by Diakonov (2000), or true modifications to nucleon structure from nuclear interactions.
