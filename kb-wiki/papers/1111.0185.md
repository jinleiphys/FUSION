# Exploring phase diagram of $N_f=3$ QCD at $μ=0$ with HISQ fermions

> We studied the QCD phase transition as a function of quark mass in the $N_f=3$ QCD at vanishing baryon density. Lattice simulations have been performed using Highly Improved Staggered Quarks on $N_τ=6$ lattices with quark masses that correspond to pion masses in the region $80 \lesssim m_π \lesssim 230 $MeV. We found no evidence of the first order phase transition in the current pion mass window. The pion mass at the critical point where the first order phase transition starts is estimated to be $m^c_π\lesssim 45$ MeV.

# Exploring phase diagram of $N_f=3$ QCD at $\mu=0$ with HISQ fermions

## Key claim
The authors find no evidence of a first-order chiral phase transition for pion masses down to approximately 80 MeV on $N_\tau=6$ lattices using the HISQ action, and estimate an upper bound on the critical pion mass of $m_\pi^c \lesssim 45$ MeV, a bound that is conditional on finite-volume effects and on using the chiral condensate as an approximate order parameter rather than the true mixed order parameter required by $Z(2)$ universality.

## Method
Lattice QCD simulations were performed with three degenerate quark flavors using the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) action on $N_\tau=6$ lattices with spatial volumes $16^3$ and $24^3$. Five quark masses were simulated, corresponding to pion masses in the range $80 \lesssim m_\pi \lesssim 230$ MeV; the chiral condensate and its disconnected susceptibility were measured as functions of the gauge coupling $\beta$, and the peak heights of the susceptibility were fit to the $Z(2)$ scaling form $\chi_M/T^2 \propto (m-m_c)^{1/\delta-1}$ to extract an upper bound on the critical quark mass.

## Key numbers
- Pion masses simulated: $m_\pi \approx 80, 90, 110, 160, 230$ MeV (from quark masses $am_q = 0.0009375, 0.00125, 0.001875, 0.00375, 0.0075$ respectively)
- Lattice temporal extent: $N_\tau = 6$; spatial volumes: $16^3$ and $24^3$
- Upper bound on critical pion mass from scaling fit: $m_\pi^c \lesssim 45$ MeV
- Maximum number of trajectories per parameter set: up to 8800 (for $am_q=0.001875$)
- Number of $\beta$ values scanned per quark mass: between 6 and 17

## Context
This work extends previous lattice studies of the 3-flavor chiral critical point using standard staggered actions (Christ 2003, de Forcrand 2004, de Forcrand 2007) which found critical pion masses around 300 MeV on $N_\tau=4$, and improved p4 actions (Karsch 2004) which found $m_\pi^c \approx 70$ MeV on $N_\tau=4$. The use of the HISQ action, which has better taste symmetry than p4 or asqtad, allows simulations on coarser lattices with reduced cutoff effects; the results on $N_\tau=6$ are consistent with stout-action results (Endrodi 2007) suggesting $m_\pi^c \approx 50$ MeV. Together these findings indicate the first-order region is very small in the quark-mass plane, implying that for the chiral critical point to exist at finite chemical potential, the critical surface must bend toward the physical point with a very large curvature.
