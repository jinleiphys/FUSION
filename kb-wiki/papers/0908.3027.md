# Momentum space trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential

> We transform the trigonometric S wave Rosen-Morse potential to momentum space in employing its property of being a harmonic angular function on the three dimensional hypersphere S^3.

# Momentum space trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential

## Key claim

The trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential, when treated as an angular function on the three-dimensional hypersphere S^3 rather than as a flat-space potential, yields a well-defined closed-form Fourier transform to momentum space; the flat-space Fourier integral would otherwise be divergent.

## Method

The authors transform the S-wave trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential to momentum space by exploiting its property as a harmonic angular function on S^3, the three-dimensional surface of constant positive curvature embedded in E_4. They calculate the 4D Fourier transform of the potential expressed as a function of the second polar angle χ on S^3, using a 4D plane wave and restricting the integration to the S^3 hypersphere via a delta function. The calculation distinguishes between the Northern hemisphere (χ in [0, π/2]) and Southern hemisphere (χ in [π/2, π]) of the hypersphere.

## Key numbers

- The momentum-space potential on the Northern hemisphere is given by Π(|q|) = c × [2 sin²(|q|/(2ħ√κ))] / [(|q|/(ħ√κ))²], with c = 2G × (2μ)/(ħ²κ), where κ is the constant curvature of S^3.
- The potential is finite at the origin (|q| = 0) and approaches the Coulomb propagator asymptotically in the ultraviolet (large |q|).
- The angular variable χ ranges over [0, π] on S^3, with the Northern hemisphere covering χ ∈ [0, π/2] and the Southern hemisphere covering χ ∈ [π/2, π].

## Context

This work extends the treatment of the trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential originally introduced by Schrodinger (1940) as an angular function on S^3, and later studied within supersymmetric quantum mechanics by Levai. The potential has found applications in quark confinement as an exactly solvable extension to the Cornell potential (Quiry), in quantum dot theory (Kurochkin), and in plasma and instanton physics. The closed-form momentum-space representation enables more efficient Faddeev few-body calculations and provides a well-defined alternative to the divergent flat-space Fourier transform previously encountered by Quiry (2008).
