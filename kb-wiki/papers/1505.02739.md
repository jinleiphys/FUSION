# Hadron mass corrections in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering

> The spin-dependent cross sections for semi-inclusive lepton-nucleon scattering are derived in the framework of collinear factorization, including the effects of masses of the target and produced hadron at finite momentum transfer squared Q^2. At leading order the cross sections factorize into products of parton distribution and fragmentation functions evaluated in terms of new, mass-dependent scaling variables. The size of the hadron mass corrections is estimated at kinematics relevant for future semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering experiments.

## Key claim

The paper derives spin-dependent semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) cross sections including both target nucleon mass and produced hadron mass corrections within collinear factorization, showing that at leading order the cross sections factorize into products of parton distribution and fragmentation functions evaluated at new mass-dependent scaling variables, with the caveat that the choice of the scattered parton virtuality is not uniquely determined and the adopted value is the one closest to the physical quark mass.

## Method

The authors expand the external and parton four-momenta in terms of light-cone vectors and derive the finite-Q² scaling variables ξ (Nachtmann variable) and ζ_h (fragmentation variable) that replace the Bjorken limit variables x_B and z_h. They compute the hadronic tensor at leading order in α_s by contracting quark-quark correlators for the distribution and fragmentation functions, then contract with the leptonic tensor to obtain spin-averaged and spin-dependent cross sections expressed with a Jacobian factor J_h and the modified scaling variables.

## Key numbers

- The Nachtmann scaling variable is defined as ξ = 2x_B / (1 + sqrt(1 + 4x_B²M²/Q²)), which reduces to x_B in the Bjorken limit.
- The upper limit on x_B for SIDIS is given by 1/x_B^max = 1 + [m_h(m_h + 2M_b) + M_b² - M²]/Q², with M_b = M for pions and M_b = M_Λ for kaons.
- At Q² = 1 GeV² and x_B ≈ 0.6, deviations of ζ_h from z_h reach approximately 30% as z_h → 1 for pion production.
- For kaon production at Q² = 1 GeV², no kaons can be produced in the current fragmentation region for x_B ≳ 0.66, and no kaons at all for x_B ≳ 0.8.
- The Jacobian factor J_h deviates from unity by about 25% below unity at large z_h for x_B = 0.8 and Q² = 1 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the collinear factorization approach to hadron mass corrections (HMCs) previously applied to inclusive DIS by Kretzer and collaborators, Accardi and Qiu (2008), and to unpolarized SIDIS by Accardi, Hobbs, and Melnitchouk (2009). It enables future SIDIS experiments at Jefferson Lab, COMPASS, and the proposed Electron-Ion Collider to reliably extract leading-twist spin-dependent parton distribution functions from data at low Q² (1-2 GeV²), where a significant fraction of the world's polarized data lies, and where none of the previous spin-PDF analyses that included polarized SIDIS data (such as Leader, Sidorov, and Stamenov (2010) or de Florian, Sassot, Stratmann, and Vogelsang (2009)) had accounted for HMCs.
