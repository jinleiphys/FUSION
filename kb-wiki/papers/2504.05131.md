# One-loop transverse-momentum-dependent soft function at higher orders in the dimensional regulator

> The transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) soft function for a generic hadroproduction process involving massive colored particles is analytically calculated at the one-loop level, extended to higher orders in the dimensional regulator $ε$. We present both the azimuthal-angle-averaged and azimuthal-angle-dependent soft functions in impact-parameter space, making them suitable for small $q_T$ resummation calculations. Their analytic expressions are provided in terms of multiple polylogarithms. Our results offer essential ingredients for a complete higher-order perturbative calculation of the TMD soft function.

## Key claim

The authors analytically compute the one-loop transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) soft function for generic hadroproduction processes involving massive colored particles, extending known results to higher orders in the dimensional regulator epsilon (up to O(epsilon^3)), whereas previous calculations were limited to O(epsilon^0) for the azimuthal-angle-dependent case.

## Method

The authors use dimensional regularization with spacetime dimension d=4-2epsilon and an analytic rapidity regulator to handle rapidity divergences. They compute the TMD soft function in impact-parameter (b_T) space by evaluating eikonal Feynman integrals for generic processes with massive colored external particles, excluding processes with jets in the final state. The results are expressed in terms of multiple polylogarithms, and both the azimuthal-angle-averaged and azimuthal-angle-dependent soft functions are presented.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop TMD soft function is computed up to O(epsilon^3) in the dimensional regulator epsilon, extending the known O(epsilon^0) results from Catani (2014) and Catani (2021)
- The spacetime dimension is d=4-2epsilon, with the dimensional regulator epsilon used for infrared singularities
- The LO soft function is given by S^(0)_(perp,ll') = delta_(l',l) * <C_l|C_l>, independent of kinematic variables
- The impact parameter b_0 = 2e^(-gamma_E), where gamma_E is the Euler-Mascheroni constant
- The gluon spin degrees of freedom are omega(g) = 2(1-epsilon)D_A = 16(1-epsilon) in CDR with d=4-2epsilon
- The quark spin degrees of freedom are omega(q) = omega(anti-q) = 2N_c = 6

## Context

This work extends the one-loop TMD soft function calculations for back-to-back heavy quark pair production by Li (2013) and Catani (2014), which were limited to O(epsilon^0). It also generalizes the results of Catani (2021) and Ju (2022), who computed the azimuthal-angle-dependent soft function at one loop up to O(epsilon^0) but with remaining one-fold integrals requiring numerical evaluation. The results provide essential ingredients for complete N^(k+1)LO computations where the one-loop TMD soft function at O(epsilon^k) with k>=0 is required, and are applicable to any process without jets in the final state.
