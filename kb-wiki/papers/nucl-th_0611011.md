# Korteweg - de Vries solitons in relativistic hydrodynamics

> In a previous work, assuming that the nucleus can be treated as a perfect fluid, we have studied the propagation of perturbations in the baryon density. For a given equation of state we have derived a Korteweg - de Vries (KdV) equation from Euler and continuity equations in non-relativistic hydrodynamics. Here, using a more general equation of state, we extend our formalism to relativistic hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

Korteweg-de Vries (KdV) solitons can exist in relativistic hydrodynamics for nuclear matter, provided the energy density contains a Laplacian of the baryon density; the soliton amplitude is enhanced by a factor of \(3/(3-c_s^2)\) relative to the non-relativistic case, indicating more efficient energy propagation in a medium with a stiffer equation of state.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic Euler and continuity equations for a perfect fluid at zero temperature, using the Gibbs relation to express the enthalpy. They consider three models for the equation of state: model I uses a relativistic mean-field energy density with a derivative coupling term (from a previous non-relativistic work); model II uses a general polynomial ansatz for the energy density with a Laplacian term, imposing the nuclear matter saturation condition; model III uses the same polynomial ansatz but without the saturation condition. For each model, they perform a Taylor expansion of the energy per nucleon around equilibrium density, introduce stretched coordinates and a small expansion parameter, and derive the KdV equation for the first-order density perturbation, obtaining analytical soliton solutions.

## Key numbers

- Speed of sound \(c_s\) in nuclear matter is typically in the range \(0.15 - 0.25\) (in natural units, relative to the speed of light).
- For model I (MQHD), the KdV equation becomes \(\frac{\partial \rho_1}{\partial \tau} + (3 - c_s^2) \rho_1 \frac{\partial \rho_1}{\partial \xi} + \left( \frac{g_v^2 \rho_0}{2 M c_s^2 m_v^4 R^2} \right) \frac{\partial^3 \rho_1}{\partial \xi^3} = 0\).
- The soliton solution for model I is \(\hat{\rho}_1(x,t) = \frac{3}{(3 - c_s^2)} \frac{(u - c_s)}{c_s} \, \text{sech}^2 \left[ \frac{m_v^2}{g_v} \sqrt{\frac{(u - c_s) c_s M}{2 \rho_0}} (x - ut) \right]\).
- In the non-relativistic limit (\(c_s^2 \rightarrow 0\)), the amplitude factor reduces from \(3/(3 - c_s^2)\) to 1, recovering the previous result \(\hat{\rho}_1(x,t) = \frac{(u - c_s)}{c_s} \, \text{sech}^2 \left[ \frac{m_v^2}{g_v} \sqrt{\frac{(u - c_s) c_s M}{2 \rho_0}} (x - ut) \right]\).

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic KdV soliton formalism for nuclear matter developed by the same authors in a previous paper (Fogaca and Navarra, 2005) to the relativistic regime, motivated by the fact that typical sound speeds (0.15-0.25) suggest relativistic effects may be sizeable. It builds on earlier suggestions that KdV solitons might form in the nuclear medium (Frass et al., 1980s) and uses equations of state consistent with density functional theory (Furnstahl, 1997). The results enable future study of supersonic motion in hot and dense hadronic matter, with potential applications to jet quenching measurements at RHIC.
