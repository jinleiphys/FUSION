# Spatial densities of the photon on the light front

> The light front densities of momentum, angular momentum, and intrinsic pressure are calculated for the photon, both in the free case and at leading order in quantum electrodynamics. In the latter case, we moreover decompose the form factors into photon and electron contributions. Circularly and linearly polarized photons are both considered, with the latter containing significant azimuthal modulations in both the momentum density and in intrinsic stresses. We find that the D-term of the photon is positive instead of negative, and accordingly the intrinsic radial pressure of the photon is negative. Despite this, the radiation pressure exerted by the photon is positive. We illustrate through explicit calculation how the intrinsic pressure associated with the D-term and the radiation pressure exerted by the photon are different quantities.

## Key claim

The D-term of the photon is positive (D(0)=1) even for a free photon, directly contradicting the widely held belief that negativity of the D-term is a necessary condition for stability; the authors argue that the masslessness of the photon prevents it from decaying, so its exception to the postulated stability criterion may not be an issue.

## Method

The authors calculate the light front densities of momentum, angular momentum, and intrinsic pressure for the photon using the formalism of gravitational form factors (GFFs) on the light front, both for the free photon and at leading order in quantum electrodynamics (QED). For the QED case, they compute the Feynman diagrams contributing to the photon EMT vertex using dimensional regularization and on-shell renormalization, decomposing the form factors into photon and electron contributions, and consider both circularly and linearly polarized photon states.

## Key numbers

- Free photon D-term: D(0) = 1 (positive), violating the postulated stability condition that D(0) < 0
- Free photon radial pressure integral: ∫ d²b⊥ p_r,γ(b⊥;σ) = -1/(4P⁺) × 1/σ² (negative and diverges as wave packet localization increases)
- Free photon mean squared mechanical radius: ⟨b⊥²⟩_mech(σ) = 2σ² (goes to zero as σ→0)
- Leading-order QED correction to F₁(t) contains terms with coefficients 35/18, -13/6(1/τ), and terms involving sinh⁻¹(√τ) where τ = -t/(4m_e²)
- Leading-order QED results for 2tF₃(t) and 2tF₂(t) contain terms with coefficients 1/3, -7/τ and 1/3, +3/τ respectively, plus hyperbolic inverse sine terms

## Context

This work extends the light front density formalism for spin-one targets developed in the companion paper (Freese and Cosyn, 2022) to the massless photon case, and builds on earlier calculations of photon gravitational form factors by Berends (1975) and Milton (1977). The finding that the photon has a positive D-term calls into question the stability criterion proposed by Polyakov (2018) and Lorce (2018) that D(0) < 0 is required for stability, following similar questions raised by the infinite positive D-term of the QED electron (Metz, 2021). The work enables further study of whether the intrinsic pressure encoded by the D-term and the radiation pressure exerted by a photon are fundamentally different quantities.
