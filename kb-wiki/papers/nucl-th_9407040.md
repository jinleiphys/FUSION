# Low energy theorem for scalar and vector interactions of a composite spin-1/2 system

> Scalar and vector interactions, with the scalar interaction coupled to a composite spin-1/2 system so as to cause a shift of its mass, are shown to obey a low-energy theorem which guarantees that the second order interaction due to z-graphs is the same as for a point Dirac particle. Off-shell and contact interactions appropriate to the composite system cancel and this is verified in a model of a composite fermion. The result provides a justification for the use of the Dirac equation as it has been in relativistic nuclear scattering and mean field theories.

# Low energy theorem for scalar and vector interactions of a composite spin-1/2 system

## Key claim
For a composite spin-1/2 system, scalar and vector interactions obey a low-energy theorem that guarantees the second-order interaction from z-graphs is identical to that of a point Dirac particle, provided the scalar interaction is coupled to the scale-breaking charge so as to shift the system's mass. The theorem holds only to second order in the interactions and in the low-energy limit where the momentum transfer q approaches zero.

## Method
The authors construct a model Lagrangian with an elementary fermion and scalar boson that forms a composite spin-1/2 bound state. They define the scalar interaction through a scale-breaking charge proportional to the divergence of the dilatation current, which shifts the bound state mass from M to M+S. Using Ward identities for both the vector vertex and the scalar vertex derived from scale invariance, they expand the forward scattering amplitude to second order in the scalar and vector potentials, including pole terms, crossed terms, off-shell propagation, vertex expansions, and contact interactions, and demonstrate that all non-universal contributions cancel.

## Key numbers
- Scalar and vector potentials of magnitude about 300 MeV characterize the proton-nucleus interaction in relativistic mean field theories.
- The second-order scalar potential from the low-energy theorem yields the repulsive term p^2 S^2 / (2 epsilon^3), where epsilon = sqrt(M^2 + p^2).
- The wave function renormalization factor Z_2 is defined as Z_2 = -{A_0 + 2M[M A'_0 + B'_0]}^{-1}, where A_0, A'_0, B'_0 are on-shell values of the self-energy functions.

## Context
This work addresses a debate in relativistic nuclear physics about whether compositeness of the nucleon suppresses the z-graph contributions that are central to Dirac equation treatments of proton-nucleus scattering. Previous arguments by Brodsky (1984), Bleszynski, Bleszynski and Brodsky (1987), Jaroszewicz and Brodsky (1991), and Cohen (1992) suggested strong suppression of z-graphs for composite systems. The present paper extends the low-energy theorem familiar from Compton scattering (Low, 1954; Gell-Mann and Goldberger, 1954) to the scalar interaction case, showing that the Dirac equation treatment used in relativistic nuclear scattering and mean field theories (Clark et al., 1983; McNeil, Shepard and Wallace, 1983; Tjon and Wallace, 1987) remains valid for composite fermions under the condition that the scalar interaction causes a mass shift. Whether these conditions apply to QCD remains an open question.
