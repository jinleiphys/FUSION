# Exact and approximate many-body dynamics with stochastic one-body density matrix evolution

> We show that the dynamics of interacting fermions can be exactly replaced by a quantum jump theory in the many-body density matrix space. In this theory, jumps occur between densities formed of pairs of Slater determinants, $D_{ab}=| Φ_a > < Φ_b |$, where each state evolves according to the Stochastic Schrödinger Equation (SSE) given in ref. \cite{Jul02}. A stochastic Liouville-von Neumann equation is derived as well as the associated Bogolyubov-Born-Green-Kirwood-Yvon (BBGKY) hierarchy. Due to the specific form of the many-body density along the path, the presented theory is equivalent to a stochastic theory in one-body density matrix space, in which each density matrix evolves according to its own mean field augmented by a one-body noise. Guided by the exact reformulation, a stochastic mean field dynamics valid in the weak coupling approximation is proposed. This theory leads to an approximate treatment of two-body effects similar to the extended Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (Extended TDHF) scheme. In this stochastic mean field dynamics, statistical mixing can be directly considered and jumps occur on a coarse-grained time scale. Accordingly, numerical effort is expected to be significantly reduced for applications.

## Key claim

The exact dynamics of interacting fermions can be replaced by a stochastic theory in one-body density matrix space, where each density matrix evolves according to its own mean field augmented by a one-body noise; however, the exact stochastic formulation remains numerically intractable for realistic systems, and an approximate stochastic mean field dynamics valid in the weak coupling regime is proposed as a practical alternative.

## Method

The author derives a stochastic Liouville-von Neumann equation and an associated BBGKY hierarchy starting from a functional integral representation of the residual two-body interaction, using the Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation on the particle-hole part of the Hamiltonian. The exact reformulation shows that the many-body density matrix can be expressed as an average over pairs of Slater determinants, each evolving according to a Stochastic Schroedinger Equation (SSE). Guided by this exact structure, an approximate stochastic mean field dynamics is constructed in which the noise acts on a coarse-grained time scale, leading to an extended Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (Extended TDHF) treatment of two-body effects.

## Context

This work extends the functional integral approach of Jul02 (2002) and the stochastic mean field theories of Ayi80 (1980), Gra81 (1981), Bal81 (1981), Rei92 (1992), and Oni95 (1995) by providing an exact stochastic reformulation of the many-body problem that preserves the Slater determinant structure. The approximate stochastic mean field dynamics enables a computationally tractable treatment of two-body correlations in mesoscopic systems such as nuclei, atomic clusters, or Bose-Einstein condensates, and is expected to significantly reduce numerical effort compared to exact stochastic methods.
