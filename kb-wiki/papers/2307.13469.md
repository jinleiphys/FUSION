# Dispersion effects in elastic electron scattering from $^{208}$Pb

> Dispersion corrections to elastic electron scattering from $^{208}$Pb at energies up to 150 MeV are estimated within the second-order Born approximation. All strong transient nuclear excited states with angular momentum up to $L=3$ and natural parity are taken into consideration. It is found that at small scattering angles the high-lying $L=1$ isovector states provide the dominant contribution to the dispersive change of the differential cross section and of the beam-normal spin asymmetry. At the backmost angles these changes are considerably smaller and are basically due to the isoscalar $L=2$ and, to a lesser extent, $L=3$ states. In comparison with the findings for $^{12}$C, the dispersive cross-section modifications for $^{208}$Pb are significantly larger, while the spin-asymmetry changes are much smaller at all angles. The latter originates from the destructive interference of the multipole contributions in $^{208}$Pb that switch sign when proceeding from $L$ to $L+1$. This mechanism might be the origin of the small beam-normal spin asymmetries recently measured in ${}^{208}$Pb at much larger energies.

## Key claim

Dispersion corrections to elastic electron scattering from $^{208}$Pb at energies up to 150 MeV are estimated within the second-order Born approximation, finding that the high-lying $L=1$ isovector states dominate at small scattering angles while isoscalar $L=2$ and $L=3$ states take over at backmost angles; the authors note that the second-order Born approximation may underpredict dispersion effects compared to a full distorted-wave treatment.

## Method

The authors calculated dispersion corrections using the second-order Born approximation (Feynman box diagram) for elastic electron scattering from $^{208}$Pb at 56 MeV and 150 MeV. They included ten transient nuclear excited states with angular momentum up to $L=3$ and natural parity, with charge and current transition densities computed from a self-consistent Hartree-Fock plus Random Phase Approximation (HF+RPA) using the Skyrme parametrization SkP. The leading-order Coulombic cross section was obtained from phase-shift analysis using a Fourier-Bessel representation of the ground-state charge density.

## Key numbers

- At 56 MeV, the total dispersive cross-section change $\Delta \sigma_{\rm box}$ is well below 0.5% at all scattering angles.
- At 150 MeV, the Sherman function $S_{\rm coul}$ has a maximum of $3.4 \times 10^{-2}$ at $\vartheta_f = 179.78^\circ$.
- At 56 MeV, the Sherman function $S_{\rm coul}$ decreases monotonously to a minimum of -0.47 at $179.4^\circ$.
- The dispersive cross-section modification for $^{208}$Pb at 150 MeV and fixed angle $50^\circ$ is a factor of 8 above that for $^{12}$C.
- The lowest $1^-$ state in $^{208}$Pb is at 5.512 MeV excitation energy; the lowest $2^+$ state is at 4.085 MeV; the lowest $3^-$ state is at 2.615 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of dispersion effects in elastic electron scattering, particularly the comparison with $^{12}$C targets studied by Jakubassa-Amundsen (2023) and the experimental measurements of beam-normal spin asymmetries in $^{208}$Pb at GeV energies by Abrahamyan (2012) and Adhikari (2021). The authors use the same HF+RPA approach previously applied by Colo (2013, 2021) and test the conjecture from Bethe and Molinari (1971) and Borie and Calmet (1972) that dipole states in the giant resonance region dominate dispersive changes. The results help explain the puzzlingly small beam-normal spin asymmetries measured in $^{208}$Pb at high energies, showing that destructive interference between multipole contributions suppresses the asymmetry even at moderate energies.
