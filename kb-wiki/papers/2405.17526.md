# Heavy-Ion Collisions as Probes of Nuclear Structure

> In this work, we perform a model-to-data comparison for U+U and Au+Au collisions performed at RHIC at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = $193$ GeV and $200$ GeV, using a multistage framework. Model calculations for various configurations of $^{238}$U and $^{197}$Au are used to compare with measurements of $ρ(v_n\{2\}^2,\left\langle p_T \right\rangle)$, the elliptic-flow-momentum correlator, for the first time. It is found that momentum-flow correlations measured in high-energy nuclear collisions, combined with the flow harmonics themselves, are excellent probes of nuclear deformation and of nuclear structure in general.

## Key claim

Momentum-flow correlations measured in high-energy nuclear collisions, combined with flow harmonics themselves, are excellent probes of nuclear deformation and of nuclear structure in general, but the model was never specifically tuned to match the new correlator quantity and the results are a genuine prediction posted before experimental data was made known.

## Method

The authors performed a model-to-data comparison for U+U collisions at 193 GeV and Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV using a multistage framework consisting of IP-Glasma initial conditions, relativistic viscous hydrodynamics via MUSIC, iS3D particlization, and SMASH for final-state interactions. They compared four different nuclear parametrizations (New U, Prev U, Spher Au, Def Au) against experimental measurements of elliptic flow cumulants, mean transverse momentum, and the elliptic-flow-momentum correlator across centrality classes.

## Key numbers

- U+U collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 193 GeV and Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV at RHIC
- New U Woods-Saxon parameters: R0 = 7.068 fm, a = 0.538 fm, beta_2^0 = 0.247, beta_4^0 = 0.081
- Prev U Woods-Saxon parameters: R0 = 6.874 fm, a = 0.556 fm, beta_2^0 = 0.2802, beta_4^0 = -0.0035
- Spher Au Woods-Saxon parameters: R0 = 6.37 fm, a = 0.535 fm, all deformation parameters zero
- Def Au Woods-Saxon parameters: R0 = 6.62 fm, a = 0.519 fm, beta_2^0 = 0.098, beta_2^2 = 0.076, beta_4^0 = -0.025, beta_4^2 = -0.018, beta_4^4 = -0.018
- The elliptic-flow-momentum correlator shows a marked shift from correlation to anti-correlation at around 7% centrality for U+U collisions
- Ratios using Spher Au in the denominator overshoot the experimental ratio by more than 20% across the 0-10% central window for the 2-particle pT correlator

## Context

This work extends prior model-to-data comparisons for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC using Woods-Saxon parametrizations from electron scattering experiments (osti_6477756) and newer Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations (Ryssens 2023). It provides the first theoretical calculation of the transverse-momentum-flow correlation in collisions of deformed nuclei, which was a genuine prediction posted before the experimental data from STAR (starcollaboration2024imaging) was made known. The findings enable future analyses to use this correlator as an indicator of deformation scale in new collision systems and affirm that any detailed initial state model should include careful nuclear structure sampling.
