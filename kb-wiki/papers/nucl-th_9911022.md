# Weak axial exchange currents for the Bethe-Salpeter equation

> We construct weak axial one-boson exchange currents for the Bethe-Salpeter equation, starting from chiral Lagrangians of the N-Δ(1236)-pi-rho-a_1-omega system. The currents fulfil the Ward-Takahashi identities and the matrix element of the full current between the two-body solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation satisfies the PCAC constraint exactly.

## Key claim

The paper constructs weak axial one-boson exchange currents that satisfy the Ward-Takahashi identities and the PCAC constraint exactly when evaluated between two-body solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, but only for the specific chiral Lagrangian of the N-Δ(1236)-π-ρ-a₁-ω system with pseudoscalar πNN coupling and with the nucleon Born terms omitted from the potential part of the currents.

## Method

The authors start from chiral Lagrangians of the N-Δ(1236)-π-ρ-a₁-ω system, using both the Yang-Mills approach and the hidden local symmetry approach, and derive the weak axial meson-exchange current operators for the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the tree approximation. They separate the currents into potential and non-potential parts, construct the π-range, ρ-range, and a₁-range exchange currents including Δ(1236) isobar excitation contributions, and verify that each set of currents satisfies the appropriate Ward-Takahashi identities and PCAC constraints when acting on off-shell nucleons.

## Key numbers

- Weak axial coupling constant g_A = -1.256 (nucleon axial charge)
- G₁ ≈ 2.6 (coupling constant from the NΔρ vertex, extracted from pion photoproduction data)
- a₁ meson anomalous magnetic moment δ_an = +3 in the hidden local symmetry construction of Bando et al., versus the commonly accepted value δ_an = -1 from Oset et al.

## Context

This work extends the construction of weak axial exchange currents from chiral Lagrangians (Oset et al., Ivanov and Truhlik, Truhlik and Smejkal) to the Bethe-Salpeter equation framework, where the nucleon Born terms are already accounted for by the one-nucleon current and must be omitted from the exchange currents. The approach enables consistent treatment of weak axial currents in two-body nuclear systems described by the Bethe-Salpeter equation, particularly for reactions in light nuclei such as muon capture on ³He (mentioned as reaction μ⁻ + ³He → ν_μ + ³H). The paper also addresses the internal inconsistency of the Yang-Mills approach for massive mesons and incorporates the hidden local symmetry formalism of Bando et al. and Meissner to generate meson masses via the Higgs mechanism.
