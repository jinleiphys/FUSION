# QED as a many-body theory of worldlines: II. All-order S-matrix formalism

> In arXiv:2206.04188, we developed a first-quantized worldline formalism for all-order computations of amplitudes in QED. In particular, we demonstrated in this framework an all-order proof of the infrared safety of the Faddeev-Kulish (FK) S-matrix for virtual exchanges in the scattering of charged fermions. In this work, we extend the worldline formalism for both the Dyson and FK S-matrix to consider further the emission and absorption of arbitrary numbers of photons. We show how Low's theorem follows in this framework and derive Weinberg's theorem for the exponentiation of IR divergences. In particular, we extend our all-order proof of the IR safety of the FK S-matrix to both virtual exchanges and real photon emissions. We argue that the worldline approach leads to a modern Wilsonian interpretation of the IR safety of the FK S-matrix and provides a novel template for the treatment of IR divergences in real-time problems. Using Grassmannian integration methods, we derive a simple and powerful result for N-th rank vacuum polarization tensors. Applications of these methods will be discussed in follow-up work.

## Key claim

The paper extends the worldline formalism proof of the infrared safety of the Faddeev-Kulish (FK) S-matrix from virtual exchanges only (demonstrated in Paper I) to include both virtual exchanges and real photon emissions and absorptions to all orders in perturbation theory, with the caveat that the proof applies to massive QED where collinear divergences are absent.

## Method

The authors reformulate QED as a first-quantized many-body theory of 0+1-dimensional worldlines, integrating out both fermion and gauge fields to express amplitudes as path integrals over open and closed worldline super-pairs (bosonic position and Grassmannian spin variables) for real and virtual fermions. They construct the Dyson S-matrix including arbitrary numbers of real photon emissions and absorptions by taking functional derivatives of generalized Wilson lines with respect to an external photon source, then demonstrate that Low's theorem and Weinberg's exponentiation theorem follow from factorizing classical worldline currents in the low-energy limit. The FK S-matrix is constructed by modifying worldline currents to include asymptotic interactions at finite initial and final times, which acts as an infrared regulator.

## Context

This work extends the worldline formalism developed in Paper I (Feal, Tarasov, Venugopalan, 2022) for all-order QED computations, building on foundational work by Feynman (1950), Schwinger (1951), and the modern worldline approach of Strassler (1992) and Schubert (2001). The proof of IR safety of the FK S-matrix generalizes the original argument of Faddeev and Kulish (1970) and connects to the work of Hannesdottir and Schwartz (2019) who showed IR finiteness via soft Wilson line factors. The formalism provides a Minkowski spacetime analog of Wilsonian lattice regularization, enabling future high-order computations of cusp anomalous dimensions in QED.
