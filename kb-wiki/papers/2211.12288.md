# Investigation of octupole collectivity near the $A =72$ shape-transitional point

> Enhanced octupole collectivity is expected in the neutron-deficient Ge, Se and Kr isotopes with neutron number $N \approx 40$ and has indeed been observed for $^{70,72}$Ge. Shape coexistence and configuration mixing are, however, a notorious challenge for theoretical models trying to reliably predict octupole collectivity in this mass region, which is known to feature rapid shape changes with changing nucleon number and spin of the system. To further investigate the microscopic configurations causing the prolate-oblate-triaxial shape transition at $A \approx 72$ and their influence on octupole collectivity, the rare isotopes $^{72}$Se and $^{74,76}$Kr were studied via inelastic proton scattering in inverse kinematics. While significantly enhanced octupole strength of $\sim 32$ Weisskopf units (W.u.) was observed for $^{72}$Se, only strengths of $\sim 15$ W.u. were observed for $^{74,76}$Kr. In combination with existing data, the new data clearly question a simple origin of enhanced octupole strengths around $N = 40$. The present work establishes two regions of distinct octupole strengths with a sudden strength increase around the $A=72$ shape transitional point.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that the sudden increase in octupole strength previously observed at neutron number N=40 is not a general feature of that neutron number, but is instead exclusively observed for 72Ge (Z=32). For the N=38 isotones, enhanced strength is seen in 70Ge and 72Se but not in 74Kr, and for N=40 the enhancement is absent in 74,76Kr, contradicting a simple picture of enhanced octupole correlations around the octupole magic number N=40.

## Method

Inelastic proton scattering experiments in inverse kinematics were performed at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory using secondary beams of 72Se, 74Kr, and 76Kr produced from projectile fragmentation of a 150 MeV/u 78Kr primary beam. Gamma rays from excited states were detected with the GRETINA array and Doppler-corrected event-by-event; cross sections were extracted by fitting simulated gamma-decay spectra to experimental spectra, and deformation parameters beta_lambda were obtained by scaling coupled-channels calculations (using CHUCK3 with global optical-model parameters) to the experimental cross sections.

- 76Kr: B(E3;3-_1 -> 0+_1) = 15.0 +/- 0.9 (stat.) +/- 1.8 (sys.) W.u.
- 74Kr: B(E3;3-_2,(p,p') -> 0+_1) = 4.3 +/- 1.1 (stat.) +/- 0.5 (sys.) W.u. (tentative)

## Context

This work extends studies of octupole collectivity in the neutron-deficient Ge-Kr mass region, where previous measurements had established enhanced B(E3) strengths of approximately 30 W.u. in 70,72Ge (Lec80a, Ros86a, Sch87a) but only approximately 15 W.u. in stable Se and Kr isotopes (Kib02a, Mat82a). The new data on 72Se and 74,76Kr establish two distinct regions of octupole strength separated by a sudden increase around the A=72 shape transitional point, where prolate-oblate-triaxial shape changes occur. The results challenge the IBM+IBFM calculations of Chuu (1993) that attributed enhanced strength at N=40 to collective f-boson configurations, and call for improved microscopic calculations incorporating configuration mixing and triaxial degrees of freedom, along the lines of the sdf IBM mapping approach of Nom22b, to understand the observed fragmentation pattern and the crossing of microscopic configurations suggested by the near-degenerate 3- states in 72Se.
