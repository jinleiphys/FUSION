# Global hyperon polarization and effects of decay feeddown

> We study the global polarizations of $Λ$, $Ξ^-$, and $Ω^-$ hyperons in noncentral Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=$ 7.7-200 GeV. We highlight the importance of effect of decay feeddown to the measured global polarization. With the decay contributions taken into account, the global polarization ordering $P_{Ω^-} > P_{Ξ^-} > P_Λ$ can be naturally explained, which is consistent with the observation recently reported from the STAR experiment from Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV. We also extend our calculations to predict expectations from the RHIC-BES II data.

# Global hyperon polarization and effects of decay feeddown

## Key claim
The observed ordering of global polarization \(P_{\Omega^-} > P_{\Xi^-} > P_\Lambda\) in noncentral Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV can be naturally explained only when decay feeddown contributions are included; without decay contributions, primary \(\Lambda\) and \(\Xi^-\) polarizations are nearly equal, and the ordering does not emerge.

## Method
The authors first computed the global polarization of primary \(\Lambda\), \(\Xi^-\), and \(\Omega^-\) hyperons using thermal vorticity fields and phase-space distributions extracted from a multi-phase transport (AMPT) model. They then used a statistical model (THERMUS) to calculate primary yields of hyperons and their decay products, applied spin-transfer rules to simulate decays, and combined primary and decay contributions via a weighted average to obtain inclusive global polarizations.

## Key numbers
- Primary hyperon polarizations approximately satisfy \(P_\Lambda : P_{\Xi^-} : P_{\Omega^-} \simeq 1 : 1 : 5/3\) across the energy range.
- About 80% of final \(\Lambda\) hyperons and about 40% of final \(\Xi^-\) hyperons originate from decays of heavier particles.
- Decay contributions reduce the global polarization of \(\Lambda\) by about 10% and increase the global polarization of \(\Xi^-\) by about 25%.
- Calculations cover Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} =\) 7.7–200 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the thermal vorticity framework previously applied to \(\Lambda\) polarization (STAR Collaboration, 2017; Adam et al., 2018) to the multi-strange hyperons \(\Xi^-\) and \(\Omega^-\). It directly addresses the recent STAR measurement at 200 GeV (Adam et al., 2020) which reported the polarization ordering. The results provide predictions for the RHIC Beam Energy Scan II program and motivate further experimental tests at lower collision energies.
