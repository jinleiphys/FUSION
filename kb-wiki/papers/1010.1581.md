# Coulombic effect and renormalization in nuclear pairing

> We investigate effects of the Coulomb force on the nuclear pairing properties by performing the Gogny Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations for the $N=20$, $28$, $50$, $82$ and $126$ nuclei. The Coulomb force reduces the proton pair energy and the even-odd mass difference by about 25%, except for nuclei at and around the proton shell or subshell closure. We then propose a renormalization scheme via a reduction factor $γ_p$ for the proton pairing channel. It is found that a single value of $γ_p(=0.90)$ well takes account of the Coulombic effect, for nuclei covering wide range of the mass number and the neutron excess including the nuclei around the shell or subshell closure.

## Key claim

The Coulomb force reduces proton pair energy and even-odd mass difference by about 25% in most nuclei, except those at or near proton shell or subshell closures; this effect can be captured by a single renormalization factor γ_p = 0.90 applied to the proton pairing channel, even for nuclei where the Coulombic effect appears exceptionally strong.

## Method

The authors performed spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny-D1S interaction plus the bare Coulomb force, with the Gaussian expansion method, for neutron-closed isotones at N = 20, 28, 50, 82, and 126. They compared results from the full Hamiltonian H (including the Coulomb pp channel) with those from a renormalized Hamiltonian \(\bar{H}(\gamma_p)\) that drops the Coulomb pp term and multiplies the nuclear proton pp term by a factor γ_p. The pair energy and even-odd mass difference were computed for even-Z and odd-Z nuclei respectively, and γ_p was adjusted to reproduce the full-Hamiltonian results.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb force reduces the proton pair energy and even-odd mass difference by about 25% (ratio of H to \(\bar{H}(1)\) is about 75%) for most nuclei studied.
- A single renormalization factor γ_p = 0.90 reproduces the full-Hamiltonian results to good precision across the entire range of nuclei studied.
- The difference in HFB energies between H and \(\bar{H}(0.90)\) is less than 0.1 MeV.
- The study covers mass numbers 30 ≤ A ≤ 220 and neutron excess values -0.13 ≤ (N-Z)/A ≤ 0.36.
- The nuclei studied include the isotone chains N = 20, 28, 50, 82, and 126.

## Context

This work extends earlier reports by AE01 and LD09 that proton pairing gaps are reduced by 20-30% when Coulomb repulsion is treated self-consistently, and by CG08 who proposed renormalizing the proton pair-EDF strength. The authors demonstrate that a constant renormalization factor works across a wide mass range, including at shell and subshell closures where the Coulombic effect appears exceptionally strong (e.g., ³⁴Si, ⁴²Si, ⁹⁰Zr, ¹⁴⁶Gd, ¹⁹⁰Gd, ²⁰⁸Pb, ²¹⁸U). This enables local or quasi-local energy density functional approaches to incorporate Coulombic effects on pairing without explicitly including the non-local Coulomb pp interaction.
