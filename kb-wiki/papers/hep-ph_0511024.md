# The effect of Kaon Condensation on Quark-Antiquark Condensate in Dense Matter

> Assuming that at sufficiently high densities the constituent quarks become relevant degrees of freedom, we study within the framework of a chiral quark model the influence of s-wave $K^-$ condensation on the quark-antiquark condensates. We find that, in linear density approximation, the presence of a $K^-$ condensate quenches the $\bar{u}u$ condensate, but that the $\bar{d}d$ condensate remains unaffected up to the chiral order under consideration. We discuss the implication of the suppressed $\bar{u}u$ condensate for flavor-dependent chiral symmetry restoration in dense matter

## Key claim

In a chiral quark model where constituent quarks become the relevant degrees of freedom at high density, the presence of an s-wave K⁻ condensate suppresses the \(\bar{u}u\) condensate while leaving the \(\bar{d}d\) condensate unaffected up to the chiral order considered, implying flavor-dependent chiral symmetry restoration in dense matter. This result holds within the linear density approximation and under the assumption that the constituent quarks can be treated as a free Fermi gas.

## Method

The authors employ a chiral quark model (\(\chi\)QM) Lagrangian to describe interactions of constituent quarks with Goldstone bosons, calculating the K⁻ self-energy at lowest chiral order from diagrams involving quark loops with density-dependent propagators. They compute the in-medium kaon mass by solving the dispersion equation \(m_K^{\star 2} = m_K^2 + \Sigma_K(q_0=m_K^{\star})\) using both non-relativistic and relativistic treatments of the quark propagator, and then use the Hellmann-Feynman theorem together with the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation to extract the quark-antiquark condensates from the energy density of quark matter in the presence of a postulated K⁻ condensate.

## Key numbers

* The constituent quark mass \(M_0 \approx 350\) MeV is used as the part generated by spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking.
* In non-relativistic approximation, the in-medium K⁻ mass at baryon density \(c = \rho_B/\rho_0\) is: \(m_{K^-}^\star \approx 410\) MeV at \(c=1\), 330 MeV at \(c=2\), 260 MeV at \(c=3\), and 193 MeV at \(c=4\).
* With relativistic treatment including the exchange diagram (Fig. pkc), the in-medium K⁻ mass becomes: \(m_{K^-}^\star \approx 424\) MeV at \(c=1\), 361 MeV at \(c=2\), 313 MeV at \(c=3\), and 283 MeV at \(c=4\).
* For illustrative parameters \(\mu_e = 270\) MeV, \(\rho_B = 3.3\rho_0\), \(\sigma_N = 30\) MeV, and \(\cos\theta = 0.89\), the ratio \(R \equiv \langle \bar{u}u \rangle_{\rho_B} / \langle \bar{u}u \rangle_{vac} \sim 0.35\) with kaon condensation, compared to \(R \sim 0.39\) without kaon condensation.

## Context

This work extends the study of kaon condensation from the hadronic chiral perturbation theory framework of Kaplan and Nelson (mid 1980s) and subsequent treatments by Lee et al. (1995) and others, into a chiral quark model appropriate near the chiral phase transition. The approach complements the top-down analysis based on hidden local symmetry near the vector manifestation fixed point by Harada and Yamawaki (2003) and the work of Brown and Rho (1996, 2001, 2004) on the relevance of constituent quarks near chiral restoration. The finding that kaon condensation quenches only the \(\bar{u}u\) condensate suggests that chiral symmetry restoration in dense matter may proceed in a flavor-dependent manner, which could have implications for the phase diagram of dense QCD and the structure of compact stars.
