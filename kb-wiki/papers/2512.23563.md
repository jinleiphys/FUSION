# Perturbative results for the matrix elements of the vector current and the role of different infrared regulators

> We investigate the twist-2 unpolarized generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of quarks for an on-shell gluon target in quantum chromodynamics. These GPDs parametrize the leading-twist matrix elements of the nonlocal light-like flavor-singlet vector current. We compute them at one-loop accuracy in perturbation theory using a quark mass and dimensional regularization as infrared regulators. In particular, we discuss the limit of vanishing momentum transfer. The present work extends our previous related study on the axial current.

## Key claim

The authors compute the twist-2 unpolarized generalized parton distributions (GPDs) of quarks for an on-shell gluon target at one-loop accuracy, showing that the limit of vanishing momentum transfer can only be taken when an additional infrared regulator (quark mass or dimensional regularization) is retained, contrary to claims that off-forward kinematics alone could serve as an infrared regulator.

## Method

The authors evaluate the one-loop Feynman diagram for the nonlocal light-like flavor-singlet vector current between external on-shell gluon states, using both a finite quark mass and dimensional regularization as infrared regulators. They extract the two twist-2 unpolarized quark GPDs (helicity-conserving H1 and helicity-flip H2) from the helicity amplitudes, and analyze their behavior in the forward limit (Δ² → 0) and in the limit of vanishing quark mass (τ → ∞). The calculations are performed in the symmetric frame with light-front gauge polarization vectors.

## Key numbers

- The unpolarized PDF for a gluon target at one loop is f₁^q(x;m) = (α_s/4π) (1/ε_UV - log(m²/μ̄²)) × [(1-x)² + x²] for 0 < x ≤ 1, with a discontinuity at x=0 of (α_s/2π)(1/ε_UV - log(m²/μ̄²))
- In the limit τ → 0 (finite mass, Δ² → 0), the helicity-flip GPD H₂^q vanishes as H₂^q = -(α_s/4π) × [-x(1-x)³/(3(1-ξ²)²) τ + O(τ²)] in the DGLAP region ξ ≤ x ≤ 1
- In the limit τ → ∞ (finite Δ², vanishing mass), H₁^q contains terms proportional to log(-Δ²/μ̄²), showing that Δ² acts as the IR regulator when m → 0
- Using dimensional regularization, the PDF is f₁^q(x;ε_IR) = (α_s/4π) × 1/(1-2ε) × (1/ε_UV - 1/ε_IR) × [(1-x)² + x²] for 0 < x ≤ 1

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study on the axial current (Castelli 2024) to the vector current case, confirming that the additional GPD found in off-forward kinematics by Bhattacharya (2022, 2023) and Tarasov (2020, 2021) vanishes in the forward limit when a proper infrared regulator is retained, consistent with angular momentum conservation. The results agree with the matching coefficients for generalized transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions presented by Bertone (2025). The findings enable consistent treatment of the forward limit in factorization theorems for deeply virtual Compton scattering and deep-inelastic scattering, resolving potential contradictions raised in earlier work about singularities in the Δ² → 0 limit.
