# Nuclear and Neutron Star Radii

> We investigate the correlation between nuclear neutron radii and the radius of neutron stars. We use a well-established hadronic SU(3) model based on chiral symmetry that naturally includes non-linear vector meson and scalar meson - vector meson couplings. The relative strengths of the couplings modify the nuclear isospin-dependent interactions. We study the dependence of nuclear and neutron star radii on the coupling strengths. The relevance of the results for parity-violating electron-nucleus scattering and the URCA process in neutron stars is discussed.

## Key claim

The correlation between the neutron skin thickness of \(^{208}\)Pb and the radius of a 1.4 solar-mass neutron star is sensitive to two distinct coupling parameters, but a 1% measurement of the lead neutron radius alone is likely not stringent enough to uniquely determine the isospin-sector parameters of the nuclear force.

## Method

The author uses a chiral SU(3) \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\) mean-field model that includes nonlinear vector-meson self-interactions and scalar-vector meson couplings. The relative strength of the scalar-vector coupling is varied via the parameter \(r_\sigma\), and the nonlinear vector coupling via \(\beta\). Nuclear ground-state properties of \(^{208}\)Pb and \(^{138}\)Ba are computed in spherical symmetry, and neutron-star radii are obtained by integrating the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations for the equation of state generated from the same Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

* Neutron-proton radius difference in \(^{208}\)Pb: best-fit estimate \(\Delta r_{np} = 0.25\) fm, with a significant dependence on \(\beta\) over the range \(-1 < \beta < +1.4\).
* Compressibility of symmetric nuclear matter: between about 205 and 225 MeV for all parameter sets studied.
* Saturation density: \(\rho_0 \sim 0.15\) GeV/fm\(^3\) for all studied parameter sets.
* Neutron star radius for a 1.4 solar-mass star with the best-fit parameter set \(\chi_M\): \(R_{\text{hyper}} = 11.42\) km (with hyperons) and \(R_{\text{nohyper}} = 12.46\) km (without hyperons).
* Variation of neutron star radius over the full range of \(\beta\) that yields reasonable nuclei: about 5.5 km; restricting \(\beta > 0\) based on asymmetry energy limits the variation to about 1 km.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Horowitz and Piekarewicz (2001) and Horowitz and Piekarewicz (2002), who in a Walecka-type model investigated the sensitivity of nuclear and neutron-star radii to isovector-vector meson couplings. The results are relevant for the planned parity-violating electron scattering measurement of the neutron radius of \(^{208}\)Pb at Jefferson Laboratory and for the URCA process in neutron-star cooling. The study shows that while a 1% measurement of the lead neutron radius covers most of the model parameter space, disentangling the effects of different couplings will require neutron-star radius measurements with accuracy well below 1 km, and also a better understanding of hyperon behavior in dense matter.
