# Impact of error analysis on the composition the outer crust of a neutron star

> By means of bootstrap technique, we perform a full error analysis on the Duflo-Zucker mass model. We illustrate the impact of such study on the predicted chemical composition of the outer crust of a non-accreting neutron star. We define an existence probability for each nuclear species as a function of the depth of the crust. We observe that, due to statistical uncertainties, instead of having a well defined transition between two successive layers, we have a mixture of two species.

# Impact of error analysis on the composition of the outer crust of a neutron star

## Key claim
Statistical uncertainties in the Duflo-Zucker mass model parameters, when propagated through the calculation of the outer crust composition, cause the transition between successive nuclear species layers to be not sharp but instead produce a mixture of two coexisting species at each depth. The authors define an existence probability for each nucleus as a function of pressure, showing that at the outer-inner crust boundary, ¹²⁰Sr and ¹¹⁸Zr appear with roughly equal probability.

## Method
The authors performed a full fit of the Duflo-Zucker 10-parameter (DZ10) mass model to the 2012 atomic mass evaluation using a non-parametric bootstrap (NPB) technique to estimate parameter uncertainties and the covariance matrix. They then generated 10⁴ synthetic nuclear mass tables by parametric bootstrap, sampling a Gaussian distribution for each mass with the DZ10 predicted binding energy as mean and the propagated theoretical error as variance, and applied the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland (BPS) minimisation procedure to determine the chemical composition of the outer crust for each table.

## Key numbers
- 2293 nuclei were included in the fit, excluding nuclei with A < 14 and those with experimental errors larger than 100 keV.
- The parameter errors from the NPB analysis are small: for example, a₁ = 0.70454 ± 0.00037 MeV, a₂ = 17.740 ± 0.0068 MeV, a₃ = 16.242 ± 0.023 MeV.
- 10⁴ Monte Carlo mass tables were generated for the parametric bootstrap procedure.
- The statistical error analysis becomes relevant for pressures greater than P ≈ 10⁻⁴ MeV fm⁻³, where the model predicts nuclei not yet measured experimentally.
- At the outer-inner crust boundary, ¹²⁰Sr and ¹¹⁸Zr each appear with roughly 50% existence probability.

## Context
This work extends the BPS method (Baym, Pethick and Sutherland, 1971) for determining the ground-state composition of the outer crust by incorporating statistical error propagation from the DZ10 mass model. It builds on previous studies of the outer crust composition (Ruster et al., 2006; Roca-Maza et al., 2008; Pearson et al., 2011) and on the NPB technique applied to simpler liquid drop models (Pastore et al., 2019). The results enable future work to define the equation of state of the outer crust in terms of confidence intervals rather than a single sharp line, and highlight the need for either more accurate mass models or experimental measurements of neutron-rich nuclei to reduce the uncertainty.
