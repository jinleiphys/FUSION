# Vector meson radiation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The sigma-omega model in mean-field approximation where the meson fields are treated classically, describes much of observed nuclear structure and has been employed to describe the nuclear equation of state up to the quark-gluon phase transition. The acceleration of the meson sources, for example, in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, should result in bremsstrahlung-like radiation of the meson fields. The many mesons emitted serve to justify the use of classical meson fields. The slowing of the nuclei during the collision is modeled here as a smooth transition from initial to final velocity. Under ultra-relativistic conditions, vector radiation dominates. The angular distribution of energy flux shows a characteristic shape. It appears that if the vector meson field couples to the conserved baryon current, independent of the baryonic degrees of freedom, this mechanism will contribute to the radiation seen in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The possible influence of the quark-gluon plasma is also considered.

## Key claim

The sigma-omega model in mean-field approximation predicts that vector meson bremsstrahlung from decelerating baryon currents in relativistic heavy-ion collisions could contribute significantly to the total radiated energy, but only if the vector meson field couples to the conserved baryon current independent of the baryonic degrees of freedom, and the results should be considered a rough guide to order of magnitude rather than precise predictions.

## Method

The author modeled the deceleration of two identical heavy nuclei in central collisions as a smooth Fermi-type transition from initial to final velocity, treating the baryon current as a collection of point particles radiating incoherently. The vector meson field was solved using the retarded Green's function for the Klein-Gordon equation, with the meson mass neglected under the assumption that most radiation occurs at energies above the omega meson mass of 0.783 GeV. An attenuation factor was included to account for strong interactions between radiated vector mesons and baryons, evaluated in the rest frame of the radiating nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy per nucleon: \(E_{\rm beam}/A = 100\) GeV (RHIC energy for Pb+Pb collisions)
- Rapidity loss: \(\delta y = 2.4 \pm 0.2\) for central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, taken from Mishustin et al. (1995)
- Stopping parameter \(\tau\) varied between 5 and 20 fm
- Vector meson-nucleon total cross-section \(\sigma\) varied between 20 and 40 mb
- Coupling constant used: \(g_v^2/4\pi = 10.8\) (from W95)
- For intermediate parameters (\(\tau = 10\) fm, \(\sigma = 30\) mb, \(\delta y = 2.4\), \(A = 208\) for Pb): total radiated energy \(E_{\rm rad} = 353\) GeV out of \(3.78 \times 10^4\) GeV total available energy
- For \(\tau = 5\) fm: \(E_{\rm rad} = 730\) GeV
- For \(\delta y = 2.2\) and \(\tau = 10\) fm: \(E_{\rm rad} = 516\) GeV out of \(3.70 \times 10^4\) GeV total available energy
- Peak of energy flux occurs at approximately \(2.5^\circ\) off the beam axis
- Vector meson mass cutoff at \(m_\omega = 0.783\) GeV reduces integrated spectrum by less than 1%

## Context

This work extends the sigma-omega model, previously successful for low and intermediate energy nuclear phenomena (W95, SW86), to ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies. It builds on the suggestion by Mishustin et al. (1995) that real omega mesons should be produced via bremsstrahlung in such collisions, and contrasts with Weber et al. (1990) who found meson radiation negligible (less than 3 MeV/nucleon) at beam energies of 1-20 GeV/A. The model enables future experimental searches for a characteristic angular distribution of vector meson radiation that is robust against parameter variation, and suggests that even if a quark-gluon plasma forms, meson-like modes may still propagate through it (Ha85), potentially leaving a detectable signal.
