# A comprehensive treatment of electromagnetic interactions and the three-body spectator equations

> We present a general derivation the three-body spectator (Gross) equations and the corresponding electromagnetic currents. As in previous paper on two-body systems, the wave equations and currents are derived from those for Bethe-Salpeter equation with the help of algebraic method using a concise matrix notation. The three-body interactions and currents introduced by the transition to the spectator approach are isolated and the matrix elements of the e.m. current are presented in detail for system of three indistinguishable particles, namely for elastic scattering and for two and three body break-up. The general expressions are reduced to the one-boson-exchange approximation to make contact with previous work. The method is general in that it does not rely on introduction of the electromagnetic interaction with the help of the minimal replacement. It would therefore work also for other external fields.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general derivation of the three-body spectator (Gross) equations and corresponding electromagnetic currents that does not rely on the minimal substitution for introducing electromagnetic interactions, and therefore works for other external fields as well; the derivation assumes no explicit three-body forces and implicitly uses the one-boson-exchange approximation for the interaction kernels.

## Method

The authors start from the three-body Bethe-Salpeter equation and its Faddeev decomposition, then apply an algebraic operator method that separates the free three-body propagator into an on-shell positive-energy part and a remainder, analogous to their previous two-body treatment. The three-body spectator equations for the t-matrix and wave functions are obtained by constraining two of the three identical particles to their positive-energy mass shells, and the consistent electromagnetic current operator is derived using the same operator algebra. The general expressions are then reduced to the one-boson-exchange approximation to connect with prior work by Gross, Stadler, and Peña (2004) and by Kvinikhidze and Blankleider (1997-1999).

## Key numbers

- The paper presents no original numerical results; it is a formal derivation of equations and current operators.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous algebraic derivation of two-body spectator equations and currents (Adam and Van Orden, 2004) to the three-body case. It provides a general framework that does not rely on minimal substitution, enabling application to other external fields beyond electromagnetism. The results are shown to be identical to those of Gross, Stadler, and Peña (2004) and, by extension, to those of Kvinikhidze and Blankleider (1997-1999) when reduced to the one-boson-exchange approximation, and are intended for future calculations of electromagnetic properties of relativistic three-nucleon systems.
