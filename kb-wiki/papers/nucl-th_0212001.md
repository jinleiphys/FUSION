# Production Mechanism for Quark Gluon Plasma in Heavy Ion Collisions

> A general scheme is proposed here to describe the production of semi soft and soft quarks and gluons that form the bulk of the plasma in ultra relativistic heavy ion collisions. We show how to obtain rates as a function of time in a self consistent manner, without any ad-hoc assumption. All the required features - the dynamical nature of QCD vacuum, the non-Markovian nature of the production, and quasi particle nature of the partons, and the importance of quantum interference effects are naturally incorporated. We illustrate the results with a realistic albeit toy model and show how almost all the currently employed source terms are unreliable in their predictions. We show the rates in the momentum space and indicate at the end how to extract the full phase-space dependence.

# Production Mechanism for Quark Gluon Plasma in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The paper proposes a general scheme for deriving source terms for semi-hard and soft quark and gluon production in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions that incorporates the dynamical nature of the QCD vacuum, non-Markovian production rates, and quasi-particle nature of partons, but only for specific "abelian configurations" of the background chromoelectric field, and only in leading order of the expansion parameter \( g \mathcal{E}_0 \).

## Method
The authors expand the Yang-Mills gauge potential into a classical background field \( C_\mu^a \) and fluctuations \( \phi_\mu^a \), then compute production amplitudes via time-dependent Schwinger-Dyson evolution of the vacuum state in Fock space, rather than from an S-matrix. The production rate at time \( t \) is obtained as the time derivative of the squared projection of the evolved state onto two-gluon or quark-antiquark states, using incomplete Fourier transforms of the background field. A toy model with an exponentially decaying chromoelectric field of the form \( \mathcal{E}^a_i = \delta_{i,z} \mathcal{E}_0 (\delta_{a,3} + \delta_{a,8}) \exp\{ (|z| - t)/t_0 \} \theta(t) \theta(t^2 - z^2) \) is used to illustrate the results.

## Key numbers
- The production rate oscillates between approximately (-500, 500) in scaled units at center-of-mass energy \( \sqrt{s} = 0.6 \) and transverse momentum \( p_T = 0.1 \) (in units of \( t_0 \), \( \mathcal{E}_0^2 \), and \( g^2 \)).
- When \( p_T \) is increased to 0.2 at the same \( \sqrt{s} = 0.6 \), the oscillation range is restricted to approximately (-100, 100).
- The toy model field is characterized by a single time scale \( t_0 \), with the relation \( g \, \mathcal{U} \, t_0^4 \sim 1 \) connecting the coupling, initial energy density \( \mathcal{U} \), and production time scale.
- Lattice computations cited indicate that even at twice the transition temperature \( T_C \), quarks and gluons do not behave as ideal gases.

## Context
This work extends the flux tube / colour rope model of particle production in heavy ion collisions, building on the Bjorken (1983) boost-invariant picture and the Schwinger mechanism for particle production from static fields. It directly addresses limitations in prior approaches: Bloch et al. (1999) and Schmidt et al. (1999) introduced non-Markovian effects but used the Schwinger mechanism valid only for static fields; Bhalerao and Ravishankar (1997) incorporated time-dependent fields but ignored non-Markovian aspects and colour dynamics. The scheme enables derivation of full phase-space-dependent source terms (including colour degrees of freedom) for use in transport equations describing QGP evolution, with the colour structure showing that gluons are produced predominantly in directions normal to each other in colour space while quark pairs prefer antiparallel directions.
