# Isospin-symmetry violation -- kaons and beyond (ISO-BREAK 25: summary and outlook)

> This report summarizes the presentations and discussions during the ISO-BREAK 25 Workshop ``Isospin symmetry violation: kaons and beyond'', which was held at Jan Kochanowski University in Kielce on October 23-25, 2025. We address the current status of the isospin-symmetry breaking discovered by NA61/SHINE in nucleus-nucleus collisions at the CERN SPS, its confirmation by other experiments and studies in \ee and deep inelastic scattering. In addition, we discuss the theoretical status as well as we outline experimental and theoretical priorities towards understanding this currently unexplained phenomenon.

## Key claim

The workshop report summarizes the current status of an unexplained charge-symmetry violation (CSV) in kaon production, where the charged-to-neutral kaon ratio $R_K$ deviates from unity beyond what Standard Model expectations can account for. The authors present three possible origins: the experimental data are incorrect, the models are incorrect, or both are incorrect, leaving open the possibility that physics beyond the Standard Model may be required.

## Method

The paper is a workshop summary report from ISO-BREAK 25, held at Jan Kochanowski University in Kielce on October 23-25, 2025. It compiles experimental results from NA61/SHINE (nucleus-nucleus collisions at CERN SPS), BESIII (e+e- collisions at 3.0-3.7 GeV), Jefferson Lab (deep inelastic scattering of 10.2 and 10.6 GeV electrons on proton and deuteron targets), HADES, FOPI, KaoS, STAR, and ALICE, alongside theoretical discussions of possible mechanisms including effective charge-to-baryon ratio effects, electromagnetic fields, finite-density effects, u-d quark mass differences, chiral anomaly contributions, and disoriented chiral condensates.

## Key numbers

* $R_K = 1.184 \pm 0.061$ for central Ar+Sc collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 11.9$ GeV reported by NA61/SHINE, quantifying the charged-to-neutral kaon yield ratio
* $R_K$ decreases with increasing transverse momentum, reaching about 1.3 at $p_T$ of several hundred MeV/$c$ and being consistent with unity at $p_T \approx 1.4$ GeV/$c$ in Ar+Sc collisions at 11.9 GeV
* In e+e- collisions at 3.050 and 3.671 GeV, charged kaon yields are on average about 1.5 times higher than those of $K^0_S$ in the measured momentum range 0.2-1.5 GeV/$c$
* HADES reports $R_C = 0.624 \pm 0.007$ (stat) $\pm 0.090$ (syst) for $\pi^-$+$^{12}$C at $\sqrt{s} = 2.02$ GeV and $R_W = 0.738 \pm 0.005$ (stat) $\pm 0.130$ (syst) for $\pi^-$+$^{184}$W at the same energy
* The relative difference between $u\overline{u}$ and $d\overline{d}$ chiral condensates amounts to $0.02\%$ from fits to experimental masses and decays of light mesons
* ALICE measurements at LHC energies show $R_K$ consistent with unity within about 10% uncertainties in pp, p+Pb, and Pb+Pb collisions

## Context

This workshop report extends the unexpected discovery by NA61/SHINE (2023) of charge-symmetry violation in kaon production in Ar+Sc collisions, which was soon followed by indications of CSV in e+e- collisions by BESIII (2025) and in deep inelastic scattering by Jefferson Lab (Bhatt et al., 2024). The report compiles world data showing $R_K$ ranging from approximately 1 to approximately 1.5 in e+e- interactions, with a world average of about 1.2. The findings challenge existing phenomenological approaches inspired by the Standard Model (Brylinski et al., 2023) and motivate a systematic experimental survey across reaction systems, collision centrality, and energies, as well as improved theoretical modeling including lattice QCD calculations. A working group with representatives from HADES, NA61/SHINE, and ALICE has been established to coordinate efforts on systematic uncertainty estimation.
