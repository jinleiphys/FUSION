# Particle-drip lines from the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory with Skyrme interaction

> We calculate positions of one- and two-particle, proton and neutron drip lines within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory using Skyrme interaction. We also determine an approximate $r$-process path defined as a line where the neutron binding energy is equal to 2~MeV. A weakening of the nuclear shell structure at drip lines is found and interpreted as resulting from a coupling with continuum states.

## Key claim

The Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory with the SkP Skyrme interaction, solved in a spherical approximation, predicts a weakening of nuclear shell structure at both the neutron and proton drip lines, which is interpreted as resulting from coupling with continuum states; the one-neutron-drip-line condition is approximated by the equation λ + min_μ E_μ(λ) = 0, which avoids explicit calculations for odd nuclei.

## Method

The authors solved the spherical HFB equations in coordinate space using the SkP Skyrme force parameterization, which was previously fitted to properties of magic nuclei and the Z=50 isotopic chain. The one-neutron-drip line was determined by solving HFB equations under the condition λ + min_μ E_μ(λ) = 0, derived from a first-order Taylor expansion and an approximation for odd-nucleus ground-state energies using the minimum quasiparticle energy. The two-neutron-drip line was determined by solving HFB equations with the condition λ = 0, corresponding to omitting the neutron-number constraint. Neutron-drip lines were calculated for even Z between 8 and 124, and proton-drip lines for even N between 8 and 210.

## Key numbers

- One-neutron-drip line positions were determined with a precision of ±2 mass units.
- Neutron-drip lines were calculated for even Z from 8 to 124.
- Proton-drip lines were calculated for even N from 8 to 210.
- The approximate r-process path was defined as the line where the neutron binding energy equals 2 MeV.
- The approximate r-process path passes through ¹⁹⁵₆₉Tm₁₂₆ at N=126.
- The approximate r-process path misses the nuclides ⁸⁰₃₀Zn₅₀ and ¹³⁰₄₈Cd₈₂ by a few mass units.
- Theoretical predictions for the neutron-drip line at Z=50 may differ by as much as 12 mass units between different approaches.

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent mean-field calculations of drip lines by using the full HFB method, which correctly treats the coupling between bound states and the continuum, unlike the BCS approximation that can produce an unphysical particle gas. The results contradict the persistence of strong shell effects at drip lines that might be expected from extrapolations of known nuclear structure, and instead support suggestions by Pethick and Ravenhall (1991) that spin-orbit splitting may be reduced at large neutron excess. The calculations enable future dynamic r-process reaction network calculations using HFB masses, as called for by Rolfs and Rodney (1988), and provide a benchmark for comparing with relativistic Hartree theory calculations by Hirata et al. (1991) and extended Thomas-Fermi calculations by Aboussir et al. (1992).
