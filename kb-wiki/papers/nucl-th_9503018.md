# Long-range behavior of the optical potential for the elastic scattering of charged composite particles

> The asymptotic behavior of the optical potential, describing elastic scattering of a charged particle $α$ off a bound state of two charged, or one charged and one neutral, particles at small momentum transfer $Δ_α$ or equivalently at large intercluster distance $ρ_α$, is investigated within the framework of the exact three-body theory. For the three-charged-particle Green function that occurs in the exact expression for the optical potential, a recently derived expression, which is appropriate for the asymptotic region under consideration, is used. We find that for arbitrary values of the energy parameter the non-static part of the optical potential behaves for $Δ_α \rightarrow 0$ as $C_{1}Δ_α + o\,(Δ_α)$. From this we derive for the Fourier transform of its on-shell restriction for $ρ_α \rightarrow \infty$ the behavior $-a/2ρ_α^4 + o\,(1/ρ_α^4)$, i.e., dipole or quadrupole terms do not occur in the coordinate-space asymptotics. This result corroborates the standard one, which is obtained by perturbative methods. The general, energy-dependent expression for the dynamic polarisability $C_{1}$ is derived; on the energy shell it reduces to the conventional polarisability $a$ which is independent of the energy. We emphasize that the present derivation is {\em non-perturbative}, i.e., it does not make use of adiabatic or similar approximations, and is valid for energies {\em below as well as above the three-body dissociation threshold}.

## Key claim

The non-static part of the optical potential for elastic scattering of a charged particle off a bound state of two charged (or one charged and one neutral) particles behaves as \(C_{1}\Delta_{\alpha} + o(\Delta_{\alpha})\) for momentum transfer \(\Delta_{\alpha} \rightarrow 0\), and its on-shell Fourier transform yields \(-a/2\rho_{\alpha}^{4} + o(1/\rho_{\alpha}^{4})\) in coordinate space, with no dipole or quadrupole terms. This result is derived non-perturbatively and is valid for energies both below and above the three-body dissociation threshold, unlike previous derivations which were restricted to energies below threshold.

## Method

The authors use the exact three-body theory in momentum space, employing the Feshbach projection operator technique to derive the optical potential for elastic scattering. They analyze the asymptotic behavior of the three-charged-particle Green function in the region where the intercluster distance \(\rho_{\alpha}\) is large and the internal coordinate \(r_{\alpha}\) is small compared to \(\rho_{\alpha}\), using a recently derived asymptotic expression for the three-body wave function in the continuum. The leading singular behavior of the optical potential for small momentum transfer is extracted from the second-order term involving the full three-body Green function, without resorting to perturbative or adiabatic approximations.

## Key numbers

- The non-static part of the optical potential behaves as \(C_{1}\Delta_{\alpha} + o(\Delta_{\alpha})\) for \(\Delta_{\alpha} \rightarrow 0\), where \(C_{1}\) is the energy-dependent dynamic polarisability.
- The on-shell Fourier transform in coordinate space yields \(-a/2\rho_{\alpha}^{4} + o(1/\rho_{\alpha}^{4})\) for \(\rho_{\alpha} \rightarrow \infty\), where \(a\) is the conventional energy-independent polarisability.
- The static potential term behaves as \(4\pi e_{\alpha}(e_{\beta}+e_{\gamma})/\Delta_{\alpha}^{2}\) for \(\Delta_{\alpha} \rightarrow 0\), with additional terms \(\sim \Delta_{\alpha}^{-1}\) and \(\sim \ln \Delta_{\alpha}\) possible for non-spherically-symmetric target states.

## Context

This work extends non-perturbative derivations of the long-range optical potential behavior, previously limited to energies below the three-body dissociation threshold by K. M. (1988) and K. S. Z. (1985, 1987, 1989), to energies both below and above that threshold. The result corroborates the standard perturbative result obtained by M. W. (1959) and S. S. (1977) for energies below threshold, and by U. C. (1989) for energies above threshold. The derivation enables rigorous construction of model optical potentials for practical applications and provides a foundation for determining appropriate effective-range expansions and convergence rates of partial-wave sums.
