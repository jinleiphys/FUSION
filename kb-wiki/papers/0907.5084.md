# Vacuum phenomenology of the chiral partner of the nucleon in a linear sigma model with vector mesons

> We investigate a linear sigma model with global chiral $U(2)_{R} \times U(2)_{L}$ symmetry. The mesonic degrees of freedom are the standard scalar and pseudoscalar mesons and the vector and axial-vector mesons. The baryonic degrees of freedom are the nucleon, $N$, and its chiral partner, $N^{*}$, which is usually identified with N(1535). The chiral partner is incorporated in the so-called mirror assignment, where the nucleon mass is not solely generated by the chiral condensate but also by a chirally invariant mass term, $m_{0}$. The presence of (axial-) vector fields modifies the expressions for the axial coupling constants of the nucleon, $g_{A}^{N}$, and its partner, $g_{A}^{N^{*}}$. Using experimental data for the decays $N^{*} \to N π$ and $a_{1} \toπγ$, as well as lattice results for $g_{A}^{N^{*}}$ we infer $m_{0}\sim500$ MeV, i.e., an appreciable amount of the nucleon mass originates from sources other than the chiral condensate. We test our model by evaluating the decay $N^{*} \to N η$ and the s-wave nucleon-pion scattering lengths $a_{0}^{(\pm)}$.

## Key claim
The nucleon mass receives an appreciable contribution (m₀ ~ 500 MeV) from sources other than the chiral condensate, such as the gluon condensate, but this does not invalidate the Ioffe formula because the nucleon mass would only drop to ~850 MeV if m₀ were switched off.

## Method
The authors constructed a linear sigma model with global chiral U(2)ₓ × U(2)ₗ symmetry, including scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector mesons, plus nucleon and its chiral partner N* in the mirror assignment. They determined the four parameters c₁, c₂, Z, and m₀ by fitting to experimental data for Γ(N* → Nπ), Γ(a₁ → πγ), and gₐ^N, plus lattice results for gₐ^N*, using a χ² procedure. They then tested the model by computing the decay N* → Nη and the s-wave πN scattering lengths a₀^(±).

## Key numbers
- m₀ = (460 ± 136) MeV, the chirally invariant mass term contributing to the nucleon mass
- gₐ^N = 1.267 ± 0.004 (experimental input), gₐ^N* = 0.2 ± 0.3 (lattice input from Takahashi)
- Γ(N* → Nπ)^exp = (67.5 ± 23.6) MeV, used as input for N(1535) as chiral partner
- Γ(a₁ → πγ)^exp = (0.640 ± 0.246) MeV, used as input
- a₀^(−) = (6.04 ± 0.63) × 10⁻⁴ MeV⁻¹, compared to experimental (6.4 ± 0.1) × 10⁻⁴ MeV⁻¹
- Γ(N* → Nη) = (10.9 ± 3.8) MeV for N(1535), compared to experimental (78.7 ± 24.3) MeV
- For local chiral symmetry case: m₀ = (730 ± 229) MeV, gₐ^N = −gₐ^N* = 0.33 ± 0.02

## Context
This work extends the mirror-assignment approach of DeTar and Kunihiro (1989) and Jido et al. (2000) by including vector and axial-vector mesons, which allows independent adjustment of the axial charges gₐ^N and gₐ^N*. It contrasts with the naive assignment where N* → Nπ decay vanishes. The results enable future studies of the nucleon mass at finite temperature and density, and suggest that an enlarged mixing scenario (e.g., including N(1440) and N(1650)) or a dilatation-invariant mass term coupling to tetraquark and gluon condensates may resolve the too-small N(1535) → Nη width.
