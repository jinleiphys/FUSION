# The Landau Pole at Finite Temperature

> We study the Landau pole in the lambda phi^4 field theory at non-zero and large temperatures. We show that the position of the thermal Landau pole Lambda_L(T) is shifted to higher energies with respect to the zero temperature Landau pole Lambda_L(0). We find for high temperatures T > Lambda_L(0), Lambda_L(T) simeq pi^2 T / log (T / Lambda_L(0)). Therefore, the range of applicability in energy of the lambda phi^4 field theory increases with the temperature.

## Key claim

The position of the thermal Landau pole \(\Lambda_L(T)\) in massless \(\lambda\phi^4\) theory is shifted to higher energies relative to the zero-temperature pole \(\Lambda_L(0)\) for all temperatures, with the effect becoming substantial at high temperatures \(T > \Lambda_L(0)\) where the pole grows approximately as \(\pi^2 T / \log(T / \Lambda_L(0))\).

## Method

The authors study the one-loop resummed running coupling constant \(\lambda(q;T)\) defined from the four-point vertex at the static symmetric point in the imaginary-time formalism, using a zero-temperature \(\mu\)-momentum subtraction renormalization scheme. They analytically compute the renormalized bubble diagram \(\Pi(s,q;T)\) for critical (massless) \(\phi^4\) theory and solve the transcendental equation for the thermal Landau pole \(\Lambda_L(T)\) in the low-temperature (\(T \ll \Lambda_0\)) and high-temperature (\(T \gtrsim \Lambda_0\)) limits using asymptotic expansions of the Gamma function.

## Key numbers

- Zero-temperature Landau pole: \(\Lambda_L(0) \equiv \Lambda_0 = \mu \, e^{16\pi^2/[3\lambda]}\), where \(\mu\) is the renormalization scale and \(\lambda\) the renormalized coupling at scale \(\mu\).
- For low temperatures \(T \ll \Lambda_0\): \(\Lambda_L(T) = \Lambda_0 + \frac{4\pi^2}{3\Lambda_0} T^2 + \mathcal{O}(T^3/\Lambda_0^2)\).
- For high temperatures \(T \gtrsim \Lambda_0\): \(\Lambda_L(T) = \frac{\pi^2 T}{\ln(4\pi e T / \Lambda_0) - \gamma} + \mathcal{O}(\Lambda_0/T)\), where \(\gamma = 0.5772157\ldots\) is the Euler-Mascheroni constant.
- For \(\lambda = 1\) and \(T = \Lambda_0\), the high-temperature formula reproduces the exact numerical solution with better than 1% accuracy.
- For soft momenta \(q \ll T\): \(\lambda(q,T) = \frac{16}{3} \frac{q}{T} + O(q^2/T^2 \log(T/\mu))\).

## Context

This work extends the classic analysis of the Landau pole in \(\lambda\phi^4\) theory (Landau, 1950s) to finite temperature, a problem the authors state had not been previously studied in the literature. The results are relevant for effective field theories such as the standard model (where the zero-temperature Landau pole provides a triviality bound, as discussed by Callaway, 1988) and the \(O(4)\) linear sigma model; the finding that the Landau pole moves to higher energies with increasing temperature implies that the range of validity of these theories as effective descriptions expands at high temperature. The authors note that analogous behavior appears to hold in spinor electrodynamics (work in progress) and that the results generalize to \(O(N)\) models through a rescaling of the coupling constant.
