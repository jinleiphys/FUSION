# Interplay among the azimuthally dependent HBT radii and the elliptic flow

> We present a calculation of the elliptic flow and azimuthal dependence of the correlation radii in the ellipsoidally symmetric generalization of the Buda-Lund model. The elliptic flow is shown to depend only on the flow anisotropy while in case of correlation radii both flow and space anisotropy play an important role in determining their azimuthal oscillation. We also outline a simple procedure for determining the parameters of the model from data.

## Key claim

The elliptic flow in the Buda-Lund model depends only on flow anisotropy (the difference between in-plane and out-of-plane expansion rates), while the azimuthal oscillation of HBT correlation radii depends on both flow anisotropy and spatial anisotropy of the fireball; this contrasts with blast-wave models where spatial anisotropy also influences elliptic flow.

## Method

The authors derived analytic expressions for elliptic flow and azimuthally dependent HBT radii within the ellipsoidally symmetric generalization of the Buda-Lund hydro model, using a Gaussian saddle-point approximation to the emission function at midrapidity. They then performed a simultaneous qualitative comparison of the full model formulas to PHENIX single-particle spectra, STAR elliptic flow data, and STAR azimuthally sensitive HBT radii from 20-30% central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Effective slope parameters extracted from PHENIX data for 20-30% central Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: pi+ Teff = 210.2 +/- 0.8 MeV, K+ Teff = 284.4 +/- 2.2 MeV, p Teff = 414.8 +/- 7.5 MeV, pi- Teff = 211.9 +/- 0.7 MeV, K- Teff = 278.1 +/- 2.2 MeV, anti-p Teff = 434.7 +/- 8.6 MeV
- Best-fit model parameters for simultaneous description: T0 = 163 MeV, rho0 = 0.61, rho2 = 0.17, X^2 = 33.5 fm^2, Y^2 = 33.9 fm^2, a^2 = 0.16
- The scaled oscillation amplitude R_{s,2}^2/R_{s,0}^2 is shown as contours for pair momenta K_t = 0.2 GeV and 0.5 GeV in Figures 2 and 3
- Contours of constant v2 for pions are shown at transverse momenta 0.5 GeV/c and 1.0 GeV/c in Figure 1

## Context

This work extends the Buda-Lund hydro model (Csorgo 1995, Csanad 2004) to describe azimuthally asymmetric collisions, building on exact hydrodynamic solutions (Csorgo 2001, 2003, Sinyukov 2004). It contrasts with the azimuthally sensitive blast-wave model of Retiere (2004) and Tomasik (2005), where elliptic flow depends on both spatial and flow anisotropy, and with numerical hydrodynamic solutions (Broniowski 2008). The results enable a procedure to disentangle spatial and flow anisotropy from combined measurements of elliptic flow and azimuthally dependent HBT radii, though the authors note that a simple sequential extraction strategy fails when temperature inhomogeneity parameter a is nonzero.
