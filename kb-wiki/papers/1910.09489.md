# Glauber Monte Carlo predictions for ultra-relativistic collisions with ${}^{16}{\rm O}$

> We explore Glauber Monte Carlo predictions for the planned ultra-relativistic ${}^{16}{\rm O}$+${}^{16}{\rm O}$ and p+${}^{16}{\rm O}$ collisions, as well as for collisions of ${}^{16}{\rm O}$ on heavy targets. In particular, we present specific collective flow measures which are approximately independent on the hydrodynamic response of the system, such as the ratios of eccentricities obtained from cumulants with different numbers of particles, or correlations of ellipticity and triangularity described by the normalized symmetric cumulants. We use the state-of-the-art correlated nuclear distributions for ${}^{16}{\rm O}$ and compare the results to the uncorrelated case, finding moderate effects for the most central collisions. We also consider the wounded quark model, which turns out to yield similar results to the wounded nucleon model for the considered measures. The purpose of our study is to prepare some ground for the upcoming experimental proposals, as well as to provide input for possible more detailed dynamical studies with hydrodynamics or transport codes.

## Key claim

The paper provides Glauber Monte Carlo predictions for flow observables in ultra-relativistic collisions with ${}^{16}{\rm O}$ beams, but the authors explicitly limit their study to initial-state eccentricities and measures that are approximately independent of the hydrodynamic or transport response, rather than making direct predictions for final-state flow coefficients.

## Method

The authors used the GLISSANDO 3 code to perform Glauber Monte Carlo simulations of ${}^{16}{\rm O}+{}^{16}{\rm O}$, p+${}^{16}{\rm O}$, and ${}^{16}{\rm O}$ on heavy targets, employing 6000 configurations from cluster Variational Monte Carlo (CVMC) simulations with the Argonne v18 two-nucleon and Urbana X three-nucleon potentials to obtain realistic correlated nuclear distributions for ${}^{16}{\rm O}$. They computed eccentricities from two- and four-particle cumulants, double eccentricity ratios, and normalized symmetric cumulants, comparing results between correlated and uncorrelated nuclear distributions, and between the wounded nucleon model and the wounded quark model with three constituents.

## Key numbers

- The mean-square radius of the ${}^{16}{\rm O}$ nucleus from the CVMC configurations is $\langle r^2 \rangle = (2.6~{\rm fm})^2$, which folded with the proton charge form factor ($\langle r^2 \rangle_p = (0.84~{\rm fm})^2$) yields a charge radius of $(2.7~{\rm fm})^2$, consistent with the experimental value of $(2.699(5)~{\rm fm})^2$.
- The NN inelastic cross section grows from 31 mb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=10$~GeV to 71 mb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7$~TeV.
- The mixing parameter $\alpha$ in the entropy deposition formula is taken as $\alpha=0.12$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=10$~GeV and $\alpha=0.15$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7$~TeV and 10~TeV.
- The effect of nuclear correlations on the double eccentricity ratio for ${}^{16}{\rm O}+{}^{16}{\rm O}$ collisions reaches a relative size of about 5\% at $c=1\%$ centrality.
- For ${}^{16}{\rm O}$ collisions on heavy targets, the double eccentricity ratio shows effects of nuclear correlations of about 10\% at $c=1\%$ centrality.
- The fit formula for the p+${}^{16}{\rm O}$ production cross section is $\sigma^{\rm prod}_{p+O}=(48.3~{\rm mb})\cdot \left(\sigma^{\rm inel}_{p+p}/{\rm mb}\right)^{0.52}$, yielding $\sigma^{\rm prod}_{p+air}\left(92~ {\rm mb}\right)\simeq 507$~mb, in agreement with the Pierre Auger Collaboration value of $505^{+28}_{-36}$~mb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=57$~TeV.

## Context

This work extends the Glauber Monte Carlo methodology previously applied to heavy-ion collisions (e.g., Pb+Pb and Xe+Xe) to the case of ${}^{16}{\rm O}$ beams, motivated by experimental proposals at the LHC (Citron et al., 2018) and RHIC (STAR Collaboration). It complements similar analyses by Sievert and Noronha-Hostler (2019) using the TRENTo code with hydrodynamics, and by Huang, Chen, Jia, and Li (2019) using the AMPT model, providing independent estimates for model uncertainties. The results enable future experimental proposals and provide input for more detailed dynamical studies with hydrodynamics or transport codes, particularly for investigating the emergence of collectivity in small systems and for cosmic-ray air shower physics.
