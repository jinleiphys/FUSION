# Interference of resonances in two-proton emission of $^{16}$Ne

> We investigate the two-proton ($2p$) emission from $^{16}$Ne with the time-dependent $^{14}$O$+p+p$ three-body calculations. Two $0^+$ resonances are suggested to participate. For the $0^+_1$ resonance, the true emission of the spatially localized two protons is dominant, if the single-particle $s_{1/2}$ resonance locates above the $2p$ energy. By evaluating a time-evolution probability, the $0^+_2$ resonance is suggested at $\cong 3$ MeV from the $2p$ threshold. The inclusion of $0^+_2$ resonance makes the true-$2p$~emission more dominant in the time-dependent calculations.

## Key claim

The paper suggests that a second $0^+$ resonance exists in $^{16}$Ne at approximately 3 MeV above the two-proton threshold, and that including this resonance makes true (direct) two-proton emission more dominant in time-dependent calculations, but only when the single-particle $s_{1/2}$ resonance in $^{15}$F lies above the two-proton energy.

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent three-body calculations of the $^{14}$O$+p+p$ system using a confining-potential method to prepare initial two-proton states. They diagonalized the three-body Hamiltonian with Woods-Saxon core-proton potentials and a proton-proton interaction that includes vacuum and surface-dependent contact terms, then solved the time evolution to extract resonance energies, widths, and decay patterns. Two parameter sets ("prompt" and "mixed") were used to reproduce different experimental data for the $s_{1/2}$ resonance in $^{15}$F.

## Key numbers

- $0^+_1$ resonance energy: $Q_{2p} = 1.404$ MeV (prompt case) and $1.401$ MeV (mixed case), consistent with the experimental value of $1.401(20)$ MeV from Wang (2021)
- $0^+_1$ decay width: $\Gamma_1 = 1.4 \times 10^{-4}$ MeV (prompt case) and $1.1 \times 10^{-3}$ MeV (mixed case), both consistent with the experimental upper limit of $\Gamma_{2p} \le 80$ keV from Brown (2014)
- $0^+_2$ resonance energy: $E_2 = 2.96$ MeV (prompt case) and $2.44$ MeV (mixed case) above the two-proton threshold
- $0^+_2$ decay width: $\Gamma_2 = 2.77$ MeV (prompt case) and $0.82$ MeV (mixed case)
- $(s_{1/2})^2$ configuration ratio in $0^+_1$: $8.5\%$ (prompt) and $32.0\%$ (mixed); in $0^+_2$: $70.1\%$ (prompt) and $61.2\%$ (mixed)
- $(d_{5/2})^2$ configuration ratio in $0^+_1$: $88.4\%$ (prompt) and $63.5\%$ (mixed); in $0^+_2$: $5.7\%$ (prompt) and $33.7\%$ (mixed)

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of two-proton emission from $^{16}$Ne by Grigorenko (2002, 2015) and Fortune (2014), and addresses the open question of whether a second $0^+$ resonance exists, as suggested experimentally by Fohl (1997) at $Q_{2p} = 3.5$ MeV. The time-dependent three-body approach, previously developed by Oishi (2014, 2017), enables direct visualization of the decay dynamics and distinguishes between true two-proton emission and sequential proton emission. The results support the experimental findings of Brown (2014) and Charity (2016) that the ground-state decay is dominated by direct two-proton emission, and predict that the $0^+_2$ resonance is $(s_{1/2})^2$ dominant, analogous to the $0^+_2$ state in $^{16}$C at $E_x = 3.03$ MeV from Fortune (1977).
