# Parton energy loss in cold nuclei

> Within the generalized high-twist factorization formalism, we express the contribution from multiple parton scattering and induced gluon radiation to the DY dilepton spectra in terms of nuclear modified effective beam quark distribution function. We show that beam quark energy loss is characterized by jet transport parameter $\hat{q}$, which is related to the local gluon density of the medium. Using the value of $\hat{q}$ determined from the deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) data, we evaluate the nuclear modification factor in Drell-Yan process in p+A collisions. Effects of parton energy loss in the DY spectra are found negligible in the Fermilab experimental data at $E_{\rm lab}=800$ GeV relative to parton shadowing while the predicted suppression of DY spectra are significant at $E_{\rm lab}=120$ GeV.

## Key claim
Within the generalized high-twist factorization formalism, beam quark energy loss in cold nuclei is characterized by the jet transport parameter $\hat{q}$, which is related to the local gluon density of the medium. Using $\hat{q}$ values determined from deeply inelastic scattering (DIS) data, the effect of parton energy loss on Drell-Yan spectra is found negligible at $E_{\rm lab}=800$ GeV relative to parton shadowing, but predicted to be significant at $E_{\rm lab}=120$ GeV.

## Method
The authors generalized the high-twist factorization formalism to express contributions from multiple parton scattering and induced gluon radiation to Drell-Yan dilepton spectra in terms of a nuclear-modified effective beam quark distribution function. They considered two double-scattering processes (annihilation-like and Compton-like) and summed leading logarithmic contributions from single and double scattering. The medium-modified splitting functions were related to the twist-4 matrix element $T_{g\bar q}$, which was factorized in terms of quark and gluon density distributions inside the target nucleus and connected to the jet transport parameter $\hat{q}$.

## Key numbers
- Jet transport parameter range: $\hat{q}_0 = 0.016 - 0.032$ GeV$^2$/fm in cold nuclei, determined from fitting HERMES data on $\pi^{\pm}$ and $K^{\pm}$ spectra in semi-inclusive DIS.
- Central value used for predictions: $\hat{q}_0 = 0.024 \pm 0.008$ GeV$^2$/fm.
- Fermilab E866 experiment: beam energy $E_{\rm lab}=800$ GeV; parton energy loss effects found negligible compared to shadowing.
- Fermilab E906 experiment: beam energy $E_{\rm lab}=120$ GeV; predicted suppression of DY spectra is significant, with invariant dilepton mass $M=4.5$ GeV.

## Context
This work extends the generalized high-twist factorization formalism (Luo, 1992) previously applied to final-state multiple scattering in DIS (Guo, 2000) to initial-state parton energy loss in Drell-Yan processes. The jet transport parameter $\hat{q}$ was previously determined from HERMES data (Airapetian, 2007) and found consistent with transverse momentum broadening of Drell-Yan dileptons in p+A collisions (Guo, 1998). The results enable unambiguous measurement of initial-state parton energy loss in the upcoming Fermilab E906 experiment, where shadowing effects are small and energy loss becomes the dominant nuclear modification.
