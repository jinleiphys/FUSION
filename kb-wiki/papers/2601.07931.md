# General gravitational properties of neutron stars: curvature invariants, binding energy, and trace anomaly

> We investigate the behavior of curvature invariants for a large ensemble of neutron stars built with equations of state (EOSs) that satisfy constraints from nuclear theory and perturbative QCD, as well as measurements of neutron-star masses, radii, and gravitational waves from binary neutron-star mergers. Surprisingly, our analysis reveals that stars with negative Ricci scalar $\mathcal{R}$ are rather common and about $\sim 50\%$ of our EOSs produce one or more stars with Ricci curvature that is negative somewhere inside the star. The negative curvature is found mostly but not exclusively at the highest densities and pressures, and predominantly for stiff EOSs and for the most compact and most massive stars. Furthermore, we improve the quasi-universal relation between the stellar gravitational mass $M$ and the baryonic mass $M_\mathrm{b}$, which allows us to express analytically one in terms of the other with a maximum variance of only $\sim 3\%$. Finally, using the relation between the Ricci scalar and the trace anomaly $Δ$, we determine the conditions under which $Δ$ vanishes or becomes negative in neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper claims that negative Ricci scalar curvature inside neutron stars is surprisingly common, with about 50% of equations of state producing at least one star with negative Ricci curvature somewhere in its interior, predominantly for stiff EOSs and the most compact and massive stars.

## Method

The authors constructed a large ensemble of approximately 3 x 10^4 equations of state using a piecewise-linear sound-speed parametrization with 7 segments, combining the BPS crust model, nuclear-theory bounds up to 1.1 times nuclear saturation density, and perturbative QCD results at high chemical potentials. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations for static, spherically symmetric neutron stars and computed curvature invariants (Ricci scalar, Kretschmann scalar, and related quantities) from the resulting density and pressure profiles, then applied Bayesian inference with astrophysical constraints from pulsar masses, NICER radius measurements, and the GW170817 tidal deformability.

## Key numbers

- About 51% of EOSs produce neutron stars with positive Ricci scalar everywhere; the remaining ~49% produce at least one star with negative Ricci scalar somewhere in the interior
- The "zero-curvature" compactness (M/R at which the Ricci scalar vanishes at the stellar center) is measured as C_0 = 0.26911 ± 0.00004, differing by only 3% from the earlier estimate of 0.262 by Podkowka (2018)
- The baryonic mass M_b relates to gravitational mass M via M_b = M (1 + 0.087 M), with a maximum residual of less than 0.08 M_sun (about 3%)
- The binding energy relation E_bin/M = (0.56270 C) / (1 - 0.86332 C) provides best-fit coefficients c1 = 0.56270 ± 0.00005 and c2 = 0.86332 ± 0.00028
- The compactness estimate from gravitational mass has an uncertainty of 15-20% for masses below about 2 M_sun

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of curvature scalars in neutron stars by Eksi (2014), He (2015), and others that focused on specific EOSs, providing instead a comprehensive statistical analysis using an agnostic EOS ensemble. It improves upon the quasi-universal relation between gravitational and baryonic mass originally studied by Timmes (1996) and Gao (2020), and refines the binding energy relation from Lattimer (2001) and Breu (2016). The results enable future work connecting curvature properties to the trace anomaly as a diagnostic for deconfinement transitions to quark matter, following the approach of Annala (2023) and Ecker (2025).
