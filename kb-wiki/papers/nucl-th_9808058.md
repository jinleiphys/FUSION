# Non-perturbative renormalization group equations and approximate discrete gamma_5-symmetry in quantum hadrodynamics

> The quantum hadrodynamics are studied by using non-perturbative renormalization group equations. Approximate discrete gamma_5-symmetry is studied. It is shown that the contributions of one-loop diagrams are important for effective potential. A relation between the local-potential approximation and the usual Hartree approximation is discussed. The local-potential approximation includes contribution of the Fock term to nucleon self-energy, contribution in the usual random phase approximation to meson self-energy and vertex corrections in part as well as the Hartree contribution to the effective potential. Approximate discrete gamma_5-symmetry is studied phenomenologically in the Hartree approximation. This approximate symmetry may exist in nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper shows that an approximate discrete $\gamma_5$-symmetry may be preserved to some extent through the non-perturbative renormalization group evolution in quantum hadrodynamics, even though the nucleon already has an explicit mass term that breaks the exact symmetry. The authors demonstrate that if all "odd" couplings (those with $l+m$ equal an odd integer) are set to zero at the initial cutoff scale, they remain zero through the whole evolution, raising the possibility that approximate symmetry persists.

## Method

The authors derive non-perturbative renormalization group (NPRG) equations for a $\sigma$-nucleon system using a sharp cutoff function in Euclidean space, then apply the local-potential approximation to reduce the functional differential equation to a partial differential equation for the generalized potential $U(\phi, \sigma; t)$. They truncate the expansion of the potential at terms with naive dimension less than or equal to 8, keeping couplings up to $\phi^8$, $\phi^5\sigma$, and $\phi^2\sigma^2$, and numerically integrate the resulting coupled differential equations from $t=0$ to $t=4$ to study the evolution of the couplings and the approximate symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The initial conditions used are $C_{2,0}=0.05$, $C_{4,0}=2.5$, $C_{0,1}=0.3$, $C_{1,1}=-2.0$ at $t=0$, with all other couplings set to zero.
- At large $t$, couplings evolve approximately proportional to $\exp(d^c_{l,m}t)$, where $d^c_{l,m}$ is the naive dimension; for example, $C_{2,0} \sim \exp(2t)$, $C_{4,0} \sim$ constant, $C_{6,0} \sim \exp(-2t)$.
- The absolute values of couplings are evaluated at $t=4$, where almost all quantum corrections have been included, with the ratio $Q/\Lambda_0 = 0.5$ used for normalization.
- The degree of freedom of isospin for fermions is $\lambda = 2$ for the nucleon system.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Kouno et al., 1997-1998) of nuclear matter properties using cutoff field theory and Lepage's method, and applies non-perturbative renormalization group techniques developed by Wilson, Wegner, Nicoll, Polchinski, Hasenfratz, Wetterich, Clark, Morris, and Aoki (various years) to quantum hadrodynamics. The paper establishes a relation between the local-potential approximation and the traditional Hartree approximation of Walecka (1974) and Serot (1986), showing that the NPRG approach includes Fock term contributions to nucleon self-energy, random phase approximation contributions to meson self-energy, and vertex corrections beyond the Hartree approximation. This enables future extension to finite baryon density and inclusion of other mesons.
