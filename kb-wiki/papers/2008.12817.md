# Combining Electromagnetic and Gravitational-Wave Constraints on Neutron-Star Masses and Radii

> We perform a joint Bayesian inference of neutron-star mass and radius constraints based on GW170817, observations of quiescent low-mass X-ray binaries (QLMXBs), photospheric radius expansion X-ray bursts (PREs), and X-ray timing observations of J0030+0451. With this data set, the form of the prior distribution still has an impact on the posterior mass-radius (MR) curves and equation of state (EOS), but this impact is smaller than recently obtained when considering QLMXBs alone. We analyze the consistency of the electromagnetic data by including an "intrinsic scattering" contribution to the uncertainties, and find only a slight broadening of the posteriors. This suggests that the gravitational-wave and electromagnetic observations of neutron-star structure are providing a consistent picture of the neutron-star mass-radius curve and the EOS.

## Key claim

The joint Bayesian inference of neutron-star mass and radius constraints from gravitational-wave (GW170817) and electromagnetic observations (quiescent low-mass X-ray binaries, photospheric radius expansion X-ray bursts, and NICER timing of J0030+0451) yields a consistent picture of the neutron-star mass-radius curve and equation of state, but the form of the prior distribution still has an impact on the posterior mass-radius curves and equation of state, though this impact is smaller than when considering quiescent low-mass X-ray binaries alone.

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian inference using two equation-of-state parameterizations: a three-polytrope model (3P) and a four-line-segment model (4L) in pressure versus energy density space. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations for each equation-of-state point, computed tidal deformability via the Yagi-Yunes correlation, and constructed conditional probabilities for quiescent low-mass X-ray binaries (as in Steiner 2018), three photospheric radius expansion X-ray burst sources (Nattila 2016, 2017), LIGO constraints on the combined tidal deformability from GW170817 using the SEOBNRv4T model with low-spin priors, and NICER mass-radius constraints on J0030+0451 (Riley 2019, Miller 2019). An intrinsic scattering parameter was introduced by convolving the probability distribution for each star with a Gaussian kernel to quantify systematic bias.

## Key numbers

- For GW data alone, the 95% credible interval for the radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star is 11.3-13.9 km (3P model) and 10.7-13.1 km (4L model)
- For the combined dataset (GW, QLMXB, PRE, NICER) without intrinsic scattering, the 95% credible interval for R1.4 is 11.28-12.58 km (model "e")
- For the combined dataset with intrinsic scattering (all+IS), the 3P model gives R1.4 median 11.98 km with 95% interval 11.18-12.75 km; the 4L model gives median 11.83 km with 95% interval 11.12-12.45 km
- For the 4L model with all+IS and requiring maximum mass greater than 2.6 solar masses, the 95% lower limit for R1.4 increases from 10.98 km to 11.47 km
- The energy density at nuclear saturation is approximately 150 MeV/fm^3; most equations of state suggested by the data imply central energy density between about 900 and 1200 MeV/fm^3

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian inference framework of Steiner (2010, 2012, 2018) by jointly analyzing gravitational-wave data from GW170817 with electromagnetic observations from quiescent low-mass X-ray binaries, photospheric radius expansion X-ray bursts, and NICER observations of J0030+0451. Previous works such as Annala (2018), De (2018), Radice (2019), Capano (2020), and others analyzed subsets of these data, but few (e.g., Baillot 2019, Raithel 2020) directly combined gravitational-wave data with quiescent low-mass X-ray binary and photospheric radius expansion X-ray burst radius constraints. The results enable more robust equation-of-state constraints and provide a framework for incorporating future observations, including potential constraints from GW190814's 2.6 solar mass object and kilonova modeling.
