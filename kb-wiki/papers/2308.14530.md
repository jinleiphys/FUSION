# Heavy quark diffusion and radiation at intermediate momentum

> We discuss heavy quark diffusion and radiation in an intermediate-momentum regime where finite mass effects can be significant. Diffusion processes are described in the Fokker-Planck approximation for soft momentum transfer, while radiative ones are taken into account by nearly collinear gluon emission from a single scattering in the Boltzmann equation. We also consider radiative corrections to the transverse momentum diffusion coefficient, which are $\mathcal{O}(g^2)$ suppressed than the leading-order diffusion coefficient but logarithmically enhanced. Numerical results show that the heavy quark distribution function depends on the energy loss mechanism so that the momentum dependence of suppression is distinguishable. Employing the heavy quark diffusion coefficient constrained by lattice QCD data, we estimate the nuclear modification factor which exhibits a transition from diffusion at low momentum to radiation at high momentum. The significance of radiative effects at intermediate momentum depends on the diffusion coefficient and running coupling constant.

# Heavy quark diffusion and radiation at intermediate momentum

## Key claim

Heavy quark transport in an intermediate-momentum regime can be described using a single transport parameter (the static momentum diffusion coefficient) to treat both collisional diffusion and gluon-bremsstrahlung consistently, but the significance of radiative effects at intermediate momentum depends on the momentum-dependent diffusion coefficient and the running coupling constant.

## Method

The Boltzmann equation for heavy quarks is formulated with a Fokker-Planck collision term for soft momentum transfer (diffusion) and a separate radiation term for nearly collinear gluon emission from a single scattering, using the same collision kernel for both processes. Radiative corrections to the transverse momentum diffusion coefficient are computed at order g^2 relative to the leading-order coefficient but with logarithmic enhancement. The static diffusion coefficient is constrained by lattice QCD data, and the running coupling constant is employed to incorporate nonperturbative effects.

## Key numbers

- The leading-order momentum diffusion coefficient at zero momentum is given by κ₀ = g⁴ C_F T³/(18π) (N_c + N_f/2) [ln(T/m_D) + O(1)]
- The radiative correction to the transverse diffusion coefficient scales as δκ_T(p) ∼ g² κ_T ln(E/m) ln(p/T)
- The running coupling constant ranges from α_s ∼ 0.23 to 0.68 for the temperatures and momenta considered
- The spatial diffusion coefficient at T_c = 157 MeV is fixed to (2π T_c) D_s(T_c) = 3–6, aligned with lattice QCD data from Banerjee (2011), Francis (2015), and Banerjee (2022)
- The initial b quark spectrum is fit to dN/(p_T dp_T) ∝ 1/(p_T² + Λ²)^α with Λ = 6.07 GeV and α = 2.85, based on CMS (2017) B meson production data
- The Bjorken expansion uses initial temperature T₀ = 475 MeV at t₀ = 0.6 fm until T_c = 157 MeV

## Context

This work extends heavy quark transport models that treat collisional and radiative energy loss separately, such as the Langevin approach with a recoil force term from Cao (2013) and opacity expansion calculations from Djordjevic (2003) and Armesto (2003). It differs from prior work by using a single transport parameter (the static diffusion coefficient) for both mechanisms, enabling study of the transition between diffusion-dominated and radiation-dominated regimes. The approach enables future quantitative comparisons with experimental heavy meson data using realistic hydrodynamic evolution and hadronization models.
