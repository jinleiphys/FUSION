# Probing the nucleon's transversity and the photon's distribution amplitude in lepton pair photoproduction

> We describe a new way to access the chiral odd transversity parton distribution in the proton through the photoproduction of lepton pairs. The basic ingredient is the interference of the usual Bethe Heitler or Drell-Yan amplitudes with the amplitude of a process, where the photon couples to quarks through its chiral-odd distribution amplitude, which is normalized to the magnetic susceptibility of the QCD vacuum. A promising phenomenology of single and double spin observables emerges from the unusual features of this amplitude.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the chiral-odd transversity parton distribution of the proton can be accessed through the photoproduction of lepton pairs, specifically via the interference of the Bethe-Heitler or Drell-Yan amplitudes with an amplitude where the photon couples to quarks through its chiral-odd distribution amplitude; this requires the kinematic condition that the transverse momentum of the dilepton is of the same order as its invariant mass.

## Method

The authors calculate the amplitude for the process γ N → l⁻ l⁺ X where the photon interacts through its twist-2 chiral-odd distribution amplitude, normalized to the magnetic susceptibility of the QCD vacuum. They compute the interference of this amplitude with the Bethe-Heitler and Drell-Yan amplitudes, and derive expressions for single and double spin observables that isolate the transversity distribution h₁(x) and the photon distribution amplitude φγ(z). The analytic structure of the amplitude yields an absorptive part that scans the photon distribution amplitude as a function of the momentum fraction z.

## Key numbers

- The product of the quark condensate and the magnetic susceptibility of the QCD vacuum, χ ⟨q̄q⟩, is estimated to be of order 50 MeV (from QCD sum rules).
- The asymptotic form of the photon distribution amplitude is φγᵃˢ(z) = 6z(1-z).
- The unpolarized Drell-Yan cross section is estimated to be of order a few picobarns for Q² values of a few GeV².
- The differential cross section involves the transversity distribution h₁(x) evaluated at x = Q²/(αs) + Q⊥²/(αᾱs) and the photon distribution amplitude φγ evaluated at z = αQ²/(Q² + Q⊥²).

## Context

This work extends the strategy of Berger and Brodsky (1980s) for accessing the pion distribution amplitude via Drell-Yan pairs to the case of the photon's chiral-odd distribution amplitude and the nucleon's transversity. It contrasts with previous approaches to transversity that rely on double spin asymmetries in Drell-Yan processes (requiring a polarized antiproton facility) or on transverse momentum dependent parton distributions in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (Anselmino, 2000s). The proposed observables could be measured at the future JLab 12 GeV facility (Hall D real photon program) or at the COMPASS experiment at CERN, enabling a scan of both the photon distribution amplitude and the transversity distribution.
