# Thermalization of parton spectra in the color-flux-tube model

> Detailed study of thermalization of the momentum spectra of partons produced via decays of the color flux tubes due to the Schwinger tunneling mechanism is presented. The collisions between particles are included in the relaxation time approximation specified by different values of the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio. At first we show that, to a good approximation, the transverse-momentum spectra of the produced patrons are exponential, irrespectively from the assumed value of the viscosity of the system and the freeze-out time. This thermal-like behaviour may be attributed to specific properties of the Schwinger tunneling process. In the next step, in order to check the approach of the system towards genuine local equilibrium, we compare the local slope of the model transverse-momentum spectra with the local slope of the fully equilibrated reference spectra characterised by the effective temperature that reproduces the energy density of the system. We find that the viscosity corresponding to the AdS/CFT lower bound is necessary for thermalization of the system within about two fermis.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio corresponding to the AdS/CFT lower bound of \(1/(4\pi)\) is necessary for the system to reach full local equilibrium within about two fermis, but this conclusion is conditional on the definition of "full thermalization" as reaching local isotropy in momentum space and three-dimensional equilibrium distributions, not merely exponential transverse-momentum spectra.

## Method

The study uses the color-flux-tube model with Abelian dominance approximation, where partons are produced via Schwinger tunneling from decaying color fields. Collisions between partons are included via the relaxation time approximation (RTA), with the relaxation time expressed through the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\bar{\eta} = \eta/s\). The system evolves under (0+1)-dimensional Bjorken boost-invariant expansion, and the transverse-momentum spectra are computed using the Cooper-Frye formula on a constant proper-time freeze-out hypersurface, with the effective temperature determined by Landau matching to the energy density.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\) (the AdS/CFT lower bound) is necessary for thermalization within about 2 fm.
- For the collisionless case (\(\eta/s = \infty\)), the slope parameter \(\lambda\) is approximately 400 MeV at \(\tau = 1\) fm, while the effective temperature \(T\) is approximately 300 MeV at the same proper time.
- For \(\eta/s = 1/(4\pi)\) at \(\tau > 1\) fm, the effective temperature from Landau matching is \(T = 285\) MeV, and the slope parameter \(\lambda_{\rm eq}\) describes the actual \(\lambda\) within \(\pm 5\%\).
- The low-\(\pt\) region below 500 MeV contains almost 45% of the particles, and particles with \(\pt < 1\) GeV make up approximately 75% of the total yield.
- The string tension for gluons is \(\sigma_g = 3\) GeV/fm, with \(\sigma_q = 1\) GeV/fm for quarks, and the transverse area of a tube is \(\mathcal{A} = 1\) fm\(^2\).
- The strong coupling constant is \(g \approx 5.48\), and the number of color charges spanning the tube is \(k = 10\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier color-flux-tube model study by Ryblewski (2013) by providing a detailed analysis of thermalization and comparing the effects of different shear viscosity values. It builds on the framework of anisotropic hydrodynamics developed by Martinez and Florkowski (2010) and Florkowski (2010), and the concept of hydrodynamization introduced by Heller et al. (2011). The results enable the application of viscous fluid dynamics to describe the early-time evolution of the quark-gluon plasma, showing that deviations from equilibrium can be handled by the Grad 14-moment approximation, and that for larger viscosities (\(\eta/s = 10/(4\pi)\)) more sophisticated approaches like anisotropic hydrodynamics may be necessary.
