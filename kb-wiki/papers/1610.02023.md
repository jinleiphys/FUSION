# Holography, Hydrodynamization and Heavy-Ion Collisions

> In the course of the past several years holography has emerged as an ab initio tool in exploring strongly-time-dependent phenomena in gauge theories. These lecture notes overview recent developments in this area driven by phenomenological questions concerning applicability of hydrodynamics (hydrodynamization) under extreme conditions occurring in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. The topics include hydrodynamization time scale, holographic collisions, as well as hydrodynamization from the point of view of the asymptotic character of the hydrodynamic gradient expansion. The emphasis is put on concepts rather than calculational techniques and a particular attention is devoted to present these developments in the context of the most recent advances and some of the open problems.

## Key claim
Holography provides an ab initio tool to study hydrodynamization, the process by which the energy-momentum tensor of a strongly-coupled gauge theory becomes accurately described by hydrodynamic equations, and the key lesson is that hydrodynamization does not imply equilibration, meaning the system can be described by hydrodynamics while still being far from thermal equilibrium.

## Method
The paper reviews a series of holographic studies that solve Einstein's equations with negative cosmological constant in (1+4) dimensions, dual to strongly-coupled conformal field theories such as N=4 super Yang-Mills. Three approaches are used to generate non-equilibrium states: turning on CFT sources, superposing gravitational shock-waves, and solving the initial value problem in AdS for arbitrary initial metrics. The resulting energy-momentum tensor profiles are then analyzed to determine when they become well-approximated by hydrodynamic constitutive relations.

## Key numbers
- The ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density at infinite 't Hooft coupling is eta/s = 1/(4 pi) ≈ 0.08, compared to the weak-coupling value of approximately 6.174/(lambda^2 log(2.36/sqrt(lambda))) for N=4 SYM.
- For small perturbations of equilibrium plasma, the hydrodynamization time scale is t_hydro * T = O(1), meaning it is set by the inverse temperature.
- The equilibrium energy density for N=4 SYM is E(T) = (3/8) N_c^2 pi^2 T^4, with pressure P(T) = (1/3) E(T).

## Context
This review extends the line of work on using holography to study time-dependent processes in gauge theories, motivated by the discovery of quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC. It builds on the fluid-gravity duality of Bhattacharyya (2008) and the quasinormal mode analysis of Kovtun (2005), and discusses how the results enable a deeper understanding of when hydrodynamics becomes applicable in heavy-ion collisions. The paper also notes that similar hydrodynamization phenomena have been observed in weakly-coupled frameworks by Kurkela (2015) and Keegan (2015).
