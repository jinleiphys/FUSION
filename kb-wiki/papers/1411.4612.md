# Thermal Dileptons as Fireball Thermometer and Chronometer

> Thermal dilepton radiation from the hot fireballs created in high-energy heavy-ion collisions provides unique insights into the properties of the produced medium. We first show how the predictions of hadronic many-body theory for a melting $ρ$ meson, coupled with QGP emission utilizing a modern lattice-QCD based equation of state, yield a quantitative description of dilepton spectra in heavy-ion collisions at the SPS and the RHIC beam energy scan program. We utilize these results to systematically extract the excess yields and their invariant-mass spectral slopes to predict the excitation function of fireball lifetimes and (early) temperatures, respectively. We thereby demonstrate that future measurements of these quantities can yield unprecedented information on basic fireball properties. Specifically, our predictions quantify the relation between the measured and maximal fireball temperatures, and the proportionality of excess yields and total lifetime. This information can serve as a "caloric" curve to search for a first-order QCD phase transition, and to detect non-monotonous lifetime variations possibly related to critical phenomena.

## Key claim

Thermal dilepton spectra can serve as a quantitative thermometer and chronometer for fireballs in heavy-ion collisions, with the measured average temperature being about 30% below the initial temperature at top RHIC energy, and the integrated low-mass excess yield tracking the total fireball lifetime within less than 10%.

## Method

The authors compute thermal dilepton emission rates using hadronic many-body theory for a melting rho meson in the low-mass region and a lattice-QCD based emission rate for the quark-gluon plasma, then integrate these rates over an isentropically expanding thermal fireball model. The fireball evolution uses a lattice-QCD equation of state at zero baryon chemical potential extended to finite chemical potential, with parameters fitted to observed bulk-particle spectra and elliptic flow at kinetic freezeout. The resulting dilepton spectra are compared to NA60 data for In-In collisions at 17.3 GeV and STAR beam-energy scan data for Au-Au collisions from 19.6 to 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Inverse slope parameter T_s from intermediate-mass dileptons (M=1.5-2.5 GeV) ranges from approximately 160 MeV at sqrt(s_NN)=6 GeV to 260 MeV at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV
- The measured average temperature is about 30% below the initial temperature at top RHIC energy, decreasing to less than 15% at sqrt(s_NN)=6 GeV
- Fireball lifetime extracted from NA60 low-mass excess data: tau_FB = 7 +/- 1 fm/c
- Temperature extracted from NA60 intermediate-mass data: T = 205 +/- 12 MeV, exceeding the pseudo-critical temperature T_pc = 150-170 MeV from lattice QCD
- Initial longitudinal fireball size z_0 varies from 2.1 fm/c at sqrt(s)=6.3 GeV to 0.63 fm/c at sqrt(s)=200 GeV
- Pseudo-critical temperature T_pc varies from 161 MeV at sqrt(s)=6.3 GeV to 170 MeV at sqrt(s)=200 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by van Hees and Rapp (2007) that used a first-order phase transition, now implementing a modern lattice-QCD equation of state that recovers accurate description of NA60 data. The predictions provide a baseline for future experiments including CBM, NA60+, NICA, and STAR BES-II at sqrt(s_NN)=5-20 GeV, where the fireball is expected to reach maximal baryon density and possibly approach a critical point in the QCD phase diagram as discussed by Stephanov (2007). The framework is also consistent with recent coarse-grained UrQMD transport calculations by Endres (2014) that agree with both NA60 and HADES data at sqrt(s_NN)=2.6 GeV.
