# On scission configuration in ternary fission

> A static scission configuration in cold ternary fission has been considered in the framework of two mean field approaches. The virial theorems has been suggested to investigate correlations in the phase space, starting from a kinetic equation. The inverse mean field method is applied to solve single-particle Schredinger equation, instead of constrained selfconsistent Hartree-Fock equations. It is shown, that it is possible to simulate one-dimensional three-center system via inverse scattering method in the approximation of reflectless single-particle potentials

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a static scission configuration in cold ternary fission can be analyzed using two mean-field approaches, but the results are limited to a one-dimensional three-center system simulated via the inverse scattering method in the approximation of reflectless single-particle potentials, with the caveat that the omitted reflection terms would smooth oscillations in the potential and correct its asymptotic behavior.

## Method

The authors first derive virial theorems from the Vlasov kinetic equation for the Wigner phase-space distribution function, truncating the moment chain at second order to obtain fluid-dynamical equations for a rotating nuclear system. They then apply the inverse mean field method, using the Gel'fand-Levitan-Marchenko integral equation to solve the single-particle Schrödinger equation instead of constrained self-consistent Hartree-Fock equations, working in the approximation of reflectless and symmetrical potentials for a slab geometry.

## Key numbers

- The light model system has approximately A ≈ 20 nucleons, with a one-dimensional thickness parameter A ≈ 1.0.
- The model uses three bound levels (N = 3, N₀ = 3) for the light system.
- The inverse method reproduces density profiles "practically identical" to results from Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculations for the ground state and for systems in external potential fields.

## Context

This work extends earlier cluster-like models of cold ternary fragmentation (Sandulescu 1989, Gönnenwein and Börsig 1990) that built the scission configuration "by hand," and addresses the lack of a developed three-center shell model beyond early steps (Scharnweber et al. 1977). The approach enables future formulation of generalized constraints in phase space for realistic three-dimensional mean-field calculations of three-center nuclear systems, and could provide initial states for dynamical mean-field calculations.
