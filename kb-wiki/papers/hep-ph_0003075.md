# Is the Polarized Antiquark Sea in the Nucleon Flavor Symmetric?

> We show that the model which naturally explains the $\bar u \ne \bar d$ asymmetry in the nucleon and is in quantitative agreement with the Gottfried sum rule data, also predicts that in the proton $Δ\bar u > 0 > Δ\bar s > Δ\bar d$ and $Δ\bar u - Δ\bar d > \bar d - \bar u > 0$. At the input scale, these results can be derived even analytically. Thus the violation of the flavor symmetry is more serious in the polarized case than in the unpolarized case. In contrast, many recent analyses of the polarized data have made a simplifying assumption that all the three $Δ\bar q$'s have the same sign and magnitude. We point out the need to redo these analyses, allowing for the alternate scenario as described above. We present predictions of the model for the $W^-$ asymmetry in polarized $pp$ scattering, which can be tested at RHIC; these are quite different from those available in the literature.

## Key claim

The statistical model of the nucleon, which naturally explains the unpolarized antiquark flavor asymmetry and agrees with Gottfried sum rule data, predicts that the polarized antiquark sea violates flavor symmetry more severely than the unpolarized sea, with the specific ordering Delta u_bar > 0 > Delta s_bar > Delta d_bar. This contradicts the common simplifying assumption in global analyses that all three polarized antiquark distributions have the same sign and magnitude.

## Method

The author uses a statistical model of parton distribution functions (PDFs) based on Fermi-Dirac statistics, where parton number densities in the nucleon rest frame are given by Fermi or Bose distribution functions with chemical potentials determined by valence number and spin sum rules. The model parameters were previously fixed by fitting only the unpolarized structure functions F2^p and (F2^p - F2^n) at Q^2 = 4 GeV^2, and all polarized results are predictions. The author derives analytic inequalities for the integrated parton numbers at the input scale Q0^2 = M^2 = 0.88 GeV^2, then evolves the PDFs in next-to-leading-order QCD in the MS-bar scheme up to Q^2 = 6500 GeV^2 to verify the inequalities hold throughout.

## Key numbers

- Input scale Q0^2 = M^2 = 0.88 GeV^2, where M is the nucleon mass
- Chemical potentials at input scale (in MeV): mu_u_up = 210, mu_d_down = 106, mu_u_down = 86, mu_d_up = 42, mu_s_down = 7, mu_s_up = -7
- Integrated spin sums used as constraints: (Delta u + Delta u_bar) = 0.83 +/- 0.03, (Delta d + Delta d_bar) = -0.43 +/- 0.03, (Delta s + Delta s_bar) = -0.10 +/- 0.03
- d(x)/u(x) ratio in the limit x -> 1 predicted to be 0.22, in agreement with the QCD prediction of 0.2
- At y=0 (x_a = x_b), the double-spin asymmetry A_LL^PV(W^+) is almost twice as large as the single-spin asymmetry A_L^PV(W^+)
- Predictions for W^- asymmetries in polarized pp scattering at sqrt(s) = 500 GeV are presented as functions of rapidity y

## Context

This work extends the author's own statistical model (Bhalerao 1996, Bhalerao, Kelkar, and Ram 2000) which successfully described unpolarized data including the Gottfried sum rule violation. It directly contradicts the assumption Delta u_bar = Delta d_bar = lambda Delta s_bar used in many global analyses of polarized deep inelastic scattering data, including those by Stratmann (1999), Gluck, Reya, Stratmann, and Vogelsang (1996), Gehrmann and Stirling (1996), and the HERMES and SMC collaborations (1999, 1998). The results are strikingly similar to those from the chiral quark soliton model (Diakonov et al. 1996, Pobylitsa et al. 1999, Dressler et al. 2000), despite very different physics inputs. The predictions for W^- asymmetries at RHIC differ substantially from those based on the parameterizations of Gluck et al. (1996), Gehrmann and Stirling (1996), and Bourrely and Soffer (1995).
