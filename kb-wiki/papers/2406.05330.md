# Formation of transfermium elements in reactions with $^{208}$Pb

> Within the Langevin framework, we investigate the dynamics of the fusion process for production of transfermium elements in reactions of $^{48}$Ca, $^{50}$Ti, $^{54}$Cr, and $^{58}$Fe with $^{208}$Pb. After the reacting nuclei have made contact, the early dynamical stage is dominated by the dissipation of the initial radial kinetic energy, while the subsequent shape evolution is diffusive. The probability for surmounting the inner barrier and forming a compound system is obtained by simulating the evolution as a Metropolis random walk in a five-dimensional potential-energy landscape. Good agreement with the available data is obtained, especially for the maximal formation probability.

# Formation of transfermium elements in reactions with $^{208}$Pb

## Key claim
Within a Langevin framework using a five-dimensional potential-energy landscape with energy-dependent shell and pairing effects, the calculated formation probabilities for transfermium elements agree well with available data, especially for the maximal formation probability, though the model does not include tunneling and thus only applies above threshold energies.

## Method
The authors simulate the fusion process in two stages: first, a drift-dominated stage where the initial radial kinetic energy is dissipated via one-body window friction along a one-dimensional fusion path; second, a diffusive stage simulated as a Metropolis random walk in a five-dimensional shape parameter space (elongation, mass asymmetry, neck radius, and deformations of the two end parts). The potential-energy surface is calculated using the macroscopic-microscopic method with the finite-range liquid-drop model, and the shape evolution is followed until the system either reaches the ground-state elongation (formation) or the neck radius falls below 1.5 fm (quasi-fission).

## Key numbers
- Maximal formation probability ranges from about 60% for $^{48}$Ca+$^{208}$Pb to just a few percent for $^{58}$Fe+$^{208}$Pb.
- For $^{50}$Ti+$^{208}$Pb at $E^*_{\rm CN}=20$ MeV and $I=0$, the formation probability is 28% and the quasi-fission probability is 72%.
- The effective formation probability for $^{50}$Ti+$^{208}$Pb decreases from 0.285 at $E^*_{\rm CN}=15$ MeV to 0.128 at $E^*_{\rm CN}=40$ MeV.
- The threshold excitation energies for formation are 12.6, 10.6, 9.2, and 6.7 MeV for reactions with $^{48}$Ca, $^{50}$Ti, $^{54}$Cr, and $^{58}$Fe, respectively.
- The friction strength parameter $\gamma$ increases from about 500 MeV/fm$c$ after contact to about 1200 MeV/fm$c$ at $q_2\approx1.9$ where the neck radius is about 4.9 fm.

## Context
This work extends the fusion-by-diffusion (FBD) model of Swiatecki (2003, 2005) by incorporating a full five-dimensional shape evolution with energy-dependent shell and pairing effects, rather than assuming a one-dimensional diffusion path. The calculations are compared to experimental formation probabilities from Banerjee (2019) and Naik (2007). The approach enables event-by-event simulation of the fusion process, allowing extraction of correlations between observables, and provides a framework that can be extended to heavier systems and to the survival stage of superheavy element production.
