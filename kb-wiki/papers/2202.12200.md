# Coherent $J/ ψ$ electroproduction on $^4$He and $^3$He at the Electron-Ion Collider: probing nuclear shadowing one nucleon at a time

> While the phenomenon of gluon nuclear shadowing at small $x$ has been getting confirmation in QCD analyses of various LHC measurements involving heavy nuclei, it has not been possible so far to establish experimentally the number of target nucleons responsible for nuclear shadowing in a given process. To address this issue, we study coherent $J / ψ$ electroproduction on $^4$He and $^3$He in the kinematics of a future electron-ion collider and show that this process has the power to disentangle the contributions of the interaction with a specific number of nucleons $k$, in particular, with two nucleons at the momentum transfer $t \neq 0$. We predict a dramatic shift of the $t$-dependence of the differential cross section toward smaller values of $|t|$ due to a non-trivial correlation between $x$ and $k$. This calculation, which makes use for the first time of realistic wave functions, provides a stringent test of models of nuclear shadowing and a novel probe of the 3D imaging of gluons in light nuclei. In addition, thanks to this analysis, unique information on the real part of the corresponding scattering amplitude could be accessed.

## Key claim

Coherent $J/\psi$ electroproduction on $^4$He and $^3$He at the Electron-Ion Collider can disentangle the contributions of interactions with a specific number of target nucleons $k$ (particularly two nucleons) at momentum transfer $t \neq 0$, provided that the one-body form factor is suppressed near its diffraction minimum, allowing the two-body rescattering contribution to dominate and reveal leading-twist gluon nuclear shadowing.

## Method

The authors employed the Gribov-Glauber multiple scattering formalism to compute the differential cross section for coherent $J/\psi$ electroproduction on $^4$He and $^3$He, expressing the nuclear scattering amplitude as a sum over interactions with $k=1,2,3,\dots$ nucleons. The $k$-body nuclear form factors $\Phi_k$ were calculated using realistic nuclear wave functions obtained from exact solutions of the Schrodinger equation with chiral potentials (N$^4$LO for $^4$He, AV18+UIX for $^3$He), and the two-body interaction strength was fixed by the diffractive cross section on the nucleon via the effective cross section $\sigma_2(x)$.

## Key numbers

* At $x=10^{-3}$, the effective two-nucleon cross section is $\sigma_2(x)=25$ mb with a relative error of approximately 15%, determined from H1 diffractive DIS data.
* The slope of the $t$ dependence for $\gamma^{\ast} p \to J/\psi p$ is $B_0(x)=4.5$ GeV$^{-2}$ with a relative error of approximately 10%, corresponding to $x \simeq 10^{-3}$.
* For $^4$He, the first diffraction minimum shifts from $-t=0.45$ GeV$^2$ in the impulse approximation to $-t=0.27$ GeV$^2$ in the full calculation including two-body rescattering.
* The one-body form factor zero occurs at $\sqrt{|t|}=0.7$ GeV/c for $^4$He and $\sqrt{|t|}=0.8$ GeV/c for $^3$He.
* The binding energy of $^4$He calculated with the N$^4$LO chiral potential is $B(^4{\rm He})=-28.15$ MeV, close to the experimental value.
* The average number of participating nucleons is $\nu \sim 1.7$ for $x=10^{-3}$.
* At $x=0.05$, the slope parameter changes to $B_0(x)=3$ GeV$^{-2}$.

## Context

This work extends the Gribov-Glauber approach to nuclear shadowing (Gribov 1968, Frankfurt 1998) from heavy nuclei studied at the LHC to light nuclei ($^4$He and $^3$He) at the EIC, where the small number of nucleons allows separation of $k=2$ and $k=3$ contributions. It builds on an old idea by Levin (1975) that the minimum in the $^4$He charge form factor can isolate multinucleon interactions, and uses for the first time realistic nuclear wave functions from chiral effective field theory (Entem 2017, Marcucci 2019). The results enable a stringent test of models of nuclear shadowing, access to the real part of the scattering amplitude, and a novel probe of 3D gluon imaging in light nuclei, complementing LHC ultraperipheral collision measurements that probe predominantly $t \simeq 0$.
