# Shell-structure fingerprints of tensor interaction

> We address consequences of strong tensor and weak spin-orbit terms in the local energy density functional, resulting from fits to the $f_{5/2} - f_{7/2}$ splittings in $^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca, and $^{56}$Ni. In this study, we focus on nuclear binding energies. In particular, we show that the tensor contribution to the binding energies exhibits interesting topological features closely resembling that of the shell-correction. We demonstrate that in the extreme single-particle scenario at spherical shape, the tensor contribution shows tensorial magic numbers equal to $N(Z)$=14, 32, 56, and 90, and that this structure is smeared out due to configuration mixing caused by pairing correlations and migration of proton/neutron sub-shells with neutron/proton shell filling. Based on a specific Skyrme-type functional SLy4$_T$, we show that the proton tensorial magic numbers shift with increasing neutron excess to $Z$=14, 28, and 50.

## Key claim

The paper shows that fitting tensor and spin-orbit coupling constants to the $f_{5/2}-f_{7/2}$ splittings in three specific nuclei yields strong attractive tensor fields and weak spin-orbit fields, and that the resulting tensor contribution to binding energies exhibits a shell-correction-like pattern with "tensorial magic numbers" that are partially smeared out by pairing and sub-shell migration, and which differ for protons versus neutrons under realistic conditions.

## Method

The authors used a spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogolubov (HFB) code (HFBRAD) with a modified Skyrme-type functional (SLy4$_T$) to compute the tensor contribution to nuclear binding energies. The tensor and spin-orbit coupling constants were fitted to reproduce the experimental $f_{7/2}-f_{5/2}$ spin-orbit splittings in $^{40}$Ca (spin-saturated, sets isoscalar spin-orbit strength), $^{56}$Ni (isoscalar spin-unsaturated, sets isoscalar tensor strength), and $^{48}$Ca (proton spin-saturated, neutron spin-unsaturated, sets isovector coupling constants). The isovector spin-orbit ratio was kept equal to the value characteristic of the SLy4 parametrization.

## Key numbers

- The isoscalar tensor coupling constant $C_0^J$ was fitted to approximately $-45$ MeV fm$^5$, and the isovector tensor coupling constant $C_1^J$ to approximately $-70$ MeV fm$^5$.
- The isoscalar spin-orbit strength $C_0^{\nabla J}$ required a circa 35% reduction compared to the original SLy4 value.
- In the extreme single-particle scenario at spherical shape, the tensorial magic numbers are $N(Z)=14$, 32, 56, and 90.
- For the SLy4$_T$ functional, the proton tensorial magic numbers shift with increasing neutron excess to $Z=14$, 28, and 50.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Zalewski (2008) and Satula (2008) for fitting tensor and spin-orbit coupling constants, which contrasts with earlier bulk-property fits (Lesinski, 2007) that gave different tensor strengths. The results confirm the interpretation by Otsuka (2005, 2006) linking tensor interaction to shell evolution, and are consistent with earlier Skyrme-EDF studies (Dobaczewski, 2006; Brown, 2006; Colo, 2007; Brink, 2007). The paper enables further study of how strong tensor interactions affect nuclear binding at drip lines, particularly around $Z\approx 14, N\approx 32$, $Z\approx 28, N\approx 56$, and $Z\approx 50, N\approx 90$.
