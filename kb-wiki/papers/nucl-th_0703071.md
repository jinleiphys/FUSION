# Dynamical symmetry of isobaric analog 0+ states in medium mass nuclei

> An algebraic sp(4) shell model is introduced to achieve a deeper understanding and interpretation of the properties of pairing-governed 0+ states in medium mass atomic nuclei. The theory, which embodies the simplicity of a dynamical symmetry approach to nuclear structure, is shown to reproduce the excitation spectra and fine structure effects driven by proton-neutron interactions and isovector pairing correlations across a broad range of nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper introduces an algebraic sp(4) shell model that reproduces the excitation spectra and fine structure effects of isobaric analog 0+ states in medium-mass nuclei, but only for the pairing-governed 0+ states in even-A nuclei where valence protons and neutrons occupy the same major shell and shape deformation is relatively weak.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian with Sp(4) dynamical symmetry using generators that describe isovector (T=1) pairing between protons and neutrons, including like-particle (pp, nn) and proton-neutron (pn) J=0 pairs, plus an isoscalar (T=0) proton-neutron force related to the symmetry energy. The six-parameter Hamiltonian is diagonalized exactly in the zero-seniority basis, and the interaction strengths are determined by fitting the lowest isobaric analog 0+ state energies (Coulomb-corrected) to experimental data for 149 nuclei across three valence spaces: the 1d3/2 level, the 1f7/2 level, and the 1f5/2 2p1/2 2p3/2 1g9/2 shell.

## Key numbers

- The model reproduces the lowest isobaric analog 0+ state energies with deviations of 0.7% for region I (1d3/2 level) and 0.5% for regions II (1f7/2 level) and III (fpg shell) relative to the experimental energy range.
- The like-particle pairing strength follows G/Omega = 23.9 +/- 1.1 / A (MeV), with R^2 = 0.96, across the three regions.
- The symmetry energy parameter follows E/(2Omega) = -50.2 +/- 3.3 / A (MeV), with R^2 = 0.93.
- The Wigner energy strength is estimated as W = -31 +/- 2 / A (MeV), with R^2 = 0.96, consistent with other estimates such as -30/A from Moller (1997) and -37/A from Duflo and Zuker.
- For the 1f7/2 level, the binding energy of proton-rich 48Ni is predicted to be 348.19 MeV, 0.07% greater than the semi-empirical estimate of Moller (1997).
- The model predicts lowest isobaric analog 0+ state energies for 165 additional even-A nuclei in the fpg shell (A=56-100), with binding energies on average 0.05% less than the semi-empirical approximation of Moller (1997).

## Context

This work extends the SO(5) symmetry approach previously applied to fp-shell N=Z nuclei by Engel (1996) and the isospin-invariant pairing plus quadrupole shell-model theory of Kaneko (1999). The Sp(4) model is simpler than the more general U(4Omega) seniority scheme of Racah and Flowers and provides a better microscopic description of isovector pairing correlations than mean-field theories or semi-empirical mass formulas. The results enable predictions of pairing-governed 0+ state energies for proton-rich nuclei relevant to the rp-process in nucleosynthesis, and the parameter systematics reveal a global 1/A dependence of the interaction strengths across the medium-mass region.
