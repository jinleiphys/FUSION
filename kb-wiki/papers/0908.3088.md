# QCD phase diagram at imaginary baryon and isospin chemical potentials

> We explore the phase diagram of two-flavor QCD at imaginary values of baryon and isospin chemical potentials analyzing the thermodynamic potential of QCD analytically and that of the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model numerically. QCD has no pion condensation at imaginary baryon and isospin chemical potentials, and therefore has discrete symmetries that are not present at real baryon and isospin chemical potentials. The PNJL model possesses all the discrete symmetries. The PNJL model can reproduce qualitatively lattice QCD data presented very lately.

## Key claim

The paper shows that at imaginary baryon and isospin chemical potentials, QCD has no pion condensation, leading to higher discrete symmetries than at real chemical potentials; the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model possesses all these symmetries and can qualitatively reproduce lattice QCD data, but the comparison is only qualitative, not quantitative.

## Method

The authors analytically analyze the QCD partition function at imaginary baryon and isospin chemical potentials to prove the absence of pion condensation and derive discrete symmetries, using chiral perturbation theory to show that the static Lagrangian is minimized at zero pion condensate for imaginary isospin chemical potential. They then numerically solve the PNJL model in the mean-field approximation, computing the thermodynamic potential as a function of the Polyakov loop, chiral condensate, and pion condensate, and verifying that the global minimum occurs at zero pion condensate for imaginary isospin chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The three-dimensional momentum cutoff Lambda = 631.5 MeV, the scalar coupling constant G_s = 5.498 GeV^{-2}, and the current quark mass m_0 = 5.5 MeV reproduce the pion decay constant f_pi = 93.3 MeV and the pion mass M_pi = 138 MeV at T = 0.
- The Polyakov potential parameter T_0 is rescaled from 270 MeV to 212 MeV to match full lattice QCD pseudocritical temperature at zero chemical potential.
- The Polyakov potential parameters are a_0 = 3.51, a_1 = -2.47, a_2 = 15.2, and b_3 = -1.75 (all dimensionless).
- The potential surface is evaluated at T = 175 MeV for theta_I = 0, pi/2, and pi, showing the global minimum at bar_pi = 0 for all cases.

## Context

This work extends the Roberge-Weiss (1986) analysis of the Z_3 periodicity at imaginary baryon chemical potential to the two-flavor case with both imaginary baryon and isospin chemical potentials. It builds on the PNJL model developed by Fukushima (2004) and others, and on the chiral perturbation theory treatment of pion condensation at real isospin chemical potential by Son and Stephanov (2001). The results enable future quantitative comparisons with lattice QCD data at imaginary isospin chemical potential from Cea (2008) and D'Elia (2009), and provide a foundation for predicting the QCD phase diagram at real baryon and isospin chemical potentials via analytic continuation.
