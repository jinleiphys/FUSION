# Elastic proton-neutron and antiproton-neutron scattering in holographic QCD

> The total and differential cross sections of the elastic proton-neutron and antiproton-neutron scattering are studied in a holographic QCD model, focusing on the Regge regime. Taking into account the Pomeron and Reggeon exchange, which are described by the Reggeized spin-2 glueball and vector meson propagator respectively, those cross sections are obtained. It is presented that the currently available experimental data of the total cross sections can be well described within the model. Once a single adjustable parameter is determined with the total cross section data, the differential cross sections can be calculated without any additional parameters. Although the available differential cross section data are limited, it is found that our predictions are consistent with those.

## Key claim

The holographic QCD model, using Reggeized spin-2 glueball and vector meson exchange, can describe elastic proton-neutron and antiproton-neutron total cross sections well, but only after a single new adjustable parameter (the Reggeon-neutron coupling constant) is determined from the total cross section data; the differential cross section predictions are then parameter-free but are only tested against limited data.

## Method

The authors model elastic pn and anti-pn scattering in the Regge regime (s >> |t|) by separately describing Pomeron exchange via a Reggeized spin-2 glueball propagator and Reggeon exchange via a Reggeized vector meson propagator. The Pomeron-nucleon coupling uses the nucleon gravitational form factor from a bottom-up AdS/QCD soft-wall model, which introduces no adjustable parameters. Most model parameters (Pomeron intercept, slope, coupling; Reggeon intercept, slope, and proton/antiproton couplings) are taken from prior fits to pp and p anti-p scattering data (Xie 2019, Liu 2022), leaving only the Reggeon-neutron coupling constant to be determined by fitting to the pn and anti-pn total cross section data at sqrt(s) >= 5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Pomeron intercept alpha_g(0) = 1.084, slope alpha'_g = 0.368 GeV^{-2}, coupling lambda_g = 9.593 GeV^{-1} (from fits to pp and p anti-p data at high energies, Xie 2019)
- Reggeon intercept alpha_v(0) = 0.444, slope alpha'_v = 0.925 GeV^{-2}, Reggeon-proton coupling lambda_{vpp} = 7.742, Reggeon-antiproton coupling lambda_{v anti-p anti-p} = 16.127 (from fits to pp and p anti-p data at medium energies, Liu 2022)
- Reggeon-neutron coupling constant lambda_{vnn} = 8.088 +/- 0.029 (determined in this work from fit to pn and anti-pn total cross section data at sqrt(s) >= 5 GeV)
- Differential cross section predictions are shown for sqrt(s) >= 10 GeV and 0.01 <= |t| <= 0.45 GeV^2
- At sqrt(s) = 50 GeV, the pn and anti-pn differential cross sections take almost the same values

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD approach previously applied to pp and p anti-p scattering (Liu 2022) to the pn and anti-pn channels, exploiting the universality of Pomeron and Reggeon parameters. The model uses the nucleon gravitational form factor from the bottom-up AdS/QCD model (Abidin 2009) and follows the Reggeization procedure detailed by Anderson (2016). The results enable predictions for anti-pn scattering where no experimental data currently exist, and the authors note that future high-energy forward scattering experiments will help constrain the model further.
