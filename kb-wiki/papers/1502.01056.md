# Analysis of pPb collisions at LHC energies in the relativistic diffusion model

> Particle production sources in pPb collisions at LHC energies are investigated in pseudorapidity space as functions of centrality. The nonequilibrium-statistical relativistic diffusion model (RDM) with three sources is applied to the analysis of preliminary charged-hadron distributions measured by the ATLAS and ALICE collaborations in pPb collisions at 5.02 TeV. The size of the midrapidity source relative to the fragmentation sources in pPb collisions is considered as function of centrality, and incident energy. At LHC energies particle production from the mid-rapidity gluon-gluon source is larger than that from the fragmentation sources at all centralities. Conclusions regarding the statistical equilibration process are drawn.

## Key claim
The relativistic diffusion model (RDM) with three sources can describe charged-hadron pseudorapidity distributions in pPb collisions at 5.02 TeV, but the model parameters are sensitive to the preliminary data sets used; the authors expect discrepancies between ATLAS and ALICE fits to vanish once more precise final data in a larger pseudorapidity range become available.

## Method
The authors applied the nonequilibrium-statistical relativistic diffusion model (RDM) based on a Fokker-Planck transport equation in rapidity space, using an incoherent superposition of three sources: two fragmentation sources (from valence quark-gluon interactions) and one midrapidity gluon-gluon source. They performed chi-squared minimizations of the analytical solutions against preliminary charged-hadron pseudorapidity distributions from the ATLAS and ALICE collaborations at 5.02 TeV, converting rapidity distributions to pseudorapidity space via a Jacobian transformation that accounts for effective transverse momentum. For centrality-dependent results, they required RDM parameters (mean values and widths) to be monotonic functions of centrality.

## Key numbers
- Beam rapidity for pPb at 5.02 TeV: y_beam = ±8.586
- For minimum-bias collisions at 5.02 TeV, the midrapidity source carries 49% of total particle content for ATLAS data and 35% for ALICE data
- For 0-5% central collisions at 5.02 TeV, the midrapidity gluon-gluon source has 58% relative charged-hadron content (ALICE data)
- For 60-80% centrality at 5.02 TeV, the midrapidity source has 78% relative charged-hadron content (ALICE data)
- Predicted total charged-hadron content in minimum-bias collisions: N_tot = 181 at 5.02 TeV, N_tot = 210 at 8.16 TeV
- For 0-5% central collisions, total particle content follows N_tot ∝ s_pN^0.38, fragmentation sources follow N_qg ∝ ln(s_pN), and gluon-gluon source follows N_gg ∝ ln^3(s_pN)
- Chi-squared per degrees of freedom for minimum-bias fits: 0.436/45 for ATLAS, 0.388/31 for ALICE

## Context
This work extends the RDM approach previously applied to symmetric systems at RHIC and LHC energies (Wolschin 2013) and to asymmetric dAu collisions at 200 GeV (Wolschin and Biro 2006). It provides predictions for future LHC pPb data at 8.16 TeV and shows that at LHC energies the midrapidity gluon-gluon source dominates over fragmentation sources at all centralities, unlike at RHIC energies where this only occurred for peripheral collisions. The analysis enables comparison with other theoretical approaches such as the statistical hadronization model, color glass condensate, and hydrodynamics.
