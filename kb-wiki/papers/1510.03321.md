# Thermal photon and dilepton production and electric charge transport in a baryon rich strongly coupled QGP from holography

> We obtain the thermal photon and dilepton production rates in a strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (QGP) at both zero and nonzero baryon chemical potential using a bottom-up Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton (EMD) holographic model that is in good quantitative agreement with the thermodynamics of $(2+1)$-flavor lattice QCD around the crossover transition for baryon chemical potentials up to 400 MeV, which may be reached in the beam energy scan (BES) at RHIC. We find that increasing the temperature $T$ and the baryon chemical potential $μ_B$ enhances the peak present in both spectra. We also obtain the electric charge susceptibility, the DC and AC electric conductivities and the electric charge diffusion as functions of $T$ and $μ_B$. We find that electric diffusive transport is suppressed as one increases $μ_B$. At zero baryon density, we compare our results for the DC electric conductivity and the electric charge diffusion with the latest lattice data available for these observables and find reasonable agreement around the crossover transition. Therefore, our holographic results may be used to constraint the magnitude of the thermal photon and dilepton production rates in a strongly coupled QGP, which we found to be at least one order of magnitude below perturbative estimates.

## Key claim

The authors use a bottom-up Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton (EMD) holographic model to compute thermal photon and dilepton production rates in a strongly coupled QGP, finding that these rates are at least one order of magnitude below perturbative estimates, with the caveat that the model is calibrated to match lattice QCD thermodynamics only for baryon chemical potentials up to 400 MeV and temperatures around the crossover transition.

## Method

The authors solve the equations of motion derived from a five-dimensional EMD action, using a black brane ansatz with a dilaton field and a Maxwell field whose boundary value gives the baryon chemical potential. They fix the dilaton potential and Maxwell-Dilaton coupling by fitting lattice QCD data for the speed of sound, normalized pressure, and baryon susceptibility at zero chemical potential. They then compute the retarded propagator of the electric current using flow equations from the gauge/gravity duality, obtaining the spectral functions for photons and dileptons, as well as electric charge transport coefficients, across the (T, μB) plane.

## Key numbers

- The holographic model matches lattice QCD thermodynamics for baryon chemical potentials up to 400 MeV, which is the maximum value reached in the beam energy scan at RHIC.
- The dimensionful scaling factor used to convert holographic results to physical units is Λ ≈ 831 MeV, obtained by matching the dip in the holographic speed of sound squared at μB = 0 with lattice results.
- The thermal photon and dilepton production rates from the holographic model are at least one order of magnitude below perturbative estimates.
- The gravitational constant is fixed as κ² = 8πG5 = 12.5.
- The dilaton potential parameters are V(φ) = -12 cosh(0.606 φ) + 0.703 φ² - 0.1 φ⁴ + 0.0034 φ⁶.

## Context

This work extends previous holographic calculations of electric charge transport at zero baryon chemical potential (Finazzo, 2013) to the full (T, μB) plane, using the same EMD model that was shown to quantitatively describe the equation of state and baryon susceptibilities (Rougemont, 2015). The results can be used as inputs for calculations of total photon and dilepton spectra in heavy ion collisions, such as those pursued by Dion (2011), Chatterjee (2013), Shen (2013), and Paquet (2015). The authors compare their zero-density results for DC electric conductivity and electric charge diffusion with the latest lattice data and find reasonable agreement around the crossover transition.
