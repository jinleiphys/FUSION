# Space-Time Picture of Fragmentation in PYTHIA/JETSET for HERMES and RHIC

> We examine the space-time evolution of (pre-)hadron production within the Lund string fragmentation model. The complete four-dimensional information of the string breaking vertices and the meeting points of the prehadron constituents are extracted for each single event in Monte Carlo simulations using the Jetset-part of Pythia. We discuss the implication on the deep inelastic lepton scattering experiments at HERMES as well as on observables in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC, using Pythia also for modeling the hard part of the interaction.

# Space-Time Picture of Fragmentation in PYTHIA/JETSET for HERMES and RHIC

## Key claim
The authors extract the full four-dimensional space-time coordinates of string breaking vertices and hadron formation points from PYTHIA/JETSET Monte Carlo simulations, showing that the widely-used assumption of multiplying a universal constant proper time by the Lorentz boost factor gamma gives misleading estimates for hadron production and formation points. The results are presented as a "proof of concept" and the authors caution that the semi-classical picture is applied to a quantum mechanical problem, so the results should not be overstressed.

## Method
The authors modified the JETSET routines within PYTHIA to extract the four-dimensional coordinates of string breaking vertices (production points) and yoyo-formation points (where prehadron constituents first meet) for each hadron in each event. They applied this to two scenarios: a virtual photon-nucleon reaction at HERMES kinematics (mean photon energy 14 GeV, mean Q^2 = 2.5 GeV^2, invariant mass W = 5 GeV) and proton-proton collisions at RHIC (sqrt(s) = 200 GeV) with mid-rapidity and high-pT cuts. The method accounts for complications including resolved photon interactions, gluon radiation, primordial transverse momenta, transverse momentum generation in string fragmentation, cluster decay, finite mass effects, quark flavor dependence, and diquark production.

## Key numbers
- String tension set to kappa = 1 GeV/fm throughout the analysis
- HERMES example: mean photon energy nu = 14 GeV, mean Q^2 = 2.5 GeV^2, invariant mass W = 5 GeV
- HERMES production and formation points lie mainly inside a radius of approximately 6 fm around the interaction point
- HERMES longitudinal coordinates of formation points range from approximately -0.4 fm to 14.5 fm in the lab frame
- HERMES average proper production times for pions: tau_1 = 0.24 fm, tau_2 = 0.81 fm; average proper formation time tau_F = 0.92 fm
- HERMES average lab-frame production times for pions: t_1 = 0.83 fm, t_2 = 3.49 fm; average lab-frame formation time t_F = 4.37 fm
- HERMES average Lorentz boost factor for pions: gamma = 13.53
- RHIC: sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, with cuts |y| < 0.5 and pT > 4 GeV
- RHIC average proper production times for pions: tau_1 = 0.36 fm, tau_2 = 0.81 fm; average proper formation time tau_F = 1.03 fm
- RHIC average lab-frame production times for pions: t_1 = 2.34 fm, t_2 = 8.19 fm; average lab-frame formation time t_F = 10.30 fm
- RHIC average Lorentz boost factor for pions: gamma = 51.04
- RHIC production points for high-pT mid-rapidity hadrons: most within a transverse distance below 2 fm
- Spurious points from faulty reconstruction in JETSET contribute less than 2% to total yield

## Context
This work extends the Lund string fragmentation model as implemented in PYTHIA/JETSET (Sjostrand, 1984) by providing the first quantitative four-dimensional space-time picture of hadron production in realistic ep and pp collisions. It builds on earlier analytic estimates by Bialas and collaborators and on the prehadron absorption model of Accardi, Kopeliovich, Falter, and others. The results are intended as input for transport simulations such as the BUU model (Falter) or the HSD model to determine the contribution of prehadronic final state interactions to hadron attenuation observed at HERMES and RHIC. The authors note that in heavy ion collisions with a deconfined QGP phase, the presented production times and lengths should be seen as lower estimates since the concept of hadronic strings becomes meaningless in that environment.
