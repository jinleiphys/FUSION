# The Critical Points of Strongly Coupled Lattice QCD at Nonzero Chemical Potential

> We study QCD at non-zero quark density, zero temperature, infinite coupling using the Glasgow algorithm. An improved complex zero analysis gives a critical point μ_c in agreement with that of chiral symmetry restoration computed with strong coupling expansions, and monomer-dimer simulations. We observe, however, two unphysical critical points: the onset for the number density μ_o, and μ_s the saturation threshold, coincident with pathological onsets observed in past quenched QCD calculations. An analysis of the probability distributions for particle number supports our physical interpretation of the critical point μ_c, and offers a new intepretation of μ_o, which confirms its unphysical nature. The perspectives for future lattice QCD calculations of the properties of dense baryonic matter are briefly discussed.

# The Critical Points of Strongly Coupled Lattice QCD at Nonzero Chemical Potential

## Key claim

The Glasgow algorithm applied to strongly coupled lattice QCD at zero temperature can identify the physical critical point for chiral symmetry restoration (μ_c), but it also produces two unphysical critical points (μ_o and μ_s) that persist even after ensemble averaging, meaning the method fails to fully suppress the pathologies seen in quenched calculations.

## Method

The authors used the Glasgow algorithm to compute the grand canonical partition function (GCPF) as an ensemble average of fermion determinants over gauge configurations generated at zero chemical potential on 6^4 and 8^4 lattices with 4 flavors of staggered fermions at infinite gauge coupling. They analyzed the number density (baryon current) as a function of chemical potential and examined the distribution of zeros of the GCPF in the complex fugacity plane, as well as probability distributions for particle number. Results were compared with monomer-dimer simulations and strong coupling expansions.

## Key numbers

- Critical chemical potential for chiral symmetry restoration: μ_c = 0.687(15) on a 6^4 lattice at quark mass m_q = 0.1, in agreement with monomer-dimer results (μ_c = 0.69(1))
- Strong coupling expansion predicts a first-order transition at μ = 0.65 for m_q = 0.1
- Quenched onset chemical potential μ_o = m_π/2 = 0.32 at m_q = 0.1
- Quenched saturation threshold μ_s ≈ m_B/3 ≈ 1.0 at m_q = 0.1
- Simulations performed on 6^4 lattices for bare masses from 0.05 to 1.5, and on 8^4 lattices for masses 0.08 and 0.1
- Number of configurations ranged from 25 (8^4, m_q = 0.08) to several hundred (6^4 lattices)

## Context

This work extends the Glasgow method of Barbour, Davies, and Sabeur (1988) to larger lattices and tests it against analytic strong coupling expansions by Damgaard, Hochberg, and Kawamoto (1985) and numerical monomer-dimer simulations by Karsch and Mutter (1989). The paper identifies a persistent discrepancy: the Glasgow method retains the unphysical onset at half the pion mass seen in quenched calculations by Barbour, Behilil, and Davies (1986) and by Kogut, Lombardo, and Sinclair (1995), whereas monomer-dimer simulations and strong coupling expansions show no such onset. The results suggest that reweighting from zero chemical potential may be insufficient, and point toward alternative approaches such as χQCD (with an added four-fermi term) as a promising direction for future finite-density lattice QCD calculations.
