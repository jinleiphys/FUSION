# Nucleon and Delta resonances in K Sigma(1385) photoproduction from nucleons

> The reaction mechanisms for $KΣ(1385)$ photoproduction from the reaction $γp \to K^+Σ^{0}(1385)$ in the resonance energy region are investigated in a hadronic model. Both contributions from $N$ and $Δ$ resonances of masses around 2 GeV as given in the Review of Particle Data Group and by the quark model predictions are included. The Lagrangians for describing the decays of these resonances into $KΣ(1385)$ are constructed with the coupling constants determined from the decay amplitudes predicted by a quark model. Comparing the resulting total cross section for the reaction $γp \to K^+Σ^{0}(1385)$ with the preliminary data from the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility, we find that the most important contributions are from the two-star rated resonances $Δ(2000) F_{35}$, $Δ(1940) D_{33}$, and $N(2080) D_{13}$, as well as the missing resonance $N\frac32^-(2095)$ predicted in the quark model. Predictions on the differential cross section and photon asymmetry in this reaction are also given.

## Key claim

The peak observed in the preliminary total cross section data for γp → K⁺Σ⁰(1385) requires the inclusion of resonance contributions, but the conclusion that most resonance contributions come from PDG-listed resonances depends on quark model predictions for their empirically unknown decay properties.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective Lagrangian model for γp → K⁺Σ⁰(1385) including t-channel K and K* exchanges, s-channel nucleon and Δ resonances, u-channel hyperon exchange, and a contact term required by gauge invariance. Coupling constants for resonance decays into KΣ(1385) and Nγ were determined by decomposing decay amplitudes according to relative orbital angular momentum and comparing with quark model predictions from Capstick and Roberts (1998) and Capstick (1992). Form factors were used at interaction vertices, with gauge invariance restored via a generalized contact current.

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant f_{KNΣ*} = -3.22, determined from the πNΔ coupling f_{πNΔ} = 2.23 via SU(3) flavor symmetry
- The missing resonance N(3/2)⁻(2095) was predicted to have a decay width Γ(N(3/2)⁻(2095) → KΣ(1385)) ≈ 60 MeV
- The radiative decay width Γ(Σ(1385) → Λγ) = 479 ± 120 keV, as measured by CLAS Collaboration
- The K* exchange contribution was only at the level of a few percent of other production mechanisms at Eγ = 3-4 GeV
- The cutoff parameters used: Λ_B = 1.0 GeV for s- and u-channel, Λ_M = 0.83 GeV for t-channel K exchange, Λ_B = 1.2 GeV for t-channel K* exchange
- All resonances were assumed to have a total decay width Γ_R = 300 MeV

## Context

This work extends the limited theoretical studies on KΣ(1385) photoproduction, where only single and double K-meson pole terms (Lutz and Soyeur, 2005) and the role of Δ(1700) near threshold (Doring et al., 2005) had been previously addressed. The model uses quark model predictions from Capstick and Roberts (1998) and Capstick (1992) to determine resonance couplings, and compares with preliminary CLAS data from 2006. The results enable future experimental studies to constrain the properties of poorly-known PDG resonances and search for missing resonances predicted by quark models, particularly through measurements of differential cross sections and photon asymmetry.
