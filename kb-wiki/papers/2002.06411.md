# Interplaying mechanisms behind inclusive jet $R_{AA}$ and extraction of jet energy loss distributions

> The observed inclusive jet suppression in heavy-ion collisions at LHC has a very weak $p_{T}$ dependence over a large range of $p_{T}$ = 50-1000 GeV and is almost independent of the colliding energy, though the initial energy density of the bulk medium has increased from $\sqrt{s}$ = 2.76 to 5.02 TeV by about 20%. This interesting phenomenon is investigated in the linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) model for jet propagation in an event-by-event 3+1D hydro background. We show that the $p_{T}$ dependence of jet $R_{AA}$ is determined by the initial spectrum in $p+p$ collisions and $ p_{T} $ dependence of jet energy loss. Furthermore, jet energy loss distributions for inclusive jet and $ γ-$jet at both LHC energies are extracted directly from experimental data through the state-of-art Bayesian analysis. The averaged jet energy loss has a weak $p_{T}$ dependence and the scaled jet energy loss distributions have a large width, both of which are consistent with the LBT simulations and indicate that jet quenching is caused by only a few out-of-cone jet medium scatterings.

## Key claim

The weak transverse momentum (p_T) dependence and near-independence of colliding energy of inclusive jet suppression R_AA in heavy-ion collisions at LHC is shown to arise from a competition between the initial jet production spectrum in p+p collisions and the p_T dependence of jet energy loss, rather than from a simple scaling of medium density.

## Method

The authors use the Linear Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model to simulate jet propagation through an event-by-event (3+1)D hydrodynamic medium generated by the CLVisc model, with initial jet shower partons from PYTHIA 8. They then perform a Bayesian analysis to extract jet energy loss distributions directly from experimental data on inclusive jet and gamma-jet suppression, parameterizing the scaled energy loss distribution as a Gamma function and the average energy loss as a power-law modulated logarithmic function of p_T.

## Key numbers

* The initial energy density of the bulk medium increases from sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV to 5.02 TeV by about 20%.
* The inclusive jet suppression is studied over p_T = 50-1000 GeV.
* There is an 18% enhancement of the averaged jet energy loss at the higher colliding energy (5.02 TeV) compared to 2.76 TeV.
* The effective strong coupling constant alpha_S in the LBT model is set to 0.15 by fitting to experimental data.
* For inclusive jet in Pb+Pb at (0-10%) centrality at 2.76 TeV, the extracted Gamma distribution shape parameter alpha = 3.87 +/- 2.93 (LBT: 1.45 +/- 0.01), beta = 1.40 +/- 1.12 (LBT: 1.39 +/- 0.06), gamma = 0.21 +/- 0.09 (LBT: 0.21 +/- 0.01).
* For inclusive jet in Pb+Pb at (0-10%) centrality at 5.02 TeV, alpha = 4.41 +/- 2.86 (LBT: 1.58 +/- 0.02), beta = 1.06 +/- 0.97 (LBT: 1.56 +/- 0.06), gamma = 0.26 +/- 0.06 (LBT: 0.23 +/- 0.01).

## Context

This work extends the LBT model studies of He et al. (2019) and the JET Collaboration (2014) extraction of jet transport coefficient by instead extracting the full jet energy loss distribution. It explains the puzzling weak p_T and energy dependence of jet R_AA observed by ATLAS (2015, 2019) and CMS (2017) at LHC, showing that the flatter initial spectrum at 5.02 TeV cancels the larger energy loss. The extracted distributions indicate that jet quenching results from only a few out-of-cone jet-medium scatterings, enabling future studies of the microscopic mechanism of parton energy loss.
