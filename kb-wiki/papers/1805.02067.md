# Diffusion-model analysis of pPb and PbPb collisions at LHC energies

> We present an analysis of centrality-dependent pseudorapidity distributions of produced charged hadrons in pPb and PbPb collisions at the LHC energy of sqrt{s_NN}=5.02 TeV, and of minimum-bias pPb collisions at 8.16 TeV within the nonequilibrium-statistical relativistic diffusion model (RDM). In a three-source approach, the role of the fragmentation sources is emphasized. Together with the Jacobian transformation from rapidity to pseudorapidity and the limiting fragmentation conjecture, these are essential for modeling the centrality dependence. For central PbPb collisions, a prediction at the projected FCC energy of sqrt{s_NN}=39 TeV is made.

## Key claim

The relativistic diffusion model (RDM) with three sources (two fragmentation sources and one midrapidity gluon-gluon source) can reproduce centrality-dependent pseudorapidity distributions of charged hadrons in pPb and PbPb collisions at LHC energies, but the fragmentation region data are supplemented by the limiting fragmentation conjecture using lower-energy RHIC data because LHC data in the tails are missing.

## Method

The authors solved a linear Fokker-Planck equation with a relaxation drift term to obtain rapidity distributions for three sources (two fragmentation sources from valence quark-gluon interactions and one central source from low-x gluon interactions), then transformed these to pseudorapidity space using a Jacobian transformation that accounts for the pion mass and an effective mean transverse momentum. Model parameters were determined through chi-squared minimizations against ALICE and CMS data for pPb at 5.02 TeV and 8.16 TeV and PbPb at 5.02 TeV, with the fragmentation region of PbPb supplemented by 200 GeV AuAu data shifted by the beam rapidity difference of 3.23 units.

## Key numbers

* Minimum-bias pPb at 8.16 TeV: total charged hadrons N_ch^tot = 270, compared to 211 at 5.02 TeV
* Central PbPb at 5.02 TeV (0-5% centrality): midrapidity density dN/dη|_(η≈0) = 1929 ± 46, with fragmentation sources contributing N_ch^(1+2) = 16896 and gluon-gluon source N_ch^gg = 21318
* Central PbPb at 5.02 TeV: mean peak positions of fragmentation sources ⟨y_(1,2)⟩ = ±4.44, FWHM of fragmentation sources Γ_(1,2) = 4.02, FWHM of gluon-gluon source Γ_gg = 7.38
* pPb at 5.02 TeV (0-5% centrality): equilibrium value y_eq = -0.780, fragmentation source particle numbers N_ch^1 = 32 (p-going) and N_ch^2 = 495 (Pb-going), gluon-gluon source N_ch^gg = 158
* Beam rapidity for 5.02 TeV collisions: y_beam = ±8.586; for 8.16 TeV: y_beam = ±9.071
* Predicted beam rapidity for FCC at sqrt(s_NN) = 39 TeV: y_beam = ±10.33

## Context

This work extends the relativistic diffusion model approach previously applied by the same group to AuAu and PbPb at lower energies (Wolschin 1999, 2013) and to the initial Pbp pilot run (Schulz and Wolschin 2015). It contrasts with equilibrium statistical models (Fermi 1950, Hagedorn 1968, Braun-Munzinger et al. 2008) that are more suitable for hadron production rates than distribution functions, and with three-fluid hydrodynamic calculations (Toneev 2006, Ivanov 2016) that have not yet been performed at LHC energies. The results enable predictions for central PbPb collisions at the projected FCC energy of 39 TeV and provide a framework for interpreting future LHC pPb data in the pseudorapidity region 2 < |η| < 5.
