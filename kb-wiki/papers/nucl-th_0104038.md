# The importance of the nucleon-nucleon correlations for the eta alpha S-wave scattering length, and the pi-eta mixing angle in the low-energy eta alpha scattering length model

> Using the new set of dd --> eta alpha near threshold experimental data, the estimate of the importance of the nucleon-nucleon correlations for the eta alpha S-wave scattering length in the multiple scattering theory is obtained using the low-energy scattering length model. The contribution turns out to be much bigger then previously believed. The pi-eta mixing angle is extracted using the experimental data on the dd --> eta alpha and dd --> pi alpha processes. The model is dominated by the subthreshold extrapolation recipe for the eta alpha scattering amplitudes. When the recipe is chosen the model is completely insensitive to the eta alpha parameters for the subthreshold value of the eta cm momentum of p_{eta}^2 = -(0.46)^2 fm^{-2}. Provided that the subthreshold extrapolation recipe is correct, a good estimate of the pi-eta mixing angle is obtained, if the experimental cross sections for the dd --> pi alpha reaction at the corresponding deuteron input energy are taken from the literature.

## Key claim

The nucleon-nucleon correlation contribution to the eta-alpha S-wave scattering length in multiple-scattering theory is much larger than previously estimated by Wil94, but the extracted pi-eta mixing angle is almost completely insensitive to the eta-alpha scattering length parameters and depends instead on the subthreshold extrapolation recipe for the eta-alpha scattering amplitudes.

## Method

The authors used the low-energy scattering length model to parametrize the eta-alpha S-wave scattering length in terms of the eta-nucleon S-wave scattering length and a nucleon-nucleon correlation factor beta, following the multiple-scattering formalism of Wil94. They fitted the model to experimental dd -> eta alpha near-threshold cross section data from Fra94 and Wil97, normalizing the squared scattering amplitude to 27.0 nb/sr at an eta center-of-mass momentum of 0.15 fm^-1, and varied beta to find the best agreement. The pi-eta mixing angle was then extracted by extrapolating the eta amplitude to the subthreshold momentum p_eta^2 = -(0.46)^2 fm^-2 and combining it with the measured dd -> pi^0 alpha differential cross section from Gol91.

## Key numbers

* Best-fit nucleon-nucleon correlation factor: beta = 0.56 fm^-1, much larger than the theoretical estimate of 0.28 fm^-1 from Wil94.
* Corresponding eta-alpha S-wave scattering length: a_eta_alpha = (-2.88 + i 0.71) fm, compared to (0.06 + i 6.02) fm from Wil94 and (-2.2 + i 1.1) fm from Wil97.
* Extracted pi-eta mixing angle: theta = 0.986 degrees, with mixing parameter lambda = 0.017, using beta = 0.56 fm^-1 and a_eta_N = (0.72 + i 0.26) fm.
* Measured dd -> pi^0 alpha differential cross section at deuteron kinetic energy T_d = 1.100 GeV: d sigma / d Omega = (1.00 +/- 0.25) pb/sr at center-of-mass angle 73 degrees.
* The squared eta-alpha amplitude at the subthreshold point p_eta^2 = -(0.46)^2 fm^-2 is nearly independent of beta, with the model insensitive to eta-alpha parameters at that momentum.

## Context

This work extends the multiple-scattering treatment of eta-alpha scattering by Wil94, using newer dd -> eta alpha experimental data from Fra94 and Wil97 to show that the nucleon-nucleon correlation contribution is significantly larger than the theoretical estimate of 0.28 fm^-1 given in Wil94. The extracted eta-alpha scattering length agrees with the direct fit result of Willis et al. (Wil97) rather than the earlier Wil94 value. The pi-eta mixing angle extraction follows the formalism of Wil94 but demonstrates that the result is dominated by the subthreshold extrapolation recipe, not the eta-alpha interaction details; the authors note that improving the dd -> pi^0 alpha measurement or approaching the eta production threshold more closely could refine the mixing angle, which they bound as no higher than 2.204 degrees even with a factor-5 systematic error.
