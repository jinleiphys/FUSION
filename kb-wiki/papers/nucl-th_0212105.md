# Radiative corrections and parity-violating electron-nucleon scattering

> Radiative corrections to the parity-violating asymmetry measured in elastic electron-proton scattering are analyzed in the framework of the Standard Model. We include the complete set of one-loop contributions to one quark current amplitudes. The contribution of soft photon emission to the asymmetry is also calculated, giving final results free of infrared divergences. The one quark radiative corrections, when combined with previous work on many quark effects and recent SAMPLE experimental data, are used to place some new constraints on electroweak form factors of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper provides a complete set of one-loop electroweak radiative corrections to parity-violating electron-nucleon scattering, but the results are restricted to one-quark contributions only, with many-quark effects (such as the anapole moment) excluded from the calculation. The infrared-divergent contributions are cancelled by including soft photon bremsstrahlung, though hard photon effects and the simultaneous treatment of QED and weak corrections are deferred to future work.

## Method

The authors model the nucleon as a collection of quasi-free quarks carrying a fraction of the nucleon four-momentum and calculate the complete set of one-loop Feynman diagrams (438 total) for electron-quark scattering using the FeynArts, FormCalc, and LoopTools packages. The one-loop amplitudes are renormalized in the On Shell Renormalization scheme in the 't Hooft-Feynman gauge, and infrared divergences from photon exchange are cancelled by adding soft-photon bremsstrahlung contributions. The resulting infrared-free radiative correction factors are computed separately for the vector and axial-vector current interactions for u, d, and s quarks, then combined into isoscalar, isovector, and isosinglet hadronic radiative corrections.

## Key numbers

* Standard Model parameters used: m_u = 47 MeV, m_d = 47 MeV, m_s = 150 MeV, M_Z = 91.1882 GeV, M_W = 80.419 GeV, M_H = 100 GeV, m_t = 174.3 GeV
* SAMPLE kinematics: E_e,lab = 194 MeV, average backward angle theta = 146.2 degrees
* The isovector axial form factor at Q^2=0: G_A^{T=1}(0) = g_A = 1.2670 +/- 0.0035 from neutron beta decay
* The SU(3) isoscalar octet form factor: G_A^{(8)} = 0.169 +/- 0.009 from hyperon beta decay fits
* The axial strange form factor at Q^2=0: G_A^s(0) = -0.086 +/- 0.024 from polarized deep inelastic scattering; taken as G_A^s(Q^2=0.1) = -0.086 +/- 0.086 in the analysis
* The strange magnetic moment at Q^2=0: G_M^s(0) = mu_s (undetermined)
* The asymmetry ratio is of order 10^{-6} at low energies

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of electroweak radiative corrections to parity-violating electron-nucleon scattering by Musolf (1989, 1994) and others, providing a complete automated calculation of all one-loop diagrams including momentum-dependent amplitudes. The results, when combined with the many-quark effects calculated previously and the SAMPLE experimental data, enable new constraints on the strange quark contributions to nucleon electroweak form factors, including the strange magnetic moment, strangeness charge radius, and the strangeness axial charge. The authors note that a more complete treatment would require including hard photon bremsstrahlung matched to specific detector setups and the simultaneous treatment of QED and weak radiative corrections.
