# Tomography of exotic hadrons in high-energy exclusive processes

> We investigated the possibility of determining internal structure of exotic hadrons by using high-energy reaction processes, where quarks and gluons are appropriate degrees of freedom. In particular, it should be valuable to investigate the high-energy exclusive processes which include generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs). The GPDs and GDAs contain momentum distributions of partons and form factors. We found that the exotic nature appears in momentum distributions of quarks as suggested by the constituent-counting rule and in the form factors associated with exotic hadron sizes and the number of constituents. Transition GPDs to exotic hadrons could probe such exotic signatures. We also propose that these exotic signatures should be found in exclusive production processes of exotic hadrons from gamma^* gamma in electron-positron annihilation. For example, the GDAs contain information on a time-like form factor of the energy-momentum tensor of a hadron h. We show that the cross section of e gamma -> e+hbar h is sensitive to the exotic signature by looking at the hbar h invariant-mass dependence by taking light hadrons, h=f_0 (980) and a_0 (980). From such GDA measurements, the tomography of exotic hadrons becomes possible, for example, by Belle and BaBar experiments and by future linear collider.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the internal quark-gluon configuration of exotic hadron candidates can be determined by measuring generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) in high-energy exclusive processes, specifically through the invariant-mass dependence of the cross section for \(e \gamma \to e h \bar h\), but they note that this approach relies on the leading-twist approximation and neglects higher-twist and higher-order QCD corrections, which would be needed for detailed comparison with experiment.

## Method

The authors derive the cross section for the exclusive process \(e \gamma \to e h \bar h\) in the kinematical region where the virtual photon satisfies \(Q^2 \gg \Lambda^2\) and the final hadron pair invariant mass \(W^2\) is much smaller than \(Q^2\), allowing factorization into a hard part (photon-quark interactions) and a soft part described by quark GDAs. They express the hadron tensor in leading-twist and leading-order \(\alpha_s\) approximation, obtaining the cross section in terms of the helicity amplitude \(A_{++}\), which depends on the quark GDA \(\Phi_q^{h \bar h}(z,\zeta,W^2)\). The GDAs are related to generalized parton distributions (GPDs) via \(s\)-\(t\) crossing, but the authors note that this correspondence maps to the unphysical region of the GPDs (\(|\xi| \ge 1\), \(t \ge 0\)), making direct estimation nontrivial.

## Key numbers

* The cross section for \(e \gamma \to e h \bar h\) is given by \(\frac{d\sigma}{dQ^2 dW^2 d\cos\theta} = \frac{\beta \alpha^3}{8 s_{e\gamma}^2 Q^2 (1-\varepsilon)} |A_{++}(\zeta,W^2)|^2\), where \(\beta = \sqrt{1-4m_h^2/W^2}\), \(\varepsilon = (1-y)/(1-y+y^2/2)\), and \(y = (Q^2+W^2)/s_{e\gamma}\).
* The variable \(\zeta\) is related to the scattering angle \(\theta\) in the hadron-pair center-of-mass frame by \(\zeta = (1+\beta\cos\theta)/2\).
* The analysis focuses on light hadron candidates \(h = f_0(980)\) and \(a_0(980)\) with masses around 1 GeV, where the invariant-mass dependence of the cross section is proposed as a probe of exotic signatures.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous proposal (Kawamura, Kumano, Sekihara 2013) to use the constituent-counting rule in hard exclusive processes for determining the number of active constituents in exotic hadrons like \(\Lambda(1405)\). It also builds on the broader field of hadron tomography, where GPDs and GDAs have been developed for nucleon structure studies (Diehl 2000, Mueller 1994). The paper enables future experimental searches at Belle, BaBar, and future linear colliders by providing a theoretical framework to distinguish tetra-quark or molecular configurations of \(f_0(980)\) and \(a_0(980)\) from ordinary meson interpretations through high-energy exclusive measurements.
