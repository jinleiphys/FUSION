# Meson-meson scattering: $K\overline{K}$-thresholds and $f_{0}(980) - a_{0}(980)$ mixing

> We study the influence of mass splitting between the charged and neutral pions and kaons in the Juelich meson exchange model for $ππ$ and $πη$ scattering. The calculations are performed in the particle basis, which permits the use of physical masses for the pseudoscalar mesons and a study of the distinct thresholds associated with the neutral and the charged kaons. Within this model we also investigate the isospin violation which arises from the mass splitting and an apparent violation of G-parity in $ππ$ scattering which stems from the coupling to the $K\overline{K}$ channel. Nonvanishing cross sections for $ππ\rightarrow π^{0}η$ indicate a mixing of the $f_{0}(980)$ and $a_{0}(980)$ states.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the mass splitting between charged and neutral kaons, when incorporated into the Jülich meson exchange model, produces a nonvanishing cross section for $\pi\pi \rightarrow \pi^{0}\eta$ which indicates mixing of the $f_{0}(980)$ and $a_{0}(980)$ states; this mixing arises from the coupling to the $K\overline{K}$ channel, which does not have a definite G-parity, and is enhanced in the region of the $K\overline{K}$-molecule resonance.

## Method

The authors reformulated the Jülich meson exchange model for $\pi\pi$ and $\pi\eta$ scattering from an isospin basis to a particle basis, allowing the use of physical masses for charged and neutral pseudoscalar mesons. They solved a coupled-channel Blankenbecler-Sugar reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for five channels ($\pi^{0}\pi^{0}$, $\pi^{+}\pi^{-}$, $\pi^{0}\eta$, $K^{+}K^{-}$, $K^{0}\overline{K^{0}}$) with S-wave interactions, including t-/u-channel $\rho$, $K^{*}(892)$, $\omega$, and $\phi$ exchanges as well as s-channel pole graphs for $\rho$, $f_{2}(1270)$, and $\epsilon(1200-1400)$. Pole positions of the scattering amplitude were determined in the complex energy plane using a five-sheet notation corresponding to the distinct thresholds.

## Key numbers

- The $f_{0}(980)$ bound state poles are found at energies $[bbbtt] (1014.1,\pm14.1)$ MeV and $[bbttt] (1014.2,\pm13.9)$ MeV in the complex energy plane.
- When the $\pi^{0}\eta$ channel is neglected, the two poles move to the same energy $(1014.2,\pm13.8)$ MeV on both sheets $[bbbtt]$ and $[bbttt]$.
- Removing the $\epsilon$ s-channel contribution moves the poles to $[bbtt] (1020.0,\pm59.4)$ MeV (with no $\pi^{0}\eta$ channel included).
- Decoupling the $\pi\pi$ channel moves the pole to $[xytt] (986.8,\pm0.0)$ MeV ($x,y=b,t$), indicating a $K\overline{K}$ molecule.

## Context

This work extends the Jülich meson exchange model of Janssen et al. (1995) by incorporating mass splitting between charged and neutral kaons and pions, and by including the $\pi\eta$ channel. The results confirm the earlier finding that the $f_{0}(980)$ is a $K\overline{K}$ molecule rather than a genuine $q\overline{q}$ state, as proposed by Weinstein (1990). The predicted two-kink structure in $\pi\pi$ cross sections near the $K\overline{K}$ thresholds is directly testable at the cooler synchrotron COSY, and the finding that the $f_{0}(980)$ can decay into $\pi^{0}\eta$ challenges the conventional interpretation of the $a_{0}(980)$ as a distinct state, suggesting that observed $\pi^{0}\eta$ decays may originate from the $f_{0}(980)$ instead.
