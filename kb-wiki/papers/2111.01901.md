# A $γ$-rigid solution of the Bohr Hamiltonian with deformation-dependent mass term for Kratzer potential and $γ= 30^\circ$

> In this work, the Davydov-Chaban Hamiltonian, describing the collective motion of $γ$-rigid atomic nuclei, is amended by allowing the mass parameter to depend on the nuclear deformation. Further, Z(4)-DDM (Deformation-Dependent Mass) model is proposed by considering the Kratzer potential for the $β$ variable, and solving the problem by techniques of asymptotic iteration method (AIM). The results of the calculated spectra and $B(E2)$ transition rates for series of $^{192-196}$Pt isotopes are compared with the corresponding experimental data as well as with other theoretical models. Exact analytical expressions are derived for spectra and normalized wave functions of the Kratzer potential. The obtained results show an overall good agreement with the experimental data and an important improvement in respect to other models

## Key claim

The Davydov-Chaban Hamiltonian with a deformation-dependent mass term (Z(4)-DDM model) using the Kratzer potential for the β variable yields improved agreement with experimental energy spectra and B(E2) transition rates for 192-196Pt isotopes compared to models with constant mass, though the improvement is qualified by some discrepancies in higher-spin ground-state band transitions and only partial agreement for β-band to ground-state interband transitions.

## Method

The authors modified the Davydov-Chaban Hamiltonian by allowing the mass parameter B(β) to depend on deformation via B(β) = B₀/f(β)² with f(β) = 1 + aβ, where a is a small parameter. They fixed γ = 30° (π/6) and used the Kratzer potential v(β) = -1/β + β₀/(2β²) for the β variable. The resulting radial Schrödinger equation was solved analytically using the asymptotic iteration method (AIM), yielding closed-form expressions for energy spectra and normalized wave functions. The model parameters a and β₀ were fitted to experimental data for each isotope using least-squares minimization.

## Key numbers

- For 192Pt: rms deviation σ = 0.500 with a = 0.002 and β₀ = 50.0, compared to σ = 0.614 (Buganu 2015) and σ = 0.593 (Budaca 2016)
- For 194Pt: rms deviation σ = 0.390 with a = 0.004 and β₀ = 70.6, compared to σ = 0.543 (Buganu 2015) and σ = 0.515 (Budaca 2016)
- For 196Pt: rms deviation σ = 0.576 with a = 0.006 and β₀ = 51.0, compared to σ = 0.682 (Buganu 2015) and σ = 0.683 (Budaca 2016)
- Ground-state band ratios R₀,₀,₄ (energy of 4⁺ state relative to 2⁺ state): 2.451 (192Pt), 2.506 (194Pt), 2.455 (196Pt) compared to experimental values 2.479, 2.470, 2.465 respectively
- B(E2) ratio 4g→2g / 2g→0g: 1.58 (192Pt) vs experimental 1.56(12); 1.56 (194Pt) vs 1.73(13); 1.63 (196Pt) vs 1.48(3)

## Context

This work extends the Z(4) model of Bonatsos (2005) by incorporating deformation-dependent mass formalism, building on prior DDMF approaches by Bonatsos (2010, 2011, 2013) and Chabab (2016). The Kratzer potential replaces the sextic potential used in earlier Z(4) models by Buganu (2015) and Budaca (2016), where mass was treated as constant. The model enables improved description of triaxial nuclei with γ-rigidity at 30°, particularly for platinum isotopes, and provides analytical expressions that can be applied to other mass regions.
