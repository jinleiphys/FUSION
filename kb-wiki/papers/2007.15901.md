# Counting Nambu-Goldstone modes of higher-form global symmetries

> We discuss the counting of Nambu-Goldstone (NG) modes associated with the spontaneous breaking of higher-form global symmetries. Effective field theories of NG modes are developed based on symmetry breaking patterns, using a generalized coset construction for higher-form symmetries. We derive a formula of the number of gapless NG modes, which involves expectation values of the commutators of conserved charges, possibly of different degrees.

## Key claim

The paper derives a generalized counting formula for Nambu-Goldstone (NG) modes associated with spontaneous breaking of higher-form global symmetries, which reduces to the known formula for ordinary (0-form) symmetries when all symmetries are 0-form. The counting depends on expectation values of commutators of conserved charges, which may be of different degrees, and the formula applies to systems that are not necessarily Lorentz-invariant.

## Method

The authors develop effective field theories for spontaneously broken higher-form symmetries using a generalized coset construction, where the Maurer-Cartan form is replaced by a closed (p+1)-form for a p-form symmetry. They derive the counting formula by analyzing the equations of motion and constraints in the linearized theory, identifying that the number of gapless modes equals the number of modes in the Lorentz-invariant case minus half the rank of a matrix M_αβ constructed from charge commutator expectation values. The derivation uses the Lagrangian L = L_0 + L_1, where L_0 contains the standard kinetic terms and L_1 contains one-derivative Wess-Zumino-like terms that encode the effects of Lorentz symmetry breaking.

## Key numbers

* For a broken p_A-form symmetry in D-dimensional Lorentz-invariant spacetime, the number of gapless modes per generator is given by the binomial coefficient (D-2 choose p_A), e.g., for D=4 and p_A=1 this gives 2 modes (two photons).
* In the example of photons under a constant θ gradient in (3+1) dimensions, the rank of the charge commutator matrix M_ij is 2, leading to N_NG = 2 - (1/2)×2 = 1 gapless mode.
* The gap squared of the gapped mode in the θ-gradient example is ω²(k=0) = C², where C = |C| = |-c e² ∇θ|.
* The dispersion relation of the gapless mode in the θ-gradient example is ω² = sin²φ k² + (cos⁴φ/C²) k⁴ + O(k⁶) at small k, where φ is the angle between momentum k and the gradient vector C.

## Context

This work extends the counting formula for NG modes in 0-form symmetries, developed by Watanabe (2011, 2012), Hidaka (2012), and others, to the case of higher-form symmetries as defined by Gaiotto, Kapustin, Seiberg, and Willett (2015). The generalized coset construction builds on the standard coset construction of Coleman, Callan, Wess, and Zumino (1969) and its extensions to spacetime symmetries. The results enable systematic counting of gapless modes in systems with spontaneously broken higher-form symmetries, such as the photon as the NG mode of a U(1) 1-form symmetry, and can be applied to condensed matter systems and magnetohydrodynamics.
