# Gamow-Teller transition strengths for selected ${fp}$ shell nuclei

> We have reported a systematic shell model description of the experimental Gamow-Teller transition strength for $^{44}$Sc $\rightarrow$ $^{44}$Ca, $^{45}$Ti $\rightarrow$ $^{45}$Sc, $^{48}$Ti $\rightarrow$ $^{48}$V, $^{66}$Co $\rightarrow$ $^{66}$Ni, and $^{66}$Fe $\rightarrow$ $^{66}$Co transitions using KB3G and GXPF1A interactions for $fp$ model space. In order to see the importance of higher orbital for $^{66}$Co $\rightarrow$ $^{66}$Ni and $^{66}$Fe $\rightarrow$ $^{66}$Co transitions, we have reported the shell model results with $fpg_{9/2}$ space using GXPF1Br+$V_{MU}$ interaction. We have obtained the qualitative agreement for the individual transitions, while the calculated summed transition strengths closely reproduce the observed ones.

## Key claim
The paper reports that shell-model calculations using KB3G and GXPF1A interactions in the fp model space achieve qualitative agreement for individual Gamow-Teller transitions, while the calculated summed transition strengths closely reproduce the observed ones, but only for the specific set of five transitions studied.

## Method
Shell-model calculations were performed in the fp model space using KB3G and GXPF1A effective interactions for five transitions: 44Sc → 44Ca, 45Ti → 45Sc, 48Ti → 48V, 66Co → 66Ni, and 66Fe → 66Co. For the two transitions involving mass 66 nuclei, additional calculations were performed in the fpg9/2 model space using the GXPF1Br+VMU interaction, with a truncation allowing maximum 2 neutrons in the g9/2 orbital and fixing minimum six particles in the f7/2 orbital. All B(GT) values were quenched by a factor q=0.66, and calculations were performed using the NuShellX@MSU code.

## Key numbers
- For 44Sc → 44Ca: experimental summed B(GT) up to 3.301 MeV is 0.046; GXPF1A gives summed B(GT) of 19.204 and KB3G gives 16.883 (unquenched values, up to higher excitation energies)
- For 45Ti → 45Sc: experimental summed B(GT) up to 1.662 MeV is 0.117; GXPF1A gives 10.022 and KB3G gives 9.384 (unquenched, up to higher excitation energies)
- For 48Ti → 48V: experimental summed B(GT) up to 12.646 MeV is 3.759; GXPF1A gives 13.048 and KB3G gives 12.983 (unquenched)
- For 66Co → 66Ni: experimental summed B(GT) up to 3.752 MeV is 0.231; GXPF1A gives 15.506, KB3G gives 19.540, and GXPF1Br+VMU gives 18.730 (unquenched)
- For 66Fe → 66Co: experimental summed B(GT) up to 2.236 MeV is 0.337; GXPF1A gives 13.546, KB3G gives 17.880, and GXPF1Br+VMU gives 13.638 (unquenched)
- The quenching factor applied to all calculated B(GT) values is q=0.66

## Context
This work extends systematic shell-model descriptions of Gamow-Teller strengths in fp-shell nuclei, building on prior experimental measurements from beta-decay and charge-exchange reactions such as the 48Ti(3He,t)48V reaction reported by Ganioglu et al. and beta-decay data for 66Co and 66Fe from Stryjczyk et al. The results provide theoretical predictions for GT strength distributions at higher excitation energies that are not yet experimentally accessible, which may guide future experiments and serve as input for electron-capture reaction rate calculations relevant to core-collapse supernovae and neutron star crust processes.
