# Fast Traveling-Wave Reactor of the Channel Type

> The main aim of this paper is to solve the technological problems of the TWR based on the technical concept described in our priority of invention reference, which makes it impossible, in particular, for the fuel claddings damaging doses of fast neutrons to excess the ~200 dpa limit. Thus the essence of the technical concept is to provide a given neutron flux at the fuel claddings by setting the appropriate speed of the fuel motion relative to the nuclear burning wave. The basic design of the fast uranium-plutonium nuclear traveling-wave reactor with a softened neutron spectrum is developed, which solves the problem of the radiation resistance of the fuel claddings material.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the radiation damage problem for fuel cladding materials in traveling-wave reactors (TWRs) can be solved by moving the nuclear fuel relative to the fixed fuel rod shells at a speed approximately ten times greater than the nuclear burning wave speed, thereby keeping the fast neutron damaging dose below the ~200 dpa limit.

## Method
The authors developed a conceptual design for a fast uranium-plutonium traveling-wave reactor of the channel type with a softened neutron spectrum. They derived analytical relations linking the fuel movement speed, active zone length, burning wave width, and neutron flux to the radiation damage dose on fuel cladding materials. Estimates were made for two variants: one without and one with an absorbing moderator layer between fuel and cladding, using parameters from prior numerical simulations of wave burning modes.

## Key numbers
- The damaging dose for fuel claddings in TWRs may reach ~500 dpa, while currently available reactor metals can withstand only 100–200 dpa.
- For a neutron flux density of ~10^17 neutrons/cm^2·s, the estimated metal campaign time in TWR is ~4.5 × 10^5 s (~0.72 years).
- The required fuel movement speed relative to the fixed cladding is v_az ~ 2.31 × 10^-4 cm/s, ten times the nuclear burning wave speed v_nucl.burn. ~ 2.31 × 10^-5 cm/s.
- For the variant with a moderator layer reducing flux to ~5 × 10^16 neutrons/cm^2·s, the required fuel movement speed is v_az ~ 1.16 × 10^-4 cm/s, with active zone length l_az ~ 25 m (compared to 50 m without moderator).
- Proposed geometrical parameters for a long-campaign reactor: fuel length l_fuel ~ 5 m, active zone length l_az ~ 25–50 m, hull diameter ~20–30 m, height ~30–60 m.

## Context
This work extends prior TWR concepts by Sekimoto (2000s), TerraPower (2010s), and others (Fomin, Chen, Pavlovich) by directly addressing the radiation resistance problem that those designs left unsolved. The authors note that in Table 1 of their paper, none of the simulated wave burning modes from prior work (Sekimoto, Rusov, Pavlovich, Fomin, Chen, TerraPower) provide acceptable radiation resistance for fuel cladding materials, with the ratio n_dpa/200 ranging from 1.75 to 62. The proposed channel-type design with moving fuel enables future experimental testing of burning wave kinetics and could allow burning of spent nuclear fuel in large amounts.
