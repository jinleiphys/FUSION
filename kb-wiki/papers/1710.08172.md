# Microscopic core-quasiparticle coupling model for spectroscopy of odd-mass nuclei

> Predictions of the spectroscopic properties of low-lying states are critical for nuclear structure studies, but are problematic for nuclei with an odd nucleon due to the interplay of the unpaired single particle with nuclear collective degrees of freedom. In this work, a microscopic core-quasiparticle coupling (CQC) model based on the covariant density functional theory is developed that contains the collective excitations of even-mass cores and spherical single-particle states of the odd nucleon as calculated from a quadrupole collective Hamiltonian combined with a constrained triaxial relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model. Predictions of the new model for excitation energies, kinematic and dynamic moments of inertia, and transition rates are shown to be in good agreement with results of low-lying spectroscopy measurements of the axially deformed odd-proton nucleus $^{159}$Tb and the odd-neutron nucleus $^{157}$Gd. Future studies with additional nuclei are planned.

## Key claim

The paper develops a microscopic core-quasiparticle coupling (CQC) model based on covariant density functional theory, where the Fermi surface and coupling strength are left as free parameters fit separately to positive- and negative-parity states, and demonstrates good agreement with low-lying spectroscopy measurements for two axially deformed nuclei, with future studies planned for additional nuclei.

## Method

The model couples a quadrupole collective Hamiltonian (describing the even-mass core's collective excitations) with spherical single-particle states of the odd nucleon, both calculated from a constrained triaxial relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model using the PC-PK1 density functional and a separable pairing force. The CQC Hamiltonian includes quadrupole-quadrupole and monopole pairing interactions between the odd nucleon and core, with the Fermi surface and coupling strength as free parameters fit to data separately for positive- and negative-parity states. The model is applied to the odd-proton nucleus 159Tb and the odd-neutron nucleus 157Gd, using cores 160Dy, 158Gd, and 156Gd.

## Key numbers

- Axial deformation parameter β ≈ 0.35 for the core nuclei 160Dy, 158Gd, and 156Gd.
- For 159Tb positive-parity bands, the Fermi surface λ = -8.50 MeV and coupling strength χ = 9.40 MeV/b².
- For 159Tb negative-parity bands, λ = -7.00 MeV and χ = 12.80 MeV/b².
- For 157Gd negative-parity bands, λ = -9.70 MeV and χ = 12.50 MeV/b².
- For 157Gd positive-parity bands, λ = -7.70 MeV and χ = 15.10 MeV/b².
- Kinematic moments of inertia for the ground state band of 159Tb are 40–50 ħ² MeV⁻¹, with discrepancy less than 3 ħ² MeV⁻¹ between theory and data.
- The calculated γ-band head of core 158Gd is 1.035 MeV; the corresponding band 5 head in 159Tb is 1.058 MeV.
- The calculated γ-band head of core 156Gd is 1.152 MeV; the corresponding band 5 head in 157Gd is 1.313 MeV.
- Band 3 in 159Tb positive parity is lowered by ~0.15 MeV when the γ band of the core is included; band 4 in 157Gd negative parity is shifted by ~0.25 MeV.
- Band 3 in 157Gd positive parity is lowered by ~0.3 MeV when the γ band of the core is included.

## Context

This work extends the covariant density functional theory (DFT) based quadrupole collective Hamiltonian approach, previously successful for even-even nuclei, to odd-mass nuclei via the core-quasiparticle coupling scheme. It differs from earlier phenomenological CQC models (e.g., Donau 1977, 1979; Prot 1994, 1996, 1997; Klein 2000, 2004) by using fully microscopic inputs from the same covariant EDF, and from recent EDF-based approaches for odd nuclei such as the full GCM calculation for 25Mg by Bally (2014) and the boson-fermion model by Nomura (2016). The model enables systematic spectroscopic studies of odd-mass medium-heavy and heavy nuclei, with planned applications to additional nuclei.
