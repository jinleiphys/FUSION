# Theoretical study of $^{173}$YbOH to search for the nuclear magnetic quadrupole moment

> CP-violating interaction of the nuclear magnetic quadrupole moment (MQM) with electrons in the ytterbium mono-hydroxide molecule, $^{173}$YbOH, is considered. Both the magnetic quadrupole moment (MQM) of the $^{173}$Yb nucleus and the molecular interaction constant WM are estimated. Electron correlation effects are taken into account within the relativistic Fock-space coupled cluster method. Results are interpreted in terms of the strength constants of CP-violating nuclear forces, neutron EDM, QCD vacuum angle $θ$, quark EDM and chromo-EDM.

# Theoretical study of $^{173}$YbOH to search for the nuclear magnetic quadrupole moment

## Key claim

The $^{173}$YbOH molecule is a promising candidate for detecting the nuclear magnetic quadrupole moment (MQM) of the $^{173}$Yb nucleus, with the molecular interaction constant $W_M$ calculated to be $-1.07(5) \times 10^{33}$ Hz/($e \cdot$ cm$^2$), and the resulting energy shifts from fundamental CP-violating parameters ($\bar{\theta}$ and quark chromo-EDMs) are estimated to be of the same order as current experimental sensitivities in ThO, but with potential for significant improvement based on proposed experimental enhancements.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Fock-space coupled-cluster calculations with single and double amplitudes (FS-CCSD) to compute the molecular constant $W_M$ for the $^{173}$YbOH molecule, which characterizes the interaction between the nuclear MQM and electrons. The nuclear MQM of $^{173}$Yb was calculated using the Nilsson model, summing over nucleons and expressing the result in terms of CP-violating strength constants, including the QCD vacuum angle $\bar{\theta}$, quark EDMs, and chromo-EDMs. Basis set convergence was checked using Dyall's all-electron basis sets (AE2Z, AE3Z, AE4Z) for Yb and augmented correlation-consistent basis sets for O and H, with corrections for inner-core electron correlation and the Gaunt interaction.

## Key numbers

- $W_M = -1.07(5) \times 10^{33}$ Hz/($e \cdot$ cm$^2$) for $^{173}$YbOH, calculated at the FS-CCSD level with the AE4Z basis set for Yb and aug-cc-pVTZ-DK for O and H
- The Dirac-Hartree-Fock contribution to $W_M$ is $-0.736 \times 10^{33}$ Hz/($e \cdot$ cm$^2$), showing correlation effects contribute more than 30% of the total value
- The collective MQM of $^{173}$Yb is expressed as $M = 14 M^p_0 + 26 M^n_0$, where $M^p_0$ and $M^n_0$ are single-particle matrix elements for protons and neutrons
- $M(\bar{\theta}) \approx 1.0 \times 10^{-27} \, \bar{\theta} \, e \cdot \text{cm}^2$ for the QCD vacuum angle contribution
- $M(\tilde{d}) \approx 0.6 \times 10^{-10} (\tilde{d}_u - \tilde{d}_d) \, e \cdot \text{cm}$ for the quark chromo-EDM contribution
- The estimated energy shift from $\bar{\theta}$ is $|\delta(\theta)| \approx 10 \times 10^{10} \bar{\theta} \cdot \mu\text{Hz}$
- The estimated energy shift from quark chromo-EDMs is $|\delta(\tilde{d}_u - \tilde{d}_d)| \approx 6 \times 10^{27} (\tilde{d}_u - \tilde{d}_d)/\text{cm} \cdot \mu\text{Hz}$
- Current limits ($|\bar{\theta}| < 2.4 \times 10^{-10}$, $|\tilde{d}_u - \tilde{d}_d| < 6 \times 10^{-27}$ cm) correspond to shifts $|\delta| < 24$ $\mu$Hz and $36$ $\mu$Hz, respectively

## Context

This work extends the proposal of Kozyryev and Hutzler (2017) to use linear triatomic molecules for electron EDM searches, applying the same molecular platform to the nuclear MQM interaction instead. The calculated $W_M$ value for YbOH ($-1.07 \times 10^{33}$ Hz/($e \cdot$ cm$^2$)) is close to that for YbF ($-1.3 \times 10^{33}$ Hz/($e \cdot$ cm$^2$)) from Titov (1996) and Quiney (1998), more than twice that for HfF$^+$ ($0.494 \times 10^{33}$ Hz/($e \cdot$ cm$^2$)) from Skripnikov (2017) and Fleig (2017), and comparable to ThO ($1.10 \times 10^{33}$ Hz/($e \cdot$ cm$^2$)) from Skripnikov (2014). The nuclear MQM calculations build on the Nilsson model results of Lackenby (2018) and Flambaum, Dzuba, and Kuchiev (2014). The work enables future experiments on $^{173}$YbOH to potentially improve limits on the QCD vacuum angle $\bar{\theta}$ and the difference of quark chromo-EDMs by several orders of magnitude, leveraging the predicted four-order-of-magnitude sensitivity gain over the ThO experiment of the ACME Collaboration (2018).
