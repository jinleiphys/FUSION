# Fusion dynamics of symmetric systems near barrier energies

> The enhancement of the sub-barrier fusion cross sections was explained as the lowering of the dynamical fusion barriers within the framework of the improved isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (ImIQMD) model. The numbers of nucleon transfer in the neck region are appreciably dependent on the incident energies, but strongly on the reaction systems. A comparison of the neck dynamics is performed for the symmetric reactions $^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni and $^{64}$Ni+$^{64}$Ni at energies in the vicinity of the Coulomb barrier. An increase of the ratios of neutron to proton in the neck region at initial collision stage is observed and obvious for neutron-rich systems, which can reduce the interaction potential of two colliding nuclei. The distribution of the dynamical fusion barriers and the fusion excitation functions are calculated and compared them with the available experimental data.

# Fusion dynamics of symmetric systems near barrier energies

## Key claim

The enhancement of sub-barrier fusion cross sections is explained as a lowering of the dynamical fusion barrier due to nucleon reorganization and neck formation, with the effect being more pronounced for neutron-rich systems. The authors hedge that this mechanism is demonstrated specifically within the framework of the improved isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (ImIQMD) model, and that the lowering of the dynamical barrier is energy-dependent, approaching the static barrier at higher incident energies.

## Method

The authors used the ImIQMD model, which evolves nucleons under a self-consistently generated mean-field via Hamiltonian equations of motion derived from a time-dependent variational principle, with the total Hamiltonian including kinetic energy, effective interaction potential from the Skyrme energy-density functional, and a shell correction term. They calculated the dynamical interaction potential as a function of distance between nuclear centers by subtracting the energies of isolated projectile and target from the total energy of the combined system, and defined the neck region as a cylindrical volume of height 4 fm where the density at the touching point reaches 0.02 times the saturation density. Fusion cross sections were computed by integrating over impact parameters with a step of 0.5 fm, where fusion probability for each impact parameter was taken as the ratio of fusion events to total simulation events.

## Key numbers

- Static fusion barrier height for 58Ni+58Ni: 97.32 MeV (center of mass)
- Dynamical barrier at incident energy E_c.m. = 105 MeV approaches the static barrier for 58Ni+58Ni
- Neck region defined as cylindrical shape with height 4 fm and density threshold 0.02 rho_0
- Impact parameter step for fusion cross section calculation: Delta b = 0.5 fm
- Fusion excitation functions calculated for 58Ni+58Ni (experimental data from Be81) and 64Ni+64Ni (experimental data from Ji04) at energies near the Coulomb barrier

## Context

This work extends the ImIQMD model previously developed by the same authors (Feng 2005) to treat heavy-ion fusion reactions near barrier energies, and contrasts with coupled channel methods such as the CCFULL code (Hagino 1999) which have difficulty describing symmetric systems. The neck dynamics approach builds on earlier phenomenological work by Vorkapic (1994) and classical dynamical modeling by Aguiar, Canto, and Donangelo (1985), and the energy dependence of the nucleus-nucleus potential was also investigated using time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory (Washiyama 2008). The results enable a microscopic understanding of sub-barrier fusion enhancement and have implications for the formation of superheavy nuclei in massive fusion reactions.
