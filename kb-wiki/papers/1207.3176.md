# Hydrodynamics for relativistic heavy ion collisions

> Simulations of the viscous hydrodynamic model for relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies are presented. Results for spectra, femtoscopy radii, and transverse momentum fluctuations are favorably compared to the experimental data. Effects of the local charge conservation on correlation observables are also studied.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a 3+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model, using both averaged and fluctuating initial conditions, can simultaneously describe multiple observables (spectra, femtoscopy radii, transverse momentum fluctuations, and charge correlations) in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies, with the caveat that the description of pion and kaon spectra at LHC energies is "too flat" and requires a larger bulk viscosity value for improvement.

## Method

The authors apply a 3+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic model with shear viscosity η/s=0.08 and bulk viscosity ζ/s=0.04 (bulk viscosity only in the hadronic phase) to Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV using averaged initial conditions from the optical Glauber model, and to Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV using fluctuating initial conditions from a Monte Carlo Glauber implementation. Particle emission and resonance decays at freeze-out are performed via the event generator THERMINATOR. For p-Pb and d-Pb collisions at LHC energies, event-by-event simulations are used to estimate collective flow.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity coefficient η/s = 0.08 (default value used in simulations)
- Bulk viscosity coefficient ζ/s = 0.04 (default, present only in hadronic phase); a larger value ζ/s = 0.08 gives slightly better description of pion and kaon spectra at LHC
- Pb-Pb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV (LHC) and Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV (RHIC) are the primary systems studied
- p-Pb collisions at 4.4 TeV and d-Pb collisions at 3.11 TeV are simulated for small-system predictions
- Scaled transverse momentum fluctuations relate to fireball size fluctuations as Δp_T/⟨p_T⟩ ≃ 0.3 Δr/⟨r⟩

## Context

This work extends the viscous hydrodynamic framework of Bozek (2011) to include both averaged and fluctuating initial conditions, building on prior studies of collective flow by Kolb (2003), Florkowski (2010), Luzum (2008), Song (2010), and Schenke (2010). The results for transverse momentum fluctuations improve upon the perfect-hydrodynamics approximation of Broniowski (2009) by including viscous effects and event-by-event fluctuations. The charge-balancing mechanism, based on earlier work by Bass (2000), Jeon (2001), and Bozek (2003, 2004), is incorporated to explain the same-side ridge structures in dihadron correlations observed by the STAR Collaboration (Agakishiev, 2011). The findings enable future experimental searches for collective flow in small systems (p-Pb and d-Pb) and caution that such systems cannot serve as reference data for medium modifications in Pb-Pb collisions if hydrodynamic interactions are present.
