# Proper effective temperature of nonequilibrium steady state

> We define a proper effective temperature for relativistic nonequilibrium steady states (NESSs). A conventional effective temperature of NESSs is defined from the ratio of the fluctuation to the dissipation. However, NESSs have relative velocities to the heat bath in general, and hence the conventional effective temperature can be frame dependent in relativistic systems. The proper effective temperature is introduced as a frame-independent (Lorentz invariant) quantity that characterizes NESSs. We find that the proper effective temperature of NESSs is higher than the proper temperature of the heat bath in a wide range of holographic models even when the conventional effective temperature is lower than the temperature of the heat bath.

## Key claim

The paper defines a Lorentz-invariant "proper effective temperature" for relativistic nonequilibrium steady states (NESSs), showing that this quantity is always higher than the proper temperature of the heat bath in a wide range of holographic models, even when the conventional (frame-dependent) effective temperature is lower than the heat bath temperature.

## Method

The authors use holographic duality (gauge/gravity correspondence) to study NESSs, where the heat bath is described by a black hole geometry and the driven system by a D-brane or fundamental string. The effective temperature of the NESS is obtained from the Hawking temperature of the effective open-string metric evaluated at the effective horizon. By performing Lorentz boosts on the open-string metric, the transformation law for the effective temperature is derived, and the proper effective temperature is identified as the boost-invariant combination \(\gamma(v_m)T_*\), where \(v_m\) is the mean velocity of the NESS.

## Key numbers

* For a fundamental string dragged through a D\(p\)-brane heat bath (\(1 \leq p \leq 4\)), the effective temperature is \(T_* = (1-v^2)^{1/(7-p)} T_0\), and the proper effective temperature is \(T_{*0} = (1-v^2)^{(p-5)/2(7-p)} T_0 \geq T_0\).
* For a D\((q+1+n)\)-brane dragged through a D\(p\)-brane heat bath, the effective temperature is \(T_* = (1-v^2)^{1/(7-p)} (1+C v^2)^{1/2} T_0\) with \(C = \frac{1}{2}(q+3-p+n\frac{p-3}{7-p})\), and the proper effective temperature is \(T_{*0} = (1-v^2)^{(p-5)/2(7-p)} (1+C v^2)^{1/2} T_0\).
* For the specific case of a D2-brane (\(q=0, n=1\)) in a D4-brane heat bath (\(p=4\)), \(C = -1/3\) and \(T_{*0} = (1-v^2)^{-1/6} (1 - \frac{1}{3}v^2)^{1/2} T_0 \geq T_0\).
* The Hawking temperature of the D\(p\)-brane geometry is \(T_0 = \frac{7-p}{4\pi} u_H^{(5-p)/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of effective temperatures in NESSs, previously investigated in holographic models by Gubser (2006), Nakamura (2013), and Hoshino (2014), where the conventional effective temperature was found to be lower than the heat bath temperature at finite velocity. The paper resolves the apparent paradox by introducing a Lorentz-invariant proper effective temperature, analogous to the proper temperature in equilibrium systems as discussed by Einstein (1907), Planck (1908), Landsberg (1966), and van Kampen (1968). This enables a frame-independent characterization of NESSs and provides a covariant formulation of the fluctuation-dissipation relation for relativistic nonequilibrium systems.
