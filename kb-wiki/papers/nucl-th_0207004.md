# Renormalization group approach to neutron matter: quasiparticle interactions, superfluid gaps and the equation of state

> Renormalization group methods can be applied to the nuclear many-body problem using the approach proposed by Shankar. We start with the two-body low momentum interaction V_{low k} and use the RG flow from the particle-hole channels to calculate the full scattering amplitude in the vicinity of the Fermi surface. This is a new straightforward approach to the many-body problem which is applicable also to condensed matter systems without long-range interactions, such as liquid 3He. We derive the one-loop renormalization group equations for the quasiparticle interaction and the scattering amplitude at zero temperature. The RG presents an elegant method to maintain all momentum scales and preserve the antisymmetry of the scattering amplitude. As a first application we solve the RG equations for neutron matter. The resulting quasiparticle interaction includes effects due to the polarization of the medium, the so-called induced interaction of Babu and Brown. We present results for the Fermi liquid parameters, the equation of state of neutron matter and the 1S0 superfluid pairing gap.

## Key claim

The renormalization group (RG) approach to neutron matter, starting from the low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction \(V_{\text{low k}}\), can generate the full scattering amplitude near the Fermi surface including polarization effects (the induced interaction of Babu and Brown), but the calculation is performed in the long-wavelength approximation where both particle-hole momentum transfers are assumed small compared to the Fermi momentum, and the one-loop truncation omits particle-particle (BCS) channel contributions from the RG flow.

## Method

The authors solve the one-loop renormalization group equations for the quasiparticle interaction and scattering amplitude at zero temperature in three dimensions, using the particle-hole channels (zero sound and its exchange) in the long-wavelength approximation. The RG flow is initialized with the low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction \(V_{\text{low k}}\) at a cutoff \(\Lambda_0 = k_{\text{F}}\), and the effective mass is updated self-consistently during the flow to enforce Galilean invariance. The quasiparticle strength \(z_{k_{\text{F}}}\) is treated either as a static density-independent value (0.9) or computed dynamically from the effective mass ratio.

## Key numbers

- The low-momentum interaction \(V_{\text{low k}}\) is significant only for relative momenta \(k < 1.3\,\text{fm}^{-1}\), with important momentum modes around the pion mass \(m_\pi \approx 0.7\,\text{fm}^{-1}\).
- The Fermi momentum of neutron matter at nuclear matter density is \(k_{\text{F}} = 1.7\,\text{fm}^{-1}\).
- The RG flow is started at \(\Lambda_0 = k_{\text{F}}\) with the direct interaction cutoff at \(\Lambda_{V_{\text{low k}}} = \sqrt{2}\,k_{\text{F}}\).
- The dynamic \(z_{k_{\text{F}}}\) factor agrees well with Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations for Fermi momenta in the range \(0.5\,\text{fm}^{-1} < k_{\text{F}} < 1.5\,\text{fm}^{-1}\).
- The average momentum grid spacing used in the numerical solution is \(\Delta q/k_{\text{F}} = \Delta q'/k_{\text{F}} = 0.033\).

## Context

This work extends the renormalization group approach to Fermi systems proposed by Shankar (1994) to neutron matter, using the low-momentum interaction \(V_{\text{low k}}\) developed by Bogner et al. (2001) as the starting point. The RG method naturally preserves the antisymmetry of the scattering amplitude and generates the induced interaction of Babu and Brown (1966), which incorporates medium polarization effects. The results enable computation of Fermi liquid parameters, the equation of state via integration of the incompressibility following Kallman (1972), and the \(^1\)S\(_0\) superfluid pairing gap using weak-coupling BCS theory, with the authors noting good agreement with the polarization potential model of Ainsworth, Wambach, and Pines (1989).
