# S-Matrix Unitarity, Impact Parameter Profiles, Gluon Saturation and High-Energy Scattering

> A model combining perturbative and non-perturbative QCD is developed to compute high-energy reactions of hadrons and photons and to investigate saturation effects that manifest the S-matrix unitarity. Following a functional integral approach, the S-matrix factorizes into light-cone wave functions and the universal amplitude for the scattering of two color-dipoles which are represented by Wegner-Wilson loops. In the framework of the non-perturbative stochastic vacuum model of QCD supplemented by perturbative gluon exchange, the loop-loop correlation is calculated and related to lattice QCD investigations. With a universal energy dependence motivated by the two-pomeron (soft + hard) picture that respects the unitarity condition in impact parameter space, a unified description of pp, pip, Kp, gamma* p, and gamma gamma reactions is achieved in good agreement with experimental data for cross sections, slope parameters, and structure functions. Impact parameter profiles for pp and longitudinal gamma* p reactions and the gluon distribution of the proton xG(x,Q^2,b) are calculated and found to saturate in accordance with S-matrix unitarity. The c.m. energies and Bjorken x at which saturation sets in are determined.

## Key claim

The paper presents a unified model of high-energy scattering that combines perturbative and non-perturbative QCD, claiming that saturation effects manifesting S-matrix unitarity can be described through a universal dipole-dipole scattering amplitude, with the caveat that the energy dependence is imposed phenomenologically via a two-pomeron (soft + hard) picture rather than derived from first principles.

## Method

The authors use a functional integral approach where the S-matrix factorizes into light-cone wave functions and a universal amplitude for scattering of two color-dipoles represented by Wegner-Wilson loops. The loop-loop correlation is calculated within the non-perturbative stochastic vacuum model of QCD supplemented by perturbative gluon exchange, and related to lattice QCD investigations. Hadrons are modeled as quark-antiquark or quark-diquark systems using Gaussian Wirbel-Stech-Bauer wave functions, while photon wave functions are derived from light-cone perturbation theory with running quark masses to interpolate between high and low virtuality.

## Key numbers

- For (anti-)protons: longitudinal width parameter \(\Delta z_p = 0.3\) and transverse extension \(S_p = 0.86\,\text{fm}\)
- For pions: \(\Delta z_{\pi} = 2\) and \(S_{\pi} = 0.607\,\text{fm}\)
- For kaons: \(\Delta z_{K} = 0.57\) and \(S_{K} = 0.55\,\text{fm}\)
- Running light quark masses: \(m_{u,d}(Q^2) = 0.178\,\text{GeV}\,(1 - Q^2/Q^2_{u,d})\,\Theta(Q^2_{u,d} - Q^2)\) with \(Q^2_{u,d} = 1.05\,\text{GeV}^2\)
- Running strange quark mass: \(m_s(Q^2) = 0.121\,\text{GeV} + 0.129\,\text{GeV}\,(1 - Q^2/Q^2_s)\,\Theta(Q^2_s - Q^2)\) with \(Q^2_s = 1.6\,\text{GeV}^2\)
- Fixed charm quark mass: \(m_c = 1.25\,\text{GeV}\)

## Context

This work extends the stochastic vacuum model approach of Dosch (1989) and the dipole model framework of Nikolaev (1991) to provide a unified description of pp, pi p, K p, gamma* p, and gamma gamma reactions. It enables predictions for impact parameter profiles and gluon saturation scales, connecting to the saturation models of Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (1998) and the color glass condensate framework of McLerran and Venugopalan (1994). The results allow determination of the center-of-mass energies and Bjorken x values at which saturation sets in, providing a bridge between non-perturbative QCD modeling and unitarity constraints.
