# Self consistent calculation of the nuclear composition in hot and dense stellar matter

> We investigate the mass fractions and in-medium properties of heavy nuclei in stellar matter at characteristic densities and temperatures for supernova (SN) explosions. The individual nuclei are described within the compressible liquid-drop model taking into account modifications of bulk, surface and Coulomb energies. The equilibrium properties of nuclei and the full ensemble of heavy nuclei are calculated self-consistently. It is found that heavy nuclei in the ensemble are either compressed or decompressed depending on the isospin asymmetry of the system. The compression or decompression has a little influence on the binding energies, total mass fractions and average mass numbers of heavy nuclei, although the equilibrium densities of individual nuclei themselves are changed appreciably above one hundredth of normal nuclear density. We find that nuclear structure in single nucleus approximation deviates from the actual one obtained in the multi-nucleus description, since the density of free nucleons is different between these two descriptions. This study indicates that a multi-nucleus description is required to realistically account for in-medium effects on the nuclear structure in supernova matter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in stellar matter at supernova densities, heavy nuclei in a multi-nucleus ensemble are either compressed or decompressed depending on the isospin asymmetry of the system, but these changes in equilibrium density have little influence on binding energies, total mass fractions, and average mass numbers, even though the equilibrium densities of individual nuclei change appreciably above one hundredth of normal nuclear density.

## Method

The authors constructed a multi-nucleus equation of state (EOS) based on a compressible liquid-drop model, calculating the equilibrium density of each heavy nucleus (proton number 6 to 1000) by minimizing its internal free energy as a function of baryon density, temperature, and local proton and neutron densities in the surrounding vapor. The full nuclear ensemble was obtained by minimizing the total free energy density under baryon number and charge conservation constraints, with free nucleons treated as an ideal Boltzmann gas with excluded-volume corrections and alpha particles included as the only light cluster. For comparison, they also computed an incompressible multi-nucleus model (fixed vacuum densities) and a single-nucleus approximation (SNA) model with a compressible representative nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Equilibrium densities of individual nuclei change appreciably above one hundredth of normal nuclear density ($n_B \gtrsim 0.01 n_0$), with noticeable changes in binding energies, total mass fractions, and average mass numbers only above $0.1 n_0$.
- For the parameter set E, the saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter is $n_0 = 0.15-0.16$ fm$^{-3}$, the incompressibility $K_0 = 240 \pm 20$ MeV, and the symmetry energy $S_0 = 29.0-33.7$ MeV.
- The slope parameter $L$ controlling the density dependence of the symmetry energy is constrained to be larger than 35 MeV and less than 131 MeV from various experiments and observations.
- The critical lines separating compressed and decompressed regimes occur when the mass fraction of free nucleons $X_n+X_p$ becomes less than approximately 0.05-0.15.
- At $T=3$ MeV and $Y_p=0.2$, the equilibrium density of $^{50}$Ca decreases with increasing baryon density; at $T=1$ MeV and $Y_p=0.4$, the equilibrium density of $^{300}$Fm increases with baryon density.
- The surface free energy reduction factor uses a critical temperature $T_c = 18$ MeV and surface tension for symmetric nuclei $\sigma_0 = 1.14$ MeV/fm$^3$ with $C_s = 12.1$ MeV.
- The thermal term in the bulk free energy uses $\epsilon_0 = 16$ MeV for the low-temperature approximation.

## Context

This work extends the multi-nucleus EOS approach of the FYSS EOS (Furusawa et al., 2011, 2013, 2017) and the SMSM EOS (Botvina et al., 2004, 2010; Buyukcizmeci et al., 2014) by self-consistently including the compression or decompression of individual nuclei in the ensemble, which had not been done previously. It contradicts the implicit assumption in single-nucleus EOSs such as those of Lattimer and Swesty (1991) and Shen et al. (1998, 2011) that a representative nucleus can capture the in-medium effects correctly, showing that the SNA underestimates equilibrium densities due to incorrect vapor nucleon densities. The work enables future improvements to supernova and neutron star merger simulations by providing a framework for more realistic in-medium modifications of nuclear structure, though the authors note that nuclear pasta phases, shell effects, and light clusters beyond alpha particles remain to be included.
