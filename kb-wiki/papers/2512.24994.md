# Dissipative corrections to the particle momentum spectrum of a decoupling fluid

> We present an \emph{ab initio} calculation within quantum statistical field theory and linear response theory, of the dissipative correction to the momentum spectrum of scalar particles emitted at decoupling (freeze-out) from a relativistic fluid assuming the initial state to be in local thermodynamic equilibrium. We obtain an expansion of the Wigner function of the interacting quantum field in terms of the gradients of the classical thermo-hydrodynamic fields - four-temperature vector and reduced chemical potential - evaluated on the initial local-equilibrium hypersurface, rather than on the decoupling (freeze-out) hypersurface as usual in kinetic theory. The gradient expansion includes an unexpected zeroth order term depending on the differences between thermo-hydrodynamic fields at the decoupling and the initial hypersurface. This term encodes a memory of the initial state which is related to the long-distance persistence of the correlation function between Wigner operator and stress-energy tensor and charged current that is discussed in detail. We address the phenomenological implications of these corrections for the momentum spectra measured in relativistic nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents an *ab initio* calculation of dissipative corrections to the momentum spectrum of scalar particles at decoupling from a relativistic fluid, using quantum statistical field theory and linear response theory. The key finding is that the gradient expansion of the Wigner function includes an unexpected zeroth-order term depending on differences between thermo-hydrodynamic fields at the decoupling and initial hypersurfaces, encoding a memory of the initial state that is not present in standard kinetic theory approaches.

## Method

The authors use the Zubarev non-equilibrium density operator formalism to define the initial state as local thermodynamic equilibrium on a space-like hypersurface, then apply linear response theory to calculate the off-equilibrium part of the Wigner function for an interacting scalar field. They expand the Wigner function in gradients of the initial thermo-hydrodynamic fields (four-temperature vector and reduced chemical potential) evaluated on the initial local-equilibrium hypersurface, rather than on the decoupling hypersurface as is standard in kinetic theory. The calculation involves analyzing dynamical correlators between conserved currents and field Fourier transforms, then working out the off-equilibrium correction in the hydrodynamic limit.

## Key numbers

* The free field spectral function is given as \(\varrho_{\rm free}(p) = 2\pi \; {\rm sign}(p^0) \delta(p^2-m^2) = 2\pi \; {\rm sign}(p^0) \delta(M^2-m^2)\), where \(m\) is the particle mass.
* The global equilibrium density operator is \(\wrho_{\rm GE}= \frac{1}{Z}\exp\left[-\beta \cdot \wP + \zeta \wQ\right]\), with \(\beta\) and \(\zeta\) constant.
* The lesser Wightman function at global equilibrium relates to the spectral function as \({\cal G}^<_{\rm GE}(q) = \frac{1}{\e^{\beta \cdot q - \zeta}-1}\, \varrho_{\rm GE}(q) = n_{\rm B}(q)\, \varrho_{\rm GE}(q)\), where \(n_{\rm B}(q)\) is the Bose-Einstein distribution.

## Context

This work extends the quantum-statistical framework for dissipative corrections previously applied to local currents (leading to relativistic Kubo formulae) by authors such as Zubarev, Becattini (2019), Jeon (1995), and Harutyunyan (2021). It contrasts with most prior calculations of dissipative corrections to the spectrum, which were carried out in classical relativistic kinetic theory by Hidaka (2016), Zhang (2019), Romatschke (2017), Weickgenannt (2022), Bhadury (2020), Yang (2020), and Wang (2025), with fewer quantum statistical field theory treatments by Buzzegoli (2025) and Li (2025). The new method developed in a recent work by the same authors (Sheng, 2025) enables the gradient expansion on the initial hypersurface rather than the decoupling hypersurface. This work enables improved modeling of particle momentum spectra in relativistic nuclear collisions, particularly for the particlization stage following the quark-gluon plasma to hadron gas transition.
