# Nucleon axial charge in 2+1 flavor dynamical lattice QCD with domain wall fermions

> We present results for the nucleon axial charge g_A at a fixed lattice spacing of 1/a=1.73(3) GeV using 2+1 flavors of domain wall fermions on size 16^3x32 and 24^3x64lattices (L=1.8 and 2.7 fm) with length 16 in the fifth dimension. The length of the Monte Carlo trajectory at the lightest m_πis 7360 units, including 900 for thermalization. We find finite volume effects are larger than the pion mass dependence at m_π= 330 MeV. We also find that g_A exhibits a scaling with the single variable m_πL which can also be seen in previous two-flavor domain wall and Wilson fermion calculati ons. Using this scaling to eliminate the finite-volume effect, we obtain g_A = 1.20(6)(4) at the physical pion mass, m_π= 135 MeV, where the first and second errors are statistical and systematic. The observed finite-volume scaling also appears in similar quenched simulations, but disappear when V\ge (2.4 fm)^3. We argue this is a dynamical quark effect.

## Key claim

The nucleon axial charge \(g_A\) exhibits a scaling with the single variable \(m_\pi L\) that can be used to eliminate finite-volume effects, yielding \(g_A = 1.20(6)(4)\) at the physical pion mass; however, this scaling breaks down in quenched and partially-quenched simulations when the volume exceeds roughly (2.4 fm)³, which the authors argue is a dynamical quark effect.

## Method

The calculation used 2+1 flavors of domain wall fermions on \(16^3 \times 32\) and \(24^3 \times 64\) lattices (spatial sizes 1.8 fm and 2.7 fm) at a fixed lattice spacing of \(1/a = 1.73(3)\) GeV, with the Iwasaki gauge action at \(\beta = 2.13\) and a fifth dimension of length 16. The axial charge was obtained from the ratio of the spatial component of the axial vector current to the temporal component of the vector current at zero momentum transfer, exploiting the common renormalization constant guaranteed by chiral symmetry. Measurements were performed at unitary points (valence quark mass equal to sea quark mass) for four light sea quark masses corresponding to pion masses from 0.33 to 0.67 GeV, with source-sink separations exceeding 1.37 fm to minimize excited state contamination.

## Key numbers

- \(g_A = 1.20(6)(4)\) at the physical pion mass \(m_\pi = 135\) MeV, where the first error is statistical and the second is systematic.
- Lattice spacing: \(1/a = 1.73(3)\) GeV.
- On the (2.7 fm)³ volume, \(g_A\) values at the four simulated pion masses: \(1.083(50)\) at \(m_\pi = 0.3313(13)\) GeV; \(1.186(36)\) at \(m_\pi = 0.4189(13)\) GeV; \(1.173(36)\) at \(m_\pi = 0.5572(5)\) GeV; \(1.197(30)\) at \(m_\pi = 0.6721(6)\) GeV.
- On the (1.8 fm)³ volume, \(g_A\) values: \(1.066(72)\) at \(m_\pi = 0.4189\) GeV; \(1.115(58)\) at \(m_\pi = 0.5572\) GeV; \(1.149(32)\) at \(m_\pi = 0.6721\) GeV.
- The lightest mass point on the larger volume is about 9% smaller than the other points on that volume.
- To keep finite-volume effects at or below 1% for \(m_\pi = 330\) MeV, spatial sizes of 3.4–4.1 fm (\(m_\pi L \approx 5.7\)–\(6.9\)) are necessary.

## Context

This work extends previous two-flavor domain wall fermion calculations by the RBC collaboration (Lin 2008) and two-flavor Wilson fermion studies (Khan 2006, Dolgov 2002, Alexandrou 2007) by using 2+1 dynamical flavors with identical sea and valence quarks. The observed \(m_\pi L\) scaling contradicts predictions from heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (Beane 2004, Detmold 2005, Khan 2006), which underestimate the finite-volume effect by an order of magnitude. The result enables future lattice QCD calculations to control finite-volume effects by targeting \(m_\pi L \gtrsim 5.7\) and provides a benchmark for comparing unitary dynamical fermion calculations with mixed-action approaches (Edwards 2005) and quenched simulations (Sasaki 2003, Sasaki 2007).
