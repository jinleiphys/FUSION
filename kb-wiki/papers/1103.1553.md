# Low-energy dipole excitations in neon isotopes and N=16 isotones within the quasiparticle random phase approximation and the Gogny force

> Low-energy dipole excitations in neon isotopes and N=16 isotones are calculated with a fully consistent axially-symmetric-deformed quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) approach based on Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) states. The same Gogny D1S effective force has been used both in HFB and QRPA calculations. The microscopical structure of these low-lying resonances, as well as the behavior of proton and neutron transition densities, are investigated in order to determine the isoscalar or isovector nature of the excitations. It is found that the N=16 isotones 24O, 26Ne, 28Mg, and 30Si are characterized by a similar behavior. The occupation of the 2s_1/2 neutron orbit turns out to be crucial, leading to nontrivial transition densities and to small but finite collectivity. Some low-lying dipole excitations of 28Ne and 30Ne, characterized by transitions involving the neutron 1d_3/2 state, present a more collective behavior and isoscalar transition densities. A collective proton low-lying excitation is identified in the 18Ne nucleus.

## Key claim

Low-energy dipole excitations in neon isotopes and N=16 isotones exhibit a small but finite collective behavior, with the occupation of the 2s₁/₂ neutron orbit being crucial for the transition densities; however, the isoscalar or isovector nature of these excitations is model-dependent and remains an open problem, contrary to the simpler picture suggested by the abstract.

## Method

The authors performed fully consistent axially-symmetric-deformed Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) plus quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations using the same Gogny D1S effective force in both steps. The HFB equations were solved in a finite harmonic oscillator basis with 9 major shells, and all 2-quasiparticle excitations were used without energy or occupation cuts. The response was computed separately for each K^π block, and the spurious translational state was removed by introducing a small renormalization factor of the residual interaction.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁶Ne, the first low-energy state at ω_n = 10.7 MeV is dominated (67.6%) by the ν 2s₁/₂ → 2p₃/₂ transition, with a 9.5% contribution from ν 2s₁/₂ → 2p₁/₂ and 8.7% from ν 1d₅/₂ → 1f₇/₂.
- For ²⁴O, the low-energy peak appears at ω_n = 9.1 MeV; in the equivalent spherical RPA calculation, N^⋆RPA_ν = 3 and N^⋆RPA_π = 2 at this energy.
- For ²⁸Ne, the first peak at 8.1 MeV is 90.4% ν 1d₃/₂ → 2p₃/₂ (8.26 MeV), and the second at 9.5 MeV is 83.1% ν 1d₃/₂ → 2p₁/₂ (10.00 MeV).
- For ³⁰Ne, the first peak at 6.6 MeV is 93.5% ν 1d₃/₂ → 2p₃/₂ (6.57 MeV), and the second at 7.9 MeV is 89.9% ν 1d₃/₂ → 2p₁/₂ (8.09 MeV).
- For ¹⁸Ne, a low-energy proton resonance appears at 14.2 MeV with total neutron contribution less than 8%; the proton 1d₅/₂ orbital has a root-mean-square radius of 3.40 fm compared to the total proton root-mean-square radius of 2.84 fm.
- For ²⁴Ne (spherical case), the low-energy peak at 13.6 MeV has B(E1) = 4.5 × 10⁻² e² fm², dominated (65.4%) by ν 2s₁/₂ → 2p₃/₂ (15.03 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous HFB+QRPA studies of giant resonances in silicon and magnesium isotopes (Peru 2008) and heavy deformed ²³⁸U, as well as spherical HF+RPA calculations for exotic nuclei (Peru 2005). It directly addresses experimental data on ²⁶Ne from Gibelin (2008) and upcoming data on ²⁴O. The results are compared with calculations by Yoshida (2008), Cao (2005), and Matsuo (2004), and the model-dependent nature of transition densities highlights the need for systematic studies of both neutron-rich and proton-rich nuclei in this mass region to resolve the nature of the pygmy dipole resonance.
