# Scission configuration in the self-consistent calculations with neck constraint

> The calculations of the potential energy surface are essential in the theoretical description of the fission process. In the constrained self-consistent approach, the smooth evolution of nuclear shape is described from the ground state until a very elongated one with a narrow neck. In all microscopic calculations, the rupture of the neck at scission is associated with a substantial change of nuclear matter density distribution and rapid energy decrease. In this paper, we show that there is no discontinuity of the potential energy surface at scission when multi-constrained calculations are applied with the neck constraint. An early rupture of the neck at lower quadrupole and octupole moments is discussed as competitive with the conventional fission path. We discuss the neck properties in the scission configuration. We find that the neck radius in the asymmetric fission mode cannot decrease below 2 fm, and the nuclear matter density cannot decrease below the saturation density. In the compact fission mode, nuclear density may go down to half of the saturation density before the rupture of the neck.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the potential energy surface (PES) of ²⁵⁸No is continuous across scission when a neck constraint is added alongside quadrupole and octupole constraints, contradicting the common appearance of a "scission cliff" in two-dimensional projections. The authors find that an early rupture of the neck at lower quadrupole and octupole moments is competitive with the conventional fission path.

## Method

The authors performed axially symmetric Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny D1S interaction for the nucleus ²⁵⁸No, with constraints on quadrupole (Q₂₀), octupole (Q₃₀), and a neck parameter (Q_N) defined by a Gaussian operator centered at the minimum of the linear density. The neck constraint allowed precise control of the scission region, and the continuity of the PES was verified using the density distance D_ρρ' between neighboring mesh points.

## Key numbers

- The neck radius in the asymmetric fission mode cannot decrease below 2.1 fm, corresponding to Q_N = 4, with r_NECK = 1.059√Q_N fm
- The limiting neck radius values are 2.1 fm (Q_N=4), 2.4 fm (Q_N=5), and 2.6 fm (Q_N=6)
- In the compact fission mode, the neck parameter goes down to Q_N = 2 before rupture, corresponding to nuclear density going down to half of saturation density
- The half-life of ²⁵⁸No is t₁/₂ = 1.2(2) ms
- The width parameter a₀ in the neck constraint was taken as 1 fm in all calculations
- The energy difference between the fission valley minimum and the pre-scission saddle point is approximately 1 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent calculations of the PES for ²⁵⁸No by Zdeb et al. (2021) using the same HFB framework, where discontinuities at scission were observed in two-dimensional Q₂₀-Q₃₀ space. The neck constraint approach builds on earlier studies by Berger et al. (1990), Warda et al. (2015, 2018), and others who demonstrated the efficiency of neck-constrained calculations. The limiting neck radius of about 2 fm agrees with macroscopic-microscopic calculations by Ivanyuk et al. (2009, 2012) and the droplet model with random walk dynamics by Randrup (2011). The results enable more precise determination of scission configurations and fragment mass distributions in fission studies.
