# Exact solution of multi-angle quantum many-body collective neutrino flavor oscillations

> I study the flavor evolution of a dense neutrino gas by considering vacuum contributions, matter effects and neutrino self-interactions. Assuming a system of two flavors in a uniform matter background, the time evolution of the many-body system in discretized momentum space is computed. The multi-angle neutrino-neutrino interactions are treated exactly and compared to both the single-angle approximation and mean field calculations. %The time unit chosen is $μ_0^{-1}=(\frac{G_F}{2\sqrt{2}V})^{-1}$. The mono-energetic two neutrino beam scenario is solved analytically. I proceed to solve flavor oscillations for mono-energetic cubic lattices and quadratic lattices of two energy levels. In addition I study various configurations of twelve, sixteen, and twenty neutrinos. I find that when all neutrinos are initially of the same flavor, all methods agree. When both flavors are present, I find collective oscillations and flavor equilibration develop in the many body treatment but not in the mean field method. This difference persists in dense matter with tiny mixing angle and it can be ascribed to non-negligible flavor polarization correlations being present. Entanglement entropy is significant in all such cases. The relevance for supernovae or neutron stars mergers is contingent upon the value of the normalization volume $V$ and the large $N$ dependence of the timescale associated with oscillations. In future work, I intend to study this dependence using larger lattices and also include anti-neutrinos.

## Key claim

The paper claims that when both neutrino flavors are initially present, the exact many-body treatment produces collective oscillations and flavor equilibration that do not appear in the mean field method, even in dense matter with a tiny mixing angle; this difference persists because flavor polarization correlations become non-negligible, and the relevance for supernovae or neutron star mergers depends on the normalization volume V and the large N dependence of the oscillation timescale.

## Method

The author discretizes momentum space into a lattice and treats the two-flavor neutrino system using SU(2) algebra generators at each lattice point, computing the exact time evolution of the many-body wavefunction. The multi-angle neutrino-neutrino interactions are solved exactly and compared to both the single-angle approximation (which replaces angular dependence with an average angle) and the multi-angle mean field method (which replaces two-body operators by products of one-body operators and their expectation values). The study examines mono-energetic two-beam systems analytically, then numerically solves cubic lattices, quadratic lattices with two energy levels, and configurations of twelve, sixteen, and twenty neutrinos.

## Key numbers

- Mixing angle in vacuum: sin θ = 0.297 (from Tanabashi 2018)
- Time unit used throughout: Δt = μ₀⁻¹ = (G_F / (2√2 V))⁻¹
- For the two-beam system with both flavors initially present, the oscillation frequency is proportional to μ (the neutrino-neutrino coupling), not to the vacuum frequency ω; the exact solution gives ⟨J_Z₁⟩ proportional to cos(2 μ (1 - cos ϑ₁₂) t)
- For the two-beam system with only one flavor initially, all methods agree and the oscillation frequency depends on ω, not μ
- For dense matter with α = 0.01, collective oscillations develop in the exact treatment but the mean field shows no oscillations
- Estimated ratio of neutrino mean free path to oscillation length: λ_(ν_e + n → p + e) / λ_osc ∝ 1.2 × 10³ × (n_ν/n_B / 10⁻⁶) × (10 MeV / ω)²

## Context

This work extends the algebraic approach of Balantekin (2006) and Pehlivan (2011) for treating neutrino many-body systems as SU(2) coherent state path integrals, providing one of the first exact lattice treatments of multi-angle neutrino-neutrino interactions. It contradicts the mean field method introduced by Pantaleone (1992) for cases where both flavors are initially present, showing that mean field fails to capture collective oscillations that develop in the exact solution. The findings also differ from fast conversion studies by Banerjee (2011), Sarikas (2012), and Izaguirre (2017) that required anti-neutrinos for fast oscillations, as this work finds fast oscillations without anti-neutrinos. The results enable future work on larger lattices and inclusion of anti-neutrinos, and suggest that quantum computing approaches may be needed to overcome the exponential growth of degrees of freedom with lattice size.
