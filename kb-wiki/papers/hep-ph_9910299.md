# Minding one's P's and Q's : from the one loop effective action in quantum field theory to classical transport theory

> The one loop effective action in quantum field theory can be expressed as a quantum mechanical path integral over world lines, with internal symmetries represented by Grassmanian variables. In this paper, we develop a real time, many body, world line formalism for the one loop effective action. In particular, we study hot QCD and obtain the classical transport equations which, as Litim and Manuel have shown, reduce in the appropriate limit to the non-Abelian Boltzmann-Langevin equation first obtained by Bödeker. In the Vlasov limit, the classical kinetic equations are those that correspond to the hard thermal loop effective action. We also discuss the imaginary time world line formalism for a hot $ϕ^4$ theory, and elucidate its relation to classical transport theory.

## Key claim

The one loop effective action in hot QCD can be expressed as a many-body world line path integral, and its saddle point yields the classical transport equations of Litim and Manuel, which in turn reduce to the non-Abelian Boltzmann-Langevin equation of Bödeker; the derivation is limited to the one loop level and to the regime where the saddle point approximation is valid.

## Method

The authors start from the one loop effective action for a massless scalar field in a background gauge field, expressed as a quantum mechanical path integral over world lines with Grassmanian variables representing color. They extend this to a many-body formalism by introducing a density matrix functional ξ(x,y,λ̃,λ̃′) that encodes the initial and final conditions of the hard modes, and then take the saddle point of the path integral to obtain classical equations of motion. For hot QCD, they use the fact that the leading high-temperature contribution to the one loop effective action for gluons is the same as that of a complex scalar in the adjoint representation, and they derive the classical transport equations by functionally differentiating the effective action.

## Key numbers

- The Debye screening mass scales as m_D² ∝ g² T², where g is the gauge coupling and T is the temperature.
- The separation of scales occurs at momenta p ∼ T, p ∼ gT, and p ∼ g² T, with the hard thermal loop (HTL) effective action describing modes at p ∼ gT.
- The sum over k in the path integral runs from k=1 to n, where n = N_c² − 1 for the adjoint representation of SU(N_c).

## Context

This work extends the vacuum world line formalism of Strassler (1992) and D'Hoker and Gagné (1995) to finite temperature and many-body systems, providing a derivation of the classical transport theory previously formulated by Kelly et al. (1994) and improved by Litim and Manuel (1999). It shows that the one loop effective action in hot QCD directly yields the same transport equations that Bödeker (1998) obtained for soft modes, and it enables future systematic corrections beyond the classical limit by going beyond the saddle point approximation.
