# Novel effects of electromagnetic interaction on the correlation of nucleons in nuclear matter

> The electromagnetic(EM) interactions between charged protons on the correlations of nucleons are discussed by introducing the Anderson-Higgs mechanism of broken U(1) EM symmetry into the relativistic nuclear theory with a parametric photon mass. The non-saturating Coulomb force contribution is emphasized on the equation of state of nuclear matter with charge symmetry breaking(CSB) at finite temperature and the breached $^1S_0$ pairing correlations of proton-proton and neutron-neutron. The universal properties given by an order parameter field with a non-zero vacuum expectation value (VEV) nearby phase transition are explored within the mean field theory(MFT) level. This mechanism can be extended to the charged or charge neutralized strongly coupling multi-components system for the discussion of binding or pairing issues.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the long-range electromagnetic interaction between protons, which is usually neglected in relativistic nuclear field theory, significantly affects both the equation of state and pairing correlations in nuclear matter, and that these effects can be incorporated via an Anderson-Higgs mechanism that gives the photon an effective mass, with the caveat that the photon mass \(m_\gamma\) appears as a free parameter controlling the strength of the Coulomb contribution.

## Method

The authors extend the quantum hadrodynamics (QHD-II) model by adding a Proca-like massive photon field via the Anderson-Higgs mechanism, breaking local \(U(1)\) electromagnetic symmetry. They then apply the mean field theory (MFT) approximation at finite temperature to derive the thermodynamic potential, energy density, and pressure for charged nuclear matter, and solve the relativistic gap equation for \(^1S_0\) proton-proton and neutron-neutron pairing using a static one-boson-exchange interaction that includes the massive photon.

## Key numbers

* The photon mass parameter \(m_\gamma\) is estimated to be about \(20-30\) MeV for a reasonable critical temperature \(T_c \sim 16\) MeV accessible in heavy ion collisions.
* The saturation density used is \(\rho_0 = 0.1484\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) with binding energy \(e^0_b = -15.75\) MeV and bulk symmetry energy coefficient \(a_{sym} = 35\) MeV for symmetric nuclear matter at zero temperature.
* For the MFT parameter set (a), the photon mass is \(m_\gamma = 30.44\) MeV with \(C_\gamma^2 = 87.27\), and the effective nucleon mass ratio at saturation is \(M^*/M|_{\rho_0} = 0.540\).
* The symmetry energy coefficient formula includes both \(\rho\)-meson and photon contributions: \(a_{sym} = \frac{k_f^3}{12\pi^2} \left( \frac{g_\rho^2}{m_\rho^2} + \frac{e^2}{m_\gamma^2} \right) + \frac{k_f^2}{6\sqrt{k_f^2 + M^{*2}}}\).
* The integral momentum cutoff for the gap equation is \(\Lambda_k = 3.6\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic nuclear field theory of Walecka (1974, 1997) and the \(\sigma-\omega\) model of Boguta (1977) by incorporating electromagnetic effects that are usually omitted. It directly addresses the long-standing problem of \(^1S_0\) pairing in nuclear matter first studied relativistically by Kucharek and Ring (1991), where gaps were found to be three times larger than non-relativistic results, and the uncomfortable non-zero gaps at zero baryon density noted by Chen et al. The paper also builds on studies of Coulomb effects in nuclear matter by Bulgac (1999) and Maruyama (2004), and on the liquid-gas phase transition work of Gulminelli (2003). The approach enables future study of Meissner and Debye screening effects in astrophysical contexts such as compact stars, and provides a framework for investigating charge symmetry breaking in finite nuclei and mirror-nuclei.
