# Boson-fermion mapping and dynamical supersymmetry in fermion models

> We show that a dynamical supersymmetry can appear in a purely fermionic system. This ``supersymmetry without bosons" is constructed by application of a recently introduced boson-fermion Dyson mapping from a fermion space to a space comprised of collective bosons and ideal fermions. In some algebraic fermion models of nuclear structure, particular Hamiltonians may lead to collective spectra of even and odd nuclei that can be unified using the dynamical supersymmetry concept with Pauli correlations exactly taken into account.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a dynamical supersymmetry unifying even and odd fermion systems can appear in a purely fermionic model, contrary to the usual expectation that supersymmetry requires explicit bosonic degrees of freedom. This "supersymmetry without bosons" is constructed via a Dyson boson-fermion mapping that exactly preserves Pauli correlations, showing that the supersymmetric classification is an alternative but equivalent description of the original fermion states.

## Method

The authors apply a recently developed Dyson boson-fermion mapping (Navratil, Geyer, Dobaczewski 1995) that maps even fermion systems to a space of collective bosons and odd fermion systems to a space of collective bosons plus a single ideal fermion, with the Pauli principle exactly preserved. They then analyze the resulting mapped Hamiltonians in terms of subgroup chains of superalgebras, specifically examining the SU(2) seniority model and the Ginocchio SO(8) model, to identify cases where the Hamiltonian can be expressed using Casimir invariants of a supergroup chain.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological supersymmetry approach of the Interacting Boson-Fermion Model (IBFM) (Iachello, Van Isacker 1991) by providing a microscopic foundation where Pauli correlations between the odd particle and even core are exactly taken into account, unlike the IBFM where they are not fully included. The formalism enables a direct link between phenomenological supersymmetric models and fermion models such as the Fermion Dynamical Symmetry Model (Pan, Ping, Feng 1996), and allows construction of transition operators (e.g., single-fermion transfer operators) that are uniquely specified by the mapping rather than requiring phenomenological parameters.
