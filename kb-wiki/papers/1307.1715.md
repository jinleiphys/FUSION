# Population of rotational states in the ground-band of fission fragments

> The population of rotational states in the ground-state band of neutron-rich fragments emitted in the spontaneous fission of $^{252}$Cf is described within a time-dependent quantum model similar to the one used for Coulomb excitation. The initial population probability of the states included in the selected basis is calculated according to the bending model at scission. Subsequently these initial amplitudes are feeding the coupled dynamical equations describing the population of rotational states in both fragments during the tunneling and post-barrier (pure Coulomb) motion. As application we consider the high yield Mo-Ba pair for different number of emitted neutrons.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the population of rotational states in fission fragments can be described by a time-dependent quantum model analogous to Coulomb excitation, where the initial spin distribution is set by the bending model at scission and then evolves through coupled dynamical equations during tunneling and post-barrier motion; the model confirms the observed increase in fragment average spin for neutron multiplicities from 1 to 4, but the authors note that knowledge of fragment configuration and energy content at scission can only indirectly be extracted from such an analysis.

## Method

The authors first calculate the initial population probabilities of rotational states in the ground-state band of fission fragments using the bending model at the scission configuration, where the fragments execute angular vibrations around the fission axis. They then solve a time-dependent Schrodinger equation for the relative motion of the two fragments by propagating a quantum wave-packet across the Coulomb-plus-nuclear barrier using the Crank-Nicolson scheme, extracting a quantum trajectory R(t). Finally, they solve a coupled system of linear ordinary differential equations for the occupation amplitudes of rotational states in both fragments, using the time-dependent interaction potential and the initial amplitudes from the bending model, with a basis including up to 36 states (maximum spin I=14 per fragment).

## Key numbers

- The average spin of fragments for the Mo-Ba splitting was measured up to 8 emitted neutrons, showing an increase up to 4 neutrons followed by a decrease (data from Pop et al. (2002)).
- The calculated average spin values are shown for 3 different excitation energies corresponding to 0, 2, and 4 emitted neutrons.
- The reported j values for Ba and Mo removed by one evaporated neutron vary from approximately 0.6 hbar to approximately 0.3 hbar when the number of neutrons emitted from the light fragment changed from 1 to 6 and from the heavy fragment from 1 to 3.
- The basis includes up to 36 states, meaning a maximum spin of I=14 can be populated in each fragment.
- The system of equations involves 72 equations when separating real and imaginary components of the complex amplitudes.

## Context

This work extends the bending model previously applied to cold fission by Misicu et al. (1999) and the time-dependent quantum approach used for alpha-decay by Misicu and Rizea (2000) and cold fission by Misicu and Sandulescu (2001). It addresses the experimental observations from the Gammasphere collaboration (Hamilton et al., 2003) and the data on average angular momentum as a function of neutron multiplicity reported by Pop et al. (2002). The model enables future estimations of other observables in binary fission such as translational kinetic energy and can be extended to ternary fission.
