# Isospin coupling in time-dependent-mean-field theories and decay of isovector excitations

> We show that isospin non-diagonal terms should appear in the mean field Hamiltonian when neutron-proton symmetry is broken. They give rise to charge mixing in the single-particle wave-functions. We study the Time Dependent Hartree-Fock response of a charge-exchange excitation which generates a charge mixing in Ca isotopes. We find an enhancement of the low energy proton emission in neutron-rich isotopes interpreted in terms of a charge oscillation below the barrier.

## Key claim

Isospin non-diagonal terms appear in the mean-field Hamiltonian when neutron-proton symmetry is broken, and these terms give rise to charge mixing in single-particle wave-functions; the authors show that this isospin coupling enhances low-energy proton emission in neutron-rich calcium isotopes following a charge-exchange excitation, but note that more quantitative predictions require a 3D-TDHF code with a full Skyrme force.

## Method

The authors derived the isospin coupling terms in the Hartree-Fock Hamiltonian from a Skyrme energy functional, then performed Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations using a simplified Skyrme force (with parameters adjusted to reproduce the saturation point of infinite symmetric matter and the equation of state of neutron matter from Akmal et al. (1998)). They applied an isovector-monopole boost to the ground states of 40Ca and 60Ca and computed the resulting nucleon emission spectra, comparing the number of protons emitted after a tau_3 boost (no isospin coupling) versus a tau_1 boost (with isospin coupling).

## Key numbers

- The simplified Skyrme force parameters: t_0 = -1803.38 MeV.fm^3, t_3 = 12912.80 MeV.fm^{3(1+beta)} with beta = 1/3, giving compressibility K_infinity = 236.2 MeV and symmetry energy a_s = 32.5 MeV at saturation density rho_sat = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- For 40Ca: proton separation energy S_p = 8.25 MeV, neutron separation energy S_n = 16.56 MeV
- For 60Ca: the TDHF calculation gives S_p = 19.35 MeV and S_n = 8.38 MeV (compared to HFB-Lipkin-Nogami values of S_p = 24.33 MeV and S_n = 3.57 MeV from Duguet and Bonche (unpublished))
- For a tau_1 boost with epsilon = 0.01 fm^{-2}, the expectation value of charge changed by Delta<Z> = -0.0044 in 40Ca and 0.064 in 60Ca

## Context

This work extends the concept of isospin symmetry breaking in mean-field methods, which Sugimoto et al. (2004) applied to Hartree-Fock calculations with explicit mixing of protons and neutrons followed by Variation After Projection, and which Perlinska et al. (2004) formulated within the Density Functional Theory framework for Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov. The present paper introduces isospin-symmetry breaking into Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock calculations for the first time, enabling studies of dynamical symmetry breaking in reactions such as charge-exchange excitations. The authors note that future work requires a 3D-TDHF code with a full Skyrme force to study complete reactions, and that inclusion of spin degrees of freedom would allow studies of Gamow-Teller transitions and spin-flip giant resonances.
