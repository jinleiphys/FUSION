# Deformation of Ne isotopes in the island-of-inversion region

> The deformation of Ne isotopes in the island-of-inversion region is determined by the double-folding model with the Melbourne $g$-matrix and the density calculated by the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD). The double-folding model reproduces, with no adjustable parameter, the measured reaction cross sections for the scattering of $^{28-32}$Ne from $^{12}$C at 240MeV/nucleon. The quadrupole deformation thus determined is around 0.4 in the island-of-inversion region and $^{31}$Ne is a halo nuclei with large deformation. We propose the Woods-Saxon model with a suitably chosen parameterization set and the deformation given by the AMD calculation as a convenient way of simulating the density calculated directly by the AMD. The deformed Woods-Saxon model provides the density with the proper asymptotic form. The pairing effect is investigated, and the importance of the angular momentum projection for obtaining the large deformation in the island-of-inversion region is pointed out.

## Key claim

The quadrupole deformation of Ne isotopes in the island-of-inversion region is determined to be around 0.4 by comparing double-folding model calculations with measured reaction cross sections, but the analysis relies on the assumption that the angle-averaged intrinsic density from the deformed Woods-Saxon model adequately approximates the angular-momentum-projected density for use in the reaction calculation.

## Method

The authors used the double-folding model with the Melbourne g-matrix effective nucleon-nucleon interaction to construct microscopic optical potentials for the scattering of Ne isotopes from a carbon-12 target at 240 MeV per nucleon. Projectile densities were obtained either directly from antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations with the Gogny D1S interaction, or from a deformed Woods-Saxon model whose deformation parameters were matched to the AMD results. The Woods-Saxon model included BCS pairing correlations and used the Kruppa prescription to handle continuum single-particle states near the drip line.

## Key numbers

- Reaction cross sections for Ne-28 through Ne-32 scattering from carbon-12 at 240 MeV/nucleon are reproduced with no adjustable parameters by the double-folding model.
- The quadrupole deformation parameter beta_2 is around 0.4 for Ne isotopes in the island-of-inversion region.
- For Ne-31, the tail correction to the AMD density changes the reaction cross section by about 3 percent.
- The breakup cross section for Ne-31 scattering from carbon-12 at 240 MeV/nucleon is about 1 percent of the reaction cross section.

## Context

This work extends the systematic analysis of Ne isotopes in the island-of-inversion region previously performed by Kimura (2011) using AMD with the Gogny D1S interaction, and by Minomo et al. (2011) who applied the deformed Woods-Saxon model to Ne-28 through Ne-32. The paper enables future studies of deformation in neutron-rich nuclei near the drip line by providing a practical method (the deformed Woods-Saxon model) that reproduces the more computationally expensive AMD results with tail corrections. The analysis confirms that Ne-31 is a halo nucleus with large deformation, consistent with the experimental findings of Takechi (2010) and Nakamura (2009).
