# Correlated fission fragment spin dynamics

> This study explores the role of nucleon exchange for the generation of the fission fragment angular momenta. For a number of typical fission cases, samples of 10,000 shape evolutions are generated by Langevin simulation and, subsequently, for each such evolution, the nucleon exchange transport theory previously developed for damped nuclear reactions is used to obtain the development of the fragment spin-spin distribution within the Fokker-Planck transport framework. The characteristic evolution of both parallel and perpendicular spin components is discussed. A common feature is that the rotational modes fall out of equilibrium before scission when the temperature rises rapidly while the concurrent shrinking of the neck suppresses further exchange. A number of fission observables are extracted from the event ensembles: the distribution of the magnitude of the fragment spin and its orientation relative to the fission axis, as well as the correlation between the two spins and the distribution of their opening angle. The dependence of these observables on the mass asymmetry is also examined.

# Correlated fission fragment spin dynamics

## Key claim

Nucleon exchange between the two nascent fission fragments, treated via the same transport theory previously developed for damped nuclear reactions, can account for the generation and evolution of fission fragment angular momenta; however, the rotational modes fall out of equilibrium before scission when the temperature rises rapidly while the concurrent shrinking of the neck suppresses further exchange, meaning the final spin distributions are generally smaller than the equilibrium fluctuations at scission and depend sensitively on the saddle-to-scission time.

## Method

The authors generated ensembles of 10,000 shape evolutions for each fission case using a Langevin simulation with three shape parameters (elongation, neck radius, and reflection asymmetry) and one-body dissipation. For each shape evolution, they applied the nucleon-exchange Fokker-Planck transport framework to obtain the time-dependent correlated spin-spin distribution of the two fragments, solving the coupled diffusion equations for the excess spin covariances from saddle to scission.

## Key numbers

- For each of eight cases (fission of ¹⁸²Hg, ²⁰²Po, and ²³⁶U at E* = 46 MeV, plus ²³⁶U at E* = 70 MeV, each with two wall dissipation strengths), samples of 10⁴ shape evolutions were generated.
- The saddle-to-scission time distributions are well represented by gamma distributions; for ²³⁶U at E* = 46 MeV with k_s = 1, events were categorized as "fast" (2 zs ≤ t_ss ≤ 3 zs), "medium" (6 zs ≤ t_ss ≤ 7 zs), or "slow" (10 zs ≤ t_ss ≤ 11 zs).
- The twisting relaxation time t_twst diverges as scission approaches and the neck closes; for slow events the mode remains close to local equilibrium until scission, while for fast events the mode falls behind the growing local equilibrium fluctuations.
- The wriggling mode relaxes very quickly and achieves approximate equilibrium even in fast events; the bending mode achieves only partial equilibration in fast events when initially unpopulated.

## Context

This work extends the nucleon-exchange transport theory originally developed for damped nuclear reactions (Randrup, NPA327, NPA383) to the fission context, building on earlier studies of dinuclear rotational modes (Randrup, NPA433). It provides a dynamical, mechanism-based alternative to phenomenological approaches such as FREYA (Vogt, PRC103) and addresses the same puzzle of fission fragment spin generation explored in recent correlation experiments (Nature590, Adili EPJ256, Travar PLB817). The results enable quantitative predictions for fragment spin magnitudes, orientations, and spin-spin correlations that can be compared with experimental data, and the framework can be extended to include other spin-generating mechanisms.
