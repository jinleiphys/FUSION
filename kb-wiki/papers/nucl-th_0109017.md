# Modeling the strangeness content of hadronic matter

> The strangeness content of hadronic matter is studied in a string-flip model that reproduces various aspects of the QCD-inspired phenomenology, such as quark clustering at low density and color deconfinement at high density, while avoiding long range van der Waals forces. Hadronic matter is modeled in terms of its quark constituents by taking into account its internal flavor (u,d,s) and color (red, blue, green) degrees of freedom. Variational Monte-Carlo simulations in three spatial dimensions are performed for the ground-state energy of the system. The onset of the transition to strange matter is found to be influenced by weak, yet not negligible, clustering correlations. The phase diagram of the system displays an interesting structure containing both continuous and discontinuous phase transitions. Strange matter is found to be absolutely stable in the model.

## Key claim

The string-flip model predicts that strange matter is absolutely stable, but only when flavor degeneracy (two or three flavors) is included; the one-flavor case yields only a local minimum in the equation of state, making the system unstable against breakup into isolated nucleons.

## Method

Variational Monte-Carlo simulations in three spatial dimensions were performed for a system of up to 120 quarks with flavor (u, d, s) and color (red, blue, green) degrees of freedom, using a many-body potential defined by optimal pairwise clustering of quarks into color-singlet objects. The variational wave function was taken as a product of a Fermi-gas Slater determinant and an exponential factor containing the many-body potential, with a single variational parameter λ that was minimized to obtain the ground-state energy at fixed baryon density and strangeness-to-quark ratio.

## Key numbers

- Critical Fermi momentum for onset of strangeness: k_Fc = 1.095 in units where k=m=1, corresponding to k_Fc = 328.634 MeV and ρ_c = 0.468 fm⁻³ in physical units (using constituent light-quark mass m = 300 MeV and heavy-to-light mass ratio M/m = 1.6)
- Low-density limit variational parameter: λ₀ = 1/√3 ≈ 0.577, giving energy per quark E₀/N - m = √3 ≈ 1.732 in units where k=m=1
- Transition from three-quark clusters to multi-quark configurations occurs at quark density ρ/ρ_c = 0.82, where the variational parameter drops discontinuously
- Transition into strange-quark matter occurs at ρ/ρ_c ≈ 1.2, where two- and three-flavor matter separate in the energy-per-quark plot

## Context

This work extends the one-dimensional, two-color, two-flavor string-flip model study of strange matter by Sanchez and Piekarewicz (1999) to three-dimensional, three-color, three-flavor simulations, and generalizes the non-strange three-dimensional string-flip model of Horowitz, Moniz, and Negele (1992) to include strangeness. The results enable more realistic predictions for the equation of state of hybrid stars (neutron stars with quark-matter cores) and address whether strange stars can be self-bound objects, relevant to interpreting the fast-rotating pulsar SAX J1808.4-3658 as a possible strange-star candidate.
