# Quadrupole-hexadecapole coupling in the rare earth region with beyond mean field correlations

> The roles of static hexadecapole deformation and beyond-mean-field quadrupole-hexadecapole configuration mixing are studied for a selected set of Yb, Hf, W and Os isotopes within the mass range $170 \le A \le 202$, using the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) and the two-dimensional Generator Coordinate Method (2D-GCM) approaches, based on the Gogny energy density functional. The 2D-GCM ground and excited states of the lighter isotopes are associated with diamond-like shapes while, for each isotopic chain, a region where those states correspond to square-like shapes has been found below the neutron shell closure $N=126$. It is shown, that for the studied nuclei the quadrupole and hexadecapole degrees of freedom are interwoven in the ground and excited states up to the mass number $A=184-188$. This structural evolution, encoded in the 2D-GCM collective wave functions, is accompanied by an enhanced prolate-oblate shape coexistence around the neutron number $N=116$. In agreement with previous studies, it is also shown that for the considered Yb, Hf, W and Os isotopes the inclusion of hexadecapole deformation in the ground state dynamics leads to a non trivial additional correlation energy comparable to the quadrupole correlation energy itself.

# Quadrupole-hexadecapole coupling in the rare earth region with beyond mean field correlations

## Key claim

The quadrupole and hexadecapole degrees of freedom are interwoven in the ground and excited states of Yb, Hf, W, and Os isotopes up to mass number A=184-188, but become decoupled for heavier isotopes; the additional correlation energy from including hexadecapole deformation in 2D-GCM calculations is comparable to the quadrupole correlation energy itself, though the authors caution that this indicates slow convergence of nuclear correlation energies with respect to the number of deformation parameters.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the Gogny-D1S energy density functional on a grid of axial quadrupole (β₂) and hexadecapole (β₄) deformation parameters, followed by two-dimensional Generator Coordinate Method (2D-GCM) calculations mixing configurations in both degrees of freedom. The calculations covered 68 nuclei across four isotopic chains (Yb, Hf, W, Os) in the mass range 170 ≤ A ≤ 202, using a large axially symmetric harmonic oscillator basis of 17 major shells. One-dimensional GCM calculations using only β₂ as a generating coordinate, as well as 1D-GCM calculations along the principal axes (A and B directions) of the mean-field potential energy surfaces, were also performed for comparison.

## Key numbers

- The 2D-GCM correlation energies E_Corr,2D-GCM for the studied isotopic chains fall within the range 1.15 MeV ≤ E_Corr,2D-GCM ≤ 1.73 MeV
- The quadrupole correlation energies from 1D-GCM calculations using only β₂ range from 0.49 MeV ≤ E_Corr,1D-GCM,β₂ ≤ 0.97 MeV
- The additional correlation energy from including hexadecapole effects when going from β₂-GCM to (β₂,β₄)-GCM calculations is in the range 0.66 MeV ≤ δE_Corr ≤ 0.76 MeV
- The largest prolate 2D-GCM ground state deformations are β₂ = 0.32-0.34 for ¹⁷⁰,¹⁷²Yb, ¹⁷⁰-¹⁷⁴Hf, ¹⁷⁴W, and ¹⁷⁶,¹⁷⁸Os
- The largest negative hexadecapole deformations from 2D-GCM are β₄ = -0.07 to -0.01 for square-like ground states in the region just below N=126, with the largest |β₄| values at ¹⁸²Yb, ¹⁸⁴Hf, ¹⁸⁸W, and ¹⁹⁰Os
- For ¹⁷⁶W, the principal axes of the MFPES around the minimum follow β₄,A = 1.39β₂ - 0.41 and β₄,B = -0.71β₂ + 0.30
- The HFB ground state prolate deformations for W isotopes range from β₂ = 0.18 (¹⁹⁰W) to β₂ = 0.34 (¹⁷⁴,¹⁷⁶W)
- Oblate HFB ground states are predicted for ¹⁹²-¹⁹⁸W with β₂ = -0.16, -0.14, -0.10, and -0.08

## Context

This work extends previous Gogny-HFB and 2D-GCM studies of quadrupole-hexadecapole coupling that were performed for Sm and Gd isotopes (large-scale-b4, 2016) and for Ra, Th, U, and Pu nuclei (Rodriguez-Guzman and Robledo, 2024), now applying the same methodology to the rare earth region where negative hexadecapole deformations are predicted to be largest. The results confirm expectations from the polar gap model (Moller and Nix, 1970s) regarding square-like shapes below neutron shell closures, and demonstrate that the hexadecapole degree of freedom provides correlation energy comparable to the quadrupole degree, which the authors note should be considered in future parametrizations of the Gogny energy density functional. The study enables future investigations of triaxial hexadecapole deformations and K=4 bands, though the authors acknowledge that triaxiality and gamma-softness may play a role around ¹⁹⁰W based on previous studies (Nomura et al., 2010s).
