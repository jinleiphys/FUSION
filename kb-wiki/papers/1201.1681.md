# Open charm effects in the explanation of the long-standing "$ρπ$ puzzle"

> A detailed analysis of the open charm effects on the decays of $J/ψ(ψ^\prime)\to VP$ is presented, where $V$ stands for light vector meson and $P$ for light pseudoscalar meson. These are the channels that the so-called "12% rule" of perturbative QCD (pQCD) is obviously violated. Nevertheless, they are also the channels that violate the pQCD helicity selection rule (HSR) at leading order. In this work, we put constraints on the electromagnetic (EM) contribution, short-distance contribution from the $c\bar{c}$ annihilation at the wavefunction origin, and long-distance contribution from the open charm threshold effects on these two decays. We show that interferences among these amplitudes, in particular, the destructive interferences between the short-distance and long-distance strong amplitudes play a key role to evade the HSR and cause the significant deviations from the pQCD expected "12% rule".

## Key claim

The paper argues that the long-standing "rho-pi puzzle" (the severe violation of the perturbative QCD "12% rule" in J/psi and psi' decays to rho-pi) can be explained by the interplay of three amplitudes: electromagnetic contributions, short-distance strong contributions from c-cbar annihilation at the wavefunction origin, and long-distance contributions from intermediate charmed meson loops (IML). The key mechanism is destructive interference between the short-distance and long-distance strong amplitudes, which evades the helicity selection rule and produces the observed deviations.

## Method

The authors decompose the total decay amplitude for J/psi(psi') -> VP (vector + pseudoscalar) into three components: electromagnetic (EM) amplitudes calculated via vector meson dominance with a universal form factor cutoff Lambda_EM; short-distance strong amplitudes from c-cbar annihilation at the origin, parameterized with SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking parameters xi and r for singly and doubly disconnected OZI processes; and long-distance amplitudes from intermediate charmed meson loops (D, D*, D_s, D_s*) computed using effective Lagrangians with a tri-monopole form factor parameterized by alpha. The EM and short-distance amplitudes are taken to have trivial relative phase (0 or 180 degrees), while the long-distance amplitude carries a relative phase theta determined by fitting to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The experimental ratio BR(psi' -> rho pi)/BR(J/psi -> rho pi) is (1.1 to 2.8) x 10^{-3}, much smaller than the pQCD expected value of ~12%
- The pQCD helicity selection rule predicts BR(psi' -> rho pi)/BR(J/psi -> rho pi) ~ (M_J/psi / M_psi')^6 x (BR(psi' -> hadrons)/BR(J/psi -> hadrons)) ~ 6.2%
- The EM form factor cutoff is fixed at Lambda_EM = 0.542 GeV from fits to isospin-violating channels
- The short-distance form factor uses beta = 0.5 GeV for the Gaussian momentum cutoff
- The IML form factor uses Lambda_QCD = 0.22 GeV with Lambda_i = m_i + alpha * Lambda_QCD
- The charmonium decay constant f_psi = 405 MeV is used for J/psi
- The pion decay constant f_pi = 132 MeV is adopted
- The SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking parameter xi is guided by f_pi/f_K = 0.838

## Context

This work extends the global fit analysis of Li et al. (2007), which parameterized EM and strong transition amplitudes for J/psi(psi') -> VP and found an overall suppression of strong decay amplitudes in psi' decays. The paper builds on a series of studies by Liu et al. (2009, 2010), Wang et al. (2010, 2012), and Zhang et al. (2009) that demonstrated intermediate D meson loops provide a natural mechanism for evading the OZI rule and helicity selection rule in charmonium decays. The results enable a quantitative understanding of the "rho-pi puzzle" and related puzzles in charmonium exclusive decays, including psi(3770) non-DDbar decay, large HSR-violating decays of eta_c to VV, and M1 transition problems with J/psi(psi') -> gamma eta_c(eta_c').
