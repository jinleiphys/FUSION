# The High-Z hydrogen-like atom: a model for polarized structure functions

> The Dirac equation offers a precise analytical description of relativistic two-particle bound states, when one of the constituent is very heavy and radiative corrections are neglected. Looking at the high-Z hydrogen-like atom in the infinite momentum frame and treating the electron as a "parton", various properties usually attributed to the quark distributions in the nucleon are tested, in particular: Bjørken scaling; charge, helicity, transversity and momentum sum rules; existence of the parton sea; Soffer inequality; correlation between spin and transverse momentum (Sivers and Boer-Mulders effects); transverse displacement of the center-of-charge and its connection with the magnetic moment. Deep inelastic experiments with photon or positron beams at MeV energies, analogous to DIS or Drell-Yan reactions, are considered.

# The High-Z Hydrogen-like Atom: A Model for Polarized Structure Functions

## Key claim

The Dirac equation solution for a high-Z hydrogen-like atom, when boosted to the infinite momentum frame and treating the electron as a parton, reproduces many properties of quark distributions in nucleons, but only when nucleus recoil and radiative corrections are neglected; the model is therefore best suited to mesons with one heavy quark rather than light-quark hadrons, and the analogy holds only at zero-th order in alpha and the electron-to-nucleus mass ratio.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation for a hydrogen-like atom with large nuclear charge Z (where Z*alpha is of order 1) in the atom rest frame, then apply a Lorentz boost to the infinite momentum frame (null-plane t+z=0). They define electron distributions in longitudinal momentum k+ (analogous to Bjorken x), transverse momentum k_T, and impact parameter b, including polarized distributions for the atom and electron spins. They compute the spin-dependent densities, sum rules for vector, axial, and tensor charges, the momentum sum rule, and the electron-positron sea contributions using Dirac hole theory with Fock states.

## Key numbers

- The axial charge (helicity sum rule) is Delta q = (1 - xi^2/3)/(1 + xi^2), where xi = Z*alpha/(1+gamma) and gamma = E/m_e = sqrt(1-(Z*alpha)^2); for Z*alpha = 1 this gives Delta q = 1/3 instead of the naive expectation of 1, demonstrating a "helicity crisis"
- The tensor charge (transversity sum rule) is delta q = (1 + xi^2/3)/(1 + xi^2)
- The average transverse displacement of the center of charge for a transversely polarized atom is <x> = -(1+2*gamma)/(6*m_e), which matches the atomic magnetic moment mu_A = -e*(1+2*gamma)/(6*m_e)
- The average potential energy is <V> = -m_e*(Z*alpha)^2/gamma
- The momentum sum rule gives P+_e = E - (4/3)*<V>, where E = M_A - M_N

## Context

This work extends the parton model analogy from QCD to QED by using the exactly solvable Dirac hydrogen atom as a "theoretical laboratory" for studying spin and transverse momentum structure functions. It builds on the formalism of Brodsky, Yuan, and Collins for gauge links and final-state interactions, and on the work of Burkardt connecting transverse impact parameter shifts to Sivers effects. The authors explicitly test the Soffer inequality (Soffer, 1995) and the positivity constraints of Bacchetta and collaborators. The results enable future investigation of non-leading twist structure functions and elastic form factors in the Isgur-Wise limit, and suggest that deep inelastic Compton, Moller, and annihilation experiments at MeV energies could test relativistic corrections to electronic wave functions in high-Z atoms.
