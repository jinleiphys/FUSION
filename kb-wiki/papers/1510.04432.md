# Introduction to low-momentum effective interactions with Brown-Rho scaling and three-nucleon forces

> Model-space effective interactions $V_{eff}$ derived from free-space nucleon-nucleon interactions $V_{NN}$ are reviewed. We employ a double decimation approach: first we extract a low-momentum interaction $V_{low-k}$ from $V_{NN}$ using a $T$-matrix equivalence decimation method. Then $V_{eff}$ is obtained from $V_{low-k}$ by way of a folded-diagram effective interaction method. For decimation momentum $Λ\simeq 2 fm^{-1}$, the $V_{low-k}$ interactions derived from different realistic $V_{NN}$ models are nearly model independent, and so are the resulting shell-model effective interactions. For nucleons in a low-density nuclear medium like valence nucleons near the nuclear surface, such effective interactions derived from free-space $V_{NN}$ are satisfactory in reproducing experimental nuclear properties. But it is not so for nucleons in a nuclear medium with density near or beyond nuclear matter saturation density. In this case it may be necessary to include the effects from Brown-Rho (BR) scaling of hadrons and/or three-nucleon forces $V_{3N}$, effectively changing the free-space $V_{NN}$ into a density-dependent one. The density-dependent effects from BR scaling and $V_{3N}$ are compared with those from empirical Skyrme effective interactions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for nucleons in a nuclear medium with density near or beyond nuclear matter saturation density, effective interactions derived from free-space nucleon-nucleon potentials are insufficient, and it may be necessary to include density-dependent effects from Brown-Rho scaling of hadrons and/or three-nucleon forces to reproduce experimental nuclear properties.

## Method

The authors employ a double decimation approach: first, a low-momentum interaction V_low-k is extracted from free-space nucleon-nucleon potentials using a T-matrix equivalence decimation method with a cutoff momentum Lambda approximately 2 fm^-1; then, a model-space effective interaction V_eff is obtained from V_low-k using a folded-diagram effective interaction method based on the Kuo-Lee-Ratcliff formalism. The density-dependent effects from Brown-Rho scaling and three-nucleon forces are compared with those from empirical Skyrme effective interactions.

## Key numbers

- Decimation momentum cutoff: Lambda approximately 2 fm^-1, at which V_low-k interactions derived from different realistic nucleon-nucleon models are nearly model independent
- Binding energies of calcium isotopes from Talmi's (0f_7/2)^n model: experimental 8.36 MeV versus calculated 8.38 MeV for 41Ca; experimental 73.95 MeV versus calculated 73.93 MeV for 48Ca
- Optimum parameters from Talmi's model: C = 8.38 +/- 0.05 MeV, alpha = -0.21 +/- 0.01 MeV, beta = 3.33 +/- 0.12 MeV

## Context

This work extends the long-standing program of deriving shell-model effective interactions from realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials, building on the foundational work of Brown and Kuo (1966, 1967). The paper reviews the folded-diagram expansion method developed by Kuo, Lee, and Ratcliff (1971) and the low-momentum interaction V_low-k approach from Bogner, Kuo, and collaborators (2001-2004). It addresses the ambiguity in choosing among different high-precision nucleon-nucleon potentials (Paris, CD-Bonn, Argonne V18, Nijmegen, Idaho chiral) by showing that V_low-k interactions become nearly model-independent at the decimation cutoff. The work enables future shell-model calculations for exotic nuclei requiring two-major-shell model spaces, where the recently proposed Z-box method can circumvent singularity difficulties in the Q-box vertex function.
