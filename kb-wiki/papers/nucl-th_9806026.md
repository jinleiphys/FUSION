# Medium Dependence of the Vector-Meson Mass: Dynamical and/or Brown-Rho Scaling?

> We discuss the similarities and differences for the theories of Rapp, Wambach and collaborators (called R/W in short) and those based on Brown-Rho scaling (called B/R), as applied to reproduce the dileptons measured by the CERES collaboration in the CERN experiments. In both theories the large number of dileptons at invariant masses $\sim$~$m_ρ/2$ are shown to be chiefly produced by a density-dependent $ρ$-meson mass. In R/W the medium dependence is dynamically calculated using hadronic variables defined in the matter-free vacuum. In B/R scaling it follows from movement towards chiral symmetry restoration due to medium-induced vacuum change, and is described in terms of constituent (or quasiparticle) quarks. We argue that the R/W description should be reliable up to densities somewhat beyond nuclear density, where hadrons are the effective variables. At higher density there should be a crossover to constituent quarks as effective variables scaling according to B/R. In the crossover region, the two descriptions must be ``dual''.

# Medium Dependence of the Vector-Meson Mass: Dynamical and/or Brown-Rho Scaling?

## Key claim

The dynamical lowering of the ρ-meson mass via coupling to N*(1520)-hole states (as in the Rapp/Wambach approach) and the scaling toward chiral symmetry restoration (Brown-Rho scaling) are not mutually exclusive but rather describe different density regimes, with a crossover from hadronic to constituent-quark degrees of freedom expected somewhere above nuclear matter density; the two descriptions must be "dual" in the crossover region, though there remains a factor of approximately 2 difference in predicted dilepton yields between the two theories.

## Method

The authors compare two theoretical frameworks for the in-medium ρ-meson mass: the Rapp/Wambach (R/W) approach, which calculates the ρ spectral function dynamically using hadronic variables defined in the matter-free vacuum and includes coupling to N*(1520)-hole states via an S-wave interaction Lagrangian with coupling constant f²_ρN*N/4π = 5.5, and the Brown-Rho (B/R) scaling approach, which describes the mass reduction as a consequence of movement toward chiral symmetry restoration using constituent-quark variables. The analysis uses a two-level toy model of the N*(1520) and the ρ-meson, solving the dispersion relation at vanishing three-momentum to locate the branches of the ρ spectral function, and compares the resulting dilepton spectra with CERES data from Pb+Au collisions at 158 GeV/u.

## Key numbers

- The N*(1520) excitation energy is 580 MeV (1520 MeV minus 940 MeV nucleon mass).
- At nuclear matter density n₀, the two solutions of the ρ dispersion relation (neglecting in-medium width) are located at q₀⁻ ≈ 540 MeV and q₀⁺ ≈ 895 MeV.
- The low-lying collective state carries only about 20% of the bare ρ-meson strength (Z₋ ≈ 0.2).
- The coupling constant extracted from photoexcitation data is f²_ρN*N/4π = 5.5.
- The natural width of the N*(1520) is Γ₀ ≈ 120 MeV, with an additional medium-dependent width of 250 MeV at nuclear matter density.
- From QCD sum rules, the ρ-meson mass scales as m_ρ(n_n)/m_ρ(0) = 1 − (0.16 ± 0.06)(n_n/n₀) − (0.014 ± 0.005)(|q|/0.5)²(n_n/n₀), where |q| is in GeV.
- At baryon densities around 5n₀ (initial conditions in central 160 GeV/u Pb+Au), the low-lying state reaches about 400 MeV.
- The fraction of the [N*(1520)N⁻¹]¹⁻ state that is ρ-meson-like is estimated to be f > 0.83.
- The N*(1520) decays approximately 80% of the time by π or 2π emission.

## Context

This paper extends the ongoing debate between two competing descriptions of in-medium vector-meson mass modifications relevant to the CERES dilepton excess observed at CERN-SpS. The Rapp/Wambach approach (Rapp, Wambach et al., 1997) uses hadronic degrees of freedom with dynamical self-energies, while Brown-Rho scaling (Brown and Rho, 1991; Li, Ko, and Brown, 1996) derives from chiral symmetry restoration in a constituent-quark picture. The work builds on earlier QCD sum-rule calculations by Hatsuda and Lee (1992) and by Jin and Leinweber (1995), and on the Landau Fermi-liquid relation between the B/R scaling parameter and the quasiparticle interaction constant F₁ established by Friman and Rho (1996) and by Song, Brown, Min, and Rho (1997). The paper enables future systematic studies of centrality-dependent dilepton yields to determine the density at which the hadronic description breaks down, and calls for explicit demonstration of quark-hadron duality in the intermediate density regime.
