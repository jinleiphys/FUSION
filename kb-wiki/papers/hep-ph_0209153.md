# Mixing and decays of rho- and omega-mesons

> Isospin violating mixing of ρ- and ω-mesons is reconsidered in terms of propagators. Its influence on various pairs of (ρ^0,ω)-decays to the same final states is demonstrated. Some of them, (ρ^0,ω)\toπ^+π^- and (ρ^0,ω)\toπ^0γ, have been earlier discussed in the literature, others (e.g., (ρ^0,ω)\toηγand (ρ^0,ω)\to e^+e^-) are new in this context. Changes in partial widths for all the decay pairs are shown to be correlated. The set of present experimental data, though yet inconclusive, provides some limits for the direct (ρω)-coupling and indirectly supports enhancement of ρ^0\toπ^0γin comparison with ρ^{\pm}\toπ^{\pm}γ, though not so large as in some previous estimates.

## Key claim

The paper reconsiders isospin-violating ρ-ω mixing using a propagator formalism and shows that the mixing affects a correlated set of decay pairs, but the enhancement of ρ⁰→π⁰γ relative to ρ±→π±γ suggested in earlier work (WJY) is strongly overestimated; the present experimental data, though inconclusive, provide limits on the direct ρω coupling and indirectly support a modest enhancement of ρ⁰→π⁰γ.

## Method

The author describes ρ-ω mixing via full propagators derived from summing general Feynman graphs, with transition vertices that may be complex and k²-dependent. The formalism is applied to pairs of decays (ρ⁰,ω)→π⁺π⁻, π⁰γ, ηγ, and e⁺e⁻, expressing physical amplitudes in terms of a single dimensionless mixing parameter \(\tilde{G}_{\rho\omega}\) and bare decay amplitudes related by quark-model predictions. Experimental branching ratios and partial widths from the Particle Data Group are used to extract constraints on the mixing parameter and to predict admissible intervals for decay ratios.

## Key numbers

- \(|\tilde{G}_{\omega\rho}| = (6.2 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-2}\) from \(\Gamma(\omega\to\pi^+\pi^-) = (144 \pm 24)\ \text{keV}\) and \(\Gamma_\rho = (149.2 \pm 0.7)\ \text{MeV}\).
- \(|G_{\omega\rho}| = (3.44 \pm 0.29) \times 10^{-3}\ \text{GeV}^2\) from the above and \(\delta M^2 = (23368 + 108443i)\ \text{MeV}^2\).
- For \(\rho^0\to\pi^0\gamma\) relative to \(\rho^\pm\to\pi^\pm\gamma\): \(0.80 \leq r_{\rho0/\rho\pm\pi} \leq 1.23\) (at 2σ), giving \(3.2\times10^{-4} \leq \text{Br}(\rho^0\to\pi^0\gamma) \leq 6.1\times10^{-4}\).
- For \(\omega\to\pi^0\gamma\) relative to \(\rho^\pm\to\pi^\pm\gamma\): \(r_{\omega/\rho\pm\pi} = 10.9 \pm 1.3\) (experimental), compared to the bare expectation of 9.
- For \(\rho^0\to\eta\gamma\) relative to \(\omega\to\eta\gamma\): \(r_{\rho0/\omega\eta} = 10.3 \pm 2.6\) (experimental), with admissible interval \(7.2 \leq r_{\rho0/\omega\eta} \leq 11.6\).
- For \(\eta'\to\rho^0\gamma\) relative to \(\eta'\to\omega\gamma\): \(r_{\eta'\rho0/\omega} = 9.74 \pm 1.05\) (experimental), with admissible interval \(7.2 \leq r_{\eta'\rho0/\omega} \leq 11.6\).
- For \(\rho^0\to e^+e^-\) relative to \(\omega\to e^+e^-\): \(r_{\rho0/\omega(ee)} = 11.42 \pm 0.42\) (experimental), with admissible interval \(7.2 \leq r_{\rho0/\omega(ee)} \leq 11.6\).

## Context

This work extends the propagator-based approach to ρ-ω mixing previously applied by WJY (2002) to a wider set of decay modes, including (ρ⁰,ω)→ηγ and (ρ⁰,ω)→e⁺e⁻, which are new in this context. It corrects the overestimated enhancement of ρ⁰→π⁰γ found in WJY by using the difference of pole masses rather than real masses in the mixing parameter, and it shows that the mixing effects in all decay pairs are correlated. The analysis uses experimental data from the Particle Data Group (PDT2) and earlier quark-model relations from Azimov (1963), Anisovich (1963), Soloviev (1965), and Bramon and Masso (1979). The results enable future experiments with better accuracy to determine the sign and phase of the ρω coupling, helping to distinguish between electromagnetic and strong isospin-violation mechanisms.
