# High-energy photon collisions at the LHC - dream or reality?

> We discuss the potential of high-energy photon collisions at the LHC for improving our understanding of QCD and studying the physics beyond the Standard Model. After reviewing briefly the legacy of past photoproduction experiments at LEP and HERA, we examine the gold-plated channels proposed for a photon collider at the ILC for their potential in a hadron collider environment. We stress that initial-state photon interactions have indeed been observed at RHIC and at the Tevatron. Three promising channels at the LHC are then presented in some detail: exclusive vector-meson production, measurements of possibly anomalous electroweak gauge-boson or top-quark couplings, and slepton production.

## Key claim

High-energy photon collisions at the LHC could provide a viable near-term alternative to the proposed ILC photon collider for studying QCD and beyond-Standard-Model physics, but the feasibility depends critically on forward proton detection and the ability to separate photon-induced events from pomeron-induced backgrounds.

## Method

The author reviews the legacy of photoproduction experiments at LEP and HERA, then examines the "gold-plated" channels proposed for a photon collider at the ILC and assesses their potential adaptation to a hadron collider environment. Three promising channels at the LHC are presented in detail: exclusive vector-meson production, measurements of anomalous electroweak gauge-boson or top-quark couplings, and slepton production, with discussion of the forward detector capabilities needed for each.

## Key numbers

- Maximal photon-photon center-of-mass energy in pp collisions at the LHC: 4504 GeV, compared to 400 GeV at a 500 GeV ILC
- Maximal photon-photon center-of-mass energy in OO collisions at the LHC: 486 GeV
- Expected detection rate of about 500 exclusively produced Upsilon(1S) mesons in ultraperipheral PbPb collisions at CMS for 0.5 nb^-1 integrated luminosity
- Signal cross section for exclusive Upsilon(1S) production in PbPb collisions: 173 microbarn
- About 10^5 J/psi mesons and 2 x 10^8 rho mesons expected to be produced and detected in ALICE
- For anomalous single top-quark production, the current world's best limit on the magnetic coupling kappa_tu_gamma is less than 0.14 from HERA; at the LHC with 1 (30) fb^-1, the limit may improve to 0.044 (0.029)
- For anomalous quartic gauge-boson couplings (WWgammagamma), precision may improve by a factor of 10^4 over present LEP bounds with 10 fb^-1 integrated luminosity
- Forward proton detectors at 420 m (FP420) can measure photon energies in the range 20 to 120 GeV with 1 to 2 GeV precision, and in the range 120 to 900 GeV with 10 to 12 GeV precision

## Context

This review extends the discussion of photoproduction from past experiments at LEP and HERA and from observations at RHIC and the Tevatron, where the STAR collaboration observed 52 electron-positron pairs and the CDF collaboration observed 16 exclusive e+e- events. The work builds on earlier reviews by Baur (2002) for ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions and by Strikman (2005) for photon physics at hadron colliders. It enables experimental searches at the LHC for Higgs bosons, anomalous couplings, and supersymmetric particles in photon-induced channels, though Higgs production in photon-photon collisions at the LHC is deemed insufficient for precision studies compared to the ILC.
