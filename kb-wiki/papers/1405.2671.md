# Geometrical scaling and its breaking in high energy collisions

> We report on recent analyses of different pieces of data, which exhibit Geometrical Scaling (GS) and its breaking. GS is a consequence of the existence of an intermediate energy scale, called saturation momentum, and allows to relate data at different energies, of different systems and also at different multiplicities and/or centralities.

## Key claim

Geometrical Scaling (GS), which allows data at different energies, system sizes, and centralities to be related via a single scaling variable \(\tau = p_T^2 / Q_s^2\), holds in hadronic collisions with an accuracy higher than originally expected, but breaks when the Bjorken \(x\) of one of the colliding partons exceeds a maximum value \(x_{\mathrm{max}} \approx 0.08\).

## Method

The analysis tests GS by forming ratios \(R_{W_1,W_2}(\tau) = dN/dydp_T|_{W_1}(\tau) / dN/dydp_T|_{W_2}(\tau)\) of charged-particle or identified-particle transverse momentum spectra at different collision energies \(W\); perfect GS requires these ratios to equal 1 over a wide range of \(\tau\). The optimal exponent \(\lambda\) of the saturation scale \(Q_s^2(x) = Q_0^2 (x/x_0)^{-\lambda}\) is determined by minimizing the deviation of the ratios from unity. For heavy-ion collisions, the saturation scale is scaled by \(A^{1/3}\) to account for nuclear size, and for identified particles the scaling variable uses \(\tilde{m}_T = m_T - m\) instead of \(p_T\).

## Key numbers

- The optimal exponent \(\lambda\) for LHC pp data is 0.27, which is "a bit smaller than in DIS."
- In DIS, GS extends up to \(x_{\mathrm{max}} \approx 0.08\).
- NA61/SHINE pp data at five scattering energies \(W = 17.28,\;12.36,\;8.77,\;7.75,\;6.28\) GeV show that GS holds at rapidity \(y=0.1\) but is no longer seen for \(y \geq 1.7\).
- For heavy ions, the saturation momentum scales as \(Q_{A\,s}^2 = A^{1/3} Q_s^2\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of Geometrical Scaling, first proposed in Deep Inelastic Scattering by Stasto, Golec-Biernat, and Kharzeev (2000), to pp and heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. It builds on earlier analyses by McLerran and Praszalowicz (2010) and Praszalowicz (2011, 2012) that demonstrated GS in minimum-bias pp data and in PHOBOS Au-Au and Cu-Cu spectra. The paper also connects GS to Tsallis-like parametrizations of particle spectra (Tsallis, 1988; Rybczynski, 2012; Cleymans, 2013) and identifies the breaking of GS at large rapidity as a tool to probe the underlying Color Glass Condensate theory.
