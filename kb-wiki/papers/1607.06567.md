# Spin-orbit interaction in relativistic nuclear structure models

> Relativistic self-consistent mean-field (SCMF) models naturally account for the coupling of the nucleon spin to its orbital motion, whereas non-relativistic SCMF methods necessitate a phenomenological ansatz for the effective spin-orbit potential. Recent experimental studies aim to explore the isospin properties of the effective spin-orbit interaction in nuclei. SCMF models are very useful in the interpretation of the corresponding data, however standard relativistic mean-field and non-relativistic Hartree-Fock models use effective spin-orbit potentials with different isovector properties, mainly because exchange contributions are not treated explicitly in the former. The impact of exchange terms on the effective spin-orbit potential in relativistic mean-field models is analysed, and it is shown that it leads to an isovector structure similar to the one used in standard non-relativistic Hartree-Fock. Data on the isospin dependence of spin-orbit splittings in spherical nuclei could be used to constrain the isovector-scalar channel of relativistic mean-field models. The reproduction of the empirical kink in the isotope shifts of even Pb nuclei by relativistic effective interactions points to the occurrence of pseudospin symmetry in the single-neutron spectra in these nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper shows that when exchange terms (Fock terms) are treated explicitly in relativistic mean-field models, the isovector structure of the effective spin-orbit potential becomes similar to that used in standard non-relativistic Hartree-Fock models, with the ratio W1/W2 approaching 1.8 rather than the value near 1 found in standard relativistic mean-field (RMF) models. This result is conditional on performing a Fierz transformation of the point-coupling relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) Lagrangian to obtain an equivalent RMF Lagrangian that includes an isovector-scalar term.

## Method

The authors performed a non-relativistic reduction of the single-nucleon Dirac equation to a Schrodinger-like form that explicitly exhibits the spin-orbit potential, deriving analytic expressions for the ratio W1/W2 of the two parameters in the spin-orbit potential for both RMF and RHF cases. For the RHF case, they applied a Fierz transformation to the point-coupling approximation of the RHF Lagrangian to obtain an equivalent RMF Lagrangian, which includes an additional isovector-scalar term not present in standard RMF models. They then calculated the radial dependence of W1/W2 for protons and neutrons in the ground states of 16O, 34Si, and 208Pb using the RMF interactions DD-ME2 and DD-PC1, and the RHF interaction PKO2, and compared the resulting isotope shifts for even-A Pb nuclei with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- In standard RMF models (DD-ME2 and DD-PC1), the ratio W1/W2 is close to 1 for both protons and neutrons in 16O, 34Si, and 208Pb.
- In the RHF case using PKO2, the ratio W1/W2 is around 1.8 for the same nuclei, significantly larger than in the simple RMF approach and much closer to the value 2 used in standard non-relativistic Hartree-Fock calculations with Skyrme forces.
- The isovector part of the spin-orbit potential in models with both rho and delta meson exchange (DD-MEdelta) is an order of magnitude weaker than the isoscalar contribution.
- The experimental isotope shifts for even-A Pb nuclei with respect to 208Pb show a kink at N=126, which is reproduced by DD-PC1, PKO2, and the Fierz-transformed PKO2, with the most pronounced kink occurring in the Fierz-transformed calculation where W1/W2 is approximately 1.8.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Sulaksono et al. (2006) by focusing specifically on the isospin dependence of the spin-orbit effect using relativistic energy density functionals with density-dependent strength parameters. It addresses the known difference between RMF models and non-relativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock models (Vautherin 1972) in their isovector properties, where RMF models give W1/W2 close to 1 while Skyrme models use W1/W2 = 2. The results enable future experimental studies of spin-orbit splittings in spherical nuclei, such as those on 35Si and 37S by Burgunder et al. (2014), to constrain the isovector-scalar channel of relativistic mean-field models, which has been difficult to determine from standard data on masses and radii. The reproduction of the empirical kink in Pb isotope shifts, previously studied by Reinhard and Flocard (1995) and Goddard et al. (2013), provides evidence for pseudospin symmetry in neutron-rich Pb nuclei.
