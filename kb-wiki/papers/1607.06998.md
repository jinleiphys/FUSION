# Spectrum of shear modes in the neutron-star crust: Estimating the nuclear-physics uncertainties

> I construct a model of the inner crust of neutron stars using interactions from chiral effective field theory (EFT) in order to calculate its equation of state (EOS), shear properties, and the spectrum of crustal shear modes. I systematically study uncertainties associated with the nuclear physics input, the crust composition, and neutron entrainment, and estimate their impact on crustal shear properties and the shear-mode spectrum. I find that the uncertainties originate mainly in two sources: The neutron-matter EOS and neutron entrainment. I compare the spectrum of crustal shear modes to observed frequencies of quasi-periodic oscillations in the afterglow of giant $γ$-ray bursts and find that all of these frequencies could be described within uncertainties, which are, however, at present too sizable to infer neutron-star properties from observations.

## Key claim

The paper systematically estimates nuclear-physics uncertainties in the spectrum of crustal shear modes of neutron stars, finding that all observed quasi-periodic oscillation (QPO) frequencies from giant gamma-ray bursts can be described within these uncertainties, but the uncertainties are currently too large to infer neutron-star properties from observations.

## Method

The author constructs a model of the inner neutron-star crust using a Gibbs construction within the Wigner-Seitz approximation, employing interactions from chiral effective field theory (EFT) with systematic uncertainty estimates. The model calculates the equation of state, shear modulus, shear velocities, and the frequency spectrum of fundamental and first-overtone crustal shear modes, varying parameters for the neutron-matter EOS, crust composition (proton number Z and surface parameters), and neutron entrainment fraction.

## Key numbers

- Crust-core transition density ranges from 0.074 to 0.090 fm^{-3}
- Symmetry energy S_v ranges from 29.7 to 33.2 MeV
- Symmetry energy density dependence L ranges from 32.5 to 57 MeV
- Incompressibility parameter K = 236 MeV and skewness K' = -384 MeV
- Shear velocities vary by a factor of 2 when varying the entrainment parameter R_e from 0 to 1
- Observed QPO frequencies considered range from 18 to 1800 Hz from SGR 1806-20 and SGR 1900+14
- Neutron-star masses varied from 1.00 to 1.97 M_sun in the frequency calculations

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of crustal shear properties by Steiner and Watts (2009) and Sotani (2013) by incorporating systematic nuclear-physics uncertainties from chiral EFT interactions, rather than using only Skyrme or phenomenological interactions. The results enable future comparisons with global magnetar oscillation models that include magnetic field coupling between crust and core, as studied by Levin (2006) and Gabler (2010), and highlight that improved constraints on the neutron-matter EOS and neutron entrainment are needed before QPO observations can be used to determine neutron-star properties.
