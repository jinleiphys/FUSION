# Hidden strange pentaquark in constituent quark models

> In the framework of the chiral quark model, we investigate the hidden strange pentaquark system of the $Nϕ$ state with the quantum numbers of IJ=$\frac{1}{2}$$\frac{3}{2}$. The results show that the $Nϕ$ state can be bound through the interaction of the $σ$ meson exchange plus the effect of the channel coupling, which means that the effect of the channel coupling has an influence on the existence of this bound state.

# Hidden strange pentaquark in constituent quark models

## Key claim
The Nφ state with quantum numbers IJ = 1/2 3/2 can become bound through the combined effect of σ meson exchange and channel coupling, but only in one of the three chiral quark model variants tested (ChQM1); in the other two variants (ChQM2 and ChQM3) the state remains unbound even with channel coupling.

## Method
The authors performed dynamical calculations using the resonating group method (RGM) within three variants of the chiral quark model (ChQM1, ChQM2, ChQM3), which differ in how the scalar meson exchange is implemented. They computed effective potentials between the nucleon and φ meson clusters, then solved the bound-state problem including up to 16 coupled channels that incorporate both color-singlet and hidden-color configurations of the five-quark system.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy in ChQM1 with five color-singlet channels coupled: B = -5.7 MeV
- Binding energy in ChQM1 with all 16 channels coupled: B = -12.3 MeV
- Single-channel Nφ state is unbound (labeled "ub") in all three models: ChQM1, ChQM2, and ChQM3
- The σ meson exchange potential provides attraction in ChQM1 and ChQM3, but in ChQM2 the σ meson is restricted to u/d quark pairs only and gives no contribution to the Nφ interaction
- Calculated masses: N = 939 MeV, φ = 1056 MeV (ChQM1), φ = 1016 MeV (ChQM2), φ = 1054 MeV (ChQM3); experimental φ mass = 1019 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous studies of hidden strange pentaquarks, particularly the Nφ system, which was proposed by Gao et al. (2001) as a possible bound state via QCD Van der Waals forces. The authors had previously studied the Nφ state in the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) of Huang et al., where a bound state was also obtained by channel coupling. The present results in ChQM1 are consistent with that earlier QDCSM work, but the model dependence shown by ChQM2 and ChQM3 indicates that the existence of a bound Nφ state is sensitive to the treatment of scalar meson exchange. The results enable future experimental searches for the Nφ bound state, such as through near-threshold φ meson production from heavy nuclei, and complement lattice QCD calculations that also support such a bound state.
