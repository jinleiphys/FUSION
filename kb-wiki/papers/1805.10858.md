# Sequential Coalescence with Charm Conservation in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> Heavy quarks are initially produced in nuclear collisions and the number is conserved during the evolution of the system. We establish a sequential coalescence model with charm conservation and apply it to charmed hadron production at RHIC and LHC energies. The charm conservation enhances the earlier formed hadrons and reduces the later formed ones, which leads to a $D_s/D^0$ enhancement and a $Λ_c/D^0$ suppression. The mass dependence of the sequential hadron formation provides us a new tool for studying the quark-gluon plasma hadronization in high energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that charm quark number conservation during the sequential hadronization of the quark-gluon plasma enhances earlier-formed charmed hadrons (like \(D_s^+\)) and suppresses later-formed ones (like \(D^0\) and \(\Lambda_c\)), leading to a \(D_s/D^0\) enhancement and a \(\Lambda_c/D^0\) suppression relative to simultaneous coalescence models that ignore the conservation constraint.

## Method

The authors solve the two-body Dirac equation with a lattice-simulated quark-antiquark potential at finite temperature to determine dissociation temperatures for charmed mesons, then use hydrodynamic equations to obtain the coalescence times when the fireball temperature drops to each hadron's dissociation temperature. They compute charmed hadron spectra via a sequential coalescence model where the charm quark density available for later hadrons is reduced by the number of charm quarks already consumed by earlier hadrons, using Wigner functions derived from the Dirac wave functions for mesons and a Gaussian parameterization for baryons.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass fixed at \(m_c = 1.31\) GeV; light quark masses \(m_u = m_d = 0.25\) GeV, \(m_s = 0.35\) GeV.
- Dissociation temperatures: \(T_{D_s^+} = 1.2\ T_c\) and \(T_{D_s^{*+}} \simeq T_{D^0} \simeq T_{D^{*0}} \simeq T_{D^{*+}} = 1.15\ T_c\), with \(T_c = 165\) MeV.
- Initial fireball temperatures: \(T_0 = 320\) MeV in central Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV (RHIC) and \(T_0 = 485\) MeV in central Pb+Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV (LHC).
- Charm quark production cross sections: \(d\sigma_{pp}^{c\bar c}/d\eta = 340\ \mu b\) at RHIC and \(1.2\ mb\) at LHC.
- Strangeness fugacity factor: \(\gamma_s = 0.85\) at RHIC, \(\gamma_s = 1\) at LHC.
- Thermalization parameters \((\alpha,\beta)\): \((0.4,0.6)\) for \(D_s^+\), \((0.5,0.5)\) for \(D^0\) and related mesons, \((0.6,0.4)\) for charmed baryons.
- Charm fraction for later \(D^0\) production: \(r \simeq 90\%\) after \(D_s^+\) formation; charm fraction for \(\Lambda_c\): \(r \simeq 0.6\).
- Predicted ratio \(\bar B_s^0/\bar B^0 \simeq 1\) in central Pb+Pb at LHC for bottomed hadrons.

## Context

This work extends the sequential hadronization picture supported by earlier studies of \(J/\psi\) dissociation (Satz 2005, Guo 2012) and multi-strange hadron freeze-out (Hecke), and applies it to open charm hadrons using a coalescence mechanism previously used for light hadron scaling laws (Molnar 2003, Fries 2003) and charmed hadron yields (Greco 2003, He 2014). The model enables predictions for \(D_s/D^0\) and \(\Lambda_c/D^0\) ratios that can be tested against STAR and ALICE data, and can be extended to bottomed hadrons where the conservation effect is predicted to be even stronger.
