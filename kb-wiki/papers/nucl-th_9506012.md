# The Path of Hot Nuclei Towards Multifragmemtation

> The initial production and dynamical expansion of hot spherical nuclei are examined as the first stage in the projectile-multifragmentation process. The initial temperatures, which are necessary for entering the adiabatic spinodal region, as well as the minimum temperatures and densities, which are reached in the expansion, significantly differ for hard and soft equations of state. Additional initial compression, occurring in central collisions leads most likely to a qualitatively different multifragmentation mechanism. Recent experimental data are discussed in relation to the results of the proposed model.

## Key claim

The expansion of hot spherical nuclei produced in peripheral relativistic heavy-ion collisions can reach the adiabatic spinodal region (mechanical volume instability) only for sufficiently high initial temperatures, and the required initial temperatures differ significantly between soft and hard equations of state; the presence or absence of initial compression (as in central collisions) leads to qualitatively different multifragmentation mechanisms.

## Method

The authors model the initial projectile residue using a geometrical abrasion model with excitation energy of 13.3 MeV per abrased nucleon (or 26.6 MeV for small mass loss), then simulate the dynamical expansion of a homogeneous spherical nucleus assuming radial flow and including neutron and proton evaporation. The expansion is calculated for two Skyrme-force equations of state (SkM* for soft, SIII for stiff) by solving energy and entropy conservation with evaporation loss terms, following the trajectories in the temperature-density plane up to turning points (density minima) within 200 fm/c.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperatures of about 8 MeV (soft EOS) and 11 MeV (hard EOS) are needed to enter the adiabatic spinodal region and stay there for more than 30 fm/c
- Turning points exist for initial temperatures up to about 14 MeV for both equations of state
- Turning points for the same initial excitation lie one to two MeV in temperature and 0.1 to 0.2 nuclear matter densities (in units of normal nuclear density) higher for the stiff EOS compared to the soft EOS
- Turning-point temperatures plateau around 4 MeV for the soft EOS and 5 MeV for the hard EOS, independent of initial excitation
- For initial temperatures larger than about 12 MeV to 14 MeV, the system reaches densities well below 0.3 normal nuclear density before approaching the turning point
- Experimental errors in excitation energy transform into errors in density of about ±0.06 normal nuclear density
- The onset of multifragmentation is around 8 MeV initial temperature in agreement with the soft EOS

## Context

This work extends the study of projectile multifragmentation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, motivated by experimental results from the ALADIN collaboration (Pochodzalla et al., 1994) on Au reactions at 600 MeV/u to 1 GeV/u. The model builds on the abrasion model of Gaimard and Schmidt and the evaporation model of H. W. Barz et al., and contrasts with the expanding emitting source (EES) model of Friedman. The results enable future dynamical studies of the fragmentation process starting from the calculated turning points, and suggest that projectile fragmentation (without compression) is optimal for studying the liquid-gas phase transition region of nuclear matter, unlike central collisions which lead to explosion.
