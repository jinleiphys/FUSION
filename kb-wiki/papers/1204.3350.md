# Effects of Symmetry Energy in the Reaction 40Ca+124Sn at 140 MeV/nucleon

> The density-dependent symmetry energy is a hot topic in nuclear physics. Many laboratories over the world are planning to perform related experiments to probe the symmetry energy. Based on the semiclassical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model, we study the effects of nuclear symmetry energy in the central reaction 40Ca+124Sn at 140MeV/nucleon in the laboratory system. It is found that the rapidity distribution of free nucleon's neutron-to-proton ratio is sensitive to the symmetry energy, especially at large rapidities. The free neutron-to-proton ratios at small or large rapidities may reflect high or low density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy. To probe the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy, it is better to give the kinetic distribution and the rapidity distribution of emitted nucleons at the same time.

## Key claim

The rapidity distribution of the free nucleon neutron-to-proton ratio in the central reaction 40Ca+124Sn at 140 MeV/nucleon is sensitive to the symmetry energy, especially at large rapidities; the free n/p ratios at small or large rapidities may reflect high or low density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy, respectively, and probing both the kinetic and rapidity distributions simultaneously is recommended.

## Method

The authors used the semiclassical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model (IBUU04) to simulate the central reaction 40Ca+124Sn at 140 MeV/nucleon in the laboratory system. The model includes nucleons, Delta and N* resonances, pions, isospin-dependent dynamics, and a momentum- and isospin-dependent single nucleon potential derived from a modified Gogny effective interaction with a parameter x that controls the density dependence of the symmetry energy. Free nucleons were identified as those with local baryon densities less than one-eighth of saturation density, and the neutron-to-proton ratio was analyzed as a function of reduced rapidity and kinetic energy.

## Key numbers

- The maximal baryon density reached in the central reaction is about 1.5 times saturation density (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3).
- The symmetry energy at normal nuclear matter density is about 32 MeV.
- The incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter at normal density is set to 211 MeV.
- The maximal longitudinal momentum at positive momentum part reached is about 0.9 GeV/c, while at negative momentum part it is only about 0.3 GeV/c.
- At the reaction time t = 50 fm/c, the reaction almost ends.
- Free nucleons are identified as those having local baryon densities less than rho = (1/8) rho_0.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of symmetry energy effects on pre-equilibrium neutron/proton ratios in heavy-ion collisions, such as those by Zhang et al. using the ImQMD model and Feng using the LQMD model, and by Ma et al. using the IQMD model. The results suggest that the free n/p ratio at large rapidities reflects low-density symmetry energy behavior, while at small rapidities it reflects high-density behavior, providing guidance for experiments planned at NSCL, GSI, RIKEN, and CSR in Lanzhou to probe the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy.
