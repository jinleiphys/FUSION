# Dynamics of the $O(4)$ critical point in QCD

> We perform a real-time simulation of the $O(4)$ critical point of QCD, which lies in the dynamic universality class of Model G. The axial charge and the order parameter $ϕ_a =(σ, \vecπ)$ exhibit a rich dynamical interplay, which reflects the qualitative differences in the hydrodynamic effective theories above and below $T_c$. From the axial charge correlators on the critical line we extract a dynamical critical exponent of $ζ=1.47 \pm 0.01 ({\rm stat})$, which is compatible with the theoretical expectation of $ζ= d/2$ (with $d=3$) when systematic errors are taken into account. At low temperatures, we quantitatively match the $O(4)$ simulations to the superfluid effective theory of soft pions.

## Key claim

The paper performs real-time simulations of the O(4) critical point in QCD and extracts a dynamical critical exponent of ζ = 1.47 ± 0.01 (statistical), which is compatible with the theoretical expectation of ζ = d/2 (with d = 3) only when systematic errors are taken into account, not as a precise confirmation.

## Method

The authors simulate the real-time dynamics of Model G, which describes the O(4) critical point, using a stochastic Langevin approach with an operator-splitting method: a symplectic integrator evolves the ideal (Hamiltonian) part while a Metropolis algorithm handles the dissipative Langevin dynamics. The system is discretized on a spatial lattice of size L³, and the equations of motion couple the O(4) order parameter φ_a = (σ, π) to the adjoint charge densities n_ab, which represent the iso-vector and iso-axial vector charge densities of QCD. Correlation functions of the order parameter and axial charge are computed at zero spatial momentum to study dynamical scaling.

## Key numbers

- Dynamical critical exponent ζ = 1.47 ± 0.01 (statistical), extracted from axial charge correlators on the critical line
- Critical mass parameter m_c² = -4.8110(4) for λ = 4
- Non-universal constant H_0 = 5.15(5)
- Ratio 𝔪²/|m_c²| = 1.03(2)
- Simulations performed on lattices up to L³ = 80³ volume
- Charge susceptibility χ̂_0 = 5, bare kinetic coefficient Γ̂_0 = 1, and D_0/Γ_0 = 1/3 used as model parameters

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Rajagopal and Wilczek (1992), who identified the dynamic universality class of QCD as Model G and predicted ζ = d/2. It builds on earlier classical-statistical simulations of O(4) models by Schlichting (2019) and Schweitzer (2020), which could not conclusively extract the dynamical exponent or study the interplay between order parameter and axial charge. The results enable future simulations of the O(4) critical point in expanding fluids, which could be matched to a pion-hydro effective field theory for T ≲ T_c and ultimately to experimental measurements of soft pion yields in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
