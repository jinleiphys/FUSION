# Superfluid Condensate Fraction and Pairing Wave Function of the Unitary Fermi Gas

> The unitary Fermi gas is a many-body system of two-component fermions with zero-range interactions tuned to infinite scattering length. Despite much activity and interest in unitary Fermi gases and its universal properties, there have been great difficulties in performing accurate calculations of the superfluid condensate fraction and pairing wave function. In this work we present auxiliary-field lattice Monte Carlo simulations using a novel lattice interaction which accelerates the approach to the continuum limit, thereby allowing for robust calculations of these difficult observables. As a benchmark test we compute the ground state energy of 33 spin-up and 33 spin-down particles. As a fraction of the free Fermi gas energy $E_{FG}$, we find $E_0/E_{FG}= 0.369(2), 0.372(2)$, using two different definitions of the finite-system energy ratio, in agreement with the latest theoretical and experimental results. We then determine the condensate fraction by measuring off-diagonal long-range order in the two-body density matrix. We find that the fraction of condensed pairs is $α= 0.43(2)$. We also extract the pairing wave function and find the pair correlation length to be $ζ_pk_F = 1.8(3) \hbar$, where $k_F$ is the Fermi momentum. Provided that the simulations can be performed without severe sign oscillations, the methods we present here can be applied to superfluid neutron matter as well as more exotic P-wave and D-wave superfluids.

## Key claim

The authors present the first *ab initio* calculations of the superfluid condensate fraction and pairing wave function of the unitary Fermi gas using two-sided expectation values of the two-body density matrix, free from the systematic errors of previous one-sided estimates. The results are obtained for a finite system of 66 particles and extrapolated to the dilute limit, with the caveat that the methods rely on the absence of severe sign oscillations.

## Method

The authors performed auxiliary-field lattice Monte Carlo simulations using a novel nonlocal S-wave lattice interaction that accelerates convergence to the continuum limit by zeroing out higher angular momentum channels and minimizing finite-density corrections. They computed the two-body density matrix via Euclidean time projection from a free Fermi gas trial state for 33 spin-up and 33 spin-down particles on periodic cubic lattices with sizes L = 5 to L = 11. The condensate fraction was extracted from off-diagonal long-range order in the projected two-body density matrix, and the pairing wave function was obtained from the asymptotic factorization of the two-body density matrix.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy ratio: E0/EFG = 0.369(2) using the thermodynamic definition and 0.372(2) using the finite-system definition, for N=66 particles at unitarity.
- Condensate fraction: α = 0.43(2) for the N=66 system at unitarity.
- Pair correlation length: ζp kF = 1.8(3) ℏ, where kF is the Fermi momentum.
- Lattice interaction parameters: smearing parameter sNL = -0.0100, coefficient C0 = -0.5573 in lattice units, corresponding to infinite scattering length as and effective range re ~ 0.05 lattice units.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice Monte Carlo calculations of the unitary Fermi gas ground state energy (e.g., Lee (2006), Bour (2011)) by providing the first two-sided calculation of the two-body density matrix, eliminating systematic errors present in earlier one-sided estimates. The condensate fraction result α = 0.43(2) agrees with experimental measurements of 0.46(7) and 0.47(7) in ultracold 6Li (Regal (2004), Zwierlein (2004)) and with the theoretical prediction of 0.43 from Ref. paper 4.48+2, while being lower than earlier quantum Monte Carlo estimates of 0.57(2) and 0.56(1). The methods can be applied to superfluid neutron matter and exotic P-wave and D-wave superfluids, provided sign oscillations are manageable.
