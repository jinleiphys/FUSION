# Relativistic Mean-Field Models with Effective Hadron Masses and Coupling Constants, and rho^- Condensation

> We study relativistic mean-field models with hadron masses and coupling constants depending self-consistently on a scalar meson field. We demonstrate that by the field redefinition some models can be equivalently transformed into each other. Thereby the large variety of scaling functions for masses and couplings can be reduced to a restricted set of functions with a constrained dependence on a scalar field. We show how by choosing properly the latter scaling functions one may stiffen or soften the equation of state at high densities and simultaneously increase the threshold density for the direct Urca process without any change of the description of nuclear matter close to the saturation density. The stiffening of the equation of state might be motivated by recent neutron star mass measurements, whereas the increase of the threshold density for the direct Urca process is required by the analysis of neutron star cooling data. We demonstrate that if a rho meson is included in a mean-field model as a non-Abelian gauge boson, then there is a possibility for a charged rho-meson condensation in dense nuclear matter. We show that such a novel phase can be realized in neutron star interiors already for sufficiently low densities, typically $\sim 3÷4 n_0$, where $n_0$ is the nuclear saturation density. In the framework of the relativistic mean field model the new phase arises in a second-order phase transition. The appearance of a $ρ^-$ condensate significantly alters the proton fraction in a neutron star but changes moderately the equation of state. The neutrino emissivity of the processes involving a $ρ^-$ meson condensate is estimated.

## Key claim

By allowing hadron masses and coupling constants to scale differently with the scalar field, one can simultaneously stiffen the equation of state at high densities (to accommodate heavy neutron stars) and increase the threshold density for the direct Urca process (to match neutron star cooling data), without altering the description of nuclear matter near saturation density.

## Method

The authors construct a generalized relativistic mean-field (RMF) Lagrangian where hadron masses and coupling constants are field-dependent functions of the scalar sigma meson field. They demonstrate that through field redefinitions, the twelve original scaling functions reduce to only three independent functions in the energy-density functional. They then impose different scaling relations for masses and couplings, solve the mean-field equations for homogeneous nuclear matter, and compute the equation of state and proton fraction. For the charged rho-meson condensation, they treat the rho meson as a non-Abelian gauge boson and solve for the onset of a nonzero charged rho mean field.

## Key numbers

- The charged rho-meson condensate appears at densities typically around 3 to 4 times nuclear saturation density \(n_0\), where \(n_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
- The modified Walecka model with input parameters \(n_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\), binding energy \(-16\ \text{MeV}\), symmetry energy \(32\ \text{MeV}\), compressibility \(K = 270\ \text{MeV}\), and effective nucleon mass \(m_N^*(n_0)/m_N = 0.8\) yields coupling constants \(C_\sigma^2 = 189.94\), \(C_\omega^2 = 90.768\), \(C_\rho^2 = 100.18\), with nonlinear parameters \(b = 6.3714 \times 10^{-3}\) and \(c = 1.6288 \times 10^{-2}\).
- The Zimanyi-Moszkowski model with the same saturation input gives \(K \simeq 224.5\ \text{MeV}\) and \(m_N^*(n_0)/m_N \simeq 0.85\), with coupling constants \(C_\sigma^2 = 169.36\), \(C_\omega^2 = 59.055\), \(C_\rho^2 = 104.56\).
- The standard Walecka model with the same saturation input produces \(K \simeq 553\ \text{MeV}\) and \(m_N^*(n_0)/m_N \simeq 0.54\), with \(C_\sigma^2 = 329.70\), \(C_\omega^2 = 249.40\), \(C_\rho^2 = 68.09\).
- The Urbana-Argonne microscopic calculation (A18+delta v+UIX*) gives a direct Urca threshold density \(n_{\text{crit}}^{\text{DU}} \simeq 5 n_0\), corresponding to a critical neutron star mass \(M_{\text{crit}}^{\text{DU}} \simeq 2 M_\odot\).
- Measured neutron star masses include \(M_{\text{B1913+16}} = 1.4408 \pm 0.0003 M_\odot\), \(M_{\text{J0737-3039B}} = 1.250 \pm 0.005 M_\odot\), and \(M_{\text{J0751+1807}} = 2.2 \pm 0.2 M_\odot\).

## Context

This work extends the Brown-Rho scaling conjecture (Brown and Rho, 1991) by allowing different scaling functions for hadron masses and coupling constants, rather than a universal scaling. It addresses the long-standing problem that standard RMF models (Walecka, 1974; Boguta and Bodmer, 1977) produce too low a direct Urca threshold density and too soft an equation of state at high densities, conflicting with neutron star cooling data and mass measurements (such as the \(2.2 M_\odot\) candidate J0751+1807 from Nice et al., 2004). The charged rho-meson condensation mechanism was first proposed in Voskresensky (1997) and is analogous to gluon condensation in QCD (Migdal, 1978) and W-boson condensation in the electroweak sector (Linde, 1976). The results enable future studies of neutron star cooling with exotic phases and provide constraints for the CBM experimental program at GSI.
