# Two-photon exchange corrections to the pion form factor

> We compute two-photon exchange corrections to the electromagnetic form factor of the pion, taking into account the finite size of the pion. Compared to the soft-photon approximation for the infrared divergent contribution which neglects hadron structure effects, the corrections are found to be < 1% for small Q^2 (Q^2 < 0.1 GeV^2), but increase to several percent for Q^2 > 1 GeV^2 at extreme backward angles.

## Key claim

The two-photon exchange (TPE) correction to the pion electromagnetic form factor, computed with a finite-size pion, is less than 1% at low momentum transfer (Q^2 < 0.1 GeV^2) but grows to several percent at Q^2 > 1 GeV^2 at extreme backward angles, relative to the standard soft-photon (Mo and Tsai) infrared approximation that neglects hadron structure.

## Method

The authors computed the TPE amplitude for elastic electron-pion scattering including box and crossed-box diagrams, using a monopole parametrization for the pion form factor with a cutoff Lambda = 770 MeV (the rho-meson mass). The loop integrals were evaluated analytically in terms of Passarino-Veltman functions using the FEYNCALC program, and the TPE correction delta was defined as the interference of the TPE amplitude with the Born amplitude normalized to the Born cross section. The full result was compared to two infrared approximations: the Mo and Tsai (1969) soft-photon prescription and the Maximon and Tjon prescription.

## Key numbers

- TPE correction delta is less than 1% for Q^2 < 0.1 GeV^2
- TPE correction reaches several percent for Q^2 > 1 GeV^2 at extreme backward angles
- At Q^2 = 0.01 GeV^2, the correction is positive and about 1% at backward angles (small epsilon)
- At Q^2 = 10 GeV^2, the correction reaches approximately 1%
- The monopole parametrization uses Lambda = 770 MeV (the rho-meson mass)
- The correction reaches its minimum values at Q^2 = 1-2 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the methodology developed for TPE corrections in electron-nucleon scattering by Blunden, Melnitchouk, and Tjon (2003, 2005) to the spin-0 pion case, motivated by the large discrepancies between Rosenbluth separation and polarization transfer measurements of the proton form factor (Jones, 2000). The results show that TPE corrections to the pion form factor are smaller than those for the proton because no longitudinal-transverse separation is needed for a spin-0 target, but they will need to be included in high-precision extractions of F_pi from electroproduction data at Jefferson Lab up to Q^2 ~ 6 GeV^2.
