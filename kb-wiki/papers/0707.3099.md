# Existence of a Density Functional for an Intrinsic State

> A generalization of the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem proves the existence of a density functional for an intrinsic state, symmetry violating, out of which a physical state with good quantum numbers can be projected.

## Key claim
The paper proves the existence of a density functional for a symmetry-violating intrinsic state (not an eigenstate of the Hamiltonian), from which a physical state with good quantum numbers can be projected, but the functional necessarily depends on both the chosen quantum numbers and the restricted variational space for the intrinsic state.

## Method
The authors generalize the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem by introducing a constrained search over trial wave functions (or density matrices) that yield a given particle density, minimizing the ratio ⟨φ|PH|φ⟩/⟨φ|P|φ⟩ where P is a projector onto good quantum numbers and H is the physical Hamiltonian. They derive variational equations for the intrinsic state using a gradient operator formalism, and show that the intrinsic Hamiltonian can be expressed as a linear combination of the projected Hamiltonian and the projector itself, with a Lagrange multiplier adjusted self-consistently.

## Key numbers
- No explicit numerical results are presented; the paper is a formal existence proof and theoretical development.

## Context
This work extends the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem (Hohenberg and Kohn, 1964) to intrinsic states used in nuclear and molecular physics, where symmetry-breaking wave packets (e.g., deformed Slater determinants) are projected onto good quantum numbers. It formalizes the connection between density functional theory and the variation-after-projection method (DFGO, 1977), and addresses the same problem of symmetry restoration in DFT that was previously treated for spin densities (Gunnarsson and Lundqvist, 1976) and particle number projection (Riedel, 1975). The approach enables a rigorous foundation for using particle density functionals with intrinsic states in nuclear structure calculations, where Skyrme energy density functionals are commonly employed but lack such a formal basis.
