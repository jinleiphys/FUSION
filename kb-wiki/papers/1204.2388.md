# Effect of spectral modification of $ρ$ on shear viscosity of a pion gas

> We evaluate the shear viscosity of a pion gas in the relativistic kinetic theory approach. The in-medium propagator of the $ρ$ meson at finite temperature is used to evaluate the $π-π$ scattering amplitude in the medium. The real and imaginary parts of the self-energy calculated from one-loop diagrams are seen to have noticeable effects on the scattering cross-section. The consequences on temperature dependence of the shear viscosity evaluated in the Chapman-Enskog and relaxation time approximations are studied.

# Effect of spectral modification of ρ on shear viscosity of a pion gas

## Key claim

The shear viscosity of a pion gas shows a noticeable but modest reduction (approximately 10% at T=150 MeV) when the in-medium spectral modification of the ρ meson is included in the π-π scattering cross-section, with the effect diminishing to less than 5% at 100 MeV and becoming negligible below approximately 60 MeV, contrary to the abstract's implication of a generally "noticeable" effect across all temperatures.

## Method

The authors evaluate the π-π scattering amplitude using an effective Lagrangian with ρ and σ exchange, where the ρ propagator is modified by one-loop self-energy diagrams computed in the real-time formulation of thermal field theory. The self-energy includes contributions from ππ, πω, πh₁, and πa₁ loops, with the heavier meson loops folded over their spectral functions to account for their finite decay widths. The shear viscosity is then computed from the in-medium cross-section using three approaches: a non-relativistic estimate, the first Chapman-Enskog approximation with generalized Laguerre polynomial expansion, and the relaxation time approximation.

## Key numbers

- The ρππ coupling constant is fixed as g_ρ = 6.05 from the ρ → ππ decay width.
- The σ meson parameters used are m_σ = 450 MeV and Γ_σ = 550 MeV, with coupling g_σ = 2.5.
- In the Chapman-Enskog approximation at T = 150 MeV, the shear viscosity shows approximately 10% reduction due to medium effects when all loops (multi-pion case) are included, compared to the vacuum cross-section.
- The medium effect reduces to less than 5% at T = 100 MeV in the Chapman-Enskog approximation.
- The upper integration limit ψ_max ≈ 2 in the Chapman-Enskog integrals corresponds to a center-of-mass energy of approximately 1 GeV for ππ scattering.

## Context

This work extends prior estimates of pion gas shear viscosity that used vacuum cross-sections from chiral perturbation theory (Dobado 2001, Nakano 2007) or phenomenological fits to phase shift data (Itakura 2007), by incorporating the temperature-dependent ρ spectral function previously shown to be crucial for dilepton production in heavy ion collisions (Rapp 1997, Ghosh 2008, Sarkar 2002). The results enable more realistic temperature-dependent transport coefficients for use in viscous hydrodynamics calculations of electromagnetic probes from heavy ion collisions, though the authors note that extension to include other mesons and nucleons is needed before such application.
