# Dilepton production by dynamical quasiparticles in the strongly interacting quark gluon plasma

> The dilepton production by the constituents of the strongly interacting quark-gluon-plasma (sQGP) is addressed. In order to make quantitative predictions at realistically low plasma temperatures (O(T_c)), experimentally relevant low dilepton mass (O(1 GeV)) and strong coupling (alphaS=0.5-1), we take into account not only the higher order pQCD reaction mechanisms, but also the non-perturbative spectral functions (off-shellness) and self-energies of the quarks, anti-quarks and gluons thus going beyond the leading twist. For this purpose, our calculations utilize parametrizations of the non-perturbative propagators for quarks and gluons provided by the dynamical quasi-particle model (DQPM) matched to reproduce lattice data. The DQPM describes QCD properties in terms of single-particle Green's functions (in the sense of a two-particle irreducible approach) and leads to the notion of the constituents of the sQGP being effective quasiparticles, which are massive and have broad spectral functions (due to large interaction rates). In the present work, we derive the off-shell cross sections of dilepton production in the reactions q+qbar->l^+l^- (Drell-Yan mechanism), q+qbar->g+l^+l^- (quark annihilation with the gluon Bremsstrahlung in the final state), q(qbar)+g->q(qbar)+l^+l^- (gluon Compton scattering), g->q+qbar+l^+l^- and q(qbar)->q(qbar)+g+l^+l^- (virtual gluon decay, virtual quark decay) in the sQGP by dressing the quark and gluon lines in the perturbative diagrams with the DQPM propagators for quarks and gluons.

## Key claim

The paper derives off-shell cross sections for dilepton production in the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) by dressing perturbative diagrams with non-perturbative spectral functions from the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM), going beyond both leading order in alpha_S and the leading twist approximation; the quantitative predictions are limited to temperatures near T_c, dilepton masses around 1 GeV, and strong coupling alpha_S between 0.5 and 1.

## Method

The authors calculate off-shell cross sections for five partonic processes (q+qbar -> l+l-, q+qbar -> g+l+l-, q+g -> q+l+l-, g -> q+qbar+l+l-, and q -> q+g+l+l-) by replacing bare quark and gluon propagators with DQPM parametrizations that reproduce lattice QCD data, including finite masses and broad spectral functions. The cross sections are derived for arbitrary virtualities of external partons, then integrated over these virtualities weighted by the DQPM spectral functions, while internal lines are dressed with effective self-energies and the running coupling depends on temperature according to lattice data.

## Key numbers

* The Drell-Yan off-shell cross section includes a kinematic threshold Q_0^2 = (m_1 + m_2 + 2 m_lept)^2, where m_1 and m_2 are quark/antiquark virtualities, shown for values such as m_1 = 0.3 GeV and m_2 = 0.15 GeV, 0.3 GeV, or 0.6 GeV
* The gluon Bremsstrahlung cross section (q+qbar -> g+l+l-) is given by (d^2 sigma)/(dQ^2 d cos Theta) = (8 alpha^2 e_q^2 alpha_S)/(27 Q^2) * (s - Q^2)/(s^2 sin^2 Theta) * (1 + cos^2 Theta + 4 Q^2 s/(s - Q^2)^2)
* The gluon Compton scattering cross section (g+q -> q+l+l-) is given by (d^2 sigma)/(dQ^2 d cos Theta) = (alpha^2 e_q^2 alpha_S)/(18 Q^2) * (s - Q^2)/(s^2 (1+cos Theta)) * {2s/(s-Q^2) + (s-Q^2)/(2s)(1+cos Theta)^2 - (2Q^2/s)(1-cos Theta)}

## Context

This work extends earlier perturbative QCD calculations of dilepton production from the quark-gluon plasma (Shuryak 1978, Halzen 1978) and the hard-thermal-loop resummation approach (Braaten and Pisarski 1990, Wong 1992) by incorporating non-perturbative spectral functions from the DQPM (Cassing 2006) matched to lattice QCD. The results enable quantitative modeling of dilepton emission from the sQGP at experimentally accessible temperatures and masses, and are intended for implementation in microscopic transport models like HSD (Hadron-String-Dynamics) to compare with data from the NA60 and PHENIX collaborations.
