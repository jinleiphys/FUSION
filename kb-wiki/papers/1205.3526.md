# The finite temperature phase transition from domain wall fermions

> We present results on the finite temperature QCD transition with 2+1 flavors using Domain Wall Fermions (DWF) with the Dislocation Suppressing Determinant Ratio (DSDR). In particular, we discuss how the use of DSDR allows us to study the finite temperature transition at the coarse lattice spacings corresponding to the transition region (T = 139 - 195 MeV) with DWF at N_τ= 8. The residual chiral symmetry breaking at these lattice spacings is sufficiently small so that a constant pion mass of m_π\approx 200 MeV is obtained in our calculations. The strange quark mass is set to near its physical value. We show results on the restoration of chiral symmetry and deconfinement at finite temperature.

## Key claim

The finite temperature QCD transition with 2+1 flavors of domain wall fermions can be studied at coarse lattice spacings (T = 139–195 MeV) by using the Dislocation Suppressing Determinant Ratio (DSDR) to control residual chiral symmetry breaking, but the results show significant differences from staggered fermion calculations for temperatures at or below the crossover temperature, possibly due to finite volume effects.

## Method

The authors used domain wall fermions with the Iwasaki gauge action augmented by the DSDR weighting factor, which suppresses near-zero eigenmodes of the Hermitian Wilson-Dirac operator that contribute most to the residual mass. They generated ensembles at 7 temperatures with spatial size N_σ = 16 and temporal extent N_τ = 8, spanning T = 139–195 MeV, with the strange quark mass near its physical value and the light quark mass tuned to keep m_π ≈ 200 MeV along a line of constant physics. They computed the disconnected chiral susceptibility and scalar/pseudoscalar susceptibilities from connected two-point functions and stochastic estimators of the chiral condensate.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range studied: T = 139–195 MeV, corresponding to lattice spacings a ≈ 0.13–0.18 fm
- Pion mass fixed to m_π ≈ 200 MeV across all ensembles
- Residual mass m_res at T = 139 MeV (β = 1.633, L_s = 48): 0.00588(39) in lattice units
- Residual mass m_res at T = 195 MeV (β = 1.829, L_s = 32): 0.00047(1) in lattice units
- Disconnected chiral susceptibility χ_{l,disc}/T^2 at T = 149 MeV (β = 1.671, L_s = 32): 44(3)
- Disconnected chiral susceptibility χ_{l,disc}/T^2 at T = 195 MeV (β = 1.829, L_s = 32): 5(3)
- Peak in disconnected chiral susceptibility observed near T ≈ 160 MeV
- For T > 170 MeV, achieving comparable residual mass without DSDR would require L_s ≥ 96 instead of L_s = 32 with DSDR
- Zero temperature lattice scales determined using r_0 = 0.487(9) fm, giving a^{-1} = 1.27(4) GeV at β = 1.70 and a^{-1} = 1.55(5) GeV at β = 1.82

## Context

This work extends earlier domain wall fermion studies of the QCD transition (Cheng 2009) that did not maintain a line of constant physics, showing that those earlier results were significantly distorted in the low temperature region. The results are compared with improved staggered fermion calculations (Bazavov 2011), showing reasonable agreement for T > T_c but significantly larger chiral susceptibility for T ≲ T_c, possibly due to the smaller aspect ratio (N_σ/N_τ = 2 versus 4 in staggered calculations). Future work is planned to extend to larger spatial volumes and smaller quark masses to control finite-volume effects and examine quark mass dependence.
