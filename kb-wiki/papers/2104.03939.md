# Energy-independent optical $^{1}S_{0}NN$ potential from Marchenko equation

> We present a new algebraic method for solving the inverse problem of quantum scattering theory based on the Marchenko theory. We applied a triangular wave set for the Marchenko equation kernel expansion in a separable form. The separable form allows a reduction of the Marchenko equation to a system of linear equations. For the zero orbital angular momentum, a linear expression of the kernel expansion coefficients is obtained in terms of the Fourier series coefficients of a function depending on the momentum $q$ and determined by the scattering data in the finite range of $q$. It is shown that a Fourier series on a finite momentum range ($0<q<π/h$) of a $q(1-S)$ function ($S$ is the scattering matrix) defines the potential function of the corresponding radial Schrödinger equation with $h$-step accuracy. A numerical algorithm is developed for the reconstruction of the optical potential from scattering data. The developed procedure is applied to analyze the $^{1}S_{0}NN$ data up to 3 GeV. It is shown that these data are described by optical energy-independent partial potential.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the Marchenko equation can be applied to non-unitary S-matrices describing absorption, allowing reconstruction of a local and energy-independent optical potential from scattering data up to 3 GeV, contradicting prior claims that optical potentials with repulsive cores exhibit strong energy dependence.

## Method

The authors developed an algebraic method for solving the Marchenko inverse scattering problem by expanding the kernel function in a separable form using triangular and rectangular wave basis sets, reducing the integral equation to a system of linear equations. For zero orbital angular momentum, they derived a linear expression relating the kernel expansion coefficients to Fourier series coefficients of a function determined by scattering data in a finite momentum range. The method was applied to reconstruct the \(^{1}S_{0}\) nucleon-nucleon optical potential using phase shift analysis data up to 3 GeV, with asymptotic extrapolation for momenta above 3 fm\(^{-1}\).

## Key numbers

- The reconstructed potential has \(Re(V(0)) \approx 14\) GeV and \(Im(V(0)) \approx 19\) GeV for the \(^{1}S_{0}\) NN system.
- The step size used for the reconstruction was \(h = 0.0125\) fm, corresponding to a maximum momentum of \(q_{max} \approx 251.3\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- For the test potential \(V(r) = V_0 \exp(-ar)\) with \(V_0 = -3\) fm\(^{-2} = -124.5\) MeV and \(a = 1.5\) fm\(^{-1}\), the reconstruction used \(h = 0.04\) and range \(R = 4\).
- The asymptotic fits for phase shift and inelasticity parameter for \(q > 3\) fm\(^{-1}\) used coefficients: \(\delta(q) \sim -54.56822/q^3 + 57.55296/q^2 - 15.36687/q\) and \(\rho(q) \sim 101.89881/q^3 - 80.13493/q^2 + 15.88984/q\).

## Context

This work extends the Marchenko inverse scattering theory (Agranovich 1963, Marchenko 1977) to non-unitary S-matrices, enabling reconstruction of energy-independent optical potentials from absorbing scattering data. The results contradict the conclusions of Fernandez-Soler (2017), who claimed that optical potentials with repulsive cores exhibit strong energy dependence, whereas the authors reconstructed a local energy-independent soft-core NN optical potential. The approach enables future use of such potentials in nuclear reaction calculations, including (d,p) transfer reactions, where local energy-independent potentials have not been previously applied.
