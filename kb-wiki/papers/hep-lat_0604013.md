# The Gell-Mann -- Okubo mass relation among baryons from fully-dynamical mixed-action lattice QCD

> We explore the Gell-Mann--Okubo mass relation among the octet baryons using fully-dynamical, mixed-action (domain-wall on rooted-staggered) lattice QCD calculations at a lattice spacing of b ~ 0.125 fm and pion masses of m_pi ~ 290 MeV, 350 MeV, 490 MeV and 590 MeV. Deviations from the Gell-Mann--Okubo mass relation are found to be small at each quark mass.

## Key claim

The paper finds that deviations from the Gell-Mann–Okubo mass relation among octet baryons are small at all four simulated light-quark masses, and that the small violation observed experimentally is not an accident of the physical quark masses but a generic feature of the baryon spectrum. The authors hedge that the calculation is performed at a single lattice spacing (b ~ 0.125 fm) and that systematic uncertainties from finite lattice spacing are expected to be small but have not been fully quantified.

## Method

The authors performed fully-dynamical, mixed-action lattice QCD calculations using domain-wall valence quarks on rooted-staggered MILC configurations with N_f=2+1 asqtad-improved sea quarks. They computed baryon correlation functions for the nucleon, Sigma, Lambda, and Xi octet baryons at four light-quark masses (bm_l = 0.007, 0.010, 0.020, 0.030) with the strange-quark mass fixed near its physical value (bm_s = 0.050). The deviations from the Gell-Mann–Okubo relation were extracted from the ratio of correlation functions G^GMO(t) = C_Lambda(t) * C_Sigma(t)^(1/3) / [C_N(t)^(2/3) * C_Xi(t)^(2/3)], which directly yields the combination M_Lambda + M_Sigma/3 - 2M_N/3 - 2M_Xi/3.

## Key numbers

- At m_pi = 293.1 ± 1.5 MeV (bm_l = 0.007): T = (2.4 ± 1.8) × 10^(-3) in lattice units, fractional violation delta_GMO = (3.0 ± 2.3) × 10^(-3)
- At m_pi = 354.0 ± 0.9 MeV (bm_l = 0.010): T = (3.3 ± 1.0) × 10^(-3) in lattice units, delta_GMO = (4.1 ± 1.3) × 10^(-3)
- At m_pi = 492.6 ± 1.1 MeV (bm_l = 0.020): T = (0.34 ± 0.56) × 10^(-3) in lattice units, delta_GMO = (0.39 ± 0.64) × 10^(-3)
- At m_pi = 591.8 ± 0.9 MeV (bm_l = 0.030): T = (0.07 ± 0.42) × 10^(-3) in lattice units, delta_GMO = (0.07 ± 0.46) × 10^(-3)
- Experimental value at physical quark masses: delta_GMO^expt = 0.00761 ± 0.00007, corresponding to T^expt = 8.76 ± 0.08 MeV
- Lattice spacing: b = 0.125 fm

## Context

This work extends earlier quenched QCD studies of octet baryon masses (Bhattacharya 1995, Bowler 1999, Aoki 2002) and full-QCD studies (Allton 1998, Aoki 2002, Aubin 2004, Namekawa 2004) by providing the first full-QCD study of the complete baryon octet in the chiral regime. The results are consistent with the leading-order one-loop heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory prediction (Jenkins 1990, Jenkins 1991, Bijnens 1985, Bernard 1993) and with the additional suppression predicted in the large-N_C limit of QCD (Dashen 1994). The calculation enables future studies at different lattice spacings to quantify discretization effects and more precise tests of chiral perturbation theory for baryon masses.
