# Double charged heavy constituents of dark atoms and superheavy nuclear objects

> We consider the model of composite dark matter assuming stable particles of charge $-2$ bound with primordial helium nuclei by Coulomb force in $O$He atoms. We study capture of such dark atoms in matter and propose a possibility of existence of stable $O$-enriched superheavy nuclei and $O$-nuclearites, in which heavy $O$-dark matter fermions are bound by electromagnetic forces with ordinary nuclear matter. $O$He atoms accumulation in stars and its possible effect in stellar evolution is also considered, extending the set of indirect probes for composite dark matter.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that stable, double-charged O-particles (O−−) bound with helium nuclei in OHe atoms could, upon capture by ordinary atomic nuclei, form absolutely stable superheavy O-nuclei and O-nuclearites, provided the O-particle mass exceeds 2.3 times the nucleon mass; the authors hedge that the existence of a potential barrier in OHe-nucleus interactions remains an open question that could either enable or rule out the entire scenario.

## Method

The authors construct a theoretical model of O-nuclearites by assuming a heavy, isospin-symmetric atomic nucleus (equal numbers of neutrons and protons) at normal nuclear density n0 = 0.16 fm−3, into which O-particles are introduced. They minimize the total energy expression (including volume nuclear energy, electromagnetic energy from the proton-O charge distribution, and O-particle kinetic energy) to find the most favorable O-particle distribution, solving the Poisson equation for the electrostatic potential. They then extend the analysis to self-gravitating O-nuclearites using the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff framework and estimate capture rates of OHe onto neutron stars and red giants using a standard dark-matter capture formalism.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear density: n0 = 0.16 fm−3
- Volume energy of ordinary nuclear matter: −16 MeV per nucleon
- O-particle mass assumed for estimates: mO ≈ 1 TeV (≈ 103 times the nucleon mass)
- Condition for self-bound O-nuclearite: mO > 2.3 mN (where mN is the nucleon mass)
- For a self-gravitating O-nuclearite with central density n ∼ (2–2.5)n0: mass M ∼ 3 × 10−8 M⊙, radius R ∼ 30 m (for mO ≈ 103 mN)
- Maximum mass of O-nuclearite before becoming a black hole: Mmax ∼ 0.3 × 10−3 M⊙, Rmin ∼ 10−3 km, central density nmax ∼ 105 n0
- Maximum number of O-particles accumulated in a neutron star over 1010 yr: NOmax ∼ 1039, corresponding to O-nuclearite mass MO-nuclearitemax ∼ 1018 g
- For a red giant (R ∼ 109 km, M ∼ 0.5 M⊙, lifetime ∼ 108 yr): NOmax ∼ 1046, MO-nuclearitemax ∼ 1025 g
- OHe Bohr radius: aOHe ≈ 2 fm
- Geometrical cross section for OHe collisions: σ = 2 × 10−25 cm2
- For a galaxy with mass M = 1010 M⊙ and radius R = 1023 cm: nσR = 8 × 10−5 ≪ 1

## Context

This work extends the OHe composite dark matter model originally proposed by Khlopov (2005) and Glashow (2005), which posits stable double-charged particles (O−−) bound to primordial helium nuclei. The paper builds on earlier studies of superheavy nuclei and nuclearites by Migdal (1971, 1977), Lee and Wick (1974), Bodmer (1971), Witten (1984), and De Rujula and Glashow (1984), applying these concepts to the OHe dark matter framework. The proposed O-nuclearites differ from earlier treatments of negatively charged particle binding to nuclei (Cahn, Pospelov, Kohri) by allowing the O-particle density to follow the proton distribution, fully compensating the Coulomb field. The work enables future searches for superheavy nuclear objects bound by both strong and electromagnetic forces, and suggests that neutron stars and red giants could accumulate O-nuclearites, potentially leading to observable astrophysical signatures or black hole formation.
