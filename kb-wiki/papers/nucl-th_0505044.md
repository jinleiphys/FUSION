# System size dependence of elliptic flows in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The elliptic flows in both Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider are studied in a multi-phase transport model. For both collisions at same reduced impact parameter and minimum bias collisions, the elliptic flow of partons in Cu+Cu collisions is about a factor of three smaller than that in Au+Au collisions at same energy. The reduction factor is similar to the ratio of the sizes of the two colliding systems and is also related to the combined effects of initial energy density and spatial elliptic deformation in the two reactions. A similar system size dependence is also seen in the elliptic flow of charged hadrons from minimum bias collisions.

## Key claim

The elliptic flow of partons in Cu+Cu collisions is about a factor of three smaller than that in Au+Au collisions at the same energy and reduced impact parameter, with the reduction factor similar to the ratio of the sizes of the two colliding systems and also related to the combined effects of initial energy density and spatial elliptic deformation.

## Method

The authors used the multi-phase transport (AMPT) model with string melting, which converts hadrons from initial string fragmentation into valence quarks and antiquarks, to simulate Cu+Cu and Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 AGeV at RHIC. Parton interactions were described by Zhang's parton cascade (ZPC) model with elastic scatterings using cross sections of 3 mb and 10 mb, and hadronization was achieved via a simple coalescence model followed by hadronic scatterings modeled by a relativistic transport (ART) model.

## Key numbers

- Parton v2 in Cu+Cu collisions at b=5.5 fm: about 1.9% for sigma_p=3 mb and 2.9% for sigma_p=10 mb
- Parton v2 in Au+Au collisions at b=8 fm: about 5.4% for sigma_p=3 mb and 7.5% for sigma_p=10 mb
- Parton v2 enhancement factor from Cu+Cu to Au+Au at same reduced impact parameter: about 3 for both cross sections
- Initial energy density in Cu+Cu collisions is about 2/3 of that in Au+Au collisions
- Ratio of initial spatial elliptic deformation for the two systems: about 1/2
- Parton differential v2 in Cu+Cu is about 0.3 (0.35) times that in Au+Au for sigma_p=3 (10) mb
- Charged hadron v2 in Cu+Cu is about 0.35 (0.4) times that in Au+Au for sigma_p=3 (10) mb
- Transverse momentum spectrum of charged hadrons in Cu+Cu is about 0.32 times that in Au+Au for both cross sections
- Estimated ratio of initial energy densities from Bjorken formula: about 0.68

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of elliptic flow dependence on impact parameter to the dependence on the size of colliding nuclei, following the recent experimental efforts by the PHOBOS collaboration (Manly, 2005). The results show that the AMPT model with string melting, which had previously reproduced the centrality and transverse momentum dependence of elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=130 AGeV (Lin, 2001), predicts a system size scaling that can be understood from the combined effects of initial energy density and spatial elliptic deformation. The findings enable future comparisons with experimental data from Cu+Cu collisions at RHIC and further investigation of how scaling by initial eccentricity might relate to the observed system size dependence.
