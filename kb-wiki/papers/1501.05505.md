# Azimuthal harmonics of color fields in a high energy nucleus

> Recent experimental results have revealed a surprisingly rich structure of multiparticle azimuthal correlations in high energy proton-nucleus collisions. Final state collective effects can be responsible for many of the observed effects, but it has recently been argued that a part of these correlations are present already in the wavefunctions of the colliding particles. We evaluate the momentum space 2-particle cumulant azimuthal anisotropy coefficients v_n{2}, n=2,3,4 from fundamental representation Wilson line distributions describing the high energy nucleus. These would correspond to the flow coefficients in very forward proton nucleus scattering. We find significant differences beteen Wilson lines from the MV model and from JIMWLK evolution. The magnitude and transverse momentum dependence of the v_n{2} values suggest that the fluctuations present in the initial fields are a significant contribution to the observed anisotropies.

## Key claim

Color field fluctuations in the high energy nucleus generate azimuthal anisotropies that are large enough to be a significant contribution to the observed multiparticle correlations in proton-nucleus collisions, but the magnitude depends strongly on the transverse size of the probe and on whether the Wilson lines are taken from the MV model or from JIMWLK evolution.

## Method

The author evaluates the 2-particle cumulant azimuthal anisotropy coefficients v_n{2} for n=2,3,4 from fundamental representation Wilson line distributions on a 1024^2 transverse lattice, using both the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model and JIMWLK-evolved configurations. The calculation uses the hybrid formalism where the quark spectrum is proportional to the Fourier transform of the two-point function of Wilson lines, with a Gaussian transverse coordinate profile for the probe of varying width sqrt(B). The anisotropy coefficients are computed using the experimental 2-particle cumulant method, correlating particles in a given p_T bin with a reference reaction plane determined by all produced particles.

## Key numbers

- MV model Wilson lines use a saturation scale Q_s a = 0.119 (lattice spacing a); JIMWLK evolution starts from Q_s a = 0.0220 and after y=10 units of rapidity evolution with running coupling ends at Q_s a = 0.117
- Realistic probe size for LHC pA collisions is roughly sqrt(B) Q_s approx 2, corresponding to B approx 4 GeV^{-2} and Q_s approx 1 GeV
- The v_n{2} coefficients are plotted against p_T/Q_s for different probe sizes sqrt(B) Q_s; the anisotropy first rises until approximately p_T/Q_s ~ 1 and then decreases
- MV model yields significantly larger v_n values than JIMWLK-evolved configurations

## Context

This work extends the coordinate-space calculations of Dumitru (2014) and Skokov (2014) by performing the Fourier transform to momentum space and using the 2-particle cumulant method rather than correlating particles with a bin-specific event plane. The results contrast with full classical Yang-Mills simulations by Schenke, Schlichting and Venugopalan (2014, 2015) that include both color field and nucleonic fluctuations as well as geometrical effects; the present work focuses specifically on quantifying the generic anisotropy from fluctuating color fields in the absence of impact parameter anisotropies. The findings suggest that initial-state color field fluctuations must be taken into account together with hydrodynamical flow contributions when interpreting experimental results from proton-nucleus collisions.
