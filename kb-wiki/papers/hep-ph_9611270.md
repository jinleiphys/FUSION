# Rare processes and coherent phenomena in crystals

> We study coherent enhancement of Coulomb excitation of high energy particles in crystals. We develop multiple scattering theory description of coherent excitation which consistently incorporates both the specific resonant properties of particle-crystal interactions and the final/initial state interaction effects typical of the diffractive scattering. Possible applications to observation of induced radiative neutrino transitions are discussed.

## Key claim

Coherent enhancement of Coulomb excitation in crystals, such as the Okorokov effect for Pomeranchuck-Shmushkevich processes like p-gamma-to-Sigma+, is dramatically suppressed by initial and final state interactions, yielding only a logarithmic growth of the transition amplitude with crystal thickness rather than the linear N-dependence predicted by the plane wave Born approximation; however, for neutrino magnetic conversion the initial and final state interactions are negligible, preserving the resonant enhancement.

## Method

The author develops a multiple scattering theory description of coherent particle-crystal interactions using Gribov's dispersion integral representation for the coupled-channel scattering amplitude, treating the projectile as propagating along straight-line trajectories at fixed impact parameter. The S-matrix is expanded into a multiple scattering series that incorporates both diagonal elastic scattering (initial and final state interactions) and off-diagonal transition amplitudes, with the crystal modeled as a chain of N identical atoms with uncorrelated thermal vibrations described by a Gaussian wave function. The transition amplitude is evaluated at fixed impact parameter and then integrated over transverse momentum to obtain the differential cross section.

## Key numbers

* For the p-gamma-to-Sigma+ transition, the transition amplitude grows only as log(Z alpha N) rather than linearly with N, where Z is the atomic number and alpha = 1/137.
* The resonance energy for coherent excitation is E_n = (Delta m^2_ba * d) / (4 pi n), where d is the lattice spacing (approximately 5 Angstrom) and n is the resonance order.
* The maximum resonance order is n_max approximately 5-10, limited by the Debye-Waller factor from thermal vibrations with mean squared amplitude <u^2>.
* For neutrino conversion, the resonance width is Gamma_n = (sqrt(3)/2) * (Delta m^2_21 * d) / (pi^2 n^2 N).
* A CERN 30 GeV neutrino beam with a crystal target could probe neutrino mass differences up to approximately (1 MeV)^2.
* The experiment could detect transition neutrino magnetic moments larger than approximately 10^-6 to 10^-8 Bohr magnetons, assuming an efficiency of about 10^-4.
* Typical momentum transfers in neutrino-crystal coherent interactions are in the range of approximately (10 keV)^2, compared to (100 MeV)^2 for existing bounds.

## Context

This work extends the Okorokov effect, originally proposed by Okorokov (1970s) for resonant excitation of atoms and nuclei in crystals, to weak radiative transitions of hyperons and neutrinos. It contradicts the earlier claim by Dubovik (1995) that the transition probability for p-gamma-to-Sigma+ would scale as N^2 up to N = 10^7, showing instead that the plane wave Born approximation used in prior work (Ter-Mikaelian, Fermi, Pomeranchuck and Shmushkevich) grossly overestimates the enhancement due to neglect of initial and final state interactions. The results enable future laboratory investigations of neutrino electrodynamics, particularly searches for neutrino magnetic moments and mass differences using crystal targets, with the possibility of probing a nontrivial q^2 dependence of the neutrino magnetic form factor as discussed by Frere (1990s).
