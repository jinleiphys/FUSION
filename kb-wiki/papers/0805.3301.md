# Neutron stars within the SU(2) parity doublet model

> The equation of state of beta-stable and charge neutral nucleonic matter is computed within the SU(2) parity doublet model in mean field and in the relativistic Hartree approximation. The mass of the chiral partner of the nucleon is assumed to be 1200 MeV. The transition to the chiral restored phase turns out to be a smooth crossover in all the cases considered, taking place at a baryon density of just $2ρ_0$. The mass-radius relations of compact stars are calculated to constrain the model parameters from the maximum mass limit of neutron stars. It is demonstrated that chiral symmetry starts to be restored, which in this model implies the appearance of the chiral partners of the nucleons, in the center of neutron stars. However, the analysis of the decay width of the assumed chiral partner of the nucleon poses limits on the validity of the present version of the model to describe vacuum properties.

## Key claim

The SU(2) parity doublet model can describe both chiral symmetry restoration and nuclear matter saturation properties simultaneously, but only if the chiral partner of the nucleon has a mass of 1200 MeV rather than the 1535 MeV of the known N'(1535) resonance; the analysis of the decay width of the assumed chiral partner poses limits on the validity of this version of the model to describe vacuum properties.

## Method

The equation of state of beta-stable, charge-neutral nucleonic matter was computed within the SU(2) parity doublet model using both mean-field theory (MFT) and the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA), which includes Dirac sea effects. The model parameters were fixed by requiring the binding energy per nucleon (-16 MeV), saturation density (0.16 fm^-3), and symmetry energy (32.5 MeV) at saturation, with the bare mass parameter m0 and the mass of the negative-parity nucleon M_N- treated as free inputs. The resulting equations of state were then used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations to obtain mass-radius relations for neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- Mass of the chiral partner of the nucleon assumed to be 1200 MeV throughout the study
- Chiral symmetry restoration begins at a baryon density of approximately 2 ρ0 (where ρ0 = 0.16 fm^-3)
- For the P1 parameter set in MFT (g4=0): maximum neutron star mass M_max = 1.85 M⊙, critical density ρ_crit = 0.32 fm^-3, compressibility K = 436.09 MeV
- For the P2 parameter set in MFT (g4=3.76 MeV): maximum neutron star mass M_max = 1.39 M⊙, critical density ρ_crit = 0.31 fm^-3, compressibility K = 374.62 MeV
- The P2 parametrization is ruled out because its maximum mass (1.39 M⊙) falls below the Hulse-Taylor pulsar mass of 1.44 M⊙
- In RHA with m0=850 MeV and g4=0: maximum neutron star mass M_max = 1.44 M⊙, compressibility K = 257.40 MeV, critical density ρ_crit = 0.43 fm^-3
- In RHA with m0=790 MeV and g4=0: maximum neutron star mass M_max = 1.78 M⊙, compressibility K = 360.27 MeV
- Stars with mass larger than 1.2 M⊙ have a core of partially chiral-restored phase for both P1 and P2 parametrizations

## Context

This work extends the SU(2) parity doublet model, previously applied to symmetric nuclear matter by Zschiesche (2006), to beta-stable matter for neutron star applications, following initial work by Dexheimer (2007). The model addresses the long-standing difficulty that the standard sigma model (Lee, 1974) does not describe nuclear matter saturation while Walecka-type models (Serot, 1997) do not incorporate QCD chiral symmetry. The results enable future studies of transport properties (viscosities, neutrino opacities) in neutron star matter where chiral partners appear, but the identification of the N'(1535) as the chiral partner remains problematic due to decay width constraints, suggesting the true partner may be a lower-mass resonance not yet experimentally detected.
