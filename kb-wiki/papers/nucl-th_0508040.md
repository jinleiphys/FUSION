# Behavior of shell-model configuration moments

> An important input into reaction theory is the density of states or the level density. Spectral distribution theory (also known as nuclear statistical spectroscopy) characterizes the secular behavior of the density of states through moments of the Hamiltonian. One particular approach is to partition the model space into subspaces and find the moments in those subspaces; a convenient choice of subspaces are spherical shell-model configurations. We revisit these configuration moments and find, for complete $0\hbarω$ many-body spaces, the following behaviors: (a) the configuration width is nearly constant for all configurations; (b) the configuration asymmetry or third moment is strongly correlated with the configuration centroid; (c) the configuration fourth moment, or excess is linearly related to the square to the configuration asymmetry. Such universal behavior may allow for more efficient modeling of the density of states in a shell-model framework.

## Key claim

For complete $0\hbar\omega$ many-body spaces, the configuration width is nearly constant for all configurations, the configuration asymmetry is strongly correlated with the configuration centroid, and the configuration excess is linearly related to the square of the asymmetry; these universal behaviors may allow more efficient modeling of the density of states in a shell-model framework, but the results are limited to single-shell spaces and may not generalize to multi-shell configurations.

## Method

The authors used the REDSTICK shell-model code to construct many-body matrix elements and directly compute configuration moments (centroids, widths, asymmetries, and excesses) for complete $0\hbar\omega$ spaces in the $sd$ and $pf$ shells. They employed realistic interactions including USD, GXPF1, FPD6G, and KB3G, as well as quadrupole-quadrupole and random two-body interactions, and also created "traceless" interactions by subtracting the monopole potential to isolate the effects of the residual interaction on the moments.

## Key numbers

- For $^{44}$Ti in the $pf$-shell with GXPF1 and FPD6G interactions, the configuration widths are nearly constant across all 100 $J$-summed $pn$-configurations, with the FPD6G widths showing a very narrow spread and GXPF1 widths showing a slightly greater range.
- Average configuration widths in the $sd$-shell with USD interaction range from 3.40 MeV ($^{34}$Si) to 9.24 MeV ($^{28}$Si), with standard deviations between 0.62 and 1.17 MeV.
- Configuration asymmetries range from approximately -2 to 2 for $J$-summed moments in $sd$- and $pf$-shell nuclei, with only rare cases of $|m^{(3)}_\alpha|>2$.
- For $^{44}$Ti in the $pf$-shell with GXPF1, the linear fit of $m_4$ vs. $m_3^2$ gives intercept 1.35 and slope 1.37; for $^{20}$Ne in the $sd$-shell with USD, intercept 0.90 and slope 1.16.
- For $^{44}$Ti, computing all second moments takes about 43 minutes on a single 2.4 GHz Pentium, but third moments take 9 days.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Kota, Potbhare, and Shenoy (1986) on configuration moments and model functions for level densities, and addresses the approach of Horoi, Kaiser, and Zelevinsky (2003) who modeled level densities as sums of Gaussians ignoring asymmetries. The findings enable more efficient modeling of nuclear level densities by potentially allowing one to compute only lower-order moments and estimate higher moments from the observed correlations, which is important for Hauser-Feshbach calculations of neutron capture reactions.
