# States of $ρB^* \bar{B}^*$ with $J=3$ within the Fixed Center Approximation to Faddeev equations

> In this work we study the $ρB^*\bar{B}^*$ three-body system solving the Faddeev equations in the fixed center approximation. We assume the $B^*\bar{B}^*$ system forming a cluster, and in terms of the two-body $ρB^*$ unitarized scattering amplitudes in the local Hidden Gauge approach we find a new $I(J^{PC})=1(3^{--})$ state. The mass of the new state corresponds to a two particle invariant mass of the $ρB^\ast$ system close to the resonant energy of the $B^\ast_2(5747)$, indicating that the role of this $J=2$ resonance is important in the dynamical generation of the new state.

## Key claim

The paper claims the existence of a new three-body state in the $\rho B^*\bar{B}^*$ system with quantum numbers $I(J^{PC})=1(3^{--})$, but this result depends on the Fixed Center Approximation to the Faddeev equations and assumes the $B^*\bar{B}^*$ pair forms a bound cluster of spin 2 and isospin 0, with the $\rho B^*$ interaction taken from a unitarized Hidden Gauge model.

## Method

The authors solve the Faddeev equations in the Fixed Center Approximation (FCA), treating the $B^*\bar{B}^*$ system as a bound cluster of mass 10616 MeV (spin 2, isospin 0) and the $\rho$ meson as the third particle scattering off this cluster. The two-body $\rho B^*$ scattering amplitudes in isospin $I=1/2$ and $I=3/2$ with spin $J=2$ are taken from a previous unitarized chiral approach using the local Hidden Gauge formalism, regularized with a three-momentum cutoff. The total three-body $T$-matrix is obtained by summing the multiple scattering series of the $\rho$ meson with the two constituents of the cluster, using a form factor derived from the cluster wave function.

## Key numbers

* The $B^*\bar{B}^*$ cluster mass is taken as $M_{\rm c}=10616$ MeV for the $I(J^{PC})=0(2^{++})$ state, obtained without coupling to the $B_s^*\bar{B}_s^*$ channel (including that channel gives 10613 MeV).
* The new $I(J^{PC})=1(3^{--})$ state has a mass of $10987\pm 40$ MeV and width $40\pm 15$ MeV, quoted as the central result from the analysis.
* For $\Lambda=800$ MeV, the state appears at mass 11005 MeV with width 38 MeV; for $\Lambda=1000$ MeV, mass 10992 MeV and width 44 MeV; for $\Lambda=1200$ MeV, mass 10978 MeV and width 48 MeV.
* The three-body threshold is at $M_{\rm c}+m_\rho=11391$ MeV, giving a binding energy of approximately 385 MeV for $\Lambda=800$ MeV.
* The $\rho B^*$ two-body energy in the three-body system is close to 5706-5727 MeV depending on the energy-sharing scheme, near the $B^*_2(5747)$ resonance mass.

## Context

This work extends the study of three-meson systems using the Fixed Center Approximation to Faddeev equations, following previous applications to the $\rho D^*\bar{D}^*$ system by Bayar et al. (2015) and to multi-$\rho$ states by Roca et al. (2010). The two-body $\rho B^*$ interaction was previously studied by Soler et al. (2015), where the $B^*_2(5747)$ resonance was found. The $B^*\bar{B}^*$ cluster used here was predicted by Ozpineci et al. (2013) using the local Hidden Gauge approach and Heavy Quark Spin Symmetry. The new $J=3$ state is dynamically generated by the combination of the attractive $B^*\bar{B}^*$ interaction in spin 2 and the attractive $\rho B^*$ interaction that produces the $B^*_2(5747)$ resonance. The result enables future experimental searches for this predicted bottom-strange three-body state.
