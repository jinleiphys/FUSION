# On the uncertainty estimates of the $σ$-pole determination by Padé approximants

> We discuss the determination of the $f_0(500)$ (or $σ$) resonance by analytic continuation through Padé approximants of the $ππ$-scattering amplitude from the physical region to the pole in the complex energy plane. The aim is to analyze the uncertainties of the method, having in view the fact that analytic continuation is an ill-posed problem in the sense of Hadamard. Using as input a class of admissible parameterizations of the scalar-isoscalar $ππ$ partial wave, which satisfy with great accuracy the same set of dispersive constraints, we find that the Roy-type integral representations lead to almost identical pole positions for all of them, while the predictions of the Padé approximants have a larger spread, being sensitive to features of the input parameterization that are not controlled by the dispersive constraints. Our conservative conclusion is that the $σ$-pole determination by Padé approximants is consistent with the prediction of Roy-type equations, but has an uncertainty almost a factor two larger.

## Key claim

The σ-pole determination by Padé approximants is consistent with the prediction of Roy-type equations, but has an uncertainty almost a factor two larger, because analytic continuation is an ill-posed problem and different parameterizations that satisfy the same dispersive constraints lead to a larger spread in the pole positions than the Roy-type integral representations.

## Method

The authors constructed five different parameterizations (v1–v5) of the scalar-isoscalar ππ partial wave, each satisfying the same set of dispersive constraints (Forward Dispersion Relations, Roy and GKPY equations, and sum rules) with equal accuracy. They then extracted the f0(500) pole position from each parameterization using two methods: analytic continuation via GKPY integral equations, and analytic continuation via Padé approximants (specifically the P³₁ and P²₂ sequences). The spread of results across the five parameterizations was used to assess the systematic uncertainty of the Padé method.

## Key numbers

- GKPY equations gave pole positions for all five parameterizations within 1% of each other, e.g., v1: (457 ± 14) – i(279 ± 11) MeV; v5: (456 ± 14) – i(280 ± 11) MeV.
- Padé approximant P³₁ for v1 gave M = 452.8 MeV, Γ/2 = 296.8 MeV, with combined error 17.3 MeV and 17.6 MeV respectively.
- Padé approximant P³₁ for v3 gave M = 456.6 MeV, Γ/2 = 303.2 MeV, with combined error 9.5 MeV and 11.3 MeV respectively.
- Padé approximant P³₁ for v4 gave M = 471.5 MeV, Γ/2 = 278.7 MeV, with combined error 14.1 MeV and 16.0 MeV respectively.
- The optimal expansion point s0 for the Padé approximants ranged from √s0 = 470 MeV to 510 MeV for P³₁ and from 390 MeV to 510 MeV for P²₂.

## Context

This work extends the Padé-based σ-pole determination of Masjuan et al. (2013) and the CFD analysis of Garcia-Martin et al. (2011) by testing the method's stability across a class of admissible parameterizations, rather than relying on a single one. It confirms the earlier finding of Caprini (2008) that direct analytic continuation cannot compete with Roy-type equations for precision, but provides a more realistic uncertainty estimate for the Padé method. The results enable future applications of Padé approximants to resonance extraction in systems where Roy or GKPY equations are not available, such as πK scattering.
