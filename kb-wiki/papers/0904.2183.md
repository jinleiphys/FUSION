# Depletion of the nuclear Fermi sea

> The short-range and tensor components of the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction induce a sizeable depletion of low momenta in the ground state of a nuclear many-body system. The self-consistent Green's function method within the ladder approximation provides an \textit{ab-initio} description of correlated nuclear systems that accounts properly for these effects. The momentum distribution predicted by this approach is analyzed in detail, with emphasis on the depletion of the lowest momentum state. The temperature, density, and nucleon asymmetry (isospin) dependence of the depletion of the Fermi sea is clarified. A connection is established between the momentum distribution and the time-ordered components of the self-energy, which allows for an improved interpretation of the results. The dependence on the underlying nucleon-nucleon interaction provides quantitative estimates of the importance of short-range and tensor correlations in nuclear systems.

## Key claim

The depletion of the nuclear Fermi sea arises from both short-range and tensor components of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, but the paper clarifies that thermal effects can mimic or add to this depletion, particularly at low densities or high temperatures, and that the momentum distribution's flat low-momentum region is only truly interaction-dominated in the degenerate regime where thermal effects are negligible.

## Method

The authors employed the self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) method within the ladder approximation at finite temperature, solving the Dyson equation and the in-medium Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the T-matrix iteratively until convergence. Calculations were performed for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter using realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions (CDBonn and Argonne v18) at various densities, temperatures, and isospin asymmetries, with partial waves up to J=8 in the Hartree-Fock contribution and J=4 in the dispersive self-energy.

## Key numbers

- At ρ=0.16 fm⁻³ and T=5 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter with Av18, the depletion of the zero-momentum state due to dynamical NN correlations is approximately 10% (states below k_F contribute 75% to the density sum-rule, compared to 86% for the Free Fermi Gas)
- At ρ=0.16 fm⁻³ and T=10 MeV for symmetric nuclear matter with Av18, the total depletion of the zero-momentum state is 15%, composed of approximately 13% from dynamical NN correlations and 3% from thermal effects
- At ρ=0.16 fm⁻³ and T=5 MeV, the high-momentum tail (above k_F) provides 52% of the total kinetic energy per particle in the correlated case, compared to 25% in the Free Fermi Gas
- The onset density for the thermally dominated depletion region is ρ ≲ 0.16 fm⁻³ for symmetric nuclear matter and ρ ≲ 0.08 fm⁻³ for pure neutron matter

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of nuclear matter momentum distributions reviewed by Vond (1991), which found a depletion of the nuclear Fermi sea of a little over 15% at normal density, with about one-third from tensor correlations. The SCGF results are compared to older calculations using the Paris interaction (Baldo 1990) and to experimental evidence from Jefferson Lab (Subedi 2008) showing a pn-to-pp knockout ratio of about 20 in ¹²C. The paper clarifies the density dependence of depletion for symmetric versus pure neutron matter, resolving an apparent contradiction with earlier work, and provides a framework for interpreting future rare-isotope experiments and neutron star physics.
