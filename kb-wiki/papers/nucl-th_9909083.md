# Integral equations for three-body Coulombic resonances

> We propose a novel method for calculating resonances in three-body Coulombic systems. The method is based on the solution of the set of Faddeev and Lippmann-Schwinger integral equations, which are designed for solving the three-body Coulomb problem. The resonances of the three-body system are defined as the complex-energy solutions of the homogeneous Faddeev integral equations. We show how the kernels of the integral equations should be continued analytically in order that we get resonances. As a numerical illustration a toy model for the three-$α$ system is solved.

# Integral equations for three-body Coulombic resonances

## Key claim
The paper proposes a novel method for calculating three-body Coulombic resonances by solving homogeneous Faddeev-Merkuriev integral equations at complex energies, using analytic continuation of the contour integral representation of the Green's operator. The method is illustrated on a toy model of the three-alpha system, but the authors note that for the width of the resonant state they obtained an order of magnitude less than a prior two-channel model by Fedorov, suggesting the prior work may have improperly implemented asymptotic boundary conditions.

## Method
The authors solve the homogeneous Faddeev-Merkuriev integral equations in a Coulomb-Sturmian basis representation, using a separable expansion approximation for the short-range potentials. The three-body Green's operator is expressed as a contour integral of two-body Green's operators, and the contour is analytically deformed onto the unphysical Riemann sheet to access complex energies corresponding to resonances. The matrix elements of the two-body Green's operators in the Coulomb-Sturmian basis are known analytically for all complex energies, allowing the convolution integral to be performed numerically.

- The three-body potential has V = -31.935 MeV and beta = 3.315 fm.
- The alpha-particle mass is M = 3.973 m where m is the nucleon mass, with hbar^2/m = 41.47 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the Coulomb-Sturmian separable expansion approach previously developed by Papp et al. for bound-state problems and p-d scattering below breakup threshold, now applied to resonant states. The method is grounded in the Faddeev-Merkuriev theory for three-body Coulomb systems, and the authors compare their results to a two-channel model by Fedorov for the three-alpha system, finding agreement on the resonance position but disagreement on the width by an order of magnitude. The approach enables calculation of three-body Coulombic resonances without imposing asymptotic boundary conditions, and is readily applicable to non-Coulomb cases by replacing Coulomb Green's operators with free ones.
