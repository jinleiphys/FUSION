# Implication of neutron star observations to the origin of nucleon mass

> We investigate the implications of neutron star observations for understanding the origin of nucleon mass using a framework that combines three complementary approaches: the equation of state based on parity doublet structure for hadronic matter below $2n_0$, the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for quark matter above $5n_0$, and a model-independent analysis of the intermediate density region based on fundamental physical principles. By systematically exploring parameter spaces and comparing theoretical predictions with recent observational constraints, we establish constraints on the chiral invariant mass. Our results suggest that more than a half of the nucleon mass originates from sources beyond spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, challenging conventional understanding of nucleon mass generation. These constraints arise solely from fundamental physical principles and observational data, independent of specific assumptions about the nature of the quark-hadron transition, providing robust insights into the microscopic origin of hadron masses.

# Implication of neutron star observations to the origin of nucleon mass

## Key claim
The authors claim that more than half (approximately 60-95%) of the nucleon mass originates from sources beyond spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, based on constraints derived from neutron star observations. This conclusion is conditional on the specific density boundaries chosen (hadronic EOS up to 2n₀ and quark matter EOS from 5n₀); when the hadronic range is reduced to 1.5n₀, the constraint on the chiral invariant mass m₀ relaxes to larger than 700 MeV rather than 800 MeV.

## Method
The authors construct a neutron star equation of state (EOS) using three complementary approaches: a parity doublet model for hadronic matter below 2n₀, an Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type quark model for matter above 5n₀, and a model-independent interpolation in the intermediate density region (2n₀ to 5n₀) based solely on causality and thermodynamic stability. They systematically vary the chiral invariant mass m₀ (from 500 to 900 MeV) and the NJL coupling parameters (diquark coupling H and vector coupling g_V), then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations, which are compared against multiple observational constraints from NICER, LIGO-Virgo, and other pulsar measurements.

## Key numbers
- The chiral invariant mass m₀ must be larger than 600 MeV to satisfy the basic observational set (NICER measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0437-4715, and GW170817 tidal deformability).
- To accommodate both the basic set and the HESS J1731-347 compact object (mass 0.77^{+0.2}_{-0.17} M⊙, radius 10.04^{+0.86}_{-0.78} km), m₀ must be larger than 700 MeV.
- To satisfy all observations simultaneously including the "black widow" pulsar PSR J0952-0607 (mass 2.35^{+0.17}_{-0.17} M⊙), m₀ must be larger than 800 MeV.
- For m₀ = 900 MeV, the combination (H, g_V)/G = (1.4, 1.3) can satisfy all observational constraints simultaneously.
- The normal nuclear density n₀ is approximately 0.16 fm⁻³; the hadronic EOS is used up to 2n₀ and quark matter EOS from 5n₀.
- The slope parameter L₀ of the symmetry energy is fixed to 57.7 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the parity doublet approach to nucleon mass generation (Detar 1988, Jido 2001) by connecting it to neutron star observations, building on earlier studies of the chiral invariant mass from lattice QCD (Aarts 2015, 2017, 2018) and QCD sum rules (Kim 2020, 2021, Lee 2023). The model-independent interpolation method follows the framework of Komoltsev (2021) and Kurkela (2024). The results challenge the conventional view that nucleon mass arises primarily from spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking (Gasser 1983, Hatsuda 1994) and point toward a more complex origin involving explicit gluon contributions. The constraints enable future work to distinguish between different mechanisms of hadron mass generation and can be refined as additional neutron star observations become available.
