# Coulomb Corrections to Three-Nucleon Moments

> The Helium-3 (${}^3\mathrm{He}$) magnetic moment and Gamow-Teller (GT) matrix element in triton (${}^3\mathrm{H}$) $β$-decay are calculated in pionless effective field theory ($\mathrm{EFT}(/\!\!\!π)$) to next-to-leading order (NLO). Coulomb corrections are included perturbatively to $\mathcal{O}(α)$ in this framework and should naively be $αM_n/p^*\!\!\sim\!8\%$ corrections, where $p^*\!\!\sim\!88.5$ MeV is related to the three-nucleon binding momentum. Fitting the two-nucleon iso-vector magnetic current low-energy constant (LEC), $L_1$, to the ${}^3\mathrm{H}$ magnetic moment and the two-nucleon iso-scalar magnetic current LEC, $L_2$, to the deuteron magnetic moment we find the NLO ${}^3\mathrm{He}$ magnetic moment in units of nuclear magnetons is -2.130 and the surprisingly small $\mathcal{O}(α)$ correction is 0.00335, $\approx\!0.18\%$ of the LO $\mathrm{EFT}(/\!\!\!π)$ prediction. The leading-order (LO) GT matrix element for ${}^3\mathrm{H}$ $β$-decay is 0.9806 while again it has a surprisingly small $\mathcal{O}(α)$ Coulomb correction of $-0.000740$, $\approx\!0.08\%$ of the LO $\mathrm{EFT}(/\!\!\!π)$ prediction. At NLO we calculate the GT matrix element of ${}^3\mathrm{H}$ $β$-decay, including the $\mathcal{O}(α)$ Coulomb correction, in terms of the two-nucleon axial current LEC $l_{1,A}$. Fitting $l_{1,A}$ to the ${}^3\mathrm{H}$ half-life we make a prediction for the proton-proton fusion reduced matrix element of $Λ(0)=2.776(331)$. Finally, we attempt to explain the unusually small size of the $\mathcal{O}(α)$ corrections by investigating the Wigner-SU(4) expansion of these observables.

## Key claim

Coulomb corrections to the Helium-3 magnetic moment and triton beta-decay Gamow-Teller matrix element are surprisingly small (approximately 0.18% and 0.08% of the leading-order predictions, respectively), far below the naive expectation of approximately 8% from dimensional analysis, and this suppression is explained by Wigner-SU(4) symmetry.

## Method

The authors calculate the Helium-3 magnetic moment and triton Gamow-Teller matrix element in pionless effective field theory (EFT(/π)) to next-to-leading order, including Coulomb corrections perturbatively to order α. They solve integral equations for the three-nucleon vertex function in a 3×3 cluster configuration space that distinguishes proton-proton, neutron-neutron, and neutron-proton spin-singlet dibaryon states, fitting the two-nucleon magnetic current low-energy constants L1 and L2 to the triton and deuteron magnetic moments respectively, and the axial current low-energy constant l1,A to the triton half-life.

## Key numbers

- NLO Helium-3 magnetic moment: -2.130 nuclear magnetons
- O(α) Coulomb correction to Helium-3 magnetic moment: 0.00335 nuclear magnetons, approximately 0.18% of the leading-order EFT(/π) prediction
- Leading-order Gamow-Teller matrix element for triton beta-decay: 0.9806
- O(α) Coulomb correction to Gamow-Teller matrix element: -0.000740, approximately 0.08% of the leading-order EFT(/π) prediction
- Prediction for proton-proton fusion reduced matrix element: Λ(0) = 2.776(331)
- Naive Coulomb correction estimate: αM_n/p* ~ 8%, where p* ~ 88.5 MeV is the three-nucleon binding momentum

## Context

This work extends previous pionless EFT calculations of three-nucleon electromagnetic and weak currents (Vanasse 2015, 2017) by including perturbative Coulomb corrections following the approach of Konig (2015) for the proton-deuteron system. The authors expand about the physical virtual bound state pole in the 1S0 channel, unlike Konig (2015) who expanded about the unitary limit. The results enable improved predictions for proton-proton fusion and demonstrate that Wigner-SU(4) symmetry, recently shown by LiMuli (2025) and Dang (2026) to dominate nuclear wavefunctions up to A=8, provides an additional suppression mechanism for Coulomb corrections beyond naive dimensional analysis.
