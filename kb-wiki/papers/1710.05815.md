# Exact evolution equation for the effective potential

> We derive a new exact evolution equation for the scale dependence of an effective action. The corresponding equation for the effective potential permits a useful truncation. This allows one to deal with the infrared problems of theories with massless modes in less than four dimensions which are relevant for the high temperature phase transition in particle physics or the computation of critical exponents in statistical mechanics.

## Key claim

The paper derives an exact evolution equation for the scale dependence of an effective action, where the one-loop computation becomes exact but requires the use of the full propagator; the previously derived renormalization group improved one-loop equation follows from this exact equation in a suitable truncation, with neglected terms expected to vanish for vanishing anomalous dimension.

## Method

The author introduces an infrared cutoff term \(\Delta S\) into the classical action, defines a \(k\)-dependent generating functional \(W_k[J]\) via the path integral, and performs a Legendre transformation to obtain \(\tilde{\Gamma}_k\). After absorbing a quadratic term into a redefinition \(\Gamma_k = \tilde{\Gamma}_k - \frac{\Omega}{2} \sum_q R_k(q) \varphi_{ka}^\ast(q) \varphi_k^a(q)\), the exact evolution equation \(\frac{\partial}{\partial t} \Gamma_k[\varphi] = \frac{1}{2} \Tr[(\Gamma_k^{(2)}[\varphi] + R_k)^{-1} \partial_t R_k]\) is derived. For an \(SO(N)\) symmetric theory with an ansatz for \(\Gamma_k\) that includes the most general terms for quadratic fluctuations around a constant field, the evolution equation for the effective potential \(U_k\) remains exact and is given by Eq. (14) in the paper.

## Key numbers

* The inverse average propagator is \(P(q) = q^2 / (1 - f_k^2(q))\) with \(f_k(q) = \exp[-a (q^2/k^2)^\beta]\); the natural choice is \(\beta = 1\), \(a = \frac{1}{2}\) such that \(P \approx k^2 + \frac{1}{2} q^2\) for \(q^2 \ll k^2\).
* The constant \(v_d\) in the flow equation for \(U^\prime\) is defined as \(v_d^{-1} = 2^{d+1} \pi^{d/2} \Gamma(d/2)\).
* The evolution equation for \(U_k\) (Eq. 14) involves an integral \(\int \frac{d^d q}{(2\pi)^d}\) over momentum, with denominators \(M_0 = Z(\rho,q^2) q^2 + R(q) + U^\prime(\rho)\) and \(M_1 = [Z(\rho,q^2) + \rho Y(\rho,q^2)] q^2 + R(q) + U^\prime(\rho) + 2\rho U^{\prime\prime}(\rho)\).

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier formulation of the average action (Wetterich, 1991) and the renormalization group improved one-loop equation (Wetterich, 1993) by providing an exact evolution equation that can systematically compute corrections to the approximate one-loop result. The exact equation enables improved calculations of critical exponents in statistical mechanics and the high temperature phase transition in particle physics, building on prior results for the Kosterlitz-Thouless phase transition (Kosterlitz and Thouless, 1973, 1974) and three-dimensional critical exponents (Tetradis and Wetterich, 1993, 1994). The approach is related to earlier exact renormalization group equations derived from infinitesimal variations of ultraviolet cutoffs (Wegner, 1976; Polchinski, 1984).
