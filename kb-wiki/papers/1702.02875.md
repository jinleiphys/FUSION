# Pressure of a weakly magnetized hot and dense deconfined QCD matter in one-loop hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory

> We consider our recently obtained general structure of two point (self-energy and propagator) functions of quarks and gluons in a nontrivial background like a heat bath and an external magnetic field. Based on this, here we have computed free energy and pressure of quarks and gluons for a magnetized hot and dense deconfined QCD matter in weak field approximation. For heat bath we have used hard thermal loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) in presence of finite chemical potential. For weak field approximations we have obtained the pressure of QCD matter, both with and without the high temperature expansion. The results with high $T$ expansions are completely analytic and gauge independent but depends on the renormalization scale in addition to the temperature, chemical potential and the external magnetic field. We also discuss the modification of QCD Debye mass of such matter for an arbitrary magnetic field. Analytic expressions for Debye mass are also obtained for both strong and weak field approximation. It is found to exhibit some interesting features depending upon the three different scales, i.e, the quark mass, temperature and the strength of the magnetic field. The various divergences appearing in the quark and gluon free energies are regulated through appropriate counter terms. In weak field approximation, the low temperature behavior of the pressure is found to strongly depend on the magnetic field than that at high temperature. We also discuss the specific problem with one-loop HTLpt associated with the over-counting of certain orders in coupling.

## Key claim
The pressure of a hot and dense deconfined QCD matter in a weak magnetic field is computed using one-loop hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt), yielding a completely analytic and gauge-independent result under a high-temperature expansion. The calculation is valid only in the weak field hierarchy where the magnetic scale is the smallest ($\sqrt{q_fB} < m_f < T$), and the authors explicitly note a problem with one-loop HTLpt associated with the over-counting of certain orders in the coupling.

## Method
The authors compute the thermodynamic free energy (and thus pressure) by summing the one-loop quark and gluon free energies in the presence of a weak external magnetic field, using the general structure of two-point functions (self-energy and propagator) for quarks and gluons in a thermomagnetic medium. The calculation is performed within the hard-thermal-loop (HTL) approximation, expanding the Schwinger propagator for fermions in a weak field up to $\mathcal{O}[(q_fB)^2]$, and the resulting expressions are evaluated using the Matsubara formalism. The high-temperature expansion is used to obtain a fully analytic result, and divergences are regulated using appropriate counterterms, including an HTL counterterm for the Debye mass.

## Key numbers
*   The ideal gas quark free energy is given as $-\frac{7\pi^2T^4}{180}N_cN_f\left(1+\frac{120}{7}\hat{\mu}^2+\frac{240}{7}\hat{\mu}^4\right)$, where $\hat \mu=\mu/2\pi T$.
*   The thermal quark mass squared is $m_{\textrm{th}}^2 = \frac{g^2 C_F T^2}{8}\left(1+4\hat{\mu}^2\right)$.
*   The magnetic mass squared for a given flavor $f$ is $M_{B,f}^2 = \frac{q_fB}{16\pi^2}\left[-\frac{1}{4}\aleph(z)-\frac{\pi T}{2m_f}-\frac{\gamma_E}{2}\right]$, which diverges in the limit of small current quark mass ($m_f\rightarrow 0$).
*   The magnetic field generated in noncentral heavy-ion collisions is estimated to be up to the order of $q_fB \sim 15 m_\pi^2$ at the LHC.
*   The quark free energy at $\mathcal{O}[g^4]$ contains a term proportional to $M_B^4$ with a factor $\frac{2N_cg^4C_F^2}{9\epsilon}$, indicating a $1/\epsilon$ divergence originating from the HTL approximation.

## Context
This work extends the systematic application of hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) for the equation of state of QCD matter, previously computed up to three loops (NNLO) in the absence of a magnetic field by Andersen et al. and Haque et al., to include the effects of a weak external magnetic field. It builds upon recent calculations of the general structure of gluon and quark self-energies in a thermomagnetic medium by Karmakar et al. (2018) and Das et al. (2017), and contrasts with the strong field approximation used by Rath et al. (2017) for the longitudinal pressure. The results enable a systematic, gauge-independent calculation of the equation of state for the quark-gluon plasma created in non-central heavy-ion collisions, where the magnetic field is initially strong but rapidly decays to a weak value by the time the system thermalizes.
