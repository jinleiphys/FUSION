# Multipole approach for photo- and electroproduction of kaon

> We have analyzed the experimental data on K+Lambda photoproduction by using a multipole approach. In this analysis we use the background amplitudes constructed from appropriate Feynman diagrams in a gauge-invariant and crossing-symmetric fashion. Results of our analysis reveal the problem of mutual consistency between the new SAPHIR and CLAS data. We found that the problem could lead to different conclusions on ``missing resonances''. We have also extended our analysis to the finite Q^2 region and compared the result with the corresponding electroproduction data.

## Key claim

The mutual inconsistency between the SAPHIR and CLAS photoproduction data sets leads to substantially different sets of nucleon resonances being required to fit each data set, which in turn affects conclusions about "missing resonances"; surprisingly, the model fitted to SAPHIR data (Fit 1) better explains the CLAS electroproduction measurements than the model fitted to CLAS photoproduction data (Fit 2).

## Method

The authors performed a single-channel multipole analysis of K+Lambda photoproduction using background amplitudes constructed from tree-level Feynman diagrams (s-, u-, and t-channel Born terms plus K*(892) and K1(1270) t-channel vector mesons) in a gauge-invariant and crossing-symmetric fashion, with resonant multipoles parameterized in Breit-Wigner form. They performed two separate chi-squared minimization fits using the CERN-MINUIT code: Fit 1 used SAPHIR and LEPS photoproduction data, while Fit 2 used CLAS and LEPS photoproduction data, both including 15 nucleon resonances listed by the Particle Data Group. The analysis was extended to finite Q^2 for electroproduction by including longitudinal amplitudes and assuming a Q^2 dependence of the resonant multipoles.

## Key numbers

- The damping parameter X was assumed to be 500 MeV for all resonances in the energy-dependent partial width formula.
- The largest discrepancy between Fit 1 and Fit 2 appears between W = 1.75 GeV and 1.95 GeV in the forward direction, and from 1.8 to 2.4 GeV in the backward direction.
- The second peak at W approximately 1.9 GeV originates from the D13(2080) resonance with a fitted mass of 1936 MeV when using SAPHIR data or 1915 MeV when using CLAS data.
- The electroproduction fitting database used 178 data points, including transferred Lambda polarization components from CLAS.
- The electroproduction comparison used CLAS data at Q^2 = 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing search for "missing" nucleon resonances predicted by constituent quark models that are strongly coupled to strangeness channels, following earlier analyses by Mart (1999) and others. The paper directly addresses the inconsistency between the new SAPHIR data from Glander (2003) and CLAS data from Bradford (2005), finding that different resonance sets are required by each data set. The finding that the P11(1710) resonance is not required by either data set corroborates the conclusion of Arndt (2003). The analysis enables future work incorporating KSigma channels using isospin symmetry, as previously explored by Mart (1995).
