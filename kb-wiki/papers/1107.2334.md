# The Zel'dovich effect in harmonically trapped, ultra-cold quantum gases

> We investigate the Zel'dovich effect in the context of ultra-cold, harmonically trapped quantum gases. We suggest that currently available experimental techniques in cold-atoms research offer an exciting opportunity for a direct observation of the Zel'dovich effect without the difficulties imposed by conventional condensed matter and nuclear physics studies. We also demonstrate an interesting scaling symmetry in the level rearragements which has heretofore gone unnoticed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the universal two-body energy spectrum of harmonically trapped ultra-cold atoms, expressed as a function of the s-wave scattering length, is exactly equivalent to the Zel'dovich effect (level rearrangement), and that existing experimental data (Stöferle et al.) already constitute a direct observation of the ground-state branch of this effect, though the authors hedge that additional data on the excited-state branch would provide a "direct experimental confirmation" of the full effect.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the s-wave radial Schrödinger equation for two harmonically trapped atoms interacting via three different finite-range short-range potentials (finite square well, modified Pöschl-Teller, and exponential) in three dimensions, with the trap length scale much larger than the potential range (b/ℓ_osc = 0.01). They compute the two-body relative energy as a function of both the dimensionless potential strength g and the s-wave scattering length a, and compare the results to the exact zero-range contact-interaction spectrum. They also derive an analytical scaling transformation that maps the energy-versus-strength spectrum from higher resonance regions back onto the first region, and prove analytically that this mapping becomes exact in the zero-range limit.

## Key numbers

* For the finite square well, the zeroth critical strength is g_c,0 = π²/4, and the value of g for which a=0 in the second region is g_0^(2) ≃ 59.67951594410.
* The width of the level rearrangement region scales as Δg/g_c ∼ b/ℓ_osc, where b is the range of the potential and ℓ_osc = √(ħ/Mω) is the harmonic oscillator length.
* The mapping error between different resonance regions scales as δE = (4b/5ℓ_osc)(A(g_k') - A(g_k)), showing δE → 0 as b → 0.
* The unperturbed harmonic oscillator energies for the 1S and 2S levels are E = 3/2 ħω and E = 7/2 ħω, respectively; at the back of the Zel'dovich spiral (|a| = ∞), the energies become E = 1/2 ħω and E = 5/2 ħω.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical study of the Zel'dovich effect from exotic atoms (Combescure et al.) and quantum dots to ultra-cold atomic gases, where the short-range interaction is tunable via Feshbach resonances. The authors connect their analysis to the experimental measurements of the two-body binding energy versus scattering length by Stöferle et al., showing that those data already realize the ground-state branch of the effect. The paper also reveals a previously unnoticed scaling symmetry in the level rearrangements, which becomes exact in the zero-range limit and is related to the quantum defect approach discussed by Kolo and Ostrovsky.
