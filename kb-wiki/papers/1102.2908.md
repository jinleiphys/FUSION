# Chiral condensate and chemical freeze-out

> We consider a chemical freeze-out mechanism which is based on a strong medium dependence of the rates for inelastic flavor-equilibrating collisions based on the delocalization of hadronic wave functions and growing hadronic radii when approaching the chiral restoration. We investigate the role of mesonic (pion) and baryonic (nucleon) fluctuations for melting the chiral condensate in the phase diagram in the (T,mu)-plane. We apply the PNJL model beyond mean-field and present an effective generalization of the chiral perturbation theory result which accounts for the medium dependence of the pion decay constant while preserving the GMOR relation. We demonstrate within a schematic resonance gas model consisting of a variable number of pionic and nucleonic degrees of freedom that within the above model a quantitative explanation of the hadonic freeze-out curve and its phenomenological conditions can be given.

# Chiral condensate and chemical freeze-out

## Key claim

The paper proposes that chemical freeze-out in heavy-ion collisions is governed by a kinetic condition where the expansion time scale equals the collision time for flavor-equilibrating reactions, with the key mechanism being the medium-dependent delocalization of hadronic wave functions driven by the melting of the chiral condensate. The authors demonstrate this within a schematic resonance gas model with artificially enhanced numbers of pionic and nucleonic degrees of freedom, rather than a full hadron resonance spectrum.

## Method

The authors employ a Polyakov-loop extended Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model beyond mean-field, incorporating mesonic (pion) and baryonic (nucleon) fluctuations to compute the chiral condensate in the (T, μ) plane. They use the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner (GMOR) relation to connect the medium-dependent pion decay constant to the chiral condensate, and adopt a geometrical Povh–Hüfner law for hadron-hadron cross sections where radii diverge as the condensate melts. The freeze-out condition τ_exp(T, μ) = τ_coll(T, μ) is evaluated using a schematic resonance gas with variable numbers of pion (d_π) and nucleon (d_N) degrees of freedom, with the expansion time scale derived from entropy conservation.

## Key numbers

- The pion radius in vacuum is r_π = 0.59 fm and the nucleon radius is r_N = 0.74 fm, giving a hard core radius r_0 = 0.45 fm.
- The pion-nucleon sigma term is σ_N = 45 MeV.
- The NJL model parameters used: GΛ² = 2.31825, Λ = 602.472 MeV, m_0 = 5.27697 MeV, M_π = 140 MeV, f_π = 92.4 MeV.
- The phenomenological freeze-out conditions reproduced are s/T³ ≈ 7 and total baryon plus antibaryon density n_B + n_antiB ≈ 0.12 fm⁻³.
- Best agreement with phenomenological freeze-out data is obtained for d_π = 8 and d_N = 14 to 20 degrees of freedom.
- The freeze-out curves correspond to a reduction of the chiral condensate to about 70% of its vacuum value, where hadron-hadron cross sections are roughly twice the vacuum value.

## Context

This work extends the percolation theory approach of Magas (2003) and the softest-point mechanism of Toneev (2000) by providing a unified description linking geometrical, hydrodynamic, and kinetic aspects of chemical freeze-out to the chiral condensate as an order parameter. The approach builds on beyond-mean-field PNJL model developments by Schaefer (2007), Blaschke (2007, 2009), and Skokov (2010), and generalizes chiral perturbation theory results of Gasser (1986) to include medium-dependent pion decay constants while preserving the GMOR relation. The results enable a quantitative connection between the phenomenological freeze-out curve from statistical model analyses (Cleymans 1998, 1999, 2005; Andronic 2009) and the fundamental chiral symmetry restoration dynamics of QCD, though the authors note that a full treatment requires replacing the schematic resonance gas with the complete hadron resonance spectrum using models for resonance mass dependence on current quark mass such as that of Leupold (2006).
