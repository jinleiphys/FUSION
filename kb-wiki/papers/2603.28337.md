# Combined analysis of the data on cross sections and spin density matrix elements for $K^*╬ú$ photoproduction reactions

> In our earlier work [Phys. Rev. C \textbf{98}, 045209 (2018)], we analyzed the differential cross-section data from the CLAS Collaboration for the reactions $╬│p \to K^{*+}╬ú^0$ and $╬│p \to K^{*0} ╬ú^+$ using an effective Lagrangian approach. We found that a satisfactory description of the data required the inclusion of the $s$-channel $╬ö(1905)5/2^+$ resonance, in addition to $t$-channel exchanges of $K$, $╬║$, and $K^*$, $s$-channel contributions from nucleons ($N$) and $╬ö$, $u$-channel exchanges of $╬ø$, $╬ú$, and $╬ú^*$, and a generalized contact term. However, the contribution from $t$-channel $╬║$ exchange is significant in one fit but negligible in the other. # Combined analysis of the data on cross sections and spin density matrix elements for $K^*\Sigma$ photoproduction reactions

## Key claim

The LEPS parity spin asymmetry $P_\sigma$ data for $\gamma p \to K^{*0}\Sigma^+$ at photon energies $E_\gamma = 1.85$ÔÇô$2.96$ GeV do **not** necessarily support a dominant role of $t$-channel $\kappa$ exchange, contrary to earlier claims in the literature; two distinct fits describe the data equally well, one with significant $\kappa$ exchange and one where its contribution is negligible.

## Method

The authors extended their earlier effective Lagrangian analysis (Wang, 2018) of CLAS differential cross-section data for $\gamma p \to K^{*+}\Sigma^0$ and $\gamma p \to K^{*0}\Sigma^+$ by incorporating LEPS spin density matrix element data for $\gamma p \to K^{*0}\Sigma^+$. The amplitude included $t$-channel $K$, $\kappa$, and $K^*$ exchanges; $s$-channel $N$, $\Delta$, and $\Delta(1905)5/2^+$ contributions; $u$-channel $\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, and $\Sigma^*$ exchanges; and a generalized contact term. The cutoff masses for $s$-channel $N$, $\Delta$, and $\Delta(1905)5/2^+$ contributions, and for $t$-channel $K$ and $\kappa$ exchanges, were treated as independent free parameters and refitted to all available data simultaneously.

## Key numbers

- Two fits (model I and model II) yielded $\chi^2_{{\rm d}\sigma}/{\rm ND}_{{\rm d}\sigma} = 1.97$ and $2.02$, respectively, for the differential cross-section data (compared to $1.84$ in the earlier work that fit only cross sections).
- The $\kappa$ exchange cutoff mass was $1000 \pm 2$ MeV in model I (negligible contribution) and $1800 \pm 2$ MeV in model II (significant contribution).
- The $\Delta(1905)5/2^+$ resonance mass was fitted to $1855 \pm 2$ MeV (model I) and $1910 \pm 0.4$ MeV (model II), within the PDG range $[1855, 1910]$ MeV.
- The $\Delta(1905)5/2^+$ resonance width was $346 \pm 91$ MeV (model I) and $400 \pm 2$ MeV (model II), within the PDG range $[270, 400]$ MeV.
- The $\Delta(1905)5/2^+$ helicity amplitudes were $A_{1/2} = 0.025 \pm 0.007$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ (model I) and $0.017 \pm 0.0001$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ (model II), within the PDG range $[0.017, 0.027]$ GeV$^{-1/2}$; $A_{3/2} = -0.038 \pm 0.013$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ (model I) and $-0.055 \pm 0.0002$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ (model II), within the PDG range $[-0.055, -0.035]$ GeV$^{-1/2}$.
- Predictions for $P_\sigma$ at $E_\gamma = 8.5$ GeV are presented: model I predicts values less than $0.5$, while model II predicts values close to $1$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier two-channel combined analysis (Wang, 2018) of CLAS cross-section data for $\gamma p \to K^{*+}\Sigma^0$ and $\gamma p \to K^{*0}\Sigma^+$, which required the $\Delta(1905)5/2^+$ resonance. It contradicts the claim in Hwang (2012) that the LEPS $P_\sigma$ data support a dominant $\kappa$ exchange, and also differs from the analysis in Oh (2006) which suggested a significant role for $\kappa$ exchange based on preliminary cross-section data. The results challenge the interpretation in Ben (2023) that proposed $N(2080)^-$ and $N(2270)3/2^-$ molecular states as alternatives to $\Delta(1905)5/2^+$. Future measurements of $P_\sigma$ at $E_\gamma = 8.5$ GeV, where $s$- and $u$-channel contributions are suppressed, could distinguish between the two models and clarify the role of $\kappa$ exchange.
