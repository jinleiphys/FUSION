# Chiral symmetry restoration and hyperon suppression in neutron stars

> The ``hyperon puzzle'' remains a fundamental challenge in nuclear astrophysics. We investigate hyperon emergence in neutron star matter using the $SU(3)$ parity doublet model with chiral representation $(3,\bar{3}) + (\bar{3},3)$. This framework naturally incorporates chiral symmetry restoration and provides a systematic description of baryon masses in dense matter through the interplay between the chiral condensate and the chiral invariant mass $m_0$. We find that the hyperon onset density exhibits strong sensitivity to $m_0$: for $m_0 = 500$ MeV, hyperons first appear at $1.9n_0$ while for $m_0 \gtrsim 750$ MeV, hyperons emerge only above $5n_0$. This delayed onset arises from the weakened density dependence of baryon masses at larger $m_0$ values. When the hyperon onset density exceeds the expected quark-hadron transition range ($2$--$5n_0$), matter undergoes deconfinement before hyperons populate, avoiding the EoS softening while maintaining consistency with massive neutron star observations. Our results demonstrate that chiral dynamics provides a natural resolution to the hyperon puzzle without requiring ad hoc repulsive hyperon interactions.

## Key claim

The hyperon onset density in neutron star matter is highly sensitive to the chiral invariant mass \(m_0\) within the SU(3) parity doublet model; for \(m_0 \gtrsim 750\) MeV, hyperons appear only above \(5n_0\), which exceeds the expected quark-hadron transition range of \(2\)–\(5n_0\), so matter undergoes deconfinement before hyperons populate, thereby avoiding equation-of-state softening without requiring ad hoc repulsive hyperon interactions.

## Method

The authors constructed an SU(3) parity doublet model using the linear realization of chiral symmetry with the chiral representation \((3,\bar{3}) + (\bar{3},3)\), incorporating the full baryon octet (nucleons, \(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma\), \(\Xi\)) and their negative-parity partners. Scalar and pseudoscalar mesons were introduced through a \(3 \times 3\) matrix field, vector mesons were included via hidden local symmetry, and model parameters were determined by fitting to vacuum baryon masses and nuclear matter saturation properties. The hyperon onset density was then calculated for different values of the chiral invariant mass \(m_0\) by solving the thermodynamic potential in neutron star matter under beta equilibrium and charge neutrality.

## Key numbers

* For \(m_0 = 500\) MeV, hyperons first appear at \(1.9n_0\) (where \(n_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is nuclear saturation density).
* For \(m_0 \gtrsim 750\) MeV, hyperons emerge only above \(5n_0\).
* The expected quark-hadron transition range is \(2\)–\(5n_0\).
* Vacuum inputs: pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 92.1\) MeV, kaon decay constant \(f_K = 110\) MeV, with \(\sigma_0 = f_\pi\) and \(\sigma_{s0} = 2f_K - f_\pi = 128\) MeV.
* Input baryon masses used for parameter fitting: \(m_{N,+} = 939\) MeV, \(m_{N,-} = 1535\) MeV, \(m_{\Lambda,+} = 1116\) MeV, \(m_{\Sigma,+} = 1193\) MeV.
* Predicted \(\Xi\) ground state mass ranges from 1324.64 MeV (for \(m_0 = 500\) MeV) to 1318.25 MeV (for \(m_0 = 900\) MeV), consistent with the experimental value of 1318 MeV.
* Predicted \(\Lambda\) chiral partner mass is 1636.72 MeV, associated with \(\Lambda^*(1670)\); predicted \(\Xi\) chiral partner mass is approximately 1918 MeV, consistent with \(\Xi^*(1950)\); predicted \(\Sigma\) chiral partner mass is 2014.85 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the SU(2) parity doublet model (Detar 1988, Jido 2001) to SU(3) to address the hyperon puzzle, where conventional approaches such as introducing repulsive hyperon-hyperon interactions via vector meson exchange (Schaffner 1995, Weissenborn 2011) or three-body forces (Vidana 2010, Yamamoto 2014) do not explicitly incorporate chiral symmetry. The results demonstrate that chiral dynamics alone can resolve the hyperon puzzle without ad hoc interactions, and the framework enables future studies of the interplay between chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement in dense matter, as well as predictions for the masses of chiral partner states of hyperons that can be tested experimentally.
