# New signatures of phase transition from Statistical Models of Nuclear multifragmentation

> The study of liquid-gas phase transition in heavy ion collisions has generated a lot of interest amongst the nuclear physicists in the recent years. In heavy ion collisions, there is no direct way of measuring the state variables like entropy, pressure, energy and hence unambiguous characterization of phase transition becomes difficult. This work proposes new signatures of phase transition that can be extracted from the observables which are easily accessible in experiments. It is observed that the temperature dependence of the first order derivative of the order parameters in nuclear liquid gas phase transition exhibit similar behavior as that of the variation of specific heat at constant volume Cv which is an established signature of first order phase transition. This motivates us to propose these derivatives as confirmatory signals of liquid-gas phase transition. The measurement of these signals in easily feasible in most experiments as compared to the other signatures like specific heat, caloric curve or bimodality. Total multiplicity, size of largest cluster are some of the order parameters which have been studied. Statistical Models based on canonical ensemble and lattice gas model has been used for the study. This temperature where the peak appears is designated to be the transition temperature and the effect of certain parameters on this has also been examined. The multiplicity derivative signature proposed in this work has been further confirmed by other theoretical models as well as in experimental study.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the first-order temperature derivative of experimentally accessible observables (total multiplicity, size of the largest cluster, and a normalized difference between the first and second largest clusters) can serve as confirmatory signatures of the liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear multifragmentation, because these derivatives exhibit a peak at the same temperature as the specific heat at constant volume \(C_v\), which is an established signature of a first-order phase transition. The authors hedge that this equivalence holds for finite systems where the discontinuity or divergence of thermodynamic variables is replaced by a sudden jump or maximum.

## Method

The authors used the Canonical Thermodynamical Model (CTM), a statistical equilibrium model implemented in the canonical ensemble, to calculate fragment yields for finite nuclear systems at a freeze-out volume of \(V=6V_0\). They computed the temperature dependence of total multiplicity \(M\), the average size of the largest cluster \(\langle A_{max} \rangle\), and the normalized difference \(a_2 = (\langle A_{max} \rangle - \langle A_{max-1} \rangle) / (\langle A_{max} \rangle + \langle A_{max-1} \rangle)\), and then took their first-order derivatives with respect to temperature. The study was performed for systems of different sizes (A=50, 100, 200, 208, and 58) and for different freeze-out volumes (\(V_f = 2V_0, 3V_0, 4V_0, 6V_0, 8V_0\)), both with and without Coulomb interaction. The multiplicity derivative signal was further confirmed using a Lattice Gas Model with nearest-neighbor interactions and a Metropolis Monte-Carlo sampling.

## Key numbers

- For a fragmenting system of A=200 identical nucleons with no Coulomb force, the derivatives \(d\textbf{a}_{max}/dT\), \(d\textbf{a}_2/dT\), \(dM/dT\), and \(C_v\) all exhibit maxima at approximately T = 6 MeV, designated as the transition temperature.
- For a system with Z=82 and N=126, the peak in \(dM/dT\) coincides with the maximum of \(C_v\); to draw them on the same scale, \(C_v\) was normalized by a factor of 1/50.
- For a system with Z=28 and N=30, the peak in \(dM/dT\) also coincides with the maximum of \(C_v\); to draw them on the same scale, \(C_v\) was normalized by a factor of 1/100.
- The peak position of \(dM/dT\) shifts to higher temperature for larger source size (A=50, 100, 200 at fixed \(V_f=6V_0\)) and to lower temperature for greater freeze-out volume (\(V_f=2V_0, 6V_0, 8V_0\) for fixed A=200).
- The multiplicity derivative signal was experimentally verified using three reactions: \(^{40}Ar + ^{58}Ni\), \(^{40}Ar + ^{27}Al\), and \(^{40}Ar + ^{48}Ti\) at 47 MeV/nucleon (Wada, year not given in text).

## Context

This work extends the search for experimental signatures of the liquid-gas phase transition in nuclear multifragmentation, which has been studied theoretically and experimentally by many groups (Dasgupta, Borderie, Gross, Chomaz, and others). The proposed derivative-based signatures are presented as more experimentally accessible alternatives to established signatures such as the specific heat peak, the caloric curve, or bimodality in the probability distribution of the largest cluster (Gulminelli, Krishnamachari, Pleimling, and others). The multiplicity derivative signal was further confirmed by other theoretical models including the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) (Lin, year not given), Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model (Liu, year not given), and Nuclear Statistical Equilibrium (NSE) model (Bakeer, year not given), and was experimentally verified by Wada and collaborators. The work enables future experimental studies to use these easily measurable derivatives to determine the transition temperature and study its dependence on system size and freeze-out volume.
