# Event shape sorting

> We propose a novel method for sorting events of multiparticle production according to the azimuthal anisotropy of their momentum distribution. Although the method is quite general, we advocate its use in analysis of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions where large number of hadrons is produced. The advantage of our method is that it can automatically sort out samples of events with histograms that indicate similar distributions of hadrons. It takes into account the whole measured histograms with all orders of anisotropy instead of a specific observable (e.g. $v_2$, $v_3$, $q_2$). It can be used for more exclusive experimental studies of flow anisotropies which are then more easily compared to theoretical calculations. It may also be useful in the construction of mixed-events background for correlation studies as it allows to select events with similar momentum distribution.

# Event shape sorting

## Key claim

The authors propose a novel Bayesian method for sorting multiparticle production events according to the full azimuthal histogram shape rather than a single anisotropy observable, but note that the initial alignment of events (e.g., by second-order or third-order event plane) strongly biases the final sorting and no automatic alignment algorithm is yet recommended.

## Method

Events are initially sorted by a chosen observable (e.g., q₂) and divided into N event bins. For each event, the Bayesian probability that it belongs to each event bin is calculated from the multinomial likelihood of its azimuthal angle histogram given the average histogram of that bin. Events are then re-sorted by their mean event bin number, and the procedure iterates until convergence, grouping events with similar angular distributions together.

## Key numbers

- 5000 events were generated with multiplicities between 300 and 3000 for the toy model studies.
- The algorithm converged after 65 iterations when starting from random ordering in the simple elliptic-flow-only case.
- For the toy model with anisotropies up to 5th order, the algorithm converged after 121 iterations from random initial ordering.
- 2000 AMPT events corresponding to the 0–20% centrality class at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV were generated, with parton screening mass set to 2.097 fm⁻¹.
- When starting the algorithm with initial ordering according to q₂ in the 5th-order toy model, it failed to converge within 5000 iterations.

## Context

This work extends the concept of "Event Shape Engineering" (ESE), which typically selects events based on the flow vector magnitude q_n (most often q₂). The authors demonstrate that sorting by a single observable like q₂ may not capture the full event shape when multiple flow harmonics are present, and that their Bayesian sorting method can reveal correlations (or lack thereof) between common observables and the true event shape. The method is adopted from Lehmann (2007) and was previously used in a different context by Jackson. It enables more exclusive experimental studies of flow anisotropies for comparison with theoretical calculations, and may improve mixed-event background construction for correlation studies and femtoscopic analyses.
