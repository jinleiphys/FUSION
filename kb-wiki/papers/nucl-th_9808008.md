# Classical Quark Models: An Introduction

> Summary of three lectures at the 11th Physics Summer School on Frontiers in Nuclear Physics: From Quark-Gluon Plasma to Supernova, Australian National University, Canberra, Australia, 12-23/Jan./1998. 41 pages, 12 figures, LaTeX, sprocl.sty, epsfig.sty, utphys.bst

## Key claim

These lectures provide an elementary introduction to several classical quark models used to understand light mesons and baryons, intended for beginning graduate students. The authors explicitly note that the presentation is limited to a few relatively simple models due to space constraints, and that over time quark models have become more complicated and abstract, potentially obscuring the original beauty and simplicity.

## Method

The lectures present the harmonic oscillator shell model for baryons, where three quarks are linked by virtual springs and the center-of-mass motion is exactly separable. The authors then introduce the non-relativistic reduction of one-gluon exchange as a residual interaction, followed by the hadronic shell model of Isgur and Karl which includes a colour Coulomb term and linear confining potential. Finally, the MIT bag model is developed from the Dirac equation with a scalar potential that gives quarks zero effective mass inside a spherical cavity and infinite mass outside, with confinement enforced by requiring no quark current flow through the bag surface.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses in the oscillator model: \(m_u \simeq m_d \simeq 340\) MeV, \(m_s \simeq 500\) MeV
- Strong coupling constant from hyperfine splitting: \(\alpha_s \sim 0.6\)
- Linear confining potential strength in the hadronic shell model: \(b \sim 0.18\) GeV\(^2\)
- First two positive roots for \(\kappa = -1\) MIT bag states: \(\Omega_{1-1} = 2.04\) (1s\(_{1/2}\)), \(\Omega_{2-1} = 5.40\) (2s\(_{1/2}\))
- Experimental nucleon excited state masses: D\(_{13}\) and S\(_{11}\) at \(\sim 1550\) MeV, N(1680) F\(_{15}\) at \(2\hbar\omega\)
- Current quark masses at the 1 GeV scale: \(m_u \sim 6\) MeV, \(m_d \sim 9\) MeV, \(m_s \sim 150\) MeV

## Context

This paper extends the pedagogical tradition of introducing quark models for hadron structure, building on the harmonic oscillator shell model and the MIT bag model developed by Bogolubov (1968) and the MIT group. The lectures discuss the Roper resonance as an open problem, noting it has been described as a breathing mode by Guichon (1985) and as resulting from coupling to inelastic two-pion channels by Pearce (1986) and Schutz (1998). The work enables students to understand the Quark Meson Coupling model of Thomas (1984) and applications to nucleon properties in medium, including neutron star matter.
