# Parity Violation in Polarized-Gamma Proton Compton Scattering

> Polarized beam gamma p Compton scattering provides a theoretically clean way to extract the isovector parity violating pion-nucleon coupling constant $h_{πNN}^{(1)}$. This channel is more tractable experimentally than the recently proposed extraction of $h_{πNN}^{(1)}$ from the Bedaque-Savage process --- polarized target gamma p compton scattering. The leading parity violating effect is calculated using Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory. The size of the asymmetry is estimated to be ~ 4 10^{-8} for 120 MeV photon energy.

## Key claim

Polarized-beam gamma-proton Compton scattering provides a theoretically clean way to extract the isovector parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling constant \(h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\), with the helicity asymmetry estimated to be \(\sim 4 \times 10^{-8}\) at 120 MeV photon energy, but with a \(\sim 25\%\) uncertainty dominated by delta resonance contributions that could be reduced by including the delta as an explicit degree of freedom.

## Method

The authors used Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory (HB\(\chi\)PT) to calculate the leading parity-violating structure function \({\cal F}_4\) from pion loop diagrams with magnetic photon-nucleon coupling, which contributes to the polarized-beam asymmetry. They combined this with previously calculated leading-order parity-violating structure functions \({\cal F}_{1-3}\) from Bedaque and Savage (2000) and next-to-leading order parity-conserving structure functions from Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1995) to compute the photon helicity asymmetry \(A_{\gamma\gamma}\) as a function of photon energy and scattering angle.

## Key numbers

- The estimated helicity asymmetry at 120 MeV photon energy and \(\theta = \pi/2\) is \(A_{\gamma\gamma}(120\text{ MeV},\pi/2) \sim -3.8 \times 10^{-8}\), with \(\sim 25\%\) uncertainty from delta resonance contributions.
- In the low-energy limit (\(\omega \ll m_\pi\)), the asymmetry at \(\theta = \pi/2\) scales as \(A_{\gamma\gamma} \sim -8.8 \times 10^{-9} \left( \frac{h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}}{5 \times 10^{-7}} \right) \left( \frac{\omega}{70\text{ MeV}} \right)^3\).
- The naive estimate for the coupling constant is \(|h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}| \sim 5 \times 10^{-7}\), consistent with the "best value" from Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1980).
- The asymmetry vanishes at backward angle \(\theta = \pi\) as a consequence of time-reversal invariance.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Bedaque and Savage (2000) for extracting \(h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\) from polarized-target Compton scattering, which is experimentally impractical due to the difficulty of rapid target polarization flipping. The polarized-beam channel is more tractable experimentally because rapid beam helicity flipping is a standard technique already employed in many parity-violating experiments. This calculation provides a model-independent way to constrain \(h_{\pi NN}^{(1)}\) with \(\sim 25\%\) uncertainty, which would greatly improve understanding compared to previous extractions from many-body systems like \(^{18}\text{F}\), \(^{133}\text{Cs}\), and \(^{205}\text{Tl}\) that differ by an order of magnitude with large uncertainties.
