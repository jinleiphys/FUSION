# Examining nuclear effects in neutrino interactions with transverse kinematic imbalance

> We present a Monte Carlo truth study examining nuclear effects in charged-current neutrino interactions using observables constructed in the transverse plane. Three distributions are introduced that show very weak dependence on neutrino flux and its associated uncertainty. Measurements comparing these distributions between quasi-elastic-like and single charged pion final states will provide new constraints of nuclear effects. It is suggested that the on-axis position in the NuMI beam provides the correct flux to take advantage of this reduced energy dependence in measuring nuclear effect-generated transverse imbalances.

# Examining nuclear effects in neutrino interactions with transverse kinematic imbalance

## Key claim
Transverse kinematic imbalance variables (δp_T, δα_T, δφ_T) in charged-current neutrino interactions show very weak dependence on neutrino flux and its associated uncertainty, but only when the neutrino beam energy is sufficiently high (e.g., the NuMI on-axis beam) such that the four-momentum transfer (Q²) shape becomes asymptotically independent of neutrino energy for quasi-elastic and resonant interactions.

## Method
The study uses Monte Carlo truth simulations, primarily with the NuWro generator (also cross-checked with NEUT, GENIE, and GiBUU), to examine nuclear effects in charged-current neutrino interactions on a carbon target exposed to the NuMI on-axis muon neutrino flux. Three transverse variables are defined from the final state lepton and hadron momenta in the plane transverse to the incoming neutrino: δp_T (total transverse momentum imbalance), δα_T (angular imbalance indicating acceleration or deceleration of the hadronic system), and δφ_T (angular difference from back-to-back transverse configuration). The analysis compares predictions for quasi-elastic (QE) and resonant single-pion production (RES) interactions, with and without final state interactions (FSIs) and Pauli blocking enabled.

## Key numbers
- For neutrino energies below approximately 2 GeV, the Q² shape changes significantly with increasing neutrino energy, while the shape change between 3 GeV and 6 GeV is minimal.
- For resonant interactions, the hadronic mass is distributed about 1.2 GeV when isolating single pion production via a delta resonance.
- The δp_T distribution is governed by Fermi motion for δp_T less than the Fermi momentum (k_f), and becomes independent of the initial state for δp_T greater than approximately 2k_f.
- Pauli blocking suppresses events at δα_T approximately 180° for muon transverse momentum (p^μ_T) less than approximately k_f.
- Over an order of magnitude of beam energies, the change in the shape of δp_T and δα_T is minimal.

## Context
This work extends previous investigations of transverse momentum imbalance in neutrino scattering, including the Big European Bubble Chamber (BEBC) study on deuterium at the CERN SPS (which was dominated by deep inelastic scattering due to higher beam energy) and NOMAD's use of transverse kinematic imbalance as a QE event selection variable. The MINERvA collaboration previously measured the coplanarity angle (φ = π - δφ_T) in their "CC with no pions" event selection. The δα_T variable was proposed for the first time in a recent truth study by the same author. This approach enables new constraints on nuclear effects by comparing transverse imbalance distributions between QE-like and single charged pion final states, providing an in-channel probe of final state interactions that is complementary to thin-target hadron scattering data typically used to tune FSI simulations (e.g., GENIE user manual Section 2.5).
