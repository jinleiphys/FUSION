# Finite Temperature QCD Sum Rules: a Review

> The method of QCD sum rules at finite temperature is reviewed, with emphasis on recent results. These include predictions for the survival of charmonium and bottonium states, at and beyond the critical temperature for de-confinement, as later confirmed by lattice QCD simulations. Also included are determinations in the light-quark vector and axial-vector channels, allowing to analyse the Weinberg sum rules, and predict the dimuon spectrum in heavy ion collisions in the region of the rho-meson. Also in this sector, the determination of the temperature behaviour of the up-down quark mass, together with the pion decay constant, will be described. Finally, an extension of the QCD sum rule method to incorporate finite baryon chemical potential is reviewed.

## Key claim

This review argues that QCD sum rules at finite temperature provide a consistent framework for studying deconfinement and chiral-symmetry restoration, with the key phenomenological order parameter for deconfinement being the perturbative QCD threshold \(s_0(T)\) rather than hadron masses, which are shown to be irrelevant for signaling the phase transition.

## Method

The authors review the extension of QCD sum rules to finite temperature using finite energy sum rules (FESR) based on Cauchy's theorem in the complex squared-energy plane, rather than Laplace transform sum rules which exponentially suppress the crucial threshold parameter \(s_0(T)\). Thermal effects are incorporated via Dolan-Jackiw thermal quark propagators at the one-loop level, yielding both annihilation (time-like) and scattering (space-like) contributions to the spectral functions. The method relates the hadronic spectral function, parametrized by a ground-state pole, resonances, and a perturbative QCD continuum above \(s_0(T)\), to the QCD operator product expansion, allowing extraction of the temperature dependence of condensates, couplings, and the deconfinement parameter.

## Key numbers

* The pion decay constant at zero temperature is \(f_\pi \simeq 93\ \text{MeV}\) (from the pion-pole contribution to the axial-vector correlator).
* The \(\rho\)-meson mass is \(M_\rho = 775.26 \pm 0.25\ \text{MeV}\), width \(\Gamma_\rho = 147.8 \pm 0.9\ \text{MeV}\), and coupling \(f_\rho = 4.97 \pm 0.07\) (from leptonic decay data).
* The perturbative QCD threshold at zero temperature in the vector channel, using a Breit-Wigner parametrization with five-loop radiative corrections, is \(s_0 = 1.44\ \text{GeV}^2\) (\(\sqrt{s_0} = 1.2\ \text{GeV}\)).
* In the axial-vector channel with pion-saturation, the first FESR gives \(s_0 = 8\pi^2 f_\pi^2 \simeq 0.7\ \text{GeV}^2\) at \(T=0\).
* The critical temperature for deconfinement from the early Bochkarev-Shaposhnikov analysis is \(T_c \simeq 150\ \text{MeV}\), where \(s_0(T)\) drops from \(s_0(0) \simeq 2\ \text{GeV}^2\) to \(s_0(T_c) \simeq 0.2\ \text{GeV}^2\).
* The experimentally measured pion decay constant is \(f_\pi = 92.21 \pm 0.02\ \text{MeV}\) (from the Particle Data Group).

## Context

This review extends the foundational work of Bochkarev and Shaposhnikov (1986), who first applied Laplace transform QCD sum rules at finite temperature to the vector meson channels, and the subsequent shift to finite energy sum rules by the authors and collaborators. The approach enables predictions for the survival of charmonium and bottonium states above the critical temperature, later confirmed by lattice QCD simulations, and provides a framework for analyzing the dimuon spectrum in heavy-ion collisions in the rho-meson region. The review also covers the extension to finite baryon chemical potential, opening the door to studies of dense QCD matter relevant for neutron star interiors and heavy-ion collision phenomenology.
