# On the Regularisation in J-matrix Methods

> We investigate the effects of the regularization procedure used in the J-Matrix method. We show that it influences the convergence, and propose an alternative regularization approach. We explicitly perform some model calculations to demonstrate the improvement.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the standard regularization of the Neumann (irregular) solution in the J-Matrix method introduces a dependence on the oscillator length \(b\) that can severely limit convergence, and proposes an alternative regularization using a different oscillator width \(b_0\) for the regularizing term to improve convergence without increasing the basis size.

## Method

The authors analyze the regularized Neumann function \(\overline{\Psi}_l^N(r|k,b)\) for zero angular momentum, deriving its analytic form and showing that the product \(kb\) controls how quickly it approaches the true Neumann function \(\Psi_l^N(r|k)\) as \(r\) increases. They then modify the regularization by replacing the source term \(\Phi_{0l}(r|b)\) with \(\Phi_{0l}(r|b_0)\) using a different oscillator length \(b_0\), leading to new expansion coefficients \(C_{nl}^{N,b}(k,b_0)\) that satisfy an inhomogeneous three-term recurrence with a source containing the overlap \(\langle \Phi_{nl}(b)|\Phi_{0l}(b_0)\rangle\). The method is tested on Gauss, exponential, square-well, and Yukawa potentials with depth \(V_0 = -80\) MeV and width \(a = 1.0\) fm, comparing s-wave phase shifts at matching point \(N=25\) to exact results from the Variable Phase Approach.

## Key numbers

- For \(b=3\) fm and \(b_0=0.6\) fm, the JMR method yields s-wave phase shifts for the square-well and Yukawa potentials that remain much closer to the exact Variable Phase Approach results than the standard JM method, especially at higher energies.
- The optimal regularization parameter \(b_0\) is approximately 0.4 fm for the square-well potential and 0.2 fm for the Yukawa potential, as inferred from figures showing phase shift dependence on \(b_0\).
- For \(kb \to 0\), the regularized Neumann function at the origin behaves as \(\overline{\Psi}_0^N(0|k,b) \simeq -1/(kb) \to -\infty\); for \(kb \to \infty\), it behaves as \(\overline{\Psi}_0^N(0|k,b) \simeq \exp((kb)^2/2)/(kb) \to +\infty\).
- The expansion coefficient \(C_{00}^N(k,b)\) diverges as \(O(1/(kb))\) for \(kb \to 0\) and as \(O(\exp((kb)^2/2)/(kb))\) for \(kb \to \infty\).

## Context

This work extends the J-Matrix method originally developed by Heller and Yamani (1970s) and applied to nuclear physics by Vasilevsky and collaborators (1990s). It addresses the slow convergence problem noted in prior applications, where large basis sizes are needed due to the regularization procedure. The new regularization is computationally only slightly more involved and can be combined with other convergence-improving techniques, such as the semiclassical potential approach of Vanroose et al. (2002), to further reduce the required matrix size.
