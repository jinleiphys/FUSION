# Liquid-Gas Phase Transition of Supernova Matter and Its Relation to Nucleosynthesis

> We investigate the liquid-gas phase transition of dense matter in supernova explosion by the relativistic mean field approach and fragment based statistical model. The boiling temperature is found to be high (T_{boil} >= 0.7 MeV for rho_B >= 10^{-7} fm^{-3}), and adiabatic paths are shown to go across the boundary of coexisting region even with high entropy. This suggests that materials experienced phase transition can be ejected to outside. We calculated fragment mass and isotope distribution around the boiling point. We found that heavy elements at the iron, the first, second, and third peaks of r-process are abundantly formed at rho_B = 10^{-7}, 10^{-5}, 10^{-3} and 10^{-2} fm^{-3}, respectively.

## Key claim

The authors claim that supernova matter can experience a liquid-gas phase transition during explosion, and that the boiling temperature remains high (≥0.7 MeV) even at very low baryon densities, with adiabatic paths crossing the coexistence boundary even at high entropy per baryon (S/B ≥ 10), suggesting that material that underwent phase transition can be ejected and serve as a pre-process for r-process nucleosynthesis.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic mean field (RMF) approach with the TM1 parameter set to compute the liquid-gas coexistence region of supernova matter under Gibbs conditions, treating liquid and gas phases as uniform and infinite. They then applied a fragment-based nuclear statistical equilibrium (NSE) model with a grand canonical ensemble, incorporating Coulomb energy reduction from electron screening via the Wigner-Seitz approximation, to calculate fragment mass and isotope distributions at densities from 10⁻⁷ to 10⁻² fm⁻³ around the boiling temperature.

## Key numbers

- Boiling temperature T_boil ≥ 0.7 MeV for baryon density ρ_B ≥ 10⁻⁷ fm⁻³
- Critical temperature T_c ~ 14 MeV for lepton fraction Y_L = 0.3–0.4
- Heavy elements at the iron peak formed abundantly at ρ_B = 10⁻⁷ fm⁻³
- First r-process peak elements formed at ρ_B = 10⁻⁵ fm⁻³
- Second r-process peak elements formed at ρ_B = 10⁻³ fm⁻³
- Third r-process peak elements formed at ρ_B = 10⁻² fm⁻³
- Coulomb energy reduction for heavy nuclei at ρ_B = 10⁻⁶ ρ_0 (with Y_e = 0.3) is around 10 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the liquid-gas phase transition in supernova matter by Lattimer and collaborators, which focused primarily on equation of state modifications rather than nuclear fragment distributions as initial conditions for the r-process. The authors introduce the "LG process" as a pre-process for r-process nucleosynthesis, and their NSE calculations with Coulomb screening show that heavy elements up to the third r-process peak can be formed statistically at densities around 10⁻² fm⁻³, providing a possible initial condition for subsequent r-process calculations in supernova ejecta.
