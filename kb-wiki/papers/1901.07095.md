# Implementing the three-particle quantization condition including higher partial waves

> We present an implementation of the relativistic three-particle quantization condition including both $s$- and $d$-wave two-particle channels. For this, we develop a systematic expansion about threshold of the three-particle divergence-free K matrix, $\mathcal{K}_{\mathrm{df,3}}$, which is a generalization of the effective range expansion of the two-particle K matrix, $\mathcal{K}_2$. Relativistic invariance plays an important role in this expansion. We find that $d$-wave two-particle channels enter first at quadratic order. We explain how to implement the resulting multichannel quantization condition, and present several examples of its application. We derive the leading dependence of the threshold three-particle state on the two-particle $d$-wave scattering amplitude, and use this to test our implementation. We show how strong two-particle $d$-wave interactions can lead to significant effects on the finite-volume three-particle spectrum, including the possibility of a generalized three-particle Efimov-like bound state. We also explore the application to the $3π^+$ system, which is accessible to lattice QCD simulations, where we study the sensitivity of the spectrum to the components of $\mathcal{K}_{\mathrm{df,3}}$. Finally, we investigate the circumstances under which the quantization condition has unphysical solutions.

## Key claim

The paper presents an implementation of the relativistic three-particle quantization condition that includes both s-wave and d-wave two-particle channels, finding that d-wave channels enter the threshold expansion of the three-particle K matrix at quadratic order, and that strong two-particle d-wave interactions can produce significant effects on the finite-volume three-particle spectrum, including the possibility of a generalized three-particle Efimov-like bound state.

## Method

The authors develop a systematic expansion about threshold of the three-particle divergence-free K matrix, \(\mathcal{K}_{\mathrm{df,3}}\), as a generalization of the effective range expansion of the two-particle K matrix, \(\mathcal{K}_2\). They implement the resulting multichannel quantization condition numerically, using a spherical cutoff on the sum over momenta with a tolerance of \(\epsilon = 10^{-9}\), and exploit cubic symmetries to optimize computation of the \(\widetilde{F}\) matrix. The method is tested by deriving the leading dependence of the threshold three-particle state on the two-particle d-wave scattering amplitude and by applying the formalism to the \(3\pi^+\) system.

## Key numbers

- The d-wave two-particle channels enter the threshold expansion of \(\mathcal{K}_{\mathrm{df,3}}\) at quadratic order.
- The cutoff parameter \(\alpha \approx 0.5\) is found to be small enough that the dependence on \(\alpha\) lies below the required accuracy.
- The tolerance for the remainder integral in the sum cutoff is set to \(\epsilon = 10^{-9}\).
- The explicit result for the \(\ell=2\) integral \(I_2^F\) is given as \(I_2^F = 4\pi\gamma \left[ -\sqrt{\frac{\pi}{\alpha^5}} \frac{3+2\alpha x^2 + 4\alpha^2x^4}{8}e^{\alpha x^2} + \frac{\pi x^5}{2}\text{Erfi}\left(\sqrt{\alpha x^2}\right) \right]\).
- The free pole at the first excited noninteracting energy \(E_1^\fr = m + 2\omega_1\) (with \(\omega_1 = \sqrt{m^2 + k_L^2}\) and \(k_L = 2\pi/L\)) is analyzed for box lengths \(4 \lesssim mL \lesssim 6\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic three-particle quantization condition framework developed by Hansen and Sharpe (2014) and the numerical implementation by Blanton, Romero-Lopez, and Sharpe (2019) to include higher partial waves beyond the isotropic s-wave approximation. The isotropic approximation introduced by Hansen and Sharpe (2015) and used numerically by Blanton, Romero-Lopez, and Sharpe (2019) is shown to be a subset of the low-energy \(A_1^+\) approximation presented here, with the key difference that the isotropic approximation projects onto a single isotropic vector while the current work retains the full matrix structure. The results enable lattice QCD simulations to extract three-particle scattering amplitudes from finite-volume spectra, particularly for systems like \(3\pi^+\) where d-wave interactions may be significant.
