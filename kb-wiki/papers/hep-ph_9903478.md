# Diagrammatic approach to soft non-Abelian dynamics at high temperature

> The dynamics of soft ($|\vec{p}|\sim g^2 T$) non-Abelian gauge fields at finite temperature is non-perturbative. The effective theory for the soft scale is determined by diagrams with external momenta $p_0\lsim g^2 T$, $|\vec{p}|\sim g^2 T$ and loop momenta larger than $g^2 T$. We consider the polarization tensor beyond the hard thermal loop approximation, which accounts for loop momenta of order $T$. There are higher loop diagrams, involving also the scale $gT$, which are as important as the hard thermal loops. These higher loop contributions are characteristic for non-Abelian gauge theories and their calculation is simplified by using the hard thermal loop effective theory. Remarkably, the effective one-loop polarization tensor is found to be gauge fixing independent and transverse at leading order in $g$. The transversality indicates that this approach leads to a gauge invariant effective theory.

## Key claim

The effective one-loop polarization tensor for soft non-Abelian gauge fields at high temperature is gauge-fixing independent and transverse at leading order in the gauge coupling \(g\), indicating that the diagrammatic approach yields a gauge-invariant effective theory for the soft scale \(|\vec{p}|\sim g^2 T\).

## Method

The author computes the polarization tensor for soft external momenta (\(p_0 \lesssim g^2 T\), \(|\vec{p}|\sim g^2 T\)) by integrating out loop momenta larger than \(g^2 T\) using the hard thermal loop (HTL) effective theory. Higher-loop diagrams involving the intermediate scale \(gT\) (self-energy insertions on hard loops and ladder-type diagrams) are evaluated in the imaginary-time formalism; after summing Matsubara frequencies and analytically continuing to real frequencies, the leading-order contributions are extracted by neglecting the external momentum \(P\) except in the imaginary part of \(p_0\).

## Key numbers

* The Debye mass squared is \(\mmdebye = (1/3)(N + N_s/2 + N_f/2)g^2 T^2\) for an SU(\(N\)) gauge theory with \(N_f\) Dirac fermions and \(N_s\) scalars in the fundamental representation.
* The characteristic frequency of soft transverse modes is \(p_0 \sim g^4 T\) (from the HTL-resummed propagator), with a logarithmic correction \(p_0 \sim g^4 T \log(1/g)\) at leading-log order.
* The leading-logarithmic contribution to the polarization tensor is proportional to \(i \mmdebye^2 N g^2 T \log(gT/\mu) \, p_0\) times an angular integral, where \(\mu\) is an infrared cutoff satisfying \(g^2 T \ll \mu \ll gT\).

## Context

This work extends the hard thermal loop approximation of Pisarski (1989) and Frenkel and Taylor (1990) by showing that higher-loop diagrams involving the scale \(gT\) are as important as the hard thermal loops in non-Abelian theories, unlike in Abelian theories where such contributions cancel. The result enables the construction of an effective Langevin equation for soft gauge fields (Bodeker 1998) and provides a diagrammatic foundation for the Boltzmann equation derived by Arnold, Son, and Yaffe (1999) and Blaizot and Iancu (1999).
