# Microscopic study of the isoscalar giant resonances in 208Pb induced by inelastic alpha scattering

> The energetic beam of (spin and isospin zero) $α$-particles remains a very efficient probe for the nuclear isoscalar giant resonances. In the present work, a microscopic folding model study of the isoscalar giant resonances in $^{208}$Pb induced by inelastic \aPb scattering at $E_{\rm lab}=240$ and 386 MeV has been performed using the (complex) CDM3Y6 interaction and nuclear transition densities given by both the collective model and Random Phase Approximation (RPA) approach. The fractions of energy weighted sum rule around the main peaks of the isoscalar monopole, dipole and quadrupole giant resonances were probed in the Distorted Wave Born Approximation analysis of inelastic \aPb scattering using the double-folded form factors given by different choices of the nuclear transition densities. The energy distribution of the $E0, E1$ and $E2$ strengths given by the multipole decomposition {analyses} of the \aap data under study are compared with those predicted by the RPA calculation.

# Microscopic study of the isoscalar giant resonances in 208Pb induced by inelastic alpha scattering

## Key claim

The isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) peak in 208Pb is observed at different energies depending on the experiment (13.96 ± 0.20 MeV at 240 MeV beam energy versus 13.4 ± 0.2 MeV at 386 MeV), and this 500 keV discrepancy corresponds to a difference of about 20 MeV in the deduced nuclear matter incompressibility K∞, which hinders further theoretical modelling of energy functionals despite some consensus on K∞ ≈ 240 ± 20 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed a double-folding model analysis of inelastic α+208Pb scattering at Elab = 240 and 386 MeV using the complex CDM3Y6 effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, with nuclear transition densities from both the collective model (Bohr-Mottelson prescription for L≥2, breathing mode for monopole, and Harakeh-Dieperink compressional model for dipole) and from fully self-consistent Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations using the SLy5 Skyrme interaction. The Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) was used to calculate inelastic scattering cross sections, and the results were compared with the multipole decomposition analysis (MDA) data from the Texas A&M and Osaka groups.

## Key numbers

- ISGMR peak in 208Pb from 240 MeV data: Ex ≈ 13.96 ± 0.20 MeV with width Γ ≈ 2.88 ± 0.20 MeV
- ISGMR peak in 208Pb from 386 MeV data: Ex ≈ 13.4 ± 0.2 MeV with width Γ ≈ 4.0 ± 0.4 MeV
- E0 strength exhausting about 37.6% of the E0 energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) in the 640 keV-wide energy bin centered at Ex = 14.1 MeV from the 240 MeV data
- E0 strength of only about 16% of the E0 EWSR in the 1 MeV energy bin centered at Ex = 13.5 MeV from the 386 MeV data
- Nuclear matter incompressibility K∞ ≈ 240 ± 20 MeV deduced from the ISGMR data
- The CDM3Y6 interaction was parametrized to reproduce K∞ = 252 MeV at saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter
- Best-fit renormalization coefficient NR close to unity and NI ≈ 1.4 for the optical model fit to elastic scattering data

## Context

This work extends the folding model studies of isoscalar giant resonances in 208Pb by using both collective model and microscopic RPA transition densities, building on earlier experimental measurements by Youngblood et al. (2004) at Texas A&M and Uchida et al. (2003, 2004) at Osaka, as well as theoretical work by Harakeh and Dieperink (1980s) on the isoscalar dipole mode and Van Giai and Sagawa on RPA calculations. The study enables a more direct and quantitative comparison between theoretical structure models and experimental inelastic scattering data, and addresses the ongoing discussion about the compatibility of K∞ values deduced from ISGMR and ISGDR compressional modes.
