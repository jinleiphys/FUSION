# Complete electric dipole response and the neutron skin in 208Pb

> A benchmark experiment on 208Pb shows that polarized proton inelastic scattering at very forward angles including 0° is a powerful tool for high-resolution studies of electric dipole (E1) and spin magnetic dipole (M1) modes in nuclei over a broad excitation energy range to test up-to-date nuclear models. The extracted E1 polarizability leads to a neutron skin thickness r_skin = 0.156+0.025-0.021 fm in 208Pb derived within a mean-field model [Phys. Rev. C 81, 051303 (2010)], thereby constraining the symmetry energy and its density dependence, relevant to the description of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The extracted electric dipole polarizability of 208Pb leads to a neutron skin thickness of 0.156+0.025-0.021 fm when interpreted within a specific mean-field model (Phys. Rev. C 81, 051303 (2010)), thereby constraining the symmetry energy and its density dependence; the abstract glosses over the model dependence of this extraction.

## Method

Polarized proton inelastic scattering at 295 MeV was performed on an isotopically enriched 208Pb target using the Grand Raiden spectrometer at RCNP, Osaka, covering an angular range of 0 to 2.5 degrees and excitation energies from 5 to 23 MeV. Separation of electric dipole (E1) and spin magnetic dipole (M1) contributions was achieved through two independent methods: multipole decomposition analysis of angular distributions and measurement of polarization transfer observables (D_SS' and D_LL'). The E1 strength distribution was converted to photoabsorption cross sections and integrated to obtain the dipole polarizability.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness r_skin = 0.156+0.025-0.021 fm in 208Pb, derived from the correlation between polarizability and skin thickness within a mean-field model
- Dipole polarizability alpha_D = 18.9(13) fm^3/e^2 for E1 strength up to 20 MeV excitation energy
- Dipole polarizability alpha_D = 20.1(6) fm^3/e^2 when averaging all available data including excitation energies up to 130 MeV
- Energy resolution Delta E = 25 keV (full width at half maximum) achieved using dispersion matching techniques
- Proton beam energy of 295 MeV with average polarization P_0 = 0.7 and intensities of 2-10 nA
- Target areal density of 5.2 mg/cm^2 for the isotopically enriched 208Pb foil
- Giant dipole resonance peaked at E_x = 13.4 MeV
- Neutron separation energy S_n = 7.37 MeV in 208Pb

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the pygmy dipole resonance in stable nuclei using (gamma,gamma') reactions (Savran et al., 2008) and neutron decay measurements, which were limited to energies below or near the neutron separation threshold. The result for the neutron skin thickness agrees with the most precise previous determinations from exotic atoms (Friedrich et al., 2007, r_skin = 0.18 +/- 0.02 fm) and hadron scattering (Zenihiro et al., 2010, r_skin = 0.211+0.054-0.063 fm), while providing a completely independent method. The precise polarizability measurement enables future systematic studies with a variety of energy density functionals and experimental tests in other nuclei to further constrain the symmetry energy and its density dependence, which are relevant for describing neutron star properties.
