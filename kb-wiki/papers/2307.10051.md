# Inclusion of $KΛ$ electroproduction data in a coupled channel analysis

> Exclusive electroproduction reactions provide an access to the structure of excited baryons. To extract electroproduction multipoles encoding this information, the Jülich-Bonn-Washington (JBW) analysis framework is extended to the analysis of differential cross sections in $KΛ$ electroproduction. This update enlarges the scope of previous coupled-channel analyses of pions and eta mesons, with photoproduction reactions as boundary condition in all analyzed electroproduction reactions. Polarization observables are predicted and compared to recent CLAS data. The comparison shows the relevance of these data to pin down baryon properties.

## Key claim

The Jülich-Bonn-Washington (JBW) coupled-channel analysis framework is extended to include differential cross section data for $K\Lambda$ electroproduction, but the newly available beam-recoil transfer polarization data from CLAS are only predicted and not fitted, serving as a test of how much they will constrain future multipole extractions.

## Method

The electroproduction multipoles for $\pi N$, $\eta N$, and $K\Lambda$ final states are constructed on the basis of the dynamical coupled-channel Jülich-Bonn (JüBo) approach, which provides the boundary condition at the photon point ($Q^2=0$) using the JüBo2017 solution that includes $\pi N$, $\eta N$, and $K\Lambda$ photoproduction. The $Q^2$ dependence is introduced via a separable ansatz with channel-dependent form factors containing polynomial and exponential terms multiplied by a dipole form factor, while longitudinal multipoles are constrained using Siegert's condition at the pseudo-threshold or derived from magnetic multipoles with normalization constants. The partial-wave basis is extended to include S-, P-, D-, and F-waves, and the energy range is enlarged from $W=1.6$ GeV to $W=1.8$ GeV with $Q^2$ up to $8$ GeV$^2$.

## Key numbers

- Total database of 110281 data points fitted, covering final states $\pi^0p$, $\pi^+n$, $\eta p$, and $K^+\Lambda$ in the energy region $W\in[1.13,1.8]$ GeV and $Q^2\in(0,8]$ GeV$^2$
- 533 free parameters in the model, with $N=2$ polynomial order for the form factors
- Best fit (${\bf FIT}_4$) achieves $\chi^2_{\rm wt, dof}=1.06$ with per-datum contributions: $\chi^2_{\rm pp}(\pi^0p)=1.42$, $\chi^2_{\rm pp}(\pi^+n)=1.44$, $\chi^2_{\rm pp}(\eta p)=1.09$, $\chi^2_{\rm pp}(K^+\Lambda)=0.32$
- 2055 $K\Lambda$ differential cross section data points from CLAS (Carman 2012) and 204 $K\Lambda$ data points for $\sigma_T+\epsilon\sigma_L$, $\sigma_{LT}$, $\sigma_{TT}$, and 156 for $\sigma_{LT'}$
- Dipole form factor parameter $b^2=0.71$ GeV$^2$, with Woods-Saxon cutoff parameters $Q_w^2=0.5$ GeV$^2$ and $Q_r^2=10.0$ GeV$^2$

## Context

This work extends the JBW coupled-channel electroproduction framework previously applied to pions (Mai 2021) and eta mesons (Mai 2021) to include $K\Lambda$ final states, building on the JüBo2017 photoproduction solution (Rönchen 2018) that already included $\pi N$, $\eta N$, and $K\Lambda$ channels. The analysis contrasts with single-channel approaches such as MAID (Tiator 2003, Drechsel 2007) and KAON-MAID (Bennhold 1999, Mart 2002), and with the Regge-plus-resonance analyses (Corthals 2007, DeCruz 2012); the coupled-channel treatment ensures that resonance transition form factors are the same for any final state. Future extraction of resonance transition form factors is planned once the analysis stage is complete, and inclusion of $K\Sigma$ electroproduction data is anticipated.
