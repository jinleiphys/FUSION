# Inhomogeneous islands and continents in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model

> We present some recent developments in our study of inhomogeneous chiral symmetry breaking phases in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. First, we investigate different kinds of one- and two-dimensional spatial modulations of the chiral condensate within the inhomogeneous "island" and compare their free energies. Next, we employ the Polyakov-loop extended version of the model to study the effects of varying the number of colors on the inhomogeneous region. Finally, we discuss the properties of an inhomogeneous "continent" which appears in our model at higher chemical potentials, and analyze its origin.

## Key claim

The authors find that within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, an inhomogeneous "continent" phase appears at high chemical potentials and extends to arbitrarily large values, but they argue this may be a regularization artifact rather than a physically robust prediction, as the behavior depends strongly on the regularization scheme and is not present in the quark-meson model.

## Method

The authors perform mean-field calculations in the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, allowing for spatially modulated chiral condensates. They study one-dimensional modulations (chiral density wave, real sinusoidal, and solitonic solutions using Jacobi elliptic functions) and two-dimensional "egg carton" modulations on a square lattice, comparing their free energies. They also extend the model by coupling to a Polyakov loop (PNJL model) to study the effects of varying the number of colors from N_c=3 to N_c=50.

## Key numbers

- The inhomogeneous "island" at T=0 exists roughly between chemical potential μ ≈ 300 MeV and μ ≈ 350 MeV for the model parameters used
- For the two-dimensional egg-carton modulation, the onset occurs around μ ≈ 310 MeV
- The Polyakov-loop extended model uses a pure glue transition temperature T_c = 270 MeV
- The favored wave number Q for the chiral density wave at high chemical potential is approximately Q ≈ 2μ
- The coefficient of the Q^4 term in the vacuum thermodynamic potential expansion is β_vac,4 = -N_f N_c/(81π^2) + O(M^2/Λ^2) in proper-time regularization

## Context

This work extends previous studies of inhomogeneous chiral symmetry breaking in NJL-type models by Sadzikowski (2000), Nickel (2009), and Carignano, Nickel, and Buballa (2010), which found an inhomogeneous "island" covering the first-order chiral phase transition region. The authors compare their results with predictions from large-N_c studies by Kojo (2009, 2010, 2011) and find that increasing N_c in the PNJL model enlarges the inhomogeneous region toward higher temperatures. The analysis of the high-chemical-potential "continent" builds on earlier work by Broniowski (1990) on the large-Q behavior of the vacuum effective potential, and the authors note that this continent does not appear in the quark-meson model, suggesting the need for further theoretical input to determine whether such phases are physical.
