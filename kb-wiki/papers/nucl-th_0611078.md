# Modern compact star observations and the quark matter EoS

> A hybrid equation of state for dense matter is presented that satisfies phenomenological constraints from modern compact star observations which indicate high maximum masses of about 2 M_sun and large radii of R> 12 km. The corresponding isospin symmetric equation of state is consistent with flow data analyses of heavy-ion collisions. The transition from nuclear to two-flavor color superconducting quark matter at n approximately 0.55 fm^{-3} is almost a crossover.

## Key claim

A hybrid equation of state (EoS) for dense matter that includes a transition to two-flavor color superconducting quark matter can simultaneously satisfy modern compact star constraints (maximum masses near 2 solar masses and radii above 12 km) and heavy-ion collision flow data, but only because the transition is almost a crossover with a tiny density jump, making the hybrid star masquerade as an ordinary neutron star.

## Method

The nuclear matter phase was described using the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach, while the quark matter phase was modeled with a color superconducting 3-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model augmented by a selfconsistent vector mean field to stiffen the EoS. The transition density was varied by adjusting the diquark coupling strength, and the resulting hybrid EoS was tested against astrophysical constraints from PSR J0751+1807 and RX J1856-3754, as well as the Danielewicz flow constraint from heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass of about 2 solar masses (M ∼ 2 M⊙) is indicated by modern compact star observations.
- Lower limit on maximum neutron star mass from PSR J0751+1807 is approximately 1.9 M⊙.
- Lower limit on neutron star radius from RX J1856-3754 is R > 12 km.
- Critical transition density from nuclear to two-flavor color superconducting quark matter is n_crit ∼ 0.55 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Klahn (2006) which found that no existing nuclear EoS could satisfy all compact star and heavy-ion flow constraints simultaneously. It provides a counterexample to the expectation that a phase transition to quark matter would soften the EoS and lower the maximum mass, as questioned by Alford (2006). The results enable future work on discriminating nuclear from quark matter interiors via cooling behavior, as proposed by Grigorian (2006) and Blaschke (1999, 2000).
