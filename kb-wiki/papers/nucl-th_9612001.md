# Variation of hadron masses in finite nuclei

> The quark-meson coupling model, based on a mean-field description of non-overlapping nucleon bags bound by the self-consistent exchange of $σ$, $ω$ and $ρ$ mesons, is extended to investigate the change of hadron properties in finite nuclei. Relativistic Hartree equations for spherical nuclei have been derived from a relativistic quark model of the structure of bound nucleons and mesons. Using this unified, self-consistent description of both infinite nuclear matter and finite nuclei, we investigate the properties of some closed-shell nuclei, and study the changes in the hadron masses of the non-strange vector mesons, the hyperons and the nucleon in those nuclei. We find a new, simple scaling relation for the changes of the hadron masses, which can be described in terms of the number of non-strange quarks in the hadron and the value of the scalar mean-field in a nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper extends the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model to finite nuclei and claims a new, simple scaling relation for hadron mass changes in the nuclear medium, where the mass shift of any hadron is proportional to the number of non-strange quarks it contains and the local scalar mean-field value, with the hedge that this relation is accurate over a wide range of nuclear density but derived under the Born-Oppenheimer approximation and the assumption that the nucleon internal structure adjusts to local fields.

## Method

The authors derived relativistic Hartree equations for spherical nuclei from a Lagrangian density (QMC-II) that includes the structure effects of nucleons, vector mesons (ω, ρ), and a parametrized σ-meson mass, all coupled self-consistently to scalar and vector mean fields. Using the MIT bag model for hadron structure, they solved the mean-field equations for infinite nuclear matter and for closed-shell nuclei (e.g., ⁴⁰Ca, ²⁰⁸Pb), computing effective masses of the nucleon, ω, ρ, σ mesons, and hyperons (Λ, Σ, Ξ) as functions of density or position.

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling model previously developed by Guichon, Saito, Rodionov, and Thomas (1995) for nuclear matter and finite nuclei, and by Saito and Thomas (1995) for hadron masses in infinite nuclear matter, to a unified self-consistent description of finite nuclei including meson structure effects. It contrasts with Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) by Walecka (1974), which treats nucleons as point-like and yields a much larger compressibility (540 MeV), and with QCD sum-rule approaches by Hatsuda and Lee (1992) and others, which have intrinsic uncertainties from four-quark condensates. The results enable quantitative predictions for the density-dependent masses of vector mesons and hyperons in finite nuclei, relevant for interpreting dilepton experiments from HELIOS-3 and CERES at CERN.
