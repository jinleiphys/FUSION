# Pairing Properties of Symmetric Nuclear Matter in Relativistic Mean Field Theory

> The properties of pairing correlations in symmetric nuclear matter are studied in the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with the effective interaction PK1. Considering well-known problem that the pairing gap at Fermi surface calculated with RMF effective interactions are three times larger than that with Gogny force, an effective factor in the particle-particle channel is introduced. For the RMF calculation with PK1, an effective factor 0.76 give a maximum pairing gap 3.2 MeV at Fermi momentum 0.9 fm$^{-1}$, which are consistent with the result with Gogny force.

## Key claim

The pairing gap at the Fermi surface calculated with the relativistic mean field (RMF) effective interaction PK1 can be made consistent with results from the Gogny force by introducing an effective factor of 0.76 in the particle-particle channel, yielding a maximum pairing gap of 3.2 MeV at Fermi momentum 0.9 fm$^{-1}$.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) equations for symmetric nuclear matter using the RMF effective interaction PK1, with a one-meson-exchange potential in the particle-particle channel. An effective factor \(f\) was introduced into the gap equation to reduce the pairing potential, and the coupled equations for the gap, scalar density, and vector density were solved self-consistently by iteration with a momentum cutoff of 20 fm$^{-1}$.

## Key numbers

- Maximum pairing gap of 3.2 MeV obtained at Fermi momentum 0.9 fm$^{-1}$ when using effective factor \(f = 0.76\) with the PK1 interaction.
- The pairing gap at the Fermi surface calculated with RMF effective interactions is approximately three times larger than the value calculated with the Gogny force.
- The effective interaction in the particle-particle channel changes from attractive to repulsive at around 1.5 fm$^{-1}$.
- The pairing gap changes sign around 2.5 fm$^{-1}$ and reaches a minimum near 4.0 fm$^{-1}$.
- A cutoff momentum \(K_C \geq 10\) fm$^{-1}$ guarantees numerical convergence; \(K_C = 20\) fm$^{-1}$ was adopted.

## Context

This work extends earlier relativistic studies of superfluidity in nuclear matter by Kucharek and Ring (1991), who found that pairing gaps from RMF calculations were about three times larger than those from the Gogny force. The authors address this known problem by introducing an effective factor in the particle-particle channel, following suggestions that the large gaps arise from the behavior of pairing matrix elements at high momenta, where RMF effective forces are not adjusted. The results enable consistent use of the same meson-exchange interaction in both the particle-hole and particle-particle channels for RMF-based calculations of pairing in nuclear matter and finite nuclei.
