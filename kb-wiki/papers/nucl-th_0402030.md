# Faddeev calculations of break-up reactions with realistic experimental constraints

> We present a method to integrate predictions from a theoretical model of a reaction with three bodies in the final state over the region of phase space covered by a given experiment. The method takes into account the true experimental acceptance, as well as variations of detector efficiency, and eliminates the need for a Monte-Carlo simulation of the detector setup. The method is applicable to kinematically complete experiments. Examples for the use of this method include several polarization observables in dp break-up at 270 MeV. The calculations are carried out in the Faddeev framework with the CD Bonn nucleon-nucleon interaction, with or without the inclusion of an additional three-nucleon force.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method to integrate theoretical predictions for three-body final-state reactions over the actual experimental phase-space acceptance using the list of measured event coordinates, thereby eliminating the need for a Monte-Carlo simulation of the detector setup, provided the experiment is kinematically complete and the polarizations and luminosities for opposite-sign measurements are equal or corrected.

## Method

The authors precalculate the desired observable (e.g., analyzing power) on a four-dimensional grid covering the phase space of the dp break-up reaction, using Faddeev equations with the CD Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, with or without the TM' three-nucleon force. For each experimental event (approximately 3 million events from a 270 MeV dp break-up experiment at IUCF), the theoretical observable is retrieved by multidimensional linear interpolation from the grid. The correctly averaged theoretical value for any bin of an independent variable is then obtained as the arithmetic mean of the theoretical values for all events in that bin, as given by Eq. (6).

## Key numbers

* Deuteron bombarding energy: 270 MeV (equivalent proton laboratory energy: 135 MeV).
* Grid size: 719,280 elements, with azimuth steps of 10 degrees, polar angle steps of 5 degrees, and 30 steps for the relative momentum p.
* Experimental acceptance boundaries: laboratory energies of both outgoing protons above 50 MeV, laboratory polar angles between 10 and 45 degrees.
* Sampling statistics errors for averaged observables: less than 0.003.
* Maximum difference between CD Bonn and CD Bonn + TM' 3NF predictions for the tensor analyzing power A_zz: 0.12 (with cross section above 0.001 fm^2/(MeV sr^2)).
* Maximum difference for the spin correlation coefficient C_xx + C_yy: 0.18 (unrestricted), 0.11 (with cross section cut).
* Maximum difference for the vector analyzing power A_z: 0.05 (unrestricted), 0 (with cross section cut).

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev framework for three-nucleon break-up reactions (Glöckle et al., 1996) by providing a practical method to compare theory with modern experiments that cover large fractions of phase space, such as the IUCF dp break-up experiment at 270 MeV (Whitaker, 2004). The method enables systematic searches for regions of enhanced sensitivity to three-nucleon forces, as demonstrated for the tensor analyzing power A_zz and spin correlation coefficients, and can guide the design of future experiments to test three-nucleon force models.
