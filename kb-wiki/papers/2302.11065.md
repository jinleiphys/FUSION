# Probing the incompressibility of dense hadronic matter near QCD phase transition in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Based on the extended hadronic transport model of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, the incompressibility of dense hadronic matter created in relativistic Au+Au heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3$ GeV is studied. By comparing experimental proton directed flow, productions of strange hadrons $ϕ$, $K^{-}$ as well as their ratio $ϕ/K^{-}$, proton high-order cumulants to the model calculations, a large incompressibility of dense hadronic matter is obtained from nucleon observabels while a rather small incompressibility is needed to fit the data of strange hadrons. This may indicate hadronic matter possesses different incompressibilities in different density regions, i.e., the incompressibility may become stiffer from saturation density to a certain baryon density and then turn to soft before reaching hadron-quark phase transition. The study also shows that the incompressibility significantly affects the critical baryon density of hadron-quark phase transition.

# Probing the incompressibility of dense hadronic matter near QCD phase transition in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The incompressibility of dense hadronic matter created in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 3 GeV appears to be large (k ~ 300 MeV or greater) when inferred from nucleon observables (proton directed flow and cumulants), but rather small (75 < k < 150 MeV) when inferred from strange hadron yields (φ, K⁻), suggesting that hadronic matter may stiffen from saturation density up to some density and then soften before reaching the hadron-quark phase transition.

## Method
The authors used an extended version of the AMPT model in pure hadron cascade mode (AMPT-HC) with density-dependent single-nucleon mean-field potentials parameterized by the incompressibility coefficient κ, and compared simulations to STAR experimental data for proton directed flow v₁, proton cumulant ratio C₄/C₂, and strange hadron yields φ, K⁻ and their ratio φ/K⁻ in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 3 GeV. They also performed simulations with the quark-transport AMPT-SM mode for comparison.

## Key numbers
- Proton directed flow v₁ data from STAR (10-40% centrality) are best fit by an incompressibility coefficient k ~ 300 MeV for the hadronic matter created at √sNN = 3 GeV.
- Proton cumulant ratio C₄/C₂ in 0-5% centrality at 3 GeV requires k > 300 MeV to match the STAR data (experimental value C₄/C₂ = -0.85 ± 0.09 ± 0.82).
- Strange φ and K⁻ yields in 0-10% centrality at 3 GeV are best fit by a soft EoS with incompressibility coefficient 75 < k < 150 MeV.
- The sensitive density region for proton observables (v₁ and cumulants) lies in the 3-4ρ₀ density region before maximum compression.
- The sensitive density region for strange meson observables lies around 3-5ρ₀, somewhat before maximum nuclear compression.
- The critical baryon density for hadron-quark phase transition may lie in the range 3-5 times saturation density, depending on the incompressibility of dense matter near the transition.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Danielewicz et al. (2002) who showed possible changes in compressibility with increasing baryon density, and contrasts with recent results by Nara et al. (2022) who explained proton directed flow with a single hadronic EoS. The study uses data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan-II program (STAR, 2021) and the extended AMPT model with hadronic mean-field potentials (Cas2021). The results imply that different observables probe different density regions of the compressed hadronic matter, and that the incompressibility of dense hadronic matter significantly affects the critical density of the hadron-quark phase transition, which has implications for neutron star structure and gravitational wave studies.
