# Evolution of collisional neutrino flavor instabilities in spherically symmetric supernova models

> We implement a multi-group and discrete-ordinate neutrino transport model in spherical symmetry which allows to simulate collective neutrino oscillations by including realistic collisional rates in a self-consistent way. We utilize this innovative model, based on strategic parameter rescaling, to study a recently proposed collisional flavor instability caused by the asymmetry of emission and absorption rates between $ν_e$ and $\barν_e$ for four different static backgrounds taken from different stages in a core-collapse supernova simulation. Our results confirm that collisional instabilities generally exist around the neutrinosphere during the SN accretion and post-accretion phase, as suggested by [arXiv:2104.11369]. However, the growth and transport of flavor instabilities can only be fully captured by models with global simulations as done in this work. With minimal ingredient to trigger collisional instabilities, we find that the flavor oscillations and transport mainly affect (anti)neutrinos of heavy lepton flavors around their decoupling sphere, which then leave imprints on their energy spectra in the free-streaming regime. For electron (anti)neutrinos, their properties remain nearly intact. We also explore various effects due to the decoherence from neutrino-nucleon scattering, artificially enhanced decoherence from emission and absorption, neutrino vacuum mixing, and inhomogeneous matter profile, and discuss the implication of our work.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that collisional flavor instabilities, caused by the asymmetry of emission and absorption rates between electron neutrinos and electron antineutrinos, generally exist around the neutrinosphere during supernova accretion and post-accretion phases; however, the growth and transport of these instabilities can only be fully captured by global simulations that include advection, as the authors implement here, rather than by local or homogeneous models.

## Method

The authors implement a multi-group, discrete-ordinate neutrino transport model in spherical symmetry that solves the quantum kinetic equations including advection, collisions (emission/absorption and neutrino-nucleon scattering), and coherent flavor evolution. They apply strategic parameter rescaling to attenuate the neutrino self-interaction potential and collisional rates, enabling simulations over a radial range of 10–85 km with 25000 radial grids, 50 angular bins, and 20 energy groups. Using four static background snapshots from a spherically symmetric core-collapse supernova simulation at post-bounce times of 144 ms, 247 ms, 503 ms, and 1000 ms, they evolve neutrino density matrices from equilibrium initial conditions for approximately 1 ms of simulation time.

## Key numbers

- Four static background snapshots are taken at post-bounce times \(t_{\rm pb}=144\) ms (Model I), 247 ms (Model II), 503 ms (Model III), and 1000 ms (Model IV) from an 18 \(M_\odot\) progenitor supernova simulation.
- The radial simulation range is from \(r_{\rm i.b.}=10\) km to \(r_{\rm o.b.}=85\) km, with total radial range \(L=75\) km and \(N_r=25000\) radial grids (high resolution), giving a grid spacing \(\Delta r = 3\) m.
- The neutrino self-interaction potential \(V_{\nu\nu}\) is attenuated using a sigmoid function with parameters \(a_1=10^{-3}\), \(a_2=35\) km, and \(a_3=3.0\) km for Model II, resulting in attenuated \(V_{\nu\nu}\) smaller than \((\Delta r)^{-1}\).
- The vacuum mixing angle is set to \(\theta_V=10^{-6}\) for all models.
- Each simulation takes approximately \(\mathcal{O}(30000)\) CPU hours for a simulation duration of \(t\sim 1\) ms.

## Context

This work extends the collisional flavor instability proposed by Johns (2021), which was based on the assumption of spatial homogeneity, by implementing global advection in spherically symmetric supernova backgrounds for the first time. It builds on recent simulations by Shalgar (2022) and Nagakura (2022) that examined fast and slow flavor oscillations with global advection, but focuses specifically on the collisional instability mechanism. The results enable future studies of how collisional flavor instabilities affect neutrino energy spectra in the free-streaming regime and how they interact with other flavor conversion mechanisms in core-collapse supernovae.
