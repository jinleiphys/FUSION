# The temperature dependence of fractional topological charge objects

> We present a novel method for defining the topological charge contained within distinct topological objects in the nontrivial ground-state fields of SU(N) lattice gauge theory. Such an analysis has been called for by the growing number of models for Yang-Mills topological structure which propose the existence of fractionally charged objects. This investigation is performed for SU(3) at a range of temperatures across the deconfinement phase transition, providing an assessment of how the topological structure evolves with temperature. This reveals a connection between the topological charge and holonomy of the system which must be satisfied by finite-temperature models of Yang-Mills vacuum structure. We find a promising consistency with the instanton-dyon model for SU(N) vacuum structure.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the dominant topological charge of objects in SU(3) lattice gauge theory is determined by the system's holonomy (Polyakov loop at spatial infinity) at all temperatures, and that this is consistent with the instanton-dyon model. The hedge is that the algorithm provides an estimate of topological charge per object, with systematic uncertainties from overlapping distributions and four-dimensional assignment ambiguities.

## Method

The authors apply a novel iterative algorithm to SU(3) lattice gauge configurations: they identify peaks in the topological charge density, then grow objects downhill by assigning neighboring lattice sites with the same sign of charge density and lower absolute density, prioritizing lower-peaked objects first. They filter out lattice dislocations by discarding peaks that fail to capture all points within a surrounding 3^4 hypercube. The algorithm is applied to six pure-gauge ensembles at fixed lattice spacing a ≈ 0.1 fm across temperatures from 0.11 T_c to 1.83 T_c, after smoothing with O(a^2)-improved gradient flow to a flow time τ = 1.45 chosen by requiring consistency between two different O(a^4)-improved topological charge density operators.

## Key numbers

- Below T_c (confined phase), the histogram mode for topological charge lies very close to 1/3 for each ensemble (N_t = 64 at T/T_c = 0.11, N_t = 12 at T/T_c = 0.61, N_t = 8 at T/T_c = 0.91).
- Above T_c, the mode falls from ≈ 0.2 at T/T_c = 1.22 (N_t = 6) to ≈ 0.1 at T/T_c = 1.83 (N_t = 4).
- The flow time used for analysis is τ = 1.45, chosen as the minimum where two different topological charge operators give consistent histogram modes.
- The lattice spacing is fixed at a ≈ 0.1 fm across all ensembles.
- Temperatures studied: 31 MeV (T/T_c = 0.11), 164 MeV (0.61), 247 MeV (0.91), 329 MeV (1.22), 395 MeV (1.46), 493 MeV (1.83).

## Context

This work extends the study of fractional topological charge objects in SU(N) gauge theory, building on the caloron construction of Lee (1998) and Kraan (1998) and the instanton-dyon model of Diakonov (2007, 2010) and Shuryak (2013). It provides the first direct lattice measurement of individual object charges across the deconfinement phase transition, enabling quantitative comparison with the instanton-dyon model predictions for SU(3) where the dyon charges are given by the holonomy parameter ν (DeMartini, 2021). The results support the instanton-dyon model's success in reproducing the first-order SU(3) phase transition and suggest the method can be extended to other N values.
