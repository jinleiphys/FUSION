# Cold and Hot Nuclear Matter Effects on Charmonium Production in p+Pb Collisions at LHC Energy

> We study cold and hot nuclear matter effects on charmonium production in p+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}}=5.02$ TeV in a transport approach. At the forward rapidity, the cold medium effect on all the $c\bar c$ states and the hot medium effect on the excited $c\bar c$ states only can explain well the $J/ψ$ and $ψ'$ yield and transverse momentum distribution measured by the ALICE collaboration, and we predict a significantly larger $ψ'$ $p_\text{T}$ broadening in comparison with $J/ψ$. However, we can not reproduce the $J/ψ$ and $ψ'$ data at the backward rapidity with reasonable cold and hot medium effects.

## Key claim

Cold nuclear matter effects on all charmonium states plus hot nuclear matter effects on only the excited states can explain the ALICE data for J/ψ and ψ' yield and transverse momentum at forward rapidity in p+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, but the same combination of effects cannot simultaneously reproduce the data at backward rapidity.

## Method

The authors use a transport approach with a Boltzmann-type equation for charmonium evolution in the hot medium, coupled to (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics for the fireball expansion. The initial charmonium distribution includes cold nuclear matter effects via EPS09 NLO shadowing and a Gaussian smearing for the Cronin effect with parameter a_gN = 0.15 GeV²/fm extracted from Pb-Pb data at 2.76 TeV. The hot medium dissociation is modeled through gluon dissociation with temperature-dependent cross sections derived from potential model radii and Mott dissociation temperatures.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: √sNN = 5.02 TeV for p+Pb collisions
- Cronin parameter: a_gN = 0.15 GeV²/fm, extracted from Pb-Pb data at √sNN = 2.76 TeV
- Initial hot medium time: τ0 = 0.6 fm
- Maximum initial temperature at fireball center: 180 MeV
- Critical temperature: Tc = 165 MeV at vanishing baryon density
- Mott dissociation temperatures: Td^J/ψ ≈ 1.5 Tc; Td^ψ' ≈ Td^χc ≈ Tc
- For forward rapidity central collisions: cold medium factor R_pA^cold = 0.8, hot medium factor R_pA^hot = 0.5, giving R_pA^ψ' = 0.4 and R_pA^J/ψ = 0.64
- For backward rapidity central collisions: to fit data would require R_pA^cold = 1.63 and R_pA^hot = 0.18, which the authors deem unreasonable
- B decay fraction parameters: for J/ψ, f0 = 0.04 and k = 0.023 c/GeV; for ψ', f0 = 0.114 and k = 0.0217 c/GeV
- p+p charmonium distribution parameters: n = 3.2, dσ_Ψ^pp/dy = 5.01 e^{-0.06418 y²} μb

## Context

This work extends transport model calculations previously applied to Pb-Pb collisions (Chen et al., 2015; Zhuang et al., various) to the smaller p+Pb system at LHC energies. It directly confronts the ALICE measurements of J/ψ and ψ' in p+Pb at 5.02 TeV (ALICE Collaboration, 2014-2016). The failure at backward rapidity contrasts with the comover model of Ferreiro (2015) which can reproduce the data with EPS09 LO shadowing and hadronic comover interactions, suggesting that either the cold nuclear matter parameterization (EPS09 NLO) or the hot medium treatment (gluon dissociation in QGP) may be incomplete at backward rapidity. The predicted strong ψ' pT broadening at forward rapidity provides a testable signal for future experimental confirmation.
