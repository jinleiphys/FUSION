# Criticality in the configuration-mixed interacting boson model : (1) $U(5)-\hat{Q}(χ)\cdot\hat{Q}(χ)$ mixing

> The case of U(5)--$\hat{Q}(χ)\cdot\hat{Q}(χ)$ mixing in the configuration-mixed Interacting Boson Model is studied in its mean-field approximation. Phase diagrams with analytical and numerical solutions are constructed and discussed. Indications for first-order and second-order shape phase transitions can be obtained from binding energies and from critical exponents, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the configuration-mixed Interacting Boson Model with U(5)–Q̂(χ)·Q̂(χ) mixing, first-order shape phase transitions occur when crossing the Maxwell line in the phase diagram, while second-order transitions can be identified from critical exponents; however, the analytical solution for critical points is only exact at (β,γ) = (0, nπ/3), and the full phase diagram requires numerical treatment for general (β,γ).

## Method

The authors used the matrix coherent-state method to derive the energy surface for the configuration-mixed Interacting Boson Model (IBM-CM) with mixing between a regular U(5) (spherical) configuration and an intruder configuration driven by the quadrupole–quadrupole interaction Q̂(χ)·Q̂(χ). They applied catastrophe theory to find degenerate critical points (where the determinant of the stability matrix vanishes) and Maxwell points (where two degenerate global minima occur), solving the criticality conditions analytically at (β,γ) = (0, nπ/3) and numerically for general (β,γ) with γ frozen to nπ/3. Phase diagrams were constructed for the two benchmark cases of U(5)–O(6) mixing (χ = 0) and U(5)–SU(3) mixing (χ = −√7/2) for N = 10 bosons and various values of the intruder excitation energy Δ′.

## Key numbers

* For U(5)–O(6) mixing with N = 10 bosons, Δ′ = 0, ε′ = 150, κ = −0.01 MeV, and χ = 0, the first derivative of the global minimum energy with respect to ω′ shows a discontinuity at the Maxwell line, indicating a first-order shape phase transition.
* For U(5)–SU(3) mixing with N = 10 bosons, Δ′ = 406, ω′ = 250, κ = −0.01 MeV, and χ = −√7/2, the first derivative of the global minimum energy with respect to ε′ shows a discontinuity at the Maxwell line, indicating a first-order shape phase transition.
* The analytical critical line for (β,γ) = (0, nπ/3) is given by ε′_c = −[(N+2)(4N+χ²)/N] × (ζ + √(ζ²+ω′²_c))/(ζ − √(ζ²+ω′²_c)), where ζ = −Δ′ + 5(N+2).
* The triple point for χ = 0 occurs at (ε′_t, ω′_t) = ([4N²(N+2) + ζ(N+1)]/N², ±4N√[(N+2)(4N²(N+2)+ζ(N+1))]/(N+1)).
* For U(5)–O(6) mixing, the triple point disappears when Δ′ = [4N²(N+2)/(N+1)] + 5(N+2).

## Context

This work extends the study of shape phase transitions in the standard Interacting Boson Model (IBM) by Gilmore et al. (1978) and Feng et al. (1979) to the configuration-mixed version (IBM-CM) introduced by Duval and Barrett (1981, 1982). It builds on the matrix coherent-state method for configuration mixing developed by Frank et al. (2004) and the specific study of U(5)–O(6) mixing by Frank et al. (2006). The results enable systematic classification of shape coexistence and phase transitions in nuclei where intruder configurations from particle–hole excitations across shell gaps play a role, and provide a framework for future studies of mixing between two deformed configurations.
