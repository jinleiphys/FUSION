# The proton-proton weak capture in chiral effective field theory

> The astrophysical S-factor for proton-proton weak capture is calculated in chiral effective field theory over the center-of-mass relative-energy range 0--100 keV. The chiral two-nucleon potential derived up to next-to-next-to-next-to leading order is augmented by the full electromagnetic interaction including, beyond Coulomb, two-photon and vacuum-polarization corrections. The low-energy constants (LEC's) entering the weak current operators are fixed so as to reproduce the A=3 binding energies and magnetic moments, and the Gamow-Teller matrix element in tritium beta decay. Contributions from S and P partial waves in the incoming two-proton channel are retained. The S-factor at zero energy is found to be S(0)=(4.030 +/- 0.006) x 10^{-23} MeV fm^2, with a P-wave contribution of 0.020 x 10^{-23} MeV fm^2. The theoretical uncertainty is due to the fitting procedure of the LEC's and to the cutoff dependence.

## Key claim

The astrophysical S-factor for proton-proton weak capture is calculated in chiral effective field theory (χEFT) to be S(0) = (4.030 ± 0.006) × 10⁻²³ MeV fm², with a theoretical uncertainty well below 1% that arises from the fitting procedure of low-energy constants and from cutoff dependence, and with P-wave contributions summing to about 1% of the total value.

## Method

The chiral two-nucleon potential at N3LO (Entem and Machleidt) was augmented by the full electromagnetic interaction including two-photon exchange and vacuum-polarization corrections. The weak current operators were derived up to N3LO, and their low-energy constants were fixed by reproducing A=3 binding energies, magnetic moments, and the Gamow-Teller matrix element in tritium β-decay. The pp scattering wave functions were obtained variationally using a Kohn variational principle with expansions in Laguerre polynomials, and contributions from both the ¹S₀ channel and the P-wave channels (³P₀, ³P₁, ³P₂) were retained.

## Key numbers

- S(0) = (4.030 ± 0.006) × 10⁻²³ MeV fm², the zero-energy astrophysical S-factor for the pp reaction, including both S- and P-wave contributions
- P-wave contribution to S(0): 0.020 × 10⁻²³ MeV fm² (approximately 1% of the total)
- For Λ = 500 MeV cutoff: cumulative S(0) values (in 10⁻²³ MeV fm²) are 4.008(5) for ¹S₀ only, 4.011(5) adding ³P₀, 4.020(5) adding ³P₁, and 4.030(5) adding ³P₂
- For Λ = 600 MeV cutoff: cumulative S(0) values (in 10⁻²³ MeV fm²) are 4.008(5) for ¹S₀ only, 4.010(5) adding ³P₀, 4.019(5) adding ³P₁, and 4.029(5) adding ³P₂
- S′(0)/S(0) = 11.94(1) MeV⁻¹ and S″(0)/S(0) = 248.8(2) MeV⁻² from a cubic polynomial fit including all S+P waves
- The pure Coulomb ¹S₀ S-factor is 4.025 × 10⁻²³ MeV fm² for Λ = 500 MeV

## Context

This work provides the first systematic study of the pp astrophysical S-factor in (pionfull) χEFT, extending previous approaches that used potential models (PM), hybrid χEFT (χEFT*), and pionless EFT (π̸EFT) as reviewed in the SFII compilation (2007). The results agree excellently with the PM approach using the AV18 potential, which gives S(0) = (4.033 ± 0.003) × 10⁻²³ MeV fm² when all S and P waves are included. The calculation enables more reliable predictions for the energy dependence of S(E) up to 100 keV, showing that a cubic polynomial fit accurately describes the results, and that at the solar Gamow peak (∼6 keV) the linear and quadratic contributions are about 7% and 0.5% respectively, while for larger-mass stars (Gamow peak ∼15 keV) these contributions become 18% and 3%.
