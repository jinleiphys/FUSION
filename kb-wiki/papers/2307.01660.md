# How to extract the electromagnetic response of $^6$He in relativistic collisions

> I investigate the difficulties in obtaining the electromagnetic response of light, halo-like, nuclei using reactions at radioactive beam facilities. A relativistic coupled-channels theory for the calculation of dissociation cross sections of halo nuclei is compared to first-order perturbation theory. A comparison with semiclassical models frequently used in experimental analysis is also performed. It is shown that the effects of relativity and of the nuclear interaction lead to sizable effects in the extraction of the electromagnetic response of 6He projectiles.

## Key claim

The extraction of the electromagnetic response of halo nuclei like \(^6\)He from Coulomb dissociation experiments is complicated by sizable effects from relativity and the nuclear interaction, meaning that first-order perturbation theory and semiclassical models are insufficient and a relativistic coupled-channels treatment is necessary for accurate results.

## Method

The author develops a relativistic continuum discretized coupled-channels (RCDCC) formalism based on the Klein-Gordon equation, solving coupled equations for the S-matrix as a function of impact parameter and longitudinal distance. The method incorporates relativistic Coulomb potentials for E1 and E2 excitations, a nuclear interaction derived from a double-folding M3Y potential with a surface-peaked form factor, and absorption at small impact parameters via an eikonal phase constructed from nucleon-nucleon scattering profile functions. The formalism is applied to the dissociation of \(^6\)He projectiles on carbon, tin, and lead targets, using a three-body model for ground-state to continuum transitions and two-body Woods-Saxon continuum waves for continuum-continuum couplings.

## Key numbers

- The rms radius of the alpha core in \(^6\)He is taken as \(\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle_\alpha} = 1.62\) fm, and the rms radius of the valence neutrons is \(\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle_n} = 3.23\) fm, based on densities from Tanihata (1992).
- The M3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction used for the nuclear potential includes a term \(276\left(1-0.005\frac{E}{A_P}\right)\delta(r)\) (in MeV and fm units), where \(E/A_P\) is the bombarding energy per nucleon.
- The diffraction dissociation cross section in the very low binding limit scales as \(d\sigma_d/dE \sim (2\pi R^2/E) J_1^2\left(\sqrt{2\mu E} R/\hbar\right)\), where \(R\) is the sum of projectile and target rms radii and \(\mu = 4m_N/3\) is the reduced mass of the \(\alpha+2n\) system.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic eikonal CDCC method introduced by Bertulani (2005) and compares it to first-order perturbation theory and semiclassical models frequently used in experimental analysis, such as those of Baur (1986) and Bertulani and Baur (1988). The paper addresses the challenge of disentangling Coulomb and nuclear contributions in breakup reactions at few hundred MeV/nucleon, which is critical for extracting the electromagnetic response of halo nuclei like \(^6\)He; the results are relevant to ongoing experimental analyses of \(^6\)He dissociation data (Lehr, in progress) and to prior measurements by Aumann (2000) and others.
