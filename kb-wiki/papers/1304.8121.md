# Confronting measured near and sub-barrier fusion cross-sections for 20O+12C with a microscopic method

> Recently measured fusion cross-sections for the neutron-rich system 20O+12C are compared to dynamic, microscopic calculations using time-dependent density functional theory. The calculations are carried out on a three-dimensional lattice and performed both with and without a constraint on the density. The method has no adjustable parameters, and its only input is the Skyrme effective NN interaction. While the microscopic DC-TDHF calculations lie closer to the experimental data than standard fusion systematics they underpredict the experimental data significantly.

## Key claim

The microscopic DC-TDHF calculations, which have no adjustable parameters and use only the Skyrme SLy4 interaction, lie closer to the experimental data than standard Bass model systematics but still significantly underpredict the measured fusion cross-sections for the neutron-rich system 20O+12C, especially at the lowest measured energy where the predicted cross-section is only 30% of the experimental value.

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations on a 3D Cartesian lattice using the Skyrme SLy4 effective NN interaction, both with and without density constraints (DC-TDHF method). For the DC-TDHF calculations, they extracted heavy-ion interaction potentials from the TDHF time-evolution by constraining the density at each time step and subtracting the binding energies of the individual nuclei, then integrated the Schrodinger equation with a coordinate-dependent mass to obtain fusion cross-sections. Because HF calculations predicted a prolate deformation for 20O (beta2=0.25) while experimental evidence indicates it is spherical, the authors averaged over initial orientation angles of the deformed nucleus and also performed unrestricted TDHF calculations with BCS/Lipkin-Nogami pairing (keeping occupation numbers frozen) to assess the impact of pairing.

## Key numbers

- For 20O+12C at E_c.m. = 9.5 MeV, the maximum impact parameter for fusion was b_max = 4.075 fm, giving a sharp cut-off fusion cross-section of 522 mb.
- The experimental data were measured at E_c.m. = 7.35, 9.29, and 15.24 MeV.
- At the highest energy (E_c.m. = 15.24 MeV), the DC-TDHF predicted cross-section for charged-particle channels was 60% of the experimentally measured value.
- At the lowest energy (E_c.m. = 7.35 MeV), the DC-TDHF predicted cross-section for charged-particle channels was only 30% of the experimental value.
- For the well-studied system 16O+12C, DC-TDHF calculations exceeded measured cross-sections by about 20% at the highest energies (E_c.m. > 7 MeV).
- The unrestricted TDHF calculations with BCS pairing (spherical 20O) showed a reduction in total cross-section of 5-20% compared to the deformed case, with the largest reduction at the lowest energy.

## Context

This work extends the DC-TDHF method, previously applied to about 20 systems with good agreement (Umar and Oberacker, 2006-2012), to the neutron-rich system 20O+12C, motivated by the role of fusion in neutron star crusts proposed by Horowitz (2008). The experimental data from Rudolph et al. (2012) showed enhancement relative to the Bass model (Bass, 1977), and the microscopic calculations confirm a larger cross-section than Bass systematics but still significantly underpredict the data, suggesting that neutron transfer channels (which the DC-TDHF method may not fully capture) play a crucial role. The discrepancy, which persists well above the barrier and shows a slower fall-off with decreasing energy in the data, motivates future measurements of total fusion cross-sections and individual decay channels to distinguish whether the underprediction arises from the fusion cross-section itself or from the statistical model treatment of charged-particle emission.
