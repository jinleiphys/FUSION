# Are the CKM anomalies induced by vector-like quarks? Limits from flavor changing and Standard Model precision tests

> Recent high precision determinations of $V_{us}$ and $V_{ud}$ indicate towards anomalies in the first row of the CKM matrix. Namely, determination of $V_{ud}$ from superallowed beta decays and of $V_{us}$ from kaon decays imply a violation of first row unitarity at about $4σ$ level. Moreover, there is tension between determinations of $V_{us}$ obtained from leptonic $K\mu2$ and semileptonic $K\ell3$ kaon decays. These discrepancies can be explained if there exist extra vector-like quarks at the TeV scale, which have large enough mixings with the lighter quarks. In particular, extra vector-like weak singlets quarks can be thought as a solution to the CKM unitarity problem and an extra vector-like weak doublet can in principle resolve all tensions. The implications of this kind of mixings are examined against the flavour changing phenomena and SM precision tests. We consider separately the effects of an extra down-type isosinglet, up-type isosinglet and an isodoublet containing extra quarks of both up and down type, and determine available parameter spaces for each case. We find that the experimental constraints on flavor changing phenomena become more stringent with larger masses, so that the extra species should have masses no more than few TeV. Moreover, only one type of extra multiplet cannot entirely explain all the discrepancies, and some their combination is required. We show that these scenarios are testable with future experiments. Namely, if extra vector-like quarks are responsible for CKM anomalies, then at least one of them should be found at scale of few TeV, and anomalous weak isospin violating $Z$-boson couplings with light quarks should be detected if the experimental precision on $Z$ hadronic decay rate is improved by a factor of $2$ or so.

## Key claim

The observed approximately 4σ violation of first-row CKM unitarity and the tension between leptonic and semileptonic kaon decay determinations of V_us could be explained by extra vector-like quarks at the TeV scale, but no single type of extra multiplet (down-type isosinglet, up-type isosinglet, or isodoublet) can simultaneously resolve all discrepancies while satisfying experimental constraints; only combinations of multiple species (e.g., two isodoublets or one isodoublet plus one isosinglet) leave any allowed parameter space.

## Method

The authors performed a global fit of the CKM elements V_us and V_ud from superallowed beta decays (determination C), kaon semileptonic decays (determination A), and the ratio from kaon leptonic decays (determination B), using updated lattice QCD results and radiative corrections. They then separately analyzed the phenomenological effects of introducing an extra down-type isosinglet quark b', an up-type isosinglet quark t', and an isodoublet (t', b'), computing constraints from flavor-changing neutral current processes (e.g., K-Kbar mixing, B-Bbar mixing) and electroweak precision observables (e.g., Z boson hadronic decay rate) as functions of the mixing angles and masses of the new quarks.

## Key numbers

- The best fit for V_us and V_ud without imposing unitarity gives |V_us| = 0.22439(36) and |V_ud| = 0.97373(16), which is 3.6σ away from the unitarity curve.
- The deficit of CKM unitarity from the best fit is δ_CKM = 1.48(36) × 10^{-3}.
- The tension between determination A (|V_us| = 0.22326(55) from Kℓ3 decays) and determination B (|V_us| = 0.22535(45) from Kμ2 decays assuming unitarity) is 2.9σ.
- The tension between the democratic average of A and B (|V_us| = 0.22451(50)) and determination C (|V_ud| = 0.97376(16)) is 3.6σ.
- The extra vector-like quarks should have masses no more than a few TeV to satisfy flavor-changing constraints, and the experimental precision on the Z hadronic decay rate must be improved by a factor of about 2 to detect anomalous weak isospin violating Z-boson couplings with light quarks.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Belfatto and Berezhiani, 2019) which first identified the CKM unitarity anomaly and proposed vector-like quarks as a possible explanation, updating the numerical inputs with new lattice QCD results from FLAG 2019 and Bazavov (2020) and new radiative correction calculations from Seng (2020) and Marciano and Sirlin (2020). The paper systematically constrains the parameter space for each type of vector-like quark against flavor-changing and electroweak precision data, finding that no single multiplet works, and shows that these scenarios are testable at future experiments, complementing other recent approaches to the anomaly by Grossman (2019), Crivellin (2020), and others.
