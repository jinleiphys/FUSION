# The role of electron-screening deformations in solar nuclear fusion reactions and the solar neutrino puzzle

> Thermonuclear fusion reaction rates in the solar plasma are enhanced by the presence of the electron cloud that screens fusing nuclei. The present work studies the influence of electron screening deformations on solar reaction rates in the framework of the Debye-Huckel model. These electron-ion cloud deformations, assumed here to be static and axially symmetric, are shown to be able to considerably influence the solar neutrino fluxes of the pp and the CNO chains, with reasonable changes in the macroscopic parameters of the standard solar model (SSM) . Various known deformation sources are discussed but none of them is found strong enough to have a significant impact on the SSM neutrino fluxes.

## Key claim

Electron-screening deformations, modeled as static and axially symmetric quadrupole distortions of the Debye-Huckel cloud, can in principle influence solar neutrino fluxes with reasonable deviations from standard solar model parameters, but none of the known deformation sources (magnetic fields, cloud-cloud interactions, non-Maxwellian distributions) is strong enough to have a significant impact on the SSM neutrino fluxes.

## Method

The author parametrizes the deformed Debye-Huckel radius using a quadrupole deformation parameter beta and spherical harmonics, assuming axial symmetry. The orientation-dependent weak-screening enhancement factor is then computed for the pp reaction at the solar region of maximum energy production (R=0.09 solar radii), and the resulting changes in neutrino fluxes are estimated using proportionality relations that connect screening factors to flux ratios for the pp, hep, Be7, B8, and CNO neutrino channels.

## Key numbers

- For the pp reaction at R=0.09 solar radii, the undeformed weak-screening enhancement factor is f0_WES = 1.049, with Gamow peak E0 = 5.599 keV and classical turning point r_c = 261 fm
- For beta = -0.4 deformation at angle theta = 0, the screening factor becomes f_D = 1.067, accelerating the pp reaction by roughly 1.7 percent
- For beta = -0.8 deformation at theta = 0, f_D = 1.16, yielding at least 23 percent uncertainty for N,O and B8 neutrinos, 9.2 percent for Be7 neutrinos, and less than 2 percent for pp and hep neutrinos
- The central temperature would decrease by 0.2 percent for beta = -0.4 and by 1.2 percent for beta = -0.8; central density would decrease by 0.6 percent and 3.7 percent respectively
- The weak-screening condition restricts the deformation parameter to -0.8 ≤ beta ≤ 0.8 for the pp reaction, based on e^2/(r_D kT) ≈ 0.05
- The intense magnetic field regime threshold is B_IMF = 4.7 × 10^9 G; fields stronger than 10^10 G would be required to affect neutrino fluxes via magnetic catalysis

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of electron screening in solar fusion by Salpeter (1954) and Mitler (1977), and specifically addresses the screening deformation effects that Mitler (1977) and Dzitko et al. (1995) had considered small under SSM conditions. The paper introduces a deformation parameter formalism independent of the source and solar model, enabling future connection of non-standard deformation sources to the parameter beta. It rules out magnetically catalyzed fusion as a solution to the solar neutrino puzzle and shows that sufficiently strong screening deformations could perturb SSM neutrino fluxes with reasonable changes in central temperature and density, warranting further search for deformation sources.
