# Impact of nuclear dependence of R=σ_L/σ_T on antishadowing in nuclear structure functions

> We study the impact of the nuclear dependence of R=σ_L/σ_T on the extraction of the F_2^A/F_2^D and F_1^A/F_1^D structure function ratios from the data on the σ^A/σ^D cross section ratios. Guided by indications of the nuclear dependence of R from the world data, we examine selected sets of EMC, BCDMS, NMC and SLAC data and find that F_1^A/F_1^D < σ^A/σ^D \leq F_2^A/F_2^D. In particular, we observe that the nuclear enhancement (antishadowing) for F_1^A/F_1^D in the interval 0.1 < x < 0.3 becomes significantly reduced or even disappears, which indicates that antishadowing is dominated by the longitudinal structure function F_L. We also argue that precise measurements of nuclear modifications of R and F_L^A have the potential to constrain the poorly known gluon distribution in nuclei over a wide range of x.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that if the ratio of longitudinal to transverse photoabsorption cross sections, \(R=\sigma_L/\sigma_T\), has a small but non-zero nuclear dependence (i.e., \(R^A \neq R^D\)), then the nuclear enhancement (antishadowing) observed in the cross section ratio \(\sigma^A/\sigma^D\) for \(0.1 < x < 0.3\) is significantly reduced or disappears entirely for the transverse structure function ratio \(F_1^A/F_1^D\), indicating that antishadowing is dominated by the longitudinal structure function \(F_L\) rather than by \(F_1\).

## Method

The authors re-express the measured cross section ratio \(\sigma^A/\sigma^D\) in terms of the structure function ratios \(F_2^A/F_2^D\) and \(F_1^A/F_1^D\) using the relation \(R = \sigma_L/\sigma_T\) and the virtual photon polarization parameter \(\epsilon\). They apply two assumptions for the nuclear dependence \(\Delta R = R^A - R^D\) (an absolute value of 0.04 and a relative value of 30% of \(R^N\)), motivated by NMC Sn/C data, to selected BCDMS, EMC, NMC, and SLAC data sets in the antishadowing region. The resulting \(F_1^A/F_1^D\) and \(F_2^A/F_2^D\) ratios are then compared to the original \(\sigma^A/\sigma^D\) ratios.

## Key numbers

*   \(\Delta R = R^{Fe} - R^{D} = 0.144 \pm 0.079 \text{ (stat.)} \pm 0.027 \text{ (syst.)}\) at \(Q^2 = 2.5\) GeV\(^2\) and \(x=0.2\) from SLAC E140.
*   \(\Delta R = R^{Fe} - R^{D} = -0.124 \pm 0.051 \text{ (stat.)} \pm 0.023 \text{ (syst.)}\) at \(Q^2 = 1.5\) GeV\(^2\) and \(x=0.2\) from SLAC E140.
*   \(\Delta R = R^{Fe} - R^{D} = -0.086 \pm 0.057 \text{ (stat.)} \pm 0.022 \text{ (syst.)}\) at \(Q^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\) and \(x=0.2\) from SLAC E140.
*   \(R^{Ca} - R^{C} = 0.027 \pm 0.026 \text{ (stat.)} \pm 0.020 \text{ (syst.)}\) at \(\langle Q^2 \rangle = 4\) GeV\(^2\) from NMC.
*   \(R^{Sn} - R^{C} = 0.040 \pm 0.021 \text{ (stat.)} \pm 0.026 \text{ (syst.)}\) at a mean \(Q^2\) of 10 GeV\(^2\) from NMC.
*   The SLAC E139 final analysis yielded an enhancement of \(\approx 3\%\) on average for the Fe/D cross section ratio in the antishadowing region.
*   A simulated L/T separation with 0.5% point-to-point uncertainties on \(\sigma^A/\sigma^D\) yields a 1-\(\sigma\) uncertainty on \(R^A-R^D\) of less than 0.007.

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear effects in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) first observed by the European Muon Collaboration (EMC) in 1983. It specifically addresses the antishadowing enhancement in the \(0.1 < x < 0.3\) region, which has been measured by EMC, BCDMS, SLAC E139, and NMC. The paper shows that the common assumption of no nuclear dependence of \(R\) (used in global QCD fits such as EPS09, HKN07, and nDS) may be incorrect, and that a non-zero \(\Delta R\) would imply antishadowing is a longitudinal effect. This finding motivates future precise measurements of \(R\) and \(F_L\) on nuclei at Jefferson Lab at 12 GeV and at a future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) to directly constrain the poorly known gluon distribution in nuclei.
