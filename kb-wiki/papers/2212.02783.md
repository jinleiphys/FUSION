# Understanding the nature of $Ω(2012)$ in a coupled-channel approach

> We perform a systematic analysis of the mass and strong decays of the $Ω(2012)$ resonance in a coupled-channel approach of a bare three-quark state and a composite meson-baryon state. Besides the coupling of the bare state and $Ξ^* \bar K$ channel, the effective meson-baryon interactions are also included. We find a pole with mass of $2008$ MeV by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The calculated decay widths and its ratios of branching fractions $\mathcal {R}_{Ξ\bar K}^{Ξπ\bar K}$ agree well with the new experimental measurement by Belle Collaboration. Our results suggest that both three-quark core and $Ξ^* \bar K$ channel are essential for the description of the $Ω(2012)$ resonance. We expect that present calculations can help us to better understand the nature of $Ω(2012)$ resonance.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the $\Omega(2012)$ resonance cannot be described purely as either a conventional three-quark state or a meson-baryon molecule; instead, both a bare three-quark core ($\Omega(1^2P_{3/2^-})$) and the $\Xi^* \bar K$ molecular channel are essential, with the coupled-channel mixing yielding a pole at 2008 MeV that matches experimental data.

## Method
The authors constructed a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation framework that includes a bare three-quark state $\Omega(1^2P_{3/2^-})$ and a composite $\Xi^* \bar K$ channel, with effective meson-baryon interactions derived from chiral Lagrangians via an $\Omega \eta$ loop. They solved for poles in the scattering amplitude and computed two-body ($\Xi \bar K$) and three-body ($\Xi \pi \bar K$) decay widths using the resulting wave function composition and coupling strengths.

## Key numbers
- Pole mass of the physical $\Omega(2012)$ state: 2007.9 MeV (below the $\Xi^* \bar K$ threshold, consistent with experimental mass $2012.5 \pm 0.7 \pm 0.5$ MeV)
- Bare three-quark component probability in the physical state: 28.7%
- Two-body decay width $\Gamma[\Omega(2012) \to \Xi \bar K] = 3.02$ MeV
- Total decay width (with energy-independent $\Xi^*$ width, no cut): $\Gamma[\Omega(2012)] = 6.29$ MeV (experimental: $6.4^{+2.5}_{-2.0} \pm 1.6$ MeV)
- Ratio of branching fractions (energy-independent $\Xi^*$ width, no cut): $\mathcal{R}_{\Xi \bar K}^{\Xi \pi \bar K} = 1.08$ (experimental 2022: $0.97 \pm 0.24 \pm 0.07$)
- Coupling strength $|g_{\Omega(2012)\Xi^* \bar K}| = 1.99$ near the physical pole

## Context
This work extends the coupled-channel approach previously applied to $a_1(1260)$ and $\sigma$ mesons by Nagahiro et al. (2013, 2014) to the baryon sector, specifically the $\Omega(2012)$ resonance discovered by Belle (2018). It addresses the disagreement between conventional quark-model predictions (e.g., Xiao 2018, Liu 2019) and molecular interpretations (e.g., Pavao 2018, Ikeno 2020) by showing that both components contribute significantly. The results enable future studies of other excited $\Omega$ states, such as $\Omega(2250)$, and motivate further experimental measurements to resolve the conflicting Belle results on the three-body branching ratio from 2019 and 2022.
