# New physical processes for extracting GPDs with a better sensitivity to partonic structure

> We introduce a new type of exclusive processes for a better study of generalized parton distributions (GPDs), which we refer to as single-diffractive hard exclusive processes (SDHEPs). We advocate a two-stage framework for picturing SDHEPs based on the separation of scales, which gives a clear description both kinematically and dynamically. We examine the sensitivity of the SDHEP to the parton momentum fraction $x$-dependence of GPDs, and demonstrate it quantitatively with two specific processes that can be readily measured at J-PARC or AMBER using a pion beam and at JLab using a photon beam, respectively. Both processes are capable of providing enhanced sensitivity to the $x$-dependence, overcoming the problem of shadow GPDs, and disentangling different types of GPDs with various spin asymmetries.

## Key claim

The paper introduces single-diffractive hard exclusive processes (SDHEPs) as a new class of exclusive reactions that provide enhanced sensitivity to the parton momentum fraction \(x\)-dependence of generalized parton distributions (GPDs), overcoming the problem of "shadow GPDs" that cannot be distinguished by conventional processes. The claim is hedged by noting that two new processes are still insufficient to map out all eight GPDs, and that more processes and lattice QCD data will be needed.

## Method

The authors propose a two-stage factorization framework for SDHEPs, separating the diffractive transition of the hadron from the hard \(2\to 2\) scattering, with the virtual state \(A^*\) connecting them. They identify that the leading-order scaling phenomenon, where the hard coefficient factorizes as \(\bar{C}(x,\xi;\hat{s},\theta) = \sum_j G_j(x,\xi) T_j(\hat{s},\theta)\), reduces sensitivity to GPDs to a moment integral and diagonal values, allowing shadow GPDs to exist. They then propose two specific processes that break this scaling: diphoton mesoproduction in nucleon-pion scattering (\(N\pi \to N'\gamma\gamma\)) and photon-pion pair photoproduction in nucleon-photon scattering (\(N\gamma \to N'\pi\gamma\)), where a new pole \(x_p(\theta)\) in the GPD integral depends on the scattering angle \(\theta\), entangling the \(x\)-dependence with the observable angular distribution.

## Key numbers

- For diphoton mesoproduction at J-PARC kinematics, the \(q_T\) distributions for the unpolarized cross section can separate different GPD models \((H_0, H_1, H_2, H_3)\) and \((\widetilde{H}_0, \widetilde{H}_1, \widetilde{H}_2)\) by both magnitude and shape.
- For photon-pion photoproduction at JLab Hall-D kinematics, the \(\cos\theta\) distributions for the cross section and polarization asymmetries (circular and linear photon polarization) can separate the same set of GPD models.
- The hard scale is defined by \(q_T \sim q_{1T} \sim q_{2T} \gg \sqrt{-t}\), with the condition \(q_T \gg \sqrt{-t}\) ensuring the factorization holds up to corrections of order \(\mathcal{O}(\sqrt{-t}/q_T)\).
- The new pole in the GPD integral for diphoton mesoproduction is given by \(x_p(\theta) = \xi \left[ (1 - z + \tan^2(\theta/2) \, z) / (1 - z - \tan^2(\theta/2) \, z) \right]\), where \(z\) is a kinematic variable.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for GPD extraction beyond deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and double DVCS, which suffer from the shadow GPD problem identified by Bertone (2021) and Moffat (2023). The proposed SDHEP framework unifies descriptions of GPD-related processes and provides two experimentally accessible reactions: one using a pion beam at J-PARC or AMBER, and one using a photon beam at JLab. The approach enables future global analyses that could incorporate lattice QCD data and additional processes with enhanced \(x\)-sensitivity to fully map out all eight GPDs.
