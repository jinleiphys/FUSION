# An in-medium full-folding model approach to quasielastic (p,n) charge-exchange reactions

> A microscopic description of the quasielastic (p,n) charge-exchange reaction (here, charge-exchange scattering between analogue states) is presented and discussed. Emphasis is focused on the spin-isospin structure of the projectile-target coupling. The model is a coupled-channel extension of the full-folding optical model approach (OMP) developed for nucleon elastic scattering, where emphasis is placed on retaining the genuine off-shell behavior of realistic effective interactions in the nuclear medium. The resulting non-local optical potentials are applied to the calculation of (p,n) differential cross sections, with particular emphasis on small-angle Fermi ($ΔS=0$) cross-sections to isobaric analog states. These parameter-free results provide a reasonable description of the $^{14}$C(p,n)-data at proton energies above $\sim$100 MeV, but deteriorate for heavier targets. These shortcomings are analyzed and possible ways to correct them are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a parameter-free, microscopic coupled-channel extension of the full-folding optical model for (p,n) charge-exchange reactions that retains genuine off-shell behavior of realistic effective interactions in the nuclear medium, but the model provides only a reasonable description of data for light targets like 14C at proton energies above ~100 MeV and deteriorates for heavier targets.

## Method

The authors constructed non-local optical potentials by convolving in-medium nuclear matter g matrices (calculated from six different NN potential models) with target ground-state mixed densities, using a Slater approximation for spin-saturated targets. They then solved coupled-channel equations in momentum space with exact treatment of Coulomb interactions, obtaining scattering amplitudes from the asymptotic behavior of radial scattering waves for the (p,n) charge-exchange reaction to isobaric analog states.

## Key numbers

- For 14C, the excitation energy to the isobaric analog state (IAS) is 2.31 MeV
- For 90Zr, the proton root-mean-square radius is 4.198 fm and the neutron root-mean-square radius is 4.363 fm, with the neutron excess characterized by a_n = 0.472 fm^-1
- For 48Ca, the proton root-mean-square radius is 3.374 fm and the neutron root-mean-square radius is 3.580 fm, with the neutron excess characterized by a_n = 0.524 fm^-1
- For 14C, the proton root-mean-square radius is 2.427 fm and the neutron root-mean-square radius is 2.611 fm, with the neutron excess characterized by a_n = 0.510 fm^-1
- The g matrix was evaluated at eight different densities and nearly twenty momentum pairs, for all allowed NN states up to J=7, in a square mesh of relative momenta up to 15 fm^-1

## Context

This work extends the full-folding optical model approach developed for nucleon elastic scattering (Arellano 2002, Arellano 1989, Arellano 1995) to charge-exchange reactions, addressing the lack of microscopic descriptions that had previously required phenomenological adjustments of the nucleon-nucleus coupling (Cheon 1992, Clark 1984). The approach enables parameter-free calculations of (p,n) differential cross sections to isobaric analog states, though the authors note that the results deteriorate for heavier targets and discuss possible corrections.
