# Transverse energy density fluctuations in the Color Glass Condensate Model

> We calculate the transverse correlation of fluctuations of the deposited energy density in nuclear collisions in the framework of the Gaussian color glass condensate model.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the transverse correlation function of energy density fluctuations in nuclear collisions using the Gaussian color glass condensate model, finding that these fluctuations are locally of similar magnitude as the energy density itself and fall over distances of the inverse saturation scale, but only when the "triumvirate" running coupling is used to remove an infrared divergence.

## Method

The authors start from the Gaussian random source approximation of the color glass condensate model, where the probability distribution of color charge density is Gaussian with saturation scale Q_s = gμ. They compute the two-point correlator of the deposited energy density by factorizing four-gauge-field correlators into products of two-field correlators, using the field correlator derived by Lappi (2006). The resulting integrals are evaluated analytically in terms of Bessel functions and numerically for parameters motivated by RHIC and LHC initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- Saturation scale: Q_s^2 = 2 GeV^2
- Running coupling values: g^2(μ^2) = 3.785
- Inverse saturation scale: Q_s^{-1} ≈ 0.14 fm
- Initial deposited energy density: ε_0 ≈ 240 GeV/fm^3

## Context

This work extends the study of event-by-event fluctuations in heavy ion collisions beyond the Monte-Carlo Glauber model of Drescher (2006), which only accounts for geometric fluctuations of nucleon positions. The authors calculate the previously unquantified contribution from color field strength fluctuations, which are governed by the saturation scale Q_s rather than the nucleon radius. The results can serve as input for calculations of early fluctuations during thermalization, including studies within the AdS/CFT paradigm using methods similar to those employed in string theory approaches.
