# Vector Meson Production in Ultraperipheral Heavy Ion Collisions

> The ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions (UPC's) are an important alternative to study the QCD dynamics until the next generation of $e^+e^-/ ep / eA $ colliders become reality. Due to the coherent action of all the protons in the nucleus, the electromagnetic field is very strong and the resulting flux of equivalent photons is large, which allows to study two-photon as well as photonuclear interactions at high energies. In this paper we present a brief review of the vector meson production in UPC's at high energies using the QCD color dipole approach to describe their photonuclear production and the perturbative QCD Pomeron (BFKL dynamics) to describe the double meson production in photon-photon process. Predictions for rates and integrated cross sections are presented for energies of RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The paper argues that ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions (UPCs) can serve as an alternative probe of QCD dynamics at high energies until next-generation lepton-hadron colliders become available, but the ability to discriminate between different QCD approaches (saturation versus BFKL dynamics) depends on the magnitude of next-to-leading-order corrections, which may suppress the energy enhancement expected from leading-order BFKL evolution.

## Method

The authors compute vector meson photoproduction in UPCs using the QCD color dipole approach with a Glauber-Gribov formalism for the dipole-nucleus cross section, employing the Golec-Biernat–Wusthoff saturation model for the dipole-proton cross section. For double vector meson production in photon-photon processes, they use the perturbative QCD Pomeron (BFKL dynamics) at leading and next-to-leading order accuracy, with the hard scale provided by the heavy meson mass. The photon flux is calculated using the Weizsacker-Williams method with an impact parameter cutoff at twice the nuclear radius.

## Key numbers

* For coherent rho production in gold-gold UPCs at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN)=130 GeV), the dipole approach predicts sigma(-3 ≤ y ≤ 3) = 410 mb, compared to the STAR measurement of 370 ± 170 ± 80 mb.
* For the same system with rapidity cut |y| ≤ 1, the prediction is sigma(-1 ≤ y ≤ 1) = 221 mb, versus the STAR result of 140 ± 60 ± 30 mb.
* The integrated cross section for J/Psi photoproduction in PbPb at LHC is 41.5 mb; for rho production in PbPb at LHC it is 10069 mb.
* For double J/Psi production in PbPb at LHC (sqrt(s)=5500 GeV), the ratio between the LLA BFKL prediction and the Born two-gluon cross section is approximately 40; including NLO corrections reduces this ratio to about 1.7.
* The maximum gamma-gamma collision energy is about 6 GeV at RHIC and 200 GeV at LHC; the maximum gamma-nucleon energy reaches 950 GeV at LHC.

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of vector meson production in UPCs by Goncalves and Machado (2004) and compares with the GVDM plus Glauber-Gribov approach of Strikman (2000) and the VDM plus classical mechanical calculation of Klein and Nystrand (1999). The predictions for coherent rho production at RHIC are tested against the first STAR data (2002), showing reasonable agreement. The double J/Psi analysis builds on the proposal by Kwiecinski (1998) to use this process as a probe of the hard QCD Pomeron, and the results suggest that only accurate measurements at LHC could discriminate between BFKL dynamics and the Born approximation once NLO corrections are included.
