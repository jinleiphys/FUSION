# Electromagnetic currents of the pion-nucleon system

> Pion photoproduction amplitudes are calculated from a set of equations that have been derived by coupling an external photon to all places in a dressed pion-nucleon vertex. The calculation is consistent with gauge invariance, charge conservation, unitarity and covariance. To provide input to the photoproduction amplitude, a photon-nucleon vertex is calculated from a set of equations derived by complete attachment of photons to a dressed nucleon propagator. We check the accuracy of this vertex by extracting its nucleon electromagnetic form factors.

## Key claim

Pion photoproduction amplitudes are calculated using a "gauging of equations" method that attaches an external photon to all possible places in a dressed pion-nucleon vertex, achieving gauge invariance in a theoretically correct manner for the first time; however, the calculation relies on a covariant model with cutoff form factors and the dressed photon-nucleon vertex is constructed by complete attachment of photons to a dressed nucleon propagator rather than using the general analytical form prescribed by Ball and Chiu (1980).

## Method

The authors apply the gauging of equations method to a Bethe-Salpeter equation for the four-point Green function describing pion-nucleon interactions, attaching photons to all places in the integral equation to generate a gauge-invariant, non-perturbative photoproduction amplitude. The dressed photon-nucleon vertex is obtained by complete attachment of photons to a dressed nucleon propagator, and the accuracy of this vertex is checked by extracting nucleon electromagnetic form factors. The calculations are carried out using the covariant spectator approach to evaluate loop integrals.

## Key numbers

* Nucleon electromagnetic form factors are extracted from the dressed photon-nucleon vertex to check its accuracy (no specific numerical values for the form factors are given in the provided text).
* The paper states that the photoproduction amplitude obeys Watson's theorem and thus has unitarity, but no numerical results for cross sections or multipole amplitudes are presented in the truncated text.

## Context

This work extends the gauging of equations method developed by Kvinikhidze, Blankleider, and collaborators (1997) and Haberzettl (1997) to construct gauge-invariant amplitudes for the pion-nucleon system. It differs from prior approaches by Nozawa (1989), Lee (1991), Surya (1995), Pascalutsa (2004), Matsuyama (2006), and Julia-Diaz (2007), which achieve gauge invariance to varying degrees but do not attach photons to all possible places in the underlying diagrams. The calculation enables a theoretically consistent input for more complicated reactions requiring a gamma N to pi N amplitude, and is relevant to current approaches used to study nucleon resonances as described by Sato (2009).
