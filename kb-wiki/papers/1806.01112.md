# Halo structure of $^{17}$C

> $^{17}$C has three states below the $^{16}$C + $n$ threshold with quantum numbers $J^P=3/2^+,1/2^+,5/2^+$. These states have relatively small neutron separation energies compared to the neutron separation and excitation energies of $^{16}$C. This separation of scales motivates our investigation of $^{17}$C in a Halo effective field theory (Halo EFT) with a $^{16}$C core and a valence neutron as degrees of freedom. We discuss various properties of the three states such as electric radii, magnetic moments, electromagnetic transition rates and capture cross sections. In particular, we give predictions for the charge radius and the magnetic moment of the $1/2^+$ state and for neutron capture on $^{16}$C into this state. Furthermore, we discuss the predictive power of the Halo EFT approach for the $3/2^+$ and $5/2^+$ states which are described by a neutron in a $D$-wave relative to the core.

## Key claim

The paper argues that $^{17}$C can be described using Halo effective field theory (Halo EFT) with a $^{16}$C core and a valence neutron, but the expansion parameter for ground state observables is relatively large at approximately 0.64, leading to slow convergence, while for the excited states the expansion parameter is about 0.5, giving 50% errors at first order and 25% at second order.

## Method

The authors construct a Halo EFT Lagrangian with $S$-wave and $D$-wave neutron-core interactions, using dimensional regularization with the power divergence subtraction (PDS) scheme. They calculate static electromagnetic properties (charge radii, magnetic moments) and electromagnetic transition rates (E2 and M1) between the three bound states of $^{17}$C at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) where applicable, fitting low-energy constants to binding energies and effective range parameters.

## Key numbers

- Neutron separation energy of $^{17}$C ground state: $S_n(^{17}{\rm C}) = 0.734(18)$ MeV
- Excitation energies of $^{17}$C states: $E^*_{1/2^+} = 0.218(1)$ MeV and $E^*_{5/2^+} = 0.332(1)$ MeV
- First excitation energy of $^{16}$C core: $E_{{}^{16}C}^* = 1.766(10)$ MeV
- Neutron separation energy of $^{16}$C: $S_n(^{16}{\rm C}) = 4.250(4)$ MeV
- Predicted charge radius of $^{17}$C $1/2^+$ state: $\sqrt{\braket{r_E^2}^{1/2^{+}}_{^{17}C}} = 2.53(5)$ fm
- Charge radius difference between $^{17}$C $1/2^+$ state and $^{16}$C at LO: $0.074$ fm$^2$
- Experimental B(M1: $1/2^+ \to 3/2^+$) = $1.04^{+0.03}_{-0.12} \times 10^{-2} \mu_N^2$
- Experimental B(M1: $5/2^+ \to 3/2^+$) = $7.12_{-0.96}^{+1.27} \times 10^{-2} \mu_N^2$
- Rough prediction for B(E2: $1/2^+ \to 3/2^+$) $\approx 3 \times 10^{-2} e^2$ fm$^4$
- Ratio of transition rates: $T(E2)/T(M1) \approx 1\times 10^{-5}$ for the $1/2^+ \to 3/2^+$ transition

## Context

This work extends Halo EFT, previously applied to $^{11}$Be by Hammer et al. (2011) and Fernando et al. (2015), to the heavier halo candidate $^{17}$C, which has not been investigated using this approach before. The paper notes that $^{17}$C is too heavy for no-core shell model combined with resonating group model calculations (Navratil 2016) and too light for coupled cluster methods that neglect center-of-mass motion (Hagen 2012), while NCSM calculations (Smalley 2015) have not achieved convergence for its spectrum. The results provide predictions for future experiments and ab initio calculations of charge radii, magnetic moments, and neutron capture on $^{16}$C into $^{17}$C.
