# Influence of the neutron-skin effect on nuclear isobar collisions at RHIC

> The unambiguous observation of a Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME)-driven charge separation is the core aim of the isobar program at RHIC consisting of ${^{96}_{40}}$Zr+${^{96}_{40}}$Zr and ${^{96}_{44}}$Ru+${^{96}_{44}}$Ru collisions at $\sqrt {s_{\rm NN}}\!=\!200$ GeV. We quantify the role of the spatial distributions of the nucleons in the isobars on both eccentricity and magnetic field strength within a relativistic hadronic transport approach (SMASH, Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons). In particular, we introduce isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleon spatial correlations in the geometric description of both nuclei, deformation for ${^{96}_{44}}$Ru and the so-called neutron skin effect for the neutron-rich isobar i.e. ${^{96}_{40}}$Zr. The main result of this study is a reduction of the magnetic field strength difference between ${^{96}_{44}}$Ru+${^{96}_{44}}$Ru and ${^{96}_{40}}$Zr+${^{96}_{40}}$Zr by a factor of 2, from $10\%$ to $5\%$ in peripheral collisions when the neutron-skin effect is included. Further, we find an increase of eccentricity by up to 10$\%$ when deformation is taken into account while neither the neutron skin effect nor the nucleon-nucleon correlations result into a significant modification of this observable with respect to the traditional Woods-Saxon modeling. Our results suggest a significantly smaller CME signal to background ratio for the experimental charge separation measurement in peripheral collisions with the isobar systems than previously expected.

## Key claim
The inclusion of the experimentally measured neutron-skin effect in the neutron-rich isobar ⁹⁶₄₀Zr reduces the magnetic field strength difference between ⁹⁶₄₄Ru+⁹⁶₄₄Ru and ⁹⁶₄₀Zr+⁹⁶₄₀Zr collisions by a factor of 2 (from 10% to 5% in peripheral collisions), thereby significantly diminishing the expected chiral magnetic effect signal-to-background ratio compared to previous estimates.

## Method
The authors used the relativistic hadronic transport approach SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons) to simulate ⁹⁶₄₀Zr+⁹⁶₄₀Zr and ⁹⁶₄₄Ru+⁹⁶₄₄Ru collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV. They introduced isospin-dependent Woods-Saxon distributions for the neutron-rich ⁹⁶₄₀Zr, implementing the neutron-skin effect with Δrnp = 0.15 fm (the upper experimental limit), and included deformation (β₂ = 0.158) for ⁹⁶₄₄Ru along with nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations via a Metropolis Monte Carlo generator. The participant eccentricity was computed from the spatial distribution of participants, and the magnetic field strength was calculated using Lienard-Wiechert potentials at the time of maximal field.

## Key numbers
- The magnetic field strength difference between ⁹⁶₄₄Ru+⁹⁶₄₄Ru and ⁹⁶₄₀Zr+⁹⁶₄₀Zr is reduced from 10% to 5% in peripheral collisions (impact parameter 8-12 fm) when the neutron-skin effect is included.
- The eccentricity ratio between the two isobar systems increases by up to 10% in ultra-central collisions due to the deformation of ⁹⁶₄₄Ru.
- The neutron-skin thickness for ⁹⁶₄₀Zr is Δrnp = 0.12 ± 0.03 fm from experimental data, with the study using the upper limit of 0.15 fm.
- The Woods-Saxon parameters for ⁹⁶₄₀Zr are: R₀ = 5.02 fm, d = 0.46 fm, β₂ = 0; for ⁹⁶₄₄Ru: R₀ = 5.085 fm, d = 0.46 fm, β₂ = 0.158.
- For the neutron-skin implementation in ⁹⁶₄₀Zr: proton R₀ = 5.08 fm, d = 0.34 fm; neutron R₀ = 5.08 fm, d = 0.46 fm.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the isobar program at RHIC, which aimed to observe the chiral magnetic effect by comparing ⁹⁶₄₀Zr+⁹⁶₄₀Zr and ⁹⁶₄₄Ru+⁹⁶₄₄Ru collisions. Prior calculations using a multiphase transport model (Deng 2018, Zhao 2019) predicted a 10% larger magnetic field for ⁹⁶₄₄Ru+⁹⁶₄₄Ru, while hydrodynamic studies (Schenke 2019) found up to 10% differences in elliptic flow due to deformation. The present study introduces the experimentally measured neutron-skin effect from antiproton measurements at CERN (Trzcinska 2001, 2004), showing that this nuclear structure detail significantly reduces the magnetic field difference between the isobars, making the CME signal extraction more challenging than previously expected.
