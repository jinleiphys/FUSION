# Fluctuations of conserved charges, chiral spin symmetry and deconfinement in an SU(2)_color subgroup of SU(3)_color above T_c

> Above a pseudocritical temperature of chiral symmetry restoration T_c the energy and the pressure are very far from the quark-gluon-plasma limit (i.e. ideal gas of free quarks and gluons). At the same time very soon above T_c fluctuations of conserved charges behave as if quarks were free particles. Within the T_c - 3T_c interval a chiral spin symmetry emerges in QCD which is not consistent with free quarks and suggests that degrees of freedom are chirally symmetric quarks bound into the color-singlet objects by the chromoelectric field. Here we analyse temporal and spatial correlators in this interval and demonstrate that they indicate simultaneously the chiral spin symmetry as well as absence of the interquark interactions in channels constrained by a current conservation. The latter channels are responsible for both fluctuations of conserved charges and for dileptons. Assuming that a SU(2)_color subgroup of SU(3)_color is deconfined soon above T_c but confinement persits in SU(3)_color/SU(2)_color in the interval T_c - 3T_c we are able to reconcile all empirical facts listed above.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the apparent contradiction between chiral spin symmetry (indicating confinement) and free-quark-like fluctuations of conserved charges (indicating deconfinement) in the temperature interval \(T_c\) to \(3T_c\) can be resolved if only an \(SU(2)_{color}\) subgroup of \(SU(3)_{color}\) is deconfined, while confinement persists in the remaining \(SU(3)_{color}/SU(2)_{color}\) sector, provided a color-isospin locking mechanism is assumed.

## Method

The author reanalyzes existing lattice QCD results for temporal and spatial correlators of fermion bilinears in \(N_F=2\) QCD with physical quark masses, using a chirally symmetric Domain Wall Dirac operator. By comparing the multiplet patterns of these correlators with those from free (noninteracting) quarks on the same lattice, the author identifies which operators show chiral spin \(SU(2)_{CS}\) and \(SU(4)\) symmetry and which operators show free-quark-like behavior. The analysis focuses on the distinction between operators constrained by a conserved current (the vector current \(V_t\) and the axial vector current \(A_t\)) and those that are not.

## Key numbers

* The analysis covers the temperature interval \(T_c - 3T_c\), with specific lattice results shown at \(T = 220\) MeV (\(1.2 T_c\)) for temporal correlators and at \(T = 380\) MeV (\(\sim 2.2 T_c\)) for spatial correlators.
* The pseudocritical temperature of chiral symmetry restoration is taken as \(T_c \sim 155\) MeV.
* The breaking of the \(SU(2)_{CS}\) symmetry in the temporal correlators at \(T = 220\) MeV is estimated at the level of less than \(5\%\).
* The spatial correlator of the conserved charge density operator \(V_t\) at \(T = 380\) MeV is identical to its free quark counterpart.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous lattice findings on chiral spin symmetry (Glozman, 2015; 2018; 2019) and addresses the puzzle that fluctuations of conserved charges (Asakawa, 2000; Koch, 2005; Ejiri, 2006; Bazavov, 2012) appear free-quark-like soon above \(T_c\), while the energy density and pressure (Bazavov, 2009; Borsanyi, 2010) are far from the Stefan-Boltzmann limit. The proposed partial deconfinement in an \(SU(2)_{color}\) subgroup builds on earlier ideas of partial deconfinement (Hanada, 2014; 2018; 2019) and uses a color-isospin locking concept analogous to color-flavor locking at high baryon density (Alford, 1998; 1999). The scenario implies that the dual superconductor picture of confinement (t Hooft, 1981; Mandelstam, 1976) is insufficient for QCD.
