# Hadronization Scheme Dependence of Long-Range Azimuthal Harmonics in High Energy p+A Reactions

> We compare the distortion effects of three popular final-state hadronization schemes. We show how hadronization modifies the initial-state gluon correlations in high energy p+A collisions. The three models considered are (1) LPH: local parton-hadron duality, (2) CPR: collinear parton- hadron resonance independent fragmentation, and (3) LUND: color string hadronization. The strong initial-state azimuthal asymmetries are generated using the GLVB model for non-abelian gluon bremsstrahlung, assuming a saturation scale Qsat = 2 GeV. Long-range elliptic and triangular harmonics for the final hadron pairs are compared based on the three hadronization schemes. Our analysis shows that the process of hadronization causes major distortions of the partonic azimuthal harmonics for transverse momenta at least up to pT = 3GeV. In particular, they appear to be greatly reduced for pT < 1÷2GeV.

## Key claim

The paper shows that hadronization causes major distortions of partonic azimuthal harmonics for transverse momenta at least up to pT = 3 GeV, with the harmonics being greatly reduced for pT below 1-2 GeV, and that this damping is an intrinsic property of hadronization rather than a consequence of the particular initial-state model chosen.

## Method

The authors used the JETSET 7.4 Monte Carlo hadronization algorithm with 30 million simulated GLVB events containing two recoiling beam jets, each represented by a high invariant mass q-qbar pair along a beam axis. They generated bremsstrahlung gluon transverse momenta and azimuthal angles from the regularized Gunion-Bertsch distribution with a saturation scale Qsat = 2 GeV, then compared the long-range elliptic and triangular harmonics for final pion pairs using three hadronization schemes: local parton-hadron duality (LPH), collinear parton-hadron resonance independent fragmentation (CPR), and LUND color string hadronization.

## Key numbers

* Saturation scale Qsat = 2 GeV used as input parameter controlling the pT range of azimuthal asymmetry
* Average number of bremsstrahlung gluons per event: ⟨Ng⟩ = 8
* Infrared regulating scales: μ = 300 MeV and ΛQCD = 200 MeV
* For pT = 0.25 GeV, v2π/v2g = 0.04 (independent fragmentation) and 0.06 (LUND model)
* For pT = 0.75 GeV, v2π/v2g = 0.10 (independent fragmentation) and 0.30 (LUND model)
* For pT = 1.25 GeV, v2π/v2g = 0.36 (independent fragmentation) and 0.65 (LUND model)
* For pT = 1.75 GeV, v2π/v2g = 0.60 (independent fragmentation) and 1.00 (LUND model)
* Average ratio ⟨pTπ⟩/⟨pTg⟩ = 0.54 for independent fragmentation and 0.58 for LUND model
* Average ratio ⟨pzπ⟩/⟨pzg⟩ = 2.08 for independent fragmentation and 2.51 for LUND model
* Hadronization scale λ ≈ 1 GeV identified as the discriminant between uniform and anisotropic regions

## Context

This work extends the GLVB model of Gyulassy (2015) for non-abelian gluon bremsstrahlung azimuthal correlations and addresses the need for systematic uncertainty estimates in CGC/Glasma phenomenology, which has been limited to the idealized local parton-hadron duality scheme of Azimov (1984). The results support the analysis of Skokov et al. indicating that CGC/Glasma predictions without detailed hadronization modelling should be limited to pT > 3 GeV. The study enables future investigations of hadronization effects on different particle flavors and mass dependence of final-state correlations.
