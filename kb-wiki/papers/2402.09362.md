# Long-Distance Nuclear Matrix Elements for Neutrinoless Double-Beta Decay from Lattice QCD

> Neutrinoless double-beta ($0νββ$) decay is a heretofore unobserved process which, if observed, would imply that neutrinos are Majorana particles. Interpretations of the stringent experimental constraints on $0νββ$-decay half-lives require calculations of nuclear matrix elements. This work presents the first lattice quantum-chromodynamics (LQCD) calculation of the matrix element for $0νββ$ decay in a multi-nucleon system, specifically the $nn \rightarrow pp ee$ transition, mediated by a light left-handed Majorana neutrino propagating over nuclear-scale distances. This calculation is performed with quark masses corresponding to a pion mass of $m_π= 806$ MeV at a single lattice spacing and volume. The statistically cleaner $Σ^- \rightarrow Σ^+ ee$ transition is also computed in order to investigate various systematic uncertainties. The prospects for matching the results of LQCD calculations onto a nuclear effective field theory to determine a leading-order low-energy constant relevant for $0νββ$ decay with a light Majorana neutrino are investigated. This work, therefore, sets the stage for future calculations at physical values of the quark masses that, combined with effective field theory and nuclear many-body studies, will provide controlled theoretical inputs to experimental searches of $0νββ$ decay.

## Key claim

This work presents the first lattice QCD calculation of the neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix element in a multi-nucleon system, specifically the $nn \rightarrow pp ee$ transition, but only at unphysical quark masses corresponding to a pion mass of 806 MeV and at a single lattice spacing and volume, meaning the results are not directly applicable to experimental searches without future extrapolations and matching to effective field theory.

## Method

The authors computed Euclidean four-point correlation functions for the $nn \rightarrow pp ee$ and $\Sigma^- \rightarrow \Sigma^+ ee$ transitions on a single ensemble of 12,136 gauge-field configurations with lattice spacing 0.145 fm and volume $32^3 \times 48$, using quark masses at the SU(3) flavor-symmetric point where the pion mass is 806 MeV. The neutrino propagator was implemented as a sum over non-zero finite-volume momenta with the zero mode subtracted to avoid exponentially growing correlation functions, and extended propagators were constructed from wall sources and point sinks with a sparse grid of $4^3$ sink points. The ratio of four-point to two-point correlation functions was formed to extract the transition amplitude, and the matching of these LQCD results to a pionless nuclear effective field theory was explored to determine a leading-order low-energy constant.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass: $m_\pi = 806$ MeV, corresponding to the unphysical quark masses used in the calculation
* Lattice spacing: $a = 0.145$ fm
* Lattice volume: $(L/a)^3 \times (T/a) = 32^3 \times 48$
* Number of gauge-field configurations: 12,136
* Common baryon mass (proton, neutron, $\Sigma^0$, $\Sigma^\pm$, $\Lambda$): 1.64 GeV
* Sparse grid of sink points: $4^3$ points, corresponding to a sparsening factor of $(L/a)/4 = 8$ in each direction
* Minimum neutrino energy from zero-mode removal: $|\bm{q}| = 2\pi/L$

## Context

This work extends previous LQCD calculations of the $\pi^- \rightarrow \pi^+ ee$ transition (Xu and Feng; Detmold and Murphy) to baryonic systems, specifically the $nn \rightarrow pp ee$ and $\Sigma^- \rightarrow \Sigma^+ ee$ transitions. The results are not yet at physical quark masses but set the stage for future calculations that, combined with nuclear effective field theory matching as outlined by Cirigliano and collaborators (2020) and many-body methods, will provide controlled theoretical inputs to experimental searches for neutrinoless double-beta decay in nuclei such as $^{136}$Xe studied by KamLAND-Zen.
