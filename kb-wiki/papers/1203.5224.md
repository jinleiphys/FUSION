# Neutral-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections based on relativistic nuclear energy density functional

> Background: Inelastic neutrino-nucleus scattering through the weak neutral-current plays important role in stellar environment where transport of neutrinos determine the rate of cooling. Since there are no direct experimental data on neutral-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections available, only the modeling of these reactions provides the relevant input for supernova simulations. Purpose: To establish fully self-consistent framework for neutral-current neutrino-nucleus reactions based on relativistic nuclear energy density functional. Methods: Neutrino-nucleus cross sections are calculated using weak Hamiltonian and nuclear properties of initial and excited states are obtained with relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model and relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation that is extended to include pion contributions for unnatural parity transitions. Results: Inelastic neutral-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections for 12C, 16O, 56Fe, 56Ni, and even isotopes {92-100}Mo as well as respective cross sections averaged over distribution of supernova neutrinos. Conclusions: The present study provides insight into neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections in the neutral channel, their theoretical uncertainty in view of recently developed microscopic models, and paves the way for systematic self-consistent large-scale calculations involving open-shell target nuclei.

# Neutral-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections based on relativistic nuclear energy density functional

## Key claim
The paper establishes a fully self-consistent framework for neutral-current neutrino-nucleus reactions using relativistic nuclear energy density functional, but the calculated cross sections show systematic differences from other models (up to an order of magnitude larger than some QRPA results), indicating substantial theoretical uncertainty that must be accounted for in supernova simulations.

## Method
The authors calculate inelastic neutral-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections using the weak interaction Hamiltonian, with nuclear ground states described by the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model and excited states by the relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA) extended to include pion contributions with pseudovector coupling for unnatural parity transitions. The DD-ME2 parameterization of the density-dependent effective interaction is employed, with Gogny D1S force for pairing correlations, and multipoles up to J=5 are included in the cross section calculations.

## Key numbers
- For 56Fe at neutrino energy 50 MeV: cross section = 2.16 × 10^{-42} cm^2 (with quenching factor 0.8 in axial-vector coupling), compared to 1.72 × 10^{-42} cm^2 (hybrid model) and 1.43 × 10^{-42} cm^2 (QRPA)
- For 56Fe at neutrino energy 100 MeV: cross section = 1.92 × 10^{-42} cm^2 (with quenching), compared to 1.49 × 10^{-42} cm^2 (hybrid model) and 1.52 × 10^{-42} cm^2 (QRPA)
- For 100Mo at neutrino energy 100 MeV: cross section = 2.75 × 10^{-42} cm^2
- For 92Mo at neutrino energy 100 MeV: cross section = 2.52 × 10^{-42} cm^2
- Ratio of flux-averaged cross sections <σ(100Mo)> / <σ(92Mo)> = 2.46 at T=2 MeV, decreasing to 1.13 at T=10 MeV
- Vector form factor cutoff mass: 840 MeV; axial vector form factor cutoff mass: 1032 MeV
- Static axial-vector coupling constant F_A(0) = -1.2617 (quenched to 1.0 in calculations)
- Weinberg angle sin^2θ_W = 0.2325

## Context
This work extends the relativistic energy density functional approach, previously applied to charged-current reactions by Paar (2011) and Niu (2011), to neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering for the first time in a fully self-consistent framework including pairing correlations for open-shell nuclei. It provides cross sections for 12C, 16O, 40Ar, 56Fe, 56Ni, and 92-100Mo isotopes, enabling comparison with the hybrid model of Juodagalvis (2005) and QRPA calculations of Balasi (2011) and Cheoun (2010, 2011). The results are relevant for supernova simulations and for neutrino detectors such as MOON (Ejiri, 2008) and NEMO (Arnold, 2005), and the systematic differences between models quantify the theoretical uncertainty that must be considered in interpreting supernova neutrino signals.
