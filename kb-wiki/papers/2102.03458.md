# Radiative corrections to nucleon weak charges and Beyond Standard Model impact

> The nucleon axial charge is a central ingredient in nuclear and particle physics, and a key observable in precision tests of the electroweak Standard Model sector and beyond. We report on the first complete calculation of its electroweak quantum corrections up to $\mathcal{O}(α)$, using a combination of current algebra techniques and QCD sum rules. We find a substantial enhancement due to the weak magnetism contribution in the elastic channel, and include higher-twist and target mass corrections at low $Q^2$ to find $Δ_R^A = 0.02881(22)$. Using analogous methods, we determine a new value for the vector charge renormalization, $Δ_R^V = 0.02474(27)$, and show how the two most recent calculations can be brought into agreement. This allows us to determine a corrected experimental $g_A^0 = 1.2730(13)$, which is a $>2σ$ shift away from the commonly quoted value. We use this new result to set constraints on exotic right-handed currents by comparing to lattice QCD results, and resolve a double-counting issue in the $|V_{ud}|$ extraction from mirror decays.

# Radiative corrections to nucleon weak charges and Beyond Standard Model impact

## Key claim
The first complete calculation of electroweak quantum corrections to the nucleon axial charge up to O(α) finds a substantial enhancement from weak magnetism in the elastic channel, yielding Δ_R^A = 0.02881(22) and Δ_R^V = 0.02474(27). This produces a corrected experimental g_A^0 = 1.2730(13) that is a >2σ shift away from the commonly quoted value of g_A^eff = 1.2756(13), and the difference Δ_R^A − Δ_R^V = 4.07(8) × 10^{-3} is substantially larger than anticipated.

## Method
The calculation combines current algebra techniques with QCD sum rules, using the on mass-shell renormalization theorem and Ward-Takahashi identities to isolate the axial-vector-specific contributions from photonic vertex and γW box diagrams. For the perturbative high-Q^2 region, the polarized Bjorken sum rule (for axial transitions) and Gross-Llewellyn Smith sum rule (for vector transitions) are stitched together with a light-front holography motivated expression at low Q^2, and higher-twist and target mass corrections are applied retroactively using Nachtmann moments. The Born contribution is integrated numerically over the full Q^2 range using experimental form factor data for g_V(Q^2), g_M(Q^2), and g_A(Q^2).

## Key numbers
- Δ_R^A = 0.02881(22), the axial charge electroweak radiative correction
- Δ_R^V = 0.02474(27), the vector charge electroweak radiative correction
- g_A^0 = 1.2730(13), the corrected experimental axial coupling after removing radiative corrections
- Δ_R^A − Δ_R^V = 4.07(8) × 10^{-3}, the difference between axial and vector radiative corrections
- |V_ud|^super = 0.9736(3), the CKM matrix element from superallowed 0^+ → 0^+ decays
- |V_ud|^mirror = 0.9730(10), the CKM matrix element from mirror T=1/2 decays
- ε_R = −5(4) × 10^{-3}, the constraint on exotic right-handed currents from comparison with lattice QCD results from Walker-Loud (2020)

## Context
This work extends the half-century-long program of radiative corrections to weak interactions initiated by Sirlin (1978), and resolves the discrepancy between the two most recent Δ_R^V calculations by Seng (2018, 2019) and Czarnecki (2019). It enables the most stringent constraints on exotic right-handed currents from a clean channel by allowing direct comparison between the corrected experimental g_A^0 and lattice QCD calculations of g_A^QCD, and resolves a double-counting issue in the |V_ud| extraction from mirror decays that previously caused internal discrepancies in that data set.
