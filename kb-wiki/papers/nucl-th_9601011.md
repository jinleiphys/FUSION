# Scaling Properties of Hadron Production in the Ising Model for Quark-Hadron Phase Transition

> Earlier study of quark-hadron phase transition in the Ginzberg-Landau theory is reexamined in the Ising model, so that spatial fluctuations during the transition can be taken into account. Although the dimension of the physical system is 2, as will be argued, both d=2 and d=4 Ising systems are studied, the latter being theoretically closer to the Ginzberg-Landau theory. The normalized factorial moments F_q are used to quantify multiplicity fluctuations, and the scaling exponent νis used to characterize the scaling properties. It is found by simulation on the Ising lattice that νbecomes a function of the temperature T near T_c. The average value of νover a range of T<T_c agrees with the value of 1.3 derived analytically from the Ginzberg-Landau theory. Thus the implications of the mean-field theory are not invalidated by either the introduction of spatial fluctuations or the restriction to a 2D system.

# Scaling Properties of Hadron Production in the Ising Model for Quark-Hadron Phase Transition

## Key claim

The scaling exponent ν from the Ginzburg-Landau mean-field theory (ν = 1.304) is not invalidated by introducing spatial fluctuations via the Ising model or by restricting to a 2D system, but ν becomes a function of temperature T near T_c, and the average value over a range of T < T_c agrees with the Ginzburg-Landau result.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of the Ising model on 2D (L = 288) and 4D (L = 24) lattices using the Wolff algorithm to avoid critical slowing down. Hadron multiplicity in a cell was defined as n_i = λ |Σ s_j|² θ(Σ s_j), where s_j are Ising spins, λ is a scale factor, and the sum is over sites in a cell of size ε = 4 (2D) or ε = 2 (4D). Normalized factorial moments F_q were computed as functions of bin size M and of each other to study M-scaling and F-scaling, with the scaling exponent ν extracted from the relation β_q = (q-1)^ν.

## Key numbers

- For the 2D Ising model at T_c = 2.315 (in units of J/k_B), the slope parameter ζ in φ_q = ζ(q-1) was found to be ζ = 0.128 ± 0.007, compared to the analytical value of 1/8 = 0.125.
- At T_c in 2D, the scaling exponent ν(T_c) was found to be 1.02–1.07, consistent with ν = 1 from the relation β_q = q-1.
- At T = 2.20 in 2D (below T_c), ν = 1.565 for λ = 1; for λ = 0.02 and 0.05, ν varied between 1.02 and 1.52 over a narrow range of T below T_c.
- The Ginzburg-Landau theory value ν = 1.304 (dashed line) lies within the range of ν(T) values from the 2D Ising simulation.
- For the 4D Ising model at T_c = 6.7, with λ = 0.01, ν ≈ 1.3, in agreement with the Ginzburg-Landau result.
- The 2D lattice size was L = 288 with 10⁴ configurations; the 4D lattice size was L = 24 with 3×10⁴ configurations.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of quark-hadron phase transition using Ginzburg-Landau theory (Hwa and collaborators, 1990s) by incorporating spatial fluctuations through the Ising model, which the mean-field Ginzburg-Landau approach lacks. The authors compare their results to analytical work on the 2D Ising model by Satz (where ζ = 1/8) and to the Ginzburg-Landau prediction of ν = 1.304 (Hwa and Nazirov, 1992). The study enables future phenomenological application of F-scaling with exponent ν as an effective parameter for phase transitions in heavy-ion collisions, where temperature is not experimentally controllable, and suggests applicability to other fields such as evolutionary biology (Cao, Hwa, and collaborators, 1996).
