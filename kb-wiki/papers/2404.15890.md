# Hadronic scattering effects on $Λ$ polarization in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The $Λ$ hyperon spin flip and non-flip cross sections are calculated in a simple hadronic model by including both the $s$-channel process involving the spin 3/2, positive parity $Σ^*(1358)$ resonance and the $t$-channel process via the exchange of a scalar $σ$ meson. Because of its large mass, the $Λ$ spin flip to non-flip cross sections is negligibly small in the $t$-channel process compared to the constant value of 1/3.5 in the $s$-channel process. With the $s-$channel $Λ-π$ spin-dependent cross sections included in a schematic kinetic model, the effects of hadronic scatterings on the $Λ$ spin polarization in Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV are studied. It is found that the $Λ$ spin polarization only decreases by 7-12\% during the hadronic stage of these collisions, which justifies the assumption in theoretical studies that compare the $Λ$ polarization calculated at the chemical freezeout to the measured one at the kinetic freezeout.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that the $\Lambda$ spin polarization in Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV decreases by only 7-12% during the hadronic stage, justifying the common theoretical assumption that the polarization computed at chemical freezeout can be compared directly to experimental measurements at kinetic freezeout. This small decrease relies on the dominance of the $s$-channel $\Sigma^*(1358)$ resonance process, which has a fixed spin non-flip to flip ratio of 3.5, while the $t$-channel scalar $\sigma$ exchange contributes negligibly to spin flip due to the large $\Lambda$ mass.

## Method

The authors computed $\Lambda$-$\pi$ spin-dependent scattering cross sections using a hadronic model with two contributions: an $s$-channel process via the spin-3/2, positive parity $\Sigma^*(1358)$ resonance treated with the Breit-Wigner formula, and a $t$-channel process via scalar $\sigma$ meson exchange with coupling constants from one-boson exchange potentials and the $\sigma$ decay width. They then thermally averaged these cross sections and incorporated them into a schematic kinetic model for the time evolution of spin-up and spin-down $\Lambda$ numbers, using a parameterized temperature evolution and pion fugacity from the AMPT model for Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV.

## Key numbers

* The ratio of $\Lambda$ spin non-flip to spin flip cross sections in the $s$-channel $\Sigma^*(1358)$ resonance process is constant at 3.5.
* The $\Sigma^*(1358)$ resonance has mass $m_{\Sigma^*}=1358$ MeV and width $\Gamma_{\Sigma^*}=36$ MeV.
* The $t$-channel $\sigma$ meson coupling constants are $g_{\Lambda\Lambda\sigma}=7.07$ and $g_{\sigma\pi\pi}=2.77$ GeV, with $m_\sigma\approx 550$ MeV and $\Gamma_{\sigma}\approx 240$ MeV.
* The chemical freezeout temperature is $T_{\rm ch}=146$ MeV and the kinetic freezeout temperature is $T_{\rm k}=129$ MeV for Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$ GeV.
* The normalized number difference between spin-up and spin-down $\Lambda$ hyperons decreases to 0.93 (a 7% reduction) over the hadronic evolution time of 1.33 fm/$c$, and to 0.88 (a 12% reduction) if the hadronic phase lifetime is doubled to 2.66 fm/$c$.

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Ko (2023) that $\Lambda$-$\pi$ scattering through the $\Sigma^*(1358)$ resonance would preserve polarization, and the study by Hidaka (2023) of $\Lambda$ spin relaxation time in a hot pion gas, by explicitly calculating both $s$- and $t$-channel cross sections and quantifying the net effect in a kinetic model. The results justify the common practice in viscous hydrodynamic models (Karpenko, 2017; Li, 2017) and chiral transport approaches (Sun, 2016; Sun, 2018) of comparing $\Lambda$ polarization at chemical freezeout to experimental data at kinetic freezeout. Future work should extend this to a coupled spin kinetic approach treating $\Sigma^0$ and $\Sigma^*$ explicitly, including their finite lifetimes and feed-down contributions to $\Lambda$ polarization.
