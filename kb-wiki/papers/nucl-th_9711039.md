# Collective flow in central Au-Au collisions at 150, 250 and 400 A MeV

> Radial collective flow and thermalization are studied in gold on gold collisions at 150, 250 and 400 A MeV bombarding energies with a relativistically covariant formulation of a QMD code. We find that radial flow and "thermal" energies calculated for all the charged fragments agree reasonably with the experimental values. The experimental hardware filter at small angles used in the FOPI experiments at higher energies selects mainly the thermalized particles.

## Key claim

The authors find that radial flow and "thermal" energies calculated for all charged fragments agree reasonably with experimental values from the FOPI collaboration, but only after applying the experimental hardware filter at small angles (excluding particles with $\theta_{lab}<1.2^\circ$), which removes unthermalized forward-directed protons and makes the remaining distribution appear nearly isotropic.

## Method

The authors used a relativistically covariant formulation of a Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) code with a momentum-dependent, relativistically invariant two-body force to simulate central Au-Au collisions at 150, 250, and 400 A MeV. They divided the solid angle into 32 equal sectors and calculated the radial flow velocity and flow energy from the positions and momenta of charged particles at the end of the calculation (150 fm/c), considering both a "gas scenario" (all nucleons as individual objects) and a "cluster scenario" (all charged fragments including protons and clusters). The "disoriented/thermal" energy was defined as the remaining kinetic energy after subtracting the flow energy.

## Key numbers

- At 400 A MeV with cluster assumption: flow energy 59.7$\pm$2.5 MeV (experimental: 56.8$\pm$6.3 MeV), "thermal" energy 32.7$\pm$2 MeV (experimental: 32.8$\pm$6.3 MeV), flow velocity $\beta$=0.338$\pm$0.006 (experimental: 0.334$\pm$0.017)
- At 250 A MeV with cluster assumption: flow energy 34.8$\pm$1.0 MeV (experimental: 34.0$\pm$3.9 MeV), "thermal" energy 16.8$\pm$0.6 MeV (experimental: 21.5$\pm$3.9 MeV), flow velocity $\beta$=0.264$\pm$0.002 (experimental: 0.263$\pm$0.014)
- At 150 A MeV with cluster assumption: flow energy 18.3$\pm$0.3 MeV (experimental: 19.9$\pm$2.3 MeV), "thermal" energy 10.2$\pm$0.5 MeV (experimental: 12.6$\pm$2.3 MeV), flow velocity $\beta$=0.194$\pm$0.002 (experimental: 0.204$\pm$0.011)
- At 400 A MeV, only 20% of nucleons collided less than three times (unthermalized); at 150 A MeV, this fraction was 60% due to 65% Pauli blocking (versus 25% blocking at 400 A MeV)
- The fluctuation of flow velocity across 32 sectors was less than 2% for all energies at b=0.5 fm; particle number fluctuation was at most 3-4%
- Using a multiplicity trigger (as in the EOS experiment) reduced flow velocities by 15-20% compared to the FOPI trigger

## Context

This work extends earlier predictions of large collective radial flow in central collisions (Danielewicz et al., 1990s) and compares directly with experimental data from the FOPI collaboration (Reisdorf et al., 1990s) and the EOS collaboration (1990s). The authors use a momentum-dependent, relativistically invariant two-body force they previously developed (Nemeth and Papp, 1997) applied to QMD calculations. The results enable further application of this force to higher energies where particle creation becomes important, and demonstrate that the FOPI experimental hardware filter at small angles effectively selects thermalized particles, while the EOS multiplicity-based trigger is insufficient for selecting only central collisions.
