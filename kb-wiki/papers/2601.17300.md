# Quarkyonic matter with strangeness in an extended RMF model

> Quarkyonic matter is expected to play a key role for the transition from hadronic matter to quark matter in compact stars. Within the framework of the relativistic mean field (RMF) model and equivparticle model with density-dependent quark masses, we construct the ``quark Fermi sea" with a ``baryon Fermi surface" to characterize the properties of the quarkyonic matter. In particular, we develop a comprehensive framework to account for the strangeness degrees of freedom, incorporating $Λ$, $Ξ$, and $Σ$ hyperons as well as strange quarks in a unified quarkyonic framework. Our calculations indicate that the inevitable emergence of hyperons softens the equations of state, leading to a reduction in the speed of sound around $n_{\rm b}\approx 2n_0$, and consequently reducing the masses and radii of neutron stars. When the quark-hadron phase transition is taken into account, the equation of state at high densities exhibits additional softening, leading to a maximum sound velocity of $v_{\rm max} \approx 0.6\,c$, which is close to the ultrarelativistic limit of $0.58\,c$, consistent with current astronomical observational constraints.

## Key claim

The paper claims that incorporating strangeness degrees of freedom (hyperons and strange quarks) into a unified quarkyonic matter framework inevitably softens the equation of state, reducing neutron star masses and radii, and that when a quark-hadron phase transition is included, the maximum sound velocity reaches approximately 0.6c, close to the ultrarelativistic limit of 0.58c, consistent with current astronomical observational constraints. The hedge is that these results depend on the specific parameter sets chosen for the baryon and quark mass scalings and the adopted density functionals, with some parameter combinations failing to reproduce the observed 2-solar-mass neutron stars.

## Method

The authors combine a relativistic mean field (RMF) model for baryonic matter (including nucleons and Lambda, Xi, and Sigma hyperons) with an equivparticle model using density-dependent quark masses for u, d, and s quarks. They construct a "quark Fermi sea" with a "baryon Fermi surface" to characterize quarkyonic matter, enforcing matching conditions where a zero-momentum baryon's energy equals three times the quark energy at the interface. The coupled equations for meson fields, particle densities, charge neutrality, and beta equilibrium are solved iteratively, and the resulting equations of state are used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation for neutron star structure.

- For the PKDD functional with parameter set (B=150 MeV/fm^3, C=0.7, sqrt(D)=150 MeV): maximum mass M_TOV = 2.01 solar masses, radius R_1.4 = 12.84 km, tidal deformability Lambda_1.4 = 639, maximum sound speed v_max = 0.64c.

- The maximum sound velocity when quark-hadron phase transition is included: v_max ≈ 0.6c, close to the ultrarelativistic limit of 0.58c.
- The Lambda hyperon emerges at n_b ≈ 0.4 fm^{-3} in purely hadronic matter.
- Observed pulsar masses: PSR J1614-2230 at 1.928 ± 0.017 solar masses, PSR J0348+0432 at 2.01 ± 0.04 solar masses.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous unified framework for quarkyonic matter (Xia 2023) by incorporating strangeness degrees of freedom, including Lambda, Xi, and Sigma hyperons as well as strange quarks. It addresses the "Hyperon Puzzle" identified by Vidaña (2015), where hyperon softening of the equation of state makes it difficult to reach 2-solar-mass neutron stars. The approach contrasts with the IdylliQ model of Fujimoto (2024), which suggests that internal quark dynamics can delay hyperon onset and mitigate softening. The results enable future comparisons with neutron star observations from gravitational wave events and pulsar timing, and can be tested against constraints on radii and tidal deformability from events such as GW170817.
