# Pairing-energy coefficients of neutron-rich fragments in spallation reactions

> The ratio of pairing-energy coefficient to temperature ($a_{p}/T$) of neutron-rich fragments produced in spallation reactions has been investigated by adopting an isobaric yield ratio method deduced in the framework of a modified Fisher model. A series of spallation reactions, 0.5$A$ and 1$A$ GeV $^{208}$Pb + $p$, 1$A$ GeV $^{238}$U + $p$, 0.5$A$ GeV $^{136}$Xe + $d$, 0.2$A$, 0.5$A$ and 1$A$ GeV $^{136}$Xe + $p$, and $^{56}$Fe + $p$ with incident energy ranging from 0.3$A$ to 1.5$A$ GeV, has been analysed. An obvious odd-even staggering is shown in the fragments with small neutron excess ($I\equiv N - Z$), and in the relatively small-$A$ fragments which have large $I$. The values of $a_{p}/T$ for the fragments, with $I$ from 0 to 36, have been found to be in a range from -4 to 4, and most values of $a_{p}/T$ fall in the range from -1 to 1. It is suggested that a small pairing-energy coefficient should be considered in predicting the cross sections of fragments in spallation reactions. It is also concluded that the method proposed in this article is not good for fragments with $A/A_{s} >$ 85\% (where $A_{s}$ is the mass number of the spallation system).

# Pairing-energy coefficients of neutron-rich fragments in spallation reactions

## Key claim

The ratio of the pairing-energy coefficient to temperature ($a_p/T$) for neutron-rich fragments produced in spallation reactions falls mostly in the range from -1 to 1, which is much smaller than the theoretical value of 5.6 derived from the standard pairing-energy coefficient of 11.2 MeV and a typical temperature of 2.0 MeV. The isobaric yield ratio method used to extract $a_p/T$ is not reliable for fragments whose mass number exceeds 85% of the spallation system mass number ($A/A_s > 85\%$).

## Method

The authors analyzed experimental fragment cross sections from a series of spallation reactions using an isobaric yield ratio (IYR) method derived from the modified Fisher model. For even-neutron-excess fragments, the pairing-energy coefficient to temperature ratio $a_p/T$ was extracted by comparing the IYR for even-$I$ fragments with the average IYR from neighboring odd-$I$ fragments, which cancels the contributions from symmetry energy and chemical potential terms. The method was applied to fragments with neutron excess $I = N - Z$ ranging from 0 to 36 across multiple spallation systems.

## Key numbers

- $a_p/T$ values for fragments with $I$ from 0 to 36 fall in a range from -4 to 4, with most values within -1 to 1
- For fragments with $A/A_s > 85\%$, $a_p/T$ increases with $A$ and the method becomes unreliable
- The theoretical expectation for $a_p/T$ is 5.6, derived from $a_p = 11.2$ MeV and $T = 2.0$ MeV
- In the $^{56}$Fe + $p$ reactions at incident energies from 0.3$A$ to 1.5$A$ GeV, absolute $a_p/T$ values are no larger than 4
- Previous results from high-energy $p$ + Xe and $p$ + Kr reactions gave $a_p/T$ values of 1.80 and 1.63, respectively

## Context

This work extends the analysis of pairing-energy coefficients from projectile fragmentation reactions (Ma 2016, Ma 2017) to spallation reactions, using the same isobaric yield ratio method. The results confirm earlier findings by Huang et al. (2010) and Ma (2011, 2016) that pairing-energy coefficients in hot fragments are significantly reduced compared to the standard value, consistent with predictions from self-consistent finite-temperature relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory (Niu et al. 2013). The findings suggest that temperature-dependent pairing energy should be incorporated into de-excitation calculations such as the statistical abrasion-ablation model and the SACA method, and are relevant for predicting fragment cross sections needed for accelerator-driven system design.
