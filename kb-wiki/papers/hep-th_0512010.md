# Nonequilibrium quantum fields from first principles

> Calculations of nonequilibrium processes become increasingly feasable in quantum field theory from first principles. There has been important progress in our analytical understanding based on 2PI generating functionals. In addition, for the first time direct lattice simulations based on stochastic quantization techniques have been achieved. The quantitative descriptions of characteristic far-from-equilibrium time scales and thermal equilibration in quantum field theory point out new phenomena such as prethermalization. They determine the range of validity of standard transport or semi-classical approaches, on which most of our ideas about nonequilibrium dynamics were based so far. These are crucial ingredients to understand important topical phenomena in high-energy physics related to collision experiments of heavy nuclei, early universe cosmology and complex many-body systems.

## Key claim
The paper argues that standard transport or kinetic equations are only valid for times sufficiently larger than a characteristic damping time \(t_{\rm damp}\), and therefore cannot describe the early-time dynamics relevant for apparent fast thermalization in heavy-ion collisions. The authors show that prethermalization of the equation of state occurs on a much shorter time scale \(t_{\rm pt} \ll t_{\rm damp} \ll t_{\rm eq}\), but this alone is insufficient for hydrodynamic applicability without isotropization.

## Method
The authors solve the exact evolution equations for the statistical two-point function \(F(x,y)\) and spectral function \(\rho(x,y)\) using a three-loop 2PI (two-particle irreducible) effective action for scalar \(\lambda \Phi^4\) theory in 3+1 dimensions, which includes direct scatterings, off-shell effects, and memory integrals without derivative expansion. They compare these "full" results to lowest-order (LO) and next-to-lowest-order (NLO) gradient-expanded transport equations in Wigner space, and also perform direct lattice simulations using stochastic quantization with a fifth Langevin-time dimension for a scalar field with quartic self-interaction.

## Key numbers
- Prethermalization time scale: \(T\, t_{\rm pt} \simeq 2 - 2.5\) for a chiral quark-meson model, where \(T\) is the average kinetic energy per mode; for \(T \gtrsim 400 - 500\,\)MeV this gives \(t_{\rm pt}\) of somewhat less than \(1\,\)fm.
- Characteristic damping time from relaxation-time approximation: \(t_{\rm damp} \simeq 4 \pi m / (3 \lambda^2 T^2)\), where \(m\) is the scalar thermal mass, \(\lambda\) the coupling, and \(T\) the temperature.
- The NLO gradient expansion becomes accurate for times larger than about \(t_{\rm damp}\), with sizeable NLO corrections already for coupling \(\lambda \simeq 1/4\) and the NLO result getting close to the full result for \(\lambda \lesssim 1\).
- Lattice simulations used a \(64^3\) spatial lattice for snapshots of anisotropic occupation number distributions.

## Context
This work extends the analytical understanding of nonequilibrium quantum fields using 2PI generating functionals, building on earlier results by Berges (2004) on prethermalization in a chiral quark-meson model. It directly tests the range of validity of standard transport or kinetic theory approaches, which had been the basis for most previous ideas about nonequilibrium dynamics. The paper also presents first direct lattice simulations of nonequilibrium quantum fields in Minkowski space-time using stochastic quantization (Berges 2005), which enables future non-perturbative studies of QCD dynamics and tests of phenomena such as plasma instabilities proposed by Arnold (2004) and Mrowczynski (1993) for faster isotropization in gauge theories.
