# Sigma term and strangeness content of the nucleon

> A status report is given for a joint project of the Budapest-Marseille-Wuppertal collaboration and the Regensburg group to study the quark mass-dependence of octet baryons in SU(3) Baryon XPT. This formulation is expected to extend to larger masses than Heavy-Baryon XPT. Its applicability is tested with 2+1 flavor data which cover three lattice spacings and pion masses down to about 190 MeV, in large volumes. Also polynomial and rational interpolations in M_π^2 and M_K^2 are used to assess the uncertainty due to the ansatz. Both frameworks are combined to explore the precision to be expected in a controlled determination of the nucleon sigma term and strangeness content.

## Key claim

The nucleon sigma term is expected to be in the range of approximately 50(10)(10) MeV, where each error slot has only one significant digit, while the strangeness content y_N is currently too imprecise to determine whether a non-zero strangeness content exists at all, with a value around 0.1(2)(1).

## Method

The authors use 2+1 flavor lattice QCD ensembles with three lattice spacings (0.065, 0.083, 0.124 fm) and pion masses from 190 MeV to 670 MeV, applying the Hellmann-Feynman theorem to extract sigma terms from the quark mass dependence of the nucleon mass. They fit the nucleon and octet baryon masses using both a covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (CBXPT) ansatz and polynomial/rational interpolations in M_pi^2 and M_K^2, evaluating derivatives at the physical mass point in a mass-independent scale setting scheme.

## Key numbers

* Pion-nucleon sigma term from one CBXPT snapshot fit: sigma_piN = 55(10)_stat MeV, with a preliminary final estimate of sigma_piN approximately 50(10)(10) MeV
* Strangeness content from one CBXPT snapshot fit: y_N approximately 0.16, with a preliminary final estimate of y_N approximately 0.1(2)(1)
* The CBXPT fit used 7 free parameters to 40 datapoints with an uncorrelated chi^2 = 8.39 and chi^2/d.o.f. approximately 0.25
* The dataset covers pion masses down to approximately 190 MeV and three lattice spacings: a approximately 0.124 fm (beta=3.3), a approximately 0.083 fm (beta=3.57), and a approximately 0.065 fm (beta=3.7)

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD spectrum analysis of Durr (2008) and the f_K/f_pi methodology of Durr (2010) to the nucleon sigma term and strangeness content, testing both CBXPT (which extends to higher pion masses than Heavy-Baryon XPT) and analytic interpolation approaches. The results are preliminary and require systematic variation over fitting windows, pion mass cuts, and discretization effects on O(1000) bootstrap samples to obtain a controlled determination, with the strangeness content requiring additional simulation points at significantly smaller strange quark mass.
