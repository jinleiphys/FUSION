# Operator Counting and Soft Blocks in Chiral Perturbation Theory

> Chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) is a low-energy effective field theory of QCD and also a nonlinear sigma model based on the symmetry breaking pattern ${\rm SU}(N_f)\times {\rm SU}(N_f)\to {\rm SU}(N_f)$. In the limit of massless $N_f$ quarks, we enumerate the independent operators without external sources in ChPT using an on-shell method, by counting and presenting the soft blocks at each order in the derivative expansion, up to ${\cal O}(p^{10})$. Given the massless on-shell condition and total momentum conservation, soft blocks are homogeneous polynomials of kinematic invariants exhibiting the Adler's zero when any external momentum becomes soft and vanishing. In addition, soft blocks are seeds for recursively generating all tree amplitudes of Nambu-Goldstone bosons without recourse to ChPT, and in one-to-one correspondence with the "low energy constants" which are the Wilson coefficients. Relations among operators, such as those arising from equations of motion, integration-by-parts, hermiticity, and symmetry structure, manifest themselves in the soft blocks in simple ways. We find agreements with the existing results up to NNNLO, and make a prediction at N$^4$LO.

## Key claim

The authors enumerate independent parity-even operators in massless chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) without external sources up to \({\cal O}(p^{10})\) (N\(^4\)LO) using an on-shell "soft block" method, finding agreement with existing results up to NNNLO and making a prediction at N\(^4\)LO; the counting is performed in the limit of massless quarks and in spacetime dimensions \(d \ge n-1\) where Gram determinant conditions are ignored.

## Method

The authors construct soft blocks, homogeneous polynomials of kinematic invariants that satisfy Adler's zero when any external momentum becomes soft, by starting with the most general polynomial in Mandelstam invariants at a given derivative order and number of external legs, then imposing the Adler's zero condition via an all-leg momentum shift. The number of independent soft blocks at each order is obtained by solving the linear constraints from the vanishing of the shifted polynomial at the soft points, and these soft blocks are in one-to-one correspondence with the low-energy constants (LECs) of the effective Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

* At \({\cal O}(p^4)\), there are 3 independent 4-pt soft blocks before imposing Adler's zero and 3 after.
* At \({\cal O}(p^6)\), there are 4 independent 4-pt soft blocks and 15 independent 6-pt soft blocks after imposing Adler's zero (from 165 monomials before).
* At \({\cal O}(p^8)\), there are 5 independent 4-pt soft blocks, 150 independent 6-pt soft blocks, and 105 independent 8-pt soft blocks after imposing Adler's zero (from 8855 monomials before for 8-pt).
* At \({\cal O}(p^{10})\), there are 6 independent 4-pt soft blocks, 621 independent 6-pt soft blocks, 3360 independent 8-pt soft blocks, and 945 independent 10-pt soft blocks after imposing Adler's zero (from 575757 monomials before for 10-pt).

## Context

This work extends the operator counting in ChPT beyond the NNNLO (\({\cal O}(p^8)\)) Lagrangian obtained by Bijnens (2018) and the NNLO (\({\cal O}(p^6)\)) Lagrangian by Bijnens (1999), using a novel on-shell method based on soft bootstrap developed by Cheung (2015) and Low (2019). The soft block approach avoids redundancies from equations of motion, integration-by-parts, and hermiticity that complicate Lagrangian-based constructions, and the results provide a starting point for building the complete N\(^4\)LO effective Lagrangian in ChPT.
