# P-Wave Polarization of the $ρ$-Meson and the Dilepton Spectrum in Dense Matter

> We study the $p$-wave polarization operator of the $ρ$-meson due to $ρN$ interactions via the $N^*$ (1720) and $Δ(1905)$ resonances and compute the corresponding production rate for $e^+e^-$-pairs at finite temperature and baryon density. At high baryon density we find a significant shift of the spectrum to lower invariant masses.

# P-Wave Polarization of the ρ-Meson and the Dilepton Spectrum in Dense Matter

## Key claim

The p-wave polarization operator of the ρ-meson due to excitation of the N*(1720) and Δ(1905) resonances in dense baryonic matter produces a significant shift of the dilepton spectrum to lower invariant masses, but this effect is strongly momentum-dependent: it primarily enhances lepton pairs with large transverse momenta (q > 500 MeV), while leaving the spectrum at small transverse momenta (q < 500 MeV) nearly unchanged.

## Method

The authors compute the ρ-meson self energy in nuclear matter to lowest order in density using the static approximation, with the scattering amplitude approximated by two resonance contributions: the N*(1720) and Δ(1905) baryons. They determine the ρN and γN coupling constants by fitting the partial widths of these resonances from Particle Data Group values, using a modified Vector Meson Dominance model of Kroll, Lee and Zumino. The resulting in-medium spectral function is then used to calculate the e+e- pair production rate at finite temperature and baryon density via an integral over all pair momenta.

## Key numbers

- The N*(1720) resonance has mass 1720 MeV, total width 100-200 MeV, and ρN branching ratio 70-85%
- The Δ(1905) resonance has mass 1905 MeV, total width 280-440 MeV, and ρN branching ratio >60%
- The fitted coupling constant for N*Nρ is f_{N*Nρ} = 7.2, obtained from Γ_{N*→Nρ} ≈ 100 MeV
- The fitted coupling constant for ΔNρ is f_{ΔNρ} = 9.0, obtained from Γ_{Δ→Nρ} = 210 MeV
- The γN branching ratios are suppressed compared to VMD by factors of approximately 1/30 for the N*(1720) and 1/5 for the Δ(1905)
- At q = 750 MeV and ρ_B = 2ρ_0, the low-energy peak carries about 20% of the spectral strength
- The dilepton production rate shows strong enhancement at invariant masses around 300-500 MeV at T = 140 MeV and densities of 2-3 ρ_0
- For momenta q > 500 MeV at ρ_B = 2ρ_0 and T = 140 MeV, the spectrum is strongly modified, while for q < 500 MeV it remains close to the vacuum shape

## Context

This work extends previous calculations that considered only the self energy of a ρ-meson at rest in nuclear matter (e.g., Herrmann, Friman, and Nörenberg; Asakawa et al.; Chanfray and Schuck; Klingl and Weise) by including the momentum-dependent p-wave polarization operator. The authors note that the CERES and HELIOS-3 data show a shift of strength in the dilepton invariant mass spectrum to lower masses, which had previously been interpreted in terms of a reduction of the in-medium ρ-meson mass (by Li, Ko, and Brown; Cassing et al.). The distinctive momentum dependence predicted here, strong enhancement at large transverse momenta but little change at small transverse momenta, provides a way to experimentally discriminate between s-wave mass-shift models and the p-wave mechanism. The work was subsequently used by Rapp, Chanfray, and Wambach in a dynamical model that found quantitative agreement with CERES data.
