# Large-Nc Properties of the rho and f0(600) Mesons from Unitary Resonance Chiral Dynamics

> We construct pi-pi amplitudes that fulfill exact elastic unitarity, account for one loop Chiral Perturbation Theory contributions and include all 1/Nc leading terms, with the only limitation of considering just the lowest-lying nonet of exchanged resonances. Within such scheme, the Nc dependence of sigma and rho masses and widths is discussed. Robust conclusions are drawn in the case of the rho resonance, confirming that it is a stable meson in the limit of a large number of QCD colors, Nc. Less definitive conclusions are reached in the scalar-isoscalar sector. With the present quality of data, we cannot firmly conclude whether or not the Nc=3 f0(600) resonance completely disappears at large Nc or it has a sub-dominant component in its structure, which would become dominant for a number of quark colors sufficiently large.

## Key claim

The paper constructs ππ amplitudes that satisfy exact elastic unitarity, include one-loop Chiral Perturbation Theory contributions, and incorporate all leading 1/Nc terms within the single-resonance-approximation, but the conclusions about the f0(600) sigma meson are less definitive than for the rho: the authors cannot firmly conclude whether the Nc=3 f0(600) resonance completely disappears at large Nc or retains a subdominant component that would become dominant for sufficiently large Nc.

## Method

The authors construct ππ scattering amplitudes using Resonance Chiral Theory (RχT) in the Single-Resonance-Approximation, including only the lowest-lying nonet of exchanged resonances (vector, scalar, and pseudoscalar). They impose leading-1/Nc short-distance constraints from asymptotic QCD, match to one-loop Chiral Perturbation Theory at O(p⁴), and then unitarize the amplitudes to satisfy exact elastic unitarity. The parameters are fixed by fitting the (I,J)=(0,0), (1,1), and (2,0) phase shifts at Nc=3, after which the Nc dependence of the rho and sigma poles is studied by analytically continuing the amplitudes in the number of colors.

## Key numbers

- The rho meson mass at Nc=3 is approximately 770 MeV, and its width scales as O(1/Nc), confirming it becomes a stable meson in the large-Nc limit.
- The pion mass used is mπ = 139.57 MeV, and the pion decay constant is fπ ≈ 93 MeV.
- The Adler sum rule is satisfied to 5% using the GKPRY parameterization with the UFD set of parameters from Martin (2011).
- The Finite Energy Sum Rules are evaluated with a matching scale √sh = 1.42 GeV, yielding T_{It=0}(0) = -29.3, T_{It=1}'(0) = -124.6 GeV⁻², and T_{It=2}(0) = -12.25 + R_{It=2} where R_{It=2} is compatible with zero.

## Context

This work extends previous analyses by Pelaez (2003), Sun (2005), and Nieves (2009) that neglected leading 1/Nc terms beyond a certain chiral order, and improves upon the treatment in Guo (2011) where leading 1/Nc contributions were not properly considered. The approach uses the single-resonance-approximation of Resonance Chiral Theory as developed by Ecker (1988, 1989) and Pich (2002), and is constrained by forward dispersion relations and Regge phenomenology. The results enable future studies of the nature of the sigma meson by clarifying which Nc trajectories are consistent with QCD, and provide a framework for distinguishing tetraquark, glueball, or conventional meson interpretations of the f0(600).
