# A novel manifestation of $α$-clustering: new '$α$ + $^{208}$Pb' states in $^{212}$Po revealed by their enhanced E1 decays

> Excited states in $^{212}$Po were populated by $α$ transfer using the $^{208}$Pb($^{18}$O, $^{14}$C) reaction and their deexcitation $γ$-rays were studied with the Euroball array. Several levels were found to decay by a unique E1 transition (E$_γ<$ 1 MeV) populating the yrast state with the same spin value. Their lifetimes were measured by the DSAM method. The values, found in the range [0.1-1.4] ps, lead to very enhanced transitions, B(E1) = 2$\times10^{-2}$ - 1$\times10^{-3}$ W.u.. These results are discussed in terms of an $α$-cluster structure which gives rise to states with non-natural parity values, provided that the composite system cannot rotate collectively, as expected in the '$α$ + $^{208}$Pb' case. Such states due to the oscillatory motion of the $α$-core distance are observed for the first time.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first observation of excited states in 212Po that decay via highly enhanced E1 transitions (B(E1) = 2×10⁻² – 1×10⁻³ W.u.), which are interpreted as evidence for an α-cluster structure where the α particle oscillates against the 208Pb core, provided the composite system cannot rotate collectively.

## Method

Excited states in 212Po were populated via α transfer using the 208Pb(18O,14C) reaction at 85 MeV beam energy, with γ-rays detected by the Euroball IV array (71 Ge detectors). Lifetimes of seven excited levels were measured using the Doppler-shift attenuation method (DSAM) with Monte-Carlo simulations of the slowing-down process. Spin and parity assignments were made from γ-ray angular distributions and anisotropy ratios (RADO = Iγ(39.3°)/Iγ(76.6°)).

## Key numbers

- Lifetimes of seven excited levels in 212Po: range [0.1–1.4] ps (specific values: 0.48(15) ps for 1744 keV 4⁻ state, 0.48(20) ps for 1751 keV 8⁻ state, 0.45(8) ps for 1787 keV 6⁻ state, 0.47(15) ps for 1946 keV 4⁻ state, 0.49(16) ps for 2016 keV 6⁻ state, 0.61(16) ps for 2465 keV 10⁻ state, 0.12(6) ps for 3155 keV 7⁺ state)
- Reduced E1 transition probabilities: B(E1) = 2×10⁻² – 1×10⁻³ W.u. (typical B(E1) values are < 10⁻⁵ W.u.)
- Recoil velocity of 212Po: v/c = 1%
- Dipole moment of the α+208Pb system: 3.7 e·fm
- Excitation energies of non-natural parity states: even-I states around 2 MeV, odd-I states around 3 MeV
- γ-ray energies for E1 transitions: all Eγ < 1 MeV (e.g., 612.3 keV, 276.1 keV, 432.3 keV, 813.6 keV, 661.3 keV, 633.0 keV, 563.8 keV, 757.2 keV, 490.2 keV, 397.7 keV, 780.4 keV, 740.2 keV)

## Context

This work extends the study of α-clustering from light nuclei (where α-core systems rotate collectively, as in the Hoyle state in 12C and nuclear molecules reviewed by Oertzen (2006)) to heavy nuclei, specifically 212Po. Previous shell-model calculations by Poletti et al. (1987) described low-lying yrast states but failed to reproduce the α-decay width, leading to hybrid shell-cluster models by Varga et al. (1992) that predicted 30% α-clustering in the ground state. The observed enhanced E1 transitions provide the first evidence for α-cluster states with oscillatory motion of the α-core distance, opening the possibility to study similar structures in heavier systems like 216Rn with two α particles.
