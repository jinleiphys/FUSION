# Coulomb corrections in the extraction of the proton radius

> Multi-photon exchange contributions are important in extracting the proton charge radius from elastic electron--proton scattering. So far, only diagrams associated with the exchange of a second photon have been evaluated. At the very low $Q^2$ values relevant to the radius extraction, higher order contributions may become important. We evaluate these corrections in the Effective Momentum Approximation, which includes the Coulomb interaction to all orders, and find small corrections with a strong $Q^2$ dependence at low $Q^2$ and large scattering angles. This suggests that the higher order terms may be important in the evaluation of the proton magnetic radius.

## Key claim

Multi-photon exchange contributions beyond the standard two-photon exchange (TPE) diagrams may be important for extracting the proton magnetic radius from elastic electron-proton scattering at very low momentum transfer, even though they have a negligible effect on the charge radius extraction.

## Method

The author evaluates Coulomb corrections to all orders using the Effective Momentum Approximation (EMA), which accounts for the acceleration of the electron in the proton's Coulomb field and shifts the kinematics at the scattering vertex. The Coulomb potential is taken as the value at a separation distance Δx = 1/q, where q is the virtual photon momentum, with a maximum of the volume-averaged potential of 1.5 MeV for scattering inside the proton. The EMA results are compared to the standard 2nd Born approximation calculations.

## Key numbers

- At large ε (ε=0.5), the EMA correction near Q²=0 is small; explaining the charge radius discrepancy would require a 1% change between Q²=0.01 and 0.03 GeV², but the EMA calculation yields a change of around 0.05% over this range.
- At ε=0.02, the EMA correction decreases by 1.5% going from Q²=0.03 GeV² to 0.2 GeV², corresponding to a change in fractional slope of -8%/GeV².
- At ε=0.2, the correction increases 0.3% going from Q²=0.01 to 0.03 GeV², corresponding to a change in fractional slope of +15%/GeV².
- The 2nd Born approximation changes the cross section by about 0.2% between Q²=0.01 and 0.03 GeV², yielding a slope change of 10%/GeV², corresponding to roughly a 1% change in the extracted radius (about 0.01 fm).
- The discrepancy between electron- and muon-based results corresponds to a change in slope of the reduced cross section of approximately 50%/GeV², or a 1% change in cross section at Q²=0.02 GeV².

## Context

This work extends previous TPE calculations (Blunden et al. 2005, Borisyuk and Kobushkin 2006-2013, Kondratyuk 2005) that only included second-photon exchange, motivated by the proton radius discrepancy between muonic hydrogen measurements (Pohl 2010, Antognini 2013) yielding Rp=0.8409(4) fm and electron scattering extractions (Bernauer 2010, Zhan 2011) yielding Rp=0.8772(46) fm. The EMA approach, previously applied to heavy nuclei (Aste 2005, 2007, 2008), is used here for the first time to estimate higher-order Coulomb effects in low-Q² elastic electron-proton scattering. The results suggest that future measurements comparing e±-p and μ±-p scattering, such as the MUSE experiment at PSI, could help constrain these corrections, but a more detailed distorted-wave Born approximation calculation is needed for quantitative estimates.
