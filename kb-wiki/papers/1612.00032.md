# Energy Conservation and the Chiral Magnetic Effect

> We analyze the chiral magnetic effect in a homogeneous neutral plasma from the point of view of energy conservation, and construct an effective potential for the growth of maximally helical perturbations of the electromagnetic field. We show that a negative curvature at the origin of the potential, indicating instability of the plasma, is induced by a chiral asymmetry in electron Fermi energy, as opposed to number density, while the potential grows at large field value. It follows that the ground state for a plasma has zero magnetic helicity; a nonzero electron mass will allow an excited state of a plasma with nonzero helicity to relax to that ground state quickly. We conclude that a chiral plasma instability triggered by weak interactions is not a viable mechanism for explaining magnetic fields in stars except possibly when dynamics drives the system far from equilibrium.

## Key claim

The chiral plasma instability triggered by weak interactions is not a viable mechanism for explaining magnetic fields in stars, except possibly when dynamics drives the system far from equilibrium; the instability is induced by a chiral asymmetry in electron Fermi energy (chemical potential) rather than number density, and a nonzero electron mass allows an excited state with nonzero helicity to relax to the ground state quickly.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective potential for maximally helical perturbations of the electromagnetic field in a homogeneous neutral plasma, deriving the chiral magnetic current from energy conservation arguments. They analyzed the time evolution of the system including finite electrical conductivity and finite electron mass, solving the coupled equations for the gauge field amplitude and the chiral chemical potential under the assumption of rapid scattering that maintains quasi-static equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- The chiral magnetic current coefficient is \(\xi = 2\alpha \mu_5 / \pi\), where \(\mu_5 = (\mu_+ - \mu_-)/2\) is the chiral chemical potential and \(\alpha\) is the fine structure constant.
- The maximally unstable mode occurs at wavenumber \(k_\star = -\alpha \mu_5(0)/\pi\), with growth time \(\tau = 2/(\sqrt{\sigma^2 + \xi^2} - \sigma)\) for constant \(\xi\).
- The relaxation time to the stationary ground state with finite conductivity is \(\tau_{k_\star} \simeq 10^{-12}~\text{sec} \times (\mu/\mu_5(0))^2 (\mu/100~\text{MeV})^{5/2} (10^8~\text{K}/T)^2\).
- The wavelength of the magnetic field at the optimal wavenumber is \(\lambda_\star = (6.2 \times 10^{-7}~\text{m}) \times (\mu/100~\text{MeV}) (10^{12}~\text{gauss}/\bar{B}_{k_\star})\).
- The chirality flipping time due to electron mass is \(\tau_m \simeq 10^{-12}~\text{sec} \times (\mu/100~\text{MeV})^3 (T/10^8~\text{K})\).
- The gauge potential at the minimum of the effective potential is \(\bar{\CA}_{k_\star} = \mu/(\sqrt{2}\alpha)\), independent of the initial chiral asymmetry.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the chiral magnetic effect by constructing an effective potential from energy conservation, resolving confusions in the literature about the correct form of the current. It contradicts claims by Dvornikov (2014, 2015) and Sigl (2015) that the chiral plasma instability could explain large magnetic fields in magnetars, showing instead that the instability is not sustained in neutron star plasmas due to rapid chirality flipping from the nonzero electron mass. The analysis enables future numerical studies of whether an inverse cascade (as studied by Boyarsky (2012), Tashiro (2012), and others) can convert short-wavelength helical fields into large-scale fields on timescales shorter than the chirality-flipping time.
