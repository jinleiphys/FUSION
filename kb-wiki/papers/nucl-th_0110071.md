# Quark-hadron duality and the nuclear EMC effect

> Recent data on polarized proton knockout reactions off He-4 nuclei suggest a small but nonzero modification of proton electromagnetic form factors in medium. Using model independent relations derived on the basis of quark-hadron duality, we relate the medium modification of the form factors to the modification at large x of the deep-inelastic structure function of a bound proton. This places strong constraints on models of the nuclear EMC effect which assume a large deformation of the intrinsic structure of the nucleon in medium.

## Key claim

Using model-independent relations based on quark-hadron duality, the medium modification of proton electromagnetic form factors (suggested by polarized proton knockout data on He-4) can be related to the modification at large x of the deep-inelastic structure function of a bound proton, placing strong constraints on models of the nuclear EMC effect that assume a large deformation of the intrinsic nucleon structure in medium.

## Method

The authors applied Bloom-Gilman local duality relations to connect the medium-modified elastic electromagnetic form factors of a bound proton (calculated within the quark-meson coupling model, constrained by polarization transfer data on He-4 at Q^2 ≈ 0.4 GeV^2) to the ratio of bound-to-free deep-inelastic structure functions F_2 at large Bjorken-x. They compared the resulting structure function modification with predictions from the point-like configuration (PLC) suppression model of the EMC effect, and also inverted the duality relation to compute the magnetic form factor ratio implied by the PLC model.

## Key numbers

- The polarization ratio in He-4 was found to differ by ≈ 10% from that in hydrogen at Q^2 ≈ 0.4 GeV^2.
- The modification of the bound nucleon form factors is 1–2% for the magnetic and of order 8% for the electric form factor at normal nuclear matter density (ρ = ρ_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}) and Q^2 ≈ 0.3 GeV^2.
- The electric-to-magnetic ratio for the proton is ~5% smaller in medium than in free space for ρ = 1/2 ρ_0, and ~10% smaller for ρ = ρ_0.
- The bound/free ratio of the electric-to-magnetic form factor deviates by ~20% from unity at Q^2 ≈ 2 GeV^2.
- The ratio F_2^{p*}/F_2^p shows a deviation from unity of < 1% for ρ = 1/2 ρ_0 and < 2% for ρ = ρ_0 over the range 0.5 < x < 0.75.
- The PLC suppression model predicts a suppression of order 20% in F_2^{N*}/F_2^N for x ~ 0.6–0.7, an order of magnitude larger than the duality-based result.
- The PLC model predicts a suppression of about 20% in the ratio G_M^{p*}/G_M^p at Q^2 ~ 1–2 GeV^2, while the QMC calculation produces a ratio 5–10% larger than unity.

## Context

This work extends the study of medium modifications of nucleon properties by using quark-hadron duality (Bloom and Gilman, 1970) to connect elastic form factor data from polarized knockout experiments on He-4 (Dieterich et al., 2001) to deep-inelastic structure functions. The results contradict models that attribute the nuclear EMC effect primarily to a large deformation of the bound nucleon's intrinsic structure, such as the point-like configuration suppression model of Frankfurt and Strikman (1980s). The analysis enables a consistent, model-independent framework for simultaneously studying exclusive and inclusive observables in nuclei, and suggests that conventional nuclear physics descriptions (including binding and Fermi motion) remain the reliable starting point for nuclear structure function calculations.
