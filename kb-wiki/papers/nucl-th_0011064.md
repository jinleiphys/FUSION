# CEM2k - Recent Developments in CEM

> Recent developments of the Cascade-Exciton Model (CEM) of nuclear reactions are briefly described. These changes are motivated by new data on isotope production measured recently in "reverse kinematics" at GSI for interactions of 208-Pb and 238-U at 1 GeV/nucleon and 197-Au at 800 MeV/nucleon with liquid 1-H. This study leads us to CEM2k, which is a new version of the CEM code that is still under development. The increased accuracy and predictive power of the code CEM2k are shown by several examples. Further necessary work is outlined.

# CEM2k - Recent Developments in CEM

## Key claim

The new CEM2k code achieves significantly better agreement with GSI reverse-kinematics isotope production data for p + 208Pb at 1 GeV/nucleon than its predecessor CEM97 or any other code tested, but only after adopting a longer intranuclear cascade stage (using a cutoff energy of 1 MeV instead of the parameter P = 0.3) combined with a preequilibrium stage that considers only exciton-number-increasing transitions (Δn = +2). The code remains under development and does not yet include a model for fission-fragment production, limiting its applicability for lighter nuclides where fission contributes.

## Method

The Cascade-Exciton Model (CEM) treats nuclear reactions in three stages: intranuclear cascade (INC), preequilibrium decay via the modified exciton model, and equilibrium evaporation. The authors modified the CEM97 code by replacing the optical-potential-based transition criterion (parameter P = 0.3) with a fixed cutoff energy T_cut = 1 MeV to lengthen the cascade stage, and by allowing only Δn = +2 exciton transitions during preequilibrium to shorten that stage, thereby increasing the excitation energy available for evaporation. They tested the resulting CEM2k code against GSI reverse-kinematics data for 208Pb + p at 1 GeV/nucleon, 197Au + p at 800 MeV/nucleon, and 238U + p at 1 GeV/nucleon, as well as against neutron double-differential cross sections from p + 208Pb at 0.8 and 1.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Mean excitation energy of compound nuclei after preequilibrium stage increases from 58.7 MeV (CEM97) to 97.5 MeV (CEM2k) for p + 208Pb at 1 GeV/nucleon.
- Mean mass number of compound nuclei after preequilibrium stage is 193.9 for both CEM97 and CEM2k for p + 208Pb at 1 GeV/nucleon.
- Fission cross section for p (1 GeV) + 208Pb is approximately 100 mb, about 6% of the total reaction cross section of 1600 mb as used by CEM97.
- Fission cross section for p (1 GeV) + 238U is about 1390 mb, approximately 80% of the total reaction cross section of 1788 mb as calculated by CEM2k.
- CEM97 predicts multiplicities for n, p, d, t, 3He, and 4He from p + 208Pb at 1 GeV/nucleon as: 9.2409 ± 0.0025, 1.9624 ± 0.0011, 0.5518 ± 0.0006, 0.1593 ± 0.0003, 0.0682 ± 0.0002, and 0.1743 ± 0.0003, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the Cascade-Exciton Model lineage from CEM95 (Mashnik, 1995) and CEM97 (Mashnik and Sierk, 1998) to address systematic discrepancies with GSI reverse-kinematics data (Wlazlo et al., 2000; Enqvist et al., 2000) that all existing codes (LAHET with ISABEL and Bertini options, YIELDX, CASCADE, INUCL) failed to describe, all producing calculated isotope distributions shifted toward heavier masses. The improved agreement for spallation products enables more reliable predictions for accelerator-driven systems such as Accelerator Transmutation of Wastes (ATW) and Accelerator Production of Tritium (APT), though further work is needed to incorporate high-energy fission models (e.g., the RAL fission model already used in MCNPX) and to address remaining discrepancies for 238U targets where fission dominates.
