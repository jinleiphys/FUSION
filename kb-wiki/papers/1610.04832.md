# Coupled-channel Dalitz plot analysis of $D^+\to K^- π^+π^+$ decay

> We demonstrate that partial wave amplitudes extracted from $D^+\to K^-π^+π^+$ Dalitz plot with a unitary coupled-channel model are significantly different from those obtained with an isobar model. The unitary coupled-channel model takes account of hadronic rescattering mechanisms involving all three mesons that have been missed in conventioanl isobar model analyses. The rescattering mechanisms contribute largely, and can triplicate the $D^+\to {K}^-π^+π^+$ decay width within our analysis. These findings deliver a warning that analysis results obtained with isobar models should be looked with a caution. The determination of the CKM angle $γ/ϕ_3$ is a highly relevant problem.

# Coupled-channel Dalitz plot analysis of $D^+\to K^- \pi^+ \pi^+$ decay

## Key claim

Partial wave amplitudes extracted from the $D^+\to K^- \pi^+ \pi^+$ Dalitz plot using a unitary coupled-channel model are significantly different from those obtained with an isobar model, because the isobar model misses hadronic rescattering mechanisms (Z-diagrams) that can nearly triple the decay width; the authors caution that isobar model results should be viewed with skepticism, particularly for determinations of the CKM angle $\gamma/\phi_3$.

## Method

The authors constructed a unitary coupled-channel model for $D^+\to K^- \pi^+ \pi^+$ that includes two-pseudoscalar-meson scattering amplitudes (fit to LASS $\pi\bar K$ data and CERN-Munich $\pi\pi$ data up to 2 GeV), then solved the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equation for three-meson scattering including Z-diagrams and a three-meson-force from vector-meson exchange. They fit the model to Dalitz plot pseudo-data generated from the E791 isobar model, adjusting only the $D^+\to {\cal R}h$ vertex parameters while keeping the two-meson interactions fixed, and compared results from the Full model, a Z-diagram-only model, and an Isobar model.

## Key numbers

- The Full model, Z model, and Isobar model achieved $\chi^2/{\rm d.o.f.}$ = 0.22, 0.17, and 0.42, respectively, when fit to the $D^+\to K^- \pi^+ \pi^+$ Dalitz plot pseudo-data.
- The three-meson-force (vector-meson exchange) suppresses the $D^+\to K^- \pi^+ \pi^+$ decay width by approximately 22%.
- The hadronic rescattering involving Z-diagrams nearly triplicates the $D^+\to K^- \pi^+ \pi^+$ decay width compared to the isobar contribution alone.
- The $(K^-\pi^+)_S\pi^+$ phase from the Full and Z models agrees well with the E791 MIPWA result for $M_{K^-\pi^+} \lesssim 1.5$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior isobar model analyses of $D^+\to K^- \pi^+ \pi^+$ (E791 Collaboration, 2002) by including three-body unitarity and hadronic rescattering via Z-diagrams, which were missed in conventional isobar models. It contradicts the explanation by Edera et al. and the FOCUS K-matrix model analysis, which attributed the phase difference between the LASS $\pi\bar K$ amplitude and the E791 MIPWA amplitude to large destructive interference between isospin states rather than three-body rescattering. The results enable more reliable extraction of the CKM angle $\gamma/\phi_3$ from $B^\pm\to \tilde D^0 h^\pm$ decays followed by $\tilde D^0\to 3$ mesons, and motivate combined analyses of $D^+\to K^- \pi^+ \pi^+$ with $D^+\to K_S^0 \pi^0 \pi^+$ and $D^+\to K^- K^+ K^+$ to improve isospin separation and discriminate between ambiguous solutions.
