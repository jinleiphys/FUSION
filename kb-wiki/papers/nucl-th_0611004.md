# Phase transition in exotic nuclei along the $N=Z$ line

> The abrupt structure change from the nuclei of $N=Z \le 35$ to those of $N=Z \ge 36$ is investigated by means of shell model calculations. The basic features of the even-even and odd-odd nuclei under consideration are nicely reproduced. A sudden jump of nucleons into the upper $g_{9/2}d_{5/2}$ shell at $N=Z=36$ is found to be the main reason that causes the qualitative structure difference. It is argued that the structure change can be viewed as a decisive change of the mean field, or a phase transition, along the $N=Z$ line.

## Key claim

The abrupt structure change between N=Z ≤ 35 and N=Z ≥ 36 nuclei is caused by a sudden jump of approximately two protons and two neutrons into the upper g9/2d5/2 shell at N=Z=36, which can be viewed as a decisive change of the mean field or a phase transition, though the authors note that their truncated model space without the p3/2 orbit cannot perfectly reproduce all experimental data.

## Method

Shell model calculations were performed in a truncated f5/2p1/2g9/2d5/2 model space using an extended P+QQ Hamiltonian with adjusted effective interactions and monopole corrections. Occupation numbers in the lower (f5/2,p1/2) and upper (g9/2,d5/2) shells were analyzed for even-even nuclei 68Se, 72Kr, 76Sr and odd-odd nuclei 70Br, 74Rb to trace the microscopic origin of the structure change. For 76Sr, an extrapolation method was used due to the large model space.

## Key numbers

- Single-particle energies for the f5/2, p1/2, g9/2, and d5/2 orbits are 0.0, 0.3, 1.5 and 2.0 MeV, respectively
- Interaction strengths: g0 = 0.25(68/A), g2 = 0.16(68/A)^(5/3), χ2 = 0.14(68/A)^(5/3), χ3 = 0.04(68/A)^2 in MeV
- The ground state of 76Sr has the dominant configuration (fp)^(n-4)(gd)^4 with probability more than 0.7
- The ground state of 72Kr has the dominant configuration (fp)^(n-4)(gd)^4 with probability about 0.45
- For 72Kr, the calculated B(E2;0_1^+ → 2_1^+) value is 0.17 e^2b^2, about one third of the observed value ~0.5 e^2b^2
- For 68Se, the calculated B(E2;0_1^+ → 2_1^+) value is 0.054 e^2b^2
- Effective charges used: e_π = 1.5e and e_ν = 0.5e

## Context

This work extends previous shell model studies of shape phase transitions in medium-mass nuclei, particularly the Monte Carlo Shell Model calculation by Shimizu and the authors' earlier work on Ge isotopes (Hasegawa, 2005) which found a similar structure change near N=40. The results support the deformed mean field picture discussed by Nazarewicz (1985) and Kobayasi (2005) that there is a rapid change in deformation parameter β from ~0.25 in 68Se to ~0.4 in 72Kr and ~0.5 in 76Sr. The paper enables future studies using larger model spaces beyond pf5/2g9/2d5/2 to properly describe deformation and quantities sensitive to wave function details.
