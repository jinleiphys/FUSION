# Nuclear Moments and Electron Density Functionals in Atoms

> An electron density functional approach for the calculation of the nuclear multipole moments is presented. The electronic matrix elements entering the experimentally observed hyperfine electron-nucleus interaction constants in atoms are expressed in terms of the electron density functionals of the charge or spin distributions. In principle, the construction of the charge or spin distribution density functionals can be obtained by means of every relativistic or non-relativistic quantum-mechanical or DFT method. The electronic matrix elements for all the electronic operators of the existing Hamiltonians of hyperfine electron-nuclear interactions are considered.

## Key claim

The paper presents a formal framework for expressing the electronic matrix elements of hyperfine electron-nucleus interaction Hamiltonians in terms of charge or spin distribution density functionals, but it does not perform any actual numerical calculations or apply the method to specific atomic systems; the approach is purely theoretical and the density functionals themselves must be obtained from separate relativistic or non-relativistic quantum-mechanical or DFT calculations.

## Method

The authors use Racah algebra and irreducible tensor operator techniques to recouple the electronic matrix elements of multipole (electric and magnetic) and Breit-Pauli (dipole-dipole, orbital-spin, contact) hyperfine interaction operators from the \(LSJM_J\) coupling scheme to the \(LM_LSM_S\) coupling scheme. The resulting matrix elements are expressed as products of angular momentum coupling coefficients (\(3j\), \(6j\), or \(9j\) symbols) with spatial integrals involving either the charge distribution density functional \(\rho(K|\mathbf{r})\) or the normalized spin distribution density functional \(D_S(\mathbf{r})\). The formalism covers all electronic operators for the Hamiltonians of hyperfine electron-nucleus interactions, including the multipole expansion Hamiltonian \(\hat{\mathbf{H}}_k\) and the Breit-Pauli Hamiltonian \(\hat{\mathbf{H}}_{en}\).

## Key numbers

- The first three hyperfine interaction constants are connected to experimental values as: \(A_1 = IJA\), \(A_2 = 1/4B\), \(A_3 = C\), where \(A\), \(B\), and \(C\) are the dipole magnetic, quadrupole electric, and octopole magnetic interaction constants respectively.
- The nuclear moments for \(k=1,2,3\) are defined as: \(M_1 = \mu\) (magnetic dipole), \(Q_2 = Q/2\) (electric quadrupole), \(M_3 = -\Omega\) (magnetic octopole).
- The constant \(C\) in the dipole-dipole operator expression is equal to \(10^{1/2}\).
- The contact interaction matrix element involves the spin distribution density functional evaluated at the nucleus: \(D_S(\mathbf{r})|_{\mathbf{r}=0}\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of hyperfine interactions found in Abragam (1961), Ramsey, and Schwinger by providing a unified density-functional-based formalism for all electronic operators in the multipole and Breit-Pauli Hamiltonians. It builds on the density functional approach previously developed by Pavlov and co-workers for relativistic corrections. The formalism enables future numerical implementation using either relativistic ab-initio results (e.g., Tokman) or DFT calculations with relativistic corrections (e.g., Pernpointner, 1998), but the paper itself contains no numerical applications or comparisons to experimental data.
