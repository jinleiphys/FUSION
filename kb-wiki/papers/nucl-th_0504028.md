# Hadron Production and Phase Changes in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We study soft hadron production in relativistic heavy ion collisions in a wide range of reaction energy, 4.8 GeV <sqrt{s_ NN}<200 GeV, and make predictions about yields of particles using the statistical hadronization model. In fits to experimental data, we obtain both statistical parameters as well as physical properties of the hadron source. We identify the properties of the fireball at the critical energy threshold, 6.26 GeV < sqrt{s_NN}^cr} <7.61 GeV, delineating for higher energies hadronization of an entropy rich phase. In terms of the chemical composition, one sees a phase which at low energy is chemically under-saturated, and which turns into a chemically over-saturated state persisting up to the maximum accessible energy. Assuming that there is no change in physical mechanisms in the energy range 15>sqrt{s_NN} \ge 200 GeV, we use continuity of particle yields and statistical parameters to predict the hadron production at sqrt{s_NN}=62.4 GeV, and obtain total yields of hadrons at sqrt{s_NN}=130 GeV. We consider, in depth, the pattern we uncover within the hadronization condition, and discuss possible mechanisms associated with the identified rapid change in system properties at sqrt{s_NN ^cr}. We propose that the chemically over-saturated 2+1 flavor hadron matter system undergoes a 1st order phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a critical energy threshold between 6.26 and 7.61 GeV in center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair, below which the hadronizing fireball is chemically under-saturated and above which it becomes chemically over-saturated, and proposes that the chemically over-saturated 2+1 flavor hadron matter system undergoes a first-order phase transition.

## Method

The authors performed statistical hadronization model (SHM) fits to total and central rapidity hadron yields from AGS (11.6 AGeV), SPS (20, 30, 40, 80, 158 AGeV), and RHIC (62.4, 130, 200 GeV) heavy ion collisions, using the SHARE software package which includes the full hadron resonance mass spectrum and allows for chemical non-equilibrium via light and strange quark phase space occupancies (γ_q, γ_s). They fitted seven parameters (volume V, temperature T, quark fugacities λ_q and λ_s, occupancy parameters γ_q and γ_s, and isospin fugacity λ_I3) to between 9 and 12 data inputs per energy, with strangeness conservation enforced as a loose constraint allowing up to 10% deviation.

## Key numbers

- Critical energy threshold: 6.26 GeV < √s_NN^cr < 7.61 GeV, corresponding to 20 and 30 AGeV Pb beams on fixed target
- Light quark phase space occupancy γ_q shifts from 0.335 ± 0.006 at √s_NN = 4.84 GeV (AGS 11.6 AGeV) to 1.66 ± 0.10 at √s_NN = 7.61 GeV (SPS 30 AGeV)
- Strange quark phase space occupancy γ_s shifts from 0.190 ± 0.009 at √s_NN = 4.84 GeV to 1.84 ± 0.32 at √s_NN = 7.61 GeV
- Freeze-out temperature T drops from 157.8 ± 0.7 MeV at √s_NN = 4.84 GeV to 123.5 ± 3 MeV at √s_NN = 7.61 GeV, then rises to 136.4 ± 0.1 MeV at higher energies
- Baryochemical potential μ_B decreases steadily from 783 MeV at √s_NN = 4.84 GeV to 227 MeV at √s_NN = 17.27 GeV (SPS 158 AGeV)
- The K+/π+ ratio shows a "horn" feature across the critical energy range, with K+ yield at √s_NN = 7.61 GeV (30 AGeV) being 55.3 ± 4.4 and π+ yield 239 ± 17.7

## Context

This work extends prior statistical hadronization model analyses by Letessier and Rafelski (2004) at AGS energies and earlier SPS studies with fewer measurements, and addresses the "horn" feature in K+/π+ ratios discovered by the NA49 collaboration (Gazdzicki, 2004). The identification of a rapid change in γ_q and γ_s across the critical energy threshold provides evidence for a phase change in the fireball hadronization mechanism, enabling future searches for the onset of deconfinement and first-order phase transition signatures in heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies, particularly at the future Facility for Antiproton and Ion Research (FAIR) and Nuclotron-based Ion Collider fAcility (NICA).
