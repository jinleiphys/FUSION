# Neutron star crust in Voigt approximation: general symmetry of the stress-strain tensor and an universal estimate for the effective shear modulus

> I discuss elastic properties of neutron star crust in the framework of static Coulomb solid model when atomic nuclei are treated as non-vibrating point charges; electron screening is neglected. The results are also applicable for solidified white dwarf cores and other materials, which can be modeled as Coulomb solids (dusty plasma, trapped ions, etc.). I demonstrate that the Coulomb part of the stress-strain tensor has additional symmetry: contraction $B_{ijil}=0$. It does not depend on the structure (crystalline or amorphous) and composition. I show as a result of this symmetry the effective (Voigt averaged) shear modulus of the polycrystalline or amorphous matter to be equal to $-2/15$ of the Coulomb (Madelung) energy density at undeformed state. This result is general and exact within the model applied. Since the linear mixing rule and the ion sphere model are used, I can suggest a simple universal estimate for the effective shear modulus: $\sum_Z 0.12\, n_Z Z^{5/3}e^2 /a_\mathrm{e}$. Here summation is taken over ion species, $n_Z$ is number density of ions with charge $Ze$. Finally $a_\mathrm{e}=(4 πn_\mathrm{e}/3)^{-1/3}$ is electron sphere radius. Quasineutrality condition $n_\mathrm{e}=\sum_Z Z n_Z$ is assumed.

## Key claim

Within the static Coulomb solid model (neglecting ion vibrations and electron screening), the Coulomb part of the stress-strain tensor obeys the contraction \(B_{ijil}=0\) exactly for any structure and composition, which forces the Voigt-averaged effective shear modulus to equal \(-2/15\) of the Madelung energy density. This result is exact under the assumption of uniform deformation at the microphysical level, but the author notes that non-uniform deformation could violate this relation, so the derived estimate should be treated as an upper limit for the actual effective shear modulus.

## Method

The author derives the elastic tensors \(S_{ijkl}\) and \(B_{ijkl}\) from the Coulomb energy of a system of point ions on a uniform neutralizing background, expanding the energy change under uniform deformation to second order in the displacement gradient. By contracting the tensor before summation over ion pairs and integrals, the term \((3\Delta_i\Delta_k - \Delta^2\delta_{ik})\delta_{ik}\) vanishes identically, giving \(S_{ijil}=0\) and \(B_{ijil}=0\). The Voigt average (assuming uniform strain across all crystallites) preserves this contraction, and combining \(B_{ijij}^\mathrm{V}=0\) with the isotropic form of the stress-strain tensor yields \(\mu_\mathrm{eff}^\mathrm{V} = -(2/15)\,\epsilon^\mathrm{M}\). Using the linear mixing rule and the ion sphere model of Salpeter (1954) to estimate the Madelung energy, the author obtains a universal estimate \(\mu_\mathrm{eff}^\mathrm{V} \approx \sum_Z 0.12\, n_Z Z^{5/3}e^2 / a_\mathrm{e}\).

## Key numbers

* For a one-component bcc crystal, the exact Voigt-averaged shear modulus is \(\mu^\mathrm{V}_\mathrm{eff,\ bcc} = 0.119457234091 \, Z^{5/3} e^2 n_Z / a_\mathrm{e}\), agreeing with the coefficient 0.1194572 from Baiko (2011).
* The universal estimate \(\mu^\mathrm{V}_\mathrm{eff} \approx 0.12 \sum_Z Z^{5/3} e^2 n_Z / a_\mathrm{e}\) deviates from the accurate one-component bcc value by only 0.05%.
* The Madelung energy for a one-component bcc crystal is \(\epsilon^\mathrm{M} = -0.895929255682 \, Z^{5/3} e^2 n_Z / a_\mathrm{e}\) (Baiko et al. 2001).
* The linear mixing rule gives \(\epsilon^\mathrm{M} \approx -(9/10) \sum_Z Z^{5/3} e^2 n_Z / a_\mathrm{e}\).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of elastic properties for one-component bcc crystals (Fuchs 1936; Ogata and Ichimaru 1990; Baiko 2011, 2012) and multicomponent crystals (Kozhberov 2019) by proving a general symmetry that makes the Voigt-averaged shear modulus depend only on the Madelung energy, independent of structure or composition. The result supports earlier neutron star models based on one-component bcc shear moduli (e.g., Ogata and Ichimaru 1990; Kozhberov 2019) by showing those numerical values are valid for arbitrary microscopic crust structure. The universal estimate enables straightforward calculation of the effective shear modulus for polycrystalline or amorphous neutron star crusts and white dwarf cores, which is important for modeling torsional oscillations (e.g., Kuan and Yagi 2020) and crust failure in magnetar bursts and pulsar glitches.
