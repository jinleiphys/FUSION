# Understanding the proton radius puzzle: Nuclear structure effects in light muonic atoms

> We present calculations of nuclear structure effects to the Lamb shift in light muonic atoms. We adopt a modern ab-initio approach by combining state-of-the-art nuclear potentials with the hyperspherical harmonics method. Our calculations are instrumental to the determination of nuclear charge radii in the Lamb shift measurements, which will shed light on the proton radius puzzle.

## Key claim

The authors present *ab-initio* calculations of nuclear structure effects (specifically two-photon exchange contributions) to the Lamb shift in light muonic atoms, showing that the theoretical uncertainty in these effects is dominated by nuclear physics and must be controlled to a 5% accuracy for the nuclear charge radius of helium isotopes to be determined to a 3×10⁻⁴ accuracy, which is instrumental for resolving the proton radius puzzle.

## Method

The authors combine state-of-the-art nuclear potentials (Argonne v18 and chiral effective field theory potentials with various parameterizations) with the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics expansion (EIHH) method to solve the nuclear many-body problem, and the Lanczos sum rules (LSR) method to compute energy-dependent sum rules of nuclear response functions. They calculate the two-photon exchange (TPE) contribution to the Lamb shift, separating it into an elastic part (proportional to the third electric Zemach moment) and an inelastic part (nuclear polarizability effect), for muonic deuterium, muonic helium-4, muonic helium-3, and muonic hydrogen-3.

## Key numbers

- The total two-photon exchange contribution in μD is δ_TPE = -1.716 ± 0.020 meV, which is the agreed value adopted by the CREMA collaboration for extracting the deuteron charge radius.
- The total two-photon exchange contribution in μ⁴He⁺ is δ_TPE = -9.54 ± 0.34 meV, with the elastic part δ_Zem = -6.29 ± 0.28 meV and the nuclear polarizability part δ^nucl_pol = -2.36 ± 0.10 meV.
- The hadronic two-photon exchange contribution in μD is δ_TPE^hadr = -0.047 ± 0.010 keV (equivalent to -47 ± 10 keV).
- The hadronic polarizability contribution in μ⁴He⁺ is estimated as δ^hadr_pol ≈ -0.34 meV, and the hadronic Zemach contribution as δ^hadr_Zem ≈ -0.54 ± 0.12 meV.
- The uncertainty from potential variations in the μD calculation is 0.6%, based on comparisons between AV18 and multiple chiral EFT parameterizations.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of nuclear structure effects in muonic deuterium by Pachucki (2011) using AV18 potentials, and by the authors themselves for μ⁴He⁺ (Ji 2013), by including chiral effective field theory potentials and analyzing order-by-order chiral convergence and cutoff dependence. The calculations are directly relevant to the CREMA collaboration's measurements of Lamb shifts in μD, μ³He⁺, and μ⁴He⁺, which aim to extract nuclear charge radii with high accuracy and benchmark them against electronic atom spectroscopy (Parthey 2010, Rooij 2011, Cancio 2012) and electron-nucleus scattering data (MAMI 2012, Sick 2014). The results enable the determination of nuclear charge radii from future muonic atom measurements, potentially resolving the 7-standard-deviation discrepancy between the proton radius from muonic hydrogen and from electronic hydrogen spectroscopy or electron-proton scattering.
