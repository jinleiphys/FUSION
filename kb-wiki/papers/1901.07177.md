# Dynamical analysis of the $X$ resonance contributions to the decay $J/ψ\toγX\toγϕϕ$

> The dynamics of the $J^{PC}=0^{-+}$, $0^{++}$, and $2^{++}$ resonance contributions to the decay $J/ψ\toγX(J^{PC})\toγϕϕ$ is analysed using the data obtained by BESIII collaboration. The effective coupling constants parameterising invariant amplitudes of the transitions $J/ψ\toγX(J^{PC})$ and $X(J^{PC})\toϕϕ$ and masses of $X(J^{PC})$ resonances are found from the fits. They are used for evaluation of the branching fractions $B_{X(J^{PC})\toϕϕ}$, relative branching fractions $B_{J/ψ\toγX(J^{PC})\toγϕϕ}$, and for obtaining the photon angular distributions.

## Key claim

The paper reanalyzes BESIII data on $J/\psi\to\gamma\phi\phi$ using a dynamical model with energy-dependent partial widths and explicit spin-orbital momentum structures, rather than the simpler constant-width Breit-Wigner model used in the original BESIII analysis (BES16), to extract effective coupling constants and branching fractions for $J^{PC}=0^{-+}$, $0^{++}$, and $2^{++}$ resonance contributions.

## Method

The author constructs Lorentz-invariant amplitudes for the transitions $J/\psi\to\gamma X(J^{PC})$ and $X(J^{PC})\to\phi\phi$ parametrized by effective coupling constants, then converts them to three-dimensional forms accounting for the transverse photon polarization. The $\phi\phi$ mass spectrum data from BESIII (BES16) are fitted separately for each $J^{PC}$ sector using these amplitudes with energy-dependent partial widths, including mixing effects for the pseudoscalar case and multiple resonances where needed.

## Key numbers

- Pseudoscalar sector (model A with mixing): $m_{X_1(0^-)} = 2.2312 \pm 0.0015$ GeV ($\eta(2225)$), $m_{X_2(0^-)} = 2.0757 \pm 0.0025$ GeV ($\eta(2100)$), $m_{X_3(0^-)} = 2.6590 \pm 0.0028$ GeV ($X(2500)$); branching fraction $B_{X_1(0^-)\to\phi\phi} = (1.55 \pm 0.02) \times 10^{-2}$
- Pseudoscalar sector (model B without mixing): $m_{X_1(0^-)} = 2.252 \pm 0.002$ GeV, $m_{X_2(0^-)} = 2.077 \pm 0.002$ GeV, $m_{X_3(0^-)} = 2.705 \pm 0.003$ GeV; $B_{X_1(0^-)\to\phi\phi} = 0.218 \pm 0.003$
- Scalar sector (two-resonance fit): $m_{X_1(0^+)} = 2.190 \pm 0.009$ GeV, $m_{X_2(0^+)} = 2.409 \pm 0.010$ GeV; $B_{X_1(0^+)\to\phi\phi} = 0.70 \pm 0.04$, $B_{X_2(0^+)\to\phi\phi} = 0.86 \pm 0.19$
- Tensor sector (single resonance): $m_{X(2^+)} = 2.621 \pm 0.012$ GeV; $B_{X(2^+)\to\phi\phi} = 0.21 \pm 0.01$; $\chi^2/n_{\rm d.o.f.} = 19.7/24 \approx 0.8$
- Total pseudoscalar contribution $N^{(0^-)} = 710 \pm 13$ (model A) or $708 \pm 16$ (model B); scalar $N^{(0^+)} = 63 \pm 5$; tensor $N^{(2^+)} = 172 \pm 12$

## Context

This work extends the partial wave analysis of the $\phi\phi$ system from BESIII (BES16) by replacing the simple constant-width Breit-Wigner model with energy-dependent partial widths and explicit spin-orbital momentum structures, particularly important for the $2^{++}$ tensor contribution where multiple independent spin structures enter. The results enable future analyses with improved statistics to distinguish between mixing and non-mixing models for the pseudoscalar resonances $\eta(2225)$, $\eta(2100)$, and $X(2500)$, and to confirm or refute the existence of a second scalar resonance near 2.4 GeV. The effective resonance peak positions and widths agree within uncertainties with those reported in BES16 and the Particle Data Group (PDG), specifically identifying the tensor resonance with $f_2(2340)$.
