# A nuclear model with explicit mesons

> A nuclear model is proposed where the nucleons interact by emitting and absorbing mesons, and where the mesons are treated explicitly. A nucleus in this model finds itself in a quantum superposition of states with different number of mesons. Transitions between these states hold the nucleus together. The model---in its simplest incarnation---is applied to the deuteron, where the latter becomes a superposition of a neutron-proton state and a neutron-proton-meson state. Coupling between these states leads to an effective attraction between the nucleons and results in a bound state with negative energy, the deuteron. The model is able to reproduce the accepted values for the binding energy and the charge radius of the deuteron. The model, should it work in practice, has several potential advantages over the existing non-relativistic few-body nuclear models: the reduced number of model parameters, natural inclusion of few-body forces, and natural inclusion of mesonic physics.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a nuclear model in which nucleons interact by explicitly emitting and absorbing mesons, treating the mesons on the same footing as nucleons; the model is applied to the deuteron in a one-meson approximation and claims to reproduce accepted binding energy and charge radius values, but the authors note this is a "first conceptual trial" using only a scalar sigma-meson rather than pions.

## Method

The deuteron is treated as a quantum superposition of a two-body neutron-proton subsystem and a three-body neutron-proton-sigma-meson subsystem, coupled by a Gaussian-shaped operator with strength and range parameters. The coupled Schrodinger equation is solved numerically using the correlated Gaussian method, where wave-functions are expanded in stochastic Gaussian basis sets with non-linear parameters chosen via Van der Corput quasi-random sampling and linear parameters obtained from a generalized eigenvalue problem.

## Key numbers

- With sigma-meson mass m_sigma = 500 MeV, neutron and proton masses m_n = m_p = 939 MeV, coupling range b_sigma = 3 fm, and coupling strength S_sigma = 20.35 MeV, the deuteron ground-state energy is E_0 = -2.2 MeV and the charge radius is R_c = 2.1 fm.
- The energy converges within 1% with about 10 Gaussians in the two-body subsystem and about 25 Gaussians in the three-body subsystem; the final calculation used n^(d) = 25 and n^(sigma) = 80.
- The contribution of the three-body sigma-np subsystem to the norm of the total wave-function is only about 2%.

## Context

This work extends the meson-exchange paradigm of Yukawa (1935) by treating mesons explicitly rather than eliminating them in favor of phenomenological nucleon-nucleon potentials as done in standard non-relativistic few-body models such as the Bonn potential and the Argonne v18 potential. The model enables future inclusion of pions and natural emergence of few-body forces from few-meson states, though the authors note the computational cost increases substantially as an N-body problem becomes a coupled N plus N+1-body problem.
