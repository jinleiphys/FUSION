# Hard Photon Triggered Jets in $p$-$p$ and $A$-$A$ Collisions

> An investigation of high transverse momentum (high-$p_T$) photon triggered jets in proton-proton ($p$-$p$) and ion-ion ($A$-$A$) collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 0.2$ and $5.02~\mathrm{TeV}$ is carried out, using the multistage description of in-medium jet evolution. Monte Carlo simulations of hard scattering and energy loss in heavy-ion collisions are performed using parameters tuned in a previous study of the nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) for inclusive jets and high-$p_T$ hadrons. We obtain a good reproduction of the experimental data for photon triggered jet $R_{AA}$, as measured by the ATLAS detector, the distribution of the ratio of jet to photon $p_T$ ($X_{\rm J γ}$), measured by both CMS and ATLAS, and the photon-jet azimuthal correlation as measured by CMS. We obtain a moderate description of the photon triggered jet $I_{AA}$, as measured by STAR. A noticeable improvement in the comparison is observed when one goes beyond prompt photons and includes bremsstrahlung and decay photons, revealing their significance in certain kinematic regions, particularly at $X_{Jγ} > 1$. Moreover, azimuthal angle correlations demonstrate a notable impact of non-prompt photons on the distribution, emphasizing their role in accurately describing experimental results. This work highlights the success of the multistage model of jet modification to straightforwardly predict (this set of) photon triggered jet observables. This comparison, along with the role played by non-prompt photons, has important consequences on the inclusion of such observables in a future Bayesian analysis.

## Key claim

The multistage JETSCAPE model, with parameters tuned only to inclusive jet and hadron suppression data, successfully reproduces photon-triggered jet observables measured by ATLAS, CMS, and STAR, but only when non-prompt photons (bremsstrahlung and decay photons) are included alongside prompt photons; the description is "good" for ATLAS and CMS data but only "moderate" for the STAR \(I_{AA}\) measurement.

## Method

The JETSCAPE framework was used with the v3.5 AA22 tune, employing a multistage parton shower: MATTER for high-virtuality evolution (including photon radiation) and LBT for low-virtuality on-shell transport, with a switching virtuality \(Q_{\mathrm{sw}} = 2\) GeV. Initial hard scatterings were generated with PYTHIA 8 (ISR and MPI on, FSR off), using two setups: one with only prompt photon channels and one with full QCD 2-to-2 plus prompt photon channels to include bremsstrahlung photons. The background medium was provided by pre-generated (2+1)-D viscous hydrodynamic profiles from TRENTO initial conditions, and hadronic decays were restricted by turning off \(\pi^0\) decays.

## Key numbers

* Photon-triggered jet \(R_{AA}\) was computed at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV and compared to ATLAS data; the model reproduces the data when non-prompt photons are included.
* The \(X_{J\gamma}\) distribution (ratio of jet \(p_T\) to photon \(p_T\)) was compared to CMS and ATLAS data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV; non-prompt photons significantly affect the region \(X_{J\gamma} > 1\).
* The photon-jet azimuthal correlation was compared to CMS data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\) TeV; non-prompt photons notably impact the distribution.
* The photon-triggered jet \(I_{AA}\) was compared to STAR data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 0.2\) TeV; the description is "moderate."
* CMS smearing parameters used: \(C^2 = 0.0036\), \(S^2 = 1.5376\) GeV for both 0%–10% and 0%–30% centrality; \(N^2 = 70.8964\) GeV\(^2\) for 0%–10% and \(N^2 = 46.6489\) GeV\(^2\) for 0%–30%.
* Switching virtuality between MATTER and LBT was set to \(Q_{\mathrm{sw}} = 2\) GeV; virtuality cutoff for vacuum shower was \(Q_{\mathrm{min}}^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the physics surveys of the JETSCAPE collaboration (JETSCAPE 2022, 2023) from inclusive jets and leading hadrons to photon-triggered jet observables, using the same parameter tune (AA22) that was fitted only to single-inclusive \(R_{AA}\) data. It demonstrates that the multistage model can describe triggered observables without retuning, and highlights the importance of including non-prompt photons for accurate comparisons. The results motivate including photon-triggered jet observables in a future Bayesian analysis to further constrain the model parameters, following the approach used in earlier Bayesian extractions by the JETSCAPE collaboration (JETSCAPE 2021, 2024).
