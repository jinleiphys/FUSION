# Energy-momentum correlators of fermions at finite temperature and density

> Equal-time commutators of different components of the energy-momentum tensor at spatially separated points are calculated for a relativistic quantum Fermi gas at finite temperature and density. Different definitions of such components, also known as different pseudogauges, are used and smeared with a Gaussian profile characterized by the width $σ$. In this way, we introduce observables that may represent measurements of energy and momentum in a spatial region of size $σ$. We find that the obtained commutators are sensitive to the pseudogauge chosen if the probed systems or the spatial separation are small. The pseudogauge dependence is expected as different quantum operators are analyzed in this case. On the other hand, we find that for sufficiently large probed systems or with large separation, the studied commutators are pseudogauge independent.

## Key claim

Equal-time commutators of smeared energy-momentum tensor components for a relativistic quantum Fermi gas at finite temperature and density are pseudogauge-dependent for small probed systems or small spatial separation, but become pseudogauge-independent for sufficiently large probed systems or large separation, with the Belinfante pseudogauge yielding a vanishing commutator for the (00,0j) component.

## Method

The authors computed equal-time commutators of energy-momentum tensor components for a free Dirac fermion gas using the imaginary time formalism and spectral functions. They considered four pseudogauge choices, Canonical, Belinfante, de Groot–van Leeuwen–van Weert (GLW), and Hilgevoord–Wouthuysen (HW), and introduced Gaussian smearing with width σ to represent measurements over a finite spatial region. The commutators were evaluated analytically and expressed in terms of thermodynamic quantities (energy density, pressure, enthalpy density) and dimensionless parameters ξ = |y|/σ, λ = σT, z = m/T, and α = μ/T.

## Key numbers

- The canonical and HW pseudogauges yield identical commutators for the (00,0j) component: ⟨[T^W_C_00(t,y), T^W_C_0j(t,0)]⟩ = iξ_j e^{-ξ²/4} / [16(√π)³] × (1/σ⁴) × ℋ, where ℋ is the enthalpy density.
- The Belinfante pseudogauge gives a vanishing (00,0j) commutator: ⟨[T^W_B_00(t,y), T^W_B_0j(t,0)]⟩ = 0.
- The GLW pseudogauge adds a correction term proportional to 1/(2σm)² × (5/2 − ξ²/4) × (2ℰ − 5℘/3), where ℰ is energy density and ℘ is pressure.
- In the limit σ → ∞ (thermodynamic limit), all commutators vanish regardless of pseudogauge choice.
- For z = m/T = 0.1, 1, and 10, the normalized commutator magnitude decreases as z increases at fixed λ and ξ.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of energy-momentum tensor commutators in quantum field theory at finite temperature (Das 2020, Das 2021) by explicitly evaluating pseudogauge dependence for a fermionic system. The results are relevant for relativistic spin hydrodynamics, where pseudogauge freedom in defining energy-momentum and spin tensors (Hehl 1976, Leader 2013) affects the interpretation of hydrodynamic quantities. The finding that commutators vanish for large smearing widths supports the classical hydrodynamic limit, while the pseudogauge dependence at small scales identifies regimes where quantum effects and operator ambiguities matter for heavy-ion collision modeling.
