# Updated analysis of recent results on electron and positron elastic scattering on proton

> We discuss recent experimental results concerning the cross section ratio of positron over electron elastic scattering on protons, and compare with the predictions of a pre-existent calculation. The deviation from unity of this ratio, $i.e.$, a charge asymmetry different from zero, is the signature of contributions beyond the Born approximation. After reviewing the published results, we compare the elastic data to a calculation which includes the diagram corresponding to two-photon exchange. It turns out that all the data on the cross section ratio, in the limit of their precision, do not show evidence of enhanced two-photon contribution beyond the expected percent level. Our results confirm that experimental evidence for a large contribution of two-photon exchange is not yet found.

## Key claim

All recent data on the positron-over-electron elastic scattering cross section ratio on protons, within their precision, show no evidence of enhanced two-photon exchange contribution beyond the expected percent level, confirming that experimental evidence for a large two-photon exchange contribution has not yet been found.

## Method

The authors compiled and analyzed data from three recent experiments (VEPP-3, OLYMPUS, and CLAS) that measured the ratio of positron to electron elastic scattering cross sections on protons. They compared these experimental ratios to a pre-existing QED calculation (Kuraev et al., 2007) that includes the two-photon exchange diagram, after removing the soft radiative correction contributions using two different formalisms (Mo-Tsai and Maximon-Tjon) to isolate the hard two-photon exchange component.

## Key numbers

- The weighted average ratio R_{2γ} for all data combined is 0.999 ± 0.001, consistent with unity (no two-photon exchange contribution)
- For individual experiments: OLYMPUS average R_{2γ} = 0.999 ± 0.001; CLAS average R_{2γ} = 0.997 ± 0.002; VEPP-3 average R_{2γ} = 1.006 ± 0.002
- The VEPP-3 data show a deviation of about 3σ from unity with χ²/N = 7.9 for a constant fit, while OLYMPUS and CLAS show χ²/N near 1.0 and 1.1 respectively
- The global linear fit in ε gives R_{2γ} = 1.023 ± 0.005 + (-0.031 ± 0.006)ε for all data combined
- The global linear fit in Q² gives R_{2γ} = 0.981 ± 0.004 + (0.014 ± 0.003)Q² (in GeV²) for all data combined
- Most individual data points deviate from unity by less than 2%
- The point-by-point difference between theory and experiment shows discrepancies at the per-thousand level, with average differences of 0.003 ± 0.001 (Maximon-Tjon subtraction) and 0.002 ± 0.001 (Mo-Tsai subtraction)

## Context

This work extends the long-standing investigation of two-photon exchange contributions in elastic lepton-proton scattering, which became a focus of attention due to the discrepancy between Rosenbluth separation and polarization transfer measurements of proton form factors (Puckett et al., 2017; Pacetti et al., 2015). The analysis contradicts earlier suggestions from the VEPP-3 collaboration (Rachek et al., 2014) of a significant two-photon exchange effect increasing with Q², showing instead that the OLYMPUS data (Henderson et al., 2016) do not confirm such enhancement. The results enable future experiments to focus on higher Q² regions or different observables (such as polarization asymmetries) where two-photon exchange effects may become more detectable.
