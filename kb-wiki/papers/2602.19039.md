# Constraining the nuclear symmetry energy from electric dipole polarizability and neutron skin in $^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$ within antisymmetrized molecular dynamics

> The electric dipole polarizability $α_D$ and the neutron skin thickness $ΔR_{np}$ of $^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$ are two powerful and clean probes to constrain the symmetry energy at subsaturation density. Within the framework of the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) model, we found that the sensitive densities of $α_D$ and $ΔR_{np}$ ranges from the 0.2$ρ_0$ to 0.57$ρ_0$. To well describe the data of $α_D$ and $ΔR_{np}$, the effective interaction parameters with $S_0\sim34$ MeV and $L=66-75$ MeV are favored. The constraints of symmetry energy over the density ranging from 0.2$ρ_0$ to 0.57$ρ_0$ are obtained, and the values of symmetry energy at the starting and ending density region are $S(ρ=0.2ρ_0)=10.5\pm 0.63$ MeV and $S(ρ=0.57ρ_0)=23.1\pm 0.4$ MeV.

## Key claim

Within the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) model, the electric dipole polarizability and neutron skin thickness of 208Pb are sensitive to the symmetry energy over a density range of 0.2ρ0 to 0.57ρ0, and simultaneously describing both observables favors effective interaction parameters with S0 ≈ 34 MeV and L = 66–75 MeV, providing constraints that are conditional on the AMD framework and the specific Skyrme-type interactions used.

## Method

The authors employed the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) model, where the nuclear wave function is represented as a Slater determinant of Gaussian wave packets, preserving fermionic statistics and the Pauli principle. They used Skyrme-type effective interactions with an additional density-dependent term to independently vary the symmetry energy at saturation S0 and its slope L while keeping the incompressibility and effective masses fixed. The isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) strength function and electric dipole polarizability αD were calculated by applying an external dipole field perturbation and evolving the system via the time-dependent variational principle, with nucleon-nucleon collisions switched off.

## Key numbers

- The sensitive density range for both αD and ΔRnp in 208Pb is from 0.2ρ0 to 0.57ρ0.
- The favored symmetry energy parameters are S0 ≈ 34 MeV and L = 66–75 MeV.
- The symmetry energy at the lower end of the sensitive density region is S(ρ = 0.2ρ0) = 10.5 ± 0.63 MeV.
- The symmetry energy at the upper end of the sensitive density region is S(ρ = 0.57ρ0) = 23.1 ± 0.4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous constraints on the symmetry energy at subsaturation density obtained from random-phase approximation (RPA) calculations based on energy density functionals, such as those by Roca-Maza (2015) and Zhang (2015), which gave S(ρ0) = 30–35 MeV and S(ρ0/3) = 15.91 ± 0.99 MeV, respectively. It also addresses the tension between the PREX-II neutron skin measurement and the electric dipole polarizability data from RCNP, which only a few models (e.g., Reinhard (2021)) have been able to simultaneously describe. The AMD approach provides a transport-model-based framework that preserves fermionic antisymmetrization, offering an independent check on the robustness of symmetry energy constraints derived from mean-field and RPA methods.
