# Neutron stars exclude light dark baryons

> Exotic new particles carrying baryon number and with mass of order the nucleon mass have been proposed for various reasons including baryogenesis, dark matter, mirror worlds, and the neutron lifetime puzzle. We show that the existence of neutron stars with mass greater than 0.7 $M_\odot$ places severe constraints on such particles, requiring them to be heavier than 1.2 GeV or to have strongly repulsive self-interactions.

## Key claim

The existence of neutron stars with mass greater than 0.7 solar masses excludes any weakly interacting particle carrying baryon number with mass below about 1.2 GeV, unless it has strongly repulsive self-interactions. This constraint applies even if the particle's coupling to neutrons is as small as 10^{-12} GeV, because the high chemical potential inside neutron stars drives efficient conversion of neutrons into the exotic particle.

## Method

The authors solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for hybrid neutron star configurations containing both ordinary nuclear matter and a new Dirac fermion (χ) that carries baryon number. They use several nuclear equations of state (APR, soft, and maximally stiff) and add the contribution of χ as a free Fermi gas with chemical potential equal to the baryon chemical potential, assuming chemical equilibrium. The resulting mass-radius relations are compared to the observed masses of the heaviest neutron stars (around 2 solar masses).

## Key numbers

- Neutron stars with mass greater than 0.7 solar masses exclude a weakly interacting dark baryon with mass m_χ = 938 MeV; even the maximally stiff equation of state gives a maximum mass below 0.8 solar masses in this case.
- For m_χ = 1.2 GeV, the maximum mass is about 1.6 solar masses for the APR equation of state and about 2.2 solar masses for the maximally stiff equation of state.
- The lower bound on m_χ from nuclear stability is 937.90 MeV (from stability of ^9Be); the bound from proton stability is m_χ > m_p - m_e = 937.76 MeV.
- The neutron lifetime anomaly (bottle vs. beam measurements) would require a branching ratio Br_{n→χγ} = (0.9 ± 0.2) × 10^{-2}, which for Δm = 1 MeV requires a mixing angle θ ≈ 7 × 10^{-10} or a coupling δ ≈ 7 × 10^{-13} GeV.
- A recent reevaluation limits any nonstandard neutron branching to less than 2.7 × 10^{-3} at 95% confidence level (Czarnecki 2018).

## Context

This work extends constraints on exotic baryon-number-carrying particles (proposed for asymmetric dark matter, mirror worlds, baryogenesis, and the neutron lifetime puzzle) by using neutron star observations. Prior bounds from nuclear stability (e.g., ^9Be stability) gave m_χ > 937.90 MeV, but the neutron star constraint raises this to about 1.2 GeV for weakly interacting particles. The analysis uses the observed masses of the neutron stars J0348+0432 and J1614-2230 (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013) as benchmarks. The result enables future constraints on related scenarios such as hylogenesis (Davoudiasl 2010, 2011) and new bosons carrying baryon number, and it identifies repulsive self-interactions (as in mirror world models or dark matter self-interaction scenarios discussed by Tulin 2017) as the primary way to evade the bound.
