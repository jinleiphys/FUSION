# Non-Perturbative Mass Renormalization in Quenched QED from the Worldline Variational Approach

> Following Feynman's successful treatment of the polaron problem we apply the same variational principle to quenched QED in the worldline formulation. New features arise from the description of fermions by Grassmann trajectories, the supersymmetry between bosonic and fermionic variables and the much more singular structure of a renormalizable gauge theory like QED in 3+1 dimensions. We take as trial action a general retarded quadratic action both for the bosonic and fermionic degrees of freedom and derive the variational equations for the corresponding retardation functions. We find a simple analytic, non-perturbative, solution for the anomalous mass dimension gamma_m(alpha) in the MS scheme. For small couplings we compare our result with recent four-loop perturbative calculations while at large couplings we find that gamma_m(alpha) becomes proportional to (alpha)^(1/2). The anomalous mass dimension shows no obvious sign of the chiral symmetry breaking observed in calculations based on the use of Dyson-Schwinger equations, however we find that a perturbative expansion of gamma_m(alpha) diverges for alpha > 0.7934. Finally, we investigate the behaviour of gamma_m(alpha) at large orders in perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The authors apply Feynman's polaron variational method to quenched QED in the worldline formulation and derive a simple analytic, non-perturbative expression for the anomalous mass dimension \(\gamma_m(\alpha)\) in the MS scheme, valid for arbitrary coupling. However, this result shows no obvious sign of the chiral symmetry breaking observed in Dyson-Schwinger equation calculations, and the authors note that the calculation is restricted to the most divergent contributions as \(\epsilon \to 0\) and to the supersymmetric massless limit of the variational equations.

## Method

The authors integrate out the photon field in the worldline formulation of quenched QED, obtaining an effective action for the electron only. They then apply a variational principle based on Jensen's inequality, using a general retarded quadratic trial action for both bosonic (position) and fermionic (Grassmann) degrees of freedom, and derive variational equations for the corresponding retardation functions. By restricting to the supersymmetric limit where the bosonic and fermionic profile functions are equal and focusing only on the ultraviolet-divergent contributions, they obtain an implicit algebraic equation for \(\gamma_m(\alpha)\) without explicitly solving the full variational equations.

## Key numbers

- The perturbative expansion of the variational result gives \(\gamma_m^{\rm var}(\alpha) = \frac{3}{2} \frac{\alpha}{\pi} - \frac{9}{32} \pi^2 \left (\frac{\alpha}{\pi} \right )^3 + \frac{27}{32} \pi^2 \left (\frac{\alpha}{\pi} \right )^4 - \frac{243}{128} \pi^2 \left ( 1 - \frac{\pi^2}{20} \right ) \left (\frac{\alpha}{\pi} \right )^5 + \mathcal{O} (\alpha^6)\)
- The perturbative expansion of \(\gamma_m^{\rm var}(\alpha)\) diverges for \(\alpha > 0.7934\), which is the radius of convergence determined by a branch cut in the complex \(\alpha\) plane
- In the strong coupling limit, \(\gamma_m^{\rm var} (\alpha) \stackrel{\alpha \to \infty}{\longrightarrow} \frac{1}{4} \sqrt{6 \pi \alpha} - \frac{1}{2} + \mathcal{O} \left ( \frac{1}{\sqrt{\alpha}} \right )\)
- The variational result for the second-order coefficient \(b_{22}^{\rm var} = 9/32\) is correct, while \(b_{12}^{\rm var} = 0\) compared to the exact value \(b_{12} = -3/64\)
- The Dyson-Schwinger result in rainbow approximation gives \(\gamma_m^{\rm DS} = 1 - \sqrt{1 - 3 \alpha/\pi}\), which becomes complex above \(\alpha = \pi/3 = 1.047\)

## Context

This work extends Feynman's variational treatment of the polaron problem (Feynman, 1955) to a renormalizable gauge theory, following previous applications to scalar super-renormalizable models by the same authors (Alexandrou, Rosenfelder, and Schreiber, 1998). The variational result for \(\gamma_m(\alpha)\) is compared to four-loop perturbative calculations (Tarasov, 1982; van Ritbergen et al., 1997) and to non-perturbative results from Dyson-Schwinger equations in rainbow approximation (Miransky, 1985). The approach enables systematic calculation of corrections and could be extended to bound state problems or more general trial actions to test whether the analytic structure is robust.
