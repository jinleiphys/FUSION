# Parity violating pion electroproduction off the nucleon

> Parity violating (PV) contributions due to interference between $γ$ and $Z^0$ exchange are calculated for pion electroproduction off the nucleon. A phenomenological model with effective Lagrangians is used to determine the resulting asymmetry for the energy region between threshold and $Δ(1232)$ resonance. The $Δ$ resonance is treated as a Rarita-Schwinger field with phenomenological $N Δ$ transition currents. The background contributions are given by the usual Born terms using the pseudovector $πN$ Lagrangian. Numerical results for the asymmetry are presented.

## Key claim

Parity-violating asymmetries in pion electroproduction are calculated for the energy region between threshold and the Δ(1232) resonance, using a phenomenological model with effective Lagrangians; the asymmetry is found to be nearly constant as a function of excitation energy and grows linearly with momentum transfer, but the hadronic axial current contributions are suppressed to only 10–20% due to the value of the Weinberg angle.

## Method

The authors compute the parity-violating asymmetry arising from γ–Z⁰ interference in pion electroproduction off the nucleon, using a phenomenological model with effective Lagrangians. The nonresonant (background) contributions are described by Born terms with pseudovector πN coupling, while the Δ(1232) resonance is treated as a Rarita-Schwinger field with phenomenological NΔ transition currents and an on-shell propagator modified by a constant width. The weak neutral current is related to the electromagnetic current by decomposing quark currents according to strong isospin, and the invariant amplitudes are decomposed into multipoles following the formalism of Adler (1968).

## Key numbers

- The asymmetry for the proton is approximately A ≈ −10⁻⁴ Q²/GeV², where Q² = −q² is the squared four-momentum transfer.
- For the neutron, the asymmetry is about 80% of the proton value.
- The Δ resonance mass used is M = 1210 MeV, with a fitted width Γ = 85 MeV (close to the PDG value of 100 MeV for the imaginary part of the pole).
- The inclusive electromagnetic cross section is reproduced within about 5% over the energy region from threshold to about 400 MeV excitation energy.
- The asymmetry at the resonance peak (W = 1232 MeV) with incident electron energy εᵢ = 800 MeV is dominated by the Δ contribution; the authors reproduce the results of Nath et al. (1982) except for an overall scaling factor of about 90%.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of parity-violating asymmetries in pion electroproduction by Nath et al. (1982), Jones et al. (1980), and Cahn et al. (1978), which were based on production of stable Δ isobars, by including non-negligible background contributions that interfere coherently with the resonance. The authors compare with estimates by Ishankuliev et al. (1980) and Li et al. (1982), finding disagreement with the former by a factor of 1/2 (attributed to a wrong coupling constant) and reasonable agreement with the latter. The results enable future precision measurements at facilities like CEBAF, MAMI, and MIT/Bates to potentially extract the πN σ-term, though the authors note this will be difficult due to suppression of the hadronic axial current.
