# Pseudo-spin symmetry restoration and the in-medium balance between nuclear attractive and repulsive interactions

> The mechanism that restores the pseudo-spin symmetry (PSS) are investigated under the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approach, by focusing on the in-medium balance between nuclear attractive and repulsive interactions. It is illustrated that the modelings of both the equilibrium of nuclear dynamics and the in-medium effects can be essentially changed by the $ρ$-tensor coupling that play the role almost fully via the Fock terms, from which the model discrepancy on the PSS restoration is verified. Specifically, the largely different density-dependent behaviors of the isoscalar coupling strengths $g_σ$ and $g_ω$, deduced from the parametrization of the RHF Lagrangian PKA1, play an essential role in restoring the PSS of the high-$l'$ pseudo-spin doublets around the Fermi levels. Qualitatively, a guidance is provided for the modelings of both the equilibrium of nuclear dynamics and the in-medium effects via the PSS restoration.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the restoration of pseudo-spin symmetry (PSS) for high-\(l'\) pseudo-spin doublets near the Fermi level is governed by the in-medium balance between attractive and repulsive nuclear interactions, and that this balance is structurally altered by the inclusion of Fock terms, specifically the \(\rho\)-tensor coupling, in the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approach. The key condition for proper PSS restoration is that the density-dependent behaviors of the isoscalar coupling strengths \(g_\sigma\) and \(g_\omega\) must be sufficiently different from each other, contrary to the approximate proportionality \(g_\sigma(\rho_b)/g_\omega(\rho_b) \approx C_0\) found in many relativistic mean-field (RMF) models.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) and relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations for the doubly magic nucleus \(^{208}\)Pb, using the RHF Lagrangians PKA1 and PKO3, and the RMF Lagrangian DD-ME2. They analyzed the pseudo-spin orbital (PSO) splittings of proton pseudo-spin doublets as a function of pseudo-orbit \(l'\), and decomposed the contributions to the binding energy and to the PSO splittings from the kinetic term, the isoscalar \(\sigma\)-scalar and \(\omega\)-vector channels, and the isovector channels. To isolate the role of the \(\rho\)-tensor coupling and the density dependence of \(g_\sigma\) and \(g_\omega\), they constructed two tentative parametrizations based on PKA1: one with identical density dependence for \(g_\sigma\) and \(g_\omega\) (1.0\(\kappa_\rho\)), and one with the \(\rho\)-tensor coupling switched off (0.0\(\kappa_\rho\)).

## Key numbers

*   For the proton PS doublet \((\pi 2f_{7/2}, \pi 1h_{9/2})\) in \(^{208}\)Pb (\(l'=4\)), the PSO splitting given by PKA1 is consistent with the experimental value, while PKO3 and DD-ME2 give distinctly larger splittings, corresponding to a spurious shell closure at \(Z=92\).
*   The total binding energy of \(^{208}\)Pb is similar for all models: \(-1636.28\) MeV (PKA1), \(-1636.80\) MeV (PKO3), and \(-1637.39\) MeV (DD-ME2).
*   The contribution from the kinetic plus isoscalar \(\sigma\) and \(\omega\) channels (\(E_{\text{kin.}+\sigma+\omega}\)) to the binding energy of \(^{208}\)Pb is \(-795.09\) MeV for PKA1, \(-1781.19\) MeV for PKO3, and \(-2559.81\) MeV for DD-ME2.
*   The contribution from the isovector channels (\(E_{\rho+\pi}\)) to the binding energy of \(^{208}\)Pb is \(-1634.84\) MeV for PKA1, \(-648.75\) MeV for PKO3, and \(+100.27\) MeV for DD-ME2.
*   In the tentative parametrization 0.0\(\kappa_\rho\) (with \(\rho\)-tensor coupling switched off), the \(E_{\text{kin.}+\sigma+\omega}\) and \(E_{\rho+\pi}\) values for \(^{208}\)Pb become \(-1764.32\) MeV and \(-640.81\) MeV, respectively, closely resembling the PKO3 results.

## Context

This work extends the study of pseudo-spin symmetry (PSS) from the relativistic mean-field (RMF) framework, where the condition for exact PSS is \(S(r)+V(r)=0\) (Meng 1998, Meng 1999), to the relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approach. It directly addresses the long-standing problem of spurious shell closures at \(N\) or \(Z = 58\) and \(92\) that appear in conventional RMF models (Geng 2006) and in the RHF parametrizations PKO\(i\) (Long 2006, Long 2008). The paper shows that the RHF Lagrangian PKA1 (Long 2007), which includes the \(\rho\)-tensor coupling, eliminates these spurious shells and restores PSS for high-\(l'\) doublets, in agreement with experimental data (Grawa 2007, M.D. Sun 2017). The results provide a qualitative guide for modeling both the equilibrium of nuclear dynamics and in-medium effects, and they enable more reliable predictions of shell structure in heavy and superheavy nuclei, such as the predicted magicity at \(Z=126\) (Li 2014).
