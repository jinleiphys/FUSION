# New estimate for the time-dependent thermal nucleosynthesis of $^{180}$Ta$^m$

> We have made a new time-dependent calculation of the supernova production ratio of the long-lived isomeric state of $^{180}$Ta. Such a time-dependent solution is crucial for understanding the production and survival of this isotope. We include the explicit linking between the isomer and all known excited states.We have also calculated the properties of possible links to a conjectured excited state that might decrease the final isomer residual ratio. We find that the explicit time evolution of the synthesis of $^{180}$Ta using the available nuclear data avoids the overproduction relative to $^{138}$La for a $ν$-process neutrino temperature of 4 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a time-dependent calculation of the thermal nucleosynthesis of the long-lived isomer $^{180m}$Ta, which explicitly includes linking transitions between the isomer and all known excited states, resolves the previous overproduction of $^{180}$Ta relative to $^{138}$La in the $\nu$ process for a neutrino temperature of 4 MeV, provided the isomer residual population ratio is taken as 0.39.

## Method

The authors developed a time-dependent model for the population ratio between the ground-state structure and the isomeric structure of $^{180}$Ta, treating the two as independently thermalized nuclear species linked only by weak interband transitions. They applied this model using all known excited states up to 600 keV excitation energy and the 9 measured linking transition widths from Belic et al. (2002), solving the differential equation for the isomer population as a function of decreasing temperature with an exponential temperature profile (time constant $\tau = 1$ s). They then applied the calculated isomer residual ratio of 0.39 to the nucleosynthesis yields from Byelikov et al. (2007) to obtain the final $^{180m}$Ta production factors.

## Key numbers

- Isomer residual population ratio at freeze-out: $P_m/(P_m + P_{gs}) = 0.39 \pm 0.01$, based on the 9 observed linking transitions.
- For a hypothetical linking transition at 592 keV, the estimated width is $g_i/g_1 \Gamma_i = 0.003$ meV, giving an isomer ratio of 0.18.
- For a hypothetical linking transition at 800 keV, the estimated width is $g_i/g_1 \Gamma_i = 0.008$ meV, giving an isomer ratio of 0.30.
- With the isomer ratio of 0.39 and charged current neutrino reactions at $T_\nu = 4$ MeV, the production factor for $^{180m}$Ta relative to solar (normalized to $^{16}$O) is 1.218, compared to 3.123 for the total $^{180}$Ta (ground state plus isomer).
- The isomer population ratio $P_1^m$ is 0.85 to 0.94 during the transitional temperature region $T_9 = 0.44$ to 0.62.
- The calculated isomer ratio is almost identical for temperature timescales $\tau = 0.3$ to 3 s.

## Context

This work extends the $\nu$-process nucleosynthesis calculations of Heger et al. (2005) and Byelikov et al. (2007), who found that charged-current neutrino reactions at 4 MeV reproduced $^{138}$La but overproduced $^{180}$Ta. The authors resolve this overproduction by incorporating a time-dependent treatment of the isomer branching, which previous studies had noted was necessary but lacked sufficient data to implement. The result enables systematic studies of neutrino oscillation effects on both light and heavy element yields, as suggested by Heger et al. (2005) and Yoshida et al. (2006), and constrains the supernova neutrino energy spectrum.
