# On the rapidity dependence of the average transverse momentum in hadronic collisions

> The energy and rapidity dependence of the average transverse momentum $\langle p_T \rangle$ in $pp$ and $pA$ collisions at RHIC and LHC energies are estimated using the Colour Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism. We update previous predictions for the $p_T$ - spectra using the hybrid formalism of the CGC approach and two phenomenological models for the dipole - target scattering amplitude. We demonstrate that these models are able to describe the RHIC and LHC data for the hadron production in $pp$, $dAu$ and $pPb$ collisions at $p_T \le 20$ GeV. Moreover, we present our predictions for $\langle p_T \rangle$ and demonstrate that the ratio $\langle p_{T}(y)\rangle / \langle p_{T}(y = 0)\rangle$ decreases with the rapidity and has a behaviour similar to that predicted by hydrodynamical calculations.

## Key claim

The ratio of the average transverse momentum at a given rapidity to that at midrapidity, \(\langle p_{T}(y)\rangle / \langle p_{T}(y=0)\rangle\), decreases with rapidity in both \(pp\) and \(pPb\) collisions when using realistic CGC models that describe the measured \(p_T\) spectra, contradicting the earlier claim by Bzdak, Bautista, and Skokov (2015) that this ratio would grow with rapidity in the CGC approach and could therefore discriminate CGC from hydrodynamics.

## Method

The authors used the hybrid formalism of the Colour Glass Condensate (CGC) approach, which convolves projectile parton distribution functions (CTEQ5L), dipole-target scattering amplitudes in momentum space, and parton fragmentation functions (KKP) to compute the invariant yield of hadrons. They employed two phenomenological models for the dipole scattering amplitude: the DHJ model, which includes geometric scaling violations, and the BUW model, which satisfies geometric scaling. The free parameters of both models were refitted to ALICE data on charged-particle \(p_T\) spectra in \(pPb\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV, and the resulting models were then validated against LHC data for \(pp\) collisions at various energies and rapidities, as well as RHIC data for \(pp\) and \(dAu\) collisions.

## Key numbers

* The BUW model was refitted with parameters \(a = 2.0\), \(b = 125\), and \(\gamma_s = 0.74\); the DHJ model was refitted with \(d = 1.0\) and \(\gamma_s = 0.7\).
* Both models describe the ALICE \(pPb\) data at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) TeV for transverse momenta \(p_T \le 20\) GeV.
* For the BUW model in \(pp\) collisions, the \(K\)-factor values used were \(K = 2.47\) (0.9 TeV), \(2.07\) (2.76 TeV), and \(1.77\) (7 TeV) for ALICE data; for the DHJ model, the corresponding \(K\)-factors were \(K = 2.3\), \(1.85\), and \(1.6\).
* The ratio \(\langle p_{T}(y)\rangle / \langle p_{T}(0)\rangle\) in \(pPb\) collisions at LHC energies decreases with rapidity, showing a behaviour similar to that predicted by hydrodynamical calculations, in contrast to the growth predicted by the GBW model.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Bzdak, Bautista, and Skokov (2015), who used the simpler GBW model and neglected parton fragmentation, finding that the CGC predicted a growth of \(\langle p_T \rangle\) with rapidity that could distinguish CGC from hydrodynamics. The present study shows that using realistic dipole models (DHJ and BUW) that describe the measured \(p_T\) spectra, and including parton fragmentation, reverses this conclusion: the ratio decreases with rapidity, similar to hydrodynamical predictions. This enables future experimental measurements of the rapidity dependence of \(\langle p_T \rangle\) in \(pp\) and \(pPb\) collisions at the LHC to test the CGC formalism, but not to discriminate it from hydrodynamics as previously claimed.
