# The resolution to the problem of consistent large transverse momentum in TMDs

> Parametrizing TMD parton densities and fragmentation functions in ways that consistently match their large transverse momentum behavior in standard collinear factorization has remained notoriously difficult. We show how the problem is solved in a recently introduced set of steps for combining perturbative and nonperturbative transverse momentum in TMD factorization. Called a ``bottom-up'' approach in a previous article, here we call it a ``hadron structure oriented'' (HSO) approach to emphasize its focus on preserving a connection to the TMD parton model interpretation. We show that the associated consistency constraints improve considerably the agreement between parametrizations of TMD functions and their large-$k_T$ behavior, as calculated in collinear factorization. The procedure discussed herein will be important for guiding future extractions of TMD parton densities and fragmentation functions and for testing TMD factorization and universality. We illustrate the procedure with an application to semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) structure functions at an input scale $Q_0$, and we show that there is improved consistency between different methods of calculating at moderate transverse momentum. We end with a discussion of plans for future phenomenological applications.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the long-standing inconsistency between TMD parametrizations and their large-transverse-momentum behavior in collinear factorization can be resolved by imposing integral constraints that relate TMD functions to collinear PDFs and FFs at the input scale, specifically through the "hadron structure oriented" (HSO) approach that preserves a parton-model interpretation.

## Method
The authors construct TMD PDFs and FFs at an input scale Q0 using an additive structure that interpolates between a nonperturbative core (Gaussian or spectator-model motivated) and the fixed-order αs perturbative tail calculated from collinear factorization. They enforce consistency by normalizing the nonperturbative core via an integral relation (Equation cutoff_def) that ensures the transverse-momentum-integrated TMD matches the collinear PDF/FF, and they implement evolution using a Collins-Soper kernel parametrized with a nonperturbative mass parameter mK. The TMD term for SIDIS structure functions is then computed and compared to the large-qT asymptotic term from collinear factorization.

## Key numbers
- The input scale Q0 is between approximately 1 GeV and 4 GeV for SIDIS.
- The nonperturbative Collins-Soper kernel parameter mK appears in Equation K_param as the argument of a K0 Bessel function.
- The hard coefficient |H|^2_j (Equation hardpart) includes terms of order αs(μ)/(4π) with coefficients -16 + π^2/3 + 6 ln(Q^2/μ^2) - 2 ln^2(Q^2/μ^2).
- The matching region where nonperturbative and perturbative contributions overlap is stated as PhT ≈ 1.0 (GeV/c)^2, referencing COMPASS (2017).

## Context
This work extends the "bottom-up" approach introduced by Gonzalez-Hernandez, Rainaldi, and Rogers (2022) by applying it to SIDIS and explicitly demonstrating resolution of the mismatch between TMD terms and asymptotic terms previously documented by Boglione (2014), Nadolsky (1999), Echevarria (2018), Moffat (2019), and Bacchetta (2019). The approach enables future extractions of TMD PDFs and FFs that maintain consistency with collinear factorization, which is essential for interpreting data from the CEBAF 12 GeV program, a potential 24 GeV upgrade (Arrington 2021), and a future Electron-Ion Collider.
