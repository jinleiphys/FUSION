# Properties of hyperons in nuclear matter from chiral hyperon-nucleon interactions at next-to-next-to-leading order

> The $Λ$ and $Σ$ single-particle potentials in infinite nuclear matter are analyzed within a recently established chiral hyperon-nucleon ($YN$) interaction up to N$^2$LO in combination with an nucleon-nucleon interaction derived in the same scheme. The self-consistent Brueckner-Hartree-Fock method with the continuous choice of the single-particle potential is employed. It is found that the $Λ$ single-particle potential is comparable to the results achieved with the NLO $YN$ interaction from 2019. The resulting $Σ$ potential becomes more attractive compared to the previous NLO results due to the constraint from the recent $ΣN$ differential cross section data measured in the J-PARC E40 experiment. An estimate of the theoretical uncertainty of the single-particle potentials is provided in terms of the truncation error in the chiral expansion.

## Key claim

The Λ and Σ single-particle potentials in infinite nuclear matter are computed using a new chiral hyperon-nucleon (YN) interaction at next-to-next-to-leading order (N²LO) with semilocal momentum space regularization, and the Σ potential becomes more attractive than previous NLO results due to constraints from recent J-PARC E40 ΣN differential cross section data, though the theoretical uncertainty is estimated via truncation errors in the chiral expansion.

## Method

The self-consistent Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) method with the continuous choice of the single-particle potential was employed to solve the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the G-matrix in infinite nuclear matter. The chiral YN interaction up to N²LO from the Jülich-Bonn group was combined with the corresponding SMS N⁴LO⁺ nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential by Reinert et al. (2017). Partial waves up to total angular momentum J=5 were included, and the channel coupling ΛN-ΣN was taken into account in the coupled-channel equations.

## Key numbers

- Λ single-particle potential at zero momentum and nuclear matter saturation density (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³): ranges from -46 to -41 MeV for SMS N²LO potentials, compared to -39 to -34 MeV for SMS NLO potentials
- Λ single-particle potential for NLO19(500): -40.9 MeV at ρ₀; for NLO13(500): -33.1 MeV at ρ₀
- Σ single-particle potential becomes more attractive than previous NLO results due to constraints from J-PARC E40 ΣN differential cross section data
- Total S-wave contribution dominates U_Λ up to 2ρ₀, with S-wave contributions at ρ₀ for SMS N²LO(550)⁸ around -42.0 MeV
- P-wave contributions to U_Λ at ρ₀ for SMS N²LO(550)⁸ total -2.6 MeV, while for NLO13(500) total +1.5 MeV
- D-wave contributions become sizable at 2ρ₀ for N²LO potentials, reaching -4.4 MeV for SMS N²LO(550)⁸

## Context

This work extends the chiral YN interaction studies from NLO (NLO13 by Haidenbauer et al. 2013 and NLO19 by Haidenbauer et al. 2019) to N²LO, using the novel semilocal momentum space regularization scheme previously applied to NN interactions by Reinert et al. (2017). The new YN potential incorporates constraints from J-PARC E40 ΣN differential cross section measurements (J-PARC E40 collaboration 2021, 2022) and has been shown to describe separation energies of light Λ hypernuclei (Le et al. 2024). The results enable improved predictions for hyperon properties in neutron stars relevant to the "hyperon puzzle" and provide the first truncation error estimate for hyperon single-particle potentials in nuclear matter.
