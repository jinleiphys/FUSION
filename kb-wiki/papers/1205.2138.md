# Fission barriers in covariant density functional theory: extrapolation to superheavy nuclei

> Systematic calculations of fission barriers allowing for triaxial deformation are performed for even-even superheavy nuclei with charge number $Z=112-120$ using three classes of covariant density functional models. The softness of nuclei in the triaxial plane leads to an emergence of several competing fission pathes in the region of the inner fission barrier in some of these nuclei. The outer fission barriers are considerably affected by triaxiality and octupole deformation. General trends of the evolution of the inner and the outer fission barrier heights are discussed as a function of the particle numbers.

## Key claim

Systematic calculations of fission barriers in superheavy nuclei with charge number Z=112-120 show that triaxial deformation and octupole deformation significantly affect the outer fission barriers, while the inner fission barriers are influenced by the softness of nuclei in the triaxial plane leading to multiple competing fission paths; however, the authors note that the average deviation from experiment for inner fission barriers in actinides (0.76 MeV) provides a benchmark for extrapolation, but no direct experimental comparison exists for the superheavy region studied.

## Method

The authors performed triaxial relativistic mean field (RMF) plus BCS calculations using three classes of covariant density functional models: nonlinear meson-nucleon coupling (NL3*), density-dependent meson-exchange (DD-ME2), and density-dependent point coupling (DD-PC1). Potential energy surfaces were computed with constraints on axial quadrupole (Q20), triaxial quadrupole (Q22), and octupole (Q30) moments, using the Broyden method and quadratic constraints for successive diagonalizations. The pairing channel was treated with monopole pairing forces in the BCS approximation, with strength parameters fitted to empirical gap formulas for the Z=112-126, N-Z=48-62 region.

## Key numbers

* The average deviation from experiment for calculated inner fission barrier heights in actinides using the same CDFT method was 0.76 MeV (from Ref. AAR.10)
* Empirical pairing gap parameters used: Δ_n^emp = 4.8/N^(1/3) MeV and Δ_p^emp = 4.8/Z^(1/3) MeV
* The cutoff energy for the pairing window was set to E_cutoff = 120 MeV
* The basis truncation included N_F=20 fermionic shells and N_B=20 bosonic shells
* The energy difference between neighboring equipotential lines in the potential energy surface plots is 0.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the systematic triaxial CDFT study of inner fission barriers in actinides by Abusara, Afanasjev, and Ring (2010) to the superheavy region (Z=112-120), filling a gap where only a single nucleus (264Hs) had been studied with triaxiality in CDFT (Bender et al., 1998). The results enable comparison with macroscopic-microscopic methods (Moller et al., 2009; Dobrowolski et al., 2007), Skyrme DFT (Staszczak et al., 2006), and Gogny DFT (Delaroche et al., 2006) for understanding the stability of superheavy nuclei against spontaneous fission and the location of the island of stability.
