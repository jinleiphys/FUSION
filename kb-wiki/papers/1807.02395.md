# Side-Jump Induced Spin-Orbit Interaction of Chiral Fluids from Kinetic Theory

> We apply the Wigner-function approach and chiral kinetic theory to investigate the angular momentum and polarization of chiral fluids composed of Weyl fermions with background electric/magnetic fields and vorticity. It is found that the quantum corrections in Wigner functions give rise to nonzero anti-symmetric components in the canonical energy-momentum tensors, which are responsible for the spin-orbit interaction. In global equilibrium, conservation of the canonical angular momentum reveals the cancellation between the orbital component stemming from side jumps with nonzero vorticity and the spin component in the presence of an axial chemical potential. We further analyze the conservation laws near local equilibrium. It turns out that the canonical angular momentum is no longer conserved even in the absence of background fields due to the presence of a local torque coming from the spin-orbit interaction involving temperature/chemical-potential gradients, which is implicitly led by collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in chiral fluids composed of Weyl fermions, the canonical angular momentum is not conserved near local equilibrium even in the absence of background fields, due to a local torque from spin-orbit interaction involving temperature or chemical-potential gradients that is implicitly induced by collisions; this non-conservation is absent in global equilibrium, where the orbital component from side jumps with nonzero vorticity cancels the spin component in the presence of an axial chemical potential.

## Method

The author applies the Wigner-function formalism and chiral kinetic theory (CKT) up to order \(\hbar\) to compute the canonical and Belinfante energy-momentum and angular-momentum tensor densities for chiral fluids composed of Weyl fermions. The Wigner functions for right- and left-handed fermions in global equilibrium are derived perturbatively from Kadanoff-Baym-like equations, incorporating side-jump terms and magnetic-field corrections, and are then used to evaluate the symmetric and anti-symmetric energy-momentum tensors, charge currents, and angular-momentum tensors. The conservation laws are analyzed both in global equilibrium (with constant temperature, chemical potentials, magnetic fields, and vorticity) and near local equilibrium, where collisions and gradients are present.

## Key numbers

- The anti-symmetric energy-momentum tensor for right-handed fermions in global equilibrium is \(T^{\mu\nu}_{AR\text{geq}} = -\frac{\hbar}{2} \xi_{\omega R} (u^\mu \omega^\nu - u^\nu \omega^\mu)\), where \(\xi_{\omega R} = \frac{T^3}{6} \left( \bar{\mu}_R + \frac{\bar{\mu}_R^3}{\pi^2} \right)\) and \(\bar{\mu}_R = \mu_R / T\).
- The transport coefficient for the chiral vortical effect (CVE) for right-handed fermions is \(\sigma_{\omega R} = \frac{T^2}{12} \left( 1 + \frac{3\bar{\mu}_R^2}{\pi^2} \right)\).
- The transport coefficient for the chiral magnetic effect (CME) for right-handed fermions is \(\sigma_{BR} = \frac{\mu_R}{4\pi^2}\).
- The axial-charge current in global equilibrium is \(J^{\mu}_{5\text{geq}} = N_5 u^\mu + \hbar \sigma_{B5} B^\mu + \hbar \sigma_{\omega 5} \omega^\mu\), with \(N_5 = \frac{\mu_5}{6} \left( T^2 + \frac{3\mu_V^2 + \mu_5^2}{4\pi^2} \right)\), \(\sigma_{B5} = \frac{\mu_V}{4\pi^2}\), and \(\sigma_{\omega 5} = \frac{T^2}{6} + \frac{\mu_V^2 + \mu_5^2}{8\pi^2}\).
- The total anti-symmetric energy-momentum tensor for both chiralities in global equilibrium is \(T_{A\text{geq}}^{\mu\nu} = -\frac{\hbar}{2} N_5 (\omega^\nu u^\mu - \omega^\mu u^\nu)\), where \(N_5 = \xi_{\omega V}\) is the axial-charge density.

## Context

This work extends the chiral kinetic theory framework developed by Hidaka, Pu, and Yang (2016-2018) to analyze angular-momentum conservation and spin-orbit coupling in chiral fluids, building on earlier studies of anomalous transport such as the chiral magnetic and vortical effects (CME/CVE) by Kharzeev (2007), Fukushima (2008), and Landsteiner (2011). It also connects to the study of spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the global polarization of \(\Lambda\) hyperons observed by STAR (2017) and the statistical-hydrodynamic model of Becattini (2013). The results enable further investigation of the spacetime evolution of polarization for Weyl fermions and the role of collisions in non-equilibrium chiral fluids, with potential applications to quark-gluon plasmas and Weyl semimetals.
