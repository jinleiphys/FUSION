# Inclusive Scattering of Polarized Electrons on Polarized 3He Effects of Final State Interaction and the Magnetic Form Factor of the Neutron

> Effects of final state interaction on asymmetries in inclusive scattering of polarized electrons on polarized 3He are investigated using consistent 3He bound state wave function and 3N continuum scattering states. Significant effects are found, which influence the extraction of the magnetic neutron form factor from A_T'. The enhancement found experimentally for A_TL' near the 3N breakup threshold, which could not be explained in calculations carried through in plane wave impulse approximation up to now, occurs now also in theory if the full final state interaction is included.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including full final-state interactions (FSI) among the three nucleons in inclusive polarized electron-\(^3\)He scattering significantly modifies the asymmetries \(A_{T'}\) and \(A_{TL'}\), and that the previously unexplained experimental enhancement of \(A_{TL'}\) near the three-nucleon breakup threshold can now be reproduced theoretically when FSI is included, contrary to plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) calculations which failed to do so.

## Method

The authors solved the three-nucleon (3N) Faddeev equations exactly using realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) forces (the Bonn B potential) to generate both the \(^3\)He bound-state wave function and the 3N continuum scattering states. They then computed the polarized inclusive cross section and the spin-dependent structure functions \(R_{T'}\) and \(R_{TL'}\) by contracting the electron and hadronic tensors, using a nonrelativistic framework with a single-nucleon current operator. The results were compared to the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIAS) and to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The principal S-state approximation accounts for about 92% of the \(^3\)He wave function norm (using the Bonn B potential).
- The convection-current correction factor \(\alpha(\omega, |\vec Q|)\) in \(R_T\) is typically of order \(10^{-3}\) and is negligible relative to the magnetic term at the momentum transfers considered.
- The transverse asymmetry \(A_{T'}\) (for \(\theta^* = 0^\circ\)) is proportional to \((G_M^{(n)})^2\) in the simplified PWIAS model.
- The transverse-longitudinal asymmetry \(A_{TL'}\) (for \(\theta^* = 90^\circ\)) is proportional to \(F_1^{(n)} G_M^{(n)}\) in the simplified PWIAS model.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by the same group (Ishikawa et al., 1998; Golak et al., 1995) on elastic and inelastic electron scattering on \(^3\)He, where FSI was shown to play a significant role. It directly addresses the failure of previous PWIA calculations (e.g., by Blankleider and Woloshyn, 1984; or by Ciofi degli Atti et al., 1993) to explain the experimentally observed enhancement of \(A_{TL'}\) near the 3N breakup threshold. The results enable more reliable extraction of the neutron magnetic form factor \(G_M^{(n)}\) from polarized \(^3\)He experiments by providing a theoretical framework that includes FSI consistently.
