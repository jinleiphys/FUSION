# Universality in the Near-Side Energy-Energy Correlator

> We investigate the energy-energy correlator (EEC) of hadrons produced on the same side in $e^+e^-$ annihilation or in leading jets in $pp$ collisions. We observe a remarkable universality of the correlator. Using a non-perturbative transverse momentum dependent (TMD) fragmentation function to model the transition from the ``free-hadron" region to the perturbative collinear region, we are able to describe the near-side shapes and peaks over a wide range of energy for both the $e^+e^-$ annihilation and the $pp$ jet substructure measurements in terms of just two parameters. We present further predictions for the ratio of the projected three-point energy correlator to the EEC. The excellent agreement between our calculations and the experimental data may provide new insights into the role of non-perturbative physics for EECs, and suggests the possibility of exploring non-perturbative TMDs using theoretical tools developed for the energy correlators.

## Key claim
The authors propose that the near-side (small-angle) energy-energy correlator (EEC) can be described by a universal non-perturbative transverse momentum dependent (TMD) fragmentation function, with only two free parameters, across a wide range of collision energies in both e+e- annihilation and pp jet substructure measurements. The hedge is that this model is an effective framework inspired by previous discussions of non-perturbative contributions, not a rigorous derivation from first principles.

## Method
The authors model the near-side EEC by expressing it in terms of a non-perturbative TMD fragmentation function, parameterized via the Collins-Soper kernel with a scale-dependent term. They fix most parameters (g1 = 0.042 GeV^2, g2 = 0.84, mu0 = 1.55 GeV, bmax = 1.5 GeV^-1) from semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) data following Sun (2014), leaving only the normalization N and energy fraction c as free parameters determined by fitting the quark-jet EEC at Q = 300 GeV. The model is then compared to Pythia simulations and experimental data from OPAL, CMS, and ALICE.

## Key numbers
- g1 = 0.042 GeV^2, g2 = 0.84, mu0 = 1.55 GeV, bmax = 1.5 GeV^-1: parameters taken from SIDIS fits by Sun (2014) for the non-perturbative TMD model.
- N = 0.8670 and c = 0.346: the two free parameters determined by fitting the quark-jet EEC distribution at Q = 300 GeV.
- The ratio E3C/EEC as chi approaches 0 is predicted to be 2c approximately 0.7, which agrees with CMS data at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV.
- For pp collisions, gluon jet fractions range from 0.82 (p_T 97-220 GeV) to 0.64 (p_T 1410-1784 GeV), with corresponding quark fractions from 0.18 to 0.36.

## Context
This work extends the study of energy-energy correlators (EECs) from the perturbative regime into the non-perturbative small-angle region, which has been previously addressed only with simple parameterizations (Andres 2024) or kinematic cutoffs (Yang 2023). The model connects the near-side EEC to the Collins-Soper kernel and TMD fragmentation functions, which have been studied in SIDIS by Sun (2014) and others. The results enable future exploration of non-perturbative TMDs using energy correlator techniques, and suggest applications to nucleon energy correlators (NEEC) at facilities like HERA, CEBAF, and future electron-ion colliders.
