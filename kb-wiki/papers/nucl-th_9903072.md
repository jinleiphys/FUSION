# Particle Freeze-out in Self-Consistent Relativistic Hydrodynamics

> The particle emission in relativistic hydrodynamic model is formulated assuming a sharp 3-dimensional space-time freeze-out hypersurface. The boundary conditions correspond to the energy-momentum and charge conservation between fluid and the gas of free particles. The emission and possible feedback of particles are included into the self-consistent hydrodynamic scheme. For the time-like parts of the freeze-out hypersurface the obtained results are different from the well known Cooper-Frye formula and lead to the shock-like freeze-out. A simple-wave hydrodynamic solution is considered in detail to illustrate some aspects of new freeze-out procedure.

## Key claim

The paper derives a self-consistent freeze-out procedure for relativistic hydrodynamics that replaces the standard Cooper-Frye formula for time-like parts of the freeze-out hypersurface, leading to a shock-like freeze-out; the authors explicitly note that for time-like freeze-out hypersurfaces their results differ from the well-known Cooper-Frye formula (Cooper and Frye, 1974) and that previous expressions for the particle flow number in the work of Csernai, Lazar and Molnar (1997) were missing terms proportional to the step functions.

## Method

The authors formulate particle emission from a sharp three-dimensional space-time freeze-out hypersurface by imposing energy-momentum and charge conservation between the fluid and the gas of free particles, including particle emission and possible feedback in a self-consistent hydrodynamic scheme. They calculate the moments of the cut-off distribution function for a relativistic massive Boltzmann gas in the fluid rest frame, performing angular and momentum integrations over the hemisphere defined by the freeze-out hypersurface normal vector. For the massless gas case, they derive closed-form expressions for the particle flow number and energy-momentum tensor components, and compute the Eckart and Landau-Lifshitz velocities to compare the two definitions.

## Key numbers

- For the massless gas, the Eckart velocity for the left hemisphere is \(v_{E,L} = (v_s - 1)/2\) and for the right hemisphere \(v_{E,R} = (v_s + 1)/2\), where \(v_s\) is the freeze-out hypersurface velocity.
- For the massless gas, the proper particle number density in the Eckart frame for the left hemisphere is \(n_{E,L} = n_g(T,\mu_c) \frac{1+v_s}{4} \sqrt{(1+v_s)(3-v_s)}\).
- In the limit \(\Delta \equiv 1 - v_s \ll 1\) for the left hemisphere, the Landau-Lifshitz velocity is \(v_{LL,L} = (3/8)(v_s - 1) + o(\Delta^2)\), while the Eckart velocity is \(v_{E,L} = (v_s - 1)/2\), showing the Eckart velocity has larger magnitude.
- The Eckart and Landau-Lifshitz velocities coincide only at \(\Delta = 0\) for the right hemisphere case.
- In the Landau-Lifshitz rest frame for the massless gas, the pressure in the X direction is always smaller than in the Y or Z direction for physical values \(0 < \Delta < 2\).

## Context

This work extends the cut-off distribution function approach introduced by Bugaev (1996) to correct the Cooper-Frye formula (Cooper and Frye, 1974) for time-like freeze-out hypersurfaces, where the standard formula produces unphysical negative particle number contributions. The paper provides explicit analytic expressions for the moments of the cut-off distribution function that were missing in prior work by Csernai, Lazar and Molnar (1997), and demonstrates that for time-like freeze-out hypersurfaces the emission leads to a shock-like freeze-out. The results enable more accurate numerical studies of particle spectra in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, particularly for the description of nucleons and baryon resonances, and provide the foundation for comparing Eckart and Landau-Lifshitz hydrodynamic frames in freeze-out calculations.
