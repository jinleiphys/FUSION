# Quark-meson coupling model with the cloudy bag

> Using the volume coupling version of the cloudy bag model, the quark-meson coupling model is extended to study the role of pion field and the properties of nuclear matter. The extended model includes the effect of gluon exchange as well as the pion-cloud effect, and provides a good description of the nuclear matter properties. The relationship between the extended model and the EFT approach to nuclear matter is also discussed.

## Key claim
The paper extends the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model by incorporating the volume coupling version of the cloudy bag model (CBM) to include pion-cloud and gluon-exchange effects, claiming that this "chiral QMC" model provides a good description of nuclear matter properties while the abstract glosses over the condition that the model's success depends on a closure approximation for intermediate baryon states (restricted to nucleon and delta) and a phenomenological form factor to prevent bag collapse at small radii.

## Method
The authors start from the volume coupling version of the CBM Lagrangian in flavor SU(2), linearize the pion field to keep terms of order 1/f_pi, and add gluon fields. They calculate second-order energy corrections to the nucleon and delta masses using quark and pion propagators, separating direct (Hartree) and non-direct (exchange plus self-energy) contributions, with intermediate baryon states restricted to the lowest mode (nucleon and delta). A phenomenological form factor based on the pion charge radius (0.56 fm) is introduced to regularize the pion-loop contributions at small bag radii, and the running strong coupling constant alpha_s is parametrized as a function of bag radius. The model is then extended to nuclear matter by adding sigma and omega mesons in a mean-field approximation, solving coupled nonlinear equations self-consistently to obtain the effective nucleon mass and saturation properties.

## Key numbers
- Free nucleon mass M_N = 939 MeV fitted with bag radius R_N = 0.6 or 0.8 fm
- Delta mass M_Delta = 1232 MeV used to fix the gluon parameter a_1
- Bag constant B^{1/4} = 231.8 MeV (for R_N = 0.6 fm) or 183.7 MeV (for R_N = 0.8 fm)
- Pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV; pion mass m_pi = 138 MeV; current quark mass m = 5 MeV
- Pion charge radius = 0.56 fm; form factor parameter b = 0.46 fm
- N-Delta mass difference from pion cloud is about 60 MeV for R_N = 0.8 fm
- At saturation density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}: effective nucleon mass M_N* = 632 MeV (R_N = 0.6 fm) or 647 MeV (R_N = 0.8 fm); nuclear incompressibility K = 362 MeV (R_N = 0.6 fm) or 365 MeV (R_N = 0.8 fm)
- At rho_0: bag radius swells by 1-2%; quark eigenvalue decreases by about 20%; nucleon rms radius swells by about 6%
- Axial vector coupling constant g_A = 1.15 in free space; reduction of about 14% at rho_0
- Sigma-N coupling g_sigma^2/4pi = 6.11 (R_N = 0.6 fm) or 4.93 (R_N = 0.8 fm); omega-N coupling g_omega^2/4pi = 10.20 (R_N = 0.6 fm) or 9.59 (R_N = 0.8 fm)

## Context
This work extends the original QMC model (developed over the past few decades) by incorporating chiral symmetry through the cloudy bag model of Thomas (1981), including both pion-cloud and gluon-exchange effects that were absent in earlier QMC formulations. The model provides a framework to study the medium modification of nucleon structure, which was observed in polarization transfer measurements at Jefferson Lab (JLab) and is relevant to the nuclear EMC effect. The authors discuss the relationship with effective field theory (EFT) approaches to nuclear matter, noting that recent N^3LO EFT calculations by Li et al. produce overbinding at large density, and suggest that the density-dependent weakening of the isoscalar central force predicted by their model may be favorable; future work should include the Weinberg-Tomozawa term at order 1/f_pi^2 and compare with EFT to investigate nucleon structure change in medium.
