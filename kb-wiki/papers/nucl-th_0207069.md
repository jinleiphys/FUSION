# Vector meson production and nucleon resonance analysis in a coupled-channel approach for energies m_N < sqrt(s) < 2 GeV II: photon-induced results

> We present a nucleon resonance analysis by simultaneously considering all pion- and photon-induced experimental data on the final states gamma N, pi N, 2 pi N, eta N, K Lambda, K Sigma, and omega N for energies from the nucleon mass up to sqrt(s) = 2 GeV. In this analysis we find strong evidence for the resonances P_{31}(1750), P_{13}(1900), P_{33}(1920), and D_{13}(1950). The omega N production mechanism is dominated by large P_{11}(1710) and P_{13}(1900) contributions. In this second part we present the results on the photoproduction reactions and the electromagnetic properties of the resonances. The inclusion of all important final states up to sqrt(s) = 2 GeV allows for estimates on the importance of the individual states for the GDH sum rule.

## Key claim

The paper claims strong evidence for the resonances P31(1750), P13(1900), P33(1920), and D13(1950) from a simultaneous coupled-channel analysis of all pion- and photon-induced data up to sqrt(s) = 2 GeV, but this evidence is conditional on the specific choice of form factor F_p for all vertices, as the alternative form factor F_t failed to produce satisfactory global fits.

## Method

The authors extended a unitary coupled-channel effective Lagrangian model (the Giessen model) to include the final states gamma N, pi N, 2pi N, eta N, K Lambda, K Sigma, and omega N up to sqrt(s) = 2 GeV. They solved the K-matrix equation for hadronic states only, then extracted photoproduction amplitudes perturbatively via the K-matrix formalism, using the same Lagrangians for both pion- and photon-induced reactions. The model incorporated s-channel, u-channel, and t-channel Born contributions, resonance electromagnetic decays, and intermediate vector meson exchanges, with hadronic form factors applied at vertices and a crossing-symmetric form factor to preserve gauge invariance in photoproduction.

## Key numbers

* Total chi-squared of 6.57 per data point for the preferred global fit C-p-gamma+ across all pion- and photon-induced channels up to sqrt(s) = 2 GeV, with 4400 photoproduction plus 2400 pion-induced data points binned into 96 energy intervals.
* Excluding pion photoproduction multipoles (which comprise 40% of all data points), the total chi-squared per data point reduces from 6.56 to 3.87 for the preferred global fit.
* For the preferred fit C-p-gamma+, the chi-squared values per channel include: gamma N -> pi N = 10.50, gamma N -> eta N = 2.45, gamma N -> K Lambda = 3.95, gamma N -> K Sigma = 2.74, gamma N -> omega N = 6.25, and gamma p -> gamma p (Compton) = 5.30.
* The Compton scattering data base was doubled from 266 to 538 data points compared to the prior analysis of Feuster and Mosel (1999).
* The omega N production mechanism is dominated by large contributions from P11(1710) and P13(1900) resonances.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel analysis of Feuster and Mosel (1999), which incorporated final states up to gamma N, pi N, 2pi N, eta N, and K Lambda, by adding the omega N and K Sigma channels and extending the energy range to sqrt(s) = 2 GeV. The authors demonstrate that single-channel T-matrix models for omega photoproduction (such as those by Friman and Soyeur, Oh and co-workers, Babacan et al., and Zhao and co-workers) are fundamentally limited because they ignore rescattering effects and cannot verify whether resonance parameters are compatible with other reaction channels. The simultaneous description of all channels enables estimates of the importance of individual resonances for the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule.
