# JUNO and Neutrinoless Double Beta Decay

> We study the impact of the precision determination of oscillation parameters in the JUNO experiment on half-life predictions for neutrinoless double beta decay. We show that the solar neutrino mixing angle can be measured by JUNO with below 1% uncertainty. This implies in particular that the minimal value of the effective mass in the inverted mass ordering will be known essentially without uncertainty. We demonstrate that this reduces the range of half-life predictions in order to test this value by a factor of two. The remaining uncertainty is caused by nuclear matrix elements. This has important consequences for future double beta decay experiments that aim at ruling out the inverted mass ordering or the Majorana nature of neutrinos.

## Key claim

The precision measurement of the solar neutrino mixing angle at JUNO (below 1% uncertainty) will reduce the range of half-life predictions for neutrinoless double beta decay in the inverted mass ordering by a factor of two, leaving only nuclear matrix element uncertainties as the remaining source of error.

## Method

The authors used the NuPro package to simulate JUNO's expected performance with a 20 kt liquid scintillator detector at 52 km from two reactor complexes (36 GW total thermal power, 4.9 effective years runtime, 3%/√E(MeV) energy resolution). They performed a Bayesian Nested Sampling analysis of the χ² function over 400 energy bins from 1.8 to 8 MeV, incorporating priors from global fits for the oscillation parameters (Δm²_s, Δm²_a, sin²2θ_r, sin²θ_s) and an overall 1% flux normalization uncertainty. The resulting posterior distributions were used to compute the effective Majorana mass and corresponding half-life ranges for eight double beta decay isotopes, using a compilation of nuclear matrix elements from the literature.

## Key numbers

- JUNO will measure sin²θ_s with uncertainty Δ[sin²θ_s] = 2.4 × 10⁻³, down from the current Δ[sin²θ_s] = 0.016 (a factor of ~7 improvement)
- The uncertainty on θ_s itself is 0.15°, roughly 4 × 10⁻³ of θ_s itself
- The atmospheric mass-squared difference Δm²_a will be measured with precision Δ[Δm²_a]/Δm²_a ≃ 6 × 10⁻³
- The current 3σ range of the minimal effective mass in the inverted ordering is ⟨m_ν⟩^{IO}_{min} = (0.0127 to 0.0198) eV, varying by a factor of 1.6
- After JUNO, the uncertainty on ⟨m_ν⟩^{IO}_{min} drops to the level of 2 × 10⁻³
- For ⁷⁶Ge, the half-life range to rule out the inverted ordering improves from T^{0ν}_{1/2} = (3.7 to 52.3) × 10²⁷ yrs to T^{0ν}_{1/2} = (5.3 to 31) × 10²⁷ yrs (factor 2.4 reduction)
- For ¹³⁶Xe, the half-life range improves from T^{0ν}_{1/2} = (1.1 to 14.3) × 10²⁷ yrs to T^{0ν}_{1/2} = (1.5 to 8.4) × 10²⁷ yrs

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Dueck (2011) which first identified that the solar mixing angle uncertainty introduces a sizable "particle physics uncertainty" in half-life predictions for neutrinoless double beta decay, comparable to nuclear matrix element uncertainties. The authors demonstrate that JUNO (and similarly RENO-50) can essentially eliminate this particle physics uncertainty, enabling future double beta decay experiments to focus on the remaining nuclear physics challenge. This is particularly important for experiments aiming to rule out the inverted mass ordering or the Majorana nature of neutrinos, as the required half-life sensitivity can now be specified with much greater precision.
