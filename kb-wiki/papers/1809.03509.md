# Weak Neutral Current Axial Form Factor Using $(\barν)ν$-Nucleon Scattering and Lattice QCD Inputs

> We present a determination of the neutral current weak axial charge $G^Z_A(0)=-0.654(3)_{\rm stat}(5)_{\rm sys}$ using the strange quark axial charge $G^s_A(0)$ calculated with lattice QCD. We then perform a phenomenological analysis, where we combine the strange quark electromagnetic form factor from lattice QCD with (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering differential cross section from MiniBooNE experiments in a momentum transfer region $0.24\lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 0.71$ GeV$^2$ to determine the neutral current weak axial form factor $G^Z_A(Q^2)$ in the range of $0\lesssim Q^2\leq 1$ GeV$^2$. This yields a phenomenological value of $G^Z_A(0)=-0.687(89)_{\rm stat}(40)_{\rm sys}$. The value of $G^Z_A(0)$ constrained by the lattice QCD calculation of $G^s_A(0)$, when compared to its phenomenological determination, provides a significant improvement in precision and accuracy and can be used to provide a constraint on the fit to $G^Z_A(Q^2)$ for $Q^2>0$. This constrained fit leads to an unambiguous determination of (anti)neutrino-nucleon neutral current elastic scattering differential cross section near $Q^2=0$ and can play an important role in numerically isolating nuclear effects in this region. We show a consistent description of $G^Z_A(Q^2)$ obtained from the (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering cross section data requires a nonzero contribution of the strange quark electromagnetic form factor. We demonstrate the robustness of our analysis by providing a post-diction of the BNL E734 experimental data.

## Key claim

The neutral current weak axial charge \(G^Z_A(0)\) can be determined with significantly improved precision using a lattice QCD calculation of the strange quark axial charge \(G^s_A(0)\), yielding a value of \(-0.654(3)_{\rm stat}(5)_{\rm sys}\) that is free from nuclear effects; however, a phenomenological extraction from MiniBooNE data combined with lattice QCD inputs gives a consistent but much less precise value of \(-0.687(89)_{\rm stat}(40)_{\rm sys}\), and the lattice-constrained value is essential for reducing uncertainties in the \(Q^2\)-dependent form factor at low momentum transfer.

## Method

The authors combined the strange quark axial charge \(G^s_A(0)\) from lattice QCD (Liang et al., 2018) with the experimental charged-current axial charge \(g_A = 1.2723(23)\) to directly compute the neutral current weak axial charge \(G^Z_A(0)\). They then performed a phenomenological analysis using MiniBooNE flux-integrated differential cross sections for (anti)neutrino-nucleon neutral-current elastic scattering in the momentum transfer range \(0.24 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 0.71\) GeV\(^2\), together with lattice QCD calculations of the strange quark electromagnetic form factors \(F^s_{1,2}(Q^2)\) (Sufian et al., 2016, 2017) and a model-independent \(z\)-expansion fit of nucleon electromagnetic form factors (Ye et al., 2017), to extract the neutral current weak axial form factor \(G^Z_A(Q^2)\) and its value at \(Q^2=0\).

## Key numbers

*   \(G^Z_A(0) = -0.654(3)_{\rm stat}(5)_{\rm sys}\) from lattice QCD input of \(G^s_A(0) = -0.035(6)_{\rm stat}(7)_{\rm sys}\) (Liang et al., 2018) and \(g_A = 1.2723(23)\).
*   \(G^Z_A(0) = -0.687(89)_{\rm stat}(40)_{\rm sys}\) from the phenomenological \(z\)-expansion fit to MiniBooNE data combined with lattice QCD inputs.
*   Dipole mass \(M_{A,\rm{dip}} = 1.057(14)\) GeV from a dipole fit to \(G^Z_A(Q^2)\) constrained by the lattice QCD value of \(G^Z_A(0)\).
*   \(G^s_A(0) = -0.102(178)_{\rm stat}(80)_{\rm sys}\) extracted from the phenomenological \(G^Z_A(0)\) and \(g_A\).
*   At \(Q^2 = 0.405\) GeV\(^2\), \(G^Z_A = -0.360(33)\) from \(\nu-N\) data and \(-0.362(14)\) from \(\overline{\nu}-N\) data when including strange quark form factors; with \(F^s_{1,2}=0\), these become \(-0.328(33)\) and \(-0.385(14)\), respectively.
*   At \(Q^2 = 0.675\) GeV\(^2\), \(G^Z_A = -0.202(44)\) from \(\nu-N\) data and \(-0.240(13)\) from \(\overline{\nu}-N\) data with strange form factors; with \(F^s_{1,2}=0\), these become \(-0.154(43)\) and \(-0.287(14)\), respectively.

## Context

This work extends prior determinations of the neutral current weak axial form factor by incorporating first-principles lattice QCD calculations of the strange quark axial charge and electromagnetic form factors, which were previously constrained only by model assumptions or global analyses (e.g., Garvey et al., 1993; Pate et al., 2003). The precise lattice QCD value of \(G^Z_A(0)\) provides a constraint that significantly reduces uncertainties in the \(Q^2\)-dependent form factor at low momentum transfer, enabling a more reliable extraction of (anti)neutrino-nucleon differential cross sections near \(Q^2=0\). This improved determination can help isolate nuclear effects such as Pauli blocking and binding energy in carbon, as observed in MiniBooNE data, and the authors demonstrate consistency by post-dicting the BNL E734 experimental data (Ahrens et al., 1986).
