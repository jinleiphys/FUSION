# Pre-equilibrium effects in charge-asymmetric low-energy reactions

> We study the pre-equilibrium dipole response in the charge-asymmetric reaction $^{132}$Sn+$^{58}$Ni at $E_{lab}=10$ MeV/A, within a semi-classical transport model employing effective interactions for the nuclear mean-field. In particular, we adopt the recently introduced SAMi-J Skyrme interactions, whose parameters are specifically tuned to improve the description of spin-isospin properties of nuclei. Within the same framework, we also discuss pre-equilibrium nucleon emission. Our results show that both mechanisms, i.e., pre-equilibrium dipole oscillations and nucleon emission, are sensitive to the symmetry energy below the saturation density $ρ_0$ (in the range $0.6ρ_0-ρ_0$), to the effective mass and to the nucleon-nucleon cross section. Finally, a covariant analysis is applied to study the correlations between the model parameters and observables of experimental interest.

## Key claim

The pre-equilibrium dipole response and nucleon emission in charge-asymmetric reactions are sensitive to the symmetry energy below saturation density (in the range 0.6ρ₀–ρ₀), to the effective mass, and to the nucleon-nucleon cross section, but the specific density region probed depends on whether momentum-dependent or momentum-independent interactions are used.

## Method

The authors performed Boltzmann-Nordheim-Vlasov (BNV) transport calculations for the reaction ¹³²Sn+⁵⁸Ni at Elab = 10 MeV/A, using both momentum-independent (MI) Skyrme interactions and the recently introduced SAMi-J momentum-dependent (MD) Skyrme interactions. They extracted the dipole moment evolution in coordinate space, computed the power spectrum of the dipole acceleration to characterize γ-ray emission, and identified pre-equilibrium nucleons as test particles in regions with density below 0.01 fm⁻³ at the final calculation time of 600 fm/c.

## Key numbers

* Initial dipole moment at contact: D₀ = 45.1 fm for the ¹³²Sn+⁵⁸Ni system.
* Saturation density for SAMi-J interactions: ρ₀ = 0.159 fm⁻³.
* Symmetry energy at saturation: J = 27 MeV (SAMi-J27), 31 MeV (SAMi-J31), and 35 MeV (SAMi-J35).
* Slope parameter L: 29.9 MeV (SAMi-J27), 74.5 MeV (SAMi-J31), and 115.2 MeV (SAMi-J35).
* Symmetry energy coefficient at ρc ≈ 0.6ρ₀: C(ρc) ≈ 22 MeV for all three SAMi-J parametrizations.
* For MI interactions: L = 14.8 MeV (asysoft), L = 79 MeV (asystiff), L = 106 MeV (asysuperstiff), all with J ≈ 30 MeV.
* Pre-equilibrium nucleon emission at b = 2 fm: total emitted nucleons range from 15.48 (asystiff with fcs=0.5) to 24.90 (SAMi-J31 with fcs=2); N/Z ratios range from 1.433 (SAMi-J27) to 2.049 (asysoft).
* Pre-equilibrium nucleon emission at b = 6 fm: total emitted nucleons range from 14.55 (asystiff with fcs=0.5) to 23.93 (SAMi-J31 with fcs=2); N/Z ratios range from 1.489 (SAMi-J27) to 2.167 (asystiff with fcs=0.5).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the dynamical dipole (DD) mode by Baran et al. (2001, 2009) and Pierroutsakou et al. (2009), which proposed the DD mechanism as a probe of the low-density symmetry energy, by incorporating the new SAMi-J Skyrme interactions that improve the description of spin-isospin properties. The results enable a more systematic connection between reaction observables (dipole γ-ray spectra and pre-equilibrium nucleon N/Z ratios) and specific nuclear matter properties (symmetry energy slope, effective mass, and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section), with the covariance analysis providing a framework for future experimental extraction of these parameters.
