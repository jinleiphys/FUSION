# An Improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics Model and its Applications to Fusion Reaction near Barrier

> An improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics model is proposed. By using this model, the properties of ground state of nuclei from $^{6}$Li to $^{208}$Pb can be described very well with one set of parameters. The fusion reactions for $^{40}$Ca+$^{90}$Zr, $^{40}$Ca+$^{96}$Zr and $^{48}$Ca+$^{90}$Zr at energy near barrier are studied by this model. The experimental data of the fusion cross sections for $^{40}$Ca+$^{90,96}$Zr at the energy near barrier can be reproduced remarkably well without introducing any new parameters. The mechanism for the enhancement of fusion probability for fusion reactions with neutron-rich projectile or target is analyzed.

## Key claim
The improved Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) model can reproduce experimental fusion cross sections for ⁴⁰Ca+⁹⁰,⁹⁶Zr near the barrier without introducing any new parameters, but the model's success depends critically on three specific improvements: a surface energy term, a system-size-dependent wave packet width, and a phase space density constraint.

## Method
The authors modified the standard QMD model by adding a surface energy term to the effective interaction, introducing a system-size-dependent Gaussian wave packet width (σ_r = 0.16 N_A^{1/3} + 0.49), and implementing a phase space density constraint (f̅_i ≤ 1) with many-body elastic scattering. Initial nuclei were prepared using density distributions from relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations, with positions sampled from those densities and momenta sampled using a local-density-approximation Fermi momentum reduced by approximately the half-width of the Gaussian wave packet in momentum space. Fusion cross sections were computed by counting fusion events from 100 bombarding events per energy and impact parameter, using the definition that a fusion event occurs when the coalesced one-body density survives through rotations or oscillations of the composite system, with up to 6 escaped nucleons allowed.

## Key numbers
- For ²⁰⁸Pb with σ_r = 1.04 fm, about 30 spurious particles were emitted by 800 fm/c; with σ_r = 1.44 fm, no particles were emitted by 800 fm/c.
- For ⁹⁰Zr with the surface term included, the central density remained stable at ρ = 0.165 fm⁻³ even at t = 800 fm/c; without the surface term, the central density reached 0.3 fm⁻³ by t = 400 fm/c.
- For ⁴⁰Ca+⁹⁰Zr at E_cm = 107.6 MeV, the average dynamic Coulomb barrier height was about 88.2 MeV, compared to a static Coulomb barrier of about 98.0 MeV.
- For ⁴⁰Ca+⁹⁶Zr at E_cm = 95.0 MeV, the dynamic Coulomb barrier was about 80.6 MeV, more than 5 MeV lower than the 85.2 MeV for ⁴⁰Ca+⁹⁰Zr at the same energy.
- For ⁴⁸Ca+⁹⁰Zr at E_cm = 107.6 MeV, the dynamic Coulomb barrier height was about 85.4 MeV, about 3 MeV lower than for ⁴⁰Ca+⁹⁰Zr and ⁴⁰Ca+⁹⁶Zr.
- The depth of the mean potential well of compound nuclei for ⁴⁰Ca+⁹⁶Zr at E_cm = 107.6 MeV was about 45.38 MeV, only about 0.3 MeV deeper than the 44.92 MeV for ⁴⁰Ca+⁹⁰Zr.

## Context
This work extends the QMD approach of Hartnack (1998) and Aichelin (1991) to low-energy fusion reactions near the Coulomb barrier, a regime where standard QMD suffers from unstable ground states and spurious particle emission. The improved model addresses limitations noted by Papa (2000) regarding the inability of standard QMD to maintain stable phase-space distributions over long times. The results enable microscopic studies of fusion enhancement mechanisms for neutron-rich systems, such as the lowering of the dynamic Coulomb barrier and the formation of favorable potential wells, which the authors plan to investigate further regarding neck dynamics and nucleon transfer.
