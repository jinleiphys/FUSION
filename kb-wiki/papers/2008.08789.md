# Measurement of $γ$+jet and $π^{0}$+jet in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$ = 200 GeV with the STAR experiment

> We present the semi-inclusive measurement of charged jets recoiling from direct-photon and $π^{0}$ triggers in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm {NN}}}$ = 200 GeV, using a dataset with integrated luminosity 13 $\mathrm {nb^{-1}}$ recorded by the STAR experiment in 2014. The photon and $π^{0}$ triggers are selected within transverse energy ($\rm E_\mathrm{T}^\mathrm{trig}$) between 9 GeV and 20 GeV. Charged jets are reconstructed with the anti-$k_{\mathrm{T}}$ algorithm with resolution parameters R = 0.2 and 0.5. A Mixed-Event technique developed previously by STAR is used to correct the recoil jet yield for uncorrelated background, enabling recoil jet measurements over a broad $\rm p_{T,jet}$ range. We report fully corrected charged-jet yields recoiling from direct-photon and $π^{0}$ triggers for the above two jet radii and also discuss the jet R dependence of in-medium parton energy loss at the top RHIC energy.

# Measurement of γ+jet and π⁰+jet in central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV with the STAR experiment

## Key claim

The semi-inclusive charged-jet yields recoiling from direct-photon and π⁰ triggers in central Au+Au collisions show significant suppression for jet radius R = 0.2 and less suppression for R = 0.5 when compared to PYTHIA-8 as a pp reference, but the difference between PYTHIA-8 and PYTHIA-6 precludes quantitative conclusions about the absolute magnitude of suppression.

## Method

Charged jets were reconstructed using the anti-kT algorithm with resolution parameters R = 0.2 and 0.5 from charged particle tracks measured in the Time Projection Chamber (TPC) with 0.2 < pT < 30 GeV/c and |η| < 1, in the 0-15% most central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV. Direct-photon and π⁰ triggers were selected within transverse energy (ET) between 9 GeV and 20 GeV using the Barrel Electromagnetic Calorimeter (BEMC) and Shower Max Detector (BSMD), with a Transverse Shower Profile method discriminating between the two trigger types. A Mixed-Event technique previously developed by STAR corrected the recoil jet yield for uncorrelated background, and unfolding methods corrected for instrumental effects and residual pT,jet fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- Integrated luminosity of the dataset: 13 nb⁻¹ recorded by STAR in 2014
- Trigger energy threshold: energy deposition greater than 5.6 GeV in a BEMC tower
- Trigger selection range: 9 < ET < 20 GeV for both direct-photon and π⁰ triggers
- Direct-photon purity in the γ-rich sample: 65-85% in the range 9 < ET < 20 GeV
- Charged particle track selection: 0.2 < pT < 30 GeV/c and |η| < 1
- Jet acceptance: |η_jet| < 1 - R (so |η_jet| < 0.8 for R = 0.2 and |η_jet| < 0.5 for R = 0.5)
- Recoil jet selection: Δφ ∈ [3π/4, 5π/4] between trigger cluster and jet axis
- PYTHIA-6 and PYTHIA-8 show negligible difference for R = 0.2 and up to 40% difference for R = 0.5 in the recoil jet yields

## Context

This work extends the STAR program of semi-inclusive jet measurements in heavy-ion collisions, following the Mixed-Event technique developed in Adamczyk et al. (2017) for hadron+jet measurements. The comparison of direct-photon+jet and π⁰+jet measurements probes the color factor and path-length dependence of jet quenching, as proposed theoretically by Wang (1996) and earlier STAR results (2016). The results are compared to theoretical calculations from Jet-fluid (Chang et al., 2016), LBT (Luo et al., 2018), and SCET (Sievert et al., 2019), which predict different pT dependence than observed. The conversion of suppression to a pT-shift enables quantitative comparison with inclusive jet and hadron+jet measurements at RHIC and LHC, including results from Adamczyk et al. (2017), Adam et al. (2020), and Adam et al. (2015). A definitive conclusion on in-medium jet broadening awaits the same measurements in pp collisions at 200 GeV, currently in progress.
