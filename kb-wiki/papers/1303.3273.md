# Scattering in Time-Dependent Basis Light-Front Quantization

> We introduce a nonperturbative, first principles numerical approach for solving time-dependent problems in quantum field theory, using light-front quantization. As a first application we consider QED in a strong background field, and the process of non-linear Compton scattering in which an electron is excited by the background and emits a photon. We track the evolution of the quantum state as a function of time. Observables, such as the invariant mass of the electron-photon pair, are first checked against results from perturbation theory, for suitable parameters. We then proceed to a test case in the strong background field regime and discuss the various nonperturbative effects revealed by the approach.

## Key claim

The authors introduce a nonperturbative, first-principles numerical method (tBLFQ) for solving time-dependent quantum field theory problems, but the method is demonstrated only for a simplified background field that does not obey Maxwell's equations in vacuum, and the results are presented as a "proof-of-concept" rather than a fully realistic calculation.

## Method

The authors use Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) to construct approximate eigenstates of the full QED Hamiltonian in a truncated basis, using two-dimensional harmonic oscillator states for transverse degrees of freedom and discretized plane waves for longitudinal degrees of freedom. They then solve the time-dependent Schrodinger equation in the interaction picture numerically, evolving an initial electron state under the influence of a time-independent but longitudinally inhomogeneous background field that models an intense laser, with the Fock space truncated to include only the electron and electron-photon sectors.

## Key numbers

- The background field strength is parameterized by the dimensionless quantity \(a_0 = eE / m_e \omega\), where \(a_0 = 1\) corresponds to an intensity of approximately \(10^{18}\) W/cm\(^2\) at optical frequency.
- The longitudinal momentum takes discrete values \(p^+ = (2\pi/L)k\) with \(L = 2\pi\) MeV\(^{-1}\), where \(k = 1, 2, 3, \ldots\) for bosons and \(k = 1/2, 3/2, 5/2, \ldots\) for fermions.
- The transverse basis truncation parameter \(N_\text{max}\) restricts the total 2D-HO quantum number \(N_\alpha = \sum_l (2n_l + |m_l| + 1) \le N_\text{max}\).
- The 2D-HO parameter is set to \(b = m_e\) (the physical electron mass) for the one-particle sector.

## Context

This work extends the Basis Light-Front Quantization method (Vary 2009) to time-dependent problems, providing an alternative to kinetic model approaches (Fedotov 2010, Sokolov 2010, Elkina 2010) that lack a first-principles derivation from QED. The method enables fully quantum, real-time calculations of processes like nonlinear Compton scattering in strong background fields, though the authors note that including realistic background fields that obey Maxwell's equations (such as those described by Ilderton 2011) remains a challenge for future work.
