# Relativistic spin hydrodynamics from novel relaxation time approximation

> With the help of a semi-classical kinetic theory, a new collision kernel is proposed, which simultaneously conserves the energy-momentum tensor and the spin tensor of a relativistic fluid of spin-1/2 particles irrespective of the frame and matching conditions, even when relaxation time is momentum dependent. The relativistic Boltzmann's equation is solved using this new collision kernel to obtain the expressions of the transport coefficients with general definitions for the frame and matching conditions. The results indicate the expected existence of Barnett-like effect and the non-existence of Einstein--de-Haas-like effects.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a new collision kernel for relativistic spin hydrodynamics that guarantees conservation of both energy-momentum and spin tensors at all orders, independent of frame and matching conditions, even when the relaxation time is momentum dependent. The results confirm the existence of a Barnett-like effect (spin dynamics influenced by fluid gradients) but find no Einstein–de-Haas-like effect (fluid dynamics unaffected by spin gradients), a condition that holds only in the small-polarization limit and under the assumption of local collisions.

## Method
The authors construct a linearized collision operator using a novel relaxation time approximation (NRTA) that includes ten counter-terms to enforce microscopic conservation of momentum four-vector and spin tensor. They solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation via a Chapman-Enskog-like expansion to first order in spacetime gradients, expanding the out-of-equilibrium distribution function in irreducible tensors and orthogonal polynomials of energy, with the spin-dependent part truncated to linear order in the spin polarization tensor. Transport coefficients are then derived under general frame and matching conditions, with the relaxation time taken as momentum-dependent but spin-independent for simplicity.

## Key numbers
- The linearized collision operator has 10 eigenfunctions with zero eigenvalue, corresponding to the 10 conserved degrees of freedom (4 from momentum, 6 from spin).
- The expansion of the distribution function involves 44 degrees of freedom (14 spin-independent, 30 spin-dependent), reduced to 34 by frame and matching conditions, with 4 spin degrees of freedom redundant due to the Frenkel condition \(p_\mu s^{\mu\nu} = 0\).
- The transport coefficients for shear viscosity \(\pi^{\mu\nu}\) are given by \(\Phi_{0}^{\ab{\mu\nu}} \ab{\left(2/15\right) \left(p\cdot\Delta\cdot p\right)^2}_{0\textbf{p}}\) (Eq. for \(\pi^{\mu\nu}\)).
- The spin density correction \(\delta \mathcal{S}^{\mu\nu}\) is expressed as \(\sum_{n\in\mathbb{S}_1^{(0)}} \Psi_{n}^{\mu\nu} \ab{E_{\textbf{p}} P_n^{(1,0)}}_{0\textbf{p}}\).
- The spin diffusion term \(\delta \Sigma^{\lambda,\mu\nu}\) is given by \(\Psi_{0}^{\ab{\mu}\ab{\nu},\ab{\lambda}} \ab{\left(p\cdot\Delta\cdot p\right)}_{0\textbf{p}}\).

## Context
This work extends the extended relaxation time approximation (ERTA) approach of Bhadury (2024) and the NRTA framework of Rocha (2021) to spin hydrodynamics, building on earlier spin kinetic theories by Florkowski (2018) and Weickgenannt (2021) that assumed separate conservation of orbital and spin angular momentum in local collisions. It contrasts with the standard RTA used in Bhadury (2020, 2022), which required specific Landau-Lifshitz frame and matching conditions to satisfy conservation laws. The results enable the development of a first-order causal and stable theory of spin hydrodynamics analogous to the BDNK formalism for unpolarized fluids, and the author plans to extend the formulation to include background magnetic fields and particle-antiparticle pair production.
