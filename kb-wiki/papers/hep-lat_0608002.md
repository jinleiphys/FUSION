# Convergence of the chiral expansion for the nucleon mass

> A number of papers recently have used fourth-order chiral perturbation theory to extrapolate lattice data for the nucleon mass; the process seems surprisingly successful even for large pion masses. This paper shows that the inclusion of the fifth-order terms spoils the agreement.

## Key claim

The inclusion of fifth-order (O(p⁵)) terms in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory spoils the surprisingly good agreement between fourth-order (O(p⁴)) chiral extrapolations and lattice data for the nucleon mass at large pion masses; the fifth-order fit yields unacceptably large low-energy constants and breaks down at higher pion masses, contrary to the impression given by fourth-order-only analyses.

## Method

The authors computed the O(p⁵) contribution to the nucleon mass in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) using the expression from McGovern and Birse (1999), reformulated for lattice extrapolation by expressing results in terms of bare parameters rather than physical ones. They performed fits to lattice data for the nucleon mass versus squared pion mass from UKQCD, QCDSF, CP-PACS, JLQCD, and Orth, Lippert and Schilling collaborations, restricting fits to data below mπ = 600 MeV, at third, fourth, and fifth chiral orders, and compared the resulting fit quality and extracted low-energy constants.

## Key numbers

- At fourth order, the fit to lattice data below mπ = 600 MeV gives χ² = 0.20 for 2 degrees of freedom, with c₁ = -0.84 GeV⁻¹ and e'(mπ^phys) = -0.52 GeV⁻³.
- At fifth order, the fit gives χ² = 0.54 for 2 degrees of freedom, but the extracted LEC d₁₆(mπ^phys) = 4.654 GeV⁻² and e'(mπ^phys) = -30.5 GeV⁻³ are far outside expected ranges.
- The fourth-order fit yields the expansion mn = 0.887 + 3.35 mπ² - 5.61 mπ³ + 3.96 mπ⁴ (in GeV), while the fifth-order fit yields mn = 0.885 + 3.20 mπ² - 5.61 mπ³ + 33.9 mπ⁴ - 108.5 mπ⁵ (in GeV), with logs evaluated at mπ = 0.2 MeV.
- At third order, the fit is unacceptable with χ² = 31.6 for 3 degrees of freedom, giving c₁ = -1.14 GeV⁻¹ and m0 = 865 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the fourth-order chiral extrapolation studies of Procura et al. (2006) and Bernard et al. (2004), which found surprisingly good fits to lattice data up to mπ ≈ 800 MeV, by including the full fifth-order calculation from McGovern and Birse (1999). The results contradict the expectation from Meißner (2006) that O(p⁵) contributions would be negligible, and instead imply that lattice data at substantially lower pion masses are required before chiral extrapolations become reliable. The paper also notes that the bulk of the fifth-order term comes from the running of the axial coupling gA, which is poorly described by NLO χPT without an explicit Δ resonance, as shown by Alexandrou et al. (2006).
