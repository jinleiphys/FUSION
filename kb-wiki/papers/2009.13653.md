# Bayesian inference of dense matter EOS encapsulating a first-order hadron-quark phase transition from observables of canonical neutron stars

> [Purpose:] We infer the posterior probability distribution functions (PDFs) and correlations of nine parameters characterizing the EOS of dense neutron-rich matter encapsulating a first-order hadron-quark phase transition from the radius data of canonical NSs reported by LIGO/VIRGO, NICER and Chandra Collaborations. We also infer the quark matter (QM) mass fraction and its radius in a 1.4 M$_{\odot}$ NS and predict their values in more massive NSs. [Method:] Meta-modelings are used to generate both hadronic and QM EOSs in the Markov-Chain Monte Carlo sampling process within the Bayesian statistical framework. An explicitly isospin-dependent parametric EOS for the $npeμ$ matter in NSs at $β$ equilibrium is connected through the Maxwell construction to the QM EOS described by the constant speed of sound (CSS) model of Alford, Han and Prakash. [Results:] (1) The most probable values of the hadron-quark transition density $ρ_t/ρ_0$ and the relative energy density jump there $\De\ep/\ep_t$ are $ρ_t/ρ_0=1.6^{+1.2}_{-0.4}$ and $\De\ep/\ep_t=0.4^{+0.20}_{-0.15}$ at 68\% confidence level, respectively. The corresponding probability distribution of QM fraction in a 1.4 M$_{\odot}$ NS peaks around 0.9 in a 10 km sphere. Strongly correlated to the PDFs of $ρ_t$ and $\De\ep/\ep_t$, the PDF of the QM speed of sound squared $\cQMsq/c^2$ peaks at $0.95^{+0.05}_{-0.35}$, and the total probability of being less than 1/3 is very small. (2) The correlations between PDFs of hadronic and QM EOS parameters are very weak. [Conclusions:] The available astrophysical data considered together with all known EOS constraints from theories and terrestrial nuclear experiments prefer the formation of a large volume of QM even in canonical NSs.

## Key claim

The available astrophysical data (radius measurements of canonical neutron stars from LIGO/VIRGO, NICER, and Chandra), combined with all known EOS constraints from theories and terrestrial nuclear experiments, prefer the formation of a large volume of quark matter even in 1.4 M$_{\odot}$ neutron stars, regardless of whether the Seidov stability condition is enforced.

## Method

The authors used meta-modelings for both hadronic and quark matter equations of state (EOS) within a Bayesian statistical framework employing Markov-Chain Monte Carlo sampling. An explicitly isospin-dependent parametric EOS for $npe\mu$ matter in neutron stars at $\beta$ equilibrium was connected through the Maxwell construction to the constant speed of sound (CSS) quark matter EOS of Alford, Han and Prakash, with nine total parameters sampled from uniform priors. The likelihood function incorporated radius data from four independent observations (GW170817 from LIGO/VIRGO, quiescent low-mass X-ray binaries from Chandra/XMM-Newton, and PSR J0030+0451 from NICER) along with filters enforcing causality, thermodynamical stability, and a maximum mass requirement of 1.97 M$_{\odot}$.

## Key numbers

- Most probable hadron-quark transition density: $\rho_t/\rho_0 = 1.6^{+1.2}_{-0.4}$ at 68% confidence level
- Most probable relative energy density jump at transition: $\Delta\varepsilon/\varepsilon_t = 0.4^{+0.20}_{-0.15}$ at 68% confidence level
- Most probable quark matter speed of sound squared: $c^2_{\rm QM}/c^2 = 0.95^{+0.05}_{-0.35}$ at 68% confidence level
- Quark matter mass fraction in a 1.4 M$_{\odot}$ neutron star peaks around 0.9 within a 10 km sphere
- Total probability of forming hybrid stars with quark matter mass fraction above 0.1 is 77.6% for M=1.4 M$_{\odot}$, 81.8% for 1.6 M$_{\odot}$, 85.2% for 1.8 M$_{\odot}$, and 88.7% for 2.0 M$_{\odot}$
- With $c^2_{\rm QM}/c^2$ fixed to 1/3, the quark matter mass fraction drops to only 2.3% for 1.4, 1.6, and 1.8 M$_{\odot}$ neutron stars

## Context

This work extends Bayesian inference approaches previously applied to neutron star EOS without phase transitions (Xie 2019, Xie 2020) by explicitly incorporating a first-order hadron-quark phase transition via the CSS model. It contrasts with forward-modeling studies that use specific microscopic theories or polytropic parameterizations, which are less flexible for statistical exploration of the full EOS parameter space. The results enable future Bayesian inferences using unified EOS models describing both neutron stars and heavy-ion reactions with possible phase transitions from combined multimessenger data.
