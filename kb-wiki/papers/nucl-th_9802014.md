# Shell corrections for finite depth potentials: Particle continuum effects

> Shell corrections of finite, spherical, one-body potentials are analyzed using a smoothing procedure which properly accounts for the contribution from the particle continuum, i.e., unbound states. Since the plateau condition for the smoothed single-particle energy seldom holds, a new recipe is suggested for the definition of the shell correction. The generalized Strutinsky smoothing procedure is compared with the results of the semi-classical Wigner-Kirkwood expansion. A good agreement has been found for weakly bound nuclei in the vicinity of the proton drip line. However, some deviations remain for extremely neutron-rich systems due to the pathological behavior of the semi-classical level density around the particle threshold.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the traditional Strutinsky plateau condition for shell corrections seldom holds for finite-depth potentials, even when the particle continuum is properly included, and proposes a new recipe based on the local linearity of the smoothed level density rather than the plateau condition.

## Method

The authors solve the radial Schrödinger equation in the complex energy plane using Gamow states to compute the single-particle level density, including both bound states and narrow resonances. The smoothed level density is obtained via a generalized Strutinsky folding procedure that uses a contour integral in the complex plane to account for continuum contributions. The resulting shell corrections are compared with those from the semi-classical Wigner-Kirkwood (WK) expansion for a Woods-Saxon potential with spin-orbit and Coulomb terms.

## Key numbers

- The r.m.s. error in the shell correction δE from the new method is always less than 250 keV for nuclei heavier than ⁴⁰Ca.
- The average deviation between the generalized Strutinsky and WK shell corrections (Δ) is about 0.45 MeV, with a maximal deviation of about 1.8 MeV for neutrons and 0.9 MeV for protons.
- For well-bound nuclei, the difference between the Strutinsky and WK Fermi levels is less than 100 keV.
- The uncertainty of the new method is estimated at approximately 400 keV based on variations in the fitting range.
- For neutrons with Fermi energy below −4 MeV, |Δ| is less than 600 keV; for protons, |Δ| is less than 900 keV in all cases considered.
- The neutron shell correction for ⁹⁰Zr shows four near-threshold Gamow poles: p₁/₂ (w=0.2−i0.19 MeV), f₅/₂ (w=2.1−i0.34 MeV), i₁₃/₂ (w=3.7−i0.004 MeV), and h₉/₂ (w=3.9−i0.03 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the macroscopic-microscopic method previously applied by Nazarewicz (1994) to nuclei far from stability, where continuum effects can cause systematic errors of several MeV in binding energies at the neutron drip line. The new generalized Strutinsky procedure resolves the failure of the plateau condition for finite potentials, which was previously assumed to be achievable based on early work by Ross and collaborators (1972) for ²⁰⁸Pb and ²⁹⁸114. The method enables reliable shell-correction calculations for weakly bound nuclei near the drip lines, including neutron-rich systems where the WK expansion fails due to singularities around the particle threshold.
