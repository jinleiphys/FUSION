# Truncation uncertainties for accurate quantum simulations of lattice gauge theories

> The encoding of lattice gauge theories onto quantum computers requires a discretization of the gauge field's Hilbert space on each link, which presents errors with respect to the Kogut--Susskind limit. In the electric basis, Hilbert space fragmentation has recently been shown to limit the excitation of large electric fields. Here, we leverage this to develop a formalism for estimating the size of truncation errors in the electric basis. Generically, the truncation error falls off as a factorial of the field truncation. Examples of this formalism are applied to the Schwinger model and a pure U(1) lattice gauge theory. For reasonable choices of parameters, we improve on previous error estimates by a factor of 10^{306}.

## Key claim

The truncation error in the electric basis for lattice gauge theories falls off as a factorial of the field truncation, and the authors develop a formalism to estimate this error quantitatively using Hilbert space fragmentation, improving on previous error estimates by a factor of up to \(10^{306}\) for reasonable parameter choices.

## Method

The authors apply time-dependent perturbation theory to the Kogut–Susskind Hamiltonian in the electric basis, leveraging Hilbert space fragmentation (HSF) which limits the excitation of large electric fields due to the quadratic growth of the electric energy gap. They derive analytic bounds on the leakage amplitude for a single plaquette by evaluating nested integrals of oscillatory phases, and then compare these bounds to numerical simulations of the Schwinger model and a pure U(1) lattice gauge theory on a plaquette ladder. The formalism estimates truncation errors without requiring the truncation to grow linearly with time to maintain accuracy.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of truncation errors in lattice gauge theories by using Hilbert space fragmentation, previously identified by the same authors (Ciavarella 2025), to derive tighter error bounds than those from earlier rigorous Dyson series bounds (Tong 2021). The formalism enables more accurate resource estimates for quantum simulations of lattice gauge theories, particularly for the Kogut–Susskind Hamiltonian, and can be applied to both eigenstate and time-dependent truncation errors. It also connects to prior work showing fast convergence of energy eigenstates with field truncation (Banuls 2017, Zache 2021, Halimeh 2021) and provides a mechanism for the factorial scaling observed in those studies.
