# Sensitivity of jet substructure to jet-induced medium response

> Jet quenching in heavy ion collisions is expected to be accompanied by recoil effects, but unambiguous signals for the induced medium response have been difficult to identify so far. Here, we argue that modern jet substructure measurements can improve this situation qualitatively since they are sensitive to the momentum distribution inside the jet. We show that the groomed subjet shared momentum fraction $z_g$, and the girth of leading and subleading subjets signal recoil effects with dependencies that are absent in a recoilless baseline. We find that recoil effects can explain most of the medium modifications to the $z_g$ distribution observed in data. Furthermore, for jets passing the Soft Drop Condition, recoil effects induce in the differential distribution of subjet separation $ΔR_{12}$ a characteristic increase with $ΔR_{12}$, and they introduce a characteristic enhancement of the girth of the subleading subjet with decreasing $z_g$. We explain why these qualitatively novel features, that we establish in \textsc{Jewel+Pythia} simulations, reflect generic physical properties of recoil effects that should therefore be searched for as telltale signatures of jet-induced medium response.

## Key claim

The paper argues that modern jet substructure measurements, specifically the groomed subjet shared momentum fraction \(z_g\) and the girth of leading and subleading subjets, can qualitatively improve the identification of jet-induced medium response (recoil effects) in heavy ion collisions, which have been difficult to detect unambiguously. The authors show that recoil effects can explain most of the medium modifications to the \(z_g\) distribution observed in data, but they hedge that the truth is expected to lie between simulations with and without recoiling partons, and the difference between these curves should be regarded as an upper bound for the expected medium response.

## Method

The authors used the event generator Jewel+Pythia to simulate di-jet events in central PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV, applying a 4-momentum subtraction technique to remove thermal background contributions. They compared simulations that included recoiling partons (medium response) with simulations that excluded them, analyzing the groomed shared momentum fraction \(z_g\) from the Soft Drop algorithm (with \(\beta=0\), \(z_{\rm cut}=0.1\), and \(\Delta R_{12}>0.1\)), the relative separation \(\Delta R_{12}\) of the two subjets, and the girth (first radial moment) of the leading and subleading subjets.

## Key numbers

- The \(z_g\) distribution in PbPb events with recoil effects shows quantitative agreement with CMS data for jet \(p_\perp\) between 140 GeV and 160 GeV at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02\) TeV.
- The \(\Delta R_{12}\) distribution for jets passing the Soft Drop condition increases with increasing \(\Delta R_{12}\) up to a separation scale set by the jet radius \(R=0.4\), peaking at a value somewhat smaller than \(R\).
- For the subleading subjet, the girth is approximately a factor 2 more strongly enhanced for small \(z_g \simeq 0.1\) than for \(z_g \simeq 0.5\) when recoil effects are included.

## Context

This work extends studies of jet quenching at the LHC, where traditional observables like jet \(p_\perp\) spectra and dijet asymmetries have been insensitive to soft large-angle recoil activity. The authors build on prior simulations with Jewel (Zapp 2013) and a validated background subtraction technique (Kunnawalkam Elayavalli 2017), and they contrast their recoil interpretation with alternative explanations based on medium-induced gluon radiation softening the perturbative splitting functions (Chien 2016, Mehtar-Tani 2016). The findings enable future searches for telltale signatures of jet-induced medium response in complementary jet substructure measurements, such as the \(\Delta R_{12}\) distribution and girth of subleading subjets.
