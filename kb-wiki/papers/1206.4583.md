# A High-Performance Fortran Code to Calculate Spin- and Parity-Dependent Nuclear Level Densities

> A high-performance Fortran code is developed to calculate the spin- and parity-dependent shell model nuclear level densities.The algorithm is based on the extension of methods of statistical spectroscopy and implies exact calculation of the first and second Hamiltonian moments for different configurations at fixed spin and parity. The proton-neutron formalism is used. We have applied the method for calculating the level densities for a set of nuclei in the sd-, pf-, and pf+g9/2 - model spaces. Examples of the calculations for 28Si (in the sd-model space) and 64Ge (in the pf+g9/2-model space) are presented. To illustrate the power of the method we estimate the ground state energy of 64Ge in the larger model space pf+g9/2, which is not accessible to direct shell model diagonalization due to the prohibitively large dimension, by comparing with the nuclear level densities at low excitation energy calculated in the smaller model space pf.

## Key claim

The method of moments can estimate the ground state energy of a nucleus in a large model space (e.g., $pf+g_{9/2}$) that is inaccessible to direct shell-model diagonalization, by comparing the calculated level densities at low excitation energy with those from a smaller model space (e.g., $pf$), provided the cut-off parameter $\eta$ and ground state energy are known or adjusted.

## Method

The algorithm uses statistical spectroscopy to exactly compute the first and second Hamiltonian moments (centroids and widths) for each configuration at fixed spin $J$ and parity $\pi$, within a proton-neutron formalism. Traces of $H$ and $H^2$ are calculated via propagation functions derived from occupation numbers, and the $J$-projected moments are obtained from $M_z$-projected traces using rotational symmetry. The code is parallelized with MPI using a master-slave dynamic load-balancing scheme, and the level density is constructed as a sum of finite-range Gaussians over configurations.

## Key numbers

- For $^{28}$Si in the $sd$-model space with the USD interaction, the ground state energy is $E_{\text{g.s.}} = -135.94$ MeV, and the cut-off parameter $\eta = 2.8$ reproduces exact shell-model level densities well.
- For $^{64}$Ge in the $pf+g_{9/2}$ model space, the ground state energy in the $pf$-shell is $E_{\text{g.s.}}(pf) = -304.25$ MeV; the calculation in the larger space uses $E_{\text{g.s.}}(pf+g_{9/2}) = E_{\text{g.s.}}(pf) - \Delta E$ with $\eta = 2.6$.
- Elapsed times on a 16-core 2.8 GHz machine: $^{28}$Si ($sd$, total dimension $10^6$) in 0.7 seconds; $^{52}$Fe ($pf$, $10^{10}$) in 13.6 seconds; $^{60}$Zn ($pf$, $10^{11}$) in 37.4 seconds; $^{64}$Ge ($pf+g_{9/2}$, $10^{14}$) in $0.76 \times 10^4$ seconds; $^{68}$Se ($pf+g_{9/2}$, $10^{15}$) in $1.03 \times 10^4$ seconds; $^{70}$Br ($pf+g_{9/2}$, $10^{15}$) in $1.07 \times 10^4$ seconds.
- The single-particle energy for the $g_{9/2}$ orbit was fixed at $-0.637$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior statistical spectroscopy approach (Sen'kov et al., 2010; also Refs. jtpden, jdenrc, nic8den, dencom, our2010) by implementing a proton-neutron formalism that increases the number of configurations and improves parallel scaling, enabling calculations for nuclei like $^{68}$Se with $m$-scheme dimensions up to $10^{15}$. The code provides spin- and parity-dependent level densities up to about 15 MeV excitation energy, which are essential inputs for Hauser-Feshbach reaction rate calculations in nuclear astrophysics (Rauscher, 1997; Moller, 2009); it also enables ground state energy estimation in model spaces too large for direct diagonalization, such as the $pf+g_{9/2}$ space for $^{64}$Ge, a waiting-point nucleus along the $rp$-process path.
