# Photon interferometry and size of the hot zone in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The parameters obtained from the theoretical analysis of the single photon spectra observed by the WA98 collaboration at SPS energies have been used to evaluate the two photon correlation functions. The single photon spectra and the two photon correlations at RHIC energies have also been evaluated, taking into account the effects of the possible spectral change of hadrons in a thermal bath. We find that the ratio $R_{side}/R_{out} \sim 1$ for SPS and $R_{side}/R_{out} <1$ for RHIC energy.

## Key claim

The ratio of HBT radii \(R_{side}/R_{out}\) extracted from two-photon correlation functions is approximately 1 for SPS energies regardless of whether the initial state is a quark-gluon plasma or a hot hadronic gas with reduced masses, but becomes less than 1 for RHIC energies.

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic simulations with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion, using an equation of state containing all hadrons up to ~2.5 GeV mass and incorporating Brown-Rho universal scaling for in-medium hadron masses. They computed the two-photon Bose-Einstein correlation function by convolving thermal photon emission rates (including two-loop QGP rates and hadronic rates from \(\pi,\rho,\eta,\omega\) reactions) with the space-time evolution, using initial conditions and freeze-out parameters that reproduce the WA98 single-photon spectra at SPS and the NA49 hadronic spectra.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature \(T_i = 200\) MeV for SPS (Pb+Pb at 158 AGeV) with formation time \(\tau_i = 1.0\) fm/c, based on dN/dy = 700.
- Initial temperature \(T_i = 264\) MeV for RHIC (Au+Au at 200 AGeV) with \(\tau_i = 0.6\) fm/c, based on dN/dy = 1100.
- Critical temperature \(T_c = 170\) MeV and freeze-out temperature \(T_f = 120\) MeV for both SPS and RHIC.
- For SPS with QGP initial state: \(R_{inv} = 3.5\) fm, \(R_{out} = 3.6\) fm, \(R_{side} = 3.5\) fm, \(R_{long} = 0.9\) fm (sum phase at \(p_T = 2\) GeV/c).
- For RHIC sum phase: \(R_{inv} = 3\) fm, \(R_{out} = 5.5\) fm, \(R_{side} = 3\) fm, \(R_{long} = 1.6\) fm (at \(p_T = 2\) GeV/c).
- Velocity of sound squared at freeze-out is \(c_s^2 \approx 0.18\) for the hadronic EOS used.

## Context

This work extends earlier photon interferometry studies by DKS and Timmermann by including two-loop photon emission rates, in-medium hadron mass modifications, a complete hadronic equation of state up to 2.5 GeV, and constraints from WA98 single-photon data and NA49 hadronic spectra. The results enable future experimental tests at RHIC to distinguish between QGP and hadronic initial states via the \(R_{side}/R_{out}\) ratio, and provide predictions for the photon HBT radii that can be compared with measurements from the PHENIX and STAR experiments.
