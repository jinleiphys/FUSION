# Systematic study of $^{9,10,11}$Li with the tensor and pairing correlations

> We make a systematic study of Li isotopes (A=9,10,11) in the tensor optimized shell model for 9Li and treat the additional valence neutrons in the cluster model approach by taking into account the Pauli-blocking effect caused by the tensor and pairing correlations. We describe the tensor correlations in 9Li fully in the tensor-optimized shell model, where the variation of the size parameters of the single particle orbits is essential for getting strong tensor correlations. We have shown in our previous study that in $^{10,11}$Li the tensor and pairing correlations in 9Li are Pauli-blocked by additional valence neutrons, which make the p-shell configurations pushed up in energy. As a result, the $s^2$ valence neutron component increases to reveal the halo structure of 11Li and the inversion phenomenon of the single particle spectrum in 10Li arises. Following the previous study, we demonstrate the reliability of our framework by performing a detailed systematic analysis of the structures of $^{9,10,11}$Li, such as the charge radius, the spatial correlation of halo neutrons of 11Li and the electromagnetic properties of Li isotopes. The detailed effects of the Pauli-blocking on the spectroscopic properties of $^{10,11}$Li are also discussed. It is found that the blocking acts strongly for the 11Li ground state rather than for 10Li and for the dipole excited states of 11Li, which is mainly caused by the interplay between the tensor correlation in 9Li and the halo neutrons. The results obtained in these analyses clearly show that the inert core assumption of 9Li is not realistic to explain the anomalous structures observed in $^{10,11}$Li. For the dipole excitation spectrum of 11Li, the effect of the final state interactions is discussed in terms of the dipole strength function.

## Key claim

The tensor and pairing correlations in the \(^9\)Li core are Pauli-blocked by additional valence neutrons in \(^{10,11}\)Li, which pushes the \(p\)-shell configurations up in energy and causes the \(s\)-\(p\) shell gap to disappear; this mechanism is claimed to explain the halo structure of \(^{11}\)Li and the inversion phenomenon in \(^{10}\)Li, but the authors note that the tensor correlation alone is not enough for quantitative description and that the pairing correlation must be added.

## Method

The authors first describe the \(^9\)Li core using the tensor-optimized shell model (TOSM), where the length parameters of single-particle orbits are varied variationally to capture strong tensor correlations, and the wave function includes up to \(2p\)-\(2h\) excitations within the \(p\)-shell. For \(^{10}\)Li and \(^{11}\)Li, they treat the additional valence neutrons in a cluster-model approach using the orthogonality condition model (OCM) to enforce the Pauli principle, solving coupled Schrödinger equations for the relative motion between the \(^9\)Li core and the valence neutron(s). The two-neutron wave function in \(^{11}\)Li is expanded with a finite number of Gaussian basis functions in the hybrid-TV model, and the \(n\)-\(n\) interaction is taken as the realistic AV8' potential.

## Key numbers

* \(^9\)Li ground state binding energy: \(-45.3\) MeV (reproduced by adjusting the central force).
* Tensor force contribution to \(^9\)Li: \(-20.7\) MeV; pairing correlation contribution: \(-3.0\) MeV.
* Charge radius of \(^9\)Li: \(2.23\) fm (TOSM), compared to experimental values \(2.217\pm0.035\) fm (Sanchez 2006) and \(2.185\pm0.033\) fm (Puchalski 2006).
* Matter radius of \(^9\)Li: \(2.31\) fm (TOSM), compared to \(2.32\pm0.02\) fm (Tanihata 1988) and \(2.44\pm0.06\) fm (Dobrovolsky 2006).
* In \(^{10}\)Li, the \(1^+\) \(p\)-wave resonance is at \(E_r = 0.22\) MeV with width \(\Gamma = 0.09\) MeV; the \(2^+\) resonance is at \(E_r = 0.64\) MeV with \(\Gamma = 0.45\) MeV.
* Scattering lengths for \(s\)-wave states in \(^{10}\)Li: \(a_s(1^-) = -5.6\) fm, \(a_s(2^-) = -17.4\) fm (the latter comparable to the \(nn\) system value of \(-18.5\) fm).
* Two-neutron separation energy \(S_{2n}\) of \(^{11}\)Li: set to \(0.31\) MeV to fix the \(\delta\) parameter.
* In the \(^9\)Li wave function, the \(0p\)-\(0h\) configuration has probability \(82.9\%\), the pairing-type \(2p\)-\(2h\) configuration \((0p_{3/2})^{-2}_{01}(0p_{1/2})^2_{01}\) has \(9.0\%\), and the tensor-type \(2p\)-\(2h\) configuration \((0s)^{-2}_{10}(0p_{1/2})^2_{10}\) has \(7.2\%\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous TOSM studies of \(^4\)He and \(^5\)He (Myo 2005, 2007) to the Li isotopes, and directly builds on their earlier treatment of \(^{11}\)Li (Myo 2007b). It contradicts the inert-core assumption of \(^9\)Li used in many conventional models, showing that the tensor and pairing correlations in the core must be explicitly included to reproduce the anomalous structures of \(^{10,11}\)Li. The results enable a consistent description of the halo structure of \(^{11}\)Li, the inversion of \(s\) and \(p\) states in \(^{10}\)Li, and the low-energy peak in the dipole strength of \(^{11}\)Li, and they provide a framework for studying other neutron-rich nuclei where tensor correlations and Pauli-blocking are important.
