# Chemical Equilibration in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> In the hadronic sector of relativistic heavy ion physics, the $ρ<=> 2π$ reaction is the strongest one, strong enough to equilibrate the $ρ$ with the pions throughout the region from chemical freezeout to thermal freezeout when free-particle interactions (with no medium-dependent effects) are employed. Above the chiral restoration temperature, only $ρ$'s and $π$'s are present, in that the chirally restored $A_1$ is equivalent to the $ρ$ and the mesons have an SU(4) symmetry, with no dependence on isospin and negligible dependence on spin. In the same sense the $σ$ and $π$ are "equivalent" scalars. Thus the chirally restored $ρ\leftrightarrows 2π$ exhaust the interspecies transitions. We evaluate this reaction at $T_c$ and find it to be much larger than below $T_c$, certainly strong enough to equilibrate the chirally restored mesons just above $T_c$. When emitted just below $T_c$ the mesons remain in equilibrium, at least in the chiral limit because of the Harada-Yamawaki "vector manifestation" that requires that mesonic coupling constants go to zero (in the chiral limit) as $T$ goes to $T_c$ from below. Our estimates in the chiral limit give deviations in some particle ratios from the standard scenario (of equilibrium in the hadronic sector just below $T_c$) of about double those indicated experimentally. This may be due to the neglect of explicit chiral symmetry breaking in our estimates. We also show that the instanton molecules present above $T_c$ are the giant multipole vibrations found by Asakawa, Hatsuda and Nakahara and of Wetzorke et al. in lattice gauge calculations. Thus, the matter formed by RHIC can equivalently be called: chirally restored mesons, instanton molecules, or giant collective vibrations. It is a strongly interacting liquid.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $\rho \leftrightarrows 2\pi$ reaction is strong enough to chemically equilibrate chirally restored mesons just above the chiral restoration temperature $T_c$, but the authors note that their estimates in the chiral limit give deviations in some particle ratios from the standard scenario of about double those indicated experimentally, which may be due to the neglect of explicit chiral symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors develop a schematic model using the Furry representation of Coulomb-bound quark-antiquark states above $T_c$, where all unperturbed $\bar q q$ states are degenerate at 1.5 GeV. They evaluate the $\rho \leftrightarrows 2\pi$ transition as a nonlinear coupling between giant collective vibrations, summing bubble diagrams in random phase approximation to include collective effects, and compare the resulting matrix element to the chirally broken case using the Harada-Yamawaki vector manifestation framework.

## Key numbers

- The free $\rho$ decay width into two pions is $\sim 150$ MeV, corresponding to a lifetime $\tau \sim 4/3$ fm/c
- The estimated width for the $\rho \leftrightarrows 2\pi$ transition at $T_c$ is $\Gamma = 380$ MeV
- The unperturbed energy of Coulomb-bound $\bar q q$ states above $T_c$ is 1.5 GeV
- The chiral restoration temperature is taken as $T_c = 175$ MeV
- The r.m.s. radius of the $\rho$-meson is $\sim 0.5$ fm
- The effective color Coulomb coupling constant $\alpha_s$ at $T_c$ is $\sim 1$
- The $\rho_0/\pi^-$ ratio measured by STAR is $0.169 \pm 0.003 (stat) \pm 0.037 (syst)$ for peripheral Au+Au collisions, exceeding thermal model predictions of 0.11 at 177 MeV

## Context

This work extends the schematic model of Brown, Lee, and Rho (2004) for relativistic heavy ion collisions, building on the observation by Braun-Munzinger, Stachel, and collaborators that hadron multiplicities are established at the phase boundary between chirally broken and chirally restored matter. The paper connects the chirally restored mesons above $T_c$ to the instanton molecules and giant multipole vibrations found in lattice gauge calculations by Asakawa, Hatsuda, and Nakahara (2003) and by Wetzorke et al., and invokes the Harada-Yamawaki vector manifestation (2001) requiring mesonic coupling constants to go to zero as $T$ approaches $T_c$ from below. The results enable a framework for understanding chemical equilibration at RHIC, though the authors note that explicit chiral symmetry breaking must be included for quantitative agreement with experimental particle ratios.
