# Jost-matrix analysis of the resonance 5He*(3/2+) near the dt-threshold

> Experimental data on the n-alpha and dt collisions in the quantum state J^pi=3/2+ near the dt-threshold are fitted using the semi-analytic multi-channel Jost matrix with proper analytic structure and some adjustable parameters. Then the spectral points are sought as zeros of the Jost matrix determinant (which correspond to the S-matrix poles) at complex energies. The correct analytic structure makes it possible to calculate the fitted Jost matrix on any sheet of the Riemann surface whose topology involves not only the square-root but also the logarithmic branching caused by the Coulomb interaction. Within a distance of 100,keV above the dt-threshold, three 3/2+ resonances are found on the non-physical sheet of the Riemann surface. Several S-matrix (shadow) poles on the other sheets of this surface are located as well.

## Key claim

Within 100 keV above the dt-threshold, three distinct 3/2+ resonances of the 5He system are found on the non-physical sheet of the Riemann surface, rather than a single resonance plus a shadow pole as many previous analyses had claimed; the authors use a semi-analytic multi-channel Jost matrix with proper analytic structure including both square-root and logarithmic branching from the Coulomb interaction.

## Method

The authors fit experimental partial cross sections for the four coupled channels (n-alpha with l=2, s=1/2; dt with l=0, s=3/2; dt with l=2, s=1/2; dt with l=2, s=3/2) using a semi-analytic two-channel Jost matrix representation where the unknown single-valued matrices A(E) and B(E) are expanded in Taylor series with real parameters. The cross sections are extracted from an existing R-matrix parametrization (Hale et al., 1990) rather than from raw experimental data. After fitting at real energies, the Jost matrix determinant is continued to complex energies to locate S-matrix poles as its zeros, with the explicit factors in the Jost matrix representation ensuring correct continuation onto any sheet of the Riemann surface.

## Key numbers

- The 5He ground-state resonance lies 798 keV above the n-alpha threshold (Tilley compilation).
- The dt-threshold opens at 16.792 MeV above the ground state (Tilley compilation).
- Three 3/2+ resonances are found within a distance of 100 keV above the dt-threshold.
- The relative kinetic energies of the fragments in the resonance state are approximately 18 MeV for the n-alpha channel and approximately 50 keV for the dt channels.

## Context

This work extends the multi-channel Jost matrix formalism developed by Rakityansky and collaborators (Rakityansky et al., 2007; Rakityansky, 2017) to include Coulomb logarithmic branching, and applies it to the long-standing controversy over the nature of the 5He*(3/2+) state near the dt-threshold. Previous theoretical analyses (Csoto and Lovas, 1995; Csoto and Hale, 1997; Barker, 1998; Hale and Brown, 1999) had claimed this state is a superposition of a conventional resonance and a shadow S-matrix pole. The present analysis finds three resonances instead, and locates several additional shadow poles on other sheets of the Riemann surface, enabling more accurate characterization of this system relevant to nuclear astrophysics and thermonuclear fusion.
