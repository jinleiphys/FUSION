# Neutron-Proton Mass Difference in Nuclear Matter and in Finite Nuclei and the Nolen-Schiffer Anomaly

> The neutron-proton mass difference in (isospin asymmetric) nuclear matter and finite nuclei is studied in the framework of a medium-modified Skyrme model. The proposed effective Lagrangian incorporates both the medium influence of the surrounding nuclear environment on the single nucleon properties and an explicit isospin-breaking effect in the mesonic sector. Energy-dependent charged and neutral pion optical potentials in the s- and p-wave channels are included as well. The present approach predicts that the neutron-proton mass difference is mainly dictated by its strong part and that it markedly decreases in neutron matter. Furthermore, the possible interplay between the effective nucleon mass in finite nuclei and the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly is discussed. In particular, we find that a correct description of the properties of mirror nuclei leads to a stringent restriction of possible modifications of the nucleon's effective mass in nuclei.

## Key claim
The neutron-proton mass difference in nuclear matter and finite nuclei is mainly dictated by its strong part and markedly decreases in neutron matter, but the model predicts that a correct description of mirror nuclei properties leads to a stringent restriction on possible modifications of the nucleon's effective mass in nuclei.

## Method
The authors employed a medium-modified Skyrme model that incorporates explicit isospin-breaking in the mesonic sector via a generalized symmetry-breaking term, combined with energy-dependent charged and neutral pion optical potentials in the s- and p-wave channels. The nucleon was treated as a skyrmion placed at the center or at varying distances from the center of finite nuclei (14N, 16O, 38K, 40Ca), and the model was quantized using collective coordinates to extract the in-medium nucleon masses and the neutron-proton mass splitting.

## Key numbers
- In free space, the total neutron-proton mass difference is 1.291 MeV, with an electromagnetic contribution of -0.686 MeV.
- At the center of 40Ca (giving 41Sc), the in-medium proton mass is 557.621 MeV, the total neutron-proton mass difference is 1.804 MeV, and the electromagnetic part is -0.478 MeV.
- At the center of 16O (giving 17F), the in-medium proton mass is 585.487 MeV, the total neutron-proton mass difference is 1.697 MeV, and the electromagnetic part is -0.517 MeV.
- The strong part of the in-medium neutron-proton mass difference in free space is 2.0 MeV (derived from a_c = 1.291 + 0.686 MeV).
- The strong part of the neutron-proton mass difference in pure neutron matter decreases very rapidly with increasing density, in contrast to the moderate increase seen in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of the neutron-proton mass difference in symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter (Meißner et al., 2009) to finite nuclei, and addresses the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly which has been investigated by many approaches (Nolen and Schiffer, 1969; Shlomo, 1978; and others) but remains not fully understood. The results enable more precise modeling of mirror nuclei properties and the stability of drip-line nuclei, and impose constraints on the effective nucleon mass in nuclear environments.
