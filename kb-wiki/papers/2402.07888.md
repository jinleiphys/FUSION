# Statistical analysis of the fluctuations of an initial-state model with independently distributed hot spots

> We determine the uncorrelated modes that characterize the fluctuations in a semi-realistic model for the initial state of high-energy nuclear collisions, consisting of hot spots whose positions are distributed independently. Varying the number of hot spots, their size, and the weights with which they contribute to the initial state, we find that the parameter that has the largest influence on the relative importance of the fluctuation modes is the source size, with more extended hot spots leading to a more marked predominance of the principal modes.

## Key claim

The parameter that has the largest influence on the relative importance of the fluctuation modes in the independent hot spot model is the source size, with more extended hot spots leading to a more marked predominance of the principal modes, while the number of hot spots and their weights have a smaller effect.

## Method

The authors generated ensembles of 2^21 initial-state profiles using an independent hot spot model (IHSM) where each profile is a superposition of Gaussian sources with width σ_src, whose positions are drawn from a two-dimensional Gaussian distribution with widths σ_x and σ_y. They computed the average state and the fluctuation modes by diagonalizing the density matrix ρ = (1/N_ev) Σ_i Φ^(i) Φ^(i)T - Ψ̄ Ψ̄^T, where the eigenvectors give the fluctuation modes and the eigenvalues λ_l give their squared norms. The relative importance of each mode was quantified by the weights w_l = √λ_l / (Σ_l √λ_l + ||Ψ̄||).

## Key numbers

- For hot spots with σ_src = 0.3 fm and σ_x = σ_y = 4 fm, the mean square width of the average state is {y^2}_Ψ̄^{1/2} = 4.00 fm, compared to the theoretical value (σ_y^2 + σ_src^2)^{1/2} = 4.01 fm.
- For hot spots with σ_src = 0.7 fm and σ_x = σ_y = 4 fm, the mean square width of the average state is {y^2}_Ψ̄^{1/2} = 4.03 fm, compared to the theoretical value (σ_y^2 + σ_src^2)^{1/2} = 4.06 fm.
- For hot spots with σ_src = 0.3 fm and σ_x = 2 fm, σ_y = 4 fm, the mean square width along x is {x^2}_Ψ̄^{1/2} = 2.03 fm, compared to the theoretical value (σ_x^2 + σ_src^2)^{1/2} = 2.02 fm.
- For hot spots with σ_src = 0.7 fm and σ_x = 2 fm, σ_y = 4 fm, the mean square width along x is {x^2}_Ψ̄^{1/2} = 2.12 fm, compared to the theoretical value (σ_x^2 + σ_src^2)^{1/2} = 2.12 fm.
- For pointlike sources (σ_src = 0) with σ_x = σ_y = 4 fm, the mean square width of the average state is {x^2}_Ψ̄^{1/2} = {y^2}_Ψ̄^{1/2} = 3.97 fm, compared to the expected value of 4 fm.
- The number of hot spots N_src = 50, 250, and 750 correspond to impact parameters b ≈ 12.5, 10, and 7.5 fm respectively for Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV.
- The grid used for simulations had N_s^2 = 128 × 128 sites with spacing a ≈ 0.24 fm.
- Each run consisted of N_ev = 2^21 initial states.

## Context

This work extends the mode decomposition method introduced by Borghini (2022) for realistic initial-state models (MC Glauber and saturation-based approaches) by applying it to a simpler independent hot spot model where parameters can be systematically varied. The IHSM is based on the model first introduced by Bhalerao (2006) with pointlike sources and later elaborated by Bhalerao (2011) with Gaussian sources and by Blaizot (2014) with fluctuating numbers of sources and weights. The results enable understanding of which physical features of the initial state control the spectrum of fluctuation modes, which is relevant for Bayesian analyses where the source size is a free parameter that significantly impacts the determination of transport coefficients of QCD matter.
