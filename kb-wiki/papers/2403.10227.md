# Role of magnetic fields on the outer crust in a magnetar

> We explore the properties of 4110 nuclides from Z = 5 to Z = 82 with the Sky3D code and the composition of the outer crust in the magnetars under extreme magnetic fields. The effects of the variation of the nuclear masses due to the magnetic fields on the outer crust are comprehensively studied. The neutron-drip transition pressure, the equation of state and neutron fraction in the outer crust have also been discussed.

# Role of magnetic fields on the outer crust in a magnetar

## Key claim
The authors show that for magnetic fields above approximately 10^17 G, the variation of nuclear masses due to the field significantly alters the outer crust composition of a magnetar, including the disappearance of magic-number plateaus at N=82 and N=126 when nuclear mass changes are included, but only if the field exceeds about 4.1 x 10^17 G where energy level rearrangement begins.

## Method
The authors used the Sky3D code based on Skyrme density functional theory (SLy6 force) to solve time-independent Hartree-Fock equations on a three-dimensional grid for 4110 nuclides from Z=5 to Z=82. They calculated nuclear masses, binding energies, radii, and deformation parameters under magnetic fields from 0 G to 10^18 G, then used the Gibbs free energy minimization method of Baym, Pethick, and Sutherland (1971) to determine the equilibrium outer crust composition. The magnetic field effects on electrons were treated via Landau-Rabi quantization, while nuclear mass changes were included by adding magnetic Hamiltonian terms for proton orbital and spin interactions and neutron spin interactions.

## Key numbers
- Binding energies of the 4110 nuclides increase by 8% to 15% at B = 10^18 G compared to B = 0 G
- The critical field B_c = 4.41 x 10^13 G (electron cyclotron energy equals rest mass energy)
- At B = 10^18 G, the neutron-drip pressure P_drip in the case including nuclear mass changes (B_n = B) increases by about 20% compared to the case without (B_n = 0)
- The maximum pressure at the outer-inner crust boundary for all models at B = 0 G is approximately 4.7 x 10^-4 MeV fm^-3
- Nuclear radii, deformation beta, and gamma are nearly unchanged for B less than or equal to 4.1 x 10^17 G
- At B = 10^16 G, the outermost nuclide is 56Fe appearing at the ionization pressure, and the neutron-drip nuclide is 120Sr at P_max = 4.49 x 10^-4 MeV fm^-3
- At B = 10^17 G with B_n = B, the outermost nuclide is 56Fe and the neutron-drip nuclide is 120Sr at P_max = 1.20 x 10^-3 MeV fm^-3
- At B = 5 x 10^17 G with B_n = B, the outermost nuclide is 92Nb and the neutron-drip nuclide is 182Ba at P_max = 6.27 x 10^-3 MeV fm^-3
- At B = 10^18 G with B_n = B, the outermost nuclide is 72Ge and the neutron-drip nuclide is 196Ce at P_max = 1.43 x 10^-2 MeV fm^-3

## Context
This work extends the method of Baym, Pethick, and Sutherland (1971) for determining neutron star outer crust composition by including magnetic field effects on both electrons and nuclear masses. It builds on calculations by Chamel et al. (2012) and Basu et al. (2015) who studied crust composition under magnetic fields but with less comprehensive treatment of nuclear mass changes. The authors use the Sky3D code following the approach of Stein et al. (2016) for nuclear structure under magnetic fields. The results enable more accurate modeling of magnetar crust properties including neutron-drip pressure, equation of state, and neutron fraction, which are relevant for understanding magnetar oscillations and cooling as studied by Carone et al. (2006), Watts et al. (2007), and Potekhin et al. (2018). The finding that N=126 nuclei can appear in the outer crust at B = 10^18 G contradicts the conclusion of Basu et al. (2015) who used a preset upper limit on nuclide mass, and agrees with Sekizawa et al. (2023) who used the HFB method without magnetic effects on nuclear masses.
