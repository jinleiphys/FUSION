# Description of moment of inertia and the interplay between anti-pairing and pairing correlations in even-even $^{244}$Pu and $^{248}$Cm

> Within the supersymmetry scheme, which includes many-body interactions and a perturbation possessing the SO(5) (or SU(5)) symmetry, the rotational bands of the $A\sim 250$ mass region are studied systematically. A novel modification is introduced, extending the Arima coefficient to the third order. This study is dedicated to the quantitative analysis of evolving trends in intraband $γ$-transition energy, kinematic, and the dynamic moment of inertia within the rotational bands of $^{244}$Pu and $^{248}$Cm. The computed outcomes exhibit an exceptional degree of agreement with experimental observations across various conditions. The significance of including a higher-order Arima coefficient is further examined by contrasting it with the previously suggested model. The calculated results demonstrate the significance of both the anti-pairing and pairing effects in the evolution of the dynamic moment of inertia. Additionally, these insights reveal the importance of a newly introduced parameter in accurately depicting complex nuclear behaviors such as back-bending, up-bending, and the downturn in the moment of inertia.

## Key claim

The paper claims that extending the Arima coefficient to include a third-order term ($f_3$) in the perturbed $SU_{sdg}(3)$ symmetry model with $SO_{sdg}(5)$ perturbation significantly improves the description of rotational bands in $^{244}$Pu and $^{248}$Cm, particularly for reproducing complex behaviors such as back-bending, up-bending, and the downturn in the dynamic moment of inertia, contingent on the inclusion of this higher-order parameter.

## Method

The authors employed a variable moment of inertia (VMI) inspired interacting boson model (IBM) with a Hamiltonian incorporating a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction and a spin-dependent term. They extended the Arima coefficient $f$ from the previously used form $f = f_1 + f_2[I(I+1)]$ to a third-order expression $f = f_1 + f_2[I(I+1)] + f_3[I(I+1)]^2$, and applied this perturbed $SU_{sdg}(3)$ symmetry with $SO_{sdg}(5)$ perturbation to calculate intraband gamma-transition energies, kinematic moment of inertia, and dynamic moment of inertia for four rotational bands each in $^{244}$Pu and $^{248}$Cm. The model parameters were obtained through least-squares fitting to experimental data, and results were compared across three parameter sets: Set A (no $f_2$ or $f_3$), Set B (including $f_2$), and Set C (including $f_2$ and $f_3$).

## Key numbers

- For $^{244}$Pu band 1, the RMS deviation $\chi$ between calculated and experimental $E_\gamma$ transitions improved from $46.6 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set A) and $49.5 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set B) to $29.8 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set C with $f_3 = -2.608 \times 10^{-10}$).
- For $^{244}$Pu band 2, the RMS deviation $\chi$ improved from $6.40 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set A) and $7.06 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set B) to $1.85 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set C with $f_3 = -5.008 \times 10^{-10}$).
- For $^{244}$Pu band 3, the RMS deviation $\chi$ improved from $7.07 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set A) and $2.49 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set B) to $0.91 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set C with $f_3 = 6.767 \times 10^{-10}$).
- For $^{244}$Pu band 4, the RMS deviation $\chi$ improved from $2.60 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set A) and $1.07 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set B) to $0.71 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set C with $f_3 = 4.978 \times 10^{-10}$).
- For $^{248}$Cm band 1, the RMS deviation $\chi$ improved from $40.8 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set A) and $11.4 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set B) to $1.60 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set C with $f_3 = -9.807 \times 10^{-11}$).
- For $^{248}$Cm band 2, the RMS deviation $\chi$ improved from $4.39 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set A) and $0.71 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set B) to $0.60 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set C with $f_3 = -4.601 \times 10^{-11}$).
- For $^{248}$Cm band 3, the RMS deviation $\chi$ improved from $8.14 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set A) and $8.50 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set B) to $5.70 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set C with $f_3 = -7.309 \times 10^{-10}$).
- For $^{248}$Cm band 4, the RMS deviation $\chi$ improved from $3.27 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set A) and $1.30 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set B) to $0.34 \times 10^{-3}$ (Set C with $f_3 = -2.903 \times 10^{-10}$).

## Context

This work extends the perturbed $SU_{sdg}(3)$ symmetry model with $SO_{sdg}(5)$ perturbation previously developed by Yuxin and collaborators (Yuxin, various years) for describing rotational bands in the $A\sim250$ mass region, and builds upon the VMI-inspired IBM approach where the Arima coefficient was first introduced by Yoshida (Yoshida) and later extended to second order by Yuxin and collaborators (Yuxin, various years). The study specifically addresses the observed upbending in heavier Pu isotopes ($A\geq241$) reported by PhysRevLett.83.2143 and the smooth up-bend followed by a downturn in dynamic MoI for Cm isotopes attributed to interplay between $j_{15/2}$ neutrons and $i_{13/2}$ protons (PhysRevLett.46.415, RBPiercey1993). The improved description of back-bending, up-bending, and downturn behaviors enables more accurate modeling of high-spin phenomena in deformed nuclei near the superheavy element region.
