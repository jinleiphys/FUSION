# End-point singularities of Feynman graphs on the light cone

> We show that some Lorentz components of the Feynman integrals calculated in terms of the light-cone variables may contain end-point singularities which originate from the contribution of the big-circle integral in the complex k_ plane. These singularities appear in various types of diagrams (two-point functions, three-point functions, etc) and provide the covariance of the Feynman integrals on the light-cone. We propose a procedure for calculating Feynman integrals which guarantees that the end-point singularities do not appear in the light-cone representations of the invariant amplitudes.

# End-point singularities of Feynman graphs on the light cone

## Key claim

End-point singularities (delta functions and their derivatives in the longitudinal momentum fraction x) appear in light-cone calculations of Feynman integrals only when some Lorentz indices are equal to the minus component; these singularities are not physically related to pair creation processes but are mathematical remnants of the big-circle integration in the complex k_- plane, and they can be entirely avoided by extracting invariant amplitudes from only the "good" (plus and transverse) components.

## Method

The authors analyze two-point and three-point Feynman integrals using light-cone variables in a frame where the transverse momentum of the external momentum is zero. They decompose the k_- integration contour into a spectator-pole contribution and a big-circle contribution, then examine the behavior of the big-circle integral as the contour radius R goes to infinity. For components with minus indices, the big-circle integral yields finite contributions proportional to delta(1-x) or delta(x) at the endpoints x=0 or x=1, while for components with only plus and transverse indices the big-circle contribution vanishes. The procedure is demonstrated explicitly for a two-point function with one scalar propagator squared and for general three-point functions.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of "longitudinal zero modes" previously discussed in the literature (cited as "zero-mode" references) for three-point functions, where singular terms of the form delta(x), delta(1-x) and their derivatives were found. The authors disagree with the interpretation in that prior work that these singularities are related to pair creation processes or indicate inconsistency of the one-body current approximation. Instead, they show the singularities arise directly at q_+ = 0 from the big-circle integration and can be avoided by using only plus and transverse components. The procedure has been applied by the authors (Melikhov and Simula, cited as "our") to electromagnetic form factors of spin-0, spin-1/2, and spin-1 bound states. The work enables clean extraction of invariant amplitudes from light-cone calculations without needing to handle singular endpoint contributions explicitly.
