# Collective Multipole excitations of exotic nuclei in relativistic continuum random phase approximation

> Journal of Combinatorial Theory, Series B, 98(1):173-225, 2008n exotic nuclei are studied in the framework of a fully self-consistent relativistic continuum random phase approximation (RCRPA). In this method the contribution of the continuum spectrum to nuclear excitations is treated exactly by the single particle Green's function. Different from the cases in stable nuclei, there are strong low-energy excitations in neutron-rich nuclei and proton-rich nuclei. The neutron or proton excess pushes the centroid of the strength function to lower energies and increases the fragmentation of the strength distribution. The effect of treating the contribution of continuum exactly are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in neutron-rich and proton-rich exotic nuclei, the neutron or proton excess pushes the centroid of the strength function to lower energies and increases fragmentation of the strength distribution, with strong low-energy excitations appearing that are absent in beta-stable nuclei; these effects are captured by treating the continuum exactly via the single-particle Green's function, which the abstract notes but does not quantify for specific isotopes.

## Method

The authors constructed a fully self-consistent relativistic continuum random phase approximation (RCRPA) in momentum representation, where the continuum contribution to nuclear excitations is treated exactly using the single-particle Green's function. They applied this method to compute isoscalar and isovector multipole response functions (L=0-3) for a set of sub-closed shell and magic nuclei: 34Ca, 40Ca, 48Ca, 60Ca, 16O, 28O, 100Sn, and 132Sn, with pairing not included in sub-closed shell nuclei. The RCRPA results were compared with standard relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA) calculations that discretize the continuum via box boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

- For 60Ca (neutron-rich), the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) response shows a sharp peak from single-particle resonances in the continuum that is not reproduced by RRPA; the RRPA artificial width was set to 2 MeV.
- The response functions of 40Ca and 48Ca (stable nuclei) are similar for the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR), while the response of 60Ca is shifted to lower-energy region remarkably, and the response of 34Ca (proton-rich) separates into higher-energy and low-energy excitations.
- In the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) and isoscalar giant dipole resonance (ISGDR), strong low-energy strength is found in 60Ca, with smaller but present low-energy excitation in 34Ca.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic continuum random phase approximation method previously developed by Yang, Ding, Cao, and Ma (2009, 2010) to study multipole excitations in exotic nuclei, building on earlier self-consistent relativistic RPA (RRPA) calculations by Ma (2001, 2002), Ring (2001), and Pie (2001). The results contrast with standard RRPA calculations that discretize the continuum via box boundary conditions, showing that exact continuum treatment is essential for neutron-rich and proton-rich nuclei where the Fermi surface is close to the particle continuum, as noted by Cao (2004). This enables more accurate predictions of giant resonance properties in nuclei far from stability, relevant for future radioactive beam facilities.
