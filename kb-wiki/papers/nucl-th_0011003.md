# Kaon Condensation in Neutron Stars

> We discuss the kaon-nucleon interaction and its consequences for the change of the properties of the kaon in the medium. The onset of kaon condensation in neutron stars under various scenarios as well its effects for neutron star properties are reviewed.

# Kaon Condensation in Neutron Stars

## Key claim

The paper reviews theoretical models for kaon-nucleon interactions in dense matter and their implications for kaon condensation in neutron stars, but emphasizes that the actual depth of the antikaon optical potential remains model-dependent, with values ranging from -45 MeV to -140 MeV depending on whether pions are dressed and which approximation scheme is used.

## Method

The authors construct in-medium antikaon-nucleon scattering amplitudes by solving the coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation, using either the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian or the Bonn meson-exchange potential as the driving term. Medium effects are incorporated through Pauli blocking on nucleons, self-consistent dressing of the antikaon propagator, and in some cases dressing of pions via their spectral functions. The resulting in-medium T-matrix is then folded over the nucleon Fermi sea to obtain the antikaon self-energy and optical potential.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum chiral condensate energy density is approximately 500 MeV/fm^3.
- Nuclear saturation density is rho_0 = 0.16/fm^3 (stated as 0.17 fm^{-1} in one passage).
- The leading-order model-independent result for the quark condensate in matter is <qq>/<qq>_0 = 1 - T^2/(8 f_pi^2) - 0.3 rho/rho_0.
- The antikaon optical potential depth at zero momentum and nuclear saturation density from the chiral model with dressed pions is -45 MeV.
- Other models give potential depths between -75 MeV and -140 MeV at nuclear saturation density.
- The transition from repulsive to attractive effective K^-N scattering length occurs at a density of about 0.1 rho_0 with only Pauli blocking, and at about 0.04 rho_0 when meson self-energies are included.
- The nucleon potential depth used is U_0^N = -70 MeV; the Lambda hyperon potential depth is U_0^Lambda = -30 MeV.

## Context

This review extends the earlier suggestion by Kaplan and Nelson (1986) that kaon condensation could occur in dense neutron star matter, and builds on subsequent work by Brown (1992), Thorsson (1994), and others on the equation of state with kaon condensates. The paper contrasts two theoretical approaches: mean-field models using chiral Lagrangians or Walecka-type models, and microscopic approaches that dynamically generate the Lambda(1405) resonance from coupled-channel calculations. The results enable more realistic assessments of whether kaon condensation sets in at densities achievable in neutron star interiors, and how the presence of hyperons affects the onset density.
