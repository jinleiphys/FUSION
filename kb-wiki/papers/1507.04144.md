# Proton-neutron pairing in N=Z nuclei: quartetting versus pair condensation

> The isoscalar proton-neutron pairing and isovector pairing, including both isovector proton-neutron pairing and like-particle pairing, are treated in a formalism which conserves exactly the particle number and the isospin. The formalism is designed for self-conjugate (N=Z) systems of nucleons moving in axially deformed mean fields and interacting through the most general isovector and isoscalar pairing interactions. The ground state of these systems is described by a superposition of two types of condensates, i.e., condensates of isovector quartets, built by two isovector pairs coupled to the total isospin T=0, and condensates of isoscalar proton-neutron pairs. The comparison with the exact solutions of realistic isovector-isoscalar pairing Hamiltonians shows that this ansatz for the ground state is able to describe with high precision the pairing correlation energies. It is also shown that, at variance with the majority of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations, in the present formalism the isovector and isoscalar pairing correlations coexist for any pairing interactions. The competition between the isovector and isoscalar proton-neutron pairing correlations is studied for N=Z nuclei with the valence nucleons moving in the $sd$ and $pf$ shells and in the major shell above $^{100}$Sn. We find that in these nuclei the isovector pairing prevail over the isoscalar pairing, especially for heavier nuclei. However, the isoscalar proton-neutron correlations are significant in all nuclei and they always coexist with the isovector pairing correlations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in N=Z nuclei, isovector and isoscalar proton-neutron pairing correlations always coexist for any pairing interaction, contrary to most Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations which find they do not coexist; however, this result is conditional on the use of pairing forces acting on time-reversed and axially-deformed states, and the isovector pairing is found to prevail over the isoscalar pairing, especially in heavier nuclei.

## Method

The authors construct a trial ground state for N=Z systems as a superposition of two types of condensates: condensates of isovector quartets (built from two isovector pairs coupled to total isospin T=0) and condensates of isoscalar proton-neutron pairs, using collective pair operators defined for axially-deformed mean fields. The amplitudes defining the collectivity of the pairs are determined variationally by minimizing the average of a Hamiltonian containing both isovector and isoscalar pairing interactions, with the particle number and isospin conserved exactly. The formalism is applied to N=Z nuclei with valence nucleons in the sd and pf shells and above 100Sn, using single-particle states from Skyrme-HF calculations with the Sly4 force and a zero-range delta pairing interaction.

## Key numbers

- For 20Ne, the exact pairing correlation energy is 11.38 (units not specified in text, presumably MeV); the PQCM result is 11.38 (0.00% error), the isovector-only result is 11.31 (0.62% error), and the isoscalar-only result is 10.92 (4.00% error); the overlap between isovector and isoscalar states is 0.976.
- For 44Ti, the exact pairing correlation energy is 7.095; the PQCM result is 7.094 (0.02% error), the isovector-only result is 7.08 (0.18% error), and the isoscalar-only result is 6.30 (10.78% error); the overlap is 0.928.
- For 104Te, the exact pairing correlation energy is 4.53; the PQCM result is 4.52 (0.06% error), the isovector-only result is 4.49 (0.82% error), and the isoscalar-only result is 4.02 (11.26% error); the overlap is 0.955.
- For 112Ba, the exact pairing correlation energy is 9.36; the PQCM result is 9.27 (0.93% error), the isovector-only result is 9.22 (1.43% error), and the isoscalar-only result is 7.50 (19.81% error); the overlap is 0.784.
- For a test system of 4 proton-neutron pairs in 10 equidistant levels with state-independent pairing strengths g1=g(1-x)/2 and g0=g(1+x)/2 (g=0.6 in units of level spacing), the PQCM results follow the exact pairing energies closely across the full range of x from -1 to 1.

## Context

This work extends the quartet condensation formalism previously developed for isovector pairing only (Sandulescu et al., 2010, referred to as qcm1) to include both isovector and isoscalar pairing interactions. It contradicts the majority of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations (Goodman, 1979; Bertsch et al., 2009) which find that isovector and isoscalar proton-neutron pairing correlations do not coexist. The formalism enables accurate treatment of pairing in N=Z nuclei with exact conservation of particle number and isospin, and the results suggest that the strong mixing of isovector and isoscalar correlations makes them difficult to disentangle experimentally. A similar conclusion about coexistence was found recently for spherically-symmetric Hamiltonians with J=0 and J=1 pairing forces (Sasabe et al., 2014, referred to as sasa_plb).
