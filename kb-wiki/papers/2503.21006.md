# First study of polarized proton-proton scattering with small-$x$ helicity evolution

> We perform a phenomenological study of helicity-dependent parton distribution functions (PDFs) using small-$x$ helicity evolution equations, incorporating for the first time single-inclusive jet production data in polarized proton-proton ($pp$) scattering at parton momentum fractions $x < 0.1$. We also simultaneously include double-longitudinal spin asymmetries in inclusive and semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering probing $x < 0.1$. Employing the polarized small-$x$ pure-glue calculation of $pp\to gX$ for the jet production cross section, we modify the large-$N_c\&N_f$ KPS-CTT evolution equations by setting $N_f = 0$ to replicate the large-$N_c$ (pure-glue) limit, while retaining external quark flavors for the spinor field operators. We find that the $pp$ data have a considerable impact on the helicity PDFs at small $x$, reducing their uncertainties and leading to a total quark and gluon helicity in the proton for $x < 0.1$ of $-0.04 \pm 0.23$. Combining our analysis with the a recent JAM helicity PDF analysis of the world polarized data, which includes $x > 0.1$, we find a total quark and gluon helicity contribution for $x > 10^{-7}$ of between 0.02 and 0.51.

## Key claim

This study incorporates for the first time polarized proton-proton scattering data for single-inclusive jet production into a small-$x$ helicity evolution analysis, but the polarized $pp$ jet production cross section used carries the important caveat that it is not in its final form because it was derived in a gluon-only approximation without including quarks in the $gg\to g$ channel or explicit quark production.

## Method

The authors performed a global analysis of helicity-dependent parton distribution functions (hPDFs) using the KPS-CTT small-$x$ helicity evolution equations in the double-logarithmic approximation, simultaneously fitting double-longitudinal spin asymmetries from inclusive DIS, semi-inclusive DIS, and polarized $pp$ single-inclusive jet production data for parton momentum fractions $x < 0.1$. They modified the large-$N_c\&N_f$ KPS-CTT evolution equations by setting $N_f = 0$ to replicate the large-$N_c$ (pure-glue) limit while retaining external quark flavors, creating what they call "large-$N_c^{+q}$" evolution equations, and employed the polarized small-$x$ pure-glue calculation of $pp\to gX$ for the jet production cross section.

## Key numbers

- Total quark and gluon helicity in the proton for $x < 0.1$: $-0.04 \pm 0.23$
- Total quark and gluon helicity contribution for $x > 10^{-7}$ (combining with JAM analysis for $x > 0.1$): between 0.02 and 0.51
- Infrared cutoff $\Lambda = 1$ GeV used in the analysis
- Light quark flavors included: $u$, $d$, $s$ only

## Context

This work extends the previous small-$x$ global analysis of polarized DIS and SIDIS data by Adamiak et al. (2023), which suffered from large uncertainties due to a "bimodality" of solutions where DIS and SIDIS data were insensitive to the sign of the prefactor. By incorporating polarized $pp$ data, which at leading order involve gluon interactions, the authors aim to better constrain the gluon-dominated polarized dipole amplitudes $\widetilde{G}$ and $G_2$. This enables improved predictions for the $g_1$ structure function and helicity PDFs at small $x$, and the authors also perform a preliminary matching exercise between their small-$x$ $\Delta g$ solutions and the DGLAP-based JAM $\Delta g$ extraction by Zhou et al. (2022) and Hunt-Smith et al. (2024).
