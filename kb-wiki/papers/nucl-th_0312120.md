# Sequence of Potentials Interpolating between the U(5) and E(5) Symmetries

> It is proved that the potentials of the form $β^{2n}$ (with $n$ being integer) provide a ``bridge'' between the U(5) symmetry of the Bohr Hamiltonian with a harmonic oscillator potential (occuring for $n=1$) and the E(5) model of Iachello (Bohr Hamiltonian with an infinite well potential, materialized for infinite $n$). Parameter-free (up to overall scale factors) predictions for spectra and B(E2) transition rates are given for the potentials $β^4$, $β^6$, $β^8$, corresponding to $R_4=E(4)/E(2)$ ratios of 2.093, 2.135, 2.157 respectively, compared to the $R_4$ ratios 2.000 of U(5) and 2.199 of E(5). Hints about nuclei showing this behaviour, as well as about potentials ``bridging'' the E(5) symmetry with O(6) are briefly discussed. A note about the appearance of Bessel functions in the framework of E(n) symmetries is given as a by-product.

# Sequence of Potentials Interpolating between the U(5) and E(5) Symmetries

## Key claim

The paper proves that potentials of the form β^(2n) (with n integer) form a continuous bridge between the U(5) symmetry of the Bohr Hamiltonian with a harmonic oscillator potential (n=1) and the E(5) model with an infinite well potential (n→∞), but the practical claim is that n=4 already yields results quite close to the E(5) limit, and the intermediate potentials β^4, β^6, and β^8 provide parameter-free predictions for spectra and B(E2) transition rates that smoothly interpolate between the two limits.

## Method

The authors solve the Bohr Hamiltonian radial equation numerically for potentials u(β) = β^(2n)/2 with n=2,3,4, using two independent methods: a matrix representation of position and momentum operators, and a direct integration of the differential equation for each seniority τ value. The eigenfunctions are classified under the common SO(5)⊃SO(3) chain of subalgebras, and B(E2) transition rates are computed from integrals of the radial wavefunctions using the standard quadrupole operator.

## Key numbers

- R₄ = E(4)/E(2) ratios: 2.093 for β^4 potential, 2.135 for β^6 potential, 2.157 for β^8 potential, compared to 2.000 for U(5) and 2.199 for E(5)
- For the control-parameter potential u(β) = (1-η)β²/2 + η(1-β²)²/4, the R₄ ratios are 2.00 (η=0), 2.06 (η=1/4), 2.09 (η=1/2), 2.11 (η=3/4), 2.13 (η=1)
- The β^4 potential (n=2) corresponds to the flat potential occurring at η=1/2 in the control-parameter family, giving R₄=2.09
- Preliminary experimental candidates: ⁹⁸Ru for E(5)-β^6, ¹⁰⁰Pd for E(5)-β^4

## Context

This work extends the E(5) model of Iachello (2001) and the X(5) model of Iachello (2001), which describe shape phase transitions in atomic nuclei using infinite well potentials in the Bohr Hamiltonian. The β^4 potential had previously received attention as the critical potential for the U(5) to O(6) shape phase transition in the Interacting Boson Model (Arias, 2003). The paper provides a systematic sequence of exactly soluble-like models that bridge the U(5) vibrational symmetry and the E(5) critical-point symmetry, enabling direct comparison with experimental data for nuclei near shape phase transitions. The authors suggest future work on sequences bridging U(5) to X(5) (carried out in a companion paper) and on Davidson-like potentials to approach E(5) from the O(6) side.
