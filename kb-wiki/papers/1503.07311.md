# Effects of the Inverse Square Potential on Nucleon-Nucleon Elastic Scattering in the Bethe-Salpeter Equation

> Bethe-Salpeter equation is applied to nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering at the intermediate energy. The differential cross section and the polarization are calculated in terms of the phase shift analysis method using the two-body potential derived from the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The lowest-order Born approximation for the K-matrix is corrected by including the inverse square part of the potential.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter equation, when corrected by including the inverse square part of the potential derived from the Feynman propagator, improves the lowest-order Born approximation for the K-matrix and yields a V-shaped differential cross section and sign-correct polarization for nucleon-nucleon elastic scattering at intermediate energy, though the polarization remains underestimated at forward angles compared to experiment.

## Method

The author solves the Bethe-Salpeter equation in configuration space by expanding the amplitude in Gamma matrices, obtaining a Schrödinger-like equation for the pseudoscalar component (singlet S=0) and the polar-tensor components (triplet S=1). The phase shift parameters are computed using the nuclear bar phase shift method, where the K-matrix elements are obtained from an integral involving the spherical Bessel function and the exact wave function under the potential. The lowest-order Born approximation is corrected by replacing the spherical Bessel function with an analytic solution that includes the inverse square potential part, derived from the pseudovector coupling of the pion with a four-dimensional cut-off function, applied specifically to the (L,J)=(0,1) state for p-n scattering and the (1,2) state for p-p scattering.

## Key numbers

- Laboratory energy E = 310 MeV for both p-n and p-p elastic scattering calculations.
- The cut-off parameter Λ₀ is tentatively set to 500–600 MeV, based on deuteron binding energy calculations.
- For p-p scattering, the pion-nucleon pseudovector coupling constant f is reduced to f ∼ 0.05 from the standard value f ≈ 1 at E = 310 MeV.
- The strength g of the inverse square potential part is given by g' = (g_σ² − 4g_ω²)/(2π)² + (3f²Λ²)/((2π)²m_π²) for the S=T=0 p-n system.
- The maximum experimental polarization reaches 0.4 at around θ_c = 30° for p-n scattering, while the calculation underestimates this value.
- The inverse square potential strength g' is constrained to −∞ < g' ≤ 1/4 to keep the phase shift method valid for p-p scattering.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous application of the ladder-approximated Bethe-Salpeter equation to the deuteron bound state (Kinpara, 2015), now treating scattering states for both p-n and p-p systems. The approach contrasts with nonrelativistic potential models by retaining the inverse square potential from the relativistic propagator, which modifies the short-range behavior. The results enable systematic higher-order calculations within the Bethe-Salpeter formalism, though the author notes that the approximate wave function used for the K-matrix correction needs improvement, and that the ladder approximation alone is insufficient for p-p scattering, requiring suppression of the π⁰ exchange force to account for higher-order multi-pion processes.
