# Coupling of giant resonances to soft E1 and E2 modes in B-8

> The dynamic coupling between giant resonance states and "soft", low-energy excitation, modes in weakly-bound nuclei is investigated. A coupled-channels calculation is reported for the reaction 8B + Pb --> p + 7Be + Pb at 83 MeV/nucleon. It is shown that the low-energy response is only marginally modified by transitions to the isovector giant dipole and isoscalar giant quadrupole resonances.

## Key claim

The dynamic coupling between giant resonance states and low-energy "soft" excitation modes in weakly-bound nuclei is investigated, and it is shown that the low-energy response is only marginally modified by transitions to the isovector giant dipole and isoscalar giant quadrupole resonances, with the correction being no larger than 2% for the whole energy interval.

## Method

A continuum discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) calculation was performed for the reaction 8B + Pb → p + 7Be + Pb at 83 MeV/nucleon, including both nuclear and Coulomb induced breakup. The giant dipole resonance (GDR, λ=1) and giant quadrupole resonance (GQR, λ=2) were described using Breit-Wigner functions centered at 30 MeV and 20 MeV respectively, with widths of 4 MeV, assuming they exhaust 100% of the energy-weighted sum-rule. The low-energy E1 and E2 soft modes were calculated using a potential model of a proton + 7Be-core, and the coupled-channels code DWEIKO was used with relativistic dynamics.

## Key numbers

- The energy-weighted sum-rule for 8B yields S_E1^total = 28 e² fm² MeV and S_E2^total = 890 e² fm⁴ MeV.
- The energy-integrated multipole response of the soft modes is S_E1^SD = 0.546 e² fm² MeV and S_E2^SD = 396 e² fm⁴ MeV.
- The E2 soft mode exhausts 44% of the EWSR.
- The total excitation cross sections for soft modes are σ_E1^SD = 370 mb and σ_E2^SD = 236 mb, while for giant resonances they are σ_GDR = 2.5 mb and σ_GQR = 6.5 mb.
- The coupled-channels correction to first-order perturbation results is no larger than 2% for the whole energy interval.
- The matter radius used for 8B is R_m = 2.38 fm, obtained from a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculation.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of continuum-continuum transitions in weakly-bound nuclei (Bertulani and Canto, 1992) and the Coulomb breakup of 8B (Christley et al., 1996; Esbensen and Bertsch, 1996). The finding that giant resonances have negligible influence on soft modes in 8B suggests a similar conclusion for other weakly-bound nuclei, but the author notes that the situation may differ for heavier neutron- or proton-rich nuclei near the dripline, where soft multipole modes are likely to be influenced by dynamic coupling to giant resonance states.
