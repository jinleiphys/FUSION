# Testing Skyrme energy-density functionals with the QRPA in low-lying vibrational states of rare-earth nuclei

> Although nuclear energy density functionals are determined primarily by fitting to ground state properties, they are often applied in nuclear astrophysics to excited states, usually through the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA). Here we test the Skyrme functionals SkM* and SLy4 along with the self-consistent QRPA by calculating properties of low-lying vibrational states in a large number of well-deformed even-even rare-earth nuclei. We reproduce trends in energies and transition probabilities associated with gamma-vibrational states, but our results are not perfect and indicate the presences of multi-particle-hole correlations that are not included in the QRPA. The Skyrme functional SkM* performs noticeably better than SLy4. In a few nuclei, changes in the treatment of the pairing energy functional have a significant effect. The QRPA is less successful with "beta-vibrational" states than with the gamma-vibrational states.

## Key claim

The Skyrme functionals SkM* and SLy4, when used with the self-consistent QRPA, reproduce trends in energies and transition probabilities for gamma-vibrational states in well-deformed rare-earth nuclei, but the results are not perfect and indicate the presence of multi-particle-hole correlations beyond the QRPA; SkM* performs noticeably better than SLy4, and the QRPA is less successful with beta-vibrational states than with gamma-vibrational states.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Vanderbilt HFB code with B-spline wave functions on a 42 by 42 cylindrical mesh, followed by self-consistent QRPA calculations using the Skyrme functionals SkM* and SLy4 with a volume-delta pairing functional. They calculated energies and E2 transition strengths for gamma-vibrational states in 27 axially-symmetric well-deformed even-even rare-earth nuclei, and for beta-vibrational states in a subset of four nuclei using SkM*.

## Key numbers

- The average logarithmic energy ratio R_E for SkM* is 0.28 with dispersion 0.18; for SLy4 it is 0.20 with dispersion 0.50
- The average logarithmic transition strength ratio R_Q for SkM* is -0.13 with dispersion 0.14; for SLy4 it is -0.004 with dispersion 0.31
- The two-quasiparticle basis dimension for gamma-vibrations is about 22000 in 172Yb, ranging from 19000 to 28000 in other rare-earth nuclei
- For beta-vibrations the two-quasiparticle space dimension is 60000 to 75000
- Calculated beta-vibrational energies: 166Yb at 1.802 MeV (experiment 1.043 MeV), 168Yb at 2.039 MeV (experiment 1.155 MeV), 172Yb at 1.605 MeV (experiment 1.117 MeV), 170Er at 1.596 MeV (experiment 0.960 MeV)
- Calculated B(E2) for beta-vibrations: 172Yb at 0.0049 e^2b^2 (experiment 0.0081(17) e^2b^2), 170Er at 0.0030 e^2b^2 (experiment 0.0079(9) e^2b^2)
- The quasiparticle cutoff energy E_cut was 60 MeV or 200 MeV, with pairing strengths adjusted to reproduce pairing gaps of 172Yb to within +/-150 keV

## Context

This work extends self-consistent QRPA calculations to heavy deformed nuclei, building on the authors' prior development of a scaled parallel Skyrme-QRPA code (Terasaki and Engel, 2010). It compares with earlier pairing-plus-QQ model calculations by Bes et al. (1965) and Marshalek et al. (1963), as well as collective model calculations by Delaroche et al. (2010) using the Gogny energy functional. The results enable future systematic studies where excited states can play an increasing role in determining nuclear density functionals, and suggest that many-body correlations beyond QRPA are needed for improved description of vibrational states.
