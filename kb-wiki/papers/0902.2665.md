# Phenomena of Time Resonances Explosions for the Compound-Clot Decays in High-Energy Nuclear Reactions

> The phenomenon of time resonances (or explosions) can explain the exponential reduction of the energy, which is accompanied for the certain degree by slight fluctuations under some conditions in the range of the energy strongly overlapped compound-resonances. These resonant explosions correspond to formation of several highly-exited non-exponentially decaying nuclear clots (partial compound nuclei consisting of several small groups of projectile nucleons and targets). This paper is a continuation and expansion of theoretical authors' work, which is a more general self-consistent version of the time-evolution approach in comparison with the traditional Izumo-Araseki time compound-nucleus model.

## Key claim

The paper argues that "time resonances" (or "explosions") can explain the exponential reduction of energy accompanied by slight fluctuations in the range of strongly overlapped compound-resonances, but this explanation is conditional on the formation of several highly-excited non-exponentially decaying nuclear clots (partial compound nuclei) and requires specific conditions where the energy spread of the wave packet is much smaller than the resonance width (ΔE ≪ Γ), contrary to the usual condition for exponential decay.

## Method

The authors develop a theoretical time-evolution approach based on the Fourier-transform of the reaction amplitude, deriving wave-packet expressions for the compound-nucleus decay probability and survival probability. They analyze the S-matrix using the Simonius representation (42) with resonance projection operators, and derive sum rules connecting the mean time delay and variance of the compound-nucleus time-delay distributions to the resonance characteristics (mean width Γ, level density ρ, and number of open channels N). The formalism is applied to the case of strongly overlapped energy resonances where πΓρ ≫ 1, leading to the time-resonance (Lorentzian in time) behavior of the wave function.

## Key numbers

- The condition for exponential decay is Γ ≪ ΔE ≪ Eγ (equation 7), where Γ is the resonance width and ΔE is the energy spread of the wave packet.
- The condition for time resonances (non-exponential decay) is ΔE ≪ Γ (equation 18), with the wave function taking a Lorentzian form in time: Ψβ(Rβ,t) ∝ 1/(t - tn + iτn/2) (equation 28).
- For strongly overlapped resonances, the condition is Γ_JSΠ ≪ N_JSΠ/(2πρ_JSΠ) (equation 41), where Γ_JSΠ is the mean resonance width, ρ_JSΠ is the level density, and N_JSΠ is the number of open channels.
- The energy-averaged compound-nucleus cross section for strongly overlapped resonances is proportional to |aαβ|² (equation 45).
- The trace of the R-matrix averaged over energy yields <Trace R_res> ≈ 4πℏ²/(DΓ) from the single-sum term (equation 58) and 4π²ℏ²⟨Trace P̃(εc,E0)P̃(ε'c,E0)⟩/D² from the double-sum term (equation 59), where D is the mean level spacing.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous theoretical paper (Olkhovsky et al., 2009) and provides a more general self-consistent version of the time-evolution approach compared to the traditional Izumo-Araseki time compound-nucleus model. The paper builds on theoretical methods from earlier works (references 13-17) and addresses difficulties in fireball models (references 1,5) regarding why statistical equilibrium forms even at high excitations above 100 MeV/nucleon. The results enable interpretation of structureless exponentially decaying energy spectra with slight oscillations observed in reactions with projectiles from p to ²⁰Ne at energies above 0.1-1 GeV/nucleon (references 1-8) and for heavier projectiles at smaller energies (reference 9), suggesting formation of high-density collision complexes.
