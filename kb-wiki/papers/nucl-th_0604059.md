# Energy Dependence of the Lambda-Sigma0 Production in Proton-Proton Collisions

> The energy dependence of the reactions pp -> p Lambda K+ and pp -> p Sigma0 K+ and the ratio R(Lambda/Sigma0} is studied in a constituent quark-gluon model, including the excitation of the baryon resonances N*(1650), N*(1710) and N*(1720) near the Lambda/Sigma0 thresholds. Representing the baryons as quark-diquark objects, the energy dependent ratio Lambda/Sigma0, which is qualitatively reproduced up to excess energies of 60 MeV above threshold, provides detailed information on the momentum spectrum of axial diquarks in the proton and the Sigma0.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the energy-dependent ratio of Lambda to Sigma0 production in proton-proton collisions can be qualitatively reproduced up to excess energies of 60 MeV above threshold using a constituent quark-gluon model, but the authors caution that the results are sensitive to diquark parameters and that the qualitative account of initial and final state interactions prevents absolute normalization of the predictions.

## Method

The authors model the reactions pp -> p Lambda K+ and pp -> p Sigma0 K+ using a constituent quark-gluon model with multiple gluon exchanges up to alpha_s^3, representing baryons as quark-diquark systems. They include the excitation of the baryon resonances N*(1650), N*(1710), and N*(1720) near the Lambda/Sigma0 thresholds, and evaluate the transition amplitudes using a Gaussian expansion of bound state wave functions, distorted waves, and the transition operator, with a final one-dimensional integration over phase space performed numerically.

## Key numbers

- The ratio R(Lambda/Sigma0) close to threshold with excess energy Q <= 10 MeV shows the Sigma0 suppressed relative to the Lambda by a factor of 30.
- At Q <= 60 MeV, the Sigma0 is still suppressed by one order of magnitude relative to the Lambda.
- For large Q > 300 MeV, the Lambda/Sigma0 ratio approaches approximately 2.5, as expected from isospin relations.
- The typical momentum transfer is Delta q ~ 900 MeV/c even at threshold.
- The scalar diquark content in the proton is set to C_S^2 = 0.95, the axial diquark content to C_A0^2 = 0.05, and the d-state admixture in the axial diquark to C_A2^2 / C_A0^2 = 0.1.
- Gaussian size parameters used are a_S = 0.5 fm for the scalar diquark and a_A = 0.55 fm for the axial diquark.
- The ratio can be reproduced nearly quantitatively with C_S^2 / C_A0^2 / C_A2^2 = 95 / 5 / 0.5 and a_S = 0.9 a_A = 0.6 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous meson-exchange models from the Julich group (involving pi and K exchange with constructive or destructive interference) and models dominated by heavy meson exchange with N* resonance excitation (references 8-15 in the paper). The quark-gluon approach provides a new perspective on the diquark structure of the proton, confirming a very large scalar diquark content of around 95% versus about 5% axial diquark, and excluding a result from another reference (ref. 34) with approximately 20% axial diquark. The analysis enables future comparisons with photo-induced hyperon production on the nucleon, where initial and final state interactions are practically absent, allowing calculations with absolute normalization.
