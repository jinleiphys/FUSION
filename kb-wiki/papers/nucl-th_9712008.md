# An efficient projection algorithm and its application to the spurious center-of-mass motion problem

> Based on the correspondence of projection to an eigenvalue problem via the underlying group structure, we generalize the projection algorithm proposed by Morrison et al. This algorithm is eligible for large-scale computations, because we can avoid the accumulation of errors. As an example, we apply it to the spurious c.m. motion in a multi-$\hbarω$ shell-model calculation. In comparison with Lawson's method, the present method is exact and nevertheless does not need longer CPU time. It is pointed out that the exact treatment of the spurious c.m. motion is crucial to some physical quantities.

# An efficient projection algorithm and its application to the spurious center-of-mass motion problem

## Key claim

The authors generalize a projection algorithm by Morrison et al. (1974) that redefines projection as an eigenvalue problem of a Casimir operator of the underlying group, and claim that for the spurious center-of-mass (c.m.) motion problem in multi-ħω shell-model calculations, this method is exact and does not require longer CPU time than Lawson's method, contrary to the abstract's implication that the comparison is straightforward, the CPU time advantage holds only when the Lawson parameter λ is large enough to require many Lanczos iterations.

## Method

The projection operator P is redefined via a hermitian operator ℋP (a Casimir operator of the relevant group) whose degenerate eigenvalues label subspaces; the relevant component belongs to the subspace with eigenvalue μ₀. The Lanczos diagonalization algorithm is applied to ℋP to generate a tridiagonal matrix, and the eigenvector corresponding to μ₀ is obtained by solving a system of coupled linear equations from the last row to the first, which avoids error accumulation. For the spurious c.m. motion, ℋP is taken as the linear Casimir operator of the U(3) group, expressed as a two-body operator in the shell-model space, and the projection is incorporated into each Lanczos iteration of the shell-model Hamiltonian diagonalization.

## Key numbers

- For the ¹⁰Be nucleus test with up to 3ħω excitations (n=4), the Lawson parameter λ was set to 0, 1, 5, and 20, with ħω = 15.5 MeV (computed as 45A⁻¹/³ − 25A⁻²/³).
- The convergence criterion for eigenstates was 0.1 keV; for λ=20, Lawson's method required almost twice as many Lanczos iterations as the present exact projection method.
- Lawson's method with λ=20 still showed a 100 keV discrepancy in energy eigenvalues compared to the exact projection.
- For the 4⁺₁ state, the expectation value of ℋP was reported as a measure of spurious component admixture (specific numerical values not given in the truncated text).
- The E1 transition strengths showed equal values between the bare operator T and the modified operator T' in the present method, while Lawson's method did not show apparent improvement.

## Context

This work extends the projection algorithm of Morrison et al. (1974), which was originally formulated for J projection and required [H, ℋP]=0, to general cases including the spurious c.m. motion where the Hamiltonian does not commute with ℋP. The method is contrasted with Lawson's method (Gloeckner and Lawson, 1974; Palumbo, 1967), which adds a penalty term λℋP to the Hamiltonian but leaves residual spurious admixture proportional to λ⁻¹. The exact projection enables reliable calculation of transition strengths sensitive to c.m. contamination, particularly for E1 transitions and small E2 transitions where Lawson's method shows significant discrepancies.
