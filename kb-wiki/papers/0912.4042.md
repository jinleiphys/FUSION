# Applications of chiral perturbation theory to lattice QCD

> These notes contain the written version of lectures given at the 2009 Les Houches Summer School "Modern perspectives in lattice QCD: Quantum field theory and high performance computing." The goal is to provide a pedagogical introduction to the subject, and not a comprehensive review. Topics covered include a general introduction, the inclusion of scaling violations in chiral perturbation theory, partial quenching and mixed actions, chiral perturbation theory with heavy kaons, and the effects of finite volume, both in the p- and epsilon-regimes.

## Key claim

These lecture notes provide a pedagogical introduction to chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) and its applications to lattice QCD, emphasizing that ChPT serves as a tool to connect "unphysical" lattice computations (e.g., at larger-than-physical quark masses, nonzero lattice spacing, finite volume, or with partial quenching) to physical quantities, rather than offering a comprehensive review of all work in the field.

## Method

The author constructs the low-energy effective field theory for the pseudo-Nambu-Goldstone bosons (pions, kaons, eta) of QCD by writing the most general local, Lorentz-invariant Lagrangian in terms of the unitary field Sigma = exp(2i phi/f), organized in a derivative expansion. The Lagrangian is required to obey the SU(3)_L x SU(3)_R chiral symmetry of massless QCD, with quark masses introduced as spurion fields that transform under the symmetry. The leading-order (two-derivative) term is unique, and higher-order terms with four or more derivatives are systematically constructed, with loop corrections controlled by the chiral expansion parameter p/Lambda_chiral.

## Key numbers

* The pion decay constant f appears in the leading-order chiral Lagrangian as the normalization factor in Sigma = exp(2i phi/f), with f proportional to Lambda_QCD (of order 1 GeV).
* The pseudoscalar meson octet masses are approximately 140 MeV for pions and about 500 MeV for kaons and eta, which are small compared to other hadron masses (order 1 GeV and up).
* The leading-order prediction for the pion scattering amplitude A(ab to cd) is given by a formula involving Mandelstam variables s, t, u and the structure constants f_{abc}, with a factor 1/(3f^2).

## Context

These lectures extend the continuum ChPT framework reviewed in works such as the review by Heiri Leutwyler (1994) and textbooks by Donoghue, Golowich, and Holstein (1992) and Georgi (1984), applying it specifically to lattice QCD computations. The work enables practitioners to use ChPT for extrapolating lattice results to physical quark masses, for parametrizing scaling violations at nonzero lattice spacing, for analyzing partially-quenched and mixed-action simulations, and for understanding finite-volume effects in both the p-regime and epsilon-regime, as well as for testing the validity of lattice methods such as fourth-rooted staggered fermions.
