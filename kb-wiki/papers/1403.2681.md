# The Chern-Simons diffusion rate from higher curvature gravity

> An important transport coefficient in the study of non-Abelian plasmas is the Chern-Simons diffusion rate, which parameterizes the rate of transition among the degenerate vacua of a gauge theory. We compute this quantity at strong coupling, via holography, using two theories of gravity with higher curvature corrections, namely Gauss-Bonnet gravity and quasi-topological gravity. We find that these corrections may either increase or decrease the result obtained from Einstein's gravity, depending on the value of the couplings. The Chern-Simons diffusion rate for Gauss-Bonnet gravity decreases as the shear viscosity over entropy ratio is increased.

## Key claim

The Chern-Simons diffusion rate in Gauss-Bonnet and quasi-topological gravity is a rescaling of the Einstein gravity result, and the corrections can either increase or decrease the rate depending on the sign of the Gauss-Bonnet coupling; the rate for Gauss-Bonnet gravity decreases as the shear viscosity over entropy ratio is increased.

## Method

The authors compute the Chern-Simons diffusion rate holographically by considering a bulk scalar field dual to the operator tr F∧F in a 5-dimensional AdS black hole background with Gauss-Bonnet and quasi-topological corrections. They solve the scalar field equation of motion at zero spatial momentum in the small-frequency limit, imposing infalling boundary conditions at the horizon, and extract the retarded Green's function from the boundary action. The result is obtained exactly in the Gauss-Bonnet coupling λ_GB and to first order in the quasi-topological coupling μ.

## Key numbers

- The Einstein gravity result is Γ_CS^Einstein = λ²/(256 π³) T⁴, where λ is the 't Hooft coupling and T is the temperature.
- The Gauss-Bonnet correction factor is H⁰(λ_GB) = ((1 - √(1-4λ_GB))/(2λ_GB))^(3/2), valid for the allowed range -0.36 ≲ λ_GB ≲ 0.12.
- The quasi-topological correction factor H¹(λ_GB) is given explicitly and is suppressed by the constraint |μ| ≲ 0.001.
- The shear viscosity over entropy ratio in these theories is η/s = (1/(4π))[1-4λ_GB-36μ(9-64λ_GB+128λ_GB²)] + O(μ²).

## Context

This work extends the holographic computation of the Chern-Simons diffusion rate from Einstein gravity (recipe, 2008) to include higher curvature corrections, following the same approach used for the shear viscosity over entropy ratio in Gauss-Bonnet gravity (Brigante et al., 2008) and quasi-topological gravity (Myers et al., 2009). The results show that the Chern-Simons diffusion rate can deviate from the Einstein gravity result, and the dependence on η/s suggests a possible microscopic interpretation; the authors note that similar α'-corrections have been solved exactly in other settings (Kats and Petrov, 2009) with qualitatively similar behavior.
