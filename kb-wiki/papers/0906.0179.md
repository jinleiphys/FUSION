# The occupancies of individual orbits and the nuclear matrix element of the $^{76}$Ge neutrinoless $ββ$ decay

> We discuss the variation of the nuclear matrix element (NME) for the neutrinoless double beta ($0νββ$) decay of $^{76}$Ge when the wave functions are constrained to reproduce the experimental occupancies of the two nuclei involved in the transition. In the Interacting Shell Model description the value of the NME is enhanced about 15% compared to previous calculations, whereas in the QRPA the NME's are reduced by 20%-30%. This diminishes the discrepancies between both approaches. In addition, we discuss effect of the short range correlations on the NME in the light of the recently proposed parametrizations based on a consistent renormalization of the $0νββ$ transition operator.

## Key claim

When the nuclear wave functions are constrained to reproduce experimental occupancies of the two nuclei involved, the Interacting Shell Model (ISM) neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix element (NME) for $^{76}$Ge is enhanced by about 15% compared to previous calculations, while the QRPA NMEs are reduced by 20%-30%, diminishing the discrepancies between both approaches.

## Method

The authors computed orbital occupancies for $^{76}$Ge and $^{76}$Se using two ISM effective interactions (gcn28.50 and rg.prolate) and compared them to experimental occupancies from one-nucleon transfer reactions. They then calculated the $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay NME for each interaction using the UCOM prescription for short-range correlations, and compared these results to QRPA calculations from the Tübingen and Jyväskylä groups that had adjusted their Woods-Saxon potentials to reproduce the experimental occupancies.

## Key numbers

- ISM NME with gcn28.50 interaction: 2.81 (dimensionless)
- ISM NME with rg.prolate interaction: 3.26 (dimensionless), an enhancement of about 15%
- QRPA(Jy) NME with original Woods-Saxon: 5.36; with adjusted Woods-Saxon: 4.11 (reduction of about 30%)
- QRPA(Tu) NME with original Woods-Saxon: 5.07-6.25; with adjusted Woods-Saxon: 4.59-5.44 (reduction of about 20%)
- $2\nu\beta\beta$ matrix element with gcn28.50: 0.32 MeV$^{-1}$; with rg.prolate: 0.41 MeV$^{-1}$; experimental value: $0.14\pm0.01$ MeV$^{-1}$
- With quenching factor 0.6, the $2\nu\beta\beta$ matrix elements become 0.12 and 0.15 MeV$^{-1}$, close to experiment
- Consistent SRC treatments modify the NME by less than 5% (Argonne potential) or about 5% (Bonn potential)

## Context

This work extends previous ISM calculations by Caurier et al. (2007) and Menéndez et al. (2009) using the gcn28.50 interaction, and QRPA calculations by the Tübingen group (Šimkovic et al., 2008) and Jyväskylä group (Suhonen et al., 2008). The authors show that constraining wave functions with experimental occupancies reduces the gap between ISM and QRPA NME values by nearly one half. The paper also demonstrates that occupation numbers alone do not fix the NME value, as seniority truncation studies show occupancies are essentially independent of maximum seniority while the NME is strongly reduced when high seniority components are included.
