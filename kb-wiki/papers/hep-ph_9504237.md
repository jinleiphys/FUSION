# The Mixed-Isospin Vector Current Correlator in Chiral Perturbation Theory and QCD Sum Rules

> The mixed-isospin vector current correlator, $\langle 0\vert T(V^ρ_μV^ω_ν)\vert 0\rangle$ is evaluated using both QCD sum rules and Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) to one-loop order. The sum rule treatment is a modification of previous analyses necessitated by the observation that those analyses produce forms of the correlator that fail to be dominated, near $q^2=0$, by the most nearby singularities. Inclusion of contributions associated with the $ϕ$ meson rectify this problem. The resulting sum rule fit provides evidence for a significant direct $ω\rightarrowππ$ coupling contribution in $e^+e^-\rightarrowπ^+π^-$. It is also pointed out that results for the $q^2$-dependence of the correlator cannot be used to provide information about the (off-shell) $q^2$-dependence of the off-diagonal element of the vector meson propagator unless a very specific choice of interpolating fields for the vector mesons is made. The results for the value of the correlator near $q^2=0$ in ChPT are shown to be more than an order of magnitude smaller than those extracted from the sum rule analysis and the reasons why this suggests slow convergence of the chiral series for the correlator given.

## Key claim

The mixed-isospin vector current correlator evaluated via QCD sum rules provides evidence for a significant direct ω→ππ coupling contribution in e⁺e⁻→π⁺π⁻, but the value of the correlator near q²=0 from Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) to one-loop order is more than an order of magnitude smaller than that extracted from the sum rule analysis, suggesting slow convergence of the chiral series for this correlator.

## Method

The QCD sum rule analysis uses the operator product expansion (OPE) for the correlator ⟨0|T(V^ρ_μ V^ω_ν)|0⟩ truncated at dimension-six operators, matched to a phenomenological spectral representation including ρ, ω, ρ′, ω′, and ϕ meson pole terms via Borel transformation. The ChPT calculation evaluates the same correlator to one-loop order using the effective chiral Lagrangian of Gasser and Leutwyler (1984), with external vector sources and the octet of pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons.

## Key numbers

- The parameter ξ (related to ρ-ω mixing strength) from the sum rule analysis is significantly different from 1.13×10⁻³; for input set I, III, IV the values are not given explicitly in the truncated text, but the value 1.13×10⁻³ is excluded by the analysis.
- The ratio f_ϕ/f_ω from the sum rule is 0.062, 0.068, and 0.066 for input sets I, III, and IV respectively, consistent with a crude estimate of 0.05–0.10.
- The ρ′ and ω′ contributions to the correlator near q²=0 are a factor of 40–60 smaller than f_ρ and f_ω.
- The ϕ contribution to the correlator near q²=0 is approximately twice as large as the combined ρ+ω contribution, while the ρ′-ω′ region contribution is less than 10% of the ϕ contribution.
- The ChPT result for the correlator near q²=0 is more than an order of magnitude smaller than the sum rule extraction.

## Context

This work extends and corrects earlier QCD sum rule analyses of the mixed-isospin vector current correlator by Maltman (1992) and by the authors of Ref. [ref12] (likely Jin and others, 1993–1994), which produced unphysical dominance of distant singularities near q²=0. The inclusion of the ϕ meson contribution rectifies this problem. The results challenge the assumption of negligible direct ω→ππ coupling used in earlier analyses of e⁺e⁻→π⁺π⁻ interference (Ref. [ref4,ref5]), and the discrepancy with ChPT indicates that the chiral expansion for this correlator converges slowly, limiting the applicability of one-loop ChPT for isospin-breaking observables. The work also clarifies that off-shell information about the vector meson propagator cannot be extracted from the current correlator without a specific choice of interpolating fields, addressing confusion in Ref. [ref12].
