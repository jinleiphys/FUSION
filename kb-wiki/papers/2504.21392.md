# Impact of isospin asymmetric nuclear medium on pseudoscalar and vector $B$ mesons

> In this study, using the light-front quark model, we examine how an isospin asymmetric nuclear medium affects the properties of pseudoscalar ($B^+, B^0$) and vector ($B^{\ast+}, B^{\ast0}$) mesons under different temperature values and degrees of isospin asymmetry. To simulate the in-medium modifications of the constituent quark masses, we employ the chiral SU(3) quark mean field model. Our analysis focuses on evaluating the effective masses, weak decay constants, and distribution amplitudes (DAs) of $B$ mesons in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter. The calculated vacuum values of the $B$ meson masses and decay constants show good agreement with existing experimental data, validating our approach to study the medium effects within the same framework.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the properties (effective masses, weak decay constants, and distribution amplitudes) of pseudoscalar and vector \(B\) mesons are modified in an isospin-asymmetric nuclear medium at finite temperature, but only insofar as the medium affects the light constituent quark; the bottom quark is treated as effectively frozen due to its large mass.

## Method

The authors combine the light-front quark model (LFQM) with the chiral SU(3) quark mean field (CQMF) model. The LFQM provides vacuum meson masses and wave functions via a variational principle applied to a QCD-inspired Hamiltonian with Coulomb, linear confining, and hyperfine potentials. The CQMF model supplies in-medium effective masses for the light constituent quarks (\(u,d\)) by solving coupled mean-field equations for scalar (\(\sigma,\zeta,\delta\)) and vector (\(\omega,\rho\)) fields at given baryonic density ratio \(\rho_B/\rho_0\), temperature \(T\), and isospin asymmetry \(\eta\). These medium-modified quark masses are then used as inputs in the LFQM to compute the in-medium meson masses, weak decay constants, and distribution amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum masses: \(B^+\) and \(B^0\) pseudoscalar mesons calculated at 5279.0 MeV and 5279.6 MeV, respectively, compared to experimental values of 5279.34 MeV and 5279.65 MeV (ParticleDataGroup 2024).
- Vacuum masses: \(B^{\ast+}\) and \(B^{\ast0}\) vector mesons calculated at 5324.0 MeV and 5324.6 MeV, compared to experimental values of 5324.71 MeV and 5324.70 MeV (ParticleDataGroup 2024).
- Vacuum decay constant for pseudoscalar \(B\) mesons: calculated value 190.0 MeV, compared to experimental 190.0 MeV (ParticleDataGroup 2024).
- Vacuum decay constant for vector \(B\) mesons: calculated value 196.0 MeV, compared to experimental 196.0 MeV (ParticleDataGroup 2024).
- The variational parameter \(\beta\) for the Gaussian trial wave function is determined by fitting the mass spectra for \(\eta_B\) and \(B^{\ast}\) mesons (specific numerical value not given in the truncated text).
- The coupling constant \(k_c\) for the confining potential in the CQMF model is taken as 98 MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^{-2}\) (Wang 2004).
- The vacuum expectation values of scalar fields: \(\sigma_0 = -92.8\) MeV, \(\zeta_0 = -96.5\) MeV, \(\chi_0 = 254.6\) MeV.
- The saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies of heavy meson modification in nuclear matter, such as those using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model (Saito 2005, Krein 2017), QCD sum rules (Tsushima 2002, Hilger 2008), and the Bethe-Salpeter equation–Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (BSE-NJL) model (Hutauruk 2018). It specifically addresses isospin asymmetry, which is relevant for neutron-rich matter encountered in experiments at the Compressed Baryonic Matter (CBM) facility at FAIR and the BM@N experiment at NICA. The results enable future studies of \(B\) meson production and transport in heavy-ion collisions at moderate to high baryonic densities, and may inform models of the internal structure of neutron stars.
