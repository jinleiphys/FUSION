# One- and Two-Nucleon Structure form Green's Function Theory

> We review some applications of self-consistent Green's function theory to studies of one- and two-nucleon structure in finite nuclei. Large-scale microscopic calculations that employ realistic nuclear forces are now possible. Effects of long-range correlations are seen to play a dominant role in determining the quenching of absolute spectroscopic factors. They also enhance considerably (e,e'pn) cross sections in superparallel kinematics, in agreement with observations.

## Key claim

Self-consistent Green's function theory in the Faddeev random phase approximation (FRPA) can reproduce the quenching of absolute spectroscopic factors and enhance (e,e'pn) cross sections, but only when long-range correlations (LRC) are included; short-range correlations (SRC) alone are insufficient.

## Method

The authors applied the FRPA method, an expansion of the many-body problem in terms of particle-vibration couplings, to compute single-particle spectral functions and two-nucleon overlap functions (TOFs) in finite nuclei. For the one-nucleon case, they used the chiral N3LO interaction with a monopole correction for missing three-nucleon forces, and separated contributions from short-range and long-range correlations. For two-nucleon emission, they computed (e,e'pN) cross sections using the Pavia model, which includes one-body and two-body currents (pi-seagull, pion-in-flight, and Delta-isobar terms), with final-state interactions treated via optical models and first-order two-nucleon interactions.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factors for the $\nu 1p_{3/2}$ orbit in $^{57}$Ni: FRPA with SRC only gives 0.95, full FRPA (SRC+LRC) gives 0.65, and after adding shell-model correction $\Delta Z_n = -0.03$ the result is 0.62, compared to the experimental value of 0.58(11).
- Spectroscopic factors for the $\nu 0f_{7/2}$ orbit in $^{55}$Ni: FRPA with SRC only gives 0.95, full FRPA gives 0.72, and after adding $\Delta Z_n = -0.03$ the result is 0.69.
- For the $\pi 1p_{3/2}$ orbit in $^{57}$Cu: FRPA with SRC only gives 0.96, full FRPA gives 0.67, and after adding $\Delta Z_n = -0.02$ the result is 0.65.
- Quasiparticle energy for $\nu 1p_{1/2}$ in $^{57}$Ni: FRPA gives -11.43 MeV, experiment gives -9.134 MeV.
- Quasiparticle energy for $\pi 1p_{1/2}$ in $^{57}$Cu: FRPA gives -1.28 MeV, experiment gives +0.417 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier self-consistent Green's function calculations by Barbieri (2007, 2009) and applies the FRPA method to both one- and two-nucleon spectral functions. The results contradict the assumption that short-range correlations alone explain spectroscopic factor quenching, showing instead that long-range correlations (particle-vibration couplings) dominate. The FRPA results for $^{16}$O(e,e'pN) cross sections are compared to earlier Jastrow ansatz calculations (which include only central SRC and underestimate cross sections) and coupled-cluster calculations by Giusti (1999). The work enables future ab-initio calculations of exotic nuclei and provides microscopic input for shell-model effective interactions.
