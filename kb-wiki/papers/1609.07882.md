# Reparametrizing the Polyakov$-$Nambu$-$Jona-Lasinio model

> The Polyakov$-$Nambu$-$Jona-Lasinio model has been quite successful in describing various qualitative features of observables for strongly interacting matter, that are measurable in heavy-ion collision experiments. The question still remains on the quantitative uncertainties in the model results. Such an estimation is possible only by contrasting these results with those obtained from first principles using the lattice QCD framework. Recently a variety of lattice QCD data were reported in the realistic continuum limit. Here we make a first attempt at reparametrizing the model so as to reproduce these lattice data. We find excellent quantitative agreement for the equation of state. Certain discrepancies in the charge and strangeness susceptibilities as well as baryon-charge correlation still remain. We discuss their causes and outline possible directions to remove them.

## Key claim

The authors claim that by reparametrizing the Polyakov loop potential parameters, the PNJL model can achieve excellent quantitative agreement with continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD data for the equation of state, but significant discrepancies remain in charge and strangeness susceptibilities and baryon-charge correlations, particularly below the crossover temperature.

## Method

The authors fixed the parameters of the Polyakov loop potential (a0, a1, a2, T0, b3, b4, kappa) to reproduce the crossover temperature around 160 MeV and the temperature dependence of pressure from continuum lattice QCD data, while keeping the NJL model parameters fixed at their zero-temperature values. They then computed thermodynamic quantities (entropy density, energy density, trace anomaly, specific heat, speed of sound) and fluctuations of conserved charges (baryon number, electric charge, strangeness susceptibilities) from the pressure and its derivatives, comparing these against continuum extrapolated results from the HotQCD and Wuppertal-Budapest collaborations.

## Key numbers

* Crossover temperature Tc = 166.5 MeV for the 6-quark interaction version and Tc = 162.5 MeV for the 8-quark interaction version, obtained from the average of the peak positions of dPhi/dT and dsigma/dT.
* Scaled pressure reaches approximately 75% of the Stefan-Boltzmann limit at high temperatures, in agreement with continuum lattice data.
* Critical end point location for 6-quark interaction: TE = 54.3 MeV (TE/Tc = 0.326) and muB_E = 960 MeV (muB_E/Tc = 5.77).
* Critical end point location for 8-quark interaction: TE = 93.0 MeV (TE/Tc = 0.572) and muB_E = 720 MeV (muB_E/Tc = 4.43).
* The height of the trace anomaly peak near Tc is reduced to almost half of what was reported in earlier parametrizations by Bhattacharyya et al.

## Context

This work extends earlier PNJL model studies by Bhattacharyya et al. and Deb et al. that used finite lattice spacing data, now reparametrizing to match continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD results from the HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov 2014) and the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi 2014). The improved parametrization enables more reliable predictions for nonzero baryon densities, but the discrepancies in charge and strangeness susceptibilities below Tc indicate that the model requires inclusion of hadronic degrees of freedom, as suggested by the work of Swagato et al. on strange hadron contributions.
