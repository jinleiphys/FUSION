# Effects of Meson Mass Decrease on Superfluidity in Nuclear Matter

> We calculate the $^1S_0$ pairing gap in nuclear matter by adopting the "in-medium Bonn potential" proposed by Rapp et al. [e-print nucl-th/9706006], which takes into account the in-medium meson mass decrease, as the particle-particle interaction in the gap equation. The resulting gap is significantly reduced in comparison with the one obtained by adopting the original Bonn potential.

# Effects of Meson Mass Decrease on Superfluidity in Nuclear Matter

## Key claim
The in-medium decrease of vector meson masses (via Brown-Rho scaling) significantly reduces the $^1S_0$ pairing gap in nuclear matter compared to calculations using the original free-space Bonn potential, but the uncertainty across different studies remains larger than the magnitude of this reduction.

## Method
The authors solved the coupled BCS gap equation and mean-field equations for symmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter, using the "in-medium Bonn potential" of Rapp et al. as the particle-particle interaction. This potential incorporates Brown-Rho scaling (with scaling parameter C = 0.15) applied to the masses and cut-off masses of the $\rho$ and $\omega$ mesons, while the mean-field sector uses standard quantum hadrodynamics with the NL1 parameter set. The gap equation was solved with a momentum cut-off of 20 fm$^{-1}$, and the $\pi$-N coupling was treated in the pseudovector form.

## Key numbers
- The scaling parameter C in the Brown-Rho scaling relation $m_{\rho,\omega}^\ast / m_{\rho,\omega} = 1 - C \rho / \rho_0$ was chosen as 0.15.
- The momentum cut-off $\Lambda_\mathrm{c}$ for numerical integration was set to 20 fm$^{-1}$.
- The calculation was performed for both symmetric nuclear matter ($\gamma = 4$) and neutron matter ($\gamma = 2$).

## Context
This work extends the relativistic treatment of pairing by Rummel and Ring (1995), who used the free-space Bonn potential and obtained gaps consistent with non-relativistic studies, by incorporating in-medium meson mass changes motivated by Brown-Rho scaling (Brown and Rho, 1991) and QCD sum rules (Hatsuda and Lee, 1992). The reduction found is consistent with the conjecture by Matsuzaki (1997) that including N-$\bar{\mathrm{N}}$ polarization would reduce the gap, though less drastic than the reduction from particle-hole polarization in non-relativistic models (Chen et al., 1993; Schulze et al., 1995). The results highlight the need for further investigations to resolve the remaining theoretical uncertainty in the pairing gap.
