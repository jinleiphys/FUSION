# Probing Early-Time Dynamics and Quark-Gluon Plasma Transport Properties with Photons and Hadrons

> We use a hybrid model which relies on QCD effective kinetic theory -- K\oMP\oST -- to dynamically bridge the gap between IP-Glasma initial states and viscous hydrodynamics, for the theoretical interpretation of heavy-ion collision results. The hydrodynamic phase is then followed by dynamical freeze-out handled by UrQMD. It is observed that the new pre-equilibrium/pre-hydro phase does influence the extraction of transport coefficients. We also show how the early dynamics is reflected in photon observables.

## Key claim
The inclusion of a pre-equilibrium phase described by QCD effective kinetic theory (KøMPøST) between IP-Glasma initial states and viscous hydrodynamics influences the extraction of transport coefficients, particularly the bulk viscosity over entropy density ratio, and is reflected in photon observables. The authors show that hadronic and photon data are consistent with this pre-hydro phase, but caution that photon results do not yet account for viscous effects and should be treated as preliminary estimates.

## Method
The authors constructed a hybrid model connecting IP-Glasma initial conditions to KøMPøST (QCD effective kinetic theory) at τ = 0.1 fm/c, then to MUSIC (second-order viscous hydrodynamics) at τ = 0.8 fm/c, followed by hadronization and UrQMD hadronic transport when local energy density reached 0.18 GeV/fm³. All components of the energy-momentum tensor, including shear and bulk viscous components, were transferred between stages. Photon production included contributions from primordial nucleon-nucleon collisions, the KøMPøST pre-equilibrium phase, and the hydrodynamic phase, with a smooth transition implemented between gluon-rich and chemically equilibrated partonic matter during the kinetic theory evolution.

## Key numbers
- Switching time from IP-Glasma to KøMPøST: τ = 0.1 fm/c
- Switching time from KøMPøST to MUSIC hydrodynamics: τ = 0.8 fm/c
- Switching time from IP-Glasma directly to MUSIC (for comparison without KøMPøST): τ = 0.4 fm/c
- Freeze-out energy density for hadronization: 0.18 GeV/fm³
- Freeze-out temperature for photon production from MUSIC: T = 105 MeV
- Pre-hydro photons represent approximately 10-20% of the net photon spectrum at higher pT
- Collision systems studied: Au+Au at √s = 200 A GeV and Pb+Pb at √s = 2.76 A TeV and 5.02 A TeV
- Centrality class for photon results: 20-40%

## Context
This work extends the hybrid modeling approach of Gale (2013) and Heinz (2013) by incorporating the KøMPøST effective kinetic theory framework developed by Kurkela (2018) to dynamically bridge IP-Glasma initial states and viscous hydrodynamics, rather than using an instantaneous matching at τ ~ 1/Qs as done previously by Gale (2012). The results enable future multivariable analyses like those discussed by Shen (2020) to quantify whether data unequivocally requires a pre-equilibrium epoch, while theory currently demands this phase for IP-Glasma states to dynamically evolve into fluid-like systems. The photon calculations build on prior work by Paquet (2015) and include pre-equilibrium photon contributions studied by Churchill (2020) and Greif (2016).
