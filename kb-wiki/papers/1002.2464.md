# Relativistic Collective Coordinate Quantization of Solitons: Spinning Skyrmion

> We develop a consistent relativistic generalization of collective coordinate quantization of field theory solitons. Our principle of introducing collective coordinates is that the equations of motion of the collective coordinates ensure those of the original field theory. We illustrate this principle with the quantization of spinning degrees of freedom of Skyrmion representing baryons. We calculate the leading relativistic corrections to the static properties of nucleons, and find that the corrections are non-negligible ones of 10% to 20%.

## Key claim

The authors develop a relativistic generalization of collective coordinate quantization for solitons, based on the principle that the equations of motion of the collective coordinates must ensure those of the original field theory; they find that the leading relativistic corrections to static properties of nucleons are non-negligible (10% to 20%) but do not generally improve agreement with experiment.

## Method

The authors introduce collective coordinates for the spinning Skyrmion by deforming the spatial coordinate of the static soliton solution with functions A(r), B(r), and C(r) that are quadratic in the angular velocity Omega, then determine these functions by requiring that the field theory equations of motion vanish identically when the collective coordinate equations of motion hold. The resulting deformed Skyrmion field is inserted into the Skyrme model Lagrangian, and the spatial integration yields a Lagrangian for the rotational collective coordinate that includes an Omega^4 correction term. The parameters f_pi and e are fixed by fitting to the experimental masses of the nucleon, Delta, and pion.

## Key numbers

* Decay constant: f_pi = 125 MeV (compared to 108 MeV from the rigid-body approximation of Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983) and the experimental value 186 MeV)
* Isoscalar charge radius: <r^2>_{I=0}^{1/2} = 0.59 fm (rigid-body: 0.68 fm, experiment: 0.81 fm)
* Isovector charge radius: <r^2>_{I=1}^{1/2} = 1.17 fm (rigid-body: 1.04 fm, experiment: 0.94 fm)
* Isoscalar magnetic radius: <r^2>_{M,I=0}^{1/2} = 0.85 fm (rigid-body: 0.95 fm, experiment: 0.82 fm)
* Isovector magnetic radius: <r^2>_{M,I=1}^{1/2} = 1.17 fm (rigid-body: 1.04 fm, experiment: 0.86 fm)
* Proton magnetic moment: mu_p = 1.65 (rigid-body: 1.97, experiment: 2.79)
* Neutron magnetic moment: mu_n = -0.99 (rigid-body: -1.24, experiment: -1.91)
* Axial coupling: g_A = 0.58 (rigid-body: 0.65, experiment: 1.24)
* Ratio of Hamiltonian contributions for nucleon: static:rotational:relativistic correction = 89:7:4; for Delta: 68:14:18

## Context

This work extends the standard rigid-body quantization of the spinning Skyrmion developed by Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983) and Adkins and Nappi (1984), which treats baryons as spherical rotors. The authors address the known problem that the rotational energy contributes about 8% of the nucleon mass and 30% of the Delta mass, implying relativistic rotation speeds where the spherical approximation breaks down. Earlier attempts to include deformation were made by Bander and collaborators (1984) and Li and collaborators (1987), but the present work provides a systematic method based on the principle that collective coordinate equations of motion must enforce the field theory equations. The approach enables calculation of leading relativistic corrections to static nucleon properties and can be applied to other soliton systems.
