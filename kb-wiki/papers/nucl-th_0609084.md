# Density-functional theory for the pairing Hamiltonian

> We consider the pairing Hamiltonian and systematically construct its density functional in the strong-coupling limit and in the limit of large particle number. In the former limit, the functional is an expansion into central moments of occupation numbers. In the latter limit, the functional is known from BCS theory. Both functionals are nonlocal in structure, and the nonlocalities are in the form of simple products of local functionals. We also derive the relation between the occupation numbers and the Kohn-Sham density.

## Key claim

The paper systematically constructs the density functional for the pairing Hamiltonian in two limiting cases (strong coupling and large particle number), showing that the resulting functionals are nonlocal but that the nonlocalities take the simple form of products of local functionals; the authors note that the Hamiltonian is not realistic and a constant pairing interaction is a crude approximation for nuclei, so the functionals require modification through orbital-dependent weight functions to be applicable to realistic systems.

## Method

The authors start from the exactly solvable Richardson pairing Hamiltonian with constant pairing interaction and compute the Legendre transform of the ground-state energy with respect to the single-particle energies to obtain the density functional in terms of occupation numbers. In the limit of large particle number (N >> 1), they use the BCS approximation to derive an analytic expression for the functional. In the strong-coupling limit (g >> |ε_k - ε̄|), they expand the Richardson equations in inverse powers of the coupling strength and construct the functional order by order in central moments of the occupation numbers. They then relate the occupation numbers to the spatial density through the Kohn-Sham orbitals.

## Key numbers

- The BCS density functional is F_BCS = -(g/4) (∑_j √[n_j(2-n_j)])², where g is the pairing strength and n_j are occupation numbers
- The leading-order strong-coupling ground-state energy is E^(1) = -N(Ω-N+1)g, where N is the number of fermion pairs and Ω is the number of single-particle orbitals
- The NLO density functional is F_NLO = E^(1) - (gΩ²/(4c_[2])) (n - n̄)², with c_[2] = -4N(Ω-N)/[Ω(Ω-1)]
- The N²LO functional adds a term (g c_[3]Ω³/(8c_[2]³)) (n - n̄)³, where c_[3] = 8N(Ω-N)(Ω-2N)/[Ω²(Ω-1)(Ω-2)]
- The N³LO functional includes a nonlocal term proportional to ((n - n̄)²)² with coefficient (4c_[2]c_[2,2]+9c²_[3])/(64c⁵_[2]) g Ω⁴

## Context

This work extends the systematic construction of density functionals from solvable Hamiltonians, following earlier work on the dilute Fermi gas with repulsive interactions (Puglia et al., 2003; Bhattacharyya, 2005) and the Fermi gas near the unitary limit (Carlson et al., 2003; Papenbrock, 2005; Bhattacharyya, 2006). The paper provides an alternative to the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach for including pairing in nuclear density-functional theory, avoiding the need for particle-number projection. The results suggest a way to incorporate pairing with particle-number conservation into nuclear DFT, potentially extending the very successful mass formula by Duflo and Zuker (1995) into a DFT setting.
