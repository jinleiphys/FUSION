# Ideal Hydrodynamics for Bulk and Multistrange Hadrons in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200\,AGeV Au-Au Collisions

> We revisit the use of ideal hydrodynamics to describe bulk- and multistrange-hadron observables in nuclear collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. Toward this end we augment the 2+1-dimensional code "AZHYDRO" by employing (a) an equation of state based on recent lattice-QCD computations matched to a hadron-resonance gas with chemical decoupling at $T_{\rm ch}\simeq$160\,MeV, (b) a compact initial density profile, (c) an initial-flow field including azimuthal anisotropies, and (d) a sequential kinetic decoupling of bulk ($π$, $K$, $p$) and multistrange ($ϕ$, $Ξ$, $Ω$) hadrons at $T\simeq110$\,MeV and 160\,MeV, respectively. We find that this scheme allows for a consistent description of the observed chemistry, transverse-momentum spectra and elliptic flow of light and strange hadrons.

## Key claim

The authors claim that ideal hydrodynamics, when augmented with a lattice-QCD-based equation of state, a compact initial density profile, initial anisotropic flow, and sequential kinetic decoupling of bulk and multistrange hadrons, can consistently describe the observed chemistry, transverse-momentum spectra, and elliptic flow of light and strange hadrons in Au-Au collisions at RHIC, contrary to previous findings that ideal hydrodynamics with partial chemical equilibrium overpredicted elliptic flow.

## Method

The authors modified the 2+1-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic code AZHYDRO by implementing an equation of state based on recent lattice-QCD calculations matched to a hadron-resonance gas with chemical decoupling at Tch ≈ 160 MeV, a compact initial entropy density profile proportional to binary-collision density, an initial flow field with azimuthal anisotropies parameterized by radial flow (α0) and anisotropy (α2) coefficients, and a sequential kinetic decoupling scheme where multistrange hadrons (φ, Ξ, Ω) freeze out at Tch ≈ 160 MeV while bulk hadrons (π, K, p) freeze out at Tfo ≈ 110 MeV. The model was tuned to simultaneously describe spectra and elliptic flow for both bulk and multistrange hadrons in central (0-5%) and semicentral (20-30%) Au-Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freezeout temperature: Tch ≈ 160 MeV; kinetic freezeout temperature for bulk hadrons: Tfo ≈ 110 MeV
- Initial entropy density in central collisions (0-5%): s0 = 159.5 fm⁻³, corresponding to initial temperature T0 = 399.9 MeV
- Initial entropy density in semicentral collisions (20-30%): s0 = 133.1 fm⁻³, corresponding to initial temperature T0 = 377.9 MeV
- Fireball lifetime reduced from ~10.6 fm/c in default AZHYDRO to ~9.0 fm/c in the fully amended case (both with e_dec = 0.1094 GeV/fm³)
- Initial flow parameters: α0 = 0.13, α2 = 0.004 for both centralities; κ = 1.0 for central (0-5%) and κ = 4.2 for semicentral (20-30%)
- Energy density at chemical freezeout: e_ch = 0.372 GeV/fm³; at kinetic freezeout: e_fo = 0.1094 GeV/fm³
- Calculated yields in central collisions (with feeddown): π⁺ = 312.2, π⁻ = 314.4, K⁺ = 48.2, K⁻ = 48.4, p = 34.0, p̄ = 26.2

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies by Kolb and Heinz (2003) using the AZHYDRO code, which employed an equation of state with a first-order phase transition (EoS-99) and found that multistrange particles like Ω⁻ had to decouple close to bulk kinetic freezeout. The authors address the tension identified by Kolb and Heinz (2002) and Huovinen (2007) where implementing partial chemical equilibrium in ideal hydrodynamics overpredicted elliptic flow. The compact initial density profile and initial flow are also key ingredients in resolving the HBT puzzle as discussed by Pratt (2008). This work enables the use of the tuned hydrodynamic background for quantitative applications to electromagnetic and heavy-flavor probes, and provides a baseline for comparison with emerging viscous hydrodynamic calculations.
