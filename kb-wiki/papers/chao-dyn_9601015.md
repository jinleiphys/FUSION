# Quantum corrections to the semiclassical quantization of a nonintegrable system

> We study the semiclassical behaviour of a two--dimensional nonintegrable system. In particular we analyze the question of quantum corrections to the semiclassical quantization obtaining up to the second order of perturbation theory an explicit analytical formula for the energy levels, which is the usual semiclassical one plus quantum corrections. We compare the "exact" levels obtained numerically to the semiclassical levels studying also the effects of quantum corrections.

# Quantum corrections to the semiclassical quantization of a nonintegrable system

## Key claim
For a two-dimensional nonintegrable system of coupled non-resonant oscillators, the quantum perturbation series can be rearranged into the classical canonical perturbation series plus quantum corrections proportional to successive powers of Planck's constant; however, these quantum corrections do not always increase the accuracy of the semiclassical approximation when compared to exact numerical levels, and for some levels the pure semiclassical quantization gives better results than the quantum perturbation theory.

## Method
The authors study a Hamiltonian of two non-resonant harmonic oscillators coupled by a quartic interaction of strength g. They apply classical canonical perturbation theory to second order in g to obtain an integrable approximate Hamiltonian depending only on action variables, then quantize it via the Einstein-Brillouin-Keller (EBK) rule. Separately, they perform Rayleigh-Schrodinger quantum perturbation theory to second order using creation and annihilation operators. The quantum corrections are identified as the difference between the quantum perturbation terms and the semiclassical terms at each order. Exact numerical energy levels are obtained by diagonalizing a truncated matrix of dimension 1225 by 1225 in the basis of unperturbed oscillators.

## Key numbers
- The Hamiltonian is H = (omega_1/2)(p_1^2+q_1^2) + (omega_2/2)(p_2^2+q_2^2) + g q_1^2 q_2^2, with omega_1 = 1 and omega_2 = sqrt(2) for numerical calculations.
- The coupling strength used for numerical comparison is g = 10^{-1}.
- Exact numerical levels are computed with double precision until the first 100 levels converge within 8 digits, using a matrix dimension of 1225 x 1225.
- For the first 25 levels at hbar = 1, the error between exact and semiclassical levels in units of mean level spacing D ranges from 1.06 x 10^{-3} to 0.905.
- For the first 25 levels at hbar = 10^{-1}, the error between exact and semiclassical levels in units of D ranges from 2.48 x 10^{-5} to 2.14 x 10^{-3}.
- The quantum correction term at second order is Q_2(I_1,I_2) = -(3/32)[(I_1 - I_2)/(omega_1 - omega_2) + (I_1 + I_2)/(omega_1 + omega_2)].

## Context
This work extends the formal results of Degli Esposti, Graffi and Herczynski (1991) on the relationship between classical perturbation theory and quantum perturbation theory for perturbed non-resonant harmonic oscillators, providing an explicit two-dimensional example. It also builds on the earlier study of Robnik (1984) where quantum corrections to EBK quantization differed only by an additive constant independent of quantum numbers, whereas here the corrections depend linearly on quantum numbers. The paper enables further investigation of semiclassical approximations for nonintegrable systems and highlights the importance of case studies comparing semiclassical and exact spectra, since both classical and quantum perturbation series typically diverge and do not necessarily describe exact levels even after resummation.
