# Lattice regularizations of $θ$ vacua: Anomalies and qubit models

> Anomalies are a powerful way to gain insight into possible lattice regularizations of a quantum field theory. In this work, we argue that the continuum anomaly for a given symmetry can be matched by a manifestly-symmetric, local, lattice regularization in the same spacetime dimensionality only if (i) the symmetry action is offsite, or (ii) if the continuum anomaly is reproduced exactly on the lattice. We consider lattice regularizations of a class of prototype models of QCD: the (1+1)-dimensional asymptotically-free Grassmannian nonlinear sigma models (NLSMs) with a $θ$ term. Using the Grassmannian NLSMs as a case study, we provide examples of lattice regularizations in which both possibilities are realized. For possibility (i), we argue that Grassmannian NLSMs can be obtained from $\mathrm{SU}(N)$ antiferromagnets with a well-defined continuum limit, reproducing both the infrared physics of $θ$ vacua and the ultraviolet physics of asymptotic freedom. These results enable the application of new classical algorithms to lattice Monte Carlo studies of these quantum field theories, and provide a viable realization suited for their quantum simulation. On the other hand, we show that, perhaps surprisingly, the conventional lattice regularization of $θ$ vacua due to Berg and Lüscher reproduces the anomaly exactly on the lattice, providing a realization of the second possibility.

## Key claim

The authors argue that a continuum anomaly can be matched by a manifestly-symmetric, local lattice regularization in the same spacetime dimensionality only if either (i) the symmetry action is offsite, or (ii) the continuum anomaly is reproduced exactly on the lattice, using the (1+1)-dimensional Grassmannian nonlinear sigma models with a theta term as a case study.

## Method

The authors analyze two lattice regularization approaches for the Grassmannian Gr_k(N) nonlinear sigma models with a theta term. For the first approach, they construct a qubit regularization using SU(N) antiferromagnetic spin chains with staggered couplings, deriving the continuum Lagrangian via coherent state path integrals and showing that the theta parameter emerges from the staggering parameter. For the second approach, they examine the conventional Berg-Luscher lattice regularization and demonstrate that it reproduces the mixed anomaly between PSU(N) and charge conjugation symmetry exactly on the lattice, using background gauge field techniques and analyzing the partition function under symmetry transformations.

## Key numbers

- The theta parameter in the spin chain regularization is given by theta = pi p (1 + gamma), where gamma is the staggering parameter defined by J_+/J_- = (1+gamma)/(1-gamma), and p is the number of columns in the Young tableau representation
- For the spin chain with J_+ = J_- (gamma = 0), the result is theta = pi p, reproducing the result of Read (1989)
- The coupling constant in the continuum limit is 1/g^2 = (1/2)p[(1-gamma)(1+gamma)]^(1/2)
- The anomaly analysis shows that for N even and k odd, there is a genuine anomaly at theta = pi; for other cases (N even/k even, N odd/k even, N odd/k odd), there is a global inconsistency rather than a genuine anomaly

## Context

This work extends the analysis of anomalies in lattice regularizations, building on the mixed anomaly identified in the CP(N-1) models by Gaiotto (2017), Komargodski (2017), and Dunne (2018). The qubit regularization generalizes the approach of Caspar (2022) for the O(3) nonlinear sigma model to the full class of Grassmannian models, while the analysis of the Berg-Luscher regularization (1981) shows that this conventional approach already realizes the second possibility of exact anomaly reproduction on the lattice. These results enable new classical Monte Carlo algorithms and provide viable realizations for quantum simulation of theta vacua.
