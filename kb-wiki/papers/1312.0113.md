# Long range correlations in high multiplicity hadron collisions:building bridges with ridges

> We discuss the physics of the ridge -- azimuthally collimated long range rapidity correlations -- in high multiplicity proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions. We outline some of the theoretical discussions in the literature that address the systematics of these ridge correlations.

## Key claim

The ridge phenomenon in high multiplicity proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions can be explained by gluon saturation and the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory, where "Glasma graphs" (QCD interference graphs) provide a significant additional contribution to di-jet graphs, enhanced by a factor of approximately α_S^{-8} (about 10^5) due to high gluon occupancy, though the author notes that final state hydrodynamic interpretations remain viable and the question is not settled.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical work comparing two classes of explanations for the ridge: initial state effects from the CGC framework (Glasma graphs plus BFKL dynamics for di-jet contributions) and final state effects from hydrodynamic flow models. The CGC calculations use unintegrated gluon distributions from solutions of the running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov equation, with saturation scales chosen at the initial rapidity scale for small x evolution. The hydrodynamic approach is examined through the IP-Glasma+MUSIC model, which matches non-equilibrium 2+1-D Yang-Mills dynamics event-by-event to viscous hydrodynamic evolution.

## Key numbers

- The enhancement of Glasma graphs in high multiplicity events is α_S^{-8}, corresponding to approximately 10^5 for typical values of the QCD fine structure constant α_S
- Proton-nucleus collisions at the LHC have center-of-mass energy √s = 5020 GeV/nucleon
- The CMS acceptance for di-hadron correlations is 2 < |Δη| < 4; ALICE has |Δη| < 1.8; ATLAS has 2 < |Δη| < 5
- The IP-Glasma+MUSIC model gives v_2 and v_3 values factors of 2-3 smaller than data for the same η/s values that describe A+A data
- The ALICE collaboration extracted a coherent fraction of 22 ± 12% for charged soft pion emission in Pb+Pb collisions
- The ridge in p+A collisions is approximately 4 times larger than in p+p collisions
- In the comparison of v_3 values, the centrality window for Pb+Pb is 55%-92%, while for p+Pb it ranges from 3% central to 1 in 10^7 events

## Context

This paper extends the author's series of works with Dusling (2013, 2012) applying the CGC framework to ridge correlations, building on earlier predictions of gluon saturation effects in deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC by Kharzeev (2003) and Marquet (2007). The ridge was first discovered in A+A collisions at RHIC, with the PHOBOS experiment demonstrating correlations over 4 units in relative rapidity. The paper contrasts the CGC approach with hydrodynamic models by Bozek (2012, 2013) and others, and discusses open questions including the origin of v_3 moments and mass ordering patterns seen by ALICE (2013), which have been interpreted as evidence for collectivity.
