# The importance of kinematic twists and genuine saturation effects in dijet production at the Electron-Ion Collider

> We compute the differential yield for quark anti-quark dijet production in high-energy electron-proton and electron-nucleus collisions at small $x$ as a function of the relative momentum $\boldsymbol{P}_\perp$ and momentum imbalance $\boldsymbol{k}_\perp$ of the dijet system for different photon virtualities $Q^2$, and study the elliptic and quadrangular anisotropies in the relative angle between $\boldsymbol{P}_\perp$ and $\boldsymbol{k}_\perp$. We review and extend the analysis in [1], which compared the results of the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) with those obtained using the transverse momentum dependent (TMD) framework. In particular, we include in our comparison the improved TMD (ITMD) framework, which resums kinematic power corrections of the ratio $k_\perp$ over the hard scale $Q_\perp$. By comparing ITMD and CGC results we are able to isolate genuine higher saturation contributions in the ratio $Q_s/Q_\perp$ which are resummed only in the CGC. These saturation contributions are in addition to those in the WeizsäckerWilliams gluon TMD that appear in powers of $Q_s/k_\perp$. We provide numerical estimates of these contributions for inclusive dijet production at the future Electron-Ion Collider, and identify kinematic windows where they can become relevant in the measurement of dijet and dihadron azimuthal correlations. We argue that such measurements will allow the detailed experimental study of both kinematic power corrections and genuine gluon saturation effects.

## Key claim

By comparing the Color Glass Condensate (CGC), transverse momentum dependent (TMD), and improved TMD (ITMD) frameworks for dijet production at the future Electron-Ion Collider, the authors isolate genuine saturation contributions that scale as \(Q_s/Q_\perp\) and are resummed only in the CGC, in addition to the kinematic power corrections of order \(k_\perp/Q_\perp\) that the ITMD framework resums.

## Method

The authors computed the differential cross section for quark anti-quark dijet production in high-energy electron-proton and electron-nucleus deeply inelastic scattering at small \(x\) using the CGC effective field theory, employing the Gaussian approximation for Wilson line correlators. They compared these CGC results with those from the TMD framework (valid for back-to-back kinematics) and the ITMD framework, which resums kinematic power corrections of the ratio \(k_\perp/Q_\perp\) but omits genuine saturation contributions. The comparison was performed for different photon virtualities \(Q^2\), relative dijet momenta \(P_\perp\), and momentum imbalances \(k_\perp\), including studies of elliptic and quadrangular azimuthal anisotropies in the angle between \(\boldsymbol{P}_\perp\) and \(\boldsymbol{k}_\perp\).

## Key numbers

- The genuine saturation contributions scale as \(\mathcal{O}(Q_s/Q_\perp)\) in the CGC, where \(Q_s\) is the saturation scale and \(Q_\perp = \mathrm{max}(Q, P_\perp)\); these are absent in the ITMD framework.
- The kinematic power corrections resummed by ITMD scale as \(\mathcal{O}(k_\perp/Q_\perp)\).
- The saturation contributions in the Weizsacker-Williams gluon TMD appear in powers of \(Q_s/k_\perp\).
- The nuclear saturation scale scales as \(Q_s^2 \propto A^{1/3}\), enhancing genuine saturation effects in electron-nucleus compared to electron-proton collisions.
- The hard scale \(Q_\perp\) is defined as \(Q_\perp = \mathrm{max}(Q, P_\perp)\), where \(P_\perp\) is the relative dijet momentum and \(Q\) is the photon virtuality.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Mantysaari (2019), which compared CGC and TMD results for dijet production at the EIC, by including the ITMD framework developed by Kotko (2015) and extended for DIS by Altinoluk (2019). The study enables experimental identification of kinematic windows at the EIC where genuine gluon saturation effects become measurable in dijet and dihadron azimuthal correlations, distinguishing them from kinematic power corrections.
