# Strings with a confining core in a Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We consider the intersection of N different interfaces interpolating between different $Z_N$ vacua of an SU(N) gauge theory using the Polyakov loop order parameter. Topological arguments show that at such a string-like junction, the order parameter should vanish, implying that the core of this string (i.e. the junction region of all the interfaces) is in the confining phase. Using the effective potential for the Polyakov loop proposed by Pisarski for QCD, we use numerical minimization technique and estimate the energy per unit length of the core of this string to be about 2.7 GeV/fm at a temperature about twice the critical temperature. For the parameters used, the interface tension is obtained to be about 7 GeV/fm$^2$. Lattice simulation of pure gauge theories should be able to investigate properties of these strings. For QCD with quarks, it has been discussed in the literature that this $Z_N$ symmetry may still be meaningful, with quark contributions leading to explicit breaking of this $Z_N$ symmetry. With this interpretation, such {\it QGP} strings may play important role in the evolution of the quark-gluon plasma phase and in the dynamics of quark-hadron transition.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the intersection of three different Z(3) interfaces in the deconfined phase of an SU(3) gauge theory forms a topological string whose core is in the confining phase, with an estimated energy per unit length of about 2.7 GeV/fm at a temperature about twice the critical temperature. The authors note that this result depends on the specific effective potential for the Polyakov loop proposed by Pisarski and that the numbers may be correct only up to factors of order unity.

## Method

The authors use the effective Lagrangian for the Polyakov loop proposed by Pisarski, with parameters fixed to reproduce lattice results for pure SU(3) gauge theory. They perform numerical energy minimization on a two-dimensional lattice (600 × 600 points with 0.01 fm spacing) to find the field configuration corresponding to the junction of three Z(3) interfaces. The string core energy is extracted by fitting the radial energy distribution E(r) to separate the core contribution from the linear contribution of the attached interfaces.

## Key numbers

- Energy per unit length of the QGP string core: about 2.7 GeV/fm at a temperature of about 2T_c (T_c ≈ 182 MeV)
- Interface (domain wall) tension: about 7 GeV/fm² at T = 400 MeV
- From the radial energy plot, the slope of the linear fit for large r gives σ ≈ 7.7 GeV/fm², consistent with the interface tension estimate
- Lattice spacing used for the string profile calculation: 0.01 fm, with a 600 × 600 lattice giving a physical size of 6 fm × 6 fm
- For the one-dimensional domain wall calculation: 10⁴ lattice points with spacing 0.002 fm, giving a physical size of 20 fm

## Context

This work extends the study of Z(3) interfaces in the quark-gluon plasma phase, which have been discussed in the literature (e.g., by the "zn" references), by considering their junction as a topological string configuration. The authors note that a similar string-like junction was discussed by Monien and others (1996) but with a different interpretation related to color magnetic monopoles. The QGP string is analogous to axionic strings formed at the junction of axionic domain walls (Vilenkin and Everett, 1982), but differs because the symmetry is broken in the high-temperature phase rather than the low-temperature phase. The results suggest that lattice simulations of pure gauge theories should be able to investigate these strings, and that they may play an important role in the dynamics of the quark-hadron phase transition, particularly as sites for heterogeneous bubble nucleation.
