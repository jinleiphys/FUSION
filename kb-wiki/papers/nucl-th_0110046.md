# What Does Free Space Lambda-Lambda Interaction Predict for Lambda-Lambda Hypernuclei?

> Data on Lambda-Lambda hypernuclei provide a unique method to learn details on the strangeness S =-2 sector of the baryon-baryon interaction. From the free space Bonn-Julich potentials, determined from data on baryon-baryon scattering in the S=0,-1 channels, we construct an interaction in the S =-2 sector to describe the experimentally known Lambda-Lambda hypernuclei. After including short--range (Jastrow) and RPA correlations, we find masses for these Lambda-Lambda hypernuclei in a reasonable agreement with data, taking into account theoretical and experimental uncertainties. Thus, we provide a natural extension, at low energies, of the Bonn-Julich OBE potentials to the S =-2 channel.

## Key claim
The free-space Bonn-Julich one-boson-exchange potentials, originally fitted to baryon-baryon scattering data in the strangeness S=0 and S=-1 channels, can be extended to the S=-2 sector to describe the experimentally known Lambda-Lambda hypernuclei, provided that both short-range (Jastrow) and long-range RPA correlations are included. The agreement with data is "reasonable" only when RPA effects are incorporated, and the results depend on the cutoff for phi-meson exchange and on possible SU(6) symmetry violations.

## Method
The authors model each Lambda-Lambda hypernucleus as a three-body system (two Lambda hyperons plus a nuclear core) and solve a variational Schrodinger equation using a Jastrow-type correlation function with seven free parameters. The free-space Lambda-Lambda interaction is constructed from sigma, omega, and phi meson exchanges using the Bonn-Julich model A couplings and SU(6) symmetry relations. The in-medium effective interaction is obtained by adding an RPA correction computed in nuclear matter via a Dyson equation for the sigma-omega propagator, then adapted to finite nuclei using a local density approximation with straight-line averaging over densities along the meson trajectory.

## Key numbers
- Experimental binding energy of 6_Lambda-Lambda_He: 7.25 (+0.38/-0.31) MeV (updated value from Nagara event, 2001)
- Experimental binding energy of 10_Lambda-Lambda_Be: 17.7 ± 0.4 MeV
- Experimental binding energy of 13_Lambda-Lambda_B: 27.5 ± 0.7 MeV
- Without RPA and without phi exchange, calculated B_LL for 6_LL_He: 6.15 MeV; with RPA and phi cutoff 2.5 GeV: 7.33 MeV (experiment: 7.25 MeV)
- Without RPA and without phi exchange, calculated B_LL for 10_LL_Be: 13.1 MeV; with RPA and phi cutoff 2.5 GeV: 16.8 MeV (experiment: 17.7 MeV)
- Without RPA and without phi exchange, calculated B_LL for 13_LL_B: 22.5 MeV; with RPA and phi cutoff 2.5 GeV: 27.0 MeV (experiment: 27.5 MeV)
- For 42_LL_Ca (no experimental data): predicted B_LL = 40.1 MeV with RPA and phi cutoff 2.5 GeV
- For 92_LL_Zr (no experimental data): predicted B_LL = 46.0 MeV with RPA and phi cutoff 2.5 GeV
- For 210_LL_Pb (no experimental data): predicted B_LL = 54.1 MeV with RPA and phi cutoff 2.5 GeV
- Varying phi-Lambda-Lambda couplings by ±10% around SU(6) values changes He binding energy in range 7.05–7.83 MeV, Be in 16.0–17.7 MeV, B in 26.0–28.2 MeV (for phi cutoff 2.5 GeV with RPA)

## Context
This work extends the Bonn-Julich one-boson-exchange potentials (Reuber et al., 1994) from the S=0 and S=-1 baryon-baryon channels into the S=-2 sector, building on earlier variational calculations by Carr et al. (1999) that fitted an effective Lambda-Lambda interaction to hypernuclear data. Unlike those earlier fits, which found the effective interaction significantly more attractive than the free-space one, the present calculation includes RPA correlations (long-range nuclear medium effects) that were omitted previously. The results contradict the conclusions of Gaitanos et al. (2001) about incompatibility between the He data and the Be/B data. The approach enables predictions for heavier double-Lambda hypernuclei (Ca, Zr, Pb) and provides a framework to test SU(6) symmetry violations in the phi-meson couplings to Lambda hyperons.
