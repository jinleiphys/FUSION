# Two-point momentum correlations of few ultracold quasi-one-dimensional trapped fermions: Diffraction patterns

> Spatial and momentum correlations are important in the analysis of the quantum states and different phases of trapped ultracold atom systems as a function of the strength of interatomic interactions. Identification and understanding of spin-resolved patterns exhibited in two-point correlations, accessible directly by experiments, are key for uncovering the symmetry and structure of the many-body wave functions of the trapped system. Using the configuration interaction method for exact diagonalization of the many-body Hamiltonian of $N=2-4$ fermionic atoms trapped in single, double, triple, and quadruple wells, we analyze both two-point momentum and space correlations, as well as associated noise distributions, for a broad range of interparticle contact repulsion strengths and interwell separations, unveiling characteristics allowing insights into the transition, via an intermediate phase, from the non-interacting Bose-Einstein condensate to the weakly interacting quasi-Bose-Einstein regime, and from the latter to the strong-repulsion Tonks-Girardeau (TG) one. The ab-initio numerical predictions are shown to agree well with the results of a constructed analytical model employing localized displaced Gaussian functions to represent the $N$ fermions. The two-point momentum correlations are found to exhibit damped oscillatory diffraction behavior. This diffraction behavior develops fully for atoms trapped in a single well with strong interatomic repulsion in the TG regime, or for atoms in well-separated multi-well traps. Additionally, the two-body momentum correlation and noise distributions are found to exhibit "shortsightedness", with the main contribution coming from nearest-neighboring particles.

## Key claim

The two-point momentum correlations of few ultracold fermions in quasi-one-dimensional traps exhibit damped oscillatory diffraction patterns, which develop fully only for atoms in a single well with strong interatomic repulsion in the Tonks-Girardeau regime or for atoms in well-separated multi-well traps; the authors additionally claim that these correlations display "shortsightedness," with the main contribution coming from nearest-neighboring particles.

## Method

The authors used the full configuration interaction (exact diagonalization) method to solve the many-body Hamiltonian for N=2-4 fermionic atoms (lithium-6) confined in quasi-one-dimensional single, double, triple, and quadruple wells, with a Gaussian-shaped short-range repulsive interaction. They computed two-point momentum and spatial correlation functions, as well as noise distributions, for a range of interaction strengths and interwell separations, and compared the numerical results to an analytical model using displaced Gaussian orbitals to represent localized fermions.

## Key numbers

- Interaction strengths used: g = 0.5486, 5.486, and 54.86 in units of ℏω l₀² (corresponding to 0.0001, 0.001, and 0.01 Ry a₀² respectively)
- Harmonic confinement frequencies: ℏω = ℏω_x = 1 kHz along the x-direction, ℏω_y = 100 kHz along the y-direction
- Gaussian interaction width: σ = √2 l₀/10 = 0.1833 μm
- For two fermions in a single well at g = 54.86 ℏω l₀² (Tonks-Girardeau regime), the separation 2d between the two spatial correlation peaks saturates
- For two fermions in a double well, interwell separations d_w are varied, with the second diffraction peaks marked by stars in the momentum correlation maps at the largest separations

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies of two-point spatial correlations for few fermions in semiconductor quantum dots (Yannouleas 2007, Li 2007, 2009) and for few trapped ultracold atoms (Yannouleas 2004, 2007, 2015, 2016; Garcia-March 2014; Pollack 2017) by providing the corresponding momentum correlations, which can be directly compared to time-of-flight measurements. The paper also addresses the lack of theoretical predictions for two-point momentum correlations of bosons in the Tonks-Girardeau regime noted by Bouchoule (2016). The results enable interpretation of the Tonks-Girardeau regime as a special limit of Wigner-molecule formation in finite systems with strongly repulsive interparticle interactions, and the demonstrated "shortsightedness" suggests that insights from few-body calculations may inform understanding of larger systems.
