# The Hussein-McVoy formula for inclusive breakup revisited. A Tribute to Mahir Hussein

> In 1985, Hussein and McVoy [Nuc. Phys. A445 (1985) 124] elucidated a formula for the evaluation of the nonelastic breakup ("stripping") contribution in inclusive breakup reactions. The formula, based on the spectator core model, acquires a particularly simple and appealing form in the eikonal limit, to the extent that it has become the standard procedure to analyze single-nucleon knockout reactions at intermediate energies. In this contribution, a critical assessment of this formula is presented and its connection with other, noneikonal expressions discussed. Some calculations comparing the different formulae are also presented for the one-nucleon removal of $^{14}$O+$^{9}$Be reaction at several incident energies.

## Key claim

The Hussein-McVoy (HM) formula for nonelastic breakup, in its original noneikonal form, is an incomplete theory because it neglects the Udagawa-Tamura (UT) term, which can be very large or even dominant; however, the eikonal version of the HM formula (EHM) incorporates three-body effects that go beyond its noneikonal counterpart and represents the Glauber limit of the full three-body formula by Austern et al., making it more accurate at high energies where the Glauber approximation is justified.

## Method

The authors revisit the 1985 Hussein-McVoy formula for inclusive breakup and its eikonal (Glauber) limit, deriving the formal connections between the HM formula, the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) model, the Udagawa-Tamura (UT) formula, and the three-body (3B) model of Austern et al. They present numerical calculations comparing the HM, EHM, and IAV formulae for one-nucleon removal from the 14O+9Be reaction at 53 MeV/u and 80 MeV/u, focusing on the nonelastic breakup (NEB) component.

## Key numbers

- The one-nucleon removal of 14O+9Be reaction is calculated at two incident energies: 53 MeV/u and 80 MeV/u.
- The eikonal HM formula (EHM) is expected to be accurate at high energies above approximately 100 MeV per nucleon.
- The ratio Rs = sigma_exp / sigma_theo from knockout studies typically yields Rs < 1, with a systematic dependence on the separation energy of the removed nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Hussein and McVoy (1985) for inclusive breakup reactions, which has become the standard procedure for analyzing single-nucleon knockout reactions at intermediate energies. The paper connects the HM formula with the IAV model of Ichimura, Austern, and Vincent (1985), the UT formula of Udagawa and Tamura (1981), and the three-body model of Austern et al. (1987). The results are relevant to ongoing debates about the interpretation of spectroscopic factors from knockout reactions, particularly the systematic reduction of Rs with increasing separation energy observed by Tostevin et al. (2014), which has been questioned by comparisons with transfer reactions (Flavigny et al., 2013) and (p,pN) reactions (Atac et al., 2018; Kawase et al., 2018; Gomez-Ramos et al., 2018).
