# Fluctuations and Symmetry Energy in Nuclear Fragmentation Dynamics

> Within a dynamical description of nuclear fragmentation, based on the liquid-gas phase transition scenario, we explore the relation between neutron-proton density fluctuations and nuclear symmetry energy. We show that, along the fragmentation path, isovector fluctuations follow the evolution of the local density and approach an equilibrium value connected to the local symmetry energy. Higher density regions are characterized by smaller average asymmetry and narrower isotopic distributions. This dynamical analysis points out that fragment final state isospin fluctuations can probe the symmetry energy of the density domains from which fragments originate.

## Key claim

The paper shows that, in nuclear fragmentation dynamics, isovector (neutron-proton) density fluctuations quickly adjust to the local density and approach an equilibrium value connected to the local symmetry energy, even in rapidly evolving unstable systems. This dynamical analysis points out that fragment final state isospin fluctuations can probe the symmetry energy of the density domains from which fragments originate, but the extracted effective symmetry free energy is reduced by about 30% at saturation density compared to the volume symmetry energy due to the finite-range (k-dependence) of the symmetry potential.

## Method

The author solves the Boltzmann-Langevin equation using the Stochastic Mean Field (SMF) model with test particles, simulating nuclear matter confined in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions at temperature T = 3 MeV. Calculations are performed for symmetric matter (I = 0) and asymmetric matter (I = 0.142) at three initial densities inside the spinodal region (rho1 = 0.0245 fm^-3, rho2 = 2*rho1, rho3 = 3*rho1), using two parametrizations of the symmetry energy (asy-stiff and asy-soft) with a momentum-independent effective interaction and compressibility modulus K = 200 MeV. Isovector fluctuations are extracted from the variance of the isovector density in cells of volume 1 fm^3, evaluated at the freeze-out time t = 200 fm/c when isoscalar density fluctuations saturate.

## Key numbers

* For stable nuclear matter at saturation density (rho_sat = 0.145 fm^-3) and T = 3 MeV, the effective symmetry free energy F_eff^v extracted from SMF simulations is 0.7 times the volume symmetry free energy F_sym (a 30% reduction), using a Gaussian smearing function with width sigma = 0.9 fm.
* The average density of high-density regions at freeze-out ranges from 0.064 fm^-3 (for initial density rho_1 = 0.0245 fm^-3) to 0.10 fm^-3 (rho_2 case) and 0.12 fm^-3 (rho_3 case).
* The asy-stiff parametrization uses C_sym^pot(rho) = 90*rho (MeV), while the asy-soft parametrization uses C_sym^pot(rho) = rho*(238 - 1009*rho) (MeV).
* The compressibility modulus of the effective interaction is K = 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous SMF studies (Baran et al., 2002; rep1) by including fluctuations in the isovector channel, which were previously neglected, and by examining the coupling between isoscalar and isovector modes during spinodal decomposition. The analysis connects to macroscopic statistical model approaches (Botvina et al., Tsang et al., Lefevre et al.) that proposed a relation between cluster isotopic properties and the symmetry energy, but provides a full dynamical description. The results enable extraction of the low-density behavior of the symmetry energy from fragment isotopic distributions and isoscaling analyses, which is relevant for understanding neutron star crust properties (Lattimer, Duc) and exotic nuclei with neutron skins (Colo).
