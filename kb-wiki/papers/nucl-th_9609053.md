# Current algebra and soft pionic modes in asymmetric quark matter

> Pionic excitations are analyzed in isospin-asymmetric Fermi gas of constituent quarks. It is found that in addition to the usual pionic excitations, there exists a very low collective mode with quantum numbers of the charged pion (spin-isospin sound). In the chiral limit the excitation energy of one of the modes scales as the current quark mass, and the mode saturates current-algebraic sum rules. This is in agreement with predictions of current algebra for isospin-asymmetric medium.

## Key claim

In isospin-asymmetric quark matter, a very low collective mode with charged pion quantum numbers (spin-isospin sound) exists; however, the linear scaling of its excitation energy with the current quark mass predicted by current algebra takes effect only at extremely small values of the quark mass (10–100 times lighter than the physical pion mass), making the formal chiral soft mode result of Cohen and Broniowski (1994) of formal rather than practical importance.

## Method

The authors study a Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model with scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and isovector interactions, treating the asymmetric medium as a Fermi gas of constituent u and d quarks in the Hartree approximation. They compute the charged pion propagator including π–A1 mixing, find the zeros of the inverse propagator determinant to obtain excitation energies, and evaluate the contributions of each mode to the generalized Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner and isovector current-algebra sum rules. The calculation is performed for two parameter sets at fixed d-quark concentration y = 2/3 (pure neutron matter) and varying baryon density up to a few times nuclear saturation density.

## Key numbers

- Spin-isospin sound excitation energy rises to only 6 MeV at x = 2 (baryon density twice nuclear saturation density) for parameter set I.
- The "big" π⁺ excitation energy drops to about 100 MeV at x = 2 for parameter set I.
- Nuclear saturation density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³.
- Parameter set I: G_σ = 7.55 GeV⁻², G_δ = 5.41 GeV⁻², G_ρ = 7.09 GeV⁻², Λ = 750 MeV, m = 3.52 MeV; vacuum constituent quark mass M = 361 MeV.
- Parameter set II: G_σ = 4.35 GeV⁻², G_δ = 3.34 GeV⁻², G_ρ = 12.4 GeV⁻², Λ = 954 MeV, m = 2.03 MeV; vacuum constituent quark mass M = 361 MeV.
- The three pionic modes saturate the current-algebra sum rules at the 99% level for all densities and quark masses studied.
- The spin-isospin sound contributes up to 10% to the GMOR sum rule and dominates the isovector sum rule at x > 2 for set I; for set II it contributes 10–20% to the GMOR sum rule with opposite sign and dominates the isovector sum rule at x > 3.
- The chiral soft mode scaling E₊ ∼ m sets in only for α < 10⁻⁴ (set I) or α < 10⁻³ (set II), where α is the ratio of current quark mass to its physical value.

## Context

This work extends the formal current-algebra predictions of Cohen and Broniowski (1994) and Lutz (1995) for isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter to a concrete dynamical model, the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model. It demonstrates that the predicted chiral soft mode exists but is realized by a collective spin-isospin sound (analogous to the mode found in conventional neutron-matter studies by Migdal (1978) and Rho and Wilkinson (1979)) rather than by the vacuum pion branch, and that the chiral-limit scaling only applies at unphysically small quark masses. The results enable future work to examine whether similar collective modes appear in more realistic approaches with nucleon degrees of freedom, particularly investigating the role of short-range correlations known to affect excitation spectra in nuclear matter.
