# Application of the Lorentz-Transform Technique to Meson Photoproduction

> We show that the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) technique which has been successfully applied to photoreactions in light nuclei can also be applied to photoreactions involving particle production. A simple model where results are easily calculable in the traditional fashion is used to test the technique. Specifically we compute inclusive $π^+$ photoproduction from deuterium for photon energies less than 200 MeV using a Yamaguchi model for the NN interaction. It is demonstrated that although the response functions for inclusive meson production do not have favourable asymptotic behavior one can nontheless extract them by inversion of the transform. The implication is that one can treat realistic problems of photo-meson production including all final state interactions by means of the LIT technique.

# Application of the Lorentz-Transform Technique to Meson Photoproduction

## Key claim

The Lorentz integral transform (LIT) technique, previously successful for photoreactions in light nuclei without particle production, can be extended to inclusive meson photoproduction, but only if the transform is inverted over a limited low-energy segment rather than over its full range, because the response function for inclusive meson production does not fall off rapidly enough at high energies.

## Method

The authors compute inclusive π⁺ photoproduction from deuterium for photon energies below 200 MeV using a non-relativistic Yamaguchi separable potential for the NN interaction and the Kroll-Ruderman operator for the photoproduction vertex. They calculate the Lorentz transform L(σ_R, σ_I) of the response function for a fixed pseudo-momentum k_p, then invert the transform using a sum-of-exponentials fit restricted to the energy range 0 ≤ σ_R ≤ 40 MeV, where the model is physically valid. The resulting response functions and cross sections are compared to directly calculated results from traditional methods.

## Key numbers

- The Yamaguchi model parameters: α = 0.2316 fm⁻¹, β₀ = 1.129 fm⁻¹, β₁ = 1.392 fm⁻¹, λ₀ = 0.02774 fm⁻²; deuteron binding energy = 2.224 MeV.
- The Lorentz transform is calculated with σ_I = k_p = 10 MeV; L reaches a maximum at σ_R ≈ 200 MeV.
- The inversion uses N = 10 basis functions; relative error between LIT-extracted and traditionally calculated response functions is in the 1% range over 0 ≤ W ≤ 40 MeV.
- For σ_R,max = 40 MeV, the error at W = 40 MeV is less than 1%; for σ_R,max = 20 MeV, accuracy is limited to energies up to 20 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the LIT technique, previously applied to photoreactions in light nuclei by Efros et al. (1990s) and others, into the NNπ subspace for meson photoproduction. It tests the method against traditional calculations by Dressler, MacDonald, and O'Connell (1979) and Gupta, Anand, and Bhasin (1975) using the same Yamaguchi potential. The next step is to add a pion-nucleon interaction to the Hamiltonian, with the dynamical model of Darwish, Arenhoevel, and Schwamb providing a benchmark, and to apply the technique to higher-A nuclei such as ³He, ³H, or ⁴He.
