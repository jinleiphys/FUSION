# EBAC-DCC Analysis of World Data of pi N, gamma N, and N(e,e') Reactions

> 6 pages, 7 figures. Contribution to the proceedings of the 8th International Workshop on the Physics of Excited Nucleons (NSTAR2011), Newport News, VA, USA, May 17-20, 2011

## Key claim

The EBAC-DCC dynamical coupled-channels model, when fit separately to single-pion production data, predicts two-pion production cross sections that are a factor of about 2 larger than the data at higher invariant masses, even though the shapes of two-particle invariant mass distributions are described very well.

## Method

The analysis uses a Hamiltonian formulation with coupled-channels integral equations for partial-wave amplitudes, including channels \(\pi N, \eta N, \pi\pi N\) (with \(\pi\Delta, \rho N, \sigma N\) resonant components), and \(\gamma N\). The model parameters for strong interactions were first determined by fitting \(\pi N\) elastic scattering data up to invariant mass \(W = 2\) GeV, then electromagnetic parts were fixed by fitting \(\gamma p \rightarrow \pi^0 p, \pi^+ n\) and \(p(e,e'\pi^{0,+})N\) data. The resulting 5-channel model was tested by comparing its predictions for \(\pi N \rightarrow \pi\pi N\) and \(\gamma N \rightarrow \pi\pi N\) production cross sections with data, and resonance poles were extracted via analytic continuation to the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers

- \(\pi N\) elastic scattering data were fit up to invariant mass \(W = 2\) GeV.
- \(\gamma p \rightarrow \pi^0 p\) and \(\gamma p \rightarrow \pi^+ n\) data were fit only up to invariant mass \(W = 1.6\) GeV.
- \(p(e,e'\pi^0)p\) and \(p(e,e'\pi^+)n\) data were fit up to \(W = 1.6\) GeV and \(Q^2 = 1.5\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
- Predicted total cross sections for \(\pi N \rightarrow \pi\pi N\) and \(\gamma N \rightarrow \pi\pi N\) were in excellent agreement with data in the near threshold region \(W \leq 1.4\) GeV.
- Predicted \(\gamma p \rightarrow \pi^+\pi^- p\) and \(\gamma p \rightarrow \pi^0\pi^0 p\) cross sections were a factor of about 2 larger than the data at higher \(W\).

## Context

This work extends the EBAC-DCC analysis program (2006-2010) which separately fit \(\pi N\) elastic scattering (Lee et al., 2007), \(\gamma N \rightarrow \pi N\) (Julia-Diaz et al., 2008), \(N(e,e'\pi)N\) (Julia-Diaz et al., 2009), \(\pi N \rightarrow \pi\pi N\) (Kamano et al., 2009), and \(\gamma N \rightarrow \pi\pi N\) (Kamano et al., 2009). The paper reports that a combined simultaneous analysis of world data for \(\pi N, \gamma N \rightarrow \pi N, \eta N, K\Lambda, K\Sigma\) began in summer 2010, with completion expected in spring 2012. This combined analysis is needed for high-precision extraction of nucleon resonance parameters and \(\gamma N \rightarrow N^*\) form factors, and the results should be interpreted using Lattice QCD and hadron models such as the Dyson-Schwinger-Equation model and constituent quark model.
