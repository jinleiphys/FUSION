# Energy-momentum tensor form factors and spin density distribution in the nucleon calculated in a quantized Skyrme model with vector mesons

> We investigate energy-momentum tensor (EMT) form factors and the spatial spin density distribution in the nucleon within a framework of the quantized Skyrme model with vector mesons. We construct both the canonical and Belinfante improved EMTs and analyze how pseudogauge uncertainty influences local spin and momentum densities while leaving the global nucleon properties unchanged. Using the inversion formulas from nucleon matrix elements in the forward limit, we extract the form factors, $A(t)$, $D(t)$, and $J(t)$, in both pseudogauges and the additional antisymmetric form factor associated with the canonical EMT. We find that the pseudogauge choice leads to sizable differences in the local spin and momentum densities. In particular, the canonical EMT naturally encodes spin density through the antisymmetric tensor structure, while the Belinfante EMT is sensitive to the total angular momentum only. Our results illustrate explicitly how different pseudogauges correspond to different spatial interpretations of nucleon spin structure within the same underlying dynamics. These findings provide a concrete model realization of the pseudogauge ambiguity in QCD-inspired nucleon structure and offer useful intuition for interpreting spatial distributions.

## Key claim

The pseudogauge choice between canonical and Belinfante energy-momentum tensors leads to sizable differences in the local spin and momentum densities within the nucleon, even though global conserved charges remain unchanged; the canonical EMT naturally encodes spin density through its antisymmetric tensor structure while the Belinfante EMT is sensitive only to total angular momentum.

## Method

The authors construct both the canonical and Belinfante-improved energy-momentum tensors within a quantized Skyrme model that includes explicit vector meson fields (rho and omega mesons) coupled to pions. They compute nucleon matrix elements of the EMT in the forward limit using collective-coordinate quantization of the classical soliton solution, extract the form factors A(t), D(t), J(t), and the additional antisymmetric form factor G(t) via inversion formulas from Breit-frame densities, and compare the resulting spatial distributions of spin and momentum densities between the two pseudogauges.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon mass M is used as the mass scale throughout; the model yields A(0)=1 from momentum conservation and J(0)=1/2 for a spin-1/2 target in the Belinfante case.
- The axial coupling g_A comes out smaller than the experimental value in this model, as noted from Meissner (1986).
- The form factor D(t) is expressed via Fourier-Bessel transforms of the pressure profile p(r) as D(t) = (6M/t) * 4π ∫ r² j₀(√(-t) r) p(r) dr, and equivalently via the shear force s(r) as D(t) = (4M/t) * 4π ∫ r² j₂(√(-t) r) s(r) dr.
- The angular momentum form factor J(t) is obtained from the symmetric combination of mixed components as J(t) = (8π/√(-t)) ∫ r² j₁(√(-t) r) ρ₊(r) dr, and the antisymmetric form factor G(t) as G(t) = (16π/√(-t)) ∫ r² j₁(√(-t) r) ρ₋(r) dr.

## Context

This work extends previous Skyrme model studies of EMT form factors by Jung et al. (2013) by explicitly analyzing the role of pseudogauge freedom, which had not been extensively investigated in this class of models. The results provide a concrete model realization of the pseudogauge ambiguity discussed in QCD-inspired nucleon structure by Lorce (2015) and Speranza (2020), and offer intuition for interpreting spatial distributions that will be accessible through future measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider. The framework enables visualization of three-dimensional angular-momentum density patterns and their decomposition into orbital and spin contributions, complementing lattice-QCD determinations by Shanahan (2018) and Hackett (2023) and phenomenological extractions from DVCS at Jefferson Lab by Burkert (2018) and Freese (2021).
