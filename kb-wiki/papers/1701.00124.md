# Legendre Analysis of Differential Distributions in Hadronic Reactions

> Modern experimental facilities, such as CBELSA, ELPH, JLab, MAMI and SPring-8 have provided a tremendous volume of data, often with wide energy and angular coverage, and with increasing precision. For reactions with two hadrons in the final state, these data are often presented as multiple sets of panels, with angular distributions at numerous specific energies. Such presentations have limited visual appeal, and their physical content is typically extracted through some model- dependent treatment. Instead, we explore the use of a Legendre series expansion with a relatively small number of essential coefficients. This approach has been applied in several recent experimental investigations. We present some general properties of the Legendre coefficients in the helicity framework and consider what physical information can be extracted without any model-dependent assumptions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a Legendre series expansion with a relatively small number of essential coefficients provides a model-independent method for presenting and extracting physical information from differential cross sections and polarization observables in two-hadron reactions, though the approach requires data of both high precision and broad angular coverage to determine the higher-order coefficients.

## Method

The authors decompose unpolarized differential cross sections for 2-to-2 hadronic reactions into a series of Legendre polynomials P_J(z), where the coefficients A_J(W) contain bilinear contributions of partial-wave amplitudes with angular momenta j_1 and j_2 satisfying |j_1-j_2| ≤ J ≤ j_1+j_2. They derive parity relations showing that even-J coefficients contain contributions from states of the same parity, while odd-J coefficients contain only interferences between states of opposite parities. The method is applied to photoproduction of pseudoscalar mesons (π^0 and η) using data from the A2 Collaboration at MAMI, fitting Legendre polynomials up to order ten for π^0 production and up to order five for η production.

## Key numbers

- 7978 dσ/dΩ data points for γp→π^0p from A2 Collaboration at MAMI, for incident photon energies E from 218 to 1573 MeV (c.m. energies W = 1136 to 1957 MeV), with ~4 MeV binning below E = 1120 MeV and 30 angular bins
- 2400 dσ/dΩ data points for γp→ηp from A2 Collaboration at MAMI, for incident photon energies E from 710 to 1395 MeV (c.m. energies W = 1488 to 1870 MeV), divided into 120 bins in E with ~4 MeV bin width from threshold to 1008 MeV
- Legendre coefficients for π^0 production required expansion up to J = 10, while η production required expansion up to J = 5 (consistent with N* resonances having spins up to 5/2)
- Renormalization factors for π^0 angular distributions were determined to be within 1% of unity

## Context

This work extends the approach previously applied by the authors and others to photoproduction of single pseudoscalar mesons (CBC, A2, CLAS collaborations) by providing a systematic theoretical framework for Legendre analysis in the helicity formalism. The method enables model-independent extraction of physical information from modern high-statistics data from facilities such as CBELSA, ELPH, JLab, MAMI, and SPring-8, and allows study of higher partial-wave amplitudes through interference effects with resonances, which is not available in other standard approaches. The paper builds on the helicity amplitude formalism of Jacob and Wick (1959) and the partial-wave decomposition of Walker (1969).
