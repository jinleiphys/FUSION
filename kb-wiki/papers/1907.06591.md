# Phases of Hadron-Quark Matter in (Proto) Neutron Stars

> In the first part of this paper, we investigate the possible existence of a structured hadron-quark mixed phase in the cores of neutron stars. This phase, referred to as the hadron-quark pasta phase, consists of spherical blob, rod, and slab rare phase geometries. Particular emphasis is given to modeling the size othis phase in rotating neutron stars. We use the relativistic mean-field theory to model hadronic matter and the non-local three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model to describe quark matter. Based on these models, the hadron-quark pasta phase exists only in very massive neutron stars, whose rotational frequencies are less than around 300 Hz. All other stars are not dense enough to trigger quark deconfinement in their cores. Part two of the paper deals with the quark-hadron composition of hot (proto) neutron star matter. To this end we use a local three-flavor Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model which includes the 't Hooft (quark flavor mixing) term. It is found that this term leads to non-negligible changes in the particle composition of (proto) neutron stars made of hadron-quark matter.

## Key claim
The hadron-quark mixed phase (pasta phase) in neutron star cores exists only in very massive stars with rotational frequencies below about 300 Hz; all other stars are not dense enough to trigger quark deconfinement. The 't Hooft flavor-mixing term in the quark model leads to non-negligible changes in the particle composition of hot proto-neutron stars.

## Method
The authors model hadronic matter using relativistic mean-field theory and quark matter using a non-local three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model for cold rotating neutron stars. For hot proto-neutron star matter, they employ a local three-flavor Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model that includes the 't Hooft (quark flavor mixing) term. The hadron-quark pasta phase is studied by considering spherical blob, rod, and slab rare-phase geometries in the mixed phase.

## Key numbers
- The hadron-quark pasta phase exists only in neutron stars with rotational frequencies less than around 300 Hz.
- The study examines neutron stars with masses high enough to reach the threshold for quark deconfinement; stars below this mass threshold contain no quark matter in their cores.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of hadron-quark mixed phases in neutron stars by incorporating the effect of rotation on the pasta-phase structure. The use of a non-local NJL model for quark matter and a local PNJL model with the 't Hooft term for proto-neutron stars builds on prior approaches by Orsaria et al. and Contrera et al. The results enable more realistic modeling of the interior composition of rapidly rotating neutron stars and proto-neutron stars, which is relevant for interpreting gravitational-wave and electromagnetic observations of compact objects.
