# Sensitivity of the elastic electron-proton cross section to the proton radius

> The precise determination of the proton radius from recent elastic scattering electron-proton data is discussed. The necessary precision on the elastic cross section to discriminate among the values coming from atomic spectroscopy is scrutinized in terms of the relevant quantity, i.e., the derivative of the form factor. It is shown that such precision is two orders of magnitude higher than the precision on the cross section, that is the measured observable. Different fits on the available data and of their discrete derivative, with analytical constraints are shown. The systematic error associated to the radius is evaluated taking into account the uncertainties from different sources, as the extrapolation to the static point, the choice of the class of fitting functions and the range of the data sample. This error is shown to be even orders of magnitude larger than commonly assumed.

## Key claim

The precision required on the elastic electron-proton cross section to discriminate between the two reference proton radius values (0.84 fm and 0.88 fm) is two orders of magnitude higher than the precision on the cross section itself, and the systematic error associated with the radius extraction is orders of magnitude larger than commonly assumed, particularly due to the extrapolation to the static point and the choice of fitting functions.

## Method

The authors used the A1 Collaboration data (Bernauer 2010) as the most precise available low-momentum-transfer electron-proton scattering data, extracting the electric form factor and its discrete derivative from both spline-based global fits and Rosenbluth-separated data. They performed polynomial fits to the electric form factor data with constraints from a genuine charge density condition (generalized Schwarz inequality), and evaluated the relative difference in cross sections corresponding to the two extreme radius values of 0.84 fm and 0.88 fm. The systematic error on the radius was assessed by considering uncertainties from extrapolation to Q^2=0, the class of fitting functions, and the range of the data sample.

## Key numbers

- The two reference proton radius values used for discrimination are R_E = 0.84 fm (consistent with muonic hydrogen) and R_E = 0.88 fm (similar to the 2010-CODATA value).
- For Q^2 = 10^{-3} GeV^2, the relative difference in cross section between the two radius hypotheses is lower than 10^{-3}, requiring sub-per-mille precision on cross section measurements.
- The ratio of relative errors on the discrete derivative to those on the electric form factor exceeds 10^2 for spline data below Q^2 = 0.02 GeV^2 and for Rosenbluth data below Q^2 = 0.1 GeV^2.
- The A1 spline data cover the Q^2 range from 0.0005 GeV^2 to 1.0 GeV^2 (38 data points), while Rosenbluth data cover 0.0152 GeV^2 to 0.5524 GeV^2 (77 data points).
- The muonic hydrogen radius value is R_E^{H^mu} = 0.84087(39) fm; the 2010-CODATA value is R_E^{CO} = 0.8775(51) fm.

## Context

This work extends the critical discussion of proton radius extraction from electron scattering, particularly following the discrepancy between muonic hydrogen results (Pohl 2010, Antognini 2013) and earlier electron-based measurements. The paper directly addresses the analyses of the A1 Collaboration (Bernauer 2010) and shows that the derivative-based extraction amplifies uncertainties by orders of magnitude compared to the cross section precision, explaining why different re-analyses (Kraus 2014, Lorenz 2014, Horbatsch 2016, Griffioen 2015) yield radii spanning the full range from muonic to electronic hydrogen values. The work enables a more realistic assessment of systematic uncertainties in future low-Q^2 scattering experiments and highlights the need for sub-per-mille cross section precision to resolve the proton radius puzzle.
