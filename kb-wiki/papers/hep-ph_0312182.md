# Chiral Doubling of Heavy-Light Hadrons and the Vector Manifestation of Hidden Local Symmetry

> Starting with a hidden local symmetry Lagrangian at the vector manifestation (VM) fixed point that incorporates heavy-quark symmetry and matching the bare theory to QCD, we calculate the splitting of chiral doublers of heavy-light mesons proposed by Nowak, Rho and Zahed, and Bardeen and Hill. We show, in the three-flavor chiral limit, that the splitting is directly proportional to the light-quark condensate <\bar{q}q> and comes out to be \sim (1/3) m_N where m_N is the nucleon mass, implying that the splitting vanishes in the chiral limit at the chiral restoration point -- temperature T_c, density n_c or number of flavors N_F^c. The result turns out to be surprisingly simple with the vector (ρ) meson playing the crucial role in quantum corrections, pointing to the relevance of the VM to QCD in the way chiral symmetry is manifested in hadronic matter. We also make predictions on the hadronic decay processes of the excited heavy (charm) -light mesons \tilde{D}.

## Key claim

The mass splitting between chiral-doublet heavy-light mesons (the odd-parity \( \mathcal{M}(0^-,1^-) \) and even-parity \( \tilde{\mathcal{M}}(0^+,1^+) \) states) is directly proportional to the light-quark condensate \( \langle \bar{q}q \rangle \) and vanishes at the chiral restoration point, but this result holds only in the three-flavor chiral limit and under the assumption that chiral restoration is described by the vector manifestation (VM) fixed point of hidden local symmetry.

## Method

The authors construct a hidden local symmetry (HLS) Lagrangian at the VM fixed point, incorporating heavy-quark symmetry, and match the bare theory to QCD via operator product expansion (OPE) correlators for the pseudoscalar and scalar channels. They then compute one-loop quantum corrections to the mass splitting using renormalization-group evolution from the matching scale \( \Lambda_M = 1.1 \) GeV down to the \( \rho \)-meson mass scale, finding that only the vector-meson (\( \rho \)) loop contributes to the splitting at one-loop order.

## Key numbers

- Bare mass splitting from matching: \( \Delta M_{\rm bare} \simeq 0.19 \) GeV, derived using \( \langle \bar{q}q \rangle_{\Lambda_M} = -(228 \pm 25\ \text{MeV})^3 \) and \( F_D = 0.205 \pm 0.020 \) GeV.
- Quantum correction factor: \( C_{\rm quantum} = 1.6 \), obtained with \( \mu = m_\rho = 771 \) MeV, \( \Lambda = \Lambda_M = 1.1 \) GeV, \( g(m_\rho) = 6.27 \), and \( k \simeq 0.59 \).
- Final quantum-corrected splitting: \( \Delta M \approx 0.31 \pm 0.12 \) GeV, consistent with the constituent quark mass \( \Sigma \sim m_\rho/3 \sim 310 \) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral-doubling predictions of Nowak, Rho, and Zahed (1993) and Bardeen and Hill (1994) by embedding them in the HLS/VM framework, which links the splitting directly to the quark condensate and the vector-meson dynamics. It contrasts with the standard linear-sigma-model scenario by showing that the vector meson, not the pion, dominates quantum corrections. The results enable predictions for hadronic decays of excited charm mesons \( \tilde{D} \) and suggest that measurements of chiral-doublet splittings at finite temperature or density could probe chiral restoration, as proposed by Nowak, Rho, and Zahed (2003).
