# Strong screening effects on resonant nuclear reaction $^{23}$Mg $(p,ﾎｳ)$ $^{24}$Al in the surface of magnetars

> Based on the theory of relativistic superstrong magnetic fields(SMFs), by using the method of the Thomas-Fermi-Dirac approximations, we investigate the problem of strong electron screening(SES) in SMFs, and the influence of SES on the nuclear reaction of $^{23}$Mg $(p, ﾎｳ)$$^{24}$Al. Our calculations show that the nuclear reaction will be markedly effected by the SES in SMFs in the surface of magnetars. Our calculated screening rates can increase two orders of magnitude due to SES in SMFs.

# Strong screening effects on resonant nuclear reaction $^{23}$Mg $(p,ﾎｳ)$ $^{24}$Al in the surface of magnetars

## Key claim
The strong electron screening (SES) in superstrong magnetic fields (SMFs) can increase the resonant nuclear reaction rates for $^{23}$Mg $(p,ﾎｳ)$ $^{24}$Al by more than two orders of magnitude in the surface layers of magnetars, but this enhancement is strongly dependent on density, temperature, and magnetic field strength, with the effect being most pronounced at low temperatures ($T_9 = 0.1$) and relatively low densities ($\rho_7 = 0.1$), while at high densities ($\rho_7 \geq 1.3$) the magnetic field influence on the screening potential becomes slight.

## Method
The author employed the Thomas-Fermi-Dirac (TFD) approximation in relativistic superstrong magnetic fields to compute the electron screening potential, incorporating electron exchange energy and Landau level quantization. The resonant reaction rates for four resonance states of $^{23}$Mg $(p,ﾎｳ)$ $^{24}$Al were calculated using the Breit-Wigner formula with resonance parameters adopted from prior experimental work (Endt 1998; Visser 2007; Herndl 1998; Wiescher 1986), and the screening enhancement factor was applied as $F_{\rm{r}}^{\rm{B}} = \exp(11.605 U_{\rm{s}} / T_9)$ where $U_{\rm{s}}$ is the screening potential in SMFs.

## Key numbers
- The electron screening potential (ESP) reaches a maximum value of 0.0188 MeV at $B_{12} = 580.7$ and $\rho_7 = 0.1$.
- The maximum screening enhancement factor (SEF) reaches 3289 at $B_{12} = 10^4$, $\rho_7 = 10$, and $T_9 = 0.1$.
- The maximum strongly screened reaction rate for the $E_2 = 0.663$ MeV resonance state reaches 350.5 cm$^3$ mol$^{-1}$ s$^{-1}$ at $\rho_7 = 100$.
- The resonance state at $E_{\rm{r}} = 478$ keV dominates the total reaction rate for $T_9 = 0.2 - 1.681$, while the $E_{\rm{r}} = 663$ keV resonance dominates at $T_9 > 1.681$.
- The screening potential decreases by about two orders of magnitude when $10^3 < B_{12} < 2 \times 10^3$ at $\rho_7 = 0.1$.

## Context
This work extends the electron screening theory of Fushiki (1989) to resonant nuclear reactions in superstrong magnetic fields, which had been neglected by prior studies of the $^{23}$Mg $(p,ﾎｳ)$ $^{24}$Al reaction such as Wallace (1981), Iliadis (2001), and Kubono (1995). It differs from the approach of Spitaleri and Bertulani (2015) who analyzed SES without magnetic fields, and from Potekhin and Chabrier (2013) who studied nonresonant reactions in neutron star envelopes. The results enable improved modeling of nucleosynthesis in magnetar surfaces, particularly the breakout from the Ne-Na cycle into the Mg-Al cycle and the synthesis of heavy elements like $^{26}$Al.
