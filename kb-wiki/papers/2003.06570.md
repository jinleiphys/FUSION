# States of $^{12}$N with enhanced radii

> The differential cross sections of the $^{12}$C($^3$He,t)$^{12}$N reaction leading to formation of the 1$^+$ (ground state), 2$^+$(0.96 MeV), 2$^{-}$(1.19 MeV), and 1$^{-}$(1.80 MeV) states of $^{12}$N are measured at $E$($^3$He)=40 MeV. The analysis of the data is carried out within the modified diffraction model (MDM) and distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA). Enhanced $rms$ radii were obtained for the ground, 2$^{-}$(1.19 MeV), and 1$^{-}$(1.80 MeV) states. We revealed that $^{12}$B, $^{12}$N, and $^{12}$C in the IAS with T=1, and spin-parities 2$^{-}$ and 1$^{-}$ have increased radii and exhibit properties of neutron and proton halo states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the 2⁻ (1.19 MeV) and 1⁻ (1.80 MeV) excited states of ¹²N, as well as its ground state, have enhanced root-mean-square radii, and that these states together with their isobaric analogue states in ¹²B and ¹²C exhibit properties of neutron and proton halo states; however, the radii for ¹²N are described as "preliminary" and the analysis carries an uncertainty of about 15-20% for the ground state estimate.

## Method

The differential cross sections of the ¹²C(³He,t)¹²N charge-exchange reaction were measured at E(³He) = 40 MeV using the K130 cyclotron at the University of Jyväskylä, with four ΔE-E detector telescopes covering a center-of-mass angular range of 8° to 69°. The data were analyzed using the Modified Diffraction Model (MDM), which extracts diffraction radii from the positions of minima and maxima in angular distributions, and the Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) using the DWUCK4 code with a semi-microscopic optical potential (SMDOM) and single-particle Woods-Saxon potentials for form factor calculations.

## Key numbers

- The ¹²C(³He,t)¹²N reaction was measured at E(³He) = 40 MeV, with a total energy resolution of about 140 keV achieved through beam monochromatization.
- The ground-state rms radius of ¹²N from the MDM analysis is 2.8 ± 0.4 fm, consistent with the DWBA estimate of about 2.9 fm and the literature value of 2.47 ± 0.07 fm from Ozawa (2001).
- The rms radius of ¹²N in the 2⁻ (1.19 MeV) state is 2.8 ± 0.3 fm from the MDM analysis.
- The rms radius of ¹²N in the 1⁻ (1.80 MeV) state is 3.0 ± 0.1 fm from the MDM analysis.
- The diffraction radius for the 1.19 MeV state was found to be 6.0 ± 0.1 fm (L=0 component) and 6.3 ± 0.3 fm (L=2 component); for the 1.80 MeV state it is 6.3 ± 0.1 fm.
- The "corrected" ground-state diffraction radius for ¹²N is 5.8 ± 0.2 fm, derived from elastic ³He + ¹²C scattering at 25.3 MeV giving R_dif(0) = 5.45 ± 0.17 fm.
- For comparison, the rms radius of ¹²B in the 2⁻ (1.67 MeV) state is 2.58 ± 0.11 fm with D₁ = 53%, and in the 1⁻ (2.62 MeV) state is 2.86 ± 0.11 fm with D₁ = 62% from Belyaeva (2018).
- For ¹²C, the rms radius in the 2⁻ (16.57 MeV) isobaric analogue state is 2.85 ± 0.06 fm with D₁ = 50% from Belyaeva (2020).

## Context

This work extends the study of halo states in the A=12 triplet by measuring radii in the proton-rich mirror nucleus ¹²N, following prior ANC analyses of neutron halos in ¹²B by Belyaeva (2018) and Liu (2001). The results confirm that the isobaric analogue states with T=1 and spin-parities 2⁻ and 1⁻ in ¹²B, ¹²N, and ¹²C all exhibit enhanced radii, supporting the charge independence of nuclear forces for halo formation. The MDM approach, previously applied to inelastic scattering and to the unbound 1/2⁺ state in ¹³N by Demyanova (2016), is here extended to charge-exchange reactions for proton-halo searches, enabling future studies of unbound proton-rich states where the ANC method is not applicable.
