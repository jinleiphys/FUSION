# Importance of the deuteron breakup in the deuteron knockout reaction

> An isoscalar $pn$ pair is expected to emerge in nuclei that have similar proton and neutron numbers and it may be a candidate for a deuteron ``cluster.'' There is, however, no experimental evidence for it. The purpose of this paper is to construct a new reaction model for the ($p,pd$) reaction including the deuteron breakup in the elementary process and the deuteron reformation by the final-state interactions (FSIs). How these processes contribute to the observables of the reaction is investigated. The distorted wave impulse approximation is extended in twofold. The elementary processes of the ($p,pd$), i.e., the $p$-$d$ elastic scattering and $d(p,p)pn$ reaction, are described with an impulse picture employing a nucleon-nucleon effective interaction. The three-body scattering waves in the final state of the ($p,pd$) reaction are calculated with the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method. The triple differential cross section (TDX) of the ($p,pd$) reaction is calculated with the new model. The elementary processes are described reasonably well with the present model. As for the ($p,pd$) reaction, the deuteron reformation can either increase or decrease the TDX height depending on the interference between the elastic and breakup channel of deuteron, while the \textit{back-coupling} effect always decreases it. It is shown that the deuteron reformation significantly changes the TDX of the ($p,pd$) reaction through the interference. It is important to include this process to quantitatively discuss the ($p,pd$) cross sections in view of the deuteron formation in nuclei. For more quantitative discussion regarding the experimental data, further improvement will be necessary.

## Key claim

The deuteron reformation process (where a deuteron broken up in the elementary process reforms via final-state interactions) significantly changes the triple differential cross section of the ($p,pd$) reaction through interference between elastic and breakup channels, but the effect can either increase or decrease the cross section depending on the target nucleus; the back-coupling effect always decreases the cross section.

## Method

The authors extended the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) by combining it with the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC), calling the new model CDCCIA. The elementary processes of the ($p,pd$) reaction (p-d elastic scattering and d(p,p)pn breakup) were described with an impulse picture using a nucleon-nucleon effective interaction, while the three-body scattering waves in the final state of the ($p,pd$) reaction were calculated with CDCC. The triple differential cross section (TDX) was then computed for the ($p,pd$) reaction on 16O, 40Ca, and 56Ni targets at 250 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The correction factor C_corr for the p-d elastic scattering cross section was determined to be 0.70 at 120 MeV, 0.70 at 135 MeV, 0.71 at 155 MeV, 0.68 at 170 MeV, 0.69 at 190 MeV, and 0.68 at 250 MeV; the average value of 0.69 was used in the ($p,pd$) calculation.
- For the 16O($p,pd$)14N reaction at 250 MeV, the deuteron reformation process decreased the TDX by about 21% around the peak.
- For the 40Ca($p,pd$)38K reaction at 250 MeV, the deuteron reformation process increased the TDX by about 11%.
- For the 56Ni($p,pd$)54Co reaction at 250 MeV, the deuteron reformation process increased the TDX by about 14%.
- The deuteron separation energies used were 24.68 MeV for 16O, 19.60 MeV for 40Ca, and 19.97 MeV for 56Ni.
- The pn continuum states included orbital angular momenta l = 0, 2, and 4 with a maximum p-n linear momentum k_max = 2.1 fm^-1.

## Context

This work extends the standard DWIA framework (Jacob 1966, Jacob 1973, Chant 1977, Chant 1983, Wakasa 2017) for knockout reactions by including deuteron breakup in the elementary process and deuteron reformation via final-state interactions, which were not treated in previous ($p,pd$) analyses such as Samanta (1982). The CDCCIA model enables more quantitative discussion of ($p,pd$) cross sections in view of deuteron formation in nuclei, though the authors note that further improvement will be necessary for comparison with experimental data. The combination of DWIA and CDCC was previously used in hadron physics for the 3He(K-, pi-)3_Lambda He reaction (Harada 2015), but the present work activates a new degree of freedom by incorporating deuteron breakup d(p,p)pn in the elementary process.
