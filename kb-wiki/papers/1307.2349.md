# Skyrme effective pseudopotential up to next-to-next-to leading order

> The explicit form of the next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) of the Skyrme effective pseudopotential compatible with all required symmetries and especially with gauge invariance is presented in Cartesian basis. It is shown in particular that for such a particular pseudopotential there is no spin-orbit contribution and that the D-wave term suggested in the original Skyrme's formulation does not satisfy the invariance properties. The six new N2LO terms contribute to both the equation of state and Landau parameters. These contributions to symmetric nuclear matter are explicitly given and discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents the explicit Cartesian-basis form of the next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) Skyrme effective pseudopotential that satisfies gauge invariance, reducing the number of independent parameters from 15 to 6; the authors show that the original D-wave term from Skyrme's formulation is not gauge invariant and that no spin-orbit contribution of the Bell-Skyrme type survives at this order.

## Method

Starting from the general N2LO pseudopotential derived in spherical-tensor representation by Raimondi et al. (2011), the authors imposed gauge invariance order by order via the condition that the commutator of the potential with an arbitrary phase function vanishes. They then rewrote the six surviving independent parameters in a Skyrme-like Cartesian form with operators \(T_e\) and \(T_o\) that mix momenta and spins, and derived the explicit contributions to the equation of state and Landau parameters of symmetric nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- The N2LO pseudopotential has **6 independent parameters** after imposing gauge invariance, down from 15 general terms at fourth order.
- The contribution to the energy per particle of symmetric nuclear matter is \((E/A)^{(4)} = \frac{9}{280} \left[ 3 t_1^{(4)} + (5 + 4 x_2^{(4)}) t_2^{(4)} \right] \rho k_F^4\), where \(\rho\) is density and \(k_F = (3\pi^2 \rho/2)^{1/3}\).
- The contribution to the incompressibility at saturation density \(\rho_0\) is \(K_v^{(4)} = \frac{9}{10} \left[ 3 t_1^{(4)} + (5 + 4 x_2^{(4)}) t_2^{(4)} \right] \left( \frac{3\pi^2}{2} \right)^{4/3} \rho_0^{7/3}\).
- The fourth-order pseudopotential contributes only to Landau parameters with \(l \le 2\) for central channels and \(l \le 1\) for tensor channels, with explicit \(L_4\) functions given for \(f, f', g, g', h, h'\) in terms of the six parameters.

## Context

This work extends the standard Skyrme interaction (Vautherin and Brink, 1972), which is limited to second order in momenta plus a density-dependent term, by adding gauge-invariant fourth-order terms. It corrects an earlier misidentification by Raimondi et al. (2011) regarding the D-wave term and confirms that the D-wave term of Skyrme (1959) is not gauge invariant. The results enable future fitting of the six N2LO parameters to nuclear properties, with the Landau parameter expressions providing constraints to avoid finite-size instabilities as studied by Pastore et al. (2012a, 2012b, 2012c).
