# A Consistent Study of the the Low Energy Baryon Spectrum and the Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction within the Chiral Quark Model

> By solving the Schrödinger equation for the three-quark system in the hyperspherical harmonic approach, we have studied the low energy part of the nucleon and $Δ$ spectra using a quark-quark interaction which reproduces the nucleon-nucleon phenomenology. The quark-quark hamiltonian considered includes, besides the usual one-gluon exchange, pion and sigma exchanges generated by the chiral symmetry breaking. The baryonic spectrum obtained is reasonable and the resulting wave function gives consistency to the ansatz used in the two baryon system.

# A Consistent Study of the Low Energy Baryon Spectrum and the Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction within the Chiral Quark Model

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a single quark-quark interaction, with all parameters fixed to reproduce nucleon-nucleon phenomenology (phase shifts, deuteron properties, and charge-exchange reactions), yields a reasonable low-energy baryon spectrum up to 0.7 GeV excitation energy, provided a linear confining potential is used. The consistency is conditional: the pion exchange between quarks is essential to obtain the correct relative energy ordering of the Roper resonance and the first negative parity state, but a complete inversion as required by experimental data would require modifying the pion interaction constants in a way that would destroy the agreement in the NN system.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrödinger equation for the three-quark system using the hyperspherical harmonic expansion, including the first two terms (k=0 and k=2) for positive parity states and the k=1 term for negative parity states. The quark-quark Hamiltonian included one-gluon exchange (with a smeared delta function term), one-pion exchange, and one-sigma exchange potentials, with a linear confining potential. All parameters were taken from previous fits to NN scattering phase shifts, deuteron properties, and the pp → nΔ⁺⁺ reaction, with no parameters adjusted to the baryon spectrum.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass m_q = 313 MeV; effective quark-gluon coupling α_s = 0.485; linear confinement strength a_c = 91.488 MeV·fm⁻¹; chiral coupling α_ch = 0.0269; cutoff parameter Λ = 4.2 fm⁻¹
- With the full potential (including pion exchange), the Roper resonance N(1440) excitation energy is 530 MeV and the first negative parity state N*(1535) excitation energy is 512 MeV, making them almost degenerate
- When the pion interaction is removed and the OGE strength is refitted to the Δ-N mass difference, the N*(1535) appears at 365 MeV (deeply bound relative to the Roper at 521 MeV)
- Using Λ = 5.0 fm⁻¹ instead of Λ = 4.2 fm⁻¹ gives 516 MeV for the Roper and 534 MeV for the first negative parity state (complete inversion), but this would destroy agreement in the NN system
- The harmonic oscillator parameter b = 0.518 fm, used in the RGM calculations of NN phase shifts and deuteron properties, produces a diagonal NN potential kernel very similar to that obtained with the full Schrödinger wave function

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark model approach of Fernandez, Valcarce, Straub and Faessler (1993) and Valcarce, Buchmann, Fernandez and Faessler (1994), which successfully reproduced NN scattering phase shifts, deuteron properties, and the pp → nΔ⁺⁺ reaction, by now applying the same interaction to the three-quark baryon spectrum. It addresses a long-standing inconsistency between spectroscopic models (such as those of Isgur and Karl, 1978-1981) that fit parameters independently for positive and negative parity sectors, and quark models of the NN interaction (such as those of Oka and Yazaki, 1980, and Faessler, Fernandez, Lubeck and Shimizu, 1982-1983). The results confirm the validity of the gaussian ansatz wave function used in resonating group method calculations and solve the controversy about the value of the harmonic oscillator parameter b. The model provides a starting point for a unified description of the baryon spectrum and baryon-baryon interaction, with future work needed in the strange sector and decay processes.
