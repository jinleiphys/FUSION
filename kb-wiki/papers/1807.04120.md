# NNNLO pressure of cold quark matter: leading logarithm

> At high baryon chemical potential $μ_B$, the equation of state of QCD allows a weak-coupling expansion in the QCD coupling $α_s$. The result is currently known up to and including the full next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) $α_s^2$. Starting at this order, the computations are complicated by the modification of particle propagation in a dense medium, which necessitates non-perturbative treatment of the scale $α_s^{1/2} μ_B$. In this work, we apply a Hard-Thermal-Loop scheme for capturing the contributions of this scale to the weak-coupling expansion, and use it to determine the leading-logarithm contribution to NNNLO: $α_s^3 \ln^2 α_s$. This result is the first improvement to the equation of state of massless cold quark matter in 40 years. The new term is negligibly small, and thus significantly increases our confidence in the applicability of the weak-coupling expansion.

# NNNLO pressure of cold quark matter: leading logarithm

## Key claim
The authors compute the leading-logarithm contribution at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N³LO) to the pressure of cold, dense QCD matter, obtaining a term proportional to αₛ³ ln² αₛ. This is the first new perturbative order in the equation of state of massless cold quark matter since the full NNLO result was derived by Freedman and McLerran in 1977, but the new term is negligibly small, significantly increasing confidence in the applicability of the weak-coupling expansion.

## Method
The authors apply a Hard-Thermal-Loop (HTL) scheme to separate soft and hard momentum contributions to the pressure, using a two-loop HTL computation originally derived for hot quark-gluon plasma but equally applicable to cold quark matter because the soft contributions are insensitive to the details of the hard scale. They extract the leading double logarithm by expanding the HTL-resummed two-loop diagrams in the doubly-semisoft limit, isolating the m∞⁴ term which yields dimensionless integrals that produce the ln² αₛ factor. The coefficient is obtained by computing the convergent angular integral from the constant term in the expansion of the integrand about P/Q = 0 and Q/P = 0.

## Key numbers
- The N³LO leading-logarithm contribution to the pressure is c₃,₂ αₛ³ ln² αₛ = (3(μB/3)⁴/(4π²)) × [-0.266075 αₛ³ ln² αₛ] for Nc = Nf = 3.
- The asymptotic HTL mass is m∞² = αₛ μB² Nf / (9π).
- The NNLO pressure expansion includes terms: 1 - 0.636620 αₛ - 0.303964 αₛ² ln αₛ - (0.874355 + 0.911891 ln(Λ̄/(μB/3))) αₛ².
- The new term is evaluated with Λ̄ = 2μB/3 and ΛMS̄ = 0.378 GeV, and constitutes a tiny correction to the existing NNLO result.

## Context
This work extends the perturbative QCD equation of state for cold dense matter beyond the full NNLO result of Freedman and McLerran (1977), which had stood for 40 years without improvement. The result builds on the HTL methodology developed for hot QCD by Andersen et al. (2002) and on the separation of soft contributions presented by Kurkela et al. (2016). The negligible size of the new term increases confidence in using the perturbative result as ab-initio input for neutron star calculations, including the work of Kurkela et al. (2014), Gorda et al. (2016), Annala et al. (2017), and Most et al. (2018), as well as gravitational-wave signal simulations from neutron-star mergers.
