# Probing properties of nuclear spin-orbit interaction with nucleon spin polarization in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions

> The nucleon spin polarization perpendicular to the reaction plane ($P_y$) and along the beam direction ($P_z$) in Au+Au collisions at the beam energy of 100A MeV with different nuclear spin-orbit interactions has been studied based on a spin- and isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (SIBUU) transport model. While the spin polarization is weaker with a weaker nuclear spin-orbit coupling as intuitively expected, a density-dependent nuclear spin-orbit coupling enhances the $P_y$ at large rapidities and leads to a less negative or large $P_y$ at high transverse momenta. The difference in the $P_y$ of free neutrons and protons at midrapidities and at small transverse momenta is sensitive to the isospin dependence of the nuclear spin-orbit interaction. While the $P_z$ is also affected by the properties of nuclear spin-orbit interaction in some sense, the behavior of the $P_y$ serves as a good probe of the strength, density dependence, and isospin dependence of nuclear spin-orbit interaction.

# Probing properties of nuclear spin-orbit interaction with nucleon spin polarization in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim
The perpendicular spin polarization ($P_y$) of free nucleons in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions can serve as a probe of the strength, density dependence, and isospin dependence of the nuclear spin-orbit interaction, but the longitudinal spin polarization ($P_z$) is less informative for the isospin dependence. The sensitivity of $P_y$ to these properties is demonstrated through simulations using a spin- and isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (SIBUU) transport model, with the caveat that the results are based on a specific choice of momentum-independent mean-field potential and Skyrme-type spin-orbit interaction.

## Method
The study employs the SIBUU transport model, which incorporates nucleon spin as an additional degree of freedom and uses a Skyrme-type spin-orbit interaction in the mean-field approximation. Mid-central (impact parameter b=8 fm) and mid-peripheral (b=12 fm) Au+Au collisions at a beam energy of 100A MeV are simulated, with the spin polarization analyzed from final-state free nucleons (local density below $\rho_0/8$) at t=100 fm/c. Different spin-orbit coupling strengths ($W_0^\star$ = 80 or 150 MeV fm$^5$), a density-dependent form ($W_0^\star = 150(\rho/\rho_0)$ MeV fm$^5$), and different isospin dependencies (default $\alpha=1,\beta=1$ versus $\alpha=2,\beta=-1$) are compared.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: 100A MeV for Au+Au collisions; impact parameters b=8 fm (mid-central) and b=12 fm (mid-peripheral).
- Default spin-orbit coupling strength: $W_0 = 150$ MeV fm$^5$; weaker case: $W_0^\star = 80$ MeV fm$^5$.
- Density-dependent form: $W_0^\star = 150(\rho/\rho_0)$ MeV fm$^5$ with saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$.
- Simulation parameters: test-particle number per nucleon N=400, lattice spacing l=1 fm, range parameter n=2.
- Spin polarization $P_y$ is generally larger in mid-peripheral collisions than in mid-central collisions; a stronger spin-orbit coupling ($W_0^\star=150$ MeV fm$^5$) leads to larger $P_y$ compared to $W_0^\star=80$ MeV fm$^5$, with growth slower than linear.
- At midrapidities, neutrons have larger $P_y$ than protons for $\alpha=1,\beta=1$, but the inverse is observed for $\alpha=2,\beta=-1$; this effect is larger in peripheral collisions.
- The second Fourier sine coefficient $\langle P_z \sin(2\phi) \rangle$ is stronger in mid-peripheral collisions than in mid-central collisions; a smaller $W_0^\star$ or density-dependent one generally leads to a smaller $\langle P_z \sin(2\phi) \rangle$.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous development of the SIBUU transport model (Xu 2015), which incorporated nucleon spin and nuclear spin-orbit interaction in the mean-field approximation, and earlier studies on spin-dependent collective flows (Xu 2012, Xia 2014) and nucleon spin polarization (Xia 2019). The study is motivated by experimental measurements of $\Lambda$ spin polarization at higher collision energies (STAR 2017, 2022) and the need to constrain the nuclear spin-orbit interaction, whose strength ranges from 80 to 150 MeV fm$^5$ from fitting nuclear properties (Lesinski 2007, Zalewski 2008, Bender 2009). The results suggest that measurements of $P_y$ in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions could help resolve uncertainties in the density and isospin dependence of the spin-orbit interaction, which are relevant to understanding bubble structures in nuclei (Sorlin 2013), charge radii kinks (PhysRevC.91.021302), and symmetry energy extraction from neutron-skin measurements (Yue 2024, Zhao 2024).
