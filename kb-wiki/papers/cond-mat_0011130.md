# Second Law of Thermodynamics and Macroscopic Observables within Boltzmann's principle, an attempt

> Boltzmann's principleS=k*ln W is generalized to non-equilibrium Hamiltonian systems with possibly fractal distributions in phase space by the box-counting volume. The probabilities P(M) of macroscopic observables M are given by the ratio P(M)=W(M)/W of these volumes of the sub-manifold {M} of the microcanonical ensemble with the constraint M to the one without. With this extension of the phase-space integral the Second Law is derived without invoking the thermodynamic limit. The irreversibility in this approach is due to the replacement of the phase space volume of the possibly fractal sub-manifold {M} by the volume of the closure of {M}. In contrast to conventional coarse graining the box-counting volume is defined by the limit of infinite resolution.

## Key claim

Boltzmann's principle \(S = k \ln W\) can be extended to non-equilibrium Hamiltonian systems with fractal phase-space distributions by replacing the standard phase-space integral with a box-counting volume integral, which allows derivation of the Second Law of Thermodynamics without invoking the thermodynamic limit, provided the system has many more degrees of freedom than the few macroscopic control parameters.

## Method

The author generalizes the microcanonical phase-space integral by replacing the standard Lebesgue measure with a box-counting volume (box-counting "measure") defined as \(\mbox{vol}_{box}[\mathcal{M}] = \underline{\lim}_{\delta \to 0} \delta^d N_\delta[\mathcal{M}]\), where \(N_\delta\) counts boxes of size \(\delta^{6N}\) covering the submanifold \(\mathcal{M}\). For a system initially confined to volume \(V_1\) that expands into volume \(V_2 > V_1\), the dynamically evolved distribution becomes fractal and dense in the larger energy shell; the box-counting volume of the fractal set equals the volume of its closure, which is the full microcanonical ensemble of the larger volume. The entropy at late times then becomes \(S = k \ln W(E,N,V_2)\), which is larger than the initial entropy \(S = k \ln W(E,N,V_1)\).

## Key numbers

- The box-counting volume of the set of rational numbers between 0 and 1 is \(\mbox{vol}_{box}\{\mathbf{Q}\} = 1\), equal to the measure of the real numbers, illustrating that the box-counting "measure" is not a true measure.
- The phase space has dimension \(d = 6N-1\) for an \(N\)-body system at fixed energy.
- The box-counting dimension is defined as \(\dim_{box}[\mathcal{M}] := \underline{\lim}_{\delta \to 0} \ln N_\delta[\mathcal{M}] / (-\ln \delta)\).

## Context

This work extends Boltzmann's (1877, 1884) original principle, which Boltzmann applied only to dilute gases, and Schrödinger's (1944) view that the principle was otherwise useless. The author previously demonstrated (Gross 2001) that Boltzmann's principle works for phase transitions in small, non-extensive systems without the thermodynamic limit. The approach contrasts with Lebowitz (1999), who required the thermodynamic limit \(N \to \infty\), and with conventional coarse-graining methods, because the box-counting volume is defined by the limit of infinite resolution rather than a finite coarse-graining scale. The work enables a foundation for thermodynamics of small systems approaching equilibrium without invoking the thermodynamic limit.
