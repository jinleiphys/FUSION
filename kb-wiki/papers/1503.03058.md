# Confining Dyon-Anti-Dyon Coulomb Liquid Model I

> We revisit the dyon-anti-dyon liquid model for the Yang-Mills confining vacuum discussed by Diakonov and Petrov, by retaining the effects of the classical interactions mediated by the streamline between the dyons and anti-dyons. In the SU(2) case the model describes a 4-component strongly interacting Coulomb liquid in the center symmetric phase. We show that in the linearized screening approximation the streamline interactions yield Debye-Huckel type corrections to the bulk parameters such as the pressure and densities, but do not alter significantly the large distance behavior of the correlation functions in leading order. The static scalar and charged structure factors are consistent with a plasma of a dyon-anti-dyon liquid with a Coulomb parameter $Γ_{D\bar D}\approx 1$ in the dyon-anti-dyon channel. Heavy quarks are still linearly confined and the large spatial Wilson loops still exhibit area laws in leading order. The t$^\prime$ Hooft loop is shown to be 1 modulo Coulomb corrections.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including classical streamline interactions between dyons and anti-dyons in the SU(2) Yang-Mills confining vacuum yields Debye-Huckel type corrections to bulk parameters, but does not significantly alter the large-distance behavior of correlation functions in leading order, provided the dyon density is sufficiently high to justify the linearized screening approximation.

## Method

The authors construct a grand-partition function for a 4-component dyon-anti-dyon Coulomb liquid in the center-symmetric phase of SU(2) Yang-Mills, combining the Diakonov-Petrov determinantal measure for like dyons with classical streamline interactions between unlike dyons. They treat the ensemble in the linearized screening (Debye-Huckel) approximation, using a numerical fit to the streamline potential from Larsen and Shuryak (2015) with a core cutoff at approximately 1/T. The analysis derives an effective 3-dimensional action and computes bulk thermodynamic properties, structure factors, and Wilson loop behavior in leading order.

## Key numbers

* Coulomb parameter in the dyon-anti-dyon channel: Gamma_{D bar D} approximately 1
* Action per SU(2) caloron at T_c: S_{L+M}(T_c) approximately 7.47, corresponding to alpha_s(T_c) = 0.84
* Action per dyon at T_c: approximately 3.75
* Coulomb asymptotic constant from streamline fit: C_D = 2.46
* Dyon-anti-dyon core size: a_{D bar D} approximately 1/T
* Dyon density scaling: n_D / T^3 proportional to (Lambda/T)^{11/3}

## Context

This work extends the dyon-anti-dyon liquid model of Diakonov and Petrov (2004) by including classical streamline interactions between dyons and anti-dyons, which were numerically studied by Larsen and Shuryak (2015). The authors differ from Diakonov's earlier treatment by arguing that the classical interactions dominate over the one-loop determinantal effects, and that the determinantal measure alone leads to negative eigenvalues for random dyon configurations as noted by Bruckmann and others in lattice studies. The paper enables analytical treatment of the strongly coupled dyonic plasma in the dense regime below T_c, with a follow-up paper planned to address fermionic zero modes and chiral symmetry breaking.
