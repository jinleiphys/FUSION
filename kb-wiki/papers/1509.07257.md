# Towards the understanding of jet shapes and cross sections in heavy ion collisions using soft-collinear effective theory

> We calculate the jet shape and the jet cross section in heavy ion collisions using soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) and its extension with Glauber gluon interactions in the medium (SCET$_{\rm G}$). We use the previously developed framework to systematically resum the jet shape at next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy, and we consistently include the medium modification by incorporating the leading order medium-induced splitting functions. The calculation provides, for the first time, a quantitative understanding of the jet shape modification measurement in lead-lead collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ TeV at the LHC. The inclusive jet suppression is also calculated within the same framework beyond the traditional concept of parton energy loss, and the dependence on the centrality, the jet radius and the jet kinematics is examined. In the end we present predictions for the anticipated jet shape and cross section measurements in lead-lead collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}\approx5.1$ TeV at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first quantitative perturbative calculation of jet shape modification and jet cross section suppression in heavy ion collisions that goes beyond the traditional parton energy loss picture, using soft-collinear effective theory with Glauber gluon interactions (SCET\(_{\rm G}\)) at next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy, but the medium-induced splitting functions are included only at leading order in the opacity expansion and higher-order correlated multiple scatterings are not resummed.

## Method

The authors calculate the integral jet shape \(\Psi(r)\) as the ratio of two jet energy functions in SCET, which describe the average collinear energy inside subcones of size \(r\) and \(R\) respectively, and resum large logarithms \(\ln(r/R)\) via renormalization group evolution between the scales \(\mu_{j_r} \approx E_J \times r\) and \(\mu_{j_R} \approx E_J \times R\). Medium modifications are incorporated by adding leading-order SCET\(_{\rm G}\) medium-induced splitting functions to the vacuum Altarelli-Parisi splitting functions in the jet energy function calculation, and the jet cross section suppression is computed by relating the medium-modified cross section to the cold-nuclear-matter-modified cross section at a shifted transverse momentum \(p_T e^{\epsilon_k}\), where \(\epsilon_k\) is the fractional jet energy loss derived from the medium-induced splitting functions.

## Key numbers

* For Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76\) TeV, the gluon rapidity densities used are \(dN/dy = 2640\) for central collisions and \(dN/dy = 750\) for mid-peripheral collisions.
* For Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} \approx 5.1\) TeV, the gluon rapidity densities used are \(dN/dy = 2750\) for central collisions and \(dN/dy = 800\) for mid-peripheral collisions.
* The jet shape modification ratio is defined as \(M_{\rho}(r) = \rho^{AA}(r) / \rho^{pp}(r)\) and the nuclear modification factor for jets is \(R_{AA} = (d\sigma_{AA}/d\eta dp_T) / (\langle N_{\rm bin} \rangle d\sigma_{pp}/d\eta dp_T)\).

## Context

This work extends the SCET-based resummation framework for jet shapes in proton collisions developed by Chien and collaborators (2014) to heavy ion collisions by incorporating medium effects via the SCET\(_{\rm G}\) formalism of Idilbi and Ovanesyan (2008, 2011). It contrasts with earlier approaches that used the modified leading logarithmic approximation (Seymour, 1997) or the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev formalism in the soft gluon limit (Vitev, 2008, 2009), and it enables precision comparisons with LHC measurements at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76\) TeV and predictions for the upcoming \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} \approx 5.1\) TeV run, with future work needed to include higher-order opacity corrections and event-by-event medium geometry fluctuations.
