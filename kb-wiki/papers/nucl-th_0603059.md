# Nuclear collective motion with a coherent coupling interaction between quadrupole and octupole modes

> A collective Hamiltonian for the rotation-vibration motion of nuclei is considered, in which the axial quadrupole and octupole degrees of freedom are coupled through the centrifugal interaction. The potential of the system depends on the two deformation variables $β_2$ and $β_3$. The system is considered to oscillate between positive and negative $β_3$-values, by rounding an infinite potential core in the $(β_2,β_3)$-plane with $β_2>0$. By assuming a coherent contribution of the quadrupole and octupole oscillation modes in the collective motion, the energy spectrum is derived in an explicit analytic form, providing specific parity shift effects. On this basis several possible ways in the evolution of quadrupole-octupole collectivity are outlined. A particular application of the model to the energy levels and electric transition probabilities in alternating parity spectra of the nuclei $^{150}$Nd, $^{152}$Sm, $^{154}$Gd and $^{156}$Dy is presented.

## Key claim

The paper derives an explicit analytic form for the energy spectrum of quadrupole-octupole collective motion by assuming a coherent interplay between the two modes, characterized by equal angular frequencies \(\omega_2 = \omega_3\), which allows the potential to depend only on an effective deformation variable \(\eta\) rather than on both \(\beta_2\) and \(\beta_3\) separately. The model specifically applies to nuclei where the quadrupole and octupole oscillations contribute on the same footing, with the condition \(d_2/C_2 = d_3/C_3\) and \(d_2/B_2 = d_3/B_3\) required for analytic solvability.

## Method

The authors construct a collective Hamiltonian with quadrupole (\(\beta_2\)) and octupole (\(\beta_3\)) axial deformation variables coupled through a centrifugal interaction term \(X(I)/(d_2\beta_2^2 + d_3\beta_3^2)\). They transform to polar variables \(\eta\) and \(\phi\) and impose conditions that make the potential depend only on \(\eta\), allowing separation of variables. The Schrödinger equation is solved analytically for three potential cases: a harmonic oscillator with centrifugal term (Case I), potentials with fixed minima (Cases II.A and II.B), and an infinite square well (Case III). The \(\phi\)-equation yields parity-dependent quantum numbers \(k\) that produce alternating parity spectra.

## Key numbers

- For the alternating parity spectra of \(^{150}\)Nd, \(^{152}\)Sm, \(^{154}\)Gd, and \(^{156}\)Dy, the model uses Eq. (spect1a) with \(n=0\) and \(X(I) = \frac{1}{2}[d_0 + I(I+1)]\), where parameters \(\omega\), \(b\), and \(d_0\) are adjusted by least squares minimization.
- The staggering effect in Case I is characterized by \(\Delta k^2 = k_{(-)}^2 - k_{(+)}^2 = 3\), where \(k_{(+)} = 1\) for even \(I\) and \(k_{(-)} = 2\) for odd \(I\).
- In Case I, the staggering amplitude decreases nearly linearly with angular momentum because the term \(k^2/(2\sqrt{\widetilde{X}(I)})\) decreases with \(I\).
- In Cases II.A and II.B, the staggering amplitude is constant at higher angular momenta because the staggering depends only on the \(I\)-independent term \(k^2/2\).

## Context

This work extends earlier one-dimensional octupole oscillator models (Krappe 1969, Leander) and the double-well potential approaches of Jolos (1993, 1994) and Drenska and Drenska (1993, 1995) by allowing the quadrupole coordinate to vary explicitly rather than being frozen. The model also relates to the X(5) critical point symmetry (Iachello 2001) studied in the same \(N=90\) isotones \(^{150}\)Nd, \(^{152}\)Sm, \(^{154}\)Gd, and \(^{156}\)Dy by Clark and others, but focuses on the common quadrupole-octupole collective properties rather than the quadrupole phase transition. The analytic solutions enable systematic study of parity shift effects and staggering patterns in alternating parity bands, with potential application to other nuclear regions exhibiting quadrupole-octupole collectivity.
