# Resonances in positron scattering on a supercritical nucleus and spontaneous production of $e^{+}e^{-}$ pairs

> We re-examine the physics of supercritical nuclei, specially focusing on the scattering phase $δ_{\varkappa}$ and its dependence on the energy $\varepsilon$ of the diving electronic level, for which we give both exact and approximate formulas. The Coulomb potential $Zα/r$ is rounded to the constant $Zα/R$ for $r < R$. We confirm the resonant behavior of $δ_{\varkappa}$ that we investigate in details. In addition to solving the Dirac equation for an electron, we solve it for a positron, in the field of the same nucleus. This clarifies the interpretation of the resonances. Our results are compared with claims made in previous works.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that resonances appear in positron scattering on a supercritical nucleus (Z > Z_cr), but clarifies that these resonances correspond to poles of the S-matrix located above the left cut on the unphysical sheet of the energy plane, with energy epsilon = -xi + i/2 gamma (xi > m, gamma > 0), and that the sign reversal proposed in prior work (Kuleshov) is consistent with the hole interpretation of the Dirac sea.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation for both electrons and positrons in the Coulomb field of a nucleus with Z > Z_cr, using a rectangular cutoff potential (constant Z alpha / R inside the nucleus, Coulombic outside). They match the interior and exterior wave functions at the nuclear radius R to compute the scattering phase delta_kappa and locate S-matrix poles, obtaining both exact numerical results and approximate analytic formulas based on small-R expansions.

## Key numbers

- For Z = 232 and kappa = -1, the exact scattering phase delta_{-1} goes through pi/2 at positron energy epsilon_p / m approximately 5.06; the approximate formula gives epsilon_p / m approximately 4.88.
- For Z = 186, the approximate imaginary part of the resonance energy differs from the exact value by about 15%.
- The ground state energy enters the lower continuum at Z = 178 (though the imaginary part is much smaller than the calculation accuracy).
- For Z = 232, the approximate formula gives about 10% accuracy for Im(epsilon) compared to the exact result.
- The nuclear radius used is R = 0.031 / m (where m is the electron mass).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Kuleshov (2017) on supercritical nuclei by providing exact numerical solutions for the S-matrix poles and scattering phases, rather than relying solely on the small-R approximation. It also resolves an interpretational puzzle by solving the Dirac equation for positrons, confirming that the resonances correspond to holes in the lower continuum (positrons) rather than electron states. The results contradict the claim in some prior work (e.g., Kuleshov) that spontaneous e+e- pair production does not occur; the authors instead support the standard picture from Greiner (1969), Popov (1970), and others that such production does occur, with characteristic time 1/gamma.
