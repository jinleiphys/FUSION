# The QCD phase diagram at nonzero quark density

> We determine the phase diagram of QCD on the μ-T plane for small to moderate chemical potentials. Two transition lines are defined with two quantities, the chiral condensate and the strange quark number susceptibility. The calculations are carried out on N_t =6,8 and 10 lattices generated with a Symanzik improved gauge and stout-link improved 2+1 flavor staggered fermion action using physical quark masses. After carrying out the continuum extrapolation we find that both quantities result in a similar curvature of the transition line. Furthermore, our results indicate that in leading order the width of the transition region remains essentially the same as the chemical potential is increased.

## Key claim

The curvature of the QCD transition line at small to moderate baryon chemical potential is determined from two observables (the chiral condensate and the strange quark number susceptibility) and yields consistent results, but the paper's abstract omits that the two observables give numerically different curvatures (0.0089 vs. 0.0066) which are only consistent within their statistical uncertainties, and that the leading-order result indicates a slight _weakening_ of the transition (width increase of about 3%) rather than a strengthening that would point toward a critical endpoint.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with a Symanzik improved gauge action and stout-link improved 2+1 flavor staggered fermions at physical quark masses on N_t = 6, 8, and 10 lattices. They computed the second derivatives of the renormalized chiral condensate and the normalized strange quark number susceptibility with respect to the light quark chemical potential using a Taylor expansion technique, then extracted the curvature parameter κ(T) from the ratio of the μ-derivative to the temperature derivative of each observable. A combined fit including continuum extrapolation (1/N_t^2 terms) and a quadratic expansion in reduced temperature t = (T-T_c)/T_c yielded the continuum curvature at T_c and the leading change in transition width.

## Key numbers

* Curvature from strange quark number susceptibility: κ^(χ_s/T²) = 0.0089(14)
* Curvature from renormalized chiral condensate: κ^(ψ̄ψ_r) = 0.0066(20)
* Relative change in transition width from strange susceptibility: ΔW/W = 0.033(16)
* Relative change in transition width from chiral condensate: ΔW/W = 0.030(18)
* Transition temperature at μ=0 (T_c) is approximately 155 MeV (from the β=3.555, N_t=6 lattice point near the pseudocritical temperature)
* Validity range estimated as μ_{u,d} ≈ T_c, corresponding to baryon chemical potential of about 500 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies at zero chemical potential (Aoki 2006, Borsanyi 2010) and provides the first continuum-extrapolated curvature determination with physical quark masses. The results are consistent with previous leading-order indications of a weakening transition from Lee-Yang zero studies (Fodor 2004) and from critical surface studies in 3-flavor (deForcrand 2008) and 2+1-flavor (Moscicki 2009) QCD. The finding that the transition width increases only slightly (a few percent up to μ ≈ T_c) implies that a critical endpoint, if it exists, would require non-monotonic behavior at higher chemical potentials beyond the reach of this Taylor expansion. The results enable comparison with the experimental freeze-out curve from heavy-ion collisions (Cleymans 1998) and provide a benchmark for future full reweighting studies.
