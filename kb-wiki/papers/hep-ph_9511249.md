# Baryons as Solitons in Effective Chiral Field Theories

> This lecture comprises some recent developments concerning the description of baryons as topological solitons in effective chiral meson theories. In the first part one-loop corrections to the classical tree approximation are discussed. This involves renormalization of low-energy coupling constants and evaluation of the finite next-to-leading-order terms in the $1/N_c$ expansion. Recent results for various nucleon observables are presented. In the second part electromagnetic nucleon form factors (FFs) with relativistic corrections are evaluated in a chiral soliton model including vector mesons. The magnetic FF $G_M^p$ is shown to agree well with new SLAC data for spacelike $Q^2$ up to 30(GeV/c)$^2$ if superconvergence is enforced. The electric FF $G_E^p$ is dominated by a zero in the few (GeV/c)$^2$ region. The third part describes how to extract the strong $πNN$ form factor from chiral soliton models, taking due care of the local metric created by the presence of the soliton. When used in a one-boson-exchange model for the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction, deuteron properties and phase parameters of NN scattering are reproduced as well as in conventional NN models that apply a hard monopole form factor at the $πNN$ vertex.

# Baryons as Solitons in Effective Chiral Field Theories

## Key claim

One-loop corrections to the classical soliton approximation, when calculated to all chiral orders (not truncated by chiral power counting), can yield baryon observables that agree well with experiment, but the axial coupling constant $g_A$ remains problematic because it requires two orders down in the $1/N_c$ expansion to satisfy the Adler-Weisberger relation. The magnetic form factor $G_M^p$ can be made to agree with SLAC data up to 30 (GeV/c)$^2$ only if superconvergence is enforced by adjusting the kinematic mass parameter $M$ to match the zero of the nonrelativistic form factor.

## Method

One-loop corrections to the classical soliton mass are computed by evaluating the Casimir energy $E_{cas} = \frac{1}{2}(tr\,h_S - tr\,h_\infty)$, where $h_S$ governs fluctuations around the hedgehog soliton and $h_\infty$ is the free-pion kinetic operator; the divergent terms ($k=0,1,2$ in the Taylor expansion of $\sqrt{h_S^2}$) are renormalized using counterterms that absorb the infinities into scale-dependent coupling constants $c_i(\mu)$ in the effective lagrangian. Electromagnetic form factors are evaluated in a chiral soliton model including $\rho$ and $\omega$ vector mesons, with relativistic corrections implemented via Lorentz boosts from the soliton rest frame to the Breit frame. The strong $\pi NN$ form factor is extracted by removing the local metric $M_{ab}$ from the kinetic part of the lagrangian through a field redefinition $\tilde{\pi}_a = \sqrt{M_T}\,\pi_a$, then taking the Fourier transform of the classical soliton profile.

## Key numbers

- Baryon mass: classical ${\cal O}(N_c)$ result 1628 MeV, one-loop ${\cal O}(1)$ correction -682 MeV, total 946 MeV (experimental 939 MeV)
- $\pi$-N sigma term: classical 54 MeV, one-loop -21 MeV, total 33 MeV (experimental 45 MeV)
- Isovector magnetic moment: classical 1.62, one-loop +0.62, total 2.24 (experimental 2.35)
- Isovector magnetic square radius: classical 0.86 fm$^2$, one-loop -0.14 fm$^2$, total 0.72 fm$^2$ (experimental 0.73 fm$^2$)
- Proton polarizability: classical $17.8 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^3$, one-loop $-8.0 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^3$, total $9.8 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^3$ (experimental $9.5 \times 10^{-3}$ fm$^3$)
- Axial coupling constant $g_A$: classical 0.90, one-loop -0.25, total 0.65 (experimental 1.25)
- Proton electric radius: $r_{Ep} = 0.93$ fm (experimental 0.86 fm)
- Neutron electric square radius: $<r_E^2>_n = -0.158$ fm$^2$ (experimental $-0.114 \pm 0.003$ fm$^2$)
- Proton magnetic moment $\mu_p = 2.90$, neutron magnetic moment $\mu_n = -2.49$ (experimental $\mu_n = -1.91$)
- Skyrme parameter $e = 4.25$ used for one-loop calculations; for vector meson model $e = 12$ and $g_\omega = 4$
- Ratio $g_\omega/g_0 = 0.58$ for isoscalar form factors

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model approach of Skyrme (1961) and Witten (1983) by computing one-loop ($1/N_c$) corrections to baryon observables, going beyond earlier tree-level calculations. The renormalization procedure follows the chiral perturbation theory framework of Weinberg (1979) and Gasser and Leutwyler, but the author emphasizes that in the soliton sector the chiral gradients are of order $m_\rho \sim 770$ MeV, so loop contributions cannot be truncated by chiral order as in the meson sector. The electromagnetic form factor results improve upon earlier work by Ji (1991) and Meier (1993) by including vector mesons and enforcing superconvergence, and the strong $\pi NN$ form factor extraction corrects earlier attempts by Cohen (1986) and Kaiser, Meissner, and Weise (1987) that neglected the local metric. The results enable comparison with conventional one-boson-exchange models of the nucleon-nucleon interaction and provide predictions for future electron accelerator measurements in the few (GeV/c)$^2$ region.
