# The $p(d,d^{\prime})$ Reaction and the $σNN^{\ast}(1440)$ Coupling Constant

> We make use of a $NN \to NN^*(1440)$ transition potential derived from a quark-model in a parameter-free way, to study the Roper excitation diagram contributing to the reaction $p(d,d^{\prime})$. We also determine the $πNN^*(1440)$ and $σNN^*(1440)$ coupling constants.

## Key claim
The authors use a quark-model-derived $NN \to NN^*(1440)$ transition potential, with no free parameters once the model is fixed from $NN$ data, to study the Roper excitation in $p(d,d^{\prime})$ scattering and to extract the $\pi NN^*(1440)$ and $\sigma NN^*(1440)$ coupling constants; however, the calculated cross section underestimates the experimental data, and the agreement improves only if a smaller value for the $N^*(1440)$ width is chosen.

## Method
The $NN \to NN^*(1440)$ transition potential was constructed in the Born-Oppenheimer approximation by sandwiching a quark-quark potential (containing confinement, one-gluon-exchange, pion-exchange, and sigma-exchange terms) between $NN$ and $NN^*(1440)$ states written in harmonic-oscillator quark wave functions. This potential was then used to compute the cross section for the target Roper excitation process in $p(d,d^{\prime})$ scattering, using the amplitude formalism of Hirenzaki et al. (2000) that involves the deuteron form factor and the $N^*(1440)$ propagator. The $\pi NN^*(1440)$ and $\sigma NN^*(1440)$ coupling constants were extracted by fitting the asymptotic behavior (5 to 9 fm) of the quark-model central potentials to one-pion-exchange and one-sigma-exchange baryonic forms.

## Key numbers
- $g_{\pi NN^*(1440)}/\sqrt{4\pi} = -0.94$ (absolute value only well-defined; sign depends on choice of $N^*(1440)$ wave function phase).
- Ratio $|g_{\pi NN^*(1440)} / g_{\pi NN}| = 0.25$, obtained within the same quark-model approximation.
- Ratio $|g_{\sigma NN^*(1440)} / g_{\sigma NN}| = 0.47$, agreeing with the experimental result of 0.48 from Hirenzaki et al. (1996).
- Model prediction: $g_{\pi NN^*(1440)} / g_{\pi NN} = 0.53 \times (g_{\sigma NN^*(1440)} / g_{\sigma NN})$.
- The $NN \to NN^*(1440)$ transition potential is repulsive at short range ($0 < R < 0.6$ fm), attractive at intermediate range ($0.6 < R < 1.4$ fm), and asymptotically repulsive.

## Context
This work extends the quark-model approach previously applied to the $NN$ system and to transition potentials involving the $\Delta$ resonance (Valcarce et al., 1994; Entem et al., 2000) to the $N^*(1440)$ Roper resonance. It directly addresses the $p(d,d^{\prime})$ reaction studied by Hirenzaki et al. (2000), where the $\Delta$ contribution was subtracted theoretically, and the $p(\alpha,\alpha^{\prime})$ experiment at Saclay (Morsch et al., 1992). The extracted coupling constant ratios provide a parameter-free prediction that can be tested in future calculations of reactions involving the Roper resonance, such as $p(\alpha,\alpha^{\prime})$ or heavy-ion collisions, and the ratio $g_{\pi NN^*(1440)}/g_{\sigma NN^*(1440)}$ is presented as an exportable model prediction.
