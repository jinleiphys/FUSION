# Structure and reactions of 11Be: many-body basis for single-neutron halo

> The exotic nucleus 11Be has been extensively studied and much experimental information is available on the structure of this system. Treating, within the framework of empirically renormalised nuclear field theory in both configuration and 3D-space, the mixing of bound and continuum single-particle states through the coupling to collective particle-hole (p,h) and pairing vibrations of the 10Be core, as well as Pauli principle acting not only between the particles explicitly considered and those participating in the collective states, but also between fermions involved in two-phonon virtual states it is possible, for the first time, to simultaneously and quantitatively account for the energies of the 1/2+,1/2- low-lying states, the centroid and line shape of the 5/2+ resonance, the one-nucleon stripping and pickup absolute differential cross sections involving 11Be as either target or residual nucleus, and the dipole transitions connecting the 1/2+ and 1/2- parity inverted levels as well as the charge radius, thus providing a unified and exhaustive characterisation of the many-body effects which are at the basis of this paradigmatic one-neutron halo system.

## Key claim

By treating the mixing of bound and continuum single-particle states through coupling to collective particle-hole and pairing vibrations of the 10Be core, including Pauli principle corrections between particles in two-phonon virtual states, the authors achieve for the first time a simultaneous and quantitative account of the energies, resonance line shapes, one-nucleon transfer cross sections, dipole transitions, and charge radius of 11Be, but this success depends on using empirically renormalized nuclear field theory (NFT)ren where the intermediate states reproduce experimental input.

## Method

The authors applied empirically renormalized nuclear field theory (NFT)ren in both configuration space and three-dimensional space, solving the Dyson equation through iterative diagonalization of the particle-vibration coupling Hamiltonian in a space of single-particle states and particle-phonon states up to a cutoff energy of 25 MeV with continuum discretization in a box of radius 30 fm. They included quadrupole and octupole particle-hole vibrations and monopole pair removal vibrations of the 10Be core, calculated in QRPA and RPA respectively, and incorporated Pauli principle corrections between the valence neutron and particles participating in the collective states as well as between fermions in two-phonon virtual states. The bare single-particle states were determined from a Saxon-Woods potential with a spatially dependent effective mass varying from 0.7m at the center to 0.91m far from the nucleus.

## Key numbers

* Parity inversion between 1/2+ and 1/2- states: bare energy difference epsilon_{1/2+} - epsilon_{1/2-} = +3.2 MeV becomes -0.3 MeV after NFT renormalization, a factor of ten reduction
* Energy difference between 5/2+ and 1/2- states: bare epsilon_{5/2+} - epsilon_{1/2-} = +9.5 MeV becomes +1.5 MeV after NFT renormalization, a factor of six reduction
* 5/2+ resonance centroid at E = 1.23 MeV with width of 160 keV calculated from elastic scattering phase shifts
* Amplitude of the (d_{5/2} ⊗ 2+)_{5/2+} component in the 5/2+ state: sqrt(0.34)
* Amplitude of the (d_{5/2} ⊗ 2+)_{1/2+} component in the 1/2+ ground state: sqrt(0.17)
* Particle-vibration coupling matrix element <(5/2+ ⊗ 2+)_{5/2+}|H_c|d_{5/2}> ≈ -4.6 MeV
* B(E1; 1/2- → 1/2+) = 0.11 e^2 fm^2 from NFT calculation, compared to experimental value 0.102 ± 0.002 e^2 fm^2
* Separation energy approximation gives B(E1) = 0.29 e^2 fm^2 with e_eff = 4/11

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of 11Be including shell model calculations by Talmi, particle-vibration coupling approaches by Otsuka and Gori, deformed mean field descriptions by Hamamoto, antisymmetrized molecular dynamics by Kanada, and ab initio approaches by Calci, none of which had simultaneously reproduced the full set of structure and reaction observables. The unified description of both structure and reactions using (NFT)ren enables quantitative predictions for one-nucleon transfer reactions such as 10Be(d,p)11Be and 11Be(p,d)10Be(2+), and demonstrates that the 5/2+ resonance plays an essential role in the parity inversion mechanism through its coupling to the s_{1/2} state via the quadrupole vibration of the core.
