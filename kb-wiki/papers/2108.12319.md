# QCD Factorization and Quantum Mechanics

> It is unusual to find QCD factorization explained in the language of quantum information science. However, we will discuss how the issue of factorization and its breaking in high-energy QCD processes relates to phenomena like decoherence and entanglement. We will elaborate with several examples and explain them in terms familiar from basic quantum mechanics and quantum information science.

## Key claim

QCD factorization theorems rely on decoherence between processes at widely separated spacetime scales, and departures from the simplest factorized pictures typically involve some form of failure of decoherence; the paper argues that the semi-classical parton model picture is only approximate and breaks down for observables with greater sensitivity to final-state details, such as those involving intrinsic transverse momentum or multiple hadrons.

## Method

The authors review the standard parton model description of deep inelastic scattering (DIS) and semi-inclusive DIS (SIDIS), presenting the factorization formulas as products of hard scattering coefficients, parton distribution functions, and fragmentation functions. They then examine how quantum mechanical effects such as entanglement (the Collins effect in semi-inclusive electron-positron annihilation) and gauge field interactions (the Basu-Ramalho-Sterman classical analogy for Drell-Yan scattering) modify or threaten the factorized picture. Finally, they analyze a specific hadron-hadron collision process producing a back-to-back hadron pair and show, using color flow arguments in Feynman diagrams, why transverse momentum dependent factorization fails for that case.

## Key numbers

* The electric field from an incoming quark in the Drell-Yan process scales as \(E^z(x^z \neq \beta t) \sim 1/s^2\) in the high-energy limit, showing that pre-collision interactions are power-suppressed.
* The vector potential of a classical abelian gauge field from a point-like quark takes the form \(\lim_{s \to \infty} A^\mu(P) = q \partial^\mu \ln (\beta t - x^z)\), a pure gauge except at the instant of passage.

## Context

This work extends the pedagogical tradition of Feynman (1972) and the textbook treatment of Ellis, Stirling, and Webber by explicitly connecting QCD factorization to concepts from quantum information science such as decoherence and entanglement. It contrasts the successful factorization for inclusive DIS, SIDIS, and Drell-Yan with the failure of transverse momentum dependent factorization for hadron-hadron collisions producing back-to-back hadron pairs, as analyzed by Bomhof (2006) and Rogers (2010). The paper enables future tests of Bell inequalities in high-energy collisions, citing recent proposals by Barr (2021) and Takubo (2021).
