# $^{235}$U(n, f) Independent Fission Product Yield and Isomeric Ratio Calculated with the Statistical Hauser-Feshbach Theory

> We have developed a Hauser-Feshbach fission fragment decay model, HF$^3$D, which can be applied to the statistical decay of more than 500 primary fission fragment pairs (1,000 nuclides) produced by the neutron induced fission of $^{235}$U. The fission fragment yield $Y(A)$ and the total kinetic energy TKE are model inputs, and we estimate them from available experimental data for the $^{235}$U(n$\rm_{th}$,f) system. The model parameters in the statistical decay calculation are adjusted to reproduce some fission observables, such as the neutron emission multiplicity $\overlineν$, its distribution $P(ν)$, and the mass dependence $\overlineν(A)$. The calculated fission product yield and isomeric ratio are compared with experimental data. We show that the calculated independent fission product yield $Y_I(A)$ at the thermal energy reproduces the experimental data well, while the calculated isomeric ratios tend to be lower than the Madland-England model prediction. The model is extended to higher incident neutron energies up to the second chance fission threshold. We demonstrate for the first time that most of the isomeric ratios stay constant, although the production of isomeric state itself changes as the incident energy increases.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that their deterministic Hauser-Feshbach fission fragment decay model (HF$^3$D) reproduces independent fission product yields for thermal neutron-induced fission of $^{235}$U well, but the calculated isomeric ratios tend to be lower than those predicted by the Madland-England model, and most isomeric ratios remain constant with increasing incident neutron energy up to the second chance fission threshold.

## Method

The HF$^3$D model applies statistical Hauser-Feshbach theory to the decay of over 500 primary fission fragment pairs (1000 nuclides) from $^{235}$U(n,f), using a deterministic integration over fragment yield, excitation energy, spin, and parity distributions rather than Monte Carlo sampling. The primary fission fragment mass distribution is represented by five Gaussians fitted to experimental data, charge distribution uses Wahl's $Z_p$ model, and total kinetic energy is parameterized from experimental data; model parameters (anisothermal parameter $R_T$ and spin scaling factor $f$) are adjusted to reproduce observables including average neutron multiplicity $\overline{\nu}$, its distribution $P(\nu)$, and mass-dependent neutron multiplicity $\overline{\nu}(A)$.

## Key numbers

- Average prompt neutron multiplicity $\overline{\nu}$ at thermal energy: 2.42 (from JENDL-4 evaluation), reproduced with scaling factor $f = 3.0$
- Anisothermal parameter $R_T = 1.2$ adopted as best fit to experimental $\overline{\nu}(A)$ data
- Five-Gaussian parameters for primary mass distribution at thermal: $F_1 = 0.7846$, $\Delta_1 = 23.00$, $\sigma_1 = 4.828$, $F_2 = 0.2032$, $\Delta_2 = 15.63$, $\sigma_2 = 2.728$, $F_3 = 0.002916$, $\sigma_3 = 8.6$
- TKE fitting parameters: $p_1 = 335.3$ MeV, $p_2 = 1.174$ MeV, $p_3 = 0.1876$, $p_4 = 69.08$; average TKE at thermal = 170.5 MeV
- Energy-dependent TKE parameters: $q_1 = 171.2$ MeV, $q_2 = 0.1800$, $q_3 = 0.0043$, $q_4 = 0.3230$ MeV
- C/E (Calculated/Experimental) for independent yields ranges from 0.10 ($^{116}$Ag) to 6.12 ($^{77}$Ga), average $1.03 \pm 0.81$
- Deviation of calculated $\overline{\nu}$ from evaluated values at 1 MeV: 2.1% (JENDL-4) and 2.5% (ENDF/B-VIII.0)

## Context

This work extends the deterministic Hauser-Feshbach approach previously developed by Kawano (2013) by eliminating Monte Carlo sampling and applying it to over 500 fragment pairs for $^{235}$U(n,f). The model provides an alternative to the Madland-England (1977) isomeric ratio model used in evaluated libraries such as ENDF/B-VI (England and Rider, 1994) and JENDL/FPY-2011, and enables calculation of fission product yields and isomeric ratios at multiple incident neutron energies up to the second chance fission threshold, addressing the limitation that current evaluated files contain only three energy points (thermal, fast, 14 MeV).
