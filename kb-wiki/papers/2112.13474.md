# Fifty ways to build a deuteron: a variational calculation of two-nucleon systems

> A variational study of two-nucleon systems with lattice quantum chromodynamics is performed using a wide range of interpolating operators: dibaryon operators built from products of momentum-projected nucleons, hexaquark operators built from six spatially localized quarks, and quasi-local operators inspired by two-nucleon bound-state wavefunctions in nuclear effective field theories. Correlation-function matrices involving products of these operators are constructed by computing timeslice-to-all quark propagators with sparsening techniques. Comparisons between results obtained using the same gauge-field ensemble but different interpolating-operator sets demonstrate that interpolating-operator dependence can lead to significant effects on the two-nucleon energy spectra obtained using both variational and non-variational methods.

## Key claim

Variational methods using large interpolating-operator sets can reliably determine two-nucleon energy spectra, but removing even a single operator that has dominant overlap with a particular state leads to that state being "missing" from the results, demonstrating that overlap quality rather than operator quantity is essential for accurate energy-level determinations.

## Method

The study constructs correlation-function matrices up to 42×42 in the deuteron channel using three classes of interpolating operators: dibaryon operators built from momentum-projected nucleons, hexaquark operators from six spatially localized quarks, and quasi-local operators with exponential wavefunctions inspired by nuclear effective field theory. Sparsening techniques with S=4 are used to compute timeslice-to-all quark propagators efficiently, and generalized eigenvalue problems (GEVP) are solved to extract energy levels from these correlation-function matrices on 167 gauge-field configurations with mπ = 806 MeV and a = 0.1453(16) fm.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass: E0 = 1.2031(30) in lattice units, consistent with previous higher-precision determination of M_N = 1.20396(83) from Wagman (2017) on the same gauge-field ensemble
- Ground-state finite-volume energy shift in the dineutron (I=1, A1+) channel using the full 16-operator set: ΔE0 = -0.0037(18) in lattice units
- Ground-state finite-volume energy shift from asymmetric correlation functions (hexaquark source, s=0 dibaryon sink): -0.0091(75), compared to previous results of -0.0111(21) from Beane (2012), -0.0127(21) from Berkowitz (2015), and -0.0137(17) from Wagman (2017)
- Quasi-local operator exponential localization scales: κ = {0.035, 0.070, 0.14} in lattice units, corresponding to binding energies of {0.0010, 0.0041, 0.016} in lattice units or {1.4, 5.5, 22} MeV
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.1453(16) fm, spatial extent L = 32, pion mass mπ = 806 MeV

## Context

This work extends variational LQCD studies of two-nucleon systems beyond the 2×2 and 3×3 correlation-function matrices used by Francis (2018), Hörz (2020), and Green (2021), enabling direct comparisons with asymmetric correlation-function results from Beane (2012), Berkowitz (2015), and Wagman (2017) on the same gauge-field ensemble. The demonstration that missing interpolating operators lead to missing energy levels mirrors similar findings in meson and baryon spectroscopy by Dudek (2012) and Wilson (2015), and establishes that future multi-baryon LQCD calculations must include a wide range of physically motivated interpolating operators to achieve fully quantified systematic uncertainties.
