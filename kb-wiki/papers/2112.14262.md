# Digital Quantum Simulation of the Schwinger Model and Symmetry Protection with Trapped Ions

> Tracking the dynamics of physical systems in real time is a prime application of digital quantum computers. Using a trapped-ion system with up to six qubits, we simulate the real-time dynamics of a lattice gauge theory in 1+1 dimensions, i.e., the lattice Schwinger model, and demonstrate non-perturbative effects such as pair creation for times much longer than previously accessible. We study the gate requirement of two formulations of the model using the Suzuki-Trotter product formula, as well as the trade-off between errors from the ordering of the Hamiltonian terms, the Trotter step size, and experimental imperfections. To mitigate experimental errors, a recent symmetry-protection protocol for suppressing coherent errors and a symmetry-inspired post-selection scheme are applied. This work demonstrates the integrated theoretical, algorithmic, and experimental approach that is essential for efficient simulation of lattice gauge theories and other complex physical systems.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that digital quantum simulation of the Schwinger model on a trapped-ion system can access non-perturbative dynamics such as pair creation for times much longer than previously achievable, but only when using the odd-even term ordering in the Trotter decomposition which preserves the global symmetry, as the symmetry-protection protocol does not consistently improve accuracy over the odd-even ordering for the observables studied.

## Method

The authors map the lattice Schwinger model in 1+1 dimensions to a purely fermionic spin Hamiltonian via the Jordan-Wigner transformation and Kogut-Susskind staggered formulation, eliminating gauge bosons through Gauss's law. They implement the time evolution using first-order Suzuki-Trotter product formulas on a trapped-ion system with up to six qubits, comparing odd-even and XYZ term orderings, and apply both a symmetry-protection protocol with random rotations and a symmetry-inspired post-selection scheme to mitigate errors.

## Key numbers

- Up to six qubits used in a trapped-ion chain of up to nine $^{171}\text{Yb}^+$ ions
- Simulation parameters: $\mu = 0.1$ and $x = 0.6$ for the dimensionless Hamiltonian
- Trotter step size $\delta t = 1$ used for the four-site simulations comparing symmetry protection methods
- Empirical gate complexity scaling of $\mathcal{O}(N^{4.2})$ for the second-order product formula, compared to the commutator bound of $\mathcal{O}(N^{6.2})$ and the exact commutator bound of $\mathcal{O}(N^{4.6})$
- Error tolerance $\epsilon = 0.01$ used for the gate complexity scaling analysis with simulation time $t = N$

## Context

This work extends the first digital quantum simulation of the Schwinger model by Martinez et al. (2016) on trapped ions, addressing larger systems (up to six sites) and longer evolution times. It compares the purely fermionic formulation against the fermion-boson formulation of Shaw et al. (2020), finding comparable empirical gate complexity despite worse theoretical bounds, and implements the symmetry-protection protocol of Tran et al. (2020) experimentally for the first time. The results enable future simulations of lattice gauge theories with improved error mitigation strategies and inform the choice of Hamiltonian formulation for near-term quantum devices.
