# Some Field Theoretic Issues Regarding the Chiral Magnetic Effect

> In this paper, we shall address some field theoretic issues regarding the chiral magnetic effect. The general structure of the magnetic current consistent with the electromagnetic gauge invariance is obtained and the impact of the infrared divergence is examined. Some subtleties on the relation between the chiral magnetic effect and the axial anomaly are clarified through a careful examination of the infrared limit of the relevant thermal diagrams.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the robustness of the classical chiral magnetic effect formula depends on the existence of infrared limits of energies and momenta, which is not always guaranteed; the authors identify exceptions where the chiral magnetic current deviates from the classical expression due to massless poles at zero temperature and chemical potential, or due to noncommutativity between zero momentum and zero energy limits at nonzero temperature or chemical potential.

## Method

The authors analyze the chiral magnetic effect via the current-current correlator and the photon self-energy tensor, using the general structure of the chiral magnetic current consistent with electromagnetic gauge invariance, Bose symmetry, and rotation symmetry. They perform one-loop calculations of the triangle diagram and photon self-energy using Pauli-Villars regularization, examining the infrared limits of dynamical form factors C0, C1, and C2, and employ the closed time path (CTP) formalism to study the subtlety of different orders of limits at nonzero temperature and chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The classical expression for the chiral magnetic current per unit charge is j = (e^2 / (2π^2)) μ5 B
- In the static limit (q → 0, ω = 0) at nonzero T and/or μ, the one-loop form factor F1(q,0) yields lim_{q→0} lim_{ω→0} F1(q,ω) = 0
- For the order of limits ω → 0 then q → 0 in the massless limit, lim_{ω→0} lim_{q→0} F1(q,ω) = 2/3, reducing the one-loop chiral magnetic current to one third of the classical magnitude
- At T = μ = 0, the form factor C2(0,0,0;ω) = 1/(3ω) as ω → 0, and lim_{ω→0} lim_{q→0} F(Q) = 2/3
- At T = μ = 0 with the covariant limit, the one-loop form factors give C1(q^2,q^2,-q^2;ω) = 1/(2(q^2 - ω^2)) and C2(q^2,q^2,-q^2;ω) = -ω/(2(q^2 - ω^2)), yielding F(0) = 1 and zero chiral magnetic current

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) proposed by Kharzeev (2007) and Fukushima (2008), and addresses the critique by Rubakov (2010) that the naive axial charge is not conserved due to the axial anomaly and that gauge invariance prevents a nonzero CME from the grand canonical ensemble defined with the naive axial charge. The authors clarify that the validity of Rubakov's statement depends on the existence of infrared limits, and that the noncommutativity of limits at nonzero temperature or chemical potential is a common feature of thermal field theories, with implications for the robustness of CME in relativistic heavy ion collision phenomenology at RHIC.
