# Update of the phase-shift analysis of the low-energy $πN$ data

> This paper presents the results of an updated phase-shift analysis of the low-energy (pion laboratory kinetic energy $T \leq 100$ MeV) pion-nucleon data; this solution will be referred to as `ZRH20'.

## Key claim

This paper presents an updated phase-shift analysis (ZRH20) of low-energy pion-nucleon data (pion kinetic energy ≤ 100 MeV), which resolves the long-standing discrepancy between the π⁻p s-wave scattering length extracted from pionic hydrogen measurements and the value obtained from extrapolation of the ETH model to threshold, but other discrepancies persist that may indicate isospin invariance violation in the hadronic part of the πN interaction.

## Method

The authors performed phase-shift analyses using two complementary approaches: (1) a phenomenological model with low-energy parameterizations of the s- and p-wave K-matrix elements (seven parameters per isospin channel) for outlier identification, and (2) the ETH model, a hadronic model based on t-channel f₀(500) and ρ(770) exchange graphs plus s- and u-channel N and Δ(1232) contributions, with additional contributions from higher baryon resonances and mesons below 2 GeV. The Arndt-Roper minimisation function was used to allow controlled rescaling of datasets by their normalisation uncertainties, and fits were performed separately to the elastic-scattering databases (π⁺p and π⁻p combined) and to the charge-exchange database (π⁺p and π⁻p CX combined).

## Key numbers

- Charged-pion coupling constant: f_c² = 0.0753(15), obtained from fits of the ETH model to the elastic-scattering databases
- The analysis is restricted to pion laboratory kinetic energy T ≤ 100 MeV
- The f₀(500) mass was treated as m_σ = 497^{+28}_{-33} MeV, with fits performed at one hundred randomly selected values
- The ETH model uses seven free parameters (G_σ, κ_σ, G_ρ, κ_ρ, g_{πNN}, g_{πNΔ}, Z) at fixed x=0 (pure pseudovector πN coupling)
- The phenomenological model uses seven parameters for I=3/2 and seven for I=1/2, with K-matrix expansions up to O(ε²)

## Context

This work extends the authors' long-running phase-shift analysis programme (previously ZRH19 and earlier solutions) by including two estimates of the π⁻p s-wave scattering length from pionic hydrogen measurements (Schroeder 2001, Hennebach 2014), which resolved the threshold discrepancy noted in prior analyses. The ETH model was made more complete by adding t-channel contributions from f₀(980), f₀(1500), f₀(1710), and ρ(1700) mesons. The analysis enables Monte-Carlo predictions for low-energy πN constants and phase shifts, and provides predictions for elastic-scattering differential cross sections at three pion momenta for the MUSE Collaboration at PSI. The results contradict the WI08 solution from Arndt (2006), which the authors argue is biased in the low-energy region.
