# Levinson's Theorem for Dirac Equation

> Levinson's theorem for the Dirac equation is known in the form of a sum of positive and negative energy phase shifts at zero momentum related to the total number of bound states. In this letter we prove a stronger version of Levinson's theorem valid for positive and negative energy phase shifts separately. The surprising result is, that in general the phase shifts for each sign of the energy do not give the number of bound states with the same sign of the energy (in units of $π$), but instead, are related to the number of bound states of a certain Schrödinger equation, which coincides with the Dirac equation at zero momentum.

# Levinson's Theorem for Dirac Equation

## Key claim

The paper proves a stronger version of Levinson's theorem for the Dirac equation that applies separately to positive and negative energy phase shifts, but contrary to earlier incorrect claims, these phase shifts do not directly count bound states of the same energy sign. Instead, the positive and negative energy phase shifts at threshold are related to the number of bound states of specific Schrodinger equations that coincide with the Dirac equation at zero momentum.

## Method

The author transforms the reduced radial Dirac equation into two uncoupled Schrodinger-like equations for the wave functions w1 and w2, which depend on energy. By constructing auxiliary Schrodinger equations (with potentials evaluated at threshold energies epsilon = +m and epsilon = -m) that are energy-independent and analytic near threshold, the author shows that the Dirac phase shifts at threshold equal the phase shifts of these auxiliary Schrodinger equations. Standard Levinson's theorem is then applied to these Schrodinger equations to obtain the separate relations for positive and negative energy phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- The standard Dirac Levinson theorem of Ma and Ni (1985) gives: eta_{m kappa}(0) + eta_{-m kappa}(0) = (N_kappa^+ + N_kappa^-) pi, where N_kappa^+ and N_kappa^- are the numbers of positive and negative energy bound states
- For the case without threshold resonance: eta_{m kappa}(0) = n_l^+ pi and eta_{-m kappa}(0) = n_{overline l}^- pi, where n_l^+ and n_{overline l}^- are bound state counts of specific Schrodinger equations
- For the case with threshold resonance (possible only for kappa = +/-1): eta_{m kappa}(0) = (n_0^+ + 1/2) pi for l=0, and eta_{-m kappa}(0) = (n_0^- + 1/2) pi for overline l=0
- The total bound state relation N_kappa^+ + N_kappa^- = n_l^+ + n_{overline l}^- holds generally, though in general N_kappa^+ does not equal n_l^+ and N_kappa^- does not equal n_{overline l}^-

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic Levinson's theorem (Levinson, 1949) and its modification for zero-energy resonances (Newton, 1960) to the Dirac equation. It corrects earlier claims by Barthelemy (1967) and Ni (1979) that Levinson's theorem holds separately for positive and negative energies in the same sense as the nonrelativistic case, which Ma and Ni (1985) had shown to be incorrect. The result enables application of Levinson's theorem to nonperturbative approaches in quantum electrodynamics, as the author indicates will be presented in a forthcoming work (Poliatzky, 1993).
