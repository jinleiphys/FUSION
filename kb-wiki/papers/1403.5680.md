# Lefschetz thimble Monte Carlo for many body theories: application to the repulsive Hubbard model away from half filling

> Recently, a new method, based on stochastic integration on the surfaces of steepest descent of the action, was introduced to tackle the sign problem in quantum field theories. We show how this method can be used in many body theories to perform fully non-perturbative calculations of quantum corrections about mean field solutions. We discuss an explicit algorithm for implementing our method, and present results for the repulsive Hubbard model away from half-filling at intermediate temperatures. Our results are consistent with those from the recent state of the art cluster dynamical mean field theory calculations.

# Lefschetz Thimble Monte Carlo for Many Body Theories: Application to the Repulsive Hubbard Model Away from Half Filling

## Key claim

The Lefschetz thimble Monte Carlo method, originally developed for quantum field theories with a unique continuum limit, can be extended to many-body fermionic systems without a unique continuum limit by interpreting integration on the thimble as a fully non-perturbative inclusion of all quantum corrections about a mean-field solution. The method yields results consistent with cluster dynamical mean field theory (DCA DMFT) for the repulsive Hubbard model away from half filling, but only at temperatures above T/t > 0.4, below which the dominant mean fields are expected to become non-uniform.

## Method

The authors construct a functional integral for the Hubbard model via a continuous Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation that couples auxiliary fields to the on-site magnetization (n_{i↑} - n_{i↓}). They identify the dominant phase associated with uniform, time-independent real mean-field solutions and restrict sampling to the Lefschetz thimble attached to one saddle point, which for real saddle points is a subsector of the original real integration domain bounded by det M[φ] = 0. Sampling is performed using hybrid Monte Carlo with a leap-frog integrator, keeping the step size small enough to prevent trajectories from crossing zeros of the fermion determinant, thereby exploiting the non-ergodicity to remain on a single thimble. Configurations on the symmetry-related thimble (under φ → -φ) are generated by applying the symmetry operation.

## Key numbers

- Double occupancy results are shown for U/t = 4 (intermediate coupling) and U/t = 8 (strong coupling) on an 8² lattice; results for 10² and 12² lattices agree within ~1% in selected cases.
- Results are limited to temperatures T > 0.4t; below this temperature the dominant mean fields are expected to be non-uniform.
- At half filling (⟨n⟩ = 1), results are in perfect agreement with the latest determinant quantum Monte Carlo (DQMC) results.
- For μ/t = -1.0, a slight enhancement of double occupancy is observed below T/t < 1.
- The effective hopping parameter t_eff/t is defined as the ratio of kinetic energy for the interacting system to that for the non-interacting system.

## Context

This work extends the Lefschetz thimble approach previously developed by Cristoforetti (2012, 2013) and Mukherjee (2013) for quantum field theories to many-body fermionic systems, where universality arguments for a unique continuum limit do not apply. The method provides a non-perturbative generalization of mean-field theory and its extensions (such as the random phase approximation). The results are compared with DCA DMFT calculations by LeBlanc (2013, 2014) and DQMC results by Paiva (2010) and Scalettar (2014), showing excellent agreement. The approach enables fully non-perturbative calculations of quantum corrections about mean-field solutions in strongly correlated systems, with potential application to low-temperature regimes where the dominant phase is unknown.
