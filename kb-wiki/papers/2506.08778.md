# Symmetry energy and neutron matter equation of state at $ρ_0/3$ from the electric dipole polarizability in $^{48}$Ca, $^{68}$Ni and $^{208}$Pb

> Based on the quasiparticle random phase approximation implemented via the finite amplitude method, we employ a set of representative relativistic mean-field models to investigate the sensitivity of the inverse electric dipole polarizability $1/α_{\mathrm{D}}$ in $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$, $^{68}\mathrm{Ni}$, and $^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$ to the symmetry energy $E_{\rm{sym}}(ρ)$ and the neutron matter equation of state $E_{\rm{PNM}}(ρ)$ at a subsaturation density of $ρ= ρ_0/3$. Combined with predictions from nonrelativistic Skyrme energy density functionals (EDFs), our results reveal strong linear correlations between $1/α_{\mathrm{D}}$ and both $E_{\rm{sym}}(ρ_0/3)$ and $E_{\rm{PNM}}(ρ_0/3)$. In particular, the $1/α_{\mathrm{D}}$--$E_{\rm{PNM}}(ρ_0/3)$ correlation for $^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$ is found to be nearly model-independent. A Bayesian analysis of the measured values of $α_{\rm{D}}$ in $^{48}\mathrm{Ca}$, $^{68}\mathrm{Ni}$, and $^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$ yields quantitative constraints of $E_{\mathrm{sym}}(ρ_0/3) = 17.8^{+1.1(1.8)}_{-0.9(1.6)}~\mathrm{MeV}$ and $E_{\mathrm{PNM}}(ρ_0/3) = 9.1^{+0.8(1.4)}_{-0.9(1.4)}~\mathrm{MeV}$ at the 68\% (90\%) confidence level, respectively. The extracted value of $E_{\mathrm{PNM}}(ρ_0/3)$ exceeds most predictions from microscopic many-body theories, suggesting a mild tension between nuclear EDF-based constraints derived from $α_{\mathrm{D}}$ data and results from \textit{ab initio} calculations.

## Key claim
The inverse electric dipole polarizability \(1/\alpha_{\mathrm{D}}\) in \(^{48}\mathrm{Ca}\), \(^{68}\mathrm{Ni}\), and \(^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\) shows strong linear correlations with both the symmetry energy \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}(\rho_0/3)\) and the neutron matter equation of state \(E_{\mathrm{PNM}}(\rho_0/3)\) at a subsaturation density of \(\rho = \rho_0/3\), with the correlation for \(^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\) being nearly model-independent. However, the extracted value of \(E_{\mathrm{PNM}}(\rho_0/3)\) exceeds most predictions from microscopic many-body theories, indicating a mild tension between nuclear energy density functional (EDF) constraints from \(\alpha_{\mathrm{D}}\) data and results from *ab initio* calculations.

## Method
The authors employed the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) implemented via the finite amplitude method (QFAM) within a set of representative relativistic mean-field (RMF) models, including nonlinear meson-exchange, density-dependent meson-exchange, and density-dependent point-coupling models. They computed the electric dipole polarizability \(\alpha_{\mathrm{D}}\) for \(^{48}\mathrm{Ca}\), \(^{68}\mathrm{Ni}\), and \(^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\) using the DIRQFM program, and combined these results with predictions from 24 nonrelativistic Skyrme energy density functionals (EDFs) taken from prior work. A Bayesian analysis of the measured \(\alpha_{\mathrm{D}}\) values was then performed to constrain \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}(\rho_0/3)\) and \(E_{\mathrm{PNM}}(\rho_0/3)\).

## Key numbers
- \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}(\rho_0/3) = 17.8^{+1.1(1.8)}_{-0.9(1.6)}~\mathrm{MeV}\) at the 68% (90%) confidence level, from Bayesian analysis of \(\alpha_{\mathrm{D}}\) in \(^{48}\mathrm{Ca}\), \(^{68}\mathrm{Ni}\), and \(^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\).
- \(E_{\mathrm{PNM}}(\rho_0/3) = 9.1^{+0.8(1.4)}_{-0.9(1.4)}~\mathrm{MeV}\) at the 68% (90%) confidence level.
- Experimental \(\alpha_{\mathrm{D}}\) values used: \(\alpha_{\mathrm{D}}^{48}=2.07\pm 0.22~\mathrm{fm}^3\) (Birkhan 2016), \(\alpha_{\mathrm{D}}^{68}=3.88\pm 0.31~\mathrm{fm}^3\) (Rossi 2013), and \(\alpha_{\mathrm{D}}^{208}=19.6\pm 0.6~\mathrm{fm}^3\) (Tamii 2011), all corrected for quasi-deuteron contributions.
- Linear fit for \(^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\): \(10^{3}/\alpha_{\mathrm{D}}^{208} = (1.80\pm 0.08) + (19.7\pm 1.3) E_{\mathrm{sym}}(\rho_0/3)\), with \(\alpha_{\mathrm{D}}\) in \(\mathrm{fm}^3\) and \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}\) in MeV.
- Pearson correlation coefficients between \(1/\alpha_{\mathrm{D}}\) and \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}(\rho_0/3)\): \(r=0.90\) for \(^{48}\mathrm{Ca}\), \(r=0.89\) for \(^{68}\mathrm{Ni}\), and \(r=0.94\) for \(^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\).

## Context
This work extends the finding of Zhang (2015) that the \(\alpha_{\mathrm{D}}\) of \(^{208}\mathrm{Pb}\) is determined by the symmetry energy or neutron matter EOS at \(\rho_0/3\), by examining the same correlations for \(^{48}\mathrm{Ca}\) and \(^{68}\mathrm{Ni}\) using a broader set of relativistic and nonrelativistic EDFs. The results enable tighter constraints on the subsaturation density behavior of the symmetry energy and neutron matter EOS, and the tension with *ab initio* calculations of \(E_{\mathrm{PNM}}(\rho_0/3)\) motivates further investigation into the consistency between EDF-based and microscopic many-body approaches. Prior constraints from isobaric analogue states and neutron skin thickness (Danielewicz 2014) and from isospin diffusion data (Tsang 2009, Zhang 2020) are compared with the present results.
