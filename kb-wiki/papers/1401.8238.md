# Chiral-Scale Perturbation Theory About an Infrared Fixed Point

> We review the failure of lowest order chiral $SU(3)_L \times SU(3)_R$ perturbation theory $χ$PT$_3$ to account for amplitudes involving the $f_0(500)$ resonance and $O(m_K)$ extrapolations in momenta. We summarize our proposal to replace $χ$PT$_3$ with a new effective theory $χ$PT$_σ$ based on a low-energy expansion about an infrared fixed point in 3-flavour QCD. At the fixed point, the quark condensate $\langle\bar{q}q\rangle_\mathrm{vac}\neq 0$ induces nine Nambu-Goldstone bosons: $π, K, η$ and a QCD dilaton $σ$ which we identify with the $f_0(500)$ resonance. We discuss the construction of the $χ$PT$_σ$ Lagrangian and its implications for meson phenomenology at low-energies. Our main results include a simple explanation for the $ΔI = 1/2$ rule in $K$-decays and an estimate for the Drell-Yan ratio in the infrared limit.

## Key claim

The paper proposes replacing conventional three-flavor chiral perturbation theory (χPT₃) with a new effective theory χPTσ based on a low-energy expansion about an infrared fixed point in QCD, where the f₀(500) resonance is identified as a QCD dilaton σ and promoted to leading order, thereby solving the convergence problem of χPT₃ for amplitudes involving scalar-isoscalar channels and O(mK) extrapolations in momenta.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral-scale perturbation theory (χPTσ) Lagrangian by extending the standard Nambu-Goldstone sector {π, K, η} to include the f₀(500) as a QCD dilaton σ, with operators classified by scaling dimension d and expanded about an infrared fixed point αIR where the gluonic trace anomaly vanishes. The effective theory consists of invariant (d=4), anomalous (d>4), and mass (d<4) terms, with the strange quark mass setting the scale for both m²f₀ and m²K, m²η. The LO strong Lagrangian is given explicitly in terms of the SU(3) field U(π,K,η) and the chiral-invariant dilaton field σ, with anomalous dimensions γm and β' evaluated at the fixed point.

## Key numbers

- Complex pole mass of f₀(500): mf₀ = 441 − i·272 MeV, with coupling |gf₀ππ| = 3.31 GeV
- Dilaton decay constant: Fσ ≈ 100 MeV, estimated via an analogue of the Goldberger-Treiman relation applied to NN-scattering analyses
- Estimate for the K_S-σ mixing coupling: |gK_Sσ| ≈ 4.4 × 10³ keV², with precision ≲ 30% expected from a 3-flavor expansion
- σ-pole amplitude for K_S → ππ: |Aσ-pole| ≈ 0.34 keV, accounting for the experimental magnitude |A₀|expt. = 0.33 keV
- Estimate for the nonperturbative Drell-Yan ratio at the infrared fixed point: RIR ≈ 5, deduced from dispersive analyses of γγ → π⁰π⁰

## Context

This work extends the approach of Crewther (1970) and Ellis (1970) on the dual role of the quark condensate for scale and chiral transformations, and addresses the failure of conventional χPT₃ expansions noted by Meissner (1991) for amplitudes involving the f₀(500) resonance. The proposal enables a systematic framework for low-energy meson phenomenology that explains the ΔI = 1/2 rule in kaon decays as a consequence of dilaton pole diagrams, and provides a method to extract the infrared Drell-Yan ratio from γγ → ππ data analyzed dispersively by Hoferichter et al. (2011).
