# Description of the two-nucleon system on the basis of the Bargmann representation of the S matrix

> For the effective-range function $k\cot δ$, a pole approximation that involves a small number of parameters is derived on the basis of the Bargmann representation of the $S$ matrix. The parameters of this representation, which have a clear physical meaning, are related to the parameters of the Bargmann $S$ matrix by simple equations. By using a polynomial least-squares fit to the function $k\cot δ$ at low energies, the triplet low-energy parameters of neutron-proton scattering are obtained for the latest experimental data of Arndt et al. on phase shifts. The results are $a_{t}=5.4030 $fm, $r_{t}=1.7494 $fm, and $v_{2}=0.163 $fm$^{3}$. With allowance for the values found for the low-energy scattering parameters and for the pole parameter, the pole approximation of the function $k\cot δ$ provides an excellent description of the triplet phase shift for neutron-proton scattering over a wide energy range ($T_{\text{lab}}\lesssim 1000 $MeV), substantially improving the description at low energies as well. For the experimental phase shifts of Arndt et al., the triplet shape parameters $v_{n}$ of the effective-range expansion are obtained by using the pole approximation. The description of the phase shift by means of the effective-range expansion featuring values found for the low-energy scattering parameters proves to be fairly accurate over a broad energy region extending to energy values approximately equal to the energy at which this phase shift changes sign, this being indicative of a high accuracy and a considerable value of the effective-range expansion in describing experimental data on nucleon-nucleon scattering. The properties of the deuteron that were calculated by using various approximations of the effective-range function comply well with their experimental values.

## Key claim

The paper derives a pole approximation for the effective-range function \(k\cot\delta\) from the Bargmann representation of the \(S\) matrix, claiming that this approximation provides an excellent description of the triplet neutron-proton phase shift up to \(T_{\text{lab}}\lesssim 1000\) MeV. However, the abstract glosses over the fact that the pole parameter \(D\) must be fixed using the experimental energy at which the phase shift changes sign, and that the description quality depends on this empirical input rather than being derived purely from first principles.

## Method

The authors used the Bargmann representation of the \(S\) matrix for the case of two physical states (\(N=2\)) to derive a one-pole rational expression for the effective-range function \(k\cot\delta\) in terms of the scattering length \(a_t\), effective range \(r_t\), shape parameter \(v_2\), and a pole parameter \(D\). They performed a polynomial least-squares fit to the function \(k\cot\delta\) at low energies (\(T_{\text{lab}}\lesssim 10\) MeV) using the experimental phase-shift data of Arndt et al. to extract the triplet low-energy parameters, then fixed the pole parameter \(D\) from the energy where the triplet phase shift changes sign.

## Key numbers

- Triplet scattering length: \(a_t = 5.4030\) fm
- Triplet effective range: \(r_t = 1.7494\) fm
- Triplet shape parameter: \(v_2 = 0.163\) fm\(^3\) (dimensionless shape parameter \(P_t = v_2/r_t^3 = 0.0304\))
- Pole parameter: \(D = -0.225526\) fm\(^2\), corresponding to laboratory energy \(T_0 = 368.026\) MeV where the triplet phase shift changes sign
- With the pole approximation (parameters from Eqs. 49-52), the triplet phase shift is described up to \(T_{\text{lab}}\lesssim 500\) MeV with absolute error not exceeding \(0.5^\circ\); up to \(T_{\text{lab}}\leqslant 40\) MeV, error does not exceed \(0.01^\circ\)
- Slightly varying \(v_2\) to \(0.168\) fm\(^3\) (pole approximation P2) yields minimum absolute error of about \(0.1^\circ\) in the range \(T_{\text{lab}}\lesssim 400\) MeV
- At \(D = -0.2147\) fm\(^2\), the absolute error in the range \(0\leq T\leq 20\) MeV is at most \(0.006^\circ\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of the Bargmann \(S\)-matrix formalism to neutron-deuteron scattering (Babenko and Petrov, 2005) and generalizes the effective-range approximation to include a pole term derived from the presence of two physical states in the system. The pole approximation coincides in form with the empirical Cini-Fubini-Stanghellini formula (Cini, Fubini, and Stanghellini, 1959), but the authors find that the one-pion-exchange-based parameters from that approach (\(v_2^{\text{CFS}} = -0.121\) fm\(^3\), \(D^{\text{CFS}} = 3.777\) fm\(^2\)) differ qualitatively from their experimentally determined values, indicating that one-pion exchange is not the dominant mechanism. The results enable a unified description of both the deuteron bound state and triplet neutron-proton scattering over a wide energy range using a small number of physically meaningful parameters.
