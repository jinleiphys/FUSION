# A Truncated partial wave analysis of a complete experiment for photoproduction of two pseudoscalar mesons on a nucleon

> A truncated partial wave analysis for the photoproduction of two pseudoscalar mesons on a nucleon is discussed with respect to the determination of a complete set of observables. For the selection of such a set we have applied a criterion previously developed for photo- and electrodisintegration of a deuteron, which allows one to find a 'minimal' set of observables for determining the partial wave amplitudes up to possible discrete ambiguities. The question of resolving the remaining ambiguities by invoking additional observables is discussed for the simplest case, when the partial wave expansion is truncated at the lowest total angular momentum of the final state $J_{max}=1/2$. The resulting 'fully' complete set, allowing an unambiguous determination of the partial wave amplitudes, is presented.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for photoproduction of two pseudoscalar mesons on a nucleon, a truncated partial wave analysis truncated at maximum total angular momentum J_max = 1/2 can yield a "fully" complete set of observables that determines the partial wave amplitudes unambiguously, but only if recoil polarization in the plane orthogonal to the quantization axis is included; this condition distinguishes the reaction from single-meson photoproduction where recoil polarization can be avoided.

## Method

The authors apply a criterion originally developed for deuteron photo- and electrodisintegration (Arenhoevel, Leidemann, and Tomusiak, 1995; 1997) to select a minimal set of 2n-1 independent observables for n complex amplitudes, then use a second criterion from Tabakin (1968) to resolve remaining discrete ambiguities by identifying unitary transformations that commute with the observable matrices. For the simplest case of J_max = 1/2, they construct the explicit 4x4 matrices F and G representing the real and imaginary parts of the observables, and determine which additional observables break the symmetry that produces ambiguities.

## Key numbers

- The partial wave expansion is truncated at J_max = 1/2, yielding 4 amplitudes t_k (k=1,...,4) for each photon helicity lambda = +/-1, with the initial nucleon helicity mu fixed by angular momentum conservation as mu = lambda/2
- The minimal complete set requires 15 observables (2n-1 with n=8 total amplitudes), selected from the 16 observables listed: sigma^0, Sigma^c, P^0_z, P^c_z, each with four f/g components for lambda = +/-1
- The "fully" complete set that resolves all discrete ambiguities requires: sigma^0, Sigma^c, P^0/c_z, and P^0/c_x (or P^0/c_y), a total of observables including recoil polarization along both the z-axis and one of the x or y axes
- The matrices F and G are 4x4; there are 10 symmetric matrices F^(n_beta,lambda lambda) and 6 antisymmetric matrices G^(n_beta,lambda lambda) for lambda=1

## Context

This work extends the truncated partial wave analysis methodology previously applied to deuteron photodisintegration (Arenhoevel, Leidemann, and Tomusiak, 1995; 1997) to the more complex case of two-pseudoscalar-meson photoproduction. It builds on the model-independent partial wave expansion formalism developed by Fix and Arenhoevel (2012). The authors note that while a complete experiment for determining spin amplitudes would require triple polarization observables as argued by Roberts (1998), the truncated partial wave analysis approach appears more feasible. The generalization to higher partial waves (J_max = 3/2) is left for a forthcoming paper, as the authors acknowledge that waves with J=3/2 are important in both pi-pi and pi-eta channels even at low energies.
