# The Hadron Spectrum from Lattice QCD

> Determining the hadron spectrum and hadron properties beyond the ground states is a challenge in lattice QCD. Most of these results have been in the quenched approximation but now we are entering the dynamical era. I review some of the ideas and methods of the lattice approach, concentrating on a few examples and on results obtained for Chirally Improved (CI) fermions.

## Key claim

Determining the hadron spectrum beyond ground states in lattice QCD is now entering the dynamical era, but most results to date have been obtained in the quenched approximation where fermion loops are disregarded; the author reviews methods and results for excited states using Chirally Improved (CI) fermions, noting that the Roper resonance N(1440) comes out 30% too heavy in quenched calculations and that dynamical sea quarks may be required to resolve this discrepancy.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD methods for computing hadron masses from two-point correlation functions, focusing on the variational method where a correlation matrix is constructed from multiple interpolating operators and a generalized eigenvalue problem is solved to extract ground and excited state masses. Quenched gauge configurations were generated with the Luscher-Weisz gauge action on lattices of size 16^3 x 32 and 20^3 x 32 at lattice spacings between 0.12 and 0.15 fm, using two mass-degenerate light quarks and one heavier s-quark adjusted to give the correct kaon mass. For dynamical fermions, the Hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm is used to include the fermion determinant as a weight factor in gauge configuration generation.

## Key numbers

- The Roper state N(1440) comes out 30% too heavy in quenched calculations
- Lattice spacing values in full QCD simulations lie between 0.07 and 0.2 fm
- Quenched simulations used lattices of size 16^3 x 32 and 20^3 x 32 with lattice spacings between 0.12 and 0.15 fm, corresponding to a spatial lattice size around 2.4 fm
- Pion masses around and above 300 MeV are used for extrapolation to the chiral limit
- Four states are seen in quenched baryon results that are not established in Particle Data Group tables: a positive parity Omega excitation near 2.3 GeV, two negative parity Xi states near 1.78 GeV, and an Omega ground state near 1.97 MeV
- Both negative parity states N(1535) and N(1650) are seen in quenched calculations

## Context

This review extends the line of lattice QCD spectrum calculations from the quenched approximation into the dynamical fermion era, building on the work of the Bern-Graz-Regensburg (BGR) collaboration using Chirally Improved fermions. The paper contrasts quenched results from Buzatu et al. (2006) with dynamical calculations and discusses the Ginsparg-Wilson condition as formulated by Ginsparg and Wilson (1982) and later realized by Hasenfratz et al. (2005) and Neuberger. The variational method for extracting excited states follows the approach of Michael (1985) and Luscher and Wolff (1990). The results enable future studies of how dynamical quarks affect excited hadron spectra, particularly the level ordering of the Roper resonance, and open the question of whether chiral symmetry restoration occurs for higher excitations as suggested by Glozman (2007).
