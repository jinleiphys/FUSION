# Beyond the Schwinger boson representation of the su(2)-algebra. II -- Some theoretical features of new boson representation and connections to the other boson representations

> Concerning the new boson representation presented in Part I, it is proved that this representation obeys the su(2)-algebra in a certain subspace in the whole boson space constructed by the Schwinger boson representation of the su(1,1)-algebra. Some other problems related to this representation are discussed.

## Key claim

The new boson representation of the su(2)-algebra, expressed in terms of two kinds of bosons with a parameter \(C_m\), obeys the su(2)-algebra only in a specific subspace \(P\) of the whole boson space constructed by the Schwinger boson representation of the su(1,1)-algebra, but does not obey it in the complementary subspace \(Q\).

## Method

The authors prove the algebra by examining the commutation relations of the generators \(\hat{\mathcal{S}}_{\pm,0}\) and the Casimir operator \(\hat{\boldsymbol{\mathcal{S}}}^2\) in the subspaces \(P\) and \(Q\) of the \(t\)-\(t_0\) plane, using a regularization parameter \(\epsilon\) that is taken to zero. They then derive the Holstein-Primakoff representation from the new representation by introducing auxiliary boson operators \(\hat{c}, \hat{c}^*\) and show that the Schwinger representation is recovered in the limit \(C_m \rightarrow \infty\).

## Key numbers

* The magnitude of the su(2)-spin \(s\) in subspace \(P\) is restricted to the range \(s = 0, 1/2, \ldots, C_m/2\), where \(C_m\) is a constant parameter related to system size.
* In subspace \(Q\), the spin magnitude takes values \(s = (C_m+1)/2, (C_m+2)/2, \ldots, \infty\).
* The minimum weight state in subspace \(P\) is \(\ket{s,\sigma} = (\hat{b}^*)^{C_m-2s}\ket{0}\) with \(\sigma = -s\), valid for \(s \leq C_m/2\).
* The minimum weight state in subspace \(Q\) is \(\ket{s,\sigma} = (\hat{a}^*)^{2s-C_m}\ket{0}\) with \(\sigma = s-C_m\), valid for \(s \geq (C_m+1)/2\).
* The norm of the state \(\ket{s,s_0}\) in subspace \(P\) is \(\braket{s,s_0}{s,s_0} = (2s)!(s+s_0)!/(s-s_0)! \cdot (C_m-2s)!\).
* The operator \(\hat{\Sigma}_+\) acting on the maximum weight state \(\ket{C_m/2, s_0}\) yields zero, \(\hat{\Sigma}_+\ket{C_m/2,s_0}=0\).

## Context

This paper (Part II) continues the work of Part I (Tsue et al., 2014) and extends the known boson representations of the su(2)-algebra, namely the Schwinger representation and the Holstein-Primakoff representation (Holstein and Primakoff, 1940). The new representation introduces a finite parameter \(C_m\) that restricts the spin magnitude to a finite range, which is relevant for systems like the pairing model in many-fermion systems where \(2C_m = 4\Omega_0\) is the total number of single-particle states. This work enables the study of su(2)-algebras with a built-in cutoff on spin size, connecting to finite-size effects in nuclear physics.
