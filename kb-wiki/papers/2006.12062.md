# Selection rules of electromagnetic transitions for chirality-parity violation in atomic nuclei

> The nuclear Chirality-Parity (ChP) violation, a simultaneous breaking of chiral and reflection symmetries in the intrinsic frame, is investigated with a reflection-asymmetric triaxial particle rotor model. A new symmetry for an ideal ChP violation system is found and the corresponding selection rules of the electromagnetic transitions are derived. The fingerprints for the ChP violation including the nearly degenerate quartet bands and the selection rules of the electromagnetic transitions are provided. These fingerprints are examined for ChP quartet bands by taking a two-$j$ shell $h_{11/2}$ and $d_{5/2}$ with typical energy spacing for $A=$ 130 nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper derives selection rules for electromagnetic transitions in nuclei exhibiting simultaneous breaking of chiral and reflection symmetries (Chirality-Parity violation), but these rules are strictly valid only for the ideal case of maximum triaxial deformation (\(\gamma=90^\circ\)), a pure particle-hole configuration, and a degenerate two-\(j\) shell with \(\Delta l = \Delta j = 3\hbar\).

## Method

A reflection-asymmetric triaxial particle rotor model (RAT-PRM) was developed for a core with triaxiality \(\gamma=90^\circ\) coupled to one particle and one hole in a two-\(j\) shell (\(h_{11/2}\) and \(d_{5/2}\)). The Hamiltonian was diagonalized in the laboratory frame to obtain energy spectra and electromagnetic transition probabilities. A new symmetry operator \(\hat{\mathcal{A}}\) (chiture) and its product with parity \(\hat{\mathcal{B}}=\hat{\mathcal{A}}\hat{P}\) (chiplex) were introduced to classify eigenstates and derive transition selection rules.

## Key numbers

* The energy splitting between chiral doublet bands (same parity) is less than 200 keV for spins \(14\hbar \leq I \leq 20\hbar\).
* The splitting between parity doublet bands is less than 20 keV for the same spin range, using core parity splitting parameter \(E(0^-)=0\) MeV.
* The two-\(j\) shell energy difference between \(h_{11/2}\) and \(d_{5/2}\) was taken as 0.89 MeV, typical for \(A=130\) nuclei.
* The moment of inertia parameter was \(\mathcal{J}_0 = 18\hbar^2/\)MeV, with deformation parameters \(\beta_{22}=0.3\) and \(\beta_{30}=0.1\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of chiral doublet bands (Frauendorf 1997) and parity doublet bands in pear-shaped nuclei (Butler 1996) to the simultaneous breaking of both symmetries, building on the observation of multiple chiral doublets (M\(\chi\)D) in octupole-soft nuclei like \(^{78}\)Br (Liu 2016). The derived selection rules provide fingerprints for identifying Chirality-Parity quartet bands in future experiments, particularly in the \(A=130\) mass region where the \(h_{11/2}\) and \(d_{5/2}\) orbitals are near the Fermi surface.
