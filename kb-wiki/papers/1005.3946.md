# Bulk and Shear Viscosity in Hagedorn Fluid

> Assuming that the Hagedorn fluid composed of known particles and resonances with masses $m<2\,$GeV obeys the {\it first-order} theory (Eckart) of relativistic fluid, we discuss the transport properties of QCD confined phase. Based on the relativistic kinetic theory formulated under the relaxation time approximation, expressions for bulk and shear viscosity in thermal medium are derived. The relaxation time in the Hagedorn dynamical fluid exclusively takes into account the decay and eventually van der Waals processes. We comment on the {\it in-medium} thermal effects on bulk and shear viscosities and averaged relaxation time with and without the excluded-volume approach. As an application of these results, we suggest the dynamics of heavy-ion collisions, non-equlibrium thermodynamics and the cosmological models, which require thermo and hydrodynamics equations of state.

# Bulk and Shear Viscosity in Hagedorn Fluid

## Key claim

The Hagedorn fluid composed of known hadron resonances with masses below 2 GeV, treated under first-order (Eckart) relativistic fluid theory, exhibits bulk viscosity about one order of magnitude larger than shear viscosity at high temperature, contrary to the behavior of weakly interacting systems; this result depends on the assumption that only decay and van der Waals repulsive processes contribute to the relaxation time, with the excluded-volume approach modifying the temperature dependence near the critical temperature.

## Method

The authors apply relativistic kinetic theory under the relaxation time approximation to a gas of hadron resonances described by the Hagedorn mass spectrum, summing over all fermion and boson resonances. The relaxation time is computed exclusively from decay processes and repulsive interactions modeled via an excluded-volume approach based on the MIT bag constant. Bulk and shear viscosity coefficients are derived from the deviation of the energy-momentum tensor from local equilibrium, with the spatial components decomposed into shear and bulk contributions.

## Key numbers

- Hagedorn mass spectrum parameters: $c = 0.5$ GeV$^{3/2}$, $m_0 = 0.5$ GeV, $T_H = 0.195$ GeV, $k = -5$
- MIT bag constant: ${\cal B}^{1/4} = 0.34$ GeV
- Nuclear saturation density: $n_0 \approx 0.145$ fm$^{-3}$
- Relaxation time from decay widths: $\tau_i \equiv \Gamma_i^{-1} = (0.151 m_i - 0.058)^{-1}$ (in GeV$^{-1}$)
- Non-relativistic relaxation time approximation for nucleon systems: $\tau(T) \approx \frac{850}{T^2}\left(\frac{n}{n_0}\right)^{1/3} \left[1+0.04 T\frac{n}{n_0}\right] + \frac{38}{\sqrt{T}(1+\frac{160}{T^2})} \frac{n_0}{n}$
- Bulk viscosity $\xi$ is about one order of magnitude larger than shear viscosity $\eta$ at high temperature
- The ratio $\eta/s$ starts from values much higher than the AdS/CFT lower bound $1/4\pi$ and approaches it with increasing temperature
- UrQMD model gives saturated $\eta/s$ values in the region $0.125 < T < 0.166$ GeV
- Viscous hydrodynamic calculations give an upper bound of $0.24$ for $\eta/s$ near $T_c$

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of transport coefficients in confined QCD matter using effective hadron resonance models, including those by V-hadron (various years), redl (various years), and etas2 (various years). The Hagedorn mass spectrum approach incorporates the full resonance degrees of freedom, building on the thermodynamic treatment of Tawfik (various years). The results enable applications to heavy-ion collision dynamics, non-equilibrium thermodynamics, and cosmological viscous models that require complete thermo- and hydrodynamic equations of state for the early universe. The authors note that comparison with lattice QCD calculations below $T_c$ (Shin, various years) requires matching configurations, which remains for future study.
