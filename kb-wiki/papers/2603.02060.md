# Spinodal instability in nuclear matter with light cluster degrees of freedom

> We investigate the thermodynamical stability of low-density isospin-symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature, explicitly including light clusters as degrees of freedom. Within a generalized mean-field framework, we compute the curvature matrix of the free-energy density and determine the spinodal region, identifying the conditions under which mechanically unstable modes may develop in the presence of clustering. Particular attention is devoted to the formal consequences of introducing an infrared momentum cutoff in the density and current moments, which effectively accounts for Pauli-blocking effects and the associated reduction of low-momentum quasiparticle states in the medium. We show that when the cutoff is density dependent, thermodynamic consistency requires additional contributions to the chemical potentials and extra terms also appear in the first hydrodynamic moment, influencing both the stability analysis and the location of the spinodal boundary. We further examine the character of the unstable modes and find that a sufficiently stiff density dependence of the cutoff may drive clusters to fluctuate out of phase with nucleons, pushing them toward low-density regions while nucleonic instabilities grow, in contrast with the in-phase pattern obtained when in-medium effects are neglected. Our results shed new light on the role of light clusters in the phase dynamics of warm, dilute nuclear matter, with implications for heavy-ion collisions and for the physics of neutron-star crusts.

## Key claim

The paper shows that when a density-dependent infrared momentum cutoff is introduced to account for Pauli-blocking effects on light clusters in nuclear matter, thermodynamic consistency requires additional contributions to chemical potentials and hydrodynamic moments, which can alter the spinodal instability boundary and, for a sufficiently stiff density dependence of the cutoff, drive clusters to fluctuate out of phase with nucleons.

## Method

The authors compute the curvature matrix of the free-energy density for low-density isospin-symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature (T greater than about 5 MeV), including deuterons and alpha particles as explicit degrees of freedom within a generalized mean-field framework. They introduce an infrared momentum cutoff in the cluster distribution functions to model the Mott effect and derive the additional rearrangement terms in the chemical potentials and hydrodynamic equations that arise when the cutoff depends on density. The spinodal region is determined by analyzing the eigenvalues of the free-energy curvature matrix, and the character of unstable modes is examined by comparing the results from the thermodynamic analysis with those from a linearized Vlasov approach.

## Key numbers

- The temperature domain considered is T greater than about 5 MeV, relevant for heavy-ion collisions at beam energies E/A approximately 30-300 MeV/nucleon and for binary neutron-star mergers and supernova explosions.
- The bare nucleon mass is taken as m = 939 MeV.
- The constituent mass of each species is defined as m_j = A_j * m - B_j, where B_j is the absolute value of the binding energy of species j.
- The spinodal instability onset in symmetric nuclear matter with one cluster species is obtained from the determinant of a 2x2 matrix (Eq. A in the text), where the standard pure-nucleonic condition is 1 + F_0 = 0.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies limited to a single cluster species (Wang 2024) by allowing simultaneous inclusion of multiple cluster species and exploring sensitivity to different cutoff parameterizations, nucleon-cluster mean-field potential strength, and in-medium binding energies. It complements the linear-response analysis of Wang (2024) by providing a thermodynamic perspective on clustering in warm dilute nuclear matter, with implications for heavy-ion collisions and neutron-star crust physics. The results shed light on how in-medium effects on clusters reshape the unstable response of dilute nuclear matter, building on prior work by Ropke (2015) on the Mott momentum and by Chomaz (2004) on spinodal instabilities.
