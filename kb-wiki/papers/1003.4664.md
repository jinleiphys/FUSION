# Radiative decay into gamma-baryon of dynamically generated resonances from the vector-baryon interaction

> We study the radiative decay into $γ$ and a baryon of the SU(3) octet and decuplet of nine and ten resonances that are dynamically generated from the interaction of vector mesons with baryons of the octet and the decuplet respectively. We obtain quite different partial decay widths for the various resonances, and for different charge states of the same resonance, suggesting that the experimental investigation of these radiative decays should bring much information on the nature of these resonances. For the case of baryons of the octet we determine the helicity amplitudes and compare them with experimental data when available.

## Key claim

The radiative decay widths of dynamically generated resonances from vector-baryon interactions into a photon and a baryon are predicted to be well within measurable range (of order 1 MeV), with striking differences of up to one order of magnitude between different charge states of the same resonance, which should constitute a clear experimental test of the model.

## Method

The authors use the local hidden gauge formalism for vector meson interactions, where photons couple to hadrons through conversion into vector mesons (ρ⁰, ω, φ). They take the couplings of resonances to vector-baryon channels from previous works (Sun et al. 2010, Geng and Oset 2009) and compute radiative decay amplitudes by projecting the ρ⁰B component from the isospin basis and summing over intermediate vector mesons. For baryon octet final states, they also evaluate helicity amplitudes A₁/₂ and A₃/₂ using the same framework.

## Key numbers

- For the S=0, I=1/2 resonance at 1850+i5 MeV (decuplet baryon case): radiative width Γ = 0.722 MeV for both charge states
- For the S=0, I=3/2 resonance at 1972+i49 MeV (decuplet baryon case): Γ = 1.402 MeV (I₃=3/2), 0.143 MeV (I₃=1/2), 0.203 MeV (I₃=-1/2), 1.582 MeV (I₃=-3/2)
- For the S=-1, I=0 resonance at 2052+i10 MeV (decuplet baryon case): Γ = 0.583 MeV
- For the S=-3, I=0 resonance at 2449+i7 MeV (decuplet baryon case): Γ = 0.330 MeV
- For the S=0, I=1/2 resonance at 1696 MeV (octet baryon case): Γ = 253 keV (I₃=1/2), 334 keV (I₃=-1/2)
- For the S=0, I=1/2 resonance at 1977+i53 MeV (octet baryon case): Γ = 79 keV (I₃=1/2), 196 keV (I₃=-1/2)

## Context

This work extends the dynamical generation approach of Geng and Oset (2009) for vector-baryon interactions, which produced ten resonances from vector-decuplet baryon interactions and nine resonances from vector-octet baryon interactions. The radiative decay predictions provide a test of the hidden gauge formalism that previously succeeded for meson decays such as f₀(1370) and f₂(1270) into γγ (Nagahiro et al. 2009). The results enable experimental investigation of these resonances' nature, particularly since no prior theoretical or experimental work existed for radiative decays into γ and a decuplet baryon.
