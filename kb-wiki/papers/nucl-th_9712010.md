# Caloric curve in Au + Au collisions

> Realistic caloric curves are obtained for $^{197}Au + ^{197}Au$ reaction with incident energy ranging from 35 to 130 MeV/nucleon in the dynamic statistical multifragmentation model. It is shown that for excitation energy 3 to 8 MeV/nucleon, the temperature remains constant in the range 5 to 6 MeV, which is close to experiment. The mechanism of energy deposition through the tripartition of colliding system envisaged in this model together with inter-fragment nuclear interaction are found to play important role. A possible signature of liquid-gas phase transition is seen in the specific heat distribution calculated from these caloric curves, and the critical temperature is found to be $\sim$ 6 to 6.5 MeV.

## Key claim

The dynamic statistical multifragmentation (DSM) model produces caloric curves for Au+Au collisions that show a plateau in temperature between 5 and 6 MeV for excitation energies of 3 to 8 MeV/nucleon, but this behavior only appears for fireball systems with more than approximately 120 nucleons; the kink in the caloric curve at about 8 MeV/nucleon excitation energy is interpreted as a possible signature of liquid-gas phase transition, with the critical temperature estimated at 6 to 6.5 MeV.

## Method

The authors used the dynamic statistical multifragmentation (DSM) model, which divides the colliding system into a fireball, projectile-like spectator, and target-like spectator via a tripartition mechanism. The fireball's excitation energy was calculated from relativistic kinematics and its decay into all possible fragments was treated statistically using a grand canonical ensemble with simultaneous conservation of baryon number, charge, and energy. Calculations were performed at two freeze-out densities (0.22 ρ₀ and 0.115 ρ₀) for fireballs of masses A=84, 150, and 196, corresponding to impact parameters of 5.8, 6.95, and 8.8 fm in ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au collisions at incident energies from 35 to 130 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Temperature remains constant at 5 to 6 MeV for excitation energies from 3 to 8 MeV/nucleon in the heavier fireball systems (A=150 and A=196)
- A kink in the caloric curve appears at excitation energy of about 8 MeV/nucleon, with corresponding temperatures of 6 to 6.5 MeV depending on mass and freeze-out density
- The plateau and kink behavior only appears when the fireball mass exceeds approximately 120 nucleons
- Calculations used freeze-out densities of 0.22 ρ₀ and 0.115 ρ₀, where ρ₀ is ground-state nuclear matter density
- For the A=150 fireball, the specific heat peak indicates a possible phase transition at T ∼ 6 MeV; for A=196, at T ∼ 6.5 MeV
- When inter-fragment nuclear interaction was switched off, the kink shifted to excitation energy of 12 MeV/nucleon with temperature of about 8 MeV

## Context

This work extends the experimental findings of Pochodzalla (1995), who observed a constant temperature of about 5 MeV for excitation energies of 3 to 10 MeV/nucleon in Au+Au reactions, by providing a theoretical model that reproduces this behavior. The DSM model results contrast with earlier Thomas-Fermi calculations by De et al. (1997) for ¹⁵⁰Sm, which found a kink at much higher excitation energy (∼18 MeV/nucleon) and temperature (∼10 MeV), and with the microcanonical model of Gross (1990), which found only a very short plateau due to the omission of inter-fragment nuclear interaction. The results enable further investigation into the order of the liquid-gas phase transition and determination of critical exponents in finite nuclear matter.
