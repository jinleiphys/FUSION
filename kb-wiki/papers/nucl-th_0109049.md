# Electron helicity-dependence in (e,e'p) reactions with polarized nuclei and the fifth response function

> The cross section for electron-induced proton knockout reactions with polarized beam and target is computed in a DWIA model. The analysis is focused on the electron helicity-dependent (HD) piece of the cross section. For the case of in-plane emission a new symmetry is found in the HD cross section, which takes opposite values for specific opposing pairs of nuclear orientations, explaining why the fifth response (involving polarized electrons, but unpolarized nuclei) is zero for this choice of kinematics. Even when this symmetry breaks down in the case of nucleon emission out of the scattering plane, it is shown that it is still present in the separate response functions. A physical explanation of these results is presented using a semi-classical model of the reaction based on the PWIA and on the concept of a nucleon orbit with its implied expectation values of position and spin.

## Key claim

The helicity-dependent (HD) cross section in (e,e'p) reactions with polarized nuclei exhibits a new nodal symmetry for in-plane emission that is exact even in the presence of final-state interactions (FSI), causing the HD cross section to take opposite values for specific opposing pairs of nuclear orientations; this symmetry breaks down for out-of-plane emission because the T' and TL' response functions have opposite parity under the nodal inversion, yet each individual response function still preserves the symmetry.

## Method

The authors computed the cross section for electron-induced proton knockout from the 1d₃/₂ shell of polarized ³⁹K using a distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) model with a relativistic on-shell current expansion in powers of the initial nucleon momentum p/M. They solved the Schrödinger equation for the ejected proton using the Schwandt optical potential parameterization, and analyzed the helicity-dependent cross section and its constituent response functions (W^T', W^TL', and \widetilde{W}^TL') over the full range of nuclear polarization angles for both in-plane (φ=0°) and out-of-plane (φ=90°) proton emission kinematics.

## Key numbers

- Momentum transfer fixed to q = 500 MeV/c and energy transfer to ω = 133.5 MeV (quasielastic peak), with electron scattering angle θ_e = 30°
- Leptonic factors: v_T' = 0.268 and v_TL' = -0.176
- Kinetic energy of ejected proton: 124.8 MeV; proton momentum: p' = 499.7 MeV/c
- The HD cross section for the two nodal polarizations (θ*=90°, Δφ=90° and θ*=90°, Δφ=270°) is of order 10⁻⁷–10⁻⁶ in the units of the figures, compatible with zero within numerical precision of the multipole expansion

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic investigation of spin observables in polarized (e,e'p) reactions (Amaro and Donnelly, 1998, 1999), which analyzed the unpolarized-electron case and found that nuclear transparency depends strongly on polarization direction. The present study adds polarized electrons and focuses on the helicity-dependent cross section, revealing a nodal symmetry that explains why the fifth response function (involving polarized electrons but unpolarized nuclei) is zero for in-plane kinematics. The results are interpreted using a semi-classical model based on the PWIA and the concept of a nucleon orbit with its expectation values of position and spin, providing geometrical insight into the reaction mechanism. This work enables future experiments with polarized targets to separate the T' and TL' response functions by exploiting their opposite parity under the nodal symmetry.
