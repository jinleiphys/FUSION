# The N to Delta electromagnetic transition form factors from Lattice QCD

> The magnetic dipole, the electric quadrupole and the Coulomb quadrupole amplitudes for the transition γN\to Δare calculated in quenched lattice QCD at β=6.0 with Wilson fermions. Using a new method combining an optimal combination of interpolating fields for the $Δ$ and an overconstrained analysis, we obtain statistically accurate results for the dipole form factor and for the ratios of the electric and Coulomb quadrupole amplitudes to the magnetic dipole amplitude, R_{EM} and R_{SM}, up to momentum transfer squared 1.5 GeV^2. We show for the first time using lattice QCD that both R_{EM} and R_{SM} are non-zero and negative, in qualitative agreement with experiment and indicating the presence of deformation in the N- Delta system.

## Key claim
Using quenched lattice QCD with a novel combination of optimized interpolating fields for the Delta and an overconstrained analysis, the authors show for the first time that both the electric quadrupole to magnetic dipole ratio (R_EM) and the Coulomb quadrupole to magnetic dipole ratio (R_SM) are non-zero and negative, in qualitative agreement with experiment. The results are obtained at a single lattice spacing and with quenched (no sea quark) approximations, introducing systematic uncertainties from the chiral extrapolation and the omission of dynamical fermions.

## Method
The N to Delta electromagnetic transition form factors were computed in quenched lattice QCD on a 32^3 x 64 lattice at beta=6.0 using Wilson fermions at three values of the hopping parameter (kappa = 0.1554, 0.1558, 0.1562). Three-point functions were evaluated using the fixed-sink method with the Delta at rest, and two novel techniques were employed: (1) optimized sink combinations (S1, S2, S3) that allow momentum transfers in all spatial directions symmetrically, and (2) an overconstrained analysis using singular value decomposition to extract the three Sachs form factors from all lattice momentum vectors contributing to a given Q^2 value.

## Key numbers
- Lattice size: 32^3 x 64 at beta = 6.0, with inverse lattice spacing a^{-1} = 2.04(2) GeV (a = 0.098 fm) set by the nucleon mass at the chiral limit
- Three hopping parameters used: kappa = 0.1554, 0.1558, and 0.1562, giving pion-to-rho mass ratios m_pi/m_rho = 0.64, 0.59, and 0.50 respectively
- Results obtained up to momentum transfer squared Q^2 = 1.5 GeV^2
- 200 configurations were used for the analysis
- The Ash form factor G_M1* at the chiral limit yields a value at Q^2 = 0 consistent with the Particle Data Group result
- Both R_EM and R_SM are negative and non-zero at low Q^2, with R_EM in agreement with experimental measurements from Bates and Jefferson Lab (CLAS) and R_SM showing a discrepancy at low Q^2

## Context
This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of the N to Delta transition (Leinweber, 1990s; Paper1) by introducing optimized sink methods and overconstrained analysis to achieve statistically accurate results for the quadrupole form factors. It also builds on earlier studies showing deformation in the rho meson and Delta via density correlators (Alexandrou et al., 2003). The results enable future investigations of sea quark contributions to the electric and Coulomb quadrupole ratios, which could clarify the physical mechanism responsible for non-zero quadrupole strength in the N to Delta transition and resolve the apparent discrepancy between quenched lattice results and experiment for CMR at low Q^2.
