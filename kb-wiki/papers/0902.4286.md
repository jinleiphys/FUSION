# Connecting the Breit Frame to the Infinite Momentum Light Front Frame: How G_E turns into F_1

> We investigate the connection between the Breit and infinite momentum frames and show that when the nucleon matrix element of the time component of the electromagnetic current, which yields G_E in the Breit frame, is boosted to the infinite momentum (or light-front) frame, the quantity F_1 is obtained.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a Breit frame matrix element of the time component of the electromagnetic current \(J^0\) that yields the Sachs electric form factor \(G_E\) is converted, via a boost to the infinite momentum (light-front) frame, into a matrix element of \(J^+\) that yields the Dirac form factor \(F_1\); this connection holds for a specific choice of initial and final spinor coefficients that correspond to light-cone spinors in the Breit frame.

## Method

The authors generalize the Breit frame formalism by allowing arbitrary spin directions for initial and final nucleon states, parameterized by coefficients \(a,b,c,d\). They choose a specific set of coefficients that, for large momentum transfer, correspond to spins in the \(-z\) and \(+z\) directions. They then boost these Breit frame spinors along the \(z\) direction to a new frame (Frame B) and decompose them into light-cone spinors. Finally, they evaluate the matrix element of \(J^+ = J^0 + J^3\) in Frame B, which in the infinite momentum frame limit yields \(F_1\).

## Key numbers

- The Breit frame matrix element of \(J^0\) with the chosen coefficients yields \(\langle f_A | J^0(0) | i_A \rangle = \frac{2M^2}{E_0} G_E(Q^2)\), where \(E_0 = \sqrt{p_0^2 + M^2}\) and \(Q^2 = 4p_0^2\).
- In the boosted frame (Frame B), the matrix element of \(J^+\) is \(\langle f_B | J^+(0) | i_B \rangle = 2 p_B^+ F_1(Q^2)\), where \(p_B^+ = p_B^0 + p_B^3\) and this relation is independent of the boost parameter \(p_z\).
- For helicity non-flip matrix elements in the infinite momentum frame, the corresponding Breit frame matrix elements include \(\langle f'_A | J^0 | i'_A \rangle = 2M \frac{M}{E_0} G_E\) and \(\langle f'_A | J^2 | i'_A \rangle = -i Q G_M\) with \(Q = 2p_0\).
- For helicity flip matrix elements in the infinite momentum frame, the corresponding Breit frame matrix elements include \(\langle f''_A | J^0 | i''_A \rangle = -M \frac{Q}{E_0} G_E\) and \(\langle f''_A | J^3 | i''_A \rangle = M \frac{Q}{E_0} G_M\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Sachs (1962) connecting Breit frame matrix elements to \(G_E\) and the infinite momentum frame analysis of Soper (1977), Burkardt (2000), and Diehl (2002) that yields transverse densities as two-dimensional Fourier transforms of \(F_1\). The paper resolves a conceptual puzzle: the central value of the neutron's transverse charge density is negative when extracted from \(F_1\) in the infinite momentum frame (Miller, 2007), which appears to contradict the long-held notion that the neutron's center is positively charged. The result establishes a formal connection between the two frames and enables future work to determine whether a connection between the rest frame charge density and the transverse density can be established.
