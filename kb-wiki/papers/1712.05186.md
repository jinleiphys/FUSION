# Matter distribution and spin-orbit force in spherical nuclei

> We investigate the possibility that some nuclei show density distributions with a depletion in the center, a semi-bubble structure, by using a Hartree-Fock plus Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer approach. We separately study the proton, neutron and matter distributions in 37 spherical nuclei mainly in the $s - d$ shell region. We found a relation between the semi-bubble structure and the energy splitting of spin-orbit partner single particle levels. The presence of semi-bubble structure reduces this splitting, and we study its consequences on the excitation spectrum of the nuclei under investigation by using a quasi-particle random-phase-approximation approach. The excitation energies of the low-lying $4^+$ states can be related to the presence of semi-bubble structure in nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors investigate whether semi-bubble (SB) structures in nuclear density distributions can be identified through their effect on the spin-orbit splitting of single-particle levels, rather than through direct electron scattering measurements, which are difficult for unstable nuclei. The relationship is conditional: the presence of a SB structure reduces the energy splitting of spin-orbit partner levels, and this reduction may be observable in the excitation spectrum of low-lying states.

## Method

The authors used a Hartree-Fock plus Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (HF+BCS) approach to describe the ground states of 37 spherical nuclei mainly in the s-d shell region, with four different Gogny-type finite-range interactions (D1S, D1M, D1ST2a, D1MTd), the latter two including tensor terms. Excited states were described using a Quasi-particle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) approach, with the same effective interaction used consistently in all three steps. The depletion fraction and a flatness index were calculated for proton, neutron, and matter density distributions to identify SB structures.

## Key numbers

- 37 spherical nuclei were investigated, with even Z values between 8 and 26
- Binding energies per nucleon were calculated with average relative differences of about 2% for HF and reduced to about 1% after including BCS pairing
- The maximum relative deviation of calculated charge rms radii from experimental data was around 3%
- For the toy calculation on 40Ca, the Woods-Saxon potential parameters were V0 = -50 MeV, R = 4.4 fm, a = 0.6 fm, b = 0.5 fm, with B = 0 and B = 140 MeV used to generate SB and non-SB proton density profiles
- The depletion fraction for matter distribution was zero for all nuclei studied except oxygen isotopes with A less than 24
- The flatness index for matter distribution had absolute values of at most about 0.2 for all nuclei investigated

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of bubble and semi-bubble structures in nuclei, which have been studied using various nuclear models by authors including Wong (1972), Dechargé et al. (2003), Grasso et al. (2009), and others. The study relates to earlier work by Burrows et al. (2011, 2014) and Mutschler et al. (2015, 2017) on using transfer reactions and gamma-ray detection to observe spin-orbit splitting effects. The results suggest that comparing spectra of isotopic or isotonic nuclei may allow identification of SB structures in proton or neutron densities, and that future facilities like ELISe at FAIR and SCRIT at RIKEN will enable direct electron scattering experiments on unstable nuclei to test these predictions.
