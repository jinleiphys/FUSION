# The nucleon recoil effect in antikaon-deuteron scattering at threshold

> We investigate the role of the nucleon recoil for antikaon-deuteron scattering using EFT. In particular, we show that the leading correction to the static term that appears at order $ξ^{1/2}$ (where $ξ=m_K/M_N$) cancels completely in the double scattering process. We calculate the higher order recoil corrections and study the pattern of convergence of the expansion method. We find that the recoil correction to the double scattering process is of order of 10-15% of the static contribution.

## Key claim

The leading nucleon-recoil correction to the static (fixed-center) term in antikaon-deuteron scattering, which appears at order ξ^(1/2) (where ξ = m_K/M_N), cancels completely in the double-scattering process for both isoscalar and isovector antikaon-nucleon interactions. The net recoil correction to the double-scattering contribution is therefore only of order 10–15% of the static contribution, despite the large value of ξ ≈ 0.5.

## Method

The authors use non-relativistic effective field theory (EFT) to compute the antikaon-deuteron scattering amplitude at threshold, treating the antikaon-nucleon interactions as point-like and the nucleon-nucleon interactions via separable potentials. They apply a perturbative uniform expansion method (Mohr, 2006; Beneke, 1998) to the Feynman diagrams for double scattering, expanding the amplitude in half-integer powers of ξ = m_K/M_N. The integrals are evaluated analytically using a Hulthen deuteron wave function (neglecting the D-wave component) and a separable NN interaction, and the expansion is carried out separately in the three kinematic regimes: the three-body singularity region (small loop momentum l), the heavy-baryon region (large l), and the intermediate region.

## Key numbers

- The parameter controlling the nucleon recoil is ξ = m_K/M_N ≈ 0.5.
- The leading recoil correction at order ξ^(1/2) cancels exactly for both isoscalar and isovector antikaon-nucleon interactions in the double-scattering process.
- The net recoil correction to the double-scattering amplitude is 10–15% of the static (fixed-center) contribution.
- For the isovector case, the expansion in ξ converges such that including terms up to order ξ^2 captures the bulk of the effect, and including terms up to order ξ^4 provides a very good approximation to the full result.

## Context

This work extends the EFT treatment of meson-deuteron scattering previously applied to pion-deuteron scattering (Baru et al., 2008; Lensky et al., 2007) and to the reaction γd → π⁺ nn (Lensky et al., 2007), where the cancellation of the leading √(m_π/M_N) correction was also observed. It directly addresses the validity of the fixed-center approximation (FCA) used in earlier multiple-scattering calculations for antikaon-deuteron scattering (Kamalov, 1990; Meißner et al., 2006). The results enable a reliable extraction of the isoscalar and isovector antikaon-nucleon scattering lengths from the combined analysis of kaonic hydrogen and kaonic deuterium data being measured by the DEAR/SIDDHARTA collaboration, by providing a controlled expansion for the recoil corrections that must be applied to the static multiple-scattering formula.
