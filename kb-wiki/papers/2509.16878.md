# Hypernuclear structure with the new leading order covariant chiral hyperon-nucleon force

> We have studied single-$Λ$ finite hypernuclear systems spanning from light to heavy masses, employing a new microscopic Lambda-N interaction derived from in-medium interactions within relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations using the leading-order covariant chiral hyperon-nucleon force. Without any adjustable parameters, we have successfully reproduced the experimental results for the single-Lambda binding energies. Although some discrepancies persist in light hypernuclei, the overall calculated binding energies show excellent agreement with the experimental data, and outperforming other microscopic interactions. This study further demonstrates the validity of the leading-order covariant chiral hyperon-nucleon potential and provides a practical set of microscopic interactions for the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock framework.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a new microscopic Lambda-N interaction, derived from relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations using the leading-order covariant chiral hyperon-nucleon force with no adjustable parameters, reproduces single-Lambda binding energies across light to heavy hypernuclei with overall excellent agreement, though some discrepancies persist in light hypernuclei.

## Method

The authors derived two parameter sets (RCh-LO550 and RCh-LO700) from in-medium Lambda-N interactions obtained via relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations using the leading-order covariant chiral hyperon-nucleon force at cutoffs of 550 MeV and 700 MeV. These interactions were implemented within the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock framework with Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer pairing correlations for nucleons, using the SLy4 parameterization for the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The Skyrme-Hartree-Fock equations were solved self-consistently in an axially symmetric coordinate space representation, and binding energies were calculated for 29 hypernuclei from light He-5 to heavy Pb-208.

## Key numbers

- The RCh-LO700 interaction yields a root-mean-square deviation of 0.71 MeV and a relative square deviation of 0.08 when compared to experimental single-Lambda binding energies across 29 hypernuclei, outperforming NSC89 (1.44 MeV, 0.12), NSC97f (1.02 MeV, 0.13), NSC97a (4.00 MeV, 0.47), and ESC08 (5.35 MeV, 0.40)
- At nucleon saturation density (0.17 fm^-3), the effective mass ratio m*_Lambda/m_Lambda is 0.750 for RCh-LO550 and 0.642 for RCh-LO700, with the RCh-LO700 value close to the empirical value of 0.780
- The central potential depth in Pb-208 for RCh-LO700 is -29.55 MeV, consistent with typical phenomenological potentials
- The mean-field depth in O-16 for RCh-LO700 is 25.54 MeV, close to the NSC89 value of 25.86 MeV
- The RCh-LO550 interaction produces a mean-field depth of only -21.81 MeV at the center of O-16, significantly shallower than the empirical Woods-Saxon potential

## Context

This work extends the leading-order covariant chiral hyperon-nucleon force, previously applied only to nuclear matter by Zheng (2025), to finite hypernuclear systems for the first time. It builds on prior efforts to derive microscopic in-medium interactions from bare potentials using the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock method, including the Nijmegen soft-core potentials NSC89, NSC97a, NSC97f, and ESC08, which were previously implemented in the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock framework by various authors. The RCh-LO700 interaction achieves the best overall agreement with experimental binding energies among all compared microscopic interactions, while the RCh-LO550 interaction systematically underbinds hypernuclei due to weaker attraction. This work provides a practical set of microscopic interactions for the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock framework and further validates the covariant chiral approach, while also highlighting that light hypernuclei remain an area requiring additional theoretical development.
