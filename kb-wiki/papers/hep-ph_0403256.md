# Hard-Loop Effective Action for Anisotropic Plasmas

> We generalize the hard-thermal-loop effective action of the equilibrium quark-gluon plasma to a non-equilibrium system which is space-time homogeneous but for which the parton momentum distribution is anisotropic. We show that the manifestly gauge-invariant Braaten-Pisarski form of the effective action can be straightforwardly generalized and we verify that it then generates all n-point functions following from collisionless gauge-covariant transport theory for a homogeneous anisotropic plasma. On the other hand, the Taylor-Wong form of the hard-thermal-loop effective action has a more complicated generalization to the anisotropic case. Already in the simplest case of anisotropic distribution functions, it involves an additional term that is gauge invariant by itself, but nontrivial also in the static limit.

## Key claim

The Braaten-Pisarski form of the hard-thermal-loop effective action generalizes straightforwardly to homogeneous but anisotropic plasmas, while the Taylor-Wong form requires an additional gauge-invariant term that is nontrivial in the static limit. The generalization holds only for systems that are space-time homogeneous but have anisotropic parton momentum distributions.

## Method

The authors generalized the manifestly gauge-invariant Braaten-Pisarski effective action by replacing the isotropic parton distribution with an anisotropic one, keeping the same operator structure but with the distribution function depending on three-momentum direction. They verified equivalence with collisionless gauge-covariant transport theory by showing that the induced current derived from the effective action matches that from kinetic theory, provided the distribution functions are x-independent. They then derived explicit expressions for the quark-gluon, triple-gluon, and four-gluon vertex functions by expanding the effective action to appropriate orders in the fields.

- In equilibrium, the quark mass parameter is m_q² = (C_F/8) g² (T² + μ²/π²)

## Context

This work extends the hard-thermal-loop effective action developed by Braaten and Pisarski (1991) and the Taylor-Wong form (Taylor, 1990; Wong, 1990) from equilibrium quark-gluon plasmas to anisotropic non-equilibrium systems relevant for early stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The anisotropic gluon polarization tensor had been derived previously by Mrowczynski (2000) and Romatschke (2003) using semiclassical transport theory and diagrammatic methods. The results enable calculation of higher n-point functions needed to study nonlinear saturation of plasma instabilities, which may drive faster thermalization in heavy-ion collisions.
