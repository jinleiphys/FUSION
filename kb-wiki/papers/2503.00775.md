# Investigation of Gamow-Teller strength of 186Hg within deformed pn-QRPA

> Recently the the total absorption gamma spectroscopy technique was used to determine the Gamow Teller (GT) distribution of \b{eta} decay of 186Hg. It was concluded that the best description of the measured data was obtained with dominantly prolate components for both parent 186Hg and daughter 186Au. Motivated by the recent findings, we investigate the effect of nuclear deformation on the energy distribution of the GT strength of the decay of 186Hg into 186Au within the framework of pn QRPA based on the deformed Nilsson potential. To do the needful, we first calculate the energy levels and shape prediction of 186Hg within the interacting boson model. The computed GT strength distribution satisfied the model independent Ikeda sum rule 100 % (99.98 %) for the prolate (oblate) case. Based on the strength distributions, the deformed pn QRPA model with separable interaction prefers a prolate shape for the ground state of 186Hg and supports the shape coexistence for this nucleus.

## Key claim
The deformed pn-QRPA model with separable interaction prefers a prolate shape for the ground state of 186Hg and supports shape coexistence for this nucleus, but the computed half-life for the prolate case (75.7 s) deviates from the experimental value (82.3 s) by about 8%, while the oblate case deviates by 34%.

## Method
The authors employed the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) based on the deformed Nilsson potential to compute Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions for the beta decay of 186Hg into 186Au. They first calculated energy levels and shape predictions for 186Hg using the interacting boson model-1 (IBM-1), then used three deformation parameters: spherical (β=0.000 from IBM-1), oblate (β=-0.146 from the FRDM approach of Moller (2016)), and prolate (β=+0.260 from the Skyrme-based QRPA calculation of Boillos (2015)). The residual interaction constants χ and κ were adopted from Homma (1996) following a 1/A^0.7 relation.

## Key numbers
- The computed GT strength distribution satisfied the Ikeda sum rule 100% for the prolate case, 99.98% for the oblate case, and 99.48% for the spherical case.
- The calculated half-lives for 186Hg were 78.9 s (spherical), 54.7 s (oblate), and 75.7 s (prolate), compared to the experimental value of 82.3 s from Algora (2021).
- The total B(GT+) within the Q_EC window (3.176 MeV from Audi (2017)) was 1.29 (spherical), 1.15 (oblate), and 0.99 (prolate).
- The total B(GT+) across the whole energy range was 13.45 (spherical), 16.02 (oblate), and 14.39 (prolate); total B(GT-) was 91.05, 94.03, and 92.39 respectively.

## Context
This work extends the recent total absorption gamma spectroscopy measurement by Algora (2021), which determined the GT distribution of 186Hg decay and concluded that dominantly prolate components for both parent and daughter best describe the data. The authors compare their results with the self-consistent deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock mean field calculation of Moreno (2006) and the SLy4 Skyrme-force QRPA calculation of Boillos (2015). The study enables further investigation of shape coexistence in neutron-deficient mercury isotopes, particularly the nature of the ground and excited 0+ states of 186Hg, which previous work by Bree (2014) could not definitively determine.
