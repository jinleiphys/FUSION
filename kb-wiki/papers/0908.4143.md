# Coulomb-assisted $\bmОЈ^-$-nucleus bound states in the ($K^-$, $ПЂ^+$) reaction

> We study a production of Coulomb-assisted $ОЈ^-$-nucleus bound states by nuclear ($K^-$, $ПЂ^+$) reactions within a distorted-wave impulse approximation, so as to examine several types of the $ОЈ$-nucleus potentials that are consistent with the available $ОЈ^-$ atomic X-ray data and nuclear ($ПЂ^-$, $K^+$) data. We theoretically demonstrate the inclusive ($K^-$, $ПЂ^+$) spectra of the $ОЈ^-$ unstable bound states on $^{28}$Si, $^{58}$Ni, and $^{208}$Pb targets at incident $K^-$ lab momenta $p_{K}= 400\text{--}800$ MeV/c. The results show that the near-recoilless ($K^-$, $ПЂ^+$) reaction on the $^{58}$Ni target gives a clear candidate to confirm properties of the $ОЈ$-nucleus potentials having a repulsion inside the nuclear surface and an attraction outside the nucleus with a sizable absorption, whereas details of the repulsion of the potential at the nuclear center cannot be determined by the inclusive spectra. This is a promising attempt to extract properties of the $ОЈ$-nucleus potential in the nucleus at forthcoming J-PARC experiments, as a full complement to the analyses of the $ОЈ^-$ atomic and ($ПЂ^-$, $K^+$) data.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the near-recoilless ($K^-$, $\pi^+$) reaction on a $^{58}$Ni target provides a clear candidate to confirm properties of $\Sigma$-nucleus potentials having a repulsion inside the nuclear surface and an attraction outside the nucleus with sizable absorption, but details of the repulsion at the nuclear center cannot be determined from inclusive spectra alone.

## Method

The authors performed distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations of inclusive ($K^-$, $\pi^+$) spectra for $\Sigma^-$ unstable bound states on $^{28}$Si, $^{58}$Ni, and $^{208}$Pb targets at incident $K^-$ lab momenta $p_K = 400$вЂ“$800$ MeV/c and $\theta_{\rm lab}=5^\circ$. They employed a Green's function technique to evaluate the inclusive spectra, using several types of $\Sigma$-nucleus potentials (DD, LDA-NF, and $t_{\rm eff}\rho$) that are consistent with available $\Sigma^-$ atomic X-ray data and nuclear ($\pi^-$, $K^+$) data. The Fermi-averaging of the $K^- + p \to \pi^+ + \Sigma^-$ elementary amplitudes was performed to account for nucleon Fermi motion in the nuclear medium.

## Key numbers

- For the DD potential on $\Sigma^-$вЂ“$^{57}$Co, the $(1s)_\Sigma$ state has binding energy $B_{\Sigma^-} = 10.5$ MeV, width $\Gamma_\Sigma = 1.89$ MeV, and rms radius $\langle r^2 \rangle^{1/2}_{1s} = 9.1$ fm; the $(1p)_\Sigma$ state has $\Gamma_\Sigma = 1.43$ MeV and $\langle r^2 \rangle^{1/2}_{1s} = 9.8$ fm.
- For $\Sigma^-$вЂ“$^{27}$Al with the DD potential, the $(1s)_\Sigma$ state has $\Gamma_\Sigma = 1.25$ MeV and $\langle r^2 \rangle^{1/2}_{1s} = 9.8$ fm.
- For $\Sigma^-$вЂ“$^{207}$Tl with the DD potential, the $(1s)_\Sigma$ state has $B_{\Sigma^-} = 10.5$ MeV, $\Gamma_\Sigma = 1.7$ MeV, and $\langle r^2 \rangle^{1/2}_{1s} = 9.8$ fm, compared to $B_{\Sigma^-} = 18.8$ MeV and $\langle r^2 \rangle^{1/2}_{1s} = 4.2$ fm for the pure Coulomb $(1s)_\Sigma$ state.
- The $\Sigma^-$ atomic $1s$ state rms radius for a $^{57}$Co core-nucleus is $\langle r^2 \rangle^{1/2}_{1s} = 4.2$ fm, where the nuclear radius is $R = 1.1 A^{1/3} = 4.23$ fm.
- The repulsion in the DD potential at the nuclear center is approximately 80 MeV, which is 2вЂ“3 times larger than that in LDA-NF or RMF (approximately 40 MeV).

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of $\Sigma$-nucleus potentials using $\Sigma^-$ atomic X-ray data (Friedman, 2007) and nuclear ($\pi^-$, $K^+$) data from KEK-E438 experiments (Noumi, 2002; Saha, 2004). The authors had previously succeeded in explaining the $^{28}$Si($\pi^-$, $K^+$) data and $\Sigma^-$ atomic X-ray data simultaneously using $\Sigma$-nucleus potentials with repulsion inside the nuclear surface (Harada, 2005). The present study enables forthcoming J-PARC experiments to extract properties of the $\Sigma$-nucleus potential, complementing the analyses of $\Sigma^-$ atomic and ($\pi^-$, $K^+$) data.
