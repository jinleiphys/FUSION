# Systematic Trends of 0$^+_2$, 1$^-_1$, 3$^-_1$ and 2$^+_1$ Excited States in Even-Even Nuclei

> The spin and parity ($J^π$) assignments in even-even nuclei were reviewed across the nuclear chart using the Evaluated Nuclear Structure Data File (ENSDF). The prevalence of 2$^+_1$ first or lowest excited states is confirmed. The properties of 0$^+_2$, 1$^-_1$, and 3$^-_1$ lowest excited states were reexamined using the ENSDF data evaluation procedures. The $J^π$ systematic trends and correlations between level quantum numbers and nuclear physics phenomena are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that 2⁺₁ is the dominant first excited state in even-even nuclei (96% of cases), but the systematic trends for 0⁺₂, 1⁻₁, and 3⁻₁ states have not been revisited since the 1970s, and the authors reexamine these using current ENSDF data to identify where non-2⁺ first excited states occur, specifically near shell or subshell closures or in self-conjugate nuclei.

## Method

The authors surveyed the ENSDF relational database using SQL queries at the IAEA Nuclear Data Section to extract spin and parity assignments for first excited states in even-even nuclei across the nuclear chart. They critically reexamined all available experimental data for non-2⁺ low-lying states (0⁺₂, 1⁻₁, 3⁻₁) using standard ENSDF library evaluation procedures, and adopted 2⁺ data from ENSDF and a dedicated horizontal evaluation of B(E2↑) from Pri16.

## Key numbers

- 631 out of 657 even-even nuclei (96.0%) have a 2⁺₁ first excited state
- 20 nuclei (3%) have a 0⁺₂ first excited state
- 2 nuclei (0.3%) have a 1⁻₁ first excited state
- 2 nuclei (0.3%) have a 3⁻₁ first excited state
- The E4⁺₁/E2⁺₁ ratio for non-2⁺ states ranges from 1.058 to 2.072, lying below the vibrational nuclei range of 2-2.2
- The 3⁻₁ state in ²⁰⁸Pb has an energy of 2614.511(10) keV and is tied to a large octupole collectivity of 34 Weisskopf units (W.u.)
- The 0⁺₂ state in ⁹⁶Zr has an energy of 1581.4 keV, and ⁹⁶Zr has the lowest quadrupole deformation parameter (β₂) of 0.0615(33) among zirconium nuclei

## Context

This work extends the historic compilations of first excited state spin and parity trends by Scharff-Goldhaber (1953) and others from the 1970s, bridging a gap of approximately 50 years in systematic assessments. The findings confirm the prevalence of 2⁺₁ states noted by Scharff-Goldhaber (1953) and provide updated tables of 0⁺₂, 1⁻₁, and 3⁻₁ states that occur near shell or subshell closures or in self-conjugate nuclei. The results enable further theoretical work on shape coexistence, as seen in the zirconium isotopes where intruder bands and low-lying 0⁺₂ states point to the limits of applicability for deformation parameters, and support the Beliaev-Zelevinsky (1962) hypothesis that formally-calculated β₂ values below 0.1 are too low for actual static deformation.
