# Vacuum quark condensate, chiral Lagrangian, and Bose-Einstein statistics

> In a series of articles it was recently claimed that the quantum chromodynamic (QCD) condensates are not the properties of the vacuum but of the hadrons and are confined inside them. We point out that this claim is incompatible with the chiral Lagrangian and Bose-Einstein statistics of the Goldstone bosons (pions) in chiral limit and conclude that the quark condensate must be the property of the QCD vacuum.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the claim that QCD condensates are confined inside hadrons (leaving the vacuum empty) is incompatible with the chiral Lagrangian and Bose-Einstein statistics of pions in the chiral limit, and therefore the quark condensate must be a property of the QCD vacuum.

## Method

The author derives the vacuum quark condensate from the chiral Lagrangian for pions under two-flavor chiral symmetry, showing that Dashen's vacuum alignment yields a nonvanishing vacuum condensate. The paper then demonstrates that the pion-to-vacuum transition amplitude used by proponents of in-hadron condensates is a consequence of chiral symmetry breaking and does not imply vanishing vacuum condensate. Finally, the author argues that if pions had random in-hadron condensates they would violate Bose-Einstein statistics, while if they all had the same condensate it would have to be imposed ad hoc.

## Key numbers

- The Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation is given as \( m_\pi^2 = \frac{2\alpha}{f_\pi^2}(m_u+m_d) \), where \(\alpha = \sqrt{\mathrm{Tr}(\langle \bar q_L q_R\rangle \langle \bar q_R q_L\rangle)/2}\) and \(f_\pi\) is the pion decay constant.
- The pion-to-vacuum transition amplitude is derived as \(\langle 0|\bar q_L \sigma^a q_R|\pi^a\rangle = i\frac{3}{f_\pi}\langle \bar q_L q_R\rangle\), showing the vacuum condensate appears explicitly.

## Context

This paper directly critiques the series of articles by Brodsky and collaborators (2008-2012) that claimed QCD condensates are confined inside hadrons and the vacuum is empty. It extends the standard chiral Lagrangian framework of Gell-Mann, Oakes, and Renner (1968) and Dashen's vacuum alignment (1971) to refute this claim. The argument enables a defense of the conventional understanding of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking in QCD and counters the suggestion that vanishing vacuum condensates could solve the cosmological constant problem.
