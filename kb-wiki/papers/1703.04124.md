# Topology and stability of the Kondo phase in quark matter

> We investigate properties of the ground state of a light quark matter with heavy quark impurities. This system exhibits the "QCD Kondo effect" where the interaction strength between a light quark near the Fermi surface and a heavy quark increases with decreasing energy of the light quark towards the Fermi energy, and diverges at some scale near the Fermi energy, called the Kondo scale. Around and below the Kondo scale, we must treat the dynamics nonperturbatively. As a typical nonperturbative method to treat the strong coupling regime, we adopt a mean-field approach where we introduce a condensate, the Kondo condensate, representing a mixing between a light quark and a heavy quark, and determine the ground state in the presence of the Kondo condensate. We show that the ground state is a topologically non-trivial state and the heavy quark spin forms the hedgehog configuration in the momentum space. We can define the Berry phase for the ground-state wavefunction in the momentum space which is associated with a monopole at the position of a heavy quark. We also investigate fluctuations around the mean field in the random-phase approximation, and show the existence of (exciton-like) collective excitations made of a hole $h$ of a light quark and a heavy quark $Q$.

## Key claim

The ground state of light quark matter with heavy quark impurities, treated via a mean-field approach with a Kondo condensate, is topologically non-trivial: the heavy quark spin forms a hedgehog configuration in momentum space, and a Berry phase associated with a monopole can be defined for the ground-state wavefunction.

## Method

The authors adopt a mean-field approximation for the color-current-current four-Fermi interaction between light and heavy quarks, introducing a Kondo condensate as the expectation value of a mixing operator between a light quark and a heavy quark. They solve the gap equation to determine the ground state and analyze fluctuations around the mean field using the random-phase approximation (RPA) to study collective excitations. The heavy quark is treated within the heavy quark effective theory (HQET) in the static frame, and the heavy quark number density is treated as a constant after averaging over random impurity configurations.

## Key numbers

* The cutoff used in numerical calculations is \(\Lambda = 0.65\) GeV, with the coupling constant set to \(G_c \Lambda^2 = (9/2) \times 4.0\).
* The heavy quark masses are \(m_c = 1.27\) GeV for charm and \(m_b = 4.18\) GeV for bottom, ensuring a hierarchy \(\mu \ll m_c, m_b\) for the light quark chemical potential \(\mu\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous mean-field analysis of the QCD Kondo effect (Yasui 2016) by providing a detailed study of the topological properties of the ground state and the stability of the mean field under quantum fluctuations. It builds on earlier perturbative and renormalization-group analyses of the QCD Kondo effect (Yasui 2013, Hattori 2015) and on the application of conformal field theory to a single heavy impurity (Kimura 2016). The results enable future studies of collective excitations (hQ bound states analogous to excitons) and the topological structure of the Kondo phase in quark matter.
