# Identifying the QCD Phase Transitions via the Gravitational Wave Frequency from Supernova Explosion

> We investigate the non-radial oscillations of newly born neutron stars (NSs) and strange quark stars (SQSs). This is done with the relativistic nuclear field theory with hyperon degrees of freedom employed to describe the equation of state for the stellar matter in NSs, and with both the MIT bag model and the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model adopted to construct the configurations of the SQSs. We find that the gravitational-mode ($g$-mode) eigenfrequencies of newly born SQSs are significantly lower than those of NSs, which is independent of models implemented to describe the equation of state for the strange quark matter. Meanwhile, the eigenfrequencies of the other modes of non-radial oscillations, e.g., fundamental ($f$)- and pressure ($p$)-modes, are much larger than those of the $g$-mode, and is related to the stiffness of the equation of states (EoSs). In the light of the first direct observation of gravitational waves, it is promising to employ the gravitational waves to identify the QCD phase transition in high density strong interaction matter.

## Key claim

The gravitational-mode (g-mode) eigenfrequencies of newly born strange quark stars (SQSs) are significantly lower than those of neutron stars (NSs), and this difference is independent of the specific model used to describe the equation of state for strange quark matter, making gravitational wave observations a promising tool to identify the QCD phase transition in high-density matter.

## Method

The authors solved Newtonian non-radial oscillation equations for non-rotating, unmagnetized fluid stars using the Cowling approximation, computing eigenfrequencies for g-mode, f-mode, and p-mode oscillations. For neutron stars, the equation of state was described using relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with hyperon degrees of freedom at finite temperature, including electrons, neutrinos, and photons; for strange quark stars, both the MIT bag model and the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model were employed to describe the quark matter equation of state.

## Key numbers

- For newly born NSs with a mass of 1.4 solar masses, the g-mode eigenfrequency is approximately 900 Hz (from Fig. 3 in the paper, though exact value depends on the specific model parameters)
- For newly born SQSs with a mass of 1.4 solar masses, the g-mode eigenfrequency is approximately 200-300 Hz (from Fig. 3, significantly lower than NS values)
- The f-mode eigenfrequencies for both NSs and SQSs are in the range of several kHz, much larger than g-mode frequencies
- The damping time through gravitational wave emission for g-mode oscillations is on the order of 10^4 to 10^5 seconds for NSs and SQSs (from Sec. V calculations)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Fu 2008) which only examined g-mode oscillations, by now including f-mode and p-mode oscillations for comparison, and by incorporating hyperon degrees of freedom in the NS equation of state. The study also extends prior work by using the NJL model (which includes dynamical chiral symmetry breaking) in addition to the MIT bag model for SQSs. The results suggest that gravitational wave observations from core-collapse supernovae could distinguish between NSs and SQSs, complementing other proposed observational differences such as mass measurements (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013), cooling rates (Schaab 1997), and spin rates (Madsen 1992).
