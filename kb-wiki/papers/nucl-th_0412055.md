# Nuclear stopping and flow in heavy ion collisions and the in-medium NN cross section

> We present transport calculations for heavy ion reactions in which the mean field and the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section are consistently based on the same effective interaction, i.e. the in-medium T-matrix from microscopic Dirac-Brueckner calculations. Doing so, the stopping in central reactions in terms of the recently proposed $var_{\rm tl}$ observable and the correlation to the behavior of the directed flow is investigated. The relation to the nuclear shear viscosity is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in-medium modifications of the nucleon-nucleon cross section, consistently derived from the same Dirac-Brueckner T-matrix as the mean field, are essential for reproducing experimental data on nuclear stopping and flow; the abstract's claim of "consistently based" is qualified by the fact that the mean field is treated in the Local Configuration Approximation while the in-medium cross section is not yet treated in the same fully consistent two-Fermi-sphere configuration.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) transport calculations for Au+Au collisions at intermediate energies, using mean fields and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections both derived from the same Dirac-Brueckner T-matrix based on the Bonn A one-boson-exchange potential. The in-medium cross sections incorporate suppression from both the effective mass scaling and Pauli blocking of intermediate states in the Bethe-Salpeter equation. The calculations were compared to FOPI collaboration data for the stopping observable var_tl and the scaled directed flow P_xdir^(0) over an energy range of 0.1 to 1.5 AGeV.

## Key numbers

- In-medium neutron-proton cross sections were calculated at Fermi momenta k_F = 0.0, 1.1, 1.34, 1.7 fm^{-1}, corresponding to densities rho ~ 0, 0.5, 1, 2 rho_0 (where rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} is nuclear matter saturation density)
- At larger laboratory energies, in-medium cross sections reach asymptotic values of 15-20 mb
- The stopping observable var_tl and directed flow show a broad maximum around 0.4-0.6 AGeV incident energy
- The shear viscosity of nuclear matter extracted from central Au+Au reactions shows a minimum at E_Lab = 0.25 AGeV, close to the maximum of var_tl
- The effective cross section (free cross section multiplied by Pauli blocking factors) shows a broad maximum at about 0.5 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Gaitanos (2001) that applied microscopic Dirac-Brueckner mean fields to heavy ion reactions but without consistent treatment of the in-medium cross section. The paper directly addresses the experimental findings of the FOPI collaboration (Reisdorf et al., 2004) who introduced the var_tl observable and observed a correlation between stopping and transverse flow. The results enable a more microscopic understanding of nuclear transport coefficients such as shear viscosity, as derived by Danielewicz (1984), and show that ideal one-fluid hydrodynamics fails at SIS energies while quantum effects like Pauli blocking are essential for describing nuclear stopping.
