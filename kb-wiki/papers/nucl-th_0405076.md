# Spin-one Color Superconductivity in Cold and Dense Quark Matter

> In this thesis, several color-superconducting phases where quarks of the same flavor form Cooper pairs are investigated. In these phases, a Cooper pair carries total spin one. A systematic classification of theoretically possible phases, discriminated by the color-spin structure of the order parameter and the respective symmetry breaking pattern, is presented. In the weak-coupling limit, i.e., for asymptotically high densities, a universal form of the QCD gap equation is derived, applicable to arbitrary color-superconducting phases. It is applied to several spin-one and spin-zero phases in order to determine their energy gaps and critical temperatures. In some of the spin-one phases the resulting gap function is anisotropic and has point or line nodes. It is shown that the phases with two different gaps violate the well-known BCS relation between the critical temperature and the zero-temperature gap. Moreover, the screening properties of color superconductors regarding gluons and photons are discussed. In particular, it turns out that, contrary to spin-zero color superconductors, spin-one color superconductors exhibit an electromagnetic Meissner effect. This property is proven by symmetry arguments as well as by an explicit calculation of the gluon and photon Meissner masses.

## Key claim

This thesis systematically classifies spin-one color-superconducting phases in cold dense quark matter, where Cooper pairs carry total spin one, and demonstrates that, contrary to spin-zero color superconductors, spin-one phases exhibit an electromagnetic Meissner effect, a property proven both by symmetry arguments and explicit calculation of gluon and photon Meissner masses.

## Method

The author derives a universal form of the QCD gap equation in the weak-coupling limit (asymptotically high densities) applicable to arbitrary color-superconducting phases, then applies it to several spin-one and spin-zero phases to determine energy gaps and critical temperatures. The classification is performed by analyzing the color-spin structure of the order parameter and the corresponding symmetry breaking patterns, and the screening properties of color superconductors regarding gluons and photons are computed via Meissner masses.

## Context

This work extends the study of color superconductivity beyond the well-known spin-zero phases (such as the 2SC and CFL phases) to spin-one pairing, providing a systematic classification that had been lacking. The finding that spin-one phases exhibit an electromagnetic Meissner effect, unlike spin-zero color superconductors, has implications for the magnetic properties of dense quark matter in neutron star interiors. The thesis enables future calculations of transport properties and astrophysical signatures of these exotic phases, building on prior work by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek on two-flavor and three-flavor color superconductivity.
