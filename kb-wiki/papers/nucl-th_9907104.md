# Rotating nuclei at extreme conditions: Cranked Relativistic Mean Field Description

> The cranked relativistic mean field (CRMF) theory is applied for the description of superdeformed (SD) rotational bands observed in $^{153}$Ho. The question of the structure of the so-called SD band in $^{154}$Er is also addressed and a brief overview of applications of CRMF theory to the description of rotating nuclei at extreme conditions is presented.

# Rotating nuclei at extreme conditions: Cranked Relativistic Mean Field Description

## Key claim

Cranked Relativistic Mean Field (CRMF) theory provides a good description of superdeformed (SD) rotational bands in $^{153}$Ho for bands 1 and 2, but a consistent single-particle interpretation for band 3 is not found; the band previously discussed as SD in $^{154}$Er is very likely less deformed than the $^{152}$Dy(1) band, contradicting the simple picture of a doubly magic core plus two additional protons.

## Method

CRMF theory, an extension of relativistic mean field (RMF) theory to the rotating frame, was applied without pairing to calculate dynamic moments of inertia $J^{(2)}$, effective alignments $i_{eff}$, and charge quadrupole moments for SD rotational bands in $^{153}$Ho and $^{154}$Er. Calculations used the NL1 force and compared configurations differing in the occupation of proton orbitals above the $Z=66$ SD shell gap, with neutron orbitals kept as in the $^{152}$Dy SD core.

## Key numbers

- The lowest transition energies in $^{153}$Ho bands are 651.3 keV (band 1, spin change $29.5^- \rightarrow 27.5^-$), 713.0 keV (band 2, $30.5^- \rightarrow 28.5^-$), and 657.0 keV (band 3, $28.5 \rightarrow 26.5$); the $^{152}$Dy(1) reference band has a lowest transition of 602.4 keV ($26^+ \rightarrow 24^+$).
- Occupation of the $\pi [530]1/2(r=+i)$ orbital increases $Q_0$ by 0.60 $e$b, the $\pi [530]1/2(r=-i)$ orbital by 0.65 $e$b (both at $\Omega_x=0.5$ MeV), and the $\pi [770]1/2(r=+i)$ orbital by 1.15 $e$b (at $\Omega_x=0.8$ MeV).
- Band 3 shows a loss in effective alignment $i_{eff}$ of approximately $0.8\hbar$ in the $\Omega_x=0.51-0.68$ MeV range.
- For $^{154}$Er, the effective alignment in the $^{152}$Dy(1)/$^{154}$Er(1) pair drops by approximately $2.1\hbar$ in the frequency range $\Omega_x=0.37-0.65$ MeV.
- A highly-deformed triaxial minimum in $^{154}$Er exists with $Q_0 \sim 10$ $e$b and $\gamma \sim 9^{\circ}$.

## Context

This work extends systematic CRMF studies of SD bands in the $A\sim 140-150$ mass region (Koning and Ring 1993, Afanasjev et al. 1996, Afanasjev et al. 1998) and addresses discrepancies found for the $^{154}$Er band reported by the Er154 collaboration. The results enable future tests of CRMF theory through precise measurements of charge quadrupole moments in $^{153}$Ho and transition quadrupole moments in $^{154}$Er, and highlight the need for improved treatment of pairing correlations and triaxiality in relativistic mean field approaches.
