# Pairing correlations and eigenvalues of two-body density matrix in atomic nuclei

> The quantum phases of superconductivity and superfluidity are characterised mathematically by the Yang concept of Off-Diagonal Long Range Order (ODLRO), related to the existence of a large eigenvalue of the density matrix. We analyse how the Yang criterion applies for various Hamiltonians commonly employed to describe superfluid-type correlations in atomic nuclei. For like-particle pairing Hamiltonians the behaviour of the largest eigenvalue of two-body density matrix shows a clear evidence for a transition between a normal to a paired phase. However, this is not the case for the isoscalar proton-neutron pairing interactions acting in self-conjugate nuclei.

## Key claim

For like-particle pairing Hamiltonians, the largest eigenvalue of the two-body density matrix shows clear evidence of a transition from a normal to a paired phase, but for isoscalar proton-neutron pairing interactions in self-conjugate nuclei this is not the case; the sudden increase of the largest eigenvalue with interaction strength for isoscalar pairing is not related to a transition towards a paired phase.

## Method

The authors calculated the eigenvalues of the reduced two-body density matrix for the ground states of various pairing Hamiltonians commonly used in nuclear physics. For like-particle pairing, they used exact Richardson solutions, BCS, and particle-number projected BCS (PBCS) approximations for systems including a picket-fence model and realistic nuclei (¹¹⁰Sn, ²⁴O). For proton-neutron pairing, they performed exact diagonalizations of an isovector-plus-isoscalar pairing Hamiltonian for the N=Z nucleus ³²S using matrix elements from the USDB interaction scaled by a factor k.

## Key numbers

- For the seniority model (single-j level, degeneracy M=2j+1, N particles), the two-body density matrix eigenvalue is N(M-N)/2M + N/M; the maximum possible eigenvalue for N fermions on M states is λ_max = N(M-N+2)/M.
- For the picket-fence model (12 particles in 12 doubly degenerate levels), the largest eigenvalue λ₀ increases with pairing strength g; its derivative dλ₀/dg peaks near the BCS critical strength g_cr.
- For ¹¹⁰Sn, the largest eigenvalue λ₀ has its fastest increase below the scaling factor k=1 (the optimal physical value), and PBCS results evolve smoothly across the region where BCS predicts a phase transition.
- For ²⁴O at k=1, the ratio between the largest and second largest eigenvalues is much smaller than for ¹¹⁰Sn, indicating weaker pairing correlations.
- For ³²S with pure isoscalar (J=1,T=0) pairing, the largest eigenvalue changes little up to k≈0.8, then increases suddenly between k≈0.8 and k≈1.0, while the second largest decreases; at k≈0.88 the first excited 0⁺ state energy comes very close to the ground state energy, indicating a level crossing.
- For ³²S with both isovector and isoscalar interactions scaled together, the largest eigenvalue for J=0 decreases for k>0.9 towards the non-interacting value, unlike the pure isovector case.

## Context

This work extends the application of Yang's criterion (Yang 1962) for Off-Diagonal Long Range Order (ODLRO) to finite nuclear systems, building on the Penrose-Onsager criterion for Bose-Einstein condensation. It contrasts with BCS theory, which predicts a sharp phase transition in infinite systems, by showing that in finite nuclei the largest eigenvalue evolves smoothly for like-particle pairing. The study addresses the debated issue of proton-neutron pairing correlations in N=Z nuclei (Frauendorf 2006, Sagawa 2006), finding that the isoscalar pairing case does not exhibit the same signature of a paired phase as like-particle pairing. The results enable future work on identifying superfluid-type correlations in finite nuclei using density matrix eigenvalues rather than BCS-based criteria.
