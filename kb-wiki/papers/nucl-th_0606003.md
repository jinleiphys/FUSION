# Double neutron-proton differential transverse flow as a probe for the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy

> The double neutron-proton differential transverse flowtaken from two reaction systems using different isotopes of the same element is studied at incident beam energies of 400 and 800 MeV/nucleon within the framework of an isospin- and momentum-dependent hadronic transport model IBUU04. The double differential flow is found to retain about the same sensitivity to the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy as the single differential flow in the more neutron-rich reaction. Because the double differential flow reduces significantly both the systematic errors and the influence of the Coulomb force, it is thus more effective probe for the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy.

# Double neutron-proton differential transverse flow as a probe for the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy

## Key claim

The double neutron-proton differential transverse flow, constructed as the difference of the single differential flows from two reaction systems using different isotopes of the same element (132Sn+124Sn and 112Sn+112Sn), retains about the same sensitivity to the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy as the single differential flow in the more neutron-rich reaction, while significantly reducing both systematic errors and the influence of the Coulomb force.

## Method

The authors performed transport model simulations using the isospin- and momentum-dependent hadronic transport model IBUU04 for semi-central collisions (impact parameter b=5 fm) of 132Sn+124Sn and 112Sn+112Sn at incident beam energies of 400 and 800 MeV/nucleon. They calculated the single neutron-proton differential transverse flow for each system and then constructed the double differential flow as the difference between the two single flows, comparing results for two parameterizations of the symmetry energy (x=0 giving a softer symmetry energy and x=-1 giving a stiffer symmetry energy).

## Key numbers

- Maximal baryon density reached in 112Sn+112Sn at 400 MeV/nucleon: about 1.9ρ0 (with ρ0 the normal nuclear matter density)
- Maximal baryon density reached in 112Sn+112Sn at 800 MeV/nucleon: about 2.4ρ0
- The double neutron-proton differential transverse flow around mid-rapidity is essentially zero for the soft symmetry energy (x=0) at both 400 and 800 MeV/nucleon
- The double neutron-proton differential transverse flow displays a clear slope with respect to rapidity for the stiffer symmetry energy (x=-1) at both beam energies

## Context

This work extends earlier proposals for the neutron-proton differential flow as a probe of the symmetry energy (Ba (2000)) and builds on constraints from isospin diffusion data from NSCL/MSU that had limited the parameter x to between 0 and -1 for densities below about 1.2ρ0 (Chen (2004), Li (2005)). The double differential flow approach is analogous to the double ratio method previously applied to neutron/proton ratios and π-/π+ ratios (Li (2005), Yong (2006), MSU (2006)). The results enable more reliable extraction of the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy by reducing systematic uncertainties and Coulomb effects that complicate single-observable analyses.
