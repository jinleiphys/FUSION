# Systematic investigation of the high-spin structures in the odd-odd nuclei $^{166, 168, 170, 172}$Re by a particle-number conserving method

> The recently observed two and four-quasiparticle high-spin rotational bands in the odd-odd nuclei $^{166, 168, 170, 172}$Re are investigated using the cranked shell model with pairing correlations treated by a particle-number conserving method. The experimental moments of inertia and alignments can be reproduced well by the present calculation if appropriate bandhead spins and configurations are assigned for these bands, which in turn confirms their spin and configuration assignments. It is found that the bandhead spins of those two rotational bands observed in $^{166}$Re~[Li {\it et al.}, Phys. Rev. C 92 014310 (2015)] should be both increased by $2\hbar$ to get in consistent with the systematics of the experimental and calculated moments of inertia for the same configurations in $^{168, 170, 172}$Re. The variations of the backbendings/upbendings with increasing neutron number in these nuclei are investigated. The level crossing mechanism is well understood by analysing the variations of the occupation probabilities of the single-particle states close to the Fermi surface and their contributions to the angular momentum alignment with rotational frequency. In addition, the influence of the deformation driving effects of the proton $1/2^-[541]$ ($h_{9/2}$) orbtial on the level crossing in $^{172}$Re is also discussed.

## Key claim

The bandhead spins of the two rotational bands previously observed in \(^{166}\)Re by Li et al. (2015) should be revised upward by \(2\hbar\) to \(10^{-}\) and \(8^{+}\), respectively, in order to bring the experimental moments of inertia into agreement with the systematics of the same configurations in the heavier isotopes \(^{168,170,172}\)Re, as confirmed by particle-number conserving cranked shell model calculations.

## Method

The cranked shell model (CSM) with a monopole pairing interaction was employed, and the pairing Hamiltonian was diagonalized directly in a truncated Fock space using a particle-number conserving (PNC) method, which strictly conserves particle number and exactly accounts for Pauli blocking effects. Deformation parameters (\(\varepsilon_2, \varepsilon_4\)) for each Re isotope were taken as averages of adjacent even-even nuclei from Bengtsson (1986), and Nilsson parameters (\(\kappa, \mu\)) were taken from Bengtsson (1985) with a slight modification of the neutron \(\mu_6\) value from 0.34 to 0.28. The calculated kinematic moments of inertia and angular momentum alignments were compared with experimental data for two- and four-quasiparticle rotational bands in \(^{166,168,170,172}\)Re.

## Key numbers

- The bandhead spin of the \(\pi h_{11/2} \otimes \nu i_{13/2}\) band in \(^{166}\)Re is revised from \(8^{-}\) to \(10^{-}\) (increase of \(2\hbar\)).
- The bandhead spin of the \(\pi h_{11/2} \otimes \nu [f_{7/2} / h_{9/2}]\) band in \(^{166}\)Re is revised from \(6^{+}\) to \(8^{+}\) (increase of \(2\hbar\)).
- The effective monopole pairing strengths used in the calculation are \(G_{\rm p} = 0.28\) MeV for protons and \(G_{\rm n} = 0.48\) MeV for neutrons, applied globally to all four isotopes.
- Deformation parameters \(\varepsilon_2\) increase with neutron number: 0.125 (\(^{166}\)Re), 0.168 (\(^{168}\)Re), 0.196 (\(^{170}\)Re), and 0.213 (\(^{172}\)Re).
- The proton orbital \(\pi 1/2^{-}[541]\) (\(h_{9/2}\)) was shifted upward by about 0.7 MeV to correct for the velocity-dependent \(l^2\) term in the Nilsson potential at high spin.
- The PNC-CSM calculation predicts two sharp upbendings in band 1 of \(^{166}\)Re at \(\hbar\omega \approx 0.25\) MeV and \(\hbar\omega \approx 0.45\) MeV, which are not observed experimentally.
- The calculated upbending frequency for the second upbending in band 2 of \(^{170}\)Re is \(\hbar\omega \approx 0.52\) MeV, higher than the experimental value.

## Context

This work extends the PNC-CSM approach, previously applied to \(^{166}\)Ta by Zhang (2016), to a systematic study of odd-odd Re isotopes \(^{166,168,170,172}\)Re. It resolves a discrepancy in bandhead spin assignments proposed by Li et al. (2015) and Hartley et al. (2016) for \(^{166}\)Re by showing that increasing both bandhead spins by \(2\hbar\) yields consistency with the systematics of moments of inertia across the isotopic chain. The calculations confirm the configuration assignments and reveal that the first backbendings in these bands are attributed to \(AB\) or \(BC\) crossings of \(i_{13/2}\) quasineutrons, as previously suggested by Hartley et al. (2016) and Hartley et al. (2013). The analysis of occupation probabilities and angular momentum contributions from individual orbitals provides a detailed understanding of the level crossing mechanism, including the role of pseudo-spin partners and the deformation-driving effect of the proton \(h_{9/2}\) orbital in \(^{172}\)Re.
