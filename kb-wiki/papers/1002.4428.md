# Two-particle spatial correlations in superfluid nuclei

> We discuss the effect of pairing on two-neutron space correlations in deformed nuclei. The spatial correlations are described by the pairing tensor in coordinate space calculated in the HFB approach. The calculations are done using the D1S Gogny force. We show that the pairing tensor has a rather small extension in the relative coordinate, a feature observed earlier in spherical nuclei. It is pointed out that in deformed nuclei the coherence length corresponding to the pairing tensor has a pattern similar to what we have found previously in spherical nuclei, i.e., it is maximal in the interior of the nucleus and then it is decreasing rather fast in the surface region where it reaches a minimal value of about 2 fm. This minimal value of the coherence length in the surface is essentially determined by the finite size properties of single-particle states in the vicinity of the chemical potential and has little to do with enhanced pairing correlations in the nuclear surface. It is shown that in nuclei the coherence length is not a good indicator of the intensity of pairing correlations. This feature is contrasted with the situation in infinite matter.

# Two-particle spatial correlations in superfluid nuclei

## Key claim

The coherence length of Cooper pairs in deformed nuclei reaches a minimal value of about 2 fm in the nuclear surface, but this small value is **not** caused by enhanced pairing correlations in the surface region; instead, it is essentially determined by the finite-size properties of single-particle states near the chemical potential, with pairing playing only a secondary role.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the D1S Gogny force in an axially-deformed harmonic oscillator basis with 13 major shells. They computed the pairing tensor in coordinate space for three geometrical configurations (xz-plane, xy-plane, and yz-plane) that allow separation of center-of-mass and relative coordinates, and extracted the local coherence length from the non-local pairing tensor. For spherical nuclei, they also analyzed the even and odd parity components of the pairing tensor separately and varied the pairing strength by a scaling factor alpha to isolate the role of pairing correlations.

## Key numbers

- Coherence length in the surface of deformed nuclei reaches a minimal value of about **2 fm** for all isotopes studied (152Sm, 102Sr, 238U) and all geometrical configurations.
- Inside the nucleus, the coherence length has large values up to about **10-14 fm**, similar to spherical nuclei.
- The S=1 (spin-triplet) pairing tensor is weaker than the S=0 (spin-singlet) channel by a factor of approximately **20**.
- In 120Sn, varying the pairing strength by a factor alpha from 1.0 (pairing energy ~19 MeV) down to 0.5 (pairing energy ~0.5 MeV) changes the coherence length at R ~ 6 fm by less than **0.2 fm**.
- In infinite symmetric nuclear matter, the coherence length varies approximately inversely with the pairing gap and reaches minimal values of about **4-5 fm** (for the D1S Gogny force), significantly larger than the 2 fm surface minimum in finite nuclei.
- The uncorrelated pair wave function (two non-interacting neutrons in the same orbit) yields a surface minimum of about **3.5 fm**, about 30% larger than the full HFB result of 2.3 fm for alpha=1.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study of spherical nuclei (Pillet et al., 2007) to axially-deformed systems, confirming that the small coherence length in the nuclear surface is a general feature independent of deformation. The results contradict the interpretation proposed in that earlier work, which attributed the small coherence length to enhanced surface pairing correlations. The analysis contrasts the behavior in finite nuclei with infinite nuclear matter, where the coherence length depends strongly on pairing intensity (Fetter and Walecka, 1971). The finding that single-particle orbital structure dominates the coherence length pattern is consistent with independent calculations by Pastore (2008) for spherical nuclei. This work enables a more nuanced understanding of Cooper pair spatial correlations in superfluid nuclei and clarifies that the coherence length is not a reliable indicator of pairing correlation intensity in finite systems.
