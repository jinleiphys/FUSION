# From Short-Range to Contact Interactions in Two-dimensional Many-Body Quantum Systems

> Quantum systems composed of $N$ distinct particles in $\R^2$ with two-body contact interactions of TMS type are shown to arise as limits - in the norm resolvent sense - of Schrödinger operators with suitably rescaled pair potentials.

## Key claim

The paper proves that Schrödinger operators with suitably rescaled short-range pair potentials in two dimensions converge in the norm resolvent sense to a self-adjoint Hamiltonian with TMS-type contact interactions, provided the coupling constants satisfy a specific asymptotic condition involving the integral of the potential; if this condition is not met for a given pair, only strong resolvent convergence (at best) is possible.

## Method

The authors express the Schrödinger operator \(H_\eps\) in terms of factorized interaction operators \(A_{\eps,\sigma}^* B_{\eps,\sigma}\) and derive a generalized Konno-Kuroda formula for its resolvent. They decompose the operator matrix \(\Lambda_\eps(z)\) into diagonal and off-diagonal parts, analyze the asymptotic behavior of each component as \(\eps\to 0\), and show that the limit of \(\Lambda_\eps(z)^{-1}\) exists for large enough \(z>0\). The limiting resolvent is then expressed via a Krein-type formula involving trace operators and an operator \(\Theta(z)\) on a direct sum of reduced Hilbert spaces.

## Key numbers

* The coupling constant scaling is \(-\frac{1}{g_{\eps,\sigma}} = \mu_\sigma [a_\sigma \ln(\eps) + b_\sigma] + o(1)\) with \(a_\sigma > 0\), where \(\mu_\sigma\) is the reduced mass of the pair.
* The condition for a non-trivial contact interaction is \(a_\sigma \geq \frac{1}{2\pi} \int V_\sigma(r) \, dr\); equality yields a non-zero contact interaction, inequality yields no contribution.
* The convergence rate of \(A_{\eps,\sigma}R_0(z)\) to \(S(z)_\sigma\) is \(O(\eps^s)\) for some \(s \in (0,1)\), under the assumption \(\int |r|^{2s} |V_\sigma(r)| \, dr < \infty\).
* For the diagonal part, the Hilbert-Schmidt norm estimate \(\| u (-\Delta + \alpha)^{-1} v + (2\pi)^{-1}[(\ln(\sqrt{\alpha}/2)+\gamma)\Ket{u}\Bra{v} + L] \|_{\text{HS}} \leq C \alpha^{s/2}\) holds for \(\alpha > 0\) with \(s \in (0,2)\).

## Context

This work extends prior results on the derivation of contact-interaction Hamiltonians from short-range potentials, which were previously limited to strong resolvent convergence in two dimensions for \(N \geq 2\) particles (Gu, Quastel and Tsai (2019)) or to one-dimensional systems (Basti (2018); Griesemer, Hofacker, Linden (2019)). The norm resolvent convergence established here implies spectral convergence, which is stronger than previous results. The paper also connects to the TMS Hamiltonian framework of Dell'Antonio et al. (Figari) and provides a new construction of these Hamiltonians as resolvent limits.
