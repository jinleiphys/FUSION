# Chemical equilibration of $π$, $ρ$ and $ω$ mesons in ultra--relativistic nuclear collisions

> I estimate the degree of local chemical equilibration for $π$, $ρ$ and $ω$ mesons in ultra--relativistic nuclear collisions. The $π$ and $ρ$ mesons remain near chemical equilibrium in all cases, while the $ω$ meson density is typically 30--50\% higher than the equilibrium value for O+Ag collisions at $\sqrt{s}=20$ GeV. If chemical reactions are turned off, the $ω$ meson density is much larger, 2.5--3 times its equilibrium value. Thus, $ω$ mesons may provide the most sensitive tests for the degree of chemical equilibration in nuclear collisions.

# Chemical equilibration of π, ρ and ω mesons in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions

## Key claim

The π and ρ mesons remain near chemical equilibrium in all cases considered, but the ω meson density is typically 30-50% higher than the equilibrium value for O+Ag collisions at √s=20 GeV; this departure from equilibrium depends on the assumed reaction rates and is smaller when chemical reactions are turned on compared to the non-interacting case.

## Method

The author models an expanding resonance gas assuming boost-invariant longitudinal expansion (Bjorken model) with no transverse expansion, local thermal equilibrium, and entropy conservation. Chemical reaction rates for eight exothermic four-body strong interactions among π, ρ and ω mesons are estimated using plausible values (around 1 fm²-c for exothermic reactions), with endothermic reverse rates derived from detailed balance. The system is evolved from a hadronic transition temperature Tc = 150-200 MeV, with initial densities set to thermal and chemical equilibrium values, and the coupled rate equations for number densities are solved as a function of proper time.

## Key numbers

- ρ → ππ decay rate: Γ = 0.77 fm⁻¹-c at T=0
- ω → πππ decay rate: Γ = 0.04 fm⁻¹-c at T=0
- For O+Ag collisions at √s=20 GeV with chemical reactions turned off (R=0) and Tc=200 MeV, the ω meson density rises to 2-3 times its equilibrium value
- For O+Ag collisions at √s=20 GeV with R=1.5 fm²-c (σ ≈ 15 mb) and Tc=200 MeV, ω mesons are noticeably further from equilibrium than π or ρ mesons
- For O+Ag collisions at √s=20 GeV with Rtot=6 fm²-c (σ ≈ 60 mb) and Tc=200 MeV, ω mesons are even further from equilibrium
- For O+Ag collisions at √s=20 GeV with R=0 and Tc=200 MeV, π and ρ meson densities were factors of roughly 1.3 and 1.6 higher than equilibrium respectively
- Initial proper time τ0 = 4.6 fm/c for O+Ag at √s=20 GeV with Tc=200 MeV
- Initial proper time τ0 = 27.8 fm/c for Pb+Pb at √s=200 GeV with Tc=200 MeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Seibert (1992) on measuring the QCD transition temperature and the work of Heinz and Lee (1991) on the lifetime of the hadronic gas using dileptons from the ρ⁰-ω peak. The author uses the Bjorken model (1983) for longitudinal expansion and compares results for O+Ag collisions at √s=20 GeV with Pb+Pb collisions at √s=200 GeV. The reported enhancement of φ mesons in nuclear collisions from the NA38 Collaboration (1991) may be related to the ω meson departure from equilibrium seen here. The results enable experimental tests of chemical equilibration in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions, particularly through measurement of ω meson enhancement relative to ρ⁰ mesons, though the feasibility depends on detector capabilities for separating final-state mesons from those decaying during earlier collision stages.
