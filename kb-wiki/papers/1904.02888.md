# Comparison of heavy-ion transport simulations: Collision integral with pions and $Δ$ resonances in a box

> We compare ten transport codes for a system confined in a box, aiming at improved handling of the production of $Δ$ resonances and pions, which is indispensable for constraining high-density symmetry energy from observables such as the $π^-/π^+$ yield ratio in heavy-ion collisions. The system in a box is initialized with nucleons at saturation density and at 60 MeV temperature. The reactions $NN\leftrightarrow NΔ$ and $Δ\leftrightarrow Nπ$ are implemented, but the Pauli blocking and the mean-field potential are deactivated in the present comparison. Results are compared to those from the two reference cases of a chemically equilibrated ideal gas mixture and of the rate equation. In the results of the numbers of $Δ$ and $π$, deviations from the reference values are observed in many codes, and they depend significantly on the size of the time step. These deviations are tied to different ways in ordering the sequence of collisions and decays, that take place in the same time step. Better agreements are seen in the reaction rates and the number ratios among the isospin species of $Δ$ and $π$. These are, however, affected by the correlations, which are absent in the Boltzmann equation, but are induced by the way particle scatterings are treated in transport calculations. The uncertainty in the transport-code predictions of the $π^-/π^+$ ratio for the system initialized at n/p = 1.5, after letting the existing $Δ$ resonances decay, is found to be within a few percent, which is sufficiently small so that it does not strongly impact constraining the high-density symmetry energy from heavy-ion collisions. Most of the sources of uncertainties have been understood, and individual codes may be further improved. This investigation will be extended in the future to heavy-ion collisions to ensure the problems identified here remain under control.

## Key claim

The uncertainty in transport-code predictions of the π⁻/π⁺ ratio for a box system initialized at n/p = 1.5, after letting existing Δ resonances decay, is within a few percent, which is sufficiently small that it does not strongly impact constraining the high-density symmetry energy from heavy-ion collisions; however, this conclusion applies only to the simplified box setup without mean-field potentials or Pauli blocking, and the investigation must be extended to realistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

Ten transport codes (five BUU-type and five QMD-type) were compared for a system of nucleons confined in a 20 fm cubic box with periodic boundary conditions, initialized at saturation density (0.16 fm⁻³) and temperature 60 MeV. The codes implemented the reactions NN ↔ NΔ and Δ ↔ Nπ with specified cross sections and decay widths, but deactivated Pauli blocking and mean-field potentials, making the calculations pure cascade simulations. Results were compared to two reference cases: a chemically equilibrated ideal relativistic Boltzmann-gas mixture and a rate-equation solution.

## Key numbers

- System initialized with 1280 nucleons in a 20 fm box at density ρ = 0.16 fm⁻³ and temperature T = 60 MeV.
- Two isospin cases: symmetric (640 neutrons, 640 protons, δ = 0) and asymmetric (768 neutrons, 512 protons, δ = 0.2 or n/p = 1.5).
- Elastic scattering cross section σ_el = 40 mb for any pair of baryons (NN, NΔ, ΔΔ), assumed isotropic.
- Δ resonance mass M_Δ = 1.232 GeV; nucleon mass m_N = 0.938 GeV; pion mass m_π = 0.139 GeV.
- Many codes overestimate N_π by 20% or more relative to the rate-equation reference; some codes (JAM and SMASH) agree well for both N_π and N_Δ.
- The π⁻/π⁺ ratio uncertainty across codes for the asymmetric system is within a few percent after Δ decay.
- Time steps used were Δt = 0.5 or 1.0 fm/c; some codes (JAM, SMASH) are time-step-free.

## Context

This work extends the transport-code comparison effort that began with Xu (2016) for Au+Au collisions at 100 and 400 MeV/nucleon and the box comparison of Zhang (2018) focusing on NN elastic collisions. It specifically addresses the production of Δ resonances and pions, which were not studied in those earlier comparisons. The results enable future extension to heavy-ion collisions to verify that the identified uncertainties remain under control, and they provide a foundation for reliably using the π⁻/π⁺ ratio as a probe of the high-density symmetry energy, as originally proposed by Li (2002, 2008).
