# Viscous damping of r-modes and emission of gravitational waves

> The Rossby mode (r-mode) perturbation in pulsars as a steady gravitational wave (GW) source has been explored. The effect of a rigid crust on viscous damping and dissipation rate in the boundary layer between fluid core and crust has been studied. The intensity of the emitted GWs in terms of the strain tensor amplitude has been estimated with the approximation of slow rotation using equation of state derived from the APR and Skyrme effective interactions with Brussels-Montreal parameter sets. The core of the neutron star has been considered to be $β$-equilibrated nuclear matter containing neutrons, protons, electrons and muons, surrounded by a solid crust. Calculations have been made for critical frequencies, the time evolution and the rate of change of the spin frequencies across a broad spectrum of pulsar masses.

## Key claim

The paper calculates that the r-mode instability in pulsars is suppressed by viscous damping in the boundary layer between the fluid core and the rigid crust, and that the computed strain tensor amplitudes for gravitational waves exceed the detection limits of advanced LIGO, but only under the approximation of slow rotation and using specific nuclear equations of state (APR and Skyrme with Brussels-Montreal parameter sets).

## Method

The authors solved for the r-mode (l=2 quadrupole) instability by computing the competing time scales for gravitational radiation and viscous damping (shear viscosity in the core-crust boundary layer, shear viscosity in the fluid core, and bulk viscosity in the fluid core) using β-equilibrated npeμ nuclear matter equations of state from APR and Skyrme (BSk22, BSk24, BSk26) interactions. They determined the critical angular velocity Ω_c where 1/τ(Ω_c,T)=0, then evolved the spin frequency over time and calculated the gravitational wave strain tensor amplitude h_0 from both thermal equilibrium and spin equilibrium saturation conditions.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star masses obtained: 2.19 M_⊙ (APR), 2.27 M_⊙ (BSk22), 2.28 M_⊙ (BSk24), 2.18 M_⊙ (BSk26)
- Constants for viscosity formulas: A_ee = 6 × 10^6, A_nn = 347, A_BV = 6 × 10^(-59) (in cgs units)
- The r-mode amplitude used for spin evolution calculations: α_r = 2 × 10^(-7)
- Initial spin frequency used for time evolution: ν_in = 700 Hz
- Thermal equilibrium calculations used red-shifted effective surface temperature T^inf = 100 eV
- The strain tensor amplitude h_0 was computed for spin frequencies of 200 Hz and 700 Hz as a function of distance

## Context

This work extends the r-mode instability formalism of Lindblom (1998, 2000) and Owen (1998) by incorporating the rigid crust boundary-layer damping originally studied by Bildsten and Ushomirsky (2000), and applying it to equations of state from APR (Ak98) and Brussels-Montreal Skyrme interactions (Gor13). The results enable predictions for continuous gravitational wave searches by LIGO-Virgo, showing that the computed strain amplitudes exceed current detection limits but may become detectable with future detector upgrades.
