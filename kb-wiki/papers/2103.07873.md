# Nuclear effects in neutrino-nucleus DIS and probe for short range correlation

> We investigate charged-current neutrino-nucleus deep inelastic scattering with particular interests in relationship of short range correlation (SRC) and EMC effect. The structure functions $F^A_2(x,Q^2)$, $x F^A_3(x,Q^2)$ and ratios of differential cross sections are presented where the nuclei $A$ are chosen to be carbon, iron and lead. We propose a kind of universal modification functions which would provide a nontrivial test of SRC universality on the platform of neutrino-nucleus scattering and improve our understanding of nucleon structure substantially.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the EMC effect in neutrino-nucleus deep inelastic scattering can be explained by short range correlation (SRC) pairs, and that universal modification functions derived from this assumption can provide a nontrivial test of SRC universality; the claim is hedged by the fact that the analysis uses the EPPS16 parametrization (which includes data from multiple processes beyond neutrino-nucleus DIS) and that the universal behavior is shown for structure function ratios normalized by SRC factors, not for raw cross sections.

## Method

The authors calculated charged-current neutrino-nucleus DIS structure functions \(F_2^A(x,Q^2)\) and \(x F_3^A(x,Q^2)\) for carbon, iron, and lead using the EPPS16 nuclear parton distribution function (NPDF) parametrization with CT14NLO as the free nucleon baseline. They computed ratios of structure functions and differential cross sections \(d^2\sigma/dxdy\) for nuclei relative to free nucleons, and then introduced universal modification functions by normalizing these ratios by the SRC scale factor \(a_2^A\) and the nuclear composition factor \(2Z_A/A_A\). The universality was tested by comparing the normalized ratios from EPPS16 with those derived from a theoretical assumption that all nuclear modifications originate from SRC pairs.

## Key numbers

* Structure functions \(F_2^A(x,Q^2)\) and \(x F_3^A(x,Q^2)\) were computed at fixed \(Q^2 = 5~\textrm{GeV}^2\) for carbon, iron, and lead.
* The SRC ratio \(a_2^A\) (defined as \((n_{src}^A/A)/(n_{src}^d/2)\)) is used to normalize the structure function ratios; the universal modification functions \(R_M(F_2^A;x,Q^2)\) and \(R_M(F_3^A;x,Q^2)\) are defined as \(\frac{2Z_A}{A_A} \frac{R(F_2^A;x,Q^2)-1}{a_2^{A}}\) and \(\frac{2Z_A}{A_A} \frac{R(F_3^A;x,Q^2)-1}{a_2^{A}}\) respectively.
* The universal behavior is observed in the region \(0.3 < x < 0.7\) where the three curves of normalized ratios for carbon, iron, and lead "almost shrink into one single line" (Fig. 5 and Fig. 6).

## Context

This work extends the established linear correlation between the EMC effect and SRC scale factors observed in electron-nucleus DIS (Weinstein 2010) to the neutrino-nucleus DIS platform. It builds on the EPPS16 NPDF parametrization (Eskola 2016) and the CT14NLO free proton baseline (Dulat 2015) to test the SRC universality hypothesis proposed by Frankfurt (1993) and Segarra (2019). The results enable future neutrino experiments (e.g., MINERvA, DUNE) to use neutrino-nucleus DIS as a direct test of whether nuclear modifications in the EMC region are universally driven by SRC pairs, potentially distinguishing nuclear physics effects from beyond-standard-model signals in neutrino scattering.
