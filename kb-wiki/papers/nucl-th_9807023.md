# What Effective Field Theory May Contribute to the Blast Program

> 17 pages, 10 figures, latex, sprocl.sty: Talk presented at the ``Second Workshop on Electronuclear Physics with Internal Targets and the Bates Large Acceptance Spectrometer Toroid (BLAST)'', May 1998

## Key claim

Effective field theory (EFT) can provide a consistent power-counting scheme for nucleon-nucleon interactions, but the expansion parameter is only about 1/2 and the power counting fails at momenta above approximately 300 MeV, limiting the theory's applicability to processes with typical momenta below that scale.

## Method

The paper reviews the construction of an EFT for two-nucleon systems using a power-divergence subtraction (PDS) scheme to renormalize loop diagrams. The leading-order amplitude consists of summed bubble diagrams with a momentum-independent four-nucleon contact interaction, while pion exchange and derivative interactions enter at subleading orders. The coefficients of local operators are determined by fitting to the Nijmegen partial-wave analysis for scattering in the 1S0 and 3S1-3D1 channels over momenta up to 200 MeV, and the resulting EFT is then applied to compute deuteron electromagnetic form factors and polarizabilities.

## Key numbers

- Scattering lengths: a(1S0) = -23.714 ± 0.013 fm and a(3S1) = +5.425 ± 0.0014 fm
- The scale Lambda_NN = 8 pi f^2 / (g_A^2 M) ~ 300 MeV, above which the power counting fails
- Fitted coefficients at mu = m_pi: C_0(1S0) = -3.34 fm^2, C_2(1S0) = 3.24 fm^4, D_2(1S0) = -0.42 fm^4
- Fitted coefficients at mu = m_pi: C_0(3S1) = -5.51 fm^2, C_2(3S1) = 9.91 fm^4, D_2(3S1) = 1.32 fm^4
- Deuteron charge radius: theoretical value 1.89 fm (subleading order) vs. experimental 1.963 fm
- Deuteron quadrupole moment: theoretical value 0.40 fm^2 (subleading order) vs. experimental 0.2859 fm^2
- Deuteron magnetic moment: theoretical value 0.88 minus 0.02 (fit) from counterterm
- Electric polarizabilities: alpha_E0 = 0.595 fm^3, alpha_E2 = -0.062 fm^3
- Magnetic polarizabilities: beta_M0 = 0.067 fm^3, beta_M2 = 0.195 fm^3

## Context

This work extends the earlier EFT approach initiated by Weinberg (1990) for nuclear forces, which was found to have an inconsistent power counting due to divergences requiring counterterms at leading order. The new PDS scheme proposed by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) provides a consistent power counting where pions are subleading to contact interactions. The results enable systematic calculations of two-nucleon observables to higher orders and open the path toward three-body and multi-nucleon systems using EFT, though the theory is currently limited to momenta below about 300 MeV and cannot yet address processes like photo-pion production off the deuteron or elastic pion-deuteron scattering.
