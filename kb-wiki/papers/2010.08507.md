# Isoscalar monopole and dipole transitions in $^{24}$Mg, $^{26}$Mg and $^{28}$Si

> Nuclei in the $sd$-shell demonstrate a remarkable interplay of cluster and mean-field phenomena. The $N=Z$ nuclei, such as $^{24}$Mg and $^{28}$Si, have been the focus of the theoretical study of both these phenomena in the past. The cluster and vortical mean-field phenomena can be probed by excitation of isoscalar monopole and dipole states in scattering of isoscalar particles such as deuterons or $α$ particles. Inelastically scattered $α$ particles were momentum-analysed in the K600 magnetic spectrometer at iThemba LABS, Cape Town, South Africa. The scattered particles were detected in two multi-wire drift chambers and two plastic scintillators placed at the focal plane of the K600. In the theoretical discussion, the QRPA and AMD+GCM were used. The QRPA calculations lead us to conclude that: i) the mean-field vorticity appears mainly in dipole states with $K=1$, ii) the dipole (monopole) states should have strong deformation-induced octupole (quadrupole) admixtures, and iii) that near the $α$-particle threshold, there should exist a collective state (with $K=0$ for prolate nuclei and $K=1$ for oblate nuclei) with an impressive octupole strength. The results of the AMD+GCM calculations suggest that some observed states may have a mixed (mean-field + cluster) character or correspond to particular cluster configurations. A tentative correspondence between observed states and theoretical states from QRPA and AMD+GCM was established. The QRPA and AMD+GCM analysis shows that low-energy isoscalar dipole states combine cluster and mean-field properties. The QRPA calculations show that the low-energy vorticity is well localized in $^{24}$Mg, fragmented in $^{26}$Mg, and absent in $^{28}$Si.

## Key claim

The paper claims that low-energy isoscalar dipole (IS1) and monopole (IS0) states in $^{24}$Mg, $^{26}$Mg, and $^{28}$Si can serve as fingerprints of both cluster and mean-field (vortical) phenomena, but the QRPA calculations show that low-energy vorticity is well localized only in $^{24}$Mg, fragmented in $^{26}$Mg, and absent in $^{28}$Si, meaning the manifestation of vortical motion depends strongly on the specific nucleus and its deformation properties.

## Method

Inelastically scattered 200-MeV alpha particles were momentum-analysed using the K600 magnetic spectrometer at iThemba LABS, with detection via multi-wire drift chambers and plastic scintillators at the focal plane. Experimental differential cross sections were extracted for scattering angles from 0 to 6 degrees and compared to DWBA calculations using CHUCK3 to extract transition strengths. The theoretical analysis employed two complementary approaches: Skyrme Quasiparticle Random-Phase Approximation (QRPA) with the SLy6 force for mean-field and vorticity studies, and Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics plus Generator Coordinate Method (AMD+GCM) for cluster-structure investigations.

## Key numbers

- The well-known $E_x = 7.555$-MeV $J^\pi = 1^-$ state in $^{24}$Mg exhausts $2.6(5)\%$ of the isoscalar dipole energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR), consistent with previous results of $3.1(6)\%$ and $3(1)\%$.
- In $^{24}$Mg, the $J^\pi = 0^+$ state at $13.89(1)$ MeV exhausts $2.6(5)\%$ of the monopole EWSR, the largest monopole strength observed in this nucleus.
- In $^{24}$Mg, the $J^\pi = 1^-$ state at $11.8649(13)$ MeV exhausts $11(2)\%$ of the dipole EWSR, the largest dipole strength observed in this nucleus.
- In $^{26}$Mg, the $J^\pi = 1^-$ state at $8.9594(5)$ MeV exhausts $3.9(8)\%$ of the dipole EWSR.
- In $^{28}$Si, the $J^\pi = 1^-$ state at $9.929(17)$ MeV exhausts $11(2)\%$ of the dipole EWSR, confirming a tentative assignment from Vanderborg (1981).
- The energy resolution was approximately 75 keV (FWHM) for zero-degree data and 65 keV for finite-angle data.

## Context

This work extends previous experimental and theoretical studies of isoscalar monopole and dipole excitations in sd-shell nuclei, particularly building on earlier measurements by Kawabata (2007) and Vanderborg (1981) for $^{24}$Mg, $^{26}$Mg, and $^{28}$Si. The QRPA calculations follow the approach of Nesterenko (2018) who predicted individual low-lying vortical IS1 states in $^{24}$Mg, while the AMD+GCM calculations extend the cluster-model studies of Kimura (2017) for $^{24}$Mg and Chiba (2019) for $^{28}$Si. The comparative analysis of three nuclei with different deformation properties (stiff prolate $^{24}$Mg, soft prolate $^{26}$Mg, soft oblate $^{28}$Si) enables systematic investigation of how clustering and vorticity depend on nuclear structure, and the identified states provide candidates for future ($e,e^\prime$) measurements to probe vortical response directly.
