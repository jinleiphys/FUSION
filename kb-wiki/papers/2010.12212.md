# Generalized parton distributions of light nuclei

> The measurement of nuclear generalized parton distributions (GPDs) in hard exclusive processes, such as deeply virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS), will be one of the main achievements of a new generation of experiments at high luminosity, such as those under way at the Jefferson Laboratory (JLab) with the 12 GeV electron beam and, above all, those planned at the future Electron Ion Collider. The CLAS collaboration at JLab has recently demonstrated the possibility to disentangle the two different channels of nuclear DVCS, the coherent and incoherent ones, a first step towards the measurement of GPDs of nuclei and of bound nucleons, respectively, opening new exciting perspectives in the field. In this scenario, theoretical precise calculations, ultimately realistic, become mandatory. Light nuclei, for which realistic studies are affordable and conventional nuclear effects can be safely estimated, so that possible exotic effects can be exposed, play an important role. The status of the calculation of GPDs for light nuclei will be summarized, in particular for $^3$He and $^4$He, and some updates will be presented. The prospects for the next years, related to the new series of measurements at future facilities, will be addressed.

## Key claim

The paper summarizes the status of generalized parton distribution (GPD) calculations for light nuclei, particularly $^3$He and $^4$He, arguing that precise theoretical calculations are mandatory for interpreting upcoming measurements at Jefferson Laboratory (JLab) with the 12 GeV electron beam and at the future Electron Ion Collider (EIC), but the actual extraction of nuclear GPDs from data remains challenging because the DVCS amplitude is integrated over the unaccessible variable $x$ and is proportional to Compton form factors rather than directly to GPDs.

## Method

The authors review the impulse approximation (IA) framework for calculating nuclear GPDs, where the nuclear GPD is obtained as a convolution between the non-diagonal spectral function of the internal nucleons and the GPD of the nucleons themselves, using realistic nuclear wave functions from the AV18 potential and the UIX three-nucleon interaction. For $^3$He, the off-diagonal spin-dependent spectral function is evaluated exactly, while for $^4$He, the calculations use a semi-realistic nuclear description and the nucleon GPD model of Goloskokov and Kroll (2007, 2008). The beam-spin asymmetry (BSA) for coherent DVCS is computed from the resulting Compton form factors.

## Key numbers

- For $^3$He, nuclear effects are evaluated at off-forward kinematics $t = -0.25$ GeV$^2$ and $\xi_3 = 3\xi = 0.2$, where the light-cone off-diagonal momentum distributions depart from a delta-like behavior.
- For $^4$He, the BSA is calculated for 9 kinematical configurations with $x_B$ ranging from 0.132 to 0.225, $Q^2$ from 1.15 to 1.87 GeV$^2$, and $t$ from -0.081 to -0.126 GeV$^2$.
- The CLAS collaboration at JLab has demonstrated the separation of coherent and incoherent DVCS channels off $^4$He (Hattawy 2017, 2018).
- In a liquid drop model for a large nucleus, the D-term $d(0)$ scales as $A^{7/3}$, implying the D-term contribution to the DVCS amplitude grows as $A^{4/3}$.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for nuclear GPDs initially developed for the deuteron by Berger (2001) and Cano (2003), and for spin-0 nuclei by Guzey (2003) and Kirchner (2003). The calculations for $^3$He build on earlier work by Scopetta (2004, 2009) and Rinaldi (2012, 2014), while the $^4$He results update the models of Guzey (2003) and Liuti (2004). The paper enables future experimental analyses at JLab and the EIC by providing realistic predictions for observables such as the beam-spin asymmetry, and supports the development of Monte Carlo event generators for DVCS off $^4$He.
