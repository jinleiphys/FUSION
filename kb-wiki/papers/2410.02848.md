# Simplified projection on total spin zero for state preparation on quantum computers

> We introduce a simple algorithm for projecting on $J=0$ states of a many-body system by performing a series of rotations to remove states with angular momentum projections greater than zero. Existing methods rely on unitary evolution with the two-body operator $J^2$, which when expressed in the computational basis contains many complicated Pauli strings requiring Trotterization and leading to very deep quantum circuits. Our approach performs the necessary projections using the one-body operators $J_x$ and $J_z$. By leveraging the method of Cartan decomposition, the unitary transformations that perform the projection can be parameterized as a product of a small number of two-qubit rotations, with angles determined by an efficient classical optimization. Given the reduced complexity in terms of gates, this approach can be used to prepare approximate ground states of even-even nuclei by projecting onto the $J=0$ component of deformed Hartree-Fock states. We estimate the resource requirements in terms of the universal gate set {$H$,$S$,CNOT,$T$} and briefly discuss a variant of the algorithm that projects onto $J=1/2$ states of a system with an odd number of fermions.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a simplified algorithm for projecting many-body fermionic states onto total angular momentum \(J=0\) that uses only one-body operators \(J_x\) and \(J_z\) rather than the two-body operator \(J^2\), but the method is restricted to projection onto \(J=0\) (or \(J=1/2\)) and preserves the amplitude of the target component only in the \(J=0\) case.

## Method

The algorithm progressively removes states with non-zero magnetic projection by applying unitary evolutions \(\exp(-it_i J_z \otimes Y_a)\) and \(\exp(-it_i J_x \otimes Y_a)\) with an ancilla qubit, using evolution times \(t_i = \pi/2, \pi/4, \ldots\) to eliminate specific \(M\) values after mid-circuit measurements. Between projections, the system is rotated between the deformed basis (where the trial Hartree-Fock state is prepared) and the spherical basis using Cartan decomposition, which expresses the required basis transformations as products of two-qubit rotations with classically optimized angles.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{20}\)Ne (2 protons, 2 neutrons in the \(sd\) shell), the initial deformed Hartree-Fock state has expectation value \(\langle J^2 \rangle \approx 16\hbar^2\), and convergence to a \(J=0\) state is achieved after 24 total mid-circuit measurements regardless of how measurements are distributed across iterations.
* The number of Pauli strings required to encode the two-body operator \(\tilde{J}^2\) in the deformed basis ranges from 24,599 for \(^{20}\)Ne to 45,051 for \(^{26}\)Mg, while the one-body operators \(\tilde{J}_x\), \(\tilde{J}_y\), and \(\tilde{J}_z\) each require between 200 and 288 Pauli strings for the same nuclei.
* The estimated number of CNOT gates for the proposed algorithm ranges from 3,048 to 3,944 depending on the iteration strategy, compared to 510,398 to 835,904 CNOTs for a single Trotter step of the \(J^2\)-based algorithm across the four \(sd\)-shell nuclei studied.

## Context

This work extends the projection method of Stetcu (2023) that used unitary evolution with the two-body operator \(J^2\) and required Trotterization, leading to deep quantum circuits. By using only one-body operators and Cartan decomposition, the algorithm eliminates the need for Trotterization and dramatically reduces gate counts, making it feasible for preparing approximate ground states of even-even nuclei by projecting deformed Hartree-Fock states onto \(J=0\). The method is demonstrated on nuclear shell-model calculations in the \(sd\) valence space using the universal \(sd\) effective interaction of Wildenthal (1984), and the authors note it could be combined with variational approaches for further improvements.
