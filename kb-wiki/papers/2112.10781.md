# Convergence of hydrodynamics in rapidly spinning strongly coupled plasma

> We compute the radius of convergence of the linearized relativistic hydrodynamic expansion around a non-trivially rotating strongly coupled N=4 Super-Yang-Mills plasma. Our results show that the validity of hydrodynamics is sustained and can even get enhanced in a highly vortical quark-gluon plasma, such as the one produced in heavy-ion collisions. The hydrodynamic dispersion relations are computed using a rotating background that is an analytic solution of the ideal hydrodynamic equations of motion with non-vanishing angular momentum and large vorticity gradients, giving rise to a particular boost symmetry. Analytic equations for the transport coeffcients of the rotating plasma as a function of their values in a plasma at rest are given.

## Key claim

The radius of convergence of the linearized hydrodynamic expansion around a rotating strongly coupled N=4 Super-Yang-Mills plasma remains finite at nonzero angular momentum and can even increase for sufficiently rapid rotation, indicating that hydrodynamics remains valid in a highly vortical quark-gluon plasma despite large gradients that would spoil the expansion in a non-rotating system.

## Method

The authors use the gauge/gravity correspondence (holography) to study a rotating N=4 Super-Yang-Mills plasma dual to a rotating black hole in AdS5 spacetime. They compute the quasinormal modes of metric fluctuations around the rotating background, which correspond to hydrodynamic modes, and analyze the critical points of the spectral curves that encode the hydrodynamic dispersion relations. The large black hole limit is taken, which yields a boost symmetry that allows the fluctuation equations in the rotating plasma to be transformed into their form in a plasma at rest, enabling analytic computation of the dispersion relations and transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The radius of convergence in the sound sector drops at worst to approximately 60% of its value in a non-rotating fluid, reaching a minimum at a/L ≈ 0.75
- The radius of convergence in the shear diffusion sector drops to approximately 35% of its value in a non-rotating fluid, reaching a minimum at a/L ≈ 0.6
- At vanishing angular momentum (a=0), the convergence radius of the sound mode is Rc ≈ 1.41421 and of the shear diffusion mode is Rc ≈ 1.49131
- The convergence is enhanced for all hydrodynamic modes in a rapidly rotating fluid with a/L > 0.95
- The shear viscosity at a=0 is given by η0 = N²πT0³/8
- The speed of sound at a=0 is vs,0 = 1/√3
- The sound attenuation coefficient at a=0 is Γ0 = 1/3
- The diffusion coefficient at a=0 is D0 = 1/2

## Context

This work extends the analysis of hydrodynamic convergence from static systems, as previously studied by Grozdanov et al. (2019) for non-rotating plasmas, to a nontrivial time- and space-dependent rotating flow with large vorticity gradients. Unlike previous works that employed constant and uniform equilibrium states, this analysis is performed in a flow that resembles the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions as studied by the STAR collaboration (2017). The results provide guidance for future hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions with nonzero angular momentum and suggest that a boost symmetry similar to the one found here might be derivable for QCD.
