# Excited-State Hadrons using the Stochastic LapH Method

> Progress in computing the spectrum of excited baryons and mesons in lattice QCD is described. Large sets of spatially-extended hadron operators are used. A new method of stochastically estimating the low-lying effects of quark propagation is utilized which allows reliable determinations of temporal correlations of both single-hadron and multi-hadron operators. The method is tested on the eta, sigma, omega mesons.

## Key claim

The stochastic LapH method, which combines Laplacian Heaviside (LapH) quark-field smearing with diluted noise vectors introduced only in the LapH subspace, enables reliable determinations of temporal correlations for both single-hadron and multi-hadron operators, with the caveat that the method is tested only on the eta, sigma, and omega mesons in this work.

## Method

The authors constructed hadron operators from covariantly-displaced, smeared quark fields using LapH smearing, which projects onto the low-lying eigenmodes of the three-dimensional gauge-covariant Laplacian. They introduced random Z4 noise vectors diluted in time, spin, and LapH eigenvector indices to stochastically estimate slice-to-slice quark propagators, solving linear systems M X = eta for each diluted noise source. The method was tested on anisotropic lattices by computing correlation matrices and solving generalized eigenvalue problems to extract stationary-state energies.

## Key numbers

- Lattice parameters for operator selection: 16^3 x 128 anisotropic lattice, spatial spacing a_s ~ 0.12 fm, aspect ratio a_s/a_t ~ 3.5, pion mass m_pi ~ 380 MeV, using 50 to 100 configurations with N_f = 2+1 quark flavors
- Lattice parameters for isoscalar meson tests: 24^3 x 128 anisotropic lattice, spatial spacing a_s ~ 0.12 fm, aspect ratio a_s/a_t ~ 3.5, pion mass m_pi ~ 220 MeV, using 198 configurations with N_f = 2+1 quark flavors
- Dilution scheme for forward-time quark lines: (TF, SF, LI8) meaning full time dilution, full spin dilution, and interlace-8 LapH eigenvector dilution
- Dilution scheme for same-sink-time quark lines: (TI16, SF, LI8) meaning interlace-16 time dilution, full spin dilution, and interlace-8 LapH eigenvector dilution
- The stochastic LapH method yields nearly an order of magnitude reduction in statistical error over previous methods

## Context

This work extends the distillation method described in Peardon et al. (2009) by introducing stochastic estimation within the LapH subspace, enabling treatment of multi-hadron operators that require slice-to-slice quark propagators. The approach addresses the challenge that as pion masses decrease toward physical values, more multi-hadron states lie below excited resonances, making multi-hadron operator correlations essential. The method enables future computations of the QCD excitation spectrum using both single-hadron and multi-hadron operators on larger lattices (24^3 x 128 and 32^3 x 256) at pion masses of 380 MeV and 220 MeV.
