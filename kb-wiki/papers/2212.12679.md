# Collective flow of light nuclei and hyper-nuclei in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 3, 14.6, 19.6, 27, and 54.4 GeV using the STAR detector

> Light nuclei and hyper-nuclei are produced in abundance in heavy-ion collisions. The production mechanism of these species in heavy-ion collisions still remains to be understood. In these proceedings, we report the transverse momentum and centrality dependence of elliptic flow ($v_2$) of $d$, $t$, and $^3\text{He}$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 14.6, 19.6, 27, and 54.4 GeV. Mass number scaling of $v_2(p_T)$ of light nuclei is shown. We also report the first observation of hyper-nuclei $^{3}_Λ$H and $^{4}_Λ$H directed flow ($v_1$) in $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 3 GeV mid-central (5-40\%) Au+Au collisions in the fixed target mode.

# Collective flow of light nuclei and hyper-nuclei in Au+Au collisions at STAR

## Key claim
The paper reports that the directed flow ($v_1$) of hyper-nuclei $^{3}_{\Lambda}$H and $^{4}_{\Lambda}$H in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 3 GeV is consistent with that of light nuclei of similar mass within statistical uncertainties, which may suggest coalescence as the dominant production mechanism; however, the elliptic flow ($v_2$) of light nuclei ($d$, $t$, $^{3}$He) follows mass number scaling only within 20-30%, not exactly.

## Method
Elliptic flow ($v_2$) of deuterons ($d$), tritons ($t$), and helium-3 ($^{3}$He) was measured in minimum bias Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 14.6, 19.6, 27, and 54.4 GeV using the STAR Time Projection Chamber (TPC) for event plane reconstruction and particle identification via TPC energy loss and Time of Flight (TOF) mass-squared cuts. Directed flow ($v_1$) of $\Lambda$, $^{3}_{\Lambda}$H, and $^{4}_{\Lambda}$H was measured in mid-central (5-40%) Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 3 GeV in fixed-target mode, with hyper-nuclei reconstructed from their decay channels ($^{3}_{\Lambda}$H $\to$ $^{3}$He + $\pi^{-}$, $^{3}_{\Lambda}$H $\to$ $d$ + $p$ + $\pi^{-}$, $^{4}_{\Lambda}$H $\to$ $^{4}$He + $\pi^{-}$) and the first-order event plane determined by the Event Plane Detector (EPD).

## Key numbers
- Elliptic flow $v_2$ of $d$, $t$, and $^{3}$He shows a monotonic increase with transverse momentum $p_T$ across all four collision energies: $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 14.6, 19.6, 27, and 54.4 GeV.
- Light nuclei $v_2$ follows mass number scaling ($v_2/A$ vs $p_T/A$) within 20-30% when compared to proton $v_2$ at all four energies.
- Centrality dependence of $d$ $v_2$ was measured in two ranges (0-30% and 30-80%) at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 19.6, 27, and 54.4 GeV, with peripheral collisions showing higher $v_2$ than central collisions.
- Directed flow $v_1$ of $^{3}_{\Lambda}$H and $^{4}_{\Lambda}$H was measured in mid-central (5-40%) Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 3 GeV, and the mid-rapidity slope $dv_1/dy|_{y=0}$ is consistent with that of light nuclei of similar mass within statistical uncertainties.

## Context
This work extends the Beam Energy Scan Phase II (BES-II) program at RHIC by providing the first observation of hyper-nuclei directed flow in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 3 GeV. The elliptic flow results for light nuclei across multiple beam energies (14.6 to 54.4 GeV) build on prior coalescence model predictions (RefCoal1-5) that composite nuclei $v_2$ should scale with mass number. The hyper-nuclei $v_1$ results enable tests of hyperon-nucleon (YN) interactions in dense nuclear matter, as discussed in prior theoretical work (NS1, NS2), and the consistency with light nuclei flow supports coalescence as the dominant production mechanism for hyper-nuclei in these collisions.
