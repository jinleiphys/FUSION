# Critical Properties of Symmetric Nuclear Matter in Low-Density Regime Using Effective-Relativistic Mean Field Formalism

> The effective field theory motivated relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) formalism is employed to study the equation of state (EoS) for the infinite symmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature using the recently developed forces FSUGarnet, IOPB-I, G3, and the well known NL3 force parameter. The EoS is then used to estimate the critical temperature $T_c$, pressure $P_c$ and density $ρ_c$ of the symmetric nuclear matter for the liquid-gas phase transition. As $T_c$ is not a constrained parameter in both experiments and theoretical calculations, there is a large uncertainty around its value. Although, the critical parameters are correlated among themselves. It is revealed that vector self-coupling $ζ_0$ of used forces play determining role in EoS in finite temperature limit. Keeping the incompressibility in acceptable limit i.e. 240$\pm$ 20 MeV, the lower value of $ζ_0$ gives a better result of $T_c$ when compared to the several experimental data. The critical parameters however show weak correlation with the properties at saturation density at zero temperature. The compressibility factors calculated with these parameters are in agreement with the universal value of liquid-gas systems. Stability conditions are examined along with binodal and spinodal regions. Besides this, the thermodynamic properties like specific heat and latent heat are also worked out. We have carried out detailed consistency check of our calculations using critical exponents and standard scaling laws. All the exponents are well within the theoretical mean-field results.

## Key claim

The critical temperature of symmetric nuclear matter is not a constrained parameter and has large uncertainty; the authors show that among E-RMF forces with incompressibility in the accepted range of 240 ± 20 MeV, those with a lower value of the vector self-coupling ζ₀ give better agreement with experimental data for T_c, but the critical parameters show only weak correlation with saturation properties at zero temperature.

## Method

The authors extended the effective field theory motivated relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) formalism to finite temperature and calculated the equation of state for infinite symmetric nuclear matter using four force parameter sets: FSUGarnet, IOPB-I, G3, and NL3. They applied the grand canonical thermodynamic potential to obtain baryon and scalar densities, energy density, and pressure, then used Maxwell construction with Gibbs conditions to determine the liquid-gas coexistence curves, binodal and spinodal regions, and derived critical parameters, compressibility factors, specific heat, latent heat, and critical exponents.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature T_c: FSUGarnet = 13.80 MeV, IOPB-I = 13.75 MeV, G3 = 15.30 MeV, NL3 = 14.60 MeV
- Critical pressure P_c: FSUGarnet = 0.171 MeV fm⁻³, IOPB-I = 0.167 MeV fm⁻³, G3 = 0.218 MeV fm⁻³, NL3 = 0.202 MeV fm⁻³
- Critical density ρ_c: FSUGarnet = 0.043 fm⁻³, IOPB-I = 0.042 fm⁻³, G3 = 0.049 fm⁻³, NL3 = 0.046 fm⁻³
- Compressibility factor C_f: FSUGarnet = 0.282, IOPB-I = 0.277, G3 = 0.292, NL3 = 0.276; the universal value for liquid-gas systems is approximately 0.292
- Incompressibility K_∞: FSUGarnet = 229.50 MeV, IOPB-I = 222.65 MeV, G3 = 243.96 MeV, NL3 = 271.38 MeV; the accepted range from ISGMR is 240 ± 20 MeV
- Flash temperature T_f: FSUGarnet = 11.3 MeV, IOPB-I = 11.2 MeV, G3 = 12.1 MeV, NL3 = 11.8 MeV
- Experimental T_c values range from 12.0 ± 0.20 MeV (Exp. 1, 1984) to 20.0 ± 3.00 MeV (Exp. 4, 2003), with the 2013 experiment giving 17.9 ± 0.40 MeV and P_c = 0.31 ± 0.07 MeV fm⁻³, ρ_c = 0.06 ± 0.01 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends previous relativistic mean-field studies of the liquid-gas phase transition in symmetric nuclear matter, comparing newer E-RMF forces (FSUGarnet, IOPB-I, G3) with the well-known NL3 force and earlier parameter sets such as G1, G2, NL1, NL2, and NL-SH. The results are compared with experimental data from multifragmentation reactions (Exp. 1-6 from 1984-2013) and microscopic calculations such as the Friedman-Pandharipande (FP) approach. The study enables better understanding of how vector self-coupling ζ₀ influences finite-temperature behavior and provides correlations among critical parameters that could help constrain T_c from other measurable quantities.
