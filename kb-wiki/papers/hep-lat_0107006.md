# The static three-quark SU(3) and four-quark SU(4) potentials

> We present results on the static three- and four-quark potentials in SU(3) and SU(4) respectively within quenched lattice QCD. We use an analytic multi-hit procedure for the time links and a variational approach to determine the ground state. The three- and four-quark potentials extracted are consistent with a sum of two-body potentials, possibly with a weak many-body component. The results give support to the $Δ$ ansatz for the baryonic area law.

# The static three-quark SU(3) and four-quark SU(4) potentials

## Key claim
The static three- and four-quark potentials in quenched lattice QCD for SU(3) and SU(4) are consistent with a sum of two-body potentials (the Δ ansatz) up to distances of about 1.2 fm, with a possible weak many-body component at larger distances that could also be explained by residual excited-state contamination.

## Method
The authors computed baryonic Wilson loops for three quarks in SU(3) and four quarks in SU(4) on 16³×32 lattices using quenched configurations. They employed an analytic multi-hit procedure for temporal links, spatial link smearing, and a variational approach with a 4×4 correlation matrix to extract the ground-state potential. The potentials were compared to the Δ ansatz (sum of two-body potentials) and the Y ansatz (minimal flux-tube configuration with Steiner points).

## Key numbers
- For SU(3) at β=5.8: a√σ = 0.329(3) from the qq̄ potential
- For SU(3) at β=6.0: a√σ = 0.224(3) from the qq̄ potential
- For SU(4) at β=10.9: aσ = 0.238(4) from the qq̄ potential
- Maximum relative difference between Y and Δ ansätze for SU(3) equilateral triangle geometry: 15.4% (theoretical value 2/√3 − 1 = 0.154...)
- Maximum relative difference between Y and Δ ansätze for SU(4) regular tetrahedron geometry: 21.96%
- For SU(4), the difference between the X ansatz (single Steiner point) and Y ansatz (two Steiner points) is at most 3.5% for planar square geometry and only 0.43% for the tetrahedron
- The three-quark potential was studied at distances up to 1.2 fm

## Context
This work extends the debate between the Δ ansatz (Cornwall, 1970s) and Y ansatz for baryonic potentials, following conflicting lattice results: Bali (β=6.0) favored the Δ ansatz while Takahashi et al. (β=5.7) favored the Y ansatz. The authors improved upon Takahashi et al. by using an analytic multi-hit procedure and variational approach not employed in that earlier study. This is the first measurement of the four-quark potential in SU(4). The results support the Δ ansatz for both SU(3) and SU(4) up to 1.2 fm, but the authors note that more refined noise-reduction techniques are needed to determine whether a genuine many-body component exists at larger distances.
