# Low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction and its application to the few-nucleon systems

> Low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interactions are derived within the framework of a unitary-transformation theory, starting with realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions. A cutoff momentum Lambda is introduced to specify a border between the low- and high-momentum spaces. By the Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations the low-momentum interactions are investigated with respect to the dependence of ground-state energies of ^3H and ^4He on the parameter Lambda. It is found that we need the momentum cutoff parameter Lambda >= 5 fm^-1 in order to reproduce satisfactorily the exact values of the binding energies for ^3H and ^4He. The calculation with Lambda =2 fm^-1 recommended by Bogner et al. leads to considerable overbinding at least for the few-nucleon systems.

## Key claim

Low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interactions derived via unitary transformation can reproduce exact few-nucleon binding energies only when the cutoff momentum Lambda is at least 5 fm^-1; the cutoff value of 2 fm^-1 recommended by Bogner et al. leads to considerable overbinding in the three- and four-nucleon systems, contrary to the expectation that such interactions are directly applicable to many-body calculations.

## Method

The authors constructed low-momentum nucleon-nucleon (LMNN) interactions from the CD-Bonn and Nijm-I realistic potentials using two independent unitary-transformation approaches: one based on the Okubo form solved via a linearized integral equation for the wave operator omega, and the other based on the unitary-model-operator approach (UMOA). They then solved the Faddeev equations for tritium (3H) and the Yakubovsky equations for helium-4 (4He) using these LMNN interactions for various cutoff momenta Lambda, and compared the resulting ground-state energies to the exact values obtained with the original untransformed potentials.

## Key numbers

- For the deuteron, the LMNN interaction reproduces the exact binding energy of -2.224576 MeV (CD-Bonn) and -2.224575 MeV (Nijm-I) for all cutoff values from Lambda = 1.0 to 7.0 fm^-1.
- The exact ground-state energy of 3H using the original CD-Bonn potential is -8.25 MeV; using the Nijm-I potential it is -8.01 MeV.
- The exact ground-state energy of 4He using the original CD-Bonn potential (in the S-wave 5+5-channel approximation without Coulomb force) is -27.74 MeV.
- At Lambda = 2.0 fm^-1, the energy shift for 3H is about 750 keV (CD-Bonn) and about 1 MeV (Nijm-I); for 4He at the same cutoff the shift amounts to about 3 MeV.
- To reproduce the exact binding energies within 1 keV for 3H, a cutoff Lambda of approximately 8 fm^-1 is needed; to avoid significant deviations, Lambda must be at least 5 fm^-1.

## Context

This work extends the low-momentum interaction approach of Bogner et al. (2003), who proposed using LMNN interactions with Lambda around 2.1 fm^-1 for nuclear structure calculations and found good results for shell-model spectra of 18O, 134Te, and 135I. The present study tests these interactions in exactly solvable few-nucleon systems (3H and 4He) using Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations, and finds that the small-cutoff LMNN interactions produce significant overbinding. The authors recommend using Lambda of order 5 fm^-1 or higher for many-body calculations, and note that eliminating high-momentum components would generate repulsive many-nucleon forces that could compensate for the missing attractive three-nucleon forces needed with realistic NN potentials.
