# Coherent and chaotic properties of nuclear pairing

> The properties of the pairing interaction in the shell model framework are considered with the aid of the exact numerical solution utilizing the quasispin symmetry. We emphasize the features which are out of reach for the usual approximate techniques based on the BCS approach supplemented by the random phase approximation treatment of pair vibrations, especially in the region of weak pairing where the BCS+RPA theory fails. For the first time chaotic aspects of the mixing generated by the pairing interaction are studied. The level repulsion and large information entropy of the eigenstates coexist with the absence of thermalization of single-particle motion. The full spectrum of pair vibration in average displays the spin dependence similar to that for a rigid rotor.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that pairing interactions in nuclei produce both coherent (collective pair vibrations) and chaotic (level repulsion, large information entropy) properties simultaneously, but this chaotic mixing does not lead to thermalization of single-particle motion. The authors emphasize that these features are particularly pronounced in the weak pairing regime where standard BCS+RPA theory fails.

## Method

The authors solved the pairing Hamiltonian exactly using the quasispin SU(2) algebra formalism, which reduces the problem to coupling of partial quasispins for individual j-levels and allows classification of states by seniority quantum numbers. They applied this method to a realistic shell-model description of 116Sn with five single-neutron orbitals (h11/2, d3/2, s1/2, g7/2, d5/2) using pairing matrix elements from a G-matrix derived from the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The exact numerical diagonalization was performed in the seniority-zero subspace (110 states), and results were compared with BCS+RPA predictions for pair vibrations.

## Key numbers

- The model space for 116Sn contains 601,080,390 many-body states, among which 272,828 have spin zero and can be partitioned into 420 different seniority sets; the fully paired set of seniority zero has dimension 110.
- Single-particle energies for the five neutron orbitals: h11/2 at -9.76 MeV, d3/2 at -8.98 MeV, s1/2 at -7.33 MeV, g7/2 at -7.66 MeV, and d5/2 at -7.57 MeV.
- The GOE limit for information entropy with 110 basis states is I_GOE = 3.97 (from ln(0.482 x 110)).
- In the two-level model with half occupancy and equal capacities, the RPA predicts pair vibration frequency omega^2 = 8 Delta^2; for many interacting levels with constant pairing, the spectrum starts at omega^2 = 4 Delta^2.
- The BCS phase transition in the two-level model occurs at critical coupling g_c = 0.25 (in units where |epsilon_1,2| = 1).
- The spectral rigidity analysis shows Poisson-like behavior for intervals up to L approximately 7, then flattens indicating regular oscillatory behavior.

## Context

This work extends the exact treatment of pairing in finite nuclei beyond the BCS+RPA framework, which fails in the weak pairing region near subshell closures such as 114Sn. The authors build on the quasispin method of Kerman (1961) and their earlier exact pairing (EP) technique (Volya, Zelevinsky, Brown, 2000) to study chaotic aspects of pairing for the first time. The results enable future investigation of how pairing interacts with other components of the residual interaction in the shell model, and the authors note that the interplay of pairing with other interactions has shown unexpected results in earlier shell model analyses (Zelevinsky, Brown, Frazier, Horoi, 1996).
