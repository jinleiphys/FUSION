# Pion-proton scattering and isospin breaking in the $Δ^0-Δ^{++}$ system

> We determine the mass and width of the $Δ^{++}\ (Δ^0)$ resonance from data on $π^+ p\ (π^- p)$ scattering both, in the pole of the $S$-matrix and conventional Breit-Wigner approaches to the scattering amplitude. We provide a simple formula that relates the two definitions for the parameters of the $Δ$. Isospin symmetry breaking in the $\d0-\dm$ system depends on the definition of the resonant properties: we find $M_0-M_{++} = 0.40 \pm 0.57\ {\rm MeV},\ Γ_0 -Γ_{++} = 6.89 \pm 0.95\ {\rm MeV}$ in the pole approach while $\wt{M}_0-\wt{M}_{++} = 2.25 \pm 0.68\ {\rm MeV},\ \wtΓ_0 - \wtΓ_{++} = 8.45 \pm 1.11\ {\rm MeV}$ in the conventional approach.

## Key claim

Isospin symmetry breaking in the masses and widths of the Δ⁰ and Δ⁺⁺ resonances depends on whether the resonance parameters are defined via the S-matrix pole approach or the conventional Breit-Wigner approach; the pole approach yields a mass difference consistent with zero (M₀ − M₊₊ = 0.40 ± 0.57 MeV), while the conventional approach gives a larger splitting (M̃₀ − M̃₊₊ = 2.25 ± 0.68 MeV).

## Method

The authors fit experimental π⁺p and π⁻p scattering cross section data from Pedroni et al. (1995) in the (I,J) = (3/2,3/2) partial wave, using an amplitude that separates the Δ resonance pole from a smooth background function x(s). For the Δ⁰, isospin breaking from π⁺−π⁰ and n−p mass differences is incorporated via energy-dependent partial widths with a residual isospin-breaking parameter ε. The pole parameters (M, Γ) are extracted directly, and the conventional Breit-Wigner parameters (M̃, Γ̃) are derived from the pole parameters using analytic relations.

## Key numbers

- Δ⁺⁺ pole mass M₊₊ = 1210.0 ± 0.5 MeV (case A fit)
- Δ⁺⁺ pole width Γ₊₊ = 97.5 ± 0.8 MeV (case A fit)
- Δ⁰ pole mass M₀ = 1210.4 ± 0.4 MeV (case C fit)
- Δ⁰ pole width Γ₀ = 104.4 ± 0.5 MeV (case C fit)
- Isospin breaking in pole approach: M₀ − M₊₊ = 0.40 ± 0.57 MeV, Γ₀ − Γ₊₊ = 6.89 ± 0.95 MeV
- Isospin breaking in conventional approach: M̃₀ − M̃₊₊ = 2.25 ± 0.68 MeV, Γ̃₀ − Γ̃₊₊ = 8.45 ± 1.11 MeV
- Ratio of background contributions at threshold: x₀(sₜₕ)/x₊₊(sₜₕ) ≈ 1.07, corresponding to Γ₀/Γ₊₊ ≈ 1.07

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Δ resonance parameters by explicitly separating pole and background contributions in πp scattering, following the S-matrix pole definition advocated by Peierls (1959) and others. The results contradict the naive expectation from the neutron-proton mass difference (which would predict M₀ − M₊₊ ≈ 2.6 MeV) when using the pole definition, but agree with that expectation when using the conventional Breit-Wigner definition. The analysis enables future studies of isospin breaking in baryon resonances using the theoretically preferred pole definition, and provides a formula relating the two parameter definitions that can be applied to other resonances.
