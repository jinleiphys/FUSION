# The Two-Nucleon System in Three Dimensions

> A recently developed formulation for treating two- and three-nucleon bound states in a three-dimensional formulation based on spin-momentum operators is extended to nucleon-nucleon scattering. Here the nucleon-nucleon t-matrix is represented by six spin-momentum operators accompanied by six scalar functions of momentum vectors. We present the formulation and provide numerical examples for the deuteron and nucleon-nucleon scattering observables. A comparison to results from a standard partial wave decomposition establishes the reliability of this new formulation.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previously developed three-dimensional (3D) spin-momentum operator formulation for two- and three-nucleon bound states to nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering, claiming that the new approach yields numerically stable results in perfect agreement with standard partial wave decomposition calculations for the deuteron and NN scattering observables, but only for energies up to 300 MeV and for the specific potentials tested (chiral NNLO and Bonn B).

## Method

The NN t-matrix is expanded into six spin-momentum operators multiplied by six scalar functions of momentum vectors, and inserted into the Lippmann-Schwinger equation. The spin dependence is removed analytically by taking traces over spin states, yielding a set of six coupled integral equations for the scalar functions that depend only on vector momenta. For the deuteron, the Schrodinger equation is similarly projected onto two scalar functions related to s- and d-wave components, and solved as a generalized eigenvalue problem. For scattering, the coupled equations are discretized on grids of momentum magnitudes and angles and solved directly as a system of linear algebraic equations, avoiding iterative methods that suffer from near-singular matrices.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy for chiral NNLO potential: Ed = -2.19993 MeV, with s-state probability Ps = 95.291%
- Deuteron binding energy for Bonn B potential: Ed = -2.2242 MeV, with s-state probability Ps = 95.014%
- Grid sizes used for scattering calculations: nx = 36 Gaussian points for angular integration, np = 36 Gaussian points for momentum grids up to 40 fm^-1, n_phi = 60 Gaussian points for azimuthal integration
- Laboratory kinetic energies tested: 13 MeV, 150 MeV, and 300 MeV for Bonn B; 13 MeV and 150 MeV for chiral NNLO

## Context

This work extends the 3D formulation previously developed for two- and three-nucleon bound states (Golak et al., 2009) to NN scattering, building on earlier helicity formulations for NN scattering (Fachruddin et al., 2000) and 3D approaches for three-boson systems (Elster, 1998; Liu, 2004). The method avoids the increasing complexity of partial wave decomposition at higher energies, where many partial waves are needed for convergence, and enables future extension to three-nucleon Faddeev equations in a 3D framework without angular momentum decomposition.
