# Charge form factors of two-neutron halo nuclei in halo EFT

> We set up a formalism to calculate the charge form factors of two-neutron halo nuclei with S-wave neutron-core interactions in the framework of the halo effective field theory. The method is applied to some known and suspected halo nuclei. In particular, we calculate the form factors and charge radii relative to the core to leading order in the halo EFT and compare to experiments where they are available. Moreover, we investigate the general dependence of the charge radius on the core mass and the one- and two-neutron separation energies.

## Key claim

The authors set up a formalism within halo effective field theory (EFT) to calculate charge form factors and charge radii of two-neutron halo nuclei with S-wave neutron-core interactions, but the calculated charge radius is interpreted as the value *relative to the core* because the core and neutrons are treated as pointlike at leading order, with core size entering only at higher orders.

## Method

The authors construct a non-relativistic effective field theory for a spin-0 core and two spin-1/2 neutrons interacting through resonant S-wave short-range interactions, using auxiliary dimer and trimer fields. They derive the particle-dimer T-matrix from a generalized Skorniakov-Ter-Martyrosian integral equation, extract the trimer wave function renormalization from the bound-state pole, and compute the charge form factor as the sum of three classes of irreducible diagrams where the photon couples to the core. The three-body coupling is fixed by requiring a bound state at the desired energy, and the charge radius is extracted from the small-momentum-transfer expansion of the form factor.

## Key numbers

- For the two-neutron halo nuclei studied, the charge radius is calculated relative to the core charge radius; the neutron charge radius is given as ⟨r²_E⟩_n = −0.115(4) fm² (from Kopecky (1997)) but is neglected in the leading-order calculation.
- The two-body bound state binding energy is given by B_i = 1/(2μ_i a_i²) where a_i is the scattering length in the respective channel.
- The form factor expansion is F_E(Q²) = 1 − ⟨r²_E⟩ Q²/6 + O(Q⁴), with the charge radius extracted via ⟨r²_E⟩ = −6 lim_{Q²→0⁺} dF_E/dQ².

## Context

This work extends previous halo EFT studies of two-neutron halo nuclei by Canham and Hammer (2008, 2009), who calculated matter density form factors and mean square radii using an effective quantum mechanics framework, and by Acharya et al. (2013) who analyzed matter-radius measurements for ²²C. The formalism also builds on earlier extensions of halo EFT to include electromagnetic currents in two-body halo nuclei by Hammer (2011), Rupak (2011, 2012), and Acharya (2013). The authors anticipate that charge radii of S-wave halo candidates will become experimentally accessible through laser spectroscopy of radioactive ions, enabling direct comparison with these predictions.
