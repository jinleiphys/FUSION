# Spectator induced electromagnetic effects in heavy-ion collisions and space-time-momentum conditions for pion emission

> We present our calculation of electromagnetic effects, induced by the spectator charge on Feynman-$x_F$ distributions of charged pions in peripheral $Pb+Pb$ collisions at CERN SPS energies, including realistic initial space-time-momentum conditions for pion emission. The calculation is performed in the framework of our simplified implementation of the fire-streak model, adopted to the production of both $π^-$ and $π^+$ mesons. Isospin effects are included to take into account the asymmetry in production of $π^+$ and $π^-$ at high rapidity. A comparison to a simpler model from the literature is made. We obtain a good description of the NA49 data on the $x_F$- and $p_T$-dependence of the ratio of cross sections $π^+/π^-$. The experimental data favors short times ($0.5<τ<2$~fm/$c$) for fast pion creation in the local fire-streak rest frame. The possibility of the expansion of the spectators is considered in our calculation, and its influence on the electromagnetic effect observed for the $π^+/π^-$ ratio is discussed. In addition we discuss the relation between anisotropic flow and the electromagnetic distortion of $π^+/π^-$ ratios, and study the influence of transverse expansion of fire streaks as well as their vorticity on this distortion. In this latter study we find that inclusion of rotation of fire streaks in our model gives a satisfactory description of the rapidity dependence of pion directed flow. We conclude that {our implementation of the} fire-streak model, which properly describes the centrality dependence of $π^-$ rapidity spectra at CERN SPS energies, also provides a quantitative description of the electromagnetic effect on the $π^+/π^-$ ratio as a function of $x_F$.

## Key claim

The fire-streak model with realistic initial space-time-momentum conditions for pion emission can quantitatively describe the electromagnetic distortion of the π⁺/π⁻ ratio as a function of Feynman-xF in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS energies, but only when the pion creation time in the local fire-streak rest frame is short (0.5 < τ < 2 fm/c) and when a linear dependence of creation time on fire-streak excitation energy is introduced, rather than a single fixed value.

## Method

The authors implemented a simplified version of the fire-streak model, where each fire streak is defined by energy-momentum conservation from the overlap of two nuclear "bricks" in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions. The initial rapidity distribution of π⁻ was taken from the fire-streak fragmentation function fitted to NA49 data, while the transverse-momentum distribution was taken from UrQMD v3.4 simulations and parametrized by an exponential function. The electromagnetic evolution of charged pions in the field of both spectator systems was computed using a Lorentz-invariant formalism for evolution times up to 10⁴ fm/c, with the initial pion creation time τ treated as a free parameter in the fire-streak rest frame and then Lorentz-transformed to the center-of-mass system.

## Key numbers

* The fire-streak fragmentation function parameters fitted to NA49 centrality-selected Pb+Pb data gave A = 0.05598, σ_y = 1.475, and r = 2.55.
* The inverse slope parameters from UrQMD v3.4 fits at midrapidity were T_π⁻ = 165 MeV and T_π⁺ = 163 MeV for peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV/c beam momentum.
* The experimental data favors short pion creation times in the local fire-streak rest frame of 0.5 < τ < 2 fm/c.
* The electromagnetic evolution required t_max > 10³ fm/c for convergence of the π⁺/π⁻ ratio, with t_max of order 10⁴ fm/c used in the calculation.
* The isospin correction parameter for positive pions was I_c = 1.175, providing agreement within 2-3% of the π⁺/π⁻ ratio from NA49 p+p data.
* The linear dependence of pion creation time on fire-streak excitation energy used τ_min = 0.5 fm/c, τ_max = 2 fm/c, and a ≈ 0.08 (for energies in GeV).

## Context

This work extends the earlier point-like source model of Rybicki and Szczurek (2007) which described the NA49 data on the π⁺/π⁻ ratio with a single free parameter d_E ≈ 0.5-1 fm, but could not provide a deeper interpretation of that distance. The present fire-streak model, previously shown to describe the centrality dependence of π⁻ rapidity spectra at SPS energies, now provides realistic initial conditions for the electromagnetic evolution and demonstrates that the success of the point-like source model arises from the cooperation of different emission zones. The inclusion of fire-streak rotation gives a satisfactory description of the rapidity dependence of pion directed flow, connecting the electromagnetic distortion to anisotropic flow observations from NA49 and WA98.
