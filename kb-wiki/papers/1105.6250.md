# Study of the transition from pairing vibrational to pairing rotational regimes between magic numbers N=50 and N=82, with two-nucleon transfer

> Absolute values of two-particle transfer cross sections along the Sn-isotopic chain from closed shell to closed shell (100Sn,132Sn) are calculated taking properly into account nuclear correlations, as well as the successive, simultaneous and non-orthogonality contributions to the differential cross sections. The results are compared with systematic, homogeneous bombarding conditions (p, t) data. The observed agreement, almost within statistical errors and without free parameters, testify to the fact that theory is able to be quantitative in its predictions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a unified nuclear structure–reaction mechanism description, without free parameters, can reproduce absolute two-particle transfer cross sections on open-shell Sn isotopes within experimental errors; the same framework then provides quantitative predictions for the pairing vibrational spectrum around the closed-shell nucleus ¹³²Sn, where the relative importance of successive versus simultaneous transfer can be disentangled by tuning bombarding energy near threshold.

## Method

Absolute differential cross sections for (p,t) reactions on Sn isotopes (A = 124, 122, 120, 118, 116, 112) were calculated using two-particle transfer spectroscopic amplitudes from the Cooper pair condensate (Bν = (jν+1/2)^(1/2) Uν Vν) and standard optical parameters. Successive, simultaneous, and non-orthogonality contributions (post representation) were all included, and the results were compared with systematic experimental data from Guazzoni et al. (1999–2011). The same formalism was then applied to predict cross sections for ¹³²Sn(p,t)¹³⁰Sn and ¹³²Sn(t,p)¹³⁴Sn at various center-of-mass energies.

## Key numbers

- Calculated cross sections for open-shell Sn isotopes agree with experiment within estimated 15% systematic experimental error, and almost within statistical errors.
- For ¹³²Sn(p,t)¹³⁰Sn(gs) at ECM = 10.07 MeV, the total integrated cross section (0°–80°) is 39.02 μb; successive contributes 44.44 μb, simultaneous 10.9 μb, non-orthogonality 22.68 μb, simultaneous+non-orthogonality 3.18 μb, and pairing force 0.97 μb.
- At ECM = 15.04 MeV, the total integrated cross section is 750.2 μb; successive contributes 863.8 μb, simultaneous 156.7 μb, non-orthogonality 233.5 μb, simultaneous+non-orthogonality 17.4 μb, and pairing force 14.04 μb.
- At ECM = 5.11 MeV, the total cross section is 1.29 × 10⁻¹⁷ μb; at ECM = 6.1 MeV, it is 3.77 × 10⁻⁸ μb.
- The two-phonon pairing vibrational 0⁺ state in ¹³²Sn is predicted at an excitation energy of 6.6 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the pairing rotational and pairing vibrational concepts of Anderson (1958) and Bes (1966) by providing quantitative, parameter-free calculations of absolute two-nucleon transfer cross sections across the entire Sn isotopic chain from N=50 to N=82. It builds on systematic (p,t) data from Guazzoni et al. (1999–2011) and the theoretical framework of Brink and Broglia (2005) and Broglia et al. (2000). The predictions for ¹³²Sn open the possibility of experimentally distinguishing successive from simultaneous transfer mechanisms by exploiting threshold-energy interference effects, analogous to the role of the Josephson effect in understanding pair tunneling.
