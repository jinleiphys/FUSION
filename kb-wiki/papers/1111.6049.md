# Hyperons and massive neutron stars: the role of hyperon potentials

> The constituents of cold dense matter are still far from being understood. However, neutron star observations such as the recently observed pulsar PSR J1614-2230 with a mass of 1.97+/-0.04 M_solar help to considerably constrain the hadronic equation of state (EoS). We systematically investigate the influence of the hyperon potentials on the stiffness of the EoS. We find that they have but little influence on the maximum mass compared to the inclusion of an additional vector meson mediating repulsive interaction amongst hyperons. The new mass limit can only be reached with this additional meson regardless of the hyperon potentials. Further, we investigate the impact of the nuclear compression modulus and the effective mass of the nucleon at saturation density on the high density regime of the EoS. We show that the maximum mass of purely nucleonic stars is very sensitive to the effective nucleon mass but only very little to the compression modulus.

## Key claim

The paper systematically investigates whether hyperons can exist in massive neutron stars consistent with the 1.97 solar mass pulsar PSR J1614-2230, and finds that varying the hyperon potential depths alone has little effect on the maximum mass; the observed mass limit can only be reached by including an additional vector meson (the phi meson) mediating repulsive interactions among hyperons, regardless of the hyperon potential values.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic mean field (RMF) model with the full baryon octet plus leptons, using the standard sigma-omega-rho meson exchange with nonlinear scalar and vector self-interactions. They systematically vary the hyperon potential depths for Sigma and Xi hyperons in the range -40 to +40 MeV, and separately vary the effective nucleon mass and compression modulus at saturation density, comparing models with and without the strange vector phi meson that mediates repulsive hyperon-hyperon interactions.

## Key numbers

- The observed pulsar PSR J1614-2230 has a mass of 1.97 +/- 0.04 solar masses, providing the tightest lower bound on the maximum neutron star mass
- In the model without phi meson (sigma-omega-rho model with GM1 parameter set), the maximum mass is 1.75 solar masses for varying Sigma potential, and ranges from 1.69 solar masses (for Xi potential -40 MeV) to 1.92 solar masses (for Xi potential +40 MeV)
- Including the phi meson increases the maximum mass by nearly 0.21 solar masses to 1.96 solar masses for the Sigma variation case, and to 2.04 solar masses for Xi potential +40 MeV
- For purely nucleonic stars, maximum masses drop from 2.88 solar masses at effective nucleon mass ratio 0.55 to 1.87-2.01 solar masses at ratio 0.8
- For hyperonic stars in the sigma-omega-rho model, maximum masses range from 2.0-2.1 solar masses at effective mass ratio 0.55 to about 1.39-1.63 solar masses at ratio 0.8
- In the sigma-omega-rho-phi model, hyperonic star maximum masses range from 2.4 solar masses at effective mass ratio 0.55 to 1.6 solar masses at ratio 0.8
- The compression modulus variation (200-300 MeV) causes a mass difference of less than 0.05 solar masses for pure nucleonic stars at moderate effective masses, and at most 0.1 solar masses at high effective masses

## Context

This work extends earlier RMF model studies by Knorren, Schaffner-Bielich (1996) and others that found hyperons soften the equation of state and reduce maximum masses, often below 1.4 solar masses in Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by Vidana (2000) and Baldo (1998, 2000). The paper directly addresses the challenge posed by the Demorest (2010) measurement of PSR J1614-2230, showing that the phi meson is necessary to satisfy the new mass constraint. It enables future work (announced as a subsequent paper) on the underlying symmetries governing repulsive interactions among hyperons and nucleons, and provides a parameter map showing which combinations of hyperon potentials and effective nucleon masses are compatible with the observed 1.97 solar mass pulsar.
