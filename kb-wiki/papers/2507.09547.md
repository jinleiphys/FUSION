# Light and heavy $Λ$ hyperclusters in nuclear matter with relativistic-mean-field models

> In the framework of relativistic-mean-field (RMF) models, we investigate the properties of light and heavy $Λ$ hyperclusters emersed in nuclear matter at various densities $n_{\mathrm{gas}}$ and proton fractions $Y_p$. In particular, the (hyper)clusters are fixed by solving the Dirac equations imposing the Dirichlet-Neumann boundary condition, while the nuclear matter take constant densities and is treated with Thomas-Fermi approximation. The binding energies of (hyper)clusters decrease with the density of nuclear matter $n_{\mathrm{gas}}$, which eventually become unbound and melt in the presence of nuclear medium, i.e., Mott transition. For light clusters with proton numbers $N_p < 4$, with the addition of $Λ$ hyperons, the binding energies per baryon for $Λ$ hyperclusters become smaller and decrease faster with $n_{\mathrm{gas}}$ due to the weaker $N$-$Λ$ attraction. For heavy clusters with $N_p \geq 4$, on the contrary, the addition of $Λ$ hyperons increases the stability of (hyper)clusters so that the Mott transition density becomes larger as nucleons occupying higher energy states while $Λ$ hyperons remain in the $1s_{1/2}$ orbital. The isovector effects on (hyper)clusters in nuclear medium are also identified, where the binding energies for (hyper)clusters with $N_p> N_n$ ($N_p< N_n$) increase (decrease) with $Y_p$. For those predicted by nonlinear relativistic density functionals, light (hyper)clusters are destabilized drastically as $n_{\mathrm{gas}}$ increases, while the binding energies of heavier (hyper)clusters vary smoothly with $n_{\mathrm{gas}}$. The binding energy shifts of various (hyper)clusters due to the impact of nuclear medium are fitted to an analytical formula, which could be employed to examine the evolutions of (hyper)clusters in both heavy-ion collisions and neutron stars.

# Light and heavy $\Lambda$ hyperclusters in nuclear matter with relativistic-mean-field models

## Key claim

The binding energies of $\Lambda$ hyperclusters in nuclear matter decrease with the density of the surrounding nuclear gas, and the Mott transition density (where clusters become unbound and melt) depends on cluster size: for light clusters with proton number $N_p < 4$, adding $\Lambda$ hyperons reduces stability and lowers the Mott density, while for heavy clusters with $N_p \geq 4$, adding $\Lambda$ hyperons increases stability and raises the Mott density because nucleons occupy higher energy states while $\Lambda$ hyperons remain in the $1s_{1/2}$ orbital.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation for nucleons and $\Lambda$ hyperons in a spherical Wigner-Seitz cell with Dirichlet-Neumann boundary conditions, treating the cluster quantum-mechanically and the surrounding nuclear matter as a uniform gas via the Thomas-Fermi approximation. Three relativistic density functionals (DD-LZ1, TM1, and PK1) are used for the nucleon-nucleon interactions, while the $\Lambda$-nucleon coupling constants $g_{\sigma\Lambda}$ and $g_{\omega\Lambda}$ are calibrated to reproduce the experimental $\Lambda$ separation energies $B_\Lambda$ for single-$\Lambda$ hypernuclei across a mass range $A=4$ to $208$, with the $\Lambda$ mean-field potential depth in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation set to $U_\Lambda(n_0) = -30$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- The $\Lambda$ mean-field potential depth in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density is set to $U_\Lambda(n_0) = -30$ MeV.
- The root-mean-square deviations of predicted $B_\Lambda$ from experimental values are $\Delta = 1.30$ MeV (DD-LZ1), $0.74$ MeV (TM1), and $0.86$ MeV (PK1); the corresponding mean squared errors are $8.54\%$, $9.85\%$, and $7.34\%$.
- For ${}^6_{\Lambda\Lambda}$He, the $\Lambda$-$\Lambda$ interaction energy is $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = -0.133$ MeV (repulsive), while the experimental value from the KEK E373 experiment is $\Delta B_{\Lambda\Lambda} = 0.67 \pm 0.17$ MeV (attractive).
- The binding energy per baryon for $^3$H is $3.747$ MeV (DD-LZ1), compared to the experimental value $2.827$ MeV; for $^4$He it is $6.832$ MeV, compared to $7.074$ MeV.
- The charge radius of $^3$H is predicted as $2.090$ fm (experimental $1.759$ fm); for $^4$He it is $1.988$ fm (experimental $1.675$ fm).
- For $^{208}$Pb, the predicted binding energy per nucleon is $7.871$ MeV (experimental $7.867$ MeV) and charge radius is $5.485$ fm (experimental $5.501$ fm).
- The $\Lambda$ separation energy $B_\Lambda$ for ${}^{208}_\Lambda$Pb is $25.49$ MeV (DD-LZ1).
- The Wigner-Seitz cell radius used in calculations is $R_\mathrm{W} = 12.8$ fm with a grid spacing of $0.1$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous unified treatment of normal nuclear clusters in a Wigner-Seitz cell (Xia 2025) to the hyperonic sector, using the same relativistic-mean-field framework. It builds on experimental constraints on $\Lambda$ separation energies from hypernuclei studies (Hashimoto 2006, Gal 2016) and on the relativistic density functionals DD-LZ1 (Wei 2020), TM1 (Sugahara 1994), and PK1 (Long 2004). The results enable improved modeling of hypernuclei production in heavy-ion collisions and the equation of state of neutron star matter containing hyperons, where the in-medium binding energy shifts of hyperclusters can be incorporated via the fitted analytical formula.
