# Symmetry energy effects on the properties of hybrid stars

> Symmetry energy is an important part of the equation of state of isospin asymmetry matter. However, the huge uncertainties of symmetry energy remain at suprasaturation densities, where the phase transitions of strongly interacting matter and the quark matter symmetry energy are likely to be taken into account. In this work, we investigate the properties of symmetry energy by using a hybrid star with the hadron-quark phase transition. The interaction among strange quark matter (SQM) in hybrid stars is based on a 3-flavor NJL model with different vector and isovector channels, while the equation of state (EOS) of the nuclear matter is obtained by considering the ImMDI-ST interaction by varying the parameters x, y, and z. Our results indicate that the various parameters and coupling constants of the interactions from the ImMDI-ST and NJL model can lead to widely different trends for the symmetry energy in the hadron-quark mixed phase and the different onsets of the hadron-quark phase transition. In addition, it has been found that the radii and tidal deformabilities of hybrid stars constrain mostly the density dependence of symmetry energy while the observed maximum masses of hybrid stars constrain mostly the EOS of symmetric nuclear and quark matter.

## Key claim

The symmetry energy in hybrid stars is highly sensitive to both the parameters of the nuclear interaction (ImMDI-ST) and the coupling constants of the quark matter model (NJL), but the radii and tidal deformabilities of hybrid stars constrain mostly the density dependence of symmetry energy while the observed maximum masses constrain mostly the equation of state of symmetric nuclear and quark matter, meaning that these two observables probe different aspects of the hybrid star structure.

## Method

The authors constructed hybrid star models with a hadronic phase at low densities described by the improved isospin- and momentum-dependent (ImMDI-ST) interaction, a mixed hadron-quark phase at moderate densities using Gibbs conditions, and a quark core at high densities described by a 3-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with vector and isovector couplings. They varied three parameters (x, y, z) in the ImMDI-ST interaction to adjust the isovector properties of nuclear matter and two coupling constants (R_V and R_IV) in the NJL model to modify the quark matter equation of state, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers

- The saturation density of nuclear matter is taken as ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, with binding energy E₀(ρ₀) = -15.9 MeV, incompressibility K₀ = 250 MeV, and symmetry energy E_sym(ρ₀) = 32.5 MeV for the default ImMDI-ST parameters (x=0, y=0, z=0)
- The hadron-quark phase transition appears in the density range approximately 2ρ₀ to 6ρ₀ depending on the parameters and coupling constants
- The slope parameter L(ρ₀) from 24 new analyses of neutron star observables since GW170817 was about 57.7 ± 19 MeV at 68% confidence level, while the PREX-II experiment implies L(ρ₀) = 106 ± 37 MeV
- The fiducial value from the 2016 survey of 53 analyses gives E_sym(ρ₀) = 31.7 ± 3.2 MeV and L(ρ₀) = 58.7 ± 28.1 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of symmetry energy constraints from terrestrial nuclear experiments and astrophysical observations, particularly following the gravitational wave events GW170817 and GW190814 which provided new constraints on tidal deformabilities and maximum masses. The authors build on the ImMDI interaction framework developed by Xu (2015) and the NJL model studies of vector and isovector couplings by Liu (2016), and compare their results with constraints from the SCGF approach by Carbone (2014, 2018) and chiral effective many-body perturbation theory by Wellenhofer (2015, 2016). The study enables future work to use combined mass, radius, and tidal deformability measurements to simultaneously constrain the isovector properties of both nuclear and quark matter in hybrid stars.
