# Coherent photo- and electroproduction of charmonia on nuclear targets revisited: Green function formalism

> We study for the first time the production of charmonia in nuclear ultra-peripheral and electron-ion collisions based on a rigorous Green function formalism. Such formalism allows to incorporate properly formation effects (color transparency), as well as the quantum coherence inherent in higher twist shadowing corrections related to the $|Q \bar{Q}\rangle$ Fock component of the photon. The leading twist gluon shadowing associated with multi-gluon photon fluctuations is also included within the same formalism. The later effect represents the dominant source of shadowing at mid rapidities in the LHC kinematic region, while the reduced effect of quark shadowing leads to a significant modification of differential cross sections $dσ/dy$ at forward and/or backwards rapidities. Model calculations for $dσ/dy$ are in a good agreement with available UPC data on coherent charmonium production at RHIC and the LHC. In addition, we also perform predictions for nuclear effects in the electroproduction of charmonia, which can be verified by new data from electron-ion colliders.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first application of a rigorous Green function formalism to charmonium production in nuclear ultra-peripheral and electron-ion collisions, which allows proper incorporation of formation effects (color transparency) and quantum coherence effects that were previously treated only approximately via effective form factors and simplified potentials.

## Method

The authors employ a light-front QCD color dipole approach with a path integral technique, solving the two-dimensional Schrödinger equation for the Green function that describes the evolution of a Q\bar{Q} pair propagating through the nuclear medium. They use realistic Wood-Saxon nuclear density distributions and adopt two Q-\bar{Q} interaction potentials (Buchmüller-Tye and power-like) in the Q\bar{Q} rest frame, boosting the resulting quarkonium wave functions to the light-front frame via the Terent'ev prescription. The leading twist gluon shadowing from multi-gluon photon fluctuations is included within the same Green function formalism, and the real part of the gamma* N to V N amplitude is incorporated via a correction factor.

## Key numbers

- Coherence length for heavy quarkonium production: l_c = 2k / (M_V^2 + Q^2), where M_V is the vector meson mass
- Formation time scale: t_f = 2k / (M_{V'}^2 - M_V^2), where M_{V'} is the mass of radially excited quarkonium
- Harmonic oscillator frequency in the Q\bar{Q} rest frame: omega_0 ≈ 0.3 GeV
- Fine-structure constant: alpha_em = 1/137.036
- Photon energy threshold for long coherence length regime: W ≲ 30-40 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis in Kopeliovich (2020) which calculated quantum coherence effects only approximately using effective form factors and a harmonic oscillator Green function with quadratic dipole cross section and constant nuclear density. The present rigorous Green function formalism removes those simplifications and provides more accurate results for both higher twist quark shadowing and leading twist gluon shadowing. The calculations are compared with available UPC data from RHIC and LHC experiments, and predictions are made for future measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), where the formalism can help distinguish saturation effects from other nuclear phenomena.
