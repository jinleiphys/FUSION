# $P$-wave dibaryon resonances in $pp$ elastic scattering and near-threshold pion production

> It is demonstrated within the dibaryon-induced model for $NN$ interaction that $pp$ scattering in $P$ waves is governed mainly by the production of the intermediate dibaryon resonances. Two dibaryon resonances with a mass of about 2200 MeV discovered recently by the ANKE-COSY Collaboration are shown to determine both elastic $pp$ phase shifts and inelasticities in the $^3P_0$ and $^3P_2$-$^3F_2$ channels from zero energy up to $T_p=0.7$-$0.9$ GeV. It is also demonstrated clearly that the $^3P_0$ dibaryon plays a decisive role in near-threshold neutral pion production in $pp$ collisions which is poorly understood to date. The missing dibaryon contribution is found to be the very possible reason for the failure of traditional approaches to explain near-threshold $π^0$ production.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the failure of traditional approaches to explain near-threshold neutral pion production in $pp$ collisions is very possibly due to a missing dibaryon contribution, specifically the $^3P_0$ dibaryon resonance, rather than the need for artificially high cutoff parameters in meson-exchange models.

## Method
The authors employed a dibaryon-induced model for the $NN$ interaction, where the total Hilbert space includes an external $NN$ channel and an internal six-quark (dibaryon) channel. The effective Hamiltonian in the $NN$ channel is derived by projecting out the internal channel, resulting in an energy-dependent pole-like interaction. The model parameters, including the bare dibaryon mass and width, were fitted to reproduce the $pp$ elastic scattering phase shifts and inelasticities in the $^3P_0$ and $^3P_2$-$^3F_2$ partial waves, and the resulting dressed resonance parameters were then used to calculate the near-threshold $pp \to pp\pi^0$ cross section.

## Key numbers
*   The dressed dibaryon mass for the $^3P_0$ channel is 2200 MeV, with a total width of 99 MeV, consistent with the experimental values of 2201(5) MeV and 91(12) MeV from the ANKE-COSY Collaboration.
*   The dressed dibaryon mass for the $^3P_2$-$^3F_2$ coupled channels is 2221 MeV, with a total width of 168 MeV, compared to the experimental values of 2197(8) MeV and 130(21) MeV.
*   The branching ratio for the dibaryon decay into the $NN$ channel is estimated to be about 7% for the $^3P_0$ dibaryon and 13% for the $^3P_2$-$^3F_2$ dibaryon.
*   The model uses a soft cutoff value for the $\pi NN$ vertex form factor, $\Lambda_{\pi NN} = 0.65$ GeV/$c$, consistent with microscopic quark-model and lattice-QCD calculations.

## Context
This work extends the dibaryon-induced model for $NN$ interaction, previously applied to $S$ and $D$ waves (Kukulin et al., 2019; Kukulin et al., 2020), to $P$-wave channels. It directly addresses the long-standing puzzle of near-threshold $\pi^0$ production, where earlier attempts using Chiral Perturbation Theory (Park et al., 1996; Hanhart et al., 2001) and phenomenological models (Lee, 1996; Horowitz, 1998) failed to provide a quantitative description without using artificially high cutoff parameters. The results enable a unified description of elastic $pp$ scattering and meson production, suggesting that dibaryon resonances located near nucleonic resonance thresholds may be responsible for other poorly understood near-threshold meson production processes, such as $\eta$-meson production.
