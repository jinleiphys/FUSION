# Partial Decay Widths of Negative Parity Baryons in the 1/Nc Expansion

> The partial decay widths of lowest lying negative parity baryons belonging to the 70-plet of SU(6) are analyzed in the framework of the 1/Nc expansion The channels considered are those with single pseudo-scalar meson emission. The analysis is carried out to sub-leading order in 1/Nc and to first order in SU(3) symmetry breaking. Conclusions about the magnitude of SU(3) breaking effects along with predictions for some unknown or poorly determined partial decay widths of known resonances are obtained.

## Key claim

The partial decay widths of negative parity baryons in the **70**-plet of SU(6) can be described by a 1/N_c expansion to sub-leading order and first order in SU(3) symmetry breaking, but the available data on strangeness partial widths are insufficient to perform a complete analysis; SU(3) breaking effects in partial decay widths are found to be larger than natural size, as demonstrated by the failure of leading-order coefficient-independent relations and by unnaturally large contributions from some SU(3) breaking operators.

## Method

The authors construct a basis of effective operators for the decay amplitudes of the **70**-plet baryons into a ground-state baryon and a single pseudoscalar meson, organized in powers of 1/N_c and first order in SU(3) breaking (parameter epsilon ~ 1/3). The operators are expressed in terms of spin-flavor generators acting on the "core" and "excited quark" components of the baryon states; the analysis includes up to 2-body SU(3)-preserving operators and 1-body SU(3)-breaking operators, with 3-body operators assumed to be dynamically suppressed. The effective coefficients are determined by fitting to the Breit-Wigner partial decay widths from the Particle Data Group (PDG) for the known resonances, using a centrifugal-barrier factor (k_P/Lambda)^(l_P) with Lambda = 200 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The SU(3) breaking expansion parameter epsilon is estimated to be epsilon ~ 1/3.
- The N(1535) has total width 150(25) MeV, with S-wave pi N branching ratio 45(10)% and eta N branching ratio 52.5(7.5)%.
- The N(1520) has total width 113(12.5) MeV, with D-wave pi N branching ratio 60(5)% and eta N branching ratio 0.23(0.04)%.
- The Lambda(1670) has total width 37.5(12.5) MeV, with S-wave anti-K N branching ratio 25(5)% and pi Sigma branching ratio 40(15)%.
- The Lambda(1690) has total width 60(10) MeV, with D-wave anti-K N branching ratio 25(5)% and pi Sigma branching ratio 30(10)%.
- The Lambda(1405) has total width 50(2) MeV, with pi Sigma branching ratio 100%.
- The Lambda(1520) has total width 15.6(1) MeV, with D-wave anti-K N branching ratio 45(1)% and pi Sigma branching ratio 42(1)%.
- The Delta(1620) has total width 143(7.5) MeV, with S-wave pi N branching ratio 25(5)% and D-wave pi Delta branching ratio 45(15)%.
- The Delta(1700) has total width 300(100) MeV, with S-wave pi Delta branching ratio 37.5(12.5)% and D-wave pi N branching ratio 15(5)%.
- The Sigma(1775) has total width 120(15) MeV, with D-wave anti-K N branching ratio 40(3)% and pi Lambda branching ratio 17(3)%.
- The N(1675) has total width 148(18) MeV, with D-wave pi N branching ratio 40(5)% and K Lambda less than 1%.

## Context

This work extends the earlier 1/N_c analysis of non-strange negative parity baryon decays in the SU(4) x O(3) framework by Goity, Schat, and Scoccola (2004) to include the strange members of the **70**-plet and decays into hyperons, improving upon the earlier analysis by Goity, Carlson, and Kogan (1999) which used an incomplete operator basis. The results enable future studies that incorporate mixing between octet, decuplet, and singlet states (which were set to zero here due to data limitations) and can be combined with mass and photocoupling analyses to further constrain the mixing angles and SU(3) breaking effects.
