# Multiscale physics of atomic nuclei from first principles

> Atomic nuclei exhibit multiple energy scales ranging from hundreds of MeV in binding energies to fractions of an MeV for low-lying collective excitations. As the limits of nuclear binding is approached near the neutron- and proton driplines, traditional shell-structure starts to melt with an onset of deformation and an emergence of coexisting shapes. It is a long-standing challenge to describe this multiscale physics starting from nuclear forces with roots in quantum chromodynamics. Here we achieve this within a unified and non-perturbative framework that captures both short- and long-range correlations starting from modern nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces from chiral effective field theory. The short-range correlations which accounts for the bulk of the binding energy is included within a symmetry-breaking framework, while long-range correlations (and fine details about the collective structure) are included via symmetry projection. Our calculations accurately reproduce available experimental data for low-lying collective states and the electromagnetic quadrupole transitions in $^{20-30}$Ne. We also reveal coexisting spherical and deformed shapes in $^{30}$Ne, which indicates the breakdown of the magic neutron number $N=20$ as the key nucleus $^{28}$O is approached, and we predict that the dripline nuclei $^{32,34}$Ne are strongly deformed. By developing reduced-order-models for symmetry-projected states, we perform a global sensitivity analysis and find that the subleading singlet S-wave contact and a pion-nucleon coupling strongly impact nuclear deformation in chiral effective-field-theory. The techniques developed in this work clarify how microscopic nuclear forces generate the multiscale physics of nuclei spanning collective phenomena as well as short-range correlations and allow to capture emergent and dynamical phenomena in finite fermion systems.

## Key claim

The authors achieve a unified, non-perturbative description of both short-range correlations (which account for the bulk of binding energy) and long-range static correlations (which govern collective deformation and rotation) in atomic nuclei, starting from chiral effective-field-theory forces. The key qualifier is that this framework accurately reproduces experimental data for low-lying collective states and quadrupole transitions in the neon isotopic chain, but only within theoretical error bars, and the angular-momentum projection is treated approximately due to truncation of the cluster expansion.

## Method

The authors start from an axially deformed Hartree-Fock reference state and include short-range dynamical correlations using the coupled-cluster singles-and-doubles approximation with perturbative triples (CCSD). Long-range static correlations are then captured by projecting the symmetry-broken coupled-cluster states onto good angular momentum using a novel disentangled approach that avoids stiffness in the differential equations and preserves kernel symmetry. For sensitivity analysis, they develop a reduced-order model based on eigenvector continuation to emulate Hartree-Fock calculations across millions of values of the low-energy constants in the chiral interaction.

## Key numbers

* Binding energies of medium-mass nuclei are about 8 MeV per nucleon.
* The lowest rotational excitation in $^{34}$Mg is only 0.26% of the total binding energy.
* The coupled-cluster singles-and-doubles approximation captures about 90% of the full correlation energy; inclusion of triples corrections brings that to about 99%.
* The effective number of posterior samples after importance resampling is $n_\mathrm{eff} = 25$.
* The reduced-order model for Hartree-Fock uses a snapshot basis of $N_\text{train}=68$ states.
* The chiral interaction ensemble uses a momentum cutoff of 394 MeV/$c$ for both NN and NNN potentials.

## Context

This work extends prior ab initio studies of deformed nuclei by Caprio (2015), Maris (2015), Dytrych (2020), and others, which did not include three-nucleon forces or failed to reproduce strong electric quadrupole transitions. It also improves upon the angular-momentum projection approach of Qiu (2017) by developing a more stable numerical method. The calculations enable predictions for neutron-rich neon isotopes near the dripline, including shape coexistence in $^{30}$Ne and strong deformation in $^{32,34}$Ne, and provide a pathway to connect nuclear deformation to specific terms in chiral effective field theory via global sensitivity analysis.
