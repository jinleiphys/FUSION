# Can neutron electromagnetic form factors be obtained by polarized inclusive electron scattering off polarized three-nucleon bound states?

> The investigation of the electromagnetic inclusive responses of polarized $^{3}$He within the plane wave impulse approximation is briefly reported. A particular emphasys is put on the extraction, from the inclusive responses at the quasielastic peak, of the neutron form factors from feasible experiments.

## Key claim
The authors propose that neutron electromagnetic form factors can be extracted from polarized inclusive electron scattering off polarized $^{3}$He at the quasielastic peak within the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation, but caution that the proton contribution dominates the transverse-longitudinal response (ranging from 80% at low $Q^2$ to 40% at high $Q^2$), making the claim that polarized $^{3}$He is an effective neutron target valid only with careful treatment of this proton background.

## Method
The authors performed calculations using spin-dependent spectral functions of the three-nucleon system, considering different realistic two-body forces (AV14, RSCv8) and three-body forces (Brazil, Tucson-Melbourne), within the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation. They analyzed the inclusive responses $R_{T'}$ and $R_{TL'}$ at the quasielastic peak for momentum transfers up to 2 $(GeV/c)^2$, and examined the ratio of proton to neutron contributions in both $^{3}$He and $^{3}$H. They also computed the critical polarization angle $\beta_{critic}$ where the proton contribution to the polarized cross section vanishes.

## Key numbers
- Proton contribution to the transverse-longitudinal response $R_{TL'}$ in $^{3}$He ranges between 80% at low $Q^2$ and 40% at the highest values of $Q^2$.
- Proton contribution to the transverse response $R_{T'}$ in $^{3}$He is approximately 10%.
- The analysis covers momentum transfer up to 2 $(GeV/c)^2$, relevant for TJNAF kinematics.
- The derivative $\partial [R^{^3He}_{TL'}/G_{M}^p G_{E}^p] /\partial Q^2$ is almost constant in the low momentum transfer range $0.1 \le Q^2 \le 0.3$ $(GeV/c)^2$.
- Changes in the inclusive responses due to different choices of two- and three-body forces and Coulomb interaction are of the order of a few percent.

## Context
This work extends earlier investigations by Jones (1997) on extracting neutron form factors from polarized $^{3}$He scattering, and addresses the difficulties noted by previous authors regarding the overwhelming role of the proton in the transverse-longitudinal response. The calculations use spectral functions from realistic potentials including AV14 (Wiringa, 1984) and RSCv8 (Reid, 1968), with three-body forces from Brazil and Tucson-Melbourne. The results enable future experiments at TJNAF to extract $G_E^n$ and $G_M^n$ separately by combining measurements on polarized $^{3}$He and $^{3}$H, and by using the critical polarization angle $\beta_{critic}$ to determine the proton contribution experimentally.
