# Upsilon production in pp and pA collisions: from RHIC to the LHC

> I discuss Upsilon production in pp collisions at RHIC, Tevatron and LHC energies, in particular the behaviour of the differential cross section in rapidity and the impact of QCD corrections on the P_T differential cross section. I also emphasise the very good agreement between the parameter-free predictions of the Colour-Singlet Model (CSM) and the first LHC data, especially in the region of low transverse momenta, which is the most relevant one for heavy-ion studies. I also show that the CSM predicts Upsilon cross-section ratios in agreement with the most recent LHC data. I then briefly discuss the nuclear-matter effects on Upsilon production at RHIC and the LHC in p(d)A collisions and, by extension, in AA collisions. I argue that a) the Upsilon break-up probability can be neglected, at RHIC and the LHC, b) gluon shadowing --although non-negligible-- is not strong enough to describe forward RHIC data, c) backward RHIC data hints at a gluon EMC effect, possibly stronger than the quark one. Outlooks for the LHC pPb run are also presented.

## Key claim

The Colour-Singlet Model (CSM) at leading order in \(\alpha_S\) and \(v\) provides parameter-free predictions that agree well with LHC data for \(\Upsilon\) production, particularly for the \(P_T\)-integrated cross section and for the differential cross section at low transverse momenta (up to 5 GeV), but the agreement is qualified by large theoretical uncertainties from renormalisation-scale dependence and by the need for NNLO contributions to describe data at higher \(P_T\).

## Method

The author compares leading-order (LO) CSM predictions for the \(P_T\)-integrated \(\Upsilon(1S)\) cross section as a function of collision energy and rapidity with data from RHIC (STAR), the Tevatron (CDF, D0), and the LHC (CMS, LHCb). For the \(P_T\) differential cross section, NLO and partial NNLO (NNLO\(^\star\)) CSM predictions are compared with LHCb data for \(\Upsilon(3S)\) up to 5 GeV and beyond. Cross-section ratios for direct \(\Upsilon(nS)\) states are computed from ratios of Schrödinger wave functions at the origin and compared with CMS and LHCb measurements. Nuclear-matter effects in \(d\)Au collisions at RHIC are discussed using nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) and compared with PHENIX and STAR data.

## Key numbers

- The LO CSM ratio of direct \(\Upsilon(3S)\) to direct \(\Upsilon(1S)\) cross sections is \(|\psi^{3S}(0)|^2 / |\psi^{1S}(0)|^2 \sim 0.34\).
- The LO CSM ratio of direct \(\Upsilon(2S)\) to direct \(\Upsilon(1S)\) cross sections is \(|\psi^{2S}(0)|^2 / |\psi^{1S}(0)|^2 \sim 0.45\).
- From CMS data at 7 TeV, \(\sigma(\Upsilon(1S) (|y|< 2)) Br_{\ell\ell} \simeq 7.4\) nb, leading to an estimated direct \(\Upsilon(1S)\) yield of \(\sim 150\) nb.
- The CSM prediction for direct \(\Upsilon(3S)\) yield at 7 TeV is \(0.34 \times 150\) nb \(\sim 50\) nb, compared to a CMS measurement of \(\sim 45\) nb (assuming 100% direct production).
- The NLO CSM prediction matches LHCb \(\Upsilon(3S)\) data up to 5 GeV in transverse momentum.
- The kinematic factor \((M_{\Upsilon(3S)}/M_{\Upsilon(1S)})^6 \simeq 1.7\) can induce up to 50% variation in cross-section ratios at low \(P_T\).
- For \(p\)Pb collisions at the LHC at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5\) TeV, an excess of \(\Upsilon\) between 5 and 15% is expected due to anti-shadowing in Pb\(p\) collisions, and a suppression of 20 to 25% is expected in forward-rapidity \(p\)Pb collisions.

## Context

This work extends the CSM analysis of \(\Upsilon\) production from RHIC through Tevatron to LHC energies, building on earlier studies by Brodsky (2009) and Lansberg (2008) that argued for the sufficiency of the colour-singlet channel. It contrasts with claims in the literature (e.g., Cooper, 2004) that the CSM yield disagrees with data. The nuclear-matter discussion extends the analysis of Ferreiro (2011) on \(\Upsilon\) survival probability and shadowing, and it suggests that fractional parton energy loss, as studied by Arleo (2010) for \(J/\psi\), may explain the anomalous forward suppression at RHIC. The results enable more precise interpretation of \(\Upsilon\) suppression in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC by quantifying cold nuclear matter effects that must be subtracted.
