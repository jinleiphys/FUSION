# Thermal and Magnetic effects on Bulk Viscosity in Binary Neutron Star Mergers

> Astrophysical scenarios such as binary neutron star mergers, protoneutron stars, and core-collapse supernovae involve finite temperatures and strong magnetic fields. Previous studies on the effect of magnetic fields on flavor-equilibration processes relied on the Fermi surface approximation, which is not a reliable approximation in the neutrino-transparent regime of matter in supernovae or neutron star mergers. In a recent study, we went beyond the Fermi surface approximation, performing the full phase space integral to obtain direct Urca rates in a background magnetic field. In this work, we extend these calculations to incorporate the collisional broadening (modified Urca) contribution. We use the recently developed nucleon width approximation, which naturally includes the magnetic field dependence of all contributions. We demonstrate the impact of magnetic fields on the flavor-equilibrium condition for two finite-temperature equations of state with different direct Urca thresholds. We also study the impact of magnetic fields on the bulk viscous dissipation of density oscillations relevant in postmerger scenarios.

## Key claim

The authors show that magnetic fields of order 10^17 G can significantly enhance Urca process rates and bulk viscosity in neutron star merger matter, but only at temperatures below about 3 MeV; at higher temperatures thermal effects dominate and the magnetic field contribution becomes negligible.

## Method

The authors use the nucleon width approximation (NWA) formalism, which naturally includes both direct Urca and modified Urca contributions, to compute full phase space integrals for Urca rates in a background magnetic field. They apply this to two relativistic mean field equations of state (IUF and QMC-RMF3) at temperatures from 1 to 5 MeV and magnetic fields from 5 × 10^16 G to 5 × 10^17 G, then calculate the resulting bulk viscosity and damping times for 1 kHz density oscillations.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic fields considered range from 5 × 10^16 G to 5 × 10^17 G
- IUF equation of state has direct Urca threshold at n_dU = 4.1 n_0 (where n_0 is nuclear saturation density)
- Nucleon width is parameterized as W_N = T^2 / T_W with T_W = 5 MeV
- Bulk viscosity is calculated for density oscillations of frequency f = 1 kHz (ω = 2π × 1 kHz)
- At T = 1 MeV, magnetic fields above 5 × 10^16 G enhance the neutron decay rate by an order of magnitude near the direct Urca threshold density
- Isospin relaxation time τ_I = 0.16 ms corresponds to the resonant condition for 1 kHz oscillations (τ_I = 1/ω)
- At density n_B = 3.5 n_0 (below direct Urca threshold), the bulk viscosity maximum shifts to lower temperature as magnetic field increases
- Damping time contours show fastest damping within the 60 ms region, which moves to lower temperatures with increasing magnetic field

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of direct Urca rates in magnetic fields (Tambe 2024) by incorporating the modified Urca contribution through the NWA formalism developed by Alford (2024). It goes beyond earlier studies by Lai (1991), Leinson (1998), and others that used the Fermi surface approximation, which is invalid at temperatures above about 1 MeV. The results enable more accurate inclusion of magnetic field effects in simulations of binary neutron star mergers and supernovae, particularly for the damping of post-merger density oscillations.
