# Dilepton production from SIS to LHC energies

> We study e+e- pair production in proton-proton and in nucleus-nucleus collisions from SIS to LHC energies within the parton-hadron-string dynamics (PHSD) approach which incorporates explicit partonic degrees-of-freedom in terms of strongly interacting quasiparticles (quarks and gluons) in line with an equation-of-state from lattice QCD as well as the dynamical hadronization and hadronic collision dynamics in the final reaction phase. We find a visible in-medium effect in the low mass dilepton sector from dynamical vector-meson spectral functions from SIS to SPS energies whereas at RHIC and LHC energies such medium effects become more moderate. In the intermediate mass regime from 1.1 to 3 GeV pronounced traces of the partonic degrees of freedom are found at SPS and RHIC energies which superseed the hadronic (multi-meson) channels as well as the correlated and uncorrelated semi-leptonic D-meson decays. The dilepton production from the strongly interacting quark gluon plasma (sQGP) becomes already visible at top SPS energies and more pronounced at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key claim

The PHSD transport model shows that dilepton production from the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) becomes visible at top SPS energies and more pronounced at RHIC and LHC energies, but the in-medium effects in the low-mass dilepton sector are visible from SIS to SPS energies and become more moderate at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Method

The authors employed the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which incorporates explicit partonic degrees-of-freedom as strongly interacting quasiparticles (quarks and gluons) with an equation-of-state from lattice QCD, along with dynamical hadronization and hadronic collision dynamics. The partonic dynamics is based on Kadanoff-Baym equations for Green functions with self-energies from the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM), where the three DQPM parameters for the temperature-dependent effective coupling were fitted to lattice QCD results. The transition from partonic to hadronic degrees-of-freedom is described by covariant transition rates for quark-antiquark fusion or three-quark fusion, obeying flavor current-conservation, color neutrality, and energy-momentum conservation.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature T_c approximately 160 MeV, corresponding to a critical energy density epsilon_c approximately 0.5 GeV/fm^3
- At SPS energies (In+In at 158 A GeV), partonic sources dominate the dilepton yield for invariant masses M > 1 GeV, with the q + qbar annihilation process contributing significantly even for M < 0.6 GeV (threshold approximately 0.211 GeV)
- At RHIC energies (Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV), partonic radiation and correlated D-meson decays dominate in the mass region M = 1-3 GeV, with quark-antiquark annihilation in the sQGP constituting about half of the observed yield in this intermediate mass range
- For M > 2.5 GeV at RHIC, the partonic yield dominates over the D-meson contribution
- At SIS energies (C+C at 1.0 and 2.0 A GeV), the HSD model with enhanced bremsstrahlung cross sections and collisional broadening agrees with HADES data, with the eta Dalitz decay providing the dominant contribution in the mass region 0.2-0.5 GeV at 2.0 A GeV
- For central Au+Au collisions from 2 to 14 A GeV, both collisional broadening and combined (dropping mass + collisional broadening) scenarios lead to an enhancement of the dilepton yield in the mass region M = 0.3-0.8 GeV by a factor of about 2

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dilepton production using the HSD and PHSD approaches, particularly the detailed investigations at top SPS and RHIC energies by Linnyk et al. (2011). The paper addresses the "DLS puzzle" from earlier DLS measurements at Berkeley, which showed dilepton yields about five times higher than conventional transport model calculations; this was later resolved by incorporating stronger pn and pp bremsstrahlung contributions in line with One-Boson-Exchange model calculations by Kaptari et al. (2005). The results enable future comparisons with upcoming HADES data for larger systems like Au+Au and with the PHENIX hadron blind detector upgrade, which should provide decisive information on the origin of low-mass dileptons at RHIC energies.
