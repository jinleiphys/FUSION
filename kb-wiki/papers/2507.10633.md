# Polarized Electron Scattering from Light Nuclei at High Energies

> We present a theoretical approach to investigate the scattering of polarized electrons from light nuclei using the multipole expansion for the scattering cross section within the framework of the unified electroweak theory. Scattering processes corresponding to different electron polarizations are analyzed and compared with the unpolarized electron scattering investigated earlier. Besides, the contribution of both polarized and unpolarized terms to the scattering cross section is examined. Numerical calculations for stable $^{6,7}$Li and unstable $^7$Be nuclei using the Weinberg-Salam model show that the longitudinal polarization and weak interaction are not explicitly correlated when electrons scatter at $ θ\simeq 0^{\circ}$ across all energy scales. A strong correlation emerges at the other scattering angles when the electron energy exceeds 10 GeV. This study provides additional information about nuclear structure and uncovers the role of electron polarization and its correlation with the weak interaction in each process, thus offering a more complete picture of electron-nucleus scattering.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for polarized electron scattering on light nuclei, the longitudinal polarization and weak interaction are not explicitly correlated at scattering angles near 0 degrees across all energy scales, but a strong correlation emerges at other scattering angles when the electron energy exceeds 10 GeV. This correlation is demonstrated through ratios of polarized to unpolarized cross sections, with the caveat that the results depend on the specific gauge model used (the Weinberg-Salam model with parameter beta_A^(0) = 0 for Li-6).

## Method

The authors employed a multipole expansion of the electroweak scattering cross section within the unified electroweak theory (Weinberg-Salam model) using the first-order Born approximation, with the scattering amplitude including both electromagnetic (photon exchange) and weak neutral current (Z0 boson exchange) contributions. Nuclear form factors were calculated using the nuclear shell model combined with fractional parentage coefficients and harmonic oscillator parameters (beta = 1.1526 fm^-2 for Li-6, beta = 1.3055 fm^-2 for Li-7 and Be-7), with nucleon form factors parameterized as dipoles. The formalism was applied to elastic scattering from ground states of Li-6, Li-7, and Be-7 nuclei, considering various combinations of initial and final electron polarization states.

## Key numbers

* Harmonic oscillator parameter beta = 1.1526 fm^-2 for Li-6 nucleus and beta = 1.3055 fm^-2 for Li-7 and Be-7 nuclei
* At energies not exceeding 10 GeV, the ratio sigma_total^pol(1,0)/sigma_total^unpol is approximately 0.5 (half) for Li-6, while sigma_total^pol(1,1)/sigma_total^unpol is almost equal to 1.0
* At energies below 10 GeV, the sigma_total^10/sigma_total^11 ratios are very small, with the highest order of magnitude being only around 10^-4 at theta = 8 degrees
* Be-7 has a half-life of approximately 53.22 days
* Calculations were performed at electron energies of 10, 100, 500, and 1000 GeV across various scattering angles

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of unpolarized electron scattering on Li-6, Li-7, and Be-7 nuclei (Truo24) by incorporating electron polarization effects, and builds upon the multipole expansion formalism for electroweak cross sections developed by earlier authors (Keri84, Luon03, Luon23). The study provides a basis for future investigations of asymmetry resulting solely from electron polarization and weak interaction, and enables experimentalists to deduce polarized scattering cross sections from unpolarized measurements (or vice versa) using the calculated ratios, particularly at energies below 10 GeV where the ratios are nearly constant.
