# Studies of parton distributions at a neutrino factory

> The determination of parton distribution functions in the nucleon and nuclei is important for obtaining precise hadron-reaction cross sections, from which any new exotic signature could be found. We show that a future neutrino factory could provide important information on the parton distributions. First, a recent effort concerning the parametrization of nuclear parton distributions is explained. It suggests that the factory should be important for determining unknown behavior of valence-quark distributions in nuclei at small $x$. Second, the facility could be used for understanding nucleon spin structure and isospin violation in the parton distributions.

## Key claim
A future neutrino factory could provide important information on parton distributions, particularly for determining the unknown behavior of valence-quark distributions in nuclei at small \(x\), but this depends on the availability of such a facility and the ability to take accurate deuteron data to properly account for nuclear modification.

## Method
The author performed a \(\chi^2\) analysis of nuclear parton distributions using electron and muon deep inelastic scattering data, parametrizing the nuclear modification as a weight function \(w_i(x,A,Z)\) with polynomial terms up to \(x^3\) (cubic fit) or \(x^2\) (quadratic fit) at \(Q_0^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\). The analysis then examined how parity-violating structure functions (\(F_3\) and \(g_3\)) accessible at a neutrino factory could separately determine valence-quark distributions, polarized distributions, flavor asymmetries, and isospin violation.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear parton distributions are provided at \(Q_0^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\) with a weight function parametrization.
- The weight function includes parameters \(a_i\), \(b_i\), \(c_i\), \(d_i\), and \(\beta_i\) in the form \(w_i(x,A,Z) = 1 + (1 - 1/A^{1/3}) (a_i + b_i x + c_i x^2 + d_i x^3)/(1-x)^{\beta_i}\).
- The parity-violating structure function combination \(\frac{1}{2}[F_3^{\nu (p+n)/2} + F_3^{\bar \nu (p+n)/2}] = u_v + d_v + (s - \bar s) + (c - \bar c)\) is used to extract valence distributions, with \(s - \bar s\) and \(c - \bar c\) expected to be small.
- For polarized distributions, \(g_3^{\nu p} + g_3^{\bar \nu p} = -(\Delta u_v + \Delta d_v) - (\Delta s - \Delta \bar s) - (\Delta c - \Delta \bar c)\) in leading order of \(\alpha_s\).
- The flavor asymmetry \(\bar u - \bar d\) is expressed as \(\frac{1}{4}[F_2^{\nu p}/x - F_3^{\nu p}] - \frac{1}{4}[F_2^{\nu n}/x - F_3^{\nu n}]\).
- Isospin symmetry violation is tested via \(\int \frac{dx}{4x} [F_2^{(\nu+\bar \nu) p} - F_2^{(\nu+\bar \nu) n}]\), which equals 0 if isospin symmetry holds.

## Context
This work extends prior parametrizations of nuclear parton distributions (Kumano, 2001) by identifying how a neutrino factory could resolve ambiguities in valence-quark distributions at small \(x\) that cannot be determined from electron and muon data alone. It also connects to earlier studies of polarized distributions (AAC collaboration) and flavor asymmetry from the NMC Gottfried-sum-rule violation and Drell-Yan experiments. The proposed measurements would enable precise hadron-reaction cross sections needed to search for new physics beyond the standard model.
