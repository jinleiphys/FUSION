# The $η$- and $η'$-nucleus interactions and the search for $η$, $η'$- mesic states

> The isoscalar $η$ and $η'$ mesons are special in QCD, being linked both to chiral symmetry and to non-perturbative glue associated with the axial anomaly. The properties of these mesons in medium are sensitive to how these dynamics work in the nuclear environment. In contrast to pionic and kaonic atoms which are mainly bound by the Coulomb interaction with some corrections due to the strong force (Coulomb assisted binding), the $η$ and $η'$ as neutral mesons can only be bound by the strong interaction. Is this interaction strong enough? This topic has inspired a vigorous program of experiments, conducted in close contact with theory, One has to determine the complex $η$, $η'$-nucleus potential. Does the real part $V$ provide a sufficiently deep potential? Is the imaginary part $W$ small enough to allow for narrow states that can more easily be detected experimentally, i.e. $|W| \ll |V|$? The $η'$ effective mass is observed to be suppressed by $\approx -40$ MeV at nuclear matter density. Bound state searches are ongoing. This article gives an overview of the status of knowledge in this field together with an outlook to future experiments.

## Key claim

The existence of η and η' mesic nuclei depends on whether the real part V of the complex meson-nucleus optical potential is sufficiently deep and the imaginary part W is small enough (|W| ≪ |V|) to allow for narrow, experimentally detectable bound states, since these neutral mesons can only be bound by the strong interaction without Coulomb assistance.

## Method

The authors review experimental determinations of the η and η' meson-nucleus optical potentials through measurements of meson production cross sections near threshold from nuclear targets, including photoproduction and hadroproduction experiments, and through direct searches for mesic bound states in light nuclei such as ³He and ⁴He. These experimental results are compared with theoretical predictions from the Quark Meson Coupling (QMC) model, chiral coupled-channel calculations, Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models, and linear sigma model calculations, which model medium modifications at the quark level through coupling to scalar and vector mean fields in the nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The η' effective mass is observed to be suppressed by approximately -40 MeV at nuclear matter density ρ₀, with a width of about 13 MeV in nuclei
- The QMC model predicts an η' mass shift of -37 MeV at nuclear matter density, in agreement with the experimental value of -40 ± 6 ± 15 MeV
- The QMC model predicts an η mass shift of approximately -100 MeV at nuclear matter density with -20° η-η' mixing angle
- The QMC model gives meson-nucleon scattering lengths with real parts Re a_ηN ≈ 0.85 fm and Re a_η'N ≈ 0.47 fm
- η bound states in helium require a real part of the η-nucleon scattering length greater than about 0.7-1.1 fm
- The η-η' mixing angle from phenomenological studies is between -15° and -20°
- The gluonic mass term in the Witten-Veneziano formula gives m̃²_η₀ = 0.73 GeV²
- The pion decay constant is reduced to f*²_π/f²_π = 0.64 ± 0.06 at nuclear matter density

## Context

This review extends and synthesizes the experimental program described in Metag (2017) and Bass (2018), building on the theoretical framework of Haider and Liu (1986) who first postulated η-mesic nuclei. The work connects to earlier studies of deeply bound pionic atoms by Suzuki (2002) and kaonic atoms, and to QMC model calculations by Guichon (1987, 1995) and Saito (2005). The results enable future searches for η' bound states in carbon using (p,d) reactions at GSI/FAIR and photoproduction studies at Spring-8 with carbon and copper targets, as well as continued searches for η bound states in helium.
