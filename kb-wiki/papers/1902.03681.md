# Deuteron helicity flip generalized parton distributions in a convolution model

> We discuss the general properties of generalized parton distributions with helicity flip (transversity) for spin-1 hadrons in the leading twist case. Using a basic light cone convolution model, we show the deuteron helicity amplitudes containing quark helicity flip GPDs and comment on the role deuteron angular momentum plays in these.

# Deuteron helicity flip generalized parton distributions in a convolution model

## Key claim
Using a basic light cone convolution model that only includes the dominant np component of the deuteron wave function, the authors show that deuteron helicity amplitudes containing quark helicity flip GPDs can be computed, but this truncation breaks Lorentz covariance and consequently the polynomiality requirements of the deuteron GPDs.

## Method
The authors decompose the off-forward quark correlator for a spin-1 hadron into nine helicity flip (transversity) GPDs at leading twist. They compute these GPDs for the deuteron using a light-front convolution model that separates nuclear and nucleon structure, employing the AV18 deuteron wave function parametrization and nucleon transversity GPDs from Goloskokov and Kroll (2011).

## Key numbers
- The helicity amplitudes are computed at skewness ξ=0.1 and momentum transfer t=-0.25 GeV².
- Helicity amplitudes are shown at two values of momentum transfer: t=-0.25 GeV² and a larger (unspecified) value.
- The first Mellin moment sum rules are zero for four transversity GPDs (i∈{2,3,5,8}).

## Context
This work extends the formalism of helicity flip GPDs for spin-1 hadrons introduced by Cosyn and Pire (2018) and builds on the helicity conserving GPDs for the deuteron introduced by Berger et al. (2001). The results could enable phenomenology of coherent deeply virtual Compton scattering (where gluon transversity enters at next-to-leading order) on the deuteron, double vector meson production (previously studied for the nucleon by Ivanov and Enberg), and deep exclusive meson production on deuteron targets.
