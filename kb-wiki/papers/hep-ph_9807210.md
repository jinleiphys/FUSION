# Neutron-Antineutron Oscillations in Nuclei Revisited

> An upper limit on $n-\bar n$ oscillations can be obtained from the stability of matter. This relation has been worked out theoretically and together with data yields $τ_{n\bar n} > (1 - 2)\times 10^8 sec$. A recent publication claims a different relation and finds a nuclear suppression of the $n\to\bar n$ oscillations which is two orders of magnitude weaker than the previous evaluations. Using the same approach we find, nevertheless, that the earlier estimates are correct and conclude that future experiments with free neutrons from reactors are capable to put a stronger limit on the $n - \bar n$ oscillation time than experiments with large amount of neutrons bound in nuclei.

# Neutron-Antineutron Oscillations in Nuclei Revisited

## Key claim

The authors argue that the nuclear suppression of neutron-to-antineutron oscillations is correctly given by earlier estimates (Dover-Gal-Richard 1983 and Kondratyuk 1987), yielding a lower limit of τ_nn̄ > (1-2)×10^8 sec, and that a recent claim by Lenz (1998) of two orders of magnitude weaker suppression is invalid because it uses an antineutron binding energy of 8 MeV that is an order of magnitude too small.

## Method

The authors solve a two-channel evolution equation for the n-n̄ system in a nuclear medium, incorporating the antineutron annihilation cross section via the imaginary part of the n̄-N scattering length (taken from LEAR data on p̄p atoms), the energy splitting between n and n̄ in the nuclear mean field, and the oscillator frequency (shell splitting) as a formation time effect. They integrate the annihilation rate over the nuclear volume using a Woods-Saxon density parameterization, with the antineutron binding energy taken proportional to the nuclear density and the annihilation radius set to 1 fm.

## Key numbers

- Lower limit on n-n̄ oscillation time from oxygen-16 stability: τ_nn̄(¹⁶O) > (0.6 - 1.5)×10⁸ sec
- Lower limit on n-n̄ oscillation time from iron-56 stability: τ_nn̄(⁵⁶Fe) > (0.75 - 1.9)×10⁸ sec
- Imaginary part of the n̄-N scattering length: |Im a_n̄N| ≈ 0.7-1.2 fm (from p̄p atom data)
- Central modified nuclear density: ρ̃(0) = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Imaginary part of antineutron nuclear potential at center: (2π/m) ρ̃(0) |Im a_n̄N| ≈ 30-50 MeV
- Antineutron fluctuation lifetime for ΔE = 8 MeV: t_c = 25 fm
- Nuclear factor T_R from exact integration: 0.20 fm⁻¹ for ¹⁶O and 0.18 fm⁻¹ for ⁵⁶Fe (for ΔE(0) ≈ 40 MeV)
- Nuclear factor T_R for ΔE(0) = 200 MeV: 1.2 fm⁻¹ for ¹⁶O and 1.3 fm⁻¹ for ⁵⁶Fe

## Context

This paper revisits the controversy between the earlier calculations of Dover-Gal-Richard (1983) and Kondratyuk (1987), which gave τ_nn̄ > (1-2)×10⁸ sec from nuclear stability data, and the recent claim by Lenz (1998) that nuclear suppression is two orders of magnitude weaker. The authors show that Lenz's result arises from using an antineutron binding energy of 8 MeV (the mean nucleon binding energy) rather than the correct value of approximately 40-200 MeV, and from incorrectly treating the low-density limit. The work enables the conclusion that future free-neutron experiments (such as the planned ORNL experiment) are more promising for improving the limit on τ_nn̄ than experiments with neutrons bound in nuclei.
