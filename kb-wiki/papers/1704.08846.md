# Probing three-nucleon-force effects via (p,2p) reactions

> We propose to use proton knockout reactions (p,2p) from a deeply bound orbit as a new probe into three-nucleon-force (3NF) effects. The remarkable advantage of using (p,2p) reaction is that we can choose an appropriate kinematical condition to probe the 3NF effects. We analyze (p,2p) reactions on a 40Ca target within the framework of distorted-wave impulse approximation with a g-matrix interaction based on chiral two- and three-nucleon forces. The chiral 3NF effects significantly change the peak height of the triple differential cross section of (p,2p) reaction. We also clarify the correspondence between the (p,2p) cross sections and the in- medium pp cross sections.

## Key claim

The authors propose that (p,2p) knockout reactions from a deeply bound orbit can serve as a new probe of three-nucleon-force (3NF) effects, but the 3NF effects are only clearly observable when an appropriate kinematical condition is chosen; for knockout from outer orbits the 3NF effects are small.

## Method

The authors analyzed (p,2p) reactions on a 40Ca target within the distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework, using a g-matrix interaction based on chiral two- and three-nucleon forces from the Julich group with cutoff parameters of 450, 550, and 600 MeV. The local density approximation was adopted to apply the g-matrix to finite nuclei, with the local Fermi momentum estimated from the matter density profile of 40Ca. Distorted waves were calculated using phenomenological global proton optical potentials from Koning and Delaroche (2003), and the single-particle wave functions were obtained from a mean-field potential derived from electron elastic scattering analysis.

## Key numbers

- For the 0p3/2 knockout at 150 MeV incident energy with angles theta1_L=30 degrees and theta2_L=40 degrees, the cross section at the peak increases by 20% due to 3NF effects, while the theoretical uncertainty from cutoff dependence is as small as 4%.
- The mean density sampled by the 0p3/2 knockout is 0.076 fm^-3, while the 1s1/2 knockout samples a mean density of 0.022 fm^-3.
- The kinematics at the peak for the 0p3/2 case correspond to kappa' = 0.983 fm^-1, kappa = 1.46 fm^-1, and theta_kappakappa' = 78.7 degrees.
- For the 0d3/2 and 1s1/2 knockouts at 148.2 MeV, spectroscopic factors of 2.58 and 1.03 respectively were used, taken from (e,e'p) reaction analysis by Kramer et al. (1989).
- The in-medium pp cross section at kappa' = 1.0 fm^-1 shows a 24% increase from 3NF effects with 4% uncertainty, consistent with the TDX changes.

## Context

This work extends the study of 3NF effects from few-nucleon systems and nuclear matter to nuclear many-body reactions, specifically proton knockout reactions. Prior work by Noro et al. (2000) and Hilaire et al. (2006) used (p,2p) reactions from deep-hole states to investigate density-dependence of nucleon-nucleon effective interactions, while Furumoto et al. (2016) recently investigated nucleus-nucleus elastic scattering as a probe of 3NF effects at high density. The results enable future experimental tests of chiral 3NF effects through (p,2p) reactions from deeply bound orbits, and potentially allow investigation of off-shell properties of the nucleon-nucleon interaction and isospin dependence of 3NFs through comparison of (p,2p) and (p,pn) cross sections.
