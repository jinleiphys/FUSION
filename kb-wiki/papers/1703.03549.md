# Limiting symmetry energy elements from empirical evidence

> In the framework of an equation of state (EoS) constructed from a momentum and density-dependent finite-range two-body effective interaction, the quantitative magnitudes of the different symmetry elements of infinite nuclear matter are explored. The parameters of this interaction are determined from well-accepted characteristic constants associated with homogeneous nuclear matter. The symmetry energy coefficient $a_2$, its density slope $L_0$, the symmetry incompressibility $K_δ$ as well as the density dependent incompressibility $K(ρ)$ evaluated with this EoS are seen to be in good harmony with those obtained from other diverse perspectives. The higher order symmetry energy coefficients $a_4,~a_6$ etc are seen to be not very significant in the domain of densities relevant to finite nuclei, but gradually build up at supra-normal densities. The analysis carried with a Skyrme-inspired energy density functional obtained with the same input values for the empirical bulk data associated with nuclear matter yields nearly the same results.

## Key claim

The higher-order symmetry energy coefficients (a4, a6, etc.) are negligible at densities relevant to finite nuclei but become significant at supra-normal densities, contrary to recent suggestions from finite-nucleus data that they might be sizeable even at saturation density.

## Method

The authors constructed an equation of state (EoS) using a momentum- and density-dependent finite-range effective two-body interaction in the modified Seyler-Blanchard (SBM) prescription. The interaction parameters were determined from empirical bulk nuclear matter data: saturation density ρ0, energy per nucleon e0, incompressibility K0, and the energy per particle of pure neutron matter at ρ = 0.1 fm−3, with the effective mass m0*/m treated as a free parameter constrained by neutron star maximum mass observations. The symmetry energy coefficients a2, a4, a6 and related quantities were then calculated from the resulting energy density functional, and the results were cross-checked using a Skyrme-inspired energy density functional with the same input data.

## Key numbers

- a2(ρ0) = 32.18 ± 0.78 MeV, a4(ρ0) = 1.02 ± 0.23 MeV, a6(ρ0) = 0.23 ± 0.04 MeV
- L0 (density slope of symmetry energy at ρ0) = 58.5 ± 6.5 MeV; total symmetry density slope Lt,0 = 63.8 ± 8.6 MeV
- Symmetry incompressibility Kδ = −382 ± 60 MeV
- Incompressibility at crossing density ρc = 0.71ρ0: Kc = 34.1 ± 1.2 MeV, with Mc = 1062 ± 102 MeV
- Fourth-order coefficient in binding energy expansion Esat,4 ≈ −6.7 MeV
- Isovector effective mass splitting at ρ0: (mn* − mp*)/m ≈ (0.209 ± 0.017)δ
- Input values: ρ0 = 0.155 ± 0.008 fm−3, e0 = −16.0 ± 0.2 MeV, K0 = 215 ± 25 MeV, en = 10.9 ± 0.5 MeV at ρ = 0.1 fm−3

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts using the SBM interaction (Bandyopadhyay 1990) and Skyrme-type interactions (Alam 2014) that fixed a2(ρ0) to a predetermined value and equated it with esym(ρ0), thereby masking higher-order effects. The present analysis avoids that assumption and additionally connects the interaction parameters to neutron star maximum mass constraints. The results are consistent with empirical extractions from nuclear masses (Moller 2012), ISGMR data (Khan 2013, De 2015), and heavy-ion collision analyses, while disagreeing with suggestions from Jiang (2014, 2015) that higher-order symmetry terms might be sizeable at normal density. The work enables more reliable extrapolations to neutron star matter at supra-normal densities.
