# A new approach for the wobbling motion in the even-odd isotopes $^{161,163,165,167}$Lu

> A new interpretation for the wobbling bands in the even-odd Lu isotopes is given within a particle-triaxial rotor semi-classical formalism. While in the previous papers the bands TSD1, TSD2, TSD3 and TSD4 are viewed as the ground, one, two and three phonon wobbling bands, here the corresponding experimental results are described as the ground band with spin equal to I=R+j, for R=0,2,4,...(TSD1), the ground band with I=R+j and R=1,3,5,...(TSD2), the one phonon excitations of TSD2 (TSD3), with the odd proton moving in the orbit $j=i_{13/2}$, and the ground band of I=R+j, with R=1,3,5,... and $j=h_{9/2}$ (TSD4). The moments of inertia (MoI) of the core for the first three bands are the same, and considered to be free parameters. Due to the core polarization effect caused by the particle-core coupling, the MoI's for TSD4 are different. The energies and the e.m. transitions are quantitatively well described. Also, the phase diagram of the odd system is drawn. In the parameter space, one indicates where the points associated with the fitted parameters are located, which is the region where the transversal wobbling mode might be possible, as well as where the wobbling motion is forbidden.

## Key claim

The paper reinterprets the four triaxial strongly deformed (TSD) bands in even-odd Lu isotopes not as multiple wobbling phonon excitations built on a single ground band, but as two distinct ground bands (TSD1 with even core angular momentum R and TSD2 with odd R) plus a one-phonon excitation of TSD2 (TSD3) and a separate ground band with a different proton orbital (TSD4), all described within a particle-triaxial rotor semiclassical formalism where the moments of inertia for the first three bands are the same free parameters while those for TSD4 differ due to core polarization.

## Method

The authors use a semiclassical formalism based on a time-dependent variational principle with a trial function that is a product of coherent states for the core and the odd proton, dequantizing a particle-triaxial rotor Hamiltonian. The classical equations of motion are linearized around the energy minimum to obtain two wobbling frequencies for each total angular momentum I, and the energies of the four TSD bands are constructed from these frequencies and the classical energy minima, with the moments of inertia, the particle-core interaction strength V, and the triaxial deformation parameter gamma treated as free parameters fitted to experimental excitation energies. The formalism is applied to the isotopes 161,163,165,167Lu, with the odd proton occupying the i13/2 orbital for TSD1-3 and the h9/2 orbital for TSD4.

## Key numbers

- For 163Lu, the fitted moments of inertia for TSD1, TSD2, TSD3 are I1 = 63.2, I2 = 20, I3 = 10 (in units of hbar^2/MeV), with V = 3.1 MeV and gamma = 17 degrees, yielding an r.m.s. deviation of 0.264 MeV for 52 states.
- For 163Lu TSD4, the fitted moments of inertia are I1 = 67, I2 = 34.5, I3 = 50 (in hbar^2/MeV), with V = 0.7 MeV and gamma = 17 degrees, yielding an r.m.s. deviation of 0.057 MeV for 10 states.
- For 161Lu, the fitted moments of inertia for TSD1 and TSD2 are I1 = 87.555, I2 = 2.773, I3 = 22.744 (in hbar^2/MeV), with V = 2.933 MeV and gamma = 20 degrees, yielding an r.m.s. deviation of 0.168 MeV for 29 states.
- For 165Lu, the fitted moments of inertia for TSD1, TSD2, TSD3 are I1 = 77.295, I2 = 16.184, I3 = 4.399 (in hbar^2/MeV), with V = 1.673 MeV and gamma = 20 degrees, yielding an r.m.s. deviation of 0.125 MeV for 42 states.
- For 167Lu, the fitted moments of inertia for TSD1 and TSD2 are I1 = 87.032, I2 = 10.895, I3 = 3.758 (in hbar^2/MeV), with V = 8.167 MeV and gamma = 19.48 degrees, yielding an r.m.s. deviation of 0.165 MeV for 30 states.
- The experimental wobbling energy for 163Lu (defined as the energy difference between TSD3 and the average of adjacent TSD2 states) increases from 144 keV at spin 33/2 to 170 keV at spin 77/2, then decreases to 143 keV at spin 85/2; the calculated wobbling energy increases from 331 keV at spin 33/2 to 570 keV at spin 85/2.
- The single particle energy difference for 163Lu is epsilon9/2 - epsilon13/2 = -0.334 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous semiclassical formalism (Raduta 2017, 2018) which treated TSD2 and TSD3 as one- and two-phonon wobbling excitations built on TSD1, and instead reassigns the bands to different ground-state configurations and a single phonon excitation. The new interpretation contradicts the multiple-phonon picture of earlier studies (Hagemann 2001) and provides an alternative to the claim that Lu isotopes exhibit transversal wobbling motion (Frauendorf 2014), instead finding longitudinal wobbling character with wobbling energy increasing with spin. The phase diagram drawn in the parameter space identifies regions where transversal wobbling might be possible and where wobbling motion is forbidden, enabling future experimental tests of the band assignments.
