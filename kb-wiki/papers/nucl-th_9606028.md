# Dilepton Production at SPS-energy Heavy Ion Collisions

> The production of dileptons is studied within a hadronic transport model. We investigate the sensitivity of the dilepton spectra to the initial configuration of the hadronic phase in a ultrarelativistic heavy ion collision. Possible in medium correction due to the modifications of pions and the pion form factor in a hadronic gas are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the dilepton enhancement observed by the CERES collaboration in SPS-energy heavy ion collisions can be explained by non-equilibrium processes in the hadronic phase, without invoking in-medium modifications of hadron properties. The authors find that variations in initial conditions can change the dilepton spectra by at most a factor of two, and that the in-medium modifications considered give only an insignificant enhancement of the total dilepton yield because pion annihilation contributes at best one third of the total yield in the relevant low mass region.

## Method

The authors use a hadronic transport model that explicitly propagates pions, rho, omega, and a1 mesons, as well as nucleons and deltas, with resonant interactions treated via Breit-Wigner cross sections and detailed balance. Rather than simulating the full collision from initial nuclei, they parameterize the initial hadronic phase-space configuration (spatial distribution, momentum distribution, particle abundances, formation time, and radial flow) subject to the constraint that final hadronic spectra and rapidity distributions are reproduced. Dilepton production is calculated from direct pion-pion annihilation using the known cross section, from Dalitz decays of eta, omega, and a1 mesons, and from direct decays of initial rho and omega mesons, with experimental acceptance cuts applied for comparison to CERES data.

## Key numbers

- Initial transverse radius assumed: R0 = 3.5 fm for S+Au collisions
- Rho meson width at peak: Gamma_0,rho = 150 MeV
- Rho meson mass: m_rho = 770 MeV
- a1 meson total width: 400 MeV; coupling constant G_a1_rho_pi = 18.1 GeV^-2
- Omega meson total width into three pions: 7.4 MeV; constant C = 48 GeV^-2
- Non-resonant scattering cross section: 20 mb
- Initial pion number needed: about 600 pions (possibly hidden in rho etc. mesons)
- Initial pion density for parameter set 1: rho_init = 2.3 fm^-3
- Initial pion density for parameter set 4: rho_init = 0.75 fm^-3
- Initial temperatures used: range from 160 MeV to 260 MeV across parameter sets
- Chemical potential for pions in some sets: mu_pi = 130 MeV
- Final proton slope parameter from calculation: 230-290 MeV across sets (experimental value about 240 MeV)
- Rho meson partial width into e+e- at peak: Gamma_0 = 6.77 keV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of dilepton production in heavy ion collisions, responding to the CERES collaboration data (CERES95) showing enhancement at invariant masses around 400 MeV in S+Au collisions, and similar results from the HELIOS collaboration (HELIOS95). The paper builds on prior transport model approaches by Li et al. (1995) and Chanfray et al. (1995) that suggested the enhancement might come from dropping vector meson masses as proposed by Pisarski (1982) and Brown and Rho (1991), as well as alternative explanations involving modified pion dispersion relations from interactions among pions in the isovector p-wave channel dominated by the s-channel rho-meson resonance. The authors find that non-equilibrium hadronic processes alone cannot fully explain the observed enhancement, and that in-medium modifications of the pion form factor and dispersion relation, as studied in Song et al. (1996), produce only a modest effect on the total dilepton yield. This work enables future studies to focus on other production channels or more detailed treatments of medium effects to resolve the discrepancy with experimental data.
