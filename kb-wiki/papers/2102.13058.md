# Axial meson exchange and the $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_{cs}(3985)$ resonances as heavy hadron molecules

> Early speculations about the existence of heavy hadron molecules were grounded on the idea that light-meson exchanges forces could lead to binding. In analogy to the deuteron, the light-mesons usually considered include the pion, sigma, rho and omega, but not the axial meson $a_1(1260)$. Though it has been argued in the past that the coupling of the axial meson to the nucleons is indeed strong, its mass is considerably heavier than that of the vector mesons and thus its exchange ends up being suppressed. Yet, this is not necessarily the case in heavy hadrons molecules: we find that even though the contribution to binding from the axial meson is modest, it cannot be neglected in the isovector sector where vector meson exchange cancels out. This might provide a natural binding mechanism for molecular candidates such as the $Z_c(3900)$, $Z_c(4020)$ or the more recently observed $Z_{cs}(3985)$. However the $Z_{cs}(3985)$ is more dependent on a mixture of different factors, which (besides axial meson exchange) include $η$ exchange and the nature of scalar meson exchange. Together they point towards the existence of two $Z_{cs}(3985)$-like resonances instead of one, while the observations about the role of scalar meson exchange in the $Z_{cs}(3985)$ might be relevant for the $P_{cs}(4459)$. Finally, the combination of axial meson exchange and flavor symmetry breaking effects indicates that the isovector $J^{PC} = 0^{++}$ $D^*\bar{D}^*$ and the strange $J^P = 2^{+}$ $D^*\bar{D}_s^*$ molecules are the most attractive configurations and thus the most likely molecular partners of the $Z_c(3900)$, $Z_c(4020)$ and $Z_{cs}(3985)$.

## Key claim

The axial meson $a_1(1260)$ provides a modest but non-negligible contribution to binding in the isovector sector of heavy hadron molecules, where vector meson exchange cancels out; this may offer a natural binding mechanism for the $Z_c(3900)$, $Z_c(4020)$, and $Z_{cs}(3985)$ resonances, though the $Z_{cs}(3985)$ depends more strongly on a mixture of factors including $\eta$ exchange and the nature of scalar meson exchange.

## Method

The authors derived the one-boson exchange (OBE) potential for charmed meson-antimeson systems, including for the first time the axial meson $a_1(1260)$ alongside the standard pion, sigma, rho, and omega exchanges. They constructed the axial meson exchange potential by modifying the axial current to include a mixing term proportional to $\lambda_1$, with the coupling determined from the weak decay constant $f_{a_1}$ and the axial coupling $g_1 = 0.6$. The resulting potentials were applied to the $D^*\bar{D}$ and $D^*\bar{D}^*$ systems corresponding to the $Z_c(3900)$, $Z_c(4020)$, and $Z_{cs}(3985)$ molecular candidates, and binding conditions were evaluated as functions of the scalar meson mass and the axial meson mixing parameter $\lambda_1$.

## Key numbers

- The axial meson to rho meson mass ratio is $m_{a_1} / m_{\rho} \sim 1.6$, with the adopted axial meson mass $m_{a_1} = 1.23$ GeV from the Review of Particle Physics.
- The mixing parameter $\lambda_1$ is estimated to be $\lambda_1 = 1.8 \pm 0.3$, based on determinations from $\tau \to 3\pi \nu_{\tau}$ decay and lattice QCD that give $\lambda_1$ in the range $1.55$ to $2.05$.
- The effective Yukawa coupling strength for axial meson exchange is $g_{\,\rm Y(a_1)}^2 / 4\pi \simeq 2.0$ to $3.5$, compared to $g_{\,\rm Y(\pi)}^2 / 4\pi \simeq 6.6 \times 10^{-2}$ for pion exchange.
- For the $I^G(J^{PC}) = 1^{+}(1^{+-})$ $D^{*} \bar{D}$ system (the $Z_c(3900)$ interpretation), the binding condition is fulfilled for $\lambda_1 \geq 1.1$ (with $m_{a_1} = 1.23$ GeV).
- The scalar meson coupling to charmed hadrons is taken as $g_{\sigma 1} = 3.4$ from the quark model relation $g_{\sigma 1} = g_{\sigma NN} / 3$, with $g_{\sigma NN}^2 / 4\pi = 8.3$ from the linear sigma model.
- The vector meson couplings are $g_{V1} = 2.9$ and $\kappa_{V1} = 2.8$, determined from vector meson dominance and the light-quark magnetic moment $\mu_L(D^{*0})$ giving $(2 M_N / e) \mu_u \simeq 1.85$.
- The ratio of $D^*$ exchange to axial meson exchange potentials at low momenta in the $Z_c$ channel is approximately $0.04$, indicating $D^*$ exchange can be neglected when axial meson exchange is included.

## Context

This work extends the one-boson exchange model for heavy hadron molecules, which previously included only pion, sigma, rho, and omega exchanges (Tornqvist 1991, Liu 2008), by adding the axial meson $a_1(1260)$ that was omitted in earlier OBE treatments due to its heavy mass. The study addresses a specific difficulty in explaining the isovector hidden-charm $Z_c(3900)$ (Ablikim 2013), $Z_c(4020)$ (Ablikim 2013), and $Z_{cs}(3985)$ (Ablikim 2020) as hadronic molecules: rho and omega exchange cancel out in these configurations, requiring alternative binding mechanisms. The results suggest that axial meson exchange makes the simultaneous description of the $X(3872)$ and $Z_c$ resonances more compatible, and that the $Z_{cs}(3985)$ may correspond to two resonances rather than one. The findings also indicate that the isovector $J^{PC} = 0^{++}$ $D^*\bar{D}^*$ and the strange $J^P = 2^{+}$ $D^*\bar{D}_s^*$ molecules are the most attractive configurations and thus the most likely molecular partners of these resonances, with implications for the $P_{cs}(4459)$.
