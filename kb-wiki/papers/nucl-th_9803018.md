# Isovector Pairing in Odd-A Proton-Rich Nuclei

> A simple model based on the group SO(5) suggests that both the like-particle and neutron-proton components of isovector pairing correlations in odd-A nuclei are Pauli blocked. The same effect emerges from Monte Carlo Shell-model calculations of proton-rich nuclei in the full fp shell. There are small differences between the two models in their representation of the effects of an odd nucleon on the competition between like-particle and neutron-proton pairing, but they can be understood and reduced by using a two-level version of the SO(5) model. On the other hand, in odd-odd nuclei with N not equal to Z SO(5) disagrees more severely with the shell model because it incorrectly predicts ground-state isospins. The shell model calculations for any fp-shell nuclei can be extended to finite temperature, where they show a decrease in blocking.

# Isovector Pairing in Odd-A Proton-Rich Nuclei

## Key claim
Both like-particle (pp, nn) and neutron-proton (np) components of isovector pairing correlations are Pauli blocked in odd-A nuclei, as predicted by a simple SO(5) model and confirmed by Monte Carlo Shell-model calculations; however, the SO(5) model disagrees more severely with the shell model for odd-odd nuclei with N ≠ Z because it incorrectly predicts ground-state isospins.

## Method
The authors used an SO(5) algebraic model with a Hamiltonian of three equally weighted isovector pairing terms to derive analytic expressions for pair-number expectation values in odd-A ground states. They then performed Shell Model Monte Carlo (SMMC) calculations in the full fp shell using a pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole interaction at temperature T = 0.4 MeV, and compared the pair-number results for Cr and Mn isotopes. A two-level version of the SO(5) model was also constructed to mimic the spin-orbit splitting between the f7/2 level and the rest of the fp shell.

## Key numbers
- The SMMC calculations used a pairing strength G_pair = 20/A MeV and quadrupole coupling constant χ = 134 A^{-11/3} MeV/fm^4.
- For the two-level SO(5) model, the levels had Ω1 = 4 and Ω2 = 6, split by ε = 10 G, where G is the pairing-force strength constant.
- The sign problem for 49Cr at T = 0.4 MeV was 0.35 ± 0.01.
- SMMC calculations for odd-A and odd-odd nuclei were performed at temperature T = 0.4 MeV, with a sign problem that could not be circumvented below T ≤ 0.8 MeV.
- Temperature-dependent SMMC calculations for 49Cr spanned the range T = 0.4 – 2 MeV, showing that for T > 1.2 MeV the neutron-neutron pairing correlations become the largest and neutron-proton the weakest.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analysis of np and like-particle pairing competition in even-even fp-shell nuclei and odd-odd N=Z nuclei (Engel, Langanke, Vogel, 1996), now focusing on odd-A nuclei where an extra nucleon blocks pairing correlations. The SO(5) model captures the essential physics despite deformation and spin-orbit splitting, but fails for odd-odd nuclei with N ≠ Z due to incorrect ground-state isospin predictions. The results enable further study of pairing competition at finite temperature, as demonstrated for 49Cr, and suggest that functional methods could be applied to study temperature dependence in SO(5) with higher seniority states included.
