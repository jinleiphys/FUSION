# Hadronic gamma-Z box corrections in M\oller scattering

> The possibility of measuring the parity-violating asymmetry in M\oller scattering with sufficient accuracy to determine sin^2 theta_W to 0.1% offers a complementary path to the discovery of new physics to that followed at high energy colliders. We present a new calculation of the gamma-Z box contribution to parity-violating electron-proton scattering, which constitutes an important uncertainty in computing the background to this process. We show that while the gamma-Z correction grows rapidly with energy, it can be relatively well constrained by data from parity-violating inelastic scattering and parton distribution functions.

## Key claim

The gamma-Z box correction to parity-violating electron-proton scattering at the 11 GeV energy of the MOLLER experiment is approximately twice as large as at the Qweak energy of 1.165 GeV, but can be constrained to an accuracy of about 7% using existing data from parity-violating inelastic scattering and parton distribution functions, keeping the resulting uncertainty on the Moller asymmetry background below the experimental budget.

## Method

The authors compute the real part of the gamma-Z box diagram using a forward dispersion relation, which relates it to an integral over the imaginary part obtained from the gamma-Z interference structure functions F1 and F2. They divide the integration region into three kinematic domains: a resonance region at low Q2 and W2 (Region I), a Regge region at low Q2 and high W2 (Region II), and a deep-inelastic region at high Q2 and W2 (Region III), each modeled with appropriate phenomenological inputs. The Adelaide-Jefferson Lab-Manitoba (AJM) model constructs the gamma-Z structure functions by starting from empirical fits to electromagnetic cross sections and rotating them to the gamma-Z case using vector meson dominance parameters constrained by parton distribution functions at the region boundaries.

## Key numbers

- Real part of the gamma-Z box correction at MOLLER energy (E=11 GeV): (11.5 ± 0.6 ± 0.6) × 10^(-3), where the first error is from the AJM model and the second from additional model dependence
- Real part of the gamma-Z box correction at Qweak energy (E=1.165 GeV): (5.57 ± 0.36) × 10^(-3)
- Effective proton weak charge at E=11 GeV: 0.0814 ± 0.0010
- Effective proton weak charge at E=1.165 GeV: 0.0757 ± 0.0007
- Contribution from Region II (Regge region) at MOLLER energy: (5.26 ± 0.49) × 10^(-3), which is approximately 46% of the total
- Contribution from Region II at Qweak energy: (0.59 ± 0.05) × 10^(-3), which is approximately 11% of the total
- Hadronic axial-vector piece (gamma-Z box with axial-vector hadron coupling) decreases from 0.0037(2) at E=1.165 GeV to 0.0035(2) at E=11 GeV
- Inelastic PV asymmetry at MOLLER kinematics (Q2 ≈ 0.004 GeV^2) is approximately constant at 85 ppm/GeV^2 at high W, with an uncertainty of about 7% in the AJM model
- The uncertainty from the elastic ep background to the Moller measurement is estimated to be approximately 0.1%, below the 0.3% limit from the experimental proposal

## Context

This work extends the AJM model calculation of Hall et al. (2013) for the gamma-Z box correction, previously applied to the Qweak experiment at 1.165 GeV, to the higher energy of 11 GeV relevant for the planned MOLLER experiment at Jefferson Lab. The calculation uses constraints from parity-violating inelastic electron-deuteron scattering data from the E08-011 experiment (Wang et al., 2013) and from the G0 experiment, as well as parton distribution functions from Alekhin et al. (2012). The result enables the MOLLER experiment to determine the weak charge of the electron to 2.3% (equivalent to sin^2 theta_W to 0.1%) with the ep background uncertainty under control, and suggests that direct monitoring of the inelastic ep asymmetry within the experiment could further constrain the model inputs.
