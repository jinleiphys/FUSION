# Fission barrier, damping of shell correction and neutron emission in the fission of A$\sim$200

> Decay of $^{210}$Po compound nucleus formed in light and heavy-ion induced fusion reactions has been analyzed simultaneously using a consistent prescription for fission barrier and nuclear level density incorporating shell correction and its damping with excitation energy. Good description of all the excitation functions have been achieved with a fission barrier of 21.9 $\pm$ 0.2 MeV. For this barrier height, the predicted statistical pre-fission neutrons in heavy-ion fusion-fission are much smaller than the experimental values, implying the presence of dynamical neutrons due to dissipation even at these low excitation energies ($\sim$ 50~MeV) in the mass region A $\sim$ 200. When only heavy-ion induced fission excitation functions and the pre-fission neutron multiplicities are included in the fits, the deduced best fit fission barrier depends on the assumed fission delay time during which dynamical neutrons can be emitted. A fission delay of (0.8 $\pm$ 0.1 )$\times 10^{-19}$ s has been estimated corresponding to the above fission barrier height assuming that the entire excess neutrons over and above the statistical model predictions are due to the dynamics. The present observation has implication on the study of fission time scale/ nuclear viscosity using neutron emission as a probe.

## Key claim

The fission barrier of \(^{210}\)Po is \(21.9 \pm 0.2\) MeV, comprised mainly of a liquid-drop component and ground-state shell correction, with no significant shell correction at the saddle point; however, even at low excitation energies (\(\sim 50\) MeV) in the A \(\sim 200\) mass region, the statistical model substantially underpredicts pre-fission neutron multiplicities, implying a dynamical fission delay of \((0.8 \pm 0.1) \times 10^{-19}\) s rather than purely statistical neutron emission.

## Method

The authors performed a simultaneous statistical-model analysis (using the code PACE with modified prescriptions for fission barrier and level density) of fission and evaporation-residue excitation functions for \(p\), \(\alpha\), \(^{12}\)C, and \(^{18}\)O induced fusion reactions forming the compound nucleus \(^{210}\)Po. They incorporated an energy-dependent damping of shell corrections at the equilibrium deformation, with thermal energies calculated using the Ignatyuk prescription, and varied the shell correction at the saddle point (\(\Delta_f\)) and the ratio of level-density parameters (\(\tilde{a}_f/\tilde{a}_n\)) to fit the data. The excess pre-fission neutrons in heavy-ion reactions were then attributed to dynamical emission during a fission delay, estimated via a Monte Carlo procedure assuming emission only at equilibrium deformation.

## Key numbers

* Fission barrier of \(^{210}\)Po: \(B_f = 21.9 \pm 0.2\) MeV, obtained from simultaneous fit to all fission and \(xn\) excitation functions.
* Ground-state shell correction for \(^{210}\)Po: \(\Delta_n = -10.6\) MeV.
* Liquid-drop component of the barrier: \(B_{LD} = 10.8\) MeV.
* Shell correction at the saddle point: \(\Delta_f = 0.4 \pm 0.3\) MeV (from \(p\) induced fission) and \(0.7 \pm 0.3\) MeV (from \(\alpha\) induced fission).
* Ratio of level-density parameters: \(\tilde{a}_f/\tilde{a}_n = 1.036\) (for \(p\)) and \(1.018\) (for \(\alpha\)).
* Fission delay time: \((0.8 \pm 0.1) \times 10^{-19}\) s, required to account for excess pre-fission neutrons assuming emission at equilibrium deformation.
* Macroscopic-microscopic model prediction for comparison: 22.1 MeV (Moller 2009).

## Context

This work extends earlier statistical-model analyses of \(^{210}\)Po decay by Charity (1986), Moretto (1995), D'Arrigo (1994), Shrivastava (1999), and Schmitt (2014), which used inconsistent treatments of shell corrections at the saddle point. The present simultaneous analysis of light- and heavy-ion data resolves ambiguities in the fission barrier and demonstrates that pre-fission neutron multiplicities in heavy-ion fusion-fission cannot be used to constrain statistical-model parameters, as previously attempted by Vigdor (1983), Ward (1983), and Mahata (2006). The finding of a significant fission delay at low excitation energies (\(\sim 50\) MeV) in the A \(\sim 200\) region enables future dynamical calculations of fission dynamics and nuclear viscosity using neutron emission as a probe.
