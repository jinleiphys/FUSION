# Nucleon-Meson Coupling Constants and Form Factors in the Quark Model

> We demonstrate the calculation of the coupling constants and form factors required by effective hadron lagrangians using the quark model. These relations follow from equating expressions for strong transition amplitudes in the two approaches. As examples we derive the NNm nucleon-meson coupling constants and form factors for m = pi, eta, eta', sigma, a_0, omega and rho, using harmonic oscillator quark model meson and baryon wavefunctions and the 3P0 decay model; this is a first step towards deriving a quark-based model of the NN force at all separations. This technique should be useful in the application of effective lagrangians to processes in which the lack of data precludes the direct determination of coupling constants and form factors from experiment.

# Nucleon-Meson Coupling Constants and Form Factors in the Quark Model

## Key claim

The quark model, using harmonic oscillator wavefunctions and the 3P0 decay model, can predict nucleon-meson coupling constants and form factors for pi, eta, eta', sigma, a0, omega, and rho mesons; however, the predicted NNpi coupling constant (7.0–12.2) falls below the experimental value of approximately 13.5 for standard quark model parameters, and the predicted NNV (vector meson) couplings disagree with fitted values from meson exchange models by about a factor of two, suggesting vector meson exchange may not be the dominant short-ranged NN interaction mechanism.

## Method

The authors equate matrix elements of an effective hadron Lagrangian for NNm vertices (e.g., NNpi, NNsigma, NNomega) to matrix elements of the 3P0 quark model decay Hamiltonian, evaluated in the rest frame of the initial nucleon using nonrelativistic harmonic oscillator quark model wavefunctions for baryons and mesons. The coupling constants are extracted by matching the near-threshold expressions, with the 3P0 pair production amplitude gamma fixed to 0.4 from meson decay studies, and the Gaussian wavefunction inverse length scales alpha (baryons, 0.25–0.4 GeV) and beta (mesons, 0.3–0.4 GeV) varied over their literature ranges. For vector mesons, the gamma_mu and sigma_mu_nu form factors are separated by considering different nucleon spin transitions.

## Key numbers

- NNpi coupling constant g_NNpi: predicted range 7.0–12.2 for alpha = 0.25–0.4 GeV, beta = 0.3–0.4 GeV, gamma = 0.4; experimental value is approximately 13.5.
- Delta++ to p pi+ width: predicted as a function of alpha, beta, gamma = 0.4; experimental width is 110 MeV.
- With alpha = beta = 0.4 GeV, a 3P0 pair production amplitude gamma of approximately 0.7 is favored for pion emission from light baryons, versus gamma = 0.4–0.5 for light meson decays.
- NNeta coupling: g_NNeta = 11.5, derived from g_NNpi = 13.5 using SU(6) flavor relations.
- NNeta' coupling: g_NNeta' = 15.3, derived from g_NNpi = 13.5.
- NNsigma coupling: predicted range 3–7 for m_sigma = 500 MeV, with preferred value approximately 5; CD-Bonn model uses m_sigma = 350 MeV with g_NNsigma ≈ 2.5 in I=0 3S1 channel and m_sigma = 452 MeV with g_NNsigma ≈ 7.3 in I=0 1S0 channel; Nijmegen model quotes g_NNsigma = 17.9.
- NNomega vector coupling: g_NNomega = (9/5) * (m_omega/m_pi)^(1/2) * g_NNpi; predicted kappa_omega = -3/2.
- NNrho vector coupling: g_NNrho = (1/3) * (m_rho/m_omega)^(1/2) * g_NNomega; predicted kappa_rho = +3/2.
- Meson exchange model fitted NNomega gamma_mu coupling is about a factor of 2 smaller than the quark model prediction.

## Context

This work extends the earlier approach of LeYaouanc et al. (1972), who computed rho-pi-pi, NNpi, and NNrho couplings using similar quark model techniques, by deriving new results for NNeta, NNeta', NNsigma, NNa0, and NNomega couplings and form factors, including the tensor (sigma_mu_nu) couplings for vector mesons. The computed NNsigma coupling (approximately 5) is numerically similar to the isospin-mean fitted NN S-wave value in the CD-Bonn model of Machleidt (2000), but the Paris model of Cottingham et al. (1973) and Lacombe et al. (1980) did not include a sigma meson, and the Nijmegen model of Nagels et al. (1978) quotes a much larger value (17.9). The disagreement between predicted and fitted vector meson couplings suggests that vector meson exchange may not be the dominant short-ranged NN interaction mechanism, motivating future calculations of NN phase shifts using quark model constraints on the vertices.
