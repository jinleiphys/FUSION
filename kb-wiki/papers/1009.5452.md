# Model ingredients and peak mass production in heavy-ion collisions

> We simulate the central reactions of $^{20}$Ne+$^{20}$Ne, $^{40}$Ar+$^{45}$Sc, $^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni, $^{86}$Kr+$^{93}$Nb, $^{129}$Xe+$^{118}$Sn, $^{86}$Kr+$^{197}$Au, and $^{197}$Au+$^{197}$Au at different incident energies for different equations of state, different binary cross sections and different widths of Gaussians. A rise-and-fall behavior of the multiplicity of intermediate mass fragments (IMFs) is observed. The system size dependence of peak center-of-mass energy E$_{c.m.} ^{max}$ and peak IMF multiplicity $<$N$_{IMF}>^{max}$ is also studied, where it is observed that E$_{c.m.}^{max}$ follows a linear behavior and $<$N$_{IMF}>^{max}$ shows a power-law dependence. A comparison between two clusterization methods, the minimum spanning tree and the minimum spanning tree method with binding energy check (MSTB), is also made. We find that the MSTB method reduces the $<$N$_{IMF}>^{max}$, especially in heavy systems. The power-law dependence is also observed for fragments of different sizes at E$_{c.m.} ^{max}$ and the power-law parameter $τ$ is found to be close to unity in all cases except A$^{max}$.

## Key claim

The peak center-of-mass energy for intermediate mass fragment (IMF) production scales linearly with system mass, while the peak IMF multiplicity follows a power-law dependence with exponent τ close to unity, but these scalings depend on the clusterization method used, with the minimum spanning tree with binding energy check (MSTB) method reducing the peak multiplicity especially in heavy systems compared to the simple minimum spanning tree (MST) method.

## Method

The authors performed quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) simulations of central collisions for seven systems ranging from 20Ne+20Ne to 197Au+197Au across incident energies from 10 to 400 MeV/nucleon. They varied the equation of state (hard, soft, hard with momentum-dependent interactions, soft with momentum-dependent interactions), nucleon-nucleon cross sections (energy-dependent Cugon cross section versus constant 55 mb isotropic cross section), and Gaussian widths (L = 1.08 and 2.16 fm²). Fragment formation was analyzed using both the minimum spanning tree (MST) method and the minimum spanning tree with binding energy check (MSTB) method.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction 86Kr+93Nb at 75 MeV/nucleon, changing the Gaussian width from L to Lbroad reduced the IMF multiplicity by approximately 30%.
- The peak center-of-mass energies (Ec.m.^max) for the reactions studied were approximately: 24 MeV/nucleon for 20Ne+20Ne, 46 MeV/nucleon for 40Ar+45Sc, 69 MeV/nucleon for 58Ni+58Ni, 78 MeV/nucleon for 86Kr+93Nb, 96 MeV/nucleon for 129Xe+118Sn, 124 MeV/nucleon for 86Kr+197Au, and 105 MeV/nucleon for 197Au+197Au.
- The power-law exponent τ for peak multiplicities of different fragment types was close to unity in all cases except for the largest fragment (A^max), which showed no clear system size dependence.
- The binding energy per nucleon for heavy fragments at 200 fm/c was around -5 to -7 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the experimental findings of Sisan et al. (2001), who observed linear scaling of Ec.m.^max and power-law scaling of peak IMF multiplicity with system mass, and the theoretical QMD study of Vermani and Puri (2009) that partially explained this behavior. The authors systematically test how different model ingredients (equation of state, cross section, Gaussian width) and clusterization methods affect these scalings, finding that the MSTB method improves agreement with experimental data for heavy systems by filtering out loosely bound fragments. The results enable more reliable comparisons between QMD simulations and experimental multifragmentation data, particularly for heavy systems.
