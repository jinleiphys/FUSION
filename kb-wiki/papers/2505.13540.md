# An Improvement of AmpRed: Analytic Continuation of Complex Integrals

> The AmpRed package has been updated with an improved method for analytic continuation of complex integrals. Compared to the previous version, the new implementation significantly enhances computational efficiency for evaluating complex integrals.

## Key claim

The paper claims that by choosing the pair of Feynman parameters \(\{x_i, x_j\}\) in the auxiliary scale \(\mathcal{E}^{(0)}\) such that the coefficients of the monomials \(x_i^a\) in the polynomial \(\mathcal{F}\) are positive (or negative) definite, the branch of the integral \(I_y\) in the auxiliary variable \(y\) is fixed by the \(-i0^+\) prescription, allowing complex integrals to be calculated with the same method and efficiency as real integrals.

## Method

The method introduces an auxiliary scale \(y\) into the parametric Feynman integral by inserting a delta function \(\delta(y - \mathcal{E}^{(0)}(x))\) with \(\mathcal{E}^{(0)} = x_i/x_j\), then integrates over the Feynman parameters to obtain an integral \(I_y\) that is evaluated using the differential-equation method. The key improvement is the selection of the parameter pair \(\{x_i, x_j\}\) such that the coefficients of \(x_i^a\) in \(\mathcal{F}\) are positive (or negative) definite, which fixes the physical branch of \(I_y\) in \(y\) and avoids the efficiency-reducing branch-determination procedure from the previous version. The method is benchmarked on integrals up to four loops, including both normal loop integrals and an integral with Wilson lines (linear propagators), with computation times compared against the AMFlow package.

## Key numbers

- For diagram (a) (two-loop integral): AmpRed computation time \(5.27 \times 10^3\) seconds; AMFlow computation time \(2.70 \times 10^3\) seconds.
- For diagram (b) (three-loop integral): AmpRed computation time \(2.86 \times 10^3\) seconds; AMFlow computation time \(8.64 \times 10^3\) seconds.
- For diagram (c) (four-loop integral): AmpRed computation time \(3.39 \times 10^4\) seconds; AMFlow computation time \(1.31 \times 10^5\) seconds.
- For diagram (d) (integral with two Wilson lines): AmpRed computation time \(1.54 \times 10^4\) seconds; AMFlow failed due to lack of memory (AmpRed used about 200 GB of memory).
- All calculations used 24 threads on an AMD EPYC 7R32 CPU at 2.8-3.3 GHz, with 520 GB RAM, KIRA2.3 as the IBP solver, and a precision goal of 20.

## Context

This work extends the AmpRed package (Chen, 2024) by improving the analytic continuation method for complex integrals, building on the iterative algorithm developed in Chen (2023). The benchmarks show that the new implementation is significantly more efficient than AMFlow (Liu, 2022) for three- and four-loop integrals, and can handle integrals with Wilson lines that AMFlow cannot compute due to memory constraints. The method enables efficient calculation of multi-loop master integrals in the Euclidean region, with the recommendation to use differential-equation methods with boundaries at singular points for multi-scale integrals.
