# My strange times with Johann Rafelski

> I will give a short review the physics of strangeness enhancement in quark-gluon plasma, and argue that it is currently the best candidate of a signature of deconfinement. I will also discuss what strangeness abundance can tell us about the bulk properties of the system created in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

Strangeness enhancement is currently the best candidate for a signature of deconfinement in heavy ion collisions, but the evidence is not yet conclusive because the effect has not been observed to turn off at energies where QGP effects would be negligible.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical basis for strangeness enhancement as a deconfinement signature, comparing the chemical equilibration timescales and equilibrium abundances of strange quarks in a quark-gluon plasma versus strange hadrons in a hadron gas. It then examines experimental data on strangeness enhancement across different collision systems (p-p, p-A, A-A) and energies, and discusses thermal model fits that include non-equilibrium parameters (γ_s, γ_q) to characterize the bulk properties of the system.

## Key numbers

- The strange quark mass is of the order of the critical temperature T_c, making it neither light nor heavy in a QGP just above deconfinement
- The threshold for s sbar pair production through gg ↔ s sbar collisions is √s ≥ 2 m_s ∼ T_c
- The threshold for hadronic reactions such as n π ↔ Λ K is √s ∼ m_K + m_Λ - m_π - m_N ∼ 500 MeV, which is much larger than T_c
- The dimensionless variable α = [χ_s(T,μ_B)/ρ_s(T,μ_B)] [dT/dτ + dμ_B/dτ] τ_s determines whether chemical equilibrium is maintained (α ≪ 1) or breaks down (α ≥ 1)
- The width of T_c is so narrow that an expanding system crosses it in less than 1 fm

## Context

This work extends the foundational insights of Hagedorn (who showed that an infinite tower of hadronic states implies an ultimate temperature) and Rafelski (who demonstrated this temperature coincides with quark deconfinement). It builds on the original strangeness enhancement predictions by Rafelski and collaborators (orig1, orig2) and discusses how current data from experiments such as ALICE at the LHC show enhancement at all energies above the Coulomb barrier. The paper contrasts the equilibrium statistical model approach (Becattini, Kaneta, Petran) with the non-equilibrium scenario involving γ_s and γ_q parameters, and identifies future experiments at FAIR, SHINE, RHIC low-energy runs, and NICA as capable of testing whether enhancement turns off at lower energies.
