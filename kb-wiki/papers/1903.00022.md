# Neutrino Quantum Kinetics in Compact Objects

> Neutrinos play a critical role of transporting energy and changing the lepton density within core-collapse supernovae and neutron star mergers. The quantum kinetic equations (QKEs) combine the effects of neutrino-matter interactions treated in classical Boltzmann transport with the neutrino flavor-changing effects treated in neutrino oscillation calculations. We present a method for extending existing neutrino interaction rates to full QKE source terms for use in numerical calculations. We demonstrate the effects of absorption and emission by nucleons and nuclei, electron scattering, electron-positron pair annihilation, nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung, neutrino-neutrino scattering. For the first time, we include all these collision terms self-consistently in a simulation of the full isotropic QKEs in conditions relevant to core-collapse supernovae and neutron star mergers. For our choice of parameters, the long-term evolution of the neutrino distribution function proceeds similarly with and without the oscillation term, though with measurable differences. We demonstrate that electron scattering, nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung processes, and four-neutrino processes dominate flavor decoherence in the protoneutron star (PNS), absorption dominates near the shock, and all of the considered processes except elastic nucleon scattering are relevant in the decoupling region. Finally, we propose an effective decoherence opacity that at most energies predicts decoherence rates to within a factor of 10 in our model PNS and within 20% outside of the PNS.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method for extending existing neutrino interaction rates to full quantum kinetic equation (QKE) source terms, and demonstrates for the first time that all major collision terms can be included self-consistently in simulations of isotropic QKEs under core-collapse supernova and neutron star merger conditions; however, the authors note that for their chosen parameters, the long-term evolution of the neutrino distribution function proceeds similarly with and without the oscillation term, showing only measurable rather than dramatic differences.

## Method

The authors developed the open-source code IsotropicSQA to evolve the QKEs under assumptions of isotropy, homogeneity, and flat spacetime using a stochastic operator-split method that separates oscillation evolution (via unitary evolution matrices) from collision evolution. They generalized flavor-incoherent neutrino interaction rates from the NuLib library to full QKE collision terms for absorption and emission by nucleons and nuclei, electron scattering, electron-positron pair annihilation, nucleon-nucleon bremsstrahlung, and neutrino-neutrino scattering, using a matching procedure that extends existing rate calculations without reevaluating Feynman diagrams. The simulations used a two-flavor system with normal hierarchy, mass splitting of Δm₂₁ = 2.43×10⁻³ eV², and a 9° mixing angle, with 50 energy bins centered on integer multiples of 2 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Mass splitting used: Δm₂₁ = 2.43×10⁻³ eV², with mass eigenstates rotated from flavor eigenstates by 9°
- Fiducial fluid parameters for test calculations: ρ = 10¹² g cm⁻³, T = 10 MeV, Yₑ = 0.3, yielding μₙ = 1.39 MeV, μₚ = −8.56 MeV, μₑ = 10.1 MeV, and μ_νₑ = 0.0977 MeV
- The proposed effective decoherence opacity predicts decoherence rates to within a factor of 10 in the model protoneutron star (PNS) and within 20% outside of the PNS
- Energy bins were centered on integer multiples of 2 MeV, each with a width of 2 MeV
- The target impact parameter I_target was set to 10⁻⁴ for the stochastic integration

## Context

This work extends the general form of collision integrals for quantum kinetic equations first constructed by Sigl (1993) and the detailed collision integrals for specific processes relevant to core-collapse supernovae and neutron star mergers fleshed out by Blaschke (2016). The paper enables future simulations that can simultaneously treat neutrino flavor oscillations and collisions in compact objects, addressing scenarios such as fast flavor conversions (Sawyer 2005, 2016; Chakraborty 2016) and the neutrino halo effect (Cherry 2012, 2013; Sarikas 2012; Mirizzi 2012; Cirigliano 2018) where these effects may not be separable. The authors note that while spin coherence effects are not expected to be important in core-collapse supernovae (Serreau 2014; Cirigliano 2015; Tian 2017), they may not be negligible in neutron star mergers (Chatelain 2017).
