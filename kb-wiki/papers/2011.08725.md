# Strange molecular partners of the $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_c(4020)$

> Quantum Chromodynamics presents a series of exact and approximate symmetries which can be exploited to predict new hadrons from previously known ones. The $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_c(4020)$ resonances, which have been theorized to be isovector $D^* \bar{D}$ and $D^* \bar{D}^*$ molecules [$I^G(J^{PC}) = 1^-(1^{+-})$], are no exception. Here we argue that from SU(3)-flavor symmetry, we should expect the existence of strange partners of the $Z_c$'s with hadronic molecular configurations $D^* \bar{D}_s$-$D \bar{D}_s^*$ and $D^* \bar{D}^{*}_s$ (or, equivalently, quark content $c\bar{c} s \bar{q}$, with $q = u, d$). The quantum numbers of these $Z_{cs}$ and $Z_{cs}^*$ resonances would be $I(J^P)$ = $\frac{1}{2}(1^+)$. The predicted masses of these partners depend on the details of the theoretical scheme used, but they should be around the $D^* \bar{D}_s$-$D \bar{D}_s^*$ and $D^* \bar{D}^{*}_s$ thresholds, respectively. Moreover, any of these states could be either a virtual pole or a resonance. We show that, together with a possible triangle singularity contribution, such a picture nicely agrees with the very recent BESIII data of the $e^+e^-\to K^+(D_s^-D^{*0}+D_s^{*-}D^0)$.

## Key claim
The authors argue that SU(3)-flavor symmetry predicts the existence of strange partners of the $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_c(4020)$ hadronic molecules, but these predicted $Z_{cs}$ and $Z_{cs}^*$ states could be either virtual poles or resonances, not necessarily bound states, and their masses depend on the theoretical scheme used.

## Method
The authors employ two effective field theory (EFT) approaches with contact-range interactions: a constant-contact potential and a resonant potential with a momentum-dependent term. They solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a Gaussian regulator to find pole positions, first fitting to the known $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_c(4020)$ poles from Albaladejo (2015), then independently fitting to BESIII data for $e^+e^-\to K^+(D_s^-D^{*0}+D_s^{*-}D^0)$ at five center-of-mass energies from 4.628 to 4.698 GeV, including triangle singularity contributions from the $D_{s2}(2573)$ resonance.

## Key numbers
- BESIII measured $M(Z_{cs}) = 3982.5^{+2.8}_{-3.3}$ MeV and $\Gamma(Z_{cs}) = 12.8^{+6.1}_{-5.3}$ MeV for the new strange hidden-charm state
- The $D \bar{D}_s^*$ and $D^* \bar{D}_s$ thresholds are separated by only 2.5 MeV, at 3979.4 and 3976.9 MeV respectively
- Predicted masses from the constant-contact EFT (using $Z_c$ input, $\Lambda=0.5$ GeV): $Z_{cs}$ at $3948^{+22}_{-27}$ MeV, $Z_{cs}^*$ at $4092^{+21}_{-26}$ MeV
- Predicted masses from the resonant EFT (using $Z_c$ input, $\Lambda=0.5$ GeV): $Z_{cs}$ at $3986 \pm 4 - i(22 \pm 7)$ MeV, $Z_{cs}^*$ at $4129 \pm 4 - i(21 \pm 7)$ MeV
- The $D_{s2}(2573)\bar{D}_s^*$ threshold that produces the triangle singularity is at $\sqrt{s}=4.681$ GeV
- The constant-contact coupling from fitting BESIII data: $C^{(O)}(\Lambda) = -0.77^{+0.12}_{-0.10}$ fm$^2$ for $\Lambda=0.5$ GeV

## Context
This work extends the molecular interpretation of the $Z_c(3900)$ and $Z_c(4020)$ (observed by BESIII in 2013) and the $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ (discovered by Belle in 2011) to the strange sector using SU(3)-flavor symmetry. The authors build on the heavy-quark spin symmetry framework of Voloshin (2016) and the contact-range EFT approach of Mehen (2011) and Valderrama (2012). The analysis enables predictions for the unobserved $Z_{cs}^*$ state as a spin partner and suggests that the full SU(3)-flavor multiplet should contain two additional isoscalar states, providing targets for future experiments at BESIII and LHCb.
