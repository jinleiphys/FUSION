# Modelling the nucleon structure

> We review the status of our understanding of nucleon structure based on the modelling of different kinds of parton distributions. We use the concept of generalized transverse momentum dependent parton distributions and Wigner distributions, which combine the features of transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions and generalized parton distributions. We revisit various quark models which account for different aspects of these parton distributions. We then identify applications of these distributions to gain a simple interpretation of key properties of the quark and gluon dynamics in the nucleon.

## Key claim

This review argues that generalized transverse momentum dependent parton distributions (GTMDs) and Wigner distributions provide a unifying framework for modeling nucleon structure, but the authors acknowledge that models oversimplify QCD dynamics and are constructed to mimic only selected aspects of the underlying theory.

## Method

The authors review the formalism for calculating GTMDs using light-front wave function (LFWF) overlap representations in quark models, working in light-cone quantization with the \(A^+=0\) gauge. They derive Wigner distributions as two-dimensional Fourier transforms of GTMDs and discuss how different choices of Wilson line paths in the gauge-invariant correlators lead to different definitions of quark orbital angular momentum (OAM), specifically comparing the Ji decomposition and the Jaffe-Manohar definition. The review then surveys calculations of transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs) and generalized parton distributions (GPDs) in various quark models, including light-front constituent quark models, the chiral-quark soliton model, and quark-diquark models.

## Key numbers

* The Wigner distributions shown for \(u\) and \(d\) quarks in an unpolarized proton are plotted at fixed transverse momentum \(k_\perp = 0.3\) GeV, with the \(x\)-integrated distributions revealing that the configuration \(\mathbf{b}_\perp \perp \mathbf{k}_\perp\) is favored over \(\mathbf{b}_\perp \parallel \mathbf{k}_\perp\).
* In the light-front constituent quark model calculation of the mean transverse momentum distribution for unpolarized quarks in a longitudinally polarized nucleon, the \(u\) quark OAM is in the positive \(\hat{z}\) direction with decreasing magnitude toward the periphery, while \(d\) quarks orbit counterclockwise in the central region and clockwise in the peripheral region, leading to a very small net OAM.
* The operator representing the average transverse momentum \(\langle \mathbf{k}_\perp(\mathbf{b}_\perp) \rangle\) with final state interactions included is given by \(\bar{\psi}(y)\gamma^+\left[-i\stackrel{\leftrightarrow}{\mathbf{D}}_{\mathbf{y}_\perp}+\int_{y^-}^\infty dr^- g\mathbf{G}^{+\perp}(r^-,\mathbf{y}_\perp)\right]\psi(y)\), where the second term represents the color Lorentz force integrated over time.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on nucleon structure modeling that began with the introduction of Wigner distributions in QCD by Ji (2003) and Belitsky (2003), and the development of GTMDs by Meissner (2009) and Lorce (2013). It provides a comprehensive comparison of different quark model approaches, including the light-front constituent quark model, the chiral-quark soliton model, and the quark-target model, and discusses how the choice of Wilson line path in the gauge link connects to the different definitions of quark OAM proposed by Ji (1996) and Jaffe and Manohar (1989). The review enables future work on the physical interpretation of the difference between these OAM definitions as arising from the torque exerted by final state interactions, and provides a framework for connecting GPDs and TMDs through simplifying assumptions about the transverse shift in impact-parameter space.
