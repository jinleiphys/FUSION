# Reinterpretation of Faddeev-Niemi Knot in Skyrme Theory

> We identify the Faddeev-Niemi knot in Skyrme theory as a vortex ring made of a helical baby skyrmion (a twisted chromomagnetic vortex which is periodic in $z$-coodinate) with the periodic ends connected together. This allows us to interpret the knot as two quantized magnetic flux rings linked together, the first one winding the second $m$ times and the second one winding the first $n$ times, whose linking number $mn$ is fixed by the Chern-Simon index of the magnetic potential. This interpretation strongly suggests that the Skyrme theory could also describe a very interesting low energy physics in a completely different environment, which puts the theory in a totally new perspective.

# Reinterpretation of Faddeev-Niemi Knot in Skyrme Theory

## Key claim

The Faddeev-Niemi knot in Skyrme theory is identified as a vortex ring made of a helical baby skyrmion (a twisted chromomagnetic vortex periodic in the z-coordinate) with its periodic ends connected together, which allows the knot to be interpreted as two quantized magnetic flux rings linked together. This interpretation is conditional on the Skyrme field being restricted to ω = π, which reduces the Skyrme Lagrangian to the Skyrme-Faddeev Lagrangian and makes the knot a dynamical manifestation rather than a purely mathematical construction from preimages of the Hopf mapping.

## Method

The author constructs a helical baby skyrmion solution using a CP¹ field ansatz in cylindrical coordinates (ϱ, φ, z), with the non-linear sigma field n expressed in terms of a profile function f(ϱ) and integers m and n that control the twist and winding. The equation of motion from the Skyrme-Faddeev Lagrangian is reduced to an ordinary differential equation for f(ϱ) with boundary conditions f(0) = π and f(∞) = 0, yielding non-Abelian vortex solutions. The helical vortex is then made into a ring by connecting its periodic ends, and the two quantized magnetic fluxes (one along the z-axis and one around it) are shown to correspond to two linked flux rings whose linking number mn equals the Chern-Simons index of the magnetic potential.

## Key numbers

- The quantized magnetic flux along the z-axis is φ_ẑ = 4πn/g, where n is an integer winding number.
- The quantized magnetic flux around the z-axis (in one period section from 0 to 2π/k) is φ_φ̂ = 4πm/g, where m is an integer winding number.
- The knot quantum number Q = mn, given by the Chern-Simons index Q = (1/32π²) ∫ ε_{ijk} C_i N_{jk} d³x = mn.
- The energy of the Faddeev-Niemi knot satisfies the bound c₁ Q^{3/4} ≤ E_Q ≤ c₂ Q^{3/4}, and numerically E_Q ≃ 252 Q^{3/4} √(α μ) for knots up to Q = 8.

## Context

This work extends the author's prior analysis of topological objects in Skyrme theory (Cho 2001, 2002) by providing a dynamical interpretation of the Faddeev-Niemi knot, which had previously been described only mathematically via the Hopf mapping π₃(S²) by Faddeev and Niemi. The reinterpretation connects the knot to non-Abelian monopole solutions similar to the Wu-Yang monopole in SU(2) QCD, and shows that the Skyrme-Faddeev Lagrangian is identical to a CP¹ model describing two-component superfluids. This enables the knot to be constructed in laboratories, specifically in two-component Bose-Einstein condensates (as suggested by experiments by Ruostekoski and others) and two-gap superconductors (as studied by Babaev).
