# Formation of sigma Mesic Nuclei in (d,t) and (d,^3He) Reactions

> We explore the possibility to observe an enhanced 2pi correlation in the scalar iso-scalar (sigma) channel in nuclei by (d,t) and (d,^3He) reactions to obtain new information on the partial restoration of chiral symmetry in the nuclear medium. The sensitivity of the reaction spectra on the change of the chiral condensate in the nuclear medium is examined.

## Key claim

The paper explores the possibility of observing an enhanced 2π correlation in the scalar-isoscalar (σ) channel in nuclei using (d,t) and (d,³He) reactions, but the shape of the spectral function becomes insensitive to the change of the chiral condensate in nuclei because the effective number (N_eff) has large weight on shallow states; only the absolute magnitude of the spectral function is sensitive to the chiral condensate change.

## Method

The authors solve the Klein-Gordon equation for the σ meson in finite nuclei using a self-energy derived from the SU(2) linear σ-model in the one-loop approximation, with the local density approximation applied to construct the σ-nucleus optical potential. They then calculate the expected spectral functions for (d,t) and (d,³He) reactions on ²⁰⁸Pb targets using the effective number approach, which accounts for the reaction mechanism and distortion effects via the Eikonal approximation. The calculations are performed at deuteron kinetic energies T_d = 1.5 GeV and 4 GeV to examine recoilless kinematics conditions.

## Key numbers

- The chiral condensate parameter C takes values 0.2, 0.3, and 0.4, where C is defined by Φ(ρ)=1-C(ρ/ρ₀) in the linear density approximation
- For ²⁰⁸Pb, the number of bound σ eigenstates (nl) found is 17 for C=0.2, 26 for C=0.3, and 32 for C=0.4
- The elementary cross section for d+n→t+σ→t+2π is estimated to be 20 μb/sr, based on d+p→³He+X spectra data from Banaigs et al.
- The estimated double differential cross sections at the dominant peak are 4 to 10 μb/sr/GeV
- The background cross section is roughly estimated to be 3 μb/sr/GeV for light nuclear target cases
- The nuclear density distribution for ²⁰⁸Pb uses Woods-Saxon parameters: radius 6.5 fm and diffuseness 0.5 fm
- An additional 5 MeV is added to ImΣ_σ to regularize the infinity at ω=ω_nl for eigenstates with zero width

## Context

This work extends the recoilless meson production method previously developed for deeply bound pionic atoms using (d,³He) reactions (Hirenzaki 1991, Yamazaki 1996, Gillitzer 2000) to the scalar-isoscalar channel, motivated by theoretical predictions that the σ meson mass and width decrease in nuclear medium due to partial restoration of chiral symmetry (Hatsuda 1984, Hatsuda 1999). The study is related to experimental observations by the CHAOS collaboration and Crystal Ball collaboration of softening in the invariant mass distribution of emitted 2π in A(π,ππ)A' reactions, and provides predictions for future experiments to search for σ mesic nuclei, though the authors note that the model of Z. Aouissat et al. (Schuck) including ph contributions gives larger half-widths that would not allow narrow bound states.
