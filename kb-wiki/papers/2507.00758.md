# Collectivity of rotational motion in $^{220}$Rn and $^{226}$Ra

> Calculations to reconstruct rotational level patterns in the $^{220}$Rn and $^{226}$Ra nuclei have been performed using a collective quadrupole+octupole approach with microscopic mass tensor and moments of inertia dependent on deformation and pairing degrees of freedom. The main objective is to quantitatively confirm the known experimental observations that the Rn nucleus passes from octupole vibrational to octupole deformed with increasing rotation frequency, while the Ra nucleus is relatively weakly affected by collective rotation, being octupole deformed from the beginning. The collective potential in a nine-dimensional collective space is determined using the macroscopic-microscopic method with Strutinsky and the BCS with an approximate particle number projection microscopic corrections. The corresponding Hamiltonian is diagonalized based on the projected solutions of the harmonic oscillators coupled with Wigner functions. Such an orthogonalized basis is additionally symmetrized with respect to the so-called intrinsic symmetrization group, specifically dedicated to the collective space used, to ensure the uniqueness of the Hamiltonian eigen-solutions in the laboratory frame. The response of the pairing and deformation degrees of freedom to external rotation is discussed in the variational approach, where the total energy is minimized by the deformation and pairing variables. Consequently, the corresponding microscopic moments of inertia increase with collective spin (Coriolis {\it antiparing} effect), resulting in effectively lower rotational energy levels I$^π$ with respect to pure classical-rotor pattern I(I+1). The obtained comparison of experimental and theoretical rotational energy level schemes, dipole, quadrupole and octupole transition probabilities of B(E$λ$) in $^{220}$Rn and $^{226}$Ra is satisfactory.

## Key claim

The paper quantitatively confirms that $^{220}$Rn transitions from octupole vibrational to octupole deformed with increasing rotation frequency, while $^{226}$Ra remains octupole deformed from the start and is only weakly affected by collective rotation; however, this confirmation depends on a variational minimization of total energy over nine collective degrees of freedom including pairing gaps, and the agreement with experiment is described as "satisfactory" rather than exact.

## Method

The authors use a macroscopic-microscopic model with Strutinsky shell correction of 6th order and particle-projected BCS pairing to compute the collective potential in a nine-dimensional space of quadrupole, octupole, and hexadecapole deformations plus neutron and proton pairing gaps. The collective Hamiltonian is diagonalized in a basis of harmonic-oscillator solutions coupled with Wigner functions, symmetrized with respect to the D4y intrinsic symmetrization group, and the rotational term uses microscopic cranking moments of inertia that depend on deformation and pairing. The total energy for each spin is minimized over all deformation and pairing variables to find equilibrium configurations, and transition probabilities B(E1), B(E2), and B(E3) are computed from the resulting wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The energy difference between the 0+ ground state and the 1- state is 253 keV in $^{226}$Ra, compared to 645 keV in $^{220}$Rn.
- The octupole energy well depth in $^{226}$Ra is approximately 0.5 MeV, consistent with other theoretical estimates.
- For $^{220}$Rn, the neutron pairing gap $\Delta_n$ falls to approximately 0 for spin $I=9\hbar$ and axial octupole deformation $\alpha_{30}\approx 0.2$, indicating a transition from superfluid to normal state.
- The moment of inertia for $^{220}$Rn at low spin is approximately 2-3 $\hbar^2$/MeV, increasing with spin due to the Coriolis antipairing effect.
- The ground-state equilibrium octupole deformation for $^{226}$Ra is $\alpha_{30}\approx 0.17$, remaining nearly constant with spin.
- For $^{220}$Rn, the ground state at zero spin is nearly spherical with $\alpha_{20}\approx 0.05$ and $\alpha_{30}=0$, shifting to $\alpha_{30}\approx 0.15$ for $I\geq 5\hbar$.

## Context

This work extends the collective quadrupole+octupole model developed by Dobrowolski, Mazurek, and collaborators (2016, 2018) to describe rotational bands in octupole-related nuclei, and directly addresses the experimental observations of Cocks et al. (1997) on $^{220}$Rn and $^{226}$Ra. The results confirm the earlier experimental classification that $^{226}$Ra has a permanent axial-octupole deformation while $^{220}$Rn is octupole vibrational at low spin and becomes octupole deformed with increasing rotation. The approach enables future studies of exotic symmetries and non-axial octupole degrees of freedom in rotating nuclei, as discussed by Dudek and Dedes (2018).
