# Quark Phase Transition Parameters and $δ$-Meson Field in RMF Theory

> The deconfinement phase transition from hadronic matter to quark matter in the interior of compact stars is investigated. The hadronic phase is described in the framework of relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory, when also the scalar- isovector $δ$-meson effective field is taken into account. To describe a quark phase the MIT bag model is used. The changes of the mixed phase threshold parameters caused by the presence of $δ$-meson field are investigated.

# Quark Phase Transition Parameters and δ-Meson Field in RMF Theory

## Key claim
The inclusion of the scalar-isovector δ-meson field in relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory alters the threshold parameters of the mixed hadron-quark phase in compact stars: it increases the lower threshold baryon density, energy density, and pressure, while slightly decreasing the upper threshold values, but the central pressure of maximum-mass neutron stars remains below the upper threshold, so hybrid stars do not contain a pure strange quark matter core.

## Method
The hadronic phase was described using an RMF Lagrangian density including σ, ω, ρ, and δ meson fields, with δ-meson coupling constant a_δ = 2.5 fm² and standard saturation properties (n₀ = 0.153 fm⁻³, binding energy −16.3 MeV, incompressibility K = 300 MeV, asymmetry energy 32.5 MeV). The quark phase was described using the MIT bag model with quark masses m_u = 5 MeV, m_d = 7 MeV, m_s = 150 MeV, bag constant B = 60 MeV/fm³, and strong coupling α_s = 0.5. The mixed phase parameters were computed by solving for the coexistence of hadronic and quark matter under the condition of two conserved charges (baryon number and electric charge).

## Key numbers
- Lower threshold baryon density with δ-meson: n_N = 0.077 fm⁻³; without δ-meson: n_N = 0.072 fm⁻³
- Lower threshold pressure with δ-meson: P_N = 72.793 MeV/fm³; without δ-meson: P_N = 67.728 MeV/fm³
- Lower threshold energy density with δ-meson: ε_N = 0.434 MeV/fm³; without δ-meson: ε_N = 0.336 MeV/fm³
- Upper threshold baryon density: n_Q = 1.083 fm⁻³ (same with and without δ-meson)
- Upper threshold pressure with δ-meson: P_Q = 1280.884 MeV/fm³; without δ-meson: P_Q = 1280.889 MeV/fm³
- Upper threshold energy density with δ-meson: ε_Q = 327.745 MeV/fm³; without δ-meson: ε_Q = 327.747 MeV/fm³
- δ-meson coupling constant: a_δ = 2.5 fm²; corresponding ρ coupling constant: a_ρ = 13.621 fm² (with δ) versus a_ρ = 4.794 fm² (without δ)

## Context
This work extends the author's previous study (Alaverdyan 2009a) which examined the first-order Maxwell construction for the hadron-quark transition with δ-meson effects, now investigating the mixed-phase scenario. The results contrast with the earlier Maxwell construction where δ-meson inclusion decreased transition pressure and densities; here the mixed-phase lower thresholds increase. The finding that maximum-mass neutron star central pressures lie below P_Q means these hybrid stars lack pure strange quark cores, which constrains models of compact star interiors and informs future work on the role of isovector scalar interactions in dense matter.
