# Superscaling analysis of inclusive electron and (anti)neutrino scattering within the coherent density fluctuation model

> The experimental data from quasielastic electron and (anti)neutrino scattering on $^{12}$C are reanalyzed in terms of a new scaling variable $ψ^*$ suggested by the interacting relativistic Fermi gas with scalar and vector interactions, which is known to generate a relativistic effective mass for the interacting nucleons. We construct a new scaling function $f^\text{QE}(ψ^*)$ for the inclusive lepton scattering from nuclei within the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM). The latter is a natural extension of the relativistic Fermi gas model to finite nuclei. In this work, on the basis of the scaling function obtained within CDFM with a relativistic effective mass $m_N^* =0.8 m_N$, we calculate and compare the theoretical predictions with a large set of experimental data for inclusive ($e,e'$) and (anti)neutrino cross sections. The model also includes the contribution of weak two-body currents in the two-particle two-hole sector, evaluated within a fully relativistic Fermi gas. Good agreement with experimental data is found over the whole range of electron and (anti)neutrino energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) with a relativistic effective mass of $m_N^* = 0.8 m_N$ and a new scaling variable $\psi^*$ can describe inclusive electron and (anti)neutrino scattering data on $^{12}$C, but only when two-particle two-hole meson exchange current (MEC) contributions are added; the pure quasielastic results without MEC agree with NOMAD data while the full model overpredicts them.

## Method

The authors construct a scaling function $f^\text{QE}(\psi^*)$ within the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) by convoluting the relativistic Fermi gas scaling function with a weight function derived from empirical proton density distributions of $^{12}$C (using the three-parameter Fermi parametrization). They compute inclusive $(e,e')$ cross sections and charge-changing (anti)neutrino cross sections using the factorized response function formalism with the new scaling variable $\psi^*$ from the relativistic mean field model, and add two-body weak meson exchange current contributions in the two-particle two-hole sector evaluated within a fully relativistic Fermi gas with effective mass $M^*=1$.

## Key numbers

- Effective mass ratio $M^* = m_N^* / m_N = 0.8$ is used for all calculations, taken from the best value obtained in the SuSAM* model by Ama15.
- The 2p-2h MEC contribution is of the order of 20-25% of the total response at the maximum for neutrinos, and approximately 30-35% compared with the pure quasielastic contribution at all neutrino energies.
- The CDFM$_{M^*}$ scaling function and weight function are normalized to unity: $\int_{0}^{\infty} |F(x)|^2 dx = 1$ and $\int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f^\text{QE}(\psi^*) d\psi^* = 1$.
- The Fermi constant used is $G = 1.166 \times 10^{-11} \text{ MeV}^{-2}$ and $\cos\theta_c = 0.975$ for the Cabibbo angle.

## Context

This work extends the SuSAM* model of Ama15, Ama17, Mar17, Ama18, which introduced the $\psi^*$ scaling variable from relativistic mean field theory with effective mass, by implementing it within the coherent density fluctuation model framework previously developed by the authors (A7, A8, A9, A10). The CDFM$_{M^*}$ model improves upon the SuSA approach by maintaining gauge invariance (which SuSA violates through an energy shift for separation energy) and describes the dynamical enhancement of transverse response. The results enable predictions for charge-changing neutrino and antineutrino scattering on other nuclei and for processes in the Delta-resonance region, and the authors note that future work should adopt an alternative parametrization of 2p2h MEC responses calculated in RMF with $M^*=0.8$ from PhysRevC.104.025501 and PhysRevD.104.113006.
