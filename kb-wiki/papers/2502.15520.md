# Kubo formulas for spin polarization in dissipative relativistic spin hydrodynamics: a first-order gradient expansion approach

> We use linear response theory to derive both the non-dissipative and dissipative effects of spin polarization for massive and massless interacting spin 1/2 particles in a relativistic fluid. We list and classify all the possible contributions up to first order in gradients of hydrodynamic fields including the axial chemical potential and the spin potential, and we obtain the corresponding Kubo formulas. We find that all the possible dissipative contributions, except those coming from the gradients of spin potential, require a chiral imbalance or parity violating interactions. In a fluid with chiral imbalance we find a chiral version of the spin Hall effect, i.e. a spin polarization is induced by the gradients of temperature and of axial chemical potential in the direction orthogonal to the momentum of the particle and to the gradients. Moreover, we identify several other new non-dissipative contributions that are not present for free fields.

## Key claim

The paper derives Kubo formulas for both non-dissipative and dissipative spin polarization in relativistic spin hydrodynamics up to first order in gradients, finding that all dissipative contributions except those from gradients of the spin potential require a chiral imbalance or parity-violating interactions; in a fluid with chiral imbalance, a chiral version of the spin Hall effect emerges where spin polarization is induced by gradients of temperature and axial chemical potential orthogonal to both the particle momentum and the gradients.

## Method

The author uses the Zubarev non-equilibrium density operator method to perform a first-order gradient expansion of the Dirac Wigner function for massive and massless interacting spin-1/2 particles, separating local equilibrium (non-dissipative) and dissipative contributions. Linear response theory is applied to obtain Kubo formulas for the thermal and transport coefficients, classifying all possible contributions by their behavior under parity (P), time-reversal (T), and charge conjugation (C) symmetries. The momentum dependence of the Wigner function is handled by decomposing it in terms constructed with the particle momentum and hydrodynamic fields, including the axial chemical potential and spin potential.

## Key numbers

* The thermal vorticity tensor is defined as \(\varpi_{\rho\sigma}(x) = -\frac{1}{2} [\partial_\rho\beta_\sigma(x) - \partial_\sigma\beta_\rho(x)]\) and the thermal shear tensor as \(\xi_{\rho\sigma}(x) = \frac{1}{2} [\partial_\rho\beta_\sigma(x) + \partial_\sigma\beta_\rho(x)]\), with \(\beta = u/T\).
* The dissipative response is expressed as \(\Delta_{\mathcal{U}, \mathrm{D}} O(x) = \mathcal{U}_{(\alpha)}(x) c_{\mathcal{U}} (\hat{O}, \hat{\mathcal{C}}_{\mathcal{U}}^{(\alpha)})_{\mathrm{D}}\), where the correlator is \((\hat{X}, \hat{Y})_{\mathrm{D}} = \frac{i}{|\beta(x)|} \int_{-\infty}^t d^4 x_2 \int_{-\infty}^{t_2} ds \langle [\hat{X}(x), \hat{Y}(s, \mathbf{x}_2)] \rangle_{\beta(x)}\).
* The dissipative correlator in momentum space is \((\hat{X}, \hat{Y})_{\mathrm{D}} = -\frac{1}{|\beta(x)|} u^\lambda \lim_{p\cdot u \to 0} \lim_{p_\perp \to 0} \frac{\partial}{\partial p^\lambda} \mathrm{Im}\, G^R_{\hat{X}\hat{Y}}(p)\), where \(G^R\) is the retarded Green's function.
* The projector orthogonal to fluid velocity is \(\Delta^{\mu\nu} = \eta^{\mu\nu} - u^\mu u^\nu\), and the symmetric traceless projector is \(\Delta_{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} = \frac{1}{2}(\Delta_{\mu\rho}\Delta_{\nu\sigma} + \Delta_{\mu\sigma}\Delta_{\nu\rho}) - \frac{1}{3}\Delta_{\mu\nu}\Delta_{\rho\sigma}\).

## Context

This work extends the spin hydrodynamics framework developed by Florkowski (2017) and Weickgenannt (2019) by providing a systematic classification of all first-order gradient contributions to spin polarization, including dissipative effects that were largely unaddressed in prior studies focusing on local equilibrium contributions from thermal vorticity (Becattini 2015) and thermal shear (Liu 2021, Becattini 2021). The results enable calculation of transport coefficients for spin polarization in the quark-gluon plasma formed in heavy-ion collisions, addressing discrepancies between experimental data on local spin polarization from STAR and ALICE collaborations and theoretical predictions, and providing a foundation for future studies of spin alignment of vector mesons.
