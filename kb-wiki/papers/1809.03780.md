# Effect of Hagedorn States on Isothermal Compressibility of Hadronic Matter formed in Heavy-Ion Collisions: From NICA to LHC Energies

> In this work, we have studied the isothermal compressibility ($κ_T$) as a function of temperature, baryon chemical potential and centre-of-mass energy ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}$) using hadron resonance gas (HRG) and excluded-volume hadron resonance gas (EV-HRG) models. A mass cut-off dependence of isothermal compressibility has been studied for a physical resonance gas. Further, we study the effect of heavier resonances ($>$ 2 GeV) on the isothermal compressibility by considering the Hagedorn mass spectrum, $ρ(m)\sim{\exp(bm)}/{(m^2+m_0^2)^{5/4}}$. Here, the parameters, $b$ and $m_0$ are extracted after comparing the results of recent lattice QCD simulations at finite baryonic chemical potential. We find a significant difference between the results obtained in EV-HRG and HRG models at a higher temperatures and higher baryochemical potentials. The inclusion of the Hagedorn mass spectrum in the partition function for hadron gas has a large effect at a higher temperature. A higher mass cut-off in the Hagedorn mass spectrum takes the isothermal compressibility to a minimum value, which occurs near the Hagedorn temperature ($T_H$). We show explicitly that at the future low energy accelerator facilities like FAIR (CBM), Darmstadt and NICA, Dubna the created matter would be incompressible compared to the high energy facilities like RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The isothermal compressibility of hadronic matter formed in heavy-ion collisions decreases with increasing collision energy and saturates above \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 10-20\) GeV, with the inclusion of Hagedorn states (resonances above 2 GeV) significantly lowering the compressibility values compared to standard hadron resonance gas models, particularly at higher temperatures.

## Method

The authors calculated isothermal compressibility (\(\kappa_T\)) using the ideal hadron resonance gas (HRG) model and the excluded-volume hadron resonance gas (EV-HRG) model, considering all hadrons and resonances with masses up to 2 GeV. They then incorporated the Hagedorn continuous mass spectrum \(\rho(m) \sim \exp(bm)/(m^2+m_0^2)^{5/4}\) with parameters \(A = 0.57\) GeV\(^{-3/2}\), \(m_0 = 0.425\) GeV, and Hagedorn temperature \(T_H = 0.180\) GeV extracted from lattice QCD simulations, integrating masses from 2 GeV up to 10 GeV. The collision energy dependence was obtained by parametrizing temperature and baryon chemical potential as functions of \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) using chemical freeze-out conditions.

## Key numbers

- Isothermal compressibility decreases by 23% in number density when the mass cut-off increases from 1.0 GeV to 1.5 GeV at \(\mu_B = 0.5\) GeV and \(T = 0.170\) GeV in the HRG model.
- The Hagedorn temperature used is \(T_H = 0.180\) GeV, with parameters \(A = 0.57\) GeV\(^{-3/2}\) and \(m_0 = 0.425\) GeV.
- Isothermal compressibility saturates for collision energies above \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 10-20\) GeV, with significant differences between EV-HRG, HRG, and HRG+HS models for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} > 10\) GeV.
- The matter created at lower collision energies (FAIR and NICA) is more compressible than that created at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of thermodynamic observables in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the speed of sound and shear viscosity to entropy ratio, which also showed modifications when Hagedorn states were included (Noronha-Hostler 2008, Majumder 2010, Jakovac 2013, Khuntia 2016). The results are compared with experimental data from Mukherjee (2017) and with transport model calculations from AMPT, UrQMD, and EPOS. The study enables the use of isothermal compressibility as a probe for locating the QCD critical point, suggesting the onset of phase transition in the collision energy range of 10-20 GeV per nucleon, which is relevant for future facilities like FAIR (CBM) and NICA.
