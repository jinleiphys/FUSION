# Higher-Order Kinematical Effects in Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering

> We study the deeply virtual Compton scattering cross-section in twist-two generalized parton distribution (GPD) approximation, and show that different choices of light-cone vectors and gauges for the final photon polarization will lead to different higher-order kinematical corrections to the cross-section formula. The choice of light-cone vectors affects kinematic corrections at the twist-three level, accounting for the differences between the cross-section formulas in the literature. On the other hand, kinematical corrections from higher-twist GPDs should eliminate the light-cone dependence at twist three. Those light-cone dependencies are studied systematically at JLab 12 GeV and future EIC kinematics. They serve as the intrinsic systematic uncertainties in extracting the Compton form factors through the cross-section formula. More importantly, they are also necessary for understanding cross-section measurements with higher-twist precision and to reconstruct higher-order Compton form factors.

## Key claim

The paper shows that different choices of light-cone vectors and photon polarization gauges in the deeply virtual Compton scattering cross-section formula produce different higher-order kinematical corrections at twist-three level, even when only leading-twist Compton form factors are included; these differences serve as intrinsic systematic uncertainties that must be accounted for when extracting Compton form factors from experimental data at JLab 12 GeV and future EIC kinematics.

## Method

The authors systematically parameterize the light-cone vectors using two free parameters α and β, which control the direction of the light-cone vectors relative to the physical momenta q, q', P, and P'. They then compute the DVCS helicity amplitudes and cross-section formulas using the twist-two Compton tensor coefficients expressed in terms of these general light-cone vectors, expanding to twist-four accuracy. The resulting formulas are compared analytically and numerically with previous conventions from Ji (1996), Belitsky-Kirchner-Müller (BKM, 2001), Braun-Manashov-Pirnay (BMP, 2011), and the UVa convention (Kriesten, 2019), and the light-cone dependence is studied at JLab 12 GeV and EIC kinematics.

## Key numbers

- The twist-four parameter related to target mass M gives M²/Q² ~ 20% at JLab 12 GeV kinematics where Q² ~ 5 GeV², described as "quite substantial for precision measurements of CFFs"
- The helicity amplitude for ΛΛ' = ++ (or --) at twist-two level is 1/2 - [x_B²(t(1-x_B)+M²x_B²)(β-1)²] / [2(1+(β-1)x_B)² Q²] + O(Q⁻³), showing β-dependence at twist-three level
- The helicity amplitude for ΛΛ' = 0+ (or 0-) is (β-1)√[-x_B²(t(1-x_B)+M²x_B²)] / [√2(1+(β-1)x_B) Q] + O(Q⁻³), also β-dependent at twist-three
- The skewness parameter ξ is expanded as ξ = x_B/(2-x_B) - [2x_B{2αM²x_B²+(x_B-1)[1-2α+(β-1)x_B]t}] / [(x_B-2)²(1+(β-1)x_B)Q²] + O(Q⁻⁴)
- For α=0, the exact solution gives ξ = x_B(1+t/Q²) / [2-x_B(1-t/Q²)]

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for DVCS cross-section calculations by systematically studying the dependence on light-cone vector conventions, which had been treated inconsistently across prior approaches including Ji (1996), Belitsky-Kirchner-Müller (2001), Braun-Manashov-Pirnay (2011), and the UVa convention (Kriesten, 2019). The paper shows that the light-cone dependence at twist-three level can be canceled by including kinematical corrections from twist-three GPDs, which is a precondition for separating higher-twist Compton form factors from kinematical contributions. This enables future work on reconstructing higher-twist GPDs from experimental measurements at JLab 12 GeV and the future Electron-Ion Collider.
