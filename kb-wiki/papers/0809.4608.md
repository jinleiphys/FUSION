# Photoabsorption off nuclei with self consistent vertex corrections

> We study photoproduction off nuclei based on a self consistent and covariant many body approach for the pion and isobar propagation in infinite nuclear matter. For the first time the t-channel exchange of an in-medium pion is evaluated in the presence of vertex correction effects consistently. In particular the interference pattern with the s-channel in-medium nucleon and isobar exchange contribution is considered. Electromagnetic gauge invariance is kept as a consequence of various Ward identities obeyed by the computation. Adjusting the set of Migdal parameters to the data set we predict an attractive mass shift for the isobar of about 50 MeV at nuclear saturation density.

# Photoabsorption off nuclei with self consistent vertex corrections

## Key claim

The paper predicts an attractive mass shift for the isobar (Delta resonance) of about 50 MeV at nuclear saturation density, but this result depends on adjusting the set of Migdal parameters to reproduce nuclear photoabsorption data, and the apparent peak position seen in the absorption cross section does not directly reflect the isobar contribution due to short-range correlation effects in the gamma-N-Delta vertex.

## Method

The authors compute the photoabsorption cross section for infinite nuclear matter using a self-consistent and covariant many-body approach for pion and isobar propagation, incorporating scalar and vector mean fields for nucleons and isobars. They evaluate the t-channel exchange of an in-medium pion with vertex correction effects for the first time, using Migdal's short-range correlation parameters to describe the gamma-pi-pi, gamma-N-Delta, gamma-pi-N-Delta, pi-N-Delta, and pi-N-N vertices, while maintaining electromagnetic gauge invariance through Ward identities.

## Key numbers

- Attractive mass shift for the isobar of about 50 MeV at nuclear saturation density (k_F = 0.27 GeV)
- Nucleon scalar mean field Sigma_S^N = 0.35 GeV and vector mean field Sigma_V^N = 0.29 GeV at saturation density
- Migdal parameters: g'_11 = 1.0, g'_12 = 0.4, g'_22 = 0.4
- Isobar scalar mean field Sigma_Delta_S = -0.25 GeV and vector mean field Sigma_Delta_V = -0.11 GeV
- Free-space coupling constants: f_N = 0.988, f_Delta = 1.85
- Electric and magnetic coupling constants: f_gamma = 0.012, f'_gamma = 0.024
- Background contribution delivers about 100 microbarns to the cross section; resonance contributes about 200 microbarns in the peak; two-particle hole final states deliver an additional 50 microbarns
- Extrapolated linearly to saturation density: 15% reduction of f_Delta and 15% increase for f_gamma compared to free-space values
- Averaged density of 0.8 times saturation density used to compute the absorption cross section

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent covariant approach for pion and isobar propagation developed in Korpa, Lutz, and Riek (2008) by incorporating vertex correction effects in the photoabsorption calculation. It contradicts earlier phenomenological spreading potential models (Hirata, Koch, Lenz, and Monitz) that suggested a small repulsive mass shift for the isobar, and instead supports the predictions of Oset and Salcedo, and Korpa and Lutz (2004) that found an attractive mass shift. The results enable future studies of how short-range correlation effects modify the apparent isobar mass seen in photoabsorption data, and demonstrate that the neglect of such effects in the pi-N-Delta vertex leads to an almost flat photoabsorption cross section.
