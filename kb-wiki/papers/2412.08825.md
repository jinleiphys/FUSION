# Scattering phase shifts from overlap relations in the $J$-matrix method

> The scattering problem can be implemented in a square-integrable basis via the so-called $J$-matrix method. While methods to compute the phase shift in the $J$-matrix approach are known, we introduce a novel formula in square-integrable bases analogous to existing integral relations or overlap integrals in a (continuous) position basis. We demonstrate the method in single-channel potential scattering. Such a result is the first step towards a more general approach to scattering and reactions in popular many-body methods such as the configuration-interaction shell model.

## Key claim

The authors derive a novel formula for computing scattering phase shifts within the J-matrix method using square-integrable basis functions, analogous to integral relations in continuous coordinate space. The method is demonstrated only for single-channel potential scattering, and the authors state it is "the first step towards a more general approach" to many-body methods such as the configuration-interaction shell model.

## Method

The authors derive a scattering overlap relation (SOR) in a discrete, square-integrable basis (specifically a harmonic oscillator basis) by manipulating the tridiagonal recursion relations for the kinetic energy operator and the free regular and irregular solutions. They compute the phase shift via tan δ = −(Σ f_n V_{nm} u_m) / (α₀ u₀ + Σ g_n V_{nm} u_m), where the sums run only over the finite range of the potential (N_pot), and they solve for the scattering coefficients u_n using the Lippmann-Schwinger equation rather than full matrix diagonalization.

## Key numbers

- Square-well potential (dimensionless parameters ℏ=μ=1, radius R=1, depth V₀=−5): phase shifts for ℓ=0,1,2,3 reproduced with N_pot=10 and oscillator length b=0.42, giving ground state energy −2.312 (agreement with coordinate-space result within numerical error).
- Daejeon-16 interaction (isoscalar ³S₁ nucleon-nucleon): phase shifts reproduced with N_pot=5, b=1.83 fm (ℏω=25 MeV), but ground state energy was −1.022 MeV (poor value due to excluding ³D₁ coupling).
- Malfliet-Tjon potential (V_a=−635 MeV, V_r=1458 MeV, μ_a=1.55 fm⁻¹, μ_r=3.11 fm⁻¹): required N_pot=50 and b_min=1.18 fm to obtain the general trend of experimental phase shifts, with slight oscillations; deuteron binding energy −2.29 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the integral-relation approach for phase shifts (previously applied in coordinate-space methods by Suzuki (2009), PhysRevC.81.034002, PhysRevC.86.044330, and PhysRevC.108.034001) to the J-matrix formalism using square-integrable bases. The method enables future application to many-body frameworks such as configuration-interaction shell model calculations, where coordinate-space asymptotics are often intractable. The authors note that the Malfliet-Tjon potential (with a strong repulsive core) required a larger model space than the softer Daejeon-16 interaction, and they plan to generalize to coupled-channel calculations and Coulomb scattering.
