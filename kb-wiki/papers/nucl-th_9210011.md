# Relativistic Corrections to the Triton Binding Energy

> The influence of relativity on the triton binding energy is investigated. The relativistic three-dimensional version of the Bethe-Salpeter equation proposed by Blankenbecler and Sugar (BbS) is used. Relativistic (non-separable) one-boson-exchange potentials (constructed in the BbS framework) are employed for the two-nucleon interaction. In a 34-channel Faddeev calculation, it is found that relativistic effects increase the triton binding energy by about 0.2 MeV. Including charge-dependence (besides relativity), the final triton binding energy predictions are 8.33 and 8.16 MeV for the Bonn A and B potential, respectively.

# Relativistic Corrections to the Triton Binding Energy

## Key claim

Relativistic effects increase the triton binding energy by about 0.2 MeV when using the Blankenbecler-Sugar (BbS) three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation with non-separable one-boson-exchange potentials; however, the magnitude of this relativistic correction is method-dependent, with the BbS approximation yielding only about 75% of the effect obtained from the full four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter equation as reported by Rupp and Tjon (1988, 1992).

## Method

The authors performed 34-channel Faddeev calculations for the triton using relativistic one-boson-exchange potentials (Bonn A and Bonn B) constructed within the Blankenbecler-Sugar framework. Relativistic corrections were incorporated through three modifications to the non-relativistic calculation: (1) use of a relativistic Faddeev propagator derived from a dispersion integral over the two-particle invariant energy, (2) covariant definition of the kinematical variables for the two interacting nucleons, and (3) use of an invariant two-body t-matrix. Charge-dependence in the $^1S_0$ two-nucleon potential was also included to provide complete predictions from the two-body force alone.

## Key numbers

- Relativistic correction to triton binding energy: 0.19 MeV for Bonn A, 0.20 MeV for Bonn B (34-channel calculation)
- Non-relativistic 34-channel result with Bonn A: 8.32 MeV; relativistic result: 8.51 MeV
- Final triton binding energy including relativity and charge-dependence: 8.33 MeV for Bonn A, 8.16 MeV for Bonn B
- Charge-dependence reduces triton binding energy by 0.18 MeV
- Largest partial relativistic effect: invariant t-matrix increases binding by 0.26 MeV; relativistic Faddeev propagator reduces binding by 0.14 MeV; covariant kinematics increases binding by 0.07 MeV (all for Bonn A, 34 channels)
- Remaining underbinding relative to experiment: 0.15–0.30 MeV depending on the potential choice

## Context

This work extends the program of triton binding energy calculations using realistic two-nucleon potentials, where previous benchmark calculations (Brandenburg 1988, Wiringa et al. 1991) had shown underbinding of 0.2–1.1 MeV when only two-body forces were applied. The results contrast with the Paris potential, which predicts only about 7.59 MeV for the triton binding energy (Wiringa et al. 1991), despite being nearly identical on-shell to Bonn B. The finding that relativistic effects plus charge-dependence nearly explain the triton binding energy from two-body forces alone challenges the conventional interpretation that three-body forces are needed to fill the gap, consistent with the work of Picklesimer et al. (1991) showing that consistent treatment of two- and three-body forces involving the Delta isobar yields an almost vanishing net three-body force contribution. The paper enables future work comparing different relativistic three-dimensional reductions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation and assessing whether the full four-dimensional equation (as used by Rupp and Tjon 1988, 1992) or three-dimensional approaches like the BbS scheme are more physically appropriate for nuclear few-body systems.
