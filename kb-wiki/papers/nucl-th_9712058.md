# Charged current weak electroproduction of Delta resonance

> We study the weak production of $Δ$ (i.e. $e^{-} + p \to Δ^{0}+ ν_{e}$ and $e^{+} + p \to Δ^{++} + \barν_{e}$) in the intermediate energy range corresponding to the Mainz and TJNAF electron accelerators. The differential cross sections $σ(θ)$ are found to be of the order of $ 10^{-39}$ cm$^2$/sr, over a range of angles which increases with energy. The possibility of observing these reactions with the high luminosities available at these accelerators, and studying the weak N-$Δ$ transition form factors through these reactions is discussed. The production cross section of N$^*(1440)$ in the kinematic region of $Δ$ production is also estimated and found to be small.

# Charged current weak electroproduction of Delta resonance

## Key claim

The differential cross sections for weak Δ production via electron beams are of order 10⁻³⁹ cm²/sr over a wide angular range, making these reactions potentially observable at Mainz and TJNAF luminosities, *provided* the Δ decay width is properly included; earlier calculations neglecting the width predicted a much narrower angular range requiring 0.1° angular resolution, which the present work shows is unnecessary.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential cross section dσ/dΩ for the charged-current weak reactions e⁻ + p → Δ⁰ + νₑ and e⁺ + p → Δ⁺⁺ + ν̅ₑ using the standard N-Δ transition current with vector form factors C₃,₄,₅^V (constrained by CVC from electromagnetic data) and axial form factors C₃,₄,₅,₆^A (constrained by PCAC and neutrino scattering data). The Δ resonance is treated with a finite decay width Γ using a Breit-Wigner propagator, and the cross section is integrated over the Δ invariant mass with a cut W < 1.4 GeV. Three different parametrizations of the form factors are compared: a phenomenological dipole set, the Hemmert et al. (1995) set, and a nonrelativistic quark model set from Liu et al. (1995). The production of the Roper N*(1440) resonance is also estimated using vector form factors extracted from Gerhardt's (1980) helicity amplitudes and axial form factors from PCAC.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross sections for Δ production are of order 10⁻³⁹ cm²/sr over a range of angles that increases with energy.
- At Eₑ = 855 MeV, the cross section for e⁻ + p → Δ⁰ + νₑ is larger than for e⁺ + p → Δ⁺⁺ + ν̅ₑ; at Eₑ = 4.0 GeV, the e⁺ reaction cross section is about a factor of two larger than the e⁻ reaction.
- The peak in the differential cross section shifts from approximately 20° at Eₑ = 855 MeV to approximately 40° at Eₑ = 4.0 GeV.
- There is a 50% gain in total cross section when increasing from the maximum Mainz energy (0.855 GeV) to 4 GeV.
- The N*(1440) production cross section is an order of magnitude smaller than the Δ production cross section.
- The N* angular cross section peaks around cos θ = 0.82, while the Δ production peaks around cos θ = 0.73.
- At Eₑ = 4.0 GeV, the estimated count rate is approximately 360 counts per hour per steradian per detector efficiency unit in the peak region near 40°.
- The vector form factor parametrization used is C₃^V(q²) = 2.05/(1 − q²/0.54 GeV²)² for the first set, and C₃^V(q²) = 1.39/[√(1 − q²/1.43 GeV²) (1 − q²/0.71 GeV²)²] for the second set.
- The axial form factors use C₅^A(0) = 1.2, C₄^A(0) = −0.3, C₃^A(0) = 0, with Mₐ = 1.0 GeV and dipole form factors modified by a factor [1 − aᵢq²/(bᵢ − q²)] with a₄ = a₅ = −1.21, b₄ = b₅ = 2 GeV².
- The Δ decay width is given by Γ = (1/6π)(f*/m_π)² (M/W) |q_cm|³ θ(W − M − m_π) with f* = 2.13.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Hwang et al. (1987), who used a bag model for N-Δ form factors and neglected the Δ decay width, finding a narrow angular range requiring 0.1° resolution. The present study incorporates the Δ width, which broadens the angular distribution considerably, and uses form factors constrained by modern neutrino scattering data (Kitagaki et al., 1990; Radecky et al., 1982) and electromagnetic data via CVC. The results enable experimental searches for weak Δ production at Mainz Microtron and TJNAF, where luminosities of 10³⁸ cm⁻² s⁻¹ or more are expected, and could allow model-independent determination of the axial form factor C₅^A if the vector form factors C₃^V and C₄^V are precisely fixed by electromagnetic measurements. The study also shows that N*(1440) contamination is negligible for Δ⁰ identification and absent for Δ⁺⁺ identification.
