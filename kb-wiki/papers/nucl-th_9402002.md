# Antisymmetrization of a Mean Field Calculation of the T-Matrix

> The usual definition of the prior(post) interaction $V(V^\prime )$ between projectile and target (resp. ejectile and residual target) being contradictory with full antisymmetrization between nucleons, an explicit antisymmetrization projector ${\cal A}$ must be included in the definition of the transition operator, $ T\equiv V^\prime{\cal A}+V^\prime{\cal A}GV. $ We derive the suitably antisymmetrized mean field equations leading to a non perturbative estimate of $T$. The theory is illustrated by a calculation of forward $α$-$α$ scattering, making use of self consistent symmetries.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard definition of the prior (post) interaction in nuclear reaction theory is inconsistent with full antisymmetrization between nucleons, and that an explicit antisymmetrization projector must be included in the transition operator definition; the authors derive antisymmetrized mean field equations that provide a non-perturbative estimate of the T-matrix, illustrated by a calculation of forward alpha-alpha scattering using self-consistent symmetries.

## Method

The authors construct three Schwinger-like variational functionals (F, F', F) of two independent trial functions, each incorporating the antisymmetrization projector A in slightly different positions, and show they all yield the same stationary value equal to the multistep transition amplitude D(z). They then introduce a time-independent mean field (TIMF) approximation where the trial functions are replaced by Slater determinants of single-particle orbitals, derive the stationarity conditions leading to coupled mean field equations with non-Hermitian channel potentials S, S', and U, and prove that a representation diagonalizing both the overlap matrix and the mean-field Hamiltonian matrix is consistent with the solutions. Self-consistent symmetries (unitary and antiunitary) are developed to reduce the number of coupled equations, and the method is applied to forward alpha-alpha scattering.

## Key numbers

* The total number of nucleons in the collision is N, with projectile a having N_a nucleons, target b having N_b nucleons, ejectile c having N_c nucleons, and residual target d having N_d nucleons, such that N = N_a + N_b = N_c + N_d.
* The functional F is defined as F = C + C' - D, where C = <Phi' | V | chi>, C' = <chi' | V' | Phi>, and D = <Phi' | (z-H) | Phi>, with z being a complex energy (eventually z -> E + i0).
* The reference energy for the mean-field Hamiltonian h is given by E_bar = <Phi' | H | Phi> / <Phi' | Phi> = sum_k <phi'_k | (t + U/2) | phi_k> / <phi'_k | phi_k>.
* The self-energy for orbital phi_i is eta_i = z - E_bar + <phi'_i | h | phi_i> / <phi'_i | phi_i>.

## Context

This work extends the time-independent mean field (TIMF) approximation introduced in prior work by the same group (referred to as "earlier" in the text) to include proper antisymmetrization between projectile and target nucleons, resolving a contradiction in the standard definition of the prior/post interaction. The formalism is designed to enable non-perturbative calculations of the T-matrix for two-fragment collisions (elastic, inelastic, or with rearrangement) using boosted shell model wave functions for the initial and final channel states, similar to those used in TDHF calculations (referenced as [3] in the text). The numerical application to forward alpha-alpha scattering demonstrates the use of self-consistent symmetries to simplify the mean field equations, and the authors suggest future work should investigate whether the "one-orbital-partner" approximation (neglecting off-diagonal parts of overlap matrices) is valid.
