> We have calculated electrical conductivity in the presence of a magnetic field by using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model.

# NJL model estimation of anisotropic electrical conductivity for quark matter in presence of magnetic field

## Key claim
The electrical conductivity of quark matter in a magnetic field is anisotropic, with the anisotropy controlled by the ratio of the thermal relaxation time τ_c to the magnetic relaxation time τ_B; when τ_c > τ_B the longitudinal conductivity σ_zz exceeds the transverse components, while when τ_c < τ_B the anisotropy is small.

## Method
The authors use the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a thermo-magnetic coupling constant G(B,T) to compute the constituent quark mass via the gap equation at finite temperature and magnetic field. They then solve the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation time approximation (RTA) to obtain the dissipative current, deriving three components of the electrical conductivity tensor σ_n (n=0,1,2) as functions of temperature and magnetic field.

## Key numbers
- Electrical conductivity components σ_0, σ_1, σ_2 are calculated at eB = 0.2 GeV² for two relaxation times: τ_c = 5 fm and τ_c = 0.2 fm.
- For τ_c = 5 fm (τ_c > τ_B regime): σ_xx = σ_yy < σ_zz, indicating larger anisotropy.
- For τ_c = 0.2 fm (τ_c < τ_B regime): σ_xx = σ_yy ≈ σ_zz, indicating smaller anisotropy.
- All conductivity components increase with temperature and show a kink near the quark-hadron phase transition temperature T_c.

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of electrical conductivity in quark matter by incorporating the NJL model's dynamical mass generation and inverse magnetic catalysis effects, building on the kinetic theory framework of Sedrakian and earlier work by Dey et al. on quark matter in magnetic fields. The results enable more realistic modeling of the lifetime of magnetic fields generated in non-central heavy-ion collisions, where the conductivity controls how quickly the magnetic field decays.
