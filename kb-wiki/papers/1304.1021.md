# Low-lying even parity meson resonances and spin-flavor symmetry revisited

> We review and extend the model derived in Phys. Rev. D83 016007 (2011) to address the dynamics of the low-lying even parity meson resonances. This model is based on a coupled channels spin-flavor extension of the chiral Weinberg-Tomozawa Lagrangian. This interaction is then used to study the S-wave meson-meson scattering involving members not only of the ПЂ-octet, but also of the ПЃ-nonet. In this work, we study in detail the structure of the SU(6) symmetry breaking contact terms that respect (or softly break) chiral symmetry. We derive the most general local (without involving derivatives) terms consistent with the chiral symmetry breaking pattern of QCD. After introducing sensible simplifications to reduce the large number of possible operators, we carry out a phenomenological discussion of the effects of these terms. We show how the inclusion of these pieces leads to an improvement of the description of J^P = 2^+ sector, without spoiling the main features of the predictions obtained in the original model in the J^P = 0^+ and J^P = 1^+ sectors. In particular, we find a significantly better description of the I^G(J^{PC})=0^+(2^{++}), 1^-(2^{++}) and the I(J^{P})=\frac{1}{2}(2^{+}) sectors, which correspond to the f_2(1270), a_2(1320) and K^*_2(1430) quantum numbers, respectively.

## Method

The authors extend the spin-flavor extension of the chiral Weinberg-Tomozawa Lagrangian from Garcia-Recio et al. (2011) by deriving the most general local (derivative-free) four-meson contact terms consistent with the chiral symmetry breaking pattern of QCD, using a single trace in the spirit of the large N_C expansion. They reduce the large number of possible operators to three chiral-invariant combinations, then introduce two new coupling constants (gв‚‚ and gв‚ѓ) for the additional operators beyond the minimal choice used previously. The resulting interaction kernel is used in a renormalized coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation to study S-wave meson-meson scattering involving the ПЂ-octet and ПЃ-nonet, and poles are searched for on the complex plane to identify dynamically generated resonances.
