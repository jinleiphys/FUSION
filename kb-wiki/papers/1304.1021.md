# Low-lying even parity meson resonances and spin-flavor symmetry revisited

> We review and extend the model derived in Phys. Rev. D83 016007 (2011) to address the dynamics of the low-lying even parity meson resonances. This model is based on a coupled channels spin-flavor extension of the chiral Weinberg-Tomozawa Lagrangian. This interaction is then used to study the S-wave meson-meson scattering involving members not only of the π-octet, but also of the ρ-nonet. In this work, we study in detail the structure of the SU(6) symmetry breaking contact terms that respect (or softly break) chiral symmetry. We derive the most general local (without involving derivatives) terms consistent with the chiral symmetry breaking pattern of QCD. After introducing sensible simplifications to reduce the large number of possible operators, we carry out a phenomenological discussion of the effects of these terms. We show how the inclusion of these pieces leads to an improvement of the description of J^P = 2^+ sector, without spoiling the main features of the predictions obtained in the original model in the J^P = 0^+ and J^P = 1^+ sectors. In particular, we find a significantly better description of the I^G(J^{PC})=0^+(2^{++}), 1^-(2^{++}) and the I(J^{P})=\frac{1}{2}(2^{+}) sectors, which correspond to the f_2(1270), a_2(1320) and K^*_2(1430) quantum numbers, respectively.

## Key claim

The authors show that including two new SU(6) symmetry-breaking contact terms (operators O₂ and O₃) that respect chiral symmetry significantly improves the description of the Jᴾ = 2⁺ sector (specifically the f₂(1270), a₂(1320), and K*₂(1430) resonances) without spoiling the predictions of the original model for the Jᴾ = 0⁺ and Jᴾ = 1⁺ sectors.

## Method

The authors extend the spin-flavor extension of the chiral Weinberg-Tomozawa Lagrangian from Garcia-Recio et al. (2011) by deriving the most general local (derivative-free) four-meson contact terms consistent with the chiral symmetry breaking pattern of QCD, using a single trace in the spirit of the large N_C expansion. They reduce the large number of possible operators to three chiral-invariant combinations, then introduce two new coupling constants (g₂ and g₃) for the additional operators beyond the minimal choice used previously. The resulting interaction kernel is used in a renormalized coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation to study S-wave meson-meson scattering involving the π-octet and ρ-nonet, and poles are searched for on the complex plane to identify dynamically generated resonances.

## Key numbers

- The vector meson nonet averaged mass used is m_V = 856 MeV.
- The chiral-limit pion decay constant used is f ~ 90 MeV.
- The dimensional regularization scale is μ = 1 GeV.
- The improved description targets the f₂(1270), a₂(1320), and K*₂(1430) resonances, corresponding to the Iᴳ(Jᴾᶜ) = 0⁺(2⁺⁺), 1⁻(2⁺⁺), and I(Jᴾ) = ½(2⁺) sectors respectively.

## Context

This work extends the spin-flavor symmetry model of Garcia-Recio et al. (2011) (Phys. Rev. D83 016007), which successfully described Jᴾ = 0⁺ and 1⁺ sectors but gave a poorer description of the Jᴾ = 2⁺ sector. The hidden gauge interaction model for vector mesons used by Molina et al. (2008) and Geng et al. (2008) was more successful for the f₂(1270) and K*₂(1430) resonances, but the present work aims to improve the SU(6) framework by adding chiral-invariant contact terms. The new terms enable a more consistent treatment of the VV sector while maintaining the successful features of the original model for the PP and PV sectors.
