# Oscillating HBT radii and the time evolution of the source - sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV Au+Au data analyzed with azimuthally sensitive Buda-Lund hydro model

> Identified particle spectra of pions, kaons and (anti)protons, and elliptic flow and azimuthal dependence of Bose-Einstein or HBT correlations of identified pions in sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions is analyzed simultaneously using an ellipsoidally symmetric generalization of the Buda-Lund hydrodynamical model. The transverse flow is found to be faster in the reaction plane than out of plane, which results in a reaction zone that gets slightly more elongated in-plane than out of plane.

## Key claim
The transverse flow is faster in the reaction plane than out of plane, which causes the reaction zone to become slightly elongated in-plane at freeze-out, contrary to the out-of-plane elongation observed in previous Blast Wave model analyses of the same data.

## Method
The authors performed simultaneous fits of the azimuthally sensitive Buda-Lund (asBuda-Lund) hydrodynamic model to PHENIX and STAR data from 20-30% centrality Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. The model uses a double saddle-point approximation to compute analytic expressions for identified particle spectra (pions, kaons, antiprotons), elliptic flow v2, and azimuthally dependent HBT radii, which were then fitted to the experimental data using the CERN Minuit package.

## Key numbers
- Central temperature T0 = 174 ± 6 MeV for 20-30% centrality, compared to 196 ± 13 MeV for 0-30% centrality
- Hubble constant in reaction plane Hx = 0.158 ± 0.002, out of plane Hy = 0.118 ± 0.002
- Geometrical radius in-plane Rx = 9.5 ± 0.5 fm, out-of-plane Ry = 7.0 ± 0.2 fm
- Mean freeze-out proper time tau0 = 5.4 ± 0.1 fm/c for 20-30% centrality
- Longitudinal distribution width Delta eta = 2.5 ± 0.3 for 20-30% centrality
- Baryochemical potential muB = 27 ± 16 MeV for 20-30% centrality
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for full fit with statistical errors: 269.4/152

## Context
This work extends the axially symmetric Buda-Lund model previously applied to central Au+Au collisions at RHIC by Csorgo et al. (2002) and Csanad et al. (2003) to non-central collisions with azimuthal sensitivity. It contradicts earlier Blast Wave model analyses by Adams et al. (2004) and hydrodynamic simulations by Heinz et al. (2002) that found out-of-plane elongation at freeze-out. The results enable further study of the time evolution of the fireball geometry and the interplay between spatial and flow anisotropies in heavy ion collisions.
