# Low-energy scattering in four nucleon systems. Method of Cluster Reduction

> A method using an expansion of the four-body Yakubovsky wave function components onto the basis of the Faddeev-equation solutions for the two-cluster sub-Hamiltonian eigenfunctions is exploited for computations of low-energy scattering parameters in four nucleon systems. Results of calculations of low-energy scattering parameters in $n-{^3}$H, $n-{^3}$He are presented.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the cluster reduction method, which expands four-body Yakubovsky wave function components onto a basis of Faddeev-equation solutions for two-cluster subsystems, yields low-energy scattering parameters for four-nucleon systems that agree with prior calculations and experimental data, but only when the basis expansion is truncated at a finite number N not exceeding 20 and the Coulomb interaction is included for charged-particle channels.

## Method

The authors start from the Yakubovsky differential equations (YDE) for four-nucleon systems and perform a cluster reduction by expanding the YDE components in a complete (but non-orthogonal) basis of Faddeev-equation eigenfunctions for the two-cluster subsystems. They project the resulting equations onto a biorthogonal basis formed by solutions of the conjugate Faddeev equations, obtaining reduced YDE (RYDE) that are one-dimensional in the relative cluster coordinate after partial-wave decomposition. The RYDE are solved numerically using finite-difference approximation, spline expansion of the integrand, and truncation of the basis sum at N ≤ 20, with the MT I-III model for nucleon-nucleon forces.

## Context

This work extends the cluster reduction method previously developed by Yakovlev and Filikhin (1998) for four-body scattering to the specific case of low-energy nucleon-trinucleon and deuteron-deuteron collisions. The results agree with the direct discretization of YDE by the Grenoble group (Carbon, Ciesiel-Fonseca) for the isospin-symmetric case and with Resonating Group Method calculations by Vasil (1998) for charged-particle channels, while also providing new complex scattering lengths for n-³He and ²H-²H systems where open rearrangement channels are present. The method enables efficient computation of four-nucleon scattering on standard workstations and can be extended to include Coulomb effects for charged-particle reactions.
