# Femtoscopic study on $DD^*$ and $D\bar{D}^*$ interactions for $T_{cc}$ and $X(3872)$

> We investigate $DD^*$ and $D\bar{D}^*$ momentum correlations in high-energy collisions to elucidate the nature of $T_{cc}$ and $X(3872)$ exotic hadrons. Single range Gaussian potentials with the channel couplings to the isospin partners are constructed based on the empirical data. The momentum correlation functions of the $D^0D^{*+}$, $D^+D^{*0}$, $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$, and $D^+D^{*-}$ pairs are computed with including the coupled-channel effects. We discuss how the nature of the exotic states are reflected in the behaviors of the correlation results.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that femtoscopic momentum correlations of $DD^*$ and $D\bar{D}^*$ pairs can reveal the molecular nature of the $T_{cc}$ and $X(3872)$ exotic hadrons, but only under the assumption that these states are dominated by their hadronic molecule components; the correlation functions are computed using empirically determined scattering lengths rather than predicted from first principles.

## Method
The authors construct single-range Gaussian potentials for the $DD^*$ and $D\bar{D}^*$ systems with complex strength parameters, including coupled-channel effects between isospin partners ($D^0D^{*+}$/$D^+D^{*0}$ for $T_{cc}$ and $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$/$D^+D^{*-}$ for $X(3872)$) and Coulomb interaction for the charged $D^+D^{*-}$ channel. The potential strengths are fitted to reproduce the experimentally determined scattering lengths from LHCb data for $T_{cc}$ and PDG eigenenergy for $X(3872)$. Momentum correlation functions are then calculated using the Koonin-Pratt-Lednicky-Lyuboshitz-Lyuboshitz (KPLLL) formula with a common static Gaussian source function for source sizes $R = 1, 2, 3, 5$ fm.

## Key numbers
- $T_{cc}$ input: scattering length $a_0^{D^0D^{*+}} = -7.16 + i1.85$ fm from LHCb experimental analysis
- $X(3872)$ input: eigenenergy $E_h = -0.04 - i0.60$ MeV measured from $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$ threshold, giving scattering length $a_0^{D^0\bar{D}^{*0}} = -4.23 + i3.95$ fm
- $DD^*$ potential strength: $V_0 = -36.569 - i1.243$ MeV
- $D\bar{D}^*$ potential strength: $V_0 = -43.265 - i6.091$ MeV
- Cusp structure in $D^0D^{*+}$ correlation appears at $D^+D^{*0}$ threshold momentum $q \simeq 52$ MeV/$c$
- Cusp structure in $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$ correlation appears at $D^+D^{*-}$ threshold momentum $q \simeq 126$ MeV/$c$
- For $D^+D^{*-}$ pair, the strong interaction deviation from pure Coulomb $\Delta C$ is less than 0.2 for $q > 50$ MeV/$c$

## Context
This work extends femtoscopic techniques previously applied in the strangeness sector (e.g., $pK^-$, $p\Xi^-$, $\Lambda\Lambda$ correlations by Kamiya (2019, 2021) and ALICE collaborations) to the charm sector, following the recent ALICE measurement of $D^-p$ correlations (2022). The study assumes molecular nature for both $T_{cc}$ and $X(3872)$, in contrast to alternative interpretations such as the $c\bar{c}$ component emphasized by Bignamini (2009) for $X(3872)$. The results enable future experimental measurements of $DD^*$ and $D\bar{D}^*$ correlations to independently determine scattering lengths and, combined with Weinberg's weak-binding relation, to quantify the compositeness (molecular probability) of these exotic states.
