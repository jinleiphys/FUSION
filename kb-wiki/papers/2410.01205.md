# Constraints on the $U(1)_{B-L}$ model from global QCD analysis

> We perform the first global QCD analysis of electron-nucleon deep-inelastic scattering and related high-energy data including the beyond the Standard Model $U(1)_{B-L}$ gauge boson, $Z'$. Contrary to the dark photon case, we find no improvement in the $χ^2$ relative to the baseline result. The finding allows us to place exclusion limits on the coupling constant of the $Z'$ with mass in the range $M_{Z'} = 2$ to 160 GeV.

## Key claim

The first global QCD analysis including the $U(1)_{B-L}$ gauge boson $Z'$ finds no improvement in $\chi^2$ relative to the baseline Standard Model fit, in contrast to the dark photon case studied by Hunt-Smith (2023). This null result allows the authors to place exclusion limits on the coupling constant $g_z$ for $Z'$ masses between 2 and 160 GeV.

## Method

The authors performed a global QCD analysis within the JAM framework using Monte Carlo sampling and $\chi^2$ minimization, fitting parton distribution functions (PDFs) at next-to-leading order (NLO) in $\alpha_s$ to world data on electron-nucleon deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) from SLAC, BCDMS, NMC, and HERA, along with Drell-Yan data from NuSea and SeaQuest, $Z$-boson rapidity and $W$-boson asymmetry data from the Tevatron, and jet production data from $p\bar{p}$ collisions. The $Z'$ boson was included via its vector couplings to Standard Model fermions in the structure functions, with the $Z'$ parameters $M_{Z'}$ and $g_z$ treated as free parameters alongside 42 PDF and nuclear correction parameters. Exclusion limits at 95% confidence level were derived from the condition $\chi^2_{Z'}(g_z) - \chi^2_{\rm baseline} = 3.8$ for each fixed $M_{Z'}$.

## Key numbers

- The $Z'$ mass range considered is $M_{Z'} = 2$ to 160 GeV.
- The 95% CL exclusion limit on $g_z$ at $M_{Z'} = 160$ GeV is 0.085 (baseline) and 0.080 after accounting for missing higher-order uncertainties.
- The muon $g-2$ discrepancy used in the analysis is $\Delta a_{\mu}^{\rm exp} = (251 \pm 59) \times 10^{-11}$, taken from Muon g-2 (2021).
- The $\chi^2$ threshold for exclusion at 95% CL for one degree of freedom is $\chi^2_{Z'}(g_z) - \chi^2_{\rm baseline} = 3.8$.

## Context

This work extends the global QCD analysis approach previously applied to the dark photon by Hunt-Smith (2023) to the $U(1)_{B-L}$ model, finding that the $Z'$ boson does not produce the $\chi^2$ improvement seen for the dark photon. The resulting exclusion limits on $g_z$ are stronger than constraints from neutrino-quark scattering (Heeck, 2014) and neutrino-nucleus scattering from COHERENT (Cadeddu, 2020) over the mass range 10 to 160 GeV, and are complementary to direct search limits from BaBar (2017) and LHCb (2019). The analysis framework can be extended to other flavor-dependent $U(1)'$ models, and future measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider, LHeC, and FCC-eh may place stronger constraints on $Z'$ parameters.
