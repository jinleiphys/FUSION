# Hadronic parity violation: successes, challenges, and future prospects

> Hadronic parity violation concerns the study of the interplay of the weak- and strong-interaction dynamics that yields low energy, parity-violating observables in systems of hadrons and nuclei. We explain its essential features, as well as our current understanding of its observed effects, describing recent theoretical and experimental progress in a pedagogical context. We provide a broad overview of ongoing research efforts to show how precision studies of few-nucleon systems can be extended to studies of complex nuclei and, ultimately, to new benchmarks for computations in the Standard Model, as well as to new searches for the dynamics beyond it.

## Key claim

Hadronic parity violation arises from the interplay of weak and strong interactions at low energies, and while the Standard Model predicts observable parity-violating effects that have been measured to be nonzero, reconciling the size of observed parity-violating asymmetries in different few-nucleon processes with the nuclear anapole moment in cesium has proven challenging, possibly due to limitations in the meson-exchange description or in the treatment of nuclear structure effects.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical description of hadronic parity violation from the weak scale down to hadronic scales, using renormalization group methods within perturbative QCD to evolve the effective Hamiltonian from the W-boson mass scale down to approximately 2 GeV, where the theory transitions from quark to hadron degrees of freedom via chiral effective theory. They describe how the effective Hamiltonian for flavor-conserving, parity-violating quark processes is constructed as an operator product expansion of twelve four-quark operators that form a closed set under QCD mixing, with Wilson coefficients computed at next-to-leading order including two-loop gluonic contributions. The authors then discuss how these quark-level operators can be matched onto hadron-level descriptions, such as the DDH meson-exchange model, to compute parity-violating meson-nucleon couplings.

## Key numbers

- The Fermi coupling constant is given as $G_F \simeq 1.166 \times 10^{-5} \, \mathrm{GeV^{-2}}$
- The characteristic size of hadronic parity-violating effects is $G_F M_\pi^2 \simeq 10^{-7}$, where $M_\pi$ is the pion mass
- The first observation of hadronic parity violation via circular polarization in ${}^{181}$Ta gave $P_\gamma = (-6 \pm 1) \times 10^{-6}$
- The strong coupling ratios at NLO are: $\alpha_s(m_b=4.18\,\text{GeV})/\alpha_s(M_W=80.379\,\text{GeV}) = 1.86$; $\alpha_s(m_c=1.27\,\text{GeV})/\alpha_s(m_b) = 1.75$; $\alpha_s(\Lambda=2\,\text{GeV})/\alpha_s(m_c) = 0.75$
- The W boson mass is $M_W \simeq 80~\mathrm{GeV}$ and the Z boson mass is approximately $M_Z \simeq 91~\mathrm{GeV}$
- The weak mixing angle enters the effective Hamiltonian with $\sin^2\theta_W$ extracted as an overall factor

## Context

This review extends the DDH framework (Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein, 1980) by constructing a rigorous effective-field-theory description of hadronic parity violation that properly accounts for QCD renormalization effects and operator mixing, addressing limitations in the phenomenological meson-exchange model. The work connects recent experimental results from the NPDGamma collaboration (measuring $\vec{n}p \to d\gamma$) and the $n^3$He collaboration (measuring $\vec{n} + {}^3\mathrm{He} \to t + p$) to theoretical developments in chiral effective theory and lattice QCD, enabling future precision tests of the Standard Model and searches for physics beyond it. The authors note that the resolution to the puzzle of reconciling parity-violating asymmetries in polarized $\vec{p}-p$ scattering, radiative decays of excited-state $^{18,19}$F, and the cesium anapole moment (Wood, 1997) may require improved treatment of both the effective interaction and nuclear structure effects.
