# Predictive power of transverse-momentum-dependent distributions

> We investigate the predictive power of transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) distributions as a function of the light-cone momentum fraction $x$ and the hard scale $Q$ defined by the process. We apply the saddle point approximation to the unpolarized quark and gluon transverse momentum distributions and evaluate the position of the saddle point as a function of the kinematics. We determine quantitatively that the predictive power for an unpolarized transverse momentum distribution is maximal in the large-$Q$ and small-$x$ region. For cross sections the predictive power of the TMD factorization formalism is generally enhanced by considering the convolution of two distributions, and we explicitly consider the case of $Z$ and $H^0$ boson production. In the kinematic regions where the predictive power is not maximal, the distributions are sensitive to the non-perturbative hadron structure. Thus, these regions are critical for investigating hadron tomography in a three-dimensional momentum space.

## Key claim

The predictive power of unpolarized transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) distributions is maximal in the large-hard-scale (Q) and small-momentum-fraction (x) region, but this conclusion is conditional on the saddle point of the TMD in b_T-space falling in the perturbative small-b_T regime; when the saddle point lies at large b_T, the distributions become sensitive to non-perturbative hadron structure and are better suited for constraining non-perturbative parameters rather than making perturbative predictions.

## Method

The authors apply the saddle-point approximation to the unpolarized quark and gluon TMD parton distribution functions (PDFs) in b_T-space (the Fourier conjugate of transverse momentum k_T), solving for the saddle point b_T^sp as a function of x and Q. They construct the full b_T-dependence by using perturbative QCD evolution at small b_T (b_T ≤ 0.5 GeV^-1) and extrapolating to large b_T via a non-perturbative function R_a^NP derived from power corrections to the Collins-Soper kernel, with continuity conditions at the matching point. The analysis is performed at next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) accuracy and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in the strong coupling, and is extended to cross sections for Z boson and Higgs boson production in pp collisions.

## Key numbers

- The matching point between perturbative and non-perturbative regions is set at b_max = 0.5 GeV^-1 throughout the paper.
- The one-loop coefficient of the QCD beta function is given as b_0 = (11 C_A - 4 T_f n_f) / (12 pi).
- The leading-order cusp anomalous dimension for quarks is Gamma_0 = 4 C_F, and the non-cusp anomalous dimension at leading order is gamma_0 = 6 C_F.
- The Euler constant gamma_E = 0.577 is used in defining mu_b = c / b_T with c = 2 e^{-gamma_E}.

## Context

This work extends the arguments of Parisi and Petronzio (1979) and Collins, Soper, and Sterman (1984) on the role of the hard scale Q relative to Lambda_QCD, and of Qiu (2000) and Berger (2002-2004) on the importance of the light-cone momentum fraction x, by placing them in the modern TMD factorization formalism of Collins (2011). The study links the predictive power of TMDs to their double-scale evolution (ultraviolet and rapidity renormalization), enabling quantitative identification of kinematic regions where non-perturbative contributions dominate and where experimental data from the Electron-Ion Collider or LHC would best constrain the non-perturbative components of TMDs.
