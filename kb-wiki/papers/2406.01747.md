# A universal reduced basis for the calibration of covariant energy density functionals

> The reduced basis method is used to construct a "universal" basis of Dirac orbitals that may be applicable throughout the nuclear chart to calibrate covariant energy density functionals. Relative to our earlier work using the non-relativistic Schrödinger equation, the Dirac equation adds an extra layer of complexity due to the existence of negative energy states. However, once this problem is mitigated, the resulting reduced basis is able to accurately and efficiently reproduce the high-fidelity model at a fraction of the computational cost. We are confident that the resulting reduced basis will serve as a foundational element in developing rapid and accurate emulators. In turn, these emulators will play a critical role in the Bayesian optimization of covariant energy density functionals.

# A universal reduced basis for the calibration of covariant energy density functionals

## Key claim

The authors construct a "universal" reduced basis of Dirac orbitals that can be applied across the nuclear chart for calibrating covariant energy density functionals, but only after mitigating the problem of negative energy states that arise from the Dirac equation. The reduced basis accurately reproduces high-fidelity model results at a fraction of the computational cost, provided one uses a two-fold prescription that filters ghost energies by selecting only the eigenstate with largest overlap with the most important element of the reduced basis.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation for spherically symmetric scalar and vector potentials approximated by Woods-Saxon functions fitted to self-consistent potentials from the FSUGarnet covariant energy density functional. They collect bound neutron orbitals from seven nuclei (16O, 40Ca, 48Ca, 68Ni, 90Zr, 132Sn, 208Pb), rescale the Dirac equation to a dimensionless form using an intrinsic distance scale proportional to A^(1/3), and apply Singular Value Decomposition to generate an optimal orthonormal reduced basis. The reduced basis is then used to diagonalize a low-dimensional Dirac Hamiltonian matrix, with the final eigenstate selected by requiring the largest overlap with the most important element of the reduced basis.

## Key numbers

- The reduced basis diagonalization uses a "tiny" 3x3 Dirac Hamiltonian matrix for each (n,κ) channel
- For all bound orbitals below the Fermi surface examined, the largest overlap with the most important element of the reduced basis exceeds 90%
- The emulator speeds up calculation of Dirac orbitals by nearly a factor of 150
- In the case of 90Zr, the full spectrum includes about 140 bound states, with only 15 having positive energy
- The reduced basis retains orbitals with singular values within 0.1% of the largest one (condition number of at least 10^-3)
- The number of basis states required when including negative energy states reached as high as 67 for some values of κ
- The optimal reduced basis covers nuclei from 16O to 302Og

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous non-relativistic reduced basis method (Anderson, 2022) from the Schrodinger equation to the Dirac equation, adding complexity due to negative energy states. It builds on eigenvector continuation (Frame, 2017) and prior reduced basis method applications in nuclear physics (Bonilla, 2022; Melendez, 2022). Unlike earlier work (Giuliani, 2022) that created a separate reduced basis for each nucleus and each Dirac orbital, this approach produces a single universal basis applicable across the nuclear chart. The resulting emulators will enable Bayesian optimization of covariant energy density functionals, which require thousands to millions of evaluations that are computationally intractable with high-fidelity models.
