# Quarkonium dissociation by anisotropy

> We compute the screening length for quarkonium mesons moving through an anisotropic, strongly coupled N=4 super Yang-Mills plasma by means of its gravity dual. We present the results for arbitrary velocities and orientations of the mesons, as well as for arbitrary values of the anisotropy. The anisotropic screening length can be larger or smaller than the isotropic one, and this depends on whether the comparison is made at equal temperatures or at equal entropy densities. For generic motion we find that: (i) mesons dissociate above a certain critical value of the anisotropy, even at zero temperature; (ii) there is a limiting velocity for mesons in the plasma, even at zero temperature; (iii) in the ultra-relativistic limit the screening length scales as $(1-v^2)^ε$ with ε=1/2, in contrast with the isotropic result ε=1/4.

## Key claim

The screening length for quarkonium mesons in an anisotropic, strongly coupled N=4 super Yang-Mills plasma can be either larger or smaller than the isotropic screening length, depending on whether the comparison is made at equal temperatures or at equal entropy densities; however, the authors caution that the sources of anisotropy in their system (an external axion field) differ from those in the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy ion collisions, so extrapolations to the real-world QGP must be interpreted with caution.

## Method

The authors use the gauge/string duality (holography) to compute the screening length for a quark-antiquark pair in an anisotropic, strongly coupled N=4 super Yang-Mills plasma, described by a type IIB supergravity solution with an axion linear in the spatial coordinate z. They model the quarkonium meson as a U-shaped string in the dual asymptotically AdS spacetime, with endpoints on the boundary, and determine the screening length by comparing the action of the bound string configuration to that of an unbound pair of straight strings descending to the horizon, for arbitrary velocities, orientations, and anisotropy values.

## Key numbers

* Static dipole in isotropic plasma: screening length L_iso(T) ≈ 0.24 / T (at fixed temperature).
* Transition velocity v_trans ≈ 0.45 between regimes where L_s < L_max and L_s = L_max in the isotropic case.
* For a/T = 12.2, the transition velocities for different dipole orientations are v_trans = 0.45, 0.29, 0.19, 0.11 for a/T = 0, 6.5, 43, 744 respectively (dipole oriented along transverse direction x, moving along anisotropic direction z).
* In the ultra-relativistic limit, the screening length scales as (1 - v^2)^ε with ε = 1/2, in contrast to the isotropic result ε = 1/4.
* The entropy density at large a/T scales as s = c_ent N_c^2 a^{1/3} T^{8/3}, with the transition between asymptotic behaviors at a/T ≈ 3.7.
* The ratio α = (4E + P_perp - P_L)/(3Ts) is well approximated by α ≈ 1 - 0.0036 (a/T)^2 - 0.000072 (a/T)^4 for 0 < a/T ≲ 20.

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of quarkonium dissociation in isotropic N=4 super Yang-Mills plasma (Liu, Rajagopal, Wiedemann 2007; Chernicoff, Garcia, Guijosa 2009) and in the same anisotropic plasma model for static dipoles (Giataganas 2012; Rebhan, Steineder 2012). The results differ from those obtained in a different anisotropic plasma model with a naked singularity (Singh 2010; Rebhan, Steineder 2012), suggesting some model dependence. The findings enable future comparisons with other strongly coupled anisotropic plasmas to identify which features of the screening length are robust and potentially relevant for the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy ion collisions.
