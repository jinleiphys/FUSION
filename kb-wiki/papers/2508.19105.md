# Dual-polarization structure and nuclear structure effect on $Λ$ polarization

> We report a novel manifestation of spin-vorticity interplay in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Using $^{16}$O+$^{197}$Au at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=7.7$ GeV as a test case, we show that the $Λ$ hyperon exhibits a clear dual-polarization structure, observed here in central $^{16}$O + $^{197}$Au collisions for the first time. The polarization is further highly sensitive to the intrinsic nuclear geometry: different $α$-cluster configurations of $^{16}$O, ranging from chain-like to tetrahedral, lead to distinct polarization patterns across centralities. In particular, the backward rapidity region and peripheral events display striking structure-dependent variations, including opposite angular distributions of local polarizations $P_x$ and $P_y$ compared with a spherical reference. These findings reveal that nuclear clustering leaves measurable imprints on hyperon spin alignment in relativistic collisions. Our results open a promising avenue for probing nuclear structure in short-lived systems and highlight a new spin-sensitive mechanism relevant for upcoming experiments at RHIC and future facilities.

## Key claim

The paper reports that in central \(^{16}\)O + \(^{197}\)Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7\) GeV, \(\Lambda\) hyperons exhibit a clear dual-polarization structure for the first time, and that this polarization is highly sensitive to the intrinsic nuclear geometry of the projectile, with different \(\alpha\)-cluster configurations of \(^{16}\)O (chain, kite, square, tetrahedral) producing distinct polarization patterns across centralities, particularly in the backward rapidity region and peripheral events.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model to simulate \(^{16}\)O + \(^{197}\)Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7\) GeV, introducing different \(\alpha\)-cluster configurations of the \(^{16}\)O projectile (spherical, chain, kite, square, and tetrahedral arrangements). The \(\Lambda\) spin polarization was calculated from the thermal vorticity tensor at freeze-out, using the mean spin vector formalism, with the velocity field and energy-momentum tensor computed from the particle distributions via a smearing function.

## Key numbers

- Global \(\Lambda\) polarization \(P_y\) reaches up to \(\sim 90\%\) at high centrality in \(^{16}\)O + \(^{197}\)Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 7.7\) GeV, with transverse momentum cut \(0.2 < p_T < 3.0\) GeV/\(c\).
- In the backward rapidity region (\(-2.0 < Y < 0.0\)), a clear hierarchy of polarization magnitude emerges at high centralities: tetrahedron \(>\) square \(>\) kite \(>\) sphere \(>\) chain.
- For peripheral collisions (90% centrality), the local polarization components \(P_x\) and \(P_y\) for tetrahedral and square configurations display angular distributions opposite to those found for the spherical reference case.

## Context

This work extends the study of spin-vorticity interplay in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, building on the global \(\Lambda\) polarization measurements by STAR (2017) and the proposal by Broniowski and Arriola (2014) that relativistic collisions could probe nuclear cluster structures. The results open a new avenue for probing nuclear clustering in short-lived systems using spin polarization as a sensitive observable, relevant for upcoming experiments at RHIC and future facilities.
