# Mean-field description of the nucleus-nucleus optical potential

> A new finite-range representation of the JLM effective nucleon-nucleon interaction is suggested based on the CDM3Y density dependent functional and M3Y-Paris interaction. The density dependence has been carefully adjusted at each energy so that the nucleon optical potential (OP) given by the new density dependent interaction in the Hartree-Fock calculation of nuclear matter closely matches the JLM nucleon OP given by the Brueckner-Hatree-Fock calculation. The new interaction has been used in the double-folding model to calculate the OP for the elastic $^{6}$Li,$^{6}$He,$^{12}$C + $^{12}$C scattering at different energies.

## Key claim

The authors construct a new complex density-dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction (CDJLM) that reproduces the JLM nucleon optical potential in nuclear matter, and they find that the mean-field absorption accounts for up to 70% of the total absorption in the carbon-12 plus carbon-12 system, though the imaginary folded potential still requires renormalization by a factor greater than unity at all considered energies.

## Method

The authors used the CDM3Y density-dependent functional and M3Y-Paris interaction to build a finite-range representation of the JLM effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. They adjusted the density dependence parameters iteratively at each energy so that the Hartree-Fock calculation of nuclear matter yields a nucleon optical potential matching the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock JLM results. This new CDJLM interaction was then applied in the double-folding model to compute the complex optical potential for elastic scattering of lithium-6, helium-6, and carbon-12 projectiles on carbon-12 targets at various energies.

## Key numbers

- The mean-field absorption accounts for up to 70% of the total absorption in the carbon-12 plus carbon-12 system.
- The imaginary folded potential for the carbon-12 plus carbon-12 system requires renormalization by a factor N_I greater than 1 at all considered energies, while the renormalization factor for the real folded potential remains close to unity.
- The nucleon optical potential U_0(E,rho) was compared at energies of 16.5 MeV and 35 MeV between the CDJLM interaction and the original JLM results.
- Elastic scattering of helium-6 and lithium-6 on carbon-12 was analyzed at 3 MeV per nucleon and 35 MeV per nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the JLM approach of Jeukenne, Lejeune, and Mahaux (1977) by providing a new finite-range density-dependent interaction suitable for double-folding model calculations of nucleus-nucleus optical potentials. The results enable a separation of mean-field absorption from the dynamic polarization potential arising from nonelastic channels, and the authors find that the breakup polarization potential for helium-6 is much weaker than for lithium-6, particularly at high energy, which is being further investigated. The approach builds on the CDM3Y functional of Khoa et al. (1997) and the M3Y-Paris interaction of Anantaraman et al. (1983).
