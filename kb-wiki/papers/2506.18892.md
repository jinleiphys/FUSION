# $g$-mode oscillations of dark matter admixed neutron stars

> We investigate $g$-mode oscillations in dark matter admixed neutron stars employing a relativistic mean field model to describe hadronic matter and a model for self-interacting fermionic dark matter motivated by the neutron decay anomaly. Following the construction of such admixed configurations, we derive the equilibrium and adiabatic speeds of sound therein, leading to a computation of the star's $g$-mode spectrum in the Cowling approximation. In particular, we explore the effect of dark matter self-interaction, the nucleon effective mass and dark matter fraction on the principal $g$-mode frequency, and its first overtone. We show that the effect on $g$-mode frequency depends predominantly on the dark matter fraction, and demonstrate an equation of state-independent constraint for the latter. Prospects of identifying the presence of dark matter in neutron stars using $g$-mode are discussed.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the effect of dark matter on g-mode frequencies depends predominantly on the dark matter fraction, and demonstrates an equation of state-independent constraint for that fraction, but the results are obtained within the Cowling approximation which neglects gravitational potential perturbations at the 5-10% level.

## Method
The authors construct dark matter admixed neutron star configurations using a relativistic mean field model for hadronic matter and a self-interacting fermionic dark matter model motivated by the neutron decay anomaly (n → χ + φ). They compute the equilibrium and adiabatic speeds of sound, then solve the fluid perturbation equations in the relativistic Cowling approximation to obtain the g-mode spectrum, varying the nucleon effective mass, dark matter self-interaction parameter G, and dark matter fraction.

## Key numbers
- Dark matter particle mass set to m_χ = 938.0 MeV, within the allowed range 937.993 < m_χ < 938.783 MeV
- Dark matter self-interaction parameter G values range from 6 fm² to 300 fm² depending on the nuclear EoS parameter set
- The Cowling approximation affects g-mode frequencies at the 5-10% level for stars around 2-2.25 M_⊙
- g-mode oscillation frequencies are of order O(100) Hz
- The fundamental g-mode studied has radial quantum number n = 1 and multipolarity l = 2

## Context
This work extends previous studies by the same authors on f-mode oscillations (Shirke 2024) and r-mode oscillations (Shirke 2023) in dark matter admixed neutron stars, and is the first study of g-modes in such systems. It builds on the formalism for core g-modes introduced by Reisenegger (1992, 1994) and Lai (1994, 1999), and follows the approach of Jaikumar (2021) who showed that g-mode frequency rises steeply with the onset of quarks. The results enable potential identification of dark matter in neutron stars through gravitational wave observations of g-mode oscillations.
