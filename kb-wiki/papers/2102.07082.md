# Heavy quark transport in an anisotropic hot QCD medium: Collisional and Radiative processes

> The impact of momentum anisotropy on the heavy quark transport coefficients due to collisional and radiative processes in the QCD medium has been studied within the ambit of kinetic theory. Anisotropic aspects (momentum) are incorporated into the heavy quark dynamics through the non-equilibrium momentum distribution function of quarks, antiquarks, and gluons. These non-equilibrium distribution functions that encode the physics of momentum anisotropy and turbulent chromo-fields have been obtained by solving the ensemble-averaged diffusive Vlasov-Boltzmann equation. The momentum dependence of heavy quark transport coefficients in the medium is seen to be sensitive to the strength of the anisotropy for both collisional and radiative processes. In addition, the collisional and radiative energy loss of the heavy quark in the anisotropic hot QCD medium have been analyzed. The effects of anisotropy on the drag and diffusion coefficients are observed to have a visible impact on the nuclear suppression factor both at the RHIC and LHC.

## Key claim

The paper claims that momentum anisotropy in the QGP medium, arising from turbulent chromo-fields and captured via non-equilibrium distribution functions, significantly modifies heavy quark drag and diffusion coefficients for both collisional and radiative processes, with the effect being more prominent in the radiative process than the collisional one, particularly at high momentum.

## Method

The authors solved the ensemble-averaged diffusive Vlasov-Boltzmann equation to obtain non-equilibrium momentum distribution functions for quarks, antiquarks, and gluons, encoding the effects of momentum anisotropy and turbulent chromo-fields. These distribution functions were then used within the Fokker-Planck approach to compute the heavy quark drag and diffusion coefficients for both elastic (two-body) collisions and inelastic (gluon bremsstrahlung) processes, with the radiative matrix element related to the collisional one via a dead-cone factor and soft-gluon approximation.

## Key numbers

*   Drag and diffusion coefficients were computed for LHC energy at a temperature of T = 480 MeV and for RHIC energy at T = 360 MeV.
*   For quantitative estimation of anisotropy effects, the jet quenching parameter was set to \(\hat{q}_k = 3.7 T^3\) with a thermalization time \(\tau = 0.6\) fm for LHC energy.
*   For RHIC energy, the jet quenching parameter was set to \(\hat{q}_k = 4.6 T^3\) with a thermalization time \(\tau = 0.9\) fm.
*   The collisional and radiative contributions to the drag coefficient were observed to increase with anisotropy for heavy quark momentum above \(p = 5\) GeV at LHC energies.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of heavy quark transport in a thermalized QGP medium (Svetitsky 1987, Mazumder 2013) by incorporating the effects of momentum anisotropy and anomalous transport due to Chromo-Weibel instability, following the formalism developed by Asakawa (2006) and Chandra (2015). It enables more realistic modeling of heavy quark dynamics and the nuclear suppression factor \(R_{AA}\) at RHIC and LHC by accounting for non-equilibrium medium effects that are expected in rapidly expanding heavy-ion collisions.
