# Heavy quarks in nucleus-nucleus collisions: from RHIC to LHC

> We present a study of the heavy-flavor dynamics in nucleus-nucleus collisions. The initial (hard) production of c and b quarks is taken from NLO pQCD predictions. The presence of a hot medium (a Quark Gluon Plasma described by hydrodynamics) affects the final spectra of open-charm (beauty) hadrons and their decay electrons with respect to what found in pp collisions. The propagation of c and b quarks in the plasma is based on a picture of multiple uncorrelated random collisions, described by a relativistic Langevin equation. A microscopic evaluation of the transport coefficients is provided within a pQCD approach (with proper resummation of medium effects). Results for the final spectra of heavy-flavor hadrons and decay-electrons are given, with particular emphasis on R_AA and v_2.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a Langevin-based model of heavy-quark propagation in a hydrodynamically expanding quark-gluon plasma, with transport coefficients evaluated from perturbative QCD (including Hard Thermal Loop resummation for soft scatterings), can reproduce the measured nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\) of heavy-flavor decay electrons at RHIC and LHC, but the model systematically underestimates the experimental \(v_2\) at RHIC, suggesting that non-perturbative effects (such as coalescence hadronization) may be needed.

## Method

The authors generate initial \(c\) and \(b\) quark pairs using the POWHEG code (NLO pQCD) with nuclear PDFs (EPS09) for nucleus-nucleus collisions, distribute them in the transverse plane according to the nuclear overlap function, and apply a \(k_T\)-broadening. The heavy-quark propagation in the expanding QGP (described by ideal or viscous hydrodynamics) is modeled via a relativistic Langevin equation, with transport coefficients \(\kappa_T(p)\) and \(\kappa_L(p)\) computed from a pQCD approach that separates hard collisions (kinetic pQCD) and soft collisions (Hard Thermal Loop resummation). Hadronization uses Peterson fragmentation functions, and hadrons are decayed into electrons with PYTHIA.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC (\(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV), the invariant differential cross section of heavy-flavor decay electrons from the model reproduces PHENIX data; at LHC (\(\sqrt{s}=5.5\) TeV), the cross section increases substantially and the spectrum hardens.
- The nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for heavy-flavor electrons at RHIC (0-92% centrality) and LHC (0-90% centrality) is shown for viscous hydrodynamics; the higher temperatures at LHC produce larger quenching, partially compensated by a stronger \(b\)-quark contribution.
- The centrality dependence of \(R_{AA}\) for heavy-flavor electrons at RHIC is reproduced by the model, using viscous hydrodynamics with \(\tau_0 = 1\) fm.
- The minimum-bias elliptic flow \(v_2\) for heavy-flavor electrons at RHIC is underestimated by the model compared to PHENIX data, even assuming very rapid thermalization; at LHC, results are shown for two choices of \(\tau_0\).

## Context

This work extends the Langevin-based approach of Beraudo et al. (2010) by applying it to both RHIC and LHC energies, using viscous hydrodynamics for the medium expansion. It contrasts with models based on medium-induced gluon radiation (Gyulassy, Wang; Armesto, Salgado, Wiedemann), resonant scattering (Rapp), and Boltzmann equation approaches (Bamps). The results enable future comparisons with LHC data on heavy-flavor hadronic decays, and the underestimation of \(v_2\) suggests that coalescence with medium quarks (Rapp) may be needed to explain the data.
