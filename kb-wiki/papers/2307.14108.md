# Cancellation of small-x divergences in the three-gluon-vertex Hamiltonian with canonical gluon mass

> The front form of Hamiltonian dynamics provides a framework within QCD in which interaction terms are invariant under 7 of 10 Poincaré transformations and the vacuum structure is simple. However, canonical expressions are divergent and must be regulated before attempting to define an eigenvalue problem. The renormalization group procedure for effective particles (RGPEP) provides a systematic way of renormalizing Hamiltonians and obtaining counterterms. One of its achievements is the description of asymptotic freedom with a running coupling defined as the coefficient of the three-gluon-vertex operators in the renormalized Hamiltonian. Yet, the results we obtain need a deeper understanding since the coefficient function shows a finite cutoff dependence, at least in the third-order terms of the perturbative expansion. In this work, we present an RGPEP computation of the three-gluon vertex with a different regularization scheme based on massive gluons. Our calculation shows that the three-gluon Hamiltonian interaction term has a finite limit as the gluon mass vanishes, but the finite function $h(x)$ that was obtained in previous calculations as a consequence of the finite dependence on the regularization is different. This result indicates a need for understanding how to eliminate finite regularization effects from Hamiltonians for effective quarks and gluons in QCD. Nevertheless, it is remarkable that all terms depending on the gluon mass cancel out in the limit of vanishing gluon mass in a non trivial way, even when each term individually diverges in such limit.

## Key claim

The RGPEP computation of the three-gluon vertex using a massive-gluon regularization scheme shows that the three-gluon Hamiltonian interaction term has a finite limit as the gluon mass vanishes, but the finite function $h(x)$ obtained in previous calculations differs, indicating that finite regularization effects in Hamiltonians for effective quarks and gluons in QCD are not yet fully understood.

## Method

The authors introduce an infinitesimal canonical gluon mass $m_g$ to regulate small-$x$ divergences in the front-form QCD Hamiltonian, then solve the RGPEP equation perturbatively to third order in the coupling constant for the three-gluon vertex in SU(3) Yang-Mills theory. The running coupling is identified as the coefficient of the canonical color, spin, and momentum factor $Y_{123}$ in the limit of small relative transverse momentum $\kappa_{12} \approx 0$, and the calculation is performed by integrating over longitudinal momentum fractions $x$ and transverse momenta $\kappa^\perp$ in three regions, with the gluon mass acting as a regulator for small-$x$ singularities.

## Key numbers

* The self-energy result is $\mu_t^2 = -\sqrt{\frac{\pi}{2}}\frac{1}{4\sqrt{t}}\left[\log\left(t m_g^4\right) + \log\left(8 e^{\gamma_E}\right) + \frac{23}{6}\right] + \mu_{0,m_g}^2$, where $\gamma_E$ is the Euler-Mascheroni constant, showing that $\mu_t^2 \to \infty$ when $m_g \to 0$.
* The running coupling is defined as $g_t = g_0 + g_0^3\left(c_t(x_0) - c_{t_0}(x_0)\right) + \mathcal{O}(g_0^4)$, where $c_t(x_1)$ is the coefficient function extracted from the third-order vertex in the limit $\kappa_{12} \approx 0$.
* The integration over $x$ is divided into three regions: I ($x_1, x_1 + \bar{m}_g$), II ($x_1 + \bar{m}_g, 1 - \bar{m}_g$), and III ($1 - \bar{m}_g, \bar{m}_g$), where $\bar{m}_g$ is a dimensionless parameter depending on $m_g$, with regions I and III producing divergent integrals and region II being finite.

## Context

This work extends previous RGPEP calculations of the three-gluon vertex by Glazek (2001) and Gomez-Rocha (2015), which used three different regularization schemes and found a finite dependence on the choice of regulating function. The new massive-gluon regularization, previously applied to other RGPEP problems by Glazek (2021) and Glazek (2019), produces a different finite function $h(x)$ than earlier results, highlighting the need to understand how to eliminate finite regularization effects. The approach differs from studies of dynamical gluon-mass generation by Cornwall (1981), Aguilar (2016), and Binosi (2017), and from the Faddeev-Popov Lagrangian with a tree-level mass term used by Pelaez (2021) to reproduce lattice correlation functions.
