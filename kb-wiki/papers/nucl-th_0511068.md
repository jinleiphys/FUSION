# Extracting the Weinberg angle at intermediate energies

> A recent experiment by the NuTeV collaboration resulted in a surprisingly high value for the weak mixing angle $\sin^2 θ_W$. The Paschos-Wolfenstein relation, relating neutrino cross sections to the Weinberg angle, is of pivotal importance in the NuTeV analysis. In this work, we investigate the sensitivity of the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation to nuclear structure aspects at neutrino energies in the few GeV range. Neutrino-nucleus cross sections are calculated for $^{16}$O and $^{56}$Fe target nuclei within a relativistic quasi-elastic nucleon-knockout model.

## Key claim

The Paschos-Wolfenstein relation, when calculated using quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus cross sections on an isoscalar oxygen target at intermediate energies (few GeV), yields a Weinberg angle consistent with the standard value to within about 0.5%, in contrast to the anomalously high value reported by the NuTeV collaboration from deep inelastic scattering on iron.

## Method

The authors calculate quasi-elastic (anti)neutrino-nucleus cross sections for 16O and 56Fe targets using a fully relativistic nucleon-knockout model within the relativistic plane-wave impulse approximation (RPWIA). Final-state interactions are optionally included via a relativistic Glauber approach based on nucleon-nucleon scattering data. The Paschos-Wolfenstein relation is then evaluated from the ratio of neutral-current to charged-current cross section differences, rather than from deep inelastic scattering cross sections as in the original NuTeV analysis.

## Key numbers

- The standard value of the weak mixing angle is given as sin²θ_W = 0.2227 ± 0.0004, while the NuTeV result is sin²θ_W = 0.22773 ± 0.00135 (stat) ± 0.00093 (syst), a deviation of about 3 standard deviations.
- For 16O at an incoming neutrino energy of 500 MeV, the calculated Paschos-Wolfenstein relation agrees with the standard sin²θ_W value to within approximately 0.5%.
- At an incoming energy of 2 GeV, the RPWIA results for 16O reach perfect agreement with the standard Paschos-Wolfenstein value.
- For 56Fe at an incoming neutrino energy of 1 GeV, the neutron excess causes a large deviation from the standard PW value, lowering it drastically compared to the isoscalar 16O case.

## Context

This work extends the investigation of the NuTeV anomaly (Zeller, 2002) by examining nuclear structure effects on the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation at intermediate energies, where quasi-elastic scattering dominates rather than deep inelastic scattering. The study contrasts with prior work by Kulagin (2004) that addressed neutron excess corrections in the DIS regime. The results enable future studies of additional nuclear effects on the Paschos-Wolfenstein relation, including the influence of nucleon strangeness content, as noted by the authors in ongoing work (Praet, 2005).
