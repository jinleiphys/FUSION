# Relativistic calculation of the pion loop correlation energy in nuclear matter in a theory including confinement

> We present a relativistic calculation of the saturation properties of nuclear matter which contains the correlation energy. Pion loops are incorporated on top of a relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approach based on a chiral theory. It includes the effect of nucleon structure through its response to the background chiral invariant scalar field. All the parameters which enter the RHF calculation are fixed or strongly constrained by hadron phenomenology or lattice data. The new input for the correlation energy is the Landau-Migdal parameter g? governing the short-range part of the spin-isospin interaction. We find that the inclusion of the correlation energy improves the description of the saturation properties of nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The inclusion of pion loop correlation energy, calculated fully relativistically on top of a relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approach that already incorporates nucleon structure effects from confinement, improves the description of nuclear matter saturation properties, provided the Landau-Migdal parameter \(g'\) governing short-range spin-isospin correlations is treated as an input.

## Method

The authors start from a chiral Lagrangian with scalar, omega, rho, and pion exchanges, where the nucleon mass depends on the scalar field \(s\) via a scalar response parameter \(\kappa_{NS}\) constrained by lattice data. They compute the pion loop correlation energy using the charging formula method, which involves integrating over a coupling constant \(\lambda\) that weights the interaction vertices. The polarization propagators in the axial-vector and vector-isovector channels are calculated in a relativistic RPA scheme using a projector technique to decompose the tensors into longitudinal, transverse, and mixed channels, and the resulting correlation energy is added to the RHF energy density.

## Key numbers

- The scalar response parameter \(C = (f_\pi^2 / 2 M_N) \kappa_{NS} \simeq 1.25\), governing the \(s^2\) contribution to the in-medium nucleon mass.
- The scalar mass parameter \(m_\sigma = 800\) MeV, taken from lattice data analysis.
- The dipole form factor cutoff \(\Lambda = 0.98\) GeV, chosen so that the pion contribution to the free nucleon sigma term is \(\sigma_N^{(\pi)} = 21.5\) MeV.
- The vector dominance model value for the rho meson coupling constant \(g_\rho = 2.65\) and tensor coupling \(\kappa_\rho = 3.7\).
- The omega meson tensor coupling \(\kappa_\omega = 0.13\) is neglected as very small.
- The rho-R channel contribution to the correlation energy at normal nuclear matter density is estimated as \(-0.65\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic Hartree-Fock calculation (Massot and Chanfray, 2008) by adding a fully relativistic treatment of the pion loop correlation energy, which had previously been incorporated only non-relativistically (Chanfray and Ericson, 2007). The approach builds on a chiral theory where the scalar field is identified with the sigma meson of Walecka-type models, and where nucleon structure effects from confinement (inspired by the QMC model of Guichon, 1988) generate three-body repulsive forces that restore nuclear matter saturation, counteracting the attractive three-body forces from chiral tadpoles that otherwise destroy saturation (Koch and Miller, 1974; Birse and Teller, 2001). The results enable a more complete description of nuclear matter saturation and asymmetry properties within a framework where parameters are fixed by hadron phenomenology and lattice data.
