# Interaction effects in ${\bm K^+}$ photoproduction on the deuteron

> Kaon photoproduction on the deuteron is studied with respect to a specific two-body contribution, namely a pion mediated production process, besides other final state interaction contributions from kaon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon scattering. In this process, a pion is first photoproduced on one nucleon and then interacts with the spectator nucleon in a strangeness exchange reaction leading to a kaon and a hyperon. A sizeable effect from this pion mediated contribution is found, considerably larger than the previously studied hyperon-nucleon rescattering, whereas kaon-nucleon rescattering is much less important. Besides total and semi-inclusive differential cross sections, tensor target asymmetries are studied with respect to the influence of such interaction effects.

## Key claim

The pion-mediated production process (π→K), in which a photon first produces a pion on one nucleon and the pion then undergoes a strangeness-exchange reaction with the spectator nucleon, gives a sizeable contribution to kaon photoproduction on the deuteron that is considerably larger than the previously studied hyperon-nucleon rescattering, while kaon-nucleon rescattering is much less important.

## Method

The authors computed kaon photoproduction on the deuteron including three types of final-state interactions: hyperon-nucleon rescattering using the Nijmegen YN potential, kaon-nucleon rescattering using a rank-one separable potential fitted to SAID phase shifts, and the pion-mediated process using a rank-one separable potential for the πN→KY transition with S-waves only. The elementary kaon photoproduction operator on the nucleon was taken from the isobar model of Lee et al. (2001), and pion photoproduction was described by the MAID model. The full three-body final state was treated in the deuteron rest frame, and the transition matrix was evaluated with partial-wave expansions and solutions of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the two-body subsystems.

## Key numbers

- The total cross section for pion photoproduction on the nucleon is about 25 μb around Eγ = 1.6 GeV, which is approximately 10 times larger than the kaon photoproduction cross section at the same energies.
- The threshold lab energy for kaon photoproduction on the deuteron is given by pγ^(thr) = [(m_YN + m_K)^2 - m_d^2] / (2m_d).
- For the kaon-nucleon separable potential, the S01 partial wave has parameters A = 617.56 MeV and B = 431.84 MeV; the S11 partial wave has A = 763.32 MeV and B = 1049.9 MeV.
- For the πN→KY transition, the S11 πN channel has A = 1039.6 MeV and B = 559.03 MeV, while the S11 KY channel has A = 179.39 MeV and B = 148.80 MeV.
- The D13 partial wave in kaon-nucleon scattering shows some effect above kaon momenta pK ≈ 1.6 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends prior calculations by Yamamura et al. (1999), who studied hyperon-nucleon final-state interactions in kaon photoproduction on the deuteron using the Nijmegen YN potential, and by Li et al. (1992), who calculated the reactions in impulse approximation only. The authors note that a very recent study by Maxwell (2004) included the pion-mediated process in lowest order using a diagrammatic approach but did not include final-state correlations like hyperon-nucleon and kaon-nucleon rescattering. The present work enables quantitative comparison with future experimental data from facilities such as MAMI, ELSA, and CEBAF, and could help constrain the hyperon-nucleon interaction and the elementary neutron amplitudes.
