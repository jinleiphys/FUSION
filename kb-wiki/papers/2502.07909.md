# Inelastic neutrino-nucleus scattering off $^{203/205}$Tl in terms of the nuclear recoil energy using a hybrid nuclear model

> Nuclear structure calculations in the context of a novel hybrid nuclear model, combining the nuclear shell model and the microscopic quasiparticle-phonon model are presented. The predictivity of the hybrid model is tested by computing inelastic neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections off the stable thallium isotopes. The cross sections are presented in terms of the incoming neutrino energy, taking also into account the effect of nuclear recoil energy. Also reported are the expected event rates assuming neutrinos emerging from pion-decay at rest and the diffuse supernova neutrino background. Regarding solar neutrino rates, new results are presented in the context of the hybrid model and compared with previously reported results based solely on nuclear shell model calculations, demonstrating the improved accuracy of the adopted hybrid model at higher neutrino energies.

## Key claim

The hybrid nuclear model, combining the nuclear shell model (NSM) for states below 3 MeV and the microscopic quasiparticle-phonon model (MQPM) for states above this threshold, yields improved accuracy for inelastic neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections at higher neutrino energies compared to pure NSM calculations, but the improvement is conditional on the neutrino energy being high enough to excite states above the 3 MeV threshold.

## Method

The authors computed inelastic neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections for stable thallium isotopes 203Tl and 205Tl using the Donnelly-Walecka formalism. Nuclear structure was described by a hybrid model: NSM states (using the jj56pn model space with khhe interaction in NuShellX@MSU) were used for excitation energies up to 3 MeV, and MQPM states (built on even-even reference nuclei 204/206Pb with a Coulomb-corrected Woods-Saxon single-particle basis) were used above 3 MeV. The cross sections were expressed as functions of both incoming neutrino energy and nuclear recoil energy, and event rates were computed for solar neutrinos, stopped-pion neutrinos, and diffuse supernova neutrino background (DSNB) neutrinos.

## Key numbers

- For 203Tl at E_nu = 5.0 MeV, the neutrino scattering cross section is 5.099 x 10^(-44) cm^2; at 100.0 MeV, it is 1.503 x 10^(-39) cm^2 (Table 1 format: B x 10^p x 10^(-44) cm^2).
- For 205Tl at E_nu = 5.0 MeV, the neutrino scattering cross section is 5.599 x 10^(-44) cm^2; at 100.0 MeV, it is 1.492 x 10^(-39) cm^2.
- At around T = 40 keV nuclear recoil energy, the inelastic cross section dominates over the coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEvNS) cross section for both isotopes.
- The folded stopped-pion neutrino cross sections for 203Tl are 1.306 x 10^(-40) cm^2 (nu_e), 0.855 x 10^(-40) cm^2 (nu_mu), and 1.502 x 10^(-40) cm^2 (anti-nu_mu); for 205Tl they are 1.326, 0.863, and 1.512 x 10^(-40) cm^2 respectively.
- At 9 MeV, the hybrid model results are over 1.5 times larger than pure NSM results for 203Tl and nearly 2 times larger for 205Tl; at 20 MeV the results differ by an order of magnitude.
- Over 50% of the contributions to the folded 8B solar-neutrino scattering cross section off 205Tl come from states centered at around 7.4 MeV with spin-parities 1/2+ or 3/2+.
- For DSNB neutrinos, the inelastic channel for the nu_x component dominates over CEvNS for T > 20 keV.

## Context

This work extends the formalism introduced by Papoulias (2024) for computing inelastic neutrino-nucleus scattering cross sections in terms of nuclear recoil energy, now applying a hybrid NSM-MQPM approach to thallium isotopes. The hybrid model addresses limitations of pure NSM calculations (which cannot describe high-lying giant resonances) and pure MQPM calculations (which have poorer accuracy for low-energy states), following similar hybrid approaches explored by Kolbe (1999), Juodagalvis (2004), and Dzhioev (2016) that combined NSM with random-phase approximation. The results enable more realistic simulations for dark matter direct detection experiments (such as COSINE-100, DAMA, PICO-LON, SABRE) where thallium is present as a dopant, and demonstrate that inelastic scattering from DSNB neutrinos can be competitive with CEvNS, requiring accurate treatment for neutrino floor calculations in experiments like ARGO and DARWIN.
