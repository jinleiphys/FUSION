# Impact of temperature dependence of the energy loss on jet quenching observables

> The quenching of jets (particles with $p_T>>T, Λ_{QCD}$) in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions has been one of the main prediction and discovery at RHIC. We have studied, by a simple jet quenching modeling, the correlation between different observables like the nuclear modification factor $\Rapt$, the elliptic flow $v_2$ and the ratio of quark to gluon suppression $R_{AA}(quark)/R_{AA}(gluon)$. We show that the relation among these observables is strongly affected by the temperature dependence of the energy loss. In particular the large $v_2$ and and the nearly equal $\Rapt$ of quarks and gluons can be accounted for only if the energy loss occurs mainly around the temperature $T_c$ and the flavour conversion is significant.Finally we point out that the efficency in the conversion of the space eccentricity into the momentum one ($v_2$) results to be quite smaller respect to the one coming from elastic scatterings in a fluid with a viscosity to entropy density ratio $4πη/s=1$.

# Impact of temperature dependence of the energy loss on jet quenching observables

## Key claim
The correlation between the nuclear modification factor, elliptic flow, and the ratio of quark to gluon suppression is strongly affected by the temperature dependence of the energy loss; the large elliptic flow and nearly equal suppression of quarks and gluons can only be accounted for if the energy loss occurs mainly around the critical temperature Tc and flavor conversion is significant, though the temperature dependence considered in this modeling including only path-length energy loss appears to be too extreme.

## Method
The authors model jet quenching in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions using a Glauber density profile for the bulk, next-to-leading order pQCD parton distributions, and AKK fragmentation functions for hadronization. The energy loss is implemented via the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) formula at first order in opacity expansion, with various temperature-dependent schemes for the strong coupling and energy loss rate, including cases where quenching is concentrated near Tc or at high temperature. The model also incorporates quark-to-gluon conversion via two-body scatterings with an enhancement factor Kc = 4-6, and a lattice QCD equation of state for the expansion cooling.

## Key numbers
- The nuclear modification factor RAA for central Au+Au collisions at 200 AGeV is fixed to approximately 0.2.
- The standard GLV with constant coupling αs = 0.27 gives a ratio RAA(quark)/RAA(gluon) around 1.8, deviating from the naive Casimir expectation of 9/4 = 2.25.
- When energy loss is dominant near Tc, RAA(quark)/RAA(gluon) increases up to about 2.2; when dominant at high temperature, the ratio becomes as small as 1.5.
- The efficiency of converting spatial eccentricity into elliptic flow v2/ε is at maximum ≤ 0.25 for the path-length mechanism studied, compared to about a factor of two larger from elastic scatterings in a fluid with viscosity to entropy density ratio 4πη/s = 1.
- The lattice QCD equation of state fit uses parameters a = 0.15 and n = 1.89 in the relation T/T0 = (ρ/ρ0)^β(T) with β(T) = 1/3 - a(Tc/T)^n.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of jet quenching puzzles at RHIC, specifically the "azimuthal puzzle" (large elliptic flow v2) and the "flavor puzzle" (protons less suppressed than pions), which were previously noted in experimental data from PHENIX. The authors build on the delayed energy loss scenario proposed by Pantuev (2005) and the core-corona effect discussed by Becattini (2009), while incorporating quark-to-gluon conversion mechanisms from Liu et al. (2006, 2008). The study enables future quantitative analyses with more sophisticated models including energy loss fluctuations, elastic energy loss, and mass-dependent formation times, as well as predictions for LHC energies.
