# Baryon number and strangeness: signals of a deconfined antecedent

> The correlation between baryon number and strangeness is used to discern the nature of the deconfined matter produced at vanishing chemical potential in high-energy nuclear collisions at the BNL RHIC. Comparisons of results of various phenomenological models with correlations extracted from lattice QCD calculations suggest that a quasi-particle picture applies. At finite baryon densities, such as those encountered at the CERN SPS, it is demonstrated that the presence of a first-order phase transition and the accompanying development of spinodal decomposition would significantly enhance the number of strangeness carriers and the associated fluctuations.

## Key claim

The correlation coefficient \(C_{BS}\) between baryon number and strangeness can distinguish between different degrees of freedom in deconfined matter produced in heavy-ion collisions, but the interpretation depends on whether the system is at vanishing chemical potential (RHIC) or finite chemical potential (SPS), and the quasi-particle picture is favored only at RHIC where lattice QCD results are available.

## Method

The authors compute the correlation coefficient \(C_{BS} = -3 \langle \delta B \delta S \rangle / \langle \delta S^2 \rangle\) from event-by-event fluctuations of baryon number and strangeness. For RHIC conditions at vanishing chemical potential, they compare values from a hadron resonance gas model, a quasi-particle plasma, a bound-state model (Shuryak-Zahed), lattice QCD susceptibilities, and Monte Carlo event generators (HIJING and JETSET). For SPS conditions at finite baryon chemical potential, they model a first-order phase transition with spinodal decomposition, where the system breaks into blobs that trap net strangeness, and compute the resulting kaon multiplicities and variances using canonical partition functions for strange hadrons.

## Key numbers

- For a hadron resonance gas at \(T = 170\) MeV and \(\mu_B = 0\), \(C_{BS} = 0.66\) (including hadrons up to \(\Omega^-\)).
- From lattice QCD at \(T = 1.5 T_c\) in the quenched approximation: \(\chi_{ss}/T^2 = 0.53(1)\) and \(\chi_{us} + \chi_{ds} = 0.00(3)\), yielding \(C_{BS} \simeq 1\).
- For the Shuryak-Zahed bound-state model at \(T = 1.5 T_c\), \(C_{BS} = 0.62\).
- From HIJING simulations for Au-Au at \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) AGeV, \(C_{BS} \approx 0.45\) nearly independent of rapidity acceptance up to \(\pm 2\) units.
- The lattice QCD crossover temperature at vanishing baryon density is 170 MeV.
- For SPS energies, freeze-out parameters are \(T \simeq 165-160\) MeV and \(\mu_B \simeq 240-300\) MeV for \(\sqrt{s} = 8-17\) GeV.
- The plasma blob volume used in the strangeness-trapping calculation is \(V_q = 50\) fm\(^3\), with an expansion factor \(\chi \sim 1.5-4\).
- The enhancement of kaon variances from spinodal decomposition exceeds both grand-canonical and restricted-canonical (only \(S_0=0\)) scenarios, especially at larger \(\mu_B\).

## Context

This work extends the use of conserved-charge correlations as probes of deconfined matter, building on lattice QCD calculations of susceptibilities by Gavai (2002) and the freeze-out parameterization of Becattini (2000). At RHIC, the result that \(C_{BS} \approx 1\) from lattice QCD favors a quasi-particle picture over the bound-state model of Shuryak and Zahed (2004), which gives \(C_{BS} = 0.62\). At SPS energies, the predicted enhancement of strangeness fluctuations from spinodal decomposition during a first-order phase transition offers a possible explanation for the observed enhancement of the \(K\)-to-\(\pi\) ratio around 20-30 AGeV beam energy reported by the NA49 collaboration. The work enables experimental measurements of \(C_{BS}\) at RHIC to discriminate between competing models of the quark-gluon plasma degrees of freedom, and motivates searches for enhanced strangeness fluctuations at SPS as signals of a first-order phase transition.
