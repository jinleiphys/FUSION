# Solar proton burning, photon and anti-neutrino disintegration of the deuteron in the relativistic field theory model of the deuteron

> The relativistic field theory model of the deuteron (RFMD) is applied to the calculation of the astrophysical factor S_{pp}(0) for the process of the solar proton burning p + p -> D + e^+ + ν_e and the cross sections for the disintegration of the deuteron by photons gamma + D -> n + p and anti-neutrinos \barν_e + D -> e^+ + n + n. Our theoretical value of the astrophysical factor S_{pp}(0) = 4.02 x 10^{-25} MeV b agrees with the classical result obtained by Bahcall and Kamionkowski S_{pp}(0) = 3.89 x 10^{-25} MeV b in the potential model approach (PMA). The cross sections for the disintegration of the deuteron by photons and anti-neutrinos calculated near thresholds are in good agreement with the PMA. An extrapolation of the cross sections for energies far from thresholds is suggested and related to the inclusion of form factors describing spatial smearing of the deuteron and the NN system. The extrapolated cross section for the disintegration of the deuteron by anti-neutrinos agrees with that calculated in the PMA in the anti-neutrino energy region from threshold up to 10 MeV. The extrapolated cross section averaged over the reactor anti-neutrino energy spectrum is obtained in agreement with the experimental data. It is shown that the RFMD enables to describe elastic low-energy NN scattering in accordance with low-energy nuclear phenomenology.

## Key claim

The relativistic field theory model of the deuteron (RFMD) yields an astrophysical factor S_pp(0) = 4.02 x 10^{-25} MeV b for solar proton burning, which agrees with the potential model approach (PMA) result of 3.89 x 10^{-25} MeV b obtained by Bahcall and Kamionkowski, despite the RFMD using completely different dynamics for strong low-energy nuclear interactions based on one-nucleon loop exchanges rather than phenomenological NN potentials.

## Method

The authors applied the RFMD, where the deuteron couples to nucleons through one-nucleon loop diagrams, using an effective local four-nucleon interaction with coupling constant G_piNN = 3.27 x 10^{-3} MeV^{-2} derived from one-pion exchange and the np scattering length a_np = -23.748 fm. They calculated the amplitude for solar proton burning via triangle one-nucleon loop diagrams with AAV (axial-axial-vector) and APV (axial-pseudoscalar-vector) vertices, finding the APV contribution divergent and negligible compared to the AAV contribution. For deuteron disintegration by photons and anti-neutrinos, they computed cross sections near threshold and extrapolated to higher energies by including form factors describing spatial smearing of the deuteron and the NN system.

## Key numbers

- Astrophysical factor S_pp(0) = 4.02 x 10^{-25} MeV b for the reaction p + p -> D + e^+ + nu_e, compared to 3.89 x 10^{-25} MeV b from Bahcall and Kamionkowski in the PMA
- Effective coupling constant G_piNN = 3.27 x 10^{-3} MeV^{-2}, derived from g_piNN = 13.4, M_pi = 135 MeV, M_N = 940 MeV, and a_np = -23.748 fm
- Cross section normalization sigma_0 = 5.9 x 10^{-27} cm^2 for photomagnetic disintegration of the deuteron near threshold in RFMD, compared to 6.3 x 10^{-27} cm^2 in PMA
- Reaction rate for neutron-proton radiative capture v*sigma_np(k) = 2.0 x 10^{-30} cm^2 in RFMD, compared to 2.2 x 10^{-30} cm^2 in PMA
- Cross section normalization sigma_0 = (4.53 +/- 0.86) x 10^{-43} cm^2 for anti-neutrino disintegration of the deuteron, compared to (4.68 +/- 1.14) x 10^{-43} cm^2 in PMA
- Averaged cross section over reactor anti-neutrino spectrum: <sigma> = (2.10 +/- 0.40) x 10^{-45} cm^2 per anti-neutrino per fission, compared to experimental values of (1.5 +/- 0.4) x 10^{-45} (Reines et al. 1976), (0.9 +/- 0.4) x 10^{-45} (Reines et al. 1980), and (1.84 +/- 0.04) x 10^{-45} cm^2 per anti-neutrino per fission (Russian groups)
- Anti-neutrino energy threshold E_th = 4.029 MeV for the reaction anti-nu_e + D -> e^+ + n + n
- Binding energy of deuteron epsilon_D = 2.225 MeV; deuteron radius r_D = 4.315 fm; nn scattering length a_nn = -17 fm

## Context

This work extends the RFMD previously applied to neutron-proton radiative capture (Ivanov et al. 1995-1997) to the processes of solar proton burning and deuteron disintegration by photons and anti-neutrinos, correcting earlier erroneous results for S_pp(0). The RFMD results agree with the potential model approach (PMA) of Bahcall and Kamionkowski and with the effective field theory (EFT) approach of Park et al. (1998), despite using fundamentally different dynamics based on one-nucleon loop anomalies rather than phenomenological NN potentials like the Argonne v_18. The agreement between the calculated averaged cross section for anti-neutrino disintegration and experimental data from Reines et al. (1976, 1980) and Russian groups confirms the RFMD value for S_pp(0), and the approach enables future incorporation of chiral perturbation theory for fine effects such as exchange-current contributions to neutron-proton radiative capture.
