# Multiple Critical Points in Effective Quark Models

> We consider the two flavor version of the Linear Sigma Model as well as of the Nambu Jona-Lasinio model, at finite temperature and quark chemical potential, beyond the Mean Field Approximation. Using parameter values for the pion and quark current masses which weakly break chiral symmetry we show that both models can present more than one critical end point. In particular, we explicitly show that the appearance of a new critical point associated with a first order line at high temperature and low densities could help to conciliate some lattice results with model predictions. Using different techniques, we perform an extensive thermodynamical analysis to understand the physical nature of the different critical points. For both models, our results suggest that the new first order line which starts at vanishing chemical potential has a more chiral character than the usual line which displays a character more reminiscent of a liquid-gas phase transition.

## Key claim

The paper shows that both the Linear Sigma Model and the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model, when treated beyond the mean-field approximation with small but finite pion and quark current masses, can exhibit more than one critical endpoint in the temperature–chemical potential plane; the new first-order line at high temperature and low chemical potential has a more chiral character than the usual line, which resembles a liquid-gas phase transition.

## Method

The authors treat the two-flavor Linear Sigma Model (LσM) by integrating out the quark degrees of freedom and then linearizing the mesonic action in a Hartree approximation that sums daisy and superdaisy diagrams, solving self-consistently for the condensate and the sigma and pion masses. For the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, they go beyond the mean-field approximation using the Optimized Perturbation Theory (OPT), which incorporates 1/Nc corrections that introduce an effective repulsive vector interaction. The phase diagrams are mapped by tracking the appearance of multiple solutions for the condensate as a function of temperature at fixed chemical potential, identifying first-order lines and critical endpoints.

## Key numbers

* For the LσM with vacuum pion mass mπ0 = 20 MeV, a first-order transition occurs at T ∼ 140 MeV and μ = 0.
* In the LσM, two critical points appear for vacuum pion masses mπ0 ≲ 50 MeV; for mπ0 = 35 MeV, the first-order line bifurcates into two critical points separated by ∼ 1 MeV in temperature.
* The LσM parameters used are: fπ = 92.4 MeV, mσ0 = 700 MeV, mq0 = 313 MeV, with mπ0 varied from 10 MeV to 140 MeV.
* In the NJL model, the OPT brings 1/Nc corrections to the pressure proportional to −G/(2Nf Nc)(2ρq2 − ρs2), where G is the NJL coupling, Nc = 3, and Nf = 2.

## Context

This work extends the findings of Bowman and Kapusta (2009) who first showed that the LσM with thermal mesonic fluctuations can produce two critical points for small pion masses, without requiring a vector interaction. It also relates to the suggestion by Fukushima (2008) that a repulsive vector coupling in the NJL model could cause an initial shrinkage of the first-order region at small chemical potential, potentially reconciling lattice QCD results with model predictions. The results enable further study of the QCD phase diagram’s rich structure, including the possibility of multiple critical points analogous to those seen in metamagnetic systems.
