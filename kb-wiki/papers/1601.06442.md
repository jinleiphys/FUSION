# Exotic neutron-rich medium-mass nuclei with realistic nuclear forces

> We present the first application of the newly developed EKK theory of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction to shell-model studies of exotic nuclei, including those where conventional approaches with fitted interactions encounter difficulties. This EKK theory enables us to derive the interaction suitable for several major shells ($sd$+$pf$ in this work). By using such an effective interaction obtained from the Entem-Machleidt QCD-based $χ\mathrm{N}^3\mathrm{LO}$ interaction and the Fujita-Miyazawa three-body force, the energies, E2 properties and spectroscopic factors of low-lying states of neutron-rich Ne, Mg and Si isotopes are nicely described, as the first shell-model description of the "island of inversion" without fit of the interaction. The long-standing question as to how particle-hole excitations occur across the $sd$-$pf$ magic gap is clarified with distinct differences from the conventional approaches. The shell evolution is shown to appear similarly to earlier studies.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first shell-model description of the "island of inversion" that uses an effective interaction derived entirely from realistic nuclear forces (the Entem-Machleidt chiral N3LO two-body interaction plus the Fujita-Miyazawa three-body force) without any fitting of the two-body matrix elements, though the single-particle energies are still fitted to selected observables.

## Method

The authors apply the Extended Kuo-Krenciglowa (EKK) method to derive an effective nucleon-nucleon interaction for a model space spanning both the sd and pf major shells, starting from a chiral N3LO interaction renormalized via the Vlowk approach with a cutoff of 2.0 fm^-1. Three-nucleon forces are included through the Fujita-Miyazawa term transformed into a medium-dependent two-body interaction. The Q-box is calculated up to third order in many-body perturbation theory with intermediate excitations up to 17 hbar-omega, and eigenvalues are obtained via Lanczos diagonalization or Monte Carlo Shell Model calculations including up to 8-particle-8-hole excitations.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energies of Ne, Mg, and Si isotopes are reproduced when three-nucleon force effects are included; without 3NF, neutron orbits are bound too deeply, yielding excessive binding energies
- The 2+_1 level drops by approximately 1 MeV from N=16 to 18 in the Ne chain, while a similar change occurs from N=18 to 20 in the Mg chain
- B(E2: 0_1+ to 2_1+) values are calculated with effective charges (e_p, e_n) = (1.25, 0.25)e
- For Mg-32, the average number of particle-hole excitations in the ground state is about 3.5, compared to the conventional 2p2h picture of Warburton-Brown-Becker (1990)
- Spectroscopic factors for one-neutron removal from Mg-32 ground state to 3/2-_1 and 7/2-_1 states are calculated as 0.77 and 1.41, compared to experimental values 0.59(11) and 1.19(36) after applying the usual quenching factor of approximately 0.7
- The harmonic oscillator parameter used is hbar-omega = 12.10 MeV for A=28

## Context

This work extends the EKK method (Takayanagi 2011, Tsunoda 2014) from formal development to actual application in neutron-rich medium-mass nuclei, providing the first microscopic shell-model description of the island of inversion without fitted two-body matrix elements. The results show definite differences from conventional approaches such as the sdpf-m interaction (PhysRevC.60.054315) and the sdpf-U-mix interaction (Caurier 2013), particularly in the pattern of particle-hole excitations across the sd-pf magic gap, while the shell evolution is shown to appear similarly to earlier studies using the VMU interaction (Otsuka 2009). This work enables further studies of exotic nuclei where microscopic theories play crucial roles, with the potential for improved predictive power as nuclear forces and many-body treatments progress.
