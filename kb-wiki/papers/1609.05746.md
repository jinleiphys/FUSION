# Properties of vector mesons in four pseudoscalars electroproduction

> Vector mesons and the $W$ gauge boson share some common features, such as the spin and a very short lifetime. The electromagnetic properties of the $W$ are linked to its gauge nature and thus a big effort has been devoted to measure them, while accounting for its instability. In this work, we elaborate on how these ideas can be translated to study vector mesons. We focus in the unstable property of such states and the restrictions from electromagnetic gauge invariance, describing the differences and similarities. Then, we describe the four pseudoscalars electroproduction as an analog to the $e^+e^- \to 4 fermions$ process, used to study the electromagnetic properties of the $W$ boson. We point out that the current experimental capabilities are reaching the possibility to measure the magnetic dipole moment of light vector mesons.

# Properties of vector mesons in four pseudoscalars electroproduction

## Key claim
The paper argues that the process $e^{+} e^{-} \to P^+ P^- P^0 P^0$ (four pseudoscalars electroproduction) can serve as an analog to the $e^+e^- \to 4$ fermions process used to study the $W$ boson electromagnetic properties, and that current experimental capabilities are reaching the possibility to measure the magnetic dipole moment of light vector mesons, though the quoted result for the $\rho$ meson carries a significant uncertainty of $\pm 0.5$ in units of $e/2 m_\rho$.

## Method
The author adapts the complex-mass scheme and boson-loop scheme from $W$ boson physics to vector mesons, accounting for their finite decay width through absorptive corrections to the propagator. The hadronic amplitude is modeled using the vector meson dominance (VMD) approach, with the three-vector-meson vertex parametrized following the same form as the electromagnetic vertex but with resonance contributions. Bose-Einstein symmetry and charge conjugation invariance are imposed on the amplitude by summing over four momentum configurations of the reduced amplitude, and gauge invariance is restored by adding counterterms to the non-gauge-invariant part of the hadronic current.

## Key numbers
- The magnetic dipole moment of the $\rho$ meson is determined to be $\mu_\rho = 2.1 \pm 0.5$ in units of $e/2 m_\rho$, from a fit to preliminary BaBar data on $e^+ e^- \to \pi^+ \pi^- 2 \pi^0$.
- The Standard Model values for the $W$ boson electromagnetic multipoles are given as: electric charge $\mathcal{Q} = g_1 = 1$ (in $e$ units), magnetic dipole moment $\mu_W = 1 + \kappa + \lambda = 2$ (in $e/2 M_W$ units), and electric quadrupole $X_E = -(\kappa - \lambda) = -1$ (in $e/M_W^2$ units).
- The correction to the multipoles of the $W$, $\rho$, and $K^*$ particles from their instability is found to be relatively small.

## Context
This work extends the methodology used by the DELPHI Collaboration (2000s) at LEP2 for measuring $W$ boson electromagnetic properties via $e^+e^- \to 4$ fermions, translating it to vector meson physics. The formalism builds on the complex-mass scheme and fermion-loop scheme developed for unstable gauge bosons, and on the boson-loop scheme of Gomez-Lopez and Toledo (2010) for vector mesons. The result for the $\rho$ magnetic dipole moment is compared with predictions from effective models of QCD and lattice QCD results at several pion mass values. This approach enables future measurements of the $K^*$ meson electromagnetic properties using $e^{+} e^{-} \to K^+ K^- 2 \pi^0$ data, though current experimental uncertainties on that process remain large.
