# Nucleon axial-vector form factor and radius from radiatively-corrected antineutrino scattering data

> The nucleon axial-vector form factor, $G_A$, is critical to determine the electroweak interactions of leptons with nucleons. Important examples of processes influenced by $G_A$ are elastic (anti)neutrino-nucleon scattering and muon capture by the proton. Sparse experimental data results in a large uncertainty on the momentum dependence of $G_A$ and has motivated the consideration of new experimental probes and first-principles lattice quantum chromodynamics (QCD) evaluations. The comparison of new and precise theoretical predictions for $G_A$ with future experimental data necessitates the application of radiative corrections to experimentally-observable processes. We apply these corrections in the extraction of $G_A$ and the associated axial-vector radius from the recent MINERvA antineutrino-hydrogen data, compare the effects from radiative corrections to other uncertainties in neutrino scattering experiments, and discuss the comparison of lattice QCD evaluations to experimental measurements.

## Key claim
The authors apply quantum electrodynamics (QED) radiative corrections for the first time in extracting the nucleon axial-vector form factor $G_A$ and its associated radius from antineutrino-hydrogen scattering data, finding that these corrections significantly improve the description of the MINERvA data but that the extracted radius depends mildly on the choice of nucleon vector form factors.

## Method
The authors perform $z$-expansion fits to 15 data points from the MINERvA muon antineutrino-hydrogen charged-current quasielastic scattering measurement, using both BBBA2005 and Borah2020 parameterizations for the nucleon vector form factors. They apply fixed-order QED radiative corrections from Tomalak et al. (2021, 2022) to the theoretical cross sections, evaluating flux-averaged corrections as a function of squared four-momentum transfer $Q^2$ for the MINERvA medium-energy flux configuration, and extract the axial-vector form factor and radius from fits with $k_\mathrm{max}=8$ and regularization parameter $\lambda=-1$, as well as unregularized fits with $k_\mathrm{max}=6$.

## Key numbers
- Axial-vector radius squared $r_A^2 = 0.49 \pm 0.14~\mathrm{fm}^2$ from the fit with Borah2020 vector form factors and radiative corrections applied (Table 1, fourth row).
- Axial-vector radius squared $r_A^2 = 0.57 \pm 0.17~\mathrm{fm}^2$ from the fit with Borah2020 vector form factors without radiative corrections (Table 1, third row).
- $\chi^2 = 8.16$ for the fit with Borah2020 vector form factors and radiative corrections, compared to $\chi^2 = 9.05$ without radiative corrections (Table 1).
- $\chi^2 = 7.78$ for the fit with BBBA2005 vector form factors and radiative corrections, compared to $\chi^2 = 8.64$ without radiative corrections (Table 1).
- The normalization of the axial-vector form factor at $Q^2=0$ is set to $G_A(0) = 1.2850$ for fits with radiative corrections, and $G_A(0) = 1.2754$ for tree-level fits (footnote 4).
- The $z$-expansion uses $t_\mathrm{cut} = 9 m_\pi^2 = 0.1616~\mathrm{GeV}^2$ and $t_0 = -0.5~\mathrm{GeV}^2$ for the hydrogen data (Section 3).

## Context
This work extends the analysis of the MINERvA antineutrino-hydrogen data (MINERvA Collaboration, 2023) by applying QED radiative corrections from Tomalak et al. (2021, 2022) for the first time in extracting the axial-vector form factor, addressing tensions between deuterium bubble chamber data (Meyer, 2016) and the hydrogen data. The results enable more precise comparisons between experimental extractions and lattice QCD calculations (Djukanovic, 2022; RQCD, 2019; Park, 2021; Alexandrou, 2020; Jang, 2023), which are consistent with the antineutrino-hydrogen data but not with the deuterium data, and support future percent-level precision goals for neutrino oscillation experiments such as DUNE and Hyper-Kamiokande.
