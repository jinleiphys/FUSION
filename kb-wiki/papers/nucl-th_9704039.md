# Semileptonic decays of heavy mesons

> The semileptonic and leptonic decays of heavy mesons are studied as a phenomenological application and exploration of a heavy-quark limit of Dyson-Schwinger equations. The single form factor, $ξ(w)$, which characterises the semileptonic decay in this limit, is calculated and compares well with recent experimental extractions. We obtain a lower bound of 1/3 on the slope-parameter $ρ^2 \equiv -ξ^\prime(1)$, which, in calculations with realistic input, is exceeded by a great deal: agreement with experimental data requiring $ρ^2 \sim 1.2 - 1.6$. The flavour and momentum dependence of the light-quark propagators has observable consequences.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that in the heavy-quark limit of Dyson-Schwinger equations, the semileptonic decay of heavy mesons is described by a single universal form factor ξ(w), which shows significant curvature on the physically accessible kinematic domain and yields a slope parameter ρ² in the range 1.2–1.6, with the lower bound of 1/3 being exceeded by a great deal in realistic calculations.

## Method
The authors employ a heavy-quark limit of Dyson-Schwinger equations where the dressed heavy-quark propagator is approximated as momentum-independent (Eq. hqa) and the quark-W-boson vertex is approximated by the bare vertex γμ (Eq. hqvtx). Using an impulse approximation for the semileptonic B⁰ → D⁻ ℓ⁺ ν decay amplitude and a Bethe-Salpeter amplitude Ansatz for the heavy mesons, they calculate the universal form factor ξ(w) and leptonic decay constants. The light-quark propagators are taken from previously determined algebraic forms that incorporate confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, with parameters fixed by fits to light-hadron observables.

## Key numbers
- Lower bound on slope parameter: ρ² ≥ 1/3, independent of model details
- Experimental fit requires ρ² = 1.53 ± 0.36 ± 0.14 (from CESR96 parametrization)
- Calculated ρ² values: 1.55 (forms A, B, C), 1.21 (form D)
- Binding energy E ≈ 0.6 GeV for B and D mesons (range 0.567–0.643 GeV across Ansätze)
- Size parameter Λ ≈ 1 GeV (range 0.843–1.32 GeV across Ansätze)
- Leptonic decay constants: f_D = 227 MeV, f_Ds = 239–245 MeV, f_B = 135 MeV (forms A, B, C)
- Ratio f_Ds/f_D ≈ 1.07, compared to heavy-quark symmetry prediction of 0.97
- Physical range for w in B → D decays: 1 ≤ w ≤ 1.6

## Context
This work extends the heavy-quark symmetry framework of Isgur and Wise (1990) by implementing it within Dyson-Schwinger equations, providing a nonperturbative QCD foundation for the universal form factor ξ(w). The calculation agrees with experimental extractions from CESR (1996) and shows qualitative similarities with results from Stech and Scholl (1996), Isgur and Morgunov (1996), and El-Hady et al. (1995). The study enables future work incorporating full DSE solutions for heavy quarks, as being explored by Chen, Jain, and Bender (1997), which would correlate the fitting parameters E and Λ with the gluon propagator mass scale and reduce dependence on existing data.
