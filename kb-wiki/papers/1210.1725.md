# Non-extensivity of hadronic systems

> The predictions from a non-extensive self-consistent theory recently proposed are investigated. Transverse momentum ($p_T$) distribution for several hadrons obtained in $p+p$ collisions are analyzed to verify if there are evidence for a limiting effective temperature and a limiting entropic factor. In addition, the hadron-mass spectrum proposed in that theory is confronted with available data. It turns out that all $p_T$-distributions and the mass spectrum obtained in the theory are in good agreement with experiment with constant effective temperature and constant entropic factor. The results confirm that the non-extensive statistics plays an important role in the description of the termodynamics of hadronic systems, and also that the self-consistent principle holds for energies as high as those achieved in the LHC. A discussion on the best $p_T$-distirbution formula for fitting experimental data is presented.

# Non-extensivity of hadronic systems

## Key claim
The non-extensive self-consistent theory predicts a limiting effective temperature and a limiting entropic index for all hadronic systems, and the authors find that experimental data from p+p collisions at LHC energies are consistent with constant values of these parameters, provided the correct Tsallis-derived formula (including the m_T factor) is used for fitting transverse momentum distributions.

## Method
The authors fit experimental p_T-distribution data from multiple LHC experiments (ALICE, ATLAS, CMS, LHCb) for various hadrons produced in p+p collisions at energies up to 7 TeV, using two different fitting formulas: one derived directly from Tsallis entropy via thermodynamic relations (Eq. 3, with an m_T factor) and an alternative empirical formula (Eq. 4, without the m_T factor). They also fit the cumulative hadron-mass spectrum using the mass spectrum formula from the non-extensive self-consistent theory, and compare the extracted temperature and entropic index values from both analyses.

## Key numbers
- Limiting effective temperature from p_T-distribution fits using Eq. 3: T_o = (60.7 ± 0.5) MeV
- Limiting entropic index from p_T-distribution fits using Eq. 3: q_o = 1.138 ± 0.006
- From hadron-mass spectrum fit: T = (52 ± 7) MeV and q = 1.103 ± 0.007
- From mass spectrum fit: gamma = (5 ± 3) × 10^{-3} GeV^{3/2}, k = 2.7 ± 0.4
- Hagedorn temperature from prior work cited: T_H = (192 ± 15) MeV, with c = -(950 ± 10) MeV in the relation T_o = T_H + (q_o - 1)c

## Context
This work extends the non-extensive self-consistent theory proposed by Deppman (2010) and tests its predictions against experimental data from p+p collisions at LHC energies up to 7 TeV, going beyond previous analyses by Cleymans and Worku (2012) and Sena (2011) that found constant temperature and entropic index values. The results contradict the standard Hagedorn bootstrap approach, which fails to describe p_T-distributions above sqrt(s) > 10 GeV and gives a Hagedorn temperature varying from 141 to 340 MeV depending on parametrization. The findings enable a complete thermodynamic description of dilute hadronic systems and allow comparison with lattice QCD results, while suggesting that microscopic mechanisms leading to non-extensivity should be incorporated into resonance hadron gas models.
