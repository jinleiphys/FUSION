# The Phase Structure of the Polyakov--Quark-Meson Model

> The relation between the deconfinement and chiral phase transition is explored in the framework of an Polyakov-loop-extended two-flavor quark-meson (PQM) model. In this model the Polyakov loop dynamics is represented by a background temporal gauge field which also couples to the quarks. As a novelty an explicit quark chemical potential and N_f-dependence in the Polyakov loop potential is proposed by using renormalization group arguments. The behavior of the Polyakov loop as well as the chiral condensate as function of temperature and quark chemical potential is obtained by minimizing the grand canonical thermodynamic potential of the system. The effect of the Polyakov loop dynamics on the chiral phase diagram and on several thermodynamic bulk quantities is presented.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the Polyakov loop potential's dependence on quark chemical potential and number of flavors can be estimated using perturbative renormalization group arguments, leading to a modified critical temperature \(T_0(N_f, \mu)\) that makes the chiral and deconfinement phase boundaries coincide at all chemical potentials, though the authors emphasize this is a rough estimate with uncertainties of at least \(\pm 30\) MeV.

## Method

The authors construct a Polyakov-loop-extended two-flavor quark-meson (PQM) model by coupling a polynomial Polyakov loop potential (fitted to pure Yang-Mills lattice data) to the chiral quark-meson model via a background temporal gauge field. They compute the grand canonical thermodynamic potential in mean-field approximation, minimizing it with respect to the chiral condensate \(\sigma\) and the Polyakov loop variables \(\Phi\) and \(\bar\Phi\) to obtain the phase structure as functions of temperature \(T\) and quark chemical potential \(\mu\). The Polyakov loop potential parameters are modified to include \(N_f\)- and \(\mu\)-dependence through perturbative estimates of the running coupling.

## Key numbers

* Critical Polyakov loop temperature for \(N_f=0\): \(T_0 = 270\) MeV; for \(N_f=2\) massless flavors: \(T_0 = 208\) MeV; for \(N_f=2+1\) flavors with \(m_s \approx 150\) MeV: \(T_0 = 187\) MeV (Table 1).
* Polyakov loop potential coefficients: \(b_3 = 0.75\), \(b_4 = 7.5\), and temperature-dependent \(b_2(T)\) with parameters \(a_0 = 6.75\), \(a_1 = -1.95\), \(a_2 = 2.625\), \(a_3 = -7.44\).
* Vacuum parameters: pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV, pion mass \(m_\pi = 138\) MeV, sigma mass \(m_\sigma = 600\) MeV, constituent quark mass \(m_q = 300\) MeV.
* Estimated \(\mu\)-dependent coefficient in the beta function: \(b_\mu \simeq (16/\pi) N_f\) for \(N_f=2\).

## Context

This work extends the Polyakov-loop-extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model approach of Fukushima (2003) and Ratti (2005) to the renormalizable quark-meson model, enabling a cleaner linkage to full QCD via functional renormalization group methods. The authors propose a novel \(N_f\)- and \(\mu\)-dependent Polyakov loop potential based on perturbative running coupling arguments, which contrasts with earlier fixed-\(T_0\) treatments. This enables future non-perturbative RG studies of the QCD phase diagram and resolves the unphysical scenario where the deconfinement transition temperature exceeds the chiral transition temperature at finite chemical potential.
