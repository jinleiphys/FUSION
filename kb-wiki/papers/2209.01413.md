# Effects of a phase transition on two-pion interferometry in heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=2.4-7.7$ GeV

> Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) correlations for charged pions in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=2.4 - 7.7~\text{GeV}$ (corresponding to beam kinetic energies in the fixed target frame from $E_{\rm{lab}}=1.23~\text{to}~30~\text{GeV/nucleon}$) are calculated using the UrQMD model with different equations of state. The effects of a phase transition at high baryon densities are clearly observed in the HBT parameters that are explored. It is found that the available data on the HBT radii, $R_{O}/R_{S}$ and $R^{2}_{O}-R^{2}_{S}$, in the investigated energy region favors a relatively stiff equation of state at low beam energies which then turns into a soft equation of state at high collision energies consistent with astrophysical constraints on the high density equation of state of QCD. The specific effects of two different phase transition scenarios on the $R_{O}/R_{S}$ and $R^{2}_{O}-R^{2}_{S}$ are investigated. It is found that a phase transition with a significant softening of the equation of state below 4 times nuclear saturation density can be excluded using HBT data. Our results highlight that the pion's $R_{O}/R_{S}$ and $R^{2}_{O}-R^{2}_{S}$ are sensitive to the stiffness of the equation of state, and can be used to constrain and understand the QCD equation of state in the high baryon density region.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the available HBT data on $R_O/R_S$ and $R_O^2-R_S^2$ in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=2.4-7.7$ GeV favors a stiff equation of state at low beam energies that softens at higher energies, and that a phase transition with significant softening below 4 times nuclear saturation density can be excluded using HBT data, but the authors note that the experimental data still has substantial errors in the most interesting energy region.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD transport model (version 3.5) with different equations of state implemented through density-dependent potentials, including a hard Skyrme EoS, a soft Skyrme EoS, a standard chiral mean field (CMF) EoS, and two CMF EoS with phase transitions at different densities (PT2 and PT3). They simulated central (0-10%) Au+Au collisions at beam energies from $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=2.4$ to 7.7 GeV, extracted the freeze-out space-time coordinates and momenta of charged pions, and fed them into the CRAB v3.0$\beta$ correlation after-burner to compute three-dimensional HBT correlation functions and extract the HBT radii $R_O$, $R_S$, and $R_L$.

## Key numbers

- The beam energy range studied is $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=2.4-7.7$ GeV, corresponding to $E_\mathrm{lab}=1.23$ to 30 GeV/nucleon in the fixed-target frame.
- The hard Skyrme EoS uses an incompressibility $K_0=380$ MeV; the soft Skyrme EoS uses $K_0=200$ MeV.
- The CMF_PT2 EoS becomes mechanically unstable at 2.5 times nuclear saturation density ($\rho_0=0.16$ fm$^{-3}$) and reaches minimum pressure at about $4\rho_0$.
- The CMF_PT3 EoS becomes mechanically unstable above about $5\rho_0$.
- The baryon density reached in 0-10% central Au+Au collisions is less than $3\rho_0$ at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=2.4$ GeV and reaches about $5\rho_0$ at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=7.7$ GeV.
- Pions freeze out mainly in the time interval 5-25 fm/$c$.
- The mean freeze-out time shows a minimum at about $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=4$ GeV for all calculations.

## Context

This work extends previous studies that implemented density-dependent equations of state in the UrQMD model (Omana Kuttan 2022) and showed effects of phase transitions on hadronic flow observables (Steinheimer 2022). It directly tests the qualitative prediction from earlier work (Pratt 1986, Bertsch 1988, Rischke 1996) that a first-order phase transition would cause a nonmonotonic behavior in HBT radii excitation functions, which had been observed by the STAR collaboration (Lacey 2014, STAR 2014) at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}\approx 20$ GeV but remained controversial. The results enable consistent comparisons between HBT and flow observables within the same framework to constrain the QCD equation of state at high baryon density, and highlight the need for more precise experimental data in the energy region around $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=4$ GeV.
