# Diffractive structure function in the dipole picture

> We calculate the contribution from the $q\bar{q}g$ component of a virtual photon state to the small-$x$ diffractive cross section in deep inelastic scattering in the saturation regime. The obtained cross section is finite by itself and a part of the full next-to-leading order result. We perform the calculation in exact kinematics in the eikonal limit, and show that the previously known high virtuality $Q^2$ and large invariant mass $M_X^2$ results for the structure functions can be extracted. We furthermore discuss the steps required to obtain the full next-to-leading order result.

## Key claim

The paper presents a calculation of the $q\bar{q}g$ Fock state contribution to the small-$x$ diffractive cross section in deep inelastic scattering, which is finite by itself and forms a part of the full next-to-leading order result, but only in the regime where $\beta$ is not parametrically small so that powers of $\alpha_s \ln 1/\beta$ do not need to be resummed.

## Method

The authors calculate the diffractive structure function in the dipole picture using exact eikonal kinematics, where the only kinematical approximation is the eikonal interaction with the target. The calculation is expressed in terms of three-particle "transfer functions" ${\cal I}_{M_X}^{(3)}$ and ${\cal I}_{\Deltat}^{(3)}$ that relate the quark, antiquark, and gluon coordinates in the amplitude and conjugate amplitude to the invariant mass $M_X$ and momentum transfer $t$, multiplied by the squared gluon emission wavefunction and tripole Wilson line operators for the $q\bar{q}g$ state interacting with the target.

## Key numbers

* The diffractive structure function depends on three Lorentz-invariant variables: $Q^2$, $\beta = Q^2/(Q^2+M_X^2)$, and $x_{\mathbb{P}}$, where $M_X$ is the invariant mass of the diffractive system.
* The 3-particle transfer function to $M_X$ is ${\cal I}_{M_X}^{(3)} = 2 \frac{z_0 z_1 z_2}{(4 \pi)^2} \frac{M_X}{Y_{012}} \ \mathrm{J}_1 \!\left( M_X Y_{012} \right)$, where $z_0, z_1, z_2$ are longitudinal momentum fractions satisfying $z_0 + z_1 + z_2 = 1$.
* The transfer function for $t$ is ${\cal I}_\Deltat^{(3)} = \frac{1}{4 \pi} \ \mathrm{J}_0 \! \left( \sqrt{-t} \norm{z_0 \xt_{\Bar 0 0} + z_1 \xt_{\Bar 1 1} + z_2 \xt_{\Bar 2 2}} \right)$.
* In the large $M_X$ limit, the result reduces to an expression involving the LO $\gamma^*\to q\bar{q}$ wavefunction, the BK kernel, and a squared Wilson line operator, with prefactor $\frac{\alpha_s N_c C_F Q^2}{16\pi^5\alpha_{\mathrm{em}}}$.
* In the large $Q^2$ limit, the Wüsthoff result is recovered, which contains an explicit logarithmic dependence $\ln \frac{Q^2}{k^2}$ and Bessel functions $K_2$ and $J_2$.

## Context

This work extends the dipole picture of deep inelastic scattering to next-to-leading order for diffractive processes, building on the leading-order $q\bar{q}$ dipole description where the cross section is dominated by different partonic configurations depending on $\beta$ (Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff, 1999). The calculation reproduces known limiting cases: the large $M_X$ result from Munier and collaborators (2003) and the Wusthoff result (1997) in the large $Q^2$ limit, providing an independent rederivation of the latter. The full next-to-leading order result will require including additional contributions such as gluon emissions after the target, virtual corrections to the $\gamma^* \to q\bar{q}$ wavefunction already calculated by Beuf (2016) and Hanninen and collaborators (2017), and final state interactions between the quark and antiquark after the target.
