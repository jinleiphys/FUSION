# van der Waals Interactions and Hadron Resonance Gas: Role of resonance widths modeling on conserved charges fluctuations

> The quantum van der Waals (QvdW) extension of the ideal hadron resonance gas (HRG) model which includes the attractive and repulsive interactions between baryons -- the QvdW-HRG model -- is applied to study the behavior of the baryon number related susceptibilities in the crossover temperature region. Inclusion of the QvdW interactions leads to a qualitatively different behavior of susceptibilities, in many cases resembling lattice QCD simulations. It is shown that for some observables, in particular for $χ_{11}^{BQ} / χ_2^B$, effects of the QvdW interactions essentially cancel out. It is found that the inclusion of the finite resonance widths leads to an improved description of $χ_2^B$, but it also leads to a worse description of $χ_{11}^{BQ} / χ_2^B$, as compared to the lattice data. On the other hand, inclusion of the extra, unconfirmed baryons into the hadron list leads to a simultaneous improvement in the description of both observables.

# van der Waals Interactions and Hadron Resonance Gas: Role of resonance widths modeling on conserved charges fluctuations

## Key claim
The quantum van der Waals extension of the hadron resonance gas model (QvdW-HRG) reproduces some qualitative features of lattice QCD susceptibilities, but the effects of QvdW interactions cancel out exactly in the ratio χ₁₁^{BQ} / χ₂^B at zero baryon chemical potential; finite resonance widths improve the description of χ₂^B but worsen the description of χ₁₁^{BQ} / χ₂^B relative to lattice data.

## Method
The authors extended the ideal hadron resonance gas (IHRG) model by including quantum van der Waals interactions (attraction parameter a ≈ 329 MeV fm³ and repulsion parameter b ≈ 3.42 fm³) between all baryon pairs and all antibaryon pairs, while neglecting baryon-antibaryon, meson-meson, and meson-baryon interactions. They computed baryon number susceptibilities and baryon-electric charge correlators at zero chemical potential by solving the coupled equations for baryon and antibaryon pressures and densities, incorporating finite resonance widths through relativistic Breit-Wigner mass distributions integrated over a range of masses, and comparing results using the standard PDG hadron list (about 380 species) versus an extended PDG+ list (about 580 species including unconfirmed states).

## Key numbers
- QvdW parameters: a ≈ 329 MeV fm³ and b ≈ 3.42 fm³, taken from fits to nuclear matter ground state
- Critical point of symmetric nuclear matter in QvdW-HRG: T_c ≈ 19.7 MeV, μ_c ≈ 908 MeV, n_c ≈ 0.07 fm³ = 0.45 n₀
- Standard PDG hadron list contains about 380 hadron species; extended PDG+ list contains about 580 hadron species
- The ratio χ₁₁^{BQ} / χ₂^B at μ_B = 0 is analytically identical between QvdW-HRG and IHRG in the Boltzmann approximation

## Context
This work extends the QvdW-HRG model of Vovchenko (2016) by simultaneously studying the effects of finite resonance widths and the choice of hadron list on conserved charge fluctuations, which had not been done previously. The breakdown of the IHRG model at T ≈ 150-160 MeV for fluctuations (Bazavov, 2013) had been interpreted as a deconfinement signal, but this work shows that QvdW interactions can explain some of the deviations. The results point to the need for more sophisticated resonance modeling, such as the S-matrix approach of Lo (2017), and suggest that in-medium mass modifications for negative-parity states (Aarts, 2017) could be combined with the QvdW approach to improve agreement with lattice data.
