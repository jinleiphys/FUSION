# Lorentz Covariant Spin-Grouping of Baryon Resonances

> A well pronounced spin--grouping of baryon resonances to O(4) partial waves is found in baryon spectra and shown to be well interpreted in terms of Lorentz group representations of the type (1/2 +l', 1/2 +l')* [(1/2, 0)+(0,1/2)] with l' integer. In this way the relativistic description of finite dimensional resonance towers containing higher-spin states becomes possible. It is further argued that the nucleon excitations into the l'=1 and l'=2 multiplets are chiral phase transitions.

## Key claim

Baryon resonances group into O(4) partial waves (Lorentz multiplets of type {1/2+l', 1/2+l'} ⊗ [(1/2,0)⊕(0,1/2)]) rather than O(3) partial waves, with the nucleon excitations into l'=1 and l'=2 multiplets argued to be chiral phase transitions.

## Method

The author re-analyzed the symmetry of all reported N, Δ, and Λ baryon excitations with masses below 2500 MeV, using speed plot analysis of pole positions on the complex energy plane performed by Höhler. The resonances were classified into Lorentz group representations of the type {1/2+l', 1/2+l'} ⊗ [(1/2,0)⊕(0,1/2)] with integer l', and an empirical recursive relation was constructed to describe the mass averages of resonances from these Lorentz multiplets.

## Key numbers

- The first excited nucleon spin-cluster with l'=0 has mass given by M_{n=2} - M_{n=1} = (1/1^2 - 1/4)(1^2 - 1/4)M_{n=1} with M_{n=1}=M_N
- For Δ and Λ baryons, the mass scale M_{n=1} is replaced by (M_N+M_Δ)/2 and (M_N+M_Λ)/2 respectively
- The spacing between relativistic multiplets with masses below 2000 MeV is about 200 MeV
- The maximal mass splitting between corresponding multiplet members is 50-70 MeV
- The first S_{11} and D_{13} states appear separated by only 15 MeV
- Five new, still unobserved non-strange resonances are predicted: F_{17} around 1700 MeV, and H_{1,11}, P_{31}, P_{33}, D_{33} between 2200 and 2400 MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Kirchbach (1997) showing that baryon spectra are governed by O(1,3)_{LS}⊗SU(2)_I symmetry rather than the non-relativistic O(3)_L⊗SU(6)_{SF} classification scheme. It contradicts the earlier approaches by Kleinert (using unitary Lorentz group representations) and Toller (using finite-dimensional non-unitary representations), which required parity duplication absent from data. The scheme enables relativistic description of higher-spin resonances as members of covariant representations, with propagators directly readable from Lorentz group representation theory, and suggests a modified Balmer-series pattern for resonance masses.
