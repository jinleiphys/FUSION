# The $0^{+}\to 0^{+}$ positron double-$β$ decay with emission of two neutrinos in the nuclei $^{96}$Ru, $^{102}$Pd, $^{106}$Cd and $^{108}$Cd

> Theoretical results for %the $0^{+}\to 0^{+}$ positron double-$β$ decay with emission of two neutrinos in the nuclei $^{96}$Ru, $^{102}$Pd, $^{106}$Cd and $^{108}$Cd are presented. The study employs the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model to obtain the wave functions of the parent and daughter nuclei, in conjunction with the summation method to estimate the double beta decay nuclear matrix elements. The reliability of the intrinsic wave functions of $^{96,102}$Ru, $% ^{96}$Mo, $^{102,106,108}$Pd and $^{106,108}$Cd nuclei are tested by comparing the theoretically calculated spectroscopic properties with the available experimental data. Calculated half-lives $T_{1/2}^{2ν}$ of $% ^{96}$Ru, $^{102}$Pd, $^{106}$Cd and $^{108}$Cd nuclei for 2$ν$ $β^{+}β^{+}$, 2$ν$ $β^{+}EC$ and 2$ν$ $ECEC$ modes are presented. The effect of deformation on the nuclear transition matrix element $M_{2ν}$ is also studied.

## Key claim

The Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model with a PPQQ interaction can reproduce the measured spectroscopic properties and double-beta decay half-lives for nuclei in the A=96-108 mass region, but the nuclear transition matrix elements are quenched by a factor of approximately 2 to 3.5 due to deformation effects, which the abstract does not quantify.

## Method

The authors employed the Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) model to generate intrinsic wave functions for parent and daughter nuclei, then used the summation method to calculate the nuclear transition matrix elements M_{2ν} for 0⁺→0⁺ transitions. The reliability of the wave functions was tested by comparing calculated yrast spectra, B(E2:0⁺→2⁺) transition probabilities, static quadrupole moments Q(2⁺), and g-factors g(2⁺) with available experimental data. The strength of the quadrupole-quadrupole (QQ) part of the effective two-body interaction was varied to study the effect of deformation on M_{2ν}.

## Context

This work extends the PHFB model calculations previously applied to ¹⁰⁶Cd (Shukla 2005) to the nuclei ⁹⁶Ru, ¹⁰²Pd, and ¹⁰⁸Cd, providing the first theoretical half-life predictions for ¹⁰²Pd and ¹⁰⁸Cd. The results differ from earlier QRPA calculations by Hirsch (1994) by factors of 3-5 and from SU(4)στ model results by Rumyantsev (1998) by factors of approximately 2, with the PHFB model generally yielding smaller matrix elements due to explicit inclusion of deformation. The predictions are intended to guide upcoming experiments such as the Osaka-OTO experiment (Itoh 1997) and COBRA (Zuber 2001), which are expected to reach the observable range for these positron double-beta decay modes.
