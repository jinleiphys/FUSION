# Production mechanisms and polarization observables for p + d -> He(3) + pi(o) near threshold

> Pion production at threshold from nucleon-deuteron collisions is considered, with reference to the outgoing channel where the three-nucleon system is bound. The available experimental data are compared with calculations using accurate nuclear wavefunctions coming from rigorous solutions of the three-nucleon quantum mechanical equations. The dominant contributions for pion production are obtained through matrix elements involving pion-nucleon rescattering mechanisms in S- and P-waves. S-wave rescattering includes also an isoscalar contribution which is enhanced because of the off-shell effects. P-wave rescattering includes also diagrams involving explicitly the Delta degrees of freedom. It is found that the pion-nucleon S-wave off-shell effects in the isospin-even channel are of considerable importance. Initial-state interactions between the proton and the deuteron have in general minor effects on the spin-averaged and spin-dependent observables, except when the polarization observable involves interference terms amongst the various helicity amplitudes. Keywords: Polarization phenomena. Pion production. Few-nucleon systems.

# Production mechanisms and polarization observables for p + d -> He(3) + pi(o) near threshold

## Key claim
The dominant contributions to pion production in the p + d -> He-3 + pi0 reaction near threshold come from pion-nucleon rescattering mechanisms in S- and P-waves, with an isoscalar S-wave contribution that is enhanced by off-shell effects and is essential for reproducing both the absolute cross-section normalization and the energy dependence of the tensor analyzing power T20. Initial-state interactions between the proton and deuteron have minor effects on spin-averaged and spin-dependent observables, except when polarization observables involve interference terms among helicity amplitudes.

## Method
The authors calculated the reaction using elementary production mechanisms from a non-relativistic reduction of a phenomenological pion-nucleon and pion-Delta Lagrangian, evaluating matrix elements between three-nucleon states in a large basis of 464 three-nucleon partial waves with total angular momenta up to 7/2 for both parities. The matrix elements were folded with nuclear wavefunctions from rigorous solutions of three-nucleon quantum mechanical equations using Bonn A, Bonn B, and Paris nucleon-nucleon potentials, with initial-state interactions treated via an Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas scheme using realistic nucleon-nucleon transition matrices.

## Key numbers
- Coupling constants used: f_piNN^2 / 4pi = 0.0735, f_piNDelta^2 / 4pi = 0.32, lambda_I = 0.045, lambda_O = 0.006
- The calculation included 464 three-nucleon partial waves with total angular momenta up to 7/2 for both parities
- Without the isoscalar S-wave rescattering term, the differential cross-section in forward direction is underestimated by a factor ranging between two and three
- The tensor analyzing power T20 very close to threshold approaches the geometrical limit of -sqrt(2) when only the rho-exchange diagram is included

## Context
This work extends the microscopic approach initiated by Green and Maqueda (1979) and Green and Sainio (1979) for pion production in few-nucleon systems, using more accurate nuclear wavefunctions and a larger number of intermediate three-nucleon states than previously possible. The results are compared with experimental data from Kerboul (1986), Nikulin (1996), and the GEM collaboration (2001). The paper enables further theoretical investigations into alternative phenomenological models for pion-nucleon amplitudes and the role of heavy-meson exchange diagrams, while also motivating additional experimental measurements of spin observables such as C_yy in both collinear and non-collinear kinematics.
