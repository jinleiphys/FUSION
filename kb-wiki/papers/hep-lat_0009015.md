# The radial wavefunctions of a heavy-light meson calculated on a lattice

> A brief review is given of attempts to understand the energies of four-quark systems calculated on a lattice in terms of nuclear-physics-inspired many-body techniques involving interquark potentials. Results are given for the next stage of this study where the wavefunctions of heavy-light mesons are also calculated on a lattice.

# The radial wavefunctions of a heavy-light meson calculated on a lattice

## Key claim

The radial wavefunctions of a heavy-light meson, specifically the light quark charge and matter distributions around a static quark, have been measured on a lattice for the first time; these wavefunctions are extracted from three-point correlation functions and fitted to an exponential form, with the charge distribution showing a longer range than the matter distribution.

## Method

The authors used lattice QCD with SU(3) gauge group and unquenched configurations to compute three-point correlation functions of the form $C_3(t,r) = \sum_{ijl} G_{jl} O G_{li} U_{ij}$, where $G_{ij}$ are light-quark propagators from Ref. (MP98), $U_{ij}$ is the static quark propagator, and the local operators $O = \gamma_4$ (probing charge distribution) and $O = 1$ (probing matter distribution) are evaluated at a distance $r$ from the heavy quark. The resulting distributions were fitted by $F^2$ with $F = A \exp(-r/r_0)$.

## Key numbers

- Charge distribution ($\gamma_4$): $r_0/a = 1.56(2)$, $A = 0.45(1)$, direct lattice sum DSum = 1.12(5)
- Matter distribution (1): $r_0/a = 1.15(5)$, $A = 0.46(2)$, direct lattice sum DSum = 0.25(5)
- The charge distribution has a considerably longer range than the matter distribution, as seen from the $r_0/a$ values

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies by the same collaboration that measured energies of four-quark systems with infinitely heavy quarks (Green et al., 1993; Green et al., 1998) and later heavy-light meson energies (Michael and Pennanen, 1998). The wavefunction measurements are of relevance to phenomenological models such as bag models and semirelativistic Schrodinger and Blankenbecler-Sugar equations, and may enable improved understanding of meson decays and meson-meson scattering.
