# Dressed-quarks and the Roper resonance

> A Dyson-Schwinger equation calculation of the light hadron spectrum, which correlates the masses of meson and baryon ground- and excited-states within a single framework, produces a description of the Roper resonance that corresponds closely with conclusions drawn recently by EBAC. Namely, the Roper is a particular type of radial excitation of the nucleon's dressed-quark core augmented by a material meson cloud component. There are, in addition, some surprises.

## Key claim

A Dyson-Schwinger equation calculation of the light hadron spectrum, correlating meson and baryon ground- and excited-state masses within a single framework, produces a description of the Roper resonance as a radial excitation of the nucleon's dressed-quark core with a material meson cloud component; however, the computed masses should not be compared directly with experiment because the kernels employed do not incorporate meson loop effects, and a fair comparison requires bare-masses inferred from coupled-channels analyses.

## Method

The authors used a Poincaré covariant Faddeev equation, whose kernel was computed from dressed-quark propagators, diquark Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, and diquark propagators obtained via a confining and symmetry-preserving regularization of a vector × vector current-current interaction within the rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations. Lessons from more sophisticated meson studies were used to improve the description of parity-partners of π- and ρ-mesons and their corresponding diquark correlations. A variant of the "static approximation" was employed for the Faddeev equation kernel, leading to baryon amplitudes independent of relative momentum.

## Key numbers

- Dressed-quark mass M = 0.37 GeV from a current-quark mass m = 7 MeV, used in the contact-interaction DSE kernel.
- Predictions for baryon dressed-quark-core masses match bare-masses from Gasparyan (2003) with a root-mean-square relative error of 10%.
- Predictions for baryon dressed-quark-core masses match bare-masses from EBAC (Suzuki, 2009) with a root-mean-square relative error of 14%.
- The coupled-channels analysis of πN scattering data covers energies up to W ≲ 2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation framework to simultaneously describe meson and baryon ground- and excited-states, building on prior studies of nucleon properties using the static approximation (Buck, 1992; Bentz, 2007). The results agree with EBAC's coupled-channels analysis (Suzuki, 2009), which finds a dressed-quark-core for the Roper resonance, but contradict the Jülich coupled-channels model (Gasparyan, 2003), which treats the Roper as dynamically-generated with no bare three-quark state. The finding that the Roper is constituted almost entirely from axial-vector diquark correlations, due to orthogonality with the scalar-diquark-dominated nucleon ground state, is expected to impact the momentum-dependence of the nucleon-to-Roper transition form factor.
