# Relativistic nuclear structure effects in (e,e' vec{p})

> Results for recoil nucleon induced polarization for $(e,e'\vec{p})$ are presented using various approximations for the relativistic nucleonic current, at the kinematics of a recent experiment at Bates. We see that the dynamical relativistic effects improve the agreement with the data. We make predictions for the induced normal polarization and responses for TJNAF 89-033 and Mainz A1/2-93 experiments.

## Key claim

Dynamical relativistic effects, specifically the enhancement of the lower components of the Dirac spinors due to negative-energy components (spinor distortion), improve the agreement with measured induced normal polarization \(P_n\) data beyond what is achieved by non-relativistic or kinematically-corrected approaches, even when the same optical potential is used.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) calculations for the \(^{12}\text{C}(e,e'\vec{p})\) reaction using Dirac-Hartree bound state wave functions (NL3 parameter set) and relativistic optical potentials (EDAI-C, EDAD-1) for the outgoing proton. They compared fully relativistic results (including negative-energy components) with results obtained after projecting out the negative-energy components and with results from the effective momentum approximation without spinor distortion (EMA-noSV), using both \(cc1\) and \(cc2\) forms of the relativistic nucleon current operator.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy of 580 MeV, kinetic energy of the final nucleon of 270 MeV, \(|\vec{q}| \simeq 760\) MeV/c, \(\omega \simeq 290\) MeV, \(Q^2 \simeq 0.5\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) for the Bates experiment kinematics.
- The Darwin factor \(A^{-1}(r) = (E+M)/(E+M+S(r)-V(r))\) is approximately 2 at the nuclear interior for typical potential values.
- Non-relativistic analyses underestimated \(P_n\) by around 10% at best.
- For the TJNAF kinematics: beam energy of 2450 MeV, kinetic energy of the ejected nucleon of about 420 MeV, \(Q^2 \simeq 0.8\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
- For the Mainz kinematics: beam energy of 855 MeV, kinetic energy of the ejected nucleon of 200 MeV.
- The enhancement of lower components becomes important for momenta \(p > 300\) MeV/c.
- Gordon ambiguities and off-shell kinematics ambiguities cause differences of up to 15% between \(cc1\) and \(cc2\) EMA-noSV unpolarized cross sections, but less than 1% effect on \(P_n\) predictions.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) analyses of Woo et al. (1998) for the Bates \(^{12}\text{C}(e,e'\vec{p})\) experiment, which systematically underestimated the induced normal polarization \(P_n\). It also builds upon the relativistic calculations of Sherif et al. (1999) by specifying which relativistic effects (spinor distortion from negative-energy components versus the Darwin term) are responsible for the improved agreement. The results enable predictions for upcoming experiments at TJNAF (89-033) and Mainz (A1/2-93) that can test the role of negative-energy components in the nucleon wave function, particularly through the \(R_{TL}^n\) response in the \(s_{1/2}\) shell where spinor distortion produces a nonzero \(P_n\) while EMA-noSV predicts zero.
