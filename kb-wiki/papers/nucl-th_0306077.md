# Fluctuations of the Color-superconducting Order Parameter in Heated and Dense Quark Matter

> Fluctuations of the color superconducting order parameter in dense quark matter at finite temperatures are investigated in terms of the phenomenological Ginzburg - Landau approach. Our estimates show that fluctuations of the di-quark gap may strongly affect some of thermodynamic quantities even far below and above the critical temperature. If the critical temperature of the di-quark phase transition were rather high one could expect a manifestation of fluctuations of the di-quark gap in the course of heavy ion collisions.

# Fluctuations of the Color-superconducting Order Parameter in Heated and Dense Quark Matter

## Key claim
Fluctuations of the di-quark gap may strongly affect thermodynamic quantities even far below and above the critical temperature, provided the critical temperature is rather high (above approximately 50-70 MeV) for heavy ion collision applications, or rather low (below approximately 30-50 MeV) for neutron star applications.

## Method
The author employs a phenomenological Ginzburg-Landau approach, expanding the thermodynamic potential up to fourth order in the di-quark field operators and assuming a second-order phase transition. Fluctuations are treated within the self-consistent Hartree approximation (one-vertex Φ functional), with the Matsubara technique used to incorporate finite-temperature effects. The specific heat density is calculated from the fluctuation contribution to the free energy, and the Ginzburg-Levanyuk criterion is applied to determine the temperature region where fluctuations dominate over mean-field contributions.

## Key numbers
- Zero-temperature pairing gap Δ predicted to be ∼ (20 ÷ 200) MeV for quark chemical potentials μ_q ∼ (350 ÷ 500) MeV.
- Critical temperature estimated as T_c ≃ 0.56 Δ, as in standard BCS theory.
- For the CFL phase in the weak coupling limit, the fluctuation temperature below T_c is estimated as T_{fl,<}^C ≃ (0.6 ÷ 0.8)T_c from the specific heat comparison, reducing to ≲ 0.5T_c when suppression factors are included.
- The fluctuation temperature above T_c is estimated as T_{fl,>}^C ∼ 2T_c for typical values T_c/μ_q ∼ 0.1 ÷ 0.3.
- The coherence length ξ ≃ 0.13/(T_c √|t|) is approximately 0.5 fm for T_c ≃ 50 MeV and |t| ∼ 1.
- The Ginzburg-Levanyuk parameter Gi ∼ 1 for color superconductivity, compared to Gi = 10^{-12}–10^{-14} for clean conventional superconductors.
- The specific heat of the quark-gluon plasma is given by ∼ 6μ_q² T + 42π² T³/15 + 32π² T³/15 (quark and gluon contributions).

## Context
This work extends the study of color superconductivity fluctuations beyond the mean-field treatment, following the re-investigation of the problem in a series of papers starting with Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1998) and Rapp et al. (1998). The paper specifically builds on the Ginzburg-Landau approach developed by Ippolito and collaborators and by Giannotti and Rischke (2003), who demonstrated that gluon fluctuations change the nature of the phase transition but only in a narrow temperature interval ΔT/T_c ≲ 0.1. The work also references Kitazawa et al. (2002), who studied fluctuating pair fields in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and found a prominent peak in the spectral function surviving in the temperature interval |T-T_c| ≲ 0.2T_c. The results enable estimates of fluctuation effects in heavy ion collisions at facilities like GSI's Compressed Baryon Matter experiment and in the initial evolution of massive neutron stars.
