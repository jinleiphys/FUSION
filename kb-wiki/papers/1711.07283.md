# Evidence of the QCD tricritical endpoint existence at NICA-FAIR energies

> We present a summary of possible signals of the chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement phase transitions which may be, respectively, probed at the center of mass collision energies at 4.3-4.9 GeV and above 8.7-9.2 GeV. It is argued that these signals may evidence for an existence of the tricritical endpoint of QCD phase diagram at the collision energy around 8.7-9.2 GeV. The equation of state of hadronic matter with the restored chiral symmetry is discussed and the number of bosonic and fermionic degrees of freedom is found.

# Evidence of the QCD tricritical endpoint existence at NICA-FAIR energies

## Key claim

The paper argues that a set of eight hydrodynamic, thermodynamic, fluctuational, and microscopic signals observed in heavy-ion collision data may collectively evidence the existence of a tricritical endpoint of the QCD phase diagram at center-of-mass collision energies around or slightly above 8.7–9.2 GeV, with separate signals for chiral symmetry restoration at 4.3–4.9 GeV and deconfinement above 8.7–9.2 GeV. The claim is hedged by the statement that these signals "may evidence" the endpoint, and the authors note that the lattice QCD cannot currently determine whether chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement are two distinct phase transitions or a single one at high baryonic densities.

## Method

The authors compiled and summarized eight previously identified signals of phase transitions from heavy-ion collision data, including hydrodynamic quasi-plateaus in entropy and particle ratios, minima in chemical freeze-out volume and generalized specific volume, peaks in trace anomaly and baryonic density, apparent chemical equilibrium of strange charge, enhancement of fluctuations, and the strangeness horn (K+/π+ ratio). These signals were obtained using a multicomponent Hadron Resonance Gas Model (HRGM) with distinct hard-core radii for different hadron species (pion radius 0.15 fm, kaon radius 0.395 fm, Λ-hyperon radius 0.085 fm, other baryons 0.365 fm, other mesons 0.42 fm), which achieved a fit quality of χ²/dof ≈ 1.04 for hadronic multiplicity ratios across AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies. The equation of state of hadronic matter with restored chiral symmetry was then determined by fitting the entropy per baryon along the shock adiabat, yielding an MIT-Bag-model-like form with coefficients extracted from data.

## Key numbers

- Chiral symmetry restoration signals are probed at center-of-mass collision energies √s_NN = 4.3–4.9 GeV; deconfinement signals are probed at √s_NN ≥ 8.7–9.2 GeV.
- The tricritical endpoint is argued to exist at collision energy around or slightly above √s_NN ≥ 8.7–9.2 GeV.
- The multicomponent HRGM uses hard-core radii: pions R_π = 0.15 fm, kaons R_K = 0.395 fm, Λ-hyperons R_Λ = 0.085 fm, other baryons R_b = 0.365 fm, other mesons R_m = 0.42 fm.
- The fit quality of hadronic multiplicity ratios is χ²/dof ≈ 1.04.
- The equation of state for chiral matter has coefficients: A_0 ≈ 2.53 × 10⁻⁵ MeV⁻³ fm⁻³, A_2 ≈ 1.51 × 10⁻⁶ MeV⁻³ fm⁻³, A_4 ≈ 1.001 × 10⁻⁹ MeV⁻³ fm⁻³, and B ≈ 9488 MeV fm⁻³.
- Total effective degrees of freedom: N_dof^tot ≈ 1770; fermionic effective degrees of freedom: N_f^eff ≈ 141; bosonic effective degrees of freedom: N_b^eff ≈ 1523.
- Hydrodynamic quasi-plateaus are seen at 3.8–4.9 GeV and at 7.6–9.2 GeV.
- Minimum of chemical freeze-out volume is seen at 4.3–4.9 GeV in one-component HRGM and at 4.9 GeV in multicomponent HRGM.
- Minimum of generalized specific volume X = (ε + p)/ρ_b² is seen at 4.9 GeV and at 9.2 GeV.
- Strong peak of trace anomaly δ = (ε - 3p)/T⁴ is seen at 4.9 GeV; small peak at 9.2 GeV.
- Strong peak of baryonic density ρ_b is seen at 4.9 GeV and at 9.2 GeV.
- Apparent chemical equilibrium of strange charge (γ_s = 1) is seen at 4.9 GeV and at √s ≥ 8.8 GeV.
- Enhancement of fluctuations is seen at 8.8 GeV.
- Strangeness horn (K+/π+ ratio) is seen at 7.6 GeV, with onset of deconfinement at/below 8.7 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the hypothesis first formulated in Bugaev (2014, 2015, 2016) that two sets of phase transition signals exist at distinct collision energy ranges, and it formalizes the conclusion of a tricritical endpoint existence first stated in Bugaev (2017). The analysis independently corroborates findings from the Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics (PHSD) model by Cassing and collaborators (2016a, 2016b), which assumed chiral symmetry restoration at about 4 GeV and deconfinement at about 9–10 GeV, and from fluctuation analyses by KABKo (2017) that identified the vicinity of 8.8 GeV as near the critical endpoint. The results enable future experimental searches at NICA and FAIR facilities to target the 8.7–9.2 GeV energy range for direct observation of the tricritical endpoint, and they provide an equation of state for the chiral phase that can be used in hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions.
