# Constraints on neutrino-Majoron couplings using SN1987A data

> Neutrino decay to a lighter neutrino and a massless or almost massless (pseudo)scalar Goldstone boson remains of wide interest, as in the search for ultralight dark matter or for neutrinoless double beta-decay, and for its implications in astrophysics and cosmology. Neutrino interactions with Majorons can affect the dynamics of supernovae and impact the emitted neutrino flux. Using a three-neutrino framework and detailed supernova simulations, we perform the first likelihood analysis of the 24 neutrino events from SN1987A, including nonradiative decay in matter to a massless (pseudo)scalar boson like a Majoron. Focusing on the induced spectral distortions, we present bounds on the neutrino-Majoron couplings, as a function of the lightest neutrino mass, that are either complementary or competitive with current ones.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first likelihood analysis of the 24 SN1987A neutrino events including nonradiative neutrino decay in matter to a massless Majoron, deriving bounds on neutrino-Majoron couplings that are complementary to neutrinoless double-beta decay limits and competitive with particle decay constraints, but only for the free-streaming regime where Majorons do not re-interact with the supernova medium.

## Method

The authors used modern one-dimensional supernova simulations from the Garching group (two neutron-star masses of 1.44 and 1.62 solar masses, each with four equations of state) to model the time-dependent density and electron fraction profiles. They calculated neutrino survival probabilities including two-body nonradiative decay in matter by determining the energy- and flavor-dependent energysphere and transportsphere radii, then performed a two-dimensional unbinned likelihood analysis of the 24 inverse-beta-decay events detected by Kamiokande-II, IMB, and Baksan, taking the diagonal coupling and lightest neutrino mass as fit parameters for both normal and inverted mass orderings.

## Key numbers

* The analysis uses 24 neutrino events from SN1987A: 11 from Kamiokande-II (2.14 kton), 8 from IMB (6.8 kton), and 5 from Baksan (0.28 kton), detected via inverse beta decay.
* For normal ordering, the bounds on the diagonal coupling g11 are about one order of magnitude tighter than the limits from Kachelriess (2000) based on neutrino spectra.
* The limits on sum over beta of g_{e beta}^2 improve by nearly 5 orders of magnitude over those from kaon decay from Lessa (2007) and pion decay from PIENU (2021).
* The limits on sum over beta of g_{mu beta}^2 improve by 4 orders of magnitude compared to muon decay limits from Lessa (2007).
* The limits on sum over beta of g_{tau beta}^2 improve by approximately 7 orders of magnitude compared to tau decay limits from Lessa (2007).
* The analysis uses neutrino mass-squared differences of Delta m^2_21 = 7.53 x 10^{-5} eV^2 and Delta m^2_32 = 2.437 x 10^{-3} eV^2 (normal ordering) or -2.519 x 10^{-3} eV^2 (inverted ordering).

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of neutrino decay from SN1987A by Kachelriess (2000) and Tomas (2001) by using a full three-neutrino framework instead of an effective two-flavor treatment, incorporating time-dependent supernova profiles from modern simulations that agree with SN1987A observations, and performing the first likelihood analysis of the event data. The results provide bounds on all diagonal neutrino-Majoron couplings (not just g_ee as from neutrinoless double-beta decay experiments like EXO-200 and NEMO-3) and improve limits from meson and lepton decay analyses by several orders of magnitude, while remaining complementary to the Majoron luminosity argument constraints.
