# Possibility of ΛΛpairing and its dependence on background density in relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model

> We calculate a ΛΛpairing gap in binary mixed matter of nucleons and Λhyperons within the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model. Lambda hyperons to be paired up are immersed in background nucleons in a normal state. The gap is calculated with a one-boson-exchange interaction obtained from a relativistic Lagrangian. It is found that at background density ρ_{N}=2.5ρ_{0} the ΛΛpairing gap is very small, and that denser background makes it rapidly suppressed. This result suggests a mechanism, specific to mixed matter dealt with relativistic models, of its dependence on the nucleon density. An effect of weaker ΛΛattraction on the gap is also examined in connection with revised information of the ΛΛinteraction.

## Key claim

The authors find that the $\Lambda\Lambda$ pairing gap in binary mixed matter of nucleons and $\Lambda$ hyperons is suppressed as the background nucleon density increases, which is opposite to the trend found in prior nonrelativistic studies by Balberg and Barnea (1998). This suppression is attributed to a mechanism specific to relativistic models, where the Dirac effective mass of $\Lambda$ hyperons decreases with density and selfconsistently weakens the attractive part of the pairing interaction.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) gap equation for $^{1}S_{0}$ $\Lambda\Lambda$ pairing in infinite matter, using a one-boson-exchange (OBE) interaction derived from the HS-m2 relativistic mean-field parameter set. $\Lambda$ hyperons are immersed in a background of either pure neutron matter or symmetric nuclear matter at fixed densities, with the pairing interaction including $\sigma$, $\omega$, $\sigma^{\ast}$, and $\phi$ meson exchanges and Bonn-type form factors with a cutoff mass of 7.26 fm$^{-1}$. The coupling ratio $\alpha_{\sigma^{\ast}} = g_{\sigma^{\ast}\Lambda}/g_{\sigma N}$ is varied between 0.4 and 0.6 to control the $\Lambda\Lambda$ attraction strength, with $\alpha_{\sigma^{\ast}} = 0.5$ chosen to reproduce the bond energy of about 1 MeV suggested by the NAGARA event.

## Key numbers

- At background density $\rho_{N} = 2.5\rho_{0}$ in pure neutron matter, the maximal $\Lambda\Lambda$ pairing gap is about 0.15 MeV (with $\alpha_{\sigma^{\ast}} = 0.5$).
- At $\rho_{N} = \rho_{0}$ in symmetric nuclear matter, the maximal gap is $\Delta(k_{\mathrm{F}} = 0.9\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}) \simeq 0.5$ MeV (with $\alpha_{\sigma^{\ast}} = 0.5$).
- The Dirac effective mass of $\Lambda$ hyperons decreases from 1068 MeV at $\rho_{N}=0$ to 605 MeV at $\rho_{N}=5\rho_{0}$ (for pure neutron background, $\alpha_{\sigma^{\ast}} = 0.5$).
- The cutoff mass in the Bonn-type form factor is $\Lambda_{\mathrm{c}} = 7.26\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$ for all mesons employed.

## Context

This work extends prior nonrelativistic studies of $\Lambda\Lambda$ pairing by Balberg and Barnea (1998) and Takatsuka et al. (1999), which used $\Lambda\Lambda$ interactions that are now known to be too attractive in light of the NAGARA event (Takahashi et al., 2001). The authors find that the relativistic treatment yields a qualitatively different density dependence, suppression rather than enhancement of the gap with increasing background density, due to the selfconsistent decrease of the Dirac effective mass. This result suggests that $\Lambda\Lambda$ pairing in dense neutron star matter is less likely than previously thought, which may necessitate invoking pairing of other hyperon species (e.g., $\Sigma^{-}\Sigma^{-}$ and $\Xi^{-}\Xi^{-}$ studied by Takatsuka et al., 2001) and modifying neutron star cooling scenarios.
