# A Complete Diagrammatic Implementation of the Kinoshita-Lee-Nauenberg Theorem at Next-to-Leading Order

> We show for the first time in over 50 years how to correctly apply the Kinoshita-Lee-Nauenberg theorem diagrammatically in a next-to-leading order scattering process. We improve on previous works by including all initial and final state soft radiative processes, including absorption and an infinite sum of partially disconnected amplitudes. Crucially, we exploit the Monotone Convergence Theorem to prove that our delicate rearrangement of this formally divergent series is correct. This rearrangement yields a factorization of the infinite contribution from the initial state soft photons that then cancels in the physically observable cross section. We derive the first complete next-to-leading order, high-energy Rutherford elastic scattering cross section in the $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ renormalization scheme as an explicit example of our procedure.

# A Complete Diagrammatic Implementation of the Kinoshita-Lee-Nauenberg Theorem at Next-to-Leading Order

## Key claim
The authors claim to show for the first time in over 50 years how to correctly apply the Kinoshita-Lee-Nauenberg theorem diagrammatically at next-to-leading order, but only for the specific case of high-energy Rutherford elastic scattering in QED. The claim is conditional on the use of the Monotone Convergence Theorem to justify a rearrangement of a formally divergent series, and the result yields a factorization of the infinite contribution from initial state soft photons that cancels in the physically observable cross section.

## Method
The authors perform a complete diagrammatic summation over all degenerate initial and final states for NLO Rutherford scattering of an electron off an infinitely massive point charge in QED, including all soft radiative processes (emission and absorption) and an infinite sum of partially disconnected amplitudes. They introduce a convergence factor to regulate the formally divergent series, then exploit the Monotone Convergence Theorem to prove that the order of limits (convergence factor to zero versus infinite sum over soft photons) can be safely exchanged. The calculation is performed in the $\overline{\mathrm{MS}}$ renormalization scheme with a fictitious photon mass $m_\gamma$ and non-zero electron mass $m_e$ as infrared regulators.

## Key numbers
- The NLO cross section result: $d\sigma_{NLO} = d\sigma_0 \left\{1+\frac{\alpha_e}{\pi^2}\left[\log\left(\frac{E^2}{\Delta^2}\right)\left(1-\log\left(\frac{-q^2}{\delta^2 E^2}\right)\right) +\frac{3}{2}\log\left(\frac{-q^2}{\delta^2 E^2}\right)+\frac{2}{3}\log\left(\frac{-q^2}{\mu^2_{\overline{\mathrm{MS}}}}\right)-\frac{28}{9}\right]\right\} + \frac{\pi\alpha_e^3 E}{q^2} \left(\frac{1}{|\vec{q}|}-\frac{1}{|\vec{p}|}\right)$, where $\Delta$ is the soft energy cutoff, $\delta$ is the angular cutoff, and $\mu_{\overline{\mathrm{MS}}}$ is the renormalization scale.
- The Bloch-Nordsieck result (before KLN summation) was: $d\sigma_{BN} = d\sigma_0 \left\{1+\frac{\alpha_e}{\pi^2}\left[\log\left(\frac{E^2}{\Delta^2}\right)\left(1-\log\left(\frac{-q^2}{m_e^2}\right)\right) +\frac{3}{2}\log\left(\frac{-q^2}{m_e^2}\right)+\mathcal{O}(1)\right]\right\}$.
- The factorization result: $\mathcal{P} = (\mathcal{P}_{00} + \mathcal{P}_{01})\sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{\mathcal{D}(n,n)}{(n!)^2}$, where $\mathcal{P}_{00}$ is the probability with no soft photons, $\mathcal{P}_{01}$ is the probability with one final-state soft photon, and $\mathcal{D}(n,n)$ counts disconnected photon pairs.

## Context
This work extends the original KLN theorem of Kinoshita (1962) and Lee and Nauenberg (1964) by providing the first complete diagrammatic implementation at NLO, correcting previous incomplete treatments. Prior work by Ito (1981) and Akhoury, Sotiropoulos, and Zakharov (1997) included all disconnected amplitudes but their rearrangement yielded a NLO result exactly zero at tree level, which the authors correct. The authors also improve on treatments by DeCalan (1972), Muta (1981), Lavelle (2005, 2010), and Mirza (2006) that summed over some but not all degenerate initial and final states. The result enables future generalization to all NLO QED and QCD processes and potentially to all orders, and the authors suggest it could be expanded to provide a complete proof that sums of transition rates free of infrared divergences are the only experimentally measurable ones, as noted by Weinberg (1995).
