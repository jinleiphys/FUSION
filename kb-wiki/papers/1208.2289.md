# Electromagnetic radiation in heavy ion collisions: Progress and puzzles

> We review the current state of photon and dilepton measurements at RHIC, emphasizing that of the theoretical work seeking to interpret them. We highlight the progress made recently in the modelling of relativistic nuclear collisions, and explore the effect on electromagnetic observables. Some outstanding puzzles are presented.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the current state of photon and dilepton measurements at RHIC, emphasizing that while progress has been made in modelling relativistic nuclear collisions and calculating electromagnetic emission rates, several outstanding puzzles remain, specifically, a persistent mismatch between theory and experiment for low-mass dilepton yields (models underpredict data by a factor of approximately 3 at M ~ 400 MeV) and a surprisingly large photon elliptic flow that current hydrodynamic models cannot reproduce.

## Method

The author surveys theoretical calculations of real and virtual photon emission rates from the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) using finite-temperature field theory, including leading-order (LO) and next-to-leading-order (NLO) perturbative QCD rates, as well as nonperturbative lattice QCD evaluations of the vector current-current correlation function for dileptons. These rates are then integrated over space-time using relativistic hydrodynamic simulations that incorporate viscous corrections (shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 1/4π) and fluctuating initial conditions (Monte Carlo Glauber approach), and the resulting yields and elliptic flow coefficients are compared to experimental data from the PHENIX and STAR collaborations at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- NLO corrections to QGP photon emission rates produce a modest ~20% enhancement in the region of momentum |k|/T ≈ 10.
- Effective temperature extracted from low-momentum photon data in central Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV: T = 221 ± 19(stat) ± 19(syst) MeV.
- Initial temperatures in theoretical models range from 300 < T_init (MeV) < 600, all exceeding the typical lattice QCD transition temperature.
- Low-mass dilepton enhancement factor over hadronic cocktail in Au+Au minimum bias events measured by PHENIX: 4.7 ± 0.4(stat) ± 1.5(syst) ± 0.9(model), concentrated at p_T < 1 GeV.
- Low-mass dilepton enhancement factor measured by STAR: 1.53 ± 0.07(stat) ± 0.41(syst).
- Viscous corrections to partonic photon yields produce a hardening of the spectra building up to a factor of approximately 2 at p_T ~ 4 GeV.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on electromagnetic probes of the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC, building on the hydrodynamic modelling successes summarized by Schenke (2011) and Luzum (2011) for hadronic observables. The paper highlights persistent puzzles that contradict earlier theoretical expectations: the discrepancy between PHENIX and STAR low-mass dilepton enhancement factors (which cannot be explained by acceptance effects according to Drees), and the unexpectedly large photon elliptic flow v_2 that cannot be reproduced by current relativistic hydrodynamic models (which successfully describe hadronic flow). The results enable future work combining viscous hydrodynamics with fluctuating initial conditions and improved nonperturbative emission rates from lattice QCD, as well as next-generation experimental results from RHIC and the LHC.
