# T-matrix approach to heavy quark diffusion in the QGP

> We assess transport properties of heavy quarks in the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) using static heavy-quark (HQ) potentials from lattice-QCD calculations in a Brueckner many-body T-matrix approach to evaluate elastic heavy-quark-light-quark scattering amplitudes. In the attractive meson and diquark channels resonance states are formed for temperatures up to ~1.5 T_c, increasing pertinent drag and diffusion coefficients for heavy-quark rescattering in the QGP beyond the expectations from perturbative-QCD calculations. We use these transport coefficients, complemented with perturbative elastic HQ gluon scattering, in a relativistic Langevin simulation to obtain HQ p_t distributions and elliptic flow (v_2) under conditions relevant for the hot and dense medium created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. The heavy quarks are hadronized to open-charm and -bottom mesons within a combined quark-coalescence fragmentation scheme. The resulting single-electron spectra from their semileptonic decays are confronted with recent data on "non-photonic electrons" in 100 AGeV Au-Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC).

## Key claim

Using static heavy-quark potentials from lattice-QCD calculations in a Brueckner many-body T-matrix approach, resonance states form in the attractive meson and diquark channels for temperatures up to approximately 1.5 T_c, increasing drag and diffusion coefficients for heavy-quark rescattering beyond perturbative-QCD expectations; however, the transport coefficients from the [Wo] potential parameterization *decrease* with increasing temperature because the dynamically generated resonances melt at higher temperatures, contrary to perturbative expectations.

## Method

The authors solved a three-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a Thompson-reduction scheme, using static heavy-quark potentials extracted from finite-temperature lattice-QCD calculations of the color-singlet free energy via the thermodynamic relation to internal energy. They included S-wave (l=0) and P-wave (l=1) partial waves for the complete set of color channels (singlet, octet for Q-qbar; anti-triplet, sextet for Q-q) with Casimir scaling, using three different parameterizations of the lattice-QCD free energy ([Wo], [SZ], [MR]). The resulting T-matrix scattering amplitudes were used to compute drag and diffusion coefficients for a Fokker-Planck equation, solved via a relativistic Langevin simulation in an expanding thermal fireball model, with heavy quarks hadronized to D and B mesons via a combined quark-coalescence and fragmentation scheme.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass fixed at m_c = 1.5 GeV; bottom quark mass fixed at m_b = 4.5 GeV; light quark mass fixed at m_q = 0.25 GeV
- Critical temperature T_c approximately 180 MeV
- Quasi-particle width for both light and heavy quarks: -2 Sigma_I = 0.2 GeV
- Effective number of light-quark flavors N_f = 2.5
- Equilibration time for charm quarks close to T_c: tau_eq = 1/gamma approximately 7 fm/c, a factor of about 4 larger than pQCD values
- Strong coupling constant alpha_s = 0.4 for perturbative HQ-gluon scattering
- QGP formation time of 0.33 fm/c leading to initial temperature of 340 MeV
- Fireball evolution ends after about 5 fm/c with radial velocity v_perp = 0.5 c at the surface and ellipticity v_2 = 5.5 percent
- Cross-section ratio sigma_bbar/sigma_ccbar approximately 5 x 10^{-3}
- Crossing of c- and b-decay electron spectra at p_t approximately 5 GeV
- Space-diffusion coefficient 2 pi T D_s = 4-6; viscosity to entropy-density ratio eta/s = 2-5/(4 pi)

## Context

This work extends earlier resonance-model approaches by van Hees (2004, 2005) that postulated D- and B-meson resonance excitations above T_c, by deriving the scattering amplitudes from first-principle lattice-QCD potentials rather than assuming resonance forms. The approach contradicts perturbative QCD calculations by Armesto (2005) and Wicks (2005) that required artificially tuned coupling strengths, and addresses the poor convergence of the perturbative series for the HQ diffusion coefficient noted by Caron-Huot (2007). The results enable comparison with non-photonic single-electron data from PHENIX and STAR collaborations at RHIC, and suggest a strongly coupled (liquid-like) quark-gluon plasma with eta/s values between 2/(4 pi) and 5/(4 pi), approaching the AdS/CFT bound of 1/(4 pi).
