# Electric dipole moments, present and future

> Upper limits on the electric dipole moments (EDM) of elementary particles and atoms are presented, and their physical implications are discussed. The bounds following from the neutron and atomic experiments are comparable. In particular, they strongly constrain P odd, T even interactions. The nuclear EDMs can be studied at ion storage rings, with the expected sensitivity much better than $10^{-24} e$ cm. It would be a serious progress in the studies of the CP violation.

# Electric dipole moments, present and future

## Key claim

The present experimental upper limits on electric dipole moments (EDMs) of the neutron, electron, and atoms are about six orders of magnitude above Standard Model predictions, meaning that current EDM searches are extremely sensitive to possible new physics beyond the Standard Model, rather than being mere precision spectroscopy exercises.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental upper limits on EDMs of elementary particles (neutron, electron, muon) and atoms (199Hg), then interprets these limits in terms of CP-odd quark-quark, quark-gluon, and gluon-gluon operators of low dimension. For nuclear EDMs, the author derives the effective T- and P-odd single-particle potential for a valence nucleon in the shell model and estimates the induced nuclear EDM in terms of a dimensionless constant xi characterizing the strength of CP-odd nuclear forces in units of the Fermi constant. The paper also proposes using ion storage rings to search for nuclear EDMs by monitoring spin precession via beta-decay electron asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- Neutron EDM upper limit: d_n < (6 - 10) x 10^{-26} e cm
- Electron EDM upper limit: d_e < 4 x 10^{-27} e cm (from atomic experiment with Tl)
- Muon EDM upper limit: d_mu < 10^{-18} e cm
- 199Hg atomic EDM upper limit: d(199Hg) < 9 x 10^{-28} e cm
- Standard Model prediction for neutron EDM: d_n ~ 10^{-32} - 10^{-31} e cm
- Standard Model prediction for electron EDM: d_e < 10^{-40} e cm
- Standard Model prediction for muon EDM: d_mu < 10^{-38} e cm
- Upper limit on CP-odd nuclear force constant xi from 199Hg result: xi < 2 x 10^{-3}
- Standard Model prediction for xi: xi ~ 10^{-9}
- Upper limit on T-odd, P-even (TOPE) admixture in nuclear forces from short-distance effects: alpha_T < 10^{-12}
- Expected sensitivity for muon EDM at storage ring: 10^{-24} e cm
- Estimated nuclear EDM bound corresponding to xi limit: d_N < 2 x 10^{-24} e cm

## Context

This plenary talk at PANIC99 (Uppsala, June 1999) reviews the status of EDM searches following the experimental limits from Smith et al. (1990), Altarev et al. (1992), Harris et al. (1999) for the neutron, Commins et al. (1994) for the electron, and Lamoreaux et al. (1987) for 199Hg. The analysis of CP-odd operators builds on the work of Khatsymovsky, Khriplovich, and Yelkhovsky (1988) and Khatsymovsky and Khriplovich (1992). The proposal for nuclear EDM searches at storage rings extends the muon EDM experiment proposed by Semertzidis (1998) and was first outlined by Khriplovich (1998). The paper enables future experimental programs at storage rings that could reach sensitivities of 10^{-24} e cm for nuclear EDMs, which would compete with the best present EDM results and potentially discover CP violation beyond the Standard Model.
