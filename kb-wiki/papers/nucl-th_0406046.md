# Resonating group method study of kaon-nucleon elastic scattering in the chiral SU(3) quark model

> The chiral SU(3) quark model is extended to include an antiquark in order to study the kaon-nucleon system. The model input parameters $b_u$, $m_u$, $m_s$ are taken to be the same as in our previous work which focused on the nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-hyperon interactions. The mass of the scalar meson $σ$ is chosen to be 675 MeV and the mixing of $σ_0$ and $σ_8$ is considered. Using this model the kaon-nucleon $S$ and $P$ partial waves phase shifts of isospin I=0 and I=1 have been studied by solving a resonating group method (RGM) equation. The numerical results of $S_{01}$, $S_{11}$, $P_{01}$, $P_{03}$, and $P_{11}$ partial waves are in good agreement with the experimental data while the phase shifts of $P_{13}$ partial wave are a little bit too repulsive when the laboratory momentum of the kaon meson is greater than 500 MeV in this present calculation.

## Key claim

The chiral SU(3) quark model, extended to include an antiquark, reproduces the experimental kaon-nucleon S- and P-wave phase shifts for isospin I=0 and I=1, except for the P13 partial wave which becomes too repulsive above a kaon laboratory momentum of 500 MeV. The key condition is that the scalar meson sigma mass is set to 675 MeV and ideal mixing between sigma0 and sigma8 is used, which reduces the sigma-meson attraction in the S01 channel.

## Method

The authors extended the chiral SU(3) quark model to include an antiquark and applied the resonating group method (RGM) to solve for kaon-nucleon elastic scattering phase shifts. The model uses the same harmonic-oscillator width parameter bu=0.5 fm, up/down quark mass mu=313 MeV, and strange quark mass ms=470 MeV as in their previous nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-hyperon work. The quark-quark and quark-antiquark interactions include one-gluon-exchange, confinement, and chiral-field-induced potentials (pseudoscalar and scalar meson exchanges), with the annihilation part for the u(d)-anti-s interaction included but found to have negligible effect on phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- Sigma meson mass set to 675 MeV (compared to 595 or 625 MeV in previous work)
- Harmonic-oscillator width parameter bu = 0.5 fm
- Up/down quark mass mu = 313 MeV; strange quark mass ms = 470 MeV
- Calculated baryon masses: N = 939 MeV, Sigma = 1194 MeV, Xi = 1334 MeV, Lambda = 1116 MeV, Delta = 1237 MeV, Sigma* = 1375 MeV, Xi* = 1515 MeV, Omega = 1657 MeV
- Experimental baryon masses for comparison: N = 939 MeV, Sigma = 1194 MeV, Xi = 1319 MeV, Lambda = 1116 MeV, Delta = 1237 MeV, Sigma* = 1385 MeV, Xi* = 1530 MeV, Omega = 1672 MeV
- P13 phase shifts become too repulsive when kaon laboratory momentum exceeds 500 MeV
- No KN bound state or resonance found up to kaon laboratory momentum of 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) quark model, previously successful for nucleon-nucleon and nucleon-hyperon interactions (Zhang et al., 1997; Li et al., 2003), to systems containing an antiquark for the first time. It improves significantly over the constituent quark model RGM calculation of Lemaire et al. (2003), which obtained incorrect signs for several partial waves, and over the quark potential model of Wang et al. (2003), which used an arbitrarily added color octet factor and a too-small size parameter. The results enable future study of the Theta+(1540) pentaquark state from a constituent quark model perspective, though the present calculation finds no KN bound state or resonance that could explain it.
