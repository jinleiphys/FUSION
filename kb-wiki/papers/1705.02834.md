# Flow equations for cold Bose gases

> We derive flow equations for cold atomic gases with one macroscopically populated energy level. The generator is chosen such that the ground state decouples from all other states in the system as the renormalization group flow progresses. We propose a self-consistent truncation scheme for the flow equations at the level of three-body operators and show how they can be used to calculate the ground state energy of a general $N$-body system. Moreover, we provide a general method to estimate the truncation error in the calculated energies. Finally, we test our scheme by benchmarking to the exactly solvable Lieb-Liniger model and find good agreement for weak and moderate interaction strengths.

## Key claim

The authors derive flow equations for cold Bose gases with a macroscopically populated state, using a generator that decouples the ground state from all other states as the renormalization group flow progresses, but the method is validated only for weak and moderate interaction strengths and fails for strongly interacting systems.

## Method

The authors apply the similarity renormalization group (SRG) approach to bosonic systems, normal-ordering the Hamiltonian with respect to a reference state that approximates the condensate. They truncate the flow equations at the level of three-body operators, discarding the three-body term in the commutator that contains no condensate operators, and solve the resulting closed system of differential equations to decouple the reference state from excitations. The method is benchmarked against the exactly solvable Lieb-Liniger model in one dimension using a plane-wave basis and a generator that relates the generator parameters to the Hamiltonian matrix elements at each flow step.

## Key numbers

* For N=4 particles, the SRG reproduces exact results at weak and moderate coupling, but at gamma = pi^2/2 the correction delta e accounts for about 25% of the SRG result, indicating failure of the truncation.
* For N=15 particles, the window of applicability is slightly smaller than for N=4, as the correction delta e grows with N.
* The cutoff in the one-body sector is n approximately 25, corresponding to a maximal energy of 288 pi^2 / L^2.
* The energy difference between the two lowest non-interacting states is one in the chosen units (L=2 pi), so decoupling occurs for s of order 1.

## Context

This work extends the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) method, previously developed for fermionic nuclear physics problems by Tsukiyama (2011) and reviewed by Hergert (2016), to bosonic systems with a condensate. The method enables study of trapped bosons, systems with impurities, and three-dimensional bosonic bound clusters such as helium clusters in condensed matter physics and alpha clusters in nuclear physics, but requires a good reference state and fails for strong interactions where correlations preclude a true Bose-Einstein condensate in one dimension.
