# Octet Spin Fractions and the Proton Spin Problem

> The relatively small fraction of the spin of the proton carried by its quarks presents a major challenge to our understanding of the strong interaction. Traditional efforts to explore this problem have involved new and imaginative experiments and QCD based studies of the nucleon. We propose a new approach to the problem which exploits recent advances in lattice QCD. In particular, we extract values for the spin carried by the quarks in other members of the baryon octet in order to see whether the suppression observed for the proton is a general property or depends significantly on the baryon structure. We compare these results with the values for the spin fractions calculated within a model that includes the effects of confinement, relativity, gluon exchange currents and the meson cloud required by chiral symmetry, finding a very satisfactory level of agreement given the precision currently attainable.

## Key claim
The suppression of the quark spin fraction observed in the proton is not a universal property of all baryons; the ratio of the quark spin fraction in the Σ and Ξ hyperons to that in the nucleon shows significant variation with baryon structure, and this variation is reproduced by the cloudy bag model within the current large lattice QCD uncertainties.

## Method
The authors extracted the quark spin content of octet baryons from lattice QCD simulations with 2+1 flavors of dynamical quarks, using Symanzik improved gluon action and non-perturbatively O(a) improved Wilson fermions on a 24³ × 48 volume with lattice spacing a = 0.083 ± 3 fm. They performed a chiral extrapolation of the lattice data to physical pion and kaon masses, fitting 24 data points with a formalism that includes chiral loop corrections involving π, η, and K mesons with octet and decuplet intermediate states. The lattice results were compared with predictions from the cloudy bag model that includes relativistic suppression, one-gluon-exchange current corrections, and meson cloud corrections.

## Key numbers
- The fraction of the proton spin carried by its quarks is 33 ± 3 ± 5 % when relying on SU(3) symmetry for the octet axial charge g_A⁸, increasing marginally to 36 ± 3 ± 5 % if g_A⁸ is reduced by 20 %.
- The ratio of the quark spin fraction for Σ:N is 0.92 ± 0.13 from the lattice extrapolation.
- The ratio of the quark spin fraction for Ξ:N is 1.61 ± 0.33 from the lattice extrapolation.
- The cloudy bag model predicts ratios of 0.97 (0.98 with bag radius 0.8 fm) for Σ:N and 1.49 (1.44 with bag radius 0.8 fm) for Ξ:N.
- The one-gluon-exchange correction matrix elements for bag radius 1 fm are: f_qq = -0.058, f_sq = -0.049, f_qs = -0.047, f_ss = -0.039.

## Context
This work extends the decades-long investigation of the proton spin crisis, originally discovered by the European Muon Collaboration (EMC), by proposing a new approach that examines spin fractions across the baryon octet rather than focusing solely on the proton. The results contradict the hypothesis that spin suppression is a universal property and instead support models where the suppression depends on baryon structure, specifically the cloudy bag model developed by Myhrer, Thomas, Schreiber, and others (1988-2007). The findings motivate further lattice QCD simulations with reduced statistical errors and quark masses closer to the physical region, as well as investigations of hyperon spin fractions in other theoretical models.
