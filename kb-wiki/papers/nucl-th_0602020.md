# Discrepancy between hadron matter and quark-gluon matter in net charge transfer fluctuation

## Key claim

The net charge transfer fluctuation observable κ in the hadronic final state (HM) is larger than that in the initial partonic state (QGM) by a factor of 3 to 5 in Au+Au collisions at √sNN=200 GeV, but only about twenty percent of the QGM fluctuation survives hadronization, meaning the partonic signal is largely erased by the hadronization process.

## Method

The authors used the PACIAE (Parton And Hadron Cascade) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at √sNN=200 GeV, decomposing nucleus-nucleus collisions into nucleon-nucleon collisions with parton-parton hard interactions described by leading-order pQCD and soft interactions treated empirically. The net charge transfer fluctuation κ was calculated within |η|<1 for four simulation scenarios: "HM" (hadronic transport only, with PYTHIA string fragmentation switched on), "QGM" (partonic stage only, counting partons with gluons contributing 2/3 to charge multiplicity), "HM w/ QGM" (full partonic evolution followed by hadronization and hadronic rescattering), and "QGM w/ remnant" (partonic stage including beam remnants). Hadronization was implemented using the Independent Fragmentation (Field-Feynman) model.

## Key numbers

- κ in hadronic final state (HM) is larger than κ in partonic initial state (QGM) by a factor of 3 to 5 across the pseudorapidity range within |η|<1 in 0-5% most central Au+Au collisions at √sNN=200 GeV
- The survival probability of net charge transfer fluctuation from QGM through hadronization is approximately 20%, based on the ~20% average difference between κ in "HM" and κ in "HM w/ QGM"
- The influence of beam remnants on κ in QGM amounts to approximately 35% in average for 0-5% most central Au+Au collisions at √sNN=200 GeV
- The STAR datum for κ(η=1) in Au+Au collisions at √sNN=130 GeV is approximately 0.27±0.02, which is reproduced by the "HM w/ QGM" simulation

## Context

This work extends prior studies of net charge transfer fluctuation as a QGP signal proposed by Jeon and Koch (Jeon 2000, Jeon 2001), who used a neutral cluster model and hadronic transport models (HIJING, RQMD, UrQMD) to study the observable. The PACIAE model results for the hadronic final state are consistent with those earlier hadronic transport models, but the key new finding is the direct comparison between partonic and hadronic stages within the same model framework. The results suggest that the net charge transfer fluctuation in the partonic phase is difficult to preserve through hadronization, which poses a challenge for using this observable as a clean experimental signature of the quark-gluon plasma phase transition.
