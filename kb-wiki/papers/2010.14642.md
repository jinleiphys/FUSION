# Thermodynamic properties of the trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential and applications to a quantum gas of mesons

> In this study we work out thermodynamic functions for a quantum gas of mesons described as color-electric charge dipoles. They refer to a particular parametrization of the trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential which allows to be transformed to a perturbation of free quantum motion on the three-dimensional hyper-sphere, $S^{3}$, a manifold that can host only charge-neutral systems, the charge dipoles being the configuration of the minimal number of constituents. To the amount charge neutrality manifests itself as an important aspect of the color confinement in the theory of strong interaction, the Quantum Chromodynamics, we expect our findings to be of interest to the evaluation of temperature phenomena in the physics of hadrons and in particular in a quantum gas of color charge dipoles as are the mesons. The results are illustrated for $f_0$ and $J/ψ$ mesons.

## Key claim

The authors derive thermodynamic functions for a quantum gas of mesons modeled as color-electric charge dipoles on a three-dimensional hypersphere \(S^{3}\), using a specific parametrization of the trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential where the parameter \(\bar a\) is restricted to integer values \(\bar a = l+1\) (with \(l = 0,1,2,\dots\)). This restriction, glossed over in the abstract, is essential because it allows the \(\csc^{2}(\chi)\) term to become part of the Laplace-Beltrami operator on \(S^{3}\), thereby enforcing charge neutrality and state multiplicities that the general real-\(\bar a\) version cannot address.

## Method

The authors start from the trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential \(V_{tRM}^{(\bar a,b)}(\chi)\) and, via the substitution \(\bar a \to l+1\) and \(\Phi(\chi)=U(\chi)/\sin(\chi)\), transform the one-dimensional Schrödinger equation into a free quantum motion on \(S^{3}\) perturbed by a \(\cot(\chi)\) term (the "curved Coulomb" or "color-dipole" potential). They then compute the canonical partition function \(Z(\beta,b,R)\) by summing over states with energy \(E_k = \frac{\hbar^2 c^2}{2Mc^2 R^2}\left((k+1)^2 - \frac{b^2}{(k+1)^2}\right)\) and multiplicity \(g_k = (k+1)^2\), where \(k = l+n\). The sum is approximated by an integral and evaluated analytically using integration by parts and complex error functions, yielding a closed-form expression that corrects a prior result from Blinder (1996). Thermodynamic functions (internal energy, heat capacity, Helmholtz free energy, entropy, chemical potential) are then derived from this partition function and illustrated for \(f_0(500)\) and \(J/\psi\) mesons.

## Key numbers

- The rotational temperature is defined as \(T_{rot} = \frac{\hbar c}{R}\); for the calculations, \(R\) takes values \(0.7\), \(0.56\), and \(0.4\) fm.
- The strong coupling \(\alpha_s\) takes values \(0.66\pi\), \(0.2\), and \(0.1\) associated with the three \(R\) values above.
- The charmonium (\(J/\psi\)) mass is given as \(m_{c\bar c} = 3097\) MeV.
- The QCD scale parameter is quoted as \(\Lambda_{QCD} \sim 200\) MeV.
- The low-temperature condition is specified as \(T_{rot} \gg T_c \gg T_0 = \frac{\hbar^2 c^2}{m R^2}\), equivalently \(m \gg \frac{\hbar c}{R}\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the trigonometric Rosen-Morse potential: Blinder (1996) evaluated the canonical partition function for the curved Coulomb potential alone (with \(b = \alpha Z/2\)) but used an integration range that led to a non-monotonous partition function, which the authors correct by adding a \(1/(2a)\) term. Abu-Shady (2019) calculated the partition function for a fixed real \(\bar a\) parameter, summing only over the node number \(n\) and thus missing the state multiplicities and charge-neutrality aspects that arise from integer \(\bar a\) on \(S^{3}\). The present work enables the study of thermodynamic properties of mesonic quantum gases (e.g., pion gases as in Begun (2006)) within a framework that respects color confinement via charge neutrality on a closed manifold, and it lays mathematical foundations for future investigations of phase transitions in QCD, particularly in the weak-coupling regime where \(R \ll \hbar c / \Lambda_{QCD}\).
