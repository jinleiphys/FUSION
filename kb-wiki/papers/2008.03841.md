# Breakdown of smooth solutions to the Müller-Israel-Stewart equations of relativistic viscous fluids

> We consider equations of Müller-Israel-Stewart type describing a relativistic viscous fluid with bulk viscosity in four-dimensional Minkowski space. We show that there exists a class of smooth initial data that are localized perturbations of constant states for which the corresponding unique solutions to the Cauchy problem break down in finite time. Specifically, we prove that in finite time such solutions develop a singularity or become unphysical in a sense that we make precise. We also show that in general Riemann invariants do not exist in 1+1 dimensions for physically relevant equations of state and viscosity coefficients. Finally, we present a more general version of a result by Y. Guo and A.S. Tahvildar-Zadeh: we prove large-data singularity formation results for perfect fluids under very general assumptions on the equation of state, allowing any value for the fluid sound speed strictly less than the speed of light.

## Key claim

The authors prove that smooth solutions to the Müller-Israel-Stewart equations with bulk viscosity can break down in finite time, but only for a class of *large* localized perturbations of constant states; the question of whether *small* perturbations also lead to breakdown remains open and is acknowledged as a much harder problem.

## Method

The authors consider the Müller-Israel-Stewart equations for a relativistic viscous fluid with bulk viscosity in four-dimensional Minkowski space, where the bulk viscosity \(\Pi\) is treated as an independent dynamical variable satisfying a relaxation-type equation that may include a quadratic term \(\lambda \Pi^2\). They construct a class of smooth initial data consisting of large perturbations of constant density, baryon number, and velocity (with zero bulk viscosity outside a fixed ball) and analyze the Cauchy problem to prove that the corresponding unique solutions cannot be extended as \(C^1\) solutions up to some finite time \(T_0\), at which they either develop a singularity or become acausal.

## Key numbers

* The breakdown occurs at a finite time \(T_0 > 0\) for which solutions cannot be continued as \(C^1\) solutions or become unphysical (acausal) at \(t = T_0\).
* The initial data are localized perturbations of constant states, meaning they have constant density, constant baryon number, constant velocity, and zero bulk viscosity outside a ball of fixed radius in \(\mathbb{R}^3\).
* The result applies to equations of state and viscosity coefficients for which the fluid sound speed is strictly less than the speed of light (set to 1 in the units used).

## Context

This work provides the first proof of finite-time breakdown for the Müller-Israel-Stewart equations, extending the study of singularity formation that has a long history for the relativistic Euler equations (e.g., Christodoulou's work on shock formation). The authors also present a more general version of a result by Guo and Tahvildar-Zadeh on large-data singularity formation for perfect fluids. The result enables future investigation of whether small-data solutions can exist globally, which remains an open question even for the relativistic Euler equations outside symmetry.
