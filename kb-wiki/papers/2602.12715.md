# Meson Form Factors

> We give an introduction to and a short overview of light meson form factors. We first discuss the classical picture and how it then fits in with amplitudes in quantum field theory. We give a short overview of the main theoretical methods and then discuss pion, kaon, eta and eta' form factors.

## Key claim

This paper provides a pedagogical introduction and short overview of light meson form factors, covering the classical picture, quantum field theory amplitudes, and the main theoretical methods (Chiral Perturbation Theory, dispersion relations, lattice QCD, and modelling) for pion, kaon, eta, and eta' form factors. The abstract's claim of a "short overview" is accurate, as the paper explicitly excludes detailed discussion of perturbative QCD methods (QCD sum rules and Efimov-Radyushkin-Brodsky-Lepage studies) and modelling approaches.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework for meson form factors, starting from the definition as the spatial Fourier transform of a charge distribution and generalizing to Lorentz-invariant amplitudes in quantum field theory. It then surveys the main theoretical methods: Chiral Perturbation Theory (χPT) at one- and two-loop order, dispersive methods using dispersion relations and Watson's theorem, lattice QCD calculations, and modelling approaches including Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) and resonance chiral theory. For each meson type (pion, kaon, eta, eta'), the paper discusses the specific form factors (vector, scalar, transition, and decay-related) and summarizes key results from both theory and experiment.

## Key numbers

* Pion charge radius: r_V^π = 0.659 ± 0.004 fm (from Particle Data Group 2024)
* Pion scalar radius: r_S^π = 0.78 ± 0.02 fm (from Ananthanarayan 2004)
* Charged kaon charge radius (experimental): r_V^{K+} = 0.560 ± 0.018 fm (from Particle Data Group 2024)
* Charged kaon charge radius (dispersive): r_V^{K+} = 0.599 ± 0.002 fm (from Stamen 2022)

## Context

This review extends the pedagogical tradition of form factor introductions in nuclear and particle physics, covering the same theoretical methods (χPT, dispersion relations, lattice QCD) that have been applied to light mesons since the foundational works of Gasser and Leutwyler (1983, 1984). It enables readers to understand how form factors are used to extract Standard Model parameters (such as V_ud from pion beta decay and V_us from K_ℓ3 decays) and to determine the contribution of hadronic effects to the muon anomalous magnetic moment. The paper explicitly builds on prior work including the Gounaris-Sakurai parametrization (1968), the VMD model of Sakurai (1960), and the two-loop χPT calculations of Bijnens and collaborators (1998-2007).
