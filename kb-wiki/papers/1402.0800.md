# More on Asymptotic behaviors of 9j Coefficients

> Previouslywe noted in numerical calculations that a certain unitary ninej coefficient U9j =((jj)^{2j} (jj)^{2j} | (jj)^{2j} (jj) ^{(2j-2)})^{I} decreases with increasing j and for small I the decrease is of the form C j^{m} e^{-αj} . The exponential decay dominates. It is here shown that α= 4 ln(2) and m=3/2.

# More on Asymptotic behaviors of 9j Coefficients

## Key claim
The unitary 9j coefficient U(I,j) for fixed small I decreases with increasing j as C j^(3/2) e^(-4 ln(2) j), where the exponential decay dominates; this asymptotic form holds for all even I up to I=32 provided j is taken sufficiently large, with convergence slowing as I increases.

## Method
The authors performed numerical calculations of unitary 9j coefficients U(I,j) for even I from 2 to 32 using Mathematica, extending to much higher angular momenta (up to j = 7000.5 for I=32) than previous work. They extracted the exponent m from the ratio RR = U(I,j+1)^2 / [U(I,j) U(I,j+2)] assuming the form A j^m e^(-α j). Analytically, they used the explicit formula from Varshalovich et al. relating the 9j coefficient to a Clebsch-Gordan coefficient, then applied the extended Stirling approximation (including the √(2πn) term) to derive α and m. For large I near I_max = 4j-2, they derived a power-law behavior U ∝ 1/j^n using Stirling approximations on factorial expressions.

## Key numbers
- The exponential decay parameter α = 4 ln(2) ≈ 2.7726, derived analytically using the extended Stirling approximation.
- The power-law exponent m = 3/2 (1.5) for all even I from I=2 to I=32 in the large-j limit.
- For I=2, the Clebsch-Gordan coefficient ⟨J(J-2)00|20⟩ falls off as 1/√J (where J=2j).
- For I = I_max = 4j-2, the unitary 9j coefficient approaches 1/2 as j becomes very large, given by U9j = √[(2j-1)(8j-1)]/(8j-2).
- For I = I_max - 2n with n much smaller than j, the asymptotic form is U9j = (-1)^n / [2√2 × 16^n] × √[(2n+2)!(2n)!] / (n! j^n).
- For n=1 (I = I_max - 2), the asymptotic value is √(3/2)/(8j).
- For large I, the Clebsch-Gordan coefficient goes as 1/j^(1/4) (alternatively the 3j symbol goes as 1/j^(3/4)), in contrast to the small-I case where the Clebsch-Gordan coefficient goes as 1/j^(1/2).

## Context
This work extends previous numerical studies by Zamick and Escuderos (2013) and Kleszyk and Zamick (2013) on maximum j-pairing Hamiltonians, where the smallness of this specific unitary 9j coefficient simplified the coupling matrix elements. The analytical derivation confirms the numerical observation that the exponential decay dominates, with α = 4 ln(2) and m = 3/2. The results contrast with the work of Zhao and Arima on maximum j-pairing, who considered 9j coefficients with identical J values (which do not show exponential decay), and with asymptotic formulas by Reinsch and Morehead for Clebsch-Gordan coefficients that fail for the I = I_max case where β vanishes. The work enables better understanding of wave functions for maximum J-pairing interactions, particularly the near-degeneracy of the lowest two states for small total angular momentum I.
