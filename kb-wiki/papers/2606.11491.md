# Polarized Nuclear DVCS at the EIC

> The Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) will enable a series of measurements at unprecedented energies and luminosities, providing new opportunities to investigate the microscopic structure of nucleons and nuclei at small $x_B$. Exclusive processes such as Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS) offer unique access to the three-dimensional structure of hadrons through Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs), while polarized electron and ion beams further enable detailed studies of spin-dependent structure. A model for coherent DVCS on polarized $^3$Heis developed and applied to simulations of for $9\times166$-GeV $e^3$He collisions at the EIC. Using this framework, the statistical precision achievable is estimated for measurements of beam-spin asymmetries and for the extraction of the Compton Form Factors (CFFs) $\mathcal H_{^3\mathrm{He}}$ and $\tilde{\mathcal H}_{^3\mathrm{He}}$. Early EIC data are found to enable precise differential measurements of the unpolarized CFF $\mathcal H_{^3\mathrm{He}}$ and to provide significant constraints on its real and imaginary components. By contrast, meaningful constraints on the polarized CFF $\tilde{\mathcal H}_{^3\mathrm{He}}$ require substantially larger integrated luminosities. The kinematics of the recoil $^3$He nucleus are also examined, and the far-forward detector capabilities at the EIC required to tag the intact nucleus and perform fully exclusive measurements of coherent nuclear DVCS are discussed.

## Key claim

Early EIC data with 1.5 fb$^{-1}$ of integrated luminosity will enable precise differential measurements of the unpolarized Compton Form Factor $\mathcal H_{^3\mathrm{He}}$ and provide significant constraints on its real and imaginary components, but meaningful constraints on the polarized CFF $\tilde{\mathcal H}_{^3\mathrm{He}}$ require substantially larger integrated luminosities (10 fb$^{-1}$) and are only achievable at larger values of $x_B$ and $\xi_A$.

## Method

The authors develop a model for coherent deeply virtual Compton scattering on polarized $^3$He using the impulse approximation, where nuclear Compton Form Factors are expressed as convolutions of nucleon CFFs with off-diagonal light-cone momentum distributions. The KM15 parametrization of nucleon CFFs is combined with nuclear form factors from Fourier-transformed coordinate-space nucleon densities and the nonrelativistic spectral function of Ciofi degli Atti (2005) for $^3$He. The model is implemented in a Monte Carlo event generator to produce pseudodata for $9\times166$-GeV $e$$^3$He collisions at the EIC, with acceptance cuts matching the ePIC detector (central detector $|\eta|<3.5$, $p_T>0.2$ GeV$/c$; far-forward Roman Pots $\theta<5$ mrad).

## Key numbers

- Beam-spin asymmetry $A_{LU}^{\sin\phi}$ measurements projected for $e$$^3$He at $9\times166$ GeV with 1.5 fb$^{-1}$ integrated luminosity and electron polarization $P_e=0.7$, enabling measurements up to $-t\simeq0.2$ GeV$^2$
- Projected extraction of $\Im \mathcal H_{^3\mathrm{He}}$ achievable up to $-t\sim0.2$ GeV$^2$ at several values of skewness $\xi_A$ with 1.5 fb$^{-1}$
- Projected extraction of $\Im \tilde{\mathcal H}_{^3\mathrm{He}}$ requires 10 fb$^{-1}$ integrated luminosity with ion polarization $P_h=0.7$, providing meaningful constraints only at larger $x_B$ and $\xi_A$
- Kinematic cuts: $0.01<y<0.7$, $0<-t<0.5$ GeV$^2$, $Q^2>1$ GeV$^2$; scattered $^3$He ions required to satisfy $\theta<5$ mrad
- Effective nucleon polarizations in $^3$He: $P_n^{^3\text{He}}=0.86$, $P_p^{^3\text{He}}=-0.028$ (from Bissey 2002)

## Context

This work extends the formalism for nuclear DVCS developed by Kirchner (2003) and Scopetta (2004, 2009) to polarized $^3$He targets at EIC energies, building on existing fixed-target measurements of coherent DVCS on $^4$He from CLAS (Hattawy 2017) and HERMES (PhysRevC.81.035202). The model is validated against these $^4$He data before being applied to $^3$He projections. The results enable future experimental planning for the ePIC detector at the EIC, particularly regarding the far-forward Roman Pot acceptance requirements for exclusive nuclear measurements, and provide a framework that can be extended to transverse-spin observables and incoherent nuclear DVCS for polarized-neutron tomography.
