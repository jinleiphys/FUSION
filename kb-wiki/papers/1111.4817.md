# Unruh gamma radiation at RHIC?

> Varying the proposition that acceleration itself would simulate a thermal environment, we investigate the semiclassical photon radiation as a possible telemetric thermometer of accelerated charges. Based on the classical Jackson formula we obtain the equivalent photon intensity spectrum stemming from a constantly accelerated charge and demonstrate its resemblances to a thermal distribution for high transverse momenta. The inverse transverse slope differs from the famous Unruh temperature: it is larger by a factor of pi. We compare the resulting direct photon spectrum with experimental data for AuAu collisions at RHIC and speculate about further, analytically solvable acceleration histories.

## Key claim

The classical electromagnetic radiation from a constantly accelerating point charge produces a photon spectrum with an exponential high-transverse-momentum tail that resembles a thermal distribution, but the effective temperature extracted from the slope is larger than the Unruh temperature by a factor of π, not equal to it.

## Method

The authors start from the classical Jackson formula for the radiation field amplitude of an accelerating point charge and compute the equivalent photon intensity spectrum for a charge undergoing constant proper acceleration (the "Unruh trajectory"). They evaluate the Fourier integral analytically in terms of a Bessel K-function, then compare the resulting transverse momentum spectrum to PHENIX direct photon data from Au+Au collisions at RHIC. They also consider several analytically solvable acceleration profiles beyond the constant-acceleration case.

## Key numbers

- The effective temperature extracted from the exponential tail of the constant-acceleration spectrum is T = ℏc/(2k_B ℓ) = ℏg/(2k_B c) = π T_U, where T_U is the Unruh temperature.
- For comparison with PHENIX data on 0-20% central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, the constant-acceleration model uses T_U = 135 MeV and an effective charge factor Z(b) = 55 (the average proton participant number for that centrality bin).
- The PHENIX experimental fit to the same data gives a pure exponential A exp(-p_T/T) with A = 45.2 and T = 240 MeV.
- For the constant-acceleration case, the high-k_⊥ asymptotic form of the photon spectrum is (dN)/(k_⊥ dk_⊥ dη dψ) → F · 2α_EM · (ℓ/k_⊥) · exp(-2ℓ k_⊥).
- The low-k_⊥ limit of the constant-acceleration spectrum approaches a conformal 1/k_⊥² form independent of ℓ.
- For a finite acceleration time, the thermal tail sets in above k_⊥* = π/(c Δτ) · (Δξ / sinh Δξ).

## Context

This work extends the proposition by Unruh (1976) that accelerating frames produce thermal vacua, and the application of that idea to heavy-ion collisions by Kharzeev and Satz (2000s). It provides a concrete classical electrodynamics calculation showing how thermal-looking photon spectra emerge from acceleration, without invoking thermal equilibrium or hydrodynamics. The authors note that the resulting spectrum is degenerate with predictions from Bjorken 1+1D perfect fluid hydrodynamics and from the Color Glass Condensate approach of McLerran (2010), and they argue that only systematic scaling studies across collision systems and energies can distinguish these physically different pictures.
