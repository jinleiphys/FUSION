# Search for a Ridge Structure Origin with Shower Broadening and Jet Quenching

> We investigate the role of jet and shower parton broadening by the strong colour field in the $Δη$-$Δϕ$ correlation of high $p_T$ particles. When anisotropic momentum broadening ($Δp_z > Δp_T$) is given to jet and shower partons in the initial stage, a ridge-like structure is found to appear in the two hadron correlation. The ratio of the peak to the pedestal yield is overestimated.

## Key claim

When anisotropic momentum broadening (with longitudinal kick width larger than transverse kick width) is applied to jet and shower partons in the initial glasma stage, a ridge-like structure appears in the two-hadron correlation, but the ratio of the peak to the pedestal yield is overestimated relative to experimental observations.

## Method

The authors used the Jet-Fluid String (JFS) model, which treats jet parton energy loss in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase, hadronisation through string formation between a jet parton and a fluid parton at the QGP surface, and subsequent string decay. They added an initial momentum broadening stage before thermalisation, modelled as opposite random Gaussian kicks to the two endpoints of a q-qbar string, with longitudinal width Δp_z larger than transverse width Δp_T, and then computed the Δη-Δϕ correlation of high-p_T hadrons after full space-time evolution using three-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers

- Momentum broadening widths used as a typical parameter set: (Δp_z, Δp_T) = (10 GeV/c, 2.5 GeV/c)
- Condition for ridge-like structure to appear: Δp_z ≳ 5 GeV/c and 2.5 GeV/c ≲ Δp_T < Δp_z
- High-p_T hadrons were selected with p_T > 2 GeV/c
- The saturation scale Q_s sets the time scale τ ∼ 1/Q_s for colour field decay

## Context

This work extends the Jet-Fluid String model of Isse (2007) to investigate the ridge structure observed in RHIC Δη-Δϕ correlations. It builds on proposals that momentum broadening from glasma (colour glass condensate collisions) could generate the ridge, as suggested by Majumder and Schenke. The approach contrasts with other proposals by Shuryak, Wong, and others. The overestimated peak-to-pedestal ratio indicates that further refinements are needed, such as considering position-dependent broadening or alternative hadronisation mechanisms beyond string fragmentation.
