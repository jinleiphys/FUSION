# Transverse Momentum Distribution as a Probe of J/psi Production Mechanism in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We investigate J/psi transverse momentum distribution in a transport approach. While the nuclear modification factor R_{AA}(N_p) at RHIC is almost the same as at SPS, the averaged transverse momentum square <p_t^2> and R_{AA}(p_t) are very different at SPS, RHIC and LHC and can be used to differentiate from the J/psi production mechanisms in high energy nuclear collisions.

# Transverse Momentum Distribution as a Probe of J/psi Production Mechanism in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
While the nuclear modification factor R_AA as a function of participant number is nearly identical at SPS and RHIC, the averaged transverse momentum square and the p_t-dependent R_AA differ significantly across SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies, and these differences can distinguish between initial production and regeneration as the dominant J/psi production mechanism. The key qualifier is that the competition between suppression and regeneration must be modeled via a transport equation with thermalized charm quarks to produce these distinct signatures.

## Method
The authors solve a coupled set of transport equations for J/psi, psi', and chi_c distribution functions in the transverse phase space at mid-rapidity, including loss terms from gluon dissociation (g + Psi -> c + cbar) and gain terms from regeneration via detailed balance. The initial charmonium distributions are set by geometrical superposition of nucleon-nucleon collisions including the Cronin effect and nuclear absorption, while the QGP evolution (temperature, baryon chemical potential, collective flow) is described by ideal hydrodynamics. The hadronic phase contribution to J/psi production is neglected for simplicity.

## Key numbers
- At SPS, the normalized averaged transverse momentum square <p_t^2>_AA/<p_t^2>_pp increases with participant number N_p, and R_AA(p_t) increases with p_t, consistent with dominant initial production, Cronin effect, and leakage effect.
- At LHC, <p_t^2>_AA/<p_t^2>_pp decreases with N_p and R_AA(p_t) decreases with p_t, with R_AA(p_t) > 1 at low p_t but vanishing at high p_t, indicating regeneration dominance.
- At RHIC, the behavior is intermediate between SPS and LHC, with both initial production and regeneration contributing.
- For LHC, the initially produced J/psi are "almost all eaten up" by the hot, long-lived fireball, and regeneration becomes dominant for N_p > 50; only for peripheral collisions with N_p < 50 is initial production important.
- For SPS and RHIC, R_AA(N_p) is nearly the same for semi-central and central collisions with N_p > 150, with a value around 0.6.

## Context
This work extends the transport approach of Zhu (2005) and Liu (2009) to systematically compare J/psi transverse momentum distributions across SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies. It addresses the "same suppression puzzle" noted by Matsui (1986) and observed at SPS and RHIC, which had been explained by sequential suppression (Satz), three-gluon fusion (Kharzeev), or regeneration (Zhao, Liu). The results show that while R_AA(N_p) alone cannot distinguish mechanisms, the p_t-dependent observables can, enabling future experimental discrimination at LHC and providing a test of charm quark thermalization in the QGP.
