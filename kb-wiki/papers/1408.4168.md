# To which densities is spin-polarized neutron matter a weakly interacting Fermi gas?

> We study the properties of spin-polarized neutron matter at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory, including two-, three-, and four-neutron interactions. The energy of spin-polarized neutrons is remarkably close to a non-interacting system at least up to saturation density, where interaction effects provide less than 10% corrections. This shows that the physics of neutron matter is similar to a unitary gas well beyond the scattering-length regime. Implications for energy-density functionals and for a possible ferromagnetic transition in neutron stars are discussed. Our predictions can be tested with lattice QCD, and we present results for varying pion mass.

## Key claim

The energy of spin-polarized neutron matter is remarkably close to that of a non-interacting Fermi gas at least up to saturation density, with interaction effects providing less than 10% corrections at that density; this holds well beyond the low-density regime where the large S-wave scattering length dominates.

## Method

The authors performed a complete next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO) calculation in chiral effective field theory, including two-neutron (NN), three-neutron (3N), and four-neutron (4N) interactions. NN contributions were computed up to second order in many-body perturbation theory with particle-particle/hole-hole diagrams to third order, while 3N and 4N contributions were calculated in the Hartree-Fock approximation. Three different NN potentials were used (EM 500 MeV and EGM 450/500 and 450/700 MeV) with a variation of 3N/4N cutoff between 2 and 2.5 fm$^{-1}$ to estimate uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- At saturation density $n_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$, the energy per particle of spin-polarized neutron matter is approximately 61.5 MeV for the EM 500 MeV potential, compared to 55.7 MeV for a free Fermi gas.
- For the EGM 450/500 and 450/700 MeV potentials, the energy per particle at $n_0$ is approximately 59.5 MeV and 56 MeV, respectively.
- Interaction effects provide less than 10% corrections to the free Fermi gas energy at saturation density.
- The NN contribution at $n_0$ for the EM 500 MeV potential is $E/N \approx 3.1$ MeV (5.6% relative to the free Fermi gas).
- The leading N$^2$LO 3N forces contribute $0.8 - 1.9$ MeV at $n_0$.
- The total 3N+4N contributions provide a net repulsion of $E/N = 1 - 2.2$ MeV at $n_0$.
- The P-wave scattering length fitted from the equation of state for $k_{\rm F} < 0.3$ fm$^{-1}$ is $a_{\rm P}=0.50 - 0.52$ fm.
- For spin-symmetric matter at $n_0$, the energy per particle is $E/N \approx 14 - 21$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spin-polarized neutron matter that used only NN interactions (Vida02, Bomb06, Samm06, Bord08) by including consistent 3N and 4N forces at N$^3$LO in chiral EFT. The results show that spin-polarized neutron matter behaves like a unitary gas well beyond the scattering-length regime, and provide an additional anchor point for energy-density functionals used in nuclear structure and neutron star predictions. The calculations can be tested with lattice QCD simulations, and the authors present predictions for varying pion mass to facilitate such comparisons.
