# Strong-coupling effects in a plasma of confining gluons

> The plasma consisting of confining gluons resulting from the Gribov quantization of the SU(3) Yang-Mills theory is studied using non-equilibrium fluid dynamical framework. Exploiting the Bjorken symmetry and using linear response theory a general analytic expressions for the bulk and shear viscosity coefficients are derived. It is found that the considered system exhibits a number of properties similar to the strongly-coupled theories, where the conformality is explicitly broken. In particular, it is shown that, in the large temperature limit, bulk to shear viscosity ratio, scales linearly with the difference $1/3 - c_s^2$, where $c_s$ is the speed of sound. Results obtained from the analysis are in line with the interpretation of the quark-gluon plasma as an almost perfect fluid.

## Key claim

The plasma of confining gluons described by the Gribov-Zwanziger dispersion relation exhibits bulk-to-shear viscosity scaling that matches strongly-coupled theories (via gauge-gravity duality) rather than weakly-coupled approaches, specifically showing \(\zeta/\eta = \frac{5}{2} (1/3 - c_s^2)\) in the large temperature limit, which is qualitatively different from the \((1/3 - c_s^2)^{3/2}\) scaling found for a massive Bose-Einstein plasma.

## Method

The authors used the Gribov-Zwanziger dispersion relation \(E(k) = \sqrt{k^2 + \gamma_G^4/k^2}\) for gluons in SU(3) Yang-Mills theory, where \(\gamma_G\) is the Gribov parameter controlling confinement effects. They imposed Bjorken symmetry (boost-invariant, transversally homogeneous expansion) and applied linear response theory with the relaxation time approximation to derive analytic expressions for bulk viscosity \(\zeta\) and shear viscosity \(\eta\) from small perturbations around the equilibrium distribution function.

## Key numbers

- Bulk viscosity \(\zeta\) and shear viscosity \(\eta\) are given by integrals over the Gribov distribution \(f_{GZ} = \{\exp[\gamma_G\sqrt{y^2+y^{-2}}/T]-1\}^{-1}\), with prefactors \(\frac{g_0\gamma_G^5}{3\pi^2} \frac{\tau_{\rm rel}}{T}\) and \(\frac{g_0\gamma_G^5}{30\pi^2} \frac{\tau_{\rm rel}}{T}\) respectively.
- In the large temperature limit (\(T \gg \gamma_G\)), the ratio \(\zeta/\eta = \kappa_{GZ} (1/3 - c_s^2)\) with \(\kappa_{GZ} = 5/2\).
- For the massive non-interacting Bose-Einstein plasma, the scaling is \(\zeta/\eta = \kappa_{BE} (1/3 - c_s^2)^{3/2}\) with \(\kappa_{BE} = 3\sqrt{15}/2\).
- At low temperatures, \(\zeta/\eta = 5/3\) for the Gribov-Zwanziger plasma and \(\zeta/\eta = 2/3\) for the Bose-Einstein plasma.
- Enhancement of scaled bulk viscosity occurs around the critical temperature \(T_c = 260\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Gribov-Zwanziger quantization approach (Gribov 1977, Zwanziger 1989) to non-equilibrium properties of gluon plasmas, building on finite-temperature results by Zwanziger (2004). The scaling relation \(\zeta/\eta \propto (1/3 - c_s^2)\) matches predictions from strongly-coupled theories based on gauge-gravity duality (Benincasa 2005, Buchel 2005) and disagrees with weakly-coupled approaches (Arnold 2006). The results support the interpretation of the quark-gluon plasma as an almost perfect fluid and enable improved viscous fluid dynamical modeling of heavy-ion collisions, particularly regarding bulk viscosity effects near the critical temperature.
