# The nuclear symmetry energy and other isovector observables from the point of view of nuclear structure

> In this contribution, we review some works related with the extraction of the symmetry energy parameters from isovector nuclear excitations, like the giant resonances. Then, we move to the general issue of how to assess whether correlations between a parameter of the nuclear equation of state and a nuclear observable are robust or not. To this aim, we introduce the covariance analysis and we discuss some counter-intuitive, yet enlightening, results from it.

## Key claim

The extraction of symmetry energy parameters from isovector nuclear excitations yields consistent results across different resonance types, but the correlations between observables and symmetry energy parameters are not universal and depend on the chosen model and fitting protocol.

## Method

The authors review Hartree-Fock plus Random Phase Approximation (HF-RPA) calculations of isovector giant resonances (IVGDR, IVGQR, PDR) and the total dipole polarizability in finite nuclei, using both Skyrme and relativistic mean-field energy density functionals. They then introduce covariance analysis to rigorously assess whether correlations between symmetry energy parameters and nuclear observables are robust within a given model framework, by examining how the Pearson-product correlation coefficient changes when the fitting protocol is varied.

## Key numbers

- From IVGDR analysis in 208Pb: 23.3 MeV < S(0.1) < 24.9 MeV, where S(0.1) is the symmetry energy at density 0.1 fm^-3
- From IVGQR analysis assuming J = 32 ± 1 MeV: L = 37 ± 18 MeV and ΔR(208Pb) = 0.14 ± 0.03 fm
- From PDR analysis: L = 64.8 ± 15.7 MeV and ΔR(208Pb) = 0.194 ± 0.024 fm
- From total dipole polarizability analysis assuming J = 31 ± 2 MeV: L = 43 ± 16 MeV
- Saturation density for symmetric nuclear matter: ρ0 ≈ 0.16 fm^-3

## Context

This work reviews and extends previous studies on extracting symmetry energy parameters from isovector excitations, including the hydrodynamical model of Lipparini and Stringari (1989) and the SAMi interaction set of Roca-Maza (2012). The covariance analysis methodology follows the approach introduced for nuclear theorists by Dobaczewski (2014) and applied in the authors' recent work (Colò et al., 2015). The results enable more rigorous assessment of whether correlations between symmetry energy parameters and nuclear observables are physically meaningful or artifacts of the fitting protocol, which is crucial for constraining the nuclear equation of state relevant to neutron star physics.
