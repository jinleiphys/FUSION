# Studying the onset of deconfinement with multi-messenger astronomy of neutron stars

> With the first multi-messenger observation of a binary neutron star merger (GW170817) new constraints became available for masses and radii of neutron stars. We introduce a class of hybrid EoS that fulfils all these constraints and predicts a region in the mass-radius diagram that could be populated only by hybrid neutron stars with quark matter cores. A confirmation of this conjecture would be provided when the NICER radius measurement for the high-mass pulsar PSR J0740+6620 yields a radius less than 11 km. Would this radius measurement yield a result in excess of 12 km, this would allow for both, a purely hadronic and a hybrid nature of this star. In the latter case the maximum mass could reach $2.6~M_\odot$ so that the lighter object in the asymmetric binary merger GW190814 could have been a hybrid star. We demonstrate that this high mass can be compatible with an early onset of deconfinement at star masses below $1~M_\odot$ and the occurrence of low-mass twin stars. In such a case the remnant of GW170817 could be a long-lived hypermassive pulsar.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a class of hybrid equations of state (EoS) that satisfy all current multi-messenger constraints and predicts a region in the mass-radius diagram that could be populated only by hybrid neutron stars with quark matter cores. The authors hedge that if the NICER radius measurement for the high-mass pulsar PSR J0740+6620 yields a radius less than 11 km, this would confirm the hybrid nature, but if it yields a result in excess of 12 km, both a purely hadronic and a hybrid nature remain possible.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid star EoS by matching a hadronic relativistic density-functional model (DD2 parametrization with excluded volume effects) to a constant speed of sound (CSS) quark matter model using the Maxwell construction for a first-order phase transition. They vary the bag pressure parameter B in the CSS model to generate families of hybrid EoS, solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius sequences, and identify a special point in the mass-radius diagram through which all hybrid sequences pass. They then compare these sequences against observational constraints from GW170817, GW190814, and pulsar mass measurements.

## Key numbers

- The maximum mass of hybrid stars could reach 2.6 solar masses, making the lighter object in GW190814 a possible hybrid star
- The mass of PSR J0740+6620 is measured as 2.14 (+0.10, -0.09) solar masses from Shapiro delay
- The radius at 1.4 solar masses from analysis of GW170817 is 11.0 (+0.9, -0.6) km
- The maximum mass on a hybrid star branch lies up to 0.2 solar masses above that of the special point
- Central densities at the onset of deconfinement range from 1.063 to 2.897 times nuclear saturation density n0 (where n0 = 0.15 fm^-3) depending on the hadronic EoS and onset mass
- The linear fit formula for maximum mass is M_max = M_SP + 0.1 (M_SP - M_onset)

## Context

This work extends the study of hybrid neutron stars with quark matter cores, building on the special point concept introduced by Yudin (2014) and mapped by Cierniak (2020). It directly addresses the hyperon puzzle problem, arguing that the APR EoS of Akmal (1998) is unsuitable as a soft hadronic reference due to this issue, and instead uses the EoS of Yamamoto (2017) as the softest realistic hadronic model. The paper enables future tests: the NICER radius measurement for PSR J0740+6620 can distinguish between purely hadronic and hybrid scenarios, and the next binary neutron star merger could confirm early deconfinement through postmerger gravitational wave signals as suggested by Bauswein (2018).
