# An introduction to chiral perturbation theory

> 40 pages, 16 figures, lectures held at the workshop "Physics and Astrophysics of Hadrons and Hadronic Matter", Visva Bharati University, Shantiniketan, India, November 6-11, 2006

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) is the low-energy effective field theory of QCD, valid only for energies well below the chiral symmetry breaking scale \(\Lambda_\chi \approx 1.2\) GeV, where the only dynamical degrees of freedom are the pseudoscalar Goldstone bosons (pions, kaons, eta). The theory is a perturbative expansion in small momenta and light quark masses, but it is not a fundamental theory, it fails once energies reach the resonance region (e.g., the \(\rho\) meson mass of 770 MeV).

## Method

The effective Lagrangian is constructed by writing the most general terms consistent with the chiral symmetry group \(\mathrm{SU}(3)_L \times \mathrm{SU}(3)_R\) and its spontaneous breaking to \(\mathrm{SU}(3)_V\), using a unitary matrix \(U = \exp(i\phi/F)\) to represent the Goldstone boson fields. Quark masses are introduced as spurion fields that transform under chiral rotations, and electromagnetic effects are included via spurion charge matrices \(Q_{L,R}\). The power counting orders terms by their chiral dimension \(d\) (derivatives and quark masses count as \(\mathcal{O}(p^2)\)), and loop diagrams are systematically included using Weinberg's power counting formula \(\nu = \sum_d V_d(d-2) + 2L + 2\).

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant in the chiral limit: \(F \approx F_\pi = 92.4\) MeV
- Quark mass ratio from leading-order ChPT: \(m_u/m_d \approx 0.66\) (without electromagnetism)
- Quark mass ratio with electromagnetic corrections: \(m_u/m_d \approx 0.55\)
- Quark mass ratio: \(m_s/m_d \approx 22\)
- Strong contribution to pion mass difference: \(M_{\pi^+} - M_{\pi^0} \approx 0.1\) MeV (experimental value is 4.6 MeV, dominated by electromagnetism)
- Leading-order \(\pi\pi\) s-wave scattering lengths: \(a_0^0 = 0.16\), \(a_0^2 = -0.045\)
- Chiral symmetry breaking scale: \(\Lambda_\chi \approx 4\pi F \approx 1.2\) GeV
- Gell-Mann–Okubo mass formula \(4M_K = 3M_\eta + M_\pi\) holds to 7% accuracy in nature

## Context

These lectures provide a pedagogical introduction to ChPT, building on the foundational work of Gasser and Leutwyler (1984, 1985) who established the \(\mathcal{O}(p^4)\) Lagrangian, and extending to higher orders as catalogued by Bijnens, Colangelo, and Ecker (1999). The formalism enables systematic calculations of low-energy hadronic processes such as \(\pi\pi\) scattering (Weinberg, 1966) and quark mass determinations, and it provides a rigorous effective-field-theory foundation for nuclear physics rooted in the standard model. Open questions include the treatment of baryons (where loop calculations are complicated by the non-vanishing nucleon mass in the chiral limit) and the inclusion of resonance degrees of freedom beyond the Goldstone boson sector.
