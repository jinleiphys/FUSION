# Direct photon production and jet energy-loss in small systems

> Two types of penetrating probes, direct photon and QCD jets, are investigated in the background of a small and rapidly expanding droplet of quark-gluon plasma. The additional thermal electromagnetic radiation results in a $\sim$50\% enhancement of the direct photons. In high multiplicity p+Pb collisions, jets can lose a sizeable fraction of their initial energy, leading to a charged hadron $R_\mathrm{pA}$ of $\sim$0.8 at a transverse momentum around 10\,GeV. Those two proposed measurements can help understand the apparent collective behaviour observed in small collision systems.

## Key claim

Thermal photon radiation from a small quark-gluon plasma droplet in high-multiplicity p+Pb collisions produces a ~50% enhancement of direct photons at low transverse momentum, and jets lose enough energy to suppress charged hadron production to an R_pA of ~0.8 at p_T around 10 GeV, but these effects depend on the system being near equilibrium and on selecting the most central (0-1%) collisions.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations using the iEBE-VISHNU framework with fluctuating Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions to compute thermal photon production in p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV and (p, d, 3He)+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. For jet energy loss, they used the MARTINI Monte-Carlo event generator with Pythia for initial jet generation and CTEQ6 parton distribution functions with EPS09 nuclear PDF, evolving partons through the hydrodynamic medium with medium-induced radiation and elastic collisions until hadronization at T_c = 165 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Thermal photons account for ~50% enhancement of direct photons in minimum bias p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV for p_T < 3 GeV
- The thermal enhancement increases to ~150% for 0-1% central p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV
- Charged hadron R_pA is suppressed to ~0.8 for p_T ~ 10-20 GeV in 0-1% central p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV
- Charged hadron R_pA increases to ~1 around p_T = 100 GeV in 0-1% central p+Pb collisions
- The space-time volumes of proton-induced collisions are 20 times smaller than those in Pb+Pb collisions, but the thermal signal is only smaller by about a factor of 2
- Fixed strong coupling constant alpha_s = 0.27 was used for jet-medium interactions
- Partons were evolved to T_c = 165 MeV before hadronization

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic and jet energy-loss calculations from large heavy-ion systems (Pb+Pb at LHC) to small collision systems (p+Pb, d+Au, 3He+Au) where collective behavior has been observed by ALICE (2012) and others. It builds on the iEBE-VISHNU framework developed by Shen (2014, 2015) and the MARTINI event generator by Schenke (2009). The results suggest that direct photon measurements and jet suppression in small systems could serve as additional probes of QGP formation in proton-nucleus collisions, complementing the existing flow measurements from ALICE (2012) and CMS (2015).
