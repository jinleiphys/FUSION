# Ground state spin 0$^+$ dominance of many-body systems with random interactions and related topics

> In this talk we shall show our recent results in understanding the spin$^{\rm parity}$ 0$^+$ ground state (0 g.s.) dominance of many-body systems. We propose a simple approach to predict the spin $I$ g.s. probabilities which does not require the diagonalization of a Hamiltonian with random interactions. Some findings related to the 0 g.s. dominance will also be discussed.

# Ground state spin 0⁺ dominance of many-body systems with random interactions and related topics

## Key claim

The 0⁺ ground state dominance observed in many-body systems with random two-body interactions can be predicted by a simple empirical counting method that does not require diagonalization of the Hamiltonian, and this dominance holds for most but not all single-j shells (with exceptions for a few small j values).

## Method

The authors study fermions in single-j shells using a two-body random ensemble (TBRE) Hamiltonian defined by random Gaussian-distributed two-body matrix elements G_J with width 1 and mean 0. They propose an empirical formula where only one G_J is set to -1 (all others zero) and the ground state spin I is recorded; the probability P(I) is then predicted as N_I/N, where N_I counts how many times spin I appears as ground state and N is the number of independent two-body matrix elements (N = j+1/2 for single-j fermions). This prediction is tested against results from 1000 diagonalizations of TBRE Hamiltonians for systems with four fermions in single-j shells from j=7/2 to 33/2, as well as for systems with four to seven fermions in two-j shells and sd boson systems.

## Key numbers

- For four fermions in a single-j shell, the 0 g.s. probability P(0) shows oscillations as a function of j, with values obtained from 1000 TBRE runs across j values from 7/2 to 33/2
- For four fermions in a j=9/2 shell, the empirical formula predicts P(0)=60%, P(4)=20%, P(12)=20% using only G_J=-1, while the TBRE diagonalization gives P^{exp}(0)=66.4%, P^{exp}(4)=11.8%, P^{exp}(12)=17.9%
- Including both G_J=+1 and G_J=-1 improves the prediction for j=9/2 four-fermion system to P(0)=65.8%, P(4)=11.5%, P(12)=15.8%
- For average energies of four fermions in j=9/2 shell, the TBRE gives P^{exp}(0)=10.2%, P^{exp}(2)=15.4%, P^{exp}(3)=28.9%, P^{exp}(12)=31.0%
- The total number of G_J values for j=9/2 is 5 (N=j+1/2=5)

## Context

This work extends the discovery by Johnson, Bertsch and Dean (1998) of 0⁺ ground state dominance in the two-body random ensemble, and the subsequent connection to generalized seniority by Johnson, Bertsch, Dean and Talmi (1999). The empirical formula provides a simple alternative to full diagonalization, and the authors find that the average energies ⟨Ē_I⟩_min follow a rotational-like I(I+1) trajectory. The work also reports by-products: quadrupole pairing (G_J = -δ_{J2}) favors ground states with spin I=n for even n and I=j-(n-1)/2 for odd n in single-j shells, and the highest-multipole pairing interaction produces asymptotic integer eigenvalues.
