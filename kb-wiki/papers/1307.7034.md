# How robust is a thermal photon interpretation of the ALICE low-p_T data?

> We present a rigorous theoretical analysis of the ALICE measurement of low-p_T direct-photon production in central lead-lead collisions at the LHC with a centre-of-mass energy of \sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76 TeV. Using NLO QCD, we compute the relative contributions to prompt-photon production from different initial and final states and the theoretical uncertainties coming from independent variations of the renormalisation and factorisation scales, the nuclear parton densities and the fragmentation functions. Based on different fits to the unsubtracted and prompt-photon subtracted ALICE data, we consistently find T = 304 \pm 58 MeV and 309 \pm 64 MeV for the effective temperature of the quark-gluon plasma (or hot medium) at p_T \in [0.8;2.2] GeV and p_T \in [1.5;3.5] GeV as well as a power-law (p_T^{-4}) behavior for p_T > 4 GeV as predicted by QCD hard scattering.

## Key claim

The thermal photon interpretation of the ALICE low-p_T direct-photon excess is robust only after accounting for all theoretical uncertainties from scale variations, nuclear parton densities, and fragmentation functions; the excess survives for p_T < 2.4 GeV and the bin 2.6-3.0 GeV, which is a narrower range than the ALICE collaboration originally claimed without including theoretical uncertainties.

## Method

The authors used the NLO QCD program JETPHOX 1.2 to compute prompt-photon production in central lead-lead collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.76 TeV, separating direct and fragmentation contributions. They varied the renormalisation and factorisation scales individually by a factor of two around the central scale p_T, used the EPS09 nuclear parton density functions with their 30 error sets plus the 40 error sets of CTEQ6.1M free-proton PDFs, and compared the BFG set I and set II photon fragmentation functions. The theoretical predictions were normalised to the ALICE data using the nuclear overlap function <T_PbPb>_{0-40%}=13 mb^{-1} and then subtracted from the experimental direct-photon yields to isolate the thermal component.

## Key numbers

* Effective temperature from fits to unsubtracted ALICE data: T = 304 ± 58 MeV for p_T in [0.8;2.2] GeV and T = 309 ± 64 MeV for p_T in [1.5;3.5] GeV, with errors from adding statistical and systematic uncertainties in quadrature
* Power-law (p_T^{-4}) behavior observed for p_T > 4 GeV, consistent with QCD hard scattering predictions
* Nuclear overlap function for 0-40% centrality: <T_PbPb>_{0-40%}=13 mb^{-1}
* Fragmentation contribution dominates over direct contribution by more than an order of magnitude in the lowest p_T bins
* Using BFG set I instead of set II fragmentation functions reduces the theoretical prompt-photon prediction by about a factor of three in the lowest p_T bins
* Scale variation changes the cross section by more than a factor of two up and down when scales are varied individually
* Photon excess (non-overlapping error bars) found for p_T < 2.4 GeV and the bin 2.6-3.0 GeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the ALICE collaboration (Wilde 2012) which first observed a low-p_T direct-photon signal at the LHC and extracted an inverse slope parameter of T_LHC=304±51 MeV, and compares to earlier PHENIX results (Adare 2008) which found T_RHIC=221±19±19 MeV in gold-gold collisions at RHIC. The paper enables a more rigorous separation of thermal and prompt photon contributions by quantifying the theoretical uncertainties from nuclear PDFs (EPS09, HKN07, DSSZ, nCTEQ fits) and photon fragmentation functions (BFG sets I and II), showing that the thermal interpretation is robust but the extracted temperature depends on the choice of fragmentation function and scale variation.
