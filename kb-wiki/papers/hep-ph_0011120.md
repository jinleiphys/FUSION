# QCD at Finite Density

> At sufficiently high temperature and density, quantum chromodynamics (QCD) predicts phase transition from the hadronic phase to the quark-gluon plasma phase. Lattice QCD is the most useful tool to investigate this critical phenomenon, which status is briefly reviewed. The usual problem in the Lagrangian formulation at finite density is either an incorrect continuum limit or its complex action and a premature onset of the transition as the chemical potential is raised. We show how the difficulties are overcome in our Hamiltonian approach.

# QCD at Finite Density

## Key claim

The paper claims that a Hamiltonian approach to lattice QCD at finite density avoids the persistent problems of the Lagrangian formulation, specifically the incorrect continuum limit, complex action, and premature onset of the transition, but the results shown are limited to the free fermion case and the strong coupling regime (1/g² << 1), with the strong coupling predictions derived analytically rather than from full numerical simulations.

## Method

The authors introduce the chemical potential naturally into the lattice Hamiltonian as H_μ = H − μN, then apply a unitary transformation to diagonalize the free fermion Hamiltonian and obtain the vacuum energy density. For strong coupling QCD, they derive an effective four-fermion interaction from gauge interactions, rewrite the Hamiltonian in terms of meson operators, diagonalize it via a Bogoliubov transformation, and compute the chiral condensate, quark number density, and thermal masses as functions of chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Free fermion subtracted energy density: ε_sub = μ⁴/(4π²), matching the continuum result
- Dynamical quark mass at μ=0: m_dyn⁽⁰⁾ = d/(g² C_N a), where d=3 spatial dimensions and C_N = (N_c²−1)/(2N_c)
- Critical chemical potential for chiral transition: μ_C = m_dyn⁽⁰⁾ = d/(g² C_N a)
- Chiral condensate at μ=0: ⟨ψ̅ψ⟩⁽⁰⁾ = −2N_C (1 − 4d/(g⁴ C_N²)) (1 − (N_f/N_c) I₁ − (N_f/N_c) I₂), with I₁ = 0.078354 ± 2×10⁻⁶ and I₂ = 0.235075 ± 4×10⁻⁶ for d=3
- Quark number density in chiral limit: n_q = Θ(μ − μ_C) (step function, not the Stefan-Boltzmann μ³ behavior)
- Pseudoscalar thermal mass: M_π* = 0 for μ < μ_C, M_π* = 4m_dyn⁽⁰⁾ for μ > μ_C
- Vector meson thermal mass at μ=0: M_V⁽⁰⁾ = 4√(d−1)/(ag²C_N)
- Nucleon thermal mass: M_N* = M_N⁽⁰⁾ − 3μ for μ < μ_C, with M_N⁽⁰⁾ ≈ 3m_dyn⁽⁰⁾

## Context

This work extends the Hamiltonian lattice approach of Luo (1998) to finite chemical potential, directly addressing the complex action problem that plagues Lagrangian Monte Carlo methods for QCD at finite density, a problem noted by Hasenfratz and Karsch (1983) and by the Glasgow group. The authors contrast their first-order chiral transition with a second-order result from Yaouanc (1988) in the same Hamiltonian formulation. The approach enables first-principles study of the QCD phase diagram at finite density, with potential applications to RHIC and LHC physics and neutron star phenomenology, though the authors note that higher-order 1/g² corrections and a recently proposed Hamiltonian Monte Carlo method are needed for quantitative predictions beyond strong coupling.
