# Inclusive Heavy-Flavor Production from Nuclei

> We describe a light-cone wave function formulation for hadroproduction of heavy-flavors at high energies. At moderate values of $x_F$ heavy-flavor production can be viewed as a diffractive excitation of heavy quark-antiquark Fock states, present in the interacting gluon from the projectile. The approach developed here is well suited to address coherence effects in heavy-quark production from nuclei at small values of $x_{t} \lsim 0.1\cdot A^{-1/3}$.

## Key claim

The paper presents a light-cone wave function formulation for heavy-flavor hadroproduction, arguing that at moderate values of $x_F$ the process can be viewed as diffractive excitation of heavy quark-antiquark Fock states present in the interacting projectile gluon. The approach is specifically suited to address coherence effects in heavy-quark production from nuclei at small values of $x_{t} \lsim 0.1\cdot A^{-1/3}$, where the coherence length of the $Q\bar Q$ fluctuation exceeds the nuclear size.

## Method

The authors decompose the incident projectile gluon into its Fock states, including heavy $Q\bar Q$ components described by light-cone wave functions in a mixed representation of light-cone momentum fraction $z$ and transverse separation $\vec r$. The scattering of the gluon from a nucleon target is treated via a two-gluon exchange eikonal S-matrix in impact parameter space, and the heavy-flavor production cross section is obtained by integrating the squared wave function over the $Q\bar Q$ dipole cross section. For nuclear targets, the frozen-size approximation allows diagonalization of the S-matrix, and the nuclear cross section is computed using the Glauber formalism applied to the three-parton $gQ\bar Q$ state.

## Key numbers

* For open-charm production at $E_{lab} = 800$ GeV (Fermilab E743 experiment), the model with $m_c = 1.5$ GeV and two parameterizations of the dipole cross section yields reasonable agreement with data in the region $0 < x_F < 0.5$.
* The nuclear transparency ratio $T_A$ is expressed as $T_A = 1 - \frac{3}{16 \pi r_0^2} \frac{\langle (\sigma^N_{gQ\bar Q})^2 \rangle}{\langle \sigma^N_{gQ\bar Q} \rangle} A^{1/3}$, with $r_0 \approx 1.1$ fm.
* For both dipole cross section parameterizations, the nuclear effects are small, corresponding to $\alpha \sim 0.99$ in the parameterization $T_A = A^{\alpha - 1}$.
* The WA82 experiment with a 340 GeV $\pi^-$ beam found $\alpha = 0.92 \pm 0.06$ at $\bar x_F = 0.24$; the E769 measurement with a 250 GeV $\pi^{\pm}$ beam found $\alpha = 1.00 \pm 0.05$ for $0 < x_F < 0.5$.

## Context

This work extends the light-cone wave function approach previously applied to deep-inelastic scattering and diffractive photoproduction (Nikolaev and Zakharov, 1991) to the case of heavy-flavor hadroproduction. The formalism is closely related to the color transparency and color opacity effects discussed in quasielastic electron scattering and heavy quarkonium production (Nikolaev, 1992; Piller, Mutz, and Weise, 1993). The approach enables a systematic treatment of nuclear coherence effects in heavy-quark production, showing that nuclear modifications are small for open charm at current energies, and that high-precision measurements with heavy nuclear targets would be needed to observe significant deviations from the impulse approximation.
