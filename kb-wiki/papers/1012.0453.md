# On the Quark Mass Dependence of Two Nucleon Observables

> We study the implications of lattice QCD determinations of the S-wave nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths at unphysical light quark masses. It is found that with the help of nuclear effective field theory (NEFT), not only the quark mass dependence of the effective range parameters, but also the leading quark mass dependence of all the low energy deuteron matrix elements can be obtained. The quark mass dependence of deuteron charge radius, magnetic moment, polarizability and the deuteron photodisintegration cross section are shown based on the NPLQCD lattice calculation of the scattering lengths at 354 MeV pion mass and the NEFT power counting scheme of Beane, Kaplan and Vuorinen. Further improvement can be obtained by performing the lattice calculation at smaller quark masses. Our result can be used to constrain the time variation of isoscalar combination of u and d quark mass m_q, to help the anthropic principle study to find the m_q range which allows the existence of life, and to provide a weak test of the multiverse conjecture.

## Key claim

Using nuclear effective field theory (NEFT) together with lattice QCD data for S-wave nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths at a single unphysical pion mass (354 MeV), the leading quark mass dependence of all low-energy deuteron matrix elements can be determined, but only for processes where the typical momentum is much smaller than the pion mass and within the radius of convergence of the chiral expansion.

## Method

The authors match the pionful nuclear effective field theory in the Beane-Kaplan-Vuorinen (BKV) power counting scheme to a pionless effective field theory, using the NPLQCD lattice calculation of the S-wave scattering lengths at a pion mass of 354 MeV to fix the leading quark mass dependence of the effective range parameters. They then compute the leading pion mass dependence of deuteron observables by expressing them in terms of the scattering length and effective range, whose quark mass dependence is now known, and by matching the two-body currents between the pionful and pionless theories.

## Key numbers

- Physical scattering lengths used for fixing parameters: \(a_{phys}^{({}^{3}S_{1})}=5.423\pm 0.005\) fm, \(a_{phys}^{({}^{1}S_{0})}=-23.714\pm 0.003\) fm
- Physical effective ranges used: \(r_{0,phys}^{({}^{3}S_{1})}=1.764\pm 0.002\) fm, \(r_{0,phys}^{({}^{1}S_{0})}=2.73\pm 0.03\) fm
- Lattice QCD input at \(m_{\pi}=353.7\pm 2.1\) MeV: \(a^{({}^{3}S_{1})}=0.63\pm 0.74\) fm, \(a^{({}^{1}S_{0})}=0.63\pm 0.50\) fm
- The deuteron is bound for \(m_{\pi}=106\) to 142 MeV in the \({}^{3}S_{1}\) channel (with \(\lambda=750\) MeV)
- A \({}^{1}S_{0}\) bound state exists for \(m_{\pi}=144\) to 165 MeV
- The Gamow-Teller two-body current at physical pion mass: \(L_{GT}=-4.513~\mathrm{fm}^{2}\)
- The measured cross section used for fitting: \(\sigma^{\mathrm{expt}}=334.2\pm 0.5~\mathrm{mb}\) for \(np\rightarrow d\gamma\) with incident neutrons of speed \(|v|=2200~\mathrm{m/s}\)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Beane, Kaplan and Vuorinen (2008) by using their power counting scheme to analytically match to a pionless theory, enabling the computation of the quark mass dependence of deuteron observables from a single lattice QCD calculation of scattering lengths by the NPLQCD collaboration (Beane et al., 2006). The results enable anthropic principle studies of the allowed range of quark masses for life, provide constraints on time variation of quark masses, and offer a weak test of the multiverse conjecture as discussed by Bousso (2009). Further improvement requires lattice calculations at smaller quark masses and higher-order EFT calculations to determine the radius of convergence of the chiral expansion.
