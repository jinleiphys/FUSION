# Saturation properties of helium drops from a Leading Order description

> Saturation properties are directly linked to the short-range scale of the two-body interaction of the particles. The case of helium is particular, from one hand the two-body potential has a strong repulsion at short distances. On the other hand, the extremely weak binding of the helium dimer locates this system very close to the unitary limit allowing for a description based on an effective theory. At leading order of this theory a two- and a three-body term appear, each one characterized by a low energy constant. In a potential model this description corresponds to a soft potential model with a two-body term purely attractive plus a three-body term purely repulsive constructed to describe the dimer and trimer binding energies. Here we analyse the capability of this model to describe the saturation properties making a direct link between the low energy scale and the short-range correlations. We will show that the energy per particle, $E_N/N$, can be obtained with reasonable accuracy at leading order extending the validity of this approximation, characterizing universal behavior in few-boson systems close to the unitary limit, to the many-body system.

## Key claim

The authors show that a soft-gaussian potential (SGP) model, consisting of a purely attractive two-body term and a purely repulsive three-body term fixed to reproduce the dimer and trimer binding energies, can describe the saturation properties of infinite liquid helium with reasonable accuracy at leading order, extending the universal behavior observed in few-boson systems near the unitary limit to the many-body system.

## Method

The authors construct a soft-gaussian potential (SGP) with a two-body gaussian term fitted to the dimer energy (0.83012 mK) and scattering length (235.547 a0), plus a repulsive three-body gaussian term whose strength is fixed to reproduce the trimer ground state energy (117.3 mK) for various values of the three-body range parameter ρ0. They solve the many-body Schrödinger equation for helium clusters up to N=112 using both hyperspherical harmonic (HH) expansions and diffusion Monte Carlo (DMC) methods, then extract the volume, surface, and curvature terms of the liquid-drop formula from the energy per particle.

## Key numbers

- Dimer energy: E2 = 0.83012 mK; two-body scattering length: a = 235.547 a0 (Bohr radii)
- Two-body gaussian parameters: V0 = 1.208018 K, r0 = 10.0485 a0 (with ħ²/m = 43.281307 K a0²)
- Trimer ground state energy from SGP: 139.8 mK; from HFDHE2 potential: 117.3 mK
- Optimum three-body force range: ρ0 ≈ 8.5 a0
- SGP liquid-drop fit (20 ≤ N ≤ 112): EN/N = 6.98 − 18.6 x + 10.3 x² (x = N⁻¹/³), with volume term Ev = 6.98 K
- HFDHE2 GFMC results: Ev = 7.02 K, Es = −18.8 K, Ec = 11.2 K; VMC results: Ev = 6.91 K, Es = −18.9 K, Ec = 12.0 K
- SGP infinite unit radius: r0(∞) = 2.24 Å; HFDHE2 GFMC result: 2.22 Å
- SGP surface tension: t = 0.29 K Å⁻²; experimental value: 0.27 K Å⁻²; HFDHE2 GFMC result: 0.28 K Å⁻²
- GFMC ratio E4/E3 = 4.55; derived saturation energy ratio Ev(0)/E4 = 12.8

## Context

This work extends the universal behavior observed in few-boson systems near the unitary limit (Efimov physics, as studied by Efimov (1970) and experimentally confirmed by Kraemer et al. (2006)) to the many-body regime of liquid helium. It builds on prior studies using the HFDHE2 potential by Aziz et al. (1979) and the liquid-drop formula analysis of helium droplets by Pandharipande et al. (1983), and on earlier SGP calculations for small clusters by Kievsky et al. (2011) and Gatto et al. (2011, 2012). The results enable estimation of the saturation energy of infinite helium from the energies of very light clusters (N=3,4) without requiring a four-body force at leading order, and suggest a universal ratio Ev/E4 at unitarity analogous to the Bertsch parameter for Fermi gases.
