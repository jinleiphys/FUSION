# Tunable ${\cal χ/PT}$ Symmetry in Noisy Graphene

> We investigate the resonant regime of a mesoscopic cavity made of graphene or a doped beam splitter. Using Non-Hermitian Quantum Mechanics, we consider the Bender-Boettcher assumption that a system must obey parity and time reversal symmetry. Therefore, we describe such system by coupling chirality, parity and time reversal symmetries through the scattering matrix formalism and apply it in the shot noise functions, also derived here. Finally we show how to achieve the resonant regime only by setting properly the parameters concerning the chirality and the PT symmetry.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the resonant regime of a mesoscopic cavity combining chirality (as in graphene) with parity-time (PT) symmetry can be achieved by tuning the relation between the chirality phase \(\varphi\) and the PT-symmetry phase \(\theta\), specifically when \(\varphi + 2\theta = 2\pi n\). The abstract glosses over the fact that this condition is derived for a specific four-lead setup with equivalent reflection and transmission probabilities, and that the resonance condition depends on the interplay of both symmetry parameters, not just on PT symmetry alone.

## Method
The authors construct a 4x4 scattering matrix for a graphene-like cavity (with chiral symmetry) and then couple amplifying-absorber sections to the leads to enforce PT symmetry, following the Bender-Boettcher formalism. They derive the full scattering matrix for the combined system including tunnel barriers with transmission probability \(\Gamma\), and then compute the shot noise (current correlation) functions at zero temperature and low frequency for both fermionic and bosonic statistics. The resonance condition is extracted by analyzing the denominators of the noise functions.

## Key numbers
- The resonance condition is \(\varphi + 2\theta = 2\pi n\), where \(\varphi \in [0, \pi/2]\) is the chirality phase and \(\theta\) is the phase of the amplifying/absorber section parameter \(t_0 = |t_0| e^{i\theta}\).
- For perfect chirality (\(\varphi = \pi/2\)), the resonance condition becomes \(\theta_{gph} = n\pi - \pi/4\).
- The noise function for full transmission (e.g., \((\Delta \hat{I}_3)^2\)) is proportional to \(\Gamma^4 / [1 + (1-\Gamma)^2 - 2(1-\Gamma)\cos(\varphi+2\theta)]^2\).
- The noise function for no transport (\(\langle \Delta \hat{I}_1 \Delta \hat{I}_2 \rangle\)) is \(4(1-\Gamma)^2 [1 - \cos(\varphi+2\theta)]^2 / [1 + (1-\Gamma)^2 - 2(1-\Gamma)\cos(\varphi+2\theta)]^2\).
- For small doping (\(\varphi \ll 1\)), the full-transmission noise acquires a correction factor \([1 + 2(1-\Gamma)\varphi^2/\Gamma^2]\).

## Context
This work extends the study of PT-symmetric scattering systems from the one-dimensional case (as in the HBT-PT setup of the authors' prior work) to a four-terminal graphene-like cavity that also exhibits chiral symmetry. It contrasts with the standard Hermitian HBT interferometer (Hanbury Brown and Twiss, 1956) and the electronic Hong-Ou-Mandel effect (refs. 18-20 in the paper). The results enable experimental determination of resonance conditions in PT-symmetric mesoscopic cavities by tuning the chirality and PT parameters, and provide a framework for analyzing other Cartan symmetry classes in noisy transport.
