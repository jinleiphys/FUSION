# Theoretical analysis of Lambda(1405) photoproduction

> We develop a model that describes the gamma p -> K^+ pi Sigma reaction in the Lambda(1405) region. The model consists of gauge invariant photo-production mechanisms, and the chiral unitary model that gives the rescattering amplitudes where Lambda(1405) is contained. The model also contains phenomenological parameters, associated with short-range dynamics, to be used in fitting data. We successfully fit recent CLAS data for the pi-Sigma invariant mass distributions (line-shape) in the gamma p -> K^+ pi Sigma reaction for all the charge states. We find that the higher mass pole for Lambda(1405) of the chiral unitary model plays an important role in the reaction. We also find the non-resonant background contribution is not negligible, and its sizable effect shifts the Lambda(1405) peak position by several MeV. This work sets a starting point for a fuller analysis in which line-shape as well as K^+ angular distribution data are simultaneously analyzed for extracting Lambda(1405) pole(s).

# Theoretical analysis of Lambda(1405) photoproduction

## Key claim
The higher mass pole of the Lambda(1405) at 1426-16i MeV plays a more important role than the lower mass pole in the gamma p -> K+ pi Sigma reaction, because the production mechanisms generate Kbar N more strongly than pi Sigma; however, the non-resonant background contribution is not negligible and shifts the Lambda(1405) peak position by several MeV, and the model contains phenomenological parameters that could introduce model-dependence when extracting the pole(s).

## Method
The authors constructed a model combining gauge-invariant tree-level photo-production mechanisms (including Weinberg-Tomozawa terms, Born terms, and vector-meson exchange) with the chiral unitary model for meson-baryon rescattering amplitudes that contain the Lambda(1405) as double poles. They introduced adjustable parameters including a common dipole form factor cutoff, W-dependent contact terms simulating short-range dynamics such as N* and Y* excitations, and adjusted subtraction constants for the first loop of rescattering, then fitted the model to CLAS data for pi-Sigma invariant mass distributions in all three charge states at center-of-mass energies between 1.95 and 2.85 GeV.

## Key numbers
- The higher mass pole of Lambda(1405) is located at 1426-16i MeV with dominant coupling to Kbar N; the lower mass pole is at 1390-66i MeV with strong coupling to pi Sigma (from the chiral unitary model, Jido 2003).
- The CLAS data cover center-of-mass energies 1.95 < W < 2.85 GeV for the gamma p -> K+ pi Sigma reaction.
- The non-resonant background shifts the Lambda(1405) peak position by several MeV (no specific number given for the shift magnitude).
- The model fits the pi-Sigma line-shape data at W=2.0 and 2.2 GeV for all three charge states (pi- Sigma+, pi0 Sigma0, pi+ Sigma-).

## Context
This work extends the chiral unitary model predictions of the two-pole structure of Lambda(1405) (Jido 2003, Hyodo 2011) to actual photo-production data from the CLAS collaboration (Moriya 2013). It provides the first analysis of the CLAS pi-Sigma invariant mass distributions for all charge states simultaneously, and sets a starting point for a fuller analysis that will also include K+ angular distribution data (Moriya 2013) to extract the Lambda(1405) pole(s). The authors note that the current model cannot fit the sharply rising K+ angular distribution at W=2.0 GeV for the pi0 Sigma0 channel, indicating the need for additional mechanisms in future work.
