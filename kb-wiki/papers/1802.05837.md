# Sensitivity to neutron captures and beta-decays of the enhanced s-process in rotating massive stars at low metallicities

> The s-process in massive stars, producing nuclei up to $A\approx 90$, has a different behaviour at low metallicity if stellar rotation is significant. This enhanced s-process is distinct from the s-process in massive stars around solar metallicity, and details of the nucleosynthesis are poorly known. We investigated nuclear physics uncertainties in the enhanced s-process in metal-poor stars within a Monte-Carlo framework. We applied temperature-dependent uncertainties of reaction rates, distinguishing contributions from the ground state and from excited states. We found that the final abundance of several isotopes shows uncertainties larger than a factor of 2, mostly due to the neutron capture uncertainties. A few nuclei around branching points are affected by uncertainties in the $β$-decay.

## Key claim

The enhanced s-process in rotating massive stars at low metallicity produces nuclei up to A ≈ 140, but the final abundances of several isotopes show uncertainties larger than a factor of 2, mostly due to neutron capture uncertainties, with only a few nuclei around branching points affected by beta-decay uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed Monte-Carlo simulations of nucleosynthesis in the enhanced s-process using a general reaction network, applying temperature-dependent uncertainties to both neutron-capture and beta-decay reaction rates. They distinguished contributions from ground states and excited states, using experimental uncertainties for ground-state contributions to neutron-capture rates and a factor of 2 for excited-state and purely theoretical rates. The simulations were based on a simplified evolutionary track from a 25 solar mass model that accounted for rotation-induced mixing by increasing the primary nitrogen-14 abundance.

## Key numbers

- The enhanced s-process produces nuclei up to A ≈ 140, extending beyond the standard weak s-process limit of A ≈ 90
- Final abundance uncertainties larger than a factor of 2 were found for several isotopes, with the probability density distribution value of 0.301 corresponding to a factor of 2 uncertainty
- Most isotopes show abundance uncertainties within the ±30% range, corresponding to a probability density distribution value of 0.114
- Neutron-capture reactions lead to a general 30% uncertainty in final abundances, with several isotopes having higher values up to a factor of 2
- Beta-decays affect only a few isotopes around branching points at A ≈ 94, 108, and 128
- The temperature at which the abundance pattern reaches A ≈ 140 is T = 236 MK, compared to T = 219 MK where the pattern resembles the standard weak s-process up to A = 90

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the weak s-process in massive stars (Kappeler et al. 2011) and the enhanced s-process in rotating metal-poor stars (Pignatari et al. 2008, Frischknecht et al. 2012, 2016). Prior work by Nishimura et al. (2014) had studied the impact of alpha-capture reactions on neutron-source and -poison reactions, but the present study is the first to systematically investigate the role of neutron-capture and beta-decay uncertainties on the enhanced s-process path. The Monte-Carlo framework and temperature-dependent uncertainty prescription follow the methodology of Rauscher et al. (2011, 2012). The results identify key reaction rates requiring further experimental study to improve predictions for the enhanced s-process nucleosynthesis.
