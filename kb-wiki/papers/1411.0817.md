# Charge and magnetization densities in transverse coordinate and impact parameter space

> Electromagnetic form factors obtained from the overlap of light front wave functions (LFWFs) have been used to study the transverse densities of charge and magnetization. The calculations have been carried out to develop a relation between the charge distribution of the quarks inside nucleon in the transverse coordinate space as well as in the impact parameter space. When a comparison is carried out, it is found that the transverse distribution in the impact parameter space, where the longitudinal momentum fraction $x$ can be fixed, falls off faster than the spatial distribution in the transverse coordinate space where there is some contribution from the longitudinal momentum as well. The anomalous magnetization density of the nucleon has also been discussed. Further, we have also presented the results of the QCD transverse AdS charge density inspired from the holographic QCD model.

## Key claim
The transverse charge distribution in impact parameter space (where the longitudinal momentum fraction \(x\) can be fixed) falls off faster than the spatial distribution in transverse coordinate space (where there is some contribution from longitudinal momentum as well), based on a light-front wave function model of a composite spin-1/2 system.

## Method
The authors computed Dirac and Pauli form factors from overlaps of light-front wave functions (LFWFs) for a two-particle Fock state consisting of a spin-1/2 fermion and a spin-1 vector boson, with masses \(M\), \(m\), and \(\lambda\) assigned to external fermion, internal fermion, and internal boson respectively. They then obtained the transverse charge density \(\rho(b_\perp)\) in impact parameter space via Fourier transform of the Dirac form factor, and the density distribution \(P(r_\perp)\) in transverse coordinate space by Fourier transforming the LFWFs with respect to transverse momentum. A relation \(\rho(x,\vec{b}_\perp) = \frac{1}{(1-x)^2} P\left(x, \frac{\vec{b}_\perp}{-1+x}\right)\) was derived to connect the two spaces. The anomalous magnetization density was obtained from the Pauli form factor, and the AdS charge density was computed using holographic QCD string modes.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon charge density \(\rho(b_\perp)\) peaks near \(b_\perp = 0\) and decreases as \(b_\perp\) increases (Fig. 1, units in fm\(^{-2}\)).
- For both \(u\) and \(d\) quarks, the peak of charge distribution in impact parameter space occurs around \(b_\perp \sim 0.4\) fm, while the peak of density distribution in transverse coordinate space occurs around \(r_\perp \sim 0.6\) fm.
- The anomalous magnetization density \(\tilde{\rho}_M(b_\perp)\) (with \(\phi = \pi/2\)) vanishes at \(b_\perp = 0\) and shows a maximum around \(b_\perp = 2.5\) fm, then falls off rapidly to negative values.
- AdS charge density for ground state \((L=0, k=1)\) is maximum at \(\zeta = 0\) and decreases as \(\zeta\) increases, computed with \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}} = 0.32\) GeV and \(z \le 1/\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}\).

## Context
This work extends the approach of Miller (2007) and Hwang (2007) on transverse charge densities using light-front wave functions, and builds on the GPD formalism of Diehl (2003) and Ji (1997) for impact parameter space. The model uses a dressed-quark/dressed-electron template from Brodsky (2001) and Brodsky (2002) to mimic nucleon structure. The results enable comparison between transverse coordinate space and impact parameter space densities for \(u\) and \(d\) quarks, and provide predictions for AdS/QCD charge densities that can be tested against future data from JLab 12 GeV and the Electron-Ion Collider.
