# The influence of pairing on the nuclear matrix elements of the neutrinoless double beta decays

> We study in this letter the behavior of the neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix elements (NME's) in the framework of the Interacting Shell Model. We analize them in terms of the total angular momentum of the decaying neutron pair and as a function of the seniority truncations in the nuclear wave functions. This point of view turns out to be very adequate to gauge the accuracy of the NME's predicted by different nuclear structure models. In addition, it gives back the due protagonism in this process to the pairing interaction, the interaction which is responsible for the very existence of double beta decay emitters. We show that low seniority approximations, comparable to those implicit in the quasiparticle RPA in an spherical basis, tend to overestimate the values of the NME's in several decays.

# The influence of pairing on the nuclear matrix elements of the neutrinoless double beta decays

## Key claim
The neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) are dominated by the decay of J=0 neutron pairs, and low-seniority approximations (such as those implicit in spherical quasiparticle RPA) systematically overestimate the NMEs by factors of 1.5–2 when multipole correlations in the nuclear wave functions are large, meaning the true NMEs should be much closer to the Interacting Shell Model predictions than to QRPA ones.

## Method
The authors performed large-scale Interacting Shell Model (ISM) calculations for seven candidate 0νββ emitters (A=48, 76, 82, 124, 128, 130, 136) using full valence spaces with newly built effective interactions fitted to experimental data. They decomposed the NMEs by total angular momentum J^π of the decaying neutron pair and by generalized seniority truncations, comparing their full ISM results to ISM results truncated at seniority s≤4 and to QRPA calculations from other groups.

## Key numbers
- ISM predictions for M^(0ν) (including higher-order current corrections): ⁴⁸Ca: 0.59; ⁷⁶Ge: 2.22; ⁸²Se: 2.11; ¹²⁴Sn: 2.02; ¹²⁸Te: 2.26; ¹³⁰Te: 2.04; ¹³⁶Xe: 1.76
- For ⁷⁶Ge → ⁷⁶Se, the J=0 pair contribution to M_GT^(0ν) (no hoc) is 5.59, while J>0 contributions cancel with -3.24, giving a total of 2.35
- For ¹³⁰Te → ¹³⁰Xe, the J=0 contribution is 6.58 and J>0 contributions are -4.46, giving a total of 2.12
- For a half-life bound of 10²⁵ y, the ISM NME gives an upper bound on the effective neutrino mass of 1.05 eV for ⁷⁶Ge, compared to 500 meV from QRPA; for ¹³⁰Te the corresponding bounds are 410 meV (ISM) and 270 meV (QRPA)
- The ISM NMEs cluster around M^(0ν) ≈ 2 for all cases except doubly magic ⁴⁸Ca
- Higher-order current contributions reduce the NMEs by about 15%

## Context
This work extends the critical analysis of 0νββ NME predictions by Bahcall et al. (2005), who noted large dispersion among calculations, and the subsequent work by Rodin et al. (2006), who showed that updated QRPA calculations reduce that spread. The authors provide a direct comparison between ISM and QRPA results, showing that QRPA calculations (Tu07 from Rodin et al. and Jy07 from another group) produce NMEs strikingly close to ISM results truncated at seniority s≤4, but overestimate the full ISM values by factors 1.5–2 for decays where multipole correlations are strong (A=76, 82, 128, 130). This enables more reliable upper bounds on the effective neutrino mass from experimental half-life limits, and suggests that future experiments should use ISM NMEs for planning sensitivity targets.
