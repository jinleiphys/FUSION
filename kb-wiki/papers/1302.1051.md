# Energy Deconvolution of Cross Section Measurements with an Application to the 12C(α,γ)16O Reaction

> A general framework for deconvoluting the effects of energy averaging on charged-particle reaction measurements is presented. There are many potentially correct approaches to the problem; the relative merits of some of are discussed. These deconvolution methods are applied to recent 12C(α,γ)16O measurements.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general framework for deconvoluting energy-averaging effects in charged-particle reaction measurements, arguing that multiple mathematically exact approaches exist but that the effective energy method has practical shortcomings (discontinuous behavior near cross section extrema and misleading energy assignments for thick targets) compared to mean or median energy approaches that include a correction factor.

## Method

The authors derive a general class of deconvolution procedures using a correction factor \(f\) and an assigned energy \(\tilde{E}\), with the effective energy method as a special case where \(f=1\). They apply these methods to recent \({}^{12}\mathrm{C}(\alpha,\gamma)^{16}\mathrm{O}\) angular distribution data from Assuncao (2006) and Fey (2004), using \(R\)-matrix parametrizations for the \(E1\) and \(E2\) cross sections and relative phase, incorporating center-of-mass motion corrections and a target depth profile model for carbon implanted in gold.

## Key numbers

- For the \({}^{9}\mathrm{Be}(\alpha,n)\) reaction, the correction factor for the median energy method was \(f_m \approx 0.77\) for both energy-target combinations reported by Makli (2009).
- The \({}^{9}\mathrm{Be}(\alpha,n)\) cross section varies by eight orders of magnitude over the energy range \(0.23 \le E_\alpha \le 2.7\) MeV.
- Three targets were used for \({}^{9}\mathrm{Be}(\alpha,n)\) measurements: \(5.08\times 10^{16}\) (target 1), \(1.23\times 10^{18}\) (target 2), and \(3.23\times 10^{18}\) atoms/cm\(^2\) (target 3) of \({}^{9}\mathrm{Be}\).
- For the \({}^{12}\mathrm{C}(\alpha,\gamma)^{16}\mathrm{O}\) analysis, the inclusion of center-of-mass motion effects reduced the extracted \(E2\) cross sections by 10-15% for center-of-mass energies between 2.0 and 2.6 MeV.
- The narrow \(E2\) resonance in \({}^{12}\mathrm{C}(\alpha,\gamma)^{16}\mathrm{O}\) is located at \(E_{c.m.}=2.68\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the energy deconvolution discussion initiated by Dyer and Barnes (1974) and later advocated by Lemut (2008), while correcting a mischaracterization by Lemut (2008) of the mean energy method used by Wrean (1994). The analysis enables a more complete treatment of the \({}^{12}\mathrm{C}(\alpha,\gamma)^{16}\mathrm{O}\) angular distribution data from Assuncao (2006) and Fey (2004), incorporating new interference information from Sayre (2012) and preparing the data for simultaneous \(R\)-matrix analysis.
