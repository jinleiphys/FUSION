# Dineutron correlations in quasi two-dimensional systems in a simplified model and possible relation to neutron-rich nuclei

> Two-neutron correlation in the $^1S$ channel in quasi two-dimensional (2D) neutron systems at zero temperature is studied by means of the BCS theory with finite-range effective nuclear forces. The dineutron correlation in low density neutron systems confined in an infinite slab is investigated in a simplified model that neutron motion of one direction is frozen. When the slab is thin enough, two neutrons form a tightly bound dineutron with a small size in the quasi-2D system, and a Bose dineutron gas is found in low density limit. With increase of Fermi momentum, the neutron system changes from the Bose-gas phase to the superfluid Cooper pair phase. The density dependence of the neutron pairing shows the BCS-BEC crossover phenomena at finite low-density region. In the transition region, the size shrinking of neutron pair and enhancement of pairing gap are found. The relation to dineutron correlation at surface of neutron-rich nuclei is also discussed.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the extreme confinement of valence neutrons in a quasi-two-dimensional surface layer (with the transverse degree of freedom frozen) could explain the surprisingly small size of Cooper pairs observed at the surface of neutron-rich nuclei, though they acknowledge this is an idealized model that may turn out to be academic.

## Method

The authors study neutron pairing in quasi-two-dimensional systems by assuming neutron motion in the transverse (z) direction is frozen into a Gaussian packet of width a, reducing the three-dimensional problem to a two-dimensional one. They solve the BCS equations for infinite quasi-2D neutron matter using the finite-range Gogny D1S and Minnesota effective nuclear forces, and also solve the two-body Schrodinger equation for isolated neutron pairs in the quasi-2D system. The pairing gap, pair size, and energy are calculated as functions of the Fermi momentum k_F for various slab thicknesses a.

## Key numbers

- For a slab width a=1 fm with the Gogny D1S force, the two-neutron bound state has energy E^{2D}_{nn} = -2.7 MeV and size xi^{nn}_perp = 3.5 fm, comparable to the deuteron size of 3.9 fm
- For a slab width a=4 fm with the Gogny D1S force, the two-neutron bound state has energy E^{2D}_{nn} = -0.03 MeV and size xi^{nn}_perp = 33 fm
- The pairing gap at Fermi momentum for a=1 fm with the Gogny D1S force peaks at about 4 MeV at k_F/k_0 ~ 0.4, where k_0 = 1.36 fm^{-1}
- The pairing gap at Fermi momentum for a=1 fm with the Minnesota force peaks at about 3 MeV at k_F/k_0 ~ 0.3
- The n-n scattering length obtained with the Gogny D1S force is a_s = -12.1 fm, and with the Minnesota force is a_s = -16.8 fm, compared to the experimental value a_s = -18.5 +/- 0.4 fm

## Context

This work extends studies of dineutron correlations in neutron-rich nuclei and neutron matter, particularly the BCS-BEC crossover phenomena predicted in dilute neutron matter by Matsuo (2005) and in the surface of medium-heavy nuclei by Matsuo (2004) and Pillet (2007). The authors note that in 2D matter a Cooper pair attains a minimal size of about 2 fm whereas in 3D this value is over twice as large. The study is motivated by the possibility that quasi-2D confinement could explain the strong bound-state features of Cooper pairs in nuclear surfaces, and could potentially relate to the Lasagne phase in neutron star crusts as studied by Ravenhall (1983) and Lorenz (1992).
