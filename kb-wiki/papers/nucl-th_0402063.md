# Gamow and R-matrix Approach to Proton Emitting Nuclei

> Proton emission from deformed nuclei is described within the non-adiabatic weak coupling model which takes into account the coupling to $γ$-vibrations around the axially-symmetric shape. The coupled equations are derived within the Gamow state formalism. A new method, based on the combination of the R-matrix theory and the oscillator expansion technique, is introduced that allows for a substantial increase of the number of coupled channels. As an example, we study the deformed proton emitter $^{141}$Ho.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new method combining R-matrix theory with harmonic oscillator basis expansion (RMHO) that can accurately compute resonance parameters for very narrow proton resonances in deformed nuclei, specifically demonstrating that coupling to γ-vibrations around an axially-symmetric shape (rather than assuming permanent triaxial deformation) can influence predictions for proton emission from deformed nuclei.

## Method

The authors model proton emission from deformed nuclei using a non-adiabatic weak-coupling approach where a single proton interacts with a deformed core described by the Bohr-Mottelson collective model, including both the ground-state rotational band and γ-vibrational bands of the daughter nucleus. The coupled-channel equations are derived within the Gamow state formalism, and a new computational method (RMHO) is introduced that solves the eigenvalue problem using spherical harmonic oscillator basis functions, then applies R-matrix theory at each radial point to extract resonance energies and widths from the oscillator-expanded wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The reference (exact) resonance width for the J^π=7/2^- state in ¹⁴¹Ho, calculated with axial deformation only (a₂=0) using the Gamow-state coupled-channel method, is 0.208×10⁻¹⁹ MeV
- The RMHO method achieves a relative error of less than 1.7% for the imaginary part (width) and less than 0.0025% for the real part of the resonance energy, tested over a range where the width changes by four orders of magnitude
- The ground-state rotational band energies of ¹⁴⁰Dy used in calculations are: 0.203, 0.567, 1.044, 1.597, 2.218, and 2.894 MeV for states up to I=10
- The assumed γ-vibrational band head (2⁺₂) energy is 750 keV, with band members at 0.934, 1.144, 1.378, 1.633, 1.907, 2.198, 2.504, 2.825, 3.159, and 3.507 MeV for I=3 through I=12
- The deformation parameter a₀ is set to 0.244, consistent with earlier investigations
- The experimental position of the J^π=7/2^- resonance in ¹⁴¹Ho is 1.19 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier weak-coupling calculations by Kruppa (2000), Barfield (2000), and Esbensen (2001) that described proton emission from deformed nuclei using rotational coupling but poorly reproduced the ground-state decay characteristics of ¹⁴¹Ho. The authors address the possibility that coupling to triaxial vibrations (γ-vibrations) around an axially-symmetric shape, rather than permanent γ deformation as assumed in adiabatic models by Davids (2003), can improve the description. The RMHO method enables calculations with up to 130 coupled channels (compared to 46 without γ-vibrations) and can be applied to other narrow proton resonances where direct solution of the coupled-channel Gamow equations becomes numerically unstable.
