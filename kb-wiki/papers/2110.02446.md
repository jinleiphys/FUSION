# Phenomenological developments for event-by-event fluctuations of conserved charges

> A brief overview of the recent developments concerning theoretical description of event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions is presented, with an emphasis on the role of exact conservation laws and calculations based on relativistic hydrodynamics.

# Phenomenological developments for event-by-event fluctuations of conserved charges

## Key claim
Event-by-event fluctuations of conserved charges in heavy-ion collisions can be used to probe QCD phase structure, but quantitative comparisons between theory and experiment require careful treatment of exact conservation laws, acceptance effects, and hadronic interactions, the grand-canonical ensemble commonly used in theoretical calculations does not directly apply to the finite, expanding system created in collisions.

## Method
The paper reviews recent theoretical developments including the Subensemble Acceptance Method (SAM), which provides model-independent corrections to cumulants of conserved charges for exact global conservation laws in a coordinate-space subsystem; SAM-2.0, which extends this to non-conserved quantities like net protons in momentum-space acceptance; and the subensemble sampler, a Monte Carlo generalization of Cooper-Frye particlization that incorporates local correlations from interactions (e.g., excluded volume) and exact global conservation of multiple charges, which can be combined with hadronic afterburners. These tools are applied to (3+1)D relativistic viscous hydrodynamics simulations of central Au-Au collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies.

## Key numbers
- For LHC Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, the Gaussian width of the charged particle rapidity distribution is sigma_y ≈ 3.86; at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, sigma_y ≈ 4.12 (from ALICE data).
- To establish a negative kappa_6/kappa_2 as a signal of chiral criticality, measurements in rapidity acceptances Delta y ≲ 1 are required, as only then the measurements are more sensitive to the equation of state than to baryon conservation.
- For net proton cumulant ratios in central Au-Au collisions at RHIC-BES, comparisons with STAR data show agreement at sqrt(s_NN) ≳ 20 GeV for kappa_3/kappa_1 and kappa_4/kappa_2, while at lower energies the data for S sigma^3/M indicate smaller suppression than predicted by the non-critical baseline.
- The D-measure of net charge fluctuations in 0-5% central 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions at LHC shows suppression that the subensemble sampler (with excluded volume and exact conservation) fails to quantitatively describe, possibly indicating QGP formation.
- The scaled second factorial cumulant of protons at LHC energies is suppressed by baryon annihilation in UrQMD; ALICE data disfavor notable suppression when only global baryon conservation is incorporated, but annihilation improves description when local baryon conservation with Delta y_cons = 3 is imposed.

## Context
This review extends the line of work on event-by-event fluctuations as probes of QCD phase structure, originally motivated by Stephanov (1998, 1999), Jeon and Koch (2000), and Asakawa, Heinz, and Muller (2000). It addresses the well-known issue that thermodynamic equivalence of statistical ensembles does not extend to fluctuations, as emphasized by Koch (2008). The SAM method (Vovchenko 2020, Poberezhnyuk 2020) provides a model-independent correction for exact conservation, generalizing earlier ideal-gas treatments by Begun (2004) and Bzdak (2012). The hydrodynamics calculations (Vovchenko 2021) provide non-critical baselines for RHIC-BES fluctuation measurements (STAR 2020, 2021), enabling future searches for critical point signals. The work enables quantitative comparisons between lattice QCD susceptibilities (Borsanyi 2018) and experimental fluctuation data, and provides tools to disentangle conservation effects, repulsive interactions, and potential critical signals in upcoming LHC Runs 3 and 4 and RHIC BES-II data.
