# Equilibrated fraction of QCD matter in high-energy oxygen--oxygen collisions

> We quantify to what degree the QCD matter created in high-energy oxygen--oxygen ($\mathrm{O}+\mathrm{O}$) collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.36$ TeV reaches a locally equilibrated state. For this purpose, we employ a novel framework based on the core--corona picture that describes the dynamics of both locally equilibrated fluids (the core) and nonequilibrium particles (the corona). Contributions from the core become larger than those from the corona above charged-particle multiplicity at midrapidity, $\langle dN_{\mathrm{ch}}/dη\rangle_{|η|<0.5} \approx 20$. We also find that nonnegligible contributions from the corona still remain even in central $\mathrm{O}+\mathrm{O}$ collisions. The yield ratios of strange baryons to charged pions exhibit an increasing behavior with increasing multiplicity at midrapidity. However, these ratios are smaller than those obtained when assuming that QCD matter has reached complete chemical equilibrium. These results demonstrate that a purely hydrodynamic approach is insufficient and that the inclusion of a corona component is essential for describing the dynamics of intermediate-size systems such as $\mathrm{O}+\mathrm{O}$ collisions.

## Key claim

The paper quantifies the degree of local equilibration of QCD matter in oxygen-oxygen collisions at 5.36 TeV, finding that even in the most central collisions the nonequilibrated corona component contributes approximately 30% of total hadron yields, meaning a purely hydrodynamic approach is insufficient for intermediate-size systems.

## Method

The authors employed the Dynamical Core-Corona Initialization (DCCI2) framework, which simultaneously treats locally equilibrated QCD matter (the core) via (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics and nonequilibrium partons (the corona) via PYTHIA8 Angantyr string fragmentation. Initial partons were generated with PYTHIA8 Angantyr using GLISSANDO 2 for nuclear geometry, and the core component was converted into hadrons at a switching temperature of 165 MeV using the iS3D Monte Carlo sampler, with hadronic rescatterings switched off to isolate core and corona contributions.

## Key numbers

* Core and corona contributions cross at charged-particle multiplicity at midrapidity, ⟨dNch/dη⟩|η|<0.5 ≈ 20.
* In 0-10% central O+O collisions, the corona component accounts for approximately 30% of the total hadron yield.
* Charged hadron yield at midrapidity reaches approximately 136 at 0-10% centrality.
* The pT where core dominance transitions to corona dominance is approximately 3.7 GeV at 0-30% centrality and approximately 2.8 GeV at 30-60% centrality.
* At 0-30% centrality, the core component dominates up to pT less than approximately 5 GeV for protons, approximately 3.8 GeV for kaons, and approximately 3.3 GeV for pions.
* The minimum-bias pseudorapidity distribution yields ⟨dNch/dη⟩ ≈ 40 in |η| < 2.4, comparable to preliminary CMS data.

## Context

This work extends the core-corona picture originally proposed by Werner (2007) to explain centrality dependence of strange hadron yield ratios, and applies the DCCI2 framework previously developed by Kanakubo (2019, 2021, 2022) for Pb+Pb collisions to the intermediate-size O+O system. The results demonstrate that O+O collisions at LHC energies provide a bridge between small systems (p+p, p+Pb) and large systems (Pb+Pb), enabling future systematic studies of nuclear structure effects such as alpha-clustering and nuclear deformation on final-state observables.
