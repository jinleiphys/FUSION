# The complete inverse Kohn-Sham problem: from the density to the energy

> A complete solution to the inverse problem of Kohn-Sham (KS) density functional theory is proposed. Our method consists of two steps. First, the effective KS potential is determined from the ground state density of a given system. Then, the knowledge of the potentials along a path in the space of densities is exploited in a line integration formula to determine numerically the KS energy of that system. A possible choice for the density path is proposed. A benchmark in the case of a simplified yet realistic nuclear system is shown to be successful, so that the method seems promising for future applications.

## Key claim

The authors propose a complete numerical solution to the inverse Kohn-Sham problem that reconstructs the total energy functional from the ground-state density, but the method is demonstrated only for a simplified nuclear system and the authors state it "seems promising for future applications" rather than claiming general validity.

## Method

The method combines two steps: first, the constrained variational (CV) method determines the effective Kohn-Sham potential from a given ground-state density by minimizing the kinetic energy under the constraint that the density matches a target density. Second, the potentials obtained along a one-parameter path of densities are inserted into a line integration formula to numerically reconstruct the energy differences between the corresponding density configurations. The density path is generated by applying a known external one-body potential to the system and performing constrained Hartree-Fock calculations to produce benchmark densities.

## Key numbers

- The constrained variational method reproduces the kinetic energy of the system to within numerical accuracy, as verified by the conservation law relating the kinetic energy change to the line integral of the Kohn-Sham potential (Eq. 19 in the paper).
- The benchmark system is a simplified yet realistic nuclear system, though no specific numerical values for the reconstructed energy differences are provided in the truncated text.

## Context

This work extends the inverse Kohn-Sham approach previously developed by the same group (Accorto 2020, Accorto 2021) from the density-to-potential step to the full density-to-energy reconstruction. It builds on the line integration formula proposed by van Leeuwen and Baerends (1995) and shares conceptual similarities with the scheme of Dobaczewski (2016) and Salvioni (2020), which also uses information from nuclei driven outside their ground state to probe the exact energy density functional. The method is intended to enable future extraction of nuclear energy density functionals from ab initio or experimental densities, complementing machine learning approaches to DFT (Kanungo 2019) and density functional perturbation theory (Naito 2019).
