# Hyperon resonances coupled to pseudoscalar- and vector-baryon channels

> We study hyperon resonances by solving coupled channel scattering equations. The coupled systems include pseudoscalar- and vector-baryon channels. The parameters of the model are restricted by making a $χ^2$-fit to the cross section data on processes: $K^- p \to K^- p$, $K^- p \to \bar K^0 n$, $K^- p \to ηΛ$, $K^- p \to π^0 Λ$, $K^- p \to π^0 Σ^0$, $K^- p \to π^\pm Σ^\mp$. Data on the energy level shift and width of the $1s$ state of the kaonic hydrogen, as well as some cross-section ratios near the threshold are also considered in the fit. Two types of fits are found as a result. In both cases, the properties of $Λ(1405)$ are well reproduced. In addition to this, a $Σ$ state is also found with mass around 1400 MeV. Cross sections, obtained with one of the two fits, are found to stay close to the data at energies away from the thresholds too, and as a result resonances with higher masses have also been studied.

## Key claim

The paper finds two types of fits (Fit I and Fit II) to low-energy $K^- p$ scattering data, both reproducing the $\Lambda(1405)$ properties, but the existence and precise pole positions of a low-lying isospin-1 $\Sigma$ state around 1400 MeV depend on which fit is adopted; the results are not unique.

## Method

The authors solve coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equations in an on-shell factorized form for pseudoscalar-baryon (PB) and vector-baryon (VB) channels, including $s$- and $u$-channel octet baryon exchange diagrams for the PB interactions. They perform a $\chi^2$-fit to total cross sections for six $K^- p$ reactions, the SIDDHARTA kaonic hydrogen shift and width, and three threshold cross-section ratios, using 14 subtraction constants, average meson decay constants, and the PB-VB coupling as free parameters.

## Key numbers

* Fit I and Fit II both yield $\chi^2_\text{d.o.f} \sim 1$ (0.89 and 0.91, respectively).
* The average pseudoscalar decay constant is $f_P = 94.62 \pm 1.46$ MeV (Fit I) and $97.24 \pm 1.56$ MeV (Fit II).
* The average vector decay constant is $f_v = 138.12 \pm 1.54$ MeV (Fit I) and $113.46 \pm 5.21$ MeV (Fit II).
* The PB-VB coupling constant $g_{PBVB}$ is $2.19 \pm 0.09$ (Fit I) and $1.81 \pm 0.07$ (Fit II).
* A $\Sigma$ state is found with mass around 1400 MeV, though the exact pole position is not given numerically in the text for both fits.

## Context

This work extends the earlier formalism of Khemchandani, Martinez Torres, and Oller (2012) by adding $s$- and $u$-channel octet baryon exchange diagrams to the pseudoscalar-baryon kernels, which were previously omitted. It directly addresses the ongoing debate about whether an isospin-1 partner of $\Lambda(1405)$ exists, following studies by Oller (2000), Guo (various), Wu (2009), and Moriya (2013) that found $\Sigma$ poles near 1400 MeV, and contrasts with a recent partial-wave analysis by Sadasivan (2018) that did not find such a state. The results enable future calculations of resonance-vector-baryon couplings needed for photoproduction and electroproduction of $\Lambda(1405)$.
