# Towards Study of Color Transparency with Medium Energy Electron Beams

> Interference between vector mesons electroproduced at different longitudinal coordinates leads within Glauber approximation to a $Q^2$-dependence of nuclear effects similar to what is expected to be an onset of color transparency (CT). We suggest such a mapping of the photon energies and virtualities, which eliminates the undesirable $Q^2$-variation and allows to measure a net CT effect. We develop a multichannel evolution equation, which for the first time incorporates CT and the effects of the coherence length in the exclusive electroproduction of vector mesons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $Q^2$-dependence of nuclear transparency in exclusive vector meson electroproduction, which is often interpreted as a signature of color transparency (CT), can be mimicked by interference effects within the Glauber approximation that depend on the coherence length $l_c$. The authors propose a specific mapping of photon energy $\nu$ and virtuality $Q^2$ that keeps $l_c$ constant, thereby isolating the net CT signal from this background.

## Method

The authors develop a multichannel evolution equation for the density matrix of a wave packet propagating through a nucleus, incorporating both coherence length and formation length effects for the first time in incoherent exclusive electroproduction of vector mesons. They solve the two-coupled-channel case (for vector mesons $V$ and $V'$) and compare the resulting nuclear transparency with predictions from the standard Glauber approximation. They then propose a mapping of $\nu$ and $Q^2$ that keeps $l_c = 2\nu/(m_V^2 + Q^2)$ constant, so that any remaining $Q^2$-dependence of transparency can be attributed to CT.

## Key numbers

- Coherence length is defined as $l_c \sim 1/q_c$, where $q_c = (Q^2 + m_V^2)/2\nu$ is the longitudinal momentum transfer in $\gamma^*N \to VN$.
- For the nonrelativistic oscillator model of $\rho$ and $\rho'$: $r=1.5$, $\epsilon=-0.5$, $R^2=0.074$ (at $Q^2=0$).
- For the relativized wave function model: $r=1.25$, $\epsilon=-0.14$, $R^2=0.22$ (at $Q^2=0$).
- The condition for a full CT effect at $Q^2 \gg m_V^2$ is $l_f m_V / \sqrt{Q^2} \gg R_A$, where $l_f \approx 2\nu/(m_{V'}^2 - m_V^2)$ is the formation length.
- For $(e,e'p)$ reactions, the authors estimate that the growth of nuclear transparency with $Q^2$ from inelastic shadowing corrections mimics the expected CT signal up to about $Q^2 \approx 20\ \text{GeV}^2$.

## Context

This work extends the Glauber approximation treatment of vector meson electroproduction on nuclei, previously developed by Bauer and others, by incorporating both coherence length and formation length effects in a unified multichannel evolution equation. The paper directly addresses the difficulty of observing CT in exclusive vector meson production, as the $Q^2$-dependence from coherence length variation (Glauber interference) is steep enough to mask the CT signal. The authors also warn that in $(e,e'p)$ quasielastic scattering, the growth of transparency with $Q^2$ due to inelastic shadowing corrections (Gribov, 1969) can be mistaken for CT up to $Q^2 \approx 20\ \text{GeV}^2$, making the interpretation of such measurements problematic. The proposed constant-$l_c$ mapping enables future experiments to isolate the net CT effect.
