# Mass and coupling magnetic field dependence in a scalar theory with charged bosons from an environmentally friendly renormalization group analysis

> We compute the running of the mass of a neutral boson and of its self-coupling in a simple model describing the self-interaction of three scalars, one of them neutral and the other two electrically charged, subject to the effects of a magnetic field, as functions of the field strength, at one-loop order. We resort to the Environmentally Friendly Renormalization Group approach, where the flow variable is taken as that describing the environmental conditions, in this case the strength of the magnetic field. We find the magnetic field dependent mass and coupling beta functions and use them to set up the differential equations satisfied by the neutral scalar mass and coupling. We solve the resulting system of coupled equations both numerically, and also analytically in the small-mass approximation. We find that the neutral scalar mass increases, while the coupling decreases with increasing field strength. The study is intended to set up the ideas to later use them in more sophisticated theories such as QED and QCD.

## Key claim

The neutral scalar mass increases and its self-coupling decreases as the magnetic field strength increases, at one-loop order in a scalar model with charged bosons, but the analysis is performed only for the neutral scalar and does not include the running of the charged scalar mass or fermionic interactions.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop self-energy and four-point function for a neutral scalar in a theory of three scalars (one neutral, two charged) with quartic self-interaction in a constant uniform magnetic field, using the Schwinger proper-time representation for charged scalar propagators. They apply the Environmentally Friendly Renormalization Group (EFRG) approach, taking the magnetic field strength as the flow variable, derive the beta functions for the neutral scalar mass and coupling, and solve the resulting coupled first-order differential equations both numerically and analytically in the small-mass approximation.

## Key numbers

- The coupling runs as \(\lambda(b) = \lambda_0 / [1 + (3/(4\pi^2)) \lambda_0 \ln(b/b_0)]\) in the small-mass approximation, where \(b^2 = |qB|\).
- The mass squared runs as \(m^2(b) = m_0^2 + [\ln(2)/(8\pi^2)] (b^2 - b_0^2) \lambda_0\) in the small-mass approximation, keeping terms up to \(\mathcal{O}(\lambda)\).
- Numerical solutions are shown for \(\lambda_0 = 1\) and \(m_0 = m_\pi = 0.14\) GeV at the reference scale \(b_0 = m_\pi\).

## Context

This work extends the EFRG approach of Alexandre (1997) and O'Connor (1993) to study the magnetic field dependence of masses and couplings, previously applied to QCD in thermo-magnetic environments by Ayala (2018, 2019) and to the QCD pressure by Fraga (2023). The results serve as a test ground for future application to QED and QCD, and the authors plan to extend the analysis to include the running of the charged scalar mass and fermionic interactions.
