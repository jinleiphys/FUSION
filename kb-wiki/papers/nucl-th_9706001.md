# Propagation of Charm Quarks in Equilibrating Quark Gluon Plasma

> We study the propagation of charm quarks, produced from the initial fusion of partons, in an equilibrating quark-gluon plasma which may be formed in the wake of relativistic collisions of gold nuclei. Initial conditions are taken from a self screened parton cascade model and the chemical equilibration is assumed to proceed via gluon multiplication and quark production. The energy loss suffered by the charm quarks is obtained by evaluating the drag force generated by the scattering with quarks and gluons in the medium. We find that the charm quarks may loose only about 10% of their initial energy in conditions likely to be attained at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider, while they may loose up to 40% of their energy while propagating in the plasma created at Large Hadron Collider. We discuss the implications for signals of quark gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The authors find that charm quarks produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions may lose only about 10% of their initial energy under conditions expected at RHIC, and up to 40% at LHC, but this energy loss is substantially smaller than earlier estimates because the quark-gluon plasma is far from chemical equilibrium and cools rapidly, reducing the drag force.

## Method

The authors model a longitudinally expanding quark-gluon plasma using initial conditions from a self-screened parton cascade model, with chemical equilibration governed by master equations for gluon multiplication and quark production. They compute the drag and diffusion coefficients for charm quarks scattering with light quarks and gluons in the medium using a Fokker-Planck approach generalized to a chemically non-equilibrated plasma, with thermal masses for gluons and quarks corrected for fugacities. The energy loss is then obtained by integrating the drag force over the lifetime of the QGP phase.

## Key numbers

- Charm quarks at RHIC lose up to 10% of their initial momentum during the QGP phase.
- Charm quarks at LHC lose up to 40% of their initial momentum during the QGP phase.
- The drag coefficient for charm quarks in a fully equilibrated plasma at T=500 MeV is a factor of about three smaller than the result of Svetitsky (1992) when using α_s=0.3 instead of 0.6.
- In a fully equilibrated plasma at fixed T=500 MeV, a charm quark with momentum 1–5 GeV travels for 8–10 fm before coming to rest.
- The initial thermalization time is taken as τ_i ≈ 0.2–0.3 fm/c.
- The QGP evolution is terminated when energy density reaches ε_f = 1.45 GeV/fm³.
- The nuclear thickness factor for central Au+Au collisions is T_AB(b=0) = 293.2 fm⁻².

## Context

This work extends the drag-force treatment of Svetitsky (1992) to chemically non-equilibrated plasmas, using initial conditions from the self-screened parton cascade model of Eskola, Kajantie, and Ruuskanen (1996). The results contradict earlier studies by Shuryak (1995) and Lin (1996) that assumed a constant dE/dx ≈ −2 GeV/fm for charm quarks, which the authors argue is inappropriate for charm quarks with p_T of a few GeV/c because gluon radiation is suppressed for massive quarks at these energies. The findings suggest that at RHIC, charm quarks can be treated as nearly penetrating probes, while at LHC the situation is more complex due to larger energy loss and possible effects of transverse flow.
