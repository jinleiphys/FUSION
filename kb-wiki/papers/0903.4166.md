# Nuclear modification factor of non-photonic electrons in heavy-ion collisions and the heavy-flavor baryon-to-meson ratio

> The nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ of non-photonic electrons in ${Au} + {Au}$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}^{}} = 200$ GeV is studied by considering the decays of heavy-flavor hadrons produced in a quark coalescence model. Although an enhanced $Λ_c/D^0$ ratio is predicted by the coalescence model, it is peaked at small transverse momenta ($\sim 2$ GeV) due to the large difference between heavy and light quark masses. As a result, the enhanced $Λ_c/D^0$ ratio, which is expected to suppress the electron $R_{AA}$ as the branching ratio of $Λ_c$ decay into electrons is smaller than that of $D^0$, does not lead to additional suppression of the electron $R_{AA}$ at large transverse momenta ($\ge 5$ GeV), where the suppression is mainly due to heavy quark energy loss in produced quark-gluon plasma. Also, the enhanced $Λ_b/\bar{B}^0$ ratio predicted by the coalescence model has even smaller effect on the non-photonic electron $R_{AA}$ as bottom baryons and mesons have similar branching ratios for semi-leptonic decays into electrons.

# Nuclear modification factor of non-photonic electrons in heavy-ion collisions and the heavy-flavor baryon-to-meson ratio

## Key claim

Although the quark coalescence model predicts an enhanced $\Lambda_c/D^0$ ratio, this enhancement is peaked at low transverse momenta (around 2 GeV) and does not lead to additional suppression of the non-photonic electron nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ at large transverse momenta (above 5 GeV), contrary to earlier claims that such enhancement could explain about 20% of the observed suppression.

## Method

The authors used a quark coalescence model for heavy-flavor hadron production in central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, considering both three-quark coalescence and diquark-quark coalescence mechanisms. They computed the electron spectra from semi-leptonic decays of charmed hadrons ($D^0$, $D^+$, $D_s$, $\Lambda_c$) and bottom hadrons ($B^\pm$, $B^0$, $B_s$, $\Lambda_b$), incorporating heavy quark energy loss parametrized from a three-body collisional energy loss model. The resulting electron $R_{AA}$ was compared to the case where heavy hadrons are produced via $\delta$-function fragmentation of charm quarks with the same energy loss.

## Key numbers

- The $\Lambda_c/D^0$ ratio enhancement from coalescence is peaked at $p_T \sim 2$ GeV, with the crossover between coalescence and fragmentation occurring at $p_T \sim 0.75$ GeV.
- The branching ratio for $\Lambda_c \to e$ decay is $4.5 \pm 1.7\%$, compared to $6.53 \pm 0.17\%$ for $D^0 \to e$ decay.
- For bottom hadrons, the branching ratios for semi-leptonic decays into electrons are similar: $10.1 \pm 0.4\%$ for $B^0$ and $9.9 \pm 2.6\%$ for $\Lambda_b$.
- In the coalescence model, the sum of multiplicities of $\Xi_Q$, $\Xi_Q'$, and $\Omega_Q$ is as large as $30$ to $50\%$ of the multiplicity of $\Lambda_Q$ for both charm and bottom quarks.

## Context

This work extends the quark coalescence model calculations of Oh et al. (2009) for heavy-flavor hadron production to compute the non-photonic electron $R_{AA}$, directly contradicting the assumptions of Sapeta and Wiedemann (2005) and Martinez-Garcia et al. (2007), who assumed a constant enhancement of the $\Lambda_c/D^0$ ratio at large $p_T$ and claimed this could explain about 20% of the observed suppression. The results show that the coalescence model predicts the $\Lambda_c/D^0$ enhancement only at low $p_T$, so the observed suppression of non-photonic electrons at RHIC must be explained by other mechanisms such as larger heavy quark energy loss or collisional dissociation. The work also enables future studies of multi-strange heavy baryon decays once their branching ratios become experimentally available.
