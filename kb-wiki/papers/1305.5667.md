# Direct Photon Production at RHIC and LHC-energies: Measured Data Versus a Model

> We attempt, in this paper, to deal with the direct photon production phenomena in $p+p$, $d+Au$ and $Au+Au$ collisions at RHIC energy, $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV and in $Pb+Pb$-collisions at LHC energy, $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV on the basis of a non-standard framework outlined in the text. Comparisons of the model-based results with the measured data on some observables reveal fair agreement; and they are modestly consistent with the results obtained by some other models of `Standard' framework.

# Direct Photon Production at RHIC and LHC-energies: Measured Data Versus a Model

## Key claim

The Sequential Chain Model (SCM), a non-standard framework where direct photons arise from vector-meson decays rather than from gluon Compton or quark-antiquark annihilation processes, yields fair agreement with measured direct photon spectra in p+p, d+Au, and Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy (sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV) and in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy (sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV), though the authors acknowledge that the agreement is neither sharply disagreeing nor very good compared to standard model approaches.

## Method

The authors apply the Sequential Chain Model (SCM), in which protons are treated as (pi+ pi0 spectator) composites and high-energy hadronic interactions reduce to pion-pion interactions; a rho-meson emitted by an incident pion emits a pi-meson and becomes an omega-meson, which then emits a direct photon, with the process continuing in a cascade. The inclusive cross-section formula combines a power-law constituent rearrangement factor (1/p_T^{N_R}) with an exponential term, where N_R depends on the average number of participating nucleons, and nuclear effects are incorporated via a fly-over model using Glauber techniques and Wood-Saxon distributions. The model is applied to compute p_T spectra and nuclear modification factors (R_AB) for various collision systems and centralities.

## Key numbers

- For p+p collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: E d^3 sigma/dp^3 = 0.23 * (1/p_T^{2.56}) * exp(-0.430 p_T)
- For d+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: E d^3 sigma/dp^3 = (8 x 10^{-4}) * (1/p_T^{2.67}) * exp(-0.430 p_T)
- For Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV: (1/(2 pi N_evt p_T)) dN/dp_T dy = 0.75 * (1/p_T^{3.33}) * exp(-0.505 p_T)
- Nuclear modification factor for d+Au at forward rapidities: R_AB = 1.20 p_T^{-0.11}
- Nuclear modification factor for Au+Au at forward rapidities: R_AB = 1.33 p_T^{-0.15}
- The parameter xi for direct photon production at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV is taken as 10.33
- The average number of participants <N_part> for p+p is 2 and for d+Au is approximately 179

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous SCM-based studies (Guptaroy 2008) by comparing against more recent PHENIX data published in 2012, rather than the 2004-2005 data used earlier. The model results are compared with NLO pQCD calculations (Adare 2012) and thermal approaches (Sinha), with the SCM showing disagreement at low p_T due to the dominance of the power-law constituent rearrangement factor, whereas NLO pQCD fits both low and high p_T data better. The approach enables an alternative, non-standard interpretation of direct photon production that does not rely on gluon radiation mechanisms, though the authors note the model cannot yet address collective phenomena such as elliptic flow in non-central collisions.
