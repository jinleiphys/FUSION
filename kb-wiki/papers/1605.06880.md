# A possible framework of the Lipkin model obeying the su(n)-algebra in arbitrary fermion number. II --- Two subalgebras in the su(n)-Lipkin model and an approach to the construction of linearly independent basis ---

> Standing on the results for the minimum weight states obtained in the previous paper (I), an idea how to construct the linearly independent basis is proposed for the su(n)-Lipkin model. This idea starts in setting up m independent su(2)-subalgebras in the cases with n=2m and n=2m+1 (m=2,3,4,...). The original representation is re-formed in terms of the spherical tensors for the su(n)-generators built under the su(2)-subalgebras. Through this re-formation, the su(m)-subalgebra can be found. For constructing the linearly independent basis, not only the su(2)-algebras but also the su(m)-subalgebra play a central role. Some concrete results in the cases with n=2, 3, 4 and 5 are presented.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a framework for constructing linearly independent basis states for the su(n)-Lipkin model by re-forming the original representation in terms of spherical tensors built under m independent su(2)-subalgebras, but the general construction is demonstrated only through the specific cases n = 2, 3, 4, and 5 rather than proven for arbitrary n.

## Method

The authors define m independent su(2)-subalgebras within the su(n)-Lipkin model for n = 2m (even) and n = 2m+1 (odd), then classify all su(n) generators into scalars, vectors, and spinors with respect to the total su(2)-algebra formed by summing these subalgebras. The set of scalar operators is shown to form an su(m)-subalgebra, and the Casimir operator of su(n) is diagonalized using an orthogonal transformation of the diagonal generators. The linearly independent basis is then constructed by operating raising operators from the su(2)-subalgebras on the minimum weight states.

## Key numbers

- For n = 2m, the total number of re-formed generators (excluding diagonal ones) is m(2m-1) = (1/2)n(n-1), matching the original count.
- For n = 2m+1, the total number of re-formed generators (excluding diagonal ones) is m(2m+1) = (1/2)n(n-1).
- In the case n = 2m, the diagonalized Casimir operator contains (m-1) scalar operators Q_0(m;r) for r = 1,...,m-1, with Q_0(m;m) disappearing.
- In the case n = 2m+1, the diagonalized Casimir operator contains m scalar operators, with the last term having coefficient 1/(2m+1) for Q_0(m;m).

## Context

This paper (Part II) continues the work of Tsue et al. (Part I, 2016) on the su(n)-Lipkin model, which is a classical Lie algebraic model for describing phenomena in nuclei. The work extends the su(2)- and su(3)-Lipkin models (m=1 cases) to arbitrary n by introducing multiple su(2)-subalgebras, and it enables the systematic construction of basis states for numerical diagonalization of Hamiltonians expressed as functions of the su(n) generators. The authors note that the choice of su(2)-subalgebras is not unique, as demonstrated by exchanging indices in the n=4 case.
