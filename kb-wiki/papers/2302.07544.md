# Preequilibrium cluster emission in massive transfer reactions near Coulomb barrier energy

> Within the framework of the dinuclear system model, the preequilibrium emission of neutron, proton, deuteron, triton, $^{3}$He, $α$, $^{6}$Li, $^{7}$Li, $^{8}$Be and $^{9}$Be in the transfer reactions of $^{12}$C + $^{209}$Bi, $^{40,48}$Ca+$^{238}$U, $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U and $^{238}$U+$^{248}$Cm has been systematically investigated. The production rate, kinetic energy spectra and emission angular distribution are calculated. It is found that the preequilibrium emission mechanism is associated with the reaction system and beam energy. The preequilibrium cross sections of proton, deuteron, triton and alpha are comparable in magnitude. The reaction with $^{40}$Ca is favorable for the cluster emission in comparison with $^{48}$Ca on $^{238}$U at the near barrier energy. A broad angular distribution of the preequilibrium cluster is found in the heavy systems $^{238}$U+$^{238}$U and $^{238}$U+$^{248}$Cm.

## Key claim

The preequilibrium cluster emission mechanism in massive transfer reactions is systematically investigated within the dinuclear system model, but the paper explicitly neglects the cluster preformation probability inside the DNS system by setting it to unity for all cluster species, and also neglects quasifission and fission of heavy fragments during the dissipation process.

## Method

The dinuclear system (DNS) model was used to simulate nucleon transfer between binary fragments via microscopically derived master equations that distinguish protons and neutrons, with the interaction time obtained from the deflection function method. The preequilibrium cluster emission probability was calculated from the Weisskopf evaporation widths using the uncertainty principle within each time step of 0.5×10⁻²² s, with kinetic energy spectra sampled by Monte Carlo from Watt spectra for neutrons and Boltzmann distributions for charged particles. The emission angles were determined from the deflection function method combining Coulomb and nuclear deflection, with the angular momentum dissipation and radial energy relaxation treated via exponential decay with relaxation times of 15×10⁻²² s and 5×10⁻²² s, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the dinuclear system model previously applied to fusion-evaporation and multi-nucleon transfer reactions by Feng and collaborators (2006, 2007, 2009) to systematically include preequilibrium cluster emission for ten different cluster species. The approach contrasts with the exciton model of Blann (1975) and Gadioli (1977), Langevin equation methods of Jin (2007), and quantum molecular dynamics models of Chen (2021), by treating cluster emission from DNS fragments during the nucleon transfer process rather than from compound nucleus decay. The results enable improved predictions for the synthesis of superheavy nuclei and neutron-rich isotopes via multi-nucleon transfer reactions, and provide guidance for detector system design in experiments measuring cluster angular distributions.
