# DIS2021 Workshop Proceedings: Backward-Angle (u-Channel) Meson Production from JLab 12 GeV Hall C to EIC

> The recent exclusive backward-angle electroproduction of omega from Jefferson Lab Hall C electron-proton fixed-target scattering experiments above the resonance region hints at a new domain of applicability of QCD factorization in a unique u-channel kinematics regime. Thanks to this effort, the interest in studying nucleon structure through u-channel meson production observables has grown significantly. In the fixed target configuration, the u-channel meson electroproduction observables feature a unique interaction picture: the target proton absorbs nearly all momentum induced by virtual photons and recoils forward, while the produced mesons (such as omega or pions) are left behind almost at rest near the target station. In this presentation, We provide a summary of the key observations of the existing u-channel meson production results, update-to-date theory insights, and a path to further exploration from JLab 12 GeV Hall C program to the future Electron-Ion Colliders.

## Key claim

The recent exclusive backward-angle electroproduction of omega from Jefferson Lab Hall C experiments above the resonance region hints at a new domain of applicability of QCD factorization in a unique u-channel kinematics regime, but the claim is conditional on further validation of the Transition Distributions Amplitudes (TDA) framework through systematic measurements across multiple meson channels and kinematic ranges.

## Method

The paper summarizes experimental results from JLab Hall C fixed-target electron-proton scattering experiments using the missing mass reconstruction technique to resolve produced nucleons, while Hall B and D directly detect decayed mesons. The theoretical framework employs baryon-to-meson Transition Distributions Amplitudes (TDAs), which describe how the target proton transitions into a meson during the final state, requiring three parton exchanges between the pi-N TDA and collinear factorization in backward angle kinematics where -t approaches -t_max and -u approaches -u_min, with t greater than Q^2 and W greater than 2 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Invariant mass W is chosen above the nucleon resonance region: W greater than 2 GeV
- KaonLT experiment (E12-09-011) data taken at W = 2.32 to 3.02 GeV, at Q^2 = 0.5 to 5.50 GeV^2, with two beam energies corresponding to Delta-epsilon approximately 0.25 for L/T/LT/TT separations
- Approved JLab E12-20-007 experiment aims to probe the H(e,e'p)pi0 reaction over Q^2 = 2 to 6.25 GeV^2 at fixed W = 3.1 GeV (s = 10 GeV^2) and -u_min
- EIC feasibility study shows optimal collision energy: 5 GeV electron beam on 100 GeV proton for fixed s = 10 GeV^2 at u approximately u_min
- EIC projected 20 to 30 percent double-gamma detection efficiency for pi0 at 40 to 60 GeV/c, where p_pi = 40 GeV/c corresponds to Q^2 approximately 10 GeV^2
- EIC provides e+p center-of-mass energy from 20 to 100 GeV at luminosity of 10^33 to 10^34 cm^-2 s^-1
- EIcC nominal 20 GeV proton beam colliding with 3.5 GeV electron beam produces u-channel pi0 at momentum less than 40 GeV

## Context

This work extends the initial successes of TDA framework validation from JLab 6 GeV data, including the exclusive backward pi+ electroproduction measurement from CLAS (Park, 2017) and the L/T separated omega cross section from Hall C (Li, 2019). The paper outlines a three-stage program to systematically study TDAs, beginning with further validation of scaling trends for multiple meson channels (pi0, eta, rho, omega, eta-prime, phi), then determining the -u dependence of cross sections, and finally extracting TDAs through spin asymmetry measurements at JLab 12 GeV and the future EIC. The work enables a systematic comparison of u-channel meson production across JLab, PANDA at FAIR, and the EIC to test the universality of TDAs in different processes.
