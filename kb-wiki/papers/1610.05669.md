# Pion Electromagnetic Form Factor at Lower and Higher Momentum Transfer

> The pion electromagnetic form factor is calculated at lower and higher momentum transfer in order to explore constituent quark models and the differences among those models. In particular, the light-front constituent quark model is utilized here to calculate the pion electromagnetic form factor at lower and higher energies. The matrix elements of the electromagnetic current, are calculated with both "plus" and "minus" components of the electromagnetic current in the light-front. Further, the electromagnetic form factor is compared with other models in the literature and experimental data.

## Key claim

The light-front constituent quark model, using either symmetric or non-symmetric vertex functions for the π-q\bar{q} interaction, can describe the pion electromagnetic form factor consistently across both low and high momentum transfer up to Q² = 20 (GeV/c)², provided that the non-valence (pair term) contributions are correctly included for the minus component of the electromagnetic current to restore covariance.

## Method

The authors calculate the pion electromagnetic form factor using a light-front constituent quark model in the Breit frame, evaluating matrix elements of both the plus (J⁺) and minus (J⁻) components of the electromagnetic current. Two types of π-q\bar{q} vertex functions are employed: a non-symmetric vertex and a symmetric vertex, each regularized by a regulator mass m_R. For the non-symmetric vertex with the minus component, non-valence (pair term) contributions are computed using the dislocation pole method and added to the valence contribution to restore covariance. The results are compared with the vector meson dominance (VMD) model and experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass used: m_π = 0.140 GeV
- Constituent quark mass: m_q = 0.220 GeV for both vertex models
- Regulator mass for non-symmetric vertex: m_R = 1.0 GeV
- Regulator mass for symmetric vertex: m_R = 0.60 GeV
- Pion decay constant from non-symmetric vertex model: f_π = 92.13 MeV (experimental value: f_π ≈ 92.1 MeV)
- Pion decay constant from symmetric vertex model: f_π = 92.40 MeV
- Pion charge radius from non-symmetric vertex model: r_π = 0.679 fm (experimental: r_exp = 0.672 ± 0.02 fm)
- Pion charge radius from symmetric vertex model: r_π = 0.740 fm
- Rho meson mass used in VMD: m_ρ = 0.767 GeV
- Asymptotic behavior for non-symmetric vertex model: Q²F_π at Q² = 15 (GeV/c)² ≈ 0.18 (GeV/c)²
- Leading-order perturbative QCD prediction: Q²F_π(Q²) ≈ 0.15 (GeV/c)² for α_s(Q²=10 (GeV/c)²) ≈ 0.3
- Dyson-Schwinger approach range: Q²F_π(Q²) ≈ 0.12–0.19 (GeV/c)²

## Context

This work extends previous light-front constituent quark model calculations by de Melo and collaborators (Pacheco99, Pacheco2002) to higher momentum transfer up to Q² = 20 (GeV/c)², comparing with experimental data from Amendolia (1984, 1986), Frascati (2001), Volmer (2001), and Horn (2006). The results are contrasted with the vector meson dominance model of Kroll, Lee and Zumino (1967) and with Dyson-Schwinger equation approaches by Maris and Roberts (2000). The study demonstrates that proper inclusion of non-valence contributions restores covariance in light-front calculations, enabling reliable form factor predictions across both non-perturbative and perturbative QCD regimes.
