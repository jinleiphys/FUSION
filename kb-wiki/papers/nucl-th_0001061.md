# Generator Coordinate Method Calculations for Ground and First Excited Collective States in $^{4}$He, $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca Nuclei

> The main characteristics of the ground and, in particular, the first excited monopole state in the $^{4}$He, $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca nuclei are studied within the generator coordinate method using Skyrme-type effective forces and three construction potentials, namely the harmonic-oscillator, the square-well and Woods-Saxon potentials. Calculations of density distributions, radii, nucleon momentum distributions, natural orbitals, occupation numbers and depletions of the Fermi sea, as well as of pair density and momentum distributions are carried out. A comparison of these quantities for both ground and first excited monopole states with the available empirical data and with the results of other theoretical methods are given and discussed in detail.

## Key claim
The generator coordinate method (GCM) with Skyrme forces can describe both ground and first excited monopole states in light doubly magic nuclei, but the nucleon-nucleon correlations it accounts for are of collective rather than short-range type, as shown by the absence of high-momentum tails in momentum distributions when using harmonic-oscillator or Woods-Saxon construction potentials.

## Method
The Hill-Wheeler equation was solved using a discretization procedure with three construction potentials (harmonic-oscillator, square-well with infinite walls, and Woods-Saxon) to generate the trial wave functions. The one-body density matrix was constructed for both the ground and first excited monopole states, from which density distributions, momentum distributions, natural orbitals, occupation numbers, and pair center-of-mass and relative distributions were calculated. Skyrme effective forces were used with parameter sets tuned to binding energies (square-well case) or the SkM* parameter set (harmonic-oscillator and Woods-Saxon cases).

## Key numbers
- Ground state rms radius of $^{4}$He: 1.89 fm (HO), 1.77 fm (SW), 1.85 fm (WS2); first excited monopole state rms radius: 3.00 fm (HO), 2.86 fm (SW), 3.29 fm (WS2)
- Ground state rms radii for $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca with Woods-Saxon potential are "very close to the experimental values" (no explicit number given in text)
- Skyrme parameter set for square-well case: $t_0 = -2765$, $t_1 = 383.94$, $t_2 = -38.04$, $t_3 = 15865$, $\sigma = 1/6$
- SkM* parameter set: $t_0 = -2645$, $t_1 = 410$, $t_2 = -135$, $t_3 = 15595$, $\sigma = 1/6$
- The difference in rms radii $\Delta r_{\text{rms}} = r_{\text{rms}}^{(1)} - r_{\text{rms}}^{(0)}$ decreases with increasing mass number (no explicit numbers given for $^{16}$O or $^{40}$Ca)

## Context
This work extends earlier GCM studies by Antonov et al. (1986, 1988, 1990, 1991) on ground-state properties of $^{4}$He, $^{16}$O, and $^{40}$Ca to include the first excited monopole state and its natural orbital representation. It contrasts with the translationally invariant cluster method of Bishop et al. (1993) for $^{4}$He and with the Jastrow correlation method of Dimitrova et al. (2000) which explicitly includes short-range correlations. The results enable calculations of one- and two-nucleon removal reaction cross sections using GCM overlap functions.
