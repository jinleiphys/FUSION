# J/Psi suppressions in a thermally equilibrating quark-gluon plasma at RHIC

> We estimate the survival probability of $J/ψ$ in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, using both short-distance QCD and nuclear absorption mechanism. The suppression is found to be almost 100 percent for a thermally equilibrating quark-gluon plasma. The measurement of such a huge suppression of $J/ψ$ may suggest the existence of a deconfined partonic medium, possibly a thermalised quark-gluon plasma, at RHIC.

## Key claim

The paper estimates that the survival probability of J/ψ in a thermally equilibrating quark-gluon plasma at RHIC is "almost 100 percent" suppressed, but this result is conditional on using the parton cascade model (PCM) for the evolution of gluon density and temperature, and it applies specifically to J/ψ produced from primary charm quarks, not including thermal charm production.

## Method

The author calculates the J/ψ survival probability by combining two mechanisms: (1) dissociation of a fully formed J/ψ by deconfined gluons in a thermally equilibrating quark-gluon plasma, using the short-distance QCD gluon-J/ψ dissociation cross section from Bhanot and Peskin (1979), with the gluon number density and temperature evolution taken from the parton cascade model (PCM) of Geiger (1992); and (2) nuclear absorption of a nascent J/ψ before it forms a physical resonance, using the Glauber model with a uniform nuclear density and an absorption cross section of 6.2 mb, as used by the NA50 collaboration.

## Key numbers

* The gluon number density in the thermally equilibrating quark-gluon plasma at RHIC evolves as n(τ) = 565 fm⁻³ (τ/τ₀)⁻⁰·⁹⁰, with τ₀ = 0.05 fm.
* The temperature evolution in the same plasma is T(τ) = 950 MeV (τ/τ₀)⁻⁰·³⁰.
* For central Au-Au collisions at RHIC (b = 0), the survival probability from nuclear absorption alone is S_{c̄c-N} ≈ 0.42 (about 60 percent suppression).
* The gluon-J/ψ dissociation yields a suppression of about 95 percent for J/ψ with high transverse momentum (P_T ≈ 10 GeV) and almost 100 percent for J/ψ with low transverse momentum.

## Context

This work extends the original proposal of J/ψ suppression in an equilibrated quark-gluon plasma by Matsui and Satz (1986) to the pre-equilibrium (equilibrating) stage, using the parton cascade model of Geiger (1992) for the time evolution of the plasma. It also incorporates nuclear absorption as described by Huffner (1990) and used by the NA50 collaboration (1996) to explain data from S-U collisions, noting that Pb-Pb collisions at SPS show an anomalous excess suppression not explained by nuclear absorption alone. The paper enables future studies that combine the color flux-tube model with pQCD-based models and incorporate the space-time evolution of quarkonium production using NRQCD, as well as the contribution from thermal charm production at RHIC and LHC.
