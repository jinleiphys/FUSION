# Coherent phi and omega meson photoproduction from deuteron and non-diffractive channels

> For coherent photoproduction of phi and omega mesons from deuteron at forward angles, the isovector pi-exchange amplitude is strongly suppressed. We show a possibility to study the non-diffractive channels associated with the unnatural parity exchange in phi-photoproduction and with the baryon resonance excitations in omega-photoproduction by measuring the spin observables.

## Key claim

By using an isoscalar deuteron target, the isovector π-exchange amplitude is strongly suppressed in coherent φ and ω meson photoproduction at forward angles, which allows the study of non-diffractive channels (unnatural parity exchange in φ-photoproduction and baryon resonance excitations in ω-photoproduction) through spin observables, provided the momentum transfer Q² is below 0.5 GeV² and the initial photon energy is below 2–3 GeV.

## Method

The authors calculate the coherent photoproduction amplitude γ D → V D using a non-relativistic deuteron wave function from the Paris potential, with the elementary amplitudes T^p and T^n from nucleon targets combined into an isoscalar amplitude T^s = ½(T^p + T^n). The elementary amplitudes include Pomeron exchange, pseudoscalar π and η exchange, and direct and crossed N and N* exchange processes, with the isovector π-exchange terms cancelling in the isoscalar combination. The total deuteron amplitude is expressed in terms of deuteron form factors (analogous to charge, quadrupole, and magnetic form factors) that depend on the transferred momentum q², and spin observables such as the decay asymmetry Σ_V and beam-target asymmetries C^{21}_{BT}, C^{20}_{BT}, and C^{10}_{BT} are computed.

## Key numbers

- The momentum transfer Q² is restricted to values smaller than 0.5 GeV², and the initial photon energy is below 2–3 GeV.
- The form factor S^N_1 has a node at q² ≃ 0.5 GeV².
- For φ photoproduction at t ≃ t_max, the decay asymmetry for the deuteron target is Σ^D_φ ≃ 1 – 0.67 |α_s|², where α_s is the isoscalar unnatural parity-exchange amplitude relative to the natural parity-exchange amplitude.
- For ω photoproduction at E_γ = 1.92 GeV, the decay asymmetry changes from about –0.9 for the γ p reaction to +1 for the γ D reaction at forward angles.
- The beam-target asymmetry C^{21}_{BT} for φ photoproduction at t_max is approximately –0.33 when only the non-exotic background (η-exchange) is present.
- The cross section of the γ D → ω D reaction at E_γ = 1.92 GeV is predicted to be of the same order of magnitude as the γ D → φ D reaction.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of vector meson photoproduction from nucleons (Titov, Lee, and collaborators, 1999; Titov and Lee, 2002) to coherent photoproduction from deuterons, building on the deuteron photoproduction formalism of Frankfurt, Koepf, and Strikman (1997). The paper enables experimental tests at facilities such as LEPS at SPring-8, Jefferson Lab, ELSA-SAPHIR, and GRAAL to isolate exotic non-diffractive channels (e.g., s\bar{s} knockout, anomalous Regge trajectories, and baryon resonance excitations) that are otherwise masked by the dominant π-exchange or Pomeron contributions in nucleon targets.
