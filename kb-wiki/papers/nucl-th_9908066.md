# Charge-dependence of the $πNN$ coupling constant and charge-dependence of the NN interaction

> The recent determination of the charged $πNN$ coupling constant, $g_{π^\pm}$, by the Uppsala Neutron Research Group implies that there may be considerable charge-splitting of the pion coupling constant. We investigate the consequences of this for the charge-independence breaking (CIB) of the $^1S_0$ scattering length, $Δa_{CIB}$. We find that $Δa_{CIB}$ depends sensitively on the difference between $g_{π^\pm}$ and the neutral $πNN$ coupling constant, $g_{π^0}$. Moreover, if $g^2_{π^\pm}$ is only about 3% larger than $g^2_{π^0}$, then the established theoretical explanation of $Δa_{CIB}$ (in terms of pion mass splitting) is completely wiped out.

## Key claim

If the charged-pion coupling constant squared is only about 3% larger than the neutral-pion coupling constant squared, the established theoretical explanation of charge-independence breaking in the singlet scattering length (based on pion mass splitting) is completely wiped out.

## Method

The authors used the Bonn meson-exchange model for the nucleon-nucleon interaction to compute the charge-independence breaking of the singlet scattering length, including one-pion-exchange, two-pion-exchange, and beyond-two-pion-exchange diagrams. They introduced charge-splitting of the pion-nucleon coupling constant alongside the conventional pion mass splitting, and performed accurate numerical calculations for the scattering length difference between the average of proton-proton and neutron-neutron versus neutron-proton scattering.

## Key numbers

- The empirical charge-independence breaking of the singlet scattering length is Δa_CIB = 5.7 ± 0.5 fm.
- With no charge-dependence of the coupling constant, the conventional calculation (Li and Machleidt, 1998) predicts Δa_CIB = 4.64 fm from pion mass splitting alone.
- Using a charge-dependent coupling constant with g²_π⁰/4π = 13.6 and g²_π±/4π = 14.4, the predicted Δa_CIB reverses sign to -4.49 fm.
- A charge-splitting of g²_π of only about 3% (e.g., g²_π⁰/4π = 13.6 and g²_π±/4π = 14.0) would erase all CIB prediction from pion mass splitting.
- The Uppsala group determination gives g²_π±/4π = 14.52 ± 0.26, implying Δg²_π/4π = 0.8 relative to the neutral value of 13.6.

## Context

This work extends the conventional explanation of charge-independence breaking in the singlet scattering length, which was developed by Ericson and Miller (1983) and Li and Machleidt (1998) using pion mass splitting in meson-exchange models. The paper responds to the Uppsala Neutron Research Group determination (Rahm et al., 1998) of a large charged-pion coupling constant, which contradicts the lower values from the Nijmegen group (Stoks et al., 1993) and the VPI group (Arndt et al., 1994). The authors conclude that significant charge-splitting of the coupling constant is unlikely, implying systematic errors in current determinations.
