# Nuclear structure properties of Si and P isotopes with the microscopic effective interactions

> In the present work, we have reported comprehensive study of the $sd$-shell nuclei Si and P with neutron number varying from $N = 9$ to $N = 20$ using the microscopic effective valence shell interactions, namely, N3LO, JISP16 and DJ16A. These effective $sd$-shell interactions are developed using the \textit{ab initio} no-core shell model wave functions and the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki transformation method. For comparison, we have also performed shell model calculations with the empirical USDB interaction. Energy spectra and electromagnetic properties of these isotopic chains have been studied. Theoretically calculated shell model results are compared with the available experimental data, to check the predictive strength of these microscopic interactions. It is found that the binding energies of the ground states are better reproduced with the DJ16A interaction as compared to other microscopic interactions and a proton subshell closure at $Z=14$ is obtained in Si. Spin-tensor decomposition of two-body interaction is presented to understand the contributions from central, vector and tensor components into these interactions. Spectroscopic strengths of $^{23}$Al($d$,$n$)$^{24}$Si are examined for the newly performed experiment at NSCL.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the microscopic effective interaction DJ16A reproduces ground-state binding energies of Si and P isotopes better than the other microscopic interactions (N3LO and JISP16), but only when the USDB single-particle energies are used instead of the ab initio derived ones; the DJ16A interaction still slightly underbinds the ground states, while N3LO and JISP16 overbind them.

## Method

Shell model calculations were performed for Si isotopes (A=23-34) and P isotopes (A=24-35) using three microscopic effective sd-shell interactions (N3LO, JISP16, DJ16A) derived from ab initio no-core shell model wave functions via the Okubo-Lee-Suzuki transformation method, with USDB single-particle energies substituted for the microscopic ones. For comparison, calculations were also carried out with the empirical USDB interaction. Energy spectra, ground-state binding energies, effective single-particle energies, spin-tensor decomposition of the two-body interaction, and spectroscopic factors for the 23Al(d,n)24Si reaction were computed using the KSHELL code.

## Key numbers

- For 24Si, the DJ16A interaction gives the first excited 2+ state at 1.786 MeV excitation energy, while the experimental value is 1.879 MeV; USDB predicts 2.111 MeV.
- For 28Si, the energy difference between ground state and first excited state is 1.068 MeV (N3LO), 1.145 MeV (JISP16), 1.296 MeV (DJ16A), and 1.932 MeV (USDB); the experimental value is 1.779 MeV.
- For 27Si, the DJ16A interaction gives the 1/2_1+ state at 0.711 MeV, close to the experimental value of 0.781 MeV.
- For 28P, the energy difference between the 2+ and 3+ states is 337 keV (N3LO), 266 keV (JISP16), and 229 keV (DJ16A); with USDB, the 2+ state is 13 keV above the 3+ state.
- The ground-state configuration of 34Si with all four interactions is |pi(d_{5/2}^6)>; the 2_1+ and 2_2+ states have configurations |pi(d_{5/2}^5 s_{1/2}^1)> and |pi(d_{5/2}^5 d_{3/2}^1)> with probabilities 89.71% and 88.77% for DJ16A.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Smirnova et al. (2019) that applied the same microscopic interactions to O, F, Na, Mg, Si, S, and K isotopes, by providing a comprehensive analysis of the full Si and P isotopic chains including electromagnetic properties and spectroscopic factors. The results are compared with calculations using the empirical USDB interaction from Brown and Richter (2006). The study enables future work on neutron-rich sd-shell nuclei and highlights the need for three-nucleon forces or larger NCSM model spaces to improve agreement with experiment, particularly for ground-state binding energies.
