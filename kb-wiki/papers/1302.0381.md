# Multichannel dynamical symmetry and cluster-coexistence

> A composite symmetry of the nuclear structure, called multichannel dynamical symmetry is established. It can describe different cluster configurations (defined by different reaction channels) in a unified framework, thus it has a considerable predictive power. The two-channel case is presented in detail, and its conceptual similarity to the dynamical supersymmetry is discussed.

# Multichannel dynamical symmetry and cluster-coexistence

## Key claim
The paper establishes that a multichannel dynamical symmetry (MUSY) connecting different binary cluster configurations in a nucleus can be rigorously derived from the dynamical symmetry of an underlying multicluster (ternary) configuration, rather than being merely a phenomenological observation. The symmetry is a partial symmetry: one quantum number (the relative motion quantum number along one Jacobi coordinate) is not simultaneously valid for both channels, so the MUSY does not provide a full multiplet structure across channels.

## Method
The authors work within the semimicroscopic algebraic cluster model (SACM), where clusters are described by the Elliott SU(3) model and relative motion by the modified vibron model with U(4) group structure. For the two-channel case, they introduce an underlying ternary configuration (e.g., 16O+4He+4He for 24Mg) and construct a U(7) algebraic structure with two sets of oscillator quanta for the two independent relative motions, plus a scalar boson. The dynamical symmetry of the ternary configuration is defined by a group chain U(7) ⊃ U(6) ⊃ U(3) ⊗ U_p(2), where U_p(2) is a pseudo-spin group acting in the cluster-index space. The binary-channel dynamical symmetries are then obtained by projecting onto fixed values of the relative motion quantum number along one Jacobi coordinate (e.g., n_t = 4 for 16O+8Be, n_t = 8 for 20Ne+4He in 24Mg).

## Key numbers
- For the 24Mg example: the 16O+8Be configuration has n_t = 4 (m_c = 4), while the 20Ne+4He configuration has n_t = 8 (m_d = 0).
- The energy functional for the ternary Hamiltonian is: E = ε + γ N_6 + δ C^(2)_SU(3) + β L(L+1) + θ N_6 L(L+1), where N_6 = n_1 + n_2 is the total number of oscillator quanta in the two relative motions.
- The projected binary Hamiltonians yield parameter relations: γ_c = γ_d = γ, ε_c = ε_d - 4γ, β_c = β_d - 4θ, θ_c = θ_d = θ, and F_c(λ,μ,L) = F_d(λ,μ,L), where the shift of 4 arises from the difference in n_t between the two channels (n_t_c = 4, n_t_d = 8).

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological introduction of MUSY by Cseh (2012) by providing its rigorous mathematical foundation within the SACM framework. The MUSY is conceptually compared to dynamical supersymmetry (SUSY) in nuclear physics (Iachello, 1980; Frank, 1994; Bijker, 2000): both are composite symmetries coupling two sectors, but MUSY is a partial symmetry (one quantum number not simultaneously valid) while SUSY preserves all quantum numbers. The pseudo-spin operators used derive from the Bargmann-Moshinsky transformation (Bargmann and Moshinsky, 1961) and related work by Gal (1971) and Horvat (1977). The approach enables construction of configuration-independent cluster-cluster interactions and predicts that the Hamiltonian of one cluster configuration can completely determine the energy spectrum of another, with first tests on 24Mg+4He and 16O+12C spectra (Cseh, 2012; S32) showing promise but requiring further validation.
