# Mirror Energy Differences and nuclear structure in 1$f_{7/2}$ nuclei

> Experimental mirror energy differences (MED) for nuclei lying in the middle and the second part of $1f_{7/2}$ shell are compared with shell model calculations in the $1f_{7/2}$ and in the full $pf$ configuration spaces, as well as with CSM calculations. MED plots are fully consistent with the description which emerges from the interpretation of SM calculations: i.e. the band crossing of the gs band with high-$K$ bands in $A$=51 and 50 mirror pairs, as well as the seniority-3 quasi band-termination at $I^π$=19/2$^-$ in $A$=49. Rotational alignment effects are excluded, contrarily to what even recently proposed. Some inconsistencies, as well as definition improprieties, are pointed out in the topical literature.

# Mirror Energy Differences and nuclear structure in 1f_{7/2} nuclei

## Key claim

Experimental mirror energy difference (MED) plots for nuclei in the middle and second half of the 1f_{7/2} shell are fully consistent with shell model descriptions involving band crossing of the ground-state band with high-K bands (in A=51 and 50) and seniority-3 quasi band-termination at I^π=19/2^- (in A=49), while rotational alignment (RAL) effects are excluded, contrary to what has been recently proposed in the literature.

## Method

The author compares experimental MED values for mirror pairs with A=51, 50, 49, and 47 against shell model calculations performed in both the pure 1f_{7/2} and the full pf configuration spaces using the code ANTOINE with the KB3G nuclear interaction and Coulomb interaction. The analysis also includes cranked shell model (CSM) calculations and examines the multipole Coulomb matrix element contribution (V_CM) to MED, while pointing out definition improprieties and inconsistencies in prior literature regarding the radial term E_ll and the interpretation of band-crossing phenomena.

## Key numbers

- The experimental MED value between the 2p_{3/2} level and the 1f_{7/2} ground state in A=41 is -227 keV
- For A=41, calculated contributions to MED using a Woods-Saxon potential: -260 keV for radial term (first order -180 keV, Thomas-Ehrman shift -80 keV), +70 keV for electromagnetic spin-orbit (V_Cls), +30 keV for short-range correlations
- For A=57, the first excited level MED is 260 keV, with bare contributions of 190 keV for V_Cr and 160 keV for V_Cls
- For A=57, the second excited 2p_{1/2} level experimental MED is -7 keV, with bare value -50 keV, V_Cls +90 keV, and V_Cr -120 keV
- The deformation parameter for low-lying levels in A=51 is approximately β≈0.24, while the K^π=17/2^- band head has β≈0.22
- For A=49, the deformation parameter for low spins is β=0.27, and the K^π=13/2^- band head has Q=51.2 efm^2 corresponding to β=0.24
- For A=47, the deformation parameter β is 0.26 at low spin

## Context

This work extends the discussion of Brandolini et al. (2008) on the nuclear structure of 1f_{7/2} nuclei, which showed that most spectroscopic data are well described by shell model calculations in the full pf configuration space. The paper directly contradicts the rotational alignment interpretation proposed by Sheikh et al. (1999, 2001) and adopted in the review by Bentley and Lenzi (2006), showing instead that deformation alignment (DAL) and seniority-based quasi band-termination provide the correct description. The analysis enables a clearer understanding of MED as a spectroscopic tool for distinguishing between proton and neutron pair-breaking and band-crossing phenomena in the 1f_{7/2} shell region.
