# Susceptibilities and the Phase Structure of a Chiral Model with Polyakov Loops

> In an extension of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model where the quarks interact with the temporal gluon field, represented by the Polyakov loop, we explore the relation between the deconfinement and chiral phase transitions. The effect of Polyakov loop dynamics on thermodynamic quantities, on the phase structure at finite temperature and baryon density and on various susceptibilities is presented. Particular emphasis is put on the behavior and properties of the fluctuations of the (approximate) order parameters and their dependence on temperature and net--quark number density. We also discuss how the phase structure of the model is influenced by the coupling of the quarks to the Polyakov loop.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the PNJL model, the coupling of quarks to the Polyakov loop shifts the chiral phase transition to higher temperatures and suppresses quark number density and susceptibility below the critical temperature, but the singular behavior of the quark number susceptibility at the tricritical point is driven entirely by the chiral order parameter (the dynamical quark mass), while the Polyakov loop contributions remain finite.

## Method

The authors extend the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model by coupling quarks to a uniform temporal background gauge field represented by the Polyakov loop, using non-local four-fermion interactions regulated by a form factor. They compute the thermodynamic potential in the mean-field approximation and solve the coupled gap equations for the dynamical quark mass, the Polyakov loop, and its conjugate at finite temperature and quark chemical potential. The susceptibilities of the order parameters and the quark number susceptibility are then derived from the thermodynamic potential and its derivatives.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV; pion mass: \(m_\pi = 135\) MeV; dynamical quark mass at zero momentum: \(M_{p=0} = 335\) MeV (used to fix model parameters)
- Model parameters for \(\alpha = 10\): \(\Lambda = 684.2\) MeV, \(G_S\Lambda^2 = 4.66\), \(m_0 = 4.46\) MeV; quark condensate: \(\langle \bar{\psi}\psi \rangle^{1/3} = -256.2\) MeV
- Tricritical point (TCP) location in the chiral limit: \((T_{TCP}=157, \mu_{TCP}=266)\) MeV
- Chiral phase transition temperature at \(\mu=0\) in the chiral limit: \(T \simeq 242\) MeV
- Pure gauge Polyakov loop model transition temperature: \(T_0 = 270\) MeV
- Reduction of chiral transition temperature due to form factor suppression: on the order of 15 MeV
- At high temperature, quark number susceptibility approaches the ideal gas limit: \(\chi_q/T^2 \simeq 2\)

## Context

This work extends the PNJL model developed by Meisinger, Fukushima, and others (2003-2004) to study the interplay between deconfinement and chiral symmetry breaking. The results are compared with standard NJL model calculations and lattice gauge theory (LGT) computations of quark number density and susceptibility. The finding that the quark number susceptibility diverges only at the tricritical point, driven by the chiral order parameter, confirms earlier Landau-Ginzburg theory predictions by Hatta and Ikeda (2003) and provides a framework for interpreting LGT results at finite baryon density where direct lattice calculations remain limited.
