# Recent Advances and Open Questions in Neutrino-induced Quasi-elastic Scattering and Single Photon Production

> The study of neutrino-nucleus interactions has recently seen rapid development with a new generation of accelerator-based neutrino experiments employing medium and heavy nuclear targets for the study of neutrino oscillations. A few unexpected results in the study of quasi-elastic scattering and single photon production have spurred a revisiting of the underlying nuclear physics and connections to electron-nucleus scattering. A thorough understanding and resolution of these issues is essential for future progress in the study of neutrino oscillations. A recent workshop hosted by the Institute of Nuclear Theory at the University of Washington (INT-13-54W) examined experimental and theoretical developments in neutrino-nucleus interactions and related measurements from electron and pion scattering. We summarize the discussions at the workshop pertaining to the aforementioned issues in quasi-elastic scattering and single photon production, particularly where there was consensus on the highest priority issues to be resolved and the path towards resolving them.

# Recent Advances and Open Questions in Neutrino-induced Quasi-elastic Scattering and Single Photon Production

## Key claim
The workshop summary concludes that while several experiments have observed enhanced cross sections in neutrino-nucleus quasi-elastic scattering relative to simple impulse-approximation predictions, the underlying nuclear physics explanation remains unresolved; the data favor an enhanced transverse response over an increased axial mass at higher energies, but this conclusion depends on the specific detector capabilities and beam energies used, and inconsistencies remain between models that best describe muon kinematics versus hadron kinematics.

## Method
The paper summarizes discussions from the INT-13-54W workshop, presenting experimental results from ArgoNeuT (a 170-liter LArTPC in the NuMI beamline), MINOS (an iron-scintillator sampling calorimeter), NOvA (a liquid scintillator detector 110 mrad off-axis), MINERvA (a solid scintillator detector with triangular extrusions), MiniBooNE (an 800-ton mineral oil Cherenkov detector), NOMAD (a low-Z drift chamber detector in a magnetic field), SciBooNE (a plastic scintillator tracking detector), and T2K (using ND280 with FGDs and TPCs). Each experiment selected charged-current quasi-elastic (CCQE) or CC0π events using different topological criteria, reconstructed neutrino energy via either the muon-only quasi-elastic hypothesis or by summing muon and hadronic energy, and compared measured cross sections to predictions from event generators such as GENIE, GiBUU, and NEUT.

## Key numbers
- ArgoNeuT: proton tracking threshold of T = 21 MeV; accumulated 7000 events with 8.5×10^18 protons-on-target in neutrino mode and 1.20×10^20 protons-on-target in antineutrino mode.
- MINOS: CCQE selection requires hadron energy less than 225 MeV; fit returned M_A^QE = 1.21^{+0.18}_{-0.10}(fit) ^{+0.13}_{-0.15}(syst.) GeV for Q^2 between 0 and 1.2 GeV^2.
- NOvA: flux uncertainty of 25%; cross section measured from 0.2 to 2.0 GeV was lower than GENIE prediction with RFG model and M_A = 0.99 GeV.
- MINERvA: selected 16,467 antineutrino events (77% purity) and 29,620 neutrino events (49% purity); non-CCQE backgrounds reduced by 5% for Q^2_QE < 0.8 GeV^2/c^2 and 15% for Q^2_QE = [0.8, 2.0] GeV^2/c^2; neutrino data show extra vertex energy consistent with an extra proton below 200 MeV emitted 29 ± 9% of the time.
- MiniBooNE: neutrino CCQE sample of 146,070 events with 26% efficiency and 77% purity; antineutrino sample of 71,176 events with 29% efficiency and 61% purity; observed approximately 40% larger cross section than impulse approximation predictions; integrated flux uncertainty of ~10%.
- NOMAD: absolute CCQE cross section for neutrinos = [0.92 ± 0.02(stat) ± 0.06(syst)] × 10^-38 cm^2; for antineutrinos = [0.81 ± 0.05(stat) ± 0.08(syst)] × 10^-38 cm^2; M_A^ν = 1.06 ± 0.02(stat) ± 0.06(syst) GeV; updated analysis gave σ^ν_QE = [0.914 ± 0.013(stat) ± 0.038(syst)] × 10^-38 cm^2 (4.4% total uncertainty).
- SciBooNE: measured cross section versus E^QE_ν agreed with NEUT prediction using RFG-based CCQE model with M_A = 1.2 GeV.
- T2K: beam peaked at ~600 MeV; flux uncertainties of 10-15%.

## Context
This workshop summary extends the discussion initiated by MiniBooNE (Aguilar-Arevalo et al., 2007, 2010, 2013) which observed enhanced CCQE cross sections relative to impulse approximation predictions, spurring a revisiting of nuclear physics connections to electron-nucleus scattering. The paper compares results across multiple experiments with different detector technologies and beam energies, including comparisons to GENIE (Andreopoulos et al., 2009) and GiBUU (Buss et al., 2011) models. The findings enable future neutrino oscillation experiments to better understand nuclear effects that bias energy reconstruction, particularly the distinction between enhanced transverse response versus increased axial mass, which have different consequences for the energy dependence of the CCQE cross section and final state particle composition.
