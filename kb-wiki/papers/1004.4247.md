# Alpha-cluster Condensations in Nuclei and Experimental Approaches for their Studies

> The formation of alpha-clusters in nuclei close to the decay thresholds is discussed. These states can be considered to be boson-condensates, which are formed in a second order phase transition in a mixture of nucleons and alpha-particles. The de Broglie wavelength of the alpha-particles is larger than the nuclear diameter, therefore the coherent properties of the alpha-particles give particular effects for the study of such states. The states are above the thresholds thus the enhanced emission of multiple-alphas into the same direction is observed. The probability for the emission of multiple-alphas is not described by Hauser-Feshbach theory for compound nucleus decay.

## Key claim

Alpha-cluster condensates form in nuclei at excitation energies near multi-alpha decay thresholds, where the binding energy per nucleon becomes equal to or less than the 7.07 MeV binding energy of nucleons in a free alpha-particle; these states are not described by the Hauser-Feshbach statistical compound-nucleus decay model because the alpha-particles interact coherently with a de Broglie wavelength larger than the nuclear diameter.

## Method

The author uses thermodynamic arguments for a second-order phase transition between a nucleon gas and an alpha-particle gas, with the condition ΔG=0 defining the critical excitation energy for condensation. Binding energies per alpha-particle and per nucleon are extracted from experimental masses (Audi 2003) and a theoretical mass for 164Pb (Isakov 2000), then tabulated for N=Z nuclei from 12C to 164Pb. The analysis invokes the Ikeda diagram (Horiuchi 1968) and the THSR condensate wavefunction (Tohsaki, Horiuchi, Schuck, Röpke 2001) for the Hoyle state in 12C, and discusses experimental signatures from inelastic alpha scattering, compound-nucleus decay, and multi-alpha correlations.

## Key numbers

* Hoyle state in 12C: excitation energy 7.654 MeV, 288 keV above the 3α threshold at 7.346 MeV.
* 8Be ground state resonance: excitation energy 92 keV; de Broglie wavelength λ = 67 fm for relative motion of two α-particles.
* 8Be 2+ state at 3.04 MeV: de Broglie wavelength λ = 12.4 fm.
* Critical excitation energies for α-condensation (Ecrit_x): 12C = 7.27 MeV, 16O = 14.44 MeV, 40Ca = 59.10 MeV, 100Sn = 117.1 MeV, 164Pb = 39.9 MeV.
* Binding energy per nucleon in free α-particle: 7.073 MeV; typical binding energy per nucleon in stable nuclei: ~8.2 MeV.
* For 12C, inelastic α-scattering at 240 MeV shows the 0+2 state (Hoyle state) diffraction pattern shifted by approximately 2 degrees to forward angles relative to elastic scattering, indicating a 10% larger radius than the ground state.
* 0+6 state in 16O at 15.1 MeV, just above the 4α threshold at 14.4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Ikeda-diagram concept (Horiuchi 1968, Ikeda 1972) by giving it a thermodynamic foundation as a second-order phase transition, and builds on the THSR condensate wavefunction (Tohsaki, Horiuchi, Schuck, Röpke 2001) which successfully describes the Hoyle state in 12C where shell-model calculations fail. The paper connects to antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations by Horiuchi and Kanada-En'yo (1997, 2001) and antisymmetrized fermionic molecular dynamics (FMD) by Feldmeier et al. (1995, 2005), which show α-clustering in loosely bound neutron-rich nuclei. It enables experimental searches for α-condensed states via inelastic scattering, coherent multi-alpha emission from compound nuclei, and fragmentation at GeV/nucleon energies, with specific predictions for systems such as 100Sn formed from a 72Kr beam on a 28Si target.
