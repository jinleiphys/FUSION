# Dipole Moments of the Rho Meson

> The electric and magnetic dipole moments of the rho meson are calculated using the propagators and vertices derived from the QCD Dyson-Schwinger equations. Results obtained from using the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude studied by Chappell, Mitchell and Tandy, and Pichowsky and Lee, are compared. The rho meson EDM is generated through the inclusion of a quark electric dipole moment, which is left as a free variable. These results are compared to the perturbative results to obtain a measure of the effects of quark interactions and confinement. The two dipole moments are also calculated using the phenomenological MIT bag model to provide a further basis for comparison.

## Key claim

The electric and magnetic dipole moments of the rho meson are calculated using non-perturbative Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter framework, with the electric dipole moment (EDM) expressed in units of a free quark EDM parameter \(d_q\) rather than predicted absolutely; the results show that confinement enhances both moments significantly over perturbative values, with the EDM enhanced by a factor of 63–78.

## Method

The quark propagator was modeled using entire-function forms that lack a Lehmann representation (ensuring confinement), with parameters fitted to \(f_\pi\) and other low-energy observables. The rho meson Bethe-Salpeter amplitude was taken in a simplified Dirac structure (dominant \(\gamma_\mu\) term, ignoring other tensor structures at the 10% level) using three different parameter sets from Chappell (1996), Pichowsky and Lee (1996), and Mitchell and Tandy (1997). The quark-photon vertex used the Ball-Chiu form plus the Curtis-Pennington transverse piece to preserve the Ward-Takahashi identity and multiplicative renormalizability; the CP-violating EDM term was introduced by adding a \(D_q \gamma_5 \sigma_{\mu\rho} q^\rho\) term to that vertex. The impulse-approximation loop integrals were evaluated numerically in Euclidean space in the Breit frame using Gaussian quadrature, with charge and dipole terms projected out via projection operators. For comparison, the same moments were also computed in the MIT bag model (with one-gluon exchange and center-of-mass motion neglected).

## Key numbers

* Magnetic dipole moment of \(\rho^+\) from the Chappell amplitude: \(\mu_{\rho^+} = 2.26\ e/2m_\rho\) (using a constituent quark mass of 340 MeV in the non-relativistic formula).
* Non-perturbative magnetic dipole moments are 29%–51% larger than the perturbative result.
* Electric dipole moment of the rho (in units of the quark EDM \(d_q\)): the non-perturbative result is 63–78 times larger than the perturbative value \(d^{\rm pert.} = 3 m_q d_q\) (in units of \(e/2m\)).
* MIT bag model magnetic dipole moment is about 10% larger than the perturbative result.
* MIT bag model electric dipole moment is about 6% larger than the largest Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter result and about 83 times larger than the perturbative result.
* Quark propagator parameters: mass scale \(D = 0.160\ {\rm GeV}^2\); current quark mass parameter \(\bar{m} = 0.00897\) (dimensionless, corresponding to \(m = \bar{m} \sqrt{2D}\)).

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach to hadron properties, previously applied to pion observables (Roberts, 1995; Frank and Roberts, 1996), kaon form factors (Burden, Roberts and Thomson, 1996), and \(\rho\)-\(\omega\) mixing (Mitchell and Tandy, 1997), to the rho meson dipole moments. The results are compared with perturbative calculations (Pallaghy, 1996) and with the MIT bag model (Chodos et al., 1974) to isolate the effects of confinement and dressed quark interactions. The study enables future work on nucleon electromagnetic moments and CP-violating observables by quantifying how strongly confinement amplifies a quark-level EDM into a hadron-level EDM.
