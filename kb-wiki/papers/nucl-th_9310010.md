# Shape-Independent Expansion for the $^3S_1-^3D_1$ Mixing Parameter

> A low-energy shape-independent expansion is suggested for the function $[tan(2ε_{BB})/(2k^2)]$, where $ε_{BB}$ is the Blatt-Biedenharn mixing parameter for the $^3S_1 -$ $^3D_1$ channel. This expansion allows an evaluation of the mixing parameter $ε_{BB}$ from a knowledge of the deuteron asymptotic $D$ to $S$ ratio, pion mass and other low-energy observables, such as the scattering lengths, deuteron binding etc., of the nucleon-nucleon system. We demonstrate that the correct long range behavior of the tensor potential is essential for a realistic reproduction of $ε_{BB}$.

## Key claim

The authors propose a shape-independent effective-range-type expansion for the Blatt-Biedenharn mixing parameter epsilon_BB in the coupled 3S1-3D1 nucleon-nucleon channel, which allows evaluation of the mixing parameter from the deuteron asymptotic D to S ratio, pion mass, and other low-energy observables. The expansion is demonstrated to work well at low energies (E_lab < 4 MeV) using only the leading term, but requires additional terms for higher energies up to 300 MeV.

## Method

The authors construct an analytic function B(k^2) = -eta_d/alpha^2 + (K_02/k^2)/(K_00 - K_22) which vanishes at the deuteron pole and has a left-hand cut starting at k^2 = -m_pi^2/4 from one-pion exchange. They perform a Taylor-series-type expansion of this function approximating the nearest branch point by a pole, yielding an expansion for tan(2*epsilon_BB)/(2k^2) in terms of the deuteron asymptotic D/S ratio eta_d, deuteron binding energy alpha^2, pion mass m_pi, triplet scattering length a_t, and the (02) channel scattering length a_02. The expansion is tested against numerically calculated mixing parameters from the Peiper modified Reid soft core (PRSC) and one-boson exchange Bonn (OBE) potentials.

## Key numbers

- For the PRSC potential: deuteron binding = 2.2298 MeV, eta_d = 0.02636, a_t = 5.386 fm, a_02 = 1.62 fm^3, B(0) = -0.190 fm^2, c_1 = -0.432 fm^2
- For the OBE potential: deuteron binding = 2.2245 MeV, a_t = 5.4218 fm, eta_d = 0.0267, a_02 = 1.71 fm^3, B(0) = -0.182 fm^2, c_1 = -0.416 fm^2
- The low-energy shape-independent approximation (Eq. 120) provides an excellent fit to numerically calculated epsilon_BB for both potentials at E_lab < 4 MeV
- For E_lab up to 100 MeV, the fit requires modified parameters: for PRSC, B(0) = -0.191 fm^2, c_1 = -0.458 fm^2; for OBE, B(0) = -0.180 fm^2, c_1 = -0.482 fm^2
- For E_lab up to 300 MeV, both c_1 and c_2 are needed: for PRSC, c_1 = -0.468 fm^2, c_2 = 0.014 fm^4; for OBE, c_1 = -0.492 fm^2, c_2 = 0.008 fm^4
- Using only the longest-range (one-pion exchange) part of the PRSC tensor potential yields B(0) = -0.162 fm^2, a 12% error compared to the full potential value of -0.184 fm^2
- The Yamaguchi tensor potential, lacking correct long-range behavior, gives B(0) = 0.02 fm^2 and fails to reproduce realistic mixing parameters

## Context

This work extends the shape-independent effective-range expansion approach, originally developed by Bethe (1949) and Blatt and Weisskopf (1952) for uncoupled partial waves, to the coupled 3S1-3D1 channel mixing parameter. It builds on earlier studies of model-independent correlations among D-state observables by Ericson and Rosa Clot (1985) and Mustafa (1993), and on the work of Amado and coworkers (1974, 1978) emphasizing the importance of the deuteron asymptotic D/S ratio eta_d. The expansion enables model-independent extraction of eta_d from future accurate low-energy measurements of epsilon_BB, and demonstrates that the correct one-pion exchange tail of the tensor potential is essential for reproducing the mixing parameter, explaining why simple potentials like the Yamaguchi (1954) tensor potential fail qualitatively.
