# Inhomogeneous Nucleation of Quark-Gluon Plasma in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> We estimate the probability that a hard nucleon-nucleon collision is able to nucleate a seed of quark--gluon plasma in the surrounding hot and dense hadronic matter formed during a central collision of two large nuclei at AGS energies. The probability of producing at least one such seed is on the order of 1-100\%. We investigate the influence of quark--gluon plasma formation on the observed multiplicity distribution and find that it may lead to noticable structure in the form of a bump or shoulder.

## Key claim

The probability that a hard nucleon-nucleon collision can nucleate a seed of quark-gluon plasma in the surrounding hot and dense hadronic matter formed during a central collision of two large nuclei at AGS energies is estimated to be on the order of 1-100%, which is about one to two orders of magnitude greater than the probability estimated in their prior work using homogeneous nucleation theory (Kapusta and Vischer, 1995).

## Method

The authors developed a kinetic model to estimate the number of high-energy nucleon-nucleon scatterings occurring in the high-density medium formed during a heavy-ion collision, accounting for the energy loss of colliding nucleons as they penetrate the hot zone. They used a phase-space distribution constructed from a momentum degradation model (Hwa, 1971; Csernai and Kapusta, 1986) and a Poisson distribution for the number of collisions a nucleon suffers, with a mean free path of 0.4 fm at a laboratory beam energy of 11.6 GeV/nucleon. The pion multiplicity distribution from these hard collisions was approximated with a binomial distribution, and the number of "star bursts" that could become nucleation sites was computed by integrating over the center-of-momentum energy squared \(s\).

## Key numbers

- The mean free path of nucleons in hot and dense hadronic matter at a laboratory beam energy of 11.6 GeV/nucleon is 0.4 fm, assuming an average hadron-nucleon cross section of 25 mb.
- The critical mass for a plasma droplet to grow rapidly is estimated to be about 4 GeV, corresponding to the production of at least 6 pions in a nucleon-nucleon collision.
- If the critical number of pions is 6, there are on average 7 such nucleon-nucleon collisions per central gold-gold collision; if 8 or 10 pions are required, there is only one such critical star burst every 1 or every 25 central gold-gold collisions, respectively.
- At a beam energy of 11.6 GeV/A, the upper limit on the number of additional negatively charged pions produced per participating baryon is 0.33.
- The mean for purely hadronic events is \(N_{\rm had} = 145\) negatively charged particles, and the upper limit for events with quark-gluon plasma production is \(N_{\rm qg} = 193\) negatively charged particles.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior study of homogeneous nucleation of quark-gluon plasma (Kapusta and Vischer, 1995) by considering a nonthermal, inhomogeneous nucleation mechanism via hard nucleon-nucleon collisions. It provides a specific prediction for the probability of plasma formation at AGS energies and suggests that the resulting structure in the multiplicity distribution (a shoulder or second maximum) could be observable, analogous to the structure seen in \(p\bar{p}\) collisions at CERN (UA5 Collaboration, 1987) and Fermilab (CDF Collaboration, 1990; E735 Collaboration, 1992), which is attributed to minijet production. The work enables experimental searches for quark-gluon plasma at the AGS by providing a quantitative estimate of the event fraction and the expected change in multiplicity.
