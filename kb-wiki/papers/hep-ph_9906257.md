# Transverse Momentum Dependence of the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal Effect

> We study the transverse momentum dependence of the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect in QED, starting from the high energy expansion of the solution of the Dirac equation in the presence of an external field. The angular integrated energy loss formula differs from an earlier expression of Zakharov by taking finite kinematical boundaries into account. In an expansion in powers of the opacity of the medium, we derive explicit expressions for the radiation cross section associated with N=1, 2 and 3 scatterings. We verify the Bethe-Heitler and the factorization limit, and we calculate corrections to the factorization limit proportional to the square of the target size. A closed form expression valid to arbitrary orders in the opacity is derived in the dipole approximation. The resulting radiation spectrum is non-analytic in the coupling constant which is traced back to the transverse momentum broadening of a hard parton undergoing multiple small angle Moliere scattering. In extending the results to QCD, we test a previously used dipole prescription by comparing to direct pQCD results for N=1 and 2. For N=1, the QCD dipole prescription reproduces exactly the Bertsch-Gunion radiation spectrum. For N=2, we find a sizeable correction which reduces to a multiplicative factor 17/8 at large separation.

## Key claim

The authors derive a general QED radiation cross section formula for the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect that includes an explicit regularization term \(e^{-\epsilon(|z|+|z'|)}\), which is necessary because the \(\epsilon \to 0\) limit does not commute with the longitudinal \(z\)-integrations; this differs from earlier work by Kopeliovich, Schäfer and Tarasov (1998) which omitted this regularization.

## Method

Starting from the high-energy expansion of the Dirac equation in an external potential (the Furry approximation), the authors express the radiation amplitude as a Fourier transform over pairs of Green's functions that describe the transverse motion of the electron. They average over scattering centers in the medium, which introduces a factor depending on the Mott cross section \(\sigma(\rho)\) times the density \(n(\xi)\). The resulting differential cross section is then regularized analytically by splitting the longitudinal integrals into six regions and taking the \(\epsilon \to 0\) limit after integration, yielding expressions suitable for numerical evaluation.

## Key numbers

- The inverse momentum transfer \(\bar{q} = x m_e^2 / [2 (1-x) E_1]\) defines the photon formation length \(l_f = 1/\bar{q}\).
- For the QCD dipole prescription with \(N=2\) scatterings at large separation, the correction reduces to a multiplicative factor of \(17/8\).
- The prefactor \(C_{\text{pre}} = \alpha_{em} / (2\pi)^4 \cdot 2 x^2 / [E_1^2 (1-x)^2]\) appears in the differential cross section formula.
- The non-flip coefficient is \(g_{\text{nf}} = (4 - 4x + 2x^2) / (4x^2)\) and the spin-flip coefficient is \(g_{\text{sf}} = m_e^2 x^2 / 2\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Kopeliovich, Schäfer and Tarasov (1998) by including a proper regularization procedure and deriving an angular integrated energy loss formula that differs from Zakharov's (1996) expression by accounting for finite kinematical boundaries. The results enable numerical calculation of the radiation spectrum as a function of medium density and extension, and provide a test of the QCD dipole prescription by comparing to perturbative QCD results for \(N=1\) (reproducing the Bertsch-Gunion spectrum exactly) and \(N=2\) (finding a sizeable correction).
