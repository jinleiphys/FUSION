# Meson exchange effects in elastic $ep$ scattering at loop level and the electromagnetic form factors of the proton

> A new form of two-photon exchange(TPE) effect is studied to explain the discrepancy between unpolarized and polarized experimental data in elastic $ep$ scattering. The mechanism is based on a simple idea that apart from the usual TPE effects from box and crossed-box diagrams, the mesons may also be exchanged in elastic $ep$ scattering by two-photon coupling at loop level. The detailed study shows such contributions to reduced unpolarized cross section ($σ_{un}$) and polarized observables ($P_t,P_l$) at fixed $Q^2$ are only dependent on proton's electromagnetic form factors $G_{E,M}$ and a new unknown universal parameter $g$. After combining this contribution with the usual TPE contributions from box and crossed-box diagrams, the ratio $μ_pG_E/G_M$ extracted from the recent precise unpolarized and polarized experimental data can be described consistently.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a new form of two-photon exchange (TPE) effect, meson exchange via two-photon coupling at loop level, can resolve the long-standing discrepancy between the proton form factor ratio \(\mu_p G_E/G_M\) extracted from unpolarized (Rosenbluth) and polarized (PT) experiments, but only if one introduces a new unknown universal parameter \(g\) that must be fitted to data at each \(Q^2\).

## Method

The authors calculate the contribution from a \(t\)-channel meson-exchange diagram (Fig. 1c) where a spin-2 tensor meson couples to both the electron and proton via effective interactions, using Regge propagators. They combine this with the standard TPE contributions from box and crossed-box diagrams (with \(N\) and \(\Delta\) intermediate states in a hadronic model) and fit the single free parameter \(g\) to unpolarized cross-section data at fixed \(Q^2\). The resulting \(g\) is then used to predict the TPE corrections to polarized observables \(P_t\) and \(P_l\) and to the ratio \(R_{e^+/e^-}\) of unpolarized cross sections.

## Key numbers

* At \(Q^2 = 2.46\) GeV\(^2\), the extracted parameter \(g = -0.439\) (with error-bar weighting), with \(G_M = 0.136\) and \(R = \mu_p G_E/G_M = 0.704\).
* At \(Q^2 = 3.2\) GeV\(^2\), the extracted parameter \(g = -1.203\), with \(G_M = 0.101\) and \(R = 0.639\).
* At \(Q^2 = 4.1\) GeV\(^2\), the extracted parameter \(g = -6.377\), with \(G_M = 0.066\) and \(R = 0.556\).
* The full TPE correction to the ratio \(R_{PT}\) at \(Q^2 = 4.1\) GeV\(^2\) and \(\epsilon = 0.71\) is about 3\%.
* The Regge trajectory slope is taken as \(\overline{\alpha}_T = 0.8 (t - 1.3^2\ \text{GeV}^2)\), and results are insensitive to the slope in the range [0.7, 0.9].

## Context

This work extends the standard TPE corrections from box and crossed-box diagrams (studied in hadronic models by Blunden et al. (2003) and others) by adding a meson-exchange mechanism previously ignored in elastic \(ep\) scattering. It directly addresses the failure of existing TPE models (hadronic model, GPDs method, pQCD, dispersion relation) to reproduce the \(\epsilon\) dependence of polarized data at \(Q^2 = 2.49\) GeV\(^2\) from Meziane et al. (2011). The approach enables a consistent description of both unpolarized Rosenbluth data from Qattan et al. (2006) and polarized PT data from Punjabi et al. (2005), and predicts that measurements of \(R_{e^+/e^-}\) at \(Q^2 \approx 2.5\) GeV\(^2\) and small \(\epsilon\) will provide a decisive test.
