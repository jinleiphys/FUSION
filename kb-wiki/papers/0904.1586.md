# Extracting Electric Polarizabilities from Lattice QCD

> Charged and neutral, pion and kaon electric polarizabilities are extracted from lattice QCD using an ensemble of anisotropic gauge configurations with dynamical clover fermions. We utilize classical background fields to access the polarizabilities from two-point correlation functions. Uniform background fields are achieved by quantizing the electric field strength with the proper treatment of boundary flux. These external fields, however, are implemented only in the valence quark sector. A novel method to extract charge particle polarizabilities is successfully demonstrated for the first time.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate a novel lattice QCD method for extracting electric polarizabilities of both charged and neutral pseudoscalar mesons using uniform background electric fields, but the external fields are implemented only in the valence quark sector while the sea quarks remain electrically neutral, which is an approximation that would require at least an order of magnitude greater computing power to correct.

## Method

The authors used an ensemble of 200 anisotropic gauge configurations with 2+1 flavors of dynamical clover fermions on a 20³ × 128 lattice, implementing constant electric fields via modified Abelian gauge links satisfying 't Hooft quantization. For neutral mesons, they extracted polarizabilities by matching the long-time behavior of Euclidean two-point correlation functions to the effective hadronic theory; for charged mesons, they used an exactly solvable relativistic scalar propagator in the background field to fit the correlation functions, including contributions from the first excited state.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_π ≈ 390 MeV on the ensemble used
- Spatial lattice spacing: a_s = 0.123 fm
- Anisotropy parameter: ξ ≡ a_s / a_t = 3.5
- Lattice temporal extent: β = 128 a_t = 4.5 fm
- Expansion parameter for hadron deformation: (e ε / m_π²)² = 0.18 n², where n is the integer in the quantization condition
- Neutral pion connected polarizability (fit I): α_E^{latt} = -2.6(5)(9) in dimensionless lattice units, where α_E^{latt} = α_E (2 α_f.s. a_t a_s²)⁻¹
- Neutral kaon polarizability (fit I): α_E^{latt} = 1.5(4)(7) in dimensionless lattice units
- Charged pion and kaon rest energies at zero field: a_t E(0) = 0.0691(4) for π⁺ and a_t E(0) = 0.0967(3) for K⁺

## Context

This work extends earlier quenched lattice QCD calculations of neutral hadron electric polarizabilities by Fiebig (1988) and Christensen (2004) at pion masses above 500 MeV, and the dynamical calculation of the neutron electric polarizability by Engelhardt (2007) at 760 MeV, by being the first to include effects from dynamical quarks for pseudoscalar meson polarizabilities. The method enables future studies to determine the unknown low-energy constants of chiral perturbation theory by matching to lattice QCD computations, and to test predictions from chiral dynamics against experiment, particularly with anticipated results from COMPASS at CERN.
