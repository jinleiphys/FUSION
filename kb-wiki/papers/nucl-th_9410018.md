# Pion Content of the Nucleon in Polarized Semi-Inclusive D.I.s

> An explicit pionic component of the nucleon may be identified by measuring polarized $Δ^{++}$ fragments produced in deep-inelastic scattering off polarized protons. The pion-exchange model predicts highly correlated polarizations of the $Δ^{++}$ and target proton, in marked contrast with the competing diquark fragmentation process.

## Key claim

The pion-exchange model predicts highly correlated polarizations of the produced Δ⁺⁺ and the target proton in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering, in marked contrast with the competing diquark fragmentation process; the authors propose that measuring this polarization asymmetry can identify the explicit pionic component of the nucleon, which inclusive DIS can only place upper bounds on.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential cross section for semi-inclusive polarized Δ⁺⁺ electroproduction from a polarized proton target, e p → e' Δ⁺⁺ X⁻, using a pion-exchange model where the proton dissociates into a π⁻ and Δ⁺⁺. They compare this with a quark-parton model background using spin-dependent fragmentation functions for diquark fragmentation, employing SU(6) spin-flavor wavefunctions and parametrizing unpolarized EMC data on Δ⁺⁺ muon production. The polarization asymmetry (σ⁺ − σ⁻)/σ_tot is computed for both models at fixed kinematics.

## Key numbers

- For the CEBAF upgrade to E ≈ 8–10 GeV, values of x ≈ 0.13–0.14 can be reached in the deep inelastic region for ν ≈ 8 GeV and Q² ≈ 2 GeV².
- The slowest possible Δ particles produced at ζ = 1 (at x = 0) have momentum |p_min| = (M_Δ² − M²)/2M ≈ 340 MeV.
- The peak in the differential cross section for the pion-exchange process occurs at |p| ∼ 600 MeV, corresponding to W² ∼ 14 GeV² and missing mass p_X² ∼ 0.8 GeV².
- The pion-exchange model uses a form factor cut-off of Λ = 800 MeV, giving ⟨n⟩_{πΔ} ≈ 0.02.
- In the quark-parton model, the ratio of polarized Δ's is s = +3/2 : +1/2 : −1/2 : −3/2 ≈ 3 : 2 : 1 : 0.
- The unpolarized Δ⁺⁺ fragmentation function is parametrized as D_uu(z → 1) = a (1 − z)^b with a ≈ 0.68 and b ≈ 0.3 from EMC data.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the pion content of the nucleon in inclusive DIS (which could only extract upper bounds on average pion number) by proposing semi-inclusive polarized measurements as a direct probe. The authors contrast their pion-exchange model with the diquark fragmentation approach of Bartl et al. (1992) and use the Carlitz and Kaur (1977) model for spin-weighted quark distributions. The measurement would enable determination of the absolute value of ⟨n⟩_{πΔ} and test the relevance of non-perturbative degrees of freedom in high-energy DIS, with feasibility at CEBAF using the Large Acceptance Spectrometer.
