# The internal color degrees of freedom for the weakly interacting quarks and gluons

> When the phase transition from the hadronic matter to the deconfined quark-gluon plasma or quark-gluon liquid is reached, the color degrees of freedom become important and appear explicitly in the equation of state. Under the extreme conditions, the color degrees of freedom can not decouple from the other degrees of freedom. The conservation of color charges is maintained by introducing the color chemical potentials and their fugacities. We demonstrate the explicit role of color degrees of freedom in the hot and dense matter of the weakly interacting quarks and gluons. In order to illustrate our approach, we calculate the effective colored quark and gluon propagators as well as the hard thermal colored quark and gluon loops. The calculations are preformed under the assumption of the hard thermal loop approximation. Finally, we present the decay rate for the hard thermal colored quark. The present calculation is relevant to the quarks and gluons which are not truly deconfined quark-gluon plasma and behave as a fluid in the RHIC energy. The model can be explored in the LHC energy.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in the hot and dense matter of weakly interacting quarks and gluons, specifically in the intermediate phases such as quark-gluon fluid or Hagedorn matter that exist below the true deconfined quark-gluon plasma, the color degrees of freedom cannot be decoupled from other degrees of freedom and must be treated explicitly via color chemical potentials and their fugacities, rather than relying solely on the Polyakov loop parametrization which becomes questionable above the deconfinement phase transition.

## Method

The author extends the imaginary-time formalism of thermal field theory to include explicit color degrees of freedom by introducing fundamental and adjoint color chemical potentials for quarks and gluons, respectively, within the Cartan sub-algebra of the SU(N_c) symmetry group. Using the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation, the effective colored quark and gluon propagators are calculated, along with the HTL self-energy for soft colored quarks and the HTL polarization tensor for soft colored gluons. The decay rate for hard thermal colored quarks is then presented to illustrate the procedure when color degrees of freedom are treated explicitly.

## Key numbers

- The soft external momentum for quarks and gluons is taken of order \(p \sim g\,T\), while the hard internal loop momentum is of order \(T\), giving a ratio \(p/k \sim g\).
- For SU(3), there are two independent color chemical potentials, \({\mu_C}_1\) and \({\mu_C}_2\), corresponding to the two independent Polyakov loop variables \(\Phi\) and \(\Phi^*\).
- The fundamental color charges number \(N_c\) for quarks, while the adjoint color charges number \(N_c^2 - 1\) for gluons.
- The effective quark-quark-gluon vertex and effective two-quarks and two-gluons vertex are calculated up to corrections of order \(g^3\) and \(g^4\), respectively.

## Context

This work extends the standard HTL re-summation program of Braaten and Pisarski (1989-1992) by incorporating explicit color degrees of freedom, which are typically integrated out or treated via the Polyakov loop parametrization. The approach resembles the double-line basis method used by Hidaka et al. (2008-2009) for studying the semi-quark-gluon plasma, though the formalisms appear different. The calculation is relevant to the quark-gluon fluid observed at RHIC energies and can be explored at LHC energies, particularly for understanding intermediate phases such as Hagedorn or quarkyonic matter that exist between hadronic matter and the true deconfined quark-gluon plasma.
