# A Coupled-Channels Study of $^{11}Be$ Coulomb Excitation

> We study the effects of channel coupling in the excitation of $^{11}Be$ projectiles incident on heavy targets. The contribution to the excitation from the Coulomb and the nuclear fields in peripheral collisions are considered. Our results are compared with recent data on the excitation of the $\halfm$ state in $^{11}Be$ projectiles. We show that the experimental results cannot be explained, unless very unusual parameters are used.

## Key claim

The authors show that the experimental cross section for Coulomb excitation of the \(^{11}\)Be first excited state (\(1/2^-\)) cannot be explained by standard coupled-channels calculations including coupling to the continuum and nuclear excitation, unless very unusual parameters are used.

## Method

The authors performed semiclassical coupled-channels calculations for 45 MeV/A \(^{11}\)Be projectiles incident on \(^{208}\)Pb targets, assuming straight-line trajectories with relativistic corrections. They included Coulomb dipole coupling between the ground state (\(1/2^+\)), the first excited state (\(1/2^-\) at 320 keV), and discretized continuum states, using the experimentally determined B(E1) values and dipole response function. Nuclear excitation via monopole and quadrupole isoscalar modes was also incorporated using a folding optical potential with Tassie model transition potentials.

## Key numbers

- Experimental cross section for excitation of the \(1/2^-\) state: \(191 \pm 26\) mb
- First-order perturbation theory prediction using B(E1) = \(0.116 \pm 0.012\) \(e^2\) fm\(^2\) gives a factor of 2 larger than the experimental value
- Coupled-channels calculation restricted to bound states (Theory 1): 405.81 mb
- Coupled-channels including continuum coupling (Theory 3): 388.2 mb, only a 4% reduction from Theory 1
- Full calculation including nuclear excitation (Theory 4): 390.2 mb
- Continuum dissociation cross section (Theory 4): 335.2 mb
- B(E1; \(1/2^+ \rightarrow\) continuum) = 1.45 \(e^2\) fm\(^2\); B(E1; \(1/2^- \rightarrow\) continuum) = 4.06 \(e^2\) fm\(^2\)
- Nuclear excitation cross sections for monopole and quadrupole states: 7.07 mb and 6.22 mb respectively

## Context

This work addresses a discrepancy reported by Anne (1995) and collaborators, where the measured Coulomb excitation cross section of the \(1/2^-\) state in \(^{11}\)Be was a factor of 2 smaller than first-order perturbation theory predictions using the B(E1) value from lifetime measurements. The authors show that including channel coupling to the continuum and nuclear excitation effects, as suggested by Hansen (1995), cannot resolve this discrepancy, contrary to expectations. The results indicate that first-order perturbation theory is actually appropriate for this system, and further studies are needed to understand the experimental data, possibly involving a resonance in \(^{11}\)Be near threshold.
