# Quadrupole collectivity and shell closure in neutron-rich nuclei around $N=126$

> We present a comprehensive study on the low-lying states of neutron-rich Er, Yb, Hf, and W isotopes across the $N=126$ shell with a multi-reference covariant density functional theory. Beyond mean-field effects from shape mixing and symmetry restoration on the observables that are relevant for understanding quadrupole collectivity and underlying shell structure are investigated. The general features of low-lying states in closed-shell nuclei are retained in these four isotopes around $N=126$, even though the shell gap is overall quenched by about 30\% with the beyond mean-field effects. These effects are consistent with the previous generator-coordinate calculations based on Gogny forces, but much smaller than that predicted by the collective Hamiltonian calculation. It implies that the beyond mean-field effects on the $r$-process abundances before the third peak at $A\sim195$ might be more moderate than that found in A. Arcones and G. F. Bertsch, Phys. Rev. Lett. 108, 151101 (2012).

## Key claim

The general features of low-lying states in closed-shell nuclei are retained in neutron-rich Er, Yb, Hf, and W isotopes around $N=126$, even though the shell gap is overall quenched by about 30% with beyond mean-field effects; these effects are consistent with previous generator-coordinate calculations based on Gogny forces but much smaller than predicted by collective Hamiltonian calculations.

## Method

The authors performed multi-reference covariant density functional theory (MR-CDFT) calculations using the relativistic energy density functional PC-PK1, with particle-number projection, angular-momentum projection, and generator coordinate method (GCM) for shape mixing. The Dirac equation was solved by expanding Dirac spinors in three-dimensional harmonic oscillator basis with 14 major shells, and pairing correlations were treated with BCS approximation using a density-independent delta force. The calculations were constrained to axial symmetry with one-dimensional angular momentum projection.

## Key numbers

- The shell gap at $N=126$ is quenched by about 30% with beyond mean-field effects
- Excitation energy of the $2^+_1$ state at $N=126$ is approximately 2.5 MeV (compared to approximately 4.5 MeV predicted by Gogny force calculations)
- Dynamic correlation energy ranges between approximately 3.5 MeV and 5.5 MeV across the studied isotopes, with the minimum located at $N=126$
- For $^{194}$Er: particle-number projection gains about 2.07 MeV, angular-momentum projection to $J=0$ adds about 1.71 MeV, and shape mixing contributes around 0.98 MeV
- Angular-momentum projection decreases the $\Delta S_{2n}$ at $N=126$ by about 1.3 MeV; particle-number projection further decreases it by approximately 1.0 MeV
- The predicted $\Delta S_{2n}$ at $N=126$ varies from -2.0 MeV to -8.0 MeV across different models
- Discrepancy between model predictions and available data for $S_{2n}$ and $\Delta S_{2n}$ is generally within 1.0 MeV, except around $N=82$ where errors reach approximately 3 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous generator coordinate method calculations based on Gogny forces (Rodriguez 2015) and Skyrme energy functionals (Bender 2005, 2006, 2008; Tomas 2015) to the covariant density functional theory framework for neutron-rich nuclei around $N=126$. The results contradict the stronger beyond mean-field effects predicted by collective Hamiltonian calculations and imply that the impact on $r$-process abundances before the third peak at $A\sim195$ might be more moderate than found by Arcones and Bertsch (2012). The study enables improved predictions for nuclear masses and separation energies in the neutron-rich region critical for understanding the $r$-process nucleosynthesis path.
