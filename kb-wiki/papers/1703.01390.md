# Possible signatures for tetraquarks from the decays of $a_0(980)$, $a_0(1450)$

> Based on the recent proposal for the tetraquarks with the mixing scheme, we investigate fall-apart decays of $a_0(980), a_0(1450)$ into two lowest-lying mesons. This mixing scheme suggests that $a_0(980)$ and $a_0(1450)$ are the tetraquarks with the mixtures of two spin configurations of diquark and antidiquark. Due to the relative sign differences in the mixtures, the couplings of fall-apart decays into two mesons are strongly enhanced for $a_0(980)$ but suppressed for $a_0(1450)$. We report that this expectation is supported by their experimental decays. In particular, the ratios of the associated partial decay widths, which depend on some kinematical factors and the couplings, are found to be around $Γ[a_0(980)\rightarrow πη]/Γ[a_0(1450)\rightarrow πη] = 2.51-2.54$, $Γ[a_0(980)\rightarrow K\bar{K}]/Γ[a_0(1450)\rightarrow K\bar{K}] = 0.52-0.89$, which seems to agree with the experimental ratios reasonably well. This agreement can be interpreted as the tetraquark signatures for $a_0(980), a_0(1450)$.

# Possible signatures for tetraquarks from the decays of $a_0(980)$, $a_0(1450)$

## Key claim

The ratios of partial decay widths between $a_0(980)$ and $a_0(1450)$ into $\pi\eta$ and $K\bar{K}$ channels, calculated from a tetraquark mixing scheme where the two spin configurations add constructively for $a_0(980)$ but destructively for $a_0(1450)$, agree reasonably well with experimental data, but the agreement depends on which experimental data set (Bugg 2008 or PDG) is used, and the PDG-based ratio for $\pi\eta$ overshoots the theoretical estimate by 0.4 to 1.4.

## Method

The authors construct tetraquark wave functions for $a_0(980)$ and $a_0(1450)$ as mixtures of two spin-color configurations: $|000\rangle_{\bar{\bm{3}}_c,\bm{3}_c}$ (spin-0 diquark) and $|011\rangle_{\bm{6}_c,\bar{\bm{6}}_c}$ (spin-1 diquark), with mixing parameters $\alpha=0.817$, $\beta=0.577$ fixed from hyperfine mass diagonalization. They compute fall-apart decay couplings into two pseudoscalar mesons ($\pi\eta$, $K\bar{K}$, $\eta^\prime\pi$) by recombining the four-quark states into color-singlet spin-0 pairs, then calculate partial decay widths using effective Lagrangians with derivative couplings, averaging over the resonance mass distribution with an exponential Breit-Wigner-like function to account for the finite width of $a_0(980)$.

## Context

This work extends the tetraquark mixing scheme proposed by Kim and Kim (2016) for light scalar mesons, where $a_0(980)$ and $a_0(1450)$ are mixtures of spin-0 and spin-1 diquark-antidiquark configurations. It contrasts with models where these states are dynamically generated from meson-meson scattering (Boglione 2002, Wolkanowski 2015, van Beveren 1986, Dudek 2016) or where $a_0(1450)$ is a tetraquark mixed with a glueball (Maiani 2006) or $a_0(980)$ is a tetraquark-quarkonium mixture (Giacosa 2006). The predicted enhancement/suppression pattern in decay couplings provides a testable signature that could distinguish the pure tetraquark mixing picture from alternative interpretations. The authors note that similar analysis applied to $K_0^*(800)$ and $K_0^*(1430)$ yields a theoretical ratio $\Gamma[K_0^*(800)\rightarrow \pi K] / \Gamma[K_0^*(1430)\rightarrow \pi K] = 1.76$, but experimental data for $K_0^*(800)$ are currently insufficient for comparison.
