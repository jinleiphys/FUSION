# Rapid Spin Deceleration of Magnetized Proto-Neutron Stars via Asymmetric Neutrino Emission

> We calculate the spin deceleration of proto-neutron stars by asymmetric neutrino absorption in the context of a fully relativistic mean field theory. We calculate for the first time the spin deceleration of neutron stars due to asymmetric neutrino absorption in a toroidal magnetic field configurations. We find a surprising effect that the deceleration can much larger for asymmetric neutrino absorption in a toroidal magnetic field than the usually presumed braking due to magnetic dipole radiation. This may explain the observation that magnetars appear to have had a more rapid deceleration in the past.

## Key claim

The paper estimates the maximum possible contribution of asymmetric neutrino absorption to the early spin deceleration of proto-neutron stars, finding that while this effect can be larger than magnetic dipole radiation braking, it is still less than the angular momentum loss from magnetically locked material in the neutrino-energized wind.

## Method

The authors calculated neutrino absorption cross-sections in hot, dense magnetized neutron-star matter using fully relativistic mean field (RMF) theory, treating the magnetic field as a perturbation. They solved a one-dimensional Boltzmann equation for the neutrino phase-space distribution function assuming straight-line propagation and quasi-equilibrium, then computed the angular momentum loss rate for a toroidal magnetic field configuration aligned with the spin axis, parameterized to approximate results from magneto-hydrodynamic simulations.

## Key numbers

- For a toroidal magnetic field configuration Mag-A (peak at r0 = 8 km) with nucleonic matter, the spin-down rate is dot-P/P = 3.45 x 10^-6 s^-1 at the surface where baryon density rho_B = rho_0, and 7.25 x 10^-7 s^-1 at rho_B = rho_0/10, for a spin period P = 10 ms and neutrino luminosity L_nu = 3 x 10^52 erg/s.
- For the same Mag-A configuration with hyperonic matter (including Lambda particles), dot-P/P = 6.39 x 10^-6 s^-1 at rho_B = rho_0 and 1.02 x 10^-6 s^-1 at rho_B = rho_0/10.
- The magnetic dipole radiation spin-down rate for the same parameters is dot-P/P = 9.86 x 10^-8 s^-1 (nucleonic) and 7.76 x 10^-8 s^-1 (hyperonic), while the Thompson et al. (2004) neutrino-driven wind mechanism gives dot-P/P = 3.56 x 10^-3 s^-1 (nucleonic) and 3.50 x 10^-3 s^-1 (hyperonic).
- The moment of inertia of the neutron star is I_NS = 1.54 x 10^45 g cm^2 with Lambda particles and 1.36 x 10^45 g cm^2 without, for a baryonic mass of 1.68 solar masses, temperature T = 20 MeV, and lepton fraction Y_L = 0.4.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Maruyama et al. (2011, 2013) of asymmetric neutrino scattering in poloidal magnetic fields to the case of toroidal magnetic field configurations, which recent MHD simulations (Braithwaite and Spruit 2004; Takiwaki et al. 2009; Kotake and Maeda 2010) show can be amplified to ~100 times the poloidal field strength. The mechanism provides an additional contribution to early spin-down that should be considered alongside the neutrino-driven wind braking of Thompson et al. (2004) and the non-magnetic neutrino transport angular momentum loss described by Baumgarte et al. (1998), and may help explain the discrepancy between magnetar spin-down ages and supernova remnant ages noted by Nakano et al. (2011).
