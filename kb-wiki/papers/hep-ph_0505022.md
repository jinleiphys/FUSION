# Beam normal spin asymmetry in the equivalent photon approximation

> The two-photon exchange contribution to the single spin asymmetries with the spin orientation normal to the reaction plane is discussed for elastic electron-proton scattering in Regge regime. For this, the equivalent photon approximation is adopted. In this case, hadronic part of the two-photon exchange amplitude describes real Compton scattering (RCS). The imaginary part of helicity conserving RCS amplitudes are related to total photoabsorption cross section. The contribution of the photon helicity flipping amplitudes is estimated by the two pion exchange in the $t$-channel. We observe the double logarithmic enhancement in the electron mass but find it's contribution to the asymmetry negligibly small in the forward kinematics. These results are in strong disagreement with the existing calculation.

## Key claim

The beam normal spin asymmetry in elastic electron-proton scattering, calculated using the equivalent photon approximation in the Regge regime, is negligibly small in forward kinematics despite a double logarithmic enhancement in the electron mass, contradicting an existing calculation that predicted asymmetries of 25-35 ppm.

## Method

The authors used the equivalent photon (quasi-real Compton scattering) approximation to evaluate the two-photon exchange contribution to the beam normal spin asymmetry, where the hadronic part of the amplitude describes real Compton scattering. The imaginary part of helicity-conserving real Compton scattering amplitudes was related to the total photoabsorption cross section, while the photon helicity-flipping amplitudes were estimated via two-pion exchange in the t-channel using a Regge model for pion and rho photoproduction.

## Key numbers

- The total photoabsorption cross section used as input was approximately 0.1 mbarn
- The exponential suppression factor for the t-dependence of Compton cross section was B = 8 GeV^{-2}, valid for -t ≤ 0.8 GeV^2
- The Regge trajectory for the pion used α'_π = 0.7 GeV^{-2}
- The coupling constants used were f_{πNN}^2/4π = 0.08 and f_{ρπγ} = 0.103
- The calculation was performed for electron lab energies of 6 GeV, 9 GeV, 12 GeV, and 45 GeV
- The impact of the backward (A_1) amplitude was below 1% for all energies considered

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of two-photon exchange contributions to single spin asymmetries in elastic electron-proton scattering, which gained renewed interest after polarization transfer data (Gorchtein cites "gegm_exp") disagreed with Rosenbluth data (Gorchtein cites "rosenbluth"). The calculation strongly disagrees with the earlier work of Afanasev et al. (2005), which predicted asymmetries of 25-35 ppm for JLab energy of 5.77 GeV, finding instead much smaller values. The non-analytic behavior of the forward doubly virtual Compton scattering amplitude at the real photon point Q^2=0 is identified as the source of the discrepancy, generalizing a known phenomenon from the low-energy limit to the entire forward axis t=0.
