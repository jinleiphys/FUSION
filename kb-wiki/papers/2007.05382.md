# Impacts of dark matter on the curvature of the neutron star

> The effects of dark matter (DM) on the curvatures of the neutron star (NS) are examined by using the stiff and soft relativistic mean-field equation of states. The curvatures of the NSs are calculated with the variation of baryon density. Also, it is found that the radial variation of the different curvatures significantly affected by the presence of DM inside the NS. The effects of DM are less pronounced on the compactness of the maximum NS mass, but still significant. The NS surface curvature is found to be more remarkable for the massive star. The binding energy of the NSs become positive with the increasing DM momentum and makes the system unstable.

## Key claim

The presence of dark matter (DM) inside a neutron star significantly affects the radial variation of all curvature quantities (Kretschmann scalar, Ricci tensor, Ricci scalar, and Weyl tensor), with the magnitude of curvature increasing as the DM Fermi momentum increases, but the effect on the compactness of the maximum-mass star is less pronounced than for the canonical 1.4 solar mass star.

## Method

The authors use the extended relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) formalism with three parameter sets (NL3, G3, and IOPB-I) to compute the equation of state of neutron star matter, including a fermionic neutralino dark matter candidate that interacts with nucleons via the Standard Model Higgs boson. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved to obtain the mass and radius, and the curvature quantities (Ricci scalar, Ricci tensor, Kretschmann scalar, and Weyl tensor) are calculated from the energy density, pressure, and mass profiles as functions of radial distance.

## Key numbers

- For the NL3 parameter set without DM, the maximum mass is 2.774 solar masses with radius 13.16 km; with DM momentum 0.04 GeV, the surface curvature K(R) changes by approximately 33% compared to the no-DM case.
- For the canonical 1.4 solar mass star without DM, the surface curvature K(R) is 1.477 x 10^14 K_sun for NL3, 2.320 x 10^14 K_sun for G3, and 1.977 x 10^14 K_sun for IOPB-I.
- For the maximum mass star without DM, the surface curvature K(R) is 1.477 x 10^14 K_sun for NL3 (canonical), while for the maximum mass star the values are not explicitly given in the truncated text but the central densities are 870 MeV fm^-3 (NL3), 1340 MeV fm^-3 (G3), and 1100 MeV fm^-3 (IOPB-I).
- The spin-independent cross-section for DM masses of 50, 100, and 200 GeV is calculated to be 9.43, 9.60, and 9.70 x 10^-46 cm^2 respectively, consistent with XENON-1T, PandaX-II, and LUX limits.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutron star curvature by Kazim (2014) and Xiao (2015), who examined curvature without dark matter using RMF and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approaches, finding that symmetry energy affects curvature of lighter stars more significantly. The authors build on their own prior work (Das 2020) where they showed that DM softens the EoS and reduces mass-radius, and here they quantify how DM alters the gravitational curvature inside the star, which is relevant for testing general relativity in strong-field regimes and for interpreting gravitational wave signals from neutron star mergers.
