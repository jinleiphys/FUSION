# Correlation femtoscopy of multiparticle processes

> Recent results on particle momentum and spin correlations are discussed in view of the role played by the effects of quantum statistics, including multiboson and coherence phenomena, and final state interaction. Particularly, it is demonstrated that the latter allows for (i) correlation femtoscopy with unlike particles; (ii) study of the relative space--time asymmetries in the production of different particle species (e.g., relative time delays or spatial shifts due to collective flows); (iii) study of the particle strong interaction hardly accessible by other means (e.g., in two-lambda system).

## Key claim

Final state interaction (FSI) effects in particle correlations allow for femtoscopy with unlike particles, access to relative space-time asymmetries in particle production (such as time delays or spatial shifts from collective flows), and study of strong interactions in systems like the ΛΛ system that are otherwise difficult to probe.

## Method

The paper reviews the formalism of two-particle correlation functions at small relative velocities, defined as the ratio of the measured two-particle distribution to a reference distribution constructed by mixing particles from different events. The correlation function is expressed as the squared Bethe-Salpeter amplitude averaged over emitter coordinates and total spin, with the amplitude approximated by stationary scattering solutions under the equal-time approximation. For identical particles, the amplitude is symmetrized, and for non-identical particles, the correlation function depends on the unsymmetrized wave function, with the averaging accounting for spin population probabilities and particle polarization.

## Key numbers

- For ππ, πK, πp, KK, Kp, and pp pairs, the Bohr radii |a| are 387.5, 248.6, 222.5, 109.6, 83.6, and 57.6 fm, respectively.
- In a Gaussian model of the source, the correlation function takes the form 1 + λ exp(-r₀²q² - τ₀²q₀²), where r₀ and τ₀ are space-time dispersions.
- The fitted s-wave π⁺π⁻ scattering length from NA49 data, scaled by a factor sisca = 0.63 ± 0.08, is significantly lower than the original value f₀ = 0.232 fm.
- For the ΛΛ system, the singlet s-wave scattering length f₀ is estimated as 2.4 ± 2.1 fm for r₀ = 2 fm and 3.2 ± 5.7 fm for r₀ = 4 fm, likely excluding a large positive scattering length comparable to the ~20 fm of the two-nucleon system.
- From LEP ΛΛ correlations, the fitted Gaussian radius r₀ = 0.11 ± 0.02 fm is smaller than the string model lower limit of ~0.2 fm, and the parameter λ = 1.2 ± 0.2 exceeds the expected value of ~0.5, indicating a repulsive core in the ΛΛ interaction potential.
- For the NA49 pΛ correlation data, the fitted Gaussian radius is 2.9 ± 0.7 fm (with purity 0.17 ± 0.11), increasing to 3.8 ± 0.4 fm when purity is fixed at 0.33.
- For the AGS pΛ data, the fitted Gaussian radius is 4.5 ± 0.7 fm with purity 0.5 ± 0.2.

## Context

This work extends the foundational correlation femtoscopy framework established by Kopylov and Podgoretsky (1972) and the GGLP effect (1960), incorporating FSI effects that were previously treated as complications. The paper reviews how FSI enables correlation femtoscopy with unlike particles, as developed by Lednicky and Lyuboshitz (1981) and others, and discusses access to relative space-time asymmetries in particle emission, as introduced by Lednicky, Lyuboshitz, Erazmus, and Nouais (1995). The results enable simultaneous analysis of correlations and single-particle spectra to disentangle freeze-out characteristics, and open the possibility of studying strong interactions in systems like ΛΛ, which are relevant to the 6-quark H dibaryon problem and chiral perturbation theory.
