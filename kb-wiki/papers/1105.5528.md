# Gluon dissociation of $J/ψ$ in anisotropic {\em Quark-Gluon-Plasma}

> We calculate the gluon dissociation cross-section in an anisotropic quark gluon plasma expected to be formed due relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions. The initial rapid longitudinal expansion of the system leads to momentum space anisotropy due which the observables are affected in such a system. We show that the thermally weighted cross-section of gluon dissociation undergoes modification in anisotropic plasma leading to a decreased probability of $J/ψ$ suppression both for RHIC and LHC energies. The dependence of the cross section as well as the survival probability on the direction of propagation of the charmonium with respect to the anisotropy axis are presented. The sensitivity of the survival probability on the initial conditions have also been demonstrated.

# Gluon dissociation of $J/\psi$ in anisotropic Quark-Gluon-Plasma

## Key claim

The thermally weighted gluon dissociation cross section for $J/\psi$ is modified in an anisotropic quark-gluon plasma (AQGP) compared to the isotropic case, leading to a decreased probability of $J/\psi$ suppression at both RHIC and LHC energies, but only under certain initial conditions (specifically, Set II initial conditions with $T_i = 440$ MeV and $\tau_i = 0.15$ fm/c at RHIC); for Set I initial conditions ($T_i = 550$ MeV, $\tau_i = 0.7$ fm/c) the survival probability shows no difference between isotropic and anisotropic plasma at central rapidity.

## Method

The gluon dissociation cross section for $J/\psi$ was calculated using the perturbative prediction of Peskin and Bhanot, then thermally averaged over an anisotropic gluon distribution function characterized by an anisotropy parameter $\xi$ and a hard momentum scale $p_{\rm hard}$. The survival probability of $J/\psi$ was computed in a longitudinally expanding plasma using two different space-time evolution models for $\xi(\tau)$ and $p_{\rm hard}(\tau)$: Model I, based on a parametrized interpolation between free-streaming and hydrodynamic behavior with an isotropization time $\tau_{\rm iso}$, and Model II, derived from the first two moments of the Boltzmann equation including shear viscosity. The calculations were performed for two sets of initial conditions at RHIC energies and one set at LHC energies, and the dependence on the direction of $J/\psi$ propagation relative to the anisotropy axis was examined.

## Key numbers

- The maximum gluon-$J/\psi$ dissociation cross section is about 3 mb in the range $0.7 \leq q^0 \leq 1.7$ GeV (in the stationary $J/\psi$ frame).
- For RHIC Set I initial conditions: $T_i = 550$ MeV, $\tau_i = 0.7$ fm/c; for Set II: $T_i = 440$ MeV, $\tau_i = 0.15$ fm/c.
- For LHC initial conditions: $T_i = 820$ MeV, $\tau_i = 0.5$ fm/c.
- The critical temperature $T_c$ used to determine $\tau_c$ is in the range $170 - 200$ MeV.
- At LHC energies in the forward rapidity region, the survival probability increases by a factor of 2 compared to the central rapidity case for a given set of initial conditions.
- For Set II initial conditions at RHIC, an order of magnitude increase in the survival probability is observed compared to Set I.
- The thermal-averaged cross section decreases with increasing anisotropy parameter $\xi$ for $p_{\rm hard}$ up to approximately 500 MeV (at $P_T = 0$) and up to approximately 200 MeV (at $P_T = 8$ GeV), then increases relative to the isotropic case.

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation of $J/\psi$ dissociation in an equilibrating (isotropic) plasma by Xu (year not specified in text) to include initial-state momentum space anisotropy, which is expected due to the rapid longitudinal expansion in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The study builds on prior work on heavy quark potential in anisotropic QGP by Strickland and collaborators (year not specified) and on photon and dilepton production in anisotropic media by Schenke, Strickland, and others (year not specified). The results enable extraction of the isotropization time $\tau_{\rm iso}$ by comparing the transverse momentum distribution of $J/\psi$ with experimental data, similar to what has been done for photons and light hadrons.
