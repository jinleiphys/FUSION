# Toward Chiral Theory of NN interactions in nuclear matter

> The effective chiral theory of the in-medium NN interactions is considered. The shallow bound states, which complicate the effective field theory analysis in vacuum do not exist in matter. We show that the next-to-leading order terms in the chiral expansion of the effective Lagrangian can be interpreted as corrections so that the expansion is systematic. The Low Energy Effective Constants of this Lagrangian are found to satisfy the concept of naturalness. The potential energy per particle is calculated. The problems and challenges in constructing the chiral theory of nuclear matter are outlined.

# Toward chiral theory of NN interactions in nuclear matter

## Key claim
The shallow bound states that complicate effective field theory (EFT) analysis of the NN system in vacuum do not exist in nuclear matter due to Pauli blocking and the nuclear mean field, making the in-medium scattering length considerably smaller and the chiral expansion systematic, provided the next-to-leading order terms can be interpreted as corrections rather than requiring resummation.

## Method
The author constructs an effective chiral Lagrangian for NN interactions in the \(^{1}S_0\) state and solves the Bethe-Goldstone (BG) equation for the G-matrix in nuclear matter, using a separable potential with parameters adjusted to reproduce the empirical potential energy per particle as the "observable." Low-energy effective constants (LECs) \(C_0\) and \(C_2\) are determined by equating the EFT G-matrix to the phenomenological G-matrix at fixed kinematical points (\(p = p_F/2\) and \(p_F/3\)), with divergent integrals renormalized by subtraction at \(\mu = 0\) following the method of Gegelia (1999). The renormalization group equations for the LECs are derived and shown to reduce to those of Kaplan et al. (1998) in the \(p_F \to 0\) limit.

## Key numbers
- Fermi momentum used: \(p_F = 1.37\ \text{fm}^{-1}\)
- In-medium nucleon mass: \(M = 0.8 M_0\), where \(M_0\) is the vacuum nucleon mass
- Separable potential parameters: \(\lambda = 2.4\), \(\beta = 1.1\ \text{fm}\)
- Leading-order LEC: \(C_0 = -1.86\ \text{fm}^2\)
- Next-to-leading order LECs: \(C_0 = -2.7\ \text{fm}^2\), \(C_2 = 0.84\ \text{fm}^4\) (approximately 40% change in \(C_0\) from LO to NLO)
- Potential energy per particle in \(^{1}S_0\) channel: LO perturbative \(\simeq -12\ \text{MeV}\), LO G-matrix \(\simeq -17\ \text{MeV}\), NLO \(\simeq -13.1\ \text{MeV}\)
- Potential energy per particle in \(^{3}S_1\) channel: LO \(\simeq -17.3\ \text{MeV}\), NLO \(\simeq -13.2\ \text{MeV}\)
- Dimensionless naturalness coefficients: \(c_0 \simeq 0.7\), \(c_2 \simeq 0.65\) (assuming \(\Lambda \simeq 600\ \text{MeV}\))

## Context
This work extends the chiral EFT approach to NN interactions, previously developed for vacuum by Weinberg (1991) and Kaplan et al. (1998), into the nuclear matter domain following the framework of the Bethe-Goldstone equation from Bethe (1956). It builds on the effective chiral Lagrangian approach to nuclear matter by Serot and Walecka (1997), who demonstrated naturalness of coupling constants, and the counting rules discussion by Lutz (1998). The results suggest that pionic effects and many-body correlations, expected to be NLO corrections, could bring the calculated potential energies closer to standard phenomenological values from Tabakin (1964), and that the relevant expansion parameter may be \(\langle p \rangle / \Lambda \sim m_\pi / \Lambda \sim 0.3-0.4\).
