# Beam energy dependence of (anti-)deuteron production in Au+Au collisions at RHIC

> We report the energy dependence of mid-rapidity (anti-)deuteron production in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}} =\ $7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV, measured by the STAR experiment at RHIC. The yield of deuterons is found to be well described by the thermal model. The collision energy, centrality, and transverse momentum dependence of the coalescence parameter $B_2$ are discussed. We find that the values of $B_2$ for anti-deuterons are systematically lower than those for deuterons, indicating that the correlation volume of anti-baryons is larger than that of baryons at $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}}$ from 19.6 to 39 GeV. In addition, values of $B_2$ are found to vary with collision energy and show a broad minimum around $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}}=\ $20 to 40 GeV, which might imply a change of the equation of state of the medium in these collisions.

## Key claim

The yield of (anti-)deuterons in Au+Au collisions at RHIC is well described by a thermal model, but the coalescence parameter B2 shows a systematic difference between deuterons and anti-deuterons at beam energies from 19.6 to 39 GeV, and exhibits a broad minimum around 20 to 40 GeV that "might imply" (rather than definitively establishes) a change in the equation of state of the medium.

## Method

The STAR experiment measured mid-rapidity (anti-)deuteron production in Au+Au collisions at eight beam energies (7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV). Deuteron yields were extracted as a function of transverse momentum and centrality, and the coalescence parameter B2 was calculated from the ratio of deuteron to proton yields. The energy, centrality, and transverse momentum dependence of B2 was analyzed and compared to thermal model predictions.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron yields measured at eight collision energies: sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 11.5, 14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV
- B2 values for anti-deuterons are systematically lower than those for deuterons at sqrt(s_NN) from 19.6 to 39 GeV
- B2 shows a broad minimum around sqrt(s_NN) = 20 to 40 GeV
- Deuteron yields are well described by the thermal model across all measured energies

## Context

This work extends previous STAR measurements of light nuclei production in heavy-ion collisions by providing a systematic beam energy scan of (anti-)deuteron production across eight energies. The observed difference between deuteron and anti-deuteron B2 values indicates a larger correlation volume for anti-baryons compared to baryons in the 19.6-39 GeV range, which constrains models of hadronization and freeze-out dynamics. The broad minimum in B2 around 20-40 GeV suggests a possible change in the equation of state of the medium, motivating further theoretical work and future measurements at additional beam energies to map the QCD phase diagram.
