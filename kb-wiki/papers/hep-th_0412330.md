# Vertex functions and infrared fixed point in Landau gauge SU(N) Yang-Mills theory

> The infrared behaviour of vertex functions in an SU(N) Yang-Mills theory in Landau gauge is investigated employing a skeleton expansion of the Dyson-Schwinger equations. The three- and four-gluon vertices become singular if and only if all external momenta vanish while the dressing of the ghost-gluon vertex remains finite in this limit. The running coupling as extracted from either of these vertex functions possesses an infrared fixed point. In general, diagrams including ghost-loops dominate in the infrared over purely gluonic ones.

# Vertex functions and infrared fixed point in Landau gauge SU(N) Yang-Mills theory

## Key claim

The three- and four-gluon vertices become infrared singular (diverging as powers of momentum) **if and only if** all external momenta vanish simultaneously, while the ghost-gluon vertex remains finite in this limit; the running coupling extracted from any of these vertex functions possesses an infrared fixed point, supporting universality of the coupling in the non-perturbative regime.

## Method

The authors employ a skeleton expansion of the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) for the primitively divergent vertex functions of SU(N) Yang-Mills theory in Landau gauge, using fully dressed propagators and vertices. They develop counting rules for the infrared anomalous dimensions of diagrams by summing the exponents of the known power-law behavior of ghost and gluon propagators, then evaluate the leading diagrams analytically at symmetric momentum configurations using scalar massless triangle integrals. The consistency of the assumed power laws for propagators and vertices is checked by re-inserting the vertex results into the gluon propagator DSE.

## Key numbers

- Ghost propagator dressing function: \( G(p^2) \to (p^2)^{-\kappa} \) as \( p^2 \to 0 \)
- Gluon propagator dressing function: \( Z(p^2) \to (p^2)^{2\kappa} \) as \( p^2 \to 0 \)
- For a bare ghost-gluon vertex, the exponent \( \kappa = (93 - \sqrt{1201})/98 \approx 0.595 \)
- Three-gluon vertex dressing function: \( H^{3g}_1(p^2) \to (p^2)^{-3\kappa} \) as \( p^2 \to 0 \)
- Four-gluon vertex dressing function: \( H^{4g}_1(p^2) \to (p^2)^{-4\kappa} \) as \( p^2 \to 0 \)
- Ghost-gluon vertex dressing function: \( H^{gh-g}_1(p^2) \to \text{const.} \) as \( p^2 \to 0 \)
- Infrared fixed point value of the ghost-gluon coupling: \( \alpha(0) \approx 8.915 / N_c \) (for a bare ghost-gluon vertex)
- For \( N_c = 3 \), the fixed point falls in a window \( 2.5 < \alpha(0) < 3 \) when including vertex dressing

## Context

This work extends the infrared analysis of Yang-Mills theory beyond the propagator level, where previous DSE and Exact Renormalization Group Equation studies (von Smekal, 1997; Zwanziger, 2001; Lerche, 2002; Fischer, 2004) had established the infrared behavior of ghost and gluon propagators and the ghost-gluon coupling. The authors complete the picture by determining the infrared exponents of the three- and four-gluon vertices, verifying the self-consistency of the ghost-dominance scenario and the Gribov-Zwanziger confinement picture. The results enable future calculations of the precise numerical values of the infrared fixed point for the three- and four-gluon couplings, and provide a systematic counting rule for the infrared behavior of arbitrary n-point functions.
