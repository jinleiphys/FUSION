# Nuclear effects on lepton polarization in charged-current quasielastic neutrino scattering

> We use a correlated local Fermi gas (LFG) model, which accounts also for long distance corrections of the RPA type and final-state interactions, to compute the polarization of the final lepton in charged-current quasielastic neutrino scattering. The present model has been successfully used in recent studies of inclusive neutrino nucleus processes and muon capture. We investigate the relevance of nuclear effects in the particular case of $τ$ polarization in tau-neutrino induced reactions for several kinematics of relevance for neutrino oscillation experiments.

## Key claim

Nuclear effects (RPA correlations and final-state interactions) have a small impact on tau lepton polarization observables in charged-current quasielastic neutrino-nucleus scattering, except at very low scattering angles where they become non-negligible; the transverse polarization component is proportional to the lepton mass and thus significant only for tau leptons.

## Method

The authors used a correlated local Fermi gas (LFG) model of the nucleus, including Pauli blocking, experimental Q-values for energy balance, Coulomb distortion of charged leptons, random-phase approximation (RPA) corrections for medium polarization, and final-state interactions (FSI) via dressed nucleon propagators. They computed the differential cross section and two polarization components (longitudinal \(P_l\) and transverse \(P_t\)) for charged-current quasielastic \((\nu_\mu,\mu)\) and \((\nu_\tau,\tau)\) reactions on \(^{40}\)Ar, using the Galster parameterization for nucleon vector form factors and a dipole axial form factor.

## Key numbers

- For \((\nu_\mu,\mu)\) at \(E_\nu = 500\) MeV and \(q = 300\) MeV/c, RPA reduces the quasielastic peak cross section by almost 50%; this reduction drops below 15% for \(q = 500\) MeV/c.
- For \((\nu_\mu,\mu)\) at \(E_\nu = 500\) MeV, the transverse polarization \(P_t\) is around \(-0.2\) and nearly independent of \(q\).
- For \((\nu_\tau,\tau)\) at \(E_\nu = 7\) GeV and \(q = 300\)–500 MeV/c, \(P_t\) ranges from approximately \(-0.2\) to \(-0.25\), increasing with \(q\).
- For \((\nu_\tau,\tau)\) at \(E_\nu = 7\) GeV and scattering angles \(\theta = 2^\circ\) and \(4^\circ\), \(P_t\) takes values between approximately \(-0.2\) and \(-0.5\).
- For \((\nu_\tau,\tau)\) at \(E_\nu = 7\) GeV and \(\theta = 0^\circ\), the total polarization \(|\vec{P}|\) ranges between 0.8 and 0.9; for \(\theta = 2^\circ\), the polarization angle \(\Theta\) is between approximately \(15^\circ\) and \(25^\circ\).

## Context

This work extends the many-body nuclear model previously applied to inclusive muon capture and neutrino-nucleus reactions by Nieves et al. (2004) and Valverde et al. (2006), which successfully described \((e,e')\) data on \(^{12}\)C, \(^{40}\)Ca, and \(^{208}\)Pb. It is the first study to evaluate nuclear effects on lepton polarization using a model tested against electron scattering, contrasting with the earlier work of Graczyk and Sobczyk (2005) which used a relativistic LFG with an effective nucleon mass but was not tested against \((e,e')\) data. The results are relevant for neutrino oscillation experiments such as CNGS, ICARUS, and OPERA, where tau lepton polarization affects the decay particle distributions used for signal identification.
