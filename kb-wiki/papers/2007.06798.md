# Hyperfine structure of $^{173}\mathrm{Yb}^+$: toward resolving the $^{173}\mathrm{Yb}$ nuclear octupole moment puzzle

> Hyperfine structure (HFS) of atomic energy levels arises due to interactions of atomic electrons with a hierarchy of nuclear multipole moments, including magnetic dipole, electric quadrupole and higher rank moments. Recently, a determination of the magnetic octupole moment of the $^{173}\mathrm{Yb}$ nucleus was reported from HFS measurements in neutral ${}^{173}\mathrm{Yb}$ [PRA 87, 012512 (2013)], and is four orders of magnitude larger than the nuclear theory prediction. Considering this substantial discrepancy between the spectroscopically extracted value and nuclear theory, here we propose to use an alternative system to resolve this tension, a singly charged ion of the same $^{173}\mathrm{Yb}$ isotope. Utilizing the substantial suite of tools developed around $\mathrm{Yb}^+$ for quantum information applications, we propose to extract nuclear octupole and hexadecapole moments from measuring hyperfine splittings in the extremely long lived first excited state ($4f^{13}(^2\!F^{o})6s^2$, $J=7/2$) of $^{173}\mathrm{Yb}^+$. We present results of atomic structure calculations in support of the proposed measurements.

## Key claim

The paper proposes using the singly charged ion $^{173}\mathrm{Yb}^+$ as an alternative system to resolve a four-orders-of-magnitude discrepancy between the spectroscopically extracted nuclear magnetic octupole moment of $^{173}\mathrm{Yb}$ (from neutral atom measurements) and nuclear theory predictions, contingent on the successful combination of precision spectroscopy of the metastable $4f^{13}(^2\!F^{o})6s^2$, $J=7/2$ state with accurate atomic structure calculations.

## Method

The authors performed atomic structure calculations for the $^{173}\mathrm{Yb}^+$ ion in its first excited state ($4f^{13}(^2\!F^{o})6s^2$, $J=7/2$) using the Dirac-Hartree-Fock (DHF) approximation, treating the state as a single $4f_{7/2}$ hole in the $\mathrm{YbIII}$ core with two $6s$ electrons. They derived expressions for first-order hyperfine energy corrections in terms of constants $A$, $B$, $C$, $D$ (magnetic dipole, electric quadrupole, magnetic octupole, electric hexadecapole) and second-order corrections from dipole-dipole, dipole-quadrupole, and quadrupole-quadrupole interactions, then computed the electronic structure factors needed to extract nuclear moments from future spectroscopic measurements.

## Key numbers

- The experimentally determined nuclear magnetic dipole moment $\mu$ of $^{173}\mathrm{Yb}$ is $-0.680\,\mathrm{\mu_N}$ and the electric quadrupole moment $Q$ is $2.80\,\mathrm{b}$.
- The spectroscopically extracted nuclear octupole moment from neutral $^{173}\mathrm{Yb}$ is $\Omega = -34.4 \,{\mathrm{b\times{\mu_N}}}$, which is approximately $10^4$ times larger than the nuclear theory prediction of $\Omega = 0.003\,{\mathrm{b\times{\mu_N}}}$ from Williams (1962).
- The single-particle nuclear shell model predicts $\Omega = 0.15\,{\mathrm{b\times{\mu_N}}}$ for $^{173}\mathrm{Yb}$, while the extracted value is $-34.4 \,{\mathrm{b\times{\mu_N}}}$.
- The estimated electric hexadecapole moment $\Pi$ for $^{173}\mathrm{Yb}$ is approximately $9 \, \mathrm{b^2}$, based on the relation $\Pi \approx Q^2$.

## Context

This work extends the line of spectroscopic determinations of high-rank nuclear multipole moments, following the substantial discrepancy found for $^{173}\mathrm{Yb}$ by Singh (2013) in neutral Yb, where the extracted octupole moment disagreed with nuclear theory by four orders of magnitude. The paper builds on prior work by Beloy and Derevianko (2008) on hyperfine structure calculations and by Dzuba (2016) on hyperfine constants for Yb$^+$, and aims to enable an independent extraction of the nuclear octupole and hexadecapole moments using the well-developed quantum information tools available for Yb$^+$ ions, potentially resolving the tension with nuclear theory.
