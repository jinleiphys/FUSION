# NEUT Pion Final State Interactions

> The pion final state interaction model in NEUT is described. Modifications and tuning of the model are validated against neutrino and non-neutrino (pion scattering and photoproduction) data. A method for evaluating the uncertainties in the model and propagation of systematic errors in a neutrino oscillation experiment is described, using T2K as a specific example.

## Key claim

The pion final state interaction (FSI) model in NEUT was modified and tuned to improve agreement with pion scattering data, but the model's uncertainties are conservatively estimated by taking the maximum deviation across all FSI variations as the 1-sigma systematic error, rather than a statistically rigorous uncertainty propagation.

## Method

The NEUT pion FSI model uses a microscopic cascade that propagates pions in finite steps through the nucleus, with mean free paths calculated from a Delta-hole model at low momentum (p_pi <= 500 MeV/c) and from free pion-proton scattering cross sections at high momentum (p_pi > 500 MeV/c). The low-energy absorption and scattering mean free paths were tuned against pion-carbon scattering data, while the high-energy model was modified to use SAID partial wave analysis phase shifts for differential cross sections and to scale quasielastic and single charge exchange cross sections by a constant factor of 1.8. A reweighting scheme was developed that stores the full cascade trajectory for each Monte Carlo event and allows recalculation of event probabilities under variations of energy- and position-independent scaling parameters for each interaction mechanism.

## Key numbers

- Low-energy model applies for pion momenta p_pi <= 500 MeV/c; high-energy model applies for p_pi > 500 MeV/c
- Quasielastic and single charge exchange cross sections at high energy are scaled by a constant factor of 1.8, motivated by pion-carbon scattering data from Fujii and Jones
- 95% of pions produced with the T2K neutrino beam have momenta below 2 GeV/c
- Three low-energy scaling parameters were simultaneously fitted to pion-carbon data, yielding eight 1-sigma parameter sets (one from each octant of the 3-parameter space)
- For the T2K near detector CC inclusive measurement, the FSI uncertainty effect is less than 1%
- For the T2K-SK nue appearance sample, the maximum deviation across all FSI variations increases towards low neutrino energy due to non-CCQE events with all pions absorbed

## Context

This work extends the NEUT generator's FSI model originally described by Hayato, improving agreement with pion scattering data from Fujii, Jones, Ashery, Allardyce, and Lee, as well as photoproduction data from Baba and Arends. The reweighting scheme enables propagation of FSI uncertainties into oscillation analyses, as demonstrated in the T2K nue appearance analysis. The model was validated against MiniBooNE neutral-current single-pi0 data and Super-Kamiokande atmospheric neutrino data, and the approach allows future oscillation analyses to properly handle correlations between near and far detectors introduced by FSI uncertainties.
