# Scattering Neutrinos, Spin Models, and Permutations

> We consider a class of Heisenberg all-to-all coupled spin models inspired by neutrino interactions in a supernova with $N$ degrees of freedom. These models are characterized by a coupling matrix that is relatively simple in the sense that there are only a few, relative to $N$, non-trivial eigenvalues, in distinction to the classic Heisenberg spin-glass models, leading to distinct behavior in both the high-temperature and low-temperature regimes. When the momenta of the neutrinos are uniform and random in directions, we can calculate the large-$N$ partition function for the quantum Heisenberg model. In particular, the high-temperature partition function predicts a non-Gaussian density of states, providing interesting counter-examples showing the limits of general theorems on the density of states for quantum spin models. We can repeat the same argument for classical Heisenberg models, also known as rotor models, and we find the high-temperature expansion is completely controlled by the eigenvalues of the coupling matrix, and again predicts non-Gaussian behavior for the density of states as long as the number of eigenvalues does not scale linearly with $N$. Indeed, we derive the amusing fact that these \emph{thermodynamic} partition functions are essentially the generating function for counting permutations in the high-temperature regime. Finally, for the case relevant to neutrinos in a supernova, we identify the low-temperature phase as a unique state with the direction of the momenta of the neutrino dictating its coherent state in flavor-space, a state we dub the "flavor-momentum-locked" state.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for a class of Heisenberg all-to-all coupled spin models inspired by neutrino interactions in supernovae, where the coupling matrix has only a few non-trivial eigenvalues relative to the number of spins N, the high-temperature partition function predicts a non-Gaussian density of states, providing counter-examples to general theorems on the density of states for quantum spin models, and the thermodynamic partition functions become generating functions for counting permutations in the high-temperature regime.

## Method

The authors calculate the large-N limit of the high-temperature expansion of the partition function for both quantum and classical Heisenberg models (rotor models) with coupling matrices derived from neutrino forward scattering, where the momenta are uniformly and randomly distributed on the sphere. They evaluate traces of products of swap operators (for the quantum case) and Pauli operators (for the Ising case) using combinatorial methods involving conjugacy classes of symmetric groups and derangements, then apply the central limit theorem to sum over site indices. For the quantum Heisenberg model, they exploit the fact that the trace of products of exchange operators depends only on the number of disjoint cycles, and that at leading order in N the exchange operators effectively commute under the trace.

## Key numbers

* The high-temperature partition function for the rotor-distributed Ising model (SK-type) is \( Z^{\rm rot.}_{\rm SK}(\beta) = \frac{\exp(\mu\beta/4)}{(1 + \mu\beta/(2d))^{d/2}} \) with corrections of order \( O(N^{-1/2}) \), where d is the dimension of the sphere embedding the momentum vectors.
* The density of states for the rotor-distributed Ising model is \( p^{\rm rot.}_{\rm SK}(\omega) = \frac{(2d)^{d/2}}{\Gamma(d/2)\mu} (\omega/\mu + 1/4)^{-1+d/2} \exp(-2d(\omega/\mu + 1/4)) \Theta(\omega/\mu + 1/4) \).
* The trace of a product of L exchange operators in the Heisenberg model evaluates to \( \text{tr}[\exch{i_1 j_1} \cdots \exch{i_L j_L}] = n_f^{N+k} \), where k is the number of disjoint cycles in the permutation product and \( n_f \) is the number of neutrino flavors (with \( n_f=2 \) for the original Heisenberg interaction).
* For a cycle of length \( \lambda > 2 \), the trace including all permutations gives \( \tr[\exch{i_1 i_2} \cdot \exch{i_2 i_3} \cdots \exch{i_\lambda i_1} + \text{perms}] = \Gamma(1+\lambda) n_f^{N+2-\lambda} \).

## Context

This work extends the study of Heisenberg spin models with all-to-all couplings beyond the classic spin-glass models of Sherrington and Kirkpatrick (1978) and Bray and Moore (1980), where coupling matrices have Gaussian-distributed entries leading to Gaussian partition functions. The authors are motivated by neutrino forward scattering models in supernovae (Pehlivan 2011, PhysRevD.99.123013) and the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis (Deutsch 1991, Srednicki 1994). The results enable analytic calculation of phase diagrams for neutrino systems, identifying a low-temperature "flavor-momentum locked" (FML) phase, and provide a new approach to mean-field models where the control parameter is the number of non-zero eigenvalues of the coupling matrix. The work uses Leib inequalities (1973) to connect quantum and classical partition functions.
