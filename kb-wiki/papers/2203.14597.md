# New insights into four-boson renormalization group limit cycles

> Using machine learning techniques, we verify that the emergence of renormalization group limit cycles beyond the unitary limit is transferred from the three-boson subsystems to the whole four-boson system. Focussing on four identical bosons, we first generate populations of synthetic singular potentials within the latent space of a boosted ensemble of variational autoencoders. After introducing the limit cycle loss for measuring the deviation of a given renormalization group flow from limit cycle behavior, we minimize it by applying an elitist genetic algorithm to the generated populations. The fittest potentials are observed to accumulate around the inverse-square potential, which we prove to generate limit cycles for four bosons and which is already known to produce limit cycles in the three-boson system. This also indicates that a four-body term does not enter low-energy observables at leading order, since we do not observe any additional scale to emerge.

## Key claim

The paper verifies that renormalization group limit cycles beyond the unitary limit transfer from the three-boson to the four-boson system, with the inverse-square potential being the unique potential generating such limit cycles for four identical bosons, indicating that no four-body term enters low-energy observables at leading order.

## Method

The authors solve the low-energy Faddeev equation for four identical bosons in hyperspherical coordinates, using piecewise-constant two-body potentials and generalized transfer matrices to connect local solutions. They define a limit cycle loss (LCLoss) measuring deviation from log-periodicity of the coupling constant derived via delta-shell regularization, then minimize this loss using an elitist genetic algorithm operating in the 24-dimensional latent space of a boosted ensemble of variational autoencoders trained on synthetic potentials.

## Key numbers

- The fittest individual achieves an LCLoss of \(1.436 \times 10^{-2}\), indicating near-perfect log-periodicity.
- The fitted slope of the transformed potential is \(a_\text{fit} = (-2.555 \pm 0.135) \times 10^{-1}\), which deviates by less than \(1\sigma\) from the theoretical inverse-square slope of \(a = -1/4\).
- The latent space dimension is \(L = 3\) per autoencoder, with \(N_C = 8\) autoencoders giving an effective dimension of \(24\).
- The number of fixed transition radii is \(F = 10^3\), and the number of poles \(N_\Pi\) is scaled to approximately \(100\) at hyperangles \(\alpha = 0\) and \(\beta = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of three-boson RG limit cycles (Kaspschak and Meißner, 2021) to the four-boson system, confirming the Amado-Greenwood theorem's implication that no additional scale emerges for \(A \geq 4\). It supports findings by Platter et al. (2004) that tetramer energies are cutoff-independent once two- and three-body subsystems are renormalized, and aligns with the Tjon line correlations observed by Tjon (1975) and subsequent generalized Tjon lines for larger clusters. The results enable predictions of four-body limit cycle behavior solely from three-body knowledge, without introducing four-body forces at leading order.
