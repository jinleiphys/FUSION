# Energy spectra of two interacting fermions with spin-orbit coupling in a harmonic trap

> We explore the two-body spectra of spin-$1/2$ fermions in isotropic harmonic traps with external spin-orbit potentials and short range two-body interactions. Using a truncated basis of total angular momentum eigenstates, non-perturbative results are presented for experimentally realistic forms of the spin-orbit coupling: a pure Rashba coupling, Rashba and Dresselhaus couplings in equal parts, and a Weyl-type coupling. The technique is easily adapted to bosonic systems and other forms of spin-orbit coupling.

## Key claim

The paper presents non-perturbative numerical calculations of two-body energy spectra for spin-1/2 fermions in isotropic harmonic traps with spin-orbit coupling and short-range interactions, but the method converges well only when the ground-state energy is not too low; for small positive scattering lengths the coupling of the ground state to higher excited states makes convergence numerically intractable.

## Method

The authors numerically diagonalize the two-body Hamiltonian within a truncated basis of total angular momentum eigenstates derived from the interacting two-body problem without spin-orbit coupling (the Busch et al. (1998) solutions). Matrix elements for the spin-orbit coupling terms (Weyl, pure Rashba, and equal-weight Rashba-Dresselhaus) are computed analytically using angular momentum algebra, and the short-range interaction is treated as a regulated s-wave contact pseudopotential. The basis is truncated by including all states with energy below a cutoff \(E_{\text{max}}\), and convergence is checked by adding additional shells.

## Key numbers

- For the pure Rashba coupling, spectra were computed with an \(E_{\text{max}}\) of \(24\hbar\omega\), requiring approximately 36,000 basis states; all displayed eigenvalues shift by less than \(10^{-2}\hbar\omega\) when an additional shell is included.
- For the equal-weight Rashba-Dresselhaus coupling with a cutoff of \(E_{\text{max}}=17\), all displayed energies shift by less than \(0.02\hbar\omega\) when the final shell is added.
- The ground-state energy for the Weyl coupling is shown for \(\tilde{a}=-1\) and the unitary limit \(|\tilde{a}|\rightarrow\infty\) as a function of \(\tilde{\alpha}_W\), with the spectrum symmetric about \(\tilde{\alpha}_W=0\).
- For the pure Rashba coupling, the first correction to energies occurs at order \(\alpha_R^2\) and is independent of the scattering length \(a\), matching the perturbative predictions of PhysRevA.89.033606.

## Context

This work extends perturbative calculations of two-particle systems with Rashba spin-orbit coupling by PhysRevA.89.033606 (2014) to the non-perturbative regime, and complements the analytical solution for Weyl-type spin-orbit coupling without two-body interactions by Anderson (2013). The results enable future studies of few-body physics with spin-orbit coupling in trapped systems, and the authors note that the weak coupling of the ground state to center-of-mass excitations suggests cold atoms with spin-orbit coupling could serve as a surrogate system for probing parity-violating weak interactions in nuclear systems, as discussed by Haxton (2013) and deVries (2014).
