# Higher-Order Nuclear-Size Corrections in Atomic Hydrogen

> Nuclear-size corrections of order $(Z α)^5$ and $(Z α)^6$ to the S-state levels of hydrogenic atoms are considered. These nuclear-elastic contributions are somewhat smaller than the polarizability (nuclear-inelastic) corrections for deuterium, but are of comparable or larger size for the hydrogen case. For deuterium the (attractive) nonrelativistic $(Z α)^5$ correction to the 2S-1S transition is 0.49 kHz, while the (repulsive) relativistic $(Z α)^6$ contribution is -3.40 kHz. For the proton the corresponding corrections are 0.03 kHz and -0.61 kHz, respectively. The $(Z α)^5$ contribution largely cancels the Coulomb-retardation part of the nuclear-polarization correction.

## Key claim

The paper calculates nuclear-elastic (finite-size) corrections of order $(Z\alpha)^5$ and $(Z\alpha)^6$ to S-state energy levels in hydrogenic atoms, finding that the $(Z\alpha)^5$ term largely cancels the Coulomb-retardation part of the nuclear-polarization correction, while the $(Z\alpha)^6$ term is larger due to a large logarithm and its relativistic origin.

## Method

The authors evaluate the relativistic $(Z\alpha)^6$ correction $F_{\rm REL}$ and the nonrelativistic $(Z\alpha)^5$ Zemach-moment term using Eq. (1), which expresses the finite-size energy shift in terms of charge-density moments. For the proton, an exponential (dipole form factor) charge distribution with radius 0.862 fm is used; for the deuteron, the charge distribution is obtained by folding a bare-deuteron wave function (from multiple potential models) with proton and neutron dipole densities, with the neutron mean-square charge radius fixed to -0.114 fm$^2$. Double and triple integrals in $I_{\rm REL}$ are performed numerically using spline-fitted folded densities.

## Key numbers

- For deuterium: the $(Z\alpha)^5$ correction to the 2S-1S transition is +0.49 kHz (attractive); the $(Z\alpha)^6$ correction is -3.40 kHz (repulsive); the net higher-order finite-size correction is -2.91 kHz.
- For the proton: the $(Z\alpha)^5$ correction is +0.03 kHz; the $(Z\alpha)^6$ correction is -0.61 kHz; the net higher-order finite-size correction is -0.58 kHz.
- The deuteron $(Z\alpha)^6$ frequency shift $\nu^{(6)}_{\rm FS}$ is -3.40(1) kHz, with $I_{\rm REL} = -3.094(4)$ fm$^2$ and $\langle \ln(2\alpha\mu r)\rangle = -9.773(3)$ (spread over second-generation potential models).
- The proton $(Z\alpha)^6$ frequency shift is -0.61 kHz, with $\langle r^2\rangle = 0.743$ fm$^2$ for the exponential charge distribution.
- The Coulomb-retardation nuclear-polarization correction is -0.48 kHz for deuterium and -0.03 kHz for the proton (not included in totals, but largely cancelled by the $(Z\alpha)^5$ term).
- The isotope shift (deuteron minus proton) for the higher-order finite-size corrections is -2.33 kHz.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of nuclear finite-size corrections in muonic atoms (Friar 1979) to electronic hydrogen, motivated by precision spectroscopy of the 2S-1S transition with experimental uncertainties below 0.2 kHz (Schmidt-Kaler et al. 1995). The $(Z\alpha)^5$ term cancels the Coulomb-retardation part of the nuclear-polarization correction calculated for deuterium by Friar and Payne (1997). The results enable extraction of the deuteron-proton mean-square-radius difference from isotope-shift measurements, with sensitivity to relativistic corrections and meson-exchange currents.
