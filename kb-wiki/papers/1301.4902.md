# A brief review of antimatter production

> In this article, we present a brief review of the discoveries of kinds of antimatter particles, including positron, antiproton, antideuteron and antihelium-3. Special emphasis is put on the discovery of the antihypertriton and antihelium-4 nucleus which were reported by the RHIC-STAR experiment very recently. In addition, brief discussions about the effort to search for antinuclei in cosmic rays and study of the longtime confinement of the simplest antimatter atom, antihydrogen are also given. Moreover, the production mechanism of anti-light nuclei is introduced.

## Key claim

This review summarizes the experimental discoveries of antimatter particles from positron to antihelium-4, with special emphasis on the recent observations of antihypertriton and antihelium-4 by the RHIC-STAR experiment, while noting that the production rate of antinuclei decreases exponentially with baryon number, making heavier antinuclei such as antilithium-6 impossible to produce with current accelerator technology.

## Method

The paper reviews historical experiments using cloud chambers (positron discovery), fixed-target proton accelerators (antiproton, antideuteron, antihelium-3), and relativistic heavy-ion colliders (antihypertriton, antihelium-4). Particle identification relied on time-of-flight measurements, ionization energy loss (dE/dx) in time projection chambers, and invariant mass reconstruction from decay vertices. The production mechanisms are described using both thermodynamic (chemical freeze-out) and coalescence models.

## Key numbers

- 60 antiprotons detected at Bevatron, with mass equal to protons within uncertainties (Chamberlain, 1955)
- 5 antihelium-3 nuclei produced by 70 GeV proton beams on aluminum target; differential production cross section of antihelium-3 at 20 GeV/c and 27 mrad is 2.0 × 10⁻³⁵ cm²/sr·GeV/c per aluminum nucleus (AntiHe3)
- Ratio of differential cross section of antihelium-3 to pion is 1.3 × 10⁻¹⁰ (AntiHe3)
- 157 ± 30 hypertriton and 70 ± 17 antihypertriton observed via decay channel antihypertriton → antihelium-3 + π⁺ (STAR, 2010)
- Antihypertriton lifetime measured as 182⁺⁸⁹₋₄₅ ± 27 ps, corresponding to cτ = 5.5⁺²·⁷₋₁·₄ ± 0.08 (STAR)
- 18 antihelium-4 candidates observed by STAR experiment from 2007 and 2010 gold-gold collisions (He4bar)
- BESS balloon experiment upper limit of 1.9 × 10⁻⁴ (m² s sr GeV/nucleon)⁻¹ for cosmic-ray antideuteron differential flux at 95% confidence level between 0.17 and 1.15 GeV/nucleon (BESS1)
- BESS combined data upper limit of 6.9 × 10⁻⁸ for antihelium relative to helium abundance at 95% confidence level (BESS2)
- ALPHA experiment achieved antihydrogen confinement time of 1000 s (Anti2trap)
- Reduction rate of 1692 for each additional antinucleon added to antinucleus, compared to 1.6⁺¹·⁰₋₀·₆ × 10³ from STAR data (Xue-Ma)
- Predicted yield of antilithium-6 reduced by factor of 2.6 × 10⁶ compared to antihelium-4 (Xue-Ma)

## Context

This review extends the historical line of antimatter discoveries from the first positron observation by Anderson (1932) and antiproton by Chamberlain and Segrè (1955) through to the recent STAR collaboration results on antihypertriton (2010) and antihelium-4 (2011). It connects laboratory production with cosmic-ray searches by BESS and AMS, and with antihydrogen trapping by ALPHA. The paper enables future searches for heavier antinuclei in cosmic rays as potential signatures of primordial antimatter, and provides a framework for testing CPT symmetry and gravitational effects on antimatter through antihydrogen spectroscopy.
