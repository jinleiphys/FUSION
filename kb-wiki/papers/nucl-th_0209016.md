# Relativistic Random-Phase Approximation with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings

> The matrix equations of the relativistic random-phase approximation (RRPA) are derived for an effective Lagrangian characterized by density-dependent meson-nucleon vertex functions. The explicit density dependence of the meson-nucleon couplings introduces rearrangement terms in the residual two-body interaction, that are essential for a quantitative description of excited states. Illustrative calculations of the isoscalar monopole, isovector dipole and isoscalar quadrupole response of $^{208}$Pb, are performed in the fully self-consistent RRPA framework, based on effective interactions with a phenomenological density dependence adjusted to nuclear matter and ground-state properties of spherical nuclei. The comparison of the RRPA results on multipole giant resonances with experimental data constrains the parameters that characterize the isoscalar and isovector channel of the density-dependent effective interactions.

# Relativistic Random-Phase Approximation with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings

## Key claim
The paper derives the relativistic random-phase approximation (RRPA) for effective Lagrangians with density-dependent meson-nucleon vertex functions, showing that the explicit density dependence introduces rearrangement terms in the residual two-body interaction that are essential for quantitative agreement with experimental giant resonance energies. Without these rearrangement terms, discrepancies of several MeV arise between calculated and experimental excitation energies.

## Method
The authors derive the RRPA matrix equations from the small-amplitude limit of the time-dependent relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory, starting from a Lagrangian with density-dependent sigma, omega, and rho meson-nucleon couplings. The residual two-body interaction is obtained by expanding the meson-nucleon couplings and their derivatives around the ground-state vector density, yielding both direct meson-exchange contributions and rearrangement terms. Fully self-consistent RRPA calculations are performed for the isoscalar monopole, isovector dipole, and isoscalar quadrupole response of 208Pb, using the DD-ME1 effective interaction and a configuration space that includes both particle-hole pairs and pairs formed from hole states and negative-energy states from the Dirac sea.

## Key numbers
- The isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) in 208Pb is used to constrain the nuclear matter compression modulus K_infinity; a consistent set of density-dependent interactions is generated with K_infinity values ranging from 220 to 280 MeV.
- The free omega meson mass is taken as m_omega = 783 MeV and the free rho meson mass as m_rho = 763 MeV.
- The density-dependent interaction DD-ME1 has its seven coupling parameters and sigma meson mass simultaneously adjusted to properties of symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter and to ground-state properties (binding energies, charge radii, differences between neutron and proton radii) of twelve spherical nuclei.
- Without rearrangement terms, discrepancies of the order of several MeV are found between experimental excitation energies and RRPA peak energies calculated with relativistic effective interactions adjusted to ground-state properties.

## Context
This work extends the density-dependent relativistic hadron field (DDRH) model, previously applied to nuclear matter and ground-state properties by Typel and Wolter (1999) and to relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations by Niksic, Vretenar, and Ring (2002), into the RRPA framework for excited states. The approach provides an alternative to RRPA calculations with nonlinear meson self-interactions developed by Ma, Giai, and Wandelt (1997) and by Vretenar, Wandelt, and Ring (2000), and enables systematic constraints on the isoscalar and isovector channels of density-dependent effective interactions through comparison with experimental giant resonance data. The results can be used to determine the nuclear matter compression modulus, complementing non-relativistic Hartree-Fock plus RPA studies by Blaizot (1980, 1995) that suggest K_infinity in the range 210-220 MeV, and relativistic studies by Vretenar et al. (1997) and Ma et al. (2001) that favor K_infinity around 250-270 MeV.
