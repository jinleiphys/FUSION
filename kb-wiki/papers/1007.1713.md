# The Equation of State and Quark Number Susceptibility in Hard-Dense-Loop Approximation

> Based on the method proposed in [ H. S. Zong, W. M. Sun, Phys. Rev. \textbf{D 78}, 054001 (2008)], we calculate the equation of state (EOS) of QCD at zero temperature and finite quark chemical potential under the hard-dense-loop (HDL) approximation. A comparison between the EOS under HDL approximation and the cold, perturbative EOS of QCD proposed by Fraga, Pisarski and Schaffner-Bielich is made. It is found that the pressure under HDL approximation is generally smaller than the perturbative result. In addition, we also calculate the quark number susceptibility (QNS) at finite temperature and finite chemical potential under hard-thermal/dense-loop (HTL/HDL) approximation and compare our results with the corresponding ones in the previous literature.

## Key claim

The pressure density of QCD under the hard-dense-loop (HDL) approximation is generally smaller than the perturbative result of Fraga, Pisarski and Schaffner-Bielich (FPS), and when the quark chemical potential tends to infinity the HDL pressure density approaches the free quark gas result much more slowly than the FPS result does. The quark number susceptibility (QNS) at finite temperature and zero chemical potential obtained directly from the HDL quark propagator matches the result of a previous calculation that used the Ward-Takahashi identity.

## Method

The authors start from a model-independent formula that expresses the pressure density at finite quark chemical potential as an integral of the quark number density, which itself is determined by the renormalized quark propagator. They insert the hard-dense-loop (HDL) approximation for the quark propagator, valid for soft momenta, and introduce a momentum cutoff \(\Lambda_{HDL}=g\mu/\pi\) to restrict the Landau-damping contribution. The quark number density is computed by evaluating the Matsubara sum via contour integration, separating quasi-particle pole contributions from Landau damping. The pressure is then obtained by integrating the density over chemical potential, and the quark number susceptibility is computed by differentiating the density with respect to chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The quark number density under HDL approximation is slightly smaller than that of free quark gas (\(\rho_{free}(\mu)=N_c N_f \mu^3 / 3\pi^2\)); the result has minor sensitivity to the cutoff: changing the cutoff from \(g\mu/\pi\) to \(2g\mu/\pi\) changes \(\rho\) by about 10%.
- For \(\mu\) smaller than about 1 GeV, the HDL pressure density is smaller than the FPS perturbative result to order \(\alpha_s\), but larger than the FPS result to order \(\alpha_s^2\).
- For \(\mu\) larger than about 1 GeV, the HDL pressure density is smaller than the FPS result.
- The FPS EOS uses \(\Lambda_{\overline{MS}} = 365\ \mathrm{MeV}\) for \(N_f=3\) and the ratio \(\bar{\Lambda}/\mu\) is taken to be 2.

## Context

This work extends the method of Zong and Sun (2008) for calculating the pressure density from the quark propagator to the hard-dense-loop (HDL) approximation, providing a non-perturbative equation of state for cold dense QCD relevant for neutron star studies. The results are compared with the cold perturbative EOS of Fraga, Pisarski and Schaffner-Bielich (FPS) and with earlier Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) results. The quark number susceptibility calculation serves as a self-check of the method used by Blaizot and collaborators (HTL), confirming that the direct differentiation approach yields the same result at zero chemical potential. The work enables future applications to quark star structure calculations, including mass-radius relations and mass-central-density relations.
