# Instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter Look at the Lightest Pseudoscalar Mesons

> Within our description of Goldstone-type pseudoscalar mesons as almost massless bound states of quark and antiquark by a three-dimensional bound-state equation of Bethe-Salpeter origin, taking into account the pointwise behaviour of the full light-quark propagators enables to characterize the effective interquark interactions more precisely than earlier studies exploiting just specific aspects.

## Key claim
Within an instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter framework, the authors show that using the full pointwise behavior of light-quark propagators (rather than only specific aspects) yields a more precise characterization of the effective interquark interactions for Goldstone-type pseudoscalar mesons, but the resulting potential is obtained only in numerical form and approximated by elementary functions over a limited range.

## Method
The authors invert a three-dimensional instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (the Salpeter equation and a generalization) to reconstruct the effective interquark potential from known solutions. They impose flavor symmetry, assume Fierz and spherical symmetry for the interactions, and extract input from a chiral-limit model solution to the QCD quark propagator equation of motion. The inversion yields a confining potential that reproduces the Goldstone limit (zero bound-state mass) and is fitted to an analytic form with five parameters.

## Key numbers
- Average interquark distance for the pseudoscalar meson: ⟨r⟩ = 0.483 fm
- Root-mean-square radius: √⟨r²⟩ = 0.535 fm
- Measured pion electromagnetic charge radius: √⟨r_π²⟩ = (0.672 ± 0.008) fm (from PDG)
- Potential at the origin: V(0) = -1.92 GeV
- Zero crossing of the potential: r = 15.7 GeV⁻¹
- Fit parameters for V(r) ≈ a₀ + a₁ r + a₂ r² + b exp(c r): a₀ = -3.229 GeV, a₁ = 1.0095 GeV², a₂ = -0.083685 GeV³, b = 1.411×10⁻⁵ GeV, c = 0.8475 GeV

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Lucha and Schoberl (2013, 2015) that used only specific aspects of quark propagators to invert the Salpeter equation for pseudoscalar mesons. By incorporating the full pointwise behavior of light-quark propagators from a chiral-limit model by Maris and Tandy (1999), the authors obtain a confining potential that resembles a smoothed square well, in contrast to earlier findings. This enables more precise modeling of Goldstone-type mesons as quark-antiquark bound states and provides a potential that can be used in future three-dimensional bound-state calculations.
