# Theoretical aspects of relativistic spin hydrodynamics coupled with electromagnetic fields

> We expand the classical phase-space distribution function to incorporate couplings between spin and electromagnetic fields. This extension has led to the derivation of modified constitutive relations for the charge current, energy-momentum tensor, and spin tensor. Due to these couplings, the new tensors are revised from their perfect fluid analogues, creating an interplay between the background and spin fluid equations of motion. The corrections introduced in our framework have the potential to shed light on the experimentally observed discrepancies in the spin polarization measurements of Lambda hyperons.

## Key claim

By expanding the classical phase-space distribution function to include a coupling between spin and electromagnetic fields, the authors derive modified constitutive relations for charge current, energy-momentum tensor, and spin tensor that deviate from perfect-fluid analogues; these corrections may help explain the experimentally observed spin polarization splitting between Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons, but the framework assumes both small spin polarization (omega << 1) and weak electromagnetic fields (eB << M^2).

## Method

The authors start from the Juttner distribution for spin-1/2 particles and introduce an exponential coupling term between the magnetic moment of the particle and the Faraday tensor, linearizing both the spin polarization tensor and the spin-EM coupling under the assumptions of small polarization and weak fields. They then compute the first, second, and spin moments of the modified distribution function to obtain constitutive relations for the baryon charge current, energy-momentum tensor, and spin tensor, expressing the results in terms of thermodynamic coefficients and modified Bessel functions. The conservation laws for charge current, energy-momentum (including the Lorentz force back-reaction), and spin tensor yield the equations of motion for the fluid and spin polarization components.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic moment of the quasiparticles is defined as mu_M = g_Q * mu_N, with the Lambda hyperon gyromagnetic factor g_Lambda = -0.6138 +/- 0.0047 (ParticleDataGroup 2020).
- The number density of spinless, neutral classical massive particles is N_(0) = g * T^3 * z^2 * K_2(z), where g = 1/(2*pi^2), z = m/T, and K_2 is the second modified Bessel function of the second kind.
- The pressure and energy density of spinless particles are given by P_(0) = N_(0) * T and E_(0) = g * z^3 * T^4 * K_1(z) + 3 * P_(0), respectively.
- The thermodynamic coefficient A_3 appearing in the transverse current and spin tensor is A_3 = -2*(E_(0) + P_(0))/(T * z^2).

## Context

This work extends the perfect-fluid spin hydrodynamics framework developed by Florkowski et al. (2017-2021) and Singh et al. (2020-2021) by incorporating spin-EM field couplings into the phase-space distribution function, going beyond earlier models that treated spin and electromagnetic fields separately. The formalism is particularly motivated by the observed spin polarization splitting between Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons reported by the STAR Collaboration (2017) and Li et al. (2021), which may arise from their opposite magnetic moments as suggested by Becattini et al. (2016). The framework enables future studies incorporating realistic conditions (e.g., multiple quark-like quasiparticles with distinct masses) to bridge the gap between theoretical predictions and experimental observations in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.
