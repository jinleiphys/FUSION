# Recent results from QMC relevant to TJNAF

> We review recent calculations using the quark meson coupling model which should be of particular interest at Jefferson Lab. In particular, we discuss the change in the proton electric and magnetic form factors when it is bound in a specific shell model orbit. Modern quasi-elastic electron scattering experiments should be able to detect effects of the size predicted. We also examine the mean field potential felt by an $ω$ in a finite nucleus, concluding that the $ω$ should be bound by between 50 and 100 MeV.

## Key claim

The quark-meson coupling (QMC) model predicts that the proton's electric and magnetic form factors are modified when it is bound in a specific shell-model orbit of a finite nucleus, with the magnitude of the effect depending on the orbit's average baryon density; these predicted changes should be detectable in modern quasi-elastic electron scattering experiments. The omega meson should be bound in nuclei by between 50 and 100 MeV, forming omega-nucleus bound states, provided the in-medium width is not too large.

## Method

The authors used the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model in mean-field approximation for spherical finite nuclei, solving self-consistently for the nucleon and meson fields. For the nucleon form factor study, they calculated the density-dependent electromagnetic form factors of a proton in nuclear matter using the MIT bag model with Peierls-Thouless projection and a perturbative pion cloud, then folded these with the shell-model proton density for a given orbit in a finite nucleus. For the omega-mesic nuclei study, they embedded an MIT bag model omega meson in the self-consistently calculated mean fields of finite nuclei, solved the Dirac equations for the quarks and antiquarks, and then solved the Klein-Gordon equation (with a phenomenological imaginary part for the in-medium width) to obtain bound-state energies.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic moment of the proton in He-4 increases by about 7% relative to free space.
- The ratio G_E/G_M for a proton in He-4 runs from 0.41 at Q^2 = 0 to 0.28 at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2.
- For the 1s-orbit in O-16, the ratio G_E/G_M is about 2% lower than for the p-orbits in O-16.
- The omega meson 1s binding energy in O-16 is -93.5 MeV (with gamma_omega = 0), -93.4 MeV (gamma_omega = 0.2), and -93.4 MeV (gamma_omega = 0.4).
- The omega meson 1s binding energy in Ca-40 is -111 MeV for all three values of gamma_omega.
- The omega meson 1s binding energy in Pb-208 is -118 MeV for all three values of gamma_omega.
- The omega meson 1s binding energy in He-6 is -55.7 MeV (gamma_omega = 0), -55.6 MeV (gamma_omega = 0.2), and -55.4 MeV (gamma_omega = 0.4).
- The omega meson 1s binding energy in B-11 is -80.8 MeV for gamma_omega = 0 and 0.2, and -80.6 MeV for gamma_omega = 0.4.
- The omega meson 1s binding energy in Mg-26 is -99.7 MeV for all three values of gamma_omega.
- The full width of the omega meson 1s state in O-16 is 8.14 MeV (gamma_omega = 0), 30.6 MeV (gamma_omega = 0.2), and 53.1 MeV (gamma_omega = 0.4).
- The full width of the omega meson 1s state in Pb-208 is 8.35 MeV (gamma_omega = 0), 33.1 MeV (gamma_omega = 0.2), and 57.8 MeV (gamma_omega = 0.4).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier QMC calculations of nucleon electromagnetic properties in nuclear matter (Thomas et al., 1997) to finite nuclei with specific shell-model orbits, making predictions directly relevant to quasielastic scattering experiments at Jefferson Lab. The omega-mesic nuclei study extends the suggestion of Hayano et al. (1997) to use the (d,He-3) reaction to produce meson-nucleus bound states, providing QMC-based predictions for binding energies across a range of nuclei from He-6 to Pb-208. The results enable experimental searches for medium modifications of nucleon form factors in different shell-model orbits at TJNAF and Mainz, and for deeply bound omega-nuclear states at TJNAF and GSI.
