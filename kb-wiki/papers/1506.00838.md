# $η$ meson production of high-energy nuclear collisions at NLO

> The transverse momentum spectrum of $η$ meson in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is studied at the next-to-leading-order (NLO) within the perturbative QCD, where the jet quenching effect in the QGP is incorporated with the effectively medium-modified $η$ fragmentation functions using the higher-twist approach. We show that the theoretical simulations could give nice descriptions of PHENIX data on $η$ meson in both $\rm p+p$ and central $\rm Au+Au$ collisions at the RHIC, and also provide numerical predictions of $η$ spectra in central $\rm Pb+Pb$ collisions with $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$~TeV at the LHC. The ratios of $η/π^0$ in $\rm p+p$ and in central $\rm Au+Au$ collisions at $200$~GeV are found to overlap in a wide $p_T$ region, which matches well the measured ratio $η/ π^0$ by PHENIX. We demonstrate that, at the asymptotic region when $p_{T} \rightarrow \infty$ the ratios of $η/π^{0}$ in both $\rm Au+Au$ and $\rm p+p$ are almost determined only by quark jets fragmentation and thus approach to the one in $e^{+} e^{-}$ scattering; in addition, the almost identical gluon (quark) contribution fractions to $η$ and to $π$ result in a rather moderate variation of $η/π^{0}$ distribution at intermediate and high $p_T$ region in $\rm A+A$ relative to that in $\rm p+p$; while a slightly higher $η/π^{0}$ at small $p_T$ in $\rm Au+Au$ can be observed due to larger suppression of gluon contribution fraction to $π^{0}$ as compared to the one to $η$. The theoretical prediction for $η/ π^0$ at the LHC has also been presented.

## Key claim

The authors show that their NLO pQCD model with medium-modified fragmentation functions, using the same jet transport parameters previously extracted from π⁰ production, can simultaneously describe PHENIX data for η meson in both p+p and central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, and they provide predictions for Pb+Pb at LHC. The key hedge is that the η/π⁰ ratio in A+A and p+p overlaps in a wide pT region because the gluon (quark) contribution fractions to η and π⁰ are nearly identical, not simply because of flavor-dependent energy loss effects.

## Method

The authors compute η meson production at NLO in perturbative QCD using the higher-twist approach for jet quenching, where medium-modified fragmentation functions (mFFs) are derived from twist-4 processes. They employ the AESSS parametrization for η fragmentation functions, CTEQ6M parton distribution functions, and EPS09 NLO nuclear PDFs, with a 3+1D ideal hydrodynamic model to describe the QGP evolution. The single free parameter, the jet transport parameter \(\hat{q}_0\tau_0\), is fixed by fitting charged hadron multiplicity and previously used for π⁰ suppression.

## Key numbers

- η invariant cross section in p+p at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV: NLO calculation with scale \(\mu=1.0 p_T\) agrees well with PHENIX data (Fig. 1).
- Nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for η in central Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV: calculated with \(\hat{q}_0\tau_0 = 0.48-1.02\) GeV², fits PHENIX data in \(p_T = 2-20\) GeV range (Fig. 2).
- Predicted \(R_{AA}\) for η in central Pb+Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV: \(\hat{q}_0\tau_0 = 0.84-1.8\) GeV², showing suppression increasing with \(p_T\) (Fig. 3).
- η/π⁰ ratio in p+p and Au+Au at 200 GeV: NLO calculations match PHENIX data, with curves overlapping in a wide \(p_T\) region (Fig. 4).
- At asymptotic \(p_T \rightarrow \infty\), the η/π⁰ ratio approaches a universal value \(\sim 0.5\), determined only by quark fragmentation functions.
- Gluon contribution fraction to η and π⁰ yields in p+p at 200 GeV: \(G^{\pi^0}(p_T) \approx G^{\eta}(p_T)\) in the \(p_T\) region of 4-20 GeV (Fig. 6 top panel).

## Context

This work extends prior NLO pQCD studies of π⁰ suppression in heavy-ion collisions by Chen et al. (2010, 2011) to the η meson, which is the second most important source of decay electrons and photons after π⁰. It provides the first theoretical calculation of η production at high pT in heavy-ion collisions, addressing a gap noted by PHENIX measurements (Adler et al., 2006). The results enable better separation of direct photon signals from decay backgrounds and constrain the flavor dependence of parton energy loss in the QGP, complementing studies of full jets by Vitev et al. (2008, 2009) and Dai et al. (2012).
