# Self-consistent description of the halo nature of 31Ne with continuum and pairing correlations

> Using a Glauber model with our relativistic fully microscopic structure model input, we give a full description of the halo nature of 31Ne that includes a self-consistent use of pairing and continuum contributions that makes predictions consistent with reaction cross section measurements. Our predictions of total reaction and one-neutron removal cross sections of 31Ne on a Carbon target were significantly enhanced compared with those of neighboring Neon isotopes, agreeing well with measurements at 240 MeV/nucleon and consistent with a single neutron halo. Furthermore, our calculations of the inclusive longitudinal momentum distribution of the 30Ne and valence neutron residues from the 31Ne breakup reaction indicate a dilute density distribution in coordinate space, another halo signature.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a self-consistent combination of the relativistic RAB structure model (which includes pairing correlations and continuum contributions) with a Glauber reaction model can reproduce all measured reaction-based halo signatures of 31Ne, whereas previous non-relativistic Skyrme Hartree Fock (SHF) models (Horiuchi 2012) and Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) plus Resonating Group Method (RGM) approaches (Takenori 2012) had failed to match the measured cross section trends within 1σ.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic RAB model (relativistic mean field theory with the analytical continuation of the coupling constant method and BCS pairing approximation) to generate core density distributions and valence neutron wave functions for 26-31Ne. These structure inputs were then fed into a Glauber reaction model, which computes total reaction cross sections, one-neutron removal cross sections, and inclusive longitudinal momentum distributions via semi-classical phase-shift integrals. The core densities were fitted with five Gaussian functions to enable analytical evaluation of the phase-shift functions.

## Key numbers

- Measured reaction cross section increase from 30Ne to 31Ne on a 12C target at 240 MeV/nucleon: 86 ± 39 mb/amu, a factor of 2.2 ± 1.1 larger than the average increase from 26Ne to 30Ne (39 ± 7 mb/amu)
- Predicted reaction cross section increase from 30Ne to 31Ne: a factor of 3 larger than the average increase from 26Ne to 30Ne, matching the measured trend
- Calculated one-neutron removal cross section for 31Ne: approximately a factor of 6 larger than those of neighboring 27Ne and 29Ne
- Calculated full width at half maximum of the inclusive longitudinal momentum distribution for 30Ne residues from 31Ne breakup: approximately 76 MeV/c
- Measured FWHM of the 30Ne momentum distribution: approximately 120 ± 10 MeV/c (Nakamura 2014)
- Using the 30Ne density from Horiuchi (2012) with the authors' valence nucleon wave function gave a reaction cross section of approximately 1435 mb, an increase of 5.7% over the SLy4 result and 3.3% over the SkM* result

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous relativistic RAB model prediction of a p-orbital single neutron halo structure for 31Ne (Zhang 2014) by adding reaction-model calculations that produce observable halo signatures. It directly addresses the failure of prior non-relativistic approaches: SHF models (Horiuchi 2012) underestimated the cross section increase from 30Ne to 31Ne (SkM* force gave a 20% lower slope, SLy4 force gave only 80% of the needed increase), and AMD plus RGM calculations (Takenori 2012) remained nearly 1σ below measured data. The self-consistent approach enables future determination of halo structure in other exotic nuclei and motivates further studies including deformed structures.
