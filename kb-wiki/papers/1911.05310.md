# A generalized seniority approach to the prediction of spectroscopic factors in odd-mass Sn isotopes

> We present a study of the spectroscopic factors for the (d,p) stripping reactions to the ${11/2}^-$ states in the chain of odd-mass Sn (A = 115 - 131) isotopes by using a multi-j generalized seniority approach. The results are in line with realistic shell model calculations and explain the experimental trend quite well. The multi-j configuration used in these calculations is consistent with earlier calculations for moments etc., and therefore, lends credence to the generalized seniority interpretation. To the best of our knowledge, this work presents the first calculation of spectroscopic factors from such an approach.

# Generalized Seniority Approach to Spectroscopic Factors in Odd-Mass Sn Isotopes

## Key claim
The generalized seniority approach, using a multi-j configuration of \(h_{11/2} \otimes d_{3/2} \otimes s_{1/2}\) orbits with pair degeneracy \(\Omega = 9\), reproduces the experimental trend of (d,p) stripping spectroscopic factors for the \(11/2^-\) states in odd-mass Sn isotopes (A = 115–131) and agrees with realistic shell model calculations, though the authors note the generalized seniority results follow the trend "if not fully the magnitudes" of the shell model calculations.

## Method
The authors calculated spectroscopic factors for (d,p) stripping reactions to the \(11/2^-\) states using the generalized seniority formalism, where the reduced matrix elements of the creation operator in a \(\tilde{j}^n\) configuration are related to those in a \(\tilde{j}^v\) configuration via the Wigner-Eckart theorem. The lower-lying \(g_{7/2}\) and \(d_{5/2}\) orbits of the 50–82 shell were frozen, leaving \(h_{11/2}\), \(d_{3/2}\), and \(s_{1/2}\) as active orbits with generalized seniority \(v = 1\); the coefficients of fractional parentage become unity for the \(v = 0\) to \(v = 1\) transition, and the spectroscopic factor trend reduces to the factor \(\left( \frac{2\Omega + 2 - n - v}{2\Omega + 2 - 2v} \right)\) with \(\Omega = 9\). For comparison, shell model calculations were performed using the Sn100PN interaction with the Nushell code, truncating the \(g_{7/2}\) orbit for \(N < 74\) isotopes due to computational limitations.

## Key numbers
- Spectroscopic factor for \(^{115}\)Sn: experimental = 0.77, generalized seniority (GS) = 1.00, Andreozzi et al. (1996) = 0.89, preliminary data from Stuart thesis = 0.843(20)
- Spectroscopic factor for \(^{117}\)Sn: experimental = 0.79, GS = 0.89, Andreozzi et al. (1996) = 0.83, preliminary data = 0.892(12)
- Spectroscopic factor for \(^{119}\)Sn: experimental = 0.69, GS = 0.78, Andreozzi et al. (1996) = 0.70, preliminary data = 0.794(9)
- Spectroscopic factor for \(^{121}\)Sn: experimental = 0.49, GS = 0.67, Andreozzi et al. (1996) = 0.61, preliminary data = 0.801(22)
- Spectroscopic factor for \(^{123}\)Sn: experimental = 0.38, GS = 0.56, Andreozzi et al. (1996) = 0.52, preliminary data = 0.544(9)
- Spectroscopic factor for \(^{125}\)Sn: experimental = 0.42, GS = 0.44, Andreozzi et al. (1996) = 0.42, preliminary data = 0.451(8)
- GS predictions for heavier isotopes: \(^{127}\)Sn = 0.33, \(^{129}\)Sn = 0.22, \(^{131}\)Sn = 0.11
- Pair degeneracy of the active space: \(\Omega = 9\) (from \(h_{11/2}\), \(d_{3/2}\), and \(s_{1/2}\) orbits)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous generalized seniority studies of electromagnetic transition rates, moments, and g-factors in semi-magic nuclei (Maheshwari et al., 2016–2019; Jain et al., 2017) to single-particle transfer reactions for the first time. The results support the sub-shell gap at \(N = 64\) and the dominance of \(h_{11/2}\), \(d_{3/2}\), and \(s_{1/2}\) orbits above that gap, consistent with the pairing Hamiltonian calculations of Andreozzi et al. (1996). The approach enables simple calculation of spectroscopic factors without large-scale shell model diagonalization, and the authors propose extending these studies to other mass regions.
