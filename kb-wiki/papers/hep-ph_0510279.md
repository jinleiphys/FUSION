# Charge screening effect in the hadron-quark mixed phase

> The Coulomb interaction effect and the surface effect are consistently taken into account in the hadron-quark mixed phase. These two finite-size effects greatly change the properties of the mixed phase and restrict its density region. In particular, the charge screening effect and the rearrangement of the charged particles are elucidated. Keeping the Gibbs conditions throughout the numerical procedure, we show the Maxwell construction effectively regain the physical meaning and the equation of state becomes similar to that given by the Maxwell construction.

## Key claim

The authors show that when the Coulomb interaction and surface effects are treated self-consistently in the hadron-quark mixed phase, the charge screening effect and rearrangement of charged particles narrow the mixed phase region in baryon chemical potential space, making the equation of state similar to that given by the Maxwell construction, contrary to the wide mixed phase region predicted by the "bulk Gibbs" treatment that neglects finite-size effects.

## Method

The authors use a density functional theory with local density approximation, dividing space into Wigner-Seitz cells with a sharp boundary between hadron and quark phases. They solve the Poisson equation self-consistently together with equations of motion for particle densities (u, d, s quarks, protons, neutrons, electrons) under Gibbs conditions, using the MIT Bag model for quarks (Bag constant 120 MeV/fm³, QCD fine structure constant 0.4, strange quark mass 150 MeV) and a non-relativistic effective potential for nucleons. The surface tension parameter σ is treated as a free parameter, and the optimal cell and lump sizes are determined by minimizing the thermodynamic potential.

## Key numbers

- The transition between hadron and quark uniform matter occurs at baryon chemical potential μ_B = 1225 MeV
- For σ = 40 MeV/fm², the mixed phase region is narrower than for σ = 60 MeV/fm²; the negative δω region shrinks with increasing surface tension
- The droplet radius R = 7.2 fm and cell radius R_W = 12.8 fm for the "no Coulomb" case at μ_B = 1189 MeV and σ = 60 MeV/fm²
- The self-consistent calculation gives R = 7.7 fm and R_W = 18.9 fm for the same parameter set (μ_B = 1189 MeV, σ = 60 MeV/fm²)
- About 2/3 of the increase in thermodynamic potential from "bulk Gibbs" comes from surface tension and 1/3 from the Coulomb interaction

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Heiselberg et al. (1993) and Glendenning and Pei (1995) on geometrical structures in the hadron-quark mixed phase, which treated the Coulomb interaction perturbatively and assumed uniform charge densities. The authors build on the work of Voskresensky et al. (2003), who studied Debye screening analytically in a linear approximation, by performing a fully self-consistent numerical calculation that includes non-linear effects. The results show that the charge screening effect makes the mixed phase properties approach those of the Maxwell construction, which has implications for the equation of state of neutron star matter and the possible existence of crystalline structures in the mixed phase.
