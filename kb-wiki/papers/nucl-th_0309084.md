# Influence of the nucleon spectral function in photon and electron induced reactions on nuclei

> We study the influence of the nucleon spectral function on eta photo- and electroproduction on nuclei. Besides kinematical effects due to groundstate correlations, also a modification of the S11(1535) decay width is taken into account, which is caused by the possible decay into nucleons with mass smaller than the pole mass in the medium. Hence, resonances with masses below the free N eta threshold can contribute to eta production.

# Influence of the nucleon spectral function in photon and electron induced reactions on nuclei

## Key claim

The nucleon spectral function (finite width of nucleons in the nuclear medium) influences eta photo- and electroproduction on nuclei through two mechanisms: groundstate correlations that shift the invariant mass distribution of photon-nucleon pairs, and a modification of the S11(1535) resonance decay width that allows resonances with masses below the free N-eta threshold to contribute to eta production. The effect is more pronounced at large momentum transfer (high Q^2 in electroproduction) than in photoproduction.

## Method

The authors used a semi-classical BUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport model to simulate eta photo- and electroproduction on calcium nuclei. The nucleon spectral function was calculated using transport-theoretical methods for groundstate correlations (collision widths at energies below the Fermi energy) and for final-state interactions (collision rates for nucleon-nucleon scattering above the Fermi energy). The in-medium decay width of the S11(1535) resonance was modified by integrating over the nucleon spectral function in the decay phase space, analogous to how vacuum decays into unstable mesons are treated. The offshell propagation of nucleons was handled using an offshell potential that drives the nucleon mass toward the pole mass when traveling to regions of low density.

## Key numbers

- The S11(1535) resonance has a pole-mass decay width Gamma_0 = 0.151 GeV
- In photoproduction, a S11 resonance with pole mass has a momentum of 0.78 GeV; in electroproduction at Q^2 = 3.6 GeV^2, a momentum of 3.3 GeV
- For resonance momenta up to 1 GeV in photoproduction, the collision width is approximately 30 MeV at nuclear saturation density rho_0
- The nucleon width in the rest frame reaches values comparable to the rho meson vacuum width (250 MeV) at high momenta
- In electroproduction at Q^2 = 3.6 GeV^2 and E_gamma = 2.71 GeV, the offshell calculation without FSI shows an enhancement of up to approximately 20% near the maximum of the cross section compared to the onshell result
- The absolute (coherent) threshold for eta electroproduction on nuclei is at E_gamma approximately 0.6 GeV
- The minimal nucleon mass used in calculations was mu_N^min = 0.4 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous onshell calculations of eta photo- and electroproduction on nuclei (Lehr and Mosel, 2003, references photoeta and electroeta) by incorporating offshell effects from the nucleon spectral function. It builds on theoretical developments for offshell nucleon propagation in transport models (Effenberger et al., Cassing and Juchem, Leupold) and on many-body calculations of the nucleon spectral function in nuclear matter (Benhar et al., Baldo). The approach is similar to earlier folding-model studies of photon-induced K+Lambda production and proton-induced heavy meson production on carbon (Sibirtsev et al.). The results enable more accurate modeling of incoherent particle production on nuclei, particularly for reactions with high momentum transfer such as J/psi production where color transparency searches require precise descriptions.
