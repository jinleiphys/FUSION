# Projectile $Δ$ and target-Roper excitation in the p (d, d')X reaction

> In this paper we compare a model that contains the mechanisms of $Δ$ excitation in the projectile and Roper excitation in the target with experimental data from two (d, d') experiments on a proton target. The agreement of the theory with the experiment is fair for the data taken at T_d = 2.3 GeV. The $Δ$ excitation in the projectile is predicted close to the observed energy with the correct width. The theory, however, underpredicts by about 40% the cross sections measured at T_d = 1.6 GeV at angles where the cross section has fallen by about two orders of magnitude. The analysis done here allows to extract an approximate strength for the excitation of the Roper [N^*(1440)] excitation and a qualitative agreement with the theoretical predictions is also found.

## Key claim
The model, which includes both Δ excitation in the projectile and Roper excitation in the target, reproduces the shape and width of the Δ peak at T_d = 2.3 GeV but overpredicts the cross section at the maximum by at least 20%; after normalizing the theoretical Δ contribution by a factor of 0.85, the extracted Roper strength agrees qualitatively with predictions, though the theoretical Roper peak is shifted about 25 MeV higher in excitation energy than the data.

## Method
The authors used a theoretical model previously applied to the (α, α') reaction, incorporating Δ excitation in the deuteron projectile and Roper [N*(1440)] excitation in the proton target, including both πN and 2πN decay modes of the Roper and the interference between the Δ and Roper πN decay channels. They compared the model to two experimental datasets from Saclay: new (d, d') data at T_d = 2.3 GeV taken with the SPES4 spectrometer and POMME polarimeter, and older data at T_d = 1.6 GeV from Baldini et al. (1973). For the 2.3 GeV data, the theoretical Δ contribution was normalized by a factor of 0.85 to match the Δ peak, and the Roper strength was extracted by subtracting the normalized Δ and interference contributions from the measured spectrum.

## Key numbers
- At T_d = 2.3 GeV, the model overpredicts the cross section at the Δ maximum by at least 20%; a normalization factor of 0.85 was applied to the theoretical Δ contribution.
- The extracted Roper excess cross section (up to 540 MeV excitation energy) is 32 ± 8 mb/sr using the theoretical subtraction method, and 34 ± 8 mb/sr using an empirical subtraction method.
- The Roper excess has a width at half maximum of about 230 MeV, with a peak around 400 MeV (theoretical subtraction) or 350 MeV (empirical subtraction).
- At T_d = 1.6 GeV, the model underpredicts the cross section by about 30% at 6.59° and about 40% at 8.05°, where the cross section has fallen by about two orders of magnitude from forward angles.
- The predicted Roper cross section from the model (normalized by 0.85) is 28 mb/sr, compared to the experimental excess of 32 ± 8 mb/sr.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Δ excitation in the projectile and Roper excitation in the target from the (α, α') reaction at 4.2 GeV (Hirenzaki et al., 1999) to the (d, d') reaction on a proton target, using the same theoretical model and parameter values. The deuteron, like the alpha particle, has isospin I = 0, so Δ excitation on the proton target is forbidden in both cases, isolating the projectile-Δ and target-Roper mechanisms. The results confirm substantial strength for the isoscalar NN → NN* transition in the scalar channel, which has implications for three-body forces and for understanding NN → NNππ reactions, and should motivate further experiments at higher energies.
