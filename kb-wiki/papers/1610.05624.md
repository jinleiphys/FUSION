# Monte Carlo Implementation of Polarized Hadronization

> We study the polarized quark hadronization in a Monte Carlo (MC) framework based on the recent extension of the quark-jet framework, where a self-consistent treatment of the quark polarization transfer in a sequential hadronization picture has been presented. Here, we first adopt this approach for MC simulations of hadronization process with finite number of produced hadrons, expressing the relevant probabilities in terms of the eight leading twist quark-to-quark transverse momentum dependent (TMD) splitting functions (SFs) for elementary $q \to q'+h$ transition. We present explicit expressions for the unpolarized and Collins fragmentation functions (FFs) of unpolarized hadrons emitted at rank two. Further, we demonstrate that all the current spectator-type model calculations of the leading twist quark-to-quark TMD SFs violate the positivity constraints, and propose quark model based ansatz for these input functions that circumvents the problem. We validate our MC framework by explicitly proving the absence of unphysical azimuthal modulations of the computed polarized FFs, and by precisely reproducing the earlier derived explicit results for rank two pions. Finally, we present the full results for pion unpolarized and Collins FFs, as well as the corresponding analyzing powers from high statistics MC simulations with a large number of produced hadrons for two different model input elementary SFs. The results for both sets of input functions exhibit the same general features of opposite signed Collins function for favored and unfavored channels at large $z$, and at the same time demonstrate the flexibility of the quark-jet framework by producing significantly different dependences of the results at mid- to low-$z$ for the two model inputs.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that all current spectator-type model calculations of the eight leading-twist quark-to-quark transverse-momentum-dependent splitting functions violate positivity constraints, and proposes a quark-model-based ansatz that circumvents this problem for Monte Carlo simulations of polarized hadronization.

## Method

The authors adapted the extended quark-jet framework for Monte Carlo simulations with a finite number of produced hadrons, expressing the relevant probabilities in terms of the eight leading-twist quark-to-quark TMD splitting functions for elementary q → q' + h transitions. They validated the framework by proving the absence of unphysical azimuthal modulations and by reproducing earlier explicit results for rank-two pions, then performed high-statistics Monte Carlo simulations with at least 10^11 hadronization chains for two different model input elementary splitting functions.

## Key numbers

- At least 10^11 hadronization chains were simulated for each computation, using 100 points to discretize z and p_perp^2, and 600 points for azimuthal angles
- The Collins function ansatz uses a coefficient of 0.4 to satisfy positivity constraints, with the form (p_perp/(z M)) * (H^perp/D) = 0.4 * (2 p_perp M_Q)/(p_perp^2 + M_Q^2)
- The unpolarized splitting function was increased by a constant factor of 1.1 relative to the tree-level NJL model result
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom values for fits to polarized fragmentation functions at rank two were sharply peaked around 1, with maximum value not exceeding 1.5

## Context

This work extends the quark-jet framework of Bentz et al. (2016) for polarized hadronization, which itself built upon the NJL-jet model developed by Ito et al. (2009) and Matevosyan et al. (2010-2013). The paper addresses the problem of unphysical higher-order Collins modulations identified in earlier work by Matevosyan et al. (2012), and provides a Monte Carlo framework that enables simulation of the Collins effect, which was previously impossible in existing event generators like PYTHIA and LEPTO. The framework enables future extraction of independent quark fragmentation functions and can be used to describe data from COMPASS and other experiments.
