# An isotopic effect in phi photoproduction at a few GeV

> A distinct isotopic effect in phi photoproduction at 2-5 GeV region is identified by examining the production amplitudes due to Pomeron-exchange and meson-exchange mechanisms. This effect is mainly caused by the pi-eta interference constrained by SU(3) symmetry and the isotopic structure of the gamma NN coupling in the direct phi-radiation amplitude. It can be tested experimentally by measuring differences in the polarization observables between the gamma-p and gamma-n reactions.

# An isotopic effect in phi photoproduction at a few GeV

## Key claim

A distinct isotopic effect in phi photoproduction exists in the 2–5 GeV region, arising from pi-eta interference (constructive on proton, destructive on neutron) and from the different gamma-NN couplings in the direct phi-radiation amplitude. This effect is most clearly observable not in cross sections but in polarization observables such as the phi decay asymmetry, provided the form-factor cutoffs are not too hard (Lambda > about 1.5 GeV).

## Method

The authors construct a model for gamma N -> phi N containing three amplitude classes: (1) two Pomeron-exchange trajectories (P1 with intercept 1.08, P2 with intercept -0.75 inspired by a glueball), parameterized in the Donnachie-Landshoff factorized form; (2) pseudoscalar pi- and eta-exchange amplitudes, with couplings constrained by SU(3) symmetry and decay widths; and (3) direct phi-radiation from the nucleon legs, with phi-NN coupling from OZI-violation estimates. The P1 and P2 strength parameters are fitted to gamma-p -> phi-p cross-section data at theta=0 and to angular distributions, then the model is applied to predict gamma-n -> phi-n observables without further adjustment.

## Key numbers

- Pomeron intercepts: alpha_1(0) = 1.08, alpha_2(0) = -0.75; both with slope alpha' = 0.25 GeV^{-2}
- Strength ratio from fit: C_2/C_1 = -0.55, with C_1 = 2.34
- Form-factor slope B = 1.7 GeV^{-2}
- pi-NN coupling: g_piNN = (4 pi * 14)^{1/2}
- eta-NN coupling from SU(3): g_etaNN = (0.99 * 4 pi)^{1/2}
- phi-gamma-pi coupling: |g_phi-gamma-pi| = 0.14
- phi-gamma-eta coupling: |g_phi-gamma-eta| = 0.707
- phi-NN coupling: g_phiNN = -0.24, kappa_phi = 0.2
- Cutoff parameters: Lambda_pi = 0.7 GeV, Lambda^pi_phi-gamma-pi = 0.77 GeV; Lambda_eta = 1.0 GeV, Lambda_phi-gamma-eta = 0.9 GeV
- Predicted phi decay asymmetry Sigma_phi at E_gamma = 2 GeV shows isotopic differences of order 0.2–0.4 between proton and neutron targets at forward angles

## Context

This work extends the Donnachie-Landshoff Pomeron-exchange model (Donnachie and Landshoff, 1987) for vector-meson photoproduction to the few-GeV region by adding a second Pomeron trajectory with negative intercept, inspired by lattice QCD glueball predictions. It builds on the analysis of Williams (1998) but uses SU(3)-constrained parameters predetermined from other sources rather than fitting them to phi photoproduction data. The paper enables experimental tests of the eta-NN and phi-NN couplings through polarization measurements at facilities such as LEPS at SPring-8 and TJNAF, complementing the spin-density-matrix analysis presented in a companion paper by the same authors.
