# Electron scattering form factors from exotic nuclei

> The elastic electron scattering form factors, longitudinal and transverse, from the He and Li isotopes and from 8B have been studied. Large space shell model functions have been assumed. The precise distribution of the neutron excess has little effect on the form factors of the isotopes though there is a mass dependence in the charge densities. However, the form factors of the proton-rich nucleus, 8B, are significantly changed by the presence of the proton halo.

## Key claim
The precise distribution of the neutron excess (halo vs. non-halo) has little effect on the elastic electron scattering form factors of neutron-rich He and Li isotopes, though there is a mass dependence in the charge densities; however, the form factors of the proton-rich nucleus 8B are significantly changed by the presence of the proton halo.

## Method
The authors used large-space no-core shell model wave functions with complete (0+2+4)ℏω configurations and the G-matrix interaction of Zheng et al. (1995) to calculate longitudinal and transverse elastic electron scattering form factors. The halo was specified by using Woods-Saxon single-particle wave functions with binding energies set to the appropriate single-nucleon separation energies (1.8 MeV for 6He neutrons, 137 keV for 8B protons), while non-halo calculations used harmonic oscillator wave functions. Bare electromagnetic operators were used, with meson-exchange-current effects incorporated implicitly in transverse electric form factors via Siegert's theorem.

## Key numbers
- 4He charge radius predicted as 1.71 fm, compared to the measured value of 1.671 ± 0.014 fm (Otten 1985)
- The single neutron separation energy used for the 6He halo specification was 1.8 MeV
- The single proton separation energy from 8B used for the halo specification was 137 keV (TUNL 2004)
- The 4He form factor calculation agrees well with data up to 2.5 fm⁻¹
- The 6He oscillator parameter used for harmonic oscillator single-particle wave functions was 1.8 fm

## Context
This work extends prior studies by Antonov et al. (2005) and Bertulani (2006), who used the Helm model to obtain only longitudinal form factors for exotic nuclei. The present study provides both longitudinal and transverse form factors using a microscopic shell model approach, finding that neutron halos do not significantly affect electron scattering form factors (consistent with Bertulani 2006), while proton halos produce dramatic changes. The results enable future measurements at facilities such as the proposed electron-ion collider at GSI and the SCRIT system at RIKEN, allowing detailed mapping of charge densities in exotic nuclei.
