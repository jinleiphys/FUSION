# Effects of a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock-corrected effective mass on speed of sound, conformality, and observables of dark matter-admixed neutron stars

> We construct an equation of state describing cold and dense matter in the core of neutron stars which includes an admixture of fermionic dark matter and incorporates nucleon effective masses derived from the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) many-body approach within a relativistic mean-field model. Such a BHF-informed mixed-model approach increases stellar compactness, with mass-radius configurations which are consistent with smaller, lighter pulsars. The model displays the expected non-monotonic behavior of sound speed hinted at by neutron star data, and is closer to the conformal bound at maximum mass. We find that the model displays tension with bounds on heavier pulsars, suggesting that the hypothesis of an aggregated dark component in neutron stars needs further critical study.

## Key claim

The paper claims that incorporating Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF)-corrected nucleon effective masses into a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model for dark matter-admixed neutron stars increases stellar compactness and brings the sound speed closer to the conformal bound, but this comes with a hedge: the model shows tension with observational bounds on heavier pulsars (e.g., PSR J0740+6620), suggesting that the hypothesis of an aggregated dark component in neutron stars requires further critical study.

## Method

The authors construct an equation of state (EoS) for cold, dense matter in neutron star cores by combining a relativistic mean-field (RMF) chiral sigma model with fermionic dark matter, where the nucleon effective mass is replaced by a density-dependent ratio derived from relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations that include two- and three-body forces. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain mass-radius relations, tidal deformabilities, and moments of inertia for both purely hadronic and dark matter-admixed neutron stars, using the BSK19 crust EoS for low-density regions. Two benchmark parameter sets for the dark matter sector (BPI and BPII) are considered, with dark matter masses of 5 GeV and 15 GeV and a fixed dark matter Fermi momentum of 80 MeV.

- Radius at maximum mass for purely hadronic BHF case: 10.41 km; for BHF with BPII dark matter: 10.04 km
- Tidal deformability at \(1.4 M_\odot\) (\(\Lambda_{1.4}\)) for purely hadronic BHF case: 134; for BHF with BPII dark matter: 109

- Tidal deformability at \(1.4 M_\odot\) (\(\Lambda_{1.4}\)) for purely hadronic RMF case: 532; for RMF with BPII dark matter: 336
- Nuclear saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.153\) fm\(^{-3}\); nucleon effective mass ratio at saturation \(Y(\rho_0) = 0.87\); nuclear incompressibility \(K \approx 231\) MeV; binding energy per nucleon \(-16.3\) MeV; symmetry energy coefficient \(J = 32\) MeV; slope parameter \(L_0 = 88\) MeV
- Dark matter fraction in BHF-corrected DMANS model achieving agreement with HESS J1731-347: approximately 0.2%

## Context

This work extends prior studies of dark matter-admixed neutron stars (DMANS) within the BHF framework by Scordino (2024), which used chiral effective field theory (ChEFT) interactions limited to densities \(1\rho_0\)–\(2\rho_0\), by instead adopting an RMF model informed by microscopic BHF results to cover higher densities. It also builds on earlier work by Guha (2021) on the SU(2) chiral sigma model for DMANS, and on studies of nucleon effective mass effects in neutron stars by Li (2018) and others. The results enable future critical assessment of whether aggregated dark matter in neutron stars is consistent with observations of massive pulsars, and they highlight the need for models that simultaneously explain both low-mass compact objects (e.g., PSR J0437-4715) and high-mass pulsars (e.g., PSR J0740+6620).
