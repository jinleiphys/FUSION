# Collective enhancement of nuclear state densities by the shell model Monte Carlo approach

> The shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) approach allows for the microscopic calculation of statistical and collective properties of heavy nuclei using the framework of the configuration-interaction shell model in very large model spaces. We present recent applications of the SMMC method to the calculation of state densities and their collective enhancement factors in rare-earth nuclei.

## Key claim

The shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) method can microscopically compute state densities and their collective enhancement factors for heavy rare-earth nuclei, but the collective enhancement factor is defined as the ratio of SMMC densities to finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) densities, and its decay with excitation energy correlates with pairing and shape phase transitions rather than being a universal constant.

## Method

The authors used the SMMC approach with a truncated spherical shell model space consisting of the 50-82 major shell orbitals plus the 1f7/2 orbital for protons, and the 80-126 major shell orbitals plus the 0h11/2 and 1g9/2 orbitals for neutrons. The effective two-body interaction included monopole pairing terms for protons and neutrons, and multipole-multipole interactions with quadrupole, octupole, and hexadecupole terms. The thermal energy was calculated as the expectation value of the many-body Hamiltonian at inverse temperature beta, the canonical partition function was obtained by integrating the thermal energy with respect to beta, and the state density was extracted by inverting the Laplace transform in the saddle-point approximation. The collective enhancement factor was defined as the ratio of SMMC densities to HFB densities.

## Key numbers

- State densities were calculated for even-even isotopes: 148-154Sm and 144-152Nd.
- The SMMC densities were compared with level counting data (histograms) and neutron resonance data (triangles) when available, and with BBF state densities (solid lines) whose parameters were determined from level counting and neutron resonance data.
- In spherical nuclei 148Sm, 144Nd, and 146Nd, the collective enhancement factor K decays to approximately 1 above the pairing transition energies.
- In deformed isotopes (152Sm, 154Sm, 150Nd, 152Nd), collectivity persists at higher excitation energies than the pairing transition energies, and above the shape transition energy the collective enhancement factor decays to K approximately 1.

## Context

This work extends the SMMC approach previously applied to describe the rotational character of 162Dy (Alhassid 2008) to families of even-even samarium and neodymium isotopes, reproducing the crossover from vibrational to rotational collectivity (Ozen 2013a, Alhassid 2013a). The results provide a microscopic alternative to phenomenological collective enhancement factors used in the RIPL database, and enable future microscopic calculations of state densities for heavy nuclei where conventional diagonalization methods are intractable due to model spaces many orders of magnitude larger than those treatable by direct methods.
