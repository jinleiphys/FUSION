# Improved empirical parametrizations of the $γ^\ast N \to N(1535)$ transition amplitudes and the Siegert's theorem

> Some empirical parametrizations of the $γ^\ast N \to N(1535)$ transition amplitudes violates the Siegert's theorem, that relates the longitudinal and the transverse amplitudes, in the pseudo-threshold limit (nucleon and resonance at rest). In the case of the electromagnetic transition from the nucleon (mass $M$) to the resonance $N(1525)$ (mass $M_R$), the Siegert's theorem is sometimes expressed by the relation $|{\bf q}| A_{1/2}= λS_{1/2}$ in the pseudo-threshold limit, when the photon momentum $|{\bf q}|$ vanishes, and $λ= \sqrt{2} (M_R -M)$. In this article, we argue that the Siegert's theorem should be expressed by the relation $A_{1/2} = λ\frac{S_{1/2}}{ |{\bf q}|}$, in the limit $|{\bf q}| \to 0$. This result is a consequence of the relation $S_{1/2} \propto |{\bf q}|$, when $|{\bf q}| \to 0$, as suggested by the analysis of the transition form factors and by the orthogonality between the nucleon and $N(1535)$ states. We propose then new empirical parametrizations for the $γ^\ast N \to N(1535)$ helicity amplitudes, that are consistent with the data and the Siegert's theorem. The proposed parametrization follow closely the MAID2007 parametrization, except for a small deviation in the amplitudes $A_{1/2}$ and $S_{1/2}$ when $Q^2 < 1.5$ GeV$^2$.

# Improved empirical parametrizations of the γ*N → N(1535) transition amplitudes and the Siegert's theorem

## Key claim

The Siegert's theorem for the γ*N → N(1535) transition should be expressed as A_{1/2} = λ S_{1/2}/|q| in the pseudo-threshold limit |q| → 0 (where λ = √2 (M_R − M)), rather than the alternative form |q| A_{1/2} = λ S_{1/2} sometimes used; this correct form follows from the orthogonality between nucleon and resonance states, which forces S_{1/2} ∝ |q| near the pseudo-threshold.

## Method

The author derives the Siegert's theorem condition from the relation between the Dirac (F₁) and Pauli (F₂) form factors at the pseudo-threshold, using the helicity amplitude definitions at the resonance rest frame. A new parametrization (MAID-SG) is constructed for the A_{1/2} and S_{1/2} amplitudes, modifying the MAID2007 form by imposing S_{1/2} ∝ |q| and adding a Q⁴ term, then fitting the coefficients to the MAID2007 data.

## Key numbers

- The pseudo-threshold for the N(1535) transition occurs at Q² = Q²_PS ≈ −0.36 GeV²
- The MAID-SG parametrization uses a₀ = 54.99 (10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/²) for A_{1/2}, compared to a₀ = 66.40 in MAID2007
- The MAID-SG parametrization uses s₀ = −9.46 × (2M_R|q|/Q²₊) for S_{1/2}, compared to s₀ = −2.00 in MAID2007
- The two parametrizations become nearly indistinguishable for Q² > 1.5 GeV²
- The form factor F₂ becomes negligible for Q² > 1.5 GeV², leading to a correlation between A_{1/2} and S_{1/2}

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Siegert's theorem constraints from nuclear physics (Bjorken 1966, Drechsel 1992) and pion electroproduction (Tiator 2006, Drechsel 2007) to the γ*N → N(1535) transition. It corrects the MAID2007 parametrization (Drechsel 2007, Tiator 2016) which violates the Siegert's theorem, and provides a framework applicable to other transitions including γ*N → Δ(1232) and γ*N → N(1520) discussed in a separate article (Ramalho 2016). The new parametrization enables consistent extraction of transition form factors free of singularities at the pseudo-threshold.
