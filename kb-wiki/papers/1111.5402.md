# Effects of quarks on the formation and evolution of Z(3) walls and strings in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We investigate the effects of explicit breaking of Z(3) symmetry due to the presence of dynamical quarks on the formation and evolution of Z(3) walls and associated QGP strings within Polyakov loop model. We carry out numerical simulations of the first order quark-hadron phase transition via bubble nucleation (which may be appropriate, for example, at finite baryon chemical potential) in the context of relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments. Using appropriate shifting of the order parameter in the Polyakov loop effective potential, we calculate the bubble profiles using bounce technique, for the true vacuum as well as for the metastable Z(3) vacua, and estimate the associated nucleation probabilities. These different bubbles are then nucleated and evolved and resulting formation and dynamics of Z(3) walls and QGP strings is studied. We discuss various implications of the existence of these Z(3) interfaces and the QGP strings, especially in view of the effects of the explicit breaking of the Z(3) symmetry on the formation and dynamical evolution of these objects.

## Key claim

The presence of dynamical quarks explicitly breaks the Z(3) symmetry of the Polyakov loop effective potential, lifting the degeneracy of the three Z(3) vacua and making the Z(3) walls and QGP strings unstable and time-dependent; however, for a sufficiently small explicit symmetry breaking parameter (b₁ = 0.005), the first-order nature of the quark-hadron phase transition is preserved over a reasonable temperature range, allowing the formation and evolution of these topological objects to be studied via bubble nucleation simulations.

## Method

The authors model the confinement-deconfinement transition using the Pisarski mean-field effective potential for the Polyakov loop, augmented with a linear term (coefficient b₁) to represent explicit Z(3) symmetry breaking from dynamical quarks. They calculate bubble profiles for the true vacuum (l = 1) and the two metastable Z(3) vacua (l = z, z²) using Coleman's bounce technique in 3+1 Euclidean dimensions, after shifting the order parameter to place the confining vacuum at zero. These bubbles are then randomly nucleated in a false vacuum background and evolved using 2+1 dimensional field equations with a leap-frog algorithm, simulating the first-order phase transition via bubble nucleation and coalescence to study the formation and dynamics of Z(3) walls and QGP strings.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature T_c is set to 182 MeV.
- The explicit symmetry breaking parameter is chosen as b₁ = 0.005, which keeps the first-order transition up to approximately 225 MeV.
- At T = 200 MeV, the shift of the confining vacuum from l = 0 along the θ = 0 direction is ε = 0.0045.
- The angular difference between the direction of the metastable Z(3) vacuum (l = z) and the top of the corresponding potential barrier is approximately 0.9 degrees.
- A closed domain wall enclosing the true vacuum is expected to expand rather than contract when its size exceeds about 50 fm.
- The simulation lattice size used for relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments is approximately (15 fm)².

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of Z(3) walls and QGP strings in pure gauge theory (Gupta et al., earlier works) by incorporating explicit Z(3) symmetry breaking due to dynamical quarks, following the approach of Pisarski and others who treat quark effects via a linear term in the Polyakov loop potential. The results are relevant for interpreting possible signatures of these topological objects in relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments, particularly energy density fluctuations and enhanced transverse momentum of heavy-flavor hadrons, and may inform future studies of the QCD phase diagram at finite baryon chemical potential where the transition is expected to be first order.
