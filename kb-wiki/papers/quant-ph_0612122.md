# A q-Oscillator with 'Accidental' Degeneracy of Energy Levels

> We study main features of the exotic case of q-deformed oscillators (so-called Tamm-Dancoff cutoff oscillator) and find some special properties: (i) degeneracy of the energy levels E_{n_1} = E_{n_1+1}, n_1\ge 1, at the {\em real value} q=\sqrt{\frac{n_1}{n_1+2}} of deformation parameter, as well as the occurrence of other degeneracies E_{n_1} = E_{n_1+k}, for k \ge 2, at the corresponding values of q which depend on both n_1 and k; (ii) the position and momentum operators X and P {\em commute on the state} |m> if q is fixed as q=\frac{m}{m+1}, that implies unusual uncertainty relation; (iii) two commuting copies of the creation, annihilation, and number operators of this q-oscillator generate the corresponding q-deformation of the {\em non-simple} Lie algebra su(2)\oplus u(1) whose nontrivial q-deformed commutation relation is: [ J_+, J_- ] = 2 J_0 q^{2J_3-1} where J_0\equiv \frac12 (N_1-N_2) and J_3\equiv \frac12 (N_1+N_2).

# A q-Oscillator with 'Accidental' Degeneracy of Energy Levels

## Key claim

The Tamm-Dancoff cutoff q-oscillator, defined by the relation $a a^\dagger - q a^\dagger a = q^N$, exhibits accidental degeneracies of energy levels at real values of the deformation parameter $q$ (specifically $0 < q < 1$), unlike the Biedenharn-Macfarlane q-oscillator which requires complex $q$ (roots of unity) for such degeneracies. These degeneracies are "accidental" in that they involve single pairs of levels without underlying symmetry, and occur for nearest-neighbor levels, next-to-nearest-neighbor levels, and the ground state with higher levels.

## Method

The authors study the Tamm-Dancoff (TD) q-oscillator defined by the commutation relation $a a^\dagger - q a^\dagger a = q^N$ with number operator $N$, where the number operator satisfies $[N, a] = -a$ and $[N, a^\dagger] = a^\dagger$. They derive the Hamiltonian $H = (\hbar\omega/2)(a a^\dagger + a^\dagger a)$ (setting $\hbar\omega = 1$) and obtain the energy spectrum $E_n = \frac12[(n+1)q^n + n q^{n-1}]$ in the q-Fock space. They then solve the degeneracy condition $E_{n_1} = E_{n_2}$ for various pairs of levels to find the corresponding real $q$ values, and also examine the commutator $[X, P]$ on basis states and the construction of a q-deformed algebra from two commuting copies of the oscillator.

## Key numbers

- Energy spectrum formula: $E_n = \frac12[(n+1)q^n + n q^{n-1}]$ for $n \ge 0$, with $E_0 = 1/2$ for any $q$
- Nearest-neighbor degeneracy $E_m = E_{m+1}$ occurs at $q = \sqrt{m/(m+2)}$ for $m \ge 1$; example: $q = \sqrt{1/3} \approx 0.57735$ gives $E_1 = E_2$
- Next-to-nearest-neighbor degeneracy $E_m = E_{m+2}$ occurs at $q = [1 + \sqrt{4m^2 + 12m + 1}]/[2(m+3)]$ for $m \ge 0$; example: $q = 1/3 \approx 0.33333$ gives $E_0 = E_2$
- Ground state degeneracy $E_0 = E_m$: $q_2 = 1/3 \approx 0.33333$, $q_3 \approx 0.45541$, $q_4 \approx 0.53156446$, $q_5 \approx 0.585442$, $q_{10} \approx 0.725405$, $q_{25} \approx 0.851675$, $q_{50} \approx 0.910968$, $q_{100} \approx 0.948094$, $q_{200} \approx 0.9704016$, $q_{400} \approx 0.98340363$
- Position-momentum commutator vanishes on state $|m\rangle$ when $q = m/(m+1)$: $[X, P]|m\rangle = 0$
- The q-deformed algebra $U_q(su(2)\oplus u(1))$ has commutation relation $[J_+, J_-] = 2 J_0 q^{2J_3-1}$

## Context

This work extends the study of q-deformed oscillators beyond the well-known Biedenharn-Macfarlane (BM) type (Biedenharn, 1989; Macfarlane, 1989) and Arik-Coon (AC) type (Arik and Coon, 1976) by analyzing the Tamm-Dancoff cutoff oscillator previously introduced by Odaka and Jagan (1990s). The degeneracies found here differ from those in oscillator-like systems with cyclic symmetry studied by Quesne (2000s), which produce infinite series of degenerate pairs. The results enable further development of the TD q-Bose gas model, a one-parameter limit of the $q,p$-Bose gas model (Gavrilik and Rebesh, 2005), which describes non-Bose correlations in relativistic heavy-ion collision experiments.
