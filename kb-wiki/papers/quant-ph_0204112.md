# Phase equivalent chains of Darboux transformations in scattering theory

> We propose a procedure based on phase equivalent chains of Darboux transformations to generate local potentials satisfying the radial Schrödinger equation and sharing the same scattering data. For potentials related by a chain of transformations an analytic expression is derived for the Jost function. It is shown how the same system of $S$-matrix poles can be differently distributed between poles and zeros of a Jost function which corresponds to different potentials with equal phase shifts. The concept of shallow and deep phase equivalent potentials is analyzed in connection with distinct distributions of poles. It is shown that phase equivalent chains do not violate the Levinson theorem. The method is applied to derive a shallow and a family of deep phase equivalent potentials describing the $^1S_0$ partial wave of the nucleon-nucleon scattering.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a procedure based on phase equivalent chains of Darboux transformations to generate families of local potentials that share the same scattering data (phase shifts) but differ in their singularity strength at short distances and in the number of discrete bound states, with the condition that the transformation functions are chosen such that the derivative of the initial Jost solution does not appear in the expression for the transformed Jost function.

## Method

The authors construct chains of Darboux transformations of order N = 2n + ν using a set of eigenfunctions of the initial Hamiltonian h0, consisting of regular functions v_j (vanishing at the origin) and singular functions u_j (non-zero at the origin). They derive an analytic expression for the transformed Jost function FN(k) in terms of the initial Jost function F0(k) and a rational function of momentum k, which is independent of the free parameters in the singular transformation functions. By varying which poles of the S-matrix are assigned to regular versus singular families, or by using pairs of transformations with equal factorization constants, they generate potentials with the same S-matrix but different singularity strengths and numbers of bound states.

## Key numbers

* The transformed Jost function for a chain of order N = 2n + ν is given by FN(k) = F0(k) ∏_{j=1}^{ν} k/(k+ib_j) ∏_{j=ν+1}^{n} (k-ia_j)/(k+ib_j), where a_j and b_j are positive real parameters related to factorization energies.
* The phase shift for the transformed potential is δ_N(k) = δ_0(k) - Σ_{j=ν+1}^{n} arctan(k/a_j) - Σ_{j=1}^{n} arctan(k/b_j).
* The scattering length a^{(N)} satisfies 1/a^{(N)} = 1/a^{(0)} + [Σ_{j=ν+1}^{n} a_j^{-1} + Σ_{j=1}^{n} b_j^{-1}]^{-1}.
* The effective range r^{(N)} is given by r^{(N)} = r^{(0)} + (2a^{(N)}/3)[1 - (a^{(N)})^{-3}(Σ_{j=ν+1}^{n} a_j^{-3} + Σ_{j=1}^{n} b_j^{-3})].
* The Levinson theorem in generalized form states δ(0) - δ(∞) = [N + (1/2)(ν - l)]π, where N is the number of bound states, l the partial wave, and ν the singularity strength; for l=0 this is satisfied by the constructed potentials.

## Context

This work extends the supersymmetric (SUSY) quantum mechanics approach to phase equivalent potentials, previously developed by Sukumar (1985), Baye (1987), and applied to nucleon-nucleon and nucleus-nucleus scattering by Sparenberg and collaborators (1997, 2000). The method improves over earlier integral-form Darboux transformations by using a determinant (Wronskian) approach that avoids unwanted oscillations in the potential tail, as obtained in Baye (1987). The results enable the systematic construction of both shallow potentials (with only physical bound states) and deep potentials (with additional unphysical bound states simulating Pauli principle effects) for the 1S0 partial wave of nucleon-nucleon scattering, and can be extended to l-changing transformations for higher partial waves in future work.
