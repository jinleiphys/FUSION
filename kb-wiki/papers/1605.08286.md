# Reply to comments on `Structure effects in the $^{15}$N($n,γ$)$^{16}$N radiative capture reaction from the Coulomb dissociation of $^{16}$N'

> We reply to the comments (arXiv:1605.07499 [nucl-th]) on "Structure effects in the $^{15}$N($n,γ$)$^{16}$N radiative capture reaction from the Coulomb dissociation of $^{16}$N". We have investigated the issue of "energy dependence of branching ratios" and believe that this energy dependence is due to the proper inclusion of the non-resonant continuum in the post-form reaction theory. Interestingly, this energy dependence is sensitive to the relative orbital angular momentum content of the state. We reiterate that we have attempted to resolve the discrepancy in the spectroscopic factors of low-lying $^{16}$N levels and that it is essential to know the low energy $^{15}$N($n,γ$)$^{16}$N capture cross section, especially below 0.25 MeV.

## Key claim

The authors defend their claim that the branching ratios in the $^{15}$N($n,\gamma$)$^{16}$N capture reaction are energy dependent, attributing this dependence to the proper inclusion of the entire non-resonant continuum in their post-form reaction theory, and they note that this energy dependence is sensitive to the relative orbital angular momentum content of the state.

## Method

The Coulomb dissociation theory is formulated within the post-form finite range distorted wave Born approximation, where the electromagnetic interaction between the fragments and the target nucleus is included to all orders, and breakup contributions from the entire non-resonant continuum corresponding to all multipoles and relative orbital angular momenta between the fragments are accounted for. The only input to the theory is the ground state wave function of the projectile, of any orbital angular momentum configuration, which the authors argue makes their method free from uncertainties associated with multipole strength distributions that require exact positions and widths of continuum states. The branching ratio for the $^{15}$N($n,\gamma$)$^{16}$N capture reaction is defined as the ratio of cross section to a given state divided by the total cross section and plotted against center-of-mass energy.

## Key numbers

* The $2^-$ and $3^-$ states, which have energy dependent branching ratios, essentially contain $d$-wave neutrons.
* The $0^-$ and $1^-$ states, which have energy independent branching ratios and account for almost all of the total cross section, contain $s$-wave neutrons.
* It is essential to know the low energy $^{15}$N($n,\gamma$)$^{16}$N capture cross section, especially below 0.25 MeV.

## Context

This reply addresses comments on the authors' prior work (Shubhchintak, Neelam, and Chatterjee, 2016) concerning structure effects in the $^{15}$N($n,\gamma$)$^{16}$N radiative capture reaction from Coulomb dissociation of $^{16}$N. The authors' calculations favor the spectroscopic factors from one set of references against those of others, aiming to resolve a long-standing controversy on the spectroscopic factors of $^{16}$N states. The work enables further investigation into the energy dependence of spectroscopic factors in open quantum systems and highlights the need for low-energy data on the $^{15}$N($n,\gamma$)$^{16}$N capture cross section, which indirect approaches like Coulomb dissociation may help provide despite experimental difficulties.
