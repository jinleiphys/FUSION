# Flavoring the Quark-Gluon Plasma with Charm

> We demonstrate that a Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) with a dilute admixture of heavy quarks has, in general, a lower speed of sound than a "pure" QGP without effects from heavy flavors. The change in the speed of sound is sensitive to the details of the theory, making the hydrodynamic response to "flavoring" a sensitive probe of the underlying microscopic dynamics. We suggest that this effect may be measured in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions by relating the event-by-event number of charm quarks to flow observables such as the average transverse momentum.

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that a Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) with a dilute admixture of heavy quarks has a lower speed of sound than a "pure" QGP, but this effect is conditional on the charm quarks being thermally equilibrated and dilute, and the change is sensitive to the details of the underlying theory (e.g., whether QCD has asymptotic freedom or a supergravity dual).

## Method
The authors model the effect of a dilute charm quark admixture on the QGP equation of state by adding a Polyakov loop density term to the free energy density, using lattice data for the 2+1 QGP entropy density and Polyakov loop expectation values. They then compute the resulting speed of sound from thermodynamic relations and compare the predictions for QCD (with asymptotic freedom) versus gauge theories with holographic supergravity duals. The observable consequences are estimated by relating the dimensionless charm density parameter \(\tilde{\rho} = \rho/s\) to experimentally measurable quantities such as the number of charm quarks per unit rapidity and charged multiplicity.

## Key numbers
- The initial temperature at LHC is predicted to be 1.5-3 times the initial temperature at RHIC, which is 500-1000 MeV.
- The abundance of \(c\bar{c}\) pairs at LHC is expected to be \(\sim 10^{1-2}\), parametrically smaller than the total \(\sim 10^{4}\) multiplicity.
- The speed of sound squared for a charmed QGP with \(\tilde{\rho}=0.1\) is shown to be lower than the 2+1 QGP value near the crossover phase transition, with the effect concentrated near \(T_c = 0.185\) GeV.
- For the confined phase, the speed of sound of a mixture of heavy mesons and pions scales as \(c_s^2 \sim 1/3 - \mathcal{O}(\tilde{\rho} \, T/M_{\text{meson}})\), parametrically smaller than the deconfined phase contribution of \(\mathcal{O}(\tilde{\rho})\).
- The Polyakov loop fit parameters for QCD are \(a_1=1.24\) and \(b_1=2.89\) (Eq. f1), and for a supergravity-like theory are \(a_2=1.75\) and \(b_2=3.62\) (Eq. f2), both fitting lattice data at \(T_c=0.185\) GeV.
- For \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM theory, the speed of sound remains \(c_s^2=1/3\) regardless of \(\tilde{\rho}\) and \(\lambda\) in the infinitely heavy quark approximation, while the change in speed of sound squared from 1/3 is \(\sim -\mathcal{O}(\tilde{\rho} \, T \sqrt{\lambda} / M_Q)\) in the D3-D7 brane setup.

## Context
This work extends the study of the QGP equation of state beyond the standard 2+1 flavor case (Bazavov et al., lattice data) by incorporating the effects of a dilute charm admixture, building on prior work in perturbation theory (Laine, 2006), lattice simulations (DeTar et al.), and sum rules (Chernodub et al.). It also contrasts the QCD prediction with holographic approaches such as \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM (Maldacena) and D3-D7 brane models (Mateos et al., Myers et al.). The proposed observable, an anti-correlation between average transverse momentum \(\langle p_T \rangle\) and charm quark number \(N_{charm}\), could be measured at LHC energies, where event-by-event charm reconstruction becomes feasible, and may help distinguish between weakly coupled and strongly coupled descriptions of the QGP.
