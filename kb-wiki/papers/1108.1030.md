# Opportunities and Challenges for Theory in the N* program

> The N*-program provides a path to understanding the essentially-nonperturbative fundamentals at the heart of the Standard Model: confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. Relating this data to QCD's basic degrees-of-freedom is a key challenge for theory. In tackling it, one steps immediately into the domain of relativistic quantum field theory where within the key phenomena can only be understood via nonperturbative methods. No one tool is yet fully equal to the challenge. Nonetheless, the last few years have seen significant progress in QCD-based theory, and the reaction models necessary to bridge the gap between that theory and experiment.

## Key claim

The N* program can provide a path to understanding confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, but no single theoretical tool is yet fully equal to the challenge of relating experimental data to QCD's basic degrees-of-freedom. Progress requires combining QCD-based theory with reaction models that bridge the gap between theory and experiment.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical approaches deployed within the N* program, including Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs), lattice-regularized QCD, constituent-quark models, generalized parton distributions, and light-cone sum rules. It describes how the Faddeev equation, an analogue of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, sums all possible interactions between three dressed-quarks to treat mesons and baryons within a unified, symmetry-preserving framework. The author also discusses how the Excited Baryon Analysis Center (EBAC) performs combined analyses of data on single pi, eta, and K photoproduction of nucleon resonances, incorporating analysis of pi-pi final states.

## Key numbers

- Roughly 98% of the proton's mass arises from dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, not the Higgs mechanism (Flambaum 2005).
- A unified spectrum of u,d-quark hadrons using a symmetry-preserving regularization of a vector x vector contact interaction produces an rms relative error of 13% per degree of freedom when compared to PDG values (Nakamura 2010).
- The DSE study produces a radial excitation of the nucleon (Roper resonance) at 1.82 +/- 0.07 GeV, matching the value determined in the analysis of Suzuki (2009).
- Two acceptable gluon mass function forms are distinguished by their infrared value: M_g = 0.67 GeV and M_g = 0.81 GeV.
- The current-quark mass of 70 MeV is the uppermost curve in the dressed-quark mass function plot, with current-quark mass decreasing from top to bottom.

## Context

This paper extends the work presented at the NStar2009 conference and describes progress since that meeting, as documented in Aznauryan (2011). It builds on DSE studies by Chang (2011) and Qin (2011) that established running masses for gluons and quarks, and on the Faddeev equation approach of Cahill (1988) and Roberts (2011). The paper enables future work where experiment-theory feedback within the N* program can help chart the evolution of the quark mass function on the domain 0.3 to 1.2 GeV, bridging nonperturbative and perturbative QCD, and assist in unfolding the relationship between confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking.
