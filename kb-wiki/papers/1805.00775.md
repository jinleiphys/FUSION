# Nonequilibrium axial charge production in expanding glasma flux tubes

> Axial charge production at the early stage of heavy-ion collisions is investigated within the framework of real-time lattice simulations at leading order in QCD coupling. Starting from color glass condensate initial conditions, the time evolution of quantum quark fields under classical color gauge fields is computed on a lattice in longitudinally expanding geometry. We consider simple color charge distributions in Lorentz contracted nuclei that realize flux tube-like configurations of color fields carrying nonzero topological charge after a collision. By employing the Wilson fermion extended to the longitudinally expanding geometry, we demonstrate the realization of the axial anomaly on the real-time lattice.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that axial charge production via the axial anomaly can be realized on a real-time lattice in a longitudinally expanding geometry (Bjorken frame) using Wilson fermions, but only for simplified fixed color charge configurations that produce flux tube-like glasma fields with nonzero topological charge, rather than the fully random color charge distributions of the McLerran-Venugopalan model.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation for quantum quark fields under classical color gauge fields on a real-time lattice with longitudinally expanding geometry, using the color glass condensate (CGC) framework at leading order in QCD coupling. They construct specific color charge distributions for two colliding nuclei that produce flux tube-like configurations of longitudinal color electric and magnetic fields with nonzero topological charge density, then evolve the quark mode functions using Wilson fermion discretization extended to the expanding geometry. The backreaction from quarks to the gauge fields is neglected in this weak-coupling, strong-field limit.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale $Q_s$ characterizes the typical field strength of the glasma, which is much larger than light-quark masses, enabling intense quark production
- The lifetime of the magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions is expected to be short, $\lesssim 1$ fm/$c$
- The lattice uses $N_\perp \times N_\perp \times N_\eta$ grids with spacings $(a_\perp, a_\perp, a_\eta)$ and transverse system size $L_\perp = N_\perp a_\perp$ and longitudinal size $L_\eta = N_\eta a_\eta$
- The improved lattice derivatives use coefficients $c_1 = 4/3$ and $c_2 = -1/6$ for $O(a^3)$ improvement
- The flux tube width parameter $\Delta$ characterizes the Gaussian profile of the color fields, with the uniform glasma limit taken as $\Delta \to \infty$

## Context

This work extends real-time lattice simulations of the axial anomaly from nonexpanding systems (Aarts 1998, Saffin 2011, Buividovich 2015, Tanji 2016, Mueller 2016, Mace 2016) to the longitudinally expanding geometry specific to the early stage of heavy-ion collisions. The Wilson fermion method for the expanding geometry was first introduced in Tanji (2017). The results enable future studies of axial charge production as an initial condition for chiral magnetic effect phenomenology, which requires understanding of both the axial charge distribution and the space-time distribution of the magnetic field at early times.
