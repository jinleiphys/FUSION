# Ground state energy of dilute neutron matter at next-to-leading order in lattice chiral effective field theory

> We present lattice calculations for the ground state energy of dilute neutron matter at next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory. This study follows a series of recent papers on low-energy nuclear physics using chiral effective field theory on the lattice. In this work we introduce an improved spin- and isospin-projected leading-order action which allows for a perturbative treatment of corrections at next-to-leading order and smaller estimated errors. Using auxiliary fields and Euclidean-time projection Monte Carlo, we compute the ground state of 8, 12, and 16 neutrons in a periodic cube, covering a density range from 2% to 10% of normal nuclear density.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new leading-order lattice action (LO₃) that reproduces the S-wave behavior of the Gaussian-smeared LO₂ action while matching the point-like LO₁ action in P-wave channels, thereby removing unphysical attractive P-wave forces that had compromised the perturbative treatment of next-to-leading order corrections at higher densities.

## Method

The authors construct the LO₃ transfer matrix using spin- and isospin-projected contact interactions that apply Gaussian smearing only in the spin-singlet/isospin-triplet and spin-triplet/isospin-singlet channels, while keeping point-like interactions in all other channels. They compute nucleon-nucleon phase shifts and the deuteron quadrupole moment using the spherical wall method on a lattice with spatial spacing a = (100 MeV)⁻¹ and temporal spacing a_t = (70 MeV)⁻¹, then fit nine next-to-leading order operator coefficients to match eight spherical wall energy levels and the deuteron quadrupole moment. For many-neutron systems, they rewrite the LO₃ transfer matrix using auxiliary fields and perform Euclidean-time projection Monte Carlo to compute ground state energies of 8, 12, and 16 neutrons in a periodic cube.

## Key numbers

- Spatial lattice spacing a = (100 MeV)⁻¹ and temporal lattice spacing a_t = (70 MeV)⁻¹, giving cutoff momentum Λ = 314 MeV ≈ 2.3 m_π
- LO₃ S-wave contact coefficients: C_{S=0,I=1} = -3.414 × 10⁻⁵ MeV⁻² and C_{S=1,I=0} = -4.780 × 10⁻⁵ MeV⁻²
- LO₃ reproduces the 1¹S₀ spherical wall energy at 0.418 MeV (target 0.407 MeV from PWA93) and the 1³S(D)₁ energy at -2.225 MeV (target -2.225 MeV)
- Deuteron quadrupole moment Q_d = 0.276 fm² from LO₃ (target 0.286 fm²)
- Density range covered: 2% to 10% of normal nuclear density, for systems of 8, 12, and 16 neutrons

## Context

This work extends the lattice chiral effective field theory calculations of Borasoy et al. (2007), who computed dilute neutron matter using the LO₂ action but found that Gaussian smearing produced unphysical attractive P-wave forces requiring large NLO cancellations. The new LO₃ action solves this problem and enables reliable perturbative treatment of NLO corrections at higher densities. The results can be compared with other approaches to neutron matter, such as the auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo calculations of Carlson et al. (2003) and the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations of Baldo and Maieron (2007).
