# Electron-induced proton knockout from neutron rich nuclei

> We study the evolution of the \eep cross section on nuclei with increasing asymmetry between the number of neutrons and protons. The calculations are done within the framework of the nonrelativistic and relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation. In the nonrelativistic model phenomenological Woods-Saxon and Hartree-Fock wave functions are used for the proton bound-state wave functions, in the relativistic model the wave functions are solutions of Dirac-Hartree equations. The models are first tested against experimental data on $^{40}$Ca and $^{48}$Ca nuclei, and then they are applied to a set of spherical calcium isotopes.

## Key claim

The evolution of the (e,e'p) reduced cross section on calcium isotopes with increasing neutron number shows a consistent trend of lowering and widening across three different mean-field models, but the dependence of the bound-state wave functions on proton-neutron asymmetry accounts for only part of the differences; final-state interactions described by phenomenological optical potentials give an important and kinematics-dependent contribution that can affect the extracted spectroscopic factors.

## Method

The authors performed nonrelativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations using the DWEEPY program and relativistic DWIA (RDWIA) calculations for exclusive (e,e'p) knockout from the 1d3/2 proton orbital in 40,48,52,60Ca. The bound-state wave functions were obtained from three different mean-field descriptions: phenomenological Woods-Saxon wells, Hartree-Fock with the Gogny D1M interaction, and Dirac-Hartree solutions from relativistic mean-field theory. Final-state interactions were described by the phenomenological optical potential of Schwandt (1982) in the nonrelativistic case and the EDAD1 optical potential in the relativistic case, and the models were first tested against experimental data from NIKHEF for 40Ca and 48Ca.

## Key numbers

- Reduction factors for DWIA-WS results on 40Ca: 0.49 in (q,omega) constant kinematics and 0.65 in parallel kinematics; for 48Ca: 0.55 in parallel kinematics
- Reduction factors for DWIA-HF results on 40Ca: 0.51 in (q,omega) constant kinematics and 0.64 in parallel kinematics; for 48Ca: 0.55 in parallel kinematics
- Reduction factors for RDWIA results on 40Ca: 0.49 in (q,omega) constant kinematics and 0.69 in parallel kinematics; for 48Ca: 0.52 in parallel kinematics
- Incident electron energy E0 = 483.2 MeV and electron scattering angle theta = 61.52 degrees with q = 450 MeV/c for (q,omega) constant kinematics; outgoing proton kinetic energy T' = 100 MeV in both kinematics
- Reduction factors for 40Ca in (q,omega) constant kinematics are about 20-25% lower than those in parallel kinematics across all models

## Context

This work extends the established DWIA and RDWIA frameworks previously applied to stable closed-shell nuclei (as reviewed by Boffi et al., 1993, 1996) to predict (e,e'p) cross sections for neutron-rich calcium isotopes, using the same program DWEEPY that analyzed NIKHEF data for 40Ca and 48Ca (Kramer et al., 1990, 2001). The results provide a reference for future electron scattering experiments on exotic nuclei at facilities such as ELISe at FAIR and the SCRIT project at RIKEN, and the comparison with data could test whether the dependence of spectroscopic factors and correlations on proton-neutron asymmetry, which the authors note becomes stronger with increasing separation energy, is correctly described by current models.
