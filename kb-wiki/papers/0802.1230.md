# Radiative energy loss in a finite dynamical QCD medium

> The radiative energy loss of a quark jet traversing a finite size QCD medium with dynamical constituents is calculated to first order in opacity. Although finite size corrections reduce the energy loss relative to an infinite dynamical QCD medium, under realistic conditions it remains significantly larger than in a static medium. Quantitative predictions of jet suppression in relativistic heavy ion collisions must therefore account for the dynamics of the medium's constituents. Finite size effects are shown to induce a non-linear path length dependence of the energy loss. Our results suggest a simple general mapping between energy loss expressions for static and dynamical QCD media.

## Key claim

Finite size and coherence effects reduce the radiative energy loss per unit path length more strongly in a dynamical medium than in a static medium, but the energy loss ratio between equally dense dynamical and static media remains significantly larger than unity under realistic RHIC and LHC conditions, contrary to the infinite-medium Bethe-Heitler limit where the ratio saturates at large quark energies.

## Method

The authors calculate the first-order-in-opacity radiative energy loss of a heavy quark jet traversing a finite-size QCD medium with dynamical constituents, using Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) resumed gluon propagators in finite temperature field theory. The medium is modeled as a thermalized quark-gluon plasma at temperature T with zero baryon density and n_f effective massless quark flavors, extending the DGLV approach to include parton recoil. All 24 contributing Feynman diagrams are evaluated, with the medium extending for length L from the quark production point and the collisional interaction occurring at a distance l < L inside the medium.

## Key numbers

* For a medium of length L=5 fm, α_s=0.3, n_f=2.5, and T=225 MeV (RHIC conditions), the ratio ΔE_dyn/ΔE_stat for light quarks ranges from about 1.25 to over 2.0 depending on jet energy.
* For the same medium length at T=400 MeV (LHC conditions), the ratio ΔE_dyn/ΔE_stat for light quarks shows a similar range of 25% to over 100% increase from the cross-section change alone.
* The dynamical mean free path λ_dyn is related to the static mean free path by λ_dyn ≈ 0.84 λ_stat for n_f=2.5, giving a common ~20% increase in energy loss from this factor alone.
* For asymptotically large jet energies, the fractional energy loss in a dynamical medium approaches ΔE_dyn/E ≈ (C_R α_s / 4) (L^2 μ^2 / λ_dyn) ln(4ET/μ^2), and the ratio ΔE_dyn/ΔE_stat approaches λ_stat/λ_dyn.
* The Debye screening mass is μ = gT sqrt(N_c/3 + N_f/6), with m_g = μ/√2 as the effective gluon mass for hard momenta k ≳ T.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation for an infinite dynamical QCD medium (Djordjevic and Heinz, 2007) by incorporating finite size and coherence effects, and is complementary to the approach of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (AMY) who study energy loss in an infinite, optically thick QGP. The results suggest a simple general mapping between energy loss expressions for static and dynamical QCD media, and the finding that finite size effects are largest for light quarks and high energies may help explain the observed large suppression of non-photonic electrons in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC.
