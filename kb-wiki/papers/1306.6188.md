# Lattice QCD-based equations of state at vanishing net-baryon density

> We present realistic equations of state for QCD matter at vanishing net-baryon density which embed recent lattice QCD results at high temperatures combined with a hadron resonance gas model in the low-temperature, confined phase. In the latter, we allow an implementation of partial chemical equilibrium, in which particle ratios are fixed at the chemical freeze-out, so that a description closer to the experimental situation is possible. Given the present uncertainty in the determination of the chemical freeze-out temperature from first-principle lattice QCD calculations, we consider different values within the expected range. The corresponding equations of state can be applied in the hydrodynamic modeling of relativistic heavy-ion collisions at the LHC and at the highest RHIC beam energies. Suitable parametrizations of our results as functions of the energy density are also provided.

## Key claim

The paper constructs realistic equations of state for QCD matter at vanishing net-baryon density that combine lattice QCD results at high temperatures with a hadron resonance gas model at low temperatures, but the key condition is that the hadronic phase can be in partial chemical equilibrium (rather than full equilibrium) with particle ratios fixed at a chemical freeze-out temperature \(T_{ch}\) that is uncertain within a range of 145 to 160 MeV.

## Method

The authors used continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD data from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi 2011) for 2+1 quark flavors with physical quark masses at high temperatures, parametrized the interaction measure \(I(T)/T^4\), and combined it with a hadron resonance gas model containing states up to 2 GeV mass at low temperatures, with a switching temperature around 172 MeV and interpolation between 165 and 180 MeV. For the partial chemical equilibrium case, they fixed effective particle numbers of 26 stable hadron species at the chemical freeze-out temperature \(T_{ch}\) and determined temperature-dependent effective chemical potentials \(\mu_i(T)\) by conserving the ratio \(\bar{n}_i/s\) below \(T_{ch}\), considering four values of \(T_{ch}\) (145, 150, 155, and 160 MeV).

## Key numbers

- The (pseudo-)critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration is \(T_c = 155 \pm 6\) MeV (Borsanyi 2009) and from the hotQCD collaboration is \(T_c = 154 \pm 9\) MeV (Bazavov 2012).
- The relative deviation of the HRG model from lattice QCD data in the overlap region is at most 9% in \(I(T)/T^4\) and 5% in \(p(T)/T^4\).
- The entropy density \(s(T)\) extrapolated to \(T = 800\) MeV reaches about 82.5% of the Stefan-Boltzmann limit for a non-interacting gas of 3 massless quark flavors.
- The chemical freeze-out temperatures considered are \(T_{ch} = 145, 150, 155, 160\) MeV, with corresponding energy densities \(\epsilon_{ch} = 0.18675523, 0.24117503, 0.30993163, 0.39623763\) GeV/fm\(^3\).
- The parametrizations for effective chemical potentials \(\mu_i(T)\) use quadratic fit functions valid in the temperature range 70 MeV \(\leq T \leq T_{ch}\) with a maximal \(\chi^2 = 9 \times 10^{-6}\).
- The squared speed of sound \(c_s^2\) from the baseline EoS agrees with lattice QCD data from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi 2010) within error bars.

## Context

This work extends prior combinations of hadron resonance gas models with lattice QCD results, such as those by Huovinen (2009) and the parametrizations by Song (2008), by specifically implementing partial chemical equilibrium in the hadronic phase to better match experimental conditions in heavy-ion collisions. The resulting equations of state enable hydrodynamic modeling of relativistic heavy-ion collisions at LHC and top RHIC energies, allowing study of the impact of a 15 MeV variation in chemical freeze-out temperature on particle spectra and determination of QGP transport properties like shear and bulk viscosity coefficients.
