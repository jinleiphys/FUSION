# An algebraic model to study the internal structure of pseudo-scalar mesons with heavy-light quark content

> The internal structure of all lowest-lying pseudo-scalar mesons with heavy-light quark content is studied in detail using an algebraic model that has been applied recently, and successfully, to the same physical observables of pseudo-scalar and vector mesons with hidden-flavor quark content, from light to heavy quark sectors. The algebraic model consists on constructing simple and evidence-based \emph{ansätze} of the meson's Bethe-Salpeter amplitude (BSA) and quark's propagator in such a way that the Bethe-Salpeter wave function (BSWF) can then be readily computed algebraically. Its subsequent projection onto the light front yields the light front wave function (LFWF) whose form allows us a simple access to the valence-quark Parton Distribution Amplitude (PDA) by integrating over the transverse momentum squared. We exploit our current knowledge of the PDAs of lowest-lying pseudo-scalar heavy-light mesons to compute their Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) through the overlap representation of LFWFs. From these three dimensional knowledge, different limits/projections lead us to deduce the related Parton Distribution functions (PDFs), Electromagnetic Form Factors (EFFs), and Impact parameter space GPDs (IPS-GPDs). When possible, we make explicit comparisons with available experimental results and earlier theoretical predictions.

## Key claim

The algebraic model, previously successful for hidden-flavor mesons, is extended to heavy-light pseudoscalar mesons (D, D_s, B, B_s, B_c) to compute their internal structure distributions; the model relies on the assumption that the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and quark propagator can be represented by simple, evidence-based *ansätze* that yield closed algebraic expressions, and the results are valid at the intrinsic scale ζ_H = 2 GeV where valence degrees of freedom fully express the hadron's properties.

## Method

The authors construct algebraic *ansätze* for the quark propagator and the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude (BSA) of heavy-light pseudoscalar mesons, using a spectral density function in a Nakanishi integral representation. The Bethe-Salpeter wave function (BSWF) is computed algebraically and projected onto the light front to obtain the light-front wave function (LFWF), which is then related to the parton distribution amplitude (PDA) via integration over transverse momentum squared. Using previously determined PDAs from the Continuum Schwinger Function Method (CSM) for D, D_s, B, B_s, and B_c mesons, the LFWFs are computed and subsequently used to derive generalized parton distributions (GPDs) through the overlap representation, from which parton distribution functions (PDFs), electromagnetic form factors (EFFs), and impact parameter space GPDs (IPS-GPDs) are obtained.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic model previously applied to pseudoscalar and vector mesons with hidden-flavor quark content (Albino 2022, Almeida-Zamora 2023) to heavy-light mesons, using PDAs computed within the Continuum Schwinger Function Method (Binosi 2018) which are compatible with results from Serna (2020). The model enables computation of GPDs, PDFs, EFFs, and IPS-GPDs for heavy-light systems where first-principles calculations remain challenging, and provides a framework for the covariant extension approach to GPDs (Mezrag 2014, Chouika 2017, Chavez 2021) that fulfills both positivity and polynomiality constraints.
