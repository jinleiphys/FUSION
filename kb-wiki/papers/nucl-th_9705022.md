# Effects of Spin-Isospin Modes in Transport Simulations

> In-medium properties derived for nuclear matter in a microscopic pi + nucleon-hole + delta-hole model are incorporated into transport simulations of nuclear collisions by means of a local-density approximation and by utilizing a local medium frame. Certain features of the transport results differ from those based on the corresponding vacuum properties. Comparisons of the pi and delta production rates, as well as pion energy spectra, are discussed in particular.

## Key claim

In-medium spin-isospin properties derived from a microscopic \(\pi + N N^{-1} + \Delta N^{-1}\) model, when incorporated into transport simulations via a local-density approximation and a local medium frame, produce modest but significant differences in pion and delta production compared to simulations using vacuum properties; the effect is a lowering of the effective transverse temperature of the pion spectra, but the results are exploratory and not yet suitable for quantitative comparison with experiment.

## Method

The authors calculated in-medium dispersion relations and partial delta decay widths for spin-longitudinal collective modes (quasi-pions) in infinite nuclear matter using a non-relativistic RPA formalism with \(p\)-wave interactions and short-range correlation parameters. These infinite-matter properties were then incorporated into a standard BUU transport code (LiBauer) by employing a local-density approximation and a local medium frame, replacing real pions with two types of quasi-pions (\(\tilde{\pi}_1\) and \(\tilde{\pi}_2\)) and using pre-tabulated partial decay widths for delta decay into these modes. Simulations were performed for central collisions of \(^{40}\text{Ca}+^{40}\text{Ca}\) and \(^{197}\text{Au}+^{197}\text{Au}\) at 500 and 1000 \(A\) MeV, using 250 test particles per nucleon and a density-dependent mean field.

## Key numbers

- For both systems and bombarding energies, the total number of free pions escaping the collision is about 10% higher in the medium-modified simulations compared to standard simulations.
- At the higher bombarding energy (1000 \(A\) MeV), about 95% of emitted pions originate from the lower pionic mode (\(\tilde{\pi}_1\)); at the lower energy (500 \(A\) MeV), this contribution is about 99%.
- For the Au system, 2-5% of the pionic modes are realized as (unphysical) \(\Delta N^{-1}\) states; for the Ca system, this realization amounts to 7-9%.
- In the medium-modified simulations, there is 5-20% less reabsorption of quasi-pions (\(N+\tilde{\pi} \rightarrow \Delta\)) during the dense phase, with the effect largest for the Au system at 1 \(A\) GeV and smallest for the Ca system at 500 \(A\) MeV.
- The reabsorption is enhanced at low densities (\(\rho < 0.5 \rho_0\)).

## Context

This work extends earlier transport treatments that used a simpler two-level \(\Delta N^{-1}\) model (Weise, Bertsch, Giessen, Texas, Tub) by employing a more consistent \(\pi + N N^{-1} + \Delta N^{-1}\) model developed in prior work (simAP). The results enable future studies to include additional in-medium effects (such as full corrections to the \(\tilde{\pi}+N\rightarrow\Delta\) cross section and higher nucleon resonances) and suggest that quantitative comparisons with experimental pion spectra will require such a more complete treatment.
