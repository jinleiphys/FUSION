# Fermion bags, duality and the three dimensional massless lattice Thirring model

> The recently proposed fermion bag approach is a powerful technique to solve some four-fermion lattice field theories. Due to the existence of a duality between strong and weak couplings, the approach leads to efficient Monte Carlo algorithms in both these limits. The new method allows us for the first time to accurately compute quantities close to the quantum critical point in the three dimensional lattice Thirring model with massless fermions on large lattices. The critical exponents at the quantum critical point are found to be $ν=0.85(1)$, $η= 0.65(1)$ and $η_ψ= 0.37(1)$.

# Fermion bags, duality and the three dimensional massless lattice Thirring model

## Key claim

The fermion bag approach, exploiting a duality between strong and weak couplings, enables accurate Monte Carlo calculations of critical exponents at the quantum critical point of the three-dimensional massless lattice Thirring model on large lattices with exactly massless fermions, without requiring extrapolation from finite fermion mass.

## Method

The authors apply the fermion bag approach to the three-dimensional massless lattice Thirring model with two Grassmann-valued fields per site and a nearest-neighbor four-fermion coupling \(U\). The partition function is expanded in powers of the coupling, yielding configurations of interaction bonds; at strong couplings the free sites form a fermion bag, while at weak couplings a duality relation rewrites the weight as a determinant of a small propagator matrix between interaction sites. Using a determinantal Monte Carlo algorithm, the authors measure the chiral condensate susceptibility, the chiral winding number susceptibility, and a fermion two-point function ratio on cubic lattices from \(12^3\) to \(40^3\), and perform combined finite-size scaling fits to extract critical exponents.

## Key numbers

- Critical coupling \(U_c = 0.2608(2)\) from combined fits on lattices from \(12^3\) to \(40^3\).
- Correlation length exponent \(\nu = 0.85(1)\) at the quantum critical point.
- Anomalous dimension \(\eta = 0.65(1)\) at the quantum critical point.
- Fermion anomalous dimension \(\eta_\psi = 0.37(1)\) at the quantum critical point, reported as a new result.
- Combined fit \(\chi^2/\text{d.o.f.} = 1.3\) for the scaling analysis.

## Context

This work extends the fermion bag approach introduced by Chandrasekharan (2009) and applies it to the three-dimensional massless lattice Thirring model, which had previously been studied using mean-field techniques (Rosenstein, 1987) and Hybrid Monte Carlo methods with finite fermion mass by Del Debbio (1995, 1997) and Barbour (1998). Those earlier HMC calculations relied on extrapolations to the massless limit and produced results with large errors (e.g., \(\nu = 0.71(4)\), \(\eta = 0.60(2)\) from Del Debbio (1997) and \(\nu = 0.88(8)\), \(\eta = 0.46(11)\) from Barbour (1998)). The present work provides the first accurate Monte Carlo computation at exactly zero fermion mass, and the first determination of \(\eta_\psi\). The results enable future renormalization group analyses of the fixed point in the space of four-fermion couplings generated by lattice symmetries.
