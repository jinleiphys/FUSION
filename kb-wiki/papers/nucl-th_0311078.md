# The 4He(gamma,n)3He Reaction with Full Final State Interaction

> The total cross sections of the processes 4He(gamma,p)3H and 4He(gamma,n)3He are calculated. For these exclusive reactions we investigate the question of the giant dipole peak height, but we also consider higher energies. The calculation includes full Final State Interaction (FSI) via the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) approach and employs the semi-realistic MTI-III potential.

# The 4He(gamma,n)3He Reaction with Full Final State Interaction

## Key claim

The total cross sections for the exclusive photodisintegration of 4He into two fragments (proton+3H and neutron+3He) are calculated with full final state interaction included rigorously even beyond the three-body break-up threshold, using the semi-realistic MTI-III potential. The calculation shows a pronounced giant dipole peak that tends toward the data of Ark74 and Cal83 rather than the flat behavior seen in other experimental data sets.

## Method

The authors employ the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) approach to compute the transition matrix elements for the exclusive (gamma,p) and (gamma,n) reactions, using the semi-realistic MTI-III potential. The final state interaction is treated rigorously by expressing the scattering solution in terms of a LIT that is evaluated via a Lanczos orthonormal basis, where the matrix elements of the resolvent operator are written as continued fractions of the Lanczos coefficients. Only unretarded dipole operator transitions are considered, and Coulomb interaction is included via Coulomb wave functions for the proton channel.

## Key numbers

- The total exclusive cross sections for 4He(gamma,p)3H and 4He(gamma,n)3He are calculated from threshold up to approximately 140 MeV photon energy.
- The theoretical giant dipole resonance peak height is compared against experimental data from Ark74, Cal83, Ber88, Fel90, Ber80, War81, and Sim98.
- The difference between the total photoabsorption cross section from BELO01 and the sum of the two exclusive cross sections is compared to three-body break-up data from Gor58, Ark70, Bal77, and Dor93.

## Context

This work extends the LIT method, previously applied successfully to the exclusive d(e,e'p)n reaction by LL00, to the more complex four-nucleon system. The calculation builds on prior total photoabsorption cross section results for 4He using the same MTI-III potential from ELO97 and BELO01, and enables determination of the sum of three- and four-body break-up cross sections by subtraction. The authors note that more work is needed on both the experimental side and the theoretical side using more realistic interactions.
