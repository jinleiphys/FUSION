# A Search for $0^+$ States in $^{50}$Cr: Implications for the Superallowed $β$-decay of $^{50}$Mn

> A $^{52}$Cr$(p,t)$$^{50}$Cr two-neutron pickup reaction was performed using the Q3D magnetic spectrograph at the Maier-Leibnitz-Laboratorium in Garching, Germany. Excited states in $^{50}$Cr were observed up to an excitation energy of 5.3 MeV. Despite significantly increased sensitivity and resolution over previous work, no evidence of the previously assigned first excited $0^+$ state was found. As a result, the $0^+_2$ state is reassigned at an excitation energy of $E_x=3895.0(5)$ keV in $^{50}$Cr. This reassignment directly impacts direct searches for a non-analogue Fermi $β^+$ decay branch in $^{50}$Mn. These results also show better systematic agreement with the theoretical predictions for the $0^+$ state spectrum in $^{50}$Cr using the same formalism as the isospin-symmetry-breaking correction calculations for superallowed nuclei. The experimental data are also compared to $ab$-$initio$ shell-model predictions using the IM-SRG formalism based on $NN$ and $3N$ forces from chiral-EFT in the $pf$-shell for the first time.

# A Search for $0^+$ States in $^{50}$Cr: Implications for the Superallowed $\beta$-decay of $^{50}$Mn

## Key claim
The previously assigned first excited $0^+$ state in $^{50}$Cr is not observed; instead, the $0^+_2$ state is reassigned at 3895.0(5) keV, which directly impacts searches for a non-analogue Fermi $\beta^+$ decay branch in $^{50}$Mn. This reassignment reduces the Towner-Hardy isospin-symmetry-breaking correction $\delta_{C1}$ for $^{50}$Mn from 0.035(20)% to 0.031(20)%, contingent on the energy scaling method used in the theoretical framework.

## Method
A $^{52}$Cr$(p,t)$$^{50}$Cr two-neutron pickup reaction was performed using a 24 MeV proton beam from the MP tandem Van de Graaff accelerator at the Maier-Leibnitz-Laboratorium in Garching, Germany. Outgoing tritons were momentum-analyzed with the Q3D magnetic spectrograph and detected at eight angles between $10^\circ$ and $50^\circ$ up to an excitation energy of 5.4 MeV, with a full-width at half-maximum energy resolution of roughly 10 keV. Identification of $0^+$ states was conducted by comparing extracted angular distributions to finite-range DWBA calculations using the FRESCO code with shell-model two-nucleon-amplitude files generated from the GXPF1A interaction.

## Key numbers
- The first excited $0^+$ state ($0^+_2$) is reassigned at $E_x = 3895.0(5)$ keV in $^{50}$Cr.
- A second excited $0^+$ state is observed at $E_x = 4068.8(5)$ keV.
- A third candidate $0^+$ state is tentatively assigned at $E_x = 4733(5)$ keV.
- The experimental cross-section at $10^\circ$ for the ground state is 6500(300) $\mu$b/sr; for the 3.895 MeV state, 72(3) $\mu$b/sr; for the 4.069 MeV state, 106(6) $\mu$b/sr.
- The $\delta_{C1}$ value for $^{50}$Mn is revised from 0.035(20)% to 0.031(20)%.
- The characteristic $\gamma$ ray from the $0^+_2 \rightarrow 2^+_1$ transition would have energy $E_\gamma = 3111.7(5)$ keV, based on the 783.32(3) keV $2^+_1$ state.

## Context
This work resolves long-standing ambiguities in the $0^+$ state spectrum of $^{50}$Cr, which had been based on three previous $(p,t)$ experiments by Bae71, Ram72, and Yam82, as well as a $(^3\text{He},n)$ measurement by Boh75. The reassignment directly impacts the Towner-Hardy (TH) isospin-symmetry-breaking correction calculations for the superallowed $\beta$ decay of $^{50}$Mn, which were recently updated by Tow15 following branching ratio measurements by the RISING collaboration (Mol15). The experimental data are compared to phenomenological shell-model calculations using the GXPF1A interaction (Hon05) and, for the first time, to ab-initio in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) calculations based on chiral effective field theory $NN$ and $3N$ forces (Str16a, Str16b). This work enables future direct searches for non-analogue Fermi $\beta$ decay branches in $^{50}$Mn at radioactive ion-beam facilities such as TRIUMF with the GRIFFIN spectrometer, by specifying the characteristic $\gamma$-ray energy (3111.7 keV) that would signal the dominant non-analogue decay branch.
