# Quark matter in compact stars?

> Ozel, in a recent reanalysis of EXO 0748-676 observational data (astro-ph/0605106), concluded that quark matter probably does not exist in the center of compact stars. We show that the data is actually consistent with the presence of quark matter in compact stars.

## Key claim

The paper argues that Ozel's (2006) conclusion that quark matter probably does not exist in the center of compact stars is not justified, because her analysis used a limited set of quark matter equations of state; when a more comprehensive set of proposed quark matter equations of state is considered, the observational data from EXO 0748-676 is actually consistent with the presence of quark matter.

## Method

The authors compare Ozel's derived lower limits on the mass and radius of EXO 0748-676 with mass-radius relations predicted by a comprehensive set of quark matter equations of state from the existing literature, including the MIT bag model, perturbative QCD corrections, and the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model. They also review additional observational constraints from cooling, spin-down, precession, glitches, magnetar oscillations, and superbursts to assess whether any of these rule out quark matter cores.

## Key numbers

- Ozel's lower limits on EXO 0748-676: mass \(M \geq 2.1 \pm 0.28 M_\odot\) and radius \(R \geq 13.8 \pm 1.8\) km.
- Hybrid or quark stars can reach a mass of \(2 M_\odot\) in calculations using the MIT bag model, perturbative QCD corrections, and the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model.

## Context

This work directly responds to Ozel (2006), who argued that her observational limits on EXO 0748-676 exclude a soft equation of state and therefore rule out quark matter. The authors show that quark matter can be as stiff as nuclear matter due to strong QCD interactions, so the same data does not exclude hybrid or quark stars. The paper also reviews prior work on cooling (Page 2005), spin-down constraints (Madsen 1999), glitches, and superbursts (Page 2005) to argue that none of these observations yet rule out deconfined quarks in compact stars.
