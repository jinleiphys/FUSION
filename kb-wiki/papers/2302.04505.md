# Doubly Charmed Tetraquark $T^+_{cc}$ from Lattice QCD near Physical Point

> The doubly charmed tetraquark $T^+_{cc}$ recently discovered by the LHCb Collaboration is studied on the basis of $(2+1)$-flavor lattice QCD simulations of the $D^*D$ system with nearly physical pion mass $m_π=146$ MeV. The interaction of $D^*D$ in the isoscalar and $S$-wave channel, derived from the hadronic spacetime correlation by the HAL QCD method, is attractive for all distances and leads to a near-threshold virtual state with a pole position $E_\text{pole}=-59\left(^{+53}_{-99}\right)\left(^{+2}_{-67}\right)$ keV and a large scattering length $1/a_0=0.05(5)\left(^{+2}_{-2}\right)~\text{fm}^{-1}$. The virtual state is shown to evolve into a loosely bound state as $m_π$ decreases to its physical value by using a potential modified to $m_π=135$ MeV based on the pion-exchange interaction. Such a potential is found to give a semiquantitative description of the LHCb data on the $D^0D^0π^+$ mass spectrum. Future study is necessary to perform physical-point simulations with the isospin-breaking and open three-body-channel effects taken into account.

## Key claim

The lattice QCD simulation at a nearly physical pion mass of 146 MeV finds that the D*D interaction in the isoscalar S-wave channel produces a near-threshold virtual state, not a bound state; the authors argue that this virtual state would evolve into a loosely bound state only if the pion mass is reduced to the physical value of 135 MeV, based on a potential modified by the two-pion-exchange interaction.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations on a 96^4 lattice with lattice spacing 0.0846 fm (L = 8.1 fm) at m_pi = 146.4 MeV, using the HAL QCD method to extract the D*D interaction potential from hadronic spacetime correlation functions. The potential was obtained at leading order in the derivative expansion, fitted to a phenomenological form including a two-pion-exchange term, and then used to solve the Schrodinger equation in infinite volume to extract scattering phase shifts, scattering length, effective range, and pole positions.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass in the simulation: m_pi = 146.4 MeV
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.0846 fm; lattice size: L = 8.1 fm
- D* mass from lattice: m_D* = 2018.1(5) MeV; D mass: m_D = 1878.2(2) MeV
- Inverse scattering length at m_pi = 146.4 MeV: 1/a_0 = 0.05(5)(+2/-2) fm^{-1}
- Pole energy at m_pi = 146.4 MeV: E_pole = -59(+53/-99)(+2/-67) keV (virtual state)
- Effective range at m_pi = 146.4 MeV: r_eff = 1.12(3)(+3/-8) fm
- Inverse scattering length at m_pi = 135.0 MeV (modified potential): 1/a_0 = -0.03(4) fm^{-1}
- Pole energy at m_pi = 135.0 MeV: E_pole = -45(+41/-78) keV (loosely bound state)
- Experimental pole energy from LHCb: E_pole = -360(40)(+4/-0) keV

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of the doubly charmed tetraquark T+_cc, which were limited to large pion masses (m_pi >= 280 MeV) and small lattice sizes (L <= 2.9 fm) by Ikeda (2013), Chen (2022), and Padmanath (2022). The present result at nearly physical pion mass shows a clear trend of the inverse scattering length approaching the unitary regime, consistent with the LHCb experimental data. The study enables future physical-point simulations including isospin-breaking and open three-body-channel effects, and provides a framework for direct comparison between lattice QCD and experimental D0D0pi+ mass spectra.
