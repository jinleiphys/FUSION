# Hot Nuclear Matter : A Variational Approach

> We develop a nonperturbative technique in field theory to study properties of infinite nuclear matter at zero temperature as well as at finite temperatures. Here we dress the nuclear matter with off-mass shell pions. The techniques of thermofield dynamics are used for finite temperature calculations. Equation of state is derived from the dynamics of the interacting system in a self consistent manner. The transition temperature for nuclear matter appears to be around 15 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper develops a nonperturbative, quantum-mechanical variational approach to infinite nuclear matter at zero and finite temperatures, where the effects of scalar-isoscalar sigma mesons are replaced by off-mass-shell pion pairs. The transition temperature for nuclear matter is reported as "around 15 MeV," but this is inferred from the vanishing of the pressure pocket in the equation of state rather than from a direct calculation of a critical point.

## Method

The authors start from an effective Hamiltonian for pion-nucleon interactions and dress the nuclear matter with off-mass-shell pion pairs using a unitary Bogoliubov transformation. At finite temperature, they employ thermofield dynamics to extend the zero-temperature formalism, introducing thermal modes for both bosons and fermions. The thermodynamic potential is extremized with respect to the pion dressing function, the thermal boson distribution, and the thermal fermion distribution to obtain self-consistent equations for the system. A phenomenological repulsive term is added to account for the composite structure of pions and short-range repulsion.

## Key numbers

* Saturation binding energy per nucleon: -15.03 MeV at saturation density ρ = 0.19 fm⁻³, corresponding to Fermi momentum k_f = 1.42 fm⁻¹.
* Nuclear matter incompressibility: K = 151.2 MeV.
* Parameters used: λ = 0.54 fm², R_π = 1.18 fm, a = 0.12 GeV.
* Transition temperature for nuclear matter: around 15 MeV (inferred from pressure curves).
* Average pion number per nucleon levels off at ρ ≥ 0.3 fm⁻³, approximately 1.5 times the saturation density at zero temperature.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature variational approach of Mishra, Mishra, and Misra (1990) to finite temperatures, using thermofield dynamics as formulated by Umezawa, Matsumoto, and Tachiki (1982). The model provides an alternative to the Walecka mean-field approach (Walecka, 1974; Serot and Walecka, 1986) and its finite-temperature extensions by Furnstahl and Serot (1990), without requiring an elementary sigma meson field. The results enable further studies of hot nuclear matter in heavy-ion collisions and cosmology, and suggest that off-shell pion pairs may annihilate to produce hard photons observable in experiments.
