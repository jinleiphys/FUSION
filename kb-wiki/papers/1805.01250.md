# Uncertainties in s-process nucleosynthesis in low mass stars determined from Monte Carlo variations

> The main s-process taking place in low mass stars produces about half of the elements heavier than iron. It is therefore very important to determine the importance and impact of nuclear physics uncertainties on this process. We have performed extensive nuclear reaction network calculations using individual and temperature-dependent uncertainties for reactions involving elements heavier than iron, within a Monte Carlo framework. Using this technique, we determined the uncertainty in the main s-process abundance predictions due to nuclear uncertainties link to weak interactions and neutron captures on elements heavier than iron. We also identified the key nuclear reactions dominating these uncertainties. We found that $β$-decay rate uncertainties affect only a few nuclides near s-process branchings, whereas most of the uncertainty in the final abundances is caused by uncertainties in neutron capture rates, either directly producing or destroying the nuclide of interest. Combined total nuclear uncertainties due to reactions on heavy elements are in general small (less than 50%). Three key reactions, nevertheless, stand out because they significantly affect the uncertainties of a large number of nuclides. These are $^{56}$Fe(n,$γ$), $^{64}$Ni(n,$γ$), and $^{138}$Ba(n,$γ$). We discuss the prospect of reducing uncertainties in the key reactions identified in this study with future experiments.

## Key claim

The total nuclear uncertainties due to reactions on heavy elements in the main s-process of low mass stars are generally small (less than 50%), but three specific neutron capture reactions, $^{56}$Fe(n,$\gamma$), $^{64}$Ni(n,$\gamma$), and $^{138}$Ba(n,$\gamma$), dominate the uncertainties for a large number of nuclides, and reducing their uncertainties would significantly improve abundance predictions.

## Method

The authors performed 10,000 Monte Carlo nuclear reaction network calculations using the PizBuin code suite, simultaneously varying temperature-dependent uncertainties for neutron capture and weak interaction rates on elements from Fe to Bi. They used a single thermodynamic trajectory extracted from a 3 $M_{\odot}$, solar metallicity MESA stellar evolution model for the $^{13}$C-pocket phase, with three different initial $^{13}$C abundances (standard, half, and double), and a separate constant-temperature trajectory for the thermal pulse phase. Key reactions were identified using Pearson correlation coefficients between rate variations and final abundance changes, with a threshold of $r \geq 0.65$ for level 1 key rates.

## Key numbers

- Combined total nuclear uncertainties due to reactions on heavy elements are less than 50% for the main s-process
- Three key reactions identified: $^{56}$Fe(n,$\gamma$), $^{64}$Ni(n,$\gamma$), and $^{138}$Ba(n,$\gamma$)
- For the standard $^{13}$C-pocket case, uncertainty factors (90% probability interval) range from e.g. Up=1.06 and Down=0.950 for $^{88}$Sr to Up=3.23 and Down=0.944 for $^{72}$Ge
- The trajectory was extracted from a 3 $M_{\odot}$, $Z=0.014$ (solar metallicity) stellar evolution model, following the 6th thermal pulse
- Standard initial $^{13}$C mass fraction used: $X_{^{13}\mathrm{C}} = 1.95\times 10^{-2}$
- TP phase trajectory: constant temperature of 0.245 GK and constant density of 5$\times$10$^{3}$ g/cm$^{3}$, lasting one year
- 10,000 Monte Carlo iterations were performed
- 109 nuclides were analysed for the $^{13}$C-pocket phase (those contributing at least 10% to final elemental mass, plus 7 additional s-only nuclides)
- 58 nuclei were analysed for the TP phase

## Context

This work extends the sensitivity study of Koloczek et al. (2016) by using a Monte Carlo framework with simultaneous variation of all reaction rates (rather than one-at-a-time variation) and temperature-dependent uncertainties based on both experimental and theoretical studies, following the methodology previously applied to the s-process in massive stars and the $\gamma$-process in core collapse supernovae by Rauscher et al. (2016) and Nishimura et al. (2017, 2018). The results enable future experiments to focus on reducing uncertainties in the three key neutron capture reactions identified, which would significantly improve main s-process abundance predictions for low mass AGB stars.
