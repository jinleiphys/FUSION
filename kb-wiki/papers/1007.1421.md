# Dark matter, neutron stars and strange quark matter

> We show that self-annihilating neutralino WIMP dark matter accreted onto neutron stars may provide a mechanism to seed compact objects with long-lived lumps of strange quark matter, or strangelets, for WIMP masses above a few GeV. This effect may trigger a conversion of most of the star into a strange star. We use an energy estimate for the long-lived strangelet based on the Fermi gas model combined with the MIT bag model to set a new limit on the possible values of the WIMP mass that can be especially relevant for subdominant species of massive neutralinos.

# Dark matter, neutron stars and strange quark matter

## Key claim
Self-annihilating neutralino WIMP dark matter accreted onto neutron stars may seed the formation of long-lived strange quark matter lumps (strangelets) that could trigger conversion of the star into a strange star, but only for WIMP masses above approximately 4 GeV/c² under typical neutron star conditions; the mechanism depends on the bag constant, central baryon density, and energy deposition efficiency.

## Method
The authors model WIMP capture onto a typical non-rotating neutron star using the standard accretion rate formula from Press & Spergel (1985) and Gould (1987), assuming a local dark matter density of 0.3 GeV/cm³ and a WIMP-nucleon cross section above 10⁻⁴⁵ cm². They calculate the energy released from WIMP self-annihilations in the thermalization volume of the neutron star core, then compare this energy to the binding energy of a long-lived strangelet computed using the Fermi gas model combined with the MIT bag model, including Coulomb corrections. The condition that a single annihilation event must deposit enough energy to create a stable strangelet yields a lower limit on the WIMP mass.

## Key numbers
- WIMP accretion rate: ℱ = 3.042 × 10²⁵ / [m_χ (GeV)] s⁻¹, assuming local DM density ρ_DM,0 = 0.3 GeV/cm³
- Thermalization radius: r_th = 64 (T/10⁵ K)^(1/2) (10¹⁴ g/cm³ / ρ_c)^(1/2) (100 GeV/c² / m_χ)^(1/2) cm; for T_c = 10⁹ K, ρ_c = 10¹⁵ g/cm³, and m_χ = 1 GeV/c²–10 TeV/c², r_th ≈ 2×10⁵–2×10² cm
- Energy release rate from annihilations: Ė_annih = 2.74×10²⁵–10²⁹ GeV/s for m_χ ≈ 1–10⁴ GeV/c² with efficiency f = 0.9
- Quark masses used: m_u = 2.55 MeV, m_d = 5.04 MeV, m_s = 104 MeV
- Minimum strangelet baryon number range: A_min ≈ 10–600, with long-lived strangelets having lifetimes of days
- Strangelet formation rate for m_χ = 1 GeV/c², f = 0.9: Ṅ_slet ≈ 10²³ s⁻¹ for A = 10
- Lower limit on WIMP mass: m_χ ≳ 4 GeV/c² for typical baryonic density ~3 n_A,0 (n_A,0 = 0.17 fm⁻³) and bag constant range B^(1/4) = 145–170 MeV
- Conversion time-scale: t_conv ≈ 100 s (from Bhattacharyya et al., 2006)

## Context
This work extends the idea of WIMP capture and annihilation in neutron stars, previously studied by Kouvaris (2008) and de Lavallaz & Fairbairn (2010) for cooling effects, to a new mechanism for seeding strange quark matter. It builds on Witten's (1984) proposal that strange quark matter may be absolutely stable and on subsequent models of neutron star to strange star conversion by Alcock et al. (1986), Olinto (1987), Olesen & Madsen (1994), and Bombaci et al. (2007, 2009). The derived WIMP mass limit complements constraints from direct detection experiments and indirect searches such as ICECUBE. The mechanism could potentially explain gamma-ray bursts from neutron star conversion, as discussed by Bombaci et al. (2006), and may be testable through observations of quark stars or strangelet production at LHC or RHIC.
