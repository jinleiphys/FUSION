# $Δ(1232)$ Resonance Contribution to Two-Photon Exchange in Electron-Proton Scattering Revisited

> We revisit the question of the contributions of two-photon exchange with $Δ(1232)$ excitation to the electron-proton scattering in a hadronic model. Three improvements over the previous calculations are made, namely, correct vertex function for $γN\rightarrowΔ$, realistic $γNΔ$ form factors, and coupling constants. The discrepancy between the values of $R\equiv μ_p G_E/G_M$ extracted from Rosenbluth technique and polarization transfer method can be reasonably accounted for if the data of Andivahis {\it et al.} \cite{Andivahis94} are analyzed. However, substantial discrepancy remains if the data of Qattan {\it et al.} \cite{Qattan05} are used. For the ratio $R^\pm$ between $e^\pm p$ scatterings, our predictions appear to be in satisfactory agreement with the preliminary data from VEPP-3. The agreement between our model predictions and the recent measurements on single spin asymmetry, transverse and longitudinal recoil proton polarizations ranges from good to poor.

## Key claim

The two-photon exchange contribution from the Δ(1232) resonance can reasonably account for the discrepancy between Rosenbluth and polarization transfer measurements of the proton form factor ratio R = μ_p G_E/G_M when the data of Andivahis et al. (1994) are analyzed, but substantial discrepancy remains if the data of Qattan et al. (2005) are used.

## Method

The authors computed the two-photon exchange amplitude with Δ(1232) intermediate states in a hadronic model, using the correct γNΔ vertex function derived from Hermiticity of the electromagnetic current, realistic γNΔ form factors fitted to pion electroproduction data, and coupling constants extracted from recent experiments. Loop integrals were evaluated numerically using FeynCalc and LoopTools, and the resulting corrections were applied to unpolarized cross sections, the ratio R, positron-proton versus electron-proton cross section ratios, single spin asymmetries, and recoil proton polarizations.

## Key numbers

- At Q² = 3 GeV², the coefficients C_ij in δ_Δ = Σ C_ij g_i g_j range from C_11 = -0.053 × 10⁻⁴ at ε = 0.1 to C_11 = 0.462 × 10⁻⁴ at ε = 0.9, with C_33 values between 0.036 × 10⁻⁶ and 0.554 × 10⁻⁶
- The coupling constants used are (g₁, g₂, g₃) = (6.59, 9.08, 7.12), extracted from the experiments of Pascalu et al. (2007)
- The form factor cutoff parameters are Λ₁ = 0.84 GeV, Λ₂ = 2 GeV, Λ₃ = √2 GeV, Λ₄ = 0.2 GeV, with a = -0.3
- The extracted G_M values from fitting data94 at ε > 0.7 are 0.249, 0.146, 0.0958, 0.0670 at Q² = 1.75, 2.5, 3.25, 4.0 GeV² respectively
- The extracted G_M values from fitting data06 at ε > 0.7 are 0.964, 0.136, 0.100, 0.0657 at Q² = 0.5, 2.64, 3.2, 4.1 GeV² respectively

## Context

This work extends the hadronic model calculations of two-photon exchange with Δ excitation by Kondratyuk et al. (2005) and Blunden et al. (2003, 2005), correcting the γNΔ vertex function sign error identified by Zhou et al. (2010) and incorporating realistic form factors and coupling constants from recent pion electroproduction experiments (Joo et al., 2002; Sparveris et al., 2005; Ungaro et al., 2006). The results enable more accurate comparisons with the dispersion relation approach of Borisyuk and Kobushkin (2006, 2008, 2011, 2012, 2014) and with experimental data from VEPP-3 on the positron-proton to electron-proton cross section ratio R^±, as well as with polarization transfer measurements at JLab.
