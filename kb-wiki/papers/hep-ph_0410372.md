# Open Charm and Beauty at Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Colliders

> Important goals of RHIC and LHC experiments with ion beams include the creation and study of new forms of matter, such as the Quark Gluon Plasma. Heavy quark production and attenuation will provide unique tomographic probes of that matter. We predict the suppression pattern of open charm and beauty in $Au+Au$ collisions at RHIC and LHC energies based on the DGLV formalism of radiative energy loss. A cancelation between effects due to the $\sqrt{s}$ energy dependence of the high $p_T$ slope and heavy quark energy loss is predicted to lead to surprising similarity of heavy quark suppression at RHIC and LHC.

# Open Charm and Beauty at Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Colliders

## Key claim
The authors predict that the nuclear modification factor R_AA for open charm and beauty mesons will be surprisingly similar at RHIC and LHC energies, because the increase in heavy quark energy loss from higher gluon density at LHC is mostly compensated by the decrease in the slope of the initial heavy quark p_T distribution at higher collision energy. This similarity holds specifically for the PHOBOS extrapolation of gluon rapidity density; under the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) extrapolation, charm suppression at LHC is predicted to be about 20% higher than at RHIC.

## Method
The authors compute heavy quark suppression using the DGLV generalization of the GLV opacity expansion to heavy quarks, including multi-gluon fluctuations. Initial heavy quark p_T distributions are generated with the MNR code using CTEQ5M parton distributions, assuming charm mass 1.2 GeV and beauty mass 4.75 GeV. The medium-induced gluon radiation spectrum is computed for central Au+Au collisions with L = 6 fm, constant alpha_S = 0.3, and gluon rapidity densities extrapolated from RHIC data (PHOBOS) or from CGC effective theory, using a 1+1D Bjorken longitudinal expansion with an effective average density approximation.

## Key numbers
- Charm mass assumed: M_c = 1.2 GeV; beauty mass assumed: M_b = 4.75 GeV
- Central collision path length: L = R_x = R_y = 6 fm
- Constant strong coupling: alpha_S = 0.3
- For PHOBOS extrapolation at LHC, gluon density is projected to be approximately 60% higher than at RHIC; for CGC prediction, initial gluon density is predicted to be about 3 times higher than at RHIC
- At LHC (sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV), a difference of 1000 in gluon rapidity density leads to only about 0.1 difference in R_AA
- Predicted D meson suppression: R_AA approximately 0.5 +/- 0.1 for dN_g/dy approximately 1000 +/- 200 inferred from pi^0
- Theoretical uncertainty in R_AA of about 20% due to different reasonable choices of kinematical bounds in the gluon radiation spectrum
- Difference of less than 0.05 in R_AA between delta-function and Peterson fragmentation choices

## Context
This work extends the DGLV formalism for heavy quark radiative energy loss (Djordjevic and Gyulassy, 2004) to predict suppression patterns at both RHIC and LHC energies, building on the GLV opacity expansion (Gyulassy, Levai, and Vitev, 2001). The predictions contrast with the stronger collision energy dependence expected for light hadron quenching (Vitev and Gyulassy, 2002), because gluon jet components do not contribute to D and B meson production. The results enable experimental tests at RHIC and LHC that can differentiate between initial condition models such as the PHOBOS extrapolation and the CGC prediction (Kharzeev, Levin, and Nardi, 2004), and provide critical consistency checks for pQCD-based radiative energy loss theory in strongly coupled Quark Gluon Plasma.
