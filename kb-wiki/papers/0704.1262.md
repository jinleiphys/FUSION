# Symmetry Breaking Study with Deformed Ensembles

> A random matrix model to describe the coupling of m-fold symmetry in constructed. The particular threefold case is used to analyze data on eigenfrequencies of elastomechanical vibration of an anisotropic quartz block. It is suggested that such experimental/theoretical study may supply powerful means to discern intrinsic symmetries in physical systems.

# Symmetry Breaking Study with Deformed Ensembles

## Key claim

The paper claims that the eigenfrequency spectra of an anisotropic quartz block with D3 point-group symmetry, previously interpreted as a two-fold symmetry breaking case (2GOE), can be better described by a three-fold symmetry breaking model (3GOE) when a small fraction of missing levels (about 10%) is taken into account, without needing to invoke pseudo-integrable effects.

## Method

The authors construct a Deformed Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (DGOE) for m-fold symmetry by writing the Hamiltonian as H = H₀ + λH₁, where H₀ is block diagonal with m uncoupled GOE blocks and λ controls the coupling strength. They perform numerical simulations with 2-block and 3-block matrices of sizes 105×105 and 70×70 each (total matrix size 210×210) using an ensemble of 1000 elements, and compare the resulting nearest-neighbor spacing distributions and spectral rigidity Δ₃(L) to experimental eigenfrequency data from an anisotropic quartz block with varying octant radii.

## Key numbers

- The quartz block dimensions are 14 × 25 × 40 mm³, with octant radii r = 0.0, 0.5, 0.8, 1.1, 1.4, and 1.7 mm for cases (a) through (f), plus a large sphere of radius 10.0 mm
- The number of eigenfrequencies measured in cases (a)-(f) are 1424, 1414, 1424, 1414, 1424, and 1419 respectively, in the frequency range 600 to 900 kHz
- The average spacing between modes is about 214 Hz
- For the 3GOE description of the r = 0.5 mm case, a missing level fraction g = 0.1 (10% missing levels) provides a perfect fit to the Δ₃ data
- For the 2GOE description of the same case, a missing level fraction g = 0.3 (30% missing levels) is required, which the authors consider too large to conform to the reported data

## Context

This work extends the Deformed Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (DGOE) approach previously developed by Pato et al. (2001-2003) and Hussein and Pato (1998) for two-block symmetry breaking, generalizing it to m-fold symmetry. It reanalyzes the elastomechanical vibration experiment of Ellegaard et al. (2001-2005) on an anisotropic quartz block, which had previously shown a discrepancy between the nearest-neighbor distribution (fitted by 2GOE) and the Δ₃ statistics (attributed to pseudo-integrable behavior by Abul-Magd, 2005). The authors show that a 3GOE description with missing levels (Bohigas and Pato, 2004) resolves this discrepancy, suggesting the quartz block may possess an additional symmetry beyond the two-fold flip symmetry. This enables more precise testing of intrinsic symmetries in physical systems using spectral statistics.
