# Gamow-Teller transitions and proton-neutron pair correlation in $N=Z=$ odd $p$-shell nuclei

> We have studied the Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions from $N=Z+2$ neighbors to $N=Z=$ odd nuclei in $p$-shell region by using isospin-projected and $βγ$-constraint antisymmetrized molecular dynamics combined with generator coordinate method. The calculated GT transition strengths from $0^+1$ states to $1^+0$ states such as ${}^{6} \textrm{He}(0_1^+1)\rightarrow{}^{6} \textrm{Li}(1_1^+0)$, ${}^{10} \textrm{Be}(0_1^+1)\rightarrow{}^{10} \textrm{B}(1_1^+0)$, and ${}^{14} \textrm{C}(0_1^+1)\rightarrow{}^{14} \textrm{N}(1_2^+0)$ exhaust more than 50\% of the sum rule. These $N=Z+2$ initial states and $N=Z=$ odd final states are found to dominantly have $S=0,T=1$ $nn$ pairs and $S=1,T=0$ $pn$ pairs, respectively. Based on two-nucleon ($NN$) pair picture, we can understand the concentration of the GT strengths as the spin-isospin-flip transition $nn(S=0,T=1)\rightarrow pn(S=1,T=0)$ in $LS$-coupling scheme. The GT transition can be a good probe to identify the spin-isospin partner states with $nn$ pairs and $pn$ pairs of $N=Z+2$ and $N=Z=$ odd nuclei, respectively.

# Gamow-Teller transitions and proton-neutron pair correlation in $N=Z=$ odd $p$-shell nuclei

## Key claim

The concentration of Gamow-Teller (GT) transition strengths from $N=Z+2$ nuclei to $N=Z=$ odd nuclei in the $p$-shell region can be understood as spin-isospin-flip transitions between $nn(S=0,T=1)$ pairs and $pn(S=1,T=0)$ pairs in the $LS$-coupling scheme, provided that the core nuclei are spin-isospin saturated and contribute negligibly to the GT transitions.

## Method

The authors applied isospin-projected and $\beta\gamma$-constraint antisymmetrized molecular dynamics ($T\beta\gamma$-AMD) combined with the generator coordinate method (GCM) to calculate wavefunctions for $N=Z=$ odd nuclei (${}^{6}\textrm{Li}$, ${}^{10}\textrm{B}$, ${}^{14}\textrm{N}$) and $\beta\gamma$-constraint AMD for $N=Z+2$ nuclei (${}^{6}\textrm{He}$, ${}^{10}\textrm{Be}$, ${}^{14}\textrm{C}$). They used the Volkov No.~2 central force with Majorana exchange parameter $m=0.6$ and the G3RS spin-orbit force with strength parameters $u_1=-u_2=1300$ MeV, with Bartlett and Heisenberg parameters $b=h=0.125$ for ${}^{6}\textrm{Li}$ and $b=h=0.06$ for ${}^{10}\textrm{B}$ and ${}^{14}\textrm{N}$.

## Key numbers

- GT transition ${}^{6}\textrm{He}(0_1^+1)\rightarrow{}^{6}\textrm{Li}(1_1^+0)$: calculated $B(\textrm{GT}) = 5.31$ (experimental: 3.02)
- GT transition ${}^{10}\textrm{Be}(0_1^+1)\rightarrow{}^{10}\textrm{B}(1_1^+0)$: calculated $B(\textrm{GT}) = 4.95$ (experimental mirror transition: 2.20)
- GT transition ${}^{14}\textrm{C}(0_1^+1)\rightarrow{}^{14}\textrm{N}(1_2^+0)$: calculated $B(\textrm{GT}) = 4.32$ (experimental: 1.70)
- GT transition ${}^{14}\textrm{C}(0_1^+1)\rightarrow{}^{14}\textrm{N}(1_1^+0)$: calculated $B(\textrm{GT}) = 0.30$ (experimental: $3.64\times10^{-6}$)
- Sum of GT strengths from ${}^{6}\textrm{He}(2_1^+1)$ to ${}^{6}\textrm{Li}(1_2^+0,2_1^+0,3_1^+0)$: approximately 50% of the sum rule $\sum B(\textrm{GT})=3(N-Z)=6$
- Sum of GT strengths from ${}^{14}\textrm{C}(2_1^+1)$ to ${}^{14}\textrm{N}(1_1^+0,2_1^+0,3_1^+0)$: more than 80% of the sum rule
- Spin expectation value $\langle \boldsymbol{S}^2 \rangle$ for ${}^{6}\textrm{He}(0_1^+1)$: 0.12 (6% mixing of $S=1$ component)
- Spin expectation value $\langle \boldsymbol{S}^2 \rangle$ for ${}^{14}\textrm{C}(0_1^+1)$: indicates up to 27% mixing of $S=1$ component

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of $pn$ correlation in ${}^{10}\textrm{B}$ using $T\beta\gamma$-AMD (Morita and Kanada-En'yo, 2016) and connects to three-body model calculations of two nucleons around a doubly magic core by Sagawa (2009). The paper provides a systematic interpretation of GT transitions in $A=6$, $A=10$, and $A=14$ systems as spin-isospin partner transitions between $nn$ pairs and $pn$ pairs in $LS$-coupling scheme, which differs from the $jj$-coupling treatment of proton-neutron pairing in medium and heavy mass nuclei by Gezerlis et al. (2011), Cederwall et al. (2011), and Qi et al. (2011). The results enable future identification of spin-isospin partner states in $N=Z=$ odd nuclei through charge-exchange reactions.
