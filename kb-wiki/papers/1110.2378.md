# Entropy Creation in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We review current ideas on entropy production during the different stages of a relativistic nuclear collision. This includes recent results on decoherence entropy and the entropy produced during the hydrodynamic phase by viscous effects. We start by a discussion of decoherence caused by gluon bremsstrahlung in the very first interactions of gluons from the colliding nuclei. We then present a general framework, based on the Husimi distribution function, for the calculation of entropy growth in quantum field theories, which is applicable to the early ("glasma") phase of the collision during which most of the entropy is generated. The entropy calculated from the Husimi distribution exhibits linear growth when the quantum field contains unstable modes and the growth rateis asymptotically equal to the Kolmogorov-Sinaï (KS) entropy. We outline how the approach can be used to investigate the problem of entropy production in a relativistic heavy-ion reaction from first principles. Finally we discuss some recent results on entropy production in the strong coupling limit, as obtained from AdS/CFT duality.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how entropy is generated during the different stages of a relativistic heavy-ion collision, arguing that most entropy is produced during the early thermalization phase rather than during the later hydrodynamic expansion, and that the Husimi distribution formalism provides a first-principles framework for calculating entropy growth in quantum field theories that is asymptotically equal to the Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy rate.

## Method

The authors review theoretical approaches to entropy production across five stages of a heavy-ion collision: decoherence of initial nuclear wave functions, thermalization of the partonic glasma phase, viscous dissipation during hydrodynamic expansion, hadronization, and hadronic freeze-out. They present the Husimi distribution as a coarse-grained phase-space distribution that yields a positive-definite entropy (Wehrl entropy), and show that for systems with unstable modes this entropy grows linearly at a rate given by the sum of positive Lyapunov exponents. They also discuss the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis as an alternative framework and review results from viscous hydrodynamics and AdS/CFT duality.

## Key numbers

- Final entropy per unit rapidity for central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: (dS/dy)_f = 5600 ± 500 from HBT analysis, and (dS/dy)_ch = 5100 ± 200 from hadron abundances
- Decoherence time: tau_dec = c Q_s^{-1} with c = O(1), corresponding to less than 0.2 fm/c
- Entropy from decoherence at midrapidity: (dS/dy)_dec ≈ 1500, roughly one quarter of the observed final entropy
- Shear viscosity bound from RHIC data: 1 ≤ 4π η/s ≤ 2.5
- Initial time for hydrodynamic simulations: tau_0 = 0.3 fm/c with initial energy density ε_0 = 50 GeV/fm^3
- Equilibration time from viscous hydrodynamics: tau_eq ≈ 1 fm/c
- Viscous entropy production after tau_eq does not exceed 10 percent of final entropy
- Entropy at local equilibration: (dS/dy)_eq ≥ 4500

## Context

This review extends earlier work on entropy production in heavy-ion collisions by Müller (2005) and Fries et al. (2008) on decoherence and viscous entropy production, and by Kunihiro et al. (2008) on the Husimi formalism for entropy growth. It connects the problem of thermalization in heavy-ion collisions to broader questions in quantum field theory, including the eigenstate thermalization hypothesis proposed by Deutsch (1991) and Srednicki (1994), and the AdS/CFT duality approach to strong coupling dynamics. The paper enables future first-principles calculations of entropy production during the glasma phase and clarifies which stages of the collision contribute most to the final observed entropy.
