# Correlations in a Many-Body Calculation of $^{\bf 11}$Li

> A many-body calculation of $^{11}$Li is presented where the only input is the well-tested, finite-range {\it D1S} effective interaction of {\it Gogny}. Pairing correlations are included in a constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculation, while long-range collective correlations are introduced using a GCM derived calculation. Correlations are found to play an important role in describing $^{11}$Li. A substantive underlying $^9$Li core of $^{11}$Li is found, which has a different density profile than a free $^9$Li nucleus. This may have significant implications in the use of a three-body framework in studies of $^{11}$Li.

## Key claim

A many-body calculation of $^{11}$Li using only the D1S Gogny effective interaction finds a substantive $^9$Li core with a density profile different from a free $^9$Li nucleus, which may require three-body frameworks to account for this difference if more accurate calculations are desired.

## Method

The authors performed a constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculation in a nineteen-shell axially-symmetric harmonic oscillator basis, using the D1S Gogny force as the sole input. Long-range collective correlations were then introduced by applying a Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) formalism, where the nuclear ground state was represented as a superposition of HFB states constrained on different values of the $\langle r^2\rangle$ collective variable, with the Hill-Wheeler equations reduced to a collective Schrödinger equation of the Bohr type via a Gaussian overlap approximation.

## Key numbers

- The unconstrained HFB calculation gives a total $rms$ matter radius for $^{11}$Li of about 2.80 fm, with separate proton and neutron $rms$ radii of 2.30 fm and 2.97 fm, respectively.
- For a similar HFB calculation of free $^9$Li, the total, proton, and neutron $rms$ radii are 2.47 fm, 2.24 fm, and 2.58 fm, respectively.
- The central neutron density of free $^9$Li is about 15% larger than that of the $^9$Li core projected from $^{11}$Li.
- The gap between the $1p3/2$ proton level and higher single-particle levels is about 6 MeV and remains for all $q$ considered.
- The occupation probabilities for the six core neutrons are almost always between 1.00 and 0.99, with a minimum of approximately 0.987.
- With correlations included but without tail correction, the full $^{11}$Li $rms$ radius is 3.42 fm; with fitted tails the largest total $rms$ radius is 2.88 fm (case a), giving an increase due to correlations between approximately 0.04 and 0.08 fm.
- The fitted tail parameters $\alpha_q$ were set between 0.10 and 0.15, corresponding to energies of about -0.21 to -0.47 MeV.
- The $rms$ radii for the $^9$Li core change by only about 2% due to correlations; differences between the free $^9$Li nucleus and the $^9$Li core are about 5% for both protons and neutrons.

## Context

This work extends earlier mean-field and renormalized-potential studies of $^{11}$Li by groups such as Bertsch and collaborators, Sagawa, and Zhu, who found that correlations among outer single-particle states were needed to reproduce experimental two-neutron separation energies and $rms$ radii. The paper also addresses the three-body framework used by Bang, Suzuki, Jensen, Hansen, and Hayes, where $^{11}$Li is treated as two neutrons surrounding an inert $^9$Li core; the present results support the existence of a distinct core but show it differs from a free $^9$Li nucleus, suggesting that three-body calculations may need to account for this difference. The calculation enables future work using better basis representations, such as collocation B-spline bases in a large box, to more accurately describe the extended tail of the neutron distribution.
