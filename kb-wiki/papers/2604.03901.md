# Phenomenological Criteria of Halo Nuclei in Ne Isotopes via Diffuseness and Helm-Model Approaches with Reaction Cross Sections

> We present a systematic study of halo characteristics in the neutron-rich isotopes 28-32Ne within the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc). Microscopic density distributions are analyzed in coordinate space, momentum space, and reaction observables to establish a quantitative and locally defined criterion for halo identification in medium-mass nuclei. The DRHBc densities reveal a pronounced neutron extension in 31Ne. A phenomenological analysis based on deformed Woods-Saxon fits shows a clear isotopic anomaly in the surface diffuseness parameter, with a value of about 1.1 fm for 31Ne, significantly larger than those of neighboring isotopes. The anomalously large diffuseness is therefore treated as the primary phenomenological halo signature, whereas the reduced fitted radius parameter is used only as a supporting consequence of the chosen normalization and tail-sensitive fit. Helm-model form-factor analysis demonstrates that deformation contributes to geometric smearing but does not fully account for the extended spatial structure, as reflected in the enhanced difference between microscopic and folded rms radii. Glauber reaction cross section calculations further confirm a robust relative enhancement of the interaction cross section for 31Ne that persists across reasonable nucleon-nucleon interaction prescriptions. These complementary analyses consistently identify 31Ne as the most prominent halo candidate within the 28-32Ne isotopic chain, while 32Ne exhibits intermediate features and 29Ne shows no clear halo signature. The present framework provides a practical and quantitative approach for identifying halo phenomena in deformed, neutron-rich nuclei beyond the light-mass region.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that an anomalously large surface diffuseness parameter extracted from deformed Woods-Saxon fits serves as the primary phenomenological signature for identifying halo nuclei in medium-mass systems, while the reduced fitted radius parameter is treated only as a supporting consequence of the chosen normalization and tail-sensitive fit, not as an independent criterion.

## Method

The authors performed deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) calculations to obtain microscopic density distributions for the neutron-rich isotopes 28-32Ne. They then fitted these densities with a deformed Woods-Saxon form using logarithmic-scale fitting to extract surface diffuseness parameters, analyzed form factors within the Helm model framework, and calculated Glauber reaction cross sections to confirm the halo signatures.

## Key numbers

* The surface diffuseness parameter for 31Ne is about 1.1 fm, significantly larger than the values of about 0.67-0.70 fm for 28Ne, 29Ne, and 30Ne
* The one-neutron separation energy for 31Ne (N=21) shows a drastic reduction compared to neighboring isotopes, approaching the weak-binding regime
* The DRHBc root-mean-square radius for 31Ne is 3.499 fm, compared to 3.167 fm for 28Ne, 3.234 fm for 29Ne, 3.288 fm for 30Ne, and 3.430 fm for 32Ne
* The neutron tail contribution ratio I2/(I1+I2) for 31Ne is 0.191, distinctly larger than the values of about 0.16 for 28Ne, 29Ne, and 30Ne, and larger than 0.180 for 32Ne
* The diffuseness for 32Ne increases to about 0.81 fm, larger than lighter isotopes but significantly smaller than for 31Ne

## Context

This work extends previous studies of halo phenomena in light nuclei, such as the first observation of halo signatures in 11Li by Tanihata (1985), to medium-mass systems where deformation, pairing correlations, and continuum coupling can obscure halo signatures. The study builds on earlier particle-rotor and Glauber-model studies by Urata (2011, 2012) that examined the deformed halo interpretation of 31Ne, and on global halo indicators proposed by Rotival (2009) and Zhang (2023). The framework enables quantitative identification of halo phenomena in deformed, neutron-rich nuclei beyond the light-mass region, with 31Ne identified as the most prominent halo candidate while 32Ne exhibits intermediate features and 29Ne shows no clear halo signature.
