# Bayesian analysis of a relativistic hadronic model constrained by recent astrophysical observations

> We use Bayesian analysis in order to constrain the equation of state for nuclear matter from astrophysical data related to the recent measurements from the NICER mission, LIGO/Virgo collaboration, and probability distributions of mass and radius from other 12 sources, including thermonuclear busters, and quiescent low-mass X-ray binaries. For this purpose, we base our study on a relativistic hadronic mean field model including an $ω-ρ$ interaction. Our results indicate optimal ranges for some bulk parameters at the saturation density, namely, effective mass, incompressibility, and symmetry energy slope ($L_0$). For instance, we find $L_0 = 50.79^{+15.16}_{-9.24}$ MeV (Case 1) and $L_0 = 75.06^{+8.43}_{-4.43}$ MeV (Case 2) in a $68\%$ confidence interval for the 2 cases analyzed (different input ranges for $L_0$ related to the PREX-II data). The respective parametrizations are in agreement with important nuclear matter constraints, as well as observational neutron star data, such as the dimensionless tidal deformability of the GW170817 event. From the mass-radius curves obtained from these best parametrizations, we also find the ranges of $11.97 \mbox{ km}\leqslant R_{1.4}\leqslant 12.73 \mbox{ km}$ (Case 1) and $12.34 \mbox{ km}\leqslant R_{1.4}\leqslant 13.06 \mbox{ km}$ (Case 2) for the radius of the $1.4M_\odot$ neutron star.

## Key claim

The paper uses Bayesian analysis to constrain the equation of state for nuclear matter from astrophysical data, finding that the symmetry energy slope \(L_0\) depends significantly on the prior range chosen: Case 1 (wide prior from PREX-II data) gives \(L_0 = 50.79^{+15.16}_{-9.24}\) MeV, while Case 2 (narrower prior from the intersection of PREX-II results) gives \(L_0 = 75.06^{+8.43}_{-4.43}\) MeV at 68% confidence, with the Bayes factor showing substantial evidence favoring the narrower prior range.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic mean-field hadronic model with cubic and quartic \(\sigma\) self-interactions and an \(\omega\)-\(\rho\) crossed interaction, fixing the saturation density \(\rho_0\) and binding energy \(E_0\) while inferring the effective mass \(m_0^*\), incompressibility \(K_0\), symmetry energy at \(2\rho_0/3\) (\(\mathcal{S}_{2/3}\)), and symmetry energy slope \(L_0\) via Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling. The likelihood was constructed from 16 astrophysical sources: 11 mass-radius distributions from thermonuclear busters and quiescent low-mass X-ray binaries (Ozel 2016), one from 4U 1702-429 (Nattila 2017), two from the GW170817 event (LIGO/Virgo Collaboration 2018), and two from NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 (Riley 2019) and PSR J0740+6620 (Riley 2021). Two prior cases for \(L_0\) were tested: Case 1 (38–143 MeV) and Case 2 (69–107.8 MeV), each with both uniform and Gaussian distributions.

## Key numbers

* Symmetry energy slope: \(L_0 = 50.79^{+15.16}_{-9.24}\) MeV (Case 1, uniform prior, 68% CI) and \(L_0 = 75.06^{+8.43}_{-4.43}\) MeV (Case 2, uniform prior, 68% CI)
* Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star: \(11.97\ \mathrm{km} \leq R_{1.4} \leq 12.73\ \mathrm{km}\) (Case 1) and \(12.34\ \mathrm{km} \leq R_{1.4} \leq 13.06\ \mathrm{km}\) (Case 2)
* Effective mass at saturation: \(m_0^* = 0.751\) (mean, Case 1 uniform) and \(m_0^* = 0.757\) (mean, Case 2 uniform)
* Incompressibility: \(K_0 = 237.54\) MeV (mean, Case 1 uniform) and \(K_0 = 235.88\) MeV (mean, Case 2 uniform)
* Symmetry energy at saturation: \(J = 31.28\) MeV (mean, Case 1 uniform) and \(J = 33.65\) MeV (mean, Case 2 uniform)
* Symmetry energy at \(2\rho_0/3\): \(\mathcal{S}_{2/3} = 24.22\) MeV (mean, Case 1 uniform) and \(\mathcal{S}_{2/3} = 24.34\) MeV (mean, Case 2 uniform)

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian inference approach of Silvia (2020) by including an \(\omega\)-\(\rho\) interaction term in the relativistic mean-field Lagrangian, allowing the symmetry energy slope and the symmetry energy at \(2/3\) saturation density to be treated as independent isovector parameters. The results are consistent with nuclear matter constraints from chiral effective field theory (Hebeler 2013) and with observational neutron star data including the tidal deformability from GW170817 (LIGO/Virgo Collaboration 2017). The Bayes factor analysis shows substantial evidence favoring the narrower \(L_0\) prior (Case 2) over the wider one, supporting recent determinations of \(L_0\) from PREX-II data that yield lower values, such as \(L_0 = (54 \pm 8)\) MeV from Reinhard (2021).
