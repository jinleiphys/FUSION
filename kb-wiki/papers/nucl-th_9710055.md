# Interacting Boson Model plus broken-pairs description of high-spin dipole bands

> The Interacting Boson Model with broken-pairs has been extended to include mixed proton-neutron configurations in the fermion model space. The extended version of the model has been used to describe high-spin bands in the transitional nucleus $^{136}$Nd. Model calculations reproduce ten bands of positive and negative parity states, including the two dipole high-spin structures based on the $(πh_{11/2})^2$ $(νh_{11/2})^2$ configuration.

## Key claim

The Interacting Boson Model with broken pairs has been extended to include mixed proton-neutron configurations in the fermion model space, enabling the first description of high-spin dipole bands in even-even nuclei within the interacting boson approximation. The model reproduces ten bands of positive and negative parity states in the transitional nucleus $^{136}$Nd, including the two dipole high-spin structures based on the $(\pi h_{11/2})^2$ $(\nu h_{11/2})^2$ configuration, but the authors note that the correct energy spacings for these dipole bands required the inclusion of a higher-order term in the boson quadrupole operator and a residual proton-neutron interaction.

## Method

The authors extended the Interacting Boson Model with broken pairs (IBM-1) to include both proton and neutron broken pairs in the fermion model space, using a Hamiltonian with boson, fermion, boson-fermion, and pair-breaking terms plus a residual proton-neutron interaction modeled by a surface delta force. For $^{136}$Nd (boson number N=8), they included only the $h_{11/2}$ orbitals for protons and neutrons in the positive-parity calculation, with single-quasiparticle energies renormalized to 1.60 MeV for protons and 1.75 MeV for neutrons, and used a prediagonalization of the boson Hamiltonian to reduce the two-broken-pair space. The calculation reproduced the complete experimental spectrum of positive and negative parity states up to angular momentum 29 $\hbar$ at more than 13 MeV excitation energy.

## Key numbers

- Boson number N=8 for $^{136}$Nd (6 neutron valence holes, 10 proton valence particles)
- Proton $h_{11/2}$ occupation probability $v^2_{\pi}(h_{11/2}) = 0.07$ and single-quasiparticle energy $E_{\pi}(h_{11/2}) = 1.74$ MeV (renormalized to 1.60 MeV)
- Neutron $h_{11/2}$ occupation probability $v^2_{\nu}(h_{11/2}) = 0.83$ and single-quasiparticle energy $E_{\nu}(h_{11/2}) = 1.13$ MeV (renormalized to 1.75 MeV)
- Boson Hamiltonian parameters: $\epsilon$=0.36, $C_0$=0.16, $C_2=-0.12$, $C_4=0.19$, $V_2$=0.11, $V_0=-0.15$ (all in MeV)
- Neutron dynamical fermion-boson interaction: $\Gamma_0$=0.3 MeV, $\chi=-1$, $\chi'=-0.2$; for protons: $\Gamma_0$=0.22 MeV, $\chi=+1$, $\chi'=+0.2$
- Residual surface delta force strengths: $V_{\nu\nu} = -0.1$ MeV, $V_{\pi\pi} = -0.1$ MeV, $V_{\nu\pi} = -0.9$ MeV
- Pair-breaking interaction strength $U_2=0.2$ MeV for both protons and neutrons
- Negative parity bands 1 and 2 based on $(\nu d_{3/2}, \nu h_{11/2})$ configuration; band 12 based on $\nu d_{3/2}(\nu h_{11/2})^3$ configuration

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous IBM with broken pairs calculations for even-even and odd-even nuclei in the Hg, Sr-Zr, and Nd-Sm regions (Vretenar 1993, Vretenar 1995, Petrache 1997) by including mixed proton-neutron configurations. The results contradict the projected shell model interpretation of Sun (1996), which assigned a $(\pi h_{11/2})^2$ $\nu f_{7/2}$ $\nu h_{11/2}$ configuration to the dipole bands based on an assumed axially symmetric deformation $\epsilon_2 = 0.20$, whereas the present calculation finds structures based on the $\nu f_{7/2}$ orbital high above yrast. The model enables description of high-spin dipole bands in transitional and spherical nuclei without assuming a geometrical picture, using a single set of parameters for collective bands, two-quasiparticle bands, and four-quasiparticle bands.
