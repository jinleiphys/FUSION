# Lattice QCD calculation of the two-photon exchange contribution to the muonic-hydrogen Lamb shift

> We develop a method for lattice QCD calculation of the two-photon exchange (TPE) contribution to the muonic-hydrogen Lamb shift. To demonstrate the feasibility of this method, we also present an exploratory study with a gauge ensemble at $m_π= 142$ MeV. By adopting the infinite-volume reconstruction (IVR) method along with an optimized subtraction scheme, we obtain a preliminary result of the TPE contribution which agrees well with previous calculation using other methods and one magnitude smaller compare to the large $\sim300~μ$eV discrepancy for the proton radius puzzle.

## Key claim

The paper develops a lattice QCD method to directly calculate the two-photon exchange (TPE) contribution to the muonic-hydrogen Lamb shift, and presents a preliminary result at a pion mass of 142 MeV that agrees well with previous data-driven analyses. The result is one order of magnitude smaller than the approximately 300 μeV discrepancy at the center of the proton radius puzzle, but the authors note this is an exploratory study and systematic errors from excited-state contamination and lattice discretization remain to be quantified.

## Method

The method computes the spin-averaged forward doubly-virtual Compton scattering (VVCS) tensor in Euclidean space using lattice QCD four-point correlation functions with proton interpolating operators and two electromagnetic currents. The infinite-volume reconstruction (IVR) method is adopted to remove power-law finite-volume effects, and an optimized subtraction scheme is employed to split the TPE contribution into a part proportional to the proton charge radius and a residual lattice-calculable part, thereby reducing long-distance contributions and suppressing both finite-volume effects and the signal-to-noise problem. The calculation uses a single gauge ensemble with 2+1-flavor domain wall fermions at mπ = 142 MeV, with five types of quark contractions considered (Type I, II, III computed; Type IV and V neglected as they vanish in the flavor SU(3) limit).

## Key numbers

- Pion mass of the gauge ensemble: mπ = 142 MeV
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.1944 fm
- Spatial extent: L = 24 (corresponding to approximately 4.7 fm)
- Number of configurations: Nconf = 131
- Time separation for ground-state saturation: Δt = 2a = 0.39 fm
- Saturation time for the plateau in ΔE(lat): ts = 4a = 0.79 fm
- Optimized subtraction coefficients: c0 = -0.17 μeV and cr = -93.72 μeV/fm²
- The final TPE correction expression: ΔETPE = 0.77 μeV + 93.72 μeV/fm² ⟨rp²⟩ - ΔE(lat)
- A finite contribution of -0.60 μeV arises from the spatial component subtraction scheme

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD approaches to the TPE correction, which has previously been addressed using dispersion relations (Pachucki 1999, Carlson 2011), baryon chiral perturbation theory (Alarcon 2013), heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (Birse 2012), non-relativistic QED (Hill 2011), and operator product expansion (Hill 2016). The method builds on the infinite-volume reconstruction technique developed for QED self-energy calculations (Feng 2018, Feng 2021) and the charge-radius extraction method used for pions (Feng 2019). This calculation enables a first-principles lattice QCD determination of the TPE contribution, which is the largest theoretical uncertainty in the muonic-hydrogen Lamb shift and is critical for resolving the proton radius puzzle. The method can be extended to TPE corrections for hyperfine splitting and electron-proton scattering.
