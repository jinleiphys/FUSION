# The splitting of directed flow for identified light hadrons ($K$ and $p$) and strange baryons ($Ξ$ and $Ω$) in Au+Au collisions at STAR

> The first measurements for rapidity-odd directed flow of $Ξ$ and $Ω$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm {NN}}}=$ 27 and 200 GeV are reported. The coalescence sum rule is examined with various combinations of hadrons where all constituent quarks are produced, such as $K^{\!^-}\!\!({ \bar{u}}{s})$, $\bar{p}({\bar{u}\bar{u}}{\bar{d}})$, $\barΛ(\bar{u}\bar{d}\bar{s})$, $ϕ({s}{\bar{s}})$, $\barΞ^{+}(\bar{d}\bar{s}\bar{s})$, $Ω^{-}(sss)$, and $\barΩ^{+}(\bar{s}\bar{s}\bar{s})$. For such combinations, a systematic violation of the sum rule is observed with increasing difference in the electric charge and the strangeness content of the combinations. Measurements are compared with the calculations of A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model and Parton-Hadron String Dynamics (PHSD) model with electromagnetic (EM) field. The PHSD model with EM field agrees with the measurements within uncertainties.

# The splitting of directed flow for identified light hadrons (K and p) and strange baryons (Ξ and Ω) in Au+Au collisions at STAR

## Key claim

The first measurements of rapidity-odd directed flow for Ξ and Ω in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 27 and 200 GeV show a systematic violation of the coalescence sum rule for hadron combinations composed entirely of produced quarks, with the violation increasing as the difference in electric charge and strangeness content of the combinations increases. The Parton-Hadron String Dynamics (PHSD) model with electromagnetic field agrees with the measurements within uncertainties, while the AMPT model does not.

## Method

The STAR detector was used to reconstruct directed flow (v1) of K−, p̄, Λ̄, φ, Ξ̄+, Ω−, and Ω̄+ in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 27 and 200 GeV, selecting only particles composed of produced constituent quarks (ū, d̄, s, s̄) to avoid background from transported quarks. The coalescence sum rule was tested by forming five independent hadron combinations with near-zero mass difference but varying electric charge difference (Δq) and strangeness difference (ΔS), and the Δv1 slope with rapidity was extracted via linear fits v1(y) = Cy.

## Key numbers

- Slope parameter C for Ξ− at √sNN = 27 GeV: −0.0083 ± 0.0020 (stat.) ± 0.00 (syst.)
- Slope parameter C for Ξ̄+ at √sNN = 27 GeV: −0.0148 ± 0.0028 (stat.) ± 0.0013 (syst.)
- Slope parameter C for Ω− at √sNN = 27 GeV: −0.0214 ± 0.008 (stat.) ± 0.0034 (syst.)
- Slope parameter C for Ω̄+ at √sNN = 27 GeV: −0.0075 ± 0.0118 (stat.) ± 0.0017 (syst.)
- d²Δv1/dy dΔq at √sNN = 27 GeV: [2.952 ± 0.489 (stat.) ± 0.367 (syst.)] × 10⁻³
- d²Δv1/dy dΔq at √sNN = 200 GeV: [1.242 ± 0.381 (stat.) ± 0.258 (syst.)] × 10⁻³
- The magnitude of the Δv1 slope is larger at 27 GeV than at 200 GeV with 4.83σ significance.

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of directed flow splitting between positively and negatively charged hadrons at STAR (Adamczyk 2016) and ALICE (Acharya 2019), which were complicated by transported u and d quarks from beam rapidity (Dunlop 2011; Guo 2012). By selecting only hadrons composed of produced quarks, the analysis isolates the electromagnetic-field-driven splitting from transport-driven background. The results enable quantitative tests of the coalescence sum rule and provide constraints for PHSD and AMPT model calculations of early-stage electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions.
