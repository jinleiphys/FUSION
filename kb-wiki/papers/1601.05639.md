# Charmonia in a Contact Interaction

> For the flavour-singlet heavy quark system of charmonia, we compute the masses of the ground state mesons in four different channels: pseudo-scalar ($η_c(1S)$), vector ($J/Ψ(1S)$), scalar ($χ_{c_0}(1P)$) and axial vector ($χ_{c_{1}}(1P)$), as well as the weak decay constants of the $η_c(1S)$ and $J/Ψ(1S)$ and the charge radius of $η_c(1S)$. The framework for this analysis is provided by a symmetry-preserving Schwinger-Dyson equation (SDEs) treatment of a vector$\times$vector contact interaction (CI). The results found for the meson masses and the weak decay constants, for the spin-spin combinations studied, are in fairly good agreement with experimental data and earlier model calculations based upon Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter equations (BSEs) involving sophisticated interaction kernels. The charge radius of $η_c(1S)$ is consistent with the results from refined SDE studies and lattice Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD).

## Key claim

A symmetry-preserving contact interaction (CI) model, without parameter readjustment from the light quark sector, yields charmonia masses in good agreement with experiment and more sophisticated Schwinger-Dyson equation (SDE) studies; however, the weak decay constants of the η_c(1S) and J/Ψ(1S) are strongly underestimated unless the model parameters (effective coupling and ultraviolet cutoff) are re-tuned specifically for the heavy quark sector.

## Method

The authors employ a vector×vector contact interaction within a rainbow-ladder truncation of the Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter equations, using a proper-time regularization scheme that preserves the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity. They solve the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for ground-state charmonia in the pseudoscalar, vector, scalar, and axial-vector channels, and compute the weak decay constants and charge radius from the normalized Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes. A phenomenological spin-orbit repulsion factor (g_SO) is introduced in the scalar and axial-vector channels to account for parity-partner splitting.

## Key numbers

- Pseudoscalar η_c(1S) mass: 2.983 GeV (fitted to experiment) with the light-sector parameter set; 2.950 GeV with the best-fit parameter set (experimental value: 2.983 GeV)
- Vector J/Ψ(1S) mass: 2.979 GeV with light-sector parameters; 3.129 GeV with best-fit parameters (experimental value: 3.096 GeV)
- Scalar χ_c0(1P) mass: 3.412 GeV with light-sector parameters and g_SO=0.24; 3.407 GeV with best-fit parameters (experimental value: 3.414 GeV)
- Axial-vector χ_c1(1P) mass: 3.442 GeV with light-sector parameters and g_SO=0.24; 3.433 GeV with best-fit parameters (experimental value: 3.510 GeV)
- Pseudoscalar decay constant f_η_c: 0.084 GeV with light-sector parameters (experimental value: 0.361 GeV)
- Vector decay constant f_J/Ψ: 0.080 GeV with light-sector parameters (experimental value: 0.416 GeV)
- Best-fit parameter set: α_IR = 0.93π/20, Λ_UV = 2.788 GeV, m_c = 0.956 GeV, M_c = 1.497 GeV

## Context

This work extends the contact interaction model previously applied to light mesons by Gutierrez-Guerrero (2010), Roberts (2011), and Chen (2012) to the heavy quark sector of charmonia. It contrasts with more sophisticated SDE-BSE calculations using momentum-dependent interactions, such as those by Jain and Munczek (1993), Blank (2011), and Rojas (2014), which naturally produce the high-momentum tails needed for correct decay constants. The results enable future application of the CI model to charmonium form factors, radiative transitions, and exotic states, while highlighting the need for parameter re-tuning when moving from light to heavy quark systems.
