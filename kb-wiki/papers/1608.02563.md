# Lattice methods and effective field theory

> Lattice field theory is a non-perturbative tool for studying properties of strongly interacting field theories, which is particularly amenable to numerical calculations and has quantifiable systematic errors. In these lectures we apply these techniques to nuclear Effective Field Theory (EFT), a non-relativistic theory for nuclei involving the nucleons as the basic degrees of freedom. The lattice formulation of [1,2] for so-called pionless EFT is discussed in detail, with portions of code included to aid the reader in code development. Systematic and statistical uncertainties of these methods are discussed at length, and extensions beyond pionless EFT are introduced in the final Section.

## Key claim

Lattice effective field theory provides a non-perturbative framework for nuclear physics with quantifiable systematic errors, but its practical utility depends on the choice of power-counting scheme and the fine-tuning of interactions near unitarity, as nuclear physics is not weakly coupled in all scattering channels.

## Method

The paper discretizes a non-relativistic pionless effective field theory on a square lattice with \(L^3 \times N_{\tau}\) points, using finite-difference operators for spatial and temporal derivatives and introducing auxiliary fields via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to render the four-fermion interaction bilinear in the fermion fields. The fermion fields are integrated out analytically, leaving a path integral over auxiliary fields that is evaluated using Monte Carlo importance sampling, with the coupling constants fixed by matching to the effective range expansion of two-body scattering data.

## Key numbers

* The number of Wick contractions for a nuclear correlation function grows with atomic number \(Z\) and mass number \(A\) as \((A+Z)!(2A-Z)!\); for He\(_4\) this corresponds to approximately \(5 \times 10^5\) terms.
* The stochastic signal-to-noise ratio \(\mathcal{R}\) degrades exponentially with nucleon number as \(\mathcal{R} \sim e^{-A(M - 3/2 m_{\pi})\tau}\), where \(M\) is the nucleon mass and \(m_{\pi}\) is the pion mass.
* The scattering lengths in nucleon-nucleon channels are \(a_{^1S_0} \sim -24\) fm and \(a_{^3S_1} \sim 5\) fm.
* The cutoff for chiral EFT is \(\Lambda_{\chi} \sim m_{\rho} \sim 700\) MeV.
* The two-body coupling \(g_0\) is related to the scattering length \(a\) and cutoff \(\Lambda\) by \(g_0 = \frac{4\pi}{M} \frac{1}{\Lambda - 1/a}\).

## Context

This lecture extends the lattice formulation of pionless EFT developed by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (KSW) and by Weinberg, addressing the challenge that nuclear physics contains multiple scales and that no consistent power-counting scheme with good convergence for all observables has yet been developed. The work enables first-principles calculations of few- and many-body nuclear systems using lattice methods, with the unitary regime (infinite scattering length) serving as a testbed for numerical methods, and points toward extensions beyond pionless EFT to include pion degrees of freedom.
