# Charge-dependent pair correlations relative to a third particle in $p$+Au and $d$+Au collisions at RHIC

> Quark interactions with topological gluon configurations can induce chirality imbalance and local parity violation in quantum chromodynamics. This can lead to electric charge separation along the strong magnetic field in relativistic heavy-ion collisions -- the chiral magnetic effect (CME). We report measurements by the STAR collaboration of a CME-sensitive observable in $p$+Au and $d$+Au collisions at 200 GeV, where the CME is not expected, using charge-dependent pair correlations relative to a third particle. We observe strong charge-dependent correlations similar to those measured in heavy-ion collisions. This bears important implications for the interpretation of the heavy-ion data.

## Key claim

The STAR collaboration reports that charge-dependent three-particle correlations in $p$+Au and $d$+Au collisions at 200 GeV, where the chiral magnetic effect (CME) is not expected, are similar in magnitude to those observed in peripheral Au+Au collisions, suggesting that background contributions could explain the heavy-ion data without invoking CME interpretation.

## Method

The STAR collaboration measured the three-point azimuthal correlator difference variable Δγ ≡ γ_OS − γ_SS, where γ = ⟨cos(φα + φβ − 2φc)⟩/v2,c, using charged particles in the Time Projection Chamber acceptance (|η| < 1) with transverse momentum from 0.2 to 2.0 GeV/c in minimum-bias p+Au (2015 data) and d+Au (2003 data) collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV. The elliptic flow v2,c was obtained via two-particle cumulants with various pseudorapidity gaps (0, 0.5, 1.0, 1.4) to gauge nonflow contamination, and the correlator was computed using the cumulant method without η gaps between the three particles.

## Key numbers

- Δγ values in p+Au and d+Au collisions are comparable to those in peripheral Au+Au collisions at similar multiplicities, with total systematic uncertainty of ∼9% in p+Au and ±9% in d+Au
- The charged particle multiplicity density dNch/dη systematic uncertainty is ∼15% in p+Au and ∼7% in d+Au
- Tracking efficiency is ∼93% in both p+Au and d+Au collisions, estimated via STAR standard embedding procedure
- The TOF detector acceptance effect in p+Au causes a −6% (single sided) systematic effect on Δγ
- The pT-dependent TPC tracking efficiency correction introduces ∼1% difference in Δγ

## Context

This work extends the study of CME-sensitive observables to small collision systems at RHIC energies, following similar measurements in p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC by CMS (2016) that challenged the CME interpretation of heavy-ion data. The results provide a possible alternative explanation for the Δγ measurements in Au+Au collisions at RHIC (Abelev 2009, Adamczyk 2013) without invoking CME interpretation, and enable quantitative comparison of background contributions between small and large systems through the scaled observable Δγ × dNch/dη / v2.
