# The Role of Mesons in the Electromagnetic Form Factors of the Nucleon

> The roles played by mesons in the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon are explored using as a basis a model containing vector mesons with coupling to the continuum together with the asymptotic $Q^2$ behavior of perturbative QCD. Specifically, the vector dominance model (GKex) developed by Lomon is employed, as it is known to be very successful in representing the existing high-quality data published to date. An analysis is made of the experimental uncertainties present when the differences between the GKex model and the data are expanded in orthonormal basis functions. A main motivation for the present study is to provide insight into how the various ingredients in this model yield the measured behavior, including discussions of when dipole form factors are to be expected or not, of which mesons are the major contributors, for instance, at low-$Q^2$ or large distances, and of what effects are predicted from coupling to the continuum. Such insights are first discussed in momentum space, followed by an analysis of how different and potentially useful information emerges when both the experimental and theoretical electric form factors are Fourier transformed to coordinate space. While these Fourier transforms should not be interpreted as "charge distributions", nevertheless the roles played by the various mesons, especially which are dominant at large or small distance scales, can be explored via such experiment--theory comparisons.

## Key claim

The paper uses the GKex vector meson dominance model (developed by Lomon) to explore how different mesonic contributions produce the measured nucleon electromagnetic form factors, but the Fourier transforms of the electric form factors to coordinate space should not be interpreted as actual charge distributions, only as a tool to examine which mesons dominate at different distance scales.

## Method

The authors employ the GKex (Gari-Krumpelmann extended) model, a vector meson dominance plus dispersion relation (VMD+DR) approach that includes vector meson poles (rho, omega, phi, rho-prime, omega-prime) with coupling to the pi-pi, pi-pi-pi, and K-Kbar continua, together with asymptotic behavior from perturbative QCD. They analyze experimental uncertainties by expanding differences between the GKex model and existing high-quality data in orthonormal basis functions, then Fourier transform both experimental and theoretical electric form factors to coordinate space to study the roles of individual mesons at different distance scales.

## Key numbers

- At Q^2 = 1 (GeV/c)^2, the ratio xi_p = G_E^p / (sqrt(tau) G_M^p) is approximately 0.6
- At Q^2 = 5 (GeV/c)^2, the ratio xi_p = G_E^p / (sqrt(tau) G_M^p) is approximately 0.1
- The rho-prime meson has a mass of 1.45 GeV and the omega-prime meson has a mass of 1.419 GeV
- The dipole-like Q^2-dependence for the nucleon's magnetic form factors holds for Q^2 less than 5 (GeV/c)^2

## Context

This work extends the VMD+DR modeling approach pioneered by Iachello, Jackson and Lande (1972) and later developed by Hohler and collaborators (1976), Gari and Krumpelmann (1992), and Lomon (2001-2006). It specifically uses the GKex model from Lomon (2006) as a reference to understand why G_M^p and G_M^n are roughly dipole in character while G_E^p falls faster than dipole. The analysis enables future comparisons with upcoming measurements at JLab, Novosibirsk, and DESY (OLYMPUS) that will use both electrons and positrons to resolve two-photon exchange contributions.
