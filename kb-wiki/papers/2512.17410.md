# Kinetic-Theory Bounds on the Equation of State of Dense QCD Matter

> We derive bounds on the equation of state of cold, dense matter by extending the causal, model-agnostic interpolation between chiral effective field theory and perturbative calculations with a microscopic constraint from relativistic kinetic theory. The additional condition restricts the stiffest admissible behavior of the equation of state and systematically reduces the range of allowed equations of state, with the strongest effect at high densities. The resulting bounds remain consistent with known low- and high-density limits, while the strength of the constraint depends on the density above which the kinetic-theory condition is applied. These bounds can be readily incorporated into future studies of cold, dense matter and used to assess the impact of microscopic stability conditions on equation-of-state inference.

## Key claim
The paper derives bounds on the equation of state of cold, dense QCD matter by adding a microscopic constraint from relativistic kinetic theory to the existing causal, model-agnostic interpolation between chiral effective field theory and perturbative QCD, but the strength of the constraint depends on the density above which the kinetic-theory condition is applied, treated as a free parameter.

## Method
The author extends the causal-integrable interpolation construction of Komoltsev and Kurkela (2021) by imposing the kinetic-theory bound on the speed of sound, which follows from positivity of the relaxation time in a causal Boltzmann equation. The onset density above which the kinetic-theory condition is enforced is treated as a free parameter, and the allowed region in the baryon density versus chemical potential plane is mapped to the energy density versus pressure plane. The construction uses thermodynamic consistency, stability, causality, and the kinetic-theory inequality to define upper and lower bounds on the equation of state.

## Key numbers
- The low-density chiral EFT constraint is fixed at chemical potential μ = 0.978 GeV, with pressure p = 3.542 MeV/fm³ and baryon density n = 0.176 fm⁻³.
- The high-density pQCD constraint is fixed at μ_H = 2.6 GeV, with pressures p = 2.334, 3.823, 4.284 GeV/fm³ and baryon densities n = 6.14, 6.47, 6.87 fm⁻³ for scale parameters X = 1, 2, 4 respectively.
- For a threshold density n_KT = 20 n_sat, the upper bound on density reduces significantly compared to the causal bound, while the lower bound remains unchanged because the lower causal bound at μ_H is approximately 3 n_sat, below the threshold.
- For n_KT ≈ 25 n_sat, the fraction of excluded area in the energy density-pressure plane reaches 10-23% as the scale parameter X varies between 1 and 4.
- The largest excluded area is 35% for n_KT = 1.1 n_sat with X = 4, though the author notes such a small threshold is probably unrealistic.

## Context
This work extends the model-agnostic interpolation framework of Komoltsev and Kurkela (2021) by incorporating a microscopic constraint from relativistic kinetic theory, which provides a stronger inequality for the speed of sound than the causality condition alone. The kinetic-theory bound has been examined in studies of dissipative hydrodynamics and applied phenomenologically to model neutron-star equations of state by Moustakidis (2016), Margaritis (2019), and Laskos-Patkos (2024). The results can be incorporated into future studies of cold, dense matter and used to assess the impact of microscopic stability conditions on equation-of-state inference, connecting microscopic QCD dynamics with astrophysical constraints from neutron-star observations.
