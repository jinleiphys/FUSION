# Chiral effective field theory beyond the power-counting regime

> Novel techniques are presented, which identify the chiral power-counting regime (PCR), and realize the existence of an intrinsic energy scale embedded in lattice QCD results that extend outside the PCR. The nucleon mass is considered as a benchmark for illustrating this new approach. Using finite-range regularization, an optimal regularization scale can be extracted from lattice simulation results by analyzing the renormalization of the low energy coefficients. The optimal scale allows a description of lattice simulation results that extend beyond the PCR by quantifying and thus handling any scheme-dependence. Preliminary results for the nucleon magnetic moment are also examined, and a consistent optimal regularization scale is obtained. This indicates the existence of an intrinsic scale corresponding to the finite size of the source of the pion cloud.

## Key claim

The paper presents a technique to identify an optimal regularization scale embedded in lattice QCD results, which allows chiral effective field theory extrapolations to describe data even outside the power-counting regime (PCR) by quantifying and handling scheme-dependence, rather than claiming the method works universally without qualification.

## Method

The authors use finite-range regularized chiral effective field theory with a dipole regulator function to analyze lattice QCD results for the nucleon mass and magnetic moment. They construct renormalization flow curves for the low energy coefficients (such as \(c_0\) and \(c_2\)) by varying the regularization scale \(\Lambda\) and sequentially including lattice data points at larger pion masses, extending beyond the PCR. A \(\chi^2\)-style analysis of the renormalization flow curves identifies the optimal regularization scale where the coefficients are least sensitive to truncation of the data.

## Key numbers

- The optimal regularization scale extracted from lattice results is approximately \(1.2\) GeV, consistent for both the nucleon mass and nucleon magnetic moment.
- The toy model pseudodata was created with a known intrinsic scale \(\Lambda_c = 1.0\) GeV, and the method successfully recovered this value.
- Lattice QCD data from CP-PACS (box size \(2.3-2.8\) fm) and PACS-CS (box size \(2.9\) fm) were used for the nucleon mass analysis; preliminary QCDSF data were used for the magnetic moment.

## Context

This work extends the finite-range regularization approach of Leinweber (2005) and Young (2009) by providing a method to identify the PCR directly from lattice data, rather than assuming its extent. The technique enables reliable chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD results at quark masses beyond the PCR, and the consistent optimal scale found for both the nucleon mass and magnetic moment suggests an intrinsic scale corresponding to the finite size of the pion cloud source. Future work could apply this method to other hadron observables and regulator functional forms.
