# Universal location of the Yang-Lee edge singularity in O(N) theories

> We determine a previously unknown universal quantity, the location of the Yang-Lee edge singularity for the O($N$) theories in a wide range of $N$ and various dimensions. At large $N$, we reproduce the $N\to\infty$ analytical result on the location of the singularity and, additionally, we obtain the mean-field result for the location in $d=4$ dimensions. In order to capture the nonperturbative physics for arbitrary $N$, $d$ and complex-valued external fields, we use the functional renormalization group approach.

## Key claim

The paper determines the previously unknown universal location of the Yang-Lee edge singularity for O(N) theories across a wide range of N and dimensions, using the functional renormalization group (FRG) approach. The authors note that their results are subject to systematic error from the LPA' truncation, with the disagreement for the critical exponent Delta relative to well-known results being about 0.9% for N=1 and 1.9% for N=2 in three dimensions.

## Method

The authors use the functional renormalization group (FRG) with a first-order derivative expansion (LPA') to compute the scale-dependent effective action for O(N) symmetric phi^4 theories. They extract the magnetic equation of state f_G(z) for complex external fields by solving the RG flow equations with a sixth-order Taylor expansion of the effective potential about its scale-dependent minimum. To locate the Yang-Lee edge singularity, they scan the complex external field plane at fixed reduced temperature, identify the peak of the magnetic susceptibility for small real parts of the field, and extrapolate to purely imaginary external fields using a quadratic fit.

## Key numbers

- For N=1 in three dimensions: |z_c| = 2.452 ± 0.025
- For N=2 in three dimensions: |z_c| = 2.032 ± 0.021
- For N=4 in three dimensions: |z_c| = 1.665 ± 10^{-3}
- Mean-field approximation (d=4) analytical result: |z_c^{MF}| ≈ 1.8899
- Large N limit in three dimensions analytical result: |z_c^{∞}| ≈ 1.6494
- The radius of convergence for QCD Taylor expansion is ≥ 420 MeV for O(4) universality class and ≥ 460 MeV for O(2) universality class

## Context

This work fills a gap in the understanding of O(N) universality classes, where almost all universal quantities were known except the location of the Yang-Lee edge singularity. Prior approaches such as the epsilon-expansion (near four dimensions) and lattice simulations were incapable of determining this quantity due to non-perturbative terms and sign problems respectively. The results enable improved parameterizations of the magnetic equation of state near the Ising model critical point (as used by Nonaka (2004) and Stephanov (2011)) and directly inform lattice QCD calculations of the equation of state at finite baryon density, where the edge singularity determines the radius of convergence of Taylor expansions about zero chemical potential (as studied by Bazavov (2017) and Borsanyi (2018)).
