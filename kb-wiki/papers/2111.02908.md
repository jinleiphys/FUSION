# Constraining the nucleon size with relativistic nuclear collisions

> The notion of the "size" of nucleons and their constituents plays a pivotal role in the current paradigm of the formation and the fluctuations of the quark-gluon plasma produced in high-energy nuclear collision experiments. We report on state-of-the-art hydrodynamic results showing that the correlation between anisotropic flow, $v_n^2$, and the mean transverse momentum of hadrons, $[p_t]$, possesses a unique sensitivity to the nucleon size in off-central heavy-ion collisions. We argue that existing experimental measurements of this observable support a picture where the relevant length scale characterizing the colliding nucleons is of order 0.5 fm or smaller, and we discuss the broad implications of this finding for future global Bayesian analyses aimed at extracting initial-state and medium properties from nucleus-nucleus collision data, including $v_n^2$-$[p_t]$ correlations. Determinations of the nucleon size in heavy-ion collisions will provide a solid independent constraint on the initial state of small system collisions, and will establish a deep connection between collective flow data in nucleus-nucleus experiments and data on deep inelastic scattering on protons and nuclei.

## Key claim

The correlation between anisotropic flow ($v_n^2$) and mean transverse momentum ($[p_t]$) in off-central heavy-ion collisions is uniquely sensitive to the nucleon size, and existing experimental data support a nucleon width of order 0.5 fm or smaller, contradicting recent global Bayesian analyses that return nucleon widths around 0.9–1.1 fm.

## Method

The authors performed hydrodynamic simulations of 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions using the TRENTo model for initial conditions (with entropy density proportional to the geometric mean of participant densities) and the full IP-Glasma+MUSIC+UrQMD framework including viscous relativistic hydrodynamics with shear and bulk viscosity. They computed the Pearson correlation coefficient $\rho(v_n^2,[p_t])$ for different nucleon widths $w$ (0.4, 0.8, 1.2 fm) and compared with preliminary ATLAS and ALICE data, as well as with calculations including sub-nucleonic hot spots.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon width $w = 0.4$ fm corresponds to a root mean square transverse radius $\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle} = \sqrt{2}w \approx 0.56$ fm, consistent with estimates from HERA diffractive $J/\Psi$ photoproduction.
- The Duke group's 2016 Bayesian analysis returned a MAP nucleon width around 0.45 fm, while their 2019 analysis returned MAP widths of about 0.96 fm.
- JETSCAPE analyses return nucleon widths $w = 0.9$–$1.1$ fm from Pb+Pb data.
- The IP-Glasma model uses a nucleon width of 0.4 fm with sub-nucleonic constituents of width $w_q = 0.11$ fm.
- For smooth nucleons with $w = 0.8$ fm or larger, the $\rho_n$ correlators show qualitative disagreement with experimental data, including negative signs not observed in ATLAS or ALICE measurements.

## Context

This work extends the line of research on initial-state fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, following the first global Bayesian analysis by the Duke group (Bernhard, 2016) and subsequent analyses (Bernhard, 2019; JETSCAPE, 2020) that returned inconsistent nucleon size estimates. The paper directly contradicts the large nucleon widths (0.9–1.1 fm) found in those Bayesian analyses, and instead supports the smaller nucleon size (≈0.5 fm) consistent with HERA data (Caldwell, 2010) and the IP-Glasma framework (Schenke, 2020). The finding enables future global Bayesian analyses to include $\rho_n$ observables for improved constraints on both initial-state properties and QGP transport coefficients, and establishes a connection between heavy-ion collision data and deep inelastic scattering experiments at the future Electron-Ion Collider.
