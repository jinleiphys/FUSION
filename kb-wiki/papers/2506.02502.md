# The Non-perturbative term for the Vector Form Factor of Pion Decay

> The vector form factor of the decay $π^{+} \rightarrow γ+ e^{+} + ν_e$ is calculated by the method for the pseudovector pion-nucleon system. The non-perturbative term is taken into account by using the parameter for the self-energy of nucleon following our previous calculation of the pion form factor. The suppression of the anomalous interaction of proton in the loop integral is significant to understand the experimental value.

# Vector Form Factor of Pion Decay: Non-perturbative Corrections

## Key claim
The vector form factor $F_V$ for the radiative pion decay $\pi^{+} \rightarrow \gamma + e^{+} + \nu_e$ is calculated including a non-perturbative term from the pseudovector pion-nucleon coupling, and the suppression of the anomalous magnetic interaction of the proton in the loop integral is required to bring the calculated value into agreement with experiment. The non-perturbative correction alone is insufficient; the anomalous interaction strength of the proton must be modified from $\kappa_p = 1.79$ to $\kappa_p^\prime \equiv \kappa_p - 2 + c$ using a parameter $c$ determined from low-energy pion-nucleon phase shifts.

## Method
The vector form factor $F_V$ is computed from a nucleon-loop diagram using the Ward-Takahashi identity for the photon-nucleon-nucleon vertex, with the vertex approximated as $\Gamma_\mu \approx \gamma_\mu - \kappa i \sigma_{\mu\nu} q^\nu / 2M$. The non-perturbative term is introduced by modifying the pion-nucleon-nucleon vertex using the nucleon self-energy $\Sigma(p)$, parameterized by a constant $c$ derived from the low-energy phase-shift analysis of elastic pion-nucleon scattering. The loop integrals are evaluated with a cutoff $\Lambda$ and the trace calculations are performed using standard gamma matrix identities, dropping terms of order $m^2/M^2$ and higher.

## Key numbers
- $F_V = 0.035$ for $\Lambda = M$ (twice the experimental value)
- Experimental value: $F_V^{\rm exp} = 0.017 \pm 0.008$ (PDG)
- With non-perturbative parameter $c = -0.39$: $F_V = 0.031$
- With modified proton anomalous interaction $\kappa_p^\prime$ and $c = -0.39$: $F_V = 0.010$
- Combined result $F_V^c + F_V^w = 0.0288$ (using conserved vector current hypothesis with $c = -0.39$)
- Predicted value from neutral pion decay under CVC hypothesis: $0.0263$ (Bryman)
- Solution for simultaneous fit to $F_V^{\rm exp}$ and $r_\pi^{\rm exp}$: $(c, \Lambda) = (-0.13, 0.97 M)$
- Pion charge radius with $c = 0$: $r_\pi = 1.06$ fm; with $c = -0.39$: $r_\pi = 0.95$ fm; with $\kappa_p^\prime$: $r_\pi = 0.567$ fm
- Experimental pion charge radius: $r_\pi^{\rm exp} = 0.672 \pm 0.008$ fm (PDG)
- Anomalous magnetic moments: $\kappa_p = 1.79$, $\kappa_n = -1.91$
- Weak magnetism strength: $\kappa_w \equiv \kappa_p - \kappa_n = 3.7$

## Context
This work extends the author's previous calculation of the pion form factor (Kinpara) by applying the same non-perturbative method to the vector form factor of radiative pion decay. The approach uses the pseudovector coupling for the pion-nucleon system and incorporates the nucleon self-energy to modify the vertex, contrasting with perturbative treatments that give smaller corrections when on-shell conditions are imposed. The results are compared with experimental data from the Particle Data Group (PDG) and with predictions from the conserved vector current hypothesis (Bryman). The method enables simultaneous determination of the non-perturbative parameter $c$ and the cutoff $\Lambda$ from experimental form factor and charge radius data, though the solution found ($c < 0$) contradicts the value $c = 2$ derived from the nucleon magnetic moment calculation, indicating that the exact form of the nucleon propagator or further off-shell vertex corrections are needed.
