# Ground-$γ$ band coupling in heavy deformed nuclei and SU(3) contraction limit

> We derive analytic expressions for the energies and $B(E2)$-transition probabilities in the states of the ground and $γ$ bands of heavy deformed nuclei within a collective Vector-Boson Model with SU(3) dynamical symmetry. On this basis we examine the analytic behavior of the SU(3) energy splitting and the B(E2) interband transition ratios in the SU(3) contraction limits of the model. The theoretical analyses outline physically reasonable ways in which the ground-$γ$ band coupling vanishes. The experimental data on the lowest collective states of even-even rare earth nuclei and actinides strongly support the theoretical results. They suggest that a transition from the ground-$γ$ band coupling scheme to a scheme in which the ground band is situated in a separate irreducible representation of SU(3) should be realized towards the midshell regions. We propose that generally the SU(3) group contraction process should play an important role for such a kind of transitions in any collective band coupling scheme in heavy deformed nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the ground-γ band coupling in heavy deformed nuclei vanishes in the SU(3) contraction limits (λ→∞ with μ finite, and λ=μ→∞), but the two limits produce qualitatively different behavior: the first gives infinite energy splitting and zero interband transitions, while the second gives a finite energy splitting ratio of 5.17 and finite interband transition ratios, suggesting that the SU(3) group contraction process provides a natural mechanism for the transition from coupled to decoupled band schemes toward midshell regions.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for energies and B(E2) transition probabilities of the 2_g and 2_γ states within the Vector-Boson Model (VBM) with SU(3) dynamical symmetry, using the Hamiltonian V = g₁L² + g₂L·Q·L + g₃A⁺A. They then examine the asymptotic behavior of the energy splitting ratio ΔE₂ and the interband transition ratios R₁(2) and R₂(2) in the two SU(3) contraction limits, and extend the analysis to all L≥2 states in the (λ,2) direction.

## Key numbers

- In the limit λ→∞ with μ finite and g₃<0: lim ΔE₂ = ∞ (complete splitting)
- In the limit λ=μ→∞ with g₃<0: lim ΔE₂ = 2/(5/√13 - 1) = 5.17 (finite splitting)
- In the limit λ→∞ with μ finite: lim R₁(2) = 0; lim R₂(2) = (10/7)[(μ+2)/(2μ)]², which for μ=2 gives 10/7 (standard Alaga value)
- In the limit λ=μ→∞: lim R₁(2) ≈ 0.172; lim R₂(2) ≈ 0.304
- In the (λ,2) direction with λ→∞: lim ΔE_L = ∞ for all even L; lim R₁(L) = 0; lim R₂(L) = 6(L-1)(2L+1)/[(L+1)(2L+3)]; lim R₃(L) = (L-1)/(L+2)

## Context

This work extends the Vector-Boson Model with SU(3) dynamical symmetry developed by the same authors (Minkov et al., 1997), which established systematic behavior of SU(3) symmetry properties in even-even deformed nuclei. The paper provides the analytic foundation for the previously observed numerical transition between coupled ground-γ band schemes and schemes where bands belong to different SU(3) irreducible representations. The SU(3) contraction mechanism connects to earlier work on group contraction by Gilmore and others, and the results enable interpretation of experimental data on rare earth nuclei and actinides as a transition from compact to non-compact dynamical symmetry groups.
