# Understanding neutrino pion production with the GiBUU model

> Pion production is a major source of systematic uncertainty in neutrino oscillation measurements. We report a systematic investigation of neutrino-induced pion production using MINERvA and MicroBooNE data within the GiBUU theoretical framework. The analysis begins by establishing baseline model parameters using inclusive and pionless data from MINERvA, MicroBooNE, and T2K experiments. We then examine the role of in-medium effects, including resonance broadening and nucleon-nucleon final-state interactions. While agreement with individual datasets can be achieved through specific model configurations, we demonstrate the difficulty of a unified description across all experiments: MINERvA measurements prefer minimum in-medium modifications, whereas MicroBooNE data require the maximum in-medium enhancement, revealing the complexity and richness of the underlying nuclear dynamics.

# Understanding neutrino pion production with the GiBUU model

## Key claim

While individual datasets from MINERvA and MicroBooNE can be described by specific model configurations within the GiBUU framework, a unified description across all experiments is not possible: MINERvA measurements prefer minimum in-medium modifications, whereas MicroBooNE data require maximum in-medium enhancement, revealing that the underlying nuclear dynamics are more complex than any single model configuration can capture.

## Method

The authors used the GiBUU (Giessen-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport-theory framework to simulate neutrino-nucleus interactions, incorporating quasielastic scattering, resonance excitations (up to 2 GeV), deep inelastic scattering, 2p2h (meson exchange current) contributions, and non-resonant 1π and 2π background terms derived from the Bosted-Christy parametrization of electron scattering data. They compared GiBUU predictions to inclusive, pionless (CC0π), and pion-production data from MINERvA (on CH at 2-50 GeV), MicroBooNE (on argon at energies up to about 2 GeV), and T2K (on hydrocarbon at 0.6 GeV peak flux), using transverse kinematic imbalance (TKI) observables and varying the isospin scaling parameter Tpar and in-medium modifications including Oset-type Delta resonance broadening and density-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross sections.

## Key numbers

- MINERvA inclusive cross-section data on CH between 2-50 GeV strongly favor T1b = 1 over T1b = 0 for the one-body isospin scaling parameter
- MicroBooNE inclusive data on argon favor T2p2h = 2 for the 2p2h isospin scaling parameter, consistent with the expected isospin value for argon
- For CC0π TKI data, T2K measurements favor T2p2h = 0 (χ² = 17 for δαT with 12 degrees of freedom), while MicroBooNE data clearly prefer T2p2h = 2 over T2p2h = 1
- The MINERvA CC0π TKI data show no clear preference between T2p2h = 0 and T2p2h = 1, with χ² values of 16 and 15 respectively for δαT (12 degrees of freedom)
- The binding energy for ¹²C used in the TKI formalism is 28.7 MeV
- The in-medium Delta suppression parameter α = 1.2 in the exponential factor σ_NN→NΔ(ρ_N) = σ_NN→NΔ(0) exp(-α ρ_N/ρ_0)
- The proton tracking threshold in existing experiments is above 0.3 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends previous GiBUU studies of pion production (Mosel 2023) that were restricted to semi-inclusive cross sections, and complements a detailed study by Bogart (2024) on medium effects in ⁴⁰Ar neutral-current interactions. The analysis demonstrates that the isospin scaling parameter Tpar, which connects electron and neutrino structure functions following the Walecka formalism, does not hold consistently across different energy regimes and target nuclei, and that the A-dependence of the 2p2h process is not well established. This work enables future tuning of in-medium effects and background contributions in neutrino event generators for DUNE, Hyper-Kamiokande, NOvA, and T2K oscillation measurements.
