# A prediction of $D^*$-multi-$ρ$ states

> We present a study of the many-body interaction between a $D^*$ and multi-$ρ$. We use an extrapolation to SU(4) of the hidden gauge formalism, which produced dynamically the resonances $f_2(1270)$ in the $ρρ$ interaction and $D^*_2(2460)$ in the $ρD^*$ interaction. Then let a third particle, $ρ$, $D^*$, or a resonance collide with them, evaluating the scattering amplitudes in terms of the Fixed Center Approximation of the Faddeev equations. We find several clear resonant structures above $2800\mev$ in the multibody scattering amplitudes. They would correspond to new charmed resonances, $D^*_3$, $D^*_4$, $D^*_5$ and $D^*_6$, which are not yet listed in the PDG, which would be analogous to the $ρ_3(1690)$, $f_4(2050)$, $ρ_5(2350)$, $f_6(2510)$ and $K^*_3(1780)$, $K^*_4(2045)$, $K^*_5(2380)$ described before as multi-$ρ$ and $K^*$-multi-$ρ$ states respectively.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the existence of new charmed resonances, \(D^*_3\), \(D^*_4\), \(D^*_5\), and \(D^*_6\), as molecular states composed of one \(D^*\) meson and an increasing number of \(\rho(770)\) mesons, but the authors acknowledge that the predicted masses and widths carry uncertainties because the resonant structures become wider as the number of \(\rho\) mesons increases, and some configurations (e.g., the six-body case) show less stable peaks.

## Method

The authors use the Fixed Center Approximation to the Faddeev equations to study multi-body scattering of a \(D^*\) meson with multiple \(\rho\) mesons. The two-body \(\rho\rho\) and \(\rho D^*\) interactions are first computed using the hidden gauge formalism extended to SU(4) and unitarized via the Bethe-Salpeter equation, dynamically generating the \(f_2(1270)\) and \(D^*_2(2460)\) resonances. Then, a third particle (\(\rho\), \(D^*\), or a resonance) is scattered off these two-body clusters, and the total scattering amplitude is evaluated iteratively for systems with up to six particles.

## Key numbers

- Three-body system: predicted \(D^*_3\) resonance mass about \(2800-2850\ \mathrm{MeV}\) with width about \(60-100\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Four-body system: predicted \(D^*_4\) resonance mass about \(3075-3200\ \mathrm{MeV}\) with width about \(200-400\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Five-body system: predicted \(D^*_5\) resonance mass about \(3360-3375\ \mathrm{MeV}\) with width about \(200-400\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Six-body system: predicted \(D^*_6\) resonance mass about \(3775\ \mathrm{MeV}\) with width about \(400\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The \(f_2(1270)\) cluster is generated with a cutoff \(\Lambda' = 875\ \mathrm{MeV}\); the \(D^*_2(2460)\) cluster uses \(\Lambda' = 1200\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the multi-\(\rho\) molecular approach of Roca (2010), which explained light meson resonances such as \(\rho_3(1690)\), \(f_4(2050)\), \(\rho_5(2350)\), and \(f_6(2510)\) as multi-\(\rho\) states, and the \(K^*\)-multi-\(\rho\) study of Yamagata (2010), which described \(K^*_2(1430)\) through \(K^*_6\) as molecules of one \(K^*(892)\) and multiple \(\rho\) mesons. The present paper predicts analogous charmed resonances not yet listed in the Particle Data Group, and suggests they could be searched for at future facilities such as FAIR.
