# Density functional theory and Kohn-Sham scheme for self-bound systems

> We demonstrate how the separation of the total energy of a self-bound system into a functional of the internal one-body Fermionic density and a function of an arbitrary wave vector describing the center-of-mass kinetic energy can be used to set-up an "internal" Kohn-Sham scheme.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a rigorous "internal" Kohn-Sham scheme can be formulated for self-bound systems (such as atomic nuclei) by separating the total energy into a functional of the internal one-body fermionic density and a function of an arbitrary wave vector describing the center-of-mass kinetic energy, provided one introduces an auxiliary translationally invariant potential that can be safely dropped to recover the isolated system.

## Method

The authors start from a translationally invariant N-body Hamiltonian and separate it into center-of-mass and internal parts using Jacobi coordinates. They introduce an auxiliary one-body potential in the center-of-mass frame that preserves translational invariance, then apply the standard Hohenberg-Kohn theorem to prove that the internal energy can be expressed as a unique functional of the internal density. From this, they derive internal Kohn-Sham equations for orbitals expressed in center-of-mass frame coordinates, where the exchange-correlation functional includes center-of-mass correlations that are absent in traditional electronic DFT.

## Key numbers

- The internal density ρ_int(r) is normalized to N (the number of particles), as shown in the derivation from Eq. (13).
- The internal kinetic energy operator involves reduced masses μ_α = m·α/(α+1) for each of the N-1 Jacobi coordinates.
- The center-of-mass kinetic energy is given by E_CM = ħ²K²/(2M) where M = Nm is the total mass and K is an arbitrary wave vector.
- The two-body internal density matrix γ_int(r,r') has normalization N(N-1)/2.

## Context

This work extends previous efforts by Engel (2007) and Barnea (2007) who demonstrated internal Hohenberg-Kohn theorems but did not achieve a rigorous internal Kohn-Sham scheme. It also contrasts with the approach of Giraud (2008) who added an oscillator potential to trap the center-of-mass, yielding a well-defined laboratory density but not an internal energy functional. The paper enables a formal foundation for nuclear density functional theory that is directly comparable to self-consistent mean-field calculations using effective interactions, while properly accounting for Galilean invariance.
