# The extended Lipkin model: proposal for implementation in a quantum platform and machine learning analysis of its phase diagram

> We investigate the Extended Lipkin Model (ELM), whose phase diagram mirrors that of the Interacting Boson Approximation model (IBA). Unlike the standard Lipkin model, the ELM (as the IBA) features both first- and second-order quantum shape phase transitions depending on the model parameters. Our goal is to implement the ELM on a quantum platform, leveraging Machine Learning techniques to identify its quantum phase transitions and critical lines. To achieve this, we offer: i) ground state energy calculations using a variational quantum eigensolver; ii) a detailed formulation for ELM dynamics within quantum computing, facilitating experimental exploration of the IBA phase diagram; and iii) a phase diagram determination using various Machine Learning methods. We successfully replicate the ELM ground-state energy using the Adaptive Derivative-Assembled Pseudo-Trotter ansatz Variational Quantum Eigensolver (ADAPT-VQE) algorithm across the entire phase space. Our framework ensures ELM implementation on quantum platforms with controlled errors. Lastly, our ML predictions yield a meaningful phase diagram for the model. Keywords: Quantum Platforms Nuclear Models ADAPT-VQE Quantum Shape Phase Transitions Interacting Boson Approximation Extended Lipkin Model Machine Learning

## Key claim

The Extended Lipkin Model (ELM) can serve as a practical surrogate for the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA) phase diagram on quantum platforms, but only for systems with a small number of particles; the authors demonstrate that the ADAPT-VQE algorithm reproduces the ground-state energy across the full phase space with controlled errors, and that Machine Learning methods yield a meaningful phase diagram, though finite-size effects remain important for small systems like N=6.

## Method

The authors first computed the ground-state energy of the ELM using the Adaptive Derivative-Assembled Pseudo-Trotter ansatz Variational Quantum Eigensolver (ADAPT-VQE) algorithm across the entire phase space defined by control parameters λ and α. They then formulated the ELM dynamics in terms of spin-1/2 Pauli operators suitable for qubit registers, enabling implementation on a digital-analog quantum platform with error estimation. Finally, they applied supervised and unsupervised Machine Learning techniques to classify the quantum phases and identify critical points from the numerical data.

## Key numbers

- For the standard Lipkin model (α=0), the second-order phase transition occurs at the critical point λ_c = 1/5 in the large-N limit.
- The critical line for the ELM is given by λ_c = 1/(5 + α²), separating spherical and deformed phases.
- The antispinodal line is constant at λ_as = 1/5, while the spinodal line is λ_s = (α² - √(α⁴ + 10α² + 16) + 6)/(α² + 10).
- All numerical ELM calculations were performed for N = 6 particles, with the Hilbert space dimension dim = N+1 = 7.
- For path 2 (α = 1/√2), the spinodal λ_s value is 0.17964.

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum shape phase transitions from the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA) to the simpler Extended Lipkin Model (ELM), which was proposed by Vida (2006) and Roma (2021) as a model that mimics the IBA phase diagram. The ELM is more straightforward to implement on quantum platforms than the IBA because it uses u(2) dynamical algebra rather than u(6). The ADAPT-VQE approach had previously been applied to the standard Lipkin model by Romero (2022), and the Agassi model had been implemented on a quantum platform by Perez-Fernandez (2022) with ML phase determination by Saiz (2022). This work enables experimental exploration of the IBA phase diagram on quantum computers for small particle numbers, and provides a test-bed for studying both first- and second-order quantum shape phase transitions in a controlled quantum setting.
