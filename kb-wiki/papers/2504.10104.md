# Automated next-to-leading order QCD and electroweak predictions of photon-photon processes in ultraperipheral collisions

> We present automated next-to-leading order QCD and/or electroweak (EW) predictions for photon-photon processes in ultraperipheral high-energy collisions of protons and ions, extending the capabilities of the MadGraph5_aMC@NLO framework together in combination with the gamma-UPC code. Key aspects of this extension are discussed. We compute QCD and/or EW quantum corrections for several phenomenologically interesting processes at LHC and FCC-hh energies.

## Key claim
The authors extend the MadGraph5_aMC@NLO framework, combined with the gamma-UPC code, to provide automated next-to-leading order (NLO) QCD and/or electroweak (EW) predictions for photon-photon processes in ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs) of protons and ions, but the automation is currently limited to processes without jets at NLO+PS accuracy and to NLO QCD matching for such cases.

## Method
The work implements collinear factorization and the equivalent photon approximation (EPA) to compute cross sections for photon-photon fusion in UPCs, using the gamma-UPC code for coherent photon fluxes (specifically the charge form factor, ChFF, flux). A hybrid alpha renormalization scheme is employed, where the fine-structure constant for long-distance (initial and tagged final) photons is taken as alpha(0) = 1/137.036, while short-distance couplings use the G_mu scheme. The FKS subtraction method is adapted to handle infrared divergences, with initial-state collinear singularities from gamma -> f fbar splitting regulated by a dedicated EW PDF counterterm that depends on the hadron radius R_A.

## Key numbers
* The fine-structure constant for long-distance photons is set to alpha(0) = 1/137.036.
* Fermi's constant G_mu = 1.16639 x 10^-5 GeV^-2 is used for the short-distance coupling scheme.
* The pole masses used for W and Z bosons are M_W = 80.3422 GeV and M_Z = 91.1539 GeV, with corresponding pole widths Gamma_W = 2.0843 GeV and Gamma_Z = 2.49457 GeV.
* The Higgs boson pole mass is M_h = 125.20 GeV with width Gamma_h = 0.00407 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the automated NLO capabilities of the MadGraph5_aMC@NLO framework (Alwall 2014, Frederix 2018) to photon-photon processes in UPCs, building on the earlier LO automation in gamma-UPC (Shao 2022). It enables systematic higher-order QCD and EW corrections for arbitrary gamma-gamma processes, moving beyond process-specific calculations (e.g., Shao 2024 for dimuon, Jiang 2024 for tau pairs). The framework allows for precision studies at the LHC and FCC-hh, including searches for beyond-Standard Model signatures and measurements of Standard Model parameters like the tau lepton anomalous magnetic moment.
