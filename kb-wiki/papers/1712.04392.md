# Aspects of Shape Coexistence in the Geometric Collective Model of Nuclei

> We examine the coexistence of spherical and $γ$-unstable deformed nuclear shapes, described by an SO(5)-invariant Bohr Hamiltonian, along the critical-line. Calculations are performed in the Algebraic Collective Model by introducing two separate bases, optimized to accommodate simultaneously different forms of dynamics. We demonstrate the need to modify the $β$-dependence of the moments of inertia, in order to obtain an adequate description of such shape-coexistence.

## Key claim

The standard Geometric Collective Model (GCM) with a sextic potential can produce coexisting spherical and γ-unstable deformed states, but only when the barrier is so high that the rotational and vibrational energy scales differ by more than two orders of magnitude, which is inconsistent with experimental patterns; this limitation can be resolved by modifying the β-dependence of the moments of inertia.

## Method

The authors diagonalize an SO(5)-invariant Bohr Hamiltonian with a γ-independent sextic potential v(β) = v₀β²(β² − β₀²)² using the Algebraic Collective Model (ACM). They employ two separate SU(1,1)×SO(5) bases with different parameters (aₛ,λₛ) and (a_d,λ_d) optimized for spherical and deformed states respectively, then introduce a modified kinetic term (1+αβ²)²/β² Λ̂² to adjust the β-dependence of the moments of inertia.

## Key numbers

- For the standard GCM with (β₀,v₀)=(3,3): E(2⁺₁)−E(0⁺₁)=0.45 (in units of ℏ²/2B=1)
- For the standard GCM with (β₀,v₀)=(3,3): E(0⁺_β)−E(0⁺₁) divided by E(2⁺₁)−E(0⁺₁)=131.06
- For the standard GCM with (β₀,v₀)=(3,3): E(0⁺_s)−E(0⁺₁) divided by E(2⁺₁)−E(0⁺₁)=98.90
- For the modified GCM with (β₀,v₀,α)=(3,3,1): spherical ground state has √⟨β²⟩=0.41
- For the modified GCM with (β₀,v₀,α)=(3,3,1): ground band L=0⁺₁ has √⟨β²⟩=2.97, β-band L=0⁺₃ has √⟨β²⟩=2.93
- Barrier height B_h = (4/27)v₀β₀⁶
- Potential stiffness ratio: v''(β=β₀)=4v''(β=0)=8v₀β₀⁴

## Context

This work extends the ACM, previously tested only for single-minimum potentials (ACM05, ACM), to potentials with multiple minima, and complements finite-N studies of shape coexistence in the Interacting Boson Model (LevGav17, 2017). The modified moment-of-inertia term is motivated by earlier GCM studies (Bona11, Panos14) and the classical limit of the IBM (Bona15). The results enable future application of the GCM to realistic shape-coexisting nuclei without the unrealistic energy-scale separation that plagues the standard irrotational-flow kinetic term.
