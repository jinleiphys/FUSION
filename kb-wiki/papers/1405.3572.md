# Continuous description of fluctuating eccentricities

> We consider the initial energy density in the transverse plane of a high energy nucleus-nucleus collision as a random field $ρ(\x)$, whose probability distribution $P[ρ]$, the only ingredient of the present description, encodes all possible sources of fluctuations. We argue that it is a local Gaussian, with a short-range 2-point function, and that the fluctuations relevant for the calculation of the eccentricities that drive the anisotropic flow have small relative amplitudes. In fact, this 2-point function, together with the average density, contains all the information needed to calculate the eccentricities and their variances, and we derive general model independent expressions for these quantities. The short wavelength fluctuations are shown to play no role in these calculations, except for a renormalization of the short range part of the 2-point function. As an illustration, we compare to a commonly used model of independent sources, and recover the known results of this model.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the initial energy density in the transverse plane of a high-energy nucleus-nucleus collision can be treated as a random field with a local Gaussian probability distribution and a short-range two-point function, and that the fluctuations relevant for calculating eccentricities have small relative amplitudes, meaning that short-wavelength fluctuations are irrelevant except for a renormalization of the short-range part of the two-point function.

## Method

The authors treat the initial energy density ρ(x) as a random field and characterize its probability distribution P[ρ] as a local Gaussian with a short-range two-point function. They derive general, model-independent expressions for the eccentricities ε_n and their variances in terms of the average density and the two-point function, assuming small fluctuations relative to the average density. They then compare their results to the independent-source model (a Glauber Monte Carlo approach) and recover known analytic formulas from that model.

## Key numbers

- The ratio characterizing fluctuation strength in the independent-source model is A / ∫_z ⟨ρ(z)⟩ = 1/N_s, where N_s is the total number of sources (Eq. 19 in the text).
- For a uniform density disk of unit radius with a surface correction A(r) = ρ(1 + α δ(r/R - 1)), the variances ⟨|ε_1|²⟩, ⟨|ε_2|²⟩, ⟨|ε_3|²⟩ are corrected by factors of 1, 1+3α, and 1+4α respectively.
- The smearing of the delta function in the two-point function by a Gaussian of width σ = 0.4 fm induces corrections of order [∫_s |s|² h(s)]/Σ, where Σ is the area of the collision zone, which are small for n ≤ 6.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for understanding initial-state fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions beyond the commonly used Glauber Monte Carlo calculations (Miller 2007, Alver 2008, Broniowski 2007) and sub-nucleonic fluctuation models (Schenke 2012, Dumitru 2012). It provides a field-theoretical description that is independent of the specific source model, enabling a cleaner interpretation of anisotropic flow measurements. The approach enables future work to connect eccentricity measurements directly to the two-point function of the initial energy density without needing to specify the microscopic fluctuation mechanisms.
