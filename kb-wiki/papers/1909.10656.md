# Cluster structures and monopole transitions of $^{14}$C

> Cluster structures of $^{14}$C were investigated with a method of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) combined with a $3Œ±+nn$ cluster model while focusing on the monopole excitations and linear-chain $3Œ±$ band. Variation after parity and angular momentum projections was performed in the AMD framework, and the generator coordinate method was applied to take into account various $3Œ±+nn$ cluster configurations. Energy spectra and monopole and $E2$ transition strengths of $0^+$, $2^+$, and $4^+$ states were calculated to assign band structures. The $0^+_3$ state with remarkable monopole transition was obtained as a vibrational mode of the triangle $3Œ±$ configuration. In addition, the linear-chain $3Œ±$ band from the band-head $0^+_4$ state was obtained. $^{10}$Be$+Œ±$ decay widths of $0^+$, $2^+$, and $4^+$ states were evaluated. $Œ±$ inelastic scattering off $^{14}$C was also investigated by the microscopic coupled-channel calculation with the $g$-matrix folding model to propose possible observation of the $0^+_3$ state via $Œ±$ scattering experiments.

## Key claim

The $0^+_3$ state in $^{14}$C, which exhibits a remarkable monopole transition strength, is identified as a vibrational mode of the triangle $3\alpha$ configuration rather than a linear-chain state, while the linear-chain $3\alpha$ band is built on the $0^+_4$ state, a result that depends on the inclusion of both AMD and $3\alpha+nn$ cluster configurations in the theoretical framework.

## Method

The authors combined antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with variation after parity and angular momentum projections (VAP) and the generator coordinate method (GCM) using $3\alpha+nn$ cluster wave functions, a framework they call "VAP+cl-GCM." Five AMD wave functions optimized for $^{14}$C($0^+_{1,2}$, $2^+_{1,2}$, $1^+_1$) were superposed with 306 $3\alpha+nn$ basis wave functions parameterized by the $\alpha$-$\alpha$ distance $d$, the third $\alpha$ position ($D_\alpha$, $\theta_\alpha$), and the neutron-pair orientation $\phi$. Energy spectra, monopole and $E2$ transition strengths, and $^{10}$Be$+\alpha$ decay widths were calculated, and $\alpha$ inelastic scattering cross sections were computed using the microscopic coupled-channel method with the Melbourne $g$-matrix folding model.

## Key numbers

- $0^+_3$ state monopole transition strengths: $B_p(E0) = 18$ fm$^4$ (proton) and $B_n(E0) = 15$ fm$^4$ (neutron) from the ground state
- $0^+_4$ state monopole transition strengths: $B_p(E0) = 1.0$ fm$^4$ and $B_n(E0) = 0.7$ fm$^4$ from the ground state
- $0^+_3$ state root-mean-square radii: $R_p = 2.98$ fm, $R_n = 2.98$ fm
- $0^+_4$ state root-mean-square radii: $R_p = 3.48$ fm, $R_n = 3.37$ fm
- $B(E2; 2^+_5 \to 0^+_4) = 162$ fm$^4$ (proton) and $B_n(E2) = 175$ fm$^4$ (neutron) for the linear-chain band
- Dimensionless reduced widths $\theta(a)^2$ for the linear-chain band at channel radius $a=5$ fm: 0.15 ($0^+$), 0.27 ($2^+$), and 0.10 ($4^+$)
- Monopole transition matrix elements for $0^+_3$: $M_p(E0) = 4.3$ fm$^2$ and $M_n(E0) = 3.9$ fm$^2$, comparable to $M_p(E0) = 5.48 \pm 0.22$ fm$^2$ for $^{12}$C($0^+_2$)

## Context

This work extends previous AMD studies of $^{14}$C by Suhara et al. (2010) and Baba et al. (2014, 2017), which identified the linear-chain state as the $0^+_3$ state but missed the low-lying monopole excitation because they did not fully include $3\alpha+nn$ cluster configurations. It also complements the $3\alpha+nn$ cluster model GCM calculation by Yoshida (2016), which described cluster features but could not properly reproduce low-energy shell-model spectra. The predicted strong monopole excitation to the $0^+_3$ state enables future $\alpha$ inelastic scattering experiments to observe this state, similar to how such experiments have been used for $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O cluster states by Kawabata et al. (2005) and Wakasa et al. (2007).
