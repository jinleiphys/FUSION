# Properties of nucleon in nuclear matter: once more

> We calculate the mass and residue of the nucleon in nuclear matter in the framework of QCD sum rules using the nucleon's interpolating current with an arbitrary mixing parameter. We evaluate the effects of the nuclear medium on these quantities and compare the obtained results with the existing theoretical predictions. The results are also compared with those obtained in vacuum to find the shifts in the quantities under consideration. Our calculations show that these shifts in the mass and residue are about $32\%$ and $15\%$, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the mass and residue of the nucleon in nuclear matter using QCD sum rules with an arbitrary mixing parameter in the interpolating current, finding shifts of about 32% and 15% respectively, but only within a restricted working region for the mixing parameter determined by the residue sum rule rather than the mass sum rule.

## Method

The authors computed the two-point correlation function for the nucleon in nuclear matter using QCD sum rules with an interpolating current containing an arbitrary mixing parameter β (where β = -1 corresponds to the Ioffe current). They evaluated the hadronic side by inserting a complete set of nucleon states and parametrizing the matrix element with a modified mass m_N* and residue λ_N*, then calculated the OPE side using light quark propagators in nuclear medium with quark, gluon, and mixed condensates expanded to linear order in nuclear density ρ_N. The sum rules were obtained by equating the two sides, applying Borel transformation and continuum subtraction, and the working region for β was determined from the residue sum rule (not the mass sum rule, which is a ratio) by requiring stability.

## Key numbers

- Mass shift in nuclear matter relative to vacuum: about 32%
- Residue shift in nuclear matter relative to vacuum: about 15%
- Borel mass squared working region: 0.8 GeV² ≤ M² ≤ 1.2 GeV²
- Continuum threshold working region: s₀ = (1.5 - 2.0) GeV²
- Nuclear matter saturation density used: ρ_N = (0.11)³ GeV³
- Vacuum quark condensate: ⟨q̄q⟩₀ = (-0.241)³ GeV³
- Vacuum gluon condensate: ⟨(α_s/π) G²⟩₀ = (0.33 ± 0.04)⁴ GeV⁴
- Sigma term: σ_N = 0.045 GeV
- m₀² = 0.8 GeV²

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of nucleon properties in nuclear matter, particularly the calculations by Cohen et al. (various years) who mostly used the Ioffe current (β = -1), and the work of Mallik (various years) on the residue as a function of nuclear density. The authors also extend the study by Nielsen (various years) on the nucleon mass by broadening the working region for the mixing parameter beyond the narrow range -1.15 ≤ β ≤ -0.95. The results are intended to enable calculations of electromagnetic properties and strong coupling constants of nucleons in nuclear matter using the same interpolating current, addressing the failure of the Ioffe current to reproduce some experimental data on electromagnetic form factors in vacuum.
