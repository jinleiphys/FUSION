# Nuclear astrophysics studies with ultra-peripheral heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

Ultra-peripheral heavy-ion collisions can be used to study nuclear astrophysics reactions via the Coulomb dissociation method, where virtual photons from the strong Coulomb field of a high-Z nucleus induce breakup reactions whose inverse (radiative capture) is relevant for astrophysics. The method relies on first-order perturbation theory being valid, but higher-order effects can be treated with semiclassical continuum discretized coupled-channels (S-CDCC) or eikonal CDCC approaches when perturbation theory breaks down.

## Method

The paper develops the theoretical framework for Coulomb excitation in ultra-peripheral collisions, starting from semiclassical coupled-channels equations and multipole expansions of the Coulomb interaction. The cross section for Coulomb excitation is expressed in terms of virtual photon numbers multiplied by photonuclear cross sections, where the virtual photon numbers depend on the projectile charge, bombarding energy, impact parameter, and multipolarity. The formalism is extended to include quantum scattering via eikonal wavefunctions and the eikonal CDCC method for treating higher-order effects in the breakup of weakly-bound nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Sommerfeld parameter condition for semiclassical approximation validity: η = Z_T Z_P e²/(mv) ≫ 1 for nucleus-nucleus collisions
- Adiabatic condition for low-energy collisions: ω a₀/v < 1, where a₀ = Z_P Z_T e²/(2E_cm)
- Adiabatic condition for high-energy collisions: ω b/(γ v) < 1, with γ = 1/√(1-v²/c²)
- Dipole energy-weighted sum rule: S[d_z] = (ℏ² e²/(2 m_N)) × (NZ/A)
- Total dipole photoabsorption cross section: σ_tot^γ = 2π² (e² ℏ/(m_N c)) × (ZN/A)
- E2 isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance sum rule: ∫ dE_γ σ_GQR^γ(E_γ)/E_γ² ≈ 0.22 Z A^{2/3} μb MeV⁻¹
- Giant dipole resonance width from hydrodynamical model: Γ = ℏ v̄/R, with v̄ = 3v_F/4, v_F = √(2E_F/m_N), E_F = 35 MeV, R = 6 fm, giving Γ ≈ 6 MeV
- Width for neutron-rich light nuclei: Γ ≈ 1 MeV, using σ_c ≈ 20 MeV/c for core fragment momentum distributions
- Maximum of single-particle response function: E₀^{EL} = (L + 1/2)S/(L + 3/2) ≈ S

## Context

This lecture note reviews and extends the theoretical framework for Coulomb dissociation, a method introduced by Baur, Bertulani, and Rebel (1986) for studying radiative capture reactions relevant to nuclear astrophysics. The most celebrated application is the ⁷Be(p,γ)⁸B reaction, which produces ⁸B in the sun and is important for solar neutrino physics. The work connects to earlier sum-rule approaches (Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn dipole sum rule) and hydrodynamical models (Myers 1977) for giant resonances, and enables future studies of pygmy resonances in neutron-rich nuclei and reactions involving halo nuclei using both semiclassical and quantum (eikonal CDCC) treatments.
