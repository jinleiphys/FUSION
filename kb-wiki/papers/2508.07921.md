# Antikaon absorption in the nuclear medium: the role of hadron self-energies and implications for kaonic atoms

> A systematic study of all relevant in-medium effects on the total $K^-$-nuclear potential is presented in this work. The $K^-N$ scattering amplitudes, including Pauli blocking effects and hadron self-energies (hyperons, nucleons, pions and kaons), are derived within a next-to-leading order chiral meson-baryon coupled-channel interaction model. These amplitudes are employed in a microscopic model of the $K^-$-nuclear potential in symmetric nuclear matter that includes one-, two- and, when the kaons and pions are dressed, also multinucleon absorption processes. The potential is then applied in calculations of the strong energy shifts and widths of 64 measured kaonic atom levels. The comparison of the results of the full model that includes Pauli correlations and hadron self-energies with data provides $χ^2 /d.p=1.5$, the lowest value obtained by a theoretical model to date and comparable with that of the best fitted phenomenological potentials. Furthermore, the calculated branching ratios for mesonic and non-mesonic absorption channels in kaonic carbon and kaonic neon are in good agreement with available data.

## Key claim

The full model that includes Pauli correlations, hadron self-energies (hyperons, nucleons, kaons, and pions), and multinucleon absorption processes achieves a χ²/d.p = 1.5 for 64 measured kaonic atom levels, the lowest value obtained by a theoretical model to date and comparable with the best fitted phenomenological potentials, but only when all these in-medium effects are included simultaneously.

## Method

The authors derived K⁻N scattering amplitudes within a next-to-leading order chiral meson-baryon coupled-channel interaction model (the BCN model), incorporating Pauli blocking effects and hadron self-energies for hyperons, nucleons, pions, and kaons. These in-medium amplitudes were employed in a microscopic model of the K⁻-nuclear potential in symmetric nuclear matter that includes one-nucleon (K⁻N → πY), two-nucleon (K⁻NN → NY), and, when kaons and pions are dressed, multinucleon absorption processes. The potential was then applied in calculations of strong energy shifts and widths for 64 kaonic atom levels across the periodic table, as well as branching ratios for mesonic and non-mesonic absorption channels in kaonic carbon and kaonic neon.

## Key numbers

- χ²/d.p = 1.5 for the full model (including Pauli correlations and hadron self-energies) when compared with 64 measured kaonic atom levels
- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ used as reference density for nuclear matter
- Nucleon potential V_N = -70(ρ/ρ₀) MeV, Λ potential V_Λ = -58(ρ/ρ₀) + 31(ρ/ρ₀)² MeV, Σ potential V_Σ = +30(ρ/ρ₀) MeV at saturation density
- Cut-off parameter Λ_C = 1200 MeV used for form factors at Yukawa vertices
- Pion decay constant f_π = 93 MeV used in meson-baryon-baryon coupling vertices

## Context

This work extends the microscopic model developed by Hrtanková et al. (2020) that included K⁻N and K⁻NN absorption but lacked proper hadron self-energies, and the earlier self-consistent calculations by Ramos and Oset (2000) that used leading-order chiral interactions. The present study achieves for the first time a χ²/d.p comparable to the best phenomenological potentials (Friedman and Gal, 2007; Yamagata-Sekihara et al., 2025) without introducing phenomenological multinucleon terms, enabling more reliable predictions for kaonic atoms and absorption processes in nuclear matter.
