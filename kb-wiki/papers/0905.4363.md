# Polarizabilities of the nucleon and spin dependent photo-absorption

> The polarizabilities $α$ (electric), $β$ (magnetic) and $γ_π$ (backward spin) of the nucleon are investigated in terms of degrees of freedom of the nucleon using recent results for the CGLN amplitudes and resonance couplings $A_{1/2}$ and $A_{3/2}$. The photon excitation strengths of the excited states are given in terms of partial integrated photoabsorption cross sections and resonant contributions of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule. As a test of the present predictions, cross section differences $(σ_{3/2}-σ_{1/2})$ of the excited states are compared with data recently measured at MAMI (Mainz) and ELSA (Bonn), In order to explain differences between proton and neutron, radiative widths of the excited states are compared with nonrelativistic and relativistic predictions based on the SU(6) harmonic oscillator (HO) quark model, A complete list of partial contributions from the s-channel and the $t$-channel are given for the polarizabilities.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the electric, magnetic, and backward spin polarizabilities of the nucleon can be understood in terms of the degrees of freedom of the nucleon using recent CGLN amplitudes and resonance couplings, but the predictions depend on the assumption that the inelasticity correction (from 2π and η channels) is independent of helicity, and that the no-subtraction hypothesis for the GDH sum rule holds.

## Method

The author uses the Walker parameterization of nucleon resonant cross sections combined with CGLN multipole amplitudes and resonance couplings A1/2 and A3/2 from recent MAID analyses to compute helicity-dependent photoabsorption cross sections. Partial integrated photoabsorption cross sections and resonant contributions to the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule are calculated for each nucleon resonance in the first three oscillator shells. Radiative widths from the nonrelativistic SU(6) harmonic oscillator quark model and two relativized quark models are compared with experimental values extracted from resonance couplings.

## Key numbers

- Integrated photoabsorption cross section for P33(1232): 80.0 × 10^3 μb MeV for both proton and neutron
- GDH integral contribution from P33(1232): +291 ± 15 μb for both proton and neutron
- GDH integral contribution from D13(1520): +77.5 ± 7.8 μb (proton), +40.2 ± 6.5 μb (neutron)
- GDH integral contribution from F15(1680): +35.0 ± 3.5 μb (proton), +1.5 ± 0.6 μb (neutron)
- Total resonant GDH sum: 374 ± 20 μb (proton), 336 ± 18 μb (neutron)
- Total GDH integral including nonresonant and Regge contributions: 216 ± 20 μb (proton), 231 ± 18 μb (neutron)
- Experimental radiative width of P33(1232): 0.69 ± 0.02 MeV for both proton and neutron
- Experimental radiative width of D13(1520): 0.61 ± 0.04 MeV (proton), 0.49 ± 0.07 MeV (neutron)
- Experimental radiative width of D15(1675): 0.011 ± 0.008 MeV (proton), 0.10 ± 0.04 MeV (neutron)
- Experimental radiative width of F15(1680): 0.36 ± 0.06 MeV (proton), 0.04 ± 0.02 MeV (neutron)

## Context

This work extends the author's systematic series of studies (Schumacher 2006, 2007a, 2007b, 2008) on the electromagnetic structure of the nucleon, following experimental work on Compton scattering and a comprehensive review (Schumacher 2005). It uses recent precise helicity-dependent cross section measurements from MAMI (Mainz) and ELSA (Bonn) that tested the GDH sum rule, as reviewed by Helbing (2006). The improved MAID analyses of CGLN amplitudes (Drechsel 2007) enable better predictions of spin-dependent cross sections and polarizabilities, while the quark model comparisons with Faiman and Hendry (1968), Copley et al. (1969), Koniuk and Isgur (1980), Close and Li (1990), and Capstick (1992) help explain proton-neutron differences in photoexcitation strengths, including the Moorhouse selection rule for the D15(1675) resonance.
