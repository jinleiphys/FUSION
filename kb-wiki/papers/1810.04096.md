# Productions of $f_{1}(1420)$ in pion and kaon induced reactions

> The $f_{1}(1420)$ productions via pion and kaon induced reactions on a proton target are investigated in an effective Lagrangian approach. Two treatments of the $t$-channel Born term, the Feynman model and the Regge model, are introduced to calculate the total and differential cross sections of the $π^{-}p\rightarrow f_{1}(1420)n$ and $K^{-}p\rightarrow f_{1}(1420)Λ$ reactions. The numerical results indicate that the experimental data of the total cross section of the $π^{-}p\rightarrow f_{1}(1420)n$ reaction can be reproduced in both the Feynman and the Regge models. Based on the parameters determined in the pion induced reaction, the cross sections of the $K^{-}p\rightarrow f_{1}(1420)Λ$ reaction, about which there is little data found in the literature, are predicted in the same beam momentum range. It is found that the line shapes of the total cross section obtained in a kaon induced reaction with two treatments are quite different. The cross sections for both reactions are at an order of magnitude of $μ$b, or larger, at a beam momenta up to 10 GeV/c. The differential cross sections for both pion and kaon induced reactions are also present. It is found that in the Regge model, the $t$ channel provides a sharp increase at extreme forward angles. The results suggest that the experimental study of the $f_1(1420)$ in the kaon induced reaction on a proton target is as promising as in the pion induced reaction. Such an experimental measurement is also very helpful to clarify the production mechanism of the $f_1(1420)$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the experimental data for the total cross section of the pion induced reaction $\pi^{-}p\rightarrow f_{1}(1420)n$ can be reproduced in both the Feynman and Regge models, but only under the assumption that the $f_{1}(1420)$ is a genuine resonance; the coupling constants are determined with this assumption, and the work does not rule out alternative interpretations such as the $f_{1}(1420)$ being a reflection of $f_{1}(1285)$ decay modes.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach with $t$-channel exchange only: $a_{0}(980)$ exchange for the pion induced reaction and $K^{\ast}$ exchange for the kaon induced reaction. They applied two treatments of the $t$-channel Born term: the standard Feynman propagator model and a Reggeized model where the Feynman propagator is replaced by a Regge propagator with linear trajectories. The cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ in the form factor was fitted to 4 experimental data points of the total cross section for the pion induced reaction, and the same coupling constants were then used to predict cross sections for the kaon induced reaction.

## Key numbers

- The $f_{1}(1420)$ has a suggested mass of $1426.4 \pm 0.9$ MeV and a suggested width of $54.9 \pm 2.6$ MeV (from PDG).
- The branching fraction of $f_{1}(1420)$ decay to $a_{0}\pi$ is $4\%$, giving $\Gamma_{f_{1}\rightarrow a_{0}\pi} \simeq 2.2$ MeV and a coupling constant $g_{f_{1}a_{0}\pi} \simeq 2.72$.
- The branching fraction of $f_{1}(1420)$ decay to $K^{\ast}K$ is $96\%$, giving $\Gamma_{f_{1}\rightarrow K^{\ast}K} \simeq 52.7$ MeV and $g_{f_{1}K^{\ast}K}/m_{f_{1}} \simeq 8.36$.
- For the $\pi^{-}p\rightarrow f_{1}(1420)n$ reaction, the fitted cutoff is $\Lambda = 1.18 \pm 0.01$ GeV (Feynman model, $\chi^{2}/dof = 1.68$) and $\Lambda = 1.60 \pm 0.03$ GeV (Regge model, $\chi^{2}/dof = 1.93$).
- At $p_{Lab} = 32.5$ GeV/c, the predicted total cross section for $\pi^{-}p\rightarrow f_{1}(1420)n$ is $\sigma \simeq 0.017$ $\mu$b (Regge model with $\Lambda = 1.6$ GeV), and for $K^{-}p\rightarrow f_{1}(1420)\Lambda$ it is about $0.14$ $\mu$b (Regge model with same cutoff).
- The total cross sections for both reactions are at an order of magnitude of $\mu$b, or larger, at beam momenta up to 10 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the effective Lagrangian approach previously applied to $f_{1}(1285)$ production by Wang (2017) to the $f_{1}(1420)$ meson, using the same $a_{0}$ exchange mechanism for pion induced reactions. It addresses the unresolved internal structure of the $f_{1}(1420)$, which has been suggested by Debastiani (2016) to be a reflection of $f_{1}(1285)$ decay modes rather than a genuine resonance. The predictions for kaon induced reactions provide guidance for future experiments at J-PARC, COMPASS, and OKA@U-70, where high-precision data could clarify the production mechanism and help distinguish between the $s\bar{s}$ interpretation and the decay-reflection interpretation.
