# Masses of Multiquark Droplets

> The mass formulae for finite lumps of strange quark matter with $u$, $d$ and $s$ quarks, and non-strange quark matter consisting of $u$ and $d$ quarks are derived in a non-relativistic potential model. The finite-size effects comprising the surface, curvature and even, the Gauss curvature were consistently obtained, which shows a converging trend. It is found that there is a possibility for the formation of metastable strangelets of large mass. The model predicts low charge to mass ratio as the characteristic signature of strange matter in agreement with the relativistic studies. This study also yields an independent estimate for the bag energy density $B$, which is in agreement with the M.I.T bag model value.

# Masses of Multiquark Droplets

## Key claim

The authors derive mass formulae for finite lumps of strange and non-strange quark matter using a non-relativistic potential model, finding that metastable strangelets of very large mass may form, but only if the system is driven toward a state of greater strangeness where it becomes stable against cascade and omega decay; the paper hedges that strangelets remain unstable against nucleon and lambda decay even at the optimum strangeness fraction.

## Method

The authors model a finite number of non-interacting quarks confined in an infinite square well potential, using the Hill-Wheeler density of states to enumerate quantum states. They derive expressions for kinetic energy including volume, surface, curvature, and Gauss curvature contributions, then add a bag term (analogous to the MIT bag model), center-of-mass correction, Coulomb energy, and rest mass energy. The bag energy density \(B\) is eliminated using the pressure balance (stability) condition, yielding mass formulae for two-flavor (udilet) and three-flavor (strangelet) systems in a Bethe-Weizsacker-like form.

## Key numbers

- Bag energy density \(B\) is estimated as 83 \(MeV fm^{-3}\), lying within the relativistic range of 58 \(MeV fm^{-3}\) to 86 \(MeV fm^{-3}\).
- Energy per baryon for strange quark matter is about 1275 \(MeV\); for non-strange quark matter (u and d quarks) it is about 1320 \(MeV\).
- The curvature energy coefficient for the massless case from Madsen (1993) is cited as about 435 \(MeV\).
- The mass formulae are considered appropriate for baryon number \(A > 50\).
- The optimum strangeness fraction \(f_s\) for strangelet stability is about 0.7.
- The minimum mass difference between strangelets and udilets is about 4 \(MeV\) for baryon number \(A=400\).
- Nuclear matter density \(\rho_{nuc}\) is taken as 0.153 \(fm^{-3}\); the baryon number density \(\rho_o\) is assumed to be about 1.3 \(\rho_{nuc}\).
- Quark masses used: \(m_u = m_d = 300\) \(MeV\), \(m_s = 400\) \(MeV\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Fermi gas studies of strange quark matter by Farhi and Jaffe (1984) and the curvature energy analysis of Madsen (1993) by developing a non-relativistic potential model that consistently includes surface, curvature, and Gauss curvature terms. The authors note that Berger and Jaffe (1987) previously calculated surface tension coefficients but dropped quark-quark interactions, and that Madsen (1993) found curvature energy could be decisive for low-mass strangelet stability. The model enables an independent estimate of the bag energy density \(B\) and predicts a low charge-to-mass ratio as a signature of strange matter, corroborating the relativistic findings of Farhi and Jaffe (1984).
