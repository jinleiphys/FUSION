# Possible S-wave Dibaryons in SU(3) Chiral Quark Model

> In the framework of the SU(3) chiral quark model, the $S-$wave baryon-baryon bound states are investigated. It is found that according to the symmetry character of the system and the contributions from chiral fields, there are three types of bound states. The states of the first type, such as $[ΩΩ]_{(0,0)}$ and $[Ξ^{*}Ω]_{(0,1/2)}$ are deeply bound dibaryon with narrow widths. The second type states, $[Σ^{*} Δ]_{(0,5/2)}$,$[Σ^{*} Δ]_{(3,1/2)}$, $[ΔΔ]_{(0,3)}$ and $[ΔΔ]_{(3,0)}$ are also bound states, but with broad widths. $[ΞΩ- Ξ^{*}Ω]_{(1,1/2)}$, $[ΞΞ]_{(0,1)}$, and $[N Ω]_{(2,1/2)}$ are third type states. They, like {\em d}, are weakly bound only if the chiral fields can provide attraction between baryons.

## Key claim
The SU(3) chiral quark model predicts three types of S-wave dibaryon bound states, but the existence and binding strength of the third type (such as [ΞΩ−Ξ*Ω]_{(1,1/2)}, [ΞΞ]_{(0,1)}, and [NΩ]_{(2,1/2)}) depend critically on whether the chiral fields provide sufficient attraction between baryons, making them only weakly bound under favorable conditions.

## Method
The authors used the Resonating Group Method (RGM) to solve the six-quark bound state problem within the SU(3) chiral quark model, where short-range QCD effects are treated by one-gluon-exchange and nonperturbative effects by chiral-quark field interactions. They calculated binding energies and root-mean-square radii for various baryon-baryon systems across three model variants: the full SU(3) chiral quark model (Model I), an extended SU(2) model without strange mesons (Model II), and a minimal SU(2) model with only π and σ mesons (Model III). The symmetry character of each system was classified by the expectation value of the antisymmetrizer in spin-flavor-color space, ⟨𝒜^{sfc}⟩.

## Key numbers
- [ΩΩ]_{(0,0)}: binding energy 113.8 MeV, RMS radius 0.66 fm (Model I, Set I)
- [Ξ*Ω]_{(0,1/2)}: binding energy 92.4 MeV, RMS radius 0.71 fm (Model I, Set I)
- [Σ*Δ]_{(0,5/2)}: binding energy 24.6 MeV, RMS radius 0.99 fm (Model I, Set I)
- [Σ*Δ]_{(3,1/2)}: binding energy 25.9 MeV, RMS radius 0.95 fm (Model I, Set I)
- [ΔΔ]_{(0,3)}: binding energy 16.0 MeV, RMS radius 1.10 fm (Model I, Set I)
- [ΔΔ]_{(3,0)} (d*): binding energy 22.2 MeV, RMS radius 1.01 fm (Model I, Set I)
- [ΞΩ−Ξ*Ω]_{(1,1/2)}: binding energy 32.9 MeV, RMS radius 0.78 fm (Model I, Set I)
- [ΞΞ]_{(0,1)}: binding energy 4.1 MeV, RMS radius 1.17 fm (Model I, Set I)
- [NΩ]_{(2,1/2)}: binding energy 3.5 MeV, RMS radius 1.18 fm (Model I, Set I)

## Context
This work extends the systematic search for dibaryons beyond the well-studied H particle (predicted by Jaffe), d* (studied by Wang and others), and d' (studied by Wagenbrunn and others), focusing on multi-strangeness systems. The authors classify dibaryon states by their symmetry character under the Pauli principle, connecting to earlier studies of ΩΩ and Ξ*Ω systems by Yu, Zhang, and Li. The results enable experimental searches for deeply bound narrow-width dibaryons in the first type (ΩΩ and Ξ*Ω) and provide a framework for testing chiral field contributions through comparison of SU(3) and SU(2) model predictions.
