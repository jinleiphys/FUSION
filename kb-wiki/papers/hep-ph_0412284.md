# Nuclear modification difference between u_v and d_v distributions and its relation to NuTeV sin^2 theta_W anomaly

> We investigate a possible nuclear correction to the NuTeV measurement of the weak-mixing angle sin^2 theta_W. In particular, a nuclear modification difference between u_v and d_v distributions contributes to the NuTeV measurement with the iron target. First, the modification differenceis determined by a chi^2 analysis so as to reproduce nuclear data on the structure function F_2 and Drell-Yan processes. Then, taking the NuTeV kinematics into account, we calculate a contribution to the sin^2 theta_W determination. In addition, its uncertainty is estimated by the Hessian method. Although the uncertainty becomes comparable to the NuTeV deviation, the effect is not large enough to explain the whole NuTeV sin^2 theta_W anomaly at this stage. However, it is difficult to determine such a nuclear modification difference, so that we need further investigations on the difference and its effect on the NuTeV anomaly.

## Key claim

The nuclear modification difference between up and down valence quark distributions in iron contributes to the NuTeV weak-mixing angle measurement, but the effect (0.0004 ± 0.0015) is not large enough to explain the full NuTeV anomaly of 0.0050, although the uncertainty is comparable to the deviation.

## Method

The authors performed a chi-squared analysis of 951 data points from structure function ratios F2^A/F2^A' (from EMC, SLAC, BCDMS, NMC, E665, and HERMES) and Drell-Yan cross-section ratios (from E772 and E866/NuSea) to determine the difference between nuclear modifications of up and down valence quark distributions. The modification difference was parameterized with four parameters at Q^2=1 GeV^2, with one parameter fixed by baryon-number or charge conservation, and the remaining three parameters were optimized using MINUIT. The uncertainty was estimated using the Hessian method with Delta chi^2=3.527 for three parameters.

## Key numbers

- The NuTeV value of sin^2 theta_W is 0.2277 ± 0.0013 (stat) ± 0.0009 (syst), compared to the average of other data at 0.2227 ± 0.0004
- The chi-squared analysis yielded chi^2_min/d.o.f. = 1.57
- The correction to sin^2 theta_W from the nuclear modification difference at Q^2=20 GeV^2 is Delta(sin^2 theta_W) = 0.0004 ± 0.0015
- The NuTeV deviation from the standard model is 0.0050
- The parameters obtained: b_v' = -0.0905 ± 0.4044, c_v' = 0.239 ± 1.300, d_v' = -0.193 ± 1.126
- The fixed parameter a_v' ranges from 0.00612 (deuteron) to 0.00615 (gold)

## Context

This work extends the nuclear correction study of the NuTeV anomaly by Kumano (2004) which proposed simple estimates based on baryon-number and charge conservation, by instead performing a global chi-squared analysis of nuclear data to determine the valence-quark modification difference. The result is smaller than needed to explain the NuTeV anomaly, unlike the earlier model estimates. The analysis enables future experimental searches at facilities such as MINERvA and neutrino factories to determine the modification difference more precisely, and the authors note that isospin-violation effects studied by other groups are related to the same epsilon_v function investigated here.
