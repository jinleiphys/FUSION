# Interplay of quark and meson degrees of freedom in near-threshold states: A practical parametrisation for line shapes

> We propose a practical parametrisation for the line shapes of near-threshold states compatible with all requirements of unitarity and analyticity. The coupled-channel system underlying the proposed parametrisation includes bare poles and an arbitrary number of elastic and inelastic channels treated fully nonperturbatively. The resulting formulae are general enough to be used for a simultaneous analysis of the data in all available production and decay channels of the (system of) state(s) under consideration for a quite wide class of reactions. As an example, we fit the experimental data currently available for several decay channels for the charged $Z_b^{(\prime)}$ states in the spectrum of bottomonia and find a good overall description of the data. We find the present data to be consistent with the $Z_b(10610)$ as a virtual state and with the $Z_b(10650)$ as a resonance, both residing very close to the $B\bar{B}^*$ and $B^*\bar{B}^*$ threshold, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a practical parametrisation for the line shapes of near-threshold states that satisfies unitarity and analyticity, and finds that the present data are consistent with the Zb(10610) as a virtual state and the Zb(10650) as a resonance, both residing very close to the B Bbar* and B* Bbar* thresholds respectively, but this interpretation depends on the specific fit and the assumption that the direct interactions in the hidden-flavour channels are negligible.

## Method

The authors solve a coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation for a system that includes bare poles, an arbitrary number of elastic (open-flavour) channels, and an arbitrary number of inelastic (hidden-flavour) channels, treating all channels fully nonperturbatively. They assume the direct interactions in the inelastic channels vanish (justified by OZI suppression) and use a separable form for the transition form factors between open- and hidden-flavour channels. The solution is obtained by successive application of the two-potential formalism, reducing the problem to inversion of matrices of dimension at most (Ne x Ne) and (Np x Np) independently of the number of inelastic channels. The formalism is applied to fit experimental data for the charged Zb and Zb' states in bottomonium decays.

## Key numbers

- The Zb(10610) is found to be consistent with a virtual state near the B Bbar* threshold at 10604 MeV; the Zb(10650) is found to be consistent with a resonance near the B* Bbar* threshold at 10650 MeV.
- The pion-heavy-quarkonium scattering lengths are estimated to be less than or equal to 0.02 fm, justifying the neglect of direct interactions in hidden-flavour channels.
- The differential decay rate formula used is dBr_x/dM = |M_x|^2 p_3 k_x / (32 pi^3 M_tot^2 Gamma_tot), where p_3 and k_x are momenta in the respective rest frames.

## Context

This work extends earlier coupled-channel treatments of near-threshold states, such as the Flatte parametrisation (Flatte 1976) and the generalisation by Baru et al. (2010) that accounts for direct interactions in the hadronic channel generating near-threshold poles. It also builds on studies of the X(3872) and Zb states by Hanhart et al. (2007), Kalashnikova et al. (2009), and Braaten et al. (2007). The formalism enables simultaneous analysis of data across multiple production and decay channels, and provides a diagnostic tool: if a high-quality fit requires complex parameters, the model is incomplete and additional channels must be included.
