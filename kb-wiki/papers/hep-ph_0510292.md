# A Weibel Instability in the Melting Color Glass Condensate

> Based on hep-ph/0510121, we discuss further the numerical study of classical SU(2) 3+1-D Yang-Mills equations for matter produced in a high energy heavy ion collision. The growth of the amplitude of fluctuations as $\exp{(Γ\sqrt{g^2μτ})}$ (where $g^2μ$ is a scale arising from the saturation of gluons in the nuclear wavefunction) is shown to be robust over a wide range of initial amplitudes that violate boost invariance. We argue that this growth is due to a non-Abelian Weibel instability, the scale of which is set by a dynamically generated plasmon mass. We find good agreement when we relate $Γ$ to the prediction from kinetic theory.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that rapidity fluctuations in the classical Yang-Mills fields produced in high-energy heavy ion collisions grow as exp(Γ√(g²μτ)), and argues this growth is due to a non-Abelian Weibel instability whose scale is set by a dynamically generated plasmon mass. The authors qualify that the growth rate Γ extracted from their simulations shows good agreement with kinetic theory predictions only up to a factor of 2, which requires further study.

## Method
The authors numerically solved classical SU(2) 3+1-D Yang-Mills equations in (τ,η,x⊥) coordinates, starting from boost-invariant initial conditions from the McLerran-Venugopalan model with small random rapidity fluctuations of amplitude Δ added. They evolved the system using a leap-frog algorithm on a lattice with N⊥=Nη=32 sites, transverse size g²μL=22.6, 67.9, or 90.5, and longitudinal extent Lη=1.6, starting at τinit=0.05 a⊥. They tracked the maximum Fourier mode amplitude of τ²Tηη as a function of proper time and fit it to an exponential of √τ.

## Key numbers
- Growth rate Γfit = 0.502 ± 0.01 for g²μL = 22.6, from a best fit to exp(Γfit τ^0.5) over the range g²μτ ≈ 150 to ~1000.
- Γtheory = 0.526 ± 0.003 for g²μL = 22.6, derived from the measured plasmon mass via ω(k⊥=0) = κ0 √(g²μ/τ) with κ0 giving m∞ = κ0 √(3g²μ/(2τ)).
- For g²μL = 67.9: Γfit = 0.427 ± 0.01, Γtheory = 0.447 ± 0.003.
- For g²μL = 90.5: Γfit = 0.46 ± 0.04, Γtheory = 0.49 ± 0.004.
- Initial seed amplitude Δ ≈ 10⁻⁷ (compared to Δ ≈ 10⁻¹¹ in prior work), with the growth rate consistent across these different seed sizes.
- The instability growth begins at g²μτ ≈ 150 and shows flattening onset at g²μτ ≈ 1000 due to non-Abelian self-interactions.

## Context
This work extends the classical Yang-Mills simulations of Krasnitz and Venugopalan (2001) by including rapidity fluctuations that break boost invariance, and connects to predictions by Arnold, Lenaghan, and Moore (2003) that in an expanding system a Weibel instability would grow as exp(√τ) rather than exp(τ). The results enable quantitative comparison between classical field dynamics and kinetic theory descriptions of non-Abelian plasma instabilities in the expanding geometry relevant to heavy ion collisions, and motivate further study of the factor-of-2 discrepancy between the measured and predicted growth rates.
