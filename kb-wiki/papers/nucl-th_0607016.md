# Pion induced double charge exchange reactions in the Delta resonance region

> We have applied the Giessen BUU (GiBUU) transport model to the description of the double charge exchange (DCX) reaction of pions with different nuclear targets at incident kinetic energies of 120-180 MeV . The DCX process is highly sensitive to details of the interactions of pions with the nuclear medium and, therefore, represents a major benchmark for any model of pion scattering off nuclei at low and intermediate energies. The impact of surface effects, such as the neutron skins of heavy nuclei, is investigated. The dependence of the total cross section on the nuclear mass number is also discussed. We achieve a good quantitative agreement with the extensive data set obtained at LAMPF. Furthermore, we compare the solutions of the transport equations obtained in the test-particle ansatz using two different schemes - the full and the parallel ensemble method.

## Key claim

The GiBUU transport model achieves good quantitative agreement with LAMPF double charge exchange data on several nuclear targets at pion kinetic energies of 120-180 MeV, but the agreement is qualified by the fact that the model misses some strength at backward angles and for pion energies below approximately 30 MeV.

## Method

The authors applied the semiclassical Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model to simulate pion-induced double charge exchange reactions on oxygen-16, calcium-40, and lead-208 targets at incident pion kinetic energies of 120, 150, and 180 MeV. The model incorporates pion-nucleon quasielastic scattering (including charge exchange) and pion absorption mechanisms, with nuclear densities implemented via Woods-Saxon or harmonic-oscillator parameterizations, and the Boltzmann equation was solved using both the full ensemble and parallel ensemble test-particle methods to compare their accuracy and computational efficiency.

## Key numbers

* Total double charge exchange cross sections on oxygen-16, calcium-40, and lead-208 were calculated at incident pion kinetic energies of 120, 150, and 180 MeV and compared to LAMPF data from Wood et al. (1992).
* The Delta(1232) resonance pole mass used in the angular distribution parameterization is 1.232 GeV.
* The constants fitted to Crystal Ball angular distribution data for the resonance angular distribution are alpha = 1.9 and beta = 26.5.
* The number of test-particles per physical nucleon used in the simulations was typically of order 300-1500.
* Woods-Saxon density parameters for rhodium-103 include a proton radius of 5.194 fm and a neutron radius of 5.358 fm.

## Context

This work extends earlier cascade model calculations by Vicente et al. (1988) and the Boltzmann equation approach by Hufner and Thies, which achieved only qualitative agreement with data on single and double charge exchange. The paper demonstrates that the GiBUU transport model successfully passes the demanding test of describing double charge exchange reactions, establishing that the semiclassical approach is well suited for pion dynamics in nuclei for pion kinetic energies above approximately 30 MeV, and enables future applications to neutrino-induced pion production and photon-induced double-pion production where pion final state interactions are important.
