# Anti-strange meson-baryon interaction in hot and dense nuclear matter

> We present a study of in-medium cross sections and (off-shell) transition rates for the most relevant binary reactions for strange pseudoscalar meson production close to threshold in heavy-ion collisions at FAIR energies. Our results rely on a chiral unitary approach in coupled channels which incorporates the $s$- and $p$-waves of the kaon-nucleon interaction. The formalism, which is modified in the hot and dense medium to account for Pauli blocking effects, mean-field binding on baryons, and pion and kaon self-energies, has been improved to implement full unitarization and self-consistency for both the $s$- and $p$-wave interactions at finite temperature and density. This gives access to in-medium amplitudes in several elastic and inelastic coupled channels with strangeness content $S=-1$. The obtained total cross sections mostly reflect the fate of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance, which melts in the nuclear environment, whereas the off-shell transition probabilities are also sensitive to the in-medium properties of the hyperons excited in the $p$-wave amplitudes [$Λ$, $Σ$ and $Σ^*(1385)$]. The single-particle potentials of these hyperons at finite momentum, density and temperature are also discussed in connection with the pertinent scattering amplitudes. Our results are the basis for future implementations in microscopic transport approaches accounting for off-shell dynamics of strangeness production in nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a chiral unitary approach for the anti-strange meson-baryon interaction in hot and dense nuclear matter that achieves full unitarization and self-consistency for both the s-wave and p-wave interactions at finite temperature and density, whereas previous work by Tolos (2008) had only achieved full self-consistency for the s-wave interaction while treating the p-wave contribution through hyperon-nucleon insertions.

## Method

The authors build upon a chiral unitary approach in coupled channels for the S=-1 sector, incorporating both s-wave amplitudes from the Weinberg-Tomozawa term and p-wave amplitudes from the pole terms of the octet hyperons Lambda(1115), Sigma(1195) and the decuplet Sigma*(1385). Medium effects are implemented within the Imaginary Time (Matsubara) Formalism to account for Pauli blocking, mean-field binding on baryons via a temperature-dependent Walecka-type sigma-omega model, and meson self-energies for pions and kaons. The in-medium meson-baryon loop functions are calculated with full spectral functions for the mesons, and the unitarized amplitudes are obtained by solving Bethe-Salpeter equations with on-shell factorization for both s-wave and p-wave interactions independently.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: rho_0 = 0.17 fm^{-3}
- Cutoff momentum for loop function regularization: q_max = 630 MeV
- Meson decay constant: f = 1.123 f_pi
- Bare masses used for hyperons: Lambda = 1030 MeV, Sigma = 1120 MeV, Sigma* = 1371 MeV
- Chiral couplings: D = 0.85, F = 0.52
- Attractive antikaon potential at normal nuclear matter density: 40-60 MeV (from prior self-consistent calculations cited in the paper)

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent chiral unitary approach developed by Tolos (2006) for cold nuclear matter and Tolos (2008) for finite temperature, by achieving full unitarization of the p-wave interaction at finite temperature and density for the first time. The results provide in-medium cross sections and off-shell transition rates for binary reactions relevant to strangeness production near threshold in heavy-ion collisions at FAIR energies, and will serve as input for microscopic transport approaches such as the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) model to analyze antikaon and hyperon production.
