# Multiple-models prediction for light neutron-rich isotopes cross section by $Q_g$ systematics in $^{40}$Ar projectile fragmentation reactions

> Precise predictions for nuclei near drip lines are crucial for experiments in new generation of rare isotope facilities. A multi-models investigation of the $Q_g$ systematics for fragments production cross sections, with $Q_g$ defined as the difference of mass excess (ME) between the projectile ($Z_{p}, A_{p}$) and the fragment ($Z_{f}, A_{f}$) nuclei $Q_{g}=ME(Z_{p}, A_{p})-ME(Z_{f}, A_{f})$, has been performed to verify the model prediction abilities for light neutron-rich isotopes in measured $^{40}$Ar + $^9$Be projectile fragmentation reactions from 57$A$ MeV to 1$A$ GeV. The models used are the FRACS parametrizations and the newly developed Bayesian neural networks (BNN) model. %method The results show that FRACS, BNN, and $Q_g$ extrapolations are generally consistent, except for fragments near the nuclear mass of the projectile. Additionally, both measured data and model extrapolations provide evidence for a shell closure at $N=$ 16 in fluorine and neon, as well as the disappearance of the traditional magic number $N=$ 20 in neon, sodium and magnesium.

## Key claim
The FRACS, BNN, and Q_g extrapolation methods are generally consistent for predicting light neutron-rich isotope cross sections in 40Ar projectile fragmentation, except for fragments near the projectile mass; both measured data and model predictions provide evidence for a shell closure at N=16 in fluorine and neon, and the disappearance of the traditional magic number N=20 in neon, sodium, and magnesium.

## Method
The authors applied three models, FRACS parametrizations, Bayesian neural networks (BNN), and Q_g systematics (based on mass excess differences between projectile and fragment), to predict production cross sections for neutron-rich fragments in 40Ar + 9Be projectile fragmentation reactions at four incident energies: 57A MeV, 90A MeV, 140A MeV, and 1A GeV. They compared the model predictions with existing experimental data from GSI, RIKEN-RIPS, NSCL, and HIRFL, fitting the Q_g systematics to extract inverse slope parameters (effective temperatures) for each fragment chain.

## Key numbers
- At 1A GeV, the inverse slope parameters (T) from Q_g fitting for boron, carbon, nitrogen, and fluorine were 1/3.12, 1/2.75, 1/1.92, and 1/1.38 MeV, respectively.
- For 39Na produced in 48Ca + 9Be at 140A MeV, the BNN prediction (0.46 fb) was very similar to the experimentally estimated value at 345A MeV at RIKEN (0.5 fb).
- The BNN model was trained on 6393 measured cross sections from 53 projectile fragmentation reactions, with an optimized structure of one input layer, one hidden layer with 46 neural units, and one output layer.
- The BNN model is applicable for incident energies from 40A MeV to 1 GeV/u, projectile nuclei from 40Ar to 208Pb, and fragments with Z > 3 but less than the projectile charge number.

## Context
This work extends the Q_g systematics approach first applied to projectile fragmentation by Tarasov et al. (2007) and the FRACS parametrizations developed by Mei (2016), while also validating the newer BNN model introduced by Ma et al. (2022). The multi-model comparison provides cross-checks for predicting yields of exotic nuclei near drip lines, which is crucial for designing experiments at next-generation rare isotope facilities. The results confirm earlier findings on the N=16 shell closure in fluorine and neon (Ozawa 2000, Stanoiu 2004) and the disappearance of N=20 in the island of inversion region (Campi 1975, Warburton 1990, Notani 2002, Ahn 2022).
