# Constraints on the Neutron Skin and the Symmetry Energy from the Anti-analog Giant Dipole Resonance in 208Pb

> We investigate the impact of the neutron-skin thickness Delta(R) on the energy difference between the anti-analog giant dipole resonance (AGDR), E(AGDR), and the isobaric analog state (IAS), E(IAS), in a heavy nucleus such as 208Pb. For guidance, we first develop a simple and analytic, yet physical, approach based on the Droplet Model that linearly connects the energy difference E(AGDR)-E(IAS) with Delta(R). To test this correlation on more fundamental grounds, we employ a family of systematically varied Skyrme energy density functionals where variations on the value of the symmetry energy at saturation density J are explored. The calculations have been performed within the fully self consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) plus charge-exchange random phase approximation (RPA) framework. We confirm the linear correlation within our microscopic apporach and, by comparing our results with available experimental data in 208Pb, we find that our analysis is consistent with Delta(R) = 0.204 \pm 0.009 fm, J = 31.4 \pm 0.5 MeV and a slope parameter of the symmetry energy at saturation of L = 76.4 \pm 5.4 MeV - the attached errors correspond to a lower-limit estimate of the systematic plus experimental uncertainties. These results are in agreement with those extracted from different experimental data albeit, L and Delta(R) are somewhat large when compared to previous estimations based on giant resonance studies.

## Key claim

The energy difference between the anti-analog giant dipole resonance (AGDR) and the isobaric analog state (IAS) in 208Pb is linearly anti-correlated with the neutron-skin thickness, and this correlation allows extraction of the neutron-skin thickness and symmetry energy parameters, though the extracted values of L and Delta(R) are somewhat large when compared to previous estimations based on giant resonance studies.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) plus charge-exchange random phase approximation (RPA) calculations using a family of systematically varied Skyrme energy density functionals (SAMi-J) with symmetry energy at saturation density J varying from 27 to 35 MeV in steps of 1 MeV. They also developed a simple analytic model based on the Droplet Model to linearly connect the energy difference E(AGDR)-E(IAS) with the neutron-skin thickness Delta(R). The calculated energy differences were compared with experimental data from two measurements of the 208Pb(p,n) reaction.

## Key numbers

* Neutron-skin thickness in 208Pb: Delta(R) = 0.204 +/- 0.009 fm
* Symmetry energy at saturation density: J = 31.4 +/- 0.5 MeV
* Slope parameter of the symmetry energy at saturation: L = 76.4 +/- 5.4 MeV
* Experimental energy difference E(AGDR)-E(IAS) from Ref. Yas13: 8.69 +/- 0.36 MeV
* Experimental energy difference E(AGDR)-E(IAS) from Ref. Kra133: 8.90 +/- 0.09 MeV
* The SAMi-J family uses nuclear incompressibility K_infinity = 245 MeV and nucleon effective mass m*/m = 0.675

## Context

This work extends the method proposed by Krasznahorkay et al. (2013-2014) for extracting neutron-skin thickness from AGDR measurements, and provides a microscopic foundation for the linear correlation previously suggested by Krmpotic et al. (1983). The results are compared with other experimental determinations including parity-violating electron scattering (PREX, Abrahamyan et al. 2012), antiproton absorption (Trzcinska et al. 2001), electric dipole polarizability (Piekarewicz et al. 2012), and pygmy dipole resonance (Carbone et al. 2010). The extracted values enable constraints on the density dependence of the symmetry energy relevant for neutron star structure and heavy-ion collision dynamics.
