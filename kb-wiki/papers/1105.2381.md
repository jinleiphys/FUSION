# Extraction of Various Five-Quark Components of the Nucleons

> We have generalized the approach of Brodsky {\it et al.} for the intrinsic charm quark distribution in the nucleons to the light-quark sector involving intrinsic $\bar u, \bar d, s$ and $\bar s$ sea quarks. We compare the calculations with the existing $\bar d - \bar u$, $s + \bar s$, and $\bar u + \bar d - s -\bar s$ data. The good agreement between the theory and the data allows the extraction of the probabilities for the $|uudu\bar{u}>$, $|uudd\bar{d}>$, and $|uuds\bar{s}>$ five-quark Fock states in the proton. We also calculate the $x$-dependence of the intrinsic charm after taking into consideration the QCD evolution of the intrinsic quark distribution.

## Key claim

The BHPS model for intrinsic five-quark Fock states, generalized to light quarks, can simultaneously describe the existing \(\bar d - \bar u\), \(s + \bar s\), and \(\bar u + \bar d - s - \bar s\) data, but the extracted probabilities for the \(|uudu\bar{u}\rangle\), \(|uudd\bar{d}\rangle\), and \(|uuds\bar{s}\rangle\) states depend on the assumed value of the initial QCD evolution scale \(\mu\) (0.5 GeV or 0.3 GeV).

## Method

The authors generalized the BHPS model (Brodsky, Hoyer, Peterson, Sakai, 1980) for intrinsic charm to light-quark five-quark Fock states \(|uud Q \bar Q\rangle\) with \(Q = u, d, s\). They solved the BHPS momentum distribution formula numerically for the \(\bar Q\) distributions, then applied QCD non-singlet evolution from an initial scale \(\mu\) (0.5 or 0.3 GeV) to the experimental \(Q^2\) values. The calculated \(\bar d - \bar u\), \(s + \bar s\), and \(\bar u + \bar d - s - \bar s\) distributions were compared with data from the Fermilab E866 Drell-Yan experiment and the HERMES semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering experiment to extract the probabilities of each five-quark Fock state.

## Key numbers

- The integrated \(\bar d(x) - \bar u(x)\) from Fermilab E866 at \(Q^2 = 54\) GeV\(^2\) is \(0.118 \pm 0.012\), which sets the difference \({\cal P}^{d \bar d}_5 - {\cal P}^{u \bar u}_5\).
- For the \(|uuds\bar{s}\rangle\) state, the extracted probability is \({\cal P}^{s \bar s}_5 = 0.024\) (for \(\mu = 0.5\) GeV) or \(0.029\) (for \(\mu = 0.3\) GeV), from fitting HERMES \(s(x) + \bar s(x)\) data at \(Q^2 = 2.5\) GeV\(^2\) in the region \(x > 0.1\).
- The combined probabilities for the three light five-quark states are: for \(\mu = 0.5\) GeV, \({\cal P}^{u \bar u}_5 = 0.122\), \({\cal P}^{d \bar d}_5 = 0.240\), \({\cal P}^{s \bar s}_5 = 0.024\); for \(\mu = 0.3\) GeV, \({\cal P}^{u \bar u}_5 = 0.162\), \({\cal P}^{d \bar d}_5 = 0.280\), \({\cal P}^{s \bar s}_5 = 0.029\).
- The total probability for the proton to be in a \(|uud Q \bar Q\rangle\) state is approximately 40%.
- The intrinsic charm probability is estimated as \({\cal P}^{c \bar c}_5 \approx 0.01\), based on the \(1/m_Q^2\) scaling.

## Context

This work extends the BHPS model (Brodsky et al., 1980) from the intrinsic charm sector to light quarks, building on the authors' earlier numerical treatment (Chang and Peng, 2011). It provides the first extraction of the \(|uuds\bar{s}\rangle\) five-quark probability from HERMES strange sea data, and refines the probabilities for the \(|uudu\bar{u}\rangle\) and \(|uudd\bar{d}\rangle\) states previously obtained. The results are qualitatively consistent with the meson-cloud model analysis by Szczurek et al., which found the proton has about 60% probability to be in a three-quark bare-nucleon state. The work enables future searches for intrinsic charm and bottom quarks, noting that QCD evolution shifts the most sensitive region for intrinsic charm to lower \(x\) (\(0.1 < x < 0.4\)) rather than the highest \(x\) region previously explored.
