# Non-Abelian behavior of $α$ Bosons in cold symmetric nuclear matter

> The ground state energy of infinite symmetric nuclear matter is usually described by strongly interacting nucleons obeying the Pauli exclusion principle. We can imagine a unitary transformation which groups four non identical nucleons (i.e. with different spin and isospin) close in coordinate space. Those nucleons, being non identical, do not obey the Pauli principle, thus their relative momenta are negligibly small (just to fulfill the Heisenberg principle). Such a cluster can be identified with an $α$ boson. But in dense nuclear matter, those $α$ particles still obey the Pauli principle since are constituted of Fermions. The ground state energy of nuclear matter $α$ clusters is the same as for nucleons, thus it is degenerate. We could think of $α$ particles as vortices which can now braid, for instance making $^8Be$ which leave the ground state energy unchanged. Further braiding to heavier clusters ($^{12}C$, $^{16}O$..) could give a different representation of the ground state at no energy cost. In contrast d-like clusters (i.e. N=Z odd-odd nuclei, where N and Z are the neutron and proton number respectively) cannot describe the ground state of nuclear matter and can be formed at high excitation energies (or temperatures) only. We show that even-even, N=Z, clusters could be classified as non-Abelian states of matter. As a consequence an $α$ condensate in nuclear matter might be hindered by the Fermi motion, while it could be possible a condensate of $^8Be$ or heavier clusters.

## Key claim

The ground state energy of infinite symmetric nuclear matter can be represented by different even-even N=Z clusters (alpha particles, Be-8, C-12, O-16, etc.) with no energy cost, making the ground state degenerate; these clusters behave as non-Abelian states of matter because exchanging them does not simply change the wave function sign and the order of exchanges matters, and as a consequence an alpha condensate might be hindered by Fermi motion while a condensate of Be-8 or heavier clusters could be possible.

## Method

The authors model infinite symmetric nuclear matter using an energy per particle expansion with virial coefficients (A1, A2, A3) including kinetic energy, potential interactions, and cluster binding energy corrected for Coulomb effects. They impose three conditions at ground state density: energy per particle E/Nx = -15*A_cl MeV, pressure P=0, and compressibility K=225*A_cl MeV, and solve for the virial coefficients for different cluster types (alpha particles, deuteron-like clusters, and heavier bosonic clusters). They compare the resulting equations of state for cluster matter against the CCSdelta3 nucleon equation of state from prior work.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of symmetric nuclear matter: E/A = -15 MeV
- Fermi energy parameter: epsilon_f_tilde = 22.5 MeV
- For alpha particles: A1 = -360 MeV, A2 = 180 MeV, A3 = 0 MeV
- For deuteron-like (d) particles: A1 = -255 MeV, A2 = 202.5 MeV, A3 = -50 MeV
- First excited state of C-12 (Hoyle state) is at about 7 MeV above ground state
- Compressibility condition: K = 225*A_cl MeV at ground state density

## Context

This work extends the concept of non-Abelian statistics from condensed matter physics (Stern, 2008) to nuclear systems, showing that even-even N=Z clusters can be classified as non-Abelian states of matter. It builds on the alpha-cluster model of nuclei (Ring, 2004) and the CCSdelta3 equation of state for nucleons (Rus, 2009). The paper enables experimental searches for Be-8 or heavier cluster condensates in heavy ion collisions, particularly in Ca-40+Ca-40 collisions at Fermi energies, and suggests that multiplicity fluctuations of protons and alpha particles could distinguish between different clustering models.
