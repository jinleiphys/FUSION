# Temperature Dependence of Even-Odd Effects in Small Superconducting Systems

> Generalized BCS equations which consistently include the projection on the particle-number parity are derived from a systematic variational method. Numerical solutions are given that are illustrative of ultra-small metallic grains. Compared to the BCS approximation, the pairing is slightly enhanced in even and noticeably inhibited in odd systems. In the latter case, the gap increases with T near T=0; for a suitably small pairing this can result in a reentrance phenomenon. Paramagnetic effects are evaluated as functions of the temperature and the magnetic field.

# Temperature Dependence of Even-Odd Effects in Small Superconducting Systems

## Key claim

Generalized BCS equations that consistently include projection onto particle-number parity show that, compared to standard BCS, pairing is slightly enhanced in even systems and noticeably inhibited in odd systems; in odd systems the gap increases with temperature near T=0, and for suitably small pairing this can produce a reentrance phenomenon with two transition temperatures.

## Method

The authors derive projected BCS equations from a systematic variational method based on an action-like functional for the characteristic function, using a trial density operator that includes projection onto subspaces with even or odd particle number via the operator P_eta = (1/2)(1 + eta exp(i pi N)). They solve the resulting self-consistent equations numerically for a schematic BCS Hamiltonian with equally spaced single-particle levels, a constant pairing interaction, and a magnetic field term, using parameters typical of ultra-small metallic grains.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff is set such that w_F * Lambda = 100, where w_F is the level density at the Fermi level and Lambda/2 is the cutoff energy above and below the Fermi level.
- For odd systems, the reentrance phenomenon occurs for values 1.0 < w_F * Delta < 1.14 (where Delta is the BCS gap at T=0 and B=0).
- The two critical temperatures in odd systems merge for w_F * Delta approximately 1, corresponding to w_F * G_tilde approximately 0.225.
- In even systems, pairing does not disappear until w_F * G_tilde < 0.182.
- For the magnetization calculations, the magnetic field values used are w_F * B = 0, 0.2, 0.4, 0.6, 0.8 with w_F * Delta = 1.19 (w_F * G_tilde = 0.233).
- At high temperatures, all magnetization curves converge to the Pauli paramagnetic value 2 * w_F * B.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior study (Balian, Flocard, Veneroni, 1997) on extended BCS theories to include magnetic field effects and temperature dependence, building on earlier treatments of even-odd effects in superconducting metallic islands (Tuominen et al., 1992) and ultra-small aluminum grains (Ralph, Black, Tinkham, 1995-1997). The results enable quantitative predictions for thermodynamic properties such as specific heat and magnetization in finite superconducting systems, and the variational framework can be extended to full particle-number projection (rather than just parity projection) to remove spurious discontinuities at critical points.
