# The Operator Product Expansion Beyond Leading Order for Two-Component Fermions

> We consider a homogeneous, balanced gas of strongly interacting fermions in two spin states interacting through a large scattering length. Finite range corrections are needed for a quantitative description of data which experiments and numerical simulations have provided. We use a perturbative field theoretical framework and a tool called the Operator Product Expansion (OPE), which together allow for the expression of finite range corrections to the universal relations and momentum distribution. Using the OPE, we derive the $1/k^6$ part of the momentum tail, which is related to the sum of the derivative of the energy with respect to the finite range and the averaged kinetic energy of opposite spin pairs. By comparing the $1/k^4$ term and the $1/k^6$ correction in the momentum distribution to provided Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) data, we show that including the $1/k^6$ part offers marked improvements. Our field theoretical approach allows for a clear understanding of the role of the scattering length and finite effective range in the universal relations and the momentum distribution.

# The Operator Product Expansion Beyond Leading Order for Two-Component Fermions

## Key claim

The operator product expansion (OPE) can be extended beyond leading order to derive finite-range corrections to universal relations for strongly interacting two-component fermions, yielding a $1/k^6$ tail in the momentum distribution that improves agreement with Quantum Monte Carlo data when the effective range is nonzero. The authors show that including the $1/k^6$ correction offers marked improvements over the leading $1/k^4$ term alone, but note that the $C'$ contribution to the $1/k^6$ tail remains unknown and is not included in their numerical comparison.

## Method

The authors use an effective field theory (EFT) with contact interactions, renormalized at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO) in the effective range $r_s$ using a sharp ultraviolet cutoff. They apply the operator product expansion (OPE) to the nonlocal one-body density operator, computing Wilson coefficients for operators up to dimension $\Delta = 6$ by matching to off-shell scattering amplitudes. The momentum distribution is expressed as an OPE series, and the contact $C$, derivative contact $D$, and pair kinetic energy operator $C'$ are identified as expectation values of renormalized local operators.

## Key numbers

- The momentum distribution tail is given by $\rho_\sigma(k) \to C/k^4 + (C' + D)/k^6 + \mathcal{O}(1/k^8)$, where $C$ is the contact and $D$ is the derivative contact.
- In the unitary limit, the dimensionless contact density is $\mathcal{C}/k_F^4 \approx 0.115$, derived from the Bertsch parameter $\zeta = 0.901(3)$ from Gandolfi et al. (2011).
- In the unitary limit, the dimensionless derivative contact density is $\mathcal{D}/k_F^6 = 0.061$, using the slope $S = 0.12(1)$ from the QMC data of PhysRevA.84.061602 and 2012PTEP.2012aA209C.
- In the BEC limit ($a \to 0^+$), the derivative contact density scales as $\mathcal{D}/k_F^6 \to -0.9(1) (k_F a)^{-3}$, obtained by fitting QMC data for $S$.
- The dimer binding energy gives the derivative contact for the dimer as $D_\text{dimer} = 16\pi / a^3$.
- The adiabatic relation for the effective range is $dE/dr_s = D/(16\pi m)$.
- The virial theorem for a trapped gas including finite-range corrections is $E = 2V - C/(8\pi m a) - r_s D/(32\pi m)$.
- The pressure relation for a homogeneous gas is $P = \frac{2}{3}\mathcal{E} + \mathcal{C}/(12\pi m a) + r_s \mathcal{D}/(48\pi m)$.

## Context

This work extends the universal relations originally derived by Shina Tan (2008) for the zero-range limit of two-component fermions, which introduced the contact $C$ as the asymptote of the $1/k^4$ momentum tail. The finite-range corrections were previously derived using a quantum mechanical framework by Castin and Werner (2012, PhysRevA.86a3626W); the present paper reproduces those results using the OPE framework and an EFT approach, which is model-independent and makes no assumptions about the microscopic interaction. The OPE method was first applied to Tan's relations by Braaten and Platter (2008) and has since been used for unitary Bose gases and other systems. The numerical values of the contact and derivative contact are extracted from Quantum Monte Carlo simulations by PhysRevA.84.061602 and 2012PTEP.2012aA209C, and the comparison in Figure 2 shows that the OPE result including the $1/k^6$ term describes QMC data well for $k > 1.5 k_F$ near unitarity. This work enables quantitative inclusion of effective range effects in experimental analyses of ultracold Fermi gases and provides a systematic framework for extending universal relations to higher orders.
