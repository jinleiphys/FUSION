# Connected Parts of Decuplet Electromagnetic Properties

> We determine the electromagnetic properties of decuplet resonances using chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order. Utilizing a partially quenched charge matrix, we isolate and remove the quark disconnected contractions. This allows us to compare the physical meson loop contributions to the connected contributions, of which the latter are currently calculated using lattice QCD. Finally we determine linear combinations of decuplet resonance and hyperon electromagnetic properties that are exactly independent of disconnected contractions in the isospin limit.

## Key claim

The paper determines the connected parts of decuplet resonance electromagnetic properties (charge radii, magnetic moments, and electric quadrupole moments) using partially quenched chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order, by setting sea quark charges to zero to mimic the omission of disconnected contractions in lattice QCD calculations. The key hedge is that differences in electromagnetic properties between different isospin multiplets are free of disconnected local contributions only up to fourth order in the SU(3) chiral expansion, where the omitted terms scale generically as approximately 0.06.

## Method

The author uses partially quenched chiral perturbation theory (PQChPT) with an SU(6|3) graded symmetry group, where valence quarks (u,d,s), ghost quarks (tilde u, tilde d, tilde s), and sea quarks (j,l,r) are distinguished. The connected contributions are isolated by setting the electric charges of the sea quarks to zero (q_j = q_l = q_r = 0) in the charge matrix, while keeping valence and ghost quark charges at their physical values. The one-loop chiral expressions for decuplet charge radii, magnetic moments, and electric quadrupole moments are then recomputed with these modified charge assignments, correcting erroneous loop integrals from prior work by Arndt (2003).

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant used: f = 132 MeV
- The error introduced by omitting disconnected diagrams in differences of magnetic moments between different isospin multiplets scales generically as m_eta^4 / (2 sqrt(2) pi f)^4 ~ 0.06
- For the Delta++ charge radius, the pion loop coefficient A_pi changes from 1 in full ChPT to 4/3 in connected ChPT; for the Delta+ it changes from 1/3 to 2/3; for the Delta0 it changes from -1/3 to 0
- For the Sigma*0, the pion loop coefficient A_pi changes from 0 in full ChPT to 2/9 in connected ChPT, and the kaon coefficient changes from 0 to -1/9
- For the Omega-, the kaon loop coefficient A_K changes from -1 in full ChPT to -2/3 in connected ChPT, while the pion coefficient remains 0

## Context

This work extends the ChPT calculations of decuplet electromagnetic properties by Butler (1993) and Arndt (2003) to address the systematic error from omitting disconnected quark contractions in lattice QCD simulations, such as those by Alexandrou (2008) and Aubin (2008). The paper enables lattice QCD practitioners to compare their connected-only results to chiral perturbation theory predictions, and identifies specific linear combinations of decuplet and hyperon electromagnetic properties that are exactly independent of disconnected contractions in the isospin limit, allowing direct comparison with experiment. The author also corrects erroneous loop integrals appearing in Arndt (2003) that affect both the partially quenched and quenched results.
