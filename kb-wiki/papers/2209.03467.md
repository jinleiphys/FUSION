# Search for the Chiral Magnetic Effect in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm{NN}}}}=27$ GeV with the STAR forward Event Plane Detectors

> A decisive experimental test of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) is considered one of the major scientific goals at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) towards understanding the nontrivial topological fluctuations of the Quantum Chromodynamics vacuum. In heavy-ion collisions, the CME is expected to result in a charge separation phenomenon across the reaction plane, whose strength could be strongly energy dependent. The previous CME searches have been focused on top RHIC energy collisions. In this Letter, we present a low energy search for the CME in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm{NN}}}}=27$ GeV. We measure elliptic flow scaled charge-dependent correlators relative to the event planes that are defined at both mid-rapidity $|η|<1.0$ and at forward rapidity $2.1 < |η|<5.1$. We compare the results based on the directed flow plane ($Ψ_1$) at forward rapidity and the elliptic flow plane ($Ψ_2$) at both central and forward rapidity. The CME scenario is expected to result in a larger correlation relative to $Ψ_1$ than to $Ψ_2$, while a flow driven background scenario would lead to a consistent result for both event planes. In 10-50\% centrality, results using three different event planes are found to be consistent within experimental uncertainties, suggesting a flow driven background scenario dominating the measurement. We obtain an upper limit on the deviation from a flow driven background scenario at the 95\% confidence level. This work opens up a possible road map towards future CME search with the high statistics data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan Phase-II.

## Key claim

In Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm{NN}}}}=27$ GeV, the charge-dependent correlator $\Delta\gamma/v_2$ measured relative to the directed flow plane ($\Psi_1$) is found to be consistent within experimental uncertainties with the same quantity measured relative to the elliptic flow planes ($\Psi_2$) at both forward and mid-rapidity, suggesting that a flow-driven background scenario dominates the measurement rather than a Chiral Magnetic Effect signal.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration measured the elliptic-flow-scaled charge-dependent correlator $\Delta\gamma/v_2$ using the scalar product method with three different event planes: the directed flow plane $\Psi_1$ from forward rapidity ($|\eta|>3.4$) using the Event Plane Detectors (EPDs), the elliptic flow plane $\Psi_2$ from forward rapidity ($2.1<|\eta|<3.4$) using the EPDs, and the elliptic flow plane $\Psi_2$ from mid-rapidity ($|\eta|<1.0$) using the Time Projection Chamber (TPC). The analysis used approximately 300 million minimum-bias events from Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm{NN}}}}=27$ GeV collected by STAR in 2018, with charged particles selected in the range $0.2 < p_T < 3.0$ GeV/$c$ and $|\eta|<1.0$ from the TPC.

## Key numbers

* In 10-50% centrality, results using three different event planes are found to be consistent within experimental uncertainties, suggesting a flow driven background scenario dominating the measurement.
* The correlation of the magnetic field with $\Psi_1(|\eta|>Y_{\rm beam})$ is $18.9 \pm 1.2\%$ stronger compared to $\Psi_2$ from TPC ($\Psi_2(|\eta|<1)$) and $11.1\pm 2.0\%$ stronger compared to $\Psi_2$ from EPD ($\Psi_2(|\eta|<Y_{\rm beam})$), as estimated from UrQMD simulations.
* The total systematic uncertainty on the double-ratio of ($\Delta\gamma/v_2$) with respect to $\Psi_1$ and $\Psi_2$ planes in 10-50% centrality is not bigger than 7%.
* The tracking efficiency of the TPC ranges from 77% to 86% for $p_T > 0.2$ GeV/$c$ based on embedding simulations.
* Approximately 300 million minimum bias events were used after event cuts, with primary vertices restricted to $|V_z|<40$ cm and $V_r<2$ cm.

## Context

This work extends previous CME searches at STAR, which had focused on top RHIC energy collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm{NN}}}}=200$ GeV (STAR 2020, STAR 2021) and isobar collisions (STAR 2021), by providing the first low-energy search at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm{NN}}}}=27$ GeV using the newly installed Event Plane Detectors. The analysis exploits the unique capability at this energy where the beam rapidity ($Y_{\rm beam}=3.4$) falls within the EPD acceptance, allowing the directed flow plane from forward protons to serve as a proxy for the reaction plane with stronger correlation to the magnetic field direction than elliptic flow planes. This work opens up a possible road map towards future CME search with the high statistics data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan Phase-II.
