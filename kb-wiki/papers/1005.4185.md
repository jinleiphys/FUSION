# On quantum mechanical transport coefficients in nonequilibrium nuclear processes with application to heavy-ion collisions

> The elements of the quantum mechanical diffusion matrix, leading to a Gibbs equilibrium state for a set of $N$ coupled quantum harmonic oscillators are derived within Lindblad's axiomatic approach. Consequences of the fundamental constraints on the quantum friction coefficients are discussed. We derive the equations of motion for the expectation values and variances, and we solve them analytically. We apply our results to the description of the charge and mass asymmetry coordinates in heavy-ion collisions, and we investigate the effect of dissipation on tunneling in sub-barrier processes.

## Key claim

The paper derives the full quantum mechanical diffusion matrix for N coupled harmonic oscillators within Lindblad's axiomatic approach, showing that off-diagonal diffusion coefficients cannot be neglected when the coupling between oscillators is strong, contrary to common assumptions in prior work.

## Method

The authors impose the condition that the asymptotic state of the reduced density matrix is a Gibbs equilibrium state for N independent quantum harmonic oscillators, then solve the resulting algebraic equations from Lindblad's master equation to obtain explicit expressions for all elements of the diffusion matrix. They derive equations of motion for expectation values and variances in the Heisenberg picture and solve them analytically using matrix exponentiation.

## Key numbers

- Diagonal diffusion coefficients: \(D_{q_kq_k} = \frac{\hbar}{2}\left(\frac{\lambda_{kk}-\mu_{kk}}{m_k\omega_k}\right)\coth\frac{\hbar\beta\omega_k}{2}\) and \(D_{p_kp_k} = \frac{\hbar}{2} m_k\omega_k(\lambda_{kk}+\mu_{kk})\coth\frac{\hbar\beta\omega_k}{2}\)
- Off-diagonal diffusion coefficients: \(D_{q_kq_j} = \frac{\hbar}{4}\left(\frac{\lambda_{jk}-\mu_{jk}}{m_k\omega_k}\coth\frac{\hbar\beta\omega_k}{2}+\frac{\lambda_{kj}-\mu_{kj}}{m_j\omega_j}\coth\frac{\hbar\beta\omega_j}{2}\right)\)
- In the high-temperature limit, Einstein's relation holds: \(D_{p_kp_k} = \tilde\lambda_{kk} m_k k_B T\) and \(D_{p_kp_j} = \Lambda_{kj} \left(\frac{m_k+m_j}{2}\right) k_B T\)
- Asymptotic variances: \(\sigma_{q_kq_k}(\infty) = \frac{\hbar}{2m_k\omega_k}\coth\frac{\hbar\beta\omega_k}{2}\) and \(\sigma_{p_kp_k}(\infty) = \frac{\hbar}{2} m_k\omega_k\coth\frac{\hbar\beta\omega_k}{2}\)
- For N=2 with \(\mu_{kj}=0\), kinetic coupling constraint: \(|\kappa_{12}| < \sqrt{1/(m_1 m_2)}\) and potential coupling constraint: \(|\nu_{12}| < \sqrt{m_1 m_2}\,\omega_1\omega_2\)

## Context

This work extends the Lindblad axiomatic approach previously applied to one-dimensional harmonic oscillators by Sandulescu and collaborators to the general N-dimensional case with full coupling between degrees of freedom. It generalizes the quantum mechanical diffusion coefficients derived for the one-dimensional oscillator by Sandulescu (2000) and others, and provides the theoretical foundation for describing dissipation in heavy-ion collisions beyond the uncorrelated approximation used by Hahn et al. (1979) and the non-dissipative model of Sandulescu et al. (2001). The results enable quantitative treatment of charge and mass asymmetry dynamics in deep-inelastic collisions and sub-barrier fusion processes with full quantum dissipation.
