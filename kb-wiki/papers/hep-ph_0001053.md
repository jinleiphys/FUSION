# Possible Studies of Parton Distributions at JHF: polarized pd Drell-Yan process

> We discuss possible studies of parton distributions at the proposed 50-GeV PS facility. First, comments are given in general on the importance of large-x physics for finding new physics. Second, polarized deuteron reactions are discussed in connection with new structure functions which are specific for a spin-1 hadron. Third, if the polarized proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process is studied at the facility, it could provide information on the light-antiquark flavor asymmetry in the polarized distributions.

## Key claim

If the polarized proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process is studied at the proposed 50-GeV PS facility, it could provide information on the light-antiquark flavor asymmetry in the polarized distributions, but the nuclear corrections in the deuteron are neglected in the present studies and would need to be taken into account carefully if experimental data are obtained.

## Method

The author develops a theoretical formalism for the polarized proton-deuteron (pd) Drell-Yan process, extending existing polarized proton-proton (pp) Drell-Yan studies by incorporating the tensor structure of the spin-1 deuteron. Using spin-density matrices and the Ralston-Soper type analysis, the hadron tensor is expanded in terms of sixteen 4x4 matrices under Hermiticity, parity conservation, and time-reversal invariance. The resulting cross-section ratios for longitudinal and transverse spin asymmetries are then evaluated numerically using a recent parton distribution parametrization at Q^2=1 GeV^2, evolved to Q^2=25 GeV^2 by LO-DGLAP evolution equations, with three assumed values for the polarized antiquark flavor asymmetry ratio.

## Key numbers

- The Drell-Yan cross section ratio R_pd = Δ_(T)σ_pd / (2 Δ_(T)σ_pp) is evaluated at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2 for the initial parton distributions, then evolved to Q^2 = M_μμ^2 = 25 GeV^2.
- Three assumed ratios for the polarized antiquark distributions are used: r_¯q ≡ Δ_(T)¯u / Δ_(T)¯d = 0.7, 1.0, or 1.3.
- In the limit x_F → 1, the ratio R_pd becomes (1/2)[1 + Δ_(T)¯d(x_2)/Δ_(T)¯u(x_2)] evaluated at x_2 → 0.
- In the limit x_F → -1, the ratio R_pd becomes (1/2)[1 + Δ_(T)¯d(x_1)/(4 Δ_(T)¯u(x_1))] evaluated at x_1 → 0.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical formalism for polarized pd Drell-Yan developed by Hino and Kumano (1999) and discusses possible measurements at the proposed 50-GeV PS facility in Japan, which would complement the RHIC-Spin project that focuses on smaller-x regions. The paper builds on previous studies of unpolarized antiquark flavor asymmetry from Fermilab Drell-Yan experiments and the Gottfried-sum-rule violation, and it enables future experimental tests of tensor-polarized distributions b_1 (which have never been measured) and polarized light-antiquark flavor asymmetry, providing new tests for nucleon structure models.
