# "Mass Without Mass" and Nuclear Matter

> This is a brief account of the chief accomplishment of the 5-year activity (2008-2013) in the World Class University III Program at Hanyang University with the theme of exploring "From Dense Matter to Compact Stars." The principal objective was to explore and foresee what could be a break-through research that could be initiated at the RIB machine in project "RAON" at the upcoming ambitious Institute for Basic Science (IBS) in Korea. What came out was a possibility for unraveling the mystery of proton's "mass without mass" via nuclear matter and its impact on RAON-type physics and compact stars.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the proton mass has a chiral-invariant component \(m_0 \approx (0.6-0.9)m_N\) that does not vanish when the quark condensate goes to zero, contradicting the standard Nambu scenario where the nucleon mass would vanish with the condensate; this component is linked to the dilaton condensate and emerges from a topological half-skyrmion transition in dense matter rather than being put in by hand.

## Method

The work combines skyrmion crystal simulations on a lattice, which reveal a topological transition at density \(n_{1/2}\) where skyrmions fractionize into half-skyrmions, with a scale-invariant hidden local symmetry (sHLS) effective field theory that includes baryons, pions, vector mesons (\(\rho, \omega\)), and a scalar dilaton. Robust features from the skyrmion crystal, such as the vanishing of the spatially averaged quark condensate while chiral symmetry remains broken, are mapped onto a baryon-included effective Lagrangian (bsHLS), and Wilsonian renormalization-group (\(V_{lowk}\)-RG) calculations are performed with sliding vacuum information from the topology change.

## Key numbers

- The proton mass is \(\sim 1\) GeV (more precisely 938 MeV), with more than 90% not coming from the Higgs mechanism or quark masses.
- The chiral-invariant component of the nucleon mass is \(m_0 \approx (0.6-0.9) m_N\).
- The half-skyrmion transition density is \(n_{1/2} \sim 2 n_0\) (where \(n_0\) is normal nuclear matter density).
- In the half-skyrmion phase, the sound velocity in dense matter is \(v_s/c = \sqrt{1/3}\), derived from the density-independence of the dilaton condensate and the trace of the energy-momentum tensor.
- The \(\rho\) meson mass scales as \(m_\rho \sim g_\rho \sim \langle \bar{q}q \rangle \to 0\) at the vector manifestation fixed point, leading to a vanishing ratio \(m_\rho^\ast / m_N^\ast \propto \langle \bar{q}q \rangle^\ast / m_0 \to 0\) as density increases.

## Context

This work extends the Brown-Rho scaling (Brown and Rho, 1991) by reformulating it in terms of scale-chiral symmetry and the dilaton condensate, correcting misinterpretations in the heavy-ion community. It builds on the parity-doublet model of DeTar and Kunihiro (1989) but shows that parity doubling emerges naturally from topology rather than being imposed. The results enable predictions for the equation of state of compact stars, including the stiffening of the symmetry energy at \(n \sim 2 n_0\) and the precocious onset of conformal sound velocity, which can be tested at RIB accelerators (RAON, FRIB, FAIR, NICA) and through gravitational-wave measurements of tidal deformability.
