# Isoscalar monopole excitations in $^{16}$O: $α$-cluster states at low energy and mean-field-type states at higher energy

> Isoscalar monopole strength function in $^{16}$O up to $E_{x}\simeq40$ MeV is discussed. We found that the fine structures at the low energy region up to $E_{x} \simeq 16$ MeV in the experimental monopole strength function obtained by the $^{16}$O$(α,α^{\prime})$ reaction can be rather satisfactorily reproduced within the framework of the $4α$ cluster model, while the gross three bump structures observed at the higher energy region ($16 \lesssim E_{x} \lesssim 40$ MeV) look likely to be approximately reconciled by the mean-field calculations such as RPA and QRPA. In this paper, it is emphasized that two different types of monopole excitations exist in $^{16}$O; one is the monopole excitation to cluster states which is dominant in the lower energy part ($E_{x} \lesssim 16$ MeV), and the other is the monopole excitation of the mean-field type such as one-particle one-hole ($1p1h$) which {is attributed} mainly to the higher energy part ($16 \lesssim E_{x} \lesssim 40$ MeV). It is found that this character of the monopole excitations originates from the fact that the ground state of $^{16}$O with the dominant doubly closed shell structure has a duality of the mean-field-type {as well as} $α$-clustering {character}. This dual nature of the ground state seems to be a common feature in light nuclei.

## Key claim

The isoscalar monopole strength function in $^{16}$O up to 40 MeV excitation energy can be explained by two distinct types of excitations: cluster-type monopole excitations (described by the $4\alpha$ cluster model) dominate below about 16 MeV, while mean-field-type excitations (described by RPA and QRPA) dominate above 16 MeV, originating from the dual mean-field and $\alpha$-clustering nature of the $^{16}$O ground state.

## Method

The authors performed a $4\alpha$ orthogonality condition model (OCM) calculation for $^{16}$O, using the Modified Hasegawa-Nagata (MHN) effective nucleon-nucleon force with phenomenological three-body and four-body forces, to compute the isoscalar monopole strength function for excited $0^+$ states up to $E_x \simeq 16$ MeV. For the higher energy region ($16 \lesssim E_x \lesssim 40$ MeV), they compared experimental data with existing RPA and QRPA mean-field calculations from the literature. They also performed a shell model calculation with $1p1h$ and $2p2h$ configurations within the $0s$, $0p$, $0d1s$, and $0f1p$ shells using Volkov No. 2 and G3RS forces.

## Key numbers

- The $4\alpha$ OCM calculation reproduces six lowest $0^+$ states up to $E_x \simeq 16$ MeV; the calculated $0_2^+$ state at $E_x = 6.37$ MeV (experiment: 6.05 MeV) has an E0 transition matrix element of $3.9$ fm$^2$ (experiment: $3.55 \pm 0.21$ fm$^2$)
- The $0_3^+$ state is calculated at $E_x = 9.96$ MeV (experiment: 12.05 MeV) with $M({\rm E}0) = 2.4$ fm$^2$ (experiment: $4.03 \pm 0.09$ fm$^2$)
- The $0_5^+$ state at $E_x = 14.12$ MeV (experiment: 14.01 MeV) has $M({\rm E}0) = 2.6$ fm$^2$ (experiment: $3.3 \pm 0.7$ fm$^2$)
- The $0_6^+$ state at $E_x = 16.45$ MeV (experiment: 15.10 MeV) is a candidate for the $4\alpha$ condensate with $61\%$ probability
- The $4\alpha$ OCM framework shares $68\%$ of the total isoscalar monopole energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR), calculated as $1 - (1.47/2.58)^2 = 0.68$ using $R(\alpha) = 1.47$ fm and $R = 2.58$ fm
- The total EWSR value is $8.83 \times 10^3$ fm$^4\cdot$MeV, derived from the experimental charge radius $R_c = 2.70$ fm
- The experimental centroid of the higher-energy monopole strength is $E_x = 21.13 \pm 0.49$ MeV, while the RRPA calculation by Ma et al. (1997) gives a centroid at 25.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) in light nuclei, where mean-field approaches (RPA, RRPA, SRPA) by Blaizot et al. (1976), Ma et al. (1997), and Gambacurta et al. (2010) failed to reproduce the low-energy $0^+$ states below 16 MeV in $^{16}$O. The paper builds on the $4\alpha$ OCM framework of Funaki et al. (2008), which successfully described the six lowest $0^+$ states, and contrasts with the $\alpha+^{12}$C OCM of Suzuki (1976) that could only reproduce three $0^+$ states. The results enable a unified understanding of monopole excitations in $^{16}$O as arising from the dual nature of its ground state, and suggest this dual character may be a common feature in light nuclei.
