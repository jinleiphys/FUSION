# Sensitivity of three-body decays to the reactions mechanism and the initial structure by example of 6Be

> The influence of the initial-state structure and the reaction mechanism on three-body decays is investigated by example of the 6Be continuum populated in neutron-knockout reactions on 7Be. The sensitivity of the 6Be excitation spectrum and the three-body correlations to the different components of the model is demonstrated. It is shown that the spin composition of the initial state may have an overwhelming effect on the three-body continuum. The characteristics and structure of the second $0^+_2$ and $2^+_2$ states in 6Be are predicted for the first time and the conditions for their reliable observations are formulated. The effects of interference and the alignment of three-body states on the three-body correlations are demonstrated.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the three-body decay spectrum and correlations of \(^{6}\)Be populated in neutron-knockout reactions are highly sensitive to the spin composition of the initial \(^{7}\)Be state and the reaction mechanism; the abstract’s claim of “overwhelming effect” is hedged by the fact that the ground-state \(0^+_1\) peak is very stable to these variations, while the sensitivity grows strongly with excitation energy above about 5–7 MeV.

## Method

The authors solve an inhomogeneous three-body Schrödinger equation for the \(\alpha + p + p\) continuum of \(^{6}\)Be, with a source term \(\Phi_{\mathbf{q}}\) constructed from a sudden neutron-removal model acting on a shell-model wave function of \(^{7}\)Be. The differential cross section is expressed via the flux of the outgoing wave function and separated into a density matrix (reaction mechanism) and three-body decay amplitudes (final-state interaction). The spin composition of the source is parametrized by coefficients \(\eta_J\) controlling the ratio of total spin \(S=0\) to \(S=1\) configurations, and the radial extent of the source is varied via the oscillator parameter \(r_0\).

## Key numbers

* The \(0^+_1\) and \(2^+_1\) states appear at 1.37 MeV and 3.05 MeV, respectively, in the conventional spectrum.
* The second \(0^+_2\) state is predicted at \(\sim 12\) MeV with a width of \(\sim 14\) MeV; the second \(2^+_2\) state is predicted at \(\sim 7\) MeV with a width of \(\sim 7\) MeV.
* The \(2^+_1\) resonance width is affected at the level of \(15\%\) by varying the radial parameter \(r_0\) of the \(^{7}\)Be wave function.
* The \(2^+_2\) position shows an uncertainty of about 0.8 MeV due to the radial extent of the source; the \(0^+_2\) peak position varies by a few MeV.
* The matter radius of \(^{7}\)Be used to set the oscillator parameter is 2.31(2) fm, corresponding to \(r_0 = 1.8\) fm.
* Local variations in Monte Carlo correlation distributions due to alignment/interference effects are about \(20\%\).

## Context

This work extends the authors’ earlier study (Egorova 2012) by providing a detailed theoretical analysis of the reaction mechanism and initial-state structure effects that were only summarized there. It contradicts the earlier three-body calculations of Vasilevsky (2001), which predicted the \(0^+_2\) and \(2^+_2\) states at much lower energies (3.5 and 5.2 MeV, respectively) because those calculations omitted the \(S=1\) spin component. The results also disagree with the correlation predictions of Garrido (2007), casting doubt on the applicability of that method to Coulombic three-body decays. The work enables future experiments to use three-body continuum spectra as a spectroscopic tool for precursor nuclei, provided the reaction mechanism is well understood, and it formulates clear conditions for observing the predicted second \(0^+\) and \(2^+\) states in \(^{6}\)Be.
