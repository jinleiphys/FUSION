# Squeezing mode in nuclear collisions

> The time dependent Schroedinger equation is solved analytically for a simplified model of moving infinite walls. A new knock-out mode is described which might occur during heavy ion collisions. The outer shell-nucleons are ionised due to the increase of level energy when two nuclei are approaching fast enough. This is analogous to the Mott effect but in contrast occurs only if the reaction time is short enough that no common ionisation threshold in the compound system is established. To demonstrate this pure nonequilibrium effect a simulation of realistic heavy ion collision by a nonlocal Boltzmann equation is performed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a new "squeezing mode" of particle emission occurs during heavy ion collisions when the reaction time is too short for a common ionization threshold to be established in the compound system, causing outer shell nucleons to be ionized by the increase of level energy as two nuclei approach. This is presented as a pure nonequilibrium effect that is distinct from the Mott transition, which relies on equilibration and a decreasing ionization threshold.

## Method

The authors solved the time-dependent Schrödinger equation analytically for a simplified one-dimensional model of moving infinite walls, where the wall distance shrinks linearly with time to represent the approaching nuclei. They then performed simulations of realistic heavy ion collisions using a nonlocal Boltzmann equation (nonlocal BUU) with a soft parameterization of the mean field and realistic nuclear potentials.

## Key numbers

- The initial binding energy for a nucleon in the infinite wall model is \(E_n^0 = \frac{\hbar^2 \pi^2 n^2}{2 m b^2}\), where \(b\) is the initial wall distance.
- The phase kinetic energy due to finite projectile velocity is \(E_\phi = \frac{E_{\rm proj}}{3} \left(1 - \frac{3}{2 n^2 \pi^2}\right)\), with projectile energy \(E_{\rm proj} = m v^2 / 2\).
- Immediate ionization occurs when projectile energy satisfies \(E_{\rm proj} \ge \frac{3}{1 - 3/(2 n^2 \pi^2)} E_c\), where \(E_c\) is the ionization threshold.
- For slower projectiles, ionization requires a reaction time \(t \ge \frac{b}{v} \left(1 - \sqrt{\frac{E_n^0}{E_c - E_\phi}}\right)\).
- The geometrical condition for ionization is \(B \le 2R \sqrt{\frac{E_n^0}{E_c - E_\phi}} \le 2R\), where \(B\) is impact parameter and \(R\) is nuclear radius.
- In the nonlocal BUU simulation, approximately 0.2% of emitted particles were identified as due to the squeezing mode, appearing at 60 fm/c and again at 120 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the study of nonequilibrium particle emission in heavy ion collisions, contrasting with the diabatic emission model of Cassing and Nörenberg (1986) which is limited to beam energies much below the Fermi energy. The squeezing mode is distinguished from the "towing mode" described by Lacroix et al. (1999) by its appearance at the beginning of the collision and its characteristic transverse angular distribution. The analytical solution provides a velocity criterion for the effect, which was previously studied only in the context of instantly removed walls by Cassing (1987). The findings enable experimental searches for this mode using plots of transverse energy versus the ratio of transverse to total energy, where the squeezing mode should appear as a correlation with small transverse energy but a large ratio between transverse and total energy.
