# Properties and microscopic structures of dense stellar matter in RMF models

> Data tables on the equation of state (EOS) and microscopic structures for cold dense stellar matter with proton fractions $Y_p =0.01$-$0.65$ and baryon number densities $n_\text{b}=10^{-8}$-$2 \ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$ are obtained adopting 13 different relativistic density functionals, i.e., NL3, PK1, PK1r, GM1, MTVTC, DD-LZ1, PKDD, DD-ME2, TW99, DD-MEX, DD-MEX1, DD-MEX2, and DD-MEY. The EOSs of dense stellar matter inside neutron stars with baryon number densities $n_\text{b}=7.6\times 10^{-11}$-$2 \ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$ are obtained as well fulfilling $β$-stability condition. In general, the dense stellar matter exhibits droplet phase at $n_\mathrm{b}\lesssim 0.015\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$, while more exotic structures such as rods, slabs, tubes, and bubbles appear sequentially as density increases. The critical proton fractions $Y_p^\mathrm{drip}$ ($\approx 0.26$-0.31) for neutron drip are obtained, where neutron gas emerges outside of nuclei at $Y_p< Y_p^\mathrm{drip}$. For dense stellar matter at small densities ($n_\text{b}\lesssim 10^{-5} \ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$) or large proton fractions ($n_\text{b}\lesssim0.1 \ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$ and $Y_p\gtrsim Y_p^\mathrm{drip}$), the EOSs and microscopic structures are generally insensitive to the adopted density functionals. With the onset of neutron drip at $Y_p\lesssim Y_p^\mathrm{drip}$, the uncertainties emerge and peak at $n_\text{b} \approx 0.02 \ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$ within the range $10^{-5} \lesssim n_\text{b}\lesssim0.1 \ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$. At $n_\text{b}\gtrsim0.1 \ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$, the dense stellar matter becomes uniform and muons eventually appear, where the uncertainties in the EOSs grow significantly.

## Key claim

The paper provides comprehensive data tables for the equation of state (EOS) and microscopic structures of cold dense stellar matter across a wide range of proton fractions and baryon number densities, using 13 different relativistic density functionals; the abstract's claim of covering these ranges is qualified by the fact that the results are obtained under the Thomas-Fermi approximation with spherical and cylindrical approximations for Wigner-Seitz cells, and only at zero temperature.

## Method

The authors employed the Thomas-Fermi approximation within relativistic mean field (RMF) models, solving the equations of motion for meson and photon fields inside Wigner-Seitz cells with reflective boundary conditions using fast cosine transformation. For each of 13 relativistic density functionals (including both nonlinear self-coupling and density-dependent coupling types), they minimized the energy per baryon at fixed baryon number density and proton fraction to determine the optimal geometrical structure among droplet, rod, slab, tube, bubble, and uniform phases. The calculation covered baryon number densities from \(10^{-8}\) to \(2\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) and proton fractions from 0.01 to 0.65, with 64610 data points per functional.

## Key numbers

- Critical proton fractions for neutron drip: \(Y_p^\mathrm{drip} \approx 0.26\)–\(0.31\), where neutron gas emerges outside of nuclei at \(Y_p < Y_p^\mathrm{drip}\)
- Droplet phase occurs at \(n_\mathrm{b} \lesssim 0.015\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), with more exotic structures (rods, slabs, tubes, bubbles) appearing sequentially as density increases
- Maximum uncertainties in EOS from functional choice peak at \(n_\mathrm{b} \approx 0.02\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) within the range \(10^{-5} \lesssim n_\mathrm{b} \lesssim 0.1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\)
- Pressure ranges 16 orders of magnitude from \(10^{-12}\ \mathrm{MeV/fm}^{3}\) to \(10^{4}\ \mathrm{MeV/fm}^{3}\) as \(n_\mathrm{b}\) increases from \(10^{-8}\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) to \(2\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) (for DD-LZ1 functional)
- For the droplet phase at \(n_\mathrm{b} \lesssim 10^{-4}\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), proton numbers are \(N_p \approx 33\) at \(Y_p \lesssim 0.3\) and drop to \(\sim 20\) at \(Y_p \approx 0.6\); droplet radii are \(R_\mathrm{d} \approx 6.2\) fm at \(Y_p \lesssim 0.3\) and drop to \(\sim 4.8\) fm at \(Y_p \approx 0.6\) (for DD-LZ1 functional)
- Uniform phase appears at densities larger than \(\sim 0.1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\)
- Saturation properties of nuclear matter across the 13 functionals include: binding energy \(B \approx -16\) MeV, incompressibility \(K = 240 \pm 20\) MeV, symmetry energy \(S = 31.7 \pm 3.2\) MeV, and its slope \(L = 58.7 \pm 28.1\) MeV (from literature, cited as constraints)

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of nuclear pasta phases in cold dense stellar matter using RMF models, following the approach of Maruyama (2005), Avancini (2008, 2009), and Gupta (2013) but with a larger set of 13 modern relativistic density functionals that satisfy the two-solar-mass neutron star constraint. The resulting EOS tables covering the full density and proton fraction ranges are intended for use in simulations of core-collapse supernovae, binary neutron star mergers, and neutron star asteroseismology, where the uncertainty band from different functionals can be further constrained by multimessenger observations from LIGO/Virgo/KAGRA, the upcoming Cosmic Explorer, Einstein Telescope, and NEMO.
