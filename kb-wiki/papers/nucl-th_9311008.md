# Deep Inelastic Scattering from off-Shell Nucleons

> We derive the general structure of the hadronic tensor required to describe deep-inelastic scattering from an off-shell nucleon within a covariant formalism. Of the large number of possible off-shell structure functions we find that only three contribute in the Bjorken limit. In our approach the usual ambiguities encountered when discussing problems related to off-shellness in deep-inelastic scattering are not present. The formulation therefore provides a clear framework within which one can discuss the various approximations and assumptions which have been used in earlier work. As examples, we investigate scattering from the deuteron, nuclear matter and dressed nucleons. The results of the full calculation are compared with those where various aspects of the off-shell structure are neglected, as well as with those of the convolution model.

## Key claim

The paper derives the general structure of the hadronic tensor for deep-inelastic scattering from an off-shell nucleon within a covariant formalism, finding that only three of the fourteen possible off-shell structure functions contribute in the Bjorken limit; however, the authors show that the conventional convolution model for nuclear structure functions cannot be derived from this formalism without additional assumptions that are generally not satisfied.

## Method

The authors constructed the most general 4x4 matrix off-shell nucleon tensor \(\chi_{\mu\nu}(p,q)\) consistent with hermiticity, covariance, parity, and time-reversal invariance, expressed in terms of fourteen scalar functions. They then used the covariant quark-parton model to separate the hard, calculable part of the forward Compton amplitude from the soft, non-perturbative component, and evaluated the scaling behavior of all structure functions in the Bjorken limit. The formalism was applied to calculate the transverse structure function of composite targets by integrating the off-shell tensor over nucleon momentum, traced with target-constituent vertex functions.

## Key numbers

* Only 3 of 14 possible off-shell structure functions contribute in the Bjorken limit: \(\chi^0_T\), \(\chi^1_T\), and \(\chi^2_T\) are of order 1, while all other \(\chi^i\) are of order \(1/\nu\).
* For a scalar quark-nucleon vertex of the form \({\cal V}^S = I\ \Phi_1^S\), only \(f_7 = (\Phi_1^S)^2\ \delta([p-k]^2-m_S^2)\) contributes to the off-shell functions.
* For a vector vertex of the form \({\cal V}^V_{\alpha} = \gamma_{\alpha} \Phi_1^V\), the relations are \(f_3 = -f_4 = -f_5 = f_6 = -2 f_7 / m_V^2 = -2 (\Phi_1^V)^2\ \delta([p-k]^2-m_V^2) / m_V^2\).
* The large-\(x\) behaviour of the nucleon structure function requires the scalar vertex function to behave as \(1/k^2\) to obtain \(W^N_T \sim (1-x)^3\), and the vector vertex function to behave as \((k^2)^{-5/2}\) to obtain the valence \(d_V/u_V\) ratio \(\sim (1-x)\).

## Context

This work extends earlier partial attempts to account for off-shell effects in nuclear deep-inelastic scattering, such as the "off-shell kinematics -- on-shell dynamics" scheme of Kusno and Moravcsik, the approach of Bodek and Ritchie, the ansatz of Dunne and Thomas, and the dynamical rescaling model of Close, Roberts and Ross (1983) and Nachtmann and Pirner (1984). The authors show that all previous derivations of the convolution model, including those by Jaffe (1985), Jung and Miller (1988), and Mulders (1992), relied on assumptions about the off-shell tensor that are not generally valid. The formalism provides a framework for calculating nuclear structure functions that avoids the ambiguities of earlier approaches and enables systematic comparison of exact results with approximations that neglect various aspects of off-shell structure.
