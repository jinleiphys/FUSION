# $α+{}^2n+{}^2n$ 3-body cluster structures and dineutron breaking in ${}^8 \mathrm{He}$

> The $0_2^+$ state of ${}^8\mathrm{He}$ has been discovered by a recent experiment, which suggested a developed cluster structure of spatially correlated neutron pairs, called "dineutrons" (${}^2n$). We aim to investigate the structure of ${}^8\mathrm{He}(0^+_1)$ and ${}^8\mathrm{He}(0^+_2)$ to clarify the monopole excitation mode in ${}^8\mathrm{He}$ system while focusing on the $α+{}^2n+{}^2n$ cluster structures and the breaking of ${}^2n$ clusters. We apply a microscopic cluster model with the generator coordinate method for the $α+{}^2n+{}^2n$ cluster and ${}^6\mathrm{He}+{}^2n$ cluster dynamics. The $p_{3/2}$-closure component, which is induced by the spin-orbit force, is also incorporated. The present calculation reasonably reproduces the experimental data of the properties of ${}^8\mathrm{He}$, such as $2n$ and $4n$ separation energies, energy spectra, and radii. The spatially developed cluster structure of the $0_2^+$ state is obtained. The $0_1^+$ and $0_2^+$ states have dominant components of $α+{}^2n+{}^2n$ 3-body cluster, but contain significant ${}^2n$ breaking components which contribute to the energy gain and size shrinking of the ${}^8\mathrm{He}$ system. The monopole excitation in ${}^8\mathrm{He}$ is regarded as a radial excitation, which is similar to that in ${}^{12}\mathrm{C}$. The $α+{}^2n+{}^2n$ cluster structures play a dominant role in ${}^8\mathrm{He}(0_{1,2}^+)$, and the mixing of the dineutron breaking contributes to significant structural effects.

## Key claim

The $0_1^+$ and $0_2^+$ states of ${}^8\mathrm{He}$ are dominated by $\alpha+{}^2n+{}^2n$ 3-body cluster structures, but contain significant dineutron breaking components (16% in the ground state and 7% in the $0_2^+$ state) that contribute to energy gain and size shrinking, with the monopole excitation being a radial excitation analogous to that in ${}^{12}\mathrm{C}$.

## Method

The authors applied a microscopic cluster model using the generator coordinate method (GCM) for $\alpha+{}^2n+{}^2n$ 3-body cluster and ${}^6\mathrm{He}+{}^2n$ 2-body cluster dynamics, incorporating the $p_{3/2}$-closure shell-model configuration via the anti-symmetrized quasicluster model (AQCM). Wave functions were expressed as linear combinations of Brink-Bloch wave functions projected onto $J^\pi=0^+$ states, with 485 sets of hyperspherical coordinates for 3-body configurations and distance parameters from 1 to 12 fm for 2-body configurations. The Hamiltonian used Volkov No.2 central force with $M=0.58$ and the spin-orbit part of the G3RS interaction with $u_1=-u_2=1600$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of ${}^8\mathrm{He}(0_1^+)$: 29.44 MeV (experiment: 31.60 MeV)
- $2n$ separation energy of ${}^8\mathrm{He}$: 1.47 MeV (experiment: 2.14 MeV)
- $4n$ separation energy of ${}^8\mathrm{He}$: 1.83 MeV (experiment: 3.12 MeV)
- Excitation energy $E_x(0_2^+)$ of ${}^8\mathrm{He}$: 5.20 MeV (experiment: 6.66(6) MeV)
- Matter radius $r_m$ of ${}^8\mathrm{He}(0_1^+)$: 2.40 fm (experiment: 2.45(7) fm)
- Matter radius $r_m$ of ${}^8\mathrm{He}(0_2^+)$: 3.91 fm
- Neutron radius $r_n$ of ${}^8\mathrm{He}(0_1^+)$: 2.55 fm (experiment: 2.69(4) fm)
- Neutron radius $r_n$ of ${}^8\mathrm{He}(0_2^+)$: 4.25 fm
- IS0 transition matrix element $M$(IS0) for ${}^8\mathrm{He}$ $0_1^+$-$0_2^+$: 15.5 fm$^2$ (experiment: $11^{+1.8}_{-2.3}$ fm$^2$)
- $\alpha+{}^2n+{}^2n$ 3-body cluster component proportion: 84.1% in ${}^8\mathrm{He}(0_1^+)$, 92.6% in ${}^8\mathrm{He}(0_2^+)$
- SM $(p_{3/2})^4$ component proportion: 48.7% in ${}^8\mathrm{He}(0_1^+)$, 8.6% in ${}^8\mathrm{He}(0_2^+)$
- ${}^6\mathrm{He}+{}^2n$ 2-body cluster component proportion: 88.3% in ${}^8\mathrm{He}(0_1^+)$, 58.3% in ${}^8\mathrm{He}(0_2^+)$
- Energy gain from mixing SM configuration into $\alpha+{}^2n+{}^2n$: 1.7 MeV for ground state, 0.78 MeV for $0_2^+$ state
- Relative energy from $\alpha+4n$ threshold for $0_2^+$ state: 3.37 MeV (experiment: 3.54 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the study of dineutron correlations in neutron-rich nuclei, following experimental discovery of the ${}^8\mathrm{He}(0_2^+)$ state by Yang (2023) which suggested developed cluster structure of spatially correlated neutron pairs. The calculation builds on earlier theoretical studies using the anti-symmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) model by Kanada-En'yo (2007) and the ${}^2n$ condensation model by Kobayashi (2013), which predicted the second $0^+$ state and suggested $\alpha+{}^2n+{}^2n$ 3-body cluster gas structure analogous to the $3\alpha$ cluster gas of ${}^{12}\mathrm{C}(0_2^+)$ as described by Yamada (2008). The present work enables quantitative understanding of how dineutron breaking due to spin-orbit interaction affects binding energies and radii, and establishes the monopole excitation in ${}^8\mathrm{He}$ as a radial excitation similar to that in ${}^{12}\mathrm{C}$.
