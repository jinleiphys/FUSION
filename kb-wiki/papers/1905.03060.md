# Calculated Inter-band B(E2)'s in the p-f Shell

> Whereas previous works for B(E2)'s in the even-even Ti isotopes focused on yrast transitions we here also consider inter-band transitions to a second group i.e. states like 1_{1} , 2_{2}, 3_{1} , 4_{2} , 5_{1}e.t.c.. We focus on variations from one even-even Ti isotope to the next. We make a qualitative comparison with similar transitions in a heavier deformed nucleus.

## Key claim

While the abstract states that the paper considers inter-band B(E2) transitions in even-even Ti isotopes, the actual results show that these inter-band transitions are generally quite small compared to yrast transitions, with no clear trend as a function of neutron number, and the two interactions (FPD6 and GX1A) often disagree on which specific states carry the strength.

## Method

Shell model calculations were performed using the code NUSHELLX@MSU with the GX1A and FPD6 interactions in the p-f shell, using effective charges of 1.5 for protons and 0.5 for neutrons. The authors computed B(E2) values for transitions within the yrast band, between the yrast band and a second group of states (0_2, 1_1, 2_2, 3_1, 4_2, 5_1, etc.), and within the second band for the nuclei 44Ti, 46Ti, 48Ti, 50Ti, and 48Cr. Results were compared with rotational model predictions and with experimental data on 98Sr from Clement et al.

## Key numbers

- B(E2, 0_1 to 2_1) in 48Cr: 1570 e^2 fm^4 (FPD6) and 1254 e^2 fm^4 (GX1A)
- B(E2, 0_1 to 2_2) in 48Ti: 108.40 e^2 fm^4 (FPD6) and 99.1 e^2 fm^4 (GX1A)
- B(E2, 2_2 to 4_2) in 44Ti: 118.0 e^2 fm^4 (FPD6); in 48Ti: 134.0 e^2 fm^4 (FPD6)
- B(E2, 2_2 to 4_2) in 46Ti: 14.90 e^2 fm^4 (FPD6); in 50Ti: 18.8 e^2 fm^4 (FPD6)
- Ratio B(E2, 14_1 to 16_1)/B(E2, 0_1 to 2_1) in 48Cr with FPD6: 78.6/1570 = 0.050, compared to the rotational model prediction of 0.400
- B(E2, 2_2 to 3_1) in 48Cr: 840 e^2 fm^4 (FPD6) and 11.2 e^2 fm^4 (GX1A)
- Static quadrupole moment Q(2_1) in 48Cr: -35.5 e fm^2 (FPD6) and -30.8 e fm^2 (GX1A)
- Static quadrupole moment Q(2_2) in 48Cr: +36.98 e fm^2 (FPD6) and +21.9 e fm^2 (GX1A)
- In 46Ti with FPD6, 91% of the B(E2) strength from the ground state to 2+ states goes to the first 2+ state (780.5 e^2 fm^4 out of sum over 50 states of 875.9 e^2 fm^4)

## Context

This work extends previous shell model studies of yrast B(E2) transitions in even-even Ti isotopes by Robinson et al. (2015) to include inter-band transitions to a second group of states. The calculations use the same framework as Honma et al. for the GX1A and FPD6 interactions. The results show that the Ti isotopes are not well described by the rotational model of Bohr and Mottelson, as the B(E2) ratios deviate strongly from rotational predictions (e.g., a factor of 8 difference for 48Cr at high spin). The paper enables future experimental work to test these predictions and highlights the need for more sophisticated shell model calculations in this mass region.
