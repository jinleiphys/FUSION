# The u-d quark mass difference and nuclear charge symmetry breaking

> The group theoretical analysis of the Coleman-Glashow tadpole picture of "meson-mixing" is quantitatively reproduced by the u-d constituent quark mass difference in quantum loop calculations of the self-energies of mesons. This demonstration that the Coleman-Glashow scales can be directly calculated from the constituent $u - d$ quark mass difference finishes the link between charge symmetry breaking in low energy nuclear physics and the $u - d$ quark mass difference in particle physics.

# The u-d quark mass difference and nuclear charge symmetry breaking

## Key claim

The Coleman-Glashow group-theoretical tadpole picture of meson-mixing, which provides a universal scale of approximately -5200 MeV² for ΔI=1 electromagnetic transitions, can be quantitatively reproduced by quantum loop calculations using a constituent u-d quark mass difference of about 4 MeV, thereby completing the link between charge symmetry breaking in low-energy nuclear physics and the fundamental quark mass difference in particle physics.

## Method

The author evaluates quantum loop diagrams for meson self-energies using a constituent quark model with a linear sigma model Lagrangian, computing the difference between u and d quark contributions to bubble graphs and tadpole graphs. For pseudoscalar mesons, the kaon mass-squared difference is calculated from quark loop graphs that include both u-d quark bubble contributions and u-d quark loops that simulate an a₀ tadpole with a₀KK coupling. For vector mesons, the ρ⁰-ω transition amplitude is computed from the difference of u-d quark polarization functions in the vector meson propagator, combined with the a₀ tadpole term and a small photon-exchange contribution evaluated via vector meson dominance.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass difference: m_d - m_u ≈ 4 MeV, derived from the K⁰-K⁺ mass difference (497.67 MeV - 493.68 MeV = 3.99 MeV)
- Kaon tadpole scale from quark loops: (Δm²_K)_qk loops ≈ -5184 MeV², reproducing the soft Coleman-Glashow kaon tadpole scale of -5220 MeV²
- π⁰-η_NS transition amplitude from quark loops: (H_πη_NS)_qk loops ≈ -5231 MeV², matching the group-theoretical value of -5220 MeV²
- ρ⁰-ω transition amplitude from quark loops: (H_ρω)_qk loops ≈ -5127 MeV², which combined with the photon-exchange term (644 MeV²) gives (H_em)_ρω ≈ -4483 MeV², near the empirical value of -4520 ± 50 MeV²
- Baryon tadpole scale: (H³_tad)_p-n ≈ -2.5 MeV, with the mass² version approximately -5700 MeV², consistent with the universal meson scale

## Context

This work extends the Coleman-Glashow tadpole picture (Coleman and Glashow, 1964) by showing that the group-theoretical universal scale for ΔI=1 electromagnetic transitions can be derived from constituent quark loops rather than requiring the existence of scalar mesons. The analysis connects to earlier evaluations of baryon electromagnetic mass splittings by Bucella (1969) and a lattice QCD calculation (1997), and to the description of nuclear charge asymmetry via off-diagonal transitions π⁰-η_NS and ρ⁰-ω in single meson exchange diagrams. The results enable a consistent quark-level understanding of charge symmetry breaking in both the NN interaction and hypernuclear systems, though the experimental status of ΛN charge asymmetry remains problematic.
