# Thermal width and gluo-dissociation of quarkonium in pNRQCD

> The thermal width of heavy-quarkonium bound states in a quark-gluon plasma has been recently derived in an effective field theory approach. Two phenomena contribute to the width: the Landau damping phenomenon and the break-up of a colour-singlet bound state into a colour-octet heavy quark-antiquark pair by absorption of a thermal gluon. In the paper, we investigate the relation between the singlet-to-octet thermal break-up and the so-called gluo-dissociation, a mechanism for quarkonium dissociation widely used in phenomenological approaches. The gluo-dissociation thermal width is obtained by convoluting the gluon thermal distribution with the cross section of a gluon and a 1S quarkonium state to a colour octet quark-antiquark state in vacuum, a cross section that at leading order, but neglecting colour-octet effects, was computed long ago by Bhanot and Peskin. We will, first, show that the effective field theory framework provides a natural derivation of the gluo-dissociation factorization formula at leading order, which is, indeed, the singlet-to-octet thermal break-up expression. Second, the singlet-to-octet thermal break-up expression will allow us to improve the Bhanot--Peskin cross section by including the contribution of the octet potential, which amounts to include final-state interactions between the heavy quark and antiquark. Finally, we will quantify the effects due to final-state interactions on the gluo-dissociation cross section and on the quarkonium thermal width.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the gluo-dissociation thermal width for quarkonium, previously used in phenomenological approaches, coincides at leading order with the singlet-to-octet thermal break-up width derived from the effective field theory pNRQCD, but only under the specific scale hierarchy \( m \gg mv \sim m\alpha_s \gg T \sim mv^2 \sim m\alpha_s^2 \gg m_D, \Lambda_{\rm QCD} \). The authors improve the Bhanot-Peskin cross section by including colour-octet final-state interactions, which were neglected in the original calculation.

## Method

The authors work within potential non-relativistic QCD (pNRQCD) at finite temperature, using the real-time formalism. They compute the singlet-to-octet break-up thermal width from the imaginary part of the leading heavy-quarkonium self-energy diagram, which involves a chromoelectric dipole interaction between the colour-singlet bound state and a thermal gluon. The matrix element is evaluated by inserting a complete set of colour-octet scattering states, including the repulsive octet Coulomb potential, and the resulting cross section is compared to the Bhanot-Peskin result obtained in the large-\( N_c \) limit where the octet potential vanishes.

## Key numbers

- The Bhanot-Peskin gluo-dissociation cross section for a 1S state is \( \sigma_{1S,\mathrm{BP}}(\mathbf{q}) = \frac{2^9 \pi \alpha_s}{9} \frac{\epsilon_{1,\mathrm{BP}}^{5/2}}{m} \frac{(\mathbf{q} - \epsilon_{1,\mathrm{BP}})^{3/2}}{\mathbf{q}^5} \), where \( \epsilon_{1,\mathrm{BP}} = 9 m \alpha_s^2 / 16 \) is the binding energy in the large-\( N_c \) limit.
- The full cross section including octet effects, Eq. (4.5), reduces to the Bhanot-Peskin result in the \( N_c \to \infty \) limit, with the asymptotic high-momentum ratio \( \sigma_{1S} / (16 \sigma_{1S,\mathrm{BP}}) \) approaching \( (17/16)^2 \approx 1.13 \) for \( N_c = 3 \).
- The asymptotic thermal width for \( T \gg |E_1| \) is given by \( \frac{\Gamma_{1S}}{E_1^2 C_F \alpha_s / m} = \frac{16}{3} \left[ (2+\rho)^2 \frac{T}{|E_1|} - (2+\rho)^2 (3+\rho) \right] + \mathcal{O}(|E_1|/T) \), where \( \rho = 1/(N_c^2 - 1) = 1/8 \) for \( N_c = 3 \).
- The full width \( \Gamma_{1S} \) approaches its asymptotic linear regime starting from \( T \approx 4 |E_1| \), where it is still larger than the asymptotic value by about a factor of 2.7.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory treatment of quarkonium at finite temperature from Brambilla et al. (2008, 2010) by explicitly connecting the singlet-to-octet break-up width to the phenomenological gluo-dissociation mechanism. It improves the original cross section calculation by Bhanot and Peskin (1979), which neglected colour-octet final-state interactions by taking the large-\( N_c \) limit. The results enable more accurate phenomenological studies of quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for the \( \Upsilon(1S) \) state at LHC energies, and clarify that the simple convolution formula for the thermal width breaks down beyond leading order in the EFT power counting.
