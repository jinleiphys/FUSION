# Limit Temperatures for Meson and Diquark Resonances in a Strongly Interacting Quark Matter

> We investigate mesons and diquarks as resonant states above chiral critical temperature $T_c$ in flavor SU(2) Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. For each kind of resonance, we solve the pole equation for the resonant mass in the complex energy plane, and find an ultimate temperature where the pole starts to disappear. The phase diagram including these limit temperatures in $T-μ$ plane is obtained. The maximum limit temperature at $μ=0$ is approximately two times $T_c$.

## Key claim

Mesons and diquarks exist as resonant states above the chiral critical temperature \(T_c\) in strongly interacting quark matter, but each resonance has a finite limit temperature \(T_r\) where its pole disappears; the maximum limit temperature at zero chemical potential is approximately two times \(T_c\), and this result depends on the diquark coupling strength and the presence of color superconductivity.

## Method

The authors used the flavor SU(2) Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in mean-field approximation for quarks and random phase approximation for mesons and diquarks. They solved the complex pole equations for the resonant masses and widths of mesons and diquarks above the chiral critical temperature, including both normal phase and color superconductivity phase, and extracted the ultimate temperature where each pole vanishes. The model parameters were fixed by fitting the constituent quark mass and pion decay constant in vacuum, with the diquark coupling varied between \(G_d^{min} \sim 0.8 G_s\) and \(G_d^{max} = 1.5 G_s\).

## Key numbers

- Maximum limit temperature at \(\mu=0\) is approximately two times the chiral critical temperature \(T_c(0)\).
- Momentum cutoff \(\Lambda = 0.65\) GeV and scalar coupling \(G_s = 5.01\) (GeV)\(^{-2}\) fixed from vacuum properties.
- Diquark coupling range: \(G_d^{min} \sim 0.8 G_s\) and \(G_d^{max} = 1.5 G_s\).
- At the critical temperature \(T_c\), the meson mass starts from \(M_m(T_c) = 0\).

## Context

This work extends earlier NJL studies of meson resonances above \(T_c\) (Hufner, Zhuang) by including diquark resonances and color superconductivity. It complements lattice calculations of meson masses above \(T_c\) (Brown) and phenomenological estimates of multibody bound states (Liao). The results provide a phase diagram of resonant states in the temperature–chemical potential plane, showing that meson and heavy diquark resonances survive in color superconductivity phase and that all resonances melt above the maximum limit temperature, leaving only quarks.
