# Double-scattering mechanism in exclusive $A A \to A A ρ^0 ρ^0$ reaction at ultrarelativistic collisions

> We calculate, for the first time, differential distributions for double $ρ^0$ meson production in exclusive ultraperipheral, ultrarelativistic collisions via a double scattering mechanism. The calculations are done in impact parameter space. The cross section for $γA \to ρ^0 A$ is parametrized based on an existing calculation. Smearing of $ρ^0$ masses is taken into account. The results of our calculations are compared at the RHIC energy to the contribution of the two-photon mechanism discussed previously in the literature. The cross section for the double scattering mechanism is found to be an order of magnitude larger at $M_{ρρ} <$ 2 GeV and more than two orders of magnitude at $M_{ρρ} >$ 3 GeV, than that for the photon-photon mechanism. Compared to the two-photon mechanism the double scattering mechanism populates somewhat larger $ρ^0 ρ^0$ invariant masses and larger rapidity distances between the two $ρ^0$ mesons and gives a significant contribution to the $A A \to A A π^+ π^- π^+ π^-$ reaction. Some observables related to charged pions are presented too. We compare the results of our calculation with the STAR collaboration results on four charged pion production. While the shape in invariant mass of the four-pion system is very similar to the measured one, the predicted cross section constitutes only 20% of the measured one. We discuss a possibility of identifying the double scattering mechanism at the LHC.

## Key claim

The double-scattering mechanism for exclusive $\rho^0 \rho^0$ production in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions is calculated for the first time and found to dominate over the previously studied photon-photon mechanism, though the predicted cross section for four-charged-pion final states constitutes only 20% of the STAR collaboration measurement, indicating that additional mechanisms may be needed to explain the data.

## Method

The authors calculate the double-scattering cross section in impact-parameter space using a probabilistic formula that convolves the probability densities for single $\rho^0$ photoproduction from each nucleus, with a survival factor for nuclear breakup. The single $\rho^0$ photoproduction cross section on nuclei is parametrized using a constant forward differential cross section of 420 mb/GeV² at RHIC and 450 mb/GeV² at LHC, combined with the nuclear form factor. The $\rho^0$ mass smearing is included using a Breit-Wigner amplitude with a mass-dependent width, and the $\rho^0 \to \pi^+ \pi^-$ decay is simulated with a transversely polarized decay angular distribution.

## Key numbers

- Double-scattering cross section for $\rho^0 \rho^0$ production at RHIC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, Au+Au): 1.5 mb (fixed mass) and 1.55 mb (smeared mass)
- Single $\rho^0$ production cross section at RHIC: 596 mb
- Single $\rho^0$ production cross section at LHC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.5$ TeV, Pb+Pb): 4000 mb
- Single $\rho^0$ production cross section at LHC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.5$ TeV, Pb+Pb): 4795 mb
- Double-scattering cross section at LHC ($\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.5$ TeV): 15.25 mb
- Photon-photon VDM-Regge cross section at RHIC: 7.5 × 10⁻³ mb
- Photon-photon low-energy bump cross section at RHIC: 95 × 10⁻³ mb
- Double-scattering cross section at RHIC with $|\eta_\pi| < 1$: 0.15 mb
- The double-scattering cross section is an order of magnitude larger than the photon-photon mechanism at $M_{\rho\rho} < 2$ GeV and more than two orders of magnitude larger at $M_{\rho\rho} > 3$ GeV

## Context

This work extends the study of exclusive vector meson pair production in ultraperipheral collisions beyond the photon-photon fusion mechanism previously calculated by the same authors (KSS2009) and by others (GM2003, GMS2006). The double-scattering mechanism was only discussed in a probabilistic formula by Klein and Nystrand (1999) for multiple vector meson production, but no differential distributions were calculated. The results enable experimental identification of the double-scattering mechanism at RHIC and LHC through distinct signatures in rapidity correlations and invariant mass distributions, and suggest that the discrepancy with STAR data on four-pion production requires further investigation of additional production mechanisms.
