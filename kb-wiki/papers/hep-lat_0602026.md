# Breakdown of staggered fermions at nonzero chemical potential

> The staggered fermion determinant is complex when the quark chemical potential mu is nonzero. Its fourth root, used in simulations with dynamical fermions, will have phase ambiguities that become acute when Re mu is sufficiently large. We show how to resolve these ambiguities, but our prescription only works very close to the continuum limit. We argue that this regime is far from current capabilities. Other procedures require being even closer to the continuum limit, or fail altogether, because of unphysical discontinuities in the measure. At zero temperature the breakdown is expected when Re mu is greater than approximately half the pion mass. Estimates of the location of the breakdown at nonzero temperature are less certain.

## Key claim

The staggered fermion determinant's fourth root, used to represent a single fermion flavor at nonzero chemical potential, can in principle be defined unambiguously by grouping eigenvalues into taste quartets, but this prescription only works very close to the continuum limit and requires the volume to be limited such that the product a*sqrt(V)*Lambda^3 remains small; the authors argue that this regime is far from current capabilities.

## Method

The authors analyze the complex eigenvalue spectrum of the staggered fermion matrix D+m at nonzero Re mu, showing that near the continuum limit the infrared eigenvalues form approximately degenerate quartets due to taste symmetry, with splittings of O(a*Lambda^2). They propose taking the fourth root of each quartet's product using the phase of the quartet center, while for ultraviolet eigenvalues near the imaginary axis the fourth root phase is taken near +/- pi/8. They then estimate the systematic errors from configurations where quartets frame the origin or are bisected by a cut, and illustrate the breakdown of quartet structure with a two-dimensional QCD model where a gauge field lifts the fourfold degeneracy and transports a single eigenvalue around the origin.

## Key numbers

- The splitting within a quartet of eigenvalues is O(a*Lambda^2), where a is the lattice spacing and Lambda is the QCD scale.
- The fraction of quenched configurations containing an eigenvalue quartet that frames the origin is O(a^2*V*Lambda^6).
- The systematic error from the quartet-prescription is O[(a*sqrt(V)*Lambda^3)^3].
- The systematic error from taking the fourth root of each eigenvalue individually (ignoring quartets) is O(a*V*Lambda^5).
- For the simulation parameters of Fodor and Katz (2004), with a=1/4 and volume (12/4)^3=27, the figure of merit a*sqrt(V)*Lambda^3 equals 1.3, indicating no quartet structure.
- The critical chemical potential at nonzero temperature is estimated at mu_c approximately 120 MeV, well above m_pi/2 for realistic light-quark masses.

## Context

This paper extends the debate on the validity of the staggered fermion fourth-root procedure, previously established for mu=0 by Adams (2004), Bernard et al. (2006), and others, to the case of nonzero chemical potential. It directly addresses the methods used by Fodor and Katz (2002, 2004) for reweighting at nonzero mu, and by Allton et al. (2002, 2003, 2005) for multiparameter reweighting. The work shows that the analytic continuation from imaginary mu (de Forcrand and Philipsen, 2002) avoids these fourth-root issues only if the continuum extrapolation is done before the analytic continuation. The analysis enables a quantitative criterion (a*sqrt(V)*Lambda^3 small) for when staggered fermion simulations at nonzero mu might become reliable, and suggests that current simulations are far from this regime.
