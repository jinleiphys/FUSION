# The state of cold quark matter: a model-independent view

> From a model-independent point of view, we address the possibility that quark clustering could occur in cold quark matter at realistic baryon densities because of the likely strong coupling between quarks in compact stars.

## Key claim

Cold quark matter at realistic baryon densities (a few times nuclear density) may exist in a solid state with quark clustering rather than in a color-superconducting state, provided the QCD coupling constant \(\alpha_s\) is greater than about 1 at the relevant energy scale, making the interaction energy between quarks comparable to or larger than the chemical potential.

## Method

The author estimates the quark chemical potential (\(\mu \sim 0.4\) GeV) and typical inter-quark separation (\(\sim 0.9\) fm) for three-flavor quark matter at \(\rho = 3\rho_0\). Using a non-perturbative Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) parametrization from Fischer et al., the coupling \(\alpha_s\) at momentum scale \(p^2 \simeq 0.16\) GeV\(^2\) is found to be close to 3. Comparing the resulting cluster binding energy \(E_q \sim 300\alpha_s^2\) MeV with the chemical potential leads to the conclusion that quark clusters (e.g., 18-quark \(Q_\alpha\)-clusters) should form, analogous to molecular clustering in water.

## Key numbers

- Average density of a \(1.4 M_\odot\) pulsar with 10 km radius: \(2.4\rho_0\) (where \(\rho_0\) is nuclear density).
- Critical density to break nucleons: \(n_c \simeq 1.5 r_1^{-3} \rho_0 \approx 2\rho_0\) for nucleon radius \(r_n = 0.9\) fm.
- Quark number density at \(\rho = 3\rho_0\): \(n_u \simeq n_d \simeq n_s \sim 0.48\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Typical inter-quark separation: \(l_Q \sim 0.9\) fm.
- Non-relativistic quark chemical potential: \(\mu_u^{\rm NR} \approx 380\) MeV.
- Extremely relativistic quark chemical potential: \(\mu_u^{\rm ER} \approx 480\) MeV.
- Coupling from DSE at \(p^2 = 0.16\) GeV\(^2\): \(\alpha_s(0) = 2.972\), with \(\alpha_s > 2\) at the relevant scale.
- Cluster binding energy estimate: \(E_q \sim 300\alpha_s^2\) MeV, exceeding \(\sim 400\) MeV if \(\alpha_s > 1\).
- Distance between \(Q_\alpha\)-clusters at \(\rho = 3\rho_0\): \(l_{Q_\alpha} \simeq 2\) fm.
- Kinetic energy to localize clusters: \(E_{\rm cluster} \sim 1\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the idea of quark clustering (Xu, 2003) as an alternative to the color superconductivity (CSC) scenario for cold dense quark matter, which is typically derived from perturbative QCD at extremely high densities. The author argues that non-perturbative effects, as modeled by Dyson-Schwinger equations (Fischer et al.), make strong coupling plausible at densities relevant to compact stars (\(\sim 3\rho_0\)). The proposed solid quark star model could explain pulsar glitches, precession, magnetar bursts, and the binding energy problem for radio pulsars (Xu, Qiao, and Zhang, 1999), and may enable tests using strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma (sQGP) from heavy-ion colliders.
