# Dispersion Relation of the Rho-Meson at Finite Temperature and Density

> The rho-meson mass shift, width broadening, and spectral density at finite temperature and nucleon density are estimated using a general formula which relates the self-energy to the real and imaginary parts of the forward scattering amplitude on the constituents of the medium. We saturate the scattering amplitude at low energies with resonances, while at high energies a Regge approach is taken in combination with vector meson dominance; experimental data is used wherever possible. The main result is that the rho-meson becomes increasingly broad with increasing nucleon density. The spectral density is suppressed in the resonance region 600 < M < 900 MeV and enhanced in the subresonance region M < 600 MeV.

## Key claim

The rho-meson becomes increasingly broad with increasing nucleon density, with pions having very little effect; the spectral density is suppressed in the resonance region 600 < M < 900 MeV and enhanced in the subresonance region M < 600 MeV, but these results are only semiquantitative because the description of nuclear matter as a noninteracting gas of nucleons and pions cannot be considered a very good one.

## Method

The authors used a general formula relating the rho-meson self-energy to the real and imaginary parts of the forward scattering amplitude on pions and nucleons in the medium, saturating the low-energy scattering amplitude with resonances and using a combination of vector meson dominance (VMD) and Regge theory at high energies. The self-energy was evaluated on the rho-meson mass shell for real momentum up to about 1 GeV/c, with the width defined in the rest frame of the thermal system rather than the rho-meson rest frame. The calculations used a Fermi-Dirac distribution for nucleons at temperatures of 100 and 150 MeV and nucleon densities of 0, 1, and 2 times normal nuclear matter density (0.155 nucleons/fm³).

## Key numbers

- Vacuum width of the rho-meson: 150 MeV
- Normal nuclear matter density: 0.155 nucleons/fm³
- At zero momentum and 1 times nuclear density, the mass shift is about 15 MeV; at momentum 1 GeV/c it reaches about 55 MeV
- At 2 times nuclear density, the mass shift is about 30 MeV at zero momentum and about 110 MeV at 1 GeV/c
- The rho-meson width is about 240 MeV at 1 times nuclear density and about 370 MeV at 2 times nuclear density
- Nucleon chemical potentials: 745 and 820 MeV for densities of 1 and 2 times normal at T = 100 MeV; 540 and 645 MeV for densities of 1 and 2 times normal at T = 150 MeV
- The mass shift from pions alone is negligible, on the order of 1 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier model-independent approaches to in-medium hadron properties (Eletsky and Ioffe, 1997; Kapusta and collaborators) by using experimental scattering data rather than low-energy effective Lagrangians. The results are roughly consistent with other analyses (Wambach, 1996; Weise, 1997; Friman, 1997) and provide a direct extrapolation from zero temperature and density to nonzero values. The findings enable applications to thermal and hydrodynamic models of heavy ion collisions, particularly for interpreting dilepton production data, though the authors note that preliminary Pb-Au collision data showing the rho-peak absent at low transverse momentum appear opposite to their findings, and no conclusion can be drawn without embedding the results in a space-time evolution model.
