# Study of the Pion-Nucleon Coupling Constant Charge Dependence on the Basis of the Low-Energy Data on Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction

> We study relationship between the physical quantities that characterize pion-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon interaction on the basis of the fact that nuclear forces in the nucleon-nucleon system at low energies are mainly determined by the one-pion exchange mechanism. By making use of the recommended proton-proton low-energy scattering parameters, we obtain the following value for the charged pion-nucleon coupling constant g$_{π^{\pm }}^{2}/4π=14.55(13)$. Calculated value of this quantity is in excellent agreement with the experimental result g$_{π^{\pm }}^{2}/4π=14.52(26)$ of the Uppsala Neutron Research Group. At the same time, the obtained value of the charged pion-nucleon coupling constant differs markedly from the value of the neutral pion-nucleon coupling constant g$_{π^{0}}^{2}/4π=13.55(13)$. Thus, our results show considerable charge splitting of the pion-nucleon coupling constant.

## Key claim
The charged pion-nucleon coupling constant extracted from low-energy proton-proton scattering data, using a one-pion-exchange Yukawa potential, is g²_{π±}/4π = 14.55(13), which differs by about 7% from the neutral pion-nucleon coupling constant g²_{π⁰}/4π = 13.55(13), indicating substantial charge dependence of the coupling constant. This result agrees with the Uppsala Neutron Research Group's experimental value of 14.52(26) but contradicts the Nijmegen group's value of 13.54(5) derived from nucleon-nucleon partial-wave analysis.

## Method
The authors used the Yukawa potential (one-pion-exchange form) to describe nucleon-nucleon interaction in the ¹S₀ spin-singlet state at low energies. They determined the potential parameters (depth V₀ and range μ) by matching to the recommended purely nuclear proton-proton scattering length a_pp = -17.3(4) fm and effective range r_pp = 2.85(4) fm, then used the experimental singlet neutron-proton scattering length a_np = -23.71(2) fm to derive an equation relating the pseudovector charged and neutral pion-nucleon coupling constants. The pseudoscalar coupling constants were obtained via the standard conversion from pseudovector values using nucleon and pion masses.

## Key numbers
- Charged pion-nucleon coupling constant: g²_{π±}/4π = 14.55(13)
- Neutral pion-nucleon coupling constant: g²_{π⁰}/4π = 13.55(13) (from Bergervoet et al. (1990))
- Experimental charged coupling constant from Uppsala group: g²_{π±}/4π = 14.52(26) (Rahm et al. (1998))
- Proton-proton scattering length (purely nuclear): a_pp = -17.3(4) fm
- Proton-proton effective range: r_pp = 2.85(4) fm
- Singlet neutron-proton scattering length: a_np = -23.71(2) fm
- Neutron-proton effective range from calculation: r_np = 2.70(4) fm, in agreement with experimental value 2.70(9) fm
- Difference in pp and np scattering lengths due to charge independence breaking: Δa_CIB = 6.41 fm (about 30% in relative units)
- Difference in effective ranges: Δr_CIB = 0.15 fm
- Pseudovector neutral coupling constant: f²_{π⁰} = 0.0749(7) (from Bergervoet et al. (1990))
- Pseudovector charged coupling constant: f²_{π±} = 0.0804(7)

## Context
This work extends the approach of using one-pion-exchange potentials to relate pion-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon interactions, building on the meson field theory framework of Hulthén and Sugawara (1957) and Bohr and Mottelson (1969). The results support the larger charged coupling constant values obtained by the Uppsala Neutron Research Group (Rahm et al. (1998, 2001)) and contradict the smaller values from the Nijmegen group's partial-wave analysis (de Swart et al. (1997), Stoks et al. (1993)) and from πN scattering analyses (Arndt et al. (2004, 2006)). The findings enable a quantitative understanding of charge independence breaking in nuclear forces as arising from the 3.4% mass difference and 7% coupling constant difference between neutral and charged pions.
