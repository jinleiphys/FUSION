# P11 Resonances with Dubna-Mainz-Taipei Dynamical Model for pi-N Scattering and Pion Electromagnetic Production

> We present the results on P11 resonances obtained with Dubna-Mainz-Taipei (DMT) dynamical model for pion-nucleon scattering and pion electromagnetic production. The extracted values agree well, in general, with PDG values. One pole is found corresponding to the Roper resonance and two more resonances are definitely needed in DMT model. We further find indication for a narrow P11 resonance at around 1700 MeV with a width of around 50 MeV in both pi-N and gamma-pi reactions.

## Key claim

Using the Dubna-Mainz-Taipei (DMT) dynamical model, the authors find that three P11 resonances are needed to describe pion-nucleon scattering data, and they find indication for an additional narrow P11 resonance at around 1700 MeV with a width of about 50 MeV in both pion-nucleon and gamma-pion reactions, though this narrow resonance is not part of the DMT model itself but is introduced within the MAID2007 framework.

## Method

The DMT meson-exchange model, based on an effective chiral Lagrangian, was used to construct a potential for coupled-channel scattering equations describing pion-nucleon and eta-nucleon channels up to center-of-mass energies of 2.0 GeV. Model parameters including bare resonance masses, coupling constants, and cutoff parameters were fitted to pion-nucleon phase shifts and inelasticity parameters in all channels up to F waves. The same DMT model was then used as the main ingredient in a dynamical model for pion photo- and electroproduction, and the extracted resonance parameters were used to describe multipole data.

## Key numbers

- Bare mass of the Roper resonance P11(1440): 1612 MeV; physical mass: 1418 MeV; total width: 436 MeV; pole position: 1371 - 95i MeV
- Bare mass of P11(1710): 1798 MeV; physical mass: 1803 MeV; total width: 508 MeV; pole position: 1746 - 184i MeV
- Bare mass of P11(2100): 2196 MeV; physical mass: 2247 MeV; total width: 1020 MeV
- Narrow P11 resonance (within MAID2007): mass 1700 MeV, width 47 MeV, improves agreement with _pM_{1-}(1/2) data near photon energy E_gamma ~ 1050 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Taipei-Argonne pion-nucleon meson-exchange model (Hung, Yang, and Lee, 2001) which described scattering up to 400 MeV pion laboratory energy, by extending it to 2.0 GeV and including higher resonances and the eta-nucleon channel. The results contrast with Suzuki et al. (2010) who found two poles for the Roper resonance, whereas the DMT model finds only one pole because it does not explicitly include the pion-Delta channel. The findings also differ from the EBAC group (Kamano et al., 2010) in showing a stronger need for three P11 resonances. This work enables improved extraction of baryon resonance properties from combined analysis of pion-nucleon scattering and pion electromagnetic production data.
