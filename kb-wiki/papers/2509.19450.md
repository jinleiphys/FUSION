# Multi-Cycle PWR Modeling with DRAGON and PARCS: Implications for Nuclear Data Assessment

> Nuclear data libraries serve as the foundation for all calculations in the nuclear field. Their quality directly affects the accuracy of computations. When new nuclear data libraries are released, they must undergo validation through the use of integral experimental data. This work introduces an automated simulation pipeline that converts raw nuclear data into predictions for various reactors, significantly enhancing the validation process. This pipeline allows for the assessment of potential biases in integral measurement predictions for nuclear power plants before the release of a new nuclear data library. To achieve this, an implementation of history variables in DRAGON was developed to determine the macroscopic cross sections of the nodal code PARCS. This method has been verified for seven depletion cycles across three pressurised water reactors (PWRs) against publicly available data. The errors on the predictions are in the uncertainty range attributed to variations in nuclear data. Additionally, a methodology to assess the quality of novel nuclear data libraries against experimental measurements in PWRs is presented.

## Key claim

This work introduces an automated simulation pipeline that converts raw nuclear data into predictions for multiple PWR cycles using the open-source codes DRAGON and PARCS, with the claim that the resulting errors on predictions fall within the uncertainty range attributed to variations in nuclear data. The key qualifier is that the pipeline's validation is limited to seven depletion cycles across three PWRs against publicly available data, and the history variable implementation in DRAGON was specifically developed and verified for this purpose.

## Method

The authors implemented a conventional two-step deterministic approach: first, lattice cell calculations were performed with DRAGON (version 5.0.8 revision 2417, modified by IRSN) to generate spatially homogenized two-group macroscopic cross sections for fuel assemblies, incorporating history effects for burnable poison rod insertion and moderator density variation. Second, full-core diffusion calculations were performed with PARCS v3.4.2 to simulate multiple consecutive depletion cycles, including fuel assembly shuffling between cycles and shutdown cooling phases. The nuclear data libraries JEFF-3.1.1, JEFF-4T3, and ENDF/B-VIII.0 were processed using PyNJOY2016 into the SHEM 295-group structure for DRAGON input.

## Key numbers

- Three PWRs were modeled, encompassing seven depletion cycles total
- The SHEM 295-group structure was used for energy condensation of nuclear data
- A nonlinear weighting factor of 2.25 was chosen for the Pyrex insertion history and 1.0 for the moderator density history in the PARCS history formula
- Depletion steps of 10^{-5} MW/kgU were used as minimal steps to simulate shutdown cooling phases without simulation termination
- The energy range of raw nuclear data spans approximately 10 μeV to 20 MeV

## Context

This work extends the PhD work of Salino (IRSN) on DRAGON procedures and builds upon prior modeling efforts for Fessenheim-2 and Tihange-1 reactors using open-source models from Salino's Git repository. The pipeline is designed to enable automated validation of new nuclear data libraries (such as those from the JEFF community) against integral PWR measurements, addressing the limitation that criticality experiments (ICSBEP) and reactor physics experiments (IRPhE) fail to fully capture complex reactor behavior during fuel evolution. The approach enables future assessment of potential biases in integral measurement predictions before the release of new nuclear data libraries.
