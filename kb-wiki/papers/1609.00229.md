# Charge symmetry breaking in $Λ$ hypernuclei: updated HYP 2015 progress report

> Ongoing progress in understanding and evaluating charge symmetry breaking in $Λ$ hypernuclei is discussed in connection to recent measurements of the $_Λ^{4}{\rm H}(0^+_{\rm g.s.})$ binding energy at MAMI [A1 Collaboration: PRL 114 (2015) 232501] and of the $_Λ^{4}{\rm He}(1^+_{\rm exc})$ excitation energy at J-PARC [E13 Collaboration: PRL 115 (2015) 222501].

## Key claim

Charge symmetry breaking (CSB) in the A=4 Λ hypernuclei, as measured by the ground-state Λ separation-energy difference ΔBΛ(0+g.s.) = 233±92 keV from recent MAMI data, can be reproduced by incorporating Λ-Σ0 mixing within a schematic ΛN↔ΣN coupling potential model, and also by using the Jülich-Bonn leading-order χEFT YN interaction in four-body no-core shell model calculations, although with appreciable momentum cutoff dependence.

## Method

The author reviews and updates the theoretical understanding of CSB in Λ hypernuclei by analyzing Λ-Σ0 mixing mechanisms, where the physical Λ hyperon contains an admixture of Σ0 due to the u-d quark mass difference and QED interactions. For s-shell hypernuclei (A=4), the CSB potential is derived from the isospin Clebsch-Gordan relation connecting the CSB matrix element to the strong-interaction ΛΣ coupling matrix element, scaled by a factor of -0.0297 from the Λ-Σ0 mass mixing operator. For p-shell hypernuclei (A=7-10), the approach is extended by summing over p-shell nucleons using effective ΛΣ central-interaction matrix elements from the NSC97e model, with Coulomb corrections taken from cluster-model calculations.

## Key numbers

- ΔBΛexp(0+g.s.) = 233±92 keV for the A=4 mirror hypernuclei, derived from the MAMI measurement of BΛ(Λ4Hg.s.) = 2.157±0.077 MeV
- The Λ-Σ0 mixing OPE contribution to the 0+g.s. splitting is approximately 95 keV from central wavefunctions, with tensor contributions adding roughly another 100 keV
- For the LO χEFT YN interaction at cutoff Λ=600 MeV, the calculated ΔBΛcalc(0+g.s.) - ΔBΛcalc(1+exc) = 330±40 keV, in agreement with the measured ΔEx = 320±20 keV from J-PARC γ-ray data
- The ΛΣ coupling contributes almost 40% of the 0+g.s. → 1+exc excitation energy in NCSM calculations using the LO χEFT YN interaction
- For p-shell hypernuclei, calculated ΔBΛcalc values range from -136 keV for 10B-10Be to +49 keV for 8Be-8Li, with experimental values from emulsion data ranging from -220±250 keV to +40±60 keV
- The Σ admixture probability PΣ in p-shell hypernuclei ranges from 0.053% (A=10) to 0.23% (A=9), compared to 0.72% for the s-shell A=4 system

## Context

This work extends the author's previous CSB review (Gal 2015) and addresses the long-standing "A=4 CSB puzzle" where old emulsion data showed ΔBΛ=350±60 keV while NSC97 model calculations gave only about 100 keV. The recent MAMI measurement (A1 Collaboration, PRL 2015) and J-PARC γ-ray measurement (E13 Collaboration, PRL 2015) provide new experimental constraints. The schematic ΛΣ coupling model builds on the Dalitz-von Hippel (1964) mixing mechanism and Akaishi's G-matrix effective interactions (2000, 2002), while the NCSM calculations use the Jülich-Bonn LO χEFT YN interaction (2006). For p-shell hypernuclei, the work extends Millener's shell-model approach (2008, 2012) and addresses failures of Hiyama's cluster-model calculations (2009, 2012) that disregarded ΛΣ coupling. The results enable future application of NLO χEFT YN interactions and motivate the approved J-PARC E63 experiment to remeasure the Λ4H γ-ray transition.
