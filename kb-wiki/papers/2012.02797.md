# Retarded Green's Function from Rotating AdS Black Holes: Emergent CFT$_2$ and Viscosity

> Using the AdS/CFT correspondence we consider the retarded Green's function in the background of rotating near-extremal AdS$_4$ black holes. Following the canonical AdS/CFT dictionary into the asymptotic boundary we get a CFT$_3$ result. We also take a new route and zoom in on the near-horizon region, blow up this region and show that it yields a CFT$_2$ result. We argue that the decoupling of the near-horizon region is akin to the decoupling of the near-throat region of a D3-brane, which led to the original formulation of the AdS/CFT correspondence, thus implying that the Kerr/CFT correspondence follows as a decoupling of the standard AdS/CFT correspondence applied to rotating black holes. As a byproduct, we compute the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio for the strongly coupled boundary CFT$_3$, and find that it violates the $1 / (4 π)$ bound.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the Kerr/CFT correspondence, which yields a CFT$_2$ description from the near-horizon region of a rotating black hole, can be understood as a decoupling limit of the standard AdS/CFT correspondence applied to rotating asymptotically AdS$_4$ black holes, thereby providing a UV completion for the Kerr/CFT conjecture. As a byproduct, they find that the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio for the dual CFT$_3$ violates the $1/(4\pi)$ bound, vanishing in the extremal limit and growing quadratically with temperature at low temperatures.

## Method

The authors consider an uncharged massless scalar field in the background of a rotating near-extremal AdS$_4$ Kerr black hole and separate the wave equation into radial and angular parts using the Killing-Yano tensor. They solve the radial equation analytically in two regimes: the asymptotic boundary region (yielding a CFT$_3$ result via the standard AdS/CFT dictionary) and the near-horizon region (yielding a CFT$_2$ result after applying a near-horizon scaling limit that produces a local AdS$_3$ factor). The solutions are glued in an overlapping region ($L \ll r \ll \omega^{-1}$) to determine integration constants, and the retarded Green's function is obtained via a Wronskian-based master equation. The shear viscosity is then computed from the absorption cross section of a graviton in the 11-dimensional uplift of the AdS$_4$ black hole, using the Kubo formula.

## Key numbers

* The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio violates the $1/(4\pi)$ bound; it vanishes in the extremal limit and grows as $\eta/s \sim T^2$ for small temperatures.
* The conformal dimensions for the massless scalar near the asymptotic boundary are $\Delta_+ = 3$ and $\Delta_- = 0$ for the CFT$_3$ dual.
* The near-horizon geometry yields a CFT$_2$ with conformal weight $\hat{h} = \frac{1}{2}\left(1 + \sqrt{1 - 4\hat{C}}\right)$, where $\hat{C} \equiv -\hat{K}/k$ and $\hat{h} \to 0$ in the low-energy limit ($\omega \to 0$, $m \to 0$).
* The gluing constant $C_2$ is fixed as $C_2 = 24\sqrt{2} / [\pi (\omega L)^{7/2}]$, with $\omega L \ll 1$.

## Context

This work extends the Kerr/CFT correspondence originally formulated by Guica et al. (2008) for asymptotically flat extremal Kerr black holes, and its generalization to near-extremal asymptotically AdS Kerr-Newman black holes by Chen et al. (2010) and others. It also builds on the holographic computation of shear viscosity from black branes by Policastro et al. (2001) and its application to AdS black holes by Buchel et al. (2003) and Kovtun et al. (2004). The paper enables a unified holographic renormalization group interpretation connecting a UV CFT$_3$ at the asymptotic boundary to an IR CFT$_2$ in the near-horizon region, and provides a framework for computing transport coefficients in rotating strongly coupled systems with broken translational symmetry.
