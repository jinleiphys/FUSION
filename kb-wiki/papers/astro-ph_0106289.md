# Nuclear Aspects of the s- and n-Processes in Massive Stars

> In order to study the processes creating intermediate and heavy nuclei in massive stars it is necessary to provide neutron capture cross sections and reaction rates close to stability and for moderately unstable neutron-rich nuclei. Furthermore, one has to know the efficiency of neutron-releasing reactions in the main evolutionary phases of a massive star. We present simulations of the nucleosynthesis in a 15 and 25 solar mass star, for the first time followed completely from main sequence hydrogen burning until the type II supernova explosion including all nuclides up to Bi. Theoretical reaction rates were calculated with the NON-SMOKER code, providing a complete library of Hauser-Feshbach cross sections and rates for nuclear and astrophysical applications. Experimental rates at stability were taken from different sources. The impact of uncertainties in the rates on nucleosynthesis are illustrated by two examples, the reactions 62Ni(n,gamma)63Ni and 22Ne(alpha,n)25Mg.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first self-consistent calculation of the complete synthesis of all stable nuclides up to bismuth in massive stars (15 and 25 solar masses), but the results are strongly sensitive to uncertainties in key nuclear reaction rates, particularly the neutron source reaction 22Ne(alpha,n)25Mg and the neutron capture reaction 62Ni(n,gamma)63Ni, where extrapolations from thermal energies to astrophysically relevant 30 keV energies introduce large systematic errors.

## Method

The authors used the KEPLER stellar evolution code with a novel adaptive reaction network of about 2350 nuclides, following nucleosynthesis from main sequence hydrogen burning through type II supernova explosion. Theoretical Hauser-Feshbach reaction rates for unstable nuclei were calculated with the NON-SMOKER code using the FRDM mass model, while experimental rates at stability were taken from compilations including the 30 keV Maxwellian Averaged Cross Sections of Bao et al. (2000) and the NACRE compilation for 22Ne(alpha,n)25Mg.

## Key numbers

- 15 and 25 solar mass stars were simulated from main sequence hydrogen burning until type II supernova explosion, including all nuclides up to bismuth
- The reaction network included about 2350 nuclides during the explosive phase
- The 12C(alpha,gamma)16O reaction rate used S(300) = 146 keV barn from the evaluation of Buchmann (1996)
- The 62Ni(n,gamma)63Ni rate changed by about a factor of three between two compilations (Bao 2000 and an earlier version), due to different extrapolations from the thermal neutron capture cross section of 14.5 barn
- Using the upper limit of the NACRE rate for 22Ne(alpha,n)25Mg instead of the recommended rate increased production factors by factors of 10-30 for all nuclides beyond gallium
- The subthreshold s-wave resonance in 62Ni was identified at -0.077 keV

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies of Woosley and Weaver (1995) and Thielemann, Nomoto, and Hashimoto (1996), which were limited to networks of about 200 nuclides extending only up to germanium, whereas the present calculation includes all nuclides up to bismuth. The results show that the 22Ne(alpha,n)25Mg rate, sometimes called the second-most important reaction in nuclear astrophysics after 12C(alpha,gamma)16O, must be measured to better accuracy in the relevant energy range, and that the claimed solution to the underproduction of light p-process isotopes in the Mo-Ru region using an enhanced rate is ruled out because it overproduces other nuclides. The work enables future experimental efforts to focus on measuring neutron capture cross sections across a wider range of energies rather than relying on extrapolations from thermal energies.
