# Fission of $^{180}$Hg and $^{264}$Fm: a comparative study

> $^{180}$Hg is experimentally found to fission asymmetrically. This result was not expected as a naive fragment shell effects study would support the symmetric mode to be the most probable. In the present study we investigate both symmetric and asymmetric $^{180}$Hg fission modes at the mean field level using various multipole moment constraints. Potential energy surfaces are analysed in terms of shell effects that shape their topographies and connections to fragment shell effects are made. The non-occurrence of low energy symmetric fission is interpreted in terms of $^{90}$Zr fragment properties. Throughout this study a comparison with $^{264}$Fm and its symmetric doubly magic $^{132}$Sn fission fragments is done.

## Key claim

The non-occurrence of low-energy symmetric fission in $^{180}$Hg is interpreted as resulting from the insufficient rigidity of the $^{90}$Zr fragment, which prevents the formation of a compact symmetric scission configuration, in contrast to the rigid doubly-magic $^{132}$Sn fragments that enable symmetric fission in $^{264}$Fm.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the Gogny D1S interaction with constraints on multipole moments ($Q_{20}$, $Q_{30}$, $Q_{40}$) to generate potential energy surfaces for both $^{180}$Hg and $^{264}$Fm. They applied the smoothed level density (sld) method to analyze shell effects at the Fermi level along symmetric and asymmetric fission paths, tracking prefragment deformations and energies through the scission region.

## Key numbers

- The symmetric scission in $^{180}$Hg occurs at a large elongation of $\beta_2 \sim 4.4$, producing two significantly deformed fragments
- The energy window for sld calculations was set to 3.0 MeV for $^{180}$Hg and 2.5 MeV for $^{264}$Fm
- At $\beta_2 = 3.1$ in $^{180}$Hg, an approximately 4 MeV barrier separates the symmetric path from the fusion valley
- The energy difference between fragment contributions $2\Delta E_{\text{frag}}$ from fission-like to fusion-like configurations is +10.0 MeV for $^{180}$Hg and +16.7 MeV for $^{264}$Fm
- The neck energy difference $\Delta E_{\text{neck}}$ is -17.3 MeV for $^{180}$Hg and -14.3 MeV for $^{264}$Fm
- The prefragment pair identified along the asymmetric path of $^{180}$Hg is $^{98}$Ru/$^{82}$Kr
- For $^{264}$Fm, the asymmetric path yields prefragments including $^{142}$Xe/$^{122}$Pd and $^{146}$Ba/$^{118}$Ru near scission

## Context

This work extends the discussion following the experimental discovery of asymmetric fission in $^{180}$Hg by Andreyev et al. (2010), which contradicted expectations based on spherical shell effects at $Z=40$ and $N=50$. The study contrasts with earlier interpretations by Moller et al. (2012) and McDonnell et al. (2014) that found no correlation between shell correction energy and prefragment identities, while supporting the importance of deformed shell effects emphasized by Scamps et al. (2018, 2019). The comparison with $^{264}$Fm, whose symmetric fission into doubly-magic $^{132}$Sn fragments is well-established, enables the authors to identify the relative softness of $^{90}$Zr as the key factor preventing compact symmetric fission in $^{180}$Hg.
