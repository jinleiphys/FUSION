# The Nucleon Anapole Moment and Parity-Violating ep Scattering

> Parity-violating (PV) interactions among quarks in the nucleon induce a PV $γNN$ coupling, or anapole moment (AM). We compute electroweak gauge-independent contributions to the AM through ${\cal O}(1/\lamchis)$ in chiral perturbation theory. We estimate short-distance PV effects using resonance saturation. The AM contributions to PV electron-proton scattering slightly enhance the axial vector radiative corrections, R_A^p, over the scale implied by the Standard Model when weak quark-quark interactions are neglected. We estimate the theoretical uncertainty associated with the AM contributions to R_A^p to be large, and discuss the implications for the interpretation PV of ep scattering.

## Key claim

The anapole moment contributions to the axial vector radiative correction \(R_A^p\) in parity-violating electron-proton scattering are computed through \({\cal O}(1/\Lambda_\chi^2)\) in chiral perturbation theory, and the authors find that even under liberal assumptions these contributions appear unable to enhance the one-quark corrections to the level apparently observed by the SAMPLE collaboration, suggesting additional sources of enhancement not considered in this work.

## Method

The authors perform a complete calculation of the isoscalar and isovector components of the nucleon anapole moment to order \(1/\Lambda_\chi^2\) using heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT), including loop diagrams not considered in prior analyses. They incorporate octet and decuplet intermediate states, magnetic insertions, and SU(3) chiral symmetry, and estimate short-distance parity-violating effects using resonance saturation with vector mesons. The results are expressed in terms of effective parity-violating hadronic couplings, some of which are constrained by nuclear and hadronic parity-violation experiments while others remain unconstrained.

## Key numbers

* The strange-quark magnetic form factor measured by SAMPLE at \(Q^2=0.1\ {\rm GeV}^2/c^2\) is \(G_M^{(s)} = 0.61 \pm 0.27 \pm 0.19\), where the first error is experimental and the second is theoretical.
* The nucleon axial charge from neutron beta decay is \(g_A = 1.267 \pm 0.004\).
* The isovector part of the axial radiative correction from the prior calculation of Ref. [mike] is \(R_A^{T=1} \approx -0.34 \pm 0.20\).
* The scale of charged current contributions is \(g_\pi = 3.8 \times 10^{-8}\).
* The expected scale of anapole contributions to \(R_A^{T=0,1}\) from dimensional analysis is approximately \(-0.01\), with net effects anticipated to be 10-20% of the one-quark corrections.
* The parity-violating \(NN\pi\) Yukawa coupling from \({}^{18}\)F data is \(h_\pi = (0.7 \pm 2.2) g_\pi\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Ref. [mike] and Ref. [kaplan] by performing a complete HBChPT calculation of the nucleon anapole moment through \({\cal O}(1/\Lambda_\chi^2)\), including loop diagrams, decuplet intermediate states, and SU(3) symmetry not considered in those earlier studies. The results are relevant for interpreting the SAMPLE collaboration's parity-violating electron-proton and quasielastic deuteron scattering measurements, which aim to determine the strange-quark magnetic form factor and the isovector axial radiative correction. The paper enables future work to identify additional sources of enhancement beyond the conventional hadronic mechanisms considered here, potentially involving new physics or higher-order chiral effects.
