# Chemical and mechanical instabilities in hot, isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter

> Using phenomenological equations of state, we study the chemical and mechanical instabilities in hot isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter. Both instabilities are found to depend strongly on the isospin asymmetry of the nuclear matter. For a chemically and mechanically stable asymmetric nuclear matter, a lower neutron excess is found in the liquid phase than in the gas phase. Furthermore, the neutron excess in the mixed phase at densities between that of the liquid and the gas phases is limited by certain maximum value. Also, for the nuclear equations of state considered here the boundary of chemical instability in the pressure-density plane is found to be more extended than that of mechanical instability. However, locations of these boundaries depend sensitively on both the bulk compressibility of nuclear matter and the density dependence of the symmetry energy.

# Chemical and mechanical instabilities in hot, isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter

## Key claim
For all phenomenological equations of state considered, the boundary of chemical instability in the pressure-density plane is more extended than that of mechanical instability, but the locations of these boundaries depend sensitively on both the bulk compressibility of nuclear matter and the density dependence of the symmetry energy, a result that extends the earlier Relativistic Mean-Field finding of Müller (1995) to a broader class of equations of state.

## Method
The authors used phenomenological equations of state with a parameterized single-particle potential, where the isospin-independent part is fixed by saturation properties and the compressibility K (200 or 380 MeV), and the isospin-dependent part is modeled by three forms of the symmetry energy density function F(u) = u², u, or u^(1/2). Chemical potentials were obtained from an analytic inversion of the Fermi distribution for T ≥ 4 MeV, and the conditions for mechanical stability (∂P/∂ρ ≥ 0) and chemical stability (∂μ_n/∂δ ≥ 0 at constant P,T) were evaluated across the (ρ, T, δ) configuration space.

## Key numbers
- The compressibility values used are K = 200 MeV and K = 380 MeV for the isospin-independent part of the equation of state.
- The symmetry energy at normal nuclear matter density S₀ is taken in the range 27–36 MeV.
- At T = 8 MeV, mechanical instability gradually disappears while chemical instability becomes more pronounced as δ increases from 0 to 0.6.
- At ρ/ρ₀ = 0.4 and T = 10 MeV, the system is mechanically unstable at δ ≤ 0.2 and chemically unstable at 0.2 ≤ δ ≤ 0.4.
- At ρ/ρ₀ = 0.5 and T = 10 MeV, the system is mechanically unstable at δ ≤ 0.4 and chemically unstable at 0.4 ≤ δ ≤ 0.6.
- The low-density limit of (∂P/∂ρ) approaches T, and the low-density limit of (∂μ_n/∂δ) at constant P,T equals T/(1+δ).

## Context
This work extends the earlier study by Müller (1995) using Relativistic Mean-Field theory, which found that the diffusive spinodal encloses more configuration space than the isothermal spinodal, to a broader set of phenomenological equations of state with different density dependences of the symmetry energy. The results are relevant for understanding multifragmentation in heavy-ion collisions with neutron-rich nuclei, as observed experimentally by Sherry (1996), Kunde (1996), and Dempsey (1996), and for neutron star properties where the symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities is critical. The findings enable extension of dynamical transport models (e.g., Ayik 1995, Randrup 1995, Colonna 1995) to include isospin fluctuations in multifragmentation simulations.
