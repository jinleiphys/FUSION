# Resonant-state solution of the Faddeev-Merkuriev integral equations for three-body systems with Coulomb-like potentials

> A novel method for calculating resonances in three-body Coulombic systems is presented. The Faddeev-Merkuriev integral equations are solved by applying the Coulomb-Sturmian separable expansion method. To show the power of the method we calculate resonances of the three-$α$ and the $H^-$ systems.

# Resonant-state solution of the Faddeev-Merkuriev integral equations for three-body systems with Coulomb-like potentials

## Key claim

The Faddeev-Merkuriev integral equations can be solved for complex energies to calculate three-body resonances in systems with either repulsive or attractive Coulomb interactions, using a Coulomb-Sturmian separable expansion method that requires no explicit boundary conditions. The method works equally for both types of Coulomb potentials, extending previous work that was limited to repulsive Coulomb interactions via the Noble splitting approach.

## Method

The homogeneous Faddeev-Merkuriev integral equations are solved by expanding the wave-function components in a Coulomb-Sturmian (CS) basis, which converts the integral equations into matrix equations whose determinant must vanish at resonance energies. The three-body Green's operator matrix elements are obtained via a contour integral convolution of analytically known two-body CS Green's operator matrix elements, with the contour deformed to access the unphysical Riemann sheet for complex energies. For the three-alpha system, Noble's splitting (treating the entire Coulomb interaction as long-range) is used, while for the H- system, the full Merkuriev splitting with a cutoff function is required to handle the attractive electron-proton Coulomb interactions.

## Key numbers

- Three-alpha ground state energy: converges to -7.283840 MeV (with N=28 basis states, L=0 total angular momentum)
- Three-alpha first resonant state: converges to approximately 0.3845512 - i 0.000013 MeV (with N=29 basis states)
- Three-alpha potential parameters: V1=125 MeV, V2=-30.18 MeV, beta1=1.53 fm, beta2=2.85 fm; three-body potential V=-31.935 MeV, beta=3.315 fm
- H- resonant state energy: converges to -0.148696 - i 0.000866 atomic units (with N=20 basis states, angular momentum channels up to l=4)
- H- cutoff function parameters: nu=2.1, x0=5, y0=10

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous method for three-body bound states (Papp et al., 1997) and for resonances with repulsive Coulomb interactions via the Faddeev-Noble equations (Z. Papp et al., 2000) to handle attractive Coulomb potentials using the full Merkuriev formalism. The approach enables calculation of resonances in systems such as H- (two electrons and a proton) and the three-alpha system relevant to nuclear astrophysics, without requiring explicit asymptotic boundary conditions for resonant states.
