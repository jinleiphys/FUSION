# Molecular structures and clustering effects in reactions induced by light nuclei

> $α$-clustering study since the pioneering discovery of $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C molecular resonances half a century ago. Our knowledge on physics of nuclear molecules has increased considerably and nuclear clustering remains one of the most fruitful domains of nuclear physics, facing some of the greatest challenges and opportunities in the years ahead. The occurrence of "exotic" shapes in light $N$=$Z$ $α$-like nuclei is investigated. Various approaches of the superdeformed and hyperdeformed bands associated with quasimolecular resonant structures are presented. Evolution of clustering from stability to the drip-lines is examined: clustering aspects are, in particular, discussed for light exotic nuclei with large neutron excess such as neutron-rich Oxygen isotopes with their complete spectrocopy.

## Key claim

The paper argues that quasimolecular resonances in light α-conjugate nuclei are directly linked to superdeformed (SD) and hyperdeformed (HD) rotational bands, but this connection remains conditional on further experimental confirmation via combined γ-ray and charged-particle coincidence measurements, as many predicted cluster states are expected to decay by particle emission with very small, possibly unmeasurable, γ-decay branches.

## Method

The author reviews experimental data from multiple reaction studies, including the \(^{24}\)Mg+\(^{12}\)C reaction at E\(_{lab}\)(\(^{24}\)Mg) = 130 MeV measured with the Binary Reaction Spectrometer (BRS) in coincidence with the Euroball IV array, and the \(^{12}\)C(\(^{16}\)O,γ)\(^{28}\)Si radiative capture reaction at five resonant energies around the Coulomb barrier using the DRAGON spectrometer at TRIUMF. Rotational band structures are extracted from excitation-energy systematics plotted as a function of J(J+1), and moments of inertia are derived from linear fits to these data. The analysis also includes α-transfer reactions and deep-inelastic orbiting collisions to deduce branching ratios and γ-decay properties of states in \(^{16}\)O.

## Key numbers

* The 3\(^{+}\) state of \(^{16}\)O at 11.085 MeV ± 3 keV has a total width \(\Gamma_{3^+} < 0.23\) eV, a value fifty times lower than previously known.
* The 3\(^{+}\) state of \(^{16}\)O decays to the 2\(^{+}\) state at 6.92 MeV with a branching ratio of 54.6 ± 2 % and to the 3\(^{-}\) state at 6.13 MeV with a branching ratio of 45.4 %.
* In the \(^{12}\)C+\(^{12}\)C radiative capture reaction, a 4\(^{+}\)-2\(^{+}\) resonance was measured at E\(_{c.m.}\) = 8.0 MeV near the Coulomb barrier.
* The 0\(^{+}_{6}\) state in \(^{16}\)O, a candidate for a dilute 4α gas state, is predicted at about 15.1 MeV, approximately 700 keV above the 4α-particle breakup threshold.
* In \(^{28}\)Si, the 2\(^{+}\) state of the SD band candidate is at 9.8 MeV, and the prolate 4\(^{+}_{3}\) state is at 9.16 MeV.
* The \(^{12}\)C+\(^{16}\)O radiative capture data show direct feeding of the prolate 4\(^{+}_{3}\) state at 9.16 MeV and the octupole deformed 3\(^{-}\) state at 6.88 MeV in \(^{28}\)Si.
* The ground state of \(^{28}\)Si is oblate; the two prolate normal deformed bands are built on the 0\(^{+}_{2}\) state at 4.98 MeV and the 0\(^{+}_{3}\) state at 6.69 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the classic Ikeda diagram (Ikeda, 1968) for N=Z α-conjugate nuclei and the extended Ikeda diagram proposed by von Oertzen (2001) for neutron-rich nuclei, by further including \(^{14}\)C cluster cores alongside \(^{16}\)O cores. The review synthesizes results from multiple experimental campaigns, including those by Svensson (2000) on SD bands in \(^{36}\)Ar, Jenkins (2012) on cluster states in \(^{28}\)Si, and Oertzen (2008, 2009, 2010) on molecular bands in neutron-rich oxygen isotopes. The findings enable future searches for the 4α Hoyle-analog state in \(^{16}\)O and for hyperdeformed configurations in medium-mass nuclei, which will require precise coincidence measurements coupled with state-of-the-art theory.
