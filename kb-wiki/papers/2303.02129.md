# Higgs-confinement continuity and matching of Aharonov-Bohm phases

> Some gauge theories with a spontaneously broken $U(1)$ symmetry exhibit fractional Aharonov-Bohm (AB) phases around vortices in the Higgs regime. We discuss continuity between confining and Higgs regimes in such gauge theories with fundamental matter fields, focusing on the AB phases. By explicit calculations in relevant lattice models, we demonstrate that the AB phase is smoothly connected between the confining and Higgs regimes, supporting the Higgs-confinement continuity. This result provides new insight into phase structures of gauge theories with superfluidity, such as dense QCD.

# Higgs-confinement continuity and matching of Aharonov-Bohm phases

## Key claim

The apparent mismatch of Aharonov-Bohm (AB) phases between confining and Higgs regimes does not contradict Higgs-confinement continuity: either a symmetry forces the AB phase to be nontrivial in both regimes, or the AB phase can continuously interpolate between the confining and Higgs limits without a phase transition.

## Method

The author constructs two lattice gauge models: a U(1) gauge model with charge-(+1) and charge-(-1) scalars plus a neutral scalar, and an SU(N) Yang-Mills-Higgs model with a U(N)-valued fundamental scalar and a neutral scalar. For each model, the AB phase around a superfluid vortex is computed analytically in two calculable limits: the strong coupling limit (confining regime, small gauge coupling) and the deep Higgs limit (large scalar kinetic term). The strong coupling expansion and explicit integration over link variables are used to extract the phase of the Wilson loop in the presence of a vortex with fixed winding number.

## Key numbers

- In the U(1) toy model, the AB phase is O_Omega = -1 in both the deep Higgs limit (kappa >> 1) and the strong coupling limit (beta << 1), demonstrating continuity.
- In the SU(N) Yang-Mills-Higgs model, the deep Higgs limit yields O_Omega = e^{2 pi i / N} for general N.
- For N=2 in the SU(N) model, the AB phase is O_Omega = -1 in both the deep Higgs and strong coupling limits, matching the U(1) toy model result.
- For N=3, a numerical evaluation at L=100 links shows the AB phase continuously interpolates between +1 (confining) and e^{2 pi i / 3} (Higgs) as kappa increases from 0 to large values.

## Context

This work addresses an open problem raised by Cherman (2020) regarding whether nontrivial AB phases around superfluid vortices could serve as order parameters distinguishing confining and Higgs regimes, potentially contradicting the Fradkin-Shenker-Osterwalder-Seiler theorem (1977, 1978). The results support the quark-hadron continuity conjecture for dense QCD proposed by Schafer (1998) and Alford (1999), where the color-flavor locked phase and nuclear superfluid phase are expected to be smoothly connected. The analysis extends previous work by Hirono (2018, 2019) showing that nontrivial braiding phases do not imply topological order, and provides explicit lattice calculations demonstrating how the AB phase matching is realized.
