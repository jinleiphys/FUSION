# On the Coupling of the $η$ Meson to the Nucleon

> The pseudoscalar and pseudovector $ηN$ coupling constants are calculated from an effective vertex associated with the $a_0(980)πN$ triangle diagram. The predicted values are in agreement with the ones concluded from fitting $η$ photoproduction amplitudes. In this context we stress the importance of the properties of the scalar meson octet for $η$ meson physics.

## Key claim

The pseudoscalar and pseudovector $\eta N$ coupling constants can be calculated from an effective $a_0(980)\pi N$ triangle diagram, and the predicted values (particularly $g_{\eta NN}^2/4\pi \approx 0.33$) agree with the value of approximately 0.4 obtained from fitting $\eta$ photoproduction amplitudes, provided one assumes complete dominance of the $a_0(980)$ decay width by the $\eta\pi$ channel and uses the version of the Bonn potential with the lowest $a_0N$ coupling constant.

## Method

The authors calculate the $\eta NN$ vertex from a one-loop triangle diagram involving $a_0(980)$, $\pi$, and $N$ intermediate states, using effective Lagrangians for the $a_0\to\eta\pi$ decay, the $\pi NN$ pseudovector coupling, and the scalar $a_0N$ coupling. They regularize the loop integral with monopole form factors at the $\pi NN$ and $a_0NN$ vertices, using cutoff parameters of 1.05 GeV and 2.0 GeV respectively, and evaluate the resulting expressions at $q^2 = m_\eta^2$ to obtain both the pseudoscalar and pseudovector coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- $g_{\eta NN} = 2.03$, giving $g_{\eta NN}^2/4\pi = 0.33$
- $f_{\eta NN} = 0.58$, giving $f_{\eta NN}^2/4\pi = 0.027$
- The mixing parameter $\lambda$ between pseudoscalar and pseudovector coupling is $\lambda = 0.504$
- The effective coupling $g_\eta = 4.03$, with $g_\eta^2/4\pi = 1.29$
- The $a_0\eta\pi$ coupling constant used is $f_{a_0\eta\pi} = 0.44$, extracted from the experimental decay width assuming the total $a_0$ decay width is from $a_0\to\eta\pi$
- The $a_0N$ coupling constant used is $g_{a_0NN}^2/4\pi = 0.77$, from the Bonn OBEPQ potential
- The ratio $r = g_{\eta NN}/g_{\pi NN}$ from the toy model universality argument is $-0.0527$, compared to the experimentally observed ratio of approximately 0.2

## Context

This work extends previous determinations of the $\eta NN$ coupling constant from $\eta$ photoproduction analyses by Tiator (1994) and from $NN$ forward dispersion relations by Grein (1980), which favored small values. It also contrasts with the larger values (3-7) obtained from one-boson-exchange potential fits by Brockmann (1990) and with SU(3) flavor symmetry predictions giving values between 0.8 and 1.9. The paper enables a consistent treatment of the $\eta NN$ vertex in photoproduction and scattering models, and highlights the need for precise measurements of $a_0(980)$ decay properties and the $a_0NN$ coupling constant.
