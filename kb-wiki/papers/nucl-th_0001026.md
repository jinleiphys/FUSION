# Solar Proton Burning Process Revisited within a Covariant Model Based on the Bethe-Salpeter Formalism

> A covariant model based on the Bethe-Salpeter formalism is proposed for investigating the solar proton burning process $pp\to De^+ν_e$ and the near-threshold deuteron disintegration via electromagnetic and weak interactions. Results of numerical calculations of the energy dependence of relevant cross sections and the astrophysical low-energy cross section factor $S_{pp}$ of the proton burning process are presented. Our results confirm previous canonical values, and the energy dependence of the $S_{pp}$ factor is rather close to phenomenological extrapolations commonly adopted in computations of solar nuclear reaction rates.

# Solar Proton Burning Process Revisited within a Covariant Model Based on the Bethe-Salpeter Formalism

## Key claim

The covariant Bethe-Salpeter model yields an S_pp factor of 3.960 × 10^{-25} MeV b at very low kinetic energies, confirming the canonical value of 4.00 × 10^{-25} MeV b from Adelberger et al., but the first derivative of S_pp is found to be 15-20% below the estimates of Adelberger et al., with the discrepancy arising from the treatment of the deuteron wave function at finite momenta.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation numerically with realistic one-boson-exchange interactions to obtain a covariant deuteron amplitude, then compute the pp → D e⁺ ν_e cross section within the relativistic impulse approximation using the Mandelstam technique. The initial-state Coulomb repulsion between protons is factorized via the Gamow penetration factor, and the hadronic tensor is evaluated with the effective Fermi model for weak interactions, including both direct and interference terms from antisymmetrization of the two-proton initial state. The deuteron wave function is extrapolated to zero momentum from a numerical solution with a minimum momentum grid point of 0.310848493733 MeV, introducing an estimated 10% numerical uncertainty.

## Key numbers

- S_pp = 3.960 × 10^{-25} MeV b at very low kinetic energies (T_pp near zero), confirming the canonical value from Adelberger et al.
- First derivative S'_pp = 4.48 × 10^{-24} b from Adelberger et al. is used as reference; the authors' computed derivative is 15-20% below this value
- Logarithmic derivative S'_pp/S = 11.2 MeV^{-1} from Adelberger et al. is used as reference; the authors' computed logarithmic derivative is 15-20% below this value
- Deuteron binding energy ε_D = 2.22455 MeV; proton mass m_p = 938.27231 MeV; deuteron mass M_d = 1875.61339 MeV; positron mass μ = 0.510999 MeV
- Threshold energies: E_thr = 2.7375 MeV for eD electromagnetic disintegration; E_thr = 0.93139 MeV for weak positron-deuteron disintegration
- Weak coupling constants: G_F = 1.16639 × 10^{-5} GeV^{-2}; R = 1.2681 (axial-to-vector ratio); Cabibbo angle θ_c = 0.24
- The most probable interaction energy T^{(0)}_pp for solar pp fusion lies in the range 5 keV to 30 keV

## Context

This work extends the low-energy potential model calculations of Adelberger et al. and the effective field theory approach of Wise, responding to the claim by Ivanov of a considerably larger S_pp value inconsistent with helioseismic data. The covariant Bethe-Salpeter formalism allows computation of matrix elements at finite energies, unlike short-range effective theories that are limited to the zero-energy limit. The results enable improved extrapolation of S_pp from zero energy to the solar Gamow peak energy T^{(0)}_pp, and the authors note that experimental investigation of the relevant matrix element via cross-channel reactions remains impossible under laboratory conditions.
