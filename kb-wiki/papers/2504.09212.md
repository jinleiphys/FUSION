# Lepton capture rates due to isotopes of vanadium in astrophysical environment

> Lepton (electron and positron) capture rates on iron-regime nuclei are an essential element for modeling the late stages in the evolution of massive stars that end as core-collapse and thermonuclear supernovae. Previous simulation studies suggest that lepton capture (LC) rates on isotopes of vanadium have a substantial effect in regulating the electron fraction (Ye) during the final evolutionary phases. The present work involves the calculation of LC rates for 22 isotopes of vanadium using the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model. The considered mass range is from A = 43 to 64. The LC rates are computed over stellar densities ranging from 10^1 to 10^11 g/cm^3 and temperatures in the range 10^7 to 3 x 10^10 K. A comparison of our LC rates with those obtained using other models (IPM and LSSM) is also presented. Compared to other models, the pn-QRPA rates at high temperature (3 x 10^10 K) are larger by 1-2 orders of magnitude.

## Key claim

The pn-QRPA model predicts lepton capture rates for vanadium isotopes that are larger by 1-2 orders of magnitude at high temperature (3 x 10^10 K) compared to rates from the Independent Particle Model (IPM) and Large-Scale Shell Model (LSSM), but this enhancement is not uniform across all isotopes and conditions; at lower temperatures and densities, IPM and LSSM rates can be larger for some isotopes, particularly for positron capture on lighter vanadium isotopes.

## Method

The authors used the deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model with a Nilsson single-particle basis, BCS pairing, and particle-particle and particle-hole Gamow-Teller residual interactions to compute Gamow-Teller strength distributions for 22 vanadium isotopes (A = 43 to 64). These strength distributions, previously reported in Shehzadi (2020), were then used to calculate electron and positron capture rates via the phase-space integral formalism of Fuller et al., summing over all parent excited states with Boltzmann occupation probabilities without invoking Brink's hypothesis. The rates were computed over stellar densities from 10^1 to 10^11 g/cm^3 and temperatures from 10^7 to 3 x 10^10 K.

## Key numbers

- pn-QRPA electron capture rates at high temperature (3 x 10^10 K) are larger than IPM and LSSM rates by 1-2 orders of magnitude for many vanadium isotopes.
- For 51V at high temperature, pn-QRPA electron capture rates exceed IPM rates by up to a factor of 18 and LSSM rates by up to a factor of 52.
- For 52V at high temperature and density, pn-QRPA rates surpass IPM and LSSM rates by approximately 2 orders of magnitude.
- For 48V at temperatures below T9 = 5 (5 x 10^9 K), IPM and LSSM positron capture rates are greater than pn-QRPA rates by about 1-8 orders of magnitude.
- For 45V at temperatures T9 less than or equal to 10, LSSM and IPM positron capture rates exceed pn-QRPA rates by about 2-3 orders of magnitude.
- For 46V and 49V, pn-QRPA electron capture rates are bigger than IPM and LSSM rates by 1-2 orders of magnitude; for 48V, the enhancement is 1-3 orders of magnitude.
- For isotopes with A greater than 57, positron capture rates are larger than corresponding electron capture rates by 1-2 orders of magnitude at low and medium densities.

## Context

This work extends the pn-QRPA calculations of weak rates for vanadium isotopes beyond the stable isotopes 50V and 51V, which were previously studied by Nabi (2007) and Muneeb (2013), to a full chain of 22 isotopes from A=43 to A=64. The results are compared to the earlier IPM calculations of Fuller et al. (FFN) and the LSSM calculations of Langanke et al., with the key difference being that pn-QRPA treats excited-state Gamow-Teller strengths microscopically rather than using Brink's hypothesis. The enhanced lepton capture rates at high temperature may affect core-collapse supernova simulations, particularly shock wave energetics and the evolution of the electron fraction Ye, and the authors encourage simulators to test these rates in stellar codes.
