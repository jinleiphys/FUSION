# On the Baryonic Density and Susceptibilities in a Holographic Model of QCD

> In this paper, we calculate analytically the baryonic density and susceptibilities, which are sensitive probes to the fermionic degrees of freedom, in a holographic model of QCD both in its hot QGP phase and in its cold dense phase. Interesting patterns due to strong coupling dynamics will be shown and valuable lessons for QCD will be discussed.

## Key claim

The paper analytically calculates baryonic density and susceptibilities in the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic model of QCD, showing that even in the deconfined QGP phase where quarks are the basic baryonic charge carriers, strong coupling dynamics produce non-perturbative patterns including alternating signs for higher-order susceptibilities and suppression at very strong coupling, which qualitatively resemble lattice QCD results near T_c.

## Method

The authors use the antipodal configuration of the Sakai-Sugimoto model, which simplifies the DBI action to depend only on the time component of the U(1) gauge field. They solve analytically for the grand potential in both the deconfined (hot QGP) phase and the confined (cold dense) phase, then compute baryonic density and susceptibilities via derivatives of the pressure with respect to chemical potential, using chain-rule techniques to avoid solving the constraint equation explicitly.

## Key numbers

- In the QGP phase, susceptibilities to order n=10 are given by d_n = chi_n * u_T^(-n+7/2) * t^(n-4) with chi_2 = 3/2, chi_4 = 3^5/(2^3*13), chi_6 = -3^8*5*31/(2^5*13^2*23), showing alternating signs for n>2
- After recovering physical dimensions, d_n = xi_n * N_s * N_c * N_f * (1/(lambda * tilde_T))^(n-3) where lambda is the 't Hooft coupling and tilde_T = T/T_c
- In the dense limit of the QGP phase, baryonic density scales as n_B/T_c^3 ~ (7/2) * N_s * N_f * N_c * lambda^(-1/2) * (mu/T_c)^(5/2)
- The energy per particle in the dense QGP limit is (5/7)mu, which is 20/21 of the Stefan-Boltzmann free gas value (3mu/4)
- In the dilute QGP limit, baryonic density scales as n_B/T_c^3 ~ N_s * N_f * N_c * lambda * (T/T_c)^3 * (mu/T_c)

## Context

This work extends the Sakai-Sugimoto model studies at finite temperature (FiniteT, 2006) and finite baryon density (FiniteD, 2007; Bergman0, 2007; Kim:Dense, 2008) to compute baryonic susceptibilities analytically. The results are motivated by lattice QCD calculations from Karsch et al (2005) and Cheng et al (2008) which showed nontrivial patterns of d_2, d_4, and d_6 near T_c, and by the bound-state explanation proposed by Liao and Shuryak (2005). The holographic results provide a strong-coupling baseline for understanding how individual quarks contribute to susceptibilities, enabling comparison with phenomenological models like PNJL (Ratti, 2005) and guiding interpretation of baryonic charge fluctuations in heavy ion collisions.
