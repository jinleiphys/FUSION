# The molecular nature of some $Ω_c^0$ states

> A vector meson exchange model based on effective Lagrangians is used to build the meson--baryon interaction in the charm $+1$, strangeness $-2$ and isospin $0$ sector. The s-wave scattering amplitudes resulting from the unitarization in coupled-channels show two resonances with masses and widths that are in very good agreement with those of the experimental $Ω_c(3050)^0$ and $Ω_c(3090)^0$ states observed by the LHCb collaboration. The interpretation of these resonances as pseudoscalar meson--baryon molecules would mean the assignment $J^P=1/2^-$ to their spin--parity.

## Key claim

The paper claims that two of the five narrow $\Omega_c^0$ states observed by LHCb, specifically $\Omega_c(3050)^0$ and $\Omega_c(3090)^0$, can be interpreted as meson–baryon molecules with spin–parity $J^P = 1/2^-$, but the match for the heavier state requires tuning of subtraction constants and the mass is about 10 MeV above the experimental value while the width is about twice as large.

## Method

The authors construct a t-channel vector meson exchange model using effective Lagrangians from the hidden gauge formalism, assuming SU(4) symmetry which is broken by using physical masses and a factor $\kappa_c = 1/4$ for charmed meson exchange. The s-wave scattering amplitudes are unitarized via the on-shell Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels, with the loop function regularized using dimensional regularization and subtraction constants mapped to a cut-off. Resonance poles are found in the second Riemann sheet of the complex energy plane, and their couplings and compositeness are extracted from the residues.

## Key numbers

- Pole from pseudoscalar–baryon interaction (Model 1, cut-off 800 MeV): $M_1 = 3051.6$ MeV, $\Gamma_1 = 0.45$ MeV; $M_2 = 3103.3$ MeV, $\Gamma_2 = 17$ MeV
- Pole from pseudoscalar–baryon interaction (Model 2, tuned subtraction constants): $M = 3050.3$ MeV, $\Gamma = 0.44$ MeV; $M = 3090.8$ MeV, $\Gamma = 12$ MeV
- Compositeness of the 3050 MeV state in $\bar{K}\Xi'_c$: $0.61 + i\,0.01$; compositeness of the 3090 MeV state in $D\Xi$: $0.91 - i\,0.01$
- Vector–baryon interaction (cut-off 800 MeV): poles at 3231 MeV ($D^*\Xi$ molecule), 3419 MeV ($\bar{K}^*\Xi'_c$ composite), and an additional intermediate pole
- Bottom sector predictions: pseudoscalar–baryon states at 6418 MeV and 6519 MeV; vector–baryon states at 6560 MeV, 6665 MeV, and 6797 MeV

## Context

This work extends the meson–baryon molecular approach previously applied to light and strange baryons (Hofmann 2005, Jimenez-Tejero 2009, Romanets 2012) to the charmed $\Omega_c^0$ sector, motivated by the LHCb observation of five narrow $\Omega_c^0$ states (Aaij 2017). The results are similar to those of other groups (Debastiani 2017, Wang 2017, Chen 2017, Nieves 2017) who also interpret some $\Omega_c$ states as quasi-bound meson–baryon systems, and the authors extend their model to predict analogous $\Omega_b^-$ resonances in the bottom sector (Liang 2017). The work enables future experimental searches for these predicted bottom-sector states and further theoretical study of the molecular nature of heavy baryon excitations.
