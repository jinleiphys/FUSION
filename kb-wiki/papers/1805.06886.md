# Chiral symmetry restoration by parity doubling and the structure of neutron stars

> We investigate the equation of state for a recently developed hybrid quark-meson-nucleon model under neutron star conditions of $β-$equilibrium and charge neutrality. The model has the characteristic feature that at increasing baryon density chiral symmetry is restored in a first order transition within the hadronic phase by lifting the mass splitting between chiral partner states, before quark deconfinement takes place. Most important for this study are the nucleon (neutron, proton) and $N(1535)$ states. We present three sets for the two free parameters which result in compact star mass-radius relations in accordance with modern constraints on the mass from PSR~J0437-4715 and on the compactness from GW170817. We also consider the threshold for the direct URCA process for which a new relationship is given and suggest as an additional constraint on the parameter choice of the model that this process shall become operative at best for stars with masses above the range for binary radio pulsars, $M>1.4~M_\odot$.

## Key claim
The paper claims that chiral symmetry restoration via parity doubling within the hadronic phase, occurring as a first-order transition before quark deconfinement, can produce neutron star mass-radius relations consistent with the 2.01(4) solar mass constraint from PSR J0348+0432 and the compactness constraint from GW170817, but only for specific choices of the chirally invariant mass parameter m0 and the confinement parameter αb0.

## Method
The authors extend the hybrid quark-meson-nucleon (QMN) model to include the isovector ρ meson for arbitrary isospin, then solve for the equation of state under neutron star conditions of β-equilibrium and charge neutrality. They fix model parameters by nuclear ground state properties (binding energy -16 MeV at saturation density 0.16 fm^-3) and the symmetry energy (31 MeV), then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain mass-radius sequences for four values of the chirally invariant mass m0 (790, 800, 820, 840 MeV) and four values of the confinement parameter αb0 (350, 370, 400, 450 MeV).

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass constraint: 2.01(4) solar masses from PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis 2013)
- Radius constraint for 1.4 solar mass stars: 12.0 km < R(1.4 M⊙) < 13.6 km from GW170817 analysis (Annala 2017)
- For m0=790 MeV and αb0=350 MeV: chiral phase transition triggered at 1.82 ρ0, mixed phase persists to 2.46 ρ0 (ρ0=0.16 fm^-3)
- For m0=790 MeV and αb0=370 MeV: mixed phase between 2.14-2.73 ρ0
- For m0=790 MeV and αb0=400 MeV: mixed phase between 2.68-3.11 ρ0
- For m0=790 MeV and αb0=450 MeV: transition becomes crossover at 3.81 ρ0
- Nuclear compressibility K=511 MeV and symmetry energy slope L=78 MeV at saturation for m0=790 MeV
- Suggested direct URCA threshold: stars above 1.4 solar masses

## Context
This work extends the hybrid QMN model previously developed for isospin-symmetric matter (Benic 2015, Marczenko 2017) to neutron star conditions, and contrasts with two-phase approaches that assume simultaneous chiral and deconfinement transitions. The results show that strong first-order phase transitions within hadronic matter (due to chiral restoration) can produce similar signatures in mass-radius diagrams as quark deconfinement transitions discussed by Alford (2013) and Alvarez-Castillo (2016). The model enables future studies of whether including repulsive interactions in the quark sector could produce high-mass twin star configurations, as advocated by Alvarez-Castillo (2017) and Kaltenborn (2017).
