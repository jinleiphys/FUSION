# Self-consistent Continuum Random Phase Approximation calculations with finite-range interactions

> We present a technique which allows us to solve the Random Phase Approximation equations with finite-range interactions and treats the continuum part of the excitation spectrum without approximations. The interaction used in the Hartree-Fock calculations to generate the single particle basis is also used in the Continuum Random Phase Approximation calculations. We present results for the electric dipole and quadrupole excitations in the $^{16}$O, $^{22}$O, $^{24}$O, $^{40}$Ca, $^{48}$Ca and $^{52}$Ca nuclei. We compare our results with those of the traditional discrete Random Phase Approximation, with the continuum mean-field results and with the results obtained by a phenomenological approach. We study the relevance of the continuum, of the residual interaction and of the self-consistency. We also compare our results with the available total photoabsorption cross section data. We compare our photoabsorption cross section in $^4$He with that obtained by a calculation which uses a microscopic interaction.

# Self-consistent Continuum Random Phase Approximation calculations with finite-range interactions

## Key claim

The authors present a technique that solves the Continuum Random Phase Approximation (CRPA) equations with finite-range interactions without approximations in the continuum part of the excitation spectrum, while maintaining full self-consistency between the Hartree-Fock (HF) and RPA calculations; the method is demonstrated for electric dipole and quadrupole excitations in six oxygen and calcium isotopes, but the formalism is general and applicable to any nucleus where a spherical HF basis can be generated.

## Method

The CRPA secular equations are reformulated by introducing channel functions f and g that depend on the radial coordinate r rather than on the continuous particle energy, converting the original integro-differential equations into a system of linear algebraic equations. These channel functions are expanded on a basis of Sturm-Bessel functions that satisfy the appropriate boundary conditions for open channels (outgoing Coulomb or Hankel functions) and closed channels (decreasing exponentials). The HF single-particle basis and the residual interaction are both generated from the same finite-range Gogny interaction (D1S or D1M parameterizations), ensuring self-consistency, and the resulting algebraic equations are solved for each excitation energy above the nucleon emission threshold.

## Key numbers

- Convergence of total photoabsorption cross sections in 16O and 40Ca was stable to the fifth significant figure with 10 expansion coefficients, independent of multipolarity and excitation energy.
- The D1M Gogny force was fitted to approximately 2000 nuclear binding energies and 700 charge radii.
- Calculations were performed for electric dipole (E1) and quadrupole (E2) excitations in 16O, 22O, 24O, 40Ca, 48Ca, and 52Ca.
- The D1S and D1M forces reproduce the empirical saturation point of symmetric nuclear matter and the behavior of equations of state calculated with microscopic approaches.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent CRPA approach, previously limited to zero-range interactions (Ber75, Shl75, Liu76, Van81, War87, Sar93, Ham96), to finite-range interactions by following the formalism of the Julich group (Bub91) but replacing their Woods-Saxon single-particle basis with a fully self-consistent HF basis. The method enables predictions for experimentally unknown nuclei, unlike the phenomenological RPA approach (Spe77, Spe80) which requires re-tuning input parameters for each nucleus. The finite-range treatment is more directly comparable to microscopic NN forces and avoids the excessive collectivity produced by zero-range interactions at higher momentum transfer (Mar07), while also providing better descriptions of unnatural parity excitations (Spe80, Co90, Don09).
