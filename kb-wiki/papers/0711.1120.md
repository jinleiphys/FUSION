# Electroexcitation of the Roper resonance from CLAS data

> The helicity amplitudes of the electroexcitation of the Roper resonance on proton are extracted at 1.7 < Q2 < 4.2 GeV2 from recent high precision JLab-CLAS cross sections data and longitudinally polarized beam asymmetry for pi+ electroproduction on protons. The analysis is made using two approaches, dispersion relations and unitary isobar model, which give consistent results. It is found that the transverse helicity amplitude for the gamma* p --> P11(1440) transition, which is large and negative at Q2=0, becomes large and positive at Q2 ~ 2 GeV2, and then drops slowly with Q2. Longitudinal helicity amplitude, that was previously found from CLAS data as large and positive at Q2=0.4,0.65 GeV2, drops with Q2. These results rule out the presentation of P11(1440) as a 3qG hybrid state, and provide strong evidence in favor of this resonance as a first radial excitation of the 3q ground state.

## Key claim
The transverse helicity amplitude for the gamma* p to P11(1440) transition, which is large and negative at Q2=0, becomes large and positive at Q2 approximately 2 GeV2 and then drops slowly with Q2; the longitudinal helicity amplitude drops with Q2. These results rule out the representation of P11(1440) as a 3qG hybrid state and provide strong evidence in favor of this resonance as a first radial excitation of the 3q ground state, though the authors note that final confirmation requires complete simultaneous description of nucleon form factors and the transition amplitudes.

## Method
The authors extracted helicity amplitudes for the electroexcitation of the Roper resonance (P11(1440)) on the proton from CLAS data on differential cross sections and longitudinally polarized beam asymmetry for the process ep to en pi+ at hadronic mass W=1.15-1.69 GeV and photon virtuality Q2=1.7-4.2 GeV2. Two conceptually different approaches were used: fixed-t dispersion relations (DR) and unitary isobar model (UIM), with resonance contributions parameterized in Breit-Wigner form with energy-dependent widths. The P33(1232) resonance was treated specially using constraints from phase-shift analyses and Watson's theorem, and all resonances from the first, second, and third resonance regions were included.

## Key numbers
- Transverse helicity amplitude A1/2 at Q2=1.72 GeV2: 72.5 +/- 1.0 (stat) +/- 4.3 (sys) x 10^-3 GeV^-1/2 from DR; 58.5 +/- 1.1 (stat) +/- 4.2 (sys) x 10^-3 GeV^-1/2 from UIM
- Transverse helicity amplitude A1/2 at Q2=4.16 GeV2: 19.3 +/- 2.0 (stat) +/- 3.9 (sys) x 10^-3 GeV^-1/2 from DR; 23.1 +/- 2.2 (stat) +/- 4.8 (sys) x 10^-3 GeV^-1/2 from UIM
- Longitudinal helicity amplitude S1/2 at Q2=1.72 GeV2: 24.8 +/- 1.4 (stat) +/- 5.3 (sys) x 10^-3 GeV^-1/2 from DR; 26.9 +/- 1.3 (stat) +/- 5.3 (sys) x 10^-3 GeV^-1/2 from UIM
- Longitudinal helicity amplitude S1/2 at Q2=4.16 GeV2: 10.8 +/- 2.8 (stat) +/- 4.5 (sys) x 10^-3 GeV^-1/2 from DR; 17.5 +/- 2.6 (stat) +/- 5.5 (sys) x 10^-3 GeV^-1/2 from UIM
- The transverse amplitude crosses zero between Q2=0.4 and 0.65 GeV2 and becomes large and positive at Q2 approximately 2 GeV2
- The relative contribution of P11(1440) compared to P33(1232) in the Legendre moment D0^(T+epsilon L) is 4 times larger for gamma* p to pi+ n than for gamma* p to pi0 p

## Context
This work extends previous CLAS analyses at lower Q2 (0.4 and 0.65 GeV2) by Aznauryan et al. (2004, 2005) and Joo et al. (2002-2005), and the Q2=0 value from the Particle Data Group (2006). The results contradict predictions from Li (1992, 1993) treating P11(1440) as a q3G hybrid state, which predicted suppressed longitudinal amplitudes. The extracted amplitudes agree qualitatively with light-front relativistic quark models by Weber, Capstick, Simula, Riska, and others (1990s-2000s) that describe P11(1440) as a first radial excitation of the 3q ground state, though these models fail at Q2=0, suggesting significant meson cloud contributions as modeled by Cano and Gonzalez (1998, 1999). The results enable future work to determine the magnitude of meson cloud contributions and better specify nucleon and P11(1440) wave functions through simultaneous description of nucleon form factors and transition amplitudes.
