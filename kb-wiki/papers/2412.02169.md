# Electromagnetic interactions in elastic neutrino-nucleon scattering

> A thorough account of electromagnetic interactions of massive Dirac neutrinos as well as their spin-flavor state in the theoretical formulation of elastic neutrino-nucleon scattering is given. The formalism of neutrino charge, magnetic, electric, and anapole form factors defined as matrices in the mass basis is employed under the assumption of three-neutrino mixing. The flavor and spin change of neutrinos propagating from the source to the detector is taken into account in the form of a spin-flavor density matrix of the neutrino arriving at the detector. The potential effects of the neutrino charge radii, magnetic moments, and spin polarization in the neutrino-nucleon scattering experiments are outlined.

## Key claim

The paper provides a complete theoretical formalism for elastic neutrino-nucleon scattering that includes all four electromagnetic form factors (charge, magnetic, electric, and anapole) of massive Dirac neutrinos as matrices in the mass basis, while also accounting for the neutrino spin-flavor state arriving at the detector via a spin-flavor density matrix. The authors emphasize that the effects of neutrino electromagnetic properties are much more pronounced in neutrino-proton scattering than in neutrino-neutron scattering, and that the cross section depends on both polar and azimuthal angles of the recoil nucleon when transverse neutrino spin components are present.

## Method

The authors constructed the neutrino-nucleon scattering amplitude by combining the weak neutral current interaction (parameterized with Dirac, Pauli, axial, and pseudoscalar nucleon form factors) with the electromagnetic interaction (parameterized with neutrino charge, anapole, magnetic, and electric form factors defined as matrices in the mass basis). They employed a spin-flavor density matrix formalism to describe the neutrino state arriving at the detector, accounting for possible spin-flavor oscillations during propagation, and derived the full angular differential cross section in the laboratory frame for ultrarelativistic neutrinos scattering on free nucleons. The nucleon form factors were parametrized using the more accurate approach of Kosmas (2016) and Alberico (2009) rather than the simple dipole approximation, with strange form factors included for the axial contribution but neglected for the vector form factors.

## Key numbers

- The minimally extended Standard Model predicts diagonal Dirac neutrino magnetic moments of approximately \(3.2 \times 10^{-19} \mu_B (m_k / 1\ \text{eV})\) and zero electric dipole moments.
- The Standard Model neutrino charge radii are: \(\langle r_{ee}^2\rangle_{SM} = -4.1 \times 10^{-33}\ \text{cm}^2\), \(\langle r_{\mu\mu}^2\rangle_{SM} = -2.4 \times 10^{-33}\ \text{cm}^2\), \(\langle r_{\tau\tau}^2\rangle_{SM} = -1.5 \times 10^{-33}\ \text{cm}^2\).
- Current experimental limits on neutrino charge radii are \(|\langle r^2_{\ell\ell}\rangle| \lesssim 10^{-32}\ \text{cm}^2\) and on magnetic moments are \(\mu_\nu \lesssim 10^{-12} - 10^{-11} \mu_B\).
- The electric neutrality of matter constrains the neutrino millicharge to \(|e_{ee}| \lesssim 3 \times 10^{-21} e_0\).
- The nucleon mass used is \(m_N = 938\ \text{MeV}\), the axial coupling constant is \(g_A = 1.267\), and the axial mass is \(M_A = 1049\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of neutrino-nucleon scattering beyond the Standard Model by incorporating all four electromagnetic form factors of Dirac neutrinos (charge, magnetic, electric, and anapole) simultaneously, building on the formalism reviewed by Giunti and Studenikin (2015). It enables precise calculations for experiments such as COHERENT, CONUS, and Dresden-II that set limits on neutrino electromagnetic properties, and it provides the necessary framework for interpreting future coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CE\(\nu\)NS) experiments. The paper also connects to the work of Alvarez Ruso and Chen on neutrino-nucleon scattering at various energies and to the spin oscillation studies of Popov (2019, 2021) in astrophysical magnetic fields.
