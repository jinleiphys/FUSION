# Vector spectral functions and transport properties in quenched QCD

> We present new results on the reconstruction of mesonic spectral functions for three temperatures $1.1T_c$, $1.2T_c$ and $1.4T_c$ in quenched QCD. Making use of non-perturbatively improved clover Wilson valence quarks allows for a clean extrapolation of correlator data to the continuum limit. For the case of vanishing momentum the spectral function is obtained by fitting the data to a well motivated ansatz, using the full covariance matrix of the continuum extrapolated data in the fit. We found that vector correlation function is almost temperature independent in the current temperature window. The electrical conductivity of the hot medium, related to the origin of the vector spectral function at zero momentum, is computed from the resulting parameters at all three temperatures, leading to an estimate of $0.2C_{em}\lesssim σ/T\lesssim0.4C_{em}$. The dilepton rates resulting from the obtained spectral functions show no significant temperature dependence.

## Key claim
The vector correlation function is almost temperature independent in the temperature window from 1.1T_c to 1.4T_c, leading to an estimate of the electrical conductivity ratio sigma/T between 0.2C_em and 0.4C_em, with the caveat that this result depends on a phenomenologically motivated ansatz for the spectral function and a systematic uncertainty from applying a low-frequency cutoff.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations in quenched QCD using non-perturbatively improved clover Wilson valence quarks at three temperatures (1.1T_c, 1.2T_c, 1.4T_c) with three lattice spacings each, then extrapolated the correlation functions to the continuum limit in a^2. The vector spectral function at zero momentum was extracted by fitting a Breit-Wigner plus modified free spectral function ansatz to the continuum-extrapolated correlator data using a fully correlated chi-squared minimization with the full covariance matrix. A systematic uncertainty was assessed by introducing a low-frequency cutoff in the free part of the ansatz and varying the cutoff parameter omega_0.

## Key numbers
- Electrical conductivity ratio: 0.217 <= sigma/(C_em T) <= 0.436 at T = 1.1T_c
- Electrical conductivity ratio: 0.211 <= sigma/(C_em T) <= 0.365 at T = 1.2T_c
- Electrical conductivity ratio: 0.217 <= sigma/(C_em T) <= 0.361 at T = 1.4T_c
- Breit-Wigner width at T = 1.1T_c: Gamma/T = 2.89(1.12) for the uncut fit
- Ratio c_BW T / Gamma at T = 1.1T_c: 0.524(146) for the uncut fit
- Modification parameter k at T = 1.1T_c: 0.039(7) for the uncut fit
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom at T = 1.1T_c: 1.24 for the uncut fit
- Lattice spacings used: 1/a = 9.65, 13.21, and 19.30 GeV for the three lattice spacings at each temperature

## Context
This work extends previous investigations by Ding (2011), Francis (2012), and Kaczmarek (2013) on vector spectral functions in quenched QCD, using continuum-extrapolated correlator data rather than finite-lattice-spacing results. The electrical conductivity results are comparable to recent studies by Brandt (2013) and Amato (2013) using Maximum Entropy Method and Wilson Clover fermions at finite lattice spacing. The thermal dilepton rates show almost no temperature dependence in the analyzed region and are compared to Hard Thermal Loop calculations by Braaten (1990) and leading order (Born) rates, with improved behavior at small omega consistent with a finite electrical conductivity.
