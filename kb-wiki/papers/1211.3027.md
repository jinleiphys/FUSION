# Stringy excitation and role of UV gluons in lattice QCD

> Using SU(3) quenched lattice QCD, we study ground-state and low-lying even-parity excited-state potentials of quark-antiquark systems in terms of the gluon-momentum component in the Coulomb gauge. By introducing UV-cut in the gluon-momentum space, we investigate the "UV-gluon sensitivity" of the ground-state and excited-state potentials and the stringy excitation quantitatively. Even after cutting off high-momentum gluon component above 1.5GeV, the IR part of the ground-state potential is almost unchanged. On the other hand, the change of excited-state potential is more significant by the cut of UV-gluons. However, even after the removal of UV-gluons, the magnitude of the low-lying gluonic excitation remains to be of the order of 1GeV.

## Key claim
Even after removing high-momentum gluon components above 1.5 GeV, the infrared part of the ground-state quark-antiquark potential remains almost unchanged, but the excited-state potential changes more significantly; nevertheless, the low-lying gluonic excitation energy remains of order 1 GeV.

## Method
Using SU(3) quenched lattice QCD on a 16^4 lattice at beta=6.0 with Coulomb gauge fixing, the authors performed a three-dimensional discrete Fourier transform of the link variables and introduced a UV-cut in momentum space by setting momentum-space link-variables to zero for momenta above a chosen cutoff. They then computed Wilson-loop matrices for multiple APE-smeared quark-antiquark states and extracted ground-state and even-parity excited-state potentials by solving the secular equation from the matrix W_T^{-1} W_{T+1}.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing a = 0.104 fm, corresponding to momentum-space lattice spacing a_p = 2 pi/(L a) = 0.74 GeV.
- UV-cut values used: Lambda_UV = 3 a_p = 2.2 GeV and Lambda_UV = 2 a_p = 1.5 GeV.
- Even after cutting off gluon components above 1.5 GeV, the low-lying gluonic excitation energy remains of order 1 GeV.
- APE smearing parameter alpha = 2.3 with iteration numbers N_smr = 0, 8, 16, 24.

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Yamamoto et al. (2008) on UV-gluon contributions to the ground-state potential in the Landau gauge, now investigating both ground-state and excited-state potentials in the Coulomb gauge. It also builds on earlier lattice studies of gluonic excitations in Q\bar Q systems by Juge et al. (2002) and Takahashi et al. (2002, 2004). The results enable further investigation of odd-parity excited-state potentials and gauge-invariant analyses using Dirac mode expansion as suggested by Gongyo (2012).
