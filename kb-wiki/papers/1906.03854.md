# Correlations in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions with strings

> While string models describe initial state radiation in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions well, they mainly differ in their end-point positions of the strings in spatial rapidity. We present a generic model where wounded constituents are amended with strings whose both end-point positions fluctuate and analyze semi-analytically various scenarios of string-end-point fluctuations. In particular we constrain the different cases to experimental data on rapidity spectra from collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$~GeV, and explore their respective two-body correlations, which allows to partially discriminate the possible solutions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a generic semi-analytic model merging the wounded constituent model with string models where both string end-point positions fluctuate, and shows that while multiple solutions for string-end-point distributions can reproduce the one-body rapidity spectra, two-body correlations can partially discriminate between these solutions.

## Method

The authors combine the wounded constituent model (using GLISSANDO Monte-Carlo simulations to obtain numbers of wounded constituents) with a generic string model where each wounded source pulls exactly one string in pseudo-rapidity with fluctuating end-points. They derive analytic expressions for the one-body emission profile and two-body correlations, then study three specific cases of string-end-point distributions (the "g1=g2" case, the "disjoint case", and an intermediate case) constrained to reproduce experimental rapidity spectra from PHOBOS at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The production rate omega is constrained to lie between f(eta_max) and 2f(eta_max), where eta_max is the position of the maximum of the emission profile f(eta)
- For the Legendre coefficient a_11 of two-body correlations, the "g1=g2" case differs from the "disjoint case" by almost a factor of 3
- The covered pseudo-rapidity range used for Legendre coefficient calculations is Y=1 for RHIC
- The analysis uses experimental data at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV from PHOBOS (Back 2003, Back 2004) for d-Au and Au-Au collisions

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Broniowski (2015) and generalizes the wounded constituent model of Bialas (1976) and the wounded constituent model of Bialas (1977, 1978) and Anisovich (1977) by merging it with string models such as the Lund model (Andersson 1983, Wang 1991, Lin 2004, Sjostrand 2014, Bierlich 2018, Ferreres-Sole 2018) and the Dual Parton model (Capella 1992, Werner 2010, Pierog 2013). The approach enables future discrimination between different string-end-point distribution scenarios using two-particle correlation measurements, and confirms the scaling behavior of emission profiles previously reported by Barej (2017).
