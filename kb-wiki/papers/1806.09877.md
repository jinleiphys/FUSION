# Reduced sensitivity of the $(d, p)$ cross sections to the deuteron model beyond adiabatic approximation

> It has recently been reported [Phys. Rev. Lett. 117, 162502 (2016)] that (d, p) cross sections can be very sensitive to the n-p interactions used in the adiabatic treatment of deuteron breakup with nonlocal nucleon-target optical potentials. To understand to what extent this sensitivity could originate in the inaccuracy of the adiabatic approximation we have developed a leading-order local- equivalent continuum-discretized coupled-channel model that accounts for non-adiabatic effects in the presence of nonlocality of nucleon optical potentials. We have applied our model to the astro- physically relevant reaction $^{26m}$Al$(d, p) ^{27}$Al using two different n-p potentials associated with the lowest and the highest n-p kinetic energy in the short-range region of their interaction, respectively. Our calculations reveal a significant reduction of the sensitivity to the high n-p momenta thus confirming that it is mostly associated with theoretical uncertainties of the adiabatic approximation itself. The non-adiabatic effects in the presence of nonlocality were found to be stronger than those in the case of the local optical potentials. These results argue for extending the analysis of the $(d, p)$ reactions, measured for spectroscopic studies, beyond the adiabatic approximation.

# Reduced sensitivity of the (d,p) cross sections to the deuteron model beyond adiabatic approximation

## Key claim

The previously reported strong sensitivity of (d,p) cross sections to the choice of n-p interaction in adiabatic distorted-wave approximation (ADWA) calculations with nonlocal nucleon-target optical potentials is significantly reduced when deuteron breakup is treated beyond the adiabatic approximation using a continuum-discretized coupled-channel (CDCC) approach. The sensitivity originates mostly from theoretical uncertainties of the adiabatic approximation itself rather than from genuine reaction mechanism dependence on the deuteron model.

## Method

The authors developed a leading-order local-equivalent CDCC model that accounts for non-adiabatic effects in the presence of nonlocality of nucleon optical potentials, using a generalized local-energy approximation to solve the coupled transcendental matrix equations for the coupling potentials. They applied this model to the astrophysically relevant reaction ²⁶ᵐAl(d,p)²⁷Al at three incident deuteron energies (9.2, 25, and 50 MeV) using two different n-p potentials, Hulthen and Reid Soft Core (RSC), which in prior ADWA work gave the lowest and highest cross sections, respectively. The local-equivalent coupling potentials were read into the FRESCO reaction code to calculate finite-range transfer cross sections.

## Key numbers

- At Ed_lab = 9.2 MeV, the ratio of ADWA cross sections between RSC and Hulthen potentials (σ_ADWA_RSC/σ_ADWA_H) ranges from 1.03 to 1.24 across final states, while the corresponding CDCC ratio (σ_CDCC_RSC/σ_CDCC_H) is reduced to between 1.00 and 1.04.
- At Ed_lab = 50 MeV, the ADWA ratio σ_ADWA_RSC/σ_ADWA_H ranges from 1.69 to 2.24, while the CDCC ratio σ_CDCC_RSC/σ_CDCC_H is reduced to between 1.05 and 1.14.
- The sensitivity to the n-p model in CDCC is less than 4% for Ed_lab = 9.2 MeV and increases up to 14% for higher energies.
- The ratio of ADWA to CDCC cross sections (σ_ADWA_H/σ_CDCC_H) ranges from 1.10 to 1.80 across energies and final states, indicating non-adiabatic effects are stronger than the average value of 1.25 reported for local optical potentials in prior work.
- Spectroscopic factors extracted from CDCC calculations at 9.2 MeV for the 0.84 MeV state are 0.13 (Hulthen) and 0.11 (RSC), compared to 0.07 from ADWA Hulthen and 0.08 from the experimental reference.

## Context

This work extends the investigation of Bai (2016), who reported strong sensitivity of (d,p) cross sections to n-p interactions in ADWA with nonlocal potentials, and addresses the puzzle that such sensitivity appeared despite low deuteron energies (~10 MeV). The authors show that the sensitivity is an artifact of the adiabatic approximation itself, and that CDCC calculations, which include couplings to continuum bins, produce nearly model-independent results. The non-adiabatic effects in the presence of nonlocality are found to be stronger than those reported for local optical potentials by Chamon (2017). This work enables more reliable extraction of spectroscopic factors and asymptotic normalization coefficients from (d,p) reactions and argues for extending theoretical analysis beyond the adiabatic approximation when nonlocal optical potentials are used.
