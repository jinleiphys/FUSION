# Azimuthally fluctuating magnetic field and its impacts on observables in heavy-ion collisions

> The heavy-ion collisions can produce extremely strong transient magnetic and electric fields. We study the azimuthal fluctuation of these fields and their correlations with the also fluctuating matter geometry (characterized by the participant plane harmonics) using event-by-event simulations. A sizable suppression of the angular correlations between the magnetic field and the 2nd and 4th harmonic participant planes is found in very central and very peripheral collisions, while the magnitudes of these correlations peak around impact parameter b~8-10 fm for RHIC collisions. This can lead to notable impacts on a number of observables related to various magnetic field induced effects, and our finding suggests that the optimal event class for measuring them should be that corresponding to b~8-10 fm.

## Key claim

The azimuthal correlation between the magnetic field direction and the participant plane harmonics (Ψ₂ and Ψ₄) is significantly suppressed in very central and very peripheral collisions, while peaking at impact parameters b ∼ 8–10 fm for RHIC collisions, meaning the common assumption that the magnetic field always points perpendicular to the reaction plane is not valid on an event-by-event basis.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event Monte Carlo Glauber simulations of Au + Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV, sampling nucleon positions from a Woods-Saxon distribution and computing the electromagnetic fields at t = 0 using Lorentz-boosted Coulomb formulas with protons treated as uniformly charged spheres of radius R_p = 0.7 fm. They determined the participant plane angles Ψ_n from the participant density distribution and analyzed the angular correlations between the magnetic field direction Ψ_B and the participant plane harmonics for a range of impact parameters.

## Key numbers

- The event-averaged magnetic field squared ⟨(eB)²⟩ and electric field squared ⟨(eE)²⟩ were computed at four spatial points on the transverse plane as functions of impact parameter b for Au + Au at √s = 200 GeV.
- The correlation factor ⟨cos[2(Ψ_B − Ψ₂)]⟩ peaks around b ∼ 8–10 fm with values −0.6 to −0.7, indicating the strongest correlation at these impact parameters.
- At b = 0 fm, the histogram of Ψ_B − Ψ₂ is almost uniform, showing negligible correlation between magnetic field direction and the second harmonic participant plane.
- The correlation ⟨cos[4(Ψ_B − Ψ₄)]⟩ shows peak values ∼ 0.2 at moderate impact parameters, weaker than the n = 2 correlation.
- Varying the proton radius R_p from 0.6 fm to 0.9 fm shifts numerical results within 15%.

## Context

This work extends earlier event-by-event calculations of magnetic field magnitude (arXiv:1111.1949, arXiv:1201.5108) by focusing on azimuthal fluctuations and their correlation with fluctuating matter geometry. The findings directly impact the interpretation of observables related to the Chiral Magnetic Effect (Kharzeev 2004, Kharzeev 2007), Chiral Magnetic Wave (Burnier 2011), and soft photon production from conformal anomaly (Basar 2012), all of which assume the magnetic field points perpendicular to the reaction plane. The results suggest that the optimal event class for measuring magnetic-field-induced effects corresponds to b ∼ 8–10 fm, and that the correlation between field strength and orientation is negligible.
