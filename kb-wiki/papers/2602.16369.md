# Rapidity dependence of mean transverse momentum fluctuation and decorrelation in baryon-dense medium

> I study the event-by-event fluctuation and rapidity decorrelation of the mean transverse momentum $\spt$, which has recently been proposed as a sensitive probe of the equation of state at finite baryon density. The investigation reveals that, in a baryon-rich medium, the event-by-event fluctuation of the mean transverse momentum is driven by the combined effects of energy-density and net-baryon-density fluctuations. Consequently, the rapidity dependence of this observable provides a promising handle to probe the three-dimensional structure of both energy and baryon density profiles. Previous studies have shown that $\spt$ decorrelation along rapidity is largely insensitive to shear and bulk viscosity; however, its dependence on baryon diffusion, another key transport coefficient in baryonic matter, has not been explored. I find that baryon diffusion has a negligible impact, establishing this observable as a robust probe of the equation of state. Furthermore, I present predictions for identified hadrons and observe a pronounced splitting in the rapidity decorrelation of mean transverse momentum between protons and antiprotons, indicating different transverse flow dynamics for baryons and antibaryons.

# Rapidity dependence of mean transverse momentum fluctuation and decorrelation in baryon-dense medium

## Key claim
The event-by-event fluctuation of mean transverse momentum in baryon-rich matter arises from combined energy-density and net-baryon-density fluctuations, not solely from size fluctuations as in baryon-free matter; the rapidity dependence of this observable is largely model-independent once the longitudinal profiles of energy and baryon density are constrained by experimental data, and baryon diffusion has negligible impact on the decorrelation observables.

## Method
The study uses a hybrid framework combining (3+1)D relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (MUSIC) with a hadronic afterburner (UrQMD) for 0–10% central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV. Initial conditions employ a tilted fireball model for energy density and a parametrized net-baryon density distribution with participant and binary-collision contributions, tuned to reproduce charged-particle pseudorapidity distributions and net-proton rapidity distributions from NA49 and STAR data. Smooth initial profiles with controlled 10% variations in energy density and net-baryon density normalizations are compared to event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions to isolate the sources of mean transverse momentum fluctuations.

## Key numbers
- The specific shear viscosity is fixed at eta T / (epsilon + P) = 0.08, with a temperature-dependent bulk viscosity peaking at T_0 = 0.16 GeV with B_zeta = 0.13 and width sigma_zeta = 0.01 GeV (T < T_0) or 0.12 GeV (T > T_0).
- The switching energy density for particlization is epsilon_SW = 0.26 GeV/fm^3.
- At |eta| approximately 2, energy-density variation alone reduces v_0 by a factor of roughly 3 relative to midrapidity, while baryon-density variation alone enhances v_0 by a factor of roughly 2, yielding a net reduction factor of about 3/2.
- The proton–antiproton splitting in R_pT is approximately 30%, while the splitting in the covariance term alone is around 10%, indicating the variance (sigma_pT) dominates the splitting.
- Model results underestimate the STAR measurement of sigma_pT / <p_T> at midrapidity, suggesting insufficient initial-state fluctuations in the present setup.

## Context
This work extends studies of mean transverse momentum fluctuations from baryon-free matter at LHC and top RHIC energies (Gardim 2019, Schenke 2020, Parida 2024) into the baryon-dense regime relevant to the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program. It builds on the finding by Liu (2025) that rapidity decorrelation of mean transverse momentum is sensitive to the equation of state but insensitive to shear and bulk viscosity, and demonstrates that baryon diffusion (parametrized via coefficient C_B following Denicol 2018) also has negligible impact, establishing these observables as robust probes of the equation of state at finite baryon density. The observed proton–antiproton splitting in the decorrelation observables provides a new handle on baryon stopping and baryon-density-dependent transport coefficients, complementing studies of directed flow splitting (Parida 2022, 2025) and v_0(p_T) splitting (Jahan 2025).
