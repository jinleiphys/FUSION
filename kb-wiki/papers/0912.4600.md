# Nonextensive relativistic nuclear and subnuclear equation of state

> Following the basic prescriptions of the Tsallis' nonextensive relativistic thermodynamics, we investigate the relevance of nonextensive statistical effects on the relativistic nuclear and subnuclear equation of state. In this framework, we study the first order phase transition from hadronic to quark-gluon plasma phase by requiring the Gibbs conditions on the global conservation of the electric and the baryon charges. The relevance of small deviations from the standard extensive statistics is investigated in the context of intermediate and high energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Small deviations from standard extensive statistics (q > 1) can significantly affect the relativistic nuclear and quark-gluon equation of state, particularly at high baryon density and energy density, and lower the critical density for the onset of the hadron-quark mixed phase in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors apply Tsallis' nonextensive relativistic thermodynamics to both a hadronic equation of state (using a relativistic mean-field model with σ, ω, ρ mesons and the GM2 parameter set) and a quark-gluon plasma equation of state (using the MIT bag model with massless up and down quarks and gluons). They then construct the mixed hadron-quark phase by imposing Gibbs conditions for global conservation of baryon number and isospin charge, solving the coupled equations numerically for different values of the nonextensivity parameter q.

## Key numbers

- For the mixed hadron-quark phase at T=60 MeV and Z/A=0.4, the transition starts at ρ=3.75 ρ₀ for q=1, at ρ=3.31 ρ₀ for q=1.05, and at ρ=2.72 ρ₀ for q=1.1.
- The second critical density (end of mixed phase, onset of pure quark-gluon matter) at T=60 MeV and Z/A=0.4 is ρ=5.57 ρ₀ for q=1, ρ=5.0 ρ₀ for q=1.05, and ρ=4.29 ρ₀ for q=1.1.
- The nuclear matter saturation density is taken as ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³.
- The critical temperature for deconfinement is T_c ≈ 170 MeV, with critical energy density ε_c ≈ 1 GeV/fm³.
- The bag parameter is fixed at B^{1/4}=170 MeV.
- The hadronic EOS and QGP EOS are plotted at T=100 MeV and Z/A=0.4.

## Context

This work extends the application of Tsallis nonextensive statistics (Tsallis 1988) to relativistic nuclear and subnuclear equations of state, building on prior studies of nonextensive effects in heavy-ion collisions by Albergo (2000), Biro (2005), and Wilk (2008). It also follows the Gibbs formalism for phase transitions with two conserved charges developed by Glendenning (1992), and uses the relativistic mean-field model of Walecka (1974) and Boguta-Bodmer (1977) with the GM2 parameter set from Glendenning (1985). The results enable future hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions at facilities such as FAIR at GSI, and suggest that nonextensive effects must be considered when extracting the equation of state from experimental data at high baryon densities.
