# Electron- and neutrino-nucleus scattering

> I review the main features of the nuclear response extracted from electron scattering data. The emerging picture clearly shows that the shell model does not provide a fully quantitative description of nuclear dynamics. On the other hand, many body approaches in which correlation effetcs are explicitely taken into account lead to a satisfactory account of electron scattering observables. The possibility of exploiting the knowledge acquired from electron scattering to reduce the systematic uncertainty of neutrino oscillation experiments is outlined.

## Key claim

The shell model fails to provide a fully quantitative description of nuclear dynamics, and many-body approaches that explicitly include nucleon-nucleon correlation effects are necessary to achieve a satisfactory account of electron scattering observables; the knowledge gained from electron scattering can be exploited to reduce the systematic uncertainty in neutrino oscillation experiments, but this depends on the ability to implement the theoretical knowledge in Monte Carlo simulations.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental evidence from proton knock-out (e,e'p) measurements across multiple laboratories, showing that spectroscopic strengths for valence shell model states are consistently lower than shell model predictions. It then describes how nuclear many-body theory (NMBT), using nonrelativistic Hamiltonians with two-body and three-nucleon interactions fitted to deuteron properties and scattering data, can be solved exactly via stochastic methods for nuclei up to mass number 10 and accurately for uniform nuclear matter, yielding spectral functions that account for correlation effects.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum is approximately 200 MeV; Fermi energy is approximately 30 MeV
- For a correlated nucleon pair, knock-out of a large-momentum nucleon requires energy E ≈ E_thr + p^2/(2m), where p is much larger than the Fermi momentum
- Systematic proton knock-out studies at Jefferson Lab extend to momenta of approximately 700 MeV and energies of approximately 200 MeV
- In lead, nucleon-nucleon correlations produce an approximately 20% effect on the transparency ratio T_A(Q^2)
- Realistic spectral functions yield average removal energy ⟨E⟩ = 40-65 MeV, compared to the Fermi gas model range of 25-35 MeV
- In the Fermi gas model, all nucleon momenta are lower than the Fermi momentum p_F ∼ 200 MeV, with typical removal energies in the range 25-35 MeV

## Context

This review extends the critique of the independent particle model initiated by the Frascati group in the mid-1960s and systematically pursued over four decades, showing that correlation effects are essential. The work enables a strategy to replace the Fermi gas model (Moniz 1969, Moniz 1971) used in neutrino oscillation analyses with parameter-free NMBT calculations, with the goal of reducing systematic uncertainties in next-generation high-precision neutrino experiments.
