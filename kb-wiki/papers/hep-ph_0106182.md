# Parametric resonance at the critical temperature in high energy heavy ion collisions

> Parametric resonance in soft modes at the critical temperature ($T_{c}$) in high energy heavy ion collisions is studied in the case when the temperature ($T$) of the system is almost constant for a long time. By deviding the fields into three parts, zero mode (condensate), soft modes and hard modes and assuming that the hard modes are in thermal equilibrium, we derive the equation of motion for soft modes at $T=T_{c}$. Enhanced modes are extracted by comparing with the Mathieu equation for the condensate oscillating along the sigma axis at $T=T_{c}$. It is found that the soft mode of $π$ fields at about 174 MeV is enhanced.

## Key claim

Parametric resonance can amplify soft pion modes at the critical temperature \(T_c\) in high energy heavy ion collisions, provided the temperature remains nearly constant for a extended period (e.g., 10 fm or more) and the condensate oscillates along the sigma axis. The amplification is conditional on the explicit symmetry breaking term being nonzero, as it generates nonzero condensate and masses at \(T_c\); in the chiral limit (\(H=0\)) the resonance structure may change essentially.

## Method

The linear \(\sigma\) model fields are divided into zero mode (condensate), soft modes, and hard modes, with hard modes assumed to be in thermal equilibrium. The equation of motion for soft modes at \(T=T_c\) is derived by expanding around the minimum of the effective potential and keeping only terms linear in the soft fields and the condensate fluctuation. The resulting equations for the sigma and pion soft modes are cast into Mathieu equations by substituting the oscillatory solution for the condensate fluctuation \(\delta\phi_{0c} = -B\cos(m_0 t + \theta)\) and transforming the time variable. Amplified modes are identified from the Mathieu equation parameters \(A_\sigma\) and \(A_\pi\), and the cutoff \(k_\Lambda\) between soft and hard modes is determined self-consistently from the condition defining \(T_c\) using a massless particle approximation.

## Key numbers

- The amplified soft mode of the \(\pi\) field is at about **174 MeV** (momentum \(k_\pi \sim 174\) MeV) for \(T_c\) above 130 MeV.
- The model parameters used are \(\lambda = 20\), \(H^{1/3} = 119\) MeV, \(v = 87.4\) MeV, which yield \(m_\pi = 135\) MeV, \(m_\sigma = 600\) MeV, and \(f_\pi = 92.5\) MeV at \(T=0\).
- The condensate at \(T_c\) is \(\bar{\phi}_{0c} = (4H/\lambda)^{1/3} \approx 69.6\) MeV, giving \(m_0 \approx 467\) MeV.
- The Mathieu equation factor for the pion is \(2Q_\pi = 0.051\ (\text{MeV}^{-1}) \times B\); for \(B = -30\) MeV, \(2Q_\pi = -1.53\).
- The characteristic exponent gives \(|e^{i\nu\pi}| \sim 3.1\) for \(A_\pi=1\) and \(-2Q_\pi/A_\pi = 1.53\), corresponding to strong amplification within about **2.65 fm** (one period in \(\xi\)) and a few (5 or more) fm.
- The cutoff \(k_\Lambda\) is above 190 MeV for \(T_c \ge 140\) MeV, ensuring the 174 MeV mode is a soft mode.
- \(T_c\) is about 123 MeV in the chiral limit; lattice QCD estimates place it between 140 MeV and 190 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of parametric resonance in the chiral phase transition at zero temperature (Muller, Kaiser, Hiro-Oka, Dumitru, Ishihara, Sornborger, Maedan) to the finite-temperature case at \(T_c\). It specifically builds on the finding by Ishihara (2001) that the condensate motion in high energy heavy ion collisions is expected to be almost along the sigma axis. The paper enables future studies to incorporate the amplified soft mode at 174 MeV as an initial condition for subsequent parametric resonance at zero temperature, potentially producing observable peaks in the final pion momentum distribution. Open questions include the strength of the peak, the time interval over which the temperature remains constant, non-linear effects, and back reaction.
