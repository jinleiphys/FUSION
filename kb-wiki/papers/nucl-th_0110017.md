# Relativisitic mean field theory of surface pion condensation in finite nuclei

> We study the possible occurrence of surface pion condensation in finite nuclei in the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory. We are led to this conjecture due to the essential role of pions in few-body systems and the recent (p,n) experiments performed at RCNP for spin-isospin excitations of medium and heavy nuclei. We calculate explicitly various N=Z closed shell nuclei with finite pion mean field in the RMF framework and demonstrate the actual occurrence of surface pion condensation.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a finite pion mean field can exist in finite N=Z closed shell nuclei within the relativistic mean field (RMF) framework, naming this phenomenon "surface pion condensation" because the pion energy scales with or stronger than the nuclear surface area, even though pion condensation is known not to occur in infinite nuclear matter at saturation density.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic mean field equations including a pseudovector pion-nucleon coupling term, using the TM1 parameter set for the meson-nucleon couplings and the Bonn A value for the pion-nucleon coupling constant. They calculate self-consistent solutions for seven N=Z closed shell nuclei (12C, 16O, 40Ca, 56Ni, 80Zr, 100Sn, 164Pb) both with and without a finite pion mean field, allowing parity mixing in the single-particle wave functions while assuming spherical symmetry for the intrinsic state.

## Key numbers

- 12C: binding energy increases from 98 MeV (without pion field) to 116 MeV (with pion field), with pion energy contribution of -108 MeV
- 16O: binding energy increases from 148 MeV to 148 MeV (no change), with pion energy contribution of -14 MeV
- 40Ca: binding energy increases from 427 MeV to 431 MeV, with pion energy contribution of -81 MeV
- 56Ni: binding energy increases from 626 MeV to 650 MeV, with pion energy contribution of -281 MeV
- 80Zr: binding energy unchanged at 931 MeV, with pion energy contribution of -43 MeV
- 100Sn: binding energy increases from 1214 MeV to 1231 MeV, with pion energy contribution of -326 MeV
- 164Pb: binding energy increases from 2094 MeV to 2109 MeV, with pion energy contribution of -409 MeV
- The pion energy per nucleon for jj-closed shell nuclei decreases more steeply than A^{-1/3} as a function of mass number

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean field approach of Sugahara (1994) by explicitly including the pion degree of freedom, motivated by experimental (p,n) data from Wakasa (1997, 1999) at RCNP showing missing Gamow-Teller strength and suppressed longitudinal spin response. The authors contrast their finite-nucleus result with the established conclusion from Toki (1979) and Oset (1982) that pion condensation does not occur in infinite nuclear matter at saturation density. The parity projection analysis connects to earlier work by Akaishi (1986) on the alpha particle and by Terasawa (1960) on the tensor force contribution to spin-orbit splitting.
