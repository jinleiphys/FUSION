# Measuring Shear Viscosity Using Transverse Momentum Correlations in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> Elliptic flow measurements at RHIC suggest that quark gluon plasma flows with very little viscosity compared to weak coupling expectations, challenging theorists to explain why this fluid is so nearly ``perfect''. It is therefore vital to find quantitative experimental information on the viscosity of the plasma. We propose that measurements of transverse momentum fluctuations can be used to determine the shear viscosity. We use current data to estimate the viscosity-to-entropy ratio in the range from 0.08 to 0.3, and discuss how future measurements can reduce this uncertainty.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that measurements of transverse momentum fluctuations can be used to determine the shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma, and uses current data to estimate the viscosity-to-entropy ratio in the range from 0.08 to 0.3, but this estimate carries significant model assumptions and the uncertainty range greatly exceeds the experimental uncertainty.

## Method

The authors develop a diffusion model for the radial momentum current density, showing that shear viscosity broadens the rapidity profile of transverse momentum correlations via a diffusion equation. They relate the transverse momentum covariance observable to the spatial correlation function of the momentum current, and then use the rapidity dependence of this covariance to extract viscosity information. The method is applied to STAR collaboration data on transverse momentum fluctuations, using the observed centrality dependence of the rapidity width to estimate the kinematic viscosity.

## Key numbers

- Viscosity-to-entropy ratio estimated from data: η/s ∼ 0.08 (lower bound) to 0.3 (upper bound)
- The lower bound η/s ∼ 0.08 corresponds to kinematic viscosity ν ∼ 0.09 fm, derived from the difference in rapidity widths between central and peripheral collisions assuming freeze-out times τ_F,c ∼ 20 fm and τ_F,p ∼ 1 fm
- The upper bound η/s = 0.3 corresponds to the case where the momentum current correlation width σ is at most twice the measured width σ_* (σ = 2σ_*)
- STAR collaboration reports a rapidity width σ_* increasing from roughly 0.45 in the most peripheral collisions to 0.75 in central collisions
- The supersymmetric Yang-Mills strong coupling limit gives η/s = 1/4π as a reference value
- Hadron gas and perturbative QCD estimates give η/s ∼ 1 near the crossover temperature ∼ 170 MeV

## Context

This work extends efforts to extract viscosity information from relativistic heavy-ion collisions beyond elliptic flow measurements, which had suggested a nearly perfect liquid (Kolb et al., Hirano and Gyulassy). The proposed method is analogous to ultrasonic attenuation in common fluids, contrasting with the approach of comparing viscous hydrodynamic calculations to radial and elliptic flow data (derek0). The paper enables future experimental measurements of the transverse momentum covariance as a function of rapidity acceptance to reduce the uncertainty range, and suggests comparing with net charge fluctuation measurements (Mohamed and Abdel-Aziz) and freeze-out time determinations (Lisa).
