# Etaprime and Eta Mesons with Connection to Anomalous Glue

> We review the present understanding of etaprime and eta meson physics and these mesons as a probe of gluon dynamics in low-energy QCD. Recent highlights include the production mechanism of eta and etaprime mesons in proton-nucleon collisions from threshold to high-energy, the etaprime effective mass shift in the nuclear medium, searches for possible eta and etaprime bound states in nuclei as well as precision measurements of eta decays as a probe of light-quark masses. We discuss recent experimental data, theoretical interpretation of the different measurements and the open questions and challenges for future investigation.

## Key claim

The $\eta'$ meson receives an extra mass contribution of approximately 300-400 MeV beyond what would be expected from pure Goldstone boson dynamics, and this mass is generated by non-perturbative gluon dynamics through the axial anomaly, with the exact details of this gluon dynamics still debated.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework of QCD symmetries, chiral symmetry breaking, and the axial anomaly to explain the $\eta$ and $\eta'$ meson masses. They present the $\eta$-$\eta'$ mass matrix in the octet-singlet basis and derive the Witten-Veneziano mass formula, which relates the gluonic mass term to the topological susceptibility of pure Yang-Mills theory. The review surveys experimental data from production experiments in proton-nucleon collisions, photoproduction, nuclear medium studies, and high-energy scattering processes.

## Key numbers

- The $\eta'$ meson mass is 958 MeV and the $\eta$ meson mass is 548 MeV, compared to the pion mass of 135 MeV
- The gluonic mass term extracted from the Witten-Veneziano formula is ${\tilde m}_{\eta_0}^2 = 0.73$ GeV$^2$
- The $\eta'$ effective mass shift in nuclear medium is about -40 MeV at nuclear matter density, measured in photoproduction experiments in Bonn
- Lattice QCD calculations give $\chi^{1/4}(0)|_{\rm YM} = 185.3 \pm 5.6$ MeV for the Yang-Mills topological susceptibility
- The $\eta$-$\eta'$ mixing angle is between $-15^\circ$ and $-20^\circ$ from phenomenological studies of decay processes
- The QCD scale parameter for three flavors is $\Lambda_{\rm QCD} = 332 \pm 17$ MeV
- Light quark masses are $m_u = 2.2^{+0.6}_{-0.4}$ MeV, $m_d = 4.7^{+0.5}_{-0.3}$ MeV, and $m_s = 95 \pm 5$ MeV at scale $\mu = 2$ GeV
- Claims from RHIC data suggest possible $\eta'$ mass suppression at finite temperature of at least -200 MeV

## Context

This review extends earlier reviews on $\eta$ and $\eta'$ meson physics, including the volume edited by Bijnens (2002), the theoretical overview by Shore (2007) on gluonic effects, and the reviews of meson production by Moskal (2002), Krusche (2014), and Wilkin (2016). The work connects experimental programs on $\eta$ and $\eta'$ production near threshold and in nuclear media to fundamental questions about gluon dynamics in low-energy QCD, and enables future searches for meson-nucleus bound states and studies of QCD symmetries at finite density and temperature.
