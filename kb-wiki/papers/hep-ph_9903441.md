# Charm in the Nucleon

> A next-to-leading order analysis of inelastic electroproduction of charm is performed using an interpolating scheme which maps smoothly onto massless QCD evolution at large Q^2 and photon-gluon fusion at small Q^2. In contrast with earlier analyses, this scheme allows the inclusion of quark and target mass effects and heavy quark thresholds, as well as possible non-perturbative, or intrinsic, charm contributions. We find no conclusive evidence in favor of an intrinsic charm component in the nucleon, although several data points which disagree with perturbative QCD expectations will need to be checked by future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper finds no conclusive evidence for an intrinsic charm component in the nucleon, but notes that several data points at large x and high Q² disagree with perturbative QCD expectations and will need confirmation by future experiments; the analysis uses an interpolating scheme that includes quark and target mass effects and heavy quark thresholds, which earlier analyses lacked.

## Method

The authors performed a next-to-leading order analysis of inelastic charm electroproduction using an interpolating scheme that smoothly connects massless QCD evolution (Variable Flavor Number Scheme) at high Q² with photon-gluon fusion (Fixed Flavor Number Scheme) at low Q². They incorporated full quark mass dependence in both the gluon and quark coefficient functions, including recent work by Kretzer and Schienbein, and tested two intrinsic charm models: a five-quark Fock state model (IC1) and a meson cloud model (IC2) with D meson and Lambda_c baryon fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- The H1 and ZEUS Collaborations at HERA probed the charm structure function down to x = 8 × 10⁻⁴ and 2 × 10⁻⁴, respectively
- At small x, the charm contribution to the total proton structure function F₂ᵖ is around 25%
- The European Muon Collaboration at CERN found charm contributed only about 1% of F₂ᵖ at somewhat larger x
- Earlier analyses by Harris, Smith and Vogt found data at ν ~ 170 GeV could best be fitted with an additional 0.86 ± 0.60% intrinsic charm component
- The best fits in the present analysis were obtained with a 0.75% IC1 charm distribution and with a 0.4% IC2 distribution
- For the IC2 model, a 1% normalization corresponds to a cutoff Λ ≈ 2.2 GeV; for a probability of 0.5%, one would need Λ ≈ 1.7 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses by Hoffman and Moore (1990s) and Harris, Smith and Vogt (1990s) that used photon-gluon fusion with intrinsic charm, and contradicts the conclusions of global parton distribution fits by MRST and GRV (1990s) which found no need for intrinsic charm. The interpolating scheme enables future experiments to test whether the large-x, high-Q² EMC data points that lie above perturbative QCD predictions are genuine evidence for non-perturbative charm or statistical fluctuations.
