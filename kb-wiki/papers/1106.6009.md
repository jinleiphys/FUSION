# Anisotropic collective flow of a Lorentz gas

## Key claim

The paper presents analytical results for anisotropic flow coefficients in a Lorentz gas of massless particles scattering on fixed centres, showing that qualitative features such as harmonic mixing, flow evolution without spatial asymmetry, and non-monotonic time evolution can arise even in a regime of very few rescatterings per particle. The authors stress that the qualitative features are more robust and important than the quantitative results, and that the Lorentz gas model may have little relevance for heavy-ion collision phenomenology but serves to illustrate general behaviours.

## Method

The authors solve the Boltzmann–Lorentz kinetic equation for a two-dimensional gas of massless particles scattering elastically on fixed scattering centres with Gaussian spatial profiles. They consider small deviations from free-streaming solutions (few rescatterings per particle) and compute the time evolution of anisotropic flow harmonics \(v_n\) by integrating the collision integral. Three cases are treated analytically: isotropic initial momentum distribution with isotropic cross section, anisotropic initial momentum distribution with isotropic cross section, and isotropic initial momentum distribution with anisotropic cross section.

## Key numbers

- Total number of rescatterings for isotropic case: \({\cal N}_{\rm scat.} = \frac{N_c N\sigma_{\rm el.}}{2\pi^{3/2}R} \sqrt{1-\epsilon} K\left(\frac{2\epsilon}{1+\epsilon}\right)\), where \(K\) is the complete elliptic integral of the first kind.
- For isotropic initial momentum and isotropic cross section, \(v_n(p_T)\) scales as \(\epsilon^{n/2}\) for small eccentricities \(\epsilon\).
- For even \(n\), the final flow coefficient is \(v_n(p_T) = (-1)^{n/2+1}\frac{N_c\sigma_{\rm el.}\sqrt{1-\epsilon^2}}{4\sqrt{\pi}R} \frac{(n-1)!!}{2^n (n/2)!} {}_2F_1\left(\frac{n+1}{4},\frac{n+3}{4};\frac{n}{2}+1;\epsilon^2\right) \epsilon^{n/2}\).
- For anisotropic cross section with coefficient \(\varsigma_2\), elliptic flow becomes \(v_2(p_T) = (1-\varsigma_2) \frac{N_c\sigma_{\rm el.}\sqrt{1-\epsilon^2}}{16\sqrt{\pi}R} {}_2F_1\left(\frac{3}{4},\frac{5}{4};2;\epsilon^2\right) \epsilon\).
- At early times, \(|v_n(t,p_T)| \propto t^{n+1}\) for the isotropic case.
- For anisotropic cross section, the gain term contributes only to \(v_2\) and vanishes for \(n \neq 2\).

## Context

This work extends the analytical approach of Heiselberg (1998) for anisotropic flow to the regime of very few rescatterings, using a Lorentz gas as a simplified limiting case of light particles scattering on massive ones as in Borghini (2010). The results show that complex qualitative behaviours like harmonic mixing and non-monotonic time evolution are not exclusive to fluid-dynamic descriptions with many rescatterings, but can appear at very early times as in Krasnitz (2002) and Broniowski (2008), or around kinetic freeze-out. The findings enable understanding of how flow harmonics evolve and mix even in dilute systems, with implications for interpreting early-time dynamics and fragile states in heavy-ion collisions.
