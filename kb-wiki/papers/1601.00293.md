# Massive dark photons in a Higgs portal model

> An extension of the Standard Model with a hidden sector which consists of gauge singlets (a Dirac fermion $χ$ and a scalar $S$) plus a vector boson $V_μ$ (dark massive photon) is studied. The singlet scalar interacts with the Standard Model sector through the triple and quartic scalar interactions, while the singlet fermion and vector boson field interact with the Standard Model only via the singlet scalar. The scalar field generates the vector boson's mass. Perspectives for future $e^{-}e^{+}$ colliders is considered.

## Key claim

The paper presents a renormalizable extension of the Standard Model with a hidden sector containing a Dirac fermion, a scalar, and a massive vector boson (dark photon), where the dark sector interacts with the Standard Model only through the scalar field (Higgs portal). The author finds that the pair production cross-section for dark photons in electron-positron collisions is too small to be observed at future colliders unless very high luminosities are made available.

## Method

The model is defined by a Lagrangian density that includes a singlet Dirac fermion χ, a singlet scalar S, and a vector boson Vμ (dark photon), with the scalar S interacting with the Standard Model Higgs doublet through triple and quartic scalar interactions. Spontaneous symmetry breaking is considered in both the Standard Model and hidden sectors, producing two mixed physical Higgs bosons h1 and h2, with the Standard Model Higgs identified as h1 with mass 125 GeV. The partial decay width of h1 into dark photons and the cross-section for e−e+ → VV annihilation are calculated analytically, and the parameter space is explored for different mixing angles and scalar masses.

## Key numbers

- Standard Model Higgs mass: m1 = 125 GeV
- Total Higgs decay width used: Γtot = 6.1 MeV
- Branching ratio assumed for h1 → VV channel: 10%
- Cross-section evaluated at center-of-mass energies: √s = 500 GeV (relevant for ILC) and √s = 3 TeV (relevant for CERN CLIC)
- Mixing angle used in cross-section calculation: θ = 10°
- Coupling λV set to approximately 1 in cross-section calculation

## Context

This work extends the class of Higgs portal dark matter models, where the interaction between the Standard Model and the dark sector occurs exclusively through the Higgs boson, as previously studied in singlet scalar extensions. The paper considers perspectives for future electron-positron colliders such as the ILC and CLIC, and notes that current LHC studies of Higgs physics may face difficulties in observing dark matter effects due to extremely small pair production cross-sections.
