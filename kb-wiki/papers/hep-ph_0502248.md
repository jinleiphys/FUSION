# Energy Loss of Leading Hadrons and Direct Photon production in Evolving Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We calculate the nuclear modification factor of neutral pions and the photon yield at high p_T in central Au-Au collisions at RHIC (\sqrt{s}=200 GeV) and Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC (\sqrt{s}=5500 GeV). A leading-order accurate treatment of jet energy loss in the medium has been convolved with a physical description of the initial spatial distribution of jets and a (1+1) dimensional expansion. We reproduce the nuclear modification factor of pion R_{AA} at RHIC, assuming an initial temperature T_i=370 MeV and a formation time τ_i=0.26 fm/c, corresponding to dN/dy=1260. The resulting suppression depends on the particle rapidity density dN/dy but weakly on the initial temperature. The jet energy loss treatment is also included in the calculation of high p_T photons. Photons coming from primordial hard N-N scattering are the dominant contribution at RHIC for p_T > 5 GeV, while at the LHC, the range 8<p_T<14 GeV is dominated by jet-photon conversion in the plasma.

## Key claim

The paper reproduces the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for neutral pions at RHIC using a leading-order thermal QCD treatment of jet energy loss that includes thermal absorption and emission processes, but the result depends primarily on the particle rapidity density \(dN/dy\) and only weakly on the assumed initial temperature, meaning the extracted medium parameters are not uniquely constrained.

## Method

The authors used the Arnold-Moore-Yaffe (AMY) formalism to compute leading-order transition rates for gluon bremsstrahlung, absorption, and pair annihilation in a thermal quark-gluon plasma, solving coupled Fokker-Planck rate equations for hard quark and gluon distributions. They convolved these energy-loss probabilities with a (1+1)-dimensional Bjorken-expanding medium having a transverse temperature profile, a spatial distribution of jet production points proportional to the nuclear thickness functions, and vacuum fragmentation functions, to obtain the final pion spectra in central Au-Au collisions at RHIC and Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature \(T_i = 370\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and formation time \(\tau_i = 0.26\ \mathrm{fm}/c\) reproduce the RHIC pion \(R_{AA}\) data, corresponding to \(dN/dy = 1260\).
- An alternative initial condition with \(T_i = 447\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and \(\tau_i = 0.147\ \mathrm{fm}/c\) also yields \(dN/dy \sim 1260\).
- The strong coupling constant is fixed to \(\alpha_s = 0.3\) throughout the calculation.
- At RHIC (\(\sqrt{s}=200\ \mathrm{GeV}\)), primordial hard N-N scattering dominates the direct photon yield for \(p_T > 5\ \mathrm{GeV}\); at the LHC (\(\sqrt{s}=5500\ \mathrm{GeV}\)), jet-photon conversion in the plasma dominates for \(8 < p_T < 14\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
- The critical temperature for the phase transition is taken as \(T_c = 160\ \mathrm{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier AMY-based calculation of Jeon and Moore (2005) by implementing a full spatial and temporal evolution of the medium, including a transverse density profile and a (1+1)-dimensional Bjorken expansion. It contrasts with prior approaches such as BDMPS, GLV, and Kovner-Wiedemann, which assumed static scatterers and omitted thermal absorption and annihilation processes; the authors note that Wang and Wang (2001) introduced thermal absorption only to first order in opacity. The results enable quantitative predictions for both pion suppression and direct photon production at LHC energies, and they suggest that the observed flattening of \(R_{AA}\) near \(p_T \sim 3\ \mathrm{GeV}\) may be due to thermal gluon absorption rather than the Cronin effect.
