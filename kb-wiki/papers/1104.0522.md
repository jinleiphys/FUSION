# Topological Fluctuations in Dense Matter with Two Colors

> We study the topological charge fluctuations of an SU(2) lattice gauge theory containing both N_f=2 and 4 flavors of Wilson fermion, at low temperature with non-zero chemical potential $μ$. The topological susceptibility, chi_T, is used to characterize differing physical regimes as mu is varied between the onset of matter at mu_o and and color deconfinement at mu_d. Suppression of instantons by matter via Debye screening is also investigated, revealing effects not captured by perturbative predictions. In particular, the breaking of scale invariance leads to the mean instanton size rho becoming mu-dependent in the regime between onset and deconfinement, with a scaling rho~1/mu^2 over the range mu_o<mu<mu_d, resulting in an enhancement of chi_T immediately above onset.

# Topological Fluctuations in Dense Matter with Two Colors

## Key claim

The topological susceptibility in SU(2) lattice gauge theory with N_f=4 flavors shows an *increase* immediately above the onset chemical potential, contrary to perturbative predictions of suppression; this enhancement is explained by the mean instanton size becoming density-dependent with scaling $\bar{\rho} \propto \mu^{-2}$ in the regime between onset and deconfinement, an effect not captured by perturbative calculations that assume fixed instanton size.

## Method

The study uses Monte Carlo-generated gauge field ensembles on a $12^3 \times 24$ lattice with inverse coupling $\beta=1.9$, employing Wilson fermions with N_f=2 and N_f=4 flavors at matched pion mass $m_\pi a=0.68(1)$. Topological charge is measured via the lattice plaquette definition of $F\tilde{F}$, with UV fluctuations removed by cooling (10 or 20 cooling steps). The topological susceptibility $\chi_T = \langle Q^2 \rangle / V$ is extracted from cooled configurations, and instanton sizes are determined from peak topological charge density using the classical relation $q_{\text{peak}} = 6/(\pi^2 \rho^4)$ with iterative bisection.

## Key numbers

- For N_f=2: $\chi_T^{1/4} = 156 \pm 3$ MeV (assuming $\sigma = (440 \text{ MeV})^2$)
- For N_f=4: $\chi_T^{1/4} = 213 \pm 9$ MeV (assuming $\sigma = (440 \text{ MeV})^2$)
- The N_f=4 topological susceptibility peak value is approximately $0.5$ times the N_f=2 value
- For N_f=2 at $a\mu=0.25$, average instanton size $\bar{\rho} \sim 0.28$ fm, with majority in range $0.18 \leq \rho \leq 0.5$ fm
- For N_f=4, average instanton size reaches minimum $\bar{\rho} \sim 0.14$ fm at $a\mu \sim 0.9$
- Deconfinement chemical potential identified as $a\mu_d \approx 0.75$ from Polyakov loop behavior
- Best fit for $\chi_T^{N_f=4}$ in range $\mu_o < \mu < \mu_d$: $f(\mu) = A \exp(-B/\mu^2)$ with $A=1.8255$, $B=0.2231$

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of two-color QCD (QC$_2$D) with Wilson fermions by Hands et al. (2006, 2010, 2011), which established the phase structure including superfluid onset at $\mu_o = m_\pi/2$ and deconfinement at $\mu_d \approx 1.1 m_\pi$. It contrasts with perturbative calculations by Shuryak (1982) predicting instanton suppression via $n(\mu) \propto \exp(-N_f (\rho \mu)^2)$ for fixed $\rho$, and with earlier lattice results by Alles et al. (2006) on hot two-color matter showing sharp $\chi_T$ drop at deconfinement. The findings enable future systematic studies of topological fluctuations in dense matter on finer lattices, and suggest that non-perturbative scale breaking effects must be incorporated into instanton-based descriptions of dense QCD matter.
