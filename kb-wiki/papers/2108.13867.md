# The BEST framework for the search for the QCD critical point and the chiral magnetic effect

> The Beam Energy Scan Theory (BEST) Collaboration was formed with the goal of providing a theoretical framework for analyzing data from the Beam Energy Scan (BES) program at the relativistic heavy ion collider (RHIC) at Brookhaven National Laboratory. The physics goal of the BES program is the search for a conjectured QCD critical point as well as for manifestations of the chiral magnetic effect. We describe progress that has been made over the previous five years. This includes studies of the equation of state and equilibrium susceptibilities, the development of suitable initial state models, progress in constructing a hydrodynamic framework that includes fluctuations and anomalous transport effects, as well as the development of freezeout prescriptions and hadronic transport models. Finally, we address the challenge of integrating these components into a complete analysis framework. This document describes the collective effort of the BEST Collaboration and its collaborators around the world.

## Key claim

The BEST Collaboration has made substantial progress toward a complete dynamical framework for describing heavy-ion collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies, but the full integrated framework is not yet complete as of this writing, with several components still under development.

## Method

The collaboration developed modular components for heavy-ion collision modeling including initial state models with baryon stopping, viscous hydrodynamics extended to propagate conserved currents and anomalous transport, a flexible equation of state incorporating a critical point in the Ising universality class, particlization with local conservation laws, and hadronic transport with mean-field interactions. These components were designed as interchangeable modules with standardized interfaces to enable Bayesian analysis of experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature at zero baryon chemical potential: T0 = 156.5 ± 1.5 MeV (HotQCD Collaboration) and T0 = 158.0 ± 0.6 MeV (WB Collaboration)
- Curvature of the phase diagram: κ2 = 0.012(4) (HotQCD) and κ2 = 0.0153(18) (WB)
- Fourth-order correction to transition line: κ4 = 0.000(4) (HotQCD) and κ4 = 0.00032(67) (WB)
- No sign of criticality observed in lattice QCD simulations at baryon chemical potential μB < 300 MeV
- Taylor expansion coefficients of pressure obtained up to O((μB/T)^6) for strangeness-neutral case
- HADES measurements available at √sNN = 2.4 GeV

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD results from the HotQCD and WB Collaborations (Bazavov 2018, Borsanyi 2020) on the QCD phase diagram and equation of state, and builds upon earlier hydrodynamic frameworks developed for higher-energy RHIC and LHC collisions. The framework enables future Bayesian analysis of BES data to constrain the possible existence and location of a QCD critical point and to study anomalous transport effects, with the AVFD model already being used by the STAR Collaboration for isobar run analysis.
