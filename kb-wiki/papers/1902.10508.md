# Flavor decomposition of the pion-nucleon $σ$-term

> We re-analyze the flavor decomposition of the pion-nucleon $σ$-term in the framework of baryon chiral perturbation to fourth order. We employ a covariant and the heavy baryon framework including also the low-lying decuplet. Using only continuum data, we find a small strangeness content of the proton. The uncertainties are, however, large and might be overcome by dedicated lattice QCD calculations.

## Key claim

Using continuum data only and fourth-order baryon chiral perturbation theory (including the low-lying decuplet), the authors find a small strangeness content of the proton, but the uncertainties are large and might only be overcome by dedicated lattice QCD calculations.

## Method

The authors calculate the octet-baryon masses up to chiral order O(p^4) in both covariant (EOMS renormalization) and heavy-baryon (HB) formulations of three-flavor baryon chiral perturbation theory, including the spin-3/2 decuplet resonances. They then apply the Feynman-Hellmann theorem to extract the pion-nucleon sigma-term σ_πN and its flavor-singlet component σ_0 from the quark-mass dependence of the nucleon mass, using continuum data only and constraining low-energy constants via matching between SU(3) and SU(2) chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- σ_πN = (59.1 ± 3.5) MeV from the Roy-Steiner analysis of pion-nucleon scattering (Hoferichter et al., 2015), used as input.
- σ_0 = (36 ± 7) MeV from the pioneering calculation of Borasoy and Meißner (1996), compared to the new results.
- c_1 = -1.11(3) GeV^{-1}, c_2 = 3.13(3) GeV^{-1}, c_3 = -5.61(6) GeV^{-1}, c_4 = 4.26(4) GeV^{-1} from matching Roy-Steiner analysis to chiral perturbation theory amplitudes (Hoferichter et al., 2015).
- The average octet-decuplet mass splitting is approximately 231 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering calculations of Gasser (1980) and Borasoy and Meißner (1996), which gave σ_0 values of (35 ± 5) MeV and (36 ± 7) MeV respectively, suggesting a small strangeness content. It addresses the unclear picture from third-order calculations (Alarcon et al., 2012) where central values of σ_0 ranged from 46 to 89 MeV with large discrepancies between HB and covariant approaches when the decuplet was included. The improved fourth-order calculation presented here enables a more precise determination of σ_0 from continuum data and provides a framework that can be applied to analyze lattice QCD results at varying light and strange quark masses.
