# Searching for the Nuclear Liquid-Gas Phase Transition in Au + Au Collisions at 35 MeV/nucleon

> Within the framework of Classical Molecular Dynamics, we study the collision Au + Au at an incident energy of 35 MeV/nucleon. It is found that the system shows a critical behaviour at peripheral impact parameters, revealed through the analysis of conditional moments of charge distributions, Campi Scatter Plot, and the occurrence of large fluctuations in the region of the Campi plot where this critical behaviour is expected. When applying the experimental filters of the MULTICS-MINIBALL apparatus, it is found that criticality signals can be hidden due to the inefficiency of the experimental apparatus. The signals are then recovered by identifying semi-peripheral and peripheral collisions looking to the velocity distribution of the largest fragment, then by selecting the most complete events.

# Searching for the Nuclear Liquid-Gas Phase Transition in Au + Au Collisions at 35 MeV/nucleon

## Key claim

The Au + Au collision at 35 MeV/nucleon shows evidence for critical behavior (nuclear liquid-gas phase transition) at peripheral impact parameters, but only when the two largest fragments are subtracted from the moment analysis; the criticality signals can be hidden by experimental apparatus inefficiencies but are recoverable by selecting events where the largest fragment has at least 75% of the beam velocity and the total detected charge is between 70 and 90.

## Method

Classical Molecular Dynamics (CMD) simulations were performed for Au + Au collisions at 35 MeV/nucleon across impact parameters from 1 to 13 fm. The asymptotic fragment charge distributions were analyzed using the method of conditional moments (Campi method), including the second moment m₂, the relative variance γ₂, and the normalized variance of the largest fragment charge σ_NV. The results were then filtered using the angular acceptance and energy thresholds of the MULTICS-MINIBALL apparatus to study the effects of experimental inefficiencies.

## Key numbers

- The second moment m₂ shows a peak at reduced impact parameter b̂ ≈ 0.8 when the two largest fragments are subtracted; this peak disappears when only the largest fragment is subtracted.
- The relative variance γ₂ peaks at b̂ ≈ 0.8 and at charged particle multiplicity N_c ≈ 20–25.
- The normalized variance σ_NV peaks at b̂ ≈ 0.8 and N_c ≈ 20–25; for peripheral collisions selecting only the forward-moving largest fragment, the peak value reaches 3.8 (compared to 2.4 when both sources are included).
- The Campi scatter plot shows two branches meeting in the region 0.77 ≤ b̂ ≤ 0.85; the upper branch corresponds to b̂ > 0.85, the lower branch to b̂ < 0.77.
- After filtering and event selection (largest fragment velocity ≥ 75% beam velocity, total detected charge ≥ 70), the fragment charge distribution in the critical region (Cut 2) exhibits a power law Z^(-τ) with τ ≈ 2.2.
- The selection criteria eliminate central collisions with b̂ ≤ 0.38.

## Context

This work extends the search for nuclear liquid-gas phase transition signals to lower incident energies (35 MeV/nucleon) compared to the EOS Collaboration (1 GeV/nucleon Au + C) and the ALADIN Collaboration (600 MeV/nucleon Au + Au). The study uses Classical Molecular Dynamics, which reproduces nuclear matter properties (equilibrium density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³, energy E(ρ₀) = -16 MeV/nucleon, compressibility ≈ 250 MeV). The analysis demonstrates that experimental inefficiencies can mask criticality signals, but these signals can be recovered through appropriate event selection, enabling comparison with experimental data from the MULTICS-MINIBALL Collaboration for the same reaction. The same procedure was successfully applied to experimental Au + Au data at 35 MeV/nucleon (Belkacem et al., 1996, Physical Review Letters) and to Xe + Sn collisions at 55 MeV/nucleon measured with INDRA (Benlliure et al.).
