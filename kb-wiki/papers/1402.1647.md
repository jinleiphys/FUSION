# Parton energy loss in high energy hard forward processes in proton-nucleus collisions

> We calculate the spectrum of energy loss differences due to gluon radiation in high energy hard forward processes in proton-nucleus collisions as compared to proton-proton collisions. We find that the nuclear induced energy loss scales linearly with the beam energy. We evaluate the spectrum and "typical" energy losses in a logarithmic and large $N_{c}$ approximation. The energy losses found appear large enough to be phenomenologically important.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the spectrum of additional energy loss due to gluon radiation in high energy hard forward processes in proton-nucleus collisions compared to proton-proton collisions, finding that the nuclear-induced energy loss scales linearly with beam energy. The authors note that their calculation is made in a logarithmic and large \(N_{c}\) approximation, and that like all leading logarithmic calculations it cannot be expected to be very reliable.

## Method

The authors evaluate the spectrum of energy loss differences by calculating gluon radiation in gluon \(\rightarrow\) quark-antiquark and quark \(\rightarrow\) quark-gluon jet production, treating the nucleus in a McLerran-Venugopalan model with the large \(N_{c}\) approximation. They separate contributions into purely initial state radiation, purely final state radiation, and initial-state-final-state interference terms, then form the difference between proton-nucleus and proton-proton spectra to isolate the nuclear-induced energy loss. Small-\(x\) evolution is included in the Gaussian approximation by making the saturation momenta \(Q_{s}\) and \(Q_{s}(\text{P})\) \(x\)-dependent.

## Key numbers

* For quark \(\rightarrow\) quark-gluon processes, the typical additional energy loss at RHIC is \(\bar{\omega}_{\text{RHIC}} \simeq E/15\) and at the LHC is \(\bar{\omega}_{\text{LHC}} \simeq E/25\), where \(E\) is the initiating quark's energy.
* For \(J/\psi\) production in a color singlet model, the estimated typical energy loss is \(\bar{\omega} \simeq E/6\) in both cases considered (\(Q_{s}^{2}/M^{2} < 1\) and \(Q_{s}^{2}/M^{2} \simeq 1\)).
* The spectrum of energy loss difference for gluon \(\rightarrow\) quark-antiquark is \(\omega \frac{d I^{\text{Nucleus}-\text{Proton}}}{d\omega} = \frac{2\alpha_{s}N_{c}}{\pi} \ln \frac{E Q_{s}}{2 M \omega}\) when \(M\omega/E > Q_{s}(\text{P})/2\), and \(\omega \frac{dI}{d\omega} = \frac{\alpha_{s}N_{c}}{\pi} \ln \frac{Q_{s}^{2}}{Q_{s}^{2}(\text{P})}\) when \(M\omega/E < Q_{s}(\text{P})/2\).
* For quark \(\rightarrow\) quark-gluon, the prefactor in the spectrum is \(8\alpha_{s}N_{c}/5\pi\), replacing the \(2\alpha_{s}N_{c}/\pi\) found for gluon \(\rightarrow\) quark-antiquark.

## Context

This work extends the first-principles calculation of nuclear-induced energy loss by Arleo and Peigné (2013), who treated very high energy \(J/\psi\) production, to both gluon \(\rightarrow\) quark-antiquark and quark \(\rightarrow\) quark-gluon jet production. The results reproduce the Arleo and Peigné result for the gluon-initiated case and provide new results for the quark-initiated case, which the authors note has no simple relationship to the color charges of the initial and final partons. The energy losses found are large enough to be phenomenologically important for forward jet production and quarkonium production in proton-nucleus collisions, and the authors note that their discussion is complementary to recent discussions of transverse momentum broadening effects in similar processes by Xiao, Yuan, Marquet, Qiu, Berger, and Kang.
