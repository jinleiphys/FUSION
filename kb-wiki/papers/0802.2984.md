# Nuclear Constraints on the Weak Nucleon-Nucleon Interaction

> I discuss the current status of efforts to constrain the strangeness-conserving weak hadronic interaction, which can be isolated in nuclear systems because of the associated parity violation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the isovector component of the weak nucleon-nucleon interaction, where neutral currents are expected to dominate, appears significantly smaller than the best-value prediction of the Donoghue-Desplanques-Holstein (DDH) potential, based on the tight upper bound on the circular polarization of gamma rays from 18F; however, the overall consistency among the various experimental constraints is not high, and error bars remain large.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental constraints on parity-nonconserving (PNC) observables in the nucleon-nucleon (NN) system, few-body systems, and complex nuclei, and compares them to theoretical predictions from the DDH meson-exchange potential and from chiral perturbation theory. The analysis uses the Danilov S-P partial-wave amplitudes and the DDH potential parameters (weak meson-nucleon couplings for pi, rho, and omega exchange) to interpret measurements of longitudinal analyzing powers, gamma-ray circular polarizations, gamma-ray asymmetries, neutron spin rotation, and nuclear anapole moments.

## Key numbers

- Longitudinal analyzing power for polarized protons on protons at 13.6 MeV: A_L^(p+p) = (-0.93 ± 0.21) × 10^{-7} (Bonn)
- Longitudinal analyzing power for polarized protons on protons at 45 MeV: A_L^(p+p) = (-1.57 ± 0.23) × 10^{-7} (SIN)
- Circular polarization of gamma rays from n+p radiative capture: P_gamma(n+p -> d+gamma) = (1.8 ± 1.8) × 10^{-7}
- Gamma-ray asymmetry in n+p radiative capture: A_gamma(n+p -> d+gamma) = (0.6 ± 2.1) × 10^{-7}
- Longitudinal analyzing power for polarized protons on 4He at 46 MeV: A_L^(p+4He) = (-3.3 ± 0.9) × 10^{-7} (SIN)
- Neutron spin rotation in n+alpha at thermal energies: d(phi)/dz = (8 ± 14) × 10^{-7} rad/m (NIST)
- Longitudinal analyzing power for polarized protons on deuterium at 15 MeV: A_L^(p+d) = (-0.35 ± 0.85) × 10^{-7} (LANL)
- Circular polarization of the 1081 keV gamma ray in 18F: P_gamma = (8 ± 39) × 10^{-5} (Queens/Florence groups)
- DDH best-value prediction for P_gamma in 18F: (208 ± 49) × 10^{-5}
- DDH recommended ranges for weak couplings (×10^{-7}): f_pi = 0.0 to 11.4, h_rho^0 = -30.8 to 11.4, h_rho^1 = -0.4 to 0.0, h_rho^2 = -11.0 to -7.6, h_omega^0 = -10.3 to 5.7, h_omega^1 = -1.9 to -0.8

## Context

This work reviews the status of constraints on the strangeness-conserving weak hadronic interaction, extending the DDH framework (Donoghue, Desplanques, and Holstein, 1986) and the Danilov amplitude approach. It highlights the tension between the 18F result (which suggests a small isovector coupling) and the 133Cs anapole moment measurement (Wieman group, 1997), which favors larger couplings. The paper enables future work at the Spallation Neutron Source (SNS) and the Facility for Rare Isotope Beams (FRIB) to improve precision on neutron spin rotation and anapole moment measurements, and calls for theoretical studies of the evolution of PNC matrix elements with shell-model space size.
