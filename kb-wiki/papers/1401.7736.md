# Superdeformed $Î›$ hypernuclei with antisymmetrized molecular dynamics

> The response to the addition of a $Î›$ hyperon is investigated for the deformed states such as superdeformation in $^{41}_Î›$Ca, $^{46}_Î›$Sc and $^{48}_Î›$Sc. In the present study, we use the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) model. It is pointed out that many kinds of deformed bands appear in $^{45}$Sc and $^{47}$Sc. Especially, it is found that there exists superdeformed states in $^{45}$Sc. By the addition of a $Î›$ particle to $^{40}$Ca, $^{45}$Sc and $^{47}$Sc, it is predicted, for the first time, that the superdeformed states exist in the hypernuclei $^{41}_Î›$Ca and $^{46}_Î›$Sc. The manifestation of the dependence of the $Î›$-separation energy on nuclear deformation such as spherical, normal deformation and superdeformation is shown in the energy spectra of $^{41}_Î›$Ca, $^{46}_Î›$Sc and $^{48}_Î›$Sc hypernuclei.

## Key claim

The paper predicts, for the first time, that superdeformed states exist in the hypernuclei 41Î›Ca and 46Î›Sc when a Î› hyperon is added to 40Ca and 45Sc respectively, but the Î›-separation energy is systematically smaller for superdeformed states than for spherical states, with the difference reaching about 1.4 MeV in 41Î›Ca.

## Method

The authors used the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) model combined with the generator coordinate method (GCM) to calculate the structure of 40Ca, 45Sc, 47Sc and the corresponding Î› hypernuclei 41Î›Ca, 46Î›Sc and 48Î›Sc. They employed the Gogny D1S effective nucleon-nucleon interaction and a Î›N G-matrix interaction derived from the extended soft-core (ESC08c) model, with the averaged-density approximation to handle the Fermi-momentum dependence. Energy variation was performed under constraint on the nuclear quadrupole deformation parameter Î², followed by angular momentum projection and GCM superposition of states with different deformations.

## Context

This work extends previous AMD studies of superdeformed states in 40Ca (Kimura 2005, 2007) and earlier hypernuclear AMD calculations for p-shell and sd-shell hypernuclei (Kimura 2011). It also builds on cluster model studies showing the gluelike role of the Î› particle in light hypernuclei (Motoba 1983, 1985) and on predictions that the Î›-separation energy depends on nuclear deformation (Kimura 2008, 2011). The results enable experimental searches for superdeformed hypernuclear states via the (e, e' K+) reaction at JLab using 46Ti and 48Ti targets, and are motivated by fusion-evaporation experiments at GANIL.
