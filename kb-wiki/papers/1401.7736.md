# Superdeformed $Λ$ hypernuclei with antisymmetrized molecular dynamics

> The response to the addition of a $Λ$ hyperon is investigated for the deformed states such as superdeformation in $^{41}_Λ$Ca, $^{46}_Λ$Sc and $^{48}_Λ$Sc. In the present study, we use the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) model. It is pointed out that many kinds of deformed bands appear in $^{45}$Sc and $^{47}$Sc. Especially, it is found that there exists superdeformed states in $^{45}$Sc. By the addition of a $Λ$ particle to $^{40}$Ca, $^{45}$Sc and $^{47}$Sc, it is predicted, for the first time, that the superdeformed states exist in the hypernuclei $^{41}_Λ$Ca and $^{46}_Λ$Sc. The manifestation of the dependence of the $Λ$-separation energy on nuclear deformation such as spherical, normal deformation and superdeformation is shown in the energy spectra of $^{41}_Λ$Ca, $^{46}_Λ$Sc and $^{48}_Λ$Sc hypernuclei.

## Key claim

The paper predicts, for the first time, that superdeformed states exist in the hypernuclei 41ΛCa and 46ΛSc when a Λ hyperon is added to 40Ca and 45Sc respectively, but the Λ-separation energy is systematically smaller for superdeformed states than for spherical states, with the difference reaching about 1.4 MeV in 41ΛCa.

## Method

The authors used the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) model combined with the generator coordinate method (GCM) to calculate the structure of 40Ca, 45Sc, 47Sc and the corresponding Λ hypernuclei 41ΛCa, 46ΛSc and 48ΛSc. They employed the Gogny D1S effective nucleon-nucleon interaction and a ΛN G-matrix interaction derived from the extended soft-core (ESC08c) model, with the averaged-density approximation to handle the Fermi-momentum dependence. Energy variation was performed under constraint on the nuclear quadrupole deformation parameter β, followed by angular momentum projection and GCM superposition of states with different deformations.

## Key numbers

- In 41ΛCa, the Λ-separation energy BΛ is 19.45 MeV for the spherical ground state (1/2+1), 19.15 MeV for the normal deformed state (1/2+2), and 18.01 MeV for the superdeformed state (1/2+3), giving a difference of about 1.4 MeV between spherical and superdeformed states.
- The superdeformed state in 41ΛCa has a nuclear quadrupole deformation β = 0.55 and RMS radius 3.58 fm, compared to β = 0.10 and RMS radius 3.38 fm for the spherical ground state.
- In 46ΛSc, BΛ is 19.95 MeV for the spherical 4−1 state, 19.66 MeV for the ND2 2−4 state, and 19.52 MeV for the SD 1+2 state.
- In 48ΛSc, BΛ is 20.12 MeV for the spherical 4−1 state and 19.69 MeV for the ND2 3−5 state.
- The Fermi momentum values used in the GCM calculations are kF = 1.26 fm−1 for 41ΛCa, kF = 1.29 fm−1 for 46ΛSc, and kF = 1.30 fm−1 for 48ΛSc.
- The superdeformed band in 40Ca is built on the 0+3 state at 5.21 MeV excitation energy.

## Context

This work extends previous AMD studies of superdeformed states in 40Ca (Kimura 2005, 2007) and earlier hypernuclear AMD calculations for p-shell and sd-shell hypernuclei (Kimura 2011). It also builds on cluster model studies showing the gluelike role of the Λ particle in light hypernuclei (Motoba 1983, 1985) and on predictions that the Λ-separation energy depends on nuclear deformation (Kimura 2008, 2011). The results enable experimental searches for superdeformed hypernuclear states via the (e, e' K+) reaction at JLab using 46Ti and 48Ti targets, and are motivated by fusion-evaporation experiments at GANIL.
