# Spin Structure Function of the Deuteron in the Resonance Region and the GDH Sum Rule for the Neutron

> The nuclear effects in the spin-dependent structure function $g_1$ of the deuteron are studied in the kinematics of future experiments at CEBAF, ($ν\leq 3~GeV, ~Q^2 \leq 2~GeV^2$). The magnitude of the nuclear effects is found to be significantly larger than the one occurring in deep inelastic scattering ($ν\to \infty, ~Q^2\to \infty$). We discuss the mechanism leading to large effects in the region of the nucleon resonances. A possibility to measure the neutron structure functions in the CEBAF experiments with deuterium is analysed, and conclusions about the experimental study of the $Q^2$ dependence of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn Sum Rule for the neutron are drawn.

# Spin Structure Function of the Deuteron in the Resonance Region and the GDH Sum Rule for the Neutron

## Key claim

Nuclear effects in the deuteron spin-dependent structure function $g_1$ are significantly larger in the resonance region ($\nu \leq 3$ GeV, $Q^2 \leq 2$ GeV$^2$) than in deep inelastic scattering, but the integrals of the structure functions (relevant for the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule) can still be estimated with better than 3% accuracy using the simple depolarization formula that works in the deep inelastic limit.

## Method

The authors calculate the deuteron spin-dependent structure function $g_1^D(x,Q^2)$ using a convolution formalism that incorporates Fermi motion and the D-wave depolarization effect, with the nucleon structure function parametrization of Burkert (1995) and the Bonn potential deuteron wave function. They compare the full calculation to the approximate extraction formula $g_1^D \approx (1-3/2 P_D)(g_1^p + g_1^n)/2$ and test a rigorous unfolding method that solves the integral equation via a linear algebraic matrix equation to extract the neutron structure function from simulated deuteron data.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear effects in the resonance region reach up to **~50%** in the maxima of the resonances, compared to **~7-9%** in the deep inelastic regime (of which **~6-7%** comes from the depolarization factor $(1-3/2P_D)$ and **~1-2%** from binding and Fermi motion)
- The deuteron binding energy used is **$\epsilon_D = -2.2246$ MeV**
- The rigorous unfolding method extracts the neutron structure function with errors **not larger than $10^{-4}$**, much smaller than expected experimental errors
- The effective number of nucleons $N_{eff}(Q^2)$ deviates from unity by only **+2% at $Q^2 = 0.1$ GeV$^2$** and **-0.3% at $Q^2 = 1.0$ GeV$^2$**
- The simple formula for integrals of structure functions is reliable to within **better than 3%** for $Q^2 = 0.1-2.0$ GeV$^2$

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nuclear corrections in deuteron spin structure functions from the deep inelastic regime (studied by the same authors in earlier papers and by others such as Melnitchouk and Thomas (1993)) into the resonance region relevant for planned CEBAF experiments. The paper demonstrates that while the simple approximate extraction formula fails for point-by-point extraction of the neutron structure function in the resonance region, a rigorous unfolding method previously developed for deep inelastic scattering can be adapted to work at finite $Q^2$. The results enable experimental study of the $Q^2$ dependence of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule for the neutron using deuterium targets, with the important finding that the integral quantities can be reliably extracted using the simple depolarization formula despite the large local nuclear effects.
