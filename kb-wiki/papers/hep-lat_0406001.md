# Leading Quenching Effects in the Proton Magnetic Moment

> We present the first investigation of the extrapolation of quenched nucleon magnetic moments in quenched chiral effective field theory. We utilize established techniques in finite-range regularisation and compare with standard dimensional regularisation methods. Finite-volume corrections to the relevant loop integrals are also addressed. Finally, the contributions of dynamical sea quarks to the proton moment are estimated using a recently discovered phenomenological link between quenched and physical QCD.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first investigation of extrapolating quenched nucleon magnetic moments using quenched chiral effective field theory, finding that the quenching corrections to the proton magnetic moment are small over a wide range of quark mass despite the qualitatively different chiral structure of quenched and physical QCD.

## Method

The authors apply finite-range regularisation (FRR) chiral effective field theory to extrapolate quenched lattice QCD results for the proton magnetic moment, using a dipole regulator with cutoff parameter Lambda. They incorporate the leading nonanalytic chiral corrections from pion loops and the eta-prime meson (which appears as a light degree of freedom in the quenched theory), and extend the formalism to finite-volume corrections by replacing continuum loop integrals with discrete momentum sums over the lattice volume. The effects of dynamical sea quarks are estimated by assuming the short-distance analytic parameters from quenched QCD are unchanged in full QCD when using FRR with Lambda = 0.8 GeV, then replacing the quenched chiral loops with their physical QCD counterparts.

## Key numbers

- The leading nonanalytic coefficient for the proton magnetic moment in quenched QCD is given by beta_p^pi(Q) = -(4/3)D^2, with D = 0.76 and F = 0.50 (g_A = D + F)
- The eta-prime double-hairpin coefficient is chi_eta'(Q) = m_0^2 (3F - D)^2 / (12 pi^2 f_pi^2), with m_0^2 = 0.42 GeV^2 and f_pi = 93 MeV
- The physical volume of the FLIC lattice used for finite-volume corrections is V = (2.56 fm)^3
- The variation of the extrapolated magnetic moment on a finite volume for Lambda in the range 0.6-1.0 GeV is 0.1 mu_N (nuclear magnetons)
- To accurately reproduce the quark mass dependence of the proton magnetic moment up to m_pi^2 ~ 0.1 GeV^2 requires keeping all terms up to m_pi^10 in the Taylor expansion; the lightest FLIC quark mass requires terms up to m_pi^26

## Context

This work extends the FRR-chiral effective field theory techniques previously applied to the nucleon mass (Leinweber 2003, Young 2002) to the case of nucleon magnetic moments, building on earlier studies of hadron properties in quenched and dynamical QCD (Savage 2001, Leinweber 2002). The authors use a phenomenological link between quenched and dynamical baryon masses (Young 2002) to estimate unquenching corrections, finding that quenched simulations provide a good approximation to the physical proton magnetic moment. This enables more reliable extraction of physical magnetic moments from existing quenched lattice QCD simulations and provides a framework for future dynamical simulations.
