# Extending the Eikonal Approximation to Low Energy

> E-CDCC and DEA, two eikonal-based reaction models are compared to CDCC at low energy (e.g. 20AMeV) to study their behaviour in the regime at which the eikonal approximation is supposed to fail. We confirm that these models lack the Coulomb deflection of the projectile by the target. We show that a hybrid model, built on the CDCC framework at low angular momenta and the eikonal approximation at larger angular momenta gives a perfect agreement with CDCC. An empirical shift in impact parameter can also be used reliably to simulate this missing Coulomb deflection.

## Key claim

The eikonal-based models E-CDCC and DEA fail at low energy (e.g., 20A MeV) because they lack the Coulomb deflection of the projectile by the target, but a hybrid model combining CDCC at low angular momenta with the eikonal approximation at larger angular momenta, or an empirical shift in impact parameter, can restore agreement with full CDCC.

## Method

The authors compared the eikonal-based models E-CDCC and DEA to the full CDCC method for the Coulomb breakup of the one-neutron halo nucleus 15C on a Pb target at 20A MeV. They analyzed the contribution of each angular momentum L of the projectile-target relative motion to the total breakup cross section, and tested two corrections: an empirical shift of the impact parameter b to the classical distance of closest approach, and a hybrid approach that uses full CDCC for low L (below L_c = 500 ℏ) and E-CDCC for higher L.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 20A MeV for the low-energy comparison, with 68A MeV used as the intermediate-energy reference where eikonal models already agree with CDCC.
- The hybrid model uses a cutoff at L_c = 500 ℏ, below which full CDCC is performed and above which E-CDCC is used.
- The empirical shift formula replaces impact parameter b with b' = η_0/K_0 + sqrt(η_0^2/K_0^2 + b^2), where η_0 is the Sommerfeld parameter and K_0 the initial wave number.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of CDCC and DEA for 15C breakup on Pb reported in Capel et al. (2012), which showed that at 20A MeV the eikonal approximation fails due to missing Coulomb deflection. The hybrid E-CDCC approach was previously introduced by Ogata et al. (2003, 2006), and the empirical impact-parameter shift follows the classical Coulomb trajectory idea from Bertulani and Danielewicz (2004). The results enable more computationally efficient analysis of breakup reactions at low-energy facilities such as HIE-Isolde, and open the question of how low in energy such corrections remain reliable.
