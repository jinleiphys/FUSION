# Shell-model results in $fp$ and ${fpg_{9/2}}$ spaces for $^{61,63,65}$Co isotopes

> Low-lying spectra and several high-spin states of odd--even $^{61,63,65}$Co isotopes are calculated in two different shell-model spaces. First set of calculations have been carried out in ${fp}$ shell valence space (full $fp$ space for $^{63,65}$Co and a truncated one for $^{61}$Co) using two recently derived ${fp}$ shell interactions, namely GXPF1A and KB3G, with $^{40}$Ca as core. Similarly, the second set of calculations have been performed in ${fpg_{9/2}}$ valence space using an ${fpg}$ effective interaction due to Sorlin {\it et al}., with $^{48}$Ca as core and imposing a truncation. It is seen that the results of GXPF1A and KB3G are reasonable for $^{61,63}$Co. For $^{65}$Co, shell-model results show that the ${fpg}$ interaction adopted in the study is inadequate and also point out that it is necessary to include orbitals higher than $1g_{9/2}$ for neutron-rich Co isotopes.

# Shell-model results in $fp$ and ${fpg_{9/2}}$ spaces for $^{61,63,65}$Co isotopes

## Key claim

The GXPF1A and KB3G interactions in the $fp$ shell space give reasonable agreement with experimental data for $^{61,63}$Co, but for $^{65}$Co the $fpg$ interaction in the ${fpg_{9/2}}$ space is inadequate, and it is necessary to include orbitals higher than $1g_{9/2}$ (specifically the $2d_{5/2}$ orbital) for neutron-rich Co isotopes approaching $N \sim 40$.

## Method

Two sets of large-scale shell-model calculations were performed for odd-even $^{61,63,65}$Co isotopes. The first set used the full $fp$ valence space ($1f_{7/2}$, $2p_{3/2}$, $1f_{5/2}$, $2p_{1/2}$ orbitals) with $^{40}$Ca as the inert core, employing the GXPF1A and KB3G effective interactions; for $^{61}$Co a truncation allowing maximum four particles to be excited from $f_{7/2}$ to the remaining $fp$ orbitals was used. The second set used the ${fpg_{9/2}}$ valence space with $^{48}$Ca as the inert core, comprising $fp$ proton orbitals and $2p_{3/2}$, $1f_{5/2}$, $2p_{1/2}$, $1g_{9/2}$ neutron orbitals (with eight $1f_{7/2}$ frozen neutrons), employing the $fpg$ interaction due to Sorlin et al. with a truncation allowing up to four particle excitations from $1f_{7/2}$ to upper $fp$ orbitals for protons and from upper $fp$ orbitals to $1g_{9/2}$ for neutrons. All calculations were carried out using the code ANTOINE.

## Key numbers

- For $^{61}$Co: yrast $9/2^-$ state predicted at 1405 keV (GXPF1A), 1426 keV (KB3G), and 1469 keV ($fpg$ interaction), compared to experimental value 1285 keV.
- For $^{61}$Co: first $3/2^-$ level calculated at 804 keV (GXPF1A), 1242 keV (KB3G), and 745 keV ($fpg$ interaction); first $1/2^-$ level at 1295 keV (GXPF1A), 1670 keV (KB3G), and 1173 keV ($fpg$ interaction); experimental candidates at 1028 keV and 1326 keV respectively.
- For $^{63}$Co: yrast $9/2^-$ state predicted at 1154 keV (GXPF1A), 1301 keV (KB3G), and 1607 keV ($fpg$ interaction), compared to experimental value 1383 keV.
- For $^{63}$Co: first $11/2^-$ state predicted at 1535 keV (GXPF1A), 1747 keV (KB3G), and 1418 keV ($fpg$ interaction), compared to experimental value 1673 keV.
- For $^{65}$Co: first $9/2^-$ level predicted at 1753 keV (GXPF1A), 1943 keV (KB3G), and 2042 keV ($fpg$ interaction), compared to experimental value 1479 keV.
- For $^{65}$Co: first $3/2^-$ state predicted too low by GXPF1A and KB3G, and at about 250 keV higher by $fpg$ interaction (experimental value 883 keV).
- For $^{65}$Co with $fpg$ interaction: B(E2) values for $9/2_1^- \rightarrow 7/2_1^-$, $11/2_1^- \rightarrow 9/2_1^-$, $11/2_2^- \rightarrow 9/2_1^-$ transitions are 105.12, 0.28, and 1.29 $e^2$fm$^4$ respectively.
- For $^{67}$Co: $fpg$ interaction gave first $1/2^-$, $3/2^-$, and $5/2^-$ at 2118 keV, 1157 keV, and 1728 keV, while experimental values are 492 keV, 680 keV, and 1252 keV.
- Computing time for $^{65}$Co: approximately 8 days for $fp$ space and 3 days for $fpg_{9/2}$ space on a 20-node cluster.

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies by the same authors on neutron-rich Fe isotopes (Srivastava 2009), odd-odd Mn isotopes (Srivastava 2009), and even-even Ni and Zn and odd-A Cu isotopes, and addresses the limited experimental information on neutron-rich Co isotopes beyond $N=40$. The calculations test the suitability of the $fp$ space interactions GXPF1A (Honma 2005) and KB3G (Poves 2001), and the $fpg$ interaction (Sorlin 2002), against experimental data from Regan (1996) for $^{61,63}$Co and Pauwels (2009) for $^{65}$Co. The results show that for $^{65}$Co and $^{67}$Co, the $fpg_{9/2}$ space is insufficient and that a good effective interaction in the $fpg_{9/2}d_{5/2}$ space (with $^{48}$Ca core) is needed, following the recent development of a hybrid interaction in that space for Cr and Fe isotopes (Nowacki, preprint).
