# Description of the plasma delay effect in silicon detectors

> A new method of modeling of the current signal induced by charged particle in silicon detectors is presented. The approach is based on the Ramo-Shockley theorem for which the charge carrier velocities are determined by taking into account not only the external electric field generated by the electrodes, but also the Coulomb interaction between the electron and hole clouds as well as their diffusion.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new microscopic model for the plasma delay effect in silicon detectors that, unlike previous phenomenological approaches, explicitly includes the Coulomb interaction between electron and hole clouds and their diffusion, but the author notes that the model still needs to be confronted with a broader collection of experimental data and that the single free parameter \(\mu_{\sigma e}\) has not yet been determined from first principles.

## Method

The model represents the charge carrier distributions as Gaussian clouds whose centroids evolve by drift in the electric field and whose variances evolve by diffusion, drift, and transport. The electric field is calculated from the Ramo-Shockley theorem and includes both the static field from the detector bias and the field from mutual Coulomb interactions between all electron and hole clouds, computed using an error-function potential. The time evolution of the linear charge densities and the Gaussian widths is solved numerically by dividing the detector thickness into intervals and tracking the coefficients of linear expansions of the densities and velocities in each interval.

## Key numbers

* Energy for electron-hole pair creation in silicon: \(w = 3.6\) eV/pair
* Silicon relative permittivity: \(\varepsilon_r = 11.7\)
* Electron mobility: \(\mu_{xe} = 135\ \mu\text{m}^2/(\text{V}\cdot\text{ns})\)
* Hole mobility: \(\mu_{xh} = 47.5\ \mu\text{m}^2/(\text{V}\cdot\text{ns})\)
* Free parameter for electron variance mobility: \(\mu_{\sigma e} = 2\ \mu\text{m}^2/(\text{V}\cdot\text{ns})\)
* Diffusion coefficient for electrons: \(D_e = 3.49\ \mu\text{m}^2/\text{ns}\)
* Diffusion coefficient for holes: \(D_h = 1.228\ \mu\text{m}^2/\text{ns}\)
* Detector thickness used in the comparison: \(d_{Si} = 310\ \mu\text{m}\)
* Computation time for one pulse of 80 MeV \(^{12}\text{C}\): about 5 hours of CPU on a standard processor

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological model of Parlog (2010) which described the plasma delay effect using polarization of electron-hole pairs and a constant dissociation probability, and contrasts with the approach of Hamrita (2004) which neglected electric field distortion and could only describe light charged particles. The model enables future theoretical searches for optimal pulse shape discrimination methods and studies of identification quality as a function of detector thickness, doping heterogeneity, and temperature.
