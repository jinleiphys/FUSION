# Cold ideal equation of state for strongly magnetized neutron-star matter: effects on muon production and pion condensationn

> Neutron stars with very strong surface magnetic fields have been suggested as the site for the origin of observed soft gamma repeaters (SGRs). In this paper we investigate the influence of such strong magnetic fields on the properties and internal structure of these magnetized neutron stars (magnetars). We study properties of a degenerate equilibrium ideal neutron-proton-electron (npe) gas with and without the effects of the anomalous nucleon magnetic moments in a magnetic field. The presence of a sufficiently strong magnetic field changes the ratio of protons to neutrons as well as the neutron drip density. We also study the appearance of muons as well as pion condensation in strong magnetic fields. We discuss the possibility that boson condensation in the interior of magnetars might be a source of SGRs.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in a strongly magnetized neutron-star interior, the presence of a sufficiently strong magnetic field (above about 1.2 × 10^14 G) changes the proton-to-neutron ratio and the neutron drip density, and can catalyze inverse beta decay rather than suppress it, but only if charged particles remain in the lowest Landau level; at higher densities where higher Landau levels fill, the behavior reverts to the nonmagnetic limit.

## Method

The authors model a degenerate, ideal, noninteracting neutron-proton-electron (npe) gas in beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality, with a uniform magnetic field along the z-axis. They solve the Dirac equation for electrons, protons, and neutrons in the field, including Landau level quantization, and compute thermodynamic quantities (number density, pressure, energy density) both with and without the anomalous magnetic moments (AMM) of the nucleons. They then determine the threshold densities for neutron appearance, muon production, and pion condensation by imposing chemical equilibrium conditions.

## Key numbers

- The critical magnetic field for electrons is B_c^e = 4.414 × 10^13 G; for B > 2.7 B_c^e ≈ 1.2 × 10^14 G, electrons reside in the lowest Landau level.
- The neutron appearance density in a strong magnetic field (B > 2.7 B_c^e) scales as ρ_n^*(B) / ρ_n^*(0) = 0.277 γ_e, where γ_e = B / B_c^e.
- The muon threshold condition is ε_e^* = m_μ / m_e, where m_μ ≈ 105.66 MeV.
- The adiabatic index Γ shows oscillatory behavior (de Haas-van Alphen effect) for magnetic field strengths above about 10^15 G (log γ_e ≳ 2) at densities above ~10^12 g cm^-3.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of magnetic white dwarfs (Suh and Mathews, 1999) to neutron-star matter, and builds on earlier treatments of magnetized equations of state by Lai and Shapiro (1991) for field strengths below log γ_e = 2. The paper also references the magnetar model of Thompson and Duncan (1993) and the direct URCA cooling calculations of Baiko and Yakovlev (1999). The results enable future magnetohydrodynamical studies of pulsational instabilities in strongly magnetized neutron stars and provide a framework for assessing whether boson condensation in magnetar interiors could explain soft gamma repeaters.
