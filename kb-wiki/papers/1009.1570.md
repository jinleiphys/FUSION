# Additional Corrections to the Gross-Llewellyn Smith Sum Rule

> We investigate some QCD corrections that contribute to the Gross-Llewellyn Smith (GLS) sum rule, but have not been included in previous analyses of it. We first review the techniques by which the xF3 structure function is extracted from combinations of neutrino and antineutrino cross sections. Next we investigate corrections to the GLS sum rule, with particular attention to contributions arising from strange quark contributions and from charge symmetry violating (CSV) parton distributions. We find that additional corrections from strange quarks and parton CSV are likely to have a small but potentially significant role in decreasing the current discrepancy between the experimental and theoretical estimates of the Gross-Llewellyn Smith sum rule.

## Key claim

The paper argues that previously neglected QCD corrections from strange quark asymmetries and charge symmetry violating (CSV) parton distributions are likely to produce small but potentially significant reductions in the discrepancy between experimental and theoretical estimates of the Gross-Llewellyn Smith (GLS) sum rule, though the authors cannot give a definitive quantitative estimate due to how isoscalar corrections have been implemented in prior analyses.

## Method

The authors first review how the xF3 structure function is extracted from sums and differences of neutrino and antineutrino charged-current deep inelastic scattering cross sections on nuclear targets, particularly iron. They then derive expressions showing that the standard extraction procedure actually yields a linear combination of the desired xF3 structure function and the previously neglected Delta F2 structure function, with a relative weighting factor B(x) that depends on the kinematic averaging. Finally, they estimate the contributions from strange quark asymmetries and parton CSV to the GLS sum rule at Q^2 = 8 GeV^2 using existing phenomenological parton distributions.

## Key numbers

- The CCFR collaboration reported S_GLS = 2.50 ± 0.018 (stat) ± 0.078 (syst) at Q^2 = 3 GeV^2, a precision of roughly 3%
- At Q^2 = 8 GeV^2, the experimental GLS sum rule is S_GLS^exp = 2.76 ± 0.14, while the theoretical value from Hinchliffe and Kwiatkowski (1996) including higher-twist corrections is S_GLS^th = 2.62
- The QCD correction factor f(alpha_s) at Q^2 = 8 GeV^2 is 0.885, using Lambda_MSbar^(5) = 233 MeV corresponding to alpha_s(Q^2 = 3 GeV^2) = 0.336
- The NuTeV collaboration analysis by Mason et al. (2007) obtained the strange quark momentum asymmetry integral S^- = 0.00196 ± 0.00046 (stat) ± 0.00045 (syst) +0.00148/-0.00107 (external) at Q^2 = 16 GeV^2
- The lowest x value contributing to the GLS sum rule in the CCFR experiment is x_min = 0.015

## Context

This work extends the theoretical analysis of the GLS sum rule by Hinchliffe and Kwiatkowski (1996), which included higher-order QCD corrections from Larin and Vermaseren (1993) and higher-twist corrections from Braun and Kolesnichenko, but found theoretical predictions lying one to two standard deviations below the CCFR experimental data. The paper identifies that strange quark asymmetries, now measurable from dimuon production experiments by the CCFR and NuTeV collaborations, and parton CSV effects studied by Londergan and Thomas (2009) and others, have not been included in previous GLS sum rule analyses. These corrections should be incorporated in future experimental analyses to improve agreement between theory and experiment.
