# CP violation and strong pion-pion interactions in the weak B charged decays into three charged pions

> An analysis of charged B decay data in a quasi two-body decay into a charged pion plus a S, P,or D state of two charged pions is performed in the QCD factorization framework. The short distance amplitudes are calculated including next-to-leading order corrections in the strong coupling constant for vertex and penguin contributions. The long distance amplitudes due to the two charged pion final state interactions are described by the pion non-strange scalar and vector form factors for the two-pion S- and P-wave states, respectively and by a relativistic Breit-Wigner formula for the D-wave state. We achieve a good fit of the data with only three parameters for the S wave, one for the P wave and one for the D wave. Our model gives a unified unitary description of the three scalar resonances, f_0(600), f_0(980) and f_0(1400) in terms of the pion scalar form factor. We predict for the B to f_2(1270) transition form factor a value of 0.098+-0.007 at a momentum transfer square equal to the pion-mass square.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a quasi two-body QCD factorization approach, using a unified unitary description of scalar resonances via the pion scalar form factor, can achieve a good fit to BABAR data for charged B decays into three charged pions with only five free parameters, but the S-wave amplitude's functional form is constrained by the data and could be used in Dalitz-plot analyses.

## Method

The authors analyze BABAR data for B± → π± π∓ π± decays by treating them as quasi two-body decays B± → π± [π+ π-]S,P,D within the QCD factorization framework, including next-to-leading order vertex and penguin corrections. The long-distance amplitudes for the S-wave and P-wave two-pion states are described by the pion non-strange scalar and vector form factors respectively, while the D-wave uses a relativistic Breit-Wigner formula for the f2(1270) resonance. A χ² fit to 170 data points from BABAR invariant mass distributions plus the experimental branching ratio for B± → ρ(770)0 π± is performed with three parameters for the S wave, one for the P wave, and one for the D wave.

## Key numbers

- χ²/d.o.f. = 231.6/(171-4) = 1.39 for the fit to 170 BABAR data points plus the branching ratio
- Fitted S-wave amplitude strength χS = -19.4 ± 2.5 GeV⁻¹
- Fitted P-wave amplitude strength deviation NP = 1.122 ± 0.034
- Fitted scalar form factor parameter c = (19.5 ± 4.2) GeV⁻⁴ controlling high energy behavior
- Predicted B to f2(1270) transition form factor F^{Bf2}(mπ²) = 0.098 ± 0.007
- Predicted CP asymmetry A_CP[B± → ρ(770)0 π±, ρ(770)0 → π+ π-] = 3.6% ± 0.2%
- Experimental branching ratio B_P for B± → ρ(770)0 π±, ρ(770)0 → π+ π- = (8.1 ± 0.5 ± 1.2 +0.4 -1.1) × 10⁻⁶

## Context

This work extends the quasi two-body QCD factorization approach previously applied by some of the authors to B → K π+ π- decays (Furman et al., 2006; Kaminski et al., 2006) and B → π± [K π∓]S,P decays (Furman et al., 2009; Leitner et al., 2010), now including the D-wave and the f2(1270) resonance. The analysis enables constraints between strong and weak interaction physics, and the determination of strong interaction phases should help in extracting the weak angle γ (or φ3). The non-strange pion scalar form factor derived here could be checked against the strange component needed for B → K π+ π- decays.
