# Neutron unpolarized structure function F_2^n(x) from deep inelastic scattering off ^{3}He and ^{3}H

> The possibility to safely extract the neutron deep inelastic structure function $F_2^n(x)$ in the range $0 \le x \le 0.9$ from joint measurements of deep inelastic structure functions of $^{3}He$ and $^{3}H$ is demonstrated. While the nuclear structure effects are relevant, the model dependence in this extraction linked to the $N-N$ interaction is shown to be weak.

## Key claim

The neutron unpolarized structure function \(F_2^n(x)\) can be reliably extracted from joint measurements of deep inelastic scattering off \(^{3}\text{He}\) and \(^{3}\text{H}\) in the range \(0 \le x \le 0.9\), provided nuclear binding effects are correctly accounted for; the model dependence linked to the choice of nucleon-nucleon (\(N-N\)) interaction is weak, but nuclear structure effects are relevant and cannot be overlooked at high \(x\).

## Method

The authors propose a self-consistent iterative procedure to extract the ratio \(r(x) = F_2^n(x)/F_2^p(x)\) from the experimental ratio \(E(x) = F_2^H(x)/F_2^T(x)\) of the unpolarized deep inelastic structure functions of \(^{3}\text{He}\) and \(^{3}\text{H}\). The extraction uses a super-ratio \(R(x)\) defined in terms of the nuclear spectral functions, which account for nucleon motion and binding in the three-nucleon systems, including the Coulomb interaction in the \(^{3}\text{He}\) wave function. Starting from a reasonable zero-order approximation for \(F_2^n(x)\), the recurrence relation \(r^{(n+1)}(x) = [E(x) - 2R(r^{(n)}(x))] / [R(r^{(n)}(x)) - 2E(x)]\) is iterated until convergence.

## Key numbers

- The extraction procedure is demonstrated for Bjorken \(x\) in the range \(0 \le x \le 0.9\).
- Using the \(RSC\) interaction plus Coulomb interaction, the iterative sequence converges to an accuracy better than \(0.1\%\) with only ten iterations, up to \(x = 0.9\).
- When the nucleon momentum distribution is used instead of the full spectral function for evaluating \(R(x)\), the extracted \(r(x)\) differs from the correct one by more than \(10\%\) for \(x \ge 0.8\).
- For different \(N-N\) interactions (e.g., \(RSC\) and \(Av14\), with and without Coulomb interaction), the extracted ratio \(r(x)\) after twenty iterations differs from the input value by less than \(2.5\%\) in the range \(0 \le x \le 0.85\).

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to extract the neutron structure function from deuteron data, which left uncertainties, particularly regarding the limiting value of \(r(x) = F_2^n(x)/F_2^p(x)\) as \(x \rightarrow 1\) (e.g., the standard treatment by Bodek (1979) giving \(1/4\), versus pQCD arguments by Farrar and Jackson (1975) suggesting \(3/7\), or SU(6) symmetry giving \(2/3\)). The proposed method improves upon the use of a single \(^{3}\text{He}\) target by employing both \(^{3}\text{He}\) and \(^{3}\text{H}\) to reduce systematic errors and theoretical model dependence, as previously discussed by J. Carlson and R. Schiavilla (1998) and by M. B. Miller and collaborators (1999). The approach enables reliable extraction of \(F_2^n(x)\) at the upgraded Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility (TJNAF), provided nucleon binding in nuclei is correctly taken into account.
