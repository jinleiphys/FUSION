# Neutrinoless double-beta decay from an effective field theory for heavy nuclei

> We study neutrinoless double-beta decay in an effective field theory (EFT) for heavy nuclei, which are treated as a spherical core coupled to additional neutrons and/or protons. Since the low-energy constants of the EFT cannot be fitted to data for this unobserved decay, we follow an alternative strategy to constrain these through a correlation with double Gamow-Teller transitions. This correlation was recently found to hold for shell-model calculations, energy-density functionals, and other nuclear structure models. We therefore first calculate the nuclear matrix elements for double Gamow-Teller transitions in the EFT for heavy nuclei. The combination of the EFT uncertainty with the correlation uncertainty enables predictions of nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay for a broad range of isotopes with quantified uncertainties. Generally the EFT predicts smaller nuclear matrix elements compared to other approaches, but our EFT results are consistent with recent ab initio calculations.

## Key claim

The paper predicts neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) using an effective field theory (EFT) for heavy nuclei, but because the decay is unobserved and the EFT's low-energy constants cannot be fitted to data, the authors instead constrain these constants through a correlation with double Gamow-Teller (DGT) transitions. The EFT generally predicts smaller NMEs compared to other nuclear structure approaches, though the results are consistent with recent ab initio calculations.

## Method

The authors treat heavy nuclei as a spherical core coupled to additional neutrons and/or protons, constructing effective Gamow-Teller (GT) and double Gamow-Teller (DGT) operators as spherical tensors from bosonic (quadrupole phonon) and fermionic creation/annihilation operators. Low-energy constants (LECs) for the GT operator are fitted to experimental beta-decay log(ft) values or charge-exchange reaction GT strengths for the intermediate odd-odd nucleus. The DGT NMEs are then calculated at leading order, and the previously established linear correlation between DGT and 0νββ NMEs is used to obtain 0νββ NMEs with quantified uncertainties from the EFT truncation and the correlation.

## Key numbers

* For the DGT NME uncertainty from neglected higher-order terms, the relative uncertainty is estimated as Δ⟨DGT⟩ ∼ Σ_{n=1} (ω/Λ)^n = 1/2 (50%).
* The ratio of next-to-leading-order to leading-order LECs for the GT operator is C_{βℓ}/C_β ∼ 0.58 ± 0.38, and for next-to-next-to-leading-order C_{βLℓ}/C_β ∼ 0.33 ± 0.22, assuming a breakdown scale Λ = 3ω.
* The beta-decay constant used is κ = 6147 s, and the axial-vector coupling is g_A = 1.27.
* For the DGT NME calculation, excited states in adjacent odd-mass nuclei are considered only if their lifetime t_{1/2} ≥ 0.1 ns and their energy is below 700 keV.

## Context

This work extends the same EFT previously applied to beta and two-neutrino double-beta decays (Coe18Gamow-Teller) to the unobserved neutrinoless double-beta decay, using the DGT-0νββ correlation found by Shimizu (2017) to hold across shell-model, energy-density functional, and interacting boson model calculations. The EFT predictions are compared to results from the nuclear shell model (Menendez 2009, 2017), energy density functionals (Rodriguez 2012), the interacting boson model (Barea 2015), the quasiparticle random-phase approximation, and ab initio calculations (Yao 2019, Belley 2020, Novario 2020). The approach enables systematic uncertainty quantification for 0νββ NMEs across a broad range of isotopes, which is currently lacking in most other nuclear structure models.
