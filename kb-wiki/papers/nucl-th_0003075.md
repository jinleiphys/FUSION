# Limitations of the number selfconsistent Random Phase Approximation

> The Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation equations are solved taking into account the Pauli Principle at the expectation value level, and allowing changes in the mean field occupation numbers to minimize the energy while having the correct number of particles in the correlated vacuum. The study of Fermi pn excitations in $^{76}$Ge using a realistic Hilbert space shows that the pairing energy gaps in the modified mean field are diminished up to one half of the experimental value when strong proton-neutron correlations are present. Additionally, the Ikeda sum rule for Fermi transitions is violated due to the lack of scattering terms in the phonon operators. These results call for a critical revision of the double beta decay half-lives estimated using the QRPA extensions when standard QRPA calculations collapse.

# Limitations of the number selfconsistent Random Phase Approximation

## Key claim

The number selfconsistent QRPA (SRQRPA), which solves the gap and number equations using the correlated RPA vacuum to enforce the correct particle number, still violates the Ikeda sum rule for Fermi transitions and produces pairing energy gaps reduced to as little as half the experimental value when strong proton-neutron correlations are present in a realistic Hilbert space, calling into question double beta decay half-lives estimated with QRPA extensions in regions where standard QRPA collapses.

## Method

The authors study Fermi beta excitations in ⁷⁶Ge using a realistic Hilbert space of six single-particle levels from the 3ℏω oscillator shell plus 1g₉/₂ and 1g₇/₂ from the 4ℏω shell, with a δ-type residual interaction. They solve the renormalized gap equation and number equations self-consistently with the renormalized QRPA (RQRPA) equations, where the mean field occupations vᵢ are adjusted so that the correlated RPA vacuum has the correct proton and neutron numbers, while the proton-neutron interaction enters through the RPA equations but not explicitly in the gap minimization. Results are compared across three formalisms: standard QRPA, RQRPA (no mean field modification), and SRQRPA (with mean field modification).

## Key numbers

- The QRPA collapses around s = 2.5 (ratio of proton-neutron J=0 particle-particle coupling constant to pairing force constant), while RQRPA and SRQRPA avoid the collapse.
- The pairing energy gaps for both protons and neutrons are reduced down to one half of their original (experimental) value as the proton-neutron residual interaction parameter s increases.
- The Ikeda sum rule S₋ − S₊, normalized to N−Z, is violated in both RQRPA and SRQRPA; the violation persists even at s = 0 due to other proton-neutron residual interaction channels (J ≠ 0) having finite values.
- The Jᵖⁱ = 1⁺ component collapses around s = 2.3, causing expectation values to diverge when all angular momentum contributions are included.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of particle number fluctuations and pair numbers in the RQRPA ground state of ⁷⁶Ge (Mariano 1998a, 1998b), which showed that the isoscalar-isovector phase transition causing QRPA collapse is missed in RQRPA. It directly addresses the Ikeda sum rule violation found in RQRPA (Hirsch 1996) and attempts to restore it through self-consistent treatment, as was achieved in schematic models (Krmpotić 1996a, Delion 1997). The results contradict the expectation that number self-consistency alone recovers the sum rule in realistic spaces, and they cast doubt on double beta decay half-life estimates from QRPA extensions (Toivanen 1995, Schwieger 1996, Muto 1997) in nuclei where standard QRPA collapses.
