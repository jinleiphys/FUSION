# Correlation energy and quantum correlations in a solvable model

> Typically in many-body systems the correlation energy, which is defined as the difference between the exact ground state energy and the mean-field solution, has been a measure of the system's total correlations. However, under the quantum information context, it is possible to define some quantities in terms of the system's constituents that measure the classical and quantum correlations, such as the entanglement entropy, mutual information, quantum discord, one-body entropy, etc. In this work, we apply concepts of quantum information in fermionic systems in order to study traditional correlation measures (the relative correlation energy) from a novel approach. Concretely, we analyze the two and three level Lipkin models, which are exactly solvable (but non trivial) models very used in the context of the many-body problem.

## Key claim

The relative correlation energy is not a good estimator of the total correlation in a system but rather a good estimator of the accuracy of the mean field approximation; regions exist where the overall entropy grows but the relative correlation energy decreases, and these regions are defined by quantum phase transitions that can be analyzed through quantum discord between orbitals at the Hartree-Fock level without computing the exact ground state.

## Method

The authors computed the exact ground state and the Hartree-Fock (mean field) solution for the two-level and three-level Lipkin models, which are exactly solvable many-body fermionic systems with N particles. They compared the relative correlation energy (defined as the difference between exact and Hartree-Fock ground state energies divided by the exact energy) with quantum information measures including the overall entropy (sum of von Neumann entropies of single-orbital reduced density matrices in the natural orbital basis) and the two-orbital quantum discord between orbitals with the same degeneracy quantum number p and opposite level indices.

## Key numbers

- For the two-level Lipkin model, the Hartree-Fock solution breaks parity symmetry when the dimensionless interaction strength chi = (N-1)V/epsilon exceeds 1; the quantum phase transition occurs at chi = 1.
- For the three-level Lipkin model, two phase transitions occur: the first at chi = 1 (parity-like breaking of the sigma = 1 level) and the second at chi = 3 (parity-like breaking of the sigma = 2 level).
- In the two-level Lipkin model, the quantum discord between up and down levels of the same quantum number p for the Hartree-Fock ground state is zero for chi less than or equal to 1 and equals h(chi) = -(1/2)(1-1/chi)ln[(1/2)(1-1/chi)] - (1/2)(1+1/chi)ln[(1/2)(1+1/chi)] for chi greater than 1.
- The second derivative of the relative correlation energy as a function of the overall entropy shows discontinuities at chi = 1 for the two-level model and at both chi = 1 and chi = 3 for the three-level model.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of quantum information measures in nuclear many-body systems, following prior studies of entanglement in the Lipkin model (Robledo, 2014) and the use of one-body entropy in nuclear physics (Yoshiko, 2015). The authors connect the relative correlation energy, traditionally used in nuclear physics and quantum chemistry (Benavides-Riveros, 2013), with quantum discord (Ollivier and Zurek, 2001) and overall entropy (Szalay, 2015) to show that quantum phase transitions in the mean field description reflect structural changes in the exact wave function. This enables the use of quantum discord computed from mean field states as a probe of quantum phase transitions without requiring the exact solution, and suggests future application to more complex models such as the N-level Lipkin model, picket fence model, or single-j shell models.
