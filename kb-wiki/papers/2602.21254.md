# Lorentz-boosted diffusion: initial value formulation and exact solutions

> It is well known that the diffusion equation, when treated as a stand-alone partial differential equation, exhibits exponential instabilities in boosted frames, which render the corresponding initial-value problem ill-posed. Recently, however, it was shown that Fick-type diffusion arises as the exact hydrodynamic sector of relativistic Fokker-Planck kinetic theory. In this work, we exploit this kinetic embedding to formulate a modified initial-value problem for one-dimensional Lorentz-boosted diffusion. We show that the resulting dynamics are well posed both forward and backward in time, provided the boosted density profiles admit a kinetic-theory realization. Such profiles form a space of band-limited functions, within which the evolution can be expressed as a discrete superposition of spatially sampled initial data, weighted by a Shannon-Whittaker-type Green function defined on the full Minkowski plane. The Green function is obtained in closed analytic form.

## Key claim

The boosted diffusion equation, which is normally ill-posed for generic initial data, becomes well-posed both forward and backward in time when the initial density profiles are restricted to band-limited functions that admit a kinetic-theory realization within relativistic Fokker-Planck theory, provided the wavenumber cutoff is enforced by the convergence condition \(|\mathfrak{Im}\, k| < 1\).

## Method

The author embeds the one-dimensional diffusion equation into the hydrodynamic sector of relativistic Fokker-Planck kinetic theory, where the dispersion relation \(\omega = -i k^2\) holds exactly. By applying a Lorentz boost to the kinetic theory and imposing the convergence condition for the momentum integral of the distribution function, a wavenumber cutoff \(\Lambda\) is derived for admissible Fourier modes in the boosted frame. The initial-value problem for the boosted diffusion equation is then restricted to the Paley-Wiener space \(PW_{\Lambda}\) of band-limited functions, and the time evolution is governed by the stable branch of the boosted dispersion relation, yielding a discrete Shannon-Whittaker-type Green function expansion.

## Key numbers

* The wavenumber cutoff is \(\Lambda = (1+2v)/\sqrt{v(1-v)}\), which diverges as \(v \to 0\) and as \(v \to 1\), with a minimum value \(\Lambda = 2\sqrt{3} \approx 3.464\) at \(v = 1/4\).
* The bound on the imaginary part of the boosted frequency is \(|\mathfrak{Im}\,\Tom| < 1/(\gamma v)\), which excludes exponentially growing solutions such as \(e^{\Tt/(\gamma v^2)}\).
* The \(L^2\) norm of the density perturbation satisfies \(\|\delta n(\Tt)\|_{L^2} \le e^{|\Tt|/(\gamma v)} \|\delta n(0)\|_{L^2}\).
* The spatial localization obeys \(\Delta \Tx > (4\Lambda)^{-1}\) due to the band-limited nature of admissible functions.

## Context

This work extends the recent realization by Gavassino (2026) that Fick-type diffusion arises as the exact hydrodynamic sector of relativistic Fokker-Planck kinetic theory, and it resolves the long-standing ill-posedness problem of boosted diffusion identified by Hiscock and Lindblom (1985) and Kostädt and Liu (2000). Unlike the Cattaneo modification (Cattaneo, 1958; Israel and Stewart, 1979), which introduces second-order time derivatives and lacks universal character, or the approximate Lorentz transformation approach of Basar et al. (2024), which sacrifices Lorentz covariance, this work preserves exact Lorentz covariance by restricting the function space to kinetically admissible band-limited profiles. The results enable a well-posed initial-value formulation for relativistic diffusion that can be extended to higher dimensions and more general kinetic embeddings.
