# Algebraic approach to quarkyoniclike configuration and stable diquarks in dense matter

> We study the color-spin interaction energy of a quark, a diquark and a baryon with their surrounding baryons and/or quark matter. This is accomplished by classifying all possible flavor and spin states of the resulting multiquark configuration in both the flavor SU(2) and SU(3) symmetric cases. We find that while the baryon has the lowest interaction energy when there is only a single surrounding baryon, the quark has the lowest interaction energy when the surrounding has more than three baryons or becomes a quark gas. As the short range nucleon-nucleon interactions are dominated by the color-spin interactions, our finding suggests that the baryon modes near other baryons are suppressed due to larger repulsive energy compared to that of a quark and thus provides a quark model basis for the quarkyoniclike phase in dense matter. At the same time, when the internal interactions are taken into account, and the matter density is high so that the color-spin interaction becomes the dominant interaction, the diquark becomes the lowest energy configuration and will thus appear in both the dense baryonic and/or quark matter.

# Algebraic approach to quarkyoniclike configuration and stable diquarks in dense matter

## Key claim

The authors show that in dense baryonic matter, the color-spin interaction energy of a quark probe becomes lower than that of a baryon probe when the surrounding medium contains more than three baryons or is a free quark gas, providing a quark-model basis for the quarkyoniclike phase; however, when internal interactions within the probe are included, the diquark (or three correlated diquarks in flavor SU(3)) becomes the lowest-energy configuration at densities where color-spin interaction dominates.

## Method

The authors classify all possible flavor, color, and spin states of multiquark configurations consisting of n baryons (n=1,2,3) plus a probe (quark, diquark, or baryon) using Young diagram techniques for both flavor SU(2) and SU(3) symmetric cases. They compute the average color-spin interaction energy difference ΔH_CS^avg between the combined system and the separated components, normalized per quark and per surrounding baryon, assuming uniform spatial distribution of quarks. The analysis is extended to a free quark gas surrounding by considering all possible diquark configurations satisfying the Pauli principle, and to color-singlet three-correlated-diquark states.

## Key numbers

- For flavor SU(3) with a single surrounding baryon, the baryon probe has ΔH_CS^avg = 5.714, while the quark probe has 6 and the diquark probe has 6.
- For flavor SU(3) with three surrounding baryons, the quark probe has ΔH_CS^avg = 4.644, lower than the baryon probe at 4.944 and the diquark probe at 5.551.
- For flavor SU(3) with a free quark gas surrounding, the quark probe has ΔH_CS^avg = 2.823, compared to 6 for the baryon probe and 6.3 for the diquark probe.
- After including internal interactions, the flavor SU(2) diquark has total energy 4 across all surroundings (1b, 2b, 3b, free quarks), lower than the baryon (4.444) and quark (8 to 4.364).
- For flavor SU(3) after internal interactions, three correlated diquarks have total energy 2 across all surroundings, lower than the diquark (2 to 2.3) and baryon (2.277 to 3.333).
- The overall constant for color-spin interaction is estimated at C_B/m_u^2 ~ 18 MeV, based on the delta-nucleon mass difference from Lee (2007).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous quark-model study (Park 2021) by considering all possible flavor-spin configurations and generalizing to surroundings with more than one baryon and free quarks, improving upon earlier calculations that focused on the lowest-energy channel. The results provide a theoretical justification for the quarkyoniclike equation of state phenomenologically developed by McLerran (2007), Jeong (2019), Sen (2020), and Duarte (2020), where short-range baryon-baryon repulsion is suppressed relative to quark-baryon repulsion. The finding that diquarks become the lowest-energy configuration at high density suggests that diquark matter or "diquarkyonic" matter may emerge in dense baryonic and quark matter, and that H-dibaryon-like configurations (Jaffe 1976) may appear in dense matter even though they are not observed in vacuum (Park 2016).
