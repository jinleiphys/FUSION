# Quantum computation of mass gap in an asymptotically free theory

> In relativistic field theories, the mass spectrum is given by the difference between the energy of the vacuum and the excited states. Near the continuum limit, the cancellation between these two values leads to loss of precision. We propose a method to extract the mass gap directly using quantum computers and apply it to a particular version of the nonlinear $σ$-model with the correct continuum limit and perform calculations in quantum hardware (at strong coupling) and simulation in classical computers (at weak coupling).

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method to extract the mass gap directly from quantum computer simulations by constructing an observable (the "dipole operator") whose time-dependent expectation value oscillates at a frequency equal to the mass gap, thereby avoiding the loss of precision that occurs when computing the gap as the difference between separately estimated ground and excited state energies near the continuum limit.

## Method

The authors implement the (1+1)-dimensional nonlinear O(3) σ-model using the "fuzzy σ-model" (also called the "Heisenberg comb") formulation, which encodes the theory using only two qubits per lattice site. They prepare an initial state that approximates a linear combination of the ground and first excited states, evolve it under the full Hamiltonian using Trotter decomposition, and measure the expectation value of a dipole operator (constructed from Pauli Z operators) as a function of time. The oscillation frequency extracted from a damped sinusoidal fit to this expectation value gives the mass gap directly.

## Key numbers

- At strong coupling g=1.2, the average mass gap extracted from quantum processor unit runs across lattice sizes L=12, 16, 20 is ω = 2.56 ± 0.02 (in lattice units), compared to the exact value m_exact ≈ 2.34, a discrepancy of about 9%.
- At weak coupling g=0.6 with L=4, the extracted gap after Trotter error extrapolation is ω = 0.05461 ± 0.0001 (lattice units), compared to the exact numerical result ω_exact = 0.0541.
- At weak coupling g=0.6 with L=10, the extracted gap after Trotter error extrapolation is ω = 0.0430 ± 0.0003 (lattice units), compared to the exact numerical result ω_exact = 0.0428.
- The overlap of the strong coupling trial state |Ψ_strong⟩ with the exact ground state is |⟨Ψ_strong|E_0⟩|² ≳ 0.9 for lattice sizes L ≤ 8.

## Context

This work extends the fuzzy σ-model approach developed by Alexandru et al. (2019, 2021, 2022) and the Heisenberg comb formulation by Bhattacharya et al. (2020) and Singh (2019), which provided a qubitization of the O(3) nonlinear σ-model that preserves the correct continuum limit with asymptotic freedom. The method addresses a fundamental challenge in quantum computing for relativistic field theories: the cancellation between large vacuum and excited state energies near the continuum limit. The technique enables direct mass gap measurements on current noisy quantum hardware at strong coupling, while demonstrating feasibility at weak coupling through noiseless simulations, with the next step being implementation on error-corrected machines for weak coupling calculations.
