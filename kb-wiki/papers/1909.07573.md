# QCD Kondo excitons

> The quantum chromodynamics (QCD) Kondo effect is a quantum phenomenon in which heavy quarks ($c$, $b$) exist as impurity particles in quark matter composed of light quarks ($u$, $d$, $s$) at extremely high density. This is analogous to the famous Kondo effect in condensed matter physics. In the present paper, we show theoretically the existence of the "QCD Kondo excitons", i.e., the bound states of light quarks and heavy quarks, as the lowest-excitation modes above the ground state of the quark matter governed by the QCD Kondo effect. These are neutral for color and electric charges, similarly to the Kondo excitons in condensed matter, and they are new type of quasiparticles absent in the normal phase of quark matter. The QCD Kondo excitons have various masses and quantum numbers, i.e., flavors and spin parities (scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axialvector). The QCD Kondo excitons lead to the emergence of the neutral currents in transport phenomena, which are measurable in lattice QCD simulations. The study of the QCD Kondo excitons will provide us with understanding of new universal properties shared by quark matter and condensed matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims the theoretical existence of "QCD Kondo excitons", bound states of light and heavy quarks that are neutral in color and electric charge, as the lowest-excitation modes above the ground state of quark matter in the QCD Kondo phase. The authors hedge that this result is obtained under the assumption of static, uniformly distributed heavy quark impurities and a mean-field approximation with a hedgehog ansatz, and that quantitative differences from other interaction models (e.g., instanton-induced) would not change the qualitative conclusion about neutral current emergence.

## Method

The authors start from a Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL)-type Lagrangian with four-point interactions between light (massless) quarks and a heavy quark field treated in the heavy quark effective theory (HQET). They introduce auxiliary bosonic fields (scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, axialvector) for heavy-light modes, apply a mean-field approximation with a hedgehog ansatz to obtain the ground state condensate Δ, and then compute the inverse propagators for fluctuations around this ground state at one-loop order. The dispersion relations for the Kondo excitons are obtained by solving the determinant equations for the inverse propagator matrices in four independent channels, distinguishing between "dressed" excitons (involving the Bogoliubov quasiparticle and quasihole from flavor a=1) and "half-dressed" excitons (involving one Bogoliubov quasiparticle and one free light quark from flavors a=2,...,N_f).

## Key numbers

- Condensate magnitude: |Δ| = 0.0845 GeV at chemical potential μ = 0.5 GeV, with coupling G = 9.47 GeV⁻² and three-dimensional cutoff Λ = 0.65 GeV.
- Dressed Kondo exciton masses at zero momentum: m₁ = 0.175 GeV (quintuply degenerate), m₂ = 0.201 GeV (triply degenerate), m₃ = 0.223 GeV (triply degenerate).
- Half-dressed Kondo exciton masses at zero momentum: m′₁ = 0.100 GeV (eight-fold degenerate), m′₂ = 0.108 GeV (eight-fold degenerate).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of the QCD Kondo effect, previously proposed by Yasui (2013) and Hattori (2015), which describes heavy quark impurities in dense quark matter via non-Abelian color SU(N_c) interactions. The authors build on their own mean-field calculations of the QCD Kondo phase (Yasui 2016, 2017) and the concept of the QCD Kondo insulator, analogous to Kondo insulators in condensed matter physics where Kondo excitons were studied by Duan, Arovas, and Sham (1997) and others. The results enable future lattice QCD simulations of neutral current transport phenomena at nonzero isospin chemical potential, as proposed by Kogut (2002), and open the question of continuity between the Kondo phase in deconfined quark matter and the Kondo effect for heavy hadrons in confined nuclear matter.
