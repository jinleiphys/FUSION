# Thermodynamics of one-dimensional SU(4) and SU(6) fermions with attractive interactions

> Motivated by advances in the manipulation and detection of ultracold atoms with multiple internal degrees of freedom, we present a finite-temperature lattice Monte Carlo calculation of the density and pressure equations of state, as well as Tan's contact, of attractively interacting SU(4)- and SU(6)-symmetric fermion systems in one spatial dimension. We also furnish a nonperturbative proof of a universal relation whereby quantities computable in the SU(2) case completely determine the virial coefficients of the SU($N_f$) case. These one-dimensional systems are appealing because they can be experimentally realized in highly constrained traps and because of the dominant role played by correlations. The latter are typically nonperturbative and are crucial for understanding ground states and quantum phase transitions. While quantum fluctuations are typically overpowered by thermal ones in one and two dimensions at any finite temperature, we find that quantum effects do leave their imprint in thermodynamic quantities. Our calculations show that the additional degrees of freedom, relative to the SU(2) case, provide a dramatic enhancement of the density and pressure (in units of their noninteracting counterparts) in a wide region around vanishing $βμ$, where $β$ is the inverse temperature and $μ$ the chemical potential. As shown recently in experiments, the thermodynamics we explore here can be measured in a controlled and precise fashion in highly constrained traps and optical lattices. Our results are a prediction for such experiments in one dimension with atoms of high nuclear spin.

## Key claim

The paper presents a finite-temperature lattice Monte Carlo calculation showing that the density and pressure equations of state, as well as Tan's contact, for attractively interacting SU(4)- and SU(6)-symmetric fermions in one spatial dimension are dramatically enhanced relative to the SU(2) case, with the caveat that quantum effects leave their imprint in thermodynamic quantities even though quantum fluctuations are typically overpowered by thermal ones in one dimension at any finite temperature.

## Method

The authors employed the auxiliary-field path-integral Monte Carlo technique on a Euclidean space-time lattice of extent N_x x N_tau with periodic spatial and antiperiodic temporal boundary conditions, using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to write the grand-canonical partition function as a field integral evaluated via the hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm. They computed the density equation of state n(lambda, beta mu, N_f) for N_f = 4 and 6, from which they obtained the pressure by integration with respect to beta mu, the isothermal compressibility by differentiation, and Tan's contact by relating it to the interaction energy via the Feynman-Hellman theorem. They also provided a nonperturbative proof that the virial coefficients of the SU(N_f) case are completely determined by quantities computable in the SU(2) case through cumulant expansion of the partition function.

## Key numbers

- Density and pressure were computed for coupling strengths lambda = 1.75, 2.0, 2.25, 2.5, 2.75, 3.0 (where lambda^2 = beta g^2) for both N_f = 4 and N_f = 6
- The temporal lattice spacing was tau = 0.05 (in lattice units), with beta = 10 corresponding to thermal wavelength lambda_T approximately 8.0
- Statistical uncertainty of order 3-4% from 1000 decorrelated samples per data point
- For Tan's contact in the linear regime at beta mu >= 1.5, the slope zeta_1 = 0.21(1) for N_f = 4 and zeta_1 = 0.48(1) for N_f = 6
- The fit amplitude parameter A varies as A(lambda) = a_A lambda with a_A(N_f) approximately alpha (N_f - 1) where alpha = 0.73(3)
- The shift parameter xi varies linearly with lambda as xi(lambda) = a_xi lambda + b_xi with a_xi(N_f) approximately 0.23(5) N_f - 0.2(2) and b_xi(N_f) approximately 0.66(3)
- The second-order virial coefficient relation: Delta b_2(N_f) = (N_f - 1) Delta b_2, where Delta b_2(N_f) = b_2(N_f) - b_2^(0)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous finite-temperature lattice Monte Carlo study for SU(2) fermions in one dimension (Hoffman et al., 2015) to the higher-flavor cases of SU(4) and SU(6), motivated by experimental advances with ultracold alkaline-earth-metal atoms such as Sr and Yb that realize SU(N_f)-symmetric systems. The results provide predictions for experiments in one-dimensional traps and optical lattices with atoms of high nuclear spin, complementing exact Bethe ansatz solutions which are restricted to uniform systems in the ground state or require solving infinite towers of coupled integral equations at finite temperature (Batchelor et al., Takahashi).
