# Charge Balance Functions for Heavy-Ion Collisions at LHC Energies

> Heavy-ion collisions at the LHC provide the conditions to investigate regions of quark-gluon plasma that reach higher temperatures and that persist for longer periods of time compared to collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. This extended duration allows correlations from charge conservation to better separate during the quark-gluon plasma phase, and thus be better distinguished from correlations that develop during the hadron phase or during hadronization. In this study charge balance functions binned by relative rapidity and azimuthal angle and indexed by species are considered. A detailed theoretical model that evolves charge correlations throughout the entirety of an event is compared to preliminary results from the ALICE Collaboration. The comparison with experiment provides insight into the evolution of the chemistry and diffusivity during the collision. A ratio of balance functions is proposed to better isolate the effects of diffusion and thus better constrain the diffusivity.

## Key claim

The paper extends a previously developed model of charge balance functions, which successfully described RHIC data, to LHC energies, arguing that the longer-lived and hotter quark-gluon plasma at the LHC provides better separation between correlations from the QGP phase and those from hadronization, but the comparison with preliminary ALICE data carries caveats regarding the extraction of diffusivity.

## Method

The authors evolve charge-charge correlation functions for up, down, and strange quarks through a hydrodynamic stage using a random-walk Monte Carlo representation of diffusion, then project these correlations onto hadron species at a switching temperature of 155 MeV using statistical arguments based on charge susceptibilities. The hadronic stage is simulated with a microscopic hadronic simulation, and the resulting correlations are binned by relative rapidity and azimuthal angle to produce species-indexed balance functions for pions, kaons, and protons. The model uses the iEBE-VISHNU hydrodynamic code with parameters tuned to LHC spectra and flow, and the diffusion constant is taken from lattice gauge theory calculations.

## Key numbers

- The switching temperature from hydrodynamics to hadronic simulation is 155 MeV.
- For every charged pion observed, nearly 90% of the balancing charge is found in pions; for charged kaons or protons, roughly 50% of the balancing charge is found in the \(K^+K^-\) or \(p\bar{p}\) channels respectively.
- The number of up and down quarks nearly triples during hadronization, while the number of strange quarks falls by approximately 20%.
- The charge susceptibility \(\chi_{uu}/s\) rises sharply as the temperature falls below approximately 200 MeV, while \(\chi_{ss}/s\) shows a small fall in the same region.
- The partial normalization \(Z_{\pi|\pi}\) is nearly unity, meaning an excess of opposite-charge pions of nearly 0.9 pions per observed charged pion.

## Context

This work extends the model previously applied to RHIC collisions by Pratt (2017, 2018, 2019) to LHC energies, where the initial energy density is approximately double that at RHIC and the QGP phase persists longer. The comparison with preliminary ALICE results aims to test whether chemical equilibrium was reached early in the collision and to constrain the diffusivity of the QGP, building on STAR measurements that showed \(B_{\pi|\pi}\) narrows with centrality while \(B_{K|K}\) and \(B_{p|p}\) do not. The proposed ratio of angular harmonics of the balance function, confined outside small relative rapidity, is intended to provide a more robust extraction of diffusivity by avoiding ambiguities from interpreting \(B(\Delta\phi)\) alone.
