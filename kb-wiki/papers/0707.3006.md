# Spin effects in $p\bar p$ interaction and their possible use to polarize antiproton beams

> Low energy $p\bar p$ interaction is considered taking into account the polarization of both particles. The corresponding cross sections are calculated using the Paris nucleon-antinucleon optical potential. Then they are applied to the analysis of the polarization buildup which is due to the interaction of stored antiprotons with polarized protons of a hydrogen target. It is shown that, at realistic parameters of a storage ring and a target, the filtering mechanism provides a noticeable polarization in a time comparable with the beam lifetime.

## Key claim

Using the Paris nucleon-antinucleon optical potential, the authors calculate that the filtering mechanism can provide a noticeable polarization of stored antiprotons in a time comparable with the beam lifetime, but they caution that the accuracy of their predictions for the spin-dependent cross sections may be worse than for the total cross section because those spin-dependent parts are rather small.

## Method

The authors calculate the cross sections for low-energy proton-antiproton scattering including polarization of both particles, using the Paris nucleon-antinucleon optical potential with parameters from Refs. (paris82, paris94, paris99). They perform a partial-wave analysis of the strong elastic triplet and singlet scattering amplitudes, the charge-exchange amplitudes, and include the Coulomb amplitude and its interference with the strong amplitude. These cross sections are then applied to the kinetic equations describing polarization buildup of stored antiprotons interacting with a polarized hydrogen gas target in a storage ring.

## Key numbers

- The spin-dependent cross sections sigma_1 and sigma_2 are calculated for antiproton kinetic energies T in the lab frame from 20 to 100 MeV, for acceptance angles theta_acc^(l) = 10, 20, and 30 mrad (shown in figures).
- The beam polarization at the optimal time t0 = 2 tau_b is given by P_B(t0) = -2 P_T sigma_1/sigma_0 for target polarization perpendicular to the beam direction, and P_B(t0) = -2 P_T sigma_2/sigma_0 for parallel polarization.
- For realistic parameters of areal density n = 10^14 cm^-2 and revolution frequency f = 10^6 sec^-1, the polarization time t0 is shown as a function of T for several acceptance angles (figure), with values on the order of hours.
- The maximal perpendicular polarization P_perp occurs at energies 50-70 MeV, with the maximum shifting to lower energies as the acceptance angle increases.

## Context

This work extends the filtering method first proposed by Csonka (1968) and realized experimentally by Haeberli (1993) for 23-MeV stored protons, to the case of antiproton beams as proposed by the PAX Collaboration (2005). The authors use the Paris nucleon-antinucleon optical potential, which is the G-parity transform of the well-established Paris NN potential, to calculate spin-dependent cross sections that are not well known theoretically or experimentally. The results enable evaluation of the feasibility of producing polarized antiproton beams via spin filtering, a key requirement for the PAX physics program, and show that the Coulomb-strong interference, previously found important for pp scattering by Meyer (1994) and Milstein and Strakhovenko (2005), is even more significant in the pbar p case.
