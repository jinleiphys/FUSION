# Nuclear structure near the neutron dripline: lattice HFB calculations with high-energy continuum coupling

> We have developed a new Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) code which has been specifically designed to study ground state properties of nuclei near the neutron and proton drip lines. The unique feature of our code is that it takes into account the strong coupling to high-energy continuum states, up to an equivalent single-particle energy of 60 MeV. We solve the HFB equations for deformed, axially symmetric even-even nuclei in coordinate space on a 2-D lattice with Basis-Spline methods. For the p-h channel, the Skyrme (SLy4) effective N-N interaction is utilized, and for the p-p and h-h channel we use a delta interaction. We present results for binding energies, deformations, normal densities and pairing densities, Fermi levels, and pairing gaps. In particular, we will discuss neutron-rich isotopes of oxygen (O-22) and tin (Sn-150).

## Key claim

The authors present a new Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) code that solves the HFB equations for deformed, axially symmetric even-even nuclei in coordinate space on a 2-D lattice, uniquely including coupling to high-energy continuum states up to an equivalent single-particle energy of 60 MeV. The paper demonstrates that this approach reproduces results from existing 1-D spherical and 2-D oscillator-basis codes for spherical nuclei like O-22, while enabling calculations for deformed nuclei where those methods are less applicable.

## Method

The authors solve the HFB equations for axially symmetric even-even nuclei on a 2-D lattice in cylindrical coordinates (r,z) using Basis-Spline methods for wavefunction and operator representation. For the particle-hole channel, they use the Skyrme SLy4 effective N-N interaction, and for the particle-particle and hole-hole channels, they use a delta interaction with a cutoff energy of 60 MeV in the equivalent single-particle energy spectrum. The HFB eigenvalue problem is solved by direct diagonalization separately for each angular momentum projection quantum number Omega and each isospin projection, with 20-30 iterations typically needed for convergence to one part in 10^5 for the total binding energy.

## Key numbers

- For O-22: total binding energy = -164.64 MeV (this work), compared to -164.60 MeV (1-D spherical code) and -164.52 MeV (2-D oscillator basis); neutron Fermi level = -5.29 MeV; neutron pairing gap = 1.36 MeV; proton pairing gap = 0.0 MeV; r.m.s. radius = 2.92 fm (all three methods agree)
- For Sn-150: total binding energy = -1130 MeV (this work), compared to -1129 MeV (1-D spherical code); neutron Fermi level = -0.94 MeV; neutron pairing gap = 0.97 MeV; proton pairing gap = 0.00 MeV; r.m.s. radius = 5.132 fm
- The pairing interaction strength used for O-22 was V0 = -218.5 MeV fm^3 (volume pairing); for Sn-150, V0 = -170 MeV fm^3
- The 2-D calculations for Sn-150 used a box size R = 20 fm with maximum j_z = 13/2, diagonalizing approximately 3000 x 3000 matrices for each j_z and isospin value

## Context

This work extends the 1-D spherical coordinate-space HFB calculations of Dobaczewski et al. (1996) to the important case of deformed axially symmetric nuclei, and provides an alternative to the 2-D oscillator-basis expansion method of Stoitsov et al. (2000). The code enables calculations of ground-state properties for neutron-rich nuclei near the dripline, including deformed systems, and the authors plan to use these results as input for coordinate-space QRPA calculations of collective excited states. The approach addresses a computational Grand Challenge identified in the 1996 DOE/NSAC Long Range Plan for unrestricted HFB theory on the lattice.
