# Time Reversal Invariance Violating and Parity Conserving effects in Neutron Deuteron Scattering

> Time reversal invariance violating parity conserving effects for low energy elastic neutron deuteron scattering are calculated for meson exchange and EFT-type of potentials in a Distorted Wave Born Approximation, using realistic hadronic wave functions, obtained by solving three-body Faddeev equations in configuration space.

## Key claim

Time reversal invariance violating parity conserving (TVPC) effects in low-energy elastic neutron-deuteron scattering are calculated using both meson exchange and effective field theory (EFT) potentials, with the caveat that the results are obtained in a Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) treating TVPC interactions as a perturbation, and that the calculated TVPC scattering amplitudes are suppressed relative to parity-violating or time-reversal-violating parity-violating amplitudes by both kinematic factors and an extra momentum dependence factor of approximately 0.1.

## Method

The authors solved three-body Faddeev equations in configuration space using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential combined with the UIX three-nucleon force to obtain realistic hadronic wave functions for neutron-deuteron scattering. TVPC nucleon-nucleon interactions were then treated as perturbations, with matrix elements calculated in DWBA using both a meson exchange model (involving rho and h1 mesons) and an EFT approach where the general TVPC Hamiltonian was expressed in terms of 18 operators with Yukawa-type radial functions. The resulting R-matrix elements were transformed from jj-coupling to lS-coupling schemes to compute the difference in zero-angle scattering amplitudes for neutrons polarized parallel and anti-parallel to the [p x I](p·I) axis.

## Key numbers

* At center-of-mass energy E_cm = 100 keV, the strong s-wave scattering amplitude is f_st/p = (-60.6 + i 25.1) fm^2, giving total cross section sigma_tot = 3.15 b.
* The TVPC difference of scattering amplitudes in EFT (with mu = m_pi) is (1/(m_N C_n^{TVPC})) (Delta f^{TVPC}/p) = [(-0.03 to 0.01) + i(-0.0004 to 0.0013)] fm^2.
* For comparison, the PV difference of scattering amplitudes in EFT is (1/(m_N C_n^{PV})) (Delta f^{PV}/p) = [(-1.93 to 2.42) + i(-0.22 to 0.67)] fm^2.
* For comparison, the TVPV difference of scattering amplitudes in EFT is (1/(m_N C_n^{TVPV})) (Delta f^{TVPV}/p) = [(-1.63 to 0.66) + i(-0.063 to 0.22)] fm^2.
* For the meson exchange model at E_cm = 100 keV, the TVPC total cross section difference is Delta sigma^{TVPC} = 10^{-6} [g_h g_bar_h (1.15) - g_rho g_bar_rho (4.22 x 10^{-3})] b.
* For the meson exchange model at E_cm = 100 keV, the TVPC spin rotation angle per unit length is (1/N)(d phi^{TVPC}/dz) = 10^{-3} [g_h g_bar_h (1.25) - g_rho g_bar_rho (5.76 x 10^{-3})] rad fm^2.
* The real part of the TVPC scattering amplitude difference scales as sqrt(E) and the imaginary part scales as E for neutron energies below 1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of TVPC effects in neutron-deuteron scattering by Gudkov (1990) that used zero-range force approximations, finding good agreement with those results. It provides a more complete treatment using realistic three-body wave functions from Faddeev equations and both meson exchange and EFT descriptions of TVPC interactions. The results enable extraction of TVPC nucleon coupling constants from future measurements at spallation neutron facilities such as the SNS at Oak Ridge National Laboratory or the J-SNS at J-PARC, and serve as a first step toward understanding how nuclear structure influences TVPC effects in more complex nuclei.
