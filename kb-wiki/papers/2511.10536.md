# Non-Resonant Alpha-Induced Neutron-Emission: A Multi- Method Comparison Of Nuclear Reaction Rates

> The precise calculation of alpha-induced neutron-emission ($α$,n) reaction rates is fundamental to understanding nucleosynthesis in diverse stellar environments. This study investigates the nuclear reaction rates for various non-resonant alpha-induced neutron-emission reactions by employing analytical, theoretical, and computational methods. We have adopted analytical expressions from existing literature, and for the computational approach, we have implemented the Monte Carlo method. To better understand the functionality and validate the established open-source Monte Carlo scripts, we also developed our own computational code and used it to test various aspects of the method. A comprehensive comparison of the reaction rates from each method is presented by plotting their dependency on temperature.

# Non-Resonant Alpha-Induced Neutron-Emission: A Multi-Method Comparison of Nuclear Reaction Rates

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for non-resonant alpha-induced neutron-emission reactions, a custom Python Monte Carlo code and the modified open-source `rates-mc` C++ code produce reaction rates that completely overlap with standard `scipy.Quad` numerical integration across the full temperature range of 0.1 GK to 5 GK, with small deviations (less than 5%) only observed at low temperatures below 2 GK where the rate changes most rapidly.

## Method

The authors calculated thermally averaged reaction rates per particle pair for five non-resonant (alpha,n) reactions using three approaches: (1) a custom Python script implementing Monte Carlo sampling with a fourth-degree polynomial S-factor from Hussein et al.; (2) the open-source `rates-mc` C++ code modified to disable resonant reaction calculations; and (3) adaptive Gauss-Kronrod quadrature via `scipy.Quad`. The S-factor was approximated as a polynomial of degree 4, and the cross section was computed using the Gamow factor for Coulomb barrier penetration. The upper integration limit was set to the threshold energy plus 25 kT, beyond which the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution is suppressed by approximately 1.4 x 10^{-11}.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range investigated: 0.1 GK to 5 GK for all five reactions
- Upper integration limit defined as E_th_keV + 25 kT_keV, justified by the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution being suppressed by e^{-25} approximately 1.4 x 10^{-11} beyond this point
- Small deviations of less than 5% observed between methods at temperatures below 2 GK
- Five reactions studied: 45Sc(alpha,n)48V, 51V(alpha,n)54Mn, 55Mn(alpha,n)58Co, 50Cr(alpha,n)53Fe, and 48Ti(alpha,n)51Cr

## Context

This work extends the methodology of the open-source `rates-mc` package (developed in C++) by providing a Python-based alternative for calculating non-resonant (alpha,n) reaction rates, deliberately chosen over existing Fortran packages such as TALYS, EMPIRE, and NON-SMOKER because `rates-mc` can isolate resonant from non-resonant reactions with minor modifications. The reactions studied are relevant to nucleosynthesis during late burning stages of massive stars (M greater than or equal to 8 solar masses) and helium intershell burning in asymptotic giant branch (AGB) stars, where these reactions serve as neutron sources for s-process branch points. The paper currently presents only median reaction rates and states that calculating reaction uncertainty is beyond its scope, to be pursued in future work.
