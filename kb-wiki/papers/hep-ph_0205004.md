# Two Specific Correlation Patterns as Indicators for Various Random Multiplicative Cascade Processes

> It is suggested and demonstrated that two specific 2-dimensional correlation patterns, fixed-to-arbitrary bin and neighboring bin correlation patterns, are efficient for identifying various random multiplicative cascade processes. A possible application of these two correlation patterns to single event analysis in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider experiments is discussed.

## Key claim

Two specific two-dimensional correlation patterns (fixed-to-arbitrary bin and neighboring bin) can distinguish between different random multiplicative cascade processes, but only when the patterns are constructed without horizontal averaging over bins, unlike conventional two-point correlation moments which fail to differentiate such processes.

## Method

The authors analytically derived the two-point correlation cumulants for three random binary cascade models: the alpha model (no probability conservation), the p model (probability conservation with fixed weights), and the c model (probability conservation with random weights). They then constructed fixed-to-arbitrary bin correlation patterns (fixing one bin at K1=1 and varying K2) and neighboring bin correlation patterns (C_{K,K+1}) for each model with J=6 cascade steps, using model parameters alpha=0.4, beta=0.4, and gamma=0.8.

## Key numbers

- For the alpha model with alpha=0.4: same-lineage cumulant A_alpha = (1/4)[ln((1+alpha)/(1-alpha))]^2, splitting cumulant B_alpha = 0
- For the p model with beta=0.4: same-lineage cumulant A_p = (1/4)[ln((1+beta)/(1-beta))]^2, splitting cumulant B_p = -(1/4)[ln((1+beta)/(1-beta))]^2
- For the c model with gamma=0.8: splitting cumulant B_c is nonzero and |A_c| != |B_c|, with no zero correlations present
- The fixed-to-arbitrary bin pattern contains 2^{J-1} * (2-1) pairs of bins with common ancestor at the initial interval (d_2=J) and 2^{J-2} * (2-1) pairs with common ancestor at the first cascade step (d_2=J-1), for J=6 giving 32 and 16 pairs respectively
- The neighboring bin pattern has one pair at center K=2^{J-1} (K=32) with d_2=J, and two pairs at K=2^{J-2} and K=2^{J-2}*3 (K=16 and K=48) with d_2=J-1

## Context

This work extends the multifractal analysis framework of Paladin and Vulpiani (1987) and the scaling moment approach of Bialas and Peschanski (1988), specifically addressing the limitation that conventional two-point correlation moments (as used in scaling analyses) cannot distinguish between different cascade mechanisms. The authors build on the correlation cumulant formalism of Greiner, Eggers, and Lipa (1998) and apply it to three established cascade models: the alpha model from scaling analyses, the p model of Meneveau and Sreenivasan (1987) from turbulence, and the c model of Wu and Liu (1993). The proposed patterns enable single-event analysis in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider experiments, potentially identifying events with Quark Gluon Plasma formation where correlations change dramatically.
