# Transport properties of hot gluonic matter

> We discuss the temperature dependence of the scaled jet quenching parameter of hot gluonic matter within a quasiparticle approach. A pronounced maximum in the vicinity of the transition temperature is observed, where the ratio of the scaled jet quenching parameter and the inverse specific shear viscosity increases above typical values for weakly coupled systems.

## Key claim

The scaled jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}/T^3\) of hot gluonic matter, derived from the shear viscosity via a general relation for weakly coupled partonic quasiparticle systems, shows a pronounced maximum near the transition temperature \(T_c\) rather than a monotonic behavior; the ratio \(X = (\hat{q}/T^3)/(\eta/s)^{-1}\) becomes comparable to estimates for strongly coupled systems near \(T_c\) even though the quasiparticle approach assumes weak coupling.

## Method

The authors used a phenomenological quasiparticle model (QPM) for pure gluodynamics, in which gluonic quasiparticles obey a temperature-dependent dispersion relation with a medium-modified gluon mass. They computed the specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s\) in relaxation time approximation via an effective kinetic theory of Boltzmann-Vlasov type, as previously done in Bluhm et al. (2011). From \(\eta/s\), they obtained the scaled jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}/T^3\) using the formal relation derived by Majumder et al. (2007), which connects \(\eta\) and \(\hat{q}\) under the assumption that the interaction between an energetic parton and the medium quasiparticles has the same structure as interactions among medium constituents.

## Key numbers

- The scaled jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}/T^3\) exhibits a pronounced maximum in the vicinity of the transition temperature \(T_c\), with a steep drop as \(T \to T_c^+\) and a significant enhancement just above \(T_c\) compared to the high-temperature region.
- For a gas of free massless bosons, the relation \(\hat{q}/T^3 \approx 1.25 \, (\eta/s)^{-1}\) holds, giving a constant ratio \(X \approx 1.25\).
- In the QPM at high temperatures, the ratio \(X\) becomes as small as 1.25, indicating weak coupling; as \(T \to T_c^+\), \(X\) increases significantly and becomes comparable to \(X \approx 2.602\), which is the value obtained in \(\mathcal{N}=4\) supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory for a 't Hooft coupling \(\lambda = 6\pi\).
- The specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s\) has a minimum near \(T_c\), while the specific bulk viscosity \(\zeta/s\) becomes large near \(T_c\) and vanishes logarithmically with increasing temperature.

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle model calculations of shear and bulk viscosities for the pure gluon plasma by Bluhm et al. (2011) to the jet quenching parameter, using the formal relation proposed by Majumder et al. (2007). The finding that \(\hat{q}/T^3\) is significantly enhanced near \(T_c\) supports arguments by Scardina et al. (2011) and Liao et al. (2009) that energy loss occurring mainly around \(T_c\) is crucial for simultaneously describing the nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow of hadrons. The non-trivial temperature dependence of the ratio \(X\) bridges weak-coupling behavior at high temperature with strong-coupling estimates from holographic approaches, enabling further study of the fundamental connection between transport coefficients in QCD matter.
