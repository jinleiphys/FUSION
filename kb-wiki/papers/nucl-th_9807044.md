# Determination of Li-6 -- He-4 interaction from multi-energy scattering data

> We present the first successful potential model description of Li-6 -- He-4 scattering. The differential cross-sections for three energies and the vector analyzing powers for two energies were fitted by a single potential with energy dependent imaginary components. An essential ingredient is a set of Majorana terms in each component. The potential was determined using a recently developed direct data-to-potential inversion method which is a generalisation of the IP S-matrix-to-potential inversion algorithm. We discuss the problems related to this phenomenological approach, and discuss the relationship of our results to existing and future theories.

# Determination of Li-6 – He-4 interaction from multi-energy scattering data

## Key claim
The authors present the first successful potential model description of Li-6 – He-4 scattering, but the fit required substantial Majorana (parity-dependent) terms and an energy-dependent imaginary component, and the potentials still exhibit unitarity breaking in one partial wave at one energy, indicating that the description is not fully satisfactory.

## Method
The authors applied the Generalised Iterative Perturbative (GIP) method, a direct data-to-potential inversion technique that generalises the IP S-matrix-to-potential inversion algorithm, to fit differential cross-sections at three centre-of-mass energies (7.85, 11.1, and 15.0 MeV) and vector analysing powers at two energies (11.1 and 15.0 MeV). The inversion determined eight potential components: real and imaginary central and vector spin-orbit terms, each split into Wigner and Majorana parts, with the imaginary components having an explicit linear energy dependence. The starting reference potential (SRP) used the M3Y density-independent folding model for the real central Wigner component, with imaginary and spin-orbit components taken from Green et al.

## Key numbers
- Volume integral of the real central Wigner component for the three-energy fit (3EN-1): J_R = 411 MeV fm^3, with rms radius 3.042 fm.
- Volume integral of the real central Wigner component for the two-energy fit (2EN-1): J_R = 339 MeV fm^3, compared to an initial unphysical potential with J_R approximately 800 MeV fm^3.
- Centre-of-mass energies fitted: 7.85 MeV, 11.1 MeV, and 15.0 MeV (corresponding to projectile energies of 19.6, 27.7, and 37.5 MeV, respectively).
- The imaginary potential energy dependence used p = 1, with E_0 = 0 and E_ref = 19.6 MeV.
- Unitarity breaking observed: S_{23} = 1.26 for l=2, j=3 at 19.6 MeV.

## Context
This work extends prior failed attempts to describe Li-6 – He-4 scattering with conventional potential models, including those by Green et al., which lacked Majorana terms and produced unreasonably deep potentials. The authors show that including Majorana terms allows a potential with a real central Wigner component close to the M3Y folding model prediction, except in the surface where breakup effects are expected. The GIP method enables future quantitative evaluation of microscopic theories such as Resonating Group Method (RGM) and alpha + d + alpha cluster models, particularly when tensor interactions and unitarity constraints are incorporated.
