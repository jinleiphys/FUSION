## Key claim

The ratio between the $\pi^0\pi^0\Lambda$ and $K^-p$ mass distributions in proton-induced reactions provides a model-independent test of the ratio of the $\Lambda(1520)$ resonance's couplings to $\pi\Sigma(1385)$ and $\bar{K}N$, but the absolute value of this ratio depends on final-state interactions and carries an uncertainty of about 0.2.

## Method

The authors used a unitarized coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter formalism including $\pi\Sigma(1385)$, $K\Xi(1530)$, $\bar{K}N$, and $\pi\Sigma$ channels, with parameters fitted to $\bar{K}N\to\bar{K}N$ and $\bar{K}N\to\pi\Sigma$ partial-wave amplitudes. They then implemented these $T$-matrices into production amplitudes for $pp\to pK^+K^-p$ and $pp\to pK^+\pi^0\pi^0\Lambda$ reactions, assuming the initial $pp$ pair is in a $^1D_2$ partial wave and including final-state interactions via Jost-function factors for the $\Lambda p$ and $pp$ subsystems.

## Key numbers

- The $\Lambda(1520)$ resonance width is $\Gamma_\Ls=15.6$ MeV.
- The effective couplings of the $\Lambda(1520)$ to the channels are: $g_1=0.91$ (to $\pi\Sigma^*$), $g_2=-0.29$ (to $K\Xi^*$), $g_3=-0.54$ (to $\bar{K}N$), and $g_4=-0.45$ (to $\pi\Sigma$).
- The ratio $(g_1/g_3)^2$ at the peak is 2.8.
- The predicted ratio $R$ between the $\pi^0\pi^0\Lambda$ and $K^-p$ mass distributions at the peak is $R\sim 0.5\pm 0.2$ (with final-state interactions included) or $R\sim 0.9$ (without final-state interactions).
- The incident proton momentum used in the calculations is $3.7$ GeV.
- The $\Lambda p$ scattering length used for final-state interactions is $a=(-1.4\pm 0.5)$ fm with effective range $r=(4\pm 1)$ fm; for $pp$ final-state interactions $a=-7.8$ fm and $r=2.79$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the unitarized coupled-channel analysis of the $\Lambda(1520)$ resonance from Roca et al. (2006) and Sarkar et al. (2005), which treated the resonance as dynamically generated from meson-baryon interactions, to proton-induced production reactions accessible at COSY (JĂĽlich) and the CSR facility (Lanzhou). The approach builds on earlier studies of dynamically generated resonances such as the $\Lambda(1405)$ by Oller et al. (2000), Jido et al. (2003), and Garcia-Recio et al. (2003), and enables experimental tests of the predicted coupling strengths of the $\Lambda(1520)$ to $\pi\Sigma(1385)$ versus $\bar{K}N$, which distinguish between a quasibound $\pi\Sigma^*$ picture and a more mixed composition.
