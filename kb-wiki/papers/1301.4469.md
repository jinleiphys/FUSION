# Production of $c \bar c c \bar c$ in double-parton scattering within $k_{t}$-factorization approach -- meson-meson correlations

> We discuss production of two pairs of $c \bar c$ in proton-proton collisions at the LHC. Both double-parton scattering (DPS) and single-parton scattering (SPS) contributions are included in the analysis. Each step of DPS is calculated within $k_t$-factorization approach, i.e. effectively including next-to-leading order corrections. The conditions how to identify the DPS contribution are presented. The discussed mechanism unavoidably leads to the production of pairs of mesons: $D_i D_j$ (each containing $c$ quarks) or $\bar D_i \bar D_j$ (each containing $\bar c$ antiquarks). We calculate corresponding production rates for different combinations of charmed mesons as well as some differential distribution for $(D^0 D^0$ + $\bar D^0 \bar D^0)$ production. Within large theoretical uncertainties the predicted DPS cross section is fairly similar to the cross section measured recently by the LHCb collaboration. The best description is obtained with the Kimber-Martin-Ryskin (KMR) unintegrated gluon distribution, which very well simulates higher-order corrections. The contribution of SPS, calculated in the high-energy approximation, turned out to be rather small. Finally, we emphasize significant contribution of DPS mechanism to inclusive charmed meson spectra measured recently by ALICE, ATLAS and LHCb.

## Key claim

The double-parton scattering (DPS) mechanism for producing two pairs of charm quarks in proton-proton collisions at the LHC gives a significant contribution to inclusive charmed meson spectra, and within large theoretical uncertainties the predicted DPS cross section is fairly similar to the cross section measured by the LHCb collaboration, with the best description obtained using the Kimber-Martin-Ryskin (KMR) unintegrated gluon distribution.

## Method

The authors calculated double-parton scattering (DPS) production of $c \bar c c \bar c$ using the $k_t$-factorization approach for each individual scattering, which effectively includes next-to-leading order corrections through unintegrated gluon distributions. Single-parton scattering (SPS) contributions were computed separately using a high-energy approximation with impact factors describing the coupling of $c \bar c$ pairs to gluons. The parton-level results were converted to meson-level predictions using Peterson fragmentation functions, and experimental cuts from the LHCb detector acceptance were applied to produce differential distributions for charmed meson pairs.

## Key numbers

- DPS cross section for $D^0 D^0$ production: 265 nb (with KMR UGDF, $\varepsilon_c = 0.05$), with uncertainties $^{+140}_{-77}$ nb from scale variation and $^{+157}_{-94}$ nb from charm quark mass variation, compared to experimental value $690 \pm 40 \pm 70$ nb
- DPS cross section for $D^0 D^+$ production: 212 nb (KMR, $\varepsilon_c = 0.05$), with uncertainties $^{+112}_{-62}$ nb and $^{+126}_{-75}$ nb, compared to experimental value $520 \pm 80 \pm 70$ nb
- DPS cross section for $D^0 D^+_S$ production: 75 nb (KMR, $\varepsilon_c = 0.05$), with uncertainties $^{+40}_{-22}$ nb and $^{+45}_{-27}$ nb, compared to experimental value $270 \pm 50 \pm 40$ nb
- DPS cross section for $D^+ D^+$ production: 42 nb (KMR, $\varepsilon_c = 0.05$), with uncertainties $^{+23}_{-13}$ nb and $^{+26}_{-15}$ nb, compared to experimental value $80 \pm 10 \pm 10$ nb
- DPS cross section for $D^+ D^+_S$ production: 30 nb (KMR, $\varepsilon_c = 0.05$), with uncertainties $^{+16}_{-9}$ nb and $^{+18}_{-11}$ nb, compared to experimental value $70 \pm 15 \pm 10$ nb
- DPS cross section for $D^+_S D^+_S$ production: 11 nb (KMR, $\varepsilon_c = 0.05$), with uncertainties $^{+5}_{-3}$ nb and $^{+6}_{-4}$ nb
- Effective cross section parameter $\sigma_{eff} = 15$ mb used in the DPS factorization formula
- Charm quark mass $m_c = 1.5 \pm 0.3$ GeV used for uncertainty estimates
- SPS contribution to $D^0 D^0$ transverse momentum distribution is more than two orders of magnitude smaller than the DPS contribution

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous leading-order collinear calculation of $c \bar c c \bar c$ production (Maciula and Szczurek, 2012) by incorporating $k_t$-factorization to effectively include NLO corrections, and provides the first differential distributions at the hadron level for comparison with LHCb data (LHCb collaboration, 2012). The results contradict the approach of Berezhnoy et al. (2012), who estimated DPS contributions based on inclusive D meson spectra without full fragmentation kinematics, and show that the DPS mechanism dominates over SPS for same-sign charmed meson pair production in the LHCb acceptance. The work enables future studies of azimuthal correlations between charmed mesons as a model-independent signature of DPS effects, and highlights the need for improved SPS calculations with complete diagram sets to resolve remaining discrepancies with experimental cross sections.
