# Probing Transverse Momentum Dependent Structures with Azimuthal Dependence of Energy Correlators

> We study the azimuthal angle dependence of the energy-energy correlators $\langle \mathcal{E}(\hat{n}_1)\mathcal{E}(\hat{n}_2)\rangle$ in the back-to-back region for $e^+e^-$ annihilation and deep inelastic scattering (DIS) processes with general polarization of the proton beam. We demonstrate that the polarization information of the beam and the underlying partons from the hard scattering is propagated into the azimuthal angle dependence of the energy-energy correlators. In the process, we define the Collins-type EEC jet functions and introduce a new EEC observable using the lab-frame angles in the DIS process. Furthermore, we extend our formalism to explore the two-point energy correlation between hadrons with different quantum numbers $\mathbb{S}_i$ in the back-to-back limit $\langle \mathcal{E}_{\mathbb{S}_1}(\hat{n}_1)\mathcal{E}_{\mathbb{S}_2}(\hat{n}_2)\rangle$. We find that in the Operator Product Expansion (OPE) region the nonperturbative information is entirely encapsulated by a single number. Using our formalism, we present several phenomenological studies that showcase how energy correlators can be used to probe transverse momentum dependent structures.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the azimuthal angle dependence of energy-energy correlators (EEC) in the back-to-back region encodes transverse momentum dependent (TMD) structures, including a new \(\cos(2\phi)\) term in \(e^+e^-\) annihilation linked to the Collins fragmentation function, and that in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) the azimuthal dependence can probe both polarized and unpolarized TMD parton distribution functions, with the caveat that these results hold in the \(\tau \to 0\) limit up to power corrections.

## Method

The authors develop a factorization formalism for the azimuthal-dependent EEC in the back-to-back region (\(\theta \to \pi\) or \(\tau \to 0\)) for both \(e^+e^-\) annihilation and DIS processes, using soft collinear effective field theory (SCET) and TMD factorization. They define a new "Collins-type" EEC jet function \(\mathcal{J}_q^{\perp}(b,\mu,\zeta)\) related to the Collins fragmentation function, and derive the full azimuthal structure of the EEC in terms of TMD parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions. For DIS, they also introduce an EEC observable using lab-frame angles (\(q_*\)) to improve experimental resolution at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC).

## Key numbers

* The back-to-back variable \(\tau = (1+\cos\theta)/2\) is related to transverse momentum as \(\tau = \bm{P}_{h_1\perp}^2/(z_1^2 Q^2)\) for \(e^+e^-\) and \(\tau = \bm{P}_{hT}^2/(z_h^2 Q^2)\) for DIS, where \(Q\) is the hard scale.
* The born cross-section for \(e^+e^-\) is \(\sigma_0 = 4\pi N_c\alpha_{\rm em}^2/3Q^2\).
* The born cross-section for DIS is \(\sigma_0 = (2\pi\alpha_{\rm em}^2/Q^2)[1+(1-y)^2]/y\).
* The hard function at NLO is \(H(Q,\mu) = 1 + (\alpha_s/2\pi)C_F[3\ln(Q^2/\mu^2) - \ln^2(Q^2/\mu^2) - 8 + 7\pi^2/6]\).
* The acoplanarity angle in the EIC lab frame relates to the Breit frame as \(\tan\phi_{\rm acop}^{\mathrm{EIC}} = -\sqrt{\tau/(1-y)}\sin\phi_h + \mathcal{O}(\tau)\).
* The lab-frame observable \(q_*\) is defined as \(q_* \equiv 2 P_{\mathrm{EIC}}^0 [e^{\eta_h}/(1+e^{\Delta\eta})] \tan\phi_{\mathrm{acop}}^{\mathrm{EIC}}\) and approximates \(q_* \approx -P_{hT}\sin\phi_h\) at leading power.

## Context

This work extends the study of energy-energy correlators beyond the usual \(\theta\)-only dependence (Basham 1978, Moult 2018, Duhr 2022) to include azimuthal angle \(\phi\) dependence, connecting EEC observables to the TMD factorization framework used in spin physics (Collins 1992, Boer 2008, Bacchetta 2006). It builds on the DIS EEC introduced by Li (2021) and the lab-frame angle \(q_*\) proposed by Gao (2022), enabling future EIC measurements to probe TMD parton distribution functions and Collins fragmentation functions through azimuthal asymmetries in energy correlators.
