# A finite range pairing force for density functional theory in superfluid nuclei

> The problem of pairing in the $^{1}$S$_{0}$ channel of finite nuclei is revisited. In nuclear matter forces of separable form can be adjusted to the bare nuclear force, to any phenomenological pairing interaction such as the Gogny force or to exact solutions of the gap equation. In finite nuclei, because of translational invariance, such forces are no longer separable. Using well known techniques of Talmi and Moshinsky we expand the matrix elements in a series of separable terms, which converges quickly preserving translational invariance and finite range. In this way the complicated problem of a cut-off at large momenta or energies inherent in other separable or zero range pairing forces is avoided. Applications in the framework of the relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov approach show that the pairing properties are depicted on almost the same footing as by the original pairing interaction not only in nuclear matter, but also in finite nuclei. This simple separable force can be easily applied for the investigation of pairing properties in nuclei far from stability as well as for further investigations going beyond mean field theory.

## Key claim

The authors construct a separable finite-range pairing force that reproduces the pairing properties of the full Gogny force in the 1S0 channel for both nuclear matter and finite nuclei, while avoiding the cut-off problems inherent in zero-range or other separable forces. The key qualifier is that the force is fitted specifically to the Gogny force's density-dependent gap curve in nuclear matter, not to the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Method

The authors start from a separable ansatz in momentum space for the pairing interaction in the 1S0 channel, using a Gaussian form p(k) = exp(-a²k²) whose parameters G and a are fitted to reproduce the pairing gap at the Fermi surface as a function of Fermi momentum obtained from the Gogny force D1 and D1S parameter sets in symmetric nuclear matter. They then transform this force to coordinate space and, using Talmi-Moshinsky brackets, expand the matrix elements in a series of separable terms in a spherical harmonic oscillator basis for finite nuclei. The resulting separable force is implemented in relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations for spherical Sn and Pb isotope chains.

## Key numbers

- For the Gogny D1 parameter set: G = 738 MeV·fm³ and a = 0.636 fm for the single-Gaussian fit
- For the Gogny D1S parameter set: G = 728 MeV·fm³ and a = 0.644 fm for the single-Gaussian fit
- For the three-Gaussian fit to D1: G = 731.25 MeV·fm³ with a₁ = 0.69 fm, a₂ = 0.16 fm, a₃ = 0.47 fm
- For the three-Gaussian fit to D1S: G = 742.09 MeV·fm³ with a₁ = 0.74 fm, a₂ = 0.26 fm, a₃ = 0.36 fm
- The sum over separable terms in Eq. (vs) converges with N₀ = 8 major oscillator shells for nuclei far from stability
- The largest discrepancy in pairing energies between the separable force and the full Gogny force is less than 10% for Sn isotopes (100Sn to 160Sn) and Pb isotopes (164Pb to 264Pb)

## Context

This work extends the approach of separable pairing forces previously applied in nuclear matter (Duguet, 2004) to finite nuclei by using Talmi-Moshinsky transformation techniques. The method provides a computationally simpler alternative to the full finite-range Gogny force (Gogny, 1980; Berger et al., 1991) for pairing in density functional theory, while avoiding the cut-off parameter problems inherent in zero-range delta forces (Bender et al., 2002) and seniority pairing models. The separable force enables systematic investigations over wide ranges of nuclei and applications beyond mean field such as generator coordinate method calculations with angular momentum and particle number projection.
