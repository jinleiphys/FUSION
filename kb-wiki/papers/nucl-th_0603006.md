# Dispersion Theory and the Low Energy Constants for Neutral Pion Photoproduction

> The relativistic amplitudes of pion photoproduction are evaluated by dispersion relations at t=const. The imaginary parts of the amplitudes are taken from the MAID model covering the absorption spectrum up to center-of-mass energies W = 2.2 GeV. For sub-threshold kinematics the amplitudes are expanded in powers of the two independent variables νand t related to energy and momentum transfer. Subtracting the loop corrections from this power series allows one to determine the counter terms of covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory. The proposed continuation of the amplitudes into the unphysical region provides a unique framework to derive the low-energy constants to any given order as well as an estimate of the higher order terms by global properties of the absorption spectrum.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that dispersion relations at fixed momentum transfer can be used to analytically continue neutral pion photoproduction amplitudes into the unphysical sub-threshold region, providing a model-independent framework to extract the low-energy constants of covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory from global properties of the nucleon excitation spectrum, with the caveat that the vector meson pole contributions must be added separately as they are not reproduced by the dispersion integrals.

## Method

The authors evaluate relativistic invariant amplitudes for pion photoproduction using fixed-t dispersion relations, taking the imaginary parts from the MAID05 unitary isobar model which covers center-of-mass energies up to W = 2.2 GeV. The dispersive amplitudes (after subtracting known nucleon and pion pole terms) are expanded in a power series in the variables ν and t around the unphysical point ν = 0, t = M_π² where all particles are on their mass shell. By subtracting the loop corrections of covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory from this power series, the counter terms (low-energy constants) of the effective field theory are determined.

## Key numbers

- The dispersion integrals use MAID05 input covering center-of-mass energies up to W = 2.2 GeV
- For isovector amplitudes, the convergence strip of the dispersion relations is -0.376 GeV² < t < 0.175 GeV²
- For isoscalar amplitudes, the convergence strip is -0.525 GeV² < t < 0.073 GeV²
- The final breakdown of dispersion relations occurs at ν = 0 and t = -1.10 GeV² for charged pions and t = -1.06 GeV² for neutral pions
- The ω meson coupling constants used are λ_V = 0.314, g_V^(V) = 16.3, g_V^(T) = -15.4; for the ρ meson: λ_V = 0.103, g_V^(V) = 1.78, g_V^(T) = 22.6
- The vector meson contributions yield 20% of the dispersive contribution for A₂ and 50% for A₄

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the Fubini-Furlan-Rossetti sum rule (Pasquini et al., 2005) by providing a systematic framework to extract low-energy constants from dispersion relations. It addresses shortcomings of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) noted by Bernard et al. (1991), which produced spurious behavior of amplitudes in the unphysical region due to the 1/M_N expansion. The newly developed manifestly Lorentz-invariant renormalization schemes (Becher and Leutwyler, 1999) and the infrared regularization approach of Bernard et al. (2005) provide covariant treatments that this dispersion framework can now constrain using global properties of the absorption spectrum.
