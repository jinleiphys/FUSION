# Nuclear pairing reduction due to rotation and blocking

> Nuclear pairing gaps of normally deformed and superdeformed nuclei are investigated using the particle-number conserving (PNC) formalism for the cranked shell model, in which the blocking effects are treated exactly. Both rotational frequency $ω$-dependence and seniority (number of unpaired particles) $ν$-dependence of the pairing gap $\tildeΔ$ are investigated. For the ground-state bands of even-even nuclei, PNC calculations show that in general $\tildeΔ$ decreases with increasing $ω$, but the $ω$-dependence is much weaker than that calculated by the number-projected Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov approach. For the multiquasiparticle bands (seniority $ν> 2$), the pairing gaps keep almost $ω$-independent. As a function of the seniority $ν$, the bandhead pairing gaps $\tildeΔ(ν,ω=0)$ decrease slowly with increasing $ν$. Even for the highest seniority $ν$ bands identified so far, $\tildeΔ(ν,ω=0)$ remains greater than 70% of $\tildeΔ(ν=0,ω=0)$.

## Key claim

The pairing gap reduction due to rotation is much weaker than predicted by the number-projected Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov approach, and for multiquasiparticle bands with seniority greater than 2 the pairing gaps remain almost independent of rotational frequency; even for the highest seniority bands identified, the bandhead pairing gap remains greater than 70% of the ground-state band value.

## Method

The authors used the particle-number conserving (PNC) formalism for the cranked shell model, diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in a cranked many-particle configuration space of dimension 1500 for both protons and neutrons. The blocking effects from unpaired particles were treated exactly by constructing many-particle configurations with specific seniority (number of unpaired particles) and enforcing Pauli blocking. The effective monopole and quadrupole pairing strengths were fixed by reproducing experimental odd-even differences in binding energies and bandhead energies of low-lying 1-quasiparticle bands.

## Key numbers

- For the ground-state band of 168Yb, the neutron pairing gap at ω=0.61 MeV/ħ is about 70% of its bandhead value, and the proton pairing gap is about 80% of its bandhead value.
- For the ground-state band of 168Hf, the neutron pairing gap at ω=0.52 MeV/ħ is about 70% of its bandhead value, and the proton pairing gap is about 83% of its bandhead value.
- For the ground-state band of 238U, the neutron pairing gap at ω=0.30 MeV/ħ is about 85% of its bandhead value, and the proton pairing gap is about 84% of its bandhead value.
- For the ground-state band of 253No, the neutron pairing gap at ω=0.30 MeV/ħ is about 84% of its bandhead value, and the proton pairing gap is about 76% of its bandhead value.
- For the seniority ν=0 superdeformed band 194Hg(1), the neutron pairing gap at ω=0.50 MeV/ħ is about 83% of its bandhead value, and the proton pairing gap is about 80% of its bandhead value.
- For the seniority ν=1 superdeformed bands 193Hg(1) and 193Hg(2b), the neutron pairing gap at ω=0.50 MeV/ħ is about 86% of its bandhead value.
- For the seniority ν=2 superdeformed band 194Hg(2), the neutron pairing gap at ω=0.50 MeV/ħ is about 90% of its bandhead value.
- The bandhead pairing gap reductions with seniority for protons in 177Ta and 178W are: about 91% for ν=1, about 86% for ν=2, about 80% for ν=3, and about 78% for ν=4, relative to the ν=0 value.

## Context

This work extends the particle-number conserving approach previously applied to moments of inertia (Zeng 1994, Zhang 2009) to systematically study pairing gaps in both normally deformed and superdeformed nuclei. It contradicts the stronger rotational suppression of pairing found in number-projected Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations by Canto (1985) and Egido (1985), and shows a weaker seniority dependence than the Lipkin-Nogami results of Dracoulis (1998). The results enable more reliable predictions for pairing in high-spin and multiquasiparticle bands without the spurious pairing collapse seen in BCS-based methods.
