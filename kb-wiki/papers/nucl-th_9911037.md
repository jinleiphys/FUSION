# Nuclear Effects in High Energy Photon and Neutral Pion Production

> We study the mechanism of the $γ$ and $π^0$ enhancement at large transverse momenta in proton-nucleus collisions. A proposed mechanism suggests an increase in the width of the transverse momentum distribution of partons in a semihard process.

# Nuclear Effects in High Energy Photon and Neutral Pion Production

## Key claim
The observed enhancement of direct photon and neutral pion production at large transverse momenta in proton-nucleus collisions (the Cronin effect) can be explained by a "saturated" model where the incoming proton undergoes at most one semi-hard collision before the hard scattering, rather than accumulating transverse momentum from many soft collisions. This mechanism yields a common value of the transverse momentum broadening parameter across different nuclear targets, unlike the soft-collision model which requires target-dependent values.

## Method
The authors used a leading-order pQCD-improved parton model with Gaussian intrinsic transverse momentum distributions for partons, incorporating nuclear thickness functions for both sharp sphere and Gaussian nuclear density profiles. They compared two models for nuclear broadening: a soft-collision model where the parton transverse momentum width increases with the number of effective nucleon-nucleon collisions, and a saturated model where the width increases from at most one semi-hard collision. The models were fitted to experimental data on neutral pion and direct photon production in proton-nucleus collisions.

## Key numbers
- For the sharp sphere nucleus in the soft-collision model: C(all)_pBe = 0.8 ± 0.2 GeV^2, C(all)_pTi = 0.4 ± 0.2 GeV^2, C(all)_pW = 0.3 ± 0.2 GeV^2
- For the sharp sphere nucleus in the saturated model: C(sat)_pBe = 0.7 GeV^2, C(sat)_pTi = 0.85 GeV^2, C(sat)_pW = 1.35 GeV^2, with a common value C(sat) = 1.1-1.2 GeV^2 describing all targets
- For the Gaussian nucleus in the soft-collision model: C(all)_pBe = 1.3 ± 0.2 GeV^2, C(all)_pTi = 0.72 ± 0.1 GeV^2, C(all)_pW = 0.65 ± 0.1 GeV^2
- For the Gaussian nucleus in the saturated model: C(sat)_pBe = 1.3 GeV^2, C(sat)_pTi = 1.15 GeV^2, C(sat)_pW = 1.55 GeV^2, with a common value C(sat) = 1.4 GeV^2
- The pp baseline average transverse momenta were parameterized as <k_T>_pp^gamma = 0.545 log(sqrt(s)) - 0.9 and <k_T>_pp^pi = 0.459 log(sqrt(s)) + 0.092 in the energy range sqrt(s) = 20-65 GeV

## Context
This work extends the study of the Cronin effect, originally observed by Cronin (1975) and recently investigated by the E706 collaboration, by proposing a new physical mechanism for nuclear enhancement in high-p_T particle production. The saturated model improves upon the standard soft-collision picture of Wang (1997-1998) by avoiding target-dependent parameters, though the extracted C(sat) still shows slight target dependence at the heaviest nucleus (tungsten), suggesting possible residual soft collision contributions. The results call for next-to-leading-order pQCD calculations to further analyze this effect and test the energy dependence of the saturation parameter across different nuclear targets.
