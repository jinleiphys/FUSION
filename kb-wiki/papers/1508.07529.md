# The No-Core Gamow Shell Model: Including the continuum in the NCSM

> We are witnessing an era of intense experimental efforts that will provide information about the properties of nuclei far from the line of stability, regarding resonant and scattering states as well as (weakly) bound states. This talk describes our formalism for including these necessary ingredients into the No-Core Shell Model by using the Gamow Shell Model approach. Applications of this new approach, known as the No-Core Gamow Shell Model, both to benchmark cases as well as to unstable nuclei will be given.

## Key claim

The No-Core Gamow Shell Model (NCGSM) extends the No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) into the complex energy plane using a Berggren basis, enabling a unified description of bound states, resonances, and scattering states for light nuclei; however, the method currently employs particle-hole truncations (e.g., 4p-4h or 3p-3h) and has not yet achieved full, untruncated calculations in the Berggren basis for systems beyond A=4–5.

## Method

The NCGSM diagonalizes a complex-symmetric, non-Hermitian Hamiltonian matrix in the Berggren basis, which includes bound states, resonant states, and scattering states along a complex contour. The nucleon-nucleon interaction is expressed in this basis using the potential separable expansion (PSE) in a harmonic-oscillator basis to avoid divergences. Diagonalization is performed with a complex extension of the Lanczos or Davidson algorithm, and the state of interest is identified via the overlap method, which maximizes overlap with a reference eigenvector from a smaller "pole approximation" space.

## Key numbers

- Benchmark calculations for \(^2\)H (1\(^+\)) at N\(_{\text{shell}}\) = 8 with JISP16 interaction and \(\hbar\omega\) = 20 MeV: NCGSM energy = -2.1419 MeV, matching MFDn to within \(\leq\) 0.1 keV.
- Benchmark for \(^4\)He (0\(^+\)) at N\(_{\text{shell}}\) = 4: NCGSM energy = -27.3684 MeV, MFDn = -27.3685 MeV, difference 0.1 keV.
- Benchmark for \(^6\)Li (1\(^+\)) at N\(_{\text{shell}}\) = 4: NCGSM = -24.9776 MeV, MFDn = -24.9778 MeV, difference 0.2 keV.
- For \(^5\)He (3/2\(^-\)), using a V\(_{\text{low}\,k}\) \(\Lambda\) = 1.9 fm\(^{-1}\) chiral N\(^3\)LO interaction with 4p-4h truncation: extracted ANC real part = 0.197 fm\(^{-1/2}\); width from ANC formula = 311 keV; width from complex diagonalization = 370 keV; one-neutron separation energy S\(_{1n}\) = -1.561 MeV; ratio \(\Gamma/(2S_{1n})\) = 11.8%.
- For \(^4\)H and \(^4\)Li with a Woods-Saxon basis and V\(_{\text{low}\,k}\) \(\Lambda\) = 2.0 fm\(^{-1}\) N\(^3\)LO interaction: \(^3\)H ground state energy = -7.92 MeV; \(^3\)He ground state energy = -7.12 MeV; calculations used up to 3p-3h truncation.

## Context

This work extends the Gamow Shell Model (GSM) formalism, previously applied with a frozen core, to a no-core framework (NCGSM) that treats all nucleons explicitly, building on the NCSM approach of Barrett (2013) and the MFDn code of Maris and Vary. The NCGSM benchmarks against MFDn for light nuclei up to \(^6\)Li and reproduces spectra for unbound \(^4\)H and \(^4\)Li, agreeing with experimental data from the TUNL compilation. It enables future calculations of asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) and widths for unstable nuclei, with the goal of quantifying truncation errors and eventually incorporating three-nucleon forces to connect with lattice-QCD predictions.
