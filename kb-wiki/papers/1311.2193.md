# Magnetic moments of odd-odd spherical nuclei

> Magnetic moments of more than one hundred odd-odd spherical nuclei in ground and excited states are calculated within the self-consistent TFFS based on the EDF method by Fayans {\it et al}. We limit ourselves to nuclei with a neutron and a proton particle (hole) added to the magic or semimagic core. A simple model of no interaction between the odd nucleons is used. In most the cases we analyzed, a good agreement with the experimental data is obtained. Several cases are considered where this simple model does not work and it is necessary to go beyond. The unknown values of magnetic moments of many unstable odd and odd-odd nuclei are predicted including sixty values for excited odd-odd nuclei.

## Key claim

Magnetic moments of more than one hundred odd-odd spherical nuclei in ground and excited states are calculated within the self-consistent Theory of Finite Fermi Systems (TFFS) based on the Energy Density Functional (EDF) method by Fayans et al., using a simple model that assumes no interaction between the odd neutron and odd proton; the authors note that in most cases this approximation yields good agreement with experiment, but several cases require going beyond this simple model.

## Method

The authors solve the TFFS equation for the effective field in the static external magnetic field using the representation of self-consistent single-particle wave functions derived from the Fayans EDF with the DF3-a parameter set. For odd-odd nuclei, the magnetic moment is calculated from the magnetic moments of the constituent odd-neutron and odd-proton nuclei using a formula that assumes no residual interaction between the two odd quasiparticles, with the odd-nucleon magnetic moments taken either from the TFFS calculation or from experimental data (phenomenological approach). The calculations use a spherical box of radius 24 fm and an upper cut of 50 MeV for the single-particle spectrum, with convergence checked by comparing to a 100 MeV cut.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic moments of more than one hundred odd-odd spherical nuclei were calculated, including sixty predicted values for excited odd-odd nuclei.
- For odd nearmagic nuclei, agreement with experiment is typically within 0.1–0.2 μ_N, except for ²⁰⁹Bi where the deviation reaches approximately 0.4 μ_N.
- For semimagic odd nuclei, agreement with experiment is also within 0.1–0.2 μ_N, except for ¹⁹¹Tl (deviation 0.26 μ_N) and ²⁰⁵Bi (deviation 0.31 μ_N).
- The spin-dependent Landau-Migdal parameters used are: C₀ = 300 MeV fm³, ζ_l = -0.05, ζ_s = 0.08, g = 0.1, g' = 1.1.
- For ²¹⁰Bi, both the phenomenological and theoretical values disagree with experiment by approximately 0.3 μ_N, indicating the need for configuration mixing.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous successful TFFS-based calculations of magnetic moments for odd magic and odd semimagic nuclei (Achakovskiy et al., 2011, 2012) to the more complex case of odd-odd nuclei, using the same self-consistent EDF approach with the DF3-a functional. The simple no-interaction model is supported by earlier work by Isakov et al. (2000, 2002) who found that low-lying states in ¹³⁴Sb are "rather pure." The results enable predictions for many unstable odd-odd nuclei and identify specific cases (such as ²¹⁰Bi) where configuration mixing beyond the simple model must be considered, motivating future work with RPA-like equations in the particle-particle or particle-hole channels.
