# Improved $K_{e3}$ radiative corrections sharpen the $K_{μ2}$--$K_{l3}$ discrepancy

> The measurements of $V_{us}$ in leptonic $(K_{μ2})$ and semileptonic $(K_{l3})$ kaon decays exhibit a $3σ$ disagreement, which could originate either from physics beyond the Standard Model or some large unidentified Standard Model systematic effects. Clarifying this issue requires a careful examination of all existing Standard Model inputs. Making use of a newly-proposed computational framework and the most recent lattice QCD results, we perform a comprehensive re-analysis of the electroweak radiative corrections to the $K_{e3}$ decay rates that achieves an unprecedented level of precision of $10^{-4}$, which improves the current best results by almost an order of magnitude. No large systematic effects are found, which suggests that the electroweak radiative corrections should be removed from the ``list of culprits'' responsible for the $K_{μ2}$--$K_{l3}$ discrepancy.

## Key claim

The electroweak radiative corrections to $K_{e3}$ decays, when recalculated using a new hybrid framework and lattice QCD inputs, achieve a precision of $10^{-4}$ and do not contain large systematic effects; therefore, these corrections are unlikely to be the Standard Model source of the $3\sigma$ discrepancy between $V_{us}$ extracted from leptonic ($K_{\mu2}$) and semileptonic ($K_{l3}$) kaon decays.

## Method

The authors performed a comprehensive re-analysis of the $\mathcal{O}(G_F\alpha)$ electroweak radiative corrections to $K_{e3}$ decay rates by hybridizing Sirlin's classical approach with chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) and incorporating recent lattice QCD results for the axial $\gamma W$-box diagram in the flavor SU(3) limit. They separated the virtual corrections into analytic pieces, Born contributions (including convection terms and resonance contributions from resonance chiral theory), and the axial $\gamma W$-box diagram, then combined these with real-photon emission contributions to compute the fractional correction $\delta_{K_{e3}}$ to the tree-level decay rate.

## Key numbers

- The new calculation achieves an unprecedented precision of $10^{-4}$ for the electroweak radiative corrections to $K_{e3}$ decays, improving the current best results by almost an order of magnitude.
- The $V_{us}$ value from $K_{\mu2}/\pi_{\mu2}$ plus superallowed beta decays is $|V_{us}| = 0.2252(5)$ (PDG 2020).
- The $V_{us}$ value from $K_{l3}$ decays is $|V_{us}| = 0.2231(4)_\mathrm{exp+RC}(6)_\mathrm{lat}$ (PDG 2020), showing a $\sim 1\%$ difference in central values and a $\sim 3\sigma$ disagreement with the $K_{\mu2}$ result.
- The tree-level decay rate normalization uses the Fermi constant $G_F = 1.1663787(6) \times 10^{-5}$ GeV$^{-2}$ from muon decay.
- The resonance contribution to $\delta_{K_{e3}}$ through the integrals in $\left(\delta M_2+\delta M_{\gamma W}^a\right)_\mathrm{int}$ and $\delta M_{\gamma W}^{b,V}$ is smaller than $10^{-4}$; a conservative uncertainty of $2\times 10^{-4}$ is assigned for neglected inelastic terms.

## Context

This work extends the ChPT-based calculations of Cirigliano et al. (2001, 2004, 2008), which estimated theory uncertainties at the $10^{-3}$ level from unknown low-energy constants and neglected $\mathcal{O}(e^2p^4)$ contributions, by introducing a new hybrid framework (Seng et al., 2019) and lattice QCD results for the axial $\gamma W$-box diagram (Ma et al., 2021). The improved precision enables a sharper test of the $K_{\mu2}$–$K_{l3}$ discrepancy; if the total uncertainty in both $V_{us}$ extractions is reduced to $4\times 10^{-4}$ or below with unchanged central values, the discrepancy would reach $5\sigma$, sufficient to claim observation of physics beyond the Standard Model.
