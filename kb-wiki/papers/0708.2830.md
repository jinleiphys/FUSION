# Symmetry Energy as a Function of Density and Mass

> Energy in nuclear matter is, in practice, completely characterized at different densities and asymmetries, when the density dependencies of symmetry energy and of energy of symmetric matter are specified. The density dependence of the symmetry energy at subnormal densities produces mass dependence of nuclear symmetry coefficient and, thus, can be constrained by that latter dependence. We deduce values of the mass dependent symmetry coefficients, by using excitation energies to isobaric analog states. The coefficient systematic, for intermediate and high masses, is well described in terms of the symmetry coefficient values of a_a^V=(31.5-33.5) MeV for the volume coefficient and a_a^S=(9-12) MeV for the surface coefficient. These two further correspond to the parameter values describing density dependence of symmetry energy, of L~95 MeV and K_{sym}~25 MeV.

## Key claim

The density dependence of the symmetry energy at subnormal densities can be constrained by the mass dependence of the nuclear symmetry coefficient, which the authors deduce from excitation energies to isobaric analog states, yielding a nearly linear symmetry energy at densities below normal nuclear density.

## Method

The authors used tabulated excitation energies to isobaric analog states (IAS) combined with microscopic corrections from Koura et al. (2005) to extract mass-dependent symmetry coefficients for individual nuclear masses. They then performed Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations for half-infinite matter and spherical nuclei, combined with Hohenberg-Kohn functional theory, to correlate the volume and surface symmetry coefficients with the density dependence parameters L and K_sym.

## Key numbers

- Volume symmetry coefficient: a_a^V = (31.5–33.5) MeV
- Surface symmetry coefficient: a_a^S = (9–12) MeV
- Slope parameter of symmetry energy: L ~ 95 MeV
- Curvature parameter of symmetry energy: K_sym ~ 25 MeV
- Deduced symmetry coefficient values range from a_a ~ 9 MeV for light nuclei (A < 10) to a_a ~ 22.5 MeV for heavy nuclei (A > 200)
- A fit to IAS data for A > 20 yields a_a^V = 32.9 MeV and a_a^S = 11.3 MeV
- Surface coefficient a_S ~ 19 MeV varies little between different Skyrme interactions, while surface symmetry coefficients a_a^S vary widely from 9 to 75 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts by Myers (1969) and Danielewicz (2003, 2004) to connect the density dependence of symmetry energy in nuclear matter to the mass dependence of the symmetry coefficient in finite nuclei. The authors improve upon previous Thomas-Fermi calculations by Danielewicz (2003, 2004), which they note may be less reliable because the model yields an evolution of density profile in the surface region not fully supported by electron scattering data. The results enable further constraints on the symmetry energy at subnormal densities through studies of neutron skin sizes and proton density distributions, which are relevant for calculations of neutron star structure as discussed by Lattimer (2006).
