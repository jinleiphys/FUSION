# How the HBT-Puzzle at RHIC might dissipate

> I compute the first-order corrections to two-particle Bose-Einstein correlations due to deviations from equilibrium. Based on that result I argue that for nearly perfect fluids, the HBT radii ``freeze out'' much later than the single-inclusive distribution. Therefore, to prevent a big longitudinal homogeneity length, the QGP presumably should hadronize not into a nearly perfect fluid of hadrons but rather into a very dissipative one. This could be achieved by a hadronization phase transition away from equilibrium.

## Key claim

The first-order corrections to two-particle Bose-Einstein correlations from deviations near equilibrium show that for nearly perfect fluids, the HBT radii "freeze out" much later than the single-inclusive distribution; therefore, to obtain a small longitudinal homogeneity length, the QGP should hadronize into a very dissipative hadron fluid rather than a nearly perfect one, possibly via a non-equilibrium phase transition.

## Method

The author computes first-order non-equilibrium corrections to the single-inclusive pion distribution and the two-pion correlation function using a schematic model with one-dimensional (longitudinal) boost-invariant expansion, focusing on the longitudinal radius R_parallel. The Boltzmann equation is solved to first order in gradients using a relaxation time approximation, with the distribution function expanded as f = f_eq + delta_f, where delta_f is proportional to the relaxation time tau_c times gradients of the temperature and flow fields. The saddle-point approximation is applied for large m_t/T to evaluate integrals over flow rapidity, and the longitudinal homogeneity length is extracted from the curvature of the correlation function at zero relative rapidity.

## Key numbers

- For ideal isentropic expansion (ultrarelativistic particles, m << T), the temperature scales as T ~ 1/tau^gamma with gamma = 1/3; for isoergic expansion near freeze-out, gamma = 1/4.
- The longitudinal homogeneity length is given by R_parallel = tau * sqrt(T/m_t) * sqrt(1 + (tau_c/tau) * (1 - gamma - zeta_T + 2*Delta_T^2) / (2*tau^2 * T^2)), where zeta_T and Delta_T^2 are curvature and fluctuation terms of the temperature field in rapidity.
- For nuclear collisions, typical values are tau ~ 10 fm and T ~ 100 MeV, giving tau*T ~ 5.
- The confinement temperature is T_c = 180 +/- 20 MeV.
- The first-order correction to the correlation function at small relative rapidity alpha is of order tau_c/(tau^3 * T^2), which can only become large when tau_c/tau ~ tau^2 * T^2.

## Context

This work extends the expression for the longitudinal homogeneity length from Makhlin and Sinyukov (1987), which applied to perfect local equilibrium, by adding first-order non-equilibrium corrections. It addresses the "HBT puzzle" observed in data from STAR and PHENIX at RHIC and from NA49, WA98, and NA44 at the CERN-SPS, where the duration of particle emission and longitudinal homogeneity length appear too short compared to ideal hydrodynamics predictions (Rischke and Gyulassy, 1996; Zschiesche et al., 2001). The analysis supports findings from kinetic theory solutions (Soff, Bass, and Dumitru, 2000) that HBT correlation functions are more sensitive to late dissipative stages than single-inclusive distributions, and suggests that parton cascade models (Lin et al.; Molnar et al.) or spinodal decomposition for a first-order transition may produce the needed cool, viscous hadron fluid.
