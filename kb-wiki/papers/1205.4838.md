# Scalar mesons moving in a finite volume and the role of partial wave mixing

> Phase shifts and resonance parameters can be obtained from finite-volume lattice spectra for interacting pairs of particles, moving with nonzero total momentum. We present a simple derivation of the method that is subsequently applied to obtain the pi pi and pi K phase shifts in the sectors with total isospin I=0 and I=1/2, respectively. Considering different total momenta, one obtains extra data points for a given volume that allow for a very efficient extraction of the resonance parameters in the infinite-volume limit. Corrections due to the mixing of partial waves are provided. We expect that our results will help to optimize the strategies in lattice simulations, which aim at an accurate determination of the scattering and resonance properties.

## Key claim

The paper presents a simple derivation of the method for extracting phase shifts and resonance parameters from finite-volume lattice spectra for interacting pairs of particles moving with nonzero total momentum, and demonstrates that using different total momenta provides extra data points for a given volume that allow for efficient extraction of resonance parameters in the infinite-volume limit, with the caveat that corrections due to partial wave mixing must be included.

## Method

The authors use the chiral unitary approach (UChPT) and the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM) to generate synthetic lattice data, which are then analyzed to extract phase shifts and resonance properties. They replace the continuous loop function G (the two-meson propagator) with its finite-volume counterpart by summing over discrete momenta allowed in a cubic box of side length L with periodic boundary conditions, applying a Lorentz boost transformation to handle moving frames. The finite-volume energy levels are obtained from the poles of the scattering matrix in the finite volume, where the determinant condition det(1 - V * G_tilde) = 0 is solved, with partial wave mixing included through the decomposition of the finite-volume loop function into spherical harmonics.

## Key numbers

- For the pi pi system in the I=0 sector (sigma/f0(600) region), synthetic lattice data are generated using box sizes with L = 2 fm and L = 3 fm, and total momenta P = (2pi/L)(0,0,0), (0,0,1), (1,1,0), and (1,1,1).
- For the pi K system in the I=1/2 sector (kappa(800) region), synthetic lattice data are generated using box sizes with L = 2 fm and L = 3 fm, and total momenta P = (2pi/L)(0,0,0), (0,0,1), (1,1,0), and (1,1,1).
- The mixing of S- and D-waves is studied for the sigma(600) case, and the mixing of S- and P-waves is studied for the kappa(800) case.
- The finite-volume corrections from the (q/q*on) factor in the partial wave mixing are shown to be very small for realistic box sizes, suppressed as 1/L^4.

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume formalism of Luscher (1986, 1990) and the earlier work of Doring et al. (2011) from the zero-momentum frame to moving frames, following and systematizing the approaches of Rummukainen and Kim (1995), Kim et al. (2005), and others. The method enables lattice QCD practitioners to extract phase shifts and resonance parameters for scalar mesons like the sigma(600) and kappa(800) more efficiently by using multiple total momenta, which provides additional energy levels at a given box volume and helps optimize lattice simulation strategies.
