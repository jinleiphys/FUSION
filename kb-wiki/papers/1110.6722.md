# Proton-antiproton annihilation into massive leptons

> We extend previous calculations of polarization observables for the annihilation reaction $\bar p +p\to \ell^{-}+\ell^{+}$ to the case of heavy leptons, such as the $τ$-lepton. We consider the case when the beam and/or the target are polarized, as well as the polarization of the outgoing leptons. We give the dependence of the unpolarized cross section, angular asymmetry, and various polarization observables on the relevant kinematical variables in the center of mass and in the laboratory system, with particular attention to the effect of the mass induced terms.

## Key claim

The paper extends previous calculations of polarization observables for the annihilation reaction \(\bar p + p \to \ell^- + \ell^+\) from the massless electron case to the case of heavy leptons (\(\mu\) and \(\tau\)), with the key qualifier that the lepton mass can no longer be neglected for \(\tau\) leptons (whose mass exceeds the proton mass), introducing new mass-dependent terms in the expressions for polarized and unpolarized observables.

## Method

The authors derive general, model-independent expressions for the differential cross section, angular asymmetry, and single, double, and triple spin polarization observables for the reaction \(\bar p + p \to \ell^- + \ell^+\) in the one-photon exchange (Born) approximation, using the formalism of leptonic and hadronic tensors expressed in terms of the complex proton electromagnetic form factors \(G_E(q^2)\) and \(G_M(q^2)\). The calculations are performed both in the center-of-mass system (CMS) and in the laboratory system (Lab), with explicit inclusion of the lepton mass \(m_\ell\) in all expressions, and numerical results are presented for \(\tau\) leptons under specific assumptions for the form factors.

## Key numbers

- The unpolarized differential cross section in CMS: \(\frac{d\sigma^C_0}{d\Omega} = \frac{\alpha^2}{4q^2} \frac{\beta_\ell}{\beta_p} \left[ \frac{|G_E|^2}{\eta_p} (1 - \beta_\ell^2 \cos^2\theta) + |G_M|^2 (2 - \beta_\ell^2 \sin^2\theta) \right]\), where \(\beta_\ell^2 = 1 - 4m_\ell^2/q^2\) and \(\beta_p^2 = 1 - 4M^2/q^2\).
- The total cross section: \(\sigma = \frac{\pi\alpha^2}{3q^2} \frac{\beta_\ell}{\beta_p} \left(2 + \frac{1}{\eta_\ell}\right) \left[ \frac{|G_E|^2}{\eta_p} + 2|G_M|^2 \right]\), with \(\eta_\ell = q^2/(4m_\ell^2)\).
- The angular asymmetry: \(\mathcal{A} = \beta_\ell^2 \frac{\eta_p |G_M|^2 - |G_E|^2}{\eta_p |G_M|^2 (2 - \beta_\ell^2) + |G_E|^2}\).
- The single spin asymmetry (analyzing power) in CMS: \(A_y^C = \frac{\beta_\ell^2 \sin 2\theta}{\sqrt{\eta_p} \mathcal{D}^C} \text{Im} G_M G_E^*\), where \(\mathcal{D}^C\) is the unpolarized differential cross section factor.
- Polarization transfer coefficients proportional to lepton mass: \(T_{ny}^C = 2 \frac{m_\ell}{M} \frac{\text{Re} G_E G_M^*}{\eta_p \mathcal{D}^C}\), \(T_{tx}^C = 2 \frac{m_\ell}{M} \frac{\cos\theta}{\eta_p \mathcal{D}^C} \text{Re} G_E G_M^*\), \(T_{tz}^C = -2 \frac{m_\ell}{M} \frac{\sin\theta}{\sqrt{\eta_p} \mathcal{D}^C} |G_M|^2\).
- For \(\tau\) leptons, the factor \(m_\ell/M \sim 2\) (since \(m_\tau \approx 1.78\) GeV and \(M \approx 0.938\) GeV), compared to \(m_e/M \sim 5 \times 10^{-4}\) for electrons.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Gakh et al. (2005) for polarization observables in \(\bar p + p \to e^- + e^+\) to heavy lepton production, and complements the calculations of Buttimore et al. (2006) for \(e^+ + e^- \to \bar p + p\) which included lepton mass but did not consider lepton polarization observables. The results are directly applicable to the PANDA experiment at FAIR, where antiproton beams will be used to study \(\bar p + p \to \tau^- + \tau^+\), enabling the extraction of the phase difference of proton electromagnetic form factors in the time-like region through measurement of single and double spin asymmetries, and providing a test of the point-like nature of the \(\tau\) lepton and the presence of two-photon exchange mechanisms.
