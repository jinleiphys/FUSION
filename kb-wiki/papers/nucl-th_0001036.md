# F-spin as a Partial Symmetry

> We use the empirical evidence that F-spin multiplets exist in nuclei for only selected states as an indication that F-spin can be regarded as a partial symmetry. We show that there is a class of non-F-scalar IBM-2 Hamiltonians with partial F-spin symmetry, which reproduce the known systematics of collective bands in nuclei. These Hamiltonians predict that the scissors states have good F-spin and form F-spin multiplets, which is supported by the existing data.

# F-spin as a Partial Symmetry

## Key claim
F-spin cannot be an exact symmetry of the IBM-2 Hamiltonian due to microscopic and empirical arguments, but a class of non-F-scalar Hamiltonians with partial F-spin symmetry can reproduce the observed systematics of collective bands, including the existence of F-spin multiplets for ground bands and scissors states while allowing F-spin mixing in other bands such as β and γ bands.

## Method
The authors construct an IBM-2 Hamiltonian (Eq. 2) composed of boson pair operators with F=1 (R operators) and F=0 (W operators), with parameters that can be chosen such that the Hamiltonian is not F-spin scalar but still has the deformed condensate intrinsic state (Eq. 1) as an exact eigenstate with good F=F_max. For a special choice of parameters (C^(2)=0, B^(1)=B^(3)=2B^(2)), the Hamiltonian H' (Eq. 5) additionally has the scissors band intrinsic state (Eq. 4) as an exact eigenstate with F=F_max-1, yielding solvable ground and scissors bands with good F-spin while other bands can be mixed.

## Key numbers
- F-spin admixtures in low-lying states are typically 2%-4%, found to be less than 10% in the majority of analyses
- For the Hamiltonian with partial F-spin symmetry, the ground band energies are E_g(L) = λ L(L+1) and scissors band energies are E_sc(L) = B N + λ L(L+1), giving equal moments of inertia for both bands
- The ratio B(M1;0⁺_g→1⁺_sc)/(C_{F,F_0})² is found to be fairly constant across F-spin partners, with the most noticeable discrepancy for ¹⁷²Yb (F=8) attributed to experimental deficiencies
- For the special case β=√2 with equal A^(2) coefficients, the Hamiltonian has both F-spin and SU(3) partial symmetries, yielding solvable bands with quantum numbers [(2N,0), F_max], [(2N-2,1), F_max-1], [(2N-4,2), F_max], and [(2N-4,2), F_max-1]

## Context
This work extends the concept of partial dynamical symmetry (PDS), previously applied to nuclei by Leviatan (1996) and to molecules by Ping (1997), to the F-spin degree of freedom in the IBM-2. It addresses the empirical observation by Brentano (1985) and Gupta (1989) that ground bands in rare earth nuclei form F-spin multiplets while β and γ bands do not. The results enable predictions for F-spin multiplets of scissors states, which are supported by data from Pietralla (1995) and Maser (1996), and agree with the recent comprehensive analysis of the scissors mode by Enders (1999) and Pietralla (1998) showing equal moments of inertia for ground and scissors bands.
