# Structure effects on fission yields

> The structure effects of the fission fragments on their yields are studied within the statical theory with the inputs, like, excitation energies and level density parameters for the fission fragments at a given temperature calculated using the temperature dependent relativistic mean field formalism (TRMF). For the comparison, the results are also obtained using the finite range droplet model. At temperatures $T =1-2$ MeV, the structural effects of the fission fragments influence their yields. It is also seen that at $T = $ 3 MeV, the fragments become spherical and the fragments distribution peaks at a close shell or near close shell nucleus.

## Key claim

The structural effects of fission fragments (their deformation and shell structure) influence the most favorable fission yields at temperatures T = 1-2 MeV, but at T = 3 MeV all fragments become spherical and the mass distribution peaks near closed-shell nuclei; this conclusion depends on using the temperature-dependent relativistic mean field (TRMF) formalism which includes thermal evolution of pairing gaps and deformation self-consistently, unlike the finite range droplet model (FRDM) which ignores these effects.

## Method

The authors applied the statistical theory of fission to calculate relative yields for binary fragmentation of 242Pu, using excitation energies and level density parameters computed from the temperature-dependent relativistic mean field (TRMF) formalism with the NL3 parameter set. For comparison, they also performed calculations using the finite range droplet model (FRDM) with single-particle energies from the Reference Input Parameter Library (RIPL-3). The quadrupole deformation parameter β2 was computed for each fragment at temperatures T = 1, 2, and 3 MeV, and the relative yield was obtained as the ratio of the folded level density for a given fragmentation to the sum over all possible fragmentations.

## Key numbers

- At T = 1 MeV, the most favorable fragmentation in both TRMF and FRDM is 91Br + 151Pr, with relative yields of 0.559 (TRMF) and 0.244 (FRDM).
- At T = 2 MeV, the most favorable fragmentation in TRMF remains 91Br + 151Pr (relative yield 0.336), while in FRDM it shifts to 110Mo + 132Te (relative yield 0.140).
- At T = 3 MeV, the most favorable fragmentation in TRMF is 109Mo + 133Te (relative yield 0.329), and in FRDM it is 110Mo + 132Te (relative yield 0.202).
- The quadrupole deformation parameter β2 shows that at T = 1 MeV fragments are prolate/oblate or spherical similar to ground state nuclei; at T = 2 MeV fragments with mass number A ≤ 150 become almost spherical; at T = 3 MeV all nuclei become perfectly spherical.

## Context

This work extends the statistical theory of fission (Fong 1956) by incorporating self-consistent temperature-dependent deformations and pairing gaps via the TRMF formalism, which were ignored in prior FRDM-based calculations (Moller 1995, MBS 2014). The results show that deformation effects on fission yields are significant at T = 1-2 MeV but vanish at T = 3 MeV, and they agree with recent experimental observations (Cha 2015). This enables future studies of fission fragment distributions that account for thermal shape transitions and shell effects in a fully self-consistent framework.
