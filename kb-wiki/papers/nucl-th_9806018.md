# Thermonuclear Reaction Rate of 23Mg(p,gamma)24$Al

> Updated stellar rates for the reaction 23Mg(p,gamma)24Al are calculated by using all available experimental information on 24Al excitation energies. Proton and gamma-ray partial widths for astrophysically important resonances are derived from shell model calculations. Correspondences of experimentally observed 24Al levels with shell model states are based on application of the isobaric multiplet mass equation. Our new rates suggest that the 23Mg(p,gamma)24Al reaction influences the nucleosynthesis in the mass A>20 region during thermonuclear runaways on massive white dwarfs.

## Key claim

The updated stellar reaction rates for 23Mg(p,gamma)24Al suggest that this reaction influences nucleosynthesis in the mass A>20 region during thermonuclear runaways on massive white dwarfs, but only for white dwarfs of masses at least 1.35 solar masses; for lower-mass white dwarfs the proton capture reaction is slower than the competing beta-decay and is of minor importance.

## Method

The authors compiled all available experimental excitation energies of 24Al levels from charge-exchange reaction studies, using the evaluated values of Endt (1998). They identified correspondences between observed 24Al levels and shell model states by applying the isobaric multiplet mass equation (IMME) using 24Na and 24Mg analog states. Proton partial widths were derived from shell model spectroscopic factors calculated with the OXBASH code using the Ormand-Brown isospin-nonconserving interaction, combined with single-particle widths from a folding potential. Gamma-ray partial widths were calculated from shell model reduced transition probabilities for M1 and E2 transitions. The total stellar reaction rate was computed as the sum of resonant contributions from four resonances and a nonresonant direct capture contribution.

## Key numbers

- Resonance energies (center-of-mass): 478 keV, 663 keV, 939 keV, and 1029 keV, corresponding to 24Al excitation energies of 2349, 2534, 2810, and 2900 keV respectively
- Resonance strengths (omega-gamma): 25 meV for the 478 keV resonance, 58 meV for the 663 keV resonance, 52 meV for the 939 keV resonance, and 12 meV for the 1029 keV resonance
- Direct capture S-factor parametrization: S(E) = 22.5 - 1.1e-2 E + 6.9e-6 E^2 keV barn for bombarding energies below 1 MeV
- At temperatures T9=0.2-0.5 (important for nova nucleosynthesis), the present reaction rates deviate up to a factor of 3 from Kubono et al. (1995) and up to a factor of 2 from Wiescher et al. (1986)
- At temperatures above T9=1, the present reaction rates are smaller than both previous results by about 70%
- The 478 keV resonance dominates the reaction rate in the range T9=0.2-1.0; the 663 keV resonance is important only above T9=1; the 939 and 1029 keV resonances are negligible over the whole temperature range
- The direct capture process determines the stellar rates at low temperatures T9 < 0.2

## Context

This work updates previous reaction rate estimates for 23Mg(p,gamma)24Al by Wallace and Woosley (1981), who considered only a single resonance, and by Wiescher et al. (1986), who considered three resonances plus direct capture. It also addresses discrepancies with the most recent estimate by Kubono et al. (1995), whose excitation energies differ from the present adopted values by about 20 keV on average, and whose spin-parity assignments for the 2810 and 2900 keV states are contradicted by the IMME-based analysis. The new rates enable more accurate modeling of nucleosynthesis in the mass A>20 region during thermonuclear runaways on massive white dwarfs, particularly for ONeMg novae; the authors state that large-scale stellar network calculations are underway to investigate the quantitative implications.
