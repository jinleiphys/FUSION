# Hadrons and Nuclei

> This document is one of a series of whitepapers from the USQCD collaboration. Here, we discuss opportunities for lattice QCD calculations related to the structure and spectroscopy of hadrons and nuclei. An overview of recent lattice calculations of the structure of the proton and other hadrons is presented along with prospects for future extensions. Progress and prospects of hadronic spectroscopy and the study of resonances in the light, strange and heavy quark sectors is summarized. Finally, recent advances in the study of light nuclei from lattice QCD are addressed, and the scope of future investigations that are currently envisioned is outlined.

## Key claim

This USQCD whitepaper argues that lattice QCD has matured to the point where it can provide first-principles calculations of hadron structure, spectroscopy, and nuclear interactions, but many of the most ambitious goals, such as calculations at physical quark masses, inclusion of three-body decays, and extraction of parton distribution functions at small Bjorken-x, remain future targets requiring continued theoretical development and exascale computing resources.

## Method

The paper surveys recent lattice QCD calculations of hadron structure (nucleon form factors, parton distribution functions), hadron spectroscopy (elastic and coupled-channel scattering amplitudes for resonances such as the ρ, σ, f0, f2, and exotic mesons), and nuclear interactions (finite-volume energy levels for few-nucleon systems up to A=4). It describes the use of variational methods, distillation, anisotropic lattices, momentum smearing, and finite-volume formalisms (Lüscher method and its extensions) to extract scattering amplitudes and resonance properties from Euclidean correlation functions computed via Monte Carlo integration on discretized space-time lattices.

## Key numbers

- Statistical precision on the order of a few percent is achievable for nucleon charge radius calculations at physical quark mass and several lattice spacings
- Momentum smearing techniques improve signal for boosted nucleon correlators by a factor of at least 10
- Calculations of the spectrum of light nuclei with atomic number A ≤ 4 at quark masses close to the physical limit are achievable with O(10^5) quark propagator sources
- The σ meson evolves from a broad resonance at light quark masses (mπ ~ 236 MeV) into a stable bound-state below ππ threshold at heavier masses (mπ ~ 391 MeV)
- Coupled-channel calculations of ππ, KK̅, ηη scattering at mπ ~ 400 MeV revealed a scalar resonance near KK̅ threshold with roughly equal coupling strength to ππ and KK̅, and two tensor resonances resembling f2(1270) and f2'(1525)
- First calculations of γ*π → ππ amplitudes at mπ ~ 400 MeV extracted the transition form factor of the unstable ρ resonance

## Context

This whitepaper extends the program of lattice QCD calculations described in earlier USQCD documents (Bazavov et al. 2018, Brower et al. 2018, Davoudi et al. 2018, Detmold et al. 2018, Joo et al. 2018, Kronfeld et al. 2018, Lehner et al. 2018) and responds to the 2015 NSAC Long Range Plan for Nuclear Physics. It builds on prior lattice calculations of the excited hadron spectrum (Dudek et al. 2010, 2013; Liu et al. 2012) that used simplified single-hadron operator bases, and on elastic scattering calculations of the ρ, σ, K*, and Δ resonances (Aoki et al. 2007; Feng et al. 2010; Lang et al. 2011; Dudek et al. 2012; Bali et al. 2015; Bulava et al. 2016; Briceno et al. 2016). The paper enables future calculations of three-body decays, photoproduction of exotic mesons for the GlueX experiment, and matrix elements in light nuclei for searches of physics beyond the Standard Model.
