# Exploring experimental conditions to reduce uncertainties in the optical potential

> Background: Uncertainty quantification for nuclear theories has gained a more prominent role in the field, with more and more groups attempting to understand the uncertainties on their calculations. However, recent studies have shown that the uncertainties on the optical potentials are too large for the theory to be useful. Purpose: The purpose of this work is to explore possible experimental conditions that may reduced the uncertainties on elastic scattering and single-nucleon transfer cross sections that come from the fitting of the optical model parameters to experimental data. Method: Using Bayesian methods, we explore the effect of the uncertainties of optical model parameters on the angular grid of the differential cross section, including cross section data at nearby energies, and changes in the experimental error bars. We also study the effect on the resulting uncertainty when other observables are included in the fitting procedure, particularly the total (reaction) cross sections. Results: We study proton and neutron elastic scattering on 48Ca and 208Pb. We explore the parameter space with Markov- Chain Monte Carlo, produce posterior distributions for the optical model parameters, and construct the corresponding 95% confidence intervals on the elastic-scattering cross sections. We also propagate the uncertainties on the optical potentials to the 48Ca(d,p)49Ca(g.s.) and 208Pb(d,p)209Pb(g.s.) cross sections. Conclusions: We find little sensitivity to the angular grid and an improvement of up to a factor of 2 on the uncertainties by including data at a nearby energy. Although reducing the error bars on the data does reduce the uncertainty, the gain is often considerably smaller than one would naively expect.

## Key claim

Including elastic scattering data at a nearby beam energy can reduce parametric uncertainties in optical-model predictions by up to a factor of 2, but the improvement depends strongly on the specific target and energy regime; reducing experimental error bars yields a gain that is often considerably smaller than one would naively expect, and the angular grid density has little effect on the resulting uncertainties.

## Method

The authors used Bayesian inference with Markov-Chain Monte Carlo sampling to fit optical-model parameters (9 Woods-Saxon parameters, initialized from the Beccatti-Greenlees global parameterization) to mock elastic-scattering angular distributions generated from the Koning-Delaroche global optical potential. They then propagated the posterior parameter distributions to (d,p) transfer cross sections calculated within the Adiabatic Wave Approximation (ADWA) using the reaction codes fresco and nlat. Four experimental conditions were tested: varying the angular coverage (full 5-160 degrees, forward-only up to 100 degrees, and halved data points), including data at a nearby energy (either simultaneously or sequentially), changing the experimental error bars (5%, 10%, 20%), and adding total reaction cross section data to the fit.

## Key numbers

- For neutron elastic scattering on 48Ca at 12 MeV, including a second data set at 14 MeV simultaneously reduced the uncertainty interval at forward angles for the transfer cross section from 57% to 41%.
- For 208Pb, including a nearby energy (32 MeV for the 30 MeV case) produced a factor of 2 reduction in the uncertainty interval at forward angles for the transfer cross section.
- Reducing the experimental error bar from 10% to 5% on the elastic scattering data gave uncertainty ratios (epsilon_10/epsilon_5) ranging from 1.52 (48Ca(d,p) at 21 MeV) to 1.94 (48Ca(n,n) at 12 MeV).
- Increasing the error bar from 10% to 20% gave uncertainty ratios (epsilon_20/epsilon_10) ranging from 1.39 (208Pb(p,p) at 30 MeV) to 1.99 (208Pb(p,p) at 61 MeV).
- The study considered proton and neutron elastic scattering on 48Ca at 12 MeV and 21 MeV, and on 208Pb at 30 MeV and 61 MeV, with corresponding (d,p) transfer reactions at deuteron energies of 21 MeV and 61 MeV respectively.

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian uncertainty quantification studies of Lovell (2018) and King (2019), which found that parametric uncertainties on transfer cross sections from optical-potential fitting ranged from 40% to over 100%, rendering such probes less useful for extracting structure or astrophysical information. The present study systematically explores experimental design strategies to reduce those uncertainties, complementing the approach of Atkinson (2018) who found success including total reaction cross sections in optical-potential fitting. The methods developed are intended to be applicable across the nuclear chart wherever the optical potential concept holds, and could be coupled with other optical potential frameworks such as the dispersive optical model.
