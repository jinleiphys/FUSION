# Global Flow of Glasma in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> We discuss the energy flow of the classical gluon fields created in collisions of heavy nuclei at collider energies. We show how the Yang-Mills analoga of Faraday's Law and Gauss' Law predict the initial gluon flux tubes to expand or bend. The resulting transverse and longitudinal structure of the Poynting vector field has a rich phenomenology. Besides the well known radial and elliptic flow in transverse direction, classical quantum chromodynamics predicts a rapidity-odd transverse flow that tilts the fireball for non-central collisions, and it implies a characteristic flow pattern for collisions of non-symmetric systems $A+B$. The rapidity-odd transverse flow translates into a directed particle flow $v_1$ which has been observed at RHIC and LHC. The global flow fields in heavy ion collisions could be a powerful check for the validity of classical Yang-Mill dynamics in high energy collisions.

# Global Flow of Glasma in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim

Classical Yang-Mills dynamics in the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework predicts that the initial gluon energy flow after a heavy ion collision contains a rapidity-odd transverse component, arising from asymmetries between the two colliding nuclei, which translates into directed particle flow $v_1$ observed at RHIC and LHC; however, this rapidity-odd term does not break the boost-invariance intrinsic to the McLerran-Venugopalan model.

## Method

The authors compute the chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic fields $\vec{E}$ and $\vec{B}$ immediately after the collision of nuclei in the classical McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model, using a small proper time $\tau$ expansion. They then construct the energy-momentum tensor $T^{\mu\nu}$ of the gluon field and analyze the transverse Poynting vector $S^i = T^{0i}$, decomposing it into rapidity-even and rapidity-odd components. The expectation values of the flow fields are calculated by averaging over configurations of the nuclear color charge densities, assuming slowly varying average charge density $\mu(\vec r)$.

## Key numbers

- The initial energy density at $\tau=0$ is given by $\varepsilon_0(\vec r) = \frac{g^6}{32\pi^2}\frac{N_c}{N_c^2-1} \ln^2 \frac{Q^2}{m^2} \mu_1(\vec r) \mu_2(\vec r)$.
- The rapidity-even transverse flow is $S^i_+ = -\frac{\tau}{2}\cosh\eta \nabla^i \varepsilon_0$, mimicking hydrodynamic flow.
- The rapidity-odd transverse flow is $S^i_- = -\frac{\tau}{2}\sinh\eta \frac{g^6}{32\pi^2}\frac{N_c}{N_c^2-1} \ln^2 \frac{Q^2}{m^2} \left( \mu_2 \nabla^i \mu_1 - \mu_1\nabla^i \mu_2 \right)$.
- Typical values of the normalized flow $V^i = T^{0i}/\varepsilon_0$ at the surface for Au+Au collisions are $\sim 0.1$ at $\tau=0.1$ fm/$c$ at midrapidity.
- The glasma exists at a time $\tau_0 \sim 1/Q_s \sim 0.1 \ldots 0.2$ fm/$c$ after the collision.

## Context

This work extends previous analytic and numerical studies of pre-equilibrium transverse flow from color glass dynamics (Fries 2005, Vredevoogd 2008, Schenke 2012) by analyzing the full 3+1-dimensional structure of the initial gluon energy flow, including the novel rapidity-odd component. The rapidity-odd transverse flow provides a classical QCD explanation for the directed flow $v_1$ observed at RHIC (Abelev 2008, Adams 2005), which had previously been modeled in hydrodynamic simulations by initializing a tilted source (Csernai 2011) or in the fire streak model (Gosset 1988). The results enable systematic tests of classical Yang-Mills dynamics through flow measurements as a function of rapidity for different nuclear systems, including asymmetric collisions such as Au+Cu.
