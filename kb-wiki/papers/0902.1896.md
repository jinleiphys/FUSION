# Flow Effects on Jet quenching with Detailed Balance

> A new model potential in the presence of collective flow describing the interaction of the hard jet with scattering centers is derived based on the static color-screened Yukawa potential. The flow effect on jet quenching with detailed balance is investigated in pQCD. It turns out that the collective flow changes the emission current and the LPM destructive interference comparing to that in the static medium. Considering the collective flow with velocity v_z along the jet direction, the energy loss is (1 - v_z) times that in the static medium to the first order of opacity. The flow dependence of the energy loss will affect the suppression of high p_T hadron spectrum and anisotropy parameter v_2 in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

# Flow Effects on Jet Quenching with Detailed Balance

## Key claim

The paper derives a new model potential for jet-medium interactions in the presence of collective flow and finds that, to first order in opacity, the total energy loss (including both stimulated emission and thermal absorption) is modified by a factor of (1 - v_z) relative to the static medium case, where v_z is the flow velocity along the jet direction. This result is conditional on the flow velocity being small (|v| << 1) and applies to the first order of the opacity expansion.

## Method

The authors start from the static Gyulassy-Wang color-screened Yukawa potential and apply Lorentz transformations to derive a new four-vector potential that accounts for collective flow of the quark-gluon medium, producing both color-electric and color-magnetic fields with non-zero energy transfer q^0 = v·q. Using this potential within the opacity expansion framework of Gyulassy, Lévai and Vitev (GLV), they calculate the induced gluon radiation probability including both stimulated emission and thermal absorption (detailed balance) at zeroth and first order in opacity, then derive analytic expressions for the radiative and absorptive contributions to the energy loss.

## Key numbers

- The energy loss to first order in opacity scales as (1 - v_z) times the static medium result, with corrections of order O(|v|^2)
- For collective flow velocities |v| = 0.1-0.3 in the positive jet direction, jet energy loss decreases by 10-30%
- The emission current J²_eff is reduced by a factor (1 - v_z)² compared to the static medium
- The gluon formation factor contains (1 - v_z) in the denominator, making the formation time τ_f = (1 - v_z)/ω₁ shorter (longer) for flow in the positive (negative) jet direction
- The radiative energy loss asymptotic result: ΔE_rad^(1)/E = (1 - v_z)(α_s C_R μ² L²)/(4λ_g E)[ln(2E/μ²L) - 0.048] + O(|v|²)
- The absorptive energy loss asymptotic result: ΔE_abs^(1)/E = -(1 - v_z)(πα_s C_R/3)(LT²/λ_g E²)[ln(μ²L/T) - 1 + γ_E - 6ζ'(2)/π²] + O(|v|²)

## Context

This work extends the static-medium jet quenching formalism of Gyulassy and Wang (1994) and the GLV opacity expansion approach by incorporating collective flow effects that were previously modeled only through a momentum shift perpendicular to the jet direction by Armesto, Salgado, and Wiedemann. It also addresses inconsistencies in the transport coefficient q̂ approach of Baier, Mueller, and Schiff (2006) and Liu (2007), where the proportionality constant c in q̂ ≈ cε³/⁴ varied from c=2 to c>8 among different authors. The new potential enables future calculations of heavy quark energy loss and will affect predictions for high-p_T hadron suppression and the anisotropy parameter v₂ in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC.
