# Origin of Borromean systems

> The complex energies of the three-body resonances for one infinitely heavy particle and two non-interacting light particles are the sum of the two contributing two-body complex resonance energies. The bound state of a Borromean system originates from a resonance when the third interaction is introduced, a finite mass is allowed and proper angular momentum coupling is included. The relative importance of these contributions are investigated and the resulting structure of Borromean systems are traced back to the two-body continuum properties. The $0^+$ and $2^+$ states in $^{6}$He result from neutron-core p-states and the ground and first excited state of $^{11}$Li originate from neutron-core $s^2$ and $sp$-states.

# Origin of Borromean systems

## Key claim

Borromean bound states arise from three-body resonances when three specific effects are introduced: a finite core mass (reducing kinetic energy), favorable angular momentum coupling, and an attractive interaction between the two light particles. The relative importance of these contributions can be traced directly back to the properties of the two-body continuum states, but the schematic model of an infinitely heavy core with two non-interacting light particles provides the starting point where three-body resonance energies are simply the sum of the two contributing two-body complex resonance energies.

## Method

The authors use the Faddeev hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method combined with complex scaling to compute three-body resonances and bound states. They begin with a schematic system where one particle is infinitely heavy and the two light particles do not interact, then progressively relax these conditions by introducing a finite core mass, allowing angular momentum coupling, and switching on a realistic neutron-neutron interaction. The method is applied to the nuclear systems $^{6}$He (pure $p$-wave core-neutron interaction) and $^{11}$Li (mixed $s$- and $p$-wave core-neutron interactions, including the case of finite core spin).

## Key numbers

- For $^{6}$He: the two-body $p_{3/2}$-resonance in $^5$He has energy and width ($E_R$, $\Gamma_R$) = (0.74, 0.61) MeV; the schematic three-body resonance for $m_1=\infty$ and $V_{23}=0$ has (1.48, 1.22) MeV.
- For $^{6}$He with realistic masses ($m_1=4m$) and full neutron-neutron interaction: the $0^+$ state becomes bound at 2 MeV (Borromean state); the $2^+$ state becomes a resonance at ($E_R$, $\Gamma_R$) = (0.34, 0.01) MeV.
- For $^{11}$Li with zero core spin: the two-body virtual $s$-state is at -0.54 MeV and the $p$-resonance at (1.38, 2.77) MeV; with full neutron-neutron interaction the $0^+$ Borromean bound state appears at $E_R = -0.42$ MeV, and the $1^-$ resonance at (0.51, 0.29) MeV.
- For $^{11}$Li with finite core spin 3/2: the $^{10}$Li continuum states are chosen at -0.10 MeV ($2^-$), -1.14 MeV ($1^-$), 0.51 - $i$0.21 MeV ($1^+$), and 0.74 - $i$0.40 MeV ($2^+$); the full calculation yields a $3/2^-$ Borromean ground state at -0.30 MeV and a second $3/2^-$ resonance at (0.86, 1.16) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the schematic model of Garrido (2003) where three-body resonance energies for an infinitely heavy core with non-interacting light particles are shown to be the sum of two-body resonance energies. The paper provides a systematic framework to understand how Borromean systems like $^{6}$He and $^{11}$Li emerge from two-body continuum properties, building on the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method of Nielsen (2001) and Jensen (1997). The results enable prediction of three-body ground and excited states directly from known two-body virtual state and resonance energies, and the authors note consistency with the spectrum observed in $^{12}$Be by Iwasaki (2000) and Shimoura (2003).
