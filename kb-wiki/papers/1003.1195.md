# Lattice based equation of state and transverse momentum spectra of identified particles in ideal and viscous hydrodynamics

> Assuming that in Au+Au collisions, a baryon free fluid is produced, transverse momentum spectra of identified particles ($π$, $K$, $p$ and $ϕ$), in evolution of ideal and viscous fluid is studied. Hydrodynamic evolution is governed by a lattice based equation of state (EOS), where the confinement-deconfinement transition is a cross-over at $T_{co}$=196 MeV. Ideal or viscous fluid was initialised to reproduce $ϕ$ meson multiplicity in 0-5% Au+Au collisions. Ideal or minimally viscous ($η/s$=0.08) fluid evolution reasonably well explain the transverse momentum spectra of pion's, kaon's, and $ϕ$ meson's in central and mid-central Au+Au collisions. Description to the data is much poorer in viscous fluid evolution with $η/s \geq$0.12. The model however under estimate proton production by a factor $\sim$ 2.

## Key claim

The paper claims that transverse momentum spectra of pions, kaons, and phi mesons in central and mid-central Au+Au collisions are reasonably well explained by ideal or minimally viscous (η/s=0.08) hydrodynamic evolution using a lattice-based equation of state with a cross-over transition at T_co=196 MeV, but the model underestimates proton production by a factor of approximately 2, and the description becomes much poorer for viscous fluid evolution with η/s ≥ 0.12.

## Method

The authors solved Israel-Stewart's second-order dissipative hydrodynamic equations for a baryon-free fluid, assuming boost-invariance, using the AZHYDRO-KOLKATA code. The equation of state combined a parametric fit to lattice QCD entropy density results from Cheng et al. (2007) with a hadronic resonance gas of all resonances below 2.5 GeV, smoothly joined at the cross-over temperature T_co=196 MeV. The fluid was initialized at τ_i=0.6 fm with zero initial velocity, an energy density profile from a Glauber model (75% participant + 25% collision number weighting), and central energy density tuned to reproduce the phi meson multiplicity in 0-5% Au+Au collisions for each viscosity value (η/s=0, 0.08, 0.12, 0.16), with freeze-out at T_F=150 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Cross-over temperature for confinement-deconfinement transition: T_co=196 MeV
- Initial central energy density for ideal fluid (η/s=0) in b=0 Au+Au collisions: ε_i=35.5 ± 5.0 GeV/fm^3 at τ_i=0.6 fm
- Initial central energy density for viscous fluid with η/s=0.08: ε_i=29.1 ± 3.6 GeV/fm^3
- Initial central energy density for viscous fluid with η/s=0.16: ε_i=20.8 ± 2.7 GeV/fm^3
- Initial central temperature for ideal fluid: T_i=377.0 ± 13.7 MeV
- Initial central temperature for viscous fluid with η/s=0.08: T_i=359.1 ± 11.5 MeV
- Minimum χ^2/N for combined π+K+φ data in 0-40% centrality: 19.43 for η/s=0.08
- χ^2/N for combined π+K+φ data in 0-40% centrality for ideal fluid: 23.61
- χ^2/N for combined π+K+φ data in 0-40% centrality for η/s=0.12: 80.67
- χ^2/N for combined π+K+φ data in 0-40% centrality for η/s=0.16: 226.80
- Proton production underestimated by a factor of approximately 2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of phi meson production (Chaudhuri, 2009) to include pions, kaons, and protons, testing whether the lattice-based equation of state and viscosity estimate (η/s=0.07 ± 0.03 ± 0.14 from phi meson data) are consistent with the bulk of particle production. It contrasts with earlier hydrodynamic calculations using first-order phase transition equations of state, such as the EOS-Q used by the QGP3 collaboration, and with the work of Huovinen (2005) who used a thermal quasiparticle model for cross-over transitions. The results enable further study of the role of baryon chemical potential and the effect of unphysically heavy quark masses in lattice simulations on proton production, and suggest that including bulk viscosity could allow even lower shear viscosity values.
