# Temperature and Density Conditions for Alpha Clustering in Excited Self-Conjugate Nuclei

> Starting from experimental studies on alpha-clustering in excited self-conjugate nuclei (from $^{16}$O to $^{28}$Si), temperature and density conditions for such a clustering are determined. Measured temperatures have been found in the range of 5.5 - 6.0 MeV whereas density values of 0.3 - 0.4 times the saturation density are deduced, i.e., 0.046 to 0.062 $fm^{-3}$. Such a density domain is also predicted by constrained self-consistent mean field calculations. These results constitute a benchmark for alpha clustering from self-conjugate nuclei in relation to descriptions of stellar evolution and supernovae.

## Key claim

Alpha clustering in excited self-conjugate nuclei (from 16O to 28Si) occurs at temperatures of 5.5-6.0 MeV and densities of 0.3-0.4 times saturation density (0.046 to 0.062 fm-3), but the theoretical constrained mean-field calculations with which these results are compared are performed at zero temperature, so the comparison is only qualitative.

## Method

The experiment used the reaction 40Ca + 12C at 25 MeV per nucleon, with charged products detected by the CHIMERA 4π multi-detector. Events were selected to isolate projectile fragmentation with Ztot = 20 composed of one projectile fragment and alpha particles only, then further filtered to remove preequilibrium alpha particles (energies above 40 MeV) and sequential decays via unbound states. Temperature was extracted by fitting alpha-particle kinetic energy spectra with a thermal Maxwellian formula using a volume pre-exponential factor, and densities were derived from freeze-out volumes determined by simulations that imposed the fitted temperatures and matched the most probable kinetic energy.

## Key numbers

- Temperatures extracted from Maxwellian fits: 6.15 (0.03) MeV for 16O, 6.22 (0.05) MeV for 20Ne, 5.92 (0.07) MeV for 24Mg, 5.40 (0.12) MeV for 28Si
- Normalized densities ρ/ρ0: 0.37 (0.04) for 16O, 0.36 (0.04) for 20Ne, 0.34 (0.06) for 24Mg, 0.34 (0.11) for 28Si
- Absolute density range: 0.046 to 0.062 fm-3 (0.3-0.4 times saturation density)
- Freeze-out volumes: 2.7-3.0 V0, where V0 is the volume at saturation density
- Mean excitation energy per nucleon: constant around 3.3-3.5 MeV across all nuclei studied
- Excitation energy thresholds for total deexcitation into alpha particles: 20 to 60 MeV as Nα moves from 4 to 7

## Context

This work extends experimental studies of alpha clustering in excited self-conjugate nuclei (Borderie 2016, Borderie 2017, Borderie 2015) by providing quantitative temperature and density benchmarks. The results are compared with constrained self-consistent mean field calculations by Girod (2013) and Ebran (2014), which predict clustering densities around 0.17 ρ0 and 0.45 ρ0 respectively before center-of-mass corrections, and with the relativistic mean field model of Typel (2010) which predicts the Mott density for alpha dissolution at ∼ρ0/3 for T=6 MeV. These benchmarks enable improved descriptions of stellar evolution and supernova core-collapse, where alpha cluster formation in low-density warm nuclear matter is critical.
