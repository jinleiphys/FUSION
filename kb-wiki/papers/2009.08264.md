# Large-P_T inclusive photoproduction of J/psi in electron-proton collisions at HERA and the EIC

> We study the inclusive J/psi production at large transverse momenta at lepton-hadron colliders in the limit when the exchange photon is quasi real, also referred to as photoproduction. Our computation includes the leading-P_T leading-v next-to-leading alpha_s corrections. In particular, we consider the contribution from J/psi plus another charm quark, by employing for the first time in quarkonium photoproduction the variable-flavour-number scheme. We also include a QED-induced contribution via an off-shell photon which remained ignored in the literature and which we show to be the leading contribution at high P_T within the reach of the EIC. In turn, we use our computation of J/psi+charm to demonstrate its observability at the future EIC and the EIC sensitivity to probe the non-perturbative charm content of the proton at high x.

## Key claim

The Color Singlet Model (CSM) incorporating NLO leading-P_T contributions can describe J/psi photoproduction at HERA for P_T > 5 GeV, contrary to earlier claims; however, the agreement with data is contingent on properly subtracting the b-quark feed-down contribution, which the authors find to be significant around P_T = 10 GeV.

## Method

The authors computed inclusive J/psi photoproduction at large transverse momenta using the leading-P_T leading-v next-to-leading alpha_s corrections (NLOstar CSM), and for the first time in quarkonium photoproduction employed the variable-flavour-number scheme (VFNS) to include the J/psi+charm contribution. They also included a previously ignored QED-induced contribution via an off-shell photon. To estimate the b to J/psi feed-down, they performed a data-driven analysis tuning Pythia 8.2 to H1 b-quark cross-section data via a chi-squared minimization, yielding a tuning factor N_FD = 3.8 ± 0.5.

## Key numbers

- Tuning factor N_FD = 3.8 ± 0.5, obtained from chi-squared minimization to reproduce H1 b cross-section data with Pythia 8.2 using CT14NLO PDF set, m_b = 4.75 GeV, and mu_F = mu_R = m_T
- At the EIC, the QED contribution becomes about half of the prompt yield at P_T approximately 10 GeV for the sqrt(s_ep) = 45 GeV configuration, and at P_T approximately 15 GeV for the sqrt(s_ep) = 140 GeV configuration
- At sqrt(s_ep) = 45 GeV, a "BHPS" valence-like intrinsic charm hypothesis can produce an enhancement as large as 4-5 compared to a "no IC" hypothesis at P_T > 5 GeV
- The non-prompt J/psi fraction was measured by H1 as 14 ± 7% for 60 < W_gamma p < 240 GeV, 0.3 < z < 0.4, and P_T > 1 GeV
- The psi'/J/psi ratio was found by ZEUS to be compatible with a LO CS prediction, considered to be on the order of 20%

## Context

This work extends the NLOstar approximation previously used in hadroproduction to photoproduction at lepton-hadron colliders, and reassesses the latest H1 data from HERA. The authors demonstrate that the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) at sqrt(s_ep) = 45 GeV offers a unique opportunity to probe the non-perturbative charm content of the proton via J/psi+charm associated production, which is not possible at the higher sqrt(s_ep) = 140 GeV configuration. The study enables future extensions to other planned colliders such as the LHeC and FCC-eh, and motivates dedicated experimental studies for J/psi+jets and feed-down from heavier quarks at the EIC.
