# Quantum Electrodynamical Density-matrix Functional Theory and Group-theoretical Consideration of its Solution

> For studying the group theoretical classification of the solutions of the density functional theory in relativistic framework, we propose quantum electrodynamical density-matrix functional theory (QED-DMFT). QED-DMFT gives the energy as a functional of a local one-body $4\times4$ matrix $Q(x)\equiv -<ψ(x)\barψ(x)>$, where $ψ$ and $\barψ$ are 4-component Dirac field and its Dirac conjugate, respectively. We examine some characters of QED-DMFT. After these preparations, by using Q(x), we classify the solutions of QED-DMFT under O(3) rotation, time reversal and spatial inversion. The behavior of Q(x) under nonrelativistic and ultrarelativistic limits are also presented. Finally, we give plans for several extensions and applications of QED-DMFT.

# Quantum Electrodynamical Density-matrix Functional Theory and Group-theoretical Consideration of its Solution

## Key claim

The paper proposes quantum electrodynamical density-matrix functional theory (QED-DMFT) as a framework for group-theoretical classification of density functional theory solutions in a relativistic setting, using a local one-body 4x4 matrix Q(x) that contains more information than the 4-current jμ(x). The authors argue that the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem can be extended to this functional, but note that the particle number N is not conserved in QED (only total charge is conserved), and that the Hartree-like term includes a gauge parameter whose dependence must be compensated by the exchange-correlation functional to retain gauge invariance.

## Method

The authors construct QED-DMFT by defining the energy as a functional of the local one-body 4x4 density matrix Q(x) = -⟨ψ(x)ψ̄(x)⟩, expanded into the 16-dimensional complete set of γ-matrices (scalar, vector, tensor, axial-vector, pseudoscalar components). They derive the Dirac-Kohn-Sham equation with 16-component fictitious potentials from the Rayleigh-Ritz variation principle under charge conservation constraints. The group-theoretical classification is performed by examining the transformation properties of Q(x) under O(3) rotation, time reversal, and spatial inversion, and by analyzing the nonrelativistic and ultrarelativistic limits of the density matrix components.

## Key numbers

- The density matrix Q(x) is a 4x4 matrix expanded into 16 components using the complete set of γ-matrices: scalar (S), vector (V, 4 components), tensor (T, 6 components), axial-vector (A, 4 components), and pseudoscalar (P, 1 component).
- At the nonrelativistic limit (small component χ vanishes), Q^S and Q^V_0 coincide and give (1/4)φ†φ, while Q^T_(A) gives (1/4)φ†σφ and Q^A gives -(1/4)φ†σφ; Q^V, Q^T_(V), Q^A_0, and Q^P vanish.
- At the ultrarelativistic limit (φ and χ coincide), Q^V_0, Q^V, Q^A_0, and Q^A remain while all other components vanish.
- For an O(3)×P×T-invariant solution, only Q^S and Q^V_0 are nonzero (with Q^S* = Q^S, Q^V*_0 = Q^V_0).
- For an O(3)×T-invariant solution, Q^S, Q^V_0, and Q^A_0 are nonzero.
- For an O(3)-invariant solution, Q^S, Q^V_0, Q^A_0, and Q^P are nonzero.

## Context

This work extends the group-theoretical classification of nonrelativistic DFT solutions by Weiner and Trickey (1998) and the generalized spin orbital DFT work by Yamanaka et al. (2001) into the relativistic QED framework, building on the authors' previous construction of QED-SCF and QED-HF theory. The paper also builds on the relativistic Hohenberg-Kohn theorem introduced by Rajagopal and Callaway (1973) and subsequent RDFT developments by Eschrig et al. (1985) and Engel and Dreizler (1996). The proposed QED-DMFT is intended to enable the study of symmetry breaking phenomena in heavy elements under strong relativistic effects, particularly for systems where both many-body effects and QED effects (negative energy states) cannot be neglected, such as highly ionized heavy atoms or inner core electrons of heavy elements.
