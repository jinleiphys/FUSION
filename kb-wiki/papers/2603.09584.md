# Joint Bayesian analysis of soft and high-$p_\perp$ probes yields tighter constraints on QGP properties

> To extract bulk QGP properties, we perform a joint Bayesian calibration of bulk-medium parameters using low-$\pt$ bulk and high-$\pt$ tomography within a common medium evolution. Low-$\pt$ observables are computed with \textsc{TRENTo}+\textsc{VISHNU}; temperature profiles are passed to \textsc{DREENA-A} to predict light/heavy $R_{\mathrm{AA}}(\pt)$ and $v_2(\pt)$. Gaussian-process emulation enables Hamiltonian Monte Carlo sampling of the low-$\pt$-only and joint posteriors. The low-$\pt$-only case underpredicts high-$\pt$ anisotropy; the joint calibration matches both sectors and markedly tightens bulk-parameter constraints, demonstrating the added power of high-$\pt$ data.

# Joint Bayesian analysis of soft and high-$p_\perp$ probes yields tighter constraints on QGP properties

## Key claim
A joint Bayesian calibration combining low-$p_\perp$ bulk observables with high-$p_\perp$ light- and heavy-flavor $R_{\mathrm{AA}}(p_\perp)$ and $v_2(p_\perp)$ within a common medium evolution substantially narrows bulk-medium parameter posteriors compared to a low-$p_\perp$-only calibration, but this result is demonstrated within a restricted three-parameter space (norm, $\tau_0$, $\eta/s$) using an averaged-medium setup and a single representative dataset per observable.

## Method
The authors couple the soft-sector medium evolution model TRENTo+VISHNU (initial entropy density profiles and $(2+1)$-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics) to the dynamical energy-loss framework DREENA-A, which computes high-$p_\perp$ $R_{\mathrm{AA}}(p_\perp)$ and $v_2(p_\perp)$ for inclusive charged hadrons and $D^0$ mesons from the same temperature profiles. Principal-component decomposition is applied separately to low-$p_\perp$ and high-$p_\perp$ observable vectors (retaining PCs capturing at least 99% of variance), independent Gaussian-process emulators are trained for each retained PC coefficient, and Hamiltonian Monte Carlo sampling is used to obtain posterior distributions for the three bulk-medium parameters from both low-$p_\perp$-only and joint low-$p_\perp$+high-$p_\perp$ likelihoods.

## Key numbers
- Three-parameter space: normalization norm (60--360), starting time $\tau_0 \in [0.2, 1.3]$ fm, specific shear viscosity $\eta/s \in [0.02, 0.2]$
- Latin-hypercube design of approximately 200 points in the three-dimensional parameter space
- Two PCs retained for low-$p_\perp$ combined $\{dN/dy, \langle p_\perp \rangle\}$ set; one PC for the four centrality values of integrated $\langle v_2 \rangle$; three PCs retained for high-$p_\perp$ combined $\{1-R_{\mathrm{AA}}(p_\perp), v_2(p_\perp)\}$ vector
- Out-of-sample $R^2 > 0.99$ for all retained PCs from Gaussian-process emulator validation
- Four centrality classes analyzed: 10--20%, 20--30%, 30--40%, and 40--50% Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV

## Context
This work extends Bayesian parameter estimation approaches previously applied to soft-sector observables (Bernhard 2016, Bernhard 2019, Auvinen 2020, Nijs 2021, JETSCAPE 2021) by incorporating high-$p_\perp$ tomography observables within the same hydrodynamic background. It builds on exploratory Bayesian studies of hard probes (He 2018, JETSCAPE 2021, Ehlers 2024, Xie 2023, Fan 2024, Du 2025, Xue 2025) but takes the next step of simultaneously constraining bulk-medium evolution parameters with light- and heavy-flavor $R_{\mathrm{AA}}(p_\perp)$ and $v_2(p_\perp)$. The results demonstrate that high-$p_\perp$ data lift degeneracies present in soft-only analyses and enable tighter constraints on QGP properties, while also resolving the long-standing difficulty of simultaneously describing high-$p_\perp$ $R_{\mathrm{AA}}$ and azimuthal anisotropy within energy-loss-based approaches.
