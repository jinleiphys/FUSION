# Medium modification of strange hadronic resonances at SIS, RHIC and LHC energies

> The properties of strange pseudoscalar and vectors mesons as well as strange baryon resonances in dense matter are reviewed. Some open questions on the properties of strange hadrons in medium are addressed, such as the experimental signatures of in-medium effects coming from the hadronic phase on the final observables in heavy-ion collisions for the experimental conditions at SIS, RHIC and LHC energies.

# Medium modification of strange hadronic resonances at SIS, RHIC and LHC energies

## Key claim

The paper reviews how the in-medium properties of strange mesons and baryon resonances may affect observables in heavy-ion collisions, but concludes that at LHC and RHIC energies the baryon densities are too low for in-medium effects to play a significant role, while at lower energies (CBM/BMN/BESII) the longer reaction times and higher densities might make such effects relevant.

## Method

The properties of strange pseudoscalar mesons (K, anti-K) are described using self-energies and spectral functions obtained from s- and p-wave in-medium KN and anti-KN interactions within a chiral unitary approach, with unitarization in coupled channels and a cutoff regularization. For strange vector mesons (K*, anti-K*), the self-energies are computed using the hidden gauge formalism for anti-K* and a T-rho approximation for K*, incorporating modified decay channels and quasi-elastic interactions with nucleons. The in-medium amplitudes are then used to extract single-particle potentials for hyperons and to compute spectral functions, which are implemented in the PHSD transport model for comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The Lambda(1115) and Sigma(1195) hyperons show potentials of roughly -50 MeV and -40 MeV, respectively, at normal matter density rho_0 and zero temperature
- The Sigma*(1385) develops an attractive potential of about -10 MeV at rho_0 and zero temperature
- The KN scattering lengths obtained are a^{0,1/2}_{KN} = -0.16 fm and a^{1,1/2}_{KN} = -0.29 fm
- The K*N scattering lengths for different isospin-spin sectors include a^{0,1/2}_{K*N} = (0.2, 0.03) fm, a^{0,3/2}_{K*N} = (-0.08, 0.04) fm, a^{1,1/2}_{K*N} = (0.1, 0.0) fm, and a^{1,3/2}_{K*N} = (-0.31, 0.03) fm
- The anti-K* spectral function at rho_0 becomes five times broader than in free space
- PHSD calculations were performed for Au+Au at sqrt(s_{NN}) = 200 GeV (STAR/RHIC) and Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_{NN}) = 2.76 TeV (ALICE/LHC)

## Context

This review extends the chiral unitary approach developed by Tolos (2006, 2008) for strange mesons in dense matter and the hidden gauge formalism for vector mesons by Oset (2009) and Khemchandani (2011, 2014). It addresses open questions raised by experimental results from the KaoS, FOPI, HADES, STAR, NA49, and ALICE Collaborations regarding the role of the hadronic phase in heavy-ion collisions. The work enables future transport model studies at lower energies (CBM/FAIR, BMN/NICA, BESII/RHIC) where in-medium effects may become observable, while clarifying that at LHC and RHIC energies the main production channel is resonant annihilation of pi + K (anti-K) in the hadronic phase rather than in-medium modifications.
