# On description of the direct nucleon decay of giant resonances

> A semimicroscopical approach is formulated to describe the direct nucleon decay of various giant resonances in intermediate and heavy mass spherical nuclei. The approach consists in: (i) the exact continuum-RPA calculations for amplitudes of the reactions accompanied by excitation and the nucleon decay of the collective particle-hole states (doorway states); (ii) the Breit-Wigner parametrization of the RPA reaction-amplitudes in the vicinity of the giant giant-resonance energy and evaluation of the doorway-state parameter (the energy, entrance and partial nucleon, escape widths); (iii) the phenomenological consideration for the doorway-state coupling to many-particle configurations with the use of reasonable statistical assumptions and derivation on this base the expressions for the energy-averaged reaction amplitudes. The nuclear mean field and quasiparticle interaction are the input quantities for calculations. Examples of description for the direct nucleon decay of a number of giant resonances in the $^{208}Pb$ parent nucleus are given together with the comparison with corresponding experimental data.

# On description of the direct nucleon decay of giant resonances

## Key claim
The semimicroscopical approach can describe the direct nucleon decay of giant resonances in intermediate and heavy mass spherical nuclei, but the quality of the description depends strongly on the choice of the particle-hole interaction (Landau-Migdal versus separable forms), with the Landau-Migdal interaction being preferable for isovector resonances (IAR, GTR, GDR) while the opposite holds for the isoscalar monopole resonance (GMR).

## Method
The approach combines exact continuum-RPA calculations of reaction amplitudes for collective particle-hole (doorway) states with a Breit-Wigner parametrization to extract doorway-state parameters (energy, entrance width, partial escape widths). The coupling of doorway states to many-particle configurations is treated phenomenologically using statistical assumptions, leading to energy-averaged reaction amplitudes. The nuclear mean field (Woods-Saxon type) and phenomenological particle-hole interaction are input quantities, with experimental decay-channel energies and averaged penetrabilities used where needed.

## Key numbers
- IAR in 208Bi: calculated partial proton escape widths for the 1/2- state are 61 keV (interaction 1a) and 63 keV (interaction 1b), compared to experimental values 51.6±1.7 keV (ref. [18]) and 51.4±5.6 keV (ref. [7]).
- IAR in 208Bi: total calculated entrance width is 176 keV (interaction 1a) and 189 keV (interaction 1b), compared to experimental total 134.6 keV (ref. [18]) and 134.3 keV (ref. [7]).
- GTR in 208Bi: calculated averaged partial proton escape width for the 1/2- state is 43 keV (interaction 2a) and 173 keV (interaction 2b), compared to experimental 48.9±9.3 keV (ref. [7]).
- GTR in 208Bi: total calculated width is 72 keV (interaction 2a) and 638 keV (interaction 2b), compared to experimental total 153.8 keV (ref. [7]); the observable total width of the GTR is 3.75 MeV (ref. [7]).
- GMR in 208Pb: calculated averaged neutron escape width for the 1/2- state is 76 keV (interaction 3a) and 49 keV (interaction 3b), compared to experimental 0 keV (ref. [5]) and 140±35 keV (ref. [4]).
- GMR in 208Pb: total calculated width is 996 keV (interaction 3a) and 574 keV (interaction 3b), compared to experimental 325 keV (ref. [5]) and 425 keV (ref. [4]).
- GDR in 208Pb: calculated branching ratio for neutron decay is about 10% at the GDR energy, compared to an experimental value of about 3-5% deduced from (17O,17O' n_c) reactions (ref. [6]).
- GDR in 208Pb: the cross section for the (n,γ0) reaction calculated with separable interaction (4) is underestimated by a factor of 3 compared to experiment (ref. [3]).

## Context
This work extends the continuum-RPA approach developed by the same authors (1995, 1996) and others (refs. [2,4,8-17]) for describing direct nucleon decay of giant resonances. It addresses the need to treat doorway-state coupling to 2p-2h configurations, which was attempted earlier by refs. [11,14]. The approach enables systematic comparison of different particle-hole interaction forms (Landau-Migdal versus separable) against experimental data for IAR, GTR, GMR, and GDR in 208Pb, and identifies which interaction types are most suitable for each resonance class. The authors conclude that continued experimental and theoretical studies of direct nucleon decay can provide information on the particle-hole structure of giant resonances and the particle-hole interaction in nuclei.
