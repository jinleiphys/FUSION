# Electroweak nuclear response in quasi-elastic regime

> The availability of the double-differential charged-current neutrino cross section, measured by the MiniBooNE collaboration using a carbon target, allows for a systematic comparison of nuclear effects in quasi-elastic electron and neutrino scattering. The results of our study, based on the impulse approximation scheme and a state-of-the-art model of the nuclear spectral functions, suggest that the electron cross section and the flux averaged neutrino cross sections, corresponding to the same target and comparable kinematical conditions, cannot be described within the same theoretical approach using the value of the nucleon axial mass obtained from deuterium measurements. We analyze the assumptions underlying the treatment of electron scattering data, and argue that the description of neutrino data will require a new {\em paradigm}, suitable for application to processes in which the lepton kinematics is not fully determined.

## Key claim
The theoretical approach based on impulse approximation and realistic spectral functions, which successfully describes quasi-elastic electron-nucleus scattering, fails to reproduce the MiniBooNE charged-current quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus cross section unless the nucleon axial mass is increased from the deuterium-measured value of 1.03 GeV to approximately 1.6 GeV, suggesting that a new paradigm is needed for neutrino scattering where the lepton kinematics is not fully determined.

## Method
The authors computed inclusive electron-carbon and neutrino-carbon cross sections within the impulse approximation scheme using a state-of-the-art nuclear spectral function model from Benhar et al. (2005) and recent vector form factor parametrizations from Bodek et al. (2008). For neutrino scattering, they employed a dipole parametrization of the axial form factor with varying axial mass values (1.03, 1.35, and 1.6 GeV) and compared the flux-integrated double-differential cross sections to MiniBooNE data from Aguilar-Arevalo et al. (2010). Final state interactions between the struck nucleon and spectator system were included, producing an approximately 10 MeV shift of the quasi-elastic peak.

## Key numbers
- The world average axial mass from deuterium measurements is M_A = 1.03 ± 0.02 GeV.
- The K2K and MiniBooNE collaborations reported effective axial masses of M_A ~ 1.2 to 1.35 GeV using oxygen and carbon targets, respectively.
- The average MiniBooNE neutrino flux energy is ⟨E_ν⟩ = 788 MeV.
- The best χ²-fit to the MiniBooNE flux-integrated Q²-distribution within the authors' approach required M_A = 1.6 GeV.
- The three-momentum transfer in the electron scattering example (E_e = 730 MeV, θ_e = 37°) is approximately |q| ~ 450 MeV, with variation less than 5% over the energy loss range shown.
- The continuum part of the spectral function from nucleon-nucleon correlations contributes less than 10% of the integrated electron spectrum.

## Context
This work extends the comparison of nuclear effects in electron and neutrino scattering made possible by the MiniBooNE double-differential charged-current quasi-elastic data from Aguilar-Arevalo et al. (2010). The results contradict the interpretation that the large effective axial mass reported by MiniBooNE and K2K can be explained by nuclear effects beyond the Fermi gas model, as most existing nuclear models (reviewed in NuInt09) fail to support this explanation, with the exception of the model by Martini et al. (2009) which includes multi-nucleon knockout contributions. The paper argues that future neutrino interaction models must account for contributions from multi-nucleon knockout, two-body currents, and pion production processes across the broad kinematical range sampled by the neutrino flux.
