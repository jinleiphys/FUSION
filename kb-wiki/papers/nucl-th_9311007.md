# Nuclear shell-model calculations for 6Li and 14N with different NN potentials

> Two ``phase-shift equivalent'' local NN potentials with different parametrizations, Reid93 and NijmII, which were found to give nearly identical results for the triton by Friar et al, are shown to yield remarkably similar results for 6Li and 14N in a (0+2)hw no-core space shell-model calculation. The results are compared with those for the widely used Hamada-Johnson hard-core and the original Reid soft-core potentials, which have larger deuteron D-state percentages. The strong correlation between the tensor strength and the nuclear binding energy is confirmed. However, many nuclear-structure properties seem to be rather insensitive to the details of the NN potential and, therefore, cannot be used to test various NN potentials. (Submitted to Phys. Rev. C on Nov. 9, 1993 as a Brief Report.)

# Nuclear shell-model calculations for 6Li and 14N with different NN potentials

## Key claim
Two phase-shift equivalent local NN potentials (Reid93 and NijmII) that give nearly identical triton properties also yield remarkably similar results for 6Li and 14N in a (0+2)hw no-core space shell-model calculation, but the authors caution that many nuclear-structure properties are too insensitive to the details of the NN potential to serve as tests of different potentials.

## Method
The authors performed no-core shell-model calculations in a (0+2)hw model space for 6Li and 14N, using Brueckner G-matrix effective interactions derived from four NN potentials: Reid93, NijmII, Hamada-Johnson hard-core (H-J), and the original Reid soft-core (RSC). The G-matrix was calculated with a shifted harmonic-oscillator single-particle Hamiltonian, with the Pauli operator Q defined to exclude two-particle states with N1+N2 ≤ 4 and to forbid scattering into occupied orbitals (0s for 6Li; 0s or 0p for 14N). The same calculation procedure was applied to all potentials to isolate differences arising solely from the NN interaction.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron D-state percentages: NijmII 5.64%, Reid93 5.70%, AV18 5.65% (from Friar et al. for context)
- Harmonic oscillator parameters: hw = 16 MeV, Delta = -38 MeV for A=6; hw = 14 MeV, Delta = -62 MeV for A=14
- The calculated binding energies for H-J and RSC potentials (which have larger deuteron D-state percentages) are much smaller than those for Reid93 and NijmII
- The B(GT) value from the ground state of 14C shows sizable changes between potentials but is not a monotonic function of the deuteron D-state percentage
- The quadrupole moment for the ground state of 6Li changes significantly when the NN potential is altered, but none of the potentials yields the experimental result with bare operators

## Context
This work extends the observation of Friar et al. (1993) that phase-shift equivalent potentials give nearly identical triton properties to the larger A=6 and A=14 systems. The results confirm the strong correlation between tensor strength (characterized by deuteron D-state percentage) and nuclear binding energy previously found for A=3 and A=4 systems by Friar et al. and Glockle et al. The finding that many nuclear-structure properties are insensitive to NN potential details limits the ability to test potentials through nuclear structure, though the authors note that specific observables like the 14C to 14N Gamow-Teller strength and the 6Li quadrupole moment remain sensitive to the tensor force and may require a weaker tensor component in truncated-space calculations.
