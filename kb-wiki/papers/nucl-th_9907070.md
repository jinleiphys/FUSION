# Coulomb distortion of pi+/pi- as a tool to determine the fireball radius in central high energy heavy ion collisions

> We compute the Coulomb distortion produced by an expanding and highly charged fireball on the spectra of low transverse momenta and mid rapidity pions produced in central high energy heavy ion collisions. We compare to data on Au+Au at 11.6A GeV from E866 at the BNL AGS and of Pb+Pb at 158A GeV from NA44 at the CERN SPS. We match the fireball expanison velocity with the average transverse momentum of protons and find a best fit to the charged pion ratio when the fireball radius is about 10 fm at freeze-out. This value is common to both AGS and SPS data.

## Key claim
The Coulomb distortion of the charged-pion ratio in central heavy ion collisions can be used to determine the fireball radius at freeze-out, provided the fireball expansion velocity is first fixed by matching the average transverse momentum of protons. The extracted freeze-out radius of about 10 fm is common to both AGS (Au+Au at 11.6A GeV) and SPS (Pb+Pb at 158A GeV) data, but the result depends on the assumption of a spherically symmetric, uniformly expanding charged sphere.

## Method
The authors model the fireball as a uniformly charged sphere whose radius increases linearly with time at a constant surface speed. They solve the Vlasov equation for the phase-space distribution of pions by the method of characteristics, evolving an initial thermal distribution forward in time using classical equations of motion integrated numerically with a sixth-order Runge-Kutta method. The surface expansion velocity is determined by matching the model's predicted average proton transverse momentum to the measured value, and then the freeze-out radius is obtained by fitting the computed π⁺/π⁻ ratio to data from E866 at the AGS and NA44 at the SPS.

## Key numbers
- Fireball radius at pion freeze-out: 10 fm, giving a best fit to the π⁺/π⁻ ratio with χ² per degree of freedom = 1.67 for Pb+Pb at 158A GeV (SPS) and 0.53 for Au+Au at 11.6A GeV (AGS).
- Surface expansion velocity v_s = 0.916 for Pb+Pb at 158A GeV, matching the measured average proton transverse momentum of 825 MeV.
- Surface expansion velocity v_s = 0.914 for Au+Au at 11.6A GeV, matching the measured average proton transverse momentum of 820 MeV.
- Effective fireball charge Z = 120 for Pb+Pb at 158A GeV, based on approximately 30 protons per unit rapidity over a rapidity span of 1 to 5 units.
- Effective fireball charge Z = 116 for Au+Au at 11.6A GeV.
- Effective temperature of the primordial pion distribution: 110 MeV for Pb+Pb (up to m_T - m = 500 MeV) and 111 MeV for Au+Au (up to m_T - m = 400 MeV).

## Context
This work extends earlier approximate models by Koch and by Barz, Bondorf, Gaardhoje and Heiselberg, which described Coulomb effects in collisions with predominantly longitudinal expansion, by instead treating a spherically symmetric expanding source. The analysis shows that the charged-pion ratio provides a quantitative measure of the fireball size at decoupling, and the extracted radius of about 10 fm is consistent with pion interferometry results from Barrette and Wiedemann, which found transverse and longitudinal radii about twice the cold nuclear radius at both AGS and SPS energies. The approach enables future refinements, such as using a two-temperature fit for the primordial distribution or relaxing the spherical symmetry assumption.
