# Nuclear Coherent versus Incoherent Effects in Peripheral RHI Collisions

> We derive simple and physically transparent expressions for the contribution of the strong interaction to one nucleon removal processes in peripheral relativistic heavy ion collisions. The coherent contribution,i.e, the excitation of a giant dipole resonance via meson exchange is shown to be negligible as well as interference between coulomb and nuclear excitation. Incoherent nucleon knock out contribution is also derived suggesting the nature of the nuclear interaction in this class of processes. We also justify the simple formulae used to fit the data of the E814 Collaboration.

## Key claim

The paper derives simple analytical expressions for the strong-interaction contribution to one-nucleon removal in peripheral relativistic heavy ion collisions, and shows that the coherent nuclear excitation of a giant dipole resonance via meson exchange is negligible, while the incoherent nucleon knock-out process (described by Glauber theory) reproduces the empirical nuclear cross section parametrization used by the E814 Collaboration.

## Method

The authors first compute the coherent contribution to one-nucleon removal by modeling the excitation of a giant dipole resonance via one-photon and one-vector-meson exchange in a covariant formalism, deriving analytical expressions for the pure Coulomb, pure strong, and interference cross sections with a macroscopic sum-rule model for the transition currents. They then treat the incoherent nucleon knock-out process using Glauber's multiple scattering theory, calculating the nuclear cross section from an impact-parameter integral that includes a distorting function and the Wigner transform of the removed nucleon's momentum distribution, with Gaussian densities for the nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction \(^{28}\text{Si} \rightarrow p + ^{27}\text{Al}\) at 14.5 GeV/nucleon on various targets, the experimental strong cross sections \(\sigma_{\text{exp-s}}\) range from \(7.13 \pm 1.00\) mb (C target) to \(12.00 \pm 1.70\) mb (Pb target).
- The calculated coherent strong cross sections \(\sigma_s\) (with \(g_v = 3.36\) and \(\mu = 300\) MeV) are negligible: \(7.00 \times 10^{-3}\) mb for C and \(9.01 \times 10^{-7}\) mb for Pb.
- The calculated interference cross sections \(\sigma_{cs}\) are also small: \(1.13 \times 10^{-2}\) mb for C down to \(2.50 \times 10^{-4}\) mb for Pb.
- The empirical nuclear cross section parametrization is \(\sigma_{\text{exp-s}} = a (A_T^{1/3} + A_P^{1/3})\) with \(a = 1.34 \pm 0.19\) fm\(^2\).
- The calculated incoherent nuclear cross sections \(\sigma_N\) range from 8.11 mb (C) to 15.23 mb (Pb), and the ratio \(\sigma_N / (A_P^{1/3} + A_T^{1/3})\) is approximately constant at 1.5–1.6.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of one-nucleon removal in peripheral relativistic heavy ion collisions, previously studied by Heckman and Lindstrom (radiochemical techniques) and by the E814 Collaboration (Takai et al.), who used empirical parametrizations separating Coulomb and nuclear contributions. The paper contradicts the assumption that the nuclear contribution arises from coherent giant resonance excitation via meson exchange (as considered by Vary and others), showing instead that the incoherent knock-out mechanism within Glauber's multiple scattering theory (as applied by Caro et al. to fragmentation reactions) quantitatively reproduces the E814 data. This enables a physically grounded prescription for isolating the nuclear contribution in future experiments.
