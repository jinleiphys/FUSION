# Four-body continuum-discretized coupled-channels calculations

> The development of a continuum-bins scheme of discretization for three-body projectiles is necessary for studies of reactions of Borromean nuclei such as 6He within the continuum-discretized coupled-channels approach. Such a procedure, for constructing bin states on selected continuum energy intervals, is formulated and applied for the first time to reactions of a three-body projectile. The continuum representation uses the eigenchannel expansion of the three-body S-matrix. The method is applied to the challenging case of the 6He + 208Pb reaction at 22 MeV, where an accurate treatment of both the Coulomb and the nuclear interactions with the target is necessary.

## Key claim

The paper formulates and applies for the first time a continuum-bin discretization scheme for three-body projectiles within the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) approach, using the eigenchannel expansion of the three-body S-matrix. The method is applied to the challenging case of the 6He + 208Pb reaction at 22 MeV, where an accurate treatment of both Coulomb and nuclear interactions with the target is necessary, and the authors show that only a few eigenchannels (approximately four) are needed for converged scattering observables.

## Method

The authors construct bin states for a three-body projectile by superposing three-body scattering states over selected continuum energy intervals, using the hyperspherical harmonics (HH) expansion basis and the eigenchannel expansion of the three-body S-matrix. For each total angular momentum j and continuum energy, the multi-channel S-matrix in the beta basis is diagonalized to obtain eigenchannels (EC), which are ordered by the magnitude of their eigenphases to establish a hierarchy of continuum states. The bin wave functions are then built from these EC and used as basis states in a four-body CDCC calculation, with a multipole expansion of the coupling potentials for the projectile-target system, solving the coupled equations with the code FRESCO.

## Key numbers

- The 6He ground state has a binding energy of 0.953 MeV and a root mean squared (rms) matter radius of 2.46 fm, assuming an alpha particle rms radius of 1.47 fm.
- The 6He low-lying continuum spectrum is dominated by a narrow j^pi = 2+ resonance at 0.825 MeV above threshold.
- The maximum hypermomentum used in the CDCC reaction calculation was K_max = 8, with 15 channels for 0+, 26 for 1-, and 46 for 2+.
- The coupled equations were solved with partial waves up to J = 150 and matched to asymptotic forms at a matching radius R_m = 200 fm.
- The contribution of the fifth eigenchannel changes the total breakup cross section by less than 2% compared to using four eigenchannels.

## Context

This work extends the CDCC method, previously formulated for two-body projectiles (Yahiro 1986, Austern 1987) and recently extended to include core excitation (Sumiyoshi 2006), to three-body projectiles using a continuum-bin discretization scheme rather than the pseudostate (PS) basis approach used in prior calculations (Matsumoto 2004, Matsumoto 2006, Rodriguez-Gallardo 2008, Egorova 2009). The authors note that their own previous PS-based calculations (Rodriguez-Gallardo 2008) showed convergence problems for reactions where the Coulomb interaction plays an important role, and the present bin technique addresses this limitation. The method enables practical four-body CDCC calculations for reactions of Borromean nuclei such as 6He, and the eigenchannel hierarchy provides a truncation scheme that makes such calculations computationally feasible.
