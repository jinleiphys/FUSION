# Electronic screening and damping in magnetars

> We calculate the screening of the ion-ion potential due to electrons in the presence of a large background magnetic field, at densities of relevance to neutron star crusts. Using the standard approach to incorporate electron screening through the one-loop polarization function, we show that the magnetic field produces important corrections both at short and long distances. In extreme fields, realized in highly magnetized neutron stars called magnetars, electrons occupy only the lowest Landau levels in the relatively low density region of the crust. Here our results show that the screening length for Coulomb interactions between ions can be smaller than the inter-ion spacing. More interestingly, we find that the screening is anisotropic and the screened potential between two static charges exhibits long range Friedel oscillations parallel to the magnetic field. This long-range oscillatory behavior is likely to affect the lattice structure of ions, and can possibly create rod-like structures in the magnetar crusts. We also calculate the imaginary part of the electron polarization function which determines the spectrum of electron-hole excitations and plays a role in damping lattice phonon excitations. We demonstrate that even for modest magnetic fields this damping is highly anisotropic and will likely lead to anisotropic phonon heat transport in the outer neutron star crust.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the strong magnetic fields of magnetars (up to ~10^15 G), electron screening of ion-ion Coulomb interactions becomes anisotropic and can produce long-range Friedel oscillations parallel to the magnetic field, with the screening length potentially becoming smaller than the inter-ion spacing in low-density regions, a qualitative departure from the unmagnetized case where screening is typically unimportant for crust structure.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop electron polarization function in a uniform background magnetic field, using the standard density-density correlation function formalism with Landau level quantization. They evaluate the static (ω=0) real part to obtain the screened ion-ion potential via the dielectric function, and the imaginary part to obtain the Landau damping rate of longitudinal lattice phonons. Analytic results are derived in the limits where only the lowest Landau level is occupied (low density or extreme field), and numerical Fourier transforms are performed for the screened potential.

## Key numbers

- For eB = 100 eB_c (B ~ 4.4×10^15 G) and μ = 0.7 MeV, the Debye screening length λ_D = 468 fm and the Friedel oscillation wavelength λ_F = π/k_f^z = 1296 fm.
- The screening length when only the lowest Landau level is occupied is given by λ_D = (π√2)/(e√(eB)) √(v_f^z), which can be smaller than the inter-ion spacing a for a > 1164 fm at eB = 10 eB_c.
- For a free electron gas at B=0, the ratio λ_D/a ≈ 1.83/(Z_26^(1/3)) √(v_f), implying λ_D > a in the relativistic regime.
- The non-relativistic Friedel oscillation amplitude along the magnetic field direction scales as ~cos(2k_f^z z) with a 1/z envelope, while perpendicular to the field the screening remains exponential.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of magnetic field effects on atomic structure in neutron star atmospheres (Ruderman 1974, Lai 1997) to the higher-density crust regime where electrons form a degenerate Fermi gas. The enhanced screening in strong fields was previously noted by Shalybkov (1987) in the context of nuclear fusion, but the new finding of anisotropic Friedel oscillations parallels well-known condensed matter results for B=0 (Fetter & Walecka 1971) and the relativistic treatment of Kapusta and Toimela (1988). The results enable predictions of anisotropic crystal structures (rod-like phases) and anisotropic phonon heat transport in magnetar crusts, relevant for interpreting crust oscillations observed in giant flares and for modeling magnetic and thermal evolution (Geppert 2004, Aguilera 2007).
