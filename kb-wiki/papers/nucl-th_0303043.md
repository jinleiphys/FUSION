# The Quark-Gluon Plasma in a Finite Volume

> The statistical mechanics of quarks and gluons are investigated within the context of the canonical ensemble. Recursive techniques are developed which enforce the exact conservation of baryon number, total isospin, electric charge, strangeness, and color. Bose and Fermi-Dirac statistics are also accounted for to all orders. The energy, entropy and particle number densities are shown to be significantly reduced for volumes less than 5 cubic fm.

# The Quark-Gluon Plasma in a Finite Volume

## Key claim
The statistical mechanics of a non-interacting quark-gluon plasma in a finite volume, treated with exact canonical conservation of baryon number, isospin, electric charge, strangeness, and color (including Bose and Fermi statistics to all orders), shows that energy, entropy, and particle number densities are significantly reduced for volumes less than 5 fm³; however, the authors argue these effects are unlikely to be important in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC, but might be quite important for pp collisions.

## Method
Recursive techniques were developed and extended from prior work on nuclear fragmentation (Chase and Mekjian) and quantum statistics (Pratt) to enforce exact conservation of additive charges (baryon number, strangeness, electric charge) and non-additive charges (isospin via SU(2) and color via SU(3)). The method uses cycle decompositions to incorporate Bose and Fermi symmetrization to all orders, and combines partition functions for gluons, up/down quarks, and strange/anti-strange quarks via convolution relations that couple SU(3) multiplets using Young tableau rules programmed numerically. Calculations were performed for a massless gluon and up/down quark gas with strange quarks of mass 150 MeV at T = 250 MeV, using continuous momentum integrals for single-particle partition functions.

## Key numbers
- Energy and entropy densities are significantly reduced for volumes less than 5 fm³.
- Temperature used in calculations: T = 250 MeV.
- Strange quark mass assumed: 150 MeV.
- The color-singlet constraint alone reduces the partition function by a factor scaling as V⁻⁴, corresponding to a reduction of -4 ln(V) to entropy and energy densities.
- For massless partons, the density of partons is expected to exceed 10 fm⁻³ during the first one or two fm/c of a RHIC collision.
- The effective volume for a pp collision might be only a few cubic fm, while the volume of a RHIC collision might be a few thousand cubic fm; even in a RHIC collision, the effective volume for charge conservation may be a few dozen cubic fm.

## Context
This work extends recursive canonical ensemble techniques previously applied to nuclear fragmentation (Chase and Mekjian) and to hadron gases with isospin conservation (Seni and Pratt) to include exact SU(3) color conservation for a quark-gluon plasma. It contrasts with grand canonical treatments that ignore finite-volume conservation constraints, and with alternative canonical approaches using imaginary chemical potentials (Becattini, Turko, Elze). The results enable quantitative assessment of when finite-volume and conservation effects matter for heavy ion collisions versus pp collisions, and are relevant to charge fluctuation and balance function studies (Bass and Pratt, Jeon and Koch, Asakawa, Gavin, Kapusta, Pruneau, Gavin, Voloshin, Shuryak and Stephanov).
