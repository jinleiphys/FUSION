# Multiplicity fluctuations in relativistic nuclear collisions: statistical model versus experimental data

> The multiplicity distributions of hadrons produced in central nucleus-nucleus collisions are studied within the hadron-resonance gas model in the large volume limit. The microscopic correlator method is used to enforce conservation of three charges - baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness - in the canonical ensemble. In addition, in the micro-canonical ensemble energy conservation is included. An analytical method is used to account for resonance decays. The multiplicity distributions and the scaled variances for negatively, positively, and all charged hadrons are calculated along the chemical freeze-out line of central Pb+Pb (Au+Au) collisions from SIS to LHC energies. Predictions obtained within different statistical ensembles are compared with the preliminary NA49 experimental results on central Pb+Pb collisions in the SPS energy range. The measured fluctuations are significantly narrower than the Poisson ones and clearly favor expectations for the micro-canonical ensemble. Thus this is a first observation of the recently predicted suppression of the multiplicity fluctuations in relativistic gases in the thermodynamical limit due to conservation laws.

## Key claim

The multiplicity fluctuations measured by NA49 in central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies are significantly narrower than Poisson expectations and clearly favor the micro-canonical ensemble (MCE) over the grand canonical ensemble (GCE) or canonical ensemble (CE), providing the first observation of the recently predicted suppression of multiplicity fluctuations in relativistic gases in the thermodynamic limit due to conservation laws.

## Method

The authors used the hadron-resonance gas model in the large volume limit, employing the microscopic correlator method to enforce conservation of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness in the canonical ensemble, with additional energy conservation in the micro-canonical ensemble. An analytical method accounted for resonance decays, and the scaled variances for negatively, positively, and all charged hadrons were calculated along the chemical freeze-out line of central Pb+Pb (Au+Au) collisions from SIS to LHC energies. The model predictions were compared with preliminary NA49 experimental results on central Pb+Pb collisions in the SPS energy range, incorporating a binomial acceptance correction for the limited detector coverage.

## Key numbers

- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17.3\) GeV (SPS), the MCE scaled variance for negatively charged hadrons is \(\omega^- = 0.318\), compared to GCE \(\omega^- = 1.108\) and CE \(\omega^- = 0.768\).
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17.3\) GeV, the MCE scaled variance for positively charged hadrons is \(\omega^+ = 0.263\), compared to GCE \(\omega^+ = 1.174\) and CE \(\omega^+ = 0.637\).
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5500\) GeV (LHC), the MCE scaled variance for all charged hadrons is \(\omega^{ch} = 0.619\), compared to GCE \(\omega^{ch} = 1.640\) and CE \(\omega^{ch} = 1.640\).
- The acceptance probabilities for positively and negatively charged hadrons in NA49 at SPS energies range from \(q^\pm = 0.038\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 6.27\) GeV to \(q^\pm = 0.163\) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17.3\) GeV.
- The chemical freeze-out temperature at LHC energy is \(T = 163.8\) MeV with baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B = 0.9\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the statistical model approach from mean multiplicities (previously studied by Fermi, Landau, and Hagedorn) to multiplicity fluctuations, which were recently found to differ between statistical ensembles even in the thermodynamic limit by the same group (Begun et al., 2005). The results enable future tests of the statistical hadronization model through fluctuation measurements and provide a baseline for searching for critical point signals, as proposed by Stephanov, Rajagopal, and Shuryak. The comparison with NA49 data constitutes the first experimental evidence that conservation laws suppress multiplicity fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, favoring the micro-canonical ensemble description.
