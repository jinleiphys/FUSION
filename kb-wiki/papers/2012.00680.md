# Estimate of the location of the neutron drip line for calcium isotopes from an exact Hamiltonian with continuum pair correlations

> The eastern region of the calcium isotope chain of the nuclei chart is, nowadays, of great activity. The experimental assessment of the limit of stability is of interest to confirm or improve microscopic theoretical models. The goal of this work is to provide the drip line of the calcium isotopes from the exact solution of the pairing Hamiltonian which incorporates explicitly the correlations with the continuum spectrum of energy. The modified Richardson equations, which include correlations with the continuum spectrum of energy modeled by the continuum single particle level density, is used to solve the many-body system. Three models are used, two isospin independent models with core 40Ca and 48Ca, and one isospin dependent model. One and two-neutron separation energies and occupation probabilities for bound and continuum states are calculated from the solution of the Richardson equations. The one particle drip line is found at the nucleus 57Ca, while the two neutron drip line is found at the nucleus 60Ca from the isospin independent model and at 66Ca from the isospin dependent one.

## Key claim

The one-neutron drip line for calcium isotopes is found at 57Ca, while the two-neutron drip line is found at either 60Ca (from the isospin independent model) or 66Ca (from the isospin dependent model), with the authors noting that all three models reproduce known data equally well, placing an uncertainty in the prediction.

## Method

The authors solved the modified Richardson equations, which incorporate correlations with the continuum spectrum of energy through the continuum single particle level density (CSPLD), for the pairing Hamiltonian with constant pairing interaction. Three models were used: two isospin independent models with cores 40Ca and 48Ca, and one isospin dependent model where the Woods-Saxon and spin-orbit strengths varied linearly with the isospin asymmetry parameter I = (N-Z)/A. One and two-neutron separation energies and occupation probabilities for bound and continuum states were calculated from the solution of the Richardson equations, with the one-neutron separation energy obtained using the pairing gap and Fermi level borrowed from BCS formalism.

## Key numbers

* One-neutron drip line found at 57Ca
* Two-neutron drip line found at 60Ca from the isospin independent model
* Two-neutron drip line found at 66Ca from the isospin dependent model
* Reduced radius r0 = 1.28 fm extracted from experimental neutron root-mean-square rn = 3.555 fm for 48Ca
* Diffuseness a = 0.75 fm
* Woods-Saxon strength for 40Ca core: 51.39(0.94) MeV; for 48Ca core: 45.39(1.47) MeV
* Spin-orbit strength for 40Ca core: 16.56(3.31) MeV fm; for 48Ca core: 18.36(6.03) MeV fm
* Reduced pairing strength chi1 = 22.850 MeV for 40Ca core model (isospin independent)
* Reduced pairing strength chi1 = 23.274 MeV for 48Ca core model (isospin independent)
* Isospin dependent pairing parameters: chi1 = 16.314 MeV, chi2 = -1.108
* Calculated binding energy of 50Ca: -427.508 MeV (experimental: -427.508(1) MeV)
* Calculated binding energy of 54Ca: -445.363 MeV (experimental: -445.36(4) MeV)
* Calculated binding energy of 58Ca: -454.400 MeV (experimental: -454.4(4) MeV)
* Derivative of Fermi level with respect to neutron number: 0.55(11) MeV for 50 <= A <= 54; 0.41(5) MeV for 56 <= A <= 60; 0.27(3) MeV for A >= 62

## Context

This work extends the exact solution of the pairing Hamiltonian (Richardson equations) to include continuum correlations via the CSPLD, applied to the calcium isotope chain. The results are compared with ab initio models (Hagen 2012, Forssen 2013), self-consistent Green's function calculations (Soma 2020), Bogoliubov perturbation formalism (Tichai 2018), Bayesian Model Averaging (Neufcourt 2019), and Gamow Shell Model (Li 2020). The isospin independent result (60Ca) agrees with several theoretical predictions, while the isospin dependent result (66Ca) is smaller than predictions from Neufcourt (2019) and Li (2020) which place the drip line around 68Ca-70Ca. The one-neutron drip line at 57Ca disagrees with the experimental finding of Tarasov (2018) which found 59Ca also bound. The work enables further investigation of the role of isospin dependence in pairing and mean-field for determining nuclear stability limits.
