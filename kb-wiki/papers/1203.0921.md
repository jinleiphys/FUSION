# Quark charge balance function and hadronization effects in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> We calculate the charge balance function of the bulk quark system before hadronization and those for the directly produced and the final hadron system in high energy heavy ion collisions. We use the covariance coefficient to describe the strength of the correlation between the momentum of the quark and that of the anti-quark if they are produced in a pair and fix the parameter by comparing the results for hadrons with the available data. We study the hadronization effects and decay contributions by comparing the results for hadrons with those for the bulk quark system. Our results show that while hadronization via quark combination mechanism slightly increases the width of the charge balance functions, it preserves the main features of these functions such as the longitudinal boost invariance and scaling properties in rapidity space. The influence from resonance decays on the width of the balance function is more significant but it does not destroy its boost invariance and scaling properties in rapidity space either. The balance functions in azimuthal direction are also presented.

## Key claim

The paper shows that hadronization via quark combination (coalescence) slightly increases the width of charge balance functions but preserves their longitudinal boost invariance and scaling properties in rapidity space, while resonance decays have a more significant effect on the width but also do not destroy these scaling properties.

## Method

The authors construct a working model for the bulk quark-antiquark system using a covariance coefficient ρ to describe the momentum correlation between quarks and antiquarks produced in the same pair, with single-particle distributions taken as Boltzmann distributions at T=165 MeV in the fluid cell co-moving frame. They then simulate hadronization using the quark combination model SDQCM (Shandong Quark Combination Model) with a Monte-Carlo program, including resonance decays using particle data group values, and compare the charge balance functions of the quark system, directly produced hadrons, and final hadrons.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses used: 340 MeV for u/d quarks and 500 MeV for strange quarks
- Temperature at hadronization taken as T=165 MeV
- Velocity function parameters for u/d newborn quarks: a=2.40, σ=2.54, β_perp_max=0.30
- Velocity function parameters for strange quarks: a=2.36, σ=2.73, β_perp_max=0.34
- The difference in averaged width ⟨δη⟩ between directly produced hadron system and bulk quark system is approximately 0.04 for all values of ρ
- For Λ→pπ and Ξ⁰→Λπ decays, the rapidity interval between daughter particles is about one third of the parent rapidity
- For ρ⁰→π⁺π⁻ decay, the rapidity difference between daughter particles can reach up to 1.7 in the rest frame
- For K*⁰→K⁺π⁻ decay, the rapidity difference can reach up to 1.3 in the rest frame

## Context

This work extends studies of charge balance functions in heavy ion collisions, which were proposed as probes of bulk matter properties by Bass et al., Jeon (2002), and Pratt (2011), and have been measured by the STAR Collaboration at RHIC in both rapidity (STAR BF130) and azimuthal (STAR BF200AApp) directions. The paper provides a theoretical framework connecting the observed scaling properties of hadron balance functions to the underlying quark system before hadronization, enabling quantitative separation of hadronization and resonance decay effects from the intrinsic properties of the quark-gluon plasma.
