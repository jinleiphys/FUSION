# Spin physics at A Fixed-Target ExpeRiment at the LHC (AFTER@LHC)

> We outline the opportunities for spin physics which are offered by a next generation and multi-purpose fixed-target experiment exploiting the proton LHC beam extracted by a bent crystal. In particular, we focus on the study of single transverse spin asymetries with the polarisation of the target.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a fixed-target experiment using the LHC’s 7 TeV proton beam extracted by a bent crystal can measure single transverse spin asymmetries (SSAs) with high accuracy, but this opportunity depends on the successful demonstration of the “smart collimator” bent-crystal extraction technique by the CERN LUA9 collaboration after the 2013 shutdown.

## Method

The authors propose extracting a fraction of the LHC proton beam using bent-crystal channeling, directing it onto a fixed polarized target (either via dynamic nuclear polarization or a hydrogen-deuterium target). They then outline measurements of single spin asymmetries in hard reactions such as prompt photon, open heavy flavor, and quarkonium production, which are sensitive to the gluon Sivers function. The study focuses on the large negative-\(x_F\) domain, which is largely unexplored in previous experiments.

## Key numbers

- The LHC proton beam energy of 7 TeV on a fixed target yields a center-of-mass energy close to 115 GeV.
- The extracted beam intensity can reach \(5 \times 10^8\) protons per second, corresponding to an average extraction per bunch per revolution of about 15 protons.
- The yearly integrated luminosity (for \(10^7\) seconds) is of the order of an inverse femtobarn.
- The heating power from the AFTER beam on a typical 1 cm thick target is about 50 \(\mu\)W, allowing target temperatures as low as 50 mK.
- Target damage from irradiation arises after \(10^{15}\) protons per cm\(^2\), corresponding to about one month of beam.
- The asymmetry for gluon-sensitive reactions such as \(p^\uparrow p \to \gamma X\) may be as large as 10% at \(\sqrt{s}\) of order 100 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of single spin asymmetries from earlier fixed-target experiments at Fermilab and Brookhaven (Adams 1991, Arsene 2008, Abelev 2008) to higher energies and the unexplored large negative-\(x_F\) region. It builds on the AFTER@LHC concept first outlined by Brodsky (2012) and Lansberg (2012), and specifically references theoretical proposals for measuring the gluon Sivers function via prompt photons (Qiu 1991, Schmidt 2005, D’Alesio 2006), photon-jet production (Bacchetta 2007), and photon-pair production (Qiu 2011). The paper also notes recent spin-physics studies with AFTER on Drell-Yan reactions (Liu 2012) and spinless quarkonium production (Boer 2012). The proposed experiment would enable systematic studies of the gluon contribution to the nucleon spin.
