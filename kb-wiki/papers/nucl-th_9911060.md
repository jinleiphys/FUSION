# The $Δ$-excitation in the nuclear charge longitudinal response

> The presence of a resonant structure corresponding to the $Δ$ excitation in the longitudinal response to an e.m. probe is investigated. It is shown that many-body effects could significantly increase the relativistic contribution suggested by M.Ericson and coworkers

# The Δ-excitation in the nuclear charge longitudinal response

## Key claim

Many-body effects (specifically those captured by the Boson Loop Expansion at one-loop order) could significantly increase the relativistic contribution to the Δ-excitation in the longitudinal response that was originally suggested by Chanfray, Delorme, Ericson and coworkers (1993), but the magnitude of this enhancement depends on transferred momentum and the results are not fully relativistic.

## Method

The authors compute the nuclear longitudinal response to an electromagnetic probe using a Relativistic Free Fermi Gas (RFFG) model for the direct M1, E2, and C2 Δ-excitation channels, then extend the calculation to include many-body correlations via the Boson Loop Expansion (BLE) at next-to-leading order (one boson loop). The BLE approach, previously developed by two of the authors, transforms the original nucleon-Δ action into an effective bosonic action via a Hubbard-Stratonovitch transformation, with the semi-classical limit corresponding to non-antisymmetrized RPA and the one-loop order including diagrams with RPA-dressed meson propagators that contain Δ-hole pairs. Because a fully relativistic one-loop BLE calculation is not feasible, the authors apply a rescaling of the energy axis to match relativistic kinematics for the peak positions.

## Key numbers

- The M1 coupling constant is chosen as f^M_{γNΔ} = 2.72, following Christillin and Dillon (1989).
- The C2 coupling constant is left free between 0 and 0.4; even at the maximum value of 0.4, the C2 contribution is hardly distinguishable from the M1-only result in the free response.
- The E2 coupling constant is expected to range between -1% and -2.5% of the M1 one, making its contribution negligible (factor of about 10^{-4} in the inclusive response).
- Calculations are performed for ^12C at a Fermi momentum of k_F = 1.1 fm^{-1}.
- Results are shown at transferred momenta q = 400 MeV/c, q = 500 MeV/c, and q = 600 MeV/c.
- The many-body excitations in the Δ-region are generally larger than the pure M1 response at q = 400 and 500 MeV/c.
- At q = 600 MeV/c, the resonant structure from many-body correlations (without the free Δ-peak) fully disappears, while the dip region is significantly filled.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Chanfray, Delorme, Ericson and coworkers (1993), who first showed that Δ-excitation can appear in the longitudinal response due to relativistic boost effects from Fermi motion. The authors improve upon that work by including E2 and C2 multipole contributions (found to be negligible) and, more importantly, many-body effects using the Boson Loop Expansion formalism developed by Cenni, Coniglio, and Saracco (1997). The results partially extend outcomes hidden in that earlier work, where a tail on the right of the quasi-elastic peak was displayed but not discussed as a resonant structure. The paper calls for fully relativistic many-body calculations and notes that the presence of Δ strength in the resonant region could require qualitative changes in derivations of the Coulomb sum rule to account for Δ degrees of freedom.
