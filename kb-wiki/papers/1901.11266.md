# Revealing minijet dynamics via centrality dependence of the double parton interactions in proton-nucleus interactions

> One of the main challenges hampering an accurate measurement of the double parton scattering (DPS) cross sections is the difficulty in separating the DPS from the leading twist (LT) contributions. We argue that such a separation can be achieved, and cross section of DPS measured, by exploiting the different centrality dependence of DPS and LT processes in proton-nucleus scattering. We developed a Monte Carlo implementation of the DPS processes which includes realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) correlations in nuclei, an accurate description of transverse geometry of both hard and soft NN collisions as well as fluctuations of the strength of interaction of nucleon with nucleus (color fluctuation effects). Our method allows the calculation of probability distributions of single and double dijet events as a function of centrality, also distinguishing double hard scatterings originating from a single target nucleon and from two different nucleons. We present numerical results for the rate of DPS as a function of centrality, which relates the number of wounded nucleons and the sum of transverse energy of hadrons produced at large negative (along the nucleus direction) rapidities, which is experimentally measurable. We suggest a new quantity which allows to test the geometry of DPS and we argue that it is a universal function of centrality for different DPS processes. This quantity can be tested by analyzing existing LHC data. The method developed in this work can be extended to the search for triple parton interactions.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the double parton scattering (DPS) cross section in proton-nucleus collisions can be cleanly separated from the competing leading twist (LT) contribution by exploiting the different centrality dependence of the two processes, specifically that the DPS contribution from interactions with two different nucleons grows roughly as the square of the number of nucleons along the projectile path, while the LT contribution grows linearly.

## Method

The authors developed a Monte Carlo implementation of DPS processes in proton-nucleus collisions, built on a Glauber model framework that includes realistic nucleon-nucleon correlations in nuclei, a finite transverse spread of parton distributions in nucleons, and color fluctuation effects (event-by-event fluctuations of the proton-nucleon interaction strength). They calculated the separate contributions to DPS from interactions with a single target nucleon (impulse approximation) and from interactions with two different target nucleons, as a function of impact parameter and centrality, using the transverse energy sum measured at large negative rapidities as the experimental centrality estimator.

## Key numbers

* For pPb collisions, the ratio of DPS to LT contributions is enhanced by a factor r(200) ~ 4 for sigma_eff = 20 mb, and r(200) ~ 5 for sigma_eff = 30 mb (minijet kinematics).
* The integral of the two-nucleon DPS geometric factor D^{2x2}(b) over impact parameter for pPb is 0.95 of the optical model integral of T^2(b), accounting for finite nucleon size, neutron skin, and NN correlations.
* The correlation factor R_corr ~ 0.15 for the kinematics discussed, leading to an enhancement of the single-nucleon DPS term by a factor (1 + 5 R_corr)/(1 + R_corr) ~ 1.5.
* The effective cross section sigma_eff = 30 mb corresponds to B = 3 GeV^{-2} via sigma_eff = 8 pi B.
* For central pA collisions, the underlying multiplicity from DPS should be enhanced by a factor of at least 2.5 compared to pp collisions, based on the DPS contribution of 30-40% to the pp cross section in the studied kinematics.

## Context

This work extends the formalism for multiparton interactions developed by Blok et al. (2010-2017) and the Monte Carlo Glauber approach for single hard triggers by Alvioli et al. (2014) to the case of double parton scattering in proton-nucleus collisions. The method builds on the observation by Strikman (2001) that MPI are parametrically enhanced in pA collisions by a factor proportional to A^{1/3}. The proposed centrality-dependent ratio R_i provides a test of the geometry of DPS that can be applied to existing LHC data from ATLAS, and the method can be extended to search for triple parton interactions.
