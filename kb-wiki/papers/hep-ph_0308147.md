# Nature of the Chiral Restoration Transition in QCD

> As the chirally restored phase ends with T coming down to T_c, a phase resembling a mixed phase is realized, during which the hadrons (which are massless at T_c in the chiral limit) get their masses back out of their kinetic energy. The gluon condensation energy is fed into the system to keep the temperature (nearly) constant. Lattice results for the gluon condensation are matched by a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio calculation. The latter shows that below T_c the chiral symmetry is barely broken, so that with an about 6% drop in the scalar coupling G it is restored at T_c. Nearly half of the glue, which we call epoxy, is not melted at T_c.

## Key claim

As the chirally restored phase ends with temperature decreasing toward T_c, a phase resembling a mixed phase is realized in which hadrons (massless at T_c in the chiral limit) regain their masses from their kinetic energy, with gluon condensation energy fed into the system to keep the temperature nearly constant; the authors emphasize that this is not a genuine mixed phase but is composed of off-shell hadrons.

## Method

The authors use a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in four dimensions (essentially the Gross-Neveu model without pions) to calculate the binding energy of soft glue, matching lattice results for the gluon condensate from Miller (2000). They treat the scalar coupling G as temperature-dependent, with the gluon degrees of freedom integrated out, and compute the temperature evolution of the soft-glue binding energy as G decreases. The model uses a cutoff Lambda = 660 MeV and an NJL coupling G Lambda^2 = 4.3 to obtain T_c = 170 MeV, with constituent quark masses m_Q = 320 MeV extracted from lattice charmonium results of Karsch (2001).

## Key numbers

* Total gluon condensate at T=0: 0.012 GeV^4, obtained with Lambda = 660 MeV and NJL G Lambda^2 = 4.3 giving T_c = 170 MeV.
* Soft glue binding energy (constituent quark sector): 0.0058 GeV^4, roughly 50% of the total glue, calculated with Lambda = 660 MeV and m_Q = 320 MeV.
* Miller's lattice results show the trace anomaly decreases from 0.012 GeV^4 at T ~ 122 MeV to 0.0056 GeV^4 at T ~ 170 MeV (taken as T_c for unquenched calculations), a drop of 53% of the total gluon condensate.
* An approximately 6% drop in the scalar coupling G near T_c is sufficient to match Miller's lattice results for the gluon condensate.
* Freeze-out temperature from RHIC: T_fo = 174 +/- 7 MeV for all hadrons (Braun 2001).

## Context

This work extends the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis (Brown and Rho, 1991) and the vector manifestation fixed point of Harada and Yamawaki (2003), which predicts that light-quark hadron masses go to zero with chiral restoration. It builds on the earlier division of the gluon condensate into soft and hard glue by Adami, Hatsuda and Zahed (1991) and Koch and Brown (1993), and matches lattice calculations of the trace anomaly by Miller (2000). The results suggest that the system above T_c is not a quark-gluon plasma but an "Instanton Molecule Liquid" as proposed by Shuryak and Zahed (2003), with nearly unchanged coupling constants and low viscosity enabling good equilibration.
