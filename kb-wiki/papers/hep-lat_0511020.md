# Chiral Extrapolation of Lattice Data for Heavy Meson Hyperfine Splittings

> We investigate the chiral extrapolation of the lattice data for the light-heavy meson hyperfine splittings D^*-D and B^*-B to the physical region for the light quark mass. The chiral loop corrections providing non-analytic behavior in m_πare consistent with chiral perturbation theory for heavy mesons. Since chiral loop corrections tend to decrease the already too low splittings obtained from linear extrapolation, we investigate two models to guide the form of the analytic background behavior: the constituent quark potential model, and the covariant model of QCD based on the ladder-rainbow truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations. The extrapolated hyperfine splittings remain clearly below the experimental values even allowing for the model dependence in the description of the analytic background.

## Key claim

The chiral loop corrections to heavy meson hyperfine splittings (D*-D and B*-B) are consistent with heavy meson chiral perturbation theory, but even after incorporating two physically motivated models for the analytic background (constituent quark model and Dyson-Schwinger equation model), the extrapolated splittings remain clearly below experimental values, 17% below for D mesons and 35% below for B mesons.

## Method

The authors fit lattice QCD data for D*-D and B*-B hyperfine splittings (obtained at unphysical light quark masses corresponding to pion masses above 680 MeV) using a form that adds chiral loop corrections from heavy meson chiral perturbation theory to an analytic background. Two models provide the analytic background: the constituent quark potential model (CQM), which gives a hyperfine splitting proportional to 1/(M_q + M_Q), and the ladder-rainbow truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE), which respects chiral symmetry constraints. The single free parameter d_P in the CQM background is fit to lattice data while constraining the parameter e_P to ranges derived from existing CQM applications to baryon physics.

## Key numbers

- Naive linear extrapolation of lattice data gives D*-D splitting of 120 MeV and B*-B splitting of 32 MeV, compared to experimental values of 140 MeV and 46 MeV respectively
- With CQM background and dipole form factor, extrapolated D*-D splitting ranges from 0.0983 GeV to 0.1150 GeV; B*-B splitting ranges from 0.0260 GeV to 0.0289 GeV
- With linear background and dipole form factor, extrapolated D*-D splitting ranges from 0.1000 GeV to 0.1162 GeV; B*-B splitting ranges from 0.0270 GeV to 0.0309 GeV
- The largest extrapolated hyperfine splittings obtainable are about 115 MeV for D mesons and 29 MeV for B mesons from the CQM background
- At m_pi^2 approximately 0.6 GeV^2, the pion loop contribution to y_D is typically -10 MeV and to y_B is typically -20 MeV
- The DSE model background at m_pi = 0 gives y_D = 0.1153 GeV and y_B = 0.0303 GeV
- The CQM background at m_pi = 0 gives y_D = 0.1224 GeV and y_B = 0.0304 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous chiral extrapolation studies by Guo, Tandy, and Thomas (2004, 2005) that used a linear analytic background, and by Leinweber and collaborators on chiral extrapolation of hadron properties. The authors improve upon prior work by replacing the purely phenomenological linear background with two physically motivated models: the constituent quark model (CQM) as parameterized by Cloet and collaborators, and the Dyson-Schwinger equation model developed by Maris, Tandy, and Roberts. The results enable future lattice QCD simulations to be compared against these extrapolation forms, and highlight the need for full QCD (rather than quenched) lattice data and improved treatment of the spin-dependent terms in NRQCD to resolve the persistent discrepancy with experiment.
