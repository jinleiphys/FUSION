# Virtual Compton Scattering off the nucleon in the linear sigma model

> Virtual Compton scattering off the nucleon has been studied in the one-loop approximation of the linear sigma model. The three generalized polarizabilities of the nucleon have been calculated and compared with the existing theoretical predictions. In particular, we find that only two of the three scalar polarizabilities are independent observables.

# Virtual Compton Scattering off the Nucleon in the Linear Sigma Model

## Key claim
The three generalized scalar polarizabilities of the nucleon are not independent: only two are independent observables, and the transverse amplitude to linear order in the outgoing photon energy is completely determined by the magnetic polarizability alone. This relation is argued to be model-independent, relying on Lorentz and gauge invariance together with charge conjugation and nucleon crossing symmetry, not a peculiarity of the linear sigma model itself.

## Method
The authors performed a one-loop calculation of virtual Compton scattering (VCS) off both proton and neutron in the linear sigma model (LSM), taking the limit of infinite sigma mass. They computed the three scalar generalized polarizabilities, electric (α), magnetic (β), and mixed (P̂), as functions of the four-momentum transfer Q₀², and derived analytical expansions in the ratio μ = m_π / m_N at the real photon point (Q₀² = 0). The relation among the polarizabilities was established both analytically at Q₀² = 0 and numerically for the full Q₀² range, and a general proof using the structure of the non-Born Compton tensor was provided.

## Key numbers
- Experimental values at the real photon point (units of 10⁻⁴ fm³): α_p_exp(0) = 12.1 ± 1.0, β_p_exp(0) = 2.1 ∓ 1.0, α_n_exp(0) = 12.6 ± 2.5, β_n_exp(0) = 3.2 ∓ 2.5 (MacGibbon 1995, Schmiedmayer 1991).
- Analytical expansion for the proton electric polarizability at Q² = 0: α_p(0) = (e² g_πN²)/(192π³ m_N³) [ (5π)/(2μ) + 18 ln μ + 33/2 + O(μ) ], with g_πN = 13.4.
- Analytical expansion for the proton magnetic polarizability at Q² = 0: β_p(0) = (e² g_πN²)/(192π³ m_N³) [ π/(4μ) + 18 ln μ + 63/2 + O(μ) ].
- Leading m_π⁻¹ term in α(0) and β(0) and leading m_π⁻³ term in their Q² derivatives coincide with heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) predictions to third order (Bernard 1992, Hemmert 1996).
- The relation (e²/4π) P̂^(01,1)0(0) = -(1/(3m_N)) [α(0) + β(0)] holds to all orders in μ.

## Context
This work extends earlier calculations of generalized nucleon polarizabilities in VCS, which had been performed in a nonrelativistic constituent quark model (CQM) by Guichon, Liu and Thomas (1995, 1996) and in an effective Lagrangian model (ELM) by Vanderhaeghen (1996). The LSM results show a different Q² dependence, rapid decrease of α and increase of β at low Q², compared to the smoother behavior in the CQM. The key finding that only two scalar polarizabilities are independent is argued to be model-independent (Knoechlein 1996), which simplifies the interpretation of upcoming VCS experiments at MAMI and CEBAF that aim to measure the Q² evolution of the electric polarizability.
