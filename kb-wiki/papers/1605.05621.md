# Drag induced radiative energy loss of semi-hard heavy quarks

> We revisited gluon bremsstrahlung off a heavy quark in nuclear matter within higher twist formalism. In this work, we demonstrate that, in addition to transverse momentum diffusion parameter ($\hat q$), the gluon emission spectrum for a heavy quark is quite sensitive to $\hat e$, which quantify the amount of light-cone drag experienced by a parton. This effect leads to an additional energy loss term for heavy-quarks. From heavy flavor suppression data in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions one can now estimate the value of this sub-leading non-perturbative jet transport parameter ($\hat e$) from our results.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that, for semi-hard heavy quarks where momentum is comparable to mass, the gluon bremsstrahlung spectrum is sensitive not only to the transverse momentum diffusion parameter \(\hat q\) but also to the drag coefficient \(\hat e\), which quantifies light-cone momentum loss per unit light-cone length. This additional sensitivity leads to an extra radiative energy loss term beyond that provided by \(\hat q\) alone, though the authors note this is a first attempt considering only single scattering and single emission.

## Method

The authors revisited gluon bremsstrahlung off a heavy quark in nuclear matter using the higher twist formalism, considering deep-inelastic scattering of a hard virtual photon off a heavy quark within a large nucleus. They introduced a power-counting scheme based on soft collinear effective theory (SCET) with a small parameter \(\lambda\) to identify the regime where mass-dependent drag terms cause detectable effects, and derived the single gluon emission spectrum by expanding the hadronic tensor in transverse momentum \(k_\perp\) and light-cone momentum \(k^-\), retaining terms only up to \(\mathcal{O}(\sqrt{\lambda})\).

## Key numbers

- The heavy quark mass scales as \(M \sim \sqrt{\lambda} Q\), where \(Q\) is the hard scale defined by the virtual photon virtuality.
- The fraction of light-cone momentum carried by the emitted gluon is \(y = l^-/p^- \sim \sqrt{\lambda}\).
- The gluon emission spectrum per unit light-cone length is given by an expression containing terms proportional to \(\hat q\), \(\hat e\), and \(\hat e_2\), with the \(\hat e\) term weighted by \(\frac{l_\perp^2}{l^-}\chi(1+\chi)^2\) where \(\chi = y^2 M^2 / l_\perp^2\).
- The momentum fractions in the definitions of \(\hat q\), \(\hat e\), and \(\hat e_2\) for heavy quark scattering involve a modified momentum fraction \(\bar \Delta = \zeta x_D + (\zeta-1)\frac{x_M}{1-y} - \zeta\frac{\eta y^2}{1-y}x_L\).

## Context

This work extends the higher twist formalism for radiative energy loss, previously applied to light flavors and energetic heavy quarks where mass effects are negligible, to the semi-hard regime where heavy quark mass is comparable to momentum. Prior calculations of heavy flavor energy loss (e.g., Qin (2009), Abir (2012)) considered radiative loss only from transverse momentum diffusion (\(\hat q\)) or focused solely on drag loss without radiative contributions. The results enable estimation of the sub-leading non-perturbative jet transport parameter \(\hat e\) from heavy flavor suppression data in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, potentially resolving the "heavy quark puzzle" of unexpectedly large suppression of single electrons and open heavy mesons.
