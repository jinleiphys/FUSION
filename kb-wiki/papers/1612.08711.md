# Subtracting IR Renormalons from Wilson Coefficients: Uniqueness and power dependences on $Λ_\mathrm{QCD}$

> In the context of OPE and using the large-$β_0$ approximation, we propose a method to define Wilson coefficients free from uncertainties due to IR renormalons. We first introduce a general observable $X(Q^2)$ with an explicit IR cutoff, and then we extract a genuine UV contribution $X_\mathrm{UV}$ as a cutoff-independent part. $X_\mathrm{UV}$ includes power corrections $\sim (Λ_\mathrm{QCD}^2/Q^2)^n$ which are independent of renormalons. Using the integration-by-regions method, we observe that $X_\mathrm{UV}$ coincides with the leading Wilson coefficient in OPE and also clarify that the power corrections originate from UV region. We examine scheme dependence of $X_\mathrm{UV}$ and single out a specific scheme favorable in terms of analytical properties. Our method would be optimal with respect to systematicity, analyticity and stability. We test our formulation with the examples of the Adler function, QCD force between $Q \bar{Q}$, and $R$-ratio in $e^{+} e^{-}$ collision.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method to define Wilson coefficients free from IR renormalon uncertainties by extracting a cutoff-independent part $X_{\mathrm{UV}}$ from an observable defined with an explicit IR cutoff, and claims that this $X_{\mathrm{UV}}$ includes power corrections $\sim (\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}^2/Q^2)^n$ that originate from the UV region rather than from renormalon ambiguities, with a specific scheme favored based on analyticity properties.

## Method

The authors start from a general observable $X(Q^2)$ in the large-$\beta_0$ approximation, introduce an explicit IR cutoff $\mu_f$ on the gluon momentum to define $X_{\beta_0}(Q^2;\mu_f)$, then rewrite the weight function $w_X(x)$ in terms of a pre-weight $W_X(z)$ analytic in the upper half-plane via the relation $2\,\mathrm{Im}\,W_X(x)=w_X(x)$. By deforming the integration contour in the complex $\tau$-plane and expanding $W_X(\tau/Q^2)$ about $\tau=0$, they separate the $\mu_f$-independent part $X_{\mathrm{UV}}$ from terms that depend on the cutoff, using the known locations of IR renormalon singularities to classify which expansion coefficients contribute to the cutoff-independent piece.

## Key numbers

* The first IR renormalon for the Adler function is located at $u_{\mathrm{IR}}=2$, giving a renormalon uncertainty of order $(\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}^2/Q^2)^2$.
* The leading power correction in $D_{\mathrm{UV}}(Q^2)$ for the Adler function is $\frac{8(4-3\zeta_3)e^{5/3}N_C C_F}{3\beta_0} \frac{\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}^2}{Q^2}$, where $N_C=3$, $C_F=4/3$, and $\zeta_3=\zeta(3)$.
* The effective coupling in the large-$\beta_0$ approximation is $\alpha_{\beta_0}(\tau)=\frac{4\pi}{\beta_0}\frac{1}{\log(\tau e^{-5/3}/\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}^2)}$, with a pole at $\tau=e^{5/3}\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}^2$.
* The asymptotic form of $X_0(Q^2)$ as $Q^2\to\infty$ is $d_0\alpha_s(Q)=\frac{4\pi d_0}{\beta_0}\frac{1}{\log(Q^2/\Lambda_{\mathrm{QCD}}^2)}$.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Ball, Beneke, and Braun (1995) on regularized Borel integrals and resummed perturbative series, and builds on the formulation used for the static QCD potential by Sumino (2003-2014). The method clarifies the relation between the cutoff-independent part and the leading Wilson coefficient in OPE using the integration-by-regions technique, and enables systematic extraction of non-perturbative matrix elements by providing a Wilson coefficient free from intrinsic uncertainties of the order of those matrix elements.
