# Confronting mass-varying neutrinos with MiniBooNE

> We study the proposal that mass-varying neutrinos could provide an explanation for the LSND signal for \barν_mu to \barν_e oscillations. We first point out that all positive oscillation signals occur in matter and that three active mass-varying neutrinos are insufficient to describe all existing neutrino data including LSND. We then examine the possibility that a model with four mass-varying neutrinos (three active and one sterile) can explain the LSND effect and remain consistent with all other neutrino data. We find that such models with a 3+1 mass structure in the neutrino sector may explain the LSND data and a null MiniBooNE result for 0.10 < \sin^2 2θ_x < 0.30. Predictions of the model include a null result at Double-CHOOZ, but positive signals for underground reactor experiments and for ν_μto ν_e oscillations in long-baseline experiments.

## Key claim
Mass-varying neutrinos (MaVaNs) with a 3+1 mass structure (three active plus one sterile) may explain the LSND oscillation signal and remain consistent with all other neutrino data, but only if MiniBooNE reports a null result; the model cannot accommodate a positive MiniBooNE confirmation of the LSND signal.

## Method
The authors constructed a minimal 3+1 MaVaN model in which MaVaN effects occur only for the third and fourth neutrino mass eigenstates, with no vacuum mixing between active and sterile neutrinos. They used oscillation probabilities in Earth matter to fit parameters from KamLAND, K2K, and LSND data simultaneously, then checked consistency with atmospheric neutrino data and solar neutrino data, assuming a power-law density dependence of the MaVaN mass terms.

## Key numbers
- LSND mass-squared difference: δm²_L ≈ 1 eV², with oscillation amplitude sin²2θ_L in the range 0.0003–0.001 for viable solutions with a null MiniBooNE result
- For a null MiniBooNE result with 5×10²⁰ protons on target, the allowed region requires 0.10 < sin²2θ_x < 0.30 and sin²θ between 0.04 and 0.10
- Vacuum mass m₃ ≈ 0.006 eV, with MaVaN parameters M₃₃, M₃₄, M₄₄ all of order 1 eV
- Atmospheric mass-squared difference δm²_a = 2.0×10⁻³ eV² used for both K2K and atmospheric neutrinos

## Context
This work extends the mass-varying neutrino proposal of Zurek (2004) and the MaVaN framework applied to solar neutrinos by Barger, Marfatia, and Whisnant (2005). It addresses the long-standing tension between LSND and other oscillation data that cannot be resolved by standard three-neutrino models (as shown by Fogli et al., 2003) or by conventional 3+1 sterile neutrino models (as shown by Maltoni et al., 2004). The model predicts a null result at Double-CHOOZ but positive signals for underground reactor experiments and for ν_μ→ν_e oscillations in long-baseline experiments, providing testable predictions for the MiniBooNE era.
