# Isospin mixing within the multi-reference nuclear density functional theory and beyond - selected aspects

> The results of systematic calculations of isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections to superallowed beta-decays based on the self-consistent isospin- and angular-momentum-projected nuclear density functional theory (DFT) are reviewed with an emphasis on theoretical uncertainties of the model. Extensions of the formalism towards no core shell model approach with basis cutoff scheme dictated by the self-consistent particle-hole DFT solutions will be also discussed.

# Isospin mixing within the multi-reference nuclear density functional theory and beyond - selected aspects

## Key claim

The self-consistent isospin- and angular-momentum-projected nuclear density functional theory can quantitatively capture isospin mixing in even-even N=Z nuclei, yielding isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections to superallowed Fermi beta decays that lead to a CKM matrix element |V_ud| consistent with unitarity at the 0.1% level, but the calculations are limited to Hamiltonian-driven Skyrme functionals because angular-momentum projection introduces singularities for modern density-dependent Skyrme and Gogny energy density functionals.

## Method

The authors start from a self-consistent Slater determinant obtained by solving Skyrme-Hartree-Fock equations without pairing, which violates both rotational and isospin symmetries. They restore rotational invariance and remove spurious isospin mixing by projecting the state onto good angular momentum and isospin, then rediagonalize the full Hamiltonian (isospin-invariant kinetic energy and Skyrme force plus isospin-non-invariant Coulomb force) in the collective space of linearly independent natural states. For the superallowed beta decay calculations, the parent even-even state is projected from the ground-state Slater determinant, while the odd-odd daughter state is projected from an anti-aligned configuration obtained by placing the odd neutron and odd proton in time-reversed single-particle orbits.

## Key numbers

- Isospin impurities in even-even N=Z nuclei calculated with the SLy4 Skyrme EDF show that pure mean-field results are lower by almost ~30% compared to the isospin-projected DFT values, due to spurious mixing from spontaneous isospin symmetry breaking.
- The calculated ISB corrections yield |V_ud| = 0.97397(27) for the SV EDF and |V_ud| = 0.97374(27) for the SHZ2 EDF.
- Both values of |V_ud| result in unitarity of the CKM matrix up to 0.1%.
- Theoretical uncertainties in the calculations are due to basis truncation (~10%), definition of the intrinsic state, and configuration selection in odd-odd nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the multi-reference density functional theory approach to isospin mixing pioneered by the authors in earlier studies (Satula 2009, Satula 2011) and applies it to superallowed Fermi beta decays, a problem critical for determining the CKM matrix element V_ud. The results are consistent with the shell-model-based calculations of Towner and Hardy (2008) but disagree with the RPA-based study of Liang (2009). The formalism is being extended beyond single-Slater-determinant projection toward a no-core shell model approach with basis truncation dictated by self-consistent particle-hole DFT solutions, with preliminary results shown for low-spin states in 32Cl.
