# Weak Decay of $Λ$ in Nuclei : Quarks vs Mesons

> Decays of $Λ$ in nuclei, nonmesonic mode, are studied by using the $ΛN \to NN$ weak transition potential derived from the meson exchange mechanism and the direct quark mechanism. The decay rates are calculated both for the $Λ$ in symmetric nuclear matter and light hypernuclei. We consider the exchange of six mesons ($π, K, η, ρ, ω, K^\ast$). The form factor in the meson exchange mechanism and short range correlation are carefully studied.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a hybrid model combining long-range meson exchange (including six mesons: π, K, η, ρ, ω, K*) and short-range direct quark (DQ) mechanisms to describe the ΛN → NN weak transition, but finds that even with this combined approach the enhancement of the neutron-induced decay is not large enough to fully explain the observed large n/p ratio in hypernuclear decay.

## Method

The authors construct a ΛN → NN weak transition potential using two components: a meson exchange (ME) part with six mesons (π, K, η, ρ, ω, K*) using coupling constants from the Nijmegen YN potential and SU(6)w symmetry, and a direct quark (DQ) part derived from an effective ΔS=1 weak Hamiltonian evaluated in the nonrelativistic valence quark model with quark antisymmetrization. The decay rates are calculated for Λ in symmetric nuclear matter using plane waves with short-range correlation functions, and for light hypernuclei using realistic nuclear wave functions, with careful study of form factor choices (double pole, single pole, and Gaussian) and their cutoff parameters.

## Key numbers

- Free Λ lifetime: about 263 ps
- Momentum of final state nucleons in nonmesonic decay: about 400 MeV/c (assuming zero relative momentum of initial Λ and N)
- Fermi momentum in nuclear matter: k_F ≃ 270 MeV/c
- Energy transfer q_0 ≃ (m_Λ − m_N)/2 ≃ 88 MeV
- OPE only (DP soft, Λ_π = 800 MeV): total decay rate = 1.850 Γ_Λ, Γ_p = 1.702, Γ_n = 0.148, Γ_n/Γ_p = 0.087
- All mesons (DP soft): total = 1.270 Γ_Λ, Γ_p = 1.152, Γ_n = 0.117, Γ_n/Γ_p = 0.101
- DQ only: total = 0.418 Γ_Λ, Γ_p = 0.202, Γ_n = 0.216, Γ_n/Γ_p = 1.071
- DQ + all mesons (DP soft): total = 1.884 Γ_Λ, Γ_p = 1.522, Γ_n = 0.361, Γ_n/Γ_p = 0.237
- Experimental Γ(¹²_ΛC): 1.14 ± 0.2 Γ_Λ (JJS, 1992); 0.89 ± 0.15 ± 0.03 Γ_Λ (HNO, 1995); 1.14 ± 0.08 Γ_Λ (PBY, 1995)
- Experimental Γ_n/Γ_p for ¹²_ΛC: 1.33^{+1.12}_{-0.81} (JJS, 1992); 1.87 ± 0.59^{+0.32}_{-1.00} (HNO, 1995)
- Experimental Γ(²⁸_ΛSi): 1.28 ± 0.08 Γ_Λ (PBY, 1995)
- Experimental Γ(ΛFe): 1.22 ± 0.08 Γ_Λ (PBY, 1995)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nonmesonic hypernuclear decay that employed only meson exchange mechanisms, particularly the one-pion exchange model of Block and Dalitz (1963) and the heavier meson exchange calculations of Parreño, Ramos, and Bennhold (1997). It also builds on the authors' own direct quark mechanism calculations (Inoue, Oka, and collaborators, 1995-1997) by combining both approaches coherently. The paper enables future work to refine the short-range part of the weak baryon-baryon interaction and to resolve the persistent discrepancy between theoretical and experimental n/p ratios, which remains a key open question in understanding the mechanism of nonmesonic weak decay.
