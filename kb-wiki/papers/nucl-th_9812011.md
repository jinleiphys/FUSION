# Pauli exclusion operator and binding energy of nuclear matter

> Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations are performed for nuclear matter with an exact treatment of the Pauli exclusion operator in the Bethe-Goldstone equation. The differences in the calculated binding energy, compared to the angle-average approximation, which is commonly used, are non-negligible. These difference exhibits a specific density dependence, which shifts the calculated saturation point towards smaller densities. This effect is observed for various versions of modern models for the NN interaction.

## Key claim

The exact treatment of the Pauli exclusion operator in the Bethe-Goldstone equation, as opposed to the commonly used angle-average approximation, produces non-negligible differences in the calculated binding energy of nuclear matter that shift the saturation point toward smaller densities, though the effects are not very large around and below the empirical saturation density.

## Method

Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations were performed for nuclear matter by solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation with the exact Pauli operator, which couples partial waves of different orbital angular momentum l and total angular momentum J and depends on the projection quantum number M. The results were compared to calculations using the standard angle-average approximation, which yields a Pauli operator diagonal in l and J and independent of M. Self-consistent single-particle spectra were determined using an effective mass parametrization for both the continuous choice and the conventional choice of the particle spectrum, and calculations were performed for the Bonn B, Bonn C, and Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potentials.

## Key numbers

- For the Bonn C potential with exact Pauli treatment: binding energy E = -14.97 MeV at Fermi momentum k_F = 1.42 fm^{-1}
- For the Argonne V18 potential (neutron-proton part) with exact Pauli treatment: binding energy E = -13.89 MeV at k_F = 1.37 fm^{-1}
- The angle-average approximation underestimates binding energy at low densities and overestimates it at higher densities, with the crossover density depending on the potential and single-particle spectrum choice
- The empirical binding energy of nuclear matter is -16 MeV per nucleon

## Context

This work extends the long-standing program of calculating nuclear matter saturation properties from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions without free parameters, following the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone hole-line expansion approach pioneered by Brueckner (1955), Bethe (1971), and Day (1967). The study tests the reliability of the angle-average approximation for the Pauli operator, which had been used in essentially all previous BHF calculations, and confirms earlier comparisons of G-matrix elements by Cheon and Redish (1989) and Legindgaard (1978) at low densities. The results enable more accurate many-body calculations beyond the BHF approximation, particularly for modern phase-shift-equivalent NN potentials such as those from the Nijmegen group (Stoks et al., 1994), the Argonne group (Wiringa et al., 1995), and the CD-Bonn potential (Machleidt et al., 1996).
