# Multiple period states of the superfluid Fermi gas in an optical lattice

> We study multiple period states of a two-component unpolarized superfluid Fermi gas in an optical lattice along the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) to Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC) crossover. The existence of states whose period is a multiple of the lattice spacing is a direct consequence of the non-linear behavior of the gas, which is due to the presence of the order parameter associated with superfluidity. By solving Bogoliubov-de Gennes equations for a superfluid flow with finite quasimomentum, we find that, in the BCS side of the crossover, the multiple period states can be energetically favorable compared to the normal Bloch states and their survival time against dynamical instability drastically increases, suggesting that these states can be accessible in current experiments, in sharp contrast to the situation in BECs.

## Key claim
Multiple period states of a superfluid Fermi gas in an optical lattice can be energetically favorable compared to normal Bloch states and have drastically increased survival time against dynamical instability in the BCS side of the BCS-BEC crossover, in sharp contrast to the situation in BECs where such states are energetically unfavorable and unstable.

## Method
The authors solved the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equations for a two-component unpolarized superfluid Fermi gas at zero temperature in a one-dimensional optical lattice, using Bloch-wave decomposition with supercells of period nu times the lattice constant d. Stationary solutions for multiple period states were obtained by self-consistently determining the pairing field and chemical potential, and dynamical stability was assessed through time-dependent BdG simulations in large computational boxes with periodic boundary conditions.

## Key numbers
- For s=1 and E_F/E_R=0.25 at 1/k_F a_s=-1, period-2 states are energetically lower than period-3 states in the range 0.245 less than or similar to E_F/E_R less than or similar to 0.4.
- The growth rate gamma of the fastest growing mode for period-doubled states at P=P_edge/2 is suppressed with decreasing 1/k_F a_s, with survival time tau_surv reaching approximately 900 hbar/E_R at 1/k_F a_s=-1.
- For typical experimental parameters (E_R,b=2pi x 7.3 kHz x hbar), 1 hbar/E_R = 0.0109 msec, giving tau_surv of order a few milliseconds or more in the BCS side.
- The BCS transition temperature T_c is roughly estimated as T_c ~ T_F e^{pi/2k_F a_s}, giving approximately 200 nK at 1/k_F a_s=-1, 50 nK at 1/k_F a_s=-2, and 10 nK at 1/k_F a_s=-3.

## Context
This work extends studies of multiple period states from BECs (Machholm et al., 2004) to Fermi superfluids along the BCS-BEC crossover, where such states were previously unexplored. The results contrast with BECs, where multiple period states are energetically unfavorable and unstable, and connect to the disappearance of swallowtail band structures and hysteresis in superfluid circuits (Eckel et al., 2012). The findings enable experimental observation of these states in ultracold Fermi gases using current techniques, and suggest analogs in condensed matter and nuclear physics systems such as superconducting electrons in solids and superfluid neutrons in neutron stars.
