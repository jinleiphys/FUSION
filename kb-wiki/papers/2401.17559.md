# Heavy flavor production under a strong magnetic field

> The magnetic field created in high energy nuclear collisions will affect the dynamical processes in the QCD medium, especially the heavy quark production that happens in the initial stage of the collisions. We calculate in a strong magnetic field the heavy quark production cross section for the elementary process gg {\rightarrow} Q{\bar Q} at leading order and the corresponding transverse momentum distribution in nucleus-nucleus collisions. In comparison to the QED process, the heavy quark production is dominated by the unique QCD channel with gluon self-interaction. Due to the dimension reduction of quark phase space in a strong magnetic field, the production is concentrated in a very narrow energy region above the threshold. Since the translation invariance is broken, the production becomes anisotropic in magnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper calculates heavy quark production in a strong magnetic field under the approximation of the lowest Landau level (LLL), finding that the cross section diverges at the production threshold due to the dimensional reduction of the quark phase space, and that the production is dominated by the QCD-specific gluon self-interaction channel rather than QED-like channels.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation in an external magnetic field in the Landau gauge to obtain modified quark spinors and propagators, then compute the leading-order scattering matrix elements for the process \(gg \to Q\bar{Q}\) in the \(s\), \(t\), and \(u\) channels. They restrict the calculation to the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation, which fixes the quark spin and reduces the phase space from three to two dimensions, and average over initial gluon polarization states to obtain the total cross section as a function of the center-of-mass energy, magnetic field strength, and polarization angle relative to the field direction.

## Key numbers

- The maximum magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions reaches \(5m_\pi^2 \sim 0.1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) at top RHIC energy and \(70m_\pi^2 \sim 1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) at LHC energy, where \(m_\pi\) is the pion mass.
- The charm quark mass is set to \(m = 1.5\ \mathrm{GeV}\) for numerical calculations.
- The scaled cross section \(\sigma/\alpha_s^2\) is computed for magnetic fields \(eB = 0\), \(1\), and \(10\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).
- At polarization angle \(\theta = 0\) (parallel to the magnetic field), the cross section is \(\sigma(s,B,0) = 3\pi m^2\alpha_s^2|qB|/(4s^3\chi)\), where \(\chi = \sqrt{1-4m^2/s}\).
- At polarization angle \(\theta = \pi/2\) (perpendicular to the magnetic field), the cross section is \(\sigma(s,B,\pi/2) = 14\pi m^2\alpha_s^2|qB|\chi/(3s^3) e^{-s/(4|qB|)}\).
- The transverse momentum distribution is computed for nucleon-nucleon collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02\ \mathrm{TeV}\) with rapidity range \(-0.5 < y_c, y_{\bar{c}} < 0.5\).

## Context

This work extends the study of heavy quark dynamics under strong magnetic fields beyond static properties (mass and shape of heavy flavor states) investigated by Marasinghe (2011), Alford (2013), Cho (2014), Iwasaki (2021), and Guo (2015), and beyond quarkonium dissociation studied by Singh (2017), Hasan (2018), and Hu (2022). The calculation adapts QED methods for pair creation in magnetic fields developed by Herold (1979), Melrose (1983), Kostenko (2018, 2019), and Pal (2018) to QCD, where the gluon self-interaction introduces a new production channel. The results enable quantitative predictions for heavy quark transverse momentum distributions in heavy-ion collisions at LHC energy, where the magnetic field is strongest, and suggest that the production is concentrated in a narrow energy region above threshold with strong anisotropy.
