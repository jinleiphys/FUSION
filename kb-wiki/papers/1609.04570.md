# MESON2016 -- Concluding Remarks

## Key claim

The paper argues that the hidden-charm pentaquark structures observed by LHCb, the nonstrange dibaryon ${\cal D}_{03}(2380)$, and the strange dibaryon candidates from J-PARC are best interpreted as hadronic molecules (loosely bound clusters of hadrons) rather than tightly bound multiquark states, but notes that no consensus has been reached on this interpretation across the conference.

## Method

The author selectively highlights three topics from the MESON2016 workshop, pentaquarks, dibaryons, and meson-nuclear bound states, and reviews experimental results and theoretical calculations presented at the conference. For each topic, the author compares hadronic-molecule interpretations against multiquark interpretations, citing specific experimental data (WASA-at-COSY, LHCb, J-PARC E27 and E15, CLAS, CBELSA/TAPS) and theoretical models (chiral EFT, coupled-channel Faddeev equations, QCD sum rules, quark-based calculations).

## Key numbers

- ${\cal D}_{03}(2380)$ dibaryon: mass approximately 2380 MeV, width $\Gamma \approx 70$ MeV, peaking $\approx 85$ MeV below the $\Delta\Delta$ threshold
- ${\cal D}_{12}(2150)$ dibaryon candidate from CLAS: mass 2115 $\pm$ 10 MeV, width 125 $\pm$ 25 MeV
- $K^-pp$ binding energies from J-PARC: deep candidate $B_{K^-pp}(E27) \approx 95$ MeV, shallow candidate $B_{K^-pp}(E15) \approx 15$ MeV, both relative to the $K^-pp$ threshold at 2370 MeV
- $P_c(4550)$ pentaquark width: order 40 MeV
- Predicted charmed dibaryon ${\cal C}_{\frac{3}{2}2^+}(3370)$: mass approximately 3370 MeV, below 3.4 GeV
- Predicted $DNN$ quasibound state: mass 3.5 GeV
- $\eta'$-nuclear attraction: roughly $-40$ MeV at saturation density from ELSA experiments
- $\eta$-nuclear attraction implied from the $\eta$-$\eta'$ mixing model: about $-90$ MeV at saturation density

## Context

This concluding talk reviews the state of hadronic spectroscopy as presented at MESON2016, extending the long-standing discussion of hadronic molecules versus multiquark states that began with Dalitz and Tuan's (1959) prediction of the $\Lambda(1405)$ as a $\bar K N$ quasibound state. The paper contrasts the hadronic-molecule interpretation favored for the LHCb pentaquarks (Karliner, 2015) and the COSY dibaryon ${\cal D}_{03}(2380)$ (Gal, 2016) with quark-based calculations (Lee, 2015; Lee, 2016) that fail to find tightly bound hexaquarks. For $\eta$-nuclear bound states, the paper builds on coupled-channel models of the $N^*(1535)$ resonance (GW, 2005; CS, 2013) and self-consistent calculations (Friedman, Gal, Mareš, 2013; Cieplý, Friedman, Gal, Mareš, 2014) to predict narrow $\eta$-nuclear states, while noting that $\eta'$-nuclear binding remains controversial (Moskal, 2000; Moskal, 2014; Bass, 2014). The work enables future searches for $\eta$-nuclear quasibound states at GSI using $(p,d)$ reactions and at J-PARC using $(\pi^+,p)$ reactions, as well as searches for charmed dibaryons with proton beams at GSI and pion beams at J-PARC.
