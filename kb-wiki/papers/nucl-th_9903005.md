# Hard Photons Beyond Proton-Neutron Bremsstrahlung in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We report on the study of extremely high energy photons, pions and etas, produced in intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions. Possibility of imaging the final-state phase space in these collisions by the Bose-Einstein correlations for photons is critically examined.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a cascade model (DCM) including radiative capture processes and off-shell nucleon/Δ evolution can successfully reproduce measured spectra of extremely high energy photons, pions, and etas in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions, but that the smoothness approximation commonly used in HBT correlation analyses is unreliable for photon sources at these energies.

## Method

The authors extended the Dubna Cascade Model (DCM) to include radiative capture processes such as np → d(γ,π,η…), off-shell evolution of nucleons and Δ resonances, and the πN → Nγ process as the dominant source of hard photons. They compared DCM calculations with experimental data for photon spectra in ¹⁸¹Ta + ¹⁹⁷Au at 39 MeV/A, pion transverse mass distributions in Ar + Ca at 180 MeV/A, and π⁺ production in p + Ar reactions across a broad energy range. For the HBT analysis, they derived exact and approximate two-photon correlation functions from a Gaussian source model and compared them for source sizes of 4 fm and 8 fm at T = 15 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Photon spectrum in ¹⁸¹Ta + ¹⁹⁷Au at 39 MeV/A: DCM calculations agree with experimental data "excellent up to the highest photon energies"
- Pion transverse mass spectrum in Ar + Ca at 180 MeV/A: DCM shows a deficit of hard pions for m_t > 0.4 GeV
- Meson ratio σ_η/σ_π⁰ ≤ 3 × 10⁻⁶ in data compared to 3 × 10⁻⁷ in DCM
- Experimental π⁺ data in p + Ar reactions contain a systematic error of about 40%
- HBT correlation functions calculated for source sizes R₀ = 4 fm and 8 fm at apparent temperature T = 15 MeV

## Context

This work extends the stochastic multiplicative process approach of Guardamar (1998) by implementing a detailed cascade model (DCM) that includes specific elementary processes rather than treating fluctuations globally. The paper critically examines the smoothness approximation used in HBT analyses by Pratt (1984) and others, showing it fails for photon sources at intermediate energies where the photon wavelength is comparable to source size. The results enable future tests of the time-evolution of energy fluctuations in off-equilibrium nuclear matter, particularly through measurements of extremely high transverse mass particles and improved HBT analyses using the exact correlation function rather than the approximate one.
