# Event engineering studies for heavy flavor production and hadronization in high multiplicity hadron-hadron and hadron-nucleus collisions

> Heavy flavor measurements in high multiplicity proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions at collider energies enable unique insights into their production and hadronization mechanism because experimental and theoretical uncertainties cancel in ratios of their cross-sections relative to minimum bias events. We explore such event engineering using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory to compute short distance charmonium cross-sections. The CGC is combined with heavy-quark fragmentation functions to compute $D$-meson cross-sections; for the $J/ψ$, hadronization is described employing Nonrelativistic QCD (NRQCD) and an Improved Color Evaporation model. Excellent agreement is found between the CGC computations and the LHC heavy flavor data in high multiplicity events. Event engineering in this CGC+NRQCD framework reveals a very rapid growth in the fragmentation of the $^3S_1^{[8]}$ state in rare events relative to minimum bias events.

## Key claim

The CGC+NRQCD framework shows that the relative contribution of the $^3S_1^{[8]}$ color-octet intermediate state to $J/\psi$ production grows very rapidly in high-multiplicity events compared to minimum bias events, which may help resolve the apparent violation of NRQCD universality between hadroproduction and $e^+e^-$ data.

## Method

The authors compute short-distance cross-sections for charm quark pair production using the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory, with unintegrated gluon distributions constrained by charged hadron multiplicity data. For $D$-mesons, the CGC cross-section is convoluted with heavy-quark fragmentation functions (BCFY and KKKS sets); for $J/\psi$, hadronization is described using both NRQCD (with color singlet and color octet channels) and the Improved Color Evaporation Model (ICEM). High-multiplicity events are modeled by increasing the saturation scales $Q_{sp,0}^2$ and $Q_{sA,0}^2$ in multiples of the minimum bias value $Q_0^2 = 0.168$ GeV$^2$.

## Key numbers

- Minimum bias saturation scale at $x=0.01$: $Q_0^2 = 0.168$ GeV$^2$, determined from fits to $e+p$ DIS data
- For event engineering, $Q_{sp,0}^2$ varied in range 1--3 $Q_0^2$ and $Q_{sA,0}^2$ varied in range 4--12 $Q_0^2$
- $D$-meson results shown for $p_\perp$ windows $1 < p_\perp < 2$ GeV and $2 < p_\perp < 4$ GeV in $p+p$ at $\sqrt{s}=7$ TeV and $p+A$ at $\sqrt{s}=5.02$ TeV/nucleon
- $J/\psi$ mass set to $m_{J/\psi}=3.1$ GeV; $2 m_D = 3.728$ GeV used in ICEM
- $D$-meson $p_\perp$ distributions reproduced out to $p_\perp \sim 5$ GeV for minimum bias events

## Context

This work extends the CGC+NRQCD framework previously applied to minimum bias heavy flavor production (Fujii 2013, Ma 2014, Watanabe 2015) to high-multiplicity "event engineering" studies. It contrasts with percolation models (Ferreiro 2012), dipole models (Kopeliovich 2013), and multiparton interaction models (Thakur 2017) that approximate effects contained in the CGC EFT. The finding that the $^3S_1^{[8]}$ channel dominates in rare events suggests a path to reconcile the LDME universality puzzle between hadroproduction data and BELLE $e^+e^-$ data (Zhang 2009), and enables future studies of $J/\psi$-hadron correlations at the LHC.
