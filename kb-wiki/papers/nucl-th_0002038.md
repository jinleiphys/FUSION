# Partial dynamical symmetry in a fermion system

> The relevance of the partial dynamical symmetry concept for an interacting fermion system is demonstrated. Hamiltonians with partial SU(3) symmetry are presented in the framework of the symplectic shell-model of nuclei and shown to be closely related to the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction. Implications are discussed for the deformed light nucleus $^{20}$Ne.

# Partial dynamical symmetry in a fermion system

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a family of fermionic Hamiltonians with partial SU(3) symmetry exists within the symplectic shell-model framework, and that these Hamiltonians are closely related to the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction. The claim is hedged by the fact that the partial symmetry holds only for a subset of "special" solvable states, while other states at the same excitation level mix SU(3) irreps.

## Method
The authors constructed a family of Hamiltonians H(β₀,β₂) within the symplectic shell model (Sp(6,R) ⊃ SU(3) ⊃ SO(3) dynamical symmetry basis) and proved analytically that certain states are annihilated by the operator B̂₀, making them solvable pure-SU(3) eigenstates. They then compared the spectra and eigenstates of these partial dynamical symmetry (PDS) Hamiltonians to those of the full quadrupole-quadrupole interaction for the deformed light nucleus ²⁰Ne, using least-squares fits to experimental energies and B(E2) values in model spaces up to 8ħω.

## Key numbers
- ²⁰Ne has Nσ = 48.5 (after center-of-mass removal) and Elliott SU(3) labels (λσ,μσ) = (8,0)
- B(E2; 2⁺ → 0⁺) for ground band: PDS result 20.3 W.u., experimental 20.3 ± 1.0 W.u.
- B(E2; 4⁺ → 2⁺) for ground band: PDS result 25.7 W.u., experimental 22.0 ± 2.0 W.u.
- B(E2; 6⁺ → 4⁺) for ground band: PDS result 21.8 W.u., experimental 20.0 ± 3.0 W.u.
- B(E2; 8⁺ → 6⁺) for ground band: PDS result 12.9 W.u., experimental 9.0 ± 1.3 W.u.
- Static quadrupole moment of 2⁺₁ state: PDS result -0.17 eb, experimental -0.23 ± 0.03 eb
- Interband B(E2; K=0₁, L=2⁺ → K=0₂, L=0⁺): 2.93 W.u. in 8ħω calculation, 5.69 W.u. in PDS calculation
- Interband B(E2; K=0₂, L=2⁺ → K=0₁, L=0⁺): 5.84 W.u. in 8ħω calculation, 12.6 W.u. in PDS calculation

## Context
This work extends the partial dynamical symmetry concept, previously introduced in bosonic systems by Leviatan (1996) and applied to deformed nuclei spectroscopy, to fermionic systems for the first time. It builds upon the symplectic shell model developed by Rosensteel and Rowe (1977, 1980) and the Elliott SU(3) model (Elliott, 1958). The paper shows that the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, a key ingredient in nuclear collective models, can be approximated by a PDS Hamiltonian that preserves solvable SU(3) states for certain rotational bands while mixing others. This enables future studies of giant monopole and quadrupole transitions and motivates further investigation of partial symmetries in dynamical systems.
