# Spectrum Generating Algebra for X$_{3}$ Molecules

> A new spectrum generating algebra for a unified description of rotations and vibrations in polyatomic molecules is introduced. An application to nonlinear X$_3$ molecules shows that this model (i) incorporates exactly the relevant point group, (ii) provides a complete classification of oblate top states, and (iii) treats properly both degenerate and nondegenerate vibrations.

# Spectrum Generating Algebra for X₃ Molecules

## Key claim

The paper introduces a U(7) spectrum generating algebra for triatomic molecules that incorporates the exact point group symmetry (D₃h for bent X₃ molecules) and treats both degenerate and nondegenerate vibrations within a single algebraic framework, overcoming difficulties encountered by the earlier U(4) × U(4) vibron model in describing bent molecules with degenerate vibrations.

## Method

The authors construct a U(7) model using a single scalar boson (σ) and two dipole bosons (π₁, π₂) associated with Jacobi coordinates, with only the total boson number N conserved. They perform a normal-mode analysis by introducing deformed vibrons and a coherent-state mean-field approach to extract the vibrational spectrum, and decompose the Hamiltonian into intrinsic (vibrational) and collective (rotational and rotation-vibration coupling) parts. The model is applied to fit the vibrational band origins of H₃⁺ using a simple Hamiltonian with four parameters.

## Key numbers

- The normal-mode analysis yields eigenfrequencies ε₁ = 4ξ₁R² for the symmetric stretching vibration (ν₁) and ε₂ = 4ξ₂R²(1+R²)⁻¹ for the degenerate antisymmetric stretching (ν₂a) and bending (ν₂b) vibrations.
- The rotational energy contribution from the collective Hamiltonian includes terms κ₃L(L+1) + κ₄K²y, with the splitting between +l and -l levels given by 8κ₄lK.
- In the fit to H₃⁺ band origins, weights wᵢ = 1.0 were used for the two fundamentals (the only experimentally known band origins) and wᵢ = 0.1 for other levels.
- The U(7) calculation showed significant improvement over the U₁(4) ⊗ U₂(4) fit for H₃⁺ vibrational band origins.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic approach to molecular spectroscopy previously developed as the vibron model (U(4) for diatomic molecules) and its U(4) × U(4) extension for triatomic molecules by Bijker, Dieperink, and Leviatan (1994). The earlier U(4) × U(4) model encountered difficulties in describing bent molecules with degenerate vibrations, such as nonlinear X₃ molecules, because it required separate conservation of boson numbers for each mode. The new U(7) model enables an exact treatment of point group symmetry (D₃h via the S₃ permutation group) and provides a complete classification of oblate top states using the |Ky| quantum number (analogous to Watson's G quantum number). This work enables future extensions to larger polyatomic molecules via a U(3n-2) spectrum generating algebra for n-atomic molecules with prescribed point group symmetries.
