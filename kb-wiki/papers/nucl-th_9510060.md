# Mesonic and Binding Contributions to the EMC Effect in a Relativistic Many Body Approach

> We revise the conventional nuclear effects of Fermi motion, binding and pionic effects in deep inelastic lepton scattering using a relativistic formalism for an interacting Fermi sea and the local density approximation to translate results from nuclear matter to finite nuclei. In addition we also consider effects from rho-meson renormalization in the nucleus. The use of nucleon Green's functions in terms of their spectral functions offers a precise way to account for Fermi motion and binding. On the other hand the use of many body Feynman diagrams in a relativistic framework allows one to avoid using prescriptions given in the past to introduce relativistic corrections in a non relativistic formalism. We show that with realistic nucleon spectral functions and meson nucleus selfenergies one can get a reasonable description of the EMC effect for x > 0.15, outside the shadowing region.

## Key claim

The paper claims that conventional nuclear effects (Fermi motion, binding, and mesonic contributions from pions and rho mesons) can provide a reasonable description of the EMC effect for Bjorken x > 0.15, outside the shadowing region, when treated within a fully relativistic many-body formalism using realistic nucleon spectral functions and meson-nucleus selfenergies, without needing ad hoc prescriptions for relativistic corrections.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic nucleon propagator for an interacting Fermi sea using nucleon Green's functions expressed in terms of spectral functions for holes and particles, derived from the nucleon selfenergy. They evaluate the nuclear structure function for deep inelastic electron scattering by computing the photon selfenergy in nuclear matter and applying the local density approximation to translate results to finite nuclei. The nucleonic contribution includes Fermi motion and binding effects through the spectral function, while the mesonic contribution is computed from the modification of the pion and rho-meson propagators in the medium, with subtraction of the free-nucleon pion cloud contribution to avoid double counting.

## Key numbers

- The calculations are performed for Bjorken x > 0.15, outside the shadowing region where the approach is expected to be valid.
- The normalization condition for the spectral function (eq. 21) is fulfilled at the level of 2-3% for the nuclei studied.
- The local Fermi momentum is defined as k_F(r) = [3 pi^2 rho(r) / 2]^(1/3) for symmetric nuclear matter.
- The ratio R(x) = 2 F_{2A}(x) / A F_{2D}(x) is the quantity used to compare with EMC effect data.

## Context

This work extends earlier nonrelativistic treatments of binding effects in the EMC effect (such as those by Akulinichev et al. (1985) and others) by constructing a fully relativistic formalism from the beginning, avoiding the flux factor prescriptions introduced by Frankfurt and Strikman (1988) to convert nonrelativistic wave functions into relativistic spectral functions. The pionic contribution is recalculated using many-body field theory with input checked against pionic reactions (Oset et al., 1982), muon capture, inclusive neutrino scattering, and photonuclear reactions (Carrasco and Oset, 1992). The approach enables a unified treatment of nucleonic and mesonic degrees of freedom without double counting, and the local density approximation allows application to finite nuclei using experimental density distributions.
