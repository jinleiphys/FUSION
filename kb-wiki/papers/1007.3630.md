# Chiral-particle Approach to Hadrons in an Extended Chiral ($σ,π,ω$) Mean-Field Model

> The chiral nonlinear ($σ,π,ω$) mean-field model is an extension of the conserving nonlinear (nonchiral) $σ$-$ω$ hadronic mean-field model which is thermodynamically consistent, relativistic and Lorentz-covariant mean-field theory of hadrons. In the extended chiral ($σ,π,ω$) mean-field model, all the masses of hadrons are produced by chiral symmetry breaking mechanism, which is different from other conventional chiral partner models. By comparing both nonchiral and chiral mean-field approximations, the effects of chiral symmetry breaking to the mass of $σ$-meson, coefficients of nonlinear interactions, coupling ratios of hyperons to nucleons and Fermi-liquid properties are investigated in nuclear matter, hyperonic matter, and neutron stars.

## Key claim

The extended chiral ($\sigma,\pi,\omega$) mean-field model generates all hadron masses ($M_N, m_\sigma, m_\pi, m_\omega$) via chiral symmetry breaking from a Lagrangian that initially contains no mass terms, unlike conventional chiral partner models that introduce the $\omega$ meson externally; the mass of the $\sigma$ meson is determined self-consistently by the saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter and the maximum mass of neutron stars, yielding $m_\sigma \approx 120.0$ MeV.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral-invariant Lagrangian with an interaction potential $V(\sigma^2 + \boldsymbol{\pi}^2 - a \omega_\mu^2)$ and a chiral symmetry breaking term $\varepsilon \sigma$, then apply spontaneous symmetry breaking to generate hadron masses. In the mean-field (Hartree) approximation, pion contributions vanish, and the classical fields $\phi_0$ and $\omega_0$ are solved from coupled equations of motion derived from the energy density, with thermodynamic consistency enforced by requiring $\delta \mathcal{E}/\delta \Sigma = 0$. Vacuum fluctuation corrections are included via dimensional regularization and counterterms to render divergent integrals finite, and the model is applied to symmetric nuclear matter, $\beta$-equilibrium ($n,p,e$) matter, and neutron star properties.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: $\rho_0 = 0.148$ fm$^{-3}$ (Fermi momentum $k_F = 1.30$ fm$^{-1}$), with binding energy $\mathcal{E}/\rho_B - M = -15.75$ MeV.
- Nucleon effective mass at saturation: $M_N^\ast / M_N \approx 0.60$; sigma meson effective mass: $m_\sigma^\ast / m_\sigma \approx 1.09$; omega meson effective mass: $m_\omega^\ast / m_\omega \approx 1.04$.
- Incompressibility at saturation: $K = 371$ MeV; symmetry energy at saturation: $a_4 = 17.4$ MeV.
- Mass of sigma meson determined from saturation and neutron star maximum mass: $m_\sigma \approx 120.0$ MeV (with pion mass $m_\pi = 139.0$ MeV and omega mass $m_\omega = 783.0$ MeV used as inputs).
- Maximum neutron star mass: $M_{\text{max}} = 2.60$ $M_\odot$ (central energy density $\mathcal{E}_c = 1.58$ GeV/fm$^3$, radius $R = 12.8$ km).
- Coupling constants: $g = 2.4095$, $g_\omega = 13.4232$.
- Ratio $a = m_\omega^2 / m_\pi^2 \approx 31.65$ in the nuclear domain.

## Context

This work extends the conserving nonlinear (nonchiral) $\sigma$-$\omega$ mean-field model (Walecka, 1974; Serot and Walecka, 1986) by incorporating chiral symmetry breaking to generate all hadron masses, contrasting with conventional chiral partner models that treat $\omega$ as an external field (Walecka, 1995; Serot, 1997). The chiral model clarifies the origin of nonlinear coupling constants and their bounds, which in nonchiral approaches (Uechi, 2000) were constrained only by thermodynamic consistency and empirical data. The predicted $\Lambda$-hyperon coupling ratio $r^\omega_{\Lambda N} \sim 1.0$ from thermodynamic consistency differs from the SU(6) quark model value of $2/3$ (Jaffe, 1977; Gibson and Schaffner-Bielich, 2000), leading to significant differences in hyperonic matter properties and neutron star masses. The model enables future extension to include $\rho$ mesons (Serot, 1992) for quantitative comparison with symmetry energy data.
