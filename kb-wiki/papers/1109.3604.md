# Electric Dipole Moments of Light Nuclei From Chiral Effective Field Theory

> We set up the framework for the calculation of electric dipole moments (EDMs) of light nuclei using the systematic expansion provided by chiral effective field theory (EFT). We take into account parity (P) and time-reversal (T) violation which, at the quark-gluon level, originates from the QCD vacuum angle and dimension-six operators capturing physics beyond the Standard Model. We argue that EDMs of light nuclei can be expressed in terms of six low-energy constants that appear in the P- and T-violating nuclear potential and electric current. As examples, we calculate the EDMs of the deuteron, the triton, and 3He in leading order in the EFT expansion.

## Key claim

The paper argues that electric dipole moments (EDMs) of light nuclei can be expressed in terms of six low-energy constants (LECs) appearing in the parity- and time-reversal-violating nuclear potential and electric current, rather than the three pion-nucleon couplings traditionally assumed; for the QCD vacuum angle term, the formally leading contribution to the deuteron EDM vanishes due to its isoscalar character, requiring extension to the first non-vanishing order.

## Method

The authors set up a chiral effective field theory (EFT) framework with nonperturbative one-pion exchange to calculate EDMs of light nuclei, incorporating parity- and time-reversal-violating sources from the QCD vacuum angle and dimension-six operators. They derive the leading-order parity- and time-reversal-violating potentials and currents, then compute the EDMs of the deuteron, triton, and helium-3 using a hybrid approach that combines chiral EFT interactions with realistic phenomenological nucleon-nucleon potentials from the literature (Stoks 1994, Wiringa 1995). The calculations are performed using existing numerical codes from Liu (2004) and Stetcu (2008), recast in the chiral EFT framework with power counting to estimate theoretical uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Neutron EDM experimental bound: |d_n| < 2.9 × 10⁻²⁶ e cm
- Proton EDM indirect bound from ¹⁹⁹Hg: |d_p| < 7.9 × 10⁻²⁵ e cm
- QCD vacuum angle constraint from neutron EDM: θ̅ ≲ 10⁻¹⁰
- Pion decay constant: F_π ≃ 185 MeV
- Pion-nucleon coupling constant: g_{NNπ} = 13.07, giving g_A = 1.29
- Pion mass splitting: m_{π±}² − m_{π⁰}² = (35.5 MeV)²
- Nucleon mass splitting: m_n − m_p = 1.29 MeV, with quark-mass contribution δm_N = 2.26 ± 0.57 ± 0.42 ± 0.10 MeV from lattice simulations
- Nucleon anomalous magnetic moments: κ₀ = −0.12 (isoscalar), κ₁ = 3.7 (isovector)
- Isospin-violating LEC β₁ from Nijmegen partial-wave analysis: β₁ = (0 ± 9) × 10⁻³

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of nuclear EDMs within traditional meson-exchange frameworks by Liu (2004) and Stetcu (2008), which used realistic nuclear-force models and a general parity- and time-reversal-violating nucleon-nucleon interaction expressed in terms of three non-derivative pion-nucleon couplings. The chiral EFT approach provides a direct link to QCD and exploits the chiral properties of fundamental parity- and time-reversal-violating sources, enabling systematic improvement and uncertainty estimation. The framework enables separation of different fundamental sources (QCD vacuum angle, quark EDM, quark chromo-EDM, gluon chromo-EDM, and four-quark operators) through measurements of EDMs in different light nuclei, building on earlier work by de Vries (2011) that showed a deuteron EDM measurement combined with nucleon EDMs could partially distinguish these sources.
