# Astrophysical neutron capture rates in s- and r-process nucleosynthesis

> Astrophysical neutron capture rates of light and heavy nuclei have been measured and calculated. For the measurements the activation technique was applied at the 3.75 MV Karlsruhe Van de Graaff accelerator, and at the Geel electron linear accelerator (GELINA) the time-of-flight (TOF) method was used. The calculations were performed using direct and compound nuclear capture models.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that astrophysical neutron capture rates for both light and heavy nuclei can be reliably determined through a combination of activation and time-of-flight measurements, but the interpretation of s-process branchings (especially for Pt isotopes) depends critically on the assumed neutron density, with the standard value of \(5.0\times 10^8\) cm\(^{-3}\) failing to reproduce the solar abundance of \(^{192}\)Pt unless a lower density (\(\le 3.2\times 10^8\) cm\(^{-3}\)) is adopted.

## Method

Activation measurements were performed at the Karlsruhe 3.75 MV Van de Graaff accelerator using a cyclic activation technique, where samples were irradiated by neutrons from the \(^7\)Li(p,n) and T(p,n) reactions and cross sections were determined relative to the well-known \(^{197}\)Au cross section. Time-of-flight (TOF) measurements were carried out at the Geel electron linear accelerator (GELINA) using a 60 m flight path with C\(_6\)D\(_6\) liquid scintillation detectors. Theoretical calculations employed direct capture (DC) and compound nuclear (CN) capture models, with statistical model calculations performed using the code SMOKER.

## Key numbers

- Neutron capture cross section of \(^{26}\)Mg measured at 33 keV using a neutron spectrum from the \(^7\)Li(p,n) reaction threshold, avoiding excitation of the 68.7 keV resonance; the cross section decreases up to 30 keV then increases toward higher energies due to a transition from s-wave to p-wave direct capture.
- \(^{48}\)Ca measurement at 52 keV confirms 1/v behavior of the cross section, consistent with direct s-wave capture; no destructive interference with a hypothetical 1/2\(^{+}\) s-wave resonance at 1.5 keV was found.
- Maxwellian average capture (MAC) cross sections of stable Pt isotopes (\(^{190,194,196,198}\)Pt) measured at \(kT = 30\) keV; experimental values agree with theoretical calculations by Beer and Rauscher (1997) within quoted uncertainties.
- Neutron density of \(5.0\times 10^8\) cm\(^{-3}\) deduced from all s-process branchings underproduces the solar \(^{192}\)Pt abundance; consistency requires neutron densities \(\le 3.2\times 10^8\) cm\(^{-3}\).
- MAC cross section of \(^{209}\)Bi at \(kT = 30\) keV reported by Mughabghab (1984) is identified as an erroneous calculation from resonance parameters.
- Ba isotopic ratios from s-process calculations: SP model gives \(^{138}\)Ba/\(^{136}\)Ba = 6.22 (with \(\tau_0 = 0.188\) mbarn\(^{-1}\)), DP model gives 6.22 (with \(\tau_0 = 0.102\) mbarn\(^{-1}\)); the ratio \((\sigma_{138})^{-1}/(\sigma_{136})^{-1} = 13.26\) at \(kT = 8\) keV.

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of neutron capture on light isotopes (e.g., \(^{36}\)S by Beer (1995), \(^{48}\)Ca by Beer (1996) and Mohr (1997)) and heavy bottleneck isotopes (\(^{138}\)Ba, \(^{208}\)Pb) to provide improved rates for s- and r-process nucleosynthesis calculations. The new Pt isotope measurements (Beer and Rauscher 1997) are compared with earlier theoretical estimates by Allen (1971), Holmes (1976), Harris (1981), and Cowan (1991). The results enable refined s-process branching analyses in the Os-Pt mass region, particularly for the \(^{191}\)Os and \(^{192}\)Ir branchings, and require determination of the MAC cross sections of unstable \(^{191}\)Os and \(^{192}\)Ir to better than 20% to confirm the freeze-out effect of the \(^{22}\)Ne(\(\alpha\),n) neutron source.
