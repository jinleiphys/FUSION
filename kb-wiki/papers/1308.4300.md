# Chirally motivated separable potential model for $ηN$ amplitudes

> We analyze the $ηN$ interaction using a coupled channel separable potential model that implements the chiral symmetry. The model predicts an $ηN$ stattering length $\Re a_{ηN} \approx 0.7$ fm and in-medium subthreshold attraction most likely sufficient to generate $η$-nuclear bound states. The energy dependence of the $ηN$ amplitude and pole content of the model are discussed. An idea of the same origin of the baryon resonances $N^{\star}(1535)$ and $N^{\star}(1650)$ is presented.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a chirally motivated coupled-channel separable potential model predicts an $\eta N$ scattering length $\Re a_{\eta N} \approx 0.7$ fm and in-medium subthreshold attraction likely sufficient to generate $\eta$-nuclear bound states, but this result is conditional on the inclusion of next-to-leading order (NLO) terms and a specific treatment of the $\pi \pi N$ channel, with the quality of the fit depending on the chosen value of the pion-nucleon sigma term ($\sigma_{\pi N} = 20$, $30$, or $40$ MeV).

## Method

The authors constructed a coupled-channel separable potential model for $s$-wave meson-baryon interactions, implementing chiral SU(3) symmetry at leading order (Tomozawa-Weinberg term) and next-to-leading order (NLO contact terms), with Yamaguchi form factors for off-shell regularization. They solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in free space for the $I=1/2$ channels ($\pi N$, $\eta N$, $K\Lambda$, $K\Sigma$) and $I=3/2$ channels ($\pi N$, $K\Sigma$), fitting four inverse-range parameters and four NLO $d$-couplings to SAID $\pi N$ $S_{11}$ and $S_{31}$ partial-wave amplitudes (up to 1600 MeV CMS energy) and $\pi^{-}p \rightarrow \eta n$ total cross-section data from the Crystal Ball collaboration and older experiments.

## Key numbers

* $\Re a_{\eta N} \approx 0.7$ fm for the $\eta N$ scattering length predicted by the model.
* For the NLO20$_{\eta}$ model: $\chi^{2}/dof = 1.33$; inverse ranges $\alpha_{\pi N} = 597$ MeV, $\alpha_{\eta N} = 1293$ MeV, $\alpha_{K\Lambda} = 256$ MeV, $\alpha_{K\Sigma} = 1032$ MeV.
* For the NLO30$_{\eta}$ model: $\chi^{2}/dof = 1.46$; inverse ranges $\alpha_{\pi N} = 538$ MeV, $\alpha_{\eta N} = 1635$ MeV, $\alpha_{K\Lambda} = 250$ MeV, $\alpha_{K\Sigma} = 939$ MeV.
* For the NLO40$_{\eta}$ model: $\chi^{2}/dof = 1.95$; inverse ranges $\alpha_{\pi N} = 508$ MeV, $\alpha_{\eta N} = 2000$ MeV, $\alpha_{K\Lambda} = 250$ MeV, $\alpha_{K\Sigma} = 842$ MeV.
* The SAID database gives $(1 - \eta^{2}) = 0.917$ at $\sqrt{s} = 1540$ MeV, corresponding to a total reaction cross section of about 3.5 mb.
* The experimental $\pi^{-}p \rightarrow \eta n$ cross section is matched by scaling the calculated $I=1/2$ cross section by a factor of 1/1.2 to account for the missing $\pi \pi N$ channel.

## Context

This work extends the chirally motivated separable potential approach previously applied to the $\bar{K}N$ system by Cieply and Smejkal (2010, 2012) to the $\eta N$ system, following the earlier separable potential model of Kaiser, Siegel, and Weise (1995) which was restricted to a narrow energy interval above the $\eta N$ threshold. The results are compared with unitary coupled-channel approaches using Bethe-Salpeter equations by Nieves and Ruiz Arriola (2001), Inoue et al. (2002), and Mai et al. (2012), which predicted $\Re a_{\eta N} \approx 0.3$ fm, and with $K$-matrix fits by Green and Wycech (2005) giving $\Re a_{\eta N} \approx 1$ fm. The model enables improved predictions for the subthreshold $\eta N$ interaction relevant to the possible existence of $\eta$-nuclear bound states, as studied by Friedman, Gal, and Mares (2013).
