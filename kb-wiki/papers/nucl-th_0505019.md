# Review of HBT or Bose-Einstein correlations in high energy heavy ion collisions

> A brief review is given on the discovery and the first five decades of the Hanbury Brown - Twiss effect and its generalized applications in high energy nuclear and particle physics, that includes a meta-review. Interesting and inspiring new directions are also highlighted, including for example source imaging, lepton and photon interferometry, non-Gaussian shape analysis as well as many other new directions. Existing models are compared to two-particle correlation measurements and the so-called RHIC HBT puzzle is resolved. Evidence for a (directional) Hubble flow is presented and the conclusion is confirmed by a successful description of the pseudorapidity dependence of the elliptic flow as measured in Au+Au collisions by the PHOBOS Collaboration.

## Key claim

This review resolves the so-called "RHIC HBT puzzle" by showing that several models (including the Buda-Lund hydro model, blast-wave model, and Cracow model) can simultaneously describe HBT radii, single-particle spectra, and elliptic flow data from Au+Au collisions at RHIC, with the common feature being a nearly direction-independent Hubble flow where the transverse and longitudinal Hubble constants are equal within errors.

## Method

The author reviews the first 50 years of Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) effect applications in high energy nuclear and particle physics, comparing existing theoretical models to two-particle correlation measurements from RHIC experiments (STAR, PHENIX, PHOBOS). The paper identifies models that pass the "HBT test" by successfully describing the measured HBT radius parameters, then further constrains these models by comparing them to single-particle spectra and elliptic flow data, extracting Hubble constants from the successful fits.

## Key numbers

- The transverse and longitudinal Hubble constants extracted from fits to Au+Au data at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV are equal within errors: H_long = H_transv = 0.13 +/- 0.02 fm^{-1}
- The critical temperature from lattice QCD for the transition to quark-gluon plasma is T_c = 164 +/- 2 MeV
- The transition from hadronic to non-hadronic matter occurs at baryochemical potentials up to mu_B approximately 350 MeV
- The Narrabri Observatory had parabolic reflectors of 22 feet diameter and focal length 36 feet, mounted on a circular railway truck of 618 feet diameter (73% of the 843 feet diameter of the AGS accelerator)
- Seven models were found to pass the HBT test at RHIC (listed in the text)

## Context

This review extends the line of work on particle interferometry (femtoscopy) in high energy heavy ion collisions, covering developments from the first HBT measurement of Sirius by Hanbury Brown and Twiss (1956) through five decades of applications. The paper resolves the "RHIC HBT puzzle" that arose when early predictions by Rischke and Gyulassy (1995) of R_out/R_side > 1 for a first-order QGP phase transition were excluded by STAR and PHENIX data, while a 1994 prediction by Csorgo and Csernai of approximately equal HBT radii remains consistent with data. The work enables further studies of non-Gaussian correlation shapes (Levy index of stability), penetrating probe interferometry (photons and leptons), and the search for squeezed states and chiral symmetry restoration at RHIC and LHC energies.
