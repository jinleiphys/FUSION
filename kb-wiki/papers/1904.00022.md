# Charting the coming synergy between lattice QCD and high-energy phenomenology

> Building upon the PDFSense framework developed in Ref. [1], we perform a comprehensive analysis of the sensitivity of present and future high-energy data to a number of quantities commonly evaluated in lattice gauge theory, with a particular focus on the integrated Mellin moments of nucleon parton distribution functions (PDFs), such as $\langle x \rangle_{u^+ - d^+}$ and $\langle x \rangle_{g}$, as well as $x$-dependent quark quasi-distributions -- in particular, that of the isovector combination. Our results demonstrate the potential for lattice calculations and phenomenological quark distributions informed by high-energy experimental data to cooperatively improve the picture of the nucleon's collinear structure. This will increasingly be the case as computational resources for lattice calculations further expand, and QCD global analyses continue to grow in sophistication. Our sensitivity analysis suggests that a future lepton-hadron collider would be especially instrumental in providing phenomenological constraints to lattice observables.

# Charting the coming synergy between lattice QCD and high-energy phenomenology

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that present and future high-energy experimental data can provide meaningful phenomenological constraints on lattice QCD observables, specifically the integrated Mellin moments of nucleon PDFs and x-dependent quark quasi-distributions, but this synergy will become fully realized only as computational resources for lattice calculations expand and QCD global analyses grow in sophistication. The authors hedge that lattice calculations currently have considerably larger uncertainties than phenomenologically determined moments, and that limitations remain due to the early stage of theoretical development of the LaMET procedure for quasi-distributions.

## Method
The authors extend the PDFSense framework (Wang et al., 2018) to compute Pearson correlations and a new statistical indicator called L2 sensitivity between experimental data residuals and PDF-derived lattice observables, using the CT14HERA2 NNLO PDF ensemble with its 56 Hessian error sets. They evaluate integrated Mellin moments by numerically integrating PDFs over x from approximately 10^-7 to 0.999 at a factorization scale of 2 GeV, matching the scale commonly used in lattice calculations. For quasi-distributions, they compute the isovector combination u-d using the LaMET matching relation that connects quasi-distributions to phenomenological PDFs via a perturbatively calculable kernel.

## Context
This work extends the PDFSense framework developed by Wang et al. (2018) to systematically map the sensitivity of high-energy experimental data to lattice QCD observables, building on the CT14HERA2 NNLO global analysis (Hou et al., 2016). It addresses the long-standing goal of using lattice QCD to constrain PDFs, which has previously been pursued through Mellin moment calculations (Detmold et al., 2003) and more recently through the quasi-distribution approach introduced by Ji (2013) and the pseudo-PDF concept of Radyushkin (2017). The analysis enables future work where lattice calculations with improved precision could be implemented as chi-squared penalties in global QCD analyses, and it identifies that a future lepton-hadron collider would be especially instrumental in providing phenomenological constraints to lattice observables.
