# Long term prospects for double beta decay

> In rather general terms the long term perspective of double beta decay is discussed. All important experimental parameters are investigated as well as the status of nuclear matrix element issues. The link with other neutrino physics results and options to disentangle the underlying physics process are presented.

## Key claim
The paper argues that if the claimed evidence for neutrinoless double beta decay in germanium-76 is not confirmed, the next benchmark sensitivity must reach effective Majorana neutrino masses of about 50 meV, requiring an improvement of 1-2 orders of magnitude in background reduction. The author emphasizes that measuring at least 3-4 different isotopes is mandatory to extract a reliable neutrino mass value, given uncertainties in nuclear matrix elements and the possibility of multiple beyond-Standard-Model mechanisms.

## Method
The paper surveys the experimental parameters governing double beta decay searches: source mass, isotopic abundance, detection efficiency, energy resolution, and background index. It reviews the status of nuclear matrix element calculations from nuclear shell model, quasiparticle random phase approximation, and interacting boson model approaches, and discusses how charge-exchange reactions and ft-value measurements can provide experimental input to constrain these calculations. The author also examines how measurements of multiple isotopes, single-electron energy spectra, and opening angles between emitted electrons could discriminate between the neutrino-mass mechanism and alternative lepton-number-violating processes such as right-handed weak currents.

## Key numbers
- Current best background levels achieved are about 0.1 counts/keV/kg/yr in the neutrinoless double beta decay peak range
- The hierarchical neutrino mass models require sensitivity to effective Majorana masses of about 50 meV
- Six isotopes have Q-values above 2.614 MeV, the energy of the highest gamma line from natural decay chains (from thallium-208 decay)
- For the next generation, contamination levels down to microBq/kg must be achieved
- The phase space factor for neutrinoless double beta decay scales with Q-value to the fifth power
- The axial-vector constant g_A is typically chosen as either 1 or 1.25 in nuclear matrix element calculations

## Context
This paper extends the discussion of double beta decay experimental prospects beyond the near-term review by Cremonesi (2009) and assumes the claimed evidence in germanium-76 from Klapdor-Kleingrothaus (2004) will not be confirmed. It builds on nuclear matrix element calculations from Caurier (2008) for shell model, Simkovic (2009) and Kortelainen (2007) for QRPA, and Iachello (2009) for the interacting boson model, as well as the experimental program proposed by Zuber (2005) to provide input via charge-exchange reactions. The work enables a roadmap for next-generation experiments by identifying the critical trade-offs between isotope choice, background reduction, energy resolution, and the need for multiple isotope measurements to disentangle the underlying physics mechanism.
