# Time Evolution of Thermal Photon Elliptic Flow

> Elliptic flow of thermal photons has a great potential to explore the early time dynamics and evolution of Quark Gluon Plasma. $p_T$ dependent temporal contours for photon spectra and elliptic flow from quark matter and hadronic phases, as well as the $p_T$ integrated results show gradual build-up of flow with time and relative contributions from different phases to that very clearly. Unlike hadrons, photon flow is quite sensitive to the initial thermalization time $τ_0$, and its value can be estimated with the experimental determination of $v_2$.

## Key claim
The elliptic flow of thermal photons is sensitive to the initial thermalization time τ₀, unlike hadronic flow, and this sensitivity can be used to estimate τ₀ from experimental measurements of v₂, but only under the condition that the particle multiplicity is held fixed.

## Method
The authors used an ideal hydrodynamic model to simulate 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC with impact parameter b=7 fm and initial thermalization time τ₀=0.2 fm. Photon production rates from quark matter were taken from Arnold et al. and from hot hadronic gas from Turbide et al. The time evolution of pT-dependent photon spectra and elliptic flow v₂ was computed separately for quark matter and hadronic matter phases, along with their sum, and the pT-integrated v₂ was tracked as a function of proper time τ.

## Key numbers
- For impact parameter b=7 fm and τ₀=0.2 fm, the quark matter v₂ saturates within a time period of 5 fm.
- The freeze-out completion time τ_f is approximately 12 fm.
- At large pT (> 3 GeV), most photons from quark matter are emitted within a time period of 2 fm.
- Hadronic matter photon contributions become significant only after 4-5 fm.
- For the τ₀ sensitivity study at b=6 fm, τ₀ values ranging from 0.2 to 1.0 fm were tested.
- Thermal photon v₂ dominates over prompt and jet photon contributions for pT < 5 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier hydrodynamic calculation of thermal photon elliptic flow at RHIC (Chatterjee et al., 2008) by providing detailed time-resolved contours of spectra and v₂ from quark and hadronic phases. The photon production rates from Arnold et al. and Turbide et al. are used, which previously gave good descriptions of single photon data at RHIC and SPS energies. The finding that photon v₂ is sensitive to τ₀ while hadron v₂ is not enables a new method to extract the initial thermalization time from experimental v₂ measurements, complementing earlier work on τ₀ constraints from hadronic observables.
