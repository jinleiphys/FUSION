# Role of the short-range dynamics in the formation of $D^{(*)}\bar D^{(*)}/B^{(*)}\bar B^{(*)}$ hadronic molecules

> We investigate potential hadronic molecular states in the $D^{(*)}\bar D^{(*)}$ and $B^{(*)}\bar B^{*}$ systems using light meson exchange interactions. Our analysis focuses on coupled-channel systems with spin-parity quantum numbers $J^{PC}=0^{++}$, $1^{+\pm}$ and $2^{++}$, examining how the $δ(r)$ potential affects states near threshold. Using coupled-channel analysis, we reproduce the $X(3872)$ mass with a given cutoff for the $(I)J^{PC}=(0)1^{++}$ state, finding a minimal impact from the $δ(r)$ term. At this cutoff, both the $(0)0^{++}$ state near the $D\bar D$ threshold and the $(0)1^{+-}$ state near the $D\bar D^*$ threshold show less sensitivity to the $δ(r)$ term compared to the three states-$(0)0^{++}$, $(0)1^{+-}$, and $(0)2^{++}$-near the $D^*\bar D^*$ threshold. As anticipated, the $B^{(*)}\bar B^{*}$ systems exhibit similar behavior but with stronger binding due to their larger reduced mass. These findings suggest promising directions for future experimental searches, particularly in the isoscalar sector, which could substantially advance our understanding of exotic tetraquark states.

## Key claim

The authors show that the short-range δ(r) term in the one-boson-exchange potential has minimal impact on the X(3872) state and on states near the D\bar D and D\bar D* thresholds, but significantly affects states near the D*\bar D* threshold, with the classification of certain states as bound or virtual depending critically on a parameter a that controls the strength of the δ(r) term.

## Method

The authors solve the stationary Schrodinger equation for D(*)\bar D(*) and B(*)\bar B* systems using a one-boson-exchange potential that includes SU(3) vector-nonet mesons, pseudoscalar octet mesons, and the sigma meson. They introduce a parameter a to phenomenologically adjust the strength of the δ(r) term in the potential, with a=0 fully including the term and a=1 excluding it, and analyze pole trajectories in the complex energy plane as a varies from 0 to 1 with a fixed cutoff calibrated to reproduce the X(3872) mass.

## Key numbers

- The X(3872) mass is reproduced with a cutoff Λ = 1.11 GeV for the (I)JPC = (0)1++ state, with minimal dependence on the parameter a
- The sigma meson mass is set to mσ = 600.0 MeV, pion mass to mπ = 138.0 MeV, eta mass to mη = 547.9 MeV, rho mass to mρ = 770.7 MeV, and omega mass to mω = 782.0 MeV
- Coupling constants used: gS = 0.76, g = -0.59, β = 0.9, λ = 0.56 GeV-1, and gV = 5.9
- The pion decay constant is taken as fπ = 132 MeV
- In the (0)0++ systems, a virtual pole is found approximately 1 MeV below the D\bar D threshold
- The cutoff parameter Λ is varied from 1 to 2 GeV in the single-channel analysis

## Context

This work extends the one-boson-exchange model approach previously applied to hidden-charm pentaquark systems by Yalikun et al. (2021) to the D(*)\bar D(*) and B(*)\bar B* tetraquark systems. The analysis reproduces the X(3872) as a molecular state, consistent with the compositeness analysis by Ji et al. (2025), and predicts a C-parity partner of X(3872) near the D\bar D* threshold that was preliminarily observed by the COMPASS collaboration (2017). The results suggest that isoscalar states are more promising for experimental searches than isovector states, where no poles are found within the cutoff range, and that the B(*)\bar B* systems exhibit stronger binding due to larger reduced mass.
