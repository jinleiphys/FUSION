# Update on $|V_{us}|$ and $|V_{us}/V_{ud}|$ from semileptonic kaon and pion decays

> We discuss the recent progress in the study of semileptonic kaon and pion decays, including new experimental results, improved electroweak radiative corrections, form factor calculations and isospin-breaking effects. As a result, we obtain $|V_{us}|=0.22309(40)(39)(3)$ from kaon semileptonic decays and $|V_{us}/V_{ud}|=0.22908(66)(41)(40)(2)(1)$ from the ratio between the kaon and pion semileptonic decay rates. We report an apparent violation of the top-row Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix unitarity at a $3.2\sim 5.6σ$ level, and a discrepancy at a $2.2σ$ level between the value of $|V_{us}/V_{ud}|$ determined from the vector and axial charged weak interactions. Prospects for future improvements in those comparative precision tests involving $|V_{ud}|$, $|V_{us}|$ and their implications for physics beyond the Standard Model are described.

## Key claim

The paper reports an apparent violation of first-row Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix unitarity at a significance level of 3.2 sigma, which could increase to as much as 5.6 sigma if nuclear structure uncertainties in the determination of |V_ud| were significantly reduced while its central value remained unchanged.

## Method

The authors performed a combined analysis of six independent K_ell3 decay channels (K_L e, K_S e, K^+ e, K_L mu, K_S mu, K^+ mu) using updated experimental inputs from the Particle Data Group, improved electroweak radiative corrections from a novel framework based on Sirlin's representation, dispersive parameterizations of form factors, and lattice QCD inputs for isospin-breaking corrections and the Kpi form factor at zero momentum transfer. They constructed a full covariance matrix accounting for correlations among experimental and theoretical uncertainties to obtain weighted averages of |V_us f_+^K(0)|, then divided by the lattice QCD form factor |f_+^K(0)| = 0.9698(17) to extract |V_us|. For the ratio |V_us/V_ud|, they combined the K_ell3 and pion semileptonic decay rates using updated radiative corrections for both processes.

## Key numbers

* |V_us| = 0.22309(40)(39)(3) from kaon semileptonic decays, where the uncertainties are from the K_ell3 data, lattice QCD form factor, and higher-order electroweak effects respectively
* |V_us/V_ud| = 0.22908(66)(41)(40)(2)(1) from the ratio of kaon to pion semileptonic decay rates
* |V_ud| = 0.97373(11)(9)(27) from superallowed nuclear beta decays, with uncertainties from experiment, single-nucleon radiative corrections, and nuclear structure respectively
* First-row CKM unitarity deficit: |V_ud|^2 + |V_us|^2 - 1 = -0.0021(2)(2)(5)(2)(2), with uncertainties from V_ud experimental, V_ud radiative corrections, V_ud nuclear structure, V_us from K_ell3, and V_us lattice respectively
* The K_S mu branching ratio was measured for the first time: BR(K_S mu) = 4.56(20) x 10^(-4)
* The lattice QCD form factor used: |f_+^K(0)| = 0.9698(17) from N_f = 2+1+1 calculations

## Context

This work extends the comprehensive 2010 FlaviaNet Working Group Report (Antonelli et al., 2010) by incorporating new experimental results, improved electroweak radiative corrections from Seng et al. (2021), and updated lattice QCD inputs from the Flavor Lattice Averaging Group (FLAG, 2019). The analysis reveals a 2.2 sigma discrepancy between |V_us/V_ud| extracted from vector-current semileptonic decays (R_V) and from axial-vector leptonic decays (R_A = Gamma(K -> mu nu)/Gamma(pi -> mu nu)), suggesting either a difference between vector and axial-vector derived CKM matrix elements or a problem with the lattice QCD form factor input. The results motivate further experimental improvements, particularly the proposed PIONEER experiment which would improve the pion semileptonic decay rate precision by a factor of 3 or better, and call for ab-initio calculations of nuclear structure corrections in superallowed beta decays to reduce the dominant uncertainty in |V_ud|.
