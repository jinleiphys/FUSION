# Fixing effective range parameters in elastic $־±$-$^{12}$C scattering: an impact on resonant $2_4^+$ state of $^{16}$O and $S_{E2}$ factor of $^{12}$C($־±$,$־³$)$^{16}$O

> Elastic $־±$-$^{12}$C scattering for $l=2$ and $E2$ transition of radiative $־±$ capture on $^{12}$C, $^{12}$C($־±$,$־³$)$^{16}$O, are studied in cluster effective field theory. Due to the problem in fixing the asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) of the subthreshold $2_1^+$ state of $^{16}$O, equivalently, the effective range parameters of the $2_1^+$ state, from the elastic scattering data, we introduce the conditions that lead to a large value of the ANC. In addition, $d$-wave phase shift data of the elastic scattering up to the $־±$ energy, $E_־±=10$~MeV, which contain resonant $2_4^+$ state of $^{16}$O, are also introduced in the study. Applying the conditions, the parameters of the $S$ matrix of the elastic scattering for $l=2$ are fitted to the phase shift data, and the fitted parameters are employed in the calculation of astrophysical $S_{E2}$ factor of $^{12}$C($־±$,$־³$)$^{16}$O; we extrapolate the $S_{E2}$ factor to the Gamow-peak energy, $E_G=0.3$~MeV. We find that the conditions lead to significant effects in the observables of the $2_4^+$ state of $^{16}$O and the estimate of the $S_{E2}$ factor at $E_G$ and confirm that the ANC of the $2_1^+$ of $^{16}$O cannot be determined by the phase shift data for $l=2$.

## Key claim

By imposing conditions on the effective range parameters that force a large asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) for the subthreshold $2_1^+$ state of $^{16}$O, the author finds significant effects on the extracted properties of the resonant $2_4^+$ state and the extrapolated $S_{E2}$ factor at the Gamow-peak energy, while confirming that the ANC cannot be determined from elastic scattering phase shift data alone.

## Method

The author employs cluster effective field theory to construct the $S$ matrix for elastic $\alpha$-$^{12}$C scattering for $l=2$, including the subthreshold $2_1^+$ state and three resonant states ($2_2^+$, $2_3^+$, $2_4^+$) of $^{16}$O. The effective range parameters ($r_2$, $P_2$, $Q_2$) are fitted to $d$-wave phase shift data from Tischhauser et al. (2009) up to $E_\alpha=6.62$ MeV and Bruno et al. (1975) up to $E_\alpha=10$ MeV, both with and without applying conditions that force the inverse propagator to be a simply decreasing function. The fitted parameters are then used to calculate the $E2$ transition amplitudes for $^{12}$C($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^{16}$O, with two additional parameters fitted to experimental $S_{E2}$ factor data below the $2_2^+$ resonance energy.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies of subthreshold states: $B_1=0.045$ MeV for the $1_1^-$ state and $B_2=0.245$ MeV for the $2_1^+$ state of $^{16}$O
- Binding momentum of the $2_1^+$ state: $\gamma_2 = 37.0$ MeV
- ANC of the $2_1^+$ state from $\alpha$-transfer reactions: $|C_b|_2 = (1.11\pm 0.11)\times 10^5$ fm$^{-1/2}$ (from Bruno et al. 1999)
- ANC from effective range parameters without conditions: $|C_b|_2 = 3.24\times 10^4$ fm$^{-1/2}$; with conditions: $|C_b|_2 = 22.8\times 10^4$ fm$^{-1/2}$
- Resonant energy of the $2_4^+$ state without conditions: $E_{R(24)} = 5.92(2)$ MeV; with conditions: $E_{R(24)} = 5.90(2)$ MeV
- Width of the $2_4^+$ state without conditions: $\Gamma_{R(24)} = 300^{+60}_{-40}$ keV; with conditions: $\Gamma_{R(24)} = 235(20)$ keV
- $\chi^2/N$ for $S_{E2}$ factor fit without conditions: 1.55; with conditions: 1.18
- Gamow-peak energy: $E_G = 0.3$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the author's previous cluster EFT studies of elastic $\alpha$-$^{12}$C scattering (Ando 2016, 2018, 2022, 2023) and the $E1$ transition of $^{12}$C($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^{16}$O (Ando 2019), addressing the problem that the ANC of the $2_1^+$ state from elastic scattering is significantly smaller than values from $\alpha$-transfer reactions reported by Bruno et al. (1999). The conditions introduced here produce ANC values consistent with the range $(2-18)\times 10^4$ fm$^{-1/2}$ reported by Sparenberg, Capel, and Baye (2011) using a potential model. The results demonstrate that adopting the ANC from $\alpha$-transfer reactions is necessary to reduce uncertainty in the effective range parameters and the extrapolated $S_{E2}$ factor at the Gamow-peak energy.
