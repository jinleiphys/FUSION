# Resolving the plasma profile via differential single inclusive suppression

> The ability of experimental signatures to resolve the spatio-temporal profile of an expanding quark gluon plasma is studied. In particular, the single inclusive suppression of high momentum hadrons versus the centrality of a heavy-ion collision and with respect to the reaction plane in non-central collisions is critically examined. Calculations are performed in the higher twist formalism for the modification of the fragmentation functions. Radically different nuclear geometries are used. The influence of different initial gluon distributions as well as different temporal evolution scenarios on the single inclusive suppression of high momentum pions are outlined. It is demonstrated that the modification versus the reaction plane is quite sensitive to the initial spatial density. Such sensitivity remains even in the presence of a strong elliptic flow.

## Key claim
The single inclusive suppression of high-momentum hadrons (the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\)) as a function of the emission angle with respect to the reaction plane in non-central heavy-ion collisions is sensitive to the initial spatial density profile of the quark-gluon plasma, and this sensitivity persists even when a strong elliptic flow is present.

## Method
Calculations were performed using the higher-twist formalism for medium-modified fragmentation functions, assuming boost invariance and restricting to mid-rapidity. The initial gluon density profile was modeled using either a hard-sphere or a Gaussian thickness function, with the temporal evolution taken as either pure Bjorken expansion (\(1/\tau\)) or including a simple parameterization of radial and elliptic flow. The normalization of the density was fixed by requiring that the \(R_{AA}\) for the most central collisions at a pion \(p_T = 8\) GeV be 0.2.

## Key numbers
- The normalization of the gluon density was set by requiring that the \(R_{AA}\) for the most central collisions at a pion \(p_T = 8\) GeV be 0.2.
- For peripheral events with centrality between 40% and 60%, the hard-sphere initial density produced a large azimuthal asymmetry in the \(R_{AA}(\Delta\phi)\); jets emitted in the reaction plane (\(\phi = 0-15^\circ\)) showed the least modification, while those emitted perpendicular to the plane (\(\phi = 75-90^\circ\)) showed the most modification.
- Replacing the hard-sphere density with a Gaussian profile drastically reduced the variation of \(R_{AA}\) with \(\phi\).
- Including transverse flow with a radial flow velocity \(v = 0.4\) (from Kolb (2003)) and an asymmetric velocity \(v_\epsilon\) chosen to restore azimuthal symmetry by \(\tau = 2 R_A\) reduced the spread of \(R_{AA}\) as a function of \(\phi\) compared to the case with no elliptic flow.
- At a pion \(p_T = 10\) GeV, the effect of increasing the asymmetric flow (via a multiplicative factor \(f_\epsilon\)) on the spread of \(R_{AA}\) between in-plane and out-of-plane directions was quantified (see Fig. 5).

## Context
This work extends the study of hard probe modification as a probe of the plasma space-time profile, following the spirit of Armesto (2004) which explored the effect of longitudinal flow. It contrasts with the finding that the integrated \(R_{AA}\) for the most central event is insensitive to the detailed initial gluon density or its time evolution (Renk, 2006). The results suggest that differential measurements such as \(R_{AA}\) versus the reaction plane can help discriminate between different initial density profiles and flow scenarios, complementing bulk-sector constraints from hydrodynamic simulations (Nonaka, 2006).
