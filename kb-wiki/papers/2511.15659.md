# Bridging the Gap: Connecting Atomic Nuclei to Their Quantum Foundations

> We extend the QCD Parton Model analysis by employing a factorized nuclear structure model that explicitly accounts for both individual nucleons and correlated nucleon pairs. This novel framework establishes a paradigm that directly links the nuclear physics description of matter (in terms of protons and neutrons) to the particle physics schema (in terms of quarks and gluons). Our analysis of high-energy data from lepton Deep-Inelastic Scattering, Drell-Yan, and W/Z production simultaneously extracts the universal effective distribution of quarks and gluons inside correlated nucleon pairs, and their nucleus-specific fractions. The successful extraction of these universal distributions marks a significant advance in our understanding of nuclear structure, as it directly connects nucleon-level and parton-level quantities.

## Key claim

The paper extends the QCD Parton Model by employing a factorized nuclear structure model that explicitly accounts for both individual nucleons and correlated nucleon pairs, enabling the first direct extraction of nuclear Parton Distribution Functions (nPDFs) that is fully consistent with independent nuclear structure determinations. The hedge is that the SRC fractions \(C_{p,n}^A\) exhibit a scaling freedom on their absolute value due to a freedom in the parameterization, though no evidence of this scaling freedom was identified in the results.

## Method

The analysis performed global fits of nuclear lepton Deep-Inelastic Scattering (DIS), Drell-Yan (DY), and \(W/Z\) boson production data at next-to-leading order (NLO) in QCD, using a linear combination of free-nucleon PDFs and universal Short-Range Correlation (SRC) PDFs. The nuclear structure dependence was fully encapsulated in the fraction of nucleons in SRC pairs, denoted by \(C_{p,n}^A\), which were independently determined for each nucleus. Two independent analyses were performed: one where \(C_p^A\) and \(C_n^A\) varied freely (baseSRC fit), and one assuming proton-neutron SRC dominance with \(C_p^A = (N/Z) \times C_n^A\) (pnSRC fit), with results compared to a traditional mean-field-like analysis using the same dataset.

## Key numbers

- The baseSRC fit achieved a total \(\chi^2_{\mathrm{tot}}/N_{\mathrm{DOF}}\) of 0.80, compared to 0.85 for the traditional fit, using 1684 data points after cuts.
- For gold (\({}^{197}_{79}\mathrm{Au}\)), \(C_p^A = 0.256\) and \(C_n^A = 0.177\), implying \(79 \times C_p^A \simeq 20.2\) protons and \(118 \times C_n^A \simeq 20.9\) neutrons are effectively modified by SRC.
- For lead (\({}^{208}_{82}\mathrm{Pb}\)), \(C_p^A = 0.295\) and \(C_n^A = 0.202\), implying \(82 \times C_p^A \simeq 24.2\) protons and \(126 \times C_n^A \simeq 25.5\) neutrons are effectively modified.
- The SRC coefficients show logarithmic growth with nuclear mass number \(A\), starting from approximately 5% for helium and reaching approximately 25% for lead.
- The pnSRC fit yielded \(\chi^2_{\mathrm{tot}}/N_{\mathrm{DOF}} = 0.82\), only minimally increased from the baseSRC value of 0.80.
- The analysis covers an \(x\) range of approximately \(10^{-3}\) to 0.75.

## Context

This work extends the nCTEQ program of nuclear PDF analyses (Kovarik 2015) by incorporating Short-Range Correlations, directly linking partonic dynamics to nuclear dynamics. The results are consistent with independent SRC abundances extracted from quasi-elastic electron scattering measurements (CLAS 2005, Fomin 2011, Schmookler 2019) and Quantum Monte Carlo nuclear calculations (Cruz-Torres 2019), establishing a connection between high-energy partonic behavior and low-energy nuclear structure. This framework enables future studies to either follow the traditional approach using averaged nucleon distributions or construct more detailed initial-state descriptions where each nucleon is represented by either a free-nucleon PDF or an SRC-modified PDF depending on its correlation state.
