# Hot medium effects on J/psi production in p+Pb collisions at sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02 TeV

> Based on a kinetic description of J/psi dissociation and production in an expanding quark-gluon plasma that is described by a 2+1 dimensional ideal hydrodynamics, we have studied the hot medium effects on J/psi production in p+Pb collisions at sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02 TeV. Including also the cold nuclear matter effects, we are able to reproduce recent experimental results on the nuclear modification factor R_{pPb}(J/psi) measured by the ALICE Collaboration. We have also made predictions for the R_{pPb} of J/psi and the double ratio R_{pPb}^{pro}(psi')/R_{pPb}^{pro}(J/psi) of prompt quarkonia produced in the most central 10% p+Pb collisions. We find that different from the cold nuclear matter effects, the R_{pPb}(J/psi) is slightly smaller than that in the minimum bias collisions, and the double ratio is significantly less than one at backward rapidity.

## Key claim

The paper shows that including hot medium effects (QGP dissociation and regeneration) alongside cold nuclear matter effects can reproduce the ALICE data on J/psi nuclear modification factor in p+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, but the difference between results with and without hot medium effects is less than 20%, making the J/psi R_pPb alone insufficient to conclusively verify QGP formation in these collisions.

## Method

The authors use a 2+1 dimensional ideal hydrodynamic model to describe the expanding quark-gluon plasma produced in p+Pb collisions, with initial conditions generated from the AMPT model. A transport equation for J/psi in phase space is solved, including a dissociation rate from gluon dissociation and a regeneration rate from the inverse process c+bar-c -> J/psi+g, with the regeneration modified by a canonical enhancement factor due to the small number of charm quark pairs. Cold nuclear matter effects (shadowing, Cronin effect, nuclear absorption) are included in the initial J/psi distribution using results from Albacete (2013) and Manceau (2013).

## Key numbers

- Maximum initial energy density epsilon_max ≈ 15 GeV/fm^3 at rapidity y = -2 in most central 10% collisions, corresponding to a maximum temperature T_max ≈ 290 MeV
- Initial proper time tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c for hydrodynamic evolution; critical temperature T_c = 165 MeV
- Average impact parameter for most central 10% collisions: ⟨b⟩ = 3.3 fm; for 40-60% centrality: ⟨b⟩ = 5.7 fm
- pp J/psi production cross section at midrapidity: dσ_pp/dy = 5.68 μb at 5.02 TeV (interpolated from 2.76 and 7 TeV data)
- Average transverse momentum in forward rapidity (2.5 < y < 4): ⟨p_T⟩ = 2.37 GeV, ⟨p_T^2⟩ = 7.73 GeV^2
- Number of charm quark pairs in central p+Pb collision: about 1.2 pairs (from total charm cross section 6.8 mb in pp)
- Average thickness function for 0-10% centrality: T(⟨b⟩) = 1.82 fm^{-2}
- Average number of binary collisions in central p+Pb: N_coll = 12.3, leading to transverse momentum broadening Δ⟨p_T^2⟩ = 4.2 GeV^2
- Thickness function ratio between 0-10% and 40-60% centrality: C = 1.85
- Relaxation time for charm quark thermalization: tau_r = 7 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the transport model previously used by Liu (2009) and Liu (2012) for J/psi suppression and regeneration in A+A collisions at RHIC and LHC to the smaller p+Pb system at LHC energies. It addresses the question raised by ALICE measurements (Manceau 2013) of whether a QGP can form in high-multiplicity p+Pb collisions, where charged hadron multiplicities reach about 50 in the most central 5% collisions (Albacete 2013). The paper predicts that the double ratio R_pPb(psi')/R_pPb(J/psi) for prompt quarkonia in central collisions would be significantly less than one at backward rapidity, providing a more sensitive probe of hot medium effects than the J/psi R_pPb alone.
