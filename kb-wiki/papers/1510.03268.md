# NuWro Monte Carlo generator of neutrino interactions - first electron scattering results

> NuWro Monte Carlo generator of events is presented. It is a numerical environment containing all necessary ingredients to simulate interactions of neutrinos with nucleons and nuclei in realistic experimental situation in wide neutrino energy range. It can be used both for data analysis as well as studies of nuclear effects in neutrino interactions. The first results and functionalities of eWro - module of NuWro dedicated to electron nucleus scattering - are also presented.

# NuWro Monte Carlo Generator of Neutrino Interactions – First Electron Scattering Results

## Key claim

The NuWro event generator can simulate neutrino-nucleus interactions across a wide energy range (approximately 100 MeV to TeV) and its new eWro module for electron-nucleus scattering allows direct comparisons with electron scattering data to test the nuclear physics models common to both modules, though the electron scattering results shown are preliminary and currently limited to Fermi gas and local Fermi gas nuclear models.

## Method

The authors developed the NuWro Monte Carlo generator in object-oriented C++ to simulate neutrino interactions with nucleons and nuclei, incorporating multiple interaction channels (quasielastic, meson exchange currents, resonant pion production, coherent pion production, and deep inelastic scattering) with various nuclear model options including global and local Fermi gas, spectral function, and effective potential approaches. They added the eWro module for electron-nucleus scattering, implementing quasielastic scattering and single pion production (including the Delta(1232) resonance with nonperturbative self-energy in nuclear matter) within Fermi gas and local Fermi gas nuclear models, and compared the resulting differential cross sections against published electron scattering data for carbon and oxygen targets.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino energy range covered: approximately 100 MeV (lower limit from impulse approximation validity) up to TeV energies
- Invariant mass threshold for resonant pion production: W < 1.6 GeV
- Invariant mass threshold for deep inelastic scattering: W > 1.6 GeV
- Smooth transition region between resonant and deep inelastic scattering: W in (1.4, 1.6) GeV
- Systematic uncertainty goal for new oscillation experiments: 1-3%
- Current knowledge of neutrino-nucleus cross sections: no better than 10-20%
- Electron scattering data comparisons shown for carbon at beam energies of 0.560 GeV (theta=145 degrees), 0.620 GeV (theta=60.0 degrees), 1.299 GeV (theta=37.5 degrees), and 2.130 GeV (theta=16.0 degrees), and for oxygen at 0.737 GeV (theta=37.5 degrees) and 0.880 GeV (theta=32.0 degrees)

## Context

This work extends the lineage of neutrino event generators including GENIE, NEUT, and GiBUU (as referenced in Agashe 2014) by providing an open-source, flexible alternative that has been under development since approximately 2004. The eWro module enables direct testing of nuclear models against precise electron scattering data from experiments such as Barreau (1983), Sealock (1989), Bagdasaryan (1988), O'Connell (1987), and Anghinolfi (1996), which is critical because the MEC contribution first highlighted by Martini (2009) in connection with MiniBooNE CCQE measurements showed that models including MEC can reproduce the standard nucleon axial mass value from MiniBooNE data. The NuWro generator enables future work on precise neutrino oscillation measurements by providing a platform for testing and implementing improved nuclear models, with the eWro module specifically allowing validation against electron scattering data before applying the same models to neutrino interactions.
