# General behavior of near-threshold hadron scattering for exotic hadrons

> We discuss the general behavior of the near-threshold scattering amplitude with channel couplings. The signal of the exotic hadrons near the threshold may manifest as a dip structure in the cross section originated from a zero point of the scattering amplitude. Such a dip structure by the zero point cannot be reproduced by the Flatté amplitude which is widely used for the analysis of exotic hadrons, because of the constraints imposed on the Flatté amplitude near the threshold. In this work, we propose the General amplitude which can describe the dip structure near the threshold, in contrast to the Flatté amplitude. Moreover, we numerically study the behavior of the near-threshold cross section in relation to the zero point.

# General behavior of near-threshold hadron scattering for exotic hadrons

## Key claim
The Flatté amplitude, widely used to analyze near-threshold exotic hadrons, cannot reproduce dip structures in cross sections because it lacks a zero point; the proposed General amplitude can describe both peak and dip structures near threshold, but only when the parameter γ is nonzero and sufficiently negative does a dip appear, while for small |γ| the cross section still shows a peak structure similar to the Flatté case.

## Method
The authors compare the Flatté amplitude with a scattering amplitude derived from non-relativistic effective field theory with contact interaction (Contact amplitude) for two-channel scattering near the threshold of the higher channel. They introduce a reparametrization of the Contact amplitude in terms of three real parameters (A₂₂, ε, γ) to construct the General amplitude, which reduces to the Flatté amplitude when γ=0 and to the Contact amplitude when γ≠0. They then numerically compute the normalized s-wave cross section for the ππ–K̅K system with f₀(980) as an example, varying γ while keeping the scattering length a_G fixed at +1.0 − i0.8 fm.

## Key numbers
- The fixed scattering length used in the numerical study is a_G = +1.0 − i0.8 fm.
- The quasibound pole position for this scattering length is E^G_p = −0.014 − i0.048 GeV.
- For γ = −10.0, the zero point of the amplitude lies at E^G_zero = −0.02 GeV on the real axis, producing a dip structure in the cross section.
- For γ = −10.0, the parameters are A₂₂ = +0.27 fm and ε = −1.42; for γ = 0.00, A₂₂ = +1.64 fm and ε = +0.20; for γ = +0.07, A₂₂ = +2.45 fm and ε = +0.31; for γ = −0.01, A₂₂ = +1.59 fm and ε = +0.19.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of near-threshold exotic hadrons beyond the Flatté amplitude (Flatté, 1976), which has known limitations near threshold (Baru, 2004). The authors show that the General amplitude can describe dip structures reported in systems such as γγ → ππ (Dai, 2014), which the Flatté amplitude cannot reproduce. This enables more accurate determination of resonance parameters and scattering lengths when fitting experimental data for exotic hadrons like X(3872) and f₀(980), and clarifies that the shape of the cross section can differ significantly from typical peak structures even when a resonance pole exists near threshold.
