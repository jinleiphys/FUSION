# Energy Correlators from Partons to Hadrons: Unveiling the Dynamics of the Strong Interactions with Archival ALEPH Data

> Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) is a remarkably rich theory exhibiting numerous emergent degrees of freedom, from flux tubes to hadrons. Their description in terms of the underlying quarks and gluons of the QCD Lagrangian remains a central challenge of modern physics. Colliders offer a unique opportunity to probe these phenomena experimentally: high energy partons produced from the QCD vacuum excite these emergent degrees, imprinting their dynamics in correlations in asymptotic energy flux. Decoding these correlations requires measurements with exceptional angular resolution, beyond that achieved in previous measurements. Recent progress has enabled precision calculations of energy flux on charged particles alone, allowing data-theory comparisons for measurements using high resolution tracking detectors. In this Letter, we resurrect thirty-year-old data from the ALEPH tracker, and perform a high angular resolution measurement of the two-point correlation of energy flux, probing QCD over three orders of magnitude in scale in a single measurement. Our measurement unveils for the first time the full spectrum of the correlator, including light-ray quasi-particle states, flux-tube excitations, and their transitions into confined hadrons. We compare our measurement with record precision theoretical predictions, achieving percent level agreement, and revealing interesting new phenomena in the confinement transitions. More broadly, we highlight the immense potential of this newly unlocked archival data set, the so called "recycling frontier", and emphasize synergies with ongoing and future collider experiments.

## Key claim

The authors resurrect thirty-year-old archival data from the ALEPH detector at LEP to perform a high-angular-resolution measurement of the two-point energy-energy correlator (EEC) on charged particles, achieving for the first time a complete view of the correlator spectrum extending below the confinement scale in both the collinear and back-to-back limits, with the caveat that the measurement is restricted to charged tracks only and relies on recent theoretical developments enabling track-based calculations.

## Method

The analysis uses approximately 1.36 million hadronic \(e^{+}e^{-}\) annihilation events at a center-of-mass energy of 91.2 GeV collected by the ALEPH experiment in 1994, reformatted into MIT Open Data format. Charged particle tracks are selected with a minimum transverse momentum of 0.2 GeV/\(c\) and \(|\cos\theta_{\text{lab}}| < 0.94\), and the EEC is constructed as an energy-weighted cross section of track pairs as a function of \(z = (1-\cos\theta_{ij})/2\). The detector-level distribution is unfolded for detector effects using the D'Agostini iterative method with a two-dimensional response matrix in \(z\) and energy weights, and systematic uncertainties are evaluated from prior dependence, matching schemes, and regularization choices.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: 91.2 GeV (LEP \(Z^{0}\) peak)
- Total events analyzed: approximately 1.36 million hadronic events
- Residual background from \(e^{+}e^{-}\rightarrow\tau^{+}\tau^{-}\): less than 0.26% of events
- Minimum transverse momentum for charged tracks: 0.2 GeV/\(c\)
- Measurement extends to \(z \sim (1-z) \sim 10^{-5}\), achieving \(z \ll (\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}/Q)^2\)
- Dominant systematic uncertainty from prior dependence: approximately 5% around \(z=0.5\), increasing to 10% in the collinear and back-to-back limits
- All other systematic uncertainties contribute less than 2% each

## Context

This work extends the legacy of early EEC measurements by PLUTO (1981), CELLO (1982), JADE (1984), and the original LEP experiments (ALEPH, DELPHI, L3, OPAL, 1990s), which focused on the bulk angular region for \(\alpha_s\) extraction and lacked the angular resolution to probe the collinear and back-to-back limits. The measurement is enabled by recent theoretical advances in track function factorization by Chang (2013), Jaarsma (2023), and Chen (2022), which allow precision calculations on charged particles alone, and by the "recycling frontier" approach of resurrecting archival LEP data as demonstrated by Badea (2019) and Chen (2021). The results enable direct experimental access to confinement transitions, flux-tube excitations, and light-ray quasi-particle states in QCD, and provide a benchmark for future \(e^{+}e^{-}\) collider experiments.
