# S-wave meson-baryon potentials with strangeness from Lattice QCD

> We study the s-wave I=2 πΣand I=1 KN interactions from 2+1 flavor full lattice QCD simulation for relatively heavy quark mass corresponding to m_π=700 MeV. The s-wave meson-baryon potentials are obtained from the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes. Potentials in both channels reveal short range repulsions, which suggest the importance of the Pauli blocking effect. The I=1 KN scattering phase shifts are calculated and compared with the existing experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper extracts s-wave meson-baryon potentials from lattice QCD at a heavy pion mass of 700 MeV, finding short-range repulsion in both the I=2 πΣ and I=1 KN channels; the authors hedge that the qualitative agreement of the I=1 KN phase shifts with experiment at this unphysical quark mass suggests the method is promising, but a definite conclusion for the I=2 πΣ scattering requires simulations at lighter quark masses.

## Method

The authors compute four-point correlation functions on 2+1 flavor full lattice QCD gauge configurations (PACS-CS Collaboration, 32^3 × 64 lattice, a=0.09 fm) and extract the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave function for the meson-baryon system. Using a time-dependent Schrödinger-type equation for the normalized R-correlator, they obtain the leading-order effective central potentials for the s-wave I=2 πΣ and I=1 KN channels. The scattering phase shifts are then calculated from these potentials.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: m_π = 705(2) MeV; kaon mass: m_K = 793(2) MeV; nucleon mass: M_N = 1590(8) MeV; Σ mass: M_Σ = 1665(7) MeV (all at the simulated quark masses).
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.09 fm; physical lattice size: (2.9 fm)^3; number of gauge configurations: 399.
- Short-range repulsion observed for r < 0.5 fm in both I=2 πΣ and I=1 KN potentials.
- In the I=0 KN potential, an attractive well is found in the mid-range 0.4 < r < 1.2 fm, while the I=1 KN repulsion is significantly stronger.

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD Collaboration method (Ishii et al., 2006; Aoki et al., 2009) for extracting hadron interactions from lattice QCD to the meson-baryon sector with strangeness, specifically the I=2 πΣ and I=1 KN systems. The results are relevant to understanding the Λ(1405) resonance, which is considered a dynamically generated state in chiral dynamics (Jido et al., 2003) and a quasi-bound state of \(\bar{K}N\) in phenomenological models (Akaishi and Yamazaki, 2002). The short-range repulsion observed supports the quark Pauli blocking mechanism first proposed by Machida and Namiki (1965). Future simulations at lighter quark masses will enable quantitative predictions for the I=2 πΣ scattering, which is needed to constrain the πΣ scattering lengths and the pole structure of the Λ(1405).
