# The Nucleon-Nucleon Potential in the 1/N_c Expansion

> The nucleon-nucleon potential is analysed using the 1/N_c expansion of QCD. The NN potential is shown to have an expansion in 1/N_c^2, and the strengths of the leading order central, spin-orbit, tensor, and quadratic spin-orbit forces (including isospin dependence) are determined. Comparison with a successful phenomenological potential (Nijmegen) shows that the large-N_c analysis explains many of the qualitative features observed in the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The 1/N_c expansion implies an effective Wigner supermultiplet symmetry for light nuclei. Results for baryons containing strange quarks are presented in an appendix.

## Key claim

The nucleon-nucleon potential has an expansion in \(1/N_c^2\) (not just \(1/N_c\)), and the leading-order strengths of the central, spin-orbit, tensor, and quadratic spin-orbit forces can be determined from large-\(N_c\) QCD, with the caveat that the analysis projects the Hartree Hamiltonian onto the nucleon sector without integrating out the \(\Delta\) baryon, which is degenerate in the \(N_c\to\infty\) limit.

## Method

The authors analyze the \(NN\) potential using the \(1/N_c\) expansion of QCD, expressing the Hartree Hamiltonian in terms of one-quark operators \(\hat S^i\), \(\hat I^a\), and \(\hat G^{ia}\) and using the \(I_t=J_t\) rule (eq. \ijrule) to determine the \(N_c\)-scaling of matrix elements. They expand the potential in multipole moments (\(\Delta L=0,1,2\)) and derive the leading-order \(N_c\) scaling for each of the ten functions \(V^i_a\) in the general form of the nonrelativistic \(NN\) potential (central, spin-orbit, tensor, and quadratic spin-orbit terms, with and without isospin dependence). The results are compared with the phenomenological Nijmegen potential by examining the \(N_c\) scaling of its coupling constants.

## Key numbers

* Central potential: \(V^0_0 \sim N_c\), \(V_\sigma^1 \sim N_c\); \(V_\sigma^0 \sim 1/N_c\), \(V_0^1 \sim 1/N_c\) (Table 1).
* Spin-orbit potential: \(V_{LS}^0 \sim 1/N_c\), \(V_{LS}^1 \sim 1/N_c\) (Table 1).
* Tensor potential: \(V_T^1 \sim N_c\), \(V_T^0 \sim 1/N_c\) (Table 1).
* Quadratic spin-orbit potential: \(V_Q^0 \sim 1/N_c^3\), \(V_Q^1 \sim 1/N_c\) (Table 1).
* The ratio \((M_\Delta - M_N)/(M_\Delta + M_N) = 0.13\) in nature, while the large-\(N_c\) prediction at \(N_c=3\) is \(R \sim 1/N_c^2 = 0.11\).
* The Nijmegen potential couplings \(g_{IS}\) scale as \(g_{IS} \propto N_c^{(1/2 - |I-S|)}\), leading to the prediction \(\hat g_{IS} \equiv g_{IS}/f_\rho = 1\) for \(|I-S|=0\) and \(=1/3\) for \(|I-S|=1\) (eq. \ratpred).
* The \(\phi\) meson coupling in the Nijmegen fit has \(g_\phi/g_\omega \approx 0.1\), a factor of three smaller than the naive \(1/N_c\) prediction.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Kaplan and Savage (1996), which only treated the central potential, to the full \(NN\) potential including spin-orbit, tensor, and quadratic spin-orbit forces. It builds on the large-\(N_c\) QCD framework developed by Dashen and Manohar (1993), Jenkins (1993), Dashen, Jenkins, and Manohar (1994, 1995), and others, which established the \(SU(4)\) spin-flavor symmetry and the \(I_t=J_t\) rule for baryons. The results are compared with the Nijmegen potential of Nagels, Rijken, and de Swart (1978) and Stoks et al. (1994), showing that the large-\(N_c\) hierarchy explains many qualitative features of the \(NN\) interaction. The analysis enables a systematic understanding of the spin-isospin structure of nuclear forces from QCD and predicts an effective Wigner supermultiplet symmetry for light nuclei, with the spin-orbit force expected to break this symmetry in heavy nuclei as its mean field grows like \(A^{1/3}\).
