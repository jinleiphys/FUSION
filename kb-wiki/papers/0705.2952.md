# Renormalization Group Analysis of Boundary Conditions in Potential Scattering

> We analyze how a short distance boundary condition for the Schrodinger equation must change as a function of the boundary radius by imposing the physical requirement of phase shift independence on the boundary condition. The resulting equation can be interpreted as a variable phase equation of a complementary boundary value problem. We discuss the corresponding infrared fixed points and the perturbative expansion around them generating a short distance modified effective range theory. We also discuss ultraviolet fixed points, limit cycles and attractors with a given fractality which take place for singular attractive potentials at the origin. The scaling behaviour of scattering observables can analytically be determined and is studied with some emphasis on the low energy nucleon-nucleon interaction via singular pion exchange potentials. The generalization to coupled channels is also studied.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a short-distance boundary condition for the Schrödinger equation must evolve with the boundary radius according to a Riccati-type renormalization group equation to guarantee phase-shift independence, and that this evolution equation is equivalent to a variable phase equation for a complementary boundary value problem.

## Method

The authors impose the physical requirement that scattering phase shifts remain unchanged when the boundary radius \(R\) is varied, deriving a renormalization group equation for the logarithmic derivative \(L_k(R) = u_k'(R)/u_k(R)\) of the reduced wave function. They analyze the infrared fixed points (\(\xi=0\) and \(\xi=1\) for the dimensionless boundary condition \(\xi = R L_k(R)\)) and study ultraviolet fixed points, limit cycles, and attractors for singular attractive potentials at the origin. The formalism is applied to low-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering using one-pion exchange (OPE) and two-pion exchange (TPE) potentials in the \(^1S_0\) channel.

## Key numbers

* The OPE potential in the \(^1S_0\) channel is \(U_{\rm OPE}(R) = -\frac{1}{a R} e^{-m R}\) with \(a = 0.7\,{\rm fm}\) and pion mass \(m = 0.699\,{\rm fm}^{-1}\).
* The TPE potential behaves as \(U_{\rm TPE}(R) \to -a^4/R^6\) for \(mR \ll 1\) with \(a \simeq 1.64\,{\rm fm}\) in the singlet channel.
* The pion weak decay constant is \(f = 0.468\,{\rm fm}^{-1}\), the pion axial coupling is \(g = 1.29\), and the nucleon mass is \(M_N = 4.758\,{\rm fm}^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the momentum-space renormalization group analysis of contact interactions by Birse (1998) to coordinate space with explicit long-range potentials, providing a transparent framework for handling singular potentials like OPE and TPE in nuclear physics. The boundary condition regularization offers advantages over delta-shell regularization (Barford, 2002) and square-well regulators (Beane, 2000) by generating a one-valued renormalization group flow and enabling non-perturbative renormalization of singular attractive potentials. The formalism enables systematic separation of short-distance and long-distance physics in nucleon-nucleon scattering within chiral effective field theory, building on the work of Weinberg (1990, 1991) and subsequent developments by van Kolck (1998), Bedaque (2002), and Epelbaum (2005).
