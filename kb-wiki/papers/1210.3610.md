# Can neutrino-induced photon production explain the low energy excess in MiniBooNE?

> This report summarizes our study of Neutral Current (NC)-induced photon production in MiniBooNE, as motivated by the low energy excess in this experiment [A. A. Aquilar-Arevalo et al. (MiniBooNE Collaboration), Phys. Rev. Lett. 98, 231801 (2007); 103, 111801 (2009)]. It was proposed that NC photon production with two anomalous photon-$Z$ boson-vector meson couplings might explain the excess. However, our computed event numbers in both neutrino and antineutrino runs are consistent with the previous MiniBooNE estimate that is based on their pion production measurement. Various nuclear effects discussed in our previous works, including nucleon Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, and the $Δ$ resonance broadening in the nucleus, are taken into account. Uncertainty due to the two anomalous terms and nuclear effects are studied in a conservative way.

# Can neutrino-induced photon production explain the low energy excess in MiniBooNE?

## Key claim

Neutral current (NC) photon production, even with two anomalous photon-Z boson-vector meson couplings, cannot explain the low energy excess observed in MiniBooNE; the computed event numbers in both neutrino and antineutrino runs are consistent with the previous MiniBooNE estimate based on their pion production measurement, and the differences are not significant enough to account for the excess, especially for reconstructed neutrino energies below 0.475 GeV.

## Method

The authors computed NC photon production cross sections and event numbers for MiniBooNE using a chiral effective field theory (quantum hadrodynamics EFT) interaction kernel calibrated to available pion data, with nuclear effects including nucleon Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, and Delta resonance broadening in the nucleus treated via a Local Fermi Gas approximation for incoherent production and an optimal approximation for coherent production. The neutrino-nucleon interaction kernel was extrapolated to higher energies (up to ~2 GeV) using fitted form factors for axial transition currents from Graczyk (2009) and vector transition currents constrained by electromagnetic interactions, with two contact terms (c1 and e1) regularized by a dipole form factor to represent anomalous photon-Z-meson couplings. Event numbers were computed using MiniBooNE's neutrino and antineutrino flux spectra, proton-on-target numbers of 6.46 x 10^20 (neutrino) and 11.27 x 10^20 (antineutrino), detector mass of 8.12 x 10^8 g of mineral oil (CH2), and published photon detection efficiencies.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous low-energy calculations of neutrino-induced photon and pion production (Zhang and Serot, 2010-2012) to higher neutrino energies relevant to MiniBooNE, where the reconstructed neutrino energy E_QE can underestimate the true energy substantially for NC photon events. The study directly tests the proposal by Hill (2011) and others that anomalous photon-Z-vector meson couplings could significantly increase NC photon production to explain the MiniBooNE low energy excess reported by Aguilar-Arevalo et al. (2007, 2009). The results contradict the anomalous coupling explanation and instead support the original MiniBooNE background estimate based on their pion production measurement (MiniBooNE Collaboration, 2011). The work enables future model-independent approaches such as dispersion analysis to resolve the remaining uncertainty in the neutrino-nucleon interaction kernel, and is also relevant to the gamma-Z box diagram in parity violation studies of electron-nucleon scattering.
