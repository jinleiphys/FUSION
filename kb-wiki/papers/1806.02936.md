# Energy Spectrum of Neutron-Rich Helium Isotopes: Complex Made Simple

> We demonstrate that the intricate energy spectrum of neutron-rich helium isotopes can be straightforwardly described by taking advantage of the low-energy properties of neutron-neutron interaction and the scale separation that is present in diluted dripline systems. By using arguments based on the halo effective field theory, we carry out a parameter reduction of the complex-energy configuration interaction framework in the $spd$ space, including resonant and scattering states. By adjusting only one parameter, the strength of the spin-singlet central neutron-neutron interaction, we reproduce experimental energies and widths of $^{5-8}$He within tens of keV precision. We predict a parity inversion of narrow resonances in $^{9}$He and show that the ground state of $^{10}$He is an $s$-wave-dominated threshold configuration that could decay through two-neutron emission.

## Key claim

The intricate energy spectrum of neutron-rich helium isotopes can be straightforwardly described by reducing the Hamiltonian to a core-neutron potential optimized to alpha-n scattering and a single central valence-neutron interaction term in the spin-singlet channel, justified by halo effective field theory arguments about scale separation in dilute dripline systems.

## Method

The authors employed the Gamow density matrix renormalization group (G-DMRG) method in a Berggren basis, which extends the configuration-interaction framework into the complex-energy plane by including Gamow resonant states and nonresonant scattering states. The one-body He-4-neutron interaction was taken as a Woods-Saxon potential optimized to s and p phase shifts in alpha-n scattering, while the two-body valence neutron interaction was reduced from the Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki interaction to only the spin-singlet central term based on halo-EFT power counting. The model space included spd partial waves with no truncation on the number of particles in the continuum.

## Key numbers

- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters optimized to alpha-n scattering phase shifts: depth V0 = 41.77 MeV, diffuseness a = 0.618 fm, radius R0 = 2.162 fm, spin-orbit strength Vso = 6.991 MeV
- The optimal spin-singlet central interaction strength Vc(opt) = -5.709 MeV with standard deviation sigma = 0.008 MeV
- The rms error on energies of He-6 (0+, 2+) and He-7 (3/2-, 5/2-) states is about 9 keV with the original interaction (L >= 0) and about 26 keV with the simplified interaction (L=0)
- Calculated ground state energy of He-6: -0.974 MeV (experimental: -0.972 MeV)
- Calculated ground state energy of He-8: -3.176 MeV (experimental: -3.10 MeV)
- Calculated ground state of He-9: J^pi = 1/2+ at -3.05 MeV with width 76 keV
- Calculated ground state of He-10: J^pi = 0+ at -3.21 MeV with width less than 1 keV
- The 2+ state of He-6 requires an abnormally large interaction strength (Vc approximately -6.8 MeV) to reproduce the experimental value, leading to a discrepancy of about 180 keV

## Context

This work extends continuum shell model approaches to the helium chain (Michel 2004, Volya 2005, Rotureau 2006, Papadimitriou 2011, Jaganathen 2017) by using effective scale arguments from halo-EFT to reduce the Hamiltonian complexity, and by removing all truncations on the number of particles in the continuum within the spd model space. The results contradict the no-core shell model with continuum calculations of Vorabbi (2018) which predicted a 1/2- ground state for He-9, and instead support the parity inversion predicted by earlier three-body models (Aoyama 2002). The predicted narrow ground state of He-10 dominated by s-waves supports the idea of a possible double-halo structure (He-4 + 4n + 2n), analogous to the atomic He-3 He-4_2 trimer (Voigtsberger 2014).
