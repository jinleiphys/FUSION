# Masses, Deformations and Charge Radii--Nuclear Ground-State Properties in the Relativistic Mean Field Model

> We perform a systematic study of the ground-state properties of all the nuclei from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line throughout the periodic table employing the relativistic mean field model. The TMA parameter set is used for the mean-field Lagrangian density, and a state-dependent BCS method is adopted to describe the pairing correlation. The ground-state properties of a total of 6969 nuclei with $Z,N\ge 8$ and $Z\le 100$ from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line, including the binding energies, the separation energies, the deformations, and the rms charge radii, are calculated and compared with existing experimental data and those of the FRDM and HFB-2 mass formulae. This study provides the first complete picture of the current status of the descriptions of nuclear ground-state properties in the relativistic mean field model. The deviations from existing experimental data indicate either that new degrees of freedom are needed, such as triaxial deformations, or that serious effort is needed to improve the current formulation of the relativistic mean field model.

## Key claim
The relativistic mean field (RMF) model with the TMA parameter set and a state-dependent BCS pairing method can describe ground-state properties of 6969 nuclei across the periodic table, but the authors note that the deviations from experimental data indicate either that new degrees of freedom (such as triaxial deformations) are needed or that serious effort is required to improve the current RMF formulation. The purpose is not to obtain numbers that agree most closely with measured quantities but to gain insight into the entire mass region to guide future work.

## Method
The authors performed axially-deformed RMF+BCS calculations using the TMA parameter set for the mean-field Lagrangian density, which includes nonlinear terms for both the sigma and omega mesons and has mass-dependent coupling constants. A zero-range delta-force pairing interaction with a mass-dependent strength formula (V0 = 300 + 120/A^(1/3)) was adopted in a state-dependent BCS method, and quadrupole-constrained calculations were performed to locate the absolute minimum on the potential energy surface for each nucleus. Fourteen harmonic-oscillator shells were used for both fermion and meson fields, and a simple blocking method without time-reversal symmetry breaking was used for odd-nucleon nuclei.

## Key numbers
- Total of 6969 nuclei with Z,N >= 8 and Z <= 100 from proton drip line to neutron drip line were calculated
- RMS deviation sigma for nuclear masses (Group II, 2157 nuclei with experimental errors < 0.2 MeV): RMF+BCS = 2.108 MeV, FRDM = 0.626 MeV, HFB-2 = 0.739 MeV
- RMS deviation sigma for one-neutron separation energies (Group II, 1994 nuclei): RMF+BCS = 0.640 MeV, FRDM = 0.372 MeV, HFB-2 = 0.466 MeV
- RMS deviation sigma for charge radii (523 nuclei in 42 isotopic chains): RMF+BCS = 0.037 fm, FRDM = 0.045 fm, HFB-2 = 0.028 fm
- Neutron skin thickness of 208Pb in RMF+BCS: 0.26 fm; HFB-2 predicts 0.12 fm; experimental results include 0.20 +/- 0.04 fm and 0.19 +/- 0.09 fm
- Nuclear matter properties for TMA: saturation density 0.147 fm^-3, binding energy -16.025 MeV, incompressibility 318.146 MeV, symmetry energy 30.661 MeV, effective mass M*/M = 0.635

## Context
This work extends earlier RMF mass table calculations by Hirata (1997) for 2174 even-even nuclei without pairing and by Lalazissis (1999) for 1315 even-even nuclei with constant-gap BCS, now covering all bound nuclei with Z,N >= 8. The results are compared with the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) and the HFB-2 mass formula, and the authors identify regions where the RMF model overestimates magicity (near Z=92,N=126 and Z=58,N=82) and regions of shape coexistence where triaxial deformations may be needed. The study enables future investigations of whether deviations arise from parameter adjustment or from the need for higher-order corrections beyond the pure mean-field approximation.
