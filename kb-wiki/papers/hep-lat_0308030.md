# A BCS Gap on the Lattice

> Monte Carlo simulations of the 3+1 dimensional NJL model are performed with baryon chemical potential mu>0. For mu>Sigma_0, the constituent quark mass in vacuum, chiral symmetry is restored and a diquark condensate <qq_+> forms. We analyse the fermion propagator and find evidence for particle-hole mixing in the vicinity of the Fermi surface and an energy gap Delta>0, both of which provide evidence for superfluidity at high baryon density induced by a BCS mechanism. At (mu a)=0.8 the ratio between the BCS gap and the vacuum quark mass is Delta/Sigma_0=0.15(2).

## Key claim
Monte Carlo simulations of the 3+1 dimensional NJL model provide the first direct observation of a BCS energy gap in a lattice simulation, with the caveat that reliable extrapolation to zero temperature is only available at one chemical potential value, \(\mu a=0.8\).

## Method
The authors simulated the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with staggered fermions on lattices of size \(96\times12^2\times L_t\) with \(L_t=12,16,20,24\), using a standard HMC algorithm with auxiliary boson fields. They measured the normal and anomalous fermion propagators as functions of momentum and extracted excitation energies via pole fits, then extrapolated results first to zero temperature (linear in \(L_t^{-1}\)) and then to zero source strength \(j\) (linear in \(j\)).

## Key numbers
- At \(\mu a=0.8\), the ratio of the BCS gap to the vacuum constituent quark mass is \(\Delta/\Sigma_0 = 0.15(2)\).
- The vacuum constituent quark mass is \(\Sigma_0 = 400\) MeV, yielding a physical prediction \(\Delta \approx 60\) MeV.
- The chiral condensate at \(\mu a=0.8\) has only 5% of its vacuum value.
- The critical chemical potential for the chiral restoration transition is \(\mu_c a \simeq 0.6\).

## Context
This work extends previous lattice simulations of the NJL model by Walters and Hands (2003) that identified diquark condensation for \(\mu > \mu_c\), but here provides the first direct measurement of the BCS gap from the fermion propagator. The results are consistent with analytic model predictions from the literature (RWA). The study enables future investigations of finite volume effects (noted as unusually large by Amore), finite temperature stability of the superfluid phase, and the effect of unequal chemical potentials for different quark flavors.
