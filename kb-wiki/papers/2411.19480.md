# Sensitivity analysis of $β$-decay half-life predictions for Ge, As, Zr and Mo nuclei within the mapped interacting boson model

> We analyze parameter sensitivities of the mapped interacting boson model (IBM) and boson-fermion-fermion model (IBFFM) in the description of $β$-decay properties of the even-mass neutron-deficient Ge and As, and neutron-rich Zr and Mo isotopes. Based on the self-consistent mean-field calculations with a given energy density functional and a pairing interaction, the IBM Hamiltonian for even-even nuclei, single-particle energies, and occupation probabilities for unpaired nucleons, which are necessary building blocks of the IBFFM Hamiltonian and Gamow-Teller and Fermi transition operators, are completely determined. A few coupling constants of the boson-fermion and residual neutron-proton interactions are only phenomenological parameters fitted to reproduce low-energy spectra of odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei. It is found that the calculated $\log{}_{10}ft$ values for the $β^+$ decays $^{68}$As$\to^{68}$Ge are particularly sensitive to the quadrupole-quadrupole boson interaction strength used for the parent ($^{68}$As) nucleus. We further incorporate higher-order terms in the one-nucleon transfer operators in the boson system, and find that, while their effects are non-negligible, they do not significantly alter qualitative features of $β$-decay properties. We report a novel application of the mapped IBM framework to compute $β$-decay half-lives, and show that the observed trend along isotopic chains are reasonably reproduced.

## Key claim

The mapped interacting boson model (IBM) framework, when combined with self-consistent mean-field calculations, can reasonably reproduce observed trends in beta-decay half-lives along isotopic chains, but the predicted log10ft values for the beta-plus decay of 68As are particularly sensitive to the quadrupole-quadrupole boson interaction strength used for the parent 68As nucleus, and higher-order terms in the one-nucleon transfer operators do not significantly alter qualitative features of beta-decay properties.

## Method

The authors performed constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations using the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov method with the DD-PC1 energy density functional and a separable pairing force to obtain potential energy surfaces for even-even Ge, Zr, Mo, and Ru nuclei, which were then mapped onto the interacting boson model (IBM-2) Hamiltonian. For odd-odd As, Nb, and Tc nuclei, they constructed the interacting boson-fermion-fermion model (IBFFM-2) Hamiltonian using single-particle energies and occupation probabilities from the mean-field calculations, with only a few coupling constants for boson-fermion and residual neutron-proton interactions fitted to reproduce low-energy spectra. The Gamow-Teller and Fermi transition operators were built from one-nucleon transfer operators in the boson representation, with coefficients determined entirely from the occupation amplitudes without phenomenological parameters.

## Key numbers

- The calculated log10ft values for the 68As to 68Ge beta-plus decay show sensitivity to the quadrupole-quadrupole boson interaction strength parameter kappa used for the parent 68As nucleus
- The study covers beta-plus decays of 68,70As and beta-minus decays of 78Ge, 98-110Zr, and 102-110Mo isotopes
- The single-particle space for As isotopes comprises the neutron and proton 2p1/2, 2p3/2, and 1f5/2 orbitals
- The single-particle space for Nb and Tc isotopes consists of neutron 3s1/2, 2d3/2, 2d5/2, and 1g7/2 orbitals, and the proton 1g9/2 orbital
- The residual neutron-proton interaction includes a delta interaction with strength vd, a spin-spin interaction with strength vsss, and a tensor interaction with strength vt
- The parameter r0 in the delta interaction is set to 1.2 times A to the 1/3 fm

## Context

This work extends the sensitivity analysis of Homma and Nomura (2024) from the beta-minus decay of Zr isotopes to the beta-plus decay of neutron-deficient Ge and As nuclei in the mass A approximately 70 region, testing whether earlier conclusions about parameter dependence hold in other mass regions. The study also incorporates higher-order terms in the one-nucleon transfer operators, building on previous mapped IBM calculations by Nomura and collaborators (2020, 2022, 2024) that were applied to beta-decay properties of Kr to Cd isotopes near N=60. The choice of the A approximately 70 and A approximately 100 regions is motivated by their proximity to 76Ge, 96Zr, and 100Mo, which are candidate nuclei for neutrinoless double-beta decay investigations.
