# Shape Coexistence in 74Ge, 74Se and 74Kr Investigated by Phenomenological and Microscopic Models

> The deformation properties of 74Ge, 74Se and 74Kr are studied within the phenomenological Bohr-Mottelson model, having as input the experimental collective energy states, as well with Covariant Density Functional theories based on microscopic structural information. The results of these approaches are shown to be compatible in what concerns the presence of coexisting shapes in the considered nuclei, while the emergence of shape mixing is deduced from the phenomenological calculated collective states.

## Key claim

The paper claims that shape coexistence is present in 74Ge, 74Se, and 74Kr, but with a key qualification: the most appropriate interpretation for 74Ge and 74Kr is coexistence between spherical and gamma-unstable shapes, while for 74Se it is coexistence between spherical and axial symmetric shapes; for 74Kr, the microscopic CDFT results show three minima (two oblate and one prolate) that do not fully align with the phenomenological model's gamma-unstable picture, requiring further investigation.

## Method

The authors used the Bohr-Mottelson model (BMM) with a sextic anharmonic oscillator potential, numerically diagonalized in a basis of Bessel functions of the first kind, to calculate energy levels, quadrupole (E2) and monopole (E0) transition probabilities for 74Ge and 74Kr. Separately, they performed constrained triaxial calculations within Covariant Density Functional Theory (CDFT) using the DD-ME2 parameter set to map potential energy surfaces (PES) in the (beta, gamma) plane for all three nuclei. The phenomenological model parameters were fitted to experimental data, and the results from both approaches were compared.

## Key numbers

- For 74Ge: the ratio E(4g+)/E(2g+) = 2.46, E(0beta+)/E(2g+) = 2.49, B(E2;4g+ -> 2g+)/B(E2;2g+ -> 0g+) = 1.24, and B(E2;0beta+ -> 2g+)/B(E2;2g+ -> 0g+) = 0.27, compared to O(6) limits of 2.50 and 1.43 respectively.
- For 74Kr: the ratio E(4g+)/E(2g+) = 2.22, E(0beta+)/E(2g+) = 1.12, B(E2;4g+ -> 2g+)/B(E2;2g+ -> 0g+) = 2.34, and B(E2;0beta+ -> 2g+)/B(E2;2g+ -> 0g+) = 0.90, compared to E(5) limits of 2.20, 3.03, 1.68, and 0.87 respectively.
- For 74Ge from CDFT: a triaxial minimum at beta2 = 0.25 and gamma = 30 degrees coexists with a spherical ground state.
- For 74Se from CDFT: an oblate minimum at beta2 = 0.2 coexists with a spherical configuration.
- For 74Kr from CDFT: three axial minima are found: two oblate at beta2 = 0.15 and beta2 = 0.35, and one prolate at beta2 = 0.45.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of shape coexistence in the A=70 mass region, particularly following the phenomenological treatment of 76Kr by Budaca (2020), 72,74,76Se by Budaca (2019), and 96,98,100Mo by Budaca (2016) using the same sextic potential approach. The microscopic CDFT results for 74Ge agree with Nomura (2016) using Gogny-D1M EDF, and for 74Se agree with Gaudefroy (2009) using Gogny-D1S EDF and with Nomura (2016). The three-minimum structure found for 74Kr in CDFT is consistent with Abusara (2017) and Bender (2006). The paper enables future work to resolve the discrepancy between the phenomenological gamma-unstable interpretation and the microscopic three-minima picture for 74Kr.
