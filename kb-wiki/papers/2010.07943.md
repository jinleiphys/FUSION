# Constraints on the two-pion contribution to hadronic vacuum polarization

> At low energies hadronic vacuum polarization (HVP) is strongly dominated by two-pion intermediate states, which are responsible for about $70\%$ of the HVP contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, $a_μ^\text{HVP}$. Lattice-QCD evaluations of the latter indicate that it might be larger than calculated dispersively on the basis of $e^+e^-\to\text{hadrons}$ data, at a level which would contest the long-standing discrepancy with the $a_μ$ measurement. In this Letter we study to which extent this $2π$ contribution can be modified without, at the same time, producing a conflict elsewhere in low-energy hadron phenomenology. To this end we consider a dispersive representation of the $e^+e^- \to 2π$ process and study the correlations which thereby emerge between $a_μ^\text{HVP}$, the hadronic running of the fine-structure constant, the $P$-wave $ππ$ phase shift, and the charge radius of the pion. Inelastic effects play an important role, despite being constrained by the Eidelman-Lukaszuk bound. We identify scenarios in which $a_μ^\text{HVP}$ can be altered substantially, driven by changes in the phase shift and/or the inelastic contribution, and illustrate the ensuing changes in the $e^+e^-\to 2π$ cross section. In the combined scenario, which minimizes the effect in the cross section, a uniform shift around $4\%$ is required. At the same time both the analytic continuation into the space-like region and the pion charge radius are affected at a level that could be probed in future lattice-QCD calculations.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the two-pion contribution to hadronic vacuum polarization (HVP) can be substantially increased, as suggested by recent lattice-QCD calculations, without creating conflicts with other low-energy hadron phenomenology, specifically the hadronic running of the fine-structure constant, the P-wave ππ phase shift, and the pion charge radius. The authors find that while such changes are possible in principle, they require modifications to the e+e−→2π cross section that are incompatible with existing data, with the most favorable combined scenario requiring a uniform shift of about 4%.

## Method

The authors use a dispersive representation of the pion vector form factor (VFF), which is expressed as an Omnès factor multiplied by a factor for ρ–ω mixing and a conformal polynomial for inelastic effects above the πω threshold. They start from a fit to e+e−→2π cross-section data and then add a hypothetical "lattice" input for aμππ as an additional χ² contribution, pulling the fit away from the central result. They consider three scenarios: (1) varying only the two phase-shift parameters δ¹₁(s₀) and δ¹₁(s₁) that determine the elastic Omnès factor; (2) varying only the parameters of the conformal polynomial describing inelastic effects; and (3) varying all parameters simultaneously. The resulting changes in aμππ are then correlated with changes in the pion charge radius ⟨rπ²⟩, the hadronic running Δαhad(5)(MZ²), and the e+e−→2π cross section.

## Key numbers

* The central two-pion contribution to aμHVP below 1 GeV from the fit to e+e− data is aμππ|≤1GeV = 495.0(2.6) × 10⁻¹⁰ (Colangelo et al. 2018).
* The reference upward shift considered is Δaμππ|≤1GeV = 18.5 × 10⁻¹⁰, corresponding to the difference between the e+e− value (6931(40) × 10⁻¹¹) and the lattice average (7116(184) × 10⁻¹¹).
* In scenario (1), a shift of 18.5 × 10⁻¹⁰ in aμππ requires changes of Δδ¹₁(s₀) = 0.8° and Δδ¹₁(s₁) = 1.7° in the phase shifts at s₀ = (0.8 GeV)² and s₁ = (1.15 GeV)².
* In scenario (3), the same shift in aμππ requires Δδ¹₁(s₀) = −0.2° and Δδ¹₁(s₁) = −0.3°.
* A shift of 18.5 × 10⁻¹⁰ in aμππ corresponds to a shift in Δαππ(5)(MZ²)|≤1GeV between 1.2 × 10⁻⁴ and 1.4 × 10⁻⁴.
* The central fit to e+e− data has a total χ² of 776 with 627 degrees of freedom; for the target shift of 18.5 × 10⁻¹⁰, even scenario (3) leads to a total χ² of 941.
* The central pion charge radius from the fit with N−1=4 is ⟨rπ²⟩|N−1=4 = 0.426(1) fm².

## Context

This work extends the dispersive analysis of the pion vector form factor from Colangelo et al. (2018) to address the tension between e+e−-based determinations of aμHVP and recent lattice-QCD results from Borsanyi et al. (2020). It builds on earlier studies by Crivellin et al. (2020), Keshavarzi et al. (2020), and Malaescu et al. (2020) that considered changes to hadronic cross sections as a whole, but focuses specifically on the dominant 2π channel using the highly constrained dispersive framework. The results enable future lattice-QCD calculations of the pion charge radius and the space-like vector form factor to provide independent cross-checks on possible modifications to the HVP contribution.
