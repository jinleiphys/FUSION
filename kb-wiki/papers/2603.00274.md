# Spin-2 Mesons in a Relativistic Hartree Description of Nuclei

> The role of the isovector spin-orbit potential in nuclear modeling has recently been explored in greater detail due largely in part to the PREX and CREX parity violating electron scattering (PVES) experiments. The result of both experiments have shown that the neutron-rich skins of $^{48}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb, as determined by the experimental analysis, are largely incompatible with current nuclear model predictions. One way to address, and potentially solve, this incompatibility has been to enhance the isovector spin-orbit sector of density functional models. Here, we explore the range of possible enhancements in the context of covariant density functional theory by introducing a new class of spin-2 \textit{massive} mesons to the Lagrangian. In doing so, we find that these mesons not only meaningfully contribute to the theory, but also mitigate problems seen in other works which enhance spin-orbit effects in finite nuclei. We also discuss the implications and future regarding this ``dilemma'' as it pertains to studies of nuclear forces and future experiments such as the Mainz Radius Experiment (MREX).

## Key claim

Introducing spin-2 massive mesons into covariant density functional theory provides a new, independent mechanism to modify the isovector spin-orbit sector without disrupting the well-ordered nuclear shell structure, a problem seen in prior attempts to enhance spin-orbit effects through other couplings.

## Method

The authors add isoscalar (T) and isovector (H) spin-2 meson fields to the relativistic mean-field Lagrangian, coupling them to nucleons via a Yukawa-like tensor interaction. They solve the resulting Dirac and Proca equations self-consistently in the Hartree approximation for spherically symmetric nuclei, using the RMFGO interaction as a baseline and fixing the meson masses to those of the physical f2(1270) and a2(1320) mesons. The sign of each Yukawa coupling squared is treated as a free parameter (Γ_T = ±1, Γ_H = ±1) to explore repulsive versus attractive contributions.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness for ⁴⁸Ca: RMFGO baseline gives 0.16 fm; with +Γ_H gives 0.20 fm; with −Γ_H gives 0.11 fm (experimental value from CREX: 0.121 ± 0.035 fm)
- Neutron skin thickness for ²⁰⁸Pb: RMFGO baseline gives 0.16 fm; with +Γ_H gives 0.17 fm; with −Γ_H gives 0.16 fm (experimental value from PREX2: 0.283 ± 0.071 fm)
- Binding energy per nucleon for ⁴⁸Ca: RMFGO baseline −8.42 MeV; with +Γ_T gives −8.25 MeV; with −Γ_T gives −8.60 MeV (experimental: −8.67 MeV)
- Binding energy per nucleon for ²⁰⁸Pb: RMFGO baseline −7.95 MeV; with +Γ_T gives −7.85 MeV; with −Γ_T gives −8.06 MeV (experimental: −7.81 MeV)
- Charge radius for ⁴⁸Ca: RMFGO baseline 3.43 fm; with +Γ_T gives 3.46 fm; with −Γ_T gives 3.40 fm (experimental: 3.48 fm)
- Charge radius for ²⁰⁸Pb: RMFGO baseline 5.40 fm; with +Γ_T gives 5.43 fm; with −Γ_T gives 5.38 fm (experimental: 5.50 fm)
- Fixed meson masses: m_T = 1270 MeV (f2(1270)), m_H = 1320 MeV (a2(1320))
- Fixed Yukawa coupling magnitudes: |g_T²| = 20, |g_H²| = 100

## Context

This work extends recent efforts to resolve the PREX/CREX neutron skin discrepancy by modifying the isovector spin-orbit sector of density functionals. Prior work by Kunjipurayil (2025) showed that enhancing spin-orbit interactions within nonlinear RMF theory disrupts the shell structure of neutron-rich doubly magic nuclei, while work by Yue (2024) and Kunjipurayil (2025) showed success with non-relativistic models. The spin-2 meson approach provides an independent degree of freedom that avoids these shell-structure problems, and the authors note that the mesons have no direct effect on infinite nuclear matter due to spin saturation, enabling future recalibration of relativistic density functionals to simultaneously satisfy PREX, CREX, and upcoming Mainz Radius Experiment (MREX) constraints.
