# Quarks, Flow and Temperature in Spectra

> Theory Summary Talk given by Tamas S. Biro at SQM 2013, Birmingham, UK.

# Quarks, Flow and Temperature in Spectra

## Key claim

The paper argues that what appears as thermal equilibrium and hydrodynamic flow in high-energy heavy-ion collisions may instead be a "pseudo-thermalization" and "pseudo-flow" arising from acceleration effects (Unruh radiation), and that the field's standard interpretations of exponential spectra as evidence of a thermal state are not uniquely determined.

## Method

The authors review competing theoretical approaches presented at the SQM 2013 conference, organizing them around six fundamental dichotomies: thermalization (exponential vs power-law spectra), hydrodynamics (initial-state vs equation-of-state driven flow), hadronization (phase space vs QCD matrix element dominance), quark matter equation of state (lattice vs dual gravity methods), nuclear medium (cross sections vs mean fields), and simulation (particle vs field based models). They present a specific calculation of photon rapidity distributions from a decelerating point charge to illustrate how acceleration alone can produce thermal-looking spectra.

## Key numbers

- Volume-charged particle yield relation: V ≈ 730 fm³ + 2.545 (dN_ch/dη)
- Time duration-charged particle relation: T ≈ 0.875 (dN_ch/dη)^(1/3)
- Unruh temperature: T = g/2π, with typical values of order 150 MeV for velocity changes of order 1 (lightspeed) over about 1 fm
- Critical temperature estimate: T_c ≈ 167 MeV, derived from the saturation point of the thermal Q² distribution at T ≈ Λ/6
- Perturbative QCD becomes reliable only at T ≈ Λ (about 1 GeV), with a mixed regime between T₁ ≈ 1/6 GeV and T₂ ≈ 1 GeV

## Context

This theory summary from SQM 2013 synthesizes and contrasts multiple competing approaches presented at the conference, including thermal models (Stachel, Cleymans), hydrodynamics (Kodama, Csernai, Song), and Unruh-effect interpretations (Unruh 1976, Satz, Casterina, Biro). The paper extends the long-standing debate between statistical and dynamical descriptions of hadronization, referencing the work of Becattini and Csernai on Lambda polarization as a new development, and Redlich's proposal of susceptibility ratios as order parameters for the QCD phase transition. It enables future work by identifying the need to distinguish genuine thermalization from acceleration-induced pseudo-thermal effects, and by questioning whether AdS/CFT methods (Trenk's review: "clear no for both light and heavy quarks") can be reliably applied to LHC energies.
