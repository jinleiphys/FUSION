# Fluctuations in the Statistical Model of the Early Stage of nucleus-nucleus collisions

> Predictions on fluctuations of hadron production properties in central heavy ion collisions are presented. They are based on the Statistical Model of the Early Stage and extend previously published results by considering the strongly intensive measures of fluctuations. In several of the considered cases a significant change in collision energy dependence of calculated quantities as a result of the phase transition is predicted. This provides an opportunity to observe new signals of the onset of deconfinement in heavy ion collisions experiments.

# Fluctuations in the Statistical Model of the Early Stage of nucleus-nucleus collisions

## Key claim

The paper predicts that strongly intensive measures of fluctuations in hadron production properties in central heavy ion collisions show a significant modification of their collision energy dependence in the phase transition region, with the most pronounced change seen for the measure Δ[N_S,N] (involving strange and total particle multiplicities). However, these predictions depend on the assumed magnitude of energy density fluctuations (scaled dispersion of energy density set to 0.17, 0.13, or 0) and on the specific normalization choices for the fluctuation measures.

## Method

The authors use the Statistical Model of the Early Stage (SMES), which assumes the matter created in the early stage of nucleus-nucleus collisions is in equilibrium with zero conserved charges, and that the fireball energy E and volume V vary from collision to collision according to a probability distribution P(E,V). The ideal gas equation of state is used for the confined hadronic phase, the bag model equation of state for the quark-gluon plasma phase, and a first-order phase transition between them is assumed. Strongly intensive fluctuation measures Δ[A,B], Σ[A,B], and Ω[A,B] are calculated for six pairs of extensive event quantities (E,V; N,V; N_S,V; N,E; N_S,E; N_S,N) as functions of collision energy in the CERN SPS energy range, using two different normalization schemes.

## Key numbers

- The onset of deconfinement (beginning of the mixed phase) is located at √s_NN(OD) ≈ 8 GeV, and the softest point (end of the mixed phase region) at √s_NN(SP) ≈ 12 GeV (from prior work cited in the paper).
- Mean volume at √s_NN = 10 GeV is set to ⟨V⟩ = 350 fm³, and mean energy density at √s_NN = 10 GeV is set to ⟨ε⟩ = 3.2 GeV/fm³.
- Three values of the scaled dispersion of energy density are used: 0.17 (solid line), 0.13 (dashed line), and 0 (dotted line); the value 0.17 is considered the upper limit based on UrQMD and HSD simulations.
- The scaled dispersion of volume fluctuations is set to 0, which is a good approximation for central Pb+Pb collisions.
- For central Pb+Pb collisions, the term 1 + ⟨(δV)²⟩/⟨V⟩² is approximately 1, as typical values of the scaled dispersion of volume are below 0.05.

## Context

This work extends previous SMES predictions on fluctuations of hadron production properties (Gazdzicki 2003, Gorenstein 2003) by calculating strongly intensive measures of fluctuations, which were introduced in recent years (Gorenstein 2011, Gazdzicki 2015). The SMES model itself was originally developed by Gazdzicki (1998) and successfully predicted the onset of deconfinement signals in single hadron properties observed at CERN SPS (Afanasiev 2002, Alt 2007). The paper aims to provide new fluctuation-based signals that could be observed in heavy ion collision experiments, as no convincing signal in fluctuations of event properties has been reported so far (Gazdzicki 2015). The results enable experimental searches for the onset of deconfinement using fluctuation measurements at facilities such as the CERN SPS.
