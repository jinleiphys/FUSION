# Femtoscopy of $D$ mesons and light mesons upon unitarized effective field theories

> Hadron femtoscopy has turned into a powerful tool for accessing space-time information of heavy-ion collisions as well as for studying final-state interactions of hadrons. Recently, heavy-flavor femtoscopy has become feasible using the ALICE detector at the LHC. We compute the correlation function of $D$ mesons and light mesons using an off-shell $T$-matrix approach to access the two-meson wave function, and predict the correlation functions involving charged $D^+, D^{*+},D_s^+$ and $D_s^{*+}$ with $ŽĆ^\pm$ and $K^\pm$. From the obtained results -- all of them accessible in $p+p$ collision experiments -- we point up the case of $D^+ ŽĆ^-$, which is sensitive to the lower state of the two-pole $D_0^* (2300)$ system. The presence of such poles imprints a depletion on the correlation function, which could potentially be detected in experiments. While preliminary ALICE data do not show evidence of this effect, we suggest to look into the $D_s^+ K^-$ system to explore the higher pole of the $D_0^* (2300)$, as the depletion in the correlation function is more pronounced. Using heavy-quark spin symmetry we also propose exploring the effect of the two poles of the $D_1(2430)$ and predict similar structures in the correlation functions of the $D^{*+} ŽĆ^-$ and $D_s^{*+} K^-$ pairs.

## Key claim

The authors compute correlation functions for charged D-mesonŌĆōlight-meson pairs using an off-shell T-matrix approach, predicting that the \(D^+ \pi^-\) channel is sensitive to the lower pole of the two-pole \(D_0^*(2300)\) system and that the \(D_s^+ K^-\) channel can probe the higher pole, though preliminary ALICE data do not yet show evidence of the depletion effect in the \(D^+ \pi^-\) correlation function.

## Method

The authors solve the half-off-shell Bethe-Salpeter equation for the T-matrix in a coupled-channel basis, using an s-wave projected interaction kernel derived from a next-to-leading-order chiral effective Lagrangian with heavy-quark symmetry. The Coulomb interaction is added to the diagonal charged-meson channels via a momentum-space regularization with a cutoff at 60 fm. The correlation functions are then obtained from the Koonin-Pratt formula using a Gaussian source of radius \(R = 1\) fm, with all channel weights set to unity.

## Key numbers

* The lower pole of the \(D_0^*(2300)\) is found at \(z = 2092.4 + i\,129.5\) MeV and the higher pole at \(z = 2647.2 + i\,264.8\) MeV in the \(J=0, (S,I)=(0,1/2)\) sector.
* The \(D_{s0}^*(2317)\) bound state appears at \(z = 2320.2 + i\,0\) MeV in the \(J=0, (S,I)=(1,0)\) sector.
* The lower pole of the \(D_1(2430)\) is at \(z = 2233.6 + i\,130.8\) MeV and the higher pole at \(z = 2719.2 + i\,330.1\) MeV in the \(J=1, (S,I)=(0,1/2)\) sector.
* The \(D_{s1}(2460)\) bound state is found at \(z = 2464.7 + i\,0\) MeV in the \(J=1, (S,I)=(1,0)\) sector.
* The scattering length for the \(D^+ \pi^-\) channel is \(0.253\) fm; for the \(D_s^+ K^-\) channel it is \(-0.114 + i\,0.693\) fm.
* The Gaussian source radius used is \(R = 1\) fm, and the cutoff for the form factor is \(\Lambda = 800\) MeV/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of meson-meson correlation functions in the \(I=1/2, S=0\) and \(I=0, S=1\) sectors by Albaladejo (2023) and Liu (2023), which only considered channels with one charged meson, to all charged pairs accessible experimentally. The predictions are intended to guide ongoing ALICE measurements (Grosa 2022, Fabbietti 2022, Battistini 2023) and to enable future femtoscopic studies of the two-pole structures of the \(D_0^*(2300)\) and \(D_1(2430)\) resonances.
