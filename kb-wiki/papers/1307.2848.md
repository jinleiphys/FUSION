# Distorted spin-dependent spectral function of an A=3 nucleus and semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering processes

> The distorted spin-dependent spectral function of a nucleon inside an A=3 nucleus is introduced as a novel tool for investigating the polarized electron scattering off polarized $^3$He in semi-inclusive DIS regime (SiDIS), going beyond the standard plane wave impulse approximation. This distribution function is applied to the study of the spectator SiDIS, $\vec{^3{\rm He}}(\vec e, e' ~{^2}{\rm H})X$, in order to properly take into account the final state interaction between the hadronizing quark and the detected deuteron, with the final goal of a more reliable extraction of the polarized parton-distribution $g_1(x)$ inside a bound proton. Our analysis allows to single out two well-defined kinematical regions where the experimental asymmetries could yield very interesting information: the region where the final state effects can be minimized, and therefore the direct access to the parton distributions in the proton is feasible, and the one where the final state interaction dominates, and the spectator SiDIS reactions can elucidate the mechanism of the quark hadronization itself. The perspectives of extending our approach i) to the mirror nucleus, $^3$H, for achieving a less model-dependent flavor decomposition, and ii) to the asymmetries measured in the standard SiDIS reactions, $\vec e + \vec{^3 {\rm He}} \to e' + h+X$ with $h$ a detected fast hadron, with the aim of extracting the neutron transversity, are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a distorted spin-dependent spectral function for a nucleon inside an A=3 nucleus, going beyond the standard plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) to account for final state interactions (FSI) between the hadronizing quark and the detected spectator system. This approach allows identification of two kinematical regions: one where FSI effects can be minimized for direct access to parton distributions in the proton, and another where FSI dominates and can elucidate the quark hadronization mechanism.

## Method

The authors evaluate the distorted spin-dependent spectral function of 3He by implementing a generalized eikonal approximation (GEA) to account for FSI between the (A-1) spectator system and the quark debris produced after deep inelastic scattering off an internal nucleon with given polarization. This distribution function is applied to the spectator semi-inclusive DIS (SiDIS) reaction 3He(e, e' 2H)X, where a deuteron is detected in the final state, using an effective cross section obtained from a model of quark debris hadronization. The nuclear structure effects are encoded in overlap integrals that form the 2x2 spin-dependent spectral function, with the antisymmetric part of the nucleon tensor expressed in terms of the polarized structure function G1N.

## Key numbers

- The two-body break-up (2bbu) threshold energy of 3He is given by E_2bbu = B_3He - B_D, where B_D is the deuteron binding energy
- The state density for three-body break-up (3bbu) is rho_3bbu = 1/(2pi)^6 * (m_N * sqrt(m_N * epsilon*_2))/2
- The normalization condition for the spin-dependent spectral function is (1/2) * sum_lambda * integral dE * integral d^3p_N * O^S_A_lambdalambda(p_N, E) = 1

## Context

This work extends the spin-dependent spectral function approach previously developed for inclusive DIS off polarized 3He (Ciofi degli Atti et al., 1990s; Pace et al., 2000s) to the semi-inclusive regime, where FSI cannot be neglected a priori. The authors build upon their earlier PWIA analysis of SiDIS off transversely polarized 3He (2010) and their successful application of the generalized eikonal approximation to unpolarized SiDIS off nuclei (2012). The approach enables more reliable extraction of the polarized parton distribution g1(x) for a bound proton and opens the possibility of extending the method to 3H for flavor decomposition and to standard SiDIS for extracting neutron transversity.
