# Curvature of the energy per particle in neutron stars

> Neutron stars (NSs) serve as laboratories for probing strongly interacting matter at the most extreme densities. Their inner cores are expected to be dense enough to host deconfined quark matter. Utilizing state-of-the-art theoretical and multi-messenger constraints, we statistically determine the bulk properties of dense NS matter. We show that the speed of sound can be expressed in terms of the slope and curvature of the energy per particle. We demonstrate that the restoration of conformal symmetry requires changing the sign of the curvature of the bulk energy per particle as a function of energy density. Furthermore, we find that such a sign change is closely related to the peak in the speed of sound. We argue that the curvature of the energy per particle may serve as an approximate order parameter that signifies the onset of strongly coupled conformal matter in the NS core.

## Key claim

The curvature of the bulk energy per particle (β) must change sign from positive to negative as conformal symmetry is restored in dense neutron star matter, and this sign change is closely related to the peak in the speed of sound, serving as an approximate order parameter for the onset of strongly coupled conformal matter in the neutron star core.

## Method

The authors constructed an ensemble of equations of state using a piecewise-linear speed-of-sound parametrization between 1.1 times nuclear saturation density and perturbative QCD densities (above 40 times saturation density), imposing constraints from the GW170817 event, the mass of PSR J0952-0607 (M_TOV ≥ 2.18 solar masses), and pQCD boundary conditions. They statistically analyzed a sample of 6×10^5 equations of state with 7 segments to determine the behavior of the curvature of the energy per particle β as a function of energy density, and compared results with a minimal parametrized model of the trace anomaly.

## Key numbers

- Energy density for vanishing curvature β=0: ε_β = 0.678^{+0.091(0.382)}_{-0.076(0.221)} GeV/fm^3 at 1σ (2σ) confidence interval
- Energy density at the peak of the speed of sound: ε_peak = 0.500^{+0.087(0.446)}_{-0.067(0.166)} GeV/fm^3 at 1σ (2σ) confidence interval
- Energy density at the center of maximally massive neutron stars: ε_TOV = 1.073^{+0.071(0.267)}_{-0.070(0.202)} GeV/fm^3 at 1σ (2σ) confidence interval
- Speed of sound at the peak: c_s,peak^2 = 0.86^{+0.06(0.13)}_{-0.07(0.26)} at 1σ (2σ) confidence interval
- Speed of sound at β=0: c_s,β^2 ≈ 0.4 in the parametrized model
- Polytropic index at β=0: γ_β ≈ 1.6 in the parametrized model
- Measure of conformality at β=0: d_c,β ≈ 0.17 in the parametrized model

## Context

This work extends the approach of Fujimoto (2022) who proposed the trace anomaly scaled by energy density as a measure of conformality, and builds on the piecewise-linear speed-of-sound parametrization introduced by Annala (2019). The authors propose the curvature of the energy per particle as a new criterion for conformality, complementing existing criteria such as γ=1.75 from Annala (2019) and d_c=0.2 from Annala (2023). The results enable future exploration within effective models of neutron star matter to determine microscopic characteristics of the QCD equation of state related to the onset of strongly coupled conformal matter.
