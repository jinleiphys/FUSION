# Spin-polarized $β$-stable neutron star matter: the nuclear symmetry energy and GW170817 constraint

> Magnetic field of rotating pulsar might be so strong that the equation of state (EOS) of neutron star (NS) matter is significantly affected by the spin polarization of baryons. In the present work, the EOS of the spin-polarized nuclear matter is investigated in the nonrelativistic Hartree-Fock formalism, using a realistic density dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction with its spin and spin-isospin dependence accurately adjusted to the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock results for the spin-polarized nuclear matter. The nuclear symmetry energy and proton fraction are found to increase significantly with the increasing spin polarization of baryons, leading to a larger probability of the direct Urca process in the cooling of magnetar. The EOS of the $β$-stable np$eμ$ matter obtained at different spin polarizations of baryons is used as the input for the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations to determine the hydrostatic configuration of NS. Based on the GW170817 constraint on the radius $R_{1.4}$ of NS with $M\approx 1.4_\odot$, our mean-field results show that up to $60~\%$ of baryons in the NS merger might be spin-polarized. This result supports the magnetar origin of the blue kilonova ejecta of GW170817 suggested by Metzger et al., and the spin polarization of baryons needs, therefore, to be properly treated in the many-body calculation of the EOS of NS matter before comparing the calculated NS mass and radius with those constrained by the multi-messenger GW170817 observation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that up to 60% of baryons in the neutron star merger GW170817 might be spin-polarized, based on the GW170817 constraint on the radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star. This conclusion is conditional on the assumption that the spin polarization of baryons is density-independent and that the mean-field results using the CDM3Y8 interaction are valid.

## Method

The authors performed nonrelativistic Hartree-Fock calculations of spin-polarized nuclear matter using a density-dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction (CDM3Y8), with the spin and spin-isospin density dependencies adjusted to reproduce Brueckner-Hartree-Fock results for spin-polarized neutron matter from Vidana et al. (2016). The equation of state of beta-stable np e mu matter was computed at different fixed spin polarizations of baryons, then used as input for the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to determine neutron star mass and radius. The neutron star crust was taken from energy-density functional calculations using the BSk24 Skyrme functional from Chamel et al. (2018, 2019).

## Key numbers

- The symmetry coefficient J increases from 29.5 MeV at Delta=0 to 37.5 MeV at Delta=1; the slope L increases from 50.6 MeV to 64.7 MeV over the same range.
- The radius R_{1.4} of a neutron star with mass 1.4 solar masses increases from 12.0 km at Delta=0 to 15.6 km at Delta=1.
- The maximal gravitational mass M_max increases from 1.98 solar masses at Delta=0 to 2.16 solar masses at Delta=1.
- The GW170817 constraint on R_{1.4} is 11.9 plus or minus 1.4 km at 90% confidence level, which limits the spin polarization to Delta less than or equal to 0.6.
- The total pressure at twice saturation density P(2n_0) increases from 3.4 times 10^34 dyn/cm^2 at Delta=0 to 5.8 times 10^34 dyn/cm^2 at Delta=1; the GW170817 constraint gives P(2n_0) approximately 3.5 plus 2.7 minus 1.7 times 10^34 dyn/cm^2 at 90% confidence level.
- The symmetry energy at twice saturation density S(2n_0) approximately 40-60 MeV from a Bayesian analysis by Xie and Li (2019) covers all spin polarizations from 0 to 1.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spin-polarized neutron matter by Vidana et al. (2002, 2016) and Tews and Schwenk (2020) to the case of partial spin polarization in beta-stable neutron star matter. It is motivated by the magnetar scenario proposed by Metzger et al. (2018) for the blue kilonova ejecta of GW170817, which requires a high electron fraction. The results enable future many-body calculations of the neutron star equation of state to include spin polarization effects when comparing with the GW170817 constraints on neutron star mass and radius.
