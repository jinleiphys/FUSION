# Shell model description of "mixed-symmetry" states in 94Mo

> Shell model calculations have been performed for the nucleus 94Mo. The calculated excitation energies and electromagnetic properties of low-lying states are in good agreement with the data, which include states with mixed-symmetry (MS) assignments in previous interacting boson model studies. In the shell model large isoscalar E2 matrix elements are found between states with MS assignments indicating that they form a class of states with similar proton-neutron symmetry.

# Shell model description of "mixed-symmetry" states in 94Mo

## Key claim
Shell model calculations using the Surface Delta Interaction reproduce the excitation energies and electromagnetic properties of low-lying states in 94Mo, supporting previous mixed-symmetry assignments for the 1+_1, 2+_3, and 3+_2 states. However, the 3+_2 state's mixed-symmetry character is less clear from the shell model results than from the IBM-2, because the 3+_ms character is spread across the first three 3+ states in the calculation.

## Method
Shell model calculations were performed for 94Mo using the Surface Delta Interaction (SDI) as the residual interaction, with 88Sr as the inert core. The model space included eight proton holes in the πg9/2 and πp1/2 orbitals (treated as four proton particles) and two neutrons distributed among the 2d5/2, 3s1/2, 1g7/2, 2d3/2, and 1h11/2 orbitals. The SDI parameters were fitted to reproduce the low-spin level schemes of 90Sr, 90Zr, 92Zr, and 94Mo, with final values A_T=1^nn = 0.23 MeV, A_T=1^pp = 0.35 MeV, and A_T=0^pn = 0.48 MeV.

## Key numbers
- A_T=1^nn = 0.23 MeV and A_T=1^pp = 0.35 MeV, obtained from fits to 90Sr and 90Zr, respectively
- A_T=0^pn = 0.48 MeV, obtained from a fit to the excitation energies of 92Zr
- The 2+_1 state exhausts 97% of the total isoscalar E2 excitation strength from the ground state to 2+ states up to 4 MeV
- The 2+_3 state carries 10% of the total E2 excitation strength of the 2+_1 state
- The normalized scalar product of the seniority ν=2 components of the 2+_1 and 2+_3 states is -0.07
- The overlap of the neutron components of the 2+_1 and 2+_3 states is ⟨2+_3,n|2+_1,n⟩ = 0.63
- The proton basis states |2+_3,p⟩ and |2+_1,p⟩ have a positive overlap of 0.98
- The seniority ν=2 component of the 2+_3 state contains γ^2 = 58% of components that form the seniority ν=2 component of the 2+_1 state
- The calculated 3+_2 state excitation energy matches experiment within 50 keV; the 3+_1 state lies 120 keV lower and the 3+_3 state lies 350 keV higher than the experimental 3+_ms state
- The calculated B(M1;2+_3→2+_1) value is about five times larger than the B(M1;2+_2→2+_1) and B(M1;2+_4→2+_1) values
- The calculated B(M1;2+_3→2+_1) value is more than four times larger than the calculated B(M1;2+_5→2+_1) value
- The ground state contains 72% components with seniority ν=0; the 2+_1 and 2+_3 states contain 70% and 73% components of seniority ν=2, respectively; the 2+_2 state contains 50% seniority ν=4 components

## Context
This work extends the interpretation of mixed-symmetry states in 94Mo from the proton-neutron Interacting Boson Model (IBM-2) framework of Arima and Otsuka (1978) and the experimental photon scattering and γγ-coincidence studies by Pietralla et al. (1999) into the nuclear shell model. The calculations use the Surface Delta Interaction of Moszkowski (1965) and follow earlier shell model studies of mixed-symmetry states in different mass regions by Scholten (1985) and Liu (1987). The results enable systematic searches for mixed-symmetry phenomena in neighboring nuclei, particularly where valence protons occupy a single high-j orbital such as 1g9/2, and demonstrate the limits of the truncated shell model approach for describing closely-spaced 3+ states.
