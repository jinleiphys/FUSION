# Effects of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section on collective flow and nuclear stopping in heavy-ion collisions in the Fermi-energy domain

> With the newly updated version of the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model, a systematic investigation of the effects of in-medium nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) elastic cross section on the collective flow and the stopping observables in $^{197}\text{Au}+^{197}\text{Au}$ collisions at beam energies from 40 to 150 MeV/nucleon is performed. Simulations with the medium correction factor $\mathcal{F}=σ^{\text{in-medium}}_{NN}/σ^{\text{free}}_{NN}=0.2,~0.3,~0.5$, and the one obtained with the FU3FP1 parametrization which depends on both the density and the momentum are compared to the FOPI and INDRA experimental data. It is found that, to best fit the experimental data of the slope of the directed flow and the elliptic flow at mid-rapidity as well as the nuclear stopping, the correction factor $\mathcal{F}$=0.2 and 0.5 are required for reactions at beam energies of 40 and 150 MeV/nucleon, respectively. While calculations with the FU3FP1 parametrization can simultaneously reproduce these experimental data reasonably well. And, the observed increasing nuclear stopping with increasing beam energy in experimental data can also be reproduced by using the FU3FP1 parametrization, while the calculated stopping power in Au+Au collisions with beam energies from 40 to 150 MeV$/$nucleon almost keeps constant when take $\mathcal{F}$ equal to a fixed value.

# Effects of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section on collective flow and nuclear stopping in heavy-ion collisions in the Fermi-energy domain

## Key claim

The in-medium nucleon-nucleon elastic cross section required to reproduce experimental flow and stopping data in Au+Au collisions is energy-dependent: a correction factor of F=0.2 works at 40 MeV/nucleon while F=0.5 is needed at 150 MeV/nucleon, but the density- and momentum-dependent FU3FP1 parametrization can simultaneously describe the data across the entire beam energy range from 40 to 150 MeV/nucleon.

## Method

The authors used the updated ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model to simulate 197Au+197Au collisions at beam energies from 40 to 150 MeV/nucleon. They varied the in-medium nucleon-nucleon elastic cross section by applying fixed medium correction factors F = 0.2, 0.3, and 0.5, as well as the density- and momentum-dependent FU3FP1 parametrization, and compared the calculated directed flow slope, elliptic flow at mid-rapidity, and nuclear stopping observables (vartl and R_E) to experimental data from the FOPI and INDRA collaborations.

## Key numbers

- At 40 MeV/nucleon, the correction factor F = 0.2 best reproduces the experimental directed flow slope, elliptic flow, and nuclear stopping data.
- At 150 MeV/nucleon, the correction factor F = 0.5 is required to reproduce the same observables.
- Calculations with F = 0.3 yield nuclear stopping values (vartl or R_E) of approximately 0.7, remaining almost constant across beam energies from 40 to 150 MeV/nucleon.
- Calculations with F = 0.5 yield nuclear stopping values of approximately 0.8, and those with F = 0.2 yield approximately 0.6, both also nearly constant with beam energy.
- At 150 MeV/nucleon, the vartl and R_E values from the FU3FP1 parametrization are about 14% larger than those from F = 0.3.
- The INDRA collaboration previously deduced F = 0.16 +/- 0.04 at 40 MeV/nucleon and F = 0.5 +/- 0.06 at 100 MeV/nucleon from stopping data.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections using transport models such as QMD and BUU, where parameterized reductions like (1 - eta*rho/rho_0) with eta = 0.2 have been used (Li 1999, Zhang 2007). The results are consistent with the energy-dependent suppression factors deduced by the INDRA collaboration (O. Lopez 2013) from proton stopping measurements. The FU3FP1 parametrization, which depends on both density and momentum, enables simultaneous description of collective flow and stopping across the Fermi-energy domain, while fixed reduction factors fail to reproduce the observed increase of nuclear stopping with beam energy. This work enables more reliable extraction of in-medium cross sections from heavy-ion collision data and improves transport model predictions for reactions in the 40-150 MeV/nucleon range.
