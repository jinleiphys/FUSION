# The single-particle space-momentum angle distribution effect on two-pion HBT correlation with the Coulomb interaction

> We calculate the HBT radius Rs for π+ particles with the Coulomb interaction by using the string melting version of a multiphase transport(AMPT) model. We study the relationship between the single-particle space-momentum angle and the particle sources and discuss HBT radii without single-particle space-momentum correlation. Additionally, we study the Coulomb interaction effect on the numerical connection between the single-particle space-momentum angle distribution and the transverse momentum dependence of Rs.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a numerical connection between the single-particle space-momentum angle distribution (quantified by the normalized \(\cos(\Delta\theta)\) distribution) and the strength of the transverse momentum dependence of the HBT radius \(R_{\rm s}\) for \(\pi^+\) in Au+Au collisions, but the specific fit parameters describing this connection depend on the impact parameter, the pion species, and whether the Coulomb interaction is included in the HBT analysis.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the AMPT model to generate freeze-out \(\pi^+\) from Au+Au collisions at six BES energies (14.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV) with an impact parameter cut of 1.4 fm. They calculated the HBT correlation function and extracted \(R_{\rm s}\) using the CRAB code, fitting with a Gaussian form both with and without the Coulomb interaction term. They then quantified the normalized \(\cos(\Delta\theta)\) distribution (the angle between the transverse momentum and transverse position vectors) and parameterized its \(K_{\rm T}\) dependence using fit functions with parameters \(c_1\) and \(c_2\), and related these to the parameter \(b\) describing the power-law \(K_{\rm T}\) dependence of \(R_{\rm s}\).

## Key numbers

- The pair transverse momentum range studied is 125–625 MeV/c, divided into 9 bins, with the last bin covering 525–625 MeV/c (twice the width of the other bins).
- The impact parameter was set to a maximum of 1.4 fm; the mid-rapidity range was \(-0.5 < \eta < 0.5\).
- For the numerical connection between the \(\cos(\Delta\theta)\) distribution parameters and the \(b\) parameter, the extremum values were set to \(b_1 = -0.2\) (without Coulomb interaction) and \(b_2 = -0.3\) (with Coulomb interaction).
- Fit results for the \(\mu\) and \(\nu\) parameters (Table in paper) include: for \(m=1, n=1\), \(\mu_{11} = -0.0004 \pm 0.0003\) and \(\mu_{12} = 3.3 \pm 0.5\); for \(m=1, n=2\), \(\mu_{11} = -0.00004 \pm 0.00003\) and \(\mu_{12} = 4.7 \pm 0.4\).
- The fit function for the normalized \(\cos(\Delta\theta)\) distribution used a settled value of 0.002, and the \(c_1\) and \(c_2\) fit functions used exponents \(-6\) and \(-4.5\) respectively (chosen by the authors).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Yang 2020, Yang 2021) which treated all three pion species as one, neglected the Coulomb interaction, and used a 0 fm impact parameter. Here they focus specifically on \(\pi^+\) and include the Coulomb interaction in the HBT analysis, finding that the Coulomb interaction decreases the \(R_{\rm s}\) values and weakens the transverse momentum dependence of \(R_{\rm s}\), especially at higher collision energies. The numerical connection between the space-momentum angle distribution and the HBT radii is intended to eventually allow experimental HBT data to directly probe the single-particle space-momentum correlation at freeze-out, and potentially serve as a probe for the critical end point (CEP) in the QCD phase diagram, though the Coulomb interaction may weaken the sensitivity of such a probe.
