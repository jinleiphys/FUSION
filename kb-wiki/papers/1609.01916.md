# Many Facets of Strangeness Nuclear Physics with Stored Antiprotons

> Stored antiprotons beams in the GeV range represent a unparalleled factory for hyperon-antihyperon pairs. Their outstanding large production probability in antiproton collisions will open the floodgates for a series of new studies of strange hadronic systems with unprecedented precision. The behavior of hyperons and -- for the first time -- of antihyperons in nuclear systems can be studied under well controlled conditions. The exclusive production of $Λ\barΛ$ and $Σ^-\barΛ$ pairs in antiproton-nucleus interactions probe the neutron and proton distribution in the nuclear periphery and will help to sample the neutron skin. For the first time, high resolution $γ$-spectroscopy of doubly strange nuclei will be performed, thus complementing measurements of ground state decays of double hypernuclei with mesons beams at J-PARC or possible decays of particle unstable hypernuclei in heavy ion reactions. High resolution spectroscopy of multistrange $Ξ$-atoms are feasible and even the production of $Ω^-$-atoms will be within reach. The latter might open the door to the $|s|$=3 world in strangeness nuclear physics, by the study of the hadronic $Ω^-$-nucleus interaction and the very first measurement of a spectroscopic quadrupole moment of a baryon which will be a benchmark test for our understanding of hadron structure.

## Key claim
Stored antiproton beams in the GeV range can serve as a factory for hyperon-antihyperon pairs, enabling studies of strange hadronic systems with unprecedented precision; however, the feasibility of the most ambitious measurements, such as Omega-atom spectroscopy, depends on achieving high luminosities and a well-understood detection system, and will not be a day-1 experiment.

## Method
The authors performed Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model simulations of exclusive hyperon-antihyperon pair production in antiproton-nucleus collisions, specifically for antiprotons on neon-20 and neon-22 targets at beam energies of 0.85 GeV and 1 GeV. They analyzed the sensitivity of transverse and longitudinal momentum asymmetries of coincident Lambda-antilambda and Sigma-minus-antilambda pairs to the depth of the antihyperon nuclear potential, and calculated production yields for different neon isotopes to probe neutron skin effects. The design of a dedicated experimental setup for double hypernuclei and hyperatom spectroscopy at the PANDA experiment was also presented, including GEANT simulations of hyperon stopping distributions.

## Key numbers
- The antiproton potential at normal nuclear density is in the range of U_pbar approximately -150 MeV, a factor of about four weaker than naively expected from G-parity relations.
- At 1 GeV beam energy, the exclusive production of Lambda-antilambda pairs in antiproton-neon-22 interactions is smaller by about 20% compared to neon-20 for the whole impact parameter range.
- The ratio of Sigma-minus-antilambda pair production in neon-22 versus neon-20 is 1.23, while the ratio for Lambda-antilambda pairs is 0.84, giving a double ratio of 1.46.
- At an average antiproton interaction rate of 5 x 10^6 per second, PANDA will produce approximately 33,000 Xi-minus hyperons per day stopped in boron absorbers, yielding about 10 detected gamma transitions per month for a specific Lambda-Lambda nucleus.
- Assuming a cross section for Omega-minus-Omega-plus pairs a factor of 100 lower than for Xi-minus-antixi-plus pairs, PANDA might produce of the order of 5 Omega-atoms per day.
- The measured charge radii of neon-20 (2.992 +/- 0.008 fm) and neon-22 (2.986 +/- 0.021 fm) differ very little, while the neutron distribution radius of neon-20 is about 0.2 fm smaller than that of neon-22 according to calculations.

## Context
This paper extends earlier schematic calculations by Pochodzalla (2008, 2009) and Sanchez Lorente (2015) on using transverse momentum asymmetries to probe antihyperon potentials, now incorporating more realistic GiBUU transport model simulations. It complements measurements of ground state decays of double hypernuclei with meson beams at J-PARC and possible decays of particle unstable hypernuclei in heavy ion reactions. The work enables future experiments at the PANDA facility at FAIR, including high-resolution gamma spectroscopy of doubly strange nuclei, spectroscopy of multistrange Xi-atoms, and potentially the first measurement of a spectroscopic quadrupole moment of a baryon via Omega-atoms, which would provide a benchmark test for lattice QCD simulations and models of hadron structure.
