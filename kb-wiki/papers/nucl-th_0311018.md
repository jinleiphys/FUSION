# Effect of P-wave interaction in 6He and 6Li photoabsorption

> The total photoabsorption cross sections of six-body nuclei are calculated including complete final state interaction via the Lorentz Integral Transform method. The effect of nucleon-nucleon central P-wave forces is investigated. Comparing to results with central potentials containg S-wave forces only one finds considerably more strength in the low-energy cross sections and a rather strong improvement in comparison with experimental data, in particular for 6Li.

## Key claim

Including central P-wave nucleon-nucleon forces (via the AV4' potential) in microscopic six-body calculations of total photoabsorption cross sections for ⁶He and ⁶Li significantly enhances low-energy strength and improves agreement with experimental data compared to S-wave-only potentials, though fully satisfactory agreement is not yet reached, especially for ⁶He.

## Method

The total photoabsorption cross section in the unretarded dipole approximation was computed using the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method, which avoids explicit treatment of continuum states by solving an inhomogeneous Schrödinger-like equation with bound-state boundary conditions. The ground state and Lorentz state were expanded in symmetrized hyperspherical harmonics (HH) with convergence accelerated via the effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) approach using the Lee-Suzuki method. Calculations were performed for the AV4' potential (including S- and P-waves) and compared to earlier results with the S-wave-only MTI-III and MN potentials.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state binding energies with AV4' potential: 32.90 MeV for ⁶He and 36.47 MeV for ⁶Li (converged at K⁰_max = 12).
- For ⁶Li with AV4', the LIT at K_max = 11 shows relative error R ≈ 1% in the lower σ_R range; for ⁶He with AV4', R ≈ 3% at the same K_max.
- HH basis state reduction for ⁶Li at K_max = 13: from N = 18402 to N = 6362 by omitting negligible spatial symmetries.
- For ⁶Li with AV4', good agreement with experimental data is obtained up to about 12 MeV photon energy.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous microscopic calculation (Bacca et al., 2002) of total photoabsorption for A=6 nuclei, which used only central S-wave potentials (MTI-III and MN) and found poor agreement with low-energy data. The present study tests the conjecture that P-wave NN interactions are important for these P-shell nuclei, using the AV4' potential. The results enable future investigations of additional NN potential components (e.g., spin-orbit force) to better describe the halo structure of ⁶He and its soft dipole resonance, and highlight the need for more experimental data on both ⁶He and ⁶Li.
