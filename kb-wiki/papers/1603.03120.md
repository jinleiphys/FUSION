# Decay dynamics of the unbound $^{25}$O and $^{26}$O nuclei

> We study the ground and excited resonance states of $^{26}$O with a three-body model of $^{24}$O+n+n taking into account the coupling to the continuum. To this end, we use the new experimental data for the invariant mass spectroscopy of the unbound $^{25}$O and $^{26}$O nuclei, and present an update of three-body model calculations for the two-neutron decay of the $^{26}$O nucleus. With the new model inputs determined with the ground state decay of $^{26}$O, we discuss the di-neurtron correlations and a halo nature of this nucleus, as well as the structure of the excited states. For the energy of the 2$^+$ state, we achieve an excellent agreement with the experimental data with this calculation. We show that the 2$^+$ state consists predominantly of the $(d_{3/2})^2$ configuration, for which the pairing interaction between the valence neutrons slightly decreases its energy from the unperturbed one. We also discuss the structure of excited 0$^+$ states of the $^{26}$O nucleus. In particular, we show the existence of an excited 0$^+$ state at 3.38 MeV, which is mainly composed of the $(f_{7/2})^2$ configuration.

# Decay dynamics of the unbound $^{25}$O and $^{26}$O nuclei

## Key claim
The three-body model of $^{24}$O+n+n, calibrated with new high-statistics experimental data from RIKEN, reproduces the ground state decay energy of $^{26}$O at 18 keV and achieves excellent agreement for the 2$^+$ excited state at 1.28 MeV, but the authors note that the decay energy spectrum depends only weakly on how the $^{26}$O nucleus is produced, meaning the decay dynamics primarily reflect final-state interactions rather than the initial reaction mechanism.

## Method
The authors solve a $^{24}$O+n+n three-body Hamiltonian using a Woods-Saxon potential for the neutron-core interaction and a density-dependent contact pairing interaction between the valence neutrons. The neutron-core potential is calibrated to reproduce the $d_{3/2}$ resonance in $^{25}$O at 749 keV with width 88 keV from Kondo (2015), and the pairing interaction strength is adjusted to match the ground state energy of $^{26}$O at 18 keV. The decay energy spectrum is computed using the Green's function method with a finite smearing parameter $\eta$, and the bound-state approximation with a 40 fm box is used to extract ground state properties.

## Key numbers
- Ground state decay energy of $^{26}$O: 18 $\pm$ 3 (stat) $\pm$ 4 (sys) keV from Kondo (2015)
- $d_{3/2}$ resonance in $^{25}$O: energy 749 (10) keV, width 88 (6) keV from Kondo (2015)
- Excited 2$^+$ state in $^{26}$O: 1.28 $^{+0.11}_{-0.08}$ MeV from Kondo (2015)
- Calculated $d_{3/2}$ resonance width in $^{25}$O: 87.2 keV (agrees with experimental 86 keV)
- Calculated $p_{3/2}$ resonance in $^{25}$O: energy 0.577 MeV, width 1.63 MeV
- Calculated $f_{7/2}$ resonance in $^{25}$O: energy 2.44 MeV, width 0.21 MeV
- Excited 0$^+$ state in $^{26}$O predicted at 3.38 MeV with width 0.737 MeV, mainly $(f_{7/2})^2$ configuration
- Ground state of $^{26}$O: mean-square neutron-neutron distance $\langle r_{nn}^2\rangle$ = 40.2 fm$^2$, core-dineutron distance $\langle r_{c-2n}^2\rangle$ = 18.5 fm$^2$
- Ground state composition: 66.1% $(d_{3/2})^2$, 18.3% $(f_{7/2})^2$, 10.5% $(p_{3/2})^2$, spin-singlet fraction 54.8%
- Calculated rms radius of $^{26}$O: 3.39 $\pm$ 0.11 fm (using empirical $^{24}$O radius 3.19 $\pm$ 0.13 fm from Ozawa (2001))

## Context
This work extends previous three-body model calculations by Hagino and Sagawa (2014, 2015) using updated experimental data from Kondo (2015) at the RIKEN RIBF, which provided higher-precision measurements of $^{25}$O and $^{26}$O decay energies compared to earlier work by Lunderberg (2012) at NSCL/MSU and by the GSI-LAND group (2013). The study enables quantitative extraction of dineutron correlations and halo structure in $^{26}$O, and predicts the existence of an excited 0$^+$ state at 3.38 MeV that could be tested in future experiments. The angular correlation analysis shows that back-to-back neutron emission is enhanced due to dineutron correlations, similar to findings in $^{11}$Li by Hagino and Sagawa (2005).
