# Rare dileptonic decays of Λ_b in a quark model

> Hadronic form factors for the rare weak transitions $Λ_{b}\rightarrowΛ^{(*)}$ are calculated using a nonrelativistic quark model. The form factors are extracted in two ways. An analytic extraction using single component wave functions (SCA) with the quark current being reduced to its nonrelativistic Pauli form is employed in the first method. In the second method, the form factors are extracted numerically using the full quark model wave function (MCN) with the full relativistic form of the quark current. Although there are differences between the two sets of form factors, both sets satisfy the relationships expected from the heavy quark effective theory (HQET). Differential decay rates, branching ratios and forward-backward asymmetries (FBAs) are calculated for the dileptonic decays $Λ_{b}\rightarrowΛ^{(*)}\ell^{+}\ell^{-}$, for transitions to both ground state and excited daughter baryons. Inclusion of the long distance contributions from charmonium resonances significantly enhances the decay rates. In the MCN model the $Λ(1600)$ mode is the dominant mode in the $μ$ channel when charmonium resonances are considered; the $Λ(1520)$ mode is also found to have a comparable branching ratio to that of the ground state in the $μ$ channel.

## Key claim

The authors calculate hadronic form factors for the rare weak transitions \(\Lambda_b \rightarrow \Lambda^{(*)}\) using a nonrelativistic quark model, finding that while the two extraction methods (analytic SCA and numerical MCN) yield different form factors, both sets satisfy the relationships expected from heavy quark effective theory (HQET); the paper hedges that the MCN method, which uses fewer approximations, should give more reliable results than the SCA method.

## Method

The form factors for \(\Lambda_b \rightarrow \Lambda^{(*)}\) transitions were extracted using two approaches within a nonrelativistic quark model: an analytic extraction using single component wave functions (SCA) with the quark current reduced to its nonrelativistic Pauli form, and a numerical extraction using the full quark model wave function (MCN) with the full relativistic form of the quark current. Differential decay rates, branching ratios, and forward-backward asymmetries (FBAs) were then calculated for the dileptonic decays \(\Lambda_b \rightarrow \Lambda^{(*)} \ell^{+} \ell^{-}\) to both ground state and excited daughter baryons, including long distance contributions from charmonium resonances via a Breit-Wigner formalism.

## Key numbers

*   Branching ratio for \(\Lambda_b \rightarrow \Lambda \mu^{+} \mu^{-}\) measured by CDF: \((1.73 \pm 0.42 \pm 0.55) \times 10^{-6}\).
*   In the MCN model, the \(\Lambda(1600)\) mode is the dominant mode in the \(\mu\) channel when charmonium resonances are considered.
*   The \(\Lambda(1520)\) mode is found to have a comparable branching ratio to that of the ground state in the \(\mu\) channel.
*   For the \(B\) meson sector, the average branching ratio for \(B \rightarrow X_s \gamma\) is \((3.55 \pm 0.24 \pm 0.09) \times 10^{-4}\).
*   For the \(B\) meson sector, the average branching ratio for \(B \rightarrow X_s \ell^{+} \ell^{-}\) is \((3.66_{-0.77}^{+0.76}) \times 10^{-6}\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of rare \(b \rightarrow s\) transitions from the meson sector (e.g., by Melikhov et al. using a relativistic constituent quark model, and by Aliev et al. and Ali et al. using light-cone sum rules) to the baryon sector, specifically for \(\Lambda_b\) decays to both ground state and excited \(\Lambda\) baryons, which had not been explored previously. It builds on the quark model approach used by Pervin et al. for semileptonic decays of \(\Lambda_b\) and \(\Lambda_c\), and it enables future comparisons with experimental data from LHCb, which estimates 800 \(\Lambda_b \rightarrow \Lambda \ell^{+} \ell^{-}\) events with 2 fb\(^{-1}\) of data. The paper also aims to identify observables like the forward-backward asymmetry that may be less dependent on form factors, following similar efforts in meson decays by Buras et al. and Roberts et al.
