# Three-Body Configuration Space Calculations with Hard Core Potentials

> We present a mathematically rigorous method suitable for solving three-body bound state and scattering problems when the inter-particle interaction is of a hard-core nature. The proposed method is a variant of the Boundary Condition Model and it has been employed to calculate the binding energies for a system consisting of three ^4He atoms. Two realistic He-He interactions of Aziz and collaborators, have been used for this purpose. The results obtained compare favorably with those previously obtained by other methods. We further used the model to calculate, for the first time, the ultra-low energy scattering phase shifts. This study revealed that our method is ideally suited for three-body molecular calculations where the practically hard-core of the inter-atomic potential gives rise to strong numerical inaccuracies that make calculations for these molecules cumbersome.

## Key claim

The paper presents a mathematically rigorous variant of the Boundary Condition Model (BCM) for solving three-body bound state and scattering problems with hard-core interactions, claiming that replacing the strong repulsive short-range part of realistic interatomic potentials with a hard core suppresses numerical inaccuracies. The method is demonstrated on the three-^4He system, but the authors note that the auxiliary spectrum σ_d^aux (eigenvalues of internal Dirichlet problems in the domains between cylinders) can cause non-uniqueness of scattering solutions at isolated energies, though this is considered an exceptional case in practice.

## Method

The authors reformulate the three-body Schrödinger equation with hard-core boundary conditions (wave function vanishes at interparticle distance r=c) into a system of Faddeev differential equations in configuration space, where inside the hard-core cylinders (|x_α|<c_α) only the Laplacian acts instead of the full Hamiltonian with a repulsive potential. For the three identical boson case, they reduce the six-dimensional equations to partial wave form using a bispherical harmonic basis, and solve the resulting boundary value problems numerically for both bound states and scattering states of the ^4He trimer.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy of the ^4He trimer calculated using two realistic He-He interactions (Aziz et al., 1979 and 1987); results compare favorably with previous methods
- Ultra-low energy scattering phase shifts calculated for the first time using this method
- The auxiliary spectrum σ_d^aux has lower boundary that behaves as 1/c^2 where c = max_α c_α for sufficiently small core radii
- The two-body potentials satisfy the condition |V_α(x_α)| ≤ C_α(1+|x_α|)^{-3-ε} for |x_α| ≥ c_α with ε>0

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev differential equation formalism (Faddeev, 1963; Merkuriev and Faddeev, 1993) to handle hard-core interactions, which were previously treated either through limiting procedures with finite repulsive cores (Kim and Tubis, Belyaev, Brayshaw) or through potential theory approaches (Merkuriev and Motovilov, 1980s). The method enables calculations for three-atomic molecular systems where the practically hard-core nature of interatomic potentials causes severe numerical difficulties, and it is applied here to the ^4He trimer which has been studied previously using Variational Monte Carlo (VMC), Green Function Monte Carlo (GFMC), Faddeev integral equations in momentum space (Nakaichi, Glöckle), and hyperspherical methods (Esry, Lin, Greene).
