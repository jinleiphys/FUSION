# A model for pion Delta electroproduction on the proton

> We have extended a model for the $γN\to ππN$ reaction to virtual photons and selected the diagrams which have a $Δ$ in the final state. With this model we have evaluated cross sections for the virtual photon cross section as a function of $Q^2$ for different energies. The agreement found with the $γ_v p\toΔ^0π^+$ and $γ_v p\toΔ^{++}π^-$ reactions is good. The sensitivity of the results to $NΔ$ transition form factors is also studied. The present reaction, selecting a particular final state, is an extra test for models of the $γ_{v} N\to ππN$ amplitude. The experimental measurement of the different isospin channels for this reaction are encouraged as a means to unravel the dynamics of the two pions photoproduction processes.

## Key claim

The authors extend a previously successful model for the real-photon reaction $\gamma N \to \pi\pi N$ to virtual photons, selecting only the diagrams that produce a $\Delta$ resonance in the final state, and find good agreement with existing electroproduction data for $\gamma_v p \to \Delta^0\pi^+$ and $\gamma_v p \to \Delta^{++}\pi^-$ at $Q^2 = 0.6$ GeV$^2$, though the agreement relies on the choice of form factors and the sign of the $N^*(1520) \to \Delta\pi$ coupling.

## Method

The authors start from a 20-diagram model of the $\gamma N \to \pi\pi N$ reaction (Oset et al., 1995) and retain only the 8 diagrams that contain a $\Delta$ and a pion in the final state. They extend the model to virtual photons by introducing the zeroth component of the photon coupling, implementing the $Q^2$ dependence of the amplitudes, and adding terms linked to the $S_{1/2}$ helicity amplitude that vanish for real photons. The $\Delta$ is not treated as a final-state particle but decays explicitly into $\pi N$ via a propagator, which naturally accounts for the finite width and allows angular correlations between the two pions to be preserved.

## Key numbers

* The model reproduces the real-photon cross section for $\gamma p \to \Delta^{++}\pi^-$ up to about $E_\gamma \simeq 1$ GeV, as shown in fig. 2.
* At $Q^2 = 0.6$ GeV$^2$, the cross section for $\gamma_v p \to \pi^-\Delta^{++}$ increases by about 10% when the pion-pole cutoff parameter $\lambda_\pi^2$ is changed from 0.5 GeV$^2$ to 0.6 GeV$^2$ (fig. 3).
* The peak in the cross section around $W = 1480$ MeV at $Q^2 = 0.6$ GeV$^2$ arises from constructive interference between the $\Delta$ Kroll-Ruderman term and the $N^*(1520)$ excitation term (fig. 4).
* The data favor an axial form factor parameter $M_A \simeq 1.16$–$1.23$ GeV, consistent with values $1.16 \pm 0.03$ GeV from earlier work (fig. 6).
* The longitudinal cross section $\epsilon \sigma_L$ at $Q^2 = 0.6$ GeV$^2$ is small compared to the transverse cross section $\sigma_T$ (fig. 8).

## Context

This work extends the model of Oset et al. (1995) for $\gamma N \to \pi\pi N$ to virtual photons, providing a test of the dynamics of two-pion photoproduction in electroproduction. It contrasts with earlier diagrammatic approaches such as Berends et al. (1970), which used a minimal gauge-invariant set of four terms, by including four additional terms, most notably the $N^*(1520)$ resonance excitation followed by $\Delta\pi$ decay, which strongly interferes with the $\Delta$ Kroll-Ruderman term. The model enables future experiments at Jefferson Laboratory to extract information on resonance form factors and the axial transition form factor, and it can be used to study the $Q^2$ dependence of the interference patterns that are crucial for distinguishing between theoretical models of two-pion production.
