# Close Binary Galaxies: Application to Source of Energy and Expansion in Universe

> Applying the microscopic nuclear physics ideas for fusion reactions to macroscopic galactic systems, we study the evolution of the compact binary galaxy in mass asymmetry (transfer) coordinate. The conditions for the formation of stable symmetric binary galaxy are analyzed. The role of symmetrization of asymmetric binary galaxy in the transformation of gravitational energy into internal energy of galaxies accompanied by the release of a large amount of energy during the symmetrization process is revealed.

# Close Binary Galaxies: Application to Source of Energy and Expansion in Universe

## Key claim

The mass asymmetry coordinate, a concept borrowed from nuclear fusion reactions, governs the evolution of close binary galaxies: asymmetric binary galaxies evolve toward symmetric configurations (symmetrization) if their initial mass asymmetry is below a critical barrier value, releasing gravitational energy on the order of $10^{48-52}$ J, but systems with mass ratios exceeding approximately 6:1 are unstable and undergo asymmetrization leading to separation.

## Method

The authors construct a total potential energy function for a close binary galaxy system as a function of the mass asymmetry coordinate $\eta = (M_1-M_2)/(M_1+M_2)$, combining the self-gravitational energies of each galaxy (using an observed mass-radius power-law relation $R_k = M_k^n / g$ with $n=2/5$ or $n=2/3$) with the galaxy-galaxy interaction potential that includes both gravitational and orbital rotational energy. They solve $\partial U/\partial \eta = 0$ to find fixed points (minima and barriers) in the driving potential, then apply the model to observational data from the catalogue of isolated pairs of galaxies, calculating energy release during symmetrization for specific binary systems.

## Key numbers

- For close elliptic binary galaxy 194 ($\eta_i=0.28$), symmetrization releases $\Delta U = 2 \times 10^{50}$ J of internal energy.
- For close elliptic binary galaxy 279 ($\eta_i=0.50$), symmetrization releases $\Delta U = 3 \times 10^{51}$ J.
- For close elliptic binary galaxy 554 ($\eta_i=0.25$), symmetrization releases $\Delta U = 2 \times 10^{50}$ J.
- The barrier position $\eta_b$ is bounded by $\eta_b < 2^{-1/2} \approx 0.71$, corresponding to a mass ratio $M_{1i}/M_{2i} > (1+2^{1/2})^2 \approx 6$ for unstable systems.
- The overall energy release from symmetrization of close binary galaxies spans $10^{48-52}$ J.
- For spiral binary galaxies 206 ($|\eta_i|=0.45$, $\eta_b=0.34$), 243 ($|\eta_i|=0.31$, $\eta_b=0.07$), and 439 ($|\eta_i|=0.63$, $\eta_b=0.56$), the initial asymmetry exceeds the barrier, leading to asymmetrization.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of nuclear physics mass-asymmetry concepts to close binary stars (Sargsyan et al., 2015, 2017) by scaling the same theoretical framework from stellar to galactic systems. The driving potentials for di-galaxy systems resemble those for microscopic dinuclear systems (Adamian et al., 2012, 2014), despite the different interaction mechanisms (gravity versus strong nuclear force). The results enable predictions of observable signatures of symmetrization (mass ratio, luminosity ratio, and radius ratio all approaching unity) and establish conditions under which binary galaxies either merge incompletely or separate, providing a mechanism for galactic expansion within binary systems.
