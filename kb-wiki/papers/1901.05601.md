# New self-consistent mean field approximation and its application in strong interaction phase transition

> In this letter, taking the Nambu--Jona--Lasinio model as an example, we propose a new self-consistent mean field approximation method by means of Fierz transformation. This new self-consistent mean field approximation introduces a new free parameter $α$ to be determined by experiments and when $α$ takes 0.5, it reduces to the mean field approximation that was commonly used in the past. Then based on this self-consistent mean field approximation, we study the influence of the undetermined parameter $α$ on the phase diagram of the two-flavor strong interaction matter. It is found that the value of $α$ plays an extremely important role in the study of strong interaction phase diagram. It not only changes the position of the phase transition point of strong interaction matter, but also affects the order of phase transition, for example, when $α$ is greater than the critical value $α_c = 0.71$, then the strong interaction matter phase diagram no longer exists critical end point. In addition, in the case of zero temperature and finite density, we also found that when $α$ is greater than 1.044, the pseudo-critical chemical potential is about 4$\sim$5 times the saturation density of the nuclear matter, which agrees with the expected results from the image of hadrons degree of freedom. The resulting equations of state of strong interaction matter at low temperatures and high densities will have an important impact on the study of the mass radius relationship of neutron stars and the merging process of binary neutron stars.

# New self-consistent mean field approximation and its application in strong interaction phase transition

## Key claim
The authors propose a new self-consistent mean field approximation for the Nambu–Jona–Lasinio (NJL) model that introduces a free parameter α, which cannot be determined by theory alone and must be fixed by experiment; when α exceeds a critical value α_c = 0.71, the chiral phase transition becomes a crossover with no critical end point (CEP), and when α > 1.044 the pseudo-critical chemical potential reaches about 600 MeV, resolving the contradiction between quark-gluon and hadron degree-of-freedom predictions for the phase transition location.

## Method
The authors start from the standard two-flavor NJL Lagrangian and perform a Fierz transformation to obtain an equivalent Lagrangian. They then define a redefined effective Lagrangian as a linear combination of the original and Fierz-transformed forms, weighted by the free parameter α: L_R = (1−α)L + α L_F. After applying the mean field approximation to this redefined Lagrangian, they derive a modified gap equation and a renormalized chemical potential μ_r that depends on α. They solve these equations numerically at zero temperature and finite chemical potential, using the chiral susceptibility to determine the order and location of the chiral phase transition for different values of α.

## Key numbers
- The critical value of the free parameter is α_c = 0.71; for α > α_c, the phase diagram no longer contains a critical end point (CEP).
- When α = 1.044, the pseudo-critical chemical potential is about 600 MeV, corresponding to approximately 4–5 times the nuclear matter saturation density.
- The critical chemical potential μ_0 (where quark-number density first becomes nonzero) is always 311 MeV, independent of α, consistent with the model-independent prediction of Halasz (1998).
- The NJL model parameters used are G = 4.93 × 10⁻⁶ MeV⁻², Λ = 653 MeV, and m = 5.0 MeV, taken from He (2005).
- For α = 0.5 (the conventional mean field approximation), the phase transition at low temperature and large chemical potential is first order.

## Context
This work addresses a long-standing contradiction between predictions from quark-gluon degrees of freedom, which place the QCD phase transition at chemical potentials around 330–380 MeV (Asakawa 1989, Buballa 2005, Cui 2018), and predictions from hadron degrees of freedom using the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) method (Walecka 1974), which indicate the transition occurs at chemical potentials above 580 MeV (corresponding to 4 times nuclear saturation density). The new self-consistent mean field approach extends the earlier suggestion by Klevansky (1992) to use a Fierz-invariant average of Hartree and Fock terms (which corresponds to α = 0.5) by introducing the general parameter α. The resulting equations of state at low temperature and high density can be applied to study neutron star mass-radius relationships and binary neutron star mergers such as GW170817 (Abbott 2017) and the mass constraint of PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis 2013).
