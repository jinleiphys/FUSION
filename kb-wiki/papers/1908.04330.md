# From Non-interacting to Interacting Picture of Thermodynamics and Transport Coefficients for Quark Gluon Plasma

> We have attempted to build first some simplified model to map the interaction of quarks and gluons, which can be contained by their thermodynamical quantity like entropy density, obtained from calculation of lattice quantum chromo dynamics (LQCD). With respect to entropy density of the standard non-interacting massless quark gluon plasma (QGP), its interacting values from LQCD simulation are reduced as we go from higher to lower temperature through the cross-over of quark-hadron phase transition. By parameterizing increasing degeneracy factor or increasing interaction-fugacity or decreasing thermal width of quarks and gluons with temperature, we have matched LQCD data.Using that interaction picture, shear viscosity and electrical conductivity are calculated. For getting nearly perfect fluid nature of QGP, interaction might have some role when we consider temperature dependent thermal width.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the reduced entropy density of interacting quark-gluon plasma (QGP) from lattice QCD can be reproduced by three simplified quasi-particle models using temperature-dependent degeneracy factors, fugacity, or thermal width, but the authors note that the interaction picture built via these methods may play a role in achieving the nearly perfect fluid nature of QGP only when temperature-dependent thermal width is considered.

## Method

The authors parameterized three distinct quasi-particle models to match lattice QCD entropy density data: (1) a temperature-dependent degeneracy factor g(T) multiplying the standard degeneracy of quarks and gluons, (2) a temperature-dependent fugacity Z(T) inserted into the thermal distribution functions, and (3) a temperature-dependent thermal width Γ_c(T) replacing delta-function mass distributions with Breit-Wigner profiles. Using these interaction pictures matched to lattice QCD thermodynamics, they then calculated shear viscosity and electrical conductivity via relaxation time approximation of kinetic theory.

## Key numbers

- The non-interacting Stephan-Boltzmann limit of entropy density for QGP is approximately 20.8 T³, while the hadronic matter limit is approximately 3 T³
- For the degeneracy factor model set-1: a₀ = 0.793, a₁ = 0.687, a₂ = 16.284, a₃ = 0.170, a₄ = 0.560
- For the fugacity model set-2: b₀ = 0.138935, b₁ = 1.445, b₂ = 0.170, b₃ = -0.77362
- For the thermal width model: a₀ = 6.76802, a₁ = 88.6265, a₂ = -37.3715, a₃ = 0.170, a₄ = 14.0653, with Γ_c saturating at approximately 0.500 GeV in the quark temperature domain
- Quark masses used: m_u = 0.005 GeV, m_s = 0.100 GeV; pion mass m_π ≈ 0.140 GeV, kaon mass m_K = 0.500 GeV

## Context

This work extends the quasi-particle model approach of Chandra and Ravishankar (2007, 2009, 2011), who introduced effective fugacity in thermal distribution functions, by providing two additional alternative parameterization methods (temperature-dependent degeneracy and thermal width) to map QCD interaction from lattice QCD data. The paper connects to prior work on shear viscosity estimation from effective QCD models by Marty, Greco, and Chandra (2009) and others, and enables systematic comparison of how different interaction parameterizations affect transport coefficients like shear viscosity and electrical conductivity, which are essential for understanding the nearly perfect fluid behavior of QGP observed at RHIC and LHC experiments.
