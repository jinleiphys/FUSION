# Baryon Electromagnetic Properties in Partially Quenched Heavy Hadron Chiral Perturbation Theory

> The electromagnetic properties of baryons containing a heavy quark are calculated at next-to-leading order in partially quenched heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory. Calculations are performed for three light flavors in the isospin limit and additionally for two light non-degenerate flavors. We use partially-quenched charge matrices that are easy to implement on the lattice. The results presented are necessary for the light quark mass extrapolation and zero-momentum extrapolation of lattice QCD and partially quenched lattice QCD calculations of heavy hadron electromagnetic properties. Additionally relations between the sextet electromagnetic form factors and transition form factors are derived.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic properties of heavy-quark baryons (triplet and sextet) are calculated at next-to-leading order in partially quenched heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory, but the results depend on the arbitrary choice of the light-quark charge matrix in the partially quenched theory, which can be exploited to determine low-energy constants from lattice data rather than directly predicting physical observables.

## Method

The author constructs a partially quenched chiral effective theory for heavy hadrons in the graded group SU(6|3), including triplet (s_ℓ = 0) and sextet (s_ℓ = 1) baryons containing a heavy quark. Electromagnetic form factors (charge radii, magnetic moments, and electric quadrupole moments) are computed at one-loop order using dimensional regularization with modified minimal subtraction, with loop contributions from pseudo-Goldstone mesons and counterterms absorbing divergences. The calculations are performed for three light flavors in the isospin limit and for two non-degenerate light flavors, using partially-quenched charge matrices that maintain supertracelessness.

## Key numbers

- The mass splitting Δ between the s_ℓ = 0 and s_ℓ = 1 baryons is treated as Δ ∼ m_π in the power counting.
- The heavy quark magnetic moment μ_Q consists of the Dirac piece plus the anomalous part: μ_Q = 𝒬_Q + 2α_s(m_Q)/(3π) + ...
- The charge radius operator for s_ℓ = 0 baryons contains the term (𝒬_Q)/(8 m_Q^2) from the heavy quark Darwin term.
- The magnetic moment operator for s_ℓ = 0 baryons from light degrees of freedom is suppressed by Λ_QCD/m_Q with coefficient μ_T Λ_QCD/(Λ_χ m_Q).
- The charge radius operator for s_ℓ = 0 baryons from light degrees of freedom has coefficient c_T/Λ_χ^2.
- The loop contributions to the Dirac form factor F_1(q^2) contain terms proportional to 1/(8π^2 f^2) and 3g_3^2/(4π f)^2.
- The magnetic moment F_2(q^2) for s_ℓ = 0 baryons receives contributions proportional to λ_g g_3 Λ_QCD M_T/(2π^2 f^2 m_Q) and λ g_3^2 Λ_QCD^2 M_T/(4π^2 f^2 m_Q).

## Context

This work extends partially quenched chiral perturbation theory for heavy hadrons, previously developed for masses and weak transitions by Arndt (2003) and Tiburzi (2004), to electromagnetic properties. It provides the necessary expressions for extrapolating lattice QCD and partially quenched lattice QCD calculations of heavy baryon electromagnetic form factors in light quark mass and zero momentum transfer. The results enable determination of the low-energy constants of heavy hadron chiral perturbation theory from lattice data, and the author derives relations between sextet electromagnetic form factors and transition form factors using heavy quark symmetry, analogous to relations previously derived for light baryons.
