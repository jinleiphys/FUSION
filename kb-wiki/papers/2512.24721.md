# $S$-wave $KN$ scattering in a renormalizable chiral effective field theory

> We investigate the $s$-wave $KN$ scattering up to next-to-leading order within a renormalizable framework of covariant chiral effective field theory. Using time-ordered perturbation theory, the scattering amplitude is obtained by treating the leading-order interaction non-perturbatively and including the higher-order corrections perturbatively via the subtractive renormalization. We demonstrate that the non-perturbative treatment is essential, at least at lowest order, in the SU(3) sector of $KN$ scattering. Our NLO study achieves a good description of the empirical $s$-wave phase shifts in the isospin $I=1$ channel. An analysis of the effective range expansion yields a negative effective range, consistent with some partial wave analyses but opposite in sign to earlier phenomenological summaries. For the $I=0$ counterpart, the $KN$ interaction is found to be rather weak and exhibits large uncertainties. Further low-energy $KN$ scattering experiments and lattice QCD simulations are needed to better constrain both $s$-wave channels.

# S-wave KN scattering in a renormalizable chiral effective field theory

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a non-perturbative treatment of the leading-order interaction is essential in the SU(3) sector of KN scattering, and that at next-to-leading order the model achieves a good description of empirical s-wave phase shifts in the isospin I=1 channel, but the I=0 interaction remains very weak with large uncertainties, requiring further experimental and lattice QCD constraints.

## Method

The authors construct a covariant chiral effective field theory framework using time-ordered perturbation theory, treating the leading-order interaction non-perturbatively via scattering integral equations and including next-to-leading order corrections perturbatively through subtractive renormalization. The effective potential includes vector-meson-exchange contributions (rho, omega, phi) and crossed-Born terms at leading order, with contact terms and higher-order spinor corrections at next-to-leading order. The four unknown low-energy constants at NLO are partially fixed using lattice QCD results from the RQCD collaboration for the b-parameters, while the d-parameters are determined by reproducing experimental scattering lengths in both isospin channels.

## Key numbers

- Leading-order non-perturbative scattering length for I=1: a_KN^{I=1} = -0.274 fm (compared to perturbative LO value of -0.421 fm and experimental range -0.32 to -0.28 fm)
- NLO parameters: b^{I=0} = -0.584 GeV^{-1} (fixed), d^{I=0} = 0.635(759) GeV^{-1}; b^{I=1} = -1.924 GeV^{-1} (fixed), d^{I=1} = -1.486(360) GeV^{-1}
- Experimental scattering lengths used for fitting: a_KN^{I=1,exp} = -0.30 ± 0.03 fm, a_KN^{I=0,exp} = 0.03 ± 0.15 fm
- NLO total cross section at threshold for I=1: 11.3 ± 1.3 mb
- HAL QCD scattering length: a_KN^{I=1} = -0.226(5)(+5/-0) fm; when reproducing this value, predicted effective range r_KN^{I=1} = -0.214 fm (compared to HAL QCD value -0.297(29)(+24/-0) fm)

## Context

This work extends the renormalizable chiral effective field theory framework previously applied to pion-nucleon scattering in the SU(2) sector by Ren (2020) and to antikaon-nucleon scattering at leading order by Ren (2021, 2024) to the strangeness S=+1 KN system at next-to-leading order for the first time. The study addresses tensions between phenomenological analyses summarized by Dover (1982) and recent lattice QCD simulations by the HAL QCD collaboration (Murakami, 2025), which found a smaller scattering length in the I=1 channel. The results enable systematic extension to next-to-next-to-leading order, where two-pion exchange contributions appear, and motivate new low-energy KN scattering experiments and lattice QCD simulations to better constrain both s-wave channels.
