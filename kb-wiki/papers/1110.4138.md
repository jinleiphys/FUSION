# Heavy Quark Dynamics in the QGP

> We assess transport properties of heavy quarks in the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) that show a strong non-perturbative behavior. A T-matrix approach based on a potential taken from lattice QCD hints at the presence of heavy-quark (HQ) resonant scattering with an increasing strength as the temperature, $T$, reaches the critical temperature, $T_c \simeq 170 \; \MeV$ for deconfinement from above. The implementation of HQ resonance scattering along with a hadronization via quark coalescence under the conditions of the plasma created in heavy-ion collisions has been shown to correctly describe both the nuclear modification factor, $R_{AA}$, and the elliptic flow, $v_2$, of single electrons at RHIC and have correctly predicted the $R_{AA}$ of D mesons at LHC energy.

## Key claim

A T-matrix approach using a potential extracted from lattice QCD suggests that heavy-quark resonant scattering with light quarks exists in the Quark-Gluon Plasma, with strength increasing as the temperature approaches the critical temperature for deconfinement from above; this mechanism, combined with hadronization via quark coalescence, simultaneously describes both the nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow of single electrons at RHIC and correctly predicted the D-meson nuclear modification factor at LHC energy.

## Method

The authors employed a finite-temperature T-matrix approach based on a static heavy-quark light-quark potential extracted from lattice QCD calculations of the color-singlet free energy, converted to the internal potential energy via the thermodynamic relation. They solved a coupled system of Bethe-Salpeter and Schwinger-Dyson equations for heavy-light quark scattering in the QGP, restricting to S-wave and P-wave partial waves, and computed drag and diffusion coefficients from the resulting scattering amplitudes. These transport coefficients were then implemented in a Langevin simulation of the Fokker-Planck equation within an isentropically expanding thermal fireball model, followed by hadronization via a quark-coalescence model combined with fragmentation and subsequent semileptonic decays to obtain single-electron and D-meson observables.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for deconfinement: T_c ≃ 170 MeV
- Charm-quark mass used in calculations: m_c = 1.5 GeV
- Bottom-quark mass used in calculations: m_b = 4.5 GeV
- Light-quark mass used in calculations: m_q = 0.25 GeV
- Light-quark width used in calculations: Γ_q = 200 MeV
- Equilibration time for charm quarks close to T_c: τ_eq ≃ 7 fm/c, approximately a factor of 4 smaller than for corresponding pQCD calculations
- D-meson resonance masses assumed at m_D = 2.0 GeV, above the c-qbar mass threshold of m_c + m_q = 1.5 GeV
- Resonance dissociation temperatures: resonances melt at T ≳ 1.7 T_c for color-singlet Q-qbar and T ≳ 1.4 T_c for color-antitriplet Q q channels
- Resonance switching-off temperature for LHC predictions: T_diss = 2 T_c = 360 MeV
- Initial temperature for LHC predictions: T_0 ≃ 520 MeV for central √s_NN = 5.5 TeV Pb-Pb collisions
- Extrapolated charged-hadron multiplicity for LHC predictions: dN_ch/dy ≃ 1400 for central collisions
- D-meson resonance widths varied in the range 300-500 MeV
- Strong coupling constant used for gluon scattering: α_s = 0.4

## Context

This work extends earlier non-perturbative approaches to heavy-quark scattering in the QGP that postulated D- and B-meson resonance excitations in the deconfined phase (vanHees 2004, vanHees 2005), moving from an effective field theory model to a T-matrix approach constrained by lattice QCD potentials. The results contradict perturbative QCD calculations (Svetitsky 1987, Armesto 2005, Wicks 2005) that predicted only moderate suppression and small elliptic flow, and are consistent with other non-perturbative approaches (Gossiaux 2008, Alberico 2011) that also found the need to go beyond pQCD. The successful prediction of D-meson suppression at LHC, confirmed by ALICE preliminary results (Dainese 2011), enables future discrimination between models through measurements of B-meson observables and elliptic flow, where the T-matrix approach predicts resonant scattering persisting to higher temperatures for bottom quarks compared to charm quarks.
