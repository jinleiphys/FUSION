# Quarkonium Production and Medium Effects in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> Color screening and regeneration are both hot medium effects on quarkonium production in high energy nuclear collisions. However, they affect in an opposite way the finally observed quarkonium spectra. Due to the competition of the two dynamical effects, the ratio of the integrated quarkonium yield between nuclear and elementary nucleon collisions loses its sensitivity. Once the information of quarkonium transverse motion is included, on the other hand, the ratio of averaged transverse momentum square reveals the nature of the QCD medium created in high energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The ratio of averaged transverse momentum square \(r_{AA} = \langle p_t^2 \rangle_{AA} / \langle p_t^2 \rangle_{pp}\) for quarkonia is a more sensitive probe of the QCD medium created in high energy nuclear collisions than the integrated yield ratio \(R_{AA}\), because the latter loses sensitivity due to the competing effects of color screening and regeneration.

## Method

The authors solve a Boltzmann-type transport equation for the quarkonium phase space distribution, including both initial production via pQCD and regeneration via coalescence in the hot medium, with the loss term from Debye screening determined by dissociation temperatures and the medium evolution from hydrodynamics. The cold nuclear matter effects (shadowing, Cronin effect, nuclear absorption) modify the initial distributions at the medium formation time. The ratio \(r_{AA}\) is then computed as a function of the number of participant nucleons for \(J/\psi\) and \(\Upsilon\) states at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

* \(J/\psi\) dissociation temperature \(T_d/T_c = 2.3\) (scaled by critical temperature \(T_c = 165\) MeV) for central collisions.
* \(\Upsilon_{1S}\) dissociation temperature \(T_d/T_c = 4.0\); \(\Upsilon_{1P}\) at \(T_d/T_c = 1.8\); \(\Upsilon_{2S}\) at \(T_d/T_c = 1.6\); \(\psi' (\chi_c)\) at \(T_d/T_c = 1.1\).
* \(J/\psi\) regeneration fraction in central collisions: near zero at SPS (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17.3\) GeV), significant at RHIC (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV), and dominant at LHC (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76\) TeV).
* \(r_{AA}\) for \(J/\psi\) at mid-rapidity: above unity and increasing with centrality at SPS; near unity with weak centrality dependence at RHIC; below unity and decreasing with centrality at LHC.
* \(r_{AA}\) for \(J/\psi\) at forward rapidity: above unity and increasing with centrality at RHIC; below unity at LHC.
* Predicted \(r_{AA}\) for \(\Upsilon_{1S}\) at LHC is above unity and increases with centrality, similar to \(J/\psi\) at SPS; for \(\Upsilon_{2S}\) at LHC, \(r_{AA}\) is predicted to be very small due to dominant regeneration.

## Context

This work extends the study of quarkonium suppression via color screening, originally proposed by Satz (1986), by incorporating regeneration via coalescence, which becomes significant at higher collision energies. The authors show that the integrated yield ratio \(R_{AA}\) measured at SPS, RHIC, and LHC shows no clear energy dependence (Digal et al., 2012), motivating the new observable \(r_{AA}\). The results enable future experimental checks at RHIC and LHC, particularly for \(\Upsilon\) states, to distinguish between the hot mediums created at different collision energies.
