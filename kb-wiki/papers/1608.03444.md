# Dynamical freeze-out criterion in a hydrodynamical description of Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=200$ GeV and Pb + Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=2760$ GeV

> In hydrodynamical modeling of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, the freeze-out is typically assumed to take place at a surface of constant temperature or energy density. A more physical approach is to assume that freeze-out takes place at a surface of constant Knudsen number. We evaluate the Knudsen number as a ratio of the expansion rate of the system to the pion scattering rate, and apply the constant Knudsen number freeze-out criterion to ideal hydrodynamical description of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC ($\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=200$ GeV) and the LHC ($\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}}=2760$ GeV) energies. We see that once the numerical values of freeze-out temperature and freeze-out Knudsen number are chosen to produce similar $p_T$ distributions, the elliptic and triangular anisotropies are similar too, in both event-by-event and averaged initial state calculations.

## Key claim

Once the freeze-out temperature (constant-temperature criterion) and freeze-out Knudsen number (dynamical criterion) are separately tuned to produce similar transverse-momentum spectra for pions and protons, the resulting elliptic and triangular flow coefficients are also similar, in both event-by-event and averaged initial-state ideal hydrodynamical calculations at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Method

The authors solved 2+1-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics with boost invariance, using two equations of state: one with full chemical equilibrium (s95p-v1) and one with chemical freeze-out at 150 MeV (s95p-PCE-v1). The dynamical freeze-out surface was defined by a constant Knudsen number, evaluated as the ratio of the hydrodynamic expansion rate to the pion scattering rate; the scattering rate was computed from resonance cross sections (Breit-Wigner formula) and elastic meson-meson cross sections in a hadron resonance gas. Particle spectra were obtained via the Cooper-Frye prescription, and the freeze-out parameters (temperature or Knudsen number) were adjusted to reproduce measured pion and proton pT distributions in 20-30% central collisions.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC (sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV Au+Au, 20-30% central), using the chemically equilibrated EoS, the freeze-out temperature T_f = 140 MeV and Knudsen number Kn_f = 1.0 gave nearly identical pion and proton spectra.
- At RHIC with the chemically frozen EoS (s95p-PCE-v1), the required parameters were T_f = 120 MeV and Kn_f = 1.3, with an initial time tau_0 = 0.2 fm.
- At the LHC (sqrt(s_NN)=2760 GeV Pb+Pb, 0-5% central), using the chemically frozen EoS, the favored freeze-out temperature was T_f = 120 MeV (same as RHIC) but the freeze-out Knudsen number was Kn_f = 1.0 (lower than at RHIC).
- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross sections used were 42 mb at RHIC and 64 mb at the LHC.
- The binary collision fraction f was 0.088 at RHIC and 0.17 at the LHC, determined from multiplicity data.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Eskola et al. (2007) and Molnar et al. (2014) that used constant Knudsen number freeze-out with simpler scattering-rate calculations or averaged initial states, by applying the criterion to event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions and using a more complete hadron-resonance-gas scattering rate. The results show that, contrary to the authors' initial expectation, the large gradients in event-by-event calculations do not make the system more sensitive to the freeze-out criterion. The paper leaves open the question of whether event-by-event distributions of average pT or anisotropy coefficients, or HBT radii, might be sensitive to the freeze-out criterion, and suggests applying the dynamical criterion to more sophisticated initial-state models such as EKRT and IP-Glasma.
