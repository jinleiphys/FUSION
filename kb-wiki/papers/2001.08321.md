# Bulk medium evolution has considerable effects on jet observables

> We consider the case, in QCD, of a single jet propagating within a strongly interacting fluid, of finite extent. Interactions lead to the appearance of a source of energy-momentum within the fluid. The remnant jet that escapes the container is analyzed along with portions of the medium excited by the jet. We study the effect of a static versus a semi-realistic expanding medium, with jets traveling inward versus outward. We consider the medium response via recoils in partonic scatterings based on a weakly-coupled description and its combination with hydrodynamical medium response based on a strongly-coupled description, followed by incorporation into a jet. The effects of these limits on the reconstructed energy, momentum and mass of the jet, as a function of the angle away from the original parton direction are studied. It is demonstrated that different flow velocity configurations in the medium produce considerable differences in jet observables. This work highlights the importance of accurate dynamical modeling of the soft medium as a foundation on which to calculate jet modification, and casts skepticism on results obtained without such modeling.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that different flow velocity configurations in the medium (static brick versus expanding medium, with jets traveling inward or outward) produce considerable differences in jet observables, and that accurate dynamical modeling of the soft medium is essential for calculating jet modification, casting skepticism on results obtained without such modeling.

## Method

The authors used the JETSCAPE 1.0 event generator with a multistage approach: the MATTER simulator for partons with virtualities above 2 GeV and the LBT simulator for partons near 2 GeV. They compared two scenarios for medium response to jet propagation: a weakly coupled description using 2-to-2 partonic scatterings with recoil partons, and a strongly coupled description where soft partons below an energy cutoff are absorbed into the fluid and evolved hydrodynamically via a causal relativistic diffusion equation. Jets were simulated starting from a single 140 GeV gluon propagating through either a static uniform brick medium at T=0.25 GeV or an expanding oblate three-dimensional Gaussian profile with initial central temperature T0=0.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Initial jet energy: E_init = 140 GeV (single gluon)
- Brick medium temperature: T = 0.25 GeV (static, uniform, infinite size)
- Expanding medium initial central temperature: T0 = 0.5 GeV
- Expanding medium transverse width: sigma_T = 1.5 fm; longitudinal width: sigma_z = 0.75 fm
- Energy cutoff for soft partons in strongly coupled description: E_dep_cut = 2 GeV
- In-medium coupling constant: alpha_S_med = 0.25
- Diffusion parameters: tau_relax = 1.0 fm, D_diff = 0.6 fm, thermalization time t_th = 1.5 fm
- Jet evolution time: t = 8 fm; freeze-out time: t_FO = 10 fm/c
- Minimum virtuality for MATTER phase: mu_min = 1 GeV; transition virtuality: mu = 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the JETSCAPE collaboration framework (JETSCAPE 1.0) beyond earlier calculations that used simplified Bjorken cylinder medium profiles (Gyulassy 2001, Vitev 2002, Salgado 2003), which yielded specious results for some high-pT hadron observables such as azimuthal anisotropy v2 (Majumder 2006, Bass 2008). The study enables future precision measurements of jet structure at angles larger than typical jet angles (theta less than about 1 radian) to distinguish between different medium response mechanisms, and supports the application of jets as acoustic probes for the flowing quark-gluon plasma medium.
