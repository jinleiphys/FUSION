# U(1)_A Topological Susceptibility and its Slope, Pseudoscalar Gluonium and the Spin of the Proton

> We review the determinations of the pseudoscalar glueball and eventual radial excitation of the η' masses and decay constants from QCD spectral sum rules (QSSR). The glueball mass is (2.05+-0.19) which one can compare with the eventual experimental candidate X(1835), while the η(1400) is likely a radial excitation of the η'-meson. Their effects on the estimates of U(1)_A topological susceptibility and its slope as well as the impact of the latter in the estimate of the spin of the proton is discussed. We predict the singlet polarized parton distributions to be a^0(Q^2=4 GeV^2)=0.32+-0.02, which is about a factor two smaller than the OZI value, but comparable with the COMPASS measurement of 0.24+-0.02.

## Key claim

The paper reviews QCD spectral sum rule (QSSR) determinations of the pseudoscalar glueball mass, the topological susceptibility and its slope, and uses these to predict the singlet axial charge of the proton, finding a value about a factor two smaller than the OZI prediction but comparable with COMPASS data, under the assumption that OZI violation in the singlet channel is entirely contained in the slope of the topological susceptibility.

## Method

The author applies QCD spectral sum rules (QSSR) to two-point correlators of the topological charge density operator and related quark currents, using Laplace/Borel sum rules with stability criteria in the Borel parameter tau and the continuum threshold t_c. The spectral functions are saturated by the pseudoscalar glueball, the eta' meson, and the QCD continuum, with input from the gluon condensate and triple gluon condensate. The resulting topological susceptibility and its slope are then used in a composite-operator plus proper-vertex approach to relate the singlet axial charge a^0 to the octet axial charge a^8.

## Key numbers

* Pseudoscalar glueball mass: M_G = (2.05 ± 0.19) GeV, with decay constant f_G = (8-17) MeV.
* Topological susceptibility in pure Yang-Mills (no quarks): chi(0)|_{no quarks} ≈ -[(106-122) MeV]^4 from QSSR; lattice SU(3) gives -[(191 ± 5) MeV]^4.
* Slope of topological susceptibility in pure Yang-Mills: chi'(0)|_{no quarks} ≈ -[(7 ± 3) MeV]^2 from QSSR; quenched lattice gives -[(9.8 ± 0.9) MeV]^2.
* Slope of topological susceptibility with massless quarks: sqrt(chi'(0))|_{massless} ≈ (26.5 ± 3.1) MeV.
* Slope of the full singlet correlator with massive quarks: sqrt(psi'_5(0)) = (33.5 ± 3.9) MeV.
* eta' decay constant from QSSR in chiral limit: f_{eta'} ≈ (24.1 ± 3.5) MeV; with massive quarks: (27.4 ± 3.7) MeV.
* Predicted singlet axial charge: a^0(Q^2=4 GeV^2) = 0.32 ± 0.02, compared to COMPASS measurement 0.24 ± 0.02.
* Predicted first moment of polarized structure function: Gamma_1^p(Q^2=4 GeV^2) = 0.137 ± 0.008.

## Context

This review extends the author's earlier QSSR work on the topological susceptibility (Narison, Shore, Veneziano, 1980s-1990s) and compares with lattice results from the Pisa group (Di Giacomo et al., 1990s) and with large-N_c results from Witten and Veneziano. The proton spin application builds on the approach of Shore (1990s) using the composite operator formalism, and the predictions are compared with experimental data from SMC and COMPASS. The work enables further lattice tests of the slope of the topological susceptibility with dynamical fermions, which the author suggests as a future direction for the Pisa group.
