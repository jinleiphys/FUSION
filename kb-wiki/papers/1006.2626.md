# Five-body cluster structure of double-$Λ$ hypernucleus $^{11}_{ΛΛ}$Be

> Energy levels of the double $Λ$ hypernucleus, $^{11}_{ΛΛ}$Be are calculated within the framework of an $ααn ΛΛ$ five-body model. Interactions between constituent particles are determined so as to reproduce reasonably the observed low-energy properties of the $αα$, $ααn$ nuclei and the existing data for $Λ$-binding energies of the $αΛ$, $ααΛ$, $αn Λ$ and $ααn Λ$ systems. An effective $ΛΛ$ interaction is constructed so as to reproduce, within the $αΛΛ$ three-body model, the $B_{ΛΛ}$ of $^6_{ΛΛ}$He, which was extracted from the emulsion experiment, the NAGARA event. With no adjustable parameters for the $ααn ΛΛ$ system, $B_{ΛΛ}$ of the ground and bound excited states of $^{11}_{ΛΛ}$Be are calculated with the Gaussian Expansion Method. The Hida event, recently observed at KEK-E373 experiment, is interpreted as an observation of the ground state of the $^{11}_{ΛΛ}$Be.

# Five-body cluster structure of double-Λ hypernucleus ¹¹_ΛΛBe

## Key claim

The Hida event observed at KEK-E373 can be interpreted as the ground state of ¹¹_ΛΛBe, with the calculated ΛΛ binding energy of 18.23 MeV for the 3/2⁻ ground state being consistent with the experimental value of 20.83 ± 1.27 MeV within 2σ, though the calculation uses no adjustable parameters for the five-body system.

## Method

The authors performed a five-body ααnΛΛ cluster-model calculation using the Gaussian Expansion Method with 35 sets of Jacobi coordinates and approximately 50,000 basis functions. All two-body interactions (ΛΛ, Λn, Λα, nα, αα) were fixed by reproducing observed binding energies of subsystems including the NAGARA event for ⁶_ΛΛHe, and a phenomenological ααn three-body force was introduced to fit the low-lying states of ⁹Be. The five-body Schrödinger equation was solved variationally with no free parameters for the full ¹¹_ΛΛBe system.

## Key numbers

- B_ΛΛ(⁶_ΛΛHe) = 6.91 ± 0.16 MeV from the NAGARA event, used to tune the ΛΛ interaction
- B_ΛΛ(¹⁰_ΛΛBe) for the 2⁺₁ state: calculated 11.88 MeV, compared to experimental 11.90 ± 0.13 MeV (Demachi-Yanagi event)
- B_ΛΛ(¹⁰_ΛΛBe) for the 0⁺₁ ground state: calculated 14.74 MeV, compared to experimental 14.6 ± 0.4 MeV
- B_ΛΛ(¹¹_ΛΛBe) for the 3/2⁻ ground state: calculated 18.23 MeV, compared to Hida event value 20.83 ± 1.27 MeV
- Ground state energy of ¹¹_ΛΛBe: -19.81 MeV from the five-body breakup threshold, bound by 5.1 MeV below the lowest ¹⁰_ΛΛBe + n threshold
- ΛΛ bond energy V_ΛΛ^bond: 0.56 MeV for ¹¹_ΛΛBe, 0.54 MeV for ⁶_ΛΛHe, and 0.53 MeV for ¹⁰_ΛΛBe
- RMS distances in ¹¹_ΛΛBe: r̄_α-α = 3.10 fm, r̄_α-n = 3.33 fm, r̄_(αα)-n = 2.94 fm, showing ~20% shrinkage from ⁹Be upon adding first Λ and ~6% further shrinkage upon adding second Λ

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous four-body ααΛΛ cluster-model study of ¹⁰_ΛΛBe (Hiyama et al., 2002), which interpreted the Demachi-Yanagi event as the 2⁺ excited state. The present five-body calculation is motivated by the Hida event reported by Nakazawa (2009) and provides the first theoretical interpretation of this new double-Λ hypernucleus. The results enable systematic comparison with future emulsion data from the J-PARC E07 experiment and demonstrate that the ΛΛ bond energy remains nearly constant (~0.5 MeV) across ⁶_ΛΛHe, ¹⁰_ΛΛBe, and ¹¹_ΛΛBe, despite the small ΔB_ΛΛ value for ¹¹_ΛΛBe. The nuclear-core shrinkage effect, originally predicted by Motoba (1983) for single-Λ hypernuclei and confirmed experimentally for ⁷_ΛLi by Tanida (2001), is shown to persist with a second Λ addition.
