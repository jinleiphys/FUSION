# Investigating the nature of light scalar mesons with semileptonic decays of D mesons

> We study the semileptonic decays of $D_{s}^{+}$, $D^{+}$, and $D^{0}$ mesons into the light scalar mesons [$f_{0} (500)$, $K_{0}^{\ast} (800)$, $f_{0} (980)$, and $a_{0}(980)$] and the light vector mesons [$Žü(770)$, $Žē(782)$, $K^{\ast} (892)$, and $ŽĢ(1020)$]. With the help of a chiral unitarity approach in coupled channels, we compute the branching fractions for scalar meson processes of the semileptonic $D$ decays in a simple way. Using current known values of the branching fractions, we make predictions for the branching fractions of the semileptonic decay modes with other scalar and vector mesons. Furthermore, we calculate the $ŽĆ^{+} ŽĆ^{-}$, $ŽĆ╬Ę$, $ŽĆK$, and $K^{+} K^{-}$ invariant mass distributions in the semileptonic decays of $D$ mesons, which will help us clarify the nature of the light scalar mesons.

## Key claim

The paper argues that semileptonic decays of $D$ mesons can clarify the nature of light scalar mesons by treating them as dynamically generated resonances from two pseudoscalar mesons in a chiral unitary approach, rather than as conventional $q\bar{q}$ states; the key hedge is that the hadronization amplitude $C$ is assumed constant and common to all scalar meson production reactions based on SU(3) flavor symmetry.

## Method

The authors compute semileptonic decay widths for $D_s^+$, $D^+$, and $D^0$ into light scalar mesons ($f_0(500)$, $K_0^*(800)$, $f_0(980)$, $a_0(980)$) and vector mesons ($\rho(770)$, $\omega(782)$, $K^*(892)$, $\phi(1020)$) using a chiral unitary approach in coupled channels. For scalar mesons, an extra $\bar{q}q$ pair with vacuum quantum numbers is introduced to hadronize the primary $q\bar{q}$ pair into two pseudoscalar mesons, and the final state interaction is described by scattering amplitudes from the chiral unitary approach. For vector mesons, hadronization proceeds directly from the primary $q\bar{q}$ pair without an extra pair. The decay widths are evaluated using the Fermi coupling constant $G_F$ and the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix elements, with the hadronization amplitude taken as a constant $C$ common to all scalar meson channels.

## Key numbers

- $\mathcal{B}[D_s^+ \to \phi(1020) e^+ \nu_e] = (2.49 \pm 0.14) \times 10^{-2}$ (observed branching fraction, used as input)
- $\mathcal{B}[D_s^+ \to f_0(980) e^+ \nu_e, f_0(980) \to \pi^+ \pi^-] = (2.00 \pm 0.32) \times 10^{-3}$ (observed branching fraction, used as input)
- $\mathcal{B}[D^+ \to \bar{K}^*(892)^0 e^+ \nu_e, \bar{K}^*(892)^0 \to K^- \pi^+] = (3.68 \pm 0.10) \times 10^{-2}$ (observed branching fraction)
- $\mathcal{B}[D^+ \to \rho(770)^0 e^+ \nu_e] = (2.18^{+0.17}_{-0.25}) \times 10^{-3}$ (observed branching fraction)
- $\mathcal{B}[D^0 \to K^*(892)^- e^+ \nu_e] = (2.16 \pm 0.16) \times 10^{-2}$ (observed branching fraction)
- $\mathcal{B}[D_s^+ \to \omega(782) e^+ \nu_e] < 2.0 \times 10^{-3}$ (upper limit)
- $D_s^+$ mean life: $(500 \pm 7) \times 10^{-15}$ s

- $D^0$ mean life: $(410.1 \pm 1.5) \times 10^{-15}$ s

## Context

This work extends the hadronization scheme previously applied to $B$ meson decays into $J/\psi$ plus light mesons by Liang (2014) and to semileptonic $B$ decays into $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ and $D_0^*(2400)$ by Navarra (2015), now applied to semileptonic $D$ meson decays. It contrasts with approaches using QCD sum rules (Bediaga 2003) or light-cone sum rules (Meissner 2013) that treat scalar mesons as $q\bar{q}$ states, and instead follows the chiral unitary approach (Oller 1997, Kaiser 1998) where scalar mesons are dynamically generated resonances. The predictions for branching fractions and invariant mass distributions of $\pi^+\pi^-$, $\pi\eta$, $\pi K$, and $K^+K^-$ in semileptonic $D$ decays are intended to help distinguish between two-quark and four-quark interpretations of light scalar mesons.
