# Canonical Ensemble of Initial States Leading to Chiral Fluctuations

> In energetic heavy ion collisions, if quark-gluon plasma is formed, its hadronization may lead to observable critical fluctuations, i.e., DCC formation. The strength and observability of these fluctuations depend on the initial state. Here we study the canonical ensemble of initial states of chiral fluctuations in heavy ion collisions and the probability to obtain observable domains of chiral condensates.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the initial states of chiral fluctuations in heavy ion collisions should be modeled by a canonical thermal ensemble at the freeze-out temperature, rather than the idealized "quench" initial conditions used in prior work, and that this more realistic ensemble significantly affects the probability of forming observable Disoriented Chiral Condensate (DCC) domains.

## Method

The authors use the linear sigma model in the mean-field approximation, assuming a one-dimensional Bjorken scaling expansion and a quench scenario where the temperature is set to zero after freeze-out. They generate a canonical ensemble of initial chiral field configurations at the freeze-out temperature \(T_0\) using a Monte Carlo rejection method, sampling from a Boltzmann distribution that includes the kinetic and potential energy densities of the chiral fields plus the quark energy density. The equations of motion are then solved numerically for each initial state to study the subsequent evolution and domain formation.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature: \(T_0 \approx 130-140\ \text{MeV}\)
- Pion decay constant: \(f_\pi = 93\ \text{MeV}\)
- Pion mass in vacuum: \(m_\pi = 138\ \text{MeV}\)
- Sigma mass: \(m_\sigma \approx 600\ \text{MeV}\) (corresponding to \(\lambda \approx 20\))
- Quark coupling constant: \(g \approx 3.36\) (from constituent quark mass \(m_N/3\))
- Freeze-out proper time: \(\tau_0 \sim 7\ \text{fm}/c\)
- Critical temperature: \(T_c = \sqrt{2} v \approx 124\ \text{MeV}\)
- Quark energy density range: \(0\) to \(257\ \text{MeV/fm}^3\) for the standard parameters
- Oscillation damping due to quarks: reduction by about \(40-50\%\)
- Phase transition duration: about \(2.5\ \text{fm}/c\) (about \(0.5\ \text{fm}/c\) slower than without quarks)

## Context

This work extends the microcanonical ensemble study of initial states with fixed chiral angular momentum by Biró, Molnár, and Csernai (1997) to a canonical ensemble at fixed temperature, which is more physically motivated for thermalized quark-gluon plasma at freeze-out. The authors contrast their approach with earlier quench scenario studies by Huang and Wang (1994), Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993), and others, which used idealized initial conditions such as \(\sigma_0=0, \vec\pi_0=0\) with small initial field derivatives. The results enable more realistic predictions for the observability of DCC fluctuations in heavy ion experiments by providing the statistical distribution of initial chiral configurations rather than assuming a single representative state.
