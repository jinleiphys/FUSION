# Charge Fluctuations at Disoriented Chiral Transition

> The signatures of the creation of a disoriented chiral condensate in heavy-ion collisions are studied in the Ginzburg-Landau description of chiral phase transition. Scaling properties are found that characterize the fluctuations of charged particles. There exists also an explicitly calculable measure that can directly be measured. Experimental procedures needed to find the signatures are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that charge fluctuations from a disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) produced in a rapid, far-from-equilibrium chiral phase transition exhibit scaling behaviors culminating in a numerical index \(\nu\) that is independent of the Ginzburg-Landau parameters \(a\), \(b\), and the rapidity window \(\delta\), but the authors later qualify that strict \(x\)-independence cannot be maintained when theoretical limitations are considered, yielding a modified value of \(\nu = 1.42 \pm 0.13\) rather than the initial value of 1.29.

## Method

The authors use a Ginzburg-Landau free energy functional for the isovector chiral field \(\vec\phi\) (neglecting the derivative term) and a coherent-state representation for pions to compute bivariate factorial moments \(f_{q_1,q_2}\) of the charged-particle multiplicity distribution. They then derive normalized factorial moments \(F_{q_1,q_2}\) and show that plotting \(\ln F_{q_1,q_2}\) versus \(\ln F_{2,2}\) yields a linear region, from which a scaling exponent \(\beta_{q_1,q_2}\) is extracted. The dependence of \(\beta_q\) on \(q\) is fitted to a power law \(\beta_q \propto (q-1)^\nu\) to obtain the index \(\nu\). Additionally, they compute a global measure \(B = (\langle \delta Q^2 \rangle - \langle N_{\rm ch} \rangle)/\langle n_+ n_- \rangle\) and find it equals 2 for the DCC scenario.

## Key numbers

- The scaling index \(\nu = 1.29\) is found from the linear fit in Fig. 3 for the region \(0.2 < \ln F_{2,2} < 0.9\).
- After accounting for the lower bound on \(\delta\) (which restricts \(\ln F_{2,2} < 0.65\)), the modified value is \(\nu = 1.42 \pm 0.13\).
- The global measure \(B\) is predicted to be exactly 2 for the DCC scenario, in contrast to \(B \leq 0\) for a quark-gluon plasma or hadron gas.
- The lower bound on \(\delta\) is given by \(\delta > x_0^2 b / a^2\) with \(x_0 = \sqrt{4\ln 2} = 1.67\).

## Context

This work extends the Ginzburg-Landau approach to multiparticle production (previously applied to isoscalar fields by Hwa and collaborators) to the isovector case of disoriented chiral condensates. It contrasts with prior studies of event-by-event charge fluctuations (e.g., Jeon and Koch; Gavin, Pruneau, and Zajc) that focused on distinguishing a quark-gluon plasma from a hadron gas, and with the \(D\) measure proposed in those works. The paper enables experimental searches for DCC via factorial-moment scaling and the \(B\) measure, provided severe \(p_T\) cuts are applied to isolate local condensates, and notes that the WA98 experiment (2001) could not test this due to lack of \(p_T\) information.
