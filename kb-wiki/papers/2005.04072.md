# Precision measurement of the $E2$ transition strength to the 2$^+_1$ state of $^{12}$C

> The form factor of the electromagnetic excitation of $^{12}$C to its 2$^+_1$ state was measured at extremely low momentum transfers in an electron scattering experiment at the S-DALINAC. A combined analysis with the world form factor data results in a reduced transition strength $B(E2; 2^+_1\rightarrow 0^+_1) =7.63(19)$ e$^2$fm$^4$ with an accuracy improved to 2.5\%. In-Medium-No Core Shell Model results with interactions derived from chiral effective field theory are capable to reproduce the result. A quadrupole moment $Q(2^+_1) = 5.97(30)$ efm$^2$ can be extracted from the strict correlation with the $B((E2)$ strength emerging in the calculations.

## Key claim

The reduced transition strength \(B(E2; 2^+_1\rightarrow 0^+_1)\) for \(^{12}\)C is measured as \(7.63(19)\) e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) with an improved accuracy of 2.5%, but this result depends on a combined analysis of new low-momentum-transfer electron scattering data with the world form factor data and a model-independent fit extrapolated to the photon point.

## Method

The form factor of the electromagnetic excitation of \(^{12}\)C to its \(2^+_1\) state was measured at the S-DALINAC using a 42.5 MeV electron beam and the LINTOTT spectrometer at scattering angles of 69°, 81°, and 93°, corresponding to momentum transfers \(q \simeq (0.25-0.32)\) fm\(^{-1}\). The inelastic form factor was determined relative to the well-known elastic scattering cross section by alternating measurements of the elastic and inelastic peaks under identical spectrometer settings to minimize systematic uncertainties. These new low-\(q\) data were combined with existing form factor data from the literature and fitted using an analytic, model-independent expansion in plane-wave Born approximation, after correcting the experimental data for distorted-wave Born approximation effects using a theoretical transition density from a No Core Shell Model calculation.

## Key numbers

*   Reduced transition strength: \(B(E2; 2^+_1\rightarrow 0^+_1) = 7.63(19)\) e\(^2\)fm\(^4\) with a relative uncertainty of 2.5%, extracted from the combined form factor fit.
*   Form factor squared at the photon point: \(|F(q)|^2 = 1.515(40) \times 10^{-8}\), obtained from the fit including the new low-\(q\) data; without these data the value is \(1.443(161) \times 10^{-8}\) with a four times larger relative uncertainty.
*   Extracted quadrupole moment: \(Q(2^+_1) = 5.97(30)\) e fm\(^2\), derived from the correlation between \(B(E2)\) and \(Q(2^+_1)\) observed in the In-Medium No Core Shell Model calculations.
*   Experimental form factors from the present work: at \(q = 0.322\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(|F(q)|^2 = 6.34(9) \times 10^{-4}\); at \(q = 0.290\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(|F(q)|^2 = 4.18(7) \times 10^{-4}\); at \(q = 0.252\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(|F(q)|^2 = 2.50(11) \times 10^{-4}\).

## Context

This work improves the precision of the \(B(E2)\) strength for the \(2^+_1\) state in \(^{12}\)C, a key benchmark for ab initio calculations and a quantity that determines the degree of alpha clustering in the ground state wave function. The new result is used to test a family of chiral two- plus three-nucleon interactions and the novel In-Medium No Core Shell Model (IM-NCSM) method, finding very good agreement. The precise \(B(E2)\) value, combined with the correlation observed in the calculations, enables an extraction of the quadrupole moment \(Q(2^+_1)\) with an uncertainty almost an order of magnitude smaller than the previous experimental determination from a Coulomb-excitation reorientation-effect measurement by Kumar et al. (2018).
