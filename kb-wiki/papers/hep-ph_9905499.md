# Hadronic Regge Trajectories: Problems and Approaches

> We scrutinized hadronic Regge trajectories in a framework of two different models --- string and potential. Our results are compared with broad spectrum of existing theoretical quark models and all experimental data from PDG98. It was recognized that Regge trajectories for mesons and baryons are not straight and parallel lines in general in the current resonance region both experimentally and theoretically, but very often have appreciable curvature, which is flavor-dependent. For a set of baryon Regge trajectories this fact is well described in the considered potential model. The standard string models predict linear trajectories at high angular momenta J with some form of nonlinearity at low J.

# Hadronic Regge Trajectories: Problems and Approaches

## Key claim
Regge trajectories for mesons and baryons are not straight and parallel lines in the current resonance region, but often have appreciable curvature that is flavor-dependent; this nonlinearity is well described by a potential model for a set of baryon trajectories, while standard string models predict linear trajectories at high angular momenta with some nonlinearity at low J.

## Method
The authors computed baryon mass spectra and Regge trajectories for u, d, s, c, b flavors by solving the Schrodinger equation with a Hamiltonian containing Coulomb, linear confinement, contact, and tensor potentials using the hyperspherical functions method in the hypercentral approximation, for angular momenta L=0 through 20 and radial quantum numbers N_r=0,1,2. Separately, they constructed Regge trajectories from four string baryon models (quark-diquark, linear q-q-q, Y-configuration, and triangle configuration) and a meson string model, using classical rotational solutions with spin-orbit corrections, and compared all results to experimental data from PDG98.

## Key numbers
- Input quark masses in the potential model: m_u=m_d=330 MeV, m_s=607 MeV, m_c=1500 MeV, m_b=5170 MeV; string tension b=350 MeV/fm
- Median Regge slopes <alpha'> for baryons with N_r=0: Up-Down 0.90 GeV^-2, Strange 0.95 GeV^-2, Charm 0.73 GeV^-2, Bottom 0.31 GeV^-2
- Median Regge slopes for N_r=1: Up-Down 0.87 GeV^-2, Strange 0.95 GeV^-2, Charm 0.77 GeV^-2, Bottom 0.34 GeV^-2
- Median Regge slopes for N_r=2: Up-Down 0.84 GeV^-2, Strange 0.94 GeV^-2, Charm 0.81 GeV^-2, Bottom 0.37 GeV^-2
- In the asymptotic regime, trajectories for all flavors become linear with slope alpha' approximately 0.9 GeV^-2
- For linear confinement (exponent nu=1), linear regime starts from L>18; for oscillator confinement (nu=2), linear regime starts only from L>20
- String model input: gamma=0.175 GeV^2 for quark-diquark, gamma_Y=2/3*gamma for Y-configuration, gamma_delta=3/8*gamma for triangle configuration; m_u=m_d=130 MeV, m_s=300 MeV, m_c=1600 MeV
- Experimental nonlinear baryon trajectories: N 3/2^- parent has average slope <alpha'>=0.74 GeV^-2 with standard deviation sigma=0.20 GeV^-2; Delta 1/2+ parent has <alpha'>=1.71 GeV^-2 with sigma=1.33 GeV^-2
- Experimental nonlinear meson trajectories: rho-a2 parent has <alpha'>=1.07 GeV^-2 with sigma=0.51 GeV^-2; J/Psi radial has <alpha'>=1.09 GeV^-2 with sigma=0.69 GeV^-2

## Context
This work extends the potential model of Inopin (1990s) for N, Delta, Omega baryons to all flavors and higher angular momenta, and compares it with string models from Kogut (1970s), the three-string Y-configuration of Artru and Mennessier (1974), and the triangle configuration. It contradicts the conventional picture of strictly linear, parallel Regge trajectories and supports earlier observations of nonlinearity by Hey and Kelly (1983) and Hendry (1985). The results enable future work on the flavor dependence of Regge trajectories, the connection between confinement potential form and trajectory curvature, and the identification of the angular momentum scale at which linearity sets in for different hadron species.
