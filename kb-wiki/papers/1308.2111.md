# Anisotropic flow of thermal photons as a quark-gluon plasma viscometer

> We present state-of-the-art calculations of viscous photon emission from nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC. Fluctuating initial density profiles are evolved with event-by-event viscous hydrodynamics. Momentum spectra of thermal photons radiated by these explosively expanding fireballs and their $p_T$-differential anisotropic flow coefficients $v_n(p_T)$ are computed, both with and without accounting for viscous corrections to the standard thermal emission rates. Viscous corrections to the rates are found to have a larger effect on the $v_n$ coefficients than the viscous suppression of hydrodynamic flow anisotropies. The benefits of taking the ratio of elliptic to triangular flow, $v_2/v_3$, are discussed, and the spacetime regions which contribute dominantly to the photon flow harmonics are identified. The directed flow $v_1$ of thermal photons is predicted for RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key claim

Viscous corrections to the thermal photon emission rates have a larger effect on the anisotropic flow coefficients \(v_n\) than the viscous suppression of hydrodynamic flow anisotropies themselves, making the ratio \(v_2/v_3\) of thermal photons a complementary viscometer that is weighted toward earlier fireball stages than hadronic observables.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event simulations using the integrated iEBE-VISHNU framework with boost-invariant viscous hydrodynamics (VISH2+1), evolving fluctuating initial density profiles from Monte-Carlo Glauber (MCGlb) and Monte-Carlo KLN (MCKLN) models. Thermal photon emission rates were computed for both the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase (including Hard Thermal Loop resummed quark self-energies with anisotropic distribution functions) and the hadron gas phase (using an SU(3) massive Yang-Mills approach), with viscous corrections included to linear order in the shear stress tensor \(\pi^{\mu\nu}\). Photon anisotropic flow coefficients \(v_n\{\mathrm{SP}\}\) were calculated using the scalar product method relative to charged hadron flow planes.

## Key numbers

- For MCKLN initial conditions with \(\eta/s = 0.20\), the off-equilibrium correction in the photon spectrum from the term proportional to \(\pi^{\mu\nu}\) remains smaller than the equilibrium contribution up to \(p_T \sim 3.5\) GeV in 0-40% Pb+Pb at LHC, and up to \(p_T \sim 2.5\) GeV in 20-40% Au+Au at RHIC.
- Hydrodynamic evolution starts at \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\) and ends at an isothermal freeze-out surface of temperature \(T_\mathrm{dec} = 120\) MeV.
- The matching of QGP and hadron gas photon emission rates occurs in the temperature region \(T_\mathrm{sw} = 150-170\) MeV.
- The second wave of thermal photon emission, which carries most of the observed flow anisotropies, comes from the temperature region \(T \sim 150-200\) MeV at times \(\tau = 4\) to \(8\) fm/\(c\).
- For MCGlb (MCKLN) initial conditions, the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio used is \(\eta/s = 0.08\) (0.20), respectively.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Dusling (2009) to use photons as a QGP viscometer by studying the full spectrum of anisotropic flow harmonics \(v_n\) for \(n=1\) through \(5\), not just elliptic flow \(v_2\) as in earlier studies by Chatterjee (2005), Dusling (2009), and van Hees (2011). The calculations build on the event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic framework previously applied to hadron production by Shen (2010, 2011) and Song (2010, 2011). The results enable combined flow analyses of direct photons and hadrons to constrain the temperature dependence of the QGP shear viscosity, complementing hadronic observables that only probe the final freeze-out stage.
