# Octet and Decuplet Baryons in a Covariant and Confining Diquark-Quark Model

> The baryon octet and decuplet masses and Bethe-Salpeter vertex and wave functions are calculated in the ladder approximation to the quark exchange between a scalar or axialvector diquark and a constituent quark. These functions reflecting full Lorentz covariance are given in terms of an expansion in Gegenbauer polynomials. In the rest frame of the baryon, a complete partial wave decomposition of the Bethe-Salpeter wave function is performed. The confinement of quarks and diquarks is implemented via a parametrisation of the corresponding propagators. We also discuss some aspects of the momentum routing in the ladder approximation to the Bethe-Salpeter equation. Numerical results for the octet and decuplet masses with broken flavour SU(3) in the conserved isospin limit are presented.

## Key claim

The paper calculates octet and decuplet baryon masses and Bethe-Salpeter wave functions in a covariant diquark-quark model with confinement implemented via a parametrisation that removes propagator poles, finding that the axialvector diquark channel is necessary for a realistic description of baryons as bound states of quarks and diquarks.

## Method

The authors solve the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation in ladder approximation for baryons as bound states of a quark and either a scalar or axialvector diquark, with quark exchange as the binding interaction. Confinement is modelled by modifying quark and diquark propagators with a multiplicative factor \(f^{-1}(x) = 1 - e^{-d(1+x)}\) that removes mass poles, and the exchanged quark propagator is regularised with a monopole form factor \(\Lambda^2/(q^2+\Lambda^2)\). The Bethe-Salpeter wave functions are expanded in a basis of Lorentz covariants projected onto positive-energy states, and a partial wave decomposition in the rest frame classifies components by orbital angular momentum and spin.

## Key numbers

- The confinement parameter \(d\) appears in the propagator modification factor \(f^{-1}(x) = 1 - e^{-d(1+x)}\); no numerical value for \(d\) is given in the truncated text.
- The monopole form factor cutoff \(\Lambda\) appears in \(\tilde S(q) = S(q)\left( \frac{\Lambda^2}{q^2+\Lambda^2}\right)\); no numerical value for \(\Lambda\) is given in the truncated text.
- The octet wave function contains 2 scalar-diquark scalar functions \(S_i\) (i=1,2) and 6 axialvector-diquark scalar functions \(A_i\) (i=1..6), for a total of 8 independent scalar functions after positive-energy projection.
- The decuplet wave function contains 8 independent scalar functions \(D_i\) and \(E_i\) after positive-energy and Rarita-Schwinger projection.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation (Oettel et al., 1997) which solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for nucleons with only scalar diquarks and calculated electromagnetic, weak, and pionic form factors. The inclusion of the axialvector diquark channel is motivated by the need to improve results for magnetic moments. The model builds on diquark-quark descriptions developed in NJL-type models (Reinhardt, 1990; Vogl, 1991; Buck, 1992; Ishii, 1993) and on a three-dimensional Salpeter approximation (Keil, 1996). The results enable future calculations of baryon form factors and other observables at intermediate momentum transfers relevant to facilities such as CEBAF, MAMI, ELSA, and COSY.
