# Small systems and the single-hit approximation in the AMY parton cascade ALPACA

> Understanding how momentum anisotropies arise in small collision systems is important for a quantitative understanding of collectivity in terms of QCD dynamics in small and large collision systems. In this letter we present results for small collision systems from the newly developed parton cascade \textsc{Alpaca}, which faithfully encodes the AMY effective kinetic theory. \textsc{Alpaca} reproduces quantitatively previously know results from a calculation in the single-hit approximation for small values of the coupling. We discuss in detail how such a comparison is to be carried out. Particularly at larger coupling a generic differences between the two approaches becomes apparent, namely that in parton cascades particles interact over a finite distance while in direct integrations of the Boltzmann equation the interactions are local. This leads to quantitative differences in the extracted values for the elliptic flow coefficient. These discrepancies appear in situations where the mean free path is not large compared to the interaction time and the applicability of kinetic theory is thus questionable.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that a sizeable elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\) can arise from just a single scattering (or fewer) per particle in kinetic theory, but shows that quantitative agreement between the parton cascade Alpaca and the single-hit approximation of Kurkela (2021) is only possible for small values of the coupling; at larger coupling, non-local interactions in the cascade reduce \(v_2\) relative to the local-scattering prediction, and the applicability of kinetic theory itself becomes questionable.

## Method

The authors implement the AMY effective kinetic theory in a new parton cascade called Alpaca, which evolves an ensemble of massless gluons via elastic \(gg \to gg\) scattering with a matrix element regulated by a Debye-like screening mass. They initialize the system with a Color Glass Condensate-like distribution (Kurkela 2015, Lappi 2011) that includes a spatial elliptic perturbation, extract the elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\) from the energy flow, and compare results from the full cascade to the single-hit approximation of Kurkela (2021). To enable comparison, they reduce the number of scatterings per particle either by considering only the first scattering of each particle ("first-hit") or by randomly rejecting scatterings to achieve an average of one scattering per particle ("single-hit").

## Key numbers

* For a toy model with constant cross section \(\sigma = 25\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-2}\) and parameters \(A=0.45\), \(Q_0=3\ \mathrm{GeV}\), \(R_0=5\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\), \(\xi=4\), \(\epsilon=0.5\), the all-hit \(v_2/\varepsilon_2\) ranges from \(0.046 \pm 0.022\) (CGC 12.1) to \(0.200 \pm 0.022\) (CGC 3.1), while the single-hit values are systematically lower (e.g., \(0.0494 \pm 0.0100\) for CGC 2.3 vs. all-hit \(0.19 \pm 0.020\)).
* In the full Alpaca simulation with AMY dynamics, at coupling \(\lambda = 0.5\) the mean number of scatterings per particle is \(\langle N \rangle = 1.66(2)\) and the mean invariant distance between scattering partners is \(\langle d_{ij} \rangle = 4.22(8)\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\); at \(\lambda = 10\) these become \(\langle N \rangle = 13.99(8)\) and \(\langle d_{ij} \rangle = 10.31(9)\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\).
* For the parameter point \(A=0.45\), \(\xi=4\), \(Q_0=3\ \mathrm{GeV}\), \(R_0=5\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\), \(\epsilon=0.5\), the single-hit \(v_2/\varepsilon_2\) from Alpaca agrees with the scaling formula of Kurkela (2021) at \(\lambda=0.5\) but diverges at larger \(\lambda\); at \(\lambda=10\) the all-hit value is significantly below the scaling formula.

## Context

This work extends the single-hit calculation of elliptic flow in small systems by Kurkela (2021) to a full parton cascade implementation of AMY kinetic theory. It confirms the earlier finding that a single scattering per particle can generate a sizeable \(v_2\), but reveals that the parton cascade Alpaca produces many more scatterings than one for the same initial conditions, and that non-local interactions (finite interaction range) reduce \(v_2\) compared to the local-scattering idealization. The results enable a quantification of theoretical uncertainty in kinetic-theory descriptions of small collision systems and highlight a regime where the Boltzmann equation's assumption of short interaction times relative to mean free path breaks down.
