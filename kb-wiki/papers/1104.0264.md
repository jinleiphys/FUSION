# Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation with Interactions from the Similarity Renormalization Group

> We have developed a fully consistent framework for calculations in the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) with $NN$ interactions from the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) and other unitary transformations of realistic interactions. The consistency of our calculations, which use the same Hamiltonian to determine the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) ground states and the residual interaction for QRPA, guarantees an excellent decoupling of spurious strength, without the need for empirical corrections. While work is under way to include SRG-evolved 3N interactions, we presently account for some 3N effects by means of a linearly density-dependent interaction, whose strength is adjusted to reproduce the charge radii of closed-shell nuclei across the whole nuclear chart. As a first application, we perform a survey of the monopole, dipole, and quadrupole response of the calcium isotopic chain and of the underlying single-particle spectra, focusing on how their properties depend on the SRG parameter $λ$. Unrealistic spin-orbit splittings suggest that spin-orbit terms from the 3N interaction are called for. Nevertheless, our general findings are comparable to results from phenomenological QRPA calculations using Skyrme or Gogny energy density functionals. Potentially interesting phenomena related to low-lying strength warrant more systematic investigations in the future.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that using the same SRG-evolved NN interaction consistently in both the HFB ground-state calculation and the QRPA residual interaction yields excellent decoupling of spurious states without empirical corrections, but the resulting spin-orbit splittings are unrealistic, suggesting that spin-orbit terms from the 3N interaction are needed.

## Method

The authors perform spherical HFB calculations using SRG-evolved Argonne V18 NN interactions supplemented by a linearly density-dependent interaction (DDI) that approximates missing 3N effects, with the DDI strength adjusted to reproduce charge radii of closed-shell nuclei. They then build the QRPA on these HFB ground states using the same Hamiltonian, constructing all possible two-quasiparticle configurations for each angular momentum J without truncation, and solve the QRPA equations to obtain excitation spectra for the calcium isotopic chain.

## Key numbers

- The spurious 0+ state in Ca-56 is found at 0.1 keV; the largest spurious 0+ energies across all calculations are approximately 20 keV
- Spurious 1- states lie between 1 and 5 keV in closed-shell nuclei and below 20 keV for open-shell nuclei
- Ground-state energy gains from increasing the single-particle basis from 13 to 15 major shells are 100 keV or less, corresponding to less than 0.1% of the total energy
- The DDI strength C3N for calcium isotopes is 4.41 GeV fm^6 at lambda = 1.78 fm^-1, 3.87 GeV fm^6 at lambda = 2.02 fm^-1, and 2.94 GeV fm^6 at lambda = 2.40 fm^-1
- The variation of individual excited state and centroid energies due to the oscillator length mesh is about 50 keV or less

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous framework using effective interactions in mean-field based approaches (Roth 2010, Paar 2006, Papakonstantinou 2007, 2010) from closed-shell RPA to open-shell QRPA. It provides a fully consistent treatment using SRG-evolved interactions, contrasting with phenomenological QRPA calculations using Skyrme or Gogny energy density functionals. The results will guide ab initio DFT efforts within the UNEDF project, and the identified need for 3N spin-orbit terms motivates future inclusion of SRG-evolved chiral 3N interactions.
