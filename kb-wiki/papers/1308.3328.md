# Exploring the QCD phase diagram through high energy nuclear collisions: An overview

> We present an overview of the status of the studies related to the phase diagram of strong interactions through high-energy nuclear collisions. We discuss both the theoretical and experimental status of establishing the various QCD phase structures in the phase diagram, such as, quark-gluon phase, quark-hadron crossover, crossover temperature, phase boundary and critical point.

# Exploring the QCD phase diagram through high energy nuclear collisions: An overview

## Key claim
The quark-hadron transition at zero baryon chemical potential is established as a crossover by lattice QCD, but the existence and location of a critical point at finite baryon chemical potential remains uncertain, with lattice calculations yielding conflicting results depending on the methodology used (reweighting, Taylor expansion, or imaginary chemical potential).

## Method
The paper reviews both theoretical lattice QCD calculations and experimental heavy-ion collision results to map the QCD phase diagram. Theoretically, chiral susceptibility, strange quark number susceptibility, and the renormalized Polyakov loop are used to determine crossover temperatures. Experimentally, the Beam Energy Scan (BES) program at RHIC varies collision energy to access different regions of temperature and baryon chemical potential, measuring observables such as nuclear modification factor, anisotropic flow, and higher moments of net-proton distributions to search for phase boundaries and the critical point.

## Key numbers
- Chiral crossover temperature at μB = 0 MeV: 151(3)(3) MeV from chiral susceptibility (Aoki 2009) and 154(9) MeV (Bazavov 2011)
- Strange quark number susceptibility crossover temperature: 175(2)(4) MeV (Aoki 2009)
- Renormalized Polyakov loop crossover temperature: 176(3)(4) MeV (Aoki 2009)
- Baryon correlation crossover temperature: 175(1)(7) MeV (Gupta 2011)
- Nuclear modification factor RAA < 1 for charged hadrons at RHIC and LHC, with values at RHIC higher than at LHC for pT < 8 GeV/c
- RAA ~ 1 for photons, W±, and Z bosons at LHC energies
- Turn-off of QGP signatures observed at √sNN = 11.5 GeV and below, where K0S meson RAA becomes > 1 at pT > 2 GeV/c
- Baryon-meson splitting of v2 vanishes at √sNN = 11.5 and 7.7 GeV
- Critical point, if it exists, must be below √sNN = 39 GeV based on κσ² measurements of net-proton distributions

## Context
This overview synthesizes the status of QCD phase diagram studies as of 2013, covering lattice QCD results from Aoki (2006, 2009), Bazavov (2011), and Endrodi (2011), alongside experimental results from RHIC and LHC. The work extends the theoretical framework of Stephanov (2004) on the critical point and the freeze-out curve of Cleymans (1998). It enables future searches for the critical point through the RHIC BES program and upcoming experiments like CBM at GSI and NICA at JINR, while also noting open questions about the quarkyonic phase proposed by McLerran (2009) at FAIR energies.
