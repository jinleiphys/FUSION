# The pionic Drell-Yan process: a brief survey

> The Drell-Yan process is an invaluable tool for probing the structure of hadrons, and the pion-induced Drell-Yan process is unique in its sensitivity to several subtleties of the partonic structure. This is a brief review of the pionic Drell-Yan process with particular emphasis on the dilepton angular distributions and nuclear modification of the parton distributions (the EMC effect).

## Key claim

This paper is a brief review of the pionic Drell-Yan process, not presenting new original results; it surveys existing experimental findings on dilepton angular distributions and the nuclear modification of parton distributions (the EMC effect), with the caveat that the existing data lack the precision needed to distinguish between competing theoretical models.

## Method

The authors review results from five pion-induced Drell-Yan experiments: CIP at BNL, NA3, Omega and NA10 at CERN, and E615 at FNAL. They examine the measured angular distributions of lepton pairs (parameters λ, μ, ν) and cross-section ratios between nuclear targets and hydrogen or deuterium targets, comparing these data to theoretical predictions including higher-twist QCD, Boer-Mulders functions, and the Cloët-Bentz-Thomas (CBT) model for flavor-dependent nuclear parton distributions.

## Key numbers

- CIP experiment used π± beams at 80, 225, and 253 GeV/c on C, Cu, W targets with dilepton invariant mass range [3.0, 9.0] GeV/c².
- NA3 experiment used π± beams at 150, 200, and 280 GeV/c on H and Pt targets with dilepton invariant mass range [4.1, 8.5] GeV/c².
- NA10 experiment used π− beams at 140, 194, and 286 GeV/c on W and D targets with dilepton invariant mass range [4.0, 8.5] GeV/c².
- Omega experiment used π± beams at 39.5 GeV/c on W targets with dilepton invariant mass ranges [2.0, 2.7] and [4.0, 5.0] GeV/c².
- E615 experiment used π− beam at 252 GeV/c on W target with dilepton invariant mass range [4.1, 8.6] GeV/c².
- E615 data covered 0 < pT < 5 GeV/c, 4.05 < m(ll) < 8.55 GeV/c², and 0.2 < xπ < 1.0, showing clear violation of the Lam-Tung relation.
- NA10 combined 286 GeV and 140 GeV data for the ratio σDY(π−+W)/σDY(π−+D) were compared to BCDMS structure function ratios.
- COMPASS collaboration proposes measurements with 190-GeV/c pion beams.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on the Drell-Yan process as a probe of hadron structure, following the original Drell-Yan mechanism proposed by Drell and Yan (1970) and the discovery of the EMC effect by the European Muon Collaboration (1983). It contrasts the pion-induced Drell-Yan results with proton-induced measurements from the E866 experiment at FNAL and antiproton-induced results from CDF, which did not observe Lam-Tung violation. The paper enables future measurements such as the COMPASS-II program at CERN, which could test flavor-dependent nuclear PDFs using the CBT model predictions of Cloët, Bentz, and Thomas (2006), and could also probe TMD distributions including the Boer-Mulders function and Sivers function.
