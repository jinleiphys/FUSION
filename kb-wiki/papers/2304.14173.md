# U(1) quasi-hydrodynamics: Schwinger-Keldysh effective field theory and holography

> We study the quasi-hydrodynamics of a system with a softly broken $U(1)$ global symmetry using effective field theory (EFT) and holographic methods. In the gravity side, we consider a holographic Proca model in the limit of small bulk mass, which is responsible for a controllable explicit breaking of the $U(1)$ global symmetry in the boundary field theory. We perform a holographic Schwinger-Keldysh analysis, which allows us to derive the form of the boundary effective action in presence of dissipation. We compare our results with the previously proposed EFT and hydrodynamic theories, and we confirm their validity by computing the low-energy quasi-normal modes spectrum analytically and numerically. Additionally, we derive the broken holographic Ward identity for the $U(1)$ current, and discuss the recently proposed novel transport coefficients for systems with explicitly broken symmetries. The setup considered is expected to serve as a toy model for more realistic situations where quasi-hydrodynamics is at work, such as axial charge relaxation in QCD, spin relaxation in relativistic systems, electric field relaxation in magneto-hydrodynamics, or momentum relaxation in condensed matter systems.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that the Schwinger-Keldysh effective field theory for a softly broken U(1) global symmetry, previously proposed in the literature, is valid in the limit where the explicit breaking scale m is much smaller than the temperature T (m/T << 1), and that the diffusion constant of the pseudo-diffusive mode does not receive corrections to leading order in the explicit breaking.

## Method

The authors study a holographic Proca model in five-dimensional anti-de Sitter space, where a small bulk mass for the gauge field introduces a controllable explicit breaking of the U(1) global symmetry in the dual boundary field theory. They perform a holographic Schwinger-Keldysh analysis to derive the boundary effective action including dissipative terms, and compute the low-energy quasi-normal mode spectrum both analytically and numerically. The analysis is performed in the probe limit with zero background charge density, keeping the stress tensor dynamics frozen.

## Key numbers

- The explicit breaking scale m is taken to satisfy m/T << 1, defining the quasi-hydrodynamic regime where the relaxation rate Gamma is small compared to temperature.
- The coefficients in the effective Lagrangian scale as g_{0-3}, f_{0-3}, h_{0-3}, w_{0-2} ~ O(m^0) and Gamma_tilde, Gamma_{0-7}, c_{0-9}, d_{0-9}, w_{3-13} ~ O(m^2) as a function of the explicit breaking parameter m.
- The diffusion constant D is given by D = sigma / chi_{rho rho}, where sigma is the electric conductivity and chi_{rho rho} is the charge susceptibility.
- The relaxation rate Gamma at zero wave-vector determines the exponential decay of charge fluctuations as ~ exp(-Gamma t).

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger-Keldysh effective field theory for a single conserved U(1) charge, originally constructed by Crossley, Glorioso, and Liu (2015), to the case of soft explicit breaking. It builds on prior holographic studies of systems with broken symmetries, including the Proca model analysis by Ammon et al. (2021) and the identification of novel transport coefficients by Armas et al. (2021). The results serve as a toy model for more realistic quasi-hydrodynamic systems such as axial charge relaxation in QCD, spin relaxation in relativistic systems, and momentum relaxation in condensed matter systems.
