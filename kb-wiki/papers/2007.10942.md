# A finite box as a tool to distinguish free quarks from confinement at high temperatures

> Above the pseudocritical temperature T_c of chiral symmetry restoration a chiral spin symmetry (a symmetry of the color charge and of electric confinement) emerges in QCD. This implies that QCD is in a confining mode and there are no free quarks. At the same time correlators of operators constrained by a conserved current behave as if quarks were free. This explains observed fluctuations of conserved charges and the absence of the rho-like structures seen via dileptons. An independent evidence that one is in a confining mode is very welcome. Here we suggest a new tool how to distinguish free quarks from a confining mode. If we put the system into a finite box, then if the quarks are free one necessarily obtains a remarkable diffractive pattern in the propagator of a conserved current. This pattern is clearly seen in a lattice calculation in a finite box and it vanishes in the infinite volume limit as well as in the continuum. In contrast, the full QCD calculations in a finite box show the absence of the diffractive pattern implying that the quarks are confined.

# A finite box as a tool to distinguish free quarks from confinement at high temperatures

## Key claim

Above the pseudocritical temperature of chiral symmetry restoration, QCD is in a confining mode even though correlators of conserved currents behave as if quarks were free; the authors propose that placing the system in a finite box reveals a diffractive pattern in the propagator of a conserved current that is present for free quarks but absent in full QCD, providing an independent test of confinement.

## Method

The authors compute spatial correlators of isovector quark bilinear operators on a finite lattice, first for free noninteracting quarks (using Wilson and overlap Dirac operators on a 32^3 × 8 lattice) and then for full QCD with N_F=2 domain wall fermions at physical quark masses at T=380 MeV (~2.2 T_c). They compare the correlators obtained with periodic and antiperiodic boundary conditions along the propagation direction z, and examine the ratio of full QCD to free quark correlators for the V_t operator.

## Key numbers

- The lattice size used is 32^3 × 8 for both free quark and full QCD calculations
- The effective "chirally symmetric mass" E for propagation of a massless quark in z direction is E=0.37395, very close to the lowest Matsubara frequency π/N_t = π/8
- Full QCD calculations are performed at T=380 MeV, corresponding to approximately 2.2 T_c
- The free quark V_t correlator becomes negative for n_z ~ 14–18 with periodic boundary conditions
- The diffractive structure in free quark correlators disappears exponentially upon increase of N_s at fixed N_t
- The ratio of full QCD to free quark V_t correlators shows deviations only at distances where the absolute value of correlators is suppressed by 5–6 orders of magnitude

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of chiral spin symmetry in QCD above T_c (Glozman 2015, 2018) and spatial correlator calculations (Rohrhofer 2019) that showed QCD in the range T_c–3T_c is characterized by chiral spin symmetry, which is a symmetry of the color charge and chromoelectric interaction but not of the Dirac action. The paper provides an independent test of confinement that complements the chiral spin symmetry evidence, addressing the puzzle that conserved current correlators appear free-quark-like while other observables indicate confinement. The results support the "stringy fluid" picture proposed earlier (Glozman 2019, Rohrhofer 2019) and rule out the scenario of deconfinement in an SU(2)_color subgroup via SU(2)_color–SU(2)_isospin locking.
