# Color Coherence Effects in Dipole-Quark Scattering in the Soft Limit

> Color coherence effects play a crucial role in the description of jet evolution at collider experiments. It is well known that the stimulated gluon emission suffered by energetic jets traversing deconfined QCD matter is also affected by color coherence effects. Through multiple soft scatterings with the medium constituents, an antenna will lose its color correlation, causing its legs to behave as independent emitters after the so-called decoherence time. In this work we provide the first computation of the properties of the recoils produced as a result of these soft scatterings between a color coherent dipole and the medium constituents. Our findings reveal that the angular phase-space of these soft recoils is strongly restricted by the opening angle of the dipole itself due to quantum interference effects. In this long wavelength limit, one can effectively consider that interactions take place with each of the legs of the dipole separately, provided that the angular constraints dictated by the corresponding color flow topologies are respected. This is in complete analogy with the case of soft gluon emission in vacuum, where the recoil quark plays the role of the emitted gluon. As a direct phenomenological application we estimate the collisional energy loss rate of a color antenna. Importantly, these results indicate the way in which color coherence effects can be implemented in jet quenching models that account for the recoils from elastic scatterings, improving in this way our description of medium response physics in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first computation of the properties of medium recoils produced by soft scatterings between a color-coherent dipole and medium constituents, showing that the angular phase-space of these recoils is restricted by the dipole opening angle due to quantum interference effects, in complete analogy with angular ordering in soft gluon emission. The authors explicitly hedge that this result applies in the soft limit where the recoil quark energy E is much smaller than the total dipole energy w (β = E/w ≪ 1).

## Method

The authors compute the leading-order QCD matrix element for t-channel gluon exchange between a soft quark and a color-singlet qq̄ dipole produced by a virtual photon or Z boson, working in the Feynman gauge and taking the soft limit where the recoil quark energy is much smaller than the dipole energy. They separate the antenna current into terms corresponding to interaction with each leg, contract with the recoil quark current, and integrate over azimuthal angle to obtain the angular distribution of the recoil quark. The collisional energy loss rate is then estimated by integrating the differential cross-section over recoil energy and angle, including running coupling effects.

## Key numbers

- The decoherence time for a dipole with opening angle θ is τ_d ∼ θ^{-2/3}, after which the legs behave as independent emitters
- The angular integral in the small-angle limit gives A(θ_0, θ_{qq̄}) ≈ 2 log(θ_{qq̄}/θ_0), where θ_0 is a regulator and θ_{qq̄} is the dipole opening angle
- The energy integral over recoil energy E from E_m to E_M gives E(E_M, E_m) = (16π²/β₀²) × log(E_M/E_m) / [log(2E_m²/Q₀²) × log(2E_M E_m/Q₀²)]
- The cross-section has a soft divergence dE/E² (Rutherford-like), compared to dE/E for soft gluon emission

## Context

This work extends the understanding of color coherence effects in jet quenching, previously studied for medium-induced radiation by Mehtar-Tani et al. (2011-2012) and Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2011-2015), to the properties of medium recoils from elastic scatterings. It provides the theoretical basis for implementing color coherence in jet quenching Monte Carlo models that already account for recoils (He 2015, Zapp 2013, Ke 2020, JETSCAPE 2021, Hulcher 2022) but currently treat partons as on-shell states. The results enable improved modeling of medium response physics in heavy-ion collisions and suggest a new observable: the shift of Z boson reconstructed mass due to collisional energy loss depending on dipole opening angle.
