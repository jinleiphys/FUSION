# Overview of Kbar-N and Kbar-nucleus dynamics

> The main features of coupled-channel Kbar-N dynamics near threshold and its repercussions in few-body Kbar-nuclear systems are briefly reviewed highlighting the I=1/2 Kbar-N-N system. For heavier nuclei, the extension of mean-field calculations to multi-Kbar nuclear quasibound states is discussed focusing on kaon condensation.

# Overview of Kbar-N and Kbar-nucleus dynamics

## Key claim
Coupled-channel Kbar-N dynamics near threshold produces two Gamow poles that dominate low-energy interactions, but the upper pole's position and trajectory are model-dependent and sensitive to off-shell effects, while the lower pole at approximately (1391, -i51) MeV primarily determines the pi-Sigma spectrum shape identified with the Lambda(1405) resonance.

## Method
The paper reviews chirally motivated coupled-channel Kbar-N - pi-Sigma models fitted to low-energy K-p scattering and reaction data, along with few-body calculations for the K-pp system using Faddeev and variational methods. For heavier nuclei, relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations of single- and multi-Kbar nuclei are compared with optical potentials derived from kaonic atom data and (K-,N) nuclear spectra.

## Key numbers
- Lower pole position at (1391, -i51) MeV from the coupled-channel model of Cieply and Smejkal (2007), identified with the Lambda(1405) resonance
- K-pp binding energy ranges: 48 MeV (ATMS single-channel), 17-23 MeV (AMD single-channel), 50-70 MeV (Faddeev coupled-channel), 60-95 MeV (Faddeev coupled-channel), 40-80 MeV (variational coupled-channel)
- K-pp mesonic width ranges: 61 MeV (ATMS), 40-70 MeV (AMD), 90-110 MeV (Faddeev), 45-80 MeV (Faddeev), 40-85 MeV (variational)
- K-pp nonmesonic width ranges: 12 MeV (ATMS), 4-12 MeV (AMD), approximately 20 MeV (variational)
- FINUDA collaboration reported K-pp signal with (B, Gamma) approximately (115, 67) MeV
- DISTO collaboration reported K-pp signal with (B, Gamma) approximately (105, 118) MeV
- Nuclear-matter depth ReV_Kbar(rho_0) approximately -(150-200) MeV from global fits to K--atom data
- Chirally based models yield ReV_Kbar(rho_0) approximately -100 MeV
- Shallower potentials with ReV_Kbar(rho_0) approximately -(40-60) MeV from Watson-like self-consistency
- Potential depths 160-190 MeV reproduce (K-,n) and (K-,p) spectra on 12C at p_K- = 1 GeV/c
- Single-Kbar quasibound states in medium and heavy nuclei bound by 100-150 MeV from RMF calculations
- Widths larger than 100 MeV near threshold, remaining of order 50 MeV or more

## Context
This review extends the coupled-channel Kbar-N framework previously developed by Hyodo and Weise (2008) and Cieply and Smejkal (2007), and contrasts with the single-pole Yamazaki-Akaishi (2002) phenomenological amplitudes. The work addresses the ongoing debate between extremely deep Kbar-nucleus potentials (from kaonic atom fits by Friedman and Gal, 2007) and shallower chirally based potentials, with implications for the existence of Kbar-nuclear quasibound states. The saturation of Kbar separation energies in multi-Kbar nuclei, demonstrated by Gazda, Friedman, Gal, and Mares (2008), provides an argument against kaon condensation under strong-interaction equilibrium conditions, while leaving open the possibility of kaon condensation in neutron stars under weak-interaction equilibrium.
