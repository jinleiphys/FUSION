# Renormalization of the three-flavor quark-meson diquark model

> We discuss the properties of the two- and three-flavor quark-meson diquark (QMD) model as a renormalizable low-energy effective model for color superconductivity in dense QCD. The effective degrees of freedom are scalars, pseudo-scalars, diquarks, and quarks. The parameters in the scalar/pseudo-scalar sector can be determined by matching the meson pole masses and decay constants to their observed values using the on-shell renormalization scheme. The remaining parameters are in the diquark sector and a priori unknown. In principle, they can be calculated from QCD, but we consider them free. We renormalize the thermodynamic potential in the 2SC phase for two flavors and in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase for three flavors, determining the counterterms of the couplings in the diquark sector. We derive a set of renormalization group equations for these couplings that are used to improve the thermodynamic potential. As an application, we calculate the gap and the speed of sound in the ideal CFL phase. It is shown that the gap approaches a constant as $μ_B\rightarrow\infty$ and that the speed of sound relaxes to the conformal limit from above.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the three-flavor quark-meson diquark (QMD) model is renormalizable and can be used to describe color superconductivity in dense QCD, but the results depend on several a priori unknown couplings in the diquark sector that are treated as free parameters rather than calculated from QCD.

## Method

The authors extend the previously studied two-flavor QMD model to three flavors by constructing a Lagrangian that includes scalar mesons, pseudoscalar mesons, diquarks, and quarks, with the meson sector organized in a \(U(3)\) matrix and diquarks treated as left-handed and right-handed fields. They renormalize the thermodynamic potential in the 2SC phase for two flavors and in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase for three flavors using dimensional regularization in the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme, determining counterterms for the diquark sector couplings by matching the ultraviolet divergences of the one-loop quark contribution. They derive renormalization group equations for the running couplings and use them to improve the thermodynamic potential, then apply the result to calculate the gap and speed of sound in the ideal CFL phase at large baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The diquark mass parameter \(m_{\Delta}\) is chosen in the range 500–900 MeV and the quark-diquark coupling \(g_{\Delta}\) is taken approximately equal to the Yukawa coupling \(g\), yielding a transition to 2SC matter at around \(\mu_B = 300\) MeV and a gap of 50–150 MeV in the two-flavor case.
- The counterterm for the diquark mass squared in the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme is \(\delta m_{\Delta,\ms}^2 = \frac{4g_{\Delta}^2 \Lambda^{-2\epsilon}}{(4\pi)^2 \epsilon}\).
- The counterterm for the quark-diquark coupling squared is \(\delta g_{\Delta,\ms}^2 = \frac{4g_{\Delta}^4 \Lambda^{-2\epsilon}}{(4\pi)^2 \epsilon}\).
- The counterterm for the diquark self-coupling is \(\delta \lambda_{\Delta}^{\ms} = \frac{4g_{\Delta}^2 [2\lambda_{\Delta} - g_{\Delta}^2] \Lambda^{-4\epsilon}}{(4\pi)^2 \epsilon}\).
- The gap in the CFL phase approaches a constant as \(\mu_B \rightarrow \infty\) and the speed of sound relaxes to the conformal limit from above.

## Context

This work extends the two-flavor QMD model previously studied by Andersen and Nødtvedt (2023, 2024) to three flavors, enabling description of the color-flavor-locked phase alongside the 2SC phase. It provides a renormalizable alternative to the nonrenormalizable Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model used in earlier studies by Baym et al. (2018) and Gholami (2023), which suffers from regularization artifacts at high chemical potentials. The results enable future calculations of the equation of state and transport properties in dense QCD matter that can be compared with Bayesian constraints from neutron star observations, such as those by Finn et al. (2023).
