# Momentum-space 3N Faddeev calculations of hadronic and electromagnetic reactions with proton-proton Coulomb and three-nucleon forces included

> We extend our approach to incorporate the proton-proton (pp) Coulomb force into the three-nucleon (3N) momentum-space Faddeev calculations of elastic proton-deuteron (pd) scattering and breakup to the case when also a three-nucleon force (3NF) is acting. In addition we formulate that approach in the application to electron- and gamma-induced reactions on 3He. The main new ingredient is a 3-dimensional screened pp Coulomb t-matrix obtained by a numerical solution of a 3-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger equation (LSE). The resulting equations have the same structure as the Faddeev equations which describe pd scattering without 3NF acting. That shows the practical feasibility of both presented formulations.

## Key claim

The authors extend their previously developed momentum-space Faddeev formalism for including the screened proton-proton Coulomb force in three-nucleon calculations to the case where a three-nucleon force is also acting, and additionally formulate the approach for electron- and gamma-induced reactions on 3He; the resulting equations have the same structure as the Faddeev equations describing pd scattering without a three-nucleon force, demonstrating practical feasibility.

## Method

The authors start from the Faddeev equation for the T-operator in a partial-wave basis, separating states into those with low two-nucleon angular momentum (j ≤ jmax) where both nuclear and screened Coulomb forces act, and high partial waves (j > jmax) where only the screened Coulomb force acts. They project the Faddeev equations onto these two sets of states, eliminate the high-partial-wave components using a 3-dimensional screened pp Coulomb t-matrix obtained by numerical solution of a 3-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger equation, and derive a coupled set of integral equations in the low-partial-wave space only. The same elimination procedure is then applied to the Faddeev-type equations that include a three-nucleon force and to the equations describing electromagnetic reactions on 3He.

## Key numbers

- The screened pp Coulomb t-matrix is obtained by a numerical solution of a 3-dimensional Lippmann-Schwinger equation (no specific energy or screening radius given in the text).
- The partial-wave basis distinguishes states with total two-nucleon angular momentum j ≤ jmax (where nuclear and Coulomb forces act) from states with j > jmax (where only Coulomb acts); no specific value of jmax is provided in the text.
- The isospin coupling coefficients for the t-matrix in t=1 states are given as fractions: 1/3 t_np + 2/3 t_pp+c^R for T=1/2, and 2/3 t_np + 1/3 t_pp+c^R for T=3/2, with off-diagonal mixing coefficient sqrt(2)/3 (t_np - t_pp+c^R).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous approach for including the pp Coulomb force in momentum-space Faddeev calculations of elastic pd scattering and breakup (Witala et al., 2009; 2010) to include three-nucleon forces, and also formulates the method for electromagnetic reactions on 3He. The approach avoids the partial-wave decomposition difficulties associated with the long-range Coulomb force that arise in earlier screened-Coulomb treatments (Deltuva et al., 2005). The resulting formalism enables consistent calculations of pd scattering, breakup, and photon/electron-induced reactions on 3He with both Coulomb and three-nucleon forces included, using the same numerical tools developed for the pairwise-force-only case.
