# Occupation probabilities from quadrupole moments in the Sn region

> It is shown that a simple BCS model with a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction provides a consistent description of the measured quadrupole moments of a sequence of odd-mass Sn and Cd isotopes and allows the extraction of the neutron single-particle occupation probabilities.

## Key claim

A simple BCS model with a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, using a weak-coupling approximation, can consistently describe measured quadrupole moments of odd-mass Sn and Cd isotopes and extract neutron single-particle occupation probabilities, provided that a single strength parameter (constant across both isotope chains) is empirically determined.

## Method

The author used a spherical BCS model with a weak-coupling approximation to include quadrupole collectivity, where states in odd-even isotopes with single quasi-particle parentage are written as a sum of a one-quasi-particle state and a state coupled to the first collective 2+ state. The quadrupole-quadrupole interaction was taken as the residual interaction, and the quadrupole moment expression was derived to first order in the coupling coefficient C2, allowing extraction of the occupation function (u^2 - v^2) from experimental quadrupole moments and B(E2) values. The strength parameter was fixed at a constant value chosen to yield reasonable occupation values for the h11/2 states in the lightest and heaviest isotopes.

## Key numbers

- The quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strength was taken as a constant value of 0.3 x 10^-3 MeV fm^-4 for both isotope chains.
- For the h11/2 quasi-particle states in Sn isotopes, the total effective charge e_n,tot/e ranges from 1.2 to 2.0.
- For the h11/2 quasi-particle states in Cd isotopes, the total effective charge e_n,tot/e ranges from 2.5 to 9.5 near mid-shell.
- The correction to the first-order calculation from the quadrupole moment of the collective 2+ state is less than 6% in Cd isotopes and less than 2% in Sn isotopes.
- The coefficients |C2|^2 are always less than 0.03 for Sn isotopes and less than 0.15 for Cd isotopes.
- For Cd-107 (5/2+ state), the experimental quadrupole moment is 0.601(3) b and the extracted (u^2 - v^2) is -0.71(3).
- For Cd-111 (11/2- state), the experimental quadrupole moment is -0.747(4) b and the extracted (u^2 - v^2) is 0.50(2).
- For Sn-113 (11/2- state), the experimental quadrupole moment is -0.41(4) b and the extracted (u^2 - v^2) is 0.91(9).
- For Sn-117 (11/2- state), the experimental quadrupole moment is -0.42(5) b and the extracted (u^2 - v^2) is 0.99(12).

## Context

This work extends the observation by Yordanov (2013) that electric quadrupole moments of 11/2- states in Cd isotopes vary linearly with neutron number and can be described by sequential filling of the h11/2 orbital with a large neutron effective charge. The present model provides a more detailed theoretical framework that allows extraction of occupation probabilities for multiple orbitals across both Sn and Cd isotope chains, and shows that while the Sn isotopes behave similarly to the Yordanov (2013) model, the Cd isotopes have a more complicated N-dependence of occupation probabilities despite the linear N-dependence of the quadrupole moments. The results enable future experimental work on heavy Sn isotopes near the doubly closed shell Sn-132 to reduce error bars and test the model predictions.
