# Do short range correlations inhibit the appearance of the nuclear pasta?

> It is well known that strongly correlated neutron-proton pairs, the short-range correlations (SRC), can modify many of the nuclear properties. In this work we have introduced, for the first time, short range correlations in the calculation of the nuclear pasta phase at zero temperature and checked how they affect its size and internal structure. We have used two different parameterizations of relativistic models in a mean field approximation and the coexistence phase approximation as a first estimation of the effects. We have seen that for very asymmetric neutron-proton-electon matter, the pasta phase shrinks considerably as compared with the results without SRC and all internal structures vanish, except the simple spherically symmetric one, the droplets. Our results indicate a possible disappearance of these complicated structures as the temperature increases.

## Key claim

Short-range correlations (SRC) cause the nuclear pasta phase to shrink considerably for very asymmetric neutron-proton-electron matter at zero temperature, and all internal pasta structures except spherical droplets vanish; the authors state this as a "first estimation" using the coexistence phase approximation, noting that more robust calculations with self-consistent surface tension are needed.

## Method

The authors introduced SRC into relativistic mean-field models (IUFSU, FSU2R, NL3) by modifying the nucleon momentum distribution to include a high-momentum tail proportional to \(k^{-4}\), then reparameterized the models to reproduce the same bulk nuclear properties as the original versions. They computed the pasta phase at zero temperature using the coexistence phase approximation (CPA) with Gibbs conditions for neutron-proton-electron matter, comparing the free energies of homogeneous matter and five pasta geometries (droplets, rods, slabs, tubes, bubbles) to determine the ground state.

## Key numbers

- For IUFSU and FSU2R with SRC at proton fraction \(Y_p = 0.1\) (relevant to neutron star inner crust), only droplets survive; all other pasta geometries vanish.
- For IUFSU at \(Y_p = 0.35\), the tubes structure vanishes when SRC are included.
- The SRC parameters used are \(C_0 = 0.161\), \(C_1 = -0.25\), \(\phi_0 = 2.38\), and \(\phi_1 = -0.56\), ensuring 28% of nucleons in the high-momentum tail for symmetric nuclear matter and 1.5% for pure neutron matter.
- The IUFSU model has saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.155\) fm\(^{-3}\), binding energy \(-16.4\) MeV, symmetry energy \(J = 31.3\) MeV, and slope \(L_0 = 47.2\) MeV; FSU2R has \(\rho_0 = 0.150\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(J = 30.7\) MeV, \(L_0 = 46.9\) MeV; NL3 has \(\rho_0 = 0.148\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(J = 37.4\) MeV, \(L_0 = 118.32\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous pasta phase calculations (e.g., oldpasta, PRC82, PRC85, PhysRevC.105.025806) by including short-range correlations for the first time, which had been identified as a missing ingredient in all prior pasta studies. The results suggest that at low proton fractions, SRC may cause the disappearance of complex pasta structures, potentially simplifying transport property calculations in neutron star crusts (Yakovlev 2016, Pelicer 2022) and affecting neutrino diffusion signals from core-collapse supernovae (neutrino, latetime). The authors note that CPA underestimates pasta size compared to Thomas-Fermi approximations (oldpasta) and that future work should use self-consistent surface tension calculations and finite-temperature treatments.
