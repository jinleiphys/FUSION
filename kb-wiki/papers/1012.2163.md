# Isospin Symmetry Breaking in the Chiral Quark Model

> We discuss the isospin symmetry breaking (ISB) of the valence- and sea-quark distributions between the proton and the neutron in the framework of the chiral quark model. We assume that isospin symmetry breaking is the result of mass differences between isospin multiplets and then analyze the effects of isospin symmetry breaking on the Gottfried sum rule and the NuTeV anomaly. We show that, although both flavor asymmetry in the nucleon sea and the ISB between the proton and the neutron can lead to the violation of the Gottfried sum rule, the main contribution is from the flavor asymmetry in the framework of the chiral quark model. We also find that the correction to the NuTeV anomaly is in an opposite direction, so the NuTeV anomaly cannot be removed by isospin symmetry breaking in the chiral quark model. It is remarkable that our results of ISB for both valence- and sea-quark distributions are consistent with the Martin-Roberts-Stirling-Thorne parametrization of quark distributions.

## Key claim
The authors show that isospin symmetry breaking (ISB) arising from mass differences between isospin multiplets in the chiral quark model produces corrections to the NuTeV anomaly that are in the opposite direction needed to resolve it, so the NuTeV anomaly cannot be removed by ISB in this framework; the main contribution to the violation of the Gottfried sum rule comes from flavor asymmetry rather than ISB.

## Method
The authors compute ISB in the chiral quark model by assuming that all ISB originates from mass differences between isospin multiplets (u/d quarks, charged/neutral pions, charged/neutral kaons). They use constituent-quark-model distributions as bare inputs, then convolve these with splitting functions for fluctuations into Goldstone bosons (pions and kaons) to obtain dressed quark distributions for the proton and neutron. The ISB of valence- and sea-quark distributions is extracted by comparing proton and neutron distributions, and the resulting corrections to the Gottfried sum rule and the Paschos-Wolfenstein ratio (NuTeV anomaly) are evaluated.

## Key numbers
- The pseudoscalar decay constant is f = 93 MeV.
- The average constituent quark mass is (m_u + m_d)/2 = 330 MeV; two values for the u-d mass difference are used: delta m = 4 MeV and delta m = 8 MeV.
- Pion masses: m_pi+/- = 139.6 MeV, m_pi0 = 135 MeV; kaon masses: m_K+/- = 493.7 MeV, m_K0 = 497.6 MeV.
- The cutoff parameter for pions is Lambda_pi = 1500 MeV; for kaons, Lambda_K is varied from 900 to 1500 MeV.
- The ISB correction to the Paschos-Wolfenstein ratio, Delta R_PW^ISB, ranges from 0.0008 to 0.0019 depending on inputs (e.g., 0.0008 for delta m=4 MeV, Lambda_K=900 MeV; 0.0019 for delta m=8 MeV, Lambda_K=1500 MeV).
- The MRST parametrization gives a best-fit value kappa = -0.2 for valence ISB and k = 0.08 for sea ISB, with a 90% confidence range -0.8 <= kappa <= +0.65.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of ISB at the parton level by Ma (1991) and others, who first pointed out that both flavor asymmetry and ISB can violate the Gottfried sum rule. The results are compared to the MRST parametrization (Martin et al., 2003) and to calculations in the meson cloudy model by Cao and Signal (2000) and in the radiatively generated ISB approach by Gluck et al. (2005). The authors find that their ISB correction to the NuTeV anomaly has the same sign as in the baryon-meson fluctuation model of Ding et al. (2006) but is one to two orders of magnitude larger, and it differs in sign from the results of Londergan et al. (2003) and Gluck et al. (2005). The work enables more precise experimental and theoretical studies of ISB in parton distributions.
