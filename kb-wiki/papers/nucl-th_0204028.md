# Effects of Baryon-Meson Intermediate States on Baryon Masses

> The mass shifts of experimentally well-known baryons due to meson-baryon self-energy loops are calculated, and their impact on the observed splitting of the baryon spectrum is studied. Configuration-mixed wave functions adapted from a `relativized' model are used with the 3P0 model to provide predictions for the strength and analytical momentum dependence of the strong vertices. Intermediate states include all the lightest pseudoscalar and vector mesons and corresponding baryons required to provide a complete set of spin-flavor symmetry related baryon-meson states. The sum over intermediate-state baryons is extended to include the second (N=3) band of negative-parity excited states, to provide the most complete calculation of its kind to date. It is found that with reduced-strength one-gluon-exchange interactions between the quarks, roughly half of the splitting between the nucleon and Delta ground states arises from loop effects. The effects of such loops on the spectrum of negative-parity excited states are also studied, and it is found that the resulting splittings are sensitive to configuration mixing caused by the residual interactions. With the extensive set of intermediate baryon-meson states used, a reasonable correspondence is found between model masses and the bare masses required to fit the masses of the states extracted from data analyses.

## Key claim
Roughly half of the nucleon-Delta ground state splitting arises from baryon-meson loop effects, but only when the one-gluon-exchange interactions between quarks are assigned a reduced strength; the result is conditional on using configuration-mixed wave functions and including intermediate baryon states up to the second (N=3) band of negative-parity excitations.

## Method
The author calculates baryon self-energy shifts from meson-baryon loop diagrams using the 3P0 model for strong vertices, with configuration-mixed wave functions adapted from a relativized quark model. Intermediate states include all lightest pseudoscalar and vector mesons paired with the corresponding baryons required for a complete set of spin-flavor symmetry related states, extending the sum over intermediate baryons to include the N=3 band of negative-parity excited states. The loop integrals incorporate physical masses for intermediate hadrons to set thresholds, and the residual interquark interactions are adjusted to account for the loop-induced splittings.

## Key numbers
- Roughly half of the splitting between the nucleon and Delta ground states arises from loop effects when reduced-strength one-gluon-exchange interactions are used.
- The sum over intermediate baryon states is extended to include the second (N=3) band of negative-parity excited states, providing the most complete calculation of its kind to date.
- In the symmetry limit where intermediate states are restricted to ground states and SU(3)_f symmetry is unbroken, the sum of spin-flavor weights for all baryons B equals 48, meaning no mass splittings are generated by loop effects in that limit.

## Context
This work extends the earlier calculations of Zenczykowski (1986), who found that at least two thirds of observed splittings arise from hadronic loops using only ground-state intermediate baryons, and of Brack and Bhaduri (1987), who showed that including excited intermediate baryons up to the N=3 band is necessary for convergence of the nucleon-Delta mass difference but found poor convergence for negative-parity excited states. The present calculation resolves that convergence issue by including both the full set of spin-flavor related intermediate states and the N=3 band of excited baryons, and demonstrates that configuration mixing in the wave functions is crucial for describing splittings of negative-parity excited states. This enables a self-consistent treatment where the residual interquark interactions can be reduced in strength, bringing model predictions into reasonable correspondence with the bare masses extracted from experimental data analyses.
