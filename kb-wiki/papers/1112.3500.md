# Heavy quark production in photon - Pomeron interactions at hadronic colliders

> The diffractive heavy quark cross sections are estimated considering photon-Pomeron ($γ\pom$) interactions in hadron - hadron at RHIC, Tevatron, and CERN LHC energies. We assume the validity of the hard diffractive factorization and calculate the charm and bottom total cross sections and rapidity distributions using the diffractive parton distribution functions of the Pomeron obtained by the H1 Collaboration at DESY-HERA. Moreover, we compare our predictions with those obtained using the dipole model. We verify that this process is a good test of the different mechanisms for diffractive heavy quark production at hadron - hadron colliders.

## Key claim

The authors estimate that diffractive heavy quark photoproduction in hadron-hadron collisions, calculated using the resolved Pomeron model with diffractive parton distribution functions from H1, can serve as a test to discriminate between different theoretical descriptions of the Pomeron, provided the soft absorption corrections are expected to be very small in photon-Pomeron interactions.

## Method

The authors calculate the total cross sections and rapidity distributions for diffractive charm and bottom photoproduction in proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions at RHIC, Tevatron, and LHC energies. They assume the validity of hard diffractive factorization, using the Pomeron flux factor and gluon distribution obtained by the H1 Collaboration from diffractive deep inelastic scattering data at HERA, and compute the photon-gluon fusion cross section at leading order. The cross sections are obtained by folding the equivalent photon flux of the hadron projectile with the diffractive photoproduction cross section on the target hadron.

## Key numbers

- Charm quark mass assumed: \(m_c = 1.5\) GeV; bottom quark mass assumed: \(m_b = 4.5\) GeV
- Center-of-mass energies considered: \(\sqrt{S_{NN}} = 0.2\) TeV (RHIC), 1.96 TeV (Tevatron), 7 and 14 TeV (LHC pp), and 8.8 TeV (LHC pPb)
- Maximum photon-proton center-of-mass energy at LHC: \(W_{\gamma p} \approx 8390\) GeV for pp collisions and 1500 GeV for pPb collisions, compared to about 200 GeV at HERA

## Context

This work extends the study of diffractive heavy quark production in hadronic collisions, previously calculated for Pomeron-hadron and Pomeron-Pomeron interactions by Goncalves et al. (2006-2007) and for exclusive double diffractive production by Antoni et al. (2010). The authors compare their resolved Pomeron model predictions with those from the dipole approach used by Kopeliovich (2005), which suggested a breakdown of factorization in diffractive hadronic collisions. The results enable future experimental tests at RHIC, Tevatron, and LHC to discriminate between the resolved and perturbative models of the Pomeron.
