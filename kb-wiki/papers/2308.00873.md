# Variational and parquet-diagram calculations for neutron matter. V. Triplet pairing

> We apply a large-scale summation of Feynman diagrams, including the class of parquet-diagrams {\em plus} important contributions outside the parquet class, for calculating effective pairing interactions and subsequently the superfluid gap in P-wave pairing in neutron matter. We employ realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions of the $v_8$ type and perform calculations up to a Fermi momentum of $1.8\,$fm$^{-1}$. We find that many-body correlations lead to a strong reduction of the spin-orbit interaction, and, therefore, to an almost complete suppression of the $^3$P$_2$ and $^3$P$_2$-$^3$F$_2$ gaps. We also find pairing in $^3$P$_0$ states; the strength of the pairing gap depends sensitively on the potential model employed. Our results for triplet pairing are relevant for assessing superfluidity in neutron star interiors, whose presence can affect the cooling of neutron stars.

## Key claim

Many-body correlations, particularly the strong reduction of the spin-orbit interaction, lead to an almost complete suppression of the $^3$P$_2$ and $^3$P$_2$-$^3$F$_2$ pairing gaps in neutron matter, while pairing in $^3$P$_0$ states may persist but with a strength that depends sensitively on the choice of nucleon-nucleon potential model.

## Method

The authors performed a large-scale summation of Feynman diagrams that includes the parquet class (self-consistent ring and ladder diagrams) plus additional "twisted chain" contributions outside the parquet class, which become important when interactions in spin-singlet and spin-triplet channels differ significantly. They employed realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions of the $v_8$ type (Reid 68, Reid 93 in two operator representations, and Argonne $v_8$) and solved the resulting gap equations for triplet P-wave pairing in neutron matter up to a Fermi momentum of $1.8$ fm$^{-1}$.

## Key numbers

- Calculations were performed up to a Fermi momentum of $1.8$ fm$^{-1}$.
- The S-wave pairing gap in neutron matter reaches a maximum of roughly 2 MeV (within about 1 MeV) at a Fermi momentum somewhat below 1 fm$^{-1}$, as cited from prior work.
- The $^3$P$_2$ and $^3$P$_2$-$^3$F$_2$ gaps are found to be almost completely suppressed due to many-body correlations reducing the spin-orbit interaction.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous series of papers on variational and parquet-diagram calculations for neutron matter (Krotscheck et al., 2019-2022), which addressed the equation of state, S-wave pairing, and spin-orbit forces. It directly addresses the long-standing uncertainty about whether triplet P-wave pairing gaps develop in neutron matter, a question of astrophysical significance because such pairing would affect neutron star cooling curves (Page and Reddy, 2006; Yakovlev and Haensel, 2003). Prior microscopic calculations at the mean-field level (Takatsuka and Tamagaki, 1993; Elgaroy et al., 1996) predicted non-vanishing triplet gaps, but studies including short-range correlations (PhysRevC.94.025802) and three-nucleon interactions (Yuan, 2007) suggested reduction or elimination of these gaps. The present work enables more reliable predictions for superfluidity in neutron star interiors by incorporating polarization and screening effects that were previously scarce in the triplet channel.
