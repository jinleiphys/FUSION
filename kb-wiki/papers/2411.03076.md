# Potential signature of new magicity from universal aspects of nuclear charge radii

> Shell quenching phenomena in nuclear charge radii are typically observed at the well-established neutron magic numbers. However, the recent discovery of potential new magic numbers at the neutron numbers $N = 32$ and $N = 34$ has sparked renewed interest in this mass region. This work further inspects into the charge radii of nuclei around the $N = 28$ shell closure using the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model. We incorporate meson exchange and point-coupling effective nucleon-nucleon interactions alongside the Bogoliubov transformation for pairing corrections. To accurately capture the odd-even staggering and shell closure effects observed in charge radii, neutron-proton correlations around Fermi surface are explicitly considered. The charge radii of Ca and Ni isotopes are used to test the theoretical model and show an improvement with neutron-proton pairing corrections, in particular for neutron-rich isotopes. Our calculations reveal a inverted parabolic-like trend in the charge radii along the $N = 28$ isotones for proton numbers $Z$ between 20 and 28. Additionally, the shell closure effect of $Z = 28$ persists across the $N = 28$, 30, 32, and 34 isotonic chains, albeit with a gradual weakening trend. Notably, the significantly abrupt changes in charge radii are observed across $Z = 22$ along both the $N = 32$ and $N = 34$ isotonic chains. This kink at $Z = 22$ comes from the sudden decrease of the neuron-proton correlation around Fermi surfaces across $Z = 22$ for $N = 30$, 32, and 34 isotones, and might provide a signature for identifying the emergence of neutron magic numbers $N = 32$ and 34. Furthermore, the calculated charge radii for these isotonic chains ($N = 28$, 30, 32, and 34) can serve as reliable guidelines for future experimental measurements.

## Key claim

The abrupt changes in charge radii observed across proton number Z=22 along the N=32 and N=34 isotonic chains, arising from a sudden decrease in neutron-proton correlations around the Fermi surface, might provide a signature for identifying the emergence of neutron magic numbers N=32 and N=34, but the authors emphasize this remains "guesswork" requiring further theoretical and experimental verification.

## Method

The authors employed the multidimensionally-constrained relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (MDC-RHB) model with both meson-exchange (NL3) and point-coupling (PC-PK1) effective nucleon-nucleon interactions, using a separable pairing force of finite range with pairing strength G=728 MeV fm³ and effective range a=0.644 fm. Neutron-proton correlations around the Fermi surface were incorporated into the charge radius formula via a term ΔD=|D_n-D_p|, where D_n and D_p sum the products of occupation probabilities u_k and v_k for quasi-particle orbitals within 20 MeV of the Fermi energy. Calculations were restricted to axial and reflection symmetry, considering only quadrupole deformation β₂₀.

## Key numbers

- Pairing strength G = 728 MeV fm³ and effective range a = 0.644 fm for the separable pairing force
- Normalization constant a₀ = 0.561 and δ = 0.355 for odd-odd nuclei (δ = 0.000 for even-even, odd-even, and even-odd nuclei) in the charge radius formula
- Quasi-particle levels satisfying |E_k - λ| < 20 MeV are summed in the neutron-proton correlation term ΔD
- Deformation parameters β₂₀ ≈ 0.20 for both Z=22 and Z=23 isotopes along the N=30, 32, and 34 isotonic chains
- Charge radius increase from Z=22 to Z=23 is about 0.1 fm in the RHB* model along N=30, N=32, and N=34 isotonic chains
- Predicted charge radii for numerous nuclei along N=28, 30, 32, and 34 isotonic chains are tabulated, e.g., for N=28 isotones: Ti(Z=22) = 3.5798 fm (NL3) and 3.5918 fm (PC-PK1); for N=32 isotones: Ti(Z=22) = 3.6355 fm (NL3) and 3.6280 fm (PC-PK1)

## Context

This work extends the approach of An (2024) which used the RMF(BCS)* model with neutron-proton correlations, but improves upon it by using the more sophisticated MDC-RHB model with Bogoliubov transformation for pairing corrections, which is more suitable for nuclei far from the beta-stability line than the BCS approximation. The results contradict the findings of Kortelainen (2022) and An (2024) that charge radii between N=28 and N=40 exhibit a universal pattern independent of proton number along isotopic chains, instead showing that N=32 and N=34 magicity can be identified through isotonic chains. The calculations provide predicted charge radii for N=28, 30, 32, and 34 isotones as guidelines for future experimental measurements, particularly for ⁵²,⁵⁴,⁵⁶Ti and ⁵³,⁵⁵,⁵⁷V where no experimental charge radii currently exist.
