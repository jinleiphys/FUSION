# Impact of pion tensor force on alpha clustering in $^{20}$Ne

> The nuclear clustering, as a quantum phase transition phenomenon governed by strong interactions, exhibits characteristics that are highly sensitive to the specific features of nuclear forces. Here, we examine how nuclear deformation and tensor forces influence $α$-cluster formation in light nuclei. The axially deformed relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model is utilized to investigate the clustering structure of the $^{20}$Ne nucleus, at both the ground state and the excited state with a superdeformed prolate. The nuclear binding energies and the canonical single particle levels are obtained at different quadruple deformation, and the role of tensor force embedded in the Fock diagram of $π$-pseudovector ($π$-PV) coupling is revealed. It is shown that the level branches from the degenerated spherical orbits at the deformed prolate case are enlarged due to the extra contribution from pion-exchanged tensor force. Correspondingly, the excitation energy in this superdeformed prolate state is reduced due to the noncentral tensor interaction, leading to a predicted value which is much closer to the referred threshold for the $2α$ decay mode of $^{20}$Ne. Possible $α$-clustering configurations in $^{20}$Ne are then characterized by examining the nucleonic localization function. Although the contribution to the ground state is relatively small, the density profile and nucleonic localization are significantly changed by the pion tensor force for the superdeformed prolate excited state, as further evidenced by characterising the level mixing in the spherical basis components. The results reveal the extra role of the tensor force, correlated to the evolved single-particle levels with nuclear deformation, in the formation and stability of nuclear clustering.

## Key claim

The pion tensor force, embedded in the Fock diagram of π-pseudovector (π-PV) coupling, significantly enhances α-cluster formation and stability in the superdeformed prolate excited state of ²⁰Ne, but its contribution to the ground state is relatively small. The tensor force reduces the excitation energy of the superdeformed state, bringing it closer to the 2α decay threshold.

## Method

The axially deformed relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (D-RHFB) model was used to calculate binding energies, canonical single-particle levels, and density distributions of ²⁰Ne at both the ground state and a superdeformed prolate excited state. Calculations were performed with four Lagrangians (PKO1, PKO2, PKO3, PKDD) to isolate the effects of π-PV coupling, and the tensor force component was further isolated by removing it from the nucleon self-energy in PKO1. α-clustering configurations were characterized using the nucleonic localization function (NLF) and the F factor, which measures density depletion at cluster boundaries.

## Key numbers

- Ground state quadrupole deformation of ²⁰Ne: β ≈ 0.55
- Excited state secondary minimum at β ≈ 2.05
- Binding energy at excited state: PKO1 gives 144.4 MeV, PKDD gives 131.7 MeV (12.7 MeV difference)
- PKO1 binding energy at excited state approaches the 2α-cluster decay threshold of 11.89 MeV
- PKO1 and PKDD show density accumulations beyond |z| > 3 fm with integrated densities exceeding 4 fm⁻³
- Density peak for PKO1 at z ≈ 4.0 fm; for PKDD and PKO1 without tensor force at z ≈ 1.5 fm
- NLF value C_{qσ} = 0.99 for all Lagrangians in both ground and excited states
- F factor at excited state for PKO1: F(R_α)_∥ = 0.78, F(R_α)_⊥ = 0.85
- F factor at excited state for PKO1 without tensor force: F(R_α)_∥ = 0.76, F(R_α)_⊥ = 0.84
- In excited state, 1/2₁⁻ orbital has ≈80% p-state and ≈15% f-state components; 1/2₂⁻ has ≈60% p and ≈30% f components

## Context

This work extends covariant density functional theory studies of α-clustering in ²⁰Ne, previously investigated using the generator coordinate method (Zhou 2023), antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (PhysRevC.69.044319), no-core shell model (PhysRevC.102.044608), and relativistic mean field theory (Ebran 2012, Zhou 2016). The D-RHFB model includes Fock terms that naturally incorporate tensor forces through π-PV coupling, which are absent in RMF approaches like PKDD. The results demonstrate that the tensor force enhances cluster compactness and stability by inducing sd/pf orbital mixing in deformed single-particle levels, and by providing long-range attraction that balances Coulomb repulsion between clusters. The study is limited by the omission of beyond-mean-field corrections and restriction to axially symmetric shapes, which precludes investigation of octupole-deformed systems such as the α+¹⁶O configuration.
