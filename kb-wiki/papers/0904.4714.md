# Modeling Nuclear Pasta and the Transition to Uniform Nuclear Matter with the 3D Skyrme-Hartree-Fock Method at Finite Temperature I: Core-Collapse Supernovae

> The first results of a new three-dimensional, finite temperature Skyrme-Hartree-Fock+BCS study of the properties of inhomogeneous nuclear matter at densities and temperatures leading to the transition to uniform nuclear matter are presented. Calculations are carried out in a cubic box representing a unit cell of the locally periodic structure of the matter. A constraint is placed on the two independent components of the quadrupole moment of the neutron density in order to investigate the dependence of the total energy-density of matter on the geometry of the nuclear structure in the unit cell. This approach allows self-consistent modeling of effects such as (i) neutron drip, resulting in a neutron gas external to the nuclear structure, (ii) shell effects of bound and unbound nucleons, (iii) the variety of exotic nuclear shapes that emerge, collectively termed `nuclear pasta' and (iv) the dissolution of these structures into uniform nuclear matter as density and/or temperature increase. In part I of this work the calculation of the properties of inhomogeneous nuclear matter in the core collapse of massive stars is reported. Calculations are performed at baryon number densities of $n_{\rm b}$ = 0.04 - 0.12 fm$^{\rm -3}$, a proton fraction of $y_{\rm p}=0.3$ and temperatures in the range 0 - 7.5 MeV. A wide variety of nuclear shapes are shown to emerge. It is suggested that thermodynamical properties change smoothly in the pasta regime up to the transition to uniform matter; at that transition, thermodynamic properties of the matter vary discontinuously.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first comprehensive three-dimensional, finite-temperature Skyrme-Hartree-Fock+BCS study of inhomogeneous nuclear matter near the transition to uniform matter, but the authors hedge that the results should be seen as a reference set because the spin-orbit force was omitted to simplify the computational setup and test the procedure.

## Method

The authors solved the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock equations in coordinate space within a single cubic unit cell with periodic boundary conditions, using the SkM* Skyrme parameterization (with some comparison to Sly4). A constraint was imposed on the two independent components of the quadrupole moment of the neutron density to systematically survey the energy of different nuclear shapes, and BCS pairing correlations were added at zero temperature while Fermi-Dirac occupation probabilities were used at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

- Baryon number densities explored: \(n_{\rm b} = 0.04\) to \(0.12\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Proton fraction fixed at \(y_{\rm p} = 0.3\)
- Temperatures studied: 0 to 7.5 MeV
- Grid spacing of 1.0 fm guaranteed accuracy of 1 part in \(10^4\) in energy for temperatures up to 5 MeV
- Convergence criterion: total variance in single particle energies less than 1.0 keV\(^2\), ensuring total energy convergence to less than 1 part in \(10^8\)
- Free energy densities of two configurations in doubled versus single cells agreed to within 1 part in \(10^4\)

## Context

This work extends the 1D Hartree-Fock calculations of Bonche and Vautherin (1981, 1982), which were restricted to spherical symmetry and could not treat the transition to uniform matter, and the 3D Hartree-Fock studies of Magierski and Heenen (2002) and Gögelein and Muther (2007), which were limited to neutron star conditions or did not constrain both quadrupole moments. The approach enables self-consistent calculation of the equation of state from neutron drip density through the pasta regime to uniform matter, avoiding artificial discontinuities from matching separate EoSs, and will be extended to beta-equilibrium neutron star matter in part II and to band structure calculations in part III.
