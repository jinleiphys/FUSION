# Lattice QCD Study of the Nucleon-Charmonium Interaction

> The $J/ψ$-nucleon interaction is studied by lattice QCD calculations. At the leading order of the derivative expansion, the interaction consists of four terms: the central, the spin-spin, and two types of tensor forces. We determine these spin-dependent forces quantitatively by using the time-dependent HAL QCD method. We find that the spin-spin force is the main cause of the hyperfine splitting between the $J=1/2$ and the $J=3/2$ states, while the two tensor forces have much smaller effects on the S-wave scattering processes.

## Key claim
The spin-spin force is the main cause of the hyperfine splitting between the J=1/2 and J=3/2 states in the J/psi-nucleon system, while the two tensor forces have much smaller effects on S-wave scattering processes; however, the tensor forces contain larger ambiguity due to small D-wave signals in the simulation setup.

## Method
The authors employed the time-dependent HAL QCD method on 2+1 flavor QCD gauge configurations with a 32^3 x 64 lattice, using the renormalization group improved gauge action and non-perturbatively O(a) improved Wilson quark action. They extracted the J/psi-nucleon potential at leading order of the derivative expansion, which consists of central, spin-spin, and two tensor force terms, by solving the time-dependent Schrodinger-like equation for the normalized four-point correlation function. The four spin-dependent forces were determined from wave functions for four of the five partial waves with total angular momentum J=1/2 or 3/2.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.0907(13) fm, spatial volume: L^3 = (2.9 fm)^3
- Hadron masses: m_pi = 700(2) MeV, m_N = 1585(16) MeV, m_psi = 3139(12) MeV
- Scattering length for J/psi N J=1/2: a = 0.656(71) fm
- Effective range for J/psi N J=1/2: r = 1.105(16) fm
- Scattering length for J/psi N J=3/2: a = 0.380(48) fm
- Effective range for J/psi N J=3/2: r = 1.476(39) fm
- Scattering length for eta_c N: a = 0.246(26) fm
- Effective range for eta_c N: r = 1.703(45) fm

## Context
This work extends the HAL QCD collaboration's approach (HAL1, HAL2) to calculate the J/psi-nucleon potential, following the authors' previous report (Sugiura_Lattice) which showed the need for the time-dependent method. The study is motivated by the LHCb collaboration's discovery of P_c(4380) and P_c(4450) pentaquark candidates in the J/psi p invariant mass distribution, and the possibility of P_c(4450) as a deeply bound state of psi(2S) and proton discussed in the QCD van der Waals framework (Polyakov1, Polyakov2). The results enable quantitative checks of the pentaquark interpretation and searches for nucleus-bound J/psi states (Yokota), while providing the first quantitative determination of spin-dependent forces in the charmonium-nucleon interaction.
