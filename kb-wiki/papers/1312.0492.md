# Chiral condensate and the structure of hadrons

> A model of hadron masses based on the quark structure of hadrons combined with effects of chiral dynamics is used to calculate the 2+1 flavour chiral condensate in the hadron resonance gas framework. Results are discussed in the context of recent lattice QCD data. Improvements of the dynamical models of hadron structure are suggested with the aim to estimate the strange sigma term of the nucleon.

## Key claim

A hadron resonance gas model using constituent quark mass formulas that incorporate chiral dynamics can reproduce lattice QCD results for the chiral condensate ratio Δ_{q,s}(T) up to temperatures of about 140-150 MeV, which is slightly below the pseudocritical temperature of about 155 MeV.

## Method

The authors compute the 2+1 flavour chiral condensate in the hadron resonance gas framework using a partition function that sums over all light and strange hadrons up to a mass of about 2 GeV. The hadron masses are determined from constituent quark mass formulas where baryon masses scale as (3-N_s)M_q + N_sM_s + κ_B and meson masses as (2-N_s)M_q + N_sM_s + κ_M, with dynamical quark masses obtained from a gap equation using a local heavy-gluon propagator model with an infrared cutoff for confinement. The resulting condensate ratio Δ_{q,s}(T) is compared to lattice QCD data from the Wuppertal-Budapest and hot QCD collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Dynamical quark sigma terms at physical current quark masses: σ_q = 7.65 MeV for up and down quarks, σ_s = 121.16 MeV for the strange quark
- Resulting nucleon sigma term: σ_N = 22.95 MeV
- Infrared cutoff in the gap equation: Λ_ir = 0.24 GeV; ultraviolet cutoff: Λ_uv = 0.905 GeV
- Dynamical gluon energy scale: m_G = 0.8 GeV; fitted coupling: α_IR = 0.9π
- The pseudocritical temperature from lattice data: T_c ∼ 155 MeV
- The baryon octet Gell-Mann-Okubo relation is satisfied with an accuracy of less than 5%

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark picture approach of Jankowski (2012) by incorporating chiral dynamics to compute the temperature dependence of the chiral condensate, and compares with lattice QCD results from Borsanyi (2010) and Bazavov (2013). The results enable improved estimates of the strange sigma term of the nucleon through potential model calculations of virtual quark loop effects, and connect to a model by Blaschke (2011) for freeze-out phenomena in heavy ion collisions based on Mott-Anderson localization of hadron wave functions driven by chiral dynamics.
