# What can we learn from the decay of $ N_X(1625)$ in molecule picture?

> Considering two molecular state assumptions, i.e. S-wave $\barΛ-K^-$ and S-wave $\barΣ^0-K^-$ molecular states, we study the possible decays of $\bar N_X(1625)$ that include $\bar N_X(1625)\to K^{-}\barΛ, π^{0}\bar{p}, η\bar{p}, π^{-}\bar{n}$. Our results indicate: (1) if $\bar N_{X}(1625)$ is $\barΛ-K^-$ molecular state, $K^{-}\barΛ$ is the main decay modes of $\bar N_{X}(1625)$, and the branching ratios of the rest decay modes are tiny; (2) if $\bar N_{X}(1625)$ is $\barΣ^0-K^-$ molecular state, the branching ratio of $\bar N_{X}(1625)\to K^{-}\barΛ$ is one or two order smaller than that of $\bar N_{X}(1625)\to π^{0}\bar{p}, η\bar{p}, π^{-}\bar{n}$. Thus the search for $\bar N_X(1625)\to π^{0}\bar{p}, η\bar{p}, π^{-}\bar{n}$ will be helpful to shed light on the nature of $\bar N_X(1625)$.

## Key claim

The paper tests whether the $\bar N_X(1625)$ enhancement observed by BES is a S-wave $\bar{\Lambda}-K^-$ or $\bar{\Sigma}^0-K^-$ molecular state, concluding that the decay pattern into $\pi^{0}\bar{p}, \eta\bar{p}, \pi^{-}\bar{n}$ versus $K^{-}\bar{\Lambda}$ can distinguish the two hypotheses, but that neither pure molecular picture is fully consistent with all experimental constraints.

## Method

The authors computed decay widths for $\bar N_X(1625)$ under two molecular assumptions using effective Lagrangians for meson-baryon interactions (PPV, BBP, BBV couplings) with SU(3) symmetry, including final-state interaction diagrams and a monopole form factor with a phenomenological parameter $\alpha$. For the $\bar{\Sigma}^0-K^-$ case, they incorporated $\Lambda-\Sigma^0$ mixing via a mixing coupling constant $\theta=0.5\pm 0.1$ MeV from QCD sum rules, and evaluated triangle-loop integrals for off-shell intermediate states.

## Key numbers

* Mass and width of $\bar N_X(1625)$ from BES: $m=1625^{+5+13}_{-7-23}$ MeV, $\Gamma=43^{+10+28}_{-7\;\;-11}$ MeV.
* Production rate: $B[J/\psi\to p \bar{N}_{X}(1625)]\cdot B[\bar{N}_{X}(1625)\to K^{-}\bar{\Lambda}] = 9.14^{+1.30+4.24}_{-1.25 -8.28}\times 10^{-5}$.
* For $\bar{\Lambda}-K^-$ molecule with $\alpha=1.5$: $\Gamma(\pi^{0}\bar{p})/\Gamma(K^{-}\bar{\Lambda}) = 1\times 10^{-4}$, $\Gamma(\eta\bar{p})/\Gamma(K^{-}\bar{\Lambda}) = 5\times10^{-7}$, $\Gamma(\pi^{-}\bar{n})/\Gamma(K^{-}\bar{\Lambda}) = 2\times 10^{-4}$.
* For $\bar{\Sigma}^0-K^-$ molecule with $\alpha=1.5$: $\Gamma(\pi^{0}\bar{p})/\Gamma(K^{-}\bar{\Lambda}) = 9$, $\Gamma(\eta\bar{p})/\Gamma(K^{-}\bar{\Lambda}) = 70$, $\Gamma(\pi^{-}\bar{n})/\Gamma(K^{-}\bar{\Lambda}) = 18$.
* Estimated branching ratios in $J/\psi$ decay for $\bar{\Sigma}^0-K^-$ case: $B[J/\psi\to p(\pi^{0}\bar{p})] \sim 1\times 10^{-3}$, $B[J/\psi\to p(\eta\bar{p})] \sim 7\times 10^{-3}$, $B[J/\psi\to p(\pi^{-}\bar{n})] \sim 2\times 10^{-3}$.
* Estimated branching ratios in $J/\psi$ decay for $\bar{\Lambda}-K^-$ case: $B[J/\psi\to p(\pi^{0}\bar{p})] = 1\times 10^{-8} \sim 3\times 10^{-8}$, $B[J/\psi\to p(\eta\bar{p})] = 4\times 10^{-11}\sim 2\times 10^{-10}$, $B[J/\psi\to p(\pi^{-}\bar{n})] = 2\times 10^{-8} \sim 5\times 10^{-8}$.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of $\bar N_X(1625)$: Zhang (2006) studied S-wave $\Lambda K$ and $\Sigma K$ systems in the chiral SU(3) quark model and found a $\Sigma K$ quasibound state with binding energy about 17 MeV; Liu and Zou (2006) suggested the enhancement comes from strong coupling between $N^*(1535)$ and $K\Lambda$. The present paper provides the first dedicated decay calculations for the molecular hypotheses, enabling experimental tests via searches for $\bar N_X(1625)\to \pi^{0}\bar{p}, \eta\bar{p}, \pi^{-}\bar{n}$ at BESIII and HIRFL-CSR. The authors note that the pure molecular picture struggles to explain $N_X(1625)$, and suggest that the enhancement may be related to the established $N^*(1535)$ and $N^*(1650)$ resonances.
