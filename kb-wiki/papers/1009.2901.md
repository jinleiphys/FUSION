# Computing singularities of perturbation series

> Many properties of current \emph{ab initio} approaches to the quantum many-body problem, both perturbational or otherwise, are related to the singularity structure of Rayleigh--Schrödinger perturbation theory. A numerical procedure is presented that in principle computes the complete set of singularities, including the dominant singularity which limits the radius of convergence. The method approximates the singularities as eigenvalues of a certain generalized eigenvalue equation which is solved using iterative techniques. It relies on computation of the action of the perturbed Hamiltonian on a vector, and does not rely on the terms in the perturbation series. Some illustrative model problems are studied, including a Helium-like model with $δ$-function interactions for which Møller--Plesset perturbation theory is considered and the radius of convergence found.

## Key claim

The paper presents a numerical method that can compute the complete set of singularities (branch points) of Rayleigh–Schrödinger perturbation series by solving a generalized eigenvalue problem, without requiring the perturbation series terms themselves; the method relies only on computing the action of the perturbed Hamiltonian on a vector, and the dominant singularity (which limits the radius of convergence) is identified by tracking eigenvalue branches from candidate branch points back to the origin.

## Method

The singularities of the eigenvalue maps \(E_n(\lambda)\) for \(H(\lambda) = H_0 + \lambda V\) are characterized as points where two eigenvalues coalesce, which are approximated by solving a generalized eigenvalue problem \(\lambda \Delta_0(\varepsilon) v = \Delta_1(\varepsilon) v\) where \(\varepsilon\) is a small regularization parameter. The matrix-vector products required for iterative solution via the Arnoldi method are reformulated as Sylvester equations, computable in \(\mathcal{O}(N^3)\) operations (or \(\mathcal{O}(N^2)\) when \(H_0\) is diagonal). The dominant branch point is found by sorting candidate critical values by magnitude and following eigenvalue paths along straight lines from each candidate to the origin until a path is found where one branch terminates at the unperturbed ground state energy.

## Key numbers

* For the harmonic oscillator with a \(\delta\)-function potential, the radius of convergence of the Rayleigh–Schrödinger series is estimated to be \(R = 2\) in the complete basis limit (all \(|\lambda| < 2\)).
* For the three-electron quantum wire model, the radius of convergence approaches infinity linearly with the number of oscillator shells \(M\) as the discretization is refined.
* For the helium-like model with \(\delta\)-function interactions and nuclear charge \(Z = 1.38\), the Møller–Plesset perturbation series radius of convergence was determined (specific numerical value not given in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior theoretical result (Jarlebring 2010) that double eigenvalues of parameter-dependent matrices can be approximated by a two-parameter eigenvalue problem, adapting it specifically to the structure of quantum many-body Hamiltonians. The method contrasts with traditional approaches that estimate singularities from perturbation series terms using Darboux's theorem (Pearce 1978, Sergeev 2006) or from expensive parameter sweeps to locate avoided crossings (Helgaker 2002, Herman 2009). The technique enables systematic determination of the radius of convergence for perturbation series in ab initio many-body calculations, which is relevant for understanding the performance of methods like coupled cluster theory and Padé approximants (Goodson 2000, Roth 2010).
