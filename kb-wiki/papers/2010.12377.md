# Recent development of hydrodynamic modeling in heavy-ion collisions

> We present a concise review of the recent development of relativistic hydrodynamics and its applications to heavy-ion collisions. Theoretical progress on the extended formulation of hydrodynamics towards out-of-equilibrium systems is addressed, emphasizing the so-called attractor solution. On the other hand, recent phenomenological improvements in the hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions with respect to the ongoing Beam Energy Scan program, the quantitative characterization of transport coefficients in the three-dimensionally expanding quark-gluon plasma, the fluid description of small colliding systems, and some other interdisciplinary connections are discussed.

## Key claim

This review argues that the applicability of relativistic hydrodynamics can be extended far beyond local thermal equilibrium through the concept of "attractor solutions," which provide a universal description of system evolution even when the traditional gradient expansion fails, though this attractor behavior has been demonstrated primarily in highly symmetric expanding systems such as Bjorken flow and Gubser flow.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical formulation of out-of-equilibrium hydrodynamics, starting from second-order viscous hydrodynamics (Israel-Stewart and BRSSS formulations) and extending to systems with large local gradients quantified by the Knudsen number. They analyze the attractor solution through fixed-point analysis of the nonlinear differential equations governing Bjorken flow, and discuss alternative approaches from kinetic theory using moments of the phase-space distribution function. The review also covers phenomenological applications of (3+1)D hydrodynamics coupled with hadronic transport models to heavy-ion collisions at intermediate and high energies.

## Key numbers

* Specific shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma has been constrained to values very close to the theoretical lower bound of \(\hbar/4\pi k_B\), suggesting a strongly coupled medium
* The QGP droplet size varies from \(O(10)\) fm in gold-gold collisions at RHIC or lead-lead collisions at the LHC to \(O(1)\) fm in small colliding systems such as proton-lead or proton-proton collisions
* For a strongly coupled QGP medium, hydrodynamization scales inversely to temperature as \(\tau_0 \sim 1/T\); for a weakly coupled system, the onset of hydrodynamics relates to the strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s\) and saturation scale \(Q_s\) as \(\tau_0 \sim \alpha_s^x Q_s^{-1}\) with exponent \(x\) being a constant negative number
* In Bjorken flow, the ideal hydrodynamic evolution gives energy density scaling as \(\epsilon \sim \tau^{-4/3}\), and the Knudsen number satisfies \(\Kn^{-1} \sim \tau/\tau_\pi\)
* For a conformal system from kinetic theory, the transport coefficients are parameterized as \(C_\eta = 1/(4\pi)\), \(C_\tau = 5\), and \(C_{\lambda_1} = (5/7)C_\eta C_\tau\)

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework of relativistic hydrodynamics developed by Israel (1979), Baier et al. (2007), and Denicol et al. (2012), and addresses the "unreasonable effectiveness" of hydrodynamics in small colliding systems noted by Romatschke (2017) and Schenke (2020). The attractor concept builds on work by Heller (2013) and has been demonstrated in Bjorken flow and Gubser flow, with numerical extensions to less symmetric systems by Romatschke (2017). The review connects to the ongoing Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC searching for the QCD critical point, and highlights interdisciplinary links to nuclear structure physics, statistics, and machine learning applications.
