# A practical approach to perturbative corrections to few-body observables

> We formulate two methods to facilitate the calculation of perturbative corrections to quantum few-body observables. Both techniques are designed for a numerical realization in combination with any tool that obtains either the entire spectrum or solely the eigenvalues of an operator corresponding to the observable of interest. We exemplify these methods in the context of the nuclear contact theory without pions (Pionless EFT) and benchmark them in the deuteron channel with available analytical, field-theoretical calculations, as well as in the triton and 3-helium channels through earlier extractions within the dibaryon formalism, where in all three systems the point-proton root-mean-square charge radius (rms) was the perturbed observable of choice. Beyond these $A\leq3$ consistency and accuracy checks, we employ the numerical methods to predict the rms of the 4-helium nuclear ground state to assess three different ways of integrating the Coulomb interaction into Pionless EFT. By comparing the respective results at leading and next-to-leading order for 3- and 4-helium, we find that the uncertainty due to the strong, short-range interaction is significantly larger compared with that due to the long-range Coulomb interaction for both bound states with their different binding momenta. Thereby, we provide strong support for simplifying extractions of bound-state observables by shutting off any Coulomb interaction if the strong part of the potential is considered only up to first order in the effective range expansion.

## Key claim

The paper presents two numerical methods for computing perturbative corrections to few-body observables that avoid the infinite sum over intermediate states required by standard Rayleigh-Schrödinger perturbation theory, and demonstrates that for the point-proton root-mean-square charge radius of light nuclei (A ≤ 4) described by Pionless EFT, the theoretical uncertainty from the short-range strong interaction is significantly larger than that from the long-range Coulomb interaction, supporting the simplification of shutting off Coulomb effects when the strong interaction is treated only up to first order in the effective range expansion.

## Method

The authors formulate two techniques: the one-multiplier method (OMM), which solves the Schrödinger equation for the eigenstate as a function of a single perturbation parameter α and expands the resulting expectation value in a power series; and the two-multiplier method (TMM), which solves for eigenenergies on a two-dimensional (α, β) grid and extracts perturbative corrections via the Feynman-Hellmann theorem. Both methods are implemented using the Stochastic Variational Method with correlated Gaussian bases to solve the few-body Schrödinger equation for Pionless EFT (πEFT) potentials at leading order (LO) with perturbative next-to-leading order (NLO) range corrections, applied to the deuteron, triton, helion, and 4-helium ground states.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron point-proton rms radius at LO: 1.52 fm; at NLO: 1.84 fm (both methods agree); experimental value: 1.990 fm
- Triton point-proton rms radius at LO: 1.13 fm; at NLO: 1.45 fm (OMM) and 1.44 fm (TMM); experimental value: 1.608 fm
- Helium-3 point-proton rms radius with non-perturbative Coulomb at LO: 1.28 fm; at NLO: 1.66 fm (OMM) and 1.65 fm (TMM); experimental value: 1.784 fm
- Helium-4 point-proton rms radius with non-perturbative Coulomb at LO: 0.912 fm; at NLO: 1.27 fm (OMM) and 1.26 fm (TMM); experimental value: 1.477 fm
- The NLO corrections calculated with OMM and TMM differ by less than 0.1% for all systems except 4-helium at higher cutoff values
- The Coulomb treatment affects the NLO contribution by less than 0.03 fm for helion and less than 0.07 fm for 4-helium, while the πEFT NLO uncertainty estimates based on cutoff variation are approximately 0.16 fm and 0.2 fm respectively
- The ratio of typical binding momenta for three-body to four-body systems is approximately 1/3

## Context

This work extends the perturbative treatment of range corrections in Pionless EFT (πEFT) from the deuteron, where semi-analytical calculations exist (Chen 1999), and from three-body systems studied with the dibaryon formalism (Vanasse 2015, 2017), to the four-body system (4-helium) where no previous πEFT study of the point-proton rms radius beyond LO was available. The methods enable systematic perturbative calculations for any observable in few-body systems without requiring explicit construction of the infinite sum over intermediate states, and the results support the practical simplification of neglecting Coulomb interactions in πEFT calculations of bound-state observables when the strong interaction is treated only up to first order in the effective range expansion. The authors suggest that significant differences between Coulomb treatments would appear in observables involving lower typical momenta, such as halo nuclei radii or transitions between ground and shallow nuclear states.
