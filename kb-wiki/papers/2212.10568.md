# Baryon Number Transport, Strangeness Conservation and $Ω$-hadron Correlations

> Although strange quarks are produced in $s\bar{s}$ pairs, the ratio of $Ω^{-}$ to ${\barΩ}^{+}$ is greater than one in heavy-ion collisions at lower RHIC energies. Thus the produced $Ω$ hyperons must carry net baryon quantum numbers from the colliding nuclei. We present results of $K$-$Ω$ correlations from AMPT model simulations of Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 14.6 GeV, to probe dynamics for baryon number transport to mid-rapidities at this beam energy. We use both the default and string-melting versions to illustrate how hadronization schemes of quark coalescence and string fragmentations could leave imprints on such correlations. Implications on the measurements of these correlations with the STAR experiment at RHIC will also be discussed.

# Baryon Number Transport, Strangeness Conservation and Omega-hadron Correlations

## Key claim
The ratio of Omega- to anti-Omega+ is greater than one in heavy-ion collisions at lower RHIC energies, implying that produced Omega hyperons carry net baryon quantum numbers from the colliding nuclei even though strange quarks are produced in s-anti-s pairs. The authors use AMPT model simulations to show that K-Omega correlations can distinguish between two production scenarios (strangeness conservation plus baryon number transport versus strangeness conservation plus baryon number conservation), but with current statistics they find no significant difference between K+-Omega- and K--anti-Omega+ correlation functions.

## Method
The authors performed AMPT model simulations (non-public version v1.25t4cu/v2.25t5cu) of Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 14.6 GeV, generating about 150 million minimum bias events (50 million default version plus 100 million string-melting version). They compared the default version (using Lund string fragmentation) with the string-melting version (using quark coalescence for hadronization) to study how hadronization schemes affect K-Omega correlations, examining both traditional event-mixing correlation functions C(k*) and correlation differences in k* and Delta-pT spaces.

## Key numbers
- About 150 million minimum bias events generated: 50 million default + 100 million string-melting for Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 14.6 GeV
- Delta N_pair(s-anti-s) between events with and without Omega-: 3.07 +/- 0.03 (AMPT string-melting), 3.16 +/- 0.03 (AMPT default)
- Delta N_K (K+ and K0) between events with and without Omega-: 2.44 +/- 0.02 (AMPT string-melting), 1.76 +/- 0.03 (AMPT default)
- Delta N_anti-B (anti-Lambda, anti-Xi, anti-Omega, anti-p, anti-n) between events with and without Omega-: 0.119 +/- 0.006 (AMPT string-melting), 0.28 +/- 0.01 (AMPT default)

## Context
This work extends studies of baryon number transport in heavy-ion collisions at beam energies below sqrt(s_NN) = 50 GeV, where anti-hyperon to hyperon ratios are significantly below one as shown by the BES-I program. The authors build on the gluonic junction mechanism proposed for baryon number transport (Junction, earlier work) and the concept of "leading mesons" (Huang, proceedings), using AMPT model simulations (Lin, 2005) to connect theoretical production scenarios to experimentally accessible observables. The results provide baselines for future measurements of these correlations with the STAR experiment at RHIC, and further investigation is needed to discern whether differences between the two AMPT versions arise from different hadronization procedures.
