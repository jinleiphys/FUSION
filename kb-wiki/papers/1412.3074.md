# Dynamical Generation of a Repulsive Vector Contribution to the Quark Pressure

> Lattice QCD results for the coefficient $c_2$ appearing in the Taylor expansion of the pressure show that this quantity raises with the temperature towards the Stefan-Boltzmann limit. On the other hand, model approximations predict that when a vector repulsion, parametrized by $G_V$, is present this coefficient reaches a maximum just after $T_c$ and then deviates from the lattice predictions. Recently, this discrepancy has been used as a guide to constrain the (presently unknown) value of $G_V$ within the framework of effective models at large-$N_c$ (LN). In the present investigation we show that, due to finite $N_c$ effects, $c_2$ may also develop a maximum even when $G_V=0$ since a vector repulsive term can be dynamically generated by exchange type of radiative corrections. Here we apply the the Optimized Perturbation Theory (OPT) method to the two flavor Polyakov--Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model (at $G_V=0$) and compare the results with those furnished by lattice simulations an by the LN approximation at $G_V=0$ and also at $G_V \ne 0$. The OPT numerical results for $c_2$ are impressively accurate for $T \lesssim 1.2\, T_c$ but, as expected, predict that this quantity develops a maximum at high-$T$. After identifying the mathematical origin of this extremum we argue that such a discrepant behavior may naturally arise within these effective quark models (at $G_V=0$) whenever the first $1/N_c$ corrections are taken into account. We then interpret this hypothesis as an indication that beyond the large-$N_c$ limit the correct high temperature (perturbative) behavior of $c_2$ will be faithfully described by effective models only if they also mimic the asymptotic freedom phenomenon.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the coefficient \(c_2\) in the Taylor expansion of the quark pressure can develop a maximum at high temperature even when the vector coupling \(G_V\) is set to zero, because finite-\(N_c\) radiative corrections (exchange-type diagrams) dynamically generate a repulsive vector contribution. This maximum, which disagrees with lattice QCD results, arises naturally in effective quark models whenever first \(1/N_c\) corrections are included, and the authors argue that faithfully reproducing the correct high-temperature perturbative behavior will require effective models to also mimic asymptotic freedom.

## Method

The authors applied the Optimized Perturbation Theory (OPT) method to the two-flavor Polyakov–Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model with the vector coupling \(G_V\) set to zero. They evaluated the free energy to first non-trivial order in \(\delta\) (the OPT bookkeeping parameter), which includes one-loop and two-loop exchange-type diagrams, and solved the coupled gap equations together with the Principle of Minimal Sensitivity (PMS) to determine the optimal mass parameter \(\bar\eta\). The results for the quark number density and the coefficient \(c_2\) (from the Taylor expansion \(P/T^4 = c_0 + c_2 (\mu/T)^2 + \dots\)) were compared with lattice QCD simulations and with the large-\(N_c\) (LN) approximation both at \(G_V=0\) and at \(G_V \ne 0\).

## Key numbers

* The OPT numerical results for \(c_2\) are impressively accurate for temperatures \(T \lesssim 1.2\, T_c\) when compared to lattice QCD data.
* The OPT predicts that \(c_2\) develops a maximum at \(T \sim 1.2\, T_c\), a feature not seen in any lattice QCD result.
* The Polyakov potential parameters used are \(a_0 = 3.51\), \(a_1 = -2.47\), \(a_2 = 15.22\), and \(b_4 = -1.75\), with \(T_0 = 270\) MeV for the pure-gauge deconfinement critical temperature.
* The standard NJL parameters are fixed to reproduce \(m_\pi \simeq 135\) MeV, \(f_\pi \simeq 93\) MeV, and \(\langle \bar\psi \psi \rangle^{1/3} \simeq 250\) MeV, yielding \(\Lambda \sim 560\)–\(670\) MeV, \(G \Lambda^2 \sim 2\)–\(3.2\), and \(m_c \sim 5\)–\(5.6\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous OPT applications to the NJL model (Pinto et al., 2012; 2013) by incorporating the Polyakov loop to simulate confinement, making the model more realistic for QCD. It directly addresses the discrepancy between lattice QCD results for \(c_2\) and predictions from large-\(N_c\) PNJL models with explicit vector repulsion, as recently studied by Schramm and Steinheimer (2014) and Sugano et al. (2014). The paper shows that finite-\(N_c\) corrections alone can produce the same qualitative maximum in \(c_2\) as an explicit vector term, and it suggests that future effective models must incorporate asymptotic freedom to correctly describe the high-temperature behavior beyond the large-\(N_c\) limit.
