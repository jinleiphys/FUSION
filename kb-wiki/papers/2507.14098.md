# Symmetrizing relativistic three-body partial wave amplitudes

> S matrix principles and symmetries impose constraints on three-particle scattering amplitudes, which can be formulated as a class of integral equations for their partial wave projections. However, these amplitudes are typically expressed in an asymmetric basis, where one of the initial and final state particles is singled out, and all quantum numbers are defined relative to this spectator. In this work, we show how to construct symmetric partial wave amplitudes, which have been symmetrized over all possible spectator combinations, using their asymmetric counterparts and sets of recoupling coefficients. We derive these recoupling coefficients for arbitrary angular momentum and isospin for arbitrary systems of spinless particles with SU(2) flavor symmetry. We propose a simple intensity observable suitable for visualizing the structure of three-body dynamics in Dalitz distributions. Finally, we provide some numerical examples of Dalitz distributions relevant for future lattice QCD calculations of $3π$ systems and provide numerical evidence that the symmetrization procedure is consistent with expected symmetries of Dalitz plots.

## Key claim

The paper shows how to construct fully symmetric three-body partial wave amplitudes (independent of which particle is chosen as the "spectator") from their asymmetric counterparts using recoupling coefficients, for arbitrary angular momentum and isospin in systems of spinless particles with SU(2) flavor symmetry, but the symmetrization is performed only after projecting to definite total angular momentum J^P, and the resulting amplitudes remain functions of the invariant masses rather than angular variables.

## Method

The authors derive recoupling coefficients that transform asymmetric partial wave amplitudes (where one initial and one final particle is singled out as the spectator) into symmetric amplitudes summed over all spectator choices. They treat angular momentum recoupling separately from isospin recoupling, providing explicit formulas for arbitrary partial wave channels. The symmetric amplitudes are then used to define intensity observables suitable for Dalitz plot visualization, and numerical examples are computed for 3π systems using the derived formalism.

## Key numbers

- The Kibble cubic function Φ({σ}) ≥ 0 defines the physical Dalitz region at fixed s, with coefficients c_j = (m_{j+1}^2 m_{j+2}^2 - m_j^2 s)(m_{j+1}^2 + m_{j+2}^2 - m_j^2 - s)
- The pair invariant mass σ_j is bounded as m_{j+1} + m_{j+2} ≤ √σ_j ≤ √s - m_j for physical kinematics
- The spectator momentum is given by p_j = (1/(2√s)) λ^{1/2}(s, m_j^2, σ_j) where λ is the Källén function
- The Mandelstam relation imposes Σ_{j=1}^3 σ_j = s + Σ_{j=1}^3 m_j^2 ≡ Δ(s)

## Context

This work completes the workflow for extracting three-body observables from lattice QCD calculations, building on the partial-wave projection of the one-particle exchange amplitude (Jackura 2023) and the integral equations for asymmetric amplitudes (Briceño 2024). It extends prior work on three-body finite-volume formalisms (Hansen 2015, Mai 2017, Jackura 2020) and provides the symmetrization step needed to connect lattice QCD results to experimental Dalitz plot observables. The recoupling coefficients enable future lattice QCD calculations of 3π systems to produce amplitudes with the correct symmetry properties under particle interchange.
