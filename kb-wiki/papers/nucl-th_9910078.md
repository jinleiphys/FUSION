# A simple model for the microscopic effective pairing interaction

> The microscopic effective pairing interaction in the $^1S_0$-channel is investigated for two different values of the chemical potential $μ$ starting from the separable form of the Paris NN-potential. It is shown that, within a high accuracy, this effective interaction can be approximated by the off-shell free T-matrix taken at the negative energy $E=2μ$.

# A simple model for the microscopic effective pairing interaction

## Key claim

The microscopic effective pairing interaction in the \(^1S_0\)-channel can be approximated by the off-shell free \(T\)-matrix taken at the negative energy \(E=2\mu\), with the qualification that this approximation holds within high accuracy for the two values of chemical potential studied (\(\mu = -8\) MeV and \(\mu = -4\) MeV) and relies on the separable representation of the Paris NN-potential.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the effective pairing interaction \(\mathcal{V}_{\text{eff}}\) in a finite slab geometry (Saxon-Woods potential well) using the Local Potential Approximation (LPA), which replaces exact slab propagators with those for infinite nuclear matter at a constant potential. The separable form of the Paris NN-potential is used, and the resulting integral equations for the effective interaction are renormalized in terms of the free \(T\)-matrix to improve convergence. The free \(T\)-matrix is computed by solving a one-dimensional equation in momentum space and then Fourier-transformed via contour integration in the complex plane to handle oscillatory behavior at large separations.

## Key numbers

- The separable Paris potential coefficients (in MeV\(\cdot\)fm\(^3\)): \(\lambda_{11} = -3659\), \(\lambda_{12} = 2169\), \(\lambda_{22} = -1485\), \(\lambda_{13} = -23.6\), \(\lambda_{23} = 57.6\), \(\lambda_{33} = 17.2\).
- The slab geometry parameters: width \(2L = 16\) fm, well depth \(V_0 = -50\) MeV, diffuseness \(d = 0.65\) fm.
- The cut-off momentum for numerical integration of the free propagator is \(k_c = 60\) fm\(^{-1}\), guaranteeing an accuracy better than 1\%.
- For the Fourier transform of the \(T\)-matrix, the contour parameters are \(a = 2\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(b = 120\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- The difference between the in-matter propagator \(B_{lm}^{\text{inf}}(V)\) and the free propagator \(B_{lm}^0\) is about 10\% in absolute value for the maximum potential depth \(V = 50\) MeV.
- For the zero-order moments of the effective interaction components, the difference from the free \(T\)-matrix is about 20\% for the 12-component and about 30\% for the 22-component, but these deviations compensate in the sum relevant for pairing.
- Two values of chemical potential are studied: \(\mu = -8\) MeV and \(\mu = -4\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of the microscopic effective pairing interaction for semi-infinite nuclear matter (Baldo et al., 1999, referred to as BLSZ1) to a finite slab geometry more representative of heavy nuclei in the lead region. The key finding is that the complicated effective interaction from the Bethe-Goldstone equation can be replaced by the much simpler free off-shell \(T\)-matrix, which enables easier applications to nuclear structure calculations. The approach uses the separable representation of the Paris potential (Plessas et al., 1982; Haidenbauer and Plessas, 1984) and the Bethe-Brueckner many-body framework (Ainsworth and Baldo, 1991; Schuck, 1990). The approximation is shown to hold even for small chemical potentials relevant to nuclei near drip-lines, and the authors suggest it could be improved further by adding a small set of positive-energy states to the model space.
