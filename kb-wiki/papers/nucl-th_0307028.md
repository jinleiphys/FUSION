# Invariant correlational entropy as a signature of quantum phase transitions in nuclei

> We study phase transformations in finite nuclei as a function of interaction parameters. The signature of a transition is given by invariant correlational entropy that reflects the sensitivity of an individual many-body state to changes of external parameters; peaks in this quantity indicate the critical regions. This approach is able to reveal the pairing phase transition, identify the isovector and isoscalar pairing regions and determine the role of other interactions. We show the examples of the phase diagram in the parameter space.

## Key claim

Invariant correlational entropy (ICE) peaks indicate critical regions of quantum phase transitions in finite nuclei, but the transitions are smoothed and shifted relative to the thermodynamic limit; for example, in the two-level pairing model the BCS critical strength λ_c = 0.25 is shifted to λ_c ≈ 0.3 in the exact solution.

## Method

The authors compute the invariant correlational entropy (ICE) of individual eigenstates by averaging the density matrix over a small interval δ of a control parameter (pairing strength λ_p, isovector pairing strength λ_{T=1}, isoscalar pairing strength λ_{T=0}, or non-pairing interaction strength λ_{np}) and then evaluating the von Neumann entropy of the resulting mixed state. They apply this to exact shell-model diagonalizations of the two-level pairing model, of ⁴⁸Ca in the fp shell, and of ²⁴Mg in the sd shell, mapping ICE as a function of interaction parameters to construct phase diagrams.

## Key numbers

- In the two-level pairing model with Ω = 16, N = 16, and ε = 1, the BCS critical strength is λ_c = 0.25, while the ICE maximum occurs at λ_c ≈ 0.3.
- For ⁴⁸Ca, the maximum number of T=1 pairs in the fully paired (s=0) state is N₁ = 28 (with Ω = 20, N = 8); the statistical background average is N̄₁ ≈ 1.5.
- For ²⁴Mg, the maximum number of isovector pairs in the fully paired state is N₁ = 22 (with Ω = 24, N = 8); the maximum number of isoscalar pairs is N₀ ≈ 3.7; the statistical averages are N̄₁ ≈ 1.8 and N̄₀ ≈ 0.3.
- In ⁴⁸Ca, the occupancy of the lowest f₇/₂ orbital falls from N₇/₂ = 8 to a "thermal" occupation N₇/₂ = 3.2 at strong non-pairing interactions.

## Context

This work extends the concept of invariant correlational entropy, introduced by Sokolov (1998) for general quantum systems, to nuclear shell-model phase transitions, building on earlier bosonic studies by Cejnar (2001) that associated ICE increases with critical points. It directly addresses the finite-system counterpart of the BCS pairing phase transition, showing that non-pairing interactions (studied earlier by Volya (2002) and others) smear the transition and shift the critical point, and it enables systematic mapping of phase diagrams for competing isovector and isoscalar pairing in N=Z nuclei like ²⁴Mg.
