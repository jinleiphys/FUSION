# Vortex Creep Heating in Neutron Star Cooling: New Insights into Thermal Evolution of Heavy Neutron Stars

> Neutron stars provide unique laboratories for probing physics of dense nuclear matter under extreme conditions. Their thermal and luminosity evolution reflects key internal properties such as the equation of state (EoS), nucleon superfluidity and superconductivity, envelope composition, and magnetic field, and so on. Recent observations [\textit{e.g.}, V. Abramkin \textit{et al.,} ApJ \textbf{924}, 128 (2022)] have revealed unexpectedly warm old neutron stars, which cannot be explained by standard neutrino-photon cooling models. The failure of the standard cooling models implies the presence of additional internal heating mechanism. Building on the previous study [M. Fujiwara \textit{et al}., JCAP \textbf{03}, 051 (2024)], which proposed vortex creep heating (VCH) from the frictional motion of superfluid vortices as a viable mechanism, we extend the cooling framework to include both VCH and direct Urca (DUrca) processes. These are implemented in our code to explore their combined impact, particularly for massive neutron stars where DUrca operates. By varying rotational parameters ($P$, $\dot{P}$, $P_0$), EoS models (APR, BSk24), pairing gaps, and envelope compositions, we examine how heating-cooling interplay shapes the temperature evolution. Our results show that VCH can substantially mitigate the rapid cooling driven by DUrca, offering new evolutionary pathways for massive neutron stars.

## Key claim

Vortex creep heating (VCH) can substantially mitigate the rapid cooling driven by direct Urca (DUrca) processes in massive neutron stars, offering new evolutionary pathways that could explain unexpectedly warm old neutron stars, but the effect depends sensitively on the interplay between initial spin period, magnetic field strength, and the onset of quantum creep.

## Method

The authors extended their Fortran90 neutron-star cooling code, based on the NSCool numerical framework, to include both vortex creep heating and direct Urca processes. They solved the general-relativistic energy-balance and transport equations for neutron star thermal evolution, varying rotational parameters (spin period P, period derivative Pdot, initial spin period P0), equation of state models (APR, BSk24), superfluid pairing gaps (CCDK, SFB, TToa), and envelope compositions. The stellar structure was obtained from the TOV equations, and observational data from the Ioffe Institute neutron-star database were used for comparison.

## Key numbers

- For a 2.0 solar mass neutron star at B = 10^12 G (BSk24 EoS, Fe envelope), cooling curves were calculated for initial spin periods P0 = 10 to 570 ms, with the proportional constant J spanning 10^42.9 to 10^43.8 erg s.
- Faster initial rotation (smaller P0) enhances early VCH, sustaining higher surface temperatures during 10^3 to 10^5 years.
- The quantum creep regime activates at temperatures T much less than Tq, where Tq is approximately 10^8 K.
- For a 1.4 solar mass neutron star at fixed P0 = 10 ms and J = 10^43.8 erg s, cooling curves were computed for different magnetic field strengths B, showing that stronger magnetic fields cause slightly faster late-time cooling.

## Context

This work extends the previous study by Fujiwara (2024), which examined VCH in low-mass stars where direct Urca processes are forbidden, by now exploring the combined impact of VCH and DUrca in massive neutron stars. The results contradict standard neutrino-photon cooling models that cannot explain the unexpectedly warm old neutron stars observed by Abramkin (2022). The findings enable future work on highly magnetized neutron stars with anisotropic thermal conduction, calibration of the proportional constant J via microscopic pinning force calculations, and inclusion of other heating mechanisms such as rotochemical heating, magnetic field decay, and dark-matter heating.
