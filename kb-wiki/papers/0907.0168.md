# Scaling patterns of the suppression of $π^0$ yields in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV: links to the transport properties of the QGP

> Suppression measurements for neutral pions ($π^0$) are used to investigate the predicted path length ($L$) and transverse momentum ($p_T$) dependent jet quenching patterns of the hot QCD medium produced in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. The observed scaling patterns show the predicted trends for jet-medium interactions dominated by radiative energy loss. They also allow simple estimates of the transport coefficient $\hat{q}$ and the ratio of viscosity to entropy density $η/s$. These estimates indicate that the short mean free path ($λ$) in the QCD medium leading to hydrodynamic-like flow with a small value of $η/s$, is also responsible for the strong suppression observed.

## Key claim

The observed scaling patterns of neutral pion suppression in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV are compatible with the predicted trends for jet-medium interactions dominated by radiative energy loss via medium-induced gluon radiation, but the paper's estimates of the transport coefficient q̂ and viscosity-to-entropy ratio η/s depend on the specific choice of coupling constant, color factor, and slope extracted from fits to the data.

## Method

The authors used published π⁰ nuclear modification factor RAA(pT) and RAA(Δφ, pT) data from PHENIX to test the "pocket formula" of Dokshitzer and Kharzeev (DK), which predicts that ln[RAA(pT, L)] should scale linearly with path length L and with 1/√(pT) if radiative energy loss dominates. The path length L was estimated from the transverse size of the system via a Monte-Carlo Glauber model, using the distribution of participant nucleons to compute root-mean-square widths σx and σy, with the initial entropy profile assumed proportional to a linear combination of participant and binary collision densities.

## Key numbers

- For central 0-5% collisions (L = 1.90 fm) and 20-30% collisions (L = 1.45 fm), ln[RAA(pT, L)] vs. 1/√(pT) for pT > 5 GeV/c shows a trend compatible with the predicted linear dependence.
- The slope extracted from ln[RAA(pT, L)] vs. L for pT > 10 GeV/c is -1.26 ± 0.06 fm⁻¹.
- The transport coefficient estimate is q̂ ≈ 0.75 GeV²/fm, using αs = 0.3, CF = 9/4, and ℒ = n = 8.1 ± 0.05.
- The intercept from the L-dependence fit indicates L ≈ 0.6 fm, suggesting a minimum path length requirement for the initiation of π⁰ suppression.
- The estimate for η/s is obtained via η/s ≈ 1.25 T³/q̂, and using T ∼ 220 MeV gives a value consistent with 4π(η/s) ≈ 1.3 ± 0.3 from flow data.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Dokshitzer and Kharzeev (2001) for medium-induced gluon radiation to experimental π⁰ suppression data from PHENIX at RHIC, providing validation tests that distinguish radiative energy loss from collisional energy loss mechanisms. The extracted q̂ value (∼0.75 GeV²/fm) is comparable to estimates from the higher twist (HT) expansion by Zhang (2007) and Majumder (2007) and the Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev (GLV) scheme, but much smaller than values from the Arnold-Moore-Yaffe (AMY) approach and the Armesto-Salgado-Wiedemann (ASW) scheme. The results enable future use of jet quenching as a quantitative tomographic probe of the QGP and should be extended to heavy quark systems for more definitive conclusions.
