# Quark structure of isoscalar- and isovector-scalar mesons and nuclear matter property

> It was found that the isoscalar-scalar and isovector-scalar mesons play significant roles in nuclear matter physics. However, the underlying structures of these resonances are not yet well understood. We construct a three-flavor baryonic extended linear sigma model including both the two- and four-quark constitutes of scalar mesons with respect to the axial transformation and study the nuclear matter properties with relativistic mean field method. The nuclear matter properties at saturation density and hadron spectrums are well reproduced with this model simultaneously and, only the two-quark component of the scalar mesons couple to baryon fields and dominates the nuclear force. A plateau-like structure is found in the symmetry energy of nuclear matter due to the multi-meson couplings, and it's crucial for understanding the neutron skin thickness of $^{208}\rm Pb$ and the tidal deformation of neutron star from GW170817. At high density regions, the vector mesons are found to be rather crucial, especially the ones coupling with four-quark configurations. This model can be easily extended to study neutron stars, even with hyperons in their interior.

## Key claim

The authors construct a three-flavor baryonic extended linear sigma model (bELSM) that includes both two-quark and four-quark components of scalar mesons, and find that only the two-quark component couples to baryon fields at lowest order due to axial transformation invariance, dominating the nuclear force. The model simultaneously reproduces nuclear matter properties at saturation density and hadron spectra, with the hedge that the four-quark configuration of vector mesons becomes crucial at high densities.

## Method

The authors build a baryonic extended linear sigma model (bELSM) with two scalar meson nonets representing two-quark (q\bar{q}) and four-quark ((qq)(\bar{q}\bar{q})) configurations, plus vector mesons as chiral representations with two-quark configurations. They apply the relativistic mean field (RMF) approximation to study nuclear matter, using power-counting rules based on valence quark number to truncate the Lagrangian, and determine parameters by fitting empirical meson spectra and nuclear matter properties simultaneously.

## Context

This work extends the extended linear sigma model (ELSM) approach of Fariborz (2005, 2007, 2009) and Harada (2004, 2012) by coupling scalar mesons with two- and four-quark configurations to baryon fields, following the diquark model of Olbrich (2015). It contrasts with earlier RMF approaches such as Walecka (1974), Boguta (1977), and Serot (1984) that treated scalar mesons as pure q\bar{q} states, and with models like TM1 from Sugahara (1993) and FSU-δ6.7 from Li (2022) that did not consider the quark substructure of scalar mesons. The plateau-like structure in symmetry energy found here is crucial for simultaneously understanding the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb from Horowitz (2000, 2001) and the tidal deformability constraint from GW170817, and the model can be extended to study neutron stars with hyperons.
