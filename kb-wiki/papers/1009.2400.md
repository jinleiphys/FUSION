# Linking the hydrodynamic and kinetic description of a dissipative relativistic conformal theory

> We use the entropy production variational method to associate a one particle distribution function to the assumed known energy-momentum and entropy currents describing a relativistic conformal fluid. Assuming a simple form for the collision operator we find this one particle distribution function explicitly, and show that this method of linking the hydro and kinetic description is a non trivial generalization of Grad's ansatz. The resulting constitutive relations are the same as in the conformal dissipative type theories discussed in J. Peralta-Ramos and E. Calzetta, Phys. Rev. D {\bfseries 80}, 126002 (2009). Our results may prove useful in the description of freeze-out in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The entropy production variational method (EPVM) provides a one-particle distribution function for a relativistic conformal fluid that generalizes Grad's ansatz, but the resulting correction to the distribution function depends essentially on the collision operator (through the relaxation time and the function F), unlike Grad's method where the correction is expressed solely in terms of shear viscosity and equilibrium quantities.

## Method

The authors use the entropy production variational method to find the distribution function that extremizes entropy production given fixed energy-momentum and entropy currents, assuming a linear collision operator that satisfies energy-momentum conservation and the H theorem. They solve the variational equation perturbatively to second order in the relaxation time τ, using a projection operator to enforce conservation constraints. The resulting distribution function is then matched to a conformal divergence-type theory (DTT) to determine the dynamics of the energy-momentum and entropy currents.

## Key numbers

- For the Anderson-Witting collision term (F = ω_p), the second-order coefficient K is K_AW = 180 τ² T² (ρ + p), where ρ is energy density and p is pressure.
- For the Marle collision term (F = T), the second-order coefficient K is K_Marle = 84 τ² T⁻¹ ⟨ω_p⁵⟩.
- The shear viscosity η is given by η = (τ / 15T) ⟨ω_p⁴ / F⟩.
- The relaxation rate Γ is given by Γ = (6/τ) ⟨ω_p⁵ / F²⟩⁻¹ ⟨ω_p⁴ / F⟩.
- The coefficient c in the DTT generating potential is c = (5T⁵ / 12) ⟨ω_p⁴ / F⟩⁻² ⟨ω_p⁵ / F²⟩.

## Context

This work extends the conformal dissipative theories developed by Peralta-Ramos and Calzetta (2009) by providing a kinetic foundation through the entropy production variational method. It generalizes Grad's method of moments (Grad, 1949) and the Israel-Stewart formalism (Israel, 1976) by allowing momentum-dependent corrections to the distribution function, as discussed by Dusling et al. (2010) and Luzum and Ollitrault (2010). The results enable improved descriptions of freeze-out in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions and provide a way to constrain the collision operator by matching hydrodynamic simulations to experimental data.
