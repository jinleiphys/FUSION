# The long journey from the giant-monopole resonance to the nuclear-matter incompressibility

> Differences in the density dependence of the symmetry energy predicted by nonrelativistic and relativistic models are suggested, at least in part, as the culprit for the discrepancy in the values of the compression modulus of symmetric nuclear matter extracted from the energy of the giant monopole resonance in 208Pb. ``Best-fit'' relativistic models, with stiffer symmetry energies than Skyrme interactions, consistently predict higher compression moduli than nonrelativistic approaches. Relativistic models with compression moduli in the physically acceptable range of K=200-300 MeV are used to compute the distribution of isoscalar monopole strength in 208Pb. When the symmetry energy is artificially softened in one of these models, in an attempt to simulate the symmetry energy of Skyrme interactions, a lower value for the compression modulus is indeed obtained. It is concluded that the proposed measurement of the neutron skin in 208Pb, aimed at constraining the density dependence of the symmetry energy and recently correlated to the structure of neutron stars, will also become instrumental in the determination of the compression modulus of nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The discrepancy between relativistic and nonrelativistic models in predicting the compression modulus of symmetric nuclear matter from the giant monopole resonance in 208Pb may be due, at least in part, to incomplete knowledge of the density dependence of the symmetry energy, rather than solely to differences in symmetric nuclear matter physics.

## Method

The author constructed three families of relativistic mean-field models (A, B, C) constrained to reproduce the saturation density, binding energy per nucleon, and charge radius of 208Pb, while varying the effective nucleon mass (M*/M = 0.6 for family A, 0.7 for families B and C) and the symmetry energy (artificially softened in family C to simulate Skyrme interactions). For each family, the isoscalar monopole response in 208Pb was computed using a nonspectral relativistic random-phase-approximation (RPA) approach, with the compression modulus varied between 200 and 300 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Experimental peak energy of the giant monopole resonance in 208Pb: E_GMR = 14.17 ± 0.28 MeV (Youngblood et al., 1999)
- Compression moduli required to reproduce the experimental GMR energy: K = 275 MeV (family A), K = 255 MeV (family B), K = 240 MeV (family C)
- Universal slope relating GMR energy to compression modulus: dE_GMR/dK = 0.026 (dimensionless)
- Intercepts for the linear relation E_GMR = E_200 + 0.026(K-200): E_200 = 12.22 MeV (family A), 12.71 MeV (family B), 13.14 MeV (family C)
- Experimental root-mean-square charge radius of 208Pb: r_ch = 5.50 ± 0.01 fm
- Neutron skin predictions for 208Pb: R_n - R_p = 0.28 fm (NL3 model), 0.27 fm (TM1 model), 0.26 fm (NLC model); for Skyrme SkX parametrization: 0.16 fm
- Symmetry energy at k_F = 1.15 fm^{-1} (ρ = 0.10 fm^{-3}) fixed to 26 MeV for all families

## Context

This work extends the microscopic approach of Blaizot and collaborators (1995, 1999) for extracting the compression modulus from the giant monopole resonance, contrasting with macroscopic (semi-empirical) formulas that the author argues are now inadequate. The paper addresses the longstanding discrepancy between nonrelativistic Skyrme and Gogny models (predicting K = 210-220 MeV) and relativistic models (predicting K ≈ 275 MeV), as seen in the work of Colo et al. (1992), Hamamoto et al. (1997), Blaizot et al. (1995), Lalazissis et al. (1997), and Vretenar et al. (2000). The proposed resolution connects to the Parity Radius Experiment (PREX) at Jefferson Laboratory, which aims to measure the neutron skin of 208Pb and thereby constrain the symmetry energy, with implications for neutron star structure as discussed by Horowitz and Piekarewicz (2001).
