# A new look at the $P_{cs}$ states from a molecular perspective

> We have a look at the $P_{cs}$ states generated from the interaction of $\bar D^{(*)} ╬×^{(\prime*)}_c$ coupled channels. We consider the blocks of pseudoscalar-baryon $({\frac12}^+, {\frac32}^+)$ and vector-baryon $({\frac12}^+, {\frac32}^+)$, and find $10$ resonant states coupling mostly to $\bar D ╬×_c, \bar D^* ╬×_c,\bar D ╬×'_c, \bar D^* ╬×'_c,\bar D ╬×^*_c$ and $\bar D^* ╬×^*_c$. A novel aspect of the work is the realization that the $\bar D ╬×_c,\bar D_s╬ø_c$ or $\bar D^* ╬×_c,\bar D^*_s╬ø_c$ channels, with a strong transition potential, collaborate to produce a larger attraction than the corresponding states $\bar D ╬Ż_c,\bar D╬ø_c$ or $\bar D^* ╬Ż_c,\bar D^*╬ø_c$ appearing in the generation of the strangenessless $P_{c}$ states, since in the latter case the transition potential between those channels is zero. The extra attraction obtained in the $\bar D ╬×_c,\bar D^* ╬×_c$ pairs preclude the association of these channels to the $P_{cs}(4338)$ and $P_{cs}(4459)$ states respectively. Then we find a natural association of the $P_{cs}(4338)$ state coupling mostly to $\bar D^* ╬×_c$ while the $P_{cs}(4459)$ is associated to the state found that couples mostly to $\bar D ╬×'_c$. Four more states appear, like in other molecular pictures, and some of the states are degenerate in spin. Counting different spin states we find $10$ states, which we hope can be observed in the near future.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the $P_{cs}(4338)$ and $P_{cs}(4459)$ states cannot be associated with the $\bar D \Xi_c$ and $\bar D^* \Xi_c$ molecular channels respectively, contrary to most prior work, because the strong transition potential between $\bar D \Xi_c$ and $\bar D_s\Lambda_c$ (or $\bar D^* \Xi_c$ and $\bar D^*_s\Lambda_c$) produces extra attraction that binds these channels too deeply; instead, the $P_{cs}(4338)$ couples mostly to $\bar D^* \Xi_c$ and the $P_{cs}(4459)$ couples mostly to $\bar D \Xi'_c$.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in coupled channels for four separate blocks: pseudoscalar-baryon with $J^P = 1/2^+$ baryons, pseudoscalar-baryon with $J^P = 3/2^+$ baryons, vector-baryon with $J^P = 1/2^+$ baryons, and vector-baryon with $J^P = 3/2^+$ baryons, using transition potentials derived from the local hidden gauge approach with cutoff regularization ($q_{max}=600$ MeV). The coefficients $C_{ij}$ for the dominant terms are evaluated from quark-model wave functions and agree with heavy quark spin symmetry expectations, while subdominant heavy-meson exchange terms are included with a suppression factor $\lambda=0.25$.

## Key numbers

* Pole at $4337.98 \pm i0.12$ MeV in the $VB(1/2^+)$ sector, coupling mostly to $\bar D^* \Xi_c$ ($g=5.07$), associated with $P_{cs}(4338)$; the experimental width is $7.0\pm1.2\pm1.3$ MeV.
* Pole at $4422.79 \pm i7.75$ MeV in the $PB(1/2^+)$ sector, coupling mostly to $\bar D \Xi'_c$ ($g=2.79$), associated with $P_{cs}(4459)$; the experimental width is $17.3\pm6.5^{+8.0}_{-5.7}$ MeV.
* Pole at $4198.94 \pm i0.11$ MeV in $PB(1/2^+)$, coupling mostly to $\bar D \Xi_c$ ($g=4.85$).
* Pole at $4565.73 \pm i15.58$ MeV in $VB(1/2^+)$, coupling mostly to $\bar D^* \Xi'_c$ ($g=2.84$).
* Single-channel poles: $\bar D \Xi^*_c$ at $4488.52$ MeV ($J^P=3/2^-$), and $\bar D^* \Xi^*_c$ at $4627.48$ MeV ($J^P=1/2^-, 3/2^-, 5/2^-$).
* The transition coefficient $C_{ij}$ from $\bar D_s\Lambda_c$ to $\bar D\Xi_c$ is $-\sqrt{2}$, compared to diagonal $C_{ii}=-1$ for $\bar D\Xi_c$.

## Context

This work extends the molecular approach of Xiao (2013) and Xiao (2019) for hidden-charm pentaquarks to the strangeness sector, using the same heavy-quark spin symmetry and local hidden gauge framework that successfully described the $P_c$ states. It contradicts the assignments made in Wang (2022), Meng (2022), and Chen (2022), who associated $P_{cs}(4338)$ with $\bar D \Xi_c$ and $P_{cs}(4459)$ with $\bar D^* \Xi_c$; the present work finds that the $\bar D \Xi_c - \bar D_s\Lambda_c$ coupling produces extra attraction that precludes those assignments. The results predict ten total resonant states, including several not yet observed, which could be tested in future LHCb runs with higher statistics.
