# Chiral dynamics and pion-nucleon scattering around the N*(1535) resonance

> We study the S-wave interaction of mesons with baryons in the strangeness S=0 sector in a coupled channel unitary approach. The basic dynamics is drawn from the lowest order meson baryon chiral Lagrangians. Small modifications inspired by models with explicit vector meson exchange in the t-channel are also considered. In addition the pi pi N channel is included and shown to have an important repercussion in the results, particularly in the isospin 3/2 sector.

# Chiral dynamics and pion-nucleon scattering around the N*(1535) resonance

## Key claim

The authors show that a chiral unitary model for S-wave meson-baryon scattering in the strangeness S=0 sector can reproduce both elastic pi N scattering and pion production cross sections from threshold to 1600 MeV, but only when two modifications are added: a vector-meson-dominance-inspired correction to the chiral coefficients and the explicit inclusion of pi pi N channels.

## Method

The authors solve a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation with on-shell factorized amplitudes for six meson-baryon channels (pi N, eta N, K Lambda, K Sigma) plus two pi pi N channels. The transition potentials are taken from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian, with the chiral coefficients modified by an integral over the vector meson propagator to account for momentum-transfer dependence. The pi N to pi pi N transition potentials and subtraction constants in the loop integrals are treated as free parameters and fitted to reproduce both elastic pi N scattering data and pion production cross sections simultaneously.

## Key numbers

- The rho meson mass used in the VMD-inspired correction is m_rho = 770 MeV
- The VMD correction reduces the pi^- p to pi^- p element by about 25% at energies around 1500 MeV
- The subtraction scale is mu = 1200 MeV, with subtraction parameters: a_pi N(mu) = 2.0, a_eta N(mu) = 0.1, a_K Lambda(mu) = 1.5, a_K Sigma(mu) = -2.8
- The model reproduces data in the energy range from threshold to 1600 MeV
- Without pi pi N channels, inelasticities of S_11 (S_31) below 1487 MeV (1690 MeV) are zero and disagree with data

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Kaiser (1997), Oller, Oset and Ramos (2000), and Nacher (2000) by adding two improvements that were necessary for quantitative agreement with pi N scattering data. The inclusion of pi pi N channels is shown to be essential for reproducing inelasticities at low energies, particularly in the isospin 3/2 sector where earlier models failed. The results enable future studies of the N*(1535) resonance structure within a consistent chiral framework that respects unitarity.
