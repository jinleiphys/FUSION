# Alternative similarity renormalization group generators in nuclear structure calculations

> The similarity renormalization group (SRG) has been successfully applied to soften interactions for ab initio nuclear calculations. In almost all practical applications in nuclear physics, an SRG generator with the kinetic energy operator is used. With this choice, a fast convergence of many-body calculations can be achieved, but at the same time substantial three-body interactions are induced even if one starts from a purely two-nucleon (NN) Hamiltonian. Three-nucleon (3N) interactions can be handled by modern many-body methods. However, it has been observed that when including initial chiral 3N forces in the Hamiltonian, the SRG transformations induce a non-negligible four-nucleon interaction that cannot be currently included in the calculations for technical reasons. Consequently, it is essential to investigate alternative SRG generators that might suppress the induction of many-body forces while at the same time might preserve the good convergence. In this work we test two alternative generators with operators of block structure in the harmonic oscillator basis. In the no-core shell model calculations for 3H, 4He and 6Li with chiral NN force, we demonstrate that their performances appear quite promising.

## Key claim

The paper tests two alternative SRG generators with block-diagonal flow operators in the harmonic oscillator basis, finding that they suppress the induction of many-body forces compared to the standard kinetic-energy generator while still improving convergence over the bare interaction, but with the caveat that the evolved Hamiltonian becomes dependent on the HO frequency and loses variationality.

## Method

The authors performed SRG evolution of the chiral N3LO NN interaction in the two-nucleon space using two alternative flow operators: Generator A (block-diagonal part of the Hamiltonian in the HO basis, analogous to Okubo-Lee-Suzuki transformations) and Generator B (kinetic energy plus the P-space part of the potential). They then applied the evolved NN-only interactions in no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for 3H, 4He, and 6Li, varying the parameters Ngen (HO excitation cutoff for the projector), the SRG flow parameter lambda, the NCSM basis size Nmax, and the HO frequency hbar-Omega.

## Key numbers

* Bare chiral N3LO NN interaction gives 3H ground-state energy of -7.85 MeV at hbar-Omega=24 MeV; with standard Trel generator at lambda=2.0 fm^-1, the energy becomes -8.33 MeV.
* For 4He, bare interaction gives -25.39 MeV; Trel generator at lambda=2.0 fm^-1 gives -28.24 MeV.
* With Generator A at Ngen=14, lambda=2.0 fm^-1, hbar-Omega=24 MeV: 3H energy = -7.90 MeV; 4He energy = -25.62 MeV.
* With Generator B at Ngen=14, lambda=2.0 fm^-1, hbar-Omega=24 MeV: 3H energy = -7.94 MeV; 4He energy = -25.86 MeV.
* For 3H with Generator A at Ngen=18, lambda=2.0 fm^-1, hbar-Omega=24 MeV: energy = -7.86 MeV, approaching the bare result of -7.85 MeV.
* The decoupling measure Tr(Pgen H_s Qgen H_s Pgen) for the 3S1-3D1 channel decreases monotonically with decreasing lambda from approximately 10^6 at lambda=3.0 fm^-1 to below 10^2 at lambda=1.0 fm^-1.

## Context

This work extends the study of alternative SRG generators beyond the standard kinetic-energy choice (Gs=Trel) used in most nuclear physics applications since the work of Furnstahl (2012) and Bogner (2006). It builds on the momentum-space block-diagonal generator introduced by Anderson (2008) and the functional generators of Li (2011), but is the first to test such alternative generators in realistic few-nucleon calculations (A>2). The results enable future investigations of whether these generators can suppress the non-negligible four-nucleon interactions induced by SRG evolution when initial chiral 3N forces are present, as observed by Roth (2011), potentially allowing consistent treatment of heavier nuclei without including induced four-body forces.
