# Valence quark contributions for the gamma N -> P11(1440) form factors

> Using a covariant spectator quark model we estimate valence quark contributions to the F1*(Q2) and F2*(Q2) transition form factors for the gamma N -> P11(1440) reaction. The Roper resonance, P11(1440), is assumed to be the first radial excitation of the nucleon. The present model requires no extra parameters except for those already fixed by the previous studies for the nucleon. Our results are consistent with the experimental data in the high Q2 region, and those from lattice QCD. We also estimate the meson cloud contributions, focusing on the low Q2 region, where they are expected to be dominant.

## Key claim
Using a covariant spectator quark model with no new adjustable parameters, the authors estimate that valence quark contributions dominate the $\gamma N \to P_{11}(1440)$ transition form factors at high $Q^2$ ($> 1.5$ GeV$^2$), while meson cloud contributions are significant at low $Q^2$, particularly at the photon point $Q^2=0$.

## Method
The authors employ a covariant spectator quark model where baryons are described as a quark-diquark system with the diquark acting as a spectator. The Roper resonance $P_{11}(1440)$ is treated as the first radial excitation of the nucleon, with its scalar wave function determined by an orthogonality condition relative to the nucleon state. The transition form factors $F_1^\ast(Q^2)$ and $F_2^\ast(Q^2)$ are computed using a relativistic impulse approximation, with the quark current parameterized by Dirac and Pauli form factors fixed from previous nucleon studies.

## Key numbers
- The Roper wave function parameters are $\beta_1=0.049$, $\beta_2=0.717$, $\beta_3=0.130$, and normalization $N_R=3.35$, all determined from the nucleon parameters and the orthogonality condition.
- The model shows excellent agreement with CLAS data in the region $Q^2 > 1.5$ GeV$^2$ for both $F_1^\ast$ and $F_2^\ast$ form factors.
- For the lattice regime with pion mass $m_\pi = 720$ MeV, the nucleon mass is $M=1.48$ GeV, Roper mass $M_R=2.53$ GeV, and $\rho$ meson mass $m_\rho=1.083$ GeV.
- The meson cloud contributions at $Q^2=0$ are estimated as $A_{1/2}^{mc}(0)=(-61.4\pm 4.9)\times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ and $S_{1/2}^{mc}(0)=(-40.2\pm 2.6)\times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$.
- The model predicts $F_2^\ast(0)=0$ as a consequence of the orthogonality condition between nucleon and Roper states.

## Context
This work extends the covariant spectator quark model previously applied to nucleon and $\Delta$ baryon ground states (Ramalho and Tsushima, 2009) to the Roper resonance, which has been a long-standing puzzle in quark models. The results support the interpretation of the Roper as the first radial excitation of the nucleon, consistent with analyses by Aznauryan (2007) and Burkert (2008). The model enables separation of valence quark and meson cloud contributions, providing a framework that can be compared with dynamical coupled-channel models such as those by Julia-Diaz (2008, 2009) and lattice QCD data from Lin (2009). Future work can extend this approach to higher mass resonances like the $S_{11}(1535)$.
