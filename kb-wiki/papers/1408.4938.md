# $D$-mesons propagation in hadronic matter and consequences on heavy-flavor observables in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We employ recently published cross sections for $D$-mesons with hadrons and calculate the drag and diffusion coefficients of $D$-mesons in hadronic matter as a function of the momentum of $D$-mesons as well as of the temperature of the medium. Calculating in our approach the spatial diffusion coefficient, $D_x$, at zero chemical potential we see a very smooth transition between our calculations for the hadron gas and the lattice QCD calculations. Applying the results for the transport coefficients of $D$-mesons in a Fokker-Planck equation, which describes the evolution of $D$-mesons during the expansion of a hadron gas created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, we find that the value of $R_{AA}$ is little influenced by hadronic rescattering, whereas in the elliptic flow the effects are stronger. We extend our calculations to the finite chemical potentials and calculate the spatial diffusion coefficients of $D$-mesons propagating through the hadronic medium following isentropic trajectories, appropriate at future FAIR and NICA heavy-ion experiments. For the isentropic trajectory with $s/ρ_B^{\rm net}=20$ we find a perfect matching of results for $D$-mesons in hadronic matter and for charm quarks in partonic matter treated within the DQPM approach.

## Key claim

The paper claims that hadronic rescattering of D-mesons has a negligible effect on the nuclear modification factor R_AA (due to compensation between extra cooling and extra radial flow) but produces a systematic increase of 1%-2% in the elliptic flow v_2 of D-mesons and decay electrons, with the caveat that these results depend on the specific Fokker-Planck implementation (scenario I vs scenario II) used to model the hadronic phase.

## Method

The authors calculated drag and diffusion coefficients for D-mesons in hadronic matter using recently published cross sections for D-meson scattering with light mesons, strange mesons, nucleons, and baryon resonances, implementing these coefficients into a Fokker-Planck equation. They used the MC@sHQ Monte Carlo propagation model with a 2+1D hydrodynamically expanding ideal plasma to simulate heavy-flavor observables at RHIC energies (sqrt(s)=200 GeV), and extended the calculations to finite chemical potentials along isentropic trajectories appropriate for future FAIR and NICA experiments.

## Key numbers

- The thermal relaxation rate of D-mesons at the critical temperature T_c=165 MeV yields a relaxation time tau_R approximately equal to 10 fm/c
- At the chemical freeze-out temperature T_ch=170 MeV, the deviation from the Einstein relation between drag and diffusion coefficients is of order 25%
- The hadronic rescattering produces a systematic increase of 1%-2% to the v_2 of D-mesons and of decay electrons
- For the isentropic trajectory with s/rho_B^net=20, there is a perfect matching of results for D-mesons in hadronic matter and for charm quarks in partonic matter treated within the DQPM approach
- The specific entropy at top RHIC energy is S/N_B^net=250, with baryon chemical potential mu_B^ch=28.3 MeV at chemical freeze-out

## Context

This work extends the model of Ref. [diff_hadron_unitarized_3] by incorporating a more complete hadronic cocktail with effective chemical potentials to conserve particle ratios during the fireball expansion, and implements the resulting transport coefficients into the MC@sHQ transport model previously developed by Gossiaux and Aichelin. The results show a smooth transition between the hadron gas calculations and lattice QCD results for the spatial diffusion coefficient at zero chemical potential, and enable improved predictions for heavy-flavor observables at RHIC as well as predictions for future FAIR and NICA experiments where finite baryon chemical potentials are important.
