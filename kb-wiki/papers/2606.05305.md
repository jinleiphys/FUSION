# Gauge field flow for chiral gauge theories on a disk boundary

> A recent non-perturbative formulation of $2n$ dimensional chiral gauge theories relies on realizing chiral fermions on the $2n$ dimensional boundary of a $2n+1$ dimensional disk manifold. It also requires extending boundary gauge configurations into the interior of the disk using some flow prescription that preserves 2n dimensional gauge invariance. In this paper we propose a concrete realization of the equation of motion flow with the disk embedded on a square lattice. In addition, we couple the flow gauge field to fermions and demonstrate the mechanism of anomaly inflow and anmaly cancellation at work on the lattice.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a concrete lattice implementation of the "equation of motion flow" that extends two-dimensional gauge field configurations into the interior of a disk-shaped extra dimension, but notes that the equation of motion alone does not yield a unique flow prescription and requires the additional condition that the gauge field be a minimum of the higher-dimensional gauge action.

## Method

The authors formulate the gauge field flow on a square lattice by first deforming the disk boundary into a square, then solving the lattice version of Maxwell's equations (the equation of motion) for gauge links inside the interior region while imposing the condition that the radial component of the gauge field vanishes. To select the minimum of the gauge action, they introduce an auxiliary gradient flow in an extra imaginary time direction, evolving the gauge links according to the derivative of the plaquette action until the equations of motion are satisfied. For a specific boundary gauge configuration, they couple the resulting flow gauge field to Wilson fermions and compute current densities to demonstrate anomaly inflow and anomaly cancellation on the lattice.

## Key numbers

* The boundary gauge field configuration used is \(A_\phi = \frac{L_t}{2\pi}E_0 \cos(\omega t)\) with \(\omega = \frac{2\pi}{L_t}\), where \(L_t\) is the length of the compact time direction.
* The fermion mass defect is set to \(m(r) = +m_0\) for \(r < R\) and \(m(r) = -m_0\) for \(r > R\), with \(m_0 > 0\) at the cutoff scale.
* The support of the chiral mode is defined as the region \(R-\Delta\) to \(R+\Delta\) where \(98\%\) of the chiral mode charge density lies.
* The interior region where the EOM is solved is a square of lattice size \((2l+1) \times (2l+1)\), with the example shown for \(l=1\) on an \(8 \times 8\) lattice.

## Context

This work extends the recent non-perturbative formulation of chiral gauge theories by Kaplan (2023) and Kaplan (2024), which uses a disk geometry with a mass defect to realize unpaired Weyl fermions on a finite lattice. It provides a concrete lattice implementation of the EOM flow proposed in Kaplan (2024), building on earlier attempts using gradient flow for domain wall fermions on a slab geometry by Grabowska (2015) and PaperA. The demonstration of anomaly inflow and cancellation for the \(3-4-5-0\) model on the lattice enables future Monte-Carlo studies of chiral gauge theories using this extra-dimensional approach.
