# Far infrared giant dipole resonances in neutral quantum dots

> A resonance behaviour of the far infrared absorption probability at a frequency \sim N^{1/4} is predicted for clusters of N electron-hole pairs (2\le N\le 110) confined in disk-shaped quantum dots. For radially symmetric dots, the absorption is dominated by a Giant Dipole Resonance, which accounts for more than 98 % of the energy-weighted photoabsorption sum rule.

# Far infrared giant dipole resonances in neutral quantum dots

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the far infrared absorption spectrum of neutral electron-hole clusters confined in disk-shaped quantum dots is dominated by a Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) that accounts for more than 98% of the energy-weighted photoabsorption sum rule, but this result is obtained under a highly simplified model assuming equal electron and hole masses and ideal parabolic confinement, with the authors noting that the equal-mass simplification is "non realistic" and justified only by the absence of experimental results.

## Method

The authors model N electron-hole pairs (2 ≤ N ≤ 110) confined in a quasi-planar quantum dot with ideal parabolic lateral confinement, using a two-band model with equal electron and hole masses and neglecting electron-hole exchange. They apply the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) to compute excitation energies and dipole transition strengths, solving self-consistent Hartree-Fock equations with 272 harmonic oscillator orbitals (16 shells) to obtain single-particle states, then constructing and diagonalizing the RPA matrices to identify collective GDR states.

## Key numbers

- The GDR excitation energy follows the approximate dependence ΔE_gdr − 1 ≈ 0.7 + 0.4 N^{1/4} at β = 1 (in oscillator units), where β is the dimensionless Coulomb coupling parameter
- For GaAs parameters with density ρ ≈ 5 × 10^{11} pairs/cm², the GDR excitation energy is ΔE_gdr ≈ 4.5 meV
- The critical coupling parameter for the strong-confinement regime is β_c ≈ 0.58 N^{1/4}, corresponding to a critical density ρ_c ≈ 4/(π a_B²) ≈ 5 × 10^{11} pairs/cm² for GaAs (Bohr radius a_B ≈ 14 nm)
- The GDR dipole matrix elements squared scale as D² ≈ 0.17 N at β = 1
- The GDR accounts for more than 98% of the energy-weighted sum rule for all systems studied, with a slight violation (fraction = 1.01) for N = 110 at β = 1 attributed to basis truncation

## Context

This work extends the concept of giant dipole resonances from nuclear physics (de Shalit and Feschbach, 1974; Goldhaber and Teller, 1948) and atomic clusters (Connerade, 1996; Bertsch and Broglia, 1994) to semiconductor quantum dots containing multiple electron-hole pairs. The predicted GDR is the finite two-dimensional analogue of the out-of-phase plasma frequency in a two-component plasma (Pines and Schrieffer, 1961). The results enable experimental investigation using optical detection of FIR resonances (ODR) techniques (Cerne et al., 1996; Salib et al., 1996) on single quantum dots, though the authors note that such experiments on dense excitonic systems have not yet been performed. The work is limited to the strong-confinement regime (β < 0.6 N^{1/4}), and the authors suggest that future calculations should incorporate BCS pairing effects through a quasiparticle RPA scheme.
