# Influence of the tetraneutron on the EoS under core-collapse supernovae and heavy-ion collisions conditions

> Recently, a resonant state of four neutrons (tetraneutron) with an energy of $E_{4n}=2.37\pm 0.38 \rm{(stat)} \pm 0.44 \rm{(sys)}$ MeV and a width of $Γ=1.75\pm 0.22 \rm{(stat)} \pm 0.30 \rm{(sys)}$ MeV was reported. In this work, we analyse the effect of including such an exotic state on the yields of other light clusters, that not only form in astrophysical sites, such as core-collapse supernovae and neutron star mergers, but also in heavy-ion collisions. To this aim, we use a relativistic mean-field formalism, where we consider in-medium effects in a two-fold way, via the couplings of the clusters to the mesons, and via a binding energy shift, to compute the low-density equation of state for nuclear matter at finite temperature and fixed proton fraction. We consider five light clusters, deuterons, tritons, heliums, $α$-particles, and $^6$He, immersed in a gas of protons and neutrons, and we calculate their abundances and chemical equilibrium constants with and without the tetraneutron. We also analyse how the associated energy of the tetraneutron would influence such results. We find that the low-temperature, neutron-rich systems, are the ones most affected by the presence of the tetraneutron, making neutron stars excellent environments for their formation. Moreover, its presence in strongly asymmetric matter may increase considerably the proton and the $α$-particle fractions. This may have an influence on the dissolution of the accretion disk of the merger of two neutron stars.

## Key claim

The inclusion of a tetraneutron resonant state (with energy E₄ₙ = 2.37 ± 0.38(stat) ± 0.44(sys) MeV and width Γ = 1.75 ± 0.22(stat) ± 0.30(sys) MeV) in relativistic mean-field calculations of hot, low-density nuclear matter increases proton and alpha-particle fractions in neutron-rich systems at low temperatures, but the effect is strongly conditional on the proton fraction and temperature, being negligible in symmetric matter or at high temperatures.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic mean-field formalism with the FSU interaction, treating five light clusters (deuterons, tritons, helions, alpha particles, and ⁶He) plus the tetraneutron as point-like particles immersed in a nucleon gas. In-medium effects were included via two mechanisms: scalar cluster-meson couplings (with factors xₛ = 0.85 or 0.92, calibrated to Virial EoS and INDRA heavy-ion collision data respectively) and a binding energy shift constructed à la Thomas-Fermi that prevents double-counting and enforces cluster dissolution. They solved for cluster abundances and chemical equilibrium constants at fixed temperatures (4, 10, and 20 MeV) and proton fractions (yₚ = 0.1 and 0.3), comparing calculations with and without the tetraneutron.

## Key numbers

- Tetraneutron resonant energy: E₄ₙ = 2.37 ± 0.38 (stat) ± 0.44 (sys) MeV, with width Γ = 1.75 ± 0.22 (stat) ± 0.30 (sys) MeV, from the RIKEN SAMURAI experiment (Duer 2022).
- For T = 4 MeV, yₚ = 0.1, and xₛ = 0.92: inclusion of the tetraneutron increases the proton fraction by more than 500% and the alpha-particle fraction by approximately 300% at a density of about 0.02 fm⁻³.
- For T = 10 MeV, yₚ = 0.1: the proton fraction increase is about 100% and the alpha fraction increase is about 33%.
- For T = 5 MeV, yₚ = 0.41 (symmetric matter typical of heavy-ion collisions): the effect of tetraneutron inclusion on chemical equilibrium constants is below 2%, making it difficult to detect.
- Cluster fraction maxima shift from approximately 0.01 fm⁻³ at T = 4 MeV to approximately 0.05 fm⁻³ at T = 20 MeV; all clusters dissolve below 0.1 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of light cluster formation in astrophysical environments, particularly the zero-temperature, beta-equilibrium study of tetraneutrons by Ivanytskyi (2019) which used a different formalism including Bose-Einstein condensates and a Van der Waals excluded-volume approach. The present work uses a relativistic mean-field model previously calibrated to Virial EoS data (Pais 2018) and to INDRA heavy-ion collision data (Pais 2020, Pais 2021), and considers finite temperatures above the condensation critical temperature. The results suggest that tetraneutrons could significantly affect transport properties and the dissolution of accretion disks in neutron star mergers (as discussed by Rosswog 2015), but their detection in current heavy-ion collisions is unlikely due to the symmetry of those systems.
