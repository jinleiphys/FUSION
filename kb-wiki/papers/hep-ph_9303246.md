# Production of $η$ Mesons Near Threshold

> It is argued that the strong energy dependence of the $p\, d \rightarrow \,^{3\!}H\!e\, η$ cross section near threshold is due to a final state interaction between the $η$ meson and the $^3$He nucleus. The large scattering length that this implies is in accord with optical potential predictions and is evidence for a nearby virtual `$\!$`bound'$\!$' state in the $η^3$He system. This model suggests that sharp structures should also be seen close to production thresholds on other light nuclei.

# Production of η Mesons Near Threshold

## Key claim
The strong energy dependence of the p d → ³He η cross section near threshold is due to a final state interaction between the η meson and the ³He nucleus, implying a large scattering length that suggests a nearby virtual "bound" state in the η-³He system, though the large imaginary component of the scattering length severely limits observation of effects from this pole.

## Method
The author analyzes the energy dependence of the p d → ³He η cross section using a final-state-interaction (FSI) model with a constant scattering length approximation, extracting the η-³He scattering length from fits to the SPES2 data. An optical potential approach using the η-nucleon scattering length derived from π⁻ p → η n data is then employed to predict the η-³He scattering length, which is compared to the FSI fits.

## Key numbers
- The squared amplitude |f_η|² decreases by over a factor of three between threshold and p_η = 0.35 fm⁻¹ (ΔT_p = 10 MeV).
- The angular distribution parameter |α_η| ≤ 0.05 for p_η ≤ 0.38 fm⁻¹, indicating near-isotropy.
- The η-nucleon scattering length extracted from π⁻ p → η n data is a(η N) = (0.55 ± 0.20 + i0.30) fm.
- The optical potential prediction for the η-³He scattering length is a(η ³He) = (-2.31 + i2.57) fm.
- Two FSI fits to the data give (a_R, a_I) = (3.10, 2.52) fm and (5.0, 0.17) fm.
- The predicted η-⁴He scattering length is (-2.00 + i0.97) fm, with a decrease in |f|² between p_η = 0.1 and 0.4 fm⁻¹ of about 2.8 for ³He but only 1.9 for ⁴He.
- The predicted η-⁷Be scattering length is (-2.92 + i1.21) fm.
- The threshold π⁻ p → η n cross section requires Im[a(η N)] ≥ (0.28 ± 0.04) fm.

## Context
This work extends the spectator-nucleon model of Germond and Wilkin (1990) for π⁰ production, which underestimates the low-energy p d → ³He η cross section, and the coupled-channel model of Laget and Lecolley (1991) which included η scattering on up to three nucleons but gave too shallow a falloff. The analysis supports the earlier optical potential predictions of Bhalerao and Liu (1985) for η-nucleon scattering and suggests that the S₁₁ N*(1535) resonance is responsible for the strong S-wave η-nucleus interaction. The results enable predictions for near-threshold η production on other light nuclei (d, ⁴He, ⁷Be) and motivate specific experimental measurements at facilities like Celsius and Saturne.
