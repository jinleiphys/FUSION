# Interplay between low-lying isoscalar and isovector dipole modes: a comparative analysis between semi-classical and quantum approaches

> We perform Time Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations to investigate the small amplitude dipole response of selected neutron-rich nuclei and Sn isotopes. A detailed comparison with the dipole strength predicted by Random-Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations is presented for the first time. TDHF results are also confronted to Vlasov calculations, to explore up to which extent a semi-classical picture can explain the properties of the nuclear response. The focus is on the low-energy response, below the Giant Dipole Resonance region, where different modes of non negligible strength are identified. We show that the relative weight of these excitations evolves with nuclear global features, such as density profile and neutron skin, which in turn reflect impor tant properties of the nuclear effective interaction. A thorough analysis of the associated transition densities turns out to be quite useful to better characterize the mixed isoscalar(IS)/isovector(IV) nature of the different modes and their surface/volume components. In particular, we show that the dipole response in the so-called Pygmy Dipole Resonance region corresponds to isoscalar-like surface oscillations, of larger strength in nuclei with a more diffuse surface. The ratio between the IV and IS Energy Weighted Sum Rule fractions exhausted in this region is shown to almost linearly increase with the neutron skin thickness in Sn isotopes.

## Key claim
The low-energy dipole response in neutron-rich nuclei, often called the Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR), corresponds to isoscalar-like surface oscillations whose strength increases in nuclei with a more diffuse surface, and the ratio of isovector to isoscalar Energy Weighted Sum Rule fractions in this region increases almost linearly with neutron skin thickness in Sn isotopes.

## Method
The authors performed Time Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations in the small-amplitude limit for selected neutron-rich nuclei and Sn isotopes, comparing the results directly with fully self-consistent Random-Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations for the first time. They also confronted TDHF results with semi-classical Vlasov calculations to assess how well a classical picture reproduces the nuclear response. The analysis focused on transition densities and the isoscalar/isovector character of the low-energy dipole modes below the Giant Dipole Resonance region, using three SAMi-J Skyrme interactions with different symmetry energy slopes (J = 27, 31, 35 MeV).

## Key numbers
- The SAMi-J interactions have symmetry energy at saturation J = 27, 31, and 35 MeV, with corresponding slope parameters L = 29.9, 74.5, and 115.2 MeV, respectively.
- The symmetry energy coefficient at the average density of medium-size nuclei (rho_c = 0.6 rho_0) is approximately 22 MeV for all three SAMi-J parametrizations.
- The incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter for the SAMi-J interactions is K = 245 MeV.
- The HF calculations used a mesh size dx = 0.8 fm and time step dt_0 = 0.36 fm/c; RPA calculations used a radial mesh dr = 0.1 fm with a single-particle energy cutoff of 120 MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the Pygmy Dipole Resonance in neutron-rich nuclei (Paar et al., 2005; Savran et al., 2011; Crespi et al., 2014) by providing the first detailed comparison between TDHF and RPA calculations, verifying their analytical equivalence in the small-amplitude limit. The analysis builds on earlier semi-classical Vlasov studies (Brink, 1986; Burgio, 1988) and connects the dipole response to macroscopic nuclear properties such as density profile and neutron skin (Roca-Maza et al., 2015). The results enable a more direct link between the isoscalar/isovector character of low-energy dipole modes and the symmetry energy of the nuclear effective interaction, with implications for heavy-ion reactions and nuclear astrophysics.
