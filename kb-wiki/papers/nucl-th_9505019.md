# Initial State Interactions for $K^-$-Proton Radiative Capture

> The effects of the initial state interactions on the $K^--p$ radiative capture branching ratios are examined and found to be quite sizable. A general coupled-channel formalism for both strong and electromagnetic channels using a particle basis is presented, and applied to all the low energy $K^--p$ data with the exception of the {\it 1s} atomic level shift. Satisfactory fits are obtained using vertex coupling constants for the electromagnetic channels that are close to their expected SU(3) values.

# Initial State Interactions for $K^-$-Proton Radiative Capture

## Key claim

The initial state interactions (ISI) in the coupled hadronic channels have a sizable effect on the $K^-p$ radiative capture branching ratios, changing $R_{\Lambda\gamma}$ by roughly a factor of 2 and $R_{\Sigma\gamma}$ by more than a factor of 10; however, satisfactory fits to all low-energy data except the $1s$ atomic level shift can be obtained using vertex coupling constants close to their expected SU(3) values only when the ISI are included from a hadronic potential that also fits the strong-channel data.

## Method

A coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equation with separable potentials was used to describe the strong interactions among the $K^-p$, $\overline{K}^0n$, $\pi^+\Sigma^-$, $\pi^0\Sigma^0$, $\pi^-\Sigma^+$, and $\pi^0\Lambda$ channels. The radiative capture amplitudes to $\Lambda\gamma$ and $\Sigma^0\gamma$ were derived from first-order Born photoproduction diagrams including $\Sigma^0$ exchange and $K^*$ exchange terms, with the initial state interactions encoded in three complex amplitudes $A_1$, $A_3$, and $A_5$ (for the charged channels $\pi^+\Sigma^-$, $\pi^-\Sigma^+$, and $K^-p$) obtained from the strong-interaction fit. Two potentials were examined: one guided by SU(3) symmetry for relative channel couplings (fitting all low-energy data except the $1s$ atomic level shift), and one from Tanaka and Suzuki (1994) that fits the atomic level shift as well.

## Key numbers

- Measured branching ratio $R_{\Lambda\gamma} = \Gamma(K^-p \rightarrow \Lambda\gamma) / \Gamma(K^-p \rightarrow \text{all}) = 0.86 \pm 0.07 \pm 0.09 \times 10^{-3}$ (Brookhaven, stopped kaons)
- Measured branching ratio $R_{\Sigma\gamma} = \Gamma(K^-p \rightarrow \Sigma^0\gamma) / \Gamma(K^-p \rightarrow \text{all}) = 1.44 \pm 0.20 \pm 0.11 \times 10^{-3}$ (Brookhaven, stopped kaons)
- Best-fit values (with $g_{Kp\Lambda}$ varied $\pm 20\%$, other parameters $\pm 50\%$): $R_{\Lambda\gamma} = 1.09 \times 10^{-3}$, $R_{\Sigma\gamma} = 1.55 \times 10^{-3}$, $\chi^2/N = 1.76$
- Without initial state interactions: $R_{\Lambda\gamma} = 0.56 \times 10^{-3}$, $R_{\Sigma\gamma} = 0.12 \times 10^{-3}$
- Best-fit coupling constants: $g_{KN\Lambda} = -10.6$, $g_{KN\Sigma} = 5.8$, $g_{\pi\Sigma\Sigma} = -7.2$, $g_{\pi\Sigma\Lambda} = -5.0$
- Hadronic branching ratios from best fit: $\gamma = 2.31$, $R_c = 0.661$, $R_n = 0.164$
- $K^-p$ scattering length from best fit: $(-0.63 + 0.76i)$ fm

## Context

This work extends earlier phenomenological analyses of the $K^-p$ system (e.g., Dalitz and Tuan, 1960; Martin, 1981) by including both hadronic and electromagnetic branching ratios within a single coupled-channel model, addressing the discrepancy between earlier calculations that overestimated $R_{\Lambda\gamma}$ by a factor of 3-4. The results support the interpretation of the $\Lambda(1405)$ as a quasi-bound $\overline{K}N$ ($\Sigma\pi$) state rather than a pure $q^3$ state, consistent with the cloudy bag model approach of Veit et al. (1985) which also included ISI. The formalism enables future experimental investigations at DA$\Phi$NE, Brookhaven, and KEK to clarify the nature of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance through precise measurements of the radiative capture branching ratios near threshold.
