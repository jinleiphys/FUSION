# Meson cloud effects on kaon quark distribution functions and the SU(3) flavor symmetry

> We present our analysis on the valence quark distribution functions of the $K^{+}$ meson, $v^{K (u)} (x, Q^2)$ and $v^{K (\bar{s})} (x, Q^2)$, taking into account the meson cloud effects in the framework of the chiral constituent quark model. Assuming appropriate bare quark distribution functions at the initial scale, evaluating the Goldstone boson dressing corrections with the model, and performing the QCD evolution, one can obtain the realistic dressed distributions at a certain scale. The phenomenologically satisfactory valence quark distribution of the pion was obtained in the previous study and there are available experimental data of the valence $u$ quark distribution ratio $v^{K(u)} (x, Q^2) / v^{π(u)} (x, Q^2)$, which enable us to obtain the kaon quark distribution functions. Besides presenting the resulting dressed distributions, we also show how the meson cloud effects affect the bare distributions in detail. A striking result is that the observed size of the SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking is considerably smaller than those seen in the preceding works based on other approaches.

## Key claim

The SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking observed in the valence quark distributions of the kaon is considerably smaller than that seen in preceding works based on other approaches, such as the Dyson-Schwinger equation study of Chen (2016), when the meson cloud effects are taken into account in the chiral constituent quark model.

## Method

The authors assume functional forms for the bare valence quark distributions of the kaon at an initial scale \(Q_0^2\), containing two adjustable parameters \(\beta\) and \(\gamma\). They evaluate the Goldstone boson dressing corrections using the chiral constituent quark model with an effective Lagrangian and a Gaussian cutoff, then perform NLO DGLAP QCD evolution to a higher scale. The parameters are determined by fitting the calculated ratio \(v^{K(u)}(x, Q^2) / v^{\pi(u)}(x, Q^2)\) to experimental data from Badier (1980) at \(Q^2 = 27\) GeV\(^2\), using the previously determined pion valence distribution from Watanabe (2016) as input.

## Key numbers

* The best-fit parameters for the bare kaon distributions are \(\beta = 1.84 \pm 0.27\) and \(\gamma = 2.01 \pm 0.19\).
* At \(Q^2 = 27\) GeV\(^2\), the first three moments of the dressed valence \(u\) quark distribution are \(\langle x \rangle = 0.23\), \(\langle x^2 \rangle = 0.091\), and \(\langle x^3 \rangle = 0.045\).
* At \(Q^2 = 27\) GeV\(^2\), the first three moments of the dressed valence \(\bar{s}\) quark distribution are \(\langle x \rangle = 0.24\), \(\langle x^2 \rangle = 0.096\), and \(\langle x^3 \rangle = 0.049\).
* For comparison, the Dyson-Schwinger equation study by Chen (2016) gives \(\langle x \rangle = 0.28\) for \(v^{K(u)}\) and \(\langle x \rangle = 0.36\) for \(v^{K(\bar{s})}\) at the same scale.
* There is an overall 33% reduction in the valence \(u\) quark distribution at large \(x\) at the initial scale due to the renormalization constant.

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model approach previously applied to the nucleon by Suzuki (1997) and to the pion by Watanabe (2016). The results contradict the larger SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking seen in the Dyson-Schwinger equation based study of Chen (2016). The predictions can be tested by future kaon-induced Drell-Yan experiments at facilities such as COMPASS at CERN and J-PARC.
