# Explicitly correlated Gaussians with tensor pre-factors: analytic matrix elements

> We consider a specific form of explicitly correlated Gaussians -- with tensor pre-factors -- which appear naturally when dealing with certain few-body systems in nuclear and particle physics. We derive analytic matrix elements with these Gaussians -- overlap, kinetic energy, and Coulomb potential -- to be used in variational calculations of those systems. We also perform a quick test of the derived formulae by applying them to p- and d-waves of the hydrogen atom.

## Key claim
The paper derives analytic matrix elements (overlap, kinetic energy, Coulomb potential) for explicitly correlated Gaussians with tensor pre-factors of rank 1 and rank 2, which are useful for variational calculations of few-body systems in nuclear and particle physics where operators such as vector and tensor couplings appear. The authors note that the rank-2 Gaussian generally contains both s-waves and d-waves, but the condition that the polarization vectors are orthogonal eliminates the s-wave contribution.

## Method
The authors start from known analytic matrix elements for shifted correlated Gaussians and perform Taylor expansions with respect to the shift vectors, collecting terms of specific orders to obtain matrix elements for Gaussians with rank-1 (vector) and rank-2 (tensor) pre-factors. They then test the derived formulae by applying them to a variational calculation of the hydrogen atom, solving the generalized eigenvalue problem for s-, p-, and d-waves using a basis of up to 5 Gaussians with optimized width parameters.

## Key numbers
- The exact hydrogen atom energies (in Hartree units) are: \(E_{l=0} = -\frac12\), \(E_{l=1} = -\frac18\), \(E_{l=2} = -\frac1{18}\).
- With a basis of 5 Gaussians, the exact energies are reproduced within 4 decimal digits.
- The overlap of two rank-0 Gaussians is \(M_0 = \left( \frac{\pi^{n}}{\det(A+B)} \right)^{3/2}\).
- The rank-1 overlap is \(M_1 = \frac12 (\ve b^\T R \ve a) M_0\) where \(R = (A+B)^{-1}\).
- The rank-2 overlap is \(M_2 = \frac14 \left[ (\ve a^\T R\ve b)(\ve c^\T R\ve d) + (\ve a^\T R\ve c)(\ve b^\T R\ve d) + (\ve a^\T R\ve d)(\ve b^\T R\ve c) \right] M_0\).

## Context
This work extends the literature on explicitly correlated Gaussians, which are a popular basis for variational few-body calculations (reviewed in Hiyama (2003) and the book by Suzuki and Varga). It provides an alternative to Gaussians with spherical harmonic pre-factors (derived in the angular paper) that is more natural for nuclear and particle physics applications involving pion operators such as \(\vec\sigma\vec r\) and \(\vec\sigma\vec p\) (used in work by Akaishi, the gamma-pi paper, and the Roper resonance paper). The derived analytic formulae enable variational calculations of few-body systems with tensor interactions without numerical integration, and the symmetrization rules provided allow treatment of identical particles.
