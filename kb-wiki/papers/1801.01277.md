# A remark on the sign change of the four-particle azimuthal cumulant in small systems

> The azimuthal cumulants, $c_2\{2\}$ and $c_2\{4\}$, originating from the global conservation of transverse momentum in the presence of hydro-like elliptic flow are calculated. We observe the sign change of $c_2\{4\}$ for small number of produced particles. This is in a qualitative agreement with the recent ATLAS measurement of multi-particle azimuthal correlations with the subevent cumulant method.

## Key claim

The sign change of the four-particle azimuthal cumulant \(c_2\{4\}\) at low multiplicity can be explained by the interplay between transverse momentum conservation (TMC) and hydro-like elliptic flow, without invoking new physics; the positive TMC contribution dominates at small \(N\) while the negative \((v_2)^4\) term dominates at large \(N\).

## Method

The authors analytically calculated the two-particle and four-particle azimuthal cumulants \(c_2\{2\}\) and \(c_2\{4\}\) from a single-particle distribution with elliptic flow \(v_2(p)\), subject to global conservation of transverse momentum. They expanded the exponential factor in the multi-particle distribution to second order (for two particles) and fourth order (for four particles) in the TMC term, then derived approximate closed-form expressions assuming \((v_2)^2 \ll 1\) and \((\bar{\bar{v}}_{2,F})^2 \ll 1\). The results were evaluated at fixed transverse momentum \(p_i = p\) for all particles.

## Key numbers

* \(c_2\{4\}\) changes sign as a function of the number of produced particles \(N\) when hydro-like elliptic flow is present; for \(v_2 = 0.05\), \(\bar{\bar{v}}_{2,F} = 0.025\), \(\langle p^2 \rangle_F = 0.5^2\), and \(p^2/\langle p^2 \rangle_F = 1.44, 1.96, 2.56\), the sign change occurs at \(N\) values visible in Fig. 1.
* For \(v_2 = \bar{\bar{v}}_{2,F} = 0\) (TMC only), \(c_2\{4\}\) remains positive for all \(N\) (Fig. 2).
* The leading TMC contribution to \(c_2\{2\}\) scales as \(p^4 / [2(N-2)^2 \langle p^2 \rangle_F^2]\) (Eq. 12).
* The leading TMC contribution to \(c_2\{4\}\) includes a term \(3p^8 / [2(N-4)^4 \langle p^2 \rangle_F^4]\) (Eq. 15).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation of TMC alone (Bzdak 2017), where \(c_2\{k\} \sim 1/N^k\) with positive sign for all \(k\). By adding hydro-like elliptic flow, they reproduce the sign change of \(c_2\{4\}\) observed by ATLAS (Aaboud 2017) using the three-subevent cumulant method in 13 TeV proton-proton collisions. The results offer a minimal, analytic explanation for the onset of collectivity in small systems, complementing final-state hydrodynamic models (Bozek 2011, Bzdak 2013) and initial-state approaches (Dumitru 2010, Dusling 2013).
