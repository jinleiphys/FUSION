# Excited hadrons on the lattice: Baryons

> We present results for masses of excited baryons from a quenched calculation with Chirally Improved quarks at pion masses down to 350 MeV. Our analysis of the correlators is based on the variational method. In order to provide a large basis set for spanning the physical states, we use interpolators with different Dirac structures and Jacobi smeared quark sources of different width. Our spectroscopy results for a wide range of ground state and excited baryons are discussed.

## Key claim
The quenched lattice calculation reproduces ground-state baryon masses reasonably well, but excited positive-parity baryon masses come out 20-25% above experimental values, likely due to missing chiral physics in the quenched approximation; negative-parity states are compatible with experiment within larger statistical errors.

## Method
The authors performed a quenched lattice QCD calculation using the Chirally Improved Dirac operator on two sets of lattices (20^3 x 32 at beta=8.15 and 16^3 x 32 at beta=7.90) with pion masses down to 350 MeV. They applied the variational method to a correlation matrix built from multiple interpolators that differ in Dirac structure and in the amount of Jacobi smearing (narrow vs. wide sources) applied to each quark, allowing the basis to span both ground and excited states. Masses were extracted from correlated fits to the eigenvalues of the generalized eigenvalue problem, with fit ranges determined by plateaus in both effective masses and eigenvectors.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacings: a = 0.119 fm (20^3 x 32, beta=8.15) and a = 0.148 fm (16^3 x 32, beta=7.90), giving spatial extent L ~ 2.4 fm for both
- Pion masses down to 350 MeV; quark masses in the range am = 0.02 to 0.2
- Narrow source width sigma ~ 0.27 fm; wide source width sigma ~ 0.41 fm
- Predicted masses for unclassified states: Omega positive parity first excited state 2300(70) MeV; Omega negative parity ground state 1970(90) MeV; Xi negative parity ground state 1780(90) MeV; Xi negative parity first excited state 1780(110) MeV
- Positive parity excited baryon masses are systematically 20-25% above experimental numbers

## Context
This work extends the BGR Collaboration's earlier test (Burch et al., 2004) and their meson analysis (Gattringer et al., 2004) to a comprehensive baryon spectroscopy using the variational method with multiple interpolators. The results for positive parity excited states are systematically higher than experiment, consistent with other quenched studies (e.g., those cited as "excitedspectro3" in the text), and the authors attribute this to missing chiral dynamics that would be present in full dynamical QCD. The finding that the negative parity Lambda(1405) is better described by the flavor-octet interpolator than the flavor-singlet one supports the interpretation of Lambda(1405) as having significant antikaon-nucleon molecular content, following the early work of Dalitz. The predictions for unclassified Omega and Xi states provide targets for future dynamical calculations and experimental searches.
