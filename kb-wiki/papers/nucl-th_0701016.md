# Critical point symmetries in boson-fermion systems. The case of shape transition in odd nuclei in a multi-orbit model

> We investigate phase transitions in boson-fermion systems. We propose an analytically solvable model (E(5/12)) to describe odd nuclei at the critical point in the transition from the spherical to $γ$-unstable behaviour. In the model, a boson core described within the Bohr Hamiltonian interacts with an unpaired particle assumed to be moving in the three single particle orbitals j=1/2,3/2,5/2. Energy spectra and electromagnetic transitions at the critical point compare well with the results obtained within the Interacting Boson Fermion Model, with a boson-fermion Hamiltonian that describes the same physical situation.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an analytically solvable model, called E(5/12), for odd nuclei at the critical point of the spherical to gamma-unstable shape transition, where the unpaired fermion occupies the three single-particle orbitals j=1/2, 3/2, and 5/2. The model extends the earlier E(5/4) single-j case to a multi-orbit scenario, and the authors claim that the energy spectra and electromagnetic transitions at the critical point compare well with results from the Interacting Boson Fermion Model (IBFM), though the E(5/12) states have exact quantum numbers that the IBFM Hamiltonian only approximately preserves.

## Method

The authors construct a Bohr Hamiltonian for a boson core coupled to a fermion moving in j=1/2, 3/2, 5/2 orbitals, using an infinite square well potential in the beta deformation variable to mimic the critical point. The Hamiltonian includes a five-dimensional scalar product term between boson and fermion orbital angular momenta and an additional fermion angular momentum squared term (absent in E(5/4)) to account for energy differences between the j=1/2 and j=3/2,5/2 orbits. The wave functions factorize into a beta-dependent Bessel function part and an angular part characterized by O(5) quantum numbers, with the pseudo-spin part contributing only degeneracies. For comparison, the authors perform IBFM calculations with N=7 bosons using a Hamiltonian designed to mimic the E(5/12) Casimir operator structure at the critical point x_crit = 20/27.

## Key numbers

- The critical point in the IBFM Hamiltonian occurs at x_crit = (4N-8)/(5N-8) = 20/27 ≈ 0.74 for N=7 bosons.
- Energy of the 1,2,0 (2,0) level: 2.20 (E(5/12)) vs 2.30 (IBFM), normalized to the first excited multiplet.
- Energy of the 2,0,0 (0,0) level: 3.03 (E(5/12)) vs 2.86 (IBFM), normalized to the first excited multiplet.
- Energy of the 1,0,1 (1,0) level: 2.20 (E(5/12)) vs 2.69 (IBFM), normalized to the first excited multiplet.
- Energy of the 1,1,1 (1,1) level: 3.00 (E(5/12)) vs 3.82 (IBFM), normalized to the first excited multiplet.
- The parameters k = -1/4 and k' = 5/2 are used in both the E(5/12) and IBFM Hamiltonians.
- All B(E2) values are normalized to 100 for the first 5/2 to 1/2 transition.

## Context

This work extends the E(5/4) model of Iachello (2001, 2003, 2005), which treated a single j=3/2 fermion coupled to a spherical-to-gamma-unstable core, to the more realistic multi-orbit case with j=1/2, 3/2, 5/2. The authors note that the restriction to a single-j orbit in E(5/4) made identification of a particular nucleus as critical difficult, whereas the multi-orbit case is likely to occur in mass regions around A=190 (odd-neutron Pt, odd-proton Ir) and A=130 (odd-neutron Ba). The model enables future searches for critical point symmetries in odd nuclei, building on the success of E(5) and X(5) symmetries in even-even nuclei, and provides analytic predictions that can be compared with IBFM calculations and experimental data.
