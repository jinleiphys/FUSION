# Open charm physics with Heavy Ions: theoretical overview

> The peculiar role of heavy-flavour observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is discussed. Produced in the early stage, $c$ and $b$ quarks cross the hot medium arising from the collision, interacting strongly with the latter, until they hadronize. Depending on the strength of the interaction heavy quarks may or not approach kinetic equilibrium with the plasma, tending in the first case to follow the collective flow of the expanding fireball. The presence of a hot deconfined medium may also affect heavy-quark hadronization, being possible for them to recombine with the surrounding light thermal partons, so that the final heavy-flavour hadrons inherit part of the flow of the medium. Here we show how it is possible to develop a complete transport setup allowing one to describe heavy-flavour production in high-energy nuclear collisions, displaying some major results one can obtain. Information coming from recent lattice-QCD simulations concerning both the heavy-flavour transport coefficients in the hot QCD plasma and the nature of the charmed degrees around the deconfinement transition is also presented. Finally, the possibility that the formation of a hot deconfined medium even in small systems (high-multiplicity p-Au and d-Au collisions, so far) may affect also heavy-flavour observables is investigated.

## Key claim
Heavy-flavour observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions provide a unique probe of the quark-gluon plasma, but the extent to which charm quarks approach kinetic equilibrium with the medium depends on the strength of their interaction; the data suggest that charm may not fully thermalize during the partonic phase, with in-medium hadronization through recombination with light thermal partons playing a crucial role in transferring collective flow to the final heavy-flavour hadrons.

## Method
The author develops a multi-step transport setup: initial heavy-quark pair production is simulated using pQCD event generators (POWHEG-BOX) validated against p-p data and supplemented with nuclear parton distribution functions; the fireball evolution is described by hydrodynamic calculations; heavy-quark propagation in the plasma is modeled via the Langevin equation, derived from the Fokker-Planck approximation of the Boltzmann equation, with transport coefficients from weak-coupling calculations and lattice-QCD; finally, hadronization is modeled either through vacuum fragmentation functions or through in-medium recombination with light thermal partons.

## Key numbers
- Charm quark mass set to \(m_c = 1.3\) GeV, with renormalization and factorization scales \(\mu_R = \mu_F = m_T\) in the POWHEG-BOX calculations
- The system reaches local thermal equilibrium within \(\tau_0 \lesssim 1\) fm/c and spends \(\sim 5-10\) fm/c in the QGP phase
- Langevin and Boltzmann equations provide identical results for beauty quarks, while for charm discrepancies are visible depending on medium parameters
- Lattice-QCD results for the momentum-diffusion coefficient \(\kappa\) of a static quark in a gluonic plasma show a large theoretical uncertainty band arising from extracting real-time information from imaginary-time correlators
- The nuclear modification factor \(R_{\rm AA}\) of \(D^0\) mesons in 0-10% Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV and the elliptic flow \(v_2\) in 30-50% Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76\) TeV are compared to STAR and ALICE data respectively
- In-medium hadronization via recombination with strange quarks produces a relative enhancement of \(D_s\) mesons compared to \(D\) mesons in central Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2.76\) TeV

## Context
This theoretical overview extends the line of work on heavy-flavour transport in heavy-ion collisions, building on prior approaches such as the FONLL calculation for inclusive heavy-quark spectra (Cacciari et al.) and the POWHEG-BOX event generator (Nason et al.). The author discusses results from lattice-QCD simulations on charm degrees of freedom around deconfinement (lat1, lat2) and compares transport calculations with experimental data from STAR and ALICE collaborations. The work enables future studies of heavy-flavour production in small systems (high-multiplicity p-Au and d-Au collisions) and aims to extract heavy-flavour transport coefficients in hot QCD, with beauty measurements at low \(p_T\) via exclusive \(B\)-meson hadronic decays identified as the most promising path forward.
