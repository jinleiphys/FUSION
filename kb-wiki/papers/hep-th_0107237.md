# Stability of bound states in the light-front Yukawa model

> We show that in the system of two fermions interacting by scalar exchange, the solutions for J$^π$=$0^+$ bound states are stable without any cutoff regularization, for values of the coupling constant $α$ below a critical value $α_c$. This latter is calculated from an eigenvalue equation.

# Stability of bound states in the light-front Yukawa model

## Key claim
The two-fermion system with scalar exchange in the light-front Yukawa model produces stable, cutoff-independent bound states for the Jπ=0+ channel, but only for coupling constants α below a critical value αc=3.72; above this value the integrals diverge logarithmically and the system collapses.

## Method
The authors solved the light-front bound-state equation for two fermions interacting via scalar exchange, reducing the problem to a one-channel equation for the spin component f1. They analyzed the asymptotic behavior of the kernel at large momenta, derived an eigenvalue equation for the coupling constant as a function of the asymptotic power β, and determined the critical coupling from the condition β(αc)=0. Numerical solutions were obtained for constituent mass m=1 and exchanged scalar mass μ=0.25, with the cutoff kmax varied to test convergence.

## Key numbers
- Critical coupling constant: αc = 3.72, obtained from the eigenvalue equation at β=0 with λc=0.269.
- For α=3 (below αc), the mass squared M2 converges as kmax → ∞.
- For α=4 (above αc), M2 decreases logarithmically with kmax and becomes negative.
- For binding energy B=0.05 (α=1.096), the measured asymptotic power β=0.820±0.002 agrees with the eigenvalue solution β=0.819.
- For B=0.5 (α=2.480), the measured β=0.548±0.002 agrees with the eigenvalue solution.

## Context
This work extends the study of relativistic bound states in the Yukawa model, previously investigated in standard light-front dynamics by Glazek (1999) and in the explicitly covariant light-front approach by Karmanov (1976) and Carbonell, Karmanov, and Mangin-Brinet (1998). The authors show that, contrary to the claim in Glazek (1999) that integrals diverge logarithmically with cutoff, the J=0+ state is stable for α below αc. The results are relevant for renormalization procedures in light-front dynamics and for constructing NN interactions that include scalar meson exchanges, such as the Bonn model.
