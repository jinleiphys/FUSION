# Chiral Condensate in Holographic QCD with Baryon Density

> We consider the chiral condensate in the baryonic dense medium using the generalized Sakai-Sugimoto model. It is defined as the vacuum expectation value of open Wilson line that is proposed to be calculated by use of the area of world-sheet instanton. We evaluate it in confined as well as deconfined phase. In both phases, the chiral condensate has a minimum as a function of baryon density. In the deconfined phase, taking into account the chiral symmetry restoration, we classify the behavior of chiral condensate into three types. One can set the parameter of the theory such that the results, in low but sufficiently higher density, is in agreement with the expectation from QCD.

## Key claim

The chiral condensate in the generalized Sakai-Sugimoto model, evaluated via the area of a world-sheet instanton, has a minimum as a function of baryon density in both confined and deconfined phases; in the deconfined phase, by tuning model parameters, the condensate can be made to decrease with density until it drops discontinuously to zero at the chiral transition point, consistent with a first-order transition.

## Method

The authors use the generalized Sakai-Sugimoto model, where flavor D8-branes and anti-D8-branes are embedded in a D4-brane background, with baryons realized as D4-branes wrapping an S^4. The chiral condensate is defined as the vacuum expectation value of an open Wilson line operator connecting left- and right-handed quarks, computed via the minimal area of a string world-sheet instanton bounded by the flavor branes. The system includes a uniform baryon density introduced via a U(1) gauge field on the D8-branes, and the force balance condition between the baryonic D4-branes and the flavor D8-branes determines the brane configuration. The regularized world-sheet area is evaluated numerically in both the confined and deconfined phases.

## Key numbers

- In the confined phase with fixed separation parameter $\tilde{\ell} = 1/2$, the regularized area $-G_{\text{conf}}$ has a minimum at $\rho_{\text{cr}} \approx 2.058$, where $\rho = d / u_{\text{KK}}^{5/2}$ is the rescaled baryon density parameter.
- Using experimental values $m_\rho \approx 776$ MeV and $f_\pi \approx 93$ MeV, the model yields $M_{\text{KK}} \approx 496$ MeV and $\lambda \approx 61.7$ for the non-antipodal model with $\tilde{\ell}=1/2$.
- The critical baryon number density at $\rho_{\text{cr}}$ is $n_{B,\text{cr}} \approx 1.76$ fm$^{-3}$, corresponding to $n_{B,\text{cr}} / n_0 \approx 11$ relative to normal nuclear density $n_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$.
- The critical density equals the normal nuclear density at $\tilde{\ell} \approx 1.022$.

## Context

This work extends the holographic approach to chiral symmetry breaking in dense baryonic matter, building on the Sakai-Sugimoto model (Sakai and Sugimoto, 2005) and its generalization by Bergman, Lifschytz, and Lippert (2007) to include baryon density via D4-branes. The method of using an open Wilson line for the chiral condensate follows Aharony and Kutasov (2008). The results contrast with the holographic NJL model studied by Edalati and Vazquez-Poritz (2009), which lacks confinement; here the compactified version with confinement is analyzed, enabling comparison with experimental hadron masses. The work enables future studies of the density dependence of hadron properties in neutron stars and nuclear matter within holographic QCD.
