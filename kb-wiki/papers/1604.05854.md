# Manifestation of Universality in the Asymmetric Helium Trimer and in the Halo Nucleus $^{22}$C

> We prove that the corner angle distributions in the bound three-body system AAB, which consists of two particles of type A and one particle of type B, approach universal form if the pair AA has a virtual state at zero energy and the binding energy of AAB goes to zero. We derive explicit expressions for the universal corner angle distributions in terms of elementary functions, which depend solely on the mass ratio m(A)/m(B) and do not depend on pair interactions. On the basis of experimental data and calculations we demonstrate that such systems as the asymmetric Helium trimer $^3$He$^4$He$_2$ and the halo nucleus $^{22}$C exhibit universal features. Thus our result establishes an interesting link between atomic and nuclear physics through the few-body universality.

## Key claim

The authors prove that corner angle distributions in the bound three-body system AAB approach a universal form when the pair AA has a virtual state at zero energy and the binding energy of AAB goes to zero, but only when the system approaches the critical point along the line where the AA coupling constant equals one (or along any line within the stable part of the stability diagram), and the convergence is logarithmically slow.

## Method

The authors derive explicit analytic expressions for universal corner angle distributions using residue calculus to evaluate integrals that arise from the asymptotic form of the three-body wave function at the critical point on the stability diagram. They compare these universal distributions to quantum Monte Carlo calculations and experimental data for the asymmetric helium trimer ³He⁴He₂, and to quantum Monte Carlo calculations for the halo nucleus ²²C modeled as a core plus two neutrons with Woods-Saxon and Gaussian potentials.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the asymmetric Helium trimer ³He⁴He₂: 1.23×10⁻⁶ eV
- Binding energy of the Helium dimer ⁴He₂: 1.14×10⁻⁷ eV
- For ²²C with core-neutron potential depth V₀ = 2.84 MeV: two-neutron separation energy S₂ₙ = 0.0052(4) MeV
- For ²²C: mean squared hyperradius ⟨ρ²⟩ = 250(12) fm², giving root mean square matter radius 4.4 fm
- For ²²C: neutron-neutron virtual state at energy about 143 keV above threshold
- Universal constant s₀ ≃ 1.00624 for the Efimov energy ratio Eₙ/Eₙ₊₁ = e^{2π/s₀}

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Efimov physics (Efimov 1970) to asymmetric three-body systems AAB, deriving universal corner angle distributions that depend only on mass ratio. It connects atomic physics (asymmetric helium trimers observed experimentally by Doerner and collaborators) with nuclear physics (the halo nucleus ²²C studied by Vaagen and collaborators, Horiuchi, and Tomio). The results enable future experiments combining laser pulse ionization techniques with traps to probe internal structures of trimers beyond helium, and provide a universal benchmark for identifying critical-point behavior in three-body systems.
