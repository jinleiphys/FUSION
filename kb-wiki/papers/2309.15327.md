# In search of beyond mean-field signatures in heavy-ion fusion reactions

> Examination of high-resolution, experimental fusion excitation functions for $^{16,17,18}$O + $^{12}$C reveals a remarkable irregular behavior that is rooted in the structure of both the colliding nuclei and the quasi-molecular composite system. The impact of the $\ell$-dependent fusion barriers is assessed using a time-dependent Hartree-Fock model. Barrier penetrabilities, taken directly from a density-constrained calculation, provide a significantly improved description of the experimental data as compared to the standard Hill-Wheeler approach. The remaining deviations between the parameter-free theoretical mean-field predictions and experimental fusion cross sections are exposed and discussed.

## Key claim

High-resolution fusion excitation functions for $^{16,17,18}$O + $^{12}$C reveal irregular oscillatory structures that can be largely explained by $\ell$-dependent fusion barriers, but a significant suppression of the fusion cross section for $^{17}$O at energies above 14 MeV remains unexplained by the parameter-free time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) model, even after improving barrier penetrability calculations.

## Method

The authors combined multiple high-resolution experimental datasets for $^{16,17,18}$O + $^{12}$C fusion reactions, including recent active-target measurements and prior thin-target measurements. They performed TDHF calculations on a three-dimensional Cartesian grid using the UNEDF1 energy density functional, simulating approximately 3000 individual trajectories across three systems to determine capture cross sections. To improve upon the standard Hill-Wheeler approximation for barrier penetrability, they developed a hybrid approach (TDHF$^*$) that extracts transmission probabilities directly from density-constrained TDHF potentials for each $\ell$-wave.

## Key numbers

- Fusion cross sections for $^{16}$O + $^{12}$C show prominent peaks at center-of-mass energies $\Ecm \approx 11$ MeV, 14 MeV, and 16.5 MeV
- For $^{17}$O + $^{12}$C, the TDHF$^*$ model significantly overestimates the measured cross section for $14 < \Ecm < 21$ MeV
- The collision energy was scanned in steps of 0.25 MeV across the reported energy range for all systems
- TDHF calculations were performed for angular momenta $8 < \ell \leq 20 \hbar$
- For $^{16}$O + $^{12}$C at $\Ecm < 14$ MeV, the TDHF$^*$ method provides a good description of the fusion excitation function

## Context

This work extends prior studies of oscillatory structure in fusion excitation functions for light systems such as $^{12}$C + $^{12}$C (Sperr 1976), $^{16}$O + $^{12}$C (Sperr 1976, Kovar 1979), and $^{16}$O + $^{16}$O (Fernandez 1978, Kolata 1979), where zigzag patterns were attributed to successive $\ell$-wave contributions (Esbensen 2012, Wong 2012, Simenel 2013, Rowley 2015). The TDHF$^*$ approach improves upon standard TDHF treatments (Simenel 2013, Rowley 2015) by using direct penetrability from DC-TDHF potentials rather than the Hill-Wheeler approximation. The remaining discrepancies, particularly for $^{17}$O, point to the need for beyond-mean-field effects such as breakup channels, neutron transfer, or transient cluster configurations, motivating future exclusive measurements and advanced theoretical frameworks.
