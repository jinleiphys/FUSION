# Application of random phase approximation to vibrational excitations of double-$Λ$ hypernuclei

> Using the Hartree-Fock plus random-phase-approximation (HF+RPA), we study the impurity effect of $Λ$ hyperon on the collective vibrational excitations of double-$Λ$ hypernuclei. To this end, we employ a Skyrme-type $ΛN$ and $ΛΛ$ interactions for the HF calculations, and the residual interactions for RPA derived with the same interactions. We find that inclusion of two $Λ$ hyperons in $^{16}$O shifts the energy of the collective states towards higher energies. In particular, the energy of the giant monopole resonance of $^{\,\,18}_{ΛΛ}$O, as well as that of $^{210}_{ΛΛ}$Pb, becomes larger. This implies that the effective incompressibility modulus increases due to the impurity effect of $Λ$ particle, if the $β$-stability condition is not imposed.

## Key claim

The inclusion of two Λ hyperons in ¹⁶O and ²⁰⁸Pb shifts the energy of collective vibrational states, particularly the giant monopole resonance, toward higher energies, implying an increase in the effective incompressibility modulus, but only when the β-stability condition is not imposed.

## Method

The authors employed the Hartree-Fock (HF) plus random-phase-approximation (RPA) method using Skyrme-type ΛN and ΛΛ interactions, with the SkM* parameter set for the NN interaction, the No.5 parameter set from Yamamoto (1988) for the ΛN interaction, and the SΛΛ1 parameter set from Lanskoy (1998) for the ΛΛ interaction. They solved the HF equations in coordinate space with a grid size of 0.1 fm, discretized continuum states with a box boundary condition at 16.0 fm, and derived residual interactions for RPA self-consistently from the second derivative of the energy functional with respect to densities.

## Key numbers

- The lowest 1s₁/₂ single-particle level in ¹⁸_ΛΛO is shifted by Δεₙ = −1.4 MeV for neutrons and Δεₚ = −1.3 MeV for protons compared to ¹⁶O.
- For the 2₁⁺ state in ¹⁶O, the excitation energy is 13.1 MeV with B(E2) = 0.726 e²fm⁴; for ¹⁸_ΛΛO, it is 13.8 MeV with B(E2) = 0.529 e²fm⁴.
- For the 3₁⁻ state in ¹⁶O, the excitation energy is 6.06 MeV with B(E3) = 91.1 e²fm⁶; for ¹⁸_ΛΛO, it is 6.32 MeV with B(E3) = 67.7 e²fm⁶.
- The centroid energy shift δE̅ for the E2 mode is +0.54 MeV (RPA) and for the E3 mode is +1.73 MeV (RPA) when comparing ¹⁸_ΛΛO to ¹⁶O.
- For the E1 mode excluding the low-lying peak, the centroid energy shift δE̅ is +1.03 MeV (RPA).
- The giant monopole resonance peak appears at 22.2 MeV for ¹⁸_ΛΛO and 21.1 MeV for ¹⁶O; for ²¹⁰_ΛΛPb it appears at 14.6 MeV and for ²⁰⁸Pb at 14.2 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme-HF plus RPA scheme, previously applied to normal nuclei and to ground-state properties of Λ hypernuclei by Rayet (1976, 1981), Yamamoto (1988), Lanskoy (1998), and Tretyakova (1999), to collective vibrational excitations of double-Λ hypernuclei. The calculations provide the upper limit of the Λ-impurity effect for single-Λ hypernuclei and enable systematic studies of how Λ hyperons modify nuclear incompressibility, which is relevant to the equation of state of neutron star matter where hyperons appear at high densities, as discussed by Dexheimer (2008) and Glendenning (1991).
