# Activation of momentum fluctuations in wave packet molecular dynamics: Impacts on momentum distributions of projectilelike fragments

> Molecular dynamics approaches use wave packets as nucleon wave functions to simulate the time evolution of nuclear reactions. It is crucial to activate the momentum fluctuation inherent in each wave packet so that it properly affects the time evolution. In the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) model, this has traditionally been done by splitting the wave packets, i.e., by introducing a random fluctuation to the wave packet center of each particle. The present work proposes an improved approach to activate the fluctuation in both the one-body mean-field propagation and the two-nucleon collision processes, consistently based on the gradual or sudden change of the degree of isolation, which is derived from the fragment number function used for the zero-point energy subtraction. This new method is applied to the 12C + 12C and 12C + p reactions at about 100 MeV/nucleon, focusing on the momentum distribution of the 11B fragments produced by one-proton removal from the 12C projectile. The results show that, with the momentum fluctuation suitably activated, the method correctly accounts for the recoil from the removed nucleon to the residue and the 11B momentum distribution is significantly improved, while without activating the fluctuation the distribution is too narrow compared to the experimental data. Furthermore, the AMD results indicate that the momentum distribution consists of two components; one is the high $P_z$ component with a small shift from the beam velocity, resulting from the simple removal of a proton after an energetic collision with a target particle; the other is the low $P_z$ component with a larger peak shift resulting from the decay of an excited 12C nucleus in a longer time scale. The activation of momentum fluctuation mainly affects the high $P_z$ component to broaden it. The role of cluster correlations in this problem is also investigated.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new method to activate the inherent momentum fluctuation of Gaussian wave packets in the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) model, consistently linking the wave packet splitting to the change in the degree of isolation derived from the fragment number function used for zero-point energy subtraction; the method is validated for one-proton removal reactions at around 100 MeV/nucleon, where the momentum distribution of the residual fragment is significantly improved compared to calculations without activated fluctuation.

## Method

The authors introduce a stochastic wave packet splitting procedure triggered by the increase of the degree of isolation μ_j for each particle, which is computed from the fragment number function N_frag. When μ_j increases during mean-field propagation (one-body) or at two-nucleon collisions (two-body), a random momentum fluctuation δp is added to the particle's centroid, with variance proportional to the change in μ_j and the original momentum width Δp; momentum conservation is restored by giving a recoil to a selected environment of nearby particles, and energy conservation is enforced by scaling the relative momentum between two particles in that environment. The method is applied to 12C+12C and 12C+p reactions at about 100 MeV/nucleon, using the AMD model with Skyrme SLy4 interaction and cluster correlations in the final state, followed by statistical decay of excited primary fragments.

## Key numbers

- Wave packet width parameter ν = 0.16 fm⁻², giving position uncertainty Δx = 1.25 fm and momentum uncertainty Δp = 78.9 MeV/c.
- Zero-point energy subtraction parameter T₀ = 8.2 MeV.
- Fragment number function parameters: ξ̂ = 0.32 fm⁻², â = 0.5 fm; ξ = 0.32 fm⁻², a = 1.5 fm; ξ̄ = 0.16 fm⁻², ā = 1.25 fm.
- Momentum-dependent factor in N_frag uses ξ_v = 0.0128 fm⁻², v₀ = 0.1c, r₀ = 0.2 fm.
- Dynamical evolution truncated at 300 fm/c before statistical decay.
- The 11B momentum distribution consists of a high P_z component with small shift from beam velocity and a low P_z component with larger peak shift; activation of momentum fluctuation mainly broadens the high P_z component.

## Context

This work extends the AMD model's treatment of momentum fluctuations beyond earlier approaches that introduced wave packet splitting without direct connection to zero-point energy subtraction (Ono 1996, Ono 2002) and the momentum boost at two-nucleon collisions (Lin 2016). The new method provides a consistent framework linking the activation of momentum fluctuation to the degree of isolation derived from the fragment number function, which was originally introduced for zero-point kinetic energy subtraction (Ono 1992). The results enable more accurate description of fragment momentum distributions in knockout reactions and can be applied to general nuclear reaction simulations where event-by-event fluctuations are important.
