# Transverse Momentum of Protons, Pions and Kaons in High Multiplicity pp and pA Collisions: Evidence for the Color Glass Condensate?

> The CMS experiment at the Large Hadron Collider has recently presented data on the average transverse momentum of protons, kaons and pions as a function of particle multiplicity. We relate the particle multiplicity to saturation momentum using recently published computations of the interaction radius determined from the theory of the Color Glass Condensate. We show that the pp and the pA experimental data scale in terms of these saturation momenta. Computing transverse momentum spectra for identified particles using Boltzmann-type distributions and relating different associated multiplicities using geometric scaling, these simple distributions reproduce the observed dependence of the mean transverse momentum on particle multiplicities seen in both pp and pA interactions for pions to good accuracy, and to fair agreement for protons and kaons.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the observed dependence of mean transverse momentum on particle multiplicity in both pp and pA collisions can be reproduced by simple Boltzmann-type distributions combined with geometric scaling, provided the saturation momentum is computed using the multiplicity-dependent interaction area from the Color Glass Condensate theory; the agreement is good for pions and fair for protons and kaons, with the caveat that the analysis ignores the weak dependence of the saturation momentum on the coupling constant and assumes the ratio of proton to nuclear saturation momenta is unity at high multiplicities.

## Method

The authors relate particle multiplicity to the saturation momentum using the relation Q_sat^2 ~ (dN/dy)/S_T, where S_T is the transverse interaction area computed from the IP-Glasma model by Bzdak et al. (2013). They parametrize the transverse momentum spectra for pions, kaons, and protons using Boltzmann-type distributions f ~ exp(-m_T/T_eff) with effective temperature T_eff = κ_i * sqrt(N_track/S_T). The parameter κ_i for each particle species is fixed by fitting one data point in pp collisions, and the resulting mean transverse momentum as a function of multiplicity is compared to CMS data for both pp and pPb collisions.

## Key numbers

- The interaction radius for pPb collisions is parametrized as R_pPb = 1 fm × f_pPb((dN_g/dy)^(1/3)), with f_pPb(x) = 0.21 + 0.47x for x < 3.5, and saturating at 2.394 fm for x ≥ 5.
- The interaction radius for pp collisions is parametrized as R_pp = 1 fm × f_pp((dN_g/dy)^(1/3)), saturating at 1.538 fm for x ≥ 3.4.
- The gluon multiplicity is related to the number of tracks by dN_g/dy ≈ (3/2) × (1/Δη) × N_track, with Δη ≈ 4.8 units of pseudorapidity; the computation is considered reliable for N_track > 20.
- The parameter κ_i for pions is fixed at N_track ≈ 30, and for protons and kaons at N_track between 50 and 60.

## Context

This work extends the geometric scaling hypothesis of Stasto et al. (2000) and earlier CGC-based analyses by McLerran et al. (2010) and Praszalowicz (2011) to identified particle species as a function of multiplicity, using the interaction area computed by Bzdak et al. (2013) in the IP-Glasma model. It directly addresses CMS data on mean transverse momentum of pions, kaons, and protons (Chatrchyan et al., 2012; CMS pPb collaboration). The results suggest that the observed flattening of mean p_T with multiplicity, often attributed to collective flow in pA collisions, can be explained by simple scaling arguments, but the authors note that future data on interferometric radii (Bozek, 2013) will be needed to distinguish between initial-state scaling and final-state collectivity scenarios.
