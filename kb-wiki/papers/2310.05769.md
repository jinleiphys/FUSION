# Collective modes of a massive fermion in a magnetized medium with finite anomalous magnetic moment

> We calculate, in a systematic way, the general structure of the self-energy of light massive fermions and the effective propagator in a thermomagnetic medium with the inclusion of anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of the fermion in the weak field approximation. It is found that the self-energy of a massive fermion in this case consists of five non-trivial structure factors in contrast to the massless case where the self-energy contains only four. We employ the real time formalism (RTF) of thermal field theory within the ambit of hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation in the evaluation of the structure factors. The collective modes are obtained from the poles of the effective propagator of the fermion. The investigation of the dispersion relations for non-degenerate ground state shows that the effect of the magnetic field is more for up quark than the down quark because of the larger charge of the former. The important observation is that in the first excited state the degeneracy, which exists for non-zero magnetic field is lifted due to the inclusion of the AMM. It is also observed that the first excited state becomes less dispersive compared to the case when AMM is not considered, whereas the second excited state becomes more dispersive when both the magnetic field and the AMM are non-zero in comparison to the case with vanishing AMM. These effects are observed in both particle and hole-like excitations. Qualitatively similar behaviour is also seen in the case of down quarks.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the collective modes of a massive fermion in a hot, weakly magnetized medium including the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM), finding that the self-energy contains five non-trivial structure factors (instead of four for massless fermions) and that the AMM lifts the degeneracy of the first excited state that exists for a non-zero magnetic field alone.

## Method

The authors use the real time formalism (RTF) of thermal field theory within the hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation to evaluate the one-loop self-energy of a massive fermion in a weak background magnetic field, including the fermion's anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) via the Schwinger ansatz. The fermion propagator is expanded to first order in the magnetic field strength \(B\), and the effective propagator is obtained by solving the Dyson-Schwinger equation. The collective modes are extracted from the poles of the effective propagator.

## Key numbers

* The thermal mass scale is defined as \(M_{\rm th}^2 = g^2 C(R) T^2 / 8\), where \(g\) is the gauge coupling, \(C(R)\) is the Casimir invariant, and \(T\) is the temperature.
* The weak field condition is \(|qB| \ll T^2\), where \(q\) is the fermion charge and \(B\) the magnetic field strength.
* The up quark charge is \(q = 2/3 e\) and the down quark charge is \(q = -1/3 e\), where \(e > 0\) is the proton charge; the magnetic field effect is larger for the up quark due to its larger charge.
* The first excited state becomes less dispersive when AMM is included compared to the case with vanishing AMM; the second excited state becomes more dispersive when both magnetic field and AMM are non-zero.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of fermion self-energy and collective modes in hot magnetized media (Das 2017, Ghosh 2021) by incorporating the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of the fermion, which had been studied in effective models (Chaudhuri 2019, 2020) but not in the HTL framework with real time formalism. The results enable future calculations of photon damping rates and real photon emission from a magnetized medium with AMM, and complement prior work on gluon self-energy and collective excitations in hot magnetized QCD (Hattori 2017, Ayala 2018, Karmakar 2018).
