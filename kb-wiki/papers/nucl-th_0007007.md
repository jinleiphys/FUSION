# Phenomenological Evidence for Gluon Depletion in pA Collisions

> The data of J/psi suppression at large x_F in pA collisions are used to infer the existence of gluon depletion as the projectile proton traverses the nucleus. The modification of the gluon distribution is studied by use of a convolution equation whose non-perturbative splitting function is determined phenomenologically. The depletion factor at x_1=0.8 is found to be about 25% at A=100.

# Phenomenological Evidence for Gluon Depletion in pA Collisions

## Key claim

The observed suppression of J/psi production at large x_F in proton-nucleus collisions can be explained by gluon depletion in the projectile proton as it traverses the nucleus, provided that the hadronic absorption factor H(x_F, A) is assumed to have negligible x_F dependence. The depletion factor at x_1 = 0.8 is about 25% for A = 100, but this result depends on a phenomenological non-perturbative splitting function determined from the data rather than from perturbative QCD.

## Method

The authors analyze Fermilab E866 data on J/psi suppression in pA collisions at 800 GeV/c, expressed as alpha(x_F) from the parametrization alpha(x_F) = 0.952 (1 + 0.023 x_F - 0.397 x_F^2). They factor the nuclear suppression ratio R(x_F, A) into three components: projectile gluon modification G(x_F, A), target nuclear shadowing N(x_F, A), and hadronic absorption H(x_F, A). Using the EKS98 parametrization for nuclear shadowing of gluons in the target and assuming H is x_F-independent, they extract the projectile gluon distribution modification by taking moments of the ratio and fitting a non-perturbative evolution equation for gluons traversing nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Depletion factor at x_1 = 0.8 is about 25% for A = 100
- For A = 100: k_0 = 1.592, k_1 = 23.42, k_2 = 97.66, k_3 = 89.17 (dimensionless fit parameters for K_n)
- For A = 200: k'_0 = 1.831, k'_1 = 27.43, k'_2 = 113.97, k'_3 = 103.80 (dimensionless fit parameters for K_n)
- Evolution kernel parameters: q_1 = 3.68 fm^{-1}, q_2 = 15.01 fm^{-1}, q_3 = 13.47 fm^{-1}, q_0 = 0.17 fm^{-1}
- Absorption cross section sigma = 6.5 mb used in H(z) for the final alpha(x_F) plot
- Average path length z = 0.9 A^{1/3} fm, with L = 3R_A/2 = 1.8 A^{1/3} fm
- Cross-over between gluon depletion and regeneration occurs at x_1 approximately 0.28
- Q^2 = 10 GeV^2 used for the gluon distributions corresponding to c-cbar production

## Context

This work extends the unconventional view proposed by Hwa, Pisut, and Pisutova (1997, 1998) that gluon distributions can be modified in nuclear medium due to depletion, contrary to the conventional assumption in heavy-ion collisions that gluon distributions are the same as in free nucleons (Kharzeev and Satz, 1995; Wang and Jacak, 1999; Vogt, 1999). It contrasts with prior attempts by He, Hufner, and Kopeliovich (2000) and Arleo et al. (2000) to explain the x_F dependence of J/psi suppression through hadronic absorption alone, which required unreasonably short octet lifetimes. The analysis enables future studies of gluon depletion effects at large x_F in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and suggests that gluon enhancement at low x may contribute to strangeness and dilepton enhancement observed in heavy-ion collisions.
