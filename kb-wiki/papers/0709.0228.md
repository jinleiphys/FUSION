# Nuclear Chemical and Mechanical Instability and the Liquid-Gas Phase Transition in Nuclei

> The thermodynamic properties of nuclei are studied in a mean field model using a Skryme interaction. Properties of two component systems are investigated over the complete range of proton fraction from a system of pure neutrons to a system of only protons. Besides volume, symmetry, and Coulomb effects we also include momentum or velocity dependent forces. Applications of the results developed are then given which include nuclear mechanical and chemical instability and an associated liquid/gas phase transition in two component systems. The velocity dependence leads to further changes in the coexistence curve and nuclear mechanical and chemical instability curves.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including a velocity-dependent (momentum-dependent) interaction via a density-dependent effective mass approximation modifies the nuclear liquid-gas coexistence curve and the mechanical/chemical instability boundaries, with a larger effect on proton-rich systems than on neutron-rich systems, when superimposed on a Skyrme mean-field model that already includes Coulomb and surface effects.

## Method

The authors use a mean-field model with a Skyrme interaction extended to include momentum-dependent forces through an effective mass approximation, applied to two-component nuclear matter (protons and neutrons) over the full range of proton fraction from pure neutrons to pure protons. They compute thermodynamic quantities including pressure, chemical potentials, and entropy using Fermi integrals, and then determine the mechanical and chemical instability regions and the liquid-gas coexistence curves for the two-component system, incorporating volume, symmetry, Coulomb, and surface effects.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density for the momentum-dependent Skyrme force: \(\rho_0 = 0.1283\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Binding energy per nucleon for the momentum-dependent force: \(E_B/A = 13.1057\) MeV
- Effective mass ratio for the momentum-dependent force: \(m^*/m = 0.895626\)
- Symmetry energy for the momentum-dependent force: \(S_V = 23.4791\) MeV
- Nuclear compressibility for the momentum-dependent force: \(\kappa = 307.780\) MeV
- Fermi energy for the momentum-dependent force: \(E_F = 31.8018\) MeV
- Saturation density for the momentum-independent force: \(\rho_0 = 0.143145\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Binding energy per nucleon for the momentum-independent force: \(E_B/A = 15.54447\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Lee and Mekjian, 2003, 2004) that included Coulomb and surface effects in a Skyrme mean-field model without momentum dependence, and differs from the momentum-dependence study of Chen et al. (2006) by also including Coulomb and surface effects. The results are relevant for understanding isospin fractionation in nuclear collisions, for future Rare Isotope Beam (RIB) experiments, and for astrophysical applications such as neutron star crust properties.
