# Nucleon Resonance Electroexcitation Amplitudes and Emergent Hadron Mass

> Understanding the strong interaction dynamics that govern the emergence of hadron mass (EHM) represents a challenging open problem in the Standard Model. In this paper we describe new opportunities for gaining insight into EHM from results on nucleon resonance ($N^\ast$) electroexcitation amplitudes (i.e. $γ_vpN^\ast$ electrocouplings) in the mass range up to 1.8\,GeV for virtual photon four-momentum squared (i.e. photon virtualities $Q^2$) up to 7.5\,GeV$^2$ available from exclusive meson electroproduction data acquired during the 6-GeV era of experiments at Jefferson Laboratory (JLab). These results, combined with achievements in the use of continuum Schwinger function methods (CSMs), offer new opportunities for charting the momentum dependence of the dressed quark mass from results on the $Q^2$-evolution of the $γ_vpN^\ast$ electrocouplings. A successful description of the $Δ(1232)3/2^+$ and $N(1440)1/2^+$ electrocouplings has been achieved using CSMs with, in both cases, common momentum-dependent mass functions for the dressed quarks, for the gluons, and the same momentum-dependent strong coupling. Parameter-free CSM predictions for the electrocouplings of the $Δ(1600)3/2^+$ became available in 2019. The experimental results obtained in the first half of 2022 have confirmed the CSM predictions. We also discuss prospects for these studies during the 12-GeV era at JLab using the CLAS12 detector, with experiments that are currently in progress, and canvass the physics motivation for continued studies in this area with a possible increase of the JLab electron beam energy up to 22\,GeV.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the momentum dependence of the dressed quark mass, which encodes the emergence of hadron mass (EHM), can be charted by comparing continuum Schwinger function method (CSM) calculations of nucleon resonance electroexcitation amplitudes with experimental data on gamma_v p N* electrocouplings for resonances up to 1.8 GeV and photon virtualities Q^2 up to 7.5 GeV^2, but only insofar as the CSM calculations successfully describe the data using common momentum-dependent mass functions for dressed quarks and gluons and the same momentum-dependent strong coupling.

## Method

The authors analyzed exclusive meson electroproduction data from the 6-GeV era of Jefferson Laboratory (JLab) experiments to extract gamma_v p N* electrocouplings for nucleon resonances in the mass range up to 1.8 GeV and Q^2 up to 7.5 GeV^2. They compared these experimental results with parameter-free CSM predictions for the electrocouplings of the Delta(1600)3/2+ resonance, which became available in 2019, and with earlier CSM descriptions of the Delta(1232)3/2+ and N(1440)1/2+ electrocouplings that used common momentum-dependent mass functions for dressed quarks and gluons.

## Context

This work extends the program of mapping the dressed quark mass function through nucleon resonance electroexcitation, building on earlier CSM calculations that successfully described the Delta(1232)3/2+ and N(1440)1/2+ electrocouplings (Roberts and collaborators, 2010s). The confirmation of CSM predictions for the Delta(1600)3/2+ electrocouplings by 2022 experimental results represents a significant validation of the CSM approach to EHM. The results enable future studies with the CLAS12 detector in the 12-GeV era at JLab and motivate potential upgrades to 22 GeV beam energy to extend the Q^2 reach and probe the transition from nonperturbative to perturbative QCD regimes.
