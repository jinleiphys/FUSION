# Diquarks in cold dense QCD with two flavors

> We derive and analyze the Bethe-Salpeter equations for spin zero diquarks in the color superconducting phase of cold dense QCD with two massless flavors. The spectrum of diquarks contains an infinite number of massive excitations and five (nearly) massless pseudoscalars. The former are singlets while the latter include a doublet, an antidoublet and a singlet with respect to the unbroken $SU(2)_{c}$. Because of an approximate parity doubling at large chemical potential, all massive states come in pairs. The decay constants, as well as the velocities of the (nearly) massless pseudoscalars are derived. The different role of the Meissner effect for tightly bound states and quasiclassical bound states is revealed.

## Key claim

The spectrum of spin-zero diquarks in the two-flavor color superconducting phase of cold dense QCD contains an infinite tower of massive excitations and five (nearly) massless pseudoscalars, but the massive states are singlets under the unbroken SU(2)c subgroup while the massless states form a doublet, an antidoublet, and a singlet; the Meissner effect is irrelevant for tightly bound states but crucial for quasiclassical bound states with small binding energy.

## Method

The authors derived and analyzed the Bethe-Salpeter (BS) equations for spin-zero diquarks in the color superconducting phase of cold dense QCD with two massless flavors, using the hard dense loop improved rainbow approximation with no wave function renormalization effects. They employed a multi-component spinor formalism to handle the quark propagators and derived Ward identities for the quark-gluon vertex functions corresponding to the broken color generators, which were then used to analyze the BS equations for both Nambu-Goldstone (NG) bosons and massive diquark states.

## Key numbers

* The mass formula for the singlet diquark states is \(M^{2}_{n} \simeq 4 |\Delta^{-}_{0} |^{2} \left(1-\frac{\alpha_{s}^{2}\kappa}{(2n+1)^{4}}\right)\) for \(n=1,2,\ldots\), where \(\kappa \simeq 0.27\) is a constant of order 1, \(|\Delta^{-}_{0} |\) is the dynamical Majorana mass of quarks, and \(\alpha_{s}=g_{s}^2/{4\pi}\).
* The superconducting gap at the Fermi surface is \(|\Delta^{-}_{0}|\simeq \frac{(4\pi)^{3/2}\mbox{e} \mu}{\alpha_{s}^{5/2}} \exp\left(- \frac{3 \pi^{3/2}}{2^{3/2}\sqrt{\alpha_{s}}}\right)\), where \(\mu\) is the chemical potential and \(\mbox{e}=2.718\ldots\).
* The Debye screening scale for electric gluons is \(l_{D}\sim 1/g_{s}\mu\), and the magnetic gluon screening involves the parameter \(M^2=N_{f}\alpha_{s}\mu^2/\pi\) with \(N_{f}=2\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of color superconductivity in dense QCD, building on the ground-breaking estimates of the superconducting order parameter by Rapp et al. (1998) and Schafer and Wilczek (1999) using instanton liquid models, and on the treatment of gluon screening effects by Pisarski and Rischke (1999) and Son (1999). The paper provides a detailed Bethe-Salpeter treatment of diquark bound states that goes beyond effective action approaches (such as those by Son and Stephanov, 2000; Casalbuoni and Gatto, 1999; Rho, 2000; and Hong, Zee, and Bedaque, 2000) which could only probe pseudo-NG bosons, and it reveals that an infinite tower of massive diquark states could be a key signature of the color superconducting phase, as previously suggested by Miransky, Shovkovy, and Wijewardhana (2000).
