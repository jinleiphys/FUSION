# The pion wave function in covariant light-front dynamics

> The structure of the pion wave function in the relativistic constituent quark model is investigated in the explicitly covariant formulation of light-front dynamics. We calculate the two relativistic components of the pion wave function in a simple one-gluon exchange model and investigate various physical observables: decay constant, charge radius, electromagnetic and transition form factors. We discuss the influence of the full relativistic structure of the pion wave function for an overall good description of all these observables, including both low and high momentum scales.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a fully relativistic constituent quark model, using the explicitly covariant formulation of light-front dynamics (CLFD), can simultaneously describe low- and high-momentum pion observables only when both spin components of the pion wave function are included, with the relativistic component \(A_2\) dominating at high transverse momentum and introducing a soft logarithmic divergence in the decay constant that must be subtracted.

## Method

The authors start from a non-relativistic parametrization of the pion wave function (either Gaussian or power-law) and compute perturbative one-gluon-exchange radiative corrections to obtain the two relativistic scalar components \(A_1\) and \(A_2\) of the pion wave function in CLFD. They then calculate the pion decay constant, electromagnetic form factor, charge radius, and transition form factor in the relativistic impulse approximation, using a running strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s(K^2)\) with \(n_f=2\) and \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}=220\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: the physical value \(F^{\text{phys}}\) is obtained by subtracting a divergent piece \(F^\infty\) that scales as \(1/\text{Log Log}[\Lambda_C/\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}]\) in the limit of large cutoff \(\Lambda_C\).
- Asymptotic behavior of the relativistic component: \(A_2 \to A_2^\infty = \frac{1}{\mathbf{R}_\perp^2 \text{Log}[\mathbf{R}_\perp^2/\Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^2]} \bar A_2^\infty(x)\) at high transverse momentum \(|\mathbf{R}_\perp|\).
- Asymptotic behavior of the electromagnetic form factor: \(F_\pi(Q^2) \sim 1/Q^2\) (up to logarithmic corrections) at large \(Q^2\), dominated by the \(A_2'\) component.
- Running coupling constant: \(\alpha_s(K^2)\) uses \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}} = 220\) MeV and \(n_f = 2\).
- Charge radius: extracted from \(F_\pi(Q^2)\) via \(\langle r_\pi^2 \rangle = -6 \, dF_\pi(Q^2)/dQ^2|_{Q^2=0}\).

## Context

This work extends the CLFD formalism previously applied to the \(J/\Psi\) meson (Dugne, 2002) and generalizes earlier light-front studies of the pion electromagnetic form factor (HWW) that used only a single distribution amplitude. It contrasts with approaches relying solely on distribution amplitudes (Brodsky-Lepage, Mikhailov, Noguera, Bakulev, Li, Yakovlev) by including the full transverse momentum and both spin components, enabling a unified description from low to high momentum scales. The results enable future studies of other pseudoscalar mesons and provide a framework for incorporating higher Fock states.
