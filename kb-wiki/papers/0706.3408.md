# Current Renormalization in Finite Volume

> For finite volume field theories with discrete translational invariance, conserved currents can be additively renormalized by infrared effects. We demonstrate this for pions using chiral perturbation theory coupled to electromagnetism in a periodic box. Gauge invariant single particle effective theories are constructed to explain these results. In such theories, current renormalization arises from operators involving the zero mode of the gauge field. No contradictions with Ward identities, or low-energy theorems are encountered.

## Key claim

Conserved currents in finite-volume field theories with discrete translational invariance can undergo additive renormalization from infrared effects, contrary to the infinite-volume protection. This renormalization arises specifically from gauge-invariant operators involving the zero mode of the gauge field, and does not contradict Ward identities or low-energy theorems when the spatial momentum quantization is properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors performed one-loop calculations in chiral perturbation theory (χPT) coupled to electromagnetism in a periodic spatial box of side length L with infinite temporal extent, evaluating the electromagnetic current matrix elements for charged pions and the zero-frequency Compton amplitude for neutral pions. They then constructed gauge-invariant single-particle effective theories using Wilson lines that cycle the compact dimensions to reproduce the finite-volume results, and verified consistency with the Ward-Takahashi identity and low-energy theorems.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: f = 132 MeV (used in the chiral Lagrangian)
- Pion mass squared: m_π² = 2λm (tree-level relation from the chiral Lagrangian)
- Charged pion current modification: ⟨π±(P)|J_μ|π±(P)⟩ = ±2e P_μ {1 - (δ_{μj}/(3f²))[2 I_{1/2}(m_π², L) + m_π² I_{3/2}(m_π², L)]}, where the finite-volume correction is exponentially suppressed for m_π L ≫ 1
- Neutral pion zero-frequency Compton amplitude: T_{μν}(ω=ω'=0) = e²(δ_{μ0}δ_{ν0} - g_{μν})(m_π⁴/f²) I_{5/2}(m_π², L), which vanishes identically in infinite volume
- Effective coupling for charged pions: 𝒬(L) = -(1/(f²L))[2 I_{1/2}(m_π², L) + m_π² I_{3/2}(m_π², L)]
- Effective coupling for neutral pions: 𝒬̄(L)² = (9/(2f²L²)) m_π⁴ I_{5/2}(m_π², L)

## Context

This work extends the findings of Detmold (2006) regarding finite-volume modifications to nucleon Compton scattering and polarizabilities, providing a theoretical explanation through gauge-invariant zero-mode interactions. The results are consistent with the finite-volume framework of Gasser and Leutwyler (1986, 1987) for chiral perturbation theory, and demonstrate that the differential form of the Ward identity fails in finite volume due to spatial momentum quantization, while the Ward-Takahashi identity remains valid. This enables proper treatment of systematic errors in lattice QCD simulations using classical background field methods, as pursued by Fucito (1982), Martinelli (1982), Bernard (1982), and subsequent authors.
