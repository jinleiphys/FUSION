# Entropy Production in Relativistic Hydrodynamics

> The entropy production occurring in relativistic hydrodynamical systems such as the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formed in high-energy nuclear collisions is explored. We study mechanisms which change the composition of the fluid, i.e. particle production and/or chemical reactions, along with chemo- and thermo-diffusion. These effects complement the conventional dissipative effects of shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and heat conductivity.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general expression for entropy production in relativistic hydrodynamics that includes, for the first time, the combined effects of particle production/chemical reactions and chemo- and thermo-diffusion alongside the conventional dissipative effects of shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, and heat conductivity; the authors note that the first-order formalism they employ may suffer from instabilities under physically admissible perturbations, as previously argued for similar theories by Hiscock and others.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic energy-momentum tensor for an imperfect fluid and generalize the continuity equations to include particle production sources and diffusion terms. They then derive the entropy four-current and the entropy production rate by imposing the First and Second Laws of Thermodynamics, requiring that the four-divergence of the entropy current be nonnegative. The resulting expression is obtained by identifying the structure of the dissipative terms (heat flow, shear tensor, bulk viscosity) and the diffusion terms (with mutual diffusion coefficients) that satisfy this positivity constraint, and by imposing the condition that the sum of chemical potentials times particle sources is nonpositive to guarantee driving towards chemical equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- The entropy production expression (Eq. result) contains terms proportional to the transport coefficients: heat conductivity $\kappa$, shear viscosity $\eta$, and bulk viscosity $\zeta$, all required to be $\geq 0$.
- The diffusion current in the local rest frame is given by $\vec{j}_i = -\sum_j \frac{\sigma_{ij}}{T}(\nabla\mu_j - \frac{\mu_j}{T}\nabla T)$, where $\sigma_{ij}$ is the symmetric matrix of mutual diffusion constants with nonnegative eigenvalues.
- In the Boltzmann approximation, the chemical potential relates to density as $\mu_j = T\ln[C\rho_j T^{-3}]$, leading to a diffusion current $\vec{j}_i = -\sum_j \sigma_{ij}(\frac{\nabla \rho_j}{\rho_j} + 3\frac{\nabla T}{T})$.
- The chemical equilibration rate is defined as $\tau^{-1} \equiv -\partial_t \ln[\mathcal{M}(\vec{\xi},\vec{\xi}_1)]$, where $\mathcal{M}$ is a distance measure in density space.

## Context

This work extends the standard relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics framework (Eckart; Landau and Lifshitz) by incorporating particle production and composition-changing processes relevant to quark-gluon plasma formed in high-energy nuclear collisions, building on earlier studies of chemical rate equations in relativistic systems by Rafelski (1982), Kajantie, and others. The formalism enables future calculations of entropy production in QGP evolution, which is needed to relate initial conditions in heavy-ion collisions to observable particle multiplicities (Letessier, 1993), and provides a foundation for incorporating microscopic transport calculations of transport coefficients (Arnold; Baym; Weinberg) into a consistent hydrodynamical description.
