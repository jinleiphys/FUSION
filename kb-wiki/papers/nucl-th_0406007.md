# Generalization of the $N_pN_n$ Scheme and the Structure of the Valence Space

> The $N_pN_n$ scheme, which has been extensively applied to even-even nuclei, is found to be a very good benchmark for odd-even, even-odd, and doubly-odd nuclei as well. There are no apparent shifts in the correlations for these four classes of nuclei. The compact correlations highlight the deviant behavior of the Z=78 nuclei, are used to deduce effective valence proton numbers near Z=64, and to study the evolution of the Z=64 subshell gap.

## Key claim

The \(N_pN_n\) scheme, previously validated only for even-even nuclei, works equally well for odd-even, even-odd, and doubly-odd nuclei across large regions of medium-heavy nuclei, with no apparent shifts in the correlations among these four classes of nuclei.

## Method

The authors plotted the quadrupole deformation parameter \(e_2\) (taken from the macroscopic-microscopic calculations of Moller) against the product of valence proton number and valence neutron number \(N_pN_n\) for nuclei in four mass regions spanning Z=50 to 104. They compared the correlations for even-even, odd-even, even-odd, and odd-odd nuclei simultaneously, and used the compactness of the correlations to extract effective valence proton numbers near Z=64 by shifting deviant data points onto the main correlation curve.

## Key numbers

- The study covers nuclei in four regions: 50<Z≤66, 82<N≤104; 66<Z<82, 82<N≤104; 66<Z<82, 104<N<126; and 82<Z≤104, 126<N<155.
- A slight difference between even-proton and odd-proton points is visible only for \(N_pN_n\) values less than 50 in one region (Fig. 1c).
- The N=84 isotones show \(e_2=0\), indicating very soft nuclei where the shallow potential energy well leads to large differences between equilibrium and expectation values of deformation.
- The Z=78 (Pt) nuclei stand out to the upper left of the correlations in multiple regions, lying in a complex region with large \(\gamma\)-softness, oblate shapes, prolate shapes, and transition regions between them.
- Effective valence proton numbers were extracted for nuclei with 59≤Z≤66 and N≤91, showing a gradual breakdown of the Z=64 shell gap that accelerates near N=90.

## Context

This work extends the \(N_pN_n\) scheme, originally introduced by Casten (1985) for even-even nuclei, to all nuclear species for the first time. Prior applications to odd-A nuclei were limited to the A=80-100 region (Bucurescu et al.) and a few nuclei near A=100, but no unified treatment existed across large mass regions. The results enable the use of the \(N_pN_n\) scheme as a tool to probe changes in shell structure and magicity in exotic nuclei far from stability, where data will soon become available.
