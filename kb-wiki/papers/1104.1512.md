# Quark matter under strong magnetic field in chiral models

> The chiral model is used to describe quark matter under strong magnetic fields and compared to other models, the MIT bag model and the two flavors Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. The effect of vacuum corrections due to the magnetic field is discussed. It is shown that if the magnetic field vacuum corrections are not taken into account explicitly the parameters of the models should be fitted to low density meson properties in the presence of the magnetic field.

## Key claim

The chiral model can describe quark matter under strong magnetic fields, but the way vacuum corrections due to the magnetic field are handled critically affects the results; if these corrections are not included explicitly, the model parameters must be refitted to low-density meson properties in the presence of the field, and the two prescriptions (renormalizing the pion decay constant versus adding an explicit B-dependent vacuum term) give similar results only for fields below about 3 × 10^19 G.

## Method

The authors compare three quark models, the chiral sigma model (linear sigma model), the two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, and the MIT bag model, for isospin-symmetric quark matter and for stellar quark matter under static uniform magnetic fields along the z-axis. For the chiral model, they implement two approaches to include magnetic-field vacuum corrections: (I) renormalizing the pion decay constant f_π so the vacuum quark mass matches the NJL value, and (II) adding an explicit B-dependent vacuum term U_mag(σ,B) to the Mexican-hat potential, following Mizher et al. (2010). The equations of state are obtained by minimizing the energy density with respect to the σ field (chiral model) or solving the gap equation for the effective quark mass (NJL model), with Landau level quantization included in all models.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant in vacuum is f_π = 93 MeV.
- The sigma meson mass is taken as m_σ = 1200 MeV.
- For the NJL model, the parameters are Λ = 587.9 MeV, m_c = 5.6 MeV, and GΛ² = 2.44, giving a quark vacuum mass of 400 MeV at B = 0.
- The MIT bag pressure is set to Bag = 0.9 fm⁻⁴ = 177.3 MeV/fm³.
- Magnetic fields below B = 10^19 G have no noticeable effect on the energy per baryon in any model; effects become strong above B = 10^19 G.
- For the chiral model II, the energy per particle in the chiral restored phase is larger than in the NJL model until B reaches about 10^20 G.
- At B = 10^20 G, the NJL and chiral model II have the same effective bag pressure.
- The scalar virial theorem limits interior magnetic field strengths in compact stars to B ∼ 1–3 × 10^18 G.
- For the chiral model, the muon fraction is always below 0.001 at B = 0; it rises above 0.001 for fields a bit below 10^18 G at a baryon density of 1.2 fm⁻³.
- At the surface of a star with no crust, the magnetic field effect on the electron fraction becomes non-negligible for B > 7 × 10^17 G in the chiral model.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of magnetized quark matter using the MIT bag model (Chakrabarty 1996) and the NJL model (Klimenko 2003; NJLSU2, NJLSU3) by applying the chiral sigma model, previously used for high-density npeμ matter with pion condensation (Takahashi 2006), to both symmetric and stellar quark matter under strong magnetic fields without pion condensation. The results are compared with the MIT bag model and the NJL model, and the treatment of magnetic-field vacuum corrections follows the approach of Mizher et al. (2010) for the Polyakov-loop-extended linear sigma model. The work enables a more systematic understanding of how different chiral models handle magnetic catalysis and the equation of state for quark stars, with implications for the crust structure of magnetars as discussed by Glendenning (1995) and Reddy et al. (2006).
