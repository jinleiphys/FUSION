# First order transition region of an equation of state for QCD with a critical point

> In addition to signals for the critical point, evidence for a first order phase transition would indicate a nontrivial structure within the QCD phase diagram. Moreover, while not a direct measurement of the critical point, the presence of a first order transition would imply its existence. This motivates the need to understand signatures of this first order transition in addition to directly studying the effect of a critical point. To this effect, we map the mean-field Ising model equation of state onto the QCD phase diagram, and reconstruct the full coexistence region in the case of a first order phase transition. Beyond the coexistence line, we maintain access to the spinodal region in the phase diagram, thus providing a description of metastable and unstable phases of matter. Thus, we describe the super-heated hadronic phase and the super-cooled quark-gluon plasma, which is useful for hydrodynamic simulations of the fireball created in a heavy-ion collision at low collision energy, where a first order phase transition is expected. We discuss the features of the pressure and other thermodynamic observables as functions of temperature and baryonic chemical potential, in particular their behavior in the coexistence region. Finally, we compare our equation of state to 3D Ising model ones available in the literature.

## Key claim

The authors map the mean-field Ising model equation of state onto the QCD phase diagram to reconstruct the full coexistence region and spinodal lines for a first-order phase transition, providing a description of metastable and unstable phases. This approach is presented as an indirect method for detecting the presence of a critical point, since the existence of a first-order transition would imply a critical point, even if the critical region itself is too small to measure directly.

## Method

The authors use Landau theory (mean-field Ising model) to derive analytic expressions for the free energy, magnetic field, and pressure, which are then mapped onto the QCD phase diagram using a linear mapping with six non-universal parameters (reduced to four by constraining the chiral crossover transition line from lattice QCD). The mapping allows them to compute the coexistence curve and spinodal lines, including the super-heated hadronic phase and super-cooled quark-gluon plasma, which are relevant for hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions at low beam energies.

## Key numbers

- Critical point location chosen as \(\mu_{B,c}=550\) MeV for the example demonstration, with scaling parameters \(w=1\) and \(\rho=2\)
- The mapping uses the mean-field Ising model with parameters \(a=3\) and \(b=1\) to reproduce a scaling equation of state
- The Second Beam Energy Scan at RHIC explored collision energies \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \leq 20\) GeV, with net-proton cumulant results showing intriguing trends but no definitive evidence for a critical point
- An isothermal curve is shown at \(T=0.98 T_c\) for the Ising model pressure as a function of density, demonstrating the mixed phase and mechanical instability region where \(\partial P/\partial n_B < 0\)

## Context

This work extends the approach of mapping the Ising model onto QCD phase diagrams previously developed by Parotto (2018), Karthein (2021), and Kahangirwe (2024), but specifically uses the mean-field Ising model rather than the 3D Ising model. Unlike the 3D Ising model approaches, which push spinodals into the complex plane due to non-trivial critical exponents (as noted by An, 2017), the mean-field approach maintains a complete description of the first-order phase transition region including coexistence and spinodal lines. This enables future hydrodynamic simulations of the fireball created in heavy-ion collisions at low collision energies where a first-order phase transition is expected.
