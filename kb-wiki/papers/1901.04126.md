## Key claim

The chromopolarizability of the $\psi^\prime \to J/\psi$ transition, extracted from $\psi^\prime \to J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-$ data using dispersion theory with model-independent $\pi\pi$ final state interactions, is reduced to about 1/2 of the value obtained without including those interactions, yielding $|\alpha_{\psi^\prime\psi}|=(1.44\pm 0.02)$ GeV$^{-3}$.

## Method

The decay $\psi^\prime \to J/\psi\pi^+\pi^-$ is analyzed using a chiral effective Lagrangian matched to the QCD multipole expansion, with the amplitude decomposed into $S$- and $D$-wave components. The $\pi\pi$ final state interaction is included model-independently via Omnֳ¨s functions constructed from experimental $\pi\pi$ phase shifts for both the $S$-wave (from the scalar form factor) and $D$-wave (from the Madridג€“Krakֳ³w parametrization). The theoretical $\pi\pi$ invariant mass spectrum and helicity angular distribution are fitted simultaneously to BES data and the total decay width to extract the chromopolarizability $\alpha_{\psi^\prime\psi}$ and the parameter $\kappa$.

## Key numbers

- Chromopolarizability with $\pi\pi$ FSI: $|\alpha_{\psi^\prime\psi}| = (1.44 \pm 0.02)$ GeV$^{-3}$.
- Chromopolarizability without $\pi\pi$ FSI: $|\alpha_{\psi^\prime\psi}| = (2.37 \pm 0.02)$ GeV$^{-3}$.
- Parameter $\kappa$ with FSI: $0.139 \pm 0.005$; without FSI: $0.135 \pm 0.005$.
- Fit quality: $\chi^2/\text{d.o.f.} = 117.6/(120-2) = 1.00$ with FSI; $115.3/(120-2) = 0.98$ without FSI.
- The $D$-wave contribution characterized by $\kappa$ is less than 2% of the total rate.

## Context

This work extends earlier extractions of the transitional chromopolarizability from $\psi^\prime \to J/\psi\pi\pi$ by Voloshin (2004) and Guo (2006), which used a chiral unitary approach for the $\pi\pi$ FSI and only considered the $S$-wave. The present dispersion-theory treatment is model-independent and includes both $S$- and $D$-waves, using updated BES data. The result $|\alpha_{\psi^\prime\psi}| = (1.44 \pm 0.02)$ GeV$^{-3}$ is consistent with the inequality $\alpha_{J/\psi} \geq |\alpha_{\psi^\prime\psi}|$ given the lattice-QCD-based value $\alpha_{J/\psi} = (1.6 \pm 0.8)$ GeV$^{-3}$ from Polyakov (2018), and provides a benchmark for studies of charmonium-nucleon interactions and the hadrocharmonium interpretation of the $P_c$ pentaquarks.
