# Regge-model predictions for K+Sigma photoproduction from the nucleon

> We present Regge-model predictions for the p(gamma,K+)Sigma0 and n(gamma,K+)Sigma- differential cross sections and photon-beam asymmetries in the resonance region. The reaction amplitude encompasses the exchange of K+(494) and K*+(892) Regge-trajectories, introducing a mere three free parameters. These are fitted to the available p(gamma,K+)Sigma0 data beyond the resonance region. The n(gamma,K+)Sigma- amplitude is obtained from the p(gamma,K+)Sigma0 one through SU(2) isospin symmetry considerations.

# Regge-model predictions for K+Sigma photoproduction from the nucleon

## Key claim

The authors present a Regge model with only three free parameters that can account for the gross features of both proton and neutron K+Sigma photoproduction differential cross sections and photon-beam asymmetries in the resonance region (W less than or about 2.5 GeV), but the model significantly underestimates the cross section at lower energies (e.g., omega_lab = 1525 MeV), hinting at the presence of resonances that the pure Regge approach does not capture.

## Method

The reaction amplitude for p(gamma,K+)Sigma0 is constructed from K+(494) and K*+(892) Regge-trajectory exchange in the t-channel, with gauge invariance restored by adding the electric part of the nucleon s-channel Born diagram. The three free parameters (the strong coupling constants g_{K+Sigma0p}, g^v_{K*+Sigma0p}, and g^t_{K*+Sigma0p}) are fitted to 57 high-energy data points (omega_lab greater than about 4 GeV) for p(gamma,K+)Sigma0 differential cross sections and photon-beam asymmetries. The n(gamma,K+)Sigma- amplitude is obtained from the proton amplitude through SU(2) isospin symmetry, converting coupling constants by factors of sqrt(2).

## Key numbers

- The K+(494) Regge trajectory is alpha_{K+}(t) = 0.70 (t - m_{K+}^2) and the K*+(892) trajectory is alpha_{K*+}(t) = 1 + 0.85 (t - m_{K*+}^2), with t and masses in units of GeV^2
- The scale parameter s_0 is set to 1 GeV^2
- The model uses 57 high-energy data points to constrain the three free parameters
- The resonance region is defined as W less than or about 2.5 GeV
- The high-energy region for fitting is defined as omega_lab greater than about 4 GeV
- At omega_lab = 1525 MeV, the Regge model significantly underestimates the p(gamma,K+)Sigma0 differential cross section

## Context

This work extends the Regge-model approach of Guidal et al. (2000s) for kaon photoproduction to the Sigma0 and Sigma- channels, building on the authors' earlier Regge-plus-resonance (RPR) framework developed for lambda production (Vancraeyveld et al., 2009). The model provides a baseline for identifying resonant contributions in the resonance region, and the authors have previously shown that adding resonant s-channel contributions to the Regge amplitude (the RPR hybrid approach) considerably improves the description of data. The neutron predictions, obtained via isospin symmetry, predict cross sections roughly twice as large as the proton ones, but scarce neutron data instead hint at cross sections of equal magnitude, indicating a possible isospin symmetry breaking or missing dynamics.
