# Soft triaxial roto-vibrational motion in the vicinity of $γ=π/6$

> A solution of the Bohr collective hamiltonian for the $β-$soft, $γ-$soft triaxial rotor with $γ\sim π/6$ is presented making use of a harmonic potential in $γ$ and Coulomb-like and Kratzer-like potentials in $β$. It is shown that, while the $γ-$angular part in the present case gives rise to a straightforward extension of the rigid triaxial rotor energy in which an additive harmonic term appears, the inclusion of the $β$ part results instead in a non-trivial expression for the spectrum. The negative anharmonicities of the energy levels with respect to a simple rigid model are in qualitative agreement with general trends in the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper presents a novel analytical solution of the Bohr collective Hamiltonian for a triaxial nucleus that is both β-soft and γ-soft, with the potential minimum located at γ = π/6; the solution shows that the naive expectation of a simple sum of rigid rotor plus harmonic vibrational terms is incorrect, because the β part produces a non-trivial combination of terms in the energy spectrum.

## Method

The author solves the Bohr Hamiltonian by choosing a separable potential of the form V(β,γ) = V₁(β) + V₂(γ)/β², with a harmonic oscillator potential in γ (displaced to γ₀ = π/6) and Coulomb-like or Kratzer-like potentials in β. The γ-angular part is solved by expanding around γ = π/6 using the small parameter x = γ − π/6, exploiting the simplification that two moments of inertia become equal at this angle, and the β part is solved by transforming the radial equation into Whittaker's differential equation.

## Key numbers

- The reduced energy eigenvalues are given by ε(nγ, nβ, L, R) = A²/4 / (√(9/4 + B + ω_{L,R,nγ}) + 1/2 + nβ)², where ω_{L,R,nγ} = √C (2nγ + 1) + L(L+1) − (3/4)R²
- The γ-vibrational term in ω is √C (2nγ + 1), where C is the strength of the harmonic potential in γ
- When B → ∞ (rigid β limit), the ground state band energies recover the rigid triaxial rotor energies

## Context

This work extends the rigid triaxial rotor model of Davydov (1960) and the Meyer-ter-vehn (1975) formula for γ = π/6 to include both β and γ vibrations in a fully solvable way. It is closely related to the Z(5) critical point symmetry introduced by Bonatsos (2004) for the prolate-to-oblate shape phase transition, but differs by using Coulomb and Kratzer potentials in β instead of an infinite square well. The solution enables systematic fitting of experimental data for triaxial nuclei where the potential minimum lies at γ ≈ 30°, particularly in mass regions where triaxiality is expected to play a relevant role.
