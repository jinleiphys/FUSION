# Efficient Method for Quantum Number Projection and Its Application to Tetrahedral Nuclear States

> We have developed an efficient method for quantum number projection from most general HFB type mean-field states, where all the symmetries like axial symmetry, number conservation, parity and time-reversal invariance are broken. Applying the method, we have microscopically calculated, for the first time, the energy spectra based on the exotic tetrahedral deformation in $^{108,110}$Zr. The nice low-lying rotational spectra, which have all characteristic features of the molecular tetrahedral rotor, are obtained for large tetrahedral deformation, $α_{32} \gtsim 0.25$, while the spectra are of transitional nature between vibrational and rotational with rather high excitation energies for $α_{32}\approx 0.1-0.2$

## Key claim

The paper presents the first microscopic calculation of energy spectra for tetrahedral nuclear states in \(^{108,110}\)Zr using a newly developed efficient quantum number projection method, but the results are obtained with a schematic Hamiltonian that cannot predict ground-state energies or deformations, only collective excitation spectra based on a given mean-field state.

## Method

The authors developed an efficient method for quantum number projection from general HFB-type mean-field states that break all symmetries (axial symmetry, number conservation, parity, time-reversal invariance). The method uses canonical basis truncation (keeping only states with occupation probability \(v_k^2 \ge \epsilon\)) and the Thouless form with respect to a Slater determinant to reduce matrix operations from \(O(M^3)\) to \(O(ML_p^2)\) where \(L_p \approx 150\) for a model space of \(M \approx 3,000\). They applied this method to calculate rotational spectra for tetrahedral deformation in \(^{108,110}\)Zr using a multi-separable Hamiltonian with Woods-Saxon single-particle potential and pairing interactions.

## Key numbers

- Model space dimension \(M = 3,542\) for \(N^{\rm max}_{\rm osc}=20\) spherical harmonic oscillator shells; after truncation with \(\epsilon \approx 10^{-3}-10^{-4}\), the \(P\)-space dimension is \(L_p \approx 150\) and core space dimension \(L_o \approx 50\)
- For \(^{110}\)Zr, the rigid-body moment of inertia is \(35.1\ \hbar^2/{\rm MeV}\); the calculated moment of inertia from the \(3^-_1\) energy is much smaller even at \(\alpha_{32} \approx 0.4\) without pairing
- The \(B(E3:3^-_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1)\) transition strength exceeds 100 Weisskopf units for \(\alpha_{32} \gtsim 0.3\)
- Tetrahedral deformation values studied: \(\alpha_{32} \approx 0.1-0.2\) (transitional spectra) and \(\alpha_{32} \gtsim 0.25\) (rotational spectra)
- A small cranking frequency \(\hbar\omega_{\rm rot} = 0.01\ {\rm MeV}\) about the \(x\)-axis was used in all calculations

## Context

This work extends earlier mean-field studies of tetrahedral nuclear shapes (Dudek 2002, and references therein) by providing the first microscopic quantum number projection calculations of excitation spectra and transition rates, which are necessary to identify tetrahedral states experimentally. The method enables future calculations with more realistic effective interactions (Skyrme, Gogny) once their density-dependence issues for projection are resolved, and the results for \(^{108,110}\)Zr connect to recent experimental work at RIKEN (Sumikama 2011) where a new isomer in \(^{108}\)Zr was speculated to be a tetrahedral state based on predictions by Schunck (2004).
