# The magnetic dual chiral density wave phase in a rotating cold quark matter

> The effect of rotation on the formation of the magnetic dual chiral density wave (\MD) in a dense and magnetized cold quark matter is studied. This phase is supposed to exist in the extreme conditions prevailing, e.g., in a neutron star. These conditions are, apart from high densities and strong magnetic fields, a relatively large angular velocity. To answer the question of whether the rotation enhances or suppresses the formation of this phase, we first determine the effect of rotation on the energy dispersion relation of a fermionic system in the presence of a constant magnetic field and then focus on the thermodynamic potential of the model at low temperature $T$ and finite chemical potential $μ$. The thermodynamic potential consists, in particular, of an anomalous part leading to certain topological effects. We show that in comparison with the nonrotating case, a term proportional to the angular velocity appears in this anomalous potential. We then solve the corresponding gap equations to the chiral and spatial modulation condensates, and study the dependence of these dynamical variables on the chemical potential ($μ$), magnetic field ($eB$), and angular velocity ($Ω$). It turns out that the interplay between these parameters suppresses the formation of the \MD~phase in relevant regimes for cold neutron stars. This is interpreted as the manifestation of the inverse magnetorotational catalysis, which is also reflected in the phase portraits $eB$-$μ$, $eB$-$ΩR$, and $μ$-$ΩR$, explored in this work.

## Key claim

The paper claims that rotation suppresses the formation of the magnetic dual chiral density wave (MDCDW) phase in cold, dense, magnetized quark matter, contrary to the expectation that this phase might be viable for neutron star interiors. The suppression is attributed to an "inverse magnetorotational catalysis" (IMRC) effect, which arises from the interplay between the angular velocity, magnetic field, and chemical potential, and the authors note that the robustness of this conclusion in a more realistic neutron star model (including beta-equilibrium, isospin symmetry, and charge neutrality) remains unclear.

## Method

The authors use a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a constant magnetic field aligned along the rotation axis, and introduce rigid rotation via an angular velocity term in the Hamiltonian. They derive the energy dispersion relations for the lowest and higher Landau levels in a cylindrical coordinate system, then compute the one-loop thermodynamic potential at zero temperature and finite chemical potential, separating it into a regularized zero-temperature part, a part depending on chemical potential and angular velocity, and an anomalous term. The gap equations for the chiral condensate (dynamical mass m) and the spatial modulation condensate (wave number b) are solved numerically to find the global minima of the effective potential.

## Key numbers

- The chemical potential range explored is μ ∈ [0, 0.8] GeV.
- The magnetic field range explored is √(eB) ∈ [0, 0.8] GeV, corresponding to eB ∈ [0, 0.64] GeV².
- The linear velocity range explored is ΩR ∈ [0, 0.1] (dimensionless, with R the cylinder radius).
- The maximum angular velocity for neutron stars is cited as Ω_max ∼ 10³ Hz, leading to linear velocities ∼ 10⁻²c to 10⁻¹c.
- The nuclear saturation mass density is given as ρ_n ∼ 2.5 × 10¹⁷ kg/m³.
- Magnetar magnetic field strengths are estimated as ∼ 10¹⁸ G for nuclear matter and ∼ 10²⁰ G for quark matter.

## Context

This work extends the study of the MDCDW phase by Incera et al. (2022), who argued that this phase is a viable candidate for neutron star matter at finite temperature and in the presence of magnetic fields, by adding the effect of rotation. It also builds on the earlier introduction of the MDCDW phase by Frolov et al. (2010) and the study of its topological effects by Ferrer et al. (2015, 2017). The paper shows that rotation, through the IMRC effect previously identified by Fukushima et al. (2015) and Sadooghi et al. (2021), suppresses both the chiral and spatial modulation condensates, potentially ruling out the MDCDW phase for rotating neutron stars. The authors note that the conclusions may change in a more realistic model incorporating beta-equilibrium, isospin symmetry, and charge neutrality.
