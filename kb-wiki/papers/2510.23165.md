# Ground-state properties of finite nuclei in relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory with an improved quark mass density-dependent model

> A relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model based on quark-meson coupling is developed, with a new parametrization derived from experimental observables. Using this model, we systematically investigate the ground-state properties of even-even nuclei spanning $8\leq Z\leq118$, including binding energies, quadrupole deformations, root-mean-square (rms) charge radii, two-nucleon separation energies, two-nucleon shell gaps, and $α$-decay energies. Comparisons with available experimental data demonstrate that this subnucleon-based RHB model reliably describes the ground-state properties of finite nuclei.

# Ground-state properties of finite nuclei in relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory with an improved quark mass density-dependent model

## Key claim

The relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model based on quark-meson coupling, with the new IQMDD3 parametrization, reliably describes ground-state properties of even-even nuclei across the entire nuclear chart from Z=8 to Z=118, but the model's performance for total binding energies is overall comparable to that of the NL3* functional, with an rms deviation of 2.893 MeV for total binding energy across 868 nuclei, which is larger than some established energy density functionals.

## Method

The authors develop a relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model that couples quark degrees of freedom to sigma, omega, and rho meson fields within the improved quark mass density-dependent (IQMDD) framework. A new parameter set called IQMDD3 is obtained by fitting to binding energies and charge radii of twenty spherical even-even nuclei using a combined simulated annealing and Levenberg-Marquardt optimization procedure. The RHB equations are solved by expanding wave functions in harmonic oscillator basis with N_f=12 and N_b=20 shells, and axially deformed calculations are performed for all nuclei.

## Key numbers

- RMS deviation of binding energy per nucleon: 0.034 MeV for IQMDD3 and 0.036 MeV for IQMDD-2* across 868 even-even nuclei from Z=8 to Z=118
- RMS deviation of total binding energy: 2.893 MeV for IQMDD3 and 2.984 MeV for IQMDD-2*
- RMS deviation of charge radii: 0.022 fm for IQMDD3 and 0.021 fm for IQMDD-2* across 368 even-even nuclei with known charge radii
- Neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb calculated with IQMDD3: 0.24 fm, falling within the experimental range of 0.283 plus or minus 0.071 fm from JLab parity-violating electron scattering
- Symmetry energy at saturation density for IQMDD3: 36.00 MeV, with slope parameter L=100.1 MeV
- Saturation density for IQMDD3: 0.153 fm^{-3}, binding energy per nucleon: -16.41 MeV, incompressibility: 292.8 MeV
- RMS deviation of two-neutron separation energies: 0.984 MeV for IQMDD3
- For nuclei from Calcium (Z=20) to Oganesson (Z=118), the rms deviation of binding energy per nucleon decreases to 0.020 MeV for IQMDD3 and 0.022 MeV for IQMDD-2*

## Context

This work extends the IQMDD model, previously applied to finite nuclei with the IQMDD-2* parameter set (Xu, 2013), by introducing a new parametrization fitted to updated experimental data from the AME2020 atomic mass evaluation and charge radii from Angeli (2013). The model provides a subnucleon-based alternative to conventional relativistic mean-field functionals such as NL3*, DD-ME2, and DD-PC1, and enables systematic investigation of ground-state properties across the entire nuclear chart including superheavy elements up to Z=118. The results enable future studies of exotic nuclei near drip lines and neutron star properties, and the observed anomalous charge radii in Cm isotopes confirm findings previously discussed by Agbemava (2014), Zhang (2022), and Guo (2024).
