# Isospin splitting of nucleon effective mass and shear viscosity of nuclear matter

> Based on an improved isospin- and momentum-dependent interaction, we have studied the qualitative effect of isospin splitting of nucleon effective mass on the specific shear viscosity of neutron-rich nuclear matter from a relaxation time approach. It is seen that for $m_n^\star>m_p^\star$, the relaxation time of neutrons is smaller and the neutron flux between flow layers is weaker, leading to a smaller specific shear viscosity of neutron-rich matter compared to the case for $m_n^\star<m_p^\star$. The effect is larger in nuclear matter at higher densities, lower temperatures, and larger isospin asymmetries, but it doesn't affect much the behavior of the specific shear viscosity near nuclear liquid-gas phase transition.

## Key claim

The specific shear viscosity of neutron-rich nuclear matter is qualitatively larger when the neutron effective mass is smaller than the proton effective mass (\(m_n^\star < m_p^\star\)) compared to the opposite case (\(m_n^\star > m_p^\star\)), but this effect does not alter the behavior of the specific shear viscosity near the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition.

## Method

The author used an improved isospin- and momentum-dependent interaction (ImMDI) to model nuclear matter, with two parameter sets chosen to produce opposite isospin splittings of the nucleon effective mass (\(m_n^\star > m_p^\star\) vs. \(m_n^\star < m_p^\star\)). The shear viscosity was calculated from a relaxation time approach, where the relaxation time depends on the in-medium nucleon-nucleon scattering cross section scaled by the square of the ratio of in-medium to free-space reduced mass. The specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s\) was then evaluated for various densities, temperatures, and isospin asymmetries, including the region of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition.

## Key numbers

- The specific shear viscosity \(\eta/s\) is smaller for \(m_n^\star > m_p^\star\) than for \(m_n^\star < m_p^\star\) in nuclear matter at saturation density \(\rho_0\) and isospin asymmetry \(\delta = 0.5\), with the difference increasing at higher densities and lower temperatures.
- At a fixed external pressure of \(P = 0.05\) MeV/fm\(^3\) and isospin asymmetry \(\delta = 0.5\), the minimum value of \(\eta/s\) near the liquid-gas phase transition occurs at a higher temperature with increasing pressure, and this minimum is not affected by the isospin splitting of the nucleon effective mass.
- The specific shear viscosity increases with increasing isospin asymmetry \(\delta\) faster than a parabolic relation, with a stronger increase at higher densities or lower temperatures.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the specific shear viscosity in neutron-rich matter by Xu (2011, 2013) using an earlier isospin- and momentum-dependent interaction, now employing the improved ImMDI interaction to flexibly control the isovector properties. The study is motivated by recent experimental data from the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory (Coupled et al., 2014) favoring a smaller neutron effective mass than proton, in contrast to the Lane potential (Li et al., 2004) which predicts a larger neutron effective mass. The results enable future experimental searches for the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition in heavy-ion collisions via measurements of the specific shear viscosity, as suggested by Zhou et al. (2014).
