# In-medium nuclear cluster energies within the Extended Thomas-Fermi approach

> A recently introduced analytical model for the nuclear density profile[1] is implemented in the Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) energy density functional. This allows to (i) shed a new light on the issue of the sign of surface symmetry energy in nuclear mass formulas, which is strongly related to the non-uniformity of the isospin asymmetry in finite nuclei, as well as to (ii) evaluate the in-medium corrections to the nuclear cluster energies in thermodynamic conditions relevant for the description of the (proto)-neutron star crust. The ground state configurations of the model are compared to Hartree-Fock calculations in spherical symmetry for some selected isotopic chains, and systematic errors are quantified. The in-medium modification of the nuclear mass due to the presence of a gas component is shown to strongly depend both on the density and the asymmetry of the nucleon gas. This shows the importance of accounting for such effects in the realistic modelizations of the equation of state for core-collapse supernovae and proto-neutron stars.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a simple Fermi-Dirac (FD) parametrization of nuclear density profiles, combined with the Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) energy density functional at second order in the h-bar expansion, reproduces Hartree-Fock binding energies with an accuracy of 150-300 keV/nucleon, and that the sign of the surface symmetry energy depends on whether the bulk asymmetry or the global asymmetry is used to define the subtracted bulk energy.

## Method
The authors implement a simple Fermi-Dirac (FD) parametrization for the total isoscalar and proton density profiles, with the diffuseness parameter depending quadratically on the bulk asymmetry delta. The Skyrme functional (SLy4 interaction) is evaluated using the ETF semi-classical expansion up to second order in h-bar, including spin-orbit currents and effective mass corrections, and the total energy is computed by spatial integration of the energy density. The model is compared to spherical Hartree-Fock calculations for selected isotopic chains (Ca, Sn, Pb) and to a generalized Fermi-Dirac (GFD) variational solution for symmetric nuclei.

## Key numbers
- The ETF model with second-order h-bar corrections reproduces HF binding energies with an accuracy of approximately 300 keV/nucleon for lighter nuclei and does not exceed 150 keV/nucleon for heavy nuclei (Fig. 5).
- The difference between ETF and HF energies for the Sn isotopic chain is comparable to the difference between HF and experimental data (full circles in Fig. 5).
- The Coulomb parameter a_C is taken equal to 0.69 MeV in the relation between bulk asymmetry delta and global asymmetry I (Eq. 8).
- The GFD model systematically produces more binding than the FD model for symmetric N=Z nuclei (Fig. 2).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analytical model (Aymard et al., 2014) which used zeroth-order Thomas-Fermi approximation, by including second-order h-bar corrections that introduce spin-orbit interaction and improved kinetic energy density. The model addresses the long-standing ambiguity in the sign of the surface symmetry energy in nuclear mass formulas, previously discussed by Myers (1985), Farine (1986), and Centelles (1998), showing that the choice between bulk asymmetry delta and global asymmetry I determines the sign. The results enable improved modeling of nuclear clusters in the inner crust of neutron stars and in supernova matter, where in-medium corrections from a surrounding nucleon gas are important, complementing the single-nucleus approximation used in equations of state by Lattimer and Swesty (1991) and Shen et al. (1998).
