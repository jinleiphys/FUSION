# Closed-shell properties of $^{24}$O with {\em ab initio} coupled-cluster theory

> We present an \emph{ab initio} calculation of spectroscopic factors for neutron and proton removal from $^{24}$O using the coupled-cluster method and a state-of-the-art chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order. In order to account for the coupling to the scattering continuum we use a Berggren single-particle basis that treats bound, resonant, and continuum states on an equal footing. We report neutron removal spectroscopic factors for the $^{23}$O states $J^π = 1/2^+$, $5/2^+$, $3/2^-$ and $1/2^-$, and proton removal spectroscopic factors for the $^{23}$N states $1/2^-$ and $3/2^-$. Our calculations support the accumulated experimental evidence that $^{24}$O is a closed-shell nucleus.

## Key claim

The authors present *ab initio* coupled-cluster calculations of spectroscopic factors for neutron and proton removal from ²⁴O that support the interpretation of ²⁴O as a closed-shell nucleus, but they note that the results remain model-dependent because the chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction used omits three-body forces, which may significantly affect the single-particle energy spacing.

## Method

The authors employed the coupled-cluster singles and doubles (CCSD) approximation with a chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N³LO) from Entem and Machleidt. The ground state of ²⁴O was computed using the coupled-cluster ansatz, while the A-1 body systems (²³O and ²³N) were obtained via particle-removal equation-of-motion coupled-cluster (PR-EOM-CCSD). To account for coupling to the scattering continuum, a Berggren single-particle basis was used for the neutron s₁/₂, d₃/₂, and d₅/₂ partial waves, treating bound, resonant, and continuum states on an equal footing.

## Key numbers

- Neutron removal spectroscopic factor for the 1/2⁺ state in ²³O: 1.832 (calculated at ħω = 30 MeV)
- Neutron removal spectroscopic factor for the 5/2⁺ state in ²³O: 5.393 (calculated at ħω = 30 MeV)
- Neutron removal spectroscopic factor for the 3/2⁻ state in ²³O: 1.919 (calculated at ħω = 30 MeV)
- Neutron removal spectroscopic factor for the 1/2⁻ state in ²³O: 1.116 (calculated at ħω = 30 MeV)
- Proton removal spectroscopic factor for the 3/2⁻ state in ²³N: 2.609 (calculated at ħω = 30 MeV)
- Proton removal spectroscopic factor for the 1/2⁻ state in ²³N: 1.254 (calculated at ħω = 30 MeV)
- Energy spacing between the 1/2⁺ and 5/2⁺ states in ²³O: 0.35 MeV (compared to 2.586 MeV from SDPF-M and 2.593 MeV from USDB shell model calculations)
- Experimental neutron removal spectroscopic factor for the 1/2⁺ state in ²³O: 1.71 ± 0.19 (from Kanungo et al., 2009)

## Context

This work extends earlier shell-model calculations using fitted interactions such as USDB (Brown, 2006) and SDPF-M (Utsuno, 1999) by providing an *ab initio* approach with reduced model dependence. The results support the experimental evidence from Kanungo et al. (2009) that ²⁴O is a doubly magic nucleus, though the authors note that the small energy spacing between the 1/2⁺ and 5/2⁺ states (0.35 MeV) contradicts the larger spacing found in those shell-model calculations. The work enables future application of *ab initio* radial overlap functions to neutron-knockout reactions on drip-line nuclei, removing a level of model dependence from reaction studies.
