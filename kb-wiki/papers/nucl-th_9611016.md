# Influence of rescattering on the spectra of strange particles

> Applying a new method of rescattering which is based on the neural network technique we study the influence of rescattering on the spectra of strange particles produced in heavy ion reactions. In contradistinction to former approaches the rescattering is done explicitly and not in a perturbative fashion. We present a comparison of our calculations for the system Ni(1.93AGeV)+Ni with recent data of the FOPI collaboration. We find that even for this small system rescattering changes the observables considerably but does not invalidate the role of the kaons as a messenger from the high density zone. We cannot confirm the conjecture that the kaon flow can be of use for the determination of the optical potential of the kaon.

## Key claim

Rescattering of kaons in Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 AGeV changes the observable spectra considerably, but does not invalidate the role of kaons as messengers from the high density zone; however, the authors cannot confirm the conjecture that kaon flow can be used to determine the optical potential of the kaon.

## Method

The authors embedded a neural-network-based method for simulating kaon-nucleon rescattering into the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) approach, which simulates heavy ion reactions on an event-by-event basis. The neural network was trained on measured differential cross sections for elastic KN channels and charge exchange channels, allowing fast and precise sampling of scattering angles. Kaon production was artificially enhanced to achieve kaon production in about 30% of collisions, and the time evolution of kaons and Λ particles was followed explicitly with rescattering, rather than using the perturbative approach employed in prior work.

## Key numbers

- The beam energy is 1.93 AGeV for the system Ni+Ni.
- The threshold for kaon production in NN collisions is E_beam = 1.583 GeV.
- A kaon is produced only in 1 event out of one thousand at central collisions of Ni+Ni at 1.93 AGeV.
- The ΛN elastic scattering cross section used is 16.4 mb.
- Rescattering increases the slope T_B of the transverse energy spectra by about 70%, almost independent of rapidity.
- The increase of the average transverse root mean square momentum due to rescattering is about 25%.
- 79% of the kaons are produced at densities ρ > ρ_0 (above normal nuclear matter density).
- 60.5% of the kaons either did not suffer rescattering or had their last collision at a density larger than normal nuclear matter density.
- The root mean square rapidity of kaons in the NN center of mass system is 70% larger than that for the Λ's.
- The variance in rapidity of kaons increases by 12% due to rescattering, compared to 39% for Λ's.

## Context

This work extends prior perturbative treatments of kaon rescattering, such as the approach by Randrup (1980) and an improved version by Fang et al., by implementing rescattering explicitly with realistic differential cross sections rather than assuming isotropic scattering. The results are compared with data from the FOPI collaboration at GSI for Ni(1.93AGeV)+Ni and with calculations by Li et al. using a relativistic RBUU program. The paper enables future studies of kaon production in heavier systems and investigations of K- properties in nuclear matter, which the authors note is under investigation.
