# The beta-delayed neutron emission in 78Ni region

> A systematic study of the total $β$-decay half-lives and $β$-delayed neutron emission probabilities is performed. The $β$-strength function is treated within the self-consistent density-functional + continuum-QRPA framework including the Gamow-Teller and first-forbidden transitions. The experimental total $β$-decay half-lives for the Ni isotopes with $A\leq$76 are described satisfactorily. The half-lives predicted from $A$=70 up to $A$=86 reveal fairly regular $A$-behaviour which results from simultaneous account for the Gamow-Teller and first-forbidden transitions. For $Z\approx$ 28 nuclei, a suppression of the delayed neutron emission probability is found when the $N$=50 neutron closed shell is crossed. The effect originates from the high-energy first-forbidden transitions to the states outside the $Q_β - S_n$-window in the daughter nuclei. PACS numbers: 23.40.Bw,21.60.Jz,25.30.Pt,26.30.+k

## Key claim

The paper predicts a suppression of beta-delayed neutron emission probabilities for nuclei near Z=28 when crossing the N=50 closed neutron shell, an effect that arises specifically because the high-energy first-forbidden transitions that dominate the total half-lives lie outside the Q_beta minus S_n energy window available for neutron emission.

## Method

The beta-strength function was calculated within a self-consistent density-functional plus continuum-QRPA framework that includes both Gamow-Teller and first-forbidden transitions on equal footing. The ground state description used the Fayans DF3 energy-density functional, and the continuum QRPA equations were solved with exact treatment of the particle-hole continuum. The total beta-decay half-lives and delayed neutron emission probabilities were computed for Ni and Ga isotopic chains, with the neutron emission probability expressed through the Hausser-Feshbach statistical model assuming gamma-emission from neutron-unbound states is negligible.

## Key numbers

- For Ni isotopes with A=74-76, the calculated half-lives slightly underestimate experimental data from Franchoo (1998) and Ameil (1998)
- For Ni isotopes with A=71-86, the total half-lives reveal a fairly regular A-behavior when both GT and FF transitions are included
- For Ni isotopes with A=79 and above, crossing N=50, the high-energy FF transitions give a dominant contribution to the total half-life
- For Ga isotopes with A=82-87, the P_n-values calculated with GT+FF are suppressed by a factor of 4-5 compared to the GT-only approximation
- For Ni-83, varying B_n within plus or minus 1 MeV changes the P_n-value from 62.7% to a range of 70.8% to 65.3%
- For Ni-80, using a quenching factor of 0.8 instead of 0.9 gives a P_n-value of 71%
- The MSk force predicts quasi-spherical shape (beta_2 less than or equal to 0.1) for Ni isotopes with A=74-94 and Ga isotopes with A=79-83

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent DF+CQRPA framework previously developed by Borzov (2003) to include the impact of first-forbidden decays on delayed neutron emission, going beyond the allowed-transitions approximation used in global calculations by Moller (1997) and Hirsch (1992). The predicted suppression of P_n-values for Z=28 isotopes above N=50 provides a testable prediction for upcoming experiments with isotope-separated beams, particularly for Ga, Zn, and Ge-As isotopic chains which are more accessible experimentally in the short term according to Franchoo (2004).
