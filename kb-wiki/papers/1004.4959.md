# Higher Moments of Net-proton Multiplicity Distributions at RHIC

> We report the first measurements of the kurtosis (κ), skewness (S) and variance (σ^2) of net-proton multiplicity (N_p - N_pbar) distributions at midrapidity for Au+Au collisions at \sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6, 62.4, and 200 GeV corresponding to baryon chemical potentials (μ_B) between 200 - 20 MeV. Our measurements of the products κσ^2 and S σ, which can be related to theoretical calculations sensitive to baryon number susceptibilities and long range correlations, are constant as functions of collision centrality. We compare these products with results from lattice QCD and various models without a critical point and study the \sqrt(s_NN) dependence of κσ^2. From the measurements at the three beam energies, we find no evidence for a critical point in the QCD phase diagram for μ_B below 200 MeV.

## Key claim

The STAR Collaboration reports the first measurements of the kurtosis, skewness, and variance of net-proton multiplicity distributions at midrapidity for Au+Au collisions at three beam energies, and finds that the products kappa*sigma^2 and S*sigma are constant as functions of collision centrality. Based on these measurements at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6, 62.4, and 200 GeV, corresponding to baryon chemical potentials between 200 and 20 MeV, the authors conclude there is no evidence for a critical point in the QCD phase diagram for mu_B below 200 MeV, though this conclusion is conditional on the specific observables and energy range studied.

## Method

The STAR experiment measured net-proton multiplicity distributions (N_p minus N_pbar) at midrapidity in Au+Au collisions at three center-of-mass energies: 19.6, 62.4, and 200 GeV per nucleon pair. The variance (sigma^2), skewness (S), and kurtosis (kappa) of these distributions were extracted as functions of collision centrality. The products kappa*sigma^2 and S*sigma, which are related to baryon number susceptibilities and long-range correlations, were computed and compared with lattice QCD calculations and models without a critical point.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6, 62.4, and 200 GeV, corresponding to baryon chemical potentials mu_B between 200 and 20 MeV.
- The products kappa*sigma^2 and S*sigma were found to be constant as functions of collision centrality at all three beam energies.
- No evidence for a critical point was found for baryon chemical potentials mu_B below 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the search for the QCD critical point using higher moments of conserved charge distributions, a program motivated by lattice QCD predictions for baryon number susceptibilities. The results are compared with lattice QCD calculations and models without a critical point, such as those by Stephanov (2004) and by Ejiri, Karsch, and Redlich (2006). The measurements provide baseline data for future critical point searches at lower beam energies in the RHIC Beam Energy Scan program, and constrain the location of the critical point to mu_B values above 200 MeV.
