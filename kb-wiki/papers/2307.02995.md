# Collective flow and the fluid behavior in p/d/$^3$He+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV

> By varying the intrinsic initial geometry, the p/d/$^3$He+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) provide a unique opportunity to understand the collective behavior and probe the possible sub-nucleon fluctuations in small systems. In this paper, we employ the hybrid model iEBE-VISHNU with Trento initial conditions to study the collective flow and the fluid behavior in p/d/$^3$He+Au collisions. With fine-tuned parameters, iEBE-VISHNU can describe the $v_2(p_T)$ and $v_3(p_T)$ data from the PHENIX and STAR collaborations. However, for some certain parameter sets with initial sub-nucleon fluctuation, the hydrodynamic simulations have already beyond their limits with the average Knudsen number $\langle K_n \rangle$ obviously larger than one. Our calculations demonstrate that, for a meaningful evaluation of the fluid behavior in the small systems, model simulations should also pay attention to the validity range of hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The hybrid model iEBE-VISHNU with Trento initial conditions can describe the $v_2(p_T)$ and $v_3(p_T)$ data from both PHENIX and STAR for p/d/$^3$He+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, but only if one accepts that for parameter sets with sub-nucleon fluctuations that fit the STAR data, the hydrodynamic simulations operate beyond their validity range with average Knudsen numbers $\langle K_n \rangle$ larger than one.

## Method

The authors used the 2+1-d event-by-event hybrid model iEBE-VISHNU, which combines VISH2+1 viscous hydrodynamics for the QGP evolution, the iSS particle sampler for particlization at a switching temperature, and the UrQMD hadron cascade for subsequent hadronic evolution. Initial entropy density profiles were generated using the Trento parameterized initial condition model, with three parameter sets: Para-I (sub-nucleon fluctuations tuned to STAR data), Para-II (nucleon fluctuations tuned to PHENIX data), and Para-III (sub-nucleon fluctuations with free-streaming tuned to PHENIX data). The authors calculated anisotropic flow coefficients $v_2$ and $v_3$ as functions of transverse momentum $p_T$, 4-particle cumulants $c_2\{4\}$, and evaluated the validity of hydrodynamics by computing the average Knudsen number $\langle K_n \rangle$ throughout the evolution.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV for p/d/$^3$He+Au collisions at RHIC
- Para-I specific shear viscosity: $(\eta/s)_{\text{min}} = 0.28$, with switching temperature $T_{\text{switch}} = 154$ MeV
- Para-II specific shear viscosity: $(\eta/s)_{\text{min}} = 0.09$, with switching temperature $T_{\text{switch}} = 151$ MeV
- Para-III specific shear viscosity: $(\eta/s)_{\text{min}} = 0.11$, with free-streaming time $\tau_0 = 0.37$ fm/c and switching temperature $T_{\text{switch}} = 151$ MeV
- For Para-I, the average Knudsen number $\langle K_n \rangle$ is always larger than 1 throughout the whole evolution for all three collision systems
- For Para-II, the average Knudsen number is about or less than 1
- STAR $v_3(p_T)$ data in p+Au and d+Au collisions are larger than PHENIX data by a factor of 3
- The $v_3/v_2$ ratio band from iEBE-VISHNU with $\langle K_n \rangle < 1$ constraint lies below the STAR data for p+Au collisions

## Context

This work extends previous hydrodynamic studies of small collision systems at RHIC, particularly following the PHENIX (2018) and STAR (2022) measurements of flow harmonics in p/d/$^3$He+Au collisions. The PHENIX measurements had largely ruled out CGC calculations with only initial state effects (Mace 2018, Nagle 2018), which predicted different $v_2$ and $v_3$ orderings of smaller magnitude. The study builds on earlier hydrodynamic calculations with MC-Glauber initial conditions (Nagle 2013, Welsh 2016, Shen 2016) and Trento-based Bayesian analyses for p+Pb and Pb+Pb collisions (Moreland 2018). The key finding is that current hybrid model calculations cannot simultaneously describe the STAR flow data in all three small collision systems with reasonable parameter ranges within the hydrodynamic limit ($\langle K_n \rangle < 1$), suggesting the need for improved theoretical approaches such as anisotropic hydrodynamics or models incorporating core-corona effects and mini-jet contributions.
