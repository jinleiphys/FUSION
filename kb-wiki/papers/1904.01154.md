# Quantized first-order phase transition and two sets of critical end point in droplet quark matter

> The finite-size effect on the chiral phase transition is investigated in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. To take into account finite-size effects, momentum integrals are replaced by momentum summations. The ground state of quark matter at finite size is favored when applying the periodic spatial boundary condition for quarks. The zero-momentum contribution is taken into account in the periodic boundary condition, and its contribution becomes important when the system size is comparable with the pion wavelength. When the zero-mode contribution becomes dominant, the conventional first-order chiral phase transition at high baryon chemical potential splits into two first-order phase transitions in small system of quark matter, and two sets of critical end point show up in the temperature and chemical potential plane.

# Quantized first-order phase transition and two sets of critical end point in droplet quark matter

## Key claim

The conventional first-order chiral phase transition at high baryon chemical potential splits into two first-order phase transitions in small systems of quark matter (size between 2 fm and 5 fm), producing two sets of critical end points in the temperature-chemical potential plane, but only when the periodic spatial boundary condition (including the zero-momentum mode) is applied to quarks.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in a cubic box of finite length L, replacing momentum integrals with momentum summations to incorporate finite-size effects. They compare periodic and anti-periodic spatial boundary conditions for quarks, finding that the periodic boundary condition (which includes the zero-momentum mode) gives the lower thermodynamical potential and is thus the favored ground state. The chiral condensate and pion mass are computed from the gap equation and the random phase approximation, respectively, using both Pauli-Villars and hard-cutoff regularization schemes.

## Key numbers

- Pion wavelength: λ_π = 1/m_π ≈ 1.41 fm; finite-size effects become significant when system size L is comparable to this value.
- The quantized first-order phase transition with two jumps appears in the size region 2 fm < L < 5 fm; for L > 5 fm only one jump survives, and for L < 2 fm only one jump survives.
- At zero temperature and zero chemical potential, the constituent quark mass M_0 (including zero-mode contribution) enhances dramatically for L < 2 fm, while M_1 (without zero-mode) decreases to zero at very small size.
- The freeze-out line used for kurtosis calculations is T(μ_B) = 0.158 − 0.14 μ_B^2 − 0.04 μ_B^4 (units in GeV), with μ_B(√s) = 1.477/(1 + 0.343√s).

## Context

This work extends the study of finite-size effects on QCD phase transitions, which have been investigated using multiple reflection expansion (Kiriyama, 2006), momentum summations (Braun, 2011; Palhares, 2009; Almasi, 2016), and lower momentum cutoffs (Pan, 2016). The authors resolve the long-standing ambiguity between periodic and anti-periodic spatial boundary conditions for quarks by showing that the periodic boundary condition gives the lower ground state. The catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking in small systems is compared to the similar phenomenon in quark matter under strong magnetic fields (Gusynin, 1994; Miransky, 2015). The predicted double-peak structure of kurtosis κσ² along the freeze-out line provides a potential experimental signature for beam energy scan experiments at RHIC, FAIR, and NICA.
