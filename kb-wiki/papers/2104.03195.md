# Fermion Pair Dynamics in Open Quantum Systems

> Three-body decay is a rare decay mode observed in a handful of unbound rare isotopes. The angular and energy correlations between emitted nucleons are of particular interest, as they provide invaluable information on the interplay between structure and reaction aspects of the nuclear open quantum system. To study the mechanism of two-nucleon emission, we developed a time-dependent approach that allows us to probe emitted nucleons at long times and large distances. We successfully benchmarked the new method against the Green's function approach and applied it to low-energy two-proton and two-neutron decays. In particular, we studied the interplay between initial-state nucleon-nucleon correlations and final-state interaction. We demonstrated that the time evolution of the two-nucleon wave function is strongly impacted by the diproton/dineutron dynamics and that the correlations between emitted nucleons provide invaluable information on the dinucleon structure in the initial-state.

# Fermion Pair Dynamics in Open Quantum Systems

## Key claim

The time evolution of two-nucleon wave functions in three-body decay is strongly impacted by diproton/dineutron dynamics, and the correlations between emitted nucleons provide information on the dinucleon structure in the initial state, but the initial-state correlations are largely lost in the final state for two-proton decay due to Coulomb repulsion, with only some fingerprints surviving in specific asymptotic observables.

## Method

The authors developed a time-dependent approach based on the Gamow coupled-channel (GCC) method, using a Berggren basis expansion in complex-momentum space to obtain the initial resonance state, then propagating the wave packet via Chebyshev expansion of the time evolution operator in real-momentum space. The method was benchmarked against a Green's function approach and applied to low-energy two-proton decay from the ground state of ⁶Be and two-neutron decay from an artificially unbound ⁶He' system, using Jacobi coordinates with hyperspherical-harmonics expansion and a model space up to hyperspherical quantum number Kmax = 50.

## Key numbers

- The two-proton decay of ⁶Be has Q2p = 1.372 MeV (experimental decay energy), with a calculated decay width of 64 keV.
- The artificially unbound ⁶He' was created with Q2n = 1 MeV, yielding a calculated decay width of 241 keV.
- The time-dependent calculations provided precise solutions at distances over 500 fm and times up to 30 pm/c.
- The model space used max(ℓx, ℓy) ≤ 7 and Kmax = 50, with the Berggren basis for K ≤ 10 channels and harmonic oscillator basis (oscillator length 1.75 fm, Nmax = 20) for remaining channels.
- The angular correlation in two-proton decay stabilizes only after times greater than 9 pm/c.
- The neighboring ground state of ⁵Li has a proton decay width of Γ = 1.23 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Gamow coupled-channel method previously developed by Wang and Nazarewicz (2017, 2018) to a time-dependent formalism capable of describing asymptotic observables at very large distances, which was not possible with earlier approaches. It contrasts with WKB approaches (Goncalves 2017, Nazarewicz 1996), R-matrix theory (Barker 2003, Brown 2003), and three-body reaction models (Grigorenko 2009, 2017) that treat internal and asymptotic regions separately, and improves upon earlier time-dependent treatments (Oishi 2014, 2017) that could not capture asymptotic dynamics. The results enable direct comparison with experimental angular and energy correlation measurements for two-proton emitters like ⁶Be and provide a framework for studying the interplay between nuclear pairing and Coulomb effects in open quantum systems.
