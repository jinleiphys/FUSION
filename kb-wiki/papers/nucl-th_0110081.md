# Scaling Calculation of Isoscalar Giant Resonances in Relativistic Thomas-Fermi Theory

> We derive analytical expressions for the excitation energy of the isoscalar giant monopole and quadrupole resonances in finite nuclei, by using the scaling method and the extended Thomas-Fermi approach to relativistic mean field theory. We study the ability of several non-linear sigma-omega parameter sets of common use in reproducing the experimental data. For monopole oscillations the calculations agree better with experiment when the nuclear matter incompressibility of the relativistic interaction lies in the range 220-260 MeV. The breathing-mode energies of the scaling method compare satisfactorily with those obtained in relativistic RPA and time-dependent mean field calculations. For quadrupole oscillations all the analyzed non-linear parameter sets reproduce the empirical trends reasonably well.

## Key claim

The authors derive analytical expressions for isoscalar giant monopole and quadrupole resonance excitation energies using the scaling method within relativistic Thomas-Fermi and extended Thomas-Fermi approaches, finding that agreement with experimental data for monopole oscillations requires the nuclear matter incompressibility of the relativistic interaction to lie in the range 220-260 MeV, though this conclusion is qualified by the dependence on the specific non-linear parameter set used.

## Method

The authors applied the scaling method to the relativistic Thomas-Fermi (RTF) and relativistic extended Thomas-Fermi (RETF) approaches within the non-linear sigma-omega model of relativistic mean field theory, deriving analytical expressions for the restoring force and mass parameter of isoscalar giant monopole and quadrupole resonances in finite nuclei. They solved the semiclassical Euler-Lagrange equations coupled to the meson field equations self-consistently for spherical nuclei, without using a leptodermous expansion as in previous scaling approaches. For the monopole resonance, they also performed constrained calculations for comparison with the scaling results.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter incompressibility range for best agreement with monopole experimental data: 220-260 MeV
- For NL1 parameter set (K_infinity = 211 MeV), RETF excitation energy for 208Pb: 12.7 MeV
- For NL2 parameter set (K_infinity = 399 MeV), RETF excitation energy for 208Pb: 18.4 MeV
- RETF energies differ from RTF energies by less than 10% for the GMR energy
- Relative difference between RETF and RTF energies for NL1 ranges from -7% in 16O to -1.6% in 208Pb
- NL1 incompressibility: 211 MeV, effective mass m*_infinity/m = 0.57
- NL3 incompressibility: 272 MeV, effective mass m*_infinity/m = 0.60
- NL-SH incompressibility: 355 MeV, effective mass m*_infinity/m = 0.60
- NL2 incompressibility: 399 MeV, effective mass m*_infinity/m = 0.67

## Context

This work extends previous scaling calculations of isoscalar giant resonances in the linear sigma-omega model by Nishizaki (1987) and Zhu (1991) to non-linear parameter sets of the relativistic mean field theory, and improves upon earlier scaling approaches by Stoitsov (1994), Eiff (1994), and Chossy (1997) by performing fully self-consistent calculations without a leptodermous expansion. The results enable systematic comparison of relativistic effective interactions with experimental giant monopole resonance data to constrain the nuclear matter incompressibility, complementing relativistic RPA calculations by Mueller (1989), Dawson (1990), and Piekarewicz (2001) and time-dependent RMF studies by Vretenar (1995, 1997).
