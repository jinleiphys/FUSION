# Landau levels in QCD

> We present first evidence for the Landau level structure of Dirac eigenmodes in full QCD for nonzero background magnetic fields, based on first principles lattice simulations using staggered quarks. Our approach involves the identification of the lowest Landau level modes in two dimensions, where topological arguments ensure a clear separation of these modes from energetically higher states, and an expansion of the full four-dimensional modes in the basis of these two-dimensional states. We evaluate various fermionic observables including the quark condensate and the spin polarization in this basis to find how much the lowest Landau level contributes to them. The results allow for a deeper insight into the dynamics of quarks and gluons in background magnetic fields and may be directly compared to low-energy models of QCD employing the lowest Landau level approximation.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first evidence that the Landau level structure of Dirac eigenmodes persists in full QCD with nonzero background magnetic fields, based on lattice simulations. The key hedge is that the identification relies on a two-dimensional projection method where topological arguments guarantee a clear separation of the lowest Landau level from higher states, but the four-dimensional modes never correspond purely to the LLL or to a HLL due to mixing via gluon fields.

## Method

The authors used lattice QCD simulations with staggered quarks on ensembles at finite temperature (around 400 MeV) and various lattice spacings. They first identified the lowest Landau level modes in two-dimensional slices of the four-dimensional lattice, where the index theorem ensures a gap separates LLL from HLL states even with QCD interactions. They then constructed a projector onto the LLL subspace by expanding four-dimensional modes in the basis of these two-dimensional LLL states, and evaluated fermionic observables (quark condensate and spin polarization) with the projector inserted only in the valence sector, while keeping the sea quarks at zero magnetic field.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic flux quantum satisfies \(N_b = qB L^2 / (2\pi) \in \mathbb{Z}\), with the lattice constraint \(N_b = 0,1,\ldots,N_s^2\) for a lattice with \(N_s^2\) points.
- For the free case in two dimensions, the squared energies are \(\lambda_n^2 = qB \cdot 2n\) with degeneracy \(\nu_n = N_b \cdot N_c \cdot (2-\delta_{n,0})\) where \(N_c=3\).
- The LLL degeneracy in two dimensions is \(N_c N_b \times 2\) (including the twofold staggered doubling), and for the magnetic flux \(N_b=8\) this gives 24 LLL modes.
- The gap width in physical units was computed for lattices with \(N_s/N_t=4\) and \(N_s=16\ldots 48\) at \(T\approx 400\) MeV, and the gap edges remain well-defined in the continuum limit.
- The localization length of the LLL-projected fermion action in the continuum limit is consistent with \(\ell_B = 1/\sqrt{q_d B} \approx 0.28\) fm for \(q_d B = 0.5\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the study of magnetic field effects in QCD from composite particles (where Landau levels were observed for charged pions by Bali et al. (2011) and Chang et al. (2015)) to the quark level, where strong interactions could potentially destroy the Landau level hierarchy. The results enable direct comparison with low-energy models of QCD that employ the lowest Landau level approximation, such as those by Fukushima (2011), Leung (2005), and Ferrer (2013), and provide a framework to test the validity of the LLL approximation for observables like the quark condensate and spin polarization.
