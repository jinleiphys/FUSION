# Skyrme N2LO pseudo-potential for calculations of properties of atomic nuclei

> We present recent developments obtained in the so-called N2LO extension of the usual Skyrme pseudo-potential. In particular, we discuss the isovector splitting mass in infinite nuclear matter and the pairing gaps of selected semi-magic even-even nuclei.

## Key claim

The N2LO extension of the Skyrme pseudo-potential, as implemented in the SN2LO1 parametrization, yields pairing gaps and effective mass behavior that are qualitatively similar to the standard SLy5* functional, but the isovector mass splitting remains incompatible with ab-initio results, although it moves in the right direction.

## Method

The authors derived the N2LO Skyrme pseudo-potential functional from a momentum expansion up to fourth order, then solved Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations using the WHISKY code for spherical semi-magic even-even nuclei. They used a density-dependent contact pairing interaction with three different forms (volume, mixed, surface) and adjusted the pairing strength to reproduce the pairing gap in 120Sn, while the mean-field parameters were fixed from the earlier SN2LO1 fit that included constraints from infinite nuclear matter and linear response theory.

## Key numbers

- Effective mass at saturation density in symmetric nuclear matter: m*/m = 0.71 for SN2LO1, compared to 0.7 for SLy5*
- Saturation density used: ρ0 = 0.16 fm−3
- Pairing strengths used: V0 = −200 MeV·fm3 (volume), V0 = −300.2 MeV·fm3 (mixed), V0 = −496.6 MeV·fm3 (surface)
- Energy cutoff in quasiparticle space: Ecut = 60 MeV
- Pairing gaps calculated for Ca, Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopic chains as function of neutron number N

## Context

This work extends the earlier study of the N2LO Skyrme pseudo-potential by Becker et al. (2017) from double-magic nuclei to open-shell semi-magic nuclei, specifically examining pairing gaps. It builds on the derivation of higher-order Skyrme terms from finite-range interactions like Gogny (Decharge and Gogny, 1980) and M3Y (Nakada, 2003), and follows the demonstration by Carlsson et al. (2010) that N2LO terms reduce discrepancies in density matrix expansion. The results enable future systematic statistical analysis of the extended functional and identification of observables that can better constrain higher-order parameters, as suggested by Dobaczewski et al. (2014) and Niksic and Vretenar (2016).
