# The Crystallography of Strange Quark Matter

> Cold three-flavor quark matter at large (but not asymptotically large) densities may exist as a crystalline color superconductor. We explore this possibility by calculating the gap parameter Delta and free energy Omega(Delta) for possible crystal structures within a Ginzburg-Landau approximation, evaluating Omega(Delta) to order Delta^6. We develop a qualitative understanding of what makes a crystal structure stable, and find two structures with particularly large values of Delta and the condensation energy, within a factor of two of those for the CFL phase known to characterize QCD at asymptotically large densities. The robustness of these phases results in their being favored over wide ranges of density and though it also implies that the Ginzburg-Landau approximation is not quantitatively reliable, previous work suggests that it can be trusted for qualitative comparisons between crystal structures. We close with a look ahead at the calculations that remain to be done in order to make contact with observed pulsar glitches and neutron star cooling.

## Key claim

Cold three-flavor quark matter at intermediate densities (not asymptotically large) may exist as a crystalline color superconductor, with two specific crystal structures (CubeX and 2Cube45z) yielding gap parameters and condensation energies within a factor of two of the CFL phase, though the Ginzburg-Landau approximation used is not quantitatively reliable and likely underestimates both quantities.

## Method

The authors evaluate the gap parameter Delta and free energy Omega(Delta) for 11 candidate crystal structures within a Ginzburg-Landau approximation, expanding the free energy to order Delta^6. They compute the Ginzburg-Landau coefficients (alpha, beta, gamma) for each structure using one-loop Feynman diagrams in a mean-field NJL model under the weak-coupling approximation, with the strange quark mass treated as a chemical potential splitting delta-mu = M_s^2/(8 mu) between Fermi surfaces.

## Key numbers

- The optimum magnitude of the pairing wavevectors is |q_I| = eta * delta-mu_I with eta = 1.1997, corresponding to pairing rings on the Fermi surfaces with opening angle 67.1 degrees
- For the CubeX structure, the lattice spacing a = sqrt(3)*pi/q = 4.536/delta-mu = mu/(1.764 M_s^2); for M_s^2/mu = 100, 150, 200 MeV the lattice spacing is a = 72, 48, 36 fm respectively
- The crystalline phases (CubeX or 2Cube45z) are favored over CFL, gCFL, and unpaired phases for 2.9 Delta_0 < M_s^2/mu < 10.4 Delta_0
- For Delta_0 = 25 MeV and M_s = 250 MeV, this translates to 240 MeV < mu < 847 MeV
- The maximum value of |Delta_I(r)| in the CubeX structure is 4 Delta, occurring along lines at the centers of the cylinders

## Context

This work extends the study of crystalline color superconductivity from two-flavor pairing (Alford 2000, Bowers 2002) to three-flavor quark matter, addressing the instability of the gapless CFL (gCFL) phase found by Alford (2003). The CubeX and 2Cube45z phases provide a resolution to the gCFL instability over a wide density range, except for a narrow window near the CFL-to-gCFL transition where current-carrying meson condensates (Kryjevski 2005) may be relevant. The results enable future calculations of neutrino emissivity for neutron star cooling, and of vortex pinning forces and shear moduli needed to connect crystalline quark matter to pulsar glitch phenomenology.
