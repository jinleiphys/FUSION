# Inclusive Charged--Current Neutrino--Nucleus Reactions

> We present a model for weak CC induced nuclear reactions at energies of interest for current and future neutrino oscillation experiments. This model is a natural extension of the work of Refs.[1,2], where the QE contribution to the inclusive electron and neutrino scattering on nuclei was analyzed. The model is based on a systematic many body expansion of the gauge boson absorption modes that includes one, two and even three body mechanisms, as well as the excitation of Delta isobars. The whole scheme has no free parameters, besides those previously adjusted to the weak pion production off the nucleon cross sections in the deuteron, since all nuclear effects were set up in previous studies of photon, electron and pion interactions with nuclei. We have discussed at length the recent charged current quasi-elastic MiniBooNE cross section data, and showed that two nucleon knockout mechanisms are essential to describe these measurements.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that two-nucleon knockout mechanisms (2p2h), rather than an artificially inflated axial mass \(M_A\), are essential to describe the MiniBooNE charged-current quasi-elastic cross section data, though the model has no free parameters beyond those previously fixed to weak pion production on the deuteron.

## Method

The authors construct a many-body expansion of the \(W^\pm\) boson self-energy in nuclear matter, incorporating one-nucleon (1p1h) quasielastic scattering, one-nucleon one-pion (1p1h\(1\pi\)) production via the \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance and background terms, and two-nucleon (2p2h) mechanisms driven by pion-mediated particle-hole excitations. Nuclear effects such as Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, RPA correlations, and dressed nucleon propagators are included within a local Fermi gas model, with the hadronic tensor obtained from the imaginary part of the in-medium gauge boson self-energy.

## Key numbers

* The axial mass from deuterium QE data is \(M_A = 1.016 \pm 0.026\) GeV, while MiniBooNE data would require \(M_A = 1.35 \pm 0.17\) GeV if fitted with a simple QE-only model.
* The NOMAD experiment at higher energies gives \(M_A = 1.06 \pm 0.02 \pm 0.06\) GeV, inconsistent with the large MiniBooNE-extracted value.
* The Fermi constant used is \(G = 1.1664 \times 10^{-11}\) MeV\(^{-2}\).
* The Landau-Migdal parameter \(g' = 0.63\) is used for short-range correlations in the pion selfenergy.
* The pion-nucleon coupling constant is \(f^2_{\pi NN}/4\pi = 0.08\) with a cutoff \(\Lambda_\pi = 1.2\) GeV.
* The axial coupling \(C_5^A(0)\) from pion production models ranges from \(1.19 \pm 0.08\) (neglecting non-resonant background) to \(1.00 \pm 0.11\) (full model), the latter implying a 20% reduction relative to the off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation.

## Context

This work extends the quasielastic model of Nieves (2004) to include pion production and two-nucleon mechanisms, building on previous studies of electron, photon, and pion interactions with nuclei by Gil (1997) and Carrasco (1989). It directly addresses the discrepancy between MiniBooNE QE data and standard nuclear models, supporting the interpretation of Martini (2009, 2010) that multinucleon processes account for the excess without modifying \(M_A\). The framework enables unified comparisons with inclusive neutrino-nucleus data across the QE peak, dip region, and \(\Delta\) resonance, and can be applied to future oscillation experiments.
