# Sensitivity of $β$-decay rates to the radial dependence of the nucleon effective mass

> We analyze the sensitivity of $β$-decay rates in 78 Ni and 100,132 Sn to a correction term in Skyrme energy-density functionals (EDF) which modifies the radial shape of the nucleon effective mass. This correction is added on top of several Skyrme parametrizations which are selected from their effective mass properties and predictions about the stability properties of 132 Sn. The impact of the correction on high-energy collective modes is shown to be moderate. From the comparison of the effects induced by the surface-peaked effective mass in the three doubly magic nuclei, it is found that 132 Sn is largely impacted by the correction, while 78 Ni and 100 Sn are only moderately affected. We conclude that $β$-decay rates in these nuclei can be used as a test of different parts of the nuclear EDF: 78 Ni and 100 Sn are mostly sensitive to the particle-hole interaction through the B(GT) values, while 132 Sn is sensitive to the radial shape of the effective mass. Possible improvements of these different parts could therefore be better constrained in the future.

## Key claim

The sensitivity of beta-decay rates to a surface-peaked effective mass (SPEM) correction in Skyrme energy-density functionals depends strongly on the nucleus: 132Sn is largely impacted by the correction, while 78Ni and 100Sn are only moderately affected, meaning that beta-decay rates in these nuclei can test different parts of the nuclear EDF.

## Method

The authors added a correction term to the Skyrme energy-density functional that modifies the radial shape of the nucleon effective mass at the nuclear surface, with the strength parameter C^(tau (nabla rho)^2)_0 set to -210 and -420 MeV fm^10 so that the isoscalar quadrupole energy-weighted sum rule in 208Pb is modified by 1% and 2% respectively. They performed Hartree-Fock calculations followed by charge-exchange random phase approximation (CERPA) using the finite-rank separable approximation to compute beta-decay half-lives and Gamow-Teller strength distributions for the doubly magic nuclei 78Ni, 100Sn, and 132Sn, using six selected Skyrme parametrizations with different effective mass properties.

## Key numbers

- For 132Sn, the experimental beta-decay half-life is 39.7 +/- 0.8 s; without SPEM, SLyIII0.7 predicts 389400 s, while with maximal SPEM (C = -420 MeV fm^10) it decreases to 1930 s
- For 132Sn, SLyIII0.9 without SPEM predicts a half-life of 6.7 s, and with maximal SPEM it decreases to 3.3 s
- For 78Ni, the experimental half-life is 0.1222 +/- 0.0051 s; SLyIII0.7 without SPEM predicts 0.157 s, and with maximal SPEM predicts 0.121 s
- For 100Sn, the experimental half-life is 1.16 +/- 0.20 s; SLyIII0.7 without SPEM predicts 0.232 s, and with maximal SPEM predicts 0.213 s
- The single-particle energy difference for the dominant transition in 132Sn shifts by about 0.3 MeV when the maximal SPEM is applied, while in 78Ni and 100Sn the shifts are similar in magnitude but the absolute energy differences are much larger (5-8 MeV)
- The isoscalar quadrupole energy-weighted sum rule in 208Pb is modified by 1% for C = -210 MeV fm^10 and 2% for C = -420 MeV fm^10

## Context

This work extends the approach of Fantina (2011) who proposed the surface-peaked effective mass correction to Skyrme functionals, and applies it to beta-decay calculations for the first time. The study is motivated by the known issue that mean-field models underestimate the single-particle level density around the Fermi energy, as noted by Brown (1964), and the need to reconcile the effective mass properties required for giant resonance descriptions with those near the Fermi surface. The results suggest that beta-decay measurements in 132Sn can constrain the radial shape of the effective mass, while 78Ni and 100Sn are more sensitive to the particle-hole interaction through the Gamow-Teller strength, potentially enabling future improvements to different parts of nuclear energy-density functionals.
