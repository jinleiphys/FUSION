# Relativistic spin hydrodynamics with antisymmetric spin tensors and an extension of the Bargmann-Michel-Telegdi equation

> We derive a formulation of relativistic spin hydrodynamics with totally antisymmetric spin tensors that satisfy the Frenkel-Mathisson-Pirani condition. In our proposed spin hydrodynamics, the second law of thermodynamics is fulfilled by the spin-induced corrections in the heat flow, the viscous tensor, and the antisymmetric part of the energy-momentum tensor. These corrections are interpreted as the inverse spin Hall effect and the anomalous Hall effect in the nonrelativistic limit. We show that our evolution equation for the spin density is interpreted as an extension of the Bargmann-Michel-Telegdi equation known in relativistic many-body systems, including the Thomas precession term, the spin-rotation term, and new coupling terms between spin and hydrodynamic variables.

## Key claim

The paper derives a new formulation of relativistic spin hydrodynamics using totally antisymmetric spin tensors that satisfy the Frenkel-Mathisson-Pirani condition, and shows that the resulting evolution equation for spin density generalizes the Bargmann-Michel-Telegdi equation to include spin-rotation coupling, Thomas precession, and new coupling terms between spin and hydrodynamic variables, while also reproducing the inverse spin Hall effect and anomalous Hall effect in the nonrelativistic limit.

## Method

The authors start from the conservation laws for energy-momentum, charge current, and total angular momentum, introducing a totally antisymmetric spin tensor \(\Sigma^{\lambda\mu\nu}\) and defining the local spin density as \(S^{\mu\nu} = u_\lambda \Sigma^{\lambda\mu\nu}\), which automatically satisfies the Frenkel-Mathisson-Pirani condition \((u\cdot S)^\mu=0\). They apply the entropy principle to derive constitutive equations, renormalizing the entropy current to absorb additional terms arising from the antisymmetric spin tensor, and obtain spin-induced corrections to the heat flow, viscous tensor, and antisymmetric part of the energy-momentum tensor. The evolution equation for the spin density is then derived by projecting the spin conservation equation and inserting the constitutive relations.

## Key numbers

- Initial orbital angular momentum in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is \(\sim 10^5\hbar\) from noncentral collisions of two nuclei.
- The spin potential \(\omega_{\mu\nu}\) and spin density \(S^{\mu\nu}\) follow the power counting \(S^{\mu\nu}\sim \mathcal{O}(1)\) and \(\omega_{\mu\nu}\sim\mathcal{O}(\hbar)\sim \mathcal{O}(\partial)\).
- The transport coefficients \(\sigma\), \(\zeta\), \(\eta\), and \(\gamma_\phi\) are non-negative, corresponding to conductivity, bulk viscosity, shear viscosity, and rotational viscosity respectively.
- The speed of sound squared at constant density, \(v_n^2 := (\partial p/\partial e)_{n,S^{\mu\nu}}\), appears in the spin-induced correction \(\pi_s^{\mu\nu}\) with a factor \(2v_n^2\).
- In the nonrelativistic limit, the heat flow correction \(\boldsymbol{h}_s\) contains terms proportional to \(\nabla\times\boldsymbol{s}\) (inverse spin Hall effect) and \(\boldsymbol{s}\times\nabla T\) (anomalous Hall effect).

## Context

This work extends previous formulations of relativistic spin hydrodynamics based on the entropy principle by Hattori (2019) and Fukushima (2020), which used a factorized form of the spin tensor \(\Sigma^{\lambda\mu\nu}=u^\lambda S^{\mu\nu}\) that lacked Hermiticity and faced stability issues as noted by Xie (2023). The new approach employs totally antisymmetric spin tensors, which are Hermitian and satisfy the Frenkel-Mathisson-Pirani condition, enabling a natural connection to the Bargmann-Michel-Telegdi equation for spin precession in relativistic many-body systems. This formulation enables the study of spin dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions and provides testable predictions for spin-induced transport phenomena such as the inverse spin Hall effect and anomalous Hall effect in tabletop experiments.
