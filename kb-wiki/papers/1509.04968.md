# Robust characteristics of nongaussian fluctuations from the NJL model

> We evaluate the third- and fourth-order baryon, charge and strangeness susceptibilities near a chiral critical point using the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. We identify robust qualitative behaviours of the susceptibilities along hypothetical freeze-out lines that agree with previous model studies. Quantitatively, baryon number fluctuations are the largest in magnitude and thus offer the strongest signal when freeze-out occurs farther away from a critical point. Charge and strangeness susceptibilities also diverge at a critical point, but the area where the divergence dominates is smaller, meaning freeze-out must occur closer to a critical point for a signal to be visible in these observables. In case of strangeness, this is attributable to the relatively large strange quark mass. Plotting the third- and fourth-order susceptibilities against each other along the freeze-out line exhibits clearly their non-montonicity and robust features.

## Key claim

The third- and fourth-order baryon, charge, and strangeness susceptibilities computed in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model exhibit robust qualitative behaviors along hypothetical freeze-out lines that agree with previous model studies, but the magnitude and visibility of critical signals depend on how close freeze-out occurs to the critical point, with baryon number fluctuations providing the strongest signal when freeze-out is farther away.

## Method

The authors computed the complete set of second-, third-, and fourth-order susceptibilities (derivatives of the partition function with respect to chemical potentials) for baryon, charge, and strangeness using the NJL model with three quark flavors (u, d, s) in the mean-field approximation. They evaluated these susceptibilities across the phase diagram in temperature and baryon chemical potential, including along hypothetical freeze-out lines, and analyzed the contributions from order-parameter fluctuations using a diagrammatic expansion in terms of sigma correlators.

## Key numbers

- Baryon number fluctuations are the largest in magnitude, offering the strongest signal when freeze-out occurs farther away from a critical point
- At collision energies sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 39, 200 GeV, mu_B/3 is approximately 130, 30, 10 MeV respectively, and mu_S is approximately 90, 20, 5 MeV respectively (from experimental data cited as Das (2014))
- The strange quark current mass is m_s greater than or approximately 90 MeV, which suppresses strange quark fluctuations relative to light quark fluctuations
- The isospin chemical potential mu_I is negligible when mu_I is much less than m_pi/2 approximately 65 MeV
- The third-order strangeness susceptibility chi_S^(3) is identically zero
- The fourth-order strangeness susceptibility chi_s^(4) shows a divergence at the critical point, but the magnitude and area of the critical region are small, requiring high numerical precision to make the effect visible

## Context

This work extends previous studies of non-Gaussian fluctuations near a chiral critical point, including the Gross-Neveu model analysis by Chen (2014) and earlier NJL studies focusing on third moments by Asakawa (2009) or electric charge susceptibilities by Skokov (2011). The results confirm model-independent features predicted by general effective potential analysis, such as the ordering of peaks in non-Gaussian susceptibilities along freeze-out lines and the negative kurtosis region occurring in the symmetric phase. The flavor-dependent NJL model enables computation of the complete set of susceptibilities, including strangeness and cross-correlations, which can be compared to heavy ion collision data from experiments such as those reported by Aggarwal (2010) and Adamczyk (2013).
