# Prompt photons at RHIC

> We calculate the inclusive cross section for prompt photon production in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies ($\sqrt{s}=130$ GeV and $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV) in the central rapidity region including next-to-leading order, $O(α_{em}α_s^2)$, radiative corrections, initial state nuclear shadowing and parton energy loss effects. We show that there is a significant suppression of the nuclear cross section, up to $\sim 30%$ at $\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, due to shadowing and medium induced parton energy loss effects. We find that the next-to-leading order contributions are large and have a strong $p_t$ dependence.

# Prompt photons at RHIC

## Key claim

The authors calculate that nuclear shadowing and medium-induced parton energy loss together suppress the prompt photon cross section in heavy-ion collisions by up to ~30% at √s = 200 GeV, but this suppression is strongly pT-dependent, falling to ~7% at pT = 10 GeV, and the next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections themselves are large and pT-dependent, meaning a constant K-factor is not justified.

## Method

The authors compute the inclusive cross section for prompt photon production in central-rapidity heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies (√s = 130 GeV and 200 GeV) using perturbative QCD at NLO, O(αemαs²), with MRS99 parton distributions modified by a parameterization of nuclear shadowing fitted to EMC, NMC, and E665 data. They incorporate medium-induced parton energy loss by modifying the photon fragmentation functions using a multiple-scattering model with energy-dependent energy loss per unit distance from Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, and Schiff, taking a screening mass μ = 1 GeV and parton mean free paths λq = λg = 1 fm.

## Key numbers

- Suppression of the nuclear cross section at √s = 200 GeV: ~30% at pT = 3 GeV, ~7% at pT = 10 GeV.
- Suppression at √s = 130 GeV: ~18% at pT = 3 GeV, ~4% at pT = 10 GeV.
- K-factor (NLO relative to Born + bremsstrahlung) in hadronic collisions: ranges from 1.4 at pT = 10 GeV to 1.8 at pT = 3 GeV.
- K-factor in nuclear collisions: ranges from 1.4 at pT = 10 GeV to 2.2 at pT = 3 GeV.
- Standard K-factor (NLO/Born) in hadronic collisions: ranges from 2 at pT = 10 GeV to 6 at pT = 3 GeV.
- Standard K-factor in nuclear collisions: ranges from 1.9 at pT = 10 GeV to 5.5 at pT = 3 GeV.
- Uncertainty from varying energy loss parameters (μ = 1 GeV, λ from 0.5 fm to 2 fm) at √s = 200 GeV: up to 25% at lowest pT.

## Context

This work extends prior NLO calculations of prompt photon production in hadronic collisions by Aurenche and collaborators to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, incorporating nuclear shadowing parameterizations and a model of parton energy loss in nuclear matter from Wang, Huang, and Sarcevic. The results provide a quantitative baseline for the PHENIX collaboration to separate hard prompt photons from thermal photons and hadronic decay photons, and enable future extension to p-A collisions at RHIC and heavy-ion collisions at LHC energies, where small-x nuclear structure functions and higher-twist effects become important.
