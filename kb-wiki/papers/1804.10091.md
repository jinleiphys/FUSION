# Predictions for cross sections of light proton-rich isotopes in $^{40}$Ca + $^{9}$Be reaction

> The cross sections for the $Z=$ 10 -- 19 with isotopes $T_{z}=-3/2$ to $-5$ in the 140$A$ MeV $^{40}$Ca + $^{9}$Be projectile fragmentation reaction have been predicted. An empirical formula based on the correlation between the cross section and average binding energy of isotope has been adopted to predict the cross section. The binding energies in the AME16, WS4, and the theoretical prediction by the spherical relativistic continuum Hartree$-$Bogoliubov theory have been used. Meanwhile, the {\sc fracs} parametrization and the modified statistical abrasion-ablation model are also used to predict the cross sections for the proton-rich isotopes. The predicted cross sections for the $T_{z}=-$3 isotopes are close to $10^{-10}$ mb, which hopefully can be studied in experiment. In addition, based on the predicted cross sections, the $Z=$ 14 is suggested to be a new magic number in the light proton-rich nuclei with $T_{z} \leq-$3/2, of which the phenomenon is much more evident than it is from the average binding energy per nucleon.

## Key claim

The cross sections for very proton-rich isotopes (Tz = -3/2 to -5) in the 140A MeV 40Ca + 9Be reaction are predicted to be measurable down to about 10^-10 mb for Tz = -3 isotopes, but the predictions depend strongly on which binding energy database (AME16, WS4, or RCHB theory) is used, and the empirical formula may be less reliable for the most exotic isotopes.

## Method

The authors used an empirical exponential correlation between isotopic cross section and average binding energy (sigma = C exp[(<B'> - 8)/tau]), fitting the parameters C and tau to measured cross sections for Z = 10-19 isotopes with Tz = -1 to 1/2 from the 140A MeV 40Ca + 9Be reaction. They then extrapolated to more proton-rich isotopes (Tz = -3/2 to -5) using binding energies from AME16, the WS4 mass model, and the spherical relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory. For comparison, predictions were also made using the FRACS parametrization and the modified statistical abrasion-ablation (SAA) model.

## Key numbers

- Predicted cross sections for Tz = -3 isotopes (e.g., 22Si, 24P, 26S, 28Cl, 30Ar, 32K) are close to 10^-10 mb, with values ranging from 9.2 x 10^-12 mb (24P using AME16) to 6.0 x 10^-10 mb (32K using RCHB)
- For Tz = -7/2 isotopes, predicted cross sections drop to approximately 10^-14 to 10^-15 mb (e.g., 25S at 1.8 x 10^-15 mb using WS4)
- For Tz = -4 isotopes, predicted cross sections are around 10^-17 to 10^-19 mb (e.g., 24S at 1.1 x 10^-19 mb using WS4)
- The fitted parameter C for Z=10 is approximately 1.6 x 10^6 mb and decreases exponentially with Z; tau is about 0.95 MeV for Z=10 and decreases weakly with Z
- The measured cross sections for Tz = -3/2 isotopes range from 7.6 x 10^-4 mb (27P) to 6.6 x 10^-3 mb (33Ar)

## Context

This work extends the empirical binding-energy correlation method previously applied to neutron-rich copper isotopes by Tsang et al. (2009) and to proton-rich isotopes in the 78Kr + 9Be reaction by Song et al. (2018). It provides predictions for the 40Ca + 9Be reaction at 140A MeV, complementing the measured data for Tz >= -1 from Mocko et al. (2006). The results suggest that Z = 14 is a new magic number in light proton-rich nuclei with Tz <= -3/2, a conclusion that is more evident from the cross section gaps than from average binding energy per nucleon. The predictions enable experimental planning at facilities such as RIKEN-BigRIPS, HIRFL-RIBLL2, and FRIB-ARIS for producing and studying very proton-rich isotopes near the proton drip line.
