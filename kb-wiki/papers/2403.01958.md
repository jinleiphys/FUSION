# Dynamical Model of $J/Ψ$ photo-production on the nucleon

> A dynamical model based on a phenomenological charm quark-nucleon($c$-N) potential $v_{cN}$ and the Pomeron-exchange mechanism is constructed to investigate the $J/Ψ$ photo-production on the nucleon from threshold to invariant mass $W=300$ GeV. The $J/Ψ$-N potential,$V_{J/ΨN}(r)$,is constructed by folding $v_{cN}$ into the wavefunction $Φ_{J/Ψ}(c\bar{c})$ of $J/Ψ$ within a Constituent Quark Model(CQM) of Ref.[43]. A photo-production amplitude is also generated by $v_{cN}$ by a $c\bar{c}$-loop integration over the $γ\rightarrow c\bar{c}$ vertex function and $Φ_{J/Ψ}(c\bar{c})$. No commonly used Vector Meson Dominance assumption is used to define this photo-production amplitude which is needed to describe the data near the threshold. The potential $v_{cN}(r)$ is parameterized in a form such that the predicted $V_{J/ΨN}(r)$ at large distances has the same Yukawa potential form extracted from a Lattice QCD(LQCD) calculation of Ref.[18]. The parameters of $v_{cN}$ are determined by fitting the total cross section data of JLab by performing calculations that include $J/Ψ$-N final state interactions(FSI). The resulting differential cross sections are found in good agreements with the data. It is shown that the FSI effects dominate the cross section in the very near threshold region, allowing for sensitive testing of the predicted $J/Ψ$-N scattering amplitudes. By imposing the constraints of $J/Ψ$-N potential extracted from the LQCD calculation, we have obtained three $J/Ψ$-N potentials which fit the JLab data equally well. The resulting $J/Ψ$-N scattering lengths are in the range of $a=(-0.05$ fm $\sim$ $-0.25$ fm). With the determined $v_{cN}(r)$ and the wavefunctions generated from the same CQM, the constructed model is used to predict the cross sections of photo-production of $η_c(1S)$ and $Ψ(2S)$ mesons for future experimental tests.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a dynamical model of J/ψ photo-production on the nucleon that avoids the commonly used Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) assumption, which is argued to be invalid for J/ψ near threshold because the VMD coupling constant is determined at q² = m²_J/ψ ≈ 9 GeV², far from q² = 0 of the photo-production reaction.

## Method

The authors build a Hamiltonian model using a phenomenological charm quark-nucleon (c-N) potential v_cN, which is folded into the J/ψ wavefunction from a Constituent Quark Model (CQM) to generate the J/ψ-N potential V_J/ψN(r). The photo-production amplitude is computed via a c-anti-c loop integration over the γ → c-anti-c vertex function and the J/ψ wavefunction, without using VMD. The c-N potential parameters are determined by fitting JLab total cross section data, with calculations that include J/ψ-N final state interactions (FSI) via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, while a Pomeron-exchange amplitude from Donnachie and Landshoff (1984) is added to describe data up to W = 300 GeV.

## Key numbers

* J/ψ-N scattering lengths are in the range a = (-0.05 fm to -0.25 fm), obtained from three different potentials that fit JLab data equally well.
* The LQCD Yukawa potential parameters used as constraints are α = 0.1 and μ = 0.6 GeV from Kawanai and Sasaki (2010).
* The Pomeron-exchange parameters for J/ψ are β_c = 0.32 GeV⁻¹ and α_0 = 1.25, with s_0 = 0.25 GeV.
* The model covers invariant mass from threshold to W = 300 GeV.
* The J/ψ mass is approximately 9 GeV² (m²_J/ψ).

## Context

This work extends the dynamical formulation of Lee et al. (1996, 2006, 2007, 2013) for vector meson photo-production to the J/ψ system, and contrasts with approaches based on VMD which the authors argue is invalid for J/ψ near threshold. The model enables extraction of J/ψ-N scattering amplitudes from JLab data and predicts cross sections for η_c(1S) and ψ(2S) photo-production for future tests at JLab and the Electron-Ion Collider. Prior theoretical estimates of the J/ψ-N scattering length include -0.24 fm from Brodsky and Miller (1997) and -0.05 fm from Kaidalov and Volkovitsky (1992), while the LQCD calculation of Kawanai and Sasaki (2010) gives -0.09 fm.
