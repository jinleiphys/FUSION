# Towards Density Functional Calculations from Nuclear Forces

> We propose a method for microscopic calculations of nuclear ground-state properties in the framework of density functional theory. We discuss how the density functional is equivalent to the effective action for the density, thereby establishing a constructive framework for density functional calculations from nuclear forces. The presented approach starts from non-interacting nucleons in a background potential (a simple approximation for the mean field). The nuclear forces are then gradually turned on, while the background potential is removed. The evolution equation yields the ground-state energy and density of a system of interacting nucleons, including exchange-correlations beyond the RPA approximation. The method can start from non-local low momentum (V_{low k}) or chiral interactions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a method for microscopic density functional theory (DFT) calculations of nuclear ground-state properties starting from nuclear forces, but the approach is presented as a framework under development rather than a completed computational scheme; the authors explicitly state they are "currently working on the feasibility of this approach for strongly-interacting one-dimensional models."

## Method

The method uses the effective action formalism to establish equivalence between the density functional and the effective action for the density, then introduces a control parameter λ that gradually turns on the two-body interaction U while removing the background potential V. The evolution equation for the density functional is derived and expanded around the evolving ground-state density, with truncation at a given order in density fluctuations (NΓ=3 or 4). The flow equations for the ground-state energy, density, dressed particle-hole propagator, and three-density correlator are solved to include exchange-correlations beyond the RPA approximation.

## Key numbers

- The control parameter λ ranges from 0 (non-interacting nucleons in a background potential) to 1 (physical system with interactions and no background potential)
- The expansion for the effective action is truncated at order NΓ=3 or NΓ=4 in density fluctuations
- The method can start from non-local low momentum (V_{low k}) or chiral interactions

## Context

This work extends the density functional theory framework, which the authors note is "the underlying theory in modern nuclear structure approaches to heavy nuclei" (citing Furnstahl, Fayans, Bertsch, and others from 2000-2004). The approach is complementary to parameterizing the nuclear density functional and aims to provide a systematic microscopic foundation for DFT from nuclear forces, in contrast to phenomenological mean-field approaches. The method enables future calculations of heavier nuclear systems from microscopic two- and three-nucleon forces, with particular importance for extrapolations to neutron/proton-rich systems and for maintaining a complete basis of induced non-central interactions.
