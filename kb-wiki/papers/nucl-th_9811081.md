# Thermal distributions in stellar plasmas, nuclear reactions and solar neutrinos

> The physics of nuclear reactions in stellar plasma is reviewed with special emphasis on the importance of the velocity distribution of ions. Then the properties (density and temperature) of the weak-coupled solar plasma are analysed, showing that the ion velocities should deviate from the Maxwellian distribution and could be better described by a weakly-nonexstensive (|q-1|<0.02) Tsallis' distribution. We discuss concrete physical frameworks for calculating this deviation: the introduction of higher-order corrections to the diffusion and friction coefficients in the Fokker-Plank equation, the influence of the electric-microfield stochastic distribution on the particle dynamics, a velocity correlation function with long-time memory arising from the coupling of the collective and individual degrees of freedom. Finally, we study the effects of such deviations on stellar nuclear rates, on the solar neutrino fluxes, and on the pp neutrino energy spectrum, and analyse the consequences for the solar neutrino problem.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the ion velocity distribution in the solar core should deviate from the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution, with the deviation parameter estimated to be of order 0.01, and that such small deviations can produce significant changes in nuclear reaction rates and solar neutrino fluxes. The authors qualify that the distribution is "not too far from the Maxwellian one" and that the Tsallis parameter satisfies |q-1| < 0.02.

## Method

The authors review three physical frameworks for calculating deviations from Maxwellian distributions in the solar plasma: (1) adding higher-order corrections to the diffusion and friction coefficients in the Fokker-Planck equation, which generates Tsallis and Druyvenstein-like distributions; (2) analyzing the influence of electric microfield stochastic distributions on particle dynamics; (3) introducing velocity correlation functions with long-time memory from coupling of collective and individual degrees of freedom. They then compute the effects of these modified distributions on stellar nuclear reaction rates using saddle-point asymptotic expansions and on solar neutrino fluxes and the pp neutrino energy spectrum.

## Key numbers

- The plasma parameter in the solar interior is Γ⊙ ≈ 0.1, placing it in the weakly non-ideal regime
- The Tsallis distribution parameter satisfies |q-1| < 0.02
- The deviation parameter δ̂ in the Druyvenstein distribution is estimated to be of order 0.01 from microfield calculations
- For the ³He + ³He reaction, the most effective energy is about 17 kT, meaning fewer than 40 particles per million have energies that large or larger
- The inverse solar plasma frequency is t_pl = ω_pl⁻¹ ≈ 10⁻¹⁷ sec, comparable to the collision time t_coll ≈ 10⁻¹⁷ sec
- The most effective energy E₀/kT ≈ 5.64 (Z₁²Z₂² A₁A₂/(A₁+A₂) × T_c/T)^(1/3) where T_c = 1.36 keV is the central solar temperature
- With q=0.98, the Tsallis tail contains about 0.26% of the total area versus 0.30% for Maxwellian, yet the resulting Gamow peak probability is smaller by a factor of about 0.3

## Context

This work extends earlier suggestions by Clayton (1974, 1975) that small deviations from Maxwellian distributions could affect stellar reaction rates, but uses new theoretical frameworks (Tsallis nonextensive statistics, Fokker-Planck corrections, microfield distributions) and better solar models and experimental data. The paper aims to assess consequences for the solar neutrino problem, where discrepancies exist between solar neutrino experiments and predicted fluxes from standard solar models by Bahcall (1995, 1996, 1997) and others. The work enables future calculations of non-Maxwellian effects on neutrino energy spectra and reaction rates in other astrophysical systems with plasma parameters in the range 0.1 ≤ Γ ≤ 1, such as brown dwarfs, the Jupiter core, and stellar atmospheres.
