# Studying short-range nuclear correlations using relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Recently, a method was developed for implementing arbitrary short-range nucleon-nucleon correlations in Monte Carlo sampled nuclei (as well as deformations of the 1-body nuclear density). We use this method to implement realistic 2-body correlations in a sample of nuclei for use in simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions and we quantify the statistical benefits. These results demonstrate that the method can be used to easily implement an arbitrary correlation function, and systematically study the effects of correlations using significantly less resources than is necessary with traditional methods.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a newly-developed "shifting nucleons" method can implement arbitrary short-range nucleon-nucleon correlation functions in Monte Carlo sampled nuclei for heavy-ion collision simulations, achieving statistical uncertainties comparable to traditional methods with approximately three orders of magnitude fewer computational resources, though the method lacks 3-body correlations present in more sophisticated approaches.

## Method

The authors take a realistic 2-body correlation function numerically extracted from 10000 previously-generated configurations of ⁹⁶Ru nuclei (Hammelmann et al., 2019) and use it as input to the shifting-nucleon algorithm of Luzum et al. (2023), which makes small changes to nucleon positions to enforce desired 1-body and 2-body densities. They then simulate central (b=0) collisions using the TrENTO model (p=0) and compute eccentricities ε_n{2} that relate to final-state flow observables v_n{2}, comparing correlated versus uncorrelated nucleon configurations.

## Key numbers

- The realistic ⁹⁶Ru nuclei use a Woods-Saxon 1-body distribution with R = 5.085 fm, a = 0.46 fm, and β₂ = 0.158
- The relative change in eccentricity ε₂{2} between correlated and uncorrelated nucleons is slightly less than 1%
- With the shifting-nucleon method, 1010 events achieve the same statistical uncertainty as 1000000 events using traditional independent sampling, a speedup of almost 3 orders of magnitude
- The Bayesian closure tests use a step-function correlation with C_len = 0.5 fm and C_str = -0.5 (repulsive) or C_str = 0.5 (attractive)

## Context

This work extends the shifting-nucleon method introduced by Luzum et al. (2023) to implement realistic short-range correlations, building on earlier methods by Alvioli et al. (2009) that generated configurations with 2-body and 3-body interactions. The approach enables systematic Bayesian parameter estimation of short-range nucleon-nucleon correlations using heavy-ion collision observables, though the authors note their method is inferior to the Alvioli approach regarding 3-body correlations while offering superior ease-of-use and computational efficiency for systematic variation studies.
