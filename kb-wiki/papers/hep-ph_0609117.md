# Relativistic Dissipative Hydrodynamics: A Minimal Causal Theory

> We present a new formalism for the theory of relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics. Here, we look for the minimal structure of such a theory which satisfies the covariance and causality by introducing the memory effect in irreversible currents. Our theory has a much simpler structure and thus has several advantages for practical purposes compared to the Israel-Stewart theory (IS). It can readily be applied to the full three-dimensional hydrodynamical calculations. We apply our formalism to the Bjorken model and the results are shown to be analogous to the IS.

## Key claim

The authors present a new formalism for relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics that introduces a memory effect in irreversible currents to satisfy causality, claiming it has a much simpler structure than the Israel-Stewart theory (IS) while producing analogous results for the Bjorken model. The hedge is that this theory is not a special case of IS and differs fundamentally in the treatment of entropy production, relaxing the requirement of algebraic positive definiteness for non-equilibrium states.

## Method

The authors introduce a memory function characterized by a single relaxation time τ_R into the Landau-Lifshitz (LL) formulation of relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics, converting the parabolic LL equations into hyperbolic ones. The irreversible currents (bulk viscosity Π, shear tensor π^μν, and heat conduction ν^μ) are expressed as integrals over past proper time of thermodynamic forces multiplied by an exponential memory kernel, which is equivalent to Maxwell-Cattaneo type differential equations. The formalism is applied to the one-dimensional scaling solution of the Bjorken model for comparison with previous IS analyses.

## Key numbers

- The maximum signal propagation velocity is given by v_max = sqrt(ζ/τ_R), where ζ is a diffusion constant and τ_R is the relaxation time
- To be consistent with special relativity, the condition τ_R ≥ ζ/c² must hold
- In the general 3D case, the IS requires a (14×14) matrix inversion in each time step, while the authors' formalism requires at most a (3×3) matrix inversion for the acceleration of the fluid
- The entropy production expression Q(τ) = f(τ) [f(τ) - τ_R df(τ)/dτ + O(τ_R²)] ensures positivity when |τ_R df(τ)/dτ| < |f(τ)|

## Context

This work extends the causal relativistic hydrodynamics program initiated by Müller (1967) and Israel and Stewart (1976), offering an alternative to both the Israel-Stewart theory and the divergence type theory (Geroch and Lindblom, 1990; Liu, Müller, and Ruggeri, 1986). The formalism is designed for straightforward implementation into existing three-dimensional hydrodynamical codes such as SPheRIO (Kodama and collaborators, 2005), enabling practical studies of dissipative effects in relativistic heavy ion collisions including shock wave generation in quark-gluon plasma.
