# Tidal Love Numbers of Neutron and Self-Bound Quark Stars

> Gravitational waves from the final stages of inspiralling binary neutron stars are expected to be one of the most important sources for ground-based gravitational wave detectors. The masses of the components are determinable from the orbital and chirp frequencies during the early part of the evolution during which tidal effects provide small correction; however, during this phase the signal is relatively clean. The accumulated phase shift due to tidal corrections is characterized by a single quantity, the Love number, which is sensitive to the compactness parameter M/R and the star's internal structure, and its determination could constrain the star's radius. We show that the Love number of normal neutron stars are much different from those of self-bound strange quark matter stars and could therefore provide an important way to distinguish between these two classes of stars.

# Tidal Love Numbers of Neutron and Self-Bound Quark Stars

## Key claim

The tidal Love number of normal neutron stars differs substantially from that of self-bound strange quark matter stars, potentially allowing gravitational wave observations to distinguish between these two classes of compact objects; however, the difference in the quadrupole polarizability λ between the two types may be too small (approximately 50% larger for strange quark matter configurations where radii overlap) to be observable in practice.

## Method

The authors compute the dimensionless tidal Love number k₂ by solving a first-order differential equation for y(r) = rH'(r)/H(r) coupled to the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for stellar structure, using the enthalpy h as the independent variable. They account for density discontinuities and phase transitions by applying a matching condition at the discontinuity boundary, and evaluate k₂ for polytropic equations of state, analytic solutions of Einstein's equations, and a comprehensive set of model equations of state for both hadronic matter and strange quark matter with and without crusts.

## Key numbers

- For an incompressible fluid (n=0 polytrope), k₂ = 3/4
- For an n=1 polytrope in the Newtonian limit, k₂ = (15-π²)/(2π²) and y_R = (π²-9)/3
- The breaking frequency of a solid crust for a 1 M⊙ neutron star using the SLY equation of state is approximately f_br ≈ 20 Hz, corresponding to a binary separation distance D_br ≈ 400 km
- The maximum quadrupole moment Q_22,max for a 1 M⊙ neutron star at crust breaking (strain σ≈0.1) is approximately 10⁴⁰ g cm²
- For strange quark matter configurations where radii overlap with hadronic models, λ values are about 50% larger than for hadronic stars

## Context

This work extends the tidal Love number calculations of Hinderer (2008), Damour and Nagar (2009), and Binnington and Poisson (2009) to a wide range of realistic equations of state, building on the earlier finding by Flanagan and Hinderer (2008) that tidal effects are potentially measurable during the early inspiral phase. The results show that Love numbers for hadronic stars cluster similarly to the moment of inertia behavior found by Lattimer and Schutz (2005), and that the quadrupole polarizability λ scales as R⁵, offering a potentially stronger radius constraint than moment of inertia measurements. The paper enables future gravitational wave observations to constrain neutron star radii and potentially identify strange quark matter stars, though the authors note that the tidal signature difference may be too small for practical discrimination.
