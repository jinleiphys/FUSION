# Parton energy loss in the reformulated weakly-coupled kinetic approach

> Interactions between hard partons and the quark-gluon plasma range from frequent soft interactions to rare hard scatterings. The larger number of soft interactions makes possible an effective stochastic description of parton-plasma interactions in terms of drag and diffusion transport coefficients. In this work, we present a numerical implementation that builds upon this systematic division between soft and hard parton-plasma interactions. We study the dependence of the single parton distribution on the cutoff between soft and hard parton-plasma interactions, both for small and phenomenological values of the strong coupling constant.

## Key claim

The reformulated weakly-coupled kinetic approach to parton energy loss, which systematically separates soft and hard parton-plasma interactions, remains numerically valid at large values of the strong coupling constant \(\alpha_s\) despite being derived in the small-coupling limit, as demonstrated by the approximate cancellation of cutoff dependence in the combined energy loss.

## Method

The authors implemented the reformulated energy loss model within the JETSCAPE framework, building on the existing MARTINI implementation. The collision kernel was divided into hard large-angle elastic and large-\(\omega\) inelastic interactions (treated with vacuum matrix elements and AMY integral equations, respectively) and soft interactions described stochastically via a Langevin equation with drag and diffusion coefficients. The cutoff independence of the combined energy loss was tested by varying the inelastic cutoff \(\mu_\omega\) and the elastic cutoff \(\mu_{q_\perp}\) for both small and phenomenological values of the strong coupling constant, using a static medium and a 20 GeV gluon for inelastic tests and a 200 GeV gluon for elastic tests.

## Key numbers

- Inelastic energy loss test: 20 GeV gluon propagating for 1 fm/c in a static quark-gluon plasma at T = 300 MeV with \(\alpha_s = 0.3\); the inelastic cutoff \(\mu_\omega\) was varied to values of \(0.25T\), \(T\), and \(2T\).
- Elastic energy loss test: 200 GeV gluon propagating in a static plasma at T = 300 MeV; the propagation length was fixed to \(\tau = \alpha_s^{-2} 0.3^2\) fm/c to keep the number of elastic collisions constant across different coupling strengths.
- The inelastic contribution to the longitudinal diffusion coefficient is given by \(\hat{q}^{inel}_L = \frac{(2-\ln2)g^4C_RC_AT^2\mu_\omega}{4\pi^3}\), where \(g = \sqrt{4\pi\alpha_s}\).

## Context

This work provides the first numerical implementation of the reformulated parton energy loss approach derived by Ghiglieri et al. (2016), which systematically factorizes soft and hard interactions in the weakly-coupled limit. It extends leading-order realizations of weakly-coupled effective kinetic theory such as MARTINI (Schenke et al., 2009), which traditionally divide interactions into elastic and inelastic processes. The reformulation enables extension to next-to-leading order (Ghiglieri et al., 2016) and provides a framework for data-driven constraints on the drag and diffusion coefficients of partons in heavy ion collisions.
