# Level scheme of $^{153}$Sm obtained from $^{152}$Sm($n_{th}$,$γ$) reaction using $γ-γ$ coincidence spectrometer

> The level scheme of the compound $^{153}$Sm nucleus formed via the $^{152}$Sm($n_{th}$,$γ$) reaction is studied by using the $γ-γ$ coincidence spectrometer at Dalat Nuclear Research Institute, Vietnam. All the gamma cascades, which correspond to the decays from the compound state to 12 final levels of 0 ($\frac{3}{2}^+$), 7.535 ($\frac{5}{2}^+$), 35.844 ($\frac{3}{2}^-$), 90.875 ($\frac{5}{2}^-$), 126.412 ($\frac{1}{2}^-$), 127.298 ($\frac{3}{2}^-$), 182.902 ($\frac{5}{2}^-$), 321.113 ($\frac{3}{2}^+$), 404.129 ($\frac{1}{2}^-$), 405.470 ($\frac{3}{2}^-$), 414.924 ($\frac{1}{2}^+$), and 481.088 ($\frac{3}{2}^+$) keV, have been measured. A total number of 386 cascades corresponding to 576 gamma transitions has been detected. Among these cascades, 103 primary gamma transitions together with their corresponding intermediate levels and 299 secondary transitions have been determined. In addition, 29 primary gamma transitions, 42 intermediate levels, and 8 secondary transitions have been found to be the same as those extracted from the ENSDF data. The remain 74 primary gamma transitions, 61 intermediate levels, and 291 secondary transitions are therefore considered as the new data. In particular, based on an assumption that most of the transitions are dipole, we have tentatively assigned the unique spin value of $\frac{3}{2}\hbar$ for 53 observed intermediate levels corresponding to the cascades from the compound state to the final ones of 7.535 ($\frac{5}{2}^+$), 90.875 ($\frac{5}{2}^-$), and 182.902 ($\frac{5}{2}^-$) keV, whereas the remain levels are assigned with the spin values in the range of $[\frac{1}{2},\frac{3}{2}]\hbar$...

## Key claim

The paper claims to have significantly extended the known level scheme of $^{153}$Sm in the energy region from 0.52 to 5.3 MeV and spin range of [1/2, 3/2] hbar, but this extension is conditional on the assumption that most observed gamma transitions are dipole (L=1), making the spin assignments tentative rather than definite.

## Method

The authors performed the $^{152}$Sm(n_th,gamma) reaction using a thermal neutron beam at the Dalat Nuclear Research Institute, Vietnam, with a gamma-gamma coincidence spectrometer employing two HPGe detectors. They measured two-step gamma cascades (TSC) decaying from the compound state to 12 final levels, identifying 386 cascades corresponding to 576 gamma transitions, from which they determined primary transitions, intermediate levels, and secondary transitions. Spin assignments were made using the dipole assumption (L=1) and the angular momentum coupling formula, with unique spin of 3/2 hbar assigned to 53 intermediate levels and spin range [1/2, 3/2] hbar for the remaining levels.

## Key numbers

- Total of 386 gamma cascades and 576 gamma transitions detected from the compound state to 12 final levels
- 103 primary gamma transitions determined, corresponding to 103 intermediate levels, and 299 secondary transitions
- 74 primary gamma transitions, 61 intermediate levels, and 291 secondary transitions are new data not present in the ENSDF library
- 53 intermediate levels tentatively assigned unique spin value of 3/2 hbar based on dipole assumption
- Maximum excitation energy E_max extended to about 1.2 MeV for total and 1.8 MeV for partial NLD data, compared to around 1 MeV from ENSDF data alone
- The target contained 98.7% $^{152}$Sm with thermal neutron flux of 1.7 x 10^5 n.cm^{-2}.s^{-1} at the irradiated position
- Measurement duration was approximately 661 hours

## Context

This work extends the level scheme of $^{153}$Sm beyond previous studies using the same $^{152}$Sm(n_th,gamma) reaction by Helmer (2006), Be41 (1971), Sm04 (1969), and Re04 (1969), as well as ion-induced reactions such as $^{152}$Sm(d,p) by Ke09 (1965) and Ka07 (1972). The gamma-gamma coincidence technique overcomes limitations of conventional Ge detector spectrometers, particularly the high Compton background and interference from $^{150}$Sm produced by thermal neutron capture on $^{149}$Sm. The new cumulative level curves enable testing of nuclear level density models, with the exact pairing plus independent-particle model at finite temperature (EP+IPM) found to be the best fitting model without adjustable parameters.
