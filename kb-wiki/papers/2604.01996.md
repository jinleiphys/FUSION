# Tackling inverse problems for PDFs from lattice QCD

> In this kick-off presentation for the "Recent developments in QCD" session at Baryons 2025 I will tie together the recent progress made on the extraction of parton distribution functions (PDFs) in lattice QCD and the long standing efforts in solving the inverse problem in the form of spectral function reconstruction.

## Key claim

This kick-off presentation argues that the extraction of parton distribution functions (PDFs) from lattice QCD involves an ill-posed inverse problem analogous to spectral function reconstruction at finite temperature, and that meaningful reconstruction requires regularization through prior information, with the caveat that the inverse Fourier transform is only well-posed when the full Brillouin zone in Ioffe time is accessible.

## Method

The paper surveys and benchmarks three reconstruction methods for extracting PDFs from discrete, noisy Ioffe-time matrix elements: the linear Backus-Gilbert (BG) method, and the nonlinear Bayesian Maximum Entropy Method (MEM) and Bayesian Reconstruction (BR) method. These are tested on two mock PDFs using realistic lattice parameters (maximum Ioffe time ν=10, 12 datapoints), with the Bayesian methods employing a phenomenological fit f_fit(x) = c x^a (1-x)^b as the default model and assessing uncertainty through Jackknife resampling and default model variation.

## Key numbers

- Access to momenta of around 30 GeV is necessary to recover the fourth moment of the pion distribution amplitude to percent accuracy in the quasi PDF approach (Nam, 2017)
- The benchmark study uses a maximum Ioffe time of ν=10, which is realistic for current state-of-the-art lattices
- The BR reconstruction improves significantly when the available Ioffe time range is extended to ν_max=20
- 12 datapoints were used in the benchmark reconstruction of mock PDFs

## Context

This work connects the lattice QCD PDF extraction community, which has developed quasi-PDF (Ji, 2013) and pseudo-PDF (Radyushkin, 2017) approaches, with the finite-temperature spectral function reconstruction community that has addressed similar ill-posed inverse problems for in-medium heavy quarkonium states (Aarts, 2014; Kim, 2018). The paper extends discussions from recent workshops at the Amherst Center, ECT*, CERN, INT, and MITP that highlight uncertainty quantification, and builds on the inverse problem analysis by Karpie (2019) and Xiong (2025).
