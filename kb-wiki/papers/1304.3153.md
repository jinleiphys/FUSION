# R-matrix analysis of reactions in the 9B compound system

> Recent activity in solving the 'lithium problem' in big bang nucleosynthesis has focused on the role that putative resonances may play in resonance-enhanced destruction of 7Li. Particular attention has been paid to the reactions involving the 9B compound nuclear system, d+7Be->9B. These reactions are analyzed via the multichannel, two-body unitary R-matrix method using code (EDA) developed by Hale and collaborators. We employ much of the known elastic and reaction data, in a four-channel treatment. The data include elastic 3He+6Li differential cross sections from 0.7 to 2.0 MeV, integrated reaction cross sections for energies from 0.7 to 5.0 MeV for 6Li(3He,p)8Be* and from 0.4 to 5.0 MeV for the 6Li(3He,d)7Be reaction. Capture data have been added to an earlier analysis with integrated cross section measurements from 0.7 to 0.825 MeV for 6Li(3He,gamma)9B. The resulting resonance parameters are compared with tabulated values, and previously unidentified resonances are noted. Our results show that there are no near d+7Be threshold resonances with widths that are 10's of keV and reduce the likelihood that a resonance-enhanced mass-7 destruction mechanism, as suggested in recently published work, can explain the 7Li problem.

## Key claim

The multichannel R-matrix analysis finds no near d+7Be threshold resonances with widths of tens of keV, reducing the likelihood that a resonance-enhanced mass-7 destruction mechanism can explain the 7Li problem in big bang nucleosynthesis, contrary to conjectures based on TUNL-NDG evaluation tables.

## Method

The authors employed the multichannel, two-body unitary R-matrix method using the EDA code developed by Hale and collaborators, treating four hadronic channels (d+7Be, 3He+6Li, p+8Be*, and the electromagnetic gamma+9B channel) with channel radii constrained between 5.5 fm and 7.5 fm for hadronic channels. Elastic 3He+6Li differential cross sections from 0.7 to 2.0 MeV, integrated reaction cross sections for 6Li(3He,p)8Be* from 0.7 to 5.0 MeV and for 6Li(3He,d)7Be from 0.4 to 5.0 MeV, and capture data for 6Li(3He,gamma)9B from 0.7 to 0.825 MeV were included in the analysis, with resonance parameters adjusted by minimizing chi-squared with respect to the R-matrix parameters.

## Key numbers

- The d+7Be threshold occurs at 16.4901 MeV excitation energy in 9B
- The discrepancy between calculated primordial 7Li abundance and observations from low-metallicity halo dwarf stars is a factor of 2.2 to 4.2, corresponding to a deviation of 4.5 sigma to 5.5 sigma
- Elastic 3He+6Li differential cross sections were analyzed from 0.7 to 2.0 MeV
- Integrated reaction cross sections for 6Li(3He,p)8Be* were analyzed from 0.7 to 5.0 MeV
- Integrated reaction cross sections for 6Li(3He,d)7Be were analyzed from 0.4 to 5.0 MeV
- Capture data for 6Li(3He,gamma)9B were analyzed from 0.7 to 0.825 MeV
- Channel radii for hadronic channels were constrained between 5.5 fm and 7.5 fm
- The electromagnetic gamma+9B channel used a channel radius of 50.0 fm

## Context

This work extends the R-matrix analysis of the 9B compound system to address the "lithium problem" in big bang nucleosynthesis, where calculations overpredict the primordial 7Li abundance compared to observations by Spite (1982) and Gonzalez Hernandez (2009). The analysis contradicts the conjecture by Cyburt (2009) that a putative 5/2+ resonance near 16.7 MeV excitation energy could enhance destruction of 7Be through reactions like 7Be(d,p)alpha-alpha and 7Be(d,gamma)9B, which was based on TUNL-NDG evaluation tables by Tilley (2004). The results enable improved reaction rate calculations for BBN models and provide updated resonance parameters for the 9B compound system for the ENDF and ENSDF communities.
