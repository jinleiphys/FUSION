# 2PI functional techniques for gauge theories: QED

> We discuss the formulation of the prototype gauge field theory, QED, in the context of two-particle-irreducible (2PI) functional techniques with particular emphasis on the issues of renormalization and gauge symmetry. We show how to renormalize all $n$-point vertex functions of the (gauge-fixed) theory at any approximation order in the 2PI loop-expansion by properly adjusting a finite set of local counterterms consistent with the underlying gauge symmetry. The paper is divided in three parts: a self-contained presentation of the main results and their possible implementation for practical applications; a detailed analysis of ultraviolet divergences and their removal; a number of appendices collecting technical details.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that all n-point vertex functions obtained from the 2PI loop-expansion of QED in covariant linear gauges can be made finite at any approximation order by adjusting a finite set of local counterterms consistent with the underlying gauge symmetry, but they note that the gauge-fixing independence of physical observables is not guaranteed at finite approximation order even when the correct Ward-Takahashi identities are satisfied.

## Method

The authors formulate QED in the 2PI functional framework using a 12-component superfield that combines the photon, electron, and positron fields, and derive expressions for both 2PI vertex functions (field derivatives of the self-energy) and 2PI-resummed vertex functions (field derivatives of the effective action). They introduce counterterms for the renormalized 2PI effective action through field and parameter redefinitions (Z factors for fields, mass, coupling, and gauge parameter), and show that the structure of ultraviolet divergences is constrained by 2PI Ward identities such that no symmetry-breaking counterterms are needed. The renormalization procedure is validated through a formal coupling expansion using dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers

- The free inverse fermion propagator: $iD_{0,\bar\alpha\alpha}^{-1}(x,y) = [i\slashchar{\partial}_x - m]_{\bar\alpha\alpha}\delta^{(4)}(x-y)$
- The free inverse photon propagator: $iG_{0,\mu\nu}^{-1}(x,y) = [g_{\mu\nu}\partial_x^2 - (1-\lambda)\partial^x_\mu\partial^x_\nu]\delta^{(4)}(x-y)$
- The renormalization factors relate bare and renormalized parameters as: $Z_0 m_R \equiv Z_2 m$, $Z_1 e_R \equiv Z_2 Z_3^{1/2} e$, $Z_4 \lambda_R \equiv Z_3 \lambda$
- Dimensional regularization parameter: $\epsilon = 4 - d$ where $d$ is the spacetime dimension

## Context

This work extends the renormalization theory of 2PI functional techniques from scalar field theories (Berges 2005) and fermionic theories (Reinosa 2005) to abelian gauge theories, building on earlier work by the same authors (Reinosa 2006) that addressed renormalization of photon and fermion two-point functions. The paper provides the complete renormalization framework needed for practical 2PI calculations in QED, such as the finite-temperature thermodynamic pressure calculation performed by Borsanyi (2007), and establishes the foundation for extending these techniques to non-abelian gauge theories.
