# Lifting the Veil on Quark Matter in Compact Stars with Core g-mode Oscillations

> Compact stars containing quark matter may masquerade as neutron stars in the range of measured mass and radius, making it difficult to draw firm conclusions on the phase of matter inside the star. The sensitivity of core $g$-mode oscillations to the presence of a mixed phase may alleviate this difficulty. In hybrid stars that admit quark matter in a mixed phase, the $g$-mode frequency rises sharply due to a marked decrease in the equilibrium sound speed. Resonant excitation of $g$-modes can leave an imprint on the waveform of coalescing binary compact stars. We present some analytic and numeric results to assess the sensitivity displayed by $g$-mode oscillations to quark matter in a homogeneous or mixed phase and find that the $g$-mode can probe the fraction of quark matter inside the star.

## Key claim
Core g-mode oscillations in hybrid stars with a quark-hadron mixed phase show a sharp rise in frequency that can distinguish them from ordinary neutron stars, potentially resolving the "masquerade problem" where hybrid stars are otherwise indistinguishable from neutron stars based on mass and radius measurements alone. The authors find that the g-mode can probe the fraction of quark matter inside the star, but note that the modes do not survive the entire extent of the mixed phase, turning off for some fraction of quark matter beyond a threshold.

## Method
The authors constructed stellar models using the TOV equations in General Relativity, then solved linearized fluid perturbation equations under the Newtonian and Cowling approximations to compute g-mode frequencies. They employed the SL23 nuclear equation of state for hadronic matter and the vBag model for two-flavor quark matter, using the Gibbs construction for the mixed phase, with parameters chosen to yield maximum masses of at least 2 solar masses.

## Key numbers
- The Brunt-Vaisala frequency for non-interacting (n,p,e-) matter is approximately 100 Hz.
- For non-interacting two-flavor quark matter, the electron fraction is fixed at approximately 0.0056, leading to zero Brunt-Vaisala frequency.
- For the vBag model with vector coupling Kv ~ (2-6) GeV^-2, the electron fraction in quark matter ranges from approximately 0.006 to 0.014 at densities relevant to compact stars (n ~ 3-6 times nuclear saturation density).
- The neutrino damping timescale for the g-mode in quark matter is approximately 8.2 years times (T_9)^(-4) times (n_sat/n)^(2/3) times (1/(delta_mu/MeV)), where T_9 is temperature in units of 10^9 K.
- The g-mode frequency for hybrid stars rises above approximately 1 kHz for masses above 1.6 solar masses, compared to lower frequencies (0.4-0.8 kHz) for neutron stars or low-mass hybrid stars.

## Context
This work extends the study of g-mode oscillations as a diagnostic of dense matter composition, following earlier work by Lai (1994) on g-modes in neutron stars and by Miniutti (2002) on discontinuity g-modes at phase transitions. It addresses the "masquerade problem" identified by Alford (2004), where hybrid stars with quark matter are indistinguishable from neutron stars. The authors note that while hyperon appearance (studied by Dommes (2015) and Yu (2017)) also affects g-modes, the effect is less dramatic than for a quark-hadron mixed phase. The results suggest that resonant excitation of g-modes during binary inspiral could leave imprints on gravitational waveforms detectable by third-generation detectors, enabling distinction between neutron stars and hybrid stars.
