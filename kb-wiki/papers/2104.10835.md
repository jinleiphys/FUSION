# Symmetry from Entanglement Suppression

> Symmetry is among the most fundamental and powerful concepts in nature, whose existence is usually taken as given, without explanation. We explore whether symmetry can be derived from more fundamental principles from the perspective of quantum information. Starting with a two-qubit system, we show there are only two minimally entangling logic gates: the Identity and the SWAP, where SWAP interchanges the two states of the qubits. We further demonstrate that, when viewed as an entanglement operator in the spin-space, the $S$-matrix in the two-body scattering of fermions in the $s$-wave channel is uniquely determined by unitarity and rotational invariance to be a linear combination of the Identity and the SWAP. Realizing a minimally entangling $S$-matrix would give rise to global symmetries, as exemplified in Wigner's spin-flavor symmetry and Schrödinger's conformal invariance in low energy Quantum Chromodynamics. For $N_q$ species of qubit, the Identity gate is associated with an $[SU(2)]^{N_q}$ symmetry, which is enlarged to $SU(2N_q)$ when there is a species-universal coupling constant.

## Key claim

Symmetry can be derived from the principle of entanglement suppression: the only two minimally entangling logic gates in a two-qubit system (Identity and SWAP) correspond to the only forms allowed for the two-body fermion S-matrix in the s-wave channel under unitarity and rotational invariance, and realizing these gates in scattering gives rise to global symmetries.

## Method

The authors classify all non-local two-qubit operators using Cartan decomposition of the symmetric coset space SU(4)/(SU(2)⊗SU(2)), parameterizing inequivalent entanglers by three angles (β_x, β_y, β_z). They compute the entanglement power (average of the squared concurrence over all direct product initial states) and find it vanishes only for β_a = 0 or β_a = π/4 for all a, corresponding to the Identity and SWAP gates. They then show that the s-wave S-matrix for fermion scattering, constrained by unitarity and rotational invariance, must be a linear combination of these same two minimal entanglers. Finally, they construct effective Hamiltonians that realize the Identity gate and derive the resulting global symmetries.

## Context

This work extends the observation by Beane (2018) that entanglement suppression in two-nucleon scattering correlates with enhanced Wigner spin-flavor symmetry and Schrödinger conformal invariance in low-energy QCD. The authors purify this observation into a universal quantum-information framework, showing the connection is not confined to QCD. The results enable a new information-theoretic approach to understanding the origin of global symmetries, with the Identity gate realized in low-energy QCD (octet baryons exhibiting SU(16) spin-flavor symmetry) and the SWAP gate potentially realizable at RG fixed points with Schrödinger symmetry.
