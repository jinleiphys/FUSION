# Generalized parton distributions from meson leptoproduction

> Generalized parton distributions (GPDs) extracted from exclusive meson leptoproduction within the handbag approach are briefly reviewed. Only the GPD E is discussed in some detail. Applications of these GPDs to virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and to Ji's sum rule are also presented.

## Key claim

The handbag approach, using a double-distribution parametrization of GPDs fitted to meson leptoproduction data, can describe both DVMP and DVCS data with a common set of GPDs, but the extracted GPDs are not optimized for the large-skewness, small-W kinematics characteristic of JLab data.

## Method

GPDs are constructed from double distributions with a weight function generating skewness dependence, where the zero-skewness GPD is parametrized as its forward limit multiplied by an exponential in Mandelstam t with a Regge-like profile function. Parameters of the double distribution are fitted to available data on rho0, phi and pi+ production from HERMES, COMPASS, E665, H1 and ZEUS, covering Q^2 from 3 to 100 GeV^2 and W from 4 to 180 GeV, with constraints from nucleon form factors and positivity bounds. The subprocess amplitudes are calculated using k_perp factorization, taking into account quark transverse degrees of freedom and Sudakov suppression.

## Key numbers

- Data coverage: Q^2 from 3 GeV^2 to 100 GeV^2, W from 4 GeV to 180 GeV
- For valence quarks the weight function parameter n=1; for sea quarks and gluons n=2
- The cross section for vector meson production falls approximately as 1/Q^4 experimentally
- The strange quark E^s positivity bound is saturated for c_e^s = +/- 0.155 (beta_e^s = 7)
- Gluon E^g normalization uses beta^g_e = 6
- Parton angular momenta at scale 4 GeV^2: J^u = 0.250, J^d = 0.020, J^s = 0.015, J^g = 0.214 (for E^s = E^g = 0); or J^u = 0.225, J^d = -0.005, J^s = -0.011, J^g = 0.286 (for E^s < 0, E^g > 0)

## Context

This work extends the handbag approach analysis of GPDs from DVMP (GK1) to DVCS (kms), showing consistency between the two reaction channels. It builds on the double distribution formalism of Mueller et al. (1994) and Radyushkin (1998), and the form factor analysis of Diehl et al. (2004). The extracted GPDs enable parameter-free calculation of DVCS observables and evaluation of Ji's sum rule for parton angular momenta, though the approach requires improvement for JLab kinematics where power corrections are strong.
