# Generalized optical potential for weakly bound nuclei. I. Two-cluster projectiles

> A generalized optical potential for elastic scattering induced by light nuclei is calculated within the Feshbach projection operator method. The model explicitly takes into account the contribution of the projectile break-up continuum treated within a microscopic few-cluster model. In this work we formulate the model, deriving an explicit expression for the optical potential, and show ability of the model applying it to deuteron elastic scattering.

## Key claim

The paper derives a generalized optical potential for elastic scattering of weakly bound two-cluster projectiles using the Feshbach projection operator method, explicitly including the projectile break-up continuum treated within a microscopic few-cluster model. The model goes beyond earlier simplified treatments by including spins, all partial waves of the continuum, Coulomb forces, and the full Green function rather than a free-particle approximation, but the authors note that continuum-to-continuum coupling is neglected and that the approximation is justified when the collision time is shorter than the internal cluster-cluster motion time.

## Method

The authors construct Feshbach projection operators using the bound and continuum states of the projectile, described as a two-cluster system within a microscopic cluster model. The generalized optical potential is separated into a cluster-folding term \(U^{(1)}\), obtained by folding cluster-target interactions over the projectile ground state, and a non-local dynamical polarization potential \(U^{(2)}\) that accounts for coupling to break-up channels. The polarization potential is computed using a separable approximation for the total Green function, with the partial-wave two-body Green function constructed from regular and irregular solutions of the Schrödinger equation with the folding potential. The method is applied to deuteron elastic scattering on heavy targets (\({}^{58}\)Ni, \({}^{120}\)Sn, \({}^{208}\)Pb) at energies of 80–110 MeV, using nucleon-target optical potentials fitted to experimental data at half the deuteron energy.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: 2.22 MeV; deuteron r.m.s. radius: \(\langle r^2_d \rangle^{1/2} = 1.97\) fm; triplet scattering length: \(a_t = 5.46\) fm (from the Gaussian proton-neutron interaction used).
- Integration over projectile excitation energy \(\varepsilon_\kappa\) is performed up to 40 MeV for the deuteron case; the sum over transfer angular momenta \(\lambda\) is truncated at \(\lambda_{\text{max}} = 6\).
- For the d + \({}^{208}\)Pb reaction at \(E_d = 110\) MeV and \(J = L = 25\), the dynamical polarization potential provides a weak real contribution (Re \(U^{(2)} < 3\) MeV) and a strong absorptive contribution (Im \(U^{(2)} \sim -10\) MeV), as shown in the weighted mean local polarization potential.
- The non-diagonal terms of the polarization potential (arising from non-central cluster-cluster interaction) are about \(10^2\) times smaller than the diagonal ones and are neglected.
- The Fourier-Bessel integration for transition form factors is truncated at \(q_{\text{max}} \sim 6\) fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the generalized optical model (GOM) of Feshbach (1958, 1962) to weakly bound two-cluster projectiles, improving upon earlier applications by Testoni (1970), Baumgartner (1971), and Zagrebaev (1978) that neglected spins, considered only s-wave continuum, treated Coulomb forces approximately, and used a free-particle Green function. The approach is an alternative to the continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CDCC) method of Soper (1979), Rawitscher (1974), and Sakuragi (1987); the authors note that CDCC is more efficient for two-cluster projectiles but that GOM becomes more feasible for few-body projectiles with more clusters. The results enable future application to reactions with weakly bound few-cluster nuclei such as \({}^{6}\)He, to be presented in a subsequent publication.
