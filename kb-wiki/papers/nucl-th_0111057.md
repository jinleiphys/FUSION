# From quark-gluon plasma to hadron spectra

> Results on initial transverse energy production based on NLO perturbative QCD calculation with final state saturation of produced minijets are used to fix the initial energy density of produced matter. Assuming rapid thermalization, this provides the initial conditions for a hydrodynamic description of the expansion of final matter. Given a prescription of the the decoupling of particles from the thermal system to free particles, final transverse spectra of hadrons and integrated quantities like multiplicity and transverse energy can be calculated in the central rapidity region. Results are reported and compared with measurements.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that combining a next-to-leading-order (NLO) perturbative QCD calculation of minijet production with final-state saturation and a hydrodynamic expansion model can reproduce measured hadron multiplicities and transverse energy at RHIC, but only if the hydrodynamic expansion transfers a large fraction (roughly a factor of 2.6) of the initial transverse energy from mid-rapidity towards the fragmentation regions via pressure work.

## Method

The initial energy density of produced matter was fixed using an NLO pQCD calculation of minijet production with final-state saturation, assuming rapid thermalization at the formation time tau_0 = 1/p_sat. The expansion was modeled with boost-invariant longitudinal flow (v_z = z/t) and cylindrically symmetric transverse flow, using an equation of state with an ideal QGP at high temperatures, a hadron gas including resonances up to 2 GeV, a bag constant B^{1/4} = 235 MeV giving T_c = 165 MeV, and a mean-field repulsion with K = 450 MeV. Final particle spectra were computed using the Cooper-Fry prescription at a decoupling temperature T_dec ~ 120 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The calculated transverse energy per unit pseudorapidity from minijets is larger than the measured value by a factor of approximately 2.6.
- At RHIC (sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV), the saturation scale p_sat = 1.16 GeV with formation time 0.170 fm/c; at LHC (sqrt(s_NN) = 5500 GeV), p_sat = 2.03 GeV with formation time 0.100 fm/c.
- The phase transition temperature is T_c = 165 MeV from a bag constant B^{1/4} = 235 MeV.
- At sqrt(s) = 130 GeV, the calculated final transverse energy dE_T/d_eta = 614 GeV, compared to the PHENIX measured value of 578 GeV (about 6% smaller); the initial value was approximately 1550 GeV.
- At sqrt(s) = 130 GeV, the calculated average transverse momentum of final particles is <p_T>_f = 0.594 GeV, compared to the measured value 0.514 GeV; the initial minijet average was <p_T>_i = 1.47 GeV.
- At sqrt(s) = 5500 GeV, the calculated initial minijet average transverse momentum is <p_T>_i = 3.2 GeV.
- Inverse slopes (Teff) at M_T - M = 0.3 GeV and sqrt(s) = 130 GeV: calculated pion 238 MeV, kaon 387 MeV, proton 652 MeV; measured pion 190 MeV, kaon 300 MeV, proton 565 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Eskola et al. (2000) and Eskola et al. (1988) by combining their NLO pQCD minijet calculation with saturation into a full hydrodynamic description of the expansion, following the framework of Bjorken (1983) for longitudinal flow and Cooper and Fry for decoupling. The results are compared with PHOBOS, PHENIX, and STAR measurements at RHIC, and predictions are made for LHC energies. The approach enables future studies of photon and lepton pair emission, strangeness evolution, and heavier flavor production in the dense final state.
