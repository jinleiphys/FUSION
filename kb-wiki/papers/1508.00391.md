# Examining the stability of thermally fissile Th and U isotopes

> The properties of recently predicted thermally fissile Th and U isotopes are studied within the framework of relativistic mean field (RMF) approach using axially deformed basis. We calculated the ground, first intrinsic excited state and matter density for highly neutron-rich thorium and uranium isotopes. The possible modes of decay like $α$-decay and $β$-decay are analyzed. We found that the neutron-rich isotopes are stable against $α$-decay, however they are very much unstable against $β$-decay. The life time of these nuclei predicted to be tens of second against $β$-decay. If these nuclei utilize before their decay time, a lots of energy can be produced within the help of multi-fragmentation fission. Also, these nuclei have a great implication in astrophysical point of view. The total nucleonic densities distribution are calculated, from which the clusters inside the parent nuclei are determined. %Most of the thorium isotopes are $α$ emitters, where as some %of them have short half-lives. In some cases, we found the isomeric states with energy range from 2 to 3 MeV and three minima in the potential energy surface of $^{228-230}$Th and $^{228-234}$U isotopes.

## Key claim

The neutron-rich thorium and uranium isotopes predicted to be thermally fissile by Satpathy et al. (2008) are stable against α-decay but highly unstable against β-decay, with β-decay lifetimes on the order of tens of seconds, meaning they could only be used for energy production via multi-fragmentation fission if utilized before decaying.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic mean field (RMF) formalism with the NL3 parameter set in an axially deformed harmonic oscillator basis with N_F = N_B = 20 oscillator quanta, including BCS pairing correlations with a constant gap prescription. They calculated ground state and first intrinsic excited state properties, binding energies, charge radii, quadrupole deformation parameters, and potential energy surfaces for a range of Th and U isotopes, then analyzed α-decay and β-decay Q-values using empirical formulas.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of ²³⁶U: 1791.7 MeV (RMF), 1790.0 MeV (FRDM), 1790.4 MeV (experimental), with corresponding β₂ values of 0.276, 0.215, and 0.272 respectively
- Binding energy of ²³²Th: 1767.0 MeV (RMF), 1766.2 MeV (FRDM), 1766.7 MeV (experimental), with β₂ of 0.251, 0.207, and 0.248 respectively
- Isomeric states found with energy range from 2 to 3 MeV for ²²⁸-²³⁰Th and ²²⁸-²³⁴U isotopes
- β-decay lifetimes predicted to be tens of seconds for the neutron-rich thermally fissile isotopes
- Binding energy change of approximately 0.2 MeV when increasing oscillator basis from N_F = N_B = 14 to 20 for ²⁴⁰Th

## Context

This work extends the prediction by Satpathy et al. (2008) that uranium and thorium isotopes with neutron numbers N=154-172 possess thermally fissile properties, using a different theoretical framework (RMF rather than macroscopic-microscopic models). The results confirm previous findings by Ren et al. (2001, 2003) that superheavy nuclei have highly deformed ground states, while contradicting Sobaczewski (1991) who argued for spherical or normally deformed ground states. The study enables future investigation of these nuclei for potential energy production and astrophysical nucleosynthesis applications.
