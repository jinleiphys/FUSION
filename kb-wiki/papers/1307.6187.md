# Pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions in chiral effective field theory with Delta(1232)-degrees of freedom

> A calculation of the pion-production operator up to next-to-next-to-leading order for s-wave pions is performed within chiral effective field theory. In the previous study [Phys. Rev. C 85, 054001 (2012)] we discussed the contribution of the pion-nucleon loops at the same order. Here we extend that study to include explicit Delta degrees of freedom and the 1/m_N^2 corrections to the pion-production amplitude. Using the power counting scheme where the Delta-nucleon mass difference is of the order of the characteristic momentum scale in the production process, we calculate all tree-level and loop diagrams involving Delta up to next-to-next-to-leading order. The long-range part of the Delta loop contributions is found to be of similar size to that from the pion-nucleon loops which supports the counting scheme. The net effect of pion-nucleon and Delta loops is expected to play a crucial role in understanding of the neutral pion production data.

## Key claim

The Delta(1232) resonance must be included as an explicit dynamical degree of freedom in chiral effective field theory calculations of pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions, because the Delta-nucleon mass splitting (about 300 MeV) is numerically of the same order as the characteristic momentum scale in the production process, and the long-range part of the Delta loop contributions at next-to-next-to-leading order is found to be of similar size to that from the pion-nucleon loops, supporting this counting scheme.

## Method

The authors extended their previous calculation of the s-wave pion-production operator up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) within chiral effective field theory by including explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom and 1/m_N^2 corrections to the production amplitude. Using the momentum counting scheme where the Delta-nucleon mass difference is treated as being of order the characteristic momentum p ~ sqrt(m_pi m_N), they calculated all tree-level and loop diagrams involving the Delta up to NNLO, and regularized ultraviolet divergences by absorbing them into low-energy constants.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter in the momentum counting scheme is chi_MCS ~ sqrt(m_pi/m_N), where m_pi is the pion mass and m_N is the nucleon mass
- The Delta-nucleon mass splitting delta = m_Delta - m_N is numerically of order p ~ sqrt(m_pi m_N), justifying its treatment as a dynamical degree of freedom
- The leading-order rescattering operator scales as chi_MCS/f_pi^3 for s-wave pions
- The Weinberg-Tomozawa vertex takes its on-shell value of 2 m_pi (even when the incoming pion is off-shell)
- The tree-level rescattering operator at LO involves the Weinberg-Tomozawa pi N vertex from L^{(1)}_{pi N} and its recoil correction from L^{(2)}_{pi N}

## Context

This work extends the previous study by the same group (Filin et al., Phys. Rev. C 85, 054001, 2012) which calculated pion-nucleon loop contributions at NNLO but did not include explicit Delta degrees of freedom. The present calculation completes the NNLO analysis of s-wave pion production and shows that Delta loops contribute at the same order as pion-nucleon loops, contrary to earlier phenomenological studies (Lee, HGM, and others) that did not consider these NNLO loop contributions. The results enable a more complete understanding of neutral pion production data in the pp to pp pi^0 channel, which directly probes higher-order contributions that are masked in charged channels by the dominant Weinberg-Tomozawa term.
