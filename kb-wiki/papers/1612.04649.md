# An effective two-body model for spectra of clusters of ${}^2$H, ${}^3$H, ${}^3$He, and $^4$He with $^4$He, and $^2$H-$^4$He scattering

> Four light-mass nuclei are considered by an effective two-body clusterisation method; $^6$Li as $^2$H$+^4$He, $^7$Li as $^3$H$+^4$He, $^7$Be as $^3$He$+^4$He, and $^8$Be as $^4$He$+^4$He. The low-energy spectrum of each is determined from single-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations, as are low-energy elastic scattering cross sections for the $^2$H$+^4$He system. These are presented at many angles and energies for which there are data. While some of these systems may be more fully described by many-body theories, this work establishes that a large amount of data may be explained by these two-body clusterisations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a simple effective two-body, single-channel cluster model can reproduce a large amount of low-energy spectral and scattering data for light nuclei (вБґLi, вБЈLi, вБЈBe, вБЄBe), though the authors acknowledge that some systems "may be more fully described by many-body theories" and that certain features, such as the 1вБЇ resonance in вБґLi, require more sophisticated approaches.

## Method

The authors solve single-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations using a Sturmian expansion method with separable potentials derived from Woods-Saxon form factors and Coulomb potentials from folded three-parameter Fermi charge distributions. For вБґLi, they treat the ¬≤H-вБіHe system as two uncoupled single-channel problems corresponding to the ¬≥SвВБ (triplet) and ¬єSвВА (singlet) states of the deuteron. For вБЈLi, вБЈBe, and вБЄBe, they use single-channel interactions between spin-0 вБіHe and the appropriate cluster partner (¬≥H, ¬≥He, or another вБіHe).
