# An effective two-body model for spectra of clusters of ${}^2$H, ${}^3$H, ${}^3$He, and $^4$He with $^4$He, and $^2$H-$^4$He scattering

> Four light-mass nuclei are considered by an effective two-body clusterisation method; $^6$Li as $^2$H$+^4$He, $^7$Li as $^3$H$+^4$He, $^7$Be as $^3$He$+^4$He, and $^8$Be as $^4$He$+^4$He. The low-energy spectrum of each is determined from single-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations, as are low-energy elastic scattering cross sections for the $^2$H$+^4$He system. These are presented at many angles and energies for which there are data. While some of these systems may be more fully described by many-body theories, this work establishes that a large amount of data may be explained by these two-body clusterisations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a simple effective two-body, single-channel cluster model can reproduce a large amount of low-energy spectral and scattering data for light nuclei (⁶Li, ⁷Li, ⁷Be, ⁸Be), though the authors acknowledge that some systems "may be more fully described by many-body theories" and that certain features, such as the 1⁺ resonance in ⁶Li, require more sophisticated approaches.

## Method

The authors solve single-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations using a Sturmian expansion method with separable potentials derived from Woods-Saxon form factors and Coulomb potentials from folded three-parameter Fermi charge distributions. For ⁶Li, they treat the ²H-⁴He system as two uncoupled single-channel problems corresponding to the ³S₁ (triplet) and ¹S₀ (singlet) states of the deuteron. For ⁷Li, ⁷Be, and ⁸Be, they use single-channel interactions between spin-0 ⁴He and the appropriate cluster partner (³H, ³He, or another ⁴He).

## Key numbers

- ⁸Be ground state (0⁺) resonance: centroid 0.092 MeV, width 5 eV (experimental: 0.092 MeV, 5.96 eV)
- ⁸Be first excited (2⁺) resonance: centroid 3.16 MeV, width 1.11 MeV (experimental: 3.03 MeV, 1.51 MeV)
- ⁷Li bound states: 3/2⁻ at -2.49 MeV (experimental -2.47 MeV), 1/2⁻ at -1.81 MeV (experimental -1.99 MeV)
- ⁷Be bound states: 3/2⁻ at -1.55 MeV (experimental -1.59 MeV), 1/2⁻ at -0.90 MeV (experimental -1.16 MeV)
- ⁷Li 7/2⁻ resonance: centroid 2.23 MeV, width 83 keV (experimental: 2.18 MeV, 69 keV)
- ⁷Be 7/2⁻ resonance: centroid 3.19 MeV, width 180 keV (experimental: 2.98 MeV, 175 keV)
- ⁶Li 3⁺ resonance: 0.7117 MeV above scattering threshold (deuteron energy 1.067 MeV)
- ⁶Li 2⁺₁ resonance: 2.8375 MeV above scattering threshold (deuteron energy 4.253 MeV)
- ⁶Li 1⁺ resonance: 4.1757 MeV above scattering threshold (deuteron energy 6.264 MeV)
- ⁴He 3pF charge distribution parameters: R_c = 1.008 fm, a_c = 0.327 fm, w = 0.445

## Context

This work extends the multi-channel algebraic scattering (MCAS) approach previously used by Canton et al. (2006) for ⁷Li and ⁷Be spectra, now applied with a program written for spin-0 ⁴He particles coupling to a nucleus. The results are compared with more sophisticated calculations: the RGM/NCSM method of Quaglioni et al. (2008), Navratil et al. (2011), and Hupin et al. (2015), and the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas three-body approach of Deltuva et al. (2006). The two-body model enables future full coupled-channel calculations of the ⁴He+⁸Be cluster leading to the Hoyle state in ¹²C, which is crucial for the triple-alpha stellar process.
