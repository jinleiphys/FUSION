# Hydrodynamics with Triangle Anomalies

> We consider the hydrodynamic regime of theories with quantum anomalies for global currents. We show that a hitherto discarded term in the conserve current is not only allowed by symmetries, but is in fact required by triangle anomalies and the second law of thermodynamics. This term leads to a number of new effects, one of which is chiral separation in a rotating fluid at nonzero chemical potential. The new kinetic coefficients can be expressed, in a unique fashion, through the anomalies coefficients and the equation of state. We briefly discuss the relevance of this new hydrodynamic term for physical situations, including heavy ion collisions.

# Hydrodynamics with Triangle Anomalies

## Key claim
Quantum triangle anomalies for global currents require a previously discarded vorticity-induced term in the conserved current constitutive equation, which is fixed uniquely by the anomaly coefficients and the equation of state rather than being merely allowed by symmetries. The derivation relies on imposing both the anomaly structure and the second law of thermodynamics, which together force the new term to appear in first-order hydrodynamics.

## Method
The authors derive the constitutive equations for a relativistic fluid with one or multiple U(1) global currents carrying triangle anomalies, using the requirement of a non-negative entropy production rate in the presence of a slowly varying external background gauge field. They solve the resulting constraints on the transport coefficients by changing variables to chemical potential over temperature and pressure, then verify the formulas explicitly through a holographic calculation in a 5D Einstein-Maxwell-Chern-Simons theory dual to strongly coupled N=4 super-Yang-Mills plasma with R-charge density.

## Key numbers
- The new kinetic coefficient for a single U(1) current is ξ = C (μ² − (2/3) (μ³ n)/(ε+P)), where C is the anomaly coefficient, μ is chemical potential, n is charge density, ε is energy density, and P is pressure.
- For multiple U(1) currents, ξ^a = C^{abc} μ^b μ^c − (2/3) n^a C^{bcd} (μ^b μ^c μ^d)/(ε+P), with C^{abc} symmetric under index permutations.
- The holographic calculation yields ξ = −(3 q² κ)/(2π G₅ m) and ξ_B = −(√3 (3R⁴+m) q κ)/(4π G₅ m R²), where q and m are black-brane charge and mass parameters, κ is the Chern-Simons coupling, G₅ is the 5D Newton constant, and R is the horizon radius.
- The anomaly coefficient C is related to the 5D Chern-Simons parameter by C = −2κ/(π G₅).

## Context
This work extends the standard relativistic hydrodynamic framework of Eckart (1940) and Landau-Lifshitz by showing that triangle anomalies, first discovered by Adler (1969) and Bell and Jackiw (1969), force a vorticity-induced current term that was previously thought to violate the second law of thermodynamics. The result confirms and generalizes earlier holographic calculations by Erdmenger et al. (2008), Banerjee et al. (2008), and Torabian and Yee (2009) which found nonzero values for ξ in specific models. The new term enables predictions for chiral separation in rotating fluids at nonzero chemical potential, with potential applications to heavy-ion collisions (the chiral magnetic effect of Kharzeev et al., 2007) and to dense neutrino gases or the early Universe with large lepton chemical potential.
