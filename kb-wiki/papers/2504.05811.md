# Effects of strange molecular partners of $P_c$ states in $γp \to K Σ$ reactions

> Our previous studies revealed evidence of the strange molecular partners of $P_c$ states, $N(2080)3/2^-$ and $N(2270)3/2^-$, in the $γp \to K^{*+} Σ^0 / K^{*0} Σ^+$ and $γp \to ϕp$ reactions. Motivated by the differential cross-section data for $γp \to K^+ Σ^0$ from CLAS 2010, which exhibits some bump structures at $W \approx$ 1875, 2080 and 2270 MeV, we extend our previous analysis by investigating the effects of $N(1535)1/2^-$, $N(1875)3/2^-$, $N(2080)1/2^- \&\ 3/2^-$ and $N(2270)1/2^- , 3/2^- \&\ 5/2^-$, as strange partners of $P_c$ molecular states, in the reactions $γp \to K^+ Σ^0$ and $γp \to K^0 Σ^+$. The theoretical model employed in this study utilizes an effective Lagrangian approach in the tree-level Born approximation. It contains the contributions from $s$-channel with exchanges of $N$, $Δ$, $N^*$ (including the hadronic molecules with hidden strangeness), and $Δ^*$; $t$-channel; $u$-channel; and the generalized contact term. The results based on the final fitted parameters are in good agreement with all available experimental data of both cross-sections and polarization observables for $γp \to K^+ Σ^0$ and $γp \to K^0 Σ^+$. Notably, the $s$-channel exchanges of molecules significantly contribute to the bump structures in cross-sections for $γp \to K Σ$ at $W \approx$ 1900, 2080 and 2270 MeV, and show considerable coherence with contributions from $s$-channel exchanges of general resonances to construct the overall structures of cross-sections. More abundant experiments, particularly for the reaction $γp \to K^0 Σ^+$, are necessary to further strengthen the constraints on the theoretical models.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the bump structures observed in the differential cross-section data for $\gamma p \to K^+ \Sigma^0$ at center-of-mass energies $W \approx$ 1875, 2080, and 2270 MeV can be explained by the $s$-channel exchange of seven hidden-strange hadronic molecular states ($N(1535)1/2^-$, $N(1875)3/2^-$, $N(2080)1/2^- \&\ 3/2^-$, and $N(2270)1/2^- , 3/2^- \&\ 5/2^-$), which are proposed as strange partners of the $P_c$ molecular states, though the authors acknowledge that the $N(2080)3/2^-$ designation is retained for consistency with their previous work and is not necessarily identified with the $N(2120)$ resonance in the Review of Particle Physics.

## Method

The authors employ an effective Lagrangian approach in the tree-level Born approximation to simultaneously analyze experimental data for $\gamma p \to K^+ \Sigma^0$ and $\gamma p \to K^0 \Sigma^+$ reactions. The model includes $s$-channel exchanges of nucleons, deltas, general resonances, and seven hidden-strange molecular candidates; $t$-channel exchanges of $K$, $K^*(892)$, and $K_1(1270)$; $u$-channel exchange of $\Sigma$; and a generalized contact term to ensure gauge invariance, with parameters fitted via $\chi^2$ minimization using MINUIT against 5784 experimental data points from multiple collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Bump structures in $\gamma p \to K^+ \Sigma^0$ differential cross-section data from CLAS 2010 appear at $W \approx$ 1875, 2080, and 2270 MeV, corresponding to the Breit-Wigner masses of the proposed molecular states.
- The masses of the seven molecular candidates are taken as 1535, 1875, 2080, and 2270 MeV for $N(1535)1/2^-$, $N(1875)3/2^-$, $N(2080)1/2^- \&\ 3/2^-$, and $N(2270)1/2^- , 3/2^- \&\ 5/2^-$, respectively.
- The fit uses 5784 total experimental data points from the $\gamma p \to K^+ \Sigma^0$ and $\gamma p \to K^0 \Sigma^+$ reactions, with center-of-mass energies from the $K \Sigma$ threshold up to 2400 MeV.
- The radiative coupling constant for $t$-channel exchange is $g_{\gamma K^{\pm}K^{*\pm}}=0.413$ and $g_{\gamma K^{0}K^{*0}}=-0.631$, while strong interaction couplings from flavor SU(3) symmetry give $g_{\Sigma NK}=2.692$, $g_{\Sigma NK^{*}}=-4.26$, $\kappa_{\Sigma NK^{*}}=-2.33$, and $g_{\Delta\Sigma K}=7.89$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Ben 2023, Wu 2023) that found evidence for $N(2080)3/2^-$ and $N(2270)3/2^-$ in $\gamma p \to K^{*+} \Sigma^0 / K^{*0} \Sigma^+$ and $\gamma p \to \phi p$ reactions, now testing seven molecular states in the $K\Sigma$ photoproduction channels. The analysis builds upon the methodology used by Wei (2022, 2023) for $\gamma n \to K^+ \Sigma^-$ and $\gamma n \to K^0 \Sigma^0$ reactions, and competes with isobar models (Mart 2019, Clymton 2021) and the Julich-Bonn dynamical coupled-channel approach (Ronchen 2022). The results enable future experimental tests, particularly for the reaction $\gamma p \to K^0 \Sigma^+$, to further constrain the theoretical models and confirm the molecular interpretation of these states.
