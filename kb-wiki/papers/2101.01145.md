# Effect of nuclear magnetization distribution within the Woods-Saxon model: Hyperfine splitting in neutral Tl

> Three models of the nuclear magnetization distribution are applied to predict the hyperfine structure of the hydrogenlike heavy ions and neutral thallium atoms: the uniformly magnetized ball model and single-particle models for the valence nucleon with the uniform distribution and the distribution determined by the Woods-Saxon potential. Results for the hydrogenlike ions are in excellent agreement with previous studies. The application of the Woods-Saxon model is now extended to the neutral systems with the explicit treatment of the electron correlation effects within the relativistic coupled cluster theory using the Dirac-Coulomb Hamiltonian. We estimate the uncertainty for the ratio of magnetic anomalies and numerically confirm its near nuclear-model independence. The ratio is used as a theoretical input to predict the nuclear magnetic moments of short-lived thallium isotopes. We also show that the differential magnetic anomalies are strongly model dependent. The accuracy of the single-particle models significantly surpasses the accuracy of the simplest uniformly magnetized ball model for the prediction of this quantity. Skripnikov [Skripnikov, J. Chem. Phys. 153, 114114 (2020)] has shown that the Bohr-Weisskopf contribution to the magnetic dipole hyperfine structure constant for an atom or a molecule induced by a heavy nucleus can be factorized into the electronic part and the universal nuclear magnetization dependent part. We numerically confirm this factorization for the Woods-Saxon single-particle model with an uncertainty less than 1%.

## Key claim

The Woods-Saxon single-particle model for nuclear magnetization distribution is extended to neutral thallium atoms with explicit electron correlation treatment, and the ratio of magnetic hyperfine anomalies is confirmed to be nearly independent of the nuclear model with uncertainty less than 1%, enabling prediction of nuclear magnetic moments for short-lived thallium isotopes.

## Method

The authors applied three nuclear magnetization distribution models (uniformly magnetized ball, uniform single-particle, and Woods-Saxon single-particle) to calculate the Bohr-Weisskopf contribution to hyperfine structure constants. For neutral thallium, electronic wavefunctions were obtained using the Dirac-Hartree-Fock method followed by relativistic coupled cluster theory with single, double, and perturbative triple amplitudes [CCSD(T)] within the Dirac-Coulomb Hamiltonian, correlating all 81 electrons. The Woods-Saxon nucleon wavefunctions were obtained by solving the radial Schrodinger equation with parameters from Rost (1968), and the BW matrix elements were computed using both exact and approximate expressions for the electron radial integrals.

## Key numbers

- For hydrogen-like Tl-203 and Tl-205 in the 1s state, the BW correction epsilon calculated with the Woods-Saxon model including spin-orbit coupling is 1.89% (using exact K_S and K_L integrals), compared to experimental values of 2.21% and 2.23% respectively.
- The hyperfine structure constant for the 6P_{1/2} state of neutral Tl-205 calculated with the Woods-Saxon model at the CCSD(T) level plus corrections is 21354 MHz, in good agreement with the experimental value of 21310.835(5) MHz.
- The hyperfine structure constant for the 6P_{3/2} state of neutral Tl-205 calculated with the Woods-Saxon model is 239 MHz, compared to the experimental value of 265.0383(1) MHz.
- The ratio of magnetic hyperfine anomalies for Tl-191m relative to Tl-205, k^2[7S_{1/2},6P_{1/2}], calculated with the Woods-Saxon model at the CCSD(T) level is 3.31.
- The differential magnetic hyperfine anomaly for Tl-191m relative to Tl-205, theta[7S_{1/2},6P_{1/2}], calculated with the Woods-Saxon model at the CCSD(T) level is -70 x 10^{-4}, compared to the experimental value of -154(60) x 10^{-4}.
- The differential magnetic hyperfine anomaly for Tl-193m relative to Tl-205, theta[7S_{1/2},6P_{1/2}], calculated with the Woods-Saxon model at the CCSD(T) level is -67 x 10^{-4}, compared to the experimental value of -129(62) x 10^{-4}.
- The factorization of the BW contribution into electronic and nuclear parts is numerically confirmed for the Woods-Saxon model with uncertainty less than 1%.

## Context

This work extends the Woods-Saxon single-particle magnetization model, previously applied to hydrogen-like ions by Shabaev (1997) and Gustavsson (2000), to neutral thallium atoms with explicit electron correlation treatment. It builds on the factorization approach of Skripnikov (2020) and the uniformly magnetized ball model calculations for thallium by Prosnyak (2020). The results enable more reliable extraction of nuclear magnetic moments for short-lived thallium isotopes from experimental hyperfine data, and demonstrate that single-particle models significantly outperform the uniformly magnetized ball model for differential magnetic anomalies when the valence nucleon has nonzero orbital angular momentum.
