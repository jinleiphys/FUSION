# A fluid-dynamic approach to heavy-quark diffusion in the quark-gluon plasma

> A fluid-dynamic approach to the diffusion of heavy quarks in the quark--gluon plasma (QGP) is presented. Specifically, we analyze the Fokker-Planck equation for the momentum transport of heavy quarks from a fluid perspective and use a mapping to second-order fluid-dynamics to determine conductivities and relaxation times governing their spatial diffusion. By investigating the relation between the two approaches, we provide new insights concerning the level of local thermalization of charm and bottom quarks inside the expanding QGP. Our results indicate that a fluid-dynamic description of diffusion is feasible for charm quarks at least for the latest stages of the fireball evolution.

## Key claim

The authors propose that heavy quarks (charm and bottom) in the quark-gluon plasma can be described by a fluid-dynamic approach, but only under the condition that the relaxation time for heavy-quark diffusion is much smaller than the expansion timescale of the fireball; they find this condition is feasible for charm quarks at least for the latest stages of the fireball evolution, while for bottom quarks the hydrodynamization timescale is comparable to or larger than the QGP lifetime.

## Method

The authors map the Fokker-Planck equation for heavy-quark momentum transport to second-order (Israel-Stewart) relativistic fluid dynamics, deriving expressions for the particle-diffusion coefficient and relaxation time in terms of the spatial diffusion coefficient D_s. They then evaluate these transport coefficients numerically using two independent estimates for D_s: one from lattice-QCD simulations in the quenched approximation (Altenkort 2021) and one from fits to ALICE experimental data on D-meson observables (ALICE 2021). Finally, they test the validity of the hydrodynamic description by comparing the heavy-quark relaxation time to the expansion timescale of a QGP undergoing Bjorken flow.

## Key numbers

- The spatial diffusion coefficient constraint from ALICE data: 1.5 < 2π D_s T_pc < 4.5 at the pseudocritical temperature T_pc = 0.155 GeV, corresponding to a thermalization time of about 3-9 fm/c for charm quarks of mass M = 1.5 GeV
- For the Bjorken flow test: initial temperature T_0 = 0.45 GeV and initialization time τ_0 = 0.5 fm/c
- The relaxation time τ_n for charm quarks goes below the expansion timescale τ_exp quite fast when using transport coefficients from fits to experimental data, and after about 5 fm/c when using lattice-QCD estimates
- In the non-relativistic limit, the relaxation time τ_n approaches the inverse of the Fokker-Planck drag coefficient A, with the relation A^{-1} T = (1/2π)(M/T)(2π D_s T)

## Context

This work extends the fluid-dynamic approach to heavy quarks, which are traditionally modeled via transport models based on the Boltzmann equation or its approximations (Prino 2016). It builds on the Statistical Hadronization Model (Andronic 2007, Andronic 2021, Braun-Munzinger 2000) by treating heavy quarks as part of the medium itself rather than as test particles. The approach enables a more economical theoretical description than numerical solutions of the Boltzmann equation and opens the possibility of coupling heavy-quark diffusion to the QGP dynamics through a system of coupled hydrodynamic equations, though the present work treats heavy quarks "on top" of the fluid without back-reaction.
