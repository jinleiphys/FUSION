# Theoretical uncertainties within the Duflo-Zuker mass model

> It is becoming increasingly important to understand the uncertainties of nuclear mass model calculations and their limitations when extrapolating to driplines. In this paper we evaluate the parameter uncertainties the Duflo-Zuker (DZ) shell model mass formulae by fitting to the latest experimental mass compilation AME2012 and analyze the propagation of the uncertainties in binding energy calculations when extrapolated to driplines. The parameter uncertainties and uncertain propagations are evaluated with the help of the covariance matrix thus derived. Large deviations from the extrapolations of AME2012 are seen in superheavy nuclei. A simplified version of the DZ model (DZ19) with much smaller uncertainties than that of DZ33 is proposed. Calculations are compared with results from other mass formulae. Systematics on the uncertainty propagation as well as the positions of the driplines are also presented. The DZ mass formulae are shown to be well defined with good extrapolation properties and rather small uncertainties, even though some of the parameters of the full DZ33 model cannot be fully determined by fitting to available experimental data.

## Key claim

The Duflo-Zuker (DZ) shell model mass formulae are well-defined with good extrapolation properties and rather small uncertainties, even though some parameters of the full DZ33 model cannot be fully determined by fitting to available experimental data, and large deviations from AME2012 extrapolations are seen in superheavy nuclei.

## Method

The parameters of the DZ10, DZ33, and a newly proposed DZ19 mass model were optimized by least-square fitting to the AME2012 experimental mass compilation, using three data sets: experimental data with errors under 100 keV, all experimental data, and all experimental plus extrapolated binding energies. Parameter uncertainties and their propagation into binding energy predictions were evaluated using the covariance matrix derived from the fitting procedure, following the same approach as Gao (2013) for Skyrme HFB calculations.

## Key numbers

- DZ33 model reproduces experimental data within an average error of around 370 keV (parameter sets I and II).
- DZ19 model reproduces experimental data within an average error of around 430 keV, only 70 keV larger than the full DZ33 model.
- DZ10 model reproduces experimental data within an average error of around 537-573 keV (parameter sets I and II).
- For DZ10, most uncertainty propagations are smaller than 200 keV, only getting larger in superheavy nuclei and a few very neutron-rich nuclei.
- The total number of bound even-even nuclei predicted by all three DZ models is around 1850, lower than the 1928-2333 range predicted by non-relativistic mean field approaches (Erler 2012).
- The root-mean-square error between DZ10 and all binding energies from AME2012 is around 1 MeV, mainly due to large deviations in the superheavy region for nuclei with Z around 110 and N around 170.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Duflo-Zuker mass model by fitting to the latest AME2012 compilation, evaluating parameter uncertainties and their propagation, which had been studied for Skyrme HFB approaches by Gao (2013) and Gorchtein (2014). The DZ model predictions are compared with the macroscopic-microscopic liquid drop model of Wang (2011), the finite-range droplet model of Moller (1995), and the HFB-21 Skyrme mean-field model of Goriely (2010), showing large deviations in dripline and superheavy nuclei. The results enable more reliable extrapolations to unknown regions and provide a benchmark for future mass measurements in the superheavy region around A=280, which will critically test the DZ mass model.
