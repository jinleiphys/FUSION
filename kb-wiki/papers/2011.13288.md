# Mass measurements of As, Se and Br nuclei and their implication on the proton-neutron interaction strength towards the N=Z line

> Mass measurements of the $^{69}$As, $^{70,71}$Se and $^{71}$Br isotopes, produced via fragmentation of a $^{124}$Xe primary beam at the FRS at GSI, have been performed with the multiple-reflection time-of-flight mass spectrometer (MR-TOF-MS) of the FRS Ion Catcher with an unprecedented mass resolving power of almost 1,000,000. For the $^{69}$As isotope, this is the first direct mass measurement. A mass uncertainty of 22 keV was achieved with only 10 events. For the $^{70}$Se isotope, a mass uncertainty of 2.6 keV was obtained, corresponding to a relative accuracy of $δ$m/m = 4.0$\times 10^{-8}$, with less than 500 events. The masses of the $^{71}$Se and $^{71}$Br isotopes were measured with an uncertainty of 23 and 16 keV, respectively. Our results for the $^{70,71}$Se and $^{71}$Br isotopes agree with the 2016 Atomic Mass Evaluation, and our result for the $^{69}$As isotope resolves the discrepancy between previous indirect measurements. We measured also the mass of $^{14}$N$^{15}$N$^{40}$Ar (A=69) with a relative accuracy of $δ$m/m = 1.7$\times 10^{-8}$, the highest yet achieved with a MR-TOF-MS. Our results show that the measured restrengthening of the proton-neutron interaction ($δ$V$_{pn}$) for odd-odd nuclei at the N=Z line above Z=29 (recently extended to Z=37) is hardly evident at N-Z=2, and not evident at N-Z=4. Nevertheless, detailed structure of $δ$V$_{pn}$ along the N-Z=2 and N-Z=4 lines, confirmed by our mass measurements, may provide a hint regarding the ongoing $\approx$500 keV discrepancy in the mass value of the $^{70}$Br isotope, which prevents including it in the world average of ${Ft}$-value for superallowed 0$^+\rightarrow$ 0$^+$ $β$ decays. The reported work sets the stage for mass measurements with the FRS Ion Catcher of nuclei at and beyond the N=Z line in the same region of the nuclear chart, including the $^{70}$Br isotope.

## Key claim

The measured restrengthening of the proton-neutron interaction (delta Vpn) for odd-odd nuclei along the N=Z line above Z=29 is hardly evident at N-Z=2 and not evident at N-Z=4, though the detailed structure of delta Vpn along these lines may provide a hint regarding the ongoing approximately 500 keV discrepancy in the mass value of 70Br that prevents its inclusion in the world average Ft-value for superallowed 0+ to 0+ beta decays.

## Method

The authors produced 69As, 70,71Se, and 71Br via fragmentation of a 124Xe primary beam at 982 MeV/u on a Be target at the FRS at GSI, then slowed and thermalized the ions in a cryogenic stopping cell. Mass measurements were performed with the multiple-reflection time-of-flight mass spectrometer (MR-TOF-MS) of the FRS Ion Catcher, using both same-turn and multi-turn calibration with stable molecular calibrants, and fitting the un-binned time-of-flight data with a Hyper-EMG function via weighted maximum likelihood estimation.

## Key numbers

* 69As: first direct mass measurement; mass uncertainty of 22 keV achieved with only 10 events, corresponding to relative accuracy delta m/m = 2.8 x 10^-7
* 70Se: mass uncertainty of 2.6 keV obtained with less than 500 events, corresponding to relative accuracy delta m/m = 4.0 x 10^-8
* 71Se: mass measured with uncertainty of 23 keV (7 counts), relative accuracy delta m/m = 3.4 x 10^-7
* 71Br: mass measured with uncertainty of 16 keV (19 counts), relative accuracy delta m/m = 2.5 x 10^-7
* 14N15N40Ar (A=69): measured with relative accuracy delta m/m = 1.7 x 10^-8, the highest yet achieved with an MR-TOF-MS
* Mass resolving power of almost 1,000,000 FWHM achieved for unstable ions

## Context

This work extends direct mass measurements toward the N=Z line in the region Z=29-37, following prior Penning trap measurements by Savory (2009) and ISOLTRAP by Herfurth (2011), and indirect measurements by Davids (1980). The results confirm trends of the proton-neutron interaction strength for N-Z=2 and N-Z=4 nuclei, and set the stage for future mass measurements of 70Br and heavier nuclei at and beyond the N=Z line using the FRS Ion Catcher, which could resolve the approximately 500 keV discrepancy preventing 70Br from inclusion in the superallowed 0+ to 0+ beta decay Ft-value world average.
