# Two-Photon Exchange Contribution to Proton Form Factors in Time-Like region

> We estimate two-photon exchange contribution to the process $e^+ +e^- \to p + \bar{p}$. The two-photon exchange corrections to double spin polarization observables and form factors in the time-like region are calculated. The corrections are found to be small in magnitude, but with a strong angular dependence at fixed momentum transfer. These two features are the same as those in the space-like region. In the view of experiment, the double spin polarization observable $P_z$ deserves to be considered.

## Key claim

The two-photon exchange corrections to the time-like proton form factors and double spin polarization observables in the process \(e^+ + e^- \to p + \bar{p}\) are small in magnitude but have a strong angular dependence at fixed momentum transfer; the double spin polarization observable \(P_z\) at \(\theta = \pi/2\) is identified as a promising experimental signal for detecting two-photon exchange effects, because the one-photon contribution vanishes at that angle.

## Method

The authors calculated the two-photon exchange amplitude from direct and crossed box diagrams using a simple monopole form factor \(G(q^2) = -\Lambda^2/(q^2 - \Lambda^2)\) with \(\Lambda = 0.84\ \text{GeV}\). Loop integrals were evaluated analytically in terms of Passarino-Veltman functions using FeynCalc and then numerically with LoopTools. The infrared divergence was handled by noting that it cancels with bremsstrahlung contributions, as in the space-like region, and the soft-photon approximation was used for comparison.

## Key numbers

- At \(q^2 = 4\ \text{GeV}^2\), the two-photon exchange contribution to the unpolarized differential cross section \(\delta_{2\gamma}\) is about \(\pm 0.6\%\) at \(\theta = \pi\) (or \(0\)).
- At \(q^2 = 16\ \text{GeV}^2\), \(\delta_{2\gamma}\) reaches nearly \(4\%\).
- The correction to the electric form factor \(\Delta G_E/G\) is about \(2.5\%\) at \(\theta = 0\) (\(\cos\theta = 1\)) and \(-2.5\%\) at \(\theta = \pi\) (\(\cos\theta = -1\)) at \(q^2 = 4\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- The correction to the magnetic form factor \(\Delta G_M/G\) varies from \(1\%\) to \(-1\%\) as \(\theta\) goes from \(0\) to \(\pi\) at \(q^2 = 4\ \text{GeV}^2\).
- The two-photon exchange contribution to the double spin polarization observable \(P_x\) reaches a maximum of about \(4\%\) at \(\theta = \pi\) (or \(0\)) at \(q^2 = 4\ \text{GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the study of two-photon exchange corrections from the space-like region (where they help resolve the discrepancy between Rosenbluth and polarization transfer measurements, as studied by Arrington (2004) and others) to the time-like region. It builds on general crossing-symmetry arguments and prior work by Brodsky (1970) and others on the angular structure of the cross section. The results enable future experimental searches for two-photon exchange effects in \(e^+ e^- \to p \bar{p}\) at high momentum transfer, particularly via the double spin polarization observable \(P_z\) at \(\theta = \pi/2\).
