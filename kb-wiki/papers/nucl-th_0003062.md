# The pion charge radius from charged pion electroproduction

> We analyze a low-energy theorem of threshold pion electroproduction which allows one to determine the charge radius of the pion. We show that at the same order where the radius appears, pion loops induce a correction to the momentum dependence of the longitudinal dipole amplitude $L_{0+}^{(-)}$. This model-independent correction amounts to an increase of the pion charge radius squared from the electroproduction data by about 0.26~fm$^2$. It sheds light on the apparent discrepancy between the recent determination of the pion radius from electroproduction data and the one based on pion-electron scattering.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a model-independent pion-loop correction, which appears at the same chiral order as the pion charge radius itself, modifies the momentum dependence of the longitudinal dipole amplitude in threshold pion electroproduction; this correction increases the extracted squared pion charge radius by about 0.26 fm², making the discrepancy with pion-electron scattering results even larger than previously thought.

## Method

The authors analyze the low-energy theorem for the longitudinal dipole amplitude \(L_{0+}^{(-)}\) in charged pion electroproduction, derived from baryon chiral perturbation theory to third order in the chiral expansion. They expand the amplitude in powers of the photon virtuality \(k^2\) and isolate the term proportional to \(k^2\) that contains the pion vector form factor, identifying a new contribution from triangle and tadpole one-loop diagrams that cannot be separated from the pion radius term by taking the derivative at \(k^2=0\) in the chiral limit.

## Key numbers

- The pion-loop correction to the squared pion charge radius extracted from electroproduction data is 0.266 fm² (derived from the expression \(\frac{3}{32 F_\pi^2} \left( \frac{16}{\pi^2} - 1 \right)\) with \(F_\pi = 92.4\) MeV).
- The conventional pion rms radius from pion-electron scattering is \((0.663 \pm 0.006)\) fm, corresponding to a squared radius of approximately 0.44 fm².
- The modified squared radius expected from electroproduction after including the loop correction is \((0.44 + 0.26)\) fm² = \((0.83\) fm)².
- The Mainz MAMI-II measurement gave a pion rms radius of \((0.74 \pm 0.03)\) fm from electroproduction data.
- The isovector anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon \(\kappa_v = 3.71\), the weak pion decay constant \(F_\pi = 92.4\) MeV, and the pion-nucleon coupling constant \(g_{\pi N} = 13.4\) are used in the amplitude expressions.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory analysis previously applied to the nucleon axial radius (Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner, 1992) to the case of the pion charge radius, showing that similar pion-loop corrections affect the extraction from electroproduction. The results contradict the assumption that Born-term analyses alone are sufficient, and they suggest that the discrepancy between the MAMI electroproduction result (0.74 fm) and the pion-electron scattering value (0.663 fm) from Amendolia et al. (1986) may be partially explained by these loop effects, though higher-order calculations and contributions from higher multipoles are needed for a complete resolution.
