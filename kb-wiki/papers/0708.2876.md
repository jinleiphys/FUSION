# Tkachenko modes as sources of quasiperiodic pulsar spin variations

> We study the long wavelength shear modes (Tkachenko waves) of triangular lattices of singly quantized vortices in neutron star interiors taking into account the mutual friction between the superfluid and the normal fluid and the shear viscosity of the normal fluid. The set of Tkachenko modes that propagate in the plane orthogonal to the spin vector are weakly damped if the coupling between the superfluid and normal fluid is small. In strong coupling, their oscillation frequencies are lower and are undamped for small and moderate shear viscosities. The periods of these modes are consistent with the observed ~100-1000 day variations in spin of PSR 1828-11.

## Key claim

Tkachenko shear modes of the vortex lattice in neutron star interiors can explain the quasiperiodic spin variations of PSR 1828-11, but only if the coupling between the superfluid and normal fluid is weak (independent of shear viscosity) or, in the strong-coupling regime, if the shear viscosity of normal matter is small to moderate.

## Method

The authors used Baym-Chandler superfluid hydrodynamics, which incorporates the vortex lattice deformation tensor, to derive linearized perturbation equations for a rotating superfluid with a triangular vortex array. They included mutual friction forces (parameterized by drag-to-lift ratios) and shear viscosity of the normal fluid, then solved the characteristic equation for plane-wave perturbations to obtain the dispersion relations for Tkachenko and inertial modes as functions of wave vector, mutual friction coefficients, and shear viscosity.

## Key numbers

- For PSR 1828-11 (rotation frequency 15.51 Hz), the fundamental Tkachenko mode period is 331 days for a superfluid shell width of 10 km, and 256 days for a shell width of 7.7 km (matching the shortest observed periodicity).
- The shear modulus of the triangular vortex lattice is μ_S = ρ_S ħΩ / (8 m_N).
- The shear viscosity of the electron fluid in neutron star cores at densities 2–3 × 10^14 g cm^−3 and temperature ~10^8 K lies between 8 × 10^17 and 40 × 10^17 dyn s cm^−2.
- For strong coupling (large drag-to-lift ratio ζ) and zero shear viscosity, the Tkachenko mode real frequency is reduced by about 25% relative to the non-dissipative value.
- The Tkachenko mode real part vanishes for shear viscosity η ~ 10^19 dyn s cm^−2 in the strong-coupling limit with d=0.
- The inertial mode real part vanishes at η ~ 5 × 10^19 dyn s cm^−2 for d=10^−14.

## Context

This work extends Ruderman (1970), who first suggested that Tkachenko modes in neutron stars could have periods of several hundred days and might explain timing residuals in the Crab pulsar. The authors go beyond Ruderman's analysis by including mutual friction and shear viscosity, which were previously neglected. The results are consistent with the observed 256-day and 511-day periodicities in PSR 1828-11 (Stairs 2000) and provide an alternative to precession models (Shaham 1977, Sedrakian 1998, Link 2003) that are incompatible with strongly coupled superfluids. The work enables future studies of how microscopic superfluid parameters (mutual friction coefficients, shear viscosity) can be constrained by pulsar timing observations.
