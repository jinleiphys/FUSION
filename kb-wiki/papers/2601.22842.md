# Multiquark bound states and resonances

> We review the chromoelectric and chromomagnetic mechanisms that tentatively lead to stable or metastable multiquark configurations. An alternative interpretation of the dynamics is the quark interchange between hadrons, as illustrated in the case of the fully-charm systems.

# Multiquark bound states and resonances

## Key claim

The constituent quark model predicts very few bound multiquark states and a limited number of resonances above their lowest dissociation threshold into two hadrons, contrary to claims that other approaches predict too many multiquarks; special circumstances such as unequal masses, spin-dependent interactions, or three-body forces are required to produce interesting states.

## Method

The authors review the chromoelectric (CE) and chromomagnetic (CM) mechanisms for multiquark binding using Hamiltonians with pairwise color-octet-exchange interactions, including operators of increasing refinement such as \( C_1 = \sum_{i<j} \tilde{\lambda}_i \cdot \tilde{\lambda}_j \, \boldsymbol{\sigma}_i \cdot \boldsymbol{\sigma}_j \), \( C_2 = \sum_{i<j} (\tilde{\lambda}_i \cdot \tilde{\lambda}_j \, \boldsymbol{\sigma}_i \cdot \boldsymbol{\sigma}_j)/(m_i m_j) \), and \( C_3 = \sum_{i<j} a_{ij} \tilde{\lambda}_i \cdot \tilde{\lambda}_j \, \boldsymbol{\sigma}_i \cdot \boldsymbol{\sigma}_j \) with coefficients borrowed from ordinary hadrons. For the fully-charmed \( cc\bar c\bar c \) system, they introduce a quark-exchange formalism using two non-orthogonal color-singlet channels with Gaussian expansions and frozen intra-meson range parameters.

## Key numbers

- For the \( QQ\bar q\bar q \) configuration governed by the chromoelectric Hamiltonian with mass ratio \( M/m \), a bound state below the \( Q\bar q + Q\bar q \) threshold exists if \( M/m \) is large enough (no explicit critical ratio given).
- For the \( cc\bar c\bar c \) system studied via quark exchange with real scaling, promising plateaus appear near 7 GeV.
- The hidden-charm pentaquark \( \bar c c qqq \) with isospin \( I = 3/2 \) is predicted to be stable in constituent models.

## Context

This review extends the line of work on multiquark states using constituent quark models, building on early studies by Jaffe (2004) on chromomagnetism and the H dibaryon, and by Ader (1981), Zouzou (1986), and Heller (1986) on chromoelectric binding in \( QQ\bar q\bar q \) systems. It addresses discrepancies with lattice QCD results for fully-heavy dibaryons from Junnarkar (2019) and with experimental observations of the \( T_{cc}^+ \) and hidden-charm pentaquarks from the Particle Data Group (2024). The quark-exchange formulation provides an alternative interpretation to the molecular picture for near-threshold resonances, as recently stressed by Huang (2024) for \( J/\psi J/\psi \) resonances.
