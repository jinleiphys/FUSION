# Cluster-breaking and reconfiguration effects in $_Λ^{12}\rm{B}$ hypernucleus

> We investigate the cluster-breaking effect and spatial distribution of negative-parity states in the $_Λ^{12}\rm{B}$ hypernucleus using the Hyper-Brink model with cluster-breaking(CB-Hyper-Brink) optimized via Control Neural Network (Ctrl.NN). The results demonstrate that the inclusion of cluster-breaking is essential for accurately reproducing the observed low-lying energy levels and for making reliable predictions of the Hoyle-analog state 1-4 in $_Λ^{12}\rm{B}$. Cluster-breaking manifests as strong spin-orbit correlations and the dissolution of ideal cluster configurations, as revealed by the analysis of one-body spin-orbit operator expectation values and the spatial overlap with projected cluster bases. The interplay between short-range repulsion and intermediate-range attraction in the Lambda N interaction induces the cluster reconfiguration effect, which is characterized by the coexistence of Lambda-alpha and Lambda-triton correlations; this reconfiguration effect leads to a modest stabilization and shrinkage of cluster structures. The variation in electric quadrupole transition strengths, B(E2), between the ground and Hoyle-analog states serves as a sensitive probe for the degree of cluster-breaking, providing direct evidence for its physical relevance. These findings highlight the crucial role of cluster-breaking in characterizing the hypernuclear structure and offer a comprehensive framework for understanding the interplay between clustering and shell-model dynamics in hypernuclei.

## Key claim

The inclusion of cluster-breaking effects in the Hyper-Brink model is essential for accurately reproducing the observed low-lying energy levels of the $_\Lambda^{12}\rm{B}$ hypernucleus and for making reliable predictions of the Hoyle-analog state $1_4^-$, with the caveat that the degree of cluster-breaking in hypernuclear states is overall comparable to that in the core $^{11}\rm{B}$ nucleus rather than being dramatically enhanced.

## Method

The authors employed the Hyper-Brink model with cluster-breaking (CB-Hyper-Brink), treating $_\Lambda^{12}\rm{B}$ as a four-body system of $\alpha+\alpha+t+\Lambda$ with complex Gaussian center parameters to represent cluster dissolution. The wave function was optimized using a Control Neural Network (Ctrl.NN) with $K$- and parity-projection during energy variation, and the Hamiltonian included Volkov No.2 nucleon-nucleon central force, G3RS spin-orbit interaction, and ESC14 $\Lambda N$ interaction with many-body effects. The expectation values of the one-body spin-orbit operator and spatial overlaps with projected cluster bases were analyzed to quantify cluster-breaking.

## Key numbers

- The Hoyle-analog state $3/2_3^-$ in $^{11}\rm{B}$ is reproduced about 1 MeV below the $\alpha+\alpha+t$ threshold
- Cluster-breaking provides an additional binding energy of about 3 MeV for the low-lying states of $^{11}\rm{B}$
- Root-mean-square matter radii for $^{11}\rm{B}$ states: $3/2^-_1$ = 2.30 fm, $1/2^-_1$ = 2.44 fm, $3/2^-_2$ = 2.50 fm, $3/2^-_3$ = 2.78 fm (present calculation)
- The experimental rms matter radius for the $3/2^-_1$ state of $^{11}\rm{B}$ is 2.09 $\pm$ 0.12 fm
- Expectation values of the one-body spin-orbit operator for $^{11}\rm{B}$: $3/2^-_1$ = 1.92 (nucleons), $1/2^-_1$ = 0.75, $3/2^-_2$ = 0.22, $3/2^-_3$ = 0.99
- Expectation values for $_\Lambda^{12}\rm{B}$: $1_1^-$ = 1.74 (nucleons), $1_2^-$ = 0.87, $1_3^-$ = 0.25, $1_4^-$ = 0.86
- The nucleon width parameter $\nu_N$ = 0.235 fm$^{-2}$, and the Fermi momentum parameter $k_F$ = 1.06 fm$^{-1}$ for the $\Lambda N$ interaction

## Context

This work extends previous studies on cluster-shell competition in hypernuclei, particularly building on findings for $^{13}_\Lambda\rm{C}$ by Itagaki (2023) which showed that the $\Lambda$ particle enhances shell-model characteristics through cluster dissolution due to spin-orbit interaction. The results are compared with GCM-Brink calculations by Zhou (2018) and AMD calculations by Suhara (2012) for $^{11}\rm{B}$ and $_\Lambda^{12}\rm{B}$. The study enables future investigations of how the interplay between short-range repulsion and intermediate-range attraction in the $\Lambda N$ interaction drives cluster reconfiguration, characterized by the coexistence of $\Lambda$-$\alpha$ and $\Lambda$-triton correlations, and establishes electric quadrupole transition strengths $B(E2)$ as a sensitive probe for the degree of cluster-breaking.
