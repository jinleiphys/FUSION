# Properties of D-mesons in nuclear matter within a self-consistent coupled-channel approach

> The spectral density of the $D$-meson in the nuclear environment is studied within a self-consistent coupled-channel approach assuming a separable potential for the bare meson-baryon interaction. The $DN$ interaction, described through a G-matrix, generates dynamically the $Λ_c$ (2593) resonance. This resonance is the charm counterpart of the $Λ$ (1405) resonance generated from the s-wave $\bar{K}N$ interaction in the I=0 channel. The medium modification of the D-meson spectral density due to the Pauli blocking of intermediate states as well as due to the dressing of the D-mesons, nucleons and pions is investigated. We observe that the inclusion of coupled-channel effects and the self-consistent dressing of the $D$-meson results in an overall reduction of the in-medium $D$-meson changes compared to previous work which neglect those effects.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including coupled-channel effects and self-consistent dressing of the D-meson, nucleons, and pions results in an overall reduction of in-medium D-meson changes compared to previous work that neglected these effects, with the D-meson potential at nuclear matter density ranging from slightly repulsive to moderately attractive depending on the parameter set.

## Method

The authors employed a self-consistent coupled-channel G-matrix approach assuming a separable potential for the bare meson-baryon interaction, solving for the in-medium s-wave DN interaction in isospin I=0 and I=1 channels. The calculation dynamically generates the Λc(2593) resonance and incorporates medium effects including Pauli blocking of intermediate nucleon states, dressing of D-mesons, nucleons, and pions, and the decay width of the Σc baryon. The D-meson single-particle potential is obtained self-consistently in the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation, from which the spectral density is derived.

## Key numbers

- The Λc(2593) resonance is reproduced at a position of 2593.9 ± 2 MeV with a width Γ = 3.6^{+2.0}_{-1.3} MeV, for coupling constants and cutoffs in the range between 0.8 and 1.4 GeV.
- At nuclear matter saturation density ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³, the D-meson potential at zero momentum ranges from 8.6 MeV (repulsive) for Λ = 0.8 GeV to -11.2 MeV (attractive) for Λ = 1.4 GeV when only the D-meson is dressed.
- For the full self-consistent calculation including nucleon and pion dressing, the D-meson potential at ρ₀ ranges from 2.6 MeV for Λ = 0.8 GeV to -12.3 MeV for Λ = 1.4 GeV.
- The DN threshold is at 2.806 GeV, and the energy region of integration for the D-meson potential at ρ₀ lies around 2.80-2.84 GeV for the case with only D-meson dressing, and around 2.71-2.78 GeV for the full calculation.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent coupled-channel methodology previously applied to antikaons in nuclear matter (Tolos 2001, Tolos 2002) to the charm sector, where the Λc(2593) resonance is the charm counterpart of the Λ(1405) resonance. The results contradict earlier predictions from QCD sum rule approaches (Arata, Weise) and the quark-meson coupling model (QMC), which found D-meson mass drops of 50-60 MeV at nuclear matter density, as well as a chiral effective model (dmeson) that predicted drops of about 200 MeV. The present calculation shows that coupled-channel effects drastically reduce these in-medium modifications, and the spectral density features could be probed experimentally with the PANDA experiment at GSI.
