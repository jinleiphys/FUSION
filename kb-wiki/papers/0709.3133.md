# The Renormalized Tensor Interaction in a Nucleus

> We show several examples were the tensor interaction of the lowest order G matrix in a nucleus is too strong. The examples include the quadrupole moment of $^{6}$Li, the isosplitting of the lowest 0$^{-}$ states in $^{16}$O, the near vanishing Gamow-Teller matrix element in the weak decay of the J=0 T=1 state of $^{14}$O to the J=1 T=0 ground state of $^{14}$N, and the magnitude of the deformation of $^{12}$C. It would appear that we could get better results by decreasing the tensor interaction strength by about a factor of two. We then examine the simple estimates of Gerry Brown concerning second order tensor effects. We note that for the triplet even channel the combination of first and second order tensor does indeed yield an effective weaker tensor interaction and helps to get better agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The bare G-matrix tensor interaction in the isospin T=0 channel is too strong for several nuclear observables, and reducing its strength by about a factor of two yields better agreement with experiment; however, the authors note that alternate explanations (such as a stronger spin-orbit interaction) could produce similar results in some cases, so the need for a weaker tensor interaction is not uniquely determined by any single observable.

## Method

The authors use a simple interaction V = V_c + x V_s.o. + y V_t, where x and y are scaling factors for the spin-orbit and tensor components, and vary these parameters to study the tensor interaction's effects in shell-model calculations across multiple model spaces (0 to 8 ℏω). They calculate static quadrupole moments, isospin splittings, Gamow-Teller matrix elements, and single-particle energy splittings for nuclei including ⁶Li, ¹⁶O, ¹⁴O/¹⁴N, and ¹²C, then compare with experimental data. They also evaluate second-order tensor contributions using the Hamada-Johnston interaction with a Moszkowski-Scott cutoff radius of 1 fm and an effective energy parameter e_eff = 250 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole moment of ⁶Li 1⁺ state: with full tensor strength (y=1.0) in the largest model space (0,2,4,6,8 ℏω) gives Q = -0.6301 e fm², while the experimental value is Q = -0.083 e fm²; with y=0.5 the same space gives Q = -0.3052 e fm²
- 0⁻ isospin splitting in ¹⁶O: experimental ΔE = 1.845 MeV; with full interaction (x=1, y=1) in the 1p-1h+3p-3h+2p-2h space gives ΔE = 1.919 MeV; in the smallest 1p-1h space the same interaction gives ΔE = 2.932 MeV
- Gamow-Teller matrix element A(GT) for ¹⁴O → ¹⁴N vanishes at y = 0.49 (about half the bare tensor strength); with y=0 the value is -1.19, with y=1.0 it is 1.38
- Single-particle splitting ε(p₁/₂) - ε(p₃/₂) in ¹²C: with x=1, y=1 gives -0.25 MeV; with x=1, y=0 gives +3.84 MeV; with x=0, y=1 gives -4.75 MeV
- M1 matrix element for J=1⁺ T=1 state in ¹²C: with y=1, x=0 gives A(M1)=0.03; with y=1, x=2.0 gives A(M1)=1.86; with x=1, y=0 gives A(M1)=1.24; with x=1, y=2.0 gives A(M1)=0.79

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Zamick and collaborators (2005, 1991) on tensor interaction effects in p-shell nuclei, and engages with the work of Gerry Brown on second-order tensor contributions. The authors show that the bare G-matrix tensor interaction (e.g., Bonn A) is too strong in the T=0 channel, consistent with findings by Otsuka et al. (2000s) who also noted a weaker tensor interaction is needed in T=0 but not T=1. The results enable improved understanding of how renormalization effects from higher shells (implicit in successful Cohen-Kurath shell-model calculations) produce an effective weaker tensor interaction, and are relevant to the problem of nuclear pairing in the T=0 channel as studied by Moya (2000s).
