# Kinetic equilibration from a radiative transport

> Kinetic equilibration during the early stage of a relativistic heavy ion collision is studied using a radiative transport model. Thermalization is found to dominate over expansion with medium regulated cross sections. Pressure anisotropy shows an approximate alpha_s scaling when radiative processes are included. It approaches an asymptotic time evolution on a time scale of 1 to 2 fm/c. Energy density is also found to approach an asymptotic time evolution that decreases slower than the ideal hydro evolution. These observations indicate that viscosity is important during the early longitudinal expansion phase of a relativistic heavy ion collision.

# Kinetic equilibration from a radiative transport

## Key claim

Kinetic equilibration during the early longitudinal expansion phase of a relativistic heavy ion collision can dominate over expansion when medium-regulated cross sections and particle-number-changing processes (2-to-3 and 3-to-2) are included, but the energy density evolution remains slower than ideal hydrodynamics, indicating that viscous corrections are important even on time scales of 1 to 2 fm/c.

## Method

The study uses a radiative transport model that extends the AMPT framework by replacing fixed parton cross sections with pQCD cross sections regulated by a medium-generated Debye screening mass, and by adding 2-to-3 and 3-to-2 processes with the 2-to-3 cross section set to 50% of the 2-to-2 cross section. The evolution of the central cell in central heavy ion collisions is simulated for proper times from 0 to 2 fm/c, starting from local thermal initial conditions at initial temperatures of 0.5 GeV and 1 GeV, with a space-time rapidity density of 1000 and a transverse disk radius of 5 fm. Pressure anisotropy (longitudinal-to-transverse pressure ratio) and energy density are tracked as functions of proper time for different values of the strong coupling constant α_s.

## Key numbers

- Pressure anisotropy approaches an asymptotic time evolution on a time scale of 1 to 2 fm/c.
- The 2-to-2 cross section is given by σ_22 = 9π α_s^2 / (2 μ^2), where μ is the Debye screening mass.
- The 2-to-3 cross section is taken as 50% of the 2-to-2 cross section.
- The 3-to-2 reaction integral is related to the 2-to-3 cross section by I_32 = 192 π^2 σ_23.
- Initial proper time is τ = 0.5 fm/c; initial temperatures are T_0 = 0.5 GeV and 1.0 GeV.
- Space-time rapidity density is dN/dη_s = 1000; transverse disk radius is 5 fm.
- For free streaming from a transverse initial condition, energy density decreases as 1/τ.
- For an isotropic initial condition with strong coupling, energy density follows ε ∝ 1/τ^{1.25}.
- Ideal hydrodynamics would give ε ∝ 1/τ^{1.33}, so the observed 1/τ^{1.25} is slower.

## Context

This work extends earlier kinetic equilibration studies using the AMPT model (Zhang 2008) which used fixed cross sections and found only partial thermalization. By introducing medium-regulated cross sections and inelastic processes, the present study shows an approximate α_s scaling for pressure anisotropy evolution, replacing the α_s T_0 scaling found when only elastic collisions were included (Zhang 2008). The results indicate that viscous hydrodynamics (Muronga 2001, Dusling 2007, Luzum 2008, Song 2007-2008) is necessary during the early longitudinal expansion phase, and the relaxation time of 1-2 fm/c provides a benchmark for more sophisticated models of viscous effects and their impact on experimental observables.
