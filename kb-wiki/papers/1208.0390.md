# Magnetic catalysis of a charged Bose-Einstein condensate

> We study the condensation phenomenon for a system of charged bosons in the presence of an external magnetic field. We show that condensation happens for a definite critical temperature instead of through a diffuse phase transition. The essential ingredient, overlooked in previous analyses and accounted for in this work, is the treatment of the plasma screening effects by means of resummation. We compute the critical temperature, for the case in which the condensate is made of charged pions and for typical densities found in compact astrophysical objects, for small and large values of the magnetic field. We show that the magnetic field catalyzes the onset of condensation at very small and at large values of the magnetic field, and that for intermediate values, the critical temperature for condensation is lower than for the zero magnetic field case.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a charged Bose-Einstein condensate in an external magnetic field does exhibit a well-defined critical temperature for condensation, contrary to previous claims of a diffuse phase transition, provided that plasma screening effects are included via resummation of ring diagrams.

## Method

The authors model a charged scalar field with quartic self-interactions and a finite chemical potential in a uniform external magnetic field, using the Schwinger proper time method to compute the one-loop grand potential and self-energy. They then apply a self-consistent resummation of ring diagrams to account for plasma screening effects, solving for the resummed self-energy near the phase transition in the low-temperature limit for small and large magnetic field regimes. The critical temperature is obtained by requiring that the effective mass squared vanishes while maintaining a finite charge density.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for zero magnetic field: \( T_0 = \frac{2\pi}{m}\left(\frac{\rho}{\zeta(3/2)}\right)^{2/3} \), where \( m \) is the pion mass and \( \rho \) is the charge density.
- The effective mass in the lowest Landau level is \( m_B \equiv \sqrt{m^2 + qB} \), where \( q \) is the boson charge and \( B \) is the magnetic field strength.
- The scaled temperature is defined as \( \tau \equiv T/(2\pi m_B) \) and the scaled magnetic field as \( \gamma \equiv qB/(m_B T) \).
- The resummed self-energy near the phase transition satisfies \( \bar\Pi \approx \frac{\lambda}{2} m_B^2 \tau^{3/2} \left[ \gamma \sqrt{\frac{2\pi m_B T}{\bar\Pi}} + \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{n^{3/2}} \frac{n\gamma}{e^{n\gamma}-1} \right] \).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of pion condensation in neutron stars by Baym (1970s) and others, and addresses the long-standing result by Schafroth (1955) that a nonrelativistic charged Bose gas does not condense in a magnetic field. The authors show that including plasma screening effects resolves the infrared divergences that previously led to claims of a diffuse phase transition by Perez (2000s). The results enable more realistic calculations of pion condensate formation in compact astrophysical objects such as neutron stars, where large magnetic fields are present.
