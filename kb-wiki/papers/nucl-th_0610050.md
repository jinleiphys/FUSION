# A QMD description of the interaction of ion beams with matter

> Heavy-ion collisions can be simulated by means of comprehensive approaches, to include the many different reaction mechanisms which may contribute. QMD models and their relativistic extensions are examples of these approaches based on Monte Carlo techniques. In this paper are shown some results obtained by coupling a new QMD code, which describes the fast stage of ion-ion collisions, to the evaporation /fission/Fermi break-up and photon de-excitation routines present in the FLUKA multipurpose Monte Carlo transport and interaction code. In particular, we compare the predicted neutron spectra to available experimental data from thin and thick target irradiations. We show also some predictions of particle and charged fragment fluences for the interaction of C and Fe ions with a thick PMMA target, which may be useful to assess the risk of side-effects in the hadron therapy of tumours.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that coupling a new non-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) code with the FLUKA de-excitation and transport modules reproduces experimental neutron spectra and fragment yields from heavy-ion collisions, with the caveat that the QMD code uses an approximate treatment of fermionic nature via Pauli blocking rather than full antisymmetrization.

## Method

The authors developed a non-relativistic QMD code that evolves nucleon wave-functions in phase-space using a Hamiltonian with Skyrme two-body and three-body interactions, a symmetry term, a surface term, and Coulomb effects. This QMD code simulates the fast stage (approximately 10^-22 s) of ion-ion collisions, after which the excited pre-fragments are passed to the FLUKA de-excitation module which handles evaporation, fission, Fermi break-up, and gamma emission. For thick target applications, the QMD code was interfaced with the full FLUKA transport code to track secondary particles and fragments through matter including ionization energy loss, bremsstrahlung, and multiple scattering.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density used in the Hamiltonian: rho_0 approximately 0.17 fm^-3
- Neutron double-differential spectra reproduced for 95 MeV/A Ar ions on a thin Al target, with calculations matching experimental data especially at intermediate emission angles (30-80 degrees)
- Neutron spectra reproduced for 135 MeV/A Ne ions on a thin Al target
- Charged fragment yields reproduced for symmetric Ca + Ca collisions at 35 MeV/A bombarding energy, with experimental constraints requiring charged fragment multiplicity greater than 5 or 10 depending on the simulation stage
- Neutron spectra reproduced for 400 MeV/A Ar beam on a thick (5.5 cm) Al target that stops the beam completely
- Spatial distributions of neutron, proton, and heavy fragment fluences calculated for 400 MeV/A C beam in a 10 cm radius, 10 cm deep PMMA cylinder
- Dose distribution for 400 MeV/A Fe beam in PMMA cylinder compared between QMD+FLUKA and RQMD+FLUKA, with results practically coinciding up to the Bragg peak

## Context

This work extends the QMD approach of Aichelin (1991) by coupling it to the established FLUKA transport code, which has been used in hadron therapy studies by Battistoni and colleagues (2004, 2005, 2006). The authors note that more advanced approaches like Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD) and Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics (AMD) exist but are too computationally expensive for systematic thick-target applications. The results enable improved predictions of particle and fragment fluences for hadron therapy treatment planning and space radiation protection, with further validation of heavy fragment distributions currently underway.
