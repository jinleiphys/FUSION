# Dihadron azimuthal asymmetry and light-quark dipole moments at the Electron-Ion Collider

> We propose a novel method to probe light-quark dipole moments by examining the azimuthal asymmetries between a collinear pair of hadrons in semi-inclusive deep inelastic lepton scattering off an unpolarized proton target at the Electron-Ion Collider. These asymmetries provide a means to observe transversely polarized quarks, which arise exclusively from the interference between the dipole and the Standard Model interactions, thereby depending linearly on the dipole couplings. We demonstrate that this novel approach can enhance current constraints on light-quark dipole operators by an order of magnitude, free from contamination of other new physics effects. Furthermore, it allows for a simultaneous determination of both the real and imaginary parts of the dipole couplings, offering a new avenue for investigating potential $CP$-violating effects at high energies.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that azimuthal asymmetries in dihadron production at the Electron-Ion Collider can probe light-quark electroweak dipole moments at leading order in the effective field theory expansion (order 1/Λ²), with the caveat that the effect depends linearly on the dipole couplings only through interference with Standard Model amplitudes, and requires no polarized proton beam.

## Method

The authors compute the semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering cross section for e⁻ + p → e⁻ + π⁺ + π⁻ + X in the Breit frame, using leading-order collinear factorization with dihadron fragmentation functions (DiFFs) from the JAM global analysis. They extract the transverse spin of the final-state quark from the off-diagonal elements of the quark spin density matrix, which arise from interference between Standard Model and dipole operator amplitudes. The azimuthal asymmetry of the dihadron pair relative to the lepton scattering plane is expressed in terms of up-down (A_UD) and left-right (A_LR) asymmetries, which are linearly proportional to the real and imaginary parts of the dipole couplings respectively.

## Key numbers

- Kinematic cuts: x ∈ [0.03, 0.9], y ∈ [0.3, 0.95], z ∈ [0.19, 0.99], M_h ∈ [0.28, 2.05] GeV, with Q > 10 GeV
- Collider energy √s = 105 GeV with integrated luminosity ℒ = 1000 fb⁻¹
- Expected 68% confidence level bounds on photon dipole couplings Γ_γ^(u,d) of order 10⁻²
- Expected 68% confidence level bounds on Z dipole couplings Γ_Z^(u,d) of order 10⁻¹
- Electron longitudinal polarization λ_ℓ = 0.7 for photon dipole sensitivity; λ_ℓ = 0 for Z dipole sensitivity
- Low-energy constraints give |Im Γ_γ^q| ~ 10⁻⁶ and |Re Γ_γ^q| ~ 0.1 from nucleon dipole moments (Liu 2017, Particle Data Group 2022)

## Context

This work extends the strategy of using transverse spin asymmetries to probe dipole moments, previously applied to leptons at e⁺e⁻ colliders (Wen 2023), to light quarks at the EIC by exploiting dihadron fragmentation functions. It improves upon constraints from LHC Drell-Yan processes (Boughezal 2021), which are sensitive only at order 1/Λ⁴ and cannot separate dipole operators from other new physics. The method enables simultaneous determination of real and imaginary parts of dipole couplings, offering a new probe of CP violation at high energies, and connects hadron structure studies (using DiFFs from JAM global fits by Cocuzza 2023, Pitonyak 2023) to electroweak precision physics.
