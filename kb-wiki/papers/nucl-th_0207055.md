# Asymptotic Freedom for Non-Relativistic Confinement

> Some aspects of asymptotic freedom are discussed in the context of a simple two-particle non-relativisitic confining potential model. In this model asymptotic freedom follows from the similarity of the free-particle and bound state radial wave functions at small distances and for the same angular momentum and the same large energy. This similarity, which can be understood using simple quantum mechanical arguments, can be used to show that the exact response function approaches that obtained when final state interactions are ignored. A method of calculating corrections to this limit is given and explicit examples are given for the case of the harmonic oscillator.

## Key claim

Asymptotic freedom in a non-relativistic confining potential model follows from the similarity of bound-state and free-particle radial wave functions at small distances for the same angular momentum and the same large energy, but this holds only for potentials that approach zero smoothly as r → 0 and requires WKB arguments to match the relative normalizations of the discrete sum and the continuum integral.

## Method

The author considers a two-particle system of equal mass m in a spherically symmetric confining potential V(r), initially in its ground state, which makes a transition to an excited state after being hit by a scalar photon transferring momentum q. The exact response function is computed using bound-state radial wave functions for discrete final states, while the asymptotic-freedom approximation uses free-particle Ricatti-Bessel functions for the same energy and angular momentum. The WKB approximation is used to relate the normalization of bound-state wave functions to the level spacing, and an integral equation (Calogero equation) is given for calculating corrections to the proportionality between bound and free wave functions. Explicit analytic examples are worked out for the harmonic oscillator potential.

## Key numbers

- Reduced mass μ = m/2 for the two-particle system.
- For the harmonic oscillator, energy levels are E_{n_r,l} = ω(2n_r + l + 3/2), with ω = √(K/μ) and α = μω.
- The ground state form factor for the oscillator is S(q) = e^{-p²}, where p = q/(2√α).
- For the oscillator, the radial integral for bound states is r_{n_r,l}(q) = √[2^{n_r+l}/(n_r! (2n_r+2l+1)!!)] (p/2)^{2n_r+l} e^{-p²/4}.
- The free-particle radial integral for the oscillator is r_l(k,q) = 2√2 κ (π/α)^{1/4} e^{-(κ²+p²)/2} i_l(κp), where κ = k/√α.
- For the oscillator, the summed structure function for discrete states is F_n(q) = [e₁²+e₂²+2e₁e₂(-1)^n] (1/n!)(p²/2)^n e^{-p²/2}.
- The summed free structure function for the oscillator is F(k,q) = [e₁²+e₂²] 2√(π/α) (κ/p)[e^{-(κ-p)²} - e^{-(κ+p)²}] + 2e₁e₂ 8√(π/α) κ² e^{-p²-κ²}.
- For an r^n potential, the leading correction to the proportionality decreases as 1/k_{n_r,l}^{n+2}.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic confining-potential approach to quark-hadron duality used by Close and Isgur (1990s) and earlier by Greenberg (1970s), who investigated scaling limits in terms of the Bjorken scaling variable. The paper provides a concrete demonstration of how asymptotic freedom emerges from the correspondence principle and WKB normalization, complementing the more formal treatment of corrections by Rinat et al. (1993). The results enable further study of more general potentials such as the Coulomb-plus-linear confining potential (Cornell model) and extensions to semi-relativistic calculations and many-body systems including the nucleon and nuclei, as discussed by West (1970s).
