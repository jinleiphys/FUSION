# The Hagedorn temperature Revisited

> The Hagedorn temperature, T_H is determined from the number of hadronic resonances including all mesons and baryons. This leads to a stable result T_H = 174 MeV consistent with the critical and the chemical freeze-out temperatures at zero chemical potential. We use this result to calculate the speed of sound and other thermodynamic quantities in the resonance hadron gas model for a wide range of baryon chemical potentials following the chemical freeze-out curve. We compare some of our results to those obtained previously in other papers.

## Key claim

The Hagedorn temperature is determined to be \(T_H = 174\) MeV by fitting the cumulative number of all hadronic resonances (mesons and baryons together) without splitting the spectrum, yielding a stable result consistent with the critical temperature from lattice QCD and the chemical freeze-out temperature at zero baryon chemical potential. The authors hedge that previous analyses splitting mesons and baryons gave a wide spread of values from 141 MeV to 340 MeV, and that the fit is reliable only up to about 3 GeV due to sparse resonance data above that mass.

## Method

The authors fit the cumulative number of all known hadronic resonances (250 baryons and antibaryons, 348 mesons and antimesons, including charm and bottom states) from the Particle Data Group to the Hagedorn spectrum form \(\rho_h(m) = c / (m^2 + m_0^2)^{5/4} \exp(m/T_H)\) with \(m_0 = 0.5\) GeV fixed. They then use this fitted spectrum in an Extended Hadron Resonance Gas Model (EHRGM) that adds the exponentially rising Hagedorn density above a cutoff mass \(\lambda\) to the known discrete resonances, and compute thermodynamic quantities including the speed of sound along the chemical freeze-out curve for non-zero baryon and strangeness chemical potentials, imposing strangeness neutrality and constant entropy per particle.

## Key numbers

- Hagedorn temperature: \(T_H = 174 \pm 11\) MeV (from fit to cumulative hadron spectrum up to 3 GeV)
- Normalization constant: \(c = 0.16 \pm 0.02\) GeV\(^{3/2}\) (from same fit)
- Fixed mass parameter: \(m_0 = 0.5\) GeV
- Number of baryons and antibaryons used: 250
- Number of mesons and antimesons used: 348
- Maximum mass of hadrons included in the cumulative distribution: 11.019 GeV
- Previous spread of Hagedorn temperature values in literature: 141 MeV to 340 MeV (depending on meson-only or baryon-only fits)

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses by Cleymans and collaborators (Cleymans et al., prior years) and by others such as Noronha (2009) and Greiner (2009) who used Hagedorn states to explain chemical equilibration and compare with lattice QCD. The authors resolve the wide spread of Hagedorn temperature values reported in recent papers (e.g., from groups in India and Poland, 2009-2010) by fitting all resonances together rather than splitting mesons and baryons. The result enables calculation of the speed of sound and other thermodynamic quantities in the extended hadron resonance gas model for non-zero chemical potentials, showing a sharp dip in the speed of sound near the critical temperature as a signature of the phase transition to quark-gluon plasma, which can be compared with lattice QCD results from Fodor (2004) and the HotQCD collaboration (2010).
