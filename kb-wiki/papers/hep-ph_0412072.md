# Formation of η'(958)-mesic nuclei and axial U_A(1) anomaly at finite density

> We discuss the possibility to produce the bound states of the $η'(958)$ meson in nuclei theoretically. We calculate the formation cross sections of the $η'$ bound states with the Green function method for ($γ$,p) reaction and discuss the experimental feasibility at photon facilities like SPring-8. We conclude that we can expect to observe resonance peaks in ($γ$,p) spectra for the formation of $η'$ bound states and we can deduce new information on $η'$ properties at finite density. These observations are believed to be essential to know the possible mass shift of $η'$ and deduce new information of the effective restoration of the chiral $U_A(1)$ anomaly in the nuclear medium.

## Key claim

The authors claim that resonance peaks from the formation of eta'(958)-mesic nuclei should be observable in (gamma,p) spectra at photon facilities like SPring-8, and that these observations can provide new information on the eta' mass shift at finite density and the effective restoration of the chiral U_A(1) anomaly in the nuclear medium. The key hedge is that the existence and clarity of these peaks depend on the sign and strength of the eta'-nucleus optical potential, which is treated as a parameter with a reasonable range of V_0 = 0 to -150 MeV, and that a repulsive real potential would produce no peak structure.

## Method

The authors calculate formation cross sections for eta'-mesic nuclei using the Green function method for the (gamma,p) reaction on a 12C target at an incident photon energy of 3 GeV, accessible at SPring-8. They model the eta'-nucleus optical potential as U(r) = (V_0 + iW_0) rho(r)/rho_0, treating V_0 as a parameter (0 to -150 MeV) and estimating W_0 from analysis of gamma p -> eta' p data using only the N*(1535) resonance. The nuclear response function S(E) is computed using the eikonal approximation, and the elementary cross section for p(gamma,p)eta' is taken as 150 nb/sr from SAPHIR collaboration data.

## Key numbers

- Incident photon energy: E_gamma = 3 GeV, chosen as the beam energy accessible at SPring-8.
- Elementary cross section for p(gamma,p)eta': 150 nb/sr in the laboratory frame, estimated from SAPHIR collaboration data.
- Real potential parameter range: V_0 = 0 to -150 MeV, based on theoretical evaluation of the eta' mass shift at nuclear saturation density rho_0.
- Imaginary potential values used: W_0 = -5 MeV and W_0 = -20 MeV, estimated from the eta' self-energy calculation yielding (+77 - 8i) MeV at rho_0.
- Four cases calculated: (V_0=0, W_0=-5 MeV), (V_0=-100 MeV, W_0=-5 MeV), (V_0=0, W_0=-20 MeV), and (V_0=-100 MeV, W_0=-20 MeV).

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies of mesic nuclei, including deeply bound pionic atoms (Suzuki et al., 2004) and eta-mesic nuclei (Nagahiro et al., previous work), to the eta'(958) meson, whose large mass is closely connected to the U_A(1) anomaly. The (gamma,p) reaction was previously proposed theoretically for meson bound state formation (Hirenzaki et al., 2001; Nagahiro et al., 2003), and the present study applies it to eta' for the first time. The results aim to stimulate experimental searches at SPring-8 and provide the first experimental information on the effective restoration of the U_A(1) anomaly at finite density, which has only been studied theoretically using models such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Kunihiro, 1989) and linear sigma models.
