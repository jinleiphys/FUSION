# Quantum Calculation of Dipole Excitation in Fusion Reaction

> The excitation of the giant dipole resonance by fusion is studied with N/Z asymmetry in the entrance channel. the TDHF solution exhibits a strong dipole vibration which can be associated with a giant vibration along the main axis of a fluctuating prolate shape. The consequences on the gamma-ray emission from hot compound nuclei are discussed.

# Quantum Calculation of Dipole Excitation in Fusion Reactions

## Key claim

The first quantum TDHF calculation of pre-equilibrium giant dipole resonance (GDR) excitation in fusion reactions shows that the dipole vibration is nonlinearly coupled to shape oscillations of the deformed compound nucleus, leading to a strong modulation of its frequency; however, the resulting enhancement of pre-equilibrium gamma emission is only a correction (about 20%) for the systems studied, not a dominant effect.

## Method

The authors performed time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations using a Skyrme mean-field with SLy4 parameters for central and peripheral fusion reactions of N/Z-asymmetric mirror nuclei (20O+20Mg at 1 MeV/u and 0.5 MeV/u, and 40Ar+40Ti at 1 MeV/u). They extracted the dipole moment Qd and its conjugate momentum Pd from the TDHF trajectories, analyzed the time-dependent oscillation period, and modeled the observed frequency modulation using a Mathieu equation with an oscillating spring constant to represent the coupling between dipole and shape (monopole/quadrupole) vibrations.

## Key numbers

- Dipole oscillation period in fused 40Ca from 20O+20Mg: approximately 150 fm/c, compared to approximately 80 fm/c for the GDR in spherical 40Ca (corresponding to E = 15.5 MeV)
- Average quadrupole deformation parameter of the fused system: epsilon = 0.23
- Number of excited phonons n0 for 20O+20Mg at 1 MeV/u: approximately 1.9 (upper limit, as two-body dissipation is not included)
- Number of excited phonons n0 for 20O+20Mg at 0.5 MeV/u (Coulomb barrier): 1.0
- Number of excited phonons n0 for 40Ar+40Ti at 1 MeV/u: 0.6
- Ratio of gamma-decay probability to equilibrium value for 20O+20Mg: P_gamma / P_gamma_bar = 1.2 (compared to 0.9 for the isospin-symmetric reaction 20Ne+20Ne)
- For 40Ca at excitation energy around 96 MeV: Gamma_evap = 0.76 MeV, Gamma_down = 7 MeV, mean number of excited phonons in equilibrium n_bar_GDR = 0.6
- Dipole oscillation period perpendicular to deformation axis (z' direction) for non-zero impact parameter: 67 fm/c

## Context

This work extends earlier semiclassical and schematic model studies of pre-equilibrium dipole excitation in fusion reactions proposed by Chomaz (1993), Baran (1996), and others, and provides the first fully quantum mechanical treatment using TDHF. The results support experimental indications reported by Flibotte (1996) for fusion reactions and Sandoli (1999) for deep inelastic collisions. The finding that the dipole frequency is modulated by coupling to shape oscillations introduces an additional spreading mechanism for the GDR line shape beyond the Landau spreading and evaporation damping already known from one-body dissipation theory. The approach enables quantitative predictions for pre-equilibrium gamma emission in N/Z-asymmetric fusion reactions and can be extended to heavier systems and non-zero impact parameters.
