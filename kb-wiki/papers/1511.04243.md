# Cassiopeia A and direct URCA cooling

> We model neutron star cooling, in particular the current rapid cooldown of the neutron star Cas A, with a microscopic nuclear equation of state featuring strong direct Urca processes and using compatible nuclear pairing gaps as well as effective masses. Several scenarios are possible to explain the features of Cas A, but only large and extended proton $^1S_0$ gaps and small neutron $^3PF_2$ gaps are able to accommodate also the major part of the complete current cooling data. We conclude that the possibility of strong direct Urca processes cannot be excluded from the cooling analysis.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that strong direct Urca (DU) cooling processes cannot be excluded from neutron star cooling analysis, even for the rapid cooldown of Cassiopeia A, provided that sufficiently large and extended proton 1S0 pairing gaps and small neutron 3PF2 gaps are employed.

## Method

The authors model neutron star cooling using a Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) nuclear equation of state (EOS) based on the Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon interaction plus Urbana-type UIX three-body force, which features strong direct Urca processes setting in at relatively low density. They compute pairing gaps in the proton 1S0 and neutron 3PF2 channels consistently within the same BHF framework, and perform cooling simulations using the NSCool code with three global scaling factors for the proton gap, neutron gap, and thermal conductivity.

## Key numbers

- DU threshold density: 0.44 fm^-3 (proton fraction x_p = 0.136) for the BHF EOS; for comparison, the APR EOS reaches DU at 0.82 fm^-3 (x_p = 0.140)
- DU process active for neutron stars with mass M > 1.10 solar masses in the BHF model; in the APR model only for M > 2.03 solar masses
- Cas A current age: 335 years; surface temperature approximately 2 x 10^6 K
- Temperature decline of Cas A: 2 to 5 percent during the last ten years
- For the neutron pair-breaking (PBF) scenario: scaling factors s_p = 2.0, s_n = 0.132, s_kappa = 1 for a 1.4 solar mass star, corresponding to maximum gaps delta_p approximately 6 MeV and delta_n approximately 0.1 MeV
- For the suppressed thermal conductivity scenario: s_p* = 1, s_n* = 0, s_kappa = 0.135 (upper panel); s_p* = 2.0, s_n* = 1.1, s_kappa = 0.13 (lower panel)
- In the scenario without Cas A constraint: Cas A mass predicted as 1.31 solar masses with s_p = 1.0, varying between 1.18 and 1.46 solar masses for s_p = 0.5 and 2.0 respectively

## Context

This work extends previous neutron star cooling studies that used the APR EOS (Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall, 1998), which has lower proton fractions and only activates DU cooling for very massive stars above about 2 solar masses. The authors contrast their approach with prior scenarios for Cas A cooling: the fine-tuned small neutron 3PF2 gap scenario (Page et al., 2011; Shternin et al., 2011; Yakovlev et al., 2011) and the reduced thermal conductivity scenario (Blaschke et al., 2012; Blaschke et al., 2013). The paper enables future work to incorporate consistent EOS and pairing gaps from the same theoretical framework, and highlights the need for precise neutron star mass measurements to constrain cooling models.
