# Role of the nonperturbative input in QCD resummed Drell-Yan $Q_T$-distributions

> We analyze the role of the nonperturbative input in the Collins, Soper, and Sterman (CSS)'s $b$-space QCD resummation formalism for Drell-Yan transverse momentum ($Q_T$) distributions, and investigate the predictive power of the CSS formalism. We find that the predictive power of the CSS formalism has a strong dependence on the collision energy $\sqrt{S}$ in addition to its well-known $Q^2$ dependence, and the $\sqrt{S}$ dependence improves the predictive power at collider energies. We show that a reliable extrapolation from perturbatively resummed $b$-space distributions to the nonperturbative large $b$ region is necessary to ensure the correct $Q_T$ distributions. By adding power corrections to the renormalization group equations in the CSS formalism, we derive a new extrapolation formalism. We demonstrate that at collider energies, the CSS resummation formalism plus our extrapolation has an excellent predictive power for $W$ and $Z$ production at all transverse momenta $Q_T\le Q$. We also show that the $b$-space resummed $Q_T$ distributions provide a good description of Drell-Yan data at fixed target energies.

## Key claim

The predictive power of the Collins-Soper-Sterman (CSS) $b$-space resummation formalism for Drell-Yan transverse momentum distributions depends strongly on the collision energy $\sqrt{S}$ in addition to its well-known $Q^2$ dependence, and this $\sqrt{S}$ dependence significantly improves predictive power at collider energies where parton distributions evolve steeply at small $x$.

## Method

The authors analyzed the CSS $b$-space resummation formalism for Drell-Yan vector boson production, examining the saddle point of the $b$-space Fourier transform integral at $Q_T=0$ and $Q_T>0$. They derived a new extrapolation formalism by adding power corrections to the renormalization group equations for the evolution kernels $K$ and $G$ in the CSS formalism, enabling a reliable extrapolation from perturbatively resummed $b$-space distributions to the nonperturbative large-$b$ region.

## Key numbers

- For $Q=6$ GeV and $\sqrt{S}=1.8$ TeV, the saddle point $b_0 \approx 0.38$ GeV$^{-1}$ (within perturbative region), compared to $b_{\rm SP} \approx 1.2$ GeV$^{-1}$ from the leading-order formula, due to small $x_A \sim x_B \sim 0.003$
- For $Z$ production at Tevatron ($\sqrt{S}=1.8$ TeV), the saddle point $b_0 \approx 0.24$ GeV$^{-1}$; at LHC ($\sqrt{S}=14$ TeV), $b_0 \approx 0.13$ GeV$^{-1}$
- The CSS formalism uses $b_{max} = 0.5$ GeV$^{-1}$ in the $b_*$ prescription
- The DWS nonperturbative function uses parameters $g_1=0.15$ GeV$^{2}$ and $g_2=0.4$ GeV$^{2}$ with $Q_0=2$ GeV
- The LY nonperturbative function uses parameters $g_1=0.11^{+0.04}_{-0.03}$ GeV$^2$, $g_2=0.58^{+0.1}_{-0.2}$ GeV$^2$, and $g_3=-0.15^{+0.1}_{-0.1}$ GeV$^{-1}$

## Context

This work extends the CSS $b$-space resummation formalism (Collins, Soper, and Sterman, 1985) by demonstrating that the collision energy $\sqrt{S}$ provides additional predictive power beyond the known $Q^2$ dependence, particularly at collider energies where parton distributions evolve rapidly at small $x$. The analysis addresses drawbacks noted in the QCD review report (1999) and by Ellis et al., showing that the $b$-space formalism works smoothly for all $Q_T \leq Q$ and that the transition to fixed-order calculations becomes less ambiguous. The new extrapolation formalism improves upon previous parameterizations by Davis, Webber, and Stirling (DWS), Ladinsky and Yuan (LY), and Landry, Brock, Ladinsky, and Yuan (LBLY), providing a clearer separation between perturbative predictions and nonperturbative physics.
