# Evidence for a new $SU(4)$ symmetry with $J=2$ mesons

> Recently, a new symmetry of mesons has been found upon truncation of the quasi-zero modes of the Overlap Dirac operator in lattice simulations. Namely, the $ρ,ρ',ω,ω',a_1, b_1,h_1$ and possibly $f_1$ $J=1$ mesons get degenerate after removal of the quasi-zero modes. This emergent symmetry has been established to be $SU(4)\supset SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R \times U(1)_A$. It is higher than the symmetry of the QCD Lagrangian and provides not only a mixing of quarks of given chirality in the isospin space, but also the mixing of left-handed and right-handed components. Here we study, with the Overlap Dirac operator, the isovector $J=2$ mesons upon the quasi-zero mode reduction and observe a similar degeneracy. This result further supports the $SU(4)$ symmetry in mesons of given spin $J \geq 1$.

# Evidence for a new $SU(4)$ symmetry with $J=2$ mesons

## Key claim
Upon removal of the lowest-lying eigenmodes of the Overlap Dirac operator from valence quark propagators, isovector $J=2$ mesons ($\rho_2$, $a_2$, $a'_2$, $\pi_2$) become degenerate in a pattern consistent with $SU(4)$ symmetry, supporting the existence of this emergent symmetry in mesons of spin $J \geq 1$ after quasi-zero mode reduction. The degeneracy onset is observed after elimination of the lowest 10-20 modes, corresponding to a truncation energy of 65-125 MeV.

## Method
The authors performed $N_f=2$ dynamical lattice QCD simulations using Overlap fermion gauge field configurations on a $16^3 \times 32$ lattice with lattice spacing $a \sim 0.12$ fm and pion mass $M_\pi = 289(2)$ MeV, provided by the JLQCD collaboration. They constructed truncated quark propagators by removing an increasing number $k$ (from 2 to 30) of the lowest-lying Dirac eigenmodes, then computed cross-correlation matrices for isovector $J=2$ meson interpolators classified into irreducible representations $E$ and $T_2$ of the hyper-cubic group $O_h$, extracting masses via the variational method with Gaussian-smeared quark sources.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: $a \sim 0.12$ fm on a $16^3 \times 32$ lattice
- Pion mass: $M_\pi = 289(2)$ MeV
- Gauge ensemble: 83 configurations with topological sector fixed to $Q_T = 0$
- Truncation steps: $k = 2 - 30$, corresponding to eigenvalue cutoff between 8-180 MeV
- Onset of $SU(4)$ symmetry: after elimination of lowest 10-20 modes (truncation energy 65-125 MeV)
- At $k=30$ truncation, masses in lattice units: $a_2$ ground state $am = 1.042 \pm 0.023$, $\rho_2$ ground state $am = 1.042 \pm 0.026$, $\pi_2$ ground state $am = 1.043 \pm 0.024$ (all consistent within errors in $T_2$ representation)
- At $k=30$, $a_2$ first excited state $am = 0.987 \pm 0.028$, $\rho_2$ first excited state $am = 1.552 \pm 0.052$, $\pi_2$ first excited state $am = 1.467 \pm 0.048$

## Context
This work extends previous findings by Denissenya, Glozman, and Pak (2014) and Denissenya, Glozman, and Pak (2014) that demonstrated $SU(4)$ symmetry in $J=1$ mesons ($\rho, \rho', \omega, \omega', a_1, b_1, h_1, f_1$) upon quasi-zero mode truncation, with earlier hints from Glozman (2012) using the Chirally Improved Dirac operator. The $SU(4)$ symmetry, proposed by Glozman (2014, 2015), is higher than the $SU(2)_L \times SU(2)_R \times U(1)_A$ symmetry of the QCD Lagrangian and implies the absence of the color-magnetic field in the system. The results enable further investigation of whether this emergent symmetry extends to all mesons with $J \geq 1$ and may be interpreted as a manifestation of the dynamical color-electric string in QCD, though the authors note that larger volumes are needed to compare $J=1$ excited states with $J=2$ ground states definitively.
