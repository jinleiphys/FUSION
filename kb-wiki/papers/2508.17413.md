# Sensitivity of neutrinoless double beta decays from a combined analysis of ground and excited states

> Next-generation neutrinoless double-beta ($0νββ$) decay experiments, with projected half-life sensitivities approaching $10^{28}$ years, aim to probe the entire parameter space of the inverted neutrino mass ordering in the light-neutrino-exchange scenario. However, this reach remains uncertain by the substantial model dependence of the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs). In this work, we propose a strategy based on a combined analysis of $0νββ$ decays to both the ground state and the first excited $0^+$ state of the daughter nucleus. We show that such a multi-channel approach can significantly enhance experimental sensitivity, depending on the underlying NME predictions. This method is particularly well-suited for large liquid xenon detectors, such as the proposed PandaX-xT and XLZD experiments, which can efficiently identify transitions of $^{136}$Xe to excited states. Our results highlight the importance of exploiting multiple decay channels in future $0νββ$ searches to maximize their discovery potential.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a combined analysis of neutrinoless double-beta decay to both the ground state and the first excited 0+ state of the daughter nucleus can significantly enhance experimental sensitivity to the effective Majorana neutrino mass, but the degree of improvement depends strongly on the ratio of the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for the two channels and on the specific detector configuration.

## Method

The authors performed Geant4-based simulations of both ground-state-to-ground-state (0νββ-gs) and ground-state-to-excited-state (0νββ-ex) decays of 136Xe using the XLZD detector configuration, with a 60-ton active natural xenon target. They constructed a χ² function from Poisson likelihoods to evaluate the 3σ sensitivity to the effective neutrino mass |mββ| from a combined analysis of both decay channels, using NMEs from seven different nuclear models (RQRPA, MCM, IBM-2, ISM, and MR-CDFT) and phase-space factors from Kotila (2012). The 0νββ-ex events were identified as multi-site events with characteristic energy depositions from the two electrons and two de-excitation gamma rays (0.76 MeV and 0.82 MeV), enabling a larger fiducial volume (20 tons versus 8.2 tons for the ground-state channel) and more effective background suppression.

## Key numbers

* The phase-space factor for 0νββ-ex decay of 136Xe is G0νex = 0.6127 × 10⁻¹⁵ yr⁻¹, compared to G0νgs = 14.58 × 10⁻¹⁵ yr⁻¹ for the ground-state transition (Kotila 2012).
* Under the nominal XLZD scenario (60-ton active target, 10-year measurement), the combined analysis improves the |mββ| sensitivity from 80.1 meV to 34.6 meV using RQRPA(RCM) NMEs, bringing it within the inverted ordering region.
* Under the ideal scenario (100-ton detector, 60-ton excited-state fiducial volume, background rate 10⁻⁴ counts/yr), the combined analysis with MCM(UCOM) NMEs yields |mββ| = 6.8⁺¹·³₋₀·₉ meV.
* The NMEs for 0νββ-gs decay range from 0.66 to 5.06 across models, while those for 0νββ-ex decay span from 0.49 to 6.28.
* The 0νββ-ex decay of 136Xe has a Q-value of 0.88 MeV, with gamma energies of 0.76 MeV and 0.82 MeV.
* The ground-state fiducial volume (FV-gs) is 8.2 tons with a background rate of 0.315 counts/yr; the excited-state fiducial volume (FV-ex) is 20 tons with a background rate of 0.126 counts/yr under the nominal scenario.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical exploration of 0νββ-ex decays by Tomoda (2000), Simkovic (2001), and Duerr (2011), who proposed such decays as a way to discriminate among decay mechanisms or as a self-consistency test. The authors apply this concept to realistic detector simulations for the proposed PandaX-xT and XLZD experiments, which are next-generation ton-scale liquid xenon detectors. The combined analysis approach could enable these experiments to fully cover the inverted neutrino mass ordering parameter space within a 10-year measurement period, without requiring larger detectors, and the authors note that similar potential exists for 100Mo and 150Nd based on their phase-space factors.
