# Final-state interactions in deep-inelastic scattering from a tensor polarized deuteron target

> Deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) from a tensor polarized deuteron is sensitive to possible non-nucleonic components of the deuteron wave function. To accurately estimate the size of the nucleonic contribution, final-state interactions (FSIs) need to be accounted for in calculations. We outline a model that, based on the diffractive nature of the effective hadron-nucleon interaction, uses the generalized eikonal approximation to model the FSIs in the resonance region, taking into account the proton-neutron component of the deuteron. The calculation uses a factorized model with a basis of three resonances with mass $W<2$ GeV as the relevant set of effective hadron states entering the final-state interaction amplitude for inclusive DIS. We present results for the tensor asymmetry observable $A_{zz}$ for kinematics accessible in experiments at Jefferson Lab and Hermes. For inclusive DIS, sizeable effects are found when including FSIs for Bjorken $x>0.2$, but the overall size of $A_{zz}$ remains small. For tagged spectator DIS, FSIs effects are largest at spectator momenta around 300 MeV and for forward spectator angles.

# Final-state interactions in deep-inelastic scattering from a tensor polarized deuteron target

## Key claim
Final-state interactions (FSIs) produce sizeable effects on the tensor asymmetry \(A_{zz}\) for inclusive DIS at Bjorken \(x>0.2\), but the overall size of the nucleonic contribution to \(A_{zz}\) remains small (on the order of \(\sim \pm 0.01\) in the resonance region). For tagged spectator DIS, FSI effects are largest at spectator momenta around 300 MeV and for forward spectator angles, and can even reverse the sign of \(A_{zz}\).

## Method
The model uses the virtual nucleon approximation (VNA) to describe DIS from the deuteron, considering only the proton-neutron component. Final-state interactions are treated via the generalized eikonal approximation (GEA), with the effective hadron-nucleon rescattering amplitude parametrized by a diffractive form with total cross section \(\sigma\), real part \(\epsilon\), and slope factor \(B\) that depend on \(Q^2\) and Bjorken \(x\). For inclusive DIS, the optical theorem is applied to the forward virtual Compton scattering amplitude, and three effective resonances at invariant mass \(W = 1.232\), 1.5, and 1.75 GeV are included as the set of intermediate hadronic states entering the FSI amplitude, along with a broad DIS continuum contribution.

## Key numbers
- For inclusive DIS at \(Q^2 = 2\) GeV\(^2\) in the resonance region, plane-wave \(A_{zz}\) reaches values of \(\sim \pm 0.01\); adding FSIs produces significant changes over the whole \(x\) range.
- At \(Q^2 = 5\) GeV\(^2\), the off-shell contribution of the FSI diagram is almost negligible.
- For Hermes kinematics, the calculated \(A_{zz} \approx 0.0015\) at \(x = 0.45\), \(Q^2 \approx 5\) GeV\(^2\), which is about two orders of magnitude smaller than the Hermes data point of \(A_{zz} = 0.157 \pm 0.69\).
- For tagged spectator DIS, FSI effects are largest at spectator momenta around 300 MeV (specifically 340 MeV) and for spectator angles \(\cos \theta_s > 0.5\); at \(p_s = 340\) MeV and \(\cos \theta_s > 0.5\), including FSIs reverses the sign of \(A_{zz}\).

## Context
This work extends the FSI model developed by Cosyn and Sargsians for unpolarized deuteron DIS (Cosyn 2013) and tagged spectator DIS (Cosyn 2010, Cosyn 2011) to the case of a tensor polarized deuteron target. It provides the first quantification of FSI effects on the nucleonic contribution to the tensor asymmetry \(A_{zz}\) at moderate \(x\), which is essential for interpreting measurements of the structure function \(b_1\) that could reveal non-nucleonic components in the deuteron (Miller 2013). The calculations are directly relevant to the upcoming Jefferson Lab experiment (Slifer 2013) and to existing Hermes data (Airapetian 2005), and show that FSI contributions from the resonance region can affect \(A_{zz}\) even in the DIS region due to Fermi motion of the struck nucleon.
