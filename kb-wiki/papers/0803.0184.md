# Two- and three-alpha systems with nonlocal potential

> Two body data alone cannot determine the potential uniquely, one needs three-body data as well. A method is presented here which simultaneously fits local or nonlocal potentials to two-body and three-body observables. The interaction of composite particles, due to the Pauli effect and the indistinguishability of the constituent particles, is genuinely nonlocal. As an example, we use a Pauli-correct nonlocal fish-bone type optical model for the $α-α$ potential and derive the fitting parameters such that it reproduces the two-$α$ and three-$α$ experimental data.

# Two- and three-alpha systems with nonlocal potential

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a Pauli-correct nonlocal fish-bone type alpha-alpha potential can simultaneously reproduce two-body phase shifts, the 8Be ground-state resonance, and the 12C three-alpha binding energy without requiring an explicit three-body potential or angular-momentum-dependent local terms, provided the fit incorporates both two-body and three-body data. The abstract's claim of reproducing "two-alpha and three-alpha experimental data" glosses over the condition that this success depends on fitting the l=4 partial wave phase shifts, which earlier fish-bone models had not done adequately.

## Method

The authors solve the two-body Lippmann-Schwinger equation and the three-body Faddeev integral equations using a Coulomb-Sturmian separable expansion method that approximates the short-range part of the potential in a bi-orthonormal basis. For the three-alpha system, they treat identical bosons with repulsive Coulomb potentials, splitting each interaction into a short-range part and a Coulomb part, and solve the resulting homogeneous algebraic equations for bound-state energies. The fish-bone optical model potential is constructed from a local part (two Gaussians plus screened Coulomb) combined with Pauli-blocking terms derived from the norm kernel eigenvalues of the alpha clusters.

## Key numbers

- The 8Be l=0 resonance state is reproduced at E = (0.09161 - 0.00000303 i) MeV, compared to the experimental value of (0.0916 - 0.000003 i) MeV
- The 12C three-alpha ground state binding energy is reproduced at E_3b = -7.27502 MeV, compared to the experimental value of -7.275 MeV
- The first excited state of the three-alpha system is calculated at E_3alpha^res = (0.54 - 0.0005 i) MeV, close to the experimental value
- The local part of the fish-bone potential uses parameters: v1 = -120.30683493 MeV, beta1 = 0.20206127 fm^-2, v2 = 49.06187648 MeV, beta2 = 0.76601097 fm^-2, and oscillator width a = 0.64874009 fm^-2
- The Ali-Bodmer potential gives three-alpha binding energy of only -2.28 MeV (l_max=8), while the earlier Kircher-Schmid fish-bone potential overbinds at -15.63 MeV (l_max=8)
- Phase shifts are fitted up to 20 MeV for S, D, and G waves

## Context

This work extends the fish-bone optical model of Schmid (1980s) by improving the fit to l=4 partial wave phase shifts, which earlier work by Kircher and Schmid had not achieved. The authors contrast their approach with the Ali-Bodmer local potential (which underbinds the three-alpha system at -2.28 MeV) and with the work of Oryu and Kamada, who added a phenomenological three-body potential to the Kircher-Schmid fish-bone potential. The method enables systematic fitting of nonlocal cluster-model potentials to both two-body and three-body observables simultaneously, and the authors suggest it can be applied to other composite-particle systems where Pauli effects are important.
