# Chiral properties of hadron correlators in nuclear matter

> The constraints imposed by chiral symmetry on hadron correlation functions in nuclear medium are discussed. It is shown that these constraints imply a certain structure for the in-medium hadron correlators and lead to the cancelation of the order $ρm_π$ term in the in-medium nucleon correlator. We also consider the effect of mixing of the chiral partners correlation functions arising from the interaction of nuclear pions with corresponding interpolating currents. It reflects the phenomena of partial restoration of chiral symmetry. The different scenarios of such restoration are briefly discussed.

# Chiral properties of hadron correlators in nuclear matter

## Key claim
Chiral symmetry imposes constraints on in-medium hadron correlators that lead to a cancellation of the order ρmπ term in the nucleon correlator, but this cancellation does not imply a direct one-to-one correspondence between changes in the quark condensate and changes in nucleon properties in matter. The mixing of chiral partner correlation functions (e.g., ρ-A1, σ-π) arises from the interaction of nuclear pions with interpolating currents and reflects partial restoration of chiral symmetry, though the identity of correlators does not necessarily imply degeneracy of effective masses.

## Method
The author derives the leading chiral corrections to in-medium hadron correlators by expanding the nuclear wave function in a Fock representation including zero, one, and two pion states, then applying soft-pion theorems to reduce matrix elements involving pions to commutators of axial charges with the interpolating currents. The formalism is applied to nucleon correlators (using QCD sum rules with the Ioffe choice of interpolating current), vector-axial vector correlators (ρ-A1 mixing), and scalar-pseudoscalar correlators (σ-π mixing), with the mixing parameter expressed in terms of the nonanalytic part of the pion-nucleon sigma term.

## Key numbers
- The nonanalytic part of the pion-nucleon sigma term, σ̅πN, is approximately -20 MeV, compared to the full sigma term σπN = 45 MeV.
- The leading chiral correction to the in-medium two-quark condensate increases the overall correction by 12-15% depending on the value accepted for σ̅πN.
- The mixing parameter for the ρ-A1 system is ξ = (4ρσ̅πN)/(3fπ²mπ²), while for the σ-π system it is ξ = (2ρσ̅πN)/(3fπ²mπ²), indicating less pronounced mixing in the scalar-pseudoscalar channel at normal nuclear density.
- The lowest order density evolution of the two-quark condensate is given by ⟨Ψ|q̅q|Ψ⟩ = ⟨0|q̅q|0⟩(1 - (σπN/(fπ²mπ²))ρ).
