# Production of the pentaquark $ﾎ録+$ in $np$ scattering

> We study $np\to ﾎ嶄録{+}$ and $np\to ﾎ｣^{0}ﾎ録{+}$ processes for both of the positive and negative parities of the $ﾎ録{+}$. Employing the effective chiral Lagrangians for the $KNY$ and $K^*NY$ interactions, we calculate differential cross sections as well as total cross sections for the $np\to ﾎ｣^0 ﾎ録+$ and $np\to ﾎ嶄録+$ reactions. The total cross sections for the positive-parity $ﾎ録+$ turn out to be approximately ten times larger than those for the negative parity $ﾎ録+$ in the range of the CM energy $\sqrt{s}_{\rm th}\le \sqrt{s}\le 3.5 {\rm GeV}$. The results are rather sensitive to the mechanism of $K$ exchanges in the $t$ -- channel.

## Key claim

The total cross sections for the positive-parity $\Theta^+$ are approximately ten times larger than those for the negative-parity $\Theta^+$ in the range of the CM energy $\sqrt{s}_{\rm th}\le \sqrt{s}\le 3.5\, {\rm GeV}$, but the results are rather sensitive to the mechanism of $K$ exchanges in the $t$窶田hannel and depend on the choice of coupling constants and cutoff parameters from different potentials.

## Method

The authors employed effective chiral Lagrangians for the $KNY$ and $K^*NY$ interactions to calculate Born diagrams with pseudoscalar $K$ and vector $K^*$ exchanges for the reactions $np\rightarrow \Lambda\Theta^{+}$ and $np\rightarrow \Sigma^{0}\Theta^{+}$. They considered both positive and negative parities for the $\Theta^+$, using coupling constants from the Nijmegen and Jﾃｼlich窶釘onn potentials, and introduced monopole-type form factors with cutoff parameters to account for the extended size of hadrons.

## Key numbers

- For the Nijmegen potential, the average total cross section for $np\rightarrow\Lambda\Theta^{+}_{+}$ is $\sim 40\ \mu b$ in the range $\sqrt{s}_{\rm th}\le \sqrt{s}\le 3.5$ GeV, where $\sqrt{s}_{\rm th}=2656$ MeV.
- For the Nijmegen potential, the average total cross section for $np\rightarrow\Sigma^{0}\Theta^{+}_{+}$ is $\sim 2.0\ \mu b$ in the same CM energy range, with $\sqrt{s}_{\rm th}=2733$ MeV.
- For the Nijmegen potential, the average total cross sections for negative-parity $\Theta^{+}$ are $\sigma_{np\rightarrow\Lambda\Theta^{+}_{-}}\sim 5.0\ \mu b$ and $\sigma_{np\rightarrow\Sigma^{0}\Theta^{+}_{-}}\sim 0.3\ \mu b$.
- For the Jﾃｼlich窶釘onn potential, the average total cross sections are $\sigma_{np\rightarrow\Lambda\Theta^{+}_{+}}\sim 100\ \mu b$ and $\sigma_{np\rightarrow\Sigma^{0}\Theta^{+}_{+}}\sim 20\ \mu b$.
- For the Jﾃｼlich窶釘onn potential, the average total cross sections for negative-parity $\Theta^{+}$ are $\sigma_{np\rightarrow\Lambda\Theta^{+}_{-}}\sim 6.0\ \mu b$ and $\sigma_{np\rightarrow\Sigma^{0}\Theta^{+}_{-}}\sim 2.0\ \mu b$.
- The $KN\Theta$ coupling constants were determined using $\Gamma_{\Theta\rightarrow KN}=15$ MeV and $M_{\Theta}=1540$ MeV, giving $g_{KN\Theta_{+}^{+}}=3.78$ and $g_{KN\Theta_{-}^{+}}=0.53$.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of $\Theta^+$ production in nucleon-nucleon interactions, including the threshold analysis of polarized $pp$ scattering by Thomas et al. (2003) and the spin correlation approach by Hanhart et al. (2003), as well as earlier cross section predictions by Liu et al. (2003) and Oh et al. (2003). The results provide a guideline for future experiments aiming to determine the parity of the $\Theta^+$ through $np$ scattering reactions, complementing the authors' previous work on $\vec p \vec p \rightarrow \Sigma^+ \Theta^+$ near threshold.
