# Beyond Intermittency: Erraticity

## Key claim

Erraticity analysis, defined through the scaling behavior of moments $C_p$ of the event-by-event distribution $P(F)$ of normalized factorial moments, is presented as the next logical step beyond intermittency because it captures the event-to-event fluctuations of spatial patterns that intermittency averages over. The claim is conditional on the existence of power-law scaling $C_p \propto M^{\psi(p)}$ in the data, which must be verified empirically.

## Method

The author defines $F_q^e$ as the normalized factorial moment for a single event, then constructs the distribution $P(F)$ of these values across many events. From this distribution, the moments $C_p = \langle F^p \rangle / \langle F \rangle^p$ are computed and examined for power-law scaling with the number of bins $M$ (or bin size $\delta$). The scaling exponent $\psi(p)$ is extracted, and an erraticity spectrum $e(\alpha)$ is obtained via Legendre transform of $\psi(p)$, analogous to the multifractal spectrum $f(\alpha)$ from $G$-moment analysis. An analytic example using a gamma distribution for $P(F)$ with parameter $a = 500/(q \, \ln M)^2$ is presented to illustrate the method.

## Key numbers

- For the analytic example with $q=2$: $\alpha_{0,2} = -0.026$ and $\alpha_{1,2} = 0.024$
- For the analytic example with $q=3$: $\alpha_{0,3} = -0.047$ and $\alpha_{1,3} = 0.04$
- The gamma distribution parameter is $a = 500/(q \, \ln M)^2$, reproducing trends from Monte Carlo simulations of jet fragmentation in pQCD (Cao and Hwa, 1995)
- The entropy index $\mu_q = \alpha_{1,q}$ is larger at higher $q$, indicating greater event-to-event fluctuations of $F_q$ at higher order

## Context

This work extends the intermittency framework introduced by Bialas and Peschanski (1986) and the multifractal $G$-moment analysis of Hwa (1990), which characterized spatial fluctuations but averaged over event fluctuations. The erraticity approach probes both spatial and event fluctuations through double moments $C_{p,q}$, building on earlier work by Cao and Hwa (1995) on entropy indices in QCD branching processes. The method enables comparison of soft interaction models against data at a deeper level than intermittency alone, and is applicable to hadronic collisions, heavy-ion collisions, and $e^+e^-$ annihilations where no geometrical fluctuations are present.
