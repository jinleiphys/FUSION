# Pion spectral properties above the chiral crossover of QCD

> Spectral functions encode a wealth of information about the dynamics of any given system, and the determination of their non-perturbative characteristics is a long-standing problem in quantum field theory. Whilst numerical simulations of lattice QCD provide ample data for various Euclidean correlation functions, the inversion required to extract spectral functions is an ill-posed problem. In this work, we pursue previously established constraints imposed by field locality at finite temperature $T$, namely that spectral functions possess a non-perturbative representation which generalises the well-known Källén-Lehmann spectral form to $T>0$. Using this representation, we analyse lattice QCD data of the spatial pseudo-scalar correlator in the temperature range $220-960 \, \text{MeV}$, and obtain an analytic expression for the corresponding spectral function, with parameters fixed by the data. From the structure of this spectral function we find evidence for the existence of a distinct pion state above the chiral pseudo-critical temperature $T_{\text{pc}}$, and contributions from its first excitation, which gradually melt as the temperature increases. As a non-trivial test, we find that the extracted spectral function reproduces the corresponding temporal lattice correlator data for $T=220 \, \text{MeV}$.

## Key claim

Using a non-perturbative representation of spectral functions that generalises the Källén-Lehmann form to finite temperature, the authors find evidence for a distinct pion state persisting above the chiral pseudo-critical temperature Tpc, along with contributions from its first excitation that gradually melt as temperature increases, with the extracted spectral function successfully reproducing temporal lattice correlator data at T = 220 MeV as a consistency test.

## Method

The authors analysed lattice QCD data of spatial pseudo-scalar correlators from Ref. Rohrhofer (2019) in the temperature range 220–960 MeV, using a spectral representation derived from field locality at finite temperature. They fitted the correlator data with a two-state exponential ansatz corresponding to the pion and its first radial excitation (π*), extracted the associated damping factors, and used these to construct an analytic expression for the spectral function via the generalised Källén-Lehmann representation.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range analysed: 220–960 MeV
- Pion screening mass at T = 220 MeV: 776(17) MeV
- Pion screening mass at T = 960 MeV: 5559(10) MeV
- π* screening mass at T = 220 MeV: 3361(115) MeV
- π* screening mass at T = 960 MeV: 8604(77) MeV
- Vacuum pion mass used: mπ = 140 ± 70 MeV
- Vacuum π* mass estimated: mπ* = 1412 ± 38 MeV
- χ²/d.o.f. for fits ranged from 0.314 (T = 480 MeV) to 1.243 (T = 960 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the axiomatic finite-temperature field theory framework developed by Bros (1992, 1995, 1998, 2001) and applies it to lattice QCD data, building on previous applications of damping factors to compute in-medium observables by Lowdon (2021, 2022). The results contradict the expectation that pions completely dissolve above the chiral crossover, instead showing a distinct pion state persists, and enable future non-perturbative calculations of transport coefficients and in-medium hadron properties using the extracted spectral functions.
