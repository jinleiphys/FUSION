# Two Photon Correlation in Anisotropic Quark-gluon plasma (aQGP)

> The only way to obtain the space-time structure of heavy ion collision is through the study of two-particle momentum correlations. Thus we have studied the intensity correlation for the photons having fixed transverse momentum of one of the photons ($k_{1T}$= 2 GeV) in anisotropic Quark Gluon Plasma (aQGP) to have an idea about emission zone in presence of initial momentum anisotropy. The {\em free streaming interpolating} model with fixed initial condition has been used for the space-time evolution for most central collision at RHIC energy. The variation of Bose-Einstein correlation function (BECF), $C_2$, for two identical photons as a function of $q_{out}$, $q_{side}$ and $q_{long}$ is evaluated. We have restricted our analysis only to QGP phase to know the effect of anisotropy on the correlation function and HBT radii extracted. It is observed that the longitudinal dimension of the reaction zone is mostly affected due to the presence of momentum space anisotropy.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the presence of initial momentum-space anisotropy in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase reduces the Bose-Einstein correlation function \(C_2\) for two identical photons compared to the isotropic case, and that this reduction is most pronounced in the longitudinal direction, leading to an increase in the extracted HBT radii. This claim is conditional on the use of a free-streaming interpolating model for the anisotropic phase and specific choices of isotropization time (\(\tau_{iso}\)) and initial conditions at RHIC energy.

## Method

The authors used a two-stage space-time evolution model: a free-streaming interpolating model for the anisotropic QGP (aQGP) phase from initial time \(\tau_i\) to isotropization time \(\tau_{iso}\), followed by (1+2)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics with cylindrical symmetry and longitudinal boost invariance for the isotropic QGP and hadronic phases. They computed the two-photon Bose-Einstein correlation function \(C_2\) as a function of \(q_{out}\), \(q_{side}\), and \(q_{long}\) by convoluting static thermal photon emission rates (from QCD Compton and annihilation processes for the QGP, and hadronic reactions for the hadronic phase) with the expansion dynamics, fixing the transverse momentum of one photon at \(k_{1T}=2\) GeV and using two sets of RHIC initial conditions (SET-I: \(\tau_i=0.147\) fm/c, \(T_i=446\) MeV; SET-II: \(\tau_i=0.24\) fm/c, \(T_i=350\) MeV).

## Key numbers

- Fixed transverse momentum of one photon: \(k_{1T}=2\) GeV.
- SET-I initial conditions: \(\tau_i=0.147\) fm/c, \(T_i=446\) MeV.
- SET-II initial conditions: \(\tau_i=0.24\) fm/c, \(T_i=350\) MeV.
- Transition temperature: \(T_c=175\) MeV.
- Freeze-out temperature: \(T_f=120\) MeV.
- Diffusion parameter for initial energy density profile: \(\sigma=0.5\) fm.
- Width parameter for the equation of state crossover: \(\Gamma=25\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of two-photon intensity interferometry in heavy-ion collisions, such as those by Peressounko, Frodermann, Alam, and collaborators, by incorporating initial momentum-space anisotropy in the QGP phase using the free-streaming interpolating model developed by Schenke, Martinez, and Strickland (2009-2010). It contrasts with the isotropic hydrodynamic treatment of Bjorken (1983) and earlier photon HBT studies by Rupa and collaborators, which varied the formation time \(\tau_i\) rather than the isotropization time \(\tau_{iso}\). The results enable future extraction of \(\tau_{iso}\) by comparing computed HBT radii with experimental data from RHIC.
