# Chiral symmetry restoration in excited hadrons, quantum fluctuations, and quasiclassics

> In this paper, we discuss the transition to the semiclassical regime in excited hadrons, and consequently, the restoration of chiral symmetry for these states. We use a generalised Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with the interaction between quarks in the form of the instantaneous Lorentz-vector confining potential. This model is known to provide spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry in the vacuum via the standard selfenergy loops for valence quarks. It has been shown recently that the effective single-quark potential is of the Lorentz-scalar nature, for the low-lying hadrons, while, for the high-lying states, it becomes a pure Lorentz vector and hence the model exhibits the restoration of chiral symmetry. We demonstrate explicitly the quantum nature of chiral symmetry breaking, the absence of chiral symmetry breaking in the classical limit as well as the transition to the semiclassical regime for excited states, where the effect of chiral symmetry breaking becomes only a small correction to the classical contributions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that chiral symmetry restoration in highly excited hadrons is a universal consequence of the transition to a quasiclassical regime, where quantum fluctuations (loops) become suppressed relative to classical contributions by a factor of \(\hbar/{\cal S}\), with \({\cal S}\) being the classical action of the intrinsic motion; the authors demonstrate this explicitly using a generalized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with an instantaneous Lorentz-vector confining potential, showing that the chiral angle \(\varphi_p\) vanishes in the classical limit \(\hbar \to 0\) and that the expansion of \(\varphi_p\) in powers of \(\hbar\) breaks down nonanalytically.

## Method

The authors employ a generalized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (GNJL) model with an instantaneous Lorentz-vector confining kernel, restricted to the time-time component \(K^{ab}_{00} = \delta^{ab} V_0(|\vec{x}-\vec{y}|)\) with a powerlike potential \(V_0(|\vec{x}|) = K_0^{\alpha+1} |\vec{x}|^\alpha\) (choosing \(\alpha=1\) for linear confinement). They solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the dressed quark propagator in the rainbow approximation (justified in the large-\(N_C\) limit), parametrizing the mass operator via a chiral angle \(\varphi_p\) that satisfies a nonlinear mass-gap equation. For heavy-light mesons, they derive a Schrödinger-like bound-state equation from the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the ladder approximation, with a static antiquark at the origin, and analyze the parity doubling of excited states.

## Key numbers

- The chiral symmetry restoration scale for mesons in the GNJL model is found to be above about \(2.5\ \text{GeV}\), regardless of the explicit form of the confining potential.
- The slowest possible rate of chiral symmetry restoration in mesons is expected to be \(\sim 1/n^{3/2}\), where \(n\) is the radial quantum number, based on matching of the Operator Product Expansion and the resonance representation of the two-point function (citing Shifman).
- In the nucleon spectrum, approximate parity doubling begins in the \(1.7\ \text{GeV}\) mass region; in the \(\Delta\)-spectrum, it starts from the \(1.9\ \text{GeV}\) region, corresponding to the same excitation energy relative to the ground state \(\Delta(1232)\).
- The chiral angle at low momentum behaves as \(\varphi_p \approx \pi/2 - \text{const} \cdot p c / \sqrt{\sigma \hbar c}\), showing a nonanalytic dependence on \(\hbar\).
- The expansion parameter for the chiral angle in the heavy-quark regime is \(\sigma \hbar c / (m c^2)^2\), with the classical action scale \({\cal S} \sim m^2 c^3 / \sigma\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier demonstration by Glozman (2004) and others that chiral symmetry is restored in excited hadrons, building on the observation that the effective single-quark potential becomes a pure Lorentz vector for high-lying states (parity2, 2004). The paper provides a microscopic explanation for this restoration by linking it to the quasiclassical regime, contrasting with models like the standard Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (NJL, 1961) or the instanton-liquid model (Shuryak, 1982) that lack confinement and cannot address excited states. The results enable a deeper understanding of the interplay between confinement, chiral symmetry breaking, and quantum fluctuations, and support the view that highly excited hadrons can be described as color-electric strings with chiral quarks at the ends (Glozman, 2004).
