# Radiation correction to astrophysical fusion reactions and the electron screening problem

> We discuss the effect of electromagnetic environment on laboratory measurements of the nuclear fusion reactions of astrophysical interest. The radiation field is eliminated using the path integral formalism in order to obtain the influence functional, which we evaluate in the semi-classical approximation. We show that enhancement of the tunneling probability due to the radiation correction is extremely small and does not resolve the longstanding problem that the observed electron screening effect is significantly larger than theoretical predictions.

## Key claim

The enhancement of tunneling probability due to radiation coupling (virtual and real photon emission during tunneling) is extremely small and does not resolve the longstanding discrepancy where observed electron screening energies are significantly larger than theoretical predictions based on the adiabatic approximation.

## Method

The authors employed a path-integral formalism for multi-dimensional tunneling to eliminate the radiation field and obtain the influence functional, which was evaluated in the semi-classical approximation along a classical tunneling path from the outer to the inner turning points. The dipole approximation was used, and the mass renormalization divergence was separated out via partial integration. The enhancement factor was computed numerically for several fusion reactions using the pure Coulomb potential from the outer turning point to an inner turning point \(R_0\).

## Key numbers

- For the \(^3\)He(d,p)\(^4\)He reaction at \(E_{\rm min}=5.88\) keV with \(R_0=4.3\) fm, the enhancement factor \(f-1 = 2.744 \times 10^{-7}\).
- For the D(\(^3\)He,p)\(^4\)He reaction at \(E_{\rm min}=5.38\) keV with \(R_0=4.3\) fm, \(f-1 = 2.731 \times 10^{-7}\).
- For the \(^6\)Li(p,\(\alpha\))\(^3\)He reaction at \(E_{\rm min}=10.74\) keV with \(R_0=3.0\) fm, \(f-1 = 3.758 \times 10^{-6}\).
- For the \(^7\)Li(p,\(\alpha\))\(^4\)He reaction at \(E_{\rm min}=12.70\) keV with \(R_0=4.3\) fm, \(f-1 = 3.598 \times 10^{-6}\).
- For the \(^{10}\)B(p,\(\alpha\))\(^7\)Be reaction at \(E_{\rm min}=18.70\) keV with \(R_0=3.3\) fm, \(f-1 = 6.095 \times 10^{-6}\).
- For the \(^{11}\)B(p,\(\alpha\))\(^8\)Be reaction at \(E_{\rm min}=16.70\) keV with \(R_0=2.0\) fm, \(f-1 = 1.169 \times 10^{-5}\).
- The enhancement factor never exceeds \(10^{-3}\%\) for any reaction considered.

## Context

This work extends the study of effects beyond electron screening by Balantekin, Bertulani, and Hussein (1997), who found that vacuum polarization, relativity, bremsstrahlung, and atomic polarization were all too small to explain the screening discrepancy. It directly re-examines the suggestion by Flambaum and Zelevinsky (1999) that virtual photon emission during tunneling could increase penetrability, but uses a more exact path-integral approach without their closure approximation. The result shows the radiation correction is even smaller than the vacuum polarization effect, leaving the large electron screening problem as an open question for future work.
