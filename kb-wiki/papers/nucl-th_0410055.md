# On the double and triple-humped fission barriers and half-lives of actinide elements

> The deformation barriers standing in the quasi-molecular shape path have been determined in the actinide region within a macroscopic-microscopic energy derived from a generalized liquid drop model, the algebraic droplet model shell corrections and analytic expressions for the pairing energies. Double and triple-humped fission barriers appear. The second barrier corresponds to the transition from one-body shapes to two touching ellipsoids. The third minimum and third peak, when they exist, come from shell rearrangements in the deformed fragment. The shape of the other almost magic one is close to the sphere. The barrier heights agreewith the experimental results, the energy of the second minimum being a little too high. The predicted half-lives follow the experimental data trend.

## Key claim

The authors claim that double and triple-humped fission barriers in actinide nuclei can be reproduced using a macroscopic-microscopic energy derived from a generalized liquid drop model (GLDM) with analytic shell and pairing corrections, but the energy of the second minimum is systematically a little too high compared to experimental data, and the third barrier appears only in asymmetric decay paths for specific nuclei.

## Method

The authors calculated deformation barriers along a quasi-molecular shape path using a macroscopic-microscopic energy: the macroscopic part came from the generalized liquid drop model (GLDM) including volume, surface, Coulomb, nuclear proximity, and rotational energies; the microscopic part used algebraic droplet model shell corrections and analytic pairing energy expressions. One-body shapes were described by two joined elliptic lemniscatoids, while two-body shapes were modeled as two coaxial ellipsoids with variable deformations. The barrier penetrability and spontaneous fission half-lives were computed using the action integral with a phenomenological inertia B(r) related to the reduced mass.

## Key numbers

- For two spherical Kr and Ba nuclei at contact, the proximity energy reaches -43 MeV.
- The radius of the compound nucleus is defined as R0 = (1.28 A^(1/3) - 0.76 + 0.8 A^(-1/3)) fm, giving R0 = 5.3 fm and r0 = 1.15 fm for 98Zr, and R0 = 7.5 fm and r0 = 1.18 fm for 255Fm.
- The assault frequency used for half-life calculations is nu0 = 10^20 s^(-1).
- For 232U decaying to 134Te + 98Zr, the experimental half-life is 2.5 x 10^21 s while the theoretical value is 3.6 x 10^16 s.
- For 256Fm decaying to 128Sn + 128Sn, the experimental half-life is 1.0 x 10^4 s while the theoretical value is 45 s.
- For 256No decaying to 128Sb + 128Sb, the experimental half-life is 110 s while the theoretical value is 0.9 x 10^(-2) s.
- The inner and outer fission barrier heights are almost constant at 5-6 MeV from Th to Am isotopes.

## Context

This work extends the generalized liquid drop model previously developed by Royer and collaborators (Royer 1984, 2002, 2003) to include ellipsoidal deformations of fission fragments and their associated shell and pairing energies, going beyond the two-separated-sphere approximation. It builds on the pioneering work of Blons et al. (1984) who identified hyperdeformed states in 231,233Th, and on the predictions of a third hyperdeformed minimum by Moller et al. (1972) and later theoretical approaches (Bengtsson 1987, Bhanja 1989, Cwiok 1994). The results enable rapid calculation of multiple-humped fission barriers and half-lives that can be integrated into larger reaction codes such as Fluka, Gnash, or Talys for applications in nuclear waste transmutation and accelerator-driven systems.
