# Two-neutrino double beta decay of 76Ge within deformed QRPA: A new suppression mechanism

> The effect of deformation on the two-neutrino double decay (2nbb-decay) for ground state transition 76Ge -> 76Se is studied in the framework of the deformed QRPA with separable Gamow-Teller residual interaction. A new suppression mechanism of the 2nbb-decay matrix element based on the difference in deformations of the initial and final nuclei is included. An advantage of this suppression mechanism in comparison with that associated with ground state correlations is that it allows a simultaneous description of the single beta and the 2nbb-decay. By performing a detail calculation of the 2nbb-decay of 76Ge, it is found that the states of intermediate nucleus lying in the region of the Gamow-Teller resonance contribute significantly to the matrix element of this process.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a difference in the quadrupole deformation between the initial nucleus (76Ge) and the final nucleus (76Se) acts as a new suppression mechanism for the two-neutrino double beta decay matrix element, which allows a simultaneous description of single beta and double beta decay. This suppression is conditional on the magnitude of the deformation difference, with the effect being strongest when the two nuclei have significantly different shapes.

## Method

The authors performed deformed proton-neutron Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (pn-QRPA) calculations using a separable Gamow-Teller residual interaction with both particle-hole and particle-particle terms, based on a deformed axially-symmetric Woods-Saxon potential. They solved the QRPA equations separately for K=0 and K=±1 states to generate the intermediate nucleus 76As states, then computed the double Gamow-Teller matrix element M²ν_GT for the ground state transition 76Ge → 76Se as a function of the quadrupole deformation parameters β₂ of both nuclei and the particle-particle interaction strength κ.

## Key numbers

- The experimental half-life of 76Ge from the Heidelberg-Moscow experiment is T²ν₁/₂ = [1.55 ± 0.01 (stat) +0.19/-0.15 (syst)] × 10²¹ years; the average value recommended by Barabash is T²ν₁/₂ = 1.43 +0.19/-0.07 × 10²¹ years.
- The deduced experimental nuclear matrix element is |M_GT²ν-exp.| = 0.138 MeV⁻¹ (assuming g_A = 1.25) or 0.216 MeV⁻¹ (assuming g_A = 1.0).
- The kinematical factor for 76Ge is G²ν = 1.49 × 10⁻²⁰ year⁻¹ MeV².
- The BCS overlap factor between 76Ge and 76Se drops to 0.009 when using the deformation parameters from relativistic mean field theory (β₂ = 0.157 for 76Ge, β₂ = -0.244 for 76Se), but increases to 0.61 when changing 76Se from oblate to prolate (β₂ = 0.244).
- The pairing gaps used are: for 76Ge Δ_p = 1.561 MeV and Δ_n = 1.535 MeV; for 76Se Δ_p = 1.751 MeV and Δ_n = 1.710 MeV.
- The recommended particle-hole strength is χ = 5.2/A⁰·⁷ MeV (χ = 0.25 MeV for A=76) and the particle-particle strength κ = 0.58/A⁰·⁷ MeV, with κ treated as a free variable.

## Context

This work extends earlier spherical pn-QRPA calculations of double beta decay by Vogel (1986), Civitarese (1987), and Muto (1989) to include deformation degrees of freedom, and builds on the deformed QRPA formalism developed by Krumlinde (1998) and Sarriguren (1998, 2001). The new suppression mechanism based on deformation difference contrasts with the earlier suppression mechanism associated with ground state correlations in spherical QRPA. The results enable more reliable predictions for 76Ge, which is a prominent isotope for ongoing and planned double beta decay experiments, and suggest that accurate knowledge of nuclear deformation is essential for calculating double beta decay matrix elements for other candidates such as 100Mo, 130Te, and 136Xe.
