# A new field theoretic method for the virial expansion

> We develop a graphical method for computing the virial expansion coefficients for a nonrelativistic quantum field theory. As an example we compute the third virial coefficient b3 for unitary fermions, a nonperturbative system. By calculating several graphs and performing an extrapolation, we arrive at b3 =-0.2930, within 0.7% of a recent computation b3 = -0.29095295 by Liu, Hu and Drummond, which involved summing 10,000 energy levels for three unitary fermions in a harmonic trap.

## Key claim

The authors develop a graphical method for computing virial expansion coefficients in nonrelativistic quantum field theory, and demonstrate it by computing the third virial coefficient b3 for unitary fermions to within 0.7% of a prior high-precision computation, though they note their estimated error is significantly smaller than the actual discrepancy with that result.

## Method

The method combines two innovations: a Poisson resummation of the Matsubara frequency sum that directly yields a fugacity expansion in terms of "chronograph" diagrams (worldline loops wrapping around compact Euclidian time), and the use of a dimer auxiliary field whose propagator is constructed to exactly reproduce the two-body scattering phase shift. For b3, the authors compute a series of loop integrals I_n for n=1 through 8, then perform a large-n extrapolation to sum the infinite series.

## Key numbers

* Free fermion contribution to b3: b3^(1) = 1/(9√3)
* I_1 = 2/(3√3 π), I_2 = 8/(9√3 π) (computed analytically)
* Large-n extrapolation fit parameters: c0 = 0.1955 ± 0.0011, c1 = -0.1943 ± 0.0044
* Interacting contribution to b3: b3^(3) = -0.3573 ± 0.0005
* Full third virial coefficient: b3 = -0.29315 ± 0.0005
* Comparison value from Liu, Hu and Drummond (2009): b3 = -0.29095295
* Discrepancy between the two results: 0.0021

## Context

This work extends the approach of Bedaque and Rupak (2002) and Rupak (2007) for computing virial coefficients using field theory techniques. The result for b3 agrees with the high-precision spectral calculation of Liu, Hu and Drummond (2009), which solved for 10,000 energy levels of three unitary fermions in a harmonic trap, and with experimental results from experiments on ultracold atomic gases. The method enables direct field-theoretic computation of virial coefficients without solving the Schrodinger equation, and could be applied to other strongly interacting systems including nucleons.
