# Spin hydrodynamics on a hyperbolic expanding background

> We study relativistic spin hydrodynamics on the hyperbolic $κ=-1$ flow background recently identified by Grozdanov. This background corresponds to an $SO(2,1)$-invariant, transversely expanding solution with finite spacetime support in Minkowski space, in contrast to the well-known Gubser flow $(κ=+1)$ which possesses $SO(3)$ symmetry and infinite transverse extent. Working within the formulation of perfect-fluid spin hydrodynamics, we derive the exact evolution equations for all spin components of the spin potential on the $κ=-1$ background. We find that the enhanced early-time expansion rate and the presence of a causal edge lead to a stronger localization of spin dynamics compared to the Gubser case. Remarkably, the azimuthal component of the spin potential oscillates as it decays in the forward lightcone, in stark contrast to the Gubser flow. Thus, our results establish the $κ=-1$ flow as a distinct and physically meaningful benchmark for studying spin dynamics in expanding relativistic fluids with finite spacetime support.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the $\kappa=-1$ hyperbolic flow background, recently identified by Grozdanov (2025), produces qualitatively different spin dynamics compared to the well-known Gubser ($\kappa=+1$) flow, with the azimuthal component of the spin potential oscillating as it decays in the forward lightcone, a feature absent in the Gubser case, but this result is derived within the perfect-fluid spin hydrodynamic formulation under the assumption of small spin potential where back-reaction on the hydrodynamic background is neglected.

## Method
The authors solved the conservation equations for net baryon number and energy-momentum for a perfect fluid on the $\kappa=-1$ background in de Sitter coordinates, obtaining analytic solutions for temperature, chemical potential, and flow velocity. They then derived and solved the six coupled evolution equations for the spin potential components from angular momentum conservation using the de Groot–van Leeuwen–van Weert (GLW) spin tensor, treating the spin sector perturbatively so it does not feed back into the background evolution.

## Key numbers
- Temperature evolution: $\hat{T} = \hat{T}_0 \sinh^{-2/3}{\rho}$, with initial condition $\hat{T}_0 = 1.2$ at de Sitter time $\rho_0 = 0.5$, corresponding to $T(\tau_0 = 1\ \mathrm{fm}, r=0) = 1.2\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$ for $q = 1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$
- Expansion scalar in de Sitter coordinates: $\hat{\theta} = 2\coth{\rho}$
- Energy density solution: $\hat{\cE} = \hat{\cE}_0 \sinh^{-8/3}{\rho}$
- Net baryon density solution: $\hat{\cN} = \hat{\cN}_0 \sinh^{-2}{\rho}$
- Analytic solution for radial spin component $\hat{a}_R$ in massless limit: $\hat{a}_R = \hat{a}_R^0 \sinh^{5/6}{\rho}$
- Analytic solution for radial spin component $\hat{b}_R$ in massless limit: $\hat{b}_R = \hat{b}_R^0 \sinh^{-7/6}{\rho}$

## Context
This work extends the study of spin hydrodynamics from the boost-invariant, transversely homogeneous setting of Florkowski et al. (2019) and the Gubser-expanding background of Singh et al. (2020) to the newly identified $\kappa=-1$ flow of Grozdanov (2025). The $\kappa=-1$ background, with its $SO(2,1)$ symmetry and finite spacetime support bounded by a causal edge, provides a qualitatively different benchmark for spin dynamics compared to the $SO(3)$-symmetric Gubser flow, and may enable phenomenological descriptions of spin polarization in high-energy heavy-ion collisions with finite droplet-like geometry.
