# Application of a coupled-channel Complex Scaling Method with Feshbach projection to the $K^-pp$ system

> Kaonic nuclei (nuclear system with anti-kaons) have been an interesting subject in hadron and strange nuclear physics, because the strong attraction between anti-kaon and nucleon might bring exotic properties to that system. In this article, we investigate $K^-pp$ as a prototype of kaonic nuclei. Here, $K^-pp$ is a three-body resonant state in the $\bar{K}NN$-$πYN$ coupled channels. ($Y$=$Λ$, $Σ$) To treat resonant states in a coupled-channel system properly, we propose newly a coupled-channel complex scaling method combined with the Feshbach projection (ccCSM+Feshbach method). In this method, the Feshbach projection is realized with help of so-called the extended closure relation held in the complex scaling method, and a complicated coupled-channel problem is reduced to a simple single-channel problem which one can treat easily. First, we confirm that the ccCSM+Feshbach method completely reproduces results of a full coupled-channel calculation in case of two-body $\bar{K}N$-$πY$ system. We then proceed to study of three-body $\bar{K}NN$-$πYN$ system, and successfully find solutions of the $K^-pp$ resonance by imposing self-consistency for the complex $\bar{K}N$ energy. Obtained binding energy of $K^-pp$ is well converged around 27 MeV, with an energy-dependent $\bar{K}N$(-$πY$) potential based on the chiral SU(3) theory, independently of ansatz for the self-consistency. This binding energy is small as ones reported in earlier studies based on chiral models. The decay width of $K^-pp$ strongly depends on the ansatz. We calculate also the correlation density of $NN$ and $\bar{K}N$ pairs by using the obtained complex-scaled wave function of the $K^-pp$ resonance. Effect of the repulsive core of $NN$ potential and survival of $Λ^*$ resonance are confirmed.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a coupled-channel complex scaling method combined with Feshbach projection (ccCSM+Feshbach) to reduce the three-body $\bar{K}NN$-$\pi YN$ coupled-channel problem to a single $\bar{K}NN$ channel, and finds that the $K^-pp$ resonance binding energy converges around 27 MeV independently of the self-consistency ansatz, while the decay width depends strongly on which ansatz (field picture or particle picture) is used.

## Method

The authors construct an effective $\bar{K}N$ potential by eliminating the $\pi Y$ channels via Feshbach projection, using the extended closure relation of the complex scaling method to represent the $Q$-space Green function with a finite set of Gaussian basis functions. They then solve the three-body $\bar{K}NN$ system as a single-channel problem with complex scaling, imposing self-consistency for the complex $\bar{K}N$ energy using two ansatze (field picture and particle picture) to handle the energy dependence of the chiral SU(3)-based potential. The trial wave function is expanded with correlated Gaussian basis functions that respect the antisymmetrization of the two nucleons.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of $K^-pp$: well converged around 27 MeV, using an energy-dependent $\bar{K}N$(-$\pi Y$) potential based on chiral SU(3) theory.
- For the $I=0$ $\bar{K}N$-$\pi\Sigma$ two-body system, the ccCSM+Feshbach method reproduces the full ccCSM resonance pole at ($B(\bar{K}N)=17.150$ MeV, $\Gamma/2=16.608$ MeV) with values ($17.156$ MeV, $16.611$ MeV) when $\bar{K}N$ is the $P$ space.
- For the lower pole of the double-pole $\Lambda(1405)$, the ccCSM+Feshbach gives ($B(\bar{K}N)=37.954$ MeV, $\Gamma/2=135.943$ MeV) with $\bar{K}N$ as $P$ space, agreeing with the full ccCSM result ($38.128$ MeV, $136.166$ MeV).
- The $\bar{K}N$ scattering length for $I=0$ from ccCSM+Feshbach is $-1.700+0.681i$ fm, identical to the full ccCSM result.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of coupled-channel complex scaling to the two-body $\bar{K}N$-$\pi Y$ system (Doté et al., 2014) and addresses the three-body $K^-pp$ system that has been studied with variational approaches (Kpp:DHW, Kpp:BGL) and Faddeev-AGS methods (Kpp:IKS). The new method enables treating $K^-pp$ as a genuine resonant state (Gamow state) rather than using a bound-state approximation with perturbative width estimation as in earlier variational studies. The obtained binding energy around 27 MeV is small compared to predictions from phenomenological potentials (which give binding energies exceeding 100 MeV) and is consistent with earlier chiral-model-based studies.
