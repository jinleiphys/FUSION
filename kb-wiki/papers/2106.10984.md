# Universal many-body diffusion from momentum dephasing

> The open dynamics of quantum many-body systems involve not only the exchange of energy, but also of other conserved quantities, such as momentum. This leads to additional decoherence, which may have a profound impact in the dynamics. Motivated by this, we consider a many-body system subject to total momentum dephasing and show that under very general conditions this leads to a diffusive component in the dynamics of any local density, even far from equilibrium. Such component will usually have an intricate interplay with the unitary dynamics. To illustrate this, we consider the case of a superfluid and show that momentum dephasing introduces a damping in the sound-wave dispersion relation, similar to that predicted by the Navier-Stokes equation for ordinary fluids. Finally, we also study the effects of dephasing in linear response, and show that it leads to a universal additive contribution to the diffusion constant, which can be obtained from a Kubo formula.

# Universal many-body diffusion from momentum dephasing

## Key claim

Under very general conditions, dephasing of the total momentum in an open quantum many-body system leads to a diffusive component in the dynamics of any local density, even in states arbitrarily far from thermodynamic equilibrium; the contribution of momentum dephasing to the diffusion constant is additive and universal, and can be obtained from a Kubo formula.

## Method

The authors model a quantum many-body system coupled to an environment via a Lindblad master equation with a dissipator that dephases the total momentum operator P (the generator of spatial translations). They derive the evolution equation for the expectation value of any local density operator, obtaining an exact diffusion-type term proportional to the dephasing strength σ². They then analyze three specific regimes: the strong-dephasing limit yielding a standard diffusion equation; a superfluid described by a modified Gross-Pitaevskii equation with a diffusion term; and linear response theory around equilibrium, where they compute the retarded Green's function and extract the total diffusion constant.

## Key numbers

- The effective diffusion constant from momentum dephasing is D_deph = ħ²σ²/2, where σ² is the isotropic dephasing rate.
- For a superfluid with contact repulsion U and background density n, the dispersion relation for excitations is ω(k) = ±k √(nU/m + (ħk/2m)²) − i (ħ²σ²/2) k²; in the long-wavelength limit this becomes ω ≈ ±c_s k − i γ_s k²/2 with γ_s = ħ²σ².
- The sound speed in the superfluid is c_s = √(nU/m).
- In linear response, the total diffusion constant satisfies D = D_uni + D_deph, where D_uni comes from the standard Kubo formula for the unitary system.
- The Kubo formula for the total diffusion constant reads lim_{ω→0} lim_{k→0} (iω G(ω,k)/k²) = lim_{ω→0} lim_{k→0} (iω G_uni(ω,k)/k²) + (ħ²σ²/2) χ, where χ = (∂n/∂μ)_T is the thermodynamic susceptibility.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of how decoherence from conserved quantities, specifically momentum, drives diffusive behavior in quantum many-body systems, complementing prior studies of boundary-driven dephasing of local density operators by Asadian (2013), Znidaric (2010a), and Landi (2021). It also connects to the broader literature on hydrodynamic emergence in systems far from equilibrium, such as the quark-gluon plasma studied by Heinz (2013) and Florkowski (2017). The results suggest that momentum dephasing introduces a Navier-Stokes-like damping in superfluids, enabling experimental tests with ultracold atoms and potential applications to open quantum systems in quantum chromodynamics.
