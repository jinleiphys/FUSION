# Elastic and dynamic form factors of an atomic nucleus in the shell model with correction for the center-of-mass motion

> Analytical expressions for the elastic and dynamic form factors (FFs) are derived in the shell model (SM) with a potential well of finite depth. The consideration takes into account the motion of the target-nucleus center of mass (CM). Explanation is suggested for a simultaneous shrinking of the density and momentum distributions of nucleons in nuclei. The convenient working formulae are given to handle the expectation values of relevant multiplicative operators in case of the 1s-1p shell nuclei.

## Key claim
Analytical expressions for elastic and dynamic form factors are derived in the shell model with a finite-depth potential well, incorporating center-of-mass motion corrections. The authors show that the simultaneous shrinking of both density and momentum distributions arises from the non-unitary transformation to relative coordinates, not merely from a renormalization of the oscillator parameter.

## Method
The authors start from the shell model wave function and apply the projection procedure of Ref. [4] to obtain a translationally invariant intrinsic state. They then derive explicit formulas for the elastic form factor F(q) and dynamic form factor S(q,τ) using operator techniques, expressing them in terms of overlap integrals of single-particle orbitals. For the 1s-1p shell nuclei, they evaluate these expressions using the harmonic oscillator model and extend the formalism to arbitrary single-particle wave functions from finite-depth potentials like Woods-Saxon or Hartree-Fock.

## Key numbers
- For 1s-1p shell nuclei in the HO model: F₀(q) = (1 - (A-4)/(6A) q²r₀²) exp(-¼q²r₀²) and S₀(q,τ) = (1 - (A-4)/(6A) b²τ²) exp(-¼b²τ²), where b = p₀q/m and p₀ = 1/r₀
- The intrinsic density distribution: ρ(r) = π^(-3/2) r̄₀^(-3) [3/(A-1) + (2/3)(A-4)/(A-1) r²/r̄₀²] exp(-r²/r̄₀²) with r̄₀ = √[(A-1)/A] r₀
- The intrinsic momentum distribution: η(p) = π^(-3/2) p̄₀^(-3) [3/(A-1) + (2/3)(A-4)/(A-1) p²/p̄₀²] exp(-p²/p̄₀²) with p̄₀ = √[(A-1)/A] p₀
- The uncertainty relation for intrinsic coordinates: ⟨r²⟩⟨p²⟩ ≥ (9/4)[(A-1)/A]², compared to ⟨r²⟩₀⟨p²⟩₀ ≥ 9/4 for the uncorrected shell model
- For the HO model with closed shells: ⟨r²⟩₀⟨p²⟩₀ = κ² ≥ 9/4 and ⟨r²⟩⟨p²⟩ = κ²[(A-1)/A]²[1 + (κ-3/2)/(κ(A-1))]² ≥ (9/4)[(A-1)/A]²

## Context
This work extends the center-of-mass correction method developed in Ref. [5] (which treated only the 1s⁴ configuration) to more complex single-particle configurations in the 1s-1p shell. It builds on the projection procedure of Ref. [4] and the Bethe-Rose-Elliot-Skyrme theorem (Refs. [6,7]) for harmonic oscillator wave functions. The results enable calculation of CM-corrected elastic and quasifree electron scattering cross sections for nuclei beyond ⁴He, and allow study of deviations from the canonical Tassie-Barker factor exp(q²r₀²/4A) when using finite-depth potentials.
