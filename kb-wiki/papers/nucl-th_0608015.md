# Suppression of p-Wave Baryons in Quark Recombination

> We show that the observed suppression of the $Λ(1520)/Λ$ ratio in central Au + Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider can be naturally understood in the constituent quark recombination model.

# Suppression of p-Wave Baryons in Quark Recombination

## Key claim
The observed suppression of the Λ(1520)/Λ ratio in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC can be explained by the internal p-wave structure of the Λ(1520) within the constituent quark recombination model, without invoking final-state interactions. The suppression is a formation effect that is insensitive to the precise choice of model parameters and is predicted to be independent of baryon momentum.

## Method
The authors used a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with Gaussian wave packets to describe quarks just before hadronization, assuming a thermal distribution at T = 160 MeV and a surface region with Gaussian thickness profile. They calculated the sudden transition matrix elements from an uncorrelated three-quark state to the Λ (s-wave) and Λ(1520) (p-wave) states, then performed thermal averaging and integration over the hadronization zone analytically to obtain the ratio of transition probabilities.

## Key numbers
- The suppression factor for p-wave baryon formation is calculated to be in the range 0.5–0.6 for realistic parameter values (b ≈ 0.6 fm, T = 160 MeV, m = 330 MeV, a = 0.5 fm)
- The ratio of transition probabilities tends to 1 in the limit R,T → ∞, confirming the result of Fries et al. (2003)
- The K*/K ratio demands a dense hadronic phase duration Δτ ≈ 2.5 fm/c, while the Λ*/Λ ratio would require Δτ ≈ 9 fm/c if interpreted via final-state interactions
- Two-pion intensity interferometry measurements give Δτ values in the range 2–3 fm/c

## Context
This work extends the recombination model by incorporating realistic internal wave functions of baryons, differing from the quark-diquark model of Zimanyi (2004) which used a direct reaction approach. The paper addresses the discrepancy noted by Adams et al. (2006) between statistical model predictions and measured hadron yield ratios at RHIC, where the Λ(1520)/Λ ratio shows stronger suppression than the K*/K ratio. The mechanism predicts that all p-wave hadrons should show similar suppression, though experimental identification of other p-wave excitations in heavy ion collisions may be difficult.
