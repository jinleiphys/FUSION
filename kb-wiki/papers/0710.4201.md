# The role of energy-momentum conservation in emission of Cherenkov gluons

## Key claim
The standard Cherenkov formula for emission angle must be modified for hadronic reactions because recoil effects impose an upper limit on the energy of the emitted gluon at a given angle, and the nuclear refractive index extracted from measured cone angles is systematically higher than the value obtained from the uncorrected formula.

## Method
The author applies energy-momentum conservation to the emission of Cherenkov gluons by partons in nuclear media, deriving a modified relation between the cone angle and the nuclear refractive index. The analysis treats the emitted gluon as subject to refraction while assuming the high-energy emitter parton experiences negligible refraction, and derives expressions for the allowed gluon energy range and the corrected refractive index as functions of the measured cone angle.

## Key numbers
- At cone angle θ = 60°, the gluon energy is bounded by ω < 0.134E; for an away-side jet energy E = 5 GeV, this gives ω < 0.67 GeV, restricting emission to the low-mass wing of the ρ-resonance.
- At cone angle θ = 70°, the gluon energy is bounded by ω < 0.06E; for E = 5 GeV, this gives ω < 0.3 GeV, allowing only the very low energy tail of the ρ-resonance.
- At θ = 60°, the corrected refractive index is n = 2(1 + 1.5ω/E) < 2.4.
- At θ = 70°, the corrected refractive index is n = 2.9(1 + 3.8ω/E) < 3.6.
- The observed cone angles in RHIC experiments vary between 60° and 70°.

## Context
This work extends the original Cherenkov formula derived by Ginzburg (1940) for photons in ordinary media, applying it to gluon emission in nuclear matter as first proposed by Dremin (1979, 1981). The correction term, negligible for photons because ω ≪ E and n ≈ 1, becomes essential for Cherenkov gluons whose energies can be comparable to the parton-emitter energy. The results provide a framework for interpreting the ring-like structure observed around away-side jets in RHIC experiments by Adare (2006), Abelev (2006), and others, enabling extraction of the nuclear refractive index and its energy dependence from experimental data.
