# The electron in three-dimensional momentum space

> We study the electron as a system composed by an electron and a photon, using lowest order perturbation theory. We derive the leading-twist transverse-momentum-dependent distribution functions for both the electron and photon in the dressed electron, thereby offering a three-dimensional description of the dressed electron in momentum space. To obtain the distribution functions, we apply both the formalism of light-front wave function overlap representation and the diagrammatic approach. We perform the calculations both in light-cone gauge and Feynman gauge, and we present a detailed discussion of the role of the Wilson lines to obtain gauge-independent results. We provide numerical results and plots for many of the computed distributions.

## Key claim

The authors compute the leading-twist transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) distribution functions for both the electron and photon inside a dressed electron at lowest order in QED perturbation theory, but explicitly exclude the endpoint \(x=1, \mathbf{k}_\perp = \mathbf{0}\) from their analysis, noting that this region would require a proper regularization procedure beyond the scope of the work.

## Method

The authors use two complementary approaches: a diagrammatic method in Feynman gauge (including eikonal propagators for the gauge link) and a light-front wave function (LFWF) overlap representation in light-cone gauge. They calculate the eight leading-twist TMDs for the electron parton and the corresponding TMDs for the photon parton, working at order \(\alpha\) and excluding self-energy and virtual vertex corrections at the endpoint. The equivalence of results between Feynman and light-cone gauges is demonstrated, with explicit treatment of Wilson lines to ensure gauge invariance.

## Key numbers

- The unpolarized TMD for the electron parton is \(f_1^e(x,\mathbf{k}_\perp) = \frac{e^2}{(2\pi)^3} \frac{\mathbf{k}_\perp^2(1+x^2) + m^2(1-x)^4}{(1-x)[\mathbf{k}_\perp^2 + m^2(1-x)^2]^2}\).
- The longitudinally polarized TMD is \(g_{1L}^e(x,\mathbf{k}_\perp) = \frac{e^2}{(2\pi)^3} \frac{\mathbf{k}_\perp^2(1+x^2) - m^2(1-x)^4}{(1-x)[\mathbf{k}_\perp^2 + m^2(1-x)^2]^2}\).
- The transverse spin-dependent TMD \(g_{1T}^e(x,\mathbf{k}_\perp) = -\frac{2e^2}{(2\pi)^3} m^2 \frac{x(1-x)}{[\mathbf{k}_\perp^2 + m^2(1-x)^2]^2}\).
- The T-odd Sivers function \(f_{1T}^{\perp e}\) and Boer-Mulders function \(h_1^{\perp e}\) are both zero at order \(\alpha\) for \(x \neq 1, \mathbf{k}_\perp \neq \mathbf{0}\).
- The function \(\varphi(x,\mathbf{k}_\perp^2) = \frac{e \sqrt{x(1-x)}}{[(1-x)^2 m^2 + \mathbf{k}_\perp^2]}\) appears as a common factor in the TMD expressions.

## Context

This work extends the QED analogue of the parton model to three-dimensional momentum space, providing explicit TMDs for the dressed electron that mirror the leading-order quark-target model results of Meissner et al. (2007) up to the QCD color factor. The study enables future investigations of spin-orbit correlations and angular momentum decomposition in QED as a testing ground for formal aspects of QCD TMDs, including the role of Wilson lines and gauge invariance. The results also complement earlier work on the electron's impact-parameter distributions from GPDs by Hoyer (2009) and Miller (2014).
