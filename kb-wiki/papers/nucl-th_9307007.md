# Color Diffusion and Conductivity in a Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Color diffusion is shown to be an important dissipative property of quark-gluon plasmas that rapidly damps collective color modes. We derive the characteristic color relaxation time scale, $t_c\approx (3α_s T \log(m_E/m_M ))^{-1}$, showing its sensitivity to the ratio of the static color electric and magnetic screening masses. This leads to a surprisingly small color conductivity, $σ_c\approx 2 T/\log(m_E/m_M)$, which in fact vanishes in the semi-classical (1-loop) limit.

## Key claim

Color diffusion is a dissipative property of quark-gluon plasmas that rapidly damps collective color modes, leading to a surprisingly small color conductivity that vanishes in the semi-classical (1-loop) limit. The key result depends on the ratio of the static color electric and magnetic screening masses, with the color relaxation time scale given by \(t_c\approx (3\alpha_s T \log(m_E/m_M))^{-1}\).

## Method

The authors derive the color relaxation time by considering the motion of a classical colored particle (heavy quark) in a background Yang-Mills field, using Wong equations and a stochastic (random phase) ansatz for the ensemble average of field fluctuations. They work in the eikonal (hard thermal loop) limit, relate the field correlation function to the retarded gluon propagator via the fluctuation-dissipation theorem, and regulate the logarithmic divergence in the transverse contribution by introducing a nonperturbative magnetic mass cutoff. The color conductivity is then obtained from linear response theory, solving the kinetic equation for the adjoint color moment density.

## Key numbers

- Color relaxation time: \(t_c\approx (3\alpha_s T \log(m_E/m_M))^{-1}\), where \(m_E\) is the color electric screening mass and \(m_M\) is the color magnetic screening mass.
- Color conductivity: \(\sigma_c\approx 2T/\log(m_E/m_M)\), which vanishes in the semi-classical (1-loop) limit.
- Color plasmon frequency: \(\omega^2_{pl}=4\pi \alpha_s T^2(1+N_f/6)/3\).
- Momentum relaxation time (for comparison): \(t_p \approx (4\alpha_s^2 \ln(1/\alpha_s)T)^{-1}\), with \(t_c \ll t_p\) in the perturbative limit.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of transport properties in quark-gluon plasmas, which had focused on momentum relaxation processes for viscosity and thermal conductivity (Baym, 1990; Thoma, 1991), by deriving a new transport coefficient (color diffusion) with no abelian analog. The result contradicts earlier estimates that used the momentum relaxation time \(t_p\) to estimate color conductivity (Gyulassy and Escobar, 1993), showing instead that the color diffusion time \(t_c\) controls the conductivity and that collective color modes are over-damped. The paper enables further study of nonperturbative magnetic screening and finite-time effects in QGP transport theory, particularly for applications to ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions.
