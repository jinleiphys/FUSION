# Electromagnetic transitions with effective operators

> In the no-core shell model formalism we compute effective one- and two-body operators, using the Lee-Suzuki procedure within the two-body cluster approximation. We evaluate the validity of the latter through calculations in reduced model spaces. In particular, we test the results for the two-body system and find that indeed the effective operators in the reduced space reproduce the expectation values or transition strengths computed in the full space. On the other hand, the renormalization for operators in the case of 6Li is very weak, suggesting the need for higher-body clusters in computing the effective interaction.

# Electromagnetic transitions with effective operators

## Key claim

The two-body cluster approximation for effective operators in the no-core shell model (NCSM) reproduces exact expectation values and transition strengths in the two-body system, but in the many-body system (⁶Li) the renormalization of electromagnetic operators is very weak, suggesting that higher-body clusters are needed to compute effective interactions and operators reliably.

## Method

The authors apply the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation within the two-body cluster approximation (a=2) to compute effective one- and two-body operators in reduced model spaces. For the two-body system, they use the Argonne V8' interaction in a 10ħω full space and transform to a 2ħω model space, adding Q-space states one harmonic oscillator shell at a time to test convergence. For ⁶Li, they use a Gaussian interaction in an 8ħω full space and compute effective operators in 2ħΩ and 4ħΩ model spaces, comparing bare and effective operator results.

## Key numbers

- For the two-body system with Argonne V8' interaction: effective E2 and M1 transition strengths and kinetic energy expectation values in a 2ħω model space converge to the exact 10ħω full-space values when most Q-space states are included (Figure 1).
- ⁶Li in 2ħΩ model space: B(E2; 3₁⁺0 → 1₁⁺0) = 1.9315 with effective operator vs. 1.9512 with bare operator; full-space (8ħΩ) value = 1.5750.
- ⁶Li in 2ħΩ model space: B(M1; 0₁⁺1 → 1₁⁺0) = 5.3549 with effective operator vs. 5.3926 with bare operator; full-space value = 5.3346.
- ⁶Li in 2ħΩ model space: ⟨1₁⁺0|T_rel|1₁⁺0⟩ = 118.46 MeV with effective operator vs. 116.15 MeV with bare operator; full-space value = 135.84 MeV.
- ⁶Li in 4ħΩ model space: B(E2; 3₁⁺0 → 1₁⁺0) = 1.7548 with effective operator vs. 1.7575 with bare operator; full-space value = 1.5750.

## Context

This work extends the NCSM formalism for effective interactions (previously applied by Navratil et al. (1997) and others) to general one- and two-body operators, including electromagnetic transitions. Prior NCSM calculations had computed effective operators only for point-proton radius, relative kinetic energy, and NN pair density (Benchmark paper, 2000). The weak renormalization found for electromagnetic operators in ⁶Li contrasts with significant renormalization of the kinetic energy operator previously found in ⁴He (Benchmark, 2000) and ¹²C (unpublished). The results motivate future investigations of semi-leptonic operators at finite momentum transfer, relevant for rare isotope accelerator experiments and double-beta decay studies.
