# Neutron star properties with relativistic equations of state

> We study the properties of neutron stars adopting relativistic equations of state of neutron star matter, calculated in the framework of the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation for electrically charge neutral neutron star matter in beta-equilibrium. For higher densities more baryons (hyperons etc.) are included by means of the relativistic Hartree- or Hartree-Fock approximation. The special features of the different approximations and compositions are discussed in detail. Besides standard neutron star properties special emphasis is put on the limiting periods of neutron stars, for which the Kepler criterion and gravitation-reaction instabilities are considered. Furthermore the cooling behaviour of neutron stars is investigated, too. For comparison we give also the outcome for some nonrelativistic equations of state.

## Key claim

The paper constructs neutron star equations of state that are tied to microscopic relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) calculations at moderate densities, but must invoke an extrapolation hypothesis controlled by an additional parameter at higher densities where hyperons and delta baryons appear, because the behavior of the Dirac mass at high density is not known from first principles.

## Method

The authors compute equations of state for charge-neutral, beta-equilibrium neutron star matter using the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) approximation for densities up to 2-3 times nuclear saturation density, restricted to nucleons and leptons. For higher densities, where additional baryons (hyperons and delta resonances) become populated, they extend the EOS using the relativistic Hartree (RH) and relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) approximations, with parameters in the nucleonic sector adjusted to reproduce the RBHF results for asymmetric and neutron star matter. The hyperon and delta couplings are varied systematically, and the extrapolation to high density is controlled by a parameter alpha that modifies the sigma-meson self-interactions to avoid negative Dirac masses.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass obtained with the TM1 parameter set: 1.561 solar masses, with central energy density 740 MeV/fm^3
- For soft equations of state, the estimated maximum mass (Oppenheimer-Volkoff limit) is about 1.5 solar masses and the minimum rotational period is about 0.6 ms
- For stiff equations of state, the estimated maximum mass is about twice the solar mass and the limiting rotational period is larger than 1.5 ms
- The ratio 2M/(R c^2) for a typical neutron star is approximately 0.3
- The Dirac mass at saturation in the RBHF scheme is about 0.6 times the nucleon mass
- The hyperon potential depth in nuclear matter is approximately -30 MeV
- The onset of the Lambda hyperon for weak couplings (x_omegaH = 0.5) occurs at a baryon density of about 0.5 fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the microscopic RBHF approach of Brockmann and Machleidt (1990) to neutron star matter by incorporating hyperons and delta baryons at high densities, improving upon earlier relativistic mean-field treatments by Glendenning (1985) and Schaffner and Mishustin (1996) that used purely phenomenological interactions. The paper enables systematic comparison of neutron star properties (masses, radii, limiting periods, cooling) across different many-body approximations and hyperon coupling schemes, and highlights the need for observational constraints from millisecond pulsars to discriminate between stiff and soft equations of state.
