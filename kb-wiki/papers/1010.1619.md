# Correlations probed in direct two-nucleon removal reactions

> Final-state-exclusive momentum distributions of fast, forward travelling residual nuclei, following two nucleon removal from fast secondary radioactive beams of projectile nuclei, can and have now been measured. Assuming that the most important reaction mechanism is the sudden direct removal of a pair of nucleons from a set of relatively simple, active shell-model orbital configurations, such distributions were predicted to depend strongly on the total angular momentum I carried by the two nucleons - the final state spin for spin 0+ projectiles. The sensitivity of these now-accessible observables to specific details of the (correlated) two-nucleon wave functions is of importance. We clarify that it is the total orbital angular momentum L of the two nucleons that is the primary factor in determining the shapes and widths of the calculated momentum distributions. It follows that, with accurate measurements, this dependence upon the L make-up of the two-nucleon wave functions could be used to assess the accuracy of (shell- or many-body) model predictions of these two-nucleon configurations. By use of several tailored examples, with specific combinations of active two-nucleon orbitals, we demonstrate that more subtle structure aspects may be observed, allowing such reactions to probe and/or confirm the details of theoretical model wave functions.

## Key claim
The paper clarifies that the primary factor determining the shapes and widths of calculated momentum distributions in direct two-nucleon removal reactions is the total orbital angular momentum L of the removed pair, not the total angular momentum I as previously emphasized. This sensitivity to L could be used to assess the accuracy of shell-model or many-body predictions of two-nucleon configurations, provided accurate measurements are available.

## Method
The authors developed a formalism in the LS-coupling representation for residue momentum distributions following sudden direct two-nucleon removal from fast secondary beams at energies above 80 MeV per nucleon. They used eikonal reaction dynamics with elastic S-matrices calculated in the optical limit of Glauber's multiple scattering theory, assuming spin-independent nucleon S-matrices and a spectator core approximation. The two-nucleon overlaps were constructed from shell-model two-nucleon amplitudes (TNA) with proper antisymmetrization and angular momentum coupling, and the authors derived expressions showing the momentum distributions are incoherent in L, S, and T.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of single-nucleon removal reactions (Hansen 1996, Baumann 1998, Gade 2008) which showed momentum distributions are characteristic of the orbital angular momentum of the removed nucleon. It builds on fully-correlated two-nucleon removal models developed by Tostevin (2004) and Tostevin and Brown (2006), and on pure-configuration studies by Simpson (2009) that demonstrated sensitivity to final state spin I. The paper clarifies that the earlier emphasis on I was incomplete, and that L is the primary determinant of momentum distribution shapes. This enables future experiments to probe detailed shell-model wave function configurations, particularly in cases of weak mixing among orbitals, and to test predictions for states populated in two-nucleon removal from projectiles such as 12C, 26Si, and 54Ti.
