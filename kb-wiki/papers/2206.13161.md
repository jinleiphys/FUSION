# Diffractive deep inelastic scattering at NLO in the dipole picture: the $q \bar q g$ contribution

> We calculate the contribution from the $q \bar q g$ state production to the diffractive cross sections in deep inelastic scattering at high energy. The obtained cross section is finite by itself and a part of the full next-to-leading order result for the diffractive structure functions. We perform the calculation in exact kinematics in the eikonal limit, and show that the previously known high-$Q^2$ and large $M_X^2$ results for the structure functions can be extracted from our results in the appropriate limits. We furthermore discuss the steps required to obtain the full next-to-leading order results for the structure functions.

## Key claim
The paper calculates the finite, self-contained $q\bar q g$ contribution to diffractive deep inelastic scattering cross sections at next-to-leading order in the dipole picture, using exact kinematics in the eikonal limit without the large-$Q^2$ approximation used in prior work (Wüsthoff, 1997). This contribution is only a subset of the full NLO result, as virtual corrections and gluon emissions after the shockwave are deferred to future work.

## Method
The authors compute the diffractive cross section for a virtual photon scattering off a hadronic target by summing over $q\bar q g$ Fock states in the Color Glass Condensate framework. They use light-cone perturbation theory to derive the $\gamma^* \to q\bar q g$ wavefunctions for gluon emission before the shockwave (including instantaneous vertices), then integrate over phase space at fixed invariant mass $M_X$ of the diffractive system. The calculation treats the kinematics within the diffractive system exactly while keeping the interaction with the target eikonal, and the result is checked against known limits (large $Q^2$ and large $M_X^2$).

## Key numbers
- The diffractive structure functions $F_2^{\textrm{D}(3)}(\beta,Q^2,\xpom)$ and $F_L^{\textrm{D}(3)}(\beta,Q^2,\xpom)$ are expressed in terms of the virtual photon cross sections via $\xpom F_{T,L}^{\textrm{D}(4)} = \frac{Q^2}{4\pi^2 \alpha_{\mathrm{em}}} \frac{Q^2}{\beta} \frac{\mathrm{d} \sigma^{\mathrm{D}}_{\gamma^*_{T,L} + A \to M_X + A}}{\mathrm{d} M_X^2 \mathrm{d} t}$ (Eq. 3).
- The diffractive cross section is integrated over the squared momentum transfer $t$ and expressed as $\frac{\mathrm{d} \sigma^{\mathrm{D}}_{e + A \to M_X + p}}{\mathrm{d} \beta \mathrm{d} Q^2 \mathrm{d} \xpom} = \frac{2\pi \alpha_{\mathrm{em}}^2}{\beta Q^4} \left[1+(1-y)^2\right] \left[ F_2^{\textrm{D}(3)} - \frac{y^2}{1+(1-y)^2} F_L^{\textrm{D}(3)} \right]$ (Eq. 1).
- The invariant mass variable $\beta$ is defined as $\beta = Q^2/(M_X^2+Q^2)$, and $\xpom \approx (M_X^2+Q^2)/(W^2+Q^2)$ (Section 2).

## Context
This work extends the leading-order dipole picture of diffractive DIS (Golec-Biernat, 1999) by including the $q\bar q g$ final state, which dominates at large $M_X^2$ and high $Q^2$. It generalizes the "Wüsthoff result" (Wüsthoff, 1997) from a large-$Q^2$ approximation to exact kinematics, and provides a necessary ingredient for the full NLO calculation of diffractive structure functions. The results enable future numerical evaluations and comparisons with HERA data and predictions for the Electron-Ion Collider, while the remaining virtual corrections and emissions after the shockwave are left for subsequent work.
