# Measuring the $ϕ$ meson width in the medium from $p$ induced $ϕ$ production in nuclei

> We study the $A$ dependence of the $ϕ$ meson production cross section in proton nucleus reactios at energies just above threshold, which are accessible in an experimental facility like COSY. This $A$ dependence has two sources: the distortion of the incident proton and the absorption of the $ϕ$ in its way out of the nucleus. This second process reduces the cross section in about a factor two in heavy nuclei. Thus we show that the $A$ dependence of the cross section contains valuable information on the $ϕ$ width in the nuclear medium.

# Measuring the φ meson width in the medium from p induced φ production in nuclei

## Key claim

The A-dependence of the φ meson production cross section in proton-nucleus reactions just above threshold can be used to extract the φ meson width in nuclear matter, provided that the two-step mechanisms (which introduce uncertainties smaller than the width variation) are properly accounted for. The method is sensitive to the φ width in the medium to the level of 25% of the width used in the calculation.

## Method

The authors model φ production in pA collisions using a many-body approach with a local Fermi sea, accounting for Fermi motion of the initial nucleon and Pauli blocking of final nucleons. The distortion of the incoming proton and outgoing φ meson is treated with an eikonal approximation, and the imaginary part of the φ selfenergy in nuclear matter is taken from a chiral unitary model that modifies the K̅K decay channel in the medium. Two-step processes are evaluated, including mechanisms with Δ intermediate states where NN → NΔ is followed by ΔN → NNφ.

## Key numbers

- The φ medium width at rest at normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ is 24 MeV, taken from the chiral model of Oset (2001) and Dani (2003).
- The two-step process with Δ excitation benefits from a factor f_{πNΔ}/f_{πNN} = 2.13 in the amplitude, hence a factor 4.5 in the cross section relative to the nucleon-only mechanism.
- The energy loss of the proton in the first collision for two-step processes is ΔE ≃ 400 MeV.
- Calculations are performed for incident proton kinetic energies T_p = 2.7 GeV and T_p = 2.83 GeV, just above threshold.
- The normalized ratio R(^A X)/R(¹²C) is shown for 15 nuclei ranging from ¹²C to ²³⁸U.

## Context

This work extends the method previously applied to φ photoproduction in nuclei by Dani (2003), which is under experimental investigation at Spring8/Osaka (Imai, 2004). The approach differs from traditional methods that look for broadening of the φ width reconstructed from decay products, as done by Klingl (1998), Oset (2001), and Pal (2002). The proposed reaction avoids complications from coherent φ production that can obscure photoproduction results, and is amenable to experimental study at facilities like COSY. The results enable extraction of the φ width in the medium by comparing the calculated A-dependence curves with future experimental data.
