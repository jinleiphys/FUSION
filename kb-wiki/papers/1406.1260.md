# Flavor Structure of the Nucleon Sea

> We review the current status and future prospects on the subject of flavor structure of the nucleon sea. The flavor structure of the nucleon sea provides unique information on the non-perturbative aspects of strong interactions allowing stringent tests of various models on the partonic structures of the nucleons as well as lattice QCD calculations. The scope of this review covers the unpolarized, polarized, and the transverse-momentum dependent sea-quark distributions of the nucleons. While the main focus of this review is on the physics motivation and recent progress on the subject of the nucleon sea, we also discuss future prospects of addressing some outstanding issues on the flavor structure of the nucleon sea.

## Key claim

This review paper surveys the current understanding of the flavor structure of the nucleon sea, covering unpolarized, polarized, and transverse-momentum dependent sea-quark distributions, and argues that these distributions provide unique non-perturbative tests of strong-interaction models and lattice QCD, while acknowledging that many outstanding issues remain unresolved.

## Method

The authors conducted a comprehensive literature review of experimental measurements and theoretical models related to the flavor asymmetry of the nucleon sea. They examined results from deep inelastic scattering (DIS) experiments, Drell-Yan processes, and neutrino-induced charm production, comparing these data with predictions from models such as the meson cloud model, chiral quark soliton model, statistical parton model, and lattice QCD calculations.

## Key numbers

- The Gottfried sum rule violation was first observed by the New Muon Collaboration (NMC) in 1991, with a value significantly below 1/3, indicating a flavor asymmetry in the light quark sea.
- The NuSea (E866) Collaboration measured the anti-d/anti-u asymmetry in the nucleon sea via Drell-Yan process, with results published in 1998 and an improved measurement in 2001.
- The NuTeV Collaboration reported a precision measurement of dimuon production cross-sections in neutrino and anti-neutrino deep inelastic scattering at the Tevatron in 2001.
- The HERMES Collaboration measured parton distributions of strange quarks in the nucleon from charged-kaon production in deep-inelastic scattering on the deuteron in 2008, and provided a reevaluation in 2014.
- The ATLAS Collaboration determined the strange quark density of the proton from measurements of W and Z boson production cross sections at sqrt(s)=7 TeV in 2012.
- The CMS Collaboration measured associated W + charm production at sqrt(s)=7 TeV in 2014.

## Context

This review extends the work of earlier reviews on nucleon sea structure, such as those by Kumano (1998), Garvey and Peng (2001), and Vogt (2000), and covers recent developments including lattice QCD calculations by Lin et al. (2014) and the five-quark component analysis by Chang and Peng (2011). The paper enables future experimental programs at facilities such as the LHC and proposed electron-ion colliders to address open questions about the momentum dependence of sea quark distributions, the strange-antistrange asymmetry, and the role of intrinsic charm components.
