# Relativistic Quark Spin Coupling Effects in the Nucleon Electromagnetic Form Factors

> We investigate the effect of different forms of relativistic spin coupling of constituent quarks in the nucleon electromagnetic form factors. The four-dimensional integrations in the two-loop Feynman diagram are reduced to the null-plane, such that the light-front wave function is introduced in the computation of the form factors. The neutron charge form factor is very sensitive to different choices of spin coupling schemes, once its magnetic moment is fitted to the experimental value. The scalar coupling between two quarks is preferred by the neutron data, when a reasonable fit of the proton magnetic momentum is found.

## Key claim

The neutron charge form factor is highly sensitive to the choice of relativistic spin coupling between constituent quarks, and the scalar coupling of the quark pair (α=1) is preferred by neutron data when the neutron magnetic moment is fitted to its experimental value; however, a simultaneous fit of both nucleon magnetic moments and mean square radii with good precision is not possible.

## Method

The authors computed the nucleon electromagnetic form factors from two-loop triangle Feynman diagrams using an effective Lagrangian with a parameter α that dials between scalar (α=1), pseudoscalar (α=0), and mixed (α=1/2) spin couplings of the constituent quarks. The four-dimensional integrations were reduced to the null-plane (light-front) by performing Cauchy integrations over the k⁻ components of the spectator quarks, and a Gaussian light-front wave function was introduced with a size parameter β adjusted to reproduce the neutron magnetic moment of -1.91 μ_N. The quark mass was fixed at 220 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Neutron magnetic moment fixed to μ_n = -1.91 μ_N (used to set the size parameter β)
- For α=1 (scalar coupling): β = 562 MeV, ⟨r²⟩_n = -0.075 fm², ⟨r²⟩_p = 0.877 fm², μ_p = 3.11 μ_N
- For α=1/2 (mixed coupling): β = 664 MeV, ⟨r²⟩_n = -0.024 fm², ⟨r²⟩_p = 0.718 fm², μ_p = 3.05 μ_N
- For α=0 (pseudoscalar coupling): β = 661 MeV, ⟨r²⟩_n = 0.079 fm², ⟨r²⟩_p = 0.686 fm², μ_p = 2.90 μ_N
- Experimental values: ⟨r²⟩_n = -0.113 ± 0.005 fm², ⟨r²⟩_p = 0.66 ± 0.06 fm² (or 0.74 ± 0.02 fm²), μ_p = 2.79 μ_N
- The model is consistent with neutron magnetic form factor data below momentum transfers of 1 GeV/c, but deviates above that value

## Context

This work extends the light-front dynamics approach to nucleon form factors previously developed by Frederico and Miller (1992) and de Melo and Frederico (1997), and builds on the authors' own earlier study of the α=1 case (de Araujo et al., 1999). The scalar coupling (α=1) gives the best description of the neutron electric form factor, while the Bakamjian-Thomas formalism (Bakamjian and Thomas, 1953) is related but not equivalent to the α=1/2 case. The results suggest that constituent quark form factors (Cardarelli, Pace, Salme and Simula, 1995) would be needed to achieve a precise simultaneous fit of all nucleon observables.
