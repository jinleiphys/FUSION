# Probing a Fifth Force in Muonic Atoms through Lamb Shifts and Hyperfine Structure

> Motivated by the ATOMKI anomalies in 8Be and 4He transitions, we study X17-induced Lamb shifts and hyperfine splittings in muonic atoms with stable nuclei up to Z <= 15. The bound-state problem is solved within the Gaussian Expansion Method using a unified Hamiltonian that includes the standard electromagnetic baseline together with vector and pseudoscalar X17 exchange. The spin-independent Lamb shift is described by a coherent vector muon-nucleus interaction, while the spin-dependent hyperfine sector is built isotope by isotope from shell-model spin fractions. We find a clear complementarity between mediator hypotheses: the vector Lamb-shift signal grows toward heavier nuclei, the vector hyperfine scenario favors odd-N nuclei, and the pseudoscalar scenario favors odd-Z nuclei. Using a signal-to-precision ratio, we identify muonic deuterium, muonic helium-3 ion, and muonic helium-4 ion as the most promising near-term Lamb-shift probes among systems with existing precision benchmarks. For future spectroscopy, the largest vector Lamb-shift signal is predicted in muonic silicon-29, while the leading 1S hyperfine targets are silicon-29 for the vector scenario and phosphorus-31 for the pseudoscalar scenario. The main theoretical uncertainty comes from the Schmidt-model treatment of nuclear spin content.

## Key claim
The paper systematically surveys X17-induced Lamb shifts and hyperfine splittings in muonic atoms with stable nuclei up to Z ≤ 15, finding that the vector Lamb-shift signal grows toward heavier nuclei while the vector hyperfine scenario favors odd-N nuclei and the pseudoscalar scenario favors odd-Z systems, though the dominant theoretical uncertainty comes from the Schmidt-model treatment of nuclear spin content rather than the atomic calculation itself.

## Method
The muon-nucleus bound-state problem was solved within the Gaussian Expansion Method using a unified Hamiltonian that includes the standard electromagnetic baseline together with vector and pseudoscalar X17 exchange. The spin-independent Lamb shift was described by a coherent vector muon-nucleus interaction summing over all Z protons and N neutrons, while the spin-dependent hyperfine sector was constructed isotope by isotope from shell-model spin fractions using proton and neutron spin fractions. Benchmark muon couplings were chosen in accordance with the 2025 Muon g-2 result, allowing Δaμ to vary within a wide interval Δaμ ± 6σa to define representative upper-scale benchmark couplings.

## Key numbers
- X17 mediator mass: mX ≃ 16.7 MeV, corresponding range mX⁻¹ ≈ 12 fm
- Vector muon coupling: h'μ such that Δaμ(V) = +0.6 σa = +3.78 × 10⁻¹⁰
- Pseudoscalar muon coupling: hμ such that Δaμ(A) = -5.4 σa = -3.402 × 10⁻⁹
- Vector proton coupling: h'p = εp e ≃ 2.42 × 10⁻⁴ (with εp = 8 × 10⁻⁴)
- Vector neutron coupling: h'n = εn e ≃ 3.03 × 10⁻³ (with εn = 1/100)
- Pseudoscalar proton coupling: hp ≃ -2.4 × 10⁻³
- Pseudoscalar neutron coupling: hn ≃ 5.1 × 10⁻⁴
- Experimental muon anomaly: aμexp = 116 592 070.5(14.5) × 10⁻¹¹
- Standard Model prediction: aμSM = 116 592 033(62) × 10⁻¹¹
- Discrepancy: Δaμ = 3.8(6.3) × 10⁻¹⁰ (0.6σ deviation)

## Context
This work extends previous discussions of X17 effects in muonic atoms that focused on only a few light systems, especially muonic hydrogen and deuterium (Jentschura 2020, Martynenko 2008, Krutov 2011), by performing a nucleus-by-nucleus survey across the light and medium-light region up to Z ≤ 15. The study is motivated by the ATOMKI anomalies observed in ⁸Be transitions (Krasznahorkay 2016) and ⁴He transitions (Krasznahorkay 2019), and builds on the protophobic vector scenario (Feng 2016) and pseudoscalar alternatives (Ellwanger 2016, Kozaczuk 2017). The results enable experimental target selection for ongoing and future muonic-atom spectroscopy programs, with the most promising near-term Lamb-shift probes being μd, μ³He⁺, and μ⁴He⁺ among systems with existing precision benchmarks.
