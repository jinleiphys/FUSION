# Comparison of different relativistic models applied to dense nuclear matter

> We explore three different classes of relativistic approaches applied to the description of dense nuclear matter: a Walecka-type relativistic mean field model (RMF), an extension including an effective chiral potential (RMF-C) and a further extension with a chiral potential and confinement effects (RMF-CC). The parameters of the latter are controlled by fundamental properties such as the chiral potential, Lattice-QCD predictions, the quark sub-structure, as well as empirical properties at nuclear matter saturation. While these models are calibrated to the same properties at saturation density, they differ in their predictions as the density increases. We take care of parameter uncertainties and propagate them to our predictions for symmetric nuclear matter by employing Bayesian statistics. We show that RMF and RMF-C share common features as the density increases, while RMF-CC behaves differently. For instance, the scalar field at $6n_\textrm{sat}$ reaches $\sim 20$ MeV for RMF-CC while it is larger than $\sim 70$ MeV for RMF and RMF-C. Interestingly, we also show that, by fixing the $ρ$ coupling constant from the quark structure of the nucleon, these three models reproduce only half of the empirical symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The three relativistic models (RMF, RMF-C, and RMF-CC) are all calibrated to reproduce the same saturation properties of symmetric nuclear matter, but they diverge in their predictions at high density; the RMF-CC model, which incorporates chiral potential and confinement effects, behaves qualitatively differently from the other two, and all three models reproduce only about half of the empirical symmetry energy when the rho coupling constant is fixed from the quark structure of the nucleon.

## Method

The authors constructed three relativistic mean-field models: a standard Walecka-type RMF model, an RMF model extended with an effective chiral potential (RMF-C), and an RMF model with both a chiral potential and confinement effects via nucleon polarizability (RMF-CC). All models were calibrated to the same nuclear empirical parameters at saturation density (binding energy and saturation density) using Bayesian Markov-Chain Monte-Carlo sampling to propagate parameter uncertainties. The RMF-CC model additionally used Lattice-QCD extrapolated parameters (a2, a4) to constrain the scalar meson mass, coupling constant, and nucleon susceptibility. The models were then compared in symmetric nuclear matter at densities up to 6 times saturation density, and the symmetry energy was evaluated using the rho meson coupling fixed from quark substructure.

## Key numbers

- Scalar field at 6n_sat: ~20 MeV for RMF-CC, larger than ~70 MeV for RMF and RMF-C
- Saturation density n_sat: 0.155 fm^-3 (with standard deviation 0.005 fm^-3)
- Saturation energy E_sat: -15.8 MeV (with standard deviation 0.3 MeV)
- RMF-CC scalar meson mass m_sigma: peaked around 820 MeV
- RMF-CC Dirac effective mass at saturation: ~0.85 +/- 0.02 times the free nucleon mass M_N
- RMF-CC incompressibility modulus K_sat: peaked at ~265 MeV
- RMF-CC omega coupling constant g_omega: peaked around 6.5
- Lattice parameters used: a2 = 1.533 +/- 0.136 GeV^-1, a4 = -0.509 +/- 0.054 GeV^-3

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field approach of Serot and Walecka (1986, 1997) by incorporating chiral symmetry breaking and confinement effects following the framework developed by Chanfray et al. (2001, 2005). It contrasts with chiral effective field theory approaches (Tews et al., 2018) that break down between n_sat and 2n_sat, and with the quark-meson coupling model of Guichon (1988). The RMF-CC model provides a bridge between QCD-motivated parameters (from Lattice-QCD extrapolations by the CP-PACS collaboration, 2001) and nuclear matter phenomenology, enabling future studies of phase transitions in dense matter and neutron star structure.
