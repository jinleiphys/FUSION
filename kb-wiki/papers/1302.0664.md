# Deformation in 28Si* produced via 16O + 12C reaction

> The energy spectra of the $α$ particles emitted in the reactions $^{16}$O (7-10 MeV/nucleon) + $^{12}$C have been measured in the center of mass angular range of 25$^\circ$ $\lesssim θ_{c.m.} \lesssim$ 70$^\circ$. The experimental energy spectra have been compared with those obtained from the statistical model calculation with "deformability" parameters predicted by rotating liquid drop model (RLDM) and also fitted the same with optimized "deformability" parameters, which are quite different from the respective RLDM values. The data have also been found to be explained quite well using "frozen" deformation approximation, where the "deformability" parameters have been kept fixed at RLDM values of the parent nucleus throughout the decay process. The effective radius in the latter case is smaller compared to that obtained using the optimized parameters; however, in both cases, the deformations (effective radii) are larger than the corresponding RLDM values. So, considering the uncertainties in the estimation of actual compound nucleus deformations, it can, only qualitatively, be said that equilibrium orbiting, which is similar to particle evaporation in time scale, could also be one of the contributing factors for the observed deformation.

## Key claim

The measured alpha-particle energy spectra from the 16O + 12C reaction can be explained by statistical model calculations using either optimized deformability parameters (which differ substantially from rotating liquid drop model predictions) or a frozen deformation approximation; however, due to uncertainties in the actual compound nucleus deformation, it can only qualitatively be said that equilibrium orbiting (similar in time scale to particle evaporation) could be one contributing factor for the observed deformation.

## Method

Alpha-particle energy spectra were measured in the center-of-mass angular range of 25 degrees to 70 degrees for 16O beams at 117, 125, 145, and 160 MeV incident on a 514 microgram/cm2 12C target, using a silicon surface-barrier telescope detector. The experimental spectra were compared with statistical model calculations using the code CASCADE, employing Hauser-Feshbach formalism with spin-dependent deformability parameters. The calculations were performed with three approaches: using rotating liquid drop model (RLDM) deformability parameters, using optimized deformability parameters fitted to the data, and using a frozen deformation approximation where the RLDM deformability parameters of the parent nucleus were kept fixed throughout the decay.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies: 117, 125, 145, and 160 MeV for 16O ions
- Target thickness: 514 microgram/cm2 self-supporting 12C
- Center-of-mass angular range: 25 degrees to 70 degrees
- Critical angular momenta used in calculations: 20, 21, 22, and 23 hbar for beam energies 117, 125, 145, and 160 MeV respectively
- RLDM deformability parameters: delta1 = 3.7 x 10^-4, delta2 = 1.1 x 10^-6 (same for all beam energies)
- Optimized deformability parameters (delta1): 1.9 x 10^-3 (117 MeV), 2.1 x 10^-3 (125 MeV), 2.3 x 10^-3 (145 MeV), 2.5 x 10^-3 (160 MeV)
- Optimized deformability parameters (delta2): 2.0 x 10^-8 for all beam energies
- Average effective radius parameter from frozen deformation: 1.48 (117 MeV) to 1.58 (160 MeV)
- Average effective radius parameter from RLDM: approximately 1.38 to 1.42
- Average effective radius parameter from fitted parameters: approximately 1.52 to 1.66
- Excitation energies of 28Si*: 67, 70, 79, and 85 MeV for the four beam energies

## Context

This work extends previous studies of deformation in light alpha-cluster nuclei using light charged particle spectroscopy, following similar investigations of 40Ca* by Fornal (2003) and Rousseau (2002) and 32S* by Aparajita (2012). The authors had previously reported enhanced boron product yield in the 16O + 12C system at excitation energies of 67-85 MeV (Kundu 2013), indicating survival of long-lived dinuclear orbiting composites. The present study uses alpha-particle evaporation spectra to probe deformation of the 28Si* compound nucleus, finding that while deformation is enhanced relative to RLDM predictions, the magnitude remains uncertain between the optimized and frozen deformation approaches, suggesting the need for more realistic Monte Carlo calculations and additional experimental inputs such as giant dipole resonance studies.
