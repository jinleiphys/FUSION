# Jet Quenching in the Smallest Hadronic Collision Systems

> We present perturbative quantum chromodynamics (pQCD) predictions for high-momentum particle yield modification in very light ion collisions - ${}^{10}\mathrm{B}+{}^{10}\mathrm{B}$, ${}^{6}\mathrm{Li}+{}^{6}\mathrm{Li}$, ${}^{4}\mathrm{He}+{}^{4}\mathrm{He}$, and ${}^{3}\mathrm{He}+{}^{3}\mathrm{He}$ - with and without medium-induced energy loss. We find non-trivial suppression in symmetric systems from ${}^{208}\mathrm{Pb}+{}^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$ to ${}^{3}\mathrm{He}+{}^{3}\mathrm{He}$ and in asymmetric $A+B$ systems, with the suppression scaling approximately as $R_{AB} \simeq (\sqrt{AB})^{1/3}$. Further, we find that ${}^{3}\mathrm{He}$ and ${}^{6}\mathrm{Li}$ offer particularly clean environments for observing final-state partonic energy loss from quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formation in extremely small systems. Finally, we show that energy loss models generically predict $v_2\{\mathrm{SP}\} \approx 0$ in small systems, indicating that the large measured $v_2 > 0$ in $p+{}^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$ is not due to energy loss.

## Key claim

The paper claims that energy loss models generically predict $v_2\{\mathrm{SP}\} \approx 0$ in small systems, which implies that the large measured $v_2 > 0$ in $p + \mathrm{Pb}$ collisions is not due to energy loss but likely due to missing physics such as initial-state correlations or experimental biases.

## Method

The authors used a perturbative QCD-based model that computes collisional and radiative energy loss of partons traversing a 2+1D viscous hydrodynamic medium with event-by-event and path-by-path fluctuations, followed by hadronization. The model includes small-system-size corrections to both radiative and collisional energy loss, with the sole free parameter $\alpha_s$ fixed by fitting $R_{AA}$ in $\mathrm{Pb}+\mathrm{Pb}$ and $\mathrm{Au}+\mathrm{Au}$ collisions. Baseline calculations were also performed using NLO pQCD computations with modifications from nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs).

## Key numbers

* Nuclear modification factor $R_{AB}$ scales approximately as $R_{AB} \simeq (\sqrt{AB})^{1/3}$ across symmetric systems from ${}^{208}\mathrm{Pb}+{}^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$ to ${}^{3}\mathrm{He}+{}^{3}\mathrm{He}$
* Predictions are presented for $\pi^0$ hadrons in the transverse momentum range $9.6\ \mathrm{GeV} \leq p_T \leq 12\ \mathrm{GeV}$ in minimum bias collisions
* Energy loss model predictions for $v_2\{\mathrm{SP}\}$ are nearly zero in $\mathrm{Ne}+\mathrm{Ne}$, $\mathrm{O}+\mathrm{O}$, and $p+\mathrm{Pb}$ collisions, while $v_2^{\mathrm{hard}}$ is small but nonzero
* In $p+\mathrm{Pb}$ collisions, the model shows a slight anticorrelation between hard and soft event planes, leading to $v_2\{\mathrm{SP}\} \lesssim 0$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous energy loss model studies (Faraday 2023, 2024, 2025; Bert 2026) to extremely light ion systems, following the 2025 LHC runs of $\mathrm{O}+\mathrm{O}$ and $\mathrm{Ne}+\mathrm{Ne}$ that showed evidence for collectivity and jet quenching (ALICE 2025; CMS 2025). The paper addresses the "small-system energy loss puzzle" where $p+A$ and $d+A$ collisions show $R_{AB} \approx 1$ (CMS 2016) despite high-$p_T$ $v_2 > 0$ (CMS 2025). The predictions for ${}^{3}\mathrm{He}+{}^{3}\mathrm{He}$ and ${}^{6}\mathrm{Li}+{}^{6}\mathrm{Li}$ collisions motivate future LHC runs to search for the smallest QGP droplet, with these systems offering particularly clean baselines due to anomalously small nPDF uncertainty.
