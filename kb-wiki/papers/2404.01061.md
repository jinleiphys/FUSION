# Wiedemann-Franz law violation domain for graphene and nonrelativistic systems

> A systematic non-fluid to fluid transition framework and comparative research on Lorenz ratios for graphene and nonrelativistic systems have been studied to identify their Wiedemann-Franz law violation domain. Here, Lorenz ratio is defined as thermal conductivity divided by electrical conductivity times temperature times Lorenz number. In non-fluid framework, Lorenz ratio become exactly one, which means that the Wiedemann-Franz is obeyed within a Fermi Liquid domain. When one enters from Fermi Liquid to Dirac Fluid domain, Lorenz ratio becomes less than one in non-fluid framework but in fluid framework, it always remain greater than one for both domain. By compiling our outcomes and connecting with experimental data, a non-fluid to fluid transition framework is expected during the transition from Fermi Liquid to Dirac Fluid domain.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Wiedemann-Franz law is obeyed (Lorenz ratio = 1) in the Fermi Liquid domain for both graphene and nonrelativistic systems, but is violated in the Dirac Fluid domain; however, the direction of violation depends on the framework used, in the non-fluid framework the Lorenz ratio becomes less than one, while in the fluid framework it always remains greater than one for both domains.

## Method

The authors calculated electrical and thermal conductivities for 2D graphene (linear dispersion) and 3D nonrelativistic systems (quadratic dispersion) using both a non-fluid (solid-state) framework based on standard transport theory and a fluid framework based on relaxation-time-approximation Boltzmann transport equations with local thermalization. They derived general expressions for the Lorenz ratio in terms of Fermi-Dirac integrals and numerically evaluated these ratios as functions of the dimensionless parameter epsilon_F/T to map the transition from Fermi Liquid to Dirac Fluid regimes.

## Key numbers

- The Lorenz number in natural units is L0 = 137*pi/12 ≈ 35.84 (dimensionless).
- In SI units, L0 = 2.445 × 10^(-8) watt·ohm/K^2.
- For the 2D graphene non-fluid description with specific heat definition [C_V]_e2, the Sommerfeld limit gives L/L0 = 2 (red dotted line in Fig. 1b).
- For the 3D graphene non-fluid description with specific heat definition [C_V]_e2, the Sommerfeld limit gives L/L0 = 3 (red dotted line in Fig. 1a).
- Deviation of the Lorenz ratio from unity begins below epsilon_F/T ≈ 10 for the non-fluid framework (black solid lines in Figs. 1 and 2).

## Context

This work extends the comparative research by Win et al. (2023) on ideal thermodynamics and by Aung et al. (2023) on viscous dissipation in graphene, which demonstrated that graphene hydrodynamics is neither relativistic nor nonrelativistic. The study connects with experimental observations by Crossno et al. (2016) of Wiedemann-Franz law violation in graphene systems, and aims to provide a systematic non-fluid to fluid transition framework that can explain the violation domain, potentially enabling better understanding of electron hydrodynamics in 2D materials.
