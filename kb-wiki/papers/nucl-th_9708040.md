# Relativistic Point-Coupling Models as Effective Theories of Nuclei

> Recent studies have shown that concepts of effective field theory such as naturalness can be profitably applied to relativistic mean-field models of nuclei. Here the analysis by Friar, Madland, and Lynn of naturalness in a relativistic point-coupling model is extended. Fits to experimental nuclear data support naive dimensional analysis as a useful principle and imply a mean-field expansion analogous to that found for mean-field meson models.

## Key claim

The paper extends the analysis by Friar, Madland, and Lynn (1997) of naturalness in relativistic point-coupling models, finding that fits to experimental nuclear data support naive dimensional analysis (NDA) as a useful principle and imply a mean-field expansion analogous to that found for mean-field meson models, but with the caveat that the point-coupling parameters are underdetermined by the fits, limiting the usefulness of direct comparisons.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic point-coupling effective Lagrangian organized according to Georgi's naive dimensional analysis (NDA), with terms expanded in powers and derivatives of nucleon scalar, vector, isovector-vector, tensor, and isovector-tensor densities scaled by the pion decay constant \(f_\pi \approx 94\) MeV and a larger mass scale \(\Lambda \sim 4\pi f_\pi/\sqrt{N_f}\) (taken as the \(\rho\)-meson mass 770 MeV). They solve the Dirac-Hartree (one-baryon-loop) equations for spherically symmetric nuclei and perform fits to experimental nuclear observables using the same observables and weights as in Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1997), with an improved optimization procedure that exploits the small difference between scalar and vector densities at ordinary nuclear densities.

## Key numbers

* The pion decay constant used for NDA scaling: \(f_\pi \approx 94\) MeV.
* The larger mass scale \(\Lambda\) is taken as the \(\rho\)-meson mass: 770 MeV.
* The dimensionless expansion parameter for densities at nuclear matter equilibrium density is between 1/4 and 1/7.
* The dimensionless expansion parameter for gradients is \(1/\Lambda\sigma \le 1/5\), where \(\sigma\) is the nuclear surface thickness.
* The anomalous magnetic moments fixed from vacuum: \(\lambda_p = 1.793\) and \(\lambda_n = -1.913\).
* The second-order electromagnetic coefficients fixed from experimental charge radii: \(\wt\beta_s = 2.36\) and \(\wt\beta_v = 2.36\).
* The fourth-order electromagnetic coefficients determined from a dipole fit to the proton form factor: \(2\wt\delta_s = -5.80\).
* The vector meson and \(\rho\)-meson masses are fixed at 782 MeV and 770 MeV respectively when imposing the relations \(m_v^2 = \wt\zeta_2/\wt\zeta_d\) and \(m_\rho^2 = \wt\xi_2/\wt\xi_d\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis by Friar, Madland, and Lynn (1997) of naturalness in relativistic point-coupling models, building on the effective field theory framework applied to relativistic mean-field meson models by Furnstahl, Serot, and Tang (1997). The point-coupling approach replaces meson exchange with contact interactions, analogous to the density functional theory formulation of the relativistic many-body problem. The results enable future two-loop calculations to test the stability of the Hartree results and suggest that a different organization exploiting cancellations characteristic of relativistic models may be more productive than direct comparison with meson model parameters.
