# Secondary phi meson peak as an indicator of QCD phase transition in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions

> In a previous paper, we have shown that a double phi peak structure appears in the dilepton invariant mass spectrum if a first order QCD phase transition occurs in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. Furthermore, the transition temperature can be determined from the transverse momentum distribution of the low mass phi peak. In this work, we extend the study to the case that a smooth crossover occurs in the quark-gluon plasma to the hadronic matter transition. We find that the double phi peak structure still exists in the dilepton spectrum and thus remains a viable signal for the formation of the quark-gluon plasma in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions.

# Secondary phi meson peak as an indicator of QCD phase transition in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The double phi peak structure in the dilepton invariant mass spectrum remains a viable signal for quark-gluon plasma formation even when the QCD phase transition is a smooth crossover rather than a first order transition, provided the crossover width is sufficiently narrow (Gamma ~ 5 MeV). The low mass phi peak persists because the temperature stays near the critical temperature for a relatively long time during the crossover.

## Method
The authors performed a hydrodynamical calculation with transverse flow for central Au+Au collisions at ultrarelativistic energies, using a parametrized entropy density that smoothly interpolates between hadron and quark phases via a hyperbolic tangent function with width Gamma. They modeled the temperature-dependent phi meson mass reduction from partial chiral symmetry restoration and computed the dilepton invariant mass spectrum including a Gaussian smearing function (sigma = 10 MeV) for experimental mass resolution.

## Key numbers
- Critical temperature Tc = 180 MeV (default) and Tc = 160 MeV (lower case)
- Crossover width parameter Gamma = 5 MeV (default) and Gamma = 10 MeV (broadened case)
- Initial temperature T0 = 250 MeV (default) and T0 = 175 MeV (subcritical case)
- Freeze-out temperature Tf = 120 MeV
- Initial proper time tau0 = 1 fm
- Phi meson vacuum lifetime ~ 45 fm
- Collisional broadening of phi meson width from hadron interactions ~ 10 MeV, with additional few MeV from parton interactions
- Entropy density ratio s_q(Tc)/s_h(Tc) = 37/3 for two-flavor bag model
- Low mass phi peak appears at approximately 916 MeV, normal phi peak at 1019 MeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous proposal (Asakawa and Ko, 1994) that a double phi peak structure signals a first order QCD phase transition, to the more realistic case of a smooth crossover suggested by lattice QCD calculations (Collins et al., 1990; Cray et al., 1992). The study rules out concerns that collisional broadening (Sorge and Koch, 1993) or parton interactions (Sorge and Koch, 1994) would wash out the secondary peak. The method for extracting the transition temperature from the low mass phi peak's transverse momentum distribution is contrasted with an earlier proposal using rho mesons (Seibert, 1992), with the phi meson offering advantages of cleaner signal and narrower width. The results enable experimental searches for the QCD phase transition in future ultrarelativistic heavy ion experiments.
