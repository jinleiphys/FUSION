# Describing the Baryon Spectrum with 1/N_c QCD

> This talk outlines recent advances using QCD in the 1/N_c limit aimed at understanding baryon scattering processes and their embedded short-lived baryon resonances. In this presentation we emphasize developing qualitative physical insight over presenting results of detailed calculations.

## Key claim
The large \(N_c\) limit of QCD provides a model-independent framework for understanding baryon resonances as poles in scattering amplitudes, where resonances appear in multiplets degenerate in both mass and width, and the quark-model and chiral-soliton pictures become compatible only in this limit. The talk emphasizes qualitative physical insight over detailed calculations, and the results rely on the expansion in powers of \(1/N_c\) being valid down to \(N_c = 3\).

## Method
The author reviews the application of the \(1/N_c\) expansion to baryon scattering amplitudes, using group-theoretical methods based on conservation of grand spin \(K = \mathbf{I} + \mathbf{J}\) in the large \(N_c\) limit. Partial-wave amplitudes are expressed as linear combinations of reduced amplitudes labeled by \(K\), with coefficients given by Clebsch-Gordan, \(6j\), and \(9j\) factors. The approach is applied to \(\pi N\), \(\eta N\), and photoproduction amplitudes, and the pattern of resonance multiplets is compared to the SU(6)\(\times\)O(3) quark-model classification.

## Key numbers
- The \(N(1535)\) and \(N(1650)\) are the two \(I = \frac12\), \(J = \frac12\) negative-parity states in the \(({\bf 70}, 1^-)\) multiplet for \(N_c = 3\).
- The \(\Lambda(1670)\) lies only 5 MeV above the \(\eta \Lambda(1116)\) threshold, yet has a 10–25% branching ratio to that channel.
- The effective coupling constant ratio \(g(\Lambda(1520) \to \overline{K} N) / g(\Lambda(1520) \to \Sigma \pi) \sim 4\)–\(5 = O(N_c)\).
- Linear relations among photoproduction multipole amplitudes agree to within 10–15% when evaluated on resonance using Particle Data Group values.
- Pentaquark \(\Theta^+(1540)\) would require \(I=1\), \(J=\frac12,\frac32\) and \(I=2\), \(J=\frac32\) partners with masses equal up to \(O(1/N_c)\) corrections (less than about 200 MeV) and widths equal up to \(O(1/N_c)\) differences.

## Context
This work extends the large \(N_c\) consistency conditions of Dashen, Manohar, and Jenkins (1990s) and the \(I_t = J_t\) rule of Kaplan, Savage, and Manohar (1990s) to baryon resonances, building on earlier soliton-model studies by Mattis and collaborators (1980s). It reconciles the constituent quark model and the chiral soliton (Skyrme) model for excited baryons, showing that both produce the same \(K\)-labeled multiplets in the large \(N_c\) limit. The approach enables model-independent predictions for resonance decay patterns, photoproduction relations, and searches for exotic states such as pentaquarks.
