# Uncertainty analysis and order-by-order optimization of chiral nuclear interactions

> Chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) provides a systematic approach to describe low-energy nuclear forces. Moreover, chiEFT is able to provide well-founded estimates of statistical and systematic uncertainties -- although this unique advantage has not yet been fully exploited. We fill this gap by performing an optimization and statistical analysis of all the low-energy constants (LECs) up to next-to-next-to-leading order. Our optimization protocol corresponds to a simultaneous fit to scattering and bound-state observables in the pion-nucleon, nucleon-nucleon, and few-nucleon sectors, thereby utilizing the full model capabilities of chiEFT. We study the effect on other observables by demonstrating error-propagation methods that can easily be adopted by future works. We employ mathematical optimization and implement automatic differentiation to attain efficient and machine-precise first- and second-order derivatives of the objective function with respect to the LECs. We use power-counting arguments to estimate the systematic uncertainty that is inherent to chiEFT and we construct chiral interactions at different orders with quantified uncertainties. Statistical error propagation is compared with Monte Carlo sampling showing that statistical errors are in general small compared to systematic ones. In conclusion, we find that a simultaneous fit to different sets of data is critical to (i) identify the optimal set of LECs, (ii) capture all relevant correlations, (iii) reduce the statistical uncertainty, and (iv) attain order-by-order convergence in chiEFT. Furthermore, certain systematic uncertainties in the few-nucleon sector are shown to get substantially magnified in the many-body sector; in particlar when varying the cutoff in the chiral potentials. The methodology and results presented in this Paper open a new frontier for uncertainty quantification in ab initio nuclear theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a simultaneous mathematical optimization of chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) low-energy constants (LECs) to pion-nucleon, nucleon-nucleon, and few-nucleon data, with inclusion of both experimental and theoretical error bars, yields smaller propagated uncertainties and more obvious order-by-order convergence compared to traditional separate or sequential fitting approaches.

## Method

The authors performed a simultaneous optimization of all LECs up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in chiEFT, fitting to pion-nucleon scattering data, nucleon-nucleon scattering cross sections, effective range parameters, and bound-state observables for A=2,3,4 nuclei (including the triton beta-decay half-life). They employed mathematical optimization with automatic differentiation for machine-precise first- and second-order derivatives of the objective function, used power-counting arguments to estimate systematic uncertainties from the chiral expansion truncation, and propagated statistical errors via covariance matrices compared with Monte Carlo sampling.

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter is defined as Q/Lambda_chi, with Lambda_chi approximately 800 MeV (the rho meson mass)
- Spectral function regularization cutoff set to 700 MeV for two-pion exchange loop integrals
- Main cutoff Lambda = 500 MeV with n=3 for the non-local regulator functions, with variations explored between 450-600 MeV in steps of 25 MeV
- The axial-vector coupling constant set to g_A=1.276 at LO and g_A=1.29 at higher orders; pion decay constant F_pi = 92.4 MeV used at all orders
- The experimentally determined comparative half-life for triton is fT_{1/2} = 1129.6 +/- 3 s, corresponding to an empirical value for the axial matrix element of 0.6848 +/- 0.0011
- The proton charge radius used is r_p = 0.8783(86) fm and neutron charge mean-squared radius r_n^2 = -0.1149(27) fm^2

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts by Ekstrom (2013) and Navarro (2013) who first introduced mathematical optimization algorithms for nuclear interactions, and by Ekstrom (2015) and Navarro (2015) who investigated statistical constraints on LECs in the NN sector. The paper provides the first common statistical regression analysis of both ab initio many-body methods and chiral effective field theory, delivering optimized chiral interactions with quantified uncertainties that can be used in other calculations such as no-core shell model (NCSM) and coupled cluster (CC) approaches.
