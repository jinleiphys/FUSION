# Nuclear Dynamics with Effective Field Theories

> These are the proceedings of the international workshop on "Nuclear Dynamics with Effective Field Theories" held at Ruhr-Universitaet Bochum, Germany from July 1 to 3, 2013. The workshop focused on effective field theories of low-energy QCD, chiral perturbation theory for nuclear forces as well as few- and many-body physics. Included are a short contribution per talk.

## Key claim

This paper is a proceedings volume summarizing talks from a workshop on nuclear dynamics with effective field theories, rather than presenting a single original result. The workshop focused on effective field theories of low-energy QCD, chiral perturbation theory for nuclear forces, and few- and many-body physics, with the goal of bringing together experimentalists and theorists to discuss recent developments and future research directions.

## Method

The workshop was held at Ruhr-Universitaet Bochum, Germany from July 1 to 3, 2013, with 41 participants. 34 of the participants presented results in talks of 35 minutes in length. The proceedings include a short contribution per talk, with a description of their contents and a list of the most relevant references, rather than full-fledged proceedings.

## Key numbers

Review-type paper: no original numerics.

## Context

This workshop proceedings extends the ongoing work in chiral effective field theory for nuclear forces, building on developments in lattice QCD for few-nucleon systems and sophisticated few- and many-body methods for nuclear structure calculations. The workshop covered topics including three-nucleon forces, halo nuclei, hypernuclei, and electroweak processes, with contributions from groups such as the Bochum group (Witala et al., 1998) and the RIKEN collaboration (Sakai et al., 2000). The proceedings enable future work by documenting the state of the field in 2013 and identifying open questions, such as the need for new low-energy experiments to resolve discrepancies in few-body observables as argued by Tornow, and the ongoing development of consistent chiral three-nucleon forces at N3LO as discussed by Witala.
