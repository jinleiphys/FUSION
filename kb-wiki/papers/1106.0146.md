# Direct Photon Production in Proton-Nucleus and Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> Prompt photons produced in a hard reaction are not accompanied with any final state interaction, either energy loss or absorption. Therefore, besides the Cronin enhancement at medium transverse momenta pT and small isotopic corrections at larger pT, one should not expect any nuclear effects. However, data from PHENIX experiment exhibit a significant large-pT suppression in central d+Au and Au+Au collisions that cannot be accompanied by coherent phenomena. We demonstrate that such an unexpected result is subject to the energy sharing problem near the kinematic limit and is universally induced by multiple initial state interactions. We describe production of photons in the color dipole approach and find a good agreement with available data in p+p collisions. Besides explanation of large-pT nuclear suppression at RHIC we present for the first time predictions for expected nuclear effects also in the LHC energy range at different rapidities. We include and analyze also a contribution of gluon shadowing as a leading twist shadowing correction modifying nuclear effects at small and medium pT.

# Direct Photon Production in Proton-Nucleus and Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

## Key claim

The unexpected large-transverse-momentum suppression of direct photons observed by PHENIX in central d+Au and Au+Au collisions is not due to coherent nuclear effects but is universally caused by the energy sharing problem near the kinematic limit, induced by multiple initial state interactions. The authors demonstrate that this suppression arises from energy conservation effects in multiple parton rescatterings, not from final state interactions, since photons have no final state interactions.

## Method

The authors use the color dipole approach in the target rest frame, treating direct photon production as bremsstrahlung radiation from a projectile quark. They employ the GBW parametrization for the dipole cross section and GRV98 lowest-order parton distribution functions at scale Q² = pT². Nuclear effects are incorporated through eikonalization of the dipole cross section for long coherence lengths, with additional gluon shadowing corrections from the leading twist shadowing factor RG, and energy conservation effects are included via a suppression factor S(x) ~ 1-x applied to the effective projectile parton distribution.

## Key numbers

- The effective cross section for multiple interactions is σeff = 20 mb, used in the suppression factor for the projectile parton distribution
- At large pT, isotopic effects give a value Rd+Au ~ 0.83 for d+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV
- Predictions are presented for nuclear modification factors at RHIC energy √sNN = 200 GeV and LHC energy √sNN = 5.5 TeV
- The coherence length condition for the onset of shadowing is lc ≥ RA, where RA is the nuclear radius

## Context

This work extends the energy conservation mechanism proposed by Kopeliovich et al. (2005) for explaining universal suppression patterns observed across different reactions at fixed target experiments and RHIC. The authors provide the first predictions for nuclear effects in direct photon production at LHC energies across different rapidities, enabling future tests of QCD factorization violation by experiments at RHIC and LHC, particularly at forward rapidities where both gluon shadowing and energy conservation effects are predicted to rise rapidly.
