# Baryonic Popcorn

> In the large N limit cold dense nuclear matter must be in a lattice phase. This applies also to holographic models of hadron physics. In a class of such models, like the generalized Sakai-Sugimoto model, baryons take the form of instantons of the effective flavor gauge theory that resides on probe flavor branes. In this paper we study the phase structure of baryonic crystals by analyzing discrete periodic configurations of such instantons. We find that instanton configurations exhibit a series of "popcorn" transitions upon increasing the density. Through these transitions normal (3D) lattices expand into the transverse dimension, eventually becoming a higher dimensional (4D) multi-layer lattice at large densities. We consider 3D lattices of zero size instantons as well as 1D periodic chains of finite size instantons, which serve as toy models of the full holographic systems. In particular, for the finite-size case we determine solutions of the corresponding ADHM equations for both a straight chain and for a 2D zigzag configuration where instantons pop up into the holographic dimension. At low density the system takes the form of an "abelian anti-ferromagnetic" straight periodic chain. Above a critical density there is a second order phase transition into a zigzag structure. An even higher density yields a rich phase space characterized by the formation of multi-layer zigzag structures. The finite size of the lattices in the transverse dimension is a signal of an emerging Fermi sea of quarks. We thus propose that the popcorn transitions indicate the onset of the "quarkyonic" phase of the cold dense nuclear matter.

## Key claim

In the large $N_c$ limit, cold dense nuclear matter must form a lattice phase, and in holographic models like the generalized Sakai-Sugimoto model, baryons are instantons of the flavor gauge theory; the authors find that these instanton configurations exhibit a series of "popcorn" transitions upon increasing density, where normal 3D lattices expand into the transverse dimension and eventually become higher-dimensional multi-layer lattices, which they propose signals the onset of the "quarkyonic" phase.

## Method

The authors study two toy models of baryonic crystals: (1) a 3D lattice of zero-size instantons treated as point charges in 5D, and (2) a 1D periodic chain of finite-size instantons, for which they solve the corresponding ADHM equations for both a straight chain and a 2D zigzag configuration where instantons pop up into the holographic dimension. They compute the energy of these configurations including 1/λ corrections to the BPS energy, and determine the phase structure by minimizing the net energy with respect to the instanton moduli (positions, radii, and orientation angles).

## Key numbers

- For the 1D chain of finite-size instantons, the straight chain becomes unstable below a critical separation distance given by equation (Dcrit) in the paper, undergoing a second-order phase transition into a zigzag structure.
- For large zigzag amplitude, the relative orientation of neighboring instantons changes from φ = π to φ ≈ 117° in a first-order phase transition, then smoothly approaches an asymptotic value of π/2.
- For ε ≫ d (where ε is the zigzag amplitude and d is the spacing), instantons in each of the two layers prefer an antiferromagnetic orientation with φ = π.
- The baryon mass scales as N_c, the baryon-baryon potential energy scales as N_c, and the kinetic energy scales as 1/N_c, giving a ratio K/Π ∼ 1/N_c².

## Context

This work extends the holographic study of baryonic matter in the Sakai-Sugimoto model, building on the phase diagram analysis of Bergman, Lifschytz, and Lippert (2008) around μ = μ_c and T = 0, and on the earlier work of Rozali, Shieh, Van Raamsdonk, and Wu (2007) who showed that a uniform instanton distribution in 3D necessarily incurs a density in the holographic dimension. The results connect to the Skyrme model analysis of baryon lattices by Rho, Sin, and Zahed (2009) and to the broader question of whether chiral symmetry restoration occurs in the nuclear matter phase, as suggested by skyrmion-to-half-skyrmion transitions in the work of Goldhaber and collaborators (1987) and Kugler and Shtrikman. The popcorn transitions provide a holographic mechanism for the onset of the quarkyonic phase proposed by McLerran and collaborators (2007).
