# Vortex rings in heavy-ion collisions at energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 3--30 GeV and possibility of their observation

> The ring structures that appear in Au+Au collisions at collision energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 3 -- 30 GeV are studied. The calculations are performed within the model of three-fluid dynamics. It is demonstrated that a pare of vortex rings are formed, one at forward and another at backward rapidities, in ultra-central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}>$ 4 GeV. The vortex rings carry information about early stage of the collision, in particular about the stopping of baryons. It is shown that these rings can be detected by measuring the ring observable $R_Λ$ even in rapidity range $0<y<0.5$ (or $-0.5<y<0$) on the level of 0.5--1.5\% at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 5 -- 20 GeV. At forward/backward rapidities, the $R_Λ$ signal is expected to be stronger. Possibility of observation of the vortex-ring signal against background of non-collective transverse polarization is discussed.

## Key claim

Vortex rings are formed in ultra-central Au+Au collisions at collision energies above 4 GeV, and the ring observable \(R_\Lambda\) can detect them even within the midrapidity window \(0<y<0.5\) at the level of 0.5–1.5% for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\)–20 GeV, though the signal is stronger at forward/backward rapidities. The paper estimates only the collective contribution to \(R_\Lambda\) from vortex rings, while the background from direct \(\Lambda\) production correlated with the beam direction is discussed separately.

## Method

The calculations were performed within the three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model, which simulates the early nonequilibrium stage of the collision by accounting for counterstreaming of leading baryon-rich matter. Two equations of state were used: a first-order phase transition (1PT) and a crossover one. The ring observable \(R_\Lambda\) was estimated using an isochronous freeze-out approximation and the thermodynamic approach relating polarization to thermal vorticity, with averaging over the freeze-out surface and particle momenta.

## Key numbers

- Vortex rings form in ultra-central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} > 4\) GeV.
- The ring observable \(R_\Lambda\) in the rapidity range \(0<y<0.5\) (or \(-0.5<y<0\)) is at the level of 0.5–1.5% for \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\)–20 GeV.
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) GeV, \(R_\Lambda\) can reach values of 5% at backward rapidities.
- The magnitude of \(R_\Lambda\) may reach 2–3% at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5\)–20 GeV if the rapidity window is extended to \(0<y<0.8\).
- Feed-down from higher-lying resonances (\(\Sigma^*\) and \(\Sigma^0\)) reduces \(R_\Lambda\) by 10–15%.
- The numerical uncertainty of the calculations is 10–15%.

## Context

This work extends earlier predictions of vortex rings in heavy-ion collisions at higher energies (Ivanov 2018, Xia 2018, Wei 2018) and at 7.7 GeV (Ivanov 2019, Zinchenko 2020) to the energy range \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3\)–30 GeV, using the 3FD model that has successfully described global \(\Lambda\) polarization (Ivanov 2022) and bulk observables (Ivanov 2013). The results enable experimental searches for vortex rings at the BES RHIC, NICA, FXT-STAR, and FAIR facilities, and suggest that measuring \(R_{\bar{\Lambda}}\) could help distinguish the collective signal from the background of direct \(\Lambda\) production.
