# Spatially extended coherence induced by pairing correlation in low-frequency vibrational excitations of neutron drip line nuclei

> Role of pairing correlation for emergence of low-frequency vibrational excitations in neutron drip line nuclei is discussed paying special attention to neutrons with small orbital angular momentum l. Self-consistent pairing correlation in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory causes the change of the spatial structure of the quasiparticle wave functions; "the pairing anti-halo effect" in the lower component and "the broadening effect" in the upper component. The resultant spatial distribution of the two-quasiparticle states among low-l neutrons, "the broad localization", brings about qualitatively new aspects, especially the large transition strength of the low-frequency vibrational excitations in nuclei close to the neutron drip line. By performing HFB plus quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculation for the first 2+ states in neutron rich Ni isotopes, the unique role of self-consistent pairing correlation is pointed out.

## Key claim

Self-consistent pairing correlation in Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory induces "the pairing anti-halo effect" in the lower component and "the broadening effect" in the upper component of quasiparticle wave functions, which together produce "broad localization" of two-quasiparticle states among low-orbital-angular-momentum neutrons and enable large transition strength for low-frequency vibrational excitations in neutron drip line nuclei, contrary to the decoupling predicted by analytic pairing potentials.

## Method

The author solved the HFB equation in coordinate space with spherical symmetry using a Woods-Saxon mean-field potential and a self-consistent density-dependent zero-range pairing interaction for neutrons around the neutron drip line nucleus 86Ni. The spatial structure of quasiparticle wave functions and two-quasiparticle states was analyzed, and HFB plus quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations were performed for the first 2+ states in neutron-rich Ni isotopes, comparing results with resonant BCS plus QRPA and RPA calculations.

## Key numbers

- The average pairing gap for neutrons around 86Ni was approximately 1.3 MeV (about 12/sqrt(86) MeV)
- The pairing strength was fixed at V_pair = -680 MeV fm^-3 with cut-off energy E_cut = 50 MeV and density parameter rho_c = 0.08 fm^-3
- For the 3s_{1/2} state at binding energy -0.052 MeV, the integrated occupation probability N^{(res)}_{s_{1/2}} was 0.430 (0.429) with R_box = 75 (400) fm, and the average effective pairing gap Delta_eff(s_{1/2}) was 1.15 (1.13) MeV with R_box = 75 (400) fm
- The pairing energy in the case of lambda = epsilon_{3s_{1/2}} = -0.052 MeV was -20.368 MeV with R_box = 400 fm and -20.238 MeV with R_box = 30 fm, a difference of 130 keV
- The average rms radius of the lower component of the s_{1/2} state was 7.18 fm, with quasiparticle energy 0.80 MeV and integrated occupation probability 0.48
- The d_{3/2} resonant state had average rms radius 6.12 fm, quasiparticle energy 1.25 MeV, and integrated occupation probability 0.26
- The d_{5/2} state had average rms radius 5.79 fm, quasiparticle energy 1.72 MeV, and integrated occupation probability 0.86
- The g_{7/2} resonant state had average rms radius 5.38 fm, quasiparticle energy 2.05 MeV, and integrated occupation probability 0.13

## Context

This work extends the discussion of pairing correlation effects in loosely bound nuclei, particularly the "pairing anti-halo effect" introduced by Bennaceur and Dobaczewski (2000), and addresses the open question of whether coherent motions among two-quasiparticle states can be realized in neutron drip line nuclei despite the variety of their spatial structure. The author contrasts self-consistent HFB results with the analytic surface-type pairing potential approach of Hamamoto et al. (2003, 2004), showing that self-consistency maintains coupling between the pairing potential and loosely-bound low-angular-momentum states even in the zero-binding-energy limit. The work enables further study of low-frequency vibrational excitations in neutron-rich nuclei and the role of spatially extended coherence induced by pairing.
