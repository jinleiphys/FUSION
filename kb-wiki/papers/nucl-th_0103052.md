# Strange decays from strange resonances

> We discuss the mass spectrum and strong decays of baryon resonances belonging to the N, Delta, Sigma, Lambda, Xi and Omega families in a collective string-like model for the nucleon. We find good overall agreement with the available data. Systematic discrepancies are found for lowlying S-wave states, in particular in the strong decays of N(1535), N(1650), Sigma(1750), Lambda(1405), Lambda(1670) and Lambda(1800).

# Strange decays from strange resonances

## Key claim

The collective string-like $qqq$ model provides good overall agreement with the mass spectrum and strong decays of baryon resonances across the $N$, $\Delta$, $\Sigma$, $\Lambda$, $\Xi$ and $\Omega$ families, but systematic discrepancies appear specifically for low-lying $S$-wave states, particularly $N(1535)$, $N(1650)$, $\Sigma(1750)$, $\Lambda(1405)$, $\Lambda(1670)$ and $\Lambda(1800)$, which may indicate the presence of non-$qqq$ configurations such as pentaquark or quasi-molecular meson-baryon states.

## Method

The authors treat baryons as three constituent quarks in a collective string-like model with a $U(7)$ spectrum generating algebra for radial excitations, combined with $SU_{\rm sf}(6) \otimes SU_{\rm c}(3)$ for internal spin-flavor-color degrees of freedom. Masses are computed from a G\"ursey-Radicati-type mass formula fitted to 48 three- and four-star resonances, while strong decay widths for two-body decays $B \rightarrow B' + M$ are calculated using an elementary emission model with a single transition operator applied uniformly across all resonances and decay channels, incorporating collective form factors.

## Key numbers

- r.m.s. deviation of mass fit: $\delta = 33$ MeV for 48 octet and decuplet resonances (compared to $\delta = 39$ MeV in a previous fit to 25 $N$ and $\Delta$ resonances)
- $\Lambda^*(1405)$ overpredicted by 236 MeV, $\Lambda^*(1520)$ by 121 MeV, $\Lambda^*(2100)$ by 97 MeV (these singlet states excluded from fit)
- $N(1535)$ observed $\eta$ decay width: $74 \pm 39$ MeV; calculated width $< 0.5$ MeV
- $\Sigma(1750)$ observed $\eta$ decay width: $39 \pm 28$ MeV
- $\Lambda(1670)$ observed $\eta$ decay width: $9 \pm 5$ MeV
- $N(1650)$ observed $\Lambda K$ decay width: $12 \pm 7$ MeV
- $\Lambda^*(1405)$ observed $\Sigma \pi$ decay width: $50 \pm 2$ MeV
- $\Lambda(1830)$ observed $N \overline{K}$ decay width: $6 \pm 3$ MeV
- Predicted $P_{11}$ nucleon resonance at 1713 MeV (associated with $^{2}8_{1/2}[20,1^+]$ configuration), compared to 1880 MeV in the relativized quark model
- Predicted $D_{13}$ state at 1847 MeV (vibrational excitation $(v_1,v_2)=(0,1)$ with $^{2}8_{3/2}[56,1^-]$), compared to 1960 MeV in the relativized quark model
- $\eta$ meson mixing angle: $\theta_P = -23^\circ$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous algebraic $U(7)$ collective model (Bijker and Leviatan, earlier work) from 25 $N$ and $\Delta$ resonances to a comprehensive fit of 48 resonances including strange baryons. The systematic discrepancies in $S$-wave states echo findings from the relativized quark model (Capstick and Isgur, earlier work) but the authors suggest these states may indicate exotic configurations rather than conventional $qqq$ states. The analysis enables future experimental searches for non-$qqq$ configurations, particularly in the resonances $N(1535)$, $N(1650)$, $\Sigma(1750)$, $\Lambda(1405)$, $\Lambda(1670)$ and $\Lambda(1800)$, and provides predictions for missing resonances that can be tested against results from the Zagreb group and kaon photoproduction analyses.
