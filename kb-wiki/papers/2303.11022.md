# Investigation of isospin-symmetry-breaking in mirror energy difference and nuclear mass with ab initio calculations

> Isospin-symmetry breaking is responsible for the energy difference of excited states in mirror nuclei. It also influences the coefficient of the isobaric multiplet mass equation. In the present work, we extensively investigate isospin-symmetry breaking in medium mass nuclei within ab initio frameworks. For this, we employ the ab initio valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group approach, in which charge-symmetry and charge-independence breakings are included in the adopted nuclear force. The mirror energies of sd- and pf- shell nuclei are computed for that matter. The effects of single-particle states on weakly bound and unbound nuclear states, especially those of the s-wave, are discussed. Predictions are also made concerning proton drip-line nuclei bearing large mirror energy difference. Finally, the coefficient of the isobaric multiplet mass equation in T = 1/2 and T = 1 isospin multiplets for A = 18 to A = 76 is calculated.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that _ab initio_ valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations with chiral two- and three-nucleon forces, including charge-symmetry breaking and charge-independence breaking, can reproduce mirror energy differences in sd- and pf-shell nuclei to within about 100 keV of experimental data, but only when three-nucleon forces are included; calculations with only two-nucleon forces fail to give the correct ground-state spin-parity.

## Method

The authors employed the _ab initio_ VS-IMSRG method using the 1.8/2.0 (EM) chiral potential, which includes next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) two-nucleon forces softened by similarity renormalization group evolution at lambda = 1.8 fm^-1 and next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) three-nucleon forces with cutoff Lambda = 2.0 fm^-1. The Hamiltonian was normal-ordered with respect to an ensemble reference state, retaining zero-, one-, and two-body terms while neglecting the normal-ordered three-body part. The effective valence-space Hamiltonians were constructed for full sd-shell (A=18 to A=39) and pf-shell (A=40 to A=75) valence spaces using 15 harmonic oscillator major shells (e_max = 14) with hbar omega = 16 MeV, then diagonalized with a shell-model code.

## Key numbers

* The 1/2_1^+ state in 23Al is lower than its counterpart in 23Ne by about 500 keV.
* The largest deviation between calculated and experimental MEDs for 23Al and 23Ne mirror nuclei does not exceed 100 keV, as seen in the 3/2_1^+ state.
* The MEDs of the 7/2_1^+ and 9/2^+ states in 23Al/23Ne are small, about 100 keV.
* The 5/2_2^+ state in 23Al/23Ne has a MED about 150 keV larger than the 3/2_1^+ state.
* Calculations used 15 harmonic oscillator major shells (e_max = 14) and hbar omega = 16 MeV, with three-body sector limited to e_3max = 14.
* The IMME coefficient was calculated for T=1/2 and T=1 isospin multiplets from A=18 to A=76.

## Context

This work extends _ab initio_ calculations of isospin-symmetry breaking to medium-mass nuclei, where previous theoretical approaches relied on the standard shell model with phenomenologically adjusted matrix elements (e.g., Bentley (2007) and Kaneko (2017)) or density functional theory (Baczyk (2018)). The VS-IMSRG method provides a systematic framework that includes Coulomb, charge-symmetry breaking, and charge-independence breaking forces from chiral effective field theory, in contrast to the Gamow shell model which is computationally prohibitive for these mass ranges. The results enable predictions of mirror energy differences and isobaric multiplet mass equation coefficients for proton drip-line nuclei that are experimentally unmeasured, particularly those with large s-wave occupation where the Thomas-Ehrman shift is significant.
