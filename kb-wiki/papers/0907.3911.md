# p-Wave pion production from nucleon-nucleon collisions

> We investigate p-wave pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions up to next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory. In particular, we show that it is possible to describe simultaneously the p-wave amplitudes in the $pn\to ppπ^-$, $pp\to pnπ^+$, $pp\to dπ^+$ channels by adjusting a single low-energy constant accompanying the short-range operator which is available at this order. This study provides a non-trivial test of the applicability of chiral effective field theory to reactions of the type $NN\to NNπ$.

## Key claim

The authors show that a single low-energy constant (LEC) accompanying the short-range contact operator can simultaneously describe the p-wave amplitudes in three different pion production channels ($pn\to pp\pi^-$, $pp\to pn\pi^+$, $pp\to d\pi^+$) up to next-to-next-to-leading order in chiral effective field theory, but only at a qualitative level because the expansion parameter $p_{\rm thr}/\Lambda_\chi \sim 0.4$ is quite large and accurate low-energy data for $pn\to pp\pi^-$ are not yet available.

## Method

The authors calculated p-wave pion production amplitudes in nucleon-nucleon collisions using chiral effective field theory with explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom, working up to next-to-next-to-leading order in a modified power counting that accounts for the scale $p_{\rm thr} = \sqrt{M_N m_\pi}$. They employed a hybrid approach where transition operators derived from chiral perturbation theory were convoluted with realistic NN wave functions from the coupled-channel CCF potential, and extracted s-wave amplitudes directly from experimental data to minimize theoretical uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- The threshold initial center-of-mass momentum is $p_{\rm thr} = \sqrt{M_N m_\pi}$, with $p_{\rm thr}/\Lambda_\chi \simeq 0.4$
- The pion decay constant used is $f_\pi = 92.4$ MeV
- The axial-vector coupling used is $g_A = 1.32$
- The Delta-Nucleon-pion coupling used is $h_A \simeq 2.1 g_A = 2.77$
- The low-energy constants used are $c_3 = -0.79$ GeV$^{-1}$ and $c_4 = 1.33$ GeV$^{-1}$
- For $pp\to d\pi^+$, the total cross section divided by $\eta$ at threshold is $\alpha = 252^{+5}_{-11}$ $\mu$b, extracted from pionic deuterium width measurements at PSI
- The best-fit value for the contact term LEC $d$ is $d = 3$ in units of $1/(f_\pi^2 M_N)$
- For $pn\to pp\pi^-$ at TRIUMF, the beam energy was $T_{lab} = 353$ MeV ($\eta = 0.66$) and the final diproton relative momentum was restricted to be less than 38 MeV/c ($M_{pp} - 2M_N \le 1.5$ MeV)
- For $pn\to pp\pi^-$ at PSI, the beam energy was $T_{lab} = 345$ MeV with $M_{pp} - 2M_N < 6$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to pion production in nucleon-nucleon collisions, addressing difficulties identified by Cohen et al. (1996) who noted that the naive power counting fails above pion production threshold due to the additional scale $p_{\rm thr}$. The study directly responds to a claim by Nakamura (2007) that the contact term strength needed for $pp\to pn\pi^+$ and $pp\to de^+\nu_e$ was inconsistent; the authors argue this discrepancy arose from incorrect partial wave decomposition in Flammang (1998) and from treating Delta isobar contributions inconsistently with the NN interactions. The results enable future quantitative tests once low-energy data for $pn\to pp\pi^-$ become available from the ANKE experiment at COSY.
