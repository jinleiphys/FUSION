# In Medium modifications to rho from finite pion density effects and dilepton spectrum

> The behavior of the pion dispersion relation in a pion medium is strongly modified by the introduction of a finite chemical potential associated to the finite pion number density. Such behavior is particularly important during the hadronic phase of a relativistic heavy-ion collision, between chemical and thermal freeze-out, where the pion number changing processes, driven by the strong interaction, can be considered to be frozen. We make use of an effective Lagrangian that explicitly respects chiral symmetry through the enforcement of the chiral Ward identities. The pion dispersion relation is computed through the computation of the pion self-energy in a non-perturbative fashion by giving an approximate solution to the Schwinger-Dyson equation for this self-energy. Given the strong coupling between rho vectors and pions, we argue that the modification of the pion mass due to finite pion density effects has to be taken into account self-consistently for the description of the in-medium modifications of rho's. We finally study some possible consequences of finite pion density effects for the low-mass dilepton spectrum produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The finite pion density (parameterized by a chemical potential) produces a moderate broadening of the dilepton spectrum distribution, a moderate increase in the position of the peak, and a decrease in the distribution at the peak position compared to the zero-chemical-potential case, but these modifications are presented as preliminary results compared to SPS data.

## Method

The author starts from the linear sigma model, integrates out the sigma degree of freedom to obtain an effective theory of pions, and then gauges the theory to include rho mesons via covariant derivatives. The pion self-energy is computed non-perturbatively by solving a transcendental equation in the tadpole approximation, and the rho self-energy is computed at one-loop order in the imaginary-time formalism at finite temperature and chemical potential. The dilepton production rate is then obtained under vector meson dominance and rho saturation, using the Bjorken model for the space-time evolution of the collision.

## Key numbers

* Pion effective quartic coupling: α = 6(m_π²/2f_π²), where m_π and f_π are the vacuum pion mass and decay constant.
* Initial hadron proper-time formation: τ₀ = 1 fm.
* Initial temperature: T₀ = 210 MeV.
* Final freeze-out temperature: T_f = 130 MeV.
* Chemical potential used in the computed spectrum: μ = 100 MeV at T = 130 MeV.
* The computed spectrum is compared to data on S + Au collisions at 200 GeV/c A.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Ayala (2002) and Ayala (2003) on pion propagation in a pion medium using the linear sigma model, and applies it to the in-medium modification of rho mesons and dilepton production. It builds on the treatment of chemical freeze-out by Bebie (1992) and Braun-Munzinger (1995), who established that pion number can be considered conserved between chemical and thermal freeze-out, and on the vector dominance framework of Weldon (1990). The results are compared to SPS data from the CERES collaboration (Agakichiev, 1998) and to the baryon-density-driven calculations of Rapp (1997).
