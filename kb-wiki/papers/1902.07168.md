# New paradigm for fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions

> Since their discovery, fluctuations in the initial state of heavy-ion collisions have been understood as originating mostly from the random positions of nucleons within the colliding nuclei. We consider an alternative approach where all the focus is on fluctuations generated by QCD interactions, that we evaluate at leading logarithmic accuracy in the color glass condensate effective theory. We validate our approach using BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and CERN Large Hadron Collider (LHC) data on anisotropic flow. In particular, we show that, compared to standard Glauber-inspired calculations, our formalism provides a better description of the centrality dependence of the ratio of elliptic flow and triangular flow. It also naturally explains the evolution of elliptic flow fluctuations between RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that initial-state fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions originate primarily from QCD interactions (gluon dynamics) rather than from random nucleon positions, evaluated at leading logarithmic accuracy in the color glass condensate (CGC) effective theory. The authors claim this approach provides a better description of the centrality dependence of the ratio of elliptic flow to triangular flow compared to standard Glauber-inspired calculations, and naturally explains the energy dependence of elliptic flow fluctuations between RHIC and LHC energies.

## Method

The authors used an analytical calculation of the energy-density two-point correlation function from the CGC effective theory, derived by Albacete et al. (2018), to compute the rms eccentricity harmonics ε₂{2} and ε₃{2} and the mean eccentricity ε̄₂. These initial-state anisotropies were then related to experimentally measured flow harmonics v₂{2}, v₂{4}, and v₃{2} through linear hydrodynamic response relations vₙ = κₙεₙ, with response coefficients κ₂ and κ₃ treated as free parameters alongside the saturation scale Qₛ₀ and infrared cutoff m.

## Key numbers

- Qₛ₀ = 1.24 GeV for Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV (LHC), with m = 0.14 GeV
- Qₛ₀ = 0.72 GeV for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV (RHIC), with m = 0.14 GeV
- The ratio Qₛ²[LHC]/Qₛ²[RHIC] = 1.57, consistent with the small-x scaling relation (√s_LHC/√s_RHIC)^0.28 from deep inelastic scattering data
- For a uniform disk of radius R = 6.5 fm with Qₛ₀ = 1 GeV and m = 0.14 GeV, the central collision values are ε₂{2} ≈ ε₃{2} ≈ 0.1
- The inelastic cross sections used: σ_inel = 685 fm² for Au+Au collisions, σ_inel = 767 fm² for Pb+Pb collisions

## Context

This work extends the analytical CGC calculation of Albacete et al. (2018) to phenomenological comparisons with heavy-ion data, offering an alternative to standard Glauber-inspired Monte Carlo models such as the TRENTo model (Moreland, 2014) and the IP-Glasma model (Schenke, 2012). The approach differs from IP-Glasma by keeping the transverse nuclear density profile fixed and attributing all fluctuations to color charge density variations, rather than sampling nucleon positions. The results enable further tests of CGC predictions using observables from deep inelastic scattering at a future electron-ion collider, and open the question of how the nontrivial scaling of fluctuations (variance proportional to Qₛ⁶ rather than Qₛ⁴) affects fireball dynamics.
