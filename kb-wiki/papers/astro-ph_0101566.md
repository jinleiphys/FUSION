# Diffusion of Neutrinos in Proto-Neutron Star Matter with Quarks

> Neutrino opacities important in the evolution of a proto-neutron star containing quark matter are studied. The results for pure quark matter are compared with limiting expressions previously derived, and are generalized to the temperatures, neutrino degeneracies and lepton contents encountered in a proto-neutron star's evolution. We find that the appearance of quarks in baryonic matter drastically reduces the neutrino opacity for a given entropy, the reduction being sensitive to the thermodynamic conditions in the mixed quark-hadron phase.

# Diffusion of Neutrinos in Proto-Neutron Star Matter with Quarks

## Key claim

The appearance of quarks in baryonic matter drastically reduces the neutrino opacity for a given entropy, but this reduction is sensitive to the thermodynamic conditions in the mixed quark-hadron phase and is not expected to produce observable effects on the neutrino flux until the proto-neutron star is 10–20 seconds old.

## Method

The authors calculated neutrino cross sections per unit volume (inverse mean free paths) for scattering and absorption by nucleons, leptons, and quarks using a current-current Lagrangian with Fermi's golden rule, reducing the cross sections to two integrals whose integrands are products of simple polynomials and thermal distribution functions. They constructed a mixed hadron-quark phase using Gibbs phase rules, describing hadronic matter with a field-theoretical model of nucleons interacting via sigma, omega, and rho mesons, and quark matter with the MIT Bag model (bag constant B = 200 MeV/fm^3). Diffusion coefficients D2, D3, and D4 were computed for two thermodynamic conditions: trapped neutrinos (entropy per baryon s ≈ 1, total lepton content Y_L ≈ 0.4) and neutrino-free matter (Y_nu ≈ 0, s ≈ 2).

## Key numbers

- The non-degenerate neutrino mean free path in cold quark matter is about ten times larger than in nucleonic matter (Iwamoto, seminal paper).
- Quarks appear in proto-neutron star matter at temperatures up to 30–40 MeV.
- The bag constant used for quark matter is B = 200 MeV/fm^3.
- Nuclear matter empirical properties used: binding energy E_B = -16.0 MeV, effective mass M*/M = 0.6, incompressibility K = 250 MeV, symmetry energy a_sym = 32.5 MeV, saturation density n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}.
- Absorption dominates over scattering by a factor of 2 to 5 at all densities interior to the central densities of proto-neutron stars investigated.
- The weak coupling constant G_F ≈ 1.17 GeV^{-2}.
- The Cabbibo angle cos(theta_C) = 0.973 and weak mixing angle sin^2(theta_W) = 0.231 are used for coupling constants.
- The baryon axial-vector coupling constant g_A = 1.23.

## Context

This work extends the seminal calculation by Iwamoto (1982) of neutrino mean free paths in cold quark matter to the hot, lepton-rich conditions of proto-neutron star evolution, and generalizes previous studies of neutrino opacities in nucleonic matter (Burrows, 1999; Pons, 1999) to include a mixed quark-hadron phase. The results enable detailed simulations of neutrino signals from proto-neutron stars containing quark matter, which could be compared with observations from a Galactic supernova in current and planned neutrino detectors, and have implications for understanding the high-density regime of QCD inaccessible to heavy-ion collider experiments and lattice QCD at finite baryon density.
