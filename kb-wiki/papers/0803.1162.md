# Lepton Scattering off Few-Nucleon Systems at Medium and High Energies

> The interpretation of recent Jlab experimental data on the exclusive process A(e,e'p)B off few-nucleon systems are analyzed in terms of realistic nuclear wave functions and Glauber multiple scattering theory, both in its original form and within a generalized eikonal approximation. The relevance of the exclusive process 4He(e,e'p)^3H for possible investigations of QCD effects is illustrated.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a generalized eikonal approximation (GEA), which partly removes the frozen approximation of standard Glauber theory by accounting for the excitation energy of the residual A-1 system, provides a parameter-free description of exclusive A(e,e'p)B processes on few-nucleon systems that agrees well with JLab data, but quantitative disagreements persist for the left-right asymmetry at high missing momentum.

## Method

The authors calculate differential cross sections for exclusive electrodisintegration of 2H, 3He, and 4He using realistic few-body wave functions (AV18 interaction for 3He; ATMS wave functions for 4He) and treat final state interactions (FSI) via both the standard Glauber approach (GA) and a generalized eikonal approximation (GEA) that includes a correction term Δ_z proportional to the removal energy. Both factorized (FA) and non-factorized (NFA) approaches are compared, and finite formation time (FFT) effects are incorporated by modifying the Glauber profile function with a Q^2-dependent formation length.

## Key numbers

- For 3He(e,e'p)2H, quantitative disagreements with experimental data persist at φ=0 in the region around |p_m| ≈ 0.6–0.65 GeV/c.
- For 4He(e,e'p)3H at JLab kinematics (Q^2 = 1.78 (GeV/c)^2, x ≈ 1.8), FFT effects are too small to be detected.
- FFT effects can unambiguously be observed in the region 5 ≤ Q^2 ≤ 10 (GeV/c)^2 and x = 1.4.
- Measuring the 4He(e,e'p)3H process at p_m ≈ 430 MeV/c and Q^2 ≈ 10 (GeV/c)^2 would be extremely interesting for observing FFT effects.

## Context

This work extends earlier Glauber-based calculations of hadron-nucleus scattering (Glauber, 1959) and their Feynman diagram reformulation (Gribov) to the exclusive A(e,e'p)B process, building on the generalized eikonal approximation developed by Misak (2005). The calculations use realistic wave functions from Kiev and the AV18 interaction (Wiringa, 1995) for 3He, and ATMS wave functions for 4He. The results enable future investigations of color transparency effects via finite formation time phenomena in 4He(e,e'p)3H at higher Q^2, as proposed by Braun.
