# Monopole and quadrupole polarization effects on the alpha-particle description of $^{8}$Be

> We investigate the effect of monopole and quadrupole modes on the elastic alpha-alpha resonance structure of $^{8}$Be. To this end we make a fully microscopic coupled channels calculation with three coupled channels, using the Algebraic Model. The continuum spectrum and wave functions are analyzed in terms of the individual channels to understand the nature of the resonances. It is shown that both monopole and quadrupole modes have a non-negligible effect on the resonances in the alpha-alpha continuum.

## Key claim
Monopole and quadrupole collective modes have a non-negligible effect on the alpha-alpha elastic scattering resonances of 8Be, but only in the energy range where the cluster channel is open and both collective channels are closed, meaning the collective effects appear only during resonance lifetimes in the compound system.

## Method
A fully microscopic coupled channels calculation was performed using the Modified J-Matrix Method (Algebraic Model) with three coupled channels: an alpha-alpha cluster channel, an Sp(2,R) quadrupole channel, and an Sp(2,R) monopole channel. The wave function was expanded in oscillator basis states with a common oscillator parameter, and effective nucleon-nucleon interactions of Gaussian form (Volkov, Minnesota, and modified Hasegawa-Nagata potentials) were used to calculate Hamiltonian matrix elements. The scattering problem was solved by matching the interaction region to asymptotic solutions, with the cluster channel treated as open and the monopole and quadrupole channels as closed.

## Key numbers
- Ground state resonance (0+1): energies of 0.120 MeV (Minnesota), 0.090 MeV (Volkov), and 0.085 MeV (modified Hasegawa-Nagata), with widths of 195 keV, 1910 keV, and 0.2 keV respectively
- Second 0+ resonance (0+2): energies of 32.78 MeV (Minnesota), 30.51 MeV (Volkov), and 33.78 MeV (modified Hasegawa-Nagata), with widths of 823 keV, 761 keV, and 827 keV respectively
- First 2+ resonance (2+1): energies of 2.94 MeV (Minnesota), 2.56 MeV (Volkov), and 2.98 MeV (modified Hasegawa-Nagata), with widths of 2126 keV, 1679 keV, and 1712 keV respectively
- First 4+ resonance (4+1): energies of 12.63 MeV (Minnesota), 10.81 MeV (Volkov), and 12.07 MeV (modified Hasegawa-Nagata), with widths of 7799 keV, 6705 keV, and 4241 keV respectively
- Oscillator parameter b: 1.28 fm (Minnesota), 1.37 fm (Volkov), 1.32 fm (modified Hasegawa-Nagata)
- Cluster channel threshold energies: -49.37 MeV (Minnesota), -54.17 MeV (Volkov), -58.02 MeV (modified Hasegawa-Nagata) relative to monopole break-up energy
- Quadrupole channel threshold energies: 39.22 MeV (Minnesota), 23.16 MeV (Volkov), 25.28 MeV (modified Hasegawa-Nagata) relative to monopole break-up energy

## Context
This work extends previous studies of cluster-monopole and cluster-quadrupole descriptions of 8Be by Filippov et al. and the quadrupolar polarization studies by Deumens et al. and monopolar distortion studies by Kruglanski (1992). It builds on the Resonating Group Method calculations of Tang (1978) and the Sp(2,R) collective model descriptions of 8Be by Caurier and Arickx. The results enable a clear identification of individual resonance states as predominantly quadrupole or monopole in nature, and suggest a rotational behavior for quadrupole-related resonances following L(L+1) energy spacing while monopole-related resonances remain at roughly constant energy.
