# Three-body bound states with zero-range interaction in the Bethe-Salpeter approach

> The Bethe-Salpeter equation for three bosons with zero-range interaction is solved for the first time. For comparison the light-front equation is also solved. The input is the two-body scattering length and the outputs are the three-body binding energies, Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes and light-front wave functions. Three different regimes are analyzed: ({\it i}) For weak enough two-body interaction the three-body system is unbound. ({\it ii}) For stronger two-body interaction a three-body bound state appears. It provides an interesting example of a deeply bound Borromean system. ({\it iii}) For even stronger two-body interaction this state becomes unphysical with a negative mass squared. However, another physical (excited) state appears, found previously in light-front calculations. The Bethe-Salpeter approach implicitly incorporates three-body forces of relativistic origin, which are attractive and increase the binding energy.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter equation for three bosons with zero-range interaction is solved for the first time, revealing that the three-body ground state can have a negative squared mass (unphysical) for two-body interactions that produce a bound two-body state, but becomes physical with positive squared mass when the two-body scattering length is negative (no two-body bound state), forming a deeply bound Borromean system.

## Method

The three-body Bethe-Salpeter equation for the Faddeev component of the vertex function with zero-range interaction was transformed via a shift of variables (k = k' + p/3, q = q' + p/3) to enable Wick rotation to Euclidean space, then solved numerically using spline decomposition. For comparison, the corresponding light-front equation was also solved numerically. The input parameter was the two-body scattering length a, and the outputs were three-body binding energies, Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, and light-front wave functions.

## Key numbers

- For the ground state at the threshold where three-body binding energy B3 = 0 (M3^2 = 9m^2), the inverse scattering length is (a m)^(-1) = -0.78 for the Bethe-Salpeter approach and -0.57 for the light-front approach.
- For the ground state at M3^2 = 0, the inverse scattering length is (a m)^(-1) = -0.51 for Bethe-Salpeter and -0.21 for light-front.
- For the first excited state at M3^2 = 0, the inverse scattering length is (a m)^(-1) = 0.34 for Bethe-Salpeter and 0.50 for light-front, corresponding to two-body binding energies B2 ≈ 0.194m and B2 ≈ 0.582m respectively.
- When the two-body binding energy B2 → 0, the ground state squared mass is M3^2 ≈ -94 m^2 for the Bethe-Salpeter equation and M3^2 = -18 m^2 for the light-front equation.
- When B2 = 0, the excited state three-body binding energy is B3 ≈ 0.066m for Bethe-Salpeter and B3 ≈ 0.013m for light-front.

## Context

This work extends the earlier light-front calculations of Tobias (2015) and Carbonell and Karmanov (2015) by solving the previously unsolved three-body Bethe-Salpeter equation with zero-range interaction. The results confirm that relativistic effects prevent the Thomas collapse (Thomas 1935) in both approaches, but the Bethe-Salpeter approach implicitly incorporates effective three-body forces of relativistic origin through antiparticle intermediate states, making the binding stronger than in the light-front approach. This enables future studies of systems with non-equal masses and provides a benchmark for understanding the role of higher Fock components in relativistic three-body systems.
