# Heavy quarkonium production: Nontrivial transition from pA to AA collisions

> Two novel QCD effects, double color filtering and mutual boosting of the saturation scales in colliding nuclei, affect the transparency of the nuclei for quark dipoles in comparison with proton-nucleus collisions. The former effect increases the survival probability of the dipoles, since color filtering in one nucleus makes the other one more transparent. The second effect acts in the opposite direction and is stronger, it makes the colliding nuclei more opaque than in the case of pA collisions. As a result of parton saturation in nuclei the effective scale is shifted upwards, what leads to an increase of the gluon density at small x. This in turn leads to a stronger transverse momentum broadening in AA compared with pA collisions, i.e. to an additional growth of the saturation momentum. Such a mutual boosting leads to a system of reciprocity equations, which result in a saturation scale, a few times higher in AA than in pA collisions at the energies of LHC. Since the dipole cross section is proportional to the saturation momentum squared, the nuclei become much more opaque for dipoles in AA than in pA collisions. For the same reason gluon shadowing turns out to be boosted to a larger magnitude compared with the product of the gluon shadowing factors in each of the colliding nuclei. All these effects make it more difficult to establish a baseline for anomalous J/Psi suppression in heavy ion collisions at high energies.

## Key claim

The paper argues that two novel QCD effects, double color filtering and mutual boosting of saturation scales, make the transition from proton-nucleus (pA) to nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions nontrivial, meaning that the cold nuclear matter effects in AA collisions cannot be reliably predicted by simply multiplying the suppression factors measured in pA collisions, especially at LHC energies.

## Method

The authors calculate the survival probability of a heavy quark-antiquark dipole propagating through nuclear matter using the color dipole formalism with a saturated parametrization of the dipole cross section fitted to HERA data. For the double-color-filtering effect, they derive analytic expressions for the nuclear suppression factor in AA collisions by averaging over the dipole size distribution, accounting for the fact that large-size dipoles are filtered out in the first nucleus, making the second nucleus more transparent. For the boosting of saturation scales, they solve a system of reciprocity equations that couple the saturation scales in the two colliding nuclei, using the MSTW2008 gluon distribution functions and an infrared cutoff parameter Q₀² ≈ 1.7 GeV² determined from pA data.

## Key numbers

- The coherence time for J/ψ production at RHIC (√s = 200 GeV) and positive rapidities is t_c > 12 fm, exceeding typical nuclear sizes.
- The dipole cross section parametrization uses σ₀ = 23.03 mb, r₀(x₂) = 0.4 fm × (x₂/x₀)^0.144, and x₀ = 3.04 × 10⁻⁴.
- The mean transverse momentum squared of J/ψ in pp collisions is ⟨p_T²⟩ ≈ 4 GeV² at RHIC and 7 GeV² at LHC.
- The infrared cutoff parameter from pA data is Q₀² ≈ 1.7 GeV², nearly independent of energy.
- At LHC (√s = 5.5 TeV) and y = 0, the boosting factors K_A and K_B for central AA collisions reach values several times larger than unity, as shown in Fig. 3.
- Gluon shadowing at LHC and y = 0 is only about 3% at x₂ = 5.5 × 10⁻³, and is neglected in the calculations.

## Context

This work extends the color dipole approach to heavy quarkonium production developed by Kopeliovich et al. (2001) and applied to pA collisions, and it contradicts the simplified "data-driven" multiplicative procedure used by PHENIX (2007) and Cassagnac (2008) to predict cold nuclear matter effects in AA collisions from pA measurements. The paper enables more accurate baseline calculations for anomalous J/ψ suppression in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, showing that the initial-state effects in AA are stronger than previously assumed, which would imply an even smaller transport coefficient of the dense medium extracted from RHIC data.
