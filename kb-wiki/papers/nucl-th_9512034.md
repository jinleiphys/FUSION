# Ion Collisions in Very Strong Electric Fields

> A Classical Trajectory Monte Carlo (CTMC) simulation has been made of processes of charge exchange and ionization between an hydrogen atom and fully stripped ions embedded in very strong static electric fields ($O(10^{10}$ V/m$)$), which are thought to exist in cosmic and laser--produced plasmas. Calculations show that the presence of the field affects absolute values of the cross sections, enhancing ionization and reducing charge exchange. Moreover, the overall effect depends upon the relative orientation between the field and the nuclear motion. Other features of a null-field situation, such as scaling laws, are revisited.

# Ion Collisions in Very Strong Electric Fields

## Key claim

Classical Trajectory Monte Carlo (CTMC) simulations show that static electric fields of order 10^10 V/m enhance ionization and reduce charge exchange cross sections in collisions between hydrogen atoms and fully stripped ions, but the magnitude of these effects depends on the relative orientation of the field with respect to the nuclear motion and on the spatial extension of the field region. The scaling laws that hold in field-free conditions are only approximately valid under strong fields, and only within strict ranges of the parameters.

## Method

The authors performed CTMC simulations treating the two nuclei and the electron as classical particles obeying Newton's laws, with initial electron conditions randomly sampled from a microcanonical distribution that reproduces the quantum mechanical momentum distribution. The equations of motion were integrated from an initial internuclear separation of 20Z atomic units (where Z is the projectile charge) until the nuclei were well separated, with 3 x 10^4 to 26 x 10^4 runs per test. Three field geometries were considered: parallel to the ion velocity, antiparallel, and perpendicular.

## Key numbers

- Electric field strengths studied: 0.03 au and 0.04 au (where 1 au = 5.14 x 10^11 V/m), corresponding to approximately 1.5 x 10^10 V/m and 2.1 x 10^10 V/m
- Impact velocity range: 1 ≤ v_p/v_e ≤ 3.5, corresponding to energies up to 300 KeV/amu
- For a hydrogen atom in the ground state, the classical field-ionization threshold is F_0/16 = 0.0625 au
- At F = 0.04 au, the mean lifetime of an electron in the hydrogen ground state is τ_e ≈ 2 x 10^-11 s (from Damburg and Kolosov 1976)
- For H-C^6+ collisions at v_p = 2 with a finite field region of L = 60 au, σ_cx values are: 9.5 x 10^-16 cm^2 (F = 0), 5.4 x 10^-16 cm^2 (F = +0.03 au), and 17.4 x 10^-16 cm^2 (F = -0.03 au)
- Statistical errors on cross section data range from 1% to a maximum of 20%

## Context

This work extends the study of Olson and MacKellar (1981), who examined electric fields attainable in ordinary laboratory conditions (much weaker than those considered here) and only with Rydberg atoms, by instead studying low-lying electronic states (n=1 and n=2) under fields of order 10^10 V/m. The authors also investigate topics not covered by Olson and MacKellar (1981), including the dependence on projectile charge, initial target quantum state, and the spatial extension of the field region. The results are relevant to double layer phenomena expected in cosmic plasmas (such as on neutron star surfaces) and in laser-produced plasmas for inertial confinement fusion, where fields of 10^8 to 10^11 V/m may be reached (Eliezer and Hora 1989).
