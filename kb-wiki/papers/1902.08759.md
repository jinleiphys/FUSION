# Properties of a potential energy matrix in oscillator basis

> Matrix elements of potential energy are examined in detail. We consider a model problem - a particle in a central potential. The most popular forms of central potential are taken up, namely, square-well potential, Gaussian, Yukawa and exponential potentials. We study eigenvalues and eigenfunctions of the potential energy matrix constructed with oscillator functions. It is demonstrated that eigenvalues coincide with the potential energy in coordinate space at some specific discrete points. We establish approximate values for these points. It is also shown that the eigenfunctions of the potential energy matrix are the expansion coefficients of the spherical Bessel functions in a harmonic oscillator basis. We also demonstrate a close relation between the separable approximation and $L^{2}$ basis (J-matrix) method for the quantum theory of scattering.

## Key claim

The eigenvalues of the potential energy matrix constructed with oscillator functions coincide with the potential energy in coordinate space at specific discrete points, and the eigenfunctions are proportional to the expansion coefficients of spherical Bessel functions in the harmonic oscillator basis. This holds for potentials that can be expanded in even powers of the coordinate or expressed via an integral transformation with Gaussian functions.

## Method

The authors constructed an N×N matrix of the potential energy operator using harmonic oscillator basis functions for a particle in a central potential. They diagonalized this matrix and compared its eigenvalues to the potential evaluated at discrete points determined by the zeros of Laguerre polynomials (for the operator r²) or by an asymptotic approximation. The eigenfunctions were analyzed in coordinate, momentum, and oscillator representations for four potentials: square-well, Gaussian, Yukawa, and exponential.

## Key numbers

- The turning point of the classical oscillator is given by R_n = √(4n + 2L + 3), which defines the region where oscillator functions have non-negligible values.
- The approximate discrete coordinates for eigenvalues of the operator r² are r_α = πb(α + L/2) / R_N for α = 1, 2, ..., N, where R_N is the turning point for the Nth oscillator function.
- The matrix elements of r² between oscillator functions are: diagonal = b²(2n + L + 3/2), off-diagonal = b²√(n(n + L + 1/2)) for m = n-1 and b²√((n+1)(n + L + 3/2)) for m = n+1.

## Context

This work extends the J-matrix method (Heller and Yamani, 1970s) and the algebraic version of the resonating group method (Filippov and Okhrimenko, 1980s) by filling a gap in understanding the properties of the potential energy matrix itself. The results provide a natural way to obtain a local form for the nonlocal potential operator and could enable studying the effect of the Pauli principle on potential energy in cluster systems. The paper is the first in a series planned to cover two-body problems, two-cluster systems, and three-cluster systems.
