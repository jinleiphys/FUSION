# Enhanced Structure Function Of The Nucleon From Deep Inelastic Electron-Proton Scattering

> The proton structure function is re-deduced from the data of deep inelastic electron-proton scattering after enhanced correction that is made due to the multiple scattering effect. The Glauber approach is used to account for the multiple scattering of electron with the quark constituents of the proton. The results are compared with the prediction of the parton model. Appreciable deviation is observed only at low values of the Bjorken scaling, which correspond to the extremely deep inelastic scattering.

## Key claim

The proton structure function \(F_2\) is re-deduced from deep inelastic electron-proton scattering data after applying a Glauber-based multiple-scattering correction; appreciable deviation from the parton-model prediction is observed only at low values of the Bjorken scaling variable \(x\) (extremely deep inelastic scattering), while at higher \(x\) the two models converge.

## Method

The authors treat the proton as a spherical bag containing three valence quarks (uud) and a variable number \(N\) of sea quark-antiquark dipoles. They apply the Glauber multiple-scattering formalism to compute the total scattering amplitude, summing contributions from single, double, and triple scattering of the incident electron off the quark constituents. The quark wave function is taken as a plane wave in the central region (\(s < s_0\)) and a harmonic oscillator wave function in the peripheral region (\(s > s_0\)), with the boundary parameter \(s_0\) adjusted to fit the e-p inelastic cross-section. The resulting differential cross-section replaces the standard \(1/q^4\) factor in the Rosenbluth formula, and the enhanced structure function \(F_2^{\text{Enh}}\) is extracted and compared with parton-model predictions from SLAC experiments E-049, E-061, and E-089.

## Key numbers

* The proton bag root-mean-square radius is taken as \(R_{\text{rms}} = 1.2\) fm.
* The boundary parameter \(s_0\) is found to be \(\frac{1}{3} R_{\text{rms}}\) at the considered energies.
* The double-scattering term contributes about 0.02 of the single-scattering term at the central region, a ratio greater than \(1/137\) (the fine-structure constant).
* The differential cross-section is calculated at \(x = 0.5\) and incident energy \(14\) GeV.
* The enhanced structure function is shown at \(x = 0.08\), \(x = 0.125\), and \(x = 0.55\) compared with parton-model data from SLAC-E-049 and SLAC-E-089.

## Context

This work extends the standard parton model (Feynman, 1969) and the Bjorken scaling framework by incorporating multiple-scattering corrections via the Glauber approach (Glauber, 1959), which had previously been applied mainly to hadron-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions. The results show that multiple scattering inside the proton bag can explain deviations in the structure function at low \(x\) without altering the spin-1/2 or fractional-charge assignments of quarks. The approach enables future studies of shadowing effects in deep inelastic scattering and may be extended to other lepton-nucleon processes.
