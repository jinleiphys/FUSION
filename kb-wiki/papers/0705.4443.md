# Charmonium dynamics in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(S)=200 GeV

> The formation and suppression dynamics of J/Psi, Chi_c and Psi-Prime mesons is studied within the HSD transport approach for Au+Au reactions at the top RHIC energy of sqrt(S)=200 GeV. Two prominent models, which have been discussed for more than a decade, are incorporated, i.e. the 'hadronic comover absorption and reformation' model as well as the 'QGP threshold' scenario, and compared to available experimental data. Our studies demonstrate that both scenarios - compatible with experimental observation at SPS energies - fail severely at RHIC energies. This combined analysis - together with the underestimation of charm elliptic flow - proves that the dynamics of c, c-bar quarks are dominated by partonic interactions in the strong QGP (sQGP) and can neither be modeled by 'hadronic' interactions nor described appropriately by color screening alone.

# Charmonium dynamics in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(S)=200 GeV

## Key claim

Both the "hadronic comover absorption and reformation" model and the "QGP threshold melting" scenario, which are compatible with experimental observations at SPS energies, fail severely at RHIC energies for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV; this failure, together with the underestimation of charm elliptic flow, proves that charm quark dynamics are dominated by partonic interactions in the strong QGP and cannot be modeled by hadronic interactions or color screening alone.

## Method

The HSD (Hadron-String Dynamics) transport approach was used to simulate Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, incorporating two models: the comover model with a single matrix element M0 fixed by SPS data, and the QGP threshold scenario where charmonia are fully dissociated to c + c-bar when local energy density exceeds threshold values (16 GeV/fm^3 for J/Psi, 2 GeV/fm^3 for Chi_c and Psi-Prime). The nuclear modification factor R_AA for J/Psi was calculated as a function of number of participants N_part and compared to PHENIX data, with charmonium reformation channels from D + D-bar included in both scenarios.

## Key numbers

- sqrt(s) = 200 GeV for Au+Au collisions at top RHIC energy
- Threshold energy densities adopted: epsilon_1 = 16 GeV/fm^3 for J/Psi, epsilon_2 = 2 GeV/fm^3 for Chi_c, epsilon_3 = 2 GeV/fm^3 for Psi-Prime (providing fair reproduction of SPS data)
- Energy density in central Au+Au collisions initially reaches values well above 30 GeV/fm^3 and drops below 1 GeV/fm^3 within roughly 5-7 fm/c
- In the simple QGP threshold scenario without reformation, practically all charmonia are dissolved for N_part > 50, incompatible with PHENIX data
- Hadronic states allowed to form only for energy density <= 1 GeV/fm^3 in HSD
- Recombination channels switched on after formation time tau_f = 0.5 fm/c in the local rest frame

## Context

This work extends previous HSD transport studies by Linnyk et al. (2006) and Bratkovskaya et al. (2003, 2004) from SPS to RHIC energies, testing the same models that described SPS data from NA50 and NA60 collaborations. The failure of both the hadronic comover model (Capella, Cassing 1997-2000) and the QGP threshold model (Blaizot, Satz) at RHIC energies, combined with the underestimation of open charm collective flow v2 and R_AA(p_T) from Bratkovskaya et al. (2005), demonstrates that charm quark dynamics require partonic interactions in the strong QGP. This enables future work focused on partonic transport descriptions of heavy quarks rather than hadronic or simple screening models.
