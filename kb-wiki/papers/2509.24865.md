# Quantum Dynamics with Time-Dependent Neural Quantum States

> We present proof-of-principle time-dependent neural quantum state (NQS) simulations to illustrate the ability of this approach to effectively capture key aspects of quantum dynamics in the continuum. NQS leverage the parameterization of the wave function with neural-network architectures. Here, we put NQS to the test by solving the quantum harmonic oscillator. We obtain the ground state and perform coherent state and breathing mode dynamics. Our results are benchmarked against analytical solutions, showcasing an excellent agreement.

## Key claim
Time-dependent neural quantum state (NQS) simulations can accurately reproduce quantum dynamics in the continuum, but only as a proof-of-principle for a single-particle one-dimensional harmonic oscillator; the abstract does not specify that the method is tested only on this simple system with exact analytic solutions available.

## Method
The authors parametrize the wavefunction using a neural network with one input neuron, one hidden layer of 5 neurons with sigmoid activation, and one output neuron, totaling 16 complex parameters. They apply the time-dependent variational principle to derive equations of motion for the parameters, integrate them with a 4th-order Runge-Kutta scheme (time step δt = 0.1), and regularize the quantum geometric tensor with a diagonal shift (λ = 10⁻⁴ for ground state search, λ = i10⁻⁶ for real-time evolution). Ground states are obtained via imaginary time propagation, and dynamics are computed for coherent state oscillations (trap center shift from x₀ = 1 to x₀ = 0) and breathing mode excitations (frequency quench from ω = 1 to ω = 0.5).

## Key numbers
- Ground state search converges when |δE| ≤ 10⁻⁸ for 10 consecutive steps, where E is the energy expectation value.
- Ground state computation takes approximately 1.5 seconds on a Dell Precision 3660.
- Dynamics up to t = 50 (in units of ω₀⁻¹) take approximately 7.5 seconds on a Dell Precision 3660.
- Energy deviations during both coherent state and breathing mode dynamics remain up to 10⁻⁶ (in units of ħω₀).
- Network parameters vary by less than 10% during both dynamical simulations.
- The simulation grid spans x ∈ [-8, 8] with n = 100 points (in units of √(ħ/mω₀)).

## Context
This work extends the NQS approach of Carleo (2017) and Medvidovic (2024) from spin systems and quantum chemistry to continuous single-particle quantum dynamics, benchmarking against analytic harmonic oscillator solutions from Zwiebach (2022). The results demonstrate that a small NQS (16 parameters) can accurately capture coherent state and breathing mode dynamics, suggesting the method could be scaled to higher-dimensional interacting systems where exact solutions are unavailable, as noted by the authors.
