# Relativistic Wave Equation for Fields with Two Mass and Spin States

> I suggest wave equations for the scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and pseudovector fields with different masses for spin zero and one states. Tensor, matrix, and quaternion formulations of fields with two mass and spin states are considered. This is the generalization of the Dirac-Kähler equation on the case of different masses of fields with spin one and zero. The equation matrices obtained are simple linear combinations of matrix elements in the 16-dimensional space. Spin projection operators and solutions of equations (for spin one) in the form of matrix-dyads are obtained. The canonical quantization of fields under consideration is studied. The anomalous interaction of the scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and pseudovector fields with the external electromagnetic field is considered. Three constants which characterize the anomalous magnetic moment and quadrupole electric moment of a particle are introduced.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a generalization of the Dirac-Kähler equation that allows scalar and pseudoscalar fields to have a different mass \(m_0\) from vector and pseudovector fields (mass \(m\)), with the condition \(a > 0\) required for real masses; the Dirac-Kähler case is recovered only when \(a = 1\) (i.e., \(m_0 = m\)).

## Method

The author introduces a system of first-order differential equations (Eqs. 1-4) coupling scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, pseudovector, and antisymmetric tensor fields, with a free parameter \(a\) relating the two mass scales via \(m_0^2 = m^2 / a\). The 16-component system is expressed in matrix form using \(16 \times 16\) matrices that satisfy the Dirac algebra, and is also decomposed into two independent 8-component subsystems describing self-dual and antiself-dual tensors. The equations are further reformulated using quaternion algebra, and spin projection operators are constructed from the Lorentz group generators to extract spin-1 and spin-0 states.

## Key numbers

- The squared mass of the scalar/pseudoscalar states is \(m_0^2 = m^2 / a\) (Eq. 10), where \(m\) is the mass of the vector/pseudovector states and \(a > 0\) is a free parameter.
- The mass spectrum of the vector field yields two states: \(p^2 = -m^2\) and \(p^2 = -m^2 / a\) (Eq. 9).
- The system of units used is \(\hbar = c = 1\), \(\alpha = e^2 / 4\pi = 1/137\), \(e > 0\).

## Context

This work extends the Dirac-Kähler equation (Kahler 1962) to allow distinct masses for spin-0 and spin-1 bosonic fields, generalizing earlier formulations such as the Proca equations (Proca), the Petiau-Duffin-Kemmer equations (Petiau, Duffin, Kemmer), and the Stueckelberg equations (Stueckelberg). The author notes that at \(a = 1\) the system reduces to the Dirac-Kähler fields, which possess an internal \(SO(4,2)\) symmetry group; the condition \(m \neq m_0\) breaks this degeneracy and destroys the higher symmetry. The formalism enables future study of anomalous electromagnetic interactions of these fields, introducing three constants characterizing anomalous magnetic moment and quadrupole electric moment.
