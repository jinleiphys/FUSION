# The bound state problem of S-wave heavy quark meson-aitimeson systems

> We investigated systematically whether the S-wave ($\bar{Q}q$) meson and the ($Q\bar{q}$) meson may form S-wave bound states in a chiral SU(3) quark model by solving the resonating group method equation. Here $Q=c$ or $b$ and $q=u$, $d$ or $s$. Our preliminary calculation disfavors the existence of $I=\frac12$ ($\bar{Q}l$)-($Q\bar{s}$) molecules ($l=u,d$) while favors the existence of isoscalar $B\bar{B}$, $B^*\bar{B}^*$ (J=2) and $B\bar{B}^*$ (C=+) molecules. The existence of isovector (charm-anticharm) and (charm-bottom) molecules is also disfavored. Therefore the resonance-like structure $Z^+(4051)$ is unlikely to be an S-wave $D^*\bar{D}^*$ molecule.

## Key claim

The authors' preliminary calculation in a chiral SU(3) quark model disfavors the existence of I=1/2 and I=1 charm-anticharm and charm-bottom hadronic molecules, as well as I=1/2 bottom-strange molecules, while favoring the existence of isoscalar B\bar{B}, B^*\bar{B}^* (J=2), and B\bar{B}^* (C=+) bound states; the resonance-like structure Z^+(4051) is therefore unlikely to be an S-wave D^*\bar{D}^* molecule, but the authors note that several approximations were used and many systems remain inconclusive.

## Method

The authors solved the resonating group method (RGM) equation for S-wave heavy quark meson-antimeson systems within a chiral SU(3) quark model, considering pseudoscalar (P), vector (V), and scalar meson exchanges between the light quarks of the two mesons. They used three parameter sets (one chiral quark model and two extended chiral quark model sets) and two cutoff values (1100 MeV and 1500 MeV) to calculate binding energies for P\bar{P}, V\bar{V}, and P\bar{V} ± V\bar{P} systems with charm and bottom quarks. Only when all parameter combinations indicated binding was a molecule considered possible.

## Key numbers

- For I=0(l) P\bar{P} systems in the chiral quark model: B\bar{B} binding energy 10.1–26.8 MeV; B\bar{D} binding energy 0.4–12.8 MeV; D\bar{D} binding energy ≤4.9 MeV
- For I=0(l) P\bar{P} systems in the extended chiral quark model: B\bar{B} binding energy 47.2–102.3 MeV; B\bar{D} binding energy 23.1–72.8 MeV; D\bar{D} binding energy 13.7–52.9 MeV
- For I=0(l) V\bar{V} (J=2) systems in the chiral quark model: B^*\bar{B}^* binding energy 34.5–59.2 MeV; D^*\bar{D}^* binding energy 3.6–22.4 MeV
- For I=0(l) V\bar{V} (J=2) systems in the extended chiral quark model: B^*\bar{B}^* binding energy 60.2–120.8 MeV; D^*\bar{D}^* binding energy 17.7–67.9 MeV
- For isospin-breaking B^+B^- P\bar{P} system: binding energy 0.1–10.3 MeV (chiral quark model) and 11.7–43.3 MeV (extended chiral quark model)
- For isospin-breaking B^{*+}B^{*-} V\bar{V} (J=2) system: binding energy 5.3–20.2 MeV (chiral quark model) and 14.9–48.7 MeV (extended chiral quark model)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hadron-level meson exchange study of D\bar{D}/B\bar{B}, D^*\bar{D}^*/B^*\bar{B}^*, and D^*\bar{D}/B^*\bar{B} systems (Liu et al., 2008) by using a quark-level chiral SU(3) model. It also builds on earlier molecular studies by Tornqvist (1990s) on deuteron-like meson-meson states bound by pions, and by Wong (2004) on heavy meson-antimeson combinations in a quark-based model. The results contradict the molecular interpretation of Z^+(4051) as a D^*\bar{D}^* molecule and are inconsistent with the D_s D^* molecule proposed by Lee et al. (2008); the predicted isoscalar bottomonium-like molecules B\bar{B}, B^*\bar{B}^* (J=2), and B\bar{B}^* (C=+) could guide experimental searches for new exotic states.
