# Light nuclei elliptic flow at mid-rapidity in $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0-3.9$ GeV Au+Au collisions using coalescence model

> Light nuclei collective flow is an important probe for understanding their production mechanisms in heavy-ion collisions. The STAR collaboration has reported that the atomic mass number ($A$) scaling of light nuclei elliptic flow $v_2$ is broken at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0-3.9$ GeV. The observations reveals that, while protons maintain negative $v_2$ values at mid-rapidity at both 3.0 and 3.2 GeV, light nuclei $v_2$ exhibit a sign change from negative at 3.0 GeV to positive at 3.2 GeV. In this study, we investigate $v_2$ of protons and deuterons in mid-central Au+Au Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =$ 3.0, 3.2, 3.5 and 3.9 GeV using the JAM2 microscopic transport model. Deuterons are formed via nucleon coalescence, with the spatial distance ${ΔR}$ and momentum difference ${ΔP}$ between constituent protons and neutrons serving as the coalescence criteria. Our calculations successfully reproduce the sign change in deuteron $v_2$ at 3.2 GeV. We observe a strong dependence of nucleon coalescence probability on the azimuthal angle relative to the reaction plane. This effect is primarily driven by the transverse momentum dependence of the mean spatial $\langle {ΔR} \rangle$ and momentum $\langle {ΔP} \rangle$ separations between nucleon pairs, which vary with the nucleon azimuthal angle. Moreover, our analysis demonstrates that the stiffness of the nuclear equation of state plays a crucial role in determining the energy dependence of this sign change in deuteron $v_2$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3.2$ GeV.

## Key claim

The authors show that the sign change of deuteron elliptic flow \(v_2\) from negative at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0\) GeV to positive at 3.2 GeV can be reproduced by a nucleon coalescence afterburner applied to the JAM2 transport model, but only if the nuclear equation of state is soft (\(\kappa = 210\) MeV); a stiff EoS (\(\kappa = 380\) MeV) keeps deuteron \(v_2\) negative at 3.2 GeV, revealing that the EoS stiffness is a crucial condition for the sign inversion.

## Method

The authors used the JAM2 microscopic transport model with a mean-field option to generate Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0, 3.2, 3.5, 3.9\) GeV for impact parameters \(b = 4.3-8.5\) fm (10-40% centrality). At a fixed time of 50 fm/\(c\), deuterons were formed via nucleon coalescence when a proton-neutron pair satisfied both a spatial distance \(\Delta R < 4.5\) fm and a momentum difference \(\Delta P < 0.3\) GeV/\(c\) in the pair rest frame. Elliptic flow \(v_2\) was calculated as \(\langle \cos(2\phi) \rangle\) and also extracted from Fourier fits to the \(dN/d\phi\) distributions.

## Key numbers

* Proton \(v_2\) is negative at mid-rapidity at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0\) and 3.2 GeV, transitioning to positive at 3.5 and 3.9 GeV (within \(0.4 < p_T < 1.0\) GeV/\(c\)).
* Deuteron \(v_2\) is negative at mid-rapidity at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0\) GeV and positive at 3.2, 3.5, and 3.9 GeV (within \(0.8 < p_T < 2.0\) GeV/\(c\)).
* The coalescence criteria used are \(\Delta R < 4.5\) fm and \(\Delta P < 0.3\) GeV/\(c\).
* Two nuclear incompressibility parameters were tested: \(\kappa = 210\) MeV (soft EoS) and \(\kappa = 380\) MeV (stiff EoS).
* At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.2\) GeV, deuteron \(v_2\) is positive for the soft EoS (\(\kappa = 210\) MeV) but remains negative for the stiff EoS (\(\kappa = 380\) MeV).

## Context

This work extends the STAR fixed-target measurements (STAR Collaboration, 2022-2023) that reported a breakdown of atomic-mass-number scaling for light nuclei elliptic flow at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 3.0-3.9\) GeV, specifically the sign change of deuteron \(v_2\) at 3.2 GeV while proton \(v_2\) stays negative. The study uses the JAM2 transport model (Nara, 2000-2022) with coalescence parameters from Xu (2023) to explain this effect via azimuthal-angle-dependent coalescence probability driven by the transverse-momentum dependence of \(\langle \Delta R \rangle\) and \(\langle \Delta P \rangle\). The results enable future constraints on the nuclear incompressibility parameter \(\kappa\) by comparing with STAR data, and deepen understanding of mass-number scaling violations and nucleon coalescence dynamics at several-GeV collision energies.
