# Neutrino many-body flavor evolution: the full Hamiltonian

> We study neutrino flavor evolution in the quantum many-body approach using the full neutrino-neutrino Hamiltonian, including the usually neglected terms that mediate non-forward scattering processes. Working in the occupation number representation with plane waves as single-particle states, we explore the time evolution of simple initial states with up to $N=10$ neutrinos. We discuss the time evolution of the Loschmidt echo, one body flavor and kinetic observables, and the one-body entanglement entropy. For the small systems considered, we observe `thermalization' of both flavor and momentum degrees of freedom on comparable time scales, with results converging towards expectation values computed within a microcanonical ensemble. We also observe that the inclusion of non-forward processes generates a faster flavor evolution compared to the one induced by the truncated (forward) Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that including the full neutrino-neutrino Hamiltonian (with non-forward scattering terms) in a quantum many-body calculation leads to faster flavor evolution compared to the truncated (forward-only) Hamiltonian, but this conclusion is explicitly limited to small systems with up to N=10 neutrinos and simple initial states.

## Method

The authors quantize the neutrino field in a finite volume using plane-wave single-particle states and construct the full Fock-space Hamiltonian including both kinetic terms (with vacuum mixing) and the complete neutrino-neutrino interaction, which contains terms that conserve total three-momentum but allow non-forward scattering. They then perform exact time evolution of simple initial states with N=2, 6, 8, and 10 neutrinos in a two-dimensional momentum setup, computing the Loschmidt echo, one-body flavor and kinetic observables, and the one-body entanglement entropy for both the full and truncated Hamiltonians.

## Key numbers

- System size: up to N=10 neutrinos, with Hilbert space dimension d_{2,4}=28 for the N=2, k=4 toy model
- Energy hierarchy: |p| ~ T >> G_F T^3 >> delta m^2/T, with T ~ 1 MeV giving V ~ 10^8 N fm^3
- Dimensionless parameters: bar T ~ 10^10, bar omega ~ 10^{-3} to 10^{-2}
- The full Hamiltonian produces faster flavor evolution compared to the truncated (forward) Hamiltonian for the small systems studied

## Context

This work extends the quantum many-body approach to neutrino flavor evolution beyond the truncated Hamiltonian used in prior studies such as Pehlivan (2011), which mapped the forward-only Hamiltonian onto a spin-spin model with all-to-all couplings. The authors address the criticism by Johns (2023) that the truncated Hamiltonian is not justified from first principles. This work enables future studies of more realistic initial states (not product states of plane waves), larger systems, and inclusion of neutrino-matter interactions, though the small system sizes and simple initial states limit direct comparison to the quantum kinetic equation approach used in astrophysical simulations.
