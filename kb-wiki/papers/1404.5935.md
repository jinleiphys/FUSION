# Anisotropic flow and flow fluctuations for Au + Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV in a multiphase transport model

> Anisotropic flow coefficients and their fluctuations are investigated for Au+Au collisions at center of mass energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV by using a multi-phase transport model with string melting scenario. Experimental results of azimuthal anisotropies by means of the two- and four-particle cumulants are generally well reproduced by the model including both parton cascade and hadronic rescatterings. Event-by-event treatments of the harmonic flow coefficients $v_n$ (for n = 2, 3 and 4) are performed, in which event distributions of $v_n$ for different orders are consistent with Gaussian shapes over all centrality bins. Systematic studies on centrality, transverse momentum ($p_{T}$) and pseudo-rapidity ($η$) dependencies of anisotropic flows and quantitative estimations of the flow fluctuations are presented. The $p_{T}$ and $η$ dependencies of absolute fluctuations for both $v_2$ and $v_3$ follow similar trends as their flow coefficients. Relative fluctuation of triangular flow $v_3$ is slightly centrality-dependent, which is quite different from that of elliptic flow $v_2$. It is observed that parton cascade has a large effect on the flow fluctuations, but hadronic scatterings make little contribution to the flow fluctuations, which indicates flow fluctuations are mainly modified during partonic evolution stage.

## Key claim

The AMPT model with string melting, parton cascade, and hadronic rescatterings generally reproduces experimental two- and four-particle cumulant flow results for Au+Au at 200 GeV, but the flow fluctuations extracted from cumulants are only upper limits because they contain non-flow contributions that cannot be fully disentangled.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model (string-melting version) with parton cross sections of 3 mb or 10 mb to simulate Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV. They calculated anisotropic flow coefficients v₂, v₃, and v₄ using both the participant plane method (event-by-event) and the Q-cumulant method (two- and four-particle cumulants), then extracted absolute and relative flow fluctuations from the difference between vₙ{2} and vₙ{4} and from the standard deviation of the event-by-event vₙ{P} distributions.

## Key numbers

- For 0%-10% central collisions, the mean number of participant nucleons ⟨Nₚ⟩ = 345.85 ± 0.10 (impact parameter 0.00-4.42 fm); for 70%-80% peripheral collisions, ⟨Nₚ⟩ = 30.60 ± 0.03 (impact parameter 11.68-12.50 fm)
- The strong coupling constant used in the parton cross section is αₛ = 0.47
- Parton cross sections of 10 mb and 3 mb were used; 10 mb gave better description of experimental relative elliptic flow fluctuation data
- Relative elliptic flow fluctuation R_{v₂} in mid-central collisions is approximately 40%, consistent with PHOBOS and STAR measurements
- For the most central collisions (0%-10%), v₂ is dominated by its fluctuation; for triangular flow v₃, the fluctuation magnitude is comparable to v₃ itself for all centrality bins
- v₃{4} is consistent with zero within errors, and v₄{4} is also consistent with zero within errors

## Context

This work extends previous studies of anisotropic flow fluctuations at RHIC by the PHOBOS and STAR experiments (Sorensen 2006, Alver 2007) and theoretical studies using initial-state fluctuation models (Andrade 2006, Petersen 2010, Han 2011). It provides a systematic AMPT-model comparison to experimental cumulant flow data and demonstrates that flow fluctuations are primarily modified during the partonic evolution stage rather than by hadronic rescatterings, which can help constrain the properties of the quark-gluon plasma formed in heavy-ion collisions.
