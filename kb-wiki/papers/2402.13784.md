# Theoretical analysis and predictions for the double electron capture of $^{124}$Xe

> We provide a complete theoretical description of the two-neutrino electron capture in $^{124}$Xe, improving both the nuclear and the atomic structure calculations. We improve the general formalism through the use of the Taylor expansion method, leading to higher order terms in the decay rate of the process. The nuclear part is treated with pn-QRPA and interacting shell model (ISM) methods. The nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) are calculated with the pn-QRPA method with spin restoration by fixing the input parameters so that the experimental decay rate is reproduced, resulting in values significantly lower than in previous calculations. The validity of the pn-QRPA NMEs is tested by showing their values to be comparable with the ones for double-beta decay with emission of two electrons of $^{128,130}$Te, which have similar pairing features. Within the ISM, we reproduce the total experimental half-life within a factor of two and predict the capture fraction to the KK channel of about 74\%. We also predict the capture fractions to other decay channels and show that for the cumulative decay to the $\rm{KL_{1}}$-$\rm{KO_{1}}$ channels, a capture fraction of about 24\% could be observed experimentally. On the atomic side, calculations are improved by accounting for the Pauli blocking of the decay of innermost nucleon states and by considering all $s$-wave electrons available for capture, expanding beyond the K and L$_1$ orbitals considered in previous studies. We also provide improved atomic relaxation energies of the final atomic states of $^{124}$Te, which may be used as input for background modeling in liquid Xenon experiments.

## Key claim

The paper provides a complete theoretical description of the two-neutrino double electron capture in $^{124}$Xe, but the nuclear matrix elements from the pn-QRPA method are significantly lower than in previous calculations, and the interacting shell model reproduces the total experimental half-life only within a factor of two.

## Method

The authors used two nuclear structure methods: the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) with isospin restoration and the interacting shell model (ISM) in the $jj55$ model space with up to four nucleon excitations. The atomic structure was treated with the Dirac-Hartree-Fock-Slater (DHFS) self-consistent framework, and the decay rate formalism was improved using a Taylor expansion method including terms up to fourth power in lepton energies, which defines new nuclear matrix element ratios $\xi_{31}^{2\nu\textrm{ECEC}}$ and $\xi_{51}^{2\nu\textrm{ECEC}}$.

- ISM nuclear matrix element $M_{GT-1}^{2\nu \rm{ECEC}}$ for the SVD Hamiltonian with jump=4: 0.0291

- $Q$-value used: $Q=2856.73$ keV

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of $2\nu$ECEC in $^{124}$Xe by Suhonen (2013), Pirinen (2015), and Coello Perez (2019), improving both the atomic and nuclear treatments. The atomic calculations go beyond the K and L$_1$ orbital captures considered in prior studies by including all $s$-wave electrons up to the O$_1$ subshell, and the nuclear calculations use full summation over intermediate $1^+$ states rather than the single-state dominance approximation used by Coello Perez (2019). The improved atomic relaxation energies and capture fractions can serve as input for background modeling in large liquid xenon experiments such as XENONnT and DARWIN.
