# Symmetries and order in cluster nuclei

> It is shown that the rotational band structure of the cluster states in 12C and 16O can be understood in terms of the underlying discrete symmetry that characterizes the geometrical configuration of the alpha-particles, i.e. an equilateral triangle for 12C, and a regular tetrahedron for 16O. The structure of rotational bands provides a fingerprint of the underlying geometrical configuration of alpha-particles. Finally, some first results are presented for odd-cluster nuclei.

## Key claim

The rotational band structure of cluster states in 12C and 16O can be understood as arising from the discrete point-group symmetry of the underlying alpha-particle configuration (equilateral triangle for 12C, regular tetrahedron for 16O), but the paper notes that these configurations do not correspond to exact dynamical symmetries of the Algebraic Cluster Model Hamiltonian; instead, approximate solutions for the rotation-vibration spectrum are obtained.

## Method

The paper applies the Algebraic Cluster Model (ACM), which uses spectrum-generating algebras U(7) for three-body clusters (12C) and U(10) for four-body clusters (16O), to describe the relative motion of identical alpha particles. The Hamiltonian is required to be invariant under the permutation group Sn of the n identical alpha particles, and the rotation-vibration spectrum is derived from the potential energy surface that yields equilibrium shapes corresponding to the point-group symmetries D3h (triangular) and Td (tetrahedral). For odd-cluster nuclei, the Cluster Shell Model (CSM) is introduced, where single-particle energies are obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation in a deformed cluster potential with the corresponding double point-group symmetry.

## Key numbers

- For 12C: beta = 1.74 fm (determined from first minimum in elastic form factor)
- For 16O: beta = 2.07 fm (determined from first minimum in elastic form factor)
- Alpha = 0.56 fm^-2 (determined by the size of the alpha particle)
- 12C: B(E2;2_1+ -> 0_1+) = 8.4 e^2 fm^4 (theory) vs 7.6 +/- 0.4 e^2 fm^4 (experiment)
- 12C: B(E3;3_1- -> 0_1+) = 73 e^2 fm^6 (theory) vs 103 +/- 17 e^2 fm^6 (experiment)
- 12C: Quadrupole moment Q(2_1+) = 5.2 e fm^2 (theory) vs 5.3 +/- 4.4 e fm^2 (experiment)
- 12C: Charge radius = 2.389 fm (theory) vs 2.468 +/- 0.012 fm (experiment)
- 16O: B(E3;3_1- -> 0_1+) = 215 e^2 fm^6 (theory) vs 205 +/- 10 e^2 fm^6 (experiment)
- 16O: B(E4;4_1+ -> 0_1+) = 425 e^2 fm^8 (theory) vs 378 +/- 133 e^2 fm^8 (experiment)
- 16O: Charge radius = 2.639 fm (theory) vs 2.710 +/- 0.015 fm (experiment)

## Context

This work extends the Algebraic Cluster Model previously developed for 12C (Marin et al., various years) and 16O (Bijker et al., various years) by providing a unified framework linking discrete symmetries to rotational band fingerprints. It builds on earlier alpha-cluster models by Wheeler (1930s), Hafstad and Teller (1930s), Brink (various years), and Robson (various years), and is motivated by recent experimental measurements of new rotational excitations in 12C (Freer 2007, Kirsebom, Marin, Itoh, Gai, Freer 2011). The Cluster Shell Model introduced for odd-cluster nuclei is analogous to the Nilsson model but with a deformed field generated by the cluster potential, and first results for 9Be and 9B have been published (Della Rocca et al. 2017), with applications to 13C in progress.
