# A new single-particle basis for nuclear many-body calculations

> Predominantly, harmonic oscillator single-particle wave functions are the choice as a basis in ab-initio nuclear many-body calculations. These wave-functions, although very convenient in order to evaluate the matrix elements of the interaction in the laboratory frame, have a too fast fall-off at large distances. In the past, in alternative to the harmonic oscillator, other single-particle wave functions have been proposed. In this work we propose a new single-particle basis, directly linked to the nucleon-nucleon interaction. This new basis is orthonormal and complete, has the proper asymptotic behavior at large distances and does not contain the continuum which would pose severe convergence problems in nuclear many body calculations. We consider the newly proposed NNLO-opt nucleon-nucleon interaction, without any renormalization. We show that unlike other basis, this single-particle representation has a computational cost similar to the harmonic oscillator basis with the same space truncation and it gives lower energies for ${}^6He$ and ${}^6Li$.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new single-particle basis, called the Localized Deuteron Basis (LDB), constructed directly from the S-wave part of the deuteron ground-state wave function using the bare NNLO-opt nucleon-nucleon interaction; the basis is claimed to yield lower ground-state energies than the harmonic oscillator basis for ⁶He and ⁶Li with comparable computational cost, but the authors note that it does not improve results for ⁴He and that the optimization performed only modifies the "tail" of all radial wave functions rather than optimizing for each single-particle angular momentum separately.

## Method

The authors construct the LDB by taking the S-wave part of the deuteron ground-state wave function from the NNLO-opt interaction, multiplying it by a center-of-mass localization function (the Bessel-Fourier transform of an exponential with parameter α), and performing a singular value decomposition of the resulting two-nucleon wave function in momentum space to extract orthonormal single-particle radial wave functions Q_{n,l}(k). These radial functions are then expanded in a large harmonic oscillator basis (up to N_{2max}=16-22) to obtain two-body matrix elements of the interaction via eq. (2.12), while kinetic energy and center-of-mass terms are evaluated directly. Many-body calculations for ⁴He, ⁶He, and ⁶Li are performed using the Hybrid-Multideterminant (HMD) method with 100-200 Slater determinants, using truncation type (a) where 2n+l ≤ e_max.

## Context

This work extends the search for alternatives to the harmonic oscillator basis in ab-initio nuclear many-body calculations, following the Coulomb-Sturm basis (refs. [15],[16]) which had the correct asymptotic behavior but produced much higher energies than the harmonic oscillator, and the natural orbit basis (ref. [18]) which gave lower energies but required a preliminary shell model calculation. The LDB is a corrected version of an earlier basis (ref. [17]) that had Gaussian falloff, now given the proper exponential asymptotic behavior exp(-μr) through the choice of center-of-mass localization. The basis enables more accurate calculations for halo nuclei and systems sensitive to large-distance behavior, though the authors note that optimization for each single-particle angular momentum separately (as suggested in ref. [15]) could further improve energies.
