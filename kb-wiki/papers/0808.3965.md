# Hadrons in Strong Electric and Magnetic Fields

> We use chiral perturbation theory to investigate hadronic properties in strong electric and magnetic fields. A strong-field power counting is employed, and results for pions and nucleons are obtained using Schwinger's proper-time method. In the limit of weak fields, we accordingly recover the well known one-loop chiral perturbation theory results for the electric and magnetic polarizabilities of pions and nucleons. In strong constant fields, we extend the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation. For the case of electric fields, we find that non-perturbative effects result in hadron decay. For sufficiently strong magnetic fields, the chiral analysis confirms that the nucleon hierarchy becomes inverted giving rise to proton beta-decay. Properties of asymptotic expansions are explored by considering weak field limits. In the regime where the perturbative expansion breaks down, the first-order term gives the best agreement with the non perturbative result.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory can be used to compute hadronic properties in strong constant electric and magnetic fields where the ratio \(e F_{\mu\nu}/m_\pi^2\) is not small, but the results are only valid at fixed order in the chiral expansion while being non-perturbative in the field strength; when the perturbative expansion in field strength breaks down, the first-order term gives the best agreement with the non-perturbative result.

## Method

The author employs a strong-field power counting where \(e F_{\mu\nu}/\Lambda_\chi^2 \sim \varepsilon^2\) alongside the usual chiral counting, so that charged pion propagators must be summed to all orders in the external field using Schwinger's proper-time method. One-loop calculations for pions and nucleons are performed in constant magnetic and electric fields using the leading-order chiral Lagrangian, with tree-level contributions from the next-to-leading order Lagrangian (terms proportional to \(\alpha_9\) and \(\alpha_{10}\)). The neutral pion effective mass is obtained from loop diagrams that do not cancel, while the charged pion mass shift receives only tree-level contributions at this order.

## Key numbers

- Neutral pion magnetic polarizability: \(\beta_M^{\pi^0} = \frac{\alpha_{f.s}}{3 (4\pi f)^2 m_\pi}\), where \(\alpha_{f.s.} = e^2/4\pi\) and \(f = 130\ \text{MeV}\)
- Charged pion magnetic polarizability: \(\beta_M^{\pi^\pm} = -8\,\alpha_{f.s.} \frac{\alpha_9 + \alpha_{10}}{f^2 m_\pi}\)
- Neutral pion energy shift in weak magnetic field: \(E_{\pi^0}(\bm{p}=\bm{0}) = m_\pi + \frac{m_\pi^3}{(4\pi f)^2}\left[-\frac{1}{6}\left(\frac{eB}{m_\pi^2}\right)^2 + \frac{7}{180}\left(\frac{eB}{m_\pi^2}\right)^4 - \frac{31}{630}\left(\frac{eB}{m_\pi^2}\right)^6 + \ldots\right]\)
- The expansion parameter \(\xi = e|B|/(\sqrt{6}\,m_\pi^2)\) is defined such that the first two terms of the asymptotic expansion are of order unity; the LO result agrees to \(\sim 20\%\) in the non-perturbative regime where \(\xi \sim 2/3\)

## Context

This work extends the model-independent chiral perturbation theory calculations of the quark condensate in external fields by Shushpanov (1997), Agasian (1999), and Cohen (2007) to the hadron spectrum itself, providing the first systematic treatment of pions and nucleons in strong fields where the field strength is comparable to or larger than \(m_\pi^2\). The results enable lattice QCD simulations using external field techniques (DeGrand, 2006) to extract hadron properties from lattices where the quantization condition forces \(e F_{\mu\nu}/m_\pi^2\) to be of order unity, and extend the method proposed by Detmold (2006) for extracting charged particle properties from lattice QCD.
