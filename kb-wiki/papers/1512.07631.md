# Observable consequences of event-by-event fluctuations of HBT radii

> We explore the effects of event-by-event fluctuations of Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) radii and show how they can be observed experimentally. The relation of measured HBT radii extracted from ensemble-averaged correlation functions to the mean of their event-by-event probability distribution is clarified. We propose a method to experimentally determine the mean and variance of this distribution and test it on an ensemble of fluctuating events generated with the viscous hydrodynamic code VISH2+1. Using the same code, the sensitivity of the mean and variance of the HBT radii to the specific QGP shear viscosity $η/s$ is studied. We report sensitivity of the mean pion HBT radii and their variances to the temperature dependence of $η/s$ near the quark-hadron transition at a level similar (10-20%) to that which was previously observed for elliptic and quadrangular flow of charged hadrons [1].

## Key claim

Event-by-event fluctuations of HBT radii can be experimentally accessed by measuring the mean and variance of their probability distribution, and these quantities show sensitivity to the temperature dependence of the QGP shear viscosity \(\eta/s\) near the quark-hadron transition at a level similar (10-20%) to that previously observed for elliptic and quadrangular flow of charged hadrons.

## Method

The authors use the viscous hydrodynamic code VISH2+1 to generate an ensemble of 5000 fluctuating 200\(A\) GeV Au+Au events at 0-10% centrality. They propose a binning technique to estimate the direct ensemble average (DEA) of HBT radii from the measured physical ensemble average (PEA), and an iterative sub-ensemble method to estimate the variance of the event-by-event HBT radius distribution. These estimators are tested on the simulated events, and the sensitivity of the mean and variance of the HBT radii to different values of \(\eta/s\) (0.00, 0.08, and 0.20) in the QGP phase is studied.

## Key numbers

* For 5000 0-10% Au+Au events at 200\(A\) GeV, the relative width \(\sigma_{o}/\langle R^{2}_{o}\rangle_{N_{\mathrm{ev}}}\) of the HBT radius \(R^{2}_{o}\) increases with pair momentum \(K_{T}\) and decreases with increasing \(\eta/s\).
* The sensitivity of the mean pion HBT radii and their variances to the temperature dependence of \(\eta/s\) near the quark-hadron transition is at the level of 10-20%.
* The variance estimation procedure uses \(n_{b}=2\) sub-ensembles and is tested with \(M=100\) and \(M=10000\) iterations, and with \(\tilde{n}_{b}=2\) and \(\tilde{n}_{b}=10\) bins for the mean estimation.

## Context

This work extends the study of event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions from momentum-space observables (such as anisotropic flow coefficients \(v_{n}\) studied by Molnar (2014)) to space-time observables via HBT interferometry. It clarifies the relation between measured HBT radii from ensemble-averaged correlation functions and the true mean of the event-by-event distribution, and proposes a method to extract both the mean and variance of that distribution. This enables new experimental probes of the temperature dependence of \(\eta/s\) near the quark-hadron transition that are complementary to flow measurements, providing additional constraints on the properties of the quark-gluon plasma.
