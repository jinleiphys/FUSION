# From fusion to total disassembly: global stopping in heavy-ion collisions

> Using the quantum molecular dynamics model, we aim to investigate the emis- sion of light complex particles, and degree of stopping reached in heavy-ion colli- sions. We took incident energies between 50 and 1000 MeV/nucleon. In addition, central and peripheral collisions and different masses are also considered. We ob- serve that the light complex particles act in almost similar manner as anisotropic ratio. In other words, multiplicity of light complex particles is an indicator of global stopping in heavy-ion collisions. We see that maximum light complex particles and stopping is obtained for heavier masses in central collisions.

## Key claim

The multiplicity of light complex particles (LCPs, fragments with 2 ≤ A ≤ 4) acts as an indicator of global stopping in heavy-ion collisions, with the relationship holding across incident energies from 50 to 1000 MeV/nucleon, but only for symmetric colliding nuclei; the authors note that asymmetric nuclei have a "quite different picture."

## Method

The authors used the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) model with a hard equation of state and free energy-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross-section to simulate thousands of events for six symmetric reactions (²⁰Ne+²⁰Ne, ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca, ⁵⁸Ni+⁵⁸Ni, ⁹³Nb+⁹³Nb, ¹³⁹La+¹³⁹La, and ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au) at incident energies between 50 and 1000 MeV/nucleon. They varied impact parameters from central to peripheral collisions and analyzed global stopping using the anisotropy ratio ⟨Rₐ⟩ and rapidity distributions, while identifying LCPs via both the minimum spanning tree (MST) method and the simulated annealing clusterization algorithm (SACA).

## Key numbers

- Incident energies studied: 50 to 1000 MeV/nucleon.
- For ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca at 50 MeV/nucleon, 60% randomization (⟨Rₐ⟩ ≈ 0.6) can be achieved.
- The mass dependence of both ⟨Rₐ⟩ and LCPs*/nucleon follows a power law ∝ A^τ with τ ≈ 0.1, 0.07, and 0.09 for different cases.
- For inclusive (impact parameter averaged) collisions, the randomization is close to 0.6, indicating no global stopping is reached.
- LCPs are defined as fragments with 2 ≤ A ≤ 4; IMFs are defined as fragments with 5 ≤ A ≤ 65.

## Context

This work extends the study of global stopping and thermalization in heavy-ion collisions, building on earlier reports by Goss (year not given) and Moret (year not given) that linked stopping to fragment production. The authors use the QMD model to systematically correlate LCP multiplicity with the anisotropy ratio ⟨Rₐ⟩ across a wide range of masses and energies, showing that LCPs can serve as a practical barometer for stopping. The results enable future studies to use LCP multiplicity as a simpler experimental observable for diagnosing the degree of randomization and thermalization in heavy-ion reactions, and they confirm earlier findings by J. Singh (year not given) that asymmetric nuclei behave differently from symmetric ones.
