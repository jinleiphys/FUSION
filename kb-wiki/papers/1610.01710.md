# Isospin decomposition of the $γ^{(*)} N \to N^*$ transitions as input for constructing models of neutrino-induced reactions in the nucleon resonance region

> We present our recent efforts to determine the matrix elements associated with the transition between the nucleon and a nucleon resonance induced by the vector current, which are necessary ingredients for models of neutrino-induced reactions in the resonance region. This is accomplished by making the comprehensive analysis of the data for various meson photo- and electro-production reactions off the nucleon within a sophisticated coupled-channels framework, which is known as the ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels model. We also give a brief introduction to our project for constructing a unified neutrino reaction model conducted at the J-PARC Branch of the KEK Theory Center.

## Key claim

The vector-current transition matrix elements between the nucleon and nucleon resonances, extracted from photo- and electro-production data via the ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels model, can serve as input for neutrino reaction models, but the extracted helicity amplitudes still show significant analysis dependence for several high-mass resonances, meaning that more complete experimental data are needed to eliminate this ambiguity.

## Method

The authors performed a comprehensive coupled-channels analysis of over 27,000 data points from pion-, photon-, and electron-induced meson-production reactions off the nucleon, using the ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) model. The model solves a coupled-channels integral equation for partial-wave amplitudes (specified by total angular momentum J, parity P, and total isospin I) for channels including πN, ηN, πΔ, ρN, σN, KΛ, and KΣ, with parameters determined by fitting to unpolarized differential cross sections and spin polarization observables. Helicity amplitudes for the γN → N* transitions were extracted from the residues of the reaction amplitudes at each resonance pole position, and the proton and neutron results were combined to decompose the electromagnetic transition form factors into isoscalar and isovector parts.

## Key numbers

- Over 27,000 data points were fitted to construct the latest published model, covering meson production reactions up to approximately 2 GeV in photon lab energy.
- For the Δ(1232) 3/2+ resonance at the photon point, the proton helicity amplitudes from this analysis (A1/2 = -133 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/², phase -16°; A3/2 = -257 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/², phase -3°) agree almost perfectly with the Bonn-Gatchina group results (A1/2 = -131 ± 3.5 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/², phase -19° ± 2°; A3/2 = -254 ± 4.5 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/², phase -9° ± 1°).
- For the N(1535) 1/2- resonance at the photon point, the proton helicity amplitude A1/2 from this analysis is 161 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/² (phase 8°), compared to the Bonn-Gatchina value of 116 ± 10 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/² (phase 7° ± 6°).
- For the neutron case, the N(1535) 1/2- resonance shows A1/2 = -112 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/² (phase 16°) from this analysis, versus -103 ± 11 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/² (phase 8° ± 5°) from Bonn-Gatchina.
- The electroproduction analysis is being performed up to Q² = 6 GeV² and W = 1.7 GeV for single-pion electroproduction off the proton.

## Context

This work extends the ANL-Osaka dynamical coupled-channels model (Kamano et al., 2013; Kamano et al., 2016) to determine the vector-current transition form factors needed for neutrino reaction models in the resonance region, complementing earlier efforts that assumed certain Q² dependence for axial current matrix elements. The extracted helicity amplitudes are compared with results from the Bonn-Gatchina group (2015), showing good agreement for well-determined resonances like Δ(1232) but significant analysis dependence for higher-mass states. The results enable the construction of a unified neutrino-nucleus reaction model being developed at the J-PARC Branch of KEK Theory Center, and the upcoming complete experiments at JLab, ELSA, and MAMI will help reduce the current analysis dependence.
