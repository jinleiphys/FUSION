# Model for Resonance Enhancement of P- and T-noninvariant Effects in Neutron Reactions

> We consider a simplified model for resonant neutron-nucleus interaction with coupled channels. An analytical solution is given for two coupled channels and arbitrary neutron orbital momentum. A case of a week channel coupling, corresponding to narrow Breit-Wigner resonance, is analyzed in details. As far as the total width of a resonance coincides with the neutron width, the model is directly appropriate only for light nuclei. We study a mixing of two narrow s- and p-wave resonances by P- and P-,T-noninvariant potentials in the first order of perturbation theory. As an example a close-lying pair of s- and p-wave resonances of the $^{35}$Cl nucleus is considered. The favorable possibilities are shown to exist on thick targets for measurements beyond the resonance widths. In particular, an interference minimum near s-wave resonance is of interest for P-odd neutron spin rotation on light spinless nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper presents a simplified, exactly solvable coupled-channels model for resonant neutron-nucleus interactions that reproduces narrow Breit-Wigner resonances, but the model is directly appropriate only for light nuclei because the total width of a resonance coincides with the neutron width. The study shows that favorable possibilities exist for measuring P- and T-noninvariant effects on thick targets beyond the resonance widths, particularly in an interference minimum near an s-wave resonance.

## Method

The author constructs a two-channel scattering model with square-well potentials of equal radius, coupling an elastic channel to a single inelastic channel representing target excitation. Analytical solutions for the radial wavefunctions are obtained using spherical Bessel functions, and first-order perturbation theory is applied to compute S-matrix corrections from P- and P,T-noninvariant single-particle potentials. The model is then applied to a close-lying pair of s- and p-wave resonances in the \(^{35}\)Cl nucleus as an illustrative example.

## Key numbers

- The enhancement of mixing amplitude in a resonance with orbital momentum \(l\) compared to potential scattering is \(\sim(\omega/\gamma_l)^{1/2}(1/(kR)^{2l+1})\), where \(\omega\) is a characteristic single-particle energy and \(\gamma_l\) is the reduced width of the resonance.
- Transmission asymmetry of neutrons with opposite helicities in p-wave resonances may be as much as \(10^{-3}\) to \(10^{-1}\), which is 5–7 orders of magnitude greater than a similar effect in N–N scattering.
- The upper limit on mixing of T-noninvariant forces to T-invariant ones has been lowered to the level of \(10^{-3}\) to \(10^{-4}\) from studies of T-noninvariant effects in nuclear processes and electric dipole moments.

## Context

This work extends the concept of resonance enhancement of P- and T-noninvariant effects introduced in Refs. [8a,8b], which investigated the energy dependence of these effects near s- and p-wave resonances. The model provides an exactly solvable alternative to phenomenological approaches such as R-matrix theory (Ref. [13a]) and the shell model approach to nuclear reactions (Ref. [14a]), which have uncertainties in the phases of S-matrix elements that can produce uncontrolled fake effects. The results enable more reliable estimates of P- and T-noninvariant observables in neutron transmission experiments on light nuclei, particularly for P-odd neutron spin rotation measurements.
