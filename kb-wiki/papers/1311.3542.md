# One pion production in neutrino-nucleon scattering and the different parametrizations of the weak $N\rightarrowΔ$ vertex

> The $N \to Δ$ weak vertex provides an important contribution to the one pion production in neutrino-nucleon and neutrino-nucleus scattering for $πN$ invariant masses below 1.4 GeV. Beyond its interest as a tool in neutrino detection and their background analyses, one pion production in neutrino-nucleon scattering is useful to test predictions based on the quark model and other internal symmetries of strong interactions. Here we try to establish a connection between two commonly used parametrizations of the weak $N \to Δ$ vertex and form factors (FF) and we study their effects on the determination of the axial coupling $C_5^A(0)$, the common normalization of the axial FF, which is predicted to hold 1.2 by using the PCAC hypothesis. Predictions for the $ν_μ p \to μ^- pπ^+$ total cross sections within the two approaches, which include the resonant $Δ^{++}$ and other background contributions in a coherent way, are compared to experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper establishes a connection between two commonly used parametrizations of the weak N to Delta vertex and form factors, showing that the different values obtained for the axial coupling C5^A(0) (ranging from 0.867 to 1.38) depend on the treatment of the Delta resonance and the consistency of vertex-propagator choices, with the PCAC-predicted value of 1.2 serving as a reference.

## Method

The authors compare two parametrizations of the weak N to Delta vertex: a covariant multipole decomposition (Sachs-type) used in their previous BLM approach and the "normal parity" (NP) decomposition used by other groups. They derive explicit relations between the two sets of vector form factors (C3^V, C4^V, C5^V, C6^V) and the multipole form factors (G_M, G_E, G_C), then compute total cross sections for nu_mu p -> mu- p pi+ including the resonant Delta++ and background contributions in a coherent way, using the complex mass scheme to handle the Delta instability.

## Key numbers

- C3^V(0) = 2.02, C4^V(0) = -1.24, C5^V(0) = 0, C6^V(0) = -0.24 (from the multipole decomposition using m_Delta = 1.211 GeV, m_N = 0.940 GeV, G_M(0) = 2.97, G_E(0) = 0.055)
- C3^V(0) = 2.13, C4^V(0) = -1.51, C5^V(0) = 0.48, C6^V(0) = 0 (from the HNV normal parity parametrization)
- The PCAC hypothesis predicts C5^A(0) = 1.2; quark models give C5^A(0) ~ 1; fitted values from different models range from 0.867 to 1.38
- The vector form factor dipole mass is M_V = 0.82 GeV (BLM) or 0.84 GeV (HNV)
- The analysis applies for pi N invariant masses below 1.4 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous BLM approach (Barbero, Lopez Castro, Mariano, 2008) and connects it to the HNV model (Hernandez, Nieves, Valverde, 2007) and the model of Lalakulich et al. (2013), which use the normal parity decomposition. The paper clarifies that inconsistent choices of the parameter A (using A = -1/3 in vertices but A = -1 in the propagator) lead to non-physical amplitudes, and that the different C5^A(0) values obtained by various groups (Fogli and Nardulli, 1979; Hemmert et al., 1995; Sato and Lee, 2003; Hernandez et al., 2007; Leitner et al., 2009; Lalakulich et al., 2013) stem from these consistency issues and different treatments of background contributions. The results enable more reliable extraction of the axial form factor from neutrino-nucleon scattering data, which is essential for reducing background in neutrino oscillation experiments like MiniBooNE, SciBooNE, and Minerva.
