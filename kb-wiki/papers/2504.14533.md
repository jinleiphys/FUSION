# An effective finite-range Gogny-type interaction for the quantum molecular dynamics like model

> In this work, we propose an effective finite-range Gogny-type interaction that can be directly used in the quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) like model. Two methods for determining the parameters of the effective interaction are discussed. The first method establishes an approach to connect the conventional Gogny interaction in nuclear structure to that in heavy-ion collisions, the second method allows for the description of the symmetry energy varying from the supersoft to stiff, as well as the momentum-dependent symmetry potential, exhibiting behaviors ranging from monotonic to non-monotonic variations. This effective interaction opens up opportunities for a deeper understanding of finite-range interactions and non-monotonic momentum-dependent symmetry potentials in future studies.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an effective finite-range Gogny-type interaction (named QG interaction) for use in quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) models, with two parameter-determination methods: one that connects the interaction to conventional Gogny interactions used in nuclear structure, and another that allows tuning the symmetry energy stiffness from supersoft to stiff and the momentum-dependent symmetry potential from monotonic to non-monotonic behavior.

## Method

The authors derive a parametrized finite-range interaction consisting of two Gaussian terms and a density-dependent zero-range term, with 11 parameters. The first method maps the conventional Gogny interaction parameters (D1, D1S) to the QG interaction by equating the potential energy in the Hartree-Fock approximation to that in the Hartree approximation used in QMD. The second method fits the symmetric nuclear matter equation of state to the Gogny D1 results within three times normal density, allowing multiple parameter solutions that yield different symmetry energy stiffnesses. The interaction is then tested in cold nuclear matter by taking the plane-wave limit of the QMD wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The range parameters are fixed at mu_1 = 0.7 fm and mu_2 = 1.2 fm, matching the conventional Gogny interaction.
- The QG interaction sets are labeled by the slope of symmetry energy L; for example, QG67 has L = 64 MeV at saturation density.
- The symmetry energy at saturation density is constrained to be around 32 MeV.
- The symmetric nuclear matter equation of state is fitted within 3 times normal density with reduced chi-squared less than 1.
- 15 parameter sets of the QG interaction are provided in an appendix for practical use.

## Context

This work extends efforts to incorporate finite-range interactions into transport models for heavy-ion collisions, following earlier implementations in the IBUU model (Das 2003), AMD model (Akira 2003), CoMD model (Papa 2024), and dcQMD model (Cozma 2021). The authors note that previous Gogny-type interactions used in transport models were not directly related to the conventional Gogny interaction used in nuclear structure. The new interaction enables systematic study of non-monotonic momentum-dependent symmetry potentials, which recent reanalysis of neutron-to-proton yield ratios by Yang (2024) suggests may be needed and which cannot be explained by zero-range Skyrme interactions (Dutra 2012).
