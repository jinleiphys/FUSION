# Longitudinal response function of 4He with a realistic force

> The longitudinal response function of 4He is calculated with the Argonne V18 potential. The comparison with experiment suggests the need of a three-body force. When adding the Urbana IX three-body potential in the calculation of the lower longitudinal multipoles, the total strength is suppressed in the quasi-elastic peak, towards the trend of the experimental data.

## Key claim

The Argonne V18 two-body potential alone overpredicts the quasi-elastic peak of the longitudinal response function of 4He, especially at q=300 MeV/c; adding the Urbana IX three-body force only in the lower multipoles (monopole and isovector dipole) suppresses the total strength toward the experimental trend, but the full effect of the three-body force is estimated to be about twice the reduction shown in the partial calculation.

## Method

The longitudinal response function was calculated using the Lorentz Integral Transform (LIT) method, which avoids direct calculation of continuum four-body wave functions by solving a Schrödinger-like equation with bound-state asymptotics. The equation was solved via a spectral resolution method based on an effective interaction in the hyperspherical harmonics basis (EIHH). The charge operator was separated into isoscalar and isovector parts and expanded in multipoles, with the total response obtained by summing multipole contributions up to convergence.

## Key numbers

- At momentum transfer q=300 MeV/c, the AV18 potential alone overpredicts the quasi-elastic peak compared to experimental data from Bates and Saclay.
- At momentum transfer q=500 MeV/c, the same overprediction is observed but less pronounced than at q=300 MeV/c.
- The lower multipoles (monopole C0 and isovector dipole C1) where the UIX three-body force was included contribute to about 50% of the total strength at q=300 MeV/c.
- The global effect of the UIX three-body force is estimated to be about twice the reduction shown in the partial calculation (where only the lower multipoles include UIX).

## Context

This work extends prior calculations of the 4He longitudinal response using semi-realistic forces (Elsayed et al., earlier work) by employing the fully realistic Argonne V18 potential. The results confirm the suggestion from earlier studies that three-nucleon forces are needed to reproduce experimental electron scattering data, specifically the Bates (1990s) and Saclay (1980s) measurements. The paper enables future full calculations of the 4He longitudinal response with the AV18+UIX force model across the entire quasi-elastic peak region and at different momentum transfers, to determine whether this nuclear force model can fully reproduce experimental data.
