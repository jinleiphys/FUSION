# Nuclear matter properties and relativistic mean-field theory

> Nuclear matter properties are calculated in the relativistic mean field theory by using a number of different parameter sets. The result shows that the volume energy $a_1$ and the symmetry energy $J$ are around the acceptable values 16MeV and 30MeV respectively; the incompressibility $K_0$ is unacceptably high in the linear model, but assumes reasonable value if nonlinear terms are included; the density symmetry $L$ is around $100MeV$ for most parameter sets, and the symmetry incompressibility $K_s$ has positive sign which is opposite to expectations based on the nonrelativistic model. In almost all parameter sets there exists a critical point $(ρ_c, δ_c)$, where the minimum and the maximum of the equation of state are coincident and the incompressibility equals zero, falling into ranges 0.014fm$^{-3}<ρ_c<0.039$fm$^{-3}$ and $0.74<δ_c\le0.95$; for a few parameter sets there is no critical point and the pure neutron matter is predicted to be bound. The maximum mass $M_{NS}$ of neutron stars is predicted in the range 2.45M$_\odot\leq M_{NS}\leq 3.26$M$_\odot$, the corresponding neutron star radius $R_{NS}$ is in the range 12.2km$\leq R_{NS}\leq 15.1$km.

## Key claim

The relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory, when applied with many different parameter sets, yields volume energy and symmetry energy near accepted values, but the symmetry incompressibility \(K_s\) is positive for all sets, opposite to the sign expected from nonrelativistic models; the authors note that this result is conditional on the specific parameter sets chosen and that the spread among predictions is large.

## Method

The authors solved the \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\)-\(\rho\) model of relativistic mean-field theory (including nonlinear \(\sigma\) and \(\omega\) self-interactions where present) for symmetric nuclear matter at the standard state \((\rho_0,0)\) and for asymmetric matter up to pure neutron matter. They used 20 published parameter sets (linear and nonlinear) to compute the volume energy \(a_1\), symmetry energy \(J\), incompressibility \(K_0\), density symmetry \(L\), and symmetry incompressibility \(K_s\). For each set they also solved the equilibrium and stability conditions to locate the critical point \((\rho_c,\delta_c)\) where the incompressibility vanishes, and integrated the Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation to obtain the maximum neutron-star mass and radius.

## Key numbers

* Volume energy \(a_1\) is around 16 MeV for most parameter sets; the linear Walecka model gives \(a_1 \approx 16\) MeV for \(r_0 = 1.14\) fm.
* Symmetry energy \(J\) is around 30 MeV for most sets; the linear \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\) model (no \(\rho\) meson) gives \(J \approx 20\) MeV.
* Incompressibility \(K_0\) in the linear model is unacceptably high (e.g., \(\approx 540\) MeV for the Walecka model with \(a_1=16\) MeV); nonlinear models reduce \(K_0\) to values near 220–300 MeV.
* Density symmetry \(L\) is around 100 MeV for most parameter sets; the linear \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\) model gives \(L \approx 70\) MeV.
* Symmetry incompressibility \(K_s\) is positive for all sets (e.g., \(\approx 88\) MeV in the linear \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\) model), opposite to the negative sign expected from nonrelativistic models.
* Critical density \(\rho_c\) lies in the range \(0.014\ \text{fm}^{-3} < \rho_c < 0.039\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
* Critical asymmetry \(\delta_c\) lies in the range \(0.74 < \delta_c \le 0.95\).
* Maximum neutron-star mass \(M_{NS}\) is in the range \(2.45\,M_\odot \le M_{NS} \le 3.26\,M_\odot\).
* Corresponding neutron-star radius \(R_{NS}\) is in the range \(12.2\ \text{km} \le R_{NS} \le 15.1\ \text{km}\).

## Context

This work extends the systematic comparison of nuclear-matter properties from the relativistic mean-field theory, using the same \(\sigma\)-\(\omega\)-\(\rho\) Lagrangian that had been applied to finite nuclei by Reinhard (1989), Serot (1992), and others. It directly contrasts the RMF predictions with the nonrelativistic Thomas-Fermi fit of Myers and Swiatecki (1996), which gave \(K_0 \approx 220\) MeV and a negative \(K_s\). The results enable future data fits that combine nuclear masses, giant-monopole resonance energies, and neutron-star observations to constrain the nonlinear RMF parameters more tightly.
