# Nucleon Structure Functions and Light-Front Dynamics

> We present a quark-parton model to describe polarized and unpolarized nucleon structure functions. The twist-two matrix elements for the QCD evolution analysis of lepton-hadron scattering are calculated within a light-front covariant quark model. The relativistic effects in the three-body wave function are discussed for both the polarized and unpolarized cases. Predictions are given for the polarized gluon distributions as will be seen in future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a light-front covariant quark model, with parameters fixed solely to reproduce the low-lying nucleon spectrum, can predict polarized and unpolarized nucleon structure functions in reasonable agreement with experimental data, provided relativistic effects (especially Melosh rotations) are included; however, the agreement for the neutron polarized structure function depends substantially on the assumed amount of negative gluon polarization at the hadronic scale, which is not fixed by the model.

## Method

The authors calculate twist-two matrix elements for QCD evolution using a light-front covariant quark model with a confining mass equation solved numerically via a hyperharmonic oscillator basis. Parton distributions at a hadronic scale \(\mu_0^2\) are assumed to consist only of valence quarks (scenario A) or valence quarks plus gluons (scenario B), and are evolved to higher \(Q^2\) using NLO perturbative QCD. The spin dynamics incorporate Melosh rotations to maintain relativistic covariance, and the model parameters \(\tau = 3.3\) and \(\kappa_l = 1.8\) fm\(^{-2}\) are fitted to reproduce the non-strange baryon spectrum up to about 1600 MeV.

## Key numbers

*   Hadronic scale for scenario A (no gluons): \(\mu_0^2 = 0.094\) GeV\(^2\) at NLO, with \([\alpha_s(\mu_0^2)/(4\pi)]_{\rm NLO} = 0.142\).
*   Hadronic scale for scenario B (with gluons): \(\mu_0^2 = 0.220\) GeV\(^2\) at NLO, with \([\alpha_s(\mu_0^2)/(4\pi)]_{\rm NLO} = 0.053\).
*   Confining potential parameters: \(\tau = 3.3\) and \(\kappa_l = 1.8\) fm\(^{-2}\) (relativistic), compared to non-relativistic fit \(\tau = 4.59\) and \(\kappa_l = 1.61\) fm\(^{-2}\).
*   Constituent quark masses: \(m_u = m_d = m_q = M_N/3\).
*   In scenario B, the gluon distribution is normalized so that \(\int G(x,\mu_0^2)\,dx = 2\) and valence quarks carry 60% of the total momentum at \(\mu_0^2\).
*   For polarized gluons, a fraction \(f \approx -0.35\) gives \(\int dx\,\Delta G(x,\mu_0^2) \approx -0.7\).
*   Structure functions are presented at \(Q^2 = 3\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous non-relativistic quark model approach (Traini et al., 1997; 1998) by incorporating relativistic covariance via light-front dynamics and Melosh rotations, following the formalism reviewed in light-front references. It builds on the Jaffe and Ross (1980) proposal that quark model matrix elements give values at a hadronic scale for perturbative QCD evolution, and addresses the Jaffe (1996) suggestion that the integral of the polarized gluon distribution is negative. The results enable predictions for polarized gluon distributions to be tested by future experiments at CERN and BNL, and show that the neutron polarized structure function data favor a large negative gluon polarization, consistent with the effect needed to split the nucleon and Delta masses.
