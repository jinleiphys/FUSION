# Massive hybrid stars with a first order phase transition

> We develop our previous study of the transition to deconfined quark phase in neutron stars, including the interaction in the quark equation of state to the leading order in the perturbative expansion within the confinement density-dependent mass model. Using the Gibbs conditions the hadron-quark mixed phase is constructed matching the latter with the hadron equation of state derived from the microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation. The influence of quark interaction parameters on threshold properties and phase diagram of dense neutron star matter are discussed in detail. We find that the leading-order quark interaction expands the density range of the mixed phase, pushing forward the disappearance of the hadron phase. Moreover, since the equation of state could turn out to be stiffer, a high-mass hybrid star is possible with mixed-phase core with typical parameter sets.

# Massive hybrid stars with a first order phase transition

## Key claim

The inclusion of leading-order perturbative quark interactions (the C term) in the confinement density-dependent mass model stiffens the equation of state and pushes quark thresholds to higher densities, making hybrid stars with masses as high as 2.17 solar masses possible with a mixed-phase core, but only for specific parameter sets where the perturbative parameter C is positive and the confinement parameter D is chosen within ranges consistent with lattice QCD constraints.

## Method

The authors combine a hadronic equation of state from the microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation (using the Argonne V18 two-body interaction with a microscopic three-body force) with a quark matter equation of state from the confinement density-dependent mass model that includes both a confinement term (D) and a leading-order perturbative interaction term (C). The hadron-quark mixed phase is constructed using Gibbs conditions with global charge neutrality and pressure balance, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations are solved to obtain hybrid star configurations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum hybrid star mass of 2.10 solar masses for (C = 0.7, D^1/2 = 170 MeV) and 2.17 solar masses for (C = 0.7, D^1/2 = 190 MeV), compared to 1.53 and 1.65 solar masses respectively for the C = 0 cases
- Quark threshold critical density increases from 0.16 fm^-3 (C = 0, D^1/2 = 170 MeV) to 0.57 fm^-3 (C = 0.7, D^1/2 = 170 MeV), and from 0.33 fm^-3 (C = 0, D^1/2 = 190 MeV) to 0.64 fm^-3 (C = 0.7, D^1/2 = 190 MeV)
- Confinement parameter D^1/2 values used: 170 MeV and 190 MeV, based on lattice QCD constraints giving a range of 161-195 MeV
- Perturbative parameter C = 0.7 used for main calculations, with an upper bound of C = 1.1676

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Li et al., 2008) that used only the confinement potential in the quark mass scaling, which produced relatively low quark thresholds and small hybrid star masses similar to calculations within the color dielectric model and MIT bag model (hybridbhf04). The results are consistent with a study using the chiral effective field theory approach joined with the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Weise, 2014). The findings enable future investigations of color superconductivity in dense quark matter and the competition between hyperons and free quarks, which a previous Dyson-Schwinger quark model study (dsm) suggested might eliminate hybrid stars entirely.
