# Bose-Einstein Correlations for Longitudinally Expanding Finite Systems

> Bose-Einstein correlations and momentum distributions are calculated for longitudinally expanding systems, like jets or high energy heavy ion collisions with light projectiles. The expansion generates a thermal length-scale in the longitudinal direction, and the geometrical length-scale is also finite. The Bose-Einstein correlation functions are dominated by the smaller while the invariant momentum distribution by the longer of the two longitudinal length-scales.

# Bose-Einstein Correlations for Longitudinally Expanding Finite Systems

## Key claim

Bose-Einstein correlation functions for longitudinally expanding finite systems are dominated by the *shorter* of the two longitudinal length-scales (thermal versus geometrical), while the invariant momentum distribution is dominated by the *longer* of the two. This result holds for systems where the width of the rapidity distribution of bosons emitted at a fixed space-time rapidity is smaller than unity and the width of the proper-time distribution is smaller than the mean proper-time.

## Method

The author models the emission function for a one-dimensionally expanding boson source using a Wigner-function formalism, with Gaussian approximations for the space-time rapidity distribution, proper-time distribution, transverse position distribution, and the conditional probability linking rapidity to space-time rapidity. The space-time integrals are evaluated in the longitudinally comoving system (LCMS) for small relative momentum components, yielding analytic expressions for both the invariant momentum distribution and the Bose-Einstein correlation function. The thermal width parameter is derived from the relativistic Boltzmann distribution for scaling Bjorken expansion.

## Key numbers

- For thermal emission, the width of the rapidity distribution of particles is given by $\Det(m_t) = \sqrt{T/m_t}$, where $T$ is the freeze-out temperature and $m_t$ is the transverse mass.
- The longitudinal radius parameter of the BECF is $R_L^2(m_t) = \tau_0^2 \Des^2(m_t)$, where $\tau_0$ is the mean freeze-out time and $\Des$ is the effective correlation length.
- The effective correlation length satisfies $1/\Des^2(m_t) = 1/\De^2 + 1/\Det^2(m_t)$, where $\De$ is the geometrical width in space-time rapidity.
- For a system with $\De = 0.5$ and $\tau_0 = 7.0$ fm/c, the finite size correction yields about a factor of 0.5 decrease in the longitudinal radius at low $m_t$ compared to the infinite Bjorken-tube formula.
- For a system with $\De = 1.0$ and $\tau_0 = 7.0$ fm/c, the finite size correction factor is about 0.8.
- In the Lund string model, the thermal correlation length is approximately $\Det \approx 0.4$.
- The mean freeze-out time in heavy ion collisions is about $\tau_0 \approx 4-5$ fm/c, with estimated duration $\Dt < 2$ fm/c.

## Context

This work extends the Bose-Einstein interferometry formalism for one-dimensionally expanding systems beyond the infinite Bjorken-tube model of Sinyukov (1994), where only a single thermal length-scale appeared. It also builds on the inside-outside cascade studies of Kolehmainen and Gyulassy (1989) and the rapidity-space-time rapidity correlation analysis for 1D expanding strings by the Lund group (1993). The paper enables simultaneous extraction of the freeze-out temperature, mean proper-time, and geometrical size in the longitudinal direction from combined measurements of the transverse-mass-dependent rapidity width of the invariant momentum distribution and the longitudinal radius parameter of the Bose-Einstein correlation function.
