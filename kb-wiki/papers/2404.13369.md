# Isotope-shift factors with quantum electrodynamics effects for many-electron systems: A study of the nuclear charge radius of $^{26m}$Al

> A method for calculating the field shift contribution to isotope shifts in many-electron atoms, incorporating quantum electrodynamics (QED) effects, is introduced. We also implement the model QED approach to incorporate QED contribution to the nuclear recoil effect at the high-order correlation effects treatment level. The proposed computational scheme is used to revise the value of the root-mean-square (rms) nuclear charge radius of the isomer of aluminium-26, $^{26m}$Al. This radius is important for the global analysis of the $V_{ud}$ element of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix. The difference in mean-square nuclear charge radii of $^{27}$Al and $^{26m}$Al, obtained by combining the calculated atomic factors with recently measured isotope shift (IS) of the $3s^23p‾^2P_{3/2} ¥to 3s^24s‾^2S_{1/2}$ transition in Al, is $0.443(44)(19)‾{¥rm fm}^2$, where the first and second uncertainties are experimental and theoretical ones, respectively. The latter is reduced by a factor of 4 with respect to the previous study. Using this value and the known value of the rms charge radius of $^{27}$Al, the resultant value $R_c(^{26m}$Al) = 3.132(10)‾fm is obtained. With the improved accuracy of the calculated IS factors the error in $R_c(^{26m}$Al) is now dominated by the experimental uncertainty. Similar revision of rms charge radii is made for the $^{28}$Al, $^{29}$Al, $^{30}$Al, $^{31}$Al and $^{32}$Al isotopes using existing IS measurements. Additionally, atomic factors are computed for the $3s^23p‾^2P_{3/2} ¥to 3s^24s‾^2S_{1/2}$, $3s^23p‾^2P_{1/2} ¥to 3s^25s‾^2S_{1/2}$ and $3s^23p‾^2P_{3/2} ¥to 3s^25s‾^2S_{1/2}$ transitions in Al, which can be used in future experimental studies.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a method for calculating field shift contributions to isotope shifts in many-electron atoms that incorporates quantum electrodynamics (QED) effects, and uses this method to revise the nuclear charge radius of the isomer $^{26m}$Al. The authors claim that the theoretical uncertainty in the extracted radius is reduced by a factor of 4 compared to the previous study, but note that the error is now dominated by experimental uncertainty rather than theoretical uncertainty.

## Method

The authors performed high-precision relativistic electronic structure calculations using the coupled cluster method with single, double, iterative triple, and perturbative quadruple excitations (CCSDT(Q)) for all electrons of aluminum. They developed an effective approach to incorporate leading-order QED effects (vacuum polarization via the Uehling potential and self-energy via a model operator) into the field shift and mass shift constants, and validated this approach against rigorous QED calculations for highly charged ions. The computed atomic factors were combined with experimentally measured isotope shifts from collinear laser spectroscopy of the $3s^23p‾^2P_{3/2} ¥to 3s^24s‾^2S_{1/2}$ transition in aluminum to extract nuclear charge radii.

## Key numbers

- Difference in mean-square nuclear charge radii between $^{27}$Al and $^{26m}$Al: $0.443(44)(19)‾{¥rm fm}^2$, where the first uncertainty (0.44 fm$^2$) is experimental and the second (0.19 fm$^2$) is theoretical
- Root-mean-square nuclear charge radius of $^{26m}$Al: $R_c(^{26m}{¥rm Al}) = 3.132(10)‾{¥rm fm}$
- The theoretical uncertainty in the extracted radius is reduced by a factor of 4 with respect to the previous study
- QED contributions to the field shift constant for the $2p_{1/2} ¥to 2s_{1/2}$ transition in Li-like ions: for Ar$^{15+}$ the total QED contribution is 40 MHz/fm$^2$, for Bi$^{78+}$ it is $0.038 ¥times 10^7$ MHz/fm$^2$, and for U$^{87+}$ it is $0.109 ¥times 10^7$ MHz/fm$^2$
- The model QED calculation for the mass shift constant in Li-like U gave a QED contribution of $-2221921$ GHz u, in agreement with the rigorous QED result of $-2221736$ GHz u

## Context

This work extends the previous study by Plattner (2023) which measured the isotope shift between $^{26m}$Al and $^{27}$Al using collinear laser spectroscopy, but where the uncertainty in the extracted charge radius was strongly dominated by atomic theory calculations. The improved accuracy of the calculated isotope shift factors enables more precise determination of the $V_{ud}$ element of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix from superallowed beta decay of $^{26m}$Al, which has the smallest nuclear structure-dependent corrections among such decays as noted by Hardy (2020). The method developed here can be applied to future experimental studies of the $3s^23p‾^2P_{3/2} ¥to 3s^24s‾^2S_{1/2}$, $3s^23p‾^2P_{1/2} ¥to 3s^25s‾^2S_{1/2}$, and $3s^23p‾^2P_{3/2} ¥to 3s^25s‾^2S_{1/2}$ transitions in aluminum, for which atomic factors are provided.
