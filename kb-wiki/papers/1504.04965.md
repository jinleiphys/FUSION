# Ground-state energies and charge radii of $^{4}$He, $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, and $^{56}$Ni in the unitary-model-operator approach

> We study the nuclear ground-state properties by using the unitary-model-operator approach (UMOA). Recently, the particle-basis formalism has been introduced in the UMOA and enables us to employ the charge-dependent nucleon-nucleon interaction. We evaluate the ground-state energies and charge radii of $^{4}$He, $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, and $^{56}$Ni with the charge-dependent Bonn potential. The ground-state energy is dominated by the contributions from the one- and two-body cluster terms, while, for the radius, the one-particle-one-hole excitations are more important than the two-particle-two-hole excitations. The calculated results reproduce the trend of experimental data of the saturation property for finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The unitary-model-operator approach (UMOA) with the CD-Bonn potential reproduces the trend of experimental saturation data for finite nuclei, but all calculated ground-state energies are underbound and all charge radii are smaller than experimental values, with the discrepancy likely requiring genuine three-nucleon forces for resolution.

## Method

The authors applied the unitary-model-operator approach (UMOA) in the particle-basis formalism to construct effective Hamiltonians from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, including Coulomb interaction in the proton-proton channel. They solved the decoupling equation for the two-body correlation operator S in a model space defined by the two-body quantum number ρ₁, then diagonalized the transformed Hamiltonian in the 0p0h and 1p1h space to obtain the ground-state energy from one- and two-body cluster terms plus a perturbative three-body cluster contribution. Charge radii were evaluated from the ground-state wavefunction using an expansion of the r² operator up to order S², with proton and neutron finite-size corrections added.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energies (MeV): ⁴He = -27.73 (expt. -28.30), ¹⁶O = -119.39 (expt. -127.62), ⁴⁰Ca = -340.28 (expt. -342.05), ⁵⁶Ni = -473.04 (expt. -483.99)
- Three-body cluster contributions (MeV): ⁴He = -1.60, ¹⁶O = -3.82, ⁴⁰Ca = -5.92, ⁵⁶Ni = -18.20
- Charge radii (fm): ⁴He = 1.67 (expt. 1.68), ¹⁶O = 2.44 (expt. 2.69), ⁴⁰Ca = 2.97 (expt. 3.48), ⁵⁶Ni = 3.19 (no experimental value available)
- For ¹⁶O, charge radius varies from 2.76 fm at ℏω = 11 MeV to 2.27 fm at ℏω = 20 MeV; for ⁵⁶Ni, from 3.56 fm at ℏω = 11 MeV to 2.87 fm at ℏω = 20 MeV
- Harmonic oscillator energies ℏω_min used: 18 MeV for ⁴He, 15 MeV for ¹⁶O, 14 MeV for ⁴⁰Ca and ⁵⁶Ni
- Proton and neutron finite-size corrections: R_p² = 0.832 fm², R_n² = -0.115 fm²

## Context

This work extends earlier UMOA calculations by Suzuki and Okamoto (1994) and Kumagai (1997) for ¹⁶O and ⁴⁰Ca, and by Fujii (2004, 2009) for ground-state energies of ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, and ⁵⁶Ni, by now including charge radii within the charge-dependent particle-basis formalism. The results are compared with the no-core shell model (NCSM) for ⁴He, the coupled-cluster method (CCM), and the Bethe-Weizsäcker empirical formula. The authors find that, similar to the CCM results of Kohno (2012), the ℏω-dependence of charge radii could be reduced by including a one-body correlation operator, and that genuine three-nucleon forces appear necessary to simultaneously reproduce binding energies and charge radii, as also suggested by Green's function Monte Carlo calculations of Pieper (2001) and Dirac-Brückner-Hartree-Fock studies of Fritz (1993).
