# Evolution of Efimov States

> The Efimov phenomenon manifests itself as an emergent discrete scaling symmetry in the quantum three-body problem. In the unitarity limit, it leads to an infinite tower of three-body bound states with energies forming a geometric sequence. In this work, we study the evolution of these so-called Efimov states using relativistic scattering theory. We identify them as poles of the three-particle $S$ matrix and trace their trajectories in the complex energy plane as they evolve from virtual states through bound states to resonances. We dial the scattering parameters toward the unitarity limit and observe the emergence of the universal scaling of energies and couplings -- a behavior known from the non-relativistic case. Interestingly, we find that Efimov resonances follow unusual, cyclic trajectories accumulating at the three-body threshold and then disappear at some values of the two-body scattering length. We propose a partial resolution to this "missing states" problem.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that relativistic three-body scattering equations reproduce the Efimov effect and its discrete scaling symmetry, but find that Efimov resonances follow unusual cyclic trajectories that accumulate at the three-body threshold and then disappear, creating a "missing states" problem that they only partially resolve by conjecturing that higher Riemann sheets supply the missing poles.

## Method

The authors solve relativistic three-body integral equations for identical spinless bosons of mass m with zero total angular momentum, using a leading-order effective range expansion for the two-body scattering amplitude and setting the three-body K matrix to zero. They analytically continue the scattering amplitude to unphysical Riemann sheets by deforming the integration contour in the complex momentum plane to avoid singularities, then trace the poles of the S matrix as functions of the two-body scattering length a.

## Key numbers

- The Efimov geometric series quotient is λ² ≈ 515 in the unitarity limit (a → ∞)
- The two-body bound state mass is m_b = 2√(m² - 1/a²)
- The three-body threshold is at E_thr^(3φ) = 3m
- The dimer-particle threshold is at E_thr^(φb) = m + m_b
- The ground state resonance reaches the three-body threshold at ma = -8.71
- The scaling quotient Q_a asymptotes to λ as ma → ∞

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic Efimov effect discovered by Efimov (1970) into a relativistic scattering framework developed by Hansen, Sharpe, and collaborators (2014-2021) and builds on previous work by Dawid et al. (2023). The cyclic trajectories of Efimov resonances were previously noticed by Hyodo (2013) using a non-relativistic approach, but that work did not address the "missing states" problem. The results enable future studies of three-particle resonances relevant to experimental, phenomenological, and lattice QCD investigations of hadronic systems with large scattering lengths.
