# Coulomb-scattering and eta - eta-prime mixing angle

> The fascinating physics underlying eta and eta-prime mesons can be studied theoretically and experimentally in various contexts. In this presentation we want to turn our attention to two important uncorrelated aspects of this vivid research field which provide yet unexpected challenges or surprises. First we discuss open questions in the theoretical treatment of Coulomb-interaction in the context of reaction processes like p p --> p p eta. Then we review eta - eta-prime and sigma(600) - f_0(980) mixing in the U(3) x U(3) Linear Sigma Model and extract information on eta - eta-prime mixing and the K*0(800) resonance from meson-meson scattering.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the theoretical treatment of Coulomb interactions in reactions like \(pp \rightarrow pp\eta\) and \(pp \rightarrow pp\eta^\prime\) is still unresolved, and that the observed threshold enhancement may not be solely due to short-ranged strong interactions but could also have a component from infinite-ranged Coulomb final-state interactions, contrary to claims that Coulomb effects in these processes are fully understood.

## Method

The author reviews the two-potential formalism for initial- and final-state interactions in \(2\rightarrow 3\) production processes, highlighting technical challenges with infinite-range Coulomb interactions including the non-smooth behavior of half-shell Coulomb Green's functions and the regularization dependence of factorization approaches. Separately, the paper applies the Resonance Spectrum Expansion (RSE), a unitarized coupled-channel framework based on the Nijmegen Unitarized Meson Model, to fit LASS data on \(I=1/2\), \(J=0\) \(K\pi\) scattering, extracting the \(\eta\)-\(\eta^\prime\) mixing angle and the \(K^\ast_0(800)\) pole position from a three-channel (\(K\pi\), \(K\eta\), \(K\eta^\prime\)) analysis.

## Key numbers

- Pseudoscalar mixing angle in the nonstrange-strange basis: \(\phi_P \simeq 41.84^\circ\) from the \(U(3)\times U(3)\) Linear Sigma Model tree-level analysis, using \(m_{\eta_{n\bar{n}}} \simeq 757.9\) MeV derived from the Equal Splitting Law with \(m_{a_0} \simeq 984.8\) MeV and \(\hat{m} \simeq 315\) MeV.
- Most recent KLOE experimental data suggest \(\phi_P = (41.2 \pm 1.1)^\circ\).
- RSE fit to LASS data yields a \(K^\ast_0(800)\) S-matrix pole at \((736.10 - i\,257.83)\) MeV, obtained with a mixing angle \(\phi_P = 39.40^\circ\).
- RSE fit parameters: range \(a \simeq 2.5497\) GeV\(^{-1}\), bare energies \(E_0 \simeq 1.5173\) GeV, \(E_1 \simeq 1.8178\) GeV, with residues \(\bar{B}_{-1} \simeq -12.7387\), \(\bar{B}_0 \simeq 4.6931\), \(\bar{B}_1 \simeq 3.1380\).
- The decoupling condition \(V_{K\eta}=0\) would give \(\phi_P = \arctan(1/\sqrt{2}) = 35.26^\circ\).

## Context

This work extends the \(U(3)\times U(3)\) Linear Sigma Model approach to \(\eta\)-\(\eta^\prime\) mixing (following earlier work by Scadron, Delbourgo, and Klabucar) and contrasts it with a fully unitarized coupled-channel treatment using the Resonance Spectrum Expansion. The paper challenges the claim by Gasparyan (2005) that Coulomb interactions in \(pp\rightarrow pp\eta\) are fully understood, noting that their dispersive method does not address the equal-mass charged-particle case or the compactness problems in Faddeev equations with Coulomb interactions summarized by Kadyrov (2005). The RSE analysis enables extraction of the \(K^\ast_0(800)\) resonance parameters and shows that the effective couplings \(\gamma_{ij}(E)\) vary strongly with energy despite a constant mixing angle, linking the tree-level and unitarized descriptions.
