# The gamma^* gamma -> pi^0 transition in a bound-state approach

> We present a recent treatment of the $π^0γ^\starγ$ transition form factor in the coupled Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter approach to stress the desirability of measuring it at CEBAF.

## Key claim

The coupled Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter approach can describe the $\pi^0\gamma^\star\gamma$ transition form factor across all spacelike momenta, from $Q^2=0$ to asymptotically large $Q^2$, but the model choice for the dressed quark propagator (specifically the function $A(q^2)$) overshoots the CLEO and CELLO data for $Q^2 \lesssim 4$ GeV$^2$, indicating that improved model solutions are needed.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled Schwinger-Dyson equation for the dressed quark propagator $S(k) = [A(k^2)\slashed{k} - B(k^2)]^{-1}$ and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the pion bound-state vertex $\Gamma_{\pi^0}(q,p)$, using a ladder-type approximation with dressed propagators and an effective gluon propagator in Landau gauge (the "Abelian approximation"). The $\pi^0\gamma^\star\gamma$ transition amplitude is then computed via the generalized impulse approximation (GIA) triangle diagram, using dressed quark propagators, the pion BS amplitude, and dressed electromagnetic vertices (Ball-Chiu, Curtis-Pennington, or modified Curtis-Pennington) that satisfy the vector Ward-Takahashi identity.

## Key numbers

* The ABJ anomaly amplitude at $Q^2=0$: $T_{\pi^0}(0,0) = 1/(4\pi^2 f_\pi)$, reproduced model-independently.
* Asymptotic behavior for renormalizable $qq\gamma$ vertices (CP, mCP): $T_{\pi^0}(-Q^2,0) = (4/3) f_\pi/Q^2$ for large $Q^2$, with ${\cal J}=4/3$.
* For the Ball-Chiu vertex, the asymptotic coefficient becomes ${\cal J} \approx 1.78$ because ${\widetilde f}_\pi \approx 1.334 f_\pi$ for the model solutions.
* For the symmetric double-off-shell case: $T_{\pi^0}(-Q^2, -Q^{\prime 2}) = (4/3) f_\pi/(Q^2 + {Q^{\prime}}^2)$.
* The effective gluon propagator parameters: $a = (0.387\ \text{GeV})^{-4}$, $\mu = (0.510\ \text{GeV})^{-2}$.
* The CLEO data cover $1.5\ \text{GeV}^2 \lesssim Q^2 \lesssim 9\ \text{GeV}^2$.

## Context

This work extends the coupled Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter approach previously applied to meson properties (Klabucar, Kekez, and Bistrovic, 1998) to the $\pi^0\gamma^\star\gamma$ transition form factor, following the framework of Jain and Munczek (1991, 1992, 1993) for the gluon propagator model. It contrasts with perturbative QCD approaches (Brodsky and Lepage, 1981; Manohar, 1990) and QCD sum rule analyses (Radyushkin and Ruskov, 1996) that indicate large nonperturbative contributions even at accessible momenta. The results enable future high-precision measurements at CEBAF (Jefferson Lab) to discriminate between different dressed quark propagator solutions and test nonperturbative QCD modeling.
