# $_Ξ^{5}H $ hypernuclei by folding the state-of-the-art $ ΞN $ interactions

> I examined a phenomenological Nijmegen and a first principles HAL QCD $ΞN$ potentials to study $αΞ$ ineractions. A Woods-Saxon type form for $αΞ$ potential in the single-folding potential approach is derived by using the spin- and isospin averaged $ΞN$ interactions. The possibility of resonance or bound state is searched and accordingly, the low energy scattering phase shift parameters of $αΞ$ are calculated. The numerical results show that even though two $ ΞN $ potentials have significantly dissimilar isospin (I) and spin (S) components, $_Ξ^{5}H $ could be only a Coulomb-assisted resonance state that appears about $ 0.5 $ MeV below the threshold of $α+Ξ^{-} $ for both model of potentials.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the $_\Xi^{5}H$ hypernucleus cannot be a pure nuclear bound state or a pure atomic (Coulomb-bound) state; instead, it exists only as a Coulomb-assisted resonance state approximately 0.5 MeV below the $\alpha + \Xi^{-}$ threshold, and this result holds for both the phenomenological Nijmegen ESC08c and the first-principles HAL QCD $\Xi N$ potentials despite their significantly different isospin and spin components.

## Method
The author used a single-folding potential (SFP) approach, folding the nucleon density of the $\alpha$-particle with spin- and isospin-averaged $\Xi N$ interactions from two sources: the phenomenological Nijmegen ESC08c potential and the HAL QCD lattice QCD potential (at imaginary-time slices $t/a = 11, 12, 13$). The resulting $\alpha\Xi$ potentials were fitted to a Woods-Saxon form, and the Schrodinger equation was solved in infinite volume to extract binding energies and low-energy scattering phase shifts, including a hard-sphere Coulomb potential for the $\Xi^{-}$ case.

## Key numbers
- For the HAL QCD potential at $t/a = 12$, the $\alpha\Xi$ Woods-Saxon fit parameters are $V_0 = 11.7$ MeV, $R = 1.90$ fm, $c = 0.37$ fm; the scattering length is $a_0 = -7.3$ fm and effective range $r_0 = 3.7$ fm (using experimental masses).
- The binding energy of $_\Xi^{5}H$ with Coulomb interaction included is $B_{\alpha\Xi^{-}} = 0.5$ MeV for the HAL QCD potential at $t/a = 12$, and $0.8$ MeV for both the ESC08c and HAL QCD $t/a = 11$ potentials.
- For the phenomenological Dover-Gal (DG) potential, the binding energy is $B_{\alpha\Xi^{0}} = 2.0$ MeV without Coulomb and $B_{\alpha\Xi^{-}} = 3.4$ MeV with Coulomb.
- The HAL QCD lattice simulation used quark masses near the physical point with $m_\pi \simeq 146.4$ MeV and $m_K \simeq 525$ MeV on a lattice volume $\simeq (8.1\ \text{fm})^4$.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of $_\Xi^{5}H$ by Hiyama et al. (2008) using Nijmegen ND and ESC04 potentials, which found a Coulomb-assisted bound state, and by Le et al. (2021) using chiral $\Xi N$ interactions, which reported a clearly bound state at $2.16 \pm 0.1$ MeV. The results are also compared with calculations by Myint and Akaishi (2006) using Nijmegen model-D, who reported 1.7 MeV binding energy largely due to Coulomb, and by Friedman and Gal (2021) who found 2.0 MeV binding. The derived $\alpha\Xi$ potentials are intended for use in few-body methods based on $\alpha$-cluster models such as Gaussian expansion, Jacobi no-core shell model, and Faddeev calculations.
