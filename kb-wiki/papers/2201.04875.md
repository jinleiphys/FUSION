# Gluon transversity and TMDs for spin-1 hadrons

> We explain a gluon transversity, transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs), and parton distribution functions (PDFs) for spin-1 hadrons. The gluon transversity exists in hadrons with spin more than or equal to one, and it does not exist in the spin-1/2 nucleons. Since there is no direct contribution from the nucleons, it is an appropriate quantity to probe an exotic component in the spin-1 deuteron beyond a simple bound system of the nucleons. We show how the gluon transversity can be measured at hadron accelerator facilities by the Drell-Yan process in addition to lepton-accelerator experiments. Next, possible TMDs are explained for the spin-1 hadrons at the twists 3 and 4 in addition to twist-2 ones by considering tensor polarizations. We found that 30 TMDs exist in the tensor-polarized spin-1 hadron at the twists 3 and 4 in addition to 10 TMDs at the twist 2. There are 3 collinear PDFs at the twists 3 and 4. We also indicate that the corresponding TMD fragmentation functions exist at the twists 3 and 4. Due to the time-reversal invariance in the collinear PDFs, there are new sum rules on the time-reversal odd TMDs. In addition, we obtained a useful twist-2 relation, a sum rule, and relations with multiparton distribution functions by using the operator product expansion and the equation of motion for quarks. These findings are valuable for experimental investigations on polarized deuteron structure functions in 2020's and 2030's at world accelerator facilities.

## Key claim

The paper provides a theoretical formalism for measuring the gluon transversity in spin-1 hadrons (specifically the deuteron) via the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process at hadron accelerator facilities, and it catalogs all possible transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs) and collinear parton distribution functions (PDFs) up to twist 4 for tensor-polarized spin-1 hadrons. The authors note that the numerical cross-section estimates assume the gluon transversity equals the longitudinally-polarized gluon distribution (from NNPDF1.1), which is "likely an overestimation."

## Method

The authors derived the cross-section difference for the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process with deuteron linear polarizations (E_x minus E_y), expressing it in terms of quark and antiquark distributions in the proton and the gluon transversity in the deuteron. They then performed a Lorentz-invariant expansion of the quark correlation function for spin-1 hadrons, including the lightcone vector n to accommodate twist-3 and twist-4 effects, and defined TMDs and collinear PDFs by taking traces with gamma matrices. Finally, they used the operator product expansion and the equation of motion for quarks to derive relations among twist-2 and twist-3 PDFs and multiparton distribution functions.

## Key numbers

* 30 TMDs exist in the tensor-polarized spin-1 hadron at twists 3 and 4, in addition to 10 TMDs at twist 2.
* 3 collinear PDFs exist at twists 3 and 4 for tensor-polarized spin-1 hadrons.
* The polarization asymmetry A_{E_xy} for the proton-deuteron Drell-Yan process at Fermilab kinematics (p_p = 120 GeV, phi=0, y=0.5, q_T=0.5 or 1.0 GeV) is "typically a few percent" as a function of M_{mu mu}^2.
* The gluon transversity is defined by the amplitude with gluon helicity flip requiring a change of two spin units (Delta s = 2).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for spin-1 hadron structure beyond the twist-2 level, which was previously given by Bacchetta and Mulders (2000) for TMDs. It provides the full set of TMDs and PDFs up to twist 4, analogous to the higher-twist investigations already performed for spin-1/2 nucleons (referenced as tmds-nucleon). The findings are intended to support experimental projects at JLab, Fermilab, NICA, LHC-spin, and electron-ion colliders (EIC, EicC) in the 2020s and 2030s, and the formalism for the Drell-Yan process enables a measurement at Fermilab within the E-1039 collaboration.
