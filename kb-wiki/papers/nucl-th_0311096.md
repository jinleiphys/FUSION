# Photoproduction of ηmesons on protons in the resonance region:The background problem and the third S_11 resonance

> We have constructed an isobar model for the $η$-photoproduction on the proton in the energy region up to the photon lab energy $K_0 = 3$ GeV. The data base involved into the fitting procedure includes precise results for the cross section and for the $T$-asymmetry of the process $γp\toηp$ near threshold obtained at MAMI and ELSA as well as recent results for the $Σ$-asymmetry and for the angular distribution measured at higher energies in Grenoble and also more recent measurements performed at JLab for the photon energies up to 2 GeV. The model includes twelve nucleon resonances: $S_{11}(1535)$, $S_{11}(1650) $, $S_{11}(1825)$, $P_{11}(1440)$,$P_{13}(1720)$, $D_{13}(1520)$, $D_ {15}(1675)$, $F_{15}(1680)$, $F_{17}(1990)$,$G_{17}(2190)$, $G_{19} (2250)$, $H_{19}(2220)$, and the background consisting of the nucleon pole term and the vector meson exchange in the $t$-channel. To explain the observed energy dependence of the integrated cross section, two $s$ -wave resonances, $S_{11}(1650)$ and $S_{11}(1825)$, have to be taken into account along with the dominating $S_{11}(1535)$. The integrated cross section as well as the angular distribution and $Σ$ asymmetry predicted by the model are in good agreement with the data. Above the photon energy $K_0 = 2$ GeV, the calculated cross section exhibits an appreciable dependence on the $ρ$- and $ω$-meson contribution, whose coupling with nucleons is not well defined. Several versions of extending the model to higher energies are considered.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an isobar model for η-photoproduction on the proton up to 3 GeV that requires a third S11 resonance (S11(1825)) to describe JLab data above 1 GeV, but the model's predictions above 2 GeV depend strongly on poorly-constrained ρ- and ω-meson exchange contributions.

## Method

The authors built an isobar model using Breit-Wigner parameterizations for twelve nucleon resonances in the s-channel, combined with background terms from nucleon pole terms (s- and u-channel) and vector meson exchange (ρ and ω in the t-channel). The model was fitted to low-energy data (K0 < 1.1 GeV) from MAMI and ELSA, plus higher-energy data from Grenoble and JLab up to 2 GeV, with resonance parameters varied within PDG-recommended ranges. The multipole amplitudes were expressed in terms of electric and magnetic partial waves, and the energy-dependent widths included penetration factors for angular momenta up to l=5.

## Key numbers

- Photon lab energy range covered: K0 = threshold to 3 GeV
- For S11(1535): Γγ/Γr ≈ 0.345%, giving ΓηpΓγ/Γr ≈ 0.27 MeV; helicity amplitude A1/2 = 0.110 GeV^{-1/2} (assuming Γηp/Γr = 0.50)
- For S11(1650): with Γηp/Γr = 0.1, Γγ/Γr ≈ 0.38% and A1/2 = 0.098 GeV^{-1/2}
- Third S11 resonance mass: Wr = 1825 MeV (from Table 1, marked by asterisk); A1/2 ranges from 0.021 to 0.151 GeV^{-1/2} depending on Γηp/Γr (0.5 to 0.01)
- ηNN coupling constant: gηNN²/4π = 0.4
- Effective radius of ηN interaction: R = 1 fm (fixed, not varied)
- Vector meson exchange effects become appreciable above K0 = 2 GeV, with significant model dependence above K0 = 3.4 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier low-energy models (e.g., TiBeKa94, BeMu95, FiAr97) that described only the near-threshold region, and addresses discrepancies between JLab data (Dug02) and previous analyses (ChiNT02, TRS03) by introducing a third S11 resonance. The model enables future studies of η-nuclear interactions and provides a framework for discriminating between competing theoretical approaches, such as the hybrid isobar-Regge model of ChiNT02, by predicting strongly anisotropic angular distributions at forward and backward angles for K0 > 1.5 GeV.
