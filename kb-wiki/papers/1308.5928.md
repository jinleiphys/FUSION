# Detecting correlated di-hadron pairs: about the extraction of transversity and beyond

> We summarize the latest achievements about the extraction of the transversity parton distribution and proton tensor charge based on an analysis of pion-pair production in deep-inelastic scattering off transversely polarized targets. Recently released data for proton and deuteron targets by HERMES and COMPASS allow for a flavor separation of the valence components of transversity. At variance with the Collins effect, this extraction is performed in the framework of collinear factorization and relies on di-hadron fragmentation functions. The latter have been taken from the first recent analysis of the semi-inclusive production of two pion pairs in back-to-back jets in e+e- annihilation. We also comment on the possibility of isolating new azimuthally asymmetric correlations of opposite pion pairs, which could arise when a fragmenting quark crosses parity-odd domains localized in Minkowski space-time and induced by the topologically nontrivial QCD background (the so-called θ vacuum).

## Key claim

The extraction of transversity parton distributions and the proton tensor charge from di-hadron production in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) is performed in the collinear factorization framework using di-hadron fragmentation functions (DiFFs), which avoids the complications of transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) evolution; the analysis relies on the assumption that the DiFFs extracted from e+e- data are universal and that the Soffer bound is imposed as a constraint at the starting scale.

## Method

The authors analyzed SIDIS data for pion-pair production off transversely polarized proton and deuteron targets from HERMES and COMPASS, combined with Belle data for e+e- annihilation into two back-to-back pion pairs. The unpolarized DiFF D1 was parametrized by fitting to PYTHIA event generator output tuned to Belle kinematics, while the chiral-odd DiFF H1^sphericalangle was extracted from the Artru-Collins asymmetry measured by Belle. Transversity distributions for up and down valence quarks were then extracted using a functional form multiplied by the Soffer bound, with fits performed via both standard Hessian and Monte Carlo replica methods.

## Key numbers

- The fit for the unpolarized DiFF D1 used approximately 32000 bins in (z, M_h) and reached an average chi-squared per degree of freedom of 1.62 with 79 parameters at the starting scale Q0^2 = 1 GeV^2.
- The fit for the chiral-odd DiFF H1^sphericalangle used 64 bins of experimental data for the Artru-Collins asymmetry and achieved a chi-squared per degree of freedom of 0.57 with 9 parameters at Q0^2 = 1 GeV^2.
- For the extra-flexible scenario at Q0^2 = 1 GeV^2, the truncated tensor charge (integrated over 0.0064 <= x <= 0.28) from the standard Hessian method gave delta u_v = 0.32 +/- 0.12 and delta d_v = -0.25 +/- 0.15; from the Monte Carlo approach, delta u_v = 0.34 +/- 0.10 and delta d_v = -0.20 +/- 0.14.
- The estimated size of the parity-odd azimuthal asymmetry from P-odd DiFFs is approximately 0.006, based on a model calculation using the spectator approximation and the instanton vacuum model.

## Context

This work extends the earlier extraction of transversity by the Torino group (Anselmino et al., 2008) based on the Collins effect in single-hadron SIDIS, but uses a collinear factorization framework with di-hadron fragmentation functions that avoids the complications of TMD evolution. The flavor separation of up and down valence transversity components was made possible by combining new COMPASS data on proton and deuteron targets with HERMES data. The results enable future studies of the tensor charge and, through the proposed parity-odd DiFFs, open a new avenue to probe local parity violation in QCD via azimuthal correlations in e+e- annihilation to pion pairs.
