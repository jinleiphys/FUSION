# Elliptic flow coefficients from transverse momentum conservation

> We calculate the $k$-particle ($k=2,4,6,8$) azimuthal cumulants resulting from the conservation of transverse momentum. We find that $c_2\{k\}>0$ and depending on the transverse momenta, $c_2\{k\}$ can reach substantial values even for a relatively large number of particles. The impact of our results on the understanding of the onset of collectivity in small systems is emphasized.

# Elliptic flow coefficients from transverse momentum conservation

## Key claim
Transverse momentum conservation (TMC) alone produces positive multi-particle azimuthal cumulants \(c_2\{k\} > 0\) for \(k = 2,4,6,8\), which can reach substantial values even for relatively large particle numbers, depending on the transverse momenta. This effect provides a non-flow background that must be accounted for when interpreting long-range azimuthal correlations in small collision systems, particularly at low multiplicities.

## Method
The authors analytically calculated the \(k\)-particle azimuthal cumulants \(c_2\{k\}\) (\(k = 2,4,6,8\)) by imposing global transverse momentum conservation on the \(N\)-particle transverse momentum distribution, using a delta-function constraint \(\delta^2(\vec{p}_1 + \ldots + \vec{p}_N)\). The central limit theorem was employed to approximate the reduced \(k\)-particle distributions, and the cumulants were derived by expanding the exponential factor in the resulting expressions to leading order in \(1/N\). The calculations assumed an exponential single-particle transverse momentum distribution \(f(p) \propto \exp(-p/T)\) with \(T = 0.25\) GeV.

## Key numbers
- For equal transverse momenta \(p_i = \sqrt{\langle p^2 \rangle_\Omega}\) with \(p > 0.3\) GeV, the cumulants scale as \(c_2\{k\} \sim 1/N^k\) for large \(N\), where \(N\) is the number of particles subject to transverse momentum conservation.
- The two-particle cumulant at given momenta \(p_1, p_2\) is given by \(c_2\{2\}|_{p_1,p_2} \approx p_1^2 p_2^2 / [2(N-2)^2 \langle p^2 \rangle_F^2]\) for \(p_1 p_2 < \frac{1}{2}(N-2)\langle p^2 \rangle_F\).
- The four-particle cumulant at given momenta is \(c_2\{4\}|_{p_1,p_2,p_3,p_4} \approx (p_1 p_2 p_3 p_4)^2 / [(N-4)^4 \langle p^2 \rangle_F^4]\).
- The six-particle cumulant yields \(\frac{1}{4} c_2\{6\}|_{p_1,\ldots,p_6} \approx \frac{3}{2} (p_1 p_2 p_3 p_4 p_5 p_6)^2 / [(N-6)^6 \langle p^2 \rangle_F^6]\).
- The eight-particle cumulant yields \(\frac{1}{33} c_2\{8\}|_{p_1,\ldots,p_8} \approx \frac{24}{11} (p_1 p_2 p_3 p_4 p_5 p_6 p_7 p_8)^2 / [(N-8)^8 \langle p^2 \rangle_F^8]\).
- For the exponential distribution with \(T = 0.25\) GeV, \(\langle p \rangle_F = 2T = 0.5\) GeV and \(\langle p^2 \rangle_F = 6T^2 = 0.375\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context
This work extends the known treatment of transverse momentum conservation as a source of azimuthal correlations, previously studied for direct flow \(v_1\) measurements by Borghini (2000), Chajecki (2008), and Bzdak (2010). The results directly address the interpretation of recent experimental observations of long-range azimuthal correlations in small systems (p+p, p+Pb, d+Au) at RHIC and the LHC, where the ATLAS collaboration (2017) reported \(c_2\{k\} > 0\) in the lowest multiplicity events. The paper shows that TMC produces positive cumulants of the same sign as those seen in low-multiplicity data, complicating the extraction of genuine collective flow via the subevent cumulant method proposed by Jia (2017). The authors note that coupling TMC with hydrodynamic flow correlations should naturally explain the sign change of \(c_2\{4\}\) and \(c_2\{8\}\) as a function of particle number, which they indicate is under investigation.
