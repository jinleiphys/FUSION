# Fluctuations of the largest fragment size in percolation and multifragmentation

## Key claim
The Aladin data on fragmentation of 197Au projectiles are remarkably well reproduced by a bond percolation model, and a critical behavior is identified on the basis of fluctuations of the largest fragment size. The authors qualify that the pseudocritical point is identified at Zbound = 54, which corresponds to a He-Li isotope temperature corrected for secondary decays of 5.2 ± 0.4 MeV, and that the percolation model reproduces the data with no free parameters and no corrections for secondary decays.

## Method
The authors performed three-dimensional bond percolation simulations on simple cubic lattices with free boundary conditions, generating events for bond probabilities uniformly distributed in the interval [0,1] and sorting them by Zbound. They computed cumulant ratios K2 (variance normalized to squared mean), K3 (skewness), and K4 (kurtosis excess) of the largest fragment size distribution P(Zmax) and compared these with Aladin S114 data on fragmentation of projectile spectators in 197Au + Au, In, Cu collisions at incident energies of 600-1000 AMeV.

## Key numbers
- The pseudocritical point in percolation is located at Zbound/Z0 = 0.84, independent of system size.
- The experimental pseudocritical point is identified at Zbound = 54.
- The mean system size at Zbound = 54 is estimated as Z0 = Zbound/0.84 ≈ 64.
- The He-Li isotope temperature corrected for secondary decays at the pseudocritical point is 5.2 ± 0.4 MeV.
- The fragment size distribution is reproduced over four orders of magnitude.
- Comparisons were performed at Zbound values in the range from 36 to 66.

## Context
This work extends percolation studies by Brzychczyk (2009) which demonstrated that cumulant analysis of largest fragment size distributions is a valuable tool for identifying phase transitions in fragmenting systems. The results are compared with Aladin S114 data previously presented by Schüttauf (1996) and the system sizes agree with experimental estimates by Pochodzalla (1995). The success of percolation suggests that clusters are formed at dense medium and isolated fragments are cold, supporting the "Little big bang" scenario of multifragmentation proposed by Campi (1986), and is consistent with the lattice gas model which is equivalent to bond percolation at normal density.
