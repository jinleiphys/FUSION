# Electron and Photon induced Reactions on Nuclei in the Nucleon Resonance Region

> We calculate total photon-nucleus and electron-nucleus cross sections $γA\to X$, $e A\to e^\prime X$ and several cross sections on pion and eta production in nuclei in the energy regime between the first and the third resonance region for photon virtualities $Q^2\le 1.0 GeV^2$ within a semi-classical BUU transport model. In both cases we discuss the varying influence of several medium modifications on the cross sections for different values of Q^2.

# Electron and Photon induced Reactions on Nuclei in the Nucleon Resonance Region

## Key claim

The authors calculate that a semi-classical BUU transport model can describe total and exclusive electron-nucleus and photon-nucleus cross sections from the Δ resonance through the third resonance region for photon virtualities up to Q² ≤ 1.0 GeV², but the agreement depends on the specific treatment of in-medium modifications, particularly the Δ absorption width and the D₁₃(1520) broadening, and the model overestimates data by about 15% at E = 1.5 GeV on ¹²C.

## Method

The authors use a semi-classical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model that propagates nucleons, 19 baryonic resonances (including P₃₃(1232), D₁₃(1520), S₁₁(1535), F₁₅(1680)), and mesons (π, η, ρ, ω, φ, σ) through a nuclear medium with a mean-field potential and collision integral. Elementary γ*N cross sections for one-pion, two-pion, and eta production are constructed from partial-wave helicity amplitudes with Breit-Wigner resonant terms and background contributions, with Q² dependence introduced via fitted form factors of the form f(Q²) = (1 + (Q²/a)ᵇ)⁻ᶜ; these are then embedded in the nucleus via impulse approximation with Fermi motion, Pauli blocking, in-medium widths, and a Δ potential U_Δ = (2/3)S.

## Key numbers

- Q² range considered: Q² ≤ 1.0 GeV²
- For the Δ(1232) collisional width from Δ-hole model: Γ_coll/2 = 40 MeV × (ρ/ρ₀)
- Compressibility values used: hard EOS K = 380 MeV; medium momentum-dependent EOS K = 290 MeV
- For the D₁₃(1520) in-medium Nρ width: total width at pole mass ≈ 335 MeV at nuclear matter density (factor ~10 enhancement)
- At E = 1.5 GeV on ¹²C, the model overestimates data by about 15%
- For Q² ≥ 0.2 GeV², the second resonance region disappears due to Fermi motion broadening
- The Δ-hole and spreading potential widths reduce π⁰ production cross sections by about 30% compared to the BUU-width calculation
- For the D₁₃(1520) produced with momenta around 800 MeV, the momentum-dependent potential gives a mass shift Δm* ≈ 50 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous BUU transport calculations for real photons (Effenberger and Mosel, 1999) to virtual photons in electron-nucleus reactions, covering the energy regime from the Δ peak through the third resonance region. It builds on Δ-hole model calculations by Gil et al. (1997) that covered the quasielastic peak and Δ resonance, and addresses the disappearance of the D₁₃(1520) resonance in photoabsorption data (Bianchi et al., 1996), previously attributed to rho meson medium modifications by Post et al. (1999) and Rapp et al. (1999). The results enable future comparisons with exclusive meson production data on nuclei at finite Q² and provide a framework for testing in-medium resonance properties across different photon virtualities.
