# Average transverse momentum of hadrons in proton-nucleus collisions in the wounded nucleon model

> In the wounded nucleon model, a proton-nucleus (p+A) collision is a superposition of independent nucleon-nucleon collisions. We use this model to calculate the average transverse momentum of pions, kaons and protons in high energy p+A collisions. For the same number of produced particles, because the number of participants differs, in the wounded nucleon model the transverse momentum of hadrons can differ between p+p and p+A collisions. In this model we find that the average transverse momentum in high multiplicity p+A collisions depends weakly on the number of produced particles, and underestimates the preliminary experimental data at the LHC. The difference is small for pions and greater for kaons and protons. The magnitude of this difference is consistent with hydrodynamic expectations.

## Key claim

In the wounded nucleon model, the average transverse momentum of hadrons in high-multiplicity p+Pb collisions depends weakly on the number of produced particles and underestimates preliminary CMS data; the discrepancy is small for pions (~100 MeV) and larger for kaons (~300 MeV) and protons (~500 MeV), consistent with hydrodynamic radial flow expectations.

## Method

The authors treat a p+A collision as a superposition of independent nucleon-nucleon (p+p) collisions, with each wounded nucleon acting as an independent source of particles. They use Glauber Monte Carlo simulations to sample the number of participants and negative binomial distributions for particle multiplicities from each source, with input parameters from p+p data at √s = 2.76 TeV and 7 TeV. The average transverse momentum in p+A collisions is then computed by summing over sources, using the approximation that the single-source ⟨p_T⟩ equals the p+p ⟨p_T⟩ at a scaled multiplicity (κ = 1.5 times the source multiplicity).

## Key numbers

- For N_track = 200 in p+Pb collisions at √s = 7 TeV, the wounded nucleon model underestimates preliminary CMS data by ~100 MeV for pions, ~300 MeV for kaons, and ~500 MeV for protons.
- The inelastic p+p cross-section used in Glauber simulations is σ = 65 mb.
- Calculations are performed for p+Pb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV and √s = 7 TeV, with the model results compared to preliminary CMS data at √s = 5.02 TeV.
- The coefficient κ in the approximation ⟨p_T^α⟩_{N_i}^s ≈ ⟨p_T^α⟩_{N_tot = κ N_i}^{pp} is varied within the range [1.3, 1.7] to estimate theoretical uncertainty.

## Context

This work extends the wounded nucleon model (Bialas 1976, Bialas 2004), which successfully describes particle multiplicities in p(d)+A collisions at RHIC and SPS energies, to the average transverse momentum at LHC energies. The results provide a baseline for distinguishing collective effects (such as hydrodynamic radial flow or CGC coherence) from a simple superposition of elementary p+p collisions. The authors note that the observed discrepancy is consistent with hydrodynamic expectations (Bozek 2011, Bzdak 2013, Qin 2013) and suggest that similar studies in d+Au collisions at RHIC (PHENIX data) could further test for radial flow.
