# Universality and scaling limit of weakly-bound tetramers

> The occurrence of a new limit cycle in few-body physics, expressing a universal scaling function relating the binding energies of two consecutive tetramer states, is revealed, considering a renormalized zero-range two-body interaction applied to four identical bosons. The tetramer energy spectrum is obtained when adding a boson to an Efimov bound state with energy $B_3$ in the unitary limit (for zero two-body binding, or infinite two-body scattering length). Each excited $N-$th tetramer energy $B_4^{(N)}$ is shown to slide along a scaling function as a short-range four-body scale is changed, emerging from the 3+1 threshold for a universal ratio $B_4^ {(N)}/B_3 \simeq 4.6$, which does not depend on $N$. The new scale can also be revealed by a resonance in the atom-trimer recombination process.

# Universality and scaling limit of weakly-bound tetramers

## Key claim

The authors claim that a new, independent four-body short-range scale exists for four identical bosons near the unitary limit, which generates a universal scaling function relating the binding energies of consecutive tetramer states. This claim is hedged by the condition that the four-body scale only becomes apparent when the three-body binding energy is held fixed while the four-body parameter is independently varied; previous calculations that appeared to require only two- and three-body scales had inadvertently embedded the four-body scale without recognizing its independence.

## Method

The authors solved the four-boson Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in momentum space using a renormalized zero-range two-body interaction with a subtraction procedure that introduces a renormalizing momentum scale μ₄ for the four-body sector. They kept the two-body scattering length at infinity (unitary limit) and the three-body binding energy fixed, while independently varying the four-body scale μ₄ relative to the three-body scale μ₃. The resulting homogeneous integral equations for total angular momentum zero were discretized with Gaussian quadrature and solved by a Lanczos-like iterative method.

## Key numbers

- At the threshold where the first excited tetramer state emerges from the 3+1 threshold, the ratio of the ground tetramer binding energy to the trimer binding energy is B₄⁽⁰⁾/B₃ ≃ 4.6, independent of N.
- The first excited tetramer state appears at a scale ratio μ₄/μ₃ ≅ 1.6, and the second excited state appears at μ₄/μ₃ ≅ 21.
- The third excited tetramer state is predicted to emerge near μ₄/μ₃ ≈ 240.
- For μ₄/μ₃ = 1, the ground tetramer binding energy ratio is B₄⁽⁰⁾/B₃ = 3.10; for μ₄/μ₃ = 400, it reaches B₄⁽⁰⁾/B₃ = 1.62 × 10⁴.
- The binding energy ratio of two consecutive tetramers at the critical thresholds is B₄⁽ᴺ⁾/B₄⁽ᴺ⁺¹⁾ ≅ 4.6.

## Context

This work extends the Efimov physics of three identical bosons (Efimov 1971) to the four-body sector by demonstrating that a new limit cycle exists for tetramers, independent of the three-body scale. It builds on the renormalization approach of Amorim (1999) for trimers and the earlier suggestion of an independent four-body scale by the same group (EPL 2006). The results contradict the conclusion of Stecher et al. (Nature 2009) that only two- and three-body scales are needed near a Feshbach resonance, and instead predict that atom-trimer recombination resonances can reveal the new four-body scale. The findings enable experimental searches in cold-atom systems where the ratio between scattering lengths for consecutive tetramer resonances may deviate from the Efimov ratio of 22.7.
