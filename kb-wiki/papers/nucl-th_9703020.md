# On the Dirac Structure of the Nucleon Selfenergy in Nuclear Matter

> The relativistic structure of the self-energy of a nucleon in nuclear matter is investigated including the imaginary and real components which arise from the terms of first and second order in the NN interaction. A parameterized form of Brueckner $G$ matrix is used for the NN interaction. The effects of the terms beyond the DBHF approximation on quasiparticle energies and the optical potential for nucleon-nucleus scattering are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper investigates the relativistic Dirac structure of the nucleon self-energy in nuclear matter including both real and imaginary components from first- and second-order diagrams in the NN interaction, going beyond the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approximation. The authors find that the 2p1h (two-particle-one-hole) and 2h1p (two-hole-one-particle) contributions modify the quasiparticle energies and optical potential, but the energy dependence of the Schrodinger equivalent potential remains dominated by the relativistic structure of the underlying Hartree-Fock self-energy rather than by these higher-order dispersive effects.

## Method

The authors calculated the imaginary parts of the nucleon self-energy for all combinations of energy and momentum, including direct and exchange terms for both 2p1h and 2h1p diagrams, using a parameterized Brueckner G-matrix based on effective sigma and omega meson exchange fitted to DBHF results with the Bonn A NN potential. The real parts of the second-order contributions were obtained from the imaginary parts via dispersion relations, and a subtracted dispersion relation was used to avoid double counting of 2p1h terms already contained in the DBHF self-energy. The resulting complex self-energy was then transformed into a Schrodinger equivalent optical potential for nucleon-nucleus scattering.

## Key numbers

- Hartree-Fock self-energy components at Fermi momentum k_F = 1.4 fm^{-1}: Sigma^s_HF = -374.9 MeV, Sigma^0_HF = -289.8 MeV
- Effective meson coupling constants at k_F = 1.4 fm^{-1}: g_sigma = 8.536, g_omega = 9.536
- Meson masses: m_sigma = 550 MeV, m_omega = 782.6 MeV
- Form factor cutoff mass: Lambda = 1500 MeV
- Inclusion of 2h1p terms reduces quasiparticle energy by 17.5 MeV for momenta near zero, 5.3 MeV at k = k_F, and becomes negligible around k = 2k_F
- Space-like vector component yields a reduction of about 3 percent in the absolute values of the renormalized scalar and vector potentials

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the nucleon self-energy beyond the mean-field approximation, specifically the DBHF approach of Brockmann and Machleidt (1990) and Muther, Polls, and Machleidt (1995), by including both 2p1h and 2h1p contributions with off-shell effects and exchange terms. Previous work by Horowitz (1984) and Steffani (1990) had studied these diagrams only at the on-shell energy. The results enable improved microscopic understanding of nucleon-nucleus scattering optical potentials and suggest that combining relativistic effects with hole-hole scattering terms may help resolve the Coester band problem in nuclear matter saturation.
