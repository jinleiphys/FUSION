# Review on recent progress in the study of the $N^*(920)$ subthreshold singularity and the $σ/f_0(500)$ meson

> We summarize recent results on studies of $ππ$ and $πN$ scatterings. They include the finding of a negative-parity nucleon pole with a mass lower than the nucleon mass, and the pole trajectory of $f_0(500)$ as the pion mass varies. The results are obtained from model-independent dispersion analyses. We also study the thermal properties of $f_0(500)$ based on the $O(N)$ $σ$ model and $N/D$ method.

## Key claim
The paper reviews evidence for a negative-parity nucleon pole, \(N^*(920)\), located below the nucleon mass and the \(\pi N\) threshold, which is counterintuitive because a resonance below threshold should not have a decay width in perturbation theory; the width is instead a non-perturbative property of the \(S\)-matrix pole.

## Method
The authors summarize dispersion analyses of \(\pi\pi\) and \(\pi N\) scatterings using Steiner or Roy–Steiner equations, which incorporate unitarity, analyticity, and crossing symmetry. For the \(N^*(920)\) pole, the analysis uses the product representation for partial wave amplitudes and tree-level or \(O(p^3)\) chiral amplitudes to estimate left-hand cut contributions. For the \(f_0(500)\) meson, modified Roy equations are constructed to handle bound-state scenarios at large pion masses, and the \(O(N)\) linear \(\sigma\) model combined with the \(N/D\) method is used to study pole trajectories at finite temperature.

## Key numbers
* \(N^*(920)\) pole position from Roy–Steiner equation: \(\sqrt{s} = (918 \pm 3) - i(163 \pm 9)\) MeV.
* Earlier product-representation estimate of the \(N^*(920)\) pole: \(\sqrt{s} = 895(81) - i\,164(23)\) MeV.
* For \(m_\pi = 239\) MeV, the \(f_0(500)\) pole from modified Roy equations: \(\sqrt{s} \sim 543 - i250\) MeV.
* For \(m_\pi = 391\) MeV, the \(f_0(500)\) becomes a bound state at \(\sqrt{s} \sim 759^{+7}_{-16}\) MeV, with a subthreshold resonance at \(\sqrt{s} = 269^{+40}_{-25} - i211^{+26}_{-23}\) MeV.
* In the \(O(N)\) model at finite temperature, the \(\sigma\) pole becomes a bound state when \(T \simeq 137\) MeV.

## Context
This review extends the dispersion-relation approach of Cao (2022) and Wang (2017) for the \(N^*(920)\) pole, and the Roy-equation analysis of Cao (2023) for the \(f_0(500)\) trajectory with varying pion mass. It contrasts with earlier \(K\)-matrix and unitarized \(\chi\)PT analyses (e.g., Doring (2009), Pelaez (2010)) that either missed the subthreshold pole or failed to reproduce the bound-state behavior at large pion masses. The results enable future lattice QCD studies of the \(N^*(920)\) pole trajectory and thermal investigations of chiral symmetry restoration, including the possible role of parity doubling.
