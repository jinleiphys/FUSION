# Q**2 Dependence of Nuclear Shadowing

> We re-examine the predictions of a two-phase model of shadowing in nuclear deep-inelastic scattering in light of new NMC data on the x, A and Q**2 dependence of the ratios of structure functions. The model, which combines vector meson dominance at low Q**2 with diffractive Pomeron exchange for large Q**2, agrees with the observed small, but non-zero, slopes in log Q**2, which indicate the importance of higher twist shadowing effects in the transition region, 0.1 < Q**2 < 1 GeV**2. We note also that the latest E665 data on the deuteron to proton ratio suggests the presence of a small amount of shadowing in the deuteron.

## Key claim

The two-phase model of nuclear shadowing, combining vector meson dominance at low Q² with diffractive Pomeron exchange at high Q², agrees with the observed small but non-zero slopes in log Q² in the transition region 0.1 < Q² < 1 GeV², indicating the importance of higher twist shadowing effects there.

## Method

The authors extended earlier calculations of shadowing in deuterium and heavier nuclei using a two-phase model. At high virtuality, the interaction is parametrized via diffractive scattering through double and triple Pomeron exchange, with the shadowing correction written as a convolution of the Pomeron structure function with a flux factor. At low virtuality, a vector meson dominance (VMD) model is applied using Glauber theory for multiple scattering of the ρ⁰, ω, and φ mesons, with the vector meson propagators producing a strong Q² dependence that peaks near Q² ~ 1 GeV².

## Key numbers

- The low-Q² extrapolation of the qq̅ component uses a factor Q²/(Q² + Q₀²) with Q₀² ≈ 0.485 GeV², interpreted as the inverse size of partons inside the virtual photon.
- The transition region where high- and low-Q² descriptions merge occurs for Q² between about 0.5 and 5 GeV².
- The reabsorption cross section σ_XN ∼ σ_VN ∼ 20-30 mb, though the F₂ᴬ/F₂ᴰ ratios are not very sensitive to the precise value in the NMC Q² range.
- Short range correlations are included through a Fermi gas correlation function that puts a "hole" in the two-body density approximately 0.5 fm wide at 1/2 maximum density.
- For the deuteron, the shadowing correction amounts to about 1.5% at x = 10⁻² up to about 3% for x ≲ 10⁻⁵.
- The NMC data for C/D and Ca/D ratios cover x values from x = 0.0003 to x = 0.07, with Q² values reaching as low as Q² ≈ 0.05 GeV².

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of shadowing in deuterium (Melnitchouk and Thomas, 1993) and heavier nuclei (Melnitchouk and Thomas, 1993) to compare with new NMC data on the x, A, and Q² dependence of structure function ratios. The two-phase model is similar to the work of Kwiecinski and Badelek (1988, 1990, 1992). The results enable predictions for future NMC data on the Sn to C ratio, where observation of non-zero slopes in log Q² would support the hypothesis that the intermediate-Q² region is dominated by non-scaling, higher-twist effects, contradicting models based solely on partonic mechanisms (Mueller and Qiu, 1986; Brodsky and Lu, 1990; Kumano, 1993).
