# Relativistic Meson Spectroscopy and In-Medium Effects

> We extend our earlier model of $q\bar q$ mesons using relativistic quasipotential (QP) wave equations to include open-flavor states and running quark-gluon coupling effects. Global fits to meson spectra are achieved with rms deviations from experiment of 43-50 MeV. We examine in-medium effects through their influence on the confining interaction and predict the confining strength at which the masses of certain mesons fall below the threshold of their dominant decay channel.

# Relativistic Meson Spectroscopy and In-Medium Effects

## Key claim

The authors extend their earlier relativistic quasipotential model of quark-antiquark mesons to include open-flavor states and a running quark-gluon coupling, achieving global fits with rms deviations of 43-50 MeV from experiment. However, the low-mass pion and kaon are excluded from fits using reduction A because that equation does poorly at representing these states, and the authors adopt the philosophy that only if a model adequately reproduces the pion and kaon masses should these states be retained.

## Method

The authors employ two different three-dimensional reductions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation (labeled reduction A and reduction B) with an interaction kernel consisting of one-gluon exchange in the ladder approximation plus a scalar Lorentz structure confining potential. They incorporate a running strong coupling anchored to the measured value at the Z-boson mass, with the momentum scale related to the meson mass through two fitted parameters, and include open-flavor mesons in the global fit. In-medium effects are modeled by varying the string tension relative to its vacuum value, and the meson masses are plotted against the ratio of in-medium to vacuum string tension to predict thresholds for decay channel closure.

## Key numbers

- Global fits to meson spectra achieve rms deviations from experiment of 43-50 MeV (45 states for reduction A, 47 states for reduction B).
- The psi(3770) mass crosses the D anti-D threshold at a mass of about 3.71 GeV when the string tension falls to about 0.9 sigma_0.
- The phi mass crosses the K anti-K threshold at a mass of about 9.8 GeV when sigma/sigma_0 is approximately 0.67.
- Using a specific model for the temperature dependence of the string tension, the psi(3770) crosses the D anti-D threshold at a temperature of 0.43 T_c, compared to 0.56 T_c obtained in a previous non-relativistic study.
- Using the same temperature model, the K anti-K decay channel of the phi closes at a temperature of about 0.74 T_c.
- The light constituent quark mass obtained in the spectrum fits is approximately 340 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier model of Ref. [3] by including open-flavor mesons and a running quark-gluon coupling, and provides a consistent relativistic framework for evaluating in-medium effects that previous non-relativistic potential models lacked. The in-medium results for the psi(3770) are compared with a non-relativistic Schrodinger equation treatment by Ref. [14] which used a bag model to determine the D anti-D threshold, and the phi results are compared with a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model study by Ref. [15] which found the K anti-K decay channel closing at a temperature of about 0.45 times the light quark mass. The authors note that work in progress includes calculations of a large set of vacuum and in-medium observables for all meson states.
