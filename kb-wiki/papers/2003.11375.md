# Nonperturbative Flavor Breaking in Topological Susceptibility at Chiral Crossover

> We demonstrate that the QCD topological susceptibility nonperturbatively gets a significant contribution signaled by flavor-nonuniversal quark condensates at around the pseudo-critical temperature of the chiral crossover. It implies a remarkable flavor breaking in the axial anomaly as well as the QCD theta vacuum in high temperature QCD, which are almost flavor universal in the vacuum. A nontrivial flavor breaking is triggered by nonperturbative thermal loop corrections at around the chiral crossover, which is different from the trivial flavor violation just scaled by the quark mass ratio, observed at asymptotically high temperatures. This critical flavor violation cannot be dictated by the chiral perturbation theory with that lattice QCD usually compares, or the dilute instanton gas approximation based on that its astrophysical implications have conventionally been made. This would give an impact on the thermal history and the cosmological evolution of QCD axion including the estimate of the relic abundance as a dark matter candidate.

## Key claim
The QCD topological susceptibility receives a significant nonperturbative contribution from flavor-nonuniversal quark condensates at around the chiral crossover temperature, implying a remarkable flavor breaking in the axial anomaly that cannot be captured by chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) or the dilute instanton gas approximation.

## Method
The authors employed a three-flavor linear sigma model with an axial-anomaly induced flavor-breaking term, using the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) formalism in the Hartree approximation to compute nonperturbative thermal loop corrections to the effective potential. The topological susceptibility was then extracted from the second derivative of the CJT effective potential with respect to the theta parameter, and quark condensates were computed from derivatives with respect to current quark masses.

## Key numbers
- Pseudo-critical temperature from the CJT analysis: T_pc* ≈ 215 MeV (compared to lattice QCD value ≈ 155 MeV, about 30% deviation)
- Vacuum topological susceptibility prediction: χ_top(T=0) ≈ 0.0263/fm^4 (consistent with lattice values 0.019(9)/fm^4 from Bonati (2015) and 0.0245(24)_stat(03)_flow(12)_cont/fm^4 from Borsanyi (2016))
- Vacuum ratio of strange to light quark condensates: ⟨s̄s⟩(0)/⟨l̄l⟩(0) ≈ 1.18
- At high temperatures, the topological susceptibility ratio χ_top(T)/χ_top^3fl(T) asymptotically converges to ≈ 3/(2+m_l/m_s) ≈ 1.5
- For T/T_pc* ~ 1.5-2, the topological susceptibility is enhanced by about 15-30% compared to the flavor universal limit

## Context
This work extends the understanding of QCD topological susceptibility beyond the Leutwyler-Smiluga relation (Leutwyler 1992) and ChPT predictions (di Cortona 2015), which fail to describe the behavior at the chiral crossover. The analysis addresses discrepancies observed in lattice QCD simulations (Bonati 2015, Borsanyi 2016) that show substantial deviation from ChPT predictions. The finding enables improved calculations of QCD axion relic abundance and thermal history in cosmological models, and may help resolve tensions in the effective restoration of U(1)_A symmetry observed on lattices with two-flavor (Cossu 2013, Tomiya 2016) and 2+1 flavor (Bazavov 2012, Buchoff 2013) near the chiral limit.
