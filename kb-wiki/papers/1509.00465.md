# Color confinement from fluctuating topology

> QCD possesses a compact gauge group, and this implies a non-trivial topological structure of the vacuum. In this contribution to the Gribov-85 Memorial volume, we first discuss the origin of Gribov copies and their interpretation in terms of fluctuating topology in the QCD vacuum. We then describe the recent work with E. Levin that links the confinement of gluons and color screening to the fluctuating topology, and discuss implications for spin physics, high energy scattering, and the physics of quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper argues that Gribov copies in QCD should not be eliminated but instead summed over, and that this summation, captured by a Veneziano ghost, leads to gluon confinement and color screening. The hedge is that this mechanism is presented as a "simplistic approach" that parameterizes topological fluctuations via a single dimensionful parameter (topological susceptibility) without identifying the microscopic dynamics.

## Method

The paper uses the Veneziano ghost, a massless pseudo-vector field with a correlation function of the form \(K_{\mu\nu}(q) \rightarrow -\mu^4 / q^2 \, g_{\mu\nu}\) at small \(q^2\), to represent the effect of fluctuating topology in the QCD vacuum. This ghost couples to gluons through an effective vertex derived from the topological current, and the gluon propagator is then computed by solving a Dyson-Schwinger equation that includes the ghost-induced self-energy \(\Sigma(p) = -\mu^4 / p^2\). The resulting dressed gluon propagator is \(G(p) = 1/(p^2 + \mu^4/p^2)\).

## Key numbers

*   The topological susceptibility of pure Yang-Mills theory is \(\mu^4\), which sets the scale for the ghost correlation function and the gluon self-energy.
*   The gluon propagator \(G(p) = 1/(p^2 + \mu^4/p^2)\) has complex poles at \(p^2 = \pm i \mu^2\), implying a confinement scale of \(\sim \mu^{-1} \sim 1\) fm.
*   The model yields an average QCD coupling \(\alpha_0 \simeq 0.59\) for a renormalization point \(k = M_Z\), compared to the experimental value \(\alpha_0 \approx 0.5\) for \(\mu_I = 2\) GeV from Dokshitzer (no year given).
*   The Witten-Veneziano relation is given as \(m_{\eta'}^2 f_{\eta'}^2 = 4 N_f \, \mu^4\), linking the \(\eta'\) mass to the topological susceptibility.

## Context

This work extends the Gribov (1977) and Singer (1978) analysis of gauge copies by proposing that they arise from the compactness of the gauge group and should be summed over, rather than eliminated. It builds on the Veneziano (no year given) ghost approach to the \(U_A(1)\) problem and the Witten-Veneziano relation for the \(\eta'\) mass, and applies it to the gluon propagator for the first time. The result enables a practical way to compute power-suppressed corrections to QCD amplitudes, addresses the Froissart bound in high-energy scattering, and suggests that deconfinement and restoration of \(U_A(1)\) symmetry occur at the same temperature, consistent with lattice data from Bonati (2013) and Kharzeev (1998).
