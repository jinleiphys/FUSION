# Finite size effects in hadron-quark phase transition by the Dyson-Schwinger method

> We study the hadron-quark phase transition, taking into account the finite-size effects for neutron star matter. For the hadron phase, we adopt a realistic equation of state within the framework of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory. For the quark phase, we apply the Dyson-Schwinger method. The properties of the mixed phase are clarified by considering the finite-size effects. We find that, if the surface tension is strong enough, the equation of state becomes to be close the one with the Maxwell condition, though we properly adopt the Gibbs conditions. This result is qualitatively the same with the one by the use of the simple bag model. We also find that the mass-radius relation by the EoS is consistent with the observations of massive neutron stars.

## Key claim

If the surface tension at the hadron-quark interface is strong enough (around 80 MeV fm\(^{-2}\)), the equation of state for the mixed phase approaches the Maxwell construction result, even though the Gibbs conditions are properly adopted; this behavior is qualitatively the same as that found with the simple bag model.

## Method

The hadron phase equation of state was computed using the nonrelativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) theory with the Bonn-B (BOB) nucleon-nucleon potential, omitting hyperons. The quark phase was described via the Dyson-Schwinger (DS) method under the rainbow approximation, using a Gaussian-type effective gluon interaction with two parameter sets (DS1 and DS2, corresponding to \(\alpha=1\) and \(\alpha=2\)). The mixed phase was treated in the Wigner-Seitz approximation with a sharp boundary, considering pasta-like structures (droplets, rods, slabs, tubes, bubbles) and including surface tension and charge screening.

## Key numbers

- Surface tension values used: \(\sigma = 40\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\) and \(\sigma = 80\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\) (range suggested by lattice gauge simulations: \(\sigma \approx 10\)–\(100\) MeV fm\(^{-2}\)).
- DS model parameters: \(\omega = 0.5\) GeV, \(D = 1\) GeV\(^2\); current-quark masses \(m_{u,d}=0\) and \(m_s = 115\) MeV.
- Maximum neutron-star masses for all models are slightly larger than the observed value \(M = (1.97 \pm 0.04) M_\odot\) from Demorest et al. (2010).
- Most neutron-star radii from the models lie around \(R \sim 12\) km, consistent with the statistical analysis of Steiner et al. (2012).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the hadron-quark mixed phase with finite-size effects, which had used the simple bag model (Maruyama et al., 2007; Yasutake et al., 2009), by instead employing the Dyson-Schwinger method for the quark phase to incorporate momentum-dependent quark mass functions. The results show that the qualitative dependence on surface tension is universal across quark models. The computed mass-radius relations are consistent with the \(\sim 2 M_\odot\) neutron-star observation of Demorest et al. (2010) and the statistical constraints of Steiner et al. (2012), enabling future studies to include hyperon degrees of freedom and more refined baryon-baryon interactions from lattice QCD.
