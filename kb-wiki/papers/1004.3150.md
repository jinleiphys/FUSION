# Two-proton radioactivity and three-body decay. V. Improved momentum distributions

> Nowadays quantum-mechanical theory allows one to reliably calculate the processes of 2p radioactivity (true three-body decays) and the corresponding energy and angular correlations up to distances of the order of 1000 fm. However, the precision of modern experiments has now become sufficient to indicate some deficiency of the predicted theoretical distributions. In this paper we discuss the extrapolation along the classical trajectories as a method to improve the convergence of the theoretical energy and angular correlations at very large distances (of the order of atomic distances), where only the long-range Coulomb forces are still operating. The precision of this approach is demonstrated using the "exactly" solvable semianalytical models with simplified three-body Hamiltonians. It is also demonstrated that for heavy 2p emitters, the 2p decay momentum distributions can be sensitive to the effect of the screening by atomic electrons. We compare theoretical results with available experimental data.

## Key claim

The classical extrapolation (CE) method can improve the convergence of theoretical energy and angular correlations for two-proton (2p) radioactivity at very large distances (up to atomic scales), but only if the starting hypersphere radius ρ_max is sufficiently large (≳500 fm); for heavy 2p emitters, the momentum distributions may also be sensitive to atomic electron screening.

## Method

The authors first solve the quantum-mechanical three-body problem using the hyperspherical harmonics (HH) method to obtain the three-body wave function Ψ₃⁽⁺⁾ with outgoing asymptotic boundary conditions at a hypersphere of radius ρ_max (typically 500–2000 fm). At this hypersphere, they convert the wave function into classical "events" via a Monte Carlo procedure, using the flux vectors to define initial conditions for Newtonian equations of motion for the Jacobi vectors. These classical trajectories are then propagated to much larger distances ρ_ext (up to 10⁵ fm) where only long-range Coulomb forces operate, and the stabilized momentum distributions are reconstructed from the ensemble of trajectories.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁵Fe with decay energy 1.154 MeV and basis size K_max = 20, radii between 500 and 2000 fm are needed to get reasonable solutions.
- For ¹⁹Mg in the direct-decay model (E_T = 0.75 MeV), visual stability of trajectories is achieved at ρ_ext ~ 7000 fm, with minor drift continuing to much larger distances.
- For ¹⁹Mg in the direct-decay model, the calculated half-life is T₁/₂ = 58 ps (Γ = 7.9 × 10⁻¹¹ MeV), compared to the experimental value of 4 ps.
- For ¹⁹Mg in the "diproton" model, the calculated half-life is T₁/₂ = 0.39 ps (Γ = 1.2 × 10⁻⁹ MeV).
- For the "diproton" model, ρ_ext = 10⁵ fm is used for complete stabilization of the classical extrapolation.

## Context

This work extends the series of papers by Grigorenko et al. (2000–2009) developing a consistent quantum-mechanical theory of two-proton radioactivity and three-body decays. It addresses a deficiency noted in earlier calculations (Grigorenko 2009) where experimental precision for heavy 2p emitters like ⁴⁵Fe exceeded the accuracy of predicted momentum distributions due to limited radial range and approximate boundary conditions. The classical extrapolation method was originally suggested in Grigorenko (2003) but not previously studied in detail. The paper enables more precise extraction of nuclear structure information from correlation measurements in 2p decays, which have recently become available for ⁶Be (Grigorenko 2009), ¹⁶Ne (Mukha 2008), ¹⁹Mg (Mukha 2007, 2008), ⁴⁵Fe (Miernik 2007), and ⁹⁴Ag (Mukha 2006).
