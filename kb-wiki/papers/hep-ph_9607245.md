# On the analysis of the pion--nucleon $σ$--term: The size of the remainder at the Cheng--Dashen point

> We calculate the one--loop contributions of order $M_π^4$ to the difference $Δ_R$ between the on--shell pion--nucleon scattering amplitude $\bar{D}^+(0,2M_π^2)$ at the Cheng--Dashen point $ν=0$, $t=2M_π^2$ and the scalar form factor $σ(2M_π^2)$ in the framework of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. We proof that to this order $Δ_R$ contains $no$ chiral logarithms and therefore it vanishes simply as $M_π^4$ in the chiral limit. Numerically, we find as an upper limit $Δ_R \simeq 2\,$MeV.

## Key claim

The remainder \(\Delta_R\) at the Cheng-Dashen point, which connects the on-shell pion-nucleon scattering amplitude to the scalar form factor, contains no chiral logarithms at order \(q^4\) in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory and therefore vanishes simply as \(M_\pi^4\) in the chiral limit; the authors find an upper limit of \(\Delta_R \simeq 2\) MeV, which they consider a conservative bound.

## Method

The authors performed a complete one-loop calculation to order \(q^4\) in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (SU(2) framework of Bernard, Kaiser, and Meißner). They evaluated the difference \(\Delta_R = F_\pi^2 \bar{D}^+(0,2M_\pi^2) - \sigma(2M_\pi^2)\) by computing pion loop diagrams with at most one insertion from the dimension-two pion-nucleon Lagrangian \(\mathcal{L}_{\pi N}^{(2)}\) and local contact terms from \(\mathcal{L}_{\pi N}^{(4)}\), with the low-energy constants estimated via resonance exchange (Delta(1232), scalar-isoscalar meson, and nucleon resonances). The scalar form factor was evaluated in the Breit frame and the scattering amplitude in the center-of-mass frame, with frame-dependent corrections verified to be of order \(q^5\) or higher.

## Key numbers

- \(\Delta_R \simeq 2\) MeV: upper bound for the remainder at the Cheng-Dashen point from the complete \(q^4\) calculation.
- \(\Delta_R^{(\Delta)} = 0.58\) MeV: contribution from tree-level \(\Delta(1232)\)-exchange, using \(g_{\pi N\Delta} = 3 g_{\pi N}/\sqrt{2}\) and the Goldberger-Treiman relation.
- \(\Delta_R^{(S)} \simeq 1.1\) MeV: estimated contribution from scalar-isoscalar meson exchange with a four-derivative coupling, allowing a factor of two uncertainty.
- \(\Delta_R^{\rm (GSS)} = 0.46\) MeV: previous one-loop result from Gasser et al. (1988) truncated at order \(M_\pi^4\).
- \(\sigma(2M_\pi^2)^{(K\eta-loop)} \simeq 0.4\) MeV: estimated \(K\eta\) loop contribution to the scalar form factor at order \(M_\pi^4\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier one-loop calculation of the \(\pi N\) scattering amplitude to order \(q^3\) by Gasser et al. (1988), which found \(\Delta_R = 0.35\) MeV, and addresses the conjecture of Pagels and Pardee (1971) that \(\Delta_R\) might contain large chiral logarithms of the form \(M_\pi^4 \ln M_\pi\). The authors prove that such logarithms are absent at order \(q^4\), confirming the earlier estimate of Brown, Pardee, and Peccei (1971) that the remainder is small. The result enables a more reliable extraction of the pion-nucleon sigma-term \(\sigma(0)\) from \(\pi N\) scattering data, and consequently the strangeness content of the proton, by showing that \(\Delta_R\) is negligible compared to current experimental uncertainties (typically \(\pm 8\) MeV).
