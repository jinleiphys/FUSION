# Extraction of Electrostrong Parameters of $N^{*}(1520)$ from Eta Photoproduction

# Extraction of Electrostrong Parameters of $N^{*}(1520)$ from Eta Photoproduction

## Key claim
The combination of recently obtained polarized target asymmetry and polarized photon asymmetry data with differential cross-section data in eta photoproduction yields model-insensitive constraints on the electrostrong parameters for the $N^{*}(1520)$ resonance, with the extracted ratio of helicity amplitudes $A_{3/2}/A_{1/2}$ being essentially independent of uncertainties in strong interaction parameters. This ratio provides a critical test for QCD-inspired hadron models, though the extraction depends on the properties of the dominant $N^{*}(1535)$ resonance and the analysis uses an effective Lagrangian approach in tree approximation.

## Method
The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach in the tree approximation, including $s$- and $u$-channel nucleon and resonance Born terms and $t$-channel vector meson ($\rho$ and $\omega$) exchanges, to analyze the reaction $\gamma + p \rightarrow p + \eta$ for photon lab energies from threshold (707 MeV) to about 900 MeV. They performed global fits using the CERN routine MINUIT to differential cross-section data from Mainz, polarized target asymmetry data from Bonn, and polarized photon asymmetry data from GRAAL, extracting the electrostrong parameters $\xi_{1/2}$ and $\xi_{3/2}$ for the $N^{*}(1520)$ resonance while varying properties of both $N^{*}(1520)$ and $N^{*}(1535)$ within PDG boundaries.

## Key numbers
- $\xi^{D}_{3/2} = 0.165 \pm 0.015 \pm 0.035$ (statistical and systematic errors) for the $N^{*}(1520)$ resonance
- $\xi^{D}_{1/2} = -0.065 \pm 0.010 \pm 0.015$ for the $N^{*}(1520)$ resonance
- $A_{3/2}/A_{1/2} = -2.5 \pm 0.2 \pm 0.4$ for the $N^{*}(1520)$ helicity amplitude ratio, extracted from eta photoproduction
- The 1996 PDG value from pionic processes was $-6.9 \pm 2.6$
- The $A_{1/2}$ amplitude for the proton target ranges from $-13$ to $-51$ in units of $10^{-3}$ GeV$^{1/2}$ in various constituent quark models; the $A_{3/2}$ amplitude ranges from 117 to 173 in the same units
- The partial width $\Gamma_{\eta}$ of $N^{*}(1520)$ is approximately 0.14 MeV; the total width $\Gamma$ is between 110 and 135 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous studies of eta photoproduction in the second resonance region (Davidson and Mukhopadhyay, 1988; Benmerrouche and Mukhopadhyay, 1991) by incorporating new polarization data from Bonn (Bock et al., 1998) and GRAAL (Ajaka et al., 1998) that were unavailable to earlier analyses. The extracted helicity amplitude ratio differs significantly from the PDG 1996 value obtained from pionic processes and from predictions of the non-relativistic quark model (Koniuk and Isgur, 1980) and the Capstick model (1992), while agreeing well with the prediction of the dynamical $U(7)$ symmetry model (Bijker et al., 1994). The results enable future lattice QCD calculations of this ratio and motivate electroproduction measurements at facilities like CEBAF to study the $q^{2}$ evolution of these helicity amplitudes.
