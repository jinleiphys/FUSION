# Polarized EMC Effect in the QMC Model

> The ratios of the in-medium to free nucleon structure functions for the unpolarized and polarized cases are obtained using the MIT bag model for the free case, along with the QMC model to incorporate the in-medium modifications of the structure functions. As discussed in earlier work, the observed nuclear EMC effect is reasonably well described. This gives us confidence to investigate the predictions of the model for the polarized EMC effect, the ratio $g_1^*(x)/g_1(x)$ for a bound proton to that of a free proton. This ratio is found to be substantially different from unity and very similar in shape and size to that found in the unpolarized case. This prediction of such a fundamental change in the valence structure of a bound nucleon needs to be tested experimentally at the earliest opportunity.

## Key claim

The polarized EMC effect (the ratio \(g_1^*(x)/g_1(x)\) for a bound proton to that of a free proton) is predicted to be substantially different from unity and very similar in shape and size to the unpolarized EMC effect, but this result is model-dependent and differs significantly from earlier predictions in the NJL model.

## Method

The authors used the MIT bag model to describe the free nucleon structure and the Quark Meson Coupling (QMC) model to incorporate in-medium modifications of the nucleon structure functions. The in-medium effects included the scalar (\(\sigma\)) and vector (\(\omega\)) mean fields and Fermi motion, with the Peierls-Yoccoz method used to construct approximate momentum eigenstates and ensure energy-momentum conservation. The model scale was set to \(\mu = 0.2~\mathrm{GeV}^2\) by fitting the free unpolarized valence quark parton distribution functions after next-to-leading-order QCD evolution.

## Key numbers

- The bag model parameters used: bag radius \(R = 1.0\) fm, scalar mean field \(g_{\sigma}\bar{\sigma} = 255\) MeV, free nucleon mass \(M = 938.3\) MeV, scalar diquark mass \(M_{n,s} = 500\) MeV, vector diquark mass \(M_{n,v} = 700\) MeV.
- Resultant in-medium values: effective quark mass \(m_q^* = -175\) MeV, bag eigenfrequency \(\Omega = 1.42\), vector mean field \(V_0 = 62\) MeV, effective nucleon mass \(M^* = 720\) MeV, effective scalar diquark mass \(M_{n,s}^* = 354\) MeV, effective vector diquark mass \(M_{n,v}^* = 554\) MeV.
- The suppression of \(g_A\) in nuclear matter was found to be 8.9% in this work, roughly half the 20% suppression found in the NJL model by Cloet et al. (2005).

## Context

This work extends the QMC model calculations of the unpolarized EMC effect (Thomas 1989, Saito 1992) to the polarized case, using the MIT bag model rather than the covariant NJL model used by Cloet et al. (2005, 2006). The results differ significantly from the NJL predictions, where the polarized EMC effect was found to be considerably larger than the unpolarized one, and also contrast with the short-range correlations approach (Hen 2016, 2013) where the polarized effect is expected to be suppressed. The upcoming experiments at Jefferson Lab (JLAB PR12-14-001) on nuclei such as \(^7\)Li are expected to measure the polarized EMC effect, which should help distinguish between these competing explanations.
