# In-medium relativistic kinetic theory and nucleon-meson systems

> Within the $σ-ω$ model of coupled nucleon-meson systems, a generalized relativistic Lenard--Balescu--equation is presented resulting from a relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA). This provides a systematic derivation of relativistic transport equations in the frame of nonequilibrium Green's function technique including medium effects as well as fluctuation effects. It contains all possible processes due to one meson exchange and special attention is kept to the off--shell character of the particles. As a new feature of many particle effects, processes are possible which can be interpreted as particle creation and annihilation due to in-medium one meson exchange. In-medium cross sections are obtained from the generalized derivation of collision integrals, which possess complete crossing symmetries.

## Key claim

Within the σ-ω model of coupled nucleon-meson systems, a generalized relativistic Lenard-Balescu equation is derived from a relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA), which includes medium effects and fluctuation effects; the derivation claims that processes interpretable as particle creation and annihilation due to in-medium one-meson exchange become possible as a new feature of many-particle effects, but this occurs only under the condition that the center-of-mass energy √s satisfies √s = -2 Re Σᵣ₀, meaning these channels open only when the zero component of the retarded self-energy becomes negative in a nuclear system.

## Method

The authors start from the Yukawa Lagrangian for scalar (σ) and vector (ω) mesons coupled to nucleons and use nonequilibrium Green's function techniques on the Keldysh contour, with the condition of weakening of initial correlations. They derive the Dyson equations for nucleon and meson Green's functions, introduce a vertex function via Schwinger's variational method, and apply a gradient expansion to obtain kinetic equations in Wigner space. The relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA) is used to close the set of equations, yielding a collision integral that includes a dynamical meson-exchange potential derived from the generalized optical theorem.

## Key numbers

- The quasiparticle energy is given by E₁/₂ = Re Σᵣ₀ ± √[(p - Re Σᵣ)² + (κ₀ + Re Σᵢ)²] evaluated at ω = E₁/₂, where κ₀ is the bare nucleon mass and Σᵣ₀, Σᵣ, Σᵢ are components of the retarded self-energy.
- The condition for new in-medium reaction channels (particle creation/annihilation) is √s = -2 Re Σᵣ₀, where √s is the center-of-mass energy.
- The in-medium proton-proton cross section is given by dσ/dΩ = (1/32π²) { [2gᵥ⁴/(s(mᵥ²+t)²)] [ (Δ(√s+Δ)+κ̃² - u/2)² + (Δ(√s+Δ)-κ̃² + s/2)² ] ... }, where Δ = Re Σᵣ₀, κ̃ = κ + Re Σᵢ, and gᵥ is the vector meson coupling constant.
- The meson quasiparticle energy is E_d² = p² + m² - Re Π, where Π is the polarization function.
- The static Yukawa potential from meson exchange has range r₀⁻² = m² - Re Π.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic Lenard-Balescu equation (Klimontovich, Kremp et al. 1986) to the relativistic domain within the σ-ω model of Walecka (1986), providing a systematic derivation of transport equations that include medium effects beyond the relativistic Vlasov treatment (Xie et al. 1990). The approach differs from earlier T-matrix approximations by Botermans and Malfliet (1990) and De Groot et al. (1980) by including pair creation and dynamical screening, and it enables a kinetic description of hot nuclear matter that incorporates Pauli blocking, inelastic processes, and density fluctuations through a self-consistent dynamical potential.
