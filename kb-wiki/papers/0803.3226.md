# On the collision of two shock waves in AdS5

> We consider two ultrarelativistic shock waves propagating and colliding in five-dimensional Anti-de-Sitter spacetime. By transforming to Rosen coordinates, we are able to find the form of the metric shortly after the collision. Using holographic renormalization, we calculate the energy-momentum tensor on the boundary of AdS space for early times after the collision. Via the gauge-gravity duality, this gives some insights on bulk dynamics of systems created by high energy scattering in strongly coupled gauge theories. We find that Bjorken boost-invariance is explicitely violated at early times and we obtain an estimate for the thermalization time in this simple system.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a strongly coupled gauge theory dual to colliding shock waves in AdS5, Bjorken boost-invariance is explicitly violated at early times after the collision, and provides an estimate for the thermalization time; however, this result depends on the specific choice of delta-function localized shock waves and may be reconciled with boost-invariant results from other approaches if transverse dynamics or different shock wave profiles are considered.

## Method

The authors construct the five-dimensional metric for two colliding ultrarelativistic shock waves in AdS5 by transforming to Rosen coordinates, then make a power series ansatz in proper time for the metric corrections after the collision. They solve the Einstein equations with negative cosmological constant order by order in proper time using GRTensor, and apply holographic renormalization to extract the boundary energy-momentum tensor for the dual strongly coupled N=4 SYM theory at early times.

## Key numbers

- The energy-momentum tensor components at central rapidity (eta=Y) grow proportional to tau^2 at leading order: T_{tau tau} = mu^2 tau^2 + O(tau^5), where mu = sqrt(2 mu_1 mu_2) and mu_1, mu_2 are the transverse energy densities of the incoming shock waves.
- The small-time expansion converges rapidly up to mu^{1/3} tau ~ 0.4, and breaks down for mu^{1/3} tau >~ 0.7, possibly signaling the onset of thermalization.
- The thermalization time is estimated as tau_therm ~ mu^{-1/3}, with a dimensionless prefactor of order 1 and a lower bound tau_therm > 0.4 mu^{-1/3}.
- The total energy E(t) = sqrt(2) bar-mu is conserved after the collision, where bar-mu = mu_1 = mu_2 for the symmetric case.

## Context

This work extends the program of using gauge-gravity duality to study real-time dynamics in strongly coupled gauge theories, following the approach of Janik and Peschanski (2005) who showed that an expanding thermal system corresponds to a moving black hole in the fifth dimension. The results differ from the classical Yang-Mills treatment of Kovner, McLerran and Weigert (1995) which found boost-invariant solutions, and from Kovchegov (2007) who predicted constant energy density at early times under the assumption of boost-invariance. The paper enables future numerical studies of black hole formation in AdS and suggests that more realistic models including transverse dynamics are needed to determine the thermalization time reliably.
