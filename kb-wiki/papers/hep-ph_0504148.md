# Neutral Color Superconductivity and Pseudo-Goldstone Modes

> Four of the five expected Goldstone modes, which will be eaten up by gauge fields, in neutral two-flavor color superconductor are actually pseudo-Goldstone modes, and their degenerated mass is exactly the magnitude of the color chemical potential, which is introduced to guarantee the color neutrality at moderate baryon density.

## Key claim

In a neutral two-flavor color superconductor, four of the five expected Goldstone modes (which would be eaten by gauge fields) are actually pseudo-Goldstone modes whose degenerated mass equals the magnitude of the color chemical potential \(\mu_8\) introduced to enforce color neutrality; only one mode remains truly massless.

## Method

The authors use the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field approximation for quarks and the random phase approximation (RPA) for mesons and diquarks. They solve the gap equation for the diquark condensate \(\Delta\) together with the color and electric charge neutrality conditions to self-consistently determine \(\mu_8\) and \(\mu_e\) as functions of baryon chemical potential \(\mu_b\) and temperature. The masses of the diquark modes are then extracted from the poles of the corresponding RPA propagators.

## Key numbers

- The four pseudo-Goldstone diquarks (\(D_1, D_2, D_{\bar 1}, D_{\bar 2}\)) have degenerate mass \(M = |\mu_8|\), where \(\mu_8\) is the color chemical potential.
- When both color and electric charge neutralities are enforced at zero temperature, \(\mu_8\) is only a few MeV over a wide range of baryon chemical potential \(\mu_b\).
- The color superconductivity phase starts at \(\mu_b/3 = 330\) MeV (using NJL parameters with cutoff \(\Lambda\) and \(G_D = 3G_S/4\)).
- The heavy diquark mode \(\tilde D_3\) (or \(\tilde D_{\bar 3}\)) has a mass around 1100 MeV in the color superconductivity phase.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Goldstone modes in two-flavor color superconductors (Goldstone-1, 2003) by incorporating the color chemical potential \(\mu_8\) needed for charge neutrality (Neutral-2, 2003). It shows that the color neutrality condition, which explicitly breaks \(SU_C(3)\) to \(SU_C(2)\otimes U_C(1)\), turns four of the five expected Goldstone bosons into pseudo-Goldstone modes with mass \(|\mu_8|\), analogous to how a nonzero current quark mass gives pions a small mass in chiral symmetry breaking (Weinberg, 1973). The result enables more realistic modeling of the low-energy spectrum of neutral color superconductors relevant for compact star interiors.
