> The chiral corrections to the magnetic moments of the spin-$\frac{1}{2}$ doubly charmed baryons are systematically investigated up to next-to-next-to-leading order with heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT). ## Key claim

The chiral corrections to the magnetic moments of spin-1/2 doubly charmed baryons are calculated up to next-to-next-to-leading order analytically, but numerical results are only presented up to next-to-leading order because the six unknown low-energy constants at next-to-next-to-leading order cannot be fixed without experimental data.

## Method

Heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) was used to compute the chiral corrections to the magnetic moments of doubly charmed and bottomed baryons order by order. The low-energy constants at leading order were estimated using a constituent quark model with quark masses m_u=m_d=336 MeV, m_s=540 MeV, m_c=1660 MeV, and the axial coupling was estimated as gÃÉ_A=0.5 from the nucleon axial charge. Six one-loop Feynman diagrams were evaluated, with analytical results carried to next-to-next-to-leading order but numerical results truncated at next-to-leading order.

## Context

This work extends previous quark-model calculations of doubly charmed baryon magnetic moments by Lichtenberg (1976), Silvestre-Brac (1996), and Faessler (2006) by incorporating chiral corrections through heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, which had been missing in most prior approaches. The results are roughly consistent with earlier model calculations including lattice QCD by Can (2013), but the chiral framework provides analytical expressions that can be used for chiral extrapolation of future lattice simulations. The paper enables future experimental measurements of doubly charmed baryon magnetic moments to test the chiral expansion convergence, particularly for the Œû^{++}_{cc} where the leading-order term and loop correction are comparable in size.
