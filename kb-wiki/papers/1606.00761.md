# Exploring QCD dynamics in medium energy $γA$ semiexclusive collisions

> We demonstrate that studies of the semiexclusive large angle photon - nucleus reactions: $γ+ A\to h_1+h_2 +(A-1)^*$ with tagged photon beams of energies $6 ÷10$ GeV which can be performed in Hall D at Thomas Jefferson National Acceleration Facility (TJNAF) would allow to probe several aspects of the QCD dynamics: establish the $t$-range in which transition from soft to hard dynamics occurs, compare the strength of the interaction of various mesons and baryons with nucleons at the energies of few GeV, as well as look for the color transparency effects.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that semiexclusive large-angle photon-nucleus reactions with tagged photon beams of energies 6-10 GeV at TJNAF Hall D would allow to probe the transition from soft to hard QCD dynamics, compare meson-nucleon interaction strengths, and look for color transparency effects, but the actual observability depends on the unresolved photon regime setting in at momentum transfers around -t ~ 2 GeV^2 and on the specific hadron channels studied.

## Method

The authors calculate nuclear transparency T for the reaction γ + A → π⁻ + p + (A-1)* using a semiclassical Glauber-type absorption model with effective cross sections that account for quantum diffusion of small-size configurations. They compare two regimes: resolved photon (RP), where the photon interacts as a hadronic state with σ_γN^eff = σ_in(πN), and unresolved photon (UP), where σ_γN^eff = 0. Color transparency effects are incorporated via the quantum diffusion model of Farrar (1988) with hadron coherence lengths depending on the CT scale M_CT^2 = min(-t, -u) and a mass denominator ΔM^2 varied between 0.7 and 1.1 GeV^2.

## Key numbers

* Photon beam energies considered: 6 to 10 GeV (tagged photon beams at TJNAF Hall D)
* Photon coherence length: l_γ = 2p_lab / m_ρ^2, comparable to or larger than medium-mass nucleus radius (~4 fm) at p_lab ~ 5-20 GeV/c
* Power-law fit T = A^(α-1) at p_lab = 10 GeV/c, t = -2 GeV^2: α = 0.704 ± 0.002 (UP regime), α = 0.509 ± 0.003 (RP regime)
* Power-law fit at p_lab = 10 GeV/c, -u = 2 GeV^2: α = 0.650 ± 0.003 (UP regime), α = 0.457 ± 0.004 (RP regime)
* At p_lab = 12 GeV, transparency is larger by ~50% due to CT effects for both light and heavy nuclear targets
* At fixed beam momentum 10 GeV/c, CT effects strongest at -t ~ 9 GeV^2 (Θ_cm = 90°), with transparency rising by ~40% for both ¹²C and ¹⁹⁷Au
* For heavy targets, RP regime gives transparency a factor of 2-3 smaller than UP regime
* Variation of inelastic meson-nucleon cross section by ~5 mb changes nuclear transparency by ~10%
* Expected transparency for φ-meson production: ~20-30% larger than for pion production
* Expected transparency for η-meson production: ~10-15% larger than for pion production
* Two-meson systems (ππ, K\bar{K}) expected to have transparency ~70-80% of the pionic one

## Context

This work extends the study of color transparency from electron-induced reactions (e.g., A(e,e'π⁺) at TJNAF, Clasie 2007) and hadronic reactions (pA → pp(A-1)*) to photon-induced semiexclusive processes, where the photon can serve as a probe of both resolved and unresolved configurations. The authors build on the quantum diffusion model of Farrar (1988) and the quark counting rules of Brodsky (1973), and they note that previous measurements of η and φ absorption in photon-nucleus interactions (Mertens 2008, Ishikawa 2004) gave cross sections much larger than additive quark model predictions, motivating the proposed comparison of transparencies across different meson channels. The work enables experimental tests at TJNAF Hall D to determine the momentum transfer where hard dynamics dominate, to measure inelastic cross sections of various mesons on nucleons, and to search for color transparency in a new kinematic regime.
