# Asymptotically conformal CFL quark matter within a nonlocal chiral quark model

> We propose a three-flavor nonlocal NJL model of quark matter with the scalar attractive, vector repulsive and diquark pairing interaction channels. The model is treated within the separable approximation to obtain the EoS of cold quark matter. The analysis of the high density asymptotics of the model allows us to conclude about its qualitative agreement with the perturbative QCD. Particularly, a color superconducting CFL state is found to be the ground one at high densities. The conformal limit of speed of sound and dimensionless interaction measure are also shown to be reached from below and above, respectively. The model is applied to modelling NSs within the scenario of early quark deconfinement triggered by the gravitational instability of the NS matter due to the BEC of a spin-color-flavor singlet three-diquark bound state, the light sexaquark, stable against the weak and strong decays. A conservative estimate of the sexaquark mass based on the most up-to-date results from QCD sum rules and a constituent quark model assumes an early quark deconfinement with the onset mass below $1~\rm M_\odot$. The scenario is shown to be consistent with the present observational constraints on the mass-radius relation and tidal deformability of NSs. Given the fact that the proposed model by construction exhibits an asymptotically conformal behavior, we consider the question about approximately conformal quark matter in NSs. We report a non-perturbative energy density range, which is inaccessible in NSs and where the speed of sound and dimensionless interaction measure of the CFL quark matter simultaneously attain the conformal values.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a three-flavor nonlocal Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model that, by construction, exhibits asymptotically conformal behavior and qualitatively agrees with perturbative QCD at high densities, but the conformal limit of speed of sound and dimensionless interaction measure is reached only in a non-perturbative energy density range that is inaccessible in neutron stars.

## Method

The author constructs a nonlocal NJL model with scalar attractive, vector repulsive, and diquark pairing interaction channels, treated within the separable approximation using a Gaussian formfactor. The model is applied to color-flavor-locked (CFL) quark matter under the assumption of degenerate light and strange quark masses (CFLL phase), and the mean-field thermodynamic potential is derived and solved at zero temperature to obtain the equation of state. The model is then used to model neutron stars within a scenario where early quark deconfinement is triggered by gravitational instability due to Bose-Einstein condensation of a sexaquark bound state.

## Key numbers

- Current quark mass fixed at \( m = 3.5 \) MeV, the mean of \( u \)- and \( d \)-quark current masses from the Review of Particle Physics.
- Vacuum effective quark mass at zero momentum is taken from the interval 300–500 MeV based on lattice QCD results.
- Vacuum chiral condensate per flavor from QCD sum rules: \(-(229 \pm 33~\text{MeV})^3\) (Dosch, 1997) and \(-(242 \pm 15~\text{MeV})^3\) (Jamin, 2002).
- Early quark deconfinement onset mass is below \( 1~\rm M_\odot \) based on a conservative estimate of the sexaquark mass.
- Maximum neutron star mass constraints exceed \( 2~\rm M_\odot \) from observations (Miller, 2021; Riley, 2021).

## Context

This work extends previous three-flavor nonlocal NJL models (de Carvalho, 2015; Blaschke, 2022) by including both vector repulsion and diquark pairing interactions, which were previously treated with drawbacks such as ignoring diquark pairing or treating vector repulsion locally. The model enables testing how close to conformal behavior QCD matter is at densities reached in neutron star interiors, and it supports the scenario of early quark deconfinement consistent with observational constraints on mass-radius relation and tidal deformability. The paper also discusses the sexaquark BEC mechanism (Blaschke, 2022; Shahrbaf, 2022) as a possible trigger for early deconfinement.
