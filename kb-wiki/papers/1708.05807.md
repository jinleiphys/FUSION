# Dependencies of Lepton Angular Distribution Coefficients on the Transverse Momentum and Rapidity of$Z$ Bosons Produced in $pp$ Collisions at LHC

> High precision data of lepton angular distributions for $γ^*/Z$ production in $pp$ collisions at the LHC, covering broad ranges of dilepton transverse momenta ($q_T$) and rapidity ($y$), were recently reported. Strong $q_T$ dependencies were observed for several angular distribution coefficients, $A_i$, including $A_0 - A_4$. Significant $y$ dependencies were also found for the coefficients $A_1$, $A_3$ and $A_4$, while $A_0$ and $A_2$ exhibit very weak rapidity dependence. Using an intuitive geometric picture we show that the $q_T$ and $y$ dependencies of the angular distributions coefficients can be well described.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the observed transverse momentum ($q_T$) and rapidity ($y$) dependencies of the lepton angular distribution coefficients $A_0$ through $A_4$ for $\gamma^*/Z$ production at the LHC can be well described by an intuitive geometric picture based on the quark-antiquark annihilation axis, without requiring full perturbative QCD calculations. The authors explicitly note that the Lam-Tung relation violation at large $q_T$ cannot be explained by the Boer-Mulders function (which is negligible at large $q_T$), and instead arises from non-coplanarity between the quark and hadron planes.

## Method

The authors derive general expressions for the angular distribution coefficients $A_0$ through $A_7$ in terms of three quantities: the polar angle $\theta_1$ and azimuthal angle $\phi_1$ of the quark-antiquark axis in the Collins-Soper frame, and the forward-backward asymmetry parameter $a$. They then obtain explicit forms for $\sin\theta_1$, $\cos\theta_1$, $\sin^2\theta_1$, and $\sin 2\theta_1$ for both the $q\bar q$ annihilation process and the $qG$ Compton process at next-to-leading order in $\alpha_s$, using the relation $\tan\beta = q_T/Q$ where $Q$ is the dilepton mass. The calculations are compared with CMS data at $\sqrt s = 8$ TeV for two rapidity bins ($|y| < 1.0$ and $1.0 < |y| < 2.1$), with the fraction of $qG$ versus $q\bar q$ processes determined from a fit to the $A_0$ data.

## Key numbers

- Best fit to $A_0$ data at $|y| < 1.0$ gives a mixture of $58.5 \pm 1.6\%$ $qG$ and $41.5 \pm 1.6\%$ $q\bar q$ processes
- The reduction factor for $A_2$ relative to $A_0$ is 0.77, corresponding to an effective noncoplanarity angle $\phi_1 \approx 20^\circ$
- Reduction factor $r_1$ for $A_1$: $0.0215$ at $|y| < 1.0$, rising to $0.11$ at $1.0 < |y| < 2.1$
- Reduction factor $r_3$ for $A_3$: $0.0113$ at $|y| < 1.0$, rising to $0.0524$ at $1.0 < |y| < 2.1$
- Reduction factor $r_4$ for $A_4$: $0.0181$ at $|y| < 1.0$, rising to $0.0732$ at $1.0 < |y| < 2.1$
- The forward-backward asymmetry parameter $a$ is $0.211$ for $u\bar u \to Z \to l^-l^+$ and $0.299$ for $d\bar d \to Z \to l^-l^+$, using $\sin^2\theta_W = 0.2315$
- The LHC data cover $0 < q_T < 600$ GeV and $0 < |y| < 3.5$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous geometric picture (Peng 2016) which focused on the $\lambda$ and $\nu$ parameters and the Lam-Tung relation, to now include all angular distribution coefficients $A_0$ through $A_4$ and their rapidity dependencies. The paper addresses the violation of the Lam-Tung relation observed in CMS data (2016) and ATLAS data (2016) at large $q_T$, which cannot be explained by the Boer-Mulders function mechanism that successfully described the violation in fixed-target pion-induced Drell-Yan experiments (Falciano 1986, Conway 1989). The results enable a simple geometric understanding of the angular distributions without requiring full perturbative QCD calculations, and suggest that higher-order QCD processes introduce noncoplanarity between the quark and hadron planes characterized by $\phi_1 \approx 20^\circ$.
