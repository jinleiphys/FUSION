# Axial vector form factor of nucleons in a light-cone diquark model

> The nucleon axial vector form factor is investigated in a light-cone quark spectator diquark model, in which Melosh rotations are applied to both the quark and vector diquark. It is found that this model gives a very good description of available experimental data and the results have very little dependence on the parameters of the model. The relation between the nucleon axial constant and the anomalous magnetic moment of nucleons is also discussed.

## Key claim

The light-cone quark spectator diquark model, with Melosh rotations applied to both the quark and vector diquark, provides a very good description of the nucleon axial vector form factor with very little dependence on the model parameters, in contrast to the case of nucleon electromagnetic form factors.

## Method

The nucleon axial vector form factor \(G_A(Q^2)\) was calculated using the light-cone Fock expansion, where the matrix element of the plus component of the axial vector current is expressed as an overlap of light-cone wave functions with the same number of Fock constituents. Melosh rotations were applied to both the quark and the vector diquark to transform from instant-form to light-cone spinors, and the momentum-space wave functions were taken as harmonic oscillator wave functions following the Brodsky-Huang-Lepage (BHL) prescription. The calculation used three different sets of parameters to test dependence on the scalar-vector diquark mass difference.

## Key numbers

- The axial constant \(g_A = 1.2670(35)\) from the Particle Data Group (2000) was used as the experimental reference value.
- The nonrelativistic SU(6) result for the axial constant is \(g_A^{NR} = 5/3\), and the physical value is reduced by 25% due to relativistic effects.
- The axial radius \(\langle r^2_A \rangle^{1/2}\) was calculated from the derivative of \(G_A(Q^2)\) at \(Q^2=0\) using \(r^2_A = -6 \frac{1}{G_A(0)} \frac{d G_A(Q^2)}{d Q^2} \big|_{Q^2=0}\).
- The experimental data for \(G_A(Q^2)\) were assumed to follow the dipole form \(G_A(Q^2) = g_A / (1 + Q^2/M_A^2)^2\).

## Context

This work extends the light-cone quark spectator diquark model, previously applied to the nucleon spin problem (Ma, 1991; Ma, 1996; Ma, 1998) and to nucleon electromagnetic form factors (Ma, 2002), into the weak interaction domain for the axial vector form factor. The results agree well with experimental data and maintain the wavefunction-independent relations between the nucleon axial coupling \(g_A\) and the nucleon magnetic moments found in earlier light-cone quark models (Brodsky, 1994; Chung, 1991). The model enables a unified description of both elastic and inelastic electromagnetic and weak nucleon data within a single relativistic framework.
