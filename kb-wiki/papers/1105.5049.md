# Search for the QCD Critical Point: Higher Moments of Net-proton Multiplicity Distributions

> Higher moments of event-by-event net-proton multiplicity distributions have been applied to search for the QCD critical point. Model results are used to provide a baseline for this search. The measured moment products, $κσ^2$ and $Sσ$ of net-proton distributions, which are directly connected to the thermodynamical baryon number susceptibility ratio in Lattice QCD and Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model, are compared to the transport and thermal model results. We argue that a non-monotonic dependence of $κσ^2$ and $S σ$ as a function of beam energy can be used to search for the QCD critical point.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a non-monotonic dependence of the moment products \(\kappa\sigma^2\) and \(S\sigma\) of net-proton multiplicity distributions as a function of beam energy can be used to search for the QCD critical point, but it notes that both the model results and the STAR data shown do not yet exhibit such non-monotonic behavior, so they serve only as a baseline for the RHIC beam energy scan.

## Method

The authors calculated the mean, variance, skewness, and kurtosis of event-by-event net-proton multiplicity distributions from published STAR Au+Au data at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.6, 62.4, 200\) GeV and from several non-critical-point models: AMPT (v2.11), HIJING (v1.35), UrQMD (v2.3), and Therminator (v1.0). The kinetic coverage for protons and anti-protons was \(0.4 < p_T < 0.8\) GeV/c and \(|y| < 0.5\). The moment products \(S\sigma\) and \(\kappa\sigma^2\) were compared across centrality and collision energy to the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model and lattice QCD predictions.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, the mean (\(M\)) and variance (\(\sigma\)) of net-proton distributions increase monotonically with number of participants (\(N_{part}\)), while skewness (\(S\)) and kurtosis (\(\kappa\)) decrease monotonically with \(N_{part}\).
- In the AMPT string melting model with parton cross section \(\sigma_{pp} = 10\) mb, \(\kappa\sigma^2\) shows almost no centrality dependence and values around unity for all energies from \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) to 200 GeV.
- The \(S\sigma\) from the same AMPT model shows a strong energy dependence, decreasing with increasing energy, and almost no centrality dependence at high energies.
- The STAR data for most central collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.6, 62.4, 200\) GeV are in good agreement with the HRG model prediction (\(\kappa_B \sigma_B^2 = 1\), \(S_B \sigma_B = \tanh(\mu_B/T)\)) and with the Therminator thermal model.

## Context

This work extends the search for the QCD critical point by applying higher moments of net-proton distributions, which are directly connected to baryon number susceptibilities from lattice QCD and the HRG model, as proposed by Stephanov (2009) and Asakawa (2009). The study uses STAR data from Au+Au collisions and compares them to transport models (AMPT, HIJING, UrQMD) and a thermal model (Therminator) to establish a non-critical baseline. The results enable the RHIC beam energy scan program to identify non-monotonic deviations that would signal the critical point, as suggested by lattice QCD calculations from Gupta (2010).
