# Towards a Quantum Information Theory of Hadronization: Dihadron Fragmentation and Neutral Polarization in Heavy Baryons

> We pioneer the application of quantum information theory to experimentally distinguish between classes of hadronization models. We adapt the CHSH inequality to the fragmentation of a single parton to hadron pairs, a violation of which would rule out classical dynamics of hadronization altogether. Furthermore, we apply and extend the theory of quantum contextuality and local quantum systems to the neutral polarization of a single spin-1 hadronic system, namely the light constituents of excited Sigma baryons $Σ^{*}_{c,b}$ formed in the fragmentation of heavy quarks.

## Key claim
The paper adapts quantum information theory tools, specifically the CHSH inequality and Kochen-Specker contextuality, to distinguish classical from quantum hadronization models, but the tests do not probe quantum nonlocality of Nature; instead they use the same mathematics to test whether the blackbox Hamiltonian of hadronization admits a classical description, with the crucial caveat that the "locality" assumption is a property checkable by inspecting a given model's dynamical rules rather than a fundamental physical principle.

## Method
The authors adapt the CHSH inequality to dihadron fragmentation functions (DiFFs) for spin-1/2 baryon pairs produced from a single parton, deriving a bound on the spin correlation coefficients \(\tilde{D}_{LL}\) and \(\tilde{D}_{TT}\) that classical hidden-variable models must satisfy. They also apply Klyachko-Can-Binicioglu-Shumovsky (KCBS) contextuality inequalities to the neutral polarization of spin-1 light constituents in excited \(\Sigma^*_{c,b}\) baryons formed during heavy-quark fragmentation, characterized by the Falk-Peskin parameter \(w_1\), and extend the analysis to transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) fragmentation where azimuthal symmetry is broken.

## Key numbers
- CHSH inequality bound for classical hadronization models: \(\tilde{D}_{TT}^2 + \max\{\tilde{D}_{LL}^2, \tilde{D}_{TT}^2\} \leq 1\) (Equation labeled chsh_diff)
- KCBS bound on the Falk-Peskin parameter: \(w_1 \geq w_1^\bigstar \equiv \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{2\sqrt{5}} \approx 0.276393\) (Equation labeled w1_kcbs_bound)
- DELPHI measurement of \(w_1\) for \(\Sigma_b^{*\pm}\): \(w_1^\mathrm{DELPHI} = -0.36 \pm 0.30_\mathrm{stat} \pm 0.30_\mathrm{syst}\), giving a 95% CL limit \(w_1 \leq 0.25\) after imposing positivity
- CLEO measurement of \(w_1\) for \(\Sigma_c^{*++}\) and \(\Sigma_c^{*0}\): \(w_1^\mathrm{CLEO} = 0.71 \pm 0.13\), close to the isotropic limit \(w_1 = 2/3\)

## Context
This work extends the application of quantum information theory to particle physics, which has previously focused on probing quantum foundations at high energy scales or in exclusive hadronic processes (e.g., Afik 2022, ATLAS 2023, Fabbrichesi 2024), or on using hadronization as an analyzer for perturbative density matrices (Aidala 2021, Cheng 2025). The authors distinguish their proposal by directly addressing the nature of hadronization itself, appealing to effective field theory and factorization theorems to isolate nonperturbative behavior from perturbative scattering and hadronic decays. The results enable experimental tests that could rule out entire classes of classical hadronization models (such as those implemented in Monte Carlo generators like Pythia or Herwig) and impose practical constraints on future hadronization modeling, while also suggesting the possibility of observing a quantum-to-classical transition in TMD measurements as transverse momentum increases.
