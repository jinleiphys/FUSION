# Pairing correlations across the superfluid phase transition in the unitary Fermi gas

> In the two-component Fermi gas with a contact interaction, a pseudogap regime can exist at temperatures between the superfluid critical temperature $T_c$ and a temperature $T^* > T_c$. This regime is characterized by pairing correlations without superfluidity. However, in the unitary limit of infinite scattering length, the existence of this regime is still debated. To help address this, we have applied finite-temperature auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFMC) to study the thermodynamics of the superfluid phase transition and signatures of the pseudogap in the spin-balanced homogeneous unitary Fermi gas. We present results at finite filling factor $ν\simeq 0.06$ for the condensate fraction, an energy-staggering pairing gap, the spin susceptibility, and the heat capacity, and compare them to experimental data when available. In contrast to previous AFMC simulations, our model space consists of the complete first Brillouin zone of the lattice, and our calculations are performed in the canonical ensemble of fixed particle number. The canonical ensemble AFMC framework enables the calculation of a model-independent gap, providing direct information on pairing correlations without the need for numerical analytic continuation. We use finite-size scaling to estimate $T_c$ at the corresponding filling factor. We find that the energy-staggering pairing gap vanishes above $T_c$, showing no pseudogap effects, and that the spin susceptibility shows a substantially reduced signature of a spin gap compared to previously reported AFMC simulations.

## Key claim

Using finite-temperature auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFMC) simulations that include the complete first Brillouin zone of the lattice and fix particle number in the canonical ensemble, the authors find that the energy-staggering pairing gap vanishes above the superfluid critical temperature \(T_c\), showing no pseudogap effects at the finite filling factor \(\nu \simeq 0.06\) studied, in contrast to previous AFMC simulations that used a spherical momentum cutoff.

## Method

The authors performed finite-temperature auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo (AFMC) simulations of the spin-balanced homogeneous unitary Fermi gas on cubic lattices of sizes \(7^3\) to \(13^3\) with particle numbers \(N=20\) to \(130\), maintaining a constant filling factor \(\nu \simeq 0.06\). Unlike previous AFMC simulations, the single-particle model space included the complete first Brillouin zone of the lattice rather than a spherical cutoff, and calculations were performed in the canonical ensemble of fixed particle number using a discrete Fourier transform projection. The condensate fraction, energy-staggering pairing gap, spin susceptibility, and heat capacity were computed, with finite-size scaling used to estimate the critical temperature.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature at filling factor \(\nu \simeq 0.06\): \(T_c \simeq 0.130(15) T_F\), determined from finite-size scaling of the condensate fraction.
- Energy-staggering pairing gap \(\Delta_E\) vanishes above \(T_c \simeq 0.130(15) T_F\), showing no pseudogap signature.
- Spin susceptibility is suppressed for \(T \lesssim 0.17\,T_F\) on the \(13^3\) lattice with \(N=130\) particles, giving an upper bound of \(T^* \lesssim 0.17\,T_F\) for the spin-gap temperature at \(\nu \simeq 0.06\).
- Heat capacity peak position for the \(11^3\), \(N=80\) system at \(T \simeq 0.135(10) T_F\), consistent with the estimated \(T_c\).
- Ratio of effective range to Fermi wavelength: \(k_{\rm F} r_e \simeq 0.41\).
- Experimental critical temperature for comparison: \(T_c = 0.167(13) T_F\) from Ku et al. (2012).

## Context

This work addresses the debated existence of a pseudogap regime in the unitary Fermi gas, where pairing correlations exist without superfluidity above \(T_c\). The results contradict previous AFMC simulations by Magierski et al. (2009, 2011) and Wlazlowski et al. (2013) that found pseudogap signatures using a spherical momentum cutoff, which the authors show does not reproduce the unitary Fermi gas even in the zero-filling limit. The findings agree with Luttinger-Ward theory by Enss (2012) and with experiments by Ku et al. (2012) that saw no pseudogap signatures, while the heat capacity enhancement above \(T_c\) confirms predictions by van Wyk et al. (2016) and Perali et al. (2011). Future work requires continuum extrapolation to \(\nu \to 0\) (equivalently \(k_F r_e \to 0\)) to reach the thermodynamic limit.
