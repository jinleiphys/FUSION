# Schiff moment of the Mercury nucleus and the proton dipole moment

> We calculated the contribution of internal nucleon electric dipole moments to the Schiff moment of $^{199}$Hg. The contribution of the proton electric dipole moment was obtained via core polarization effects that were treated in the framework of random phase approximation with effective residual forces. We derived a new upper bound $|d_p|< 5.4\times 10^{-24} e\cdot$cm of the proton electric dipole moment.

## Key claim

The paper derives a new upper bound on the proton electric dipole moment, \(|d_p| < 5.4 \times 10^{-24} \, e\cdot\text{cm}\), from the atomic EDM limit of \(^{199}\)Hg, but this bound carries a conservative 30% theoretical uncertainty in the core-polarization calculation of the Schiff moment coefficient \(s_p\).

## Method

The authors calculated the contribution of internal nucleon electric dipole moments to the Schiff moment of \(^{199}\)Hg using the random phase approximation (RPA) with a Landau-Migdal residual interaction. The single-particle basis was obtained from a partially self-consistent Woods-Saxon mean-field potential, and the renormalized Schiff moment operator was solved via integral equations in coordinate space using Green functions. The proton EDM contribution arises through core polarization effects mediated by the proton-neutron residual interaction, which is absent in the independent-particle model.

## Key numbers

- Upper bound on the proton EDM: \(|d_p| < 5.4 \times 10^{-24} \, e\cdot\text{cm}\), derived from the atomic EDM limit of \(^{199}\)Hg.
- Upper bound on the Schiff moment of \(^{199}\)Hg: \(|S(^{199}\text{Hg})| < 0.75 \times 10^{-11} \, e\,\text{fm}^3\), obtained from the experimental atomic EDM limit \(d(^{199}\text{Hg}) < 2.1 \times 10^{-28} \, e\cdot\text{cm}\).
- Neutron EDM constraint from the same analysis: \(|d_n| < 4.0 \times 10^{-25} \, e\cdot\text{cm}\), which is weaker than the existing experimental limit \(d_n < 0.63 \times 10^{-25} \, e\cdot\text{cm}\).
- Schiff moment coefficients: \(s_p = 0.20 \pm 0.02 \, \text{fm}^2\) (from parameter variation) and \(s_n = 1.895 \pm 0.035 \, \text{fm}^2\); with a conservative 30% uncertainty, \(s_p = 0.2 \pm 0.06 \, \text{fm}^2\).
- The atomic EDM of \(^{199}\)Hg is related to the Schiff moment by \(d = -2.8 \times 10^{-17} \, S \, [e\,\text{fm}^3]\) (from Ref. dfgk).

## Context

This work extends earlier simplified calculations of nuclear Schiff moments by Flambaum et al. (1984) and by Fittipaldi and Gattobigio (2002), which did not include residual interactions between valence nucleons and the core. It also complements a contemporaneous microscopic study of the \(^{199}\)Hg Schiff moment by Dmitriev and Sen'kov (2003) that focused on the parity- and time-reversal-violating nucleon-nucleon interaction contribution. The result enables a new, more stringent constraint on the proton EDM, which is an order of magnitude below the existing direct experimental limit from the Particle Data Group (2002).
