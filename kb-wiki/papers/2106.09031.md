# An effective field theory of the Delta-resonance

> We present an effective field theory of the $Δ$-resonance as an interacting Weinberg's $(3/2,0)\oplus (0,3/2)$ field in the multi-spinor formalism. We derive its interactions with nucleons $N$, pions $π$ and photons $γ$, and compute the $Δ$-resonance cross-sections in pion-nucleon scattering and pion photo-production. The theory contains only the physical spin-3/2 degrees of freedom. Thus, it is intrinsically consistent at the Hamiltonian level and, unlike the commonly used Rarita-Schwinger framework, does not require any additional ad hoc manipulation of couplings or propagators. The symmetries of hadronic physics select a unique operator for each coupling $NπΔ$ and $γπΔ$. The proposed framework can be extended to also describe other higher-spin hadronic resonances.

## Key claim

The paper presents an effective field theory of the Delta-resonance using Weinberg's (3/2,0)⊕(0,3/2) representation in the multi-spinor formalism, which contains only physical spin-3/2 degrees of freedom and is intrinsically consistent at the Hamiltonian level, unlike the commonly used Rarita-Schwinger framework that requires ad hoc manipulation of couplings or propagators.

## Method

The authors construct a Lorentz-invariant effective field theory where the Delta-resonance is described as a field in the (3/2,0)⊕(0,3/2) representation using the multi-spinor formalism, with an internal SU(2)×U(1)_Y symmetry corresponding to nuclear isospin and hypercharge. They derive the lowest-dimensional interaction operators (dimension-7) for Delta couplings to nucleons, pions, and photons, applying parity invariance to reduce the number of independent coupling constants. The cross-sections for pion-nucleon scattering and pion photo-production mediated by the Delta-resonance are then computed at tree level and compared to results from the consistent Rarita-Schwinger interactions of Pascalutsa (1998-2000).

## Key numbers

* The Delta-resonance mass is close to the mass of the nucleon, and it is the lightest known particle with spin-3/2.
* The pion decay constant is f_pi ~ 100 MeV, and the chiral symmetry breaking scale is Lambda ~ 4 pi f_pi ~ 1 GeV.
* The lowest-dimensional interaction operators containing at least one Delta-field have dimension-7.
* The Delta-field in the multi-spinor formalism has mass dimension 5/2, in contrast to the Rarita-Schwinger framework where its mass dimension is 3/2.
* The pion-nucleon scattering and gamma-nucleon to pion-nucleon scattering are controlled by two complex parameters c_pi and c_gamma.

## Context

This work extends the multi-spinor effective field theory formalism previously applied to generic higher-spin dark matter (Criado, 2020) and higher-spin particles at colliders (Criado, 2021) to the specific case of the Delta-resonance. It provides an alternative to the Rarita-Schwinger framework (Rarita and Schwinger, 1941) which has known inconsistencies in interacting theories (Johnson and Sudarshan, 1960; Velo and Zwanziger, 1970) and to the ad hoc consistent interaction methods of Pascalutsa (1998-2000). The framework can be extended to describe other higher-spin hadronic resonances and enables systematic computations of Delta-resonance processes using simple Feynman rules without additional manipulation of couplings or propagators.
