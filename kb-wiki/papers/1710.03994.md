# Translation invariance and antisymmetry in the theory of the nucleon optical model

> The first step in any formalism that aims to connect a many-nucleon theory of nucleon-nucleus scattering and the concept of an optical model potential in the sense pioneered by Feshbach is to explain what is meant by the optical model wavefunction. By definition this is a function of a single space coordinate plus a set of single nucleon internal variables. This article gives a critique of the definitio as it is frequently expressed in 2nd Quantisation language and suggest a new definition which is more consistent with the requirements of antisymmetry and translational invariance. A modification of the time-dependent Green's function formalism is suggested.

# Translation invariance and antisymmetry in the theory of the nucleon optical model

## Key claim
The standard definition of the optical model wavefunction as the projection of the exact antisymmetrised many-body scattering wavefunction onto the target ground state, expressed in second-quantisation notation as $\xi_{E^+}(\mathbf{r}) = \langle \Psi_0 | \psi(\mathbf{r}) | \Psi_{E^+} \rangle$, is shown to be independent of the spatial coordinate $\mathbf{r}$ when the many-nucleon states have definite zero total momentum, and therefore cannot be an acceptable definition. A modified definition is proposed that uses Jacobi coordinates to express the overlap in terms of the distance of the extra nucleon from the centre-of-mass of the target, which is not generally independent of $\mathbf{r}$.

## Method
The paper demonstrates analytically, using both configuration-space integrals and Fock-space commutator algebra, that the standard overlap $\langle \Psi_0 | \psi(\mathbf{r}) | \Psi_{E^+} \rangle$ yields a result independent of $\mathbf{r}$ when $\Psi_0$ and $\Psi_{E^+}$ are zero-momentum eigenstates of a translationally invariant Hamiltonian. A new definition is constructed by expressing the $A$-nucleon and $(A+1)$-nucleon wavefunctions in terms of Jacobi coordinates and the centre-of-mass coordinate, leading to a Fock-space matrix element $\langle \langle \Psi(0, \mathbf{x}=0) | \psi(\mathbf{r}) | \Psi_1 \rangle \rangle$ where the bra state has the target centre-of-mass fixed at the origin. A source equation is derived for this overlap, and a time-dependent Green's function formalism is developed to express the optical model wavefunction in terms of a one-body Green's function that differs from the standard groundstate-groundstate Green's function.

## Key numbers
- The reduced mass for nucleon-$A$ motion is $\mu_{mA} = \frac{A}{A+1} m$, where $m$ is the nucleon mass.
- The incident nucleon kinetic energy in the centre-of-mass system is $\epsilon_{\mathbf{k}_1} = \frac{\hbar^2 k_1^2}{2m}$, with $k_1$ the incident nucleon momentum.
- The target centre-of-mass kinetic energy is $E_{1A} = \frac{\hbar^2 k_1^2}{2Am}$.
- The scattering state eigenvalue is $E_1 = E_0 + \frac{\hbar^2 k_1^2}{2\mu_{mA}}$, where $E_0$ is the $A$-nucleon ground state intrinsic energy.

## Context
This work critiques the standard definition of the optical model wavefunction going back to Feshbach (1958) and used by Bell and Squires, Capuzzi and Mahaux (1995), and more recent work by Rotureau (2016) and Idini, Barbieri, and Navratil (2016), all of which employ the problematic overlap $\langle \Psi_0 | \psi(\mathbf{r}) | \Psi_{E^+} \rangle$. The paper extends the translationally invariant treatment of overlap functions familiar from bound-state studies of $(d,p)$ and $(p,d)$ reactions, as developed by Timofeyuk (2011, 2014), to the scattering context. The new definition enables a connection to self-consistent Green's function methods for medium-mass targets, but the author notes that further work is needed to assess the quantitative consequences of the translation-invariance violation in existing approaches.
