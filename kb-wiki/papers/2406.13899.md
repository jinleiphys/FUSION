# Faddeev calculations of low-energy $Λ$-deuteron scattering and momentum correlation function

> Faddeev calculations of low-energy $Λ$-deuteron elastic scattering are performed up to $E_{cm}=20$ MeV across the deuteron threshold. Phase shifts of the $s$-wave with $J=1/2$ and $J=3/2$ are calculated using strangeness $S=-1$ hyperon-nucleon interactions in chiral effective field theory NLO13 and NLO19 parametrized by the J{ü}lich-Bonn group. Effective range parameters, such as a scattering length and an effective range, are determined through the calculated phase shifts. $Λ$-deuteron momentum correlation functions are evaluated using the $Λ$-deuteron relative wave function constructed from half-off-shell $t$-matrices. They are compared with those evaluated using an approximate formula.

# Faddeev calculations of low-energy Λ-deuteron scattering and momentum correlation function

## Key claim

The Faddeev calculations of Λ-deuteron elastic scattering using chiral NLO13 and NLO19 YN interactions produce phase shifts that are almost indistinguishable for the J=1/2 channel, but show noticeable differences in the J=3/2 channel, indicating that the spin-triplet parts of the two interactions are not identical despite their nearly identical ΛN ³S₁ phase shifts. The calculated Λd correlation functions agree well with the approximate Lednický-Lyuboshitz formula except for small source radii (R=1.2 fm), where a slight deviation is observed.

## Method

The authors solved Faddeev equations for Λ-deuteron elastic scattering in momentum space using partial wave representation, restricting orbital angular momenta ℓ_{p_j} and λ_{q_j} to zero for both Jacobi coordinate sets. The calculations employed chiral N⁴LO⁺ NN interactions with 550 MeV cutoff and two versions of chiral NLO S=-1 YN interactions (NLO13 and NLO19) from the Jülich-Bonn group, with ΛN-ΣN coupling included in the ΛN t-matrices but no three-body forces and no explicit ΣNN three-body states. Half-off-shell t-matrices from the Faddeev solutions were used to construct Λd relative wave functions in coordinate space, from which momentum correlation functions were evaluated and compared with the Lednický-Lyuboshitz approximate formula using the extracted effective range parameters.

## Key numbers

- Phase shifts calculated up to E_cm = 20 MeV across the deuteron breakup threshold
- J=1/2 scattering length: a_s = 23.4 fm (NLO13), 24.3 fm (NLO19); effective range: r_e = 2.77 fm (NLO13), 2.80 fm (NLO19)
- J=3/2 scattering length: a_s = -11.2 fm (NLO13), -18.7 fm (NLO19); effective range: r_e = 3.25 fm (NLO13), 2.87 fm (NLO19)
- Virtual state pole energy in J=3/2: -0.17 MeV (NLO13), -0.072 MeV (NLO19)
- Hypertriton separation energy from Bethe formula using effective range parameters: 57.9 keV (NLO13), 53.3 keV (NLO19); from full Faddeev bound-state calculations: 79 keV (NLO13), 87 keV (NLO19)
- Deuteron breakup threshold momentum: k ~ 0.28 fm⁻¹
- Correlation functions evaluated for source range parameters R = 1.2, 2.5, and 5 fm

## Context

This work extends prior Faddeev calculations of the hypertriton bound state by Kohno, Kamada, and Miyagawa (2022) to the scattering regime, using the same chiral YN interactions. It provides the first explicit Faddeev calculation of Λd elastic scattering with modern chiral YN interactions, complementing earlier effective range parameter estimates from bound-state Faddeev calculations by Garcilazo (1975, 1976) and studies within pionless effective field theory by Hammer and Higa (2002, 2019). The calculated correlation functions can be compared with preliminary STAR collaboration data from 3 GeV Au+Au collisions reported by Hu (2024), and the method can be extended to Ξd scattering to study ΞN interactions.
