# Intriguing properties of multiplicity distributions

> Multiplicity distributions exhibit, after closer inspection, peculiarly enhanced void probability and oscillatory behavior of the modified combinants. We discuss the possible sources of these oscillations and their impact on our understanding of the multiparticle production mechanism. Theoretical understanding of both phenomena within the class of compound distributions is presented.

## Key claim
Multiplicity distributions in high-energy hadron collisions exhibit two overlooked features, enhanced void probability (P(0) > P(1)) and oscillatory modified combinants C_j, which cannot be reproduced by standard single or multi-component Negative Binomial Distributions, but can be simultaneously described by compound distributions based on a Binomial Distribution compounded with a Negative Binomial Distribution.

## Method
The authors extract modified combinants C_j from experimental charged-particle multiplicity distributions P(N) using a recurrence relation that connects all multiplicities. They analyze non-single diffractive (NSD) data from CMS and ALICE at LHC energies (0.2–8 TeV) and compare the observed C_j oscillations and void probabilities against predictions from single NBD, multi-component NBD, and compound Binomial–Negative Binomial distributions (CBD). The CBD is constructed by taking a Binomial Distribution for the number of clusters and a Negative Binomial Distribution for their decay products.

## Key numbers
- CMS data at √s = 7 TeV, |η| < 2: C_j oscillations show a period roughly equal to 16, which gradually disappears with increasing rank j.
- For a single NBD fit to CMS data at 7 TeV: ⟨N⟩ = 25.5, k = 1.45; the corresponding C_j are all positive with no oscillations.
- For a 2-component CBD fit to ALICE data at 7 TeV, |η| < 2: parameters include w₁ = 0.30, p₁ = 0.75, K₁ = 3, k₁ = 3.8, m₁ = 4.75; w₂ = 0.70, p₂ = 0.70, K₂ = 3, k₂ = 1.30, m₂ = 15.9.
- For ALICE data at 7 TeV, |η| < 2: the second component satisfies K₂ p₂ m₂ ≈ 32 for K₂ values of 3, 6, 9, 12, 20.
- Fisher g-statistic test on ALICE data at 7 TeV, |η| < 3: probability that the observed spectral peak in C_j is statistically insignificant is 10⁻¹⁶.

## Context
This work extends earlier observations of enhanced void probability (EVP, Void) and the authors' own introduction of modified combinants (JPG, IJMPA). It contradicts the common assumption that multi-component NBD fits (e.g., 2-NBD by GU, PG; 3-NBD by Z) fully describe multiplicity distributions, showing they fail at small N and for C_j oscillations. The compound distribution approach enables simultaneous description of P(N), void probability, and C_j oscillations, and suggests that the Binomial Distribution component is essential for oscillatory behavior while the Negative Binomial component provides damping. The results open the possibility of using C_j as a new experimental observable to discriminate between production models, including fractional NBD (Hegyi, 1997) and Poisson transform approaches.
