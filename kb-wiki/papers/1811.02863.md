# Doubly-heavy baryons, tetraquarks, and related topics

> We review the physics of doubly-heavy baryons $QQq$ and tetraquarks $QQ\bar q\bar q$. For the latter, the stability is reached for large enough mass ratio $M/m$, even when spin forces and color mixing are neglected. It is thus customarily claimed that $bb\bar q\bar q$ in its ground state cannot decay into $b\bar q+b\bar q$. In some model, $cc\bar u\bar d$ is shown to be stable if color mixing and spin effects are properly taken into account. It is conjectured that some $bc\bar q\bar q'$ benefits from favorable adjustments of the gluon tubes in the confinement regime. Some recent studies of pentaquarks and hexaquarks are also summarized.

# Doubly-heavy baryons, tetraquarks, and related topics

## Key claim
The stability of tetraquarks $QQ\bar q\bar q$ depends on the heavy-to-light mass ratio $M/m$, with $bb\bar q\bar q$ being stable in most models when spin forces and color mixing are neglected, but $cc\bar u\bar d$ becomes stable only if color mixing and spin effects are properly included. For $bc\bar q\bar q'$ states, additional binding may arise from favorable adjustments of gluon-tube configurations in the confinement regime.

## Method
The authors review constituent quark model calculations for doubly-heavy baryons $QQq$ and tetraquarks $QQ\bar q\bar q$, analyzing the four-body problem via Born-Oppenheimer potentials, color-mixing between $\bar 33$ and $6\bar 6$ configurations, and chromomagnetic spin-spin interactions. They also examine string confinement topologies (flip-flop and connected double-Y) for tetraquarks, and apply variational methods and real-scaling techniques to pentaquark and hexaquark systems.

## Key numbers
- For $bb\bar q\bar q$ in its ground state: the system cannot decay into $b\bar q+b\bar q$ when spin forces and color mixing are neglected (stability from mass ratio alone).
- For $cc\bar u\bar d$: stability is achieved in a specific quark model when chromoelectric and chromomagnetic effects are properly combined (Janc and Rosina, 2004).
- For the $\bar Q qqqq$ pentaquark with infinitely heavy $Q$ and $qqqq = uuds, ddus, ssud$: bound by about 150 MeV below the $\bar Q q+qqq$ threshold under SU(3)$_F$ symmetry assumptions.
- For the all-heavy tetraquark $QQ\bar Q\bar Q$: found unstable in standard quark models, in contrast to the positronium molecule Ps$_2$ which is bound.

## Context
This review extends the long history of doubly-heavy hadron studies, from the early predictions of double-charm baryons by Gaillard, Lee, and Rosner (1974) and the first tetraquark calculations by Ader et al. (1981) and Zouzou et al. (1986). It builds on the demonstration of $cc\bar u\bar d$ stability by Janc and Rosina (2004) and the subsequent confirmation by Barnea et al. (2006). The work enables future searches for $bc\bar q\bar q'$ tetraquarks and new pentaquark states with different quantum numbers, and highlights the need for rigorous few-body methods such as real scaling (Hiyama et al., 2018) to distinguish genuine resonances from continuum artifacts.
