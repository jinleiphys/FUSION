# Quantifying neutron-proton interactions in $N=51$ isotones: from NEEC candidate $^{93}$Mo to $^{99}$Cd

> We present a shell-model analysis of $N=51$ isotones, $^{93}$Mo, $^{95}$Ru, $^{97}$Pd, and $^{99}$Cd, to quantify the role of neutron-proton interactions in shaping the location and half-life of isomeric states. The study is motivated by the anomalous behavior of the ${21/2}^+$ isomeric state in $^{93}$Mo, a prominent candidate for nuclear excitation by electron capture (NEEC), which misses an $E2$ decay branch due to a higher-lying ${17/2}^+$ state and instead proceeds via a long-lived $E4$ isomeric transition. Employing a consistent configuration space and empirically derived effective interaction, we extract and compare the proton-proton and neutron-proton matrix elements for the four $N=51$ isotones. Our results show a distinct dominance of the neutron-proton interaction in $^{93}$Mo, in contrast to its neighbors--$^{95}$Ru, $^{97}$Pd, and $^{99}$Cd--where no analogous isomeric behavior emerges due to structural evolution. These findings reveal that the favorable structure for NEEC in $^{93}$Mo stems from subtle interaction systematics that do not persist across the chain. We find that the $E2$ strength of the key NEEC transition is reduced by 40\% compared to the previously estimated value. The analysis provides microscopic insights into the origin of long-lived isomerism in medium-mass nuclei and outlines a framework for identifying future candidates in other mass regions for exploiting the potential energy storage capacities of isomeric states.

## Key claim

The anomalous inversion of the ${21/2}^+$ and ${17/2}^+$ states in $^{93}$Mo, which makes it a candidate for nuclear excitation by electron capture (NEEC), arises from a subtle dominance of the neutron-proton interaction that does not persist in the heavier $N=51$ isotones $^{95}$Ru, $^{97}$Pd, and $^{99}$Cd, and the $E2$ strength of the key NEEC transition is reduced by 40% compared to the previously estimated value.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for the $N=51$ isotones $^{93}$Mo, $^{95}$Ru, $^{97}$Pd, and $^{99}$Cd using a restricted $\nu 1d_{5/2} \otimes \pi 0g_{9/2}^n$ configuration space, where $n$ is the number of valence protons relative to a $Z=40$ closed shell. Empirically derived two-body matrix elements for the proton-proton interaction were taken from $^{92}$Mo and $^{98}$Cd spectra, and the neutron-proton interaction was fixed from the $^{92}$Nb spectrum, with analytic expressions for matrix elements obtained via coefficients of fractional parentage and numerical diagonalization for larger proton numbers. Reduced $E2$ transition probabilities were calculated using effective charges $e_\pi = 1.32$ and three choices of neutron effective charge ($e_\nu = 1.18$, $1.48$, and $1.78$), and results were compared to large-scale shell-model calculations using the GLEKPN interaction.

## Key numbers

- The energy difference between the ${17/2}^+$ and ${21/2}^+$ states in $^{93}$Mo is calculated as 16 keV (experimental gap is 5 keV), with the ${21/2}^+$ isomer lying below the ${17/2}^+$ state.
- The $B(E2; {17/2}^+ \rightarrow {21/2}^+)$ value in $^{93}$Mo is calculated as 2.02 $e^2b^4$ (with $e_\nu=1.18$), 2.40 $e^2b^4$ (with $e_\nu=1.78$), and 2.21 $e^2b^4$ (with $e_\nu=1.48$), compared to the literature value of 3.5 $e^2b^4$ from Hasegawa et al. (2011).
- The $B(E4; {21/2}^+ \rightarrow {13/2}^+)$ in $^{93}$Mo is calculated as 1.25, 1.37, and 1.50 W.u. for $e_\nu = 1.18$, 1.48, and 1.78 respectively, compared to the measured value of 1.449(17) W.u.
- The ${21/2}^+$ isomer in $^{95}$Ru has a half-life of 10.05 nanoseconds, much shorter than in $^{93}$Mo, because the ${17/2}^+$ state lies below the isomeric state.
- The $\pi\pi$ interaction matrix element difference $V^6_{\pi\pi} - V^8_{\pi\pi}$ is 149 keV, favoring a ${17/2}_1^+$ level below ${21/2}^+$ in $^{93}$Mo.

## Context

This work extends the shell-model analysis of Auerbach and Talmi (1964), who first explained the ${21/2}^+$ and ${17/2}^+$ inversion in $^{93}$Mo using the $\nu 1d_{5/2} \otimes \pi 0g_{9/2}^2$ configuration, and Hasegawa et al. (2011), who attributed the inversion to strong neutron-proton interactions but did not provide quantitative matrix-element decomposition. The study provides a framework for identifying future NEEC candidates in other mass regions by quantifying how subtle neutron-proton interaction systematics govern isomeric behavior, and it enables more accurate theoretical estimates of NEEC probabilities by revising the $B(E2)$ value for the key transition downward by 40%.
