# Calculation of $g_{πNS_{11}}$ and $g_{ηNS_{11}}$ Couplings in QCD Sum Rules

> We calculate the coupling constants, $g_{πNS_{11}}$ and $g_{ηNS_{11}}, using QCD sum rules in the presence of an external meson field. A covariant derivative is introduced within the S_{11} interpolating field so that in the nonrelativistic limit the field dominantly reduces to two quarks in the s-wave state and one quark in the p-wave state. Our result for the couplings obtained by further making use of the soft-meson theorem qualitatively agrees with its phenomenological value extracted from the S_{11}(1535) decay width. The prediction for the couplings however depend on the value of quark-gluon condensate, $< {\bar q} g_s σ\cdot {\cal G} q >$, which is also important in the calculation of the S_{11}(1535) mass itself within the sum rule approach.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the coupling constants \(g_{\pi NS_{11}}\) and \(g_{\eta NS_{11}}\) using QCD sum rules with an external meson field, finding that the prediction for \(g_{\eta NS_{11}}\) agrees well with the empirical value of 2, while the prediction for \(g_{\pi NS_{11}}\) depends sensitively on the poorly known quark-gluon condensate parameter \(\lambda_q^2\) and only matches the empirical value of 0.7 when \(\lambda_q^2 = 0.5\) GeV\(^2\).

## Method

The authors construct an S\(_{11}\) interpolating field containing a covariant derivative, which in the nonrelativistic limit reduces to two quarks in an s-wave and one quark in a p-wave state, and which couples to the negative-parity S\(_{11}\) resonance but not to the nucleon. They compute the two-point correlation function between this S\(_{11}\) current and the usual Ioffe nucleon current in the presence of an external pion or eta meson field, using the soft-meson theorem to relate vacuum-to-meson condensates to vacuum-to-vacuum condensates. The OPE is calculated up to dimension 7, and the sum rule is obtained by equating the Borel-transformed phenomenological side (proportional to the structure \(\gamma_5 \not\!z \not\!q\)) with the OPE side, extracting the coupling constants for various values of the quark-gluon condensate parameter \(\lambda_q^2\).

## Key numbers

*   Empirical coupling constants extracted from S\(_{11}\)(1535) decay widths: \(g_{\pi NS_{11}} \sim 0.7\) and \(g_{\eta NS_{11}} \sim 2\), each corresponding to a partial decay width \(\Gamma \sim 70\) MeV.
*   QCD parameters used: \(\langle \bar{q} q \rangle = -(0.23\ \text{GeV})^3\), \(\langle \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} \mathcal{G}^2 \rangle = (0.35\ \text{GeV})^4\), and pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 0.093\) GeV.
*   Nucleon parameters from the conventional sum rule: \(|\lambda_N| = 0.0258\) GeV\(^3\) and \(M_N = 0.977\) GeV.
*   For \(\lambda_q^2 = 0.5\) GeV\(^2\) (where \(t = -47.52\), \(|\lambda_{N^*}| = 0.24\) GeV\(^4\), \(M_{N^*} = 1.59\) GeV): \(|g_{\pi NS_{11}}| = 1.1\) (1.51 with continuum) and \(|g_{\eta NS_{11}}| = 2.27\) (3.03 with continuum).
*   For \(\lambda_q^2 = 0.435\) GeV\(^2\): \(|g_{\pi NS_{11}}| = 1.55\) (2.11 with continuum) and \(|g_{\eta NS_{11}}| = 1.99\) (2.67 with continuum).
*   For \(\lambda_q^2 = 0.8\) GeV\(^2\): \(|g_{\eta NS_{11}}| = 2.47\) (3.3 with continuum); \(g_{\pi NS_{11}}\) could not be extracted due to instability.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QCD sum rule study of the S\(_{11}\)(1535) mass (Lee and Kim, 1997) by calculating its couplings to pions and etas, using the same interpolating field with a covariant derivative. It contrasts with the approach of Oka, Jido, and Hosaka (1997), who used a different S\(_{11}\) current without the covariant derivative and found that the \(\gamma_5 \not\!q\) structure vanishes in the soft-pion limit, leading to a zero prediction for \(g_{\pi NS_{11}}\). The results enable further tests of the quark-gluon condensate's role in baryon spectroscopy and motivate going beyond the soft-meson limit, especially for the eta, and investigating mixing with the \(\eta'\).
