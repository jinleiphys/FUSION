# The composite nature of the $Λ(1520)$ resonance

> Recently, the Weinberg compositeness condition of a bound state was generalized to account for resonant states and higher partial waves. We apply this extension to the case of the $Λ(1520)$ resonance and quantify the weight of the meson-baryon components in contrast to other possible genuine building blocks. This resonance was theoretically obtained from a coupled channels analysis using the s-waves $πΣ^*$, $KΞ^*$ and the d-waves $\bar{K}N$ and $πΣ$ channels applying the techniques of the chiral unitary approach. We obtain that this resonance is essentially dynamically generated from these meson-baryon channels, leaving room for only $15 \%$ weight of other kind of components into its wave function.

# The composite nature of the Λ(1520) resonance

## Key claim
The Λ(1520) resonance is essentially dynamically generated from meson-baryon channels (πΣ*, KΞ* in s-wave, and K̅N, πΣ in d-wave), with less than 15% weight for other possible genuine components in its wave function, though the authors note that the small deviation from unity in the sum rule may reflect uncertainty in the method rather than a genuine component.

## Method
The authors apply a generalization of the Weinberg compositeness condition for resonant states and higher partial waves to the Λ(1520) resonance. They use a coupled-channels chiral unitary approach with four channels: πΣ(1385) and KΞ(1530) in s-wave, and K̅N and πΣ in d-wave, fitting five d-wave coefficients and two momentum cutoffs to experimental K̅N and πΣ scattering data. The pole positions and couplings are extracted from the scattering amplitudes in the second Riemann sheet, and the compositeness sum rule is evaluated to determine the weight of each meson-baryon component.

## Key numbers
- Pole position of the Λ(1520): approximately 1518.3–1519.9 MeV with half-width 6.4–6.7 MeV (from five parameter sets)
- Total meson-baryon compositeness (1−Z): 0.87 ± 0.10, averaged over five fit sets
- Weight of πΣ* channel (X_πΣ*): 0.072–0.093 across the five sets
- Weight of KΞ* channel (X_KΞ*): 0.001–0.002 across the five sets
- Weight of K̅N channel (X_K̅N): 0.494–0.541 across the five sets
- Weight of πΣ channel (X_πΣ): 0.214–0.239 across the five sets
- Coupling to πΣ*: largest coupling, with values 0.63–0.78 (real part) across the five sets
- Cutoff for s-wave channels (p_max^(s)): 1324–1798 MeV across the five fit sets
- Cutoff for d-wave channels (p_max^(d)): 865–911 MeV across the five fit sets

## Context
This work extends the Weinberg compositeness condition, previously generalized to resonances and higher partial waves by Aceti et al. (2014) and applied to the ρ and K* mesons, to a baryonic resonance for the second time (after the Δ(1232) and decuplet baryons studied by Aceti et al. (2014)). It builds on the coupled-channels analysis of the Λ(1520) by Luis et al. (2012) and Luis et al. (2013), which provided the scattering amplitudes and couplings used here. The result that the Λ(1520) is predominantly a dynamically generated resonance from meson-baryon interactions, rather than a compact quark state, enables future studies of its decays and production reactions to be interpreted in terms of its composite structure, and provides a methodology for quantifying the hadronic molecular content of other baryonic resonances.
