# A schematic model for QCD I: Low energy meson states

> A simple model for QCD is presented, which is able to reproduce the meson spectrum at low energy. The model is a Lipkin type model for quarks coupled to gluons. The basic building blocks are pairs of quark-antiquarks coupled to a definite flavor and spin. These pairs are coupled to pairs of gluons with spin zero. The multiplicity problem, which dictates that a given experimental state can be described in various manners, is removed when a particle-mixing interaction is turned on. In this first paper of a series we concentrates on the discussion of meson states at low energy, the so-called zero temperature limit of the theory. The treatment of baryonic states is indicated, also.

# A schematic model for QCD I: Low energy meson states

## Key claim

The paper presents a Lipkin-type schematic model for QCD that can reproduce the low-energy meson spectrum, but only after a particle-mixing interaction is turned on to remove the multiplicity problem (where a given experimental state can be described in multiple ways). The model is restricted to the zero-temperature limit and focuses on meson states, with baryonic states only indicated but not treated.

## Method

The model consists of two fermion levels at energies ±ωf (with ωf = 0.33 GeV) representing quarks and antiquarks, coupled to a boson level at ωb = 1.6 GeV representing gluon pairs with spin zero. Quark-antiquark pairs are coupled to definite flavor (either (0,0) or (1,1)) and spin (0 or 1), and these pair operators are mapped onto boson operators using a boson mapping scheme. The Hamiltonian includes a cutoff factor (1 − nf/2Ω) that simulates the Pauli principle, and the basis states are constructed in a seniority scheme for the four types of bosons (one, three, eight, and 24 dimensional harmonic oscillators).

## Key numbers

- ωf = 0.33 GeV (one third of the nucleon mass)
- ωb = 1.6 GeV (gluon pair energy, fixed from lattice gauge calculations)
- 2Ω = 18, with ns = 2, nc = 3, nf = 3 (spin, color, and flavor degrees of freedom)
- The uncorrected mass for the octet before flavor mixing is m8 = 615 MeV for the (1,1) 0− and (0,0) 0− multiplets
- The uncorrected mass for the octet before flavor mixing is m8 = 940 MeV for the (1,1) 1− and (0,0) 1− multiplets
- Theoretical prediction for f0(400-1200): 0.656 GeV (experimental: 0.600 GeV)
- Theoretical prediction for η′(958): 0.885 GeV (experimental: 0.892 GeV, after mixing correction)
- Theoretical prediction for ω(782): 0.851 GeV (experimental: 0.861 GeV, after mixing correction)
- Theoretical prediction for φ(1020): 0.943 GeV (experimental: 0.940 GeV, after mixing correction)
- Theoretical prediction for ω(1600): 1.639 GeV (experimental: 1.650 GeV)

## Context

This work extends the schematic model introduced in Lerma et al. (2002) (Ref. [simple]), which demonstrated a quantum phase transition at zero temperature and a Goldstone boson with negative parity. The model builds on the Lipkin model (Lipkin, 1965) and its application to many-gluon systems (Hess et al., 1999) that reproduced the lattice gauge spectrum. The approach is contrasted with lattice gauge theory (which can only calculate the lowest states for given quantum numbers) and the MIT bag model. The paper is the first in a series; the second paper (Ref. [papII]) will address high-energy behavior and the transition to the Quark-Gluon Plasma. The model enables systematic study of meson states up to about 2 GeV and provides a framework for understanding the multiplicity problem in hadron spectroscopy.
