# Solar Axions from Nuclear Transitions

> We investigate the possibility of detecting 14.4 keV and 9.4 keV solar axions and axion-like particles that could be produced in the M1 nuclear transitions of $^{57}$Fe and $^{83}$Kr, respectively. To do so, we used data from soft X-ray observations of the quiet Sun collected by the Solar X-ray Monitor (XSM) on board India's Chandrayaan-2 lunar mission. We observe that although the effective axion-nucleon couplings for $^{83}$Kr and $^{57}$Fe differ only slightly, their fluxes differ by nearly three orders of magnitude. Consequently, the limit on $|g_{aN}^{\rm eff} \times g_{aγγ}|$ and only $g_{aγγ}$ vs. $m_a$ provide more than an order-of-magnitude stronger constraint for Fe than for Kr.

## Key claim

The authors use quiet-Sun soft X-ray data from the Chandrayaan-2 Solar X-ray Monitor to search for monochromatic 14.4 keV and 9.4 keV photons from axion-photon conversion in the solar magnetic field, and in the absence of such spectral features they derive upper limits on the product of axion-nucleon and axion-photon couplings; the limits from the iron channel are more than an order of magnitude stronger than those from the krypton channel, even though the effective axion-nucleon couplings for the two isotopes differ only slightly.

## Method

The authors calculate the solar axion flux from M1 nuclear de-excitation of 57Fe (14.4 keV transition) and 83Kr (9.4 keV transition) using the B23-AGSS09 standard solar model for radial temperature, density, and hydrogen mass fraction profiles, with photospheric abundances from Asplund et al. (2021). They compute the axion-to-photon conversion probability in the solar magnetic field by solving the axion-photon mixing equations perturbatively out to 29 solar radii, using solar magnetic field and electron density profiles from Ruz et al. (2024) and photon absorption coefficients from the CHIANTI atomic database and NIST XCOM database. They then construct a Gaussian likelihood over the 3.4-15 keV energy range using background-subtracted residual spectra from the Chandrayaan-2 XSM data, processed under three distinct background subtraction schemes (Conservative, Realistic, and Optimistic), to derive upper limits on the coupling product |g_aN^eff x g_agg|.

## Key numbers

* The normalization flux for 57Fe axions at Earth is Phi_0^Fe = 2.18 x 10^23 cm^-2 s^-1, while for 83Kr it is Phi_0^Kr = 4.92 x 10^20 cm^-2 s^-1, a difference of nearly three orders of magnitude due to the much lower photospheric abundance of krypton (epsilon_Kr approx 3.12) versus iron (epsilon_Fe approx 7.46).
* The 57Fe M1 transition energy is 14.4 keV and the 83Kr M1 transition energy is 9.4 keV.
* The XSM energy resolution is FWHM = 175 eV, used to convolve the monoenergetic signal with a Gaussian.
* The conversion probability P_a-gamma is constant for axion masses m_a less than about 10^-5 eV and falls rapidly for higher masses.
* The limit on |g_aN^eff x g_agg| and on g_agg versus m_a provides more than an order-of-magnitude stronger constraint for iron than for krypton.

## Context

This work extends previous searches for solar axions from nuclear transitions, including CAST (2009) and CUORE (2012) searches for 14.4 keV 57Fe axions, and the Baksan Neutrino Observatory search for 9.4 keV 83Kr axions via resonant nuclear absorption (Gavrilyuk et al., 2014). It also builds on the same authors' prior analysis (Kumar and Nath, 2025) that used Chandrayaan-2 data to search for solar axions from Primakoff and electron-induced processes, and on a similar NuSTAR analysis by Ruz et al. (2024). The results enable future experiments to target the 14.4 keV iron channel as the more promising of the two nuclear transitions for constraining axion couplings.
