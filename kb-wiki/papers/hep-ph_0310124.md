# BFKL Pomeron at non-zero temperature and integrability of the Reggeon dynamics in multi-colour QCD

> We consider the QCD scattering amplitudes at high energies sqrt{s} and fixed momentum transfers sqrt{-t} in the leading logarithmic approximation at a non-zero temperature T in the t-channel. It is shown that the BFKL Hamiltonian has the property of holomorphic separability. The Pomeron wave function for arbitrary T is calculated using an integral of motion. In multi-colour QCD, the holomorphic Hamiltonian for n-reggeized gluons at temperature T is shown to coincide with the local Hamiltonian of an integrable Heisenberg model and can be obtained from the T=0 Hamiltonian by an unitary transformation. We discuss the wave functions and the spectrum of intercepts for the colourless reggeon states.

## Key claim

The BFKL Hamiltonian at non-zero temperature retains the property of holomorphic separability and, in multi-colour QCD, coincides with the local Hamiltonian of an integrable Heisenberg model, obtainable from the zero-temperature Hamiltonian by a unitary transformation; the Pomeron wave function for arbitrary temperature is calculated using an integral of motion, with the caveat that the momentum transfer is considered to be of order \(T\) and temperature effects in the direct channels are neglected.

## Method

The authors introduce temperature in the center-of-mass frame of the \(t\)-channel, quantizing the euclidean energies of intermediate gluons as \(k_4^{(l)} = 2\pi l T\) and imposing periodicity of the holomorphic impact-parameter \(\rho\) with imaginary period \(i/T\). They rescale coordinates and momenta to dimensionless variables, compute the gluon Regge trajectory at temperature \(T\) in one-loop approximation, and derive the holomorphic BFKL Hamiltonian \(h_{12}\) for the Pomeron in a thermostat. The eigenfunctions of the Hamiltonian are obtained using an integral of motion \(A = 4\sinh^2(\rho_{12}/2)\, p_1 p_2\), leading to a differential equation solved by hypergeometric functions, and the mapping \(\rho' = e^{2\pi T \rho}/(2\pi T)\) is shown to relate the zero-temperature and finite-temperature dynamics.

## Key numbers

- The gluon trajectory at temperature \(T\) includes a term \(\Omega(q) = \frac{\pi T}{\lambda} + \frac{1}{2}[\psi(1+iq) + \psi(1-iq) - 2\psi(1)]\), where \(\lambda\) is an infrared regulator mass for the gluon.
- The effective gluon propagator in coordinate space is \(G(\vec{\rho}_{12}) = -\frac{\pi T}{\lambda} + \ln(2\sinh\frac{\rho_{12}}{2}) + \ln(2\sinh\frac{\rho_{12}^*}{2})\).
- The zero-temperature BFKL Pomeron intercept is \(\Delta = 4 \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} N_c \ln 2\) (at \(m=\tilde{m}=1/2\)); in the next-to-leading approximation the intercept is \(\Delta \sim 0.2\) for QCD.
- The hadronization temperature scale is \(\sim 160\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the BFKL approach of Balitsky, Fadin, Kuraev, and Lipatov (BFKL) and the BKP equations for \(n\)-reggeized gluons to non-zero temperature, building on the integrability properties established by Lipatov (1990s) and the conformal invariance of the BFKL Hamiltonian. The exponential mapping relating zero-temperature and finite-temperature dynamics is noted to be analogous to mappings used for quantum fields in accelerated frames and black hole backgrounds (e.g., Unruh effect). The results enable the generalization of the non-linear Balitsky-Kovchegov equation to finite temperature and provide a framework for studying the temperature dependence of the Pomeron intercept and glueball properties in a quark-gluon plasma.
