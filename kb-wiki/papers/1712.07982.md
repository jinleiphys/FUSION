# Eta-mesic nuclei

> In this contribution we report on theoretical studies of $η$ nuclear quasi-bound states in few- and many-body systems performed recently by the Jerusalem-Prague Collaboration [1-5]. Underlying energy-dependent $ηN$ interactions are derived from coupled-channel models that incorporate the $N^*(1535)$ resonance. The role of self-consistent treatment of the strong energy dependence of subthreshold $ηN$ amplitudes is discussed. Quite large downward energy shift together with rapid decrease of the $ηN$ amplitudes below threshold result in relatively small binding energies and widths of the calculated $η$ nuclear bound states. We argue that the subthreshold behavior of $ηN$ scattering amplitudes is crucial to conclude whether $η$ nuclear states exist, in which nuclei the $η$ meson could be bound and if the corresponding widths are small enough to allow detection of these $η$ nuclear states in experiment.

# Eta-mesic nuclei

## Key claim
The existence and properties of η-mesic nuclei depend critically on the strong energy dependence of subthreshold ηN scattering amplitudes, which must be treated self-consistently; the resulting binding energies and widths are considerably smaller than those obtained in other comparable calculations, but the predictions remain highly model-dependent across different coupled-channel interaction models.

## Method
The authors performed self-consistent calculations of η nuclear quasi-bound states in few-body systems (A=2-6) using Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations and the Stochastic Variational Method with correlated Gaussian basis, and in many-body systems by solving the Klein-Gordon equation with a self-energy constructed from in-medium ηN scattering amplitudes. The underlying energy-dependent ηN interactions were derived from three coupled-channel meson-baryon models (GW, CS, GR) that incorporate the N*(1535) resonance, with the energy argument shifted substantially below threshold due to binding energies and Fermi motion of nucleons.

## Key numbers
- Common value of Im F_ηN at threshold: approximately 0.2-0.3 fm across all three models
- For ηNNN (η³He): weakly bound state found only with Minnesota NN potential and one variant of ηN potential reproducing GW amplitudes, with η separation energy below 1 MeV
- For η⁴He: binding requires Re a_ηN ≥ 0.7 fm; no bound state found with more realistic NN interaction models
- For η⁶Li: preliminary calculation with Minnesota NN and GW potentials gave B(⁶Li)=34.66 MeV (with one spin-isospin configuration)
- Binding energies B_η for η nuclear states across periodic table: GW and CS models produce widths of 2-4 MeV, nearly constant with A; GR model yields widths of order 20 MeV increasing with A
- Average nucleon binding energy B_N ≈ 8.5 MeV; kinetic energy T_N = 23.0 MeV at ρ₀

## Context
This work extends the Jerusalem-Prague Collaboration's theoretical studies of η-mesic nuclei (Cieply et al. 2013, 2014; Barnea et al. 2015) by applying self-consistent treatment of subthreshold energy shifts to both few-body and many-body systems. The results contradict earlier calculations by Carrasco (2002) that used a simpler self-consistency condition δ√s = -B_η, which produced larger binding energies and widths. The calculations enable experimental searches for η nuclear bound states, though the WASA-at-COSY facility has not yet found evidence for a narrow η⁴He state. The strong model dependence means that other models either produce substantially larger widths or predict no η nuclear bound state in a given nucleus.
