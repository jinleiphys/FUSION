# Including particle-vibration coupling in the Fayans functional. Odd-even mass differences of semi-magic nuclei

> A method to evaluate the particle-phonon coupling (PC) corrections to the single-particle energies in semi-magic nuclei, based on the direct solution of the Dyson equation with PC corrected mass operator, is presented. It is used for finding the odd-even mass difference between even Pb and Sn isotopes and their odd-proton neighbors. The Fayans energy density functional (EDF) DF3-a is used which gives rather highly accurate predictions for these mass differences already at the mean-field level. In the case of the lead chain, account for the PC corrections induced by the low-laying phonons $2^+_1$ and $3^-_1$ makes agreement of the theory with the experimental data significantly better. For the tin chain, the situation is not so definite. In this case, the PC corrections make agreement better in the case of the addition mode but they spoil the agreement for the removal mode. We discuss the reason of such a discrepancy.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method for including particle-vibration coupling (PC) corrections to single-particle energies in semi-magic nuclei by directly solving the Dyson equation, and applies it to odd-even mass differences in lead and tin isotope chains. The Fayans functional DF3-a already gives accurate predictions at the mean-field level, and adding PC corrections from the low-lying \(2^+_1\) and \(3^-_1\) phonons significantly improves agreement for the lead chain, but for the tin chain the corrections improve the addition mode while worsening the removal mode.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson equation for the single-particle Green function with a mass operator that includes a mean-field part from the Fayans energy density functional DF3-a plus a PC correction term. The PC correction is the sum of a pole diagram and a non-pole (tadpole) term for each low-lying phonon, using the surface-peaked approximation for the phonon creation vertex. For semi-magic nuclei where perturbation theory fails due to small energy denominators, the Dyson equation is solved directly in a restricted model space of one hole and one particle shell near the Fermi surface, yielding a set of solutions \(\varepsilon_\lambda^i\) with spectroscopic factors \(S_\lambda^i\). The average single-particle energy and \(Z\)-factor are then extracted from these solutions, and the odd-even mass differences (chemical potentials) are identified with the appropriate solutions for the first particle and first hole states.

## Key numbers

- For the lead chain, the root-mean-square deviation \(\langle \delta \mu_+ \rangle_{\rm rms}\) for the addition mode \(\mu_+\) is 0.465 MeV at mean-field level (DF3-a), improving to 0.261 MeV with the \(2^+_1\) phonon and 0.252 MeV with both \(2^+_1\) and \(3^-_1\) phonons (16 experimental data points).
- For the lead chain removal mode \(\mu_-\), \(\langle \delta \mu_- \rangle_{\rm rms}\) is 0.344 MeV at mean-field level, 0.370 MeV with only the \(2^+_1\) phonon, and 0.220 MeV with both phonons (18 experimental data points).
- The combined average deviation for lead (both \(\mu_+\) and \(\mu_-\), 34 data points) is \(\langle \delta \mu \rangle_{\rm rms}({\rm DF3{-}a}) = 0.389\) MeV at mean-field level and \(\langle \delta \mu \rangle_{\rm rms}({\rm DF3{-}a})^{\rm PC} = 0.218\) MeV with PC corrections.
- For comparison, the Skyrme EDF HFB-17 gives \(\langle \delta \mu_+ \rangle_{\rm rms}({\rm HFB-17}) = 0.486\) MeV and \(\langle \delta \mu_- \rangle_{\rm rms}({\rm HFB-17}) = 0.253\) MeV.
- In \(^{204}\)Pb, the \(2^+_1\) phonon energy is \(\omega_2 = 0.882\) MeV and the \(3^-_1\) phonon energy is \(\omega_3 = 2.785\) MeV; the corresponding surface vibration amplitudes are \(\alpha_2 = 0.22\) fm and \(\alpha_3 = 0.31\) fm.
- In \(^{118}\)Sn, the perturbation theory \(Z\)-factor for the \(2d_{3/2}\) state is 0.03, indicating catastrophic failure of perturbation theory.

## Context

This work extends the method of direct Dyson equation solution for PC corrections, previously applied to semi-magic nuclei by the same group (Saperstein et al., 2016), to the systematic calculation of odd-even mass differences across entire isotope chains. It builds on the self-consistent theory of finite Fermi systems (TFFS) developed by Migdal and collaborators, and uses the Fayans energy density functional DF3-a, which was designed to incorporate PC effects on average. The results for the lead chain show that including the two lowest collective phonons improves agreement with experimental mass differences beyond the Skyrme EDF HFB-17 of Goriely et al. (2009), while the mixed results for the tin chain highlight the need for further theoretical development, possibly including additional phonons or improved treatment of the non-pole terms.
