# Transverse Dynamics of Hard Partons in Nuclear Media and the QCD Dipole

> We derive the non-abelian generalization of the Furry approximation which describes the transverse dynamical evolution of a hard projectile parton inside a spatially extended colour target field. This provides a unified starting point for the target rest frame description of the nuclear dependence of a large class of observables. For the case of the virtual gamma -> q-qbar photoabsorption cross section, we investigate then in detail under which conditions the nuclear dependence encoded in the Furry wavefunctions can be parametrized by a q-qbar QCD dipole cross section. The important condition is colour triviality, i.e., the property that for arbitrary N-fold rescattering contributions the only non-vanishing colour trace is N_c C_F^N. We give proofs for the colour triviality of the inelastic, diffractive and total photoabsorption cross section measured inclusively or with one jet resolved in the final state. Also, we list examples for which colour interference effects remain. Colour triviality allows us to write the gamma -> q qbar contribution to the DIS nuclear structure function F_2 for small Bjorken x in terms of a path integral which describes the transverse size evolution of the q-qbar pair in the nuclear colour field. This expression reduces in an opacity expansion to the N=1 result of Nikolaev and Zakharov, and in the eikonal approximation to the Glauber-type rescattering formulas first derived by Mueller. In the harmonic oscillator approximation of the path integral, we quantify deviations from the eikonal limit. Their onset is characterized by the scales L/l_f and E_t L which relate the longitudinal extension L of the nuclear target to the coherence length l_f and the total transverse energy E_t accumulated by the q-qbar pair.

## Key claim

The non-abelian Furry approximation provides a unified starting point for describing the transverse dynamical evolution of hard projectile partons inside a spatially extended colour target field, but its reduction to a QCD dipole cross section requires the condition of colour triviality, the property that for arbitrary N-fold rescattering contributions the only non-vanishing colour trace is \(N_c C_F^N\), which holds for inclusive and one-jet-resolved photoabsorption but fails for certain more exclusive processes where colour interference effects remain.

## Method

The author derives the non-abelian generalization of the Furry approximation by summing N-fold rescattering diagrams of a hard parton in a static colour potential (Gyulassy-Wang model) to leading order \(1/E\) in norm and next-to-leading order in phase, obtaining a path-ordered Green's function \(\bar{G}\) that describes the transverse Brownian motion of the projectile. For the \(\gamma^* \to q\bar{q}\) photodissociation process, the author then proves colour triviality diagrammatically for inclusive, diffractive, and one-jet-resolved cross sections, and expresses the nuclear structure function \(F_2\) at small Bjorken \(x\) as a path integral over the transverse size evolution of the \(q\bar{q}\) pair. The harmonic oscillator approximation of this path integral quantifies deviations from the eikonal limit.

## Key numbers

- The condition for colour triviality is that the only non-vanishing colour trace for arbitrary N-fold rescattering is \(N_c C_F^N\).
- The onset of deviations from the eikonal limit is characterized by the scales \(L/l_f\) and \(E_\perp^{\rm tot} L\), where \(L\) is the longitudinal extension of the nuclear target, \(l_f\) is the coherence length, and \(E_\perp^{\rm tot}\) is the total transverse energy accumulated by the \(q\bar{q}\) pair.
- The free non-interacting Green's function is given by \(G_0({\bf r},z;{\bf r}',z'|p) \equiv \frac{p}{2\pi i(z'-z)} \exp\left\{ \frac{ip\, ({\bf r}-{\bf r}')^2}{2(z'-z)} \right\}\).
- The photodissociation cross section is related to the deep inelastic structure function by \(F_2(x,Q^2) = \frac{Q^2}{4\pi^2 \alpha_{\rm em}} \sigma_{total}^{\gamma^*}(x,Q^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the abelian Furry approximation (KST formalism, 1998) to non-abelian gauge theory, providing a unified framework that generalizes earlier approaches by Nikolaev and Zakharov (1991) for the N=1 opacity result and by Mueller (1990) for the eikonal Glauber-type rescattering formulas. The formalism enables quantitative study of nuclear shadowing in deep inelastic scattering and the non-abelian Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect, with the path integral representation allowing systematic inclusion of transverse dynamics beyond the eikonal approximation used in the Gyulassy-Wang model (1994) and the BDMPS work (1996-1999).
