# Flavor non-singlet parton distribution functions from lattice QCD at physical quark masses via the pseudo-distribution approach

> One of the great challenges of QCD is to determine the partonic structure of the nucleon from first principles. In this work, we provide such a determination of the flavor non-singlet ($u-d$) unpolarized parton distribution function (PDF), utilizing the non-perturbative formulation of QCD on the lattice. We apply Radyushkin's pseudo-distribution approach to lattice results obtained using simulations with the light quark mass fixed to its physical value; this is the first ever attempt for this approach directly at the physical point. The extracted coordinate-space matrix elements are used to find the relevant physical Ioffe time distributions from a matching procedure. The full Bjorken-$x$ dependence of PDFs is resolved using several reconstruction methods to tackle the ill-conditioned inverse problem encountered when using discrete lattice data. We consider both the valence distribution $q_v$ and the combination with antiquarks $q_v+2\bar{q}$, related to, respectively, the real and imaginary part of extracted matrix elements. Good agreement is found with PDFs from global fits already within statistical uncertainties and it is further improved by quantifying several systematic effects. The results presented here are the first ever \emph{ab initio} determinations of PDFs fully consistent with global fits in the whole $x$-range. Thus, they pave the way to investigating a wider class of partonic distributions, such as e.g.\ singlet PDFs and generalized parton distributions. Therefore, essential and yet missing first-principle insights can be achieved, complementing the rich experimental programs dedicated to the structure of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The authors provide the first *ab initio* lattice QCD determination of flavor non-singlet parton distribution functions that are fully consistent with global fits across the entire Bjorken-\(x\) range, but only when using the pseudo-distribution approach with light quark masses fixed to their physical value and after quantifying several systematic effects; the results are limited to the specific ensemble and lattice setup used, and full quantification of all systematics is not yet possible.

## Method

The authors compute reduced Ioffe-time distributions (pseudo-ITDs) from lattice QCD matrix elements using Radyushkin's double-ratio renormalization procedure, then apply one-loop perturbative matching to evolve these to the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme at a scale of 2 GeV. The full Bjorken-\(x\) dependence of the valence distribution \(q_v\) and the combination \(q_v+2\bar{q}\) is reconstructed from the matched ITDs using three methods: naive Fourier transform, the Backus-Gilbert method (with and without preconditioning), and a functional fit ansatz \(q(x) = N x^a (1-x)^b\). The lattice calculation uses one ensemble of \(48^3\times96\) sites with two degenerate flavors of maximally twisted mass fermions at physical pion mass (130.4 MeV) and lattice spacing 0.0938 fm, with nucleon boosts from 0 to 1.38 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: \(m_\pi = 130.4(4)\) MeV; nucleon mass: \(m_N = 932(4)\) MeV; lattice spacing: \(a = 0.0938(2)(3)\) fm; lattice volume: \(48^3 \times 96\) sites, corresponding to \(L \approx 4.5\) fm.
- Nucleon boosts used: \(P_3 = 0, 0.28, 0.55, 0.83, 1.11, 1.38\) GeV (corresponding to \(0, 2\pi/L, 4\pi/L, 6\pi/L, 8\pi/L, 10\pi/L\)).
- Source-sink separation: \(t_s = 12a = 1.13\) fm, used to suppress excited state effects.
- Matching scale: \(\mu = 2\) GeV; the scale \(1/z \approx 2.9\) GeV corresponds to \(z \approx 0.72a \approx 0.067\) fm.
- The reduced ITDs for \(z/a < 8\) are compatible within statistical uncertainties across different \((P_3, z)\) combinations giving the same Ioffe time.

## Context

This work extends the pseudo-distribution approach from quenched calculations (Orginos et al., 2017) and from dynamical quarks at non-physical masses (Joo et al., 2019) to the physical pion mass for the first time. It uses the same matrix elements previously computed for quasi-PDFs by Alexandrou et al. (2018, 2019), but applies the pseudo-distribution framework to also exploit low-momentum data. The results demonstrate that lattice QCD can now produce PDFs consistent with global fits across the full \(x\)-range, enabling future first-principles studies of singlet PDFs and generalized parton distributions to complement experimental programs at Jefferson Lab and the Electron-Ion Collider.
