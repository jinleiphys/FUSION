# Transport Coefficients in Yang--Mills Theory and QCD

> We calculate the shear viscosity over entropy density ratio $η/s$ in Yang--Mills theory from the Kubo formula using an exact diagrammatic representation in terms of full propagators and vertices using gluon spectral functions as external input. We provide an analytic fit formula for the temperature dependence of $η/s$ over the whole temperature range from a glueball resonance gas at low temperatures, to a high-temperature regime consistent with perturbative results. Subsequently we provide a first estimate for $η/s$ in QCD.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity over entropy density ratio in Yang-Mills theory can be described over the entire temperature range by a direct sum of a glueball resonance gas contribution at low temperatures and a high-temperature contribution consistent with hard-thermal-loop resummed perturbation theory, with a minimum value of 0.14 at approximately 1.26 times the critical temperature; the QCD estimate is then obtained by adjusting the running coupling, adding perturbative quark contributions, and replacing the glueball gas with a hadron resonance gas.

## Method

The authors compute the shear viscosity from the Kubo formula using an exact diagrammatic representation of the energy-momentum tensor correlation function in terms of full gluon propagators and vertices, evaluated in the real-time Schwinger-Keldysh formalism. The calculation includes all two-loop diagrams (sunset, Maki-Thompson, eight, squint, and one-loop with vertex correction) with fully dressed propagators and vertices, using gluon spectral functions obtained via maximum entropy method continuation from Euclidean functional renormalization group data as the only nontrivial input besides the running coupling. The running coupling is extracted from the ghost-gluon vertex dressing functions and evaluated at a temperature-dependent momentum scale of approximately 7 times the temperature to minimize the impact of neglected momentum dependence in the vertices.

## Key numbers

- Minimum of eta/s in Yang-Mills theory: 0.14 at T_min approximately 1.26 T_c
- Global fit parameters for Yang-Mills: a = 0.15, b = 0.14, c = 0.66, delta = 5.1, gamma approximately 1.6
- Vertex coupling at T_c: alpha_s,vert(T_c) approximately 0.76, corresponding to alpha_s in the MSbar scheme of approximately 0.35
- Heavy quark potential coupling at T_c: alpha_s,HQ(c T/T_c) at T=T_c approximately 1.77
- Scale identification factor for QCD: c_QCD = 0.79
- Minimum of eta/s in QCD estimate: 0.17 at T_min approximately 1.3 T_c
- Perturbative correction factors for QCD: Delta eta/eta_YM approximately 2.9, Delta s/s_YM approximately 2.0 for N_f = 3, giving an overall factor of approximately 4/3

## Context

This work extends the one-loop calculation of Haas (2013) to full two-loop order, resolving the non-perturbative temperature regime below approximately 2 T_c where perturbation theory breaks down. The results agree well with lattice data from Meyer (2007, 2009) and provide an analytic fit formula that interpolates between the glueball resonance gas regime and the high-temperature perturbative regime described by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2003). The QCD estimate enables phenomenological applications in heavy-ion collision hydrodynamics, where the viscosity over entropy ratio governs the conversion of initial spatial anisotropy into final-state momentum anisotropy.
