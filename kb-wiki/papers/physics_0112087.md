# Theory of Quantum Electrodynamical Self-consistent Fields

> To obtain the basis for combining various many-body techniques to QED in a consistent manner, we investigate the theory of quantum electrodynamical self-consistent fields. The reserch interest was born mainly of the electronic structure theory, thus we consider of atomic and moleculer systems as our main subjects. But the formalism is more fundamental. First, we derive the quantum electrodynamical Hartree-Fock theory by using the Green's function method. Then we construct a relativistic Hamiltonian written by creation-annihilation operators for electron and positron in a general form, and check that it reproduces the Hartree-Fock theory. We use this Hamiltonian to derive the time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory and random phase approximation, in the operator formalism. The relativistic Slater determinant of the Thouless parametrization is also used. Finally we discuss the applications and futher possible developments.

## Key claim

The paper derives a quantum electrodynamical (QED) Hartree-Fock theory and a fully normal-ordered relativistic Hamiltonian that includes both electron and positron degrees of freedom, but the author acknowledges that the theory still faces unresolved divergences requiring a non-perturbative renormalization procedure that has not yet been developed for this framework.

## Method

The author first derives the QED Hartree-Fock theory using the Green's function method, starting from the QED Lagrangian and obtaining the HF total energy through Dyson equation summation of vacuum polarization and fermion self-energy diagrams. A relativistic Hamiltonian is then constructed in the Schrödinger representation using creation-annihilation operators for electrons and positrons, with matrix elements containing retardation effects through the exponential factor in the photon propagator. The operator formalism is used to derive the time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory and random phase approximation, employing a relativistic Slater determinant in the Thouless parametrization and a modified definition of contractions for same-time operator products.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic many-body operator formalism to QED, aiming to provide a consistent basis for combining techniques such as Hartree-Fock, time-dependent Hartree-Fock, and random phase approximation with quantum electrodynamics. The paper contrasts with the Dirac-Coulomb-Breit no-sea Hamiltonian approach (which neglects the Dirac sea) and the Furry-picture perturbative QED method (which treats QED corrections only for few-electron systems), as both prior approaches have limited applicability to systems where vacuum polarization and electron self-energy effects are significant, such as inner-core electrons of heavy elements or highly ionized heavy ions. The formalism enables future development of non-perturbative many-body methods in QED, though the author notes that a non-perturbative renormalization scheme applicable to this theory remains an open problem.
