# Radiative Corrections and the Renormalization Group for the Two-Nucleon Interaction in Effective Field Theory

> We use a combination of effective field theory and the renormalization group to determine the impact of radiative corrections on the nucleon-nucleon potential and the binding energy of the deuteron. In order to do so, we present a modified version of pionless effective field theory inspired by earlier work in nonrelativistic quantum electrodynamics. The renormalization group improvement of the deuteron binding energy leads to a shift on the order of a few percent and is consistent with the experimental value. This work serves as a starting point for a dedicated study of radiative corrections in few-body systems relevant for precision tests of the Standard Model in an effective field theory framework.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that renormalization group improvement of radiative corrections in a modified pionless effective field theory produces a percent-level shift in the deuteron binding energy that is consistent with the experimental value, but only when the subtraction velocity is chosen near ν ≈ 0.04 (corresponding to momenta around 38 MeV).

## Method

The authors reformulate pionless effective field theory in the language of velocity nonrelativistic QED, splitting photon fields into soft and ultrasoft modes and multipole expanding the ultrasoft modes. They compute the O(α/v) anomalous dimensions for the nucleon-nucleon potential coefficients from ultrasoft photon dressing of the leading-order contact interaction, then integrate the velocity renormalization group equations to sum the leading logarithm series in α into the running couplings. The deuteron binding energy is calculated to next-to-leading order and next-to-next-to-leading order using renormalization group improved perturbation theory, with boundary conditions fixed by the scattering parameters of the Argonne v18 potential without electromagnetic interactions.

## Key numbers

- The neutron-proton scattering length used as boundary condition: a_np = 5.402 fm
- The neutron-proton effective range used as boundary condition: r_np = 1.752 fm
- The shape parameter used as boundary condition: P_np = 0.040 fm^{-3}
- At subtraction velocity ν ≈ 0.04, the renormalization group improved next-to-leading order binding energy shifts by about 2.5% relative to the fixed-order result
- At next-to-next-to-leading order, the renormalization group improvement shifts the binding energy by about 7%
- The experimental deuteron binding energy is B = 2.224575 MeV
- At the hard scale ν = m_π/M_N, the next-to-next-to-leading order binding energy is about 10% larger than the next-to-leading order result
- At ν ≈ 0.04, the next-to-next-to-leading order result is around 17% larger than the next-to-leading order result

## Context

This work extends the velocity renormalization group formalism developed by Luke, Manohar, and collaborators (2000) for nonrelativistic QED to the nuclear effective field theory context, specifically pionless effective field theory as formulated by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1996, 1998) and van Kolck (1999). The paper provides a starting point for incorporating radiative corrections into few-body calculations relevant for precision tests of the Standard Model, including beta-decay, muon capture, and muonic atom spectroscopy experiments. The framework can be applied to chiral effective field theory and halo effective field theory with trivial modifications.
