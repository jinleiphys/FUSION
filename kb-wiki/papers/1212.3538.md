# Approach to equilibrium in the Caldeira-Leggett Model

> The Caldeira-Leggett model describes a microscopic quantum system, represented by a harmonic oscillator, in interaction with a heat bath, represented by a large number of harmonic oscillators with a range of frequencies. We consider the case when the system oscillator starts out in the ground state and then thermalizes due to interactions with the heat bath, which is at temperature $θ$. We calculate the position autocorrelation function $<x(t')x(t)>$ of the system oscillator at two different times and study its behaviour in the small and large time limits. Our results show that the system oscillator thermalizes as expected. We also confirm by explicit calculation that the position autocorrelation function exhibits periodicity for imaginary values of the time difference $t'-t = iτ$ at late (real) times $t$.

# Approach to equilibrium in the Caldeira-Leggett Model

## Key claim

The system oscillator in the Caldeira-Leggett model thermalizes as expected, but the periodicity of the position autocorrelation function in imaginary time holds only on the compactified interval 0 ≤ τ ≤ 1/θ, not on the entire imaginary time axis.

## Method

The authors start with the Caldeira-Leggett Hamiltonian, where a system harmonic oscillator interacts linearly with a heat bath of many harmonic oscillators at temperature θ. They compute the reduced density matrix of the system oscillator using the Feynman-Vernon influence functional method, starting from the ground state, and extract the time-dependent coefficients a(t), b(t), and c(t) that characterize the Gaussian density matrix. They then derive an analytic expression for the two-time position autocorrelation function ⟨x(t+˜t)x(t)⟩ and compare its long-time limit to the known thermal equilibrium correlation function for a harmonic oscillator.

## Context

This work extends the Caldeira-Leggett model (Caldeira and Leggett, 1983) by explicitly computing the two-time position autocorrelation function and demonstrating how thermal equilibrium properties emerge from real-time evolution. The authors confirm that the system oscillator thermalizes and show that the imaginary-time periodicity of thermal Green functions emerges only on the compactified interval [0, 1/θ], clarifying a subtle point about the domain of periodicity. This enables detailed studies of thermalization dynamics in open quantum systems and provides a benchmark for more complex models where analytic solutions are not available.
