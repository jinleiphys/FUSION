# Ground-State Masses and Magnetic Moments of Heavy Baryons

> In this work we study single, double, and triple heavy-flavor baryons using the hypercentral approach in the framework of the non-relativistic quark model. Considering two different confining potentials and an improved form of the hyperfine interaction, we calculate the ground-state masses of heavy baryons and also the ground-state magnetic moments of single charm and beauty baryons with J^(P) =3/2^(+). The obtained results are in good agreement with experimental data and those of other works.

## Key claim

The authors calculate ground-state masses and magnetic moments of single, double, and triple heavy-flavor baryons using a hypercentral approach in the non-relativistic quark model, finding that the confining potential which includes a harmonic oscillator term (case II) yields results closer to experimental data than the Cornell potential alone (case I), particularly for double and triple heavy baryons.

## Method

The authors solve the three-body Schrodinger equation numerically using the hypercentral approach, which reduces the problem to a single variable (the hyperradius x). They consider two confining potentials: the Cornell potential (bx - c/x) alone (case I) and the Cornell potential plus a harmonic oscillator term (ax^2 + bx - c/x) (case II). A spin- and isospin-dependent hyperfine interaction, modeled as Gaussian functions of the hyperradius, is added as a perturbation to first order. The baryon mass is obtained as the sum of constituent quark masses plus the ground-state energy eigenvalue and the hyperfine correction.

## Key numbers

*   Constituent quark masses used: m_u = 330 MeV, m_d = 335 MeV, m_s = 469 MeV, m_c = 1600 MeV, m_b = 4980 MeV.
*   For case I, the parameter mu in the Schrodinger equation was fitted to the experimental mass of the Sigma_c^(++) baryon, resulting in mu = 0.844 fm^(-1).
*   Case I predicts the Sigma_c^(++) mass as 2454 MeV, matching the experimental value of 2454 MeV (0.0% error).
*   Case II predicts the Sigma_b^(+) mass as 5808 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 5807 MeV (0.0% error).
*   Case II predicts the magnetic moment of Sigma_c^(++)* (J^P = 3/2^+) as 3.766 mu_N (nuclear magneton).
*   Case II predicts the mass of the triple-charm Omega_ccc^(++)* as 4880 MeV.
*   Case II predicts the mass of the triple-beauty Omega_bbb^(-)* as 15023 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy baryons using the hypercentral approach by Ghalenovi et al. (2011, 2012), who solved the Schrodinger equation analytically or variationally, by solving it numerically. The results are compared to those of Patel et al. (2007, 2008) who used a non-relativistic quark model with hypercentral Coulomb plus linear and harmonic oscillator potentials, and to experimental data from the BaBar, BELLE, and CDF collaborations. The approach can be applied to other three-body systems in nuclear, atomic, and molecular physics.
