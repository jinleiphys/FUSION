# What Can We Learn from Transverse Momentum Spectra in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions?

> We compare the predictions of different models for transverse momentum spectra in relativistic heavy ion collisions. Particular emphasis is given to hydrodynamic flow with different assumptions for the radial expansion, and to models based on a superposition of fireballs along the transverse direction (random walk). It is shown that present experimental data cannot distinguish between the models under consideration.

## Key claim

The paper shows that present experimental data on transverse momentum spectra in Pb-Pb collisions at CERN cannot distinguish between hydrodynamic flow models and random walk (superposition of fireballs) models, because both approaches can reproduce the observed broadening with comparable quality.

## Method

The authors compared three variants of hydrodynamic expansion (full 2+1 dimensional hydrodynamics, a transverse velocity profile with linear radial dependence, and a constant transverse velocity) against a random walk model where successive nucleon-nucleon collisions produce fireballs whose transverse rapidities follow a Gaussian distribution. Each model was fitted to published NA44 and NA49 data for negative hadrons, positive hadron surplus, protons, neutral hyperons (Lambda and Sigma0), and kaons (Ks0 and K+), using freeze-out temperatures and average transverse velocities as free parameters.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature in the constant transverse velocity model: T_f = 0.15 GeV, with average transverse velocity <v_perp> = 0.37
- Freeze-out temperature in the transverse velocity profile model: T_f = 0.12 GeV, with <v_perp> = 0.43
- Freeze-out temperature in the full 2+1 dimensional hydrodynamics model: T_f = 0.115 GeV, with <v_perp> = 0.5
- Freeze-out temperature in the random walk model: T_f = 0.15 GeV (no transverse velocity parameter)
- Freeze-out temperatures from particle ratio analyses: 0.17 - 0.19 GeV, cited as a comparison range
- The random walk model uses a Gaussian distribution with width delta_AB^2 = (N_A + N_B - 2) * delta^2, where delta is the kick per collision determined from p-A data

## Context

This work extends the debate between hydrodynamic flow models (Heinz, 1990s; Ruuskanen, Blaizot, 1990s) and the random walk approach (Leonidov, 1990s) for explaining the transverse momentum broadening observed in Pb-Pb collisions at CERN by NA44 and NA49. The paper shows that single-particle spectra alone are insufficient to distinguish these mechanisms, and suggests that two-particle correlations (Esumi, 1990s) may be needed. It also highlights unresolved issues: all models require freeze-out temperatures (120-150 MeV) lower than those needed to explain hadron abundances from particle ratios (170-190 MeV), and the random walk model cannot reproduce the observed increase in strangeness production.
