# Study of Differential Scattering Cross-section using Yukawa term of medium-modified Cornell potential

> In the present work we have studied the Differential Scattering Cross-section for ground states of charmonium and bottomonium in the frame work of the medium modified form of quark-antiquark potential and Born-approximation using the non-relativistic quantum chromo-dynamics approach. To reach this end, quasi-particle (QP) Debye mass depending upon baryonic chemical potential ($μ$b) and temperature has been employed and hence the variation of Differential Scattering Cross-section with baryonic chemical potential and temperature at fixed value of the scattering angle ($θ=90^\circ$) has been studied. The variation of Differential Scattering Cross-section with scattering angle $θ$ (in degree) at fixed temperature and baryonic chemical potential has also been studied. We have also studied the effect of impact parameter and transverse momentum on differential scattering cross-section at $θ=90^\circ$.

## Key claim

The differential scattering cross-section for ground-state charmonium (J/ψ) and bottomonium (Υ) is computed using only the Yukawa (Coulombic) term of the medium-modified Cornell potential, neglecting the constant and string terms, within the first-order Born approximation and a non-relativistic QCD framework; the results show that the cross-section decreases exponentially with temperature and baryonic chemical potential, with the baryonic chemical potential having a smaller effect than temperature.

## Method

The authors employed the first-order Born approximation to compute the scattering amplitude from a spherically symmetric, medium-modified quark-antiquark potential, retaining only the Yukawa term (the exponential-screened Coulomb part) and discarding the constant and string terms. The Debye mass was calculated using a quasi-particle (QP) model with effective fugacities for gluons and quarks, incorporating the baryonic chemical potential (μb) and temperature (T) for full QCD with Nf=3. The differential scattering cross-section was then evaluated as a function of scattering angle (θ), temperature, baryonic chemical potential, impact parameter, and transverse momentum for the J/ψ (mass 1.5 GeV) and Υ (mass 4.5 GeV) ground states.

## Key numbers

- The mass of J/ψ used in the calculation is 1.5 GeV; the mass of Υ used is 4.5 GeV.
- The differential scattering cross-section as a function of θ was computed at T = 250 MeV and μb = 500 MeV (Figure 1).
- The variation of differential scattering cross-section with μb and T/Tc was studied at fixed θ = 90° (Figures 2 and 3).
- The variation with impact parameter was studied at fixed transverse momentum PT = 5 GeV and θ = 90° (Figure 6).
- The variation with transverse momentum was studied at μb = 500 MeV, θ = 90°, impact parameter b = 5 fm, and T = 150 MeV (Figure 8).

## Context

This work extends the study of quarkonium scattering in a hot, dense medium by using a quasi-particle Debye mass that depends on both temperature and baryonic chemical potential, building on prior medium-modified Cornell potential approaches (e.g., the authors cite works from 1994-2015 on the potential and Debye mass). The results are relevant for understanding quarkonium production and scattering rates in heavy-ion collisions, particularly at moderate to high transverse momentum where non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) is applicable. The authors note that the baryonic chemical potential has a smaller effect than temperature, and the work may help interpret nuclear effects on production cross-sections in the currently accessible transverse momentum range.
