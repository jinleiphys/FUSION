# Microscopic coupled-channel calculations of nucleus-nucleus scattering including chiral three-nucleon-force effects

> We analyze $^{16}$O-$^{16}$O and $^{12}$C-$^{12}$C scattering with the microscopic coupled-channels method and investigate the coupled-channels and three-nucleon-force (3NF) effects on elastic and inelastic cross sections. In the microscopic coupled-channels calculation, the Melbourne g-matrix interaction modified according to the chiral 3NF effects is used. It is found that the coupled-channels and 3NF effects additively change both the elastic and inelastic cross sections. As a result, the coupled-channels calculation including the 3NF effects significantly improves the agreement between the theoretical results and the experimental data. The incident-energy dependence of the coupled-channels and 3NF effects is also discussed.

## Key claim

The coupled-channels and three-nucleon-force (3NF) effects additively change both elastic and inelastic cross sections for \(^{16}\)O-\(^{16}\)O and \(^{12}\)C-\(^{12}\)C scattering, and including both effects significantly improves agreement with experimental data, though some discrepancies remain at backward angles.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic coupled-channel calculations for \(^{16}\)O-\(^{16}\)O and \(^{12}\)C-\(^{12}\)C scattering using the Melbourne \(g\)-matrix interaction modified to include chiral 3NF effects. Transition densities for \(^{12}\)C states (\(0_1^+\), \(2_1^+\), \(0_2^+\), \(2_2^+\)) were obtained from a \(3\alpha\) resonating group method (RGM) model, while those for \(^{16}\)O states (\(0_1^+\), \(3_1^-\), \(2_1^+\)) came from an orthogonality condition model (OCM) with an \(\alpha+^{12}\)C structure. The coupled-channel equations were solved without adjustable parameters, and the frozen density approximation was used for the density dependence of the \(g\)-matrix.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{16}\)O-\(^{16}\)O scattering at 70 MeV/nucleon, the calculation including both 3NF and coupled-channel effects reproduces elastic scattering data up to \(\theta = 15^\circ\).
- The total reaction cross section \(\sigma_R\) for \(^{16}\)O-\(^{16}\)O at 70 MeV/nucleon is 1453 mb with coupled-channel effects and 1426 mb without; at 44 MeV/nucleon, 1519 mb and 1473 mb, respectively.
- For \(^{12}\)C-\(^{12}\)C at 85 MeV/nucleon, \(\sigma_R\) is 1078 mb with coupled-channel effects and 1052 mb without; experimental values are \(998 \pm 13\) mb (at 86.3 MeV/nucleon) and \(960 \pm 30\) mb (at 83 MeV/nucleon).
- For \(^{12}\)C-\(^{12}\)C at 30 MeV/nucleon, \(\sigma_R\) is 1262 mb with coupled-channel effects and 1214 mb without; experimental values are \(1209 \pm 32\) mb (at 32.5 MeV/nucleon) and \(1315 \pm 40\) mb (at 30 MeV/nucleon).
- The 3NF effects are negligibly small at incident energies lower than about 30 MeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Minomo et al. (2014) and Toyokawa et al. (2014) that investigated chiral 3NF effects in nucleon-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus elastic scattering, but now includes coupled-channel effects for collective excitations. It also builds on microscopic coupled-channel analyses by Khoa et al. (2008) and Takashina et al. (2010), which focused mainly on inelastic cross sections. The results enable future studies to include higher excited states and refine effective interactions and transition densities for more quantitative comparisons with experimental data.
