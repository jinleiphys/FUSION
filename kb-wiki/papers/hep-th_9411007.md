# Light-Front QCD and the Constituent Quark Model

> A general strategy is described for deriving a constituent approximation to QCD, inspired by the constituent quark model and based on light-front quantization. Some technical aspects of the approach are discussed, including a mechanism for obtaining a confining potential and ways in which spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking can be manifested. (Based on a talk presented by K.G. Wilson at ``Theory of Hadrons and Light-Front QCD,'' Polana Zgorzelisko, Poland, August 1994.)

## Key claim

The paper proposes a strategy for deriving a constituent quark model (CQM) from QCD using light-front quantization, but the bridge between the two is not yet complete; the authors explicitly state that whether the resulting theory allows reasonable approximations to hadronic states using only a few constituents is an open question, and that the approach requires extrapolating from an artificially small coupling back to the physical value.

## Method

The authors start from the canonical light-front Hamiltonian for QCD in light-cone gauge, including constituent masses for both quarks and gluons and a cutoff on constituent energies. They then apply a similarity renormalization group transformation to generate an effective Hamiltonian that removes high-energy gluon emission and absorption processes, leaving only off-diagonal potentials within Fock sectors. The effective Hamiltonian is split into an unperturbed part containing constituent masses and dominant potentials, and a perturbation containing particle-number-changing operators, with bound states computed via a second perturbation theory. The running coupling is artificially taken to be small at the constituent mass scale, and the extrapolation back to the physical coupling constitutes the bridge to QCD.

## Key numbers

- Constituent up and down quark masses are taken to be roughly 300 MeV, approximately half the average mass of nonstrange mesons.
- The cutoff on constituent energies is imposed as (p_perp^2 + m^2)/p^+ < Lambda^2/P^+, where Lambda is a transverse momentum cutoff and P^+ sets a longitudinal scale.
- The confining potential derived from the similarity transformation grows logarithmically with separation, behaving as ln(Lambda^2 |x_perp|^2 / P^+) or ln|x^-| depending on which coordinate is larger.
- The gluon mass mu appears in the one-gluon exchange term as q_perp^2/(q_perp^2 + mu^2), which suppresses cancellation of the instantaneous term when |q_perp| is much less than mu.

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model tradition by attempting to derive it from QCD, in contrast to earlier approaches that treated the CQM as purely phenomenological. The paper builds on the similarity renormalization group method developed by Perry (1994) and references earlier work by the OSU group (Ref. [1] in the paper). The approach enables a systematic program to compute hadronic bound states from light-front QCD, with the key open problems being the proper treatment of confinement cancellation and the manifestation of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking through zero longitudinal momentum modes.
