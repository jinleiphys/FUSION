# Toward relativistic mean-field description of $\bar{\text{N}}$-nucleus reactions

> In this work we study the antinucleon-nucleus optical potential in the framework of the non-linear derivative (NLD) model with momentum dependent mean-fields. We apply the NLD model to interaction of antinucleons ($\bar{\text{N}}$) in nuclear matter and, in particular, to antiproton scattering on nuclei. In nuclear matter a strong suppression of the $\bar{\text{N}}$-optical potential at rest and at high kinetic energies is found and caused by the momentum dependence of relativistic mean-fields. The NLD results are consistent with known empirical $\bar{\text{N}}$-nucleus observations and agree well with antiproton-nucleus scattering data. This makes the NLD approach compatible with both, nucleon and antinucleon Dirac phenomenologies. Furthermore, in nuclear matter an effective mass splitting between nucleons and antinucleons is predicted.

## Key claim

The non-linear derivative (NLD) model, which introduces momentum-dependent mean-fields, can describe both in-medium nucleon and antinucleon interactions consistently within a G-parity conserving framework, contrary to conventional relativistic mean-field (RMF) models that require a violation of G-parity or rescaling of couplings to match antiproton-nucleus data.

## Method

The authors extended the NLD model, previously applied to nuclear matter and nucleon-nucleus scattering, to antinucleon interactions in nuclear matter by applying G-parity arguments to construct the in-medium antinucleon interaction without readjusting any parameters. They calculated the scalar and vector self-energies for antiprotons in nuclear matter at various densities, derived the Schrodinger-equivalent optical potential, and used the dispersion relation to obtain its imaginary part. Finally, they computed total antiproton reaction cross sections on various nuclear targets (C, Al, Cu, Pb) using the eikonal approximation with the NLD optical potential and empirical Wood-Saxon density distributions.

## Key numbers

- At saturation density (rho_sat = 0.155 fm^{-3}), an antiproton at zero in-medium kinetic energy has a single-particle momentum of approximately 750 MeV, compared to the Fermi momentum for a proton.
- At this momentum, the NLD scalar self-energy is 163 MeV and the vector self-energy is 134 MeV for the antiproton, giving a sum of 297 MeV.
- The conventional Walecka model (NL3 parametrization) gives a value of (Sigma_vp - Sigma_s) approximately -700 MeV for antiprotons at zero kinetic energy, which remains constant in energy.
- The NLD model reproduces antiproton-nucleus total reaction cross section data for C, Al, Cu, and Pb targets across a range of beam momenta, with data from Refs. [exp1, exp2, exp3, exp4].

## Context

This work extends the NLD model of Gaitanos (2012) from nuclear matter and proton-nucleus scattering to antinucleon interactions, addressing a known problem where conventional RMF models (e.g., Walecka model, NL3 parametrization) cannot describe antiproton-nucleus data using G-parity arguments alone. Previous work by Larionov required rescaling antinucleon-meson couplings by a factor of 0.2-0.3 to match antiproton-induced reaction data, suggesting a possible violation of G-parity. The NLD approach achieves consistency without such rescaling, and the results are relevant for upcoming experiments at FAIR, particularly the PANDA experiment.
