# Massive Yang-Mills for Vector and Axial-Vector Spectral Functions at Finite Temperature

> The hadronic mechanism which leads to chiral symmetry restoration is explored in the context of the rho-pi-a_1 system using Massive Yang-Mills, a hadronic effective theory which governs their microscopic interactions. In this approach, vector and axial-vector mesons are implemented as gauge bosons of a local chiral gauge group. We have previously shown that this model can describe the experimentally measured vector and axial-vector spectral functions in vacuum. Here, we carry the analysis to finite temperatures by evaluating medium effects in a pion gas and calculating thermal spectral functions. We find that the spectral peaks in both channels broaden along with a noticeable downward mass shift in the a_1 spectral peak and negligible movement of the rho peak. The approach toward spectral function degeneracy is accompanied by a reduction of chiral order parameters, i.e., the pion decay constant and scalar condensate. Our findings suggest a mechanism where the chiral mass splitting induced in vacuum is burned off. We explore this mechanism and identify future investigations which can further test it.

## Key claim

The hadronic mechanism for chiral symmetry restoration in the rho-pi-a_1 system involves a broadening of spectral peaks in both vector and axial-vector channels along with a noticeable downward mass shift in the a_1 spectral peak and negligible movement of the rho peak, suggesting that the chiral mass splitting induced in vacuum is "burned off" as temperature increases.

## Method

The authors used Massive Yang-Mills (MYM), a hadronic effective theory implementing vector and axial-vector mesons as gauge bosons of a local chiral gauge group, to calculate thermal spectral functions at finite temperature in a pion gas. They performed a loop expansion with fully dressed rho propagators in the a_1 self-energy, included vertex correction diagrams to preserve PCAC relations, and incorporated a chirally invariant continuum plus excited states (rho' and a_1') to fit vacuum spectral functions from tau-decay data before extending to finite temperature.

## Key numbers

- The pseudo-critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration in QCD is around T_pc approximately 160 MeV (from prior lattice QCD results cited as Borsanyi (2010) and Bazavov (2011))
- The physical pion mass is set to M_pi = 139.6 MeV and the pion decay constant to F_pi = 131 MeV
- The cut-off scale for the effective theory is set to Lambda_cut = 1.5 GeV
- In the non-linear realization, the rho mass parameter is M_rho = 0.86 GeV and the a_1 mass parameter is M_a1 = 1.20 GeV
- In the linear realization, the rho mass parameter is M_rho = 0.92 GeV, the a_1 mass parameter is M_a1 = 1.26 GeV, and the sigma mass is M_sigma = 0.80 GeV
- The rho-pi-pi coupling constant is g_rho_pi_pi = 6.01 (non-linear) and 6.36 (linear)
- The continuum parameters are alpha_s = 0.5, E_0 = 1.55 GeV, and delta = 0.22 GeV
- The a_1 width from the imaginary part of its self-energy near the resonance peak (s approximately 1-1.5 GeV^2) amounts to about 100-150 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous vacuum analysis (Hohler and Rapp, 2013) where MYM successfully described experimentally measured vector and axial-vector spectral functions from tau-decay data (ALEPH collaboration, 1998). It builds on earlier finite-temperature MYM studies by Song (1993) and Pisarski (1995) which found a reduction of the a_1 mass with an increase in the rho mass, but those calculations did not survive loop-level scrutiny needed to match vacuum data. The approach contrasts with global gauge symmetry models by Urban (2001) and Parganlija (2010) which found rho broadening without mass shift, and with the HLS framework by Harada (2008) which also found a_1 mass reduction but without satisfactory vacuum spectral function description. The results enable future tests of chiral restoration mechanisms through comparison with heavy-ion collision data and further microscopic calculations.
