# Nucleon-Nucleon Short-Ranged Correlations, $β$ Decay and the Unitarity of the CKM Matrix

> The influence of nucleon-nucleon short-ranged correlations (SRC) on nuclear super-allowed $β$ decay is examined. The need for this is driven by the observed depletion of spectroscopic strength obtained in studies of $(e,e')$ and $(d,^3{\rm He})$ reactions on a wide variety of nuclei. We show that the influence of SRC is model-dependent, but may be very substantial. The $^{46}$V nucleus is used as an example. The resulting impact on studies of the unitarity of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa (CKM) matrix element is discussed.

## Key claim

The influence of nucleon-nucleon short-ranged correlations (SRC) on the isospin-breaking correction δ_C for superallowed beta decay is strongly model-dependent, and depending on the detailed features of the SRC, the correction can be either increased or decreased substantially relative to the Towner-Hardy formalism, potentially changing the extracted value of V_ud by an amount several times the quoted uncertainty.

## Method

The authors extend the formalism of Miller and Schwenk (2008, 2009) by developing an effective one-body beta-decay operator that includes dominant isospin-violating effects, then evaluate its matrix element in a strongly-correlated system using a Jastrow correlation approximation. They model the short-ranged correlations using a function f(s) that is negative at small separations and vanishes beyond about 1 fm, and compute the correction δ_C(v) for a single proton in a 0f_7/2 state outside an inert Z=22 core (representing ⁴⁶V) using harmonic-oscillator single-particle wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The Towner-Hardy value of V_ud is 0.97373 ± 0.00031 (Hardy 2020).
- The SRC contribution to spectroscopic factor quenching is 22% ± 8% from Paschalis (2018), with the long-range correlation contribution at 14% ± 10%.
- For the 0f_7/2 state in ⁴⁶V, the depletion parameter κ = 1 - Z_S = 0.14, taken from Aumann (2020) for ⁵⁵Ni.
- The leading Coulomb correction δ_C0 for ⁴⁶V is 0.267%, matching the Towner-Hardy result.
- Using a Gaussian correlation shape (γ² = 8) with κ = 0.14 gives Δδ_C0 = 0.27 δ_C0, yielding an overall increase of about 13%; using a square shape (γ² = 1) gives negligible correction, an 11% decrease.
- With κ = 0.2 (stronger short-range repulsion), the spread ranges from a 20% decrease to a 30% increase.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Miller and Schwenk (2008, 2009) for evaluating the isospin correction δ_C, which was described by Hardy and Towner as "not a practical proposition." It directly challenges the Towner-Hardy approach (Hardy 2020) that neglects radial excitations above 2ħω and thus excludes short-ranged correlations. The paper is motivated by experimental evidence from Subedi (2008), Fomin (2011), and Hen (2016) showing that SRC exist and reduce spectroscopic factors by about 20%, and by the recent claim from Shiells (2020) of a 4-standard-deviation violation of CKM unitarity. The authors argue that detailed state-of-art nuclear calculations of superallowed beta decay are a high priority for nuclear theorists to resolve the model dependence.
