# Rescattering effects in coherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron in the $Δ$ resonance region

> Within a dynamical model, rescattering effects are studied in coherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron in the $Δ$(1232) resonance region. The model consists of a system of coupled equations for the N$Δ$, NN$π$ and NN channels. Pion rescattering leads in general to a significant reduction of total and differential cross sections resulting in a better description of experimental data. Only a few polarization observables are sensitive to rescattering effects. In particular $T_{20}$ allows an analysis of the reaction mechanism for specific kinematics. The question how rescattering affects the sensitivity to the E2 excitation of the $Δ$ resonance is also studied.

## Key claim

Pion rescattering effects in coherent pion photoproduction on the deuteron are significant and generally reduce cross sections, but the authors find that a perturbative (Born) treatment of rescattering underestimates the full dynamical effect by more than half, providing only a qualitative description. The claim that charged pion exchange contributions become small in the Δ resonance region (made by Kamalov, Tiator and Bennhold) is shown to be incorrect.

## Method

The authors constructed a dynamical model with coupled integral equations (Lippmann-Schwinger type) for the NΔ, NNπ, and NN channels, solved numerically in momentum space using a partial wave decomposition. The hadronic interaction includes a retarded, energy-dependent pion exchange potential for the NΔ system, and the model was first tested against elastic pion-deuteron scattering data before being applied to coherent pion photoproduction. Rescattering contributions were included via the dominant RΔΔ process (intermediate Δ excitation with subsequent hadronic interaction) and the smaller RΔN process, along with meson exchange current contributions from charged pion and ρ exchange.

## Key numbers

- At 300 MeV photon energy, the rescattering amplitudes within the KMT approach reduce the cross section by up to a factor three at 180 degrees, while the present model shows different quantitative behavior.
- The ratio of the isospin matrix element for neutral pion exchange alone to the full (neutral plus charged) exchange is 2, meaning neglect of charged pion exchange overestimates the rescattering effect by a factor of two in the perturbative treatment.
- For the E2 sensitivity study at 320 MeV photon energy, the cross section difference Δσ shows an E2 effect of order 50% in the region between 20 and 30 degrees, with the rescattering effect suppressed by about a factor of 5 compared to the E2 effect.
- The cross sections to be measured for the E2 study are approximately dσ⁺/dΩ ≈ 15 μb/sr and dσ⁻/dΩ ≈ 45 μb/sr (assuming pz ≈ 1, pzz ≈ 0).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous impulse approximation study (Wilhelm and Arenhövel, 1996, referred to as I) by adding a full dynamical treatment of pion rescattering, going beyond perturbative approaches used in earlier work such as Osborn (1976), L'vov and Machabeli (1976), and Bosted and Laget (1978). It contrasts with the Faddeev approach of Blaazer, Bakker and Boersma (1993), who found rescattering effects of only a few percent, and with the KMT multiple scattering approach of Kamalov, Tiator and Bennhold (1995), which neglects charged pion exchange and coupling to break-up channels. The results enable improved extraction of the Δ resonance's E2 excitation strength from polarization observables like T₁₁, and suggest that measurement of T₂₀ at backward angles could distinguish between competing theoretical models.
