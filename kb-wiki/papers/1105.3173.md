# Similarity-Transformed Chiral NN+3N Interactions for the Ab Initio Description of 12-C and 16-O

> We present first ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations using similarity renormalization group (SRG) transformed chiral two-nucleon (NN) plus three-nucleon (3N) interactions for nuclei throughout the p-shell, particularly 12-C and 16-O. By introducing an adaptive importance truncation for the NCSM model space and an efficient JT-coupling scheme for the 3N matrix elements, we are able to surpass previous NCSM studies including 3N interactions regarding, both, particle number and model-space size. We present ground and excited states in 12-C and 16-O for model spaces up to N_max=12 including full 3N interactions, which is sufficient to obtain converged results for soft SRG-transformed interactions. We analyze the contributions of induced and initial 3N interactions and probe induced 4N terms through the sensitivity of the energies on the SRG flow parameter. Unlike for light p-shell nuclei, SRG-induced 4N contributions originating from the long-range two-pion terms of the chiral 3N interaction are sizable in 12-C and 16-O.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for 12-C and 16-O using similarity renormalization group (SRG) transformed chiral NN+3N interactions, but the abstract's claim of "converged results" is qualified by the finding that SRG-induced 4N contributions originating from the long-range two-pion terms of the chiral 3N interaction are sizable in these nuclei, unlike for lighter p-shell nuclei.

## Method
The authors performed SRG evolution of chiral NN (N3LO) and 3N (N2LO) interactions, solving the flow equation in a harmonic-oscillator Jacobi-coordinate basis for the three-body system. They then applied the importance-truncated NCSM (IT-NCSM) with an adaptive importance measure and a JT-coupling scheme for 3N matrix elements, enabling calculations up to N_max=12 for 12-C and 16-O. Ground-state energies were extrapolated to infinite model space using exponential fits based on the last 3 or 4 data points, and the flow-parameter dependence was used to diagnose omitted beyond-3N contributions.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state energy of 12-C with NN+3N-full Hamiltonian: -96.1(4) MeV at alpha=0.05 fm^4, -96.8(3) MeV at alpha=0.0625 fm^4, -97.6(1) MeV at alpha=0.08 fm^4 (experimental: -92.16 MeV)
- Ground-state energy of 16-O with NN+3N-full Hamiltonian: -143.7(2) MeV at alpha=0.05 fm^4, -145.6(2) MeV at alpha=0.0625 fm^4, -147.8(1) MeV at alpha=0.08 fm^4 (experimental: -127.62 MeV)
- Ground-state energy of 16-O with NN+3N-induced Hamiltonian: -119.5(3) MeV at alpha=0.05 fm^4, -119.7(6) MeV at alpha=0.0625 fm^4, -119.5(2) MeV at alpha=0.08 fm^4
- Binding energy per nucleon for 16-O with NN+3N-induced Hamiltonian: 7.48(4) MeV, in agreement with a coupled-cluster Lambda-CCSD(T) result of 7.56 MeV for the bare chiral NN interaction (Hagen and Papenbrock, 2010)
- Flow parameter values used: alpha = 0.04, 0.05, 0.0625, 0.08, and 0.16 fm^4, corresponding to momentum scales Lambda = 2.24, 2.11, 2.0, 1.88, and 1.58 fm^-1, respectively
- Model spaces up to N_max=12 were used for 12-C and 16-O, compared to previous NCSM studies limited to N_max=8 (Marrone and Vary, 2011)

## Context
This work extends previous NCSM studies of light p-shell nuclei using SRG-transformed chiral NN+3N interactions (Jurgenson and Navratil, 2009; Jurgenson et al., 2011) to heavier nuclei 12-C and 16-O, surpassing earlier NCSM calculations with full 3N interactions that were limited to N_max=8 (Marrone and Vary, 2011). The finding that SRG-induced 4N contributions are sizable in 12-C and 16-O, driven by the long-range two-pion terms of the chiral 3N interaction, contrasts with the behavior in lighter p-shell nuclei and motivates exploration of alternative SRG generators to suppress induced 4N terms. This enables future detailed ab initio studies of p- and sd-shell nuclei from full chiral NN+3N interactions.
