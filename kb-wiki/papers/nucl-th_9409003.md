# Chiral Symmetry in Nuclear Physics

> The role of chiral symmetry in nuclear physics is summarized. The topics treated are the chiral bag model for nucleon structure resulting from large $N_c$ QCD, the pion cloud in chiral perturbation theory for low-energy electroweak nuclear response functions, ``swelled hadrons" in nuclear matter, chiral symmetry restoration in terms of the Georgi vector limit and pseudo-Goldstone meson condensation in nuclear medium.

# Chiral Symmetry in Nuclear Physics

## Key claim
Chiral symmetry, rather than confinement or asymptotic freedom, is argued to be the dominant QCD ingredient controlling most nuclear phenomena, but this claim is hedged by the recognition that the strange quark's mass (130–180 MeV) is not truly light on the QCD scale, so results based on the lightness assumption for the s quark "may not be very accurate."

## Method
The paper reviews four interconnected theoretical frameworks: (1) the chiral bag model for nucleon structure, derived from large \(N_c\) QCD and formulated as a Cheshire Cat Principle where the bag radius is a gauge artifact; (2) chiral perturbation theory (\(\chi\)PT) in the heavy-baryon formalism applied to nuclear forces and exchange currents, with one-loop calculations using a momentum cut-off; (3) Brown-Rho (BR) scaling, where in-medium effective Lagrangian parameters are scaled by a density-dependent factor \(\Phi(\rho)\) derived from the trace anomaly and a scalar field \(\chi\); and (4) the Georgi vector limit as a description of chiral symmetry restoration at high temperature, where the hidden gauge coupling \(g\) goes to zero and vector mesons become massless.

## Key numbers
- u and d quark masses: less than 10 MeV; s quark mass: 130–180 MeV (relevant scale ~1 GeV, the \(\rho\) meson mass)
- Pion mass: ~140 MeV; kaon mass: ~500 MeV
- Ratio of experimental to single-particle axial-charge matrix element in A=205–208 nuclei: \(M^{exp}/M_1 \sim 1.6\) to 2.0
- Soft-pion two-body contribution relative to single-particle term: \(M_2^{soft}/M_1 \sim 0.6\) to 0.8
- Loop correction to soft-pion term: \(\delta \lesssim 0.1\)
- BR scaling parameter: \(\Phi(\rho) \approx 1 - a(\rho/\rho_0)\) with \(a \approx 0.15\) to 0.2, where \(\rho_0\) is normal nuclear matter density
- Chiral transition temperature from lattice QCD: \(T_c \approx 140\) MeV
- Kaon-nucleon vector repulsion: \(V_{K^+ N} \approx 90\ \text{MeV} \cdot (\rho/\rho_0)\)
- Kaon-nucleon scalar attraction: \(S_{K^+ N} \approx -45\ \text{MeV} \cdot (\rho_s/\rho_0)\)
- Critical density for \(K^-\) condensation: \(\rho_c = (3\ \text{to}\ 4)\rho_0\), where \(\rho_0 \approx \frac{1}{2} m_\pi^3\)

## Context
This 1994 review extends the earlier work of Rho and Brown (1981) arguing for chiral symmetry's central role in nuclei, and the chiral bag model of Rho, Goldhaber, and Brown (1983). It connects to the heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory formalism of Jenkins and Manohar, the nuclear force calculations of Weinberg and van Kolck, and the axial-charge calculations of Kubodera and Park et al. The paper enables a unified framework for understanding nucleon structure, nuclear forces, in-medium hadron properties via BR scaling, and kaon condensation in neutron stars (here called "nuclear stars") as predicted by Kaplan, Nelson, Politzer, and Wise, with refined calculations by Lee, Brown, and Rho.
