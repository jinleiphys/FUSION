# Finite- to zero-range relativistic mean-field interactions

> We study the relation between the finite-range (meson-exchange) and zero-range (point-coupling) representations of effective nuclear interactions in the relativistic mean-field framework. Starting from the phenomenological interaction DD-ME2 with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings, we construct a family of point-coupling effective interactions for different values of the strength parameter of the isoscalar-scalar derivative term. In the meson-exchange picture this corresponds to different values of the $σ$-meson mass. The parameters of the isoscalar-scalar and isovector-vector channels of the point-coupling interactions are adjusted to nuclear matter and ground-state properties of finite nuclei. By comparing results for infinite and semi-infinite nuclear matter, ground-state masses, charge radii, and collective excitations, we discuss constraints on the parameters of phenomenological point-coupling relativistic effective interaction.

## Key claim

The authors construct a family of point-coupling (zero-range) relativistic mean-field interactions starting from the finite-range DD-ME2 meson-exchange interaction, but find that the mapping is not exact in finite nuclei: the surface properties and nuclear matter compression modulus of the point-coupling models differ from those of the parent meson-exchange interaction, and the optimal point-coupling interactions require a lower nuclear matter compression modulus (K_nm ≈ 230 MeV) than the 250-270 MeV range typical of relativistic meson-exchange models.

## Method

Starting from the density-dependent meson-exchange interaction DD-ME2, the authors map the meson-exchange self-energies to point-coupling self-energies via expansion of the Klein-Gordon propagator, yielding strength parameters α_φ = ∓g_φ²/m_φ² and derivative terms δ_φ = ∓g_φ²/m_φ⁴. They then adjust the isoscalar-scalar and isovector-vector parameters of the point-coupling Lagrangian (including the derivative term δ_S) to reproduce nuclear matter properties and ground-state data (binding energies, charge radii, neutron skins) for 12 spherical nuclei, while keeping the isoscalar-vector channel fixed from the nuclear matter mapping. The resulting interactions are tested on semi-infinite nuclear matter surface properties and on collective excitations (giant resonances) using relativistic (quasiparticle) random-phase approximation.

## Key numbers

- The DD-ME2 interaction has an rms error of 0.017 fm for charge radii and charge isotope shifts.
- The microscopic estimate for the derivative coupling δ_S is approximately -0.7 fm⁴.
- For the point-coupling parameter sets B through E, δ_S ranges from -0.80 fm⁴ to -0.86 fm⁴.
- The optimal unconstrained value for δ_S (set F) is -0.8342 fm⁴.
- The DD-ME2 surface thickness is t = 2.108 fm and surface energy is a_s = 17.72 MeV.
- The nuclear matter compression modulus for DD-ME2 is K_nm = 251 MeV; for set F it is K_nm = 250 MeV; for set G it is constrained to K_nm = 230 MeV.
- The four PC interactions reproduce charge radii with deviations generally smaller than 0.5% and binding energies with agreement better than 0.2%.
- The empirical input for nuclear matter fitting uses E/A = -16 MeV (5% error), ρ_sat = 0.153 fm⁻³ (10% error), K_0 = 250 MeV (5% error), and a_4 = 33 MeV (5% error).

## Context

This work extends the density-dependent relativistic mean-field framework by systematically constructing point-coupling equivalents of the successful DD-ME2 meson-exchange interaction (Niksic et al., 2005). It builds on prior point-coupling models (BMM, 2002) and on effective field theory approaches to relativistic point-coupling interactions (Finelli et al., 2003-2006; Fries, 2004). The key finding, that point-coupling interactions require a lower compression modulus (K_nm ≈ 230 MeV) than meson-exchange models (250-270 MeV), narrows the gap between relativistic and nonrelativistic mean-field models (Skyrme forces typically use K_nm = 220-235 MeV, Gogny forces use K_nm ≤ 230 MeV), though the puzzle is not fully resolved. This enables future construction of point-coupling relativistic interactions that can be directly compared with Skyrme and Gogny energy density functionals.
