# Zero-temperature thermodynamics of dense asymmetric strong-interaction matter

> Employing constraints derived from the microscopic theory of the strong interaction, we estimate the zero-temperature phase structure of dense isospin-asymmetric matter with two quark flavors. We find indications that strong-interaction matter along trajectories relevant for astrophysical applications undergoes a first-order phase transition from a color-superconducting phase to an ungapped quark-matter phase when the density is increased. Such a phase transition is found to be absent in isospin-symmetric matter. Moreover, by taking into account constraints from $β$-equilibrium, charge neutrality, and color neutrality, we provide an estimate for the speed of sound in neutron-star matter. Notably, we observe that the speed of sound in neutron-star matter exceeds the asymptotic value associated with the noninteracting quark gas and even increases towards lower densities across a wide range, in agreement with recent results for isospin-symmetric matter. Considering results from studies based on chiral effective field theory at low densities, our findings suggest the existence of a maximum in the speed of sound for $n/n_0 \lesssim 10$, where $n_0$ is the nuclear saturation density.

## Key claim

Using a low-energy model constrained by renormalization-group studies of QCD, the authors find indications that dense isospin-asymmetric strong-interaction matter undergoes a first-order phase transition from a color-superconducting phase to an ungapped quark-matter phase when density is increased, but this transition is absent in isospin-symmetric matter. The speed of sound in neutron-star matter is estimated to exceed the asymptotic value of the noninteracting quark gas and to increase towards lower densities, suggesting a maximum exists for densities below about ten times nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors construct a low-energy model for two-flavor QCD at intermediate and high densities by including a diquark four-quark interaction channel (associated with two-flavor color-superconducting pairing) and a four-diquark interaction channel, with parameters constrained by renormalization-group studies of gluon-induced four-quark interactions. They compute the effective potential in a one-loop approximation with a three-dimensional sharp cutoff, then impose beta-equilibrium, electric charge neutrality, and color neutrality (via chemical potentials mu_3 and mu_8) to obtain the speed of sound in neutron-star matter.

## Key numbers

- The speed of sound in neutron-star matter is found to exceed the asymptotic value associated with the noninteracting quark gas and increases towards lower densities across a wide range.
- The results suggest the existence of a maximum in the speed of sound for n/n_0 less than or approximately equal to 10, where n_0 is the nuclear saturation density.
- The critical isospin-asymmetry parameter delta_mu_cr satisfies delta_mu_cr^2 less than the squared gap in the isospin-symmetric limit, |Delta_gap(mu_bar, delta_mu=0)|^2.
- The effective potential includes contributions from electrons proportional to mu_e^4/(12 pi^2) and from blue spectator quarks proportional to mu_u,b^4/(12 pi^2) and mu_d,b^4/(12 pi^2).

## Context

This work extends the authors' series of studies of dense strong-interaction matter (Braun 2017, 2018, 2019, 2020, 2021) from isospin-symmetric to isospin-asymmetric matter, which is required for astrophysical applications. It builds on renormalization-group analyses of gluon-induced four-quark interactions (Braun 2019, Leonhardt 2019) that identified the diquark channel as dominant at high densities, and on chiral effective field theory results at low densities (Hebeler 2013, Leonhardt 2019). The findings enable improved constraints on the equation of state of neutron-star matter and can serve as a starting point for including strange quarks in future work.
