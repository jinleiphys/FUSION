# Electron-nucleus cross sections from transfer learning

> Transfer learning (TL) allows a deep neural network (DNN) trained on one type of data to be adapted for new problems with limited information. We propose to use the TL technique in physics. The DNN learns the details of one process, and after fine-tuning, it makes predictions for related processes. We consider the DNNs, trained on inclusive electron-carbon scattering data, and show that after fine-tuning, they accurately predict cross sections for electron interactions with nuclear targets ranging from helium-3 to iron.

## Key claim

Transfer learning from a deep neural network trained on inclusive electron-carbon scattering data can accurately predict cross sections for electron interactions with other nuclear targets (helium-3 through iron), provided that even sparsely distributed measurements cover the relevant kinematic region and demonstrate that the same interaction mechanisms apply to the considered targets and to carbon.

## Method

The authors pre-trained an ensemble of 50 deep neural networks (each with ten hidden layers) on inclusive electron-carbon scattering data, then fine-tuned these networks on measurements for other nuclear targets. Two fine-tuning strategies were compared: full fine-tuning (optimizing all network parameters) and shallow fine-tuning (optimizing only the last two layers). The networks take as input the incoming electron energy, energy transfer, scattering angle, cosine of the scattering angle, and four-momentum transfer squared, with the output multiplied by a scale factor of A/12 (where A is the target mass number and 12 is carbon's mass number) before fine-tuning.

## Key numbers

- The pre-trained model required over 40,000 epochs for optimization, while fine-tuning required only 450 to 1,200 epochs
- Data were analyzed for targets including helium-3 (³He), lithium (⁶Li), oxygen (¹⁶O), calcium (⁴⁰Ca), aluminum (²⁷Al), and iron (⁵⁶Fe) for electron energies up to 8 GeV
- Training-to-test dataset splits of 7:3 and 1:9 were used to evaluate performance with limited training data
- For the 1:9 split, the full fine-tuned model reproduced cross section shapes and strengths accurately for almost all targets, while shallow fine-tuning failed for helium-3

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kowal (2023) who developed the bootstrap ensemble of DNNs for electron-carbon scattering, and builds on the consensus articulated by Ankowski (2022) that similarities between electron and neutrino interactions at the nuclear level can reduce neutrino cross section uncertainties. The method addresses the scarcity of electron-scattering measurements for argon and oxygen, which are critical for next-generation neutrino oscillation experiments DUNE and Hyper-Kamiokande, by leveraging the abundant carbon data from the database compiled in RevModPhys.80.189. The technique enables future modeling of neutrino-nucleus cross sections through transfer learning from electron-nucleus data.
