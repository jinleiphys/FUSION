# Dirac Kondo effect under magnetic catalysis

> We develop a mean-field theory of a novel Kondo effect emerging in systems without a Fermi surface, which instead emerges under strong magnetic fields. We determine the magnitude of the Kondo condensate which is a particle pairing composed of conducting Dirac fermions and localized impurities. We focus on the competition between the Kondo effect and the energy gap formation that stems from the pairing among the Dirac fermions leading to the dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. We find that this competition induces a quantum critical point. We also investigate finite-temperature effects. This system at vanishing fermion density can be studied with Monte Carlo lattice simulations which do not suffer from the sign problem.

## Key claim

The paper develops a mean-field theory of a Kondo effect that occurs in systems without a Fermi surface, instead emerging under strong magnetic fields at vanishing fermion density. The key hedge is that this "Dirac Kondo effect" relies on magnetic catalysis, meaning the Kondo condensate grows only when the magnetic field exceeds a critical strength, and its formation competes destructively with the chiral condensate that arises from dynamical chiral symmetry breaking.

## Method

The authors constructed a (3+1)-dimensional Lagrangian with four-point interactions between light Dirac fermions (confined to the lowest Landau level) and nonrelativistic heavy impurity fermions, analogous to a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. They applied a mean-field approximation to compute the thermodynamic potential at one-loop level, assuming homogeneous chiral and Kondo condensates, and solved the stationary conditions analytically and numerically to determine the ground state as a function of magnetic-field strength and temperature.

## Key numbers

- Critical magnetic-field strength for the quantum critical point: \( q_l B_c = \frac{4\pi^3}{G_{hl} \, \arcsinh\left( \Lambda \frac{|G_{hl}-G_{ll}|}{m_l G_{hl}} \right)} \), which simplifies to \( q_l B_c \sim \frac{4\pi^3 m_l}{\Lambda |G_{hl}-G_{ll}|} \) for small \( |G_{hl}-G_{ll}| \).
- The Kondo condensate above the critical field is given by \( \Delta_2 = \frac{1}{2} \sqrt{ \frac{\Lambda^2}{\sinh^2(2\pi^2 / \rho_B G_{hl})} - \frac{m_l^2 G_{hl}^2}{(G_{hl}-G_{ll})^2} } \), where \( \rho_B = |q_l B|/(2\pi) \) is the Landau degeneracy factor.
- The chiral condensate above the critical field saturates at a constant value \( M_2 = \frac{m_l G_{ll}}{G_{hl}-G_{ll}} \).
- Numerical results used parameters \( m_l/\Lambda = 0.01 \), \( G_{ll}\Lambda^2 = 1.0 \), and \( G_{hl}\Lambda^2 = 3.0 \) for the figures showing magnetic-field and temperature dependences.

## Context

This work extends the concept of magnetic catalysis, originally established for chiral condensate formation by Gusynin et al. (1994-1995) and later applied to the Kondo condensate by Ozaki (2015), to a system where both condensates compete. It builds on prior mean-field studies of the QCD Kondo effect by Yasui et al. (2016-2017) and Suzuki et al. (2017), which examined competition at finite baryon density, whereas this paper considers vanishing density. The results enable future lattice Monte Carlo simulations (which avoid the sign problem at zero density) and experimental studies in Dirac semimetals or twisted bilayer graphene systems, where the magnetic-field-induced Kondo effect could be observed.
