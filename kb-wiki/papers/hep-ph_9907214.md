# Perturbative Pion Wave function in Coherent Pion-Nucleon Di-Jet Production

> A perturbative QCD treatment of the pion wave function is applied to computing the scattering amplitude for coherent high relative momentum di-jet production from a nucleon.

# Perturbative Pion Wave function in Coherent Pion-Nucleon Di-Jet Production

## Key claim

The authors show that leading-order perturbative QCD can be used to compute the scattering amplitude for coherent di-jet production from a nucleon, and that the amplitude is dominated by the term they computed in 1993 (Frankfurt, Miller, Strikman 1993), with additional contributions (T2, T3, T4) being suppressed by color coherent effects. The worry raised by Jennings and Miller (1994) that the T2 term might cause a near-complete cancellation with T1 is not validated; instead, T2 has the same sign as T1 and is typically about 50% as large.

## Method

The authors compute the forward scattering amplitude for the process πN → N JJ (two jets) using the Brodsky-Lepage analysis of the pion wave function in light-cone gauge, retaining only the one-gluon exchange contribution to the effective interaction. The soft interaction with the target nucleon is modeled via two-gluon exchange, expressed as a b²-dependent operator acting on the high-momentum component of the pion wave function, with the skewed gluon distribution of the nucleon entering through the QCD factorization theorem. The authors evaluate the leading term T1 and the final-state interaction term T2, using the asymptotic quark distribution amplitude φ(x) = a₀ x(1-x) with a₀ = √3 fπ.

## Key numbers

- Beam momentum: approximately 500 GeV/c for the incident pion
- Transverse relative momentum of the di-jet: κ⊥ > 1-2 GeV/c
- Pion decay constant: fπ ≈ 93 MeV, used to set a₀ = √3 fπ in the asymptotic distribution amplitude
- The differential cross section scales as dσ(κ⊥)/dκ⊥² ∝ 1/κ⊥⁸
- The amplitude T2 is typically about 50% as large as T1, with a maximum factor of 0.693 at x = 0.5 from the logarithmic term 2[(1-x)ln(1/(1-x)) + x ln(1/x)]
- The asymptotic quark distribution amplitude is valid for k⊥² ≥ 2-3 GeV²
- The parameter λ(x=10⁻³) = 9 in the gluon distribution function (from Frankfurt, Koepf, and Strikman 1996)
- QCD scale parameter: Λ ≈ 0.2 GeV, used in the 1/ln(κ⊥²/Λ²) correction term

## Context

This work extends the 1993 prediction by Frankfurt, Miller, and Strikman (1993) for coherent di-jet production, incorporating perturbative QCD to compute the high-momentum component of the pion wave function rather than relying on a factorization assumption. The calculation is motivated by preliminary experimental results from Fermilab experiment E791 (Weiss-Babai 1997, Ashery 1998) comparing Pt and C targets, which show an A-dependence of approximately A¹·⁵⁵±⁰·⁰⁵, qualitatively similar to the 1993 prediction and much stronger than the A¹/³ behavior suggested by Bertsch, Brodsky, Goldhaber, and Gunion (1981). The work enables future detailed comparison with experiment and provides a framework for computing the κ⊥⁻⁸ behavior of the cross section as a definitive test of the color transparency mechanism.
