# Transverse spin effects and light-quark dipole moments at lepton colliders

> We propose to probe light-quark dipole interactions at lepton colliders using the azimuthal asymmetry of a collinear dihadron pair $(h_1h_2)$ produced in association with another hadron $h'$. This asymmetry, arising from quantum interference in the quark spin space, is exclusively sensitive to dipole interactions at the leading power of the new physics scale and simultaneously probes both the real and imaginary components of the dipole couplings. By combining all possible channels of $h'$, this method allows for disentangling the up and down quark dipole moments and has the potential to significantly strengthen current constraints by one to two orders of magnitude.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the azimuthal asymmetry of a collinear dihadron pair produced in association with a third hadron at lepton colliders is exclusively sensitive to light-quark dipole interactions at leading power in the new physics scale, and that combining multiple associated hadron channels can disentangle up and down quark dipole moments. The hedge is that this method requires the dihadron pair to be close in angle while widely separated from the third hadron, and that the current analysis relies on dihadron fragmentation functions only available for the \((\pi^+\pi^-)\) channel.

## Method

The authors consider the inclusive associated production process \(e^- e^+ \to [h_1 h_2] + h' + X\) in the center-of-mass frame, where the dihadron pair \((h_1 h_2)\) is collinear and the third hadron \(h'\) is well separated. They factorize the cross section using dihadron fragmentation functions (DiFFs) and single-hadron fragmentation functions, where the transverse spin of the fragmenting quark induces a \(\phi_R\) modulation in the dihadron azimuthal distribution. The transverse spin is generated by the interference between Standard Model amplitudes and dipole interaction amplitudes at \(\mathcal{O}(1/\Lambda^2)\), and the real and imaginary parts of the dipole couplings are separately probed by the \(\sin\phi_R\) and \(\cos\phi_R\) modulations, respectively. The analysis uses three associated hadron channels (\(h' = \pi^\pm, K^\pm, p/\bar{p}\)) to provide different flavor combinations that allow disentangling up and down quark dipole couplings.

## Key numbers

- Expected upper limits of \(\mathcal{O}(10^{-2})\) for \(\Gamma_{\gamma}^{u,d}\) (photon dipole couplings) at \(\sqrt{s} = 10\) GeV with polarized electron beam (\(\lambda_\ell = 0.8\)) and \(\mathcal{L} = 1000\) fb\(^{-1}\)
- Expected upper limits of \(\mathcal{O}(10^{-3})\) for \(\Gamma_{Z}^{u,d}\) (Z-boson dipole couplings) at \(\sqrt{s} = 91\) GeV (Z-pole) with unpolarized beams and \(\mathcal{L} = 1000\) fb\(^{-1}\)
- Kinematic selection: \(z \in (0.4, 0.8)\), \(\bar{z} \in (0.2, 0.8)\), and \(M_h \in (0.4, 1.4)\) GeV
- Relative uncertainties from DiFFs in the spin asymmetries are estimated at less than 20%
- The method can strengthen current constraints from Drell-Yan and other LHC processes by one to two orders of magnitude

## Context

This work extends the transverse spin approach previously applied to electron-proton collisions at the Electron-Ion Collider by Wen (2024) and Boughezal (2023), where the quark transversity distribution from initial-state polarized protons was used. The lepton collider version is a crossed process of the \(ep\) dihadron production, offering a much cleaner background and the ability to use multiple associated hadron channels. Unlike the \(ep\) case which only constrains a specific flavor combination, this method enables separate constraints on up and down quark dipole couplings for the first time at a lepton collider. The approach complements constraints from Drell-Yan, Higgs boson production via vector boson fusion, diboson production, and electroweak precision observables, which only constrain dipole couplings quadratically at \(\mathcal{O}(1/\Lambda^4)\) and cannot distinguish real from imaginary parts.
