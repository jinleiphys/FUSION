# Local gauge invariance implies Siegert's hypothesis

> The nonrelativistic Ward-Takahashi identity, a consequence of local gauge invariance in quantum mechanics, shows the necessity of exchange current contributions in case of nonlocal and/or isospin-dependent potentials. It also implies Siegert's hypothesis: in the nonrelativistic limit, two-body charge densities identically vanish. Neither current conservation, which follows from global gauge invariance, nor the constraints of (lowest order) relativity are sufficient to arrive at this result. Furthermore, a low-energy theorem for exchange contributions is established.

# Local gauge invariance implies Siegert's hypothesis

## Key claim
The nonrelativistic Ward-Takahashi identity, derived from local gauge invariance, proves that two-body charge densities identically vanish in the nonrelativistic limit (Siegert's hypothesis), whereas current conservation alone, which follows from global gauge invariance, does not exclude modifications to the charge density.

## Method
The author starts from the nonrelativistic Ward-Takahashi identity relating electromagnetic charge and current operators to the full Green's function of the interacting N-particle system. By subtracting the noninteracting version of this identity and exploiting the fact that none of the operators depend on energy, the author shows that the exchange charge density must vanish identically for arbitrary off-shell energies, while the exchange current is generally nonzero. The argument is contrasted with the weaker constraint from current conservation alone, which allows nonvanishing two-body charge densities.

## Key numbers
- The exchange charge density ρ_e is proven to vanish "up to order v/c", where v is a typical velocity in the system.
- The exchange current j_e is of the same order as the one-body current j_0, both O(v/c), while relativistic corrections to the current are O((v/c)^3).
- Relativistic corrections to the one-body charge density ρ_0 are O((v/c)^2), and the lowest-order relativity constraints would allow an exchange charge density of O(1), i.e., nonrelativistic order, if not excluded by local gauge invariance.

## Context
This work extends the derivation of the nonrelativistic Ward-Takahashi identity by Naus and collaborators (Nabo, year not given) and proves the low-energy theorem proposed in that same reference. It directly addresses a claim by Hyuga (year not given) that two-boson exchange modifies the charge density, contradicting Siegert's hypothesis. The paper also contrasts with the approach of Friar (year not given), who classified exchange currents using lowest-order relativity constraints but could not exclude two-body charge densities a priori. The result provides a theoretical foundation for the phenomenologically well-established Siegert hypothesis, which is widely used in nuclear electromagnetic calculations (see, e.g., Arenhovel, year not given).
