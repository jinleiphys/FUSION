# An accurate relativistic chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction up to the next-to-next-to-leading order

> We report on the construction of an accurate relativistic chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction up to the next-to-next-to-leading (NNLO) order. We compare the so-obtained neutron-proton phaseshifts with the next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO) nonrelativistic ones and we show that up to $T_\mathrm{lab.}=200$ MeV, the relativistic chiral nuclear force can describe the PWA93 phaseshifts and inelasticities as well as its N$^3$LO nonrelativistic counterparts. As a result, the relativistic chiral nuclear force can be readily used for relativistic ab initio nuclear structure, reaction, as well astrophysical studies.

## Key claim

The authors construct a relativistic chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) that describes neutron-proton phase shifts up to T_lab = 200 MeV as well as nonrelativistic N^3LO forces do, but the claim is hedged by the fact that the fit is performed only for partial waves with total angular momentum J ≤ 2, while peripheral waves are predictions rather than fitted results.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic Blankenbecler-Sugar (BbS) equation using a chiral potential composed of contact terms at LO and NLO, one-pion exchange, leading and subleading two-pion exchange contributions, and an iterated one-pion exchange subtraction term. They perform a global fit to the neutron-proton phase shifts from PWA93 for all partial waves with J ≤ 2, using eight data points per partial wave at laboratory kinetic energies T_lab = 1, 5, 10, 25, 50, 100, 150, and 200 MeV, minimizing a chi-squared-like function. The potential is regularized with a sharp cutoff theta function at Lambda = 0.9 GeV for the NNLO results.

## Key numbers

- The relativistic NNLO chiral force describes np phase shifts up to T_lab = 200 MeV at a level similar to nonrelativistic N^3LO results.
- The fit uses eight data points per partial wave at T_lab = 1, 5, 10, 25, 50, 100, 150, and 200 MeV.
- The cutoff for the NNLO relativistic results is Lambda = 0.9 GeV; the NLO relativistic results use a smaller cutoff of Lambda = 0.6 GeV.
- The pion decay constant is taken as f_pi = 92.4 MeV, the axial coupling constant g_A = 1.29, the nucleon mass m_n = 939 MeV, and the pion mass m_pi = 139 MeV.
- The low-energy constants used are c1 = -1.39, c2 = 4.01, c3 = -6.61, c4 = 3.92, all in units of GeV^{-1}.

## Context

This work extends the covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory approach previously applied at leading order by Ren et al. (2016) and Xiao et al. (2018) to construct a relativistic NN interaction up to NNLO. It provides the first accurate relativistic chiral NN interaction suitable for relativistic ab initio nuclear structure and reaction studies, complementing the nonrelativistic chiral forces such as the N^3LO forces of Entem and Machleidt (2003) and Epelbaum et al. (2014). The results enable future applications to relativistic nuclear structure, reactions, and astrophysical studies, as well as extensions to hyperon-nucleon interactions relevant for neutron star physics.
