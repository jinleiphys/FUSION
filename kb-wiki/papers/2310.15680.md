# Dissociation and thermodynamical properties of heavy quarkonia in an anisotropic strongly coupled hot QGP: using baryonic chemical potential

> We extended the recent work Phys. Rev. D 97(9), 094033 (2018) to investigate quarkonium dissociation in presence of baryonic chemical potential (mu_b) and anisotropy (ξ) using quasi-particle approach in hot quantum chromodynamics (QCD) medium. We have determined binding energy and thermal width of S-states of charmonia and bottomonia for n=1 and n=2 (radial quantum number) with anisotropic parameter (ξ) and baryonic chemical potential. We have also determined the effects of baryonic chemical potential and anisotropy on mass spectra of 1S-states of quarkonia and the results obtained were consistent with theoretical and experimental works. But the key result obtained was dissociation temperature of the S-states with the effect of {mu_b} and ξ. At last, we have calculated the thermodynamical properties of QGP (i.e., pressure, energy density and speed of sound) using the parameter ξ and {mu_b}, which is the main key to study suppression of the quarkonium with latest determined value of energy density psNN after incorporating the effect of ξ and (mu_b).

## Key claim

The authors extend a quasi-particle approach from Phys. Rev. D 97(9), 094033 (2018) to study quarkonium dissociation in a hot QCD medium, claiming that both baryonic chemical potential (mu_b) and momentum-space anisotropy (xi) significantly modify the dissociation temperatures and thermodynamical properties of the quark-gluon plasma, with the caveat that the results depend on the specific choice of equation of state (EoS1 or EoS2) and the perturbative treatment of anisotropy.

## Method

The authors use an effective fugacity quasi-particle model to compute the Debye screening mass as a function of temperature and baryonic chemical potential, then incorporate momentum-space anisotropy through a stretched/squeezed distribution function. They modify the Cornell potential via Fourier transform with a dielectric permittivity derived from the static gluon propagator, obtaining real and imaginary parts of the heavy quark potential. Binding energies are computed by solving the Schrodinger equation to first order in the anisotropy parameter, and dissociation temperatures are determined using both the mean thermal energy criterion (B.E. = T_D or 3T_D) and the thermal width criterion (2B.E. < Gamma).

## Context

This work extends the quasi-particle approach of Phys. Rev. D 97(9), 094033 (2018) by adding baryonic chemical potential alongside momentum anisotropy, building on earlier studies of quarkonium suppression by Matsui and Satz (1986) and subsequent work on anisotropic QGP by various authors. The results enable more realistic modeling of quarkonium dissociation in non-central heavy-ion collisions at finite baryon density, relevant for experiments at RHIC and the future FAIR facility, and provide a framework for comparing with lattice QCD results on the heavy quark free energy.
