# Study of Nucleon Resonances with Double Polarization Observables of Pion Photoproduction

> The role of the nucleon resonances in the double polarization observables of pion photoproduction is investigated by using the resonance parameters predicted by Capstick and Roberts. As an example, we show that the not-well-determined two-star resonance N$^{-}_{3/2}$(1960) can be examined by performing experiments on beam-recoil polarization at large angles.

## Key claim

Using resonance parameters from the Capstick and Roberts constituent quark model, the double polarization observable beam-recoil polarization at large angles can isolate the poorly-determined two-star resonance N$^{-}_{3/2}$(1960) from the background of other resonances, even though the unpolarized differential cross section shows little sensitivity to individual nucleon resonances.

## Method

The authors constructed a pion photoproduction amplitude by combining a background amplitude extracted from the SAID program's empirical multipole fits (SM01 solution) with resonant amplitudes calculated from the Capstick and Roberts quark model predictions for 30 N* and 26 Delta* resonances. They computed the beam-target polarization C$^{BT}_{zy}$ and beam-recoil polarization C$^{BR}_{zy}$ for the reactions $\gamma p \rightarrow \pi^+ n$, $\gamma n \rightarrow \pi^- p$, and $\gamma p \rightarrow \pi^0 p$ in the invariant mass range 1.6 to 2.1 GeV, using a fixed cutoff parameter Lambda = 650 MeV and average widths of 300 MeV for N* and 120 MeV for Delta* resonances.

- The study covers the invariant mass range 1.6 GeV less than W less than 2.1 GeV, corresponding to photon laboratory energies from approximately 1.6 to 2.1 GeV.
- The cutoff parameter Lambda was set to 650 MeV for all resonances, based on the value determined in the investigation of Delta to pi N decay by Sato and Lee (1996).
- The total width Gamma_0 was set to 300 MeV for all N* resonances and 120 MeV for all Delta* resonances.
- The N$^{-}_{3/2}$(1960) resonance has a mass of 1960 MeV, helicity amplitudes A_{1/2} = +36 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2} and A_{3/2} = -43 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2}, and a partial-wave transition strength G(2) = 8.2 MeV^{1/2}.
- At c.m. angle 120 degrees, adding only the N$^{-}_{3/2}$(1960) resonance to the background plus four-star resonances shifted the predicted beam-recoil polarization from the dashed curves to the dot-dashed curves, nearly matching the full calculation including all 56 resonances.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical formulation of Sato and Lee (1996) for the Delta resonance to higher energies where many "missing resonances" predicted by constituent quark models have not been observed in pion-nucleon scattering, as first noted by Koniuk and Isgur (1980). The authors argue that double polarization observables in pion photoproduction are more sensitive to these missing states than unpolarized cross sections or single polarization observables, complementing ongoing experiments on vector meson photoproduction (Oh, Titov, and Lee, 2001) and kaon photoproduction. The results suggest that experiments at Jefferson Lab and Spring-8 could test the Capstick and Roberts predictions for the two-star resonance N$^{-}_{3/2}$(1960) through beam-recoil polarization measurements in the $\gamma p \rightarrow \pi^+ n$ channel.
