# Study of the effects of Pauli blocking and Pauli non-locality on the optical potential

> Elastic scattering angular distributions for systems with reduced mass between 3 and 34 and energies varying between 25 and 120 MeV/nucleon were analyzed. The stable $^4$He, its exotic partner $^6$He, and the weakly bound $^{6,7}$Li nuclei were included as projectiles in the systematics. Optical model data analyzes were performed with an adjustable factor of normalization included in the imaginary part of the potential. These analyzes indicated a reduction of absorption for systems with small reduced masses that was detected due to the refractive nature of the scattering by light systems.

# Study of the effects of Pauli blocking and Pauli non-locality on the optical potential

## Key claim

The reduction of absorption observed for light nuclear systems with small reduced masses is compatible with the expected effect of Pauli blocking, but the analysis cannot discern whether this behavior is also connected with effects of Pauli non-locality on the imaginary part or Pauli blocking on the real part of the optical potential.

## Method

Elastic scattering angular distributions for systems with reduced masses between 3 and 34 at energies from 25 to 120 MeV/nucleon were analyzed using the Sao Paulo potential for the real part (which includes Pauli non-locality via a velocity-dependent exponential factor) and the Lax interaction for the imaginary part (based on the nucleon-nucleon total cross section). An adjustable normalization factor N_I was introduced in the imaginary part of the optical potential, and notch tests were performed to determine the radial region of sensitivity for each system.

## Key numbers

- Reduced mass range of analyzed systems: 3 to 34 (dimensionless, units not specified in text)
- Energy range of analyzed data: 25 to 120 MeV/nucleon
- Matter density radius parameterization: R_0 = 1.31 A^(1/3) - 0.84 fm, with average diffuseness a = 0.56 fm
- Normalization factor N_I ≈ 0.6 obtained for the ^12C + ^12C system
- Energy range for ^12C + ^12C reaction data: 25 ≤ E ≤ 120 MeV/nucleon, corresponding to 300 ≤ E_Lab ≤ 1440 MeV
- RMS radii for matter densities agree with charge distributions within about 5%, except for ^12C where a difference of 10% was found
- Diffuseness of ^6He was much greater than that of ^4He, comparable to values obtained for heavy ions

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of heavy-ion optical potentials using the Sao Paulo potential approach previously developed by the same group (Alvarez et al., earlier work). The analysis includes stable ^4He, the exotic halo nucleus ^6He, and the weakly bound ^6,7Li projectiles, building on prior studies of nuclear rainbow scattering (noted as "nova4" in the text) and the Lax interaction approach (Ref. 15 in the paper). The results enable better understanding of how Pauli blocking and Pauli non-locality affect the optical potential at different internuclear distances, particularly for light systems where the scattering probes internal regions.
