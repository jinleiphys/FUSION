# Hydro+Cascade, Flow, the Equation of State, Predictions and Data

> A Hydro+Cascade model has been used to describe radial and elliptic flow at the SPS and successfully predicted the radial and elliptic flow measured by the both STAR and PHENIX collaborations . # Hydro+Cascade, Flow, the Equation of State, Predictions and Data

## Key claim

A combined analysis of radial and elliptic flow for different particle species restricts the Equation of State (EoS) and points toward an EoS with a phase transition to the Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP), but the model-data discrepancy for EoSs other than the best-fit LH8 is not large at SPS energies; the differences between EoSs become magnified only at RHIC energies where the QGP phase contribution increases.

## Method

The authors used a hybrid Hydro+Cascade model in which a hydrodynamic evolution is followed by a microscopic transport cascade starting at a switching temperature near the critical temperature (approximately 165 MeV). They studied a family of EoSs with first-order phase transitions labeled by latent heat values (LH4, LH8, LH16, LH∞) and a Resonance Gas (RG) EoS without a phase transition, comparing model predictions for transverse mass slope parameters and elliptic flow (v₂) to SPS data from NA49 and to preliminary RHIC data from STAR and PHENIX.

## Key numbers

- Elliptic flow (v₂) at RHIC was predicted to increase by approximately 40% over the SPS value, a prediction borne out by the first STAR measurements.
- The switching temperature used in the model is T_switch ≈ T_c ≈ 165 MeV.
- Latent heat values studied: LH4 = 0.4 GeV/fm³, LH8 = 0.8 GeV/fm³, LH16 = 1.6 GeV/fm³, and LH∞ as a limiting case.
- At the SPS, pure hydrodynamics calculations gave v₂ a factor of two above the data; the Hydro+Cascade model reduced this to only 20% above the data.
- STAR fits slope parameters over ranges: π⁻ 0.12–0.45 GeV (M_T − m), K⁻ 0.04–0.34 GeV, and antiprotons 0.04–0.45 GeV.
- PHENIX fits slope parameters over ranges: π⁻ 0.19–0.87 GeV (M_T − m) and protons 0.175–2.2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier Hydro+Cascade approach of Bass and Dumitru (2000), who first removed the freezeout temperature indeterminacy by injecting particles into a microscopic transport model at T_switch. The present study goes further by simultaneously reproducing both radial and elliptic flow for multiple particle species as a function of impact parameter, which was not possible with pure hydrodynamics (Kolb, 1999). The results enable parameter-free predictions for RHIC that can test whether the hydrodynamic description must be taken seriously, implying that equilibration times and transport cross sections from perturbative parton cascades are only a coarse guide to the non-perturbative quantum glue of the initial state.
