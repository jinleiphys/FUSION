# Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio phase diagrams and quarkyonic phase from order parameters

> We show that the magnitude of the order parameters in Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, given by the quark condensate and the Polyakov loop, can be used as a criterium to clearly identify, without ambiguities, phases and boundaries of the strongly interacting matter, namely, the broken/restored chiral symmetry, and confinement/deconfinement regions. This structure is represented by the projection of the order parameters in the temperature-chemical potential plane, which allows a clear identification of pattern changes in the phase diagram. Such a criterium also enables the emergence of a quarkyonic phase even in the two-flavor system. We still show that this new phase diminishes due to the influence of an additional vector-type interaction in the PNJL phase diagrams, and is quite sensitive to the effect of the change of the $T_0$ parameter in the Polyakov potential. Finally, we show that the phases and boundaries constructed by our method indicate that the order parameters should be more strongly correlated, as in the case of entanglement PNJL (EPNJL) model. This result suggests a novel way to pursue further investigation of new interactions between the order parameters in order to improve the PNJL model.

## Key claim

The magnitude of the order parameters (quark condensate and Polyakov loop) in the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model can serve as a criterion to unambiguously identify phases and boundaries of strongly interacting matter, including a quarkyonic phase in the two-flavor system, but the precise boundaries depend on the chosen fixed values of the order parameters (e.g., \(\rho_s/\rho_{s(\text{vac})}=1/2\) and \(\Phi=0.4\)) and are sensitive to the vector interaction strength and the \(T_0\) parameter in the Polyakov potential.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled mean-field equations of the PNJL model for the constituent quark mass \(M\) and the Polyakov loop \(\Phi\) at each point in the temperature-chemical potential (\(T\)-\(\mu_q\)) plane, using the saddle point approach with the assumption \(\Phi=\Phi^*\) (real fields). They then projected the order parameter surfaces (quark condensate \(\rho_s\) and Polyakov loop \(\Phi\)) onto the \(T\)-\(\mu_q\) plane and defined phase boundaries by fixed values of \(\rho_s/\rho_{s(\text{vac})}=1/2\) for chiral symmetry and \(\Phi=0.4\) for confinement/deconfinement, comparing this method to the conventional peak-derivative criterion. The analysis was performed for the RRW06 Polyakov potential parametrization, with additional studies including a vector interaction term (\(G_V\)) and rescaling of the \(T_0\) parameter, as well as the entanglement PNJL (EPNJL) model.

## Key numbers

* For the RRW06 parametrization at \(\mu_q=0\), the maxima of \(\partial\rho_s/\partial T\) and \(\partial\Phi/\partial T\) occur at \(T\sim 220\) MeV.
* The vacuum parameters used are \(\Lambda = 651\) MeV, \(m_0 = 5.5\) MeV, and \(G_s=5.04\) GeV\(^{-2}\), reproducing \(m_\pi=140.51\) MeV, \(f_\pi=94.04\) MeV, and \(|\langle\bar{u}u\rangle|^{1/3}=251.32\) MeV.
* The Polyakov potential parameter \(T_0\) is set to 270 MeV for the pure gluon sector; rescaling to \(T_0=205\) MeV yields a transition temperature at \(\mu_q=0\) compatible with lattice QCD results (\(173\pm 8\) MeV), with boundaries defined by \(\Phi=0.44\) and \(\rho_s/\rho_{s(\text{vac})}=0.77\).
* For the vector interaction case with \(G_V=0.2G_s\), the double peak structure in \(\partial\Phi/\partial T\) disappears, leaving only one peak.
* The quarkyonic phase region diminishes as the vector coupling \(G_V\) is increased, with the boundary moving toward higher \(\mu_q\) values.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group (Dutra et al., 2012; Dutra et al., 2013) on PNJL phase diagrams, proposing a new method based on order parameter magnitude rather than the conventional peak-derivative criterion used by Ratti et al. (2006) and Costa et al. (2009). The method enables the identification of a quarkyonic phase (McLerran and Pisarski, 2007) even in the two-flavor PNJL model, which was previously only seen in SU(3) versions (Fukushima, 2008). The results suggest that order parameters should be more strongly correlated, as in the entanglement PNJL (EPNJL) model, and provide a framework for studying quark matter in compact stars and future experiments at FAIR and NICA.
