# Overlap functions in correlation methods and quasifree nucleon knockout from $^{16}$O

> The cross sections of the ($e,e'N$) and ($γ,p$) reactions on $^{16}$O are calculated, for the transitions to the $1/2^{-}$ ground state and the first $3/2^{-}$ excited state of the residual nucleus, using single-particle overlap functions obtained on the basis of one-body density matrices within different correlation methods. The electron-induced one-nucleon knockout reaction is treated within a nonrelativistic DWIA framework. The theoretical treatment of the ($γ,p$) reaction includes both contributions of the direct knockout mechanism and of meson-exchange currents. The results are sensitive to details of the different overlap functions. The consistent analysis of the reaction cross sections and the comparison with the experimental data make it possible to study the nucleon--nucleon correlation effects.

## Key claim

The cross sections of ($e,e'N$) and ($\gamma,p$) reactions on $^{16}$O are sensitive to details of different single-particle overlap functions derived from correlation methods, but the comparison with experimental data is limited by the fact that the overlap functions used do not include long-range correlations, which would produce further depletion of quasihole states.

## Method

The authors calculated reduced cross sections for the $^{16}$O($e,e'n$) and $^{16}$O($e,e'p$) reactions using a nonrelativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) framework with the code DWEEPY, employing single-particle overlap functions extracted from one-body density matrices obtained via four different correlation methods: Jastrow correlation method, Correlated Basis Function theory, a model using Fermi Hypernetted Chain Technique, and the Green function method. For the $^{16}$O($\gamma,p$) reaction, they used a theoretical treatment that includes both direct knockout mechanism and meson-exchange currents from seagull diagrams, with the same overlap functions and spectroscopic factors.

## Key numbers

- The transitions studied are to the $1/2^{-}$ ground state and the first $3/2^{-}$ excited state of the residual nucleus, at excitation energy $E_{\mathrm x}=6.18$ MeV for $^{15}$O and $E_{\mathrm x}=6.3$ MeV for $^{15}$N
- The ($\gamma,p$) reaction calculations cover the photon-energy range between 50 and 100 MeV
- Spectroscopic factors from different correlation methods are listed in Table I (column I) of the paper, with values lower for overlap functions including tensor correlations compared to those with only short-range central correlations
- The Hartree-Fock wave function used for comparison has a norm equal to one, while all correlated overlap functions contain spectroscopic factors accounting for correlation-induced depletion

## Context

This work extends previous studies of overlap functions extracted from one-body density matrices that were applied to $^{16}$O($p,d$) and $^{40}$Ca($p,d$) pickup reactions by Gaidarov et al. (1998, 1999) and Dimitrova et al. (1997). It builds on earlier investigations of correlation effects in $^{16}$O($e,e'p$) reactions using spectral functions containing short-range correlations by Picklesimer and Van Orden (1989) and long-range correlations by Amirov et al. (1990). The consistent analysis of both ($e,e'p$) and ($\gamma,p$) reactions with the same theoretical ingredients enables testing overlap functions over a wider range of momenta, particularly at large values where correlation effects are expected to be more sizable, and allows comparison with experimental data from NIKHEF (Leuschner et al., 1994).
