## Key claim

The authors present a momentum-space framework for calculating inclusive (anti)neutrino scattering cross sections on deuteron and trinucleon targets, but they restrict themselves to the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential and a single-nucleon weak current operator, explicitly stating that they "definitely cannot reach yet the high level of accuracy achieved by the predictions" of prior work that included two-nucleon currents. The observed disagreement with the results of Shen et al. (2012) is attributed to differences in kinematics and in the weak current operator.

## Method

The authors solve the nuclear matrix elements in momentum space for seventeen charged-current and neutral-current reactions on ôýH, ô°He, and ô°H, using the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential and a single-nucleon weak current operator with relativistic corrections. For deuteron reactions, they compare two-nucleon scattering states obtained in momentum and coordinate spaces and study relativistic effects in the final state kinematics by contrasting relativistic and nonrelativistic phase space factors and threshold energies. For trinucleon reactions, they compute examples of response functions in addition to total cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Threshold energy for \(\bar{\nu}_e + d \rightarrow e^+ + n + n\): 4.03323 MeV (relativistic and both nonrelativistic prescriptions agree to this precision)
- Threshold energy for \(\nu_e + d \rightarrow e^- + p + p\): 1.44279 MeV (relativistic and both nonrelativistic prescriptions agree)
- Threshold energy for \(\nu_l + d \rightarrow \nu_l + p + n\): 2.22589 MeV (relativistic and nonrelativistic prescription \({\cal E}_{thr}^{nrl}\) agree)
- At initial neutrino energy \(E = 300\) MeV and lepton scattering angle \(\theta = \pi/2\), the relativistic phase space factor exceeds the nonrelativistic one by more than 20% for small outgoing lepton momenta \(k^\prime\)
- At initial neutrino energy \(E = 50\) MeV and \(\theta = \pi/2\), the relativistic phase space factor exceeds the nonrelativistic one by about 3%
- The maximal difference between relativistic and nonrelativistic maximal outgoing lepton momentum at \(E = 300\) MeV does not reach 0.5%

## Context

This work extends the momentum-space treatment of neutrino-nucleus reactions to trinucleon systems (ô°He and ô°H), complementing prior coordinate-space calculations by Shen et al. (2012) for the deuteron and by Nakamura et al. (2001, 2002) for energies up to 150 MeV. The authors explicitly compare their results with Shen et al. (2012) and attribute disagreements to kinematic and current operator differences. The framework is designed to be ready for future incorporation of chiral effective field theory potentials and current operators, which are typically formulated in momentum space, and may serve as a benchmark for three-dimensional calculations that avoid partial wave decomposition.
