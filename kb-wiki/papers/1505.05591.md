# $^{10}$B+$־±$ states with chain-like structures in $^{14}$N

> I investigate $^{10}$B+$־±$ cluster states of $^{14}$N with a $^{10}$B+$־±$ cluster model. Near the $־±$-decay threshold energy, I obtain $K^ֿ€=3^+$ and $K^ֿ€=1^+$ rotational bands having $^{10}$B($3^+$)+$־±$ and $^{10}$B($1^+$)+$־±$ components, respectively. I assign the band-head state of the $K^ֿ€=3^+$ band to the experimental $3^+$ at $E_x$=13.19 MeV of $^{14}$N observed in $־±$ scattering reactions by $^{10}$B and show that the calculated $־±$-decay width is consistent with the experimental data. I discuss an $־±$-cluster motion around the $^{10}$B cluster and show that $^{10}$B+$־±$ cluster states contain significant components of a linear-chain 3$־±$ configuration, in which an $־±$ cluster is localized in the longitudinal direction around the deformed $^{10}$B cluster.

## Key claim

The paper claims that $^{10}$B+$\alpha$ cluster states in $^{14}$N near the $\alpha$-decay threshold contain significant components of a linear-chain $3\alpha$ configuration, but this assignment is tentative and depends on the calculated $\alpha$-decay width of the $3^+(K^\pi=3^+)$ state being quantitatively consistent with experimental data, while the $1^+(K^\pi=1^+)$ state assignment remains uncertain due to insufficient experimental information on partial $\alpha$-decay widths.

## Method

The author uses a $^{10}$B+$\alpha$ cluster model where the $^{10}$B cluster is described by a $2\alpha+(pn)$ three-body wave function with harmonic oscillator configurations, and the $\alpha$ cluster is placed at various distances ($D_\alpha=2$ to $6$ fm) and angles ($\theta_\alpha=0$, $\pi/4$, $\pi/2$) relative to the deformed $^{10}$B cluster. Positive-parity states of $^{14}$N are obtained by superposing $J^\pi$-projected wave functions and diagonalizing the Hamiltonian with the Volkov central force and G3RS spin-orbit force, and the $^{10}$B($3^+$)$\otimes L_\alpha$ and $^{10}$B($1^+$)$\otimes L_\alpha$ components are evaluated via squared overlap calculations at a channel radius of $D_\alpha=5$ fm.

## Key numbers

- The calculated binding energy of $^{14}$N($1^+_{\rm g.s.}$) is 102.6 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 104.66 MeV.
- The calculated magnetic moment of $^{14}$N($1^+_{\rm g.s.}$) is $\mu=0.36$ $\mu_N$, compared to the experimental $\mu=0.4038$ $\mu_N$.
- The calculated electric quadrupole moment of $^{14}$N($1^+_{\rm g.s.}$) is $Q=2.4$ $e{\rm fm}^2$, compared to the experimental $Q=1.93(8)$ $e{\rm fm}^2$.
- The $3^+$ state at $E_x=13.19$ MeV ($E_r=1.58$ MeV) has an experimental total width $\Gamma=0.065(10)$ MeV; the calculated $\alpha$-decay width for the $3^+(K^\pi=3^+)$ state is $\Gamma_\alpha=0.05$ MeV with dominant $^{10}{\rm B}(3^+)\otimes(L_\alpha=0)$ decay.
- The $1^+$ state at $E_r=2.11$ MeV ($E_x=13.72$ MeV) has an experimental total width $\Gamma=0.16(2)$ MeV; the calculated $\alpha$-decay width for the $1^+(K^\pi=1^+)$ state is $\Gamma_\alpha=0.01$ MeV.
- The $^{10}$B($3^+$)+$\alpha$ component in the $3^+(K^\pi=3^+)$ state from the full calculation is $P_{^{10}{\rm B}(3^+)\otimes L_\alpha=0}=0.21$ and $P_{^{10}{\rm B}(3^+)\otimes L_\alpha=2}=0.10$ at $D_\alpha=5$ fm.
- The $^{10}$B($1^+$)+$\alpha$ component in the $1^+(K^\pi=1^+)$ state from the full calculation is $P_{^{10}{\rm B}(1^+)\otimes L_\alpha=0}=0.05$ and $P_{^{10}{\rm B}(1^+)\otimes L_\alpha=2}=0.09$ at $D_\alpha=5$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the study of linear-chain $3\alpha$ configurations previously investigated in $^{14}$C by Suhara and Kanada-En'yo (2010), where $^{10}$Be+$\alpha$ cluster states were found to contain linear-chain structures, and in $^{15}$C as suggested by von Oertzen (1997). The present paper applies a similar $^{10}$B+$\alpha$ cluster model to $^{14}$N, identifying $K^\pi=3^+$ and $K^\pi=1^+$ rotational bands near the $\alpha$-decay threshold that correspond to resonances observed in $\alpha$ elastic scattering experiments by Mo (1973). The results enable future experimental confirmation of linear-chain $3\alpha$ structures in $^{14}$N through more precise measurements of $\alpha$-decay widths and $E2$ transition strengths.
