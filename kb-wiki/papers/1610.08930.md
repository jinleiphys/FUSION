# Groomed jets in heavy-ion collisions: sensitivity to medium-induced bremsstrahlung

> We argue that contemporary jet substructure techniques might facilitate a more direct measurement of hard medium-induced gluon bremsstrahlung in heavy-ion collisions, and focus specifically on the "soft drop declustering" procedure that singles out the two leading jet substructures. Assuming coherent jet energy loss, we find an enhancement of the distribution of the energy fractions shared by the two substructures at small subjet energy caused by hard medium-induced gluon radiation. Departures from this approximation are discussed, in particular, the effects of colour decoherence and the contamination of the grooming procedure by soft background. Finally, we propose a complementary observable, that is the ratio of the two-pronged probability in Pb-Pb to proton-proton collisions and discuss its sensitivity to various energy loss mechanisms.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the "soft drop declustering" jet substructure technique can provide a more direct measurement of hard medium-induced gluon bremsstrahlung in heavy-ion collisions than inclusive jet observables, but only under the assumption of coherent jet energy loss where the jet interacts with the medium as a single colour charge.

## Method

The authors compute the modified splitting probability distribution \(p(z_g)\) for groomed jets in heavy-ion collisions by combining vacuum-like collinear splittings (resummed via Sudakov form factors) with rare, hard medium-induced BDMPS-Z gluon emissions that remain within the jet cone. They assume a separation of scales where soft multiple gluon radiation at large angles causes energy loss (described by quenching weights) while hard in-cone medium-induced radiation modifies the substructure. The analysis is performed for purely gluonic cascades in a static medium with length \(L = 5\) fm and transport parameter \(\hat q = 1-2\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, using the soft drop grooming condition with \(\beta = 0\) and a minimal resolution angle \(R_0 = 0.1\).

## Key numbers

- Characteristic medium scale \(\omega_c = \hat q L^2/2 = 80\) GeV for \(L=4\) fm and \(\hat q \simeq 1\) GeV\(^2\)/fm
- Soft scale \(\omega_s \equiv \bar\alpha^2 \omega_c \simeq 7\) GeV for \(\bar\alpha \simeq 0.3\)
- Characteristic emission angles for hard BDMPS-Z gluons: \(0.025 < \theta_{\rm BDMPS} < 0.28\) for 7 GeV \(< \omega <\) 80 GeV
- Jet transverse momentum values considered: \(\pT = 140\) GeV and \(\pT = 250\) GeV
- Jet radius \(R = 0.3\) and minimal resolution angle \(R_0 = 0.1\) as in CMS data
- Formation times for primary splittings: \(10^{-2}\) fm \(\lesssim t_f \lesssim 1\) fm for \(\pT = 200\) GeV jets with \(z \sim 0.1-0.5\) and \(\Delta R_{12} \sim 0.1-0.4\)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of jet substructure in heavy-ion collisions beyond inclusive jet spectra measurements (Chatrchyan 2012, Aad 2014, Abelev 2013) and earlier jet substructure measurements (Chatrchyan 2012, Chatrchyan 2013). The authors build on the soft drop declustering procedure developed by Larkoski (2014) and the first CMS measurement of the \(z_g\)-distribution in heavy-ion collisions (CMS 2016). The analysis uses the BDMPS-Z formalism for medium-induced radiation (Baier 1996, Zakharov 1996) and the concept of colour coherence in multi-parton systems (Mehtar-Tani 2010, Casalderrey-Solana 2011). The proposed ratio of two-pronged probabilities in Pb-Pb to proton-proton collisions is a new observable designed to discriminate between coherent and incoherent energy loss scenarios, enabling future experimental tests of the underlying mechanisms of jet quenching.
