# Quantum Tunneling and Scattering of a Composite Object: Revisited and Reassessed

> This work presents an extensive exploration of scattering and tunneling involving composite objects with intrinsic degrees of freedom. We aim at exact solutions to such scattering problems. Along this path we demonstrate solution to model Hamiltonians, and develop different techniques for addressing these complex reaction-physics problems, discuss their applicability, and investigate the relevant convergence issues. As examples, we study the scattering of a two-constituent deuteron-like systems either with an infinite set of intrinsic bound states or with a continuum of states that allows for breakup. We show that the internal degrees of freedom of the projectile and its virtual excitation in the course of reactions play an important role in shaping the S-matrix and related observables, giving rise to enhanced or reduced tunneling in various situations.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the internal degrees of freedom of a composite projectile and its virtual excitations during scattering can either enhance or reduce tunneling, but the formally exact projection method for solving such problems fails to yield convergent results for large mass ratios of the projectile constituents, requiring instead a variable phase method that properly handles virtual channels.

## Method

The authors study one-dimensional scattering of a two-constituent "deuteron-like" projectile, where only one constituent interacts with an external potential (an infinite wall or a barrier). They compare two approaches: the Projection Method, which expands the wave function in the intrinsic basis of the composite system and solves a matrix equation for reflection amplitudes; and the Variable Phase Method (VPM), which solves differential equations for the reflection amplitude matrix as a function of the center-of-mass coordinate, extended here to include virtual (closed) channels. The intrinsic confinement is modeled either as an infinite square well or a harmonic oscillator potential.

## Key numbers

- For the infinite square well intrinsic potential with equal masses (m1/m2 = 1) and incident kinetic energy E = 0.1 (in units of epsilon = hbar^2/(2m*lambda^2)), virtual channel reflection amplitudes scale approximately as exp(pi*n/2) with channel number n.
- For the harmonic oscillator intrinsic potential with incident kinetic energy E = 1 (which equals 0.5*hbar*omega), the Projection Method fails to converge for mass ratios m1/m2 = 3, 5, and 10, while the VPM yields stable phase shifts.
- The energy unit is defined as epsilon = hbar^2/(2m*lambda^2), where lambda is the characteristic width of the intrinsic potential; for the harmonic oscillator, lambda = sqrt(hbar/(m*omega)) and the ground state energy is epsilon_0 = (1/2)*hbar*omega.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of tunneling with internal degrees of freedom by Flambaum (2005), Bertulani (2007), Balantekin (1998), and others, which often imposed restrictions on projectile energy, mass ratios, or number of intrinsic states. The paper demonstrates limitations of the projection-based approach previously used by MORO (2000), Sakharuk (1999), and Zelevinsky (2000), and provides a convergent VPM framework that can handle arbitrary mass ratios and includes virtual excitations. This enables exact treatment of breakup and scattering for composite projectiles with a continuum of intrinsic states.
