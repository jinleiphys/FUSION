# Theoretical Constraints for Observation of Superdeformed Bands in the Mass-60 Region

> The lightest superdeformed nuclei of the mass-60 region are described using the Projected Shell Model. In contrast to the heaviest superdeformed nuclei where a coherent motion of nucleons often dominates the physics, it is found that alignment of $g_{9/2}$ proton and neutron pairs determines the high spin behavior for superdeformed rotational bands in this mass region. It is predicted that, due to the systematics of shell fillings along the even--even Zn isotopic chain, observation of a regular superdeformed yrast band sequence will be unlikely for certain nuclei in this mass region.

# Theoretical Constraints for Observation of Superdeformed Bands in the Mass-60 Region

## Key claim
The high-spin behavior of superdeformed rotational bands in the mass-60 region is dominated by alignment of $g_{9/2}$ proton and neutron pairs, not by coherent nucleon motion as in heavier superdeformed nuclei. Due to systematics of shell fillings along the even-even Zn isotopic chain, observation of a regular superdeformed yrast band sequence will be unlikely for certain nuclei in this mass region.

## Method
The authors applied the Projected Shell Model (PSM) to even-even Zn isotopes from $^{60}$Zn to $^{66}$Zn, using a deformed basis with $\epsilon_2 = 0.45$ and three full major shells ($N = 2, 3, 4$) for both neutrons and protons. The Hamiltonian included spherical single-particle terms, residual quadrupole-quadrupole, monopole pairing, and quadrupole pairing interactions, with the monopole pairing strength multiplied by 0.90 relative to standard values and a quadrupole-to-monopole pairing ratio $C = G_Q/G_M = 0.28$. The many-body wavefunction was constructed as a superposition of angular-momentum-projected multi-quasiparticle states (vacuum, two-quasiparticle, and four-quasiparticle states) with a basis dimension of about 50, and two-body interactions were diagonalized after projection.

## Key numbers
- Maximum rotational frequency in mass-60 SD bands: $\hbar\omega \approx 1.8$ MeV; in contrast, mass-190 SD bands have maximum rotational frequency typically 0.4 MeV.
- Deformation fixed at $\epsilon_2 = 0.45$ for all calculated nuclei, based on $^{60}$Zn having $\beta_2 = 0.47$.
- Monopole pairing strengths $G_M$ multiplied by 0.90 relative to Ref. Tod (year unspecified); quadrupole pairing ratio $C = G_Q/G_M = 0.28$.
- First band crossing occurs at $I \approx 12$ for all four Zn isotopes.
- Second band crossing occurs at $I = 18$ for $^{60}$Zn, $I = 20$ for $^{62}$Zn, and $I = 22$ for $^{64}$Zn; for $^{66}$Zn the crossing is at $I = 26$.
- Pairing gaps at $I = 0$ are about 0.9 MeV for both neutrons and protons; beyond $I = 18$ they drop to about 40% of their initial values.
- For $^{62}$Zn, the measured first SD $E_\gamma$ is predicted to correspond to the transition from $I = 20$ to $I = 18$.

## Context
This work extends the Projected Shell Model, previously applied to normally deformed nuclei and superdeformed nuclei in the mass-130 and mass-190 regions (Hara and Sun, year unspecified; Sun et al., year unspecified), to the lightest known superdeformed region. It contrasts with mean-field calculations (cranked relativistic mean-field theory, cranked Nilsson model by Ring et al., year unspecified; cranked Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method by Svensson et al., year unspecified) that neglected pairing correlations entirely. The results explain why the observed SD band in $^{60}$Zn (Svensson et al., year unspecified) is long while that in $^{62}$Zn (Svensson et al., year unspecified) is short, and why no SD yrast band has been seen in $^{64}$Zn (Galindo-Uribarri et al., year unspecified). The work predicts that $^{66}$Zn should be a good candidate for observing a long SD yrast band, and suggests that including neutron-proton pairing in the PSM is of interest for future work.
