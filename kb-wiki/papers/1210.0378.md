# Density functional approaches to collective phenomena in nuclei: Time-dependent density-functional theory for perturbative and non-perturbative nuclear dynamics

> We present the basic concepts and our recent developments in the density functional approaches with the Skyrme functionals for describing nuclear dynamics at low energy. The time-dependent density-functional theory (TDDFT) is utilized for the exact linear response with an external perturbation. For description of collective dynamics beyond the perturbative regime, we present a theory of a decoupled collective submanifold to describe for a slow motion based on the TDDFT. Selected applications are shown to demonstrate the quality of their performance and feasibility. Advantages and disadvantages in the numerical aspects are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that time-dependent density-functional theory (TDDFT) with Skyrme functionals can describe both perturbative linear response and non-perturbative large-amplitude collective dynamics in nuclei, but the theoretical justification for nuclear DFT relies on a wave-packet formalism that fixes the center-of-mass wave function, and the extension to deformed (rotationally symmetry-broken) states remains only approximately justified.

## Method

The author presents the Hohenberg-Kohn theorem adapted for self-bound nuclear systems by introducing a fixed center-of-mass wave-packet state, then derives the Kohn-Sham scheme for ground-state calculations. For dynamics, the Runge-Gross theorem for time-dependent densities is reviewed, and the time-dependent Kohn-Sham equations are given in the adiabatic approximation. For non-perturbative collective motion, a theory of a decoupled collective submanifold in the classical phase space is outlined, though the detailed formalism is referenced to later sections.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: -2.2 MeV, compared to the potential depth V0 ≈ 100 MeV, illustrating that the zero-point kinetic energy nearly cancels the potential.
- Nucleon-nucleon scattering length in the 1S0 channel: a ≈ -18 fm, much larger than the mean inter-nucleon distance inside nuclei (kF|a| ≫ 1).
- The nucleon mean-free path inside nuclei is proportional to {εF/(ε−εF)}², significantly enhanced for nucleons near the Fermi energy.
- The GFMC method has been applied to nuclei up to mass number A ≈ 10; the NCSM is successful up to p-shell nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the DFT framework for nuclear structure, previously developed by Hohenberg and Kohn (1964) and Kohn and Sham (1965), to time-dependent phenomena in self-bound systems. It builds on the wave-packet DFT justification by Giraud et al. (2008) and addresses the open issue of separating center-of-mass and rotational degrees of freedom, which is only approximate for deformed nuclei. The approach enables future studies of large-amplitude collective motion such as fission, shape coexistence, and anharmonic vibrations, and can be constrained by new data from radioactive beam facilities on neutron halos, skins, and long isotopic chains.
