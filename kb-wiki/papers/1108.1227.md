# Signatures of three-nucleon interactions in few-nucleon systems

> Recent experimental results in three-body systems have unambiguously shown that calculations based only on nucleon-nucleon forces fail to accurately describe many experimental observables and one needs to include effects which are beyond the realm of the two-body potentials. This conclusion owes its significance to the fact that experiments and calculations can both be performed with a high accuracy. In this review, both theoretical and experimental achievements of the past decade will be underlined. Selected results will be presented. The discussion on the effects of the three-nucleon forces is, however, limited to the hadronic sector. It will be shown that despite the major successes in describing these seemingly simple systems, there are still clear discrepancies between data and the state-of-the-art calculations.

# Signatures of three-nucleon interactions in few-nucleon systems

## Key claim

Calculations based solely on nucleon-nucleon forces fail to accurately describe many experimental observables in three-body systems, and three-nucleon forces (3NFs) are required; however, despite major successes, clear discrepancies remain between data and state-of-the-art calculations, and the model dependence of 3NF predictions (up to 15% differences between different theoretical approaches) must be taken into account when making definitive conclusions.

## Method

The authors review the current status of three-nucleon force models, including phenomenological approaches (Fujita-Miyazawa, Tucson-Melbourne TM/TM', Urbana-IX, Illinois series) and chiral effective field theory (EFT) at N²LO and N³LO, and compare their predictions to experimental data from nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering and break-up reactions at intermediate energies (50–250 MeV). Experimental techniques using polarized beams and targets at facilities such as KVI, RIKEN, RCNP, PSI, and IUCF are surveyed, and calculations from the Bochum-Cracow group (using CD-Bonn NN potential with TM' 3NF) are compared to those from the Hanover-Lisbon group (using CD-Bonn with explicit Δ isobar excitations in a coupled-channel framework).

## Key numbers

- Relative differences of up to 15% emerge between the Bochum-Cracow and Hanover-Lisbon calculations for proton-deuteron elastic-scattering cross sections when 3NF effects are included, for incident nucleon energies between 50 and 200 MeV
- The first non-vanishing 3NF in chiral EFT emerges at N²LO in the chiral expansion
- Predictions from chiral EFT at N²LO are useful for quantitative comparison with data only at energies below 100 MeV
- Polarizations of 60–80% of the maximum theoretical value have been achieved in polarized-ion sources
- Measurements at intermediate energies cover the range from 50 MeV/nucleon to energies around the pion-production threshold

## Context

This review extends the line of work on three-nucleon forces that began with the Fujita-Miyazawa force (1957) and continued through the Tucson-Melbourne (Coon, 1974; Coon, 1978) and Urbana (Carlson, 1983; Pudliner, 1995) models, and it contrasts these phenomenological approaches with the systematically improvable chiral EFT framework (van Kolck, 1994; Epelbaum, 2002). The paper enables future work by identifying the need for N³LO 3NFs in chiral EFT (Bernard, 2007; Bernard, 2011) and by highlighting specific observables and kinematic regions where discrepancies between data and calculations persist, such as the Ay puzzle and the space star anomaly.
