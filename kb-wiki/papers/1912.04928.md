# Chemical freeze-out conditions from net-kaon fluctuations at RHIC

> We compare the mean-over-variance ratio of the net-kaon distribution calculated within a state-of-the-art hadron resonance gas model to the latest experimental data from the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC by the STAR collaboration. Our analysis indicates that it is not possible to reproduce the experimental results using the freeze-out parameters from the existing combined fit of net-proton and net-electric charge mean-over-variance. The strange mesons need about 10-15 MeV higher temperatures than the light hadrons at the highest collision energies. In view of the recent lambda fluctuation measurements, we predict the net-lambda variance-over-mean at the light and strange chemical freeze-out parameters. We observe that the lambda fluctuations are sensitive to the difference in the freeze-out temperatures established in this analysis. Our results have implications for other phenomenological models in the field of relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

# Chemical freeze-out conditions from net-kaon fluctuations at RHIC

## Key claim
The mean-over-variance ratio of net-kaon distributions measured by STAR cannot be reproduced using freeze-out parameters obtained from combined fits of net-proton and net-electric charge fluctuations. Strange mesons require approximately 10–15 MeV higher freeze-out temperatures than light hadrons at the highest collision energies of the Beam Energy Scan.

## Method
The author calculated the mean-over-variance ratio (χ₁/χ₂) for net-kaons within a hadron resonance gas (HRG) model, including experimental acceptance cuts on rapidity (|y| < 0.5) and transverse momentum (0.2 < p_T < 1.6 GeV/c). Freeze-out parameters were determined by finding the overlap between the HRG calculation along lattice QCD isentropic trajectories and the experimental data from the STAR collaboration across multiple Beam Energy Scan collision energies.

## Key numbers
- Freeze-out temperature for net-kaons at the highest collision energy: T ≈ 163–185 MeV
- Separation between freeze-out temperature for kaons and light hadrons at the highest collision energy: 10–15 MeV
- The analysis covers the highest five energies of the Beam Energy Scan at RHIC

## Context
This work extends the approach of Alba (2015), who showed that fluctuations provide a better thermometer than yields for strange mesons, and builds on the combined fit of net-proton and net-electric charge fluctuations by Alba (2014). The results enable predictions for net-lambda fluctuations that can be compared to upcoming STAR data, and have implications for other phenomenological models in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The author notes that higher-order moments of the net-kaon distribution had errors too large to provide precise freeze-out parameter determination, so lattice QCD isentropic trajectories from Guenther (2017) were used instead to constrain the relationship between temperature and baryonic chemical potential.
