# Supersymmetric and Shape-Invariant Generalization for Non-resonant Jaynes-Cummings Systems

> A class of shape-invariant bound-state problems which represent transitions in a two-level system introduced earlier are generalized to include arbitrary energy splittings between the two levels. We show that the coupled-channel Hamiltonians obtained correspond to the generalization of the non-resonant Jaynes-Cummings Hamiltonian, widely used in quantized theories of laser. In this general context, we determine the eigenstates, eigenvalues, the time evolution matrix and the population inversion matrix factor.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes a previously introduced class of shape-invariant bound-state problems representing two-level system transitions to include arbitrary energy splittings between the two levels, showing that the resulting coupled-channel Hamiltonians correspond to a generalization of the non-resonant Jaynes-Cummings Hamiltonian. The authors determine eigenstates, eigenvalues, the time evolution matrix, and the population inversion matrix factor for this generalized system, with the caveat that the results reduce to standard non-resonant Jaynes-Cummings results only when the starting Hamiltonian is taken to be the harmonic oscillator.

## Method

The authors start from a shape-invariant Hamiltonian defined by operators satisfying the condition \(\hat A(a_1) \hat A^\dagger(a_1) = \hat A^\dagger(a_2) \hat A(a_2) + R(a_1)\) and introduce a generalized non-resonant Jaynes-Cummings Hamiltonian \(\hat{\bf H} = \hat A^\dagger\hat A + \frac{1}{2}[\hat A,\hat A^\dagger](\hat\sigma_3+1) + \sqrt{\hbar\Omega}(\hat\sigma_+\hat A+\hat\sigma_-\hat A^\dagger) + \hbar\Delta\,\hat\sigma_3\). They decompose this Hamiltonian into \(\hat{\bf H}_o = \hat{\bf S}^2\) and \(\hat{\bf H}_{int} = \sqrt{\hbar\Omega}\,\hat{\bf S} + \hbar\Delta\,\hat\sigma_3\) using the operator \(\hat{\bf S} = \hat\sigma_+\hat A + \hat\sigma_-\hat A^\dagger\), then find common eigenstates of \(\hat{\bf S}^2\) and \(\hat{\bf H}_{int}\) by solving the eigenvalue equation in matrix form using the \(B\)-operator formulation of shape invariance. The time-dependent Schrodinger equation is solved by introducing an interaction picture and deriving the time evolution matrix \(\hat{\bf U}_i(t,0)\), and the population inversion factor \(\hat\sigma_3(t)\) is obtained by solving a non-homogeneous linear differential equation with constant coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Energy eigenvalues for the generalized non-resonant Jaynes-Cummings system: \(E_m^{(\pm)} = {\cal E}_{m+1} \pm \sqrt{\hbar\Omega\; {\cal E}_{m+1} + \hbar^2\Delta^2}\), where \(\Omega\) is a constant related to coupling strength and \(\Delta\) is a constant related to detuning.
- For the resonant limit (\(\Delta = 0\)): \(E_m^{(\pm)} = {\cal E}_{m+1} \pm \sqrt{\hbar\Omega\; {\cal E}_{m+1}}\) with coefficients \(C_{m+1}^{(\pm)} = C_m^{(\pm)} = 1/\sqrt{2}\).
- For the standard Jaynes-Cummings limit (harmonic oscillator): \(E_m^{(\pm)} = (m+1)\hbar\omega \pm \hbar\sqrt{\Omega\omega (m+1) + (\omega-\omega_o)^2}\), with \(\Delta = \omega - \omega_o\) and coefficients \(\gamma_m^{(\pm)} = \sqrt{1 + \delta_m^2} \mp \delta_m\) where \(\delta_m = (\omega-\omega_o)/\sqrt{(m+1)\Omega\omega}\).
- Frequencies for the time evolution: \(\hbar\hat\omega_1 = \sqrt{\hbar\Omega\,\hat H_2 + (\hbar\Delta)^2}\) and \(\hbar\hat\omega_2 = \sqrt{\hbar\Omega\,\hat H_1 + (\hbar\Delta)^2}\).
- Frequencies for the population inversion: \(\hbar\hat\nu_1 = 2\sqrt{\hbar\Omega\,\hat H_2}\) and \(\hbar\hat\nu_2 = 2\sqrt{\hbar\Omega\,\hat H_1}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier shape-invariant treatment of two-level system transitions (Aleixo, Balantekin, and Ribeiro, 2000) to include arbitrary energy splittings, generalizing the non-resonant Jaynes-Cummings Hamiltonian widely used in quantum optics for modeling two-level atom-single field mode interactions. The results reduce to the standard non-resonant Jaynes-Cummings approach when the starting Hamiltonian is the harmonic oscillator, and the authors note that a further extension to intensity-dependent couplings will be taken up in a following paper.
