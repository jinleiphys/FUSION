# A key factor to the spin parameter of uniformly rotating compact stars: crust structure

> We study the key factor to determine the dimensionless spin parameter $j\equiv cJ/(GM^2)$ of different kinds of uniformly rotating compact stars, including the traditional neutron stars, hyperonic neutron stars, and hybrid stars, and check the reliability of the results on various types of equations of state of dense matter. The equations of state from the relativistic mean field theory and the MIT bag model are adopted to simulate compact stars. Numerical calculations of rigidly rotating neutron stars are performed using the RNS code in the framework of general relativity by solving the Einstein equations for stationary axis-symmetric spacetime. The crust structure of compact stars is found to be a key factor to determine the maximum value of the spin parameter $j_{\rm max}$. For the stars with inclusion of the crust, $j_{\rm max}\sim 0.7$ is sustained for various kinds of compact stars with $M>0.5 M_{\odot}$, and is found to be insensitive to the mass of star and selected equations of state. For the traditional neutron stars, hyperonic neutron stars and hybrid stars without crust structure, the value $j_{\rm max}$ lies in the range of $[0.7, 1.0]$. Thus, not $j>0.7$ but $j>1$ could be treated as the candidate criterion to distinguish the strange quark stars from the other kinds of compact stars. Furthermore, a universal formula $j=0.48(f/f_K)^3-0.42(f/f_K)^2+0.63(f/f_K)$ is suggested to calculate the spin parameter at any rotational frequency for all kinds of compact stars with crust structure and $M>0.5M_{\odot}$.

## Key claim

The crust structure of compact stars, not the interior composition, is the key factor determining the maximum dimensionless spin parameter \(j_{\rm max}\). For stars with a crust and mass \(M > 0.5 M_\odot\), \(j_{\rm max} \sim 0.7\) holds universally across traditional neutron stars, hyperonic neutron stars, and hybrid stars, whereas without a crust \(j_{\rm max}\) lies in the range \([0.7, 1.0]\) and depends sensitively on mass and equation of state.

## Method

The authors used the RNS code to solve the Einstein field equations for stationary, axisymmetric spacetime, constructing models of uniformly rotating compact stars. Equations of state were taken from relativistic mean field theory (TW99 and PKDD interactions, with and without hyperons) for hadronic stars, and from the MIT bag model (bag constants \(B = 90\) and \(150\ \mathrm{MeV\,fm^{-3}}\)) combined with PKDDN for hybrid stars. Crust effects were included by replacing the low-density EOS (\(\rho < 0.08\ \mathrm{fm^{-3}}\)) with two sets of crust EOSs: NV+BPS and HP+DH.

## Key numbers

- For stars with crust and \(M > 0.5 M_\odot\), \(j_{\rm max} \sim 0.7\) for all EOS types (traditional NS, hyperonic NS, hybrid stars).
- Without crust, \(j_{\rm max}\) lies in the range \([0.7, 1.0]\) for traditional NS, hyperonic NS, and hybrid stars with \(M > 0.5 M_\odot\).
- For stars with baryonic mass \(M_b = 1.0 M_\odot\), the difference in \(j_{\rm max}\) due to crust effects is up to 24%; for \(M_b = 1.4 M_\odot\), the difference is up to 18%.
- A universal formula is given: \(j = 0.48 (f/f_K)^3 - 0.42 (f/f_K)^2 + 0.63 (f/f_K)\) for all compact stars with crust and \(M > 0.5 M_\odot\).
- The empirical relation \(f_K(M) = 1.08\ \mathrm{kHz} (M/M_\odot)^{1/2} (R_S/10\ \mathrm{km})^{-3/2}\) holds to within about 2% for \(0.5 M_\odot < M < 0.9 M^{\rm stat}_{\rm max}\) for all cases with and without crust.

## Context

This work extends the study of Lo and Lin (2011) who first showed that \(j_{\rm max} \sim 0.7\) for neutron stars without hyperons and that strange quark stars can have \(j > 1\). The present paper demonstrates that the same \(j_{\rm max} \sim 0.7\) holds for hyperonic neutron stars and hybrid stars when a crust is included, contradicting the earlier implication that \(j > 0.7\) alone could identify strange quark stars. The results enable a refined criterion: only \(j > 1\) can distinguish strange quark stars from other compact stars, and the universal \(j\)–\(f/f_K\) formula allows spin parameter estimation from observed frequencies. The work also informs models of gamma-ray burst progenitors and the interpretation of kHz QPOs in sources like Cir X-1.
