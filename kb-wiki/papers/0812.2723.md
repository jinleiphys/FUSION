# Hadronic Interactions with Lattice QCD

> I discuss recent results of the NPLQCD Collaboration regarding the calculation of hadronic interactions with lattice QCD. A particular emphasis will be spent on pi-pi scattering and other meson interactions.

# Hadronic Interactions with Lattice QCD

## Key claim

The NPLQCD Collaboration's lattice QCD calculation of the I=2 ππ scattering length, when extrapolated to the physical charged pion mass using mixed-action chiral perturbation theory, yields a result consistent with Weinberg's tree-level prediction across the full range of quark masses studied, with no evidence of higher-order chiral corrections beyond those already absorbed into the pion mass and decay constant.

## Method

The collaboration computed domain-wall valence quark propagators on Asqtad-improved coarse MILC lattices with 2+1 dynamical flavors (up, down, strange) and applied Lüscher's method to relate the volume-dependent distortion of two-particle energy levels to the infinite-volume scattering phase shift. The calculation used mixed-action chiral perturbation theory to treat lattice-spacing corrections, exponentially suppressed finite-volume corrections, and to perform the chiral extrapolation to the physical pion mass. An order-of-magnitude increase in statistics over the original calculation was included, with multiple ensembles at different quark masses.

## Key numbers

- m_π a_ππ^(I=2) = -0.04330 ± 0.00042 at the charged pion mass, from the high-precision update (Beane 2007)
- m_π a_ππ^(I=2) = -0.0426 ± 0.0019 from the original calculation (Beane 2005)
- Lattice spacing b ~ 0.125 fm and volume L ~ 2.5 fm for the coarse MILC ensembles
- Pion masses used: {290, 350, 490, 590} MeV with corresponding kaon masses {580, 595, 640, 675} MeV
- Statistics: 468 configurations × 16 sources at the lightest pion mass; 658 × 20, 486 × 24, and 564 × 8 for the heavier masses

## Context

This work extends the NPLQCD Collaboration's program of computing hadronic interactions from lattice QCD, following their first dynamical calculation of the I=2 ππ scattering length (Beane 2005). The precision update enabled systematic treatment of lattice artefacts using mixed-action chiral perturbation theory (Chen 2005) and finite-volume corrections (Bedaque 2006). The same methodology has been applied to I=3/2 Kπ and I=1 KK scattering lengths (Beane 2006), and the collaboration has also computed nucleon-nucleon and hyperon-nucleon scattering lengths (Beane 2006), though those suffer from exponential signal-to-noise degradation. The results enable future calculations of multi-baryon systems and provide benchmarks for chiral perturbation theory.
