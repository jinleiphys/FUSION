# P and T Odd Electromagnetic Moments of Deuteron in Chiral Limit

> P odd anapole moment of the deuteron is found in the chiral limit, $m_π \to 0$. The contact current generated by the P odd pion exchange does not contribute to the deuteron anapole. Being combined with usual radiative corrections to the weak electron -- deuteron interaction, our calculation results in a sufficiently accurate theoretical prediction for the corresponding effective constant $C_{2d}$. The experimental measurement of this constant would give valuable information on the P odd $π$NN constant and on the $s$-quark content of nucleons. We calculate also in the same limit $m_π \to 0$ the deuteron P odd and T odd multipoles: electric dipole moment and magnetic quadrupole moment.

## Key claim

The deuteron anapole moment is calculated in the chiral limit (pion mass \(m_\pi \to 0\)), and the contact current generated by the P-odd pion exchange does not contribute to it; the resulting theoretical prediction for the effective constant \(C_{2d}\) is sufficiently accurate to extract the P-odd \(\pi\)NN constant and the strange-quark content of nucleons from a future measurement, provided the experimental difficulty can be overcome.

## Method

The authors use the zero-range approximation (ZRA) for the deuteron \(^3S_1\) wave function and treat the P-odd \(\pi\)-meson exchange as a perturbation to compute the admixed \(^3P_1\) state via stationary perturbation theory with plane-wave intermediate states. The deuteron anapole moment is derived from a generalized operator that includes the isovector magnetic moments of the nucleons, and the same formalism is applied to the P- and T-odd electric dipole and magnetic quadrupole moments using three independent P-odd, T-odd \(\pi\)NN Lagrangians classified by isospin change.

## Key numbers

* Deuteron anapole moment: \(\mathbf{a}_d = -2.60 \frac{e g \bar{g}}{6 m_p m_\pi} \mathbf{I}\), where \(g = 13.45\) is the strong \(\pi\)NN coupling constant and \(\bar{g}\) is the P-odd \(\pi\)NN constant.
* Effective axial constant from the anapole: \(C^a_{2d} = 0.014 \pm 0.003\) at the "best value" \(\bar{g} = 3.3 \times 10^{-7}\).
* Total effective constant for the deuteron: \(C_{2d} = C^r_{2d} + C^a_{2d} = 0.028 \pm 0.005\), where the radiative correction \(C^r_{2d} = 0.014 \pm 0.003\) from Marciano and Sirlin (1984).
* Deuteron electric dipole moment: \(\mathbf{d} = -\frac{e g g_1}{12\pi m_\pi} \frac{1+\xi}{(1+2\xi)^2} \mathbf{I}\), with \(\xi = \kappa/m_\pi = 0.32\) and \(\kappa = \sqrt{m_p \epsilon}\), \(\epsilon = 2.23\) MeV.
* Deuteron magnetic quadrupole moment: \(\mathcal{M} = -\frac{e g}{12\pi m_p m_\pi} \frac{1+\xi}{(1+2\xi)^2} [ (3g_0+g_1)\mu_p + (3g_0-g_1)\mu_n ]\), with \(\mu_p = 2.79\) and \(\mu_n = -1.91\).

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological treatment of the deuteron anapole moment by Flambaum and Khriplovich (1984) and others, providing an explicit expression in terms of the P-odd \(\pi\)NN constant \(\bar{g}\) in the chiral limit. It corrects and refines the earlier calculation by Savage and Springer (1999), who initially omitted the leading isovector magnetic moment contribution. The result, combined with the radiative corrections of Marciano and Sirlin (1984), yields a precise theoretical prediction for \(C_{2d}\), enabling future atomic-physics experiments to extract the P-odd \(\pi\)NN constant and the strange-quark content of nucleons, analogous to the information obtained from the cesium anapole measurement by Wood et al. (1997).
