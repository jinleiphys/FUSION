# Modeling Short-Range Nucleon Pair and Triplet Abundances in Atomic Nuclei

> Short-range correlated (SRC) nucleon pairs provide a sensitive probe of the short-distance structure of atomic nuclei and the underlying nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction. We present a simple numerical method to estimate the number of two- and three-nucleon SRC clusters using an independent-particle shell model with a harmonic-oscillator basis. The relative abundances of proton-neutron (pn), proton-proton (pp), and neutron-neutron (nn) SRC pairs are calculated for Al, Fe, and Pb nuclei, normalized to carbon, and compared with existing analytical predictions and available extractions from experimental data. We extend the analysis to the isotopes 40Ca, 48Ca, and 54Fe (collectively, the CaFe nuclei), which have been recently measured but whose SRC results are not yet published. These isotopes provide insight into the role of 1f7/2 shell occupancy in SRC pair formation. Finally, we present a reference baseline for three-nucleon SRC cluster abundances across the studied nuclei, assuming 3N clusters originate from independent two-nucleon interactions. The predicted baseline is about 2.5% 3N-SRC/2N-SRC ratio for all medium and heavy nuclei. Deviations from this baseline may indicate the presence of additional short-range nuclear dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper presents a simplified numerical model, based on an independent-particle shell model with a harmonic-oscillator basis, that can estimate the abundances of short-range correlated (SRC) nucleon pairs and triplets across nuclei. The authors hedge that the model is intentionally minimal, with only two adjustable parameters, and that the predicted 3N-SRC/2N-SRC ratio of about 2.5% for all medium and heavy nuclei serves as a baseline; deviations from this baseline may indicate additional short-range nuclear dynamics beyond independent two-nucleon interactions.

## Method

The authors model each nucleus using a three-dimensional harmonic oscillator potential with a single averaged oscillator length b for protons and neutrons, determined from empirical formulas. They embed the nucleus in a cubic volume discretized into smaller cubes of edge length r_src (0.9-1.1 fm), evaluate nucleon wavefunction probabilities at each cube center, and compute joint probabilities for two or three nucleons to occupy the same SRC volume. The model uses two adjustable parameters: the SRC volume size r_src and a probability p=0.22 (for spin=0 pairs) that reflects the lower pairing probability for same-isospin pairs compared to spin-aligned proton-neutron pairs (which are assigned probability 1).

## Key numbers

- The predicted 3N-SRC/2N-SRC ratio is about 2.5% for all medium and heavy nuclei.
- For 40Ca, the model predicts 7.37 np pairs, 0.629 pp pairs, and 0.629 nn pairs.
- For 48Ca, the model predicts 8.49 np pairs, 0.580 pp pairs, and 0.940 nn pairs.
- For 54Fe, the model predicts 9.68 np pairs, 0.791 pp pairs, and 0.887 nn pairs.
- Adding eight neutrons to 40Ca (forming 48Ca) increases nn-SRC pairs by approximately 50% and np-SRC pairs by about 15%.
- Adding six protons to 48Ca (forming 54Fe) increases pp-SRC pairs by 35% and np-SRC pairs by 14%.
- The nominal parameters used are r_src=1 fm and p=0.22, obtained by requiring pp/np=7.5% and number of pairs per nucleon=17.5%.
- The total number of SRC pairs per nucleon falls within the range of 15-20% for the parameter scan.

## Context

This work extends the analytical predictions of Colle (2015) and the zero-range approximation (ZRA) calculations of Colle (2015) by providing a simpler numerical method that reproduces key trends in SRC pair abundances for Al, Fe, and Pb nuclei. The model enables predictions for the CaFe isotopes (40Ca, 48Ca, 54Fe) whose SRC results have been measured but not yet published, allowing future comparison with experimental data from Jefferson Lab. The authors contrast their constant 3N/2N ratio prediction with the work of Ryckebusch (2012), who found the 3N-SRC contribution to increase monotonically with A^1.5, implying a mass-dependent 3N/2N ratio.
