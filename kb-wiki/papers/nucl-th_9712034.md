# Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov description of ground-state properties of Ni and Sn isotopes

> The Relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov (RHB) theory is applied in the description of ground-state properties of Ni and Sn isotopes. The NL3 parameter set is used for the effective mean-field Lagrangian, and pairing correlations are described by the pairing part of the finite range Gogny interaction D1S. Fully self-consistent RHB solutions are calculated for the Ni ($28\leq N\leq 50$) and Sn ($50\leq N\leq 82$) isotopes. Binding energies, neutron separation energies, and proton and neutron $rms$ radii are compared with experimental data. The model predicts a reduction of the spin-orbit potential with the increase of the number of neutrons. The resulting energy splittings between spin-orbit partners are discussed, as well as pairing properties calculated with the finite range effective interaction in the $pp$ channel.

## Key claim

The Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with the NL3 mean-field parameter set and the Gogny D1S pairing interaction provides an excellent description of ground-state properties of Ni and Sn isotopes across two major neutron shells, but the agreement is somewhat worse for Ni isotopes with neutrons in the 1f$_{7/2}$ orbital (proton-rich side) and the model predicts weaker binding for Ni with $N\leq 40$ and overbinding for Sn isotopes, with larger differences for $^{100-102}$Sn where additional correlations may be needed.

## Method

The RHB equations were solved self-consistently by expanding nucleon spinors and meson fields in a basis of spherical harmonic oscillators for N=20 oscillator shells. The NL3 parameter set was used for the effective mean-field Lagrangian in the particle-hole channel, and pairing correlations were described by the finite-range Gogny interaction D1S in the particle-particle channel. For odd-A isotopes, the blocking procedure for neutron levels was included, though spatial components of vector fields were neglected as they do not significantly contribute to the ground-state properties investigated.

## Key numbers

- For Ni and Sn isotopes, absolute differences between calculated and experimental masses are less than 2 MeV, except around $^{60}$Ni and for $^{100-102}$Sn
- The reduction of the spin-orbit term from $^{56}$Ni to $^{78}$Ni is approximately 35% in the surface region
- The neutron skin $r_n - r_p$ increases to approximately 0.4 fm at the closed shells N=50 for Ni and N=82 for Sn
- Surface thickness and diffuseness parameters for Sn increase approximately forty percent from $^{100}$Sn to $^{132}$Sn
- The diffuseness parameter for Ni is approximately 0.45 fm for N < 40 and approximately 0.50 fm for neutrons in the 1g$_{9/2}$ orbital
- Average pairing gaps at the Fermi surface for $^{120}$Sn are around 1.5 MeV
- Average pairing gaps at the Fermi surface for $^{70}$Ni are between 1 and 1.5 MeV
- Average neutron pairing gaps $< \Delta_N >$ are above 2 MeV in the middle of the corresponding shells for both Ni and Sn

## Context

This work extends the RHB approach previously applied to light neutron-rich nuclei (C, N, O, F, Ne, Na, Mg) by Lalazissis, Vretenar, Poschl, and Ring (1997) to medium-heavy Ni and Sn isotopes, testing the NL3 effective force and Gogny D1S pairing interaction against experimental data. The results confirm the predicted reduction of the spin-orbit potential with increasing neutron number, previously found to be approximately 40% for Ne isotopes at the drip-line by Lalazissis, Vretenar, and Ring (1997), and demonstrate that this effect is stronger in Ni than in Sn. The calculated pairing gaps for $^{120}$Sn reproduce results from Dobaczewski, Nazarewicz, Werner, Berger, Chinn, and Decharge (1996) using the Gogny HFB+D1S model, though with different single-neutron energies due to the different effective forces in the particle-hole channel. This work enables future predictions for medium-heavy nuclei at the neutron drip-line and provides a foundation for developing a fully relativistic theory of pairing consistent with modern mean-field Lagrangians.
