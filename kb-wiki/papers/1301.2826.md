# Collective flow and viscosity in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Collective flow, its anisotropies and its event-to-event fluctuations in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and the extraction of the specific shear viscosity of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) from collective flow data collected in heavy-ion collision experiments at RHIC and LHC are reviewed. Specific emphasis is placed on the similarities between the Big Bang of our universe and the Little Bangs created in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
This review argues that the specific shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is small, near the AdS/CFT lower bound of 1/(4π), but the exact value must be extracted phenomenologically by comparing viscous hydrodynamic simulations with experimental data from RHIC and LHC, with the caveat that the extraction is sensitive to initial-state fluctuations, the equation of state, and the treatment of the hadronic stage.

## Method
The authors review the theoretical framework of second-order relativistic viscous fluid dynamics (Israel-Stewart theory and its generalizations) applied to heavy-ion collisions, describing how initial energy density fluctuations are converted into anisotropic harmonic flow coefficients via viscous hydrodynamic evolution. They discuss hybrid approaches that couple viscous hydrodynamics for the QGP stage (using codes such as VISH2+1 or MUSIC) to microscopic hadron cascade simulations (such as UrQMD) for the late hadronic stage, and compare these simulations with experimental measurements of anisotropic flow coefficients v_n extracted from multi-particle correlations.

## Key numbers
- The AdS/CFT correspondence establishes a lower limit for the specific shear viscosity of the QGP at η/s ≃ 1/(4π) (Policastro 2001).
- The pseudo-critical temperature for the QCD crossover transition is T_c ≃ 155–160 MeV (Borsanyi 2010).
- The pre-equilibrium stage lasts approximately 0.2–1.5 fm/c before the hydrodynamic stage begins.
- Leading-order perturbative QCD values for η/s are too large by a factor of 3–10 to explain experimental anisotropic flow data (Csernai 2006, Kapusta 2008).
- Without accounting for non-equilibrium hadronic chemical composition below T_chem, extracted QGP shear viscosity values can be wrong by 100% (Song 2008, Heinz 2009).

## Context
This review extends the earlier work on ideal fluid dynamics (Kolb 2003) which first showed quantitative agreement with RHIC data, and addresses the subsequent realization that dissipative effects are essential (Hirano 2005, Romatschke 2007). It builds on the discovery that event-by-event fluctuations of nucleon positions (Miller 2003, Alver 2010) generate all harmonic eccentricity coefficients, driving anisotropic flow of any order. The paper enables the ongoing program of extracting QGP transport coefficients from precision flow data at RHIC and LHC, using hybrid codes like VISHNU (Song 2010) and MUSIC+UrQMD (Ryu 2012), and highlights the need for improved treatment of pre-equilibrium dynamics via the IP-Glasma model (Schenke 2012).
