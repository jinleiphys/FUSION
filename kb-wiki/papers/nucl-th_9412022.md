# Effects of T- and P-odd weak nucleon interaction in nuclei: renormalizations due to residual strong interaction, matrix elements between compound states and their correlations with P-violating matrix elements

> Manifestations of P-,T-odd weak interaction between nucleons in nucleus are considered. Renormalization of this interaction due to residual strong interaction is studied. Mean squared matrix elements of P-,T-odd weak interaction between compound states are calculated. Correlators between P-,T-odd and P-odd, T-even weak interaction matrix elements between compound states are considered and estimates for these quantities are obtained.

## Key claim

The paper calculates that the residual strong interaction does not enhance T- and P-odd weak interaction matrix elements in heavy nuclei (unlike the P-odd, T-even case), but that compound nuclear states provide a statistical enhancement of about 10^3 for mixing between opposite-parity states compared to single-particle mixing, with correlations between T-,P-odd and P-odd, T-even matrix elements estimated at about 10%.

## Method

The authors start from a two-body T-,P-odd weak nucleon interaction and derive a single-particle T-,P-odd weak potential by averaging over core nucleons. They then apply a unitary transformation to compensate this single-particle potential and solve the compensation equation using the Landau-Migdal residual strong interaction (a contact interaction with spin- and isospin-exchange terms) to obtain an effective two-body T-,P-odd interaction. Mean squared matrix elements between compound states are calculated using a statistical model with a canonical ensemble at effective nuclear temperature T=0.6 MeV, expanding compound states in a single-particle basis from a Woods-Saxon potential for 233Th. The correlator between T-,P-odd and P-odd, T-even matrix elements is computed via thermal averaging with proper symmetrization.

## Key numbers

- The renormalized T-,P-odd potential constants are reduced by a factor of 1.5...2 times compared to their initial values due to the residual strong interaction.
- The mean squared T-,P-odd matrix element between compound states for 233Th is sqrt( overline{ {W^{P,T}}^2 } ) = 0.20 eta_0 meV, where eta_0 is the dimensionless weak interaction constant.
- The ratio of T-,P-odd to P-odd matrix elements is sqrt( overline{ {W^{P,T}}^2 } ) / sqrt( overline{ {W^{P}}^2 } ) = 0.1 eta / g.
- The mixing coefficient for compound states is |F_sp| approx 1.0 x 10^{-5} eta_0, about 10^3 times larger than the single-particle mixing f_{12} approx 10^{-8} eta_0.
- The absolute value of the correlator between T-,P-odd and P-odd, T-even matrix elements for 233Th is |C(W^P, W^{P,T})| approx 0.1.
- The effective nuclear temperature used in the statistical model is T = 0.6 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous statistical method for calculating mean squared matrix elements of P-odd, T-even weak interaction (Flambaum and Vorov, 1993) to the T-,P-odd case. It contrasts with the P-odd, T-even case studied in earlier work (IPNCI, 1993) where the induced interaction is enhanced by about A^{1/3} times; here the induced T-,P-odd interaction causes only a renormalization of order 1-2. The results enable estimates of T-,P-odd effects in compound nuclear resonances, where experimental upper limits exist for nuclear electric dipole moments and Schiff moments (Haxton, 1983; FKSNP, 1988). The weak correlation found (about 10%) means that measured P-odd effects cannot straightforwardly predict the magnitude or sign of T-,P-odd effects in compound states.
