# A New Renormalization Group for Hamiltonian Field Theory

> The Schrodinger equation with a two-dimensional delta-function potential is a simple example of an asymptotically free theory that undergoes dimensional transmutation. Renormalization requires the introduction of a mass scale, which can be lowered perturbatively until an infrared cutoff produced by non-perturbative effects such as bound state formation is encountered. We outline the effective field theory and similarity renormalization group techniques for producing renormalized cutoff hamiltonians, and illustrate the control of logarithmic and inverse-power-law errors both techniques provide.

# A New Renormalization Group for Hamiltonian Field Theory

## Key claim
The two-dimensional delta-function potential provides a solvable example showing that both effective field theory (EFT) and similarity renormalization group (SRG) techniques can produce renormalized cutoff hamiltonians with controlled errors, but the SRG approach systematically removes inverse-logarithmic errors through running coupling renormalization while EFT removes errors through power-law expansions in the cutoff.

## Method
The authors solve the Schrodinger equation with a two-dimensional delta-function potential in momentum space, regulating the logarithmic divergence with a momentum cutoff and renormalizing by requiring the coupling to run with the cutoff so that the binding energy remains fixed. They then construct EFT hamiltonians by truncating a separable potential expanded in powers of momentum with Gaussian cutoff factors, fitting the coefficients to reproduce the exact inverse on-shell K-matrix. Separately, they apply Wegner's similarity renormalization group transformation, solving the flow equation perturbatively to obtain a running coupling and irrelevant operators that are functions of the flow parameter.

## Key numbers
- The exact binding energy is given by \(E_0 = \Lambda^2 / (e^{-4\pi/\alpha_0} - 1)\), where \(\Lambda\) is the momentum cutoff and \(\alpha_0\) is the bare coupling.
- The renormalized running coupling is \(\alpha_\Lambda = 4\pi / \ln(1 + \Lambda^2/E_0)\), which vanishes as \(\Lambda \rightarrow \infty\).
- The exact on-shell K-matrix is \(K_0(k) = -2 / \ln(k^2/E_0)\), and the exact phase shifts satisfy \(\cot \delta_0 = (1/\pi) \ln(k^2/E_0)\).
- For EFT with one parameter, the leading error in the inverse K-matrix is of order \(k^2/\lambda^2\), with coefficient \((1/2)[\gamma - \ln(\lambda^2/E_0) - 1]\).
- SRG calculations use a large cutoff \(\Lambda = 50\) and exact binding energy \(E_0 = 1\) to define the exact theory; errors scale as inverse powers of \(E_0/\lambda^2\) when \(\lambda \gg \Lambda\) and become logarithmic or inverse-logarithm squared for lower cutoffs.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of the two-dimensional delta-function potential originally studied by Jackiw (Jackiw) as a toy model for dimensional transmutation and asymptotic freedom. The EFT approach follows Lepage's treatment (Lepage, 1997) and the method of Steele and Furnstahl (Steele and Furnstahl) for fitting coefficients to the momentum expansion of the inverse K-matrix. The SRG approach builds on the similarity renormalization group developed by Glazek and Wilson (Glazek and Wilson, 1993) and Wegner (Wegner, 1994), and the results are compared to Glazek and Wilson's discretized analog study. The paper enables systematic error control in nonperturbative calculations for light-front QCD, where finite cutoffs are required and the SRG can be used to compute hamiltonians order-by-order in the canonical QCD coupling.
