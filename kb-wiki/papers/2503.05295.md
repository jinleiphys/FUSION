# Calculation of low-energy scattering parameters using artificial oscillator trap

> We introduce a recipe to estimate the low-energy scattering parameters of a quantum few-body system - scattering length, effective range, and shape parameter - by using only discrete state calculations. We place the system in an artificial oscillator trap of appropriate size and calculate the energies of the resulting discrete states close to the threshold of the system. The low-energy scattering parameters are then extracted - using a simple analytic formula - from the functional dependence of these energies upon the trap size. We first test the recipe against a simple model problem and then apply it to low-energy nucleon-nucleon scattering within the nuclear Model with Explicit Mesons in one sigma-meson approximation.

# Calculation of low-energy scattering parameters using artificial oscillator trap

## Key claim

The authors introduce a recipe to extract low-energy scattering parameters (scattering length, effective range, and shape parameter) from discrete spectrum calculations by placing a quantum few-body system in an artificial harmonic oscillator trap and fitting the trap-size dependence of near-threshold energies to an analytic formula derived for a zero-range potential. The recipe assumes that if two interaction models produce similar energy levels in the artificial trap, their scattering parameters must also be similar.

## Method

The system is placed in an artificial harmonic oscillator trap with variable range parameter b_o, and the energies of the two lowest discrete states near threshold are calculated as a function of b_o using correlated Gaussian expansions. These energies are then fitted to an analytic eigenvalue equation derived for a zero-range potential in an oscillator trap, which relates the trap-size-dependent energies to the scattering length a, effective range r_e, and shape parameter P. The method is first tested on the Volkov potential and then applied to neutron-proton scattering in the sigma-meson version of the Model with Explicit Mesons (sigmaMEM).

## Key numbers

- Volkov potential test: extracted scattering length a = -10.08 fm, effective range r_e = 2.38 fm, shape parameter P = 0.028; the reported scattering length for this system is a = -10.082 fm and bound state energy E_b = -0.54592 MeV (Volkov, year not specified in text)
- sigmaMEM neutron-proton scattering: extracted scattering length a = 5.82 fm, effective range r_e = 2.24 fm, shape parameter P = 0.008; experimental values are a = 5.41 fm and r_e = 1.74 fm (reference given as a-re in text)
- Volkov potential parameters: V_R = 144.86 MeV, b_R = 0.82 fm, V_A = -83.34 MeV, b_A = 1.60 fm; particle mass m = 469.471 MeV
- sigmaMEM coupling operator: W = 20.35 MeV times a Gaussian with range 3 fm
- Convergence: as few as 4 Gaussians in the np-channel and 12 Gaussians in the np-sigma-channel provide results converged to within three decimal digits

## Context

This work extends the class of artificial confining potential methods for extracting continuum observables from discrete spectrum calculations, which includes approaches by Busch et al. (year not specified), Suzuki (year not specified), and others cited in the paper. The recipe differs from existing methods by explicitly targeting low-energy parameters (scattering length, effective range, and shape parameter) and extracting them directly from calculated energies by fitting an analytic formula. The application to sigmaMEM shows that this simplified meson-exchange model overestimates the experimental triplet neutron-proton scattering parameters by about 10% to 20%, indicating the model is incomplete and likely requires inclusion of pion exchange contributions, which the authors state is under ongoing investigation.
