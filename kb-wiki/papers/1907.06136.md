# Power corrections in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scatterings at fixed target energies

> The COMPASS collaboration published precise data on production cross section of charged hadrons in lepton-hadron semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering, showing almost an order of magnitude larger than next-to-leading order QCD calculations when $P_{h_T}$ and $z_h$ are sufficiently large. We explore the role of power corrections to the theoretical calculations, and quantitatively demonstrate that the power corrections are extremely important for these data when the final-state multiplicity is low and the production kinematics is near the edge of phase space. Our finding motivates more detailed studies on power corrections for upcoming experiments at Jefferson Lab, as well as the future Electron-Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that next-to-leading power (NLP) corrections to semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) cross sections, which are formally suppressed by inverse powers of the large momentum transfer, can become extremely important when the final-state multiplicity is low and the production kinematics is near the edge of phase space, potentially explaining the order-of-magnitude discrepancy between COMPASS data and next-to-leading order QCD calculations for charged hadron production at large \(P_{h_T}\) and \(z_h\).

## Method

The authors calculate the leading-order perturbative contributions in \(\alpha_s\) to the partonic subprocess \(\gamma^{*}+q\to [q\bar{q}'(\kappa)]+q'\) that produces a quark-antiquark pair matching the valence flavor combination of the observed meson, using the QCD collinear factorization formalism extended to next-to-leading power. They compute the squared invariant amplitudes for both transversely and longitudinally polarized virtual photons with the quark-antiquark pair in axial-vector and vector spin states, and approximate the nonperturbative quark-antiquark fragmentation functions by relating them to the square of the meson distribution amplitude under the assumption of no additional hadrons in the final state.

## Key numbers

- COMPASS data show cross sections "almost an order of magnitude larger" than next-to-leading order QCD calculations when \(P_{h_T}\) and \(z_h\) are sufficiently large.
- The leading power contribution is suppressed by powers of \((1-z)\) from fragmentation functions with \(n\ge 1\) for meson production, while the NLP contribution can have a leading transition proportional to \(\delta(1-z)\) without this power suppression.
- The power suppression of NLP hard parts relative to leading power hard parts is by \(1/P_{h_T}^2\) (or \(1/Q^2\)) for spin-averaged cross sections.
- The color factors for the squared diagrams are \(C^{[1]} = (N_c^2-1)^2/(4N_c^2)\) for color-singlet and \(C^{[8]} = (N_c^2-1)/(4N_c^3)\) for color-octet states, with \(N_c=3\) in QCD.

## Context

This work extends the earlier recognition by Berger et al. (1979-1980) of the importance of power corrections to high-energy pion production in SIDIS, \(e^+e^-\) collisions, and hadron-hadron collisions, and builds on the factorization formalism for next-to-leading power contributions developed by Qiu (1990) and Kang et al. (2011, 2014). The findings motivate more detailed studies of power corrections for upcoming experiments at Jefferson Lab and the future Electron-Ion Collider, and provide a framework for understanding hadronization and color neutralization in QCD near the edge of phase space where hadron multiplicity is very low.
