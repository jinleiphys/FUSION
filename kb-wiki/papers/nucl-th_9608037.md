# Light Vector Mesons at Finite Baryon Density

> We summarize the current theoretical and experimental status of the spectral changes of vector mesons ($ρ$, $ω$, $ϕ$) at finite baryon density. Various approaches including QCD sum rules, effective theory of hadrons and bag models show decreasing of the vector meson masses in nuclear matter. Possibility to detect the mass shift through lepton pairs in $γ-A$, $p-A$ and $A-A$ reactions are also discussed.

# Light Vector Mesons at Finite Baryon Density

## Key claim

QCD sum rules and effective hadronic theories predict decreasing masses of the ρ, ω, and φ mesons in nuclear matter at zero temperature, but the magnitude of the shift depends on theoretical uncertainties in the density-dependent condensates and on the OZI breaking parameter y for the φ meson; the mass shift is not a universal constant but carries error bars of roughly ±0.05 to ±0.08 in the fractional change per normal nuclear density.

## Method

The paper reviews two main theoretical approaches. In the QCD sum rule approach (Hatsuda and Lee), the retarded current correlation function in nuclear matter is expanded via the operator product expansion (OPE) and matched to a phenomenological spectral function containing a resonance pole, continuum, and Landau damping term using Borel sum rules with a stability analysis. In effective theories, the vector-meson mass shift is derived from the Dirac-sea polarization of nucleons in nuclear matter within quantum hadrodynamics (QHD), where the decreasing effective nucleon mass in medium leads to a wave-function renormalization factor Z < 1 that reduces the vector-meson pole mass.

## Key numbers

- At normal nuclear matter density (ρ₀ = 0.17/fm³), the chiral condensate ⟨ūu⟩ is reduced by (34 ± 8)% from its vacuum value, based on the fermi-gas approximation with the πN sigma term Σ_πN = (45 ± 10) MeV.
- For ρ and ω mesons: m*_{ρ,ω} / m_{ρ,ω} = 1 − (0.16 ± 0.06) (ρ/ρ₀), from QCD sum rules (Hatsuda and Lee, 1991).
- For the φ meson: m*_φ / m_φ = 1 − (0.15 ± 0.05) y (ρ/ρ₀), where the OZI breaking parameter y = 0.1–0.2.
- Continuum threshold shift: √(S₀*/S₀) = 1 − (0.15 ± 0.05) (ρ/ρ₀).
- Resonance residue shift: F*/F = 1 − (0.24 ± 0.07) (ρ/ρ₀).
- In a bag model combined with QHD (Saito and Thomas): m*_{ρ,ω} / m_{ρ,ω} ∼ 1 − 0.09 (ρ/ρ₀).
- Monte Carlo based error analysis (Jin and Leinweber) gives m*_{ρ,ω} / m_{ρ,ω} = 1 − (0.22 ± 0.08) (ρ/ρ₀) and m*_φ / m_φ = 1 − (0.01 ± 0.01) (ρ/ρ₀).
- Total widths in vacuum: ρ = (1.3 fm)⁻¹, ω = (23 fm)⁻¹, φ = (45 fm)⁻¹.

## Context

This talk from the RHIC Summer Study 1996 summarizes the theoretical status of vector-meson mass shifts in nuclear matter, extending earlier work by Chin (1977) using quantum hadrodynamics and the general scaling hypothesis of Brown and Rho. It contrasts with calculations that found increasing ρ-meson mass from Fermi-sea polarization alone (Herman et al.) and instead supports the decreasing-mass scenario from vacuum polarization effects (Saito, Maruyama, Soutome; Kurasawa and Suzuki; Pisarski and others). The paper enables experimental searches for mass shifts via lepton pairs in γ-A, p-A, and A-A reactions, and it connects to the unexplained low-mass lepton pair enhancement observed by CERES/NA45 and HELIOS-3 at CERN.
