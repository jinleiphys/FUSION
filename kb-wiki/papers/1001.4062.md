# Treatment of confinement in the Faddeev approach to three-quark problems

> A method is presented that allows to solve the Faddeev integral equations of the semirelativistic constituent quark model. In such a model the quark-quark interaction is modeled by a infinitely rising confining potential and the kinetic energy is taken in a relativistic form. We solve the integral equations in Coulomb-Sturmian basis. This basis facilitate an exact treatment of the confining potentials.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method to solve Faddeev integral equations for three-quark systems with infinitely rising confining potentials without resorting to the approximate treatment of confinement used in prior work (Papp 1998, Papp 2000). The key improvement is an exact treatment of the asymptotic confinement via a contour integral of two-body Green's operators evaluated with a matrix continued fraction, though the numerical difference from the earlier approximate method is small and within experimental errors.

## Method

The authors split each quark-quark potential into a long-range confining part and a short-range non-confining part, applying the Faddeev procedure only to the short-range parts. They solve the resulting integral equations using a Coulomb-Sturmian (CS) separable expansion approach, where the channel Green's operator containing the confining interactions is evaluated via a contour integral of two-body Green's operators. The two-body Green's operators for semirelativistic Hamiltonians with linear confinement are computed using a matrix continued fraction method, treating the relativistic kinetic energy operator's CS matrix as approximately penta-diagonal for large basis indices.

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev approach to three-quark systems previously developed for harmonic oscillator confinement (Friar 1980) and later applied to non-relativistic Hamiltonians with linear confinement and hyperfine interactions (Silvestre-Brac 1985, Glozman 1996). It improves upon the authors' own earlier semirelativistic Faddeev calculations (Papp 1998, Papp 2000) by treating the confining potential exactly rather than approximately, using a technique inspired by Merkuriev's treatment of the Coulomb potential in three-body problems. The method enables reliable calculation of baryon ground and excited state wave functions for semirelativistic constituent quark models with confining interactions, with results compared to variational calculations (Glozman 1997).
