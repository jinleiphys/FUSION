# Momentum-space second-order pion-nucleus potential including medium effects in the $Δ(1232)$ region

> In this work, we develop an updated model for pion-nucleus scattering in the framework of the distorted wave impulse approximation in momentum space. We construct the second-order pion-nucleus potential, which involves analysis of pion-nucleus elastic scattering as a solution of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation. The potential is based on the individual pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes extracted from SAID, and its second-order correction is presented in detail. We estimate optimal energy-independent parameters of the potential by a multi-energy fit of the pion-${}^{12}$C total, reaction, and differential elastic cross sections. We show the predictive power by applying it to pion elastic scattering on ${}^{16}$O, ${}^{28}$Si, and ${}^{40}$Ca.

## Key claim

The paper develops an updated momentum-space model for pion-nucleus elastic scattering that includes a second-order optical potential with realistic two-body correlation functions and medium modifications to the resonant \(P_{33}\) pion-nucleon amplitude, but the model's predictive power is demonstrated only for spin-zero nuclei and the medium effects are parameterized through an effective \(\Delta\) self-energy fitted to \(\pi^\pm\)-\(^{12}\)C data rather than derived from first principles.

## Method

The authors construct the pion-nucleus optical potential within the Kerman-McManus-Thaler multiple scattering formalism, using the impulse approximation with pion-nucleon amplitudes from the SAID phase shift analysis (WI08) and a relativistic \(\Delta\)-isobar model for the \(P_{33}\) channel. The second-order potential includes a realistic two-body density with correlation functions, and medium effects are incorporated by modifying the \(\Delta\) propagator with an effective self-energy \(\Sigma_\Delta\) treated as a free parameter. The model parameters are determined by a multi-energy fit to \(\pi^\pm\)-\(^{12}\)C total, reaction, and differential elastic cross sections in the laboratory kinetic energy range 80–180 MeV, solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation in momentum space.

## Key numbers

- The \(\Delta\) decay width at resonance (\(W = m_\Delta\)) is \(\Gamma_\Delta \approx \SI{115}{MeV}\), derived from the relativistic \(\Delta\)-isobar model coupling constant \(f_\Delta^2/4\pi = 0.37\).
- The off-shell vertex factor uses \(\Lambda = 1.25 \text{ GeV}\) for the separable form of the pion-nucleon amplitude.
- The model is fitted to data in the energy range \(T_\text{lab} = 80\)–\(\SI{180}{MeV}\) laboratory kinetic energy for \(\pi^\pm\)-\(^{12}\)C scattering.
- The Roper resonance \(N^*(1440)\) mass is taken as \(m_{N^*} = \SI{1450}{MeV}\) with coupling \(f_{N^*}^2/4\pi = 0.015\), and the nucleon \(u\)-channel term contributes approximately 50% at \(T_\text{lab} = \SI{50}{MeV}\).
- The model is applied predictively to pion elastic scattering on \(^{16}\)O, \(^{28}\)Si, and \(^{40}\)Ca.

## Context

This work extends the multiple-scattering approach of Gmitro (1985) and the \(\Delta\)-hole model of Hirata (1977) and Oset (1979) by constructing a momentum-space second-order potential with more realistic two-body correlation functions than used in earlier works. The model enables direct application to pion photoproduction and neutrino-induced pion production on spin-zero nuclei, addressing final-state interaction corrections needed for neutrino oscillation measurements as discussed by Balantekin (2022) and Ankowski (2022).
