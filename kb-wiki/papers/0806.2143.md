# Two-Gluon Production and Longitudinal Correlations in the Color Glass Condensate

> We derive an analytical expression for the two-gluon production in the pA (light-heavy) collisions, and focus specifically on the rapidity dependent part. We approximate the gauge field from the heavy target as the Color Glass Condensate which interacts with the light projectile whose source density allows for a perturbative expansion. We discuss the longitudinal correlations of produced particles. Our calculation goes in part beyond the eikonal limit for the emitted gluons so that we can retain the exponential terms with respect to the rapidity difference. Our expression can thus describe the short-range correlations as well as the long-range ones for which our formula is reduced to the known expression. In a special case of two high-pt gluons in the back-to-back kinematics we find that dependence on the rapidity separation is only moderate even in the diagrammatically connected part.

## Key claim

The authors derive an analytical expression for two-gluon production in pA (light-heavy) collisions that goes beyond the eikonal limit for emitted gluons, retaining exponential terms in the rapidity difference. The calculation is valid only for rapidity separations parametrically less than \(1/\alpha_{\text{s}}\) because quantum evolution between the two gluons is not included.

## Method

The authors use the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism with a dense heavy target and a dilute light projectile, treating the projectile source density as a small perturbation. They solve the classical Yang-Mills equations in the light-cone gauge \(A^+=0\) to obtain the background gauge field and propagator, then compute the two-gluon production cross section via the LSZ reduction formula, keeping terms up to \(\mathcal{O}(g^4)\) and focusing on the connected (short-range correlation) diagram.

## Key numbers

- The disconnected, interference, and connected contributions to two-gluon production are all \(\mathcal{O}(g^4)\): (A) = \(\mathcal{O}(\rhop^4)\), (B) = \(g\mathcal{O}(\rhop^3)\), (C) = \(g^2\mathcal{O}(\rhop^2)\), where \(\rhop = \mathcal{O}(g)\).
- The rapidity separation \(|y_1 - y_2|\) is limited to be parametrically less than \(1/\alpha_{\text{s}}\) due to the neglect of quantum evolution.
- In the special case of two high-\(p_t\) gluons in back-to-back kinematics, the dependence on rapidity separation is only moderate even in the diagrammatically connected part.

## Context

This work extends the two-gluon production calculation of Jalilian-Marian and Kovchegov (2004), which assumed \(y_1 \gg y_2\) for simplicity, and is consistent with Baier et al. (2005) in the eikonal limit where emitted gluons are energetic enough to overwhelm transverse recoil. The results enable future studies of longitudinal correlations in pA collisions, which may provide information on the initial dense CGC state and connect to experimental observations of long-range rapidity correlations at RHIC.
