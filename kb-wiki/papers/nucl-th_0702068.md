# Jet-fluid string formation and decay in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> We propose a new hadronization mechanism, jet-fluid string (JFS) formation and decay, to understand observables in intermediate to high-$p_{T}$ regions comprehensively. In the JFS model, hard partons produced in jet lose their energy in traversing the QGP fluid, which is described by fully three-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations. When a jet parton escapes from the QGP fluid, it picks up a partner parton from a fluid and forms a color singlet string, then it decays to hadrons. We find that high-$p_T$ $v_2$ values in JFS are about two times larger than in the independent fragmentation model.

## Key claim

The Jet-Fluid String (JFS) model produces high-$p_T$ hadron elliptic flow ($v_2$) values about two times larger than the independent fragmentation model, but still underestimates the observed $v_2$ of pions at high-$p_T$ and yields only about half the data values at intermediate $p_T$ (3-6 GeV/$c$).

## Method

The authors combine mini-jet production via pythia (version 6.4) with first-order Gyulassy-L\'evai-Vitev (GLV) energy loss of jet partons traversing a quark-gluon plasma fluid described by fully three-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations. When a jet parton escapes the QGP fluid, it picks up a fluid parton at the phase boundary to form a color singlet string ($\bar{q}q$ or $gg$) with mass above 2 GeV, which then decays into hadrons via the Lund string fragmentation model in pythia. The overall energy loss factor $C$ is treated as an adjustable parameter fitted to reproduce the pion $p_T$-spectrum in mid-central Au+Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- Energy loss factor $C_{\rm JFS} \sim 6$ is needed to fit the high-$p_T$ pion spectrum in JFS, compared to $C_{\rm IF} \sim 2$ for independent fragmentation
- JFS gives $v_2 \sim 0.08$ at $p_T > 6$ GeV/$c$ for charged pions in mid-central (20-30%) Au+Au collisions, while data may be around 0.10
- The picked fluid parton contributes to enhance hadron $v_2$ by around 0.01
- Minimum $p_T$ of jet partons set to $p_{{\scriptscriptstyle T}0} \sim 2$ GeV, with $K$-factor $K \sim 1.85$ in pythia tuning
- Energy loss formula parameters: $\alpha_s=0.3$, $\mu=0.5$ GeV, $L=3$ fm
- $R_{AA}$ saturates at around $p_T \sim 6$ GeV/$c$ in JFS

## Context

This work extends the Hydro+Jet model of Hirano and Nara (2004) by introducing a new hadronization mechanism that bridges hydrodynamics and fragmentation. It contrasts with the independent fragmentation picture and is comparable to the Recombination+Fragmentation model (Duke group) and the quark coalescence model (Texas group), which consider direct hadron formation from quarks. The JFS model uniquely includes gluons as well as quarks forming strings that decay into multiple hadrons, and it enables future inclusion of lower-mass strings and fluid-fluid string formation to address the intermediate-$p_T$ $v_2$ deficit.
