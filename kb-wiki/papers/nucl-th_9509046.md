# Effects of Instantons on the Excited Baryons and Two-Nucleon Systems

> The effects of the spin-orbit and the tensor parts of the instanton-induced interaction on the excited nonstrange baryons up to $N$=2 and on the two-nucleon systems are investigated. The spin-orbit force from the instanton-induced interaction cancels most of that from the one-gluon exchange in the excited baryons while the spin-orbit force in the nucleon systems remains strong after the inclusion of the instanton-induced interaction. The model including the spin-orbit and the tensor terms of the instanton-induced interaction as well as the one-gluon exchange is found to reproduce successfully the excited baryon mass spectrum and the scattering phase shifts of two nucleons in the spin-triplet relative $P$-wave state.

## Key claim

The instanton-induced interaction (III) cancels most of the one-gluon exchange (OGE) spin-orbit force in excited baryons while leaving the spin-orbit force in two-nucleon systems strong, but this cancellation is channel-specific and occurs only for flavor-singlet quark pairs in relative odd-partial wave states within color-singlet systems.

## Method

The author employed a nonrelativistic quark potential model including both OGE and III, with the III derived from the instanton liquid model via nonrelativistic reduction to lowest order in (p/m). The model was applied to calculate the mass spectrum of excited nonstrange baryons up to N=2 using harmonic oscillator wave functions, and to compute scattering phase shifts for two-nucleon systems in the spin-triplet relative P-wave state using the resonating group method with effective meson exchange potentials added for long-range physics.

## Key numbers

- The N-Delta ground state mass difference used to set interaction strengths is 293 MeV (equation 6).
- The parameter p_III, representing the fraction of the S-wave N-Delta mass difference explained by III, is taken as 0.3-0.4 based on the eta-eta' mass difference.
- The harmonic oscillator size parameter b is taken slightly smaller than the real nucleon size; the quark mass m is 1/3 of the nucleon mass.
- For the excited baryon mass spectrum, the central Hamiltonian parameters are: E_0 = 1090 MeV + p_III * mu/2, Omega = 440 MeV, delta = 400 MeV.
- The spin-orbit force between 0p-wave quark pairs reduces by a factor of 0.32 from the p_III=0 case when p_III=0.4, while that between 0d-wave pairs reduces by (1-p_III)=0.6.
- The calculated Delta(3/2+) lowest energy level is 1734 MeV, about 100 MeV higher than the experimental Delta(1600).
- The decay matrix element for the higher Delta(5/2+) is about 30% smaller than that of the lower Delta(5/2+) state.

## Context

This work extends previous valence quark models (Isgur and Karl, 1978; Isgur, 1992) that had to remove the spin-orbit force by hand to reproduce the excited baryon spectrum, and earlier studies of the two-nucleon system using OGE (Oka and Yazaki, 1984; Takeuchi, Shimizu, and Yazaki, 1989). The paper builds on earlier work by Takeuchi (1994) demonstrating that III may solve the spin-orbit problem in P-wave systems. The results enable a unified description of both single-baryon and two-nucleon systems without artificially removing spin-orbit terms, and suggest that the flavor-singlet instanton-induced interaction plays an essential role in the QCD vacuum structure.
