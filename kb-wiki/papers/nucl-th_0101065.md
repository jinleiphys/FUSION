# Pairing Properties In Relativistic Mean Field Models Obtained From Effective Field Theory

> We apply recently developed effective field theory nuclear models in mean field approximation (parameter sets G1 and G2) to describe ground-state properties of nuclei from the valley of $β$-stability up to the drip lines. For faster calculations of open-shell nuclei we employ a modified BCS approach which takes into account quasi-bound levels owing to their centrifugal barrier, with a constant pairing strength. We test this simple prescription by comparing with available Hartree-plus-Bogoliubov results. Using the new effective parameter sets we then compute separation energies, density distributions and spin--orbit potentials in isotopic (isotonic) chains of nuclei with magic neutron (proton) numbers. The new forces describe the experimental systematics similarly to conventional non-linear $σ-ω$ relativistic force parameters like NL3.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a modified BCS approach incorporating quasibound states (due to centrifugal and Coulomb barriers) can provide a qualitatively reliable description of ground-state properties of nuclei far from stability, including near the drip lines, when used with relativistic mean field models derived from effective field theory (parameter sets G1 and G2), despite the fact that the asymptotic behavior of the particle densities does not match that of full Hartree-plus-Bogoliubov calculations.

## Method

The authors applied the relativistic mean field approximation using the effective field theory parameter sets G1 and G2, which include scalar-vector and vector-vector self-interactions beyond the standard nonlinear sigma-omega model. For open-shell nuclei, they employed a BCS approach with a constant pairing strength G = C/A, but modified it to include quasibound states (states bound by their centrifugal barrier, plus Coulomb barrier for protons) to approximately account for continuum contributions near the drip lines. They restricted the pairing space to one harmonic oscillator shell above and below the Fermi level, and tested this prescription by comparing with available relativistic Hartree-plus-Bogoliubov (RHB) results using the NL-SH and NL3 parameter sets.

## Key numbers

- Pairing strength constants: C_n = 21 MeV and C_p = 22.5 MeV for G1; C_n = 19 MeV and C_p = 25 MeV for G2; C_n = 20.5 MeV and C_p = 23 MeV for NL3
- For Ni isotopes, the drip line was reached at N = 66 with G1 and NL3, at N = 68 with G2, and at N = 72 with NL-SH
- For Sn isotopes, the drip line was reached at A = 176 for NL3; for G1 and G2, S_{2n} did not vanish at N = 126 and the neutron chemical potential became positive at N = 128
- For Pb isotopes, the drip line was found at N = 184 with a non-vanishing two-neutron separation energy
- For N = 28 isotones, S_{2p} vanished at Z = 30 for NL3 and at Z = 32 for G1 and G2
- For N = 126 isotones, the last nucleus stable against two-proton emission was 218U according to G1 and NL3, and 220Pu according to G2
- The effective mass at saturation M*_infinity/M for the EFT sets was approximately 0.65, compared to approximately 0.60 for typical RMF parameter sets

## Context

This work extends the application of the effective field theory nuclear models (parameter sets G1 and G2) developed by Furnstahl, Serot and Tang (1996, 1997) to nuclei far from the stability line, building on previous analyses of these models' nuclear matter and surface properties by Del Estal et al. (1999, 2000). The modified BCS approach with quasibound states follows the non-relativistic treatment of Dobaczewski et al. (1984, 1996) and Chabanat et al. (1998) for Skyrme forces, and the results are compared with relativistic Hartree-plus-Bogoliubov calculations by Lalazissis et al. (1998) and Meng et al. (1998, 1999) using the NL3 and NL-SH parameter sets. The work enables systematic surveys of nuclear properties across isotopic and isotonic chains using the EFT parameter sets, though the authors note that full RHB calculations would be needed to verify certain predictions, particularly regarding shell quenching at N = 184.
