# E(5), X(5), and Prolate to Oblate Shape Phase Transitions in Relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov Theory

> Relativistic mean field theory with the NL3 force is used for producing potential energy surfaces (PES) for series of isotopes suggested as exhibiting critical point symmetries. Relatively flat PES are obtained for nuclei showing the E(5) symmetry, while in nuclei corresponding to the X(5) case, PES with a bump are obtained. # E(5), X(5), and Prolate to Oblate Shape Phase Transitions in Relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov Theory

## Key claim

Relativistic Mean Field calculations with the NL3 force produce relatively flat potential energy surfaces for nuclei suggested as exhibiting E(5) critical point symmetry, but for nuclei suggested as exhibiting X(5) symmetry the potential energy surfaces show a bump (a deeper minimum in the prolate region and a shallower minimum in the oblate region) rather than a flat potential. The potential energy surfaces for the Pt chain of isotopes suggest a prolate to oblate shape transition at 186-Pt, not at 194-Pt as previously suggested by Linnemann based on experimental data.

## Method

The authors used the Relativistic Hartree Bogoliubov (RHB) model, a variation of Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) theory, with the NL3 Lagrangian parametrization to calculate potential energy surfaces (PES) for chains of isotopes. The nucleon spinors and meson fields were expanded in eigenfunctions of a deformed axially symmetric oscillator potential, and the pairing field was treated using the pairing part of the Gogny interaction, which provides a finite range and automatic momentum-space cutoff. Constrained calculations were performed to map the PES as functions of the quadrupole deformation parameter beta_2.

## Key numbers

- For the E(5) candidates: 100-Pd and 102-Pd show quite flat PES; 108-Pd and 110-Pd also show quite flat PES; 126-130-Xe exhibit flat PES; 132-134-Ba exhibit rather flat PES
- For the X(5) candidates: the N=90 isotones 150-Nd, 152-Sm, 154-Gd, and 156-Dy all show PES that are not flat, with a deeper minimum in the prolate (beta_2 > 0) regime and a shallower minimum in the oblate (beta_2 < 0) region
- For the prolate to oblate transition in the Pt chain: the transition occurs between 186-Pt (prolate) and 188-Pt (oblate), with the prolate minimum lower than the oblate minimum in 184-Pt and 186-Pt, and the opposite in 188-192-Pt
- For the Os chain: the prolate to oblate transition occurs between 192-Os and 194-Os, with the prolate minimum lower in 188-192-Os and the opposite in 194-Os and 196-Os
- The beta_1-bandheads (normalized to the energy of the 2_1+ state) exhibit a minimum at 186-Pt, and the crossover of the normalized bandheads of the beta_1 and gamma_1 bands also occurs at 186-Pt

## Context

This work extends the study of critical point symmetries E(5) (Iachello 2000) and X(5) (Iachello 2001) by testing their fundamental assumption of a flat potential using a completely different theoretical framework, Relativistic Mean Field theory. The E(5) results support the infinite square well potential assumption of Iachello (2000), while the X(5) results agree with earlier RMF calculations by Meng (using NL3, NL1, NLSH, and TM1 forces) and with Nilsson-Strutinsky-BCS calculations by Zhang, which also did not find flat PES for the N=90 isotones. The finding that 186-Pt is the critical nucleus for the prolate to oblate transition, rather than 194-Pt as suggested by Linnemann, is supported by multiple pieces of evidence including bandhead systematics and mapping onto the Interacting Boson Model symmetry triangle. The results enable further investigation using PES as functions of both beta_2 and gamma, and using methods beyond the mean field approximation.
