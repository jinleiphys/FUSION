# A Mean Field Theory of the Chiral Phase Transition

> The recent discussions by Kocić and Kogut on the nature of the chiral phase transition are reviewed. The mean-field nature of the transition suggested by these authors is supported in random matrix theory by Verbaarschot and Jackson which reproduces many aspects of QCD lattice simulations. In this paper, we point out physical arguments that favor a mean-field transition, not only for zero density and high temperature, but also for finite density. We show, using the Gross-Neveu model in 3 spatial dimensions in mean-field approximation, how the phase transition is constructed. In order to reproduce the lowering of the $ρ=0$, $T=0$ vacuum evaluated in lattice calculations, we introduce {nucleons} rather than constituent quarks in negative energy states, down to a momentum cut-off of $Λ$. We also discuss Brown-Rho scaling of the hadron masses in relation to the QCD phase transition, and how this scaling affects the CERES and HELIOS-3 dilepton experiments.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the chiral phase transition in QCD is mean-field in nature not only at zero density and high temperature, but also at finite density, contrary to earlier expectations based on dimensional reduction and universality arguments. They hedge that relatively firm arguments about the nature of the phase transition can be made only for zero density and finite temperature, and they assume mean-field behavior for finite densities and temperatures.

## Method

The authors use the Gross-Neveu model in three spatial dimensions in mean-field approximation to construct the chiral phase transition, writing down gap equations for finite density that include both negative-energy quark states (down to a momentum cut-off Λ) and positive-energy states up to the Fermi momentum. They extend Walecka mean-field theory by introducing nucleons rather than constituent quarks in negative energy states to reproduce the lowering of the zero-density, zero-temperature vacuum energy evaluated in lattice calculations. The finite-temperature extension is made by integrating over positive and negative energy quark states with inclusion of fermion temperature weighting factors following the work of Bernard et al. (1987).

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass m_Q = 310 MeV from the mean-field Goldberger-Treiman relation using g_σQQ = 10/3 and f_π = 93 MeV
- Effective bag constant B_eff = 280 MeV/fm³ from Walecka theory with m_σ = 705 MeV
- Full gluon condensate B = 471 MeV/fm³ = (254 MeV)⁴
- Bag constant from Gross-Neveu model with m_Q ≈ 500 MeV and Λ = 560 MeV: B = 219 MeV/fm³, giving f_π = 91 MeV and ⟨ūu⟩ = -(239 MeV)³
- Bag constant using nucleons in negative energy states with Λ = 550 MeV: B = 278 MeV/fm³, giving ⟨ūu⟩ = (-249 MeV)³
- Adopted effective bag constant B_eff ≈ 250 MeV/fm³
- Critical temperature T_c = 147 MeV from setting pressure to zero with B_eff = 250 MeV/fm³
- Energy density just above phase transition: ε ≈ 1 GeV/fm³ for B_eff ≈ 250 MeV/fm³
- Critical Fermi momentum k_F^c expressed in terms of Λ, m_σ, and g_σQQ
- Phase transition at T=0 occurs at about 2.5 times nuclear matter density for quark masses of ~500 MeV, and about 4.5 times nuclear matter density for nucleon-based calculation
- Freezeout temperature T_fo ∼ 120-140 MeV from Si + A collisions at AGS

## Context

This paper reviews and extends the discussions by Kocić and Kogut (1995) on the mean-field nature of the chiral phase transition, supported by random matrix theory work by Verbaarschot (1995) and Jackson and Verbaarschot (1995) that reproduces aspects of QCD lattice simulations. The work contradicts the earlier Pisarski-Wilczek (1984) and Wilczek (1992) picture that the chiral transition should lie in the same universality class as the three-dimensional O(4) magnet. The authors connect their mean-field description to Brown-Rho scaling (1991) of hadron masses and discuss implications for the CERES and HELIOS-3 dilepton experiments at CERN SPS, as well as for the "cool kaons" observed in E814 and E877 experiments at AGS.
