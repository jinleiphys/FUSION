# Theoretical predictions for extraction of GEn from semi-inclusive electron scattering on polarized 3He based on various nucleon-nucleon interactions

> The process 3He(e,e'n) with polarized electrons and 3He in the initial state is theoretically analyzed with the aim to search for sensitivity to the electric form factor of the neutron, GEn. Faddeev calculations based on five high precision nucleon-nucleon force models are employed and stability versus exchange of the nucleon-nucleon forces is demonstrated.

# Theoretical predictions for extraction of GEn from semi-inclusive electron scattering on polarized 3He based on various nucleon-nucleon interactions

## Key claim

The ratio A_perp / A_par in the polarized 3He(e,e'n) process is stable under exchange of five different high-precision nucleon-nucleon potentials, but only when the analysis is restricted to the upper end of the neutron energy spectrum; at lower neutron energies the spread among NN force predictions increases substantially, especially at higher q^2 values.

## Method

Faddeev calculations for the 3He(e,e'n) reaction were performed using five high-precision nucleon-nucleon force models: AV18, CD Bonn, Nijmegen 93, Nijmegen I, and Nijmegen II. The spin asymmetries A_perp and A_par were computed including final state interactions, and the ratio A_perp / A_par was evaluated as a function of neutron energy for several q^2 values. For AV18, consistent meson-exchange currents were included; for the other four potentials, only the standard nonrelativistic single nucleon current operator was used.

## Key numbers

- At q^2 = 0.45 (GeV/c)^2 and neutron energy E_n = 260 MeV, the spread in A_perp / A_par due to different NN potentials is about 13%, while shifts caused by varying G_E^n by +/- 25% reach 70%.
- For the highest neutron energies, A_par is stable under exchange of NN forces with effects below 1%, and insensitive to G_E^n variations with changes below 2%.
- MEC effects are significant for q^2 = 0.05 - 0.2 (GeV/c)^2 but decrease strongly at the upper end of the neutron spectra.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Golak et al., 2002) which analyzed the same process using only the AV18 potential with consistent meson-exchange currents. The present paper demonstrates that the theoretical predictions for the ratio A_perp / A_par are robust against changing the NN interaction model, provided measurements are restricted to the highest neutron energies. This stability enables experimental extraction of G_E^n from the 3He(e,e'n) reaction without significant theoretical uncertainty from the choice of NN force, though MEC effects must still be accounted for at lower q^2 values.
