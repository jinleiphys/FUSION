# On the chiral low-density theorem

> We show how the linear "low-density theorem" of Drukarev and Levin can be extended to arbitrary positive integer power of the baryon density $ρ$. The n^th coefficient in the McLaurin expansion of the fermion condensate's $ρ$ dependence is the connected n-nucleon sigma term matrix element. We calculate the $O(ρ^2)$ coefficient in lowest-order perturbative approximation to the linear sigma model and then show how this and other terms can be iterated to arbitrarily high order. Convergence radius of the result is discussed.

# On the chiral low-density theorem

## Key claim

The linear low-density theorem of Drukarev and Levin can be extended to arbitrary positive integer powers of baryon density, with the nth coefficient in the McLaurin expansion of the fermion condensate's density dependence being the connected n-nucleon sigma term matrix element; however, this series need not uniquely determine the condensate for arbitrary densities because its radius of convergence may be small or even zero, and the function may have poles or branch cuts in the complex density plane.

## Method

The author derives a general formula relating the finite-density sigma term to connected n-nucleon sigma term matrix elements using current-algebraic identities and S-matrix decomposition into disconnected and connected parts, then applies this to the linear sigma model in lowest-order perturbative (Born) approximation. The O(ρ²) coefficient is calculated from pion-two-nucleon connected diagrams, including pion-rescattering, sigma-meson-in-flight, and initial-/final-/intermediate-state interaction graphs, and these primitive contributions are iterated to infinite order via geometric series resummation.

## Key numbers

- The single-nucleon sigma term ⟨Σ⟩_N lies between 45 and 65 MeV.
- The sigma-nucleon coupling constant g₀ = (g_A M / f_π) = 12.6.
- The sigma meson mass lies between 400 MeV and 1200 MeV.
- The one-nucleon pion-rescattering series has a critical density ρ_c1 = (f_π m_π)² / ⟨Σ⟩_N, which equals roughly 1.8 times normal nuclear density for ⟨Σ⟩_N = 65 MeV, or 3 times normal nuclear density for ⟨Σ⟩_N = 45 MeV.
- The two-nucleon sigma term in the linear sigma model is ⟨Σ⟩_2N = (g₀ m_π / m_σ²)², which is positive.
- The two-nucleon resummation yields poles at imaginary densities ±i ρ_c2, a factor √2 larger in absolute value than ρ_c1.
- The kaon-nucleon sigma term ⟨Σ⟩_{KN} = 200 MeV, implying the radius of convergence for kaon condensation is at best a fraction of one normal nuclear density.

## Context

This work extends the linear low-density theorem of Drukarev and Levin (1990-1991) to arbitrarily high orders in baryon density, placing earlier intuitive calculations on formally sounder quantum field theory grounds. The result generalizes the resummation of single-nucleon sigma terms into a geometric progression, as noted by Ericson (1993), and comes close to the O(ρ²) prediction formulated by Birse and McGovern (1993). The diagrammatic methods build on techniques developed in the 1960s and 1970s by Hubbard (1957), Campbell (1977-1979), and Foldy and Walecka (1969) for describing pion propagation in nuclear matter. The work enables systematic calculation of higher-order density corrections to the quark condensate and provides a clear definition of NN correlations in this context, with implications for the behavior of pseudoscalar meson masses at finite baryon density and the kaon condensation problem.
