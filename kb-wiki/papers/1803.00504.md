# Hyperons: the strange ingredients of the nuclear equation of state

> In this article we will review the role and properties of hyperons in finite and infinite nuclear systems. In particular, we will revise different production mechanisms of hypernuclei, as well as several aspects of hypernuclear $γ$-ray spectroscopy, and the weak decay modes of hypernuclei. Then we will discuss the construction of hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions on the basis of the meson-exchange and chiral effective field theories. Recent developments based on the so-called V$_{low\,\, k}$ approach and lattice QCD will also be adressed. Finally, we will go over some of the effects of hyperons on the properties of neutron and proto-neutron stars with an emphasis on the so-called "hyperon puzzle", {\it i.e.,} the problem of the strong softening of the equation of state, and the consequent reduction of the maximum mass, induced by the presence of hyperons, a problem which has become more intringuing and difficult to solve due the recent measurements of $\sim 2M_\odot$ millisecond pulsars. We will discuss some of the solutions proposed to tackle this problem. We will also re-examine the role of hyperons on the cooling properties of newly born neutron stars and on the development of the so-called r-mode instability.

## Key claim

This review argues that hyperons (strange baryons) are essential components of dense nuclear matter and neutron star interiors, but their presence softens the equation of state and reduces the maximum neutron star mass, creating the "hyperon puzzle" that conflicts with the observed ~2 solar mass millisecond pulsars.

## Method

The paper reviews the construction of hyperon-nucleon (YN) and hyperon-hyperon (YY) interactions using meson-exchange models and chiral effective field theory, along with recent developments from the V_low_k approach and lattice QCD. It surveys production mechanisms of hypernuclei including strangeness exchange (K-,pi-), associated production (pi+,K+), and electroproduction (e,e'K+) reactions, as well as hypernuclear gamma-ray spectroscopy and weak decay modes. The review then examines how hyperons affect neutron star properties, focusing on the equation of state, maximum mass, cooling, and r-mode instability.

## Key numbers

- The bond energy of two Lambdas in double-Lambda hypernuclei was measured as Delta B_LambdaLambda = 1.01 +/- 0.2 (+0.18/-0.11) MeV for 6_LambdaLambda He, later revised to 0.67 +/- 0.17 MeV due to a change in the Xi- mass
- A deeply bound state of the Xi--14N system was observed with binding energy 4.38 +/- 0.25 MeV
- The momentum transferred to the Lambda in the n(K-,pi-)Lambda reaction is much lower than in n(pi+,K+)Lambda or p(gamma,K+)Lambda reactions
- The non-mesonic weak decay mode (Lambda+N -> N+N) produces nucleons with momentum ~420 MeV/c, while the two-nucleon induced mode produces ~340 MeV/c
- The mesonic decay mode (Lambda -> N+pi) produces nucleons with momentum ~100 MeV/c, which is smaller than the typical Fermi momentum in nuclei (~270 MeV/c)
- Recent measurements of millisecond pulsars show masses of ~2 solar masses, which creates tension with the hyperon-induced softening of the equation of state

## Context

This review extends the work of Balberg and Gal (Skyrme-type forces with hyperons), Glendenning (relativistic mean field treatment of hyperonic matter), and various microscopic many-body calculations using the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation. It enables future comparisons between theoretical predictions of neutron star properties and astrophysical observations from gravitational wave detections by LIGO/Virgo and new experimental facilities at FAIR/GSI, JLAB, and J-PARC. The paper also addresses the unresolved "hyperon puzzle" where the strong softening of the equation of state from hyperons reduces the maximum neutron star mass below the observed ~2 solar mass threshold.
