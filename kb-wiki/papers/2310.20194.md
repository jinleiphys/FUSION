# Evolution of topological charge through chiral anomaly transport

> Built upon the state-of-the-art model a multiphase transport (AMPT), we develop a new module of chiral anomaly transport (CAT), which can trace the evolution of the initial topological charge of gauge field created through sphaleron transition at finite temperature and external magnetic field in heavy ion collisions. The eventual experimental signals of chiral magnetic effect(CME) can be measured. The CAT explicitly shows the generation and evolution of the charge separation, and the signals of CME through the CAT are quantitatively in agreement with the experimental measurements in Au+Au collision at $\sqrt{s}=200 {\rm GeV}$, and the centrality dependence of the CME fraction follows that of the fireball temperature.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Chiral Anomaly Transport (CAT) module, built on the AMPT model, can quantitatively connect the initial topological charge created via sphaleron transitions to the final experimental chiral magnetic effect (CME) signal, but only under the specific condition that the initial chiral chemical potential is parameterized as \(\mu_5 = (2.1 T + \sqrt{eB})\,\rm{GeV}\), where \(T\) is the local temperature and \(eB\) the magnetic field strength.

## Method

The authors developed the CAT module to replace the parton cascade (ZPC) component of the AMPT model. The module solves the chiral kinetic equation for massless quarks in a magnetic field using a particle-in-cell method, tracking the evolution of helicity and charge separation. The initial magnetic field is calculated from spectator protons in HIJING and decays with an effective lifetime \(\tau_B = 0.4\) fm, while the initial chirality imbalance is set by a chiral chemical potential \(\mu_5\) derived from the sphaleron transition rate at finite temperature and magnetic field. The simulation proceeds through parton evolution, quark coalescence, and hadron rescattering to produce final-state observables.

## Key numbers

* The CME fraction \(f_{CME}\) decreases from central to peripheral collisions in Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, following the centrality dependence of the fireball temperature, in agreement with STAR data (STAR 2021).
* The chiral chemical potential is parameterized as \(\mu_5 = (2.1 T + \sqrt{eB})\,\rm{GeV}\), where \(T\) is the local temperature and \(eB\) the magnetic field strength.
* The magnetic field evolution is modeled as \(e\mathbf{B}(t,\mathbf{x}) = e\mathbf{B}(0,\mathbf{x}) / [1 + (t/\tau_B)^2]\) with \(\tau_B = 0.4\) fm.
* Binary elastic collisions between partons are modeled with a cross-section of \(\sigma = 3\) mb.
* The initial electromagnetic field is calculated at \(t_0 = 0\) fm using the Liénard-Wiechert potentials from spectator protons in HIJING, with the fine-structure constant \(\alpha_{EM} \approx 1/137\).

## Context

This work extends the AMPT model (Lin 2004) by adding chiral anomaly transport, addressing a limitation of the anomalous-viscous fluid dynamics (AVFD) model (Shi 2017, Huang 2021) which does not incorporate the non-equilibrium stage when the magnetic field is strongest. The CAT module provides a quantitative link between initial topological charge and final CME signals, enabling future studies of isobar collisions and low-energy Au+Au collisions to further test the chiral magnetic effect.
