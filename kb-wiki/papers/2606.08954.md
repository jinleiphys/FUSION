# Nuclear matrix element of $2νββ$ decay of $^{76}$Ge: roles of high-lying states and two-body currents

> We present a microscopic analysis of the nuclear matrix element (NME) of the two-neutrino double-$β$ ($2νββ$) decay for open-shell heavy deformed nuclei, taking into account the fact that nuclear level density increases rapidly with excitation energy as well as the contribution of two-body current (2BC). Taking $^{76}$Ge $\rightarrow$ $^{76}$Se decay as an example, we found that due to the rapid increase of the level density of the intermediate nucleus $^{76}$As with excitation energy $E_n$, the single-$β$ Gamow-Teller (GT) matrix elements become highly fragmented with very small magnitude, and exhibit seemingly random sign patterns at high $E_n$ region. This leads to an effective cancellation at high $E_n$ region in calculating the $2νββ$ NME which then turns out to converge at $E_n \lesssim 5$ MeV, indicating that the contribution of high-lying states of the intermediate nucleus to $2νββ$ NME is negligible. Besides, the 2BC in the transition operator is found to contribute $\sim 10\%$ quenching to the $2νββ$-decay NME of $^{76}$Ge.

## Key claim

The two-neutrino double-beta decay nuclear matrix element of 76Ge converges at excitation energies of the intermediate nucleus below approximately 5 MeV, because the single-beta Gamow-Teller matrix elements become highly fragmented with random sign patterns at higher energies and cancel effectively; the two-body current contributes roughly 10% quenching to the NME, but this effect depends on the coupling constants used.

## Method

The authors performed projected shell model (PSM) calculations with a large multi-quasiparticle configuration space (up to about 25,000 intermediate 1+ states in 76As within 18 MeV excitation) and angular-momentum projection, using an effective Hamiltonian with monopole, quadrupole, and two-body Gamow-Teller forces. They computed the 2νββ NME by explicitly summing over all intermediate 1+ states without closure approximation, including both the one-body axial current (Gamow-Teller operator) and the leading two-body axial current operator with coupling constants (c3, c4) and cD taken from the literature.

## Key numbers

- The 2νββ-decay NME of 76Ge converges at intermediate excitation energy En ≲ 5 MeV, saturating at a value of approximately 0.144 when only the one-body current is considered.
- The two-body current provides about 10% quenching to the NME, reducing it from 0.144 to 0.127 (about 12% quenching) for the coupling set (c3, c4) = (-3.2, 5.4) and cD = -0.2.
- The corresponding 2νββ half-life increases from 1.0 × 10^21 yr (one-body current only) to 1.3 × 10^21 yr (including two-body current with the above couplings).
- Phenomenological quenching of gA to 1.0 yields a half-life of about 2.5 × 10^21 yr.
- The phase space factor used is G^2ν = 0.4817 × 10^-19 yr^-1 for 76Ge.
- The energy denominator parameter E0 = 1.94103 MeV, derived from Qββ(0+) = 2.03906 MeV and the 76As-76Ge mass difference of 0.9215 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of cancellation in 2νββ NMEs: shell model calculations for 48Ca (Brown 1990) found saturation near 10 MeV, while QRPA calculations for 48Ca (Raduta 2005) and for 76Ge (Fang 2010) found saturation above 10-15 MeV. The present PSM calculation with large configuration space finds saturation at the lower energy of about 5 MeV for 76Ge, and includes two-body current contributions for the first time without closure or normal-ordering approximations. The results enable improved constraints on nuclear-structure models used for 0νββ decay NME calculations, and highlight the need to reliably determine the two-body current coupling constants.
