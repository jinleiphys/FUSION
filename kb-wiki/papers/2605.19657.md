# The stability of color-flavor-locked quark matter and massive CFL quark stars

> Owing to the emergence of attractive interactions between quarks, color superconductivity is expected to occur, with the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase favored at high densities. This work investigates the absolute stability of beta-equilibrated CFL quark matter in bulk within the modified Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, under color and electric charge neutrality conditions relevant to compact stars. Motivated by the possible existence of an ultra-low-mass central compact object in the supernova remnant HESS J1731-347 and the "mass-gap" secondary component in the GW190814 event, we systematically explore how vector repulsion, attractive diquark pairing, and nonperturbative vacuum effects influence the stiffness of CFL quark matter and its stability. Our findings suggest the existence of a physically viable region of parameter space in which the CFL phase is the true ground state of strongly interacting matter, thereby theoretically supporting the scenario of self-bound quark stars. This configuration is not only consistent with current astrophysical constraints from NICER and LIGO/Virgo observations, but also provides a possible explanation for both the $~2.6\ M_{\odot}$ secondary component in GW190814 and the ultra-low-mass compact object with $M = 0.77^{+0.20}_{-0.17}\ M_{\odot}$in HESS J1731-347.

# The stability of color-flavor-locked quark matter and massive CFL quark stars

## Key claim

The authors claim that there exists a physically viable region of parameter space within the modified Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model where the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase is the true ground state of strongly interacting matter, but this result depends on the diquark coupling strength being sufficiently large (H/G > 1.5) and the vacuum bag constant B being within a specific range that simultaneously satisfies both the absolute stability condition and the requirement that baryon density at zero pressure exceeds nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors employ a three-flavor NJL-type model with quark-antiquark vector interactions and diquark pairing interactions, treating the vector coupling g_V and diquark coupling H as free parameters while fixing the scalar coupling G, current quark masses, and the six-fermion 't Hooft interaction K from vacuum phenomenology using the Hatsuda-Kunihiro (1994) parameter set. They solve the coupled gap equations for chiral condensates and diquark condensates under electric and color charge neutrality conditions at zero temperature, then compute the equation of state and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius relations for CFL quark stars.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum constituent quark masses are M_u^vac = 324.6 MeV and M_s^vac = 528.1 MeV, obtained from the Hatsuda-Kunihiro parameter set with cutoff Lambda = 631.4 MeV, current masses m_u,d = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 135.7 MeV, G*Lambda^2 = 1.835, and K*Lambda^5 = 9.29
- For the parameter set (g_V/G = 1.2, H/G = 1.2), the diquark gaps reach Delta_us = 142.8 MeV and Delta_ud = 152.5 MeV at quark chemical potential mu = 600.6 MeV
- The absolute stability condition requires energy per baryon E/A(CFL) < 930 MeV, which is the energy per baryon of iron-56
- For the parameter set (g_V/G = 0.5, H/G = 1.6, B^{1/4} = 90.4 MeV), the diquark pairing gap Delta_us is approximately 153.5 MeV at the stellar surface and reaches about 248.9 MeV at the stellar center
- The central baryon number densities of CFL quark stars lie in the range n_B = 6.17-7.34 n_0, where n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} is nuclear saturation density
- The model can simultaneously explain the ultra-low-mass compact object with M = 0.77^{+0.20}_{-0.17} M_sun in HESS J1731-347 and the secondary component with mass 2.59^{+0.08}_{-0.09} M_sun in GW190814
- At baryon chemical potential mu_B = 2.6 GeV, the CFL pairing gap is constrained to be below 457 MeV at 95% confidence under conservative assumptions and below 216 MeV under more realistic assumptions, consistent with the NJL model results

## Context

This work extends previous studies of color-superconducting quark matter within NJL-type models (Alford et al., 2008; Buballa, 2005) and specifically builds on the authors' prior investigation of 2SC quark matter (Yuan et al., 2024), which found no parameter space supporting absolutely stable 2SC quark stars. The study is motivated by the Bodmer-Witten hypothesis (Bodmer, 1971; Witten, 1984) and recent observations of the ultra-low-mass compact object in HESS J1731-347 (Doroshenko et al., 2022) and the mass-gap secondary in GW190814 (Abbott et al., 2020). The results enable future investigations of CFL phase effects on compact-star cooling, r-mode instabilities, pulsar glitches, and gamma-ray bursts, and are broadly consistent with model-agnostic constraints on the CFL pairing gap from neutron star observations (Kumar et al., 2024).
