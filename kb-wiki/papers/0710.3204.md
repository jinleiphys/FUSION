# Computational Model for Electron-Nucleon Scattering and Weak Charge of the Nucleon

> We show how computational symbolic packages such as FeynArts and FormCalc can be adopted for the evaluation of one-loop hadronic electroweak radiative corrections for electron-nucleon scattering and applied to calculations of the nucleon weak charge. Several numerical results are listed, and found to be in good agreement with the current experimental data.

# Computational Model for Electron-Nucleon Scattering and Weak Charge of the Nucleon

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that computational symbolic packages (FeynArts, FormCalc, LoopTools) can be adapted to evaluate one-loop hadronic electroweak radiative corrections for electron-nucleon scattering, but only after introducing fictitious vector bosons of mass Λ to remove momentum dependence from the monopole form factor couplings. The resulting numerical predictions for nuclear weak charges agree with experiment within uncertainties that are dominated by electromagnetic form factor measurements rather than the NLO calculation itself.

## Method

The authors constructed a FeynArts model file for electron-nucleon scattering by treating the proton and neutron as explicit particles with Dirac and Pauli-type couplings, using a monopole form factor approximation (Λ² = 0.83 m_N²) for the electromagnetic and weak form factors. To handle the momentum-dependent couplings introduced by the monopole form factors, they expanded each Feynman diagram with a structured nucleon into a set of diagrams with point-like nucleons by introducing fictitious vector bosons (δγ, δZ, δ₁Z, δ₂Z) of mass Λ, allowing the automated evaluation of 36 box diagrams, 116 self-energy graphs plus 6 counterterms, and hadronic vertex corrections using FormCalc and LoopTools. For the hadronic vacuum polarization, they used effective quark masses (m_u = m_d ≈ 45 MeV at low momentum transfer) fitted to reproduce the dispersion integral result Δα_hadr^(γ(5))(m_Z²) = -0.02761.

## Key numbers

- Monopole form factor parameter: Λ² = 0.83 m_N², obtained from fitting electromagnetic form factors in the low momentum transfer region
- Light quark effective masses: m_u = m_d = 53 MeV (reproduces Δα_hadr^(γ(5))(m_Z²) = -0.027609); at low momentum transfer (√s < 4.0 GeV): m_u = m_d ≈ 45 MeV
- Extrapolated form factors at zero momentum transfer: C_1p = 0.0360 ± 0.0005, C_1n = -0.4950 ± 0.0020
- Theoretical nuclear weak charges: Cs_55^133 = -73.26 ± 0.26, Tl_81^205 = -114.9 ± 0.4, Bi_83^209 = -118.8 ± 0.4
- Experimental nuclear weak charges for comparison: Cs_55^133 = -72.65 ± 0.28 ± 0.34, Tl_81^205 = -114.8 ± 1.2 ± 3.4, Bi_83^209 = -140 ± 40
- NLO corrections contribute approximately 1.5% to the nuclear weak charge results
- The Q_weak experiment aims to measure the proton's weak charge with approximately 4% combined statistical and systematic errors at Q² = 0.03 GeV²

## Context

This work extends the earlier tree-level analysis of the nucleon weak charge by Marciano and Sirlin (1984) and the updated Standard Model prediction with QCD structure uncertainties by Erler, Kurylov, and Ramsey-Musolf (2003). The authors had previously tested the automated approach for one-quark radiative corrections (Aleksejevs, Barkanova, Blunden, 2002). The computational model enables full kinematic dependence in NLO calculations, making it adaptable to current and future parity-violating experiments such as the Q_weak collaboration's measurement of the proton weak charge, which will test the running of the weak mixing angle to low Q².
