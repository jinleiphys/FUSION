# Femtoscopic signature of strong radial flow in high-multiplicity $pp$ collisions

> Hydrodynamic simulations are used to calculate the identical pion HBT radii, as a function of the pair momentum $k_{\rm T}$. This dependence is sensitive to the magnitude of the collective radial flow in the transverse plane, and thus comparison to ALICE data enables us to derive its magnitude. By using hydro solutions with variable initial parameters we conclude that in this case fireball explosions start with a very small initial size, well below 1 ${\rm fm}$.

## Key claim

Hydrodynamic simulations of high-multiplicity proton-proton collisions at the LHC show that the observed decrease of identical-pion HBT radii with pair transverse momentum can only be reproduced if the fireball explosion starts with an initial size well below 1 fm, implying the presence of strong collective radial flow in an unprecedentedly small system.

## Method

The authors used ideal relativistic hydrodynamics, starting with a Gubser conformal solution for the early evolution (parametrized by a size parameter q and an energy density parameter epsilon_0 fixed by charged particle multiplicity), then switching at tau_0 = 0.6 fm to a numerical solution with a realistic lattice-based equation of state. Pions were sampled from the freezeout surface at T_f = 120 MeV using the Cooper-Frye formula, and two-particle correlation functions were constructed in the out-side-long parametrization to extract HBT radii as functions of k_T.

## Key numbers

- The HBT volume (R_o R_s R_ell) from simulations with q = 1.5 to 1.7 fm^{-1} reproduces the ALICE data well for dN_ch/d_eta = 27.
- The ratio R_o/R_s decreases with k_T; for the largest q values the suppression at high k_T is strongest, with ALICE data showing even further suppression than the q = 1.7 fm^{-1} result.
- The reduction in R_o over the interval k_T = 0.1 - 0.7 GeV reaches about a factor of 4 in magnitude for the highest multiplicity bin.
- Freezeout times for both small and large fireballs are similar (approximately 4 fm), but the flow magnitude is significantly larger for smaller fireballs (larger q).

## Context

This work extends the use of HBT interferometry, originally developed for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC (Pratt, 2008) and applied to pp collisions by the ALICE Collaboration (Aggarwal, 2010), to extract the radial flow strength in high-multiplicity pp events. Previous analyses of radial flow in such collisions relied on identified particle spectra (Shuryak, 2013; Ghosh, 2014); this paper provides an independent cross-check via femtoscopy. The results enable future studies of the initial-state geometry in the most extreme density conditions created at the LHC, and challenge models of the initial state to produce fireballs smaller than 1 fm.
