# Collective modes and current-algebraic sum rules in nuclear medium

> In-medium sum rules following from the chiral charge algebra of QCD are reviewed, and new sum rules are derived. The new sum rules relate the I^G(J^{PC})=1^-(0^{++}) excitations (quantum numbers of a_0(980)) to the scalar and isovector densities, and are nontrivial for the isospin-asymmetric medium. We present an extensive illustration of the sum rules with help of quark matter in the Nambu-Jona--Lasinio model. Collective excitations different from the usual meson branches (spin-isospin sound modes) are shown to contribute significantly to the sum rules and to play a crucial role in the limit of vanishing current quark masses.

## Key claim

The paper derives new current-algebraic sum rules for scalar-isovector excitations (quantum numbers of the a₀(980) meson) that are nontrivial only in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, and shows that collective spin-isospin sound modes, rather than the usual meson branches, can dominate the saturation of these sum rules in the chiral limit.

## Method

The authors start from the SU(2)⊗SU(2) chiral charge algebra of QCD and sandwich operator identities between nuclear matter states with fixed baryon density and isospin density. They derive sum rules relating in-medium condensates to sums over excitation energies and matrix elements of axial and vector currents. The sum rules are then illustrated using quark matter in the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the Hartree approximation, with two parameter sets fitted to vacuum meson properties and a sharp three-momentum cutoff regulator.

## Key numbers

- The quark condensate at nuclear saturation density drops to about 70% of its vacuum value (from model-independent predictions of Drukarev and Grieg).
- Parameter set I: G_σ = 7.55 GeV⁻², G_δ = 5.41 GeV⁻², G_ρ = 7.09 GeV⁻², Λ = 750 MeV, m_u = 2.52 MeV, m_d = 4.52 MeV.
- Parameter set II: G_σ = 4.35 GeV⁻², G_δ = 3.34 GeV⁻², G_ρ = 12.4 GeV⁻², Λ = 954 MeV, m_u = 1.03 MeV, m_d = 3.03 MeV.
- In isospin-asymmetric medium with ρ_{I=1} < 0, the positive-charge pion excitation energy scales as E_{π+} ∼ (m_u + m_d) (linear in current quark mass), not as √(m_u + m_d) as in vacuum.
- For the δ⁺ excitation in isospin-asymmetric medium, the excitation energy scales as E_{δ+} ∼ (m_u − m_d) in the isovector limit.
- The low-density splitting of charged pion energies follows E_{π+} − E_{π−} = ρ_{I=1} / F_π² (Weinberg-Tomosawa relation).
- For isospin-asymmetric medium with multiple contributing modes, the chiral scaling exponent α for excitation energies satisfies 2/3 ≤ α ≤ 1.

## Context

This work extends previous current-algebraic sum rules for pionic excitations in nuclear matter (Lutz (1998), Broniowski and Hiller (1997)) to the scalar-isovector (a₀/δ) channel. It generalizes the in-medium Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relations and provides model-independent constraints on meson properties in dense matter. The results enable predictions for very soft modes in neutron-rich matter (relevant for neutron stars and heavy-ion collisions) and demonstrate that collective spin-isospin sound modes, rather than vacuum meson branches, can saturate the sum rules in the chiral limit.
