# Evaluation of phase shifts for non-relativistic elastic scattering using quantum computers

> Simulations of scattering processes are essential in understanding the physics of our universe. Computing relevant scattering quantities from ab initio methods is extremely difficult on classical devices because of the substantial computational resources needed. This work reports the development of an algorithm that makes it possible to obtain phase shifts for generic non-relativistic elastic scattering processes on a quantum computer. This algorithm is based on extracting phase shifts from the direct implementation of the real-time evolution. The algorithm is improved by a variational procedure, making it more accurate and resistant to the quantum noise. The reliability of the algorithm is first demonstrated by means of classical numerical simulations for different potentials, and later tested on existing quantum hardware, specifically on IBM quantum processors.

## Key claim

The paper presents a quantum algorithm (TEPS) that can extract phase shifts for non-relativistic elastic scattering from direct real-time evolution, but the method only recovers the absolute value of the phase shift; a variational extension (V-TEPS) is needed to recover both the magnitude and the correct sign, and the algorithm's accuracy depends on careful choice of lattice parameters and filter width.

## Method

The authors encode the radial Schrodinger equation on a spatial lattice and prepare an initial state approximating a plane wave multiplied by a Fermi filter function to suppress amplitude in the interaction region. They evolve the state under the radial Hamiltonian using Trotter decomposition, then measure the overlap probability between the evolved state and a detector state defined as a plane wave in an asymptotic region; the phase shift is extracted from this probability via a cosine-squared relation. A variational improvement (V-TEPS) introduces a fictitious phase in the detector state and finds the phase that maximizes the overlap probability, which yields both the magnitude and sign of the phase shift.

## Key numbers

* For a Gaussian potential (V0=1 MeV, sigma=2 fm) at k_in=1.99 fm^-1, the TEPS algorithm yields delta_L = 0.46(3) rad, compatible within two standard deviations with the exact value of -0.47 rad.
* For the same Gaussian potential at k_in=1.466 fm^-1, TEPS gives delta_L = 0.65(3) rad versus exact |delta_L| = 0.646 rad.
* For the H-Ar Lennard-Jones potential at k_in=0.602 A^-1, TEPS gives delta_L = 0.76(3) rad versus exact |delta_L| = 0.753 rad.
* For the Gaussian potential (V0=1 MeV, sigma=2 fm) at k_in=2.67 fm^-1, V-TEPS recovers delta_L = -0.33(2) rad versus exact -0.33 rad, including the correct sign.
* For the H-Ar Lennard-Jones potential at k_in=1.58 A^-1, V-TEPS gives delta_L = -0.48(2) rad versus exact -0.49 rad.

## Context

This work extends the line of quantum algorithms for scattering initiated by Jordan-Lee-Preskill (2012) for quantum field theory and more recent proposals by Gustafson (2021) and Sharma (2023) for scattering phase shifts. The method provides an alternative to classical approaches such as Lusher's formula, R-matrix theory, and the Numerov method, and is designed to be implementable on near-term noisy quantum devices like IBM processors. The variational improvement (V-TEPS) specifically addresses sensitivity to noise and poor parameter choices that plague the basic TEPS algorithm, enabling future applications to multi-channel scattering and more complex nuclear systems.
