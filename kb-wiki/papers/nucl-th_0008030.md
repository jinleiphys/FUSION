# Anti-Kaon Induced Reactions on the Nucleon

> Using a previously established effective Lagrangian model we describe anti-kaon induced reactions on the nucleon. The dominantly contributing channels in the cm-energy region from threshold up to 1.72 GeV are included (K N, πΣ, πΛ). We solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in an unitary $K$-matrix approximation.

## Key claim

Using a previously established effective Lagrangian model extended to the strangeness -1 sector, the authors describe anti-kaon induced reactions on the nucleon from threshold up to 1.72 GeV center-of-mass energy, but the extracted resonance parameters are subject to large uncertainties because the available experimental data have error bars larger than 10% and are often contradictory.

## Method

The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation in a unitary K-matrix approximation, reducing the system of coupled integral equations to a matrix inversion problem. They include the dominant channels (K̅N, πΣ, πΛ) and calculate all elementary tree-level diagrams using a Lagrangian with pseudovector coupling for pseudoscalar mesons and vector/tensor coupling for vector mesons, with hadronic form factors applied at vertices. The model parameters (resonance masses, widths, coupling constants) are adjusted by fitting to experimental data including a partial wave analysis by Gopal (1977) and total cross sections from the compilation by Londergan and Beder (1988).

## Key numbers

- The calculation covers the center-of-mass energy region from threshold up to 1.72 GeV.
- For the partial wave analysis by Gopal (1977), the authors assign errors as ΔT = Max(0.12 T; 0.012).
- The K-matrix analysis gives a pole mass of 1710 MeV for the P01 Λ(1600) resonance, while the speed analysis yields 1545 MeV.
- The S01 Λ(1670) resonance shows a speed-technique width of 52 MeV, but the actual decay width calculated from the K-matrix coupling is almost 960 MeV.
- The extracted resonance masses and widths agree with the values given in the Particle Data Group (1998) compilation.

## Context

This work extends the model of Feuster and Mosel (1998) from π and γ induced reactions on the nucleon to the strangeness -1 sector, addressing the poorly known properties of N* and Δ resonances with strangeness -1. The approach is complementary to the work of Bennhold et al. (2000) on photoproduction of kaon-hyperon states. The results enable a consistent treatment of all hadronic reactions on the nucleon below 1.72 GeV, but better experimental data are needed to disentangle resonant and background contributions and to establish the properties of resonances such as those in the P01 partial wave.
