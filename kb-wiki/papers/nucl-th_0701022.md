# Strange form factors of the nucleon

> The strangeness content of nucleon form factors is analyzed in a two-component model with a quark-like intrinsic structure surrounded by a meson cloud. A comparison with the available experimental data from the SAMPLE, PVA4, HAPPEX and G0 collaborations shows a good overall agreement for the strange form factors.

# Strange form factors of the nucleon

## Key claim
The strange electric and magnetic form factors of the nucleon can be described by a two-component model (intrinsic three-quark structure plus meson cloud) with no free parameters beyond those already fixed by the proton and neutron electromagnetic form factors, yielding a positive strange magnetic moment that agrees with the surprising experimental finding that strange quarks reduce the proton's magnetic moment.

## Method
The author constructs a two-component model in which the external photon couples both to an intrinsic three-quark structure (described by a dipole form factor) and to a meson cloud via vector-meson dominance (VMD) with rho, omega and phi mesons. The strange form factors are derived from the isoscalar form factors using the mixing angle between ideally mixed omega and phi states, with all strange couplings determined from the fitted isoscalar couplings. Five coefficients (gamma from the intrinsic form factor, beta_phi and alpha_phi from isoscalar couplings, beta_rho and alpha_rho from isovector couplings) are obtained by least-square fitting to the electric and magnetic form factors of the proton and neutron.

## Key numbers
- Strange magnetic moment: mu_s = 0.315 mu_N (nuclear magnetons), calculated as G_M^s(0) = 1/2 (alpha_omega^s + alpha_phi^s)
- Strange couplings from fit: beta_phi^s = -beta_omega^s = 0.202, alpha_phi^s = 0.648, alpha_omega^s = -0.018
- HAPPEX experimental value for strange electric form factor: G_E^s = -0.038 +/- 0.042 +/- 0.010 at Q^2 = 0.091 (GeV/c)^2
- SAMPLE experimental value for strange magnetic form factor: G_M^s = 0.37 +/- 0.20 +/- 0.26 +/- 0.07 at Q^2 = 0.1 (GeV/c)^2
- World data analysis value: G_M^s = 0.55 +/- 0.28 at Q^2 = 0.1 (GeV/c)^2 (Aniol 2005)
- Intrinsic three-quark structure spatial extent: approximately 0.49 fm
- Omega-phi mixing angle: epsilon = 0.053 rad

## Context
This work extends the two-component model of Bijker and Iachello (2004) by incorporating the treatment of strange quark content of vector mesons following Jaffe (1989), and applies it to the first generation of parity-violating electron scattering data from SAMPLE, PVA4, HAPPEX and G0 collaborations. The model produces a positive strange magnetic moment (0.315 mu_N), in contrast to most QCD-inspired models which favor negative values in the range -0.6 to 0.0 mu_N, but consistent with the surprising experimental finding. Future experiments at backward angles (PVA4 and G0) and neutrino scattering (FINeSSE) are expected to further disentangle quark flavor contributions to nucleon form factors.
