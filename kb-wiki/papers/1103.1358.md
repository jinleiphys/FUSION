# Be-$α$ correlations in the linear-chain structure of C isotopes

> We investigate the linear-chain structures in highly excited states of $^{14}$C using a generalized molecular orbital model, by which we incorporate an asymmetric configuration of three $α$ clusters in the linear-chain states. By applying this model to the $^{14}$C system, we study the $^{10}$Be+$α$ correlation in the linear-chain state of $^{14}$C. To clarify the origin of the $^{10}$Be+$α$ correlation in the $^{14}$C linear-chain state, we analyze linear $3α$ and $3α+n$ systems in a similar way. We find that a linear 3$α$ system prefers the asymmetric 2$α+α$ configuration, whose origin is the many body correlation incorporated by the parity projection. This configuration causes an asymmetric mean field for two valence neutrons, which induces the concentration of valence neutron wave functions around the correlating 2$α$. A linear-chain structure of $^{16}$C is also discussed.

## Key claim

The authors find that a linear 3α system intrinsically prefers an asymmetric 2α+α configuration after parity projection, and this asymmetry creates a mean field that concentrates valence neutrons around the correlating 2α, producing the 10Be+α correlation in 14C linear-chain states, but only when the bending mode is omitted and the linear configuration is assumed.

## Method

The authors used a generalized molecular orbital model in which three α clusters are placed on a straight line with variable inter-cluster distances d1 and d2, and valence neutrons occupy p-orbital wave functions centered on individual α clusters. The total wave function is a superposition of configurations where each valence neutron's p-orbit is centered on the left, middle, or right α cluster, with coefficients determined by diagonalization. Parity and angular-momentum projections are applied, and the method is applied to 12C, 13C, 14C, and 16C systems with the linear-chain constraint.

## Key numbers

- At the minimum of the 14C energy surface, the linear-chain length d1+d2 = 5.2 fm and the asymmetry d2−d1 = 1.5 fm.
- The 0+ energy minimum for 14C has depth 0.73 MeV at d2−d1 = 1.7 fm; the positive-parity minimum has depth 0.56 MeV at d2−d1 = 1.5 fm.
- For 12C, the 0+ minimum depth is 1.02 MeV at d2−d1 = 2.4 fm; the positive-parity minimum depth is 0.67 MeV at d2−d1 = 2.0 fm.
- For 13C, the 3/2− minimum depth is 0.79 MeV at d2−d1 = 2.0 fm; the negative-parity minimum depth is 0.57 MeV at d2−d1 = 1.7 fm.
- At the 14C energy minimum (d2−d1 = 1.7 fm), the ratio of valence neutron wave function coefficients is C3/2,L : C3/2,M : C3/2,R = 1.0 : 4.3 : 0.6.
- The positive-parity component in the intrinsic wave function is 89% for the 3α system without valence neutrons and 82% for the full 14C system at the energy minimum.
- For 16C, the positive-parity energy curve shows no minimum at an asymmetric configuration; the overlap between one Slater determinant and the generalized molecular orbital model at d2−d1 = 0 fm is 95%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous AMD study of 14C (Suhara and Kanada-En'yo, 2010) which found linear-chain states with 10Be+α correlation, and contrasts with the symmetric 3α cluster model of Itagaki et al. (2001) which assumed a symmetric configuration and found no local minimum against bending. The present analysis shows that the asymmetric 2α+α correlation originates from the 3α system itself (8Be+α correlation in 12C) and is enhanced by valence neutrons concentrating around the middle α cluster. The results enable future searches for linear-chain states in 16C, where the symmetric configuration appears favored, and raise the question of whether such a structure exists in that nucleus.
