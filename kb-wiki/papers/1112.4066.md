# Suppression of elliptic-flow-induced correlations in an observable of possible local parity violation

> We show that fluctuations in elliptic anisotropy in heavy-ion collisions can be used to significantly reduce the contribution of transverse-momentum conservation, and of all background effects independent on the orientation of the reaction plane, from an observable of the chiral magnetic effect. We argue that for a given impact parameter, the magnetic field is approximately independent of the fluctuating shape of the fireball.

# Suppression of elliptic-flow-induced correlations in an observable of possible local parity violation

## Key claim
Fluctuations in elliptic anisotropy ($v_2$) in heavy-ion collisions can be used to significantly reduce the contribution of transverse-momentum conservation and all other background effects that are independent of the reaction-plane orientation from the chiral magnetic effect (CME) observable $\gamma$, because for a given impact parameter the magnetic field is approximately independent of the fluctuating shape of the fireball.

## Method
The author derives an analytic expression for the two-particle correlator $\gamma = \langle \cos(\phi_1 + \phi_2 - 2\Psi_{RP}) \rangle$ in terms of the single-particle elliptic flow $v_2(x)$ and the Fourier coefficient $a_1$ of the correlation function $C(\Delta\phi)$. A Monte Carlo Glauber calculation is used to compute the distribution of participant eccentricity $\epsilon_2$ at fixed impact parameter, and the out-of-plane magnetic field $B_y$ is computed as a function of $\epsilon_2$ from wounded and spectator protons. A Monte Carlo model with transverse-momentum conservation and elliptic anisotropy is then used to evaluate $\gamma$ as a function of the selected $v_2$ for different numbers of observed particles.

## Key numbers
- At impact parameter $b = 10$ fm in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s} = 200$ GeV, the participant eccentricity $\epsilon_2$ distribution is broad, with 3D event-by-event hydrodynamics giving $\langle v_2 \rangle = 0.08$ and $[\langle v_2^2 \rangle - \langle v_2 \rangle^2]^{1/2} = 0.04$ for 40-50% centrality.
- In the Monte Carlo model, $N_{all} = 50$ particles are sampled with $p_t$ from a thermal distribution $p_t e^{-p_t/T}$ with $2T = 0.45$ MeV, and $v_2(p_t) = 0.14 p_t$ for $p_t < 2$ GeV and $v_2(p_t) = 0.28$ for $p_t > 2$ GeV, yielding integrated $v_2 \approx 0.06$.
- Transverse-momentum conservation is imposed by accepting events where total $|p_{t,x}| < 0.5$ GeV and $|p_{t,y}| < 0.5$ GeV.
- For $N_{all} = N_{obs} = 50$, $\gamma \rightarrow 0$ as $v_2 \rightarrow 0$; for $N_{obs} = 20$, $\gamma$ becomes slightly positive as $v_2 \rightarrow 0$, scaling as $\gamma \sim \bar{\bar{v}}_{2,F} / N_{all} > 0$.

## Context
This work extends the discussion of background contributions to the CME observable $\gamma$ from transverse-momentum conservation (Pratt 2010, Bzdak 2010, Ma 2011), local charge conservation (Schlichting 2010), and resonance decay (Wang 2009). It builds on the suggestion by Longacre (2011) to remove these correlations by selecting events where $v_2(x) \approx 0$. The paper enables a method to isolate the CME signal in peripheral heavy-ion collisions by exploiting event-by-event fluctuations in elliptic flow, and notes that the multiparticle charge-sensitive correlator $C_c$ proposed by Ajitanand (2010) is insensitive to flow, jets, and momentum conservation, providing a complementary approach.
