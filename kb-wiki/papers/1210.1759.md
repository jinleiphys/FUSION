# The role of momentum dependent dressing functions and vector meson dominance in hadronic light-by-light contributions to the muon g-2

> We present a refined calculation of the quark-loop contribution to hadronic light-by-light scatter- ing that focuses upon the impact of the transverse components of the quark-photon vertex. These structures are compared and contrasted with those found within the extended NJL-models. We discuss similarities and differences between the two approaches and further clarify the important role of momentum dependent dressing functions. As expected we find that the transverse structures of the quark-photon vertex lead to a suppression of the quark-loop contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon. However, we find evidence that this suppression is overestimated within models with simple approximations for the quark-photon interaction.

## Key claim
The transverse components of the quark-photon vertex suppress the quark-loop contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, but this suppression is overestimated in models with simple approximations for the quark-photon interaction, such as the extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (ENJL) model, compared to the more complete Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) approach.

## Method
The authors calculated the quark-loop contribution to hadronic light-by-light scattering using Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) in rainbow-ladder truncation with the Maris-Tandy effective interaction, employing fully dressed quark propagators and quark-photon vertices. They compared these results with those from the ENJL model, which uses momentum-independent constituent quark masses and a simpler vector meson dominance (VMD) vertex structure, to isolate the effects of momentum-dependent dressing functions.

## Key numbers
- The ENJL model quark-loop contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment is quoted as \(a_\mu^{\mathrm{LBL,ql, ENJL}} = 21(3) \times 10^{-11}\).
- The Maris-Tandy model parameters include an energy scale \((\omega D)^{1/3} = 0.72\) GeV, with \(\omega = 0.4\) GeV, and light quark masses of 3.7 MeV at \(\mu = 19\) GeV.
- The DSE quark mass function saturates in the infrared at \(M(0) \approx 450\) MeV, while the ENJL model uses a constituent quark mass of \(\sim 300\) MeV.
- The fit to the leading transverse component of the DSE quark-photon vertex uses parameters \(\omega = 0.66\) GeV, \(\alpha = 0.15\), and \(N_V f_V / M_V = 0.152\), with the \(\rho\)-meson mass \(M_V = 0.77\) GeV.

## Context
This work extends previous DSE calculations of hadronic light-by-light contributions (Fischer 2010, Goecke 2010) that included pseudoscalar meson exchange and non-transverse quark-loop parts, by now incorporating the leading transverse structures of the quark-photon vertex. It contrasts the DSE approach with the ENJL model (Bijnens 1995) and other low-energy effective models such as the Hidden Local Symmetry model (Hayakawa 1995), VMD approaches (Knecht 2001, Melnikov 2003), and the non-local chiral quark model (Dorokhov 2008), arguing that the full momentum dependence of dressing functions is necessary for quantitative precision. The work enables more reliable calculations of the light-by-light contribution needed for the upcoming Fermilab muon g-2 experiment.
