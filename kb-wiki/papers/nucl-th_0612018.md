# A new puzzle for random interaction

> We continue a series of numerical experiments on many-body systems with random two-body interactions, by examining correlations in ratios in excitation energies of yrast $J$ = 0, 2, 4, 6, 8 states. Previous studies, limited only to $J$ = 0,2,4 states, had shown strong correlations in boson systems but not fermion systems. By including $J \ge 6$ states and considering different scatter plots, strong and realistic correlations appear in both boson and fermion systems. Such correlations are a challenge to explanations of random interactions.

## Key claim
By extending the analysis of yrast excitation energy ratios to include \(J \ge 6\) states and using scatter plots of \(R_{62}\) versus \(R_{42}\) and \(R_{82}\) versus \(R_{42}\), strong and realistic correlations appear in both boson and fermion systems with random two-body interactions, whereas previous studies limited to \(J = 0,2,4\) had shown such correlations only in boson systems. The authors present these correlations as an empirical puzzle with no broad or quantitative explanation, challenging existing theories of random interactions.

## Method
The authors performed shell-model calculations for four fermion systems (10 identical particles in a \(pf\) space, 4 protons and 4 neutrons in an \(sd\) space, 10 identical particles in a \(j = 21/2\) space, and 4 protons and 4 neutrons in a \(j = 13/2\) space) using an \(M\)-scheme code, selecting only cases where the ground state has \(J=0\). They computed excitation energy ratios \(R_{42} = E_x(J=4)/E_x(J=2)\), \(R_{62} = E_x(J=6)/E_x(J=2)\), and \(R_{82} = E_x(J=8)/E_x(J=2)\) from the yrast band and generated scatter plots of \(R_{62}\) vs. \(R_{42}\) and \(R_{82}\) vs. \(R_{42}\). For comparison, they also performed similar calculations for the interacting boson model (IBM) with 16 bosons using the program PHINT.

## Key numbers
- For the fermion system \((pf)^{10}\) (colloquially "\(^{50}\)Ca"), the frequency distribution of \(R_{42}\) shows a broad peak near \(R_{42} = 1\), closest to the seniority model value.
- For the fermion system \((sd)_\pi^4 (sd)_\nu^4\) (colloquially "\(^{24}\)Mg"), the frequency distribution of \(R_{42}\) also shows a broad peak near \(R_{42} = 1\).
- For the fermion system \((21/2)^{10}\) (10 identical particles in a \(j = 21/2\) space), the frequency distribution of \(R_{42}\) shows a broad peak near \(R_{42} = 1\).
- For the fermion system \((13/2)_\pi^4 (13/2)_\nu^4\) (4 protons and 4 neutrons in a \(j = 13/2\) space), the frequency distribution of \(R_{42}\) shows a broad peak near \(R_{42} = 1\).
- In the scatter plots of \(R_{62}\) vs. \(R_{42}\) and \(R_{82}\) vs. \(R_{42}\) for all four fermion systems, strong correlations appear, with clusters near the vibrational (\(R_{42} = 2\), \(R_{62} = 3\), \(R_{82} = 4\)) and rotational (\(R_{42} = 3.33\), \(R_{62} = 7\), \(R_{82} = 12\)) limits.
- For the IBM with 16 bosons, similar strong correlations appear in scatter plots of \(R_{62}\) vs. \(R_{42}\) and \(R_{82}\) vs. \(R_{42}\), with clusters near the vibrational and rotational limits, and similar correlations persist even for \(J = 10\).

## Context
This work extends the earlier study by Bijker and Frank (2000), who found strong correlations in the IBM with random interactions using only \(R_{42}\) and E2 transition ratios, but found no such correlations in fermion systems. The present results contradict the expectation that fermion systems with random interactions lack band-like correlations, and challenge existing explanations such as mean-field models (e.g., Bijker and Frank, 2001) and geometric chaoticity (Zhao and collaborators, 2004). The findings enable future theoretical work to develop a comprehensive theory of random interactions that can account for these new empirical correlations, and suggest that higher-spin yrast states are a sensitive probe of collectivity in random-interaction ensembles.
