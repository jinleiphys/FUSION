# Quasi-classical descendants of disordered vertex models with boundaries

> We study descendants of inhomogeneous vertex models with boundary reflections when the spin-spin scattering is assumed to be quasi--classical. This corresponds to consider certain power expansion of the boundary-Yang-Baxter equation (or reflection equation). As final product, integrable $su(2)$-spin chains interacting with a long range with $XXZ$ anisotropy are obtained. The spin-spin couplings are non uniform, and a non uniform tunable external magnetic field is applied; the latter can be obtained when the boundary conditions are assumed to be quasi-classical as well. The exact spectrum is achieved by algebraic Bethe ansatz. Having realized the $su(2)$ operators in terms of fermions, the class of models we found turns out to describe confined fermions with pairing force interactions. The class of models presented in this paper is a one-parameter extension of certain Hamiltonians constructed previously. Extensions to $su(n)$-spin open chains are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a one-parameter extension of previously known integrable spin-chain Hamiltonians by assuming that not only the bulk spin-spin scattering but also the boundary conditions themselves admit a quasi-classical expansion in the quantum parameter η, thereby introducing a tunable non-uniform external magnetic field that was absent in earlier treatments with purely classical boundaries.

## Method

The authors start from the six-vertex model with diagonal boundary K-matrices and perform a power-series expansion of the transfer matrix in the quantum parameter η (the quasi-classical limit). By allowing the boundary parameters ξ± to depend on η (a "non-classical boundary"), they ensure that the first non-trivial commuting operator in the expansion is at order η², which yields a long-range interacting su(2) spin chain with XXZ anisotropy. The exact spectrum and eigenstates are obtained via the algebraic Bethe ansatz, and the resulting Hamiltonians are expressed in terms of spin operators with couplings that depend on the disorder parameters zj and the boundary parameter ξ = it.

## Key numbers

* The Hamiltonian has the form H = Σⱼ 2hⱼ Sⱼᶻ – Σⱼₖ (Jⱼₖ⁽ᶻ⁾ Sⱼᶻ Sₖᶻ + (Jⱼₖ Sⱼ⁺ Sₖ⁻ + h.c.)), with couplings given by explicit expressions involving the parameters p, zⱼ, t, and a function J(Sᶻ) = J (1 – J Sᶻ)⁻¹.
* The Bethe equations in the rational parameterization are Σ_{β≠α} 1/(λ_α – λ_β) – Σⱼ Sⱼ/(λ_α – xⱼ) + (1/(2J(Sᶻ))) [(1+J(Sᶻ)(1+Sᶻ))/(1+λ_α) + (1–J(Sᶻ)(1+Sᶻ))/(1–λ_α)] = 0.
* The eigenvalues τⱼ are given by τⱼ = Sⱼ (1 – J(Sᶻ) Σ_{k≠j} Sₖ (1 – xⱼ xₖ)/(xⱼ – xₖ) + J(Sᶻ) Σ_α (1 – xⱼ λ_α)/(xⱼ – λ_α)).
* The parameter J is defined as J = –1/(2ς), where ς = (ξ₊⁽¹⁾ + ξ₋⁽¹⁾)/2 comes from the η-expansion of the boundary parameters.

## Context

This work extends the quasi-classical treatment of disordered vertex models with boundaries initiated by Hikami (1995), which produced integrable spin chains but with no external magnetic field. By allowing the boundary parameters to depend on η, the authors introduce a tunable non-uniform magnetic field, generalizing the class of Hamiltonians previously constructed by Amico et al. (2001). The resulting models describe confined fermions with pairing and exchange forces, and the authors note that the rational limit (p → 0) recovers XXX-type couplings, while the cases t = 0 and pt = π/2 reduce to the earlier models of Amico et al. (2001). Extensions to su(n) open chains are discussed in an appendix.
