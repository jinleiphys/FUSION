# Deuteron Disintegration by Protons in Kinematics of Quasi-Elastic Backward pd-Scattering

> The deuteron break-up exclusive reaction $p+d \to N(180^{\circ}) + (NN)(0^{\circ})$ is considered in the framework of the one-nucleon-exchange mechanism at the incident proton kinetic energy 0.2--2 GeV and the relative energy of nucleons in the forward going final NN-pair $E_{NN}=0$--20 MeV, that is close to the kinematical conditions of the planned experiment at COSY. The off-energy-shell effects resulting, in particular, in the node of the half-off-shell $^1S_0$-state partial NN-scattering amplitude and the role of higher partial waves in the subprocess $pN\to pN$ are analysed and found to be very important, especially for the reaction $p+d \to n(180^{\circ}) + (pp)(0^{\circ})$. The accuracy of some approximate relations between the cross sections of elastic $pd$-scattering and deuteron break-up is examined in the framework of the mechanism in question.

## Key claim

The one-nucleon-exchange (ONE) mechanism predicts a distinct minimum in the cross section for the reaction \(p+d \to n(180^\circ)+(pp)(0^\circ)\) at an incident proton kinetic energy of 0.77 GeV, but this minimum is absent for the analogous reaction producing a forward-going \(pn\)-pair, because the "nodeless" triplet amplitude \(t^{11}_{20}(q',k)\) fills in the minimum.

## Method

The authors calculated the differential cross section of the exclusive deuteron break-up reaction \(p+d \to N(180^\circ)+(NN)(0^\circ)\) in the center-of-mass frame using the ONE mechanism within relativistic quantum mechanics of three-body systems. They solved the Lippman–Schwinger equation for the half-off-shell \(NN\)-scattering amplitude using the Paris potential, including partial waves up to \(G\)-waves (\(L'=0,1,2,3,4\)) in the final nucleon pair, and used the deuteron wave function from the same potential. The calculations were performed for incident proton kinetic energies \(T_p = 0.2\)–2 GeV and relative energies of the forward-going \(NN\)-pair \(E_{NN} = 0\)–20 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The minimum in the cross section for \(p+d \to n(180^\circ)+(pp)(0^\circ)\) occurs at \(T_p = 0.77\) GeV for relative energies \(E_{pp} \sim 0\)–5 MeV.
- The node of the half-off-shell \(^1S_0\)-state partial amplitude \(t^{00}_{00}(q',k)\) occurs at \(q' \approx 0.4\) GeV/c.
- The ratio of the \(pp\)-pair to \(pn\)-pair formation cross sections at \(E_{pp}=E_{pn}=0\) does not exceed 2.77 over the range \(T_p = 0.2\)–2 GeV.
- As \(E_{NN}\) increases from 0 to 20 MeV, both \(pp\)- and \(pn\)-pair formation cross sections reduce by almost two orders of magnitude.
- The triplet scattering length is \(a_t = 5.41\) fm and the triplet effective radius is \(r_t = 1.75\) fm.
- The parameter \(\alpha_t = \sqrt{m|\varepsilon|} = 0.232\) fm\(^{-1}\).
- The node of \(t^{00}_{00}(q',k)\) shifts approximately by 60 MeV toward smaller \(T_p\) when \(E_{NN}\) varies from 0 to 20 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier approach of Uzikov (1990) by including higher partial waves (\(P\)-, \(D\)-, \(F\)-, \(G\)-waves) in the \(NN\)-scattering amplitude within the ONE mechanism, and by examining a wider range of final \(NN\)-pair relative energies (0–20 MeV) relevant to the planned experiment at COSY. The analysis of off-energy-shell effects and the node in the half-off-shell \(^1S_0\)-state amplitude builds on the earlier finding that the ONE mechanism dominates over \(\Delta\)-isobar contributions for singlet \(NN\)-pairs. The paper also tests the approximate relations of Anisovich, Dakhno and Makarov (ADM) and Boudard, Fäldt and Wilkin (BFW) connecting the cross sections of elastic backward \(pd\)-scattering and triplet \(pn\)-pair formation, which could allow estimation of the latter from elastic scattering data.
