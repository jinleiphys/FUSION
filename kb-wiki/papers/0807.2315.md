# Deformed quantum statistics in two-dimensions

> It is known from the early work of May in 1964 that ideal Bose gas do not exhibit condensation phenomenon in two dimensions. On the other hand, it is also known that the thermostatistics arising from q-deformed oscillator algebra has no connection with the spatial dimensions of the system. Our recent work concerns the study of important thermodynamic functions such as the entropy, occupation number, internal energy and specific heat in ordinary three spatial dimensions, where we established that such thermostatistics is developed by consistently replacing the ordinary thermodynamic derivatives by the Jackson derivatives. The thermostatistics of q-deformed bosons and fermions in two spatial dimensions is an unresolved question and that is the subject of this investigation. We study the principal thermodynamic functions of both bosons and fermions in the two dimensional q-deformed formalism and we find that, different from the standard case, the specific heat of q-boson and q-fermion ideal gas, at fixed temperature and number of particle, are no longer identical.

## Key claim

In two spatial dimensions, the specific heat of a $q$-deformed boson ideal gas and a $q$-deformed fermion ideal gas, at fixed temperature and particle number, are no longer identical for $q \neq 1$, contrary to the standard (undeformed) case where they are equal; this difference becomes more pronounced as the deformation parameter $q$ deviates further from unity.

## Method

The authors construct the thermostatistics of $q$-deformed bosons and fermions in two dimensions by consistently replacing ordinary thermodynamic derivatives with Jackson derivatives and ordinary integrals with $q$-integrals, as required by the $q$-deformed oscillator algebra. They numerically evaluate the $q$-deformed functions $h_n^\kappa(z,q)$ for the particle number and internal energy, and compute the specific heat from the thermodynamic definition using the Jackson derivative prescription. The fugacity is obtained numerically by inverting the $q$-deformed number equation, and the specific heat is compared for bosons and fermions at the same temperature and particle number.

## Key numbers

- For $q=0.7$, the specific heat $C_v \lambda^2 / V_2$ for bosons and fermions shows a clear separation as a function of the variable $y = N \lambda^2 / V_2$ (Figs. 3 and 4).
- For $q=0.9$, the specific heat difference between bosons and fermions is smaller but still present (Figs. 3 and 4).
- The dimensionless coefficient $\alpha_q$ in the relation $\Delta h_2(y_q,q) = \alpha_q y_q^2$ approaches $1/2$ in the limit $q \rightarrow 1$ (Fig. 1).
- The $\chi^2$ of the quadratic fit for $\Delta h_2(y_q,q)$ increases significantly for $q$ far from 1, indicating the temperature independence of the internal energy difference breaks down (Fig. 2).
- The fugacity range for $q$-bosons is limited by $z_B < q^{-2}$ for $q>1$ and $z_B < q^2$ for $q<1$ (Eq. zq).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of $q$-deformed thermostatistics in three dimensions (Lavagno and Swamy, 2000, 2002) to the two-dimensional case, which is known from May (1964) to have no Bose-Einstein condensation for standard bosons and identical specific heats for bosons and fermions. The paper shows that $q$-deformation breaks this equality, a result with no counterpart in standard quantum statistics, and suggests this may be relevant to complex systems with many-body interactions or long-time memory effects, as previously discussed in nuclear physics (Bona et al.) and Fermi liquid theory (Chubukov). The connection between $q$-deformation and two-dimensional anyon statistics remains an open question.
