# The parity-violating asymmetry in the 3He(n,p)3H reaction

> The longitudinal asymmetry induced by parity-violating (PV) components in the nucleon-nucleon potential is studied in the charge-exchange reaction 3He(n,p)3H at vanishing incident neutron energies. An expression for the PV observable is derived in terms of T-matrix elements for transitions from the {2S+1}L_J=1S_0 and 3S_1 states in the incoming n-3He channel to states with J=0 and 1 in the outgoing p-3H channel. The T-matrix elements involving PV transitions are obtained in first-order perturbation theory in the hadronic weak-interaction potential, while those connecting states of the same parity are derived from solutions of the strong-interaction Hamiltonian with the hyperspherical-harmonics method. The coupled-channel nature of the scattering problem is fully accounted for. Results are obtained corresponding to realistic or chiral two- and three-nucleon strong-interaction potentials in combination with either the DDH or pionless EFT model for the weak-interaction potential. The asymmetries, predicted with PV pion and vector-meson coupling constants corresponding (essentially) to the DDH "best values" set, range from -9.44 to -2.48 in units of 10^{-8}, depending on the input strong-interaction Hamiltonian. This large model dependence is a consequence of cancellations between long-range (pion) and short-range (vector-meson) contributions, and is of course sensitive to the assumed values for the PV coupling constants.

## Key claim

The longitudinal asymmetry \(A_z\) in the \(^3\)He(\(\vec{n},p\))\(^3\)H reaction at vanishing neutron energies is predicted to range from \(-9.44\) to \(-2.48\) in units of \(10^{-8}\), depending on the strong-interaction Hamiltonian used, but this large model dependence arises from cancellations between long-range (pion) and short-range (vector-meson) contributions and is sensitive to the assumed values for the parity-violating coupling constants.

## Method

The authors derived an expression for the parity-violating longitudinal asymmetry \(A_z\) in terms of \(T\)-matrix elements for transitions from the \(^1\)S\(_0\) and \(^3\)S\(_1\) states in the incoming \(n\)-\(^3\)He channel to states with \(J=0\) and 1 in the outgoing \(p\)-\(^3\)H channel. Parity-conserving \(T\)-matrix elements were obtained from solutions of the strong-interaction Hamiltonian using the hyperspherical-harmonics method, while parity-violating \(T\)-matrix elements were computed in first-order perturbation theory using Quantum Monte Carlo techniques. The calculations employed realistic or chiral two- and three-nucleon strong-interaction potentials (AV18, AV18/UIX, N3LO, N3LO/N2LO) combined with either the DDH meson-exchange model or the pionless effective-field-theory model for the weak-interaction potential.

## Key numbers

* Predicted asymmetries \(a_z\) range from \(-9.44\) to \(-2.48\) in units of \(10^{-8}\), depending on the strong-interaction Hamiltonian (N3LO/N2LO vs AV18/UIX) with DDH "best values" for PV coupling constants.
* The coefficient \(C_\pi^1\) for the one-pion-exchange contribution ranges from \(-0.1110(75)\) (N3LO/N2LO) to \(-0.1989(87)\) (N3LO) in units of \(10^{-8}\).
* The coefficient \(C_\rho^0\) ranges from \(+0.0379(56)\) (N3LO/N2LO) to \(-0.0380(70)\) (AV18/UIX).
* The coefficient \(C_\omega^0\) ranges from \(+0.0457(36)\) (N3LO/N2LO) to \(-0.0334(29)\) (AV18).
* The DDH "best values" for the PV coupling constants are \(+4.56 \times 10^{-7}\) for the pion and \(-16.4 \times 10^{-7}\) for the \(\rho\)-meson.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic studies of parity-violating observables in few-nucleon systems, including the longitudinal asymmetry in \(\vec{p}\)-\(p\) scattering (Carlson 2002), PV observables in the \(np\) system (Schiavilla 2004), and neutron spin rotation in \(\vec{n}\)-\(d\) (Schiavilla 2008) and \(\vec{n}\)-\(\alpha\) (Arriaga 2010) scattering. The calculations are directly relevant to the planned measurement of the longitudinal asymmetry in the \(^3\)He(\(\vec{n},p\))\(^3\)H reaction at cold neutron energies (Bowman 2007). The results enable future constraints on the low-energy constants of the parity-violating potential from a suite of experiments involving \(A=2\)–\(5\) systems, including photon asymmetries in radiative captures such as \(^2\)H(\(\vec{n},\gamma\))\(^3\)H and \(^3\)He(\(\vec{n},\gamma\))\(^4\)He.
