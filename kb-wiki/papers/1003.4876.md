# Phase ambiguity of the threshold amplitude in pp -> ppπ^0

> Measurements of spin observables in pp -> {\vec p}{\vec p}π^0 are suggested to remove the phase ambiguity of the threshold amplitude. The suggested measurements complement the IUCF data on {\vec p}{\vec p} -> ppπ^0 to completely determine all the twelve partial wave amplitudes, taken into consideration by Mayer et.al. [15] and Deepak, Haidenbauer and Hanhart [20].

## Key claim
Measurements of final-state proton spin polarizations and spin correlations in \(pp \rightarrow \vec{p}\vec{p}\pi^0\) with an unpolarized beam and target can resolve the relative phase ambiguity of the threshold \(Ss\) amplitude \(f_1\) with respect to the \(Ps\) and \(Pp\) amplitudes, which previous analyses (Mayer et al., Deepak, Haidenbauer and Hanhart) left undetermined by assuming \(f_1\) real.

## Method
The authors extend a model-independent irreducible-tensor formalism for the reaction \(pp \rightarrow pp\pi^0\) to include final-state spin observables. They derive expressions for the double-differential cross section, the individual polarizations \(\mathbf{P}_1\) and \(\mathbf{P}_2\) of the two outgoing protons, and their spin correlations \(C_{\alpha\beta}\) in terms of the twelve partial-wave amplitudes \(f_1\) through \(f_{12}\). By isolating terms that depend linearly on the threshold amplitude \(f_1\) and the known \(Pp\) amplitudes, they show that measuring the combination \(P^1_\mu(1,0) - \sqrt{3}P^1_\mu(0,1)\) (from \(\mathbf{P}_1 - \mathbf{P}_2\)) and \(P^1_\mu(1,0) + \sqrt{3}P^1_\mu(0,1)\) (from spin correlations such as \(C_{xy} - C_{yx}\)) yields the relative phase \(\delta_1\) of \(f_1\) without trigonometric ambiguity.

## Key numbers
- The threshold \(Ss\) amplitude \(f_1\) contributes to the irreducible tensor amplitude \(M^1_\mu(0,1) = \frac{1}{4\sqrt{3}\pi} f_1 Y_{1\mu}(\hat{\mathbf{p}}_i)\) (Eq. m1mu).
- The twelve partial-wave amplitudes considered are \(f_1\) through \(f_{12}\), listed in Table I, covering \(Ss\), \(Ps\), and \(Pp\) channels; the \(Sd\) and \(Ds\) amplitudes (four total) are neglected following Mayer et al. and Deepak et al.
- The linear combinations \(F_1, F_2, F_3\) of the \(Pp\) amplitudes are given explicitly (e.g., \(F_1 = \frac{1}{32\pi^{3/2}}[f_4 - \frac{5}{6}f_5 + \frac{5}{2\sqrt{3}}f_6 + \frac{1}{3\sqrt{3}}f_9 - \frac{1}{6}f_{10} - \frac{\sqrt{5}}{6\sqrt{3}}f_{11}]\)).
- The relative phase \(\delta_1\) of \(f_1\) is extracted from the observables \(P^1_\mu(1,0) \pm \sqrt{3}P^1_\mu(0,1)\), which involve products \(R_\alpha = |F_\alpha||f_1|\) and the known phases \(\Delta_\alpha\) from Deepak et al. (2010).

## Context
This work extends the model-independent analysis of \(pp \rightarrow pp\pi^0\) previously applied to IUCF data by Mayer et al. and Deepak, Haidenbauer and Hanhart (2010), who determined the magnitudes and relative phases of the \(Ps\) and \(Pp\) amplitudes but left the phase of the threshold \(Ss\) amplitude ambiguous by setting it to zero. The proposed measurements of final-state proton polarizations and spin correlations with an unpolarized beam and target would complement the existing spin observables from polarized-beam experiments, enabling a complete empirical determination of all twelve partial-wave amplitudes without phase ambiguity. This approach is motivated by the failure of the Julich meson-exchange model to reproduce the full set of polarization observables for this reaction, as noted by Mayer et al.
