# Long-range and short-range multiparticle correlation in collisions of $_{79}Au^{197}$ 10.7 A GeV with photoemulsion nuclei

> A search and research of long-range and short-range multiparticle correlations in pseudorapidity distribution of secondary particles on the base of analysis of individual interaction of nuclei of $^{197}Au$ at energy 10.7 AGeV with photoemulsion nuclei, is carried out. Events with long-range multiparticle correlations (LC), short-range multiparticle correlations (SC) and mixed type (MT) in pseudorapidity distribution of secondary particles, are selected by Hurst method in accordance of Hurst curve behavior. These types have significantly different characteristics. At first, they have different parameters of fragmentation. Events of LC type are processes of full destruction of projectile nucleus, in which multicharge fragments are absent. In events of mixed type several multicharge fragments of projectile nucleus are discovered. Secondly, these two types have significantly different multiplicity distribution. Mean multiplicity of LC type events is significantly more than in events of mixed type. On the basis of research of dependence of multiplicity versus target-nuclei fragments number for events of various types it is revealed, that the most considerable multiparticle correlations are observed in interactions of mixed type, which correspond to the central collisions of gold nuclei and nuclei of CNO-group, i.e. nuclei with strongly asymmetric volume, nuclear mass, charge, etc. Such events are characterised by full destruction of target-nucleus and disintegration of projectile-nucleus on several multi-charged fragments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Hurst method can classify individual Au+Em collisions at 10.7 A GeV into events with long-range, short-range, or mixed-type multiparticle correlations, and that the most pronounced correlations occur in central collisions between gold nuclei and light CNO-group nuclei, not in collisions with heavy emulsion nuclei.

## Method

The authors analyzed pseudorapidity distributions of secondary particles from individual 10.7 A GeV 197Au interactions with nuclear photoemulsion (BR-2 type, containing H, CNO-group, and AgBr nuclei). For each event, they computed the Hurst index h from the normalized range of pseudorapidity fluctuations over a central interval Δη = 4 subdivided into 1024 bins, then classified events by comparing the Hurst curve behavior to the threshold h = 0.64 (corresponding to two-particle decay processes). Events were further separated by target-nucleus fragment number Nh and projectile fragment multiplicity to distinguish interactions with light versus heavy emulsion nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The Hurst index threshold for correlated events was set at h = 0.64, based on the criterion that this value corresponds to processes where all secondary particles are produced from two-particle decays.
- For events of explosive type (long-range correlations, full destruction of projectile nucleus, no multi-charge fragments), the average multiplicity was ⟨n^e⟩ ∼ 272.
- For events of mixed type (several multi-charge fragments of projectile nucleus), the average multiplicity was ⟨n^e⟩ ∼ 97.
- The central pseudorapidity interval used for analysis was Δη = 4, subdivided into k = 1024 bins with δη = Δη/k.
- The nuclear emulsion composition was: hydrogen 39.2%, CNO-group nuclei 35.3%, and AgBr nuclei 25.5%.
- For interactions with light photoemulsion nuclei, the maximum number of target-nucleus fragments cannot exceed 8, corresponding to the largest charge of a light photoemulsion nucleus (oxygen).

## Context

This work extends the Hurst method previously applied by the same group (Gaitinov et al., prior work) to discriminate dynamic multiparticle correlations from statistical ones in nuclear collisions. The study uses data from the EMU01 collaboration for 197Au+Em at 10.7 A GeV. The results enable classification of individual events by correlation type and suggest that the strongest multiparticle correlations arise in asymmetric collisions (Au on CNO-group nuclei), which may be relevant for studying phase transitions from hadronic matter to quark-gluon plasma, as discussed in theoretical work (references 1-3, 10 in the paper).
