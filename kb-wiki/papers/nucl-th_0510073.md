# Separability of a Low-Momentum Effective Nucleon-Nucleon Potential

> A realistic nucleon-nucleon potential is transformed into a low-momentum effective one (LMNN) using the Okubo theory. The separable potentials are converted from the LMNN with a universal separable expansion method and a simple Legendre expansion. Through the calculation of the triton binding energies, the separability for the convergence of these ranks is evaluated. It is found that there is a tendency for the lower momentum cutoff parameter $Λ$ of LMNN to gain good separability.

## Key claim

The low-momentum effective nucleon-nucleon (LMNN) potential, derived via the Okubo unitary transformation, exhibits better separability (i.e., converges to the exact result with a lower-rank separable expansion) when the momentum cutoff parameter Λ is decreased, though the exact binding energy itself depends on the chosen cutoff.

## Method

The authors start from the CD-Bonn realistic nucleon-nucleon potential and transform it into a low-momentum effective potential using the Okubo theory with a cutoff Λ. They then convert the LMNN potential into separable form using two methods: a simple Legendre polynomial expansion (SSE) and a universal separable expansion (USE). The triton binding energy is computed from the resulting finite-rank separable potentials by solving the 5-channel coupled Faddeev equation, and the convergence of the binding energy with increasing rank n is evaluated for cutoff values Λ = 3, 5, 10, and 20 fm⁻¹.

## Key numbers

- The true triton binding energy (Λ = ∞) for the CD-Bonn potential is −8.312 MeV.
- For Λ = 3 fm⁻¹, the exact LMNN binding energy (without separable approximation) is −8.532 MeV; the USE with rank n = 6 already gives −8.014 MeV, and with n = 8 gives −8.509 MeV, while the SSE with n = 8 gives −8.326 MeV.
- For Λ = 5 fm⁻¹, the exact LMNN binding energy is −8.355 MeV; the USE with rank n = 10 gives −8.311 MeV, and with n = 12 gives −8.351 MeV, while the SSE with n = 12 gives −8.328 MeV.
- For Λ = 10 fm⁻¹, the exact LMNN binding energy is −8.329 MeV; the USE with rank n = 14 gives −8.305 MeV, and with n = 18 gives −8.327 MeV, while the SSE with n = 20 gives −8.319 MeV.
- For Λ = 20 fm⁻¹, the exact LMNN binding energy is −8.322 MeV; the USE with rank n = 20 gives −8.320 MeV, and the SSE with n = 20 gives −8.317 MeV.
- The difference of 43 keV between the Λ = 5 fm⁻¹ exact value (−8.355 MeV) and the true value (−8.312 MeV) represents a 0.1% error in the potential expectation value.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Fujii (2005) which established that the LMNN cutoff Λ must be at least 5 fm⁻¹ to reproduce exact few-body binding energies, and it applies the separable expansion methods previously developed by Koike (1996) for realistic potentials. The finding that lower Λ yields better separability suggests that the LMNN interaction, despite its non-analytic functional form, can be efficiently represented as a low-rank separable potential for use in few-body scattering calculations. This enables future applications of the complex energy method (Kamada, 2005) to three- and four-body scattering problems without requiring analytic form factors.
