# Heavy quark potential and QCD beta function from a deformed $AdS_5$ model

> We show that in a deformed ${\rm AdS}_5$ model with an explicit infrared cutoff included in the logarithmic correction $-c_0\log[(z_{IR}-z)/z_{IR}]$, the heavy quark Cornell potential can be fitted very well, the corresponding beta-function agrees with the QCD beta-function at 2-loop level reasonably well, and its dual dilaton potential is bounded from below in infrared. The results are compared with those in the Andreev-Zakharov model and the Pirner-Galow model.

## Key claim

In a deformed AdS₅ model with an explicit infrared cutoff in the logarithmic correction term \(-c_0\log[(z_{IR}-z)/z_{IR}]\), the heavy quark Cornell potential can be fitted well, the corresponding beta function agrees with the QCD beta function at 2-loop level reasonably well, and the dual dilaton potential is bounded from below in the infrared, but only for the case with purely logarithmic correction, not for the case with purely quadratic correction.

## Method

The authors propose a deformed warp factor \(h(z)=\exp\left(-\frac{\sigma z^2}{2}-c_0\ln\left(\frac{z_{IR}-z}{z_{IR}}\right)\right)\) and compute the heavy quark potential from the Nambu-Goto string action using the standard holographic procedure. They then solve the Einstein equations from the Gürsoy-Kiritsis-Nitti (GKN) framework to obtain the dilaton field and dilaton potential, and compute the QCD beta function from the relation \(\beta = e^{\phi(z)}\phi'(z)/A'(z)\). Two cases are considered separately: purely quadratic correction (\(c_0=0\)) and purely logarithmic correction (\(\sigma=0\)), each with two types of boundary conditions for the dilaton field.

## Key numbers

- Best fit for the purely quadratic correction case (\(c_0=0\)) gives \(\sigma = -0.22\ \text{GeV}^2\), corresponding to the Andreev-Zakharov model.
- Best fit for the purely logarithmic correction case (\(\sigma=0\)) gives \(c_0 = 0.272\ \text{GeV}^2\) and \(z_{IR} = 2.1\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\).
- The Cornell potential parameters used for comparison are \(\kappa \approx 0.48\), \(\sigma_{str} \approx 0.183\ \text{GeV}^2\), and \(V_0 = -0.25\ \text{GeV}\).
- The Pirner-Galow model parameters are \(\Lambda = 264\ \text{MeV}\) and \(\epsilon = \Lambda^2 l_s^2 = 0.48\).
- The QCD 2-loop beta function coefficients for \(N_c=3\) and \(N_f=4\) are \(b_0 = 25/(6\pi)\) and \(b_1 = 77/(12\pi^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the "bottom-up" holographic QCD approach by proposing a deformed metric that combines features of the hard-wall model (explicit IR cutoff) with logarithmic corrections, improving upon the Andreev-Zakharov model (positive quadratic term) and the Pirner-Galow model (Pirner and Galow, 2009). The key advance is that the purely logarithmic correction produces a stable dilaton potential bounded from below in the IR, unlike the Andreev-Zakharov model and the Pirner-Galow model where the dilaton potential is unstable (Galow, 2009). This enables further exploration of holographic models that simultaneously describe the heavy quark potential, QCD running coupling, and have a stable gravity dual.
