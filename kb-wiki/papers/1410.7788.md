# On Recent Weak Single Pion Production Data

> MiniBooNE [1] and MINERvA [2] charge current π + production data in the Delta region are discussed. It is argued that despite the differences in neutrino flux they measure the same dynamical mechanism of pion production and should be strongly correlated. The correlation is clearly seen in the Monte Carlo simulations done with NuWro generator but is missing in the data. Both normalization and the shape of the ratio of measured differential cross sections in pion kinetic energy are different from the Monte Carlo results, in the case of normalization a discrepancy is by a factor of 1.49.

## Key claim

The paper argues that MiniBooNE and MINERvA charge current π⁺ production data in the Δ region should be strongly correlated because they measure the same dynamical mechanism, but the published data show a normalization discrepancy by a factor of 1.49 and shape differences that are absent in Monte Carlo simulations with the NuWro generator.

## Method

The authors re-binned MiniBooNE differential cross section data in pion kinetic energy into MINERvA bin sizes using linear interpolation and a weighted average method with assumed full covariance between neighboring bins. They then performed Monte Carlo simulations with the NuWro event generator, including resonant (Δ excitation and decay), coherent pion production, deep inelastic scattering, quasi-elastic, and meson exchange current contributions with final state interactions, and compared the ratio of MINERvA to MiniBooNE differential cross sections to the experimental ratio. Systematic errors in the Monte Carlo were estimated by varying the axial coupling C₅ᴬ(0) and axial mass M_AΔ of the Δ resonance, and by testing different pion angular correlation parameterizations from ANL and BNL experiments.

## Key numbers

- The normalization discrepancy between MiniBooNE and MINERvA data relative to NuWro predictions is a factor of 1.49 ± 0.15.
- MiniBooNE neutrino flux peaks at approximately 700 MeV, while MINERvA flux peaks at approximately 3 GeV, a factor of 4-5 difference in typical neutrino energies.
- In NuWro simulations, resonant interactions account for 87.1% of the MiniBooNE signal and 84.7% of the MINERvA signal.
- Coherent pion production contributes 6.7% (MiniBooNE) and 10.7% (MINERvA) of the signal in NuWro.
- The axial coupling C₅ᴬ(0) was varied as 1.19 ± 0.08 and M_AΔ as 0.94 ± 0.03 GeV for systematic error estimation.
- According to NuWro, 23% of MiniBooNE signal events correspond to hadronic invariant mass W > 1.4 GeV.

## Context

This work extends comparisons of single-pion-production data from MiniBooNE (AguilarArevalo 2010) and MINERvA (Eberly 2014) experiments, which are important for reducing systematic errors in neutrino oscillation experiments such as T2K, NOvA, LBNE, and MicroBooNE. The paper highlights that previous generators like GENIE (Andreopoulos 2009) and GiBUU (Lalakulich 2012) also had difficulty reproducing π⁺ production data, with GiBUU paradoxically reproducing MiniBooNE data better when final state interactions were neglected. The results suggest that the discrepancy is unlikely to be resolved solely by improving the NuWro model, because the ratio of cross sections should cancel common model deficiencies, and they call for more dedicated experimental measurements of pion production with controlled nuclear effects.
