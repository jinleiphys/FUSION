# Hyperon puzzle and the RMF model with scaled hadron masses and coupling constants

> The equation of state of cold baryonic matter is studied within a relativistic mean-field model with hadron masses and coupling constants depending on a scalar field. We demonstrate that if the effective nucleon mass stops to decrease with a density increase at densities $n>n_*>n_0$, where $n_0$ is the nuclear saturation density, the equation of state stiffens for these densities and the limiting neutron star mass increases. The stabilization of the nucleon mass can be realised if in the equation of motion for the scalar mean-field there appear a term sharply varying in a narrow vicinity of the field value corresponding to the density $n_*$. We show several possible realizations of this mechanism getting sufficiently stiff equations of state. The appearance of hyperons in dense neutron star interiors is accounted for. The obtained equations of state remain sufficiently stiff if the reduction of the $ϕ$ meson mass is incorporated. Thereby, the hyperon puzzle can be resolved.

## Key claim

The hyperon puzzle (the softening of the neutron star equation of state when hyperons are included) can be resolved if the effective nucleon mass stops decreasing with density above a threshold density \(n_* > n_0\), and if the reduction of the \(\phi\) meson mass is incorporated; the mechanism works only for specific choices of scaling functions and coupling constants that keep the equation of state sufficiently stiff while still satisfying heavy-ion collision flow constraints.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model where hadron masses and meson-baryon coupling constants depend on a scalar field \(f\). They introduce a "\(\sigma\)-cut" scheme that adds a sharply rising term to the scalar-field potential, quenching the growth of \(f\) and thus stabilizing the nucleon mass above a density \(n_*\). They also implement "\(\omega\)-cut" and "\(\rho\)-cut" variants (KVORcut and MKVOR models) by modifying the scaling functions \(\eta_\omega(f)\) and \(\eta_\rho(f)\). Hyperons from the baryon SU(3) octet are included, with coupling constants set by SU(6) symmetry and empirical binding energies; the \(\phi\)-meson mass is allowed to scale with \(f\), and the hyperon-\(\sigma\) coupling constants are reduced at high density in some variants. Neutron star masses and radii are computed via the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation, matched to the BPS crust equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density \(n_0 = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\), binding energy per nucleon \(\mathcal{E}_0 = -16\ \text{MeV}\), compressibility modulus \(K = 250\ \text{MeV}\), effective nucleon mass \(m_N^*(n_0)/m_N = 0.8\), symmetry energy \(E_{\text{sym}} = 30\ \text{MeV}\) (NLW model parameters).
- Maximum neutron star mass in the NLW model (no hyperons): \(1.92\ M_\odot\).
- Maximum masses in NLWcut models (no hyperons): \(1.96\ M_\odot\) (\(c_\sigma = 0.4\)), \(2.03\ M_\odot\) (\(c_\sigma = 0.3\)), \(2.12\ M_\odot\) (\(c_\sigma = 0.2\)).
- Maximum mass in the KVOR model without hyperons: \(2.01\ M_\odot\).
- Reduction of maximum mass when hyperons are added: KVORH loses \(0.35\ M_\odot\) (to below \(1.67\ M_\odot\)), KVORHcut03 loses \(0.33\ M_\odot\).
- Maximum masses with hyperons and \(\phi\)-meson scaling: KVORHcut03\(\phi\) = \(1.97\ M_\odot\), KVORHcut03\(\phi\sigma\) = \(2.07\ M_\odot\), MKVORH\(\phi\) = \(2.22\ M_\odot\), MKVORH\(\phi\sigma\) = \(2.29\ M_\odot\).
- Empirical hyperon binding energies at \(n_0\): \(\mathcal{E}_{\text{bind}}^{\Lambda} = -28\ \text{MeV}\), \(\mathcal{E}_{\text{bind}}^{\Sigma} = 30\ \text{MeV}\), \(\mathcal{E}_{\text{bind}}^{\Xi} = -15\ \text{MeV}\).
- Threshold density for nucleon-mass flattening in NLWcut models: \(n_* \simeq 1.9\,n_0\), \(3.0\,n_0\), \(4.0\,n_0\) for \(c_\sigma = 0.2\), \(0.3\), \(0.4\) respectively.

## Context

This work extends the KVOR model of Kolomeitsev et al. (2013) by introducing a mechanism to stiffen the equation of state at high density, addressing the hyperon puzzle identified in earlier studies (e.g., the softening problem noted in the literature). The \(\sigma\)-cut and \(\omega\)-cut schemes are novel modifications of the standard non-linear Walecka model of Boguta and Bodmer (1977) and the KVOR model. The results enable the construction of neutron star equations of state that simultaneously satisfy the \(2.01 \pm 0.04\ M_\odot\) mass constraint from PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis et al., 2013) and the flow constraint from heavy-ion collisions (HICflow). The MKVORH\(\phi\sigma\) model in particular produces maximum masses well above the observational limit while respecting radius constraints from thermal radiation of RX J1856 (Trumper et al.) and quasi-periodic oscillations in 4U 0614+09 (van Straaten et al.).
